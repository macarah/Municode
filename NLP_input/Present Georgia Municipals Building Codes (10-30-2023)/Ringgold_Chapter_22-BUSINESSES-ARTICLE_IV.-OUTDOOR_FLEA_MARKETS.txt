DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 22-116. - Title.
This article shall be known and cited as "The Outdoor Flea Market Ordinance of Ringgold, Georgia".
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-117. - Purpose.
This article is adopted in order to promote the health, safety and general welfare of the citizens of the city by regulating outdoor flea markets.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-118. - Function.
This article establishes minimum standards for outdoor flea markets.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-119. - Definitions.
(a)
Generally. For the purposes of this article, certain words and tenses used herein shall be interpreted or defined as follows:
Words used in the present tense include the future tense; the singular number shall include the plural, and the plural the singular.
The word "structure" shall include "arranged," "designed," "constructed," "altered," "converted," "rented," "leased," or "intended to be used."
The word "person" includes any individual, firm, trust, corporation, partnership, public or private association or corporation, or other entity.
The word "shall" is always mandatory and not merely directory.
Terms not herein defined shall have the meanings customarily assigned to them.
The term "governing body" shall mean the mayor and aldermen of the City of Ringgold, Georgia.
Any reference to flea markets shall mean "outdoor flea markets" as defined herein.
(b)
Definitions.
Accessory structure means a subordinate building or structure on the same lot, or part of the main building, occupied by or devoted to an accessory use.
Booth means a structure constructed for the purpose of displaying and selling merchandise.
Building means a structure having a roof, supported by columns or by walls and intended for the shelter, housing, or enclosure of any person, animal or chattel.
Building inspector means the building inspector of the city, or his authorized representative.
Common area means any area or space designed for the joint use of tenants occupying flea markets.
Enclosed structure means a building enclosed on all sides with permanent walls and having a permanent roof.
Enforcing agencies mean the city manager, the building inspector, the county fire department, and the county health department; charged by law with the duty to enforce the provisions of this article.
Flea market stand means that part of an individual lot that has been reserved for the placement of flea market booths, appurtenant structures or additions.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-120. - Existing flea markets.
Upon enactment of this article, all existing outdoor flea markets, including any outdoor operations of any flea market, shall be inspected by the building inspector and county health department to determine compliance or noncompliance with the terms as set forth within this article. All outdoor operations of existing flea markets shall be required to come into compliance with all applicable provisions of this article within six months of the date of inspection.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-121. - Inspections.
The building inspector/code enforcement officer can make inspections as necessary to determine satisfactory compliance with this article as authorized by section 18-32.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-122. - Revocation of permits.
As authorized by section 18-32, building permits for any flea market may be revoked by the inspections and code enforcement department when a flea market is found to be in violation of this article.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-123. - Revocation of licenses.
Any permit or license issued pursuant to this article for the operation of a flea market may be revoked by the mayor and alderman, after notice and hearing, for any of the following causes:
(1)
Any fraud, misrepresentation or false statement contained in the application for permit or license;
(2)
Any fraud, misrepresentation or false statement made by the permittee in connection with the selling of goods, wares or merchandise;
(3)
Any violation by the permittee of this article;
(4)
The conviction of the permittee of any felony, or of a misdemeanor involving moral turpitude;
(5)
The conducting of the business permitted under this article by the permittee in an unlawful manner or in such a manner as to constitute a menace to the health, safety or general welfare of the public.
Notice of hearing for revocation of a permit issued under this article shall be given in writing, setting forth specifically the grounds of the complaint and the time and place of the hearing. Such notice shall be served on the permittee by handing the same personally to the person in charge of the permitted business, or by mailing the same, postage prepaid, to the permittee at his or her last known address, at least five days prior to the date set for hearing.
The giving of such notice shall suspend the permit pending the outcome of the hearing, and business operations under the permit shall cease.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-124. - Emergencies.
Whenever the building inspector finds that an emergency exists which requires immediate action to protect the public health, including violations of any of the applicable rules and regulations of the county health department or state department of public health, he may without notice or hearing issue an order reciting the existence of such an emergency, and requiring that such action be taken as he may deem necessary to meet the emergency, including the suspension of the permit or license. Notwithstanding any other provisions of this article, such order shall be effective immediately. Any person to whom such an order is directed shall comply therewith immediately, but upon petition to the board of zoning appeals, he shall be afforded a hearing as soon as possible.
The provisions of section 22-125 shall be applicable to such hearing and the order issued thereafter.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-125. - Appeals.
An appeal may be taken to the board of zoning appeals of any person, firm, or corporation, or by any officer, department, board or bureau affected by a decision of the building inspector. Such appeals shall be taken within such time as shall be prescribed by the board of zoning appeals by general rule by filing with the planning commission a notice of appeal specifying the grounds thereof. See App. B, sections 10.1—10.6.
The filing of the request for a hearing shall operate as a stay of the notice and the suspension, except in the case of an order issued under section 22-124.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-126. - Variances.
Variances from certain requirements of this article may be granted by the city council according to the provisions of App. B, section 10.3.2.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-127. - Enforcement.
Any person violating or neglecting or refusing to comply with any of the provisions of this article shall, upon conviction thereof, be punished by imposition of a fine not to exceed $500.00 or by imprisonment for a period not to exceed 60 days, or by imposition or both fine and imprisonment in the discretion of the court of proper jurisdiction. Each day that a violation is permitted to exist shall constitute a separate offense.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Secs. 22-128—22-140. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - APPLICATION PROCEDURES AND REQUIREMENTS

Sec. 22-141. - Permits and licenses required.
(a)
Building permit. It shall be unlawful for any person to locate a flea market or to construct, alter or extend any flea market within the limits of the city, unless he holds a valid building permit issued by the building inspector. The right to construct, alter or extend any flea market shall expire 180 days after the issuance of a permit.
(b)
Certificate of occupancy. It shall be unlawful and prohibited to use or permit the use of any flea market land, building or structure for which a building permit is required, and to use or permit to be used any flea market building or structure hereafter altered, extended, erected, repaired, or moved, until the building inspector shall have issued a certificate of occupancy stating that the provisions of these regulations have been complied with.
A certificate of occupancy shall be applied for concurrently with the application for a building permit and shall be issued within ten days after the erection or alteration of such building shall have been completed in conformity with the provisions and requirements of the building codes and these regulations. If such certificate is refused for cause, the applicant therefore shall be notified in writing of such refusal and the cause thereof within ten days.
(c)
Food service permit. Vendors displaying for sale any prepared food item shall first obtain a permit from the county health department to sell such food, and shall continuously display such permit in a conspicuous location on the front of the booth where said activity has been permitted to occur.
(d)
Retail food sales license. Vendors displaying any food item for the purpose of retail food sales shall obtain a retail food sales license from the department of agriculture, and shall continuously display such license in a conspicuous location on the front of the booth where said activity has been licensed to occur.
(e)
Occupational tax. The flea market management must be licensed by the city for the operation of an outdoor flea market. This license shall be a license of privilege that may be revoked by the mayor and council according to section 22-123.
(1)
All vendors shall be licensed for business specifically at the flea market location by the city; or
(2)
All vendors not licensed for business specifically at the flea market location by the city shall file a certificate of registration with the flea market management, and shall pay the registration fee, required for the daily flea market retail sales without a business license for each day that merchandise is displayed for sale. This fee is assessed by the city and collected for the city by the market management.
(3)
Any vendor operating any business in the city outside of the flea market, and selling the same or similar merchandise at the flea market location, shall obtain a business license from the city for the flea market location, and shall pay a license fee based on the items available for sale by the merchant.
(4)
A city occupational tax or valid certificate of registration shall be continuously displayed in a conspicuous location on the front of each flea market booth. A certificate of registration is valid only if it is labeled by the market management to indicate payment of the registration fee, required for daily flea market retail sales without a business license.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-142. - Applications.
(a)
Special approval required. Outdoor flea markets shall not be permitted without special approval of the mayor and council in accordance with App. B, section 7.2, and under such condition shall be permitted only in the following zoning districts: C-2.
Completed applications, a letter of intent and a site plan for special approval must be submitted to the planning commission. Applications must be complete, and site plans must satisfy all requirements of subsection (b) before receiving consideration for special approval by the city council.
(1)
A recommendation of approval, denial, or approval with conditions of the site plan and of the intended location for the proposed flea market shall be made by the planning commission.
(2)
After receiving a recommendation from the planning commission, the city council will hold a public hearing on the application. The city council will approve, deny, or approve with conditions the flea market use for the site proposed and the plan for that site.
(b)
Site plan requirements. Four black or blue line prints of the final site plan, prepared by a registered surveyor or engineer, shall be submitted to the planning commission, and shall contain the following information:
(1)
Name and address of applicant; if the applicant is a partnership, the names and addresses of the partners; if the applicant is a corporation, the names and addresses of the officers and directors.
(2)
Name and address of owner of land.
(3)
Scale of at least one inch = 100 feet.
(4)
North arrow.
(5)
Location (vicinity map) and legal description of the flea market property.
(6)
Area and dimensions of the tract of land.
(7)
Number, location and size of all flea market stands.
(8)
Location of service buildings and any other proposed structures.
(9)
Location of all existing and proposed infrastructure.
(10)
Location of all proposed improvements including fences and buffers.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Secs. 22-143—22-155. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - DEVELOPMENT STANDARDS

Sec. 22-156. - Generally.
(a)
Flea market location. No flea market structure, facility or parking lot shall be located within 300 feet of any residential zoning district boundary.
(b)
Zoning district requirements. Flea markets shall comply with all zoning ordinance area, height, setback, lot coverage, and signage requirements of the zoning district within which the market is located. If the standards of the zoning ordinance conflict with standards of this article, the more restrictive shall apply.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-157. - Site improvement requirements.
(a)
Generally. The condition of soil, groundwater level, drainage and topography shall not create hazards to the property or the health or safety of the occupants. The site shall not be exposed to objectionable smoke, noise, odors, or other adverse influences.
(b)
Soil and ground cover. Exposed ground surfaces in all parts of every flea market shall be paved, or covered with stone screenings, or other solid material, or protected with a vegetative growth that is capable of preventing soil erosion and of eliminating objectionable dust and/or mud.
(c)
Site drainage requirements. The ground surface in all parts of every flea market shall be graded and equipped to drain all surface water in a safe, efficient manner in order to eliminate water pockets, low areas or bogs.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-158. - Setbacks, buffer strips and screening.
(a)
All flea market structures and parking facilities shall be located at least 20 feet from any public street right-of-way or highway right-of-way, and at least 20 feet from the property boundary lines.
(b)
All flea markets shall be provided with screening of a "well-maintained" opaque fence separating the market and adjacent zoning and uses, except along public road frontage used for primary access to the flea market. Said screening shall be a minimum of six feet in height.
(c)
Flea market sites shall be screened from view along street right-of-way frontage used for primary access to the flea market in R-1 and R-2 zoning districts.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-159. - Street system and walks.
(a)
Streets and roads. All flea markets shall be provided with safe and convenient vehicular access from abutting public streets, roads, or highways. Access shall not be permitted from residential streets.
Access to flea markets shall be designated to minimize congestion and hazards at the entrance(s) or exit(s) and allow free movement of traffic on adjacent streets.
(b)
Walks. All pedestrian access to every flea market shall be paved, and shall be of adequate width for the intended use. Sudden changes in alignment and gradient shall be avoided.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-160. - Off-street parking.
(a)
Quantity. Off-street parking areas shall be provided in all flea markets. Such areas shall be furnished at the rate of five and five-tenths spaces per 1,000 square feet of gross sales area.
(b)
Area required. The area required for a parking space for one vehicle shall consist of 300 square feet of lot area, including the parking stall and access aisle.
(c)
Space dimensions. A parking space, exclusive of the area required for access to the space, shall be of an area not less than nine feet wide by 20 feet long for each motor vehicle, such space being fully accessible for the storage or parking of permitted vehicles.
(d)
Handicap parking. Adequate handicap parking shall be provided with such spaces being the closest parking spaces to the market facilities.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-161. - Buildings, structures and signs.
(a)
Structural requirements. All structures, to include booths, shall comply with the building code of the city.
(b)
Booth. Flea market booth shall be of a standard design approved by the building inspector, and shall be provided to all vendors by the flea market management.
(c)
Stands. The flea market stand shall be constructed of materials that will provide an adequate foundation for the placement of flea market booths, thereby securing the superstructure against uplift, sliding, rotation and overturning. The flea market stands shall not heave, shift or settle unevenly under the weight of the flea market booth due to inadequate drainage, vibration, or other forces acting on the superstructure.
(d)
Identification. Each booth shall be numbered. Each number shall be easily identified from the nearest street, driveway, or walkway.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-162. - Infrastructure requirements.
(a)
Water supply. An accessible, adequate, safe and potable supply of water shall be provided in each flea market. Where a public supply of water of satisfactory quantity, quality, quality and pressure is available, connection shall be made thereto and its supply used exclusively. When a satisfactory public water supply is not available, a private water supply system may be developed and used as approved by the state department of public health and state department of natural resources in accordance with rules and regulations for water supply quality control and other applicable rules and regulations. The water supply system of the flea market shall be constructed and connected in accordance with the Standard Plumbing Code, subsection 18-31(5).
(b)
Transient hookup facilities. Transient water, sewer and electrical hookup facilities shall be provided for weekend (Friday through Sunday only) overnight campers, motor homes, travel trailers and recreational vehicles (including self-contained), not to exceed 72 hours in any one week. All vehicle hookups shall comply with the requirements of the Standard Plumbing Code, subsection 18-31(5). The number of such vehicles on site overnight shall not exceed the number of operating overnight hookups provided.
At no time shall any overnight camper, motor home, travel trailer, or recreational vehicle utilize any electrical, sewer, or water connection provided for a flea market booth.
(c)
Restrooms and sewage disposal. There shall be a minimum of one permanent central restroom facility located on the premises. Within that central restroom there shall be separate facilities for men and women.
(1)
Hot and cold water shall be furnished to every lavatory and sink. Cold water shall be furnished to every water closet and urinal.
(2)
Liquid soap, contained in dispensing units, paper towels, and toilet paper shall be provided in every restroom.
(3)
Restrooms shall contain at least the minimum of fixtures required by the Standard Plumbing Code, subsection 18-31(5).
(4)
All flea markets shall be connected to the city sewer system pursuant to the Code.
(d)
Electrical distribution system. Every flea market shall contain electrical wiring systems for each flea market stand requiring electricity. Electrical distribution systems shall be installed and maintained in accordance with applicable electric codes of the city.
(e)
Fire hydrants. Fire hydrants shall be installed as required by the city fire department.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Secs. 22-163—22-175. - Reserved.
DIVISION 4. - REQUIREMENTS FOR OPERATION

Sec. 22-176. - Generally.
The flea market site shall not be exposed to objectionable smoke, noise, odors or other adverse influences.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-177. - Fire protection.
Flea markets shall be subject to all fire prevention ordinances of the city.
(1)
Litter. Flea markets shall be kept free of litter, rubbish and other flammable materials.
(2)
Fire extinguishers. Portable fire extinguishers of a type approved by the fire prevention bureau shall be kept in service buildings and at all other locations designated by the fire prevention bureau, and shall be maintained in good operating condition.
(3)
Cooking.
a.
Fires shall be made only in stoves, incinerators and other equipment intended for cooking.
b.
Cooking shelters, barbecue pits, fireplaces, grills, and woodburning stoves shall be so located, constructed, maintained, and used as to avoid fire hazards and smoke nuisance, both on the property on which used, and on neighborhood property.
c.
No fuel shall be used, and no material burned which emits dense smoke or objectionable odors.
d.
No refuse shall be burned at any time.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-178. - Food service.
(a)
All vendors intending to engage in the sale of prepared food items at the flea market shall first obtain a permit from the county health department for the flea market location before applying for a business license.
(b)
All vendors intending to engage in retail food sales operations at the flea market location shall first obtain a license from the state department of agriculture. All retail food sales licenses shall be continuously displayed in a conspicuous location on the front of the booth where said activity has been licensed to occur.
(c)
All food shall meet health department regulations.
(d)
Any vendor requiring a food service permit or retail food sales license shall at all times conspicuously display said permit on the front of the vending booth where said activity has been licensed to occur at all times.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-179. - Refuse.
The storage, collection and disposal of refuse in the flea market shall meet the requirements of the city solid waste ordinance, and shall be conducted so as to create no health hazards, rodent harborage, insect breeding areas, accident, fire hazards, or air pollution. No building permit shall be issued until the solid waste director approves the proposed plans for the storage and collection of solid waste.
(1)
Refuse storage. All refuse shall be stored in flytight, watertight, rodent-proof containers provided by the flea market management. Such containers shall be provided in sufficient number and capacity to properly store all refuse. The containers may be placed at curbside for collection only on days of scheduled collection. At other times, containers shall be located in a protected manner and restricted from general view.
(2)
Refuse collection stands. Where refuse collection stands are provided for common or joint use, such stands shall be located not more than 100 feet from a using flea market stand, and shall be so designed as to prevent containers from being tipped, minimize spillage and container deterioration, and facilitate cleaning around them. Refuse collection containers shall be located throughout the flea market for the convenient use of patrons.
(3)
Bulk waste containers. Where bulk waste containers of at least three cubic yards are utilized, a suitable concrete pad shall be provided for parking the container. Such pads shall be supplied with a drain connected to sewage disposal facilities, and available water connections convenient for washing the container.
(4)
Refuse collection. All refuse shall be collected at least weekly; up to six times per week if the amount of refuse generated requires more frequent pickup. Where suitable collection service is not available from municipal or private agencies, the flea market management shall provide this service. All refuse shall be collected and transported in covered vehicles or covered containers to the disposal site designated by the city.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-180. - Insect and rodent control.
Grounds, buildings and structures shall be maintained free of insect and rodent harborage and infestation. Extermination methods and other measures to control insects and rodents shall conform to the requirements of the county health department.
Debris. Flea markets shall be maintained free of accumulations of debris which may provide rodent harborage or breeding places for flies, mosquitoes and other pests.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-181. - Fuel supply and storage.
Natural gas, liquified petroleum, fuel oil, and any other fuel supply or fuel storage piping systems shall be installed and maintained in accordance with applicable gas codes of the city.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-182. - Prohibited.
The sale of animals is prohibited in any flea market including, but not limited to, reptiles, birds, felines and canines.
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
Sec. 22-183. - Responsibilities of the flea market management.
The person or persons to whom a license for flea market operation is issued (the flea market managers) shall operate a flea market in compliance with this article and regulations issued hereunder, and shall provide adequate supervision to maintain the park, landscaping, fences, and its facilities and equipment in good repair, and in a clean and sanitary condition. The flea market management shall be available to any city department, official, or agency during all hours of flea market operation, for the purpose of enforcing compliance with the requirements of this article.
(1)
The flea market management shall notify market tenants of all applicable provisions of this article, and inform them of their duties and responsibilities under this article and regulations issued hereunder.
(2)
The flea market management shall supervise the placement of each flea market booth on its stands which includes securing its stability and installing all utility connections.
(3)
The flea market management shall immediately notify the county health department of any suspected communicable or contagious disease within the flea market.
(4)
The flea market management shall notify the inspections and code enforcement department of any overnight campers, motor homes, travel trailers and recreational vehicles remaining on the flea market site in violation of subsection 22-148(b).
(5)
The flea market management shall not rent, lease, nor otherwise provide space to any vendor not in possession of a city business license, unless a certificate of registration has been issued, and the daily retail sales fee has been collected in accordance with subsection 22-141(e). All such registration receipts and sales fees shall be delivered to the license inspector at the end of every business week.
(6)
The flea market management shall not allow any vendor to sell foodstuffs unless that vendor displays a valid retail food sales license, and/or a food service permit, as required in subsections 22-141(c) and (d).
(Ord. No. 2009-0223, § 1, 2-23-09)
