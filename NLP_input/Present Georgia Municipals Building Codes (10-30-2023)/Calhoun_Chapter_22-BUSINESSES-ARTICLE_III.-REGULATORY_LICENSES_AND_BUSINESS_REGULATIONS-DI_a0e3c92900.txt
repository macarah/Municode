Subdivision I. - In General

Sec. 22-191. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Billiard room means any public place where the game of billiards is permitted to be played and for which a charge is made for use of equipment and the revenue received by the proprietor of the room from the charge for the use of the billiard equipment constitutes the primary source of revenue to the proprietor from the operation of the place of business.
Billiards means any of the several games played on a table surrounded by an elastic ledge of cushions, with balls which are impelled by a cue, and shall include all forms of games known as carom billiards, pocket billiards, also known as pool, and English billiards.
(Code 1988, § 12-60)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 22-192. - Location.
No billiard room or pool room may be operated in the city on any level other than the ground floor or first floor of a building.
(Code 1988, § 12-66)
Sec. 22-193. - Hours of operation.
It shall be unlawful for the owner, operator, or manager of any billiard or pool room in the city to operate the billiard or pool room between the hours of 12:00 midnight and 6:00 a.m., or to permit any person to remain in such billiard room between such prohibited hours, except regular employees performing necessary labor on the premises. It shall further be unlawful for any billiard room to operate billiard tables on Sunday.
(Code 1988, § 12-65)
State Law reference— Limitation on local authority to regulate hours, O.C.G.A. § 43-8-2.
Sec. 22-194. - Admission of persons under 18 years of age.
It shall be unlawful for any person who has not reached the age of 18 years to play billiards, or to be permitted to remain in a billiard room for any purpose, unless accompanied by a parent or guardian, or with a written permit from his parent or guardian witnessed by a notary public with seal. If the proprietor of a billiard room is of the opinion that any person desiring admission thereto is under the age of 18 years, such proprietor shall require such person to certify such person's age in writing or to produce a legal permit.
(Code 1988, § 12-67)
Sec. 22-195. - Partitions and screens.
No billiard room shall permit any screens, curtains, blinds, partitions, or other obstructions to be used to prevent a clear view of the interior, which clear view must be maintained at all times. No partitions forming rooms, stalls or other enclosures shall be permitted. This provision, however, shall not be construed to prohibit the maintenance of washrooms and toilet rooms for proper purposes, or the maintenance of closets for storage purposes exclusively.
(Code 1988, § 12-68)
Sec. 22-196. - Gambling.
It shall be unlawful for the owner, operator, or manager of any billiard room or pool room open for public use to allow the use of any dice, cards, dominoes or other games of chance or any form of gambling on the premises.
(Code 1988, § 12-69)
State Law reference— Gambling prohibited, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-20 et seq.

Subdivision II. - License

Sec. 22-211. - Required.
No person shall operate, maintain or keep a billiard or pool table, or any other table of like character, within the city for use by the public without first having obtained a license therefor as required in this article.
(Code 1988, § 12-61)
State Law reference— Authority to license, O.C.G.A. § 43-8-2.
Sec. 22-212. - Application.
In addition to the other information required by this article upon application for a billiard room license, the application shall also specifically state the intended location for the billiard room and the number of tables to be used therein.
(Code 1988, § 12-64)
Sec. 22-213. - Proof of compliance with state law.
Every applicant for a license for operation of a billiard table or billiard room must first show proof of compliance with state requirements precedent to granting of such license by the city.
(Code 1988, § 12-62)
Sec. 22-214. - Fee.
(a)
The annual business license fee for each operator of a billiard or pool room in the city shall be an amount prescribed by the governing body, plus an additional amount so prescribed per billiard table in the proposed location.
(b)
The annual business license fee for each billiard table to be used and operated in a business location not qualifying as a billiard room shall be, per table, an amount prescribed by the governing body.
(c)
The fee schedule shall be on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1988, § 12-63)
