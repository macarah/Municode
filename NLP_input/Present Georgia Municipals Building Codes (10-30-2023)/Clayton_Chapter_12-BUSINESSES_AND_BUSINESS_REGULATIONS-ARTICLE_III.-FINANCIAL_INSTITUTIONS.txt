State Law reference— Authorization to levy and collect a business license tax from depository financial institutions, O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.

Sec. 12-115. - Tax levied.
Pursuant to the authority contained in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93 there is levied for each year, upon each state and national bank, each federal savings and loan association, and each state building and loan association located in the city, not exempted by state law, an annual business license tax at the rate of 0.25 percent of the gross receipts of each such institution, or $1,000.00, per annum, whichever amount is greater. The taxes levied in this section for each year shall be levied on the amount of gross receipts received by each such depository financial institution during the immediately preceding calendar year.
(Code 1999, § 62-37; Ord. of 12-22-1983, § 1)
State Law reference— Municipal and county business license tax on financial institutions, O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.
Sec. 12-116. - Returns.
Each depository financial institution subject to the tax levied by this article shall file with the city not later than March 1 of each year a return of its gross receipts received by it during the immediately preceding calendar year. The returns shall be made in the manner and in the form prescribed by the commissioner of the department of banking and shall be based on the allocation method set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(d). The tax levied pursuant to this article shall be assessed and collected based upon the information provided in the return.
(Code 1999, § 62-38; Ord. of 12-22-1983, § 2)
Sec. 12-117. - When taxes due and payable.
The taxes levied by this article shall be due and payable on April 1 of each year.
(Code 1999, § 62-39; Ord. of 12-22-1983, § 2)
