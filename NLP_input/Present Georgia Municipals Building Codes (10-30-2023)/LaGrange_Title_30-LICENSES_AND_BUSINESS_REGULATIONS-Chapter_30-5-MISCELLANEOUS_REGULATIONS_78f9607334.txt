Sec. 30-5-91. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the following terms shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them in this section:
Pawnbroker: Any person engaged in whole or in part in the business of lending money on the security of pledged goods, or in the business of purchasing tangible personal property on the condition that it may be redeemed or repurchased by the seller for a fixed price within a fixed period of time, or in the business of purchasing tangible personal property from persons or sources other than manufacturers or licensed dealers as a part of or in conjunction with the business activities described in this paragraph.
Pawnshop: The business operated by a "pawnbroker," as defined in this section.
(Code 1972, § 15-102)
State Law reference— Definitions, O.C.G.A. § 44-12-130.
Sec. 30-5-92. - Permit application.
(a)
Any person, before beginning the business of operating a pawnshop or similar place of business, shall file an application with the assistant city manager for a permit to conduct such business. The application for permit shall state the name and address of the applicant, the street and number at which the applicant proposes to operate such business and the names and addresses of all persons having a financial interest in such business.
(b)
The application for license shall list all convictions, guilty pleas, or pleas of nolo contendere by or against the applicant for a period of ten (10) years preceding the date of such application for violation of any felony or misdemeanor of any state, or of the United States, or any municipal ordinance other than for traffic violations, together with a waiver sufficient to authorize investigation of the criminal record of the applicant, if any.
(Code 1972, § 15-103; Ord. No. 90-68, § 1, 1-8-91; Ord. No. 07-12, § 7, 4-24-07; Ord. No. 16-12, § 8, 12-13-16)
State Law reference— Power of city to exercise supervision of pawnbrokers, O.C.G.A. § 44-12-136.
Sec. 30-5-93. - License—Issuance.
(a)
The applicant for a license pursuant to this article must be a person of good moral character and a citizen of the United States. The issuing authority in determining or considering the good moral character of the applicant shall have the right to take into consideration any appropriate facts revealed by its investigation and by consideration of the application for a permit.
(b)
No license shall be issued under this article to any person until the permit required by this article shall have been granted by the council. After the permit has been issued by the council, the assistant city manager shall issue such license as may be required for the conduct of such business on payment of the amount required therefor.
(Code 1972, § 15.104; Ord. No. 90-68, § 2, 1-8-91; Ord. No. 07-12, § 7, 4-24-07; Ord. No. 16-12, § 8, 12-13-16)
State Law reference— Power of city to license pawnbrokers, O.C.G.A. § 44-12-136.
Sec. 30-5-94. - Same—Revocation; suspension.
Any person failing to comply with the provisions of this article or such other ordinances and regulations as may be adopted by the council for the conduct of the business of a pawnshop, shall be subject to having his permit and license to conduct such business revoked or suspended for a definite period of time in the discretion of the council. Such forfeiture and suspension shall result from conviction in the recorder's court of a violation of any provision of this article or any other ordinance or regulation covering the conduct of the business for which such permit and license shall have been issued. The mayor and council may also consider a failure of the permit application to contain any record of convictions required by section 30-5-92 of this article.
(Code 1972, § 15-105; Ord. No. 90-68, § 3, 1-8-91)
State Law reference— Power of city to revoke pawnbroker licenses, O.C.G.A. § 44-12-136.
Sec. 30-5-95. - Record of transactions; inspection by police.
All pawnbrokers shall keep books wherein shall be entered an accurate, detailed description of all property pledged or sold to them. Such description shall include the name of the maker of the article, any identifying mark or number and a description of the kind of material of which it is made so that such article can be reasonably and definitely identified. There shall also be entered in such book the name, address and age of the person by whom such article was deposited or sold, and the date and hour the transaction was made. If the pawnbroker accepting such article is not personally acquainted with the person making such deposit or sale, the pawnbroker shall require identification sufficient to show the name, age, and address of such person and sufficient to show that the identification information given is not fictitious or false. Such book and the articles pledged or sold shall at all times be subject to inspection and examination by the police. Upon refusal of the proprietors at any time to submit their shops to a thorough inspection by the police, the shops shall be immediately closed and the permit and license revoked.
(Code 1972, §§ 15-7, 15-106)
State Law reference— Pawnbrokers' records, O.C.G.A. § 44-12-132 et seq.
Sec. 30-5-96. - Holding period for goods.
Any pawnbroker or person operating under a pawnshop license who takes goods on pawn or buys goods, taking full title thereto, the word "goods" being used in this section in the broadest sense and including all kinds of personal property, shall hold such goods so taken in pawn or purchased for at least thirty (30) days before disposing of the same by sale, transfer, shipment or otherwise.
(Code 1972, § 15-107)
Sec. 30-5-97. - Reports of transactions.
Every pawnbroker shall complete a daily report, in such form as may be prescribed by the chief of police, of all property pledged to or bought by him during each twenty-four-hour period ending at 8:00 p.m. on the date of such report, such report to show the name and address of the pawnbroker; the time of each transaction; the serial number on the pawn ticket; the amount paid or advanced; a full description of the article, including the kind, style, material, color, design, kind and number of stones in jewelry and all identifying names, marks and numbers; and a description of the person selling or pawning, including the name, address, race, sex, weight and height and such other information as may be required by the chief of police. The form required by this section shall be picked up from each pawnbroker by the police of the city on Monday and Thursday of each week, and the same shall be completed and up-to-date at the time it is picked up.
(Code 1972, § 15-108)
Sec. 30-5-98. - Purchases from minors.
It shall be unlawful for any pawnbroker, pawnshop operator or his agents or employees to receive in pawn or to purchase any goods, wares, merchandise or personal property of any character or description from any person who is less than eighteen (18) years of age. Any pawnbroker, pawnshop operator or his agents or employees shall have the burden of requiring identification sufficient to show that any person making a pawn or sale is not less than eighteen (18) years of age.
(Code 1972, § 15-109)
State Law reference— Age of legal majority, O.C.G.A. § 39-1-1.
Sec. 30-5-99. - Sale of knives, etc.
It shall be unlawful for pawnbrokers to sell, offer for sale or expose for sale any kind of metal knuckles, dirks, sword-in-canes, spears, bowie knives, switchblade knives or any blackjack or any other similar weapons, the possession of which is prohibited by the ordinances of the city.
(Code 1972, § 15-110)
State Law reference— Carrying concealed weapon, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-126.
Sec. 30-5-100. - Hours of operation.
The hours within which pawnshops may remain open shall be from 7:00 a.m. to 9:00 p.m.
(Code 1972, § 15-111)
