Sec. 2-31. - Meetings.
(a)
The board of commissioners shall hold regular meetings on the second Tuesday of each month at 9:00 a.m. and on the fourth Tuesday of each month at 7:00 p.m., unless otherwise ordered by the board of commissioners.
(b)
Special meetings may be held upon call by the chairman of the board of commissioners or any three members of the board of commissioners. Due notice of such meetings shall be held in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(d).
(c)
Meetings shall be held in the county commission chambers, Screven County Courthouse, 216 Mims Road, Sylvania, Georgia.
State Law reference— Due notice requirements for other than regular meetings, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(d); requirement to prescribe the time, place and dates of regular meetings of governing authority, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(d); meetings to be open to public, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1.
Sec. 2-32. - Agenda.
There shall be a written agenda prepared prior to each meeting of the board of commissioners. Persons who desire to address the board of commissioners shall notify the county manager by the close of business on the Wednesday preceding the next meeting. The chairman of the board of commissioners may, at his discretion, allow a person not otherwise on the agenda to address the board of commissioners.

DIVISION 2. - COUNTY MANAGER[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— County manager authorized, O.C.G.A. § 36-5-22.

Sec. 2-81. - Creation of office; appointment; removal.
There is created the office of county manager. The county manager shall be appointed in the manner provided by the board of commissioners to serve at the pleasure of such board. No member of the board shall be appointed county manager during the term of office for which he is elected or for a period of one year thereafter. The county manager shall be appointed by an affirmative vote of not less than four members of the board and shall be chosen solely upon the basis of his administrative qualifications with specific reference to his actual experience in or knowledge of accepted practices in respect to the duties of his office as set forth in section 2-82. The county manager may be removed at any time by an affirmative vote, upon notice, of not less than four members, after a hearing, if such is requested by the county manager.
(Ord. No. 89-02, § 1, 11-14-1989)
Sec. 2-82. - Powers and duties.
The county manager shall be the chief administrative officer of the county, head of the administrative branch of the county government, and responsible for the proper administration of all departments of the county. To that end, the manager may, by way of enumeration and not by way of limitation, have the following specific powers and duties:
(1)
Administer and carry out the directives and policies of the board of county commissioners and to enforce all orders, resolutions, ordinances and regulations of the board to ensure that they are faithfully executed. The manager shall not necessarily take action based on the directive of any individual commissioner.
(2)
Provide the board of commissioners, or individual members thereof, upon request, with data or information concerning county government and to provide advice and recommendations on county government operations to the board.
(3)
Prepare and submit to the board of commissioners for its consideration and adoption an annual budget by May 30.
(4)
Establish the schedule and procedures to be followed by all county departments, offices, and agencies in connection with the budget and supervise and administer all phases of the budgetary process.
(5)
Keep the board of commissioners fully advised as to the financial condition and needs of the county.
(6)
Supervise the care and custody of all county property.
(7)
Select, employ and supervise all personnel, and fill all vacancies, positions or employment under the jurisdiction of the board; provided, however, that the employment of all department heads shall require confirmation by the board of commissioners.
(8)
Suspend, discharge or remove any employee under the jurisdiction of the manager. This does not include any elected officials, the county attorney or the county physician.
(9)
See that all terms and conditions in all leases, contracts and agreements are performed and notify the board of any noted violation thereof.
(10)
Order, upon advising the board, any agency under his jurisdiction to undertake any task for any other agency on a temporary basis if he deems it necessary for the proper and efficient administration of the county government to do so.
(11)
Attend all meetings of the board unless duly excused, participate in the discussion of any matter but shall not have a vote.
(12)
Perform such other duties as may be legally required of him by the board of commissioners.
(13)
Negotiate leases, contracts and other agreements, including consultant services, for the county subject to approval of the board, supervise and regulate all purchases of materials and supplies for the county within such limitations as may be prescribed by the board, and make recommendations concerning the nature and location of county improvements.
(14)
Develop or supervise the development of applications for and administration of grants such as the community development block grant, and grants from the Georgia Department of Community Affairs, the Environmental Protection Agency, the Georgia Department of Transportation, and any other state or federal granting agency.
(15)
Delegate purely administrative duties to subordinates in the county government whose work the county manager shall supervise and direct; provided, however, that the delegation or assignment of duties to subordinates shall not relieve the county manager of responsibility for administration of county affairs.
(16)
Act as liaison between the board of commissioners and the public by responding to inquiries and resolving conflicts.
(17)
Perform such other duties as may be prescribed by this division or other duties as may from time to time be required by the board of commissioners.
(Ord. No. 89-02, § 2, 11-14-1989; Ord. No. 2000-04, § 2, 12-28-1999)
Sec. 2-83. - Appearance before the board.
The board of commissioners shall have the right to require the county manager to appear before it at any time and make such reports, oral or written, as it may deem necessary.
(Ord. No. 89-02, § 3, 11-14-1989)
Sec. 2-84. - Bonding requirement.
In lieu of taking and subscribing an oath for the faithful performance of his duties, the county manager shall be required to execute and deliver good and sufficient bond payable to the board of commissioners and conditioned upon faithful performance of his duties. The amount of such bond shall be determined and paid for by the board of commissioners. Such bond shall be delivered to the board within 30 days of the county manager assuming his duties and, upon receipt, shall be filed in the office of the county probate court.
(Ord. No. 89-02, § 4, 11-14-1989)

Sec. 2-91. - Election or appointment of; compensation.
(a)
The board of commissioners may annually elect or appoint a clerk whose duties shall be defined by the board after consultation with the county manager. The clerk's compensation shall be paid out of the general funds of the county.

The county clerk, if appointed or elected, shall be under the direct supervision of the county manager.

DIVISION 4. - CODE ENFORCEMENT OFFICER[5]

Footnotes:
--- (5) ---
Editor's note— Retitled by the editor from "Ordinance Enforcement Officer" to reflect the change in name of this position.

Sec. 2-111. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Ordinance means all ordinances properly adopted by the board of commissioners including, but not limited to, those ordinances codified as part of the Code.
(Ord. No. 2001-03, § 2, 4-10-2001; Ord. of 12-14-2004, § 2)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 2-112. - Creation of position.
There is hereby created for the county the position of code enforcement officer. The board of commissioners may designate by resolution one or more current or new employees of the county as a code enforcement officer as the board determines the duties of the employee may require. Unless otherwise specifically required by state law or the Code, code enforcement officers are the agents of the county specifically authorized, as to all provisions of the Code, to:
(1)
Investigate Code violations;
(2)
Issue citations for violations of the Code or uncodified ordinances;
(3)
Serve citations upon persons accused of Code or ordinance violations; and
(4)
Represent the county in the prosecution of Code or ordinance violations.
(Ord. No. 2001-03, § 3, 4-10-2001; Ord. of 12-14-2004, § 2)
Sec. 2-113. - Prosecution of violations upon citation.
All violations of the Code or uncodified county ordinances may be tried upon citations issued by the county's code enforcement officers with or without a prosecuting attorney as well as upon accusations.
(Ord. No. 2001-03, § 4, 4-10-2001; Ord. of 12-14-2004, § 2)
