ARTICLE II. - RESIDENTIAL DISTRICTS (R-1, R-1A, R-2, R-3)
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 90-36. - Appearance standards for all residential districts.
This section shall apply only to principal dwellings and accessory structures which require a roof and solid exterior walls in residential districts:
(1)
The minimum width of a principal dwelling located on a lot outside of a RMH district shall be 20 feet.
(2)
All principal buildings and all detached garages, carports or other accessory structures located on the front half of a lot shall have a roof surface of wood shakes, asphalt, composition, wood shingles, clay, concrete or metal tiles, slate or built up gravel materials. Metallic sheet roofing surfaces shall not be permitted on the buildings or structures on the front half of the lot. Basic roof construction as to slope and design must be compatible with other residential dwellings and other accessory structures in the subdivision or general district.
(3)
All principal buildings and other accessory structures located on the front half of the lot shall have exterior covering of nonreflective materials. Acceptable materials include but are not limited to wood, masonite, stucco, brick, stone, or other masonry material, so long as the material is compatible to other exterior covering within the subdivision or district.
(4)
All principal dwellings must have a continuous and complete frost-protected perimeter enclosure for the main body. This perimeter enclosure construction is to be constructed of masonry material or of any material approved by the planning commission. This construction must be placed on sound footing.
(5)
All principal dwellings must have a covered porch or landing consisting of a minimum footage of four feet by five feet for the front or main entrance of the dwelling. Construction of this porch or landing is to be compatible with principal dwelling construction and shall be constructed in conformance with the city building and housing codes.
(6)
Every other principal dwelling egress door, not considered as a main entrance, shall have a landing of not less than three feet by three feet and must be in conformance with the city building and housing codes.
(7)
Where the application of the standards within this article will conflict or are inconsistent with the application of character district requirements, the character district requirements shall prevail.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-19; Ord. of 5-27-2009)

Sec. 90-66. - Purpose; applicability.
The R-1 agriculture residential district is established to contain those residences where the principal use of land and structures is predominantly for agricultural purposes, where such areas are not generally recognized as part of the community's immediate urbanizing area, and where intensive land development is unlikely to occur. This division shall apply in the R-1 district and this division is designed to encourage the maintenance of a rural character in the district until more intensive development is feasible.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-20)
Sec. 90-67. - Permitted uses of land and structures.
In the R-1 agriculture residential district, the following uses are permitted:
(1)
Residences, one-family and two-family only, at a density not greater than one dwelling per acre.
(2)
Customary home occupations provided that no person other than members of the family residing on the premises are employed, except as outlined in section 90-645.
(3)
Agricultural pursuits, including field crop farming and forestry, but excluding poultry houses, ranges, and feedlots for the commercial sale of meat or eggs.
(4)
Principal use advertising signs.
(5)
Accessory uses, including barns and garages, except that any automobile in inoperable condition shall not be permitted to park between the residence and the street the property abuts. All automobiles not in operating condition shall be parked in the rear yard or in a garage or carport. All automobile parts shall be stored within a garage building. The presence of any inoperative or junk vehicle as defined in section 90-2 present on any single lot shall constitute a junkyard and shall comply with section 90-646.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-21; Ord. of 3-8-1999)
Sec. 90-68. - Conditional uses of land and structures.
The following uses may be permitted in the R-1 agriculture residential district subject to application to the planning commission and favorable decision thereon in accordance with division 3 of article VII of this chapter:
(1)
Recreational developments, including but not limited to private or public fishing lakes, swimming pools, golf courses, or other recreational developments, provided that a comprehensive development plan for the area is submitted to and approved by the planning commission. The development plan shall include a plot plan of the site and proposed development on maps of a scale one inch equals 100 feet, the location and function of all buildings and modifications to the natural landscape, the location and surface treatment of all proposed streets, appropriate details for providing water and sewer facilities, plus a time schedule setting forth a development program.
(2)
Signboards, panels, billboards, or other advertising signs, excluding principal use advertising signs.
(3)
Public and private schools, provided that a complete site development plan is submitted with the application to the planning commission.
(4)
Churches, clubs, fraternal organizations, and similar places of public assembly, provided that a complete site development plan is submitted with the application to the planning commission and with the provision for off-street parking as specified in division 3 of article VI of this chapter. Such uses shall meet the minimum setback requirements as provided in sections 90-69 and 90-70.
(5)
Public utility substations, if essential for the provision of service to the district, and further provided that no vehicle or equipment is stored on the premises.
(6)
Railroad lines.
(7)
Cemeteries.
(8)
Plant nurseries, provided a complete site development plan is submitted with the application to the planning commission.
(9)
Veterinary clinics, kennels, and similar places for the boarding and care of animals.
(10)
Hospitals and medical clinics.
(11)
Bed and breakfast inns.
(12)
Home-based catering services.
(13)
Group day care homes.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-22; Ord. of 3-8-1999)
