Chapter 48 - TRAFFIC AND VEHICLES
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 48-1. - Uniform rules of the road adopted.
The provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-1 through 40-6-395, known as the Uniform Rules of the Road, are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of this city with like effect as if recited in this section.
(Code 1984, § 10-1-1; Code 1996, § 86-1)
Sec. 48-2. - Temporary regulations authorized.
In cases where the traffic upon the streets of the city may become congested, or upon occasions of parades, or at theaters, churches or schools, or any public gathering or assemblage at any park, building or public place, the police may make temporary rules regulating the traffic. Any person who violates such regulations, after having been warned of such temporary traffic regulations, shall be guilty of a violation of this chapter.
(Code 1984, § 10-1-2; Code 1996, § 86-3)
Sec. 48-3. - Traffic, speed, truck parking and other zones; designation by signs and traffic control devices; schedule; specifications of devices; violations.
(a)
Designation and maintenance of traffic control signs, markings and devices. The chief of police, or his designee, upon approval by ordinance of the mayor and council, is authorized to designate and maintain, with appropriate traffic control signs, markings and devices, after engineering and traffic investigations:
(1)
Speed zones;
(2)
Truck routes and streets to be designated specifically to prohibit various classes of trucks;
(3)
One-way streets and other directional control devices;
(4)
Freight loading zones and regulations;
(5)
Parking and no parking zones and regulations thereon;
(6)
Stop, yield and other right-of-way signs; and
(7)
Stop signals and other traffic signals.
(b)
Issuance of orders designating certain areas. The chief of police, or his designee, is authorized to issue written orders designating by appropriate traffic control signs, markings and devices, after engineering and traffic investigations:
(1)
Pedestrian crosswalks;
(2)
Other safety zones for pedestrians;
(3)
Traffic lanes; and
(4)
Any other sign, marking or zone necessary for orderly and safe conditions on the streets of the city.
(c)
Schedule. The chief of police shall maintain or cause to be recorded a current schedule of all traffic rules, regulations and orders under this chapter, which record shall be available for inspection by the public. For items listed in subsection (a) of this section, this schedule, and any amendments thereto, shall become effective only upon approval thereof by ordinance of the mayor and council. This schedule is hereby incorporated in this section, and copies shall be available for public inspection in the office of the city clerk.
(d)
Specifications of devices. All traffic control signs, signals, devices and markings shall conform to specifications in the "Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices" adopted by the state transportation board. All signs and signals required under this section for a particular purpose shall, so far as practicable, be uniform as to type and location throughout the city. All traffic control devices so erected and not inconsistent with the provisions of state law or this section shall be official traffic control devices of the city.
(e)
Violations. Any violation of any traffic zone, marking, sign or other traffic control device established under this section shall be a violation of this chapter.
(Code 1984, § 10-1-3; Code 1996, § 86-4)
Sec. 48-4. - Authority to close streets and divert traffic.
The police shall have the authority, in the interest of public safety or convenience, to close any street, alley or portion of the same temporarily to vehicular or foot traffic or divert such traffic therefrom.
(Code 1984, § 10-1-4; Code 1996, § 86-5)
Sec. 48-5. - Use of closed streets.
Whenever any street, alley or sidewalk, or any portion thereof, is closed by order of the mayor or police department, or any other designated authority, whether the closing is for repairs, improvements, protection against danger or for any other purpose, it shall be unlawful for any person to enter, cross or travel upon the portion so closed except by permission of the authorities closing the same.
(Code 1984, § 10-1-5; Code 1996, § 86-6)
Sec. 48-6. - Duty to secure load.
It shall be unlawful for the operator of a vehicle to haul trash, refuse, ashes, dirt, sand, concrete mix, gravel, stone, coal, brick, steel shavings or any other material in such a manner as to cause such material to be scattered over any street of the city.
(Code 1984, § 10-1-6; Code 1996, § 86-7)
Sec. 48-7. - Hitching to vehicles.
No person shall hitch or climb onto any moving vehicle, either with or without the consent of its operator, provided that this section shall not apply to the coupling of trailers or proper towing of other vehicles.
(Code 1984, § 10-1-7; Code 1996, § 86-8)
Sec. 48-8. - Speed of trains within city.
Railroad trains traveling within the city shall not be operated at a speed in excess of 40 miles per hour. At no time shall railroad trains be operated at a speed in excess of what is reasonably safe with due regard to attendant circumstances.
(Code 1984, § 10-1-8; Code 1996, § 86-9)
Secs. 48-9—48-34. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - VEHICLE OPERATION

Sec. 48-35. - Free flow of traffic; obstruction.
All persons are prohibited from engaging in driving procedures, or any other activities, which obstruct the free flow of traffic in, around, over and through the streets, alleyways and other public ways of the city. Any person is prohibited from stopping his vehicle (except at a stop signal or stop sign, or to honor another driver's right-of-way, or in an emergency situation) in a manner as to obstruct the free and orderly flow of traffic by the maneuver, or to engage in unduly slow driving procedures so as to obstruct the free flow of traffic or to needlessly circle and block or dawdle or make unnecessarily rapid accelerations and decelerations or to in any other manner constitute a traffic nuisance or hazard.
(Code 1984, § 10-2-1; Code 1996, § 86-36)
Sec. 48-36. - Speed limits established.
(a)
No person shall operate any motor vehicle or tractor upon any of the streets and highways of the city at a greater speed than is reasonable and proper, having regard to the width, traffic and use of the streets and highways, or so as to endanger the property or life or limb of any person.
(b)
Thirty miles per hour shall be the maximum speed on all streets or portions of streets within the city, unless otherwise indicated by officially posted signs designating the maximum vehicular speed upon those streets, as approved by the mayor and council.
(Code 1984, § 10-2-2; Code 1996, § 86-37)
Sec. 48-37. - Slow-moving, heavily laden vehicles to keep to right.
Slow-moving or heavily laden vehicles shall not be driven abreast upon any highway, but one must follow behind the other and keep as near to the right-hand curb as practicable.
(Code 1984, § 10-2-3; Code 1996, § 86-38)
Sec. 48-38. - U-turns.
It shall be unlawful to turn a vehicle on a street so as to proceed in the opposite direction, commonly known as making a U-turn, at any intersection at which a traffic control signal is in place or at any other intersection or place where signs have been erected by the police department prohibiting such turns.
(Code 1984, § 10-2-4; Code 1996, § 86-39)

ARTICLE III. - STOPPING, STANDING AND PARKING

Sec. 48-65. - Vehicle to stop at curb; exceptions.
A vehicle shall not stop elsewhere than at the curb except in an emergency or to allow another vehicle or pedestrian to cross in front of it, or pursuant to the directions of a traffic control signal or the signal of a traffic officer.
(Code 1984, § 10-3-1; Code 1996, § 86-66)
Sec. 48-66. - Parking in alleys.
Vehicles shall not park in public alleys; provided, however, that vehicles may park in public alleys not longer than necessary to load and unload, and when so parked, such vehicles shall stand as close as possible to one side of such alley in order to permit other vehicles to traverse such alley.
(Code 1984, § 10-3-2; Code 1996, § 86-67)
Sec. 48-67. - Duty to remove disabled or stalled vehicle.
Whenever a vehicle becomes stalled upon a street or when, because of damage or other reason, it cannot be moved by its own power and consequently blocks the street or interferes with the use of the street by other vehicles, it shall be the duty of its owner or operator to remove the vehicle by towing or otherwise within a reasonable time.
(Code 1984, § 10-3-3; Code 1996, § 86-68)
Sec. 48-68. - Regulations at specific locations.
The mayor and council may from time to time regulate, restrict or prohibit the standing or parking of vehicles on specified streets or parts of streets or in specified areas. Signs or markings shall be installed and maintained giving notice of such regulations, restrictions and prohibitions, and it shall be unlawful to violate or fail to comply with the directions of any such sign or marking.
(Code 1984, § 10-3-4; Code 1996, § 86-69)
Sec. 48-69. - Vehicles to be parked within marked spaces.
Whenever a space is marked off on any street for the parking of an individual vehicle, every vehicle there parked shall be parked within the lines bounding that space.
(Code 1984, § 10-3-5; Code 1996, § 86-70)
Sec. 48-70. - Manner of loading and unloading freight and merchandise.
When practicable, merchandise and freight shall be discharged and loaded from the side of the vehicle nearest the curb. When such handling is impracticable because of the size or weight of the merchandise or freight, the vehicle may back to the curb, but it shall not stand at any angle which will interfere with the operation of other vehicles.
(Code 1984, § 10-3-6; Code 1996, § 86-71)

ARTICLE IV. - TRUCK ROUTES

Sec. 48-99. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Truck means all utility motor vehicles, which together with load, weigh in excess of 8,000 pounds and which are designed for the purpose of transporting materials, goods, freight, liquids, farm products, machinery, or other items upon the streets or roads of the city.
(Code 1996, § 86-96)
Sec. 48-100. - Designation of streets and parts of streets on which truck traffic is prohibited; signs required.
When appropriate signs are erected giving notice, all trucks are hereby prohibited upon the streets or parts of streets so designated; however, such restriction shall not apply to trucks making local deliveries within the areas so posted when proper documentation is provided to verify such delivery.
(Code 1996, § 86-97)
Sec. 48-101. - Truck routes designated; use regulated.
(a)
Truck routes within the city are hereby designated as follows:
(1)
All state department of transportation (DOT) routes or federal highway system routes.
(2)
Broome Avenue, from W. Third Street (GA 38 - US 84) to W. Crawford Street (GA 39 North).
(3)
Crawford Street, from N. Tennille Ave. (GA 91) to Eastgate Avenue.
(4)
Friendship Avenue, from E. Third Street (GA 38 - US 84) to Crawford Street.
(b)
All trucks other than trucks making local deliveries within the city shall use the streets designated in this section and no others.
(Code 1996, § 86-98)
Sec. 48-102. - Vehicles weighing more than 8,000 pounds; authorized routes through city designated.
All motor vehicles which, together with load, weigh in excess of 8,000 pounds, passing through, entering or leaving the city shall be restricted to such streets and parts of streets designated for such traffic by the mayor and council from time to time, subject to the exceptions set out in section 48-104.
(Code 1996, § 86-99)
Sec. 48-103. - Parking trucks in residential areas.
(a)
All other provisions of this Code notwithstanding, it shall be unlawful for any person to stop or park a tractor, trailer or semitrailer upon any public street in the city in a residential area.
(b)
For the purpose of this section, the term "residential area" shall mean any area within the city which is zoned R-A or R-B.
(c)
For the purpose of this section, the term "trailer" or "semitrailer" shall mean a vehicle with or without motive power, other than a pole trailer, designed for carrying persons or property, and for being drawn by a motor vehicle and so constructed that some of the weight and that of its load rests upon or is carried by another vehicle.
(d)
For the purpose of this section, the term "tractor" shall mean a motor vehicle designed for and principally used for the pulling and movement of trailers and semitrailers.
(Ord. of 3-6-2007, § 86-99(a))
Sec. 48-104. - Vehicles weighing more than 8,000 pounds; duties of operator; exceptions.
Each motor vehicle, falling within the provisions of section 48-101, entering the city by any other road or street than those set out in section 48-101 shall proceed by the shortest route to the nearest street or part of a street set out in section 48-101 and shall proceed thence along the streets or parts of streets named through the city, subject to the following exceptions:
(1)
Vehicles based within city. Motor vehicles having a base within the corporate limits may proceed to such base by the shortest route from the streets and parts of streets set out in section 48-101 and shall likewise return to such streets or parts of streets by the shortest route.
(2)
Servicing vehicle. Motor vehicles may leave the streets and parts of streets set out in section 48-101 for the purpose of purchasing fuel and oil or securing repairs, but in such case they shall be permitted only upon such streets and parts of streets as are upon the most direct and shortest route from the streets and parts of streets set out in section 48-101.
(3)
Loading and unloading. Motor vehicles may leave the streets and parts of streets set out in section 48-101 for the purpose of making deliveries consigned to points upon other streets, roads and alleys in the city and for the further purpose of receiving goods or other property for transportation from points upon other roads, streets and alleys within the corporate limits, but shall proceed to such points by the shortest direct route from the streets and parts of streets set out in section 48-101 and shall return by the same route.
(Code 1996, § 86-100)
