ARTICLE III. - HOUSE NUMBERING AND STREET SIGNS

Sec. 70-61. - House numbering system.
All houses, residences and businesses shall be marked with and by numbers running consecutively from one end of the street to the other, and where any property or lots are vacant, the proper numbers shall be assigned to such vacant lots until houses are built thereon.
(Code 1969, § 20-40)
Sec. 70-62. - Supervision by city clerk.
The house numbering system shall be under the supervision of the city clerk, who shall assign to each property owner or tenant the proper number for each residence or place of business.
(Code 1969, § 20-41)
Sec. 70-63. - Display of number.
The property owner shall be required to mark the residence or place of business with the number assigned at his own expense. Should the property owner fail to mark the residence or place of business with the number assigned within 60 days from the time the number was assigned, then the city shall mark the residence or place of business with the number assigned at the expense of the property owner, and in the event of a failure to pay for the actual expense of the same, it shall be collected from the property owner by execution.
(Code 1969, § 20-42)
Sec. 70-64. - Street signs.
There shall be erected at the intersections of the different streets in the city at the expense of the city, signposts or other markers showing the names of the various streets in the city.
(Code 1969, § 20-43)
Sec. 70-65. - Mutilation, interference with numbers; street signs.
It shall be unlawful for any person to mutilate, destroy or in any manner interfere with any street signs or numbers displayed in the city.
(Code 1969, § 20-44)
