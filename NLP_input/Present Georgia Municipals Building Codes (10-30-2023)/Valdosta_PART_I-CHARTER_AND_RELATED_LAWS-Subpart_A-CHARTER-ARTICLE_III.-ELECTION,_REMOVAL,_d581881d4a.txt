Section 3.2. - Polling place or places.
All elections for mayor and councilmembers, and all other elections, shall be held at a polling place or at polling places designated by the mayor and council.
CHAPTER 2. - CONDUCT OF ELECTIONS

Section 3.11. - Applicability of general laws.
Regular and special elections shall be conducted in accordance with O.C.G.A. title 21, ch. 2, the "Georgia Election Code," as now or hereafter amended.
State Law reference— The Georgia Municipal Election Code, O.C.G.A. § 21-1-1 et seq.
Section 3.12. - Time of election.
Elections shall be held biennially on the Tuesday after the first Monday in November of each odd-numbered year, as provided in O.C.G.A. title 21, ch. 2, the "Georgia Election Code."
Section 3.13. - Candidates to specify office or district for which they run.
Every person who offers for election as mayor or councilmember of the city shall designate with the proper authority in the general election the specific office of mayor or special council district for which he or she offers by naming the office of mayor or the specific council district by number, and, thereupon, he or she shall be qualified, if otherwise qualified, as a candidate for such office of mayor or designated council district.
Section 3.14. - Results of elections; how declared.
The superintendent of elections shall duly declare the result of the elections and shall issue certificates of election to such person as receives a majority of the legal votes polled for the office of mayor and for each of the seven council districts.
Section 3.15. - Special elections.
The mayor and council shall have power to call special elections as provided by law. All special elections shall be conducted in accordance with the applicable provisions of O.C.G.A. title 21, ch. 2, the "Georgia Election Code."
Section 3.16. - Runoff elections.
Should no person receive a majority of the legal votes polled for the office of mayor or for any one or more of the seven council districts, the superintendent of elections shall certify to the mayor and council the names of the two persons who shall have received the largest numbers of legal votes polled for the office of mayor or any council district being considered at the time of election for which no person received a majority of legal votes polled, and the mayor and council shall call a special election for voters to choose between the two persons receiving the largest numbers of legal votes polled for such office of mayor or any council district. This special election shall be held in accordance with O.C.G.A. title 21, ch. 2, the "Georgia Election Code," and it shall be held in the manner provided in section 3.1 of this Charter. The superintendent of this special election shall duly declare the results thereof and shall issue certificates of election to such persons as receive a majority of legal votes polled in the special election, who shall at such time and place as may be prescribed by the ordinance of the city qualify by taking an oath to well and truly perform the duties of their respective offices as mayor and councilmembers during their terms of office, which oath, with certificates of election given by the superintendent, shall be entered on the minutes by the mayor and council, and the originals filed in the office of the city clerk.
Section 3.17. - Voter qualifications.
Each resident of the City of Valdosta possessed of all other qualifications prescribed by law shall be qualified to vote at any election held in the City of Valdosta, for any purpose whatever.
CHAPTER 3. - VACANCY IN OFFICE

Section 3.21. - Occurrence of vacancies.
A vacancy in the office of mayor or councilmember occurs when a duly elected person fails to qualify or when a person who has been duly elected and qualified either dies, resigns, or no longer meets the qualifications of office.
Section 3.22. - Filling of vacancies.
In the event the office of mayor or councilmember shall become vacant for any cause whatsoever, the mayor and council shall, within ten days after the occurrence of such vacancy, call a special election as provided by law to fill the balance of the unexpired term of office; provided, however, if a regular election for the office of mayor or councilmember is to be held within six months after such vacancy occurs, the mayor and the remaining members of the council shall by majority vote appoint a qualified person to the remainder of the unexpired term.
