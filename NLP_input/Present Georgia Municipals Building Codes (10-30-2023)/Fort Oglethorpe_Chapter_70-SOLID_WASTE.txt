ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 70-1. - Business establishments.
It shall be unlawful for the owner, operator, or manager of any public place of business within the corporate limits to allow paper plates, paper napkins, paper cups, paper sacks or any other similar material to collect on or about those premises.
(Code 1986, § 5-2-8)
Cross reference— Businesses, ch. 22.
Sec. 70-2. - Unauthorized accumulation of solid waste; nuisance.
Any unauthorized accumulation of solid waste on any premises is declared to be a nuisance and is prohibited. Failure to remove any existing accumulation of solid waste within a reasonable time as may be fixed by written notice from the city shall be deemed a violation of this Code.
(Code 1986, § 5-2-9)
Sec. 70-3. - Scavenging.
No person other than the owner shall interfere with any container placed for the purpose of storing solid waste pending collection, or remove or take any of its contents, or remove any container from the location where the container has been placed by its owner.
(Code 1986, § 5-2-10)

ARTICLE III. - COLLECTION AND DISPOSAL

Sec. 70-71. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Ashes means and includes the waste products from coal, wood and other fuels used for cooking and heating from all public and private residences and establishments.
Building rubbish means waste material resulting from construction, remodeling, repairs and demolition operations on houses, commercial buildings and other structures, including driveways and walks; and it comprises waste and rejected matter such as excavated earth, stones, bricks, plaster, wallpaper, sheetrock and lathes, lumber, shingles, tile, concrete and waste parts occasioned by the installation or replacement of plumbing, heating systems, electrical work and roofing.
Industrial waste means solid waste materials from factories, processing plants, wholesale establishments, assembling food processing wastes, cinders and ashes, lumber scraps, sawdust, excelsior, shavings, floor sweepings, metal scrap and shavings, glass and other waste products.
Scavenge means uncontrolled picking from discarded solid waste materials.
Standard container means and includes a durable, rust-resistant, nonabsorbent, rodent proof, watertight plastic or metal container having a tight fitting cover, that may be designated by the city from time to time by resolution.
(Code 1986, § 5-2-1; Ord. No. 97-16, § 2, 11-14-1997; Ord. No. 2005-01, 3-14-2005)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 70-72. - Waste acceptable for collection.
Garbage, rubbish and industrial waste constitute waste acceptable for collection by the city.
(Code 1986, § 5-2-2)
Sec. 70-73. - Waste unacceptable for collection.
(a)
The following items constitute waste unacceptable for collection by the city:
(1)
Dangerous materials or substances such as poisons, acids, caustics and explosives;
(2)
Building rubbish;
(3)
Ashes;
(4)
Yard rubbish except as otherwise provided;
(5)
Furniture and appliances;
(6)
Automobile and motor vehicle tires; and
(7)
All other solid waste not specifically authorized in this article.
(b)
Any person responsible for waste not acceptable for collection by the city shall make any arrangements as may be necessary for the collection and disposal of the unacceptable waste.
(Code 1986, § 5-2-3)
Sec. 70-74. - Preparation and storage of acceptable waste.
(a)
Each single-family residence within the city shall utilize an approved standard container for the collection of household garbage and solid waste products generated within that household that is provided for in the city's solid waste ordinance. The city may assess a monthly fee to each single-family residence for the pick-up and removal of the household garbage and solid waste. The monthly fee assessed to single-family residence may be set from time to time by a resolution of the city council.
(b)
Except for apartment complexes, each unit of a multifamily building shall be included in the term "single-family residence" for the purpose of this section.
(c)
Any residence or business which requires a greater quantity than one cart shall be assessed an additional monthly fee that may be set from time to time by the city council by resolution.
(d)
The occupier, owner, or operator of each office or business establishment shall utilize an approved standard container for the collection of it's garbage and solid waste products generated within that office or business that is provided for in the city's solid waste ordinance. The city may assess a monthly fee to each office or business establishment for the pick-up and removal of the garbage and solid waste. The monthly fee assessed to businesses may be set from time to time by a resolution of the city council.
(e)
Any office, apartment complex, or business premises requiring the use of a commercial dumpster by the city shall be exempt of this section.
(f)
No household garbage, solid waste, brush, or yard trimmings, as defined by this article, that is generated within a single-family or multi-family residence, or a business, occupier, owner or operator of a commercial establishment, shall be placed at the curbside for pickup earlier than 48 hours prior to the scheduled date of pickup.
(g)
No person shall transfer household garbage, solid waste, brush or yard trimmings, as defined by the article, to any place within the city for the purpose of disposal at a location other than that person's own residence or commercial establishment.
(h)
The penalty for each violation of this article shall be as prescribed by the municipal court for the City of Fort Oglethorpe as a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 and/or imprisonment not to exceed 60 days.
(Code 1986, § 5-2-4; Ord. No. 2005-01, 3-14-2005; Ord. No. 2020-17, 1-25-2021)
Sec. 70-75. - Dumpsters prohibited in right-of-way.
It shall be unlawful for any person to place refuse dumpsters on city rights-of-way.
(Code 1986, § 5-2-6)

State Law reference— Yard trimmings disposal restrictions, O.C.G.A. § 12-8-40.2.

Sec. 70-106. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Commercial solid waste means all types of solid waste generated by stores, offices, restaurants, warehouses and other nonmanufacturing activities, excluding residential and industrial wastes.
Composting means the controlled biological decomposition of organic matter into a stable, odorfree humus.
Leachate collection system means a system at a landfill for collection of the leachate which may percolate through the waste and into the soils surrounding the landfill.
Municipal solid waste means any solid waste derived from households, including garbage, trash and sanitary waste in septic tanks and includes solid waste from single-family and multifamily residences, hotels and motels, bunkhouses, campgrounds, picnic grounds, and day use recreation areas. The term includes yard trimmings and commercial solid waste but does not include solid waste from mining, agriculture or silvicultural operations or industrial processes or operations.
Municipal solid waste disposal facility means any facility or location where the final deposition of any amount of municipal solid waste occurs, whether or not mixed with or including commercial or industrial solid waste, including but not limited to municipal solid waste landfills.
Municipal solid waste landfill means a disposal facility where any amount of municipal solid waste, whether or not mixed with or including commercial waste, industrial waste, nonhazardous sludges or small quantity generator hazardous waste, is disposed of by means of placing an approved cover thereon.
Yard trimmings means leaves, brush, grass clippings, shrub and tree prunings, discarded Christmas trees, nursery and greenhouse vegetative residuals and vegetative matter resulting from landscaping development and maintenance, other than mining, agricultural and silvicultural operations, provided however, tree trimmings and shrubbery cuttings must be in lengths of three feet or less and not more than four inches in diameter. When placed in bundles, the bundles shall not exceed 35 pounds and shall be placed in an orderly manner at the curb site on the day of collection. No other materials shall be mixed with the bundles.
(Ord. No. 97-16, § 1(a), 11-14-1997; Ord. No. 2005-01, 3-14-2005)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 70-107. - Placement and disposal of yard trimmings; prohibitions.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to place or mix yard trimmings with municipal solid waste within the city.
(b)
Yard trimmings shall not be disposed at any municipal solid waste disposal facility having a liner and leachate collection system or requiring vertical expansion. This includes any solid waste disposal facility with which the city has a contractual agreement to utilize for the disposal of municipal solid waste.
(Ord. No. 97-16, § 1(b), 11-14-1997)
Sec. 70-108. - Sorting, storing, composting and collecting yard trimmings.
(a)
Yard trimmings shall be sorted and stored in the following manner:
(1)
Yard trimmings shall be placed at curbside on the day of collection, separate and apart from other municipal solid waste. All yard trimmings shall be placed as near as possible to the street or sidewalk right-of-way adjacent to the property owner's property, but not so as to get into the street or block the sidewalk. At no time shall yard trimmings be placed in gutters, drains, walkways, alleys or streets of the city.
(2)
All persons engaged in the business of building, clearing of land, clearing of trees, roots and stumps, removal of refuse and debris from premises on which the refuse was or is located, shall have the full responsibility to dispose of such debris, refuse, stumps, trees or other waste by hauling it to a private disposal facility. Except as otherwise provided in this subsection, no such waste shall be piled or placed upon adjoining premises or upon any property which is not permitted by the state environmental protection division as a private waste dump within the city.
(3)
To assist the city in reducing the amount of yard trimmings to be disposed of, the city adopts and recommends the following hierarchy to property owners for handling yard trimmings:
a.
Implementation of naturalized, low-maintenance landscaping requiring little or no cutting;
b.
Implementation of grass cycling by mowing it high and letting it lie;
c.
The stacking of branches into brush piles for use as wildlife habitats and for gradual decomposition into the soil;
d.
Composting on the site where the material was grown, followed by incorporation of the finished compost into the soil at that site;
e.
Chipping woody material on the site where such material was generated;
f.
Chipping woody material for later use as fiber fuel; and
g.
If subsection (a)(3)a.—f. of this section is implemented and still results in yard trimmings which need to be transported to another site, the requirements of section 70-107 and subsections (a)(1) and (a)(2) of this section should be complied with.
(4)
Every person providing contractual services for residential or business property shall be responsible for the disposal of all waste generated as a result of the services rendered. Examples include, but are not limited to, tree cutting and trimming, shrub or vegetation cutting and trimming, remodeling, renovations, landscaping or any other services provided or performed on said premises. The city will not be responsible for the collection and removable of any such waste generated on said property by said services performed.
(b)
Any person violating subsection (a) of this section may be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 or imprisonment of 12 months, or both, as is provided for in the Fort Oglethorpe City Charter, section 4.13.
(Ord. No. 97-16, § 1(c), (d), 11-14-1997; Ord. No. 2005-01, 3-14-2005)

ARTICLE V. - LITTER CONTROL[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— Litter Control Law, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-40 et seq.; littering highways, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-249.

Sec. 70-141. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Litter means all discarded sand, gravel, slag, brickbats, rubbish, waste material, tin cans, refuse, garbage, trash, debris, dead animals, or other discarded materials of every kind and description which are not waste as such term is defined in O.C.G.A. § 16-7-51(6).
Public or private property means the right-of-way of any road or highway; any body of water or watercourse or its shores or beaches; any park, playground, building, refuge, or conservation or recreation area; and residential or farm properties, timberlands, or forests.
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 70-142. - Littering public or private property or waters.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to dump, deposit, throw or leave, or to cause or permit the dumping, depositing, placing, throwing or leaving of litter on any public or private property in this city or any waters in this city unless:
(1)
The property is designated by the state or by any of its agencies or political subdivisions for the disposal of litter and the person is authorized by the proper public authority to use such property;
(2)
The litter is placed into a litter receptacle or container installed on such property; or
(3)
The person is the owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property or has first obtained consent of the owner or tenant in lawful possession or unless the act is done under the personal direction of the owner or tenant, all in a manner consistent with the public welfare.
(b)
Any person who violates subsection (a) of this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction, shall be punished as follows:
(1)
By a fine of not less than $100.00 nor more than $1,000.00;
(2)
In the sound discretion of a court in which conviction is obtained, the person may be directed to pick up and remove from any public street or highway or public right-of-way for a distance not to exceed one mile any litter the person has deposited and any and all litter deposited thereon by anyone else prior to the date of execution of sentence; or
(3)
In the sound discretion of the judge of a court in which conviction is obtained, the person may be directed to pick up and remove from any public park, private right-of-way, or, with the prior permission of the legal owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property, any private property upon which it can be established by competent evidence that the person has deposited litter, any and all litter deposited by anyone prior to the date of execution of sentence.
(c)
The court may publish the names of persons convicted of violating subsection (a) of this section.
(Code 1986, § 5-2-7)
Sec. 70-143. - Prima facie evidence; rebuttable presumption.
(a)
Whenever litter is thrown, deposited, dropped or dumped from any motor vehicle, boat, airplane or other conveyance in violation of section 70-142, it shall be prima facie evidence that the operator of the conveyance has violated this article.
(b)
Except as provided in subsection (a) of this section, whenever any litter which is dumped, deposited, thrown or left on public or private property in violation of section 70-142 is discovered to contain any article, including but not limited to letters, bills, publications or other writings which display the name of a person in such a manner as to indicate that the article belongs or belonged to such person, it shall be a rebuttable presumption that such person has violated this article.
Sec. 70-144. - Enforcement.
All law enforcement agencies, officers and officials of the city or any enforcement agency, officer or any official of any commission of the city is authorized, empowered and directed to enforce compliance with this article.
Sec. 70-145. - Receptacles to be provided; notice to public.
The city may maintain receptacles for the deposit of litter at appropriate locations where the property is frequented by the public, post signs directing persons to the receptacles and serving notice of the provisions of this article, and otherwise publicize the availability of litter receptacles and requirements of this article.
Sec. 70-146. - Designation of containers for household garbage; misuse or vandalization of container.
(a)
As used in this section, the term "household garbage" means animal, vegetable and fruit refuse matter and other refuse matter ordinarily generated as by-products of a household or restaurant, such as tin cans, bottles, paper, cardboard, plastics, and wrapping or packaging materials.
(b)
The city may designate any or all such containers as being suitable for the dumping of household garbage only. If a container is clearly marked "household garbage only," it shall be unlawful for any person to dump any refuse or other material into the container other than household garbage.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to set fire to the contents of, indiscriminately scatter or disperse the contents of, or otherwise vandalize any containers provided by the city for the dumping of trash or garbage.
(d)
Any person who violates subsection (b) or (c) of this section shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in Charter section 4.13.
Sec. 70-147. - Prohibition of littering of unsolicited publications.
(a)
Any person, firm or corporation who places unsolicited publications within the city shall retrieve any such publications which are not picked up by a resident within five days of delivery.
(b)
Should any papers be found in the public rights of way or public drainage ditches which here appear to be papers which residents have not picked up in a timely manner and which have not been picked up by the person or business entity who distributes such papers, such person or business entity who failed to retrieve the papers within five days of delivery shall be prosecuted for a violation of this section.
(c)
The chief of police or his designee, or such other person as the mayor and council may hereafter designate, shall enforce the provisions of this section and issue citations for a violation.
(d)
A violation of this section shall be punished by a fine of up to $100.00, for which prosecution shall lie in the municipal court.
(Ord. No. 2001-02, § 1, 2-26-2001; Ord. No. 2001-16, § 1, 8-13-2001)
