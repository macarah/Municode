DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Secs. 2-82—2-106. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - BONDS AND BENEFITS

Sec. 2-107. - Bonds.
Except as otherwise provided, by law, the mayor and council may require any department heads, city officials and employees, before entering upon the discharge of their duties, to give good and sufficient bond in amounts decided by the mayor and council. Said bonds shall be payable to the city for the faithful performance of the respective duties of said persons and to secure against corruption, malfeasance, misappropriations or unlawful expenditures. Said surety bonds shall be obtained from a surety company licensed to do business in the state and approved by the mayor and council, and the premiums thereon shall be paid by the city.
(Code 1975, § 3-201)
Sec. 2-108. - City officials.
The mayor and council shall appoint a qualified person to each of the following positions: city clerk, city superintendent, maintenance engineer, and chief of police.
(Code 1975, § 3-203)
Sec. 2-109. - Contents of classifications.
The duties, qualifications, classifications, salary scale, job description and supervision for jobs as a city employee shall be provided in the official job classification and pay schedule as approved by the mayor and council and on file in the office of the city. The official job classification and pay schedule, and any amendments or changes thereto, is hereby incorporated into this Code and made a part hereof as fully as if set forth in this division.
(Code 1975, § 3-204)
Sec. 2-110. - Social security benefits.
It is hereby declared to be the policy and purpose of the city to extend at the earliest date possible under the agreement entered into by the state and the Federal Social Security Administration, to the employees and officials thereof, and its instrumentalities, not excluded by law or by this division, the benefits of the system of Old Age and Survivor's Insurance as authorized by the Federal Social Security Act and amendments thereto, including Public Law 734 of the 81st Congress, and as provided for in 1951 Georgia Laws, p. 457, approved February 21, 1951. In pursuance of said policy, and for that purpose, the city shall take such action as may be required by applicable state or federal laws or regulations.
(Code 1975, § 3-205)
Sec. 2-111. - Employees' Retirement System of Georgia—Employees covered.
City employees, including those in elective positions, shall be covered by the insurance system of the Employees' Retirement System of Georgia.
(Code 1975, § 3-205A)
Sec. 2-112. - Same—Agreements authorized.
The mayor, or other chief executive officer, of the city is hereby authorized and directed to execute all necessary agreements and amendments thereto with the employees' retirement system of the state and officials as provided for in the act of the General Assembly cited in section 2-110.
(Code 1975, § 3-206)
Sec. 2-113. - Withholdings from salaries.
Withholdings from salaries or wages of employees and officials for the purpose provided in section 2-110 are hereby authorized to be made in the amounts and at such times as may be required by applicable state or federal laws or regulations, and shall be paid over to the state agency.
(Code 1975, § 3-207)
Sec. 2-114. - Appropriations for retirement system.
There shall be appropriated from general funds such amounts at such times as may be required by applicable state or federal laws/regulations for employees' contributions, administrative expenses, and a guaranty fund to assure compliance with section 2-112. Such funds shall be paid over to the employees' retirement system of the state in accordance with the regulations established by said agency.
(Code 1975, § 3-208)
Sec. 2-115. - Records; reports.
The city clerk shall keep such records and make such reports as may be required by applicable state or federal laws or regulations.
(Code 1975, § 3-209)
Sec. 2-116. - Exclusions.
There is hereby excluded from this article any authority to make any agreement with respect to any position or any employee or official now covered, or authorized to be covered, by any other ordinance or law creating any retirement system for any employee or official of the city or its instrumentalities.
(Code 1975, § 3-210)
Sec. 2-117. - Ineligible persons.
There is hereby excluded from this article any authority to make any agreement with respect to any position, or any employee or official not authorized to be covered by applicable state or federal laws or regulations.
(Code 1975, § 3-211)
Sec. 2-118. - Amendment to original plan.
The mayor (or other designated official) of the city is hereby authorized and directed to execute an agreement amending the original plan and agreement with the employees' retirement system of the state providing for the release of social security escrow deposits.
(Code 1975, § 3-212)
Editor's note— The City of Pearson entered into a Plan and Agreement with the Employees' Retirement System of Georgia on July 2, 1954, to extend the insurance system to city employees and officials, and amends that original plan from time to time as allowed under state law.

DIVISION 3. - RESTRICTIONS

Sec. 2-139. - Limitation on amount of purchases.
The purchasing power of all employees authorized to make purchases (city superintendent, city clerk, and police chief) and the mayor shall be limited to an amount as set by resolution of the mayor and council from time to time, unless the purchase is preapproved by a majority of the council.
(Code 1975, § 3-215; Mo. of 11-12-1992; Mo. of 1-12-1993)
Sec. 2-140. - Purchase orders.
No purchases shall be made for the city without a purchase order issued from the city clerk's office, itemized and signed by the mayor or city clerk.
(Code 1975, § 3-216; Ord. of 12-12-1995)
Sec. 2-141. - Payment of bills.
No bills shall be paid without the approval of the mayor and council at a regular meeting excluding normal operating expenses, utilities, group insurance, etc.
(Code 1975, § 3-217; Ord. of 8-9-1994)
Sec. 2-142. - City vehicles.
All persons using city vehicles shall include the mileage reading on all purchase orders for gasoline. No city vehicle shall be used for any purpose outside the city limits except in emergency cases approved by the mayor and council.
(Code 1975, § 3-218)
Sec. 2-143. - Conduct of officers.
Should any officer appointed by the mayor and council be intoxicated while on duty or be guilty of gross neglect of his duties, or refuse to perform the same, or be guilty of any abuse of the powers of his office, or official malpractice of any kind, he shall be notified to appear before the council to answer said charge, and may be reprimanded, fined, or removed from office, or all upon a vote of a majority of the council. The mayor in his discretion may suspend such officer and appoint someone to fill his place until a decision of the council is rendered.
(Code 1975, § 3-219)
State Law reference— Code of ethics for public officers and employees, O.C.G.A. § 45-10-1 et seq.
Sec. 2-144. - Acceptance of gratuities.
No municipal officer or employee shall accept any money or other consideration or favor from anyone other than the city for the performance of an act which he would be required or expected to perform in the regular course of his duties; nor shall any officer or employee accept, directly or indirectly, any gift, gratuity or favor of any kind which might reasonably be interpreted as an attempt to influence his actions with respect to city business.
(Code 1975, § 3-220)
State Law reference— Conflicts of interest for public officers and employee generally, O.C.G.A. § 45-10-20 et seq.
Sec. 2-145. - Outside employment.
No full-time officer or employee of the city shall accept any outside employment without a written authorization from the mayor. The mayor shall not grant such authorization if the work is likely to interfere with the satisfactory performance of the officer's or employee's duties, or is incompatible with his municipal employment, or is likely to cast discredit upon or create embarrassment for the city.
(Code 1975, § 3-221)
Sec. 2-146. - Use of municipal time, facilities, etc.
No municipal officer or employee shall use or authorize the use of municipal time, facilities, equipment or supplies for private gain or advantage of himself or any other private person or group. Provided, however, that this prohibition shall not apply where the mayor and council or city clerk has authorized the use of such time, facilities, equipment or supplies, and the city is paid at such rates as are normally charged by private sources for comparable services.
(Code 1975, § 3-222; Ord. of 3-8-1977)
Sec. 2-147. - Unethical use of position.
No municipal officer or employee shall make or attempt to make private purchases, for cash or otherwise, in the name of the city, nor shall he otherwise use or attempt to use his position to secure unwarranted privileges or exemptions for himself or others.
(Code 1975, § 3-223)
Sec. 2-148. - Strikes and unions.
No municipal officer or employee shall participate in any strike against the city.
(Code 1975, § 3-224)
Secs. 2-149—2-179. - Reserved.
