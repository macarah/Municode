ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 6-1. - Definitions.
The definitions set forth in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2 (Alcoholic Beverages; General Provisions; Definitions), shall be effective as definitions of the words, terms and phrases used in this chapter. All words, terms and phrases used herein, other than those specifically defined elsewhere in this chapter, shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2, and shall have the same scope and effect that the same words, terms and phrases have where used in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2.
Sec. 6-2. - Retail license required.
Each retail dealer of malt beverages, wines or spirituous liquors, as such terms are defined under state law, who does business within this city shall be required to obtain a license from the city clerk in the manner specified in this chapter.
(Code 1969, § 3-20; Code 1981, § 3-1; Ord. of 5-15-1978, § 1)
Sec. 6-3. - Wholesale beer license and payment of tax prerequisite to doing business.
It is unlawful for any person to engage in the business of wholesaling malt beverages within the city without first obtaining the license from the city clerk and paying the required license tax.
(Code 1969, § 3-20; Code 1981, § 3-2)
Sec. 6-4. - License application, personnel statement—Required.
(a)
Each applicant for either a new or renewal licenses as a retail dealer of spirituous liquors shall submit an application for license upon a form approved by the council. Said application shall be complete in every detail and shall be accompanied by the appropriate license fee. If the application is for a new license or transfer of an existing license to a new owner or a different location, the application shall be submitted to the clerk who shall submit the same to council for consideration under the guidelines set forth under this chapter. The council shall consider the application at its next regular meeting which occurs no less than five days subsequent to the application being submitted. If the application is for a renewal license with no change of ownership and no change of location, the application must be submitted to the clerk prior to December 1 of the year preceding the year for which the application is made. The clerk shall review said application and if there are no changes in ownership or location, then the clerk shall issue a license for the coming calendar year to said applicant. All application shall be accompanied by a verified personnel statement for said applicant.
(b)
Each applicant for either a new or renewal licenses as a retail dealer of malt beverages and wines shall submit an application for license upon a form approved by the council. Said application shall be complete in every detail and shall be accompanied by the appropriate license fee. If the application is for a new license or transfer of an existing license to a new owner or a different location, the application shall be submitted to the clerk who shall review the same under the guidelines set forth under this chapter and issue said license if the application meets the guidelines. If the application is for a renewal license with no change of ownership and no change of location, the application must be submitted to the clerk prior to December 1 of the year preceding the year for which the application is made. The clerk shall review said application and if there are no changes in ownership or location, then the clerk shall issue a license for the coming calendar year to said applicant.
(Code 1981, § 3-3; Ord. of 5-15-1978, § 3; Ord. No. 1992-2, 9-21-1992; Ord. No. 2008-9, § 1, 3-18-2002)
Sec. 6-5. - Same—Misstatement or concealment of material fact.
Any misstatement or concealment of a material fact in the application or verified personnel statement required by this chapter by the licensee or any employee or any director or officer of any corporation shall be grounds for the revocation of the license issued under this chapter.
(Code 1981, § 3-4; Ord. of 5-15-1978, § 24)
Sec. 6-6. - License fees—Schedule.
The annual business license fee for retail dealers whose place of business is located within the city shall be as established by the city council from time to time.
(Code 1981, § 3-5; Ord. of 5-15-1978, § 2)
Sec. 6-7. - Same—Payment; fee to defray investigative and administrative cost.
Each application for initial license or transfer of an existing license, excluding renewals, under this chapter shall be accompanied by a certified check for the full amount of the license fee, together with a separate check or cash, to defray investigative and administrative costs. If the application is denied and the license refused, or if the applicant withdraws the application prior to its being submitted to the council, the deposit representing the license fee shall be refunded, but the cost paid for investigation shall be retained. The amount of the fee to defray the license investigation and administrative costs shall be as established by the city council from time to time.
(Code 1981, § 3-6; Ord. of 5-15-1978, § 11)
Sec. 6-8. - Factors for council consideration.
The council, in passing upon an initial application for a license or a transfer of an existing license shall be guided by the following factors as to whether to grant or deny such application:
(1)
The proximity of other establishments selling malt beverages, wines or spirituous liquors to the proposed location;
(2)
The character of the neighborhood immediately adjacent to the proposed location;
(3)
The proximity of churches, schools and playgrounds to the proposed location;
(4)
Whether the proposed location has adequate off-street parking facilities or other parking available for its patrons;
(5)
Whether the location would tend to increase and promote traffic congestion and resulting hazards therefrom;
(6)
Whether any similar license previously issued for such location was ever revoked for cause by the city council; and
(7)
Whether the applicant has ever sold malt beverages, wine or spirituous liquors illegally.
(Code 1969, § 3-1; Code 1981, § 3-7; Ord. of 5-15-1978, § 4)
Sec. 6-9. - Licensee—Qualifications.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to issue a license for the retail sale of spirituous liquors to:
(1)
A person who is not a resident of the county for a period of 12 months prior to such person's making application.
(2)
A person who is not a citizen of the United States.
(3)
A person disqualified under section 6-10.
(4)
A partnership, unless all partners qualify under this chapter and subsections (a)(1), (2) and (3) of this section.
(5)
A corporation, unless all shareholders, officers and directors qualify under this chapter and subsections (a)(1), (2) and (3) of this section.
(6)
A person whose place of business is conducted by a manager or agent unless said manager or agent possesses the same qualifications as a licensee.
(7)
Any law enforcing public official or employee or elected public official of the city.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to issue a license for the retail sale of malt beverages and wine to:
(1)
A person who is not a resident of the state.
(2)
A person who is not a citizen of the United States.
(3)
A person disqualified under section 6-10.
(4)
A partnership, unless all partners qualify under this chapter and subsection (b)(2) and (3) of this section.
(5)
A corporation, if any officer, manager or director thereof, or any stockholder owning 25 percent or more of the stock of such corporation, would not be eligible to receive an alcoholic beverage license hereunder section 6-10.
(6)
A person whose place of business is conducted by a manager or agent unless said manager or agent possesses the same qualifications as a licensee.
(7)
Any law enforcing public official or employee or elected public official of the city.
(Code 1981, § 3-8; Ord. of 5-15-1978, § 14; Ord. No. 1990-1, § 1, 3-5-1990; Ord. No. 2008-9, § 2, 3-18-2002)
Sec. 6-10. - Same—Moral character; criminal record.
In no event shall any license be granted to any person or officer or director of any such corporation who has been convicted or has plead guilty or entered a plea of nolo contendere to any crime involving moral turpitude, lottery or illegal possession or sale of narcotics or other controlled substances, within a period of ten years immediately prior to the filing of such application, nor shall any such person sell spirituous liquors, malt beverages or wine. Should any applicant's partner, officer or employee engaged in the sale of spirituous liquors, malt beverages or wine, after a license has been granted, be convicted or plead guilty or nolo contendere to a crime involving moral turpitude or the violation of any laws regulating the sale of narcotics, or other controlled substances, liquor, wine or beer, or the lottery laws of the state, such license shall be instantly revoked and cancelled.
(Code 1969, § 3-21; Code 1981, § 3-9; Ord. of 5-15-1978, § 22)
Sec. 6-11. - Qualifications of manager or agent.
The manager or agent of any place of business shall be required to possess the same qualifications as a licensee; and the licensee shall be responsible for the manager or agent making application on a form furnished by the city and being approved before beginning such duties.
(Code 1981, § 3-10; Ord. of 5-15-1978, § 17)
Sec. 6-12. - License expiration and renewal.
All licenses granted hereunder shall expire on December 31 of each year. Licensees who desire to renew their license shall file an application with the city clerk on the form provided by the council for the renewal of the license for the ensuing year. Application for renewal must be filed before December 1 of each year; otherwise, no renewal license shall be granted, but an application for renewal, in the event that it is not timely filed, shall be treated as an initial application, and the applicant shall be required to comply with all the rules and regulations for the granting of licenses as if no previous license had been held. All licenses granted hereunder shall be for the calendar year and the full license fee must be paid for a license issued at any time during a calendar year except for the transfer of an existing license. The requisite license fee shall be paid to the business license clerk.
(Code 1981, § 3-11; Ord. of 5-15-1978, § 12)
Sec. 6-13. - Transfer of license—Conditions.
A license for the retail sale of malt beverages, wine or spirituous liquors shall not be transferable to another person or location without the expressed consent of the city council under the guidelines set forth under sections 6-4, 6-8 through 6-10, 6-14 and 6-21. The transfer of an existing license shall not require the payment of an additional license fee.
(Code 1981, § 3-12; Ord. of 5-15-1978, § 5)
Sec. 6-14. - Same—Change of ownership.
(a)
Nothing in this chapter shall prohibit one or more of the partners in a partnership holding a license or one or more of the shareholders in a corporation holding a license to withdraw from the partnership or corporation in favor of one or more of the other partners or shareholders, who were partners or shareholders at the time of the issuance of the license. However, such a withdrawal shall not serve to bring any new ownership into the partnership or corporation.
(b)
Where a transfer of an existing store location is approved and there is no change in the ownership of the business, the license fee paid for the old location shall be applied to the new location.
(c)
A licensee may take in partners of additional stockholders. Under this section, an additional partner or new stockholder must be approved by the city council, in accordance with the requirements of this chapter, and a new license must be obtained, but said license will be issued without the payment of a new license fee.
(Code 1981, § 3-13; Ord. of 5-15-1978, § 13)
Sec. 6-15. - Consumption on the premises.
(a)
No malt beverages or wine shall be sold for consumption on the premises except in an establishment which derives at least 75 percent of its total annual gross food and beverage sales from the sale of prepared meals or food and which is located within a proper zoning district.
(b)
Any establishment where malt beverages or wine is sold for consumption on the premises shall have a seating capacity of 40 persons, excluding stools and counters.
(1)
Premises. No malt beverage or wine license shall be issued to any person unless the building in which the business will be located is complete and detailed plans of the building and outside premises are attached to the application, or unless proposed plans and specifications and a building permit of a proposed building to be built are attached to the application. The completed building or the proposed building shall comply with ordinances of the city, regulations of the state revenue commissioner and the state. The proposed building shall also be subject to final inspection and approval when completed by the building inspector. Each building in which the business will be located shall contain sufficient lighting so that the building itself and the premises on all sides of the building are readily visible at all times from the front of the street on which the building is located so as to reveal all of the outside premises of such building. Each applicant for a malt beverage and wine license shall attach to the application evidence of ownership of the building or proposed building or a copy of the lease if the applicant is leasing the building. If the applicant is a franchisee, then such applicant shall attach a copy of the franchise agreement or contract with the application. All premises for which a malt beverage or wine license shall be issued shall afford therein adequate sanitary toilet facilities and shall be adequately illuminated so that all hallways, passageways and open areas may be clearly seen by the customers therein.
(2)
Open area and patio sales.
(3)
Malt beverage and wine sales can be made by a licensed consumption on-premises establishment in a patio/open area type environment if the establishment has been approved to do so by the mayor and city council.
(4)
The requirement for approval is that the patio/open area be enclosed by some structure providing for public ingress/egress only through the main licensed premises. The purpose of this requirement is to prevent a customer from leaving the outside sales area with an open drink without the licensee's knowledge.
(5)
The height of such structure shall be a minimum of four feet above ground level. It does not have to be solid nor does it have to restrict visibility into and out of the patio/open sales area. It must be permitted and approved by the city's building inspection department and all other applicable governing regulations or codes.
(6)
The only exit from this type area is to be through the licensed establishment's main premises and through an approved fire exit (not for general public use unless an emergency exists). The fire exit should be of the type that sounds an alarm so that the establishment will be alerted in the event of unauthorized use when no emergency exists.
(7)
If a licensee desires a patio/open sales area inside an existing structure, plans will be reviewed and approved on an individual basis by the mayor and city council. Interior type patio/open sales areas must also meet the requirements of the city's development and fire codes.
(8)
Nothing contained in this section shall prohibit a hotel or motel with a on-premises consumption license from making sales and allowing consumption of malt beverages and wine in ballrooms, meeting rooms, reception rooms, or patio areas of such hotel or motel provided such functions are catered in connection with a meeting, conference, convention or similar type gathering at such hotel or motel.
(9)
No outside consumption.
(10)
It is prohibited for customers to leave the premises with open beer or wine beverages and it is the licensee's responsibility to ensure that no open beverages are sold and carried out.
(11)
It is prohibited for customers to gather outside a malt beverage and/or wine establishment and consume beer and/or wine.
(12)
It is prohibited for the manager or any employee to allow persons to gather outside a beer and wine establishment and consume a malt beverage or wine.
(13)
Noise from establishments prohibited. Noise will not be allowed that can be heard outside the physical boundaries of establishments licensed under this chapter.
(14)
Types of entertainment, attire and conduct prohibited. The following types of entertainment, attire and conduct are not permitted upon any premises licensed to sell, serve or dispense beer or wine or upon any premises licensed under the business and occupational ordinances of the city in which malt beverages and/or wine are consumed, allowed or dispensed on the premises:
a.
The employment or use of any person, in any capacity, in the sale or service of beer and/or wine while such person is unclothed or in such attire, costume or clothing as to expose to view any portion of the female breast below the top of the areola or of any portion of the pubic hair, anus, cleft of the buttocks, vulva or genitals; or
b.
The holding, promotion or allowance of any contest, promotion, special night or any other activity where patrons of the licensed establishment are engaged or allowed to engage in any of the conduct prohibited in this section or prohibited under O.C.G.A. § 3-3-41.
(15)
Enforcement. The chief of police will cause periodic inspections of all establishments licensed to sell beer and/or wine under this chapter during the hours in which the premises are open for business to ensure compliance with this chapter and state law. If a violation of this chapter is observed the licensee or employee who is in violation of this chapter will be issued a summons to appear at the next session of municipal court. A thorough investigation of the violation will be conducted and the results of the investigation forwarded to the city manager.
(16)
Establishment closure in cases of emergency. The chief of police, or his/her designee, may immediately close an establishment licensed under this chapter in case of emergency, for the safety of the public or to investigate a crime, for a period of time not to exceed 24 hours.
(17)
Any prior license for on-premises consumption. Any prior license for on-premises consumption now in existence shall be allowed to continue and said license may be renewed and transferred under the applicable provisions of this chapter.
(Code 1981, § 3-14; Ord. of 5-15-1978, § 8; Ord. No. 2008-7, 3-15-2004)
Sec. 6-16. - Number of licenses available.
(a)
The maximum number of licenses for the retail sale of spirituous liquors which may be outstanding at any one time shall not exceed five; provided, however, that an additional license for the retail sale of spirituous liquors shall become available for each population increase in the city of 700 residents according to any future decennial census or adjusted census report published by the U.S. Bureau of Census as compared to the population of the city for the year 2000 according to the 2000 decennial census.
(b)
For the purposes of issuing a new license, applications for a new license shall be considered by the mayor and council in the chronological order in which said applications are submitted to the city clerk, after the additional license becomes available under the terms of this section. Any new license shall be granted to the first qualified applicant and no other applications shall be considered by the council. The requisite license fee shall be paid to the business license clerk.
(Code 1981, § 3-15; Ord. of 5-15-1978, § 21; Ord. No. 2008-6, § (A), 2-18-2002)
Sec. 6-17. - Proximity to churches, schools, etc.
No license for off-premises consumption shall be issued hereunder unless the business premises described in the license application meets the distance requirements of O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21. Every application for a license shall include a scale drawing of the location of the proposed premises showing the distance to the uses described as set forth in this section and state law and a certificate of a registered land surveyor or professional engineer that such location complies with these requirements.
(Code 1981, § 3-16; Ord. of 5-15-1978, § 23; Ord. No. 1991-1, § 2, 3-5-1990; Ord. No. 2008-6, § (B), 2-18-2002)
Sec. 6-18. - Hours of sale.
It shall be unlawful to sell, or offer for sale, in or upon any licensed premises, any malt beverages, wine or spirituous liquors, except between the hours of 6:00 a.m. and 12:00 midnight on weekdays and Saturdays; provided, however, that any premises which is licensed for on-premises consumption shall be allowed to remain open on Friday nights until 12:00 midnight and for three hours thereafter until 3:00 a.m., Saturday.
(Code 1969, § 3-4; Code 1981, § 3-17; Ord. of 5-15-1978, § 7; Ord. No. 1983-2, § 1, 5-21-1983; Ord. No. 1985-8, § 1, 12-16-1985)
Sec. 6-19. - Closing on Sundays and election days.
It shall be unlawful to open any malt beverage, wine or spirituous liquor store in the city on Sundays or on election days.
(Code 1969, § 3-5; Code 1981, § 3-18)
State Law reference— Sale of alcoholic beverages on Sundays and election days prohibited, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20.
Sec. 6-20. - Safe and sanitary conditions.
All premises used for the sale of malt beverages, wine or spirituous liquors shall be kept in a safe and sanitary condition as required by the ordinances of the city and the laws of the state.
(Code 1981, § 3-19; Ord. of 5-15-1978, § 6)
Sec. 6-21. - Zoning.
No license for the retail sale of malt beverages, wine or spirituous liquors shall be issued unless the location subject to the license is properly classified as B-2 according to the zoning ordinances of the city (as set forth in chapter 94).
(Code 1981, § 3-20; Ord. of 5-15-1978, § 10)
Sec. 6-22. - Employment of minors.
No retail dealer in malt beverages, wines or spirituous liquors in the city shall employ or permit to be employed, in the establishment or place of business at which malt beverages, wines or spirituous liquors are sold or dispensed, any person under the age of 18 years.
(Code 1981, § 3-22; Ord. of 5-15-1978, § 16)
Sec. 6-23. - Regulations of package store for off-premises consumption—Interior visibility.
For the purpose of regulating the package store establishment, the following regulations shall apply:
(1)
Each licensed establishment shall have an open glass front and shall be well-lighted for clear vision from the outside.
(2)
No sign of advertisement shall be posted on the glass which may mar or obstruct the view.
(3)
Prices shall not be advertised for either wine, malt beverages or spirituous liquors by painting on or attaching to glass or by standing or hanging up inside or outside of glass in such manner as to reduce vision from the outside to the inside of the package store.
(Code 1981, § 3-23; Ord. of 5-15-1978, § 18)
Sec. 6-24. - Same—Opening or consuming alcoholic beverage.
It shall be unlawful for the operator of any package store to permit any person to open any containers of alcoholic beverages or to drink any alcoholic beverages in such store.
(Code 1981, § 3-24; Code 1969, § 3-6)
Sec. 6-25. - Procedure for suspension or revocation of licenses.
A suspension or revocation of any malt beverage license, wine license or spirituous liquor license shall be as follows:
(1)
Whenever the police chief or assistant police chief has knowledge of a violation of this chapter or any state or federal law on the part of an employee or licensee, this information shall be immediately brought to the attention of the city manager, or in the city manager's absence, to the attention of the mayor.
(2)
The chief of police or assistant chief of police with concurrence of the city manager or the mayor must as soon as possible advise the licensee of the time and place of a hearing before the council to determine whether the license involved shall be suspended pending the outcome of the case in court. Said licensee shall be notified in writing at least 24 hours prior to said hearing. Said notice shall specify the charges against said licensee. Said licensee may be represented by counsel at the hearing.
(3)
If for any reason a quorum of the council is not available to convene promptly for a hearing, then the city manager and mayor may suspend the license involved pending the hearing, which shall be held at the earliest possible time, and in any event no later than five days, excluding Saturdays, Sundays and legal holidays.
(4)
At a hearing before the council, all parties shall present any evidence or facts they deem necessary. The appearance of the licensee at the hearing shall be entirely voluntary on such licensee's part. From the facts and information submitted without attempting to determine a verdict of guilty or not guilty the council shall decide what will or will not be done regarding the suspension of the license pending the outcome of the case in court. The licensee shall have the right to present witnesses on such licensee's behalf and the right to cross examine other witnesses.
(5)
After conviction in court, the council, no later than its regular meeting, shall decide if the license involved shall be restored, revoked or remain in suspension, and if the suspension is continued, for what length of time. If a not guilty verdict shall result in court, any suspension shall be immediately lifted. Any bond forfeiture or plea of nolo contendere, for the purpose of this section, may be treated as a conviction by the court.
(6)
For the purpose of this chapter and this section, the term "licensee" includes all partners in a partnership which holds a license, as well as all shareholders, officers and directors of a corporation which holds a license.
(Code 1969, § 3-22; Code 1981, § 3-27; Ord. of 5-15-1978, § 15)
Sec. 6-26. - Customer regulations where beverages sold for off-premises consumption.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to remain on the premises wherein malt beverages, wines, spirituous liquors are sold for off-premises consumption for a period of time exceeding 15 minutes. It shall also be unlawful for any person to park or permit a vehicle to be parked on the premises where malt beverages, wines or spirituous liquors are sold for off-premises consumption for a period of time longer than 15 minutes. This section shall not apply to the licensed dealer, manager or licensed employee of said establishment provided that the requirements of section 6-4 have been complied with.
(b)
Any automobile parked in violation of subsection (a) of this section may be impounded by the city police and shall not be released to the owner or operator until such time as the tow bill has been paid in addition to an impoundment fee of $3.00 per day for each day said automobile shall remain impounded.
(c)
The owner of any establishment wherein malt beverages, wines or spirituous liquors are sold for off-premises consumption shall be required to post at two conspicuous places on the premises a sign in substantially the following language:
NOTICE
Section 3-28(a) of the Code of Ordinances for the City of Alma Makes it Unlawful for any Customer or any Customer's Automobile to Remain on these Premises for a Period of Time Exceeding 15 Minutes. Any Violation may be Punishable by a Fine Not Exceeding $1,000.00 or Imprisonment for a Period of Time Not Exceeding 12 Months or Both. Any Automobile Found in Violation of this Ordinance may be Subject to Impoundment by the City of Alma.
(d)
For purposes of this chapter, the term "premises" means the building in which the malt beverage, wine or spirituous liquor is sold and any area within 150 feet of any part of said building.
(Code 1981, § 3-28; Ord. No. 1984-8, 12-3-1984; Ord. No. 2008-9, § 3, 3-18-2002)

Sec. 6-56. - Levied.
(a)
There is hereby imposed, levied and assessed a special or excise tax upon the wholesale sale of malt beverages, wines and distilled spirits, at the following rates:
(1)
Wholesale dealers in distilled spirits, per liter (excluding fortified wines) .....$0.22
(2)
Wholesale dealers in wines, per liter ..... 0.22
(3)
Wholesale dealers in malt beverages, commonly known as tap or draft beer, sold in or from a barrel or bulk container, per each container sold containing not more than 15½ gallons ..... 6.00
(4)
Wholesale dealers in malt beverages sold in bottles, cans or other containers, except barrel or bulk containers, per 12 ounces ..... 0.05
(b)
The taxes assessed by subsection (a) of this section shall be in addition to any and all other taxes or licenses now imposed upon such wholesale dealers.
(Code 1969, § 3-40; Code 1981, § 3-30)
Sec. 6-57. - When and by whom paid.
Each wholesale liquor dealer shall pay the taxes imposed under this article, measured or computed with respect to containers of liquor purchased from such wholesale liquor dealer. Said taxes shall be by such wholesaler paid to the city treasurer in advance, in the manner provided by the state revenue commissioner.
(Code 1969, § 3-41; Code 1981, § 3-31)
Sec. 6-58. - Violations; penalties.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any retail liquor dealer to own, hold, possess or sell any liquor within the city on which the excise tax herein imposed has not been paid to the city.
(b)
Any such violation by a retail liquor dealer or a wholesale liquor dealer shall be deemed a sufficient ground for revocation of the license issued to the violator by the city, and the refusal thereafter again to issue any license or permit to such violator.
(c)
Any person who shall violate the excise tax requirements, or who shall assist or aid another in so doing, or who shall assist another in evading the payment of such taxes, shall be punished as provided in section 1-8, for each such offense, and in addition such person's license as a liquor dealer in the city may be revoked at the discretion of the council.
(Code 1969, § 3-42; Code 1981, § 3-32)
Sec. 6-59. - Wholesale dealers selling malt beverages—Tax levy and payment.
There is hereby levied and imposed upon all wholesale dealers selling malt beverages within the city a specific excise tax to be paid as follows:
(1)
Each wholesale dealer selling malt beverages within the city shall file a report by the tenth day of each month showing for the preceding calendar month the exact quantities of malt beverages, by size and type of container, constituting a beginning and ending inventory for the month, sold within the city. Each such wholesale dealer shall remit to the city on the tenth day of the month next succeeding the calendar month in which such sales were made, the amount of excise tax due in accordance with this section.
(2)
No decal, stamp or other identifying marking shall be required on malt beverages sold within the city.
(Code 1969, § 3-50; Code 1981, § 3-33; Ord. of 5-7-1973, § 1)
Sec. 6-60. - Same—Tax additional to other fees.
The excise tax provided for in section 6-59 shall be in addition to any license fee, tax or charge which may now or in the future be imposed upon the business of selling malt beverages at retail or wholesale, within the corporate limits of the city.
(Code 1969, § 3-50; Code 1981, § 3-34; Ord. of 5-7-1973, § 2)
Sec. 6-61. - Same—Delinquency penalties.
The failure to make a timely report and remittance shall render a wholesale dealer liable for a penalty equal to ten percent of the total amount due during the first 30-day period following the date such report and remittance were due and a further penalty of five percent of the amount of such remittance for each successive 30-day period or any portion thereof, during which such report and remittance are not filed. The filing of a false or fraudulent report shall render the wholesale dealer making such report liable for a penalty equal to 50 percent of the amount of the remittance which would be required under an accurate and truthful report.
(Code 1981, § 3-35; Ord. of 5-7-1973, § 3)
Sec. 6-62. - Same—Punishment.
Any violation of section 6-59 by a retail beer dealer or a wholesale beer dealer shall be deemed a sufficient ground for revocation of the license or permit issued to the violator by the city. Any person who shall violate any provision of such section, or who shall assist any other person in evading the payment of the tax imposed, may be punished as provided in section 1-8 for each offense.
(Code 1969, § 3-53; Code 1981, § 3-36)
