ARTICLE IV. - SOLICITATION PERMITS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 20-212. - No solicitation on streets or highways.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 40-6-97(b), the city prohibits any person from standing or entering on a highway and public streets for the purpose of soliciting employment, business solicitations, and/or contributions from the occupant of any vehicle, except as to events by bona fide charitable organizations pursuant to a proper permit.
(Ord. No. 09-09-24, § 97.001, 9-24-2009)
Sec. 20-213. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Event means any charitable solicitation within the city by a bona fide charitable organization pursuant to a proper permit.
(Ord. No. 09-09-24, § 97.002, 9-24-2009)
Sec. 20-214. - Exceptions.
Pursuant to the authority conferred unto the city by O.C.G.A. § 40-6-97.1, the city adopts the procedures and permitting requirements for charitable organizations soliciting on highways and public streets as stated in this article.
(Ord. No. 09-09-24, § 97.003, 9-24-2009)
Sec. 20-215. - Hearing officer.
The city council appoints the city attorney as the hearing officer for all appeals pursuant to section 20-245.
(Ord. No. 09-09-24, § 97.004, 9-24-2009)
Secs. 20-216—20-238. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - CHARITABLE ORGANIZATIONS

Sec. 20-239. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Charitable organizations, for the purpose of this division, means organizations which have a 501(c) certificate from the Internal Revenue Service, including, but not limited to, nonprofit corporations, churches and/or public and private schools.
(Ord. No. 09-09-24, § 97.005, 9-24-2009)
Sec. 20-240. - Unlawful solicitation without a permit.
No charitable organization is allowed to solicit funds on any public street or highway without first obtaining a permit as provided herein.
(Ord. No. 09-09-24, § 97.006, 9-24-2009)
Sec. 20-241. - Permit requirements.
(a)
All requests for permits must be submitted to the city at least five business days before the requested activity.
(b)
All applications for permits must include, at a minimum, the following information:
(1)
Name of organization;
(2)
Type of organization:
a.
501(c) corporation, including a certificate of incorporation;
b.
Church;
c.
Public or private school;
d.
Nonprofit corporation under the laws of the state, including a certificate of incorporation;
(3)
Name of organizer;
(4)
Address;
(5)
Proposed activity, purpose, area of activity, including street and/or intersection;
(6)
Adult supervisor, name;
(7)
Date and times of activity;
(8)
Participants and ages;
(9)
Solicitation registration with the state secretary of state, if applicable.
(Ord. No. 09-09-24, § 97.007, 9-24-2009)
Sec. 20-242. - Required application and permit; exceptions.
(a)
An application and permit shall be required for each such event, provided, however, only one application and permit shall be required for each proposed event. All such events shall be conducted under the supervision of the person making application for the same, and shall be conducted in a peaceable and orderly manner in compliance with the laws and ordinances applicable thereto, and shall not disrupt the safe and orderly movement of traffic.
(b)
An event permit shall be obtained from the city administrator or his designee. The requirement for an event permit is not applicable to a governmental agency acting within the scope of its functions.
(Ord. No. 09-09-24, § 97.008, 9-24-2009)
Sec. 20-243. - Application.
(a)
Filing generally. A person seeking issuance of an event permit shall file an application with the city administrator or his designee on forms provided by such officer.
(b)
Filing period. An application for an event permit shall be filed with the city administrator or his designee not less than five business days nor more than 60 calendar days before the date on which it is proposed to conduct the event.
(c)
Late applications. The city administrator or his designee, where good cause is shown therefor, shall have the authority to consider any application under this division which is filed less than five business days before the date such event is proposed to be conducted.
(d)
Contents. The application for an event permit shall set forth the following information:
(1)
The name, address and telephone number of the person seeking to conduct such event;
(2)
If the event is proposed to be conducted for, by or on behalf of an organization, the name, address and telephone number of the headquarters of the organization, and the authorized and responsible heads of such organization;
(3)
The name, address and telephone number of the person who will be the event chairperson and who will be responsible for its conduct;
(4)
The date when the event is to be conducted;
(5)
The location of the event;
(6)
The approximate number of persons and their ages, to constitute such event;
(7)
The hours when such event is to start and terminate;
(8)
The location by streets of any assembly areas for the event, and streets and/or intersections where the event is to be held;
(9)
The time at which units of the event will begin to assemble at any such assembly area and/or street;
(10)
If the event is designed to be held by or on behalf of any person other than the applicant, a communication in writing from the person proposing to hold the event, authorizing the applicant to apply for the permit on his behalf;
(11)
Any additional information that the city administrator or his designee shall find reasonably necessary for a fair determination as to whether the proposed event will endanger public health, safety or welfare.
(e)
Application fee. Applicants shall pay an event fee per event. The fee shall be in an amount as established by the mayor and council from time to time.
(f)
Release. Applicants shall provide a release from each participant in the event regarding solicitation of contributions from traffic on city streets.
(Ord. No. 09-09-24, § 97.009, 9-24-2009)
Sec. 20-244. - Issuance.
(a)
The city administrator or his designee shall issue a permit in accordance with the requirements of this division from a consideration of the application and from such information as may otherwise be obtained, unless he finds that:
(1)
The conduct of the event will substantially interrupt the safe and orderly movement of other traffic contiguous to its route;
(2)
The conduct of the event will interfere with the movement of firefighting equipment en route to a fire, or other emergency services;
(3)
The conduct of the event is contrary to the public's health, safety and general welfare.
(b)
The city administrator or his designee may impose such restrictions or conditions upon the event as are necessary for the safe and orderly movement of traffic and to protect the public health, safety and welfare.
(Ord. No. 09-09-24, § 97.010, 9-24-2009)
Sec. 20-245. - Appeal from denial.
(a)
Any person aggrieved by the denial or restriction of an event permit shall have the right to appeal the denial or restriction to the hearing officer. The hearing officer shall act upon the appeal at a hearing to be scheduled for the consideration of such appeals, but in any event, at least 48 hours before the proposed starting time of the event.
(b)
The hearing officer shall grant an applicant's appeal of restriction or denial of a permit as provided in this section from a consideration of the application and from such information as may otherwise be obtained, unless it finds that one or more of the factors enumerated in section 20-244 is present. In considering the factors for denying an applicant's appeal of denial or restriction of a permit, the only consideration shall be those of public health, safety and welfare. No distinction may be made on the basis of the content of the message, the identification of the participants, or any other matter which does not directly and substantially impact on the imminent public health, safety and welfare.
(c)
At least 48 hours before the proposed starting time of the event, the hearing officer will provide the appellant with a written decision on the appeal, stating whether any denial or restriction is being upheld, and if so, the factual basis and reason for upholding each such denial or restriction.
(d)
The hearing officer shall have the authority to conduct said hearing and tender a final decision. The appellant and appellee shall have all rights accorded to individuals as if such hearing was conducted by the municipal court of the city and the rule of evidence and procedures thereof.
(Ord. No. 09-09-24, § 97.011, 9-24-2009)
Sec. 20-246. - Contents.
Each event permit shall state the following information:
(1)
Starting time;
(2)
Type of activity;
(3)
Applicant's name;
(4)
Date;
(5)
Signature of city administrator or his designee;
(6)
Approximate duration of event;
(7)
Restrictions or conditions on event allowed by section 20-244(b) and reasons therefor.
(Ord. No. 09-09-24, § 97.012, 9-24-2009)
Sec. 20-247. - Alternate permit.
The city administrator or his designee, in denying an application for an event, shall be empowered to authorize the conduct of the event on a date, at a time, or a location different from that named by the applicant. An applicant desiring to accept an alternate permit shall, within three days, provide notice of acceptance to the city administrator or his designee. An alternate permit shall conform to the requirements of, and shall have the effect of a permit under this division.
(Ord. No. 09-09-24, § 97.013, 9-24-2009)
Sec. 20-248. - Officials to be sent copies.
Immediately upon the issuance of an event permit, the city administrator or his designee shall send a copy thereof to the following:
(1)
The mayor;
(2)
The fire chief or county equivalent;
(3)
The chief of police;
(4)
The city building inspector and/or zoning compliance officer.
(Ord. No. 09-09-24, § 97.014, 9-24-2009)
Sec. 20-249. - Duties of permittee; permit to be carried.
(a)
A permittee shall comply with all permit directions and conditions and with all applicable laws and ordinances.
(b)
The event chairperson or other person heading or leading such activity shall carry the event permit upon his person during the conduct of the event and shall make event supervisors aware of his location.
(Ord. No. 09-09-24, § 97.015, 9-24-2009)
Sec. 20-250. - Revocation.
The city administrator or his designee, the chief of police, building inspector or other designated agent of the city shall have the authority to revoke an event permit issued under this division where a permittee has failed to comply with reasonable permit directions and restrictions, and where the actions of a permittee pose an immediate threat to the safety of participants or the public.
(Ord. No. 09-09-24, § 97.016, 9-24-2009)
Sec. 20-251. - Issuance requirements.
The city shall not issue any permit which does not fully comply with the requirements of this division.
(Ord. No. 09-09-24, § 97.017, 9-24-2009)
Sec. 20-252. - Prohibited times and street and highways.
(a)
No solicitation is allowed during the following times:
(1)
Between the hours of 11:30 a.m. and 1:30 p.m., Monday through Friday;
(2)
After 4:00 p.m., Monday through Friday;
(3)
Between the hours of 12:00 midnight through 8:00 a.m. and after 6:00 p.m. on Saturday;
(4)
Between the hours of 12:00 midnight and 1:30 p.m. and after 5:00 p.m. on Sunday.
(b)
Prohibited streets:
(1)
U. S. Highway 515 and all intersections thereof;
(2)
All bridges;
(3)
Any and all streets or highways which have more than three lanes of travel, including turn lanes.
(Ord. No. 09-09-24, § 97.018, 9-24-2009)
Sec. 20-253. - Persons under 18 years of age.
All organizations who are permitted to solicit funds must have one adult supervisor over the age of 21 for every four persons under the age of 18. All participants in the solicitation of funds must be 13 years of age or older. No minor under 13 years of age shall be allowed to solicit funds.
(Ord. No. 09-09-24, § 97.019, 9-24-2009)
Sec. 20-254. - Fines and rules.
(a)
Any person, organization, business or entity violating the provisions of this division is subject to a fine or community service, or both, up to the maximums allowed under the city Charter or general state law per day per violation per individual participant.
(b)
Any person, organization, business or entity found guilty of violating this division may not be issued a permit for a two-year period from the date of the violation.
(Ord. No. 09-09-24, § 97.020, 9-24-2009)
Secs. 20-255—20-271. - Reserved.
