Sec. 30-5-66. - Definition.
For the purposes of this article, any person who shall, for compensation, sign any bond for the appearance of any accused person in the recorders court, procure bond for such person or deposit collateral for such person in lieu of bond, shall be deemed a professional bondsman and engaged in business as such.
(Code 1972, § 15-89)
State Law reference— Persons deemed professional bondsmen, O.C.G.A. § 17-6-50.
Sec. 30-5-67. - Violations.
Any professional bondsman violating this article shall, on conviction in the recorder's court, be punished for a violation of this Code and shall automatically be barred from signing bonds in the city.
(Code 1972, § 15-96)
State Law reference— Violations of laws pertaining to professional bondsmen, O.C.G.A. § 17-6-55.
Sec. 30-5-68. - Permit—Required; prerequisite to issuance of license.
No person shall carry on or be engaged in the business of professional bondsman until authorized so to do by a permit granted by the mayor and council upon recommendation of the public safety committee. No business license shall be issued to a professional bondsman until such permit has been granted.
(Code 1972, § 15-90)
Sec. 30-5-69. - Same—Petition; bond schedules.
All persons engaged or desiring to engage in the business of professional bondsmen shall on or before the first day of January, in each year, file with the public safety committee a petition for a permit to engage in such business. Such petition shall contain:
(1)
A description of all real property in this county owned in fee simple by such person, and the clear market value of each item of real property so scheduled;
(2)
A description of mortgages, liens or other encumbrances affecting such real property, showing the maturity date and amount thereof; and
(3)
A statement of the total amount of all bonds upon which the petitioner may, at that time, appear as surety; and a subschedule of all bonds upon which the petitioner may, at that time, appear as surety which have been forfeited and not paid. The mayor and council may require the petitioner to file, in addition, a schedule showing separately the date and penal sum of all of the bonds upon which the petitioner then appears as surety.
All schedules and subschedules required by this section shall be made under oath by the petitioner; provided, if the petitioner is a firm, each member of the firm shall sign the same, and if a corporation, two (2) principal officers thereof authorized by the corporation's bylaws shall sign the same and the corporate seal shall be affixed.
(Code 1972, § 15-91)
Sec. 30-5-70. - Same-Investigation; verification.
It shall be the duty of the public safety committee to investigate thoroughly all petitions and to verify all schedules required in this article. Such committee shall not recommend the issuance of a permit to a professional bondsman unless:
(1)
The petition and accompanying schedules shall clearly show that the petitioner is the owner of real property in this county of a fair market value, in excess of encumbrances, equal to the total of all bonds upon which the petitioner may at that time appear as surety, plus the amount allowed as exemption under the homestead laws of the state; or
(2)
The petitioner procures and submits to city, in lieu of the real property schedule described herein, a fifty thousand dollars ($50,000.00) surety bond.
(Code 1972, § 15-92; Ord. No. 08-26, § 1, 11-6-08)
Sec. 30-5-71. - Requiring additional schedules.
The mayor and council may at all times require professional bondsmen to submit schedules of property and of bonds upon which they appear as surety. In the event such schedules disclose the insolvency or inability of a bondsman to sign bonds because of an excess of liability over assets, the council may bar such bondsman from signing further bonds in the recorder's court for so long as such insolvency or inability exists. Any professional bondsman who shall fail to furnish such schedules within five (5) days after demand, or who shall furnish a false schedule, shall thereafter be barred from signing bonds in connection with cases in the recorder's court.
(Code 1972, § 15-93)
Sec. 30-5-72. - Visiting prisoners; signing bonds.
No professional bondsman or agent of such bondsman shall be permitted to visit prisoners or to sign bonds for any person imprisoned unless such person shall have previously procured permission from the chief of police or his designated agent.
(Code 1972, § 15-94)
State Law reference— Bondsmen in places where prisoners are confined, O.C.G.A. § 17-6-52.
Sec. 30-5-73. - Loitering, etc., in police station or grounds; calling bondsmen.
No professional bondsman shall loiter in the police station or grounds, nor enter the same except when attending court or on business in connection with a case wherein he is surety on a bond, nor shall he speak to any prisoner at the police station unless authorized so to do by the police officer on duty. The police officer shall not grant such authority until he has received from the prisoner a written request naming the bondsman he wishes to see. In cases where no bondsman is named by the prisoner, the station lieutenant shall call bondsmen in rotation until one is secured.
(Code 1972, § 15-95)
State Law reference— Bondsmen loitering around courts, jails, etc., O.C.G.A. § 17-6-52.
