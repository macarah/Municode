Chapter 5.16 - INSURANCE COMPANIES
Sections:

5.16.010 - Insurer defined.
For the purposes of this chapter, the term "insurer" means a company which is authorized to transact business in any of the classes of insurance designated in OCGA Section 33-3-5.

5.16.020 - Insurer license fee levied.
Each insurance company doing business in the city shall pay an annual license fee, allowed by O.C.G.A. Section 33-8-8, and additional fee in the same amount for each business location in excess of one operated and maintained by such company within the city.
(Ord. of 5-11-15(2); Ord. dated 10-11-93 ยง 1 (part))
5.16.030 - Separate business locations.
Each separate business location of an insurance company operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing and which, in connection with the loans or sales involving term financing, offers, solicits, or takes application through a licensed agent of the insurance company shall pay an additional annual fee of ten dollars ($10.00) or thirty-five (35) percent as provided in O.C.G.A. Section 33-8-8(b)(1-2).
