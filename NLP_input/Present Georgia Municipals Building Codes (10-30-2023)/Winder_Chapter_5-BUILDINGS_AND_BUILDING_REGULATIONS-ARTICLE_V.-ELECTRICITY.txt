DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 5-123. - License—Required.
Every person desiring to engage in the business of electrical construction and of the installation of wiring and apparatus for electric light, heat or power in the city shall, before doing so, obtain an electrical contractor's license issued by the Georgia Secretary of State before a permit shall be issued by the permit clerk.
(Code 1968, § 9-6; Ord. ( W-1-20 ), § XXII, of 4-7-20)
Cross reference— Licenses and business regulations, ch. 13.
Sec. 5-124. - Same—Fee.
The fees for permits in this division shall be fixed from time to time by the mayor and council.
(Code 1968, § 9-7; Ord. ( W-1-20 ), § XXIII, of 4-7-20)

Sec. 5-138. - Penalties.
Section 106.3(a) of the electrical code adopted in this article is hereby amended to read as follows:
"A person who shall violate a provision of this code or fails to comply therewith or with any of the requirements thereof, upon conviction thereof in the recorder's court, shall be punished as provided in section 1-8 of the Code of Ordinances of the city. Where any offense continues from day to day, each day's continuance thereof shall be deemed a separate offense."
(Code 1968, § 9-18)
Sec. 5-139. - Failure to correct defects.
Any person who shall fail to correct any defect in his work, after having been duly notified for ten (10) days by the building inspector acting as electrical inspector, shall not receive any further permit until such defect has been corrected; and in any case in which any person shall continue to violate this chapter or the orders of said officer touching the same, the permit of such person shall be forfeited.
(Code 1968, § 9-15; Ord. ( W-1-20 ), § XXIV, of 4-7-20)
Sec. 5-140. - Authority to discontinue electrical service.
In any case of failure to comply with this article, the building inspector acting as electrical inspector shall have authority, after due notice, to disconnect the electrical current to the premises concerned, and to enforce discontinuance of the same until said requirements are complied with.
(Code 1968, § 9-16)
Sec. 5-141. - Supervision of work.
The building inspector acting as electrical inspector is hereby authorized, empowered and directed to regulate and determine the placing of electric wires or other appliances for electric light, heat or power in the city, and to cause all such wires or appliances to be so placed, constructed and guarded as not to cause fires or accidents, endangering life or property.
(Code 1968, § 9-9)

Sec. 5-143. - Supplementary to other laws.
In interpreting and applying the provisions of the electrical code adopted in this article, they shall be held to be the minimum requirements for the promotion of public health, safety and general welfare, and as supplementary to all other laws, codes or regulations of the state, county or city, formulated in the interest of the public health, safety or general welfare; and where the electrical code imposes a greater restriction than is imposed by other laws, codes or regulations, the electrical code shall govern, but where any other laws, ordinances, rules or regulations impose greater restrictions than are required by the electrical code, such other provisions shall govern.
(Code 1968, § 9-3)
Charter reference— Power of city to protect the public health, safety and welfare, § 1-3(33), (37).
Sec. 5-144. - Interpretation.
The building inspector, acting as electrical inspector, is hereby given authority to interpret all of the rules in this article and the National Electrical Code. The construction given by the electrical inspector shall have the legal effect of, and shall be enforceable as, an administrative determination of the legislative intent.
(Code 1968, § 9-5; Ord. ( W-1-20 ), § XXVI, of 4-7-20)
Sec. 5-145. - Attestation, filing of copies.
One (1) copy of the electrical code adopted in this division shall be on file in the building inspection office.
(Code 1968, § 9-2; Ord. ( W-1-20 ), § XXVII, of 4-7-20)
Sec. 5-146. - Painting, repairs, etc.
Whenever builders or persons engaged in repairing, painting, etc., find it necessary to remove wires from buildings in prosecuting their work, the owner of such building or the contractor engaged thereon shall serve the building inspector acting as electrical inspector with written notice twenty-four (24) hours before such contemplated work is begun, and said officer shall have authority in his discretion to direct the owner of such wires to remove the same.
(Code 1968, § 9-14)
Sec. 5-147. - Inspections.
The building inspector acting as electrical inspector shall have the right in the discharge of his duties to enter any building, manhole or subway, or climb any pole, for the purpose of examining and testing the electrical appliances thereon or therein contained; and for that purpose he shall be given prompt access to all buildings, public and private, and to all manholes, subways or poles, on application to the individual or company owning or in charge of same.
(Code 1968, § 9-10)
