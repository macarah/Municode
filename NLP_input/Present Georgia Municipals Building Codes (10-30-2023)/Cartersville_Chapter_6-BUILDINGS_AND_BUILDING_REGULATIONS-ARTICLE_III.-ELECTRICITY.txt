ARTICLE III. - ELECTRICITY
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 6-51. - National Electrical Code—Adopted.
The National Electrical Code, to adopt and enforce the latest edition, as adopted and amended by the Georgia Department of Community Affairs.
(Code 1976, § 8-1031; Ord. No. 35-91, § 4, 9-19-91; Ord. No. 41-95, § 5, 9-28-95; Ord. No. 32-96, § 2, 7-18-96; Ord. No. 37-01, § 7, 9-6-01)
Sec. 6-52. - Service wires to be extended to connection point most convenient for city electricians.
It shall be the duty of builders and contractors when wiring a building to extend their service wires to such part of the building as may be nearest and most convenient for the city to make connection with its service wires. It shall not be the duty of the city's electrician to make connections otherwise.
(Code 1976, § 8-1043; Ord. No. 35-91, §§ 5, 6, 9-19-91)

DIVISION 2. - PERMITS AND INSPECTIONS

Sec. 6-63. - Permit required.
No person shall install or repair any electrical appliance or wiring, except repairs not involving the removing or alteration of present installations, until a permit has been secured from the electrical inspector. Not less than four (4) hours before any electrical work is started, except as noted above, a permit must be secured from the electrical inspector for such work to be done.
(Code 1976, § 8-1034)
Sec. 6-64. - Plans, specifications, etc.
(a)
Before any permit will be issued, electrical work must be laid out in plan and other necessary views to show location, type of fixtures, etc., together with specifications of materials to be used. Each set of plans and specifications must show the name of the owner and location of the proposed building.
(b)
In case of dispute between any electrician and the electrical inspector as to layout, plans, specifications or materials, either may appeal to the board of zoning appeals. If the decision of the electrical inspector is reversed, the records shall show the reason therefor.
(Code 1976, § 8-1035; Ord. No. 6-91, 2-21-91)
Sec. 6-65. - Authority of electrical inspector to enter building for inspection purposes.
The electrical inspector shall have the authority to enter any building at reasonable hours for the purpose of inspecting the electrical installations. Any person attempting to prevent such inspection, whether owner, lessee, or his agent, shall be in violation of this article.
(Code 1976, § 8-1042)
Sec. 6-66. - Inspection required; procedure.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the electrical inspector, either personally or by assistants, to inspect all electrical work during the progress of such work, as well as when it is completed; such inspection shall be made from time to time and must be thorough and complete.
(b)
Inspection notices, upon blanks furnished by the electrical inspector, shall be filed in the office of the electrical inspector by each person doing electrical work, giving notice to the electrical inspector of the location, kind of work, and time when the work will be ready for inspection. The work involved will be inspected as soon as possible thereafter. When the work has been completed, the electrician must immediately so notify the electrical inspector. At the time the final inspection is made, the electrician or his designated representative must be present. No electrical work may be covered until the necessary inspection has been made. It shall be unlawful for any person to fail to comply with the foregoing procedure. Upon final inspection, if the electrical inspector determines the work and material to be in accordance with the provisions of this chapter, a certificate of final inspection shall be issued, certifying that the work has been done in accordance with the laws of the city. If the electrical inspector finds that any electrical work has not been properly installed, or does not comply with the electrical code of the city, the inspector shall so notify the owner, agent or lessee of such property, and upon notice so to do, the owner, agent or lessee shall make such changes and corrections in the electrical work as may be ordered by the electrical inspector; failure to make such changes within five (5) days after notice so to do shall subject the owner, agent or lessee to punishment as prescribed for a violation of this Code. In addition, if the necessary corrections are not made, the city may refuse to furnish service to the premises.
(Code 1976, § 8-1036)
Sec. 6-67. - Inspection of vacant buildings.
Any building which becomes vacant shall be inspected for adequate wiring. If it is determined that the wiring is inadequate, then the rewiring of the building shall be done in accordance with this article.
(Code 1976, § 8-1032; Ord. No. 13-87, § II, 4-23-87)
Sec. 6-68. - Permit fees.
The permit fees are as referenced on the fee schedule at City of Cartersville Code, section 17-79.
(Ord. No. 61-99, § 3, 1-7-00; Ord. No. 42-05, § 2, 7-7-05)
Sec. 6-69. - Copies of building permits.
In order to secure the greatest cooperation between the departments, the building official shall furnish the electric department a list of all permits issued by him.
(Code 1976, § 8-1040)
