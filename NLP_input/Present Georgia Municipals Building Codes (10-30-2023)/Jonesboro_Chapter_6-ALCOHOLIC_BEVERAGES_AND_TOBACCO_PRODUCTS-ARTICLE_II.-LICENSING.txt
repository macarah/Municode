ARTICLE II. - LICENSING

Sec. 6-36. - Licenses required to sell alcoholic beverages.
Any person desiring to sell alcoholic beverages in the city, whether for consumption on the premises or via retail package sale, shall apply for and obtain a license from the city prior to commencing operations. Operating without a license shall be a violation of this chapter and shall be punishable as provided in sections 6-181 and 6-182.
(1)
Licenses for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall be issued by the city clerk or his designee at an annual license fee as established herein:
a.
Retail consumption on the premises (distilled spirits, malt beverages and wine) .....$4,500.00
b.
Retail consumption on the premises (malt beverages only, including brew pubs) .....$1,000.00
c.
Retail consumption on the premises (wine only) .....$1,000.00
d.
Retail package sales (malt beverages and wine) .....$2,000.00
e.
Retail package sales (malt beverages only) .....$1,000.00
f.
Retail package sales (wine only) .....$1,000.00
g.
Retail package sales (distilled spirits) .....$5,000.00
h.
Art gallery license .....$  300.00
i.
On-premise art license .....$  500.00
j.
Microbrewery license .....$3,500.00
(2)
All licenses issued herein shall constitute a mere grant of a privilege to carry on such business during the term of the license subject to all the terms and conditions imposed by this chapter, the charter, and related ordinances of this Code, and the Constitution, laws and regulations of the state and the United States of America applicable to such business.
(3)
All licenses issued under this article shall have printed on the face the following words:
"THIS LICENSE IS A PRIVILEGE AND IS SUBJECT TO BEING REVOKED AND ANNULLED BY THE MAYOR AND CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF JONESBORO AND IS SUBJECT TO LAWS, ORDINANCES AND REGULATIONS HEREAFTER ADOPTED."
(4)
Both the licensee and/or license representative shall be the authorized and duly constituted agent for service of all notices and processes required to be served on or given hereunder for any action or proceeding or uses of any nature whatsoever permitted under the provisions of this Chapter or under any other provisions of this Code.
(Ord. No. 2015-006, § 1(Exh. A), 8-10-2015; Ord. No. 2019-006 , § 1, 3-11-2019; Ord. No. 2019-012 , § 2, 7-8-2019; Ord. No. 2019-014A , § 2, 12-9-2019)
Sec. 6-37. - License eligibility.
No license for the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises shall be issued to any applicant who operates a restaurant, private club, hotel, or golf club unless said establishment satisfies the requirements set forth in its respective definition in section 6-2 hereof and the restaurant/dining room of said establishment serves meals, which excludes the sale of alcoholic beverages, and said serving of meals accounts for at least 55 percent of the gross revenue of that restaurant/dining room.
(Ord. No. 2015-006, § 1(Exh. A), 8-10-2015)
Sec. 6-38. - Contents of application for license.
(a)
All persons desiring to obtain a license under this chapter for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall apply to the city upon forms to be prepared and provided by the city and shall be considered by the mayor and city council. Such application shall state:
(1)
The location of the proposed business;
(2)
The nature and character of the proposed business;
(3)
If the business is to be operated by an individual, the name, address, and telephone number of that individual;
(4)
If the business is to be operated by a partnership, the names, addresses, and telephone numbers of all general partners, the licensee, and the license representative;
(5)
If the business is to be operated by a close corporation, the names, addresses, and telephone numbers of all officers, directors, stockholders, the licensee, and the license representative;
(6)
If the business is to be operated by a corporation other than a close corporation, the name of the corporation, the address and telephone number of the corporate office, the name, address, and telephone number of the registered agent for service of process for the corporation, and the name, address, and telephone number of the licensee and license representative;
(7)
If the business is to be operated by a limited liability company ("LLC"), the name of the managing partner or partners, the address and telephone number of the principal place of business of the LLC, the name, address, and telephone number of the registered agent for service of process for the LLC, and the name, address, and telephone number of the licensee or license representative;
(8)
Individuals who shall be listed on an application pursuant to subsections (a)(3) through (a)(7) above shall be required to undergo a background investigation, which may include a check through Georgia Crime Information Center and/or National Crime Information Center of such person, and shall fully comply with the process of conducting said investigation and shall supply information necessary to conduct said investigation as requested on the application; and
(9)
Such other appropriate information as may be required by the governing body.
(b)
The truth of the contents of an application for a license shall be sworn to by the licensee and the license representative.
(c)
The licensee shall also file with the city a completed State of Georgia Department of Revenue Alcohol Tax and Control Unit form ATT-17, which has been filed with the state, if applicable.
(d)
The application, when filed with the city, shall be accompanied by a processing fee of $500.00, plus $35.00 per individual that is subject to a background check pursuant to this section or section 6-101 or as otherwise required under this chapter. The processing fee and background check fee is payable by certified check or cash and is separate from and shall not be credited to the license fee. Said processing fee will not be refunded if the requested license is not granted.
(Ord. No. 2015-006, § 1(Exh. A), 8-10-2015)
Sec. 6-39. - Supporting documents to be filed with application.
Those applying for a license shall submit in support of the application for license the following documents:
(1)
A certificate from a Georgia registered land surveyor showing a scale drawing of the location of the proposed premises and the distance measured from the front door, where the alcoholic beverages are sold, in a straight line to the nearest public sidewalk, walkway, street, road or highway; along such public sidewalk, walkway, street, road or highway by the nearest route to the main public entrance of the building, or the nearest portion of the grounds, of any church building or school building according to the following standards:
a.
For those establishments selling distilled spirits, regardless of whether malt beverages and/or wine are sold thereby; a distance of no less than 100 yards from the nearest church and 200 yards from any school or education building, school ground, or college campus as defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.
b.
For those establishments selling malt beverages and/or wine but not distilled spirits; a distance of no less than 100 yards from any school or education building, school ground, or college campus as defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.
c.
For those establishments selling distilled spirits, wine, or malt beverages; a distance of 100 yards from any alcoholic treatment center as defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.
(2)
Fingerprint card and approval to conduct a background check of each person whose name appears on an application for a license, pursuant to section 6-38, ensuring that each person has not, within five years prior to the date of the application, been convicted of or entered a plea of guilty or nolo contendere to any felony, misdemeanor, or other charge relating to the sale, manufacture, distribution, taxability, possession or use of alcoholic beverages or illegal drugs including the offense of driving a motor vehicle under the influence of alcohol or drugs; has not entered a plea of guilty or nolo contendere or been convicted of a felony or a misdemeanor of a crime opposed to decency and morality. Provided, however, that a person designated as a registered agent of a close corporation or a limited liability company on an application shall not be required to submit the affidavit required by this subsection unless such person is also designated as the licensee or license representative on such application.
(3)
A copy of a deed showing the applicant to be the owner of the premises for which the license is sought or a copy of a lease showing any interest the owner of the premises has in the business for which the license is sought as well as contact information for said owner of the premises.
(4)
Inspections of the premises by the following:
a.
Fire marshal;
b.
Building department;
c.
Health department; or
d.
Planning department.
(5)
Notification from the police chief of the city certifying that each person named in an application pursuant to section 6-38 has been investigated and found not to have been convicted of nor have entered a plea of guilty or nolo contendere to any felony, misdemeanor, or other charge relating to the sale, manufacture, distribution, taxability, possession or use of alcoholic beverages or illegal drugs within five years prior to the date of the application for the license; has not entered a plea of guilty or nolo contendere or been convicted of a felony or a misdemeanor of a crime opposed to decency and morality. For those applicants who, within the last five-year period, have resided or do reside in a state other than Georgia, the applicant must furnish a certified copy of a driver history and criminal background history from the state or states in which he or she has resided or resides to the city clerk of the city or his designee. A person designated as a registered agent of a close corporation or a limited liability company on an application, however, shall not be required to submit a certificate to the city unless such person is also designated as the licensee or license representative on such application.
(6)
If the same person is serving as the licensee and the license representative, he/she shall submit an affidavit certifying that he/she is at least 21 years of age, and a manager of the business.
(7)
If the licensee is not the license representative, an affidavit from the license representative certifying that the license representative is at least 21 years of age and a manager of the business.
(Ord. No. 2015-006, § 1(Exh. A), 8-10-2015)
Sec. 6-40. - Misleading, omitted or obsolete information.
(a)
Any untrue or misleading information contained in or material omission left out of an original or renewal application for an alcoholic beverage license shall be cause sufficient for the denial thereof.
(b)
Any information which changes or otherwise becomes obsolete shall be reported immediately.
(c)
When any license is issued on the basis of an application containing misleading or untrue information or omitted or unreported changed material information, such circumstances shall be cause for revocation of same.
(Ord. No. 2015-006, § 1(Exh. A), 8-10-2015)
Sec. 6-41. - Required citizenship.
No license under this article shall be granted to any applicant who is not a citizen of the United States or a permanent resident alien, as defined by the Immigration and Nationality Laws of the United States, for at least 12 months prior to submitting the application for the license. If the applicant is a partnership, the requirements of this section shall apply to all partners. Once submitted, the applicant must maintain his residency status required in this section or the license shall be automatically revoked.
(Ord. No. 2015-006, § 1(Exh. A), 8-10-2015)
Sec. 6-42. - Required license representative.
No individual may be the license representative for more than one business. "Business" shall be defined for the purposes of this section as any business establishment in possession of a license to sell alcoholic beverages pursuant to this chapter at any other location, including locations outside the city.
(Ord. No. 2015-006, § 1(Exh. A), 8-10-2015)
Sec. 6-43. - Persons who are not eligible to obtain a license.
It shall be unlawful to grant a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages to:
(1)
A person who is not of good character and reputation in the community in which he resides.
(2)
A person who has entered a plea of nolo contendere within a five-year period prior to the date of application for license, entered a guilty plea or been convicted of a felony, crime involving illegal drugs or of a crime opposed to decency and morality; or who has entered a plea of nolo contendere within a five-year period prior to the date of application for a license; entered a guilty plea or been convicted of a crime involving violation of the ordinance of the city relating to the use, sale, manufacture, distribution, taxability or possession of beer, wine, or liquor, or violations of the laws of the state and federal government pertaining to the manufacture, possession, transportation, use, or sale of beer, wine or intoxicating liquors, or taxability thereof.
(3)
A person whose license under this chapter has been revoked for cause or who has had a license under this chapter revoked for cause.
(4)
A person whose place of business is conducted by a manager or agent unless such manager or agent qualifies as the license representative.
(5)
Any person who is under the age of 21 years old or a person who is not of sound mind and memory.
(6)
Any law enforcement officer or employee of the city, or any elected or appointed official of the city.
(7)
Any person who is not eligible for a state alcoholic beverage license or whose license has been revoked by the state.
(Ord. No. 2015-006, § 1(Exh. A), 8-10-2015; Ord. No. 2019-014A , § 2, 12-9-2019)

Sec. 6-45. - Business entrance.
The front entrance of all licensed premises shall be clearly visible from a public street; however, this restriction shall not apply where the premises are located in a hotel, motel, private club, shopping center or multiple-story business building. No license shall be issued to an applicant under this chapter unless this requirement is satisfied.
(Ord. No. 2015-006, § 1(Exh. A), 8-10-2015)
Sec. 6-46. - Payment of taxes and other debts to city.
The city clerk or his designee shall cause an inquiry to be made into the tax records to determine if any applicant or other parties interested in an application have any outstanding taxes, special assessments, unpaid utilities or any other monies owed to the city. No license shall be issued, renewed or considered for issuance to an applicant which has any outstanding payments to the city.
(Ord. No. 2015-006, § 1(Exh. A), 8-10-2015)
Sec. 6-47. - No licenses issued within prohibited distances.
(a)
Licenses shall not be issued to authorize the sale of alcoholic beverages at any location which does not meet or exceed the following minimum distance separation requirements from the establishments set forth in this section. Provided, however, the minimum distance of separation specified herein shall be required only on the initial license application and will not be required on license renewals for the same premises.
(1)
For dealers of distilled spirits, regardless of whether malt beverages and/or wine is also sold, the licensee's premises cannot be located within 100 yards of any church building or within 200 yards of any school or education building, school ground, or college campus as defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.
(2)
For dealers of wine or malt beverages, and not distilled spirits, the licensee's premises cannot be located within 100 yards of any school or education building, school ground, or college campus as defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.
(3)
For dealers of distilled spirits, wine or malt beverages, the licensee's premises cannot be located within 100 yards of any alcoholic treatment center as defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.
(b)
For the purposes of this section, all distances from the proposed licensed establishment to the applicable building, structure or grounds shall be measured by the most direct route of travel and shall be measured in the following manner:
(1)
From the front door of the structure or partial building unit from which beverage alcohol is sold or offered for sale; thence,
(2)
In a straight line, regardless of obstructions, to the nearest public sidewalk, walkway, street, road or highway; thence,
(3)
Along such public sidewalk, walkway, street, road or highway by the nearest route; thence,
(4)
To the front door of the building or to the nearest portion of the grounds, whichever is applicable.
(c)
For the purposes of this section, the term "building" shall mean any non-accessory structure located on the school ground, college campus or alcoholic treatment center which would not be considered an accessory use under the appropriate interpretations of the zoning ordinance of the county.
(d)
As to any location licensed by the city, if the distance requirements in this section are met at the time of issuance of any license, or if a license has not yet been issued, but the structure in which an establishment is to be located has commenced construction pursuant to a lawfully issued building permit, the subsequent opening and operation of a church or school within the distance prohibited herein shall not prevent the continuance of an existing license or the renewal thereof or the issuance of a new license to any owner of such property. Provided, however, that the distance requirements herein shall not apply to any location for which a new license is applied for if the sale of alcoholic beverages was lawful at such location at any time during the 12 months immediately preceding such application.
(Ord. No. 2015-006, § 1(Exh. A), 8-10-2015)
Sec. 6-48. - Notice of new application advertisement.
(a)
The city shall give notice of a new application by placing a notice in the county legal organ for 10 calendar days prior to the date when the application shall be heard by the mayor and city council. An affidavit from the publisher of said legal organ shall be filed with the city clerk or his designee prior to the hearing.
(b)
Said notice shall contain the location of the proposed business, names of all persons as they appear on the application as required by section 6-38, and the date and time the mayor and city council will hear the application. The advertisement shall be the type used for legal ads in the legal organ of the county, and the notice referred to shall be in the following form:
"NOTICE OF APPLICATION FOR RETAIL LICENSE TO SELL ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES
An application has been submitted to the City of Jonesboro Mayor and City Council for a retail _______ license to sell alcoholic beverages at the following location: _______. The business name is _______. _______ has requested to be the License Representative. The application will be granted or denied by Mayor and City Council at ___________o'clock ___________.m. on the ___________ day of _______, _______
Signed:. ___________"
Note: Names of the individual, general partners, corporation, licensee and license representative must be shown.
(c)
The city shall place signs upon the location of the proposed business. Said signs shall read as follows:
"Alcoholic beverage license applied for by ___________ for the establishment located at ___________. Hearing before the Mayor and City Council of the City of Jonesboro, Georgia at _______ a.m./p.m. on _______ day of _______, _______."
The signs described above shall be posted on the location of the proposed business for two weeks prior to the day of the hearing by the mayor and city council. Each sign shall be not less than 24 inches long and 36 inches wide, and shall face toward all public roads adjoining the proposed location. Such signs shall be placed where they can be easily seen from all public roads adjoining the proposed location.
(Ord. No. 2015-006, § 1(Exh. A), 8-10-2015)
Sec. 6-49. - Standards to be applied by mayor and city council in granting licenses; failure of applicant to attend public hearing.
(a)
The mayor and council shall hear and consider an applicant's application for a license to conduct retail package sales or a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises within 45 calendar days of the city's receipt of said application.
(b)
The standards to be applied by the mayor and city council, acting in its judicial capacity to grant or deny a license, shall include whether, in the best judgment of the council, the applicant, based on all information obtained in the application process as set forth in this chapter, possesses the qualities of sound judgment and discretion necessary for one who dispenses alcoholic beverages to the public generally.
(c)
In addition to the foregoing standards, should any license representative of the applicant fail to attend a meeting at which it is considered before the mayor and city council regarding a new license, a request to change a licensee or a license representative, or a request to relocate the business of a licensee, the mayor and city council may consider such application or request to have been withdrawn by the applicant. The application fee is nonrefundable.
(d)
In determining whether or not any application shall be granted and a license issued, the mayor and city council shall also consider the following information in the public interest and welfare:
(1)
If the license representative or any individual listed on the application or partner has ever violated any federal, state, city or county law or regulation regarding alcoholic beverages and illegal drugs, their sale, consumption, distribution or manufacture;
(2)
The manner in which the license representative or any individual listed on the application or partner has conducted any business within the city as to the necessity for unusual law enforcement observation and inspection in order to prevent the violation of any law, regulation or ordinance or as to the necessity for city action to compel the applicant's and/or licensee's adherence to any city law, regulation or ordinance;
(3)
The location for which the license is sought as to traffic congestion, public safety, the general character of the neighborhood and the effect of such an operation on surrounding property values;
(4)
Whether the license representative or any individual listed on the application or partner has ever had an alcoholic beverage or business license suspended or revoked by the state or any political subdivision thereof; and
(5)
Whether the applicant is in compliance with all provisions of this chapter and the application meets the requirements of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 2015-006, § 1(Exh. A), 8-10-2015)
Sec. 6-50. - Approval of application; issuance of license by city clerk.
(a)
All applicants for a license meeting the ascertainable standards of this chapter will be granted the license or licenses requested upon the hearing by the mayor and council, and said decision to approve the application shall be stated in writing and entered upon the minutes. Written notification of said approval shall be mailed to the applicant at the address listed in the application, or personally delivered to the applicant, within 7 calendar days of the hearing of the mayor and council.
(b)
A copy of the license shall be issued to the applicant upon approval of the application by the mayor and council, but the original license shall not be issued by the city clerk or his designee to the applicant until:
(1)
The licensee fee, as further described in sections 6-36 and 6-52 hereof: is paid by the applicant by certified check or cash and submitted to the city clerk or his designee, which must occur within 30 calendar days of the date of the hearing of the application by mayor and council; and
(2)
The applicant provides evidence to the city clerk or his designee that the state license has been issued. If the applicant fails to receive a license from the State of Georgia, any license fees paid to the city, other than the nonrefundable processing fee, shall be refunded.
(c)
Once the requirements of subsection (b) above are met, the city clerk or his designee shall issue the original license to the applicant in the following manner:
(1)
If issued to a corporation, said license shall be issued in the name of said corporation, the licensee, and the license representative.
(2)
If issued to a partnership, said license shall be issued in the name of the partnership, the licensee, and the license representative.
(3)
If issued to an individual, said license shall be issued to that individual as licensee and the license representative.
(d)
If the mayor and council neither formally approves nor denies an application in writing within 45 days of receipt of the application from applicant, then on expiration of the 45th day, the application shall be deemed approved with the understanding that the mayor and council may, at any time thereafter, consider and deny said application pursuant to subsection 6-51(a) below, at which time the applicant must cease operating as a licensee under this chapter.
(Ord. No. 2015-006, § 1(Exh. A), 8-10-2015)
Sec. 6-51. - Denial of application; appeal from decision of mayor and council.
(a)
Whenever an application for license is denied by the mayor and council, the reasons for such denial shall be stated in writing and entered upon the minutes. A written notice of the denial shall also be mailed to the applicant at the address listed in the application, or personally delivered to the applicant, within seven calendars days of the decision to deny the application. Upon denial, the applicant may not reapply for a license under this chapter for 24 months after the date of the denial.
(b)
Should the applicant desire to appeal a decision of the mayor and council to deny the applicant's initial application for a license under this chapter, the applicant may appeal said decision to the Superior Court of Clayton County by filing a petition for writ of certiorari with said Superior Court in accordance with state Law.
(Ord. No. 2015-006, § 1(Exh. A), 8-10-2015; Ord. No. 2019-014A , § 2, 12-9-2019)
Sec. 6-52. - Payment of license fee; license period.
Any license issued under this chapter shall be valid from January 1 of the year in which it was issued until December 31 of that year. The full license fee amount, corresponding to the type of license applied for, as set forth in section 6-36 above must be paid for a license application filed prior to July 1 of the year. One-half of the full license fee amount shall be paid for a license application filed after July 1 of the year, for the year of issuance only.
(Ord. No. 2015-006, § 1(Exh. A), 8-10-2015)
Sec. 6-53. - Time limit for commencement of business in licensed establishment; forfeiture for nonuse.
(a)
All holders of licenses hereunder must within six months after the issuance of said license open the establishment referred to in said license. If said license holder fails to open the licensed establishment or receive a time extension from the mayor and city council within said six-month period the license shall be canceled and all fees forfeited.
(b)
Except as provided in this section, any holder of a license who, after beginning operation, ceases to operate the business for a three-month period automatically forfeits his or her license, and said license shall be automatically canceled and all fees forfeited. In the event a business ceases to operate due to a fire, flood or other physical damage to the building beyond the control of the license holder, the city may, at the request of the license holder, elect to merely suspend the holder's license until the license holder's business is again operational. If the license holder's business is not operational within the same calendar year of its destruction and said license shall be automatically canceled and all fees forfeited.
(Ord. No. 2015-006, § 1(Exh. A), 8-10-2015)
Sec. 6-54. - License transfers; change of licensee; business relocation.
(a)
Licenses granted pursuant to this chapter are not transferable as to person or premises.
(b)
(1)
All requests for a change in licensee or license representative must be approved by the mayor and city council and shall provide all appropriate information and supporting documents required under sections 6-38 and 6-39 as outlined in an approved form to be supplied by the city clerk or his designee for that purpose.
(2)
A fee in the amount of $100.00 per applicant, licensee, or license representative is paid to the city upon the filing of the first request in a year (Jan.—Dec.). Subsequent requests in that same year will require payment in full of the initial application fee.
(c)
All business relocations must be approved by the mayor and city council and the licensee and license representative shall provide all appropriate information and supporting documents as are required under sections 6-38 and 6-39. Requirements of section 6-48 relating to advertising and posting must be met. A processing fee established by the mayor and city council shall be required.
(d)
Any violation of this section shall constitute due cause for suspension or revocation of the license or licenses granted by the council.
(Ord. No. 2015-006, § 1(Exh. A), 8-10-2015)
Sec. 6-55. - Renewal of licenses; maintaining current list of employees.
All licenses shall be issued on a calendar year basis and shall expire at midnight on December 31 of the year in which they are issued unless they are renewed as set forth herein:
(1)
The city clerk or his designee shall send a letter to all license holders by October 31 of each year to remind said license holders of the renewal deadline for the subsequent year.
(2)
The licensee shall file a renewal application form with the city on or before November 15 of each year. A renewal application form, which is available from the city clerk or his designee, filed with the city after November 15 shall be subject to the following penalties:
a.
Seventy-five dollars, if filed with the city after November 15 and on or before December 1;
b.
One hundred fifty dollars, if filed with the city after December 1 and on or before December 15; and
c.
The greater of $250.00 or 20 percent of the licensee renewal fee if filed with the city after December 15.
(3)
All renewals require a new background check for all individuals for which background checks are required per sections 6-38 and 6-l01. Background checks shall also be required on any new shareholders, partners or owners that have been added to the business holding the license and would have been requited to submit to a background check had the renewal been a new application.
(4)
As part of the renewal application, the licensee shall submit to the city a complete list of the names, social security numbers, and photos of the current shareholders, partners, owners, license representatives, and employees of the licensed establishment for whom fingerprint cards and background checks are required under sections 6-38 and 6-101 of this chapter so that the city may confirm all such persons are being properly investigated under this chapter and may confirm all employees are being issued the requisite employee identification cards as discussed in section 6-101 below. Furthermore, this list must be continually maintained and kept up to date by the licensee and the licensee representative in order to maintain compliance with this chapter. The city clerk or his designee may request a copy of this list from the licensee at any time during the course of the year upon seven calendar days' written notice to the licensee. Should this list not be maintained, not be submitted upon request from the city clerk, or not be submitted as a part of the license renewal application. such a failure shall constitute grounds for license suspension, revocation, or non-renewal as may be the case.
(5)
If the police chief of the city or the city clerk reports any activity which constitutes due cause for not renewing a license, the renewal shall not be made. "Due cause" shall consist of the violation of any laws or ordinances of the State of Georgia or the city regulating the sale or use of alcoholic beverages, or violation of any regulations made pursuant to authority granted for the purpose of regulating such activities, or violation of any state or federal law, or violation of any city or county ordinance.
a.
Written notice of the reason or reasons for such nonrenewal shall be mailed or delivered by the city clerk or his designee to the licensee at the licensed premises or mailed to the address shown on the renewal application. Licensee shall have 10 calendar days from the date of the written notice to request a public hearing before the mayor and city council, as provided for in sections 6-132 and 6-133.
b.
Provided that "due cause" does not exist for not renewing the license, the city clerk or his designee shall issue the renewal license upon payment of the respective annual license fee set forth in section 6-36 above by the licensee on or before the last business day in December preceding the year for which the license is sought. Sec. 6-56. - Automatic revocation.
Whenever the state shall revoke any permit or license to sell alcoholic beverages, the city license to deal in such products, issued pursuant to this chapter, shall thereupon be automatically revoked without any action by the mayor and city council or any other city officer being necessary.
(Ord. No. 2015-006, § 1(Exh. A), 8-10-2015)
Sec. 6-57. - Temporary license for consumption on the premises.
(a)
Notwithstanding the preceding provisions of this chapter, a person may apply for a temporary license to sell, dispense, or serve alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises at a restaurant, hotel, private club, or golf club for up to 3 calendar days. Application for a temporary license must be made on forms maintained and available from the city clerk. The fees for such temporary licenses shall be as follows:
(1)
Temporary license for one day: $100.00.
(2)
Temporary license for two days: $200.00.
(3)
Temporary license for three days: $300.00.
(b)
The applicant for a temporary license must also pay a non-refundable $100.00 processing fee in addition to the above-stated license fees, plus $35.00 per individual who shall pour, handle, dispense, or serve alcoholic beverages on the licensed premises or anyone who manages or supervises said individual(s). These fees are required to be submitted simultaneously with the application.
(c)
Approval or denial of an application for a temporary license may be decided directly by the city clerk. The applicant shall provide all information required on the application for temporary license, including information regarding any individual(s) who shall pour, handle, dispense, or serve alcoholic beverages on the licensed premises or anyone who shall manage or supervise said individual(s). The applicant shall submit his or her application to the city clerk no less than 14 calendar days before the scheduled date(s) the temporary license is needed to give the city clerk time to process the application and receive the fee amount.
(d)
The city clerk shall issue a written determination either approving or denying the application for temporary license within seven calendar days of receiving said application.
(1)
If the application is approved, the applicant must submit the license fee set forth in section (a) above within three calendar days of the city clerk's decision. Upon receipt of the fee, the city clerk shall issue the temporary license to the applicant. No person shall be eligible to obtain more than one such temporary license per consecutive three-month period.
(2)
If the application is denied, the applicant may appeal the decision to mayor and council by filing a written notice of appeal with the city clerk within seven calendar days of the date of the city clerk's written denial of his or her application. The mayor and council shall hold a hearing on the applicant's application within 30 calendar days of said applicant's notice of appeal. The mayor and council shall issue a written determination of the appeal within seven calendar days of said hearing. Should the mayor and council uphold the city clerk's denial of the person's application for a temporary license, the applicant may appeal said decision to the Superior Court of Clayton County by filing a petition for writ of certiorari with said Superior Court. Said appeal must be made within ten calendar days from the date of the written notice of the denial of the application by mayor and council or it shall be deemed waived.
(Ord. No. 2015-006, § 1(Exh. A), 8-10-2015)
