ARTICLE III. - FLEA MARKETS

Sec. 12-40. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the following terms shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them:
Indoor flea market: A market for the sale or exchange of articles and antiques which is inside a building having four (4) walls and a roof. No stalls shall be allowed outside of the building.
Outdoor flea market: An open market for the sale or exchange of articles and antiques.
Opaque fence: A fence through which one cannot see, constructed of uniform wood boards, plywood panels, chain link fence with vinyl slats or such other uniform material approved by the office of planning and zoning.
Stall: A booth, stand or counter at which articles are displayed for sale.
(Ord. of 12-18-79, § a)
Sec. 12-41. - License required; fees.
No person may operate a flea market without obtaining from and paying the required fee to the business license office. The fee for such license is as adopted by the board of commissioners and is on file in the business license office.
Sec. 12-42. - Fencing.
Each outdoor flea market shall have placed on the portion of the property fronting on public roads an opaque fence at least eight (8) feet high, and on the remaining sides of the property a natural buffer area, hedge or shrubs, approved by the office of planning and zoning, or an opaque fence at least eight (8) feet high.
(Ord. of 12-18-79, §§ b, o)
Sec. 12-43. - Sewage disposal system.
Each flea market shall have at least one (1) permanent sewage disposal system approved by the health department.
(Ord. of 12-18-79, § c)
Sec. 12-44. - One-way passageways.
The way providing ingress and egress to an outdoor flea market sales area shall be one (1) way only, with a traffic island dividing the entrance from the exit.
(Ord. of 12-18-79, §§ d, o)
Sec. 12-45. - Length of entrance from street; paving.
The entrance way of an outdoor flea market shall extend at least forty (40) feet from the public road and each one-way directional street shall be paved to a width of at least ten (10) feet with triple surface treatment or better quality paving.
(Ord. of 12-18-79, §§ e, o)
Sec. 12-46. - Acceleration and deceleration lanes.
There shall be an acceleration and deceleration lane at the main entrance or at any other entrance or exit of the property of an outdoor flea market, which lanes shall be approved by the engineering department.
(Ord. of 12-18-79, §§ f, o)
Sec. 12-47. - Directional signs.
The owner of the outdoor flea market shall provide directional signs at both the entrance and exitway of the property.
(Ord. of 12-18-79, §§ g, h)
Sec. 12-48. - Additional exits.
If an outdoor flea market has more than forty (40) stalls, the owner shall provide one (1) additional exit from the stall area which shall not be closer than one hundred (100) feet to the main entrance.
(Ord. of 12-18-79, §§ h, o)
Sec. 12-49. - List of renters.
The owner of an indoor flea market shall keep, on a monthly basis, a daily list of persons renting stall space and the home address and telephone number of the person. This list shall be available for viewing by the business license official and any law enforcement officer upon request.
(Ord. of 12-18-79, § i)
Sec. 12-50. - Posting theft and stolen property sign.
The owner shall post in a conspicuous place near the entrance to the flea market the following sign:
"The crime of theft by receiving stolen property is defined as:
Receiving, disposing of or retaining stolen property which one knows or should have known was stolen, unless the property is received, disposed of or retained with intent to restore it to the owner. If the value of the article received is more than five hundred dollars ($500.00) the crime is a felony and is punishable by a sentence of one (1) to ten (10) years in the state prison."
(Ord. of 12-18-79, § j)
Sec. 12-51. - License classes.
(a)
All flea markets shall be assigned by the business license office to a license class as follows:
EXPAND
Class I 1 stall
Class II 2 to 20 stalls
Class III 21 to 40 stalls
Class IV 41 or more stalls
  (b)
Business license fees shall be based on these classes and the board of commissioners shall adopt license fees applicable to each class.
(Ord. of 12-18-79, §§ h, 1)
Sec. 12-52. - Compliance with article.
All requirements of this article shall be complied with by all flea markets. All prior to the issuance of any license.
(Ord. of 12-18-79, § m)
Sec. 12-53. - Exemptions.
Individual residents of the county and tax exempt organizations located in the county shall be allowed to conduct a flea market twice yearly for not more than three (3) consecutive days each without having to comply with the requirements of this article. Exemptions granted shall be obtained from the business license office, which may impose reasonable registration requirements.
(Ord. of 12-18-79, § n)
Sec. 12-54. - Additional miscellaneous provisions.
In addition to the requirements of this article and where not inconsistent with the provisions hereof, each applicant is subject to and shall comply with the provisions of Article I of this chapter.
Secs. 12-55—12-59. - Reserved.
