Article II - BILLIARD ROOMS[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— Billiard rooms, O.C.G.A. § 43-8-1 et seq.; "billiard room" and billiards defined, O.C.G.A. § 43-8-1.

Sec. 30-5-31. - Definitions.
Billiard room means any business operated in the city utilizing a minimum of three (3) billiard tables at which persons are permitted to play the game of billiards and for which a charge is made for use of said equipment.
Billiards means any of the several games played on a table surrounded by an elastic ledge of cushions with balls which are impelled by a cue and shall include all forms of the game known as "carom billiards," "pocket billiards," and "English billiards."
Alcoholic beverages shall be defined as set forth in section 30-20-7 of the Code.
(Ord. No. 99-28, § 1, 7-27-99; Ord. No. 13-05, § 1, 2-26-13)
Sec. 30-5-32. - Requirement of license; filing of application.
No person shall operate a billiard room in the city without a license. No license to operate a billiard room shall be issued to any person who is not at least twenty-one (21) years of age and a citizen of the United States, nor to one who has been convicted of a felony. Application for a license to operate a billiard room shall be first made to the assistant city manager.
(Ord. No. 99-28, § 1, 7-27-99; Ord. No. 07-12, § 7, 4-24-07; Ord. No. 16-12, § 8, 12-13-16)
Sec. 30-5-33. - Regulatory fee.
Upon approval of the application, the city shall issue the license for the current year or unexpired portion thereof upon the payment of any applicable business license fee, occupation tax, or administrative fee and the payment of a regulatory fee in the amount of two hundred fifty dollars ($250.00).
(Ord. No. 99-28, § 1, 7-27-99; Ord. No. 99-33, § 1, 9-28-99)
Sec. 30-5-34. - Hours of operation.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any establishment which is authorized to sell alcoholic beverages to operate a billiard room between the hours of 1:00 a.m. to 6:00 a.m., Tuesday thru Saturday; from 12:00 midnight on Saturday to 12:30 p.m. on Sunday; and from midnight on Sunday to 6:00 a.m. on Monday. It shall also be unlawful to permit any person to remain in such billiard room between such prohibited hours except regular employees or agents performing necessary labor on the premises.
(b)
The billiard room operating hours prohibition as set forth in this section shall not apply to a business which includes a billiard room if the billiard room is not operated during the prohibited hours.
(Ord. No. 99-28, § 1, 7-27-99; Ord. No. 00-26, § 1, 10-5-00; Ord. No. 12-23, § 1, 12-11-12)
Sec. 30-5-35. - Age of admission; gambling prohibited; view of premises.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person under the age of eighteen (18) years to play billiards in, or for any other purpose to enter or remain in, a billiard room in which alcoholic beverages are offered for sale.
(b)
No gambling of any kind shall be permitted upon the premises of billiard rooms.
(c)
Every billiard room shall be open to the common public, in accordance with the laws of this state. No part of the room shall be concealed from other parts for the operation of tables; that is, no participants shall be permitted in an enclosed room to utilize private tables. The billiard room shall always be open to inspection by the city police during operating hours.
(Ord. No. 13-05, § 1, 2-26-13)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 13-05, § 2, adopted February 26, 2013, deleted the former § 30-5-35, and enacted a new § 30-5-35 as set out herein. The former § 30-5-35 pertained to admission of persons under eighteen (18) years of age and derived from Ord. No. 99-28, adopted July 27, 1999.
Sec. 30-5-36. - Sale or consumption of alcoholic beverages.
(a)
No licensee shall allow, condone or permit customers or patrons to bring, possess or consume alcoholic beverages of their own on the premises. Brown bagging, as defined in section 30-21-1 of the Code, is prohibited on any premises licensed under this article.
(b)
No alcoholic beverages shall be sold, served or allowed to be used in and on the premises of a billiard room or any place operated in connection therewith, unless and except such premises or establishment is an establishment which is authorized to sell alcoholic beverages and derives at least fifty (50) percent of its total annual gross revenues from the sale of products or services other than alcoholic beverages.
(Ord. No. 99-28, § 1, 7-27-99)
Sec. 30-5-37. - Exceptions.
This chapter shall not apply to billiard tables or billiard rooms operated by private industrial concerns, Young Men's Christian Associations, religious orders, charitable institutions, state, county or city institutions, fraternal orders or bona fide clubs using such tables for members or employees only.
(Ord. No. 99-28, § 1, 7-27-99)
Sec. 30-5-38. - Penalty.
Every person, firm or corporation operating a billiard room within the city without having applied for a license shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, and, upon conviction thereof, shall be subject to the penalties or punishment set forth in Code section 1-1-6. Each day that the billiard room is operated without a license shall be a separate offense.
(Ord. No. 99-28, § 1, 7-27-99)
Sec. 30-5-39. - Grounds for suspension or revocation; notice; hearing; refund.
(a)
Any license which has been issued or which may hereafter be issued by the city to any licensee under this article may be suspended or revoked for due cause as hereinafter defined, and after a hearing has been held by the mayor and council for the purpose of considering any such suspension or revocation. At least five (5) days prior to such hearing, written notice of the time, place and purpose of such hearing, and a statement of the charge or charges upon such hearing is to be held shall be given to the holder of such license for which suspension or revocation is to be considered.
(b)
Due cause for the suspension or revocation of a license shall consist of a violation of any laws or ordinances applicable to or regulating such business, violation of regulations made pursuant to authority granted for the purpose of regulating such business; or failure of the licensee or his employees to promptly report to the police department any violation of the law or ordinances, any breach of the peace, disturbance or altercation resulting in violence which may occur in or upon the licensed premises.
(c)
The mayor is hereby authorized to suspend any license issued hereunder in an emergency situation, or any situation wherein the mayor deemed such an immediate suspension necessary for the protection of the health and welfare of the citizens of LaGrange, and said suspension may be made effective immediately and shall remain in force until the mayor determines such an emergency is over or until the next regular meeting of the mayor and council, or a meeting called prior to the next regular meeting.
(d)
When a license is suspended or revoked hereunder, the city shall not refund any portion of the license, occupation, administrative or regulatory fee to the holder of such suspended or revoked license.
(Ord. No. 99-28, § 1, 7-27-99)
Secs. 30-5-40—30-5-65. - Reserved.
