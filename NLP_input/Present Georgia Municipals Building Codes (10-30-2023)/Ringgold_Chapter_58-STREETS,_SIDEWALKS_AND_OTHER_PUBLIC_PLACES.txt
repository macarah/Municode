ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 58-1. - Placement of unsolicited publications without containers prohibited.
(a)
No person, firm, or corporation shall place unsolicited publications within in the city limits except as provided in this section.
(b)
Exceptions. Unsolicited publications may be placed in a box or container designed for holding the publication until receipt by the intended recipient. However, nothing in this section shall provide the unrestricted right of placement of such containers on private property or city streets and rights-of-way without permission of the owner.
(c)
The city does not intend to regulate the placement or delivery of unsolicited publications through mail or directly into the hands of an intended recipient. The city intends to regulate all unsolicited publications, without regard to content, that are thrown or deposited outside of a container specifically designated for the publication.
(d)
Enforcement. The chief of police or his designee, or such other person as the council may hereinafter designate, shall enforce the provisions of this section and issue citations for violation.
(e)
Penalty. Any violation of this section shall also be punished by imposition of a fine or imprisonment, or both, as prescribed in section 1-11.
(Ord. No. 00-1211-3, §§ 1, 2, 12-11-00)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 00-1211-3, §§ 1, 2, adopted Dec. 11, 2000, did not specifically amend this Code. Hence inclusion of said ordinance provisions as § 58-1 was at the discretion of the editor to read as herein set out. See the Code Comparative Table.
Sec. 58-2. - Prohibition of parking on sidewalks.
(a)
There shall be no parking of any motor vehicle on any sidewalk located within the city. "Motor vehicle" means every vehicle that is self-propelled other than an electric personal assistive mobility device (EPAMD).
(b)
The first violation of this section shall result in a $25.00 fine. A second offense, within five years of the first offense, shall result in a $50.00 fine. A third or subsequent offense, within five years of the prior offenses, shall result in a $100.00 fine.
(Ord. No. 2008-0801, § I, 8-11-08)
Sec. 58-3. - Requirement of sidewalk construction.
(a)
Whenever the city permits any future residential or commercial structure the owner or his agent shall be required to submit to the city building inspector a proposal to build sidewalks adjacent to any public right of way abutting the tract upon which the proposed building is to be situated, if no such sidewalks presently exist. The location of the sidewalk which shall be required to be constructed shall be a minimum of 24 inches off the back of the curb and the minimum width being 60 inches and the minimum depth four inches. The concrete must be approved by the city prior to pouring the concrete.
(b)
No building permit shall be issued without a sidewalk plan attached to the permit application, and the sidewalk plan shall be approved as a part of the building permit.
(c)
If the owner fails to construct the sidewalk as specified in his building permit, he is subject to the penalties of section 1-11, in addition to any other equitable remedies which may be available under the charter and state law to enforce the requirements of this Code section.
(Ord. No. 2008-1202-5, § I, 12-2-08)
Sec. 58-4. - Special event prohibitions.
(a)
Definitions.
In-line skates shall mean roller skates whose wheels are arranged in a straight line, including, but not limited to, roller blades.
Motor scooter shall mean a two-wheeled motorized vehicle with a step-through frame and a low-powered gasoline engine geared to the rear wheel.
Motorized cart shall mean every motor vehicle having no less than three wheels and an unladen weight of 1,300 pounds or less and which cannot operate at more than 20 miles per hour.
Service animal shall mean any guide dog, signal dog, or other animal individually trained to provide assistance to an individual with a disability.
Special event shall mean any organized activity having as its purpose entertainment, recreation and/or education, such as a festival or celebration, crafts fair, parade or march, rally or assembly that takes place on a public property, street, sidewalk or right-of-way, or occurs on private property and impacts government services on public rights-of-way.
(b)
The following are prohibited within the boundaries of a special event held in the city:
(1)
Pets, except for service animals;
(2)
Bicycles, except for official use;
(3)
Skate boards;
(4)
Motorized carts and motor scooters, except for official or handicap use;
(5)
In-line skates.
(Ord. No. 2010-0510, § I, 5-10-10)

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 58-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Administrator means the city manager, who shall be the administrator for the receipt and processing of applications for a private organization or group of persons to hold a parade, assembly, demonstration or other use of public property or roads for private purposes. The city manager may be assisted by other employees of the city.
Private organization or group of private persons means any firm, partnership, corporation, association or a group of individuals more than three in number, or their representatives, acting as a unit.
Private purpose means any purpose not commanded or directed by statute, ordinance or other regulation to be performed by the state, county or other governmental entity.
(Ord. of 2-8-88, § 1)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 58-32. - Administration.
The administrator shall administer and enforce the provisions of this article for the application for and issuance of permits under this article.
(Ord. of 2-8-88, § 4)
Cross reference— Administration, ch. 2.
Sec. 58-33. - Violations and punishment.
(a)
Any person who violates or fails to comply with any provision of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, and on conviction shall be punished as provided in section 1-11. All prosecutions for violations of this article shall be conducted in the municipal court.
(b)
Any person, being an officer or employee of the city, who shall knowingly violate any provision of this article may be punished by discharge from employment or other appropriate discipline.
(Ord. of 2-8-88, § 7)
Sec. 58-34. - Defense to prosecution.
It shall not be a defense to any prosecution under this article for failure to obtain a permit before engaging in any activity described in this article that a permit has in fact been issued unless the defendant produces a valid written permit, or a certified copy of the permit.
(Ord. of 2-8-88, § 8)
Sec. 58-35. - Deposits and financial responsibility.
(a)
Nothing in this article shall relieve any person or organization from liability for any injuries or damages to persons or property occasioned by their acts or omissions arising from the activity for which any permit under this article was issued.
(b)
After examining an application for a permit and receiving the advice and assistance of any other officer named in this article, the administrator or mayor and council, after consideration of such application and advice, may require, as a condition precedent to the issuance of the permit, that a fee be paid in an amount reasonably necessary to provide for, among other things, cleanup after the event, special first aid, security for vehicles at any staging area, special traffic considerations, temporary toilet facilities and similar extraordinary expenses occasioned by permitted activity. This amount shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges.
(c)
As a condition precedent to issuance of a permit, the administrator may require the private organization itself or group of private persons to provide personnel for trash cleanup of affected areas littered during the permitted activity, the provision of first aid, proof of sufficient storage areas for a large influx of motor vehicles occasioned by the permitted activity, provision of temporary toilet facilities and other similar extraordinary items considered to be necessary for the permitted activity in the opinion of the administrator. If additional requirements are placed on private organizations or groups of private persons in accordance with this subsection, and those requirements are not met despite assurances by the private organization, failure to comply with the aforementioned requirements shall be grounds for the denial of any subsequent permit requested by such private organization or group of private persons and for any other claims for funds expended by the city for those extraordinary expenses agreed to but not provided by the applicant.
(d)
If the private organization is other than individuals, a permit will not issue without making of the necessary deposit. Individuals may be excused from such deposit by the execution by the individual applicant, or by each individual in a group applying for the permit, of a pauper's affidavit.
(Ord. of 2-8-88, § 9)
Secs. 58-36—58-55. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PERMIT

Sec. 58-56. - Required.
Every private organization or group of private persons who wishes to use public property or public rights-of-way or streets within the city limits for private purposes in holding a parade, assembly, demonstration, road closing or other activity is required to have a permit from the city for the privilege of engaging in any such activity within the city unless such a permit is prohibited under state law or the activity is otherwise exempted by law, ordinance or other valid regulation.
(Ord. of 2-8-88, § 2)
Sec. 58-57. - Application; fee.
(a)
Every private organization or group of private persons required to procure a permit under the provisions of this division shall submit an application for the permit to the city manager, which application shall conform to the requirements of this section, in addition to any other provision of this article.
(b)
Each application shall be in writing, upon forms provided by the city manager, and submitted within a reasonable time prior to the planned activity, to allow for security checks, verifications and arrangements to be made, the city manager to act within ten business days of his receipt of the completed application.
(c)
Each application shall contain the following information:
(1)
Name, home address and residence telephone of the applicant, if an individual, or home office address if a corporation or partnership, and a business telephone number of the corporation or partnership.
(2)
Date, time and place where the proposed activity is to be carried on, including proposed routes of passage of parades, demonstration marches or other such proceedings.
(3)
The kind, type and class of activity to be carried on.
(4)
Names and addresses of the officers and directors if a corporation, or the names and home addresses of the partners if a partnership.
(5)
Any additional information the administrator or the mayor and council may find reasonably necessary to the fair administration of this article, which may include a complete record of all arrests and convictions against the applicant, and every partner, officer, director or member of the applicant group for violations of any and all laws of the city, county, state and federal government.
(d)
Each application shall be signed and sworn to in the presence of a notary public by the applicant if an individual, or by a partner if a partnership, or by an officer of the corporation if a corporation.
(e)
All information furnished or secured under the authority of this section shall be kept in strict confidence by the city and shall not be subject to public inspection except as is required by state law, and such information shall be utilized by the officers of the city responsible for administering the provisions of this article.
(f)
False statements in any application for a permit shall be grounds for denial of the application, immediate revocation of the permit, and/or denial of future applications.
(g)
Any application, permit and registration fees, as fixed by the mayor and council, required under this article shall accompany the application for a permit.
(Ord. of 2-8-88, § 3)
Sec. 58-58. - Duties of administrator.
The administrator shall have, among others, the duty to:
(1)
Provide the necessary forms for the application for a permit and for the submission of any required information as may be necessary to properly administer and enforce this article.
(2)
a.
Review all applications for completeness;
b.
Collect whatever fee may be required;
c.
Designate or coordinate sites and set time schedules;
d.
Coordinate with the county authorities on all matters concerning activities; and
e.
Where appropriate, receive the approval of the state department of transportation, Georgia state patrol, Georgia Bureau of Investigation, city police chief, and the county sheriff, or any other necessary public officer, for the requested activity.
(3)
Issue to the private organization or group of private persons a permit within ten business days of such approval. The administrator may, however, where he deems it appropriate, defer the issuance of a permit until the application for the permit shall have been approved by the mayor and council.
(Ord. of 2-8-88, § 5)
Sec. 58-59. - Procedure for issuance.
The following procedure shall be followed in the issuance of all permits under this division:
(1)
Upon the receipt of an application, the administrator shall take action upon the application, although he may refer the application to the mayor and council for consideration and action at its next public meeting.
(2)
Unless some significant interference with the rights of nonparticipant citizens exists, or the orderly flow of traffic would be obstructed unreasonably, or a clear and present danger to citizens of the city, or there is other unreasonable interference with the public welfare, peace, safety, health, good order, and convenience of the general public, the permit shall be granted.
(3)
Where the proposed route would result in interference as described in subsection (2) of this section, the administrator shall have the authority to designate reasonable sites and/or routes and set time schedules for the beginning and ending of the activity. The administrator shall have authority to cancel the permit where the activity fails to begin after the time set for it to begin, based on other activities for which permits have been granted or based on the unreasonable interference caused by such delay with the public welfare, peace, safety, health, good order and convenience to the general public.
(4)
The administrator shall issue a permit to the applicant, which permit shall state the nature of the activity authorized and bear the date of issuance and the signature of the city manager.
(5)
Any applicant whose application for a permit is denied by the city manager may appeal such denial to the mayor and council, which shall consider the appeal at the next regularly scheduled meeting next following the receipt of the applicant's appeal.
(6)
The mayor and council shall consider such appeal by requiring the administrator to explain why the permit was denied. Unless the administrator can show significant interference with the rights of nonparticipating citizens; unreasonable obstruction of the orderly flow of traffic; a clear and present endangerment of the citizens of the city; other specific unreasonable interference with the public welfare, peace, safety, health, good order and convenience of the general public; or failure to comply with the dictates of any part of this article, the permit shall be granted.
(7)
In no event shall any private organization or group of private persons be permitted to bring signs, banners, posters or printed material of any such size or shape containing any message intended to influence any judge, magistrate, juror, witness or other officer in the discharge of his duty, to the county courthouse and the grounds upon which it stands. Further, no private organization or group of private persons may use the roads immediately adjacent to and those roads that lead directly to the county courthouse grounds for the private purpose of holding a parade, assembly, demonstration or similar activity on any nonholiday weekday prior to 8:00 a.m. or after 6:00 p.m., or on any Saturday, Sunday or public holiday prior to 1:00 p.m. or after 6:00 p.m.
(Ord. of 2-8-88, § 6)
Secs. 58-60—58-90. - Reserved.

Sec. 58-91. - Purpose and intent.
The purpose and intent of this article is to:
(1)
Provide a uniform system of road naming and assigning of addresses for all properties and buildings throughout the city;
(2)
Conform to the county addressing plan in order to facilitate provision of adequate public safety and emergency response service; and
(3)
Minimize difficulty in locating properties and buildings for public service agencies and the general public.
(Ord. of 3-28-94, § 2)
Sec. 58-92. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Address program administrator means the official of the county charged with the administration of this article, including his authorized representative. The address program administrator shall be the 911 administrator of the county.
Building means a structure having a roof supported by columns or walls for the shelter of persons, for residential or business use. When separated by division walls from the ground up without openings, each such enclosure may be deemed a separate building.
Driveway means a private way, beginning at the property line of a lot abutting a public road, private road easement or private right-of-way, giving access from a public road, recorded easement, recorded private road or private right-of-way, and leading to a building on that lot.
Private road means any one-way or two-way road for ingress and/or egress that has not been officially accepted as a public road by the city that serves two or more properties. A private road may receive the assignment of an official road name for the purposes of this article, but this naming in no way implies that the private road will become a publicly maintained road.
Public road means any road, street, highway, thoroughfare or other way of passage that has been irrevocably dedicated to the public or in which the public has acquired rights by prescription, and has been officially accepted by the city, without regard to whether or not it is open for travel.
Road address means the road names assigned by the city council, and numbers by the address program administrator that identifies a particular building or lot in the city.
(Ord. of 3-28-94, § 3(1), (2), (4)—(7))
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 58-93. - Concurrence.
The city council shall have the right to disapprove any action of the address program administrator by affirmative action of a majority of the council acting. If the city council fails to act within two regular meetings after the action of the address program administrator, the city council shall be deemed to have concurred.
(Ord. of 3-28-94, § 3(3))
Sec. 58-94. - Administration and application.
The address program administrator, with concurrence of the city council, will be responsible for the interpretation and administration of this article, including:
(1)
Assigning all numbers for properties and buildings.
(2)
Compiling a database of addresses of each property and building.
(3)
Recommending changes of existing addresses when necessary to facilitate house numbers along a road in order to comply with the standard county addressing system.
(4)
Designating individual unit addresses for multiple housing units in conformity with this article and the standard county addressing system.
(Ord. of 3-28-94, § 4)
Sec. 58-95. - Display of road address numbers.
(a)
Every owner of improved property shall purchase and display official address numbers so that they may be visible from the street on which the property is addressed. The following criteria must be used to properly display the number:
(1)
Address numbers must be a minimum of three inches in height for residential uses so as to be seen easily from the road.
(2)
Numbers for multiple dwelling units and nonresidential buildings shall be at least six inches in height and shall be placed on the front of the building facing the street address or on the end of the building nearest the road. Individual units shall be required to display unit numbers at least three inches in height on the front door or immediately adjacent to the door.
(3)
Address numbers must be set on a background of a contrasting color.
(4)
On a corner lot, the house number should face the street named in the address.
(5)
If a building is more than 75 feet from the street named in the address, the address number shall be displayed on a sign attached to a fence, lawn stake, mailbox, etc., adjacent to the road right-of-way.
(6)
Address numbers should be plain block numeric numbers and not alpha print.
(b)
The address program administrator will have the right to authorize and approve alternate methods of displaying house numbers that meet the intent of this article when strict adherence to these standards can not reasonably be met.
(Ord. of 3-28-94, § 5)
Sec. 58-96. - Existing road names.
The names of roads listed on the official county road map sheet, including the city, as developed by 911, that are located in the incorporated areas of the city, whether public or private, shall not be changed by any method except as set forth in this article. A copy of this article and a list of road names shall be forwarded to the state department of transportation. The official county 911 road registry shall include the city and shall cross-reference the old and new numbering systems.
(Ord. of 3-28-94, § 6)
Sec. 58-97. - New road names.
(a)
No new public roads in the city shall be named without the approval of the city council, and with the concurrence of the address program administrator.
(b)
No new private road names shall be recognized by this article or added to the city list of road names without prior approval of the city council and with the concurrence of the address program administrator.
(c)
The name of any new road, public or private, shall not duplicate or be similar to any other road name already named by this article or any subsequent ordinances or amendments to this article. This subsection shall not apply to extensions of existing roads.
(d)
New subdivision roads, whether public or private, shall be named when created through the subdivision process, which shall include submission of the road names to the city council and with concurrence of the address program administrator for approval.
(e)
Developers establishing new public roads shall pay the City of Ringgold for the actual cost of purchasing and erecting street signs that meet the standards of the state department of transportation.
(f)
Developers desiring to use any type of sign other than the style that meets the standards set forth by the state department of transportation on a public road shall have the style and design approved by the city council and with the concurrence of the address program administrator.
(g)
The names of private roads, including mobile home park roads, that require naming to facilitate postal delivery and emergency response shall be recognized by the city at the request of the citizens who own property adjacent to the road. Such a road must serve at least three households. The name of the private road shall be commonly known to the community. If there are two or more recommendations of names, the city will recognize the name preferred by at least 51 percent of the property owners on the road provided there are no other conflicting road names.
(Ord. of 3-28-94, § 7)
Sec. 58-98. - Renaming roads.
Property owners who want to have the name of a road changed must submit a petition to the address program administrator for verification. The address program administrator will make recommendations to the city council after the following criteria have been met:
(1)
A nonrefundable fee as set forth for each road shall accompany any petition requesting that an existing road name be changed.
(2)
The petition shall include the existing road name, the proposed road name, and the signatures of 75 percent of those persons owning property adjacent to the road.
(3)
The address program administrator shall cause the request to be advertised pursuant to applicable city ordinances.
(4)
If the address program administrator and the city council approve the request, the petitioners shall be required to pay the city for the cost of purchasing and erecting new road signs.
(Ord. of 3-28-94, § 8)
Sec. 58-99. - Numbers for future buildings.
All residential and business buildings erected after March 28, 1994, shall be assigned a number prior to the issuance of a building permit. Owners shall purchase and display such numbers as provided in section 58-95.
(Ord. of 3-28-94, § 9)
Sec. 58-100. - Enforcement.
(a)
No building permit shall be issued until an official house number has been assigned for the lot.
(b)
Owners or occupants of buildings constructed prior to March 28, 1994, that do not comply with this article will be notified and requested to meet requirements within 60 days from the date of notification. A warning notice will be issued after 60 days if the requirements have not been met. The owner or occupant who does not voluntarily comply within 30 days after the delivery of a warning notice by registered or certified mail shall be subject to criminal prosecution.
(c)
Any person violating a provision of this article shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be punished as provided in section 1-11; and such violation of this article may also be enforced by seeking an order to compel compliance or civil remedies against the violator. Prosecutions for violation of this article shall be upon citation as provided in O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63.
