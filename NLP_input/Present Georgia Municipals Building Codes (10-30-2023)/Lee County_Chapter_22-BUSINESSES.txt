Sec. 22-31. - Occupation tax required.
Each person engaged in any business, trade, profession or occupation in the unincorporated areas of the county, whether with a location in the unincorporated areas of the county or in the case of an out-of-state business with no location in Georgia exerting substantial efforts within the state pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7, shall pay an occupation tax for such business, trade, profession or occupation, which tax and any applicable registration shall be displayed in a conspicuous place in the place of business, if the taxpayer has a permanent business location in the county. If the taxpayer has no permanent business location in the county, such business tax registration shall be shown to the county clerk, the county sheriff, or their respective designees.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 1)
Sec. 22-32. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Administrative fee means a component of an occupation tax that approximates the reasonable cost of handling and processing the occupation tax.
Dominant line means the type of business, within a multiple-line business, that the greatest amount of income is derived from.
Location of office means any structure or vehicle where a business, profession or occupation is conducted, but shall not include a temporary or construction work site that serves a single customer or project or a vehicle used for sales or delivery by a business or practitioner of a profession or occupation that has a location or office.
Occupation tax means a tax levied on persons, partnerships, corporations or other entities for engaging in an occupation, profession or business for revenue-raising purposes.
Practitioner of profession or occupation means one who by state law requires state licensure regulating such profession or occupation. The term does not include a practitioner who is an employee of a business if the business pays an occupation tax.
Regulatory fees means payments, whether designated as license fees, permit fees, or by another name, required by the county as an exercise of its police power and as a part of or an aid to regulation of an occupation, profession or business. The amount of a regulatory fee shall approximate the reasonable cost of the actual regulatory activity performed by the county. A regulatory fee may not include an administrative fee. Development impact fees as defined by O.C.G.A. § 36-71-2(8) or other costs or conditions of zoning or land development are not regulatory fees.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 2)

Sec. 22-35. - Paying occupation tax of business with no location in state.
Registration and assessment of an occupation tax is imposed on those businesses and practitioners of professions with no location or office in the state if the business' largest dollar volume of business in the state is in the county, and the business or practitioner:
(1)
Has one or more employees or agents who exert substantial efforts within the jurisdiction of the unincorporated areas of the county for the purpose of soliciting business or serving customers or clients; or
(2)
Owns personal or real property that generates income and is located within the jurisdiction of the unincorporated areas of the county.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 5)

Sec. 22-39. - Purpose and scope of tax.
The occupation tax levied in this article is for revenue purposes only and is not for regulatory purposes, nor is the payment of the tax made a condition precedent to the practice of any such profession, trade or calling. The occupation tax only applies to those businesses and occupations covered by the provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 48-13-5—48-13-26. All other applicable businesses and occupations are taxed by the local government pursuant to the pertinent general and/or local law and ordinance.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 9)
Sec. 22-40. - Business license issued; violation for failure to pay and obtain license.
(a)
Upon payment of an occupation tax, administrative fee and regulatory fee, or any such combination as required in this article, the applicant shall receive a business license, which shall be displayed in a conspicuous place in the place of business if the taxpayer has a permanent business location in the county. If the taxpayer has no permanent business location in the county, such business license shall be shown to any police officer or enforcement officer of the county upon request.
(b)
It shall be a violation of this article for any person to operate or conduct a business, trade, profession or occupation in the county, whether with a location in the unincorporated areas of the county or in the case of an out-of-state business with no location in the state exerting substantial efforts within the state pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7, or to exercise, conduct or operate any business subject to a regulatory fee, without having first filed a return for payment of the applicable occupation tax, administrative fee and regulatory fee (or such combination as is applicable to such person) paid the applicable occupation tax, regulatory fee and administrative fee (or such combination as is applicable to such person), and obtained and displayed a business license. Where a person is subject to paying an occupation tax, administrative fee and regulatory fee under this article, such person shall obtain one business license, which shall reflect payment of all such charges.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 10)
Sec. 22-41. - When tax due and payable; effect of transacting business when tax delinquent.
(a)
Each occupation tax, administrative fee and regulatory fee shall be payable for the calendar year 1998 and succeeding calendar years thereafter unless otherwise specifically provided. This registration and occupation tax shall be payable on January 1 of each year and shall, if not paid by January 15 of each year, be subject to penalties for delinquencies prescribed in this article. On any new profession, trade or business begun in the unincorporated area of the county in 1998, or in succeeding years thereafter, the administrative fee, regulatory fee and occupation tax, as applicable, shall be delinquent if not obtained immediately upon beginning business, and a penalty of $50.00 shall be imposed. The tax registration provided in this section shall be issued by the revenue collection officer; and if any person whose duty it is to obtain a registration shall, after the registration, occupation tax, regulatory fee, or administrative fee becomes delinquent, transacts or offers to transact, in the unincorporated areas of the county, any of the kind of profession, trade or business subject to this article without having first obtained registration, such offender shall, upon conviction by the magistrate court, be punished by a fine not to exceed $500.00, or imprisonment not to exceed ten days, either or both, in the discretion of the judge of the court.
(b)
In addition to the above remedies specified in subsection (a) of this section, the sheriff may proceed to collect in the same manner as provided by law for tax executions.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 11)

Sec. 22-47. - Occupation tax inapplicable where prohibited by law or provided for pursuant to other existing law.
An occupation tax shall not apply to the gross receipts of any part of a business where such levy is prohibited or exempted by the laws of the state or of the United States.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 17)
Sec. 22-48. - Occupation tax limitations.
(a)
The county shall not require the payment of more than one occupation tax for each location of a person.
(1)
If a person with no location or office in the county provides to the county proof of payment of a local business or occupation tax in another state that purports to tax the person's gross receipts in this state, the person shall be exempt from the occupation tax levied by this article.
(2)
A person who has locations or offices elsewhere in the state who is subject to occupation tax by more than one local government in the state shall be subject to occupation tax by the county unless the person provides documentation to the county of current payment of an occupation tax, measured by the gross receipts of the person, to other state local governments.
(b)
If a person commences business in the county on or after July 1 of any year, the county shall be authorized to levy the customary rate on the gross receipts of the business or practitioner from the date of commencement of the business. The administrative fee authorized by this article shall not be reduced for any such business commencing on or after July 1 of any year.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 18)
Sec. 22-49. - Businesses at more than one location.
Wherever a business or occupation is conducted at more than one location or place within the county, each such location shall be considered a separate business for the purpose of the occupation tax imposed by this article.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 19)
Sec. 22-50. - Liability of officers and agents; registration required; failure to obtain.
All persons subject to the occupation tax levy pursuant to this article shall be required to obtain the necessary registration for the business as described in this article, and in default thereof the officer or agent soliciting for or representing such persons shall be subject to the same penalty as other persons who fail to obtain a registration. Every person commencing business in the county after January 1 of each year shall likewise obtain the registration provided for in this section before commencing business.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 20)
Sec. 22-51. - When registration and tax due and payable; effect of transacting business when tax delinquent.
(a)
Each registration shall be for the calendar year in which the registration was obtained unless otherwise specifically provided. There is imposed a penalty upon each business that fails to apply for and obtain and appropriate business registration and pay all taxes and fees as provided in this article before January 15 of each year. Each person commencing business in the unincorporated areas of the county after January 1 of each year shall obtain the registration required before commencing such business. Any person transacting or offering to transact in any unincorporated areas of the county any business, trade, profession or occupation without first having obtained the registration shall be subject to the penalties provided in section 22-52. These penalties shall be in addition to all other penalties, civil and criminal provided in this article, and may be collected by the remedies provided in this article for the collection of the occupation tax, and shall have the same lien and priority as the occupation tax to which the penalty is applied.
(b)
The registration provided for in this article shall be issued by the revenue collection officer; and if any person whose duty it is to obtain a registration shall, after the occupation tax becomes delinquent, transact or offer to transact, in the unincorporated areas of the county, any kind of business, trade, profession or occupation without having first obtained registration, such offender shall be subject to the penalties provided.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 21)
Sec. 22-52. - Due date; court penalty and interest.
A penalty is imposed on each business and practitioner of an occupation that fails to apply for and obtain appropriate registration and to pay any and all regulatory and administrative fees and occupation taxes required by this article. This penalty shall be in the amount of $50.00. When such regulatory and administrative fees and occupation taxes remain due and unpaid beyond 30 calendar days from the due date of the tax or fee, the total penalty shall be $100.00. Penalties shall be in addition to any other penalties, civil or criminal, that may be applicable. Such penalties should become effective when such regulatory and administrative fees and occupation taxes remain due and unpaid for 15 days from the due date of the tax or fee.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 22; Res. No. Z05-033, 9-23-2005)
Sec. 22-53. - Criminal penalty for violation of this article.
Any person violating any provision of this article shall, upon conviction before the magistrate court, be fined in an amount not exceeding $500.00 or imprisoned for a period not exceeding ten days, either or both, in the discretion of the judge.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 23)
Sec. 22-54. - Inspection of records.
The revenue collection agency of the county, through its officers, agents, employees or representatives, may inspect the books or records of any business pertaining to employees of the business or person for which the returns are made. The revenue collection agent shall have the right to inspect the books or records for the business for which the return was made in the county; and upon demand of the revenue collection agent, such books or records shall be submitted for inspection by the revenue collection agent or his designee within 30 days. Failure to submit such books or records within the 30-day period shall be grounds for revocation of the tax registration currently existing to do business in the county. Adequate records shall be kept in the county for examination by the revenue collection agent at that officer's discretion. If after examination of the books and records it is determined that a deficiency occurs as a result of underreporting, a penalty of $150.00 shall be assessed for the period delinquent. The right of inspection shall be limited to those records that reflect the number of employees of the person, business, trade, occupation or profession of the business.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 24)
Sec. 22-55. - Confidentiality.
Information provided by a person to the county for the purpose of determining the applicability and the amount of the occupation tax or the levy or collection of the occupation tax levied pursuant to this article is confidential and exempt from disclosure. Such information may be provided by the governing authority of another local government for occupation tax purposes or pursuant to court order or for the purpose of collecting the occupation tax or prosecution for failure or refusal to pay an occupation tax.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 25)
Sec. 22-56. - Revocation.
Failure of any person to pay an applicable administrative fee, regulatory fee, occupation tax, or any part of a tax, before it becomes delinquent, or upon the failure to permit inspection of books as required, shall result in any business tax certificates or regulatory fee certificates granted by the county under this article permitting such person to do business in the unincorporated areas of the county for the current year being automatically revoked without any further action required of the county. No new occupation tax certificate or regulatory fee certificate shall be granted by the county for the operation of a business for which any part of the occupation tax or fees required in this article are unpaid, nor shall such certificates be issued to a person who has failed to submit adequate records for inspection as required by section 22-54.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 26)
Sec. 22-57. - Filing of occupation tax returns.
Any persons, their managers, agents or employees, who do business in the unincorporated areas of the county, after the registration for the business has been revoked, are required to make occupation tax returns in a form to make such returns within the time and manner provided in this article, who refuse to amend such returns so as to set forth the truth, or who shall make false returns; and any persons, their managers, agents or employees who refuse to permit an inspection of books and may charge when the officers, agents, employees or representatives of the county request such inspection, during normal business hours, for the purpose of determining the accuracy of the returns provided for in this article, shall be subject to penalties provided in this article. In the case of those practitioners where the local government cannot suspend the right of the practitioner to conduct business, the imposition of civil penalties shall be permitted and pursued by the local government as in the case of a delinquent occupation tax. These penalties shall consist of either ten percent of the amount due or a fine of $50.00, whichever is less, as determined by the revenue collection agency.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 27)
Sec. 22-58. - Execution; liens.
(a)
The revenue collection agent or his designee, upon any tax or installment of any tax becoming delinquent and remaining unpaid, shall issue an execution for the amount of the tax against the person liable for the tax, including interest at the rate of 1.5 percent per month accruing on the tax. The lien shall cover the property in the unincorporated areas of the county of the person liable for the tax, all as provided by ordinances of the county and the laws of the state. The lien for the occupation tax shall become fixed on and date from the time that such tax or installment becomes delinquent. The execution shall be levied by the sheriff upon the property of the defendant located in such jurisdiction, and sufficient property shall be advertised and sold to pay the amount of the execution, with interest and costs added. All other related proceedings shall be as provided by ordinances of the county and the laws of the state; and the defendants at execution shall have the rights of defense, by affidavit of illegality of the tax or otherwise as provided by the applicable laws of the state in regard to tax executions.
(b)
When a nulla bona entry has been entered by the proper authority upon execution issued by the county against any person defaulting on an occupation tax, the person against whom the entry was made shall not be allowed or entitled to have or collect any fees or charges whatsoever for services rendered after the entry of the nulla bona. If at any time after the entry of the nulla bona has been made, the person against whom the execution pays the tax in full, together with all interest penalties and costs accrued on the tax, the person may collect any fees and charges for services and goods due the person as though the person had never defaulted in the payment of the taxes.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 28)
Sec. 22-59. - Public hearings required.
(a)
In any year when revenue from occupation taxes is greater than revenue from occupation taxes for the preceding year for the county, the county shall hold one or more public hearings as a part of the process of determining how to use the additional revenue derived from such occupation taxes. This hearing shall be held pursuant to the requirements of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-28.
(b)
The board of commissioners shall conduct at least one public hearing before adopting any future ordinance or resolution regarding the occupation tax as required in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-6(c).
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 29)
Sec. 22-60. - Applications of provisions to prior ordinance.
This article does not repeal or affect the enforcement of any part of any ordinance heretofore passed where taxes levied under such prior ordinance have not been paid in full. So much and such parts of ordinances heretofore and hereinafter passed as provided for the issuing and enforcing of execution for any tax or assessment required by such ordinances, or that imposed fines or penalties for the nonpayment of such tax, or for failure to pay regulatory fees provided for in such ordinances, or failure to comply with any other provisions of this article, shall continue and remain in force until such tax, regulatory fee or assessment shall be fully paid.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 30)
Sec. 22-61. - Amendment; repeal of provisions.
This article shall be subject to amendment or repeal, in whole or in part, at any time; and no such amendment or repeal shall be construed to deny the right of the board of commissioners to assess and collect any of the taxes or other charges prescribed. The amendment may increase or lower the amounts and tax rates of any occupation and may change their classification. Payment of any occupation tax provided for shall not be construed as prohibiting the levy or collection by the board of commissioners of additional occupation taxes upon the same person, property or business.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 31)
Sec. 22-62. - Option to establish exemption or reduction in occupation tax.
The board of commissioners may by subsequent ordinance or resolution provide for an exemption or reduction in occupation taxes or a credit against occupation taxes owed to one or more types of businesses or practitioners of occupations or professions as part of a plan for economic development or attracting, encouraging or maintaining selected types of businesses or practitioners of selected occupations or professions. Such exemptions or reductions in occupation taxes shall not be arbitrary or capricious, and the reasons shall be set forth in the minutes of the board of commissioners. Such exemptions, reductions or credits may include but shall not be limited to those exemptions, reductions or credits more particularly set out in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-10(f)(2).
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 32)
Sec. 22-63. - Conflicts between specific and general provisions.
Where there is an apparent conflict in this article between specific and general provisions, it is the intention of this article that the specific shall control.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 33)
Sec. 22-64. - No limitation of authority of board of commissioners.
Nothing in this article shall be construed to limit the authority of the board of commissioners to regulate the exercise of business in any unincorporated areas of the county and, where authorized by federal, state, or local law or ordinance, to deny persons seeking a business registration or license, or both; nor shall this article in any way limit the authority of the board of commissioners to establish ordinances or regulations setting forth conditions for the registration or licensure of businesses, trades, occupations or professions. Nothing in this article is intended, nor shall it be construed, to limit the police powers of the county with respect to business practices as they may be established by federal, state or local law, ordinances or regulations.
(Ord. of 1-8-1998, § 34)
Secs. 22-65—22-99. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - MOBILE FOOD TRUCKS

Sec. 22-100. - Mobile food trucks.
Mobile food trucks shall be allowed to sell, serve, and deliver food and food-related items within the unincorporated areas of Lee County. Any such mobile food truck, whether owned by an individual or legal entity, shall only be authorized to operate as a mobile food truck in accord with this section if the person or legal entity owning the mobile food truck obtains an appropriate permit or license from the Lee County Health Department and pays the applicable occupation taxes and regulatory fees imposed pursuant to chapter 22, article II of the Lee County Code.
(Ord. of 12-8-2015(3))

Sec. 22-120. - Authorization of event centers.
Subject to the provisions of this article, the provisions of chapter 6, and the provisions of chapter 70, of the Code of Ordinances of Lee County, there shall be authorized within the unincorporated limits of the county event centers to operate as privately owned for-profit commercial business enterprises in accord with the terms of this article.
(Ord. of 10-12-2021 )
Sec. 22-121. - Definitions.
Event means an event consists of a celebration, reception, party, concert, holiday gathering, family gathering, corporate function, general gathering, birthday party, retirement event, employee appreciation event, wedding, wedding reception, anniversary celebration, funeral, and other similar parties, receptions and events. An event center does not include retail sales, amphitheaters, rodeos, circuses, or similar public events. An event center may host either public or private events. An event center shall be open only for scheduled event-based operations.
Event center means a commercial building or facility where events are permitted to occur under this article. Any such center shall be a permanent structure (not a temporary structure) which meets all local and state-wide building codes applicable to the type of commercial building to be used as an event center. An event center, building or facility, or the portion of a building or facility utilized as an event center, shall consist of not more than 10,000 square feet. No residential structure shall be considered to be an event center.
Private event means an event held under this article that is invitation-only.
Public event means an event held under this article where the public is invited or allowed to attend. For purposes of this article, an event for which tickets or other evidence of authority to attend the event are required for persons in attendance at the event, whether such tickets are purchased or distributed without any cost or considerations, shall be considered to be a public event.
Zoning Code means chapter 70 of the Code of Ordinances of Lee County
(Ord. of 10-12-2021 )
Sec. 22-122. - Occupation tax required.
The owner of an event center shall be required to pay the applicable occupation taxes and regulatory fees imposed pursuant to chapter 22 article II of the Lee County Code.
(Ord. of 10-12-2021 )
Sec. 22-123. - Location access on an arterial or collector street and parking requirements.
(a)
All event facilities shall be located with primary access on a collector or arterial street.
(b)
An event center shall have one parking space for each four seats in the center or one parking space for each 150 square feet of gross area of the event center, whichever is greater. With respect to calculating required parking spaces for an event center, the provisions of section 70-605(21) Indoor and Outdoor Recreational Facilities of the County Code shall be used for each event center.
(Ord. of 10-12-2021 )
Sec. 22-124. - Times of operation and noise control.
(a)
Event centers may utilize indoor and outdoor spaces, provided that any outdoor activities hosted at event centers adjacent to residentially zoned property shall be limited to the hours of 10:00 a.m. to 10:00 p.m. Monday through Saturday and 12:00 noon to 10:00 p.m. on Sundays.
(b)
An event center shall authorize the consumption or serving of alcoholic beverages in compliance with section 6-162, Hours of sale.
(c)
Event centers shall observe chapter 34—Environment, article III, Noise regulations.
(Ord. of 10-12-2021 )
Sec. 22-125. - Capacity limitations.
No event center shall be authorized to exceed capacity limitations with respect to the event center as established by the county fire chief pursuant to applicable regulations.
(Ord. of 10-12-2021 )
Sec. 22-126. - Health and safety.
(a)
Each event center shall be obligated to comply with all applicable health and safety regulations during an event, including, but not limited to, health and safety regulations relating to the wearing of masks, social distancing, and other similar regulations established by state law, gubernatorial executive order, or local ordinances.
(b)
Any on-site kitchen or catering facility shall comply with all applicable local and state regulations, including, but not limited to, the rules and regulations of the environmental health department, the Georgia Department of Agriculture, and compliance with chapter 6—Alcoholic Beverages in its entirety; particularly including section 6-37, Alcoholic beverage caterers.
(Ord. of 10-12-2021 )
