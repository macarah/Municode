ARTICLE II. - PAWNBROKERS

Sec. 48-19. - Definitions.
The definitions set forth in O.C.G.A. § 44-12-130 ("Pawnbrokers—Definitions"), shall be effective as definitions of the words, terms and phrases used in this article. All words, terms and phrases used herein, other than those specifically defined elsewhere in this article, shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them in O.C.G.A. § 44-12-130, and shall have the same scope and effect that the same words, terms and phrases have where used in O.C.G.A. § 44-12-130.
(Code 1985, § 23-16)
Sec. 48-20. - Requirements generally.
No license shall be granted to any person to engage in the business of a pawnbroker until the applicant shall have first applied therefor to the license inspector, paid the license fee prescribed, and obtained approval of such application from the board of city commissioners.
(Code 1975, § 27-41; Code 1985, § 23-17)
Sec. 48-21. - Contents of application for license.
The application for a license under the provisions of this article shall state the location of the place of business and the names of the owners or parties interested directly or indirectly, and if a corporation, the names of all the officers and stockholders shall be stated.
(Code 1975, § 27-42; Code 1985, § 23-18)
Sec. 48-22. - Daily report to police department.
(a)
Every pawnbroker shall maintain a permanent electronic record of its pawn transactions in which an accurate description of all property pledged, traded or sold to the pawnshop can be transmitted to the city police department via an electronic automated reporting system. Each of these transactions shall contain an accurate description of all property pledged, traded or sold to the pawnshop and shall be made at the time of each transaction, and shall contain the following information:
(1)
The date and time of the purchase, pawn or sale of the property.
(2)
The name, address and telephone number of the customer making the pledge, trade or sale.
(3)
A description of the customer in terms of sex, race, date of birth, height and weight, as well as the driver's license number of the customer or some other identification card which contains a photograph of the customer.
(4)
A description of the pledged or purchased property by serial, model or other number, if available, and by any identifying marks (e.g., brand name, color, style, etc.).
(5)
The number of the receipt or pawn transaction issued for the property pawned or bought.
(6)
The price paid or the amount loaned.
(7)
The maturity date of the transaction, if a pawn.
(8)
Right thumbprint of the customer will be obtained on the pawn slip at the time of the transaction.
(9)
The signature of the customer.
(b)
Every pawnbroker shall make a daily report to the police department of the described information in subsection (a) of this section in such form and manner as directed by the chief of police or the chief of police's designee of all property pledged, received, traded, bartered, bought or otherwise acquired by the pawnbroker during the 24 hours, ending at 8:00 p.m. on the date of such report. This report is required via an Internet based system, in a manner directed by the chief of police or the chief of police's designee using such software compatible to that used by the city police department.
(c)
In the event that the electronic automated reporting system becomes temporarily or permanently disabled, pawnshops and pawnbrokers will be notified as soon as possible. In this event, the pawnbrokers will be required to make records of transactions in paper form. Such paper forms must include all information as enumerated in subsection (a) of this section. Pawnbrokers shall maintain at least a three-day supply of these paper forms.
(d)
Any duly authorized law enforcement officer may, during the ordinary hours of business or any other reasonable time, inspect any pawnbroker's electronic records at the pawnbroker's place of business to ensure compliance with this section.
(e)
The chief of police or the chief of police's designee shall select and designate the required automated reporting system.
(f)
Pawnbroker's are hereby notified that failure or refusal to comply with these requirements is grounds for a forfeiture of the pawnbroker's license and occupation tax certificate.
(g)
Records shall be maintained for at least the time period as required by O.C.G.A. § 44-12-134.
(Code 1975, § 27-44; Code 1985, § 23-19; Ord. No. 08-138, § 2(23-19), 9-23-2008)
Sec. 48-23. - Waiting period.
(a)
Any pawnbroker who makes a loan on pledged goods or buys pledged goods on the condition that the seller may repurchase such goods, shall hold said goods for at least 30 days before disposing of them by sale, transfer, shipment or otherwise. Nonpledged goods bought under this section shall be held for at least 30 calendar days before disposition by sale, transfer, shipment or otherwise.
(b)
Whenever the police department shall have probable cause to suspect any property listed in the daily report of the pawnbroker is stolen property or constitutes evidence of a crime, the police department shall notify the pawnbroker not to sell any such property or permit the same to be redeemed, for at least 48 hours in addition to the time provided for in subsection (a) of this section. Such hold can be extended in 30 day increments by the police department based upon probable cause to suspect such property is stolen or constitutes evidence of a crime.
(Ord. No. 08-138, § 2(23-20), 9-23-2008)

ARTICLE III. - CLOSING OUT SALES[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Fair Business Practices Act of 1975, O.C.G.A. § 10-1-390 et seq.; unlawful to misrepresent true ownership of business for purposes of liquidation sale, auction sale, or "going out of business" sale, O.C.G.A. § 10-1-425.

Sec. 48-56. - Exceptions.
(a)
The provisions of this article shall not apply to commercial travelers or selling agents selling goods, wares and merchandise to dealers at wholesale.
(b)
No provision of this article shall be applicable to trustees in bankruptcy, executors, administrators, receivers or public officials acting under judicial process.
(Code 1975, § 27-22; Code 1985, § 23-36)
Sec. 48-57. - Inventory list, etc., to be filed prior to sale.
Whenever any person desires to advertise, represent or hold or conduct any sale of goods, wares or merchandise that is to be advertised or represented as a closing out sale, a going-out-of-business sale, a quitting business sale, or a sale designated in any manner by which it is represented that the business of such person is being terminated or closed out, such person shall file with the license inspector of the city an inventory of the goods, wares or merchandise to be sold. The inventory required shall contain a complete and accurate list of the stocks of goods, wares or merchandise to be sold, together with their wholesale price.
(Code 1975, § 27-21; Code 1985, § 23-37)
Sec. 48-58. - Original stock only.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell, offer or expose for sale, or to include in the inventory provided for in section 48-57, any goods, wares or merchandise which are not an actual part of the original stock of goods of the applicant.
(Code 1975, § 27-23; Code 1985, § 23-37)
Sec. 48-59. - Sales restricted.
It shall be unlawful for any person at any sale under the provisions of this article to sell any goods, wares or merchandise not specifically described in the inventory filed with the city for such sale.
(Code 1975, § 27-24; Code 1985, § 23-38)
Sec. 48-60. - Additions.
It shall be unlawful for any person conducting a sale under the provisions of this article to make any replenishments or additions to such person's stock for the purpose of such sale.
(Code 1975, § 27-25; Code 1985, § 23-40)
Sec. 48-61. - Duration.
It shall be unlawful for any person conducting a sale under the provisions of this article to allow such sale to exceed 60 days; provided, however, in the event a longer time is necessary or required, the city commission may grant an additional 30 days upon the sufficient showing of such necessity.
(Code 1975, § 27-26; Code 1985, § 23-41)
Secs. 48-62—48-109. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - SECONDHAND DEALERS[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
Editor's note— Ord. No. 10-104, §§ 1, 2, adopted Jan. 26, 2010, deleted the former Art. IV, §§ 48-110—48-112, and enacted a new Art. IV as set out herein. The former Art. IV pertained to secondhand dealers and derived from Code 1985, §§ 23-81—23-83; Ord. No. 89-172, §§ 1—3, 10-24-1989.

Sec. 48-110. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the following words and terms shall have the meanings respectively ascribed:
Secondhand dealer means any person, corporation or other business organization or entity which is engaged in the business of buying, selling, bartering, or exchanging in any manner at retail or wholesale, or otherwise dealing for profit in, secondhand goods.
Secondhand goods means personal property previously owned or used, which is not regulated metals property and which is offered for sale, barter, exchange, bailment, or in any other such transaction as used property. Such secondhand goods shall be limited to stoves, refrigerators, air conditioners, and other household appliances; jewelry, gold, silver, platinum or other precious metals; watches; diamonds, gems and other precious stones; fishing rods, reels and tackle; audio and video electric equipment, including television sets, compact disc players, radios, amplifiers, receivers, turntables, tape recorders; video tape recorders; speakers and citizens band radios; computer equipment; radar detectors; depth finders; trolling motors; outboard motors; sterling silver flatware and serving pieces; photographic equipment, including cameras, video and film cameras, lenses, electronic flashes, tripods and developing equipment; firearms; tools, calculators; musical instruments; lawnmowers; bicycles; and typewriters.
Transaction means any purchase, trade, consignment or exchange of secondhand goods by a secondhand dealer.
(Ord. No. 10-104, § 2, 1-26-2010)
Sec. 48-111. - Reports required.
(a)
Each secondhand dealer shall file daily written reports at 10:00 a.m. with the city police department's chief of police. The chief of police may delegate this authority to a deputy police chief or other official, as the police chief deems appropriate in connection with any transaction involving secondhand goods by such dealer within the preceding 24 hours. The report shall contain the following information:
(1)
The name of the person or other entity whom the secondhand goods is purchased;
(2)
The residence, address or place of business of such person or entity;
(3)
Identification of such person or entity, including the driver's license number and vehicle license plate number;
(4)
The date of birth and Social Security number of the person from whom the secondhand goods are purchased;
(5)
A description of the secondhand goods (brand name, model and serial number) acquired in the purchase transaction; and
(6)
A statement from the person stating he or the entity he represents is the lawful owner of the secondhand goods.
(b)
Every secondhand dealer shall maintain a permanent record book, in English, in which all purchase transactions, shall be in ink and in chronological order. Corrections shall be made by drawing a line of ink through the entry without destroying its legibility. The following information is required:
(1)
The date of the transaction;
(2)
The name and employee number of the person conducting the transaction;
(3)
The name of the maker of each article, any identifying mark or number including the serial number, and a detailed description of the article to include, but not limited to, the type of material which the article is made of, any owner applied numbers or names on the article. In regards to articles of jewelry there must be the type of material, type of gems, weight/type stone cut for the gems, any identifying marks, i.e. Masons, named college, high school name, military branch, years, engraving, etc.
(4)
The name, address, and age of the customer by whom same was pledged, pawned, or sold.
(5)
The price paid or credited for each article and a total for the transaction.
(c)
This record book shall be retained by such secondhand dealer for a minimum of two years following the date of recording of the purchase therein.
(d)
This record book shall be made available for inspection by any federal, state, county or local law enforcement officer. The officer will show proper identification and the inspection will take place during the usual customary business hours of the secondhand dealer, at the normal place of business of the secondhand dealer.
(Ord. No. 10-104, § 2, 1-26-2010)
Sec. 48-112. - Waiting period.
(a)
All secondhand transactions require the secondhand dealer to hold said goods for at least 30 days before disposing of them by sale, transfer, shipment or otherwise.
(b)
Whenever the city police department shall have probable cause to suspect any property listed in the daily report of the secondhand dealer is stolen property or constitutes evidence of a crime, the police department shall notify the secondhand dealer not to sell or otherwise transfer such property for at least 48 hours in addition to the time provided for in subsection (a) of this section. Such hold can be extended in 30-day increments by the police department based upon probable cause to suspect such property is stolen or constitutes evidence of a crime.
(c)
Each secondhand dealer who (i) maintains a permanent electronic record of its transactions in which an accurate description of all property traded, purchased or sold to the secondhand dealer can be transmitted to the city police department via an electronic automated reporting system; and (ii) who complies with this subsection as well as subsections (d), (e), (f), (g) and (h) below is granted an exemption from subsections (a) and (b) above. Each of these transactions shall contain an accurate description of all property traded, purchased or sold to the secondhand dealer and shall be made at the time of each transaction, and shall contain the following information:
(1)
The date and time of the purchase or sale of the property.
(2)
The name, address and telephone number of the customer making the trade or sale.
(3)
A description of the customer in terms of sex, race, date of birth, height and weight, as well as the driver's license number of the customer or some other identification card that contains a photograph of the customer.
(4)
A description of the purchased property by serial, model or other number, if available, and by any identifying marks (e.g., brand name, color, style, etc.).
(5)
The number of the receipt or invoice issued for the purchased property.
(6)
The price paid.
(7)
Right thumbprint of the customer will be obtained on the receipt/invoice at the time of the transaction.
(8)
The signature of the customer.
(d)
Every secondhand dealer shall make a daily report to the police department of the above described information in such form and manner as directed by the chief of police or his designee of all property received, traded, bartered, bought or otherwise acquired by the secondhand dealer during the 24 hours, ending at 8:00 p.m. on the date of such report. This report is required via an internet based system, in a manner directed by the chief of police or his designee using such software compatible to that used by the city police department.
(e)
In the event that the electronic automated reporting system becomes temporarily or permanently disabled, secondhand dealer will be notified as soon as possible. In this event, the secondhand dealer will be required to make records of transactions in paper form. Such paper forms must include all information as enumerated in subsection (a) of this section. Secondhand dealers shall maintain at least a three-day supply of these paper forms.
(f)
Any duly authorized law enforcement officer may, during the ordinary hours of business or any other reasonable time, inspect any secondhand dealer's electronic records at the secondhand dealer's place of business to ensure compliance with this section.
(g)
The chief of police or his designee shall select and designate the required automated reporting system.
(h)
All secondhand transactions require the secondhand dealer to hold said goods for at least 15 days before disposing of them by sale, transfer, shipment or otherwise.
(Ord. No. 10-104, § 2, 1-26-2010)
Sec. 48-113. - Exemption.
This article shall not apply to purchasers of secondhand goods from:
(1)
Any organization or entity registered with the state as a non-profit, religious or charitable organization, or any school-sponsored association or organization.
(2)
A trustee in bankruptcy, executor, administrator, or receiver who has presented proof of such status to the secondhand dealer.
(3)
A sale on the execution, or by virtue of any process issued by a court, if proof thereof has been presented to the secondhand dealer. The secondhand dealer shall keep such proof for two years.
(4)
Any dealer who sells household personal property as an agent for the property owner or their representative pursuant to a written agreement at that person's residence.
(Ord. No. 10-104, § 2, 1-26-2010)
