Chapter 10 - CEMETERIES
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 10-1. - Burials to be in city cemetery.
It shall be unlawful for any person to bury, or cause to be buried, any dead person in any place in the city other than within the city cemetery or some other duly authorized and recognized cemetery. Any burial contrary to this section is hereby declared a nuisance and shall be abated as such.
(Code 1974, §§ 6-1—6-4; Code 1991, § 2-142(c))

Sec. 10-21. - Powers and duties of city clerk.
(a)
The city clerk shall have the care and control of the city cemetery. He shall:
(1)
Keep a correct map of the cemetery and book showing the number of each lot sold, size of the lot, the price paid and when paid.
(2)
Collect all sums due the city for cemetery lots.
(3)
Show and sell lots in the cemetery and draft the deed for any lot sold.
(4)
Pay all sums realized from the sale of cemetery lots into the city treasury.
(b)
The city clerk shall make a report to the mayor and council, at its last meeting in September, of the amount of money realized from the sale of cemetery lots, the number sold, to whom sold, and the price of each, the amount of money collected on account of cemetery lots previously sold, and from whom collected, the amount of money due the city for lots sold, and by whom due.
(Code 1974, §§ 6-1—6-4; Code 1991, § 2-142(a), (b))
Sec. 10-22. - Destroying, injuring or removing property.
It shall be unlawful for any person to mutilate, destroy, deface, damage, injure or remove any tomb, monument, gravestone or other structure placed in the city cemetery, or any fence, railing or other work or property located in the cemetery, or to willfully cut, break, deface, injure or remove any tree, shrub or flower planted or growing in the cemetery.
(Code 1974, §§ 6-1—6-4; Code 1991, § 2-142(d))
Sec. 10-23. - Littering.
It shall be unlawful for any person to place any trash, grass or other refuse or litter of any sort within the city cemetery. Any person cleaning any cemetery lot shall place all trash and litter resulting therefrom at such location as the person in charge of the cemetery shall direct.
(Code 1974, §§ 6-1—6-4; Code 1991, § 2-142(e))
