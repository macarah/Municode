Sec. 10-1. - Gambling devices prohibited.
Gambling devices, as that term is defined in O.C.G.A. § 16-12-20(2) are prohibited in the city, and the ownership, use, or transport thereof shall be a misdemeanor pursuant to state law, except as exempted pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(a) through (k).
(Ord. No. 15-17, § 1, 11-2-2015)
Sec. 10-2. - Gambling places prohibited.
Gambling places, as that term is defined in O.C.G.A. § 16-12-20(3) are prohibited in the city, and the operation thereof shall be a misdemeanor pursuant to state law.
(Ord. No. 15-17, § 1, 11-2-2015)
Sec. 10-3. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, or phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meaning ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Amusement game room means any location as provided in O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(b), (c) or (d) where one or more bona fide coin operated amusement machines are operated that permit non-cash redemption as provided in O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(d)(1)(B), (C), or a combination thereof.
Bona fide coin operated amusement machine means the same as this term is defined in O.C.G.A. § 50-27-70(b)(2)(A) and any applicable regulations of the State of Georgia. Examples of bona fide coin operated amusement machines include, but are expressly not limited to, the following:

Crane machines;

Claw machines;

(7)
Bowling machines;
(8)
Novelty arcade games;
(9)
Foosball or table soccer machines;
(10)
Miniature racetrack, football or golf machines;
(11)
Target or shooting gallery machines;
(12)
Basketball machines;

Shuffleboard machines;
(14)
Kiddie ride games;

Location owner or location operator means an owner or operator of a business where one or more bona fide coin operated amusement machines are available for commercial use and play by the public, or shall have the same definition as found in the O.C.G.A. § 50-27-70, should that definition differ.
(Ord. No. 15-17, § 1, 11-2-2015)
Sec. 10-4. - Occupation tax required.
No person, firm or corporation shall engage in the business of an owner or proprietor of amusement game room, as the term is herein defined, without first having paid the required occupational tax and obtained an occupational tax certificate.
(Ord. No. 15-17, § 1, 11-2-2015)
Sec. 10-5. - Privilege; number of bona fide coin operated amusement machines at a location.
It shall be a privilege for the location owner or location operator within the city to offer bona fide coin-operated amusement machines to the public at the business location. No business owner or business operator shall offer to the public more than nine Class B bona fide coin-operated amusement machines that reward the player exclusively with noncash merchandise, prizes, toys, gift certificates, or novelties at the same business location.
(Ord. No. 15-17, § 1, 11-2-2015)
Sec. 10-6. - Notice requirements.
(a)
Every amusement game room shall post a conspicuous sign with the following or substantially similar language:
"GEORGIA LAW PROHIBITS GIVING OR RECEIPT OF ANY MONEY FOR WINNING A GAME OR GAMES ON AN AMUSEMENT MACHINE; GIVING OR RECEIPT OF MONEY FOR FREE REPLAYS WON ON AN AMUSEMENT MACHINE; GIVING OR RECEIPT OF MONEY FOR ANY MERCHANDISE, PRIZE, TOY, GIFT CERTIFICATE, OR NOVELTY WON ON ANY AMUSEMENT MACHINE; OR AWARDING ANY MERCHANDISE, PRIZE, TOY, GIFT CERTIFICATE, OR NOVELTY OF A VALUE EXCEEDING $5.00 FOR A SINGLE PLAY OF AN AMUSEMENT MACHINE."
(b)
The owner or proprietor of each amusement game room shall inform every employee of the acts and omissions prohibited by O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35 and by this article, and of the penalties for violation of O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35 and this article.
(Ord. No. 15-17, § 1, 11-2-2015)
Sec. 10-7. - Alcohol license suspension; revocation or non-renewal.
The city may suspend, revoke or refuse to renew the city issued alcohol beverage license of any location owner or location operator upon the conviction of the location owner or location operator of a violation of the O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(e), (f), or (g).
(Ord. No. 15-17, § 1, 11-2-2015)
Sec. 10-8. - Suspension or revocation of privilege; monetary sanction.
The privilege of a location owner or location operator to offer bona fide coin-operated amusement machines to the public may be suspended or revoked upon a finding that such location owner or location operator violated this article. The conviction of the location owner or location operator of a violation of the O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(e), (f), or (g) shall cause the automatic revocation of the privilege of such location owner or location operator to offer bona fide coin-operated amusement machines to the public for a period of 60 months. A violation of this article shall also subject the violator to the imposition of monetary sanctions.
(Ord. No. 15-17, § 1, 11-2-2015)
Sec. 10-9. - Notice of violation.
The city clerk shall notify the location owner or location operator of any charge of conduct or offense subject to monetary sanction, suspension or revocation or any combination thereof. If the potential sanction is suspension or revocation the notice shall also state the place and time the public safety commission will hear the charges against the location owner or location operator. The notice may also contain such additional information, as the city clerk may deem appropriate. The notice shall be delivered to the location owner or location operator at least five days prior to the hearing date by personal delivery or first class mail addressed to the location owner or location operator at the address contained in the occupation tax application of the location owner or location operator. In the case of delivery by first class mail the notice shall be deemed delivered two days after being deposited into the US mail properly addressed and with adequate postage.
(Ord. No. 15-17, § 1, 11-2-2015)
Sec. 10-10. - Action by public safety commission; sanctions.
Unless waived by the location owner or location operator, the public safety commission shall conduct a hearing on any charge against a location owner or location operator alleging conduct or offense that is subject to a suspension or revocation, at its next regularly scheduled meeting, but not later than 60 days from the date of notice to the location owner or location operator unless a continuance is granted at the request of the city or the location owner or location operator. The public safety commission shall have the discretion to call a special meeting to conduct a meeting or to reschedule or continue any hearing upon the request of the city or the location owner or location operator. The location owner or location operator shall be allowed to appear at said hearing and to present evidence and cross-examine witnesses. Upon hearing evidence the public safety commission shall, no later than 30 days after the hearing, either dismiss all or some the charges against the location owner or location operator and/or, make a finding that a violation has occurred as to all or any one of the charges. If a violation is found to have occurred and it is the first violation under this article by the location owner or location operator, the public safety commission shall levy a monetary sanction of $100.00. If a violation is found to have occurred and it is the second offense under this article, the public safety commission shall levy a monetary sanction of $500.00 and shall make a recommendation to the mayor and council that the privilege to offer bona fide coin-operated amusement machines to the public be suspended for a period of time not to exceed 60 days. If a violation is found to have occurred and it is the third offense or more under this article, the public safety commission shall levy a monetary sanction of $1,000.00 and shall make a recommendation to the mayor and council that the privilege of the location owner or location operator to offer bona fide coin-operated amusement machines to the public be suspended be suspended for a period of time not to exceed 120 days, or revoked. The public safety commission may also make a finding that a location owner or location operator shall be allowed to pay any applicable monetary sanction within a time certain not to exceed 30 days.
(Ord. No. 15-17, § 1, 11-2-2015)
Sec. 10-11. - Action by mayor and council.
The mayor and council shall perform an on-record review of the hearing record before the public safety commission. The mayor and council shall determine from the hearing record whether there is sufficient evidence to support the finding of the public safety commission. The location owner or location operator shall not be permitted to present additional evidence or arguments before the mayor and council. If the matter before the mayor and council concerns a suspension recommendation, then upon a finding by the mayor and council that sufficient evidence exists to support the recommendation, the mayor and council may accept the recommended days of suspension, decrease the number of days of suspension, or increase the number of days of suspension up to the maximum set forth in section 10-11 of this article. If the matter before the mayor and council concerns a revocation recommendation, then upon a finding by the mayor and council that sufficient evidence exists to support the recommendation, the mayor and council shall revoke the privilege of the location owner or location operator to offer bona fide coin-operated amusement machines to the public for a period not to exceed 60 months. The mayor and council shall, after deliberation, make such determination as to sufficient evidence at its first regular or called meeting after delivery of the hearing record, but in any event no later than 60 days after the hearing before the public safety commission, and shall notify the location owner or location operator of its determination at such meeting or in writing no later than 30 days after such meeting. Failure of the mayor and council to confirm the findings of the public safety commission within 60 days after the hearing before the public safety commission shall be deemed a finding that insufficient evidence exists to support the findings of the public safety commission and shall result in a dismissal of the charge.
(Ord. No. 15-17, § 1, 11-2-2015)
Sec. 10-12. - Appeal to mayor and council; notice.
(a)
Any location owner or location operator adversely affected by a determination by the public safety commission with regard to a violation of this article may appeal such decision to the mayor and council. Any such appeal must be filed with the city clerk within 15 days of the date of determination by the public safety commission.
(b)
The city clerk shall give a location owner or location operator at least five business days written notice of the time and date of the meeting of the mayor and council at which the appeal will be considered.
(Ord. No. 15-17, § 1, 11-2-2015)
Sec. 10-13. - Unlawful operation; penalty.
It shall be unlawful for any person to offer one or more bona fide coin operated amusement machine games in violation of an order suspending or revoking the privilege of such person to offer bona fide coin-operated amusement machines to the public. Such person shall be subject to citation to the municipal court of the city, and upon conviction shall be subject to a fine not to exceed $1,000.00.
(Ord. No. 15-17, § 1, 11-2-2015)
Sec. 10-14. - Kept in plain view.
All bona fide coin operated amusement machines shall be placed and kept in plain view and accessible to any person at the business location.
(Ord. No. 15-17, § 1, 11-2-2015)
Sec. 10-15. - Costs.
If due cause to assess sanctions is found to exist, then the location owner or location operator shall be charged with and shall pay, in addition to any sanction, the actual costs of the take down and transcription of the hearing proceedings and, if applicable, the cost of translation services.
(Ord. No. 15-17, § 1, 11-2-2015)

ARTICLE III. - DANCEHALLS

Sec. 10-61. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Dancehall means any place of business or leased nonresidential building where dancing is indulged in or permitted either by the owner or the lessee if applicable, of such premises, whether on a regular basis or not, whether a fee or admission is charged or not, and where music therefore is supplied by any means whatsoever.
(Ord. No. 00-20, § 1(12-121), 11-20-2000)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 10-62. - Exemptions.
Governmental facilities, educational facilities, and churches shall not be deemed dancehalls and shall be exempt from this article. The term "churches" as used in this article shall have the same definition as the term "church" contained in section 6-1.
(Ord. No. 00-20, § 1(12-122), 11-20-2000)
Sec. 10-63. - Right of entry for purpose of inspection.
Dancehalls shall be at all times open to the public authorities for entrance and inspection.
(Ord. No. 00-20, § 1(12-123), 11-20-2000)
Sec. 10-64. - Duty of operator of dancehall.
The operator of a dancehall shall have the responsibility for compliance with this article. If the operator fails to do so, either intentionally or by inadvertence, then such operator shall be in violation of this article and upon conviction of each such violation shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 or by imprisonment for a period not to exceed 20 days, or both.
(Ord. No. 00-20, § 1(12-124), 11-20-2000)
Sec. 10-65. - Frequenting by intoxicated persons.
No person while in an intoxicated condition shall enter into or remain in a dancehall when dances are in progress.
(Ord. No. 00-20, § 1(12-125), 11-20-2000)
Sec. 10-66. - License required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate or conduct a dancehall within the city without a license. The fee for the license shall be $500.00 annually.
(Ord. No. 00-20, § 1(12-126), 11-20-2000)
Sec. 10-67. - Application for license.
Application for a dancehall license shall be made to the city clerk's office on such form or forms as may be prepared by the city clerk from time to time.
(Ord. No. 00-20, § 1(12-127), 11-20-2000)
Sec. 10-68. - Requirements.
A dancehall must meet the following requirements at all times in the operation of the business:
(1)
No operator or stockholder, member or partner, if the owner or lessee is other than an individual, or security employee or contractor of the operator of a dancehall shall have been convicted of any felony or crime of moral turpitude within a period of ten years prior to the date of application.
(2)
The building or structure in which the dancehall is located shall meet all applicable building, electrical, plumbing, sanitary, and fire codes, statutes, and regulations of both the city and the state.
(3)
No dancehall shall be located in any geographical location within the city zoned under any residential zoning classification.
(4)
A dancehall shall cease operations at 1:00 a.m. and require all guests or patrons to leave the premises prior to 2:00 a.m. with the exception of personnel required to clean up the premises. Notwithstanding the foregoing, on January 1 the dancehall need not be closed and vacated by customers, patrons or other persons until 2:00 a.m.
(5)
During all times of operation, the dancehall must have a minimum of one security person and at least two security personnel for each 100 persons present while a dance is in progress. A minimum of two security personnel shall be required for any dancehall event where alcoholic beverages are served or allowed. Security personnel must be at least 21 years of age with no criminal convictions other than moving traffic violations. Security personnel must wear clothing while on duty which clearly identifies them as security.
(Ord. No. 00-20, § 1(12-128), 11-20-2000)
Sec. 10-69. - Public safety commission review of application.
The clerk of the city shall within five days of the receipt of such completed application transmit the same to the public safety commission for purposes of determining whether the applicant meets the objective criteria of the mayor and council set forth in section 10-68. For purposes of such application, the applicant must propose to locate its business in a completed building or structure at the time the application is made. The public safety commission shall have the right to require that each applicant or stockholder, member or partner, if the applicant is other than an individual and security employee or security contractor of the proposed applicant be fingerprinted and consent to a criminal background investigation by city personnel.
(Ord. No. 00-20, § 1(12-129), 11-20-2000)
Sec. 10-70. - Findings of public safety commission.
The public safety commission shall consider any application for a dancehall within 60 days of the date of transmittal of the application by the city clerk to the public safety commission. The public safety commission shall report its findings to the city clerk. If the public safety commission shall report to the city clerk that the applicant meets all of the requirements of section 10-68 then the city clerk shall issue a business license to the applicant upon payment of the license fee. If the public safety commission shall find an applicant does not meet the requirements of section 10-68, then the clerk shall notify the applicant in writing as to the requirements which have not been met and the city clerk shall not issue a business license to the applicant.
(Ord. No. 00-20, § 1(12-130), 11-20-2000)
Sec. 10-71. - Compliance with public safety commission requirements prerequisite to issuance of license.
Any applicant who shall not have met the requirements of section 10-68 upon investigation provided in section 10-69, shall have a period of 60 days from the date of receipt of written notice from the city clerk as provided in section 10-70 to comply with all of the requirements of section 10-68 and to appear before the public safety commission and show that such requirements have been met. If the public safety commission shall concur with the applicant that such requirements have been met within the 60-day period allowed, the public safety commission shall report said finding to the city clerk who shall then issue a business license to the applicant upon the applicant's paying the required license fee. If the applicant shall be found by the public safety commission to have failed to meet all requirements of section 10-68 within the 60 days allowed, then the city clerk shall notify the applicant that a business license cannot be issued upon its application. Upon such notification the application shall be precluded from further consideration by the public safety commission for a period of one year from the date of such notification.
(Ord. No. 00-20, § 1(12-131), 11-20-2000)
Sec. 10-72. - Dancehalls operated exclusively for minors.
Any person desiring to operate a dancehall exclusively for minors, whether or not an admission fee is charged, must have in attendance at least one security person and two security personnel per 50 persons at all times that a dance is in progress. Security personnel must be at least 21 years of age with no criminal history other than moving traffic violations. Security personnel must wear clothing while on duty which clearly identifies them as security. A dancehall shall terminate operation on or before 11:00 p.m. and the participants therein shall be limited to minors between the ages of at least 14 years of age and under the age of 18. No alcoholic beverages shall be allowed on the premises where a dancehall for minors is operated.
(Ord. No. 00-20, § 1(12-132), 11-20-2000)
ARTICLE IV. - SPECIAL EVENTS

Sec. 10-101. - Definitions.
The following definitions shall apply to the interpretation and enforcement of this chapter:
DDDA. The Downtown Dalton Development Authority.
Downtown business district. The downtown business district shall have the following boundaries:
All that tract or parcel of land, situate, lying and being in the City of Dalton, Whitfield County, Georgia, more particularly described as follows:
Beginning at a point where the L & N Railroad (main track) intersects the northern right-of-way of Hawthorne Street; then westerly along the northern right-of-way of Hawthorne Street to its intersection with the eastern right-of-way of Selvidge Street; then southerly along the eastern right-of-way of Selvidge Street to its intersection with the northern right-of-way of Waugh Street; then westerly along the northern right-of-way of Waugh Street to its intersection with the western right-of-way of Thornton Avenue; then southerly along the western right-of-way of Thornton Avenue to its intersection with the southern right-of-way of Morris Street; then easterly along the southern right-of-way of Morris Street to the point where the southern right-of-way of Morris Street intersects the L & N Railroad (main track); then northerly along the L & N Railroad (main track) to the point of beginning.
Producer. Any person, group of persons, organization, association, club, or other entity responsible for planning, producing and conducting an event.
Special event. The term "special event" or "event" shall mean any organized activity having as its purpose entertainment, recreation and/or education, such as a festival or celebration, foot or vehicle race, parade or march, rally or assembly which takes place on a public street, sidewalk or right-of-way, or occurs on private property and impacts government services on public rights-of-way.
Vendor. Any person or persons who engage in the sale to the public of any food or food products, goods, services, or merchandise of whatever nature from any location, either mobile or stationary, on a temporary itinerant basis on any public street, sidewalk, or right-of-way. The definition of vendor, as used in this chapter, shall not be construed to include or prohibit a permanent business located within the downtown business district from displaying goods or merchandise on a public sidewalk immediately in front of and adjacent to the permanent business structure so long as such displays do not impede the normal flow of pedestrian traffic and so long as other laws and ordinances pertaining to such business or displays are complied with. Only persons who hold an alcoholic beverage license from the city to sell, serve, or purvey at retail alcoholic beverages whether beer, wine, or distilled spirits, by the drink which license is active and in good standing shall qualify as a "vendor" for special events as defined this this article.
(Ord. No. 03-09, § 1, 5-5-2003; Ord. No. 22-14, § 1, 5-2-2022)
Sec. 10-102. - Permits.
(a)
Required. It shall be unlawful for any person, group, organization, association, club or other entity to conduct or cause to be conducted any special event in downtown business district without having obtained a valid permit for such event.
(b)
Geographic limitation; responsibility for police and sanitation service. A special event shall be limited to the downtown business district within which the event is to take place and the producer of a special event shall be responsible for the provision of police and sanitation services within the boundaries of the event for a period commencing 30 minutes prior to the scheduled start of the special event and concluding when the area is cleared of all activities and structures related to such event.
(c)
Time limitation. Except for clearing of the area, no special event shall be allowed to continue beyond the time approved on the permit for the event.
(d)
Delineation of boundaries; preservation of areas for circulation. The outermost boundaries of the special event shall be fully and clearly delineated on a map, which shall be no smaller than eight and one-half by 11 inches in size, and attached to the application for a permit. All public streets and/or sidewalks within and adjacent to such area(s) shall be clearly identified. The DDDA shall require, as part of the approval for the event, that areas no less than five feet in width shall be designated and preserved for public circulation and access to adjoining business establishments outside the area(s) delineated for the event.
(e)
Maps for walks or runs. The producer of a special event such as a walk or run, as to which the provisions of subsection (d) above would not be practicable, shall submit with the application for a permit a map, which shall be no smaller than eight and one-half by 11 inches in size, showing the exact route of the course for the walk or run.
(Ord. No. 03-09, § 1, 5-5-2003)
Sec. 10-103. - Application; fee.
(a)
A producer of a special event shall make application for a permit for such event at the office of the DDDA on a form prescribed by the city. Application forms may be obtained from the office of the DDDA.
(b)
An application for a special event should be filed at least 60 days prior to the date the event is scheduled to occur. However, no application shall be accepted earlier than one year prior to the date of the event.
(c)
A nonrefundable application fee of $25.00 shall be paid at the time the application for a permit is filed. Such fee shall be payable to the DDDA by cash, money order, or cashier's check. Provided, however, no producer with a principal place of business located within the downtown business district shall be required to pay the application fee.
(Ord. No. 03-09, § 1, 5-5-2003)
Sec. 10-104. - Identification of producer.
The producer of an event, if a group or organization, shall be fully identified on the application. However, a special event permit shall be issued only to an individual person, who may be the actual producer or a designated agent of the producer, and such individual person shall be jointly and severally responsible with the producer for compliance with all provisions, including all financial requirements, of this chapter and other applicable laws.
(Ord. No. 03-09, § 1, 5-5-2003)
Sec. 10-105. - Administrative review; waiver.
(a)
The DDDA shall cause the application to be circulated to the city administrator's office, the police department, the fire department, the public works department, the building inspector's office and to each government department and/or other agency whose services the DDDA determines would be affected by the nature and activities of the proposed event. Each such department or agency shall review the application and note the services which it will be required to perform, the number of personnel to perform such services, the length of time to perform such services, the cost to perform such services, and any other information which will assist the DDDA in facilitating the event and services required.
(b)
The city administrator shall approve, approve with conditions or deny any such application no later than 30 days after the date of his receipt of the application unless the applicant requests additional time to provide further information to the DDDA. The failure of the city administrator to act on the application as provided herein as of said 30-day period or the agreed extended date shall be deemed an approval of the application.
(c)
Decisions of the city administrator that adversely affect or aggrieve any applicant may be appealed to the mayor and council. Any such appeal shall be by written petition filed in the office of the city clerk within seven days after the final decision by the city administrator.
(d)
The mayor and council shall perform a review of the application no later than 30 days after the date the written petition is received by the city clerk. No additional information may be submitted on the issue unless it specifically requested by the mayor and council. The mayor and council may approve, approve with conditions or deny the application. The failure of the mayor and council to act on the application as provided herein as of said 30-day period shall be deemed an approval of the application and its decision shall be final.
(e)
If the applicant is a qualified nonprofit or governmental entity, the city administrator is authorized to waive any special condition or other requirement of this article that will not affect the public health, safety, or welfare.
(Ord. No. 03-09, § 1, 5-5-2003; Ord. No. 11-01, § 1, 2-21-2011)
Sec. 10-106. - Special conditions on permit.
Each department of the government reviewing an application may recommend in writing certain conditions or restrictions as deemed necessary to facilitate the event, to comply with other laws or regulations, and/or to ensure the safety, health and welfare of the community. Unless expressly waived under section 10-105, the recommendations of the departments shall be imposed as a part of the permit and such special conditions so imposed shall be construed to have the full force and effect of law as a provision of this chapter. A violation of such conditions or restrictions shall be deemed a violation of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 03-09, § 1, 5-5-2003)
Sec. 10-107. - Final approval of permit; minimum review period; appeal.
(a)
If the application has not been denied by the city administrator, the DDDA is authorized to approve and issue a permit for a special event after considering all comments and conditions of the reviewing departments and agencies as well as other information pertaining to the proposed event as described on the application, and after the payment of all applicable fees and costs.
(b)
A minimum of 30 days shall be allowed for the administrative review of an application and the issuance of a permit.
(c)
The DDDA shall either issue or deny the permit no later than 30 days following the date the completed application is filed. Failure of the DDDA to act within the time provided herein shall be deemed a denial of the permit. If the permit is denied, the producer shall be notified in writing of the denial and the reason(s) for the denial.
(d)
The denial of a permit may be appealed to the mayor and council. Any such appeal must be filed with the city administrator within 15 days of the date the application was denied. The mayor and council may approve the application, with or without conditions, or deny the application.
(Ord. No. 03-09, § 1, 5-5-2003)
Sec. 10-108. - Emergency issuance of permit.
In extraordinary circumstances, the city administrator or his designee shall be authorized to waive or otherwise expedite any or all of the review process and to issue a special event permit upon payment by the producer of all applicable fees and costs for such event.
(Ord. No. 03-09, § 1, 5-5-2003)
Sec. 10-109. - Permit fees.
(a)
The fee for a permit to conduct a special event shall be based on all services to be provided by the government for such event and shall be equal to the estimated actual cost to the government to provide such services unless expressly waived by the city administrator. The initial permit fee shall be the aggregate of the estimated costs of such services calculated by each government department. If, at the conclusion of the event, the cost of government services is greater than the initial payment, the producer of the event shall be billed for the difference. Failure to pay the outstanding amount within 30 days of the billing date shall be a violation of this chapter.
(b)
The initial permit fee must be paid in full prior to the issuance of a permit and no later than 72 hours prior to the date of the event.
(c)
The fees required in this section shall be in addition to any other fees which may be required by any other ordinances or regulations that might be applicable.
(d)
No producer of any special event, except as otherwise provided for, shall be exempt from the payment of the appropriate fees and charges required under this chapter.
(Ord. No. 03-09, § 1, 5-5-2003)
Sec. 10-110. - Liability.
The producer of any special event and its designated agent, if applicable, shall hold harmless the city, the DDDA, their respective officers, employees and agents from liability of damages arising from any acts or omissions emanating from a special event. The producer shall, as a condition precedent to obtaining a permit, obtain such releases and waivers as may be required by the city administrator.
(Ord. No. 03-09, § 1, 5-5-2003)
Sec. 10-111. - Vendors.
(a)
Sales permitted. The sale of food, including the sale of beer and/or wine as provided herein, or any merchandise or services of any type by a vendor shall be allowed as a component of a special event provided such vendor is approved and authorized in writing by the producer of the event and has paid all required taxes and fees for applicable permits or licenses. All sales shall be conducted in accordance with such conditions and limitations as shall be imposed in writing by the producer and submitted as part of the application for a permit.
(b)
Authorization of vendors. The producer of a special event shall have sole responsibility and authority to allow or disallow sidewalk or street vending as a component of an event and to designate the location and activities of such vendors. Except as otherwise provided, it shall be unlawful for any vendor to engage in such business at any location or in any manner not authorized by the producer of the event.
(c)
Identification required. Any vendor authorized by the event producer shall be required to prominently display on his or her person a badge identifying the vendor as an authorized participant in the event. Such identification shall be not less than three inches by three inches, shall state that the bearer is an official participant in the event, and shall bear the signature of the producer of the event.
(d)
Permit not required. Vendors authorized by the producer of an event as a component of the event shall not be required to obtain any separate vendor permit to operate during the period of the event.
(e)
Unauthorized vending prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any vendor not authorized by the producer of a special event as provided under this section to engage in such business within a distance of 25 yards of such event from one hour before until one hour after the event. This prohibition shall not apply to merchants located within the downtown business district; however, such merchants shall display and sell merchandise on the street or sidewalk within the boundaries of the special event during the special event time period in compliance with all other laws and ordinances pertaining to such business or displays.
(f)
Food sales. The vendor of any food, whether hot or cold, as a part of a special event shall be subject to all rules and regulations of the county health department. It shall be the responsibility of the producer of an event to assure compliance with this section by such vendors.
(Ord. No. 03-09, § 1, 5-5-2003)
Sec. 10-112. - Sale of beer and/or wine.
Subject to written approval by the city administrator or his designee beer and/or wine may be dispensed and consumed at a special event subject to the following conditions:
(a)
Definitions.
Festival. A special event sponsored by a nonprofit civic organization as defined in section 6-111, where the majority of the net proceeds of the special event benefit a charitable cause or the promotion of the city.
Festival area. The area within which beer and/or wine may be dispensed and consumed during a festival.
(1)
Primary festival area. The boundaries of the primary festival area are as follows: Beginning at the point where the sidewalk on the eastern right-of-way of Selvidge Street abuts the northern right-of-way of Crawford Street, then extending north along the sidewalk on the eastern right-of-way of Selvidge Street to its intersection with the sidewalk on the southern right-of-way of King Street, then east along the sidewalk on the southern right-of-way of King Street to the eastern right-of-way of Pentz Street, then north along the eastern right-of-way of Pentz Street to the sidewalk on the northern right-of-way of King Street, then west along the northern right-of-way of King Street to its intersection with the sidewalk on the eastern right-of-way of Selvidge Street, then north along the sidewalk on the eastern right-of-way of Selvidge Street to the entrance to the BB&T parking lot, then west across Selvidge Street to the sidewalk on the western right-of-way of Selvidge Street, then south along the sidewalk on the western right-of-way of Selvidge Street to the northern right-of-way of Crawford Street, the east across Selvidge Street to the point of beginning.
(2)
Alternative festival area. Any area within the C-3 commercial district that is approved by the city administrator, the chief of police and the director of public works.
Special outdoor area. The area, within a special event, that is not a festival where beer and/or wine may be dispensed and consumed.
(b)
Conditions applicable to festivals only.
(1)
The producer of a festival shall supply wristbands for purchase by attendees that wish to consume beer and/or wine within the festival area. The wristbands shall be of a type that cannot be removed without tearing. The wristbands must be attached at the point of purchase by representatives of the producer.
(2)
No wristband shall be sold or given away to an attendee without first determining that the attendee is at least 21 years of age.
(3)
No beer or wine shall be dispensed to an attendee who is not wearing the proper wristband for the event.
(c)
Conditions applicable to special outdoor areas only.
(1)
The producer of a special event that is not a festival may apply for one special outdoor area within the delineated boundaries of the event.
(2)
Reserved.
(3)
The special outdoor area shall be placed at a location and of a size approved by the city administrator or his designee.
(4)
The application for a special event where a special outdoor area is requested shall include, in addition to a map identifying the outermost boundaries of the event, a map no smaller than 8 ½ x 11 inches in size, clearly identifying the area, including all public streets and sidewalks within and adjacent to the area, where the special outdoor area is proposed. The application shall include the proposed size for the special outdoor area. The application also shall contain a description of the methods and structures that will be used to secure and separate such area from other public areas as required in subsection (2) above.
(d)
Conditions applicable to festivals and special outdoor areas.
(1)
The dispensing of beer and/or wine at a special outdoor area shall be restricted to the holder of a valid alcoholic beverage license to sell retail beer and/or wine by the drink. The name of such licensee shall be required as part of the application for a special event permit.
(2)
The producer of the special event shall provide, at the producer's expense, at such security personnel as required by the chief of police, or his designee.
(3)
The festival area or the special outdoor area shall be conspicuously posted at all times at each point of entrance/exit with signs stating the following:
"The possession of alcoholic beverages beyond this point is prohibited."
The letters of such signs shall be not less than three inches in height and one-half inch in width and shall be in black letters on a contrasting light background.
(4)
It shall be unlawful for patrons of the outdoor area of a special event in which beer and/or wine is permitted to bring into or take outside the designated area any alcoholic beverage or to furnish any alcoholic beverage to any person outside the designed area where beer and/or wine is permitted.
(5)
The fee for a permit to dispense and/or consume beer and/or wine at a special event shall be $300.00.
(6)
Notwithstanding the provisions of this section, any person dispensing and/or consuming beer and/or wine in accordance with this section shall comply with all other laws and ordinances pertaining to the sale, possession and consumption of alcoholic beverages.
(Ord. No. 04-14, § 1, 4-5-2004; Ord. No. 09-10, § 1, 4-20-2009; Ord. No. 13-07, § 1, 8-19-2013; Ord. No. 14-04, §§ 1, 2, 8-4-2014; Ord. No. 22-14, § 1, 5-2-2022)

ARTICLE V. - SIDEWALK CAFES

Sec. 10-130. - Definition.
Sidewalk cafe means an area of food or beverage service located outside, but connected with, a part of, and adjacent to, an indoor restaurant or cafe located in the downtown Dalton area.
(Ord. No. 11-07, § 1, 7-18-2011)
Sec. 10-131. - Conditions for sidewalk cafes.
(a)
Sidewalk cafes shall be so located and maintained that the following portion of the contiguous sidewalk shall remain unused and unobstructed:
(1)
On Hamilton Street, where the streetscape has been completed, at least a ten-foot portion of the sidewalk measured from the face of the Hamilton Street curb perpendicularly toward the property line of the adjacent indoor restaurant.
(2)
On all other streets within the downtown district, where streetscape has been completed, at least an eight-foot wide portion of the sidewalk measured from the face of the curb of the street perpendicularly toward the property line of the adjacent indoor restaurant.
(3)
A minimum of four feet shall be provided at all times between the sidewalk cafe area and the curb or nearest obstacle on all streets to allow for continuous unobstructed pedestrian traffic on the sidewalk.
(4)
Permanent plantings, city owned waste receptacles, streetlight poles, and other permanent fixtures along the sidewalk must be considered when determining the boundaries set forth in a permit.
(b)
Any special construction proposed to encroach upon or occupy any of the public right-of-way shall be permitted only by prior approval of such plans by the city building inspector.
(c)
The sidewalk cafe operator shall at all times ensure that:
(1)
No encroachment on public rights-of-way, other than as stated in this section, shall be allowed to exist.
(2)
All general provisions and clean community regulations of the refuse collection and disposal provisions are complied with.
(Ord. No. 11-07, § 1, 7-18-2011)
Sec. 10-132. - Regulations.
Except as otherwise provided, a sidewalk cafe shall comply with the following:
(a)
Limitations on area. The area in which a sidewalk cafe is operated shall abut the outside front wall of the restaurant of which it is an extension and shall not extend in either direction beyond the outside front wall of the restaurant. Use of any storefront and/or sidewalk areas in front of premises immediately next door shall be permitted only to the extent permitted by a written agreement between the owners of such adjacent premises. Adjacent property owners may not charge a fee or receive compensation for the use of the sidewalk areas in front of their property.
(b)
Divider required. The area permitted for a sidewalk cafe shall be separated by a divider in compliance with section 6-10 of the Code which will serve to contain crowds and maintain the boundaries of the cafe. The system shall be designed and constructed such that it resists movement and can be disassembled and removed if necessary. Such divider shall be not less than three feet and no more than four feet in height. All tables and chairs shall be located totally within the limits of the divider. It shall be the responsibility of the sidewalk cafe operator to maintain the divider in its exact/approved location at all times.
(c)
Sanitation. It shall be the responsibility and duty of the sidewalk cafe operator to maintain the area in a clean, neat, and orderly manner at all times. It shall also be the responsibility of the sidewalk cafe operator to properly clean the area as specified by the city public works department. All tables and chairs are to be kept clean, sanitary, safe, and in structurally sound condition at all times.
(d)
Removal of furnishings and suspension of operation. All tables, chairs, and dividers of a sidewalk cafe shall be removed from the public sidewalk area from time to time and outside operations suspended as required by the city for sidewalk upkeep or for such other purposes as the city may determine, in its sole discretion for such purposes as, but not limited to:
(1)
Any permitted special event;
(2)
Any street, sidewalk or utility construction;
(3)
Any emergency situations; or
(4)
The protection of the health, safety and welfare of the public.
(e)
Furnishings. Furnishings shall not be secured to lamp posts, streetlights, trees or any other public street fixtures. Furnishings may not be stacked or stored outside on the public right-of-way at any time.
(1)
Fences/barriers. A physical barrier in compliance with [this] Code shall serve to maintain the boundaries of the sidewalk cafe. Flower boxes or planters may be used to define the corner boundaries of the area.
(2)
Menu boards and signs. Menu boards, both portable and on walls, shall be subject to sign permit approval and shall comply with [this] Code.
(3)
Umbrellas. Each table may be equipped with an umbrella that, when open, shall extend to at least the same diameter as the table it serves and shall be anchored with a weighted base. Any umbrella protruding into public space shall be positioned at a minimum height of seven feet. Each umbrella shall be maintained in good, clean, and operable condition.
(4)
Tables and chairs. Materials—Tables and chairs shall match and be made of safe, sturdy and durable material, such as wood, steel, or wrought iron. All furniture shall be commercial grade and manufactured for outdoor commercial use. Vinyl tablecloths are not recommended. Small round or square tables shall seat no more than four people. Use of larger tables may be granted provided that all clearance requirements are met. Round tables shall be no larger than three feet diameter. Square tables shall be no larger than three feet wide and rectangular tables no more than three × four feet.
(f)
Landscaping. Flower boxes or planters may be used to define the corner boundaries of the seating area. The combined height of the planters and live plants shall not exceed four feet from sidewalk grade and no wider than two feet at the base. The planting areas shall be planted with seasonal blooming or ornamental evergreen live plants year round. The permit holder shall maintain flower boxes and planting areas. The flower boxes, planters and trash receptacles shall be portable and made of safe, durable and attractive materials such as wood, steel or colored concrete.
(g)
Hours of operation. A sidewalk cafe may be open for breakfast, lunch and/or dinner and any combination thereof, consistent with the business's normal hours of operation.
(h)
Guidelines. The city shall adopt standards and guidelines for consideration without amendment to this article.
(Ord. No. 11-07, § 1, 7-18-2011)
Sec. 10-133. - Liability and insurance.
Sidewalk cafe operators shall indemnify and hold harmless the city and its officials and employees from claims and liability arising out of the use of a sidewalk cafe. The sidewalk cafe operator shall obtain insurance covering the use of a sidewalk cafe and name the city as an additional insured with respect to such insurance. The city shall prepare guidelines with respect to any indemnification and insurance requirements of sidewalk cafe operators.
(Ord. No. 11-07, § 1, 7-18-2011)
Sec. 10-134. - Americans with Disabilities Act.
All sidewalk cafes shall fully comply with all requirements of the Americans with Disabilities Act as currently existing or as may be hereafter amended.
(Ord. No. 11-07, § 1, 7-18-2011)
