Sec. 1-15-1. - Advisory board established.
There is hereby established the Keep Athens-Clarke County Beautiful Advisory Board.
(Ord. of 12-1-92, § 1; Ord. of 4-7-98, § 1; Ord. of 9-5-2000, § 1)
Sec. 1-15-2. - Powers of the advisory board.
The powers of such advisory board shall be to adopt bylaws to facilitate the purposes of the organization; to initiate, plan and direct community-wide efforts to achieve organizational goals; to solicit and accept donations and appropriations of money, services, products, property, and facilities for use by the advisory board for the accomplishment of its objectives; and to make recommendations to the governing body as well as the private sector regarding measures which it deems necessary to accomplish its objectives.
(Ord. of 12-1-92, § 1; Ord. of 9-5-2000, § 2)
Sec. 1-15-3. - Duties.
The duties of such advisory board shall be to promote public interest in the general improvement of the environment throughout the Athens-Clarke County Community; to initiate, plan, direct and coordinate programs for litter control for the community on a sustainable basis; to implement and maintain the Keep America Beautiful Program; to study, investigate and develop plans for improving the health, sanitation, safety and cleanliness of Athens-Clarke County by beautifying the streets, highways, alleys, river and stream banks, lots, yards, and other similar places in Athens-Clarke County; to aid in the prevention of fires, diseases and other casualties by the removal and elimination of trash and other debris from the streets, highways, alley, lots, yards, plots and other similar places; to encourage the placing, planting, and/or preservation of trees, flowers, plants, shrubbery and other objects or ornamentation in Athens-Clarke County; and to advise and recommend plans to other agencies of Athens-Clarke County for the beautification of the entire community and otherwise to promote public interest in the general improvement of the environment of Athens-Clarke County; provided, however, that nothing herein shall be construed to abridge or change the powers and duties of other departments, boards and like agencies of Athens-Clarke County.
(Ord. of 12-1-92, § 1; Ord. of 9-5-2000, § 2)
Sec. 1-15-4. - Membership.
(a)
The advisory board shall consist of no more than 20 and not less than eight voting members. Six of these voting members shall serve by virtue of their office as set forth in paragraph (b) below. The remaining voting members shall be appointed by majority vote of the Mayor and Commission of Athens-Clarke County. Such members shall be residents of Athens-Clarke County. Appointed members of the advisory board should be appointed with precedence given to broad area representation of the community.
(b)
Those persons holding the following positions, or their designees, shall serve as voting members of the advisory board by virtue of their office:
(1)
Public works director, Athens-Clarke County.
(2)
Superintendent, Clarke County School District.
(3)
Executive director, Athens Housing Authority.
(4)
Executive director, Athens Area Chamber of Commerce.
(5)
Director, State Botanical Gardens of Georgia.
(6)
Community Relations Director, University of Georgia.
(c)
In addition to those members provided for under paragraphs (a) and (b), the following persons, or their designees, shall serve as ex officio nonvoting members of the commission.
(1)
Mayor, Athens-Clarke County.
(2)
Solid waste Director, Athens-Clarke County.
(3)
Chairman, Athens-Clarke County Community Tree Council.
(d)
Recommendations for membership shall be submitted to the mayor and commission by a nominating committee selected from the members of the advisory board by the chairman of such advisory board.
(Ord. of 12-1-92, § 1; Ord. of 7-2-96, § 1; Ord. of 4-7-98, § 1; Ord. of 7-6-99, § 1; Ord. of 9-5-2000, §§ 2—5; Ord. of 2-1-2005, §§ 1, 2; Ord. of 7-3-07, § 1; Ord. of 7-3-2007, §§ 1, 2)
Sec. 1-15-5. - Terms of service of members.
(a)
The terms of service of the appointed members of the advisory board shall be for three years each with the Mayor and Commission of Athens-Clarke County appointing three to six members each year.
(b)
A member who is absent for more than three meetings a year may have his or her board membership revoked by a majority vote of the board.
(c)
Board members, who are appointed by the Unified Government, shall be eligible to serve for no more than two consecutive three-year terms. Second terms of service must be approved by the Board at the June meeting each year. At least one year must elapse after the second term before eligibility to serve is restored. Members whose terms have expired shall continue as members until their successors have been appointed. A member who is rotating off of the Board is encouraged to find his/her replacement. Board members whose terms of service have expired may continue to serve on board committees.
(d)
Designees of Board members who serve by virtue of their office shall be eligible to serve for no more than two consecutive three-year terms. Second terms of service must be approved by the Board at the June meeting each year. The Nominating committee will seek Designee recommendations from Board members who serve by virtue of their office. At least one year must elapse after the second term before eligibility to serve is restored.
(Ord. of 12-1-92, § 1; Ord. of 7-2-96, § 1; Ord. of 7-6-99, § 1; Ord. of 9-5-2000, §§ 2, 6; Ord. of 2-1-2005, § 3; Ord. of 7-3-2007, § 3)
Sec. 1-15-6. - Election of officers.
(a)
The advisory board shall elect from its membership a chairman, vice-chairman, secretary and treasurer and such other officers as may be desired as set forth below. Officers shall serve for a term of one year beginning on July 1 and ending June 30 of the following year. Members of the advisory board may be re-elected for succeeding terms.
(b)
The nominating committee shall nominate members of the advisory board for all executive positions to be voted on by the full advisory board membership.
(c)
Officer elections will be held at the June Board meeting. Officers may serve a maximum of two consecutive terms in the same office. At least one year must lapse before eligibility for that office is restored.
(d)
In the event an office becomes vacant between annual elections, the Chairperson shall appoint an acting replacement until the Board, at its next meeting, elects a replacement from the Board for the remainder of the term.
(Ord. of 12-1-92, § 1; Ord. of 9-5-2000, § 2; Ord. of 7-3-2007, § 4)
Sec. 1-15-7. - Executive committee.
(a)
The chairman, vice-chairman, secretary, treasurer, and one voting member of the advisory board shall serve on the executive committee.
(b)
Duties of the executive committee shall be to make decisions on behalf of the advisory board between regular meetings.
(Ord. of 12-1-92, § 1; Ord. of 4-7-98, § 1; Ord. of 9-5-2000, §§ 2, 7; Ord. of 2-1-2005, § 4)
Sec. 1-15-8. - Advisory board chair.
The chair of the Keep Athens-Clarke County Beautiful Advisory Board shall appoint committees to accomplish any work programs.
(Ord. of 12-1-92, § 1; Ord. of 4-7-98, § 1; Ord. of 9-5-2000, §§ 2, 8; Ord. of 2-1-2005, § 5)
Secs. 1-15-9, 1-15-10. - Reserved.
Editor's note— Sections 9 and 10 of an ordinance enacted Sept. 5, 2000, repealed §§ 1-15-9, 1-15-10 of the Code in their entirety. Former §§ 1-15-9, 1-15-10 pertained to the executive director and management of funds, respectively, and derived from an ordinance of Dec. 1, 1992, § 1; an ordinance of July 2, 1992, § 1; and an ordinance of July 6, 1999.
Sec. 1-15-11. - Compensation.
All members of the advisory board shall serve without compensation.
(Ord. of 12-1-92, § 1; Ord. of 9-5-2000, § 2)
