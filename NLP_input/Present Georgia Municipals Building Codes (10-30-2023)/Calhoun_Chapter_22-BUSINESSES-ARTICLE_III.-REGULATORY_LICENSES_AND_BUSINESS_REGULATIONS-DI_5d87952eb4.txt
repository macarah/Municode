Sec. 22-311. - Purpose.
To promote the public health, safety and welfare, to protect and preserve the scenic and natural beauty of the area; to preserve property values and protect the integrity of the community; and to protect the public from health nuisances and safety hazards.

Sec. 22-312. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Abandoned motor vehicle. A motor vehicle that is either physically inoperable, or missing essential parts to be operable, and has been stored in the same geographic location for no less than 60 days, or lacks a current license plate and is not stored within a completely enclosed structure or is not currently for sale and stored at a facility licensed for such sales.
Automobile junk yard. A facility where more than five abandoned motor vehicles are located or stored for a period in excess of 60 consecutive days.
Construction and demolition debris. A facility that processes material for recycling that is generated during the construction, remodeling, repair, or demolition of buildings, bridges, pavements, and other structures. Construction and demolition debris includes concrete, asphalt, lumber, steel girders, steel rods, wiring, drywall, carpets, window glass, metal and plastic piping, tree stumps, soil, and other miscellaneous items related to the activities listed above. This category also includes natural disaster debris.
End-user. Person, business enterprise or organization that actually use a product or material following purchase or sale for intended use or consumption.
Expansion. Any increase in the size or scope of a recycling processing center and includes, but is not limited to, an increase in the total amount of space devoted to use by the recycling preceding center, the placement and/or used of additional machinery or an addition of services offered onsite.
Glass beneficiation plant. A glass processing facility where recovered glass cullet is cleaned of contaminants and processed into a form that is ready to be manufactured into a new product (mill-ready).
Junk. Any motor vehicle, appliance, scrap material or other item that is in a condition which prevents its use for the purpose for which it was originally manufactured.
Junkyard. Any lot, land, parcel, building or structure, or part thereof, used for the storage, collection, processing, purchase, sale, salvage or disposal of junk.
Paper processor. An intermediate operating facility where recovered paper products and materials are sorted, cleaned of contaminants, and prepared for final recycling. Examples include paper stock dealers and paper packers.
Post-consumer materials. This category includes materials that would otherwise be destined for solid waste disposal, having completed its intended end use and product life cycle. Post-consumer material does not include materials and byproducts generated from, and commonly reused within an original manufacturing and fabrication process. Post-consumer materials are generally such waste materials generated by households or by commercial, industrial and institutional facilities in their role as end-users of a product.
Post-industrial materials. This category of materials involves waste materials generated as surplus, excess or scrap substances that are byproducts generated from, and commonly reused with an original manufacturing and fabrication process. This category of materials also includes glass beneficiation, the processing of construction and demolition debris, the processing of abandoned automobiles and paper processors. These materials typically require the use of heavy powered equipment to crush, break, shred or compact materials or chemicals to strip or clean.
Public road. Any road or right of way owned, maintained or funded by the federal, state or local government.
Recycling processing center. An individual, business entity or financial enterprise operating a physical facility and location that process recyclable materials from collectors and generators for the purpose of preparing material for recycling, including construction and demolition debris recyclers, material recovery facilities, scrap metal yards, paper processors, and glass beneficiation plants. Processing techniques include baling, compacting, flattening, grinding, crushing, and shredding. For all purposes of this division, there are three subcategories of recycling processing center:
(1)
Commercial recycling collection center. A non-governmental facility for the collection of recyclable materials for reuse, including aluminum cans, paper, glass, plastic, etc. These are privately owned where only post-consumer materials recyclables are merely purchased and collected on site for transport elsewhere off site for further processing or consumption.
(2)
Scrap metal processor. An intermediate operating facility where recovered metal is sorted, cleaned of contaminants and subject to minimal processing that is ready to be recycled. These are commercial enterprises or businesses that purchase to collect post-consumer materials and post-industrial materials for processing as a recyclable elsewhere than on site. Examples include scrap metal yards and scrap metal dealers. This use shall not include vehicle storage, vehicle dismantling or any other process that requires dismantling and item as part of the processing for re-sale or transport.
(3)
Industrial recycling processing center. A facility that process recyclable materials from collectors and generators for the purpose of preparing material for recycling, including construction and demolition debris recyclers, material recovery facilities, scrap metal yards, paper processors, junkyards, automobile junkyards, and glass beneficiation plants. Processing techniques include baling, compacting, flattening, grinding, crushing, shredding, cutting, melting, composting or other form of processing to create a "raw" base product for reuse.
Recyclable. Materials generated by a person, household or business which can be separated from general waste and returned to commerce to be reused as a resource in the development of useful products. Recyclables, include at a minimum, the following: newsprint, aluminum cans, bimetallic cans, clear and/or colored glass, plastic beverage containers, high grade office paper, corrugated paper and paperboard, leaf waste and such other materials as may be designated or deleted from time to time by resolution of the mayor and council.
Screen. A method of visually shielding or buffering one abutting or nearby structure or use from another by fencing, walls, berms, or densely planted vegetation.
Setback. The shortest straight line distance between a street right-of-way or lot line and the nearest point of a structure or building or projection therefrom.
Substantial modification. A material change or alteration to the site or operations of the recycling processing center. A substantial modification includes, but is not limited to, a change in the layout of structures, machinery and junk on the site; a change in the volume, intensity or frequency of use of the property and/or a change in the operations taking place on the site.
(Ord. No. 944, § 1, 10-26-2015)
Sec. 22-313. - Applicability.
This division applies to all recycling processing centers created, expanded or substantially modified after the date this division is enacted. Any such business operating a recycling processing center as of the date ordinance enactment shall be grandfathered as prior nonconforming uses so long as pre-existing recycling processing centers register with the city clerk within 90 days of enactment of this division.
(Ord. No. 944, § 1, 10-26-2015)
Sec. 22-314. - Required permit.
(a)
A permit shall be required to create, expand or substantially modify any recycling processing center. No person, business entity or other commercial enterprise shall establish, operate or maintain a junkyard without first obtaining a permit.
(b)
A permit shall be required for any expansions or substantial modification of new and existing recycling processing centers.
(c)
No permit is required for recycling processing centers existing at the time of enactment of this division, however existing recycling processing centers are required to register with the city clerk within 90 days of enactment of this division.
(Ord. No. 944, § 1, 10-26-2015)
Sec. 22-315. - Permit application.
(a)
A permit application for the creation, expansion or modification of a recycling processing center shall be made to the city clerk.
(b)
A plan detailing the proposed new recycling processing center, expansion, or substantial modification shall be included with each permit application, and shall include the following:
(1)
Setbacks;
(2)
Location of public rights-of-way;
(3)
All structures within 300 feet of the recycling processing center;
(4)
Driveways;
(5)
Entrances;
(6)
Current and proposed structures;
(7)
Screening;
(8)
Buffers;
(9)
Gross acreage of the site:
(10)
The types of materials that shall be collected and means of storage for said materials;
(11)
What category of recycling processing center is to be operated and all equipment that will be employed, or is expected to be employed for any form of processing required;
(12)
Any and all permits required by all state and/or federal environmental protections agency or department (EPA or EPD) for the particular venture or enterprise and materials handled;
(13)
Owner's name and address; and
(14)
The preparer of the plan's name and address.
The city clerk may require additional information regarding the plans depending upon site specific concerns such as topography or neighboring properties. The plan shall be additionally submitted to the office of the building inspector prior to approval to ensure review of all information provided and applicable statutes, regulations or ordinances.
(c)
All permit applications shall include payment in full for the permit fee as designated in sections 22-317(h), 22-318(l) and 22-319(l).
(d)
When a recycling processing center is sold, or ownership interest is otherwise transferred to someone other than the original applicant, the new owner shall notify the city clerk within 30 days of any such change in ownership and provide the new owner's name, address and other contact information.
(e)
The permit shall then be renewed annually for continued operation of the recycling processing center with the renewal fee to be paid no later than December 31st of the current calendar year.
(Ord. No. 944, § 1, 10-26-2015)
Sec. 22-316. - Existing recycling processing centers, scrap yards, scrap metal dealers, and commercial recycling operations handling postconsumer materials and junkyards.
(a)
Existing commercial enterprises or operations, other than municipal, county or state facilities, involved in the collection, processing, purchase or sale of post consumer materials or other recyclables at the time of the enactment of this division shall be grandfathered as prior nonconforming uses so long as they have registered within 90 days of enactment of this division. From thence forward, regardless of the prior description used to name or describe the business or operation, they shall hereinafter be referred to as recycling processing centers and categorized by size and materials as set forth in this Code. There shall be no fee to register and existing enterprise or operation. To register, the owner or operator must provide the following:
(1)
Name and address of the owner and operator;
(2)
Date the business or enterprise was created or founded;
(3)
Location and/or address of the business or enterprise;
(4)
The size of the operational facility, or location for the business or enterprise;
(5)
A detailed description or list of operations currently taking place on the site.
(b)
Should a pre-existing recycling processing center undergo an expansion or substantial modification it shall be subject to the setback and screening requirements provided in this Code.
(Ord. No. 944, § 1, 10-26-2015)
Sec. 22-317. - Regulations for commercial recycling collection centers.
(a)
Commercial recycling collection centers shall be a permissible use in all areas zoned by the City of Calhoun as C-2 General—Business District or Ind-G—General Industrial District. In addition to being subject to all applicable provisions found in Section 7.9 or Section 7.11 of Appendix A of this Code. Commercial recycling collection centers shall be conducted either in an enclosed structure or container or in an area enclosed on all sides by a screening wall or solid fence.
(b)
The minimum lot area for commercial recycling collection centers shall be two acres.
(c)
Commercial recycling collection centers shall have a minimum 50-foot wide buffer along all abutting nonresidential property lines and a 100-foot wide buffer abutting residential property lines to provide a visual screen in accordance with Section 6.5 of the Zoning Ordinance of the City of Calhoun, Georgia, Appendix A of this Code.
(d)
Commercial recycling collection centers shall be surrounded by an opaque wood, plastic or chainlink screening fence not less than six feet in height. In no event shall materials be stacked or stored to exceed the height of the screening fence and shall not be subject to the vegetation screen requirement. There shall be a minimum setback from the front property line to the fence of 20 feet. There shall be a minimum setback from the side and rear property lines to the fence of 15 feet.
(e)
Commercial recycling collection centers shall utilize off-site and on-site management techniques used to protect against litter and debris. At no time shall said materials, litter or waste be permitted in any public road.
(f)
Commercial recycling collections centers shall be required to provide the appropriate number of off-street parking spaces for similarly situated businesses or enterprises as set forth elsewhere in the Zoning Ordinance of the City of Calhoun, Georgia, Appendix A of this Code.
(g)
Commercial recycling collection centers shall not intrude into public roads so as to impede circulation or line of sight for any period in excess to ten minutes to move transport in or out of the facility.
(h)
The initial permit application fee for this classification of recycling processing center shall be $1,000.00, and the annual renewal fee shall be $750.00, said fee being due on or before December 31st of the current calendar year.
(Ord. No. 944, § 1, 10-26-2015)
Sec. 22-318. - Regulations for scrap metal processors.
(a)
Scrap metal processors shall be a permissible use only in all areas zoned by the City of Calhoun as "Ind-G—General Industrial District," and shall be subject to all provisions of Section 7.11 of Appendix A of this Code.
(b)
The minimum lot area for scrap metal processors shall be ten acres.
(c)
Scrap metal processors shall have a minimum 100-foot wide buffer along all abutting nonresidential property lines and a 250-foot wide buffer abutting residential property lines. On-site residences of the owner or operator are exempted from this provision.
(e)
Scrap metal processors shall be surrounded by an opaque wood or masonry fence not less than eight feet in height. There shall be a minimum setback from the front property line to the fence of 50 feet. There shall be a minimum setback from the side and rear property lines to the fence of 25 feet.
(f)
In addition to the fence, scrap metal processors shall be completely surrounded by vegetation that provides a year round opaque screen that may reasonably be expected to be not less than six feet tall within four years of planting. Plants shall be placed at regular intervals so as to provide a continuous hedgerow without gaps or open spaces. The vegetation shall be maintained as a continuous unbroken hedgerow for the entire period the property is employed as a scrap metal processor and shall be planted not more than ten feet outside of the required fence. Dead or diseased foliage shall be replace at the next appropriate planting time. Each owner, operator or maintainer of a scrap metal processor shall use good husbandry techniques, such as pruning and fertilizing, to maintain maximum density and foliage.
(g)
The required fence shall enclose all operations, equipment, recyclables, junk and inoperable motor vehicles at all times unless the equipment, recyclables, junk or inoperable motor vehicles are in transport to or from the site.
(h)
Scrap metal processors shall be maintained in a manner to protect the public from health and safety nuisances and hazards. Types of maintenance actions may include upkeep of the vegetative screen, maintenance of the fence so that there are no holes, keeping all junk and operations within the confines of the fence and keeping machinery in good working order. At no time shall said materials, litter or waste be permitted in any public road.
(i)
The permit application plan mandated by section 22-315(b) shall indicate off-site and on-site management techniques used to protect against litter and debris. The use of power-driven light processing activities required for the temporary storage and shipment of materials, including compacting, baling and paper, or plastic shredding shall be permitted for scrap metal processors through development plan review mandated by section 22-315(b).
(j)
Customer parking shall be provided at a ratio of one paved space per 1,000 square feet of gross area occupied by the scrap metal processor including all buffers and setbacks required herein. In addition, all such facilities shall have a paved drive from the nearest roadway entrance to the property to the paved parking area, said drive being no less than 25 feet wide at the narrowest point.
(k)
Scrap metal processor operations shall not intrude into public roads so as to impede circulation or line of sight for any period in excess to ten minutes to move transport in or out of the facility.
(l)
The initial permit application fee for this classification of recycling processing center shall be $2,500.00, and the annual renewal fee shall be $1,500.00, said fee being due on or before December 31st of the current calendar year.
(Ord. No. 944, § 1, 10-26-2015)

(a)
Industrial recycling processing centers shall be a permissible use only in all areas zoned by the City of Calhoun as "Ind-G—General Industrial District," and shall be subject to all provisions of Section 7.11 of Appendix A of this Code.

The vegetation shall be maintained as a continuous unbroken hedgerow for the entire period the property is employed as an industrial recycling processing center and shall be planted not more than ten feet outside of the required fence. Dead or diseased foliage shall be replace at the next appropriate planting time. (g)
The required fence shall enclose all operations, equipment, recyclables, junk and inoperable motor vehicles at all times unless the equipment, recyclables, junk or inoperable motor vehicles are in transport to or from the site.
(h)
Industrial recycling processing centers shall be maintained in a manner to protect the public from health and safety nuisances and hazards. Types of maintenance actions may include upkeep of the vegetative screen, maintenance of the fence so that there are no holes, keeping all junk and operations within the confines of the fence and keeping machinery in good working order. At no time shall said materials, litter or waste be permitted in any public road.
(i)
The permit application plan mandated by section 22-315(b) shall indicate off-site and on-site management techniques used to protect against litter and debris. (k)
Shall not intrude into public roads so as to impede circulation or line of sight for any period in excess to ten minutes to move transport in or out of the facility.
(l)
The initial permit application fee for this classification of recycling processing center shall be $5,000.00, and the annual renewal fee shall be $3,000.00, said fee being due on or before December 31st of the current calendar year.
(Ord. No. 944, § 1, 10-26-2015)
Sec. 22-320. - Enforcement.
(a)
The office of the city building inspector shall serve as the ordinance administrator. The ordinance administrator shall enforce this division and may call upon the other agencies as necessary to assist in enforcement of this division. In addition, whenever the administrator receives a written or verbal complaint alleging a violation of this division, the administrator shall investigate the complaint, take whatever action is warranted and inform the complainant what actions have been or will be taken.
(b)
All recycling processing centers shall be subject to inspection by the ordinance administrator to ensure compliance with this article, and all other applicable federal, state and local laws.
(c)
The owner, tenant or occupant of any building, land or part thereof and any agent or other person who participates in, assists, directs, creates or maintains any situation that is contrary to the requirements of this division may be held responsible for the violation and suffer the penalties and be subject to the remedies provided in this division.
(d)
If the administrator finds that any provision of this division is being violated, the administrator shall send or give a written notice to the person responsible for such violation indicating the nature of the violation, ordering the action necessary to correct it and advising the violator of the number of days or months within which the violation shall be corrected. The violator shall be informed of the right to appeal to the mayor and city council.
(e)
In cases where delay would seriously threaten the effective enforcement of this division or pose a danger to the health, safety or welfare of the public, the administrator may seek enforcement without prior written notice by involving any of the penalties or remedies authorized by this division.
(Ord. No. 944, § 1, 10-26-2015)
Sec. 22-321. - Penalties for violations.
Penalties for violation of this division shall be as follows:
(a)
Violations of the provisions of this division or failure to comply with any of its requirements, including violations of any conditions and safeguards established, shall constitute a misdemeanor offense with a criminal penalty not to exceed $1,000.00 and/or up to one year of imprisonment.
(b)
Any act constituting a violation of the provisions of this division or a failure to comply with any of its requirements shall subject the offender to an additional civil financial penalty of $100.00 per violation. If the offender fails to pay this penalty within ten days after being cited for a violation, the penalty may be recovered by the City of Calhoun in a civil action in the nature of a debt.
(c)
Each day that any violation continued after notification by the administrator that such violation exists may be considered a separate offense for purposes of the penalties specified in this division.
(d)
Any recycling processing center owner or operator found in violation of this division shall, in addition to any fine imposed, be required to bring the recycling processing center into compliance with all provisions of this division at the owner or operator's expense.
(e)
Any other municipal permits associated with the property for a recycling processing center permit may be revoked by the City of Calhoun, in accordance with the provisions of this division, if the permit recipient fails to develop or maintain the property in accordance with the plans submitted, the requirements of this division, or any additional requirements lawfully imposed by the mayor and city council.
Before such other permits may be revoked, the permit recipient shall be given ten days' written notice to revoke any relevant permit. The notice shall inform the recipient of the alleged reasons for the revocation and of the right to obtain a hearing on the allegations before the mayor and city council. If any relevant permit is revoked, the administrator shall provide to the permittee a written statement of the reasons for the decision.
(Ord. No. 944, § 1, 10-26-2015)
Sec. 22-322. - Donation collection containers prohibited.
(a)

Any person, company, agency or other form of organization that shall fail to remove a donation collection container on any property after the removal period of 30 days from the effective date [of the ordinance from which this section derived], or cause to place a donation collection container on any property following the effective date of [of the ordinance from which this section derived], shall be in violation of this section, and the offense for such violation shall be punishable of a fine of not less than $250.00 per offense.
