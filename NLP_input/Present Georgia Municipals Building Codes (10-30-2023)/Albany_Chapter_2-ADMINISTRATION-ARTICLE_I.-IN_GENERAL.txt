ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 2-1. - City seal—Adoption; form.
There shall be and there is hereby adopted a corporate seal for the city, to be of substantially the following form: a circular device with the inscription "City of Albany, Georgia" around the outer edge of the circle, and "Artesian City" with a representation of a flowing artesian well in the inner part of the circle. The seal shall be procured by the mayor and by the mayor delivered to the city clerk and shall thereupon become the official seal of the city.
(Code 1975, § 2-1; Code 1985, § 2-1; Ord. No. 00-114, § 1, 4-11-2000)
Sec. 2-2. - Same—Custodian.
The city clerk shall be the official custodian of the city seal, and shall keep the same in a safe place free from molestation.
(Code 1975, §§ 2-2, 2-102; Code 1985, § 2-2)
Sec. 2-3. - Same—Use.
The corporate seal shall be, by the city clerk, affixed to all orders of the mayor and the board of city commissioners, deeds, contracts, ordinances, resolutions, bonds, notes, certificates of records, and to such other instruments as the mayor and the board may direct.
(Code 1975, § 2-3; Code 1985, § 2-3)
Sec. 2-4. - Construction for private persons restricted.
The city shall be without power or authority to do or perform any type of construction work for private persons, firms or corporations, including the construction or extension of any public utility service, without first requiring the advance payment into the city treasury of the full amount of the estimated costs thereof.
(Code 1975, § 2-4; Code 1985, § 2-4)
Sec. 2-5. - Joint city/county authority, responsibility respecting certain functions.
Under O.C.G.A. § 36-70-1 et seq., cities and counties must formulate agreements regarding the delivery of local government services. The department of community affairs has established standards and procedures. In 1999, the city and county began the process and have subsequently made timely changes so as to update the service delivery strategy. Copies of all such agreements available for inspection in the offices of city clerk and city attorney
State Law reference— Intergovernmental contracts, Ga. Const. 1983, art. IX, § III, ¶ I; municipal authority to contract with other political subdivisions for use of certain public facilities, O.C.G.A. § 36-34-3.
Sec. 2-6. - City records—Records retention schedules.
(a)
The records retention schedules approved by the state records committee under the date of March 7, 1983, a copy of which is on file with the city clerk, are hereby approved and adopted as records retention schedules for the city.
(b)
The city clerk is responsible for records management activities, and is charged with the responsibility for directing and coordinating all records management matters.
(c)
No record shall be destroyed except in accordance with approved retention schedules.
(d)
Whenever any records are destroyed, a certificate of destruction shall be prepared and forwarded to the city clerk which shall show the type of records destroyed, the approved retention schedule number, the dates covered and the volume destroyed.
(Code 1985, § 2-6; Ord. No. 83-196, §§ 1—4, 12-27-1983)
State Law reference— The state Records Act, O.C.G.A. § 50-18-90 et seq.
Sec. 2-7. - Same—Fees for copies; exemption.
(a)
There shall be a standard fee schedule for the reproduction of records in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 50-18-70 et seq., relating to the inspection of public records. The standard fee schedule shall be established by ordinance or resolution of the board of city commissioners, from time to time, and made available for public examination in the office of the city clerk.
(b)
The board of commissioners, by a majority vote either at a committee of the whole meeting or at a regularly scheduled meeting of the board of commissioners may waive the costs imposed by this section.
(c)
The agendas for committee meetings and regular meetings of the board of commissioners of the city will be furnished to the public, at no cost.
(d)
The agenda packages for the committee meetings and the regular meetings of the board of commissioners of the city will be furnished to the news media, at no cost.
(e)
Upon request, any individual appointed to a committee or a board by the board of commissioners shall be furnished the minutes of the committee meetings or other meetings of the board of commissioners, at no cost.
(Code 1985, § 2-6.1; Ord. No. 88-161, §§ 1, 2, 9-17-1988; Ord. No. 96-299, § 1, 9-10-1996; Ord. No. 96-332, § 1, 11-12-1996; Ord. No. 97-107, § 1, 3-12-1997; Ord. No. 98-112, § 1, 2-24-1998; Ord. No. 00-134, § 1, 7-25-2000)
Sec. 2-8. - Same—Abstracts of real property.
The city clerk shall keep a record of all real estate belonging to the city, together with all original title papers relating thereto, and shall note any sale of city real estate on such record. It shall be the duty of the city attorney to abstract and pass upon the title to all real estate purchased by the city and no title shall be accepted without the city attorney's written approval of the title to same.
(Code 1975, § 2-181; Code 1985, § 2-7)
State Law reference— Acquisition, sale, lease, etc., of real and personal property generally, O.C.G.A. § 36-37-1 et seq.
Sec. 2-9. - Processing and payment of certain claims for damages.
(a)
Whenever in the performance of any work or the conducting of any activity the city shall cause damage to the property of other persons, liability for which is not covered by the city insurance, the city manager shall have the authority to determine the legitimacy and the amount of such property damage claims, based on such documentation as the city manager shall require satisfactory to him or her, and may pay the same, provided the amount does not exceed $100,000.00, without further authority from the board of commissioners.
(b)
The water, gas and light commission is directed to provide and implement provisions granting its general manager the same authority.
(c)
In all cases, payment shall be made by the entity causing the damage.
(Code 1985, § 2-8; Ord. No. 86-143, §§ 1—3, 4-22-1986)
State Law reference— Liability of municipal corporations for acts or omissions generally, O.C.G.A. § 36-33-1 et seq.
