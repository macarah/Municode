CHAPTER 10. - BLASTING [3]

Editor's note— At the discretion of the editor, §§ 1—20, of Ord. No. 2021-028, adopted Nov. 9, 2021, has been codified as herein set out in Ch. 10, §§ 3-10001—3-10020. Said provisions did not expressly amend the Code.

Sec. 3-10001. - Authority.
This chapter is adopted pursuant to the authority of state law currently codified as Georgia Blasting Standards Act of 1978.
(Ord. No. 2021-028, § 1, 11-9-2021)
Sec. 3-10002. - Title.
This chapter shall be known as and may be cited as the City of South Fulton Blasting Ordinance.
(Ord. No. 2021-028, § 2, 11-9-2021)
Sec. 3-10003. - Definitions.
Blasting shall mean the detonation of an explosive device.
Fire chief or chief shall mean the Chief of the City of South Fulton Fire & Rescue Department or the department member designated by the chief to enforce this ordinance.
Fire department shall mean the City of South Fulton Fire & Rescue Department.
Police chief shall mean the Chief of the City of South Fulton Police Department or the member designated by the chief.
Police department shall mean the City of South Fulton Police Department.
(Ord. No. 2021-028, § 3, 11-9-2021)
Sec. 3-10004. - Administrative authority.
The fire department shall administer this chapter, issue the necessary permits, conduct proper oversight of all blasting and, in cooperation with other officials, departments and agencies, enforce the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 2021-028, § 4, 11-9-2021)
Sec. 3-10005. - Permit required.
No blasting shall be conducted within the city limits unless a permit issued in accordance with Chapter 120-3-10 of the Rules and Regulations of the Safety Fire Commissioner of Georgia and O.C.G.A. §§ 25-2-4, 25-2-17, 25-8-9 has first been obtained for that purpose. Each permit shall identify the permit holder and designate the date(s) and time(s) when, and location(s) where blasting may be conducted and shall be valid for no more than 30 days. Blasting without a permit shall constitute a violation of this Ordinance and subject to the penalties set forth herein.
(Ord. No. 2021-028, § 5, 11-9-2021)
Sec. 3-10006. - Application.
Application for a permit to conduct blasting operations shall be made to fire department during normal business hours, on a form prescribed by the fire department. A current and valid federal level III Competency Card, a State of Georgia Blasters Certification Card , a certificate of insurance, map of area where blasting is to be located and a hold harmless letter indemnifying the city shall be submitted as part of the application. An application shall be submitted no less than ten calendar days prior to the first date for which the permit is being requested.
(Ord. No. 2021-028, § 6, 11-9-2021)
Sec. 3-10007. - Permit fee required.
No permit shall be issued unless and until a fee of $200.00 has been paid. A check in this amount made payable to the City of South Fulton must accompany the permit application.
(Ord. No. 2021-028, § 7, 11-9-2021)
Sec. 3-10008. - Authority to issue.
Permit applications shall be received, reviewed and the information contained therein verified as necessary by the fire chief. Prior to issuing any permit the chief may consult with such federal, state and/or town agencies, officials and employees whose area(s) of responsibility are or may be affected by the proposed blasting operation. Upon review and verification, the chief shall approve the application and issue the permit with any condition(s) necessary to meet the purpose of this chapter or deny the application. Any denial, along with the reason(s) for the denial therefore, shall be provided in writing to the applicant.
(Ord. No. 2021-028, § 8, 11-9-2021)
Sec. 3-10009. - Notification.
Upon issuance of the permit, the chief shall notify by electronic mail, the offices of the building inspector, city manager, police chief and the public works director citing dates and times of the blasting operation.
(Ord. No. 2021-028, § 9, 11-9-2021)
Sec. 3-10010. - Requirements.
Prior to any blasting, the permit holder shall conduct a pre-blast inspection. This inspection shall include all structures within a minimum distance of 500 feet or such extended distance as maybe specified on the permit, from the site of the proposed blast. A written report describing this inspection shall be completed and signed by the permit holder and be available for review by the fire department. All blasting shall be conducted in compliance with Chapter 120-3-10 of the Rules and Regulations of the Safety Fire Commissioner of Georgia and O.C.G.A. §§ 25-2-4, 25-2-17, 25-8-9. Except as previously approved by the chief, all blasting within 1,000 feet of any structure shall be covered by a blasting mat(s). The fire department shall be notified at least 48 hours prior to any blasting within a project and both fire and police departments shall be notified at least one hour prior to any shot. Owner or occupants of occupied structures within 750 feet of the blast must be notified of the blast in writing 72 hours prior to the blast. The blasting site is required to be inspected by a fire inspector from the fire marshal's office prior to blasting. Blasting permit applications must be submitted to the permit office before inspections can be scheduled.
(Ord. No. 2021-028, § 10, 11-9-2021)
Sec. 3-10011. - Records.
The permit holder is responsible for maintaining a record of each blast in accordance with Chapter 120-3-10 of the Rules and Regulations of the Safety Fire Commissioner of Georgia and per O.C.G.A. §§ 25-2-4, 25-2-17, 25-8-9. All original blasting records must be retained by the permit holder responsible for the blasting activity for at least three years following the conclusion of the blasting activity and include all required information in accordance with Chapter 120-3-10 of the Rules and Regulations of the Safety Fire Commissioner of Georgia and per O.C.G.A. §§ 25-2-4, 25-2- 17, 25-8-9.
(Ord. No. 2021-028, § 11, 11-9-2021)
Sec. 3-10012. - Prohibitions.
No blasting shall be conducted without a valid permit having first been obtained. Permit holders shall only conduct blasting operations between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 4:00 p.m. No blasting shall be conducted on weekend or on national or state holidays. No blasting shall be conducted after an order to cease blasting has been issued by the chief and communicated to the permit holder. No drilling shall take place within a 100 feet of any loaded holes.
(Ord. No. 2021-028, § 12, 11-9-2021)
Sec. 3-10013. - Pre-blast survey.
A pre-blast survey shall be conducted by the permit applicant or a party designated by the permit applicant for an area within 500 feet of the proposed blasting site. The cost of the pre-blast survey shall be borne by the blasting company. Any reports, measurements and/or video tapes shall be made available upon request by any or all abutters within the pre-blast survey.
(Ord. No. 2021-028, § 13, 11-9-2021)
Sec. 3-10014. - Abutter notification.
All abutters within half mile of the blasting site shall receive notification by certified mail three full business days (excluding Saturday, Sunday and holidays) in advance of the blasting. The city's building inspector and both fire and police departments shall also receive by certified mail, copies of the abutter notification.
(Ord. No. 2021-028, § 14, 11-9-2021)
Sec. 3-10015. - Pre-blast inspections.
Prior to visiting any properties for the purpose of conducting a pre-blast inspection, the permit applicant or his/her designated agent(s) shall obtain from the City of South Fulton Police Chief, official credentials in the form of identification cards to be displayed on their person for the purpose of showing that the reason for their visit is known to and approved by the City of South Fulton.
(Ord. No. 2021-028, § 15, 11-9-2021)
Sec. 3-10016. - Revocation.
The chief may revoke any permit upon a showing that the permit holder has violated any provision of this ordinance or any requirement or condition of that permit. In addition, any permit may be suspended or revoked upon a showing that a condition or conditions exist which warrant such revocation or suspension. The chief may restore any permit that has been revoked upon a showing that the condition(s) leading to the revocation has(have) been corrected.
(Ord. No. 2021-028, § 16, 11-9-2021)
Sec. 3-10017. - Appeal.
Any permit denial, suspension or revocation may be appealed within ten working days and not thereafter, to the city manager. Any such appeal shall be in writing and shall clearly specify the facts and circumstances warranting issue or reinstatement of the permit. The city manager shall order such issuance or reinstatement only on the appellant' s showing that the denial, suspension or revocation complained of was erroneous. The city manager may also order that appropriate conditions be attached to any permit that it finds should be issued or reinstated.
(Ord. No. 2021-028, § 17, 11-9-2021)
Sec. 3-10018. - Penalty.
Any person found to have violated any provision of this chapter shall be punished by a maximum fine as provided by applicable state law, which is currently $1,000.00. Each violation and/or each day of violation shall be regarded as a separate offense.
(Ord. No. 2021-028, § 18, 11-9-2021)
Sec. 3-10019. - Emergency conditions.
In situations that pose an imminent danger to public safety, the chief may, for a limited time, waive such provisions of this ordinance as may be necessary to avoid and/or lessen that danger. The details of any waiver granted under the authority of this section shall, as soon as practicable, be reported to the city manager.
(Ord. No. 2021-028, § 19, 11-9-2021)
Sec. 3-10020. - Interpretation.
This chapter is intended to complement and supplement and not conflict with federal and state regulation of blasting operations and explosives and shall be interpreted in accordance with that intent. To that end, should the requirements of this ordinance differ from any requirement or regulation prescribed by any law, statute, ordinance, rule or other regulation governing blasting operations and explosives, the provision that imposes the higher standard shall govern.
(Ord. No. 2021-028, § 10, 11-9-2021)
