Chapter 2.04 - GOVERNING BODY

Article II. - Council Meetings

2.04.020 - Meeting times—Calling special meeting.
(a)
The mayor and council shall normally meet for a regular session on the second Monday in each month and for a work session on the last Tuesday of each month at City Hall in such rooms and at such times as it may fix via resolution; provided, nevertheless, the mayor may convene the council at any time the mayor may think necessary; and always, upon the application of any three (3) members of the council.
(b)
The council may by resolution approve a schedule of actual meeting dates and times consistent with the provisions of subsection (a) above each year.
(Code 1971, Sec. 2-21)
(Ord. No. 17-447, § 1(Exh. A), 11-28-2017)
2.04.030 - Calling meeting to order—By mayor—Reading of previous minutes.
The mayor shall take the chair at the hour appointed for any regular, adjourned, extra or special meeting, shall immediately call the members to order, and upon the appearance of a quorum, shall cause the minutes of the preceding meeting or meetings to be read.
(Code 1971, Sec. 2-22)
2.04.040 - Calling meeting to order—By mayor pro tem or clerk—Appointing chairperson pro tem.
In the absence of the mayor, the mayor pro tem shall call the meeting to order, or if both are absent at the hour appointed, the clerk of council shall call the council to order, and the members present shall appoint one of their number to act as chairperson pro tem.
(Code 1971, Sec. 2-23)
2.04.050 - Order of business.
The order of business of the council at all regular or adjourned meetings shall be as set out in the agenda prepared in accordance with policies established by the city council by the city manager and submitted by the city clerk for such meeting.
(Code 1971, Sec. 2-24)
(Ord. No. 15-381, § 1, 12-14-2015; Ord. No. 18-453, § 1, 1-30-2018)

2.04.060 - Preserving order—Method of appointing committees.
The mayor, or chairperson, shall preserve order and decorum. The mayor shall appoint all committees, unless the council shall otherwise direct, in which case they shall be appointed by ballot.
(Code 1971, Sec. 2-25)
2.04.070 - Committee membership, notice and publication of list.
The clerk shall give notice to the members of council of the several committees to which they may have been appointed, and shall cause to be published, once a year, a list of the standing committees.
(Code 1971, Sec. 2-26)
2.04.080 - Committee reports.
Every committee shall report upon the subject or subjects referred to them, whether such reference be with power to act or not, at the succeeding regular meeting or shall show cause why such report is not made.
(Code 1971, Sec. 2-27)
2.04.090 - Officers' reports.
Every officer whose duty it is made, shall submit his report to the mayor and council at the regular meeting of council.
(Code 1971, Sec. 2-28)
2.04.100 - Speaking rules.
No member, while council is in session, shall speak on the subject under discussion without first being recognized by the presiding officer, nor shall such member speak longer than three (3) minutes, without the consent of council and no portion of such time may be reserved for additional opportunities to speak. Only one (1) minute shall be allowed for rebuttal and if deemed necessary by the presiding officer. When more than one (1) member of council desires to speak at, or nearly at, the same time, the presiding officer shall decide in favor of that one who first attracts his attention. Every speaker shall address the chair, and no member shall interrupt another while speaking, except to call him to order.
(Code 1971, Sec. 2-29)
(Ord. No. 18-453, § 2, 1-30-2018)
2.04.110 - Deciding questions of order.
The presiding officer shall decide all questions of order, but any member dissatisfied with any of his decisions shall have the right to appeal to council.
(Code 1971, Sec. 2-30)
2.04.120 - Motions—In writing if requested.
Any motion shall be offered in writing if any member of the council desires it.
(Code 1971, Sec. 2-31)
2.04.130 - Motions—Adjournment always in order.
A motion to adjourn shall always be in order.
(Code 1971, Sec. 2-32)
2.04.140 - Motions—To reconsider.
A motion to reconsider any of the proceedings of council shall not be entertained unless it be made by a member who voted with the majority, and such motion shall be made at the same or succeeding meeting.
(Code 1971, Sec. 2-33)
2.04.150 - Order of motions—Undebatable motions.
A.
When a question is before the mayor and council, no motion shall be received unless it is:
1.
To adjourn;
2.
To refer;
3.
The previous question;
4.
To lay on the table;
5.
To postpone to a certain time;
6.
To amend.
B.
The motions just enumerated shall take precedence in the order of enumeration, and the first four shall be decided without debate.
(Code 1971, Sec. 2-34)
2.04.160 - Division of question.
Any member may call for a division of the question when the sense will admit of it.
(Code 1971, Sec. 2-35)
2.04.170 - Calling for vote count.
Any member may call for the yeas and nays on any question.
(Code 1971, Sec. 2-36)
2.04.180 - Additional rules authorized when—Alteration of rules.
Any additional rules may be made from time to time by council, but no alteration of a rule shall take place without the consent of two-thirds (⅔) of the members present.
(Code 1971, Sec. 2-38)
2.04.190 - Appointment of city officers—Method.
All appointments of city officers made by the mayor and council shall be by ballot, and a majority of the votes cast shall be necessary to a choice; provided, by unanimous consent of those present, the election may be by acclamation; and, provided further, by unanimous consent, any one (1) member may be authorized to cast the vote of council, and said vote, when so cast, shall be considered as an appointment by council.
(Code 1971, Sec. 2-39)
2.04.200 - Book of minutes and of ordinances.
The city clerk shall keep a separate book in which shall be entered the minutes of the meeting, and also a book in which shall be entered the several ordinances passed by council.
(Code 1971, Sec. 2-42)
2.04.203 - Consent agenda.
The city clerk shall be authorized to utilize a consent agenda for all public meetings. Items eligible for placement on the consent agenda shall be limited to those items which are administrative in nature, those for which no further discussion is necessary, and those items which the city council has directed the clerk to list in said section of the agenda.
Consent agenda items may be added or removed from the consent agenda utilizing the same procedure by which regular agenda items are considered.
During any public meeting for which a consent agenda is before the Council, the presiding officer shall announce the consent agenda items and call for a vote. Unless a member of city council makes a timely objection and requests to either remove a consent agenda item or re-open discussion regarding said item, the council shall vote on all consent agenda items at once. An affirmative vote by the city council shall result in passage of all announced consent agenda items.
(Ord. No. 10-228, § 1, 10-11-2010)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 10-228, § 1, adopted Oct. 11, 2010, added provisions designated as § 2.04.210. In order to avoid conflicts in section numbering the editor has renumbered these provisions as § 2.04.205.
2.04.207 - Summary minutes.
The city clerk shall be authorized to utilize summary minutes in order to fulfill the clerk's custodial duties with respect to maintaining a record of the official actions of the city council. Said minutes shall comply with all applicable general law. including but not limited to the Georgia Open Meetings Act and any amendments thereto.
(Ord. No. 10-227, § 1, 10-11-2010)

Article III. - Committees of Council

2.04.210 - Appointment of committees.
At the first regular meeting of the mayor and council for any calendar year, committees shall be appointed to serve for the ensuing year.
(Amended during 1991 codification: Code 1971, Sec. 2-51)
2.04.220 - Committee members to be familiar with all aspects of committee function.
Each committee member shall familiarize himself with the city charter, and shall as nearly as possible commit to his memory those portions of the charter dealing specifically with his area of responsibility. He shall first diligently read, inquire into, and fully understand each ordinance, resolution, rule and regulation as it relates to his area of responsibility. He shall diligently study the budget and shall, not less than monthly, study and inquire into expenditures in the operations of his area of responsibility.
(Amended during 1991 codification: Code 1971, Sec. 2-52)
2.04.230 - Specific duties of committee members.
A.
Ordinances, Etc. Each committee member shall discuss any ordinance, resolution or rule that he proposes for adoption with the city attorney, who shall draft or approve as to form all proposed ordinances and resolutions. Upon the introduction of a proposed ordinance, resolution or rule, the committee member shall be prepared to explain such ordinance, resolution or rule in detail as it relates to the city budget, city employees, and public in general, and otherwise. At any such time, he shall anticipate general questions and inquiries of the council and be prepared to answer the same.
B.
Information Forms. Each committee member may, in his discretion, construct such forms as he deems fit and proper for the gathering of information concerning areas under his committee, and make them available to each department head at the office of the city clerk.
(Amended during 1991 codification: Code 1971, Sec. 2-53)
