ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 20-1. - Comprehensive solid waste management plan adopted.
In order to attain compliance with the tenets set forth in the Georgia Comprehensive Solid Waste Management Act as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-20 et seq., the city hereby adopts the comprehensive regional solid waste management plan heretofore approved by the Three Rivers Regional Development Commission and the state department of community affairs.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)
Sec. 20-2. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Approved container means the receptacle provided by the city or its authorized agent for placement of garbage and rubbish.
Bulky waste means stoves, refrigerators, water heaters, automobile parts, washing machines, furniture, and other waste materials other than construction debris, dead animals or hazardous waste with weights or volumes greater than those allowed for approved containers.
Cart means a receptacle, not to exceed 95 gallons, with wheels and lid, designed for the purpose of curbside collection of refuse which shall be provided by the city or its authorized agent.
Commercial establishment means any hotel, motel, apartment house, roominghouse, business, industrial, public or semipublic establishment of any nature or kind whatsoever, including a condominium and multifamily residential unit and excluding a residential unit.
Commercial solid waste means refuse from a commercial establishment, including but not limited to waste material from industrial processes, manufacturing processes, slaughterhouses, packing plants, poultry processing plants, or similar industries, and large quantities of waste foods. The term "commercial solid waste" also includes waste material from the construction, remodeling and repair operations on houses, commercial buildings, multiple dwelling and other structures, such as concrete, bricks, plaster, stone, earth, lumber, roofing materials, gutters, shavings and sawdust. The term "commercial solid waste" shall not include any hazardous wastes.
Condominium means individual ownership units in a multifamily structure with a front and back entrance to each unit on the ground level.
Construction debris means waste building materials resulting from construction, remodeling, repair or demolition operations.
Dead animals mean animals or portions thereof equal to or greater than ten pounds in weight that have expired from any cause, except those slaughtered or killed for human use.
Dumpster means a metal receptacle designed to be lifted and emptied mechanically for use primarily at commercial establishments.
Electronics means older, outdated or discarded electronic items such as televisions, computers, computer components such as keyboards, monitors and mice, printers, copiers and fax machines, CD/DVD/Blu-ray players, microwaves, cell phones and home phones.
Garbage means organic animal and vegetable wastes resulting from the handling, preparation, cooking and consumption of food, including waste from markets, storage facilities, handling and sale of produce and other food products, except such materials that may be serviced by garbage grinders and any or all dead animals less than ten pounds in weight. The term "garbage" shall not include any hazardous wastes as defined herein.
Hazardous waste means any chemical, compound, mixture, substance or article which is designated by the United States Environmental Protection Agency or appropriate agency of the state to be hazardous as that term is defined by or pursuant to federal or state law or regulations. The term "hazardous waste" includes but is not limited to batteries, tires, paint and paint cans and bulky waste containing chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs) and freon gas.
Landfill means an area of land on which or an excavation in which solid waste is placed for permanent disposal and which is not a land application unit, surface impoundment, injection well or compost pile.
Multifamily residential unit means individual units in a multifamily structure which units are not separately owned but are owned by one common entity.
Owner means any person owning, leasing, renting, occupying or managing any premises in the jurisdiction of the city council.
Organic waste means wastes that are capable of being decomposed by microorganisms. Examples of organic waste include but are not necessarily limited to kitchen wastes, animal manure, offal, hatchery and poultry processing plant wastes, and garbage.
Reclamation means a controlled method for sorting and storing material from solid wastes for future uses.
Recyclable materials means materials which have a known use, reuse, or recycling potential; which can be feasibly used, reused, or recycled; and have been diverted or taken from the waste stream for reclamation by sale, use, reuse or recycling, whether or not they require subsequent separation and processing. Such materials are not considered solid waste upon removal from the waste stream.
Recycling means any process by which reclaimed materials or other materials that could otherwise become solid waste are collected, separated or processed and reused or returned to use in the form of raw materials or products.
Refuse refers to all garbage and rubbish generated at a residential unit, condominium, multifamily residential unit or commercial establishment unless the context otherwise requires. The term "refuse" shall not include any hazardous wastes.
Residential household means any freestanding structure containing one to four separate units, each of which is used or constructed for use as a residence and each of which is provided a cart.
Rubbish means nonorganic solid waste consisting of both combustible and noncombustible wastes. The term "rubbish" includes discarded waste paper, cartons, boxes, wood, metals, cans, glass, crockery, used and discarded clothing, dunnage and/or similar materials.
Sanitary landfill means a disposal site where organic solid wastes are disposed of by means of placing an earth cover thereon and which is approved by the state department of natural resources for such purpose.
Solid waste means discarded organic and nonorganic waste, except water-carried body waste and recyclable materials and includes garbage, rubbish, cartons, boxes, wood, tree branches, yard trimmings, furniture and appliances, metal, tin cans, glass, crockery, or dunnage, ashes, street refuse, dead animals, sewage sludges, animal manures, industrial waste, such as waste materials generated in industrial operations, residue from incineration, food processing waste, demolition waste, abandoned vehicles, dredging waste, construction waste and any other waste material in a solid, semisolid or liquid state not otherwise defined in this chapter. The term "solid waste" shall not include any hazardous wastes as defined herein.
Special waste means any solid, liquid, semisolid, gaseous material and associated containers generated as a direct or indirect result of a manufacturing process or from the removal of contaminant from the air, water or land.
Waste stream means the total flow of solid waste from residential units, commercial establishments, condominiums, apartments, institutions and the like to its ultimate disposal site or facility.
Yard waste means grass clippings, leaves, trimmings, tree trunks, limbs, sticks, and other organic matter discarded from yards and gardens.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)
Sec. 20-3. - Refuse not acceptable for collection by city.
The following refuse shall be considered to be not acceptable for collection by the city:
(1)
Hazardous materials or substances, such as poisons, acids, caustics, infected materials and explosives.
(2)
Materials resulting from the repair, excavation or construction of buildings or structures such as earth, rock, plaster, mortar, roofing materials, lumber and carpet.
(3)
Materials which have not been prepared for collection in accordance with the provisions of this article.
(4)
The solid wastes resulting from industrial processes.
(5)
Tree trimmings where trees are trimmed or cut for compensation whether the compensation be in cash or in the wood from the trees trimmed or cut except in the case of a natural disaster.
(6)
Grass clippings and other yard debris resulting from commercial landscaping services.
(7)
Materials and furnishings resulting from evictions from residential and/or commercial properties.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)
Sec. 20-4. - Inspection.
It is hereby made the duty of the director of the public works director or his/her designee, to make inspection trips at regular intervals to determine whether or not garbage, trash, recyclable materials and rubbish are being properly collected, removed and disposed of as required by this Code or any applicable health ordinance. In the event it is found that such ordinance or regulations are being violated, appropriate and timely action shall be taken to insure full compliance with the provisions.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)
Sec. 20-5. - Accumulation.
No owner shall allow the accumulation on his premises of solid waste where such solid waste creates or may create a health hazard to neighbors or other citizens, or is unsightly, or emits foul or obnoxious odors that constitute either a public or private nuisance.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)
Sec. 20-6. - Service required; exceptions; penalties; fee increases.
(a)
Every household in the city shall subscribe to the sanitation service provided or contracted by the city for the collection of garbage, trash, recyclable materials and rubbish.
(b)
Those persons found guilty of improper disposal of their household waste shall be fined the maximum allowed by law per occurrence. Improper disposal shall include placing waste in commercial dumpsters, along roadways, vacant lots, another person's cart, and similar situations.
(c)
The monthly sanitation fee shall be established by resolution, which may be amended from time to time, and shall be maintained in the office of the city clerk. The fee shall be adjusted automatically to account for any increases in landfill dumping fees.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)

ARTICLE II. - COLLECTION PRACTICES

Sec. 20-16. - Collection by city.
All refuse accumulated in the city shall be collected, conveyed and disposed of by the city or its authorized agent. All refuse accumulated in the city shall be collected, conveyed and disposed of by the city under the supervision of the city administrator or by the authorized agent of the city. The city administrator shall have the authority to make regulations concerning the administration of this article as he finds necessary, and to change and modify the same after notice as required by law, provided that such regulations are not contrary to the provisions hereof.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)
Sec. 20-17. - Residential collection.
(a)
The city or its authorized agent for refuse collection and removal shall provide minimum weekly curb service collection of refuse at residential households except as otherwise set out herein.
(b)
The city or its authorized agent for refuse collection and removal shall provide collection of refuse for disabled persons.
(c)
Owners of residential households shall place the refuse in the carts and the carts shall be placed, on assigned collection days, at front curbside locations in such manner as not to obstruct passage. Owners shall place the carts at the appropriate curbside location at or before 7:00 a.m. or prior to the arrival of the collection vehicles. This placement shall not be made before dusk on the day prior to collection day. Any refuse placed on the curb after departure of the pickup crew of the city or its authorized agent shall subject the owner to prosecution for violation of this chapter should the street become littered from refuse.
(d)
Owners shall remove the carts from the curbside location to a storage location where practical, which storage location shall be nearer to the residential household located on the premises than to any street abutting the premises where practical; removal of the cart should be accomplished within a reasonable time following collection on the day the contents are emptied and collected where practical.
(e)
Owners shall prevent the continued, excessive and unsightly accumulation of refuse upon their property or the public thoroughfares bounding upon the owner's property.
(f)
It shall be a violation of this article to place or cause to be placed for collection any acid, explosive material, inflammable liquids or dangerous or corrosive material of any kind.
(g)
Bulky waste may be collected at the curb after notice is given to the city or its authorized agent. An additional fee may be assessed for collection of bulky waste and the amount of such fee will be determined prior to the collection based upon the number and weight of the items of bulky waste to be collected. Owners shall be responsible for the removal of any CFCs and freon from any items of bulky waste prior to its collection.
(h)
Refuse generated from the conduct of customary home occupations carried on from residential households will be collected on a residential fee basis.
(i)
All refuse will be collected by the city or its authorized agent if placed in the cart. No open containers shall be permitted.
(j)
It shall be a violation of this article to co-mingle yard waste and household refuse in the cart for pick-up.
(k)
Yard waste shall be collected by the city or its authorized agent once every week and must be placed at curb side. Grass and clippings shall be placed in securely tied paper bags not exceeding 32-gallon capacity and placed at curbside for collection. Limbs not exceeding four inches in diameter nor more than five feet in length must be placed at curbside for collection. Collection of limbs in excess of the size required herein may be collected on a special fee basis as requested.
(l)
Owners of a residential household shall be responsible for the proper disposition of any dead animals.
(m)
During any week in which there is an observed holiday or extremely harsh weather conditions, such as snow or ice, the city or its authorized agent may collect refuse in accordance with an altered collection schedule as needed.
(n)
Nothing in this chapter shall prevent owners from contracting with the city or its authorized agent for additional services at additional costs.
(o)
The city at all times reserves the right to direct and control all relevant aspects of the time, place and manner of residential refuse service. Nothing in this article is intended to abridge the city's right to ownership and control of the waste stream.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)
Sec. 20-18. - Plastic carts—Provided by city.
(a)
Plastic wheeled carts shall be required for street-side collection service. The responsibility for obtaining such cart shall be upon the owner or occupant of the premises desiring and otherwise eligible, by the terms of this article, for street-side collection service. The owner or occupant of the premises is required to contact the department of public works and request the delivery of a cart.
(b)
Additional carts may be acquired from the city or from the city's authorized agent by the owner or occupant and will be serviced according to the provision of this article subject to the following limit, which is hereby imposed upon the number of carts that will be serviced by the city:
Number of Carts.
(1)
Residences: Maximum of two
(2)
Commercial establishments: Maximum of three
Each additional cart will be serviced at the regular rate.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)
Sec. 20-19. - Recycling.
In order to protect the environment by conserving natural resources and preserving rapidly dwindling landfill space as well as minimizing waste disposal costs, the city encourages recycling for all residential occupants within the city limits to recycle all recyclable materials.
(1)
Collection and container requirements for recyclable materials will be the same as those outlined in sections 20-19, 20-20, 20-21, and 20-22 of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)
Sec. 20-20. - Placement for collection.
All refuse, ashes, garbage, trash and recyclable materials shall be placed in carts for collection. The carts shall be placed at street-side for collection purposes. The collection schedule shall be announced by the department of utilities and public works. If the carts are not near the street-side at collection time, this will indicate no service is needed.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)
Sec. 20-21. - Non-street-side collection.
Non-street-side collection of refuse will be provided for households within the city limits if all persons abiding in the household meet the following conditions:
(1)
Physically unable to walk a cart to the curb, due to being confined to a bed or confined to a wheelchair; or
(2)
Physically unable to roll a garbage cart to street side, as certified by a medical doctor; or
(3)
Older than 65 years of age.
Each household desiring to receive backyard refuse service must apply to city hall with the appropriate information. The household information will be verified and a decision provided. If the applicant is denied, the decision may be appealed to the governing body.
The exception will be reevaluated each 12 months.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)
Sec. 20-22. - Disabled.
Any person who is a full-time resident of a residential household and who is disabled to the extent that he is incapable of moving his refuse shall obtain a physician's certificate which states with particularity the nature and extent of such disability. Disabled persons shall not be required to place the refuse at curbside. This section shall not apply unless all of the adult persons in a residential household are disabled and obtain such physician's certificate. Certificates will be mailed to the city administrator. This section also applies to temporary disability not to exceed 90 days. The city or its authorized agent may make reasonable rules for non-curbside collection for disabled persons. The city or its authorized agent shall provide recycling services to disabled persons to the same extent such services may be provided under section 20-18.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)
Sec. 20-23. - Time limit at street-side.
Carts shall be placed at street-side no earlier than dusk on the day before the scheduled collection day and carts shall be removed from street-side as soon as practical the day of collection.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)
Sec. 20-24. - Material too large for containers generally.
Any garbage or rubbish that cannot be put into containers as provided in this chapter will not be collected. Customers that consistently generate garbage in excess of the one standardized wheeled cart will be required to purchase an additional container as needed to fully containerize all refuse.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)
Sec. 20-25. - Collection schedules.
(a)
The public works director or his/her designee shall be authorized to establish schedules for the collection of garbage and trash within the corporate limits.
(b)
The public works department or its authorized collection agency shall collect and remove all garbage in the residential sections of the city once each week, except in weeks when holidays occur and during emergencies. Collection shall be made at least once per week or as otherwise determined by the public works director from the business section of the city and in either of the sections as often as required by the department.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)
Sec. 20-26. - Collection of household furniture and appliances.
Upon request and for an extra charge, the city shall collect normal discarded household furniture and appliances, including sofas, chairs, beds, refrigerators, washers, dryers, hot water heaters, and similar items, but not including furnaces. This service is for curbside pickup only. Persons requesting this service shall be notified by the department of public works as to the date collection will occur. Items for collection shall be placed at curbside no earlier than 12:00 noon on the day preceding the scheduled collection. This service shall be available to residential areas only. This service shall be provided pursuant to the requirements of subsections 20-27(d) and 20-36(e) of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)
Sec. 20-27. - Trash collections.
(a)
The collection of bulky and yard waste, if collected separately, shall be at a time and in a manner prescribed by the public works director. Bulky and yard waste collection must be at the curbside location. No backdoor collection will be provided for this service. At no time shall trash be placed in such a manner as to obstruct gutters, drains, walkways, or streets. Tree limbs and branches shall not exceed five feet in length or 50 pounds in weight in any household to be handled by collection workers. The fee schedule for tree limbs and branch collections are defined in subsection 20-36(f) of this chapter. Leaves, grass clippings, straw and similar items must be placed for collection in paper bags or containers separate from other trash. All grass clippings shall be bagged in paper bags or containerized as authorized by the public works department. Rocks, dirt, concrete blocks, or bricks are not considered trash and shall not be collected. No material shall be collected by the public works department resulting from the construction, remodeling or demolition of buildings or fixtures, or containing building materials, including floor and carpet scraps. No debris from burned houses or their contents shall be collected by the public works department.
(b)
Only household garbage is to be placed in the container provided by the city for normal garbage pickup.
(c)
Household garbage includes the normal garbage generated by a residence, not including the materials listed in sections 20-24, 20-26 and subsection 20-27(a).
(d)
Effective January 1, 2019, the following items, prior to collection by the city, shall require a sticker to be purchased and placed on said item. The items are as follows: appliances, freezers, dressers, bicycles, mattresses, window air conditioning units, refrigerators, dishwashers, end tables, commodes, box springs, stoves, sofas, tables, sinks, beds, TV sets, recliners, chairs, grills, frames, washers and dryers, microwaves, hot water heaters, swing sets (disassembled), lawnmowers, and any other items which, in the discretion of the public works director, shall comply with this requirement. The fee shall be as described in the schedule of fees as established from time to time by the city council.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)
Sec. 20-28—20-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - SCRAP TIRES

Sec. 20-31. - Stacking or storing unlawful.
It shall be unlawful for any person to stack or store new or used automobile, truck or tractor tires in an outdoor area within the city. The storage of all such tires shall be in an enclosed and covered area. Nothing in this section shall prohibit or prevent an orderly outdoor display of tires for sale during normal business hours, provided that such tires are stored in an enclosed and covered area after normal business hours.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)
Sec. 20-32—20-35. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - BILLING

Sec. 20-36. - Billing.
(a)
The rates and charges for the use of sanitation/solid waste service shall be established from time to time by the city council and set forth in the schedule of fees and charges.
(b)
Said fee may be adjusted automatically by the public works department to cover the increase in landfill disposal costs.
(c)
All bills for garbage service shall be sent to customers on the billing date for utilities. These bills are due and payable at the city customer service office on the specified date on the bills. After that date, service may be ceased until all amounts due are paid.
(d)
A penalty of ten percent shall be added to all bills for garbage service where bills are not paid on the due date as specified on the bill. If a customer is in arrears for a previous month or months, payment shall first be applied to previous bills.
(e)
Stickers for bulk item pickup may be purchased in person or by mail order from the customer service office at city hall. The cost of these stickers is defined in the rate schedule established from time to time by the city council.
(f)
Fees for bulk tree limbs and branch collections are described in the rate schedule established from time to time by the city council. Payment must be made by the customer prior to the service being delivered.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)
Sec. 20-37—20-40. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - ENFORCEMENT

Sec. 20-41. - Enforcing provisions.
(a)
The provisions of this chapter regarding collection and removal of refuse shall be enforced by the city administrator. All such provisions regarding collection and removal of refuse may also be enforced by the city code enforcement officer if so requested by the city administrator.
(b)
Violations of any provisions of this chapter shall be reported initially to the city administrator whose office is charged with administration and interpretation of all sections herein. The city administrator or his/her designee shall be authorized to issue citations and prosecute violations of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)
Sec. 20-42. - Fines and penalties.
A fine of up to $1,000.00 per day per violation may be assessed for any of the requirements of this chapter. This fine is in addition to any other penalties established herein.
(Ord. No. 12-18-CCO , Att., 10-23-2018, eff. 1-1-2019)
