ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 2-2-1. - Boards; appointment procedure.
The following provisions shall constitute a policy and procedure to be followed by the board of commissioners in the appointment of persons to county boards:
(1)
General notification shall be given to the public of impending vacancies on boards to be filled by appointment by the board of commissioners by advertising a notice in the official county organ one (1) time at least one (1) month prior to the date on which such appointment will be considered.
(2)
The notice shall advise persons who are interested to submit resumes to the county administrator giving their backgrounds and stating the appointments in which they are interested. The county administrator shall receive such resumes, and shall distribute them to the commissioners.
(3)
No persons will be considered for appointment to county boards except residents of the county.
(4)
If any person appointed to any board by the board of commissioners shall miss three (3) consecutive meetings of such board, such person shall stand automatically removed from the board, and a vacancy shall exist.
(Res. No. 85-029, § 1, 12-2-85)
Cross reference— Fire code board of appeals, appointment, § 2-7-22.
Sec. 2-2-2. - County attorney; duties.
The county attorney shall be the legal advisor and representative of the county and in such capacity shall:
(1)
Advise the board of commissioners, county administrator and constitutional officers, when requested, upon all legal questions arising in the conduct of county business;
(2)
Prepare or revise ordinances when so requested by the board of commissioners;
(3)
Give opinions upon any legal matter or question submitted by the board of commissioners or the county administrator;
(4)
Attend or have an assistant attend all regular or special meetings of the board of commissioners for the purpose of giving any legal advice requested by its members;
(5)
Prepare or review for execution all contracts and instruments to which the county is a party and approve, as to form, all bonds required to be submitted to the county;
(6)
Prepare, when authorized by the board of commissioners, all complaints filed, and appear in any court in the prosecution of every case to protect the rights of the county; see to the full enforcement of all judgments or decrees rendered or entered in favor of the county;
(7)
Defend suits and actions at law or equity brought against the county unless otherwise directed by the board of commissioners,
(8)
Make immediate report to the board of commissioners on the outcome of any litigation in which the county has an interest;
(9)
Keep complete and accurate records of the following, which records shall remain the property of the county:
(a)
All suits in which the county had or has an interest, giving the names of the parties; the nature of the action; the disposition of the case; its status, if pending; and the briefs of counsel, and
(b)
All written opinions prepared by the county attorney and all certificates or abstracts of titles furnished by the county attorney to the county, or any department or official thereof; and
(10)
Render such other legal services as may be required by the board of commissioners.
(Code 1979, § 1-3-4)
Cross reference— County attorney, appointment and compensation, § 1-3-4.
Sec. 2-2-2.1. - Albany-Dougherty Hospital Authority—Manner of appointment; method of filling vacancies.
(a)
From and after July 2, 1990, all appointments to the Albany-Dougherty Hospital Authority, with the exception of the position held by the president of the medical staff of Phoebe Putney Memorial Hospital (Resolution No. 78-130), shall be made directly by the board of commissioners without the necessity of receiving a submission of names from the authority.
(b)
From and after July 2, 1990, each vacancy in the membership of the authority because of death, resignation, removal of residency from the county, or for any other reason other than the normal expiration of a members term of office shall be filled under the same method of appointment as stated in subsection (a) above.

No authority member shall be appointed to serve in excess of two (2) full five-year terms with the exception of those members appointed to fill vacancies. Should a vacancy occur, an Authority member may be appointed for the duration of the term created by the vacancy as well as for two (2) full five-year terms. Sitting members of the authority, having now served in excess of two (2) full five-year terms, will remain as authority members for the duration of their current term but will not be eligible for reappointment.

Sec. 2-2-4. - Worker's compensation coverage.
The elected county officers and elected members of the governing authority of Dougherty County shall be considered employees of Dougherty County for purposes of workers' compensation coverage, and thereby subject to the Workers' Compensation Act of the State of Georgia. The purpose of this resolution is to allow elected officials in Dougherty County to be eligible for workers' compensation coverage as any other employee of Dougherty County. Those elected officials this resolution specifically intends to include are: the board of commissioners, sheriff, probate judge, magistrate judges, state court judge, superior court judges, clerk of court, and coroner.
(Res. No. 83-035, § 1, 11-7-83; Ord. No. 12-020, § I, 7-16-12)

Sec. 2-2-20. - Rules of order; agenda; quorum; special meeting notice; voting; minutes.
(a)
Roberts' Rules of Order Newly Revised (Sarah Corbin Robert edition, revised 1970) shall be used as a guide to proper parliamentary procedure, in all commission meetings. All meetings shall be conducted in a businesslike, orderly manner.
(b)
An agenda shall be prepared on all regular meetings twenty-four (24) hours prior to the meeting and commissioners shall be provided the agenda twenty-four (24) hours prior to the meeting. It shall be acceptable to receive this agenda by telephone. This shall not prevent the placing of additional items on the agenda by unanimous consent of the board of commissioners.
(c)
Four (4) members shall constitute a quorum.
(d)
The chairman shall give all members of the board three-day notice of any specially called meeting of the board, and such notice to the public as is reasonably possible. By attendance at a specially called meeting any member waives the requirement of a three-day notice.
(e)
All commissioners shall have one (1) vote each. When the chairman or vice-chairman is presiding, that member shall be recorded as having voted for the motion unless that member registers a vote against the motion.
(f)
All actions of the board of commissioners with any legal effect on the county shall be taken at a meeting of the board of commissioners under this section and shall be entered in the official minutes of the meeting by the clerk of the board of commissioners. The official minutes shall be open to the public for inspection and copying.
(Code 1979, § 1-1-2; Res. of 10-1-79, § 1)
Sec. 2-2-21. - Use of county property by commissioners.
No individual commissioner shall use or command to be used any county equipment, facilities, supplies, manpower, employee, vehicle, or other instrumentality without first seeking the approval of the county administrator or the approval by a majority vote of the board of commissioners at a meeting. Such use shall only be for directly related county business affecting the citizens of the county.
(Res. No. 80-123, § 1, 1-7-80)
Sec. 2-2-22. - Expenditures outside of county—Limits.
Expenditures outside of the county by individual commissioners on the business of the citizens of the county as hereinafter set forth, shall be reimbursed to such commissioners for the following itemized and approved purposes, upon the receipt by the county administrator by such individual commissioner of an itemized voucher, together with receipts for such approved expenses. Advance funds may be disbursed at the discretion of the county administrator or upon approval of the majority of the board of commissioners upon application of an individual commissioner.
(1)
Approved expenditure for travel expense:
(a)
For travel by private conveyance, using standard state roadways for computing distances: Twenty cents ($0.20) per mile.
(b)
For travel by common carrier; bus, rail, or air: Actual cost of the travel ticket. Note: Air travel will usually be at tourist rate.
(c)
Parking fees in commercially operated lots or garage.
(2)
Approved expenditure for lodging: Actual cost for single room.
(3)
Approved expenditure for meals: In conjunction with the above-mentioned travel and lodging excluding expenditures for alcoholic beverages.
(4)
Approved expenditure for telephone expense: Directly relating to county business. (All above-listed expenses must be supported by receipt)
(5)
Approved expenditure for gratuities: In conjunction with meals, not to exceed fifteen (15) percent of the actual expense of each meal.
(6)
Approved expenditure for individual entertainment expense: Other than the above-listed items will not be reimbursed.
(7)
Approved business outside the county:
(a)
Any regular meeting, convention, or seminar of any organization of which the county is a member.
(b)
Normal activities of county government which are directly related to county issues which do not extend outside of the bounds of the state.
(Res. No. 80-123, § 2, 1-7-80)
Cross reference— Reimbursable expenses generally, § 2-6.3.

DIVISION 2. - POLICE DEPARTMENT

Sec. 2-2-40. - Creation; composition; supervision.
A police department is hereby created for the county. The department shall be composed of a chief of police and any police officers and other employees as may be necessary. Subject to the direction of the county administrator and board of commissioners, the supervision and control of the department is vested in the chief of police.
(Code 1979, § 1-5-3)
Annotations—The board of commissioners may not delegate its authority to appoint county police, fix salaries, levy tax to pay same, fix bond or discharge officers. Levine v. Perry et al., 204 Ga. 323, 49 S.E. 2d 820 (1948). The creation of a county police force does not diminish the power and authority of the sheriff to enforce the law and preserve the peace. Welfe et al. v. Huff et al., 232 Ga. 44, 205 S.E. 2d 254 (1974).
State Law reference— Authority to create county police, O.C.G.A. § 36-8-1 et seq.
Sec. 2-2-41. - Chief of police; powers and duties.
The chief of police, or county police officers under the chief's direction, shall:
(1)
Execute all lawful orders and directions of the county administrator or board of commissioners;
(2)
Maintain the peace, good order and tranquility of the county by the enforcement of all county ordinances and state and federal laws;
(3)
Strive to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of the police department, and make recommendations from time to time to the board of commissioners for improvements which the board may assist in making in the department;
(4)
Make a monthly report in writing to the board of commissioners on the actions of the police department;
(5)
Insure that at all times there are sufficient officers on duty to carry out the tasks assigned to the police department;
(6)
Discipline officers in accordance with the county personnel policies and procedures;
(7)
Maintain written rules and regulations for the police department, approved by the board of commissioners;
(8)
Post copies of all police department rules and regulations and this division in a conspicuous place in the department.
(Code 1979, § 2-1-1)
Sec. 2-2-42. - Patrol powers.
(a)
The police officers of the county shall have the authority to patrol any street, alley, path, sidewalk or park, in and around any stores, cafes taverns, restaurants, dance halls, service stations or any other establishment in the unincorporated areas of the county.
(b)
An officer while on duty for the county shall have authority to enter any type of business while open to the public and check to see if everything is being carried on in a lawful manner. After business hours, and while close to the public, an officer for the county has authority to check places of business, to see if the doors and windows are safely locked.
(c)
An officer for the county shall have the authority to check and investigate, enter and patrol any park, business, residence or public place within the unincorporated areas of the county if the officer has probable cause or sufficient reason to believe some act is being committed which would involve the violation of a law or a county ordinance; a personal injury or damage to property; or if any of the above appear to be imminent.
(d)
No police officer shall enter a private dwelling without a search or arrest warrant, unless such officer has the voluntary consent of the owner or occupant or is in pursuit of a fugitive of whom the officer has personal knowledge or of whom there is probable cause to believe has committed or attempted to commit a crime.
(Code 1979, § 2-1-2)
Sec. 2-2-43. - Abuse of position.
(a)
No police officer shall use official position or official identification cards or badges:
(1)
For personal or financial gain;
(2)
For obtaining privileges not otherwise available to the officer except in the performance of duty; or
(3)
For avoiding consequences of illegal acts.
(b)
Police officers may not lend their identification cards or badges to another person, or permit them to be photographed or reproduced without approval of the chief.
(Code 1979, § 2-1-3)
Sec. 2-2-44. - Abuse of process; withholding evidence.
Police officers shall not convert to their own use, manufacture, conceal, falsify, destroy, remove, tamper with, or withhold, evidence or information, or make false accusations of a criminal or traffic charge.
(Code 1979, § 2-1-4)
Sec. 2-2-45. - Departmental reports; truthfulness.
(a)
Police officers shall submit all necessary reports on time and in accordance with established departmental procedures.
(b)
Reports submitted by officers shall be truthful and complete and no officer shall knowingly enter or cause to be entered any inaccurate, false, misleading or improper information.
(Code 1979, § 2-1-5)
Sec. 2-2-46. - Endorsements and referrals.
Police officers shall not recommend or suggest in any manner, except in the transaction of personal business, the employment or procurement of a particular product, professional service or commercial service such as an attorney service, ambulance service, towing service, bondsman, mortician or the like. In the case of ambulance or towing service, when such service is necessary and the person needing the service is unable or unwilling to procure it or requests assistance, officers shall proceed in accordance with established departmental procedures.
(Code 1979, § 2-1-6)

ARTICLE V. - TRAVEL POLICY

Sec. 2-2-131. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Authorizing party means the individual authorized to approve or disapprove all travel-related requests. Individual is assumed to be in a level of authority such that he/she can accurately evaluate the need for and the cost and benefit of such travel.
County business shall pertain to either of the following:
(1)
Business travel means travel for the purpose of conducting official county business.
(2)
Professional/educational travel means travel to attend meetings, conferences and training programs for professional growth and development as well as the mutual benefit of the county.
Requesting party means the individual who will be reimbursed for travel costs incurred.
(Res. No. 06-024, 6-19-06)
Sec. 2-2-132. - Guidelines for authorization of travel.
(a)
Decisions as to what travel is authorized begin with the budgetary process. Travel needs must be anticipated and submitted in the budget each year for approval by the board of commissioners.
(b)
The authorizing party's responsibility is to stay within his/her departmental budget's travel allocation as approved by the board of commissioners.
(c)
Prior to approving a travel request, the authorizing party is responsible for determining that a sufficient, unexpended appropriation remains in the travel budget to reimburse all expected costs of the travel.
(d)
Personal items, alcoholic beverages, and expenses of family members are not authorized. Expense reports must be prepared to reflect only actual expenses of the employee which are essential to the conduct of county business.
(e)
Requests for travel, travel advances, and actual expense reimbursement should be authorized by the county administrator upon recommendation of the department manager or elected official.
(Res. No. 06-024, 6-19-06)
Sec. 2-2-133. - Expense reimbursement policies.
The following reimbursement guidelines are intended to set forth maximum standards. Employees and officials of the county are expected to spend funds prudently:
(1)
Lodging. Reimbursement for lodging is authorized when the individual's travel requires overnight accommodation. Overnight accommodations are not considered necessary within the Albany-Dougherty County area. Receipts are required for all lodging costs.
a.
The county will pay only actual room rental costs supported by the hotel bill for each day that lodging away from home is required for business reasons.
b.
All employees and officials shall attempt to incur only reasonable hotel expenses when out of town. Employees shall request standard rooms and governmental rates.
c.
Hotel receipts must be attached to the expense report. Charges other than actual room cost should be itemized on the expense report.
d.
Employees and officials must obtain a hotel/motel tax exemption request form and a sales tax exemption form from the finance department and present it to the hotel at time of check-in when traveling on official business within the State of Georgia.
e.
When two (2) employees or officials share a room, one (1) employee or official shall pay and request reimbursement for the full lodging charge. The second employee or official shall not be entitled to any reimbursement since he/she did not incur any expense.
(2)
Meals. Employees are entitled to reimbursement for meals based on a per diem amount of thirty-eight dollars ($38.00/day) for travel out of the county for a minimum of twenty-four (24) hours. For travel increments of less than twenty-four (24) hours, meals will be reimbursed as follows:
Breakfast .....$8.00
Lunch .....$10.00
Dinner .....$20.00
a.
No reimbursement will be allowed when meals are included as part of a registration fee or are provided free by a common carrier.
b.
Where scheduled official conference or convention meals are not included as part of the registration fees, and where it can be clearly shown that the cost of such meal(s) exceeds the per diem meal limit, the applicable limit may be increased to the required amount when supported by receipts and approved by the county administrator.
c.
In conjunction with meals, gratuities are not to exceed fifteen (15) percent of actual expense for each meal.
d.
Occasionally, due to the location of the travel, the thirty-eight dollars ($38.00) per diem may be insufficient. Reimbursement in excess of thirty-eight dollars ($38.00) per day will be considered but only when supported by receipts and approved by the county administrator. This is intended to reimburse the traveler for higher costs in major cities and not to encourage additional daily expenses.
(3)
Transportation. The county will reimburse the cost incurred for transportation by use of common carrier, county vehicles, or private vehicles in accordance with the following guidelines:
a.
Airline:
1.
It is the obligation of each employee and official to choose the least expensive flight from alternatives.
2.
The approved class of transportation for all employees is coach/tourist. Most major airlines offer excursion or other discounted fares under certain conditions. Every effort should be made to take advantage of these rates by planning and ordering tickets within the required time frames.
3.
A copy of the ticket coupon and travel itinerary must be attached to each expense report.
b.
Rail and bus fare:
1.
Receipts are required for reimbursement of these transportation costs.
2.
Transportation reservations shall be obtained at the most economical rate available.
c.
County vehicles:
1.
The use of a County vehicle is authorized for travel to destinations of up to four hundred (400) miles from Dougherty County. Operation of a county vehicle outside the State of Georgia requires the prior approval of the county administrator or department head.
2.
Only the minimum number of county vehicles should be used when more than one (1) employee travels to the same location.
3.
Receipts must be obtained for all county vehicle expenses, including gasoline purchases.
4.
County vehicles may be driven only by authorized county employees and officials.
d.
Personal vehicles:
1.
Use of a personal vehicle is authorized only if one (1) of the following conditions is met:
(i)
No suitable county vehicle is available;
(ii)
Round trip travel mileage is less than one hundred (100) miles;
(iii)
The requesting party has a physical handicap which required the use of a specially equipped vehicle;
(iv)
An employees' family member(s) accompany the employee on the trip.
2.
Advance approval must be obtained.
3.
When more than one employee is traveling to the same location, as separate car allowances will not be approved unless separate arrival and departure times are dictated by other county business.
4.
The reimbursement rate for personal mileage is forty cents ($0.40) per mile.
e.
Rental cars:
1.
Rental vehicles are not to be used unless the cost is less than that of other available transportation such as taxis, airport limousine, or hotel/airport shuttle services. If a car rental is necessary to reduce costs, the employee or officials should share rental cars.
2.
Employee or official shall accept the collision damage waiver ("CDW") insurance, but reject any other insurance offered by the rental agency.
3.
Car rental contracts and/or express checkout receipts are to be submitted as documentation for reimbursement. Receipts for gasoline purchases are required for reimbursement.
f.
Local ground transportation:
1.
Local transportation cost incurred while out of town on county related business will be reimbursed.
2.
Receipts for the above services should be obtained when possible and provided to support reimbursement.
(4)
Registration fees. Registration fees for conferences or seminars are reimbursable when supported by receipts. Whenever possible, advance registration should be used.
(Res. No. 06-024, 6-19-06)
Sec. 2-2-134. - Miscellaneous.
(a)
Parking and shuttle will be reimbursed when supported by receipts.
(b)
Telephone charges for county related business are reimbursable when supported by receipts.
(Res. No. 06-024, 6-19-06)
Sec. 2-2-135. - Nonallowable expenses.
(a)
It is the county's policy to reimburse the employee or official for all reasonable and necessary expenses incurred in transacting the affairs of business; however, there are specific types of expenses which are considered to be truly personal nature and are nonallowable:
(1)
Air travel and other trip insurance;
(2)
Barber, manicurist, shoe polisher, masseur;
(3)
Briefcases or other items of personal use;
(4)
Entertainment expenses (such as airline headsets, theater, movies, and sporting events) of the employees and officials;
(5)
Personal reading materials or services;
(6)
Repairs, maintenance, or insurance on personal vehicles;
(7)
Traffic fines and court costs;
(8)
Unauthorized club or other membership dues or fees;
(9)
Alcoholic beverages, entertainment, gifts, or donations.
(b)
Employees and officials are responsible for lost or stolen personal property while traveling on county business.
(Res. No. 06-024, 6-19-06)
Sec. 2-2-136. - Procedure for request for authorization to travel.
(a)
When it becomes necessary to travel, the requesting party shall prepare and submit a travel requisition and accounting form provided by county finance department. The form must be approved by the authorizing party.
(b)
All county officials authorized to travel on behalf of the county may request a travel advance to cover the projected cost of transportation, lodging, meals, and registration. A travel advance may be requested no more than two (2) weeks prior to the date of travel.
(1)
No travel advance over five hundred dollars ($500.00) will be issued without the prior approval of the county administrator.
(2)
Employees are encouraged to use personal credit cards for travel expenses wherever possible, rather than carrying large amounts of cash.
(c)
Exceptions to the two-week rule may be made where advance registration for a seminar is necessary. In this case, only the registration fee may be requested.
(Res. No. 06-024, 6-19-06)
Sec. 2-2-137. - Expense reimbursement procedures.
(a)
Within seven (7) days after returning from an authorized trip, the requesting party shall present to the authorizing party a complete itemization of all trip expenses on a travel requisition and accounting form. The requesting party shall include payment for any travel advance not used.
(b)
The authorizing party shall review, sign and submit the expense statement to the finance department within two (2) working days.
(c)
The finance department shall audit all travel requisition and accounting forms to determine mathematical accuracy; cross-reference amounts claimed to appropriate receipts; and review the appropriateness of expenditures in accordance with this policy.
(d)
In the event the actual expenses exceed any advance taken, the finance department will issue a check to the employee to reimburse the net amount due.
(Res. No. 06-024, 6-19-06)
