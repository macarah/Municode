ARTICLE III. - PAY PLAN

Sec. 16B-3-1. - New appointees.
(a)
Generally, a new employee shall be paid the minimum rate of pay for his class.
(b)
If the personnel officer determines that it is not possible to recruit qualified employees at the established entrance salary, after having been notified of the vacancy and having been allowed a reasonable time to recruit for the position, he may, at the request of the appointing authority, authorize the appointment of a qualified applicant at a higher step of the range, subject to approval by the director of administration and with the concurrence of the city manager, provided that all other employees in the same class of position in the same department are adjusted in salary to the same step not later than the beginning of the next quarter. If the salary of any employee in the same class in the same department is not adjusted in accordance with this section the appointing authority must obtain prior approval from the director of administration and with the concurrence of the city manager.
(Ord. No. 71-213, Ch. 3, § 1, 8-17-71; Ord. No. 75-20, § 8, 3-18-75)
Sec. 16B-3-2. - Promotions.
When an employee is promoted to a position in a higher class, his salary shall be increased to the minimum rate for the higher class. In the case of overlapping ranges and the employee to be promoted is at or above the minimum step of the class to which promoted, the promoted employee shall be increased to the dollar step immediately above his present salary which provides a minimum bi-weekly increase of $25.00.
(Ord. No. 71-213, Ch. 3, § 2, 8-17-71; Ord. No. 89-106, § 1, 9-26-89)
Editor's note— Section 2 of Ord. No. 89-106, adopted Sept. 26, 1989, § 1 of which amended § 16B-3-2, reads as follows: "Section 2. This Ordinance shall be retroactive for those employees promoted or appointed after July 1, 1988, provided that at the time of the promotion or appointment, such employees were in a regular step as a result of the implementation of the pay plan in effect on July 1, 1987.
Sec. 16B-3-2.1. - Promotional pay policy guidelines for public safety and general government pay programs.
Promotional pay policy guidelines for the public safety and general government pay programs are:
(1)
Public safety and general government employees that are selected for promotion will receive the greater of (a) a three percent pay increase of current salary for each pay grade that the new position exceeds the current position, limited to a maximum increase of ten percent or, (b) the minimum rate for the new position.
(2)
This section is retroactive to April 29, 1997.
(Ord. No. 97-129, § 2, 11-11-97; Ord. No. 01-88, § 1, 9-18-01)
Editor's note— The provisions of Ord. No. 97-129, § 2, adopted Nov. 11, 1997, which amended Ch. 16B of the Code, have been included herein as § 16B-3-2.1 at the discretion of the editor.
Sec. 16B-3-3. - Demotions.
When an employee is demoted to a lower class position, he shall be paid at a rate which is within the approved range for the lower class position. The rate of pay shall be set by the director of administration with the recommendation of the department head concerned.
(Ord. No. 71-213, Ch. 3, § 3, 8-17-71)
Sec. 16B-3-4. - Reallocations downward.
When an employee's position is reallocated to a lower class position and his present salary is above the maximum for the lower class, the employee shall be permitted to continue at his present rate of pay during period of incumbency (except in event of general service-wide reductions) but shall not be entitled to a salary increase.
(Ord. No. 71-213, Ch. 3, § 4, 8-17-71)
Sec. 16B-3-5. - Reinstated employees.
A reinstated employee shall be paid at a salary rate within the approved salary range for the position in which he is reinstated. The rate of pay shall be set by the director of administration.
(Ord. No. 71-213, Ch. 3, § 5, 8-17-71)
Sec. 16B-3-6. - Part-time employment.
When employment is on a part-time basis, only the proportionate part of the rate for the time actually employed shall be paid.
(Ord. No. 71-213, Ch. 3, § 6, 8-17-71)
Sec. 16B-3-7. - Reserved.

Sec. 16B-3-8. - Administrative wage adjustment.
(a)
The wages of each employee in Steps A through C of his job classification shall be reviewed annually by his immediate supervisor and appointing authority for the purpose of making recommendations for the following year. The wages of each employee in Steps D and E of his job classification shall be reviewed biennially for the purpose of making recommendations for the following year. All of the employee's personnel records, including attendance, tardiness, performance, and length of service shall be considered in making a wage recommendation, with major emphasis placed on the objective evaluation of services rendered. Continued development of personal skills through education and training, established as criteria by the department heads with the approval of the city manager, will be considered in making recommendations for pay increases.
(b)
In all cases where wage increases are recommended, a certificate or a performance rating from the immediate supervisor, with the approval of the appointing authority, stating the reason for the recommendation, shall be submitted to the personnel officer.
(c)
Departmental requests for wage adjustments shall be endorsed by the personnel officer and forwarded to the city manager via the director of administration for action. The city manager shall have the authority to approve or deny any request for a merit raise, based upon his review of length of service requirements and his evaluation of the employee's job performance record.
(d)
The merit raise review contemplated by subsections (a), (b) and (c) herein shall be conducted annually for an effective pay raise in each year hereafter commencing July 1, 1988, for employees in Step A through G of their grade. The merit raise review contemplated by subsections (a), (b) and (c) herein shall be conducted biennially for an effective pay raise at two-year intervals commencing July 1, 1988, for employees in Steps H and I of their grade. All merit raises for employees in Steps A through G of their grade shall be effective at the beginning of the pay period in which the anniversary date of their appointment or promotion occurs. All merit raises for employees in Step H and I of their grade shall be effective at the beginning of the pay period in which the bi-anniversary date of their appointment or promotion occurs.
(e)
When an employee is promoted to a higher class or pay grade, his promotional salary adjustment will be in accordance with section 16B-3-2.
(Ord. No. 71-213, Ch. 3, § 8, 8-17-71; Ord. No. 73-2, § 1, 1-2-73; Ord. No. 74-11, § 1, 1-2-74; Ord. No. 88-70, 6-21-88; Ord. No. 89-107, 9-26-89)
Sec. 16B-3-8.1. - Tenure eligibility for promoted or individually reclassified employees; permanent anniversary or appointment date.
(a)
In order to preserve equity amount all Columbus Consolidated Government employees with respect to their classifications and tenure, it is hereby ordained by the Council of Columbus, Georgia as follows:
Effective July 1, 1998, the employee's original date of hiring will replace and supersede all anniversary dates whether established by date of employment, reclassification, promotion, or demotion as the date for implementation of any annual pay for performance increase for general government employees and public safety employees.
Effective July 1, 1998, regular full-time employees are to be evaluated and rated at least once a year during the 30-day period prior to the date of hiring (anniversary). The evaluation will serve as a performance indicator to the employee and as a basis for performance increases.
(b)
The tenure required for any merit increase shall not be deemed interrupted or negated by an individual reclassification or promotion within such tenure.
(Ord. No. 74-49, 3-12-74; Ord. No. 89-108, 9-26-89; Ord. No. 97-95, 9-9-97)
Sec. 16B-3-9. - Pay plans in effect.
The pay plans in effect on the date of the adoption of the Charter shall remain in effect until modified in accordance with the provisions of the Charter and these regulations. When adopted, the pay plan for the government shall be considered a part of this article and have the same force and effect as these regulations.
(Ord. No. 71-213, Ch. 3, § 9, 8-17-71)
Sec. 16B-3-10. - Revision of pay plan.
The personnel officer, when he deems it necessary, shall examine the pay plan for the purpose of revision and on the basis of conclusions reached through this examination, make recommendations for amendments as prescribed by section 8-304 of the Charter.
(Ord. No. 71-213, Ch. 3, § 10, 8-17-71)
Sec. 16B-3-11. - Adoption and amendment of pay plan.
When approved as prescribed by section 8-304 of the Charter, such plan shall constitute the consolidated city's pay schedule for classes of positions in the merit system as prescribed by the Charter, section 8-302, for the ensuing fiscal year and thereafter until a new pay plan shall be adopted in the manner prescribed.
(Ord. No. 71-213, Ch. 3, § 11, 8-17-71)
