Sec. 10-1. - State permit.
No person shall engage in handling food or food products, in any form or container, for distribution in the city, without a permit from the state.
(Code 1968, § 11-5)
State Law reference— Permits for food service establishments, Ga. Code Ann., § 26-2-371 et seq.
Sec. 10-2. - Employment of food handlers.
All employers of any of the classes of persons described and specified in the following section shall make certain that any person employed by him is a holder of the certificate provided for in sections 10-3 and 10-4, and the employer shall before allowing the employee to enter upon duties of the employment actually have exhibited to him the said certificate.
(Code 1968, § 11-4)
Sec. 10-3. - Health certificate prerequisite to handling food.
All persons employed in handling food in public places including markets, stores, hotels, restaurants, lunchrooms and stands or places for dispensing food to the public and both fountain and bottle drinks shall on or before January fifteenth each year have a physical examination by some regularly licensed and practicing physician in the city or by the county board of health and shall obtain from the said physicians or board of health a certificate that such person is not afflicted with or contaminated by a contagious disease.
(Code 1968, § 11-1)
Sec. 10-4. - License.
All applicants for a business license who are within the purview of the preceding section shall exhibit to the clerk of the city the certificate described in said section, and upon any applicant's failure to produce such certificate the clerk shall not issue such license.
(Code 1968, § 11-2)
Sec. 10-5. - Approval prerequisite to operation of food establishment.
Before a business license is issued or renewed to any food establishment in the city, written approval from the county department of public health shall be on file with the city, stating that the premises have been inspected and that the establishment meets the requirements of the state food laws. Any such license shall be revoked upon the recommendation of said department of public health if a nuisance or health hazard is created or exists. A recommendation to revoke such license shall not be made, however, until the establishment has failed or refused to comply, within a reasonable time, with the rules and regulations applicable.
(Code 1968, § 11-7)
Charter reference— Power of mayor and council to prohibit nuisances, § 1-3(4).
State Law reference— Nuisances, Ga. Code Ann., title 41; nuisances in cities, § 41-2-5.
Sec. 10-6. - Issuance of permit.
Permits to handle food or food products shall be issued to a person upon said person's furnishing evidence, in the form of a public health certificate or doctor's certificate, that he does not have any type of contagious or communicable disease.
(Code 1968, § 11-6)
Sec. 10-7. - Food establishment required to have emergency phones.
Any food establishment in the city serving or handling food for consumption on the premises shall be required to provide within the premises a telephone for use by customers and employees at no charge for local calls in the event of an emergency.
(Ord. (W-5-94) of 8-2-94)
