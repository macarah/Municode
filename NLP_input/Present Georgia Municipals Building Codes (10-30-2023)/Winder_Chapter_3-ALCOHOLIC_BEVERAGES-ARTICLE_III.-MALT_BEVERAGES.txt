ARTICLE III. - MALT BEVERAGES
DIVISION 1. - EXCISE TAX

Sec. 3-100. - Excise tax—Imposed.
There is hereby imposed and levied an excise tax on malt beverages, in addition to the excise taxes levied by the state, in the sum of five cents ($0.05) per twelve (12) ounces, or proportionately thereof, so as to graduate the tax on bottles, cans and containers of various sizes. The excise tax on tap or draft malt beverages shall be in the sum of six dollars ($6.00) for each container sold as specified in this section, containing not more than fifteen and one-half (15½) gallons and at a like rate for fractional parts. The excise tax shall be imposed upon and shall be paid by the licensed wholesale dealer in malt beverages.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Secs. 3-101—3-105. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 3-106. - Types of beer, ale, or malt beverage licenses.
Licenses for the sale of beer, ale and other malt beverages shall be of three (3) kinds, to wit:
(1)
Manufacturer;
(2)
Wholesaler;
(3)
Retail by the package;
(4)
Retail for consumption on premises; and
(5)
Brew pubs.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-107. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to manufacture or sell or offer for sale at wholesale or retail in the city any beer, ale or other malt beverages without having obtained the license required in this chapter.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-108. - Effect of license—Manufacturer.
A manufacturer's license under this division will entitle the licensee to manufacture only malt beverages, at one (1) location only.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-109. - Same—Wholesale dealer.
A wholesale dealer's license under this division will entitle the licensee to sell only malt beverages, and only in the original unbroken container, to persons holding a retail license for the sale of malt beverages for the purpose of resale only.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-110. - Effect of retail dealer's license—Generally.
A retail dealer license for sale by the package under this article will entitle the licensee to sell only malt beverages, at one (1) location only, and only in the original and unopened container, and shall not permit the opening of containers in the package store where sold, and shall not permit the drinking of the contents of said containers in the package store where sold. All malt beverages possessed by a licensee shall be kept on the premises.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-111. - Effect of consumption license—Generally.
A consumption license under this article will entitle the licensee to sell only malt beverages at only one (1) location, and only by the drink for consumption on the premises. such sale is authorized in restaurants and private clubs, and in bars, pubs, taverns, brew pubs and public entertainment facilities in the downtown overlay district.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-112. - Ancillary growler malt beverage tasting license.
(a)
The holder of a malt beverages package sales license shall be eligible for an ancillary growler malt beverage tasting license to provide samples of malt beverages offered for sale to customers under the conditions set forth in this section.
(b)
Samples shall not exceed two (2) ounces per sample, and no customer shall consume more than ten (10) ounces per day. Sample shall be poured only by the licensee or his employees.
(c)
No open containers shall be removed from the licensed premises.
(d)
Growler malt beverage sampling and tasting is only permitted within the enclosed interior portion of the premises. The premises shall not be used for the sale of distilled spirits.
(e)
Minors under the age of twenty-one (21) are not allowed in the tasting portion of the premises.
(f)
Holders of an ancillary growler malt beverage tasting license shall not charge for samples or tastings.
(g)
The annual fee for an ancillary growler malt beverage tasting license fee shall be set and may be revised from time to time by the mayor and council.
( Ord. (W-3-15) of 6-2-15 )
Secs. 3-113—3-115. - Reserved.
