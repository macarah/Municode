ARTICLE X. - YARD SALES

Sec. 14-302. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Yard sale means the sale or trading of clothing, furniture, household items, food, dishes, antiques or a similar goods or merchandise on a residentially zoned lot or parcel or on the right-of-way immediately adjacent to that lot or parcel. Also, the term "yard sales" shall include sales or trading of goods as outlined in this definition, when 50 percent or more of the frontage of the block upon which the lot or parcel to be used fronts is still used for residential purposes, even if such lot or parcel to be used is not residentially zoned. The term "yard sale" shall include garage sales, carport sales and similar types of sales or events.
(Code 1966, § 16-30; Ord. No. 87-6, 11-9-1987)
Sec. 14-303. - Parking restricted.
The individual in charge of the premises upon which the sale is conducted shall control the parking of vehicles of patrons so as to avoid congestion and hazardous conditions. Failure to do so shall be sufficient cause for the sale to be immediately terminated.
(Code 1966, § 16-30; Ord. No. 87-6, 11-9-1987)
Sec. 14-304. - Location, duration, and number restricted.
Yard sales shall be allowed on a lot or parcel for not more than two sales not to exceed three days each during a 12-month period. No individual or firm shall be associated with or involved in more than two sales during a 12-month period. No individual or firm shall engage in the business of promoting or conducting yard sales for other persons for a fee or other consideration.
(Code 1966, § 16-30; Ord. No. 87-6, 11-9-1987)
Sec. 14-305. - Registration required; fee; standardized sign.
Prior to conducting any yard sale, not later than 12:00 noon on the preceding business day, the individual conducting the yard sale must register at city hall the fact that a yard sale is to be held, the location of the yard sale, the duration of the yard sale and the name of the individual conducting the yard sale. A registration fee shall be paid in the amount of $5.00. The city shall provide the individual conducting the yard sale with a standardized yard sale sign, which shall show proof that the registration fee has been paid. This sign shall then be displayed at the location of the yard sale for the duration of the yard sale.
(Code 1966, § 16-30; Ord. No. 87-6, 11-9-1987; Ord. No. 2012-06)
Sec. 14-306. - Advertising deemed for yard sale.
Advertising of merchandise for sale or trading, or display of goods and merchandise for public viewing, shall be deemed a yard sale when conducted in a residentially zoned area or on a block where 50 percent or more of the frontage on the block upon which the lot or parcel to be used fronts is still used for residential purposes, even if such lot or parcel to be so used is not residentially zoned. Such goods or merchandise do not need to be attended for a sale to be deemed to be in existence.
(Code 1966, § 16-30; Ord. No. 87-6, 11-9-1987)
Sec. 14-307. - Placement of signs restricted.
In conducting any yard sale, no signs shall be permitted to be placed upon public right-of-way property or highway utility poles. No signs shall be placed upon any private property without the owner's permission.
(Code 1966, § 16-30; Ord. No. 87-6, 11-9-1987)
