Sec. 46-19. - Department created.
The county parks and recreation department, referred to as the "department" in this article, is created and established by the county, pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 36-64-1, et seq.; and such department shall be organized and empowered as set forth in this article for the purpose of establishing and maintaining a system of supervised recreation within the county.
( Ord. No. 2019-006 , § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2019)
Sec. 46-20. - Board created.
The county parks and recreation board, referred to as the "parks and recreation board" in this article, is created and established by the county, pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 36-64-5; and shall be organized and empowered as set forth in this article.
( Ord. No. 2019-006 , § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2019)
Sec. 46-21. - Appointment of members; terms; vacancy; removal.
(a)
The parks and recreation board, when established, shall consist of seven appointed members, serving without pay. Five members shall be appointed by the county, one member shall be appointed by the city of Camilla, and one member shall be appointed by the city of Pelham for initial terms, as provided in subsection (b) of this section. The replacement terms of office for the members of the parks and recreation board shall be for five years or until their successors are appointed and qualified. Each member may be reappointed.
(b)
In order that the term of at least one member shall expire annually, the original appointments shall be made by the parties as follows:
(1)
The county, by and through its board of commissioners, shall appoint one member who resides in the geographical boundaries that coincide with Commissioner District 1 for the board of commissioners, for an initial term of five years;
(2)
The county, by and through its board of commissioners, shall appoint one member who resides in the geographical boundaries that coincide with Commissioner District 2 for the board of commissioners, for an initial term of four years;
(3)
The county, by and through its board of commissioners, shall appoint one member who resides in the geographical boundaries that coincide with Commissioner District 3 for the board of commissioners, for an initial term of three years;
(4)
The county, by and through its board of commissioners, shall appoint one member who resides in the geographical boundaries that coincide with Commissioner District 4 for the board of commissioners, for an initial term of two years;
(5)
The county, by and through its board of commissioners, shall appoint one member who resides in the geographical boundaries that coincide with Commissioner District 5 for the board of commissioners, for an initial term of one year;
(6)
The City of Camilla, by and through its mayor and council, shall appoint one member for an initial term of one year; and
(7)
The City of Pelham, by and through its mayor and council, shall appoint one member for an initial term of one year.
Upon the expiration of each initial term, replacement member positions on the parks and recreation board shall be each shall be appointed in the same manner as the initial appointments were made, for a five-year term.
(c)
All members of the parks and recreation board may be reimbursed for actual expense incurred in connection with their official duties.
(d)
Any vacancy on the parks and recreation board shall be filled for the unexpired term by the governing body whose appointee vacates the position.
(e)
Each governing body shall have complete authority to remove its appointed member(s) of the parks and recreation board for cause. Cause for removal includes, but is not limited to, a member's absence from three consecutive parks and recreation board meetings without just cause.
( Ord. No. 2019-006 , § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2019)
Sec. 46-22. - Purpose and responsibility.
(a)
The purpose of the parks and recreation board is to carry on a parks and recreation program for the citizens of the county without duplication of services and expense and with fairness and equity to all political subdivisions within the county. The parks and recreation board shall provide for the continual improvement of programs, facilities, and lands while consistently maintaining the existing facilities in an attractive, safe, and functional condition. The parks and recreation board shall accomplish this purpose by providing proposals and recommendations to the parks and recreation director, as provided for in this article.
(b)
The parks and recreation director shall ensure that the parks and recreation system is administered and operated in the best interests of all citizens of the county and that any funds appropriated for the department are expended in an effective and efficient manner.
( Ord. No. 2019-006 , § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2019)
Sec. 46-23. - Organization and rules.
(a)
The parks and recreation board shall elect its chairman and such other officers as are necessary from among its appointed members. The terms of the chairman and any such other officers shall be one year or until their successors are duly elected and qualified. Members elected to serve as chairman and any such other officers as are necessary may be reelected for successive terms for their elected positions.
(b)
The parks and recreation board shall appoint a secretary, who may be a member of the parks and recreation board or a non-voting official or employee of the county, to keep minutes of its meetings and perform such other functions as may be required.
(c)
The parks and recreation board shall make its own rules of procedure which are consistent with the law of the state and with the authority granted it by this article.
( Ord. No. 2019-006 , § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2019)
Sec. 46-24. - Powers and duties; budget; fees.
(a)
The parks and recreation board shall be vested, except as restricted in this article, with all powers and duties as provided for in O.C.G.A. §§ 36-64-3, 36-64-5, 36-64-6, and 36-64-11, as amended.
(b)
The parks and recreation board shall, each year, advise and assist the parks and recreation director in submitting in a timely manner to the board of commissioners a budget for the administration, operation, and maintenance of the parks and recreation system which shows expected expenditures in each of the areas for which the department is responsible. In addition, the parks and recreation board may advise and assist the parks and recreation director in presenting any proposal for capital improvements to the board of commissioners and/or the political subdivision whom owns title to the land or improvements in which a proposal is being made.
(c)
The parks and recreation board is empowered to establish, revise, and collect fees and rentals for the use of recreation areas and to impose conditions and set requirements governing such use. Accurate records of such fees shall always be maintained and open to inspection. Anticipated fees shall be accounted for in the annual budget.
(d)
Nothing in this section shall prevent the parks and recreation board from contracting with any local government entity or private party to operate, maintain, or provide services in the recreations areas.
( Ord. No. 2019-006 , § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2019)
Sec. 46-25. - Parks and recreation director and other employees; duties.
(a)
There shall be a full-time parks and recreation director to administer and oversee all work necessary to accomplish the mission and goals of the department. Where there is a vacancy in the position, the parks and recreation board may recommend the names of qualified individuals to the county administrator, who shall hire a parks and recreation director based on a job description drawn by the parks and recreation board and in accordance with any applicable county hiring policies and procedures. The county administrator may either hire one of the candidates recommended by the parks and recreation board, or the county administrator may reject any and all candidates recommended by the parks and recreation board and hire another qualified candidate in accordance with any applicable county hiring policies and procedures. The parks and recreation director shall not be eligible to serve as a member of the parks and recreation board. The parks and recreation director shall be considered a county employee for purposes of pay, benefits, sick leave, vacation, termination of employment, and other purposes. The parks and recreation directors shall be subject to direction, evaluation, and corrective action by the county administrator.
(b)
The duties of the parks and recreation director shall be to plan, organize, direct, and control a countywide recreation system pursuant to the policies of the parks and recreation board, as established in the budget approved by the board of commissioners. The parks and recreation director, with the advice and assistance of the parks and recreation board and as provided for in this article, shall prepare and present to the board of commissioners a suggested budget showing anticipated revenues and required expenditures.
(c)
Other employees and staff of the department shall be hired by the county administrator as it may deem necessary to properly and adequately administer the department, subject to the budget approved by the board of commissioners. Any such employees and staff shall be considered county employees for purposes of pay, benefits, sick leave, vacation, termination of employment, and other purposes.
( Ord. No. 2019-006 , § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2019)
Sec. 46-26. - Retention of title to land and capital improvements; operation and maintenance.
(a)
The county, cities, and any other political subdivision that may contribute land or capital improvements for use by the department shall, and do respectively, retain title to all their respective lands and capital improvements connected with and used in and by the department, and the parks and recreation board shall protect and maintain such properties. Further capital investment shall be made with respect to such property by the political subdivision having title thereto.
(b)
Notwithstanding the provisions of subsection (a) of this section, the parks and recreation board shall be responsible for the administration, operation, and maintenance of said land and capital improvements provided to the parks and recreation board for the purpose of a countywide recreation system.
(c)
The parks and recreation director shall report to the political subdivisions annually on the condition of their respective lands and capital improvements thereof and shall immediately notify them of any damage thereto or any condition requiring repair and may make any such recommendations thereof.
( Ord. No. 2019-006 , § 1(Exh. A), 12-10-2019)
