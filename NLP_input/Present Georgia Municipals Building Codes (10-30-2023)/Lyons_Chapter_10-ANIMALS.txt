ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 10-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Abandoned animal means any animal which shall have been placed upon public property or within a public building unattended or uncared for or upon or within the private property of another without the express permission of the owner, custodian or tenant of such private property and unattended or uncared for. An animal shall also be considered abandoned if the animal shall have been upon or within the property of the owner or custodian of the animal and unattended or without proper food and water for a period in excess of 36 hours.
Animal at large means an animal not under restraint.
Animal control center means the facility designated by the city manager for the detention of animals.
Animal control superintendent means that person designated by the city manager to perform the duties prescribed by this chapter.
Animal under restraint means any animal:
(1)
Secured by a leash or lead or enclosed by a fence or other enclosure;
(2)
Under the control of a responsible and competent person and obedient to that person's commands—that person being actually present with the animal; or
(3)
Confined within a parked vehicle or a vehicle in motion.
Companion animal means a domesticated animal kept as a pet for pleasure, rather than utility. Pets include but are not limited to birds, cats, dogs, hamsters, horses, mice, reptiles, domesticated wild animals, exotic animals and other animals associated with man's environment.
Domesticated animal means any animal which is accustomed to living in or about the habitation of humans, including but not limited to cats, cows, dogs, fowl, horses, swine, domesticated wild animals and/or exotic animals.
Guard dog means any dog which has been trained to attack persons or other animals independently or upon oral command or any dog which, while not so trained, is reasonably expected to perform as a guardian of the property upon and within which it is located.
Owner means any person owning, possessing, keeping, harboring or acting as custodian of a domesticated animal.
Vicious animal means:
(1)
Any animal with a propensity, tendency or disposition to:
a.
Attack;
b.
Cause injury to; or
c.
Otherwise endanger the safety of humans or other domestic animals.
(2)
Any animal which attacks a human being or other domestic animal one or more times without provocation; or
(3)
Any pit bull terrier, which is defined as any Staffordshire bull terrier breed of dog or any mixed breed of dog which contains an element of the breed of Staffordshire bull terrier or American Staffordshire terrier as to be identifiable as partially of the breed of Staffordshire bull terrier or American Staffordshire terrier by a qualified veterinarian duly licensed as such by the state.
(Code 1977, § 4-22)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 10-2. - Purpose.
The purpose of this chapter is to promote the health, safety, convenience, order, prosperity and welfare of the city's inhabitants. This chapter sets forth necessary controls on the unrestrained activities of animals which threaten the safety and pleasantness of streets, parks, sidewalks, yards and all areas of the city; and the lack of knowledge or intent is not a defense in the violation of this chapter.
(Code 1977, § 4-21)
Sec. 10-3. - Public nuisance.
(a)
Any animal is declared to be a public nuisance if the animal:
(1)
Is found at large in violation of section 10-4, 10-5, or 10-6;
(2)
Is vicious, as defined in section 10-1, and while on the property of its owner is not kept enclosed by a fence at least five feet in height, the sides of which are embedded into the ground no less than one foot and the gate of which is secured by a child-proof lock, and restrained by a leash or chain to prevent it from wandering out of the confined area or while, off the real property of its owner, is not muzzled and kept secured by a leash or chain held by a competent person;
(3)
Produces, because of quality, manner or method in which the animal is domesticated or maintained, unsanitary conditions in the city;
(4)
Attacks passersby or passing vehicles;
(5)
Makes or causes frequent or long continued noise which disturbs the comfort or repose of any person in the vicinity; or
(6)
Is inimical to the public health, welfare or safety according to rules and regulations promulgated by the county board of health.
(b)
Any person who knowingly keeps, owns, harbors or acts as custodian of an animal constituting a nuisance shall be guilty of an unlawful act and shall be punishable as provided in this chapter.
(Code 1977, § 4-23)
State Law reference— Dangerous Dog Control Law, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq.
Sec. 10-4. - Running at large.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for the owner of any animal or anyone having an animal in his custody to allow it to run at large unattended on or about the city streets and highways or on the property of another person without permission of the owner of the property or of the person in possession of that property.
(b)
Penalties for violation of subsection (a) of this section are as follows:
(1)
First offense .....$ 25.00
(2)
Second offense .....50.00
(3)
Third offense .....100.00
(4)
Fourth offense .....200.00
(Code 1977, §§ 4-24, 4-28(d))
Sec. 10-5. - Restraint on property.
It shall be the duty of every owner of any animal or anyone having any animal in his possession or custody to ensure that the animal is enclosed by a fence or other enclosure or is restrained by a chain or leash or in some other physical manner or under control by a competent person so that it cannot wander off the real property limits of its owner, possessor or custodian, except as provided in this chapter, it being the intent of this chapter that all animals be prevented from leaving while unattended the real property limits of their owners, possessors or custodians.
(Code 1977, § 4-25)
Sec. 10-6. - Restraint while off property.
It shall be the duty of the owner of any animal or anyone having an animal in his possession to keep the animal under control at all times while the animal is off the real property limits of the owner, possessor or custodian. For the purpose of this section, an animal is deemed under control when it is confined within a vehicle, parked or in motion; is secured by a leash or other device held by a competent person; or is properly confined within an enclosure with permission of the owner of the property where the enclosure is located.
(Code 1977, § 4-26)
Sec. 10-7. - Prohibited in city parks and buildings.
Animals are prohibited in city parks and city buildings; provided, however, that:
(1)
Every totally or partially blind person shall have the right to be accompanied by a guide dog, and every physically disabled person and every deaf person shall have the right to be accompanied by a service dog, especially trained for the purpose, in any city park or city building; provided, however, that such person shall be liable for any damage done to the premises or facilities by such dog. The guide dog or service dog must be identified as having been trained by a school for Seeing Eye, hearing, service or guide dogs.
(2)
Every person engaged in the training of a guide dog or service dog for the purpose of accompanying a person as provided in subsection (1) of this section shall have the same right to be accompanied by such dog being trained as the totally or partially blind person, deaf person or physically disabled person has under subsection (1) of this section so long as such trainer is identified as an agent or employee of a school for Seeing Eye, hearing, service or guide dogs.
(Code 1977, § 4-24.1)
Cross reference— Buildings and construction, ch. 14; parks and recreation, ch. 42.
State Law reference— Right of disabled persons to use dogs in certain places, O.C.G.A. § 30-4-2.
Sec. 10-8. - Wild animal permit.
(a)
No person shall have, keep or maintain any wild, exotic, dangerous or nondomestic animal or reptile without first applying to and receiving from the police department a permit. The charge for this permit shall be $10.00. No such permit shall be issued if a permit required by O.C.G.A. title 27, ch. 5 (O.C.G.A. § 27-5-1 et seq.) has not been obtained.
(b)
The police department is invested with the authority to issue a permit to any person for the keeping or maintaining of any wild, exotic, dangerous or nondomestic animal or reptile when, in the opinion of the police chief, any such animal or reptile may be kept or maintained without menacing the safety of any person or property. However, the police department may require any such animal or reptile to be properly caged or secured or may make additional rules or regulations regarding the keeping or maintaining of any such animal or reptile and may revoke any permit for any violation of any of the sections of this chapter or of any of the rules and regulations of the police department or when, in the opinion of the police chief, the safety of any person or property is menaced by the keeping of any such animal or reptile.
(Code 1977, §§ 4-38, 4-39)
Sec. 10-9. - Bird sanctuary.
(a)
The city is declared a bird sanctuary.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to kill, destroy, harm or molest wild birds or fowl or their nests or eggs, except when a nuisance or threat to the public health exists, and then only as authorized by the city council.
(Code 1977, § 4-1)
Sec. 10-10. - Legal number of dogs and cats.
It shall be unlawful and a public nuisance for any person in charge of a residence to keep or allow to be kept more than four dogs or four cats, or any combination of such animals exceeding four in number, over the age of 120 days at such residence unless the residence of all of the dogs and cats kept there are within a licensed kennel.
(Ord. of 5-9-2006, § 10-2.6)

Sec. 10-36. - Animal control center.
The city council shall designate an area to be known as the city animal control center.
(Code 1977, § 4-29(a))
Sec. 10-37. - Animal control superintendent.
(a)
The city manager shall appoint a person to be known as animal control superintendent to supervise such other persons necessary to operate the animal control center.
(b)
The animal control superintendent shall be charged with responsibilities, including but not limited to the following:
(1)
Cooperating with the rabies control officer and county board of health and assisting in the enforcement of state laws with regard to dogs and especially with regard to the vaccination of such dogs against rabies and the confinement or leashing of vicious dogs;
(2)
Investigating all complaints regarding animals;
(3)
Issuing warning citations to offenders of this chapter;
(4)
Enforcing, only through duly authorized personnel, all state laws, this Code and city ordinances for the care, control and custody of animals;
(5)
Exacting, collecting, reporting and remitting fees and costs; and
(6)
Supervising and being in charge of the city animal control center.
(Code 1977, § 4-29(b), (c))
Cross reference— Administration, ch. 2.
Sec. 10-38. - Violations.
Upon information made known to the chief of police that any owner of any animal is in violation of this chapter, he shall cause a summons to be issued requiring such owner of such animal to appear before the judge of the municipal court on a day and time certain, then and there to stand trial for violation of this chapter. If a violation of this chapter has been personally witnessed to the chief of police, a subpoena shall be issued to the person making the complaint to appear on the day and time set for trial, then and there to testify on behalf of the city.
(Code 1977, § 4-28(a))
Sec. 10-39. - Impoundment, redemption and destruction of animals.
(a)
Except as stated in this section, any animal within the city without an owner, any animal running at large at any time or any animal within the city whose owner fails to have the animal vaccinated by a rabies inspector or licensed veterinarian, in accordance with state laws, shall be impounded or confined in the animal control center for a period of 120 hours. Any animal not redeemed in accordance with this section shall be destroyed in a humane manner.
(b)
To the extent practicable, an animal running at large unattended and whose owner is known shall be taken to the owner's residence. If no adult person shall be present, it shall be taken to the animal control center. In either event, a redemption fee shall be paid by the owner in an amount of $25.00.
(c)
If the owner of any animal is not known and such animal is upon the public streets, alleys, sidewalks, school grounds or other public places or premises or upon the property of another without the property owner's permission as prohibited by this chapter, upon complaint made to or information made known to the animal control department, it shall be the duty of the animal control superintendent to take possession of the animal and impound it in the animal control center as provided in this section.
(d)
No later than two days after any animal has been taken to the animal control center, notice shall be given to its owner or, if the owner is unknown, notice shall be posted for five days, or until the animal is disposed of, on a bulletin board at city hall with a description of the animal and the time and place of taking the animal, together with the time and date of posting the notice. The time for the redemption of the animal as provided in this section shall not begin to run until such notice has been given or posted.
(e)
In order for an owner to redeem an animal, the owner must have the animal fully vaccinated for rabies by an authorized rabies inspector or licensed veterinarian, if it has not been so vaccinated within one year from the redemption date, and shall pay a penalty in the amount of $10.00, plus the applicable fees as follows:
(1)
Receiving at the pound .....$10.00
(2)
Housing, per day .....5.00
(3)
Redemption .....25.00
(f)
Any proceeds thus derived by the animal control center shall be turned over to the city clerk.
(Code 1977, §§ 4-28(b), (c), 4-30, 4-31)

ARTICLE III. - DOGS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 10-66. - Vaccinations for rabies.
All dogs within the incorporated city limits shall be inoculated by a licensed veterinarian for the prevention of rabies.
(Code 1977, § 4-27)
Sec. 10-67. - Guard dogs.
It shall be the duty of each owner, tenant or custodian of property upon or within which a guard dog is located to conspicuously and prominently post the following notice on the outside of the property:
Warning
Guard Dog
The notice shall be in either red or black letters upon a white background, large enough to be easily read at a distance of 25 feet. If the dog is in a building, the notice shall be conspicuously posted on or about each and every entrance and exit. If the guard dog is located in a general enclosure, the notices shall be conspicuously posted upon the enclosure at a distance not greater than 50 feet apart as well as upon each and every entrance and exit through the enclosure. If the guard dog is solely located within an apartment, a condominium or a building within a larger building, the notice shall be conspicuously posted on or about each and every entrance and exit. Failure to comply with this section shall be unlawful and shall be punishable as provided in this chapter.
(Code 1977, § 4-32)
Sec. 10-68. - Impoundment of rabies suspect or diseased dogs.
Any dog impounded which appears to be suffering from rabies or is affected with hydrophobia shall not be redeemed but shall be kept for ten days under observation. Thereafter if the animal control officer determines that the dog is affected with the disease, the dog shall be destroyed in a humane manner. Otherwise it shall be subject to redemption as provided in this chapter. Any dog which is suffering from any infectious, contagious or dangerous disease may be redeemed by the owner in order to provide proper treatment for the dog.
(Code 1977, § 4-33)
Sec. 10-69. - Confinement for symptoms of rabies or for biting.
(a)
Any dog which shows symptoms of rabies or which has bitten anyone shall be penned up immediately by its custodian, and the animal control center shall be notified immediately. The dog shall be confined in a place approved by the animal control superintendent and under conditions approved by him for not less than ten days, and the dog shall be subject to observation at all times during this period by a person designated by the animal control superintendent.
(b)
If rabies does not develop within this period, the dog shall be released to its owner. If rabies does develop, the dog shall be destroyed in a humane manner.
(Code 1977, § 4-34)

DIVISION 2. - VICIOUS DOGS

Sec. 10-76. - Requirements for possessing vicious dog or potentially vicious dogs.
(a)
It is unlawful for an owner to have or possess within this state and the City of Lyons a vicious dog or potentially vicious dog without a certificate of registration issued in accordance with the provisions of this Code section. This certificate shall be in addition to any standard dog license and fees or other requirements of this chapter.
(b)
Subject to the additional requirements of subsection (c) of this Code section for vicious dogs, the dog control officer shall issue a certificate of registration to the owner of such dog if the owner presents to the dog control officer or the dog control officer other wise finds sufficient evidence of:
(1)
A proper enclosure to confine the vicious dog or potentially vicious dog, section 10-3.
(2)
The posting of the premises where the vicious dog or potentially vicious dog is located with a clearly visible sign warning that there is a vicious dog on the property. This sign shall be designed by the department of natural resources and shall be posted in a conspicuous place so that it may be seen by the general public.
(c)
In addition to the requirements of subsection (b) of this section, the owner of a vicious dog shall present to the dog control officer evidence of:
(1)
A policy of insurance in the amount of at least $50,000.00 issued by an insurer authorized to transact business in this state insuring the owner of the vicious dog against liability for any personal injuries inflicted by the vicious dog; or
(2)
A surety bond in the amount of $50,000.00 or more issued by a surety company authorized to transact business in this state payable to any person or persons injured by the dangerous dog.
(d)
The owner of a vicious dog or potentially vicious dog shall notify the dog control officer within eight hours if the dog is on the loose, is unconfined, has attacked a human, has dies or has been sold or donated. If the dog has been sold or donated, the owner shall also provide the dog control officer with the name, address and telephone number of the new owner of the dog.
(e)
The owner of a vicious dog or potentially vicious dog shall notify the dog control officer if the owner is moving from the City of Lyons. The owner of a vicious dog or potentially vicious dog who moves from this city to another jurisdiction with the state of Georgia shall register the vicious dog or potentially vicious dog in the new jurisdiction within ten days after becoming a resident.
(f)
Issuance of a certificate of registration or renewal of a certificate of registration by the city does not warrant or guarantee that the requirements specified in subsections (b) or (c) of this section are maintained by the owner of a vicious dog or potentially vicious dog on a continuous basis following the date of issuance of the initial certificate of registration or following the date of any annual renewal of such certificate.
(g)
A dog control officer is authorized to make whatever inquiry is deemed necessary to ensure compliance with the provisions of this chapter.
(h)
The City of Lyons does hereby levy and charge against the owner of any vicious dog for the purpose of registration pursuant to this section, and in addition to any other license fees required generally for dogs within the city, the sum of $25.00 as a fee to register the dog as a vicious dog pursuant to the ordinances of the city. All certificates of registration shall be renewed on and annual basis. Annual renewal shall be at such time as all other business license is due (January 1—March 31). At the time of any renewal, the animal control officer shall require evidence from the owner or make such investigation as may be necessary from the owner or make such investigation as may be necessary to verify that the vicious dog, or potentially vicious dog, is continuing to be confined in a proper enclosure and that the owner is continuing to comply with all of the provisions of this Code which might apply to this situation.
(Ord. of 5-9-2006, § 10-2.1)
Sec. 10-77. - Restrictions on permitting vicious or potentially vicious dogs to be outside proper enclosure.
(a)
It is unlawful for an owner of a vicious dog to permit the dog to be outside a proper enclosure unless the dog is muzzled and restrained by a substantial chain or leash and is under the physical restraint of a responsible person. The muzzle shall be made in a manner that will not cause injury to the dog or interfere with its vision or respiration but will prevent it from biting any person.
(b)
It is unlawful for the owner of a potentially vicious dog to permit the dog to be outside a proper enclosure unless the dog is restrained by a substantial chain or leash and is under the restraint of a responsible person.
(Ord. of 5-9-2006, § 10-2.2)
Sec. 10-78. - Confiscation of dogs; grounds; disposition.
(a)
A vicious dog shall be immediately confiscated by the dog control officer or by a law enforcement officer or by another person authorized by the city:
(1)
Owner of the dog does not secure the liability insurance or bond required by subsection 10-76(c);
(2)
Dog is not validly registered by section subsection 10-76(a);
(3)
Dog is not maintained in a proper enclosure; or
(4)
Dog is outside a proper enclosure in violation of subsection 10-3(a)(2)
(b)
A potentially vicious dog shall be confiscated in the same manner as a vicious dog if the dog violates any part of section 10-3.
(c)
Any dog that has been confiscated under the provisions of subsection (a) or (b) of this Code section shall be returned to its owner upon the owner's compliance with the provisions of this article and upon the payment of reasonable confiscation costs. In the event the owner has not complied with the provisions of this article within 120 hours the animal control officer will follow guidelines set forth in section 10-39. (Impoundment, redemption and destruction of animals)
(Ord. of 5-9-2006, § 10-2.3)
Sec. 10-79. - Violations; penalties.
(a)
The owner of a vicious dog who violates the applicable provisions of sections 10-76 or 10-77 or whose vicious dog is subject to confiscation under subsection 10-78(a), be guilty of a misdemeanor of high and aggravated nature. In addition to any confinement that might be imposed for a conviction under this subsection, for a second conviction a fine of not less than $500.00 shall be imposed and for a third or subsequent conviction a fine of not less than $750.00 shall be imposed.
(b)
The owner of a potentially vicious dog who violates the applicable provisions of sections 10-76 or 10-77 or whose potentially vicious dog is subject to confiscation under subsection 10-78(a), shall be guilty of a misdemeanor. In addition to any confinement that might be imposed for a conviction under this subsection, for a second conviction a fine of not less than $150.00 shall be imposed and for a third or subsequent conviction a fine of not less than $300.00 shall be imposed.
(c)
If an owner who has a previous conviction for a violation of this article knowingly and willfully fails to comply with the provisions of this division, such owner shall be guilty of a felony if the owner's vicious dog attacks or bites a human being under circumstances constituting another violation of this division. The owner of a vicious dog who is convicted for a violation of this subsection shall be punished by a fine of not less than $1,000.00 nor more than $5,000.00 or by imprisonment for not less than one nor more than five years or by both such fine and imprisonment.
(d)
In addition to the penalties for violations under subsection (c) or (d) herein, the vicious dog involved shall be immediately confiscated by the dog control officer or by a law enforcement officer or another person authorized by the city and placed in quarantine for the proper length of time as determined by the county board of health, and, thereafter, the vicious dog shall be destroyed in and expeditious and humane manner.
(e)
No owner of a vicious dog shall be held criminally liable under this division for injuries inflicted by said owner's dog to any human being while on the owner's properties.
(Ord. of 5-9-2006, § 10-2.4)
Sec. 10-80. - Liability for damages.
It is the intent of the State of Georgia and the City of Lyons that the owner of a vicious dog or potentially vicious dog shall be solely liable for injury to or death of a person caused by such a dog. Under no circumstances shall the city or any employee or official which enforces or fails to enforce the provisions of this division be held liable for any damages to any person who suffers and injury inflicted by a dog or by a dog that has been identified as being a vicious or potentially vicious dog or by a dog that has been reported to the proper authorities as being a vicious or potentially vicious dog or by a dog that the city has failed to identify as a vicious dog or potentially vicious dog but has not been kept or restrained in the manner described in subsection 10-3(2) or by a vicious dog or potentially vicious dog whose owner has not maintained insurance coverage or a surety bond as required by subsection 10-76(c). Enacted by GA. L. 1989, p. 159, section 5).
(Ord. of 5-9-2006, § 10-2.5)

ARTICLE IV. - LIVESTOCK

Sec. 10-96. - Keeping, maintaining, raising or feeding.
It shall be unlawful to keep, maintain, raise or feed any livestock within the city, except as authorized in this article.
(Code 1977, § 4-2)
Sec. 10-97. - Maximum period for keeping or maintaining.
Except as authorized in this article, it shall be unlawful to maintain or keep or cause to be maintained or kept within the city any livestock for any purpose whatsoever for a period longer than 48 hours, including the keeping or maintaining of any such animals for the purpose of sale or shipping.
(Code 1977, § 4-3)
Sec. 10-98. - Animal hospital conditions.
(a)
Livestock, while under medical treatment at an animal hospital operated by a licensed veterinarian, may be kept within the city only when:
(1)
Screened in;
(2)
Proper fly extermination measures are practiced;
(3)
The city sewer system is not stopped up or damaged in carrying off the waste products of such animals; and
(4)
The premises are maintained in a sanitary manner.
(b)
Any animal hospital shall use a flow of water in cleaning the premises sufficient to prevent stopping and clogging up the city sewer system. In addition to punishment of the offending party as provided in section 1-7, the city shall assess the offending party with the full expense to the city, including labor and materials, caused by stoppage of the sewer system due to waste products of any animals.
(c)
It shall be unlawful to maintain any animal hospital in the city without having first obtained a certificate or written statement from the state health department that proper sanitary standards are maintained.
(Code 1977, §§ 4-4—4-6)

Sec. 10-126. - Teasing, molesting and poisoning.
It shall be unlawful for any person to tease, molest, bait, poison or in any way bother any animal not belonging to him or legally under his control.
(Code 1977, § 4-35)

It shall be unlawful for any person injuring a companion animal by running over it or into the animal or coming into contact with it with an automobile, motorcycle, bicycle or other vehicle to fail to immediately notify the owner of the animal, the animal control center or the police department.
(Code 1977, § 4-36)

It shall be unlawful for anyone to knowingly abandon any animal within the city. Each person who does abandon knowingly or who willingly permits such abandonment or who aids in the abandonment of any animal shall be in violation of the law.
(Code 1977, § 4-37)
