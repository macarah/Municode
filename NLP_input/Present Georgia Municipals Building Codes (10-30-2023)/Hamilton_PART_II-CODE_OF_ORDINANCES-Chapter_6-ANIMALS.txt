ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 6-1. - Provisions incorporated by reference.
The animal control ordinance of the county, currently codified as chapter 5, article III in the Harris County Code, is adopted by this reference and incorporated in this chapter and made a part of this Code in as full and complete a manner and with like effect as though set out in full in this section.
Sec. 6-2. - Public nuisance animals.
(a)
The harboring of any animal found to be dangerous or a public nuisance is hereby prohibited.
(b)
The classification of a dangerous or public nuisance animal will be determined by the county animal control officer under the existing intergovernmental agreement between the city and the county.
(Ord. No. 2006-11-13, § 1, 11-13-2006; Ord. No. 2015-11-10, § 1, 12-14-2015)
Sec. 6-3. - Penalties.
Any person who shall violate the provisions of this article, upon conviction, shall be subject to a penalty according to the penalties described in the general provisions chapter of this Code. Any designated representative of the city police department, county animal control officer, or the county sheriff's department may enforce the provisions of this article. All fines so assessed shall be recovered for use of the city through the municipal court.
(Ord. No. 2006-11-13, § 3, 11-13-2006)
Secs. 6-4—6-24. - Reserved.

State Law reference— Responsible Dog Ownership Law, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq.

Sec. 6-25. - Responsibility of owner to keep dog under control by leash or confinement.
Dogs in the city are required to be penned up, fenced in, chained, or otherwise confined to the premises of the owner or, if off the premises of the owner, must be leashed and under the immediate control of the owner. If any dog shall be at large and not on a leash and under the immediate control of the owner thereof, the owner of the property on which any such dog running at large may enter may take such action to remove such dog as is reasonable under the circumstances.
(Ord. No. 90-4-9, § 1, 4-9-1990)

ARTICLE III. - LIVESTOCK[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— Livestock running at large or straying, O.C.G.A. § 4-3-1 et seq.

Sec. 6-54. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Livestock means domestic animals and fowl customarily kept on a farm including, but not limited to, horses, mules, donkeys, cattle, sheep, goats, ducks, geese, chickens, and turkeys.
(Ord. No. 2006-11-13, § 1, 11-13-2006; Ord. No. 2015-11-10, § 2(A), 12-14-2015)
Sec. 6-55. - Space requirements.
No livestock shall be permitted on a lot containing less than four acres. On lots greater than four and less than ten acres, there shall be allowed one livestock animal per acre, or its portion. For lots in excess of ten acres, there shall be no restriction as to the number of livestock animals allowed.
(Ord. No. 2006-11-13, § 1, 11-13-2006; Ord. No. 2015-11-10, § 2(B), 12-14-2015)
Sec. 6-56. - Proximity to property lines.
On any lot upon which livestock are lawfully kept as allowed in this section, all such livestock shall be kept at least 50 feet from any property line.
(Ord. No. 2006-11-13, § 1, 11-13-2006; Ord. No. 2015-11-10, § 2(C), 12-14-2015)
