ARTICLE II. - ROAD NAMES AND ADDRESSES

Sec. 54-19. - Violations.
If the owner or occupant of, or other person responsible for, a house or building neglects or refuses to comply with the terms of this article, an official notice shall be served to the owner, occupant, or such other person, at the address. The owner, occupant or other person shall have ten days to bring the property into compliance after which the owner, occupant or other person responsible for such house or building shall be charged with violation of this article, and, if convicted, may be punished as provided in section 1-14.
(Code 2004, § 58-31; Ord. No. 2001-018, § 4, 12-11-2001)
Sec. 54-20. - Enforcement.
The responsibility for making inspections provided in this article, and for the enforcement of the provisions of this article, shall rest upon the county sheriff.
(Code 2004, § 58-32; Ord. No. 2001-018, § 5, 12-11-2001)
Sec. 54-21. - Assignment of road names, requests for names or changes.
(a)
All existing and/or new public roads and/or streets within the unincorporated boundaries of the county shall be assigned a name approved by the board of commissioners as set forth by the county subdivision regulations, which are presented in chapter 62.
(b)
All requests for road and/or street names and/or changes shall be submitted to the county planning commission and forwarded to the board of commissioners for their review and approval. All requests for road names and/or street names shall conform to the requirements of the county subdivision regulations, as set forth in chapter 62.
(Code 2004, § 58-33; Ord. No. 2001-018, § 1, 12-11-2001)
Sec. 54-22. - Designation of road and/or street numbers.
(a)
Road and/or street numbers for each dwelling and place of business on all public and private roads shall be assigned by the building official of the county or his designee in accordance with the uniform system of numbering structures in effect within the county.
(b)
The building official, in conjunction with the county 9-1-1 system, shall keep a record of all numbers that are assigned under this article.
(c)
No building or structure for which a house number is required shall be occupied prior to the assignment and display of such number and numbers shall be affixed in the proper manner prior to electricity being connected to the building.
(d)
All persons requiring a house number assignment prior to the occupancy must apply for such number at least three working days in advance of occupancy.
(Code 2004, § 58-34; Ord. No. 2001-018, § 2, 12-11-2001)
Sec. 54-23. - Posting of designated road and/or street addresses.
(a)
The owner or occupant of, or other person responsible for, any house or building to which a number has been assigned shall be notified by the county building official or his designee.
(b)
Within seven days after the receipt of such notification, the owner or occupant of, or other person responsible for, a house or building to which a number has been assigned shall affix the number in a conspicuous place as set forth in subsection (d) of this section.
(c)
It shall be the responsibility of each owner or occupant of, or other person responsible for, a house or building to remove any different number which might be mistaken for, or confused with, the number assigned to such structure by the building official or his designee.
(d)
It shall be the responsibility of each owner or occupant of, or other person responsible for, a house or building to which a number has been assigned to display such number in figures at least 2½ inches high and in such a manner that such figures are readable, at the following locations:
(1)
The number shall be posted on the house or building, at its main entrance or such other place as will render it visible from the right-of-way, driveway or other usual means of approaching the house or building from the closest public road, and in such manner that the numbers are readable from 100 feet; and
(2)
If the property receives rural mail delivery, the number shall posted on the mailbox; or if the property does not receive rural mail delivery, the number shall be posted at the entrance of the driveway from the right-of-way.
However, if the house or building is located less than 100 feet from the public right-of-way and if the number is clearly readable from such public right-of-way, then the number need only be posted on the house or building in accordance with subsection (d)(1) of this section.
(Code 2004, § 58-35; Ord. No. 2001-018, § 3, 12-11-2001)

ARTICLE III. - TIMBER OPERATIONS[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Local governing authority may require notice of timber harvesting and bond or letter of credit from harvesting entity, O.C.G.A. § 12-6-24.

Sec. 54-50. - Violations.
Violations of this article are punishable as provided in section 1-14. Prosecutions for violations of this article shall be upon citation as provided in O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63 or upon accusation by the county attorney or such other attorney as the board of commissioners shall designate.
(Code 2004, § 58-71; Ord. of 5-11-1993, §§ 7, 8)
Sec. 54-51. - Cash bond required.
Prior to beginning a tree harvesting operation in the county, all loggers shall post a cash bond in the amount set from time to time by the board of commissioners with the clerk of the board for each and every separate tract of land from which trees are to be removed. For each such tract, the logger shall also provide:
(1)
His name, address and telephone number.
(2)
The name, address and telephone number of the foreman on each tract.
(3)
The name, address and telephone number of the owner of each tract.
(4)
The approximate location of the site and the county roads that will be used to get to the site.
(5)
The route that will be used to haul trees from the site to the nearest state route.
(6)
The anticipated date for the commencement of the removal of trees for each tract.
(7)
The anticipated date for the completion of the removal of trees for each tract.
(Code 2004, § 58-72; Ord. of 5-11-1993, § 1)
Sec. 54-52. - Culverts.
Upon receipt of the information required by section 54-51, the county road superintendent shall inspect each site of a tree harvesting operation to determine if a culvert is required to provide ingress and egress from the county road to the tract and, if so, the length and diameter of the culvert. Should a culvert be required, it shall be installed at the expense of the logger under the supervision of the road superintendent. Loggers shall have the right to remove any culvert installed upon completion of tree removal.
(Code 2004, § 58-73; Ord. of 5-11-1993, § 2)
Sec. 54-53. - Duties of loggers.
During the removal of trees from the tract of a tree harvesting operation, the logger shall:
(1)
Conduct all loading of logs at a site off the county right-of-way and behind the established ditch line maintained by the county.
(2)
Refrain from loading or skidding any logs upon or across the county's right-of-way.
(3)
Keep ditches clear of debris and residue so as to not interfere with proper drainage.
(4)
Keep the road adjacent to the site clean and serviceable at all times to accommodate the passing public, emergency vehicles, school buses, mail carriers, etc.
(5)
Use necessary means to prevent trucks and other equipment from transporting mud from the site on to the adjacent roadway to such an extent as creates a danger to the passing public.
(6)
Notify the clerk of the board within three days of the completion of tree removal at each site, whereupon the road superintendent shall inspect the site. Should the road superintendent determine that the road and right-of-way is in as good condition as prior to the commencement of tree removal, then the bond shall be void and the logger's money returned. If corrective repairs are necessary, the reasonable cost thereof shall be deducted from the bond and the remainder, if any, shall be returned to the logger.
(Code 2004, § 58-74; Ord. of 5-11-1993, § 5)
Sec. 54-54. - Cessation of operations.
The board authorizes the road superintendent to order all logging operations to cease if it is determined that a logger is failing to comply with this article.
(Code 2004, § 58-75; Ord. of 5-11-1993, § 6)
Secs. 54-55—54-73. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - DRIVEWAYS

Sec. 54-74. - Driveway permit.
(a)
Property owners shall secure a permit from the county for the construction of driveways leading from county roads, to give access to their property.
(b)
Application for permit shall be made on the appropriate county forms. Forms may be obtained from the building department.
(c)
A permit approving driveway construction shall be issued by the building inspector or his designee.
(d)
A fee shall be charged to the applicant to cover the cost of processing the permit application. The fee shall be in an amount established by the board of commissioners from time to time.
( Ord. No. 2021-002 , § 1(Exh. A), 1-13-2021)
Sec. 54-75. - Specifications.
(a)
The building inspector shall maintain minimum specifications for driveways to properly tie into the shoulder point of the county road.
(b)
A driveway shall not exceed 50 feet in length.
(c)
There shall be no less than 12 feet between driveways.
(d)
A dead ramp may be used on the break of a hill not to exceed 50 feet in length.
(e)
Slopes shall be placed on the county right-of-way and shall be no steeper than 4:1.
(f)
Only safety headwalls or those specifically approved by the building inspector or his designee, may be used.
( Ord. No. 2021-002 , § 1(Exh. A), 1-13-2021)
Sec. 54-76. - Driveway pipe and slope.
(a)
Upon receipt of the application, the building inspector or his designee, in conjunction with road department, shall inspect the site to determine if a driveway pipe is required and that the slope is in accordance with county standards. If a pipe is required, the building inspector shall establish the size and length of pipe needed.
(b)
If a driveway pipe is needed, it shall be furnished and installed by the applicant under the supervision of the building inspector, or his designee, to ensure installation in accordance with county standards.
( Ord. No. 2021-002 , § 1(Exh. A), 1-13-2021)
Sec. 54-77. - Maintenance and repair.
(a)
The county will maintain driveways as needed in the course of regularly scheduled road maintenance up to the edge of the county right-of-way.
(b)
The county will repair any damage caused by the county to a driveway pipe within the county right-of-way during the course of county-approved maintenance.
( Ord. No. 2021-002 , § 1(Exh. A), 1-13-2021)
Sec. 54-78. - Enforcement and penalties.
(a)
The building inspector, or his designee, shall be authorized to revoke a driveway permit for failure to comply with any provision of this article and close a noncompliant driveway until compliance is obtained.
(b)
Where compliance is not obtained within a reasonable period of the closure of a noncompliant driveway, the building inspector, or his designee, shall be authorized to issue a citation. Upon conviction of a citation in a court of competent jurisdiction, the convicted person shall be subject to a fine and/or imprisonment as provided in section 1-14.
( Ord. No. 2021-002 , § 1(Exh. A), 1-13-2021)
