ARTICLE V. - HOTEL-MOTEL TAX

Sec. 10-154. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the following words, terms and phrases shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except when the context clearly indicates a contrary meaning.
Due date means the 20th day after the close of the monthly period for which the tax is to be computed.
Guestroom means a room occupied, or intended, arranged or designed for occupancy by one or more occupants for the purpose of living quarters or residential use.
Hotel-motel means:
(1)
Any structure or any portion of structure, including any lodginghouse, studio, hotel, motel, motor hotel, auto court, inn, public club, lodge, tourist camp, tourist cabin or private club containing guestrooms and which is occupied; or
(2)
Any other place in which rooms, lodgings or accommodations are regularly furnished for value.
Occupancy means the use or possession, or the right to the use or possession, of any room or apartment in a hotel or motel, or the right to the use or possession of the furnishings or to the services and accommodations accompanying the use and possession of the room or apartment.
Occupant means any person who, for a consideration, uses, possesses, or has the right to use or possess, any room in a hotel or motel under any lease, concession, permit, right of access, license, agreement or otherwise.
Operator means any person operating a hotel-motel in the city including, but not limited to:
(1)
The owner or proprietor of the premises;
(2)
Lessee;
(3)
Sublessee;
(4)
Lender in possession;
(5)
Licensee; or
(6)
Any other person otherwise operating the hotel-motel.
Permanent resident means any occupant who as of a given date shall have occupied, or has or shall have the right of occupancy, of any guestroom in a hotel-motel for more than ten consecutive days.
Person.
(1)
The term "person" means an individual, firm, partnership, joint venture, association, social club, fraternal organization, joint stock company, corporation, nonprofit corporation or cooperative nonprofit membership, estate, trust, business trust receiver, trustee, syndicate, business entity, or any other group or combination acting as a unit, to include the plural as well as the singular number.
(2)
The term "person" does not include the federal or state government or any political subdivision thereof the taxing of which is beyond city authority.
Rent means:
(1)
The considerations or value received in money or otherwise, including all receipts, cash, credits and property or services of any kind or nature; and
(2)
The amount allowed for credit by the operator to the occupant, without any deduction therefrom.
Return means any return filed or required to be filed as provided in this article.
Seminar or training group means any group, association, party, class, family, business, school, religious group or other aggregation of room occupants where each of the following criteria is present:
(1)
The group size is equal to or greater than eight individuals;
(2)
The length of stay is equal to or greater than two days;
(3)
The rooms and meeting rooms are furnished as a single contract price, either on a group basis or a per-individual basis; and
(4)
The purpose of the contract for accommodations and meeting rooms is to bring the aggregation of individuals together for a business purpose of training, meeting, convening or other business purpose.
(Code 1989, § 11-50; Ord. of 10-12-1992)
Sec. 10-155. - Levied.
(a)
Amount; due date. There is hereby set and levied on each occupant of a guestroom of any hotel located within the city a tax in the amount of eight percent of the rent for the occupancy. The tax imposed in this section shall be paid upon any occupancy occurring on or after December 1, 2014, although the occupancy was had pursuant to a contract, lease or other agreement entered into prior to the date.
(b)
Exemptions. [No] tax shall be levied under this section for the use of:
(1)
Charges made for any rooms, lodgings, or accommodations provided to any persons who certify that they are staying in such room, lodging, or accommodation as a result of the destruction of their home or residence by fire or other casualty;
(2)
The use of meeting rooms and other such facilities or any rooms, lodgings, or accommodations provided without charge;
(3)
Any rooms, lodgings, or accommodations furnished for a period of one or more days for use by Georgia state or local governmental officials or employees when traveling on official business. Notwithstanding the availability of any other means of identifying the person as a state or local government official or employee, whenever a person pays for any rooms, lodgings, or accommodations with a state or local government credit or debit card, such rooms, lodgings, or accommodations shall be deemed to have been furnished for use by a Georgia state or local government official or employee traveling on official business for purposes of the exemption provided by this paragraph. For purpose of the exemption provided under this paragraph, a local government official or employee shall include officials or employees of counties, municipalities, consolidated governments, or county or independent school districts; or
(4)
Charges made for continuous use of any rooms, lodgings, or accommodations after the first 30 days of continuous occupancy.
(Code 1989, § 11-51; Ord. of 10-12-1992; Ord. No. 2014-03, § 1, 10-6-14)
Sec. 10-156. - Duty of operator to collect.
It shall be the duty of every operator of a hotel located within the incorporated area of the city to collect the tax on occupants as imposed in this article.
(Code 1989, § 11-52; Ord. of 10-12-1992)
Sec. 10-157. - Exemption for permanent residents and government employees.
Notwithstanding any other provision of this section, no tax shall be imposed under this article upon permanent residents, state or local government officials or employees when traveling on official business, or seminar or training groups.
(Code 1989, § 11-53; Ord. of 10-12-1992)
Sec. 10-158. - Operator registration required.
Every person engaging in or about to engage in business as an operator of a hotel-motel in the city shall immediately register the business with the city manager on a form provided by the city manager for the purpose.
(1)
Contents. The required registration under this section shall set forth the name under which the operator transacts business or intends to transact business, the location of his place of business, and the other information as would facilitate the collection of the tax by the city.
(2)
Signatures. The registration shall be signed by the owner (if a natural person), by an officer (if a corporation) or a partner (if a partnership).
(3)
For each place of business. A separate registration shall be required for each place of business of an operator.
(Code 1989, § 11-54; Ord. of 10-12-1992)
Sec. 10-159. - Due date; report required.
All taxes levied by this article shall be due and payable to the city monthly on or before the 20th day of every month next succeeding each respective month in which the taxes are collected, and payment shall be accompanied by a return for the preceding monthly period showing the gross taxable rent, and the other information as may be required by the city.
(Code 1989, § 11-55; Ord. of 10-12-1992)
Sec. 10-160. - Recordkeeping.
Each operator collecting a tax under the provisions of this article shall keep for a period of at least three years all records, receipts, invoices and other pertinent documents of whatever media, setting forth:
(1)
The rental charged for each occupancy;
(2)
The date of occupancy; and
(3)
Any other information as the city may require.
(Code 1989, § 11-55; Ord. of 10-12-1992)
Sec. 10-161. - Operator permitted deduction as reimbursement for collection.
Operators collecting the tax levied under this article shall be allowed a percentage of the tax due and accounted for and shall be reimbursed in the form of a deduction in submitting, reporting and payment of the amount due, if the amount is not delinquent at the time of payment. The rate of the deduction shall be three percent of the amount collected.
(Code 1989, § 11-56; Ord. of 10-12-1992)
Sec. 10-162. - Penalty and interest.
The operator, in the event of operator's failure to pay tax when due, shall pay a penalty of 15 percent of the amount due plus interest on the total amount of delinquent taxes at the rate of 8½ percent per annum.
(Code 1989, § 11-57; Ord. of 10-12-1992)
Sec. 10-163. - Disposition of proceeds to comply with state law.
The proceeds of the tax collected pursuant to this article shall be expended as follows:
(a)
An amount equal to the amount of total taxes collected which would have been collected at a rate of five percent shall be expended in accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(a)(3);
(b)
An amount equal to not less than 50 percent of the total amount of taxes collected that exceed the amount of taxes that would be collected at the rate of five percent shall be expended for promoting tourism, conventions, and trade shows by a destination marketing organization designated by the City of Hartwell pursuant to ordinance or resolution; and
(c)
The remaining amount of taxes collected that exceed the amount of taxes that would be collected at the rate of five percent which are not otherwise expended under paragraphs (a) and (b) of this section shall be expended for tourism product development.
As used in this section, the terms "destination marketing organization" and "tourism product development" shall have the definitions set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-50.2.
(Ord. No. 2005-04, § 1(11-58), 6-6-2005; Ord. No. 2014-03, § 2, 10-6-14)
Sec. 10-164. - Amendments submitted to state department of community affairs.
Any amendments to this article shall be submitted to the state department of community affairs within 30 days of the date on which the ordinance implementing such amendment is approved and adopted.
(Ord. No. 2005-04, § 1(11-59), 6-6-2005)
