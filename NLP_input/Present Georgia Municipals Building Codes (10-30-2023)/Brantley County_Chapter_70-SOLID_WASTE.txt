Sec. 70-61. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Garbage means household or office waste, to include, paper, cardboard, tin cans, food waste, glass, plastic containers, cloth, fruit and vegetable refuse, and such other nondescript items of household waste as may be otherwise disposable after human use.
Litter means all sand, gravel, slag, brickbats, rubbish, waste material, refuse, trash, debris, dead animals, furniture, appliances, garbage, or discarded materials of every kind and description.
(Res. of 5-15-1991, § 2)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 70-62. - Use of waste receptacles.
No person shall:
(1)
Deposit, place or leave any items other than garbage in any receptacle (dumpster) provided by the county for the collection of garbage;
(2)
Dump, deposit, throw, leave or cause the dumping, depositing, throwing, placing or leaving of garbage or litter in the immediate area of any garbage collection receptacle;
(3)
Place, throw or deposit any animal carcass, or any live animal in any garbage collection receptacle;
(4)
Place, throw or deposit any burning or smoldering material in any garbage collection receptacle;
(5)
Set fire to the contents of any garbage collection receptacle;
(6)
Deposit, place or leave any explosive or spontaneous combustible materials in any garbage collection receptacle;
(7)
Sort through, scatter or remove any of the garbage which has been placed and left in a garbage collection receptacle. This subsection shall not apply to any county employee or agent who is in the performance of his official duties;
(8)
Enter, climb in or on, or go into any garbage collection receptacles. This subsection shall not apply to any county employee or agent who is in the performance of his official duties; or
(9)
Deposit, place or leave any items in a garbage collection receptacle unless that party is a resident of the county. However, this subsection shall not apply to a nonresident who is traveling through the county, and who has not entered the county for the purpose of disposing of garbage.
(10)
Place, deposit, or leave or cause the placing, depositing or leaving of any motor vehicle tire in any garbage collection receptacle, or in the immediate area of any garbage collection receptacle.
(Res. of 5-15-1991, § 3; Ord. of 3-12-2002, § 1)
Sec. 70-63. - Littering prohibited.
(a)
No person, company or corporation shall dump, deposit, throw, or leave or cause or permit the dumping, depositing, placing, throwing, or leaving of litter or garbage or tires on any property of the county, unless the property or location is designated for same, i.e., garbage collection receptacles, trash cans, and/or the county solid waste transfer site during operating hours, and only such items as are specifically approved for deposit at such locations.
(b)
Motor vehicle tires may be deposited at the county's solid waste transfer station without being a violation of this section upon payment of the disposal fee as hereafter set forth:
EXPAND
(1) Passenger tires $2.00 each off the rim, with an additional $1.00 on the rim
(2) Big truck tires $7.00 each off the rim, with an additional $4.00 on the rim
(3) Tractor tires $25.00 each
(4) Big tractor tires $35.00 each
(5) Skidder tires $65.00
  (c)
No person, company, or corporation shall, with intent, dump, deposit, throw or leave or cause or permit, the dumping, depositing, placing, throwing or leaving litter or garbage on private property.
(d)
A reward of $1,000.00 shall be paid by the county commission to any person who provides information leading to the citation and conviction of any person(s) and/or entity violating this section.
(Res. of 5-15-1991, § 4; Ord. of 3-12-2002, § 2; Amd. of 3-3-2016(2))
Sec. 70-64. - Penalties.
Any person violating this article, or any provision thereof, upon conviction as heretofore provided in 70-65, Enforcement, the defendant shall be punished by a mandatory penalty of not less than 40, nor more than 100 hours of community service. However, in keeping with a defendant's health status, upon evidence submitted by the defendant, as an alternative punishment, shall be a mandatory fine of not less than $350.00 nor more than $1,000.00.
(Res. of 5-15-1991, § 6; Ord. of 8-13-1998, § 2; Amend. of 1-13-2009; Amend. of 3-3-2016)
Sec. 70-65. - Enforcement.
(a)
The sheriff of the county, any deputies thereof, any agent of the county authorized by the board of commissioners to issue citations for the enforcement of county ordinances, and all other law enforcement officers shall be authorized to issue citations to violators of any provisions of this article.
(b)
No person accused of violating this article shall be arrested prior to trial, but any person who fails to appear for trial shall be arrested thereafter on the warrant of the magistrate, and required to post bond for his future appearance.
(c)
Violations of this article shall be tried upon citations which shall be issued to an alleged violator of any sections of this article. Such citation shall state the time and place at which the accused is to appear for trial, shall identify the offense with which the accused is charged, shall have an identifying number by which it shall be filed with the court, shall indicate the identity of the accused and the date of service, and shall be signed by the representative of the county who completes and serves it. The citation may be served by certified mail, return receipt requested.
(d)
Citations issued under this article may be tried without a prosecuting attorney; however, if the prosecuting attorney is needed, the county attorney shall serve as the prosecuting attorney.
(e)
Violations of this article shall be tried in the magistrate court of the county and shall be tried in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq.
(f)
Nothing in this article shall prevent the board of commissioners from bringing any civil action for injunction, or other proceedings to present, correct, or abate any violation of this article or other state or federal law. No sanction, penalty or remedy prescribed in this article shall be considered exclusive of any other remedy, but shall be available in addition to any other sanctions, penalty or remedy provided by law.
(Res. of 5-15-1991, § 5; Ord. of 8-13-1998, § 1)

Sec. 70-91. - Short title.
This article shall be known and may be cited as the "Brantley County Waste Disposal Facility Siting Moratorium Ordinance."
(Ord. of 4-12-2005, § 1)
Sec. 70-92. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the terms:
Biomedical waste means any such matter, things or material as described and defined in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22 to such solid waste.
Disposal facility means any such facility as described and defined in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(8) which code section is incorporated herein as if it were fully set out, and any amendments hereafter made to said code section shall also be made a part hereof.
Materials recovery facility means a solid waste handling facility that provides for the extraction from solid waste of recoverable materials, materials suitable for use as a fuel or soil amendment, or any combination of such materials.
Municipal solid waste means all such matter, things or material as described and defined in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(18) which code section is incorporated herein as if it were fully set out, and any amendments hereafter made to said code section shall also be made a part hereof.
Person means those entities and people as described and defined in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(22) which code section is incorporated herein as if it were fully set out, and any amendments hereafter made to said code section shall also be made a part hereof.
Solid waste means all such matter, things or material as described and defined in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(33) which code section is incorporated herein as if it were fully set out, and any amendments hereafter made to said code section shall also be made a part hereof.
Solid waste handling facility means any such facility as described and defined in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(35) which code section is incorporated herein as if it were fully set out, and any amendments hereafter made to said code section shall also be made a part hereof.
(Ord. of 4-12-2005, § 2)
Sec. 70-93. - Facility siting moratorium.
No person not presently operating a waste handling facility in Brantley County, Georgia, shall site, locate, operate or apply for any permits required by the State of Georgia or of an agency of the United States of America to site, locate or operate, any solid waste handling, storage or disposal facility; any municipal solid waste disposal facility; any biomedical waste treatment and/or disposal facility; any solid waste thermal treatment technology facility; any hazardous waste treatment handling, storage or disposal facility; any special solid waste handling, storage or disposal facility; or any recovered materials processing facility, for a period of one year from the effective date of this article or until such time as the board of commissioners of the county has adopted and the state has approved the county's revised and updated land use plan and solid waste management plan for the county, as required by the laws of the state, which ever occurs first.
(Ord. of 4-12-2005, § 3)
