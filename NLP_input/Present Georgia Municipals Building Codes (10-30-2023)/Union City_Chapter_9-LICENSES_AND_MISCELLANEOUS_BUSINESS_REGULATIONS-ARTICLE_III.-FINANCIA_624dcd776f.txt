ARTICLE III. - FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS

Sec. 9-71. - Business license tax levied.
In accordance with OCGA section 48-6-93, there is hereby levied each year an annual business license tax upon all depository financial institutions located within the city at a rate of 0.25 percent of the gross receipts of the depository financial institutions. "Gross receipts" shall mean gross receipts as defined in OCGA section 48-6-93. Depository financial institutions shall mean state and national banks, state building and loan associations, and federal savings and loan associations.
(Ord. No. 83-9, § 1, 11-1-83)
Sec. 9-72. - Minimum tax.
The minimum annual amount of business license tax due from any depository financial institution pursuant to section 9-71 shall be one thousand dollars ($1,000.00).
(Ord. No. 83-9, § 2, 11-1-83)
Sec. 9-73. - Filing of return.
Pursuant to OCGA section 48-6-93(c), each depository financial institution subject to this tax shall file a return of its gross receipts with the mayor and council of the city on March first of the year following the year in which such gross receipts are measured. This return shall be in the manner and in the form prescribed by the commissioner of the department of revenue based on the allocation method set forth in subsection (d) of OCGA section 48-6-93. The mayor and council of the city shall assess and collect the tax levied pursuant to this article based upon the information provided in the return.
(Ord. No. 83-9, § 3, 11-1-83)
Sec. 9-74. - Due date of taxes.
Taxes levied pursuant to this article shall be due no later than thirty (30) days after filing of the return prescribed by section 9-73 unless extended by the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. 83-9, § 4, 11-1-83)
Sec. 9-75. - Administrative provisions.
The city clerk is hereby directed to forward a copy of this article to each depository financial institution in the city and to the home office of each depository financial institution if it is located outside the city.
(Ord. No. 83-9, § 5, 11-1-83)
