1-10-6-020 - Americans with Disabilities advisory board.
A.
An Americans with Disabilities advisory board is created and established. The purpose of this board is to provide commentary and technical assistance to the city council in regard to the city's self evaluation process and transition plan in compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act.
B.
The ADA advisory board shall consist of eight members of which seven members shall be either:
1.
Residents of the City of Marietta; or
2.
Residents of Cobb County who also reside within a ten-mile distance from the corporate limits of the City of Marietta; or
3.
Any eligible individual who is employed by an organization located within the corporate limits of the City of Marietta. Such members shall be appointed by the mayor and city council. The eight members of the ADA advisory board shall serve for the terms set forth herein:
Posts 1 and 4: These members shall serve an initial one-year term. Thereafter, all subsequent appointments shall be for a three-year term.
Posts 2 and 5: These members shall serve an initial two-year term. Thereafter, all subsequent appointments shall be for a three-year term.
Posts 3, 6 and 7 These members shall serve an initial three-year term. Thereafter, all subsequent appointments shall be for a three-year term.
Post 8: This post is reserved for the member of the council who is appointed by the mayor as chairman of the city council's personnel committee. This member shall serve a term equivalent to the length of appointment as chairman of the city council personnel committee.
4.
The ADA advisory board shall have as its chairman, the member of the city council who is appointed by the mayor as the chairman of the council personnel committee. All other committee members shall be either representative of the disabled community or an advocate of the disabled.
5.
Members of the board shall serve for no compensation.
6.
The board shall elect its secretary from among its members or request city staff to fulfill that function on behalf of the board. The board shall meet whenever called by the chairman, and shall make its own rules of procedure. All meetings of the board at which official action is taken shall be open to the public and all records of the board shall be a public record.
7.
Four members of the board shall constitute a quorum of the advisory board. Any vacancy in membership shall be filled for the unexpired term by the mayor and city council. The mayor and council shall have the authority to remove any member for cause or written charges, after a public hearing.
(Code 1978, § 1-4050; Ord. No. 5062, 7/8/92, § 1-5; Ord. No. 5253, 1/12/94, § 1; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96; Ord. No. 6755, 12/8/2004, § 1; Ord. No. 6898, 3/8/2006, § 1)
1-10-6-030 - Board of appeals—Construction, building, electric, plumbing.
The board of appeals for construction shall consist of seven members appointed by the city council. Each member shall live in a separate ward of the city so that at all times each and every ward shall be represented on the board. Should a member move from the ward or become unable to serve, his or her successor shall be appointed by the council for the remainder of the unexpired term. In the event of reapportionment, any member or members shall continue to serve their term until the first city council meeting in January following reapportionment at which time the member or members shall be appointed from the vacant ward or wards. Present members, as of December 1993, not representing their ward due to reapportionment shall continue to serve until the first city council meeting in January of 1996. No elected official or holder of any other public office shall be eligible to serve on the board. All members shall be appointed for five-year terms unless serving an unexpired term. The expiration date of all appointments, both present and future terms of office, shall be January 31.
(Code 1978, § 1-4042; Ord. No. 3607, 6/13/79, § 1; Ord. No. 3833, 1/20/82, § 1; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)

1-10-6-050 - Litigation management committee.
A.
There is hereby created and established a committee within the City of Marietta entitled "Litigation Management Committee" with the charge of carrying out the matters set forth in this section.
B.
The committee shall consist of the mayor of the City of Marietta as chairman, the chair of the personnel and insurance committee of the City of Marietta and the chair of the public works committee of the City of Marietta. Any vacancies on said committee occurring by virtue of a conflict, resignation or otherwise shall be filled by a vote of a majority of the Marietta City Council.
C.
The litigation management committee is hereby authorized to carry out the following matters related to the purposes for which this section has been created:
1.
To engage special legal counsel, if and where deemed appropriate, to advise and defend the City of Marietta related to any pending or potential litigation against the City of Marietta.
2.
To request any legal opinion deemed necessary or appropriate by the litigation management committee from the Attorney General of the State of Georgia, the State Bar of the State of Georgia or other appropriate entity.
3.
To respond to any demand letters received by the City of Marietta, Constitutional Challenges received by the City of Marietta or other matters which attempt to create liability upon the city, any of its agencies or employees or officers or which challenge or seek to challenge any action by the City of Marietta.
4.
To meet, respond, convene, take action and/or assemble based upon receipt of any notice, correspondence or other notice or suit.
5.
To make decisions related to any pending or potential litigation and to make decisions related to any matter set forth in this section, including the right to authorize any appeal related to any matter described in this section.
6.
To report to the city council at any time deemed advisable by the litigation management committee or pursuant to a request by the city council.
7.
To take any other or further action as deemed necessary or appropriate related to any pending or potential litigation or any matter related to this section.
8.
To make all decisions regarding the method of defending any suits, including the right to file any counterclaim, and the right to make all of the day-to-day management decisions regarding the litigation subject, however, to the fact that any final settlement shall be brought back to the mayor and council for review and final approval.
D.
The activities of the litigation management committee shall be conducted in compliance with the Georgia Open Meetings Law and the Georgia Open Records Act.
E.
The litigation management committee shall be a permanent committee and is hereby authorized to commence immediately upon passage of this section by the Marietta City Council.
F.
Notwithstanding anything contained herein and except as provided herein, this section is not intended to alter or change the current system by which the City of Marietta handles claims, suits and/or other legal matters which are not brought by elected or appointed officials of the City of Marietta.
(Ord. No. 7527, 3/29/2011, § 1)

1-10-6-100 - Planning commission.
A.
Membership. The planning commission of the city shall consist of seven members appointed by the council. Each member shall live in a separate ward of the city so that at all times each and every ward shall be represented on the commission. Should a member move from the ward or become unable to serve, his or her successor shall be appointed by the council for the remainder of the unexpired term. No elected official or holder of any other public office shall be eligible to serve on the commission.
B.
Term of Office. All members shall be appointed for three-year terms unless serving an unexpired term.
C.
Removal of Members. The council shall also have the authority to remove any member for cause, on written charges, after a public hearing. Compensation may be granted to members in connection with their official duties, as determined by the council.
D.
Organization, Rules. The planning commission shall elect its presiding officer from among its members. The term of the presiding officer shall be one year with eligibility for reelection. The planning commission shall appoint a secretary who may be an officer or employee of the municipality. The planning commission shall operate under the Robert's Rules of Order. Such rules may be modified upon approval by city council. All meetings of the planning commission, at which official action is taken, shall be open to the public and all records of the planning commission shall be a public record.
E.
Powers and Duties. From and after the time when the planning commission shall have organized and selected its officers and shall have adopted its rules of procedure, then the planning commission shall have all the powers, duties and responsibilities as follows:
1.
It shall be the function and duty of the municipal planning commission to make such careful and comprehensive surveys and studies of existing conditions and probably future developments and to prepare such plans for physical, social, and economic growth as will best promote the public health, safety, convenience, prosperity, or the general welfare as well as efficiency and economy in the development of its political jurisdiction. In particular, the planning commission shall have the power and duty to:
a.
Review and recommend for adoption to the appropriate governing authority or authorities a comprehensive plan, amendments or parts thereof for the development of its political jurisdiction.
b.
Review and recommend for adoption to the appropriate governing authority or authorities a zoning ordinance or resolution and map for its political jurisdiction.
c.
Review and recommend for adoption to the appropriate governing authority or authorities regulations for the subdivision of land within its political jurisdiction, and to administer the regulations that may be adopted.
d.
Review and recommend for adoption to the appropriate governing authority or authorities, a plat or plats or an official map showing the exact location of the boundary lines of existing, proposed, extended, widened or narrowed streets, public open spaces or public building sites, together with regulations to control the erection of buildings or other structures within such lines, within its political jurisdiction or a specified portion thereof.
e.
Review and recommend to the council policies, ordinances, administrative procedures, and other means for carrying out plans in a coordinated and efficient manner. In addition, unincorporated areas adjacent to municipalities may be added to and included in the area under the jurisdiction of a municipal planning commission for general planning and for master planning preparation and for the preparation and administration of zoning ordinances or resolutions, land subdivision regulations, and official maps, provided that the governing bodies of the county and the municipality shall agree to the boundaries of such additional areas, procedures for joint action, procedures for the adoption of administration of ordinances and resolutions, and regulations applying to the area, and the manner of obtaining equitable representation on the municipal planning commission and board of zoning appeals. Such agreement shall be formerly established by appropriate official action by the governing authorities involved.
2.
The planning commission, its members and employees, in the performance of its functions, may enter upon any land, make examinations and surveys, and place and maintain necessary monuments and marks. In the event that any member of the planning commission should cause damage or injury while on the premises of any applicant or on the adjoining premises and said official acts outside the scope of the duties of a member of the planning commission, the member of the planning commission shall be responsible for such damage or injury. In general, the planning commission shall have such powers as may be necessary to enable it to perform its functions and promote the planning of its political jurisdiction.
F.
The attorney for the board of zoning appeals and the planning commission shall be served and advised by a common attorney as engaged by the city with concurrent advisement from said two boards. Current serving city attorney may be considered to represent concurrently one or both of said boards.
(Code 1978, § 1-4043; Ord. No. 3337, 11/9/77, §§ 1—5; Ord. No. 5566, 6/12/96; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96; Ord. No. 8004, 2/14/2018, § 1)

1-10-6-150 - Appointment process.
A.
Application:
1.
Develop and implement a website that describes all City of Marietta authorities, boards and commissions.
2.
A detailed general application form for people who want to volunteer to serve the city should be developed and made available on the City of Marietta web page that can be filled out online. Upon request the form would be sent by U.S. Mail or fax. Applications should be kept on file by the city for two years.
B.
New Member Agreement: Each appointee shall fill out and sign an agreement form in which they agree to comply with the attendance requirements, attend an orientation and read any other related material, follow the conflict of interest statement, abide by the Marietta Code of Ethics and follow any other legal requirements of their service.
C.
Nominations and Appointments:
1.
Current authority, board or commission members, having served one term, must reapply to serve a second, consecutive term. For ward appointments, the member may indicate desire to serve another term to the appointing councilperson.
2.
The personnel committee could serve as a nominating committee only in the case of nonward appointments made by the entire council.
D.
Staff Liaisons:
1.
The city manager will appoint a senior member of city staff to be a liaison with each authority, board or commission. The liaison should be familiar with the role of the assigned authority, board or commission in city government affairs. The staff liaison for each authority, board or commission should prepare an orientation manual and conduct an orientation session for each new member within 30 days of appointment. Such manual shall be presented to city council for review.
2.
The city clerk will monitor upcoming vacancies and shall be responsible for preparing standard notices of availability to city council.
E.
Attendance Requirements: When a member misses 25 percent of the regularly scheduled meetings over any 12-month period, which period commences on the date of appointment, the staff liaison will send the member a letter advising him/her that further absences may be cause for his/her removal from the authority, board or commission. Members must attend at least 75 percent of any board, authority or commission meeting to be counted as present. In the case of an authority, board or commission that does not hold regular meetings but meet as required, when a member misses 50 percent of the called meetings over a three-year period, which period commences on the date of appointment, the staff liaison will send the member a letter advising him/her that further absences may be cause for his/her removal from the authority, board or commission. The staff liaison will be responsible for submitting attendance reports to the city clerk. The city clerk will compile the attendance reports for submission to city council every six months, unless directed otherwise. The city council may take appropriate action including, but not limited to, excusing the absences, removing the board member or commissioner, or take other action, as the council deems appropriate.
F.
Conflict of Interest: Conflict of Interest Standards and the Marietta Code of Ethics will apply to decisions in which any authority, board or commission member or his/her family member has a financial interest or direct personal interest in the outcome of a decision. A member having such a conflict of interest must disclose it in writing and shall recuse himself/herself from voting.
G.
End of Term Appreciation: The mayor and council should provide each authority, board or commission member a certificate of appreciation when his or her term ends.
(Ord. No. 6512, 5/14/2003, § 1)
