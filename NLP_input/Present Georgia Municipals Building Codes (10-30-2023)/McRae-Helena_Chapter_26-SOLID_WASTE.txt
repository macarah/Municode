Chapter 26 - SOLID WASTE[1]

Sec. 26-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Ashes means and includes the waste product from coal, wood, or other fuels used for cooking and heating.
Brown goods includes such items as chairs, mattresses, sofas, tables, and other furniture items.
Building refuse means solid waste generated from construction or demolition activities undertaken on property located within the corporate limits of the city. The term "building refuse" includes, but is not limited to, lumber, brick, block, stone, carpet, plumbing materials, plaster, concrete, roofing, floor tile, gutters, and other substances accumulated as a result of construction of or repairs or additions to structures or accessory structures or the demolition thereof.
Bulk waste means appliances, furniture, bedding material, automobile parts, mechanical equipment, mechanical parts, and carpet.
Commercial solid waste means all types of solid waste generated by stores, offices, restaurants, warehouses, and other nonmanufacturing activities, excluding residential and industrial wastes.
Composting means the controlled biological decomposition of organic matter into a stable, odor-free humus.
Department means the public works department or its successor.
Director means the director of the public works department of his designee.
Discard means to throw, abandon, place, deposit, discharge, dump, bury, burn or dispose of a substance.
Disposable trash containers means plastic bags and cardboard, wooden and corrugated boxes which shall be nonreturnable and shall be collected and packed into the garbage truck collecting such items.
Disposal facility means any facility or location where the final deposition of solid waste occurs and includes, but is not limited to, landfilling and solid waste thermal treatment technology facilities.
Dump means to throw, discard, place, deposit, discharge, bury, burn or dispose of a substance.
Front-loading container means a heavy-duty steel container designed for use in commercial, industrial, and apartment units for storage of garbage, miscellaneous rubbish, trash and other waste.
Garbage means all vegetable and animal matter, but not dead animals, food and other refuse from a kitchen, market, store, or elsewhere, including all matter that decays, attracts flies, and creates disagreeable odors.
Hazardous waste means solid or liquid waste material resulting from the manufacture or use of chemicals, pesticides and drugs, other than normal household use, pathological waste, highly flammable, explosive waste, toxic waste, sewer sludge, insecticides, poisons, corrosives, caustics, acids, infectious materials, compressed gases, biological materials, radioactive materials, petroleum products and other waste material that is determined to be a hazard to public health, safety or environment.
Industrial waste means solid waste generated by factories, processing plants, wholesale establishments, assembling plants, food processing wastes, cinders and ashes, lumber scraps, sawdust, shavings, including solids, semisolids, sludges, and liquids, floor sweepings, metal scrap and shavings, and any other waste products from industrial and manufacturing processes.
Leachate collection system means a system at a landfill for collection of the leachate, which may percolate through the waste and into the soils surrounding the landfill.
Litter means all discarded manmade materials, including, but not limited to, garbage, lottery tickets, household trash, industrial waste, building materials, appliances, or portions thereof, refuse, waste materials, sand, gravel, slag, brick bats, rubbish, tin cans, cigarette and cigar butts, other tobacco products, rags, cloths, newspapers, aluminum cans, trash, debris, dead animals, or any other discarded, used or unconsumed substance which is not handled in accordance with the provisions of this chapter.
Multiple residential unit means any duplex, dwelling place or apartment, or group of apartments under a single roof, or in a single apartment complex, used for a dwelling place for more than a one-family unit and which is served by a single water meter.
Municipal solid waste means any solid waste derived from households, including garbage, trash and sanitary waste in septic tanks, and includes solid waste for single-family and multifamily residences, hotels and motels, bunkhouses, campgrounds, picnic grounds, and day use recreation areas. The term "municipal solid waste" includes yard trimmings and commercial solid waste, but does not include solid waste from mining, agricultural or silvacultural operations or industrial processes or operations.
Municipal solid waste disposal facility means any facility or location where the final deposition of any amount of municipal solid waste occurs, whether or not mixed or including commercial or industrial solid waste, including, but not limited to, municipal solid waste landfills.
Municipal solid waste landfill means a disposal facility where any amount of municipal solid waste, whether or not mixed with or including commercial waste, industrial waste, nonhazardous sludges or small quantity generator hazardous waste, is disposed of by means of placing an approved cover thereon.
Owner means any person, firm, or corporation owning, leasing, renting, occupying or managing any premises.
Plastic bag means a polyethylene or other heavy-duty plastic bag meeting the National Sanitation Foundation Standard of 1.1 mills and not exceeding a 30-gallon capacity, with a securing twist tie. An untied plastic bag shall not be an approved container.
Retail tire dealer means a person actively engaged in the business of selling new and used replacement tires.
Rubbish means a variety of nonrecyclable household waste derived from places of residence and shall include paper, rags, plastics, cartons, boxes, broken glass bottles and jars, crockery, rubber, discarded clothing, and similar materials.
Scrap tire means a tire that is no longer suitable for its original intended purpose because of wear, damage or defect.
Scrap tire carrier means any person engaged in picking up or transporting scrap tires not otherwise exempted in the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management for the purpose of removal to a scrap tire processor, end user or disposal facility.
Scrap tire generator means any person who generates scrap tires. The term "scrap tire generator" may include, but is not limited to, retail tire dealers, retreaders, scrap tire processors, automobile dealers, private company vehicle maintenance shops, garages, service stations, and city, county and state governments.
Scrap tire processor means any person who is approved by the environmental protection division to receive scrap tires from scrap tire generators or scrap tire carriers for the purpose of scrap tire processing.
Scrap tire sorter means any person, other than the original scrap tire generator, who handles mixed tires by separating used tires and retreadable casings from scrap tires.
Single residential unit means any dwelling place occupied by one family unit and which is served by a single water meter.
Solid waste means any garbage or refuse; sludge from wastewater treatment plants; solid, liquid, semisolid, or contained gaseous material resulting from operations; mineral matter; metal and wooden furniture; appliances; large metal scraps and wires; wooden crates, boxes or pallets; dead animals; and other waste in a solid or semisolid state not otherwise defined in this section.
Waste means discarded material, including, but not limited to, ashes, building refuse, garbage, hazardous waste, industrial refuse, recyclable waste, rubbish, solid waste, yard debris, tires, sand, gravel, slag, brick bats, trash, debris, dead animals, bottles, cans, boxes, containers, papers, tobacco products, appliances, mechanical equipment or parts, building or construction materials, tools, machinery, wood, motor vehicles or parts, vessels, aircraft equipment, waste oil, batteries, antifreeze, sludge from wastewater treatment plants or air pollution control facilities, and any other discarded material or substance of every kind and description resulting from domestic, industrial, commercial, governmental, or other operations.
Wheeled container means a covered plastic or rubber container on wheels designed primarily for use by residential units for storage of garbage, miscellaneous refuse, trash and other waste.
White goods means appliances such as stoves, refrigerators and other bulky household items primarily constructed of metal, parts of appliances, metal parts and miscellaneous metal.
Yard debris means tree limbs, branches, stumps and logs, twigs, grass and shrub trimmings and clippings, bushes, weeds, leaves, and general yard and garden waste materials, and includes stone and dirt rakings and similar waste materials resulting from landscaping activities.
Yard trimmings means leaves, brush, grass clippings, shrub and tree prunings, discarded Christmas trees, nursery and greenhouse vegetative residuals, and vegetative matter resulting from landscaping development and maintenance, other than mining, agricultural and silvacultural operations.
(Ord. No. O-024-17, § 1, 5-11-2017)
Sec. 26-2. - Administration and enforcement.
(a)
The city manager or his designee shall be responsible for the administration and enforcement of the provisions of this chapter. He may call upon the police department or code enforcement officer for assistance in its enforcement.
(b)
Upon determining that a violation of this chapter exists, the city manager or his designee shall cause written notice of such violation to be issued to the owner or occupier of the subject premises, setting forth the violation of the chapter and granting such owner or resident a reasonable time, not to exceed ten days, for the owner to correct said condition.
(c)
Upon determining that the owner has failed or refused to correct the condition within the time prescribed in the notice, the city manager or his designee shall cause a notice to be issued directing the owner or occupier of said premises to appear in the municipal court to answer the charge of violation of this chapter.
(Ord. No. O-024-17, § 2, 5-11-2017)
Sec. 26-3. - Waste collection responsibility by the city.
(a)
The city shall be responsible for the collection and disposal of all garbage, solid waste, yard debris and rubbish within the city, and no person, unless otherwise authorized by a contract with the city, shall collect and dispose of or transport over the streets of the city any of such waste without first obtaining a written permit from the city to do so. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the city shall be authorized to enter into a contract with a private corporation or a public authority to provide for the collection and disposal of all garbage, solid waste, yard debris, and rubbish within the city. Such contract shall contain such terms and conditions as authorized by the city council and shall be for such length of time as may be determined by the city council and its citizens, but in no event shall such contract extend a length of time not otherwise authorized by the constitution and laws of the state.
(b)
The city shall not be responsible for the collection of hazardous waste and industrial refuse except as otherwise provided herein. The city shall not be responsible for the collection of building refuse. Such material must be disposed of by the builder, property owner, or tenant. In the case of new buildings, no certificate of occupancy shall be issued until the aforesaid material is removed by the owner or contractor. The disposal of all waste that is not subject to collection by the city shall be the responsibility of the person generating such waste. Such waste shall be disposed of in accordance with local, state and federal statutes, ordinances and regulations.
(c)
No person who owns or occupies any premises in the city shall permit trash, garbage, or other waste or rubbish to collect or remain on such premises, except during such periods between waste pickup; and no person who owns or occupies any premises in the city shall permit garbage, trash or other waste to be scattered from such premises into the public streets of the city.
(d)
Any owner of property who permits garbage, solid waste, building refuse, yard debris, recyclable waste and rubbish, hazardous waste or industrial refuse to become in a condition which is hazardous or dangerous to the public health, safety and welfare shall be notified in writing by the city manager or his designee to abate such condition within ten days of the date of such notice.
(Ord. No. O-024-17, § 3, 5-11-2017)
Sec. 26-4. - Refuse collection by city.
(a)
The city, or any entity with which the city has contracted for such purposes, shall make collections of waste from residential premises which have wheeled containers at least once weekly. The particular days of each week during which such collection shall occur shall be determined by the city manager or his designee or agent.
(b)
The city, or any entity with which the city has contracted for such purposes, shall make collections of waste from commercial and industrial premises which have wheeled containers at least weekly. The particular days of each week during which such collection shall occur shall be determined by the city manager or his designee or agent.
(c)
Yard debris and yard rubbish shall be collected by the city, or by any entity with which the city has contracted for such purposes, at least once a week on a schedule at such times as may be determined by the city manager or his designee or agent of the city. Such waste shall only be collected by the city when placed curbside. Wheeled containers shall not be utilized for disposal of such waste.
(Ord. No. O-024-17, § 4, 5-11-2017)
Sec. 26-5. - Garbage containers.
(a)
It shall be the duty of any owner or occupant of any single or multiple residential or commercial unit to place the wheeled container provided for such purpose on the assigned collection day at a front curbside location in such a manner as not to obstruct pedestrian passage, except that the city manager or his designee or agent may require the wheeled container to be placed on any side of a parcel of property for the reasons of topography or efficiency.
(b)
It shall be the duty of the owner or occupant of a single or multiple residential or commercial unit to place the wheeled container at an appropriate curbside location at or before 7:00 a.m. or prior to the arrival of the sanitation truck.
(c)
It shall be the duty of the owner or occupant of any single or multiple residential or commercial unit to remove the wheeled container from the curbside location to a storage location, which storage location shall be nearer to the unit located on the premises than to any street abutting such premises; provided, however, that said removal shall be accomplished not later than 7:00 p.m. on the day the contents thereof are collected.
(d)
Garbage containers must be placed at least three feet away from vehicles, poles, fire hydrants, fences, overhead wires, low-hanging trees, branches, limbs, or other types of cover.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for any person to set fire to the contents of, indiscriminately scatter or disperse the contents of, or otherwise vandalize any container provided by the municipality or its agent for the dumping of garbage.
(Ord. No. O-024-17, § 5, 5-11-2017)
Sec. 26-6. - Pre-collection practices; prohibitions.
(a)
Front-loading containers. The city manager or his designee or agent shall determine the type, size, specifications, number and location of the garbage containers and shall determine the condition.
(b)
Secure containers. All containers shall be maintained at all times with tight-fitting lids or covers, and secured in such a manner as to prevent the overturning by the elements or by dogs or other animals.
(c)
Garbage and rubbish. Garbage and rubbish placed in containers for collection shall be enclosed in disposable watertight plastic bags with the top secured and placed in the container.
(d)
Injurious garbage or rubbish items. Garbage or rubbish placed for pickup with the potential for injury, such as broken glass, lightbulbs, sharp pieces of metal, fluorescent tubes, etc., shall be securely packaged or wrapped in such a manner to prevent injury to collection crews or other persons.
(e)
Pet litter. Pet litter must be placed in securely tied plastic bags and placed in the container.
(f)
Clean container. It shall be the responsibility of the owner or lessee of the property to keep the container clean.
(g)
Burying or burning litter or waste; written consent. No owner or occupant of any such property within the corporate limits shall bury or burn litter or waste without prior authorization and written consent from the applicable regulatory agency, including, but not limited to, the United States Environmental Protection Division and/or the state forestry commission. Nothing in this provision shall authorize or be construed to permit the burial or burning of any material which is otherwise prohibited by state and/or federal law.
(h)
Accumulation. No owner or occupant of any property shall cause, suffer, or allow the accumulation on his premises of garbage, litter or waste where such material creates or causes a health hazard to neighbors or other citizens, or which is unsightly or emits foul or obnoxious odors.
(i)
Stormwater collection system. It shall be unlawful for any person to dump any solid waste on any property, in any waters or in any stormwater collection system, except as authorized by applicable permits. It shall be the burden of the alleged violator to show proof of any applicable permits.
(j)
Storm drains. No person shall place any litter, refuse, trash, trees, grass, or other rubbish over or within five feet of a storm drain, drop inlet, culvert, or manhole.
(k)
Dumping. It shall be unlawful to dump, open dump, or permit the dumping of litter, waste, scrap tires, municipal, commercial, or industrial solid waste or recyclables at any place within the corporate city limits, including, and without limitations, in or on any public highway, road, street, alley, or thoroughfare, including any portion of the right-of-way, any public or private property in the city or any waters in the city, unless such litter or waste originates within the corporate limits of the city and the property is designated by the city council or its duly designated agent for the disposal of the above, and the material is placed into a receptacle or container designed for such property, and the property has a valid solid waste handling permit issued by the state environmental protection division (EPD) when required.
(Ord. No. O-024-17, § 6, 5-11-2017)
Sec. 26-7. - Garbage collection for disabled persons.
Any person who is a full-time resident of a residential unit and who is disabled to the extent that such person is incapable of moving the wheeled container shall not be required to place the wheeled container at curbside as required in section 26-5. This section shall not apply unless all of the persons residing in a residential unit are also disabled. Provision for the collection of garbage from disabled residents shall be included in any contract approved by the city council for waste collection.
(Ord. No. O-024-17, § 7, 5-11-2017)
Sec. 26-8. - Sorting, storing, composting, and collecting yard debris.
(a)
All yard debris shall be subject to the following provisions:
(1)
Yard debris shall not be placed in or mixed with municipal solid waste.
(2)
Yard debris shall not be disposed of at municipal solid waste disposal facilities having liners and a leachate collection system or requiring vertical expansion located within the city (or at any such facility with which the city contracts).
(3)
Yard debris shall be sorted and stored for collection in such a manner as to facilitate collection, composting, or other handling.
(b)
The solid waste division of the public works department shall provide this service throughout the city.
(c)
The solid waste division shall not collect more than one load at any one location per predetermined route unless collected as an unscheduled service. A "load" is defined as six cubic yards.
(d)
Yard debris shall be placed between the curb and sidewalk where feasible. Where there is no sidewalk, limbs may be placed on the owner's property near the street. Limbs should be trimmed to six feet long and be no more than five inches in diameter. Limbs shall be stacked no closer than 50 feet to an intersection and placed in such a manner to not obstruct the view of traffic. Dirt, rocks, and roots shall not be collected by the city.
(e)
Leaves, limbs, grass, or other yard debris shall be placed in an orderly manner so as not to obstruct the free and normal flow of any drainage system or the safe vision or movement of any pedestrian or vehicular traffic.
(f)
Limbs cut and placed near the street for collection by any person other than the owner or legal occupant of the property shall not be collected or disposed of by the city. Individuals or companies under contract as privately employed tree trimmers, tree surgeons, landscape contractors, and operators of tree and shrubbery leaves and limbs maintenance shall haul and dispose of all such debris if said debris exceeds the definition of a load of yard debris. This requirement also applies to utility or railroad companies which trim vegetation away from their wires, equipment or tracks to maintain their right-of-way over public and private land. It shall be the duty of the occupant or owner of any premises to separate yard debris from all other municipal solid waste.
(g)
The on-site composting of yard trimmings is encouraged. Further, the development of low-maintenance landscaping is encouraged for commercial and industrial developments.
(Ord. No. O-024-17, § 8, 5-11-2017)
Sec. 26-9. - Brown and white goods collection.
(a)
Brown and white goods, as defined in section 26-1, must be separated from other waste. Brown and white goods shall be placed away from automobiles, mailboxes, fences, low-hanging limbs and wires or other items which might hamper collection.
(b)
The city will not pick up bulk brown and white goods from commercial establishments.
(c)
No more than two pieces of brown and white goods shall be placed out for pickup at the same time.
(Ord. No. O-024-17, § 9, 5-11-2017)
Sec. 26-10. - Unscheduled service.
(a)
The minimum charge for an unscheduled service for yard debris collection exceeding a load as defined in section 26-8(c) shall be $150.00 per load.
(b)
There shall be no unscheduled services for other solid waste collection. If collection for roll-out cart service was missed and the cart was placed at the curb by the time prescribed in section 26-5, the missed collection should be reported to city hall.
(c)
Payment must be received prior to collection.
(Ord. No. O-024-17, § 10, 5-11-2017)
Sec. 26-11. - Move outs and eviction.
(a)
For rental properties, garbage, trash, or other solid waste originating from the property during the cleanup between tenants or the eviction of tenants shall be the responsibility of the landlord and/or the property owner. Following an eviction, any property, trash, belongings, or other items shall be placed on some portion of the landlord's property, immediately behind the sidewalk or, if there is no sidewalk, a minimum of five feet behind the curb or off the pavement, and shall not be located as to impede or obstruct or divert either pedestrian or vehicular traffic or the flow of surface water or block drains.
(b)
Any property, trash, belongings, or other items placed curbside following an eviction shall be removed within 48 hours of the physical eviction by the property owner.
(c)
If not removed within 48 hours after notification by the city, that will constitute a violation of this Code and be subject to penalty as provided in section 1-8.
(Ord. No. O-024-17, § 11, 5-11-2017)
Sec. 26-12. - Collection routes and districts established.
The areas of the city shall be divided into districts and collection routes by the public works director, and a map shall be available at city hall and any other area deemed appropriate for public viewing.
(Ord. No. O-024-17, § 12, 5-11-2017)
Sec. 26-13. - Service charges.
The monthly bill for utilities sent to each utilities consumer for water and sewer service shall include charges for solid waste removal service and trash, leaf and limb collection at such rates therefor as are prescribed by resolution of the city council. The total amount of each monthly bill for the utility services shall be promptly paid by the consumer in full in accordance with the water connection policy.
(Ord. No. O-024-17, § 13, 5-11-2017)
Sec. 26-14. - Discontinuance of services to premises when charges remain unpaid.
Any person failing for any reason or refusing to pay monthly the charge provided for in section 26-13 shall be subject to the discontinuance of city services in accordance with the same provisions of the water connection policy.
(Ord. No. O-024-17, § 14, 5-11-2017)
Sec. 26-15. - State of emergency.
If a state of emergency has been declared by a person authorized to make such declaration or a natural disaster or occurrence has significantly impacted the area, residential yard debris collection shall be made in accordance with the emergency plans of the city, or as authorized by the city manager or his designee.
(Ord. No. O-024-17, § 15, 5-11-2017)
Sec. 26-16. - Scrap tires.
(a)
The owner or occupant of any premises shall be responsible for the sanitary handling and disposal of all scrap tires on the premises used or occupied by such person.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to cause, suffer or allow the dumping of scrap tires at any place, including, and without limitations, in or on any public highway, road, street, alley, or thoroughfare, including any portion of the right-of-way thereof, any public or private property or any waters, unless the property is designated by the city council and the state environmental protection division for the collection or disposal of scrap tires and the person is authorized to use such property. The scrap tires must be placed into a receptacle or container installed specifically for such property and the property shall have a valid solid waste handling permit or other applicable identification numbers, and approvals and/or permits issued by the state environmental protection division (EPD) when required.
(c)
All persons defined as scrap tire generators, scrap tire carriers, scrap tire processors, including scrap tire sorters, and retail tire dealers shall be subject to rules as defined in chapter 391-3-4 et seq., of the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management, as amended, and handle scrap tires in accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 12-8-20 et seq., and the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management, chapter 391-3-4 et seq., as amended, applicable to solid waste, except where provisions of this section are more stringent, in which case the provisions of this section shall be applicable unless preempted by state law.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person in a residential zone to accumulate any amount of scrap tires on or around property, which they own or occupy.
(e)
A retail tire dealer may hold up to 100 scrap tires per garage bay in storage, up to a maximum of 1,000 scrap tires.
(f)
Tires must be stored in covered or enclosed areas to prevent the accumulation of water.
(g)
Any person, firm, or corporation violating any portion of this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor.
(1)
The court may order the publication of the names and photographs of persons convicted of violating this section;
(2)
The court may order the person convicted hereunder to repair or restore property damaged, or pay damages resulting from such violations, or perform public service related to the repair or restoration of property damaged by the violation;
(3)
In case of a tire dump, the property owner, contractor, developer, builder or other person responsible for the property shall, upon conviction, cause the property to be cleaned and to come into full compliance with this section. The city council shall not be responsible for any costs of cleanup or remediation;
(4)
The expenses incurred by the city council for cleanup, enforcement of violations and penalties shall be chargeable to the person convicted hereunder, such expenses include, but are not limited to, court costs, filing fees, special investigations, mutual aid assistance from other agencies and other costs necessary for the reasonable enforcement of this section;
(5)
In addition to actions filed by the city council for violations of this section, any state or federal agency may independently file separate or concurrent charges within their respective applicable authority and seek conviction within a court of competent jurisdiction;
(6)
Enforcement of this section shall be the responsibility of the city manager or his designee; and
(7)
Any person authorized to enforce this section shall be empowered to enter any property, upon reasonable cause, at reasonable or necessary times in order to properly inspect for violations of this section, subject to the condition that to allow entry onto private property for inspection, the alleged violation of this section must be visible from a public road or right-of-way, or upon said person having received a valid complaint alleging a violation of this section, or by a judge's order upon said person having received information/allegations that constitute reasonable suspicion that a serious unlawful act or threat to the health and safety of the community and/or the environment has occurred or is about to occur.
(Ord. No. O-024-17, § 16, 5-11-2017)
Sec. 26-17. - Evidence of violations.
(a)
Whenever litter is thrown, deposited, dropped or dumped from any motor vehicle, boat, airplane or other conveyance in violation of this chapter, it shall be prima facie evidence that the operator of the conveyance violated this chapter.
(b)
Whenever any litter which is dumped, deposited, thrown or left on public or private property in violation of this chapter is discovered to contain any articles, including, but not limited to, letters, bills, publications or other writings, which display the name of a person thereon in such manner as to indicate that the article belongs or belonged to such person, it shall be a rebuttal presumption that such person has violated this chapter.
(Ord. No. O-024-17, § 17, 5-11-2017)
Sec. 26-18. - Enforcement and penalty.
(a)
In the event that any person violates any provision of this chapter, the city council or other appropriate authority may, in addition to other remedies, institute an action for injunction, cleanup or stop-work orders, mandamus, irreversible damage fines, liens on property or other appropriate action or proceeding to prevent such unlawful acts or to correct or abate any such violation. In addition, the city may immediately revoke or suspend any and all business, building, development or any and all other city-issued permits related to the properties involved with the violation until such time that compliance is met, or until the ruling of a court of competent jurisdiction is obtained, at which time respective permits may be reissued.
(b)
Upon finding evidence, a written notice of violation may be issued at the discretion of the enforcing officer, in lieu of a citation. In the absence of corrective action or in the event that a second violation occurs, the evidence constituting the notice of violation may be submitted as evidence for consideration as a first offense before a court of competent jurisdiction and the pending case treated as a second offense by the court.
(c)
If a person is found guilty of a violation of the provisions of this chapter, the court may cause written notice to be incorporated into the court order to the violator instructing that person to properly address any provision still remaining in violation of this chapter for which said violator is convicted. Such notice shall be by personal service and, in the event the violator cannot be so served, then by registered mail sent to the violator's last-known address.
(d)
Upon failure, neglect or refusal of any person so notified to properly address said provisions within ten days after receipt of notice as provided in this section, the city council is hereby authorized and empowered to cause the cleanup, removal or disposal of scrap tires dumped, deposited, thrown, or left on public or private property in violation of this chapter on behalf of the city. The expenses incurred by the city shall be chargeable to the violator and the city council and/or the court shall send a statement of the amount due for said expenses by registered mail.
(Ord. No. O-024-17, § 18, 5-11-2017)
