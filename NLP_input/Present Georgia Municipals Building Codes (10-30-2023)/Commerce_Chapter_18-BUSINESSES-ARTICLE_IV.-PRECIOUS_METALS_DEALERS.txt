Sec. 18-91. - Payment of license fee.
Every person dealing in, buying or selling gold, silver or precious metals within the city shall pay the same special license fee as that established for pawn brokers.
(Code 1987, § 18-203(2))
Sec. 18-92. - Fingerprints and photographs of applicant and employees.
There shall be attached to the application of an applicant for a license under this article a photograph and a fingerprint record of the applicant and all persons who will or may operate the dealership in the city. Such photograph and fingerprint record must be obtained at the police department, where facilities shall be available, and the appropriate police official shall deliver the photographs and fingerprint records to the city manager for attachment to the license application.
(Code 1987, § 18-203(1))
Sec. 18-93. - Records required—General contents.
Every dealer in gold, silver or precious metals shall maintain a book in permanent form in which shall be entered in legible English at the time of each transaction or purchase a record thereof which shall contain the following information:
(1)
The date of transaction.
(2)
The name of the person conducting the transaction.
(3)
The name, date of birth, address of the customer, and general appearance of the customer and a distinctive number from a valid driver's license, other similar identification card, or a photograph of the customer.
(4)
An identification and description of the purchased and pledged goods, including any distinctive markings, numbers or initials.
(5)
The signature of the customer.
(Code 1987, § 18-203(3))
Sec. 18-94. - Same—Preparation, inspection, disposal.
Entries in the dealer's records required in this article shall appear in chronological order in ink. There shall be no blank lines between entries. Corrections must be made by drawing a permanent line through the transaction without destroying its legibility. The book shall be open to the inspection of any duly authorized law enforcement officer during the ordinary hours of business or at any reasonable time. This record must be maintained for at least one year.
(Code 1987, § 18-203(4))
Sec. 18-95. - Punishment.
Violation of any provision of this article which shall continue after written notice from the city shall be punished as provided in section 1-12.
(Code 1987, § 18-203(5))
Sec. 18-96. - Retention of goods.
Every person dealing in buying or selling gold, silver or precious metals who takes such goods or buys such goods, taking full title thereto, shall hold such goods so taken or purchased for at least ten days before disposing of same by sale, transfer, shipment, removal from the city limits or otherwise. During said ten-day period such goods shall be made available for inspection by City of Commerce Police officers. This section cannot be waived by contract but it does not apply and is not intended to cover purchases from reputable wholesale supply houses and manufacturers.
(Ord. No. 2010-015, 10-11-10)
