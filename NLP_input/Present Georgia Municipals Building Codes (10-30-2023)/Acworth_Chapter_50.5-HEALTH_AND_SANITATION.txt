Sec. 50.5-31. - Scope of article.
Nothing in this article shall be interpreted or applied so as to create any power or duty in conflict with the preemptive effect of any federal or state law.
(Ord. No. 2010-20, Art. I, 9-2-2010)
Sec. 50.5-32. - Adoption of interlocal agreement.
(a)
The City of Acworth has adopted an ordinance granting to the Cobb County Board of Health the power to enforce certain public health, sanitation and safety matters; and
(b)
O.C.G.A. §§ 31-3-4(a)7;-5(a)(3) and (4) authorize the city and the Cobb County Board of Health to enter into a contract pursuant to said laws.
(c)
The Cobb County Board of Health and the city desire to enter into this agreement on (effective date).
(d)
This contract shall be effective for a period of 12 months from the date signed and shall automatically renew each year unless either party objects by providing written notice to the other party at least 60 days prior to the expiration of the contract.
(e)
Either party may terminate this agreement upon 60 days written notice to the other party.
(f)
The board of health will provide copies of all those rules and regulations and any future amendments thereto enumerated in this agreement to the clerk of the city.
(g)
The chief judge of the Municipal Court of Acworth has approved said Agreement as evidenced by the chief judge's signature on the agreement, as maintained on file with the city clerk's office.
(Ord. No. 2010-20, Art. I, 9-2-2010)
Sec. 50.5-33. - Duties of municipal court services.
The City hereby agrees to furnish municipal court services for the purposes of the enforcement of the rules and regulations specified in section 50.5-36 of this agreement. Said enforcement services shall be supplied through the officers and employees of the Cobb County Board of Health.
(Ord. No. 2010-20, Art. II, 9-2-2010)
Sec. 50.5-34. - Terms of agreement.
(a)
Pursuant to the terms of this agreement and to effect its intent and purpose, those officers or employees of the board of health designated as environmental health specialist, environmental health county manager, environmental health district director, district health director or his/her or their designees shall be empowered and permitted to enforce the rules and regulations enumerated in section 50.5-6 within the municipal boundaries of the city without limitation and as fully as if those said individuals were code enforcement officers for the city. Included within this grant of enforcement authority is permission to issue any and all appropriate citations to city residents for violation of the rules and regulations specified in this agreement. This code enforcement authority shall be strictly limited to the enforcement of those rules and regulations specified in this agreement.
(b)
The board of health and its officers and employees will comply with all city ordinances, rules and regulations for the issuance of citations and the enforcement by citation of Board of Health rules and regulations adopted by the city as part of its Code of Ordinances.
(Ord. No. 2010-20, Art. III, 9-2-2010)
Sec. 50.5-35. - Enforcement and fines.
(a)
The city will amend its Code of Ordinances as necessary to permit the full and effective enforcement of Cobb County Board of Health rules and regulations as enumerated in Section 50.5-36 of this agreement by officers and employees of the county board of health.
(b)
The city will not be responsible for the actions of the county board of health officers, employees, or their designees.
(c)
Punishment for violation of the municipal ordinances may be imposed and shall not exceed a fine of $1,000.00 or 60 days imprisonment, or both.
(d)
The city will provide to the board of health or its designee any code citation forms drafted and/or presently in use by the city for the notification and enforcement of city code violations.
(e)
The director of the board of health or his/her designee will provide to the city manager on at least a quarterly annual basis the names of any and all board of health employees who will be undertaking code enforcement activities as code enforcement officers within the municipal boundaries of the city.
(f)
Citations for violations of the rules and regulations enumerated in Section 50.5-36 of this agreement shall be prosecuted in and administered and adjudicated by the Municipal Court of Acworth. Cobb County Board of Health officers, employees or their designees shall present and prosecute citation violations. If assistance is needed in the prosecution of any violation to which a citation has been issued pursuant to the authority granted in this agreement, the county attorney's office shall furnish such assistance.
(g)
All fines levied and collected by the municipal court shall remain the property of the city to offset the costs incurred in the administration and hearing of board of health code violation matters.
(Ord. No. 2010-20, Art. IV, 9-2-2010)
Sec. 50.5-36. - Adoption of future agreements by the Cobb County Board Of Health.
This agreement shall apply to the following rules and regulations and any future amendments that have been adopted by the Cobb County Board of Health:
(1)
Rules and Regulations for On-Site Sewage Management Systems, Article 290-5-26 as revised by the Department of Human Resources and as adopted by Cobb County Board of Health on February 21, 2008.
(2)
An Ordinance concerning Nuisances, as adopted by the Cobb County Board of Health on April 1, 1948.
(3)
Regulations Concerning Trailer Parks pursuant to the authority of O.C.G.A 31-3-6 and as adopted by the Cobb County Board of Health on June 17, 1999.
(4)
Rules and Regulations for Food Service, Article 290-5-14 as revised by the Department of Human Resources and as adopted by the Board of Health on February 21, 2008.
(5)
Rules and Regulations for Swimming Pools as adopted by the Cobb County Board of Health on April 16, 2009.
(6)
Rules and Regulations for Tourist Accommodations as adopted by the Cobb County Board of Health on June 18, 1998.
(7)
Rules and Regulations for Hand Watering with Gray Water as adopted by the Cobb County Board of Health on April 16, 2009.
(8)
Rules and Regulations for Body Art as adopted by the Cobb County Board of Health on August 19, 2010.
(Ord. No. 2010-20, Art. V, 9-2-2010)
