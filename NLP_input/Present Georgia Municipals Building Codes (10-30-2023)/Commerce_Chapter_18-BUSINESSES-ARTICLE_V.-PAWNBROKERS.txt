ARTICLE V. - PAWNBROKERS[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— Pawnbrokers, O.C.G.A. § 44-12-130 et seq.

Sec. 18-116. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Pawnbroker means any person with whom property is deposited as security for the permit of a debt.
(Code 1987, § 18-201(1))
Sec. 18-117. - Shop operated with other business.
When a pawnbroker shop is operated in conjunction with another business, the hours as set forth in section 18-123 shall not apply. When operating a pawnbroker business in conjunction with another business, the opening and closing hours shall be the same as with the other business; provided however, that such closing hour shall in any event be no later than 10:00 p.m. However, no pawnbroker may open his place of business or transact any business therein on Sunday.
(Code 1987, § 18-201(3))
Sec. 18-118. - Weekly report; false statements.
All pawnbrokers shall make a correct and truthful weekly report, in writing, on Monday of each week to the chief of police at his office in the police department, wherein he shall supply the information required by section 18-125, pertaining to all property sold or pledged to them during the previous week. Such report shall be signed by the pawnbroker making such report. Any pawnbroker making any willfully false or misleading statements in such report shall be punished as provided in section 1-12.
(Code 1987, § 18-201(4))
Sec. 18-119. - Transaction with minors.
No pawnbroker shall receive any article in pawn or purchase any article for resale from any person under 18 years of age.
(Code 1987, § 18-201(5))
Sec. 18-120. - Penalties.
Any pawnbroker or person running a pawnshop who violates the provisions of this article shall be punished as provided by section 1-12, and in addition the municipal court judge or the city council may also, on proper proof, revoke the license of any pawnbroker violating the provisions of any law or ordinance pertaining to pawnbrokers.
(Code 1987, § 18-201(6))
Sec. 18-121. - Premises open to police.
It shall be the duty of every pawnbroker to admit to his premises the chief of police or any other police officer who may desire at any time to search for articles missing or stolen, or to make any inspection authorized by this article, without the formality of a search warrant.
(Code 1987, § 18-201(7))
Sec. 18-122. - Inspection of pledges and purchases.
All articles pledged or sold to pawnbrokers shall, at all times, be subject to inspection and examination by the police.
(Code 1987, § 18-201(8))
Sec. 18-123. - Business hours.
No pawnbroker shall open his place of business or conduct any business therein between the hours of 6:00 p.m. and 8:00 a.m.; provided, however, that such a place of business may remain open until 6:30 p.m. on Fridays and until 8:00 p.m. on Saturdays.
(Code 1987, § 18-201(9))
Sec. 18-124. - Display of pledges.
Every pawnbroker shall have his pledges openly displayed so that the inspection authorized by this article may be easily made.
(Code 1987, § 18-201(10))
Sec. 18-125. - Records—Generally.
Every pawnbroker shall maintain a book, in permanent form, in which shall be entered in legible English at the time of each loan, purchase or sale:
(1)
The date of transaction;
(2)
The name of the person conducting the transaction;
(3)
The name, age and address of the customer, a description of the general appearance of the customer; and the distinctive number from the customer's driver's license or other similar identification card;
(4)
An identification and description of the pledged or purchased goods, including, if reasonably available, the serial, model or other number, and all identifying marks inscribed thereon;
(5)
The number of the receipt or pawn ticket;
(6)
The price paid or the amount loaned;
(7)
The number of the check issued for the purchase price or loan if payment is made by check;
(8)
The maturity date of the transaction; and
(9)
The signature of the customer.
(Code 1987, § 18-201(11))
Sec. 18-126. - Same—Receipt.
A receipt providing all of the information specified in section 18-125 shall be signed and a copy given to the person pledging or selling property.
(Code 1987, § 18-201(12))
Sec. 18-127. - Same—Inspection.
The records required to be kept by section 18-125 shall be subject to inspection and examination by the police during the business hours and when the proprietor or any of his agents or employees are present.
(Code 1987, § 18-201(13))
Sec. 18-128. - Same—When entries made.
The entries required by section 18-125 shall be made as soon after the transaction as is possible and, in no event, more than one hour thereafter.
(Code 1987, § 18-201(14))
Sec. 18-129. - Same—False entries.
It shall be unlawful for any person engaged in the business of a pawnbroker or his clerk, agent or employee to make any false entry in any books kept as required by section 18-125.
(Code 1987, § 18-201(15))
Sec. 18-130. - Retention of goods.
Any pawnbroker or person operating under a pawnbroker's license who takes goods on pawn or buys goods, taking full title thereto, the goods being used in the broadest sense and including all kinds of personal property, shall hold such goods so taken in pawn or purchased for at least ten days before disposing of the same by sale, transfer, shipment or otherwise. This section cannot be waived by contract, but it does not apply and is not intended to cover purchases from reputable wholesale supply houses and manufacturers.
(Code 1987, § 18-201(16))
Secs. 18-131—18-150. - Reserved.
