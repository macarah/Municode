Sec. 12-1. - Advertising, circulars, handbills, etc.—Attaching to structures, placing in vehicles or on private premises without consent.
It shall be unlawful for any person to paste, paint, or place upon any bridge or any lamppost or corner post or utility post or on any building or any fence or in or on any vehicle or to distribute or throw on any private porch, yard, lawn, steps, driveway, or on any vacant property in the city, without the express written consent of the owner thereof, any unsolicited handbills, circulars, cards, posters, dodgers, pamphlets, booklets, brochures, or advertising samples.
Sec. 12-2. - Same—Distributing on streets.
It shall be unlawful for any person to distribute commercial handbills, circulars, cards, posters, pamphlets, brochures, or booklets on the portion of public streets used for vehicular traffic.
Sec. 12-3. - Soliciting alms.
It shall be unlawful for any person to ask, beg, or solicit alms in the city from any person or any business whatsoever, unless a permit is first obtained from the chief of police.
Sec. 12-4. - Conspiracy.
It shall be unlawful for any two (2) or more persons and each of them to conspire to do any act contrary to the ordinances of this city. They may be jointly or severally prosecuted.
Sec. 12-5. - Disorderly conduct.
(a)
Any person shall be guilty of disorderly conduct if he:
(1)
Acts in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another whereby any person is placed in fear of safety or his life, limb or health;
(2)
Acts in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another whereby public property or property of any other person is placed in danger of being destroyed or damaged;
(3)
Endangers lawful pursuits of another by acts of violence, angry threats and abusive conduct;
(4)
Causes, provokes, or engages in any fight, brawl, or riotous conduct so as to endanger the life, limb, health, or property of another or public property;
(5)
Assembles or congregates with another or others for the purpose of causing, provoking, or engaging in any fight or brawl;
(6)
Jostles or roughly crowds or pushes any person in any public place;
(7)
Collects in bodies or crowds for unlawful purposes;
(8)
Assembles or congregates with another or others for the purpose of or with the intent to engage in gaming;
(9)
Frequents any public place with intent to obtain money from another by any illegal and fraudulent scheme, trick, artifice or device;
(10)
Assembles with another or others for the purpose of engaging in any fraudulent scheme, device or trick to obtain any valuable thing in any place or from any person in the city or aids or abets therein;
(11)
Utters, while in a state of anger, in the presence of another, any lewd or obscene words or epithets;
(12)
Frequents any place where gaming or the illegal sale or possession of alcoholic beverages or narcotics or dangerous drugs are practiced, allowed or tolerated;
(13)
Acts in a dangerous manner toward others;
(14)
Uses "fighting words" directed towards any person who becomes outraged and thus creates turmoil;
(15)
Assembles or congregates with another or others for the purpose of doing bodily harm to another;
(16)
By acts of violence, interferes with another's pursuit of a lawful occupation;
(17)
Congregates with another or others in or on any public way so as to halt or impede the flow of vehicular or pedestrian traffic and refuses to clear such public way when ordered to do so by a peace officer or other person having authority;
(18)
Makes any unreasonably loud of unnecessary noise;
(19)
Damages, befouls or disturbs public property or the property of another so as to create a hazardous, unhealthy or physically offensive condition;
(20)
Willfully makes, continues or causes to be made or continued, any loud, unnecessary, unusual or excessive noise between the hours of 10:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m. or at any time in a residential area which disturbs the peace or quiet of any neighborhood or which causes discomfort or annoyance to any reasonable person of a normal sensitiveness residing in the area;
(21)
Makes or allows to be emitted from the radio or stereo system from any vehicle any excessive noise, loud music or loud sounds likely to annoy the senses of the average person;
(22)
Without provocation, uses to or of another person in such other person's presence, opprobrious or abusive words which by their very utterance tend to incite to an immediate breach of the peace, that is to say, words which as a matter of common knowledge and under ordinary circumstances will, when used to or of another person in such other person's presence, naturally tend to provoke violent resentment, that is, words commonly called "fighting words"; or
(23)
Without provocation, uses obscene and vulgar or profane language in the presence of or by telephone to a person under the age of fourteen (14) years which threatens an immediate breach of the peace.
(b)
Any person who commits the offense of disorderly conduct shall be quilty of a misdemeanor.
(Ord. of 6-11-90; Ord. No. 2012-3, Exh. A, 4-9-12)
Sec. 12-6. - Disturbing divine worship.
It shall be unlawful for any person to be engaged in loud talking or laughing, or any other disorderly conduct in or near the entrance or vestibule of any church or other place of worship during divine service or to congregate in and around any such place and behave in such a rude and disorderly manner as to become offensive to persons attending such service.
Sec. 12-7. - Disorderly houses.
(a)
A person who keeps and maintains, either by himself or others, a common, ill-governed, and/or disorderly house, to the encouragement of gaming, drinking, or other misbehavior, or where noisy or riotous persons assemble or other activity occurs to the common disturbance of the neighborhood or orderly citizens, is guilty of a misdemeanor.
(b)
It shall also be unlawful for any owner having control over a house to permit the use of same as a disorderly house as described in this section; provided, however, before charging under this subsection written notice shall be provided to the owner, giving fair notice of this subsection and the conduct proscribed thereby. Such violation by an owner of property shall also be guilty of a misdemeanor.
(c)
Each violation of this section shall be considered a separate and distinct offense, punishable as a misdemeanor with penalties as set forth under the Code of the city.
(Ord. No. 2012-3, Exh. A, 4-9-12; Ord. No. 20-04, § 1, 10-12-20)
Sec. 12-8. - Fraudulent sales.
It shall be unlawful for any person, by advertising by newspapers, radios, posters or otherwise, to represent that he is operating, offering or maintaining "fire sales," "wreck sales," "bankrupt sales," "closing out sales" or "going-out-of-business sales" and similar sales whereby members of the public are led to believe that they are being offered merchandise at reduced rates on account of fires, wrecks, bankruptcies, closing out or discontinuance of business, when in fact sales are not bona fide, but are fakes and frauds, and the advertisements and representations are untrue and false.
Sec. 12-9. - Bad checks.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to obtain money, property or credit by check, draft or order when such check, draft or order is not paid by the drawee upon presentation thereof and such check, draft, or order is issued with fraudulent intent.
(b)
The drawing or issuing of a check, draft, or order and the obtaining of money, property or credit therein without having sufficient money or property with which to pay the same shall be prima facie evidence of fraudulent intent.
Sec. 12-10. - Gambling—Generally.
It shall be unlawful for any person to play or bet for money or other thing of value at any game played with cards, dice or balls or at any other game or device or on any horse race or chicken or other animal fight within the city.
Sec. 12-11. - Same—Possessing implements therefor.
It shall be unlawful for any person in this city to have in his possession or custody any cards, dice, balls or other gaming implements with the intent to use the same in playing or betting for money or other thing of value.

Sec. 12-14. - Indecency, obscenity—In dress; pictures; performance.
It shall be unlawful for any person to appear in any public place naked or in an indecent or lewd dress or to make any indecent exposure of his or her person or be guilty of any indecent or lewd act or behavior or to exhibit or sell or offer for sale any obscene, vulgar or licentious book, picture or painting or to exhibit or perform in a hall, house, or public place any indecent, immoral or lewd play or dance or to sing to a public audience any sacrilegious, indecent, vulgar or lewd song.
Sec. 12-15. - Prostitution.
Prostitution, as defined by state law, shall be unlawful in the city.
Sec. 12-16. - Maximum length of knives.
It shall be unlawful for any person to have on his person any knife of any kind, the blade of which when open projects from the handle more than three (3) inches, unless the knife is securely wrapped so as not to be readily available for harmful use. This shall include butcher knives or any other knife which does not close and shall include straight razors and ice picks. Such prohibition shall not apply within the person's own home, on his premises or on the premises of the parent or the guardian of the person. The provision as to ice picks shall not apply to any person who is delivering ice within the city while he is making such deliveries.
Sec. 12-17. - Switchblade knife—Possession by minor.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any minor to be in possession of any switchblade knife or any knife which has a blade which opens automatically by hand pressure applied to a button, spring or other device in the handle or any knife with any other obstruction or other device of any kind which holds the blade partly open, unless such minor is upon the premises of his home or upon the premises of his parent or guardian.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any parent or guardian to knowingly permit his minor child to be in possession of a switchblade knife which has a blade which opens automatically by hand pressure applied to a button, spring or other device in the handle or any knife with any other obstruction or other device of any kind which holds the blade partly open, unless such minor is upon the premises of his home or upon the premises of his parent or guardian.
Sec. 12-18. - Same—Sale to minor.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell to any minor a switchblade knife or any knife which has a blade which opens automatically by hand pressure applied to a button, spring or other device in the handle or any knife with any other obstruction or other device of any kind which holds the blade partly open. If a knife as described above is sold to a minor, it shall be presumed that the seller had knowledge of the fact that the person was a minor.
Sec. 12-19. - Police officers generally.
(a)
Resisting, obstructing, etc. It shall be unlawful for any person to resist, obstruct or oppose any police officer in making or attempting to make a legal arrest of any person or in serving or attempting to serve any legal process or order issued by the authorities of the city, county or state.
(b)
Authority to summon aid; compliance. Any police officer of the city shall have the power to summon to his aid any number of citizens, if any person shall resist his authority or attempt to escape custody or to avoid an arrest, and it shall be unlawful for any person so summoned to refuse to obey the call of such police officer or give the assistance required. It shall be unlawful for any person summoned by any police officer to aid in suppressing an affray, riot, breach of the peace or other outrage to refuse to do so or to refuse when summoned as aforesaid to aid in arresting any offender.
(c)
Escape; attempted escape. It shall be unlawful for any person to escape or attempt to escape from any city police officer or from the city jail, city vehicle, or from any other person in charge of a work gang.
(d)
Impersonating police officer. It shall be unlawful for any person to appear on the streets wearing clothes or uniforms or badges which closely resemble the uniforms or badges adopted by the police force or to do any other similar act or to, in any way, impersonate a police officer.
(e)
Failure to obey a police officer. It shall be unlawful for any person refuse to obey any lawful order, request or demand of any police officer, while performing his lawful duties.
(f)
Unlawful fleeing from a police officer. It shall be unlawful for any person to flee from a police officer, who has a lawful reason to confront approach or detain this person. It shall prim facial evidence that the confrontation was lawful if the person continues or refuse to obey any lawful order, request or demand of any police officer, to stop his flight.
(g)
Obstructing or hindering law enforcement officers. Whoever knowingly and willfully resists, obstructs, or opposes any law enforcement officer, prison guard, correctional officer, probation supervisor, parole supervisor, or conservation ranger in the lawful discharge of his official duties by offering or doing violence to the person of such officer or legally authorized person is guilty of a felony and shall, upon conviction thereof, be punished by imprisonment for not less than one (1) nor more than five (5) years.
(Ord. No. 2012-3, Exh. A, 4-9-12)
Sec. 12-20. - Property damage.
(a)
Street and traffic signs. No person shall remove or mutilate, deface, destroy or injure any street identification or traffic-control sign, signal, marker or device.
(b)
Utility poles, etc. It shall be unlawful for any person to cut or in any way deface any telegraph, telephone, electric light or other public utility pole or to break any lamp or insulator or to throw any missile at any wire or in way or manner interfere with any pole, line or lamp of any electric light, telegraph, telephone or other public utility to the injury of any such property.
(c)
Buildings, etc. It shall be unlawful for any person to write, paint, draw, carve, or cut any letter, word or device or in any way mutilate or deface any church, bridge, public or private building or its appurtenances or any statue, tombstone, gravestone, marker or memorial that may be erected in the city or any wall or fence belonging to another.
(d)
Trees. It shall be unlawful for any person to cut, injure or destroy any shade tree on the sidewalks, streets or public areas in the city, except as may be required by the city.
(e)
Disturbing survey monument or marker. It shall be unlawful for any person to interfere with or disturb in any manner the stone monuments or iron markers used in the survey of the city and to be used in the future for future reference points without consent of the city council.
Sec. 12-21. - Discharging airguns, crossbows, etc.
It is unlawful for any person to discharge any airgun or air pistol or any similar device or any longbow, crossbow, or any other type of bow designed to discharge arrows in or onto any of the streets, sidewalks, alleyways, or other public property in the city or in any of the lots or properties thereof unless permission for the discharge of such devices has been given to the person discharging same by the owner of the property on which the devices are discharged and unless the discharging of same is so controlled that no pellet, shot, arrow or any matter so discharged shall cross the property line of the person granting such permission.
Sec. 12-22. - Firing guns, pistols, other firearms.
It shall be unlawful to discharge or fire any gun, pistol, or other firearm of any type within the city, provided this section shall not apply to gun clubs or military companies upon such part of the city as may be set apart and designated for the use of such guns clubs or military companies or to law enforcement officers in the discharge of their duties.
Sec. 12-23. - Possession of firearm by minor.
It shall be unlawful for any minor to be in possession of any firearm while not under the direct supervision of his parent or guardian.
Sec. 12-24. - Slaughtering animals; curing hides.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to establish or operate any abattoir or butcher pen or slaughter barn inside the city.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to cure hides in the city.
Sec. 12-25. - Public drunkenness.
It shall be unlawful for any person to be and appear within the corporate limits of the city:
(1)
In an intoxicated condition in any public place or within the curtilage of any private residence not his own unless by invitation of the owner or lawful occupant, which intoxicated condition is exhibited or may be manifest by boisterousness or by an indecent condition or act or by stupor or a deep sleep or by impairment of ambulation or by vulgar, profane, loud or unbecoming language. For the purposes of this section, public place shall mean any place where the conduct so exhibited or manifested may reasonably be expected to be viewed by persons other than members of the actor's family or household;
(2)
Consume alcoholic beverages on any public street, sidewalk or any other public property within the corporate limits of the city. Possession of open containers of alcoholic beverages or containers with the seal-broken shall be prima facie evidence of consumption;
(3)
Consume alcoholic beverages in a vehicle on or not on any public or private parking lot within the corporate limits of the city. Possession of open containers of alcoholic beverages or containers with the seal broken shall be prima facie evidence of consumption, it shall be unlawful for any person to consume while in public any alcoholic beverage unless the original container is concealed from view, except those maintained at establishments holding a valid on-premises license for the retail sale of beer or wine.
(Ord. No. 2012-3, Exh. A, 4-9-12)

Sec. 12-26. - Open containers of alcoholic beverages, consuming alcoholic beverages in motor vehicles.
It shall be unlawful for any person to consume alcoholic beverages or to have in possession any open container or package of alcoholic beverages while aboard any motor vehicle operated or parked on any public street or public property or on any private property open to public access or vehicular traffic unless such person is a passenger in a motor vehicle driven by a hired chauffeur or other driver which is licensed by the city or state to operate a motor vehicle for hire or is a passenger aboard a chartered carrier or a carrier wherein the possession or consumption of alcoholic beverages is allowed by law and unless such open container or package is in the trunk of the motor vehicle or kept in a locked glove compartment or in some other area of the vehicle not normally occupied by the driver or passenger. A container or package shall not be deemed open when the original seal is unbroken, and any receptacle with an open top or with a straw shall be classified as an open container or package.
Sec. 12-27. - Unauthorized accumulation of vehicles in a broken-down condition or without a license plate.
(a)
The unauthorized accumulation of one (1) or more vehicles, or any major part thereof, which does not have a current license plate or which are in any one (1) of the following conditions:
(1)
Resting on blocks;
(2)
Without wheels;
(3)
With major parts missing;
(4)
Without tires;
(5)
In a state of repair which precludes further use;

Remain motionless for an unreasonable period of time shall be declared unlawful, except where vehicles are stored in an enclosed building or located on the premises of licensed business enterprise operated in a lawful place or manner when necessary to the operation of such licensed business enterprise; provided, however, where any such motor vehicles, or a major part thereof, are not located in an enclosed building, such premises shall be screened on all sides by fence or shrubbery so as to shield such accumulation from sight;
(7)
A vehicle lacking a current, valid Georgia tag shall be considerable inoperable for the purposes of this section;
(8)
For the purposes of this section, either the owner of the real property upon which such vehicle is situated, or the lawful possessor in control of said real property, or both, may be held liable for violation of this section.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to discard and abandon on the streets of the city any broken-down vehicles. Such vehicles shall be properly disposed of by taking to a junkyard or other suitable place, and shall not be abandoned on the streets of the city.
(c)
Derelict motor vehicles; determination of status; disposition; violations and penalties.
(1)
If a motor vehicle has been left unattended on private property for not less than two (2) days or on public property for not less than three (3) days without the owner or driver making any attempt to recover such vehicle or to leave a conspicuously placed note that such owner or driver intends to return for such vehicle; or, if conspicuous note was left, if the motor vehicle has been left unattended for not less than five (5) days and if because of damage, vandalism, theft, or fire the vehicle is damaged to the extent that its restoration to an operable condition would require the replacement of one (1) or more major component parts or involves any structural damage that would affect the safety of the vehicle; or if there is evidence that the vehicle was inoperable due to major mechanical breakdown at the time it was left on the property, such as the engine, transmission, or wheels missing, no coolant in the cooling system, no oil in the engine, or burned fluid in the transmission; or if the vehicle is seven (7) or more years old; or if the vehicle is not currently tagged or is not verifiable by the state as to who is the current owner or lien holder of the vehicle; or if the vehicle has been abandoned to a wrecker service by an insurance company and the owner following the insurance company's making a total loss payment, then any person removing such vehicle shall within seventy-two (72) hours of removing such vehicle obtain the identity of and address of the last known registered owner of the vehicle, the owner of the vehicle as recorded on the certificate of title of such vehicle, and any security interest holder or lien holder on such vehicle from the local law enforcement agency of the jurisdiction in which the vehicle was located. If the law enforcement agency shows no information on the vehicle, then a request for such information shall be sent to the Department of Revenue. Within seventy-two (72) hours after obtaining such information, the person removing such vehicle shall, by certified mail or statutory overnight delivery, return receipt requested, notify the registered owner, title owner, and security interest holder or lien holder of the vehicle that such vehicle will be declared a derelict vehicle and the title to such vehicle will be canceled by the department of revenue if such person or persons fail to respond within ten (10) days of receipt of such notice. The state revenue commissioner shall prescribe the form and content of such notice. If the registered owner, title owner, or security interest holder or lien holder fails to respond within thirty (30) days from the date of such notice by certified mail or statutory overnight delivery, and if the vehicle is appraised as having a total value of less than three hundred dollars ($300.00), the vehicle shall be considered to be a derelict vehicle. The value of the vehicle shall be determined as fifty (50) percent of the wholesale value of a similar car in the rough section of the National Auto Research Black Book, Georgia Edition, or if a similar vehicle is not listed in such book or, regardless of the model year or book value of the vehicle, if the vehicle is completely destroyed by fire, flood, or vandalism or is otherwise damaged to the extent that restoration of the vehicle to a safe operable condition would require replacement of more than fifty (50) percent of its major component parts, the person shall obtain an appraisal of the motor vehicle from the local law enforcement agency's auto theft section with jurisdiction in the county or municipality where such vehicle is located. Any person removing a vehicle shall complete a form, to be provided by the department of revenue, indicating that the vehicle meets at least four (4) of the above-stated eight (8) conditions for being a derelict vehicle and shall file such form with the department of revenue and the law enforcement agency with jurisdiction from which such vehicle was removed.
(2)
For purposes of this Code section, the term "derelict vehicle" shall not include a vehicle which does not bear a manufacturers vehicle identification number plate or a vehicle identification number plate assigned by a state jurisdiction.
(3)
Any person who abandons a derelict motor vehicle on public or private property shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and upon conviction shall be fined not more than five hundred dollars ($500.00) and shall pay all costs of having such derelict motor vehicle removed, stored, and sold as provided for in this Code section.
(Ord. of 9-12-94; Ord. No. 2012-3, Exh. A, 4-9-12)
Sec. 12-28. - Loitering or prowling.
(a)
A person commits the offense of loitering or prowling when he is in a place at a time or in a manner not usual for law-abiding individuals under circumstances that warrant a justifiable and reasonable alarm or immediate concern for the safety of persons or property in the vicinity.
(b)
Among the circumstances which may be considered in determining whether alarm is warranted is the fact that the person takes flight upon the appearance of a law enforcement officer, refuses to identify himself, or manifestly endeavors to conceal himself or any object. Unless flight by the person or other circumstances make it impracticable, a law enforcement officer shall, prior to any arrest for an offense under this Code section, afford the person an opportunity to dispel any alarm or immediate concern which would otherwise be warranted by requesting the person to identify himself and explain his presence and conduct. No person shall be convicted of an offense under this Code section if the law enforcement officer failed to comply with the foregoing procedure or if it appears at trial that the explanation given by the person was true and would have dispelled the alarm or immediate concern.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to gather, loiter or remain for any period of time on any public or private property where such activity is clearly prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any person to gather, loiter or remain within one hundred (100) feet of any sign or intersection where it is clearly posted that such activity is unlawful.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person to loiter in any area which is clearly posted and has been declared a drug free zone or a designated bus stop area, without lawful cause.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for any person to loiter, gather or remain around any business or establishment which is closed or is not open for business.
(f)
A person committing the offense of loitering or prowling shall be guilty of a misdemeanor.
(Ord. No. 2012-3, Exh. A, 4-9-12)
Sec. 12-29. - Possession of marijuana.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to possess one (1) ounce or less of marijuana within the limits of the city.
(b)
The Municipal Court is granted jurisdiction to try and dispose of cases where a person is charged with the possession of one (1) ounce or less of marijuana if the offense occurred within the corporate limits of the city. The jurisdiction of the municipal court shall be concurrent with the jurisdiction of any other courts within the county having jurisdiction to try and dispose of such cases.
(c)
For an offense in violation of this Code section, a civil fine shall be assessed according to the following schedule: a civil fine not to exceed one hundred dollars ($100.00) for the first offense; a civil fine not to exceed two hundred dollars ($200.00) for the second offense; and, a civil fine not to exceed three hundred fifty dollars ($350.00) for any third or subsequent offense of this Code section. The finding of a violation under this Code section shall be a civil infraction and shall not constitute a criminal offense for any purpose.
(d)
Any person charged with possession of one (1) ounce or less of marijuana under this section shall be entitled, upon request, to have their case transferred to the court within the county having general misdemeanor jurisdiction over the alleged offense.
(e)
Each violation of this Code section shall be considered a separate and distinct offense, punishable as a civil offense with penalties as set forth under this Code section.
(Ord. No. 2012-3, Exh. A, 4-9-12; Ord. No. 20-05, § 1, 11-14-20)

Sec. 12-31. - Littering on public or private property or waters; enforcing personnel.

It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to dump, deposit, throw, or leave or to cause or permit the dumping, depositing, placing, throwing, or leaving of litter on any public or private property in this state or any waters in this state, unless:

Sec. 12-32. - Criminal trespass.
(a)
A person commits the offense of criminal trespass when he or she intentionally damages any property of another without consent of that other person and the damage thereto is five hundred dollars ($500.00) or less or knowingly and maliciously interferes with the possession or use of the property of another person without consent of that person.
(b)
A person commits the offense of criminal trespass when he or she knowingly and without authority:
(1)
Enters upon the land or premises of another person or into any part of any vehicle, railroad car, aircraft, or watercraft of another person for an unlawful purpose;
(2)
Enters upon the land or premises of another person or into any part of any vehicle, railroad car, aircraft, or watercraft of another person after receiving, prior to such entry, notice from the owner, rightful occupant, or, upon proper identification, an authorized representative of the owner or rightful occupant that such entry is forbidden; or
(3)
Remains upon the land or premises of another person or within the vehicle, railroad car, aircraft, or watercraft of another person after receiving notice from the owner, rightful occupant, or, upon proper identification, an authorized representative of the owner or rightful occupant to depart.
(c)
For the purposes of subsection (b) of this Code section, permission to enter or invitation to enter given by a minor who is or is not present on or in the property of the minor's parent or guardian is not sufficient to allow lawful entry of another person upon the land, premises, vehicle, railroad car, aircraft, or watercraft owned or rightfully occupied by such minor's parent or guardian if such parent or guardian has previously given notice that such entry is forbidden or notice to depart.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person to enter or to remain on any property where he or she has been given notice that such entry has been forbidden. It shall be unlawful for any person to enter onto the property of another for any unlawful purpose.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for any person to enter or to remain in any building, residence, motor vehicle or on any premises without authorization of the owner or his/her designee.
(f)
A person who commits the offense of criminal trespass shall be guilty of a misdemeanor.
(Ord. No. 2012-3, Exh. A, 4-9-12)
Sec. 12-33. - Open containers and possession and/or consumption of alcoholic beverages on public property; exceptions.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to have possession of an open container or to drink or to consume alcoholic beverages on the streets, sidewalks, alleyways or other public thoroughfares of the city or upon parks, playgrounds or other public property either inside or outside of any vehicle whether moving or parked, or any building or facility thereon, at any time. The prohibition contained herein shall also extend to and shall include the parking lots, public areas and other outdoor premises of any private business which is licensed to sell alcoholic beverages or which is otherwise licensed to conduct business where such areas are normally open to the general public.
