ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 4-1. - Planning commission.
(a)
A planning commission is hereby created for the county, as provided by the board of commissioners.
(b)
The planning commission shall have all the powers, duties and responsibilities set forth in the former statute known as the General Planning and Zoning Enabling Act of 1957, (Act No. 358), as amended.
(Res. of 3-2-82)
Cross reference— Administration, ch. 2; payment of expenses for members of county boards, § 2-2.
Sec. 4-2. - Area planning; county participation.
The county shall participate in the area planning commission region and shall participate on an equal financial basis ($0.25 per capita of population) with each county and city government comprising the Lower Chattahoochee Area Planning and Development Commission.
(Res. of 9-5-72)
Cross reference— Administration, ch. 2.
State Law reference— Regional development centers, O.C.G.A. § 50-8-30 et seq.
Sec. 4-3. - Regional land use policy plan.
The regional land use policy plan written by the staff of the Lower Chattahoochee Area Planning and Development Commission is hereby adopted, to use as a guide for the future growth and development of the county and to be a vital element of the county's ongoing comprehensive planning program.
(Res. of 2-21-78)
Sec. 4-4. - Regional housing policy plan.
The regional housing policy plan written by the staff of the Lower Chattahoochee Area Planning and Development Commission is hereby adopted, to use as a guide for the betterment of the housing conditions in the county.
(Res. of 2-21-78)
Cross reference— Housing authority, § 2-3.
Sec. 4-5. - Development authority.
(a)
There is hereby determined and declared to be a pressing, existing and future need for a development authority (as more fully described and defined in the Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.), to function in the county for the purpose of developing and promoting, for the public good and general welfare, trade, commerce, industry and employment opportunities in the county, thereby promoting the general welfare of the citizenry.
(b)
There is hereby activated in the county the public body corporate and politic known as the Development Authority of Harris County, which was created upon the adoption and approval of the Development Authorities Law and particularly section 2 thereof (O.C.G.A. § 36-62-4).
(c)
There shall be appointed seven members of the board of directors of the development authority, each of whom is a resident and taxpayer of the county and none of whom is an officer or employee of the county. They shall serve for staggered terms of six years.
(d)
If at the end of any term of office of any director a successor thereto shall not have been elected, then the director whose term of office shall have expired shall continue to hold office until his successor shall be so elected.
(e)
The board of directors shall organize itself, carry out its duties and responsibilities and exercise its powers and prerogatives in accordance with the terms and provisions of the Development Authorities Law as it now exists and as it may be amended.
(Res. of 5-18-76)
Cross reference— Administration, Ch. 2.
State Law reference— Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.
State Constitution reference— Development authorities, revenue bonds, Ga. Const. art. IX, § VI, ¶ III.
Sec. 4-6. - Construction codes.

(1)
International Building Code, 2006 Edition;
(2)
International Fuel Gas Code, 2006 Edition;
(3)
International Mechanical Code, 2006 Edition;
(4)
International Plumbing Code, 2006 Edition;
(5)
National Electric Code NFPA 70, 2005 Edition;
(6)
International Fire Code, 2006;
(7)
International Energy Conservation Code, 2006 Edition;
(8)
International Residential Code, 2006 Edition.
(b)
Additional codes adopted. The following state minimum optional codes are adopted to include any future additional or amendments as may be approved by the State of Georgia or the Georgia Department of Community Development:
(1)
International Property Maintenance Code, 2003 Edition;
(2)
International Existing Building Code, 2003 Edition.
(c)
Administration and enforcement of mandatory codes. Pursuant to Uniform Code Act (O.C.G.A. § 82-20 et seq.) specifically allowing local units of government to adopt reasonable administrative procedures for the local enforcement of the foregoing statewide mandatory codes in subsection 4-6(a), the following administrative enforcement procedures are adopted:
(1)

International Fuel Gas Code, 2006 Edition, chapter 1, Administration, except section 103, entitled "Department of Inspection"; and section 109, entitled "Means of Appeal" which sections are not adopted herewith;
(3)
International Mechanical Code, 2006 Edition, chapter 1, Administration, except section 103, entitled "Department of Mechanical Inspection"; and section 109, entitled "Means of Appeal" which sections are not adopted herewith;
(4)
International Plumbing Code, 2006 Edition, chapter 1, Administration, except section 103 entitled, "Department of Plumbing Inspection"; and section 109, entitled "Means of Appeal" which sections are not adopted herewith;

National Electric Code NFPA 70, 2005 Edition, except section 301, entitled "Department of Electrical Inspection" of chapter 3; and chapter 11, entitled "Means of Appeal"; and chapter 12, entitled "Administrative Provisions, 2006" which sections are not adopted herewith;
(6)
International Fire Code, 2006, chapter 1, Administration, except section 103, entitled "Department of Fire Prevention"; and section 108, entitled "Board of Appeals" which sections are not adopted herewith;
(7)
International Energy Conservation Code 2006 Edition, chapter 1, Administration;
(8)
International Residential Code 2006, Edition, chapter 1, Administration, except section R103, entitled "Department of Building Safety"; and section R112, entitled "Board of Appeals" which sections are not adopted herewith.
(d)
Administration and enforcement of additional codes. Pursuant to the Uniform Code Act (O.C.G.A. § 82-20 et. seq.) which specifically allows local units of government to adopt reasonable administrative procedures for the local enforcement of the foregoing additional state codes in subsection 4-6(b), the following administrative enforcement procedures sections are adopted:
(1)
International Property Maintenance Code, 2003 Edition, chapter 1, Administration, except section 103, entitled "Department of Property Maintenance Inspection" and section 111, entitled "Means of Appeals," which sections are not adopted herewith.
(2)
International Existing Building Code, 2006 Edition, chapter 1, Administration, except section 103, entitled "Department of Building Safety"; and section 112, entitled "Board of Appeals" which sections are not adopted herewith.
(e)
Appendices. Appendix G to the International Residential Code, 2006 Edition is adopted except definitions of above-ground/on-ground pool, hot tub, spa, portable and that portion of the definition swimming pool designated as "above-ground and in-ground" and "hot tubs and spas" of section AG102.1-General; section AG103.2-Above-ground and Underground Swimming Pool; section AG104.1-Permanently Installed Spas and Hot Tubs; AG104.2-Portable Spas and Hot Tubs; numbered Paragraph 10 of section AG105.2-Outdoor Swimming Pool; section AG105.4-Prohibited Locations; section AG105.5-Barrier Exceptions, which sections are not adopted; attachment E4106.5.1 and attachment E4103.3 entitled Disconnecting Means for Pool Utilization Equipment of the National Electrical Code are adopted but modified so as to read "One or more means to disconnect all underground conductors for utilization equipment, other than lighting, shall be provided. Each of such means shall be readily accessible and within sight from the equipment it serves."
(f)
Appeals. Board of construction appeals. All appeals under the foregoing mandatory codes and the additional codes shall be filed and determined as provided hereafter by the board of construction appeals, which shall consist of five members appointed by the county board of commissioners.
(1)
Membership. The members of the board of construction appeals shall be composed of individuals with knowledge and experience in the construction codes. A board member shall not act in a case in which he has a personal or financial interest.
(2)
Terms. The terms of office of the board member shall be staggered so no more than one-third of the board is appointed or replaced in any 12-month period. Vacancies shall be filled for an unexpired term in the manner in which original appointments are required to be made. Continued absence of any member from required meetings of the board shall, at the discretion of the county board of commissioners, render any such member subject to immediate removal from office.
(3)
Quorum and voting. A simple majority of the board shall constitute a quorum. In varying any provision of the construction codes, the affirmative votes of the majority present shall be required. In modifying a decision of the building official, not less than two affirmative votes shall be required. In the event that regular members are unable to attend a meeting, the alternate members, if appointed, shall vote.
(4)
Secretary of the board. The building official shall act as secretary of the board and shall make a record of all its proceedings, which shall set forth the reasons for its decision, the vote of each member, the absence of a member and any failure of a member to vote.
(5)
Powers. The construction board of adjustments and appeals shall have the power, as further defined in section 4-5, to hear the appeals of decisions and interpretations of the building official and consider variances of the construction codes.
(6)
Appeals of decisions of the building official. The owner of a building, structure or service system, or his duly authorized agent, may appeal a decision of the building official to the board of construction appeals when any one of the following conditions are claimed to exist:
a.
The building official rejected or refused to approve the mode or manner of construction proposed to be followed or materials to be used in the installation or alteration of a building, structure or service system in contradiction or contravention of any adopted code.
b.
The provisions of the construction codes do not apply to the specific case appealed.
c.
The true intent and meaning of the construction codes or any of the regulations thereunder have been misconstrued or incorrectly interpreted.
(7)
Variances. The board of construction appeals, when so appealed to and after a hearing, may reverse or alter the decision of the building official when, in its opinion, the enforcement thereof would do manifest injustice and would be contrary to the spirit and purpose of this or the construction codes or public interest, and the board as follows:
(a)
That the decision of the building official is in contradiction or contravention of any adopted code.
(b)
That the special conditions and circumstances appealed from do not result from the action or inaction of the applicant.
(c)
That reversal or alteration will not confer on the applicant any special privilege that is denied by the construction codes to other buildings, structures or service system.
(d)
That the grant of the relief sought by the appeal will be in harmony with the general intent and purpose of the construction codes and will not be detrimental to the public health, safety and general welfare.
(8)
Notice of appeal. Notice of appeal shall be in writing and filed within 30 calendar days after the building official renders the decision. Appeals shall be in a form acceptable to the building official. The board shall meet within 30 calendar days after notice of appeal has been received or on call of the chairman.
(9)
Unsafe or dangerous buildings or service systems. In the case of a building, structure, or service system, which, in the opinion of the building official, is unsafe, unsanitary or dangerous, the building official may, in his order, limit the time for such notice of appeals to a shorter period.
(10)
Decisions. The board of construction appeals shall in every case reach a decision without unreasonable or unnecessary delay. Each decision of the board shall also include the reasons for the decision. If a decision of the board reverses or modifies a refusal, order, or disallowance of the building official, the building official shall immediately take action in accordance with such decision. Every decision shall be promptly filed in writing in the office of the building official and shall be open to public inspection. A certified copy of the decision shall be sent by mail to the appellant. Every decision of the board shall be final with no right of appeal to the county board of commissioners.
(Ord. No. 6-84, § 1, 11-6-84; Ord. No. 2-86, §§ 1, 4—7, 11-21-86; Ord. No. 3-86, §§ 1, 3, 12-16-86; Res. No. 4-91, 10-15-91; Ord. No. 1-95, 1-17-95; Ord. No. 1-96, 5-7-96; Ord. No. 2-97, 5-20-97; Ord. No. 08-07, § 1, 11-20-2007)
Sec. 4-7. - Agricultural use structures.
Structures which are built for agricultural purposes and are located in an agricultural (A-1) zoning district shall not require a building permit so long as such structures meet all of the following criteria:
(1)
The structure is to be used only for the storage of agricultural products, farm equipment and machinery, or as shelter for farm animals and/or livestock, and the structure shall not be used for human habitation, and;
(2)
The structure shall be served by not more than 100 amp of electricity, provided, however, an electrical permit and designated inspection are required. No permit is required for a building without electricity, and;
(3)
The structure shall not contain toilet facilities, and;
(4)
Prior to beginning construction, the owner shall complete a zoning compliance form verifying, under oath, that the structure meets the criteria set forth herein, and;
(5)
Should the structure be altered or converted to a use which is inconsistent with the criteria outlined herein, this exemption shall not apply to such alterations and the owner must apply for a building permit and otherwise comply with all of the requirements of the applicable building code before proceeding with such alteration.
(6)
The structure shall not be greater in size than 1,500 square feet, nor shall it be taller than one story; provided, however, that such story shall not exceed 20 feet in clearance height.
(7)
Regardless of whether or not a building qualifies for a permit exemption, all structures must be built according to the standards as required by the applicable state and county building codes.
(Ord. No. 08-07, § 2, 11-20-2007)

Sec. 4-10. - Permits.
(a)
Required.
(1)
It shall be unlawful to construct or alter any building or structure in the county, excepting fences, when the cost of such construction exceeds $1,000.00 or where the effect of such construction or alteration is to enlarge the capacity or affect the bearing walls, or change the occupancy of any building or the roof thereof without having first secured a permit from the county governing authority therefor.
(2)
In the event an owner, agent or contractor is in violation for existing permit(s), the community development director is authorized not to issue a new permit to a separate location until the existing permit is in conformance with the minimum state codes and/or ordinances of the county.
(b)
Application. Application for such permits shall be made to the community development director and shall be accompanied by plans and specifications in duplicate showing the work to be done; such plans shall be verified by the signature of either the owner of the premises or by the architect, engineer, or contractor in charge of the operations as the situation may require.
(c)
Approval of application required. Such applications with plans shall be examined by the community development director to determine whether the proposed construction or alteration will comply with all provisions relative thereof. Upon his approval, one set of plans shall be returned to the applicant with a permit, and the other set of plans shall be retained by the community development director. No permit shall be issued until after approval of the plans and payment of proper fees.
(d)
Variations. It shall be unlawful to vary materially from the submitted plans and specifications unless such variations are submitted in an amended plan to the community development director and approved by him.
(e)
Building permit fees.
(1)
A minimum fee as provided for in the current permit fee schedule approve by the board of commissioners for the department of community development (hereinafter referred to as "schedule") shall be charged, as applied to building permits only.
(2)
The community development director shall determine, from the plans submitted, the square foot area of the proposed buildings or alteration.
a.
Single-family dwellings and multifamily dwellings shall be charged at the rate as denoted in the schedule. This fee covers all additional permits required, including plumbing, electrical, mechanical (HVAC), and gas.
b.
Manufactured housing units shall be charged the amount as denoted in the schedule.
c.
Out buildings of 800 square feet of less and without utilities: No fee.
d.
Out-buildings exceeding 800 square feet with utilities: Charged the amount as denoted in schedule.
e.
Barns, poultry houses, and other livestock structures including utilities: charged such rate as denoted in the schedule.
f.
Commercial and industrial permit fees shall be in accordance with the schedule. Valuations utilized in establishing fees will be compiled by the county tax assessor's office using the latest data of the "Marshall Valuation Service" as published by the Marshall and Swift Publication Company.
g.
Moving of building or structures will be at that fee in accordance with the schedule.
h.
Demolition of buildings or structures will be at that fee in accordance with the schedule.
i.
Remodeling or additions to residential units shall be charged at the rate denoted in schedule, including all additional permits.
(3)
Swimming pool fees:
a.
Residential (private) and commercial (public) swimming pools are at such fee as denoted in schedule.
(4)
Doubled fees: Where work for which a permit is required is started or proceeded with prior to obtaining the necessary permit, the fees herein specified shall be doubled. However, the payment of such doubled fees shall not relieve any person, firm, or corporation from complying fully with the requirements of the appropriate code nor from any other penalties prescribed by the appropriate code.
(5)
When it is necessary to make reinspection of an item originally rejected of any nature, such appropriate fee as denoted in the schedule to be paid at the office of community development prior to reinspection.
(Ord. No. 1-87, §§ 1—6, 7-21-87; Ord. No. 4-91, §§ 1—6, 9-3-91; Ord. No. 3-97, 6-17-97; Ord. No. 08-07, § 4, 11-20-2007)
Sec. 4-11. - Plan review; procedures; fees.
(a)
When deemed necessary by the community development director, plans that are submitted to the department of community development for the purpose of obtaining a permit for the fabrication, erection, construction, enlargement, alteration, or repair of buildings and structures shall be forwarded to the Southern Building Code Congress International, Inc., for its review and comments.
(b)
When plans are required by the community development director to be reviewed by the Southern Building Code Congress International, Inc., a nonrefundable plan review fee shall be paid to the board of commissioners by the applicant at the time application is made for a building permit. The fee shall be in accordance with the schedule established from time to time by the Southern Building Code Congress International, Inc., and as said schedule is retained by the department of community development.
(c)
The fees described in subsection (b) are for a plan review using the Standard Building Code. There is a base fee multiplier of 20 percent additional for reviews including any other Standard Codes (plumbing, gas, mechanical, etc.).
(Ord. of 10-21-86; Ord. No. 9-95, 12-19-95)
Sec. 4-12. - Driveway permit.
(a)
Property owners shall secure a permit from the county for the construction of driveways leading from county roads, to give access to their property.
(b)
Any person desiring a driveway leading from a county road shall make application for a permit from the director of public works at the county correctional institute to construct the driveway.
(c)
The application for a permit shall contain the following information:
(1)
The name, address and phone number of the applicant;
(2)
The location of the property; and
(3)
The date the driveway is to be installed.
The permit shall contain the same information that is contained on the application.
(d)
Upon receipt of the application, the director of public works shall inspect the site to determine if a driveway pipe is required and that the slope is in accordance with county standards. If a pipe is required, he shall establish the size and length of pipe needed.
(e)
If a pipe is needed, it shall be installed under the supervision of the director of public works to ensure installation in accordance with county standards.
(f)
The permit shall be posted in a conspicuous place at the site during installation.
(g)
If an extension of time is required by the applicant to install the pipe or construct the driveway, an extension may be granted by the director of public works if request is made seven days prior to the expiration of the permit.
(h)
Failure to comply with any of the conditions in this section may result in the driveway being closed by the county until compliance is obtained.
(Ord. No. 2-84, 2-21-84)
