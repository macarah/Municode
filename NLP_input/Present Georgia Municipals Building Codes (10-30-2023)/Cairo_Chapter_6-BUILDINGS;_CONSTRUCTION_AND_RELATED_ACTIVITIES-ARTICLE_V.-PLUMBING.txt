ARTICLE V. - PLUMBING
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 6-120. - Compliance with article.
All plumbing done within the city shall be done under the rules and regulations set out in this article.
(Code 1965, § 25-1; Ord. No. 166, § 1, 12-14-70)
Sec. 6-121. - Plumbing inspector.
All plumbing done in the city, or outside the city when the work is or shall be connected to the sewer system or water system of the city, shall be done under the direction of the plumbing inspector. Whenever in this article, the term "inspector" or "plumbing inspector" is used, it shall mean the building official or someone duly authorized by the building official as such inspector.
(Code 1965, §§ 25-2, 25-3(1); Ord. No. 166, § 2, 12-14-70)
Sec. 6-122. - Appeals.
Any person shall have the right of appeal from any ruling of the plumbing inspector to the governing body, which ruling shall be final.
(Code 1965, § 25-3(3))
Sec. 6-123. - Plumbing code adopted by reference.
Except as otherwise provided in this article, all installations of plumbing and all materials used shall be in conformity with the provisions of the statutes of the state and any orders, rules and regulations issued by authority thereof; and with approved plumbing standards for safety to persons and property. Where no specific standards are prescribed by this article or by the statutes of the state or by any orders, rules, or regulations issued by authority thereof, conformity with the provisions of the latest edition as adopted and amended by the Georgia Department of Community Affairs (DCA) of the Standard Plumbing Code shall be prima facie evidence of conformity with approved standards for safety to persons or to property. Such code is hereby adopted by reference as fully as if set out at length herein, subject however, to all amendments and conflicting provisions contained in this Code of Ordinances. One copy of such code is on file in the office of the city clerk which shall be the official copy of said code and so designated by said city clerk.
(Code 1965, § 25-60; Ord. No. 159, § 3, 4-13-70; Ord. No. 286, § 4, 7-8-85; Ord. No. 334, § 7, 9-26-88; Ord. No. 440, § 13, 12-12-94; Ord. No. 501, § 5, 1-12-98)
State Law reference— State code, OCGA, § 8-2-20.
Sec. 6-124. - Reserved.
Editor's note— Ord. No. 501, § 6, adopted Jan. 12, 1998, provided for the deletion of § 6-124, water conservation fixtures, derived from Ord. No. 392, § 1, adopted Feb. 11, 1991.
Sec. 6-125. - Election to exempt from state provisions.
Pursuant to the authority contained in section 3 of Act No. 1046 and enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Georgia during the 1996 session of the legislature, section (d) of Code section 8-2-26 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated (O.C.G.A. section 8-2-26(d)) relating to self inspection of water and sewer projects by master plumbers or utility contractors, shall not be applicable within the city.
(Ord. No. 466, § 1, 5-28-96)

DIVISION 2. - CERTIFICATION OF PLUMBERS

Sec. 6-130. - Certificate and registration required.
Every master plumber desiring to do business in the city shall be the holder of a valid and current certificate of competency issued by the state board of examiners of plumbing contractors and shall register with the city clerk as provided by this Code before engaging in plumbing business in the city.
(Code 1965, § 25-25; Ord. No. 166, § 4, 12-14-70; Ord. No. 440, § 15, 12-12-94)
Cross reference— Occupational licenses, § 14-13; septic tank cleaner regulations, § 14-16.
State Law reference— Plumbers, OCGA § 43-14-1.
Sec. 6-131. - Bond.
(a)
Every master plumber shall execute a bond with surety, payable to the city in the sum of five thousand dollars ($5,000.00), conditioned to protect the city against all loss or damage occasioned by the negligence of the principal therein in failing to properly execute and protect all work done by such principal or employees of such principal, or under such principal's direction or supervision; from all loss or damage occasioned by or arising in any manner from any such work which is not caused by the negligence of the city or its agents or employees; that principal will keep and observe all provisions of this Code and all ordinances at any time enacted by the city relating to plumbing; that the principal shall, without additional cost to the person for whom the work is done, remedy any defects therein due to faulty workmanship or incorrect construction or due to faulty material furnished or used; that the principal shall reconstruct and repair such work to the satisfaction of the plumbing inspector at any time within one year after the construction, repair or installation thereof, and after forty-eight (48) hours' notice from the plumbing inspector.
(b)
The bond shall be approved by the city attorney and city clerk before it becomes effective, and may be sued on, in any court of competent jurisdiction in the state.
(Code 1965, § 25-26; Ord. No. 445, § 1, 3-13-95)
Sec. 6-132. - Allowing use of name by another.
No licensed plumber shall allow such plumber's name to be used by any person, directly or indirectly, for the purpose of obtaining a permit or to do any plumbing work.
(Code 1965, § 25-28)

Sec. 6-140. - Scope of work under permit; city performing work beyond property line.
(a)
Work to be done under a plumbing permit is all of that work beyond the street line or property line.
(b)
The city shall make all connections to the city sewer, make all excavations, lay all pipe, and repair all paving necessary to construct the sewer line to the street line or property line.
(Code 1965, § 25-53(1), (2)
Sec. 6-141. - Separate plumbing system for separate buildings.
(a)
The drainage and plumbing system of each new building and of new work installed in existing buildings shall be separate from and independent of that of any other building, except as provided in this section, and every building shall have an independent connection with a public or private sewer when available.
(b)
Where one building stands in the rear of another building on an interior lot and no private sewer is available or can be constructed to the rear building through adjoining alley, court, yard, or driveway, the house drain from the front building may be extended to the rear building and the whole will be considered as one house drain, provided both properties are owned by the same person.
(Code 1965, § 25-59)
Sec. 6-142. - Inspection fees.
There shall be charged and collected by the city an inspection fee for all new, old and remodeled plumbing inspected by the plumbing inspector as set forth within this code.
(Code 1965, § 25-51; Ord. No. 440, § 15, 12-12-94)
