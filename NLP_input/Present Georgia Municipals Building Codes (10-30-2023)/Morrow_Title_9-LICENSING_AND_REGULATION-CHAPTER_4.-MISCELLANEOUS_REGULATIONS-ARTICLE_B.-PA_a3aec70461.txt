ARTICLE B. - PAWNBROKERS[6]

Footnotes:
--- (6) ---
State Law reference— Pawnbrokers, O.C.G.A. § 44-12-130 et seq.; municipal supervision, O.C.G.A. § 44-12-136.

Sec. 9-4-20. - Purposes of article; authority.
This article is enacted in accordance with the plan designed for the purposes, among others, of promoting the health and general welfare of the community, of establishing reasonable and ascertainable standards for the regulation and control of pawnshops or similar places where money is advanced on goods or other effects or where merchandise of any kind is taken in pawn, and also similar places, better known as swap shops, that engage solely in buying, selling and trading used merchandise from and with an individual.
(Ord. No. 2003-08, § 1B.(1), 7-22-03)
Sec. 9-4-21. - Definitions.
The following words and terms shall have the following meanings for purposes of this section.
(1)
Pawnbrokers or pawn or swap shops means any establishments which (a) engage in the business of providing money to customers on a temporary basis, wherein such loans are secured by post-dated check, paycheck or car title, or (b) are registered as lenders under state or federal law. This classification does not include a state or federally chartered bank, savings association, credit union, or industrial land company. Further, this classification does not include establishments selling consumer goods, including consumables, where the cashing of checks or money orders is incidental to the main purpose of the business.
(2)
Pledged goods means tangible personal property other than choses in action, securities, or printed evidences of indebtedness, which property is purchased by, deposited with, or otherwise actually delivered into the possession of a pawnbroker in the course of his business.
(3)
Pledgor means the person who promises performance by a pledge.
(Code 1977, § 8-4011(a); Ord. No. 2003-08, § 1B.(2), 7-22-03)
Sec. 9-4-22. - License required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell, attempt to sell, buy, barter, or otherwise deal in the lending of money on the security of purchased goods, without first registering with the business license administrator and obtaining a business license for such transactions. These transactions will be limited to businesses that are eligible for licensing in the city as dominant business activities, and that have been granted a conditional use permit.
(Code 1977, § 8-4011(b))
Sec. 9-4-23. - Licensee required to have at least a one year lease on business site.
A prospective licensee must have at least a one-year lease on the business site for which the application applies or own the business site. The express purpose of this provision is to prohibit lending from temporary locations.
(Code 1977, § 8-4011(c))
Sec. 9-4-24. - Review of application.
A background investigation of the applicant will be conducted by the police department, and such findings shall be furnished to the city manager, who shall make his recommendations to the planning and zoning board and the mayor and council. No license shall be issued except upon the recommendation of the planning and zoning board and approval of the mayor and council under the conditional use provision of this Code.
(Code 1977, § 8-4011(k))
Sec. 9-4-25. - Limitation on issuance.
(a)
Only one pawnbrokers license shall be issued per 6,000 population.
(b)
No pawnbrokers license shall be issued to any person or to any pawnbroker who employs anyone who has been convicted of any crime involving as an element thereof, the theft of property.
(Code 1977, § 8-4011(l); Ord. No. 88-11, § 4, 1-10-89; Ord. No. 2001-16, § 1, 9-11-01)
Sec. 9-4-26. - Regulatory fee.
The annual regulatory fee for each new pawnbroker doing business within the city, or for each individual establishment maintained for the purpose of doing business as pawnbrokers within the city, shall be $4,500.00. For subsequent calendar years, the actual number of pawn tickets processed by the police department shall be tallied at the end of each calendar year and a refund will be issued or an additional amount will be charged to existing establishments based on the processing fee of $0.75 per pawn ticket.
(Code 1977, § 8-4011(m(1); Ord. No. 89-01, § 1, 2-28-89; Ord. No. 94-15, § 1.E, 12-13-94; Ord. No. 98-13, § 2, 11-24-98)
Sec. 9-4-27. - Hours of operation.
The hours within which pawnbrokers may keep open their places of business shall be from 7:00 a.m. to 8:00 p.m. except Saturdays and the week before Christmas, when the hours of closing shall be 12:00 midnight.
(Code 1977, § 8-4011(m(2))
Sec. 9-4-28. - Pawn tickets.
Pawn tickets will be regulated by the following and reflected on a form approved by the license administrator:
(1)
Positive identification will be made of any prospective pledgor to include, but not be limited to, name, date of birth, address, right thumb print on form provided, driver's license number, social security number, race, sex, and signature of pledgor.
(2)
Transactions will not be made with a minor under 18 years of age.
(3)
Any pawnbroker or person operating under a pawnbroker's license who takes goods on pawn or buys goods, taking full title thereto, shall hold such goods so taken in pawn or purchased for at least 14 days before disposing of same by sale, transfer, shipment or otherwise.
(Code 1977, § 8-4011(d))
Sec. 9-4-29. - False names.
It shall be unlawful for any person to use a false or fictitious name or address as his own, or to give the name or address of any other person without that person's knowledge and approval, as a means of identification while selling or attempting to sell, or pawning, anything of value.
(Code 1977, § 8-4011(e))
Sec. 9-4-30. - Records to be kept; information to be shown; inspection authorized.
(a)
All pawnbrokers shall keep books wherein shall be entered an accurate description of all property pledged or sold to them. Such description shall include: (i) the name of the manufacturer of the article; (ii) any identifying marks or numbers; (iii) a statement of the kind of material of which it is made.
(b)
In such books there shall be entered also (i) the name of the person by whom same was deposited or sold; and (ii) the time when the transaction was accomplished.
(c)
These entries shall be made as soon after the transaction as possible, but in no event later than one hour thereafter.
(d)
Such books and the articles themselves so pledged or sold shall at all times be subject to inspection and examination by any duly authorized law enforcement officer and the license administrator.
(e)
Any person engaged in the business of a pawnbroker, who shall fail or refuse to keep books as provided in this section, or who shall make false entries concerning the transactions named, or who shall fail or refuse to permit an inspection and examination by any duly authorized law enforcement officer of the books and of the property pledged with or sold to them shall be considered to be in violation of this section and shall be punished as provided in this Code.
(Code 1977, § 8-4011(f))
Sec. 9-4-31. - Daily report to police.
(a)
Every pawnbroker shall make a daily report in writing to the chief of police, in such form as may be prescribed by the chief, of all property pledged to or brought by him during the 24 hours ending at 8:00 p.m. on the date of the report. Such report shall be typewritten. In addition to any other information required by the chief of police, they shall show:
(1)
The name and address of the pawnbroker;
(2)
The time of each and every transaction;
(3)
Serial number of pawn tickets;
(4)
Amount paid or advanced for each transaction; and
(5)
Full description of articles, including kind, style, material, color, design, kind and number of stones in jewelry, together with all identifying names, marks, and numbers thereon.
(b)
Insufficient reports shall be rejected and any pawnbroker making such shall be deemed guilty of a violation of this section.
(Code 1977, § 8-4011(g))
Sec. 9-4-32. - Pledge of building glass and plumbing material.
It shall be unlawful for pawnbrokers to purchase, advance money on, or take in pawn, glass taken from buildings, such as mantle mirrors, art glass, and the like, or copper wire, copper lining of bathtubs, lead pipes, or any kind of plumbing material sold as junk, where same is offered for sale or pawn by other than persons of known good character. In all cases where such goods are purchased or money advanced, the pawnbroker shall keep a record of such purchases or advances, showing the names of the persons from whom received, their residence, the goods so taken, and the amount of money paid or advanced, and this shall be at all times open for inspection by any duly authorized law enforcement officer. In addition, it shall be unlawful for pawnbrokers to purchase any such goods, or to advance money thereon, where their appearance indicates that they have been tampered with, or there is an effort to conceal identity, or the goods are mutilated or otherwise show an effort to destroy evidence of the former location of the property. While such goods are being kept for 14 days before being disposed of, they shall be so arranged that they can be inspected by all duly authorized law enforcement officers upon demand.
(Code 1977, § 8-4011(h))
Sec. 9-4-33. - Minors.
No pawnbroker shall have any business dealings as a pawnbroker with a minor, nor shall a pawnbroker's license be issued to a minor, nor shall a pawnbroker employ a minor to assist him in his business.
(Code 1977, § 8-4011(i))
Sec. 9-4-34. - Stolen goods.
It shall be the duty of every pawnbroker to report to the police any article pledged with him or which is sought to be pledged with him, if he shall have reason to believe the article was stolen, or lost and found by the person attempting to pledge it in the case of a lost article. No pawnbroker shall accept any item, good, or ware upon which a manufacturer's identity or serial number has been removed, obliterated, or in any way caused to become unreadable.
(Code 1977, § 8-4011(j))
