Sec. 22-381. - Purpose and authority.
This article shall apply to the establishment, operation, and maintenance of all outdoor cafes within the City of Madison on public sidewalks. The purpose of article is to promote the general economic development and atmosphere of the city for the benefit of businesses and citizens located there, and no right of individuals or individual businesses are created therein, and to create an aesthetic ambiance which will attract tourists to the Madison area. Further, this article is designed to insure the primary purpose of the public rights-of-way by maintaining the efficiency of the pedestrian path for both safety and convenience. This article is adopted pursuant to the authority granted by the Official Code of Georgia Annotated and the constitution of the state, for the purpose of maintaining the health, safety and welfare of the city, and to ensure that sidewalk cafe operations within the city on public property are conducted in a manner consistent with the health, safety and welfare of the residents of the city.
(Amd. of 11-12-07(1), § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 22-382. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Access zone means that portion of the public sidewalk reserved for continuous unobstructed pedestrian traffic and ingress/egress to businesses and structures lining the public rights-of-way.
Amenity zone means that portion of public sidewalk for siting of municipal streetscape elements, such as street trees, street lights, benches, trash cans, sign posts, etc.; approved sidewalk cafe areas; and designated street vending locations.
Appearance zone means that portion of the sidewalk as approved for downtown beautification or business enhancement, including planters and benches, product display and sidewalk signs, tables and chairs, and literature racks.
License means the sidewalk cafe license issued for the lawful use of the public property, specifically sidewalks, by restaurants.
Licensee means the holder of a sidewalk cafe license under the terms and provisions of this ordinance.
Manager means the city manager, or his designee.
Obstacle means any object or item whether situated upon or hanging over the right-of-way, including but not limited to noticeable changes in grade or paving materials.
Restaurant means a commercial establishment characterized by table service where food and beverages are prepared, served, and consumed primarily within the principal building. The term encompasses cafes, grills, and lunch counters but excluding without limitation drive-in and drive-thru restaurants, as well as temporary, mobile, and free-standing food service vendors. For the purposes of this article only, the term shall also encompass confectionary and dessert shops.
Restaurant facade means the front of the building, specifically the elevation containing the primary entrance of the restaurant, unless otherwise determined by the manager.
Public property means property owned, leased, or maintained by the City of Madison including but not limited to streets, byways or rights-of-way, sidewalks, parking lots, lots of record with or without structures, parks, and easements.
Sidewalk means that portion of the right-of-way improved for use by pedestrians located between the curb line or the edge of an improved roadway and the adjacent property line.
Sidewalk cafe means the use of a sidewalk cafe area for the service of food and beverage.
Sidewalk cafe area means the area within the amenity zone for placement of cafe furnishings on the public sidewalk and of operation of a sidewalk cafe as an extension and incidental activity of a city-licensed restaurant. Sidewalk frontage means the length of frontage along the property line of that portion of any building occupied by the licensed restaurant to which the sidewalk cafe is incidental.
(Amd. of 11-12-07(1), § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 22-383. - Exceptions.
Outdoor dining areas on private property are not encompassed herein but are addressed in the official zoning ordinance and other city development regulations.
(Amd. of 11-12-07(1), § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 22-384. - License required and fee; license non-transferable and expiration.
It shall be unlawful for any person or business to operate a sidewalk cafe in the city without having first obtained a license or to fail to comply with all sections of this article. A sidewalk cafe license is valid for one year from January 1 to December 31. A new license shall be procured each year, which shall be the responsibility of the license holder. The application fee for the license shall be the fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and shall be paid at city hall upon the filing of an application for the license no later than December 15 of the preceding year.
No license shall be transferable or assignable to any person or other location. Licenses will expire upon transfer of ownership, change in use of facility, and on December 31 of each year, unless revoked or suspended prior to expiration. The issuance of the license shall not be construed, interpreted, or considered to grant or convey any property rights, any lease holder rights, or any interest in the public right-of-way. The provisions of this article are subject to change and amendment by the city from time to time.
(Amd. of 11-12-07(1), § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 22-385. - Items needed for license application; notification.
An applicant for a license shall submit the following items for review:
(1)
A completed application form;
(2)
A copy of a valid city occupational/business license to operate a restaurant in front of which the sidewalk cafe is proposed to be located;
(3)
A copy of the current certificate of insurance in the amounts and categories required by section 22-390 of this article;
(4)
A scaled drawing identifying at a minimum the following:
a.
The restaurant footprint;
b.
The width of the sidewalk frontage, known as the restaurant facade and the distance between the restaurant facade and the curb in front of the restaurant facade;
c.
The dimensions of the proposed sidewalk cafe;
d.
The distance between the proposed sidewalk cafe and the nearest obstacle around the perimeter of the sidewalk cafe; and,
e.
The quantity, design, and materials of the proposed cafe furnishings for the sidewalk cafe.
(5)
A nonrefundable license application fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges.
The city manager, or his designee, shall process applications, ensure site sufficiency based upon the application materials in section 22-385 and observing considerations of section 22-386. The applicant shall not change, increase, or decrease the sidewalk cafe or modify its parameters as outlined in the application without written authorization from the manager.
(Amd. of 11-12-07(1), § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 22-386. - Consideration of license.
In deciding whether or not to grant, renew, suspend, or revoke a license under this article, the manager shall consider the following:
(1)
Compliance by the applicant or license holder with the provisions of this article and all other relevant laws or ordinances, regulations and resolution of the city and any other governmental body or agency;
(2)
Payment record and status of occupation tax, alcohol license fees, or other monies owed by the applicant or restaurant submitting the license application;
(3)
The character and reputation of the applicant or license holder;
(4)
The conduct of the restaurant of the applicant or license holder;
(5)
The actual or proposed effect of the sidewalk cafe on the primary purpose and use of the public right-of-way, including but not limited to the efficiency and clearance of the pedestrian access zone; on-street parking; vehicular travel lanes; loading/unloading areas; hydrants and utility access; crosswalks and accessibility ramps; driveways and alleys; transit or bus service stops; and intersection visibility;
(6)
The actual or proposed effect of the sidewalk cafe given the proximate right-of-way area; sidewalk dimensions, materials, and existing condition; obstacles and obstructions; street signs; streetscape elements such as street trees, street lights, benches, trash cans, sign posts, etc.; and proximity of other sidewalk cafes;
(7)
The actual or proposed effect of the sidewalk cafe on nearby properties, including but not limited to the impact upon adjoining property owners and/or tenants as well as the proximity of nearby sensitive uses such as residential uses, healthcare facilities, lodging establishments, public buildings and restrooms, parks, and other areas which may be impacted by heightened pedestrian activity and traffic, noise, odor, smoke, litter, or property values and all other relevant considerations;
(8)
All other relevant issues related to the public health, safety, welfare, morals, interest, and convenience.
(Amd. of 11-12-07(1), § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 22-387. - Location by zoning district.
No sidewalk cafe license shall be allowed except in areas as provided within the current official zoning ordinance.
(Amd. of 11-12-07(1), § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 22-388. - Incidental seating limitations.
Seating in the sidewalk cafe area shall not exceed 30 percent of the seating in the restaurant to which the sidewalk cafe is incidental. No sidewalk cafe seating authorized herein shall be used for calculating seating requirements pertaining to the location of, applications for, or issuance of a license to operate a restaurant or to sell alcohol, nor shall sidewalk cafe seating be used to claim any exemption from any other requirements of any city, county or state codes, ordinances and/or laws.
(Amd. of 11-12-07(1), § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 22-389. - Conditions for operation.
Except as otherwise provided herein, a sidewalk cafe operating in the city shall comply with the following conditions:
(1)
Pedestrian clearance. A minimum of five feet shall be reserved at all times for continuous unobstructed pedestrian traffic along the public sidewalk. This reserved access zone shall not be encroached upon even if it precludes a sidewalk cafe. Additionally, a minimum of one foot of unobstructed space shall be reserved at all times between any sidewalk cafe perimeter and the nearest obstacle, including but not limited to streetscape elements and the curb.
(2)
Limitation on area. The area in which a sidewalk cafe is operated shall be located in the amenity zone, limited to the width of sidewalk frontage of the licensed restaurant, and limited to the depth defined by the existing street tree islands. Only the approved sidewalk cafe area shall be used, and said area shall not be modified without the written consent of the manager.
a.
The determination of whether and to what extent the sidewalk cafe interferes with the considerations outlined in section 22-386 shall be made by the manager at the time of application based on the characteristics of each proposed site.
b.
For corner locations or where extreme right-of-way limitations exist, the manager may consider alternative areas of the same size or less in the immediate proximity for sidewalk cafe locations provided that [sub]section 22-389(1) is met.
c.
To allow for maximum sidewalk cafe potential in the city and where obstacles prevent the sidewalk cafe from utilizing the full width of the sidewalk frontage, the manager at his reasonable discretion may consider an extension of the sidewalk cafe equal to the area unable to be used because of the obstacle provided: the adjacent area is not a clear passage area as per [sub]section 62-4(2), the adjacent area is not currently licensed as a sidewalk cafe area or a street vending location, and all other provisions of this ordinance are met. The manager may also take into consideration the basis, not fact, of any objections proffered by an adjoining property or business owner(s). In no instance shall a sidewalk cafe area extension exceed 55 feet from the primary service entrance.
(3)
Cafe furnishings. Cafe furnishings shall be limited to tables, chairs, umbrellas, and cigarette receptacles, which are subject to the manager's reasonable approval. Only approved furnishings shall be located in the sidewalk cafe, and said furnishings shall not be modified without written consent of the manager.
a.
Design. All furnishings shall be fire-retardant or manufactured of fire resistant material. Tables and chairs shall be metal and either black or dark green in color. Umbrellas shall be sized appropriately to be at least the same diameter as the table and not protrude beyond the side cafe area, shall be anchored with a weighted base, and shall be color coordinated with the restaurant to which the sidewalk cafe is incidental.
b.
Condition. Cafe furnishings are to be kept clean, attractive, sanitary, safe, operable, in good repair, and in structurally sound condition at all times. If the manager judges any furnishing to be non-compliant with this section, said furnishing is subject to immediate removal by the licensee.
(4)
Sanitation. It shall be the responsibility and duty of the restaurant to which the sidewalk cafe license is issued to maintain the area covered by the license in a clean, neat, and orderly manner at all times. The area shall be clear of litter, food scraps, soiled dishes and other debris, including but not limited to cigarette butts. The licensee shall daily remove from the public right-of-way grease, stains, food spots, etc. caused by the operation of the sidewalk cafe.
a.
It shall also be the responsibility of the license holder to wash the sidewalk surface on which the sidewalk cafe is located no less often than once weekly and pressure wash upon request by the manager.
b.
Licensee shall sweep the sidewalk and collect debris prior to pressure washing and shall maintain records of the date sidewalk was pressure washed and by whom, and such records shall be open to inspection by the city. Pressure washing shall be conducted using water only and the use of cleaning agents is prohibited.
(5)
Service and activity privileges. The following service privileges are also extended as part of the sidewalk cafe license:
a.
Beverage and food. Any sidewalk cafe that serves beverages must also provide food service. No outside service of food or beverages is permitted after 11:00 p.m. and all outside consumption shall cease at 11:30 p.m. Each table shall have a provide a posting provided by the city to inform patrons of relevant downtown ordinances.
1.
Sidewalk cafes are considered part of the restaurant premises and operated as an extension and incidental activity of a restaurant including both food and beverage service, provided that the restaurant to which the sidewalk cafe is licensed maintains valid business and pouring licenses under the applicable Georgia law and the regulations of the city and maintains compliance with all sections of this article.
2.
Sidewalk cafes are exempt from any restrictions on open containers upon the restaurant obtaining a permit from the state department of revenue. However, all alcoholic beverages shall be served in clear containers (e.g. no beer bottles, taps, kegs, coolers, etc.). If a restaurant prefers not to decant a wine bottle, then said bottle shall be fully obscured by placement within an opaque serving vessel or opaque cloth.
3.
If an alcoholic beverage is served to a patron, the patron must be advised that he is not allowed to leave the premises of the sidewalk cafe area with the alcoholic beverage, except to go inside the restaurant to which the sidewalk cafe is incidental. Patrons shall not walk from one establishment to another with an alcoholic beverage.
b.
Glass containers. The city reserves the right to suspend the privilege of using glass containers, including other types of ceramic serving vessels or plates, within the sidewalk cafe during any permitted special events or when streets are closed. The use of such containers will be revoked if an incident jeopardizes the health, safety and welfare of customers or the general public.
c.
Smoking. Sidewalk cafes may be designated as outdoor smoking areas for the restaurant provided that an approved cigarette receptacle is proposed and located with the cafe furnishings.
d.
Specific limitations. Licensees must comply with the following requirements:
1.
No encroachment at any time into the access zone provided for pedestrian travel and areas reserved for ingress, egress, and circulation.
2.
No relocation, alteration, or modification to the right-of-way, sidewalk surface, or streetscape elements.
3.
No furnishings used in connection with a sidewalk cafe shall be attached, chained or in manner affixed to any tree, post, sign or other fixture or surface.
4.
No overnight storage, parking, or leaving any items on the sidewalk except for approved furnishings, which may be kept in the sidewalk cafe area at the licensee's risk. No use of the sidewalk cafe areas to stock or store tables, chairs, or other items not in use.
5.
No exposed flames or use of oil or torches. Small votives, where the flame is fully surrounded by a non-flammable container, are permitted.
6.
No use of streetscape elements such as public trash cans by licensee for either the sidewalk cafe or the restaurant.
7.
No use of the sidewalk cafe area to place, display, or sell items other than food or beverages.
8.
No signage in the sidewalk cafe area, upon furnishings, or on serving vessels.
9.
No extension of electrical cords to provide music, lighting, etc. to the sidewalk cafe areas. Nor shall a licensee shall allow sound or a device which produces a loud noise or use or operate any loudspeaker, public address system, radio, sound amplifier, or similar device in violation of the city noise regulations. The city reserves the right to decrease noise levels of businesses if affecting public activities in the area.
10.
No food preparation shall be allowed on the sidewalk. There shall be no cooking, storage, cooling or refrigeration or other equipment in the sidewalk cafe area.
11.
No sublicense of the sidewalk cafe area. No licensee shall, nor shall a licensee allow others, to use the sidewalk cafe area to place displays, vend, distribute information or display goods, or other activities other than those specifically permitted in this article.
e.
Violations. Any violation of the above requirements, or state or local laws, can result in suspension or revocation of the license. Repeated offenses may result in denial of subsequent sidewalk cafe license applications.
(6)
Removal of furnishings. The manager at his reasonable discretion may require temporary removal for cleaning or special circumstances. If such temporary removal(s) exceeds 30 days during a calendar year, the city shall apply a prorated credit to the licensee's account for each additional day the sidewalk cafe is subject to removal. The licensee shall not be entitled to any refund for the removal period nor shall the city be responsible for any costs associated with the removal or return of sidewalk cafe furnishings.
a.
Temporary removal for cleaning. All tables and chairs of a sidewalk cafe shall be removed from the public sidewalk as notified by the city for right-of-way cleaning. The licensee shall be responsible for removing all sidewalk cafe furnishings at least one day prior to the dated identified by the manager.
b.
Suspension or modification of operation. The manager shall have the authority to require any sidewalk cafe operating in an area created by this section to suspend operation and/or clear such area, or to move or modify the location or operation of the sidewalk cafe without prior notice or prior hearing when in the reasonable discretion of the manager any such use, occupation, or obstruction may interfere with special circumstances such as:
1.
Any permitted special event;
2.
Any street, sidewalk, or utility construction;
3.
Any emergency situation; or
4.
The protection of the health, safety, and welfare of the public.
c.
Failure to remove furnishings. Under [sub]section 22-389(6)a. and b. and section 22-391, failure to remove all furnishings and other items associated with the operation of the sidewalk cafe shall result in a removal of such furnishings and other items by the manager. The licensee shall be responsible for all expenses incurred by the city for the removal and storage of such furnishings and other items.
(7)
Additional conditions. The manager may, at his reasonable discretion under this article, place additional reasonable conditions upon the issuance of approval in order to insure the protection of the public rights-of-way and the rights of all adjoining property owners and the health, safety, and general welfare of the public.
(Amd. of 11-12-07(1), § 1(Exh. A); Amd. of 5-12-2014(2), § 1)
Sec. 22-390. - Liability and insurance.
(a)
Except for actions arising out of the City of Madison's sole negligence, the license agrees to indemnify, defend, save, and hold harmless the city and its officers, officials, representatives, agents, and employees, from any and all claims, liability, damages, or causes of action which may arise out of the license or licensee's service or activity regarding the sidewalk cafe area.
(b)
The licensee agrees to maintain during the entire license period, at its own expense, the following:
(1)
Commercial general liability in the amount of $1,000,000.00 per occurrence for bodily injury and property damage. The city must be named as an additional insured on this policy, and an endorsement must be issued as part of the policy reflecting compliance with this requirement.
(2)
Workers' compensation and employer's liability as required by the State of Georgia.
(3)
All policies must be issued by companies authorized to do business in the State of Georgia and listed on the U.S. Treasury Circular 570.
(4)
The city must receive 30 days written notice prior to any cancellation, non-renewal, or material change in the coverage provided.
(5)
The license must provide an original certificate of insurance as evidence that the above requirements have been met prior to the issuance of the license.
(6)
All proper permits required by the Morgan County Health Department.
(c)
The licensee is responsible for immediate and proper workmanlike repair of any damage to public property caused by the sidewalk cafe.
(Amd. of 11-12-07(1), § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 22-391. - Revocation or suspension of license.
The approval of a sidewalk cafe license is conditional at all times. Said license may be revoked or suspended up to 12 months by the manager if it is found that:
(1)
Any necessary business or health permit/license/certificate has been suspended, revoked, or cancelled.
(2)
The licensee does not have insurance as described in section 22-390.
(3)
The licensee has violated this article or any other city or state regulation.
(4)
The licensee has a history of violations of this article of two or more within a two-year period.
(Amd. of 11-12-07(1), § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 22-392. - Fines for violators.
The following minimum fines shall be imposed for violations of this article:
(1)
First citation: $100.00 fine.
(2)
Second citation (within one-year period): $250.00 fine and a seven-day suspension of sidewalk cafe operation.
(3)
Third citation (within one-year period): $500.00 fine and a thirty-day suspension of sidewalk cafe operation.
(Amd. of 11-12-07(1), § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 22-393. - Americans with Disabilities Act.
Any person or entity receiving a license hereunder agrees to comply with all the requirements of the Americans with Disabilities Act and all other applicable regulations as currently existing or as may be hereafter amended.
(Amd. of 11-12-07(1), § 1(Exh. A))
