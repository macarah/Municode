ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 22-1. - Fire limits.
The fire limits of the city shall be all territory within the city limits.
(Code 1974, § 5-3; Code 1995, § 22-1)
Sec. 22-2. - Moving structures; maximum height and width.
The moving of any structure into or out of or within the city which exceeds 18 feet in height or 30 feet in width is prohibited except on streets where overhead utilities would not be affected by such moving.
(Code 1974, § 5-4; Code 1995, § 22-2)

ARTICLE II. - BUILDING CODES

Sec. 22-23. - Adoption of codes.
(a)
The state minimum standard codes enumerated in or promulgated pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 8-2-20 (9)(B)(i)(I) through O.C.G.A. § 8-2-20 (9)(B)(i)(VIII) including applicable appendices, as now or hereafter modified or altered pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 8-2-20 (9)(B)(ii), are adopted.
(b)
In addition, the following codes (with Georgia State Amendments) are adopted:
(1)
International Existing Building Code (ICC), 2012 edition;
(2)
International Property Maintenance Code (ICC), 2012 edition.
(3)
International Swimming Pool and Spa Code (ICC), 2018 edition.
(c)
Within such codes when reference is made to the duties of certain officials named therein, designated officials of the city who have duties corresponding to those of the named officials in such codes shall be deemed to be the responsible officials insofar as enforcing the provisions of the codes are concerned.
(d)
Penalties for violation of the codes referenced within this section 22-23 are punishable as provided in chapter 1, section 1-8.
(Code 1995, § 22-26; Ord. No. 92-3, § 1, 4-20-1992; Ord. No. 98-4-1, 5-18-1998; Undated ord., § 22-26; Ord. No. 2016-02-04, § 2, 3-21-2016; Ord. No. 2020-06-01, § 2, 6-15-2020)
Sec. 22-24. - Administration of codes; enforcement official.
The administration of the codes adopted in section 22-23 will be accomplished in accordance with the administration chapter of each code. When reference is made to the duties of a certain official named therein, that designated official of the city who has duties corresponding to those of the named official in such code shall be deemed to be the responsible official insofar as enforcing the provisions of such code are concerned.
(Code 1995, § 22-27; Ord. No. 92-3, § 2, 4-20-1992; Undated ord., § 22-27)
Sec. 22-25. - Penalty for violation of codes.
Any person violating any of the provisions of the codes adopted in this article shall be punished as prescribed in section 1-8.
(Code 1974, § 5-2; Code 1995, § 22-26; Undated ord., § 22-28)
Sec. 22-26. - Conflicting provisions.
Any matters in the codes adopted in this article which are contrary to existing ordinances of the city shall prevail over such ordinances to the extent of such conflict.
(Code 1995, § 22-29; Ord. No. 92-3, § 3, 4-20-1992; Undated ord., § 22-29)

Sec. 22-57. - Applicability to homeowner.
Nothing contained in this article shall prevent or prohibit an owner from doing electrical work in a dwelling in which he resides; provided, however, such owner shall pay required permit fees and shall make such installations as required by this article and the electrical code for safety purposes; provided, however, such person does not employ a licensed electrician to assist in the work.

DIVISION 2. - ADMINISTRATION AND ENFORCEMENT

Sec. 22-88. - Building inspector; powers and duties; appeal.
(a)
The powers and duties of the building inspector shall be as follows:
(1)
To see that all provisions of this article and the electrical code, and amendments thereto, and ordinances of the city council in reference to regulation and placing of electric wires or other appliances for electric lighting, power, heat, or signaling systems in the city are properly carried out.
(2)
The inspector is authorized, empowered and directed to regulate and determine the placing of electrical wires or other appliances for electric lighting, heat, power, or signaling systems in the city, and to cause all such wiring or appliances to be placed and constructed so as not to cause fires or accidents or the endangering of life or property.
(3)
The inspector shall see that all of the provisions, rules and regulations now of force or which may be adopted by the city council for installing of electric appliances or appurtenances are enforced.
(4)
The inspector is authorized to enter upon the premises where there is any building, structure or manhole upon which, or in which, electric wiring for any purpose may be installed; and to climb any pole or to enter any place for the purpose of examining electric appliances therein contained.
(5)
Whenever, in the opinion of the inspector, any electric wires or electric wiring or electric appliances of any sort are not sufficiently insulated, or for other cause he shall deem to be unsafe, or a menace to life or property, he shall be authorized, and is hereby clothed with authority, to enter upon such premises, manholes, poles or other such places where such conditions are found to exist, and disconnect such wires, or disconnect such appliances.
(6)
In case of failure to comply with the provisions of this article and the electrical code respecting the rules, regulations and conditions for the doing of electrical work in the city, the electrical inspector shall have the authority, after due notice, to cut off lights or current in any building or structure concerned, and to force discontinuance of such current until such requirements are complied with.
(7)
It shall be the duty of the inspector, at suitable intervals, to make inspection of old work in the city; and when defects are discovered in such work, to require the removal of such defects and the installation to be brought up to condition of safety within a reasonable time, the extreme limit not to exceed 20 days.
(8)
The inspector shall perform such other duties as to electric wiring or other electrical appliances or appurtenances for electric lighting, heat, power, or signaling systems in the city as may from time to time be assigned to him.
(b)
Any person aggrieved by the decision of the inspector or any requirements contained in this article may appeal therefrom as in the case of other appeals under this Code.
(Code 1974, § 5-22; Code 1995, § 22-53)
Sec. 22-89. - Cutting off current by fire chief.
In case of fire, the chief of the fire department or, in his absence, the officer of the fire department next in charge shall have full authority to order the central station of any electric light or power company to shut off the current from any wires whose position endangers the lives of the firefighters.
(Code 1974, § 5-23; Code 1995, § 22-54)
Sec. 22-90. - Electrical permits and inspections.
(a)
Work without permit a violation. If any person commences any work on an electrical installation before obtaining the necessary permit from the city, he shall be subject to the penalty prescribed in section 1-8.
(b)
Issuance of permit and certificate; fees. On application and after payment of the required fee, the building inspector shall issue a permit to perform electrical work. After approving work completed in the city, a certificate shall be issued to the owner of the premises, which certificate shall be presented to the utility company to supply power, and to ensure that the work has been approved by the city electrical inspector. The fee for each permit issued shall be as established by the city.
(c)
Permit to alter existing work. No repairs, changes or additions shall be made in or to wiring already installed in buildings without a permit from the building inspector, nor shall any change be made in any isolated or private plant without obtaining a permit from the electrical inspector. However, this subsection shall not require a permit for, nor prohibit a utility company from, consolidating metering provisions in or on dwellings, as follows: The metering provisions may be changed by the utility company supplying electrical service in order to allow the consolidation of existing water heater services previously installed. Such consolidation shall be made through one meter within the enclosure only. Under these conditions, two main disconnect switches per meter loop are acceptable.
(d)
Correction of defects. Any individual, firm, corporation, agent or employee thereof who shall fail to correct any defects in his work within five days, after having been duly notified of such defects by the building inspector, shall not receive any further permits until such defects have been corrected. Immediately after the correction of such defects, the building inspector shall be notified of such correction. No certificate shall be issued to the owner of the premises until such time as all defects have been corrected.
(e)
Time limit for starting work after securing permit; granting of. Any permit issued shall become invalid unless the work authorized by it shall have been commenced within six months after its issuance, or if the work authorized by such permit is suspended or abandoned for a period of one year after the time the work is commenced; provided, for cause, one or more extensions of time for periods not exceeding 90 days each may be allowed in writing by the building inspector.
(f)
Removal of wires for other work. Whenever contractors, builders or persons engaged in repairing, painting, putting up signs, repairing or altering plumbing or wires on or in buildings and the activity requires the removal of electrical wires of another, such contractor, builder or person shall serve the building inspector with written notice at least 24 hours before such contemplated work is begun. It shall be the duty of the building inspector to direct the owners of such wires to remove such wires, and upon their failure to do so within the time given or agreed upon, after such instructions, the obstructing or interfering wires may be removed by the contractor under the supervision of the building inspector. Whenever such pipes or wires are to be removed under authority of this subsection, such work shall be done under the supervision of the building inspector and by a competent electrician at the expense of the person securing the change.
(Code 1974, § 5-29; Code 1995, § 22-59)
Sec. 22-91. - Approval of wiring before concealment.
No builder, contractor, electrician or his agents or employees shall cover or conceal any electric wiring without such work first being approved by the building inspector.
(Code 1974, § 5-30; Code 1995, § 22-60(1); Ord. No. 1057, 4-4-1983)
Sec. 22-92. - Illegal use of electrician's name to obtain permit; subpoenas.
No electrician shall allow his name to be used by any other person, directly or indirectly, for the purpose of obtaining a permit if that electrician will not be performing the electrical work or doing work under his license or certificate, nor shall he make any misrepresentations or omissions in his documents submitted in connection with a permit application. When any person is found guilty of permitting the use of his name in this manner or of a violation of any of the provisions, sections, or subsections in or described in this article, the building inspector shall have the authority to issue or cause to be issued a subpoena to the person violating same. Any person found guilty of using another person's electrical license or certificate for the purpose of obtaining a permit or doing electrical work under a license other than his own shall be subject to a penalty as provided in section 1-8. No person engaged in the business of plumbing in the city shall allow his name to be used by any other person, directly or indirectly, to obtain a permit, or for the construction of any work under his name or license; nor shall he make any misrepresentations or omissions in a permit application.
(Code 1974, § 5-24; Code 1995, § 22-55(d))

Sec. 22-114. - City to turn on valves and make meter connections.
No person, contractor or builder, except city personnel, shall at any time turn on any valve or cock or in any way tamper or make any connection with any gas main, meter, meter connection or gas service pipe between the main and the meter. DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 22-144. - Building inspector; office created; powers and duties.
The building inspector shall enforce the provisions of this article and the plumbing code, and he may enter any building, structure, or premises to perform any duty imposed upon him under this article.
(Code 1974, § 5-60; Code 1995, § 22-106)
Sec. 22-145. - Applicability to homeowner.
Nothing in this article or the plumbing code shall prevent any homeowner from installing or maintaining plumbing within his own property boundaries, provided such plumbing work is done by himself and is used exclusively by him or his family. Such privilege does not convey the right to violate any of the provisions of this article or the plumbing code, nor is to be construed as exempting any such property owner from obtaining a permit and paying the required fees therefor.
(Code 1974, § 5-64; Code 1995, § 22-108)
Sec. 22-146. - Self-inspection of water and sewer projects.
Pursuant to the authority contained in 1996 Georgia Laws (Act No. 1046), page 1631, § 3, the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 8-2-26(d), relating to self-inspection of water and sewer projects by master plumbers or utility contractors, shall not be applicable within the city.
(Ord. No. 96-07-1, § 1, 8-19-1996)
Secs. 22-147—22-175. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PERMIT

Sec. 22-176. - Required; application; drawings and specifications.
(a)
Any duly licensed plumber who desires to connect any plumbing work with any sewers, sanitary or storm, septic tanks or sewage disposal system of any kind, or private connection, or install fixtures or appliances in new or existing systems, structures or premises, or repair or add to any existing plumbing, shall first make application to the city and obtain the required permit therefor. Ordinary minor repairs may be made with the approval of the building inspector without a permit, provided such repairs shall not violate any of the provisions of the plumbing code.
(b)
Whenever, in the opinion of the building inspector, drawings and specifications are needed to show the nature and character of the work for which the application is made, the applicant shall furnish such drawings and specifications. These drawings and specifications shall be drawn to scale and submitted in duplicate, and, if approved, one set shall be returned to the applicant, marked approved, and one set shall be retained and filed as a permanent record in the city manager/clerk's office. The applicant's approved set shall remain at all times on the job. Such information or drawings and specifications shall be specific.
(Code 1974, § 5-66; Code 1995, § 22-126)
Sec. 22-177. - Term.
Any permit issued shall become invalid unless the work authorized by it shall have been commenced within six months after its issuance, or if the work authorized by such permit is suspended or abandoned for a period of one year after the time the work is commenced; provided, for cause, one or more extensions of time for periods not exceeding 90 days each may be allowed in writing by the building inspector.
(Code 1974, § 5-68; Code 1995, § 22-128)
Sec. 22-178. - Commencing work without a permit.
If any person commences any work on a plumbing installation before obtaining the necessary permit from the city, he shall be subject to the penalty prescribed in section 1-8.
Sec. 22-179. - Fees.
Permit fees shall be as established by the city.
(Code 1974, § 5-70; Code 1995, § 22-130)
Sec. 22-180. - Allowing one's name or license to be used to obtain a permit.
No person engaged in the business of plumbing in the city shall allow his name to be used by any other person, directly or indirectly, to obtain a permit, or for the construction of any work under his name or license; nor shall he make any misrepresentations or omissions in a permit application.
(Code 1974, § 5-63; Code 1995, § 22-131)
