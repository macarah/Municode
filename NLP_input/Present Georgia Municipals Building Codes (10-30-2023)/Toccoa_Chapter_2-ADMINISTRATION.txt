Sec. 2-26. - Mayor; duties.
The city commission shall select one of its members to serve as mayor. The mayor shall serve as the presiding officer and shall perform such duties and responsibilities as authorized by the Charter of the city.

The city commission shall select one of its members as vice-mayor to act in the place of the mayor in the performance of official duties in the event the mayor is unable or unavailable to perform those official duties.
(Code 1985, § 2-1-2)

The city commissioners shall perform such duties as authorized by the Charter of the city.
(Code 1985, § 2-1-3)
Charter reference— Powers and duties of commissioners, § 6.
Sec. 2-29. - Compensation; reimbursement for travel expenses; etc.
(a)
Compensation to the mayor and city commissioners shall be the following:
(1)
The mayor shall receive compensation for services to the city in the amount of $1,000.00 per month.
(2)
The vice-mayor shall receive compensation for services to the city in the amount of $958.33 per month.
(3)
Each commission member shall receive compensation for services to the city in the amount of $916.67 per month.

In the event any one or more of the city commissioners, the vice-mayor or the mayor travels beyond the limits of the county in connection with the performance of his duty as commissioner, or vice-mayor or mayor he shall be reimbursed for all necessary expenses incurred for travel, lodging and meals.

The contract between the City of Toccoa and GMEBS providing that the city pay premiums for health insurance for the city commissioners (including the mayor) as elected officials is hereby amended to delete said contract provision; provided nothing contained herein would prevent a city commissioner from otherwise personally paying the city the applicable premium to maintain coverage under the GMEBS group health plan, subject to any requirements or limitations established now or in the future by the city or GMEBS. This provision shall not be construed to establish a right to individual (i.e., non-group) health insurance coverage through GMEBS.

Sec. 2-41. - Dates, time.
The city commission will meet on the second and fourth Mondays of each month at the city hall at 5:00 p.m.
(Code 1985, § 2-1-11)
Sec. 2-42. - Call of special meetings.
Special meetings of the city commission may be called by the mayor or any three members of the commission.
(Code 1985, § 2-1-11)
Sec. 2-43. - Mayor to preside; power to vote.
(a)
The mayor shall be the presiding officer of the city commission.
(b)
The mayor, as a member of the city commission, shall have the right to vote on each question coming before the commission and shall have the right to second all motions.
(Code 1985, § 2-1-12)
Sec. 2-44. - Quorum.
Three city commissioners shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business.
(Code 1985, § 2-1-13)
Sec. 2-45. - Rules of order.
Except as otherwise provided by ordinance, Robert's Revised Rules of Order shall govern the procedures and meetings of the commission as far as they are applicable.
(Code 1985, § 2-1-14)
Sec. 2-46. - Adoption of ordinances and resolutions; required readings; waiver; form and attestation.
(a)
All proposed ordinances, before they are voted upon by the city commission, shall be read twice at separate meetings; however, the second reading may be waived and the ordinance passed at the first meeting, upon unanimous consent of the members of the city commission.
(b)
All proposed resolutions may be passed with one reading at the first meeting.
(c)
All adopted ordinances and resolutions shall be in writing, and shall be signed by the mayor or the officer presiding over the city commission at the time of their passage, and attested to by the clerk of the commission.
(Code 1985, § 2-1-15)
Sec. 2-47. - Recording of vote.
Upon the call of any member of the city commission, the yeas and nays on any question shall be taken and recorded in the journal by the clerk of the commission.
(Code 1985, § 2-1-16)

Sec. 2-81. - Duties generally.
The city manager shall be the chief executive officer of the city government and as such shall be responsible for the day to day operation of the city. His responsibilities shall include:
(1)
Direct control of the activities of all department heads in conformance with the city Charter and this Code.
(2)
Preparation and maintenance of an annual budget and capital improvements plan for all city functions and operations.
(3)
Public relations and disbursement of public information.
(4)
Development and maintenance of a personnel plan.
(5)
Maintenance and enforcement of administrative procedures.
(6)
Advise the city commission on a continuous basis as to the operation of the city government.
(7)
Preparation of agenda for all meetings of the city commission.
(8)
Execution of official contracts or agreements as approved by the commission.
(9)
Perform all other duties in keeping with his position as city manager as authorized or instructed by the city commission.
(Code 1985, § 2-3-3)

ARTICLE V. - PURCHASING

Sec. 2-146. - Purchasing criteria; authorization.
All purchases by the city, regardless of price, shall be made on a competitive basis. The city manager shall be responsible for preparing and maintaining purchasing procedures and policies which shall incorporate, at a minimum, the following criteria:
(1)
All purchases over $5,000.00, except as provided for herein, must be approved by the commission and shall require sealed bids to be opened and publicly read aloud at a regular or called commission meeting.
(2)
The city manager shall be authorized to purchase any item or service specifically listed in the current approved budget of the city, provided the following conditions are met:
a.
At least three competitive bids/proposals must be received, if possible.
b.
Prior to such purchases, the city commission must receive copies of the request for bids, a list of all bidders and their bid amounts, and the intended vendor and amount of purchase.
c.
The actual purchase must not be made prior to seven days after the commission receives the information referred to in section 2-146(2)(b).
(3)
The city manager shall be authorized to secure term bids for materials or products which are used on a repetitive basis, and to purchase such materials, provided that individual units of such purchases do not exceed $5,000.00.
(4)
The city manager shall be authorized to make payments, regardless of amount, pursuant to a legally authorized contract, provided such contract has been publicly authorized by the city commission.
(5)
The city manager shall be authorized to make all tax, FICA, insurance and utility payments, regardless of amount.
(6)
The city manager shall be authorized to purchase budgeted items that are included in state government contracts awarded by the state department of administrative services through a competitive bidding process.
(7)
In situations of emergency, which are defined as anything that would interrupt the normal operations of the city, the city manager is authorized to make necessary purchases, provided such purchases are ratified at the next commission meetings if they are over $5,000.00.
(Code 1985, § 2-3-4; Ord. No. 88-012, 6-20-88; Ord. No. 88-026, § 1, 8-8-88)

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-166. - Personnel plan—Preparation; contents.
A personnel plan shall be prepared and maintained by the city manager and shall include:
(1)
Leaves and holidays to be provided to city employees.
(2)
Objective standards to be used by the city when recruiting, selecting or advancing employees.
(3)
Salary and pay classification schedules.
(4)
Position classifications.
(5)
Grievance and appeal procedures.
(6)
Grounds and procedures for disciplinary action against employees.
(7)
All other information necessary for the proper personnel administration of city government.
(Code 1985, § 2-4-1)
Sec. 2-167. - Same—Coverage; at-will employment.
(a)
All offices and positions of the city shall be and are hereby allocated to the career service. The career service shall be a service to which the personnel plan shall apply and shall include positions in the city now existing or hereafter established, except the following:
(1)
The members of the city commission and other elected positions.
(2)
Members of boards and commissions, judges, the city manager and the city attorney.
(3)
Persons employed to make or conduct a temporary or special inquiry, investigation, or examination on behalf of the city commission, a committee thereof, or the city manager.
(b)
The city is an "at-will employer." All employees may be terminated with or without cause, except that nothing in this section or the personnel policies shall permit the city or any of its officials or officers to violate an employee's statutory or constitutional employment rights in an at-will employment situation.
(Code 1985, § 2-4-2)

ARTICLE VII. - TOCCOA-STEPHENS COUNTY PROBATION DEPARTMENT[9]

Footnotes:
--- (9) ---
Editor's note— A resolution, adopted May 4, 1993 did not specifically amend the Code. Therefore, inclusion of §§ 1—17 as §§ 2-201—2-217 was at the discretion of the editor.
Cross reference— Municipal court, ch 12.

Sec. 2-201. - Creation and establishment.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 42-8-100, the county board of commissioners as the governing authority of the county, does approve the creation and establishment of a county probation system to provide general supervision and collection services for all moneys to be paid by a defendant according to the terms of the sentence imposed on the defendant as well as any monies which by operation of law are to be paid by the defendant in consequence of the conviction, to provide counseling and to provide other probation services for persons convicted of a misdemeanor in the state court of the county and placed on probation in the county.
(Res. of 5-4-93, § 1)
Sec. 2-202. - Services to municipal court.
The probation system established hereby shall likewise provide the same probation services to the municipal court of the city upon approval of this article by the city and upon the concurrence of the judge of the municipal court of the city.
(Res. of 5-4-93, § 2)
Sec. 2-203. - Services to the magistrate's court.
The probation system established hereby shall likewise, with the concurrence of the chief judge of the magistrate's court of the county, provide the same probation services to the county magistrate court.
(Res. of 5-4-93, § 3)
Sec. 2-204. - Approval by county commissioners and city commissioners.
This article shall not become effective and the probation system described herein shall not be created until and unless this article is approved both by the county board of commissioners and the city commissioners of the city.
(Res. of 5-4-93, § 4)
Sec. 2-205. - Period of existence of system.
The probation system established hereby shall be effective July 1, 1993, and shall be initially created for a period of one year. The probation system established hereby shall continue in existence after the initial year on a year-to-year basis and until the occurrence of the earlier of the following events:
(1)
The adoption of a resolution by the board of commissioners of the county terminating the probation system; or
(2)
The adoption of a resolution by the city terminating the city's participation in the probation system created by this article.
(Res. of 5-4-93, § 5)
Sec. 2-206. - Cost of probation system.
The cost of the probation system established hereby shall be paid by the county and the city in accordance with this article.
(Res. of 5-4-93, § 6)
Sec. 2-207. - Definition.
The probation system created hereby shall be known as "The Toccoa-Stephens County Probation Department" (herein referred to as "the department").
(Res. of 5-4-93, § 7)
Sec. 2-208. - Chief probation officer—Employee of county.
(a)
The department shall initially be staffed by one employee who shall be known as the chief probation officer of the department. The chief probation officer shall be an employee of the county, and except as may be inconsistent with this article, shall be subject to the general personnel policies established for county employees by the county board of commissioners and shall be entitled to the same employee benefits, such as health insurance, as made available to the employees of the county.
(b)
The county board of commissioners shall furnish a suitable office and utilities, telephone, desk, typewriter and appropriate chairs for the use of the department which office will be located, if at all possible, in the county courthouse annex.
(Res. of 5-4-93, § 8)
Sec. 2-209. - Same—Filling of position.
The position of chief probation officer shall be filled as follows:
(1)
The chief judges of the affected courts shall establish a procedure for advertisement and solicitation of applications of persons interested in the position of chief probation officer. From the applications submitted, the chief judges of the affected courts shall establish a procedure for selection of that person deemed best qualified.
(2)
The chief judges of the affected courts (or a majority thereof) shall select the person deemed best qualified and shall submit the name of the person recommended for appointment as chief probation officer of the department along with a proposed salary and method of computation of salary for approval to the county board of commissioners. The recommendation shall also propose an effective date of employment.
(3)
Upon approval by the county board of commissioners, the person nominated by the majority of the chief judges of the affected courts, shall be elected to the position of chief probation officer whose term of employment shall initially be for a period of one year or until June 30, 1994, whichever first occurs.
(Res. of 5-4-93, § 9)
Sec. 2-210. - Same—Chief judges as supervisors.
(a)
The chief judges of the affected courts shall coordinate court schedules so that, if possible, the chief probation officer may be in attendance at all sessions of the court from which the department receives probationers. The chief judges shall cooperate in an effort to avoid conflicting court schedules. In the event of a conflict regarding instruction to the chief probation officer regarding trial attendance at conflicting times, the chief judge of the state court of the county shall issue appropriate directions to the chief probation officer to resolve the conflict.
(b)
The chief probation officer shall consider each of the judges of the affected courts to be the chief probation officer's supervisor in regard to cases originating from the court of that particular judge. In the event of conflicting instructions from two or more judges, the chief judge of the state court of the county shall resolve the conflicting instructions and the chief probation officer shall act in accordance with instructions of the chief judge of the state court of the county.
(c)
The chief judges of the affected courts shall develop and approve uniform written forms for use by the chief probation officer as well as guidelines for the handling of probation services to the end that all cases handled by the department shall be uniformly administered and the chief probation officer shall administer all cases, as uniformly as possible, regardless of the court from which the case originated.
(d)
If the chief judges, or a majority thereof, of the affected courts become dissatisfied with the chief probation officer of the department, the chief judges or a majority thereof, may relieve the chief probation officer from his or her duties in such courts, in which event the majority of such judges shall immediately recommend to the county board of commissioners that such chief probation officer be discharged, giving reasons therefor, whereupon the board of commissioners shall immediately discharge the chief probation officer.
(Res. of 5-4-93, § 10)
Sec. 2-211. - Duties.
The chief probation officer shall have such duties, responsibilities and authority as a probation officer of the department of corrections under existing Georgia law, except as may be inconsistent with this article or except as may be specifically modified, from time-to-time, in writing by the majority of the chief judges of the affected courts.
(Res. of 5-4-93, § 11)
Sec. 2-212. - Same—Job description.
The chief judges of the affected courts shall prior to July 1, 1993, establish a job description for the chief probation officer, which job description shall set out in such detail as such judges may deem appropriate, the duties, responsibilities and authority of the chief probation officer.
(Res. of 5-4-93, § 12)
Sec. 2-213. - Probation supervision fee.
The department shall not have jurisdiction to supervise any defendant from the affected courts, unless, as a part of the sentence of probation, the defendant is ordered to pay a probation supervision fee of not less than $15.00 per month and not more than the maximum amount authorized by law, such amount to be established, from time-to-time, by the majority of the chief judges of the affected courts.
(Res. of 5-4-93, § 13)
Sec. 2-214. - Budget.
At such time as requested by the board of commissioners of the county, the majority of the chief judges of the affected courts shall submit a proposed budget for the forthcoming fiscal year, which budget shall be subject to approval by the board of commissioners of the county. In submitting the proposed budget, the judges shall realistically assess the projected collection of probation supervision fees for the forthcoming fiscal year and shall not, except in unusual circumstances, submit a proposed budget wherein the projected anticipated collection of probation fees shall be less than the proposed budget. In the event a budget is submitted greater than the projected probation supervision fees collected, the judges shall specify the reasons for the anticipated shortfall.
(Res. of 5-4-93, § 14)
Sec. 2-215. - Chief probation officer—Bonded.
The chief probation officer shall be bonded in an amount not less than the annual budget of the department. The cost of the surety bond shall be an expense of the department. The bond shall be approved by the chief judge of the state court of the county and thereafter filed with the clerk of the county board of commissioners.
(Res. of 5-4-93, § 15)
Sec. 2-216. - Same—Collection of probation supervision fees.
The chief probation officer shall collect all probation supervision fees assessed defendants given probated sentences and shall deposit such fees in a separate account daily as received. The chief probation officer shall remit to the county board of commissioners at least monthly a check representing all probation supervision fees collected during the second preceding month. For example, on September 1, the chief probation officer shall remit all fees collected during the month of July.
(Res. of 5-4-93, § 16)
Sec. 2-217. - Same—Accounting of probation supervision fees.
(a)
The chief probation officer shall maintain an accurate accounting of all probation supervision fees collected. The accounting shall include the total fees collected from defendants sentenced by the municipal court of the city, defendants sentenced by the magistrate court of the county and defendants sentenced by the state court of the county.
(b)
Within one month following the close of the fiscal year of the county board of commissioners, the chief probation officer shall prepare a summary accounting reflecting thereon the entire cost of the operation of the department, including salary, fringe benefits, office overhead (such as telephone, stationery, long distance telephone calls and support personnel, if any), as well as the employer's share of state and federal tax items (such as social security and unemployment tax) and worker's compensation insurance. The cost of utilities, equipment and supplies provided by the county board of commissioners shall be added as a cost of operation of the department, provided however, that the value of the office space (rental value) shall not be considered as a cost of the department.
(c)
From the total cost of the operation of the department as determined in accordance with subparagraph (b) hereof, the chief probation officer shall allocate the cost between the city and the county according to the percentage of the fees collected from the respective courts of such governmental entities. The cost allocated to the city shall be in direct proportion to the fees collected from the defendants sentenced by the municipal court of the city.
(d)
From the total fees collected during the preceding fiscal year from defendants sentenced by the municipal court of the city, the total cost allocated to the city shall be deducted. If the resulting balance is a positive amount, the county board of commissioners shall remit a check to the city for the positive amount of the balance within 30 days after receipt of the accounting by the chief probation officer. If the resulting balance is negative, the county board of commissioners shall submit a bill to the city upon receipt of the accounting of the chief probation officer. The city shall pay to the county board of commissioners the balance of any such bill within 30 days after the receipt thereof.
(Res. of 5-4-93, § 17)
