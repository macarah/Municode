ARTICLE VII. - HOUSE MOVERS

Sec. 10-207. - House movers.
(a)
License required. It shall be unlawful to engage in the business of house moving, or raising or shoring structures without first having secured a license therefor. Application for such license shall be made in writing to the city clerk.
(b)
Fee. The fees for such licenses shall be $5.00 for a daily license; or in accordance with the existing established license fees and taxes set forth under "Class II Services" by employee, whichever amount is larger.
(c)
Regulations. Persons licensed under this section must comply with all the ordinance provisions relating to the work being done, and must pay the permit fee required for moving buildings, and must keep lights and warnings maintained as is required by ordinance.
(d)
Inspections. It shall be the duty of the chief of police to make or cause to be made such inspections as may be necessary to comply with the provisions of this section.
(e)
Bond. Every person licensed under the provisions of this section shall file with the clerk a bond in the sum of $5,000.00 with sureties to be approved by the city council, conditioned to indemnify the city for any loss, damage or expense occasioned by such person, by any act or failure to act of the licensee, or by any failure of the licensee to comply with the ordinances of the city relating to the business of house moving, or raising or shoring structures.
(f)
Planking street. The pavement of any street, alley or sidewalk on or along which any house is moved shall be protected by two-inch planks along the route; provided, however, that where the structure moved is conveyed on a trailer or truck equipped with pneumatic tires, in compliance with the state traffic law, such planking shall be unnecessary.
(Code 1989, § 12-19; Ord. No. 173, § 15, 1-22-1980; Ord. No. 207, 3-25-1985)
