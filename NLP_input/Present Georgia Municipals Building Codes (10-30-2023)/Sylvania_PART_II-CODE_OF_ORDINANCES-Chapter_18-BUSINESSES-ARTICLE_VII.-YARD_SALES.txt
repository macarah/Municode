ARTICLE VII. - YARD SALES

Sec. 18-156. - Definition.
A "yard sale" is defined as the sale or trading of used clothing, furniture, household items, food, dishes, antiques or similar goods or merchandise on a lot utilized primarily for residential purposes. The term "yard sale" shall include garage sales, carport sales and similar types of sales or events.
(Ord. No. 308, § 1, 2-5-91)
Sec. 18-157. - Location.
Yard sales may be held on a lot or parcel of land upon which there is situated a house, apartment or other structure which is utilized primarily for residential purposes. Yard sales are prohibited upon vacant lots and upon lots upon which there is situated a structure that is utilized primarily for any use other than residential purposes.
(Ord. No. 308, § 2, 2-5-91)
Sec. 18-158. - Permit.
It shall be unlawful for any person to conduct a yard sale in the city without a permit. Any person who occupies a house, apartment or other structure in the city as his primary residence shall, upon request, be issued a permit by the city clerk or his designated representative to conduct a yard sale at the location of such house, apartment or residential structure. There shall be no fee charged for such permit. The permit shall contain the date of the yard sale, the duration of the permit, the address of the yard sale and the name or names of the permittees.
(Ord. No. 308, § 3, 2-5-91)
Sec. 18-159. - Additional permitted persons.
The person or persons issued a permit under section 18-158 may designate additional permittees who are not occupants of the structure on the permitted premises as their primary residence. Such persons so designated shall be added as additional permittees on the permit.
(Ord. No. 308, § 4, 2-5-91)
Sec. 18-160. - Posting of permit.
The holder or holders of a yard sale permit shall cause the permit to be conspicuously posted on the permitted premises at all times during which the yard sale is being conducted.
(Ord. No. 308, § 5, 2-5-91)
Sec. 18-161. - Restriction on goods sold.
Only the sale or trading of used clothing, furniture, household items, food, dishes, antiques or similar goods or merchandise that are actually owned by the permittee or permittees shall be allowed at the permitted yard sale location.
(Ord. No. 308, § 6, 2-5-91)
Sec. 18-162. - Number permitted.
(a)
Per year. No person shall be permitted to conduct or participate in more than four yard sales per year.
(b)
Per location. No particular location shall be permitted for more than four yard sales per year.
(c)
Number of days. Any given yard sale shall not continue for a period exceeding two consecutive days.
(Ord. No. 308, § 7, 2-5-91)
Sec. 18-163. - Time, days of sale.
No yard sale shall be conducted on Sunday, and shall be allowed only between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m.
(Ord. No. 308, § 8, 2-5-91)
Sec. 18-164. - Charitable and religious organizations.
No charitable or religious organization shall conduct a yard sale without a permit. Organizations existing for charitable or religious purposes, and only those which have a regularly functioning organizational structure and regularly functioning board of directors, shall be issued a permit to conduct a yard sale only upon private property, and not upon the city streets or sidewalks. Such organizations shall not be restricted to residential property, but shall conduct such yard sale upon its own property, or upon private property with the written permission of the owner or lessee of such private property. Yard sales by charitable or religious organizations shall otherwise be subject to the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 308, § 9, 2-5-91)
Sec. 18-165. - Penalty.
Any person violating this article shall be subject to punishment as provided in section 1-12 upon conviction.
(Ord. No. 308, § 10, 2-5-91)
Secs. 18-166—18-185. - Reserved.
