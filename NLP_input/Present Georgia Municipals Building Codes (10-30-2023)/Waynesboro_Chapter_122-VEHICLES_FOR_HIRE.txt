ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 122-1. - Compliance required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in the business of operating any vehicle for hire on the streets of the city until he has complied with the provisions of this chapter and state law.
(Code 1974, § 23-1; Code 1995, § 114-1)
Sec. 122-2. - Vehicle condition.
Every vehicle for hire operated in the city will be maintained at all times in such mechanical condition as to be safely operated on the streets of the city.
(Code 1974, § 23-5; Code 1995, § 114-2)
State Law reference— Motor vehicle emission inspection and maintenance, O.C.G.A. § 12-9-40 et seq.
Sec. 122-3. - Vehicle markings.
Every vehicle operated for hire on the streets of the city shall have a sign plainly painted on each side and the rear with letters not less than four inches high, identifying the name of the company and/or operator operating such vehicle, and also the word "taxi," or a similar word used to denote that such vehicle is for hire. In lieu of the marking on the rear of the vehicle, a lighted sign on top may be used.
(Code 1974, § 23-6; Code 1995, § 114-3)
Sec. 122-4. - Passenger consent to additional passengers.
No operator of a vehicle for hire which normally carries less than seven passengers shall carry any other passenger than the passenger first employing the vehicle for hire without the consent of the first passenger.
(Code 1974, § 23-7; Code 1995, § 114-4)
Sec. 122-5. - Service rates, posting.
The schedule of rates shall be conspicuously posted where the passengers can see it in each vehicle operated for hire on the streets of the city.
(Code 1974, § 23-8; Code 1995, § 114-5)
Secs. 122-6—122-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - LICENSES

Sec. 122-31. - Liability insurance.
No person shall be issued a license to operate a vehicle for hire in the city unless and until he has acquired a liability insurance policy in the minimum amount required by the laws of the state. Prior to a license being issued, the applicant shall furnish the city administrator/clerk a copy of such policy which has been issued by a company licensed to do business in the state and annotated with words to the effect that such policy will not be cancelled nor amended unless and until the insurance company furnishes, in writing, ten days in advance thereof to the city administrator/clerk, notice that such policy will be cancelled or amended.
(Code 1974, § 23-4; Code 1995, § 114-31)
State Law reference— "Proof of financial responsibility" defined, O.C.G.A. § 40-9-2(5).
Sec. 122-32. - Revocation.
The city council may at any time after notice and opportunity to be heard, revoke any license issued under the provisions of this article.
(Code 1974, § 23-9; Code 1995, § 114-32)
