ARTICLE II. - OFFENSES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-29. - Open container restrictions.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to drink or have in his possession an open container of any alcoholic beverage while:
(1)
On any public street, sidewalk, park, or other public place within the city, or upon or within any motor vehicle while on the streets, sidewalks, parks, and public places of the city; or
(2)
On private property, open to public view, without the express permission of the owner, agent, or person in lawful possession thereof.
(b)
The provisions of this section shall not be deemed to prohibit the drinking of alcoholic beverages by passengers in passenger vehicles.
(Code 2011, § 4-29; Ord. No. 89-10, 8-1-1989)
Sec. 4-30. - Brown-bagging prohibited.
Brown-bagging is prohibited in the city. Brown-bagging includes the following prohibited acts:
(1)
Any person who brown-bags;
(2)
Any person participating in consumption of any alcoholic beverage being brown-bagged;
(3)
Any person who consumes an alcoholic beverage on any premises holding a business license, except for those premises exempted from the definition of brown-bagging by virtue of the private residential character of the occupancy; and
(4)
Any employee of the business establishment in whose presence brown-bagging knowingly or with reckless indifference occurs.
(Code 2011, § 4-30; Ord. No. 96-13, 1996)

Sec. 4-49. - Prohibited acts.
(a)
Except as otherwise authorized by state law or the exceptions in this division:
(1)
No person knowingly, directly or through another person, shall furnish, cause to be furnished or permit any person in such person's employ to furnish any alcoholic beverages to any person under 21 years of age;
(2)
No person under 21 years of age shall purchase or knowingly possess any alcoholic beverages;
(3)
No person under 21 years of age shall misrepresent such person's age in any manner whatever for the purpose of obtaining illegally any alcoholic beverages;
(4)
No person knowingly or intentionally shall act as an agent to purchase or acquire any alcoholic beverages for or on behalf of a person under 21 years of age; or
(5)
No person under 21 years of age shall misrepresent his identity or use any false identification for the purpose of purchasing or obtaining any alcoholic beverage.
(b)
The prohibitions contained in subsections (a)(1), (2) and (4) of this section shall not apply with respect to the:
(1)
Sale, purchase, or possession of alcoholic beverages for consumption for medical purposes pursuant to a prescription of a physician duly authorized to practice medicine in the state or at a religious ceremony.
(2)
Possession of alcoholic beverages for consumption by a person under 21 years of age when the parent or guardian of the person under 21 years of age gives the alcoholic beverage to the person and when the possession is in the home of the parent or guardian and such parent or guardian is present.
(3)
Sale of alcoholic beverages by a person when such person has been furnished with proper identification showing that the person to whom the alcoholic beverage is sold is 21 years of age or older. For purposes of this subsection, the term "proper identification" means any document issued by a governmental agency containing a description of the person, such person's photograph, or both, and giving such person's date of birth, and includes, without being limited to, a passport, military identification card, driver's license, or an identification card authorized under O.C.G.A. §§ 40-5-100 through 40-5-104. Proper identification shall not include a birth certificate and shall not include any traffic citation and complaint form.
(Code 2011, § 4-49)
Sec. 4-50. - Conduct not prohibited by this division.
If such conduct is not otherwise prohibited in this Code or by state law, nothing contained in this division shall be construed to prohibit any person under 21 years of age from:
(1)
Dispensing, serving, selling, or handling alcoholic beverages as a part of employment in any licensed establishment;
(2)
Being employed in any establishment in which alcoholic beverages are distilled or manufactured; or
(3)
Taking orders for and having possession of alcoholic beverages as part of employment in a licensed establishment.
(Code 2011, § 4-50)
Sec. 4-51. - Violations; penalties.
(a)
Any person violating any provision of this article shall be subject to penalties as set forth in section 1-15, except as otherwise provided in this section.
(b)
Whenever any person who has not been previously convicted of any offense under this article or under any other federal or state law relating to alcoholic beverages pleads guilty to or is found guilty of a violation of section 4-49(a) or (b), the municipal court, without entering a judgment of guilt and with consent of such person may defer further proceedings and place him on probation upon such reasonable terms and conditions as the court may require.
(c)
The terms of probation shall preferably be such as to require the person to undergo a comprehensive rehabilitation program (including, if necessary, medical treatment), not to exceed three years, designed to acquaint him with the ill effects of alcohol abuse and to provide him with knowledge of the gains and benefits which can be achieved by being a good member of society.
(d)
Upon the violation of a term or condition of probation, the court may enter an adjudication of guilt and proceed accordingly.
(e)
Upon fulfillment of the terms and conditions of probation, the court shall discharge such person and dismiss the proceedings against him. Discharge and dismissal under this subsection shall be without court adjudication of guilt and shall not be deemed a conviction for purposes of this section or for purposes of disqualifications or disabilities imposed by law upon conviction of a crime. Discharge and dismissal under this subsection may occur only once with respect to any person.
(Code 2011, § 4-51)
Sec. 4-52. - Use of citations authorized for certain violations.
(a)
Unless the officer has reasonable cause to believe that such person is intoxicated, a law enforcement officer may arrest by issuance of a citation a person accused of violating only section 4-49(b).
(b)
The citation shall enumerate the specific charges against the person and either the date upon which the person is to appear and answer the charges or a notation that the person will be later notified of the date upon which the person is to appear and answer the charges.
(c)
Nothing in this section shall be construed to invalidate an otherwise valid arrest by citation of a person who is intoxicated.
(d)
A law enforcement officer arresting a person by the issuance of a citation under this section may require any such person having a driver's license or instruction permit to deposit said license or permit with the arresting officer in order to ensure the appearance of such person to answer the charges against him. The procedures and rules connected with the acceptance of such license or permit and subsequent disposition of the case shall be the same as provided for the acceptance of a driver's license as bail on arrest for traffic offenses pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 17-6-11.
(Code 2011, § 4-52)
Sec. 4-53. - Transfer to county misdemeanor jurisdiction.
Any person charged with a first offense violation of this article shall be entitled, upon request, to have the case against him transferred to the court having general misdemeanor jurisdiction in the county in which the alleged offense occurred.
(Code 2011, § 4-53)
