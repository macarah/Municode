ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 46-1. - Uniform rules of the road adopted.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-372—40-6-376, O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-2—40-6-395, known as the uniform rules of the road, and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 are hereby adopted as the traffic regulations of the county with the same effect as if recited in this section.
(b)
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by state law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this section shall be punished in accordance with section 1-11.
Sec. 46-2 - Off-road vehicles.
(a)
As used in this section, the following definitions shall apply:
Access means to a place where ORVs can be lawfully operated.
Operate means to ride in or on, and be actual control of the operation of an ORV.
ORV or off-road vehicle means a motor driven off-road recreational vehicle capable of cross-country travel with out benefit of a road or trail, on or immediately over land, snow, ice, marsh, swampland, or other natural terrain. It includes, but is not limited to, a multi-track or multi-wheel drive or low pressure tire vehicle, and amphibious machine, a ground effect air cushion vehicle, or other means of transportation driven by power from a source other than muscle or wind. "ORV" or vehicle does not include a snowmobile, a farm vehicle being used for farming, a vehicle used for military, fire, emergency, or law enforcement purposes, a construction or logging vehicle used in performance of its common function. This includes, but is not limited to, ORVs commonly known as three-wheelers and four-wheelers.
Public highway or public roadway means that portion of a county road that is improved, maintained and used by the public for vehicular travel, including any improved shoulders.
(b)
Any person operating an "ORV" vehicle under any of the following conditions shall be deemed to be in violation of this section and shall be punishable as provided by subsection (c);
(1)
Without operative brakes or without mufflers or other silencing equipment.
(2)
On any public property, including, but not limited to, any road or right-of-way under the possession or control of the county, except property designated by the board of commissioners for use by "ORV" vehicles;
(3)
On any private property without the express written permission of the owner of the property or his agent, proof of which shall be in the immediate possession of the operator of a off-road vehicle at all times when operating such vehicle and which shall be displayed upon demand of any law enforcement officer.
(c)
Any person who violates this article is guilty of a misdemeanor, punishable by a fine of no more than $500.00 or imprisonment for 90 days, or both, and full restitution of any damages caused or experienced as a result thereof.
(Ord. of 1-10-2012)

ARTICLE II. - TRUCK ORDINANCE[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
Editor's note— Ord. of 5-14-2019(1), added provisions regulating trucks in residential neighborhoods but did not specify a Code sections. Its inclusion herein was at the discretion of the editor.

Sec. 46-11. - Short title.
This article shall be known and may be cited as the "Truck Ordinance."
(Ord. of 5-14-2019(1), § 1)
Sec. 46-12. - Definitions.
Residential subdivision means an area with streets designed primarily for residential and accessory traffic that are primarily interior subdivision streets and specifically including all residential areas platted and regulated by the Peach County Subdivision Regulations.
Truck means any vehicle with more than six wheels, except vehicles or operators of local origin, recreational vehicles, buses used in transportation of chartered parties and government owned vehicles.
(Ord. of 5-14-2019(1), § 2)
Sec. 46-13. - Parking.
No parking on rights-of-way or roadways in a residential subdivision except in cases of emergency or mechanical breakdown, or temporarily to off- load a delivery.
(Ord. of 5-14-2019(1), § 3)
Sec. 46-14. - Overnight stay.
No truck or tractor-trailer shall park on a street in a residential subdivision or park on roadway and right-of-way in Peach County for the purpose of an overnight stay by the driver or operator.
(Ord. of 5-14-2019(1), § 4)
Sec. 46-15. - Violations.
Any person violating this article shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 and/or up to 12 months in county jail. Each day that the offense continues shall constitute a separate violation.
(Ord. of 5-14-2019(1), § 5)
