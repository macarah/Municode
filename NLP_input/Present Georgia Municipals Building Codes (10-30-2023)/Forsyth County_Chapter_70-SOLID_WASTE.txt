ARTICLE II. - COLLECTION AND DISPOSAL; LITTERING
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 70-31. - Purpose and intent.
The board of commissioners hereby declares the purpose and intent of this article is to establish a comprehensive regulatory approach addressing the matter of solid waste management in the county and to provide uniform and consistent procedures regulating the storage, collection, transportation and disposal of solid waste in order to:
(1)
Protect the public health, safety and welfare by preventing the dissemination of, and providing for the removal of, inorganic refuse materials laden with bacterial elements and contaminants detrimental to the general well-being of the citizens and the environment;
(2)
To provide for the separation of yard trimmings from municipal solid waste as required by state law; and
(3)
To protect and enhance the quality of the water, air and overall environment by guarding against any of the degradations thereto which stem from the collection, transport, burial, deposition, burning, dumping, discarding or disposal of litter and solid waste in the county.
(Ord. No. 84, art. I, pt. 1, 6-28-1999)
Sec. 70-32. - Applicability.
(a)
This article shall apply to all activities relating to solid waste in the county, including storage, collection, transportation and disposal thereof. The provisions contained in this article prescribe rules and regulations for solid waste collection and disposal; provide for the permitting, licensing and regulation of any person involved in solid waste collection, transportation and disposal; and provide for a schedule of fees applicable to the aforementioned activities. This article applies to all waste collectors and disposal facilities located within or doing business within the county, whether publicly or privately owned, and also applies equally to all property owners, residents and businesses in the county.
(b)
Any and all persons falling within the purview of this article must fully comply with all state and federal rules, regulations and standards governing the collection transport, storage and/or disposal of solid waste. Nothing contained in this article shall be construed to relieve any person of their obligations under state or federal law.
(Ord. No. 84, art. I, pt. 3, 6-28-1999)
Sec. 70-33. - Exemptions.
(a)
This article shall not apply to any individual, corporation, partnership or cooperative disposing of livestock feeding facility waste from facilities, provided that such individual, corporation, partnership or cooperative provides an approved waste disposal system which is capable of properly disposing the runoff from a ten-year storm. Nothing in this article shall limit the right of any person to use poultry or other animal manure for fertilizer.
(b)
Provisions of this article relating to the collection and transport of solid waste shall not apply to owners of record and/or residents of all one-family and two-family dwellings, condominiums, or multifamily dwelling units who dispose of solid waste, originating from his own residence only, by self-hauling such waste to an approved solid waste disposal facility; provided, however, that such disposal does not adversely affect the public health or otherwise violate local, state or federal law. Such persons are nonetheless required to separate their yard trimmings from other municipal solid waste and properly dispose of the yard trimmings in accord with the regulations contained in this article.
(c)
Composting is expressly allowed, permitted and encouraged pursuant to this article and shall be governed in accordance with section 391-3-4.16 of the rules of the state department of natural resources, environmental protection division.
(d)
Dead animals shall be disposed of pursuant to state law.
(Ord. No. 84, art. I, pt. 4, 6-28-1999)
Sec. 70-34. - Definitions.
(a)
Definitions used in the Georgia Comprehensive Solid Waste Management Act, as may be amended from time to time (O.C.G.A. § 12-8-20 et seq.), and the Georgia Hazardous Waste Management Act, as may be amended from time to time (O.C.G.A. § 12-8-60 et seq.), are specifically incorporated in this section by reference, to the extent that they are relevant and appropriate to the context in which they may be used in this article.
(b)
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Board means the board of commissioners of the county.
Bulky waste means a type of solid waste that by weight or volume exceeds the designated capacity of solid waste storage or collection containers. The term "bulky waste" shall include, without limitation, appliances, furniture, crates, corrugated cardboard and other similar items. The term "bulky waste" shall not include yard debris, tires or other solid waste requiring special handling under state law.
Commercial solid waste means a type of solid waste generated by stores, offices, restaurants, warehouses and other nonmanufacturing activities. The term "commercial solid waste" includes solid waste generated by premises having three or more residential dwelling units on the premises such as apartment buildings, condominiums and mobile home parks. The term "commercial solid waste" does not include other residential wastes, industrial wastes or construction and demolition waste.
Composting means solid waste handling which consists of the controlled biological decomposition of organic matter into a stable, odor-free humus.
Construction and demolition waste means a type of solid waste that is generally considered to be not water soluble and nonhazardous in nature, including, but not limited to, building materials and rubble resulting from construction, remodeling, repair and demolition operations on pavements, houses, commercial buildings and other structures. Such waste includes but is not limited to steel, glass, brick, concrete, asphalt roofing material, pipe, gypsum wallboard, lumber, inert debris and other nonputrescible waste which has a low potential for ground water contamination. Such waste does not include any municipal or agricultural solid waste. Mixing of construction or demolition waste with other types of solid waste, including material from a construction or demolition site which is not from the actual construction or demolition of a structure, will cause it to be classified as other than construction or demolition waste.
Containerized waste means solid waste (other than that which requires special handling) which is placed in cans, plastic garbage bags or bulk containers or in bundles, as in the case of bundled yard debris.
County solid waste management system (CSWMS) means the aggregate of those solid waste management facilities owned by, operated under contract with, or operated pursuant to licenses and permits issued by the county.
Curbside means the area between the sidewalk and the street edge or, in areas without sidewalks, the area between the edge of the traveled portion of any public or private street and the property line, which area shall not exceed ten feet. However, the term "curbside" shall also denote and include any other permissible areas on a property where solid waste is regularly placed or deposited for collection, such as, for example, by a garage door.
Customer means a person who uses the solid waste or recycling collection services of a permitted solid waste collector or the appropriate governmental agency or who uses solid waste or recycling disposal facilities, whether publicly or privately owned, located in the county.
Department means the county department of sanitation.
Designated recovered/recyclable material collection location means the location or locations where the owner of the recyclable material places such material for the purpose of materials recovery and recycling, including curbside and recovered material storage containers.
Disposal facility means any facility or location where the disposition of solid waste occurs and includes, but is not limited to, landfills, transfer stations and solid waste thermal treatment technology facilities.
Disposal operation means the performance of solid waste disposal and includes the administration, personnel, land, equipment, design and other elements necessary or used in the work of solid waste disposal.
Garbage means a type of solid waste that includes any accumulation of animal, fruit or vegetable matter that attends the preparation, use, cooking and dealing in, or storage of, edibles, and any other matter of any nature whatsoever, which is putrescible, subject to decay, and/or the generation of noxious or offensive gases or odors, which during or after decay, may serve as breeding or feeding material for flies or other germ carrying insects, rodents or other pests.
Hazardous waste means any solid waste which, because of its quantity, concentration or physical, chemical or infectious characteristics, when improperly transported, disposed of, stored, treated or otherwise managed, may cause or significantly contribute to an increase in mortality, or an increase in serious or incapacitating illness, or may pose a substantial present or potential hazard to human health or the environment when improperly transported, disposed of, stored, treated or otherwise managed. Hazardous waste also includes those solid wastes defined as hazardous by any federal law, rule or regulation and/or by the Georgia Hazardous Waste Management Act, O.C.G.A. § 12-8-60 et seq., or in regulations promulgated by the state department of natural resources, chapter 391-3-11, as may be amended from time to time.
Industrial solid waste means solid waste generated by manufacturing or industrial processes that is not a hazardous waste as defined in this section or by the Georgia Hazardous Waste Management Act, O.C.G.A. § 12-8-60 et seq., or in regulations promulgated by the state department of natural resources as may be amended from time to time.
Inert debris means land clearing debris, such as stumps, limbs, leaves and brush, as well as concrete, cured asphalt, brick, rock and earth and earth-like products which are not likely to cause production of leachate of environmental concern.
Inert waste landfill means a disposal facility accepting only inert debris that will not or is not likely to cause production of leachate of environmental concern.
Institutional solid waste means municipal solid waste generated by educational, health care and research facilities, including but not limited to schools, hospitals, nursing homes, laboratories and similar establishments.
Landfill means an area of land on which, or an excavation in which, solid waste is placed for permanent disposal and which is not a land application unit, surface impoundment, injection well or compost pile.
Leachate collection system means a system at a landfill for collection of the leachate which may percolate through the waste and into the soils surrounding the landfills.
Litter means all discarded material of every kind and description which, when thrown or deposited in a manner as prohibited by this article, tends to create an eyesore and a danger to public health, safety and well-being. The term "litter" includes all uncontrolled, uncontainerized solid waste.
Materials recovery facility means a solid waste handling facility that provides for the extraction from solid waste of recoverable/recyclable material, material suitable for use as fuel or soil amendment, or any combination of such material.
Municipal solid waste means any solid waste derived from households, including garbage, trash and sanitary waste in septic tanks, and includes solid waste from single-family and multifamily residences, hotels and motels, bunkhouses, campgrounds, picnic grounds and day use recreation areas. The term "municipal solid waste" includes yard trimmings, commercial solid waste and institutional solid waste, but does not include solid waste from mining, agricultural or silvicultural operations or industrial processes or operations.
Municipal solid waste disposal facility means any facility or location where the deposition of any amount of municipal solid waste occurs, including but not limited to municipal solid waste landfills.
Municipal solid waste landfill means a disposal facility, either privately or publicly owned, where any amount of municipal solid waste, whether or not mixed with or including commercial waste, industrial waste, construction and demolition waste, inert debris, nonhazardous sludge or small quantity generator hazardous waste, is disposed of by means of landfilling or burial and then placing an approved cover thereon.
Nuisance means:
(1)
Anything which satisfies that definition of the term as defined by O.C.G.A. § 41-1-1 as it maybe amended; or
(2)
Anything which occurs during or as a result of the handling or disposal of solid waste to the detriment of the public welfare in that it injures health, offends the senses, obstructs the reasonable and comfortable use of property, or causes inconvenience or discomfort to a community or neighborhood;
(3)
Any of the following conditions:
a.
Toilets, plumbing, sewers, septic tank systems, containers or any other sanitary waste disposal facility in active failure or visibly in need of repair.
b.
Conditions causing the breeding and attraction of rodents, flies, ticks, mosquitoes or any other disease carrying insects, including discarded accumulations of trash, garbage and/or litter.
c.
Sewage, kitchen waste discharge, laundry waste discharge, or any other wastes deposited upon or allowed to remain upon the ground's surface or any other place so as to be or to become detrimental to human health or well-being.
d.
The keeping of animals or fowl in such a manner as to permit the visible accumulation of excrement.
Subsections (3)(b) and (3)(d) of this definition shall have no application to active farming operations on property zoned agricultural, including active poultry and cattle farms.
Offal waste means a type of solid waste which denotes animal matter, whether of land or marine origin, generated by the processing, preparation or packaging of food or animal matter at butcher shops; slaughterhouses; and/or food processing, packaging, rendering or fertilizer plants.
Owner of recovered recyclable material means the generator of recovered material or its authorized agent, contractor, the recovered material collector, the private recycler, the person having possession of recovered material, or the person to whom the generator has transferred title to the recovered material. The owner shall be deemed to have transferred title to the material at the earliest to occur of (i) when the material is placed or deposited in any curbside storage container or recovered material collection container owned by someone other than the generator of the material for the purpose of recycling, or (ii) the time the collector, recycler, or other person takes possession of the material.
Private industry solid waste facility means a disposal facility which is operated exclusively by and for a private solid waste generator for the purpose of accepting solid waste generated exclusively by the private solid waste generator.
Putrescible waste means solid waste that is capable of being quickly decomposed by microorganisms at ambient temperature and conditions and usually associated with offensive odors and disease vectors. Examples include, but are not limited to, kitchen waste, animal manure, offal waste, dead animals, garbage and other solid waste contaminated by such waste.
Recovered/recyclable material means that material which has a known use, reuse, or recycling potential; can be feasibly used, reused or recycled; and has been diverted or removed from the solid waste stream for sale, use, reuse or recycling whether or not requiring subsequent separation and processing. Such material, once recovered, is not solid waste, but all byproducts of such recovery process which are not recovered material shall remain solid waste.
Recovered/recyclable material collector means a person engaged in the business of separating recovered material, with or without compensation, from residential, business, industrial, commercial, institutional or other similar premises, including persons under contractor agreement with the owner or generator of solid waste, recovered material for collection.
Recycling means any process by which material which would otherwise become solid waste is collected, separated or processed and reused or returned to use in the form of raw materials or products.
Residential collection service means the collection and transportation of recyclable material, yard trash, and other solid waste from residential property by a contractor to a solid waste management facility.
Residential solid waste means any garbage, rubbish, yard trash or bulk item that is usual to housekeeping. Residential solid waste is solid waste generated by the customer at the dwelling unit at which the residential solid waste is collected and does not include items defined in this section as commercially generated waste or exempt waste.
Rubbish means waste material other than garbage, usually attendant to domestic households or housekeeping, attendant to the operation of stores, offices, and other business places. Rubbish is nonputrescible and includes but is not limited to, paper, magazines, packaging, receptacles, textile materials, bottles, cans, and ceramic material.
Solid waste means discarded putrescible and nonputrescible wastes, except water-carried body waste and recovered material, and shall include garbage, rubbish, ashes, street refuse, dead animals, sewage sludge, animal manure, industrial waste, residue from solid waste thermal treatment technology, food processing waste, demolition waste, yard trimmings, inert debris, abandoned automobiles, dredging waste, construction and demolition waste, and other waste material in a solid or semisolid or liquid state not otherwise defined in this section or by O.C.G.A. § 12-8-20 et seq. The term "solid waste" shall not include any materials regulated under the Georgia Water Quality Control Act, O.C.G.A. § 12-5-20 et seq., or the Georgia Air Quality Act of 1978, O.C.G.A. § 12-9-1 et seq. Solid waste includes, but is not limited to, commercial solid waste, construction and demolition waste, containerized and uncontainerized waste, garbage, hazardous waste, industrial solid waste, inert debris, institutional solid waste, municipal solid waste, offal waste, putrescible waste, residential solid waste, rubbish, special wastes, white goods waste and yard trimmings or yard trash. Recovered materials are not solid waste.
Solid waste collector means a person permitted by or, in the case of noncounty businesses, registered with, the county to engage in the business of the collection or transportation of solid waste, but excluding a private citizen who collects or transports solid waste from his own dwelling or residence only or any person who picks up litter.
Solid waste management costs means costs incurred to provide solid waste management services, including, without limitation:
(1)
The cost for construction, reconstruction or completion of any facility;
(2)
The cost of acquisition or purchase of any facility;
(3)
The cost of labor, materials, machinery and equipment;
(4)
The cost of fuel, parts, supplies, maintenance, repairs and utilities;
(5)
The cost of computer services, data processing and communications;
(6)
The cost of all lands and interest therein, leases, property rights, easements and franchises of any nature whatsoever;
(7)
The cost of any indemnity or surety bonds and premiums for insurance;
(8)
The cost of salaries, workers compensation insurance or other employment benefits;
(9)
The cost of uniforms, training, travel and per diem;
(10)
Amounts necessary to pay redemption premiums or other costs associated with the early retirement of obligations;
(11)
The creation of reserve or debt service funds;
(12)
Costs and expenses related to the issuance of obligations, all financing charges and any expenses related to the issuance of obligations, all financing charges and any expenses related to any liquidity facility or credit facility;
(13)
The cost of construction plans and specifications, surveys and estimates of costs;
(14)
The cost of engineering, financial, legal and other professional services;
(15)
The costs associated with any inspections required by this article;
(16)
The costs associated with the implementation and enforcement of this article;
(17)
Fees and charges designated to offset the impact of any solid waste handling facility;
(18)
Fees and charges designated to assist in public education efforts for solid waste management and the administration of the comprehensive solid waste management plan; and
(19)
Any and all other costs and expenses properly attributable to providing solid waste management in the county.
Solid waste management facility means any solid waste disposal area, volume reduction plant, transfer station, materials recovery facility, or other facility the purpose of which is the disposal, recycling, processing or storage of solid waste.
Solid waste management plan and solid waste management comprehensive plan mean the county ten-year solid waste management plan, as adopted in 1992 and updated in 1998, which sets forth the goals, objectives and polices for solid waste management in the county.
Solid waste thermal treatment technology means any solid waste management facility that reduces the amount of solid waste to be disposed of through a process of combustion, with or without the production of waste energy.
Special wastes means solid wastes that can require special handling and management under federal, state or local laws, including but not limited to, white goods waste, waste tires, used oil, lead-acid batteries, construction and demolition debris, ash residue, yard trimmings and biological wastes.
Transfer station means a solid waste management facility used to transfer solid waste from one transportation vehicle to another for transportation to a disposal facility or other processing operation.
White goods waste means solid waste consisting of discarded or abandoned large appliances, including refrigerators, freezers, stoves, ranges, water heaters and similar domestic or commercial large appliances. The term "white goods waste" does not include material that is recovered material.
Yard trimmings and yard trash mean leaves, brush, grass clippings, shrub and tree prunings, discarded Christmas trees, nursery and greenhouse vegetative residuals and vegetative matter resulting from landscape development and maintenance, other than mining, agricultural and silvicultural operations.

Sec. 70-35. - Prohibited acts.
(a)
No person shall engage in solid waste handling, except in such a manner as to conform to and comply with this article and all applicable state and federal legislation, rules, regulations and orders.
(b)
No person shall engage in solid waste handling in a manner which will be conducive to insect and rodent infestation or the harboring and feeding of wild dogs or other animals; impair the air quality; impair the quality of the ground or surface waters; or impair the public health, safety or well-being as may be determined by local, state or federal authorities.
(c)
No person shall collect and dispose of solid waste for a fee without obtaining authorization to provide service from the county and any other requisite governing authority as required by state or federal law.
(d)
No individual, partnership, corporation or other entity shall collect or dispose of construction or demolition debris or inert debris without obtaining an authorization to provide such service from the county and any other requisite governing authority as required by state or federal law.
(e)
No solid waste may be disposed of by any person, on property located in the county, in an open dump, nor may any person cause, suffer, allow or permit open dumping on his property as defined by the Rules of the state department of natural resources, environmental protection division, chapter 391-3-4-04.
(f)
The owner, occupant, manager or other person in charge of any premises, office, business establishment, institution, industry, or similar facility, including construction or demolition sites, shall be responsible for the collection and transportation of all solid waste accumulated at the premises, office, business establishment, institution, or similar facility to a solid waste disposal facility operating in compliance with the state environmental protection division rules and regulations unless arrangements have been made for such services with a solid waste collector operating in compliance with this article.
(g)
No owner, occupant, manager or other person in charge of any premises, place, or facility shall allow the accumulation on premises under his control, or on or about his residential unit or commercial establishment, of solid waste where such solid waste creates or may create a health hazard to any other person, or is unsightly, or emits foul or obnoxious odors which constitute either a public or private nuisance. Such conduct shall constitute a violation of this article. Each day the solid waste remains on the premises may constitute a separate violation of this article and shall subject such owner, occupant, manager or other person to the penalties provided in this article.
(h)
No person may dispose of yard trimmings in the county nor dispose of yard trimmings generated in the county unless such disposal is carried out in accordance with section 70-37.
(i)
No person shall create, maintain, support, aid or fail to correct any condition declared to be a nuisance as defined in section 70-34.
(Ord. No. 84, art. I, pt. 6, 6-28-1999; Amendment A of 11-12-2002, art. I, pt. 6, 11-12-2002)
Sec. 70-36. - Fees.
(a)
The board of commissioners hereby establishes and retains the right to amend, at any regular or special commission meeting, the minimum and maximum rates to be charged for: (i) use of a county solid waste disposal facility or site; and (ii) any licensing, permitting, regulatory and/or tipping fees required in connection with solid waste collection, transportation and disposal in the county. The board of commissioners also reserves the right to develop and implement any necessary bookkeeping, auditing, reporting or other programs designed to ensure and facilitate compliance with this article.
(b)
Mindful of the environmental impacts associated with solid waste disposal, the potential for significant environmental damage, and the full range of costs to the county citizens associated with hosting solid waste disposal facilities, no solid waste disposal facility, whether publicly or privately owned, located in the county shall impose upon the county, for use of such disposal facility, fees in excess of those charged to any other entity, including, but not limited to, corporations, companies, businesses, partnerships and governmental entities that enjoys use such facility.
(c)
The rates in this article and established for solid waste disposal activities shall be reviewed and revised from time to time by affirmative action to defray solid waste management costs as defined in section 70-34. A notice specifying the rate to be so established or revised shall be published in the official county organ at least 15 but not more than 45 days prior the adoption or final implementation of any such rate or revision.
(d)
Schedule A, a schedule of all applicable rates and fees with respect to solid waste collection, transportation and disposal, shall be available from both the clerk of the board of commissioners and the county manager or his designee.
(Ord. No. 84, art. I, pt. 7, 6-28-1999)
Sec. 70-37. - Individual placement and disposal of yard trimmings.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to place or mix yard trimmings with other solid waste within the county.
(b)
Yard trimmings shall not be disposed of at any solid waste disposal facility with liners or leachate collection systems or any municipal solid waste landfills which have received a vertical expansion under O.C.G.A. § 12-8-40.2.
(c)
Yard trimmings shall be sorted and stored for collection in a manner that facilitates collection, composting, or other lawful handling.
(d)
Yard trimmings shall be placed in separate bags or containers. Such bags or containers may include reusable receptacles provided by residents which meet the requirements of this part, watertight paper bags manufactured for yard trimming disposal which, when full, do not exceed 50 pounds in weight, or special containers provided by a solid waste collector, but may not include plastic bags. Notwithstanding the foregoing provisions of this subsection, tree branches, tree trunks, and heavy brush which cannot be containerized and do not exceed four feet in length or six inches in diameter may be neatly stacked. No further preparation shall be required.
(e)
Upon collection, yard trimmings shall be sorted and stockpiled until their ultimate disposal by chipping, mulching, other beneficial use, or recycling.
(f)
The county manager or his designee is authorized to modify the requirements for preparation of yard trimmings and to arrange collection services for organized community cleanup programs which are coordinated with the county manager or his designee expressly and solely for the cleanup of massive property destruction resulting from fires and natural disasters.
(g)
Yard trimmings shall not be placed at the curb of or adjacent to the street in front of any residence, place or premises for more than two weeks. Each day such wastes remain at the curb after the 14th day shall constitute a separate violation of this article. It shall be unlawful for any person to place yard trimmings for removal by any solid waste collector which are not prepared, placed and separated as required by this article.
(h)
It shall be the duty of the solid waste collector to notify the county manager or his designee of any noncompliance with this part. Upon such notice of noncompliance, the county manager or his designee shall notify the property owner or occupant in writing to remove all yard trimmings not prepared and placed as required by this section, at the property owner's expense or to prepare them in accordance with this article. Upon the failure of the property owner or occupant to remove or prepare such yard trimmings within the time set forth in the notice, the property owner or occupant shall receive a citation as provided in division 4 of this article.
(Ord. No. 84, art. I, pt. 8, 6-28-1999)

DIVISION 2. - SOLID WASTE PLACEMENT, COLLECTION AND DISPOSAL

Sec. 70-56. - Requirements for solid waste collectors.
All solid waste collectors doing business in the county must meet the following minimum requirements:
(1)
Indemnification. Solid waste collectors shall, at their sole cost and expense, fully indemnify, defend and hold harmless the county, its officers, boards, commissions, employees and agents against any and all claims, suits, actions, liability and judgments from third parties for damages which may be the result of willful, negligent or tortious conduct, acts, or omissions arising out of the business of collection, transportation and disposal of solid waste, whether or not the conduct, action, or omission complained of is authorized, allowed or prohibited by this article.
(2)
Vehicles.
a.
All vehicles and containers used for collection operations shall comply with the requirements of rule 391-3-4-.06 of chapter 391-3-4 (solid waste management) of the rules of the state department of natural resources, environmental protection division.
b.
Vehicles and containers shall meet all requirements of the state department of transportation for highway safety and local ordinances governing weight and size for the streets, which must be traveled for pickup.
c.
All vehicles shall have, in letters at least six inches high and conspicuously placed on both sides of the vehicle, the name and telephone number of the solid waste collector.
(3)
Collection. Solid waste collectors must agree to provide collection pursuant to the criteria set forth in this article, and in residential areas may pick up only between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 7:00 p.m., Monday through Saturday.
(Ord. No. 84, art. II, div. I, pt. 1, 6-28-1999)
Sec. 70-57. - Solid waste disposal facilities.
No person shall dispose of any solid waste within the county at any premises other than at a site designated and approved by the board of commissioners. All solid waste disposal facilities shall limit their operations to the hours of 8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m., Monday through Saturday. No unloading or dumping at disposal facilities shall occur other than during the hours of operation set out in this section.
(Ord. No. 84, art. II, div. II, 6-28-1999)
Sec. 70-58. - Publicly owned disposal facilities; payment for use of facilities.
(a)
Dumping at county transfer stations. Only private citizens disposing of their own residential solid waste may use county transfer stations. Each time a person delivers a load of solid waste to a transfer station, each bag of solid waste will be counted and recorded by the attendant. Payment will be made on a per bag basis to the attendant in an amount sufficient to cover the disposal charge.
(b)
Payment. All persons using the county's disposal facilities will be required to pay for the use of the facilities at the time they are used according to the fees outlined in schedule A, which shall be available from both the clerk of the board of commissioners and the county manager or his designee. All monies received by the county will be remitted to the finance department.
(c)
Separation of yard trimmings and recovered/recyclable materials. Private citizens who dispose of their personal, residential solid waste at county transfer stations are nonetheless required to separate and dispose of their yard trimmings and recovered/recyclable materials in accordance with the guidelines expressed in this article.
(Ord. No. 84, art. II, div. II, pt. 1, 6-28-1999)
Sec. 70-59. - Hazardous wastes; household hazardous wastes.
(a)
No person shall be permitted to dispose of any hazardous wastes at any of the solid waste disposal facilities operated by the county.
(b)
All solid waste disposal facilities located in the county, whether privately or publicly owned, shall make reasonable efforts to educate the public and work toward a reduction in the amount of household hazardous waste that is disposed of in the county.
(c)
It shall be a violation of this article to place or cause to be placed for collection any acid, explosive material, flammable liquids or dangerous or corrosive material of any kind. Such materials must be handled in accordance with all federal, state and local laws, rules, directives and orders.
(Ord. No. 84, art. II, div. II, pt. 2, 6-28-1999)

Sec. 70-60. - Privately operated solid waste disposal facilities.
(a)
Municipal solid waste landfills.
(1)
Permit required. Any person desiring to operate a municipal solid waste landfill within the county must first obtain a written permit to establish and operate the landfill from the board of commissioners. The permit shall be renewable annually and must be obtained prior to the issuance of a business license.

DIVISION 3. - LITTER CONTROL

Sec. 70-81. - Littering public or private property or waters.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to dump, throw or leave litter on any public or private property in the county unless:
(1)
The property is designated by the county for the disposal of litter.
(2)
The litter is placed into a litter receptacle or container installed on such property in such a manner as to prevent it from being carried and deposited by the elements upon any public or private property.
(3)
The person is the owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property or unless the act is done under the personal direction of the owner or tenant, all in a manner consistent with the public welfare and not in violation of any other law, rule or regulation.
(b)
Any person who violates this part shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction thereof, shall be punished as follows:
(1)
If the litter is ten pounds in weight or less or 15 cubic feet in volume or less, by a fine as set forth in division 4 of this article; or
(2)
If litter is in excess of ten pounds in weight or 15 cubic feet in volume, notwithstanding the fines set forth in division 4 of this article, by a fine of $1,000.00 for each occurrence and each occurrence shall be deemed a separate offense;
(3)
In addition to the fines set out in subsections (b)(1) and (b)(2) of this section, the violator shall reimburse the county for the reasonable cost of removing the litter when the litter is or is ordered removed by the county; and
(4)
In the sound discretion of a court in which a conviction is obtained, the violator may be directed to pick up and remove from any public street or highway or public right-of-way for a distance not to exceed one mile any litter that has been deposited thereon by anyone and/or repair or restore property damaged by such littering.
(Ord. No. 84, art. III, pt. 1, 6-28-1999)
Sec. 70-82. - Vehicle loads causing litter.
No person shall drive or move any vehicle within the county unless such vehicle is constructed or loaded so as to prevent any load, contents, litter, or debris contained in such vehicle from being blown or deposited upon any street, alley or other public place. It shall be a violation of this article for any portion of a load, contents, litter, or debris to be blown or deposited upon any street, alley, or public place from a vehicle traveling within the county. Additionally, no person shall drive or move any truck or vehicle upon, nor shall a person drive or move any truck or vehicle from any solid waste disposal facility or site in the county unless such vehicle is covered by canvas or other material providing complete coverage, properly secured, so as to prevent any load, contents, litter, or debris from becoming loose, detached or blown from the vehicle, or from dropping or escaping from the vehicle during the movement of such truck or vehicle.
(Ord. No. 84, art. III, pt. 2, 6-28-1999; Ord. No. 84-C, § 1, 12-5-2019)
Cross reference— Traffic and vehicles, ch. 78.
Sec. 70-83. - Newspapers, pamphlets fliers and other publications distributed without charge.
(a)
Newspapers, pamphlets, fliers and other publications protected under the First Amendment to the United States Constitution and state Constitution, may be distributed on public property and to private premises so long as upon any request from a property owner or tenant in lawful possession to be removed from the distributor's distribution list, the name of such person or entity is removed, and no further delivery of the unsolicited material is effected to the subject property. Any person who causes a publication to be distributed after having received notice from a person or an entity to be removed from the distribution list shall be in violation of this article.
(b)
The distributor and/or publisher of newspapers, pamphlets, fliers and other publications is responsible for collecting and retrieving any papers that residents do not pick up within five days of distribution.
(c)
It is considered a violation of this article for any publisher or distributor to allow their distributed materials to litter the county streets, waters, sewers and drainage facilities.
(Ord. No. 84, art. III, pt. 3, 6-28-1999)
Sec. 70-84. - Evidence and presumptions.
(a)
Whenever litter is thrown, deposited or dumped from any motor vehicle, boat, airplane or other conveyance in violation of this article, it shall be prima facie evidence that the operator of the conveyance has violated this article.
(b)
Whenever any litter that is dumped, deposited, thrown or left on public or private property in violation of this article is discovered to contain any item or items or writings which display the name of a person thereon in such a manner as to indicate that the item belongs or belonged to such person, it shall be a rebuttal presumption that such person has violated this article.
(Ord. No. 84, art. III, pt. 4, 6-28-1999)

Sec. 70-152. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Biomedical waste means:
(1)
Pathological waste, which means recognizable human tissues and body parts except teeth which are removed during surgery, obstetrical procedures, autopsy, and laboratory procedures.
(2)
Biological waste, which means bulk blood products, exudates, secretions, suctionings, and other bulk body fluids which cannot or are not directly discarded into a county sewer system.
(3)
Cultures and stocks of infectious agents and associated biologicals, including cultures from medical and pathological laboratories, cultures and stocks of infectious agents from research and industrial laboratories, wastes from the production of biologicals, discarded live and attenuated vaccines, and culture dishes and devices used to transfer, inoculate, and mix cultures.
(4)
Contaminated animal carcasses, body parts, their bedding, and other wastes from such animals which have been exposed to infectious agents, capable of causing disease in man, during research, production of biologicals, or testing of pharmaceuticals.
(5)
Sharps, which means any discarded article that may cause punctures or cuts. Such waste includes, but is not limited to, items such as needles, IV tubing and syringes with needles attached, and scalpel blades.
(6)
Chemotherapy waste, which means any disposable material which has come in contact with cytotoxic antineoplastic agents (agents toxic to cells) masks, gloves, gowns, empty IV tubing bags and vials, and other contaminated medical materials, parts and equipment.
(7)
Discarded medical equipment and parts, not including expendable supplies and materials, which have not been decontaminated, and that were in contact with infectious agents.
Hazardous waste means any solid waste which has been defined as a hazardous waste in regulations promulgated by the state board of natural resources, chapter 391-3-11, and under federal regulations promulgated by the United States Environmental Protection Agency and codified at 40 CFR 261.3.
Incineration means a controlled process by which solid, liquid and gaseous combustible wastes are burned and changed into gases and a residue produced which is relatively free of combustible materials.
Industrial waste means solid waste generated by manufacturing or industrial processes that is not a hazardous waste under regulations promulgated by the state board of natural resources, chapter 391-3-11.
Medical facility means, but is not limited to, clinics, dental offices, funeral homes, health maintenance organizations (HMOs), hospitals, laboratories, medical buildings, physicians offices, veterinary offices, research and manufacturing facilities, nursing homes, and blood banks.
Pyrolysis means the chemical decomposition of a material by heat in the absence of oxygen.
(Ord. No. 10, § 2, 8-24-1981; Ord. No. 10A, § 2, 8-27-1990)

After the hearing, the commissioners' designee shall have 60 days in which to determine if the application is complete and shall mail notice of its determination to the applicant. If it is not complete, the applicant will have six months to complete the application. However, the applicant may at the end of six months make a showing of cause to the board of commissioners and if the board finds that the delay is justified and in good faith, they can grant the applicant a maximum three month extension.
(e)
Each application shall require an analysis conducted by the county staff and a consultant or consultants selected by the board of commissioners. The analysis shall be completed within 90 days from the day the application is determined to be complete. In certain instances where the complexity of the application requires more than the usual 90 days, the county staff and/or consultant may request an additional 60 days from the board of commissioners and the proponent has the option of requesting the board of commissioners to extend the analysis period to allow time for responding to staff and/or consultant request for additional information on a completed application.
(f)
The commissioners' designee and each consultant shall make reports on the application to the board of commissioners.
(g)
The board of commissioners shall call a public meeting for public comment on the completed application along with the analysis of county staff and consultants. The purpose of this meeting shall be for public review of the application. The staff shall give notice by regular mail of the time and place of the public meeting to the owner and adjacent property owners as specified on the map. Such notice shall be mailed not less than 14 days prior to the date specified thereon. Such posted notices shall be at intervals of not greater than 1,500 feet. Notice shall also be placed by the applicant in the county newspaper not less than 14 days prior to the dates specified thereon.
(h)
Within 45 days after receipt of the final analysis, completed application and public comment, the board of commissioners will render its decision at a public meeting whether to accept the application, deny it, or accept it with modifications and/or conditions. The board of commissioners' decision shall be made upon the following determinations:
(1)
That the construction and operation of the facility will not pose an unreasonable health or environmental risk to the surrounding locality.
(2)
That the applicant or facility operator has the capability and financial resources to construct, operate and maintain the facility.
(3)
That the applicant or operator has taken or consented in writing to take any and all reasonable measures to comply with applicable federal, state and local regulations and ordinances.
(4)
That the applicant's plan represents the best available technology for handling the waste for which the applicant will be permitted and that the applicant has demonstrated that it will employ the best management practices in handling the waste at the proposed facility.
(i)
A permit shall be valid for no more than 18 months from the date it is granted by the board of county commissioners unless the applicant begins construction of the facility prior to the expiration of the permit and continues to operate the facility according to specified conditions. If a permit becomes invalid and the application is unchanged from when the permit was granted, the applicant shall follow the procedure of section 70-154 and pay the filing fee of section 70-155.
(Ord. No. 10, § 6, 8-24-1981; Ord. No. 10A, § 6, 8-27-1990)
Sec. 70-157. - Privilege license tax.
A privilege license tax will be required and paid annually. The privilege license tax will be in direct proportion to the costs incurred by the county to monitor the facility to ensure compliance with the regulations contained in this article and the amount necessary to prepare the county to respond to emergencies which may result from any emergency caused by the facility. This fee will be calculated during the application fee process and updated annually.
(Ord. No. 10, § 7, 8-24-1981; Ord. No. 10A, § 7, 8-27-1990)
Sec. 70-158. - Penalty for violation.
Any person violating the prohibitions of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall upon conviction be punished as provided in section 1-12. In addition to the foregoing, the board of commissioners may modify or revoke any permit issued pursuant to this article if the holder of the permit is found to be in violation of any of the permit conditions, if the holder of the permit fails to perform such activity in accordance with the approved plan, or if such activity creates a threat to human health or the environment. In the event of modification or revocation of a permit, the board of commissioners shall serve written notice of such action on the permit holder and shall set forth in such notice the reason for such action.
(Ord. No. 10, § 8, 8-24-1981; Ord. No. 10A, § 8, 8-27-1990)
