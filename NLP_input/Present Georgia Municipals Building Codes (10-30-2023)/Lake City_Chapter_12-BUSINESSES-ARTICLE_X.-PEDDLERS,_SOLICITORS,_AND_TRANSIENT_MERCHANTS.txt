DIVISION 2. - PEDDLERS AND SOLICITORS

Sec. 12-369. - Registration; permit.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell, attempt to sell, take order for future delivery or promote any product, picture, map, book, magazine, household device, equipment or goods at any residence in the city without first registering with the chief of police. The person shall provide the chief with a recognizable photograph of himself and shall pay a regulatory fee for a 30-day permit. The regulatory fee will be in an amount as established by the mayor and council from time to time.
(Code 1972, § 14-1; Code 1994, § 78-1; Ord. No. 99-01, § 1(78-1), 6-14-1999)
Sec. 12-370. - Notification of division provisions.
It shall be the duty of the chief of police to provide a copy of the ordinance from which this division is derived to any person to whom he issues a permit under section 12-369.
(Code 1972, § 14-5; Code 1994, § 78-2)
Sec. 12-371. - Hours of operation.
It shall be unlawful for any person holding a valid permit under section 12-369 to make any calls except between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m. No calls shall be made on Sunday.
(Code 1972, § 14-2; Code 1994, § 78-3)
Sec. 12-372. - Calls prohibited on certain property.
It shall be unlawful for any person holding a valid permit under section 12-369 to make a call at any residence where the owner or any person residing therein has posted a sign reading as follows: "No Solicitors."
(Code 1972, § 14-3; Code 1994, § 78-4)
Sec. 12-373. - Failure to leave premises upon request.
It shall be unlawful for any person holding a valid permit under section 12-369 to fail to immediately leave the premises of any person when requested to do so.
(Code 1972, § 14-4; Code 1994, § 78-5)

DIVISION 3. - TRANSIENT MERCHANTS[9]

State Law reference— Transient Merchant Act of Georgia, O.C.G.A. § 43-46-1 et seq.

Sec. 12-405. - Definitions.
For the purposes of this division, the following words and phrases shall have the meanings respectively ascribed to them by this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Merchandise means any samples, model goods, wares, or merchandise sold or offered for sale by a transient merchant.
Transient merchant.
(1)
The term "transient merchant" means any person, firm, or corporation, as principal or agent, or both, which is not a regular retail or wholesale merchant with a permanent place of business in the city, but rather one who displays samples, model goods, wares, or merchandise in any lot, building, room, vehicle, trailer or structure of any kind, whether fixed or mobile, for the purpose of retail sales and the securing of orders for the retail sale of such items or items of like kind or quality for the immediate or future delivery.
(2)
The term "transient merchant" shall not include any person, firm, or corporation which:
a.
Sells agriculture or forestry products;
b.
Makes house-to-house or personal calls for the purpose of displaying samples or taking orders for shipment directly from a manufacturer;
c.
Conducts business at any industry or association trade show; or
d.
Sells items at a rummage, garage, antique, or similar sale when such person, firm, or corporation is a resident of the city or has a permanent place of business within the city and such sale has been authorized by city permit.
(Code 1994, § 100-1; Ord. No. 95-3, § 1, 9-11-1995)
Sec. 12-406. - Registration; permit.
modified
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to act as a transient merchant within the city without first registering with the city manager. Any person desiring to operate as a transient merchant within the city shall make application for a permit in writing and file the application with the city manager. The application shall contain the following:
(1)
The name and permanent address of the transient merchant;
(2)
The name and permanent address of the transient merchant's registered agent or office;
(3)
The type of business to be conducted, including a description of the merchandise to be sold by the transient merchant;
(4)
The form of the transient merchant's business (i.e., corporation, partnership, sole proprietorship), including the date and state of incorporation, when said transient merchant is a corporation;
(5)
A recognizable photograph of the transient merchant and any person who will be working with the transient merchant within the city;
(6)
Documentation in writing from the owner of the property where the transient merchant intends to be located, by lease or otherwise, stating that the transient merchant has permission from the owner to transact business on the property;
(7)
A map or diagram indicating the parking to be used by customers of the transient merchant. Any such parking must not diminish the available parking for the underlying use of the property below that required by city ordinances;
(8)
A copy of a liability insurance policy acceptable to the mayor and council and providing liability insurance in an amount as established by the mayor and council from time to time;
(9)
A bond in an amount payable to the city so as to provide recourse to the city in the event that a complaint is made by a citizen of defective merchandise sold by the transient merchant and the citizen has no recourse against the transient merchant. The bond will be good for a period through six months for an amount as established by the mayor and council from time to time;
(10)
A description of the lot, building, room, vehicle, trailer or structure of any kind, whether fixed or mobile, in which the transient merchant intends to conduct business within the city; and
(11)
A set of fingerprints prepared by the chief of police for the transient merchant and all agents or employees who will sell merchandise on behalf of the transient merchant within the city.
(b)
The application shall be made under oath and the giving of false information shall result in the revocation of any permit issued in reliance thereon.
(c)
A permit fee must be filed contemporaneously with the application for a 30-day permit. The permit fee will be in an amount as established by the mayor and council from time to time.
(d)
No more than two permits shall be issued to any transient merchant in any calendar year.
(e)
Notwithstanding permission from the property owner, no more than two permits in non-consecutive months shall be issued for the same property in any calendar year.
(f)
The permit(s) issued shall be nontransferable.
(Code 1994, § 100-2; Ord. No. 95-3, § 1, 9-11-1995; Ord. No. 2023-02, § 2, 6-12-2023)
Sec. 12-407. - Hours of operation.
It shall be unlawful for any person holding a valid permit under section 12-406 to conduct any business or sell any merchandise except between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 8:00 p.m.
(Code 1994, § 100-3; Ord. No. 95-3, § 1, 9-11-1995)
Sec. 12-408. - Location of business; signs.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any transient merchant to conduct business or to be located on any city-owned rights-of-way or to be situated so as to block or impede traffic or interfere with traffic by blocking vision at intersections.
(b)
The location of the business of a transient merchant must comply with all commercial setback requirements as provided in chapter 42, pertaining to zoning. Establishment of sales and/or display areas within required front yards is specifically prohibited. The parking required for all businesses shall also be provided by transient merchants and such parking shall not diminish the required parking of any business also located on the lot or parcel utilized by the transient merchant.
(c)
No signs or other visual displays shall be posted by a transient merchant without first obtaining a sign permit as provided in this Code.
(Code 1994, § 100-4; Ord. No. 95-3, § 1, 9-11-1995)
Sec. 12-409. - Business operations.
(a)
All merchandise must be displayed by the transient merchant in a neat and orderly fashion.
(b)
All merchandise displayed by the transient merchant must be secure from wind, rain, or other inclement weather. In the event that merchandise is not under a cover or in an enclosed structure, all merchandise must be removed and safely secured from the rain, wind or other inclement weather.
(c)
All merchandise must be in a secured location during non-business hours. In the event that a transient merchant does not have a building, structure, or vehicle in which the merchandise can be locked, all merchandise must be removed from the property during non-business hours.
(Code 1994, § 100-5; Ord. No. 95-3, § 1, 9-11-1995)
Sec. 12-410. - Inspection authority.
The chief of police or his agent shall have the right to enter onto the property where the transient merchant is conducting business, at reasonable times, to inspect the property and business operation of the transient merchant for compliance with this division.
(Code 1994, § 100-6; Ord. No. 95-3, § 1, 9-11-1995)
Sec. 12-411. - Penalties.
(a)
Upon conviction or the entering of a plea of nolo contendere to any citation under this division, the person cited shall be subject to penalties set out in section 1-8.
(b)
In addition to the criminal penalties prescribed by subsection (a) of this section, upon conviction or plea of guilty or nolo contendere, the permit of the transient merchant shall be revoked. No new permit shall issue to a transient merchant whose permit has been forfeited within the city for a period of 12 months. Revocation of permits shall be accomplished by action of the mayor and council following notice and opportunity to be heard.
(Code 1994, § 100-7; Ord. No. 95-3, § 1, 9-11-1995)
