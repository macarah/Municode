Sec. 20-26. - Composition.
The police force of the city shall consist of such officers and employees as shall be determined by the mayor and council.
(Code 1977, § 3-1001)
Sec. 20-27. - Rank, authority of police chief.
The police chief shall be the ranking head and chief in command of the department and shall be the chief administrative officer of the police force. He shall be responsible for the orderly and efficient operation of the police department.
(Code 1977, §§ 2-7059, 3-1002)
Sec. 20-28. - Power to be exercised by the official holding office.
Any and all rights, powers and duties vested in or imposed upon the chief of police shall, in absence of either from duty, devolve upon the official next in rank or the person who by law or otherwise is discharging the duties of the office.
(Code 1977, § 2-7054)
Sec. 20-29. - Function of police department.
(a)
Preserve order. It shall be the duty of the chief of police and all members of the police department to preserve order in the city and to arrest all drunken, disorderly or riotous persons who are disturbing the peace and quiet of the city. In case of their inability to suppress these disorders, the chief of police shall have power to call to their assistance a sufficient number of persons to act as additional officers for the occasion, whose compensation shall be fixed by the mayor and council.
(b)
Enforce ordinances, execute orders. It shall be the duty of the chief and all members of the police department to enforce and carry into effect all laws and ordinances, to obey and execute all the commands and orders of the mayor, in relation to any matter or thing affecting the interests of the city.
(c)
Arrest violators. It shall be the duty of the chief of police and regularly appointed police of the city to use all proper diligence in the arrest of any person accused of a violation of a law or ordinance, summon the witnesses in a legal manner, bring them before the recorder and enforce his decisions without unnecessary delay.
(d)
Issue, serve summons, processes. The chief of police and all members of the police department shall be vested with full power and authority to issue and to serve summons for any violation of any law or ordinance; and to serve all processes, executions, attachments, rules and orders of the chief of police, the municipal court and/or the mayor and council.
(Code 1977, § 3-1003)
Sec. 20-30. - Members subject to civil service rules, regulations.
The police department and the members thereof shall be governed and controlled by and under civil service rules and regulations adopted by the city.
(Code 1977, § 2-7052)
Sec. 20-31. - Hiring of P.O.S.T. certified law enforcement officers.
(a)
Purpose. The purpose of this section is to provide a mechanism for the hiring of law enforcement officers who have been certified by the Georgia P.O.S.T. program or other comparable state agency(ies).
(b)
Applicable definitions.
(1)
Assessment center means a testing procedure conducted by the city for the assessment of an applicant's ability to serve as a police officer.
(2)
Law enforcement officer means an officer in a law enforcement agency specifically not including military police, security guards or school campus police.
(3)
P.O.S.T. means State of Georgia Police Officers Standards and Training Council.
(c)
General application.
(1)
A law enforcement officer who meets the requirement of this section shall be exempted from the requirement of satisfactory completion of an assessment center prior to hiring by the city.
(2)
In order to be exempt from the assessment center requirements, applicant must meet each of the following criteria:
a.
The officer must be P.O.S.T. certified in the State of Georgia or must be certified by a comparable state agency in a state other than Georgia.
b.
If an applicant is certified in a state other than Georgia, the chief of police, with the approval of the human resources director, will make a recommendation concerning the certification requirements of the state in question. Approval for exemption under this policy shall be granted only if an applicant is a certified officer in a state whose requirements meet or exceed those of the State of Georgia.
c.
An applicant which meets the requirements of this policy shall be required to take and satisfactorily complete a medical examination, psychological test and physical stamina test.
(Code 1977, § 2-7062; Ord. No. 34-94, 8-1-94; Ord. No. 45-94, §§ 1—3, 10-17-94)
State Law reference— Georgia Peace Officers Standards and Training Act, O.C.G.A. § 35-8-1.
Sec. 20-32. - Eligibility for employment.
Before any person shall become a member of the police department, he/she shall be at least twenty-one (21) years of age before completing field training, shall be required to pass a medical examination and shall meet all the requirements of the mayor and council including a physical readiness test.
(Code 1977, § 2-7051; Ord. No. 43-95, § 1, 9-5-95; Ord. No. 36-00, § 1, 6-5-00; Ord. No. 23-02, § 1, 2-19-02)
State Law reference— Residency requirements for police, O.C.G.A. § 45-2-5.
Sec. 20-33. - Oath of office.
Before any officer shall assume the duties of his office, the officer shall take an oath to be administered by the mayor.
(Code 1977, § 2-7055; Ord. No. 20-93, § 1, 3-1-93; Ord. No. 29-97, § 1, 4-7-97)
Sec. 20-34. - Clothing allowance.
There shall be clothing allowance for the chief of police and for non-uniformed personnel authorized by the chief of police, as provided in the city's annual budget ordinance.
(Code 1977, § 2-7061; Ord. No. 64-96, § 1, 8-5-96)
