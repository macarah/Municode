ARTICLE V. - DISPOSITION OF PROPERTY

Sec. 2-111. - Surplus stock.
All using departments, constitutional officers, boards and authorities shall submit to the county administrator at such times and in such form as he shall prescribe, reports showing stocks of all supplies and equipment which are no longer used or which have become obsolete, worn out, or scrapped.
(Code 1991, § 2-150; Ord. No. 2011-06, 10-25-2011)
Sec. 2-112. - Disposition of real property.
Disposition of real property shall be conducted as a public sale in compliance with O.C.G.A. § 36-9-3 or as otherwise provided for by state law. The following exceptions apply to this public sale requirement:
(1)
A redemption of real property acquired by a tax deed.
(2)
A grant of easement or license.
(3)
A grant or conveyance of right-of-way or other transportation purpose.
(4)
A conveyance to any other unit of government.
(5)
A conveyance of recreational area to the recreation authority.
(Code 1991, § 2-151; Ord. No. 2011-06, 10-25-2011)
Sec. 2-113. - Disposition of personal property.
The board of commissioners shall be the sole determinant of whether a particular item or group of items has no further value to the county. They may deem such personal property as surplus and order it sold by public auction, sealed bids, spot bids or by other means most advantageous to the county.
(Code 1991, § 2-152; Ord. No. 2011-06, 10-25-2011)
