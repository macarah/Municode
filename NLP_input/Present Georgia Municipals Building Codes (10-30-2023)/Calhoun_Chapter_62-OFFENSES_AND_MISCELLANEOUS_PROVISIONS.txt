ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 62-1. - Misdemeanors under state law.
Any act the commission of which constitutes a misdemeanor under the laws of the state is prohibited within the city, and if committed within the city, is hereby declared to be a misdemeanor; provided, however, that the penalty therefor shall not exceed the penalty prescribed in section 1-7.
(Code 1988, § 13-1)
Sec. 62-2. - Attempt to commit misdemeanor.
An attempt by any person to commit an act, the commission of which is prohibited, shall be prohibited and shall be punished as provided in section 1-7.
(Code 1988, § 13-2)
Sec. 62-3. - Improper use of police insignia.
It shall be unlawful for any person not authorized by the proper authorities of the city to wear or display any insignia of the city police department, including, but not limited to, what is commonly known as the shoulder patch. It shall be unlawful to wear such emblem, insignia or shoulder patch attached to any part of a person's clothing in any manner where it can be seen by the public.
(Code 1988, § 13-3)
Sec. 62-4. - Assemblies prohibited near fire hydrants.
It shall be unlawful for any person to cause a congregation of people at any place within the city within a distance of 100 feet from any fire hydrant installed by the city, unless such congregation of people is wholly confined within the walls of a building.
(Code 1988, § 13-4)
Cross reference— Fire hydrants, § 50-101 et seq.
Sec. 62-5. - Disorderly conduct.
A person commits the misdemeanor of disorderly conduct if:
(1)
Such person unreasonably and knowingly disturbs or alarms another person by:
a.
Loud noise;
b.
Offensive and indecent language which is likely to produce an immediate violent response from a reasonable recipient;
c.
Threatening to commit a crime against any person;
d.
Fighting; or
e.
Creating a noxious and offensive odor.
(2)
Such person is in a public place or on private property of another without consent and purposely causes inconvenience to another person by unreasonably and physically obstructing:
a.
Vehicular or pedestrian traffic; or
b.
The free ingress or egress to or from a public or private place.
(3)
Such person acts in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another, whereby any person is placed in danger of safety of his life, limb or health.
(4)
Such person acts in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another, whereby public property or property of any other person is placed in danger of being destroyed or damaged.
(5)
Such person endangers lawful pursuits of another by acts of violence or threats of bodily harm.
(6)
Such person causes, provokes or engages in any fight, brawl or riotous conduct so as to endanger the life, limb, health or property of another or public property.
(7)
Such person collects in bodies or in crowds and engages in unlawful activities.
(8)
Such person assembles or congregates with another or others and engages or attempts to engage in gaming.
(9)
Such person frequents any public place and obtains money from another by an illegal and fraudulent scheme, trick, artifice or device, or attempts to do so.
(10)
Such person uses fighting words, as defined in section 62-10, directed towards any person who becomes outraged and thus creates turmoil.
(Code 1988, § 13-5; Ord. No. 534A, 3-28-1994)
State Law reference— Disorderly conduct, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-39.
Sec. 62-6. - Damaging or defacing private property.
It shall be unlawful for any person to write, print, draw or cut any letter, word, sign or symbol or in any way to damage, destroy, mutilate or deface any private property belonging to another, without the latter's consent.
(Code 1988, § 13-6)
Sec. 62-7. - Damaging or defacing public property.
It shall be unlawful for any person to damage, deface, mutilate or destroy any streetlights, traffic signs or signals or any other public property in the city.
(Code 1988, § 13-7)
State Law reference— Damage to government property, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-24.
Sec. 62-8. - Public drunkenness.
A person who shall be and appear in an intoxicated condition in any public place or within the curtilage of any private residence not such person's own other than by invitation of the owner or lawful occupant, which condition is made manifest by boisterousness, by indecent condition or act, or by vulgar, profane, loud, or unbecoming language, is guilty of a misdemeanor.
(Code 1988, § 13-10)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-41(a).
Sec. 62-9. - Discharge of firearms or airguns.
It shall be unlawful for any person to discharge or shoot any firearm or airgun within the city.
(Code 1988, § 13-12)
Sec. 62-10. - Use of fighting words or obscene or profane language.
A person who commits any of the following acts commits a misdemeanor:
(1)
Without provocation, uses to or of another, in such other's presence, opprobrious or abusive words which by their very utterance tend to incite to an immediate breach of the peace, that is to say, words which as a matter of common knowledge and under ordinary circumstances will, when used to or of another person in such other person's presence, naturally tend to provoke violent resentment, that is, words commonly called "fighting words."
(2)
Without provocation, uses obscene and vulgar or profane language in the presence of or by telephone to a person under the age of 14 years which threatens an immediate breach of the peace.
(Code 1988, § 13-13)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-39.
Sec. 62-11. - Prostitution.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep, frequent or be in any house or building used for the purpose of prostitution, lewdness or assignation; or for any person to offer to commit or to commit or engage in prostitution, lewdness or assignation; or to solicit, induce, entice or procure another to engage in prostitution, lewdness or assignation; or to aid, abet or participate in the doing of any of the foregoing.
(b)
A person commits the offense of prostitution when such person performs or offers or consents to perform an act of sexual intercourse for money.
(Code 1988, § 13-14)
State Law reference— Prostitution, O.C.G.A. § 16-6-9.
Sec. 62-12. - Possession of one ounce or less of marijuana.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to possess or have under his control marijuana, as defined in this section, where the quantity of such marijuana is one ounce or less, anywhere within the corporate limits of the city.
(b)
For purposes of this section, the term "marijuana" means all parts of the plant of the genus Cannabis, whether growing or not, the seeds thereof, the resin extracted from any part of such plant, and every compound, manufacture, salt, derivative, mixture, or preparation of such plant, its seeds, or resin, and shall include the completely defoliated mature stalks of such plant, fiber produced from such stalks, oil, or cake, or the completely sterilized samples of seeds of the plant which are incapable of germination.
(Code 1988, § 13-16)
Sec. 62-13. - Theft by shoplifting.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to commit the offense of theft by shoplifting as defined in this section within the corporate limits of the city where the property which was the subject of the theft was valued at $300.00 or less.
(b)
A person commits the offense of theft by shoplifting when he, alone or in concert with another person, with the intent of appropriating merchandise to his own use without paying for the merchandise or to deprive the owner of possession thereof or of the value thereof, in whole or in part, does any of the following:
(1)
Conceals or takes possession of the goods or merchandise of any store or retail establishment;
(2)
Alters the price tag or other price marking on goods or merchandise of any store or retail establishment;
(3)
Transfers the goods or merchandise of any store or retail establishment from one container to another;
(4)
Interchanges the label or price tag from one item of merchandise with a label or price tag for another item of merchandise; or
(5)
Wrongfully causes the amount paid to be less than the merchant's stated price for the merchandise.
(c)
The municipal court for the city may exercise concurrent jurisdiction of violation of this section for persons as to either the first or second offense which occurs inside the corporate limits of the city, and the penalties upon conviction shall be in accordance with section 1-7; however, such court, in exercising this jurisdiction, in addition to or in lieu of any imprisonment which might be imposed, shall fine such person not less than $250.00, which fine shall not be suspended or probated.
(Code 1988, § 13-18)
Charter reference— Municipal court jurisdiction, § 6.104.
State Law reference— Shoplifting, O.C.G.A. § 16-8-14; municipal court granted jurisdiction to try and dispose of shoplifting charges, O.C.G.A. § 36-32-9.
Sec. 62-14. - Littering.
(a)
Intent. It is the intention of the mayor and council by this section to provide for uniform prohibition throughout the city of any and all littering on public or private property, and to curb thereby the desecration of the beauty of the city and harm to the health, welfare and safety of its citizens caused by individuals who litter.
(b)
Definitions. As used in this section:
(1)
Litter means all discarded sand, gravel, slag, brickbats, rubbish, waste materials, metal or aluminum cans, refuse, garbage, trash, concrete or asphalt not an integral part of any road, street, driveway or parking lot, and dead animals of every kind and description.
(2)
Public or private property means the right-of-way of any road or highway; any body of water or watercourse or the shores or banks thereof; any park, playground, building, refuge or conservation or recreation area; all commercial and residential properties, timberlands, and woods; and all litter receptacles, containers, and dumpsters.
(c)
Prohibited acts. It shall be unlawful for any person to dump, deposit, throw or leave or cause or permit the dumping, depositing, placing, throwing, or leaving of litter on any public or private property in the city or any water or watercourse in the city unless;
(1)
The property is designated by the city, the state, or any political subdivision of the state for the disposal of litter and the person is authorized by the proper public authority to use such property, and the litter is placed into a litter receptacle or container installed on such property; or
(2)
The person is the owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property or has first obtained consent of the owner or tenant in lawful possession or unless the act is done under the personal direction of the owner or tenant, all in a manner consistent with the public welfare, and the litter is placed into a litter receptacle or container installed on such property.
(d)
Penalties. Any person who violates subsection (c) of this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction thereof in the municipal court, shall be punished as follows:
(1)
Such person may be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00, by imprisonment not to exceed six months, or both;
(2)
In the sound discretion of the judge of the municipal court, the person may be directed to pick up and remove from any public street, highway or right-of-way for a distance not to exceed one mile any litter the person has deposited and any and all litter deposited thereon by anyone else prior to the date of execution of sentence; or
(3)
In the sound discretion of the judge of the municipal court, the person may be directed to pick up and remove from any public park, private right-of-way, or, with the prior permission of the legal owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property, private property upon which it has been established by competent evidence that the person has deposited litter, any and all litter deposited thereon by anyone prior to the date of execution of sentence.
(e)
Presumptions regarding responsibility for violation.
(1)
Whenever litter is thrown, deposited, dropped or dumped from any motor vehicle or other conveyance in violation of subsection (c) of this section, it shall be prima facie evidence that the operator of the conveyance has violated this section.
(2)
Whenever any litter which is dumped, deposited, thrown or left on public or private property in violation of subsection (c) of this section is discovered to contain any articles which display the name of a person thereon in such a manner as to indicate that the article belongs or belonged to such person, it shall be a rebuttable presumption that such person has violated this article.
(Ord. No. 632, 2-9-1998)
Cross reference— Solid waste, ch. 78.
State Law reference— Litter control, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-48.

Sec. 62-41. - Generally.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person, after having been directed by an officer or member of the police department to move away therefrom, to remain or loiter in front of any church or other place of worship during services therein, or in front of any theater, coffeehouse, restaurant, store or other public place, or to loiter or idle away time on the sidewalks or public streets or roads of the city.
(b)
A person commits the offense of loitering when the person is in a place at a time or in a manner not usual for law-abiding individuals under circumstances that warrant a justifiable and reasonable alarm or immediate concern for the safety of persons or property in the vicinity.
(c)
Among the circumstances which may be considered in determining whether alarm is warranted is the fact that the person takes flight upon the appearance of a law enforcement officer, refuses to identify himself, or manifestly endeavors to conceal himself or any object. Unless flight by the person or other circumstances make it impracticable, a law enforcement officer shall, prior to any arrest for an offense under this section, afford the person an opportunity to dispel any alarm or immediate concern which would otherwise be warranted by requesting the person to identify himself and explain his presence and conduct. No person shall be convicted of any offense under this section if the law enforcement officer failed to comply with the foregoing procedure or if it appears at trial that the explanation given by the person was true and would have dispelled the alarm or immediate concern.
(Ord. No. 729, § 13-9, 5-14-2001)
State Law reference— Loitering prohibited, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-36.
Sec. 62-42. - Allowing or encouraging violation by minor.
It shall be unlawful for any parent, guardian or other person having the custody or control of any minor to permit, allow or encourage such minor to violate section 62-41.
(Ord. No. 729, § 13-9.1, 5-14-2001)
Sec. 62-43. - Responsibility of proprietors and managers.
It shall be unlawful for the proprietor, manager or other person having charge or control of any public or other place to permit, allow or encourage any person to violate section 62-41.
(Ord. No. 729, § 13-9.2, 5-14-2001)
Sec. 62-44. - Loitering for purpose of procuring others to engage in sexual acts for hire.
It shall be unlawful for any person to loiter in public for the purpose of soliciting or procuring others to engage in any sexual acts for hire.
(Ord. No. 729, § 13-9.3, 5-14-2001)
Sec. 62-45. - Loitering for purpose of engaging in drug-related activity.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to loiter in or near any thoroughfare or place open to the public, or near any public or private place, in a manner and under circumstances manifesting the purpose to engage in drug-related activity contrary to any of the provisions of O.C.G.A. Title 16, Ch. 13 (O.C.G.A. § 16-13-20 et seq.).
(b)
Among the circumstances which may be considered in determining whether such purpose is manifested are:
(1)
Such person is a known unlawful drug user, possessor, or seller. For purposes of this section, a known unlawful drug user, possessor, or seller is a person who has, within the knowledge of the arresting officer, been convicted in any court within this state of any violation involving the use, possession or sale of any controlled substance as defined in O.C.G.A. Title 16, Ch. 13 (O.C.G.A. § 16-13-20 et seq.), or such person has been convicted of any violation of any of the provisions of O.C.G.A. Title 16, Ch. 13 (O.C.G.A. § 16-13-20 et seq.), or substantially similar laws of any political subdivision of this state or of any other state; or a person displays physical characteristics of drug intoxication or usage, such as needle tracks, burned or calloused thumb and index fingers, an underweight condition, or nervous and excited behavior.
(2)
Such person is currently subject to a court order prohibiting his presence in a high drug activity geographic area.
(3)
Such person behaves in such a manner as to raise a reasonable suspicion that he is about to engage in or is then engaged in an unlawful activity, including, by way of example only, such person acting as lookout or hailing or stopping cars.
(4)
Such person is physically identified by the officer as a member of a gang or association which has been known to be engaged in illegal drug activity.
(5)
Such person transfers small objects or packages in a furtive fashion.
(6)
Such person takes flight or manifestly endeavors to conceal himself upon the appearance of a police officer.
(7)
Such person manifestly endeavors to conceal any object which reasonably could be involved in an unlawful drug-related activity.
(8)
Such person possesses any instrument, article, or thing whose customary or primary purpose is for the sale, administration or use of controlled substances, such as, but not limited to, crack pipes, push wires, chore boys, hand scales, hypodermic needles, razor blades, or other cutting tools.
(9)
The area involved is by public repute known to be an area of unlawful drug use and trafficking.
(10)
Any vehicle involved is registered to a known unlawful drug user, possessor, or seller, or a person for whom there is an outstanding warrant for a crime involving drug-related activity.
(Ord. No. 729, § 13-10, 5-14-2001)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-36.
