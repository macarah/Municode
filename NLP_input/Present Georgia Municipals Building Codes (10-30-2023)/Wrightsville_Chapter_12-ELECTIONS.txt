Chapter 12 - ELECTIONS[1]

State Law reference— Georgia Municipal Election Code, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.

ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 12-1. - Adoption of state rules and regulations.
The rules and regulations promulgated by the state election board which pertain to municipal elections, together with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1, are hereby adopted as the rules, regulations, and provisions that shall govern the conduct of municipal general and special elections in the city.
(Code 2007, § 2-101)
Sec. 12-2. - Expenses.
Such funds as are necessary for the conduct of elections and for the performance of the duties that are specified by this article shall be budgeted and appropriated annually, and from time to time.
(Code 2007, § 2-102)
Sec. 12-3. - Delegation of authority.
Whenever a provision requires an officer of the city to do some act or perform some duty, it shall be construed to authorize any other officer or person employed by the city, and charged under the terms of employment, or otherwise authorized by the city council, to do the required act or perform the required duty.
Secs. 12-4—12-26. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - CANDIDATES

Sec. 12-27. - Notice of candidacy; filing.
Each candidate desiring to have his name placed on the ballot for an office to be filed in a municipal general or special election shall file personally, or by his agent, notice of his candidacy in the manner and accompanied by the documents and information required by O.C.G.A. § 21-2-153.1.
(Code 2007, § 2-301)
Sec. 12-28. - Qualification fees.
The qualification fee for the candidates filing a notice of candidacy in any general or specific election shall be established by the city in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 21-2-131, and shall be paid to the municipal superintendent at the time of filing such notice of candidacy.
(Code 2007, § 2-302)
Sec. 12-29. - Campaign financing disclosure.
All candidates for municipal public office shall keep all such records and file all such reports, and are otherwise bound by the Ethics in Government Act, O.C.G.A. § 21-5-1 et seq.
(Code 2007, § 2-303)
Secs. 12-30—12-46. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - VOTING

Sec. 12-47. - Election districts.
The area comprising the corporate limits of the city, as the same is now or shall hereafter exist, shall constitute the sole election district of the city.
(Code 2007, § 2-402)
Sec. 12-48. - Polling places.
The polling place within the city shall be the city hall located at 190 East Elm Street.
(Code 2007, § 2-403)
Sec. 12-49. - Date of election.
All municipal elections shall be held on the first Tuesday in November in each election year.
(Code 2007, § 2-404)
Sec. 12-50. - Conduct of elections.
(a)
The county board of elections is authorized to conduct such elections pursuant to the Georgia Election Code, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq., and shall perform all duties of municipal election superintendent as set forth in that code.
(b)
The chief registrar of the county is appointed as absentee ballot clerk for the city and shall perform the duties set forth in the Georgia Election Code, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.
(c)
The use of electronic voting equipment, as provided to the county board of elections by the secretary of state, shall be used for casting and counting votes in all future municipal elections. If the board of elections, for the reasons set out in O.C.G.A. § 21-2-334, finds the use of electronic voting equipment to be impossible or impractical for any municipal election or special municipal election, the casting and counting of votes shall be conducted by paper ballot in the manner provided in the Georgia Election Code.
