ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 50-1. - Zoning provisions not affected by Code.
Nothing in this Code or the ordinance adopting this Code shall affect any ordinance or resolution enacting or amending the zoning ordinance of the county. All such ordinances or resolutions are hereby recognized as continuing in full force and effect to the same extent as if set out at length in this Code.
(Code 1981, § 19-1)
Cross reference— Zoning, App. A.
Sec. 50-2. - Miscellaneous ordinances not affected by Code.
Nothing in this Code or the ordinance adopting this Code shall affect any ordinance or resolution relating to the county's consolidated comprehensive management plan or the cooperative joint planning agreement. All such ordinances or resolutions are hereby recognized as continuing in full force and effect to the same extent as if set out at length in this Code.

ARTICLE II. - MOULTRIE-COLQUITT COUNTY PLANNING COMMISSION[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— City Business Improvement District Act, O.C.G.A. § 36-43-1 et seq.; Redevelopment Powers Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-44-1 et seq.; automatic membership of counties and municipalities in regional development centers, O.C.G.A. § 50-8-33.

Sec. 50-31. - Created.
There is hereby created a planning commission to be known as the Moultrie-Colquitt County Planning Commission.
(Code 1981, § 19-16)
Sec. 50-32. - Membership.
(a)
The Moultrie-Colquitt County Planning Commission shall consist of 12 members. Six of the members shall be residents of the City of Moultrie appointed by the mayor and council of the city, and six of the members shall be residents of the county living outside the corporate limits of the City of Moultrie appointed by the board of commissioners. The terms of the members shall be for three years, except in the appointment of the first Moultrie-Colquitt Planning Commission under the terms of this article, two of the members appointed by both governing bodies shall initially serve for three years, two for two years and two for one year, all thereafter serving three-year terms.
(b)
Any vacancy in membership shall be filled for the unexpired term by the body originally filling the position in which the vacancy occurs. The appointing body shall also have the authority to remove any member appointed by it for cause, on written charges, after a public hearing.
(c)
All members shall serve without compensation but may be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in connection with their official duties.
(Code 1981, § 19-17)
Sec. 50-33. - Organization, rules, staff and finances.
The Moultrie-Colquitt County Planning Commission shall elect its chairman from among its members. The term of the chairman shall be for one year with eligibility for re-election. The commission shall in like manner elect a vice-chairman, except that if the chairman be elected from members appointed by one governing body, the vice-chairman shall be elected from members appointed by the other governing body. The term of the vice-chairman shall be for one year with eligibility for re-election. The commission shall appoint a secretary who may be an officer or an employee of the municipality or the county. The planning commission shall make its own rules of procedure and determine its time of meeting. All meetings of the planning commission in which official action is taken shall be open to the public and all records of the planning commission shall be a public record. The planning commission may appoint such employees and staff as it may deem necessary for its work and may contract with the state planning agency and with city planners and other consultants for such services as it may require. The expenditures of the planning commission, exclusive of gifts, shall be within the amounts appropriated for the purpose by the governing authorities of the cities and the county.
(Code 1981, § 19-18)
Sec. 50-34. - Powers and duties.
The planning commission created in this article shall have all the powers, duties, and responsibilities set forth in state law.
(Code 1981, § 19-19)
Sec. 50-35. - Right to abolish.
Notwithstanding anything to the contrary contained in this article, the planning commission may be abolished at any time by a majority vote.
(Code 1981, § 19-20)
Secs. 50-36—50-65. - Reserved.

DIVISION 2. - BYLAWS

Sec. 50-91. - Name, headquarters, purpose.
(a)
This organization shall be known as the Southwest Georgia Regional Development Center, hereinafter referred to as the "center."
(b)
The center headquarters shall be located at a site within the area to be selected by the center.
(c)
This public agency is formed for the purpose of conducting studies of area resources and services, using findings in such studies to prepare local and area plans for the area and such local jurisdictions as may request them, to cooperate with local, state and federal agencies, institutions, and organizations in the conduct of studies by the center or such other agencies and organizations, to provide technical assistance to its member governments and local and area development organization's and administer such programs as the local governments may from time to time request or approve, and, whether enumerated here or not, to undertake, as its budget and personnel capabilities allow, any and all activities provided for in O.C.G.A. § 50-8-35.
(1)
It shall also be the purpose of this organization to provide a forum and mechanism for the joint discussion of multijurisdictional problems and opportunities and to assist in affecting joint solutions and programs for the common good.
(2)
In no case is the purpose of this organization to be interpreted as including the delivery of direct services to individuals.
Sec. 50-92. - Membership.
The members of this center shall be those counties encompassed in boundaries specified by the department of community affairs and approved by the Georgia Legislature.
Sec. 50-93. - Organization government.
(a)
The center shall be governed by a board composed of certain elected officials and certain nonpublic representatives from each governmental unit within the boundaries as may be established.
(b)
Membership on the board shall be determined as follows:
(1)
One elected or appointed municipal government official from each member county, selected by the municipal governments, if more than one, acting in concert;
(2)
One elected or appointed county government official from each member county; and
(3)
One nonpublic individual from each member county, selected by the county and municipal government of the member county acting in concert.
(c)
Additional board members may be appointed as follows:
(1)
Member counties with population of 50,000 residents or more, according to the latest census, would be entitled, but not required, to appoint two additional board members. These additional members may be either nonpublic individuals or they may be elected or appointed government officials.
(2)
Member counties with a population exceeding 25,000 residents, with fewer than 49,999 residents, according to the latest census would be entitled, but not required, to appoint one additional board member. This additional member may be either a nonpublic individual or an elected or appointed government official.
In selecting the additional board members as provided in subsections (1) and (2) of this subsection, the county government and the municipal governments of the affected member counties should concur on the appointments allocated.
(d)
The term of a member shall be for a period of one year and until the member's successor is elected and qualified.
Sec. 50-94. - Officers.
(a)
The board shall elect a chairperson, vice-chairperson, and a treasurer from among the members of the board. At the November meeting of the board, the chairperson shall name a nominating committee comprised of not fewer than three members of the board, which committee shall present a slate of officers at the following January meeting for board consideration.
(b)
The executive director of the center will serve as secretary of the board.
(c)
The chairperson shall preside at all board and executive committee meetings and shall serve as the chief spokesperson for the center. The chairperson shall serve as an ex officio member of all appointed committees.
(d)
The vice chairperson shall fill the role of the chairperson in the absence or incapacity of the chairperson.
(e)
The treasurer shall see that all funds due the center are collected, all expenditures are within an approved budget, a record of accounts kept in a timely manner and an annual audit is performed and periodic reports made to the board and executive committee.
(f)
The secretary shall keep the official records of the center, including the minutes of all meetings. The duties of recording minutes of the executive committee and of the full board may be delegated by the secretary to an employee of the center.
Sec. 50-95. - Executive committee.
(a)
The executive committee shall be elected annually at the January meeting of the full board, and shall have all the powers and authority of all the duties, responsibilities, and functions of the full board and which may take any action which the full board can take, except that the executive committee shall not employ an executive director, adopt the annual budget and work program for the regional development center, or elect members of the executive committee. Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the executive committee shall develop a proposed annual budget and work program which shall be submitted to the board for adoption.
(b)
The executive committee shall be comprised of the officers of the board, plus four other board members. The composition of the executive committee shall be such that there shall be at least one elected or appointed municipal government official, at least one elected or appointed county government official and at least one nonpublic member. The four at-large members of the executive committee shall be selected at the January board meeting upon nominations from the officer nominating committee in the same manner as provided in section 50-94(a).
Sec. 50-96. - Meetings, quorum, voting.
(a)
Regular board meetings shall be held monthly at a time and place determined by the board.
(b)
The executive committee shall meet monthly at a time and place determined by the executive committee.
(c)
Special or called meetings of the board may be held at the call of the chairman or on petition of the board members representing ten percent of the votes with each board member having one vote.
(d)
A seven-day written notice of special or called meetings shall be required except that a meeting called by the chairperson may proceed if, prior to transacting business, a majority of the representatives present or the majority of a quorum, whichever is greater, sign a waiver of notice.
(e)
A quorum of the board shall be defined as members present after due notice. A seven-day written notice shall be required for a special or called meeting or any change in a regular meeting, however once the board establishes a regular meeting day and time, each member is charged with the knowledge thereof and will be deemed to have received "due notice." One-half of the members of the executive committee will constitute a quorum at executive committee meetings.
(f)
Meetings shall be scheduled and held in conformity with the Georgia Open Meetings and Records Act.
(g)
The June meeting of the full board shall be designated as the annual meeting.
Sec. 50-97. - Vacancies.
(a)
Vacancies caused by disability, death, resignation or change in the qualifying status of the person holding a board seat shall be filled by the same method used in making a regular appointment.
(b)
Vacancies on the executive committee shall be filled by the same method as for the regular election.
Sec. 50-98. - Bonding.
Officers of the center board shall furnish bond with the premium paid by the center.
Sec. 50-99. - Authorities and responsibilities.
(a)
It shall be the authority and responsibility of the board to cause the timely appointment of representatives to the board, employ an executive director, develop and adopt an annual work program, adopt an annual budget, and elect an executive committee and provide oversight of the center operation. The board shall provide for the review of local plans, the adoption of regional plans and the operation of a conflict resolution process.
(b)
The executive committee shall prepare and recommend to the board an annual budget and shall manage the operation of the center. It shall prepare such reports as deemed necessary or requested by the board. Unless specifically delegated to it by the board the executive committee may not adopt regional plans, studies, or policies affecting regional development.
(c)
The chairperson may, on his authority or at the instigation of either the board or executive committee, appoint from time to time committees of the board or the executive committee to study and make recommendations to the appropriate body. No committee will have the authority to act unless such a charge is specifically made by the body from which the committee is drawn.
(d)
The board shall cause an annual audit to be performed by a certified public accountant.
Sec. 50-100. - Amendments.
These bylaws may be altered, amended, or repealed and new bylaws may be adopted by a vote of a majority of the voting strength of the center at any meeting of the board provided each member is given at least ten days written notice of proposed changes.
