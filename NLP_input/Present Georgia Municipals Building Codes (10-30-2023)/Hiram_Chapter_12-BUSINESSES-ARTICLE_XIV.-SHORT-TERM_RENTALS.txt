ARTICLE XIV. - SHORT-TERM RENTALS

Sec. 12-600. - Definitions.
Short-term rental or STR. A short-term rental means a single-family residential dwelling that is rented for less than 30 days at a time, excluding government-owned dwellings and , facilities licensed by the state as health care facilities. The term includes within its scope, but without limitation, those single-family residential dwellings managed by marketplace innkeepers such as Airbnb and VRBO. Excluded from the scope of the term are rentals for non-residential commercial purposes.
(Ord. No. 2022-07, § 1(Exh. A), 10-4-2022)
Sec. 12-601. - Short-term rental permit required.
No person shall rent, lease, or otherwise exchange for compensation all of any portion of a single-family residential dwelling as a short-term rental without first obtaining a permit from the city. No permit may be transferred, assigned, or used by any person other than the person to whom it was issued, or at any location other than the one for which it was issued. Short-term rentals for residential purposes shall not be permitted in any structure that is not designed as a single-family residential dwelling.
(Ord. No. 2022-07, § 1(Exh. A), 10-4-2022)
Sec. 12-602. - Short-term rental regulations.
(a)
An STR placard provided by the city must be prominently displayed on the front facade of the structure in a location clearly visible from the street during all periods of occupancy.
(b)
Short-term rentals are not permitted in an accessory structure (e.g. shed, garage, etc.), a recreational vehicle located on the lot of a single-family residential structure, or any other non-single-family residential dwelling.
(c)
Use of the short-term rental for any commercial event or any private event which exceeds 12 persons in attendance on the premises, including renters, is prohibited.
(d)
Short-term rentals shall not adversely affect the residential character of the neighborhood, nor shall the use generate noise, vibration, glare, odors, or other effects that unreasonably interfere with any person's enjoyment of his or her residence.
(e)
Short-term rentals shall meet all applicable zoning, building and fire codes.
(f)
Occupancy is limited to two guests per bedroom or a total of ten guests, whichever is less.
(g)
A short-term rental may only be used for a maximum of 30 consecutive rental nights and a total maximum of 90 rental nights per license year.
(h)
The rental agent for the short-term rental must be available by telephone 24 hours per day, 365 days per year.
(i)
Upon request by telephone, text, or email from a renter or the city, either the owner or the rental agent must be physically present on the premises of the short-term rental within three hours of the request.
(j)
The owner of the property must keep a current guest register including names, addresses, telephone numbers, and dates of occupancy of all guests. The register shall be provided to the city within 24 hours of being requested.
(Ord. No. 2022-07, § 1(Exh. A), 10-4-2022)
Sec. 12-603. - Short-term rental permit application.
(a)
Applicants for a short-term rental permit shall annually submit an application to the city annually. Such application shall include:
(1)
The name, address, telephone number, and email address of the owner(s) of record of the single-family residential dwelling unit for which the permit is sought;
(2)
The address of the single-family residential dwelling unit to be used as a short-term rental;
(3)
The name, address, telephone number, and email address of the short-term rental agent, which shall constitute his or her 24-hour contact information;
(4)
The owner's sworn acknowledgement that he or she has reviewed a copy of this article, has reviewed it, and understands its requirements;
(5)
The number and location of parking spaces allotted to the premises;
(6)
The owner's agreement to use his or her best efforts to assure that use of the premises by short-term rental occupants will not disrupt the neighborhood, and will not interfere with the rights of neighboring property owners to the quiet enjoyment of their properties;
(7)
The city shall have the authority to obtain additional information from the applicant as necessary to achieve the objectives of this section; and
(8)
The application fee as established by the city council. Once the application is submitted, this fee is non-refundable regardless of any subsequent determination that the application is incomplete.
(b)
Incomplete applications shall not be accepted for filing. Any application which has been reviewed by the city but which is subsequently determined to be incomplete shall be returned to the applicant and dismissed without prejudice.
(c)
If the rental agent changes, the property owner shall notify the city within five business days.
(d)
Any STR permit holder must also pay an occupation tax.
(Ord. No. 2022-07, § 1(Exh. A), 10-4-2022)
Sec. 12-604. - Permit denial, probation, suspension, or revocation.
(a)
Grounds for denial or sanction. An application for a short-term rental permit may be denied, or a permit place on probation, suspended, or revoked by the city manager, if there is any evidence of the following:
(1)
The applicant(s) is less than 21 years of age;
(2)
The applicant has failed to provide information required by this article for issuance of a permit or has falsely answered a question or request for information on the application form;
(3)
The applicant is not the owner of the premises;
(4)
Any applicable annual regulatory fee is not paid within ten days of approval;
(5)
The city has revoked a short-term rental permit at the premises, or otherwise held by the applicant, within the previous 12 months;
(6)
There is evidence of failure to comply with short-term rental requirements herein or the property or activities thereon have been declared by a court of law to be a nuisance; or
(7)
The single-family residential structure does not comply with all applicable building codes (including, but without limitation, the International Property Maintenance Code), or does not have a valid, current certificate of occupancy.
(b)
Hearings.
(1)
The city manager shall hear all matters relating to permits under this article, including, but not being limited to denials, probation, suspensions, revocations, and any other matters affecting such permits.
(2)
Applicants and permit holders shall be given written notice of the date, time, place, and purpose when the matter at issue will be heard. The applicant or permit holder shall be afforded the opportunity to be heard and present evidence. Ten calendar days' notice shall be required.
(3)
Upon close of the hearing, a decision shall be rendered in writing by the city manager and issued no later than within 48 hours of the meeting absent the occurrence of circumstances beyond the reasonable control of the city manager.
(4)
An applicant whose application has been denied or permit holder whose permit has been suspended, revoked, or placed on probation by the city manager may appeal to the governing authority by filing a written notice of appeal within five business days of the adverse decision or action. Such notice shall be provided to the city clerk. The appeal shall be placed on the agenda of the next governing authority meeting occurring at least ten days after the notice is received. The governing authority shall be provided a copy of all evidence heard by the city manager, along with a copy of the city manager's written decision. The applicant, licensee or permit holder may make a statement on their behalf before the governing authority, but no new evidence shall be taken. A vote on the appeal shall be taken either on the date of appeal hearing or at the next subsequent regular meeting of the governing authority. The decision of the governing authority shall be based on the evidence and standards of this chapter. If the governing authority determines the city manager acted in accordance with the standards of this chapter, it shall affirm his decision. If the governing authority determines the city manager acted contrary to the standards of this chapter, it shall overturn his decision and remand back to the city manager for a corrected decision.
(5)
An applicant or permit holder who is dissatisfied with the decision of the governing authority may appeal by filing for writ of certiorari with the superior court Paulding County, Georgia.
(Ord. No. 2022-07, § 1(Exh. A), 10-4-2022)
