ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 2-2-1. - Legal opinions of county attorney.
(a)
Legal opinions from the county attorney may be obtained:
(1)
By the board of commissioners, or a member thereof, by request made at a meeting of the board of commissioners;
(2)
By any county officer or employee, by filing a written request with the county administrator. The administrator shall forward the written request to the county attorney;
(3)
By the Lamar County Sheriff, The Lamar County Tax Commissioner, The Lamar County Probate Judge, and the Lamar County Clerk of Superior Court, at any time in the performance of his/her duties he/she determines that a legal opinion is necessary.
(b)
The county attorney is not obligated to render legal opinions except under the circumstances specified in subsection (a).
(Res. of 6-5-50; Res. of 6-3-97)
Sec. 2-2-2. - Records.
The clerk of the board of commissioners is responsible for records management activities of the county. No record shall be destroyed except in accordance with approved retention schedules.
(Res. of 10-4-83)
State Law reference— Georgia Records Act, O.C.G.A. § 50-18-90 et seq.; records management programs for local governments, O.C.G.A. § 50-18-99.
Sec. 2-2-3. - County car leaving county.
No person shall take the county car out of the county for more than one (1) day without the approval of the board of commissioners.
(Mo. of 11-16-84)
Sec. 2-2-4. - Rental of county machinery to private persons.
No person shall rent county machinery to private persons.
(Mo. of 6-6-58)
Sec. 2-2-5. - Courthouse office hours.
The office hours of the courthouse shall be from 8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m., Monday through Friday.
(Mo. of 6-22-84)
State Law reference— Courthouse to remain open during normal working hours, O.C.G.A. § 36-1-12.
Sec. 2-2-6. - Use of county property prohibited.
No county owned equipment, autos, trucks, instruments, tools, supplies, machines, computers, or any other item which is the property of the county shall be used by an employee or elected official while said employee or elected official is engaged in any outside employment or activity, either for compensation or otherwise, except upon prior approval of the board of commissioners. No employee or elected official shall allow any unauthorized person to rent, borrow or use any of the items mentioned above.
(Ord. No. 2018-08, § 1, 12-18-18)
Secs. 2-2-7—2-2-25. - Reserved.
