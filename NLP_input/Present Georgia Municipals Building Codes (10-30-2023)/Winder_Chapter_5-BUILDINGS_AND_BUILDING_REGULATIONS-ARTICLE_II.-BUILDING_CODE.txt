Sec. 5-19. - Supplementary to other laws.
In interpreting an applying the provisions of the building code adopted in this article, they shall be held to be the minimum requirements for the promotion of public health, safety an general welfare, and as supplementary to all other laws, codes or regulations of the state, county or city, formulated in the interest of the public health, safety or general welfare; and where the building code imposes a greater restriction than is imposed by other laws, codes or regulations, the building code shall govern, but where any other laws, ordinances, rules or regulations impose greater restrictions than are required by the building code, such other provisions shall govern.
(Code 1968, § 5-3)
Charter reference— Power of the city to protect the public health, safety and welfare, § 1-3(33), (37).

Sec. 5-22. - Fire district boundaries.
The boundaries of the fire district established in Section 301 of the building code adopted in this article are hereby described as follows:
The first fire district of the city shall embrace that part of the city within the following zone:
Beginning at the intersection of Park Avenue and Stephens Street and extending thence along Stephens Street to Center Street; thence along Center Street to May Street; thence along May Street to the point where the intersection would be if Park Avenue were extended on a projection from where Candler Street crosses said Park Avenue to said May Street.
All electrical wiring in this fire district shall be installed in conduit, steel tubing or flexible metallic cable.
The second fire district shall embrace all the territory within the corporate limits outside the boundaries of the first fire district.
No dwelling unit, private or rental, will be allowed in the first (downtown) fire district unless the entire building is provided with an automatic sprinkler.
This requirement shall not apply to a single story building containing a single dwelling unit provided that the single dwelling unit is separated from adjacent space in the building by minimum two-hour fire rated walls and one and one-half-hour fire rated doors and the entire building is equipped with a fire alarm system in compliance with NFPA 72 current edition as adopted by Georgia State Fire Marshal's office as Georgia Minimum Fire Code including, but not limited to the following:
(1)
Pull stations shall be located at all means of egress,
(2)
The alarm system shall be monitored by a UL listed monitoring company,
(3)
There shall be smoke and heat detection devices throughout the entire building, and
(4)
Audio/visual warning devices shall be placed throughout the building as needed to alert all occupants of any emergency or fire.
(Code 1968, § 5-5; Ord. (25-W-88) of 12-13-88; Ord. (10-W-96) of 9-3-96, §§ I, II; Ord. (W-8-00) of 8-8-00; Ord. (W-7-13) of 6-4-13)
Charter reference— Power of city to fix and establish fire limits, § 1-3(6).
Cross reference— Fire prevention and protection, ch. 9.

Sec. 5-24. - Penalties.
Section 106.3(a) of the building code adopted in this article is hereby amended to read as follows:
"A person who shall violate a provision of this code or fails to comply therewith or with any of the requirements thereof, upon conviction thereof in the recorder's court, shall be punished as provided in section 1-8 of the Code of Ordinances of the city. Where any offense continues from day to day, each day's continuance thereof shall be deemed a separate offense."
(Code 1968, § 5-4)
Sec. 5-25. - Flood hazards.
The building inspector shall review all building permit applications for new construction or substantial improvements to determine whether proposed building sites will be reasonably safe from flooding. If a proposed building site is in a location that has a flood hazard, a letter of map amendment will be required from FEMA before a permit will be issued.
(Res. of 8-3-76, § 1; Ord. ( W-1-20 ), § II, of 4-7-20)
Cross reference— Flood damage prevention ordinance, ch. 9.5.

Sec. 5-27. - Concrete slab construction.
The minimum thickness of concrete floor slabs supported directly on the ground shall not be less than four (4) inches unless designed by an architect or engineer. An approved vapor barrier shall be installed underneath the slab. Additionally, the building inspector shall require a four-inch layer of gravel for the safety and stability of the building.
(Ord. (12-W-90) of 12-4-90; Ord. (W-3-10) of 3-9-10; Ord. ( W-1-20 ), § IV, of 4-7-20)
Sec. 5-28. - Miscellaneous building regulations.
(a)
Limbs, debris and/or trash is not to be buried in the city limits of Winder.
(b)
Water meters must be paid for at the time a building permit is issued.
(c)
A certificate of occupancy will not be issued if water is standing under a house.
(d)
There shall be installed behind all exterior walls, hardboard material at least one-half (½) inch in thickness, which may consist of either plywood or OSB board.
(Ord. (W-2-93) of 2-2-93; Ord. (W-29-07) of 10-2-07; Ord. ( W-1-20 ), § V, of 4-7-20)
