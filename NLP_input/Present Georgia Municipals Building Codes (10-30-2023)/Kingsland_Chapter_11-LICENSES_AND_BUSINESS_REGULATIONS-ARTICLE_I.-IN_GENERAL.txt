Sec. 11-1. - Definitions.
For purposes of this article, the following words shall have the following meanings:
Chief of law enforcement refers to that chief officer of law enforcement, or his or her designee, as the case may be, who is charged with protecting and serving the public's safety in the jurisdiction, including the activities hereby covered.
Community planning and development director or office of the community planning and development director refers to that local governmental position whose official capacity oversees regulation and licensure of the activities hereby covered.
Registrant or registered solicitor means any person who is subject to this article who has obtained a valid certificate of registration from the office of the community planning and development director of the jurisdiction.
Residence means any living unit contained within any building or structure that is occupied by any person as a dwelling.
Soliciting means any of the following activities:
(1)
Seeking to obtain orders for the sale or exchange of goods, wares, merchandise or perishables of any kind, for any kind of remuneration or consideration, regardless of whether advance payment is sought;
(2)
Seeking to obtain subscriptions to publications, or publications;
(3)
Seeking to obtain donations of anything of value for the benefit of any association, organization, corporation or program;
(4)
Seeking to obtain orders or prospective customers for goods or services.
Solicitor means a person engaged in the activities defined by this article as soliciting.
(Ord. No. 2003-17, § II, 6-30-2003)
Sec. 11-2. - Registration requirements.
(a)
Registration. All natural persons desiring to engage in soliciting at the residences in the city or with the police jurisdiction thereof shall apply to the community planning and development director, at a cost of $150.00, for an annual certificate of registration. As an alternative, the natural persons desiring to engage in solicitations at residences in the city may apply for a 30 day temporary license at a cost of $50.00. The certificate, executed by the community planning and development director or his designee, shall be maintained by the community planning and development director, and a legible copy shall be provided to and carried by the registrant at all times while soliciting. Upon request for inspection by an occupant of a residence or a police officer while the registered solicitor is transacting his or her business or seeking to transact his or her business at any residence, the registrant shall show his or her registration certificate.
(b)
Application for certificate. The city shall provide a standard form for use for registration of solicitors. The applicant for the certificate shall state upon his or her oath or affirmation that the information is truthfully provided to the best of his or her knowledge and belief. The applicant shall supply upon the form the following information:
(1)
Applicant's true, correct, and legal name, including the name and address of any entity by which applicant may be employed in sales or for whose benefit application will be soliciting donations.
(2)
Address of applicant's permanent home and address of any temporary local residence.
(3)
The purpose for which soliciting will be done.
(4)
A brief description of the method of presentation that will be made (such as presentation of samples and taking of written orders).
(5)
The dates for when soliciting is planned.
(6)
Whether a permit, license, or certificate of registration in connection with soliciting has ever been revoked, explaining the circumstances.
(c)
Nonendorsement. In addition, the applicant shall certify on the application form that if his or her registration is granted, he or she will not state, imply, or represent to any person that the issuance of the certificate is an endorsement by the city nor any of its agencies or departments of the product, service or solicitor.
(d)
Proof of identification required. The application shall be accompanied by a copy of one of the following:
(1)
A valid drivers license of any state if applicant possesses such and intends to operate a motor vehicle in the jurisdiction, or
(2)
A valid United States Uniform Service Identification, or
(3)
A valid passport, or
(4)
A valid identification issued by any agency of a state for the purpose of identification of the holder.
(e)
Registration complete with issuance. Registration shall be complete when the community planning and development director issues the certificate of registration, which shall not be unreasonably withheld or delayed, said period not to exceed three days.
(f)
Form of certificate. Each certificate shall be numbered, shall list the name of the individual solicitor and of any organization on whose behalf he or she is authorized to solicit (including self if an independent contractor), the date on which the certificate expires, and shall be dated and signed by the community planning and development director or the community planning and development director's designee.
(g)
Maintenance of registry. The office of the community planning and development director shall maintain for public inspection a record of every application received and acted upon, together with any information pertaining thereto, all certificates of registration issued, and all denials of applications. Each application shall be numbered in consecutive order as filed, and each certificate issued shall be assigned a number exclusive to the registrant. Every certificate renewed shall be identified with the duplicate number of the application upon which it was initially issued. The community planning and development director shall furnish to the chief of law enforcement of the jurisdiction a listing of all certificates issued and renewed and the chief of law enforcement shall maintain such listing for public inspection and for identification.
(h)
Renewal. Annual certificates shall be valid for the calendar year in which they were issued and shall expire at midnight on December 31 of such year. Any certificate in good standing may be renewed upon request of the registrant and upon payment of $150.00. A temporary license may also be renewed every 30 days upon request of the registrant and upon payment of $50.00. The applicant shall be required to fill out a renewal application providing the information required in the initial registration.
(i)
Nontransferablility. Certificates of registration shall be issued only in the name of the applicant and the firm, corporation or association on whose behalf he or she is authorized to solicit (self if an independent contractor). Certificates shall be nontransferable from individual to individual, from individual to firm, from firm to firm, and from firm to individual. It shall be a violation for a solicitor whose certificate authorizes soliciting on behalf of a firm named in the certificate to solicit on behalf of any other firm, organization, or association or for any purpose other than that specified on the application.
(Ord. No. 2003-17, § III, 6-30-2003)
Sec. 11-3. - Exemptions from registration.
The registration provisions of this article do not apply to:
(1)
Persons making solicitations solely for a church, religious organization or charitable organization, including any unincorporated association or corporation under the supervision and control of any church, charitable organization or religious organization, if the church or organization is tax-exempt under the provisions of the United States Internal Revenue Code; provided, however, that any person who seeks to solicit a charitable contribution shall first file with the chief of law enforcement of the jurisdiction a notice describing the charitable purpose, signed by at least two officers or authorized representatives of the charitable organization.
(2)
Students soliciting contributions to finance extracurricular social, athletic, artistic, scientific or cultural programs, provided that the solicitation and its purpose have been approved by the principal, president or chief administrator of the students' school and said approval has been filed in writing with the chief of law enforcement of the jurisdiction.
(3)
Persons canvassing for the purpose of distributing political literature or materials on behalf of a political party, candidate or public issue or introducing themselves as supported of a candidate, political party, cause or issue provided, however, the persons so exempted from registration as described above will nonetheless remain subject to all other laws and ordinances of the jurisdiction including without limitation the rights of property owners and consumers.
(Ord. No. 2003-17, § IV, 6-30-2003)
Sec. 11-4. - Soliciting through placement of boxes.
It shall be a violation of this article for any person to solicit charitable contributions by placing a receptacle for donations in any public place without first filing a notice of intent with the chief of law enforcement of the jurisdiction. Each person soliciting must in all other respects comply with the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 2003-17, § V, 6-30-2003)
Sec 11-5. - Deceptive soliciting practices prohibited.
(a)
No solicitor or canvasser shall intentionally make any materially false or fraudulent statement in the course of soliciting.
(b)
No solicitor shall represent, directly or by implication, that the jurisdiction or any other governmental entity endorses the solicitation or that the granting of a certificate of registration implies any endorsement of the solicitor's product or service or of the individual solicitor.
(Ord. No. 2003-17, § VI, 6-30-2003)
Sec. 11-6. - Registration disqualifiers.
No certificate of registration shall be issued to any person who has been convicted of a felony within the ten years next preceding the date of application or has been convicted of any sex offense or who advocates or commits seditious action against the United States of America or any of its governmental subdivisions including this jurisdiction. All individuals soliciting within the city shall have a background check performed by the city police department for a fee of $15.00.
(Ord. No. 2003-17, § VII, 6-30-2003)
Sec. 11-7. - Revocation of certificate.
Any certificate of registration issued hereunder may be revoked by the chief of law enforcement if the registrant is convicted of a violation of this article, subject to any appeal of the conviction reversing any such conviction. Notice of revocation shall be immediately given to the registrant by personal service or by certified mail to the address listed on the application. Immediately upon the giving of such notice, the certificate of registration shall become null and void and shall remain so unless the revocation is ordered rescinded.
(Ord. No. 2003-17, § VIII, 6-30-2003)
Sec. 11-8. - Appeal from decisions of chief of law enforcement.
Any applicant whose registration is not timely issued by the community planning and development director or any registrant whose certificate is revoked by the chief of law enforcement shall have the right to appeal the revocation to the governing body of the jurisdiction for a hearing and reconsideration. Any appeal must be demanded in writing sent by certified mail to the chief executive officer of the jurisdiction, with a copy filed with the office of the community planning and development director of the jurisdiction within seven calendar days of the effective date of the decision giving rise to the appeal. The notice of appeal will describe in detail sufficient to provide the governing body with notice of the action complained of and the grounds of appeal. The right of appeal to the governing body is not in derogation of any other rights in law or equity the applicant might have.
(Ord. No. 2003-17, § IX, 6-30-2003)
Sec. 11-9. - Residential "No Soliciting" notice.
(a)
Any adult owner or tenant of a residence desiring to secure protection under this article shall give notice of his or her desire to refuse solicitors by displaying a clearly visible weatherproof placard no smaller than 32 square inches stating in bold black letters against a white background, "No Soliciting" which shall be posted on or near the main entrance door of the individual's dwelling.
(b)
The display of the placard shall be deemed to constitute notice to any solicitor that the inhabitant of the residence does not desire to invite solicitors. If a solicitor does attempt to contact the owner or tenant of a residence desiring secure protection under this article the registered solicitor's license shall be revoked and reapplication shall not be permitted for one year from the date of suspension.
(Ord. No. 2003-17, § X, 6-30-2003)
Sec. 11-10. - Time of day restrictions on soliciting.
It is a violation of this article for any person, whether registered or not, to solicit any person at his or her residence before 9:00 a.m. or after 5:00 p.m. local time, Monday thru Saturday, unless the solicitor has an appointment to do so.
(Ord. No. 2003-17, § XI, 6-30-2003)
Sec. 11-11. - Penalty for nonregistration or violation.
Any person who engages in soliciting, without complying with the registration requirements of this article, or who otherwise fails to comply with the provisions of this article, is chargeable with a violation and shall, on conviction, be fined not more than $250.00 per violation.
(Ord. No. 2003-17, § XII, 6-30-2003)
Sec. 11-12. - Termination for cessation of business.
Any business license holder who ceases operation for a period exceeding 90 days shall have that license terminated without prejudice. The city may waive termination if it has received written notice of cessation of operations for reasons such as renovation, rebuilding, or relocation; if cessation was due to official actions against the business for which the business was subsequently found innocent; or if cessation was due to circumstances beyond the control of the business.
(Ord. No. 2012-03, Art. 1, 6-11-2012)
