ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 16-1. - Outdoor burning.
(a)
Outdoor burning of household domestic garbage, refuse, rubbish, tires and trash is prohibited within the city limits.
(b)
Outdoor burning of building materials on construction sites is prohibited within the city limits.
(c)
For any fires found in violation of this section, the police chief, fire chief or building inspector shall direct the extinguishment of the fire.
(d)
The following activities are exempt from the prohibition contained in this section:
(1)
Carrying out recognized agricultural procedures necessary for production or harvesting of crops. Georgia Forestry Burn Permit required.
(2)
Fires for recreational purposes (e.g., campfires) or for cooking food for immediate human consumption.
(3)
Fires set for purposes of training firefighting personnel when authorized by the appropriate governmental authority.
(4)
Operation of devices using open flames, such as tar kettles, blow torches, welding torches, portable heaters and other flame-making equipment.
(5)
Outdoor burning, on the premises of origin, of yard waste, leaves, pine straw, pine cones, trees, logs, wood, brush, grass clippings, stumps, branches, and land clearing debris such as stumps, limbs, leaves. Georgia Forestry Burn Permit required.
(6)
Prescribed burning and slash burning. Georgia Forestry Burn Permit required.
(e)
Each day of a continuing violation shall be a separate and distinct violation and may be so punished.
(Code 2002, § 9-1-10; Ord. No. 03-009-2010, § 1(9-1-10), 9-14-2010; Ord. No. 2017-11-01 , § 2, 11-7-2017)
Sec. 16-2. - Interfering with equipment.
No person other than a duly enrolled member of the fire department shall ride upon the fire trucks of the city at any time, nor use, borrow or interfere with any fire department equipment, or attempt to use the equipment at the scene of a fire unless authorized to do so by the fire chief.
(Code 2002, § 3-2-2)
Sec. 16-3. - Enforcement.
Whenever the fire department is answering an alarm or operating at the scene of a fire or other emergency every active member of the department is hereby empowered and authorized to control and direct motor vehicle traffic, stop or move vehicles, and enforce all provisions of this chapter and any other code provisions the enforcement of which is deemed necessary to assist in the control of the fire or other emergency.
(Code 2002, § 3-2-3)
Sec. 16-4. - Tampering with alarm system.
It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to willfully, maliciously or mischievously interfere or tamper with any fire alarm box or any of the appliances or apparatus connected therewith, located within the corporate limits of the city.
(Code 2002, § 3-2-4)
Sec. 16-5. - Fire limits defined.
The fire limits of the city are hereby defined as all that area within the city limits, as those limits now exist, or as they may hereafter be amended.
(Code 2002, § 3-2-22)

ARTICLE III. - FIRE DEPARTMENT

Sec. 16-60. - Fire chief and members.
The fire department shall be headed by a fire chief and shall be composed of such other paid and volunteer members and employees as may be determined necessary by the mayor and council.
(Code 2002, § 3-2-1)
Sec. 16-61. - Volunteers covered by workers' compensation.
Volunteer firefighters who are active members of the fire department of the city are hereby extended coverage for workers' compensation insurance in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 34-9-1 et seq.
(Code 2002, § 3-2-5)
