DIVISION 2. - BUDGETS[6]

Footnotes:
--- (6) ---
State Law reference— Local government budgets and audits, O.C.G.A. § 36-81-1 et seq.; appointment of budget officer, O.C.G.A. § 36-81-4; preparation of proposed budget, O.C.G.A. § 36-81-5; adoption of budget ordinance, O.C.G.A. § 36-81-6.

Sec. 2-421. - Preparation.
(a)
The county administrator shall be responsible to see that the proposed budget for the upcoming fiscal year is submitted to the board of commissioners no later than May 15 of each year. The proposed budget shall be prepared in accordance with the county financial management plan and shall be accompanied by a budget message, which shall explain the proposed budget for the ensuing fiscal year; describe the important features of the proposed budget; indicate any major changes in revenues and expenditures from the current year; summarize the county's debt position; and include such other material as will provide a complete synopsis of the financial condition of the county. The proposed budget shall be prepared and presented in accordance with the requirements of O.C.G.A. § 36-81-5.
(b)
On the date that the proposed budget is submitted to the board of commissioners for consideration, a copy of the proposed budget shall be placed in a public location which is convenient to the residents of the county. Residents shall be provided convenient access to the proposed budget during the regular business hours of the county government so they may review it prior to its adoption. Copies of the proposed budget shall also be made available, upon request, to the news media.
(c)
A notice advising county residents of the availability of the proposed budget shall be published in a newspaper of general circulation in the county during the week in which the proposed budget is submitted to the board of commissioners. The notice must also advise the residents that a public hearing will be held at which any person wishing to be heard on the budget may appear. This notice must be a prominently displayed advertisement or news article and shall not be placed in the section of the newspaper where legal notices appear.
(d)
The county administrator shall arrange for a public hearing on the proposed budget to be held at least one week prior to the meeting of the board of commissioners at which adoption of the budget will be considered, at which hearing any person wishing to be heard on the budget may appear. Notice of the date, time and place of the public hearing on the budget shall be published in a newspaper of general circulation in the county at least one week prior to the date of such hearing. Such notice shall be a prominently displayed advertisement or news article and shall not be placed in the section of the newspaper where legal notices appear. This notice may be included in the notice published pursuant to subsection (c) of this section in lieu of a separate publication of such notice.
(Code 1979, § 2-2-39; Ord. No. 99-16, § 2-2-52, 12-16-1999)
Sec. 2-422. - Adoption.
No sooner than one week following the hearing required by section 2-421(d), the county budget shall be adopted by the board of commissioners. The budget adopted shall make appropriations in such sums as the board of commissioners shall deem sufficient, whether greater or less than the sums presented in the proposed budget. Such adoption must occur prior to the July 1 beginning of the fiscal year for which the budget is prepared. The budget shall be adopted at a public meeting which shall be advertised in accordance with the procedures set forth in section 2-421(d).
(Code 1979, § 2-2-40; Ord. No. 99-16, § 2-2-53, 12-16-1999)
Sec. 2-423. - Administration and reporting.
Subsequent to the adoption of each budget, the director of internal services, at the direction of the county administrator, shall prepare and submit to the board of commissioners monthly reports detailing the budgeted versus actual revenues and expenditures, and identifying any revised estimates of revenues or expenditures.
(Code 1979, § 2-2-41; Ord. No. 99-16, § 2-2-54, 12-16-1999; Ord. No. 17-08 , § 15(Exh. O), 2-6-2018)

DIVISION 3. - PURCHASING, SALE AND CONTRACTS[7]

Footnotes:
--- (7) ---
State Law reference— Purchases through state, O.C.G.A. § 50-5-100 et seq.

Sec. 2-446. - Purchasing generally.
Operating under the oversight of the county administrator and the supervision of the director of internal services, all purchases for the county shall be made in accordance with the provisions of the county financial management plan, any other requirements established by the board of commissioners and any requirements contained in the laws of the state. If there is any conflict in such requirements, the laws of the state shall control.
(Code 1979, § 2-2-42; Ord. No. 99-16, § 2-2-55, 12-16-1999; Ord. No. 02-8, § 5, 8-6-2002; Ord. No. 17-08 , § 15(Exh. O), 2-6-2018)
Sec. 2-447. - Contracts for public works.
All contracts to build or repair any public building, bridge, causeway, road or other public work shall be let and contracted for in the manner prescribed by O.C.G.A. tit. 36, ch. 10.
(Code 1979, § 2-2-43; Ord. No. 99-16, § 2-2-56, 12-16-1999)
Sec. 2-448. - Bidding procedures.
All purchases which are required to be made by competitive bid shall be made in accordance with formal competitive bidding procedures as set forth in the county financial management plan and the laws of the state. In the event of any conflict in the procedures in the county financial management plan and the laws of the state, the procedures set out in the laws of the state shall control.
(Code 1979, § 2-2-44; Ord. No. 99-16, § 2-2-57, 12-16-1999)
Sec. 2-449. - Sale of surplus personal property.
The director of internal services shall have the authority to sell personal property of the county which has become unsuitable for use, provided that surplus, unusable or obsolete supplies and equipment shall be sold only upon the adoption of a resolution by the board of commissioners specifying the manner in which such sale shall be conducted.
(Code 1979, § 2-2-45; Ord. No. 99-16, § 2-2-58, 12-16-1999; Ord. No. 17-08 , § 15(Exh. O), 2-6-2018)
Sec. 2-450. - Sale of small parcels of surplus real property.
The county shall have the authority to sell and convey parcels of small or narrow strips of land so shaped or so small as to be incapable of being used independently as zoned or under applicable subdivision or other development ordinances or land use plans, or as streets, whether owned in fee or used by easement, to abutting property owners, upon the following terms and conditions:
(1)
Such sale and conveyance facilitates the enjoyment of the highest and best use of the purchaser's abutting property;
(2)
The property has been declared by the board of commissioners to be surplus real property;
(3)
Each owner of property which abuts the surplus property has been notified, at least 14 calendar days prior to such conveyance, by certified mail, return receipt requested, that the surplus property is being offered for sale and is given the opportunity to purchase such property under the terms and conditions set forth in this section;
(4)
The sale has been authorized by resolution of the board of commissioners;
(5)
Title to the surplus real property shall be conveyed by quit claim deed or limited warrant deed as the board of commissioners shall determine;
(6)
The purchase price shall be payable in cash at closing, which closing shall occur within 30 days of the approval of the sale by the board of commissioners; and
(7)
The purchaser shall be responsible for payment of all costs associated with the purchase of the surplus property, including, but not limited to, any attorney's fees, surveyor's fees and recording fees in connection with such purchase.
(Code 1979, § 2-2-45.1; Ord. No. 99-16, § 2-2-59, 12-16-1999)
Sec. 2-451. - County contracts.
(a)
Execution. Unless otherwise specified by a majority of the entire board of commissioners, the chairman or, in his absence the vice-chairman, shall execute all contracts to be entered into by the county. The clerk or, in his absence any deputy clerk, may attest the signature of the chairman or vice-chairman on any such contract.
(b)
Authorization. All contracts entered into by the board of commissioners shall be authorized as required by Local Laws section 1-2-15 of this Code of Ordinances.
(Code 1979, § 2-2-46; Ord. No. 99-16, § 2-2-60, 12-16-1999)
Sec. 2-452. - Waiver of appraisal requirement for purchase or donation of low fair market value real property.
When seeking to obtain a fee simple interest in real property estimated to be valued at or below $75,000.00, the county shall have the authority to acquire, by sale or donation, such real property without first obtaining an appraisal of the same, upon following the below-described procedure.
(1)
County, or its designee, shall prepare a value estimate of the real property based on a review of available, relevant, data.
(2)
The value estimate of the real property shall be effective for 90 days.
(3)
In the event the value estimate of the real property is reasonably determined to be $75,000.00 or less, the county may take any and all necessary steps to acquire the same without first obtaining an appraisal.
(Ord. No. 08-06, § 1, 12-16-2008; Ord. No. 22-05 MEETING , § 1(Exh. A), 9-20-2022)
