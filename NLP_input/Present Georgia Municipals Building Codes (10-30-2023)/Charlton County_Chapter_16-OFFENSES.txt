Sec. 16-1. - Possession and use of alcohol.
(a)
No person shall consume or possess an opened container of distilled spirits, wine or malt beverages at or on any public place in the county.
(b)
No person shall be and appear in an intoxicated condition in any public place or within the curtilage of any private residence not his own other than by invitation of the owner or lawful occupant, which condition is made manifest by:
(1)
Boisterousness;
(2)
Indecent condition or act; or
(3)
Vulgar, profane, loud or unbecoming language.
(c)
The term "public place" includes any public park, public schoolyard, public building, public highway or road right-of-way, cemetery, any property which is owned or leased by the county or any privately owned business premises where the general public has regular access. The term "public place" does not include the area within a building which composes a business establishment properly licensed by the county to sell alcoholic beverages for on-premises consumption, nor does it include the area inside a motor vehicle traveling upon any public highway or road.
(d)
Violations of this section shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of sections 1-10 through 1-14.
(Code 1999, § 13-1; Ord. of 8-2-1990, § 1; Ord. of 7-5-1990, § 1; Ord. of 8-22-1989, §§ 1—4)

Sec. 16-2. - Tampering with public signs.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to alter, change, rearrange or damage in any way any public road sign or public directional sign erected by the county or its agents on any public right-of-way or any other location in the county.
(b)
It shall be the duty of any citizen of the county or any member of the public to:
(1)
Report to any city or county law enforcement officer the violation of this section in the manner described in this section by any member of the public;
(2)
Sign a written complaint against such offender;
(3)
Appear as a witness against such offender in a court of law; and
(4)
Make a citizen's arrest of any such offender in the manner authorized by the laws of the state.
(c)
Any person charged with and found guilty of a violation of this section shall be punished for a misdemeanor.
(Code 1999, § 13-2; Ord. of 8-1-1979, §§ 1—3)
Sec. 16-3. - Loud, offensive and disturbing noises.
(a)
Prohibited. No person shall make or continue, or cause to be made or continued, loud, offensive and disturbing noises within the unincorporated areas of the county.
(b)
Enumerated. Noises which shall be deemed to be loud, offensive and disturbing are as follows:
(1)
Radios, pianos, phonographs, etc. Any noise made within 1,000 feet of an occupied church, school, hospital, nursing home or residence which either annoys, injures or endangers the comfort, repose, health or safety of others, unless the making and continuing of such noise shall be absolutely necessary for the protection or preservation of property or the health, safety, life or limb of some person. It is not the intention of this section to prohibit or interfere with the use of any radio, piano, phonograph, Victrola or other similar instrument or device used in the usual and customary manner and where sufficiently muted so as to not unduly disturb others in the same neighborhood, but applying to the use of such instruments when so operated as to cause an unusually loud and offensive disturbance to others residing in the same neighborhood. Such a noise must first be reported to the county sheriff or his lawful deputy, and the maker of such noise informed by the sheriff or his deputy that such noise is annoying, injurious or endangering the comfort, repose, health or safety of a person who is within 1,000 feet of the point of creation of the noise, and after such notice the noise is continued, before such noise shall be deemed to be a loud, offensive and disturbing noise;
(2)
Unnecessary blowing of bells, horns, whistles, etc. Any unnecessary blowing of any bell, horn, whistle or like sound device on any vehicle within 1,000 feet of any occupied church, school, hospital, nursing home, residence or operating business when not being sounded for emergency use in keeping with proper traffic operating procedures; provided, however, this shall not be construed to prevent the use of such sounding devices during a parade or other such organized gala celebration which occurs between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 8:00 p.m.; or
(3)
Vehicles. Any vehicle that is left unattended with its engine running for more than five minutes and situated within 1,000 feet of any occupied church, school, hospital, nursing home or residence.
(c)
Penalty. Violations of this section shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of sections 1-10 through 1-14.
(Code 1999, § 13-3; Ord. of 3-2-1989, §§ 2, 3)
Sec. 16-4. - Disorderly conduct.
(a)
Prohibited. No person shall engage in or exhibit disorderly conduct within the unincorporated areas of the county.
(b)
Definition. The term "disorderly conduct" means conduct which shall be deemed to be offensive and adverse to public safety and which is exhibited or occurs when:
(1)
Any person acts in a violent or tumultuous manner towards another whereby any person fears for the safety of his life, limb or health;
(2)
Any person acts in a violent or tumultuous manner towards another whereby the property of any person is placed in danger of being destroyed or damaged;
(3)
Any person endangers the lawful pursuits of another by acts of violence, angry threats and abusive language;
(4)
Any person causes, provokes or engages in any fight, brawl or riotous act which endangers the life, limb, health or property of another;
(5)
Any person utters, while in a state of anger in a public place, any bawdy, lewd, obscene or fighting words or epithets directed toward another person to demean or provoke such person; or
(6)
Any person congregates with another in or on any public way so as to halt the flow of vehicular or pedestrian traffic and refuses to clear such public way when so ordered by a lawful authority.
(c)
Penalty. Violations of this section shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of sections 1-10 through 1-14.
(Code 1999, § 13-4; Ord. of 2-1-1990, §§ 2, 3)
Sec. 16-5. - Debris in ditches and canals.
No person or corporation shall place or permit anyone to place any debris of any kind, including, but not limited to, logs, limbs and/or general trash, in any drainage ditches or canals along county roads or located on lands of the county, or on lands for which the county has an easement.
(Code 1999, § 13-5; Ord. of 12-6-1984, § 1)
Sec. 16-6. - Interfering with automobile traffic on roads.
(a)
No person or corporation shall engage or permit anyone to engage in any activities on county roads which, while being carried on, interferes with or impedes the normal automobile traffic thereon.
(b)
Violations of this section shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of sections 1-10 through 1-14.
(Code 1999, § 13-6; Ord. of 12-6-1984, § 2)
Sec. 16-7. - Hunting on county-owned land.
(a)
Prohibited generally. There shall be no hunting permitted upon any property owned by this county.
(b)
Exceptions. There shall be no exceptions.
(c)
Violation of section. Any person hunting on lands of the county other than as permitted in this section shall be deemed a trespasser. Violations of this section shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of sections 1-10 through 1-14.
(Code 1999, § 13-7; Ord. of 11-1-1984, §§ 1, 2; Ord. of 11-2-1995, § 1; Ord. of 12-4-1997, § 2; Ord. No. 6 of 2016, 4-7-2016)
Sec. 16-8. - County boat ramps, docks or piers.
(a)
Swimming by any individual shall be prohibited on and within 30 feet of any and all boat ramps, docks or piers owned, maintained or operated by the county.
(b)
No adult shall knowingly allow any child in their care, custody, or control to swim on or within 30 feet of any and all boat ramps, docks or piers, owned, maintained or operated by the county.
(c)
Violation of this section may be prosecuted by summons in the magistrate court or state court.
(d)
Violation of this section may be punished according to the general penalties described in sections 1-14 and 1-15.
(Ord. of 11-2-2006; Ord. of 6-5-2007)
