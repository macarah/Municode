ARTICLE II. - DAMAGE TO COUNTY ROADS

Sec. 28-19. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Damage means any and all injury to the roadbed, road, surface, ditches, drainage system, or right-of-way of a road which is part of the county public road system. Acts of injury shall include, but not be limited to, the following:
(1)
Diversion of or the filling in of any drainage ditch, culvert or pipe parallel to or underneath a public road, so as to restrain or redirect the flow of water.
(2)
The operation of any vehicle, machinery, or equipment which will gouge, rip, break, depress or otherwise deface a public road, its adjoining ditches, or any part of the public right-of-way.
(3)
The continued ingress or egress, to and from a public road at a particular location which results in a build-up of rock, gravel, mud, dirt, debris or other materials, which present a potential hazard to other persons using the public road.
(4)
The creation or erection of any dam, berm, ditch or wall which restrains the natural flow of waters and results in the redirecting of the waters onto the road surface or right-of-way of any public road within the county.
(5)
Any act or acts which interferes with or impedes normal motor vehicle traffic or water flow or run off on or over a county road, ditch or right-of-way in this county, or adversely affects the safety of anyone or any other vehicle using the road, ditch or right-of-way.
Enforcement officer means the county sheriff, the county road superintendent, or their deputies or designees, and any other law enforcement officer in the county.
Public road means the entire width of any and all roads and roadway rights-of-way that are a part of the county public road system, as delineated in accordance with the road mapping system of the county, as amended from time to time. All drainage ditches within a county road right-of-way which adjoin or run parallel to, or beneath, a county road are included in the term "public road".
Repair means the repair of a public road to its condition prior to the occurrence of the damage caused.
(Ord. of 2-11-1999, § II)
Sec. 28-20. - Damaging public roads prohibited.
It is unlawful for any person to damage any public road, or to engage or permit any other person to damage a public road in the county.
(Ord. of 2-11-1999, § III)
Sec. 28-21. - Temporary ramps excluded.
(a)
The temporary placement or construction of a ramp across a ditch for purposes of loading and unloading machinery and equipment, or the removing of timber, crops or other materials or substances shall not constitute a violation of this article, providing that the temporary ramp is placed or constructed so as not to significantly restrain or divert the water flow from the roadbed or restrain or divert the water flow through the ditch on or over which the ramp is placed or constructed.
(b)
For the temporary ramp to be excluded as a violation of this article the temporary ramp must provide for continued normal water flow either by the installation of an adequate size pipe under the ramp or by some other means; furthermore, the person, company or other entity placing the temporary ramp over the ditch must restore the ditch and right-of-way to same or better condition as the ditch and right-of-way were prior to the placement of the temporary ramp.
(c)
For purposes of this section, the term "temporary" means a period of time not to exceed ten days, except for the harvesting and removal of timber, in which case the term "temporary" means the reasonable amount of time required to harvest and remove the timber.
(d)
If a temporary ramp is needed for a period of time in excess of that authorized in subsection (c) of this section, then the person desiring to extend the temporary period shall apply to the county administrator for an extension of time. Upon good cause shown, the county administrator, or in his absence, the county clerk or chairperson of the board of commissioners, may grant a reasonable extension of the period for a temporary ramp.
(Ord. of 2-11-1999, § VII(a))
Sec. 28-22. - Permanent entrance ways excluded.
(a)
Ramps and culverts constructed as permanent entrance ways are excluded from this article providing they are constructed in such a manner as to not restrain or divert the water flow through the right-of-way and ditch over which the ramp or culvert is constructed to any greater extent than the normal water flow was restricted or restrained prior to construction of the ramp or culvert.
(b)
Any person or entity desiring to construct a permanent entrance way may, but is not required to, consult with the county road superintendent or engineer, or the county administrator prior to constructing such a permanent entranceway to obtain verification that the proposed entrance way will not constitute a violation of this article.
(Ord. of 2-11-1999, § VII(b))
Sec. 28-23. - Enforcement; violations and penalties.
(a)
Enforcement officers. This article shall be enforced by the sheriff or any deputy or other law enforcement officer, or any other designated county enforcement officer.
(b)
Citations. Citations for violations may be issued by any enforcement officer requiring an individual defendant to appear before the magistrate's court of the county at the court's next regularly set court date or such other date as set by the judge of the court. Citations shall be personally served upon the defendant or his authorized agent in accordance with state law. Such citation shall state the time and place at which the accused is to appear for trial, shall identify the offense with which the accused is charged, shall have an identifying number by which it shall be filed with the court, and shall be signed by the officer who completes and serves it.
(c)
Continuing violations; separate offenses. Each day of a continuing violation may be treated as a separate offense, and a citation may be issued each day a violation exists. Each violation shall constitute a separate offense which may be punished separately.
(d)
Prosecuting attorney options; accusations. The offender may be tried with or without a prosecuting attorney as well as upon accusations.
(e)
Penalties. A first conviction of this article within a five-year period shall be punishable by a fine of not less than $100.00, and not more than $500.00, or imprisonment of up to 60 days, or both. Upon a second or subsequent conviction of the offense within a five-year period, such conviction shall be punishable by a fine of not less than $500.00, and not more than $1,000.00, or imprisonment of up to 60 days, or both. Additionally the magistrate may require restitution to repair the damage to the public road or require the defendant to complete community service work to repair the damage to the public road.
(f)
Court costs. In addition, upon conviction, the offender shall be required to pay all court costs as determined by the magistrate.
(g)
Arrest before trial; bond. No person accused of violating this article shall be arrested prior to trial, but any defendant who fails to appear for trial shall be arrested thereafter on the warrant of the magistrate, and required to post a bond for his future appearance.
(h)
Jury trial. There shall be no jury trials in the magistrate court.
(i)
Remedies not exclusive; civil remedies. Nothing in this article shall prevent the county board of commissioners from bringing any civil action to prevent, correct or abate any violation of this article. No penalty or remedy prescribed herein shall be considered exclusive of any other remedy, but shall be available in addition thereto.
(Ord. of 2-11-1999, §§ IV, VI)

ARTICLE III. - STREET NUMBERS

Sec. 28-50. - Display of street numbers required.
Every house, mobile home, apartment, business, or other building having a street number assigned to it under the system of numbering in unincorporated the county shall display such number in a readily visible manner and in compliance with the requirements of this article. Mobile home parks shall also be required to have each individual pad or lot number displayed in a readily visible manner and in compliance with the requirements of this article.
(Ord. of 8-12-1999, § 1)
Sec. 28-51. - Method of display.
(a)
Assigned street numbers may be affixed to the house, apartment, business or other building itself, provided that such house, apartment, business or other building is not more than 50 feet from the curbline, pavement edge, or edge of travel way of the street in front of the house, apartment, business, or other building, and the number is readily visible from the street by persons traveling along the street in each direction. Street numbers may also be placed on mailboxes or signs located on the premises in front of the house, apartment, business or other building provided such signs are of a durable type and are located not more than 50 feet from the curbline, pavement edge, or edge of travel way of the street in front of the property, and provided that the numbers on such sign shall be readily visible from the street by persons traveling along the street in each direction.
(b)
If the house, apartment, business or other building to be numbered is more than 50 feet from the curb, pavement edge, or edge of travel way of the street in front of the property, or if for any reason a street number affixed to the building would not be readily visible from the street by persons traveling along the street in each direction, the street number shall not be displayed on the building, but must be displayed on a sign or mailbox complying with the requirements of this article.
(Ord. of 8-12-1999, § 2)
Sec. 28-52. - Size and design of numbers.
Street numbers affixed to buildings or signs shall be a minimum of three inches in height. Those affixed to mailboxes shall be a minimum of two inches in height. All such numbers shall be of a color contrasting to the background so as to be clearly visible. Street numbers and signs or mailboxes to which they are affixed shall be maintained at all times in good condition.
(Ord. of 8-12-1999, § 3)
Sec. 28-53. - Duty of owner; violations.
(a)
The obligation of complying with the provisions of this article shall be upon the owner of the property. The property owner shall have 30 days from the date of notification of his number to comply with the provisions herein.
(b)
If any of the provisions of this article shall be found to have been violated, notice shall be given to the owner of the property, or his agent, specifying the manner of the violation, and giving the owner ten days to bring the property into compliance. The notice shall be given by the E-911 office.
(c)
Violation of this article shall be punishable as a misdemeanor pursuant to state statute governing the violation of county ordinances. The maximum penalty for violation of this article shall be a fine not to exceed $100.00. Each day a violation of this article continues after the expiration of the ten-day period shall constitute a separate offense, and shall be punishable as provided by law.
(Ord. of 8-12-1999, § 4)
Secs. 28-54—28-79. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - CONNECTING TO PUBLIC ROADS

Sec. 28-80. - Applicability.
This article shall apply to the construction of both temporary and permanent non-public ramps, drives and roadways if they are to be joined or connected with a public road. This article does not apply to the construction or improvement of non-public roadways and drives that are not to become, to be joined to or connected with a public road or street.
(Ord. of 6-12-1984, §§ 13, 14)
Sec. 28-81. - Penalties for violation.
Violators of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and upon conviction of any such violation shall be punished by a fine of not more than $200.00 or 90 days imprisonment for such violation.
(Ord. of 6-12-1984, § 12)
Sec. 28-82. - Written permission required to connect ramp, drive or roadway to public road.
It is unlawful for any person, corporation or other entity to join or connect any non-public ramp, roadway or drive to or with a public road or street in this county without first complying with the foregoing provisions of this article. Any person, corporation, or other entity desiring to build a private roadway, ramp or drive which is to join or connect with a public road in the county shall first obtain written permission from this board prior to joining or connecting the non-public roadway or drive to a public road.
(Ord. of 6-12-1984, §§ 3, 11)
Sec. 28-83. - Application.
Any person, corporation or entity desiring to join or connect a non-public roadway, ramp or drive to or with a public road in the county shall first apply in writing to this board on a form, as prescribed from time to time by this board, setting forth the following information and any other information that may be required by this board from time to time:
(1)
Name, street, mailing address and home and business telephone numbers;
(2)
Name or location of public road or street with which applicant desires to join or connect;
(3)
Description of non-public roadway, ramp or drive applicant desires to build, including construction materials, length, width and drainage measures;
(4)
The type, size and total length of pipe to be used in the ramp.
(Ord. of 6-12-1984, § 4)
Sec. 28-84. - Construction standards and specifications.
Each non-public roadway, drive and ramp in this county must be constructed in a workmanship-like manner, taking into consideration the terrain, soil type and anticipated usage. In addition, each non-public roadway, drive and ramp must meet as a minimum the following specifications and requirements:
(1)
All road and street rights-of-way conveyed to the county must have an overall minimum width of 80 feet, with a graded roadbed of not less than 30 feet in the center. In addition, the outside edges of the graded road must be graded, ditched and sloped appropriately to accommodate all normal water runoff from the graded roadbed.
(2)
All ramps constructed to join or connect a non-public driveway with a public or non-public roadway must be at least 20 feet wide if the non-public roadway or drive is a private driveway for a private single-family residence, and 30 feet wide if the non-public roadway is for any other purpose other than a driveway for a private single-family residence.
(3)
The roadway, drive or ramp must be constructed of materials normally suitable for the type of roadway, driveway or ramp being constructed and must comply or meet with the standards of the current edition of the current edition of the state department of transportation standard specifications or such other standards which this board may adopt from time to time without the necessity of amending this article.
(4)
Ditches, pipe and other water drainage and run-off measures will be provided and installed by applicant for each such roadway, ramp and drive as required to adequately drain and protect such roadway, ramp, or drive as well as the adjoining public and private roadways which will be affected by the water runoff from such non-public roadway, ramp, or drive. The final resolution of such drainage measures must also provide for the protection from any potential runoff damage to any then proposed streets or roadways that may be subsequently affected by the water runoff from such non-public roadway, ramp or drive.
(5)
Construction of the roadway, driveway or ramp shall not interfere with, impede, or create a hazard to existing drainage or traffic.
(6)
All work will be required to be performed in such a manner as not to present the county with an inferior or substandard roadway which would result in additional expenditures or undue future maintenance by the county.
(7)
Paving is not mandatory. However if a paving is to be done, the roadway, drive or ramp must comply with all requirements of the state department of transportation standard specifications relating to paved county roads.
(8)
Upon request of an applicant and if the board of commissioners determines it to be in the best interests of the public, the county may construct a ramp from a public right-of-way to the edge of applicant's private property, providing applicant pays in advance the cost of the pipe to be used. The county will not construct ramps from on or across non-public property or from non-public roadways.
(9)
All pipe and other materials used by a landowner or applicant in the construction of a ramp, drive or roadway which is on, or subsequently becomes a part of, a public right-of-way become the property of the county and may not be removed by the applicant or landowner, regardless of whether the pipe or other materials were furnished by the landowner or the county.
(10)
The location of the point of connection or joining with a public road must be first approved by the board of commissioners of the county or its authorized representative.
(Ord. of 6-12-1984, § 5; Ord. of 2001, § 1)
Sec. 28-85. - Requirements for acceptance of private ramp, drive or roadway by county.
(a)
Once the non-public ramp, drive or roadway has been completed by the applicant and accepted by this board, then the applicant or owner may convey the ramp, drive or road to the county. The county shall refuse to accept such ramp, drive or roadway if it does not comply with the requirements of this article. The landowner, applicant or contractor should consult with the county board of commissioners or its authorized representative as needed during the construction of any roadway, drive or ramp if there is any question regarding compliance with this article.
(b)
All non-public ramps, drives and roadways must be inspected by the road superintendent or some other official designated by this board prior to final approval. Approval will not be unreasonably withheld.
(c)
Any ramp, drive or roadway conveyed to the county pursuant to this article must be free of all liens and encumbrances at the time of conveyance to the county, and proof of such must be furnished to the county prior to its acceptance of such ramp, drive or roadway.
(Ord. of 6-12-1984, §§ 6—10)
Sec. 28-86. - County will not aid in construction or maintain private ramps, roads or drives.
The county shall neither furnish free of charge nor sell, lease, or rent any county property, including dirt, pipe, labor, equipment or machinery, for the construction, repair, maintenance or improvement of roadways, ramps, or driveways on or across non-public property. The county shall not cut, grade, build, or improve any roadways, ramps or driveways across non-public property.
(Ord. of 6-12-1984, §§ 2, 3)

ARTICLE V. - TIMBER OPERATIONS[2]

Sec. 28-100. - Purpose and intent.
This article is intended to require notice of timber harvesting operations within the unincorporated areas of Jeff Davis County pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 12-6-24, and, in accordance with Title 32 of the Official Code of Georgia, provide regulations further governing use of public roads by persons engaged in said timber harvesting operations in order to assure the public safety and to protect the county roadways and environment.
(Ord. of 11-17-2012)
Sec. 28-101. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
County administrator means the county administrator for Jeff Davis County, or his or her designee.
Notice means the notice required to be given to the public works department by the timber harvester as provided herein.
Person means any natural person, firm, corporation, limited liability company, partnership, or other legal entity of whatever form.
Road department means the Jeff Davis County Road Department.
Road department superintendent means the superintendent of the Jeff Davis County Road Department.
Surety bond means a valid surety bond, executed by a surety corporation authorized to transact business in the State of Georgia, protecting the county against any damage caused by such person or firm in an amount specified by the governing authority of Jeff Davis County not exceeding $5,000.00 or, at the option of the timber harvester, a valid irrevocable letter of credit issued by a bank or savings and loan association, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 7-1-4, in the amount of an in lieu of such bond. Any such surety bond or letter of credit shall be in form prescribed by or acceptable to the county administrator and shall be for the calendar year in which delivered.
Timber means softwood or hardwood trees harvested for commercial purposes.
Timber harvester means any person harvesting standing timber in any unincorporated area of Jeff Davis County, Georgia, for delivery as pulpwood, logs, poles, posts, or wood chips to any wood yard or processing plant located inside or outside the State of Georgia.
(Ord. of 11-17-2012)
Sec. 28-102. - Notice required.
Prior written notice shall be required of any timber harvester harvesting timber in the unincorporated areas of the county for each separate tract to be harvested. Said written notice shall be delivered to the road department in such form and containing such information as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 12-6-24(b)(1). Notice shall be effective only as to the tract described therein upon receipt by the road department as confirmed by written acknowledgment from said road department to the timber harvester and compliance with all other applicable requirements of this article.
(Ord. of 11-17-2012)
Sec. 28-103. - Surety bond.
(a)
A surety bond shall not be required prior to notice becoming effective. However, following the giving of any notice, in the event that the road department superintendent determines that any activities relating to the timber harvesting are being conducted in a manner that poses likelihood of damage to any public rights-of-way or danger to the public safety, the road department shall be authorized to demand a surety bond from the timber harvester. The surety bond shall be delivered to the road department within three business days following receipt of written demand for the same, unless within said time a written notice of appeal is received by the road department contesting the requirement of the surety bond. In the event of such contest, the road department shall, no later than five business days, schedule an administrative hearing in such matter before the county administrator, who shall deliver a decision within three business days of said hearing. The decision of the county administrator shall be conclusive and binding for all purposes.
Following any contest as provided above, all timber harvesting authorized pursuant to the applicable notice shall be suspended until the surety bond, if any, is delivered to the road department as required by the county administrator.
(b)
The amount of the bond shall not exceed $5,000.00. The bond shall indemnify the county against any and all damage caused, as a result of the timber harvesting and related activities, to any county roads, rights-of-way, points of egress or other public property. The surety bond shall also indemnify the county for any costs incurred in the cleanup of waste or debris left on public property as a result of the timber harvesting.
(c)
Upon completion of any necessary repairs to public property and removal of all waste and debris, the county will release the timber harvester from the surety bond.
(d)
In no event shall the delivery of a surety bond limit the liability of any person engaged in timber harvesting and related activities for damages to public property or rights-of-way or for other personal injury or property damage.
(Ord. of 11-17-2012)
Sec. 28-104. - Culverts.
Prior to commencing timber harvesting operations, the road department superintendent shall inspect the point of access to the public road from the tract described in the notice in order to determine its suitability. If a culvert is required at the point of access, it shall be promptly installed by the timber harvester as directed by the road department superintendent.
(Ord. of 11-17-2012)
Sec. 28-105. - Signs.
Prior to commencing any timber harvesting operations, the timber harvester shall post two signs in each direction from the point of access onto the involved public road. One sign at a distance of 1,000 feet from the point of access stating "Warning: Logging Operations Ahead," and one sign at a distance of 500 feet stating "Slow Truck Entering Highway." All signs shall be 36 inches by 36 inches, erected in a diamond shape, painted orange with black legend and posted at least three feet from the travel surface of the roadway.
(Ord. of 11-17-2012)
Sec. 28-106. - Roadway safety.
During the period of operations, the timber harvester shall comply with the following:
(1)
All harvesting operations shall be conducted on the tract identified in the notice and off public roads and rights-of-way. Logging and skidding of logs on public roads and rights-of-way are strictly prohibited.
(2)
Ditches constituting a part of the public drainage system or otherwise benefitting a public right-of-way shall be kept clear of all debris and residue at all times to permit proper drainage.
(3)
There shall be no blockage of public roads or rights-of-way at the access site, and all public roads and rights-of-way must remain open and kept free of debris and residue at all times.
(4)
No equipment, trucks, trailers, or other personal property shall be parked or located on any public road or right-of-way.
(5)
Mud shall not be carried onto paved public roads by logging vehicles. Where necessary, a temporary construction exit of gravel shall be maintained by the timber harvester to eliminate any hazard to traffic or unsightly conditions on the public right-of -way.
(6)
During periods of rain or inclement weather, logging vehicles shall not be operated over the unimproved public roads when such travel causes sufficient damage to the road system that the passage of smaller and lighter vehicles is hampered.
(Ord. of 11-17-2012)
Sec. 28-107. - Enforcement.
(a)
Upon notice from the issuing authority, work on any project that is being done contrary to the provisions of this article shall be immediately stopped. Such notice shall be in writing and shall be given to the timber harvester and the owner of the subject property, and shall state the conditions under which work may resume. When an emergency exists, written notice shall not be required.
(b)
If the conditions are not brought into compliance within five business days of the notice, the road department shall be authorized to accomplish the work with costs thereof to be reimbursed by the timber harvester, and any violation of the provisions of this article shall be punished as for a misdemeanor.
(Ord. of 11-17-2012)
Sec. 28-108. - Concluding operations.
(a)
The timber harvester shall give written notification to the road department supervisor upon completion of the timber harvesting operations. The road department supervisor shall inspect all affected public property and public rights-of-way to assure that the same has not been damaged or has been restored to its original condition, including any shaping of ditches, grading or seeding as required. The timber harvester shall be notified in writing of any deficiencies and given 15 business days to correct said deficiencies. If not accomplished during that period, the road department or its designee shall be authorized to complete the work at the cost of the timber harvester.
(b)
When the road department superintendent determines that the road and access site are in a proper state of repair, the superintendent shall provide a written release of liability to the timber harvester.
(Ord. of 11-17-2012)
Sec. 28-109. - Prosecution of violations.
(a)
Any person violating any of the provisions of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor prosecutable in the Magistrate Court of Jeff Davis County and punishable by a fine not to exceed $500.00 and imprisonment in the county jail for a period of no longer than 12 months.
(b)
The imposition of any fine or other sentence pursuant to this section shall in no way limit the liability of any person for damage done to public property or public rights-of-way, and the county shall be authorized to additionally pursue recovery for any such damage by any and all means permitted by law or in equity, to include, without limitation, the filing of a suit for damages, or the seeking of injunctive relief to stop any such damage.
(Ord. of 11-17-2012)
Sec. 28-110. - Timber harvesting notice.
(a)
Prior to initiating any timber harvesting activities in the county on or after the effective date of the ordinance from which this section derives, the timber harvesting contractor shall give written notification to the board of assessors of the county within 24 hours prior to commencing start-up activities moving in of equipment or cutting of any trees. The notice shall be in the following form:
Upon completion of the harvesting of the timber identified in the above form, the timber harvesting contractor shall give oral notification in person or by telephone to the tax assessor's office that the harvesting activities at the above described location have been completed.
(b)
The penalty for failure to comply with the reporting requirements set out in subsection (a) of this section shall be a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 and/or 12 months imprisonment in the county jail.
(c)
Citations for violation of this section shall be prosecuted in the Magistrate Court of Jeff Davis County.
(Ord. of 11-17-2012)
