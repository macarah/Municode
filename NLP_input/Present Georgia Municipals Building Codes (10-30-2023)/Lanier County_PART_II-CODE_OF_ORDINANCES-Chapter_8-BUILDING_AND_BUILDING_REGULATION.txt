Chapter 8 - BUILDING AND BUILDING REGULATION[1]
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 8-1. - Building permit required.
Prior to the erection of any building of any nature or an addition or alteration to any building in which the cost will exceed more than $300.00 or prior to the location of any mobile home or the relocation of any mobile home within the county, a building permit must be acquired from the land development administrator.
(Ord. of 12-12-1994, § 1)
Sec. 8-2. - Building permit application.
The application for the permit shall be filed with the land development administrator and disclose the following information:
(1)
The name and address of the applicant.
(2)
The address of the location of the building that is to be constructed or the address of the building being repaired or the location and address of the mobile home upon which land such mobile home is to be placed.
(3)
The size and estimated cost of the construction of building alterations or repairs.
(4)
The manufacturer, size, year model and serial number of the mobile home to be located or relocated within said county.
(5)
Sewage certification: verification of adequate sewage disposal system.
(Ord. of 12-12-1994, § 2)
Sec. 8-3. - Issuance.
It shall be the duty of the land development administrator to issue the applicant a building permit at no cost.
(Ord. of 12-12-1994, § 3)
Sec. 8-4. - Permit and decals.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the land development administrator to issue to the applicant a building permit for the construction or alteration of any building and the land development administrator to issue a decal for the location or relocation in case the application is for the location of a mobile home.
(b)
Renewal of the decals shall be granted by the land development administrator each year thereafter, upon payment of taxes due if applicable. In such cases where the mobile home is homestead there shall be no fee. Said renewal of decals must be made on or before May 1 of each year thereafter.
(Ord. of 12-12-1994, § 4)
Sec. 8-5. - Affixing mobile home decal.
All decals issued pursuant to this chapter shall be affixed on the mobile home in such manner as to be clearly visible from the front or from the street or roadway.
(Ord. of 12-12-1994, § 5)
Sec. 8-6. - Location or relocation of mobile home.
It shall be unlawful for any mobile home dealer or mover to locate or relocate a mobile home on any land in the county, whose owner has not made application for a building permit from the land development administrator and obtained a decal from the land development administrator of the county and have the same affixed thereto.
(Ord. of 12-12-1994, § 6)
Sec. 8-7. - Connection of utilities.
It shall be unlawful for any electric, gas or water utility company, whether a person, firm, corporation, municipality, or cooperative to connect electricity, gas or water to any building under construction or to any mobile home to be located in the county, unless a building permit has been issued by the land development administrator and in the case of a mobile home, a decal attached thereto.
(Ord. of 12-12-1994, § 7)
Sec. 8-8. - Penalty.
Any person, firm or corporation who shall violate the provisions of this chapter by erecting, constructing or repairing a building, or by moving, locating or relocating a mobile home in the county, or connecting electricity, gas or water to any building or mobile home on which there has not been issued a building permit and a proper decal attached, in the case of a mobile home, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be subject to a maximum penalty of $1,000.00 for each offense.
(Ord. of 12-12-1994, § 8)
Sec. 8-9. - Failure to renew.
Any mobile home owner who fails to renew annually a permit for a decal to be attached to said mobile home in such manner as hereinbefore set out, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be subject to a maximum fine of $1,000.00 for each offense.
(Ord. of 12-12-1994, § 9)
Sec. 8-10. - Self-inspection of sewer lines by plumbers and utility contractors.
The governing authority of Lanier County, the 1996 Ga. Laws (Act No. 1046), § 3, p. 1632, does hereby exempt the county from the provisions of said Act codified as O.C.G.A. § 8-2-26(d).
(Res. of 5-13-1996)
Secs. 8-11—8-38. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - TECHNICAL CODES

Sec. 8-39. - Resolution.
(a)
It is the desire of the county board of commissioners to adopt, in all respects, administrative procedures and enforce the various standard code relating to building, fire prevention, gas, mechanical, plumbing and electrical, dwelling, energy.
(b)
The adoption of these procedures is done to facilitate proper inspection activities by the county relating to construction and to maintenance of buildings within said county and relating to public safety, health and general welfare.
(c)
No buildings, structures, or land shall be used or occupied; and no building, structure, or part thereof shall be erected, constructed, reconstructed, moved, enlarged, or structurally altered unless in conformity with the regulations of this article. All contractors, builders, or landowners shall be required to purchase permits before any construction within the county shall begin. Georgia Power, Slash Pine REA, or any other electrical supplier shall not connect or supply any building, structure or mobile home with power until an electrical permit has been issued.
(d)
Although a permit is required, any farm shelter or structure which does not have plumbing and electricity shall not be required to pay a fee for said permit. Nothing contained herein shall be construed to be restrictive in any way to the operation of a bona fide farm or the production of agricultural products including produce, livestock, and forest products. This exclusion shall not apply to buildings constructed for human occupancy.
(e)
The county board of commissioners, shall employ inspectors and any other personnel necessary for the enforcement of said codes. There shall be no right to self-inspections by plumbing and utility contractors. All inspections shall be made by the county inspection officer.
(f)
The county board of commissioners shall establish charges for said permits.
(g)
Within said codes, when reference is made to the duties of a certain official named therein, that designated official of the county who has duties corresponding to those of the named official in said code shall be deemed to be the responsible official insofar as enforcing the provisions of said code are concerned.
(Res. of 10-31-1996)
Sec. 8-40. - Interpretations.
The building official is hereby authorized to render interpretations of this Code and the adopted state codes and to adopt policies and procedures in order to clarify the application of their provisions. Such interpretations, policies and procedures shall be in compliance with the intent and purpose of this article. Such policies and procedures shall not have the force and effect of law and shall not have the effect of waiving requirements specifically provided for in this Code or in the adopted state codes.
Sec. 8-41. - Adoption of technical codes.
(a)
The following technical and building codes, as adopted and amended by the state department of community affairs, are adopted by reference and incorporated into this Code as if set out fully herein, including the administration and appendix chapters:
(1)
International Building Code, 2006 edition, with Georgia Amendments 2007.
(2)
International Residential Code, 2006 edition, with Georgia Amendments 2007 and 2008.
(3)
International Fire Code, 2006 edition, with Georgia Amendments 2008.
(4)
International Plumbing Code, 2006 edition, with Georgia Amendments 2007 and 2008.
(5)
International Mechanical Code, 2006 edition, with Georgia Amendments 2007 and 2008.
(6)
International Fuel Gas Code, 2006 edition, with Georgia Amendments 2007 and 2008.
(7)
National Electrical Code, 2005 edition, with Georgia Amendments 2006.
(8)
International Energy Conservation Code, 2006 edition, with Georgia Supplements and Amendments 2008.
(9)
CABO One- and Two-Family Dwelling Code, Appendix G, as the Standard Swimming Pool Code.
(b)
If any of the publications listed in subsection (a) of this section are adopted as a state minimum code pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 8-2-20 et seq., then the edition adopted by the state shall control. If any of the publications listed in subsection (a) of this section are less stringent than the state minimum codes promulgated pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 8-2-20 et seq., then the state minimum code shall control.
(c)
A copy of each of the publications listed in subsection (a) of this section, as adopted by the state, is filed in the county office and available for review by the public. The same is adopted and incorporated as fully as if set out at length herein, and from the date on which this section shall take effect, the provisions thereof shall be controlling within the unincorporated area of the county.

The purpose of this article is to enhance rapid location of properties by law enforcement, fire, rescue, and emergency medical service personnel in the county.

Sec. 8-73. - Standards for the proper display of addresses.
modified
(a)
The owner and occupant of all property located in the unincorporated limits of the county shall conspicuously display the number assigned to said property.
(b)
The owner and occupant of any property to which a number has been assigned shall affix the number in a conspicuous place as set forth in this article.
(c)
It shall be the duty of such owner and occupant of the property upon affixing the new number to remove any different number that might be mistaken for or confused with the number assigned.
(d)
All property shall have the correct address posted on the main structure of the property, or at the entrance of the property as provided for in subsection (c) of this section, where it will be clearly visible to persons or vehicles approaching the property.
(e)
When property is more than 35 feet from the road, or when the view of the same is blocked by trees, shrubs, or other material, the number shall be on a mailbox, fence, gate, or lawn stake at the entrance of the property on the same side of the road as the entrance to the property and clearly visible and legible from the road.
(f)
All addresses shall be legible and shall be large enough to be seen easily from the road. Each number must be a minimum of three square inches.
(g)
Numbers shall be of contrasting color to their background for easy visibility.
(h)
Numbers shall be of reflective material and must be easily visible at night.
(i)
On corner lots, numbers shall be displayed on the frontage to which the property is facing.
(j)
In cases where more than one property has a single access, the principal address shall be displayed at the access and the sub address displayed on the property to which it applies. This is applicable to apartments, condominiums, mobile and manufactured home parks as well.
(Ord.*, § 3)
*Editor's note—Number and adoption date for this ordinance were not available at the time of publication of this Code.
Sec. 8-74. - Enforcement and penalties for violations.
modified
(a)
Whenever the owner and occupant of an officially address property shall neglect or refuse to affix or inscribe the proper numbers as required by this article, the chairperson or his designee shall serve notice on the owner and the occupant at the address of a violation of this article. The owner and occupant shall affix the assigned number to the property in accordance with this article within tens days of the service of notice. If the owner and occupant shall fail to affix the number within said ten-day period, such owner or occupant may be fined and imprisoned as set forth in this section and the numbers caused to be affixed by the chairperson or his designee.
(b)
If the owner and the occupant or person of any property to which a number has been assigned refuses to comply with the terms of this article or if any person shall take down, alter, injure or deface any number affixed or inscribed, such person shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 or imprisonment for a term not to exceeding six months, or any combination thereof. Each day any violation of this article shall continue shall constitute a separate offense.
(c)
The magistrate court of the county shall have jurisdiction over violations of this section and all procedures for enforcement hereof shall be as provided in O.C.G.A. title 15, ch. 10, art. 4, as amended. Complainants of violations of any provision contained herein shall be brought before the magistrate court on a citation issued by the chairperson, or his designee, and shall be prosecuted through the court. Violations of this section will be tried upon citations either with or without a prosecuting attorney as determined by the chairperson. Service of citation shall be performed by the constable of the magistrate court of the county. Citations shall meet all the official requirements as specified in the O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63, as amended.
(Ord.*, § 4)
