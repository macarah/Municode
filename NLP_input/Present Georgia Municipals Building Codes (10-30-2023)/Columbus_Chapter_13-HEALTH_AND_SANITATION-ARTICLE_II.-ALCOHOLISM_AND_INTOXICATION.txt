ARTICLE II. - ALCOHOLISM AND INTOXICATION[4]

Sec. 13-1. - Statement of policy.
It is hereby declared to be the policy of Columbus, Georgia, that persons may not be subjected to criminal prosecution solely because of their consumption of alcoholic beverages, but rather should be afforded an opportunity for treatment in order that they may lead normal lives as productive members of society.
(Ord. No. 81-31, § 2, 5-5-81)
Sec. 13-2. - Definitions.
The terms used herein shall have the meanings defined in the Uniform Alcoholism and Intoxication Treatment Act of 1974 (Act No. 882, Ga. Laws 1974, p. 200).
(Ord. No. 81-31, § 2, 5-5-81)
Sec. 13-3. - Juveniles excepted from article provisions.
Any person under the age of 17 years and subject to the jurisdiction of the juvenile court shall not be subject to this article. Such person may be referred by order of juvenile court to an approved treatment facility.
(Ord. No. 81-31, § 2, 5-5-81)
Sec. 13-4. - Protective custody—Options of detaining officer.
A person who appears to be incapacitated by alcohol shall be taken into protective custody by the police or other law enforcement officer and forthwith brought to an approved treatment facility for emergency treatment. If no approved treatment facility is available, such person shall be taken to an emergency medical service facility customarily used for incapacitated persons. A person incapacitated by alcohol who does not require emergency treatment may be taken home or, if the person will not go home, may be taken into protective custody and held in the jail until such person is no longer incapacitated by alcohol, but for no longer than a period of 12 hours.
(Ord. No. 81-31, § 2, 5-5-81)
Sec. 13-5. - Same—Duty of officer to protect subject; right to protect self.
The policeman or other law enforcement officer, in taking such person into protective custody, shall make reasonable effort to protect such person; provided, however, that the detaining officer may take reasonable steps to protect himself.
(Ord. No. 81-31, § 1, 5-5-81)
Sec. 13-6. - Same—Not to constitute arrest.
The taking into protective custody of a person incapacitated by alcohol under section 13-4 shall not constitute an arrest, nor shall any record be made to indicate that the person has been arrested or charged with a crime; provided, however, that such person may be searched for weapons or other items which may be used to cause harm to the detainee, police officer, or other person.
(Ord. No. 81-31, § 1, 5-5-81)
Sec. 13-7. - Same—Right to telephone call.
Persons taken into protective custody shall be allowed to make one telephone call at such person's own expense.
(Ord. No. 81-31, § 1, 5-5-81)
Sec. 13-8. - Actions of law enforcement officer deemed course of official duty.
Any policeman or law enforcement officer who acts in compliance with this article is hereby declared to be acting in the course of official duty.
(Ord. No. 81-31, § 1, 5-5-81)
Sec. 13-9. - Scope of article provisions; intoxication not a defense against other charges.
Nothing herein shall be deemed to excuse or justify any other crime simply because the perpetrator is intoxicated. Nothing in this article shall repeal, annul or otherwise affect any law, ordinance, resolution or rule against drunken driving, driving under the influence of alcohol, or other similar offense involving the operation of a vehicle, aircraft, boat, machinery or other equipment, or regarding the sale, purchase, dispensing, possessing or use of alcoholic beverages at stated times and places or by a particular class of persons, or any other criminal act.
(Ord. No. 81-31, § 1, 5-5-81)
Sec. 13-10. - Repeal of conflicting provisions.
All ordinances or parts of ordinances in conflict herewith are hereby repealed.
(Ord. No. 81-31, § 1, 5-5-81)
Secs. 13-11—13-97. - Reserved.
