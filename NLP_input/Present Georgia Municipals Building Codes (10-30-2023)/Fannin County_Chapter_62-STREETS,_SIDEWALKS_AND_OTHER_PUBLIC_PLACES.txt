ARTICLE II. - PRIVATE DRIVEWAYS

Sec. 62-19. - Title, authority, purpose, and jurisdiction.
(a)
This article shall be known and may be cited as the "Driveway Ordinance for the County of Fannin, Georgia."
(b)
The purpose of this article is to provide the manner of installation of driveways along public streets and roadways in order to facilitate provision of adequate public safety.
(c)
The board of commissioners of Fannin County, a political subdivision of the State of Georgia, is charged with providing for the safety and well-being of the citizens of Fannin County, Georgia. To that end, it is that all driveways installed along public streets and roadways be done in such a manner as to minimize runoff onto public streets and roadways.
(d)
This article, the regulations, and the procedures contained herein shall apply to and govern each and every driveway installed along the public streets and roadways of Fannin County.
(Ord. of 8-10-1999, art. I)
Sec. 62-20. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Board of commissioners means the board of commissioners of Fannin County, a political subdivision of the State of Georgia, which includes the chairman, and two post commissioners, designated as post one commissioner and post two commissioner.
Developer means a person, firm or corporation submitting an application for development for a subdivision, planned unit development, industrial park or mobile home park and upon whom final responsibility for ensuring compliance with the terms and conditions of this article rests.
Driveway means a private way, beginning at the property line of a lot abutting a public road, private road, easement or private right-of-way, giving access from that public road, recorded easement, recorded private road or private right-of-way, and leading to a building, use or structure on that lot.
Dwelling, single-family, means a building arranged or designed to be occupied by one family.
Private road means a street not maintained by the Fannin County department of roads which is not intended to become a public street but which shall be used for access to a particular site, group development or business.
Public road means a street located on public right-of-way and which meets the total improvement requirements for a public street as set forth by the Georgia Department of Transportation.
Roadway means any road, street, drive, lane, cartway, tramway, easement, right-of-way, access area, thoroughfare, highway, boulevard, or any other corridor used for or having the potential use as a means of conveyance by a motor vehicle.
(Ord. of 8-10-1999, art. II)
Sec. 62-21. - Driveway installation.
(a)
Slope; angle of entrance. Any developer or individual installing a driveway which shall connect to a public road or a road which may become public shall do so in a manner that conforms to the sloping of the road. Such installation shall be such that any runoff from the driveway is angled away from the road.
(b)
Nofication by developer of installation location. Any developer or individual installing a driveway which shall connect to a public road or a road which may become public shall notify the Fannin County right-of-way department as to the location of said installation.
(c)
County inspection of slope or angle of interest. The Fannin County right-of-way and engineering department shall have a period of 60 days from the date it was notified of said installation to inspect the same and determine if the slopping or angle of entrance is sufficient to minimize runoff on the road.
(d)
Notification by county of necessary modifications. The Fannin County right-of-way and engineering department shall notify the property owner, in writing, via certified mail, return receipt requested, of any modifications deemed necessary to said installation.
(e)
Responsibility for corrections or modifications. The developer or individual installing the driveway shall be solely responsible for the correction or modification deemed necessary to said installation. In the event that the property has been sold or title has changed hands, it shall be the responsibility of the party installing said driveway to make the necessary modifications.
(f)
Record of notification; written receipt. The Fannin County right-of-way and engineering department shall maintain a log including the date it was notified of the installation, the date of inspection and the result of the inspection. It shall also provide a written receipt to the individual or developer indicating the date of notification to the department.
(Ord. of 8-10-1999, art. III)
Sec. 62-22. - Enforcing officers.
(a)
Any peace officer of the State of Georgia or Fannin County or the Fannin County board of commissioners may name, by appropriate resolution, an employee of Fannin County to serve as its designee and to oversee all matters concerning the enforcement of this article.
(b)
The Fannin County board of commissioners may name by appropriate resolution such other persons to be under the designee described in subsection (a) of this section to insure enforcement of this article.
(c)
The designee of the board of commissioners shall be authorized to issue citations to violators of any provisions of this article or to the owner or any other person who may be in the possession of any property upon which any condition exists which constitutes a violation of any provision of this article.
(d)
Such citations shall be on a form approved by the Fannin County board of commissioners and shall state the time and place at which the accused is to appear for trial, shall identify the offense with which the accused is charged, shall have the identifying number by which it shall be filed with the court, and shall be signed by the representative of Fannin County who completes and serves it.
(Ord. of 8-10-1999, art. IV)
Sec. 62-23. - Penalty.
(a)
Any person violating this article or any part thereof, upon conviction shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be subject to the following punishment as provided in Georgia Code section 15-10-60.
(1)
Owners of property which do not comply with this article will be notified and requested to meet these requirements within 30 days from the date of notification. A warning notice will be issued after 30 days if the requirements have not been met.
(2)
Any person violating the provisions of this article after having been issued a warning notice upon conviction will be guilty of a misdemeanor, and will be fined $50.00 for the first offense.
(3)
Any subsequent violations after the first offense upon conviction will be subject to a fine of $100.00.
(b)
Any violation of this article may be subject to civil remedies as set forth in Georgia Code section 15-10-40 to 15-10-51, article 3.
(Ord. of 8-10-1999, art. V)
Sec. 62-24. - Severability and conflict.
(a)
Should any section or provision of this article be declared by the courts to be unconstitutional or invalid such decisions shall not affect the validity of this article as a whole, or any part thereof other than the part declared to be unconstitutional or invalid.
(b)
Insofar as the provisions of this article are inconsistent with the provisions or any other local laws, the provisions of this article shall control. Insofar as the provisions of this article are inconsistent with the provisions of any state or federal law, the state or federal law shall control.
(Ord. of 8-10-1999, art. VI)
Sec. 62-25. - Appeal.
Any action taken by the property address coordinator or under guidelines set forth in this article may be appealed by the property owner(s) to the Fannin County board of commissioners. Such an appeal must be filed in writing within 30 days of written notification of required owner action or within 30 days of the action from which the property owner takes objection. Said appeal shall be filed with the Fannin County clerk.
(Ord. of 8-10-1999, art. VII)
Sec. 62-26. - Amendment procedure.
This article may be amended from time to time by the Fannin County board of commissioners, as specified under board policy for amending county ordinances.
(Ord. of 8-10-1999, art. VIII)

ARTICLE III. - DESTRUCTION OR REMOVAL OF COUNTY SIGNS, CHARACTERS, MARKERS OR INDICATORS

Sec. 62-56. - Unauthorized removal of signs.
It shall be illegal for any person to remove, vacate, alter, destroy, demolish, steal or borrow without authorization any signs, characters, markers or indicators adjacent to or on the right-of-way of streets, roads, avenues, and lanes which are a part of Fannin County, Georgia.
(Ord. of 8-1-1996, art. I)
Sec. 62-57. - Possession of signs.
It shall be illegal for any person to have in his/her possession any signs, characters, markers or indicators adjacent to or on the right-of-way of streets, roads, avenues, and lanes which can reasonable be assumed to have been a part of Fannin County, Georgia.
(Ord. of 8-1-1996, art. II)
Sec. 62-58. - Enforcing officers.
(a)
Any peace officer of the State of Georgia or Fannin County or the board of commissioners may name, by appropriate resolution, an employee of Fannin County to serve as its designee and to oversee all matters concerning the enforcement of this article.
(b)
The board of commissioners may name, by appropriate resolution, such persons as it may feel necessary, if any, in addition to the Fannin County sheriff's office, to be road sign monitors. Such monitors shall be under the supervision of the designee described in subsection (a) of this section and shall patrol, inspect and monitor road sign sites to insure compliance with this article, and shall monitor all areas of Fannin County for violations of this article.
(c)
The designee of the board of commissioners, as the road sign monitor, shall be authorized to issue citations to violators of any provision of this article or to the owner or any other person who may be in possession of any property upon which any condition exists which constitutes a violation of any provision of this article. Such citations shall be on a form approved by the board of commissioners and shall state the time and place at which the accused is to appear for trial, shall identify the offense with which the accused is charged, shall have an identifying number by which it shall be filed with the court, shall indicate the identity of the accused and the date of service, and shall be signed by the representative of Fannin County who completes and serves it.
(Ord. of 8-1-1996, § 3.0)
Sec. 62-59. - Penalty.
(a)
Any person violating this article, or any provision hereof, upon conviction, shall be punished as follows: By fine of not less than $250.00 and not more than $500.00, and/or by imprisonment in the Fannin County Jail for a period not to exceed 60 days, which is in accordance with Official Code of Georgia Annotated, section 15-10-62.
(b)
No person accused of violating this article shall be arrested prior to trial, but any defendant who fails to appear for trial shall be arrested thereafter on the warrant of the magistrate and required to post a bond for his/her future appearance.
(Ord. of 8-1-1996, § 3.1)
Sec. 62-60. - Court proceedings.
(a)
Violations of this article shall be tried upon citations or accusations as described in section 62-59(a) and may be tried with or without a prosecuting attorney as well as upon accusations. The county attorney or another attorney designated by the board of commissioners shall serve as prosecuting attorney.
(b)
Violations of this article shall be tried in the magistrate court of Fannin County, Georgia, and shall be tried in accordance with the Official Code of Georgia, chapter 15-10.
(c)
Nothing in this section shall prevent the board of commissioners from bringing any civil action for injunction, mandamus or other proceedings to present, correct, or abate any violation of this article. No sanction, penalty or remedy prescribed herein shall be considered exclusive of any other remedy by law.
(d)
Each violation of this article shall constitute a separate offense.
(Ord. of 8-1-1996, § 3.2)
