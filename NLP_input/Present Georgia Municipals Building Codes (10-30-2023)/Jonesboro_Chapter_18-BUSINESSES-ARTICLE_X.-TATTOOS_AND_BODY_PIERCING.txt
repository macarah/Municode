ARTICLE X. - TATTOOS AND BODY PIERCING

Sec. 18-400. - Definitions.
For the purposes of this article, the following terms shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this article:
Body piercing establishment: Any location where piercing occurs either for compensation or in conjunction with a purchase of goods.
Communicable disease: An illness or disease that can be communicated from one person to another through the air, through routine contact with the human skin, or through open wounds.
Pierce: To attach earrings, studs, or other jewelry, either precious, semiprecious or costume, or accessories to the human epidermis by piercing through the skin at any location on the human body, including but not limited to the ear, nose, tongue, eyelid, eyebrow, lip, or nipple.
Tattoo: Shall have the meaning set forth in O.C.G.A. Section 31-40-1.
Tattoo studio: Shall have the meaning set forth in O.C.G.A. Section 31-40-1.
(Ord. No. 03-05, § 1, 3-10-2003)
Sec. 18-401. - Tattooing/piercing prohibited under certain circumstances.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to tattoo or pierce any person who is under 18 years of age, any person who is under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs, any person who exhibits symptoms of any communicable disease, or any person of unsound mind.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person, when under 18 years of age, under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs, infected with any communicable disease, or of unsound mind, to tattoo or pierce any other person.
(c)
It shall be unlawful to sell at any tattoo studio or body piercing establishment any 'drug related object' as that term is defined in O.C.G.A. Section 16-13-32 or any device or paraphernalia that is designed principally for sexual activities, but not including contraceptive devices.
(Ord. No. 03-05, § 1, 3-10-2003)
Sec. 18-402. - Permit, compliance with article required.
It shall be unlawful to operate a tattoo studio or a body piercing establishment without a permit from the city. It shall be unlawful to operate a tattoo studio or body piercing establishment in violation of this article.
(Ord. No. 03-05, § 1, 3-10-2003)
Sec. 18-403. - Application; minimum age; fingerprinting, photographing, medical certificate required.
(a)
Any person desiring to operate a tattoo studio or a body piercing establishment shall make application for a permit to the city clerk on forms supplied by the department.
(b)
The minimum age of each applicant shall be 18 years of age.
(c)
Fingerprints of all applicants shall be taken by the department of police prior to submission of the application to the city clerk.
(d)
An applicant for a permit shall present to the city clerk proof of identification with a photograph of applicant.
(e)
An applicant for a permit shall present to the city clerk, for each person who will be tattooing or piercing, a medical certificate not more than one month old from a medical doctor certifying that the person is sound physically and mentally, has good eyesight and is not infected with a communicable disease.
(Ord. No. 03-05, § 1, 3-10-2003)
Sec. 18-404. - Approval of health authorities required for tattoo studios.
It shall be unlawful for any tattoo studio to operate without a permit from the Clayton County Board of Health or the state department of human resources, as appropriate.
(Ord. No. 03-05, § 1, 3-10-2003)
Sec. 18-405. - Permit contents, transferability; term; fee; time for action.
(a)
Every permit granted under this article shall be issued in the name of the individual person applying therefor, shall contain the location of the tattoo studio or body piercing establishment where the applicant shall operate, and shall not be transferable.
(b)
Each permit issued under this article shall be valid until December 31 of the year of issuance.
(c)
No application for a permit under this article shall be accepted by the city unless accompanied by payment of a permit fee in such amount as the mayor and council may set from time to time.
(d)
All applications for new permits shall be passed upon by the city clerk within 60 days of the date of filing of the completed application. Date of filing shall be the time that a properly completed and executed application, along with the fee, is received by the city clerk. Upon the expiration of the 60-day period without a decision being made on the application by the city clerk, the applicant shall be permitted to begin and conduct business as a tattoo studio or a body piercing establishment under this statutory provision unless and until such time as the city clerk denies the application for a new license. No person or entity conducting business as a tattoo studio or a body piercing establishment under this provision shall acquire any vested rights to continued conduct of such business, and if the city clerk later denies the application, the business must cease.
(Ord. No. 03-05, § 1, 3-10-2003)
Sec. 18-406. - Reporting and records requirements.
All permittees under this article shall adhere to the following reporting and records requirements:
(1)
Monthly updates of the medical certificate or certificates required by section 18-403(e) shall be retained at the place of business and subject to inspection by city personnel. Failure to present said certificate updates upon request of city personnel shall result in suspension of the permit until such time as proper updated certificates can be presented to the city.
(2)
A list shall be maintained on the premises of every person on whom tattooing or piercing is performed. As to each such person, the list shall include the following information:
a.
Name;
b.
Address;
c.
Home and work phone numbers, if applicable;
d.
Social Security Number or driver's license identification number; and
e.
Proof of age.
The above information shall be retained at the tattoo studio or body piercing establishment for at least three years as to each person and is subject to inspection by city personnel.
(Ord. No. 03-05, § 1, 3-10-2003)
Sec. 18-407. - Distance requirements.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to operate a tattoo studio or a body piercing establishment within 100 yards of any establishment that sells malt beverages, wine, or distilled spirits.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to operate a tattoo studio or a body piercing establishment within 100 yards of any school.
(c)
It shall be unlawful to operate a tattoo studio or a body piercing establishment within 100 yards of any church building or school building.
(d)
It shall be unlawful to operate a tattoo studio or a body piercing establishment within 100 yards of any property zoned R-1, R-2, R-2D, R-3 or R-4.
(e)
It shall be unlawful to operate a tattoo studio or a body piercing establishment within 100 yards of any adult entertainment business, establishment or facility as described in the city Code.
(Ord. No. 03-05, § 1, 3-10-2003)
Sec. 18-408. - Limitation on number of permits.
Only two permits shall be issued per 1,500 persons living in the city according to the most recent decennial census of the United States as published by the United States Bureau of the Census (in making this calculation, fractions of the required population minimum shall not be counted toward the establishment of an available license).
(Ord. No. 03-05, § 1, 3-10-2003)
Sec. 18-409. - Revocation, suspension.
Any permittee who shall violate any provision of this article shall subject the permit to revocation or suspension upon appropriate notice of hearing. Additionally, revocation of a permit procured by the Clayton County Board of Health or the department of human resources, as appropriate, shall, upon notice of hearing, constitute grounds for revocation of the permit issued by the city.
(Ord. No. 03-05, § 1, 3-10-2003)
Sec. 18-410. - Appeal.
(a)
Should the city determine that grounds for the denial, suspension or revocation of a permit exist under this article, the city shall send by certified mail to the permit holder written notice of the permit suspension or revocation with a statement of the grounds supporting such action. Such suspension or revocation shall be effective beginning three business days from the date of the notice. Any permit holder may request a hearing within ten days of the date of such notice before the city manager for the purpose of presenting evidence and asking for reconsideration of such action. Failure to request such reconsideration within ten days of the date of the notice shall result in a waiver of any appeal rights hereunder.
(b)
If a hearing for reconsideration before the city manager is requested, a hearing shall be held not more than 30 days from the date the written notice requesting such hearing is received by the city manager. Upon the expiration of the 30-day period without a hearing, the permit holder shall be permitted to begin or resume business as a tattoo studio or a body piercing establishment under this statutory provision unless and until such time as the city manager sustains or modifies the original determination. No person or entity conducting business as a tattoo studio or a body piercing establishment under this provision shall acquire any vested rights to continued conduct of such business, and if the city manager later sustains or modifies the original determination, the business must be brought into compliance with this article.
(c)
The city manager may sustain, overrule, or modify the original determination. The decision of the city manager following the hearing on the matter shall be issued within 20 days of the date of the hearing and shall be final. Upon the expiration of the 20-day period without a decision of the city manager being issued on the matter, the permit holder shall be permitted to resume business as a tattoo studio or a body piercing establishment under this statutory provision unless and until such time as the city manager sustains the original determination. No person or entity conducting business as a tattoo studio or a body piercing establishment under this provision shall acquire any vested rights to continued conduct of such business, and if the city manager later sustains or modifies the original determination, the business must be brought into compliance with this article.
(Ord. No. 03-05, § 1, 3-10-2003)
