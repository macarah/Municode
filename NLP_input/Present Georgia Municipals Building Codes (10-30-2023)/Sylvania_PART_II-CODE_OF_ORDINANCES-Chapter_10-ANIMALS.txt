Sec. 10-1. - Poisoning animals.
No person shall put out or cause to be put out any poison within the city for the killing of any dog or other domestic animal.
(Code 1976, § 10-5001)
Sec. 10-2. - Manner of keeping; complaints.
(a)
No person shall keep, maintain or permit any animal or fowl in the city in such manner as to create an unsanitary or obnoxious condition, or to become noisy, offensive or a nuisance to the neighbors or citizens of the city, or to disturb the peace of the city.
(b)
Should a complaint arise from the causes referred to in subsection (a) of this section the person involved will be properly notified and given adequate time to either dispose of the animal or fowl or eliminate the cause of the complaint.
(Code 1976, § 10-5002)
Sec. 10-3. - Vicious animals.
(a)
No owner or other person shall maintain or harbor within the limits of the city a manifestly vicious dog or other animal unless it is securely, but humanely, confined or restrained in such manner as to prevent this dog or other animal from attacking or biting a person or another animal.
(b)
It shall be prima facie evidence of viciousness if a dog or other animal, without provocation, fiercely attacks or bites persons or animals not on the owner's premises.
(Code 1976, § 10-5003)
Sec. 10-4. - Disposal of dead animals.
(a)
No person shall bury the carcass of any dead animal other than household pets anywhere inside the city limits.
(b)
No person owning or interested in any animal which has died shall leave the body or carcass thereof anywhere inside the city limits.
(Code 1976, § 10-5004)
Sec. 10-5. - Bird sanctuary.
(a)
The entire area embraced within the corporate limits of the city is hereby designated as a bird sanctuary.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to trap, hunt, shoot or attempt to shoot or molest in any manner any bird or wildfowl or to rob bird nests or wildfowl nests. However, if starlings or similar birds are found to be congregating in such numbers in a particular locality that they constitute a nuisance or menace to health or property in the opinion of the proper health authorities of the city, the health authorities shall meet with representatives of the Audubon Society, Bird Club, Garden Club or Humane Society, or as many of the clubs as are to be found in the city, after having given the clubs at least three days' actual notice of the time and place of the meeting to the representative of the clubs.
(c)
If as a result of the meeting no satisfactory alternative is found to abate this nuisance, the birds may be destroyed in such numbers and in such manner as is deemed available by the health authorities under supervision of the chief of police.
(d)
Anyone violating this section shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-12.
(Code 1976, § 10-5005)
Sec. 10-6. - Rabies inoculation required.
No person shall keep a dog or cat that is three months old or older unless the person has had the dog or cat inoculated for rabies.
(Code 1976, § 10-5011)
Sec. 10-7. - Dogs, cats to wear inoculation tags.
All dogs or cats inoculated as provided in section 10-6 shall have attached to or affixed about their necks a collar, with a tag attached thereto, stating the date of the inoculation.
(Code 1976, § 10-5012)
Sec. 10-8. - Dogs, cats running at large—Prohibited.
No owner or keeper of any dog or cat shall allow it to run at large upon the streets of the city.
(Code 1976, § 10-5013)
Sec. 10-9. - Same—Impoundment; redemption procedure, fees.
(a)
Any dog or cat running at large shall be caught by the police department and placed in a compound for three days.
(b)
The owners of these dogs or cats may appear within the three days' time and redeem the dogs or cats by paying the required amount per day for board.
(c)
If the dog or cat is not called for within a period of three days, the police department may destroy the dog or cat in a sound, painless manner.
(d)
No dog or cat shall be released unless proof of inoculation for rabies is furnished by the owner.
(e)
The owner of any impounded dog or cat is hereby required to pay the sum of $1.75 per day while his dog or cat is impounded and the additional sum as charged by the person in charge of the pound for the dog's or cat's maintenance. These sums are to be paid to the city clerk.
(Code 1976, § 10-5014)
Sec. 10-10. - Nuisances.
(a)
No person shall allow a dog or cat over which he has custody or control to remain on his property or premises if it shall constitute a nuisance as the term is defined by the law of this state.
(b)
For purposes of this section it shall constitute a nuisance per se for a dog or cat to bark, howl, whine or emit any noises whatsoever in a continuous manner for a period of 30 minutes or longer so as to disturb any occupant of any adjoining or adjacent property within hearing distance of such property.
(Code 1976, § 10-5015; Ord. No. 325, 10-20-92)
Sec. 10-11. - Livestock running at large—Prohibited.
No person shall permit any horses, cattle, sheep, goats or other livestock to run at large on the streets of the city, or to be staked so that they can go upon the streets of the city, or shall drive or lead these animals or livestock along or on the sidewalks in the city, or shall drive or lead these animals or livestock along and over the streets of the city, unless fastened to a rope, chain or other substantial leading device which rope, chain or other substantial leading device is securely held by a responsible person during all the time that these animals or livestock are being led or driven along and over the streets of the city.
(Code 1976, § 10-5021)
Sec. 10-12. - Same—Impoundment.
Any livestock running at large or so staked to go upon the streets, or so driven upon the streets as prohibited in section 10-11 shall be impounded by the city.
(Code 1976, § 10-5022)
Sec. 10-13. - Same—Redemption or sale; disposition of proceeds of sale.
(a)
If any animal impounded as provided in section 10-12 is not claimed and redeemed by the owner, or keeper thereof, within three days after impounding, the chief of police shall advertise it for sale by posting three written or printed notices of this sale, one at the gate or door of the pound and two at any other conspicuous place within the city, for not less than five consecutive days prior to this sale.
(b)
On the day named for this sale, unless the animal shall be redeemed, the chief of police shall sell the animal at public auction to the highest bidder for cash.
(c)
The chief of police shall pay over the proceeds to the city clerk, who shall pay out of these proceeds all expenses, and hold any balance subject to the order of the mayor and council.
(Code 1976, § 10-5023)
Sec. 10-14. - Same—Charges for impounding, selling, etc.
The charges for impounding, keeping and selling livestock as provided in this chapter shall be as follows:
(1)
For taking up and impounding each animal .....$ 5.00
(2)
For feeding each animal, per day .....1.00
(3)
For posting notice for sale .....0.50
(4)
For selling each animal .....0.50
(Code 1976, § 10-5024)
Sec. 10-15. - Same—Records and reports of impoundment, sale, etc.
(a)
The chief of police shall keep a record, in a book to be kept for this purpose, of all livestock impounded, fully describing the animal, the date impounded, the name of the owner, if known, the day of redemption or sale, the penalty, costs and charges due, the amount received and the amount paid the city clerk.
(b)
The chief of police shall present to the city manager monthly an abstract of this record for the preceding month.
(Code 1976, § 10-5025)
Sec. 10-16. - Stables, pens, yards for cows or hogs.
(a)
No person shall erect any stables, pens or yards within the corporate limits of the city for the keeping, storing or selling of cows or hogs.
(b)
No person shall use any stables, pens or yards within the corporate limits of the city for the keeping, storing or selling of cows or hogs, unless this person shall first obtain the approval of the mayor and council as to the location, erection and use of these stables, pens or yards.
(Code 1976, § 10-5026)
Sec. 10-17. - Commercial poultry raising prohibited.
No person shall raise poultry on a commercial basis within the city limits.
(Code 1976, § 10-5031)
Sec. 10-18. - Fowl running at large.
(a)
No owner of chickens or other fowl shall allow them to run at large within the city limits.
(b)
The owner shall keep the chickens or fowl confined to his own premises.
(Code 1976, § 10-5032)
Sec. 10-19. - Tampering with animal control device.
(a)
Definitions.
(1)
Animal control device means an object designed to trap, capture, or restrain an animal.
(2)
Animal control officer means the duly appointed animal control officer of the city, or other person or persons acting under the authority of the animal control officer.
(b)
Damaging and tampering prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any person to damage or to tamper with any animal control device utilized by the animal control officer within the city limits.
(c)
Penalty. Any person who violates the provisions of this section shall be subject to a fine not exceeding five hundred dollars ($500.00) and/or imprisonment not exceeding six (6) months.
(Ord. No. 334, §§ III—V, 6-7-94)
Sec. 10-20. - Tethering of dogs.
(a)
Definitions.
Tethering means attaching a dog to a stationary object by a chain, cable or similar device commonly used for the size and type of dog involved. A dog is not considered tethered when the dog is attached to a stationary object, as long as the owner or custodian is physically within reach of the dog.
(b)
Tethering prohibited. Tethering of a dog is prohibited.
(c)
Running cable or trolley system. A dog may be attached to a running cable or trolley system provided that:
(1)
Only one dog may be attached to each running cable line or trolley system;
(2)
Tethers and cables attaching the animal to the running cable line or trolley system must be made of a substance which cannot be chewed by the animal and shall not weigh more than five percent of the body weight of the animal tethered;
(3)
The running cable line or trolley system must be at least ten feet in length and mounted at least four feet and no more than seven feet above ground level;
(4)
The length of the tether from the running cable line or trolley system to the dog's collar should allow access to the maximum available exercise area and allow the dog free access to food, water and shelter;
(5)
The tethers and cables shall be attached to a properly fitted harness or collar not used for the display of a current rabies tag and other identification, and with enough room between the collar and the dog's throat through which two fingers may fit. Choke collars and pinch collars are prohibited for the purpose of attaching a dog to a running cable line or trolley system;
(6)
The cable or trolley system shall be set up such that the dog is reasonably protected from injury and strangulation;
(7)
A dog may not be attached to a cable or trolley system for more than 12 hours in a 24-hour period.
(Ord. No. 444, §§ I—III, 8-9-07)
