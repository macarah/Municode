Chapter 14 - ELECTIONS

Sec. 14-1. - Qualification for office—Mayor.
To be eligible for election to, and to hold at any time, the office of mayor, a person shall be at least 21 years of age, shall have been a resident of the city for 12 months prior to the date of the election for the office of mayor, and shall be duly registered and qualified to vote in municipal elections of the city and the state.
(Code 1970, § 6-1; Code 1983, § 2-2-1)
Sec. 14-2. - Same—Councilmembers.
To be eligible for election, and to hold at any time, the office of councilmember, a person shall be at least 21 years of age, shall have been a resident of the city for 12 months prior to the date of the election for the office of councilmember, and shall be duly registered and qualified to vote in municipal elections of the city and the state.
(Code 1970, § 6-2; Code 1983, § 2-2-2)
Sec. 14-3. - Election officials.
The mayor and council shall by resolution appoint such election officials as are necessary to comply with the Georgia Municipal Election Code.
(Code 1983, § 2-2-3; Ord. No. 18-2018 , § 1, 11-8-2018)
Sec. 14-4. - Superintendent to incur expenses.
The city is authorized to conduct elections through the Chatham County Board of Elections and pay expenses associated therewith. In the event it is necessary to do so, the election superintendent is authorized to incur expenses in the preparation and conduct of an election, including the cost of purchasing identification badges for poll officers, the printing of ballots and voters' certificates, and the purchasing of all other supplies necessary to conduct an election. The superintendent is further authorized to incur all necessary expenses to provide voting booths for the proper conduct of all elections.
(Code 1970, § 6-4; Code 1983, § 2-2-4; Ord. No. 19-2018 , § 1, 11-8-2018)
Sec. 14-5. - Polling places.
The area within the corporate limits of the city shall constitute an election district within the city. Persons shall vote at such locations as may be established by the municipal elections superintendent or the board of elections. All polling places shall be provided with sufficient light and heat.
(Ord. No. 20-2018 , § 1, 11-8-2018)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 20-2018 , § 1, adopted Nov. 8, 2018, amended § 14-5 in its entirety to read as herein set out. Former § 14-5 pertained to district; polling places, and derived from Code 1970, § 6-5; Code 1983, § 2-2-5; and Ord. of Apr. 27, 2006(1).
Sec. 14-6. - Voter qualifications.
(a)
A person shall be eligible and qualified to vote in any election of the city when he shall be:
(1)
A citizen of the state and of the United States;
(2)
At least 18 years of age;
(3)
A resident of the city;
(4)
Possessed of all other qualifications prescribed by law; and
(5)
Registered as an elector in the manner prescribed by law.
(b)
Notwithstanding any other provision of this section, any person who was not qualified and registered to vote at the time of the adoption of this section shall not be required to re-register under the terms of this section unless that person shall have become or becomes disqualified to vote by reason of having been purged from the list of electors or for any other reason whatsoever, in which event those persons shall, in order to become registered to vote, re-register under the terms of this section.
(Code 1970, § 6-6; Code 1983, § 2-2-6)
Sec. 14-7. - Application for registration; time limit.
A resident of the city who desires to vote in a municipal election must apply to be registered as an elector with the board of registrars of the city at least 30 days prior to that election. The applicant must appear in person before the registrar and must possess those qualifications herein prescribed in order to qualify as an elector of the municipality. The procedure and form of the registration card shall conform to the provisions prescribed by the O.C.G.A. § 21-2-219 et seq.
(Code 1983, § 2-2-7)
Sec. 14-8. - Procedure for challenging registration of a voter.
Any elector of the municipality shall be allowed to challenge the right of registration of any person whose name appears upon the electors list or who is applying for registration with the registrar. The elector making the challenge shall appear before the registrar on or before the election day, and state the grounds for the challenge in writing. The initial decision shall be made forthwith by the registrar with a right of appeal to the governing body of the city. If possible, the registrar will notify the challenged elector and provide him with an opportunity to answer the challenge. If a registrar does not find probable cause for sustaining the challenge, the challenged elector will be given the right to vote or the right to be registered to vote. If in doubt as to the merit of a challenge, the registrar shall notify the chief manager in the challenged elector's election district, and the challenged elector shall be allowed to cast a challenged ballot.
(Code 1970, § 6-8; Code 1983, § 2-2-8)
Sec. 14-9. - Notice of candidacy; time periods.
Each candidate or a designee shall file a notice of candidacy in the office of the municipal clerk of such candidate's candidacy.
(1)
In the case of a general election held in an odd-numbered year as provided by state law as currently existing or as hereinafter amended.
(2)
In the case of a special election, not earlier than the date of the call and at least 25 days prior to the election or as provided by state law as currently existing or as hereinafter amended.
(Code 1983, § 2-2-9; Ord. No. 21-2018 , § 1, 11-8-2018)
Sec. 14-10. - Designation of office sought; election of councilmembers.
A candidate seeking one of two or more public offices each having the same title and to be filled at the same election by the vote of the same electors shall not designate the specific office he is seeking. The candidates for city council receiving the highest number of lawful votes cast shall be elected as councilmembers of the city.
(Code 1970, § 6-11; Code 1983, § 2-2-10; Ord. No. 22-2018 , § 1, 11-8-2018)
Sec. 14-11. - Election to be nonpartisan.
All elections held for the purpose of filling municipal offices in the city shall be nonpartisan, and no political party shall conduct primaries for the purpose of nominating candidates for any municipal elections.
(Code 1970, § 6-12; Code 1983, § 2-2-11)
Sec. 14-12. - Plurality required to elect; election of mayor.
A candidate for municipal office shall be elected in a general or special election by receiving a plurality of the votes cast to fill that public office. The candidate for mayor receiving the highest number of lawful votes cast shall be elected as mayor.
(Code 1970, § 6-13; Code 1983, § 2-2-12)
Sec. 14-13. - Tie votes; runoff election.
In event of a tie vote in any election between two or more candidates for any office, whether mayor or councilmember, a new election between only the candidates thus tied in number of votes for the particular office shall be ordered held by the mayor and council on the 14th day after the holding of the first election in accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 21-2-501, and the person who shall receive the highest number of votes cast in the second or runoff election, shall be declared to have been elected to the office.
(Code 1970, § 6-14; Code 1983, § 2-2-13)
Sec. 14-14. - Secret ballots; use of voting machines.
All votes cast in every municipal election held in the city whether that election be regular, special, general or other shall be by secret ballot; but the ballots or votes in any such municipal election may, at the option of the mayor and council be cast, registered, recorded and counted by means of voting machines meeting the requirements of voting machines that are allowed to be used as provided by the provisions of statute.
(Code 1970, § 6-15; Code 1983, § 2-2-14)
Sec. 14-15. - Absentee ballots.
Absentee ballots shall be used in all municipal elections. The use of absentee ballots shall be governed by O.C.G.A. § 21-2-280 et seq.
(Code 1970, § 6-16; Code 1983, § 2-2-15)
Sec. 14-16. - Contested elections.
The procedure for contested elections shall be as provided by state law.
(Code 1970, § 6-17; Code 1983, § 2-2-16)
Sec. 14-17. - Qualifying fee.
A qualifying fee shall be paid to the municipal city clerk or a designee at the time a candidate files the candidate's notice of candidacy. Such fee shall be three percent of the annual salary of the office if a salaried office.
(Code 1983, § 2-2-17)
State Law reference— Qualification fees generally, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-131.
Sec. 14-18. - Time of regular election; succession by incumbent.
On the first Tuesday in November of each odd-numbered year, beginning in 1983, a regular election shall be held in the city or at such place or places as may be designated for the election of a mayor, when appropriate, and/or council members, as appropriate. Any incumbent mayor or incumbent council member who shall qualify as a candidate and meet the qualifications to hold such office may be a candidate to succeed himself for office or for office as a member of the city council.
(Code 1983, § 2-2-18; Ord. No. 23-2018 , § 1, 11-8-2018)
