ARTICLE VI. - TURNING MOVEMENTS

Sec. 20-6.1. - Reserved.
Editor's note— Section 20-6.1 was repealed by Ord. No. 78-65, § 1, enacted May 23, 1978. Said section pertained to the required position and method of turning and was derived from Ord. No. 71-198, § 7, adopted July 20, 1971, and Ord. No. 72-12, §§ 1—3, adopted Jan. 25, 1972.
Sec. 20-6.2. - Authority to place and obedience to turning markers.
(a)
The city traffic engineer is authorized to place markers, buttons, or signs within or adjacent to intersections indicating the course to be traveled by vehicles turning at such intersections and such course to be traveled as so indicated may conform to or be other than as prescribed by law or ordinance.
(b)
When authorized markers, buttons, or other indications are placed within an intersection indicating the course to be traveled by vehicles turning thereat, no driver of a vehicle shall disobey the directions of such indications.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 7, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-6.3. - Authority to place restricted turn signs.
The city traffic engineer is hereby authorized to determine those intersections at which drivers of vehicles shall not make a right, left or U turn, and shall place proper signs at such intersections. The making of such turns may be prohibited between certain hours of any day and permitted at other hours, in which event the same shall be plainly indicated on the signs or they may be removed when such turns are permitted.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 7, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-6.4. - Obedience to no-turn signs.
Whenever authorized signs are erected indicating that no right or left or U turn is permitted, no driver of a vehicle shall disobey the directions of any such sign.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 7, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-6.5. - Limitations on turning around.
The driver of any vehicle shall not turn such vehicle so as to proceed in the opposite direction upon any street in a business district and shall not upon any other street so turn a vehicle unless such movement can be made in safety and without interfering with other traffic.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 7, 7-20-71)

Sec. 20-6.8. - "U" Turns.
(a)
Generally. No driver of a vehicle shall make a "U" turn, that is a turn of 180 degrees or more, in the streets of the city. This provision shall not prohibit "U" turns at street intersections except at those street intersections where "U" turns are specifically prohibited by ordinance of the city.
(b)
Veterans Parkway. It shall be unlawful for any person to make U-turn maneuvers along the following described portions of the Veterans Parkway in the city; as described herewith:
(1)
Southbound Veterans Parkway at Airport Thruway/54th Street.
(2)
Northbound Veterans Parkway at Whitesville Road.
(3)
All median openings along Veterans Parkway between Airport Thruway/54th Street and Whitesville Road.
The U-turn prohibitions described in this subsection shall apply only on Friday, Saturday and Sunday nights between the hours of 7 p.m. and 1 a.m.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 7, 7-20-71; Ord. No. 91-30, §§ 1, 2, 5-7-91)
Sec. 20-6.9. - Left turns prohibited on certain streets.
(a)
Designated streets. It shall be unlawful for any driver of a vehicle to make a left turn movement at the intersection of Farr Road and Ford Drive in the following described directions:
(1)
Northbound Farr Road.
(2)
Eastbound Ford Drive.
(b)
Traffic-control signs. Official traffic-control signs indicating "No Left Turn" shall be erected along the designated areas described in subsection (a) herein.
Editor's note— Ordinance No. 88-90, adopted Sept. 20, 1988, did not specifically amend this Code; hence, inclusion of §§ 1, 2 as § 20-6.9 was at the discretion of the editor.
