ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 18-1. - Applicability of state law.
All elections shall be held and conducted in accordance with the Georgia Election Code, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq., as now or hereafter amended.
(Code 2004, § 2-101; Ord. No. O-23-05, § 2-101, 6-23-2005)
Sec. 18-2. - Election district; polling place.
The area comprising the corporate limits of the city, as the same is now or shall hereafter exist, shall constitute the sole election district of the city. Those persons living within the election district shall vote at the city hall or such other place in the city as the city council shall direct and designate.
(Code 2004, § 2-102; Ord. No. O-23-05, § 2-102, 6-23-2005)
Sec. 18-3. - Regular elections; time for holding.
(a)
The city council shall cause an election to be held at the city hall or such other place in the city as the city council shall direct and designate. All general municipal elections shall be held as provided by law, biennially on the Tuesday next following the first Monday in November of each year.
(b)
There shall be elected the mayor and two councilmembers at one election, and at every other election thereafter, the election cycle set forth herein shall be perpetuated. The remaining city councilmembers' seats shall be filled at the election alternating with the first election so that a continuing body is created, Charter section 2.11.
(Ord. No. O-23-05, § 2-103, 6-23-2005)
Sec. 18-4. - Election by plurality.
The mayor and each councilmember shall be elected at large by the voters of the entire municipality by simple plurality of the votes cast.
(Ord. No. O-23-05, § 2-104, 6-23-2005)
State Law reference— Date of municipal elections generally, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-9(b).
Sec. 18-5. - Nonpartisan elections.
Political parties shall not conduct primaries for city offices and all names of candidates for city offices shall be listed without party designation, Charter section 5.12.
(Ord. No. O-23-05, § 2-105, 6-23-2005)
Sec. 18-6. - Vacancies; special election.
Vacancies shall be filled pursuant to section 2.12 of the Charter. Any required special election shall be held and conducted in accordance with the Georgia Election Code, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq., as now or hereafter amended.
(Ord. No. O-23-05, § 2-106, 6-23-2005)
Sec. 18-7. - Expenses.
Such funds as are necessary for the conduct of elections and for the performance of the duties that are specified by this chapter shall be budgeted and appropriated annually, and from time to time.
(Ord. No. O-23-05, § 2-105, 6-23-2005)
Secs. 18-8—18-32. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - REGISTRATION

Sec. 18-33. - Electors to be registered by city.
Pursuant to the authority provided in O.C.G.A. § 21-2-45 et seq., the city elects to use the county voter registration system for the registration of electors in city elections. Any person who is a resident of the city and who is registered as an elector with the city shall be eligible to vote in any municipal primary or election.
(Code 2004, § 2-201; Ord. No. O-23-05, § 2-201, 6-23-2005)
Secs. 18-34—18-54. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - ELECTION OFFICIALS

Sec. 18-55. - Municipal election superintendent.
The municipal election superintendent shall be appointed by the mayor and council. The municipal election superintendent shall serve at the pleasure of the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. O-23-05, § 3-301, 6-23-2005)
State Law reference— Powers, duties, and appointment of municipal elections superintendent, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-70.1.
Secs. 18-56—18-83. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - IMPEACHMENT AND REMOVAL

Sec. 18-84. - Removal of mayor and councilmembers from office.
The mayor, councilmembers, or other appointed officers shall be removed from office for any one or more of the causes provided in O.C.G.A. § 45-11-1 et seq., or such other applicable laws as are or may hereafter be enacted.
(Ord. No. O-23-05, § 4-401, 6-23-2005)
