ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

ARTICLE II. - LOCAL PUBLIC HEALTH REGULATIONS

Sec. 27-30. - State promulgated rules and regulations.
The following rules and regulations of the Georgia Department of Public Health (DPH), including all subsequent amendments, revisions or modifications thereto, and which are expressly incorporated herein, are hereby adopted and approved for enforcement as set forth herein:
(1)
On-Site Sewage Management System Rules and Regulations (Chapter 511-3-1)
(2)
Food Service Rules and Regulations (Chapter 511-6-1)
(3)
Tourist Accommodations Rules and Regulations (Chapter 511-6-2)
(4)
Public Swimming Pools, Spas and Recreation Water Parks Rules and Regulations (Chapter 511-3-5)
(5)
Rabies Control Rules and Regulations (Chapter 511-2-7)
(6)
Solid Waste Rules and Regulations (Chapter 511-3-4)
(7)
The Georgia Smoke Free Air Act Rules and Regulations (Chapter 511-3-7)
(8)
Drinking Water Supply Rules and Regulations (Chapter 511-3-3)
(9)
Portable Sanitation Contractors Rules and Regulations (Chapter 511-3-6)
Copies of those DPH rules and regulations are on file in the office of the city clerk, where the same are available for inspection and copying by the public-during regular city operating hours. ·
(Ord. No. 2018-CO-02-757, § 1(Exh. A), 3-12-2018)
Sec. 27-31. - Adopted by reference—Local Board of Health Enactments.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 31-3-1, et seq., in addition to the above-referenced rules and regulations of the Georgia DPH, the following rules and regulations have been enacted by the Hall County Board of Health and are hereby adopted by the City Council for the City of Oakwood in accordance with state law and are further hereby adopted and approved for enforcement as set forth herein:
(1)
Rules and regulations concerning Operational Permits and Maintenance Requirements for Advanced Treatment Systems, enacted by resolution dated August 25, 2005.
(2)
Rules and regulations concerning On-Site Water Well Supplies, enacted by resolution dated November 25, 2008.
(3)
Rules and regulations concerning Body Art, enacted by resolution dated November 25, 2008.
(4)
Rules and regulations concerning Rabies Control, enacted by resolution dated July 27, 2000.
Copies of those rules and regulations enacted by the Hall County Board of Health and having been adopted by ordinance of the City Council for the City of Oakwood are on file in the office of the city clerk, where the same are available for inspection and copying by the public during regular city operating hours.
(Ord. No. 2018-CO-02-757, § 1(Exh. A), 3-12-2018)
Sec. 27-32. - Conflict.
Should any provision of the rules and regulations adopted in sections 30 and 31 above conflict with any other provision of the Official City Code of the City of Oakwood, Georgia, that provision which is most restrictive shall control.
(Ord. No. 2018-CO-02-757, § 1(Exh. A), 3-12-2018)
Sec. 27-33. - Rules and regulations of minimum lot sizes for on-site sewage management systems.
In addition, the rules and regulations concerning minimum lot sizes for the orderly and safe development of property utilizing on-site sewage management systems, having been enacted by the Hall County Board of Health are hereby adopted and made a part of this ordinance as though fully set out at length herein. The City of Oakwood may, however, require larger minimum lot sizes for utilization of on-site sewage management systems in zoning or land use districts providing for septic service and, in the event there is a conflict between the minimum lot size(s) established by the Hall County Board of Health and that established by the City of Oakwood, the larger lot size(s) established by the City of Oakwood shall control and take precedence.
(Ord. No. 2018-CO-02-757, § 1(Exh. A), 3-12-2018)
Sec. 27-34. - Fee schedule.
A fee schedule for the implementation and confirmation of compliance with the above-referenced State Promulgated Rules and Regulations and Local Board of Health Enactments, including permit and inspection fees, has been established by the Hall County Board of Health, and is hereby approved and adopted by the City of Oakwood as part of the fees set forth in chapter 22 hereof.
(Ord. No. 2018-CO-02-757, § 1(Exh. A), 3-12-2018)
Sec. 27-35. - Enforcement.
In addition to any other provision of the adopted DPH rules and regulations, the enforcement and prosecution of this chapter shall be within the jurisdiction of the City of Oakwood and the Hall County Board of Health and their authorized designee(s) or agent(s), and all law enforcement personnel of the city.
(Ord. No. 2018-CO-02-757, § 1(Exh. A), 3-12-2018)
Sec. 27-36. - Prohibition.
It shall be unlawful for any natural person or persons or any legal entity including, and not limited to, a corporation, partnership, limited liability company, firm or trust, to violate any of the above-identified rules and regulations or fail or refuse to pay any fee established by the Hall County Board of Health and approved and adopted by the City of Oakwood, for the implementation and confirmation of compliance with the above-identified rules and regulations, including all permit and inspection fees.
(Ord. No. 2018-CO-02-757, § 1(Exh. A), 3-12-2018)
Sec. 27-37. - Violation.
Any person, firm, corporation, association or partnership violating any provision of this chapter as the same exists or as it may hereinafter be amended or shall fail to do anything required by this chapter as the same exists or as it may hereafter be amended, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, amenable to the process of the Municipal Court of the City of Oakwood, and upon conviction, shall be punished as provided in section 1-11 the Oakwood City Code for each violation in the discretion of the court. Each day such violation is committed or permitted to continue shall constitute a separate offense and shall be punishable as such hereunder.
(Ord. No. 2018-CO-02-757, § 1(Exh. A), 3-12-2018)
Sec. 27-38. - Abatement.
In addition to the rights and remedies set forth herein, or as otherwise authorized by law, the City of Oakwood and the Hall County Board of Health retain and reserve the right to seek equitable relief, injunction, abatement or any appropriate action or proceeding to protect the public health, safety and welfare. Failure or refusal to pay any fee approved for the implementation and confirmation of compliance with the above-identified rules and regulations, including all permit and inspection fees, may result in suspension and/or revocation of any permit issued by the Hall County Board of Health.
(Ord. No. 2018-CO-02-757, § 1(Exh. A), 3-12-2018)
Sec. 27-39. - Compliance.
This article and the enforcement and prosecution of this article by the City of Oakwood and the Hall County Board of Health and their authorized agent(s) or designee(s) does not in any way affect, estop, prescribe, regulate, limit or in any way control or prohibit the Georgia OHR and its agent(s) or designee(s) from initiating or undertaking any action or proceeding to enforce compliance with its rules and regulations.
(Ord. No. 2018-CO-02-757, § 1(Exh. A), 3-12-2018)
Secs. 27-40—27-69. - Reserved.
