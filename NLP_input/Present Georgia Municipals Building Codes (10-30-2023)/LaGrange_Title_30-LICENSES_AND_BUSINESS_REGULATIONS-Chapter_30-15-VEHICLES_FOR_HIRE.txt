Article I - IN GENERAL

Sec. 30-15-1. - Definitions.
As used in this chapter, the following terms shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them in this section:
Ambulance: Any privately owned vehicle equipped or used for transporting the wounded, injured, sick and dead, and shall include emergency vehicles used for such purposes, but shall specifically exclude funeral coaches and hearses.
Bus terminal or bus passenger station: Only the drive-in terminal or station of a bus company.
Chauffeur's permit: The written authority given by the chief of police pursuant to the provisions of this chapter to any person to drive a taxicab on the public streets of the city.
Taxicab: A motor-driven vehicle for the transportation of passengers for hire, which is operated wholly or principally within the city, or which picks up and discharges passengers within the city, and which is not operated over any fixed route. This definition applies to taxicabs only and shall have no effect upon section 30-15-21.
(Code 1972, § 25-1)
State Law reference— Definitions relating to emergency medical services, O.C.G.A. § 31-11-2.
Sec. 30-15-2. - Driving or parking vehicle on property of another commercial establishment.
It shall be unlawful for any person owning or operating a vehicle for the transportation of passengers for hire to enter or drive or place any such vehicle on or upon premises, grounds, driveways or areas of another used for and in connection with the operation of a railroad or bus station or depot or a rooming house, hotel, motel, motor hotel, tourist hotel, tourist camp, tourist court, motor court or trailer camp or court, without the prior consent of the operator of such station, depot, rooming house, hotel, motel, motor hotel, tourist hotel, tourist camp, tourist court, motor court or trailer camp or court.
(Code 1972, § 25-2)
Sec. 30-15-3. - Soliciting passengers on property of another.
It shall be unlawful for any person operating a vehicle for the transportation of passengers for hire to go upon the private premises of any person for the purpose of soliciting passengers, without first obtaining the consent of the owner or tenant of such premises, or to pick up or discharge passengers thereon when forbidden so to do by the owner or tenant.
(Code 1972, § 25-3)
Sec. 30-15-4. - Providing off-street terminal.
All persons operating inter-city buses into and out of this city shall provide a place on private property, off of the public streets, for the purpose of loading and unloading passengers.
(Code 1972, § 25-5)
Sec. 30-15-5. - Parking buses on streets.
All persons operating inter-city buses into, out of or through the city are hereby prohibited from using the public streets of the city for the purpose of parking such buses while stopping in the city.
(Code 1972, § 25-7)
Sec. 30-15-6. - Taking on or discharging passengers.
It shall be unlawful for the operators of buses operating into and out of the city to stop on any of the streets within the city for the purpose of taking on or discharging passengers, or at any place except at their respective terminals or passenger stations.
(Code 1972, § 25-6)
Secs. 30-15-7—30-15-20. - Reserved.
Article II - TAXICABS AND LIMOUSINES
Division 1 - GENERALLY

Sec. 30-15-21. - Application to limousines.
The regulations in this chapter applicable to taxicabs shall also apply to limousines.
Sec. 30-15-22. - Notice of violations; revocation, suspension of license.
The license issued pursuant to this article may be suspended or revoked by the mayor and council after reasonable notice to the licensee and an opportunity for a hearing thereon upon the violation by the licensee, owner, operator, or driver of a taxicab of any laws or ordinances applicable to or regulating such business, violation of regulations made pursuant to authority granted for the purpose of regulating such business, or for the violation of any provision of this article or the traffic laws of the Code of the City of LaGrange.
(Code 1972, § 25-42; Ord. No. 96-62, § 1, 12-19-96)
Sec. 30-15-23. - Licenses.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to carry on, engage in, conduct or operate a taxicab business within the city without a current, unrevoked license from the city, and having first paid any business occupation tax and/or regulatory license fee applicable thereto.
(b)
All licenses issued under this article shall constitute a mere grant or privilege to carry on or conduct a business covered by such license during the term of the license, subject to the terms and conditions imposed by the Charter of the city, this article, and other ordinances of the city and the constitution, laws and regulations of the state and the United States of America applicable thereto.
(Code 1972, § 25-22; Ord. No. 96-62, § 2, 12-19-96)
Sec. 30-15-24. - Insurance—Required; amounts; placard.
It shall be unlawful for any person to drive or operate a taxicab on the public streets of the city unless the operator shall first have secured and filed with the assistant city manager a liability insurance policy, issued by a good and reliable insurance company. Said insurance company must have the authority to do and conduct business within the state and must have been approved by the mayor and council. The liability insurance for each taxicab shall be in an amount not less than the minimum limits allowed by state law for automobiles. Said policy of insurance may be in the form of a "fleet policy" covering all taxicabs operated by such person, in which latter event said policy of insurance shall provide at least the same minimum liability coverage for each taxicab operated in service. Every taxicab while in service shall have conspicuously placed therein some sign evidencing the fact that the provisions of this section have been complied with, the form thereof to be prescribed by the chief of police.
(Code 1972, § 25-23; Ord. No. 07-12, § 7, 4-24-07; Ord. No. 16-12, § 8, 12-13-16)
State Law reference— Motor vehicle accident insurance coverage, O.C.G.A. § 33-34-4; "proof of financial responsibility" defined, O.C.G.A. § 40-9-2(5).
Sec. 30-15-25. - Same—Cancellation notice.
No policy of insurance for taxicabs shall be cancelled until the expiration of thirty (30) days' notice of such intention to cancel has been given in writing to the assistant city manager by registered mail or personal delivery, and a provision therefor shall be made a part of the policy of insurance.
(Code 1972, § 25-24; Ord. No. 96-62, § 3, 12-19-96; Ord. No. 07-12, § 7, 4-24-07; Ord. No. 16-12, § 8, 12-13-16)
Sec. 30-15-26. - Terminal and headquarters—Required; parking on streets.
All persons engaged in the taxicab business in the city shall have drive-in terminals or stations at which all vehicles shall be parked while not engaged in the actual transportation of persons. No taxicab shall be left parked on the streets of the city.
(Code 1972, § 25-31)
Sec. 30-15-27. - Same—Headquarters; separate license for each terminal.
All persons engaged in the business of operating taxicabs shall have a designated place of business or headquarters, the location of which shall be stated in the application for license when filed, and upon the issuance, such license shall be good for only one (1) place of business. The location of such headquarters or terminal must be approved by the mayor and council and a license fee must be paid for each terminal or business location.
(Code 1972, § 25-32; Ord. No. 92-20, § 1, 6-23-92)
Sec. 30-15-28. - Taxicab markings and lettering.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a taxicab in the city unless such taxicab shall be equipped as follows:
(1)
The name of the entity, enterprise or company shall be painted or affixed by decal to and shall appear on the left and right front doors of all vehicles operated as taxicabs;
(2)
A dome light shall be permanently affixed to the top of any vehicle operated as a taxicab, bearing the term "taxi" or the name of the entity, business or company operating the taxi business. The sign shall be lighted at all times while the headlights of such vehicle are required to be displayed pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 40-8-20;
(3)
Each vehicle operated as a taxicab by licensee shall either have painted or affixed by decal an identifying number, unique to each vehicle, on the left and right rear quarter panel and on the right-hand side of the most vertical surface of the rear trunk.
(Code 1972, § 25-33; Ord. No. 96-62, § 4, 12-19-96)
Sec. 30-15-29. - Stopping in traffic lanes.
It shall be unlawful for any driver of a taxicab to stop his vehicle in a traffic lane on the streets of the city for the purpose of receiving, discharging or waiting for passengers.
(Code 1972, § 25-34)
State Law reference— Stopping, standing and parking, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-200 et seq.
Sec. 30-15-30. - Cruising.
It shall be unlawful for any taxicab driver to cruise the streets of the city for the purpose of soliciting business.
(Code 1972, § 25-35)
Sec. 30-15-31. - Number of passengers; compliance with seatbelt and child restraint laws.
The operators and drivers of taxicabs in the city are authorized to carry that number of passengers equal to the number of seatbelts available for passenger use. It shall be the responsibility of the operators and drivers of taxicabs to insure that passengers comply with all applicable seatbelt and child restraint laws.
(Code 1972, § 25-36; Ord. No. 97-9, § 2, 2-25-97)
Sec. 30-15-32. - Use of vehicle for lewdness, etc.
It shall be unlawful for any driver of a taxicab operating within the city to permit any person to occupy or use such taxicab for the purpose of prostitution, lewdness or assignation, or to direct, take or transport, or offer to take, direct or transport, any person with knowledge or reasonable cause to know the purpose of such directing, taking or transporting is for prostitution, lewdness or assignation.
(Code 1972, § 25-37)
State Law reference— Public indecency, O.C.G.A. § 16-6-8; prostitution, O.C.G.A. § 16-6-9; pimping O.C.G.A. § 16-6-11.
Sec. 30-15-33. - Drivers' hours.
It shall be unlawful for any individual to drive a taxicab for more than twelve (12) hours in any one (1) day.
(Code 1972, § 25-38)
Sec. 30-15-34. - Type of vehicle; maintenance; inspection.
To insure the safety of taxicab passengers and other motorists on the streets of the city, the chief of police, or his designee, shall inspect each taxicab licensed hereunder on an annual basis, the regulatory fee for such inspection being twenty-five dollars ($25.00) per inspection. Such inspection shall include the following:
(1)
Interior and exterior vehicle. The interior and exterior of each vehicle shall remain in a clean condition and be free of foreign matter and offensive odor and shall present a clean environment for passengers.
(2)
Equipment and/or conditions. Headlights, tail lights, signal lights, brake lights, interior lights, exhaust system, muffler, tires (including spare tire), vehicle glass including windshield, steering and brakes.
(3)
It is the intent of this article that any vehicle used as a taxicab will adhere to the minimum requirements and regulations contained in the Code of the City of LaGrange and the laws of the State of Georgia. Vehicle defects disclosed in the above-referenced inspection shall be repaired by licensee immediately. Depending on the nature of the inspection failure or defect, in the discretion of the chief of police, a licensee may be allowed to continue to operate the vehicle as a taxicab prior to reinspection and confirmation that the defect has been corrected. However, in the discretion of the chief of police, should the defect or inspection failure be deemed to significantly affect the safety of the public or taxicab passengers, licensee will not be allowed to operate the vehicle as a taxicab on the streets of the city until the defects disclosed in the inspection are repaired, and the vehicle is reinspected and approved for use by the chief of police.
(4)
Pursuant to the regulatory power exercised herein, the operator of any taxicab licensed or permitted hereunder shall be subject to periodic unscheduled inspections by the city police department to determine compliance with the provisions of this article. Anyone operating a taxicab within the city limits and subject to the regulations exercised herein shall permit such reasonable, timely and appropriate inspections, the denial of same being grounds for revocation of the applicable license, chauffeur's permit, or both.
(Code 1972, § 25-39; Ord. No. 96-62, § 5, 12-19-96)
State Law reference— Equipment and inspection of motor vehicles, O.C.G.A. § 40-8-1 et seq.
Sec. 30-15-35. - Fares—Schedule.
(a)
The per passenger charge for all persons transported in taxicabs in the city, excluding children age four (4) and under who are in a child passenger restraining system as defined in O.C.G.A. § 40-8-76, and who are in the charge of a nurse, parent or adult person in loco parentis shall, for groups of two (2) or more be a maximum of five dollars ($5.00) for the first passenger and one dollar ($1.00) for each additional passenger and otherwise shall be five dollars ($5.00) per passenger. These rates shall be effective twenty-four (24) hours per day, seven (7) days per week.
(b)
Each taxicab in operation in the city must have posted continuously in plain view of the passengers riding therein, the rates currently being charged by such taxicab.
(Code 1972, § 35-40; Ord. No. 96-7, § 1, 2-13-96; Ord. No. 97-9, § 1, 2-25-97; Ord. No. 05-16, § 1, 10-11-05; Ord. No. 08-17, § 1, 7-8-08)
Sec. 30-15-36. - Same—Posting in vehicle; compliance.
Each taxicab shall have posted in a conspicuous place therein a schedule of rates for the transportation of persons within the city. No charge for transportation of any person within the city shall be made in excess of the rates so posted. Said schedule of rates shall be within full view of the passengers of such taxicab, and displayed in such a manner as to be easily ascertained and understood by the passengers.
(Code 1972, § 25-41)
Sec. 30-15-37. - Smoking prohibited.
Smoking shall be prohibited in all taxicabs operating within the City of LaGrange. For purposes of this code section, the term "smoking" shall have such meaning as is established in the Georgia Smoke-Free Air Act of 2005, as amended.
(Ord. No. 06-07, § 1, 4-25-06)
Secs. 30-15-38—30-15-50. - Reserved.
Division 2 - PERMIT TO ENGAGE IN BUSINESS

Sec. 30-15-51. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate or cause to be operated in the city any taxicab unless he possesses a current, unrevoked, written permit for the operation thereof issued by the city through the mayor and council.
(Code 1972, § 25-18)
Sec. 30-15-52. - Application.
An application for a permit under this division shall be made to the mayor and council by petition, which shall set forth the name, address and place of business of the applicant, if an individual, and if a partnership, the name and address and place of business of each partner, and the place of business, and if a corporation, the name and address of each of the officers of such corporation and the place of business of such corporation; the trade name under which the applicant does or proposes to do business; the name of the insurance company with which the applicant has made arrangements to secure his insurance as required by the provisions set forth in this chapter, together with the financial statement of such insurance company; a color scheme desired to be used by the applicant; and such other information as may be required or requested by the mayor and council.
(Code 1972, § 25-19)
Sec. 30-15-53. - Investigation.
The mayor and council shall make or cause to be made an investigation, including any hearing deemed necessary, as to each application for permit for the operation of taxicabs. In determining whether a permit shall be issued, the mayor and council may investigate the fitness of the applicant to engage in the business of operating taxicabs, and shall make or cause to be made an investigation in order to determine whether the demands of public convenience, necessity and safety shall justify the issuance of a permit.
(Code 1972, § 25-20)
Sec. 30-15-54. - Issuance; denial; transfer.
If the mayor and council decide that a permit for the operation of taxicabs shall be granted, they shall grant such permit to the applicant under the provisions set forth in this chapter and the ordinances of the city. Such permit shall not be transferable except upon application to and consent by the mayor and council, and any person operating under such transfer shall not undertake such operation until final approval and transfer has been approved by the mayor and council, and completed, and a new license issued. Should the mayor and council find that the public convenience, necessity and safety do not justify the issuance of such permit, or that the insurance company with or in which the applicant has made arrangements to secure insurance as set forth in this chapter is not a good and responsible company, or that the provisions of the proposed policy do not give ample protection to the public, or that the applicant is not a fit person to conduct the taxicab business, no permit shall be issued, and notice of the action taken by the mayor and council setting forth the reason for the refusal to issue a permit shall be given to the applicant.
(Code 1972, § 25-21)
Sec. 30-15-55. - Miscellaneous provisions.
(a)
The operator of any taxicab licensed hereunder shall display inside the vehicle at all times during the operation of the vehicle as a taxicab the valid chauffeur permit of the vehicle operator. The operator shall retain within said vehicle at all times during operation the following information: the current occupational tax receipt and/or regulatory license(s) and proof of insurance as required in this article.
(b)
It shall be the responsibility of each taxicab driver, operator or owner, upon finding personal property of a passenger, to immediately return such personal property to its owner, if known, and if not known to immediately forward such property to the city police department.
(Ord. No. 96-62, § 6, 12-19-96; Ord. No. 05-16, § 2, 10-11-05)
Secs. 30-15-56—30-15-65. - Reserved.
Division 3 - CHAUFFEUR'S PERMIT

Sec. 30-15-66. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to drive a taxicab on the streets of the city without a current, unrevoked chauffeur's permit from the chief of police.
(Code 1972, § 25-25)
Sec. 30-15-67. - Application.
Any person being eighteen (18) years of age or older may make application to the chief of police for a chauffeur's permit. It shall be the duty of the chief of police to make an investigation of the applicant and the knowledge of the applicant of the traffic regulations of the city and the motor vehicle laws of the state. The chief may issue a chauffeur's permit if he finds that said applicant possesses sufficient knowledge of the traffic regulations and laws. Said permit shall be in writing, signed by the chief of police, and shall bear the corporate seal of the city. Said permit shall state the name, address, sex, race and age, and shall have the signature, of the applicant. The chief of police shall cause to be secured, before the issuance of the permit, a copy of the fingerprints of the applicant, and two (2) photographs. Copies of the fingerprints and photograph shall be retained in the files of the police department if said permit is granted. The applicant shall pay the sum of five dollars ($5.00) to the chief of police upon issuance of said chauffeur's permit, which fund shall be remitted to the city.
(Code 1972, § 25-26; Ord. No. 04-02, § 1, 1-27-04)
Sec. 30-15-68. - Disqualification.
A permit shall not be issued under this division to any person who was imprisoned for a felony and has been released within the last three (3) years.
(Code 1972, § 25-27; Ord. No. 02-01, § 1, 1-8-02)
Sec. 30-15-69. - Defacing, etc.
It shall be unlawful for any person holding a chauffeur's permit to deface, obliterate, change or destroy the same, or cause the same to be obliterated, defaced, changed or destroyed.
(Code 1972, § 25-28)
Sec. 30-15-70. - Display.
Every driver of a taxicab in the city shall have his chauffeur's permit displayed in such taxicab in a prominent place at all times so that said permit shall be easily discernible by any passenger of such taxicab.
(Code 1972, § 25-29)
Sec. 30-15-71. - Revocation; suspension; renewal.
(a)
Chauffeurs' permits shall be renewed annually, not later than January 1, regardless of the date of issuance.
(b)
The mayor and council, upon recommendation of the chief of police or upon motion of one (1) of its members, may suspend or revoke any chauffeur's permit issued by the chief of police, if upon investigation or knowledge, they shall find that the holder of said permit has violated any of the provisions of this chapter, or has been convicted of driving a taxicab while under the influence of intoxicating liquor or drugs, or if said person is suffering from a contagious or communicable disease or defect or impairment of vision or other physical handicap that interferes with his ability to perform adequately his duties as a chauffeur under this chapter, or if said person is not otherwise qualified as described in this article. Before such permit shall be suspended or revoked, notice of intention to revoke or suspend shall be served upon the holder either by registered mail or personal delivery, and said person, if he so desires, shall be given an opportunity to be heard at a hearing conducted by the mayor and council.
(Code 1972, § 25-30)
State Law reference— Discrimination against the handicapped in employment, O.C.G.A. § 34-6A-1 et seq.
Secs. 30-15-72—30-15-79. - Reserved.

Sec. 30-15-80. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the following terms shall have the respective meanings described to them in this section:
Advanced life support means the application of procedures and/or administration of drugs which are included in the cardiac technician or paramedic course as approved by the department of human resources.
Ambulance service means the providing of emergency care and transportation on the public streets and highways of this city for a wounded, injured, sick, invalid or incapacitated human being to or from a place where medical or hospital care is furnished.
Cardiac technician means a person who, having been trained and certified as an emergency medical technician and having completed additional training in advanced cardiac life support techniques in a training course approved by the department of human resources, is so certified by the composite state board of medical examiners.
Emergency medical technician means a person who has been certified by the department of human resources of the State of Georgia after having successfully completed emergency medical care training approved by that department.
Paramedic means any person who has been certified as an advanced emergency medical technician by the composite state board of medical examiners before July 1, 1988, or any person who has been certified by that board on or after July 1, 1988, as having been trained in emergency care techniques in a paramedic training course approved by the department of human resources, but all such persons shall be designated on or after July 1, 1988, as paramedics.
Person means any individual, firm, partnership, association, corporation, company, group of individuals acting together for a common purpose, or organization of any kind, including any governmental agency other than that of the United States.
(Ord. No. 96-55, § 1, 10-22-96)
Sec. 30-15-81. - Applicability of article generally.
Except as provided in section 30-15-82 hereof, the provisions of this article shall apply to all emergency ambulances operated within the city, but shall not apply to vehicles operated solely for non-emergency services.
(Ord. No. 96-55, § 1, 10-22-96)
Sec. 30-15-82. - Exclusions.
This article shall not apply to ambulance calls for service originating outside of the city limits, and all ambulances carrying persons from outside such city limits to a point within the city or through the city shall not be subject thereto.
(Ord. No. 96-55, § 1, 10-22-96)
Sec. 30-15-83. - Requirement of franchise.
No person shall operate an ambulance or ambulance service on the streets of the city without first obtaining from the mayor and council of the city a franchise as herein provided.
(Ord. No. 96-55, § 1, 10-22-96)
Sec. 30-15-84. - Application; requirements for issuance of franchise.
An application for a franchise under this article shall be made with the assistant city manager by petition, which shall set forth the name, address and place of business of the applicant, if an individual, and if a partnership the name and address and place of business of each partner, and the place of business, and if a corporation, the name and address of each of the officers of the corporation, and the place of business of such corporation; the trade name under which the applicant does or proposes to do business; the name of the insurance company with which the applicant has made arrangements to secure insurance as required by the provisions set forth herein; together with the following information, to wit:
(1)
Written assurance and proof that all personnel of applicant who will provide ambulance services are certified emergency medical technicians, cardiac technicians or paramedics as those positions are certified by the State of Georgia Department of Human Resources;
(2)
Written assurance and proof that each ambulance to be used by applicant within the city is certified by the State of Georgia Department of Human Resources;
(3)
Written assurance and proof that applicant will maintain within the city limits a minimum of two (2) ambulances, neither of which shall exceed sixty (60) months in age (remounted vehicles will be deemed new as of the date the chassis is originally titled), equipped and staffed to provide advanced life support services, as defined by the State of Georgia Department of Human Resources, at all times except when such ambulance(s) shall be outside of the city limits while responding to or on a call for ambulances services, in addition to written assurance and proof that each ambulance maintained hereunder shall be staffed by at least one (1) paramedic;
(4)
Written assurance and proof that applicant is properly licensed by the State of Georgia to provide ambulance services within the state;
(5)
Written assurance and proof that the applicant will either install mobile or make available portable radio units for each ambulance operated within the city which are capable of communications with city police and fire department personnel;
(6)
Written assurance and proof that applicant will maintain a written log of requests for service calls and will in addition make such written records available for inspection by the city upon request all in order to determine compliance with the requirements hereunder;
(7)
Written assurance and proof that all requirements of this article and all other applicable ordinances of the city and statutes of the State of Georgia have been met and fully complied with in order to engage in ambulance services;
(8)
Written assurance of state mandated workers' compensation insurance and proof of public liability insurance in an amount not less than 1.5 million dollars ($1,500,000.00) for one injury, not less than 1.5 million dollars ($1,500,000.00) for all injuries out of one (1) accident, not less than 1.5 million dollars ($1,500,000.00) property damage, and not less than 1.5 million dollars ($1,500,000.00) for injuries caused by malpractice;
Together with the written application described herein, applicant shall submit to the assistant city manager by certified check a three hundred dollar ($300.00) processing fee to cover expenses of investigation and processing of the application, which fee shall not be refundable.
(Ord. No. 96-55, § 1, 10-22-96; Ord. No. 07-12, § 7, 4-24-07; Ord. No. 16-12, § 8, 12-13-16)
Sec. 30-15-85. - Approval and denial of license; public hearing.
Upon determination by the assistant city manager that the application is complete as to form, it shall be presented to and received by the mayor and council at a regular meeting and shall either be granted, if the application is found to comply with the provisions of this article and if the mayor and council in their legitimate discretion determine that such issuance is in the best interest of the citizens of the City of LaGrange, or denied at such meeting, or any subsequent meeting if the matter is tabled or otherwise postponed. The mayor and council may, in their discretion, call for, advertise and hold a public hearing in order to receive comments on the proposed application for franchise prior to decision. If the application for franchise is approved by the mayor and council, the application will be returned to the assistant city manager with the mayor and council approval thereon.
(Ord. No. 96-55, § 1, 10-22-96; Ord. No. 07-12, § 7, 4-24-07; Ord. No. 16-12, § 8, 12-13-16)
Sec. 30-15-86. - Revocation.
(a)
The franchise issued pursuant to this article may be revoked by the mayor and council after reasonable notice to the licensee and an opportunity for a hearing thereon for any one (1) or more of the following reasons:
(1)
Fraud, misrepresentation or false statements contained in the petition for franchise;
(2)
Fraud, misrepresentation or false statements made in conjunction with the provision of ambulance services hereunder;
(3)
Violation of any of the provisions of this article;
(4)
Conducting the business franchise pursuant to the provisions of this article in an unlawful manner, in a manner to constitute a public nuisance, or in manner to constitute a breach of peace or constitute a menace to the health, safety or the general welfare of the public.
(b)
Notice of hearing for revocation of a franchise shall be given in writing to the franchisee, setting forth specifically the grounds of the complaint and the time, date and place of the hearing thereon. Such notice shall be served on the franchisee by handing the same to the person in charge of the franchised business or by mailing the same, postage prepaid to the franchisee at his last known address or to the designated agent for the service of process or the registered agent of the franchisee, at least five (5) days prior to the date set forth the hearing.
(c)
Such service of the notice shall suspend the license pending the outcome of the hearing, and any business operation conducted pursuant to the provisions of the franchise shall promptly cease and terminate.
(Ord. No. 96-55, § 1, 10-22-96)
Sec. 30-15-87. - Renewal.
Any franchise granted under this article, after issuance, must be renewed annually not later than January 1 of each year; and such franchise shall be considered for renewal only in the event a current franchisee is in full and complete compliance with all of the provisions of this article and all other appropriate ordinances of the city and the laws of this state.
(Ord. No. 96-55, § 1, 10-22-96)
Sec. 30-15-88. - Non-transferable.
No franchise under this article may be sold, transferred or assigned by franchisee, or by operation of law, to any other person, firm or corporation. Any such sale, transfer or assignment or attempted sale, transfer or assignment shall be deemed to constitute a voluntary surrender of such franchise, which will thereafter be null and void; provided and excepting, however, that if the franchisee is a partnership and one (1) or more of the partners should die, one (1) or more of the surviving partners may acquire, by purchase or otherwise the interest of the deceased partner or partners without effecting a surrender or termination of the franchise; and in such case the franchise, upon notification to the city, shall be placed in the name of the surviving partner.
(Ord. No. 96-55, § 1, 10-22-96)
Sec. 30-15-89. - Automatic revocation.

Article IV - NONEMERGENCY VEHICLES

Sec. 30-15-96. - Definition.
A nonemergency vehicle shall mean a motor vehicle or invalid car used solely to transport persons who are convalescent, sick or otherwise non-ambulatory, but shall not include and specifically excludes an authorized emergency ambulance.
(Ord. No. 88-123, § 1, 11-22-88)
Sec. 30-15-97. - License required.
It shall be unlawful to operate or cause to be operated in the city, a nonemergency vehicle without a current unrevoked license therefor issued pursuant to authority granted by the mayor and council of the city.
(Ord. No. 88-123, § 1, 11-22-88)
Sec. 30-15-98. - Application for license; insurance and other requirements.
(a)
An application for license to operate a nonemergency vehicle in the city shall be made to the assistant city manager and the applicant therefor shall provide proof of public liability insurance in an amount not less than five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000.00) for one (1) injury, not less than five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000.00) for all injuries out of one (1) accident and not less than five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000.00) property damage and shall meet any and all requirements of and provide the necessary certification for authority to operate a nonemergency vehicle required by the State of Georgia.
(b)
Each non-emergency vehicle shall have printed on each side thereof appropriate language showing that such vehicle is a non-emergency vehicle.
(Ord. No. 88-123, § 1, 11-22-88; Ord. No. 89-13, § 1, 4-25-89; Ord. No. 92-23, § 1, 7-14-92; Ord. No. 07-12, § 7, 4-24-07; Ord. No. 16-12, § 8, 12-13-16)
Sec. 30-15-99. - Passenger rates.
The charge per passenger for all persons transported in nonemergency vehicles shall be the current reimbursement rate authorized and approved by the Medicaid Reimbursement System in Georgia as such reimbursement rate may, from time to time, be amended or changed.
(Ord. No. 88-123, § 1, 11-22-88)
