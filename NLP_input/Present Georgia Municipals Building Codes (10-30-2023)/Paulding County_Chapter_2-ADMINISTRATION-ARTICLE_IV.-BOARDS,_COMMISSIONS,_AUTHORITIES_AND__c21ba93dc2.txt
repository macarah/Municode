DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-191. - Resign to run restriction on membership.
No person who has been appointed to any county or regional board or commission over which the board of commissioners has jurisdiction and appointive authority shall qualify for or hold public office in any elected county post. Any person who is a member of any board or commission appointed by the board of commissioners and over which the board of commissioners has jurisdiction, shall, upon qualifying for any public office for any elected county post, immediately resign. Upon the failure of any person who is a member of any such board or commission who has qualified for elective public office for any county post to resign, this shall result in removal of that person from their position on the board or commission by operation of law.
(Res. No. 98-009, 4-14-1998)
Sec. 2-192. - Timing of appointments.
Where the term of office of an appointee to a county or regional board, commission, authority, committee, or other organization is made by the board of commissioners as a whole, the chairman, or an individual post commissioner and the expiring term is based upon a calendar year, the new appointment or reappointment to such position shall be made by or voted upon at the first meeting of the board of commissioner in January following the expiration of the previous term.
(Ord. No. 20-14, § 1, 12-8-2020)

DIVISION 3. - WATER AND SEWER ADVISORY BOARD[7]

Footnotes:
--- (7) ---
Cross reference— Sewers, ch. 66; water, ch. 74.

Sec. 2-256. - Established.
The Paulding County Water and Sewer Advisory Board is hereby created and established.
(Res. No. 97-06, § 1, 1-28-1997)
Sec. 2-257. - Composition, appointment.
(a)
The water and sewer advisory board shall consist of seven members, each of whom shall be residents of the county. All appointments to the water and sewer advisory board shall be subject to the nomination and confirmation process whereby recommendations for nominations shall be submitted to the chair of the board of commissioners who shall present formal nominations to the board of commissioners for a vote and confirmation.
(b)
The composition of the water and sewer advisory board shall be as follows: the chair of the board of commissioners shall recommend three members to serve on the water and sewer advisory board from the county at large and each of the four post commissioners shall recommend one member to serve on such board from the county at large.
(c)
Such recommendations shall be presented to the chair for formal nominations and are subject to confirmation by a vote of the board of commissioners.
(d)
If any recommendation is not received or not nominated by the chair of the board of commissioners or not confirmed by the board of commissioners, the chair of the board of commissioners may make a substitute nomination of another resident of the county subject to confirmation by the board of commissioners.
(Res. No. 97-06, § 2, 1-28-1997; Res. No. 98-28, § 1, 11-10-1998; Ord. No. 22-03 , § 1, 4-12-2022)
Sec. 2-258. - Terms.
Each member of the water and sewer advisory board shall serve a term of one year. There will be no limit as to the number of consecutive terms a member of the water and sewer advisory board may serve.
(Res. No. 97-06, § 3, 1-28-1997)
Sec. 2-259. - Purpose.
The purpose of the water and sewer advisory board will be to gather public input and submit recommendations to the board of commissioners regarding the water and sewer system in the county and to advise and provide consultation regarding the water and sewer system.
(Res. No. 97-06, § 4, 1-28-1997)
Sec. 2-260. - Meetings.
The water and sewer advisory board shall meet monthly to address issues regarding the water and sewer needs of the county.
(Res. No. 97-06, § 5, 1-28-1997)
Sec. 2-261. - Monthly report.
On a monthly basis, the water and sewer advisory board shall prepare a report for submission to the board of commissioners for consideration in its decision-making process regarding the county's water and sewer needs.
(Res. No. 97-06, § 5, 1-28-1997)
Secs. 2-262—2-280. - Reserved.
DIVISION 4. - KEEP PAULDING BEAUTIFUL COMMISSION

Sec. 2-281. - Created.
The "Keep Paulding Beautiful" commission, created in 2002, shall consist of not less than seven nor more than 15 members who shall be appointed for a term of not more than one year each. All proposed appointments to the "Keep Paulding Beautiful" commission shall be subject to the nomination and confirmation process whereby recommendations for nominations shall be submitted to the Chairman of the Paulding County Board of Commissioners who shall present formal nominations to the board of commissioners for a vote and confirmation.
(Res. No. 02-22, § 1, 10-22-2002; Res. No. 03-09, § 1, 3-25-2003)
Sec. 2-282. - Members.
In making these nominations for the "Keep Paulding Beautiful" commission, the Chairman of the Paulding County Board of Commissioners shall seek proposed recommendations of members to make up the "Keep Paulding Beautiful" commission as follows:
(1)
One member shall be the Paulding County Recycling Coordinator or her designee and said coordinator shall act as the chairman of said commission;
(2)
Each post commissioner shall recommend two members who are residents of Paulding County;
(3)
The chairman of the board of commissioners shall recommend three members, at large, who are residents of Paulding County or who own and operate businesses located within Paulding County;
(4)
The Paulding County Chamber of Commerce shall recommend one member; and
(5)
The board of commissioners may recommend other members for nomination, if desirous, pursuant to the nomination and confirmation process of the board of commissioners until the committee reaches it maximum number of members set forth herein.
Each member so appointed shall serve for a term as designated and until a successor shall be appointed to replace such member as provided herein.
In the event any recommendation is not received or not nominated by the chairman of the board of commissioners or not confirmed by the board of commissioners, the chairman of the board of commissioners may make a substitute nomination subject to confirmation by the board of commissioners.
(Res. No. 02-22, § 3, 10-22-2002; Res. No. 03-09, § 3, 3-25-2003; Ord. No. 22-03 , § 2, 4-12-2022)
Sec. 2-283. - Purpose.
The "Keep Paulding Beautiful" commission's purpose is to advise the Paulding County Board of Commissioners as to issues concerning litter prevention, beautification, and the minimization of the impact of waste in Paulding and to educate citizens as to litter and environmental responsibility through programs and community activities.
(Res. No. 02-22, § 3, 10-22-2002)
Sec. 2-284. - Authority.
In order to complete its task, the "Keep Paulding Beautiful" commission is hereby authorized and empowered to:
(1)
Conduct public meetings with the frequency of same occurring at least once each calendar quarter;
(2)
Make and prescribe rules and by-laws regarding the conduct of the meetings of the "Keep Paulding Beautiful" commission;
(3)
Create sub-committees to study issues within the mission of the mission of the commission as may need special focus;
(4)
Report its finding and advisory recommendations as to programs or other activities which may enhance litter prevention, beautification, and the minimization of the impact of waste in Paulding to the Paulding County Board of Commissioners at least on a semi-annual basis;
(5)
To inquire into all matters as may be requested by the Paulding County Board of Commissioners.
(Res. No. 02-22, § 4, 10-22-2002)
Sec. 2-285. - Expenses.
The members of the "Keep Paulding Beautiful" commission do not receive a salary and are not considered employees of Paulding County by virtue of such appointment. In the event the "Keep Paulding Beautiful" commission or any member thereof is required to incur any expenses in association with their duties as a member of the commission, said members shall submit said proposed expense to the Paulding County Finance Department for pre-approval prior to the expense being made. Approval of all expense or reimbursement requests is subject to budgetary restrictions and shall be at the discretion of the comptroller. If approval is granted, the "Keep Paulding Beautiful" commission or any member thereof shall be reimbursed at the current rate or reimbursement as is available to the office of the board of commissioners of Paulding County pursuant to the written policy thereon.
(Res. No. 02-22, § 5, 10-22-2002)
Secs. 2-286—2-309. - Reserved.
DIVISION 5. - WEST GEORGIA JOINT DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY OF DOUGLAS AND PAULDING COUNTIES

Sec. 2-310. - Created.
The Paulding County Board of Commissioners hereby determines that it is in the best interest of the citizens of Paulding County to create a joint development authority with Douglas County, as such joint authority is defined in the development authorities law, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et. al., for the purpose of developing and promoting for the public good and general welfare, trade, commerce, industry and employment opportunities in Paulding and Douglas Counties, thereby promoting the general welfare of the citizens of each county.
(Res. No. 02-23, § 1, 11-12-2002)
Sec. 2-311. - Title.
Subject to the adoption of an organizing resolution by the Board of Commissioners of Douglas County, there by is hereby activated in Paulding County, Georgia, a public body corporate and politic known as the West Georgia Joint Development Authority of Douglas and Paulding Counties (the "joint authority").
(Res. No. 02-23, § 2, 11-12-2002)
Sec. 2-312. - Composition.
The joint authority shall consist of six members, with Paulding County and Douglas County each providing three members respectively. The three Paulding County members placed on the joint authority shall include the chairman of the industrial building authority, one additional officer from the industrial building authority, as may be nominated and confirmed by the Paulding County Industrial Building Authority, and the staff member of the industrial building authority of Paulding County. The terms of office of such members shall run concurrently with that member's respective term of office with the industrial building authority of Paulding County.
(Res. No. 02-23, § 3, 11-12-2002)
Sec. 2-313. - Purpose.
The Joint Authority of Douglas and Paulding Counties is being activated in part to increase the dollar amount of job tax credits for business located in the geographical area of the joint authority, and it shall not have authority to issue revenue bonds. The joint authority shall be automatically deactivated when the advantage of a joint authority to make available such job credits no longer exists.
(Res. No. 02-22, § 4, 10-22-2002; Res. No. 03-09, § 4, 3-25-2003)
Sec. 2-314. - Compliance.
The clerk of the board of commissioners of Paulding County is hereby ordered and directed to forthwith furnish the Secretary of State of the State of Georgia a certified copy to this resolution in compliance with the mandate of O.C.G.A. § 36-62-4.
(Res. No. 02-22, § 5, 10-22-2002; Res. No. 03-09, § 5, 3-25-2003)
Sec. 2-315. - Title.
After the adoption of all such resolutions, the Joint Authority of Douglas and Paulding Counties shall be deemed fully created and activated and named the West Georgia Joint Development Authority (WGJDA).
(Res. No. 02-22, § 6, 10-22-2002; Res. No. 03-09, § 6, 3-25-2003)
Sec. 2-316. - Permitted additions.
This resolution may be amended by appropriate resolutions of the participating counties in the Joint Authority of Douglas and Paulding Counties, in accordance with law, so as to permit the addition of additional counties as participants in the Joint Authority of Douglas and Paulding Counties.
(Res. No. 02-22, § 7, 10-22-2002; Res. No. 03-09, § 7, 3-25-2003)
Sec. 2-317. - Authority.
Nothing herein shall be deemed to affect the organization and operation of the Industrial Building Authority of Paulding County, including without limitation its existence, purpose, organizational powers or functions.
(Res. No. 03-09, § 8, 3-25-2003)
Secs. 2-318—2-325. - Reserved.

DIVISION 6. - FIRE ADVISORY BOARD

Sec. 2-330. - Established.
The Paulding County Fire Advisory Board is hereby created and established.
(Res. No. 10-10, § 1, 6-22-2010)
Sec. 2-331. - Composition; appointment; removal.
(a)
The fire advisory board shall consist of five members, each of whom shall be residents of the county. All appointments to the fire advisory board shall be subject to the nomination and confirmation process whereby nominations shall be submitted to the chair of the board of commissioners who shall present the nominations to the board of commissioners for vote and confirmation.
(b)
Nominations shall be Paulding County residents and made as follows:
(1)
The chair of the board of commissioners shall make one nomination;
(2)
Each of the four post commissioners shall each make one nomination.
(c)
In the event that a nomination is not confirmed, the individual making said nomination shall submit a different nominee for vote and confirmation.
(d)
No person shall serve on the fire advisory board who is less than 21 years of age; who holds a public elective office; who is currently or formerly employed in the field of public safety to including, but without limitation, fire, law enforcement, emergency medical, or emergency communication services; who is physically or mentally unable to discharge the duties of a member; or who is not qualified to be a registered voter in Paulding County.
(e)
Members of the fire advisory board may be removed by:
(1)
A majority vote of the board of commissioners, or
(2)
The written request of the individual chair or commissioner who originally made the member's nomination to the board of commissioners.
(Res. No. 10-10, § 1, 6-22-2010; Ord. No. 22-03 , § 3, 4-12-2022)
Sec. 2-332. - Terms.
(a)
Each member of the fire advisory board shall serve a term of one year ending December 31 of each year, and may continue in office until a successor is appointed. There is no limit to the number of consecutive terms a member may serve.
(b)
The terms of the initial members of the fire advisory board first appointed following the enactment of this division shall expire on December 31, 2012.
(Res. No. 10-10, § 1, 6-22-2010)
Sec. 2-333. - Purpose.
The purpose of the fire advisory board shall be to make recommendations to the board of commissioners regarding general programs, facilities, equipment, other capital expenditures, and budgetary matters regarding the fire department. Personnel matters, day-to-day operational issues, and matters controlled by other federal, state, or other local law or regulation shall not fall within the purview of the fire advisory board.
(Res. No. 10-10, § 1, 6-22-2010)
Sec. 2-334. - Meetings; chairperson; compensation; supplies.
(a)
The fire advisory board shall meet on a quarterly basis but may meet more frequently to the extent determined necessary by the chairperson of the fire advisory board.
(b)
The fire advisory board shall elect from its membership one member to serve as its chairperson for a one-year term ending December 31 of each year.
(c)
Each member of the fire advisory board shall be paid at a flat rate to help defray the costs and expenses incurred as a member of such board at a rate of $100.00 per fire advisory board meeting attended. The board of commissioners shall provide meeting space for the fire advisory board and, subject to budgetary procedures and requirements of the county, such supplies and equipment as may be reasonably necessary for the fire advisory board to perform its duties and responsibilities.
(Res. No. 10-10, § 1, 6-22-2010)
Secs. 2-335—2-349. - Reserved.
DIVISION 7. - RADIO SYSTEM USER BOARD

Sec. 2-350. - Established.
The Paulding County Radio System User Board ("RSUB") is hereby created and established.
(Res. No. 12-07, § 2, 4-10-2012)
Sec. 2-351. - Definitions.
[The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:]
System means the countywide trunked radio system operated by Paulding County, Georgia.
User means a governmental jurisdiction other than Paulding County that has entered into a trunked radio services agreement ("user agreement") with Paulding County.
(Res. No. 12-07, § 2, 4-10-2012)
Sec. 2-352. - Composition and chair.
(a)
The radio system user board shall consist of one voting representative from each jurisdiction that has entered in a trunked radio services agreement with Paulding County. The chief executive officer of each user, or his or her designee, shall serve as the voting representative.
(b)
The radio system user board shall also consist of one voting representative from each county department or agency that utilizes the system, to be designated by the department or agency head. At no time shall the radio system user board consist of more user voting representatives than county voting representatives.
(c)
County departments/agencies and users may designate more than one representative to attend meetings and for technical support; however, only one representative from each county department/agency and one representative from each user will be a voting representative.
(d)
The radio system user board shall be chaired by the emergency 9-1-1 director or his or her designee, who shall also be a voting member. In addition, the emergency 9-1-1 department shall be authorized to have one additional voting representative on the board. A vice-chair will be elected from the remaining members of the radio system user board.
(Res. No. 12-07, § 2, 4-10-2012)
Sec. 2-353. - Powers and duties.
The radio system user board shall have oversight on all matters affecting the system, shall have the authority to create by-laws; shall review the budget prepared for the system; will hear and decide disputes among users who participate in the system; will approve additional users who wish to be added to the system; and shall decide, by majority vote, upon the expulsion of users in accordance with the terms of this agreement. In addition, the radio system user board shall have authority over all matters specifically granted to it in the user agreement. The RSUB shall have final decision making authority in regard to all issues concerning the system and the user agreements pertaining thereto. Issues regarding day-to-day operations of the system shall rest solely with the Paulding County Emergency 9-1-1 Department.
(Res. No. 12-07, § 2, 4-10-2012)
Sec. 2-354. - Meetings; no compensation.
(a)
The radio system user board shall meet on a quarterly basis but may meet more frequently to the extent determined necessary by the chairperson.
(b)
No member of the radio system user board shall be entitled to any compensation for service on the board.
(Res. No. 12-07, § 2, 4-10-2012)
