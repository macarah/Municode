DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-23. - License declared privilege.
Notwithstanding anything in this article to the contrary, the distribution, sale, handling, and other dealing in or possession of alcoholic beverages in the city is a privilege and not a right, and the issuance of a license under this chapter shall not create any property rights in the license holder. Furthermore, such privilege shall not be exercised in the city except as licensed under the terms of this chapter.
State Law reference— Similar provision, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-1.
Sec. 4-24. - License required.
No malt beverage, wine, distilled spirits or any other alcoholic beverage shall be sold or manufactured in the city except under a license issued under this chapter by the city clerk's officer.
(Code 1992, § 3-32; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)

Sec. 4-25. - Compliance with applicable law required.
The requirements of this chapter shall be in addition to any other requirements for business licenses under this Code and if other provisions of this Code conflict with this chapter, then this chapter shall control. In addition, all licensees shall at all times comply with federal and state laws, municipal ordinances and the rules and regulations of the state revenue commissioner, including licensing qualifications.
(Code 1992, § 3-32; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)

DIVISION 2. - SPECIAL EVENTS LICENSES

Sec. 4-49. - Caterers required to obtain special event permits.
Any establishment holding a license for consumption off the premises at catered functions shall purchase a special event permit for each event where the licensee intends to distribute or sell alcoholic beverages by the drink, off the premises, and in connection with an authorized catered function; provided, however, the issuance of an event permit shall in no way be considered a substitute for the consent of the owner or operator of the off-premises location to distribute or sell alcoholic beverages at the off-premises location. An event permit is limited in scope to the category of alcohol for which the establishment is already licensed. The fee for an event permit shall be in an amount provided in the city fee schedule in chapter 22.
(Code 1992, § 3-32; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-50. - Temporary special events license for persons other than caterers.
The city council may, upon proper application and payment of fees as set forth in the city fee schedule in chapter 22, issue a temporary license to persons not holding a caterer's license as for the sale of alcoholic beverages to be consumed on the premises at a banquet, picnic, bazaar, fair, or similar private or public assembly where food or drink is sold, served, or dispensed. The temporary license shall be issued to a club, society or fraternal or benevolent organization not for pecuniary profit, and shall be for a period of not more than three days. Not more than one temporary permit shall be granted to any one organization in any one period of 30 days.
Sec. 4-51. - Applications.
Applications for special events licenses shall be made in the same manner as for all other licenses as provided in this article, except that the clerk may waive investigations, administrative fees, listed in chapter 22, and any inapplicable supporting documentation otherwise required. The application for special event license shall be made on a form provided by the city clerk.

DIVISION 3. - QUALIFICATIONS

Sec. 4-76. - Required for licensing.
No alcoholic beverage license shall be granted to any applicant, unless that applicant meets the following qualifications, in addition to all other requirements of this chapter:
(1)
All applicants must be at least 21 years of age at the time of filing an application for license under this chapter.
(2)
All license holders desiring to offer for sale alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises on Sunday must derive at least 50 percent of their gross revenue at the licensed premises from the sale of food and beverages other than alcoholic beverages. A separate license shall not be required for Sunday sales under this section.
(3)
All applicants for retail licenses must provide with the application for license proof of financial responsibility. The city council may require all applicants to provide financial statements and other evidence of financial responsibility in conjunction with any application.
(4)
The applicant must be a citizen of the United States or a resident alien, and a resident of the county for a period of one year preceding the date of filing the application; provided, however, that county residency requirements may, at the discretion of the city council, be waived if the applicant designates a resident of the city or county who shall be responsible for any matter relating to the license.
(5)
No applicant who has a criminal record, except minor traffic offenses, shall be granted a license. The plea of nolo contendere and the forfeiture of bond or collateral shall be considered a conviction for the purposes of this subsection. Additionally, no applicant who has been convicted of violating any ordinance of the city or any law or regulation of the state or any political subdivision thereof, governing the sale of any alcoholic beverage shall be granted a license.
(6)
No license shall be issued to an applicant who has previously had revoked, within five years next preceding his application, any license issued to him by any city or county in this or any other state to sell alcoholic beverages of any kind.
(7)
No license for the sale of alcoholic beverages at retail shall be granted until the application, accompanied by a cash, check, or credit card payment in the amount required for the license, setting forth the name of the owners, the business and its location, shall have been approved by the chief of police, the fire chief, and the building inspector. No person shall sell or attempt to sell alcoholic beverages at wholesale or retail until they have received their city and state licenses authorizing them to do so. After all department heads named have approved the issuance of the license and all other requirements for licenses under this chapter have been met, the license shall be issued by the city clerk. Applications for licenses under this chapter shall be made on forms furnished by the city clerk.
(Code 1992, § 3-38; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007; Ord. No. 2011-6, 11-22-2011; Ord. No. 2013-10, 12-3-2013)
Sec. 4-77. - Moral character of applicant.
(a)
All applicants for new and renewal alcoholic beverage licenses must be of good character or the application will not be considered.
(b)
Ascertainable standards of good moral character of the applicant that may be considered by the city clerk in deciding if a license will be issued include, but are not limited to the following:
(1)
Present and prior compliance with city ordinances.
(2)
Prior supervision over employees and customers.
(3)
Truthfulness in submitted application.
(4)
Criminal and civil court records.
(5)
Employment history.
(6)
Credit reports.
(Code 1992, § 3-39; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007; Ord. No. 2013-10, 12-3-2013)
Sec. 4-78. - Active involvement in business required.
(a)
Applicants for license and licensees under this chapter shall be either the owner of the premises for which the license is held or the owner of a lease thereon, and a copy of any applicable premises lease shall be provided with the license application. If the applicant is a franchisee, then such applicant shall attach a copy of the franchise agreement or contract with the application.
(b)
The applicant for a license under this chapter must be active in, and solely responsible for, the management and operation of the business for which the license is granted.
Sec. 4-79. - Partnership applicants.
If the applicant is a partnership, all members of the partnership must be qualified to obtain a license, one member of the partnership must be a resident of the county, and all members of the partnership must make sworn statements of those qualifications.
(Code 1992, § 3-38; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-80. - Corporate applicants.
A license may be granted to a corporation existing under the law of the state or to a foreign corporation authorized to do business in the state; provided, however, that any such corporation shall be required to designate a resident of the county as its local agent responsible for compliance with all the requirements of this chapter and authorize to accept all services of notice.
(Code 1992, § 3-38; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)

DIVISION 4. - APPLICATION PROCEDURE

Sec. 4-104. - Form and filing of application; oath.
(a)
All applications for licensure shall be filed in person by the applicant or his agent.
(b)
All applications shall be submitted to the city clerk, on a form provided by the clerk and shall contain a form of oath providing that the information disclosed in the application is true and correct, and providing further that the applicant will abide by, observe and conduct his other business according to the rules and regulations prescribed by the city and all applicable state and federal rules and regulations. The oath shall also provide that all documents submitted in support of the application are true and correct and that the applicant meets all qualifications of this chapter for licensure.
(c)
The application for a license for off-premises consumption at catered functions shall include applicable fees and a photostatic or other copy of all the appropriate state and city applications and forms and a photostatic or other copy of the appropriate city licenses already issued under this chapter. All other licenses shall be accompanied by supporting documentation as detailed in section 4-105.
(Code 1992, § 3-38; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-105. - Documents to be submitted with application.
The application for all other licenses shall include a photostatic or other copy of all the appropriate state applications and forms along with the following:
(1)
Proof of planning and zoning compliance.
(2)
Proof of building code compliance.
(3)
A legal description of the property upon which the new premises is located.
(4)
A current photograph of the applicant.
(5)
Fingerprints of the applicant taken by the city police department.
(6)
A letter of clearance from the clerk of the federal district court, and a letter of clearance from the clerk of the county superior court, indicating that the applicant does not have any criminal charges currently pending, and/or that the applicant has no convictions from the respective court in the past ten years.
(7)
A letter of clearance from the clerk of the state court of the county showing that the applicant has no criminal charges pending and/or no convictions in the past two years.
(8)
A letter signed by the city building inspector stating that the establishment complies with any distance or location requirements contained in this chapter together with a receipt showing that the city engineer fee specified in the city fee schedule in chapter 22 has been paid.
(9)
A financial statement providing comprehensive information concerning the applicant's financial status.
(10)
A personal performance bond conditioned upon the applicant and the applicant's employees at the licensed establishment complying with all laws, ordinances, rules and regulations of the city governing the issuance and control of alcoholic beverage licenses, to remain in force as long as the license and its renewal are valid and subsisting, in the amount of $750.00 for retail licenses for the package sale of malt beverages or wine, $750.00 for the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises, and $1,000.00 for retail licenses for package sales of distilled spirits.
(11)
A financial statement providing comprehensive information concerning the applicant's financial status.
(12)
A consent form, provided by the clerk and signed by the each applicant, consenting to the city's procurement of all necessary investigative reports regarding the applicant and his employees, including, but not limited to, credit reports and reports from law enforcement agencies, and consenting to the furnishing of such investigative information to the city by third-party providers.
(13)
An affidavit from the publisher of the legal organ of the county showing that the applicant has caused to be published in such legal organ, once a week for two consecutive weeks with the last published at least seven days, but not more than 30 days prior to the council meeting, a notice showing the name and address of the applicant, the location for which the license is sought, the type of license applied for, and the time and place the application shall be considered. If the applicant is a corporation, the names, addresses, and titles of all corporate officers.
(Code 1992, § 3-38; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-106. - Effect of false statements in application or accompanying documents.
Any statement on an application for a license to sell alcoholic beverages or in the documents submitted in support of the application that is later found to be false or to constitute an omission of facts shall be grounds for immediate revocation of the license and shall subject the applicant to penalties for violation of this chapter as provided in section 1-15 in addition to prosecution for perjury under the laws of the state.
(Code 1992, § 3-38; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-107. - Discretion of city clerk.
The city clerk may, in her discretion, consider any extenuating circumstances that may reflect favorably or unfavorably on the applicant, application, or the proposed location of the business. If in the city clerk's judgment, circumstances are such that the granting of the license would not be in the best interest of the general public, these circumstances may be grounds for denying the application.
(Code 1992, § 3-38; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007; Ord. No. 2013-10, 12-3-2013)
Sec. 4-108. - Authority of city clerk to decline to consider application.
The city clerk may decline to consider an application for a city license under this chapter on any of the following grounds:
(1)
Failure of the applicant to meet state requirements for state license;
(2)
Failure of the applicant to pay required fees and taxes;
(3)
Failure of the applicant to provide required valid information, documents and the like;
(4)
False information in the application or attached documents;
(5)
Failure of the applicant to pass review by the chief of police;
(6)
Improper residency of the applicant, owner or registered agent;
(7)
Prior convictions of the applicant as provided in this chapter; or
(8)
Failure to meet any other requirements in this chapter for a license of the class applied for.
(Code 1992, § 3-39; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007; Ord. No. 2013-10, 12-3-2013)
Sec. 4-109. - Administrative and license fees.
(a)
Prerequisite to license issuance. The payment of administrative and license fees shall be a prerequisite to the issuance of a license under this chapter.
(b)
Nonrefundable administrative fee. Each application for license issuance or renewal under this chapter, with the exception of temporary special event licenses, shall be accompanied by an administrative fee, in the amount determined by the city council, to defray, in part, the costs to the city of inspection and investigation during application processing. The administrative fee shall be nonrefundable.
(c)
Annual license fee; refunds. In addition, each application for license issuance or renewal shall be accompanied by the annual license fee, in the amount determined by the city council. In the event application for initial issuance or renewal of a license is denied, the license fee shall be refunded.
(d)
Proration of fees. If application for initial issuance of a license is made after January 31 in any year, the license fees for all licenses, except a wholesale and manufacturer's licenses, shall be prorated with 1/12 of the annual license fee being due for each month or fraction thereof remaining between the date of issuance and December 31 of the same year. There shall be no proration of annual fees for late renewals. There shall be no proration of annual fees for initial issuance or renewals of wholesale or manufacturer licenses.
(e)
No refund upon suspension or revocation. The suspension or revocation of any license granted pursuant to this article shall not entitle the licensee to a return of any portion of the administrative or license fee.
(Code 1992, §§ 3-34—3-35.1; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-110. - Lapse of license; application for extension.
All businesses issued licenses under this chapter must, within 90 days after the issuance of a city license, open for business. Failure to open for business shall constitute forfeiture and cancellation of the issued licenses and no refund of license fees or business taxes shall be made. Any applicant unable to comply with the time limit of this section may make written request to the city council for an extension of time not to exceed 90 days, and the city council may, at its discretion, grant or deny the request.
(Code 1992, § 3-32; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)

DIVISION 5. - RENEWAL

Sec. 4-136. - Term of license; expiration.
All licenses issued under this chapter shall expire on December 31 of each year unless sooner renewed as provided herein.
(Code 1992, § 3-37; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-137. - Permitted; conditions.
All alcoholic beverage licenses in this article may be renewed by payment of the fees set forth in the city fee schedule in chapter 22; provided that:
(1)
Applications for renewal of existing licenses are made in person by the applicants at the office of the city clerk between December 1 and December 15 of each year for the succeeding year and the prescribed license fee paid at time of filing.
(2)
The licensee and location remain the same.
(3)
The licensee's circumstances would enable him to qualify for a license if the renewal was a new application.
(4)
The licensee is still in compliance with the requirements of state and municipal laws and regulations pertaining to the original issuance of the license and pertaining to the operation of the business under the license at the time of application for renewal.
(5)
The city has received no complaints regarding the licensee's operation of the licensed premises.
Sec. 4-138. - Effect of complaint on license renewal; complaint procedure.
If a complaint is received as to the licensee's alleged violation of state or municipal law or regulation before December 31 of the licensed year or 30 days after the application for renewal is received whichever date is later, the license shall not be renewed but shall be referred to the city council for decision as to whether renewal shall be granted as follows:
(1)
The city council shall conduct a hearing after giving the licensee reasonable notice of the time and place of such hearing, as well as the purpose thereof.
(2)
The licensee may remain open for business until a decision by the city council is made and shall have a period of three days following that decision in which to pay the required fees if the decision of the city council is to permit renewal of the license.
(Code 1992, § 3-41; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)

DIVISION 6. - REVOCATION AND SUSPENSION

Sec. 4-175. - Due cause required.
No license which has been issued or which may hereafter be issued by the city under this article shall be suspended or revoked, except for due cause as hereinafter provided.
(Code 1992, § 3-52; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-176. - Investigations incident to disciplinary action.
The city council shall have the right to make such inquiry or investigation as it may find to be reasonably necessary to determine the licensee's compliance or noncompliance with this chapter. Such investigation may consist, among other actions, of calling licensees for examination under oath, obtaining evidence under oath from other persons, the procurement of documents and records, including records of the licensee, and inspection and examination of records and documents from whatever source obtainable.
Sec. 4-177. - Grounds for revocation.
Upon failure of the licensee to comply with the terms, provisions, requirements and responsibilities of this chapter, or upon the failure of the licensee to timely pay the annual license fee called for herein, or upon the violation by the licensee of any law regulating the sale of alcoholic beverages or upon the conviction of the licensee of any felony, aggravated misdemeanor or drug-related conviction or upon the licensee's permitting or maintaining a breach of the peace at the licensee's establishment, or upon the existence of any other condition which would make the continued operation of the licensee's establishment detrimental, harmful or undesirable to the community, then the license provided for hereinunder may be revoked by the city.
(Code 1992, § 3-52; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-178. - Notice of hearing; time for hearing.
Upon information indicating possible grounds for revocation, the city shall serve notice upon licensee, by mail addressed to the licensee's establishment, requiring him to appear before the city council and show cause why his license should not be suspended or revoked. This hearing shall not be held sooner than three days from the receipt of this notice, and the notice shall set forth the date, time and place of the hearing on the matter; this notice shall also state the condition alleged which could, if sustained, result in revocation of the licensee's license.
(Code 1992, § 3-52; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-179. - Decision of council; notice to licensee.
Within five working days following the hearing on this matter, the city council shall render a decision on the matter, notifying the licensee in writing of its decision by mail or by personally delivering it to the licensee.
(Code 1992, § 3-52; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
