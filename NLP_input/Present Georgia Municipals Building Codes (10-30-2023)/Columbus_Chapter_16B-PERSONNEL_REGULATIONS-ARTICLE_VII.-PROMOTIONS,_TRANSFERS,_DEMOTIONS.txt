ARTICLE VII. - PROMOTIONS, TRANSFERS, DEMOTIONS

Sec. 16B-7-1. - Policy.
Vacancies in higher positions in the merit system shall be filled as far as practicable by promotion from lower classes. To accomplish this purpose, closed examinations may be held. When the personnel officer determines that there is an insufficient number of well qualified eligibles within the service, he may direct that an open competitive examination be held.
(Ord. No. 71-213, Ch. 7, § 1, 8-17-71)
Sec. 16B-7-2. - Political or partisan endorsement prohibited.
No consideration shall be given to political or partisan endorsement for promotions; only merit and fitness for promotion shall be considered.
(Ord. No. 71-213, Ch. 7, § 2, 8-17-71)
Sec. 16B-7-3. - Promotional examinations.
The personnel officer shall conduct competitive promotional examinations in accordance with these regulations. In competitive promotional examinations, the personnel officer shall admit to the examination all employees who meet the published qualification requirements. In unassembled examinations, he may establish appropriate rating schedules which may credit, as appropriate, the experience, training and performance ratings of the applicants. Care shall be taken that the examinations shall not be prejudicial to any applicant, regardless of race, color, creed, sex or national origin. Furthermore, the examinations and other requirements shall be limited to the qualifications needed for the particular job in question.
(Ord. No. 71-213, Ch. 7, § 3, 8-17-71)
Sec. 16B-7-4. - Intra-departmental transfers.
The appropriate department head may, at any time, transfer an employee under his jurisdiction from one position to another in the same class in the same department. An intra-departmental transfer of an employee to a position of another class shall be made only with the approval of the personnel officer and only between classes within the same pay range. The personnel officer shall be notified of such changes in assignments.
(Ord. No. 71-213, Ch. 7, § 4, 8-17-71)
Sec. 16B-7-5. - Inter-departmental transfers.
A transfer of an employee from one department to another shall have the approval of both appointing authorities concerned and the personnel officer. Requests for such transfer shall show how the employee concerned meets the qualification requirements of the class to which the transfer is proposed.
(Ord. No. 71-213, Ch. 7, § 5, 8-17-71)
Sec. 16B-7-6. - Pay grade after transfer.
An employee who is transferred shall continue at his same pay rate except as otherwise provided.
(Ord. No. 71-213, Ch. 7, § 6, 8-17-71)
Sec. 16B-7-7. - Voluntary demotions.
An employee may be demoted at his own request to a vacant position in a lower class, subject to the approval of the personnel officer. The personnel officer shall determine whether the employee is qualified to perform the duties and responsibilities of the lower class of positions.
(Ord. No. 71-213, Ch. 7, § 7, 8-17-71)
Sec. 16B-7-8. - Reserved.
Editor's note— Ord. No. 97-139, adopted Dec. 16, 1997, repealed former § 16B-7-8 of the Code, which pertained to pay grade after voluntary demotion and derived from Ord. No. 71-213, Ch. 7, § 8, adopted Aug. 17, 1971.
