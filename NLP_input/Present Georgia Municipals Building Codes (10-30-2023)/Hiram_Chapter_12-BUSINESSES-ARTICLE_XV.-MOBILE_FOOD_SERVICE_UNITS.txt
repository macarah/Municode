ARTICLE XV. - MOBILE FOOD SERVICE UNITS

Sec. 12-700. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Commissary shall mean an approved catering establishment, restaurant, or other approved place in which food, containers or supplies are kept, handled, prepared, packaged or stored and has also obtained approval from the Georgia Department of Public Health.
Mobile food service unit means an independent trailer, motor driven or manually propelled pushcart, food truck, watercraft, movable portable structure, vehicle vendor or any other similar conveyance which is not connected to a permanent water supply or sewer disposal system and from which food is offered for sale or service.
Red zones are zones that mobile food vendors are not permitted to do business. Red zones may be established by majority vote of the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. 2022-08, § 1(Exh. A), 10-4-2022)
Sec. 12-701. - License requirement.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell, or offer for sale, food of any type from a mobile food service unit without a permit first having been granted under this section.
(b)
An application for permit hereunder shall be submitted to the occupational tax clerk setting forth all information required hereunder and in compliance with this article. The occupational tax clerk shall develop a form of application for the purpose of compliance with this article.
(c)
The following information shall be provided with each application for a mobile food service unit permit:
(1)
Name of the mobile food vendor;
(2)
Make, model, and license plate number of vending unit;
(3)
Photo and dimensions of the mobile unit, which cannot exceed 26 feet in length and ten feet in width;
(4)
Owner's contact information;
(5)
Commissary information;
(6)
Type of food vendor;
(7)
Copy of a valid occupation tax certificate issued by a jurisdiction in Georgia;
(8)
A valid health permit from the Georgia Department of Public Health stating that the mobile food service unit meets all applicable standards;
(9)
List of operating locations and times;
(10)
Written permission from the owner(s) of the private properties upon which the permittee will be operating;
(11)
Signature of applicant indicating agreement to the listed requirements;
(12)
Proof of current liability insurance in the amount of $1,000,000.00, issued by an insurance company licensed to do business in Georgia, protecting the mobile food vendor, the public and the city from all claims for damage to property and bodily injury, including death, which may arise from operation under or in connection with the permit. Such insurance shall name the city as an additional insured and shall provide that the policy shall not terminate or be canceled prior to the expiration date without 30 days advanced written notice to the city;
(13)
A sketch illustrating access to the site, all parking areas, routes for ingress and egress, placement of the mobile food unit, distance from property lines, garbage receptacles and any other feature associated with the mobile food unit.
(14)
Permits may be issued hereunder by the City Manager and shall expire after 60 days from date of issuance. Permits are not renewable or transferred and are only valid for the location specified in the application.
(15)
A licensing fee of $150.00 must be paid to the City of Hiram, in full, at the time of application either by cash, credit card, check, money order or cahiers check.
(Ord. No. 2022-08, § 1(Exh. A), 10-4-2022)
Sec. 12-702. - Operating requirements.
Mobile food service units shall comply with the following:
(a)
The license and all applicable health certificates under which a mobile food service unit is operating must be firmly attached and visible on the mobile food service unit at all times.
(b)
Any driver of a mobile food service unit motorized vehicle must possess a valid Georgia driver's license.
(c)
The permit holder shall comply with all state, federal and local health and safety regulations and requirements and shall obtain and maintain any and all licenses required by any other health, organization or governmental organization having jurisdiction over this subject matter.
(d)
The mobile unit is allowed no more than two tables at the maximum size of three feet by two feet, designated only for eating. Tables must be positioned within five feet of the mobile vendor unit and must not impede traffic through a right-of-way or parking lot.
(e)
All signage shall the following requirements:
(1)
A total of one singular sandwich board sign located on the premises of where the mobile vendor unit is operating while the mobile food vendor unit is actually operating.
(2)
Wall signage that is safely affixed to the mobile food service unit so as to be flat against the surface of the mobile food service unit.
(3)
May not include any profanity or provocative language.
(f)
During operation a mobile food service unit must maintain a minimum distance of 200 feet from any building that contains an established licensed eating and/or drinking establishment. This distance will be measured from the serving window in a straight line to the property line of the nearest brick and mortar food establishment. If a mobile food service unit operates less than 200 feet from any building housing an established licensed eating and/or drinking establishment, it must have the express written consent of all affected establishments. This provision shall not apply to mobile food service units operating by invitation of the city at city-sponsored or city-approved events.
(Ord. No. 2022-08, § 1(Exh. A), 10-4-2022)
Sec. 12-703. - Prohibited conduct.
A mobile food service unit shall not:
(a)
Sell anything other than food and non-alcoholic beverages and items;
(b)
Set up more than one table, with a maximum size of three feet by six feet, which must be covered, to provide condiments to patrons;
(c)
Play any music, sound effect or noise that is audible outside of the vehicle. At all times the mobile food vendor shall be in compliance with the noise ordinance;
(d)
Place or utilize any signage that is not permanently affixed to the mobile food unit or otherwise permitted by the city;
(e)
Fail to provide receptacles and properly dispose of all trash, refuse, compost, and garbage that is generated by the use;
(f)
Cause any liquid wastes to be discharged from the mobile food service unit;
(g)
Locate a mobile food service unit within single-family or two-family residentially zoned property
(h)
Locate a mobile food service unit within 100 feet of a single-family or two-family residentially zoned property as measured from its property line;
(i)
Conduct business or operate in the public right-of-way, unless so authorized on municipal right-of- way by the city;
(j)
Operate on any private property without the written consent of the owner;
(k)
Be located within 15 feet of any street intersection or pedestrian crosswalk or ten feet of any driveway.
(l)
Operate between 10:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m.
(m)
Be left unattended or stored at any time on the property for which the permit is issued when vending is not taking place or during restricted hours of operation.
(Ord. No. 2022-08, § 1(Exh. A), 10-4-2022)
Sec. 12-704. - Penalty for violations.
(a)
Violations of this article are subject to the following fines and penalties, which may not be waived or reduced and which may be combined with any other legal remedy available to the city:
(1)
First violation: $250.00.
(2)
Second violation within the 12 months following the first violation: $350.00.
(3)
Third violation within the 12 months following the first violation: $500.00 and revocation of the mobile food service unit.
(b)
Nothing in this article shall limit the city from enforcement of its code, state or federal law by any other legal remedy available to the city. Nothing in this article shall be construed to limit or supplant the power of any city code enforcement, or other duly empowered officer under the city's ordinances, rules and regulations or the authority granted under state law, as amended, to take necessary action, consistent with the law, to protect the public from operation of a mobile food service unit as a nuisance, including abatement thereof by lawful means.
(Ord. No. 2022-08, § 1(Exh. A), 10-4-2022)
Sec. 12-705. - Denials, suspension and revocation of permits.
(a)
Permits required under the provisions of this article may be denied, suspended or revoked by the city manager for any of the following causes:
(1)
Fraud, misrepresentation or false statement contained in the application for permit;
(2)
Fraud, misrepresentation or false statement made in the course of carrying on any business contemplated hereunder;
(3)
Any violation of this article;
(4)
Conducting any business contemplated hereunder, in an unlawful manner or in such a manner as to constitute a breach of the peace or to constitute a menace to the health, safety, or general welfare of the public.
(b)
Hearings.
(1)
The city manager shall hear all matters relating to permits under this article, including, but not being limited to denials, probation, suspensions, revocations, and any other matters affecting such permits.
(2)
Applicants and permit holders shall be given written notice of the date, time, place, and purpose when the matter at issue will be heard. The applicant or permit holder shall be afforded the opportunity to be heard and present evidence. Ten calendar days' notice shall be required.
(3)
Upon close of the hearing, a decision shall be rendered in writing by the city manager and issued no later than within 48 hours of the meeting absent the occurrence of circumstances beyond the reasonable control of the city manager.
(4)
An applicant whose application has been denied or permit holder whose permit has been suspended, revoked, or placed on probation by the city manager may appeal to the governing authority by filing a written notice of appeal within five business days of the adverse decision or action. Such notice shall be provided to the city clerk. The appeal shall be placed on the agenda of the next governing authority meeting occurring at least ten days after the notice is received. The governing authority shall be provided a copy of all evidence heard by the city manager, along with a copy of the city manager's written decision. The applicant, licensee or permit holder may make a statement on their behalf before the governing authority, but no new evidence shall be taken. A vote on the appeal shall be taken either on the date of appeal hearing or at the next subsequent regular meeting of the governing authority. The decision of the governing authority shall be based on the evidence and standards of this chapter. If the governing authority determines the city manager acted in accordance with the standards of this chapter, it shall affirm his decision. If the governing authority determines the city manager acted contrary to the standards of this chapter, it shall overturn his decision and remand back to the city manager for a corrected decision.
(5)
An applicant or permit holder who is dissatisfied with the decision of the governing authority may appeal by filing for writ of certiorari with the superior court Paulding County, Georgia.
(Ord. No. 2022-08, § 1(Exh. A), 10-4-2022)
Sec. 12-706. - Indemnity.
As part of the permitting process set forth herein, any person or entity receiving a permit set forth herein shall execute an indemnity agreement indemnifying and releasing the City of Hiram, its agents, employees and elected officials from any and all liability against any and all claims, actions and suits of any type whatsoever.
(Ord. No. 2022-08, § 1(Exh. A), 10-4-2022)
