Sec. 11-275. - Definitions.
(a)
For the purpose of this article,
(1)
Adult Entertainment means any establishment which offers for commercial purposes on its premises:
a.
Permitting, performing or engaging in, in person, on the premises, in acts of or acts which simulate:
1.
Sexual intercourse, masturbation, sodomy, bestiality, oral copulation, flagellation or any sexual acts which are prohibited by law.
2.
The touching, caressing or fondling, either of one's self or of another, of the breasts, buttocks, anus or genitals.
b.
The opportunity to view any model, dancer or other person who appears live and unclothed or live and in such attire, lingerie, costume or clothing so as to expose to view whether continually, intermittently or occasionally any portion of the female areola, any portion of the anus, vulva or genitals, cleft of the buttocks, or attached male or female pubic hair.
c.
Showing or permitting the showing or viewing of films, still pictures, electronic reproductions, video graphic or other visual images depicting masturbation, or acts of sexual intercourse, sodomy, oral copulation, flagellation or any sexual acts which are prohibited by law and in which the unclothed contact of the genitalia of two (2) or more persons are clearly visible.
d.
Sales or rental of films, still pictures, electronic reproductions, video graphic or other visual images depicting masturbation, or acts of sexual intercourse, sodomy, oral copulation, flagellation or any sexual acts which are prohibited by law and in which the unclothed contact of the genitals of two (2) or more persons is clearly visible so long as such sales or rentals account for more than ten (10) per cent of gross income for the establishment.
e.
Sales or rental of any device or paraphernalia that is designed principally for sexual activities, but not including contraceptive devices, so long as such sales or rentals account for more than ten (10) per cent of gross income for the establishment.
f.
Sale or rental of any "drug related object" as that term is defined in O.C.G.A Section 16-13-32, so long as such sales or rentals account for more than ten (10) per cent of gross income for the establishment.
Provided, however, that random acts of patrons or employees, whose actions do not constitute actions taken pursuant to encouragement or acquiescence of the management of the establishment and are not for the purposes of entertainment, promotion, publicity or notoriety for the establishment shall not constitute adult entertainment as defined in this section; provided further that nothing herein shall be deemed to make legal any conduct that otherwise may be prohibited by law.
(2)
Adult entertainment facility means any commercial establishment in the City of College Park where adult entertainment is sponsored, allowed, encouraged, condoned, presented, sold or offered to the public, or an establishment with a segment or section devoted to such activity. An establishment meets this definition even if such activity is allowed on only one occasion. Examples of adult entertainment facilities include, but are not limited to, lingerie modeling studios, establishments where adult entertainment is offered, adult book stores, adult video stores, and adult theaters.
(3)
Dressing room means an area within an adult entertainment facility which is used by employees for the purpose of changing clothes or using the restroom.
(4)
Employee means any person who works or performs in an adult entertainment facility, irrespective of whether said person is paid a salary or wage, contract rate or has the opportunity to obtain tips or prize money. Employee shall also include any person who performs at an adult entertainment facility and who pays any form of consideration to an owner or manager of the facility for the privilege to work or perform within the facility.
(5)
Private performance means adult entertainment being offered or performed in a private room.
(6)
Private room means any area within an adult entertainment facility not open to the public and/or not fully visible from an area within the facility open to the public. For purposes of this definition, "fully visible" means that no less that twenty-five (25) per cent of the perimeter or circumference of the area can be viewed, without obstruction, from the common area of the facility which is open to the public.

It is hereby further declared to be the intention of the mayor and council that, to the greatest extent allowed by law, no paragraph, sentence, clause or phrase of this section is mutually dependent upon any other section, paragraph, sentence, clause or phrase of this section.
In the event that any phrase, clause, sentence, or paragraph of this section shall, for any reason whatsoever, be declared invalid, unconstitutional or otherwise unenforceable by the valid judgement or decree of any court of competent jurisdiction, it is the express intent of the mayor and council that such invalidity, unconstitutional or otherwise unenforceable any of the remaining phrases, clauses, sentences, or paragraphs of this section and that, to the greatest extent allowed by law, all remaining phrases, clauses, sentences, and paragraphs of this section shall remain valid, constitutional, enforceable, and of full force and effect.
(Ord. No. 96-14, § 3, 4-15-96; Ord. No. 2002-09, § 1, 2-18-02)
Sec. 11-276. - Permitted locations.
Adult entertainment facilities shall be allowed only in the following areas:
(1)
On West Point Avenue, from five hundred (500) feet west of its intersection with Edison Drive and running west to five hundred (500) feet east of its intersection with Sullivan Road.
(2)
Edison Drive.
(Ord. No. 96-14, § 3, 4-15-96; Ord. No. 2002-12, § 1, 3-18-02)
Sec. 11-277. - Other location and structural requirements.
Adult entertainment facilities shall be subject to the provisions of the zoning code, this article and the following standards of use:
(1)
The minimum lot area for an adult entertainment facility shall be one (1) acre.
(2)
In addition to zoning development standards governing commercial zoning districts, building and structures established in connection with an adult entertainment facility shall be separated at least forty (40) feet from the building and structures of any other business establishment. The minimum rear yard shall be seventy-five (75) feet.
(3)
In addition to the development standards of the zoning ordinance, adult entertainment facilities shall not be located in any strip shopping center, mall or other group structures and must be placed in a separate building on its own lot.
(4)
No adult entertainment establishment shall be located within five hundred (500) feet of the property boundaries of any hotel or motel, as measured by the most direct route of travel on a public roadway.
(5)
Parking requirements: Parking shall meet the standards of a restaurant under the city zoning ordinance.
(Ord. No. 96-14, § 3, 4-15-96)
Sec. 11-278. - Licensing.
(a)
Any person, firm, partnership or corporation which owns or operates an establishment offering adult entertainment shall be required to obtain a license for an adult entertainment facility. No person, firm, partnership or corporation shall be permitted to operate an adult entertainment facility unless and until such a license is obtained.
(b)
To obtain an adult entertainment facility license, an applicant must submit a completed, sworn license application to the city clerk, which application must contain the following:
(1)
If the applicant is:
a.
An individual, the applicant must provide his legal name, any aliases, and date of birth;
b.
A partnership, the applicant must provide the full and complete name of the partnership, and the legal names of all partners, dates of birth, and aliases used by all of the partners, whether the partnership is general or limited, and, if in existence, a copy of the partnership agreement; or
c.
A corporation, the applicant must provide the exact and complete corporate name, the date of its incorporation, evidence that the corporation is registered to do business in the State of Georgia, the name of the registered corporate agent and the address of the registered office for service of process, and the legal name, date of birth, all aliases used by, and title of each officer, director or principal stockholder. For purposes of this article, principal stockholder shall mean any person owning fifteen (15) per cent or more of the shares of or any interest in the corporation.
(2)
Whether, within a period of fifteen (15) years immediately prior to the filing of the application, the applicant or any of the other individuals listed pursuant to subsection 11-278(b)(1) above has ever been convicted of or entered a plea of nolo contendere to any crime involving lottery, gambling, illegal possession or sale of any narcotics, drugs or chemicals for which the sale thereof is controlled or prohibited by law, the control of the sale or distribution of alcoholic beverages, prostitution, solicitation of prostitution, sodomy, solicitation of sodomy, masturbation for hire, or receiving stolen property.
(3)
Whether the applicant or any of the other individuals listed pursuant to subsection 11-278(b)(1) above has had a previous license under this Code suspended or revoked, as well as the date of the suspension or revocation, and whether the applicant has been a partner in a partnership or an officer, director or principal stockholder of a corporation whose license under this Code has previously been suspended or revoked, including the name and location of the establishment for which the license was suspended or revoked, as well as the date of the suspension or revocation;
(4)
Whether the applicant or any other individual listed pursuant to subsection 11-278(b)(1) above holds any other licenses under this Code, and, if so, the type of license held and the names and locations of such other licensed establishments;
(5)
The location of the proposed establishment, including a legal description of the property site, and a legal street address;
(6)
The names, dates of birth of; and aliases used by, all employees of the proposed establishment, if known, or, if presently unknown, a statement to that effect;
(7)
The applicant's mailing address;
(8)
A site plan and a certified survey drawn to appropriate scale of the proposed establishment indicating, but not limited to, all property lines, rights-of-way, and location of buildings, parking areas and spaces, curb cuts, and driveways;
(9)
A floor plan drawn to appropriate scale of the proposed establishment indicating, but not limited to:
a.
All windows, all doors, all entrances and exits; and
b.
All fixed structural and interior features, including but not limited to doors, walls, stages, partitions, dressing rooms, and private areas.
(10)
Payment of an investigative fee in the amount of two hundred fifty dollars ($250.00).
(c)
No person, firm, partnership, or corporation shall be granted a license if any person, partner, officer or owner of fifteen (15) per cent or more of the shares of or any interest in any such business, firm, partnership or corporation has been convicted of or has plead guilty or entered a plea of nolo contendere to any crime involving lottery, gambling, illegal possession or sale of any narcotics drugs or chemicals for which the sale thereof is controlled or prohibited by law, the control of the sale or distribution of alcoholic beverages, prostitution, solicitation of prostitution, sodomy, solicitation of sodomy, masturbation for hire, or receiving stolen property, with[in] a period of fifteen (15) years immediately prior to the filing of the application. Should any such person, partner, officer or shareholder of a corporation, after a license has been granted, be convicted of or plead guilty or nolo contendere to any such crime, said license shall be subject to suspension and revocation. Withdrawal of an owner, officer or partner or enumerated shareholder, after conviction of or pleading guilty or nolo contendere to the above enumerated crimes shall not necessarily mitigate against the penalty imposed hereby.
(d)
The city shall issue no more than one (1) license to an establishment meeting the definition of subsection 11-275(1)a. or b. per each ten thousand (10,000) persons living in the city according to the most recent decennial census of the United States as published by the United States Bureau of the Census.
(e)
It is the belief of the mayor and council that the sale of items described in subsections 11-275(a)(1)c., 11-275(a)(1)d. and 11-275(a)(1)f. is illegal under Georgia Law and therefore not licensable under city ordinance. However, if any court competent jurisdiction declares to the contrary, the city shall issue no more than one (1) license to an establishment meeting the definitions of subsections 11-275(a)(1)c., 11-275(a)(1)f. per each ten thousand (10,000) persons living in the city according to the most recent decennial census of the United States as published by the United States Bureau of the Census.
(f)
The license shall be renewed each year. A change of ownership shall require a new license and a new license fee.
(g)
The fee for the adult entertainment facility shall be three thousand dollars ($3,000.00) per year.
(h)
Any person, firm, partnership or corporation which holds an adult entertainment facility license must also comply with all applicable local, state and federal laws or regulations otherwise pertaining to these establishments.
(i)
All applications for new licenses shall be passed upon by the city clerk within sixty (60) days of the date of filing of the completed application. The date of filing shall be the date by which a properly completed and executed application form and the investigative fee are received by the city clerk. Upon the expiration of the sixty (60) day period without a decision being made on the application by the city clerk, the applicant shall be permitted to begin and conduct business as an adult entertainment facility under this statutory provision unless and until such time as the city clerk notifies the applicant of a denial of the application and states the reason(s)for the denial. No person or entity conducting business as an adult entertainment facility under this provision shall acquire any vested rights to continued conduct of such business, and if the city clerk later denies the application, the business must be brought into compliance with this article.
(j)
(1)
In the event that the city clerk denies an application for a license, the denial and specific reasons for the denial shall be transmitted to the applicant in writing. The applicant shall have the right to appeal the denial to the city manager. Such appeal shall be filed in writing no later than ten (10) days from the date of the city clerk's denial. The applicant's failure to appeal the city clerk's denial within ten (10) days shall constitute a waiver of any appeal rights hereunder. If a timely appeal is filed, a hearing shall be held not more than thirty (30) days from the date of the city manager's receipt of the written notice of appeal. Upon the expiration of the thirty (30) day period without a hearing, the applicant shall be permitted to begin and conduct business as an adult entertainment facility under this statutory provision unless and until such time as the city manager sustains the decision of the city clerk. No person or entity conducting business as an adult entertainment facility under this provision shall acquire any vested rights to continued conduct of such business, and if the city manager later sustains or modifies the city clerk's denial of the application, the business must be brought into compliance with this article.
(2)
The city manager may sustain, overrule, or modify the decision of the city clerk. The decision of the city manager following the hearing on the matter shall be issued within fifteen (15) days of the date of the hearing and may be appealed to the mayor and council. Upon the expiration of the fifteen (15) day period without a decision of the city manager, the applicant shall be permitted to begin and conduct business as an adult entertainment facility under this statutory provision unless and until such time as the city manager sustains the decision of the city clerk. No person or entity conducting business as an adult entertainment facility under this provision shall acquire any vested rights to continued conduct of such business, and if the city manager later sustains or modifies the city clerk's denial of the application, the business must be brought into compliance with this article.
(Ord. No. 96-14, § 3, 4-15-96; Ord. No. 2001-06, § 1, 2-5-01; Ord. No. 2002-09, § 2, 2-18-02)
Sec. 11-279. - Regulation of live conduct on premises.
(a)
Any person, firm, partnership or corporation licensed hereunder and offering adult entertainment as described in subsection 11-275(1)a. or b. or any person patronizing such business shall comply with the following rules and regulations pertaining to the operation of the adult entertainment facility:
(1)
No licensee shall offer adult entertainment between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and noon.
(2)
No person under the age of eighteen (18) shall be permitted on the premises.
(3)
No patron shall touch, fondle or caress any employee, nor shall any employee touch, fondle or caress a patron.
(4)
No tips for performing shall be placed by a patron on the person of the employee.
(5)
All live performers of adult entertainment shall be restricted to fixed stages and no patrons shall be permitted on such stages for any purpose. Fixed stage shall be defined as a raised-floor area or stage at least four (4) feet high and designed exclusively for, and used only by, performers. Tables used for seating and service to customers do not meet these standards and shall not be used for performing purposes.
(6)
The license shall be displayed in a prominent place on the premises at all times.
(7)
No licensee shall permit any alcoholic beverages to be served, offered or consumed on the licensed premises or in any parking area dedicated to use of the premises.
(8)
In all areas where adult entertainment as described in subsection 11-276(1)a. or b. occurs, lighting equivalent to three and five tenths (3.5) foot candles per square feet shall be maintained.
(9)
No private performances shall occur within an adult entertainment facility.
(10)
No adult entertainment shall occur in a private room or dressing room within an adult entertainment facility.
(11)
No patron shall be allowed in any dressing area or private room nor shall any employee and patron jointly occupy any private room or dressing area.
(Ord. No. 96-14, § 3, 4-15-96)
Sec. 11-280. - Regulation of conduct on premises where no live conduct occurs.
(a)
It is the belief of the mayor and council that the sale of items described in subsections 11-275(a)(1)c., 11-275(a)(1)d. and 11-275(a)(1)f. is illegal under Georgia Law and therefore not licensable under city ordinance. However, if any court of competent jurisdiction declares to the contrary, any adult entertainment facility which sells the items described in subsections 11-275(a)(1)c., 11-275(a)(1)d. and 11-275(a)(1)f. shall comply with the regulations set forth in this section.
(b)
Any person, firm, partnership, corporation or other business entity licensed hereunder and offering adult entertainment as described in subsections 11-275(a)(1)c., 11-275(a)(1)d. and 11-275(a)(1)f. or any person patronizing such business shall comply with the following rules and regulations relating to the operation of the adult entertainment facility:
(1)
No licensee shall offer adult entertainment between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 12:00 noon.
(2)
No person under the age of eighteen (18) shall be permitted on the premises.
(3)
The license shall be displayed in a prominent place on the premises at all times.
(4)
No licensee shall permit any alcoholic beverages to be served, offered or consumed on the licensed premises or in any parking area dedicated to use of the premises.
(5)
No adult entertainment as described in subsection 11-275(1)c. shall occur in a private room within an adult entertainment facility.
(6)
All areas where adult entertainment as described in subsection 11-275(1)c. occurs shall be physically arranged in such a manner that the entire interior portion of the booths, cubicles, rooms or stalls wherein adult entertainment is provided shall be visible from the common area of the premises, so that no private room is created. Visibility shall not be blocked or obscured by doors, curtain, partitions, drapes, or any other obstruction whatsoever.
(Ord. No. 96-14, § 3, 4-15-96; Ord. No. 2002-09, § 3, 2-18-02)
Sec. 11-281. - Employee investigation; fees.
(a)
No person may be employed or allowed to work as an independent contractor delivering adult entertainment by an establishment holding a license hereunder until such person has been fingerprinted by the police department and has been issued a letter of clearance by the police department indicating the person has not, within the preceding five (5) years, been convicted of or plead guilty or nolo contendere to any law catalogued by subsection 11-278(c) of this article. A letter of compliance shall be issued by the police department indicating the person is eligible for such employment. This letter must be kept on file at the licensed establishment where such person may be performing. This section shall apply to performers, entertainers and musicians engaged in temporary work or independent contractors, as well as regular employees.
(b)
(1)
The police department shall conductor procure a criminal background search of the person fingerprinted to discover any police record of the person fingerprinted. In the event that the search reveals a violation of law as catalogued in subsection 11-278(c) of this article, the police department shall issue a letter to the person fingerprinted stating that the person is ineligible for employment. This determination must be made within seven (7) days from the date that a complete application is received by the police department from the person fingerprinted. Upon the expiration of the seven (7) day period without a determination being made on the application by the police department, the person fingerprinted shall be permitted to be employed or work as an independent contractor at an adult entertainment facility under this statutory provision unless and until such time as the police department notifies the person fingerprinted of a determination o ineligibility. No person employed or working as an independent contractor at an adult entertainment facility under this provision shall acquire any vested rights to continued conduct of such business, and if the police department later determines that the person fingerprinted is ineligible, the person fingerprinted must comply with this article.
(2)
In the event that the police department issues a letter to the person fingerprinted indicating that the person fingerprinted is ineligible for employment, this determination may he appealed to the city manager. Such appeal shall be filed in writing no later than ten (10) days from the date of the letter of ineligibility from the police department. Failure to appeal the police department's decision within ten (10) days of the letter shall constitute a waiver of any appeal rights hereunder. If an appeal is filed, a hearing shall be held not more than thirty (30) days from the date of the written notice of appeal. Upon the expiration of the this (30) day period without a hearing, the person fingerprinted shall he permitted to be employed or work as an independent contractor at an adult entertainment facility under this statutory provision unless and until such time as the city manager sustains the determination of the police department. No person employed or working as an independent contractor at an adult entertainment facility under this provision shall acquire any vested rights to continued conduct of such business, and if the City manager later sustains or modifies the police department's determination of ineligibility, the person fingerprinted must comply with this article.
(3)
The city manager may sustain, overrule, or modify the decision of the police department. The decision of the city manager following the hearing on the matter shall be issued within fifteen (15) days of the date of the hearing and may be appealed to the mayor and council. Upon the expiration of the fifteen (15) day period without a decision of the city manager, the person fingerprinted shall be permitted to be employed or work as an independent contractor at an adult entertainment facility under this statutory provision unless and until such time as the city manager sustains the decision of the police department. No person employed or working as an independent contractor at an adult entertainment facility under this provision shall acquire any vested rights to continued conduct of such business, and if the city manager later sustains or modifies the police department's determination of ineligibility, the person fingerprinted must comply with this article.
(c)
Any letter of eligibility for employment issued hereafter shall expire twelve (12) months from the date of issue.
(d)
The mayor and council may prescribe reasonable fees for certifying the eligibility of employment.
(Ord. No. 96-14, § 3, 4-15-96; Ord. No. 2001-06, § 2, 2-5-01)
Sec. 11-282. - Exemptions.
Nothing in this article shall be construed as applying to State of Georgia licensed barbers, cosmetologists, manicurists, pedicurists, physical therapists, physical therapists' assistants, midwives, practical nurses, agents, servants or employees and licensed hospitals or nursing homes or other licensed medical institutions, licensed physicians, osteopaths, chiropractors, podiatrists, naturopathic physicians or other licensed medical practitioners, licensed psychologists or other agents, servants or employees of such persons acting in the course of such profession, agency, service or employment.
(Ord. No. 96-14, § 3, 4-15-96)
Sec. 11-283. - Suspension or revocation of license; appeal.
(a)
The following shall constitute grounds for the suspension or revocation of adult entertainment facility license:
(1)
Any violation of this article regulating adult entertainment facilities or other ordinances of the city relating to conduct of the business.
(2)
Violation of any federal or state law applicable to the licensed premises.
(3)
Maintaining the premises or permitting conduct on the premises in such manner as to constitute a nuisance.
(b)
Any violation by an agent or employee of a license holder which would subject the licensee to suspension or revocation under subsection 11-283(a) shall be considered a violation by the licensee for the purpose of the suspension and revocation provisions of this section.
(c)
(1)
Should the city clerk determine that grounds for suspension or revocation exist under subsection 11-283(a), the city clerk shall send notice by first-class mail to the license holder of the license suspension or revocation with a statement of the grounds supporting such action, Any revocation or suspension shall be effective on the third (3rd) business day after the date of the notice.
(2)
Any license holder may request within ten (10) days of the date of notice of revocation or suspension from the city clerk a hearing before the city manager for the purpose of presenting evidence and asking for reconsideration of such action. Failure to request such reconsideration in writing within ten (10) days of the date of the notice shall result in a waiver of any appeal rights hereunder. If a hearing for reconsideration before the city manager is requested, a hearing shall be held not more than thirty (30) days from the date that the written notice requesting such hearing is received by the city manager. Upon the expiration of the thirty, (30) day period without a hearing, the license holder shall be permitted to resume business as an adult entertainment facility under this statutory provision unless and Until such lime as the city manager sustains the decision of the city clerk. No person or entity conducting business as an adult entertainment facility under this provision shall acquire any vested rights to continued conduct of such business, and if the city manager later sustains or modifies the city clerk's suspension or revocation of the license, the business must be brought into compliance with this article.
(3)
The city manager may sustain, overrule, or modify the decision of the city clerk. The decision of the city manager following the hearing on the matter shall be issued within fifteen (15) days of the date of the hearing and may be appealed to the mayor and council. Upon the expiration of the fifteen (15) day period without a decision of the city manager, the license holder shall be permitted to resume business as an adult entertainment facility under this statutory provision unless and until such time as the city manager sustains the decision of the city clerk, No person or entity conducting business as an adult entertainment facility under this provision shall acquire any vested rights to continued conduct of such business, and if the city manager later sustains or modifies the city clerk's suspension or revocation of the license, the business must he brought into compliance with this article.
(d)
In addition to the procedure for license suspension and/or revocation as outlined in the preceding subsections of this section, any violation of this article shall subject the offender, upon conviction, to punishment as provided by section 1-8 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 96-14, § 3, 4-15-96; Ord. No. 2001-06, § 3, 2-5-01)
Sec. 11-284. - Appeal to mayor and council.
(a)
Any decision of the city manager under this article may he appealed in writing to the mayor and council within ten (10) days of the date of the decision by the city manager. Failure to request such reconsideration in writing within ten (10) days of the date of the decision of the city manager shall result in a waiver of any appeal rights hereunder. Within thirty (30) days of the notice of appeal, the mayor and council shall consider the appeal at a hearing. Upon the expiration of the thirty (30) day period without a hearing, the appellant shall be permitted to begin or resume business as an adult entertainment facility under this statutory provision unless and until such time as the mayor and council sustain the decision of the city manager. No person or entity conducting business as an adult entertainment facility under this provision shall acquire any vested rights to continued conduct of such business, and if the mayor and council sustain the decision of the city manager, the business must be brought into compliance with this article.
(b)
The mayor and council may sustain, overrule, or modify the decision of the city manager. The decision of the mayor and council following the hearing on the matter shall be issued within fifteen (15) days of the date of the hearing and shall he final. Upon the expiration of the fifteen (15) day period without a decision of the mayor and council, the appellant shall be permitted to begin or resume business as an adult entertainment facility under this statutory provision unless and until such time as the mayor and council sustain the decision of the city manager. No person or entity conducting business as an adult entertainment facility under this provision shall acquire any vested rights to continued conduct of such business, and if the mayor and council later sustain or modify the city manager's decision, the business must be brought into compliance with this article.
(Ord. No. 2001-06, § 4, 2-5-01)
