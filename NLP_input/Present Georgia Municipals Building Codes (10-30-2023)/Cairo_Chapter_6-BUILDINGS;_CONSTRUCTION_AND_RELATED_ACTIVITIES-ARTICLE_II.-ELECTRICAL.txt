ARTICLE II. - ELECTRICAL
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 6-20. - Electrical inspector designated.
The office of electrical inspector shall be performed by the building official or someone duly authorized as such by, and under control and direction of, the building official.
(Code 1965, § 10-2)
Sec. 6-21. - Promulgation of rules and regulations; supervision of electrical work.
All electrical work and wiring done within the city, or outside the city when performed on buildings served by the city electrical distribution system, shall be under the rules and regulations promulgated by the governing body and under the direction of the electrical inspector.
(Code 1965, § 10-5)
Sec. 6-22. - Applicability of provisions.
The provisions of this article shall apply to all installations of electrical material, fittings, devices, appliances, fixtures and apparatus, herein referred to as "electrical equipment," within or on public and private buildings, structures and premises, with the following general exceptions:
(1)
The provisions of this article shall not apply to installations in mines, ships, railway cars, automotive equipment, or the installation of equipment in or on buildings or on premises of electrical railway companies used exclusively for the conduct of their business.
(2)
The provisions of this article shall not apply to installations used by electricity supply or communication agencies in the generation, transmission, or distribution of electricity, or for the operation of signals or the transmission of intelligence, in the exercise of their function as such agencies and located outdoors or in buildings used exclusively for that purpose.
(3)
The provisions of this article shall not apply to electrical equipment used for radio transmission, or other electrical equipment for radio transmission in amateur radio transmitting stations, and all electrical equipment used for power supply to radio transmitting equipment.
(Code 1965, § 10-3)
Sec. 6-23. - Standards for electrical work; adoption of electrical code.
(a)
Except as otherwise provided in this article all installations of electrical equipment shall be in conformity with the provisions of this article and the statutes of the state, any order, rules, and regulations issued by authority thereof, and with approved electrical standards for safety to persons and property.
(b)
Where no specified standards are prescribed by this article, or statues of the State of Georgia, or by any orders, rules or regulations issued by authorities thereof, conformity with the regulations set forth within the N.F.P.A. National Electric Code, latest edition as adopted and amended by the Georgia Department of Community Affairs (DCA) shall be prima facia evidence of conformity with the previous standards for safety to person(s) or property; which code is hereby adopted by reference and incorporated herein, three (3) copies of which are on file in the office of clerk of the city.
(Code 1965, § 10-4; Ord. No. 207, § 1, 12-5-73; Ord. No. 334, § 4, 9-26-88; Ord. No. 440, § 4, 12-12-94; Ord. No. 461, § 1, 3-11-96; Ord. No. 501, § 2, 1-12-98)
State Law reference— State code, OCGA § 8-2-20.
Sec. 6-24. - Construction of television antennas near power lines.
No person shall construct, build, erect or maintain a television or other communication antenna in such a manner or location that if it should fall it would fall across any city line above one hundred twenty (120) volts to ground.
(Code 1965, § 10-69)
Sec. 6-25. - Appeals.
Any person aggrieved by any ruling or order of the electrical inspector shall have the right of appeal to the governing body, which ruling upon review shall be final.
(Code 1965, § 10-6)
Secs. 6-26—6-29. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - CERTIFICATION OF ELECTRICIANS

Sec. 6-30. - License required; fee as prescribed.
Every person to whom a state certificate of competency has been issued shall pay to the city the registration fee provided within the building code.
(Code 1965, §§ 10-26, 10-33; Ord. No. 440, § 5, 12-12-94)
Sec. 6-31. - State certificate required.
Any person desiring to engage within the city for work at electrical work or wiring shall be required to hold a state license signifying competency. The clerk shall only register those applicants exhibiting a state certificate certifying to competency of the applicant to perform.
(Code 1965, § 10-22; Ord. No. 440, § 6, 12-12-94)
State Law reference— Electrical contractors, plumbers and conditioned air contractors, OCGA § 43-14-1 et seq.
Sec. 6-32. - All work performed by or under direction of licensee.
No electrical work or wiring shall be performed anywhere within the city, or outside the city when performed on buildings served by the city electrical distribution system, except by or under the direction of a master electrician.
(Code 1965, § 10-34)
Sec. 6-33. - Allowing use of name by another.
No licensed electrician shall allow such electrician's name to be used by any person, directly or indirectly, for the purpose of obtaining a permit or to do any work under such electrician's license.
(Code 1965, § 10-35)
Sec. 6-34. - Bond—Required; conditions.
(a)
Before any person shall be issued a license under this division, such person shall execute a bond with a surety or sureties, payable to the city, in the sum of five thousand dollars ($5,000.00), conditioned to protect the city against all loss or damage occasioned by the negligence of the principal therein in failing to properly execute and protect all work done by such person or by such person's employees, or under such person's direction or supervision, and from all loss or damage occasioned by or arising in any manner from any such work done by such principal or principal's employees or under principal's direction or supervision, which is not caused by the negligence of the city or its agents or employees; conditioned further, that the principal therein shall keep and observe all ordinances at any time enacted by the city relating in any way to electrical work and wiring.
(b)
Such bond shall be further conditioned that such person shall, without additional cost to the person for whom the work is done, remedy any defects therein due to faulty workmanship and incorrect construction or due to faulty material furnished or used by such person and that such person shall reconstruct and repair such work to the satisfaction of the electrical inspector at any time within one year after the construction, repair, or installation thereof, and after forty-eight (48) hours' notice from the electrical inspector to reconstruct or repair the same.
(Code 1965, § 10-29; Ord. No. 443, § 1, 3-13-95)
Sec. 6-35. - Same—Approval.
The bond shall be approved by the city attorney and city clerk before it becomes effective.
(Code 1965, § 10-30)
Sec. 6-36. - Same—Suing on.
The bond may be sued on in any court of competent jurisdiction in the state.
(Code 1965, § 10-31)
Sec. 6-37. - Same—Expiration.
(a)
All bonds shall expire no sooner than one (1) year from the date of issue, and no bond shall be null and void until all liability thereon has been adjusted, settled and extinguished.
(b)
No electrical work or wiring shall be performed by, nor shall any permit be issued to, a master electrician unless such electrician shall have a valid bond in full force and effect.
(Code 1965, § 10-32; Ord. No. 461, § 2, 3-11-96)
Sec. 6-38. - Revocation of registration.
Any person who shall continue persistently to violate the provisions of this article and the lawful orders of the electrical inspector shall be subject to have his registration revoked.
(Code 1965, § 10-28; Ord. No. 440, § 7, 12-12-94)
Secs. 6-39—6-43. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - PERMITS AND INSPECTIONS

Sec. 6-44. - Permit required.
No person shall do any electrical work or wiring of any character, make any change, extension, or disconnect wiring, or change the location of any fixture or service connection without having in such person's possession a permit from the building inspector.
(Code 1965, § 10-41; Ord. No. 440, § 8, 12-12-94)
Sec. 6-45. - Permit application—City to furnish forms.
Forms for application for electrical permits shall be furnished by the city.
(Code 1965, § 10-42)
Sec. 6-46. - Same—Contents; accompanying schedules.
(a)
An application for an electrical permit, describing the work to be done, shall be made in writing to the electrical inspector by the electrician who will install the work.
(b)
The application shall be accompanied by such specifications and schedules as will allow the electrical inspector to determine whether the installation will be in conformity with the requirements of this article and, when deemed necessary by the inspector, the application shall be accompanied by drawings.
Code 1965, § 10-43)
Sec. 6-47. - Issuance of permit—Upon compliance.
If the electrical inspector shall find that the proposed installation conforms with all legal requirements and if the applicant has complied with the provisions of this article, a permit for such installation shall be issued.
(Code 1965, § 10-45)
Sec. 6-48. - Same—To whom.
A permit to do electrical work or wiring shall be issued only to a master electrician possessing a valid license.
(Code 1965, § 10-46)
Sec. 6-49. - Deviations from permit.
No deviation shall be made from the installation described in the permit without the express approval of the electrical inspector.
(Code 1965, § 10-47)
Sec. 6-50. - Annual permit under certain conditions.
(a)
In lieu of an individual permit for each installation or alteration, an annual permit shall, upon application therefor, be issued to any person regularly employing one or more electricians for the installation, alteration and maintenance of electrical equipment in or on buildings or premises owned or occupied by the applicant for the permit.
(b)
The application for an annual permit shall be made in writing to the electrical inspector and shall contain a description of the premises on which work is to be done under the permit.
(c)
The person to whom an annual permit is issued shall keep a record of all electrical equipment installed under such permit, and the electrical inspector shall have access to such records.
(d)
Each annual permit shall expire on the thirty-first day of March next after issuance.
(Code 1965, § 10-48)
Secs. 6-51, 6-52. - Reserved.
Editor's note— Ord. No. 440, § 9, adopted Dec. 12, 1994, provided that §§ 6-51 and 6-52 be stricken from the Code. Formerly, such sections pertained to inspection fees as derived from the 1965 Code, §§ 10-57 and 10-58.
Sec. 6-53. - Inspection prior to concealment.
When any electrical equipment authorized by an individual permit or an annual permit is to be concealed against inspection by the permanent placement of parts of a building, the person installing the equipment shall notify the electrical inspector, and such equipment shall not be concealed until after it has been inspected and approved by the electrical inspector.
(Code 1965, § 10-60)
Sec. 6-54. - Notifications to electrical inspector for final inspection.
Upon completion of the wiring of any building for light, heat or power, the person performing the work shall notify the electrical inspector, who shall inspect such wiring and appliances.
(Code 1965, § 10-59)
Sec. 6-55. - Certificates of satisfactory inspection—Issuance.
(a)
If the electrical inspector shall approve any installation inspected, such inspector shall issue a certificate of satisfactory inspection, which shall contain the date of such inspection.
(b)
No certificate of satisfactory inspection shall be issued unless electrical wiring and appliance has been found to be in strict conformity to the provisions of this article.
(Code 1965, § 10-61)
Sec. 6-56. - Same—Required before turning on current.
No current shall be turned on for any installation until a certificate of satisfactory inspection has been issued.
(Code 1965, § 10-62)
Sec. 6-57. - Failure to remedy defects.
Any person who shall fail to correct any defect in such person's work after having been duly notified by the electrical inspector, in addition to being subject to punishment as provided in section 1-8, shall receive no further permits until such defect has been corrected.
(Code 1965, § 10-63)
Secs. 6-58—6-69. - Reserved.
