Article 10.5 - Pawnbrokers

Section 10.5.1 - Definition.
For the purpose of this article, a pawnbroker is any person whose business is to lend money, usually in small sums, on security of personal property deposited with him or left in pawn, or whoever loans money on deposit of pledges of personal property or who purchases personal property or chooses in action on condition of selling the same back again at a stipulated price.
Section 10.5.2 - Business Hours.
No pawnbroker shall open his place of business or conduct any business therein between the hours of 9:00 p.m. and 8:00 a.m.; provided however, that such a place of business may remain open until 9:00 p.m. on Fridays and until 9:00 p.m. on Saturdays.
Section 10.5.3 - Sunday Operation Prohibited.
No pawnbroker shall open his place of business or conduct any business therein on Sunday.
Section 10.5.4 - Pledges to be Openly Displayed.
Every pawnbroker shall have his pledges openly displayed so that the inspection authorized by this article may be easily made.
Section 10.5.5 - Premises to be Open to Police.
It shall be the duty of every pawnbroker to admit to his premises the chief of police or any other police officer who may desire at any time to search for articles missing or stolen, or to make any inspection authorized by this article, without the formality of a search warrant.
Section 10.5.6 - Inspection of Pledges and Purchases.
All articles pledged or sold to pawnbrokers shall, at all times, be subject to inspection and examination by the police.
Section 10.5.7 - Maintenance of Records.
All pawnbrokers shall keep records as required by O.C.G.A. §§ 44-12-132—44-12-134. The entries required shall be made as soon after the transaction as is possible and, in no event, more than one (1) hour thereafter. All pawnbrokers shall furnish to the chief of police or his designated representative a weekly report of all property pledged or sold to them with an accurate description of the property to include, but not be limited to, the maker of the articles, the serial number and any other identifying mark. These reports shall be made on each Wednesday for the preceding week, unless sooner requested by the chief of police.
Section 10.5.8 - Same—Inspection.
The records required to be kept by section 10.5.7 and state law shall, at all times, be subject to inspection and examination by the police.
Section 10.5.9 - Transactions with Minors Prohibited.
No pawnbroker shall receive any article in pawn or purchase any article for resale from any person under eighteen (18) years of age.
Section 10.5.10 - Duration of Pawn Transactions.
The provisions of O.C.G.A. § 44-12-131 shall govern the duration of all pawn transactions. In addition there shall be a grace period on pawn transactions as required by O.C.G.A. § 44-14-403.
Section 10.5.11 - License.
A pawnbroker's license must be taken out in the name of an individual, even though such business is operated as a partnership or a corporation. The license fee for a pawnbroker is $300.00.
