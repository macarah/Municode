Chapter 6 - ANIMALS
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 6-1. - Definitions.
modified
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Abandoned animal, means any animal considered abandoned under the following conditions:
(1)
Any animal placed upon public property or within a public building and left unattended or uncared for;
(2)
Any animal placed upon private property of another without the express permission of the owner, custodian, or tenant and left unattended or uncared for; or
(3)
Any animal left on the property of the owner or custodian of such animal for more than 36 hours unattended and without food and water.
Adequate food and water means food and water that is sufficient in amount and appropriate for the particular type of animal to prevent starvation, dehydration, or a significant risk to the animal's health from a lack of food or water.
Animal means any description of vertebrate, excluding Homo Sapiens.
Animal control, animal control officer, and animal control officers mean designated employees or officers of the county.
Animal establishment means any pet shop, kennel, grooming shop, auction, performing animal exhibition or other facility engaged in the handling of animals, excluding licensed veterinarians and veterinary clinics and hospitals.
Animal nuisance means an animal shall be considered a nuisance if it:
(1)
Damages, soils, defiles, or defecates on private property (other than the property of the owner of the animal), or public property and recreational areas;
(2)
Causes unsanitary, dangerous, or offensive conditions;
(3)
Causes a disturbance by excessive barking or other noisemaking;
(4)
Attacks or interferes with persons on public or private property, unless the animal is a guard dog actively performing its duties while confined to the property of the owner or responsible person;
(5)
Chases vehicles;
(6)
Attacks other animals; or
(7)
Runs uncontrolled.
Animal shelter means any facility operated by any governmental entity, humane society, or other organization for the purpose of impounding or harboring seized, stray, homeless, abandoned, or unwanted dogs, cats, and other animals held under the authority of this chapter or state law.
At large means any animal off the premises of the owner of the animal and is not under the control of a responsible person.
Auctions mean any place or facility where animals are regularly bought, sold or traded, except for those facilities otherwise defined in this chapter. Individual sales of animals by owners are not auctions as herein defined.
Cruelty means every act, omission or neglect whereby unjustifiable pain, suffering, maiming or death may be caused or permitted to any animal as defined in this section.
Dangerous dog means any dog that, according to the records of an appropriate authority:
(1)
Inflicts a severe injury on a human being without provocation on public or private property; or
(2)
Aggressively bites, attacks, or endangers the safety of humans without provocation after the dog has been classified as a potentially dangerous dog and after the owner has been notified of such classification.
Dog control officer means an individual selected by the board of commissioners to aid in the administration and enforcement of the provisions of this chapter.
Fowl means any warm-blooded, feathered, flying or non-flying animals.
Humane manner means care of an animal to include, but not to be limited to, safety, adequate heat, ventilation, sanitary shelter, wholesome fresh food, and access to fresh, clean, wholesome water at all times, consistent with the normal requirements and feeding habits of the animal's size, species and breed.
Kennel means any premises wherein any person engaged in the business of boarding, breeding, buying, letting for hire, training for a fee or selling animals.
Livestock means any animal that has hooves.
Neutered or spayed means an animal that was surgically made to be incapable of sexual reproduction.
Owner means any person who has title to or the right to possession of an animal, including, but not limited to, the caretaker or custodian of any animal or person harboring or having control of an animal. An animal is deeded in a person's possession if it is fed or sheltered for three consecutive days or more.
Pet means any animal kept for pleasure, excluding livestock.
Pet shop means any person engaged in the business of buying or selling two or more species of live animals with the intent that they be kept as pets.
Potentially dangerous dog means any dog that without provocation bites a human being on public or private property.
Proper enclosure means an enclosure for keeping a dangerous dog, potentially dangerous, or vicious dog while on the owner's property securely confined indoors or in a securely enclosed and locked pen, fence, or structure, suitable to prevent the entry of young children and designed to prevent the dog from escaping. Any such pen or structure shall have secure sides and a secure top, and, if the dog is enclosed within a fence, all sides of the fence shall be of sufficient height and the bottom of the fence shall be constructed or secured in such a manner as to prevent the dog's escape whether from over or from under the fence. Any such enclosure shall also provide protection from the elements for the dog.
Records of an appropriate authority mean records of any state, county, or municipal law enforcement agency; records of any county or municipal animal control agency; records of any county board of health; records of any federal, state, or local court; or records of a dog control officer provided for in this article.
Responsible person means any accountable human being.
Severe injury means any physical injury that results in broken bones or disfiguring lacerations requiring multiple sutures or cosmetic surgery or a physical injury that results in death.
Under control means any animal that is confined by fence, pen, cage, electronic device, chain, leash or secure enclosure to the premises of its owner, of sufficient strength to prevent it from escaping from the premises of its owner, any person owning, possessing, harboring, keeping or having custody or control of any animal subject to this chapter. This definition is intended to embrace any person who is a custodian.
Veterinary clinic or veterinary hospital means any clinic or hospital operated by a licensed veterinarian.
Vicious dog means any dog that:
(1)
Inflicts a severe injury on a human being without provocation after the owner has notice that the dog has previously bitten or attacked or endangered the safety of a human being; or
(2)
Is owned, possessed, kept, harbored, trained, or maintained for the purpose of fighting.
(Ord. No. 20-125, § 5.1-1, 4-9-1991; Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-2, 10-11-1994)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. §§ 4-8-21, 4-8-41.

The provisions of this chapter shall be enforced by animal control and by the county sheriff's department.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-80, 10-11-1994)

(a)
The animal control officers, sheriff's deputies and other enforcement officers of the county are hereby authorized to enter upon any premises or parcel of land for the purpose of seizing and impounding any animal found therein or thereon to be in violation of this chapter, including, but not limited to, an animal that has bitten a person within ten days.
(b)
Animal control may take all necessary steps to remove any animal locked in a closed vehicle if the animal is subject to danger of injury due to temperature. No person shall interfere with or hinder animal control or any officer in the performance of their duty or seek to release any animal in the custody of animal control, except as herein provided.

Representatives of animal control and officers of the county sheriff's department are hereby authorized to issue a summons to any person for violation of any provision of this chapter. The summons shall be in a form approved by the county magistrate court, shall designate the offense charged and shall require the person so charged to appear before the county magistrate court on a date certain to answer the charges therein contained.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-83, 10-11-1994)
Sec. 6-6. - Use of equipment authorized.
Animal control is authorized to employ any equipment it deems necessary to enforce the provisions of this chapter, including, but without limitation, make available humane box traps, and animal control may, subject to conditions it may determine, loan or lease traps or other equipment to private persons, residing in the county for the purpose of preventing nuisances resulting from animals at large.

ARTICLE II. - ANIMAL CONTROL
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 6-31. - Cruelty to animals.
It shall be unlawful for any person, either by commission or omission to:
(1)
Confine or transport an animal in any other than a humane manner;
(2)
Overdrive, overload, overwork, torture, beat, mutilate, kill needlessly, carry or contain in a vehicle in an inhumane manner, or otherwise mistreat any animal;
(3)
Fail to provide any animal with proper food consistent with the normal requirements and feeding habits of the animal's size, species and breed;
(4)
Fail to provide any animal with access to shelter adequate to protect it from all types of weather, 24 hours daily;
(5)
Fail to provide any animal access to good and wholesome fresh water at all times;
(6)
Fail to provide necessary veterinary care;
(7)
Knowingly or willingly permit or aid in the release of any animal on any property with the intention of abandoning the animal;
(8)
Intentionally poison any animal;
(9)
Allow any animal to be kept in unsanitary conditions; or
(10)
Allow or promote any fight between animals, or to allow or permit any such fight in or upon any premises in such person's possession or under such person's control.
(Ord. No. 20-150, §§ 5-42, 5-50, 10-11-1994)
Sec. 6-32. - Animals locked in vehicles.
Animal control may take all necessary steps to remove any animal locked in a closed vehicle if the animal is subject to danger of injury due to the temperature. The operator of the vehicle will be charged with cruelty to animals as defined in this chapter. No injunction or claim for damages may be placed against the animal control or its officers for this action.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-82, 10-11-1994)
Sec. 6-33. - Removal of animals; costs and fees.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the animal control officer to remove any animal suspected of being mistreated by the owner, from the property of the owner, if the officer deems it hazardous to the animal's welfare to leave the animal under the care of said owner.
(b)
The cost of any veterinary fees, impounding fees, and transport fees are to borne by the owner of the animal.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-50(b), (c), 10-11-1994)
Secs. 6-34—6-54. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - DOGS AND CATS

Sec. 6-55. - Nuisance prohibited.
An animal owners' premises shall be maintained in such a manner as not to constitute either a private nuisance to adjoining property owners of a nuisance to the public in general. Pens or enclosures in which animals are confined or maintained shall at all times be kept and maintained in a proper, clean, and sanitary condition so they are kept free from offensive odors which would disturb any person residing within a reasonable distance of the premises. The animals themselves shall be restrained in such a fashion that they shall not disturb such persons by making noise.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-40, 10-11-1994)
Sec. 6-56. - Confinement required.
(a)
In any city that has adopted these provisions and contracted for county enforcement of them, the owner of a dog or cat residing within any city limit boundaries in any platted subdivision in which the average lot size is two acres or less, or in any mobile home park, shall confine the dog or cat in a secure enclosure by a chain or leash affixed to the animal's collar and attached to some substantial stationary object adequate to prevent the dog or cat from running at large.
(b)
It shall further be the duty of any owner or keeper of any dog or cat regardless of the size of the owner's lot or property, to keep the dog or cat under such control so as to:
(1)
Prevent the animal from becoming a danger to persons or property at any location, or trespassing upon another person's property without that person's permission.
(2)
Prevent such animal from running at large upon the streets, roads, sidewalks, alleys, parks, or other public places.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-42(a), 10-11-1994)
Sec. 6-57. - Females in heat.
Every female dog or cat in heat shall be confined in a building or secure enclosure or in a veterinary clinic or hospital or in a kennel or under control in such a manner that such female dog or cat cannot come into contact with a male dog or cat, except for intentional breeding purposes.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-42(b), 10-11-1994)
Sec. 6-58. - Hunting dogs.
Hunting dogs in the custody of persons hunting in accordance with state game and fish department laws, rules, and regulations are exempt from these mandates. The owners of such dogs shall be civilly liable for any damage occasioned by the actions of their dogs.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-42(d), 10-11-1994)

ARTICLE III. - RABIES CONTROL

Sec. 6-111. - Adoption of state regulations.
All applicable rules and regulations of the state department of human resources pertaining to rabies control and vaccination are incorporated herein by reference as though fully set forth in this chapter.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-31(a)(2), 10-11-1994)
Sec. 6-112. - Vaccination required.
All dogs and cats over four months of age within the county shall have their animals inoculated for the prevention of rabies. No person shall own, keep or harbor any dog or cat within the county unless the dog or cat is vaccinated, as herein provided.
(Ord. No. 20-150, §§ 5-3, 5-31(a), 10-11-1994)
Sec. 6-113. - Rabies reporting.
(a)
Every veterinarian shall report promptly to the state department of human resources and the county animal control all cases of rabies in animals treated by such veterinarian, giving the name and address of the owner. Every veterinarian shall also report the names and addresses of the owner of any animals bitten by such rabid animal, so far as known.
(b)
Any person who knows that an animal, domestic or wild, is rabid or suspects an animal of having rabies, or knows that an animal has been bitten by a rabid animal, shall promptly report such information, to the extent known, to the state department of human resources and the county animal control.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-43(a), (b), 10-11-1994)
Sec. 6-114. - Impoundment required.
It shall be the duty of the owner, the health department, physicians, hospitals or other person gaining information that any domestic animal has been bitten or is probably infected with rabies, to incarcerate or impound or have the animal incarcerated or impounded in the facility of some competent veterinarian within this county, where the animal shall be held for observation for such period of time as may be reasonably necessary to determine whether the animal is infected with rabies. All costs of this incarceration shall be borne by the owner of the animal.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-43(c), 10-11-1994)

A dog that inflicts an injury upon a person when the dog is being used by a law enforcement officer to carry out the law enforcement officer's official duties shall not be a dangerous, potentially dangerous, or vicious dog within the meaning of this article.

DIVISION 2. - ANIMAL CONTROL OFFICER

Sec. 6-170. - Dog control officer; appointment; duties.
The board of commissioners shall appoint a dog control officer who shall have the following duties:
(1)
To make such investigations and inquiries as may be necessary to identify dangerous dogs and dangerous dog owners within the county, and within the corporate limits of any municipal corporation in the county that contracts with the county for enforcement of this chapter.
(2)
To classify or reclassify dogs as dangerous dogs or as potentially dogs as defined in this chapter.
(3)
To follow all post-classification procedures as set out in this chapter and to present evidence at any hearings held pursuant to this code chapter.
(4)
To issue certificates of registration to owners of dangerous and potentially dangerous dogs as provided in this chapter.
(5)
To ensure that dangerous dogs and potentially dangerous dogs are properly enclosed in accordance with the terms of this chapter.
(6)
To confiscate and, in certain cases in accordance with this chapter, destroy certain dangerous and potentially dangerous dogs.
(7)
To prosecute violations of this chapter as provided by law.
(Ord. No. 20-125, § 5.1-2, 4-9-1991)

Sec. 6-196. - Investigation.
Upon receiving a report of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog within a dog control officer's jurisdiction from a law enforcement agency, animal control agency, rabies control officer, or county board of health, the dog control officer shall make such investigations and inquiries with regard to such report as may be necessary to carry out the provisions of this article.

Sec. 6-198. - Registration—Potentially dangerous dogs.
The dog control officer shall issue a certificate of registration to the owners of potentially dangerous dogs when the owner shows the dog control officer sufficient evidence of the following:
(1)
A proper enclosure sufficient to confine the dog.
(2)
The posting on the premises where the dog is located with a clearly visible sign warning that there is a potentially dangerous dog located on the premises and containing a symbol reasonably calculated to inform children of the presence of the potentially dangerous dog.
(Ord. No. 20-125, § 5.1-6(a), 4-9-1991)
Sec. 6-199. - Same—Dangerous dogs.
The dog control officer shall issue a certificate of registration to the owners of dangerous dogs when the owner shows the dog control officer sufficient evidence of the following:
(1)
A proper enclosure sufficient to confine the dog;
(2)
The posting on the premises where the dog is located with a clearly visible sign warning that there is a dangerous dog located on the premises and containing a symbol reasonably calculated to inform children of the presence of the dangerous dog;
(3)
An insurance policy in the amount of at least $15,000.00 issued by a surety company authorized to do business in the state payable to any person injured by a dangerous dog.
(Ord. No. 20-125, § 5.1-6(b), 4-9-1991)
Sec. 6-200. - Renewal of registration.
Certificates of registration shall be renewed on an annual basis. At the time of the annual renewal of a certificate of registration, a dog control officer shall require evidence from the owner or make such investigation as may be necessary to verify that the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog is continuing to be confined in a proper enclosure and that the owner is continuing to comply with other provisions of this division.
(Ord. No. 20-125, § 5.1-6(c), 4-9-1991)
Sec. 6-201. - Annual registration fees.
The annual fee for the issuance or renewal of a certificate of registration for a potentially dangerous or dangerous dog shall be as established by resolution of the board of commissioners, but not less than $500.00.
(Ord. No. 20-125, § 5.1-6(c), 4-9-1991)
Sec. 6-202. - Duty of owner to notify regarding changes in status.
(a)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall notify the dog control officer within 24 hours if the dog is on the loose, is unconfined, has attacked a human, has died, or has been sold or donated. If the dog has been sold or donated, the owner shall also provide the dog control officer with the name, address, and telephone number of the new owner of the dog.
(b)
The owner of a dangerous or potentially dangerous dog shall notify the dog control officer if the owner is moving from the dog control officer's jurisdiction. The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog who is a new resident of the state shall register the dog as required in this division within 30 days after becoming a resident. The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog who moves from one jurisdiction to another within the state shall register the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog in the new jurisdiction within ten days after becoming a resident.
(Ord. No. 20-125, §§ 5.1-6, 5.1-7, 4-9-1991)

DIVISION 4. - VICIOUS DOGS

Sec. 6-234. - Restraint required.
It is unlawful for an owner of a vicious dog to permit the dog to be outside a proper enclosure unless the dog is restrained by a leash and is under the physical restraint of a responsible person.
Sec. 6-235. - Confiscation.
(a)
A vicious dog shall be immediately confiscated by the dog control officer or by a law enforcement officer or by another person authorized by the dog control officer if the dog is not maintained in a proper enclosure or is outside a proper enclosure.
(b)
Any dog that has been confiscated under the provisions of this section shall be returned to its owner upon the owner's compliance with the provisions of this division and upon the payment of reasonable confiscation costs. In the event the owner has not complied with the provisions of this division within 40 days of the date the dog was confiscated, the dog shall be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner.

ARTICLE V. - IMPOUNDMENT AND DISPOSITION

Sec. 6-264. - Recordkeeping.
It shall be the duty of animal control to keep or cause to be kept, accurate and detailed records of the impoundment and disposition of all animals coming into its custody.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-90, 10-11-1994)
Sec. 6-265. - Confinement; ownership; disposition.
(a)
Any animal at large or otherwise in violation of the provisions of this article may be impounded in the animal shelter in a humane manner, for a period of not less than three days. If within such time the animal is not reclaimed by its owner as provided herein the animal shall become the absolute property of the county.
(b)
The county may convey ownership to any responsible person on such conditions as county animal control may prescribe, or county animal control may humanely destroy the animal.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-70(a), 10-11-1994)
Sec. 6-266. - Sick or injured animals.
(a)
Any impounded animal that is determined to be injured and in pain will be transported promptly to a veterinarian for appropriate medical care. The cost of that care shall be borne by animal control unless the animal is redeemed by the owner, in which event the veterinary costs shall be paid by the owner as provided herein.
(b)
Notwithstanding any provision of this article to the contrary, animal control may refuse to release any animal impounded in the animal shelter for rabies or contagious disease quarantine or for the use as evidence in a criminal prosecution, for such time period as the animal control may determine.
(c)
Notwithstanding any provision of this article to the contrary, animal control may humanely destroy any animal impounded in the animal shelter upon the written opinion of a licensed veterinarian that destruction of the animal is necessary to prevent disease or injury to the animals or to humans due to overcrowding in the animal shelter, the presence or threatened presence of contagious disease, the likelihood of danger or injury to humans or animals, or any other condition.
(d)
Notwithstanding any provision of this article to the contrary, animal control may humanely destroy any animal impounded in the animal shelter when a veterinarian has determined and animal control reasonably believes the animal has sustained an injury or disease that will likely result in maiming, prolonged or severe suffering, or death.
(e)
In the event an impounded animal must be destroyed as a result of illness or injury prior to the expiration of three days of impoundment, a reasonable effort shall be made to immediately contact the animal's owner, notwithstanding the remaining provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-70(b), (e)—(g), 10-11-1994)
Sec. 6-267. - Notice to owner.
Following the impoundment of an animal that has displayed on its collar its owner's address, or if the animal is found to be microchipped, and prior to its destruction, animal control shall give the owner seven day's notice of the proposed destruction by certified letter, return receipt requested and by regular mail. If the identification includes the owner's telephone number, animal control may also attempt to notify the owner by telephone.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-70(d), 10-11-1994)
Sec. 6-268. - Redemption of impounded animals.
(a)
The owner of an animal impounded in the animal shelter may reclaim the animal upon presenting evidence satisfactory to animal control of compliance with all provisions of this article, and upon payment of fees and charges as hereinafter provided, credited to the account of the animal control. Fees and charges paid pursuant to this article shall not be in lieu of any fine or penalty otherwise provided by law.
(b)
Owners of impounded animals shall bring proof of vaccination when claiming their animals from the shelter. If the owner is unable to provide proof of vaccination, he may redeem the animal as provided herein, but shall be required to bring proof of rabies vaccination to the shelter within 48 hours of release of the animal. Failure to provide proof of vaccination within 48 hours of release of the animal shall result in issuance of a citation to the owner.
(c)
Fees for reclaiming impounded animals shall be established by the board of commissioners. A schedule of fees shall be available at animal control. Fees for animals impounded for rabies quarantine or for use as evidence in a criminal prosecution shall include the cost to the animal shelter. Costs may, in animal control's discretion, include veterinary costs arising from treatment of sick or injured animals.
(d)
The owner of an animal impounded in the animal shelter shall be liable for the foregoing fees and charges notwithstanding the destruction or adoption the animal.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-71, 10-11-1994)
Sec. 6-269. - Repeat offenses.
If an animal has been impounded for a third offense, the owner shall be required to have the animal microchipped as a permanent means of identification, and bring proof to the animal shelter within 48 hours of reclaiming the animal. Failure to comply shall result in issuance of a citation to the owner.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-71(b)(2), 10-11-1994)
Sec. 6-270. - Adoption; fees and costs.
Animal control may convey ownership of any animal that has become the property of animal control to any responsible person subject to meeting conditions as may be prescribed by animal control, including, but without limitation, the payment of:
(1)
An adoption fee in the amount established by the board of commissioners.
(2)
Rabies vaccination costs for vaccinations given by a licensed veterinarian and any associated fees.
(3)
Veterinary fees associated with the spaying or neutering of any animal adopted from the shelter.
(4)
Any fees associated with the microchipping of the animal.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-72, 10-11-1994)
Sec. 6-271. - Relinquishment.
Any owner that no longer wants his animal and wishes to relinquish all rights to the animal, shall sign a written agreement giving all legal right in and to the animal to the county. Animal control shall notify the owner, in writing and verbally, that the animal will be euthanized, at the discretion of animal control, so as to prevent overcrowding at the shelter of unwanted animals.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-73, 10-11-1994)
Secs. 6-272—6-290. - Reserved.
ARTICLE VI. - ANIMAL ESTABLISHMENTS

Sec. 6-291. - Penalties; suspension or revocation of license or permit.
In addition to penalties provided in section 1-9, any violation of this section may result in suspension or revocation of any business license or permit issued to the establishment by the county.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-62, 10-11-1994)
Sec. 6-292. - Sale prohibited without rabies vaccination.
No animal establishment shall sell, trade, or give away any animal over three months of age unless the animal has been vaccinated as required by this article.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-62(a), 10-11-1994)
Sec. 6-293. - Inspection.
Animal control shall be permitted to inspect any animal establishments, including the animals and the premises where the animals are kept, at any reasonable time during normal business hours to ensure compliance with all provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-62(b), 10-11-1994)
Sec. 6-294. - Minimum standards.
All animal establishments shall, in addition to the other requirements of this article, comply with the following minimum standards:
(1)
Enclosures must be provided that allow adequate protection against weather extremes.
(2)
Floors of buildings, runs, and walls shall be of an impervious material to permit proper cleaning and disinfection.
(3)
Building temperatures shall be maintained at a comfortable level to ensure adequate ventilation.
(4)
Each animal shall have sufficient space to stand up, lie down, and turn around without touching the sides or top of cages.
(5)
Cages are to be of a nonporous material and construction that permit cleaning and sanitizing.
(6)
Cage floors shall be of concrete, unless radiantly heated, and shall have a resting board or some type of bedding.
(7)
Runs shall provide an adequate exercise area and protection from the weather.
(8)
Runs shall have an impervious surface.
(9)
All animal quarters and runs are to be kept clean, dry, and in a sanitary condition.
(10)
The food shall be free from contamination, and shall be wholesome, palatable, and of sufficient quantity and nutritive value to meet the normal daily requirements for the condition and size of the animals.
(11)
All animals shall have fresh water available at all times.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-62(c)1, 10-11-1994)
Sec. 6-295. - Additional requirements for pet shops.
All pet shops, including pet shops operated in conjunction with another holding facility, shall, in addition to the other requirements of this article, comply with the following minimum standards:
(1)
Hot water at a minimum temperature of 160 degrees shall be available for washing and disinfection.
(2)
Cold water shall be easily accessible in all parts of the shop.
(3)
Fresh water for consumption shall be available to all species of animals at all times.
(4)
Food and water containers are to be cleaned and disinfected each day.
(5)
The room temperature of the shop shall be maintained at a level that is healthy for every species of animal kept in the shop.
(Ord. No. 20-150, § 5-62(c)2, 10-11-1994)
