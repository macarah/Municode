Sec. 32-19. - Maintenance and repair.
The Public Works Department shall maintain and repair the streets and sidewalks of the city as may be necessary.
(Code 1979, § 5-1-1; Code 1998, § 66-1)
Sec. 32-20. - Display of merchandise.
No person shall use any part of the streets or sidewalks of the city for the display of his goods or merchandise, except that goods or merchandise may be displayed on sidewalks in the historic business district provided such display does not interfere with pedestrian travel.
(Code 1979, § 5-1-3; Code 1998, § 66-2)
Sec. 32-21. - Obstructions.
No person shall obstruct, or cause to be obstructed, any of the streets, sidewalks or alleys of the city.
(Code 1979, § 5-1-4; Code 1998, § 66-3)
Sec. 32-22. - Temporary obstructions; permission.
(a)
Notwithstanding the provisions of sections 32-20 and 32-21, the Public Works Department Head may grant written permission for the temporary obstruction of streets and sidewalks, but written permission shall not be granted except in cases of obvious necessity. The Public Works Department Head is authorized and directed to cause any obstruction placed in violation of this section to be immediately removed upon discovery.
(b)
For the purpose of this section, the term "obstruction" shall include any barricade, excavation, trash, lumber, wood, glass or other object of any kind which will interfere with the free use by the public of any city street or sidewalk.
(Code 1979, § 5-1-5; Code 1998, § 66-4)
Sec. 32-23. - Street cutting; permission required.
(a)
No person shall cut into, tear up, or remove, for any purpose, any street or sidewalk paving within the city, without first having filed a written request with the Public Works Department Head and received from him written permission for cutting into, tearing up or removing the pavement.
(b)
The written request shall set forth the location and dimensions of the pavement sought to be removed and for what purpose such is to be done.
(c)
The Public Works Department Head, upon receipt of the application, may issue the applicant written permission.
(d)
No person shall cut a larger amount, or remove more pavement than specified in the request, and as set forth in the permission issued by the Public Works Department Head; nor cut into, tear up or remove any other pavement without first having obtained written permission.
(Code 1979, § 5-1-6; Code 1998, § 66-5)
Sec. 32-24. - Inspection of street cuts.
The Public Works Department shall generally supervise and inspect all street cuts authorized by the Department.
(Code 1979, § 5-1-7; Code 1998, § 66-6)
Sec. 32-25. - Excavation and obstruction markers.
All excavations, building materials or obstructions of any kind in any street or sidewalk within the city shall be properly barricaded at all times and lighted at night for the protection of vehicles and pedestrians.
(Code 1979, § 5-1-8; Code 1998, § 66-7)
Sec. 32-26. - Street cleaning; litter collection.
The Public Works Department is hereby authorized and directed to take steps to prevent the casting and leaving upon the streets and public places of the city, wastepaper and other litter, in violation of any provisions of this Code or of any ordinance of the city. For the purpose of taking such actions, the Public Works Department shall cause to be erected and maintained at convenient and suitable places upon the city's streets, alleys and public places, suitable containers for the collection, casting and temporary deposit therein of any wastepaper and other similar articles of litter as are likely to be cast upon the streets. The Public Works Department shall provide and arrange for the cleaning and keeping clean of the containers and the removal and disposition of the wastepaper and litter.
(Code 1979, § 5-1-9; Code 1998, § 66-8)
