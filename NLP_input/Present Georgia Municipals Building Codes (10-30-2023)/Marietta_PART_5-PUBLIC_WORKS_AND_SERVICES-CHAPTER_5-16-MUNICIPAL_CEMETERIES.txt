CHAPTER 5-16 - MUNICIPAL CEMETERIES

5-16-010 - Entrance permit required during certain hours.
No person shall enter upon, loiter in or otherwise use or occupy in any manner any public cemetery in this city between the hours of sunset to sunrise, without a written permit from the parks and recreation director or the police chief of the city. The permit shall be carried upon the person of anyone entering a cemetery during the time frame set out in this section.
(Code 1952, Ch. 9, § 1; Code 1961, § 6-1; Code 1978, § 5-4001; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
5-16-020 - Burning in cemeteries.
It is unlawful for any person to burn any grass or debris upon any lot owned by such person in the city cemetery or other burying ground or to damage any part of any lot or the grass, flowers or tombstones thereon.
(Code 1952, Ch. 9, § 2; Code 1961, § 6-2; Code 1978, § 5-4002; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
5-16-030 - Burials to be made in cemeteries; exemption.
No person shall bury or cause to be buried any dead body in any place in the city, other than the public cemetery or other burying ground, without permission of the city council.
(Code 1952, Ch. 9, § 3; Code 1961, § 6-3; Code 1978, § 5-4003; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
5-16-040 - Obtrusive bushes, shrubs, and plants.
It is unlawful for any person to permit any bush, shrub, arbor, tree or other plant growing on any cemetery lot to extend, overhang or protrude into or on any adjacent cemetery lot which is not the property of the person owning, planting or controlling the bush, shrub, arbor, tree or plant.
(Code 1952, Ch. 9, § 5; Code 1961, § 6-5; Code 1978, § 5-4004; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
5-16-050 - Conveyance of lots in city cemetery; records; interments.
A.
The city clerk may maintain a properly indexed record book providing the name of the person purchasing any lots hereunder, the amount paid and the person interred in each lot.
B.
No person shall be interred in any city owned cemetery without the person's full name being provided to the city clerk.
(Code 1978, § 5-4005; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
5-16-060 - Violation—Penalty.
Any person violating the provisions of this chapter shall, upon conviction thereof, be guilty of a misdemeanor, punishable by a fine not to exceed five hundred dollars ($500.00) per violation or by imprisonment for a period not to exceed ninety (90) days, or by both such fine and imprisonment.
(Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
