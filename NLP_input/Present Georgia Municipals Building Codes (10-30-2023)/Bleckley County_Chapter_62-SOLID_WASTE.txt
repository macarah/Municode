ARTICLE II. - USER FEES

Sec. 62-19. - Purpose and intent.
The county has constructed a convenience station for the receipt, transportation, and disposal of the solid waste of the county in an approved class D landfill. In connection therewith, the county establishes regulations and a reasonable user fee schedule for the use and operation of the county's solid waste convenience station and the transportation and disposal of solid waste therein.
(Ord. of 12-29-1994, art. II(intro.))
State Law reference— Local solid waste regulation, O.C.G.A. § 12-8-30.9; local solid waste management plans, O.C.G.A. § 12-8-31.1.
Sec. 62-20. - Fee schedule.
Users of the county solid waste convenience station shall pay resident or nonresident user fees in the amounts established from time to time by the sole commissioner.
(Ord. of 12-29-1994, § 2.1(1—5))
State Law reference— Cost reimbursement fees and surcharges for local solid waste facilities, O.C.G.A. § 12-8-39; authority for enforcement of fees, O.C.G.A. § 12-8-39.3.
Sec. 62-21. - Terms of payment.
(a)
Each person who brings solid waste to the convenience station will first place recyclable items in bins prior to having the disposable waste weighed. Users shall make payment to the county for waste disposal according to the fee schedule herein within 30 days after the end of the month in which the accumulative user fees for that user reaches the total sum of $25.00. For users whose accumulative fee does not exceed the total sum of $25.00 by the end of December of each year, such accumulated amount shall be paid on or before January 30 of the following year.
(b)
Any user account that has not been paid within 30 days after the same is due shall be turned over to the magistrate court for collection.
(Ord. of 12-29-1994, §§ 2.2, 2.7)

ARTICLE III. - RULES AND REGULATIONS

Sec. 62-46. - Recyclable items; asbestos shingles.
(a)
There will be no charge made for solid waste involving recyclable items (except the required certification that certain items such as refrigerators, freezers and air conditioners are free of Freon) that are placed in separate bins and which include the following:
(1)
Clear glass;
(2)
Dark glass;
(3)
Aluminum;
(4)
Scrap metal;
(5)
White goods, such as washing machines, stoves, hot water heaters, dryers and appliances;
(6)
Wood products, such as limbs, leaves and yard waste;
(7)
Newspaper.
(b)
No asbestos shingles shall be accepted; however, regular non-asbestos shingles will be accepted subject to payment of appropriate user fees.
(Ord. of 12-29-1994, § 2.1(6, 7))
State Law reference— Asbestos Safety Act, O.C.G.A. § 12-12-1 et seq.
Sec. 62-47. - Information to be provided by user.
All users shall be required to provide at the weigh station basic information such as name, telephone number, address, vehicle tag number, and a summary statement of types of solid waste being disposed of.
(Ord. of 12-29-1994, § 2.3)
Sec. 62-48. - Smoking prohibited.
No smoking shall be allowed at weigh station or at any point on the waste disposal site.
(Ord. of 12-29-1994, § 2.4)
Sec. 62-49. - Littering prohibited.
Each user hauling solid waste to the convenience station shall do so in such a manner as to prevent any litter, trash, debris, or any solid waste of any nature from blowing off or falling off the vehicle in which such solid waste is being transported.
(Ord. of 12-29-1994, § 2.5)
Sec. 62-50. - Ashes and hot residue prohibited.
No solid waste of any nature shall be brought to the convenience station that contains hot ashes or other hot residue.
(Ord. of 12-29-1994, § 2.9)
Sec. 62-51. - Penalties.
Any person who violates any of the provisions of this chapter shall be subject to penalties as provided in section 1-7.
(Ord. of 12-29-1994, § 2.8)
Sec. 62-52. - Restraining system required.
new
Any person hauling gravel, soil, garbage, trash, waste, papers, junk, rubbish, debris, or other materials which are likely to blow or fall off any vehicle, truck or trailer hauling such materials shall be required to have the same covered by a tarpaulin, canvas, or other restraining system. It shall unlawful for any person to operate a vehicle, truck, or trailer in violation of this section and it shall also be unlawful for any owner of such vehicle, truck or trailer to knowingly permit the operation of such vehicle, truck or trailer.
Violation of this section may be punishable by a fine up to $1,000.00 and/or 60 days in jail and/or public works.
Any violation of this section may be heard in the magistrate court, the probate court, or the superior court of Bleckley County, Georgia.
(Ord. No. 2017-ORD-001, 5-18-2017)

ARTICLE IV. - SOLID WASTE MANAGEMENT AUTHORITY

Sec. 62-76. - Establishment; purpose and intent.
The county, after diligent study and review, hereby joins with the other participants in finding and declaring that there is a need for an authority to function in the area of solid waste management. In conjunction with the other participants, the county hereby activates the Heart of Georgia Regional Solid Waste Management Authority (the "authority"), a public corporation created as an institution of purely public charity, to perform an essential governmental function on the exercise of the power conferred upon it by this article.
(Ord. of 7-16-1992, § 1)
State Law reference— Local development authorities, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.
Sec. 62-77. - Board of directors.
(a)
The board of directors of the authority shall meet and shall appoint and declare by resolution of the authority an additional member of the board of directors. The members of the board of directors of the authority shall elect one of their members as chairman and another as vice-chairman and shall also elect a secretary and a treasurer or a secretary-treasurer, either of whom may be, but need not be, a member of the board of directors.
(b)
The directors shall receive no compensation for their services but shall be reimbursed for their actual expenses incurred in the performance of their duties.
(c)
The directors may make bylaws and regulations for the governing of the authority and the operation of any projects of the authority and may delegate to one or more of the officers, agents and employees of the authority such powers and duties as may be deemed necessary and proper. The authority shall adopt bylaws providing in detail for its operation, meetings, and other functions, and the authority may provide in such bylaws for the establishment of an executive committee to operate in certain functions of the authority to be specified in such bylaws. The bylaws shall be adopted by the vote of a two-thirds majority of the members of the board of directors of the authority.
(Ord. of 7-16-1992, § 2)
State Law reference— Boards of directors of local development authorities, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-5.
Sec. 62-78. - Powers.
The authority shall have all of the powers granted by law to regional solid waste management authorities, including, but not limited to, the power to research, study, and plan for the solid waste management needs of the participants and to gather data and research all phases of the solid waste management needs of the participants; provided, however, that the authority shall not have the power to borrow money, issue bonds or enter into any financial obligations without the authorization of each of the participants who will participate in the repayment of such obligation.
(Ord. of 7-16-1992, § 3)
State Law reference— Powers of authorities, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-6.
