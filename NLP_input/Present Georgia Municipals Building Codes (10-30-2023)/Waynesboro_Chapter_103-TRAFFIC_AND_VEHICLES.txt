ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 103-1. - Uniform rules of the road adopted; penalty.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 40-6-372, O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 and O.C.G.A § 40-6-1 through O.C.G.A. § 40-6-397, known as "Uniform Rules of the Road", are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of the City of Waynesboro, Georgia, with like effect as if recited in full herein.
(b)
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-8.
(Ord. No. 2016-02-02, § 2, 3-21-2016)
Sec. 103-2. - Pedestrians interfering with traffic.
Pedestrians shall not interfere with the proper use of the public streets, roads and alleys.
(Ord. No. 2016-02-02, § 2, 3-21-2016)
State Law reference— Rights and duties of pedestrians, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-90; pedestrians soliciting rides or business, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-97.
Sec. 103-3. - Parades and demonstrations.
(a)
Definitions. As used in this section:
Assemble shall be construed to mean any group of persons who assemble or congregate on any public street, alley, sidewalk or public park in the city for the purpose of displaying, advertising, promoting, exhibiting, advocating, or otherwise attracting public attention to or for any cause or thing.
Parade shall be construed to mean any group of persons marching or promenading on and along any public street, alley, sidewalk or public park in the city for the purpose of displaying, advertising, promoting, exhibiting, advocating, or otherwise attracting public attention to or for any cause or thing.
(b)
Permit required; unlawful participation. It shall be unlawful for persons to parade or assemble or congregate on any public street, alley, sidewalk or public park in the city for the purposes as defined in this section unless the city council shall have previously approved and authorized such parade or assembly. Any person participating in or attending any parade or assembly which has not been authorized and approved by the city council shall be considered as a part of the group constituting such parade or assembly, and upon conviction thereof shall be punished as provided in section 1-6.
(c)
Application for permit. Any persons desiring to parade or assemble on any of the public streets, alleys, sidewalks or public parks of the city for any of the purposes defined in this section shall file an application, at least 15 days before the date set for such parade or assembly, with the city manager setting forth the time, place and purpose of such parade or assembly, and when such application is approved by the city council, such parade or assembly will be authorized.
(d)
The city council shall issue a parade or assembly permit when, from a consideration of the application and from such other information as may otherwise be obtained, the council finds that:
(1)
The conduct of the parade or assembly will not substantially interrupt the safe and orderly movement of other traffic contiguous to its route;
(2)
The conduct of the parade or assembly will not require the diversion of so great a number of police officers of the city to properly police the line of movement and the areas contiguous thereto as to prevent normal police protection to the city;
(3)
The conduct of such parade or assembly will not require the diversion of so great a number of ambulances as to prevent normal ambulance service to portions of the city other than that to be occupied by the proposed line of march and areas contiguous thereto;
(4)
The concentration of persons, animals and vehicles at assembly points of the parade or assembly will not unduly interfere with proper fire and police protection of, or ambulance service to, areas contiguous to such assembly areas;
(5)
The conduct of such parade or assembly will not interfere with the movement of firefighting equipment en route to a fire;
(6)
The conduct of the parade or assembly is not reasonably likely to cause injury to persons or property, provoke disorderly conduct or create a disturbance;
(7)
The parade or assembly is scheduled to move from its point of origin to its point of termination expeditiously and without unreasonable delays en route;
(8)
The parade or assembly is not to be held for the sole purpose of advertising any product, goods or event, and is not designed to be held purely for private profit.
(Ord. No. 2016-02-02, § 2, 3-21-2016)
State Law reference— Operation of motor vehicles in parades, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-7; power of local authorities to regulate or prohibit processions or assemblages on the highways, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-371(a)(3).
Sec. 103-4. - Driving on sidewalks.
The operator of a vehicle or bicycle shall not drive or ride within any sidewalk area, including sidewalks in shopping centers, except at a permanent or temporary driveway.
(Ord. No. 2016-02-02, § 2, 3-21-2016)
State Law reference— Parking on sidewalk, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-203.
Sec. 103-5. - Truck routes.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for loaded trucks and empty trucks or any other vehicle heavier than five tons to travel on or use the streets of the city other than those streets which are marked "truck route". Nothing in this section shall prevent trucks from entering other streets and the business section of the city for the purpose of collecting solid waste, loading or unloading freight, packages or merchandise. This section shall not apply to city fire and rescue vehicles and city service vehicles.
(b)
Any motor vehicle weighing more than five tons, when loaded or empty, making a scheduled or definite stop within the city for the purpose of loading or unloading shall proceed over or upon designated truck routes set out in this section to the nearest point of its scheduled or definite stop and shall proceed thereto, load or unload and return, by the most direct route to its point of departure from said designated truck route.
(c)
The owner, or any other person, employing or otherwise directing the driver of any vehicle shall not require or knowingly permit the operation of such vehicle upon a street in a manner contrary to this section.
(Ord. No. 2016-02-02, § 2, 3-21-2016)
State Law reference— Power of local authorities to regulate the use of heavily traveled streets, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-371(a)(13).
Sec. 103-6. - Load limits on residential streets.
(a)
When signs are erected giving notice thereof, it shall be unlawful for any person to operate a vehicle with a gross weight in excess of the amounts specified on such signs at any time upon any of the streets or parts of streets for which said signs are erected.
(b)
The council may, upon application and good cause being shown, issue a special permit in writing authorizing the applicant to operate or move a vehicle or combination of vehicles of a size or weight exceeding the maximum gross weight specified by posted signs over those streets named in the permit which are under the jurisdiction of the city.
(Ord. No. 2016-02-02, § 2, 3-21-2016)
Sec. 103-7. - Obstructing traffic at schools.
It shall be unlawful for any person to park a vehicle on the street in front of any school where signs are erected or markings placed indicating no parking.
(Ord. No. 2016-02-02, § 2, 3-21-2016)
Sec. 103-8. - Operating vehicle to avoid traffic control device.
It shall be unlawful for any person to cause any vehicle to travel over or through any private property in an attempt to avoid or fail to comply with any traffic control devices or any lawful order or direction of any law enforcement officer with authority to direct, control or regulate traffic within the limits of the city.
(Ord. No. 2016-02-02, § 2, 3-21-2016)
State Law reference— Traffic signs, signals and markings, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-20 et seq.
Sec. 103-9. - Operation of recreational vehicles on public streets.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a recreational vehicle upon the public streets, paved recreational paths, sidewalks, and other areas accessible by the public located within the city unless the recreational vehicle is both designed for use on public roads and subject to motor vehicle registration. "Recreational vehicles" shall be defined as any pleasure cart which is powered by electricity, battery, or gasoline/petroleum product and specifically includes those vehicles commonly referred to as go-carts, four-wheeler vehicles, ATV's, mini-bikes, dune buggies or any other land motor vehicle designed for recreational use off public roads unless such vehicle has been licensed for use on public roadways by a competent licensing authority. Golf carts are excluded from the definition of recreational vehicles contained in this section.
(Ord. No. 2016-02-02, § 2, 3-21-2016)

Sec. 103-31. - Maximum limit generally.
The maximum speed limit at all points within the city, unless otherwise provided, shall be 30 miles per hour. This maximum limit shall not be construed to permit the operation of vehicles at such rate if common prudence upon the part of the operator would require a lower rate of speed under existing circumstances.
(Ord. No. 2016-02-02, § 2, 3-21-2016)

The council may from time to time establish speed limits on particular streets or parts of streets greater or less than 30 miles per hour. Such streets and portions of streets and the speeds thereon are as shown in enactments of council on file in the city manager's office.

ARTICLE III. - PARKING[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— Stopping, standing and parking, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-200 et seq.; authority to regulate or prohibit stopping, standing, or parking, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-371(a)(1).

Sec. 103-56. - Definitions.
For the purpose of this article, the following words shall have the definitions as provided:
(a)
Controlled parking space shall mean any parking space that is designated for a specified purpose and to be used by certain vehicles only, such as, but not limited to, bus zones, loading zones, official vehicle parking spaces and handicapped parking spaces.
(b)
Improper parking charge shall mean the monetary charge assessed against the owner or operator of a vehicle that is occupying a parking space beyond the maximum legally established time for that parking space or which is parked contrary to the provisions of this article.
(c)
Notice shall mean the notice or ticket issued by an officer of the city informing the owner or operator of a vehicle that such vehicle is parked contrary to the provisions of this article.
(d)
Officer shall mean either an authorized civilian employee of the city police department or an authorized police officer of the city police department.
(e)
Official vehicles shall mean any authorized vehicle operated by the city for official purposes, including but not limited to vehicles operated by the city police department, fire department, and public works department.
(f)
Operator shall mean and include every individual who operates a vehicle as the owner thereof, or as the agent, employee or licensee of the owner, who is in actual control of a vehicle.
(g)
Owner shall mean the registered owner of a vehicle, according to the records maintained by the state department of motor vehicles.
(h)
Park or parking shall mean the standing or stopping of a vehicle, whether occupied or not, upon a street, except temporarily for the purpose of, and while actually engaged in, receiving or discharging passengers; loading or unloading goods or merchandise; or in the obedience to traffic regulations, signs or signals.
(i)
Parking space shall mean any area or space on a street or public place where a vehicle legally may park.
(j)
Person shall mean and include any individual, firm, partnership, corporation, or other association.
(k)
Public place shall mean any real property owned or leased by the city, including but not limited to public parking areas.
(l)
State department of motor vehicles shall mean any state governmental agency responsible for the issuance of certificates of title and/or certificates of registration for vehicles.
(m)
Street shall mean the entire width between the boundary lines of any public highway, street, road, right-of-way, alley, or way, located in the city and maintained by the state or city or subdivisions thereof.
(Ord. No. 2016-02-02, § 2, 3-21-2016)
Sec. 103-57. - Regulations.
(a)
No person shall park a vehicle or permit it to stand whether attended or unattended upon the following streets for more than two consecutive hours.
(1)
Liberty Street between 8 th Street and 6 th Street.
(2)
6 th Street from Shadrack Street to Commerce Street.
(3)
Myrick Street adjacent to the Burke County Courthouse grounds.
(b)
No vehicle used in the transportation of flammable or explosive materials or substances shall be parked within the limits of any street, or other public place, except supervised public parking lots, between the hours from sunset to sunrise, provided, however, that this restriction shall not apply to the incidental stopping or parking of such vehicle in the carrying out of loading or unloading operations or in the performance of errands incidental to the business use of such vehicles if such vehicle is lighted with at least two white headlights and two red rear lights so constructed and so placed as to be observed under ordinary atmospheric conditions at a distance of not less than 500 feet.
(c)
No truck, trailer, semi-trailer, tractor, or other such type of vehicle shall be parked within the limits of any street, right-of-way or other public place, except public parking lots, for a period of greater than two hours between 12:00 a.m. and 7:00 a.m. and then only if such vehicle is lighted with two white headlights and two red rear lights so constructed and so placed as to be observed under ordinary atmospheric conditions at a distance of not less than 300 feet.
(d)
No automobile, truck or other vehicle shall be parked within the limits of any street or other public place for the purpose of repairing, remodeling or otherwise working upon said vehicle.
(e)
No automobile, truck or other vehicle shall be parked or allowed to stand on the portion of street or highway between the curb or gutter and the sidewalk nor on any sidewalk except temporarily and during the process of loading or unloading.
(f)
No automobile, truck or other vehicle shall be parked or allowed to stand within the limits of any street and in a perpendicular position to the curb except temporarily for loading or unloading.
(g)
No automobile, truck or other vehicle other than fire trucks, ambulances or vehicles of law enforcement agencies during the performance of their respective duties, shall be parked or permitted to stand within 15 feet of any fire hydrant nor nearer than ten feet from the nearest street line of any intersecting street, nor in front of any crosswalks or driveways, nor in any passenger bus loading zones.
(h)
No automobiles, trucks or other vehicles shall be parked or permitted to stand within the boundaries of any street and within 500 feet of any building on fire.
(i)
No automobiles, trucks or other vehicles shall be parked further than two feet from the curb line if there is one, and if there is no curb, they shall not be parked within the main traveled portion of the street or highway.
(j)
No automobile, truck or other vehicle shall be parked or permitted to stand unless the brakes thereon are in good working order and properly set, nor shall such vehicle be parked or permitted to stand with its motor running, unless a competent operator is in charge and at the controls thereof.
(k)
When signs have been erected by any competent authority designating a "no parking" zone, the parking by any automobile, truck or other vehicle contrary thereto shall be a violation of this article.
(Ord. No. 2016-02-02, § 2, 3-21-2016)
Sec. 103-58. - No stopping or parking of vehicles except as permitted by this article.
No person shall stop or park a vehicle on any street or public place except in accordance with the requirements of this article.
(Ord. No. 2016-02-02, § 2, 3-21-2016)
Sec. 103-59. - Restrictions on parking.
(a)
No person shall park a vehicle:
(1)
Within ten feet of any street intersection;
(2)
Within five feet of a private driveway, except where the surface of the street has been marked or designated by painted lines;
(3)
Within ten feet of a private driveway on a one-way street;
(4)
Where prohibited by an official traffic sign;
(5)
On any sidewalk; and
(6)
Where prohibited by markings painted on the surface of the street.
(b)
Vehicles shall be parked within any painted lines marking or designating a parking space. No vehicle shall stop or park in a manner such that the vehicle is straddling a painted line marking or designating a parking space or the vehicle is not within a parking space designated by such painted lines.
(c)
No person shall stop or park a vehicle upon any street or other public place if that vehicle is displayed as being for sale or is being used for the primary purpose of displaying advertising.
(d)
Vehicles parked within angular parking spaces shall have the front of the vehicle facing the curb of the street.
(e)
No person shall park a vehicle on any street so designated in section 103-57(a) for a period in excess of two hours. The city council may, after providing notice to the general public, by resolution, establish or modify the maximum parking time on any street. Section 103-57(a) shall then be amended to include the maximum parking limit on the street or streets identified in the resolution.
(f)
The city may, after providing notice to the general public, by resolution, establish or modify the location of any controlled parking space.
(g)
No person shall stop or park any vehicle in any area designated as a loading zone, except temporarily for the purpose of and while actually engaged in loading and unloading of passengers or merchandise.
(h)
No person shall stop or park any vehicle in any area designated as an official vehicle parking space, unless the vehicle is an official vehicle.
(i)
No person shall park a vehicle in, allow the parking of a vehicle in, obstruct or allow the obstruction of a designated fire lane. Each day or part of a day during which the unlawful act or violation occurs shall constitute a separate offense.
(j)
No person shall stop or park a vehicle in any area designated as "handicap parking," except vehicles that have either a special license plate, decal, tag or card, issued by the state department of motor vehicles indicating the vehicle is for the use of a handicapped person.
(Ord. No. 2016-02-02, § 2, 3-21-2016)
Sec. 103-60. - Charges, penalties, and enforcement.
(a)
No person shall permit or cause a vehicle registered in his/her name to park contrary to any provision of this article. If the identity of the operator of a vehicle used or parked contrary to this article cannot be determined, the name of the person listed as the owner of the vehicle with the state department of motor vehicles shall be prima facie evidence that the registered owner was the operator of the vehicle.
(b)
Whenever an officer observes an unattended vehicle that is parked contrary to any provision of this article, he shall attach to the vehicle a notice informing the operator or owner that the vehicle has been parked contrary to the provisions of this article. The notice shall include the following information:
(1)
The location where the vehicle is parked;
(2)
The state registration or license number of the vehicle;
(3)
The date and time that the officer observed the vehicle parked or operated contrary to the provisions of this article;
(4)
A statement that if the owner or operator of the vehicle does not submit payment of an improper parking charge within 14 days after the notice is placed on the vehicle, the owner of the vehicle may be served with a summons to appear in municipal court to answer to charges of violating this article;
(5)
A statement that if the owner or operator wishes to challenge or contest the charges of violating this article before the municipal court, he may do so; and
(6)
Any other facts which the officer may determine necessary for a thorough understanding of the circumstances.
(c)
Officers shall have the authority to issue notices to any vehicle parked contrary to the provisions of this article or to issue a citation to any owner or operator of any vehicle parked contrary to the provisions of this article. Officers shall have the authority to issue notices by using either: (1) a pre-printed notice form to be filled in manually by the officer; or (2) an electronic device that produces a single paper copy of the notice to be issued. An electronic record or memory of the notice issued by an officer using an electronic device shall be maintained. Any paper printout of that electronic record or memory of the notice shall be prima facie evidence that it is a valid copy of the original notice issued.
(d)
The operator or owner of a vehicle receiving a notice that the vehicle was observed by an officer to be parked contrary to the provisions of this article shall submit full payment of the appropriate improper parking charge within 14 days of the time the notice was attached to the vehicle by placing the required sum of money in the envelope attached to the notice and: (1) delivering the notice envelope and payment to the city police department; or (2) mailing the notice envelope and payment to the City of Waynesboro at 628 Myrick Street, Waynesboro, Georgia 30830. The amount of the improper parking charge shall be as follows:
(1)
The improper parking charge shall be $20.00 regularly or $30.00 if issued in an area designated by the city council as an "enhanced penalty" area;
(2)
For violation of section 103-59(i) of this article, the improper parking charge shall be $250.00.
(e)
The failure, neglect, or refusal of an owner or operator to submit full payment of an improper parking charge within 14 days of the time the notice was attached to the vehicle owned or operated by him shall be unlawful and constitute a violation of this article.
(f)
The chief of police shall have the authority, at any time after the expiration of the 14 day period from the attachment of the notice to the vehicle or the issuance of a towing notice, but before a court summons has been issued, to send or cause to be sent notice(s) informing the owner of the vehicle that late penalty fees shall be assessed against him, unless the owner has submitted a written request to the municipal court asking that a hearing be scheduled regarding the violation of this article.
(g)
The amount of the initial late penalty fee for each violation shall be equal to the improper parking charge for such violation. The amount of the late penalty fee shall include any towing charge that was initially assessed.
(h)
If an owner or operator fails, neglects, or refuses to make full payment of the improper parking charge or towing charge, within 14 days of the time the notice was attached to the vehicle or the date that the towing notice was issued, then the chief of police, or an officer, may issue a summons to the operator or owner of the vehicle used in violation of this article to appear in court to answer to charges of violating this article.
(i)
Any person found by a court of law to be guilty of a violation of this article shall be required to pay:
(1)
For failure to pay the improper parking charge assessed, the minimum fine shall be $50.00 and the maximum fine shall not be more than $500.00;
(2)
For failure to pay the improper parking charge assessed under of section 103-59(i) of this article, the minimum fine shall be $510.00 and the maximum fine shall not be more than $1,000.00;
(3)
For failure to pay a towing charge assessed, the minimum fine shall be $110.00 and the maximum fine shall not be more than $500.00.
(Ord. No. 2016-02-02, § 2, 3-21-2016)
Sec. 103-61. - Removal of vehicles.
(a)
In the event that the chief of police or his designee determines that it is necessary to cause the removal, towage and storage of a vehicle parked contrary to the provisions of this article, the owner shall be required to pay a towing charge.
(b)
Whenever a vehicle is towed and stored the officer causing the vehicle to be towed and stored shall cause a towing notice to be sent to the owner by placing such towing notice in the mail within 24 hours of the time the vehicle was towed or removed. The towing notice shall include the following information:
(1)
The state registration or license number of the vehicle;
(2)
The location where the vehicle was parked;
(3)
The date and approximate time that the officer caused the vehicle to be removed or towed;
(4)
A statement that if the owner or operator of the vehicle does not submit payment of a towing charge within 14 days from the date of the notice, the owner of the vehicle may be served with a summons to appear in court to answer to charges of violating this article;
(5)
A statement that if the owner or operator wishes to challenge or contest the towing charge, the owner may submit a written request for a hearing before the municipal court within seven days of the issuance date of the towing notice. The written request shall be mailed or delivered to the Waynesboro Police Department at 628 Myrick Street, Waynesboro, Georgia 30830;
(6)
The issuance date of the towing notice;
(7)
A brief statement of the reason for the removal or towage of the vehicle;
(8)
The location of where the vehicle is being stored; and
(9)
Any other facts which the chief of police or his designee may determine necessary for a thorough understanding of the circumstances.
(c)
In the case of vehicles towed, the amount of the towing charge shall be $100.00.
(d)
The operator or owner of a vehicle receiving a towing notice shall submit full payment of the towing charge within 14 days of the date the towing notice was issued by: (1) delivering payment to the city police department; or (2) mailing such payment to the city police department at 628 Myrick Street Waynesboro, Georgia 30830.
(e)
The failure, neglect, or refusal of an owner or operator to submit full payment of a towing charge within 14 days of the date the notice was issued shall be unlawful and constitute a violation of this article.
(f)
In the event that a decision is made by the municipal court judge to vacate the assessment of a towing charge, any sums paid by the owner or operator in satisfaction of the towing charge shall be refunded within ten days of the decision.
(Ord. No. 2016-02-02, § 2, 3-21-2016)
Sec. 103-62. - Multiple unpaid parking violations.
(a)
At any time subsequent to the accumulation of ten unpaid parking violations or the accumulation of unpaid parking fines in excess of $90.00 attributable to motor vehicle(s) registered to an individual, the chief of police or his designee may send a notice by certified mail to the registered owner of said vehicle(s). Said notice may also be given by affixing same to the driver's side window of any vehicle known to be registered to an individual that meets the criteria as detailed in this paragraph. This notice shall contain the following information:
(1)
The date, time, and location of the violations leading to the issuance of the notice;
(2)
A statement to the clear effect that failure to resolve the violations prior to a certain specified date not less than five days subsequent to the date of the notice, will lead to the towing of any vehicle registered in the name of the individual pending such resolution;
(3)
A statement that the recipient of the notice may contact the chief of police or his appropriate designee in writing within three days of the owner's receipt of the notice or the date the notice was affixed to owner's vehicle to request a hearing on the subject of the violations.
(b)
Upon receipt of a properly submitted request from an owner pursuant to this section, the chief of police or his designee shall arrange a hearing before the municipal court.
(Ord. No. 2016-02-02, § 2, 3-21-2016)
Secs. 103-63—103-70. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - ADMINISTRATION AND ENFORCEMENT
DIVISION 1. - IMPOUNDING VEHICLES

Sec. 103-71. - Authorized.
(a)
Whenever a police officer or other officer acting under authority or direction of the city shall find any vehicle parked on any street or other place in violation of law or in violation of the ordinances of the city, or abandoned for at least five days on any street or other public place, and such officer is unable to find the owner, operator, or other person responsible for such vehicle in the immediate vicinity after making a reasonable search and investigation, it shall be his duty to take possession of such vehicle and tow it to a place of storage and keep it in possession of the city until claimed by the person entitled to its possession, if claimed within the time fixed in this article; provided, however, that the person claiming such vehicle shall pay all lawful costs as fixed in this article. If deemed necessary by such officer, he shall call upon a garage man or other person equipped for towing and storing such vehicles.
(b)
Whenever any officer shall find upon any street, or at any other place within the city, any vehicle broken-down, wrecked, disabled, stalled, or in any condition or situation as to render the vehicle likely to endanger life, limb or property, and the owner, operator or person in charge is unable or unwilling to take the proper steps to remove the danger or to make the necessary correction with reasonable promptness, such officer shall cause the vehicle to be removed, impounded and kept in the manner specified in subsection (a) of this section, to be released upon compliance with the requirements of section 103-72 as to payment of costs and expenses.
(c)
In all cases where the operator of a vehicle is found upon the public streets or public places of the city ill, injured, intoxicated, or for any reason in a condition rendering him unfit to operate the vehicle, the officers shall tow away and store such vehicle in the manner provided in this section, until released by compliance with the requirements of section 103-72.
(Ord. No. 2016-02-02, § 2, 3-21-2016)
State Law reference— When police officers may remove vehicles, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-206; abandoned motor vehicles, O.C.G.A. § 40-11-1 et seq.
Sec. 103-72. - Redemption of impounded vehicles.
The owner or person entitled to possession of a vehicle impounded pursuant to section 103-71 may redeem such vehicle at any time within 30 days of the date of impoundment by paying to the city the actual costs of impounding, towing and storing such vehicle plus any penalty that may be assessed for violation of this article or any ordinance.
(Ord. No. 2016-02-02, § 2, 3-21-2016)
State Law reference— Duty of person removing or storing motor vehicle, O.C.G.A. § 40-11-2.
Sec. 103-73. - Sale of unredeemed vehicles.
If the owner or person entitled to possession of a vehicle impounded under section 103-71 fails to redeem such vehicle as provided in section 103-72, the chief of police may proceed to effectuate its disposition by selling it after advertising such sale in the local legal organ one time and posting notice of such sale in the lobby of City Hall. Such sale shall be to the highest sealed bidder for cash, bids to be opened not less than ten days after publication and posting of notice.
(Ord. No. 2016-02-02, § 2, 3-21-2016)
Secs. 103-74—103-95. - Reserved.
