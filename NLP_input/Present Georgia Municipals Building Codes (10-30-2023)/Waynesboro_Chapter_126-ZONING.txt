Sec. 126-1. - Adoption of ordinance; purpose; review.
(a)
The document consisting of text and maps entitled "Zoning Ordinance of the City of Waynesboro," and dated February 17, 2003, as amended, is hereby adopted as the zoning ordinance of the city.
(b)
The regulations contained in the zoning ordinance shall:
(1)
Divide the city into districts and establish the boundaries thereof;
(2)
Regulate the location, height, bulk, number of stories, and size of buildings and other structures;
(3)
Regulate the percentage of lot which may be occupied;
(4)
Regulate the sizes of yards, courts, and other open spaces;
(5)
Regulate the density and distribution of population;
(6)
Regulate the uses and locations of buildings, structures and land for trade, industry, residence, recreation, agriculture, forestry, conservation, water supply, sanitation, protection against floods, and public activities;
(7)
Provide for the method of administration and amendment of such regulation;
(8)
Define the powers and duties of the planning commission with respect to such regulations;
(9)
Define certain terms used therein; and
(10)
Provide penalties for violation of such regulations.
(c)
In order that the zoning ordinance shall at all times be kept current with the needs of the city and shall represent the best plan of the council, planning commission, and the public in the light of changing conditions, the planning commission shall periodically review the zoning ordinance and recommend to the council extensions, changes or additions to the text and maps which the commission considers necessary. Should the commission find that no changes are necessary, this finding shall be reported to the council.
(Ord. No. 01-01-2003, 2-17-2003)
