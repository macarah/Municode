Sec. 30-1. - Adoption of state rules, regulations.
The rules and regulations promulgated by the state election board pertaining to municipal elections, together with the provisions of the Georgia Municipal Election Code contained in O.C.G.A. § 21-3-1 et seq., are hereby adopted as the rules, regulations and provisions that shall govern the conduct of municipal general and special elections in the city.
(Code 1980, ch. 2-2)
Sec. 30-2. - Filing of notices of candidacy.
Notices of candidacy shall be filed by candidates for offices of the mayor and council no sooner than 32 days prior to the election in the case of a general election and no sooner than 25 days prior to the election in the case of a special election. The deadline for filing the foregoing notices of candidacy shall be 22 days prior to the election in the case of a general election and 15 days prior to the election in the case of a special election. Notices of candidacy shall be filed in the office of the municipal election superintendent during normal business hours.
(Code 1980, § 2-2-3; Ord. of 3-12-90, § 1)
State Law reference— Filing notice of candidacy for office, O.C.G.A. § 21-3-91.
Sec. 30-3. - Polling places.
The city shall consist of one voting district, with the polling place being located at the city hall.
(Code 1980, § 2-2-6)
Sec. 30-4. - Expenses.
Such funds as are necessary for the conduct of elections and for the performance of the duties that are specified by this chapter shall be budgeted and appropriated annually, and from time to time when required.
