ARTICLE II. - DOWNTOWN DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Downtown Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-42-1 et seq.; downtown development authority created, O.C.G.A. § 36-42-4.

Sec. 32-19. - Adoption of state law.
The city adopts the Downtown Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-42-1 et seq.
(Code 1999, § 50-36; Ord. of 10-2-1981)
Sec. 32-20. - Creation.
The area within the city limits as they now exist and as they may be annexed from time to time, or changed as provided by law, shall be the geographical area served by the downtown development authority pursuant to state law. The mayor and council declare that there is a need for a downtown development authority to function in the city. Such authority is hereby activated as defined in state law and shall be known as the city downtown development authority.
(Code 1999, § 50-37; Ord. of 10-2-1981)
Sec. 32-21. - Membership; powers, rights, duties and immunities.
The downtown development authority shall be governed by a board of seven members who shall be the directors of the authority, and each director shall be a resident of the city. The directors shall be vested with all the powers, rights, duties and immunities set forth in state law.
(Code 1999, § 50-38; Ord. of 10-2-1981)
State Law reference— Qualifications of directors and officer, O.C.G.A. § 36-42-7.
Sec. 32-22. - Terms of members after initial terms.
After expiration of the respective initial terms of all members of the board of directors, all subsequent terms shall be for six years.
(Code 1999, § 50-39; Ord. of 10-2-1981)
Sec. 32-23. - Areas exempted from article provisions.
Notwithstanding anything to the contrary stated in this article, the areas within the city limits of the city presently having a zoning classification or designation other than central business district are expressly excluded from the geographical area to be served by the downtown development authority.
(Code 1999, § 50-40; Ord. of 10-2-1981)
