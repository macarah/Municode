Sec. 10A-33. - Same—Incentives.
The council hereby ordains and declares that upon renewal and update of the area as an Enterprise Zone, the consolidated government shall continue providing the following incentives in the area, including tad incentives, to qualifying businesses in accordance with the definition of such businesses outlined in the Act, which are not applicable throughout the city:
(1)
The consolidated government shall exempt qualifying businesses as outlined in the Act and further in Exhibit B, from state, county, and municipal ad valorem property taxes, excluding property taxes imposed by school districts, that would otherwise be levied on the qualifying business and service enterprises in accordance with the following schedule:
a.
100 percent of the property taxes shall be exempt for the first five years;
b.
80 percent of the property taxes shall be exempt for the next two years;
c.
60 percent of the property taxes shall be exempt for the next year;
d.
40 percent of the property taxes shall be exempt for the next year; and
e.
20 percent of the property taxes shall be exempt for the last year.
(2)
Other incentives that may be granted will be negotiated on a case-by-case basis by the Columbus Consolidated Government through the planning department and could include exemption from any or all of the following:
a.
Building permit fees.
b.
Sign permit fees.
c.
Business license administrative fee.
d.
Rezoning fees.
e.
Engineering fees.
f.
Other local fees authorized by council, as applicable.
(Ord. No. 98-30, § 1, 4-7-98; Ord. No. 00-106, § 1, 12-12-00; Ord. No. 01-29, § 1, 3-20-01; Ord. No. 08-69, § 1, 12-9-08; Ord. No. 14-48, § 1, 9-9-14; Ord. No. 18-54, § 1, 12-11-18)
Editor's note— Exhibit B of Ord. No. 18-54, adopted December 11, 2018, has not been included within the Code, but may be found attached to Ord. No. 18-54, on file in the office of the city clerk.
Sec. 10A-34. - Council to act as authorized agency of enterprise zone.
The council is the authorized agency to act in all matters pertaining to the enterprise zone and reserves the power to grant the incentives listed above [section 10A-33] to qualifying businesses in accordance with the authorization powers granted local governments in the administration of the enterprise zone in the Act.
(Ord. No. 98-30, § 1, 4-7-98; Ord. No. 00-106, § 1, 12-12-00; Ord. No. 01-29, § 1, 3-20-01; Ord. No. 08-69, § 1, 12-9-08; Ord. No. 14-48, § 1, 9-9-14; Ord. No. 18-54, § 1, 12-11-18)

The council has the power to administer, require, and enforce compliance with the provisions of this article and such administrative rules or regulations adopted hereinafter by way of resolution or as contained in Exhibit B, including but not limited to reports and data information from businesses within the enterprise zone to verify compliance with this article and state law.

Sec. 10A-37. - Contractual agreement with qualifying business.
A qualifying business or service enterprise, or residential development shall consent to enter into a contractual agreement that outlines the incentives offered to the business and a guideline for the recapture, revocation, or reimbursement should the terms of the contract be violated by the target business.
(Ord. No. 98-30, § 1, 4-7-98; Ord. No. 00-106, § 1, 12-12-00; Ord. No. 01-29, § 1, 3-20-01; Ord. No. 08-69, § 1, 12-9-08; Ord. No. 14-48, § 1, 9-9-14; Ord. No. 18-54, § 1, 12-11-18)
