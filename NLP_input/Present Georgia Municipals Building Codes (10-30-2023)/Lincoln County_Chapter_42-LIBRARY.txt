Sec. 42-51. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Board of trustees means the Lincoln County Library Board of Trustees.
(Ord. No. 15, § 1, 2-3-1987)
Sec. 42-52. - Created.
There is hereby created and established a board of trustees for the county public library which shall be known as the "Lincoln County Library Board of Trustees."
(Ord. No. 15, § 1, 2-3-1987)
Sec. 42-53. - Membership.
The board of trustees created in this division shall consist of five members, appointed by each of the members of the board of commissioners. The five trustees shall serve in post 1, post 2, post 3, post 4 and post 5, respectively.
(Ord. No. 15, § 2, 2-3-1987)
Sec. 42-54. - Eligibility; terms of office; vacancy and removal.
(a)
To be eligible for appointment to the board of trustees, a member must be a resident of the county and reside within the boundaries of the district the member shall represent.
(b)
Trustees shall serve for four-year staggered terms beginning on January 1 and ending on December 31 four years later, or until a successor has been appointed.
(c)
Vacancies in memberships of the board of trustees shall be filled in the same manner as provided for the appointment of trustees. Any person appointed as the result of a vacancy on the board shall serve the unexpired term left by the vacant member.
(d)
Any trustee may be removed for cause by majority vote of the board of commissioners.
(Ord. No. 15, § 3, 2-3-1987)
Sec. 42-55. - Oath of office.
No trustee shall begin to discharge his duties as a member of the board of trustees without first having taken the oath of office.
(Ord. No. 15, § 4, 2-3-1987)
Sec. 42-56. - Organization.
(a)
Immediately after appointment, the membership of the board of trustees shall meet and organize by electing a chair, a vice-chair and a secretary.
(b)
The board of trustees shall have a written constitution and bylaws stating policy which shall be approved by the board. Such constitution and bylaws shall be drafted in accordance with the current edition of the Handbook on Constitutions, Bylaws and Contracts for Georgia Public Libraries.
(c)
Policies stated in the constitution of the county board may not be in conflict with the policies of the constitution of the regional board and state and federal laws and regulations.
(d)
All current constitutions and bylaws must be on file in the Office of Public Library Services of the Board of Regents of the University System of Georgia, and all amendments must be filed with the office immediately upon adoption.
(Ord. No. 15, § 5, 2-3-1987)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 20-5-47.
Sec. 42-57. - Meetings.
(a)
The board of trustees shall hold not less than six regular meetings each calendar year. The meetings shall be held on the second Tuesday of the months of January, March, May, July, September and November.
(b)
Nothing in this section shall prohibit the board of trustees from holding special meetings on the call of the chair of the board of trustees.
(c)
Any trustee failing to attend three consecutive meetings shall be removed, pursuant to the library systems's constitution and bylaws or the local constitution and bylaws.
(Ord. No. 15, § 6, 2-3-1987)
Sec. 42-58. - Reports.
The board of trustees shall submit a written report to the board of commissioners of its transactions, findings, proceedings and recommendations at least once a year at the first regular meeting of the commissioners following December 31 of the previous year.
(Ord. No. 15, § 7, 2-3-1987)
Sec. 42-59. - Duties.
The board of trustees has as its primary responsibility the coordination, organization and the establishment of policies and regulations governing the use of the library, to serve the best interest of the needs and desires of the citizens of the county. The board of trustees shall advise and recommend to the board of commissioners as to the assessment and establishment of needs for the library program and use of facilities.
(Ord. No. 15, § 8, 2-3-1987)
