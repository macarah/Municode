ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 6-1. - Building code adopted by reference.
There is hereby adopted by reference as fully and to the same extent as if set out at length herein, subject to all amendments and conflicting provisions contained in this Code of Ordinances, that certain published code known as the Standard Building Code, the latest edition as adopted and amended by the Georgia Department of Community Affairs (DCA). One (1) copy of which is on file in the office of the city clerk which will be the official copy of said code and so designated by the city clerk.
(Code 1965, § 7-11; Ord. No. 286, § 1, 7-8-85; Ord. No. 334, § 1, 9-26-88; Ord. No. 440, § 1, 12-12-94; Ord. No. 501, § 1, 1-12-98)
State Law reference— Building, etc., codes, OCGA § 8-2-1 et seq.
Sec. 6-2. - Exceptions to Standard Building Code.
Section 105.5 of the Standard Building Code is repealed in its entirety in lieu thereof a new section 105 is hereby adopted which section should read as follows:
Section 105.1 Appointment.
There is established a board to be called, "board of adjustments and appeals," which shall consist of the governing body of the City of Cairo.
Section 1209.1 of the Standard Building Code is repealed in its entirety and in lieu thereof a new Section 1209.1 is hereby adopted which section shall read as follows:
An accessible shut-off valve controlling delivery to a service shall be provided on the consumer's premises within three (3) feet of the water meter on the distribution pipe. Such valve shall be located and accessible in a valve box with a readily removable access cover which extends to grade level. When drain valves are provided for the distribution piping or other portion of the water supply system, such drains shall be above grade or otherwise located to prevent the possibility of backflow into the piping system after the system has been drained.
(Code 1965, § 7-13; Ord. No. 157, §§ 1, 2, 3-23-70; Ord. No. 334, § 2, 9-26-88; Ord. No. 442, § 1, 3-13-95)
Sec. 6-3. - Water and sewer connections.
No building permit shall be issued by the building official for the erection of any residence or dwelling house within the city unless the plans and specifications thereof include proper sanitary water and sewer connections, provided the location at which such residence is to be built is accessible to a sewer line.
(Code 1965, § 7-19)
Cross reference— Sewer connections, §§ 12-60 et seq., 13-2 et seq., 13-20 et seq.
Sec. 6-4. - Permit required.
No person shall construct, alter, repair, remove or demolish a building, swimming pool, manufactured home, or structure including perform any electrical work or wiring, install, enlarge, repair, move or replace the gas system, mechanical system, plumbing or sewage system, or roof of any building or structure that may be regulated by this Code without first making application to the building inspector and obtaining the proper permit for the work.
(Ord. No. 440, § 2, 12-12-94)
Sec. 6-4.1. - Bond requirements.
No license holder shall be required to maintain a bond with surety or sureties payable to the city in a sum in excess of five thousand dollars ($5,000.00) notwithstanding the number of licenses or types of licenses held by the license holder. The license holder shall fully comply with each requirement regarding maintenance of a bond contained within this chapter.
(Ord. No. 442, § 1, 3-13-95)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 442, § 1, adopted Mar. 13, 1995, added provisions pertaining to bond requirements, and designated such provisions as § 6-4. Such section was renumbered as § 6-4.1 in order to avoid duplicate section numbers.
Sec. 6-5. - House mover's insurance.
Any person who desires a permit to move any house or building along any street or public way of the city to furnish satisfactory proof to the building official at the time such person applies for the moving permit that such person has liability insurance covering any moving operation carried on in the city for one hundred thousand dollars ($100,000.00) for injury to any one person and three hundred thousand dollars ($300,000.00) for injury to any two (2) or more persons and one hundred thousand dollars ($100,000.00) property damage. The proof of such insurance may be made by producing the original policy or by submitting a separate certificate from the insurance agent or company that such person has a liability policy with such coverage.
(Ord. No. 187, § 1, 9-25-72)
Sec. 6-6. - Building numbers.
(a)
The plan for numbering buildings within the city is as follows:
(1)
Even numbers shall be placed on the east side of all streets running north from the CSX Railroad and on the west side of all streets running south from the CSX Railroad.
(2)
Odd numbers shall be placed on the west side of all streets running north from CSX Railroad and on the east side of all streets running south from CSX Railroad.
(3)
Even numbers shall be placed on the south side of all streets running east from Broad Street and on the north side of all streets running west from Broad Street.
(4)
Odd numbers shall be placed on the north side of all streets running east from Broad Street and on the south side of all streets running west from Broad Street.
(5)
Not more than one hundred (100) numbers shall be used to the block and each block shall commence with the next succeeding one hundred (100).
(b)
It shall be the duty of the owner, agent or occupant of any dwelling house or other building to affix or inscribe the proper numbers thereon in plain and legible letters at least two (2) inches in length in a conspicuous place in front of the building or dwelling house. Yard markers may be used in lieu of the above.
(c)
Whenever any dwelling house or building shall be without numbers or shall have incorrect numbers affixed or inscribed thereon, it shall be the duty of the city manager or his designated representative, to serve on the owner, agent or occupant, a written or printed notice notifying the owner, agent or occupant to affix or inscribe, within ten (10) days from the date of service of such notice, on the dwelling house or building owned by him or for which he may be the agent in any capacity or the occupant, the proper numbers as designated in the notice.
(d)
The owners, agents or occupants of dwelling houses or buildings which are without numbers, or which are incorrectly numbered, may apply to the building inspector for assignment of correct number.
(e)
Where the owner, agent or occupant of any such dwelling house or building shall neglect or refuse to affix or inscribe the proper numbers to the dwelling house or building after being duly notified as herein provided, such owner, agent or occupant may be fined not to exceed twenty-five dollars ($25.00) and the numbers ordered affixed or inscribed by an official of the city.
(f)
It shall be unlawful for any person to take down, alter, injure or deface any street sign, or any number affixed or inscribed as herein provided.
(g)
For the violation of this section the person violating same, or in the case of a corporation, its agents or employees, shall upon conviction therefore be punished as provided in section 1-8 of this Code.
(h)
The application of the above penalty shall not be held to prevent the enforced removal of prohibited conditions.
(Ord. No. 378, § 1, 7-23-90)

Sec. 6-8. - Penalty for violation.
Enforcement of the various portions of chapter 6 including the various code provisions adopted by reference shall be by the building inspector or designated employees of the City of Cairo. Inspections regarding compliance with this chapter shall be at such times and locations as the city building inspector or designated employees shall determine. Failure to comply with this code shall permit the building inspector or designated employees to issue citation to the municipal court for violation of the code(s). Punishment shall be in accordance with section 1-8 of the Code of Ordinances. Each day in violation of this chapter shall constitute a separate offense. Procedures regarding prosecution of violations shall be established within the municipal court.
(Ord. No. 429, § 1, 4-11-94; Ord. No. 501, § 16, 1-12-98)
Editor's note— Provisions of Ord. No. 429, § 1, adopted Apr. 11, 1994, which pertaining to penalty for violation, were designated as § 6-7. The editor has redesignated such provisions as § 6-8 in order to avoid duplicate section numbers.
