3.01.010 - Establishment of fiscal year.
The fiscal year for the City of McDonough shall commence on July 1 and shall end on June 30. The fiscal year shall constitute the budget year and the year for financial accounting and reporting of each and every office, department, agency and activity of the city government, unless otherwise provided by state law.
( Ord. No. 14-02-17(B) , § 1, 2-17-2014)
3.01.020 - Appointment of Budget Officer.
The City Administrator shall serve as the Budget Officer.
( Ord. No. 14-02-17(B) , § 1, 2-17-2014)
3.01.030 - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Annual budget means that governmental action which appropriates revenues and fund balances for specified purposes, functions, or activities for a budget period.
Budget means a plan of financial operation embodying an estimate of proposed expenditures during a budget period and the proposed means of finance.
Budget officer means the official charged with budget preparation and administration for the City.
Budget period means the period for which a budget is proposed or a budget ordinance or resolution is adopted.
Capital projects fund means a fund used to account for financial resources to be used for the acquisition or construction of major capital facilities other than those financed by resources from proprietary type activities which are accounted for in enterprise funds or those financed with funds held by the City in a Trustee capacity.
Debt service fund means a fund used to account for the accumulation of resources for and the payment of general long-term debt principal and interest.
Fiscal year means the period for which a budget is proposed or a budget ordinance or resolution is adopted for the local government's general fund, each special revenue fund, if any, and each debt service fund, if any.
Fund means a fiscal and accounting entity with a self-balancing set of accounts recording cash and other financial resources, together with all related liabilities and residual equities or balances, and changes therein, which is segregated for the purpose of carrying on specific activities or attaining certain objectives in accordance with special regulations, restrictions or limitations.
General fund means the fund used to account for all financial resources except those required to be accounted for in another fund.
Legal level of control means the lowest level of budgetary detail at which a local government's management or budget officer may not reassign resources without approval of the governing authority.
Special revenue fund means a fund used to account for the proceeds of specific revenue sources, other than those for major capital projects or those held by the government in a trustee capacity, that are legally restricted to expenditure for specified purposes.
( Ord. No. 14-02-17(B) , § 1, 2-17-2014)
3.01.040 - Requirement of an annual balanced budget.
A.
The City of McDonough shall operate on a balanced budget. A budget resolution or ordinance is balanced when the sum of estimated revenues and appropriated fund balances is equal to appropriations. No later than April 30 of each fiscal year, the Budget Officer shall prepare and present to the Mayor and Council a proposed balanced budget for the ensuing budget period. It shall be the duty and responsibility of the Budget Officer to assist the Mayor and Council to adopt the annual budget prior to the commencement of the applicable budget year.
B.
The proposed budget, at a minimum, shall:
1.
Provide an estimate of the financial requirements for each fund requiring a budget for the appropriate budget period;
2.
Provide, for the appropriate budget period, a statement of the amounts budgeted for anticipated revenues by source and the amounts budgeted for expenditures;
3.
Provide a statement of the amounts budgeted for expenditures by department for each fund, including the general fund, special revenue fund, and each debt service fund in use by the City; and
4.
Include any additional items as required by the Mayor and Council.
C.
Upon adoption, the budget shall serve as an appropriation authorization at the legal level of control, which is the department level, not to exceed $10,000.00.
D.
Mayor and Council shall approve adjustments at the department level that exceed $10,000.00, adjustments between departments or funds, and/or adjustments that change the total budget.
( Ord. No. 14-02-17(B) , § 1, 2-17-2014)
CHAPTER 3.04 - FISCAL PROVISIONS

3.04.010 - Tax sales, authorized officers.
A.
O.C.G.A. § 48-5-359 is adopted and incorporated into this code as it appears or is amended from time to time by the legislature. The adoption of the code section shall be cumulative to any other laws, articles, sections, provisions or ordinances of the state of Georgia, or Charter and code for the municipality of McDonough.
B.
The city clerk is appointed as a duly authorized ex officio marshal, for the city, to conduct sales for taxes due. The city clerk's authority as an ex officio marshal is limited only to sales for taxes due.
C.
The marshal may, by written authorization, empower any person to act as an ex officio marshal for purposes of levy and sale under any tax execution.
(Prior Code § 8-4; Ord. of 10-17-1988)
CHAPTER 3.08 - EXEMPTIONS FROM AD VALOREM TAXES

Sections:
3.08.010 - Exemptions from ad valorem taxes.
Inventories of goods in process of manufacture or production and inventory of finished goods are exempt from ad valorem taxation, including all such taxation levied for education purposes and for state purposes.
(Prior Code § 20-1; Ord. No. 97-11-17T, 11-17-1997)
