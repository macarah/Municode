ARTICLE IV. - DEPARTMENTS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-205. - Established; enumerated.
Pursuant to the authority granted to the board of commissioners by the city's Charter, the following departments of the city are established:
(1)
City clerk.
(2)
Community development department.
(3)
Customer service department.
(4)
Utilities department.
(5)
Finance department.
(6)
Fire department.
(7)
Gas department.
(8)
Human resources department.
(9)
Parks and recreation department.
(10)
Police department.
(11)
Public works department.
(12)
Vehicle maintenance department.
(13)
Water and wastewater department.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-161)
Sec. 2-206. - City manager's authority.
(a)
The city manager is hereby vested with full power and authority to maintain and operate each of the departments created in this article and each director or superintendent shall be under the direct supervision of the city manager.
(b)
The city manager shall have full authority, in cooperation with each director or superintendent, to hire employees of the department at such salaries as the city manager may deem adequate with the advice of the board of commissioners; and shall have, when not in conflict with the city Charter or this Code, full power to remove employees of the department.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-162)
Sec. 2-207. - Directors and superintendents.
There shall be a director or superintendent of each of the departments created in this article who shall be the head of the department and who shall have jurisdiction over all employees employed in the department who are not under the jurisdiction of the city manager. The director or superintendent shall be under the direct supervision of the city manager. The clerk shall be the head of the city clerk-revenue collections department.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-163)
Sec. 2-208. - Promulgation of regulations.
(a)
The director or superintendent of each of the departments enumerated in section 2-205 may propose written regulations or amendments thereto to organize and operate the department of the city for which he is responsible in the most efficient manner reasonably possible consistent with the Charter and this Code.
(b)
These regulations shall become effective only after approval by the city manager.
(c)
Violation of department regulations may be grounds for reprimand, demotion, suspension or discharge for department employees.
(d)
Each officer and employee of the departments created in this article shall be furnished with a copy of the department regulations.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-164)
Sec. 2-209. - Records.
Each director or superintendent shall, subject to the jurisdiction of the city clerk, be in charge of all records of the department and shall keep a full and complete record of all documents as well as receipts and disbursements of all moneys paid to and disbursed through the department and shall keep the records available at all reasonable times to the inspection of the city manager, city clerk, board of commissioners and state and federal grantor agencies.
(Code 1978, § 2-235; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-165)
Sec. 2-210. - Funds for operating; enforcement of laws.
It shall be the duty of the board of commissioners to provide each year for the budgetary allocation of funds for the operation of each of the departments created in this article as in its judgment it shall deem best; and it shall be the duty of the director or superintendent of each of the departments created in this article, in cooperation with the city manager, to see that all ordinances, regulations and conditions relating to such department are properly observed and enforced.
(Code 1978, § 2-236; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-166)
Sec. 2-211. - Salaries.
The director or superintendent of each of the departments created in this article shall be paid such salary as may be fixed by the city manager, with the advice of the board of commissioners.
(Code 1978, § 2-222; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-167)
Secs. 2-212—2-230. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - CITY CLERK-CUSTOMER SERVICE DEPARTMENT

Sec. 2-231. - Mission.
The mission of the city clerk-customer service department is to collect all revenues due the city and to deposit same in a timely manner to the scheduled bank depository so designated by the board of commissioners. This division also is charged with the responsibility of assuring that all appropriate fees/rates for utilities are billed to each user.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-181)
Sec. 2-232. - Objectives.
The objectives of the city clerk-customer service department are to:
(1)
Collect and receipt all revenues.
(2)
Make daily bank deposits.
(3)
Generate work and service orders for utility customers.
(4)
Follow up on work and service orders.
(5)
Collect data from meter reading devices and generate billing accordingly.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-182)
Secs. 2-233—2-257. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - COMMUNITY DEVELOPMENT DEPARTMENT

Sec. 2-258. - Mission.
The mission of the community development department is to accept the responsibility for and administer, in the most efficient and effective manner, the different divisions of the community development department: airport, animal control, building inspection, code enforcement, grants administration, main street, planning and zoning, and tourism.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-196)
Sec. 2-259. - Objectives.
The objectives of the community development department are as follows:
(1)
Administration develops and implements creative community-based strategies and awareness, through adopted policies that will enhance economic opportunities, build strong neighborhoods, business districts, attract visitors, improve quality of life; ensuring growth and development for the city.
(2)
Airport manages the airport and provide quality service and facilities to patrons and community, ensures our airport is a vital part of local, state, and global economy, and maintains compliance with all local, state, and federal laws and policies.
(3)
Animal control provides services to the public and enforces animal control ordinances, which increases safety, welfare and the quality of life for the citizens and visitors of the city.
(4)
Building inspection and permits is responsible for issuing building permits and residential and commercial new construction and renovations, to review plans and conduct building inspections, ensuring compliance with all municipal construction codes, according to federal, state, and local ordinances.
(5)
Code enforcement is responsible to enforce the technical codes adopted in section 103-21, codes of the city and state, in order to protect the health, safety, and welfare of the community and, where violations occur, to work with citizens to achieve compliance through an efficient and fair process. The city marshal shall update and enforce the technical codes adopted in section 103-21 and enforce any state and federal laws.
(6)
Economic development assists with plans for future economic growth of the city and works with industrial prospects and industrial development authority on economic development projects.
(7)
Grants administration researches and secures funding that supports citywide initiatives, maintains data and compliance, provides quality grant writing and leveraging strategic partnerships to enhance city services and revenue streams.
(8)
Main street fosters and enhances the quality of life, supports a thriving downtown, improves the level of activity, preserves historic charm, and ensures economic stability through the efforts of organization, promotion, design, and economic restructuring.
(9)
Planning and zoning ensures that all land use and development proposals conform to the city's comprehensive plan and Code of Ordinances, provides information and services regarding existing and future land use and zoning.
(10)
Public information increases awareness and comprehensive communication services to our internal and external customers and citizens via mass media platforms.
(11)
Tourism strengthens awareness of local attractions and things to do through mass media, and attracts visitors to stimulate economic development.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-197)
Secs. 2-260—2-281. - Reserved.
DIVISION 4. - DOUGLAS UTILITIES DEPARTMENT

Sec. 2-282. - Mission.
The mission of the utilities department is to supply a reliable source of utility service to all the residential, commercial and industrial consumers of the city's utility service, provide street lighting for the city streets and public areas, assist the other departments of the city and strive to furnish all the services in the most efficient manner at the least possible cost.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-236)
Sec. 2-283. - Objectives.
The objectives of the utilities department are to:
(1)
Maintain the utility distribution system.
(2)
Upgrade the distribution system to serve new or expanded loads.
(3)
Work with the city's consulting engineers in performing work plans, rate reviews and system studies.
(4)
Assist the utility consumers in correcting utility problems and expansion of their service requirements.
(5)
Develop specifications and requisitions for equipment, materials and supplies.
(6)
Maintain and expand the street lighting system as needed.
(7)
Assist and furnish expertise to other city departments as needed.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-237)
Sec. 2-284. - Rules and regulations.
The board of commissioners is vested with the duty to draft rules and regulations governing the price to be charged for utility service, rules and regulations to be enforced between the customers and the city, rules regulating the cutting off of delinquents, and rules regarding the collection of rents from customers, and all other regulations necessary to the operation of the utilities department.
(Code 1978, § 2-193; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-238)
Sec. 2-285. - Sinking fund.
It shall be the duty of the board of commissioners to establish and maintain a sinking fund to retire the indebtedness of the city for bonds known as utility bonds now existing and unretired by the city and any bonds or certificates as may be issued in the future. The board shall invest the sinking fund as provided by the laws of the state.
(Code 1978, § 2-195; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-239)
Sec. 2-286. - Profits and surplus money.
All profits and surplus money derived from the operation of the utilities department shall be considered general funds of the city and may be used by the board of commissioners in any manner that the board may deem necessary in the operation of the city, provided the use is not in conflict with the Charter and this Code.
(Code 1978, § 2-196; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-240)
Secs. 2-287—2-304. - Reserved.
DIVISION 5. - FINANCE DEPARTMENT

Sec. 2-305. - Purchasing division.
(a)
Mission. The mission of the purchasing division is to purchase supplies, materials and equipment for all departments of the city. It is also the mission of this division to warehouse supplies and materials that each division has deemed necessary to operate their division efficiently.
(b)
Objectives. The objectives of the purchasing division are to:
(1)
Purchase supplies, materials and equipment for all divisions.
(2)
Warehouse the necessary supplies and equipment for all divisions.
(3)
Receive all supplies and materials, unless safety considerations will not allow.
(4)
Issue supplies to all divisions at their request.
(c)
Purchasing manual. Procurement and purchasing procedures shall be approved by the board of commissioners in accordance with the city's purchasing manual. Such manual shall include procedures for bidding, acquisition, advertisements and disposal of surplus property.
(d)
Travel reimbursement. Reimbursement for travel on official city business shall be in accordance with travel regulations approved by the board of commissioners.
(e)
Vehicle usage. City vehicle assignments and reimbursement for use of privately-owned vehicles shall be in accordance with city vehicle policy as approved by the board of commissioners.
(Code 1993, pt. II, §§ 2-331—2-335)
Sec. 2-306. - Mission.
The mission of the finance department is to:
(1)
Maintain accounting records of the city ensuring that all transactions are properly classified and recorded.
(2)
Ensure that all expenditures are budgeted and do not exceed budgeted amounts.
(3)
Reconcile bank checking accounts monthly.
(4)
Maintain records of city's investments of surplus funds.
(5)
Verify and pay all claims of creditors in a timely manner.
(6)
Under the direction of the city manager, prepare an annual budget for all city funds.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-251)
Sec. 2-307. - Objectives.
The objectives of the finance department are to:
(1)
Maintain accounting records of the city on a current basis.
(2)
Verify and pay all claims of creditors in a timely manner.
(3)
Reconcile bank statements to city's records each month.
(4)
Monitor city's investment of surplus funds.
(5)
Prepare a monthly statement of revenues and expenditures of the city with a comparison on annual budget.
(6)
Under the direction of the city manager, prepare an annual budget of all city operations for the succeeding fiscal year and have it approved by the city commission prior to the beginning of that year.
(7)
Ensure daily operation of a central computer system for users.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-252)
Sec. 2-308. - Responsibilities.
The responsibilities of the finance department include:
(1)
Payment of city obligations for debts incurred in its operation. The duties of the accounts payable clerk are to receive, process and pay all claims of creditors after verification with receiving departments.
(2)
Acquisition, operation and maintenance of a computer system for use in utility billing and general accounting functions of the city. A computer systems operator will be appointed by the city manager. The duties of the computer systems operator are the operation and maintenance of the computer system, including hardware, system programs and operating programs.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-253)
Sec. 2-309. - Audits of accounts against city.
All accounts against the city shall be audited by the city finance director and city manager periodically and shall be audited by a competent auditing firm at least once each year. The city commission shall select such auditing firm annually.
(Code 1978, § 2-100; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-254)
Secs. 2-310—2-336. - Reserved.
DIVISION 6. - FIRE DEPARTMENT

Sec. 2-337. - Mission.
The mission of the fire department is:
(1)
To save lives and protect property.
(2)
Fire suppression in the city and county.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-266)
Sec. 2-338. - Objectives.
The objectives of the fire department are to:
(1)
Conduct a fire prevention program.
(2)
Meet training requirements.
(3)
Tour and inspect all business and industrial type buildings.
(4)
Test and maintain all fire hydrants.
(5)
Maintain all rescue and firefighting equipment and buildings.
(6)
Ensure that all personnel meet physical fitness requirements.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-267)
Sec. 2-339. - Position of chief.
The fire chief shall be under the direct supervision of the city manager and shall be the head of the fire department of the city. The fire chief can be removed from office by the city manager, with the consent of the board of commissioners, after stating in writing to the board the reasons and causes for such removal.
(Code 1978, § 2-160; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-268)
Sec. 2-340. - Officers.
The officers of the fire department shall consist of chief, assistant chief, a captain for each company, and such other officers as may, from time to time, be designated by the city manager or the department, with the approval of the board of commissioners.
(Code 1978, § 2-162; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-269)
Sec. 2-341. - Command of department; authority at fires.
The chief of the department shall at all times have absolute command of the department and the entire apparatus that may be employed for the extinguishment of fires, and shall have authority to require any of the force to work at such place and with or on such apparatus as may seem to him to be conducive to best results. In the absence or disability of the chief, the control and authority shall devolve upon the senior officer present. If no officer is present at a fire, the city manager shall appoint some competent and efficient firefighter to act instead.
(Code 1978, § 2-163; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-270)
Sec. 2-342. - Inspection, repairs of buildings and equipment.
The fire chief shall examine or cause to be examined, four times a year or more often if necessary or required, the condition of the houses in which are stored the reels or wagons, trucks and other apparatus, hose, etc., and also the fire hydrants belonging to the city; and if same or any part thereof are not in condition to be used in case of fire, he shall at once report the same to the city manager. If any immediate repairs are required, he shall cooperate with the committee and the foreman of the companies in having such additions or repairs made, but no expensive additions or repairs shall be made without the approval of the city manager or board of commissioners.
(Code 1978, § 2-164; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-271)
Sec. 2-343. - Rules and regulations.
The fire chief is authorized and empowered to prescribe rules and regulations for the government of the fire department, not inconsistent with the ordinances of the city.
(Code 1978, § 2-166; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-272)
Secs. 2-344—2-374. - Reserved.
DIVISION 7. - GAS DEPARTMENT

Sec. 2-375. - Mission.
The mission of the gas department is to supply a reliable source of natural gas service to all the residential, commercial and industrial consumers of the city's natural gas service, assist the other departments of the city and strive to furnish all the services in the most efficient manner at the least possible cost.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-286)
Sec. 2-376. - Objective.
The objective of the gas department is to maintain efficient supply of natural gas to the city.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-287)
Sec. 2-377. - Profits and surplus money.
After all bonds outstanding against the gas system of the city have been retired and after all indebtedness against the gas system have been retired, all profits and surplus money derived from the operation of the gas system shall be considered general funds of the city and may be used by the board of commissioners in any manner that the board of commissioners may deem necessary in the operation of the city, provided the use is not in conflict with any bond issue that might be outstanding or any other indebtedness that might be outstanding against the gas system and provided such use is not in conflict with the Charter and ordinances of the city.
(Code 1978, § 2-205; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-288)

Sec. 2-398. - Purpose and functions.
(a)
The purpose of the human resources department is to assist the departments within the city by advertising job vacancies, accepting applications, screening, interviewing and helping to select the most qualified applicant for the job. DIVISION 10. - POLICE DEPARTMENT

Sec. 2-452. - Mission.
The police department is dedicated to working in partnership with the community to provide exemplary service by maintaining a proud tradition of honor, integrity, and a commitment to service.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-311)
Sec. 2-453. - Primary objectives.
The primary objectives of the police department are:
(1)
To protect the lives and property of the citizens we serve.
(2)
The prevention, detection and investigation of criminal activity.
(3)
Apprehension of offenders.
(4)
The recovery of property and its return to rightful owners.
(5)
To develop highly trained officers.
(6)
Maintenance of ethical standards.
(7)
Traffic control.
(8)
Performance of community services.
(9)
To serve and to protect.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-312)
Sec. 2-454. - Chief of police.
The chief of police shall be appointed by and under the direct supervision of the city manager. The chief of police shall be the head of the police department.
(Code 1978, § 2-110; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-313)
Sec. 2-455. - Acting chief.
During the sickness or absence from the city of the chief of police, or upon the chief's death, suspension or removal from office, the city manager shall designate one of the police officers to act as chief temporarily, or until the appointment of a chief for the vacancy.
(Code 1978, § 2-111; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-314)
Sec. 2-456. - Official oath.
(a)
The chief and each member of the police force shall take and subscribe the following oath:
GEORGIA—Coffee County.
I, swear (or affirm) that I will well and truly discharge the duties of the Police Officer of the City of Douglas, and that I will faithfully perform and discharge the duties of the position, conscientiously and without malice or partiality, to the best of my ability.
I further swear (or affirm) that I am not the holder of any public monies due this or any other State or political subdivision unaccounted for; and In the foreign states, and I am otherwise qualified to hold said office to which I am appointed, and for so long as I am employed with the City of Douglas Police Department, according to the Constitution and Laws of Georgia, and I will support the Constitution of the United States and this State.
I do further swear (or affirm) to obey, to adhere to, to uphold and to enforce the laws of the United States of America and the State of Georgia at all times; and do further swear (or affirm) that I will bear faith and allegiance to the Governor of the State of Georgia, the Legal Authority appointed over me by the City of Douglas and the officers appointed over me, according to law, SO HELP MY GOD.
(b)
The oath may be administered by the mayor or any judicial officer of the state. The oath shall be filed with the clerk.
(Code 1978, § 2-112; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-315)
Sec. 2-457. - Enforcement of law by chief.
It shall be the duty of the chief of police at all times, day or night, to preserve the public peace; prevent the commission of crime and arrest offenders; to prosecute offenders before the superior and city courts of the county and municipal court for all crimes committed within the city; to protect the rights of persons and property; to regard the public health; to report nuisances in the streets, alleys and other places; to provide proper police force at fires; to protect the firefighters and property thereat; to protect strangers and travelers at carrier stations; to see that the police officers patrol all portions of the city; to attend himself, or to designate some member of the force to attend, all meetings of the board of commissioners, and all sessions of the municipal court. He shall enforce obedience to all ordinances of the city which are applicable to police or health.
(Code 1978, § 2-113; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-316)
Sec. 2-458. - Disposition of fees.
All fees collected by the police department shall be paid into the city treasury.
(Code 1978, § 2-142; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-317)
Sec. 2-459. - Divisions.
The police department is composed of the following divisions:
(1)
Administrative services;
(2)
Criminal investigative unit;
(3)
Field operations;
(4)
Staff operations;
(5)
Professional standards and training.
Secs. 2-460—2-516. - Reserved.

DIVISION 13. - WATER AND WASTEWATER DEPARTMENT

Sec. 2-571. - Mission.
The mission of the water and wastewater department is to supply, maintain and expand a safe dependable water and wastewater service to the public at a reasonable cost.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-376)
Sec. 2-572. - Objectives.
The objectives of the water and wastewater department are to:
(1)
Maintain all water and wastewater department's deep wells and lift stations.
(2)
Assist in the upgrading and extension of lines and equipment.
(3)
Keep updated evaluations of the operations of the system.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-377)

(Code 1978, § 2-193; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-378)
Sec. 2-574. - Maintenance, operation, etc.
The city manager is vested with full power and authority to maintain, extend, improve, modify and operate the water and wastewater department and shall have full authority to do all things necessary to successfully and properly operate the department with the advice of the board of commissioners.
(Code 1978, § 2-194; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-379)
Sec. 2-575. - Sinking fund.
It shall be the duty of the board of commissioners to establish and maintain a sinking fund to retire the indebtedness of the city for bonds known as water and wastewater bonds and water and wastewater revenue certificates now existing and unretired by the city and any bonds or certificates as may be issued in the future. The board shall invest the sinking fund as provided by the laws of the state.
(Code 1978, § 2-195; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-380)
Sec. 2-576. - Profits and surplus money.
All profits and surplus money derived from the operation of the water, wastewater and wastewater treatment department shall be considered general funds of the city and may be used by the board of commissioners in any manner that the board may deem necessary in the operation of the city, provided the use is not in conflict with the Charter and the ordinances of the city.
(Code 1978, § 2-196; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-381)
Secs. 2-577—2-600. - Reserved.
