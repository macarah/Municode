Sec. 16-31. - Abandonment generally.
It shall be unlawful for any vehicle to be left unattended at any place within the city for such time and under such circumstances described in this article as to cause such vehicle reasonably to appear to have been abandoned.
(Ord. of 4-11-1970, § 1; Code 1987, § 9-20; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 16-32. - Leaving wrecked vehicle on street prohibited.
It shall be unlawful to leave unattended any partially dismantled, nonoperating, wrecked or junked vehicle on any street or other public property within the city.
(Ord. of 4-11-1970, § 2; Code 1987, § 9-21; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Cross reference— Streets, sidewalks and other public places, ch. 34.
Sec. 16-33. - Removal of vehicles abandoned on public property.
The police department, upon discovery of any vehicle left unattended on public property shall determine if the vehicle poses an immediate threat to public health or safety or traffic congestion, perform an unattended vehicle check and place an unattended vehicle check card on the vehicle, or review the unattended vehicle check card as prescribed by O.C.G.A. § 40-11-14. Should the vehicle pose an immediate threat to public health or safety or traffic congestion or if the vehicle bears a vehicle check card that indicates that the vehicle has been left unattended for more than five days, the police may cause it to be immediately removed to a garage or other place of safety. Within 24 hours or completing an unattended vehicle check or having a vehicle which poses an immediate threat removed, the police shall query the criminal justice information system. If a vehicle has been reported as stolen, the police shall notify the law enforcement agency that filed such report and provide such information as required by state law.
(Ord. of 4-11-1970, § 4; Code 1987, § 9-22; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 2021-14 , adopted Feb. 14, 2022, amended the title of § 16-33 to read as herein set out. The former § 16-33 title pertained to removal of vehicle.
Sec. 16-34. - Time limit for leaving wrecked vehicle on private property.
It shall be unlawful for any person in charge or control of any property within the city, whether as owner, tenant, occupant, lessee or agent thereof, to permit or allow any partially dismantled, nonoperating, wrecked, junked or discarded vehicle of any kind or character to remain on such property within the city longer than 15 days unless the vehicle is stored in an enclosed building or located on the premises of a business enterprise operated in a lawful place and manner, when necessary to the operation of such business enterprise, or such vehicle is stored in an appropriate place and maintained in a lawful manner.
(Ord. of 4-11-1970, § 3; Code 1987, § 9-23; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 16-35. - Notice to landowner to remove wrecked vehicle on private property.
When a vehicle is abandoned, stored, parked or left on any vacant lot, property or premises within the city for a period of more than 15 days as provided in section 16-34, notice shall be served personally by the police department or other authorized official designated by the city to the owner or person in charge of the property or premises if occupied or if unoccupied by certified U.S. mail to the owner or person in charge of the property or premises, with an additional notice posted on the vehicle to remove, store or dispose of the vehicle within ten days from the date of the notice. Such notice shall set forth the date and time of a hearing before the municipal court, the purpose of which will be to determine whether the vehicle is abandoned, stored or parked in violation of this article. Such notice shall further state that upon failure to remove, properly store, house or otherwise dispose of the vehicle, the court may order the vehicle removed, sold or disposed of by the city, and any proceeds arising from the sale thereof will be applied first to payment of expenses incurred in connection with the removal and sale of vehicle. Any remaining balance shall be paid to the owner thereof.
(Ord. of 4-11-1970, § 5; Code 1987, § 9-24; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 16-36. - Notice to vehicle owner; payment of costs.
Any vehicles removed from the streets or other public property in the city pursuant to this article shall be similarly posted with the notice as provided in section 16-35 and a copy thereof sent by certified mail to the owner of the vehicle if known or capable of ascertainment. Any owner claiming a vehicle removed and stored prior to the disposition thereof by the city shall present proof of ownership and pay all charges and costs incurred in connection with removal and storage thereof before the vehicle shall be returned to the owner.
(Ord. of 4-11-1970, § 7; Code 1987, § 9-25; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 16-37. - Sale of vehicle.
Upon failure of the owner to claim any vehicle removed from the streets or other public property in the city within 30 days after it has been impounded by the police department or other authorized official pursuant to this article, it shall be advertised and sold by the city. Such advertisement shall be published according to the legal requirements in the newspaper in which official city notices are published and shall describe and identify the vehicle to be sold, the fact that it has been impounded by the police department as an abandoned vehicle and will be sold at the place where stored to the highest bidder for cash on a date certain ten days after the publication of the last notice unless the vehicle shall have been claimed by the owner and all costs incurred in connection with the removal, storage and advertisement thereof are paid prior thereto.
(Ord. of 4-11-1970, § 6; Code 1987, § 9-26; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 16-38. - Punishment of violators.
Regardless of impoundment and sale of the vehicle as provided in this article, any person found in violation shall be punished as provided in section 1-8. Each day in which a person is in violation of this article constitutes a new and separate violation and may be punishable as such.
(Ord. of 4-11-1970, § 7; Code 1987, § 9-27; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
