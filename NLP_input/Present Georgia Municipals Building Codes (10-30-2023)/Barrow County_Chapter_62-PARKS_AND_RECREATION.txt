DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 62-31. - Name.
The name of the board created by this article shall be "Barrow County Parks and Recreation Board".
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 1)
Sec. 62-32. - Purpose.
The purpose of the parks and recreation board is to carry on a recreation program.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 3(1))
Sec. 62-33. - Composition; appointment.
The membership of the county parks and recreation board shall consist of seven persons appointed by the board of commissioners. A general guideline regarding members of the board shall be one member from each commission district and one member at large. All members shall be selected without regard to race, sex, religion or national origin.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 2A)
Sec. 62-34. - Terms.
For the appointment of the initial members of the parks and recreation board, three members, appointed by commission districts 1, 2 and 3, will have one-year terms; two members, appointed by commission districts 4 and 5, will have two-year terms; and two members, appointed by commission district 6 and the chairman of the board of commissioners, will have three-year terms. Thereafter, all members of the board shall serve for terms of three years.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 2A)
Sec. 62-35. - Compensation.
All seven members of the parks and recreation board shall serve on a voluntary basis and without remuneration of any kind.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 2A)
Sec. 62-36. - Ex officio members.
The board of commissioners shall serve as ex officio members of the parks and recreation board without a vote.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 2A)
Sec. 62-37. - Nominations for appointment.
The parks and recreation board shall have the privilege of submitting to the board of commissioners such names of persons for appointment they feel are suitable, but there shall be nothing binding on the board of commissioners in these appointments suggestions.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 2A)
Sec. 62-38. - Reappointment; removal.
Persons appointed to the parks and recreation board may be reappointed by the board of commissioners on expiration of a term on the board. However, any appointee to the board may be removed, by the commissioner who made the appointment, for cause.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 2A)
Sec. 62-39. - Resignations.
Resignations from the parks and recreation board shall be tendered in writing to the board of commissioners, with a copy thereof to the chairperson of the board.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 2B)
Sec. 62-40. - Vacancies.
The absence from three consecutive regular meetings by any member of the parks and recreation board, without due cause, shall constitute a vacancy on the parks and recreation board.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 8E)
Sec. 62-41. - Filling vacancies.
The board of commissioners shall move expediently to appoint a new member to fill an unexpired term.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 2B)
Sec. 62-42. - General responsibilities.
The parks and recreation board shall formulate, implement, operate, administer, and maintain such recreation program to the best interest of all county citizens, expending such sums as are appropriated for its purposes by the county governing bodies, with such expenditures subject to the approval of the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 3(2))
Sec. 62-43. - Expansion of facilities.
The parks and recreation board, subject to the approval and direction of the board of commissioners, shall provide for expansion of facilities open to all citizens of the county.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 3(3))
Sec. 62-44. - Statutory powers; duties.
The parks and recreation board shall be vested, except as restricted herein, with all powers and duties as granted under O.C.G.A. § 36-64-3, as it now exists, or as it may hereafter be amended.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 5(1))
Sec. 62-45. - Budget.
The parks and recreation board and director shall, each year, timely submit to the governing body of the county a budget for the administration, operation and maintenance of the recreation program and showing expected expenditures in each of the recreation areas.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 5(2))
Sec. 62-46. - Fees, charges.
The parks and recreation board is empowered to establish, revise and collect fees and rentals for the use of recreation areas and to impose and set requirements governing such use. Accurate records of such fees shall be always maintained and open to inspection by the county governing bodies. Anticipated fees shall be accounted for in the annual budget.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 5(3))
Sec. 62-47. - Reports to governing bodies.
The parks and recreation board shall periodically report to the governing bodies in the county the condition of the respective lands and capital improvements thereof, and shall immediately notify such bodies of any damage thereto or condition requiring repair and make recommendations concerning such condition and/or required repairs.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 5(4))
Sec. 62-48. - Amendments.
The bylaws of the parks and recreation board may be amended with a simple majority vote of a quorum of the membership at any meeting, provided notification of the bylaws change has been given to the membership at the meeting prior to the meeting at which the bylaws amendment is voted on. This approved amendment is a recommendation to the board of commissioners for adoption.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 9)
Secs. 62-49—62-70. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - OFFICERS AND EMPLOYEES

Sec. 62-71. - Organization.
The parks and recreation board shall elect its chairman, and such officers as are necessary, from among its appointed members. The terms of the chairman and such other officers shall be one year, or until their successors are duly elected and qualified.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 4(1))
Sec. 62-72. - Officers.
The officers of the parks and recreation board shall be chairperson, vice-chairperson, secretary and treasurer. These officers shall be elected from the parks and recreation board members by a majority vote of the parks and recreation board members present and voting.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 7)
Sec. 62-73. - Selection.
The officers of the parks and recreation board shall be elected by secret ballot annually, and shall serve for the term of one-year, not to exceed two consecutive terms.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 7)
Sec. 62-74. - Chairperson.
The chairperson of the parks and recreation board shall be responsible for presiding at all parks and recreation board meetings; shall serve as parks and recreation board liaison between the parks and recreation board and the board of commissioners and other funding agencies; shall be spokesperson for the parks and recreation board voicing those policies of the parks and recreation board which have been determined by a majority vote of the parks and recreation board; shall appoint such standing committees or temporary committees as the chairperson deems necessary to the efficient operation of the business of the parks and recreation board; shall be responsible for making an annual report on the parks and recreation board to the board of commissioners. The director and other officers of the parks and recreation board shall assist the chairperson in compiling this report which shall cover financial as well as activities of the recreation program.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 7)
Sec. 62-75. - Vice-chairperson.
The vice-chairperson shall preside at any meetings from which the chairperson must be absent; shall assist the chairperson in any of the chairperson's duties, where necessary; shall be responsible for taking an interested oversight in the general activities of the recreation program; and shall assist the director in an advisory capacity, wherever needed and/or requested in improving activities already in the program or in adding new activity areas to the existing program. The vice-chairperson shall become the chairperson, should that office become vacant by resignation or otherwise.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 7)
Sec. 62-76. - Secretary.
The parks and recreation board shall appoint a secretary who may be an officer or employee of the county, or of the parks and recreation board. The secretary shall be responsible for taking the minutes of each parks and recreation board meeting; shall assist the chairperson in compiling the annual report (a copy of which shall be attached to that year's minutes as a permanent reference); and perform such other functions as may be required of the secretary.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, §§ 4(2), 7)
Sec. 62-77. - Treasurer.
The treasurer shall receive all monies for the parks and recreation board and deposit all monies in such depositories as the parks and recreation board may direct; shall pay all bills and charges approved by the parks and recreation board; shall render to the parks and recreation board a financial statement at such intervals as the parks and recreation board may direct; shall itemize the expenses and income for the period reported; and shall attend to such other duties as may be assigned by the chairperson.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 7)
Sec. 62-78. - Recreation director.
(a)
The board of commissioners shall appoint a recreation director and such other staff and employees as it may deem necessary to properly and adequately conduct the recreation program. However, the salaries of such officers and employees shall be within and consistent with the budget established and approved. All such personnel shall be subject to the personnel plan of the county.
(b)
The duties of the director shall be to plan, organize, direct and control a countywide recreation program, pursuant to the policy established by the board of commissioners and within the budget submitted to and approved by the county's governing bodies. The director shall report all progress on activities to the parks and recreation board as requested by the board of commissioners. Checks issued by the parks and recreation board shall require the signature of the director and the chairperson of the parks and recreation board.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 6)
Secs. 62-79—62-100. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - MEETINGS

Sec. 62-101. - Regular meetings.
The parks and recreation board shall hold regular bimonthly meetings at a time and place to be designated by the parks and recreation board.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 8A)
Sec. 62-102. - Special meetings.
Special meetings of the parks and recreation board may be called by the chairperson at the request of three members of the parks and recreation board.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 8B)
Sec. 62-103. - Quorum.
A quorum of the parks and recreation board shall consist of four members for any meeting of the parks and recreation board.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 8C)
Sec. 62-104. - Minutes.
Minutes of all meetings of the parks and recreation board shall be taken to reflect an accurate reporting of the business conducted. These minutes shall be available to all persons and organizations upon request.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 8D)
Sec. 62-105. - Annual meeting.
The annual meeting of the parks and recreation board shall be held at the September meeting each year. At this meeting the officers shall be elected for the coming year.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, § 8E)
Sec. 62-106. - Rules of procedure.
The parks and recreation board shall make its own rules of procedure, consistent with state law and the authority granted it by resolution of the county. Generally, the procedure at meetings of the parks and recreation board shall be governed by the latest edition of Robert's Rules of Order, Newly Revised.
(Res. of 2-11-1997, §§ 4(3), 10)
