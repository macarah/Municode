ARTICLE II. - SOLID WASTE MANAGEMENT PLAN

Sec. 58-19. - Findings.
The Board of Commissioners of Mitchell County, the Mayor and Council of Baconton, the Mayor and Council of Camilla, the Mayor and Council of Pelham, and the Mayor and Council of Sale City have found it necessary to prepare a solid waste management plan for the Cities of Baconton, Camilla, Pelham, Sale City and Mitchell County to meet the needs associated with the collection, disposal, and reduction of solid waste in the communities and to ensure the planned orderly strategy for solid waste management policies that protect the public health, safety and welfare. The state department of community affairs has reviewed this solid waste management plan and found the plan to be in compliance with the minimum planning standards and procedures for solid waste management.
(Code 2004, § 62-31; Ord. of 3-9-1993)
Sec. 58-20. - Adoption.
The county board of commissioners adopts the revised consolidated solid waste management plan for the county.
(Code 2004, § 62-32; Ord. of 3-9-1993)
Secs. 58-21—58-43. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - SHORT-TERM WORK PROGRAM

Sec. 58-44. - Findings.
The chairman and board of commissioners have found it necessary to adopt an update to the short-term work program portion of their solid waste management plan to meet the requirements of the state's minimum planning standards for solid waste management. The update and the adoption of this new short-term work program will ensure that the county maintains its qualified local government status. The county elected officials will use the new short-term work program to guide public expenditures and public programs.
(Code 2004, § 62-61; Ord. of 6-7-1998(1))
Sec. 58-45. - Adoption.
The updated short-term work program shall be adopted as approved by the state department of community affairs. The board of commissioners certify that adequate citizen participation requirements have been met.
(Code 2004, § 62-62; Ord. of 6-7-1998(1))
Secs. 58-46—58-63. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - LITTER[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Litter Control Law, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-40 et seq.; littering highways, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-249.

Sec. 58-64. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Household garbage means animal, vegetable, and fruit refuse matter and all other refuse ordinarily created as by-products of a household or restaurant, such as tin cans, bottles, paper, cardboard, plastics, and wrapping or packaging materials.
Litter means discarded sand, gravel, slag, brickback, rubbish, waste material, tin cans, refuse, garbage, trash, debris, dead animals, solid waste or other discarded materials of every kind and description which are not biomedical waste, hazardous waste, a hazardous substance or which are not dumped for commercial purposes.
Public or private property means the right-of-way of any road or highways; any body of water or watercourse, any park, playground, building, refuge, or conservation or recreation area; and residentially formed properties, or farm properties, timberlands, or forests; or any commercial or industrial property.
Written advertisement means any circular, dodger, sign, advertising matter, or other written or printed commercial, business, or mercantile information, either on paper or other material used for advertising or information purposes.
(Code 2004, § 62-91; Ord. No. 2001-009, § 1, 5-8-2001)
Sec. 58-65. - Littering on public or private property.
It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to dump, deposit, throw, or leave or to cause or permit the dumping, depositing, placing, throwing, or leaving of litter on any public or private property in the county unless:
(1)
The property is designated by the state or by any of its agencies or political subdivisions for the disposal of litter and the person is authorized by the proper public authority to use such property;
(2)
The litter is placed into a litter receptacle or container or a garbage bin installed on such property; or
(3)
The person is the owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property or has first obtained consent of the owner or tenant in lawful possession or unless the act is done under the personal direction of the owner or tenant, all in a manner consistent with the public welfare.
(Code 2004, § 62-92; Ord. No. 2001-009, § 2, 5-8-2001)
Sec. 58-66. - Evidence.
(a)
Whenever litter is thrown, deposited, dropped or dumped from any motor vehicle, boat, airplane, or other conveyance in violation of section 58-65, it shall be prima facie evidence that the operator of the conveyance has violated said section 58-65.
(b)
Except as provided in subsection (a) of this section, whenever any litter, household garbage, written advertisement or other material which is dumped, deposited, thrown, posted or left on public or private property in violation of section 58-65, 58-67, 58-68 or 58-69 is discovered to contain any articles, including, but not limited to, letters, bills, publications, or other writings which display the name of the person thereon in such a manner as to indicate that the article belongs or belonged to such person, it shall be a rebuttable presumption that such person has violated this article.
(Code 2004, § 62-93; Ord. No. 2001-009, § 3, 5-8-2001)
Sec. 58-67. - Littering on public areas.
It shall be unlawful to place, attach, post, throw down, deposit or leave any written advertisement, litter, household garbage, used appliances or construction debris on any of the public areas, roads, rights-of-way, or garbage bins provided for garbage collection at the county maintained convenience centers; provided, however, that it shall not be unlawful for residents of the county to place or deposit household garbage in the garbage bins provided for garbage collection at the county-maintained convenience centers.
(Code 2004, § 62-94; Ord. No. 2001-009, § 4, 5-8-2001)
Sec. 58-68. - Household garbage only for garbage bins at county maintained convenience centers.
The garbage bins provided by the county at the county-maintained convenience centers are suitable for the dumping therein of household garbage only, and it shall be unlawful for any person to place in such garbage bins any thing except household garbage.
(Code 2004, § 62-95; Ord. No. 2001-009, § 5, 5-8-2001)
Sec. 58-69. - Unlawful for nonresident use of county maintained garbage bins.
It shall be unlawful for any nonresident of the county to deposit household garbage in any garbage bin provided by the county at a county-maintained convenience center.
(Code 2004, § 62-96; Ord. No. 2001-009, § 6, 5-8-2001)
Sec. 58-70. - Unlawful to inventory, sort or reclaim refuse within garbage bins at county maintained convenience centers.
It shall be unlawful for any person, except the agents or employees of the company or companies contracting with the county for the removal and disposal of the refuse placed within the garbage bins at the county-maintained convenience centers and except a person who erroneously placed items in said garbage bins, to remove, examine, or in any manner inventory, sort or reclaim the refuse within the garbage bin or bins placed at the county maintained convenience centers. It shall further be unlawful for any person to remain at a county-maintained convenience center for the purpose of examining, removing, sorting or obtaining material deposited in, or to be deposited in, a garbage bin located at the county-maintained convenience centers.
(Code 2004, § 62-97; Ord. No. 2001-009, § 7, 5-8-2001)
Sec. 58-71. - Violations.
A violation of any portion of this article may be prosecuted by any law enforcement officer or by any person authorized by resolution of the county board of commissioners to issue citations, which citations shall be returnable to the magistrate court of the county and shall conform to the requirements of O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63. Any person convicted of or pleading guilty to a first offense of violating this article shall be punished by a fine of not less than $250.00 and not more than $1,000.00 or imprisonment for no more than 60 days, or both. Any person convicted of or pleading guilty to a subsequent offense of violating this article shall be punished by a fine of not less than $500.00 and not more than $1,000.00 or imprisonment for no more than 60 days, or both.
(Code 2004, § 62-98; Ord. No. 2001-009, § 8, 5-8-2001; Ord. No. 2003-002, § 1, 3-11-2003)
