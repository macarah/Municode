ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 86-1. - Assessments for street improvement.
Where any work of making any improvements upon the streets, avenues, lanes, or alleys is done by the city, the cost shall include the entire cost of all materials used and labor done in the performance of the work, and shall include all engineering, legal and other expenses incurred in connection with such work; and all such expenses shall be included in the cost of improvements to be assessed against the lots or parcels of land abutting upon such improvements.
(Code 1976, § 4-1006; Ord. No. 76-24, § 1, 8-4-1976)
State Law reference— Executions for collection of municipal assessments for paving streets and laying sewers, O.C.G.A. § 48-5-358.
Sec. 86-2. - Use of city right-of-way.
It shall be unlawful for any person to build upon, use, obstruct or otherwise encroach upon the right-of-way of any public street within the city limits without the express written permission of the city engineer or city manager. Failure to remove any such obstruction after proper notice from the city engineer shall constitute a separate offense. Each day such offense occurs or continues after proper notice shall constitute a separate offense.
(Code 1976, § 4-1007; Ord. No. 82-26, § 1, 5-6-1982)
Sec. 86-3. - Street carnivals, festivals.
All persons offering to hold carnivals or any festive occasions of any kind within the city are required to first obtain a permit from the mayor and council and to give bond in a sum full and sufficient to settle all bills contracted by them and to exhibit all attractions advertised by them.
(Code 1976, § 7-2001)
Sec. 86-4. - Placing merchandise on sidewalk.
It shall be unlawful for any person within the city to display or permit to be displayed along or on the sidewalk or by or in front of his place of business in the city any goods, wares or merchandise or other articles.
(Code 1976, § 7-2002)
Sec. 86-5. - Selling goods on sidewalks.
(a)
Except as provided in subsection (b) of this section, it shall be unlawful for any person doing business in the city and having a regular place of business in the city to make any sales of any goods, wares or merchandise on any of the streets, sidewalks or alleys of the city.
(b)
All vendors operating trucks or other types of equipment for the sale of sandwiches or other merchandise on the streets of the city shall, before and during any sale:
(1)
Park at the right curb at least eight feet from any other vehicle and at least 100 feet from any intersecting street;
(2)
Use no sound equipment while the vehicle is stopped, nor after 9:30 p.m. daily, nor between 9:30 a.m. and noon on Sundays;
(3)
Service customers only on the curbside; and
(4)
Remain a distance of at least one block away from any public school that is in session.
(Code 1976, § 7-2003)
Sec. 86-6. - Interruption of utility service—Permission required.
It shall be unlawful for any public utility, concern, contractor, plumber, electrician, city employee, or other person to cut off or interrupt any electrical distribution service, gas distribution service, water distribution service or public sewer service, whether such service is operated or maintained by the city or by a public utility concern, without having first obtained permission from the city engineer and from the public utility concern or maintaining such service, if any.
(Code 1976, § 7-2004)
Sec. 86-7. - Same—Regulations.
It shall be unlawful for any person having the permission required by section 86-6 to cut off or interrupt any of the several services referred to in that section without complying with the following regulations:
(1)
Notice shall first be given to the city engineer and to the public utility concern affected, if any, of the area of the city to be affected and of the approximate time that such service will remain cut off or interrupted; and similar notice shall be given, in such manner and at such time as may be reasonably directed by the city engineer, to all residents and business concerns to be affected;
(2)
If the service to be cut off or interrupted is a water distribution service, similar notice shall first be given to the fire chief;
(3)
No such cutoff or interruption shall continue longer than is reasonably necessary to accomplish the purpose authorized by the city engineer.
(Code 1976, § 7-2005)
Sec. 86-8. - Protection of vehicular traffic.
(a)
No person shall intentionally cause, either directly or indirectly, any object to be placed upon any street, highway, bridge, alley, driveway, road or other passageway with the city normally and customarily utilized in whole or in part for the movement of motor vehicular traffic that obstructs, retards, or tends to obstruct or retard the movement of vehicular traffic on or that has the potential to damage vehicles that travel on such place or to harm the occupants of such vehicles.
(b)
No person while in a motor vehicle shall follow any other person in another motor vehicle upon any street, highway, alley or road within the city with the intent to cause such person to fear bodily harm or otherwise intimidate or coerce such person.
(c)
No person shall intentionally obstruct the free, convenient and normal use of any public sidewalk, street, highway, bridge, alley, road or other passageway, or the entrance, exit, corridor or other passageway to or from any adjacent building or structure by impeding, hindering, blocking, retarding, delaying or detaining vehicular or pedestrian traffic on such place; however, this subsection shall not be construed to punish handbilling or soliciting for any lawful purpose by isolated individuals unless conducted in a manner that in fact impedes, blocks, delays or detains pedestrian or vehicular traffic.
(d)
The restrictions in subsections (a)—(c) of this section shall not apply when owners of existing buildings provide access to their buildings for handicapped persons. This exception shall only apply provided that a ramp, not more than 36 inches in width and parallel to the right-of-way, may be installed provided the area from the back of the curb to the right-of-way is a distance of six feet or more. This exception in the installation of any ramp shall be subject to the approval of the office of the city engineer.
(Code 1976, § 7-2006; Ord. No. 79-38, 8-8-1979)

ARTICLE II. - PAVING GENERALLY

Sec. 86-41. - Paving of sidewalks, crosswalks, driveways, curbs and gutters.
All paving of sidewalks, crosswalks, driveways, curbs and gutters within the city that join or touch any street or sidewalk within the city shall be done in accordance with the written specifications for such paving on file in the office of the city engineer, and shall be done under the supervision and direction of the city engineer; and the grade for such paving shall be fixed by the city engineer unless otherwise expressly provided by resolution of the mayor and council.
(Code 1976, § 4-1004(a))
Sec. 86-42. - Copy of specifications to property owner.
The city engineer shall furnish to the property owner or his duly authorized agent a copy of specifications when called for by such owner or agent and, upon the request of such owner or agent, shall immediately fix the grade for the paving, all of which shall be without cost or expense to the property owner.
(Code 1976, § 4-1004(b))
Sec. 86-43. - Paving petition.
No petition presented to the mayor and council by abutting property owners pursuant to chapter 1 or article VII of the Charter requesting the paving of any street, avenue, lane or alley, or any part, within the limits of the city shall be considered or approved by the mayor and council unless the petition shall also include a request for installation of curbing and gutter work as a part of the improvements requested.
(Code 1976, § 4-1005(a); Ord. No. 76-23, § 1, 8-4-1976)
Secs. 86-44—86-80. - Reserved.

Sec. 86-81. - Consideration of the renaming of streets and alleys by the mayor and council.
The mayor and council shall consider the changing of the name of a municipally owned public street or alley within the corporate limits of the City of Valdosta only upon receipt of an applicant's petition signed by 60 percent of the owners or verifiable residents of all parcels of land abutting the public street or alley for which a name change is being requested. A parcel is a definitive platted lot or other tract of land shown as a tax parcel on the most recent official tax map of Lowndes County, Georgia, and assigned its own unique tax parcel number by the Lowndes County Board of Assessors office. Each abutting parcel of land shall be entitled to only one applicable signature which may come from the owner or a verifiable resident of said property. A verifiable resident is an individual or business entity that resides on the subject parcel via a written lease agreement with the owner, or his/her agent, on both the dates of the submission of the application and submission of the petition. Upon submission of the petition as provided in subsection 86-82(b), a reimbursement fee, which shall include a reimbursement to the city for actual costs of renaming a public street or alley including, but not limited to, costs for: (1) advertising; (2) new signs; and (3) installation of new signs.
(Ord. No. 2019-2, 2-21-2019)
Sec. 86-82. - Procedure for renaming a public street or alley.
(a)
Pre-submittal meeting required. An applicant requesting the mayor and council to officially rename a public street or alley, or any portion thereof, shall first schedule an appointment and meet with the city engineer or his designee to discuss the nature of the request, determination of filing fees, the applicable city procedures and requirements, and other items that may be required as part of the submitted request. A determination shall also be made as to the actual numbers of abutting parcels that are relevant to the request. After this meeting, the applicant shall then have up to 90 calendar days to submit the required petition, on forms provided by the city, to the city engineer for review and processing.
(b)
Submittal of petition. The applicant shall submit their petition request to the city engineer or his designee, and this shall include the required filing fees and other required materials as discussed during the pre-submittal meeting described above. The city engineer or his designee shall have up to 15 calendar days to determine the completeness and accuracy of the applicant's petition and other submitted materials. The city engineer or his designee shall verify that the petition contains the actual requisite signatures of 60 percent of the owners or verifiable resident of all parcels of land abutting the petitioned public street or alley. Each abutting parcel of land shall be allowed one signature per parcel. These parcels may represent single-family structures, multi-family structures (townhomes, duplexes, condominiums, and apartments), commercial structures, and vacant parcels. Upon determination that the submittal is complete and accurate, the submittal will be deemed as officially accepted and its formal review and processing will commence. Copies of the petition and other relevant materials shall then be distributed to city departmental staff and other relevant parties as determined by the city engineer, for review and comment. The city engineer or his designee shall then forward a copy of the petition and other relevant materials to the city clerk and schedule the request for a public hearing with the mayor and council as properly advertised in accordance with subsection 86-82(c) below.
(c)
Advertisement. A legal notice of public hearing shall be placed in a newspaper of general circulation within the city, and shall be published a minimum of two separate times at least ten calendar days before the scheduled public hearing with mayor and council. This notice shall contain the date, time and place of the public hearing, a location description of the street or alley or portion thereof to be renamed, and both the existing and proposed names. After the advertisement requirements have been met, the proposed street renaming will be placed upon the mayor/council meeting agenda for approval or denial.
(d)
Post-notification and effective date of change. The renaming of any public street or alley by the mayor and council shall not become effective until at least 90 calendar days after the approval, or as otherwise stipulated by the mayor and council. Within 15 calendar days of final action by mayor and council, official notification of an approved renaming of a street or alley shall be sent regular mail by the city clerk to the owner of each parcel of land abutting the street or alley portion that was closed. The city clerk shall also send official notification of the approved name change and its effective date to all applicable governmental entities.
(e)
Declination by mayor and council. If mayor and council declines to rename the street or alley, the applicant shall be reimbursed the reimbursement fee within 30 days. The mayor and council shall only consider requests for the changing of a specific street or alley name no more than twice per calendar year.
(Ord. No. 2019-2, 2-21-2019)
Sec. 86-83. - Consideration of the closing of streets and alleys by the mayor and council.
The mayor and council shall consider the closing of a municipally owned public street or alley within the corporate limits of the City of Valdosta only upon receipt of an applicant's petition signed by 60 percent of the owners of all parcels of land abutting the public street or alley for which the closing is being requested. All closures shall be made by mayor and council upon the determination that said street or alley has ceased to be used by the public to the extent that no substantial public purpose is served by it or that its removal from the municipal street system is otherwise in the best public interest.
(Ord. No. 2019-2, 2-21-2019)
Sec. 86-84. - Procedure for closing a public street or alley.
(a)
Pre-submittal meeting required. An applicant requesting the mayor and council to officially close a public street or alley, or any portion thereof, shall first schedule an appointment and meet with the city engineer or his designee to discuss the nature of the request, determination of filing fees, the applicable city procedures and requirements, and other items that may be required as part of the submitted request. A determination shall also be made as to the actual numbers of abutting parcels that are relevant to the request. After this meeting, the applicant shall then have up to 90 calendar days to submit the required petition, on forms provided by the city, to the city engineer for review and processing.
(b)
Submittal of petition. The applicant shall submit their petition request to the city engineer or his designee, and this shall include the required filing fees and other required materials as discussed during the pre-submittal meeting described above. The city engineer or his designee shall have up to 15 calendar days to determine the completeness and accuracy of the applicant's petition and other submitted materials. The city engineer or his designee shall verify that the petition contains the requisite actual signatures of 60 percent of the owners of all the parcels of land abutting the petitioned public street or alley. Each abutting parcel of land shall be allowed one signature which represents the owner of said parcel. Upon determination that the submittal is complete and accurate, the submittal will be deemed as officially accepted and its formal review and processing will commence. Copies of the petition and other relevant materials shall then be distributed to city departmental staff and other relevant parties as determined by the city engineer, for review and comment. The city engineer or his designee shall then forward a copy of the petition and other relevant materials to the city clerk and schedule the request for a public hearing with the mayor and council as properly advertised in accordance with subsection 86-84(c) below.
(c)
Advertisement. A legal notice of public hearing shall be placed in a newspaper of general circulation within the city and shall be published a minimum of two separate times at least ten calendar days before the scheduled public hearing with mayor and council. This notice shall contain the date, time and place of the public hearing, as well as a description of the street or alley or portion thereof to be closed. At least 15 calendar days before the date of said scheduled public hearing, an official notification letter containing the same information as the newspaper ad, shall be mailed to the owner of each parcel of land that abuts the subject street or alley or portion thereof, and a notice of public hearing sign shall also be posted at the termini of the subject street or alley as determined by the city engineer or his designee.
(d)
Post-notification and effective date of change. The closing of any public street or alley by the mayor and council shall become immediately effective upon approval, or as otherwise stipulated by the mayor and council at the time of final action. Within seven calendar days of final action by mayor and council, official notification of an approved closing of a street or alley shall be sent regular mail by the city clerk to the owner of each parcel of land abutting the street or alley portion that was closed. If the public street or alley is also vacated by the mayor and council and resulting in a transfer of ownership and realignment of property boundaries, then such action shall also result in compliance with the subdivision replatting requirements of the city's land development regulations (LDR).
(Ord. No. 2019-2, 2-21-2019)

ARTICLE IV. - SIDEWALK CONSTRUCTION

Sec. 86-121. - Required.
When any parcel of land within the corporate limits of the city abutting existing public streets is developed with new construction, or over half of an existing structure is remodeled or renovated, the owner or developer shall install a four-foot-wide sidewalk along the entire frontage of the parcel, the sidewalk to be installed within the city right-of-way in accordance with plans and specifications approved by the city engineer. Compliance with this article shall not be required for single-family residential lots of record prior to May 4, 1984.
(Code 1976, § 4-1008(a); Ord. No. 84-14, § 1, 5-4-1984)
Sec. 86-122. - Installation in new subdivisions.
In new subdivisions where the developer or owner is installing public streets, sidewalks shall be installed at the time of construction of residence dwellings or other structures. These sidewalks shall be installed within the city right-of-way in accordance with the requirements set forth in section 86-121. On residential streets, sidewalk installations shall be required only on the northern right-of-way of east-west residential streets and only on the eastern right-of-way of north-south streets. Installation shall be required on both the sides of collector or higher classified roads.
(Code 1976, § 4-1008(b); Ord. No. 84-14, § 1, 5-4-1984)
Sec. 86-123. - Limitation on granting certificate of occupancy.
No certificate of occupancy shall be granted if the provisions of this article have not been complied with and construction of the sidewalks have not been completed.
(Code 1976, § 4-1008(c); Ord. No. 84-14, § 1, 5-4-1984)

ARTICLE V. - ASSEMBLIES IN PLACES OF PUBLIC TRAVEL

Sec. 86-161. - Purpose.
The city has a duty to prevent violence, maintain order and protect the right of persons and property to move upon public streets, roads, sidewalks, alleys, and all other places subject to a public easement of travel; to provide an environment conducive to the effective administration of justice and other essential governmental functions and the transaction of public and private business; and to promote the right of all persons to be free from substantial invasions of their privacy in an essentially intolerable manner. This duty is limited by the fundamental right of every person to express his opinions, disseminate information and opinion, to peaceably assemble, and to petition government for redress of grievances as guaranteed by the First and Fourteenth Amendments to the United States Constitution. The purpose of this article is to promote the discharge of these duties while preserving the fundamental freedoms enumerated.
(Code 1976, § 7-2011)
Sec. 86-162. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Block means that portion of any street or sidewalk that falls between its intersections with two other streets.
Fighting words means words which are not protected as speech by the First Amendment to the United States Constitution, are not in any reasonable sense communication of information or opinion, and are generally epithets or personal verbal abuse directed towards another person in his presence that by their very utterance inflict injury or tend to incite an immediate breach of the peace; fighting words frequently include threatening, profane or obscene reviling, mass name-calling (that is, concerted or coordinated name-calling by two or more persons with intent to annoy, ridicule, insult or provoke a violent reaction) and will not ordinarily include derisive or annoying words except when these have the characteristic of plainly tending to incite the addressee to commit an immediate breach of the peace; in every case the circumstances of the utterance control whether particular words fall within this definition.
Handbilling means the activity of a person in a place open and accessible to the general public, distributing any circulars, handbills, advertising or other printed, written or symbolic matter on any subject and for any purpose.
Mass picketing means picketing by a sufficient number of pickets in a manner that effectively prevents other persons with a right to be in the vicinity of the picketing from exercising their right to utilize the area, or facilities in the area, for the purposes to which it has been dedicated either by physical domination of the area or by threats, intimidation or coercion actually arousing or having a tendency to arouse a fear of bodily harm with respect to any person who does not comply with the wishes of the pickets; however, the congregation of three or more pickets for a common cause at the same location, in the presence of other persons not pickets within a distance of 15 feet or less of one another, shall be presumed to be mass picketing unless the contrary is shown by clear evidence.
Parade means a coordinated movement of two or more pedestrians or vehicles upon the streets, parks or other public grounds within the city with an intent of attracting public attention that interferes with or has a tendency to interfere with the normal flow or regulation of traffic upon the street, park or other public ground; however, the term shall not be construed to include picketing within a limited geographic area.
Picket means any person engaged in the act of picketing.
Picketing means either the act of patrolling a particular geographic area by one or more persons as an expression of a point of view with respect to any subject of actual or potential dispute for the purpose of publicizing that viewpoint and with the further purpose of inducing a specific response from persons actually found or likely to be found in the locality, or of handbilling or soliciting by four or more persons in furtherance of a common purpose within a single block or within 100 feet or less of one another, whichever is the shorter distance.
Procession means a public assembly that moves as a unit on the streets, sidewalks, parks or other public grounds or places within the city for the dominant purpose of moving the location of the assembly from its point of origin to a predetermined destination.
Public assembly means a gathering of more than eight persons for a common purpose in a place open to the general public as a result of prior planning or a spontaneous gathering for a common purpose in a place open to the general public that continues in existence for more than 30 minutes.
Public park means any land area within the city, owned by the city, predominantly open to the elements, open and freely accessible to the general public, and primarily dedicated to providing a public place to relax, meet, socialize, eat and drink, and/or engage in recreational activities or other pleasurable pursuits.
Soliciting means the activity of a person in a place open and accessible to the general public who by spoken language directed to persons in the vicinity attempts to disseminate facts or opinions regarding any subject with intent to induce a specific response from any listener.
Special pickup means appliances, furniture or white goods/rubbish that weigh more than 25 pounds gross weight and will not fit in the roll-out container.
Tire means a continuous solid or pneumatic rubber covering designed for encircling the wheel of a vehicle and which is neither attached to the motor vehicle nor a part of the vehicle as original equipment.
(Code 1976, § 7-2012)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 86-163. - Limitations on the right to distribute handbills and solicit.
(a)
Any person may hand out or distribute, without charge to the recipient, any handbill, in any public place to any person willing to accept such handbill, provided that those engaged in such handbilling activities shall not engage in them in such manner so as to block any public doorway, driveway, sidewalk or street intersection or so as to unduly interfere with the flow of traffic, whether pedestrian or vehicular. Such activity shall be conducted in a manner consistent with the maintenance of public health and safety.
(b)
The blocking of the path of any pedestrian or vehicle by any handbiller or solicitor with intent to compel such pedestrian or vehicle to halt its movement for the purpose of rendering such pedestrian or vehicle a captive audience for the solicitations of the picket or to compel the acceptance of any leaflet or handout in return for passage is prohibited.
(c)
The making by a handbiller or solicitor of any threat of bodily harm or property damage to any person whether directed to such person or said with respect to some other person bearing a close relationship to such person or the use of any fighting words by a handbiller or solicitor is prohibited.
(d)
Handbilling or soliciting in a manner that interferes with or is likely to interfere with another person's performance of the duties of his employment is prohibited.
(e)
The possession by any handbiller or solicitor of any object or instrumentality with an apparent potential to cause physical injury to persons or damage to property is prohibited. Objects and instrumentalities encompassed by this prohibition include, but not to the exclusion of others not enumerated, walking or hiking sticks, canes, clubs, knives, firearms, brass knuckles, slingshots, zip guns, pellet guns, rocks, glass bottles, stones and bricks, or any other potential missile or club, teargas, mace, or any other chemical projectile, and any incendiary, stench, coloring or drying chemical, or vicious animal.
(f)
No person shall handbill or solicit in violation of any of the prohibitions of this section.
(Code 1976, § 7-2013; Ord. No. 96-34, 7-25-1996)
Sec. 86-164. - Limitations on the right to picket.
(a)
Mass picketing is prohibited as an unlawful infringement upon the public right to travel freely on public streets, sidewalks, parks and grounds without fear of personal bodily harm or coercive or intimidating persuasion.
(b)
Picketing in streets, alleys, roads, highways, driveways or other places predominantly dedicated to the use of vehicular traffic shall be conducted as to not block the free flow of vehicular traffic. Picketing in streets, alleys, roads, highways, driveways or other places predominantly dedicated to the use of vehicular traffic which blocks the free flow of traffic or creates a traffic hazard is prohibited.
(c)
The blocking of the path of any pedestrian or vehicle by any picket with intent to compel such pedestrian or vehicle to halt its movement for the purpose of rendering such pedestrian or vehicle a captive audience for the solicitations of the picket or to compel the acceptance of any leaflet or handout in return for passage is prohibited.
(d)
The making by a picket of any threat of bodily harm or property damage to any person whether directed to such person or said with respect to some other person bearing a close relationship to such person or the use of any fighting words by a picket is prohibited.
(e)
The picketing of any private home, residence or domicile is prohibited as an unlawful infringement on the right of privacy and the right to be free from fear and intimidation.
(f)
Picketing in a manner that interferes with or is likely to interfere with another person's performance of the duties of his employment is prohibited.
(g)
The possession by any picket of any object or instrumentality with an apparent potential to cause physical injury to persons or damage to property is prohibited. Objects and instrumentalities encompassed by this prohibition include, but not to the exclusion of others not enumerated, clubs, knives, firearms, brass knuckles, slingshots, zip guns, pellet guns, rocks, glass bottles, stones and bricks, or any other potential missile or club, teargas, mace, or any other chemical projectile, any incendiary, stench, coloring or dying chemical, and any vicious animal.
(h)
No person shall picket in violation of any of the prohibitions of this section.
(Code 1976, § 7-2014; Ord. No. 95-2, 2-23-1995; Ord. No. 96-34, 7-25-1996)
Sec. 86-165. - Limitations on the right to assemble.
(a)
The public parks of the city are declared to be public forums dedicated to, among other purposes, the dissemination of information and opinion protected by the First Amendment to the United States Constitution, or other protected expressive conduct in furtherance of which purpose the parks may be used for public gatherings and assemblies. All other public and quasi-public areas of the city may also be used for such purposes. However, public assemblies shall be subject to the following limitations:
(1)
Public assemblies shall be conducted in compliance with the other laws and ordinances of the state and the city.
(2)
Public assemblies upon private property without the consent of the owner are prohibited.
(3)
Public assemblies are not permitted in areas of public buildings dedicated to clerical and office work.
(4)
Public assemblies that thwart the purpose to which the property upon which they occur is dedicated or that interfere with the normal use of such property by others with an equal right of access are prohibited.
(5)
Public assemblies that are inconsistent with or interfere with the rights of another group to assemble pursuant to a valid permit issued by the city manager pursuant to section 86-166 are prohibited.
(6)
Whenever the number of attendees at a public assembly exceeds the natural capacity of the facility so as to threaten the public safety or convenience the excess may be ordered to disperse by city law enforcement officials and, failing to do so, may be punished as provided by this article. Therefore, the city manager, after careful study of facilities within the city limits that are available for public assemblies, shall issue regulations setting forth the maximum capacity of each such facility so that the existence of an overflow crowd may be determined by a numerical count.
(7)
Public assemblies that violate the antinoise ordinances of the city are prohibited.
(8)
Public assemblies shall not interfere, hinder, or stop vehicular or pedestrian traffic, nor create a traffic or safety hazard.
(9)
No person present at a public assembly shall conduct himself in a fashion that will establish a purpose or likelihood of causing or encouraging immediate violence.
(10)
No person present at an assembly may be in possession of a weapon prohibited by O.C.G.A. §§ 16-11-127 and 16-11-127.1.
(11)
The use of fighting words by any person in any public assembly is prohibited.
(b)
No person shall assemble in violation of any of the prohibitions of this section.
(Code 1976, § 7-2015; Ord. No. 96-34, 7-25-1996)
Sec. 86-166. - Preferential treatment of licensed assemblies.
(a)
Although the acquisition of a permit is not a condition to the right to assemble within public parks of the city, nevertheless where two or more groups seek to use simultaneously the same area of the same park or otherwise make overlapping or inconsistent demands for public facilities, the group having the first duly issued permit shall have the right to use such facilities pursuant to the terms of the permit.
(b)
No group, person or organization shall be issued a permit in preference to a valid written request for the same facility at the same time by another group that has utilized the same facility fewer times within the preceding 90-day period.
(c)
An application for an assembly permit to be deemed valid must be filed at the office of the city manager during normal business hours not less than 72 hours prior to the proposed commencement hour for the applicant assembly and must set forth the following information:
(1)
The full legal name of the sponsoring person;
(2)
A bona fide mailing address for each sponsoring person;
(3)
The state of incorporation of any incorporated sponsoring person;
(4)
With respect to any resident agent for service of process of any sponsoring person:
a.
The full legal name of the agent; and
b.
The address of the agent's place of business and his personal residence and the telephone number for each;
(5)
If a sponsoring person has no registered agent for service of process:
a.
A brief statement of its reason for failing to appoint one; and
b.

Sec. 86-167. - Limitations on parades.
(a)
Parades within the city are declared unlawful except when conducted pursuant to the authority of a lawfully issued parade permit. The parade route must be sealed off by traffic enforcement officers of the city before the period during which the parade permit authorized the parade, and no other traffic shall be permitted on the route for the duration of the parade.
(b)
The city manager shall issue a parade permit if the following conditions are satisfied: A written application for a parade permit, signed by the sponsoring person or his legal representative, must be filed at least seven days in advance of the proposed parade date. In cases of emergency or hardship, the city manager, at his discretion, may waive the seven-day advance notice requirement.
(c)
The application for a parade permit must contain the following information:
a.
The full legal name of the sponsoring persons;
b.
A bona fide mailing address for each sponsoring person; and if the sponsor is a corporation, the state of incorporation or state where the corporation is subject service of process;
c.
The full name, business and residential address of the local resident adult who will stand surety to the sponsoring person for any damage caused by participants in the parade; and
d.
The requested date, hours, place of assembly for the parade; the route, the length, and expected duration for such parade; and the place and time of disbandment of such parade.
(d)
The proposed parade route must not intrude into the hospital zones of the city.
(e)
The aggregate length of the proposed parade (including people, vehicles and equestrians) must be at least 100 yards.
(f)
No fee shall be charged for a parade or assembly permit.
(g)
Parade applications shall be processed in the order they are received by the city manager. The city manager shall deny a parade permit if another parade permit has already been issued for the same day.
(h)
The city manager shall utilize the approved parade application form when processing any application for a parade permit.
(i)
All parade permits shall be issued subject to the conditions that:
(1)
No vehicle may travel abreast of another vehicle during the parade, and each vehicle measuring more than ten feet in width must parade in the center of the street.
(2)
No vehicle or equestrian may come within six feet of either side of the street, the curb of any sidewalk, or the nearest protrusion of any parked automobile or other vehicle along the street.
(j)
If the city manager or his designee fails to issue a parade permit within two business days after the filing of such application, the applicant shall have the right to appeal the denial or the right to appeal the imposition of any restriction imposed by the city manager or his designee to the mayor and council within five business days after the denial or notice. The mayor and council shall act upon the appeal at its next scheduled meeting. If the proposed starting time of the event is to occur before the next scheduled meeting of the mayor and council, the applicant may file an emergency appeal to the mayor, who shall act in place of and with all powers delegated to the mayor and council as the appellate body.
(k)
In the conduct of appeals, whether to the mayor and council or an emergency appeal, the review shall be limited to a determination as to whether or not each restriction imposed by the city manager or his designee and appealed from by the applicant is, in fact, required in order to protect from imminent or grave danger to public health or safety.
(l)
Any person aggrieved by the decision of the mayor and council (or an emergency appeal heard by the mayor) may seek prompt judicial review of such decision by petitioning the county superior court for a writ of mandamus, or by resorting to any other legal or equitable remedy provided by law.
(Code 1976, § 7-2017; Ord. No. 96-34, 7-25-1996)
Sec. 86-168. - Limitations on processions.
(a)
A permit shall not be required for any procession.
(b)
A procession unaccompanied by a police escort must comply with each and every traffic law and regulation of the city irrespective of the impact such compliance has upon the continuity or regularity of that procession. A nonescorted procession must be in continuous motion except for traffic lights and signals and must not obstruct the entrances or exits of any establishment of any kind or interfere with free passage upon any road, sidewalk, alley, driveway or other passageway. Should a pedestrian procession unlawfully cease its motion on a public street or sidewalk or other passageway, it ceases to be a procession subject to regulation under this section and becomes a public assembly subject to regulation under section 86-165.
(c)
A procession accompanied by a police escort may continue without break through stop signs, traffic lights, yield signs and the like so long as each vehicle maintains a close but safe distance with the immediately preceding vehicle and keeps its headlights on to indicate its participation in the procession.
(d)
A police escort will be provided to any procession for a lawful purpose upon application identical to requirements of the parade permit application form but filed at least 24 hours prior to the designated commencement of the parade upon the further condition that the city has a police officer available at the designated time for this service.
(e)
A pedestrian procession may not consist of more than two files nor more than six ranks; except that after six ranks of two files followed by a distance of 20 feet, another six ranks of two files may follow ad seriatim until the entire procession is accommodated. Unorderly pedestrian processions are prohibited as an interference with the rights of others to use the public sidewalks and streets. A pedestrian procession must comply with every traffic regulation and signal. Pedestrian procession participants may carry placards or signs not exceeding two feet in width and two feet in length nor carried in a fashion that tends to obstruct the fire movement of other members of the public using same sidewalk for movement or otherwise create a safety hazard.
(f)
Any member of a procession who violates a traffic regulation or ignores any traffic signals or creates an obstruction not permitted by this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-11.
(g)
Any procession that engages in loud and boisterous conduct or expression thereby loses its character as a procession within the meaning of this article and becomes a parade within the meaning of this article and subject to the rules and regulations applicable to a parade.
(h)
Any parade or procession involving the following conduct is unlawful and must disperse upon order of law enforcement officials:
(1)
The use of any fighting words by any participants; or
(2)
The possession of any deadly weapons as defined in section 86-163 by any participant.
(Code 1976, § 7-2018)
Sec. 86-169. - Interference with public agencies.
(a)
No person, singly or in concert with others, shall engage in picketing in such a manner as to obstruct or unreasonably interfere with free ingress or egress to and from any public premises, state property, county or municipal courthouse, city hall, public office building, jail or any other public building or facility owned by the United States, the state, the county, or the city, or with the transaction of public business or the administration of justice conducted in or on such place, or so as to obstruct or unreasonably interfere with the free use of public streets, sidewalks or other public ways adjacent or continuous to such place.
(b)
No person, with intent to interfere with, obstruct or impede the administration of justice, or with intent to influence any judge, juror, witness or officer of a court in the discharge of his duty, shall picket or parade in or near a building housing a court of the United States, the state, the county, or any other duly constituted governmental agency.
(Code 1976, § 7-2019)
Sec. 86-170. - Scope of article; construction.
(a)
The express enumeration of powers and rights in this article shall not be deemed a waiver of similar or related powers or rights inherent to municipal governments or delegated to municipal governments by the state.
(b)
No person shall hamper, obstruct or interfere with any parade, picket line or public assembly being conducted under the authority of a permit duly issued by the city manager.
(c)
This article shall be construed liberally in a manner that will effectuate its purposes so as to avoid the creation of any conflict with any valid state or federal law and so as to avoid regulation of any conduct or activity, the regulation of which has been preempted by a valid state or federal law.
(Code 1976, § 7-2020)

Sec. 86-201. - Method.
(a)
On all streets running north and south, the numbers shall start at 100 at Hill Avenue and increase north and south at the rate of 100 numbers to each block or space between streets and beginning with each consecutive 100 at each street intersection, such as northward from Hill Avenue, Central Avenue (200), Valley Street (300), and so on to the city limits; southward, Savannah Avenue (200), Crane Avenue (300) and so on to the city limits.
(b)
On all streets running east and west, the numbers shall start with 100 and increase at the rate of 100 to each block or space between each street and beginning each consecutive 100 at each street intersection such as westward from Patterson Street, Toombs Street (200), Stephens Street (300), and so on to the city limits.
(Code 1976, § 7-2031)
Sec. 86-202. - How numbers placed.
The odd numbers shall be placed on the east and south sides of all streets and the even numbers on the north and west sides.
(Code 1976, § 7-2032)
Sec. 86-203. - Renumbering.
If the street number assigned to or used or displayed upon any house or other property is or becomes erroneous due to resubdivision of lots, opening of new streets, or any other case, the director of inspections shall assign a new number to such house or property in accordance with the provisions in this article and shall notify the owner and occupant of the house or property of the error in the number used and the new number assigned.
(Code 1976, § 7-2033)
Sec. 86-204. - All houses to be numbered.
(a)
All persons owning or occupying houses and buildings erected within the city limits must place on the house or building the proper identifying street number of the house or building so that the number may be read from the street. Identifying numbers may be placed upon the house or building, on a mailbox, on the curb, or as a marker in the yard, or in the front of the house or building. If the building or house is over 50 feet from the road, the numbers shall be at the road and on the house or building.
(b)
It shall be the duty of the director of inspections to assign a proper street number to each house or building within the city in accordance with the provisions of this article; and it shall be unlawful and a violation of this article for any property owner, after being notified of the number assigned to his property by the director of inspections pursuant to this article, to have posted or displayed upon his property a number different from the number so assigned for the property.
(Code 1976, § 7-2034; Ord. No. 76-29, 10-6-1976)
