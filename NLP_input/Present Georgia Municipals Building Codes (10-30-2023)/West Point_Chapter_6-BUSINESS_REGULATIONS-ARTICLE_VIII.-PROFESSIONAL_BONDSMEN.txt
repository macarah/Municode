ARTICLE VIII. - PROFESSIONAL BONDSMEN[9]

Footnotes:
--- (9) ---
State Law reference— Persons deemed professional bondsmen, O.C.G.A. § 17-6-50; unlawful for elected official, officer of the court, law enforcement officer or attorney to engage in bail bond business, O.C.G.A. § 45-11-8.

Sec. 6-191. - Definition.
Any person who shall, for compensation, sign any bond for the appearance of any accused person in the municipal court, or who shall procure bond for such person, or who shall deposit collateral for such person in lieu of bond, shall, for the purposes of this article, be deemed a professional bondsman and engaged in business as such.
(Code 1967, § 15-101)
Sec. 6-192. - License required.
No person shall engage in the business of professional bondsman without first securing a license from this city to engage in such business.
(Code 1967, § 15-102)
Sec. 6-193. - Fee.
The business license fee for a professional bondsman licensed under the provisions of this article shall be in an amount determined by the city council from time to time and listed in the schedule of fees and charges maintained in the clerk-treasurer's office.
(Code 1967, § 15-102)
Sec. 6-194. - Qualifications of applicants; investigation.
It shall be the duty of the public safety committee to investigate, or have investigated, all applicants for a license. Such committee shall not recommend the issuance of a permit to a professional bondsman unless the application shall clearly show that the petitioner is the owner of real property in this county of a fair market value, in excess of encumbrances, equal to the total of all bonds upon which the petitioner may at that time appear as surety, plus the amount allowed as exemption under the homestead laws of the state; or other surety acceptable to the committee.
(Code 1967, § 15-103; Ord. of 6-8-92)
Sec. 6-195. - Permission required prior to visiting, or signing bonds for, prisoners.
No professional bondsman or agent of such bondsman shall be permitted to visit prisoners or to sign bonds for any person imprisoned unless such person shall have previously procured permission from the chief of police or his designated agent.
(Code 1967, § 15-104)
Sec. 6-196. - No loitering on police station grounds; request by prisoner for bondsman; use of rotation method when bondsman not named.
No professional bondsman shall be allowed to loiter in the police station or grounds, or to enter the same except when attending court or on business in connection with a case wherein he is surety on a bond, nor shall he be allowed to speak to any prisoner at the police station unless authorized so to do by the police officer on duty. The police officer shall not grant such authority until he has received from the prisoner a written request, naming the bondsman he wishes to see. In cases where no bondsman is named by the prisoner, the station lieutenant shall call them in rotation until a bondsman is secured.
(Code 1967, § 15-105)
Sec. 6-197. - Violations; penalties.
Any professional bondsman violating this article shall, on conviction in the municipal court, be punished as provided in section 1-14. In addition thereto, any bondsman convicted of any violation of this article shall automatically be barred from signing bonds in the city.
(Code 1967, § 15-106)
