ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 50-1. - Establishment of a City of Smyrna Fire Department.
For the protection of life and property, there is hereby established a fire department charged with: prevention, investigation, control, and extinguishment of fires, emergency medical service, emergency management, and other duties as specified in this and other referenced documents, state, federal, and local law and other traditional fire department responsibilities. The fire department shall be governed by the city mayor and council.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)
Sec. 50-2. - Fire department rules and regulations.
The fire department rules and regulations, a copy of which is on file in the office of the fire chief, are hereby adopted by reference.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)
Sec. 50-3. - Damaging fire hose.
It shall be unlawful for any person to damage, by running over with a vehicle or in any other manner, any fire hose belonging to the city. Upon conviction, such act or acts shall be a misdemeanor.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)
State Law reference— Crossing fire hose with vehicle, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-248.
Sec. 50-4. - Parking next to a fire hydrant.
Except when necessary to avoid conflict with other traffic, or in compliance with law or the directions of a police officer or official traffic-control device, no person shall stand or park a vehicle, whether occupied or not, within 15 feet of a fire hydrant, except momentarily to pick up or discharge a passenger or passengers.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-203(a)(2)(B).
Sec. 50-5. - Parking in fire lane.
Where official signs prohibit parking, except when necessary to avoid conflict with other traffic, or in compliance with law or the directions of a police officer or official traffic-control device, no person shall stand or park a vehicle, whether occupied or not, except momentarily to pick up or discharge a passenger or passenger or passengers.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-203(a)(3)(B).
Sec. 50-6. - Enforcement of city ordinances.
Members of the fire department, as designated by the fire chief may enforce city fire safety ordinances by issuing a citation returnable to municipal court of the City of Smyrna.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)
Sec. 50-7. - Outdoor burning.
No outside burning of rubbish/yard waste (as defined in section 50-27) is allowed at any time within the city limits.
(1)
Restrictions on outdoor burning do not apply to the following:
a.
Grilling or cooking using charcoal, clean wood, propane or natural gas.
b.
Burning clean wood after obtaining a residential burn permit. This burning must comply with the following provisions:
1.
The residential burn permit allows burning clean wood, as defined in section 50-27, only.
2.
Residential burn permits will only be issued from October 1 through March 30.
3.
Residential burn permits can only be utilized from 9:00 a.m. until one hour before the official time of sunset.
4.
Residential burn permits will not be permitted for: the burning of leaves, clean wood bigger than six inches in diameter, plastic, rubber, rubbish or trash.
5.
Residential burn permits can be obtained through the fire department on forms or through electronic media as determined by and provided by the city and must be obtained in advance.
6.
An applicant who is denied a residential burn permit may appeal that decision to the license and variance board within 30 days of the date of the denial.
7.
Any denial of a residential burn permit shall be in writing on forms or through electronic media as determined by and provided by the city.
c.
Burning of a recreational fire as defined in section 50-27. However, recreational fires within any property used for anything other than a residence for one or two families shall require a special use permit from the fire marshal's office under the following provisions:
1.
All special use permit applications must be filed with the fire marshal on forms provided by the city a minimum 14 days prior to the date of the proposed recreational fire.
2.
The applicant, as a condition precedent to the issuance of the permit, shall pay for the permit at the price fixed by the mayor and council.
3.
The fire marshal shall make an inspection of the proposed ignition site and assess any potential hazardous conditions. If the fire marshal determines that there are potential hazards at the proposed ignition site the application shall be denied. The fire marshal, within ten business days, shall issue a written decision.
4.
This decision may be appealed to the license and variance board within 30 days of the date of issuance.
5.
A permit under this chapter shall be valid for only the dates, times, and locations approved.
d.
Burning in a chimenea, outdoor fireplace, fire bowl or similar device.
e.
Fire department practice burns following state and local codes.
(2)
Safeguards/limitations for outdoor burning include the following:
a.
Burning of petroleum-based products, such as tires, plastics, and roof shingles, which produce black smoke, is prohibited.
b.
Burning of any material in a barrel is prohibited.
c.
Recreational fires shall not be located within 25 feet of any structure or other combustible material. Conditions which could cause a fire to spread within 25 feet of a structure shall be eliminated prior to ignition.
d.
Recreational fires referenced in this section must be constantly attended by an adult who must be outdoors within 100 feet of the fire and watching the fire at all times. A charged water hose that can reach the fire or minimum 4A fire extinguisher must be on hand and ready for use if needed for fire control/extinguishment. The nozzle of the charged water hose or fire extinguisher must be kept within 25 feet of the fire.
e.
Upon event completion, all fires must be extinguished to where no smoldering or hot coals remain.
(3)
The fire chief or designee shall be authorized to ban outdoor burning when:
a.
Atmospheric or local conditions make outdoor burning hazardous.
b.
It adversely interferes with the enjoyment of life or use of property.
c.
A resident is adversely affected by the smoke due to a medical condition.
(Ord. No. 2019-09 , 5-6-19; Ord. No. 2021-08 , 4-19-21; Ord. No. 2021-13 , 7-6-21)
Sec. 50-8. - New Year's fireworks.
On December 31, fireworks may be ignited from 10:00 a.m. up to and including an ending time of 1:00 a.m. on January 1. On all other days, it shall be unlawful to ignite fireworks between 12:00 midnight and 9:59 a.m.
(Ord. No. 2021-07 , 5-3-21)

(b)
All persons desiring to ignite consumer fireworks within any park, historic site, recreational area, or other property which is owned by or operated by, for, or under the custody and control of the city shall obtain written permission from the city administrator or his/her designee to do so and must also obtain a special use fireworks permit and be subject to all the rules and regulations of this chapter.
(c)
No person shall be authorized to use ignite or cause to be ignited fireworks on any day, other than on those days allowed by O.C.G.A. § 25-10-2(b)(3)(B)(ii) & (iii), from 9:00 a.m. to 11:59 p.m. until he/she obtains a special use permit from the fire marshal. All special use consumer fireworks permit applications shall be submitted electronically through the City's permit application system.
(d)
The applicant, as a condition precedent to the issuance of the permit, shall pay for the permit at the price fixed by the mayor and council.
(e)
Consumer fireworks shall be used in accordance with their affixed caution and warning labels.
(f)
Consumer fireworks shall only be used in accordance with all applicable local, state and federal laws.
(g)
A permit under this chapter shall be valid for only the dates, times and locations approved.
(h)
(1)
Every licensed distributor selling consumer fireworks pursuant to this Code section shall have within the retail display area for consumer fireworks at least one sign providing the following information:
a.
"PLEASE CHECK YOUR LOCAL ORDINANCES PRIOR TO USING OR IGNITING CONSUMER FIREWORKS";
b.
"PLEASE USE CONSUMER FIREWORKS IN ACCORDANCE WITH THEIR AFFIXED CAUTION AND WARNING LABELS"; and
c.
"PLEASE BE A GOOD NEIGHBOR AND BE MINDFUL THAT UNANNOUNCED IGNITION NEAR SOME MILITARY VETERANS AND OTHER PERSONS AND NEAR SOME PETS CAN BE TRAUMATIC".
(2)
Such signs shall be at least 22 inches by 28 inches in size, be printed in at least 40 point boldface type in a color contrasting from such sign's background color, and kept free from obstruction and in plain sight of customers.
(j)
Any person violating the provisions of this ordinance shall be punished as provided in section 1-8 of this Code.

Sec. 50-27. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article or the codes adopted herein, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Chief of the bureau of fire prevention or fire official means the fire marshal of the city.
Class I liquids means those products with a flash point at or below 100 degrees Fahrenheit.
Class II liquids means those products with a flash point at or above 100 degrees Fahrenheit.
Clean wood means natural wood which has not been painted, varnished or coated with a similar material; has not been pressure treated with preservatives; and does not contain resins or glues as in plywood or composite wood products.
Corporation counsel means the attorney for the city.
Judge means judge of the municipal court.
Municipality or authority having jurisdiction means the city.
Recreational fire means an outdoor fire burning in which only logs or clean wood are being burned where the fuel is not contained in an incinerator, outdoor fireplace, barbeque grill or barbeque pit and has a total fuel area of three feet or less in diameter and two feet or less in height for pleasure, religious, ceremonial, cooking, warmth or similar purposes, and which is not used to dispose of rubbish or yard waste.
Rubbish means any waste material other than charcoal or clean wood that includes but is not limited to plastic products, styrofoam, fiberglass, recycling wire (burning the wire casing to access the metal), tires, painted/varnished wood, clothing, furniture, mattresses, boxes and paper.
Yard waste means yard debris: leaves, kudzu, pine straw, dry brush and limbs that have fallen or been cut from growth on one's own property.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97; Ord. No. 2016-14 , § 2, 6-6-16)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 50-28. - Establishment and duties of bureau of fire prevention.
(a)
The fire prevention code and the state minimum fire safety standards shall be enforced by the bureau of fire prevention in the fire department of the city, which is hereby established and which shall be operated under the supervision of the chief of the fire department.
(b)
The bureau of fire prevention shall be formed as a division of the fire department. The personnel of the bureau shall consist of a fire marshal and as many fire investigators or fire inspectors, clerks and other personnel as may be authorized.
(c)
The fire marshal and the fire investigators or inspectors shall be designated in writing by the fire chief.
(d)
The chief of the fire department may detail as fire investigators or fire inspectors such members of the fire department as shall, from time to time, be necessary.
(e)
In the absence of the chief of the fire department, the assistant chief of the department shall have full authority to act in his/her behalf.
(f)
A report of the bureau of fire prevention shall be made annually and transmitted to the fire chief for distribution to the mayor and council at a regularly scheduled meeting on or before February 15 each year; it shall contain all proceedings under the fire prevention code, with such statistics as the chief of the fire department may wish to include therein.
(g)
The fire marshal shall recommend to the chief of the fire department any amendments to the codes, which in his/her judgment shall be desirable, for passage to the city administrator to present to the governing body for adoption.
(h)
The service of orders for the correction of violations of this code may be made upon the owner, occupant or such other person responsible for the conditions, either by delivering the orders to such person or by delivering the orders to and leaving it with any person in charge of the premises, or by affixing a copy of such orders in a conspicuous place on the door to the entrance of the premises. If buildings or other premises are owned by one person and occupied by another under lease or otherwise, the orders issued in connection with enforcing this code shall apply to the owner and occupant.
(i)
The bureau of fire prevention shall investigate the causes and circumstances of every fire occurring in the city which is of suspicious nature or which involves loss of life or injury to any person. Such investigations shall begin immediately upon the occurrence of such fire, and, if it appears that such fire is of criminal origin, the investigator shall immediately take charge of any physical evidence and begin an investigation.
(j)
The fire chief, fire marshal, or fire inspectors/investigators permanently assigned to the division of the bureau of fire prevention shall have the power of arrest in connection with performance of their duties. If the fire chief, assistant fire chief, fire marshal, and inspectors/investigators are recognized as a certified peace officer, they shall have the full power of arrest.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97; Ord. No. 2016-14 , § 3, 6-6-16)
Cross reference— Administration, ch. 2.
Sec. 50-29. - Establishment of limits of districts in which storage of flammable liquids in outside aboveground tanks is to be prohibited.
(a)
The storage of prohibited type I and type II liquids in outside aboveground tanks may be permitted for the purpose of storage of fuel for standby electric generators. The tanks shall be vaulted as per NFPA 30 and plans for the installations of the tank and piping shall be reviewed by the fire marshal before a permit will be issued. All other aboveground storage tanks for type I and type II liquids within the fire district of the City of Smyrna are prohibited, with the exception that a propane gas tank not exceeding 500 gallons liquid shall be installed and maintained in strict accordance with the current National Fire Protection Code, standard 58.
(b)
The limits referred to in section 906.1 of the standard fire prevention code, in which new bulk plants for flammable or combustible liquids are prohibited, are hereby established as the fire district of the city.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)

Sec. 50-33. - Establishment of fire lanes on private property devoted to public use.
(a)
The fire lanes, as established by the chief of the fire department and the head of the bureau of fire prevention or the designees in the bureau of fire prevention, shall be those lanes established and so designated, where the parking of motor vehicles or other obstructions may interfere with the ingress and egress of the fire department vehicles necessary for the protection of persons and property on private property devoted to public use, such as shopping centers, apartment complexes, schools, theaters, hospitals, churches and other similar commercial establishments. The width of all fire lanes shall be a minimum of 20 feet.
(b)
Additional requirements are as follows:
(1)
Fire lanes shall be marked with red or other distinctive colors as approved by the fire marshal. The approved color shall be applied to the curb or ground markings where no curb exists. Fire lanes shall be posted with signs stating "No Parking Fire Lane." (The international no parking symbol and the words Fire Lane is acceptable.)
(2)
It shall be the owner's responsibility to maintain the painted curbing and the visibility of the signs.
(3)
Signs shall be 12 inches by 18 inches or 24 inches by 30 inches or 18 inches by 24 inches. (Maximum of 50 feet between signs.) Where sign installation is deemed impractical by the fire marshal, painting "NO PARKING FIRE LANE" in white minimum four inch block letters along the red painted curb face will be allowed at the above required distances.
(4)
Fire lanes shall be able to withstand live loads of fire apparatus and have a minimum height clearance of 13 feet and six inches.
(5)
An approved turnaround for fire apparatus shall be provided where an access road is a dead end and is in excess of 150 feet in length. The turnaround shall have a minimum centerline radius of 50 feet.
(6)
It shall be unlawful and punishable as a misdemeanor to denote a fire lane that has not been so designated as described in this section.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97; Ord. No. 2005-18, 6-6-05; Ord. No. 2012-17, 8-6-12 ; Ord. No. 2016-14 , § 4, 6-6-16)
Cross reference— Traffic and vehicles, ch. 98.
Sec. 50-34. - Heating.
All garage heating appliances fired with gas, liquid or solid fuel, except unit heaters installed not less than eight feet above the floor level, shall be located in a room separate from other parts of the garage by approved noncombustible material having a fire resistance of not less than two hours. Such separate room shall not be used for any other purpose and shall have no direct access from the garage or repair area. Openings in the wall separating the heater room from the garage storage and repair areas shall be restricted to those necessary for heating pipes and ducts, and such ducts shall be protected with approved fire dampers. All air for combustion purposes entering such separated room shall be from outside the building. Where gasoline dispensing equipment is located within 25 feet from the entrance to such heater room, the floor of such room shall not be below the outside grade level.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)

Sec. 50-37. - Multifamily buildings.
(a)
The fire prevention code, Firewall and Firestop Requirements for Apartments, Condominiums from One to Three Stories, is amended as follows:
(1)
Firewall definition. A firewall shall be defined as self-supporting from the foundation and shall extend through, and 36 inches above, combustible roofs. Firewalls need not extend through fire-resistive roof decks. Firewalls shall be 12 inches thick and 75 percent solid or of UL-approved blocks with a four-hour rating. A combustible roof shall not project around the end of the firewall. There shall be no unprotected openings in firewalls.
(2)
Hotels, motels or apartments less than 75 feet in height, which are fully provided with a total automatic sprinkler system as per National Fire Protection Association Standard 13, Standards 13D and 13R, or the Smyrna Residential Sprinkler Code, may not be required to contain firewalls as specified in paragraph (1) above.
(3)
Requirements for Early Fire Detection Systems in all Multifamily Occupancies (Duplex, Triplex, Quadruplex, Apartments, Condominium, Town Home or by Other Names), is amended to read as follows:
a.
There shall be early fire detection systems in all sections of the buildings, as required by the bureau of fire prevention.
b.
All multifamily residential occupancies shall be sprinkled with an approved automatic sprinkler system.
c.
One ABC extinguisher (4A 60BC rating) shall be provided for every 3,000 square feet of building, or one ABC extinguisher (4A 60BC rating) for every four living units, or one ABC extinguisher meeting the state minimum standard for fire extinguisher classification inside each occupied living unit.
(4)
Any building three stories or less in height with no floor below the level of exit discharge or, in case there is such a floor, with the street floor construction of at least one-hour fire resistance, may have a single exit.
(5)
Control of flame spread. The flame spread on carpet, wall covering, paneling or wood in multifamily dwellings shall be as follows:
a.
Vertical exits and corridors and breezeways shall have class B (26-75) flame spread rating.
b.
The interior of the units shall be class C flame spread rating (76-200).
c.
Control of flame spread through openings in floors.
1.
Any openings through floors from one unit to another for piping or plumbing must be sealed with lightweight concrete or other permanently attached noncombustible material.
2.
Any opening through any floor for any purpose shall be sealed in a like manner.
3.
The double wall furnace vent must be sealed tight at each floor level and at the ceiling level of the top unit. The double wall furnace vent is not required to be enclosed in the attic space; proper clearance must be maintained.
4.
If a combustion air vent pipe is required for combustion air to the furnace, it shall extend up through the floor-ceiling assembly and into the attic 18 inches above the ceiling, with upper fire stopping at each floor level.
5.
Each attic space shall have an attic access provided. This door cannot be in a closet, unless it is a walk-in closet.
6.
If an opening is made in a smoke-stop wall, it must be self-closing; a screen door type spring is acceptable.
(6)
Chimney chases.
a.
This section shall apply to all chimney chases for single-family, one- and two-family and multifamily dwellings and other applicable building as designated by the city building inspector or the city fire marshal.
b.
All metal chimney flues shall be of double wall construction, U.L. listed, and shall be installed according to the manufacturer's recommendations.
(7)
When a structure is equipped with a sprinkler system meeting the requirements of the National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) 13D, 13R or the City of Smyrna Residential Sprinkler Code, the following exceptions to the building code shall be allowed:
a.
Firewall requirements:
1.
Buildings up to 10,500 square feet per floor, no firewall is required.
2.
Buildings over 10,500 square feet per floor, a four-hour firewall extending up to 36 inches above the roof surface and out flush with the roof overhang is required for every 10,500 square feet.
3.
Standard one-half-inch gypsum wallboard may be substituted for the five-eighths-inch type X gypsum wallboard relating to one-hour tenant separation walls.
4.
Standard one-half-inch gypsum wallboard may be substituted for the five-eighths-inch type X gypsum wallboard, and one-half-inch plywood may be substituted for three-quarter-inch plywood relating to one-hour, floor-ceiling assemblies.
5.
Fee simple townhouses may have one-hour rated party walls in lieu of two-hour rated party walls.
6.
Attic smoke stop at 3,000 square feet will be allowed in lieu of smoke stops between every unit.
7.
Exit travel may be extended to a maximum of 35 feet in lieu of the existing 20 feet.
8.
Tenants' egress may pass by other tenants' doors and windows, without those doors and windows being rated.
9.
Interior finish shall be class C or better.
10.
One-hour rating of walls and doors of hazardous areas will not be required.
11.
Unless otherwise specified within this Code, all other requirements of applicable codes of the City of Smyrna shall be complied with.
12.
Certification of flow must be acceptable to the authority having jurisdiction.
13.
In the interest of life safety, other exceptions may be allowed when warranted by the bureau of fire prevention.
b.
This section applies to multifamily residential structures with a maximum of three stories in height, except duplex and freestanding single-family residences which shall have a separation distance of at least 15 feet between structures.
1.
A story is defined as "that portion of a building included between the upper surface of the floor and the upper surface of the floor or roof next above."
2.
Commercial buildings that are four stories or less that are not required to be sprinkled by other codes.
3.
In structures described in paragraph b. above, a combination fire protection sprinkler system may be allowed. A combination fire protection system, for purposes of this code, means that the sprinkler system and the domestic water system utilize the same (common) service water supply pipe to the premises. Such system shall comply with the following:
a.
Any calculations of pipe size shall be based on a 24-hour chart and must be approved by the authority having jurisdiction prior to permitting the sprinkler system.
b.
No antifreeze solution or any other additive shall be allowed in any combination sprinkler system.
c.
When a dead-end system is used, the sprinkler system shall be isolated from the domestic water system by not less than a dual check valve backflow preventer that conforms to ASSE standard 1024 and provides the flow requirements of the system without exceeding the allowable pressure drop of the standard or the system, whichever is less. A union and full-port shutoff valve, acceptable to the administrative authority, shall be installed on both sides of the backflow preventer to facilitate removal for test and repair. If sprinkler system pressure exceeds 120 psi, a pressure reducing valve must be used.
d.
Exception. In low pressure areas only, where residual pressure or flow GPMs do not meet minimum requirements, one head must flow a minimum of 18 GPMs with a 41 psi residual; two heads must flow a minimum of 30 GPMs with a 30 psi residual, unless otherwise UL listed, a circulating system may be used. The terminal end of the sprinkler supply shall be tied directly into the supply to a regularly used water closet or other approved fixture. Branch lines shall not exceed 25 feet on any circulating system. The backflow preventer requirement is waived on such systems.
e.
When the dual check and shutoff valves are installed outside the building, they shall not be buried in earth but shall be installed in such as meter boxes and protected from freezing, at a location acceptable to the administrative authority. The top of the protective housing shall extend one-half-inch above the finished landscaping or be installed in an area protected from weather conditions, subject to approval of the bureau of fire prevention, Smyrna fire department.
f.
All shutoffs in the sprinkler system shall be secured with lock and key in the open position. A sign on valves should read "Fire Department should be notified when valves are turned off and when turned back on." All shutoffs shall be readily accessible at all times.
g.
A supply line to the sprinkler system shall be tapped off the water service line at a point that would be downstream (customer side of meter) of any backflow preventer the water purveyor might subsequently require in the service line to contain the domestic system from the public mains.
h.
All sprinkler heads used shall be UL listed, fast acting domestic residential sprinkler heads.
i.
The spacing of pendant heads shall be a maximum of eight feet from any wall, unless UL listed for greater coverage.
j.
The spacing of side wall heads shall be in accordance with UL listing.
k.
Sprinkler heads are not required in closets, not over 12 square feet or attics. All other areas require sprinkler heads, including drive-in garages and basements, finished or unfinished. Exception: Must meet hazardous area hourly fire rating for drive-in garages.
l.
All mechanical rooms (furnace, water heater) shall have 135- to 165-degree commercial head with maximum three-eighths-inch orifice.
m.
Dropped escutcheons shall be used if sprinkler head is obstructed by light fixtures or beams. Builder may use additional heads for areas where obstructions block spray pattern.
n.
When ceiling fans are used, sprinkler heads shall be located so fan escutcheon does not interfere with the pattern. Relocation of head shall still conform to spacing requirements. If sprinkler head location exceeds spacing requirements, additional heads shall be used.
o.
All spacing of sprinkler heads in relation to obstructions, such as fireplaces and heaters, shall be to sprinkler head manufacturers' requirements.
p.
Minimum flow must be established, as approved by the office of the Smyrna fire marshal.
q.
All sprinkler piping in unheated areas shall be protected from freezing.
r.
An accepted water flow switch with bell is required. Both shall be accessible for checking and repair.
s.
Each system shall have an accessible test drain valve.
t.
If sprinkler system is designed for more than one living unit and separate electric service is used for each unit, the alarm switch with bell shall be on "house electric meter."
u.
All condominiums shall have the following wording in the recorded declaration of covenants, and a copy of the document shall be provided to the Smyrna fire marshal and the Smyrna chief building inspector:
"Sprinkler system. If any unit should be equipped with a sprinkler system, nothing shall be hung from the sprinkler heads comprising a part of the system, nor shall any such sprinkler heads be painted, covered or otherwise changed, tampered with or altered.
"Prior to any alteration, amendment, modification or change thereof, he/she/they will submit such alteration, amendment, modification or change to the Smyrna fire marshal for his/her approval and agrees to comply with all domestic sprinkler requirements of said fire marshal."
v.
The installer shall be a state licensed fire protection sprinkler company.
w.
A minimum of one 1½-inch fire department connector shall be required per building; connector shall be located on front of street side of building.
4.
The Smyrna fire department shall inspect all sprinkler systems as set forth in section 50-347(h) of the city Code. Inspection shall be made annually or as often as deemed necessary by the fire marshal.

Sec. 50-38. - Occupancy and use permits.
Buildings shall be occupied or utilized only after first obtaining an occupancy and use permit from the fire department, certifying that all provisions of the codes have been met. Such buildings shall include those newly constructed or remodeled, or additions thereto, change of occupancy (usage) excluding only single-family residential dwellings. Under special conditions a temporary certificate of occupancy may be issued for a period not to exceed 90 days.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)
Sec. 50-39. - Modifications of code requirements.
The fire marshal shall have the power to modify any of the provisions of the codes as they apply to a particular project, and upon application in writing by the owner or lessee, or his/her duly authorized agent, when there are practical difficulties in the way of carrying out the strict letter of the codes, provided that the spirit of the codes shall be observed, public safety secured and substantial justice done. The particulars of such modification when granted or allowed and the decision thereof shall be entered upon the permanent records of the department, and a signed copy shall be furnished to the applicant, the fire chief, and members of the fire committee.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97; Ord. No. 2016-14 , § 8, 6-6-16)
Sec. 50-40. - New materials; processes or occupancies which may require permits.
The chief of the fire department and the fire marshal shall act as a committee to determine and specify, after giving affected persons an opportunity to be heard, any new materials, processes or occupancies which shall require permits, in addition to those enumerated in the fire codes. The fire marshal shall post such list in a conspicuous place in his/her office and distribute copies thereof to interested persons.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97; Ord. No. 2016-14 , § 9, 6-6-16)
Sec. 50-41. - Appeals.
(a)
The fire prevention code is amended by deleting section A105.
(b)
Whenever the fire marshal shall disapprove an application or refuse to grant a permit applied for, or when it is claimed that the provisions of the codes do not apply or that the true intent and meaning of the codes have been misconstrued or wrongly interpreted, the applicant may appeal in writing from the decision of the fire marshal to the chief of the fire department within seven days from the date of the decision appealed.
(c)
If the applicant is dissatisfied with the decision of the chief of the fire department, further appeal in writing may be made to the mayor and council within seven days from the date of the decision appealed.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97; Ord. No. 2016-14 , § 10, 6-6-16)
Sec. 50-42. - Penalties.
(a)
Any person who shall violate any of the provisions of the codes hereby adopted or fail to comply herewith, or who shall violate or fail to comply with any order made pursuant to the provisions of such codes, or who shall build in violation of any detailed statement of specifications or plans submitted and approved pursuant to the provisions of such codes, or any certificate or permit issued pursuant to the provisions of such codes and from which no appeal has been taken, or who shall fail to comply with such an order as affirmed or modified by the chief of the fire department or by a court of competent jurisdiction within the time fixed in this section, shall severally for each such violation and noncompliance respectively be guilty of a misdemeanor, punishable by a fine as provided in section 1-8. The imposition of one penalty for any violation shall not excuse the violation or permit it to continue, and all such persons shall be required to correct or remedy such violations or defects within a reasonable time. When not otherwise specified, each ten days that prohibited conditions are maintained shall constitute a separate offense.
(b)
The application of the penalty in subsection (a) of this section shall not be held to prevent the enforced removal of prohibited conditions.

Sec. 50-61. - Penalties.
(a)
Any violation of any provision of this article found by the bureau of fire prevention shall be cause for suspension or revocation of the self-service permit.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to permit the dispensing of class 1 liquids by an individual other than the owner, operator or employee without a self-service permit. Violation is punishable as provided in section 1-8. Each incident shall be deemed a new offense.

Sec. 50-62. - Enforcement of article; report of violations.
It shall be the duty of the bureau of fire prevention to enforce this article. Charges of violations shall be reported to the mayor and council, and the chief of the fire department.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)
Sec. 50-63. - Permit required; procedure for obtaining.
Self-service motor vehicle fuel service stations will be permitted only with specific approval of, and the issuance of a permit by, the fire marshal. Procedure for obtaining a self-service permit shall be that any person desiring to operate a self-service service station shall make application to the fire marshal in writing on a form provided by the city. The application shall be accompanied with plans of the proposed station, whether a new station or an existing station is being modified. Plans shall be submitted as follows:
(1)
Plans for a new or a modified existing service station to provide for self-service shall be of scale and shall include a general arrangement layout, storage tank locations and installation details, control devices or arrangements for self-service operations, piping and vent piping details, dispenser arrangements and details, emergency control arrangements, location of fire extinguishers or other firefighting arrangements and such other details and specifications as may be necessary to ensure the safe installation and safe operation when completed.
(2)
Plans shall be submitted in triplicate, one set to be retained by the fire marshal, one set to be retained by the building inspector and the other returned to the applicant, with approval or disapproval indicated thereon.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)
Sec. 50-64. - Approval of plans required prior to construction.
Construction of new stations or modifications to existing stations shall not commence until plans have been approved and returned to the applicant.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)
Sec. 50-65. - Reserved.
Sec. 50-66. - Permit nontransferable; revocation for cause.
A permit for self-service operation is not transferable, and it may be revoked for cause. Except where amended by the city, all sections of N.F.P.A. chapter 30 shall be required to be enforced by the operators of public self-service stations.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)
Secs. 50-67—50-69. - Reserved.
Sec. 50-70. - Emergency power cutoff.
Clearly identified and easily accessible switches shall be installed not more than 100 feet from dispensers that will shut off the power to all dispensing devices in the event of an emergency. It must cut off power to lighting and any other low voltage circuits.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)
Sec. 50-71. - Key lock or automatic remote control pumping dispensers required.
(a)
All self-service service stations must have either automatic remote control or key lock pumping dispensers.
(b)
Automatic remote control systems must have a two-way intercom from the office or island house to each pump dispensing island.
(c)
Key lock pumping dispensers must not be cleared for dispensing until a customer is ready to use them.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)
Sec. 50-72. - Attendants—Required to be present.
No person shall operate a self-service motor fuel dispensing pump, unless an attendant is present on the premises. A cashier housed inside a building is classified as an attendant.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)
Sec. 50-73. - Same—Qualifications and duties.
(a)
The attendant shall be at least 18 years of age, experienced with and physically capable of performing the required duties, careful, capable and able to read and write the English language. He/She shall be familiar with the provisions of this article.
(b)
The attendant shall supervise, observe and control the dispensing of class 1 liquid while the liquid is being dispensed. It shall be the responsibility of the attendant to prevent the dispensing of class 1 liquid into portable containers, unless they are metal and properly labeled in accordance with state law, and to immediately handle accidental spills and fire extinguishers, if needed.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)
Sec. 50-74. - View of dispensing area by automatic console operator.
The automatic console operator must have full view of the fuel dispensing area.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)
Sec. 50-75. - Minimum age of pump operator.
It shall be unlawful for any person under the age of 16 years to operate any self-service motor fuel dispensing pump.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)
Sec. 50-76. - Intoxicated persons not to dispense liquids.
Persons under the influence of intoxicants, narcotics or drugs shall not be permitted to dispense flammable or combustible liquids.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)
Sec. 50-77. - Private dispensing units exempted.
This article does not apply to motor fuel dispensing units which are not open to the public.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)
Sec. 50-78. - Adoption of state fire protection and safety standards.
As required by O.C.G.A. § 25-2-12, the state fire protection and safety standards, including accessibility are adopted and shall be enforced by the bureau of fire prevention.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)
Sec. 50-79. - Electrical service to single-family dwellings.
All new single-family dwellings shall be required to provide an exterior disconnect for electrical service. Existing dwellings shall also be required to up-grade to an exterior disconnect when electric entrance equipment is replaced.
(Ord. No. 97-14, 11-17-97)

The owner of the property where any device, equipment, alarm, system and any other feature is required for compliance of the provisions of chapter 50 or is otherwise installed on the property shall cause periodic maintenance, testing and inspections of all such devices, equipment, alarms, systems and features in accordance with this Code and applicable adopted standards at specified intervals. If following inspection and testing, any device, equipment, alarm, system and any other feature does not meet manufacturer's specifications or fails to perform as intended, then it shall be repaired or replaced in accordance with subsection (3) below.

Fire alarm system, NFPA 72.

Fire doors, NFPA 80.
•
Fire pumps, NFPA 25.

Foam system (including five-year test), NFPA 25.
•
Private hydrant system, NFPA 25.

Smoke control system, NFPA 90 and 92B.

Special suppression system, NFPA 12 and 12A.

Spray booth, NFPA 33.

Standpipe (including five-year), NFPA 25.

Reporting of inspections and tests.

Records of all installations, inspections, tests and maintenance required by chapter 50 shall be provided electronically to the city's designated electronic reporting system. A record of any device, equipment, alarm and/or system inspection, test and/or maintenance required by the state minimum fire safety standards adopted in the rules and regulations promulgated pursuant to O.C.G.A. tit. 25 and the referenced National Fire Protection Association Codes and Standards, shall be maintained for not less than three years, or a different period of time where specified and shall be copied to the fire department and its contractors pursuant to either the provisions of this Code or upon the request of the fire code official.

Repairs.

If any device, equipment, alarm, system and any other feature fails its inspection or operational test, within 30 days of such inspection or test, the property owner shall make all necessary repairs to make the item fully operational. If repair is not possible, the item must be replaced with devices, equipment, alarms, systems and features in accordance with this chapter and applicable referenced standards. Completed records shall be submitted no later than 15 days following the applicable maintenance, repair or replacement. The submitted report shall contain all information required by the city's designated electronic reporting system. The fee for any report filed using the electronic reporting system shall be $15.00.
b.
Additional inspections and testing may be required of the repaired or replaced device, equipment, alarm, system or other feature upon the request of the fire code official.
