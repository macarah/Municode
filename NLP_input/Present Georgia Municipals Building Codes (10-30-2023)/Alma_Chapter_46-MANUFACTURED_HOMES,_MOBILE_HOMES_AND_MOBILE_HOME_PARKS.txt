Chapter 46 - MANUFACTURED HOMES, MOBILE HOMES AND MOBILE HOME PARKS[1]
Footnotes:

State Law reference— Standards and requirements for installation, and licensing of manufactured homes and mobile homes, O.C.G.A. § 8-2-110 et seq.; the Uniform Standards Code for Manufactured Homes Act, O.C.G.A. § 8-2-130 et seq.

ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 46-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Accessory structure means any structural addition to the mobile home which includes awnings, cabanas, carports, Florida rooms, porches, ramadas, storage cabinets and similar appurtenant structures.
Building means a roofed structure erected for permanent use.
Common area means any area or space designed for joint use of tenants occupying a mobile home park.
Density means the number of mobile homes or mobile home stands per gross acre.
Driveway means a minor private way used by vehicles and pedestrians on a mobile home lot or stand or for common access to a small group of lots or common facilities.
Easement means a vested or acquired right to use land, other than as a tenant, for a specific purpose; such right being held by someone other than the owner who holds title to the land.
Enforcing agency means the code enforcement department.
Lot line means a line bounding the lot as shown on the accepted plat plan.
Manufactured home means a structure, transportable in one or more sections, which, in the traveling mode, is eight body feet or more in width or 40 body feet or more in length or, when erected on site, is 320 or more square feet and which is built on a permanent chassis and designed to be used as a dwelling with or without a permanent foundation when connected to the required utilities and includes the plumbing, heating, air conditioning, and electrical systems contained therein; except that such term shall include any structure which meets all the requirements of this definition, except the size requirements and with respect to which the manufacturer voluntarily files a certification required by the secretary of housing and urban development and complies with the standards established under the National Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards Act of 1974, 42 USC 5401 et seq.
Mobile home means a structure, transportable in one or more sections, which, in the traveling mode, is eight body feet or more in width or 40 body feet or more in length or, when erected on site, is 320 or more square feet and which is built on a permanent chassis and designed to be used as a dwelling with or without a permanent foundation when connected to the required utilities and includes the plumbing, heating, air-conditioning, and electrical systems contained therein and manufactured prior to June 15, 1976.
Mobile home lot means a parcel of land for the placement of a single mobile home and the exclusive use of its occupants.
Mobile home park means a parcel of land where one or more mobile homes can be and are intended to be parked, designed or intended to be used as long-term living facilities for one or more families.
Mobile home stand means the part of an individual lot which has been reserved for the placement of the mobile home, appurtenant structures or additions.
Occupied area means the area of an individual mobile home lot which has been covered by a mobile home and its accessory structures.
Park management means the person who owns or has charge, care or control of the mobile home development.
Permit means a written document issued by the code enforcement department allowing a person to locate a mobile home or to construct, alter or extend a mobile home development under the provisions of this chapter.
Private street means a private way which affords principal means of access to an abutting individual mobile home stand and auxiliary buildings.
Public street means a public way which affords principal means of access to abutting properties.
Right-of-way means the area, either public or private, over which the right of passage exists.
Service building means a structure housing sanitary, operational, office, recreational, maintenance and other facilities built to conform to required standards.
Sewer connection means pipes, fittings and appurtenances from the drain outlet of the mobile home to the inlet of the corresponding sewer system serving the mobile home park.
Sewer riser pipe means that portion of the sewer lateral which extends vertically to the ground elevation and terminates at a designated point at each mobile home stand.
Skirt means an opaque or semi-opaque structure, fence or vegetation which extends from the ground to the base of the mobile home and completely obscures the superstructure of the mobile home.
Structure includes the term "building."
Used includes "arranged," "designed," "constructed," "altered," "converted," "rented," "leased" and "intended to be used."
Water connection means all pipes, fittings and appurtenances from the water riser pipe to the water inlet pipe of the distribution system within the mobile home.
Water riser pipe means the water riser pipe is that portion of the water service pipe which extends vertically to the ground elevation and terminates at a designated point at each mobile home stand.
Water service pipe means the water service pipe consists of all pipes, fittings, valves and appurtenances from the water main of the park distribution system to the water outlet of the distribution system within the mobile home.
(Code 1969, § 16-1; Code 1981, § 13-1; Ord. of 7-2-1973, §§ 211, 212)
Sec. 46-2. - Purpose.
This chapter is adopted in order to promote the health, safety, morals and general welfare of the inhabitants of the city by regulating the occupancy and maintenance of mobile homes and mobile home parks.
(Code 1981, § 13-2; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 112)
Sec. 46-3. - Scope.
This chapter:
(1)
Establishes minimum standards for mobile homes and mobile home parks;
(2)
Establishes requirements for the design, construction, alteration, extension and maintenance of mobile homes and mobile home parks;
(3)
Authorizes the inspection of mobile homes and mobile home parks; and
(4)
Limits the use of mobile homes and mobile home parks.
(Code 1981, § 13-3; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 113)
Sec. 46-4. - Most restrictive provision prevails.
In any case where a provision of any other section of this Code or other ordinance or code of the city is in conflict with this chapter, the provision which establishes the higher standards shall prevail.
(Code 1981, § 13-4; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 612)
Sec. 46-5. - Modification.
The standards and requirements of this chapter may be modified by the planning commission in the case of a plan or program of such a nature which in the judgment of the planning commission provides adequate public spaces and improvements for the circulation, recreation, light, air and service needs of the tract when fully developed and populated, and which also provides such covenants or other legal provisions or as will ensure conformity to an achievement of the comprehensive plan.
(Code 1981, § 13-5; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 614)
Sec. 46-6. - Emergencies.
Whenever the code enforcement officer finds that an emergency exists which requires immediate action to protect the public health, including violations of any of the applicable rules and regulations of the city, county or state health authorities, such officer may without notice or hearing issue an order reciting the existence of such an emergency and requiring that such action be taken as such officer may deem necessary to meet the emergency including the suspension of the permit. Notwithstanding any other provisions of this chapter, such order shall be effective immediately. Any person to whom such an order is directed shall comply therewith immediately, but upon petition to the board of zoning appeals, such person shall be afforded a hearing as soon as possible.
(Code 1981, § 13-6; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 419)
Sec. 46-7. - Park inspection; enforcement.
(a)
The code enforcement is authorized and directed to make such inspections as are necessary to determine satisfactory compliance with this chapter.
(b)
The code enforcement department shall have the power to enter at reasonable times upon any private or public property for the purpose of inspecting and investigating conditions relating to enforcement of this chapter.
(c)
The department may request inspections by and verifications of compliance from the public health authorities relating to any of the rules and regulations of state and/or local health authorities. Representatives of the public health authorities shall have the same powers of entry and inspection as provided for the code enforcement department.
(d)
The health authorities, upon finding by inspection existing violations of requirements relating to the protection of health and/or safety of residents of the mobile home parks or others, shall notify the code enforcement department of such violations and requirements for corrections. The department shall then proceed with enforcement under the provisions of appropriate sections of this chapter.
(Code 1981, § 13-7; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 414)
Sec. 46-8. - Appeals.
(a)
Petition. Any person affected by any notice which has been issued in connection with the enforcement of any provision of this chapter, or of any regulation adopted pursuant thereto, may request and shall be granted a hearing on the matter before the board of zoning appeals, provided that such person shall file in the office of the code enforcement department a written statement of the grounds for such request. The filing of the request for a hearing shall operate as a stay of the notice and the suspension except in the case of an order issued under section 46-76. Upon receipt of such request, the board of zoning appeals shall set a time and place for such hearing and shall give the petitioner written notice thereof.
(b)
Hearing. At such hearing, the petitioner shall be given an opportunity to be heard and to show why such notice should be modified or withdrawn.
(c)
Order of board of zoning appeals. After such hearing the board of zoning appeals shall make findings as to compliance with the provisions of this chapter and regulations issued thereunder and shall issue an order sustaining, modifying or withdrawing the notice. Upon failure to comply with any order sustaining or modifying a notice, the permit of the mobile home park affected by the order shall be revoked.
(Code 1981, § 13-8; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 418)
Sec. 46-9. - Payment of annual park license fees and taxes.
All owners and/or operators of mobile home parks shall be required to pay the annual business license tax, and the mobile home park property annual ad valorem tax, excluding mobile home units and their equipment.
(Code 1969, § 16-6; Code 1981, § 13-9; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 415)
Sec. 46-10. - Locating mobile home in city—Compliance with restrictions; permit required.
Regardless of location, any mobile home located within the limits of the city shall not be so located, relocated, occupied or otherwise used until and unless the same has been inspected by the code enforcement officer and found in compliance with zoning and occupancy regulations of city building codes, and a city mobile home permit issued therefor.
(Code 1969, § 16-4; Code 1981, § 13-10; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 314)
Sec. 46-11. - Same—Application for permit; certificate of occupancy; travel trailers excepted.
(a)
Prior to locating a mobile home within the legal limits of the city:
(1)
An application shall be filed with the code enforcement officer, showing the type, size, design, utility equipment used therein, and the location proposed to situate such mobile homes.
(2)
The applicant shall pay the $100.00 house moving fee in advance with the application.
(3)
The applicant shall submit the application to the city council and obtain approval of a permit to locate same.
(4)
The applicant shall obtain a certificate of occupancy from the code enforcement officer, showing that all legal requirements have been met before occupying same.
(5)
The mobile home shall be inspected before moving it into the city limits.
(b)
Section 46-10 and this section shall not apply to travel trailers for overnight stops, or short stays not exceeding ten days.
(Code 1969, § 16-2; Code 1981, § 13-11; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 311)
Sec. 46-12. - Same—Mobile home park location required; exceptions, temporary permit.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to locate a mobile home in the city except within a lawfully approved and licensed mobile home park; provided, however, that mobile homes and/or trailers already located within the city under a previous city permit shall be permitted to continue in that location according to the terms of the existing permit approved by the city, for a period of one year, and; provided, further, that in cases of genuine hardship where good and sufficient justification is shown, the city council may authorize the issuance of a temporary permit and license for periods of six months, within its discretion.
(b)
As to existing mobile homes in unapproved locations, it shall be unlawful for the owner thereof, or any other person, to make any additions to or repairs upon the same, and when such mobile home is removed and/or relocated, it shall thenceforth be unlawful for such space to be occupied by a mobile home.
(Code 1969, § 16-3; Code 1981, § 13-12; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 312)
Sec. 46-13. - Same—Inspections.
All approved mobile homes for residential occupancy must be inspected by the building inspector for:
(1)
Proper setbacks as required by the zoning laws for the city.
(2)
Sufficient off-street parking for each mobile home.
(3)
Connection to the city water system.
(4)
Connection to the city sewer system or installation of an approved septic tank.
(5)
Proper installation of the base, panel and wiring to mobile home before connection to electrical service.
(6)
To ascertain that all applicable codes, as adopted by the city, are complied with before a certificate of occupancy will be granted.
(Code 1981, § 13-13; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 313)
Sec. 46-14. - Same—Annual license fee.
Owners and/or occupants of mobile homes located within the city shall be required to pay to the tax commissioner an annual mobile home license fee per mobile home unit, which shall be due and payable January 1 of each year, for mobile homes located in the city on such date. All such mobile home license fees for mobile homes located within the city after January 1 of the same calendar year shall be prorated for the remaining months of the same calendar year. Such mobile home license fees shall be in lieu of all city ad valorem taxes for the same calendar year. The amount of the annual mobile home license fee shall be as established by the city council from time to time.
(Code 1969, § 16-4.1; Code 1981, § 13-14; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 315)
Sec. 46-15. - Same—Revocation of permits.
(a)
Any permit for a mobile home may be revoked when it is found to be in violation of this chapter.
(b)
Should the code enforcement officer find that conditions or practices exist which are in violation of any provision of this chapter, notice shall be given to the permittee or owner that unless such conditions or practices are corrected within 30 days, the permit will be revoked.
(c)
If at the end of 30 days, a further inspection reveals that the conditions or practices have not been corrected, the enforcement officers shall revoke the permit and give notice of such revocation in writing to the permittee or owner.
(Code 1981, § 13-15; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 316)
Secs. 46-16—46-72. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - MOBILE HOME PARK PERMIT

Sec. 46-73. - Required; lapse.
It shall be unlawful for any person to locate a mobile home park or to construct, alter or extend any mobile home park within the city unless such person holds a valid permit issued by the code enforcement inspection department. Construction must begin within 180 days after issuance of the permit or the permit will be invalid.
(Code 1981, § 13-20; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 411)
Sec. 46-74. - Application.
(a)
All applications for permits shall be submitted to the code enforcement department, after submission and approval of the final site plan by the planning commission.
(b)
All persons, or their duly authorized agents, desiring permits for the construction, alteration or extension of any mobile home park within the city may discuss possible development site plans with the planning commission prior to formal submission of their mobile home park site plan.
(Code 1969, § 16-5; Code 1981, § 13-21; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 412)
Sec. 46-75. - Issuance; contents.
(a)
Any time after approval of the final site plan by the planning commission, application may be made to the code enforcement department for a permit for the construction of the mobile home park. Two complete sets of construction plans shall be submitted to the department, with one set to be returned to the planning commission upon issuance of a permit containing the following:
(1)
The approved final site plan.
(2)
Location of water and sewer lines and user pipes and manholes.
(3)
Plans and specifications of the water supply, sewer lines, storm drainage system, refuse and sewage disposal facilities.
(4)
Plans and specifications of all buildings to be constructed, altered or extended within the mobile home park.
(5)
The location and details of lighting and electrical systems.
(b)
Upon review of the application, and subject to evidence that the mobile home park meets the minimum requirements of the state and county health authorities, the code enforcement officer shall issue a permit when the review of the application and inspection of the site demonstrates that the proposed or existing mobile home park meets the requirements of this chapter.
(Code 1981, § 13-22; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 412)
Sec. 46-76. - Revocation.
Any permit for a mobile home park may be revoked when it is found to be in violation of this chapter.
(1)
Should the code enforcement officer find that conditions exist which are in violation of any provision of this chapter, notice shall be given to the permittee or owner that unless such conditions or practices are corrected within 30 days, the permit will be revoked.
(2)
If, at the end of 30 days, a further inspection reveals that the conditions or practices have not been corrected, the officer shall then revoke the permit and give notice of such revocation in writing to the permittee or owner. Upon notice of revocation, the permittee or owner shall cease operation and/or construction of the mobile home park.
(Code 1981, § 13-23; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 416)
Sec. 46-77. - Site plan layout—Contents; review; disposition of prints.
(a)
A mobile home park site plan layout shall be drawn at a scale no smaller than 100 feet to one inch. Three black or blue line prints of the final site plan on or before the first working day of the month shall be submitted to the planning commission and shall contain the following information:
(1)
Name and address of applicant; if the applicant is a partnership, the names and addresses of the partners; if the applicant is a corporation, the names and addresses of the officers and directors.
(2)
Name and address of the owner of the land.
(3)
Location (vicinity map) and legal description of the mobile home park.
(4)
Scale.
(5)
North arrow.
(6)
The area and dimensions of the tract of land.
(7)
The number, location and size of all mobile home lots or stands.
(8)
The location, width and area of roadways, parkways, streets, driveways and walks.
(9)
The location of service buildings and any other proposed structures.
(10)
Location and design of parking spaces.
(11)
Location and acreage of recreational land.
(12)
Gross density.
(13)
Topography of the mobile home park site.
(b)
Before acting on the mobile home site plan layout, the planning commission may request a report from any person or agency directly concerned with the proposed development, such as the district highway engineer, superintendent of schools, and public utilities. Such reports shall certify compliance with or note deviations from the requirement of this chapter, and include comments on other factors which bear upon the public interest. The responsibility of securing said reports is that of the planning commission.
(c)
Review and comment of the mobile home site plan layout shall be noted in a report of the planning commission and on all three prints as submitted.
(d)
Approval of the final site plan shall be noted on all three prints and attested to by the signature of the chairperson of the planning commission. One print shall be returned to the applicant, one print retained in the files of the planning commission, and one print retained in the files of the code enforcement officer.
(Code 1981, § 13-24; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 412)
Sec. 46-78. - Same—Procedure for approval.
The procedure for obtaining site plan layout approval of a mobile home park is as follows:
(1)
All plats submitted to the planning commission in acceptable order on or before the first working day of the month will be reviewed and action taken no later than the 15th of the next month.
(2)
All mobile home site plan layouts submitted to the planning commission's staff in acceptable order on or before the 15th day of each month will be reviewed and action taken no later than the first working day of the following month. When the first day of the month falls on a weekend or holiday preliminary site plans may be submitted on the following work day.
(3)
At least three black or blue line prints of the site plan layout prepared in accordance with the requirements of these regulations shall be submitted.
(4)
Review, comment and/or approval of the site plan shall be made by the chairperson of the planning commission, said review, comment and/or approval shall be noted on all copies of the site plan as submitted. One print shall be returned to the applicant, one print retained by the planning commission and one print retained by the code enforcement officer.
(Code 1981, § 13-25; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 413)
Sec. 46-79. - Existing parks.
Mobile home parks operating under a valid permit or license issued prior to the effective date of the ordinance from which this article is derived may continue to operate under the terms of such permit or license.
(Code 1981, § 13-26; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 417)
Secs. 46-80—46-101. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - MOBILE HOME PARK CONSTRUCTION AND OPERATION

Sec. 46-102. - Minimum size.
The minimum size of a mobile home park shall be eight acres.
(Code 1969, § 16-7(1); Code 1981, § 13-30; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 511)
Sec. 46-103. - Maximum density.
The maximum number of mobile homes or mobile home stands shall be eight per gross acre exclusive of all recreation areas.
(Code 1981, § 13-31; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 512)
Sec. 46-104. - Mobile home lots.
(a)
Mobile homes may be located on individual lots within a mobile home park.
(b)
Each mobile home lot shall contain not less than 5,000 square feet of area.
(Code 1969, § 16-7(2); Code 1981, § 13-32; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 513)
Sec. 46-105. - Hazardous conditions.
Condition of soil, groundwater level, drainage, and topography shall not create hazards to the property or the health or safety of the occupants. The site shall not be exposed to objectionable smoke, noise, odors or other adverse influences, and no portion shall be subject to unpredictable or predictable flooding.
(Code 1981, § 13-33; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 514.1)
Sec. 46-106. - Soil and ground cover.
Exposed ground surfaces in all parts of every mobile home park shall be paved, or covered with stone screenings, or other solid material, or protected with a vegetative growth that is capable of preventing soil erosion and of eliminating objectionable dust and mud.
(Code 1981, § 13-34; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 514.2)
Sec. 46-107. - Site drainage requirements.
The ground surface in all parts of every mobile home park shall be graded and equipped to drain all surface water in a safe, efficient manner in order to eliminate water pockets, low areas or bogs.
(Code 1981, § 13-35; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 514.3)
Sec. 46-108. - Nonresidential uses.
(a)
No part of any park shall be used for nonresidential purposes, except as permitted by this chapter and by the zoning ordinance (set forth in chapter 94).
(b)
Nothing contained in this chapter shall be deemed as prohibiting the sale of a mobile home located on a mobile home stand and connected to the pertinent utilities.
(Code 1981, § 13-36; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 514.4)
Sec. 46-109. - Separation between mobile homes.
(a)
Mobile homes shall be separated from each other and from other buildings by at least 20 feet of open space.
(b)
An accessory structure which has a floor area exceeding 25 square feet and which is attached to a mobile home or located within ten feet of its window, and which has an opaque top or roof that is higher than the nearest window, shall, for purposes of all separation requirements, be considered to be part of the mobile home.
(Code 1969, § 16-7; Code 1981, § 13-37; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 514.5)
Sec. 46-110. - Recreation area.
(a)
In all parks accommodating or designed to accommodate five or more mobile homes, there shall be one or more recreation areas, which shall be easily accessible to all park residents.
(b)
The size of such recreation areas shall be based upon a minimum of six percent of the gross area of the mobile home park. No outdoor recreation area shall contain less than 5,000 square feet.
(c)
Recreation areas shall be so located as to be free of traffic hazards and should, where the topography permits, be centrally located.
(d)
Recreational equipment shall be provided for each required recreation area.
(e)
Recreational areas shall be protected with suitable vegetation except in areas of extreme wear in which case paving, shaker sand, or other solid material shall be permitted.
(Code 1981, § 13-38; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 514.6)
Sec. 46-111. - Setbacks, buffer strips and screening.
(a)
All mobile homes shall be located at least 35 feet from any park property boundary line abutting upon a public street or highway right-of-way and at least 20 feet from the exterior park property boundary lines.
(b)
There shall be a minimum distance of ten feet between an individual mobile home and adjoining pavement of a park walk, street or common parking area or other common areas.
(c)
All mobile home parks adjacent to commercial, industrial or residential uses shall be provided with screening of a well-maintained opaque fence along the property boundary line separating the park and adjacent zoning and uses. Said screening shall be a minimum of six feet in height.
(Code 1981, § 13-39; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 514.7)
Sec. 46-112. - Street system.
(a)
General requirements. All mobile home parks shall be provided with safe and convenient vehicular access from abutting public streets or roads or highways to each mobile home stand. Said access shall be provided by streets, driveways or other means. Alignment and gradient shall be properly adapted to topography.
(b)
Access. Access to mobile home parks shall be designed to minimize congestion and hazards at the entrance or exit and allow free movement of traffic on adjacent streets. The entrance road connecting the park streets with a public street or road shall have a minimum road pavement width of 24 feet with parking prohibited on both sides of the road.
(c)
Internal streets. Paved streets shall be of adequate width to accommodate anticipated traffic, and in any case shall meet the following minimum requirements:
(1)
All streets shall have a minimum of 40 feet of right-of-way with at least 20 feet of pavement.
(2)
Dead-end streets shall be limited in length to and shall be provided at the closed end with a turnaround having an outside roadway diameter at least 60 feet.
(3)
One-way streets at least 16 feet in width may be permitted.
(d)
Driveways. Paved driveways shall be provided to each mobile home stand where necessary for convenient access to each mobile home. The minimum width shall be ten feet.
(e)
Required illumination of park street system. All parks shall be furnished with lighting units so spaced and equipped with luminaries placed at such mounting heights as will provide the following average maintained levels of illumination for safe movement of pedestrians and vehicles at night: all parts of the park street system, 0.6 footcandle, with a minimum of 0.1 footcandle.
(f)
Street construction and design standards.
(1)
Pavement. All streets shall be provided with a smooth, hard and dense bituminous surface which shall be durable and well-drained under normal use and weather conditions. Pavement edges shall be protected to prevent raveling of the wearing surface and shifting of the pavement base. Street surfaces shall be maintained free of cracks, holes and other hazards.
(2)
Grades. Grades of all streets shall be sufficient to ensure adequate surface drainage, but shall be not more than eight percent. Short runs with a maximum grade of 12 percent may be permitted, provided traffic safety is ensured by appropriate paving, adequate leveling areas and avoidance of lateral curves.
(3)
Intersections. Within 100 feet of an intersection, streets shall be at approximately right angles. A distance of at least 150 feet shall be maintained between centerlines of offset intersecting streets. Intersections of more than two streets at one point shall be avoided.
(Code 1981, § 13-40; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 514.8)
Sec. 46-113. - Off-street parking.
(a)
Off-street parking areas shall be provided in all mobile home parks for the use of park occupants and guests. Such areas shall be furnished at the rate of at least two car spaces for each mobile home stand.
(b)
Required car parking spaces shall be so located as to provide convenient access to the mobile home, but shall not exceed a distance of 200 feet from the mobile home that it is intended to serve.
(Code 1981, § 13-41; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 514.9)
Sec. 46-114. - Walks.
All parks shall be provided with safe, convenient, all-season, pedestrian access walks of adequate width for intended use and durable and convenient to maintain. Sudden changes in alignment and gradient shall be avoided.
(Code 1981, § 13-42; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 514.10)
Sec. 46-115. - Landscaping.
A minimum of one tree shall be provided at each mobile home stand.
(Code 1981, § 13-43; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 514.11)
Sec. 46-116. - Mobile home support.
The area of the mobile home piers shall be improved to provide an adequate foundation for the placement of the mobile home; thereby securing the superstructure against uplift, sliding, rotating and overturning.
(1)
Each mobile home shall be placed on masonry piers or stands.
(2)
The mobile home piers or stands shall not heave, shift or settle unevenly under the weight of the mobile home due to inadequate drainage, vibration or other forces acting on the superstructure.
(3)
Mobile home parks must include provisions for attachment to anchoring devices at the mobile home site.
(4)
Skirts shall be required around each mobile home from the ground up to the base of the mobile home.
(Code 1981, § 13-44; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 514.12)
Sec. 46-117. - Identification.
Each lot, space or stand shall be numbered. Each number shall be easily identified from the nearest street or driveway.
(Code 1981, § 13-45; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 514.13)
Sec. 46-118. - Water supply requirements.
(a)
Generally. An accessible, adequate, safe and potable supply of water shall be provided in each mobile home park. Where a public supply of water of satisfactory quantity, quality, and pressure is available, connection shall be made thereto and its supply used exclusively. When a satisfactory public water supply is not available, a private water supply system may be developed and used as approved by the state health authorities in accordance with rules and regulations for water supply quality control.
(b)
Distribution system. The water supply of the mobile home park shall be constructed by the contractor, or owner of the mobile home park and connected in accordance with the plumbing code of the city. Each mobile home stand shall be provided with a water connection. Such connection shall be in accordance with the provisions of the plumbing code (as set forth in article III of chapter 14), and the director of public works must inspect all phases of construction and such director's approval given before connection to city water supply.
(Code 1969, § 16-7(4); Code 1981, § 13-46; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 515)
Sec. 46-119. - Sewage disposal.
An adequate and safe sewer system shall be provided by the owner or contractor in all mobile home parks for conveying and disposing of all sewage. Each mobile home stand shall be provided with a sewer connection. Such system and connection shall be designed, constructed and maintained in accordance with the provisions of the plumbing code (as set forth in article III of chapter 14) and the approval of the state health authorities and water quality control board. The director of public works must inspect the mobile home park sewer system before approval to connect to the city sewer system is granted.
(Code 1969, § 16-7(4); Code 1981, § 13-47; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 516)
Sec. 46-120. - Electrical distribution system.
Every park shall contain an electrical wiring system consisting of underground wiring, fixtures, equipment and appurtenances, and each mobile home stand shall be equipped with a minimum of 150 ampere service, which shall be installed and maintained in accordance with the city electrical code (as adopted in section 14-24). If a larger service is needed for an individual mobile home, then a service to meet that mobile home need must be supplied before a certificate of occupancy will be issued by the code enforcement officer.
(Code 1969, § 16-7(4); Code 1981, § 13-48; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 517)
Sec. 46-121. - Fuel supply and storage.
(a)
Natural gas system. Natural gas piping systems shall be installed and maintained in accordance with applicable gas codes of the city (as adopted in section 14-24).
(b)
Liquefied petroleum gas systems. Liquefied petroleum gas systems shall be installed and maintained in accordance with applicable gas codes of the city.
(c)
Fuel oil supply systems. All fuel oil supply systems shall be installed and maintained in accordance with applicable codes of the city.
(Code 1981, § 13-49; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 518)
Sec. 46-122. - Community service facilities.
The requirements of this chapter shall apply to service buildings, recreation buildings and other community service facilities such as:
(1)
Management offices, repair shops and storage areas;
(2)
Sanitary facilities;
(3)
Laundry facilities;
(4)
Indoor recreation areas; and
(5)
Retail shopping facility for the mobile home park.
(Code 1981, § 13-50; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 519)
Sec. 46-123. - Structural requirements; illumination; water.
All structures shall comply with the following city building codes in addition to the requirements contained herein:
(1)
Illumination levels shall be maintained as follows:
a.
General seeing tasks: five footcandles;
b.
Laundry room work area: 40 footcandles;
c.
Toilet room in front of mirrors: 40 footcandles.
(2)
Hot and cold water shall be furnished to every lavatory, sink, bathtub, shower and laundry fixture; and cold water shall be furnished to every water closet and urinal.
(Code 1981, § 13-51; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 519.1)
Sec. 46-124. - Fireplaces, etc.
Cooking shelters, barbecue pits, fireplaces and woodburning stoves shall be so located, constructed, maintained and used as to avoid fire hazards and smoke nuisance both on the property on which used and on neighboring property. No open fire shall be permitted except in facilities provided. No open fire shall be left unattended. No fuel shall be used and no material burned which emits dense smoke or objectionable odors. No refuse shall be burned at any time.
(Code 1981, § 13-52; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 519.2)
Sec. 46-125. - Refuse handling.
Refuse shall be handled in the manner prescribed in chapter 70.
(Code 1981, § 13-53; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 520)
Sec. 46-126. - Fire protection.
(a)
The mobile home shall be subject to fire prevention regulations of this Code and other ordinances adopted by the city council.
(b)
Mobile home parks shall be kept free of litter, rubbish and other flammable materials.
(c)
Portable fire extinguishers of a type approved by the fire department shall be kept in service buildings and at all other locations designated by the fire department and shall be maintained in good operating condition.
(d)
Fires shall be made only in stoves, incinerators and other equipment intended for such purposes.
(e)
Fire hydrants shall be installed by the contractor or owner as required by the fire department and approved by the department of utilities.
(Code 1981, § 13-54; Ord. of 7-2-1973, § 521)
