ARTICLE XI. - EVENT CENTER REGULATIONS

Sec. 32-330. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Event center means a properly licensed business that uses or rents or charges a fee for a commercial business premises, building, structure or location for the primary intended purpose of hosting or conducting parties, social gatherings or other social events. Excluded from this definition are restaurants serving prepared food, city buildings and places of worship/churches.
Security detail means an off-duty P.O.S.T. certified peace officer employed by the owner of the premises or employed by the renter of the event center to provide security at the party or gathering. The owner and/or renter of the premises shall be responsible for paying the fees of security personnel.
(Ord. No. 2018-11-01 , § 2, 11-20-2018)
Sec. 32-331. - Event attendance; security detail required.
All event centers are required to provide a security detail at any party or other social gathering taking place on the premises in accordance with the requirements listed below. The number of officers required in the security detail is based upon the number of people attending the event or party.
EXPAND
Attendance Security Detail Requirement
50 or fewer None
51 to 100 One officer required
100+ Two officers minimum required
  (Ord. No. 2018-11-01 , § 2, 11-20-2018)
Sec. 32-332. - Service and sale of alcoholic beverages prohibited.
Service and/or sale of alcoholic beverages at event centers is prohibited unless the business owner or renter possesses a valid alcoholic beverage license issued by the city. Pursuant to chapter 4, article II, section 4-35(b) of the City Code, only restaurants (as defined by City Code) are permitted to obtain a license to sell and serve malt beverages and wine by the drink for on-premises consumption.
A licensed alcoholic beverage caterer holding a valid off-premises license issued pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-11-2 is authorized to sell and serve malt beverages and wine at event centers following compliance with O.C.G.A. § 3-11-3.
(Ord. No. 2018-11-01 , § 2, 11-20-2018)
Sec. 32-333. - No activity in parking lots.
Parties or social gatherings at event centers shall be conducted solely inside the business premises. The owner and/or renter of the premises shall not permit gatherings or congregations of attendees in parking lots.
(Ord. No. 2018-11-01 , § 2, 11-20-2018)
Sec. 32-334. - Noise restrictions.
The owner and/or renter of the premises shall comply with chapter 26, article III, section 26-86 of the City Code with regard to noise generated by invitees or live music, a band, stage performance or DJ.
(Ord. No. 2018-11-01 , § 2, 11-20-2018)
Sec. 32-335. - Penalties for violation.
Violations of this article shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $500.00 and/or imprisonment for a term not exceeding 180 days. Repeated violations of this article may also result in the revocation of the owner's or renter's occupation tax certificate.
(Ord. No. 2018-11-01 , § 2, 11-20-2018)
