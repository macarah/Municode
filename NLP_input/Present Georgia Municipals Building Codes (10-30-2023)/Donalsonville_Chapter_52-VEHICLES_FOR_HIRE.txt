ARTICLE II. - TAXICABS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 52-19. - Presumption of operation.
Any automobile marked, labeled or identified by lettering or numbers as a taxicab and found upon the streets of the city, or regularly used for transportation of passengers, shall be presumed to be used as a taxicab and for transportation of passengers for hire, for the purpose of this article.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-47; Code 1996, § 94-31)
Sec. 52-20. - Identification and marking of vehicles.
(a)
All motor vehicles operated as taxicabs in the city shall be marked on the outside with the following information:
(1)
The name of the owner;
(2)
The word "taxicab" or "taxi";
(3)
If a fleet or more than one taxicab is operated by one company or owner, a number shall be assigned to each vehicle, which shall be individual and identifying; and
(4)
Any other information which may be deemed necessary by the mayor and council.
(b)
The markings described in subsection (a) of this section shall be stenciled or painted on each side of the taxicab in a permanent paint of contrasting color to the color of the vehicle or affixed by decal or some like manner. The letters of such markings shall not be less than three inches in height and must be printed so that the markings may be easily read at a distance of at least 50 feet.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-21; Code 1996, § 94-32)
Sec. 52-21. - Certificate of vehicle inspection required.
The owner and operator of each taxicab licensed to operate within the city shall, before having his license either granted or renewed, produce to the city manager a written inspection report from a qualified automobile mechanic engaged in business within the city. Such mechanic shall be approved by the mayor and council, showing that the brakes and lights on each unit operated by him are in safe, serviceable condition. The license shall in no case be granted until this section is complied with.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-22; Code 1996, § 94-33)
Sec. 52-22. - Sale, trade or exchange of vehicle.
(a)
Notice required. If any owner or operator of a taxicab in the city shall sell, trade or exchange any taxicab being operated in the city and substitute another car therefor, it shall be the duty of the seller to notify the city clerk within ten hours of the sale, trade or exchange of the vehicle, giving the number under which the car traded is identified in the policy of liability insurance on file with the mayor and council.
(b)
Transfer of insurance required. It shall also be the duty of such owner or operator of the car substituted to furnish the clerk written endorsement of the insurance company issuing the public liability policy, the endorsement to be attached as an endorsement on the liability policy, showing that the car substituted is insured under the terms of the liability insurance.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-23; Code 1996, § 94-34)
Sec. 52-23. - Drivers—Minimum age.
No person shall drive a taxicab upon the streets of the city who is less than 19 years of age.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-24; Code 1996, § 94-35)
Sec. 52-24. - Drivers—Effect of felony conviction.
No person shall drive a taxicab upon the streets of the city who has been convicted of an offense constituting a felony or who has pleaded guilty upon a charge of having committed an offense constituting a felony.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-25; Code 1996, § 94-36)
Sec. 52-25. - Drivers—Driving under influence of intoxicating liquor.
It shall be unlawful for any person licensed to drive a taxicab under the provisions of this article to operate such taxicab while he is under the influence of intoxicating liquor. A violation of this section shall be grounds for license revocation.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-26; Code 1996, § 94-37)
Sec. 52-26. - Transporting alcoholic beverages.
(a)
Prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any person operating a licensed taxicab service in the city, or any driver operating a taxicab in the city under the license, to knowingly haul wine, liquor or beer in such taxicab, provided that this section shall not apply to a driver transporting a fare-paying passenger in possession of alcoholic beverages so long as such alcoholic beverages remain in the possession and control of the passenger and are not opened within the vehicle.
(b)
Revocation of license. If the driver of any taxicab in the city knowingly allows or permits intoxicating liquor, wine or beer to be hauled in the taxicab operated by him in violation of this section, the license issued by the city shall automatically be revoked. If any license is forfeited and revoked because a driver is guilty of hauling intoxicating liquor, wine or beer, and desires to be heard, he shall have a right to appeal to the mayor and council for a hearing and be heard at such time and place as the mayor and council shall designate. Pending the hearing before the mayor and council, the licensee shall not operate the taxicab service under and by virtue of any such license.
(c)
Forfeiture of driver's rights. It shall be unlawful for any driver guilty of knowingly hauling liquor, wine or beer in any taxicab driven by him to again drive a taxicab in the city.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-27; Code 1996, § 94-38)
Sec. 52-27. - Number of passengers.
No person shall drive a taxicab upon the streets of the city when such vehicle contains more persons than such vehicle is designed to carry.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-28; Code 1996, § 94-39)
Sec. 52-28. - Receiving and discharging passengers.
Drivers of taxicabs shall not receive or discharge passengers in the roadway but shall drive to the sidewalk or curb on the right-hand side of such roadway, and there receive or discharge passengers.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-29; Code 1996, § 94-40)

DIVISION 2. - OCCUPATIONAL LICENSE

Sec. 52-60. - Required for drivers.
It shall be unlawful to drive a taxicab or vehicle of any kind for the purpose of transporting passengers or property for hire without first applying for and obtaining an occupational license therefor.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-1; Code 1996, § 94-56)
Sec. 52-61. - Required to engage in taxicab business.
It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in the business of furnishing taxicab service in the city and maintaining a place of business in the city without first obtaining a license approved by the mayor and council.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-9; Code 1996, § 94-57)
Sec. 52-62. - Application; manner and form.
Application for a taxicab driver's license shall be made in such manner and form as prescribed by the city manager.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-2; Code 1996, § 94-58)
Sec. 52-63. - Statement to accompany application.
Application for a license to drive a taxicab shall be supported by a sworn statement of the applicant setting forth the following:
(1)
Name and address of the applicant;
(2)
The applicant's age;
(3)
The applicant's habits as to sobriety; and
(4)
Whether or not the applicant has been convicted of any offense against the laws of the state or of any city, and particularly whether or not he has been convicted of violating any traffic regulation of any state or city.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-3; Code 1996, § 94-59)
Sec. 52-64. - Approval of application.
No license shall be issued to drive a taxicab until the application has been approved by the city manager.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-4; Code 1996, § 94-60)
Sec. 52-65. - Personal appearance of applicant.
The applicant shall submit himself personally before the city manager, if required, or if he so desires, at the time the application for a taxicab driver's license is considered.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-5; Code 1996, § 94-61)
Sec. 52-66. - Action on application; issuance and denial; fees.
If, upon a consideration of an application for a license to drive a taxicab, the city manager shall decide that the applicant is qualified and entitled to operate a taxicab and in all manners in full compliance with all local, state and federal laws, having due regard to the safety and morals of the public, the application shall be granted and a license issued by the city clerk, upon payment of the business or occupation tax legally demandable. If the city manager shall determine that the applicant is not a fit and proper person to be so licensed, having due regard for the safety and morals of the public, the mayor and council shall refuse such license.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-6; Code 1996, § 94-62)
Sec. 52-67. - Suspension or revocation authorized for good cause.
(a)
The city manager shall have authority to suspend or revoke any and all licenses issued under this article, for good cause shown, or in the event the licensee fails to maintain compliance with any local, state or federal law relevant to the operation of the licensed business, after five days' notice given the licensee, at either a regular or special meeting. It shall be unlawful for any licensee to operate during the period of suspension.
(b)
The term "good cause" shall mean any violation of this Code or of any state statutes pertaining to the operation of motor vehicles.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-10; Code 1996, § 94-65)
Sec. 52-68. - Revocation for failure to provide service.
It is the declared policy of the mayor and council that, in licensing the operation of vehicles under this article, a reasonable service will be rendered to the general public and to the inhabitants of the city by the persons holding those licenses, and if such service cannot or will not be rendered by any person holding such license, the license should be revoked. Among the causes justifying such revocation shall be unreasonable failure or refusal to render such service.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-12; Code 1996, § 94-67)

DIVISION 3. - INSURANCE

Sec. 52-95. - Compliance with division required.
It shall be unlawful to operate upon the public roads, streets and highways within the corporate limits of the city any taxicab which is subject to the regulations and ordinances of the city unless such operation is done in conformity to this division with respect to insurance protection.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-41; Code 1996, § 94-86)
Sec. 52-96. - Coverage required.
Before using or operating any taxicab which is subject to the provisions of this division for transporting passengers, and before allowing any other person to do so within the city upon the roads, streets and highways, the owner shall provide for each vehicle to be so used, and for the benefit of any and all persons having claims against the owner for damage by way of loss of life, damage to property, and/or for injury to the person, minimum liability insurance coverage as follows:

Sec. 52-97. - Approval.
Insurance coverage required by this division shall be subject to approval by the city manager and shall be with a company satisfactory to the city manager and in a form satisfactory to the city manager.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-43; Code 1996, § 94-88)
Sec. 52-98. - Deposit of policies with city manager.
It shall be the duty of the owner to deposit such policies with the city manager, where they shall remain while in force.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-44; Code 1996, § 94-89)
Sec. 52-99. - Notice of cancellation required.
It shall be the duty of the insurer to give reasonable notice to the city before cancelling any coverage. Any company failing to do so will not be considered an acceptable company for the purpose of furnishing such coverage.
(Code 1984, § 9-3-45; Code 1996, § 94-90)
