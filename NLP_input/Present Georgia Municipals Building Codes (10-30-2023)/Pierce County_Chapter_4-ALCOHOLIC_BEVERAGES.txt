ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 4-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcohol means ethyl alcohol, hydrated oxide of ethyl, or spirits of wine, from whatever source or by whatever process produced.
Alcoholic beverage means and includes all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, malt beverages, wine or fortified wine.
County means the area of Pierce County, Georgia, outside the limits of incorporated municipalities.
Distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 24 percent alcohol by volume.
Fortified wine means any alcoholic beverage containing more than 24 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "fortified wine" includes, but is not limited to, brandy.
Malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than 14 percent alcohol by volume and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer, and strong beer. The term "malt beverage" does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Package store license means a license issued to a retail dealer for the sale of alcoholic beverages, which alcoholic beverages are not to be consumed on the premises of the retail dealer, and which alcoholic beverages are sold in sealed containers.
Pouring license means a license issued to a retail dealer for the sale of alcoholic beverages, which alcoholic beverages are to be consumed on the premises of the retail dealer, and which alcoholic beverages are served in unsealed containers.
Retail dealer means, except as to distilled spirits, any person who sells alcoholic beverages, either in unbroken packages or for consumption on the premises, at retail only to consumers and not for resale. With respect to distilled spirits, the term "retail dealer" means any person who sells distilled spirits in unbroken packages at retail only to consumers and not for resale.
Sheriff means the sheriff or any deputy sheriff of the county.
Wholesaler or wholesale dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages to other wholesale dealers, to retail dealers, or to retail consumption dealers.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 24 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "wine" includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products. The term "wine" does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this section.
(Code 2005, § 6-1; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. I)

ARTICLE II. - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGE CONTROL
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-21. - Hours and days of sale.
No alcoholic beverages will be sold between the hours of 12:00 midnight on Saturday night until the hour of 7:00 a.m. on Monday morning, and no alcoholic beverages will be sold on election days, Christmas Day or any other day on which the sale of alcoholic beverages may be prohibited by order of the commissioners. An election day, for the purposes of this article, is hereby defined as that period of time beginning with the opening of the polls and ending with the closing of the polls. The prohibition against the sale of alcoholic beverages to any person on election days shall apply only within the territorial boundaries for which the election is being held, but such territorial boundaries shall not include any property owned or operated by a county, municipality or other political subdivision of the state for airport purposes if no person resides on such publicly-owned or operated property. No alcoholic beverages will be sold between the hours of 1:00 a.m. and until 7:00 a.m. on all other days. Alcoholic beverages may be sold at any time not specifically prohibited under this section.
(Code 2005, § 6-31; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. III, § 7)
Sec. 4-22. - Pouring license required.
No retail dealer for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall allow alcoholic beverages to be consumed on his premises unless such dealer has a pouring license. A person who has a package store license but not a pouring license shall be held responsible for such consumption by his customers or other parties.
(Code 2005, § 6-32; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. III, § 8)
Sec. 4-23. - Copy of ordinance on premises.
All holders of a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall keep a copy of this article on his premises and instruct any person working there with respect to the terms herein, and each licensee and person selling alcoholic beverages shall, at all times, be familiar with the terms of this article.
(Code 2005, § 6-33; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. III, § 9)
Sec. 4-24. - Inspection.
The business premises of a holder of a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages, including outbuildings, shall be opened to inspection at any and all reasonable times by the sheriff.
(Code 2005, § 6-34; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. III, § 10)
Sec. 4-25. - Posting of sign required.
The holder of a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall post in a conspicuous place in his place of business a notice with letters not less than four inches in height, which shall be clearly visible to all customers entering the establishment, as follows: "Sale of Alcoholic Beverages to Persons Under 21 Years of Age is Strictly Prohibited."
(Code 2005, § 6-35; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. III, § 11)
Sec. 4-26. - Employment of persons under 21 years of age.
No wholesale dealer or retail dealer in alcoholic beverages shall employ or permit the employment of any minor to sell or otherwise handle or deal with alcoholic beverages, except as permitted under O.C.G.A. § 3-3-24.
(Code 2005, § 6-36; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. III, § 12)
Sec. 4-27. - Habitual drunkards.
No wholesale dealer or retail dealer in alcoholic beverages shall sell any alcoholic beverage to any person who is under the influence of any intoxicants or drugs or who is an habitual drunkard whose intemperate habits are known to such dealer.
(Code 2005, § 6-37; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. III, § 13)
Sec. 4-28. - Consumption in public place with valid pouring license.
No alcoholic beverages shall be consumed in any public place within the county except where a valid pouring license is in effect.
(Code 2005, § 6-38; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. III, § 14)
Sec. 4-29. - Violation of chapter.
In addition to forfeiture of license for violation of any provision of this article, or the failure to abide by this article, all provisions of punishment for violation as set out in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-9 shall be enforced.
(Code 2005, § 6-39; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. V, § 4)
Secs. 4-30—4-46. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - LICENSES

Sec. 4-47. - Application required.
All applications for licenses for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall be made in person at the office of the county clerk and shall be accompanied by a completed form furnished for this purpose by such clerk.
(Code 2005, § 6-51; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. II, § 1A)
Sec. 4-48. - Fingerprints required.
(a)
At the time any application for a license hereunder is filed at the office of the county clerk and as a prerequisite to the issuance of any license under this article, the applicant shall furnish a complete set of fingerprints, which shall be forwarded to the state bureau of investigation, which shall search the files of the state crime information center for any instance of criminal activity during the two years immediately preceding the date of the application. The state bureau of investigation shall also submit the fingerprints to the Federal Bureau of Investigation under the rules established by the United States Department of Justice for processing and identification of records. The federal record, if any, shall be obtained and returned to the governing authority submitting the fingerprints.
(b)
If the license is to be issued to a firm or corporation, fingerprints shall be supplied by all persons who serve in a supervisory and/or managerial position with respect to the sale of alcoholic beverages. If the firm or corporation changes the person in such supervisory and/or managerial positions during any year, and designate such functions to a person who has not previously been responsible for such functions (hereinafter referred to as new person), then such firm or corporation shall notify the county clerk in writing of the change in such supervisory and/or managerial personnel, giving the name of the new person, his address, the effective date of the change and shall furnish the clerk with the fingerprints of the new person. The notification and furnishing of the fingerprints shall be within five regular business days of the commissioners' meeting. Should the fingerprints or investigation of the new person reveal that he is objectionable, the clerk shall notify the licensee immediately. Upon the receipt of such notice, the new person shall not serve in such capacity with licensee until authorized by the commissioners. The licensee may appeal such objections to the commissioners within three days of the receipt of such notice, by delivering written notice of the appeal to the clerk. As soon as possible, the commissioners will hear evidence on such appeal, but the new person shall not serve in such capacity until and unless authorized by the commissioners.
(Code 2005, § 6-52; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. II, § 1B)
Sec. 4-49. - Applicant to be of good moral character; prior convictions.
All applicants for alcoholic beverage licenses shall be of good moral character or such persons' applications shall not be considered; and in no event shall any license be granted to any person, firm or corporation where any of the individuals, or any of the corporate officers, directors or stockholders, as the case may be, have, within ten years immediately prior to the filing of the application, entered a plea of guilty, a plea of nolo contendere, or shall have suffered any conviction of any crime involving moral turpitude or involving alcohol control laws of the state or the United States, or of any other state. Should any licensee, partner, officer, director or stockholder of a licensee corporation, after a license has been granted hereunder, be convicted or plead guilty or nolo contendere to a crime involving moral turpitude or to a violation of any laws of the United States or of the state relating to alcohol, the licenses issued to such licensee shall stand revoked and cancelled.
(Code 2005, § 6-53; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. II, § 2)
Sec. 4-50. - Performance bond.
All applicants for licenses for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall file with such application a personal performance bond in the amount of $1,000.00, issued by an insurance company licensed to do business in the state, such bond to ensure compliance with all laws, rules, regulations and resolutions of the county. If the applicant is approved, such bond shall remain in force so long as the license is valid.
(Code 2005, § 6-54; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. II, § 3)
Sec. 4-51. - Consideration of extenuating circumstances.
The commissioners, in their sole discretion, may consider any extenuating circumstances which may reflect favorably or unfavorably on the applicant, application, or the proposed location of the business. If, in their judgment, circumstances are such that the granting of the license would not be in the best interest of the general public, such circumstances may be grounds for denying the application. In addition, the commissioners shall consider the following factors before making any decision of any application:
(1)
Is there any statutory prohibition of the law of the state against the issuance of such a license to such person at such location?
(2)
Is the proposed location primarily a residential neighborhood or business neighborhood?
(3)
Are there schools, churches and numerous dwelling houses in the immediate vicinity of the proposed license site?
(4)
Are there other such liquor, beer or wine licenses in the immediate neighborhood of the proposed site?
(5)
Would the issuance of such a license be compatible to the neighborhood as it is now constituted?
(6)
Would the issuance of such a license in such location tend to diminish the property values of the adjoining lands?
(Code 2005, § 6-55; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. II, § 4)
Sec. 4-52. - Sales of alcoholic beverages near churches, school buildings, or other sites.
(a)
Prohibited.
(1)
No person knowingly and intentionally may sell or offer to sell:
a.
Any distilled spirits in or within 100 yards of any church building or within 200 yards of any school building, educational building, school grounds, or college campus;
b.
Any wine or malt beverages within 100 yards of any school building, school grounds, or college campus. This subsection shall not apply at any location for which a license has been issued prior to July 1, 1981, nor to the renewal of such license. Nor shall this subsection apply at any location for which a new license is applied for if the sale of wine and beer was lawful at such location at any time during the 12 months immediately preceding such application;
c.
Any distilled spirits, wine, or malt beverages within 100 yards of an alcoholic treatment center owned and operated by the state or any county or municipal government therein. This subsection shall not apply to any business having a license in effect on July 1, 1981.
(2)
As used in this subsection, the term "school building" or "educational building" shall apply only to state, county, city, or church school buildings and to such buildings at such other schools in which are taught subjects commonly taught in the common schools and colleges of the state and which are public schools or private schools as defined in O.C.G.A. § 20-2-690(b).
(b)
Exceptions. Nothing contained in this section shall prohibit the licensing of the sale or distribution of alcoholic beverages by:
(1)
Hotels of 50 rooms or more which have been in continuous operation for a period of at least five years preceding July 1, 1981;
(2)
Bona fide private clubs, as that term is used in O.C.G.A. § 3-7-1(1); and
(3)
Licensees for the retail sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises only, who shall be subject to regulation as to distances from churches, schools, and colleges by counties and municipalities.
(c)
Measurement of distances. For purposes of this section, distances shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground.
(Code 2005, § 6-56; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. II, § 5)
Sec. 4-53. - False statements.
The making of any statement of an application for a license to sell alcoholic beverages which shall be later found to be false shall constitute grounds for revocation of such license.
(Code 2005, § 6-57; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. II, § 6)
Sec. 4-54. - Fees and taxes.
No license shall be issued under this article unless the applicant therefor shall have fully paid all license fees and ad valorem taxes due the county.
(Code 2005, § 6-58; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. II, § 7)
Sec. 4-55. - Publishing of application.
Prior to the issuance of any license under this article, a notice of such pending application must be advertised in the official gazette of the county in no less than four issues thereof immediately preceding the week of the next regular meeting of the board, and any interested person shall be afforded the opportunity at such meeting, or any subsequent meeting thereafter at which such application might be considered, to voice any objections to the issuance of such license. Such notice shall be in the form shown on schedule "A," which is incorporated herein by reference and on file in the office of the county clerk, and the costs of publication thereof shall be paid by the applicant.
(Code 2005, § 6-59)
Sec. 4-56. - License required.
No alcoholic beverage may be sold in the county, except under a license issued in accordance herewith.
(Code 2005, § 6-60; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. III, § 1)
Sec. 4-57. - Transfer.
No license for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall be transferable, and in the event a licensed business is sold or closed, the licensee must immediately surrender his license to the sheriff. All licenses shall be issued to specific persons, firms, or corporations and for a specific location.
(Code 2005, § 6-61; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. III, § 2)
Sec. 4-58. - Types of licenses.
Licenses for the retail sale of alcoholic beverages shall be of two types:
(1)
Package store licenses; and
(2)
Pouring licenses.
Package store licenses shall include convenience stores.
(Code 2005, § 6-62; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. III, § 3)
Sec. 4-59. - Possession of both licenses.
A retail dealer may obtain both a package store license and a pouring license. However, in order to obtain a pouring license, a retail dealer must first have obtained a package store license; however, no business establishment shall be issued a pouring license where the general public of all ages are permitted to enter. The holding of a package store license does not automatically entitle the holder to a pouring license.
(Code 2005, § 6-63; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. III, § 4)
Sec. 4-60. - Duration of licenses.
Licenses shall be issued for a calendar year, and no license fees are to be prorated for a fraction of a year.
(Code 2005, § 6-64; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. III, § 5)
Sec. 4-61. - Sales to underage persons prohibited.
No licensee shall sell alcoholic beverages to any person who is under 21 years of age under state law, and a licensee shall be held responsible for any such sale made by his employees or others in his licensed establishment. It shall be unlawful for any person under the age set out in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23 to have in his possession within the limits of the county any alcoholic beverages, unless such person shall have in his possession such alcoholic beverages as permitted in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23.
(Code 2005, § 6-65; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. III, § 6)
Sec. 4-62. - Compliance required.
No person shall continue to hold a license issued under this article, if that person has failed to comply with all licensing requirements of the state, as set out in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-1 et seq.
(Code 2005, § 6-66; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. III, § 15)
Sec. 4-63. - Revocation.
The performance of any act prohibited in this article or the failure to perform any act required hereby, as well as the violation of any law, state or federal, relating to the sale of alcoholic beverages shall constitute grounds for revocation of a license.
(Code 2005, § 6-67; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. IV, § 1)
Sec. 4-64. - Temporary suspension and closure of business.
A license for the sale of alcoholic beverages may be temporarily suspended and the place of business closed upon receipt of evidence by the commissioners from the sheriff of any violation referred to in this article.
(Code 2005, § 6-68; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. IV, § 2)
Sec. 4-65. - Suspension.
Any license for the sale of alcoholic beverages under this article may be suspended for a definite length of time, revoked, and/or the personal performance bond posted by the licensee forfeited in whole or in part.
(Code 2005, § 6-69; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. IV, § 3)
Sec. 4-66. - Notification of revocation; hearing.
No license shall be revoked and no bond shall be forfeited unless the licensee has been given at least three days' written notice, in person or by registered or certified mail, of the intention of the commissioners to recommend such action. Such notice shall specify the time, place and purposes of the hearing and statement of the charges upon which the commissioners propose to take action. At such hearings conducted by the commissioners, the licensee shall have the right to appear in person and by attorney, and both the commissioners and licensee shall have the right to present evidence relating to the question as to whether or not a violation, as set forth in this article, has occurred, as well as evidence relating to any extenuating or mitigating circumstances.
(Code 2005, § 6-70; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. IV, § 4)
Sec. 4-67. - Additional business license.
The license required under this article shall be required in addition to any license required in the county business license ordinance.
(Code 2005, § 6-71; Ord. of 3-14-1995, pt. IV, § 5)
Sec. 4-68. - Fee schedule.
The license fee for businesses that sell beer, wine and liquor will be determined from time to time by action of the board of commissioners and set forth in the consolidated fee schedule.
(Code 2005, § 6-72; Res. of 3-14-1995)

ARTICLE III. - EXCISE TAXES

Sec. 4-95. - Excise taxes imposed.
The following excise taxes are hereby imposed upon alcoholic beverages sold and distributed to dealers in the county, outside of the corporate limits of the Cities of Blackshear and Patterson:
(1)
Malt beverages.
a.
Where malt beverages, commonly known as tap or draft beer, are sold in or from a barrel or bulk container, a tax of $6.00 on each container sold containing not more than 15½ gallons and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 15½;
b.
Where malt beverages are sold in bottles, cans or other containers, except barrel or bulk containers, a tax of $0.05 per 12 ounces and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 12 ounces.
(2)
Distilled spirits. $0.22 per liter of distilled spirits, excluding fortified wine, and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter.
(3)
Wine. $0.22 per liter of wine and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter.
(Code 2005, § 6-91; Ord. of 11-1-1971, § 4)
Sec. 4-96. - Inspections.
The retail dealers shall, during regular business hours, permit an inspection of the alcoholic beverages held and possessed by them for resale by any person designated by the board of commissioners for such purpose.
(Code 2005, § 6-92; Ord. of 11-1-1971, § 7)
Sec. 4-97. - Violation of article.
Any violation of this article by any dealer of liquor, wine and/or beer in the county, outside of the limits of the City of Blackshear and the City of Patterson, shall be deemed as sufficient grounds for the revocation of the license issued to him by the board of commissioners and the refusal thereafter again to issue a new license to such violator of this article. Such action may be taken by the board of commissioners with or without notice to the violator.
(Code 2005, § 6-93; Ord. of 11-1-1971, § 9)
Sec. 4-98. - Appointment of inspector.
The board shall appoint an inspector to make periodic inspections of the places of business of retail dealers in the county, outside of the corporate limits of the City of Blackshear and the City of Patterson, to see that the terms and provisions of this article are carried out by such dealers. His compensation shall be such as may be fixed by the board. Such inspector may be the sheriff or any deputy sheriff.
(Code 2005, § 6-94; Ord. of 11-1-1971, § 12)
Sec. 4-99. - Statement of sales; payment of taxes.
Wholesale dealers shall render to the board of commissioners a true statement of all alcoholic beverages sold to each retail dealer in the county, and shall simultaneously, with such statement, pay to the county the tax imposed under section 4-95. Such statement shall be made by copy of invoices promptly furnished at such times as required by the board of commissioners.
(Code 2005, § 6-95; Ord. of 11-1-1971, § 14)
