ARTICLE II. - PUBLIC DANCEHALLS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 6-21. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Public dancehall means any place open to the general public where dancing is permitted, to which an admission fee is charged, or for which compensation is in any manner received, either directly or indirectly, by cover charge or otherwise, or where refreshments or food or any form of merchandise are served for compensation before, during or after dancing. The sale of any refreshments, food or any form of merchandise at any such place, or the exhibiting of such for sale, shall be deemed direct compensation for any such dance hall within the meaning of this section. This section, however, shall not apply to dances held for benevolent or charitable purposes, or when the same are conducted under the auspices of religious, educational, civic or military organizations. In addition, the term "dancehall" as used in this article, shall not apply to hotels and inns within the definition of O.C.G.A. §§ 43-21-1 and 43-21-2 nor to persons who, incidental to their principal business or occupation, accept from time to time seasonal boarders in their private residences.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-11)
Sec. 6-22. - Board approval required for change of ownership, management or location.
The ownership, management or location of a public dancehall shall not be changed without the approval of the board of commissioners.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-12)
Sec. 6-23. - Right of entry of police.
Members of the city police department may enter any public dancehall operated pursuant to a permit issued under the provisions of this article at all hours to ensure that the peace and quiet of the city are preserved and that the conditions and restrictions of this article are observed.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-13)
Sec. 6-24. - Minimum seating requirements.
The total seats available in a public dancehall shall not be less than the total number of patrons present at any one time.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-14)
Sec. 6-25. - Closing hours.
The closing hour of public dancehalls shall not be later than 2:00 a.m. and they shall remain closed until 12:00 p.m. noon of the following day.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-15)
Sec. 6-26. - Operator not to permit intoxicated or disorderly persons on premises.
It shall be unlawful for any person operating a public dancehall in the city to suffer or permit any person under the influence of alcoholic beverages or any disorderly person to enter or remain in such dancehall, whether participating in the dancing or not.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-16)
Sec. 6-27. - Minors prohibited if alcoholic beverages sold; exception.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any minor to enter, be or remain in any public dancehall in the city if alcoholic beverages are consumed or are sold or dispensed for consumption therein, except on business, and when on business, such minor shall be required to depart therefrom as soon as his or her business is transacted.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for the owner, operator or person in charge of any public dancehall in the city where alcoholic beverages are consumed or are sold or dispensed for consumption therein, to allow or permit any minor to enter, be or remain in any such public dancehall or place, except on business, and when on business, such minor shall be required to depart therefrom as soon as his or her business has been transacted.
(c)
The presence of any minor in any public dancehall for the purpose of dancing or watching dancing shall not be considered as being therein on business.
(d)
The term "alcoholic beverage," when used in this section, shall have the meaning prescribed by section 3-1-2 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated (O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2).
(e)
The owner, operator or person in charge of any such public dance hall shall have and keep posted conspicuously at the entrance or entrances thereof a sign or signs in bold letters, not less than two inches in height, reading: "NO MINORS ALLOWED."
(Code 1986, § 3.5-17)
Sec. 6-28. - Lewd, indecent, etc., dances, etc.
It shall be unlawful for any person operating a public dance hall in the city to suffer or permit in such dancehall any lewd, indecent or immoral dance, posture, performance or conduct by any person.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-18)
Secs. 6-29—6-59. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PERMIT

Sec. 6-60. - Required.
(a)
No person shall operate a public dancehall in the city without having first obtained a permit issued pursuant to this division.
(b)
No license shall be granted by the city for the operation of a public dancehall in the city until the permit required by this section has been issued.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-24)
Sec. 6-61. - Application.
Any person desiring to obtain a permit for the operation of a public dancehall shall make written application therefor to the board of commissioners. Such application shall be filed with the city clerk and shall contain the following information:
(1)
The location of the proposed dancehall.
(2)
The names and addresses of all persons who are owners, operators or managers of the dance hall, together with the names and addresses of all person having a financial interest in the dancehall, including stockholders, lien holders or partners.
(3)
If the owner or operator is a corporation, the names and addresses of the true or equitable owners of the stock of such corporation.
(4)
A detailed statement of the facilities to be provided, including type of food or beverages to be offered, the seating capacity of the dancehall and the amount of off-street parking space available for patrons.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-25)
Sec. 6-62. - Fee.
The fee for a permit required by this division shall be such as is prescribed, from time to time, by the board of commissioners.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-26)
Sec. 6-63. - Referral of application to city manager.
The city clerk is hereby authorized and directed to transmit directly to the city manager, for investigation and report to the board at a subsequent meeting, each application for a permit under this division.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-27)
Sec. 6-64. - Board hearing on application; issuance or refusal.
Upon the filing of an application for a public dancehall permit, and upon receipt of the report referred to in this division, the board of commissioners may hear statements and receive evidence as to the suitability of the location of the proposed public dancehall, preference being given to ground floor locations, as to the suitability and adequacy of the facilities, and as to the fitness and financial responsibility of the persons who will own, conduct or manage the same. The board shall, by resolution upon a record vote, grant or refuse the permit; provided, however, that no such permit shall be issued unless it shall appear that all persons having a financial interest in or connected with the management or operation of the dancehall, including stockholders or lien holders, are of good reputation and are otherwise acceptable to the council.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-28)
Sec. 6-65. - Permit subject to article provisions.
Every permit to operate a public dancehall in the city shall be subject to the conditions and restrictions of this division
(Code 1986, § 3.5-29)
Sec. 6-66. - Transfer.
A permit to operate a public dancehall shall not be transferable, without the approval of the board of commissioners.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-30)
Sec. 6-67. - Revocation.
Upon the violation of any provision of this article, the board of commissioners shall have the right, in addition to any other remedies allowed by law, to revoke the permit for the operation of the public dance hall involved in such violation, after due hearing and upon not less than five days' notice in writing to the holder of the permit. Such notice shall be sent by registered mail to the address given by the holder of the permit when applying for the permit.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-31)

ARTICLE III. - COMMERCIAL RECREATION CENTERS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 6-93. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Commercial recreation center or center means any establishment where foosball, table tennis, shuffleboard or any other game of recreation or amusement is displayed for public patronage, including electronic game machines or devices, pinball machines and the like, where the insertion of a coin, slug or token or the payment of a fee is required and where two or more such tables or game machines or devices are used or displayed for use, irregardless of other additional goods or services which might be available on the premises.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-42; Ord. No. 409, 4-7-1992)
Sec. 6-94. - Management.
Every center shall at all times be under management and control by a person at least 18 years of age who shall be on the premises of the center at all times during the hours of operation.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-43)
Sec. 6-95. - Ground floor location required; window space.
Every center shall be located on the ground level floor of the building in which it is situated and shall have a window or windows with a total of not less than 100 square feet of glass which shall not be covered or otherwise obstructed during hours of business. This section shall not apply to centers lawfully operating prior to the effective date of this Code.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-44)
Sec. 6-96. - Partitioned areas prohibited; exceptions.
No center shall have partitioned or closed off areas other than those for toilet facilities, office space and storerooms.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-45)
Sec. 6-97. - Illumination.
Every center shall be illuminated throughout its interior at a level of 40 footcandles.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-46)
Sec. 6-98. - Rest room facilities.
Every center shall provide separate rest room facilities for both sexes.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-47)
Sec. 6-99. - Off-street parking facilities.
Every center shall be provided with off-street parking facilities as required by the zoning ordinance.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-48)
Sec. 6-100. - Prohibited hours for minors; exceptions.
No person under the age of 18 years shall be allowed to remain in any center between the hours of 12:01 a.m. and 5:00 a.m., unless accompanied by his or her parent or guardian.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-49)
Sec. 6-101. - Sale of beer.
Commercial recreation centers shall be allowed to apply for a license to sell beer.
(Ord. No. 522, § 3.5-50, 6-14-2005)
Sec. 6-102. - Age requirements of customers where beer is sold.
No commercial recreation center shall allow anyone under 16 years of age on the premises at any time if said center has a license to sell beer. It shall be the responsibility of the owner to require proof of age of anyone seeking entrance to the premises. Allowing persons under age 16 to be on the premises shall constitute a violation of this article, which is punishable according to the general penalties as described in section 1-12 and suspension or revocation of the license to sell beer by the city. Violation of this article shall be grounds to revoke the permit to operate a commercial recreation center.
(Ord. No. 522, § 3.5-51, 6-14-2005)
Sec. 6-103. - Patrons between 16 and 21 years of age.
(a)
Each patron of a commercial recreation site that has a license to sell beer shall affix an orange wrist band to the left wrist of each patron between 16 and 21 years of age as the patron enters the premises. The wrist band shall state that the wearer is under 21 years of age and not authorized to purchase any alcoholic beverages. The wrist band shall be in a form approved by the chief of police of the city.
(b)
It shall be the responsibility of the owner to ensure that each patron between the ages of 16 and 21 years of age does not remove his or her wrist band while on the premises. Failure to do so is a violation of this article and subjects the owner to punishment according to the general penalties described in section 1-12 and revocation or suspension of the license to sell beer. Violation of this article shall also be grounds to suspend or revoke the permit to operate a commercial recreation center.
(c)
Failure of the owner to issue a wrist band to patrons 16 to 21 year of age shall be a violation of this article and subject the owner to punishment according to the general penalties as described in section 1-12 and the revocation or suspension of the license to sell beer by the city. Violation of this article shall also be grounds to revoke or suspend the permit to operate a commercial recreation center.
(d)
Any person between the ages of 16 and 21 years of age who is on the premises of a commercial recreation center shall wear an orange wrist band the entire time that said person is on the premises. Failure to wear or removing while on the premises said wrist band shall constitute a violation of this article and subject the offender to punishment according to the general penalties described in section 1-12. Covering up or hiding in some fashion the wrist band by an individual so as to conceal the same shall be a violation of this article and shall subject the offender to punishment according to the general penalties described in section 1-12
(e)
The owner of a commercial recreation center shall have the right to deny entrance on the premises to any person who fails to present a picture identification establishing identity and age of the person or who refuses to wear the required orange wrist band. The owner of a commercial recreation center shall have the right to remove from the premises any person who removes or conceals the orange wrist band after being admitted to the premises.
(Ord. No. 522, § 3.5-52, 6-14-2005)

DIVISION 2. - PERMIT

Sec. 6-135. - Required.
No person shall operate a commercial recreation center within the city, without first obtaining a permit from the city manager or his or her authorized representative.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-60)
Sec. 6-136. - Filing and contents of application.
Application for a permit required by this division shall be filed in the office of the city manager and shall contain the following information:
(1)
The name, address, age, social security number, race and sex of the applicant, date and place of his or her birth and, where pertinent, the relationship of the applicant to any company, corporation or other entity which has any interest in the ownership or management of the center.
(2)
Prior criminal convictions, excluding traffic offenses, of the applicant, if any.
(3)
Prospective location of the center.
(4)
Maximum number of patrons the center will accommodate.
(5)
A list of all employees who are expected to serve in a managerial capacity in the center.
(6)
A certification by the applicant that all requirements of this article have been complied with.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-61)
Sec. 6-137. - Fee.
Each application for a permit under this division shall be accompanied by a fee in such amount as is prescribed by the board of commissioners.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-62)
Sec. 6-138. - Investigation of applicant and proposed center; grant or denial.
(a)
Upon receipt of an application for a permit under this division, the city manager shall immediately cause an investigation to be made concerning the qualifications of the applicant and whether the proposed center is in compliance with this article. The city manager shall grant or deny such permit and shall notify the applicant of his or her action within 30 days of the filing of the application. If the city manager denies such permit, he or she shall state, in writing, his or her reasons for such action. The following factors shall constitute grounds for denial of the permit:
(1)
A finding by the city manager that the applicant has falsified his or her application in a material manner.
(2)
A finding by the city manager that the applicant or any of his or her designated managerial employees has a criminal record which reveals any convictions for violations of any drug control laws or any other pattern of criminal misconduct involving moral turpitude.
(3)
A finding by the city manager that the applicant consistently fails to cooperate with law enforcement personnel in the exercise of their official duties.
(4)
A finding by the city manager that the application is not properly completed as required in this division.
(5)
A finding by the city manager that the proposed center fails to comply with the provisions of this article.
(b)
No other factors shall be sufficient grounds for denial of a permit applied for under this division.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-63)
Sec. 6-139. - Not assignable; void if center not operated for 60 days.
No permit issued under this division shall be assignable in any manner whatsoever. When the permittee shall cease to operate the center for a period of 60 days, the permit shall be void.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-64)
Sec. 6-140. - Revocation or suspension.
(a)
The city manager may revoke or suspend, for a period of 30 days, the permit for the operation of a commercial recreation center if he or she finds:
(1)
That the premises have been declared a common nuisance by a court of competent jurisdiction.
(2)
That the permittee has consistently failed to operate the center in an orderly manner.
(3)
That the permittee has failed to take prompt and vigorous action to prevent violations of criminal statutes occurring on the premises which are known or should have been known to the permittee.
(4)
That circumstances exist which would have justified a denial of such permit at the time of its issuance.
(5)
That the permittee has failed to comply with the provisions of this article.
(b)
A permittee shall be entitled to a hearing before the city manager before his or her permit is revoked or suspended under this section. The city manager shall give the permittee 30 days' notice of his or her intention to revoke or suspend such permit along with his or her grounds for revocation or suspension by certified mail, return receipt requested. The hearing shall be held not more than 20 days after giving of the notice.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-65)
Sec. 6-141. - Permanent revocation.
(a)
The city manager may recommend to the board of commissioners that a permit for the operation of a commercial recreation center be suspended in excess of 30 days or permanently if he or she finds one or more of the following and, if in the opinion of the city manager, a revocation or suspension in excess of 30 days is recommended or if other action permitted under this section in addition to or in lieu of suspension or revocation is recommended:
(1)
That the premises have been declared a common nuisance by a court of competent jurisdiction.
(2)
That the permittee has consistently failed to operate the center in an orderly manner.
(3)
That the permittee has failed to take prompt and vigorous action to prevent violations of criminal statutes occurring on the premises or immediately adjacent to the premises which are known or should have been known to the permittee.
(4)
That circumstances exist which would have justified a denial of such permit at the time of its issuance.
(5)
That the permittee has failed to comply with any provisions of this article or any other provisions of this Code.
(6)
That the operation of the commercial recreation center results in the premises being frequented by drug dealers and/or drug users and the operation results in a significant danger to passers-by or neighboring residences or businesses.
(b)
A permittee shall be entitled to a hearing before the board of commissioners before his or her permit is revoked or suspended under this section. The city manager shall give the permittee ten days' notice of the time and place of the hearing along with a copy of his or her recommendations to the board of commissioners, including the specific grounds for the recommendations, by certified mail, return receipt requested. The hearing shall be held not more than 20 days after the giving of the ten-day notice. The notice by certified mail shall be to the address upon the license issued by the city unless the permittee has filed with the city clerk a different address for the acceptance of notices under this section.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-66; Ord. No. 382, 2-6-1990)
Sec. 6-142. - Hearing before the board of commissioners.
(a)
The permittee shall be entitled to be represented by an attorney at a hearing before the board of commissioners for the revocation or suspension of his or her business permit or license. The city shall be represented by the city attorney at the hearing, who shall present evidence supporting the recommendation of the city manager for the board of commissioners' consideration. The permittee shall be allowed at the close of the city's evidence to offer any evidence relevant to the position of the permittee. The hearing shall be conducted informally, and any objections or rulings or other matters shall be made by the chairperson of the board of commissioners.
(b)
At the conclusion of the evidence, the board of commissioners shall make a decision regarding the revocation or suspension of the permittee's license; and upon a finding that one or more of the grounds for suspension or revocation provided for in section 6-141 exists, the board shall have the authority to do one or more of the following:
(1)
Suspend the license of the permittee to do business for a period of from one day to permanently or any period in between.
(2)
Require that additional lights be installed inside and/or outside of the premises at the sole and exclusive cost of the permittee in lieu of suspension or revocation to discourage disorderly or criminal conduct and to protect the public welfare.
(3)
Require a security guard to be present during certain hours of operation in order to maintain order upon the premises and to reduce danger to passers-by and adjoining businesses or residences in lieu of suspension or revocation.
(4)
Require that a fine up to the maximum amount allowed under state law be paid by the permittee for violation of section 6-141
(5)
Require that the permittee daily pick up and remove debris and other trash from outside of the premises prior to the normal business operational hours of adjoining businesses in order to ensure that the streets and sidewalks around the permittee's establishment are maintained in a clean and orderly fashion in lieu of suspension or revocation.
(6)
That the hours of operation of the permittee's amusement center be changed or reduced in lieu of suspension or revocation.
(7)
That the number of amusement devices located on the premises be limited.
(8)
Revoke the permittee's beer license if the permittee is licensed to sell beer. The board may place the permittee on probation for a specified period of time and require that any of the above be complied with by the permittee as a condition of probation.
(Code 1986, § 3.5-67; Ord. No. 382, 2-6-1990)
