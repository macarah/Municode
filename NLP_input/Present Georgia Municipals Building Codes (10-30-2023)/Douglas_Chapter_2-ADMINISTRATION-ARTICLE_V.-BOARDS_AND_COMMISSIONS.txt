DIVISION 2. - PARKS AND RECREATION COMMISSION

Sec. 2-619. - Established.
There is hereby established the parks and recreation commission for the purpose of advising the board of commissioners on administrative matters through the parks and recreation director and the city manager, and advising the city commission committee responsible for legislative matters concerning the commission's purposes.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-411; Ord. No. 07-26.01-99, 7-26-1999)
Sec. 2-620. - Membership; terms; appointment; organization; vacancies.
(a)
The parks and recreation commission shall consist of 20 citizens of the city, 18 years of age or older. The 21st member of the parks and recreation commission shall be the elected city commissioner appointed to the board by the mayor of the city and confirmed by the city commission.
(b)
Members shall serve without compensation for a term of three years or until their successors are appointed and qualified. Members completing their term are eligible for reappointment.
(c)
Appointments to the board shall be made by the mayor and approved by the board of commissioners by December 31 of each year.
(d)
Each year, immediately after appointments are made to the board, the board shall meet, reorganize and elect new officers.
(e)
Vacancies on the board occurring otherwise than by expiration of term shall be filled by the mayor and approved by the board of commissioners for the unexpired term.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-412; Ord. No. 07-26.01-99, 7-26-1999)
Sec. 2-621. - Authority to establish rules and regulations.
(a)
The parks and recreation commission is authorized to establish rules and regulations pertaining to facilities and programs under the direction of the department having jurisdiction.
(b)
Such rules and regulations shall relate to such subjects as the parks and recreation commission and its committees deem necessary and appropriate.
(c)
An official copy of such rules and regulations shall be on file in the administrative office of the community development department.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-413; Ord. No. 07-26.01-99, 7-26-1999)
Sec. 2-622. - Functions.
The parks and recreation commission shall perform the following functions:
(1)
Adopt bylaws for the proper performance of its functions. These bylaws may not conflict with this division.
(2)
Advise the board of commissioners in the development of a sound financial plan.
(3)
Advise the board of commissioners in the development of a variety of innovative and quality programs and a variety of facilities to meet the needs of all of the citizens of the city.
(4)
Assist the board of commissioners by keeping the public informed and educated as to the importance of and need for recreation programs, facilities and services.
(5)
Advise and aid the board of commissioners in maintaining an effective and efficient public relations program.
(6)
Advise the board of commissioners concerning public needs and desires for recreation programs, facilities and services.
(7)
Assist the board of commissioners in securing financial contributions and physical properties for the advancement of community services.
(8)
Set the fees for the use of city facilities and programs. Fee amounts shall be as set forth in the city's schedule of fees and charges.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-414; Ord. No. 07-26.01-99, 7-26-1999)
Sec. 2-623. - Deposit and use of funds.
All money received by the parks and recreation commission, from whatever source derived, shall be deposited with the finance officer of the city to the account of the parks and recreation department to be used for the financing of the city community services programs.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-415; Ord. No. 07-26.01-99, 7-26-1999)

DIVISION 3. - MAYOR'S YOUTH COUNCIL

Sec. 2-650. - Created.
There is created a board to be known as the city mayor's youth council, which shall consist of nine members, serving as an advisory youth council to the city commission. The youth members will be city residents who are actively enrolled in a public, private, or home school, in grades ninth through 12th, between the ages of 14 to 19. The youth members shall be appointed annually by the city commission and each member shall serve one four-year term, at which time the position shall be declared vacant. Unlike other boards, the term of the members will expire in August and be appointed in September or October of each year. The commissioners will attend board meetings and, along with staff, coordinate activities.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-421; Ord. No. 07242017(1) , § 3.11, 7-24-2017)
Sec. 2-651. - Election of officers.
The youth council shall elect a chairperson to conduct meetings and a vice-chairperson to conduct meetings in the absence of the chairperson. Elections shall be held at the first regular meeting after annual appointments are made by the city commission.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-422; Ord. No. 07242017(1) , § 3.11, 7-24-2017)
Sec. 2-652. - Vacancies.
Irregular vacancies on the youth council shall be filled as they occur and regular vacancies shall be filled by appointment in September or October of each year.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-243; Ord. No. 07242017(1) , § 3.11, 7-24-2017)
Sec. 2-653. - Meetings; records.
The youth council shall hold at least one regular meeting per month, with the option to meet more often as needed or desired. Public record of such meetings shall be kept by the office of the city clerk. All meetings shall be public.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-424; Ord. No. 07242017(1) , § 3.11, 7-24-2017)
Sec. 2-654. - Absences from meetings.
Absences from three consecutive regular meetings of the youth council shall cause a member to be removed from his seat, unless such absence is excused by a majority vote of the board, with such excuse duty entered upon its minutes.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-425; Ord. No. 07242017(1) , § 3.11, 7-24-2017)
Sec. 2-655. - Duties and responsibilities.
The youth council shall have the duty and responsibility to:
(1)
Evaluate and review problems facing youth in the city.
(2)
Facilitate neighborhood meetings with youth to discuss problems, needs, and recommendations for community improvements.
(3)
Meet regularly with the mayor and city commission to share ideas and discuss issues, concerns, and needed improvements.
(4)
Attend city commission meetings and participate in vision and goal sessions.
(5)
Present recommended projects and programs to the city commission and city manager of public projects and programs.
(6)
Assist in planning youth/recreation activities.
(7)
Evaluate and advise the city commission and/or city manager on issues forwarded to the city youth council for advice.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-426; Ord. No. 07242017(1) , § 3.11, 7-24-2017)
