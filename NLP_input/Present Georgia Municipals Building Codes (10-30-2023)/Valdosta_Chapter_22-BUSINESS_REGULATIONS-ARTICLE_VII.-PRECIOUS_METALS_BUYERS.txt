ARTICLE VII. - PRECIOUS METALS BUYERS[6]

Sec. 22-291. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Gold and silver buyers means any person engaged in the business of purchasing or otherwise dealing in gold or silver or other precious metals of any kind or description, from members of the general public within the corporate limits of the city. Included in this definition are buyers of secondhand jewelry and gems. Isolated transactions between persons not engaged in the business are not covered by the terms of this article.
(Code 1976, § 7-1191; Ord. No. 80-55, § 1, 12-10-1980)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 22-292. - Findings.
Engaging in the business of buying gold, silver, other precious metals, precious gems, or jewelry within the corporate limits of the city is declared to affect the public interest because of the opportunity it affords for the disposal of stolen property.
(Code 1976, § 7-1192; Ord. No. 80-55, § 2, 12-10-1980)
Sec. 22-293. - Compliance with article required.
It is unlawful and punishable as provided in section 1-11 to engage in the business of buying precious metals, precious gems or jewelry from members of the general public within the corporate limits of the city without first complying with the registration and other provisions of this article and obtaining a registration as provided in this article. Each day such offense occurs or continues shall constitute a separate offense.
(Code 1976, § 7-1193; Ord. No. 80-55, § 3, 12-10-1980)
Sec. 22-294. - Prerequisite to advertising.
No person covered by this article shall advertise, either in the news media, or portable signs, or in any other manner, the buying, selling, trading or otherwise dealing in precious metals, precious gems or jewelry until he has complied with the provisions of this article. The violation of this section shall be unlawful and punishable as provided in section 1-11.
(Code 1976, § 7-1194; Ord. No. 80-55, § 4, 12-10-1980)
Sec. 22-295. - Registration with chief of police required.
All persons engaging in the business of buying precious metals, precious gems or jewelry shall register with the chief of police on a form to be provided by the chief of police.
(Code 1976, § 7-1195; Ord. No. 80-55, § 5, 12-10-1980)
Sec. 22-296. - Information required.
All applicants for registration under the provisions of this article shall furnish, in addition to such other information as the chief of police shall require, the following information:
(1)
Name of the applicant;
(2)
Permanent address and local address of the applicant;
(3)
If the applicant is a partnership, the name and address of each member of the partnership;
(4)
If the applicant is a corporation, the name and address of each stockholder, each officer and each director of the corporation;
(5)
The name and address of each employee of the applicant who is actively engaged in the performance of such services; and
(6)
If a resident, the length of time the applicant has been a resident of the state and the city.
(Code 1976, § 7-1196; Ord. No. 80-55, § 6, 12-10-1980)
Sec. 22-297. - Certain persons ineligible.
No person shall engage in the business or activity for which registration under this article is required, or shall be a member of a partnership, or an officer, stockholder or director in a corporation engaged in such business, who shall have been convicted of a felony or a misdemeanor involving moral turpitude or theft.
(Code 1976, § 7-1197; Ord. No. 80-55, § 7, 12-10-1980)
Sec. 22-298. - Bond.
Every person engaging in the business of buying precious metals, precious gems or jewelry shall file with the chief of police for the city a surety bond executed by such applicant secured by a corporate surety company, authorized to do business in the state, in an amount as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk, conditioned upon compliance with the provisions of this article, and upon the prompt report of all suspicious persons dealing with the obligor, and of stolen property or property suspected of being stolen, and for the returning to the chief of police or to the true and lawful owner of any such property of making payment. The bond shall be taken in the name of the city, and the city or any person injured by the breach of the bond may bring an action on the bond in his own name to recover damages.
(Code 1976, § 7-1198; Ord. No. 80-55, § 8, 12-10-1980)
Sec. 22-299. - Investigation of applicant; issuance of registration.
Within five days after receipt of an application as provided for in this article, the chief of police shall cause an investigation to be made of the applicant and his proposed operation. If upon investigation the chief of police shall find that the applicant is of good moral character and has never been convicted of a felony or a misdemeanor involving moral turpitude, and has met all of the requirements of this article, he shall approve the issuance of a registration to the applicant. The registration shall be issued, and the registration tax provided by this article shall be paid.
(Code 1976, § 7-1199; Ord. No. 80-55, § 9, 12-10-1980)
Sec. 22-300. - Responsibility of applicant to file notice of registration approval with law enforcement agencies.
If the registration required by the provisions of this article is approved, the applicant shall file a notice with all law enforcement agencies of the county and the city within 15 days after approval.
(Code 1976, § 7-1200; Ord. No. 80-55, § 10, 12-10-1980)
Sec. 22-301. - Register to be kept.
Every registrant under this article shall keep a register upon which the registrant shall record the name, address, social security number or driver's license number, a brief description of the person, and other identification, of every person from whom the registrant purchases any precious metals, precious gems or jewelry, together with the date and time of day of the purchase, a description of each article purchased, including the name, make and serial or other identifying number or mark, and the amount paid. Such entry shall be made at the time the transaction occurs. Before buying any precious metals, precious gems or jewelry, the registrant shall require positive identification from the seller, either from a driver's license or other document.
(Code 1976, § 7-1201; Ord. No. 80-55, § 11, 12-10-1980)
Sec. 22-302. - Daily reports.
Each registrant under this article shall file electronic daily written reports by 5:00 p.m. with the chief of police, showing the name and description of the persons from whom precious metals, precious gems or jewelry were purchased by the licensee during the preceding 24 hours, and a description in detail of the nature, type and quantity of the precious metals, precious gems or jewelry. It is the duty of the chief of police to obtain such reports promptly each day. It shall be unlawful, and an offense punishable as provided in section 1-11, for any registrant to fail or refuse to make such daily report in the manner described by this section; and such failure shall be grounds for the revocation of the registration.
(Code 1976, § 7-1202; Ord. No. 80-55, § 12, 12-10-1980)
Sec. 22-303. - Holding period.
No articles of personal property made of gold, silver or other precious metals, or precious gems or jewelry shall be sold, melted, altered or otherwise disposed of by any secondhand dealer until three business days have elapsed from the time the chief of police has been given a copy or transcript of the register in which such articles of personal property made of gold, silver, platinum or other precious metals or gems or jewelry are entered; and such articles shall not be transferred from within the city limits during a ten-day period of time.
(Code 1976, § 7-1203; Ord. No. 80-55, § 13, 12-10-1980)
Sec. 22-304. - Registrations nontransferable.
Registrations issued under this article shall not be transferred.
(Code 1976, § 7-1204; Ord. No. 80-55, § 14, 12-10-1980)
Sec. 22-305. - Registrant to surrender certain items.
If it is determined that any item covered by this article is the subject of any theft, embezzlement or other crime, the registrant shall surrender the item upon demand to the owner or the chief of police. Disposition of property will be in accordance with O.C.G.A. title 17.
(Code 1976, § 7-1205; Ord. No. 80-55, § 15, 12-10-1980)
Sec. 22-306. - Purchases from minors.
It shall be unlawful and punishable as provided in section 1-11 for any person engaged in the business of buying precious metals, precious gems or jewelry to purchase any of such items from a person less than 18 years of age.
(Code 1976, § 7-1206; Ord. No. 80-55, § 16, 12-10-1980)
Sec. 22-307. - Suspension or revocation of registrations.
Registrations issued pursuant to this article shall be subject to revocation or suspension by the city manager, upon recommendation of the chief of police, for the violation of or the failure to comply with any provision of this article or for the violation of any provision of law, after reasonable notice and an opportunity to be heard has been given the registrant. The city manager shall immediately notify any registrant of such suspension or revocation.
(Code 1976, § 7-1207; Ord. No. 80-55, § 17, 12-10-1980)
