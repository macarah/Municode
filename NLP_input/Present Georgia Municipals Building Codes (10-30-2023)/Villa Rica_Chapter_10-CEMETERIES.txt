ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 10-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
City means the City of Villa Rica, Georgia, a municipality.
City cemetery means the area of real property belonging to the city through purchase or gift, and set aside for the purpose of interment of the remains of deceased persons.
Council or city council means the elected governing body including the mayor and all councilmembers collectively.
Grave or grave plot or grave site means the deeded portion of property measuring five feet by ten feet (one-person plot) or ten feet by ten feet (two-person plot) set aside for the specific purpose of receiving the remains of a deceased person.
Interment means the burial of human remains but shall not include the opening and closing of a grave space, crypt or niche; nor the installation of a vault.
Plot owner is the purchaser of the grave plot, heirs to that purchaser, or executor of deceased's estate. Also referred to as "grave owner" or "lot owner."
Vault means an enclosure into which a casket is placed and includes, but is not limited to, containers made of concrete, steel, fiberglass, copper or other metals, sectional concrete enclosures, or crypts.
Villa Rica Cemetery Commission is a committee appointed by the mayor to review and make recommendations regarding the city cemeteries and other public cemeteries within the City of Villa Rica.
(Ord. No. 09-13-CCO, § 7-1, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 10-2. - Location of city cemeteries.
The city owns two properties used as cemeteries. The term "city cemeteries" shall refer to both locations unless otherwise specified herein.
(1)
Hillcrest Cemetery, located at Hwy. 78 and Hillcrest Drive, has 494 sites with two to eight grave sites per lot on 10.79 acres.
(2)
Garden of Rest Cemetery, located on Brooks Street between Harper and Atlanta Streets, has 36 double-plots for a total of 72 grave sites on a portion of a seven acre parcel.
(Ord. No. 09-13-CCO, § 7-2, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 10-3. - Ownership of city cemeteries.
(a)
Control by city. The city shall have control of all city cemeteries.
(b)
Council to set fees. The fees to be paid for use of and activities in the cemetery shall be as prescribed by the council and set forth in the schedule of fees and charges.
(c)
Records. A cemetery coordinator, appointed by the city manager, shall maintain written or electronic records of lot purchases, burials, and any other pertinent information necessary by state law, council action, and accepted practice.
(Ord. No. 09-13-CCO, § 7-3, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 10-4. - Cemetery commission.
The mayor of the city may appoint up to seven members of the community to serve as the cemetery commission. The city manager and cemetery coordinator shall serve as ex officio members. The duties of the cemetery commission are to recommend the setting of rates, fees and ordinance amendments for the benefit of the city and the community and any other duties or recommendations necessary to improve the final resting places of the deceased.
(Ord. No. 09-13-CCO, § 7-4, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 10-5. - General maintenance.
(a)
The general care provided by the city shall mean cutting of grass at reasonable intervals, raking and cleaning of the grounds, and the pruning of shrubs and trees that may be placed by the city; and general preservation of the lots and grounds, walks, roadways, boundaries and structures, to the end that such grounds shall remain and be reasonably cared for as cemetery grounds forever.
(b)
General care provided by the city shall in no case mean the maintenance, repair or replacement of any private memorial, tomb, or mausoleum placed or erected upon lots; nor the doing of any special or unusual work; nor does it mean the reconstruction of any marble or granite work on any section or plot caused by the elements, an act of God, common enemy, thieves, vandals, malicious mischief, explosions, unavoidable accidents, invasion, insurrection, riot, or otherwise not by the order of any court authority, whether the damage is direct or collateral.
(Ord. No. 09-13-CCO, § 7-5, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 10-6. - Driving in cemetery.
It shall be unlawful to operate or drive any motor vehicle in the city cemeteries, except on the designated streets and thoroughfares. It shall be illegal for any person to drive any motor vehicle across or over any concrete walls or markers in the city cemeteries.
(Ord. No. 09-13-CCO, § 7-6, 12-1-2009)
Secs. 10-7—10-20. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - RULES AND REGULATIONS

Sec. 10-21. - Lot sales.
Sale of any grave site will be transacted by the cemetery coordinator or as assigned by the city manager. Evidence of ownership will be provided by the city to the purchaser. It is the responsibility of the purchaser to permanently maintain the original evidence of ownership and for any recording of said document. The city reserves a perpetual easement to go with any and all burial plots as may be necessary for maintenance and to fulfill other duties and rights set forth in this article.
(Ord. No. 09-13-CCO, § 7-21, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 10-22. - Fees.
(a)
The cemetery coordinator shall collect fees and issue receipts, permits, and any other documents required. Permits must be obtained 12 hours prior to the planned activity. In the event of an emergency occurring after business hours, the police department sergeant on duty will be contacted and, at his discretion, issue emergency permit. A copy of the emergency permit will be forwarded to the cemetery coordinator the next business day for recording.
(b)
A schedule of current fees is available at city hall.
(Ord. No. 09-13-CCO, § 7-22, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 10-23. - Records.
(a)
The cemetery coordinator shall maintain maps of all cemetery lots with owners designated on those sold and provide a current copy of same to the department.
(b)
The cemetery coordinator will keep a written or electronic record of all burials in the city cemetery, giving the names, nativity, age, and gender of each person.
(c)
The cemetery coordinator will also keep a record of the number of lots in which persons are interred and if the bodies are removed to any other lot, will make a record of the fact. This record shall be subject to inspection of persons desiring to see such book.
(d)
No cemetery lots shall be reserved except by sale in any section of the cemetery. No lots will be sold in sections not opened by the city.
(e)
A cemetery lot price schedule will be on file in the office of the city clerk in the schedule of fees.
(Ord. No. 09-13-CCO, § 7-23, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 10-24. - Burial notification.
(a)
The grave plot owner shall notify the cemetery coordinator no less than 12 hours before a planned burial and present a copy of the evidence of ownership or otherwise showing entitlement to the plot. The owner shall also provide information on the deceased as to name, date of death, nativity, age, gender, and last residence address, if known.
(b)
The city shall erect signs near all entrances to city cemeteries notifying the public of the requirement to contact the city prior to burial, a phone number for contact, the hours the number is staffed and an emergency number for after hours.
(Ord. No. 09-13-CCO, § 7-24, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 10-25. - Grave opening, closing and maintenance.
(a)
Contract for service. The plot owner or funeral director may contract for grave opening and closing with any grave service company with a valid business license/occupation tax certificate.
(b)
Opening and closing graves. No interment will be made in a city cemetery without written consent of the lot owner or burial rights holder and a city-issued interment permit or copy thereof. An application for an interment permit shall be made by 2:00 p.m. for next-day burials. All grave openings shall be according to the following:
(1)
Only approved persons or firms with a valid business license/occupation tax certificate may dig graves in the city cemeteries.
(2)
An approved persons or firms, before digging in the city cemeteries, must obtain an interment permit from the city or be in receipt of a copy of the city-issued permit from the family.
(c)
Depth. No grave shall be less than four feet to the bottom of the vault.
(d)
Maintenance following closing of grave.
(1)
At least two feet of dirt shall cover the top of the vault and shall be leveled off in a timely manner and a permanent grave site covering be made or placed on the grave.
(2)
The lot owner and grave service company are responsible for insuring that all excess dirt, rock and other debris from the burial is removed from the city cemeteries within 30 days of the date of burial.
(3)
Graves not leveled and cleared of dirt, rock and debris within 30 days, shall be leveled and cleared by the city; and, the lot owner or family shall be billed $25.00 per hour for the service.
(e)
General maintenance. General maintenance may be done on the lots by plot owners or their contractors at anytime the cemeteries are opened except while a burial is in progress.
(f)
Liability for damages and violations. The contractor shall assume responsibility for all damages attending the execution of any work in the cemetery and shall be held liable for any violations of the rules and regulations of the cemetery or the laws and ordinances of the city.
(g)
Duty to protect work, repair damage. The contractor shall provide adequate protection for his work and shall bear the cost of repairing or replacing any damage done by him, his workmen, or his equipment.
(h)
When work should stop. All work of any description shall cease while a funeral or interment is being conducted nearby, and trucks and workmen will withdraw to a reasonable distance from the service. All workmen employed by the contractors while within the cemetery are subject to all regulations of this article and the city.
(i)
Confining of equipment; observance of regulations. The contractor must confine his equipment and supplies to the owner's lot or an area agreed upon with the city. The contractor and any person employed by him shall observe all rules and regulations of the cemetery.
(Ord. No. 09-13-CCO, § 7-25, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 10-26. - Burials.
(a)
The city cemeteries will not accommodate burials of other than human remains.
(b)
No interment shall occur without a licensed funeral director and a city-issued permit.
(c)
Vaults.
(1)
Vaults are required for all burials.
(2)
Vaults must be a minimum of 38 inches wide.
(3)
Vaults must be made of concrete, fiberglass, steel, or an equally leak-proof substance. Wooden boxes shall not be used for vaults.
(4)
No grave shall be less than four feet to the bottom of the vault.
(d)
Cremation burial policy.
(1)
Burial of cremains shall follow section 10-24.
(2)
Cremation vaults are required and shall have a minimum of 24 inches of dirt covering the cremation vault top.
(3)
Burial of cremains are limited to no more than four per grave.
(Ord. No. 09-13-CCO, § 7-26, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 10-27. - Disinterment.
No disinterment shall be allowed, except as provided by state law. Any court order for disinterment must be brought before the Carroll County Coroner or Villa Rica Chief of Police or his assignees. The coroner or chief of police will notify the city manager or his assignee.
(Ord. No. 09-13-CCO, § 7-27, 12-1-2009)
State Law reference— Coroner's authority, O.C.G.A. § 45-16-45.
Sec. 10-28. - Erection of monuments, gravestones, and markers.
(a)
Grave headstones, markers and monuments must be inside the boundaries of the owner's lot by six inches on all sides.
(b)
Repair of damage to headstones, markers and monuments shall be the responsibility of the owner and be completed within 45 days of notification to the owner.
(c)
Each headstone and foot stone shall have a six-inch concrete base or footing, except in the Garden of Rest City Cemetery where foot stones shall be flush with grade.
(d)
Walls and lot enclosures.
(1)
Walls and lot enclosures, where permitted, shall be constructed in accordance with the specifications, rules and regulations prescribed by the city.
(2)
No walls or lot enclosures are allowed in the Garden of Rest City Cemetery.
(Ord. No. 09-13-CCO, § 7-28, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 10-29. - Aboveground vaults, crypts, or mausoleums.
(a)
Interments in mausoleums or tombs, either wholly or partially aboveground, are permitted, provided the following general conditions are met:
(1)
A sarcophagus with one or more crypts shall be considered as a tomb. A building having a vestibule, fitted with a bronze door or doors, and having one or more crypts, is a mausoleum.
(2)
Tombs and mausoleums shall be constructed:
a.
By a qualified, bonded mausoleum contractor using Best Management Practice (BMP) for the industry;
b.
With granite or equivalent exteriors; and
c.
Using precast concrete or equivalent for the interior.
(3)
Plans of the mausoleum or tomb and location on the plot are subject to the approval of the city building official.
a.
A request for a building permit to construct a mausoleum or tomb must be accompanied by a complete set of working plans which shall include front, side and rear elevations, longitudinal and cross section plan, together with foundation plans.
b.
The city reserves the right to request changes in the construction plans that do not meet the requirements set forth in specifications for mausoleums and tombs as may be adopted from time to time by the city and by BMP.
c.
The city shall refuse a permit for the construction of a mausoleum or tomb which it considers to be inferior in construction or if inferior materials are specified for its construction.
d.
Provisions for a satisfactory endowment for the care and maintenance of the tomb or mausoleum shall be provided to the city prior to the issuance of a building permit. The amount of the endowment or perpetual care fund shall be determined in each individual case by the city council, and will vary in accordance with the design, the material to be used and the cost of the mausoleum or tomb.
e.
Duplication of design is discouraged except under special conditions and may result in the rejection of a design which otherwise might be satisfactory.
(4)
Caskets to be placed in mausoleums or tombs shall be metal or metal-lined and the crypt shall be hermetically sealed immediately after the interment is made.
(5)
Upon the completion of a mausoleum, one key to each lock on or in the building must be deposited with the cemetery coordinator and remain permanently in possession of the city.
(Ord. No. 09-13-CCO, § 7-29, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 10-30. - Trees and shrubs.
(a)
Shrubs and plants may be planted within the confines of a grave plot. Trees are not permitted.
(b)
If trees or plants currently exist on a grave plot, the city has the right to remove them if they become unsightly or interfere with the maintenance or orderly operation of other plots.
(c)
The cemetery coordinator will make an attempt to notify the lot owner of record when maintenance needs are required.
(d)
If the city maintenance crew is required to perform necessary lot maintenance, the owner will be billed for the work at the city's cost.
(Ord. No. 09-13-CCO, § 7-30, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 10-31. - Maintenance—Plot owner responsibilities.
(a)
General maintenance. It is the responsibility of the plot owner and members of the family of the deceased to maintain the area of the plot described in the warranty deed. Maintenance which must be performed by the city due to owner neglect shall be charged to the plot owner at cost or as may be otherwise prescribed by the city council.
(b)
Placing of flowers, wreaths and other decorations. Lot owners may place flowers, wreaths, etc., on their plots at any time.
(c)
Weeds, debris. It shall be unlawful for the owner of any cemetery plot to allow such plot to grow up in weeds or tall grass or to become littered with debris of any kind.
(d)
Removal of trees or shrubs. If any trees or shrubs situated in any lot in any city cemetery shall by their roots or branches become detrimental to adjacent lots or avenues, or unsightly or inconvenient to passersby, it shall be the duty of the city manager or his agents, and they shall have the right, to enter such lot and remove the trees and shrubs, or such parts thereof as they shall determine to be detrimental, unsightly or inconvenient.
(e)
Access to cemetery. The plot owners and their families will be allowed access to the cemetery grounds at all reasonable times, observing the rules which are or may be adopted for the regulation of visitors. It shall be unlawful for any person to enter or be upon the grounds or premises of any city cemetery at any time after sundown and before sunup, without the consent of the appropriate city official.
(f)
Care of cut or planted materials. It is the responsibility of the plot owner and members of the family of the deceased to water and care for cut and potted flowers or plants so as to help keep them alive and fresh. Cut and potted flowers or plants will not be removed by the city so long as they are kept in proper condition to prevent being unsightly. However, planted materials which violate subsection (c) of this section shall be removed as prescribed.
(g)
Artificial floral material. Florals of artificial materials shall be removed when they become faded, weather-beaten, or otherwise unsightly and objectionable to the general appearance of the cemetery.
(Ord. No. 09-13-CCO, § 7-31, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 10-32. - Same—City responsibilities.
(a)
General maintenance. Parks and recreation department work crews, under the direction of the deputy city manager—utilities, shall cut grass, trim, rake and otherwise clean the grounds at reasonable intervals, including the pruning of shrubs and trees that may be placed by the city and other duties which promote general preservation of the lots and grounds, walks, roadways, boundaries and structures to the end that such grounds shall remain and be reasonably cared for as cemetery grounds forever.
(b)
Plans for grounds. The city council, from time to time, may lay out or alter such avenues or walks, or make such rules and regulations for the government of the ground as they may deem requisite or proper to promote the general objects of the cemetery.
(c)
Clearing or cleaning of vandalism. If lot arrangements are vandalized, the cemetery coordinator may immediately clean the lot without notifying the owner.
(d)
Removal of improper objects. If any memorial, bench, seat, vase, urn, or any structure or any item whatever, or any inscription placed upon any lot of the old section of the cemetery, is determined by the council to be improper, offensive, or injurious to the appearance of surrounding lots or grounds, then the city shall have the right and duty to remove such item.
(Ord. No. 09-13-CCO, § 7-32, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 10-33. - Cemetery hours.
The hours for the city cemeteries will be established by the city council and posted at the main entrance to each cemetery.
(Ord. No. 09-13-CCO, § 7-33, 12-1-2009)
Sec. 10-34. - Burial of paupers.
The burial of paupers must be approved by the mayor and council or their assigned representatives. At the request of the assigned representative, the party requesting the burial of the deceased shall disclose pertinent information that is related to the burial.
(Ord. No. 09-13-CCO, § 7-34, 12-1-2009)
State Law reference— Burial of paupers, O.C.G.A. § 31-21-20 et seq.
Sec. 10-35. - Vandalism.
It shall be unlawful to mutilate or deface any portion of a grave in the city cemetery. Anyone convicted of this offense in the municipal court may be sentenced within the maximum sentencing guidelines of state law and the city Charter.
(Ord. No. 09-13-CCO, § 7-35, 12-1-2009)
