Chapter 13 - PARKS AND RECREATION

Sec. 13-1. - Advisory recreation board—Created.
There is hereby created for the city an advisory recreation board.
(Code 1963, § 17-16)
Sec. 13-2. - Members; appointment, term, filling of vacancies.
(a)
The advisory recreation board shall be appointed by the mayor and shall consist of six (6) prominent citizens of the city. The term of office of each citizen member shall be five (5) years.
(b)
Vacancies occurring other than by expiration of term shall be filled by another appointment.
(c)
Absences of members shall be noted in the minutes of the appropriate meetings as being either excused or unexcused. All absences shall be considered unexcused unless the absent member, prior to the applicable meeting, notifies either the chairman of the board or the secretary of the city manager that he or shell will be unable to attend the meeting. In the event that a member accumulates three (3) unexcused absences from consecutive regularly scheduled meetings, the secretary of the board shall notify the city manager of such situation. Upon receipt of such notice, the city manager shall issue a letter to the member and to the mayor and council. Upon the city manager's issuance of such letter, the member shall be deemed to have resigned and vacated his or her position on the advisory recreation board.
(Code 1963, § 17-17; Ord. No. 2002-14, § 1, 4-1-02)
Sec. 13-3. - Same—Function; purchases.
(a)
The advisory recreation board shall generally advise and recommend overall procedures and plans in the administration of the recreation program of the city in conformity with the laws and ordinances of the city, and said procedures and plans shall be followed by the recreation director unless otherwise directed by the governing body.
(b)
Said board shall approve all purchases made by the recreation department; provided however, that one member of said board and the recreation director shall be privileged to make purchases not to exceed one hundred dollars ($100.00) at any one time.
(Code 1963, § 17-18)
Sec. 13-4. - Same—Recreation budget, recommendation.
The advisory recreation board shall recommend the annual budget for the recreation department of the city.
(Code 1963, § 17-19)
Sec. 13-5. - Same—Reports; recommendations.
The advisory recreation board may from time to time make such recommendations as it may deem proper relative to recreation activities within the city to the governing body and shall make an annual report to the governing body, and such other reports as may be requested.
(Code 1963, § 17-20)
Sec. 13-6. - Use of parks.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to be in any public or public recreation grounds of the city between the hours of 9:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m. unless such person is engaged in activities specifically authorized in writing by the Department of Recreation and Cultural Arts or the mayor and council.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person who is not an employee, officer or agent of the city or a resident of the city to be in any public park or public recreation grounds of the city except under the following circumstances:
(1)
Such person is engaged in activities specifically authorized in writing by the department of recreation and cultural arts or the mayor and council;
(2)
Such person is accompanied by at least one (1) employee or resident of the city, provided that no two (2) or more such persons may be accompanied by the same employee or resident;
(3)
Where the general public is invited to an event through an advertisement placed by the city with a newspaper, magazine, radio station, or television station, or through signs erected by the city that do not state that the event is limited to city residents and/or employees; or
(4)
Such person is attending an organized event previously approved in writing by the department of recreation and cultural arts, in which case the requirement of subsection (2) above shall not apply.
(c)
It shall be unlawful to host or attend any organized event in any public park or public recreation grounds of the city without the prior written approval of the department of recreation and cultural arts. Requests for organized events may only be made by city residents. The department of recreation and cultural arts may refuse any request for such approval based on prior actions of the person or entity making the request, or for any other lawful reason. The mayor and council may by resolution from time to time set fees for organized events. For the purposes of this subsection, the term "organized event" shall mean any activity sponsored by any person or any formal or informal entity, other than the department of recreation and cultural arts, where at least ten (10) persons are present, for a common purpose or event, at a public park or recreation grounds of the city.
(d)
For the purposes of this section, "public park or recreation grounds of the city" shall mean:
(1)
Barrett Park: Rugby Ave. and West Walker;
(2)
Bill Badgett Stadium;
(3)
Bill Evans Field: College Street;
(4)
Brannon Memorial Park: Charlestown Drive;
(5)
Richard D. Zupp Park: Temple and Hawthorne Ave.;
(6)
Charles E. Phillips Park: Riverdale Road;
(7)
Subrenia M. Willis Park: Camp Creek Parkway.
(Code 1963, § 17-3(c); Ord. No. 2002-39, § 1, 9-3-02; Ord. No. 2019-07, § 1, 5-20-19)
Sec. 13-7. - Use of motor vehicles.
It shall be unlawful for any person in any public park or public recreation grounds of the city to park or drive a motor vehicle of any kind off the driveways or upon the grass of said parks or grounds. This shall not apply to any person who parks a motor vehicle in any area set aside for parking.
(Code 1963, § 17-3(b))
Editor's note— Ord. No. 2006-28, § 3, adopted Sept. 18, 2006, repealed former § 13-7 in its entirety which pertained to littering, throwing missiles and damaging property in parks and derived from the Code of 1963. Said ordinance also consecutively renumbered the remaining sections of this chapter.
Sec. 13-8. - Nonresident use of tennis courts.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any nonresident of the city to use the tennis courts within the city that are under the control and supervision of the recreation department of the city.
(b)
This section shall not apply to a nonresident who has paid an approved nonresident recreation fee to the city and has evidence of the same on the person, nor shall this section apply to a nonresident who is playing as a guest of a citizen of the city who is present at the time.
(Code 1963, § 16-21; Ord. No. 2006-28, § 3, 9-18-06)
Editor's note— Formerly numbered as § 13-9.
Sec. 13-9. - City auditorium; smoking prohibited,
It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in smoking at any time in the city auditorium; provided no person shall be found guilty of a violation of this section without first having failed or refused to desist from smoking after being requested to do so by a police officer or some other official deputized by the governing body to enforce this section.
(Code 1963, § 17-1; Ord. No. 2006-28, § 3, 9-18-06)
Editor's note— Formerly numbered as § 13-10.
Sec. 13-10. - City lake; regulations.
(a)
Swimming, boating. It shall be unlawful for any person to swim or ride in any boat on the city lake on the city park property on the west side of the city.
(b)
Fishing: It shall be unlawful for any person to fish in any manner in, or to take fish from, said city lake.
(c)
Golf: It shall be unlawful for any person except the owner or the owner's agent, or a duly authorized employee of the city, to remove any golf balls from the city lake.
(Code 1963, § 17-2; Ord. No. 2006-28, § 3, 9-18-06)
Editor's note— Formerly numbered as § 13-11.
Sec. 13-11. - Swimming pool—Regulations for use.
(a)
Established. The following regulations are established for the operation and use of the city swimming pool and bathhouse. Violation of any of these regulations shall be unlawful:
(1)
All persons using bathhouse and pool shall purchase tickets before entering same.
(2)
No person shall be allowed to use the swimming pools without being clothed in a bathing suit. Bathers will be allowed to use any decent bathing suit. The manager shall at all times be the judge as to whether a bathing suit is decent and proper.
(3)
No one shall enter the bathing pool except through the regular entrance provided for that purpose.
(4)
Bathers shall use the shower bath. before entering the pool, and also the foot bath before entering and after departing from the pool.
(5)
Persons showing the slightest evidence of being under the influence of intoxicants will not be permitted entrance to the bathhouse or pool.
(6)
Eating or drinking shall not be permitted in the enclosure where the pool is located.
(7)
Bottles, paper cups, drinking straws, cigarette butts or other foreign matter shall not be taken to or thrown into the pool.
(8)
No profane language shall be permitted in the bathhouse or pools.
(9)
Any person finding articles shall turn them in at the concession stand.
(10)
All persons shall refrain from making unnecessary noise in the bathhouse or pools.
(11)
No running around the pool shall be allowed.
(12)
No person shall enter or remain in the swimming pool or bathhouse when the regularly employed life guard is not present and on duty at the swimming pool.
(b)
Authority of manager: The manager of the bathhouse and swimming pool shall be clothed with police powers for the purpose of enforcing these rules and regulations, and may in such manager's discretion prevent the further use of the bathhouse and swimming pool during that day to any person who violates any of the foregoing regulations.
(Code 1963, § 17-4; Ord. No. 2006-28, § 3, 9-18-06)

Sec. 13-12. - Same—Closing.
(a)
Grounds: Whenever in the judgment of the manager of the swimming pool or the city manager or the mayor, the conditions of weather, the swimming pool premises, the swimming pool equipment, the actions of the public or any member of the public using, or at or near the swimming pool facilities, shall be such as to endanger the life, health or property of persons using or in proximity to the swimming pool, the same shall be immediately closed at the direction of either the swimming pool manager, city manager or mayor.
(b)
Effect of closing: When the swimming pool shall have been closed under any such conditions, it shall be unlawful for any person to remain on the swimming pool premises after being requested to leave such premises by either of such officials or city employees mentioned in subsection (a) or by any police officer of the city.
(Code 1963, § 17-5; Ord. No. 2006-28, § 3, 9-18-06)
Editor's note— Formerly numbered as § 13-13.
