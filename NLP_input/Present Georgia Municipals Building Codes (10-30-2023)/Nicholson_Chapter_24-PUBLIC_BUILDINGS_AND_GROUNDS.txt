Chapter 24 - PUBLIC BUILDINGS AND GROUNDS
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Secs. 24-1—24-18. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - CEMETERIES

Sec. 24-19. - Intent.
It is the desire of the city to operate and maintain a cemetery that is beautiful, dignified, and an appropriate final resting place for departed loved ones. The city desires to accommodate the needs of its residents, while still preserving those rules and regulations necessary to maintain a high standard of beauty and efficiency at the cemetery.
(Code 2018, § 9.102; Ord. of 10-3-2016)
Sec. 24-20. - Hours of operation.
Nicholson Cemetery grounds shall be open to the public from 6:00 a.m. to 10:00 p.m., every day.
(Code 2018, § 9.103; Ord. of 10-3-2016)
Sec. 24-21. - Restrictions.
Interments, disinterments, and grave marker installations shall not be allowed on city holidays including New Year's Day, Martin Luther King, Jr. Day, Memorial Day weekend beginning the Saturday morning before and through Memorial Day, Independence Day (July 4), Labor Day, Thanksgiving Day and the following Friday, Christmas Eve, Christmas day, or any other city observed holiday. No interments, disinterments, or grave marker installations shall be allowed in Nicholson Cemetery on any Sunday.
(Code 2018, § 9.104; Ord. of 10-3-2016)
Sec. 24-22. - Cemetery records.
A map of all burial lots within Nicholson Cemetery shall be maintained by the City Clerk showing both those burial lots that have been utilized for interment as well as burial lots sold, including the names of those individuals buried in the cemetery and individuals who have purchased a burial lot.
(Code 2018, § 9.105; Ord. of 10-3-2016)
Sec. 24-23. - Burial lot size.
A burial lot shall consist of enough land in which persons may be buried in single grave spaces, as defined on the plat of Nicholson Cemetery maintained by the City Clerk.
(Code 2018, § 9.106; Ord. of 10-3-2016)
Sec. 24-24. - Purchase of burial lot.
(a)
The cost of a burial lot shall be as shown on the city's schedule of fees, rates, and charges maintained in the office of the City Clerk and available to the public upon request, and as may be amended from time to time. Designation of the person who may be buried in each burial lot shall be made at the time of purchase. Lots may also be purchased for future needs (pre-need).
(b)
Burial lots shall be conveyed by cemetery deed to the person tendering the purchase price of the burial lot.
(c)
At any time, the burial lot may be sold back to the city; the resale price will be the amount the grantee originally paid for the burial lot.
(Code 2018, § 9.107; Ord. of 10-3-2016)
Sec. 24-25. - Transfer or assignment of burial lot.
(a)
In the event a purchaser of any burial lot wishes to convey to any other person the right to use a burial lot for burial or interment, the purchaser shall notify the city in writing of such designation and/or conveyance.
(b)
Upon transfer of any interest in a burial lot, the seller must notify the city in writing within 48 hours of the sale. No conveyance of the burial lot shall be effective unless in writing and recorded with the superior court of the county.
(c)
In the event any purchaser of a burial lot dies and does not utilize a burial lot, the rights and title to the unused burial lots shall pass to the heirs and assigns of the decedent in accordance with state law.
(Code 2018, § 9.108; Ord. of 10-3-2016)
Sec. 24-26. - Interments.
(a)
Specification. All interments must be inside containers of minimum standard as approved by the City Clerk (pine construction or equal) and shall be placed in vaults, placed underground at a minimum depth of four feet. Vault construction shall be of fiberglass, steel, or concrete only.
(b)
Funeral services. All funeral services shall be conducted in accordance with state law and shall be overseen by a funeral director licensed with the state board of funeral service.
(c)
Notice to city. The city requires 24-hour notice for all pending funeral services in Nicholson Cemetery. This 24-hour notice must be given during regular city business hours.
(Code 2018, § 9.109; Ord. of 10-3-2016)
Sec. 24-27. - Monuments.
(a)
Before installation, all monument contractors must secure a marker installation request permit from the City Clerk.
(b)
All monuments must be of solid materials base and die, marble or granite only.
(c)
All monuments or memorials of four feet height or less shall have a concrete foundation, not less than four inches thick, of adequate strength and construction. All monuments or memorials of height between four and six feet shall have a concrete foundation, not less than six inches thick, of adequate strength and construction. All monuments or memorials in excess of six feet in height shall have the foundation designed by a licensed professional engineer. This design must be submitted to the City Clerk and approved in writing prior to installation.
(d)
All bronze monuments must be placed on a granite base.
(e)
All foot markers and corner markers shall be installed so that the top of the markers are level with the existing ground.
(Code 2018, § 9.110; Ord. of 10-3-2016)
Sec. 24-28. - Mausoleums.
No mausoleums are permitted within Nicholson Cemetery.
(Code 2018, § 9.111; Ord. of 10-3-2016)
Sec. 24-29. - Benches and other improvements.
(a)
Benches will be permitted if design is deemed appropriate by the City Clerk, constructed of granite or marble only, and placed so as not to interfere with the use and visitation by others of Nicholson Cemetery, as may be decided in the sole discretion of the City Clerk.
(b)
No new improvements such as coping, walls, fences, walks, sidewalks, fixtures, or enclosures will be allowed in Nicholson Cemetery, except as approved in writing by the City Clerk.
(c)
Should such improvements be made without written consent, the city shall have the right to remove, alter, or change such improvements or alterations at the expense of the lot or grave space owner.
(d)
No rocks, gravel, stones, blocks, bricks, wire, wire stands, signs, shells, crockery, glass, ceramics, figurines, toys, or other materials deemed hazardous to personnel and maintenance equipment shall be allowed on gravesites.
(Code 2018, § 9.112; Ord. of 10-3-2016)
Sec. 24-30. - Floral arrangements, trees and shrubs.
(a)
If any tree or shrub situated in any burial lot shall by means of its roots or branches become detrimental in any way to the adjoining lot, avenue, or walk, it shall be the duty of the city, and it hereby reserves the right, to enter upon the burial lot and remove the trees or shrubs, or any part thereof. However, no tree growing in any burial lot or border shall be pruned or removed without the consent of the City Clerk.
(b)
The city shall have the authority to remove all floral designs, flowers, weeds, trees, shrubs, plants, or herbage of any kind from the cemetery when, in the judgment of the City Clerk or his designee, they become unsightly, dangerous, detrimental, or diseased. The city shall not be liable for damage to floral pieces, baskets, or frames in which or to which such floral pieces are attached.
(c)
Only one floral arrangement shall be placed on the ground at each gravesite. Arrangements shall be placed at the base of the monument. Any flowers which are determined by the City Clerk or his designee to be outside of the appropriate area may be removed by the city. Floral arrangements may be placed in monument vases and a saddle arrangement may also be placed on the monument stone. All floral arrangements shall be placed in clay or plastic containers. Glass, tin, wire, or cement containers are prohibited. Any other type of container must be approved by the City Clerk.
(d)
When a new burial is made on any burial lot, all live flowers will be removed after ten days, except as otherwise noted in this article.
(Code 2018, § 9.113; Ord. of 10-3-2016)
Sec. 24-31. - Responsibility for damage.
(a)
The city disclaims all responsibility for loss or damage from causes beyond its reasonable control, and especially from damage caused by the elements, an act of God, a common enemy, thieves, vandals, strikers, malicious mischief-makers, explosions, unavoidable accidents, invasions, insurrections, riots, or orders of any military or civil authority, whether the damage is direct or collateral.
(b)
The city assumes no liability for any property damage, including damage to a casket, vault, memorial, etc., or for bodily injury sustained during a disinterment from causes beyond its reasonable control. The city is not liable for acts or omissions of any third party for any reason. The city is not liable for damage to the contents of any casket or adjacent lots from causes beyond its reasonable control. Any expenses incurred due to damage to an adjacent lot/vault will be the responsibility of the requester. The city is not liable for mental anguish, shock, or intentional or negligent infliction of emotional distress arising out of any disinterment.
(Code 2018, § 9.114; Ord. of 10-3-2016)
Sec. 24-32. - Disinterment.
(a)
The city must balance its responsibilities for the health, welfare, and safety of its employees and the public with the requests of a burial lot owner when asked to disinter human remains.
(b)
Any request to disinter human remains shall comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws.
(c)
All requests for disinterment must be made by appointment only at the city hall.
(d)
The city reserves the right to deny any requests for disinterment in its sole discretion.
(Code 2018, § 9.115; Ord. of 10-3-2016)
