ARTICLE II. - TAXICABS[2]
Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
Charter reference— Power to regulate taxicabs, § 28.1.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 25-26. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
License shall mean a business license issued under chapter 12, article II.
Taxicab shall mean a motor-driven vehicle of not more than eight-passenger capacity operated by the owner thereof or an agent, lessee or employee of the owner and which is regularly available for hire to transport persons within or outside the city.
Taxicab business shall mean any separately owned and operated taxicab; or a fleet of owned or leased taxicabs; or a fleet of taxicabs owned separately but operating under one name.
Sec. 25-27. - Inspection of cabs.
The city police are authorized to make inspection of all taxicabs, from time to time, with full authority to approve or disapprove the operation thereof when the taxicab is not in good mechanical condition or is not in good sanitary condition.
Sec. 25-28. - Schedule of fares to be posted.
The schedule of fares shall be posted in each taxicab and in the office of each taxicab company.
Secs. 25-29—25-40. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 25-41. - Required; application.
(a)
Every person desiring to operate a taxicab business in the city shall make application for license or privilege to so operate to the city administrator or his designee on blanks furnished by the city. Such application shall contain the name and address of the owner, the make and vehicle serial number, model, year, total mileage and passenger-carrying capacity for each vehicle operated. Names of each operator are to be listed.
(b)
The application shall be reviewed by the police department for any record of excessive recklessness on the part of the applicant or those operating taxicabs under the applicant's name. No person shall operate a taxicab in the city that has been convicted, pled guilty or nolo contendere to any felony within the three (3) years immediately preceding the issuance of the license or been convicted, pled guilty or nolo contendere to an offense involved in prostitution, soliciting for prostitution, or the illegal manufacture, sale or distribution of alcoholic beverages or controlled substances in the three (3) years immediately preceding the issuance of the license.
Sec. 25-42. - Liability insurance required.
(a)
Every applicant for a taxicab business license shall, before the license is granted, deposit with the city administrator, for each taxicab for which the privilege to operate is sought, a policy or certificate of liability insurance, indemnifying the applicant in the sums required by law. The certificate or policy of liability insurance shall be issued by a company or companies authorized to do business in the state and, if obtainable, licensed to do business in the city. The certificate or policy of insurance shall identify by serial number all vehicles insured under the policy or policies. The license for the operation of the taxicab business shall expire upon the lapse or cancellation or termination or revocation of any policy covering any taxicab operated under the business.
(b)
If any person owning, leasing or operating a taxicab in the city shall fail or refuse to satisfy and discharge any final judgment, within sixty (60) days after the date of the final judgment, such failure or refusal shall be grounds for suspension or revocation of any license issued to any person or business found liable under that final judgment.
Sec. 25-43. - Issuance, display.
If the application meets all requirements, a license shall be issued upon the payment of the proper license fee prescribed by the governing body. This license or a copy thereof shall be displayed on each taxicab operated in the city.
Sec. 25-44. - License amendment.
(a)
Each licensee under this division shall amend the license application originally filed for the license to include new taxicabs or exclude old taxicabs promptly upon adding or deleting taxicabs from the licensee's business so that an up-to-date list of operating taxicabs may be maintained.
(b)
Each licensee hereunder shall also provide the city administrator proof that any new taxicab added to the application is fully insured as required under this division.
Sec. 25-45. - Revocation.
Any license under this division may be suspended or revoked by the municipal court upon a finding that any provision of this article has been violated or that the person or business holding the license or permit no longer meets the standards required for the issuance of the license or permit.
Secs. 25-46—25-70. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - NON-EMERGENCY TRANSPORTATION
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 25-71. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Non-emergency transportation business means any separately owned and operated non-emergency transportation vehicle; or a fleet of owned or leased non-emergency transportation vehicles; or a fleet of non-emergency transportation vehicles owned separately but operating under one name.
Non-emergency transportation vehicle means any motor vehicle operated for hire, the destination of which is under the direction of the passenger transported therein, which is not a taxicab as defined under this chapter or a limousine as defined under state law.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 5-6-2013)
Sec. 25-72. - Inspection of vehicles.
The city police are authorized to make inspection of all non-emergency transportation vehicles, from time to time, with full authority to approve or disapprove the operation thereof when the non-emergency transportation vehicle is not in good mechanical condition or is not in good sanitary condition.
(Ord. No. 2013-03, § 1, 5-6-2013)
