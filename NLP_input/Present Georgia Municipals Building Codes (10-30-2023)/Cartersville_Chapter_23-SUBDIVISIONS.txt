Sec. 23-16. - Scope.
This article applies to subdivisions.
(Ord. No. 32-98, § 2, 8-27-98)
Sec. 23-17. - Monuments.
Monuments shall be placed by the subdivider at all subdivision boundary lots, block corners, angle points, points of curves in streets, and at such intermediate points as shall be required by the city engineer or other appropriate city official, and shall meet the following specifications:
(1)
Boundary monuments shall be concrete posts at least four (4) inches in diameter or four (4) inches square and three (3) feet long. They shall be set flush with the ground surface or six (6) inches above the ground surface. The top of the monument shall have an indented cross to identify the location.
(2)
Other monuments shall be iron pins not less than twelve (12) inches long and one-half (½) diameter set flush with the ground surface.
(Code 1976, § 8-2121; Ord. No. 32-98, § 2, 8-27-98)
Sec. 23-18. - Streets and related improvements.
Each subdivider shall be required to install, at his own expense, streets and related improvements according to the following specifications:
(1)
All streets shall be cleared and grubbed to the full right-of-way width.
(2)
All streets shall be graded to a sufficient width to provide at least a seven-foot walkway on each side of the roadway surface. Finished grades shall be approved by the city engineer or other appropriate city official.
(3)
The planning commission may require sidewalks near commercial areas, schools, and other places of public assembly, and areas of intensive residential development such as apartment areas.
(4)
Streets shall be surfaced according to standards and specifications adopted by the council.
(5)
Curbs and gutters shall be installed according to standards and specifications approved by the council, and installation shall be done according to plans and specifications approved by the city engineer.
(Code 1976, § 8-2122; Ord. No. 32-98, § 2, 8-27-98)
Sec. 23-19. - Sanitary sewers.
(a)
Except as herein provided, each lot or other site in a subdivision shall be connected to a public sanitary sewer system by lines of sufficient size to accommodate the anticipated sewage load. Service connections shall be stubbed into each lot so that cutting into the pavement for future connections will not be necessary.
(b)
Wherever a public sanitary sewer is not available within a reasonable distance from the subdivision, the health department shall specify and approve one (1) of the following types of sewage disposal:
(1)
Community sewage system. A sewage collection system consisting of connections to each lot with necessary collector lines for transporting the sewage to a central treatment facility within or in proximity of the subdivision. Treatment may consist of an oxidation pond, package treatment facility, etc.
(2)
Septic tanks. A septic tank on each lot, provided the minimum lot size shall be fifteen thousand (15,000) square feet which shall comply with the city requirements for frontage. This minimum lot area may be increased by the health department if necessary. The following information shall be submitted to the health department for consideration of septic tank approval.
a.
Topographic information for each parcel of land on which a septic tank is to be installed. Contour intervals shall not exceed five (5) feet.
b.
Location of all drainage facilities, either natural or proposed.
c.
Percolation data indicated on a plat as specified by the health department.
d.
Proposed number of sanitary fixtures per dwelling unit.
(Code 1976, § 8-2123; Ord. No. 32-98, § 2, 8-27-98; Ord. No. 33-19, § 1, 8-15-19)
Cross reference— Septic tanks, § 24-81.
Sec. 23-20. - Water supply.
(a)
Where a public water supply is within a reasonable distance, the subdivider shall install or have installed a system of water mains, including fire hydrants, and connections to each lot. All water mains and lot connections shall be installed prior to paving of the streets.
(b)
Where a public water supply is not available, each lot in a subdivision shall be furnished with a water supply system approved by the health department.
(Code 1976, § 8-2124; Ord. No. 32-98, § 2, 8-27-98)
Sec. 23-21. - Storm drainage.
An adequate system of drainage, including necessary open ditches, pipes, culverts, storm sewers, intersectional drains, drop inlets, bridges, and other necessary appurtenances shall be installed by the subdivider according to plans and specifications approved by the appropriate city official.
(Code 1976, § 8-2125; Ord. No. 32-98, § 2, 8-27-98)
Sec. 23-22. - Other utilities.
(a)
All underground utilities, such as telephone lines and gas mains, shall be installed before streets are paved.
(b)
The subdivider shall have installed at his expense by the city the street lights for the proposed development according to all city specifications. The city specifications are available at the office of the electric superintendent for the city.
(c)
Existing subdivisions or individuals desiring to have street lights installed must do so according to all city specifications.
(Code 1976, § 8-2126; Ord. No. 04-93, § I, 2-4-93; Ord. No. 32-98, § 2, 8-27-98)
Sec. 23-23. - Street name signs.
Street name signs shall be installed at all intersections within a subdivision. The location and design of such signs shall be approved by the city engineer or other appropriate city official.
(Code 1976, § 8-2127; Ord. No. 32-98, § 2, 8-27-98)
