ARTICLE III. - ABANDONED MOTOR VEHICLES[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Authority to provide by ordinance for removal and disposal of discarded or junked vehicles, O.C.G.A. § 36-60-4; abandoned motor vehicles, O.C.G.A. § 40-11-1 et seq.; when police officers may remove vehicles, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-206; removal of improperly parked cars or trespassing personal property, O.C.G.A. § 44-1-13.

Sec. 74-61. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Abandoned motor vehicle means:
(1)
Any motor vehicle incapable of operation under its own power which is seven years or older and which, at the time and place found, has a value of $100.00 or less, which is:
a.
Located on public street, highway or property of this county; or
b.
Placed on private property without the express consent of the owner or person in lawful possession thereof;
(2)
Left on a public street, highway or other public property without a current registration tag being displayed thereon;
(3)
Left upon a public street, highway or other public property 72 hours beyond the lawfully established parking time at the place where found;
(4)
Left on the property of another without the express consent of the owner or person in lawful possession thereof; or
(5)
Left on the private property of another for seven consecutive days beyond any time the owner or occupant of such property may have authorized.
Impounding authority means the reference to the paragraph under the first definition in this section which describes the circumstances under which the described vehicle was impounded or the reason therefor.
Junk car dealer means any person engaged in the buying or selling of motor vehicles which, because of damage, disrepair, or any other reason, have been rendered substantially without value for other than junk or scrap purposes or any person with two or more junk cars on their premises who has failed to remove such vehicles after notice from the local police department.
Person means any individual, firm, partnership, association, corporation, company or group of individuals acting together, including any governmental agency other than the United States and the state.
Police department means any county law enforcement agency including, but not limited to, the county sheriff's office or any county department responsible for enforcement of this article.
Scrap processor means any person engaged in the collection of scrap metal including junk or abandoned automobiles who processes such scrap metal by crushing, shredding or other means into the physical forms or grades required for its resale to steel mills, foundries, or other customers.
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 74-62. - Title.
Title to all motor vehicles shall be deemed to have vested in the police department having jurisdiction of the site of abandonment of any such motor vehicle.
Sec. 74-63. - Impounding.
(a)
Any police department within this county is hereby authorized to impound any motor vehicle abandoned under section 74-61.
(b)
Any police department within this county may impound any motor vehicle abandoned under section 74-61 upon the written request of the owner or lawful occupant of such private premises.
Sec. 74-64. - Notice.
(a)
Within five days after any impoundment under section 74-61, the police department shall forward to the department of revenue, motor vehicle unit, the make, model, year, serial number, motor number, if any, and license registration number, if known, on all impounded vehicles for the purpose of identifying the last known owner, and the last known lienholder, including the post office address, as reflected on the records in the motor vehicle unit, of such owner and lienholder, if any.
(b)
The police department shall, within three days of the receipt of the information in subsection (a) of this section, notify each such last known owner and lienholder, if any, by certified mail, directed to such owner and lienholder, if any, at his last known address, in an envelope bearing the return address of the police department, which notice shall include reference to the impounding authority under this article. All such notices shall be proof of service on such last known owner or lienholder unless such envelope and contents are returned to the police department unopened.
(c)
Each notice so served shall contain a description of the impounded vehicle by manufacturer, year, model, serial number, motor number, if any, and registration number, the address where impounded, the accumulated charges, including towing and storage, and the date, not less than five days from the date of the notice, on which such vehicle will be sold at public auction unless reclaimed by the lawful owner or valid lienholder as provided in section 74-66.
Sec. 74-65. - Constructive notice.
In all impoundments under section 74-61, where the identity of the last known owner and lienholder are not ascertained within 15 days, publish in a newspaper of general circulation in the area of impoundment, a notice setting forth:
(1)
A description of each such vehicle impounded listing the manufacturer, year, model, serial number, motor number, if any, and registration number; and
(2)
Stating that such motor vehicle will be sold at public auction at a specific location in not less than three days from the date of publication.
Such notice may include a description of more than one vehicle.
Sec. 74-66. - Redemption.
(a)
Any owner, upon proof of ownership through a duly issued certificate of title, or any lienholder, upon proof of a valid lien, may redeem an impounded vehicle prior to public sale by paying the charges of towing, storage and publication, together with all other costs of impoundment and any penalty imposed by law in this county for abandonment.
(b)
No lienholder shall be subject to any penalty unless such vehicle is abandoned by the lienholder, or his agent or servant. No owner of a motor vehicle which has been stolen and thereafter abandoned shall be liable for any charges or penalties imposed in this article. A motor vehicle shall be deemed to be stolen when the owner notifies a police department of this county or state, and such report is accepted and carried on the records of the police authority as a stolen vehicle.
(c)
The redemption provided for in this section must be prior to the sale of such vehicle at public sale.
Sec. 74-67. - Bill of sale; certificate of title.
All purchasers of motor vehicles sold under the authority of this article shall receive a bill of sale from the police department conducting such sale, setting forth the name of the manufacturer, the model, year, serial number and motor number, if any, and such sale shall be free of any claims from the former owner or lienholder. Any such purchaser may obtain a certificate of title to any such motor vehicle so purchased by filing the prescribed application, accompanied by a certified copy of the bill of sale, and the payment of lawful fees therefor. Such new certificate shall be free and clear of all claims of former owners and lienholders.
Sec. 74-68. - Scrap processor records.
All scrap processors shall maintain records of all motor vehicles which are demolished by their operations, showing in such records the date of acquisition of each motor vehicle, the name and address of the seller, the manufacturer, year, model, serial number and motor number. All such records shall be maintained for not less than three calendar years and shall be available to any police officer or to the department of health during regular business hours.
Sec. 74-69. - Sale of impounded motor vehicles.
(a)
All motor vehicles may be sold by the impounding police department without search or notice to the last owner of record or lienholder unless the wholesale value of such vehicle exceeds $100.00. Such sales may be made in bulk lots, at prices established through competitive bidding per unit, and shall be to automobile demolishers only.
(b)
Title to such motor vehicles shall be shown by a bill of sale from the police department as defined in section 74-67, and each such bill of sale may contain multiple listings. Each such bill of sale shall be forwarded to the motor vehicle unit of the department of revenue.
Sec. 74-70. - Statutory lienholders.
All persons holding a lien for work or services under the laws of this state and/or county who have a motor vehicle abandoned on their premises may, within 30 calendar days following such abandonment, elect to pursue their rights to reimbursement under the laws of this state or report the abandonment to the police department who shall impound such motor vehicle and sell it pursuant to the provisions of this article. The proceeds of such sale shall be distributed first to the lienholder, for work, second to the costs of impoundment, including towing and storage, and any excess funds shall be held for the lawful owner or lienholder for a period of 30 days following the sale. If unclaimed in that time, such excess funds shall be distributed as provided in this article.
Sec. 74-71. - Proceeds of sales.
(a)
After the payment of any liens under section 74-70 and after deducting the costs of impoundment, including towing, storage and advertising and after the expiration of the 30 days' time following such sale, each police department shall forward any surplus funds in its possession to the county treasurer. Such remittances may be made on a quarterly basis.
(b)
Any police department in this county may draw on such funds held by the treasurer where such police department's impounding activity has operated under a deficiency for 60 consecutive days. In such case, any surplus funds remitted to the county treasurer shall be credited to such draws, and then to the general fund.
(c)
The treasurer shall audit the records of all police departments acting under the authority of this section not less than one time each year and may make recommendations based on such audit which will bring about a more efficient and economical operation.
Sec. 74-72. - Permits.
Any person operating as a junk car dealer or scrap processor shall apply to the board of commissioners for a permit, upon forms prescribed by the commission. Each such permit shall apply to the described premises only. No such permit may be transferred to another person or location.
Sec. 74-73. - Rules and regulations.
The board of commissioners is hereby authorized to promulgate, revise and revoke rules and regulations for the fulfillment of this article and the ultimate disposal of junk cars, including the issuance of permits for junk car dealers and/or scrap processors and the delegation of impounding authority to private agencies by police departments within this county including therein minimum insurance requirements and bonding.
Sec. 74-74. - Immunity.
No person, police department employee or person acting under this article shall be liable in damages to the owner of any motor vehicle impounded pursuant to the provisions of this article, unless such person, police department employee or person acting under the provisions of this article is found to have acted willfully or wantonly in the performance of his duties.
Sec. 74-75. - False records.
Any person who is convicted of making any false statements concerning a motor vehicle impounded under this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction, be punished as provided by law.
Sec. 74-76. - Penalty for violation of article.
Any person convicted of abandoning a motor vehicle upon the public streets or highways of this state, the property of a political subdivision of the state or upon private property without the consent of the owner or lawful occupant thereof shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction, shall be punished as provided in section 1-11.
