ARTICLE II. - DRAINING OF MOTOR VEHICLE FLUIDS

Sec. 16-19. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Fluid includes but is not limited to oil, lubricants, transmission fluid, antifreeze, gasoline, brake fluid, windshield washer antifreeze and detergent, Freon, power steering fluid, battery acid and like substances.
(Code 1994, § 38-26; Ord. No. 91-7, § 2, 9-9-1991)
Sec. 16-20. - Applicability of article.
This article applies to all businesses licensed in the city that buy, sell or service motor vehicles, whether such businesses are identified as service stations, automobile dealers, salvage dealers, auto auctions or by some other classification. This article further applies to all individuals within the city who drain fluids from motor vehicles or motor vehicle parts.
(Code 1994, § 38-27; Ord. No. 91-7, § 1, 9-9-1991)
Sec. 16-21. - Penalty for violation.
Any person violating this article shall, upon conviction or upon the entering of a plea of guilty or nolo contendere, be punished as provided in section 1-8. Any owner or manager who participates or directs the violation of this article shall likewise be subject to such criminal penalties.
(Code 1994, § 38-28; Ord. No. 91-7, § 6, 9-9-1991)
Sec. 16-22. - Suspension or revocation of license for violations.
In addition to the penalties referred to in section 16-21, the business license of any business under this article shall be subject to suspension or revocation for repeated violations of this article. As used in this section, the term "repeated violations" means three or more prosecutions which result in convictions or pleas of guilty or nolo contendere to violation of this article or any single instance of violation of this article following notice by the city that past violations have been observed and directing that all such violating conditions cease immediately.
(Code 1994, § 38-29; Ord. No. 91-7, § 7, 9-9-1991)
Sec. 16-23. - Compliance.
The owner and manager of a business to which this article applies shall each be responsible for ensuring full compliance with this article.
(Code 1994, § 38-30; Ord. No. 91-7, § 5, 9-9-1991)
Sec. 16-24. - Discharge of fluids prohibited.
(a)
No business to which this article pertains shall permit the discharge, leaking, dumping or spilling of any fluid from a motor vehicle or from a motor vehicle part onto the ground or onto any paved, graveled or cemented surface.
(b)
No individual, whether acting within the scope of his employment or on his own, shall permit the discharge, leaking, dumping or spilling of any fluid from a motor vehicle or from a motor vehicle part onto the ground or onto any paved, graveled or cemented surface.
(Code 1994, § 38-31; Ord. No. 91-7, § 3, 9-9-1991)
Sec. 16-25. - Disposal of fluids.
All fluids to be drained from motor vehicles or from motor vehicle parts shall be captured, containerized and disposed of by such methods as approved by state and federal environmental rules.
(Code 1994, § 38-32; Ord. No. 91-7, § 4, 9-9-1991)
