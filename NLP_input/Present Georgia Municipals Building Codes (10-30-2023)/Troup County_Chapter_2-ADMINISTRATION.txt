DIVISION 2. - MEETINGS[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— Requirement to prescribe the time, place and dates of regular meetings of governing authority, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(d); meetings to be open to public, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1; exemptions from open meeting requirements, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-3.

Sec. 2-51. - Open meetings.
All meetings of the board of commissioners shall be held in accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1 et seq. The public shall at all times be afforded access to all meetings other than executive sessions.
(Code 1987, § 2-25)
Sec. 2-52. - Quorum.
A quorum must be present for conducting meetings of the board. A quorum is a majority of all of the members of the board then in office. It is the duty of the chairman to enforce this rule. Any commissioner may raise a point of order directed to the chairman if he believes that a quorum is not present. If, during the course of a meeting, a commissioner leaves and a quorum no longer exists, the meeting cannot continue. If a quorum is not attained within 30 minutes, the meeting will be rescheduled by the chairman with the approval of the commissioners present.
(Code 1987, § 2-26)
Sec. 2-53. - Voting majority.
Passage of a motion shall require the affirmative vote of a minimum of three commissioners present and voting at a meeting at which a quorum is present.
(Code 1987, § 2-27)
Sec. 2-54. - Abstentions.
A commissioner shall vote on all motions unless he has a conflict of interest which would prevent him from making a decision in a fair and legal manner. If a conflict of interest does exist, the commissioner shall explain for the record his decision to abstain on any vote.
(Code 1987, § 2-28)
Sec. 2-55. - Chairman.
The chairman of the board of commissioners is responsible for the orderly conduct of the meeting. In order to fulfill this duty, the chairman must enforce the rules of procedure that are adopted by and the board of commissioners. The chairman shall be impartial and conduct the meetings in a fair manner. The chairman may not introduce motions or second a motion except that the chairman may introduce or second a motion to go into executive session as authorized by section 2-67. The chairman is not required to vote except to break a tie.
(Code 1987, § 2-29)
Sec. 2-56. - Vice-chairman.
The board shall select a vice-chairman from the board members at the beginning of each calendar year. The vice-chairman shall fulfill the duties of the chairman if the chairman is not in attendance. If the chairman and the vice-chairman are absent and a quorum is present, the remaining commissioners shall select a commissioner to serve as temporary chairman of the meeting until either the chairman or vice-chairman is present at the meeting.
(Code 1987, § 2-30)
Sec. 2-57. - Agenda.
(a)
The county manager, with the advice of the chairman and the commissioners, shall prepare an agenda of subjects to be acted on for each meeting. Any commissioner may request an item be placed on the agenda by notifying the county manager at least three business days before the board meeting. With the chairman's approval, such items will be placed on the agenda. The chairman maintains the right to allow exceptions to the three-day advance notice as deemed appropriate or necessary. The agenda shall be made available to the commissioners, and others as appropriate, at least one business day before every board meeting.
(b)
Members of the public may also request that a particular subject be placed on the agenda. To be considered, however, this request must be submitted in writing to the county manager and received at least three business days before the meeting. With the chairman's approval, such items will be placed on the agenda.
(c)
The order of the agenda may be changed during a meeting by a majority vote of the board. A new subject not on the agenda that requires attention may be added to the agenda during a meeting by a majority vote of the board.
(d)
A copy of the agenda and a list of those members present shall be made available to the public for inspection within two business days of the adjournment of any meeting.
(Code 1987, § 2-31)
Sec. 2-58. - Minutes.
(a)
The clerk of the board shall promptly record the minutes for each board meeting. The minutes serve as the official written record of the board meeting and shall be open for public inspection once approved as official by the board but in no case later than immediately following the next regular meeting of the board. The minutes shall specify the names of commissioners present at the meeting, a description of each motion or other proposal made at the meeting, the commissioner who proposed each motion, the commissioner who seconded each motion and a record of all votes. In the case of a roll call vote, the name of each commissioner voting for or against a proposal shall be recorded. It shall be presumed that a commissioner has voted in the affirmative unless the minutes show otherwise. More detailed information may be included in the minutes at the request of the board.
(b)
The board must approve the minutes before they can be considered as an official record of the board. A copy of the minutes from the previous meeting shall be distributed to the commissioners at least one business day before the following meeting. The minutes of the previous meeting shall be corrected and approved by the board at the beginning of each meeting. A majority vote is required for approval. Conflicts about the content of the minutes shall be decided by majority vote. Upon being approved, the minutes shall be signed by the chairman and attested to by the clerk of the board of commissioners.
(Code 1987, § 2-33)
Sec. 2-59. - Order of business.
All regular board meetings should follow an established order of business. The order is as follows:
(1)
Opening.
(2)
Pledge of allegiance.
(3)
Approval of minutes.
(4)
People to address the board.
(5)
Special business.
(6)
Public hearings.
(7)
Committee reports.
(8)
Manager's reports.
(9)
Adjournment.
(Code 1987, § 2-34)
Sec. 2-60. - Decorum.
(a)
All commissioners must conduct themselves in a professional and respectful manner. All remarks should be directed to the chairman and not to individual commissioners, staff or citizens in attendance. Personal remarks are inappropriate. A commissioner may not speak at a meeting until he has been recognized by the chairman. All comments made by a commissioner shall address the motion that is being discussed.
(b)
The chairman shall enforce these rules of decorum. If a commissioner believes that a rule has been broken, he can raise a point of order. A second is not required. The chairman can rule on the question or he can allow the board to debate the issue and decide the issue by majority vote.
(Code 1987, § 2-35)
Sec. 2-61. - Public participation.
Public participation in meetings of the board of commissioners shall be permitted in accordance with the following provisions:
(1)
Public comments. All members of the public who wish to address the board must submit their name and the topic of their comments to the county manager at least three business days before the board meeting. These individuals will be allotted five minutes to make their comments which must be limited to their chosen topic. These limits can be waived by a majority vote of the board.
(2)
Public participation on agenda items. By concurrence of a majority of the members present, the board may allow public comment on an agenda item at the time the item is being considered by the board. These comments must be limited to the subject that is being debated. Members of the public may speak for five minutes and may only speak once. These limits can be waived by a majority vote of the board. Anyone wishing to speak at any board meeting must be recognized by the chairman before addressing the board.
(3)
Decorum. Members of the public shall not make inappropriate or offensive comments at a board meeting and are expected to comply with the rules of decorum that are established for commissioners. Individuals who violate any rules of the board may be ruled out of order by the chairman or on a point of order made by a commissioner. A majority vote of the board will rule on the point of order. An individual who violates the rules of decorum may be removed from the meeting at the direction of the chairman.
(4)
Public hearings. The board may schedule public hearings for the purpose of soliciting public comment on any subject of interest to the board, Hearings may be held at any time, as scheduled, during a meeting of the board or at such other places and times as the board may determine. The public hearing should be closed by motion, second and majority vote, before any official action shall be taken.
(Code 1987, § 2-36)
Sec. 2-62. - Parliamentarian.
The county attorney shall serve as the parliamentarian for board meetings.
(Code 1987, § 2-37)
Sec. 2-63. - Amendments to rules.
Any amendments to the rules of order must be submitted by a commissioner in writing to the county manager three business days before a regular meeting of the board. The proposed amendment shall be included in the agenda for that meeting and distributed to all board members. All amendments require a four-fifths vote of the board to be adopted.
(Code 1987, § 2-38)
Sec. 2-64. - Reserved.

Sec. 2-65. - Regular meetings, special meetings and rescheduled regular meetings.
In accordance with local act, the board of commissioners shall hold at least one regular meeting each month at the Troup County Government Center located at 100 Ridley Avenue on the first Tuesday of each month, or if this day falls on a legal holiday, on the next succeeding day which is not a holiday. Other regular meetings may be cancelled, rescheduled or moved to a new location within the county by the chairman for any reason. Special meetings may be scheduled by the chairman or at the request of two commissioners. Whenever a rescheduled regular meeting or any other special meeting is to be cancelled, or held at a time or place other than the regularly scheduled time or place, written notice of the change shall be posted for at least 24 hours at the place of the regular meetings. In addition, written or oral notice shall be given by the clerk at least 24 hours in advance of the meeting to either the legal organ of the county or a newspaper having a general circulation at least equal to that of the legal organ and to each member of the county board of commissioners.
(Code 1987, § 2-39.1)
Editor's note— At the discretion of the editor, the title of § 2-65 has been changed from "Special meetings and rescheduled regular meetings" to read as herein set out.
Sec. 2-66. - Emergency meetings.
When emergency circumstances occur, the board may hold a meeting with less than 24 hours' notice to the public. When such meetings are to be held, the clerk shall provide notice to the legal organ of the county or a newspaper with a general circulation at least equal to that of the legal organ and to each member of the county board of commissioners as soon as possible. The notice shall include the subjects expected to be considered at the meeting. In addition, the minutes shall reflect the reason for the emergency meeting and the nature of the notice given to the media.
(Code 1987, § 2-39.2)
Sec. 2-67. - Executive sessions.
(a)
Executive sessions of the board may be held for the purpose of conducting business excepted from public access requirements as authorized by O.C.G.A. §§ 50-14-2 and 50-14-3. Where a meeting of the board is devoted in part to matters with the authorized exceptions to public access requirements, any portion of the meeting not subject to any such exceptions shall be open to the public.
(b)
No executive session shall be held, except pursuant to a majority affirmative vote of the board of commissioners taken in a public meeting. The minutes of the public meeting shall reflect the names of the commissioners present, those voting for the executive session and the specific reasons for the executive session. Minutes of the executive session may be maintained by the clerk of the board of commissioners at the direction of the chairman. Any such minutes shall be maintained in a confidential file and shall not be subject to disclosure, except that disclosures of such portions of minutes identifying real estate to be acquired by the board may only be delayed until such time as the acquisition of the real estate has been completed, terminated, or abandoned or court proceedings have been initiated.
(Code 1987, § 2-39.3)
Sec. 2-68. - Suspending the rules of order.
Rules of order may be suspended in the case of emergency. A motion to suspend the rules requires a second, is debatable and requires a four-fifths vote of the board. Rules governing quorum (section 2-51), voting methods and requirements (section 2-53 and section 2-54), the notification to commissioners of meetings (sections 2-65 and 2-66) and rules necessary for compliance with state law cannot be suspended.
(Code 1987, § 2-39.4)
Sec. 2-69. - Committees.
The chairman may create committees of members of the board to study any issue before the board. Any such committees may make recommendations to the board of commissioners, but no committee shall be empowered to make any final decision on any matter before it for consideration unless authorized by appropriate action by the board of commissioners to do so. In addition to board members, committees may include other county officials, staff or citizens at large. Whenever a committee is created, its duties, any limitation on the scope of its duties, and the times, places and periods of time for which the committee may operate shall be determined by the chairman.
(Code 1987, § 2-39.5)
Sec. 2-70. - Visual and sound recordings.
Visual, sound, and visual and sound recordings shall be permitted for all public meetings.
(Code 1987, § 2-39.6)

There is hereby created the office of county manager. The county manager shall be appointed by a majority of the members of the board of commissioners. No member of the board shall be appointed county manager or acting county manager during the term of office for which such member was elected or for a period of one year thereafter.
(Code 1987, § 2-40; Ord. No. 2014-03 , § 1(6.1(a)), 1-7-2014)
Sec. 2-102. - Compensation as fixed.
The county manager shall be paid an annual salary to be fixed by the board of commissioners. The chairman of the board of commissioners shall conduct periodic performance evaluations of the county manager, report upon the same to the board, and, unless otherwise determined by the board, may make adjustments to the county manager's compensation.
(Code 1987, § 2-41; Ord. No. 2014-03 , § 1(6.1(b)), 1-7-2014)
Sec. 2-103. - Qualifications.
The county manager shall be chosen by the board of commissioners solely upon the basis of the person's executive and administrative qualifications, with special reference to the person's actual experience in, or knowledge of, accepted practices in respect to the duties of the office as set forth in section 2-104.
(Code 1987, § 2-42; Ord. No. 2014-03 , § 1(6.1(c)), 1-7-2014)
Sec. 2-104. - Duties generally.
The county manager shall be the head of the administrative branch of the county government. Such manager shall be responsible to the board of commissioners for the proper administration of all affairs of the county, except as otherwise provided by law. Specific duties, powers and responsibilities shall be as follows:
(1)
To appoint, hire, engage, enter into employment contracts, remove and provide for the terms and conditions of severance incidental to employment separation, and discipline all officers, and employees of the county whom the board of commissioners has been heretofore empowered to appoint and discharge, except the county attorney, county physician and county auditor, whose employment and service shall be determined by the board of commissioners.
(2)
To see that all ordinances, resolutions and regulations adopted by the board are enforced and duly carried out in accordance with the directions of such board.
(3)
To exercise administrative control over all departments or divisions of the county over which the board or manager has heretofore exercised such control, or that may hereafter be created.
(4)
To attend all meetings of the board of commissioners, except those executive sessions dealing with the county manager's employment, with the right to take part in the discussion; provided such manager shall have no vote before the board.
(5)
To examine at regular intervals, not to exceed 30 days, the accounts, records and operations of the office of the board of commissioners and to keep the board advised of the financial condition and future needs of the county and to make such recommendations on the financial planning of the county as such manager deems necessary for the efficient operation of the county.
(6)
To prepare and submit to the board of commissioners an annual budget.
(7)
To appear before the board at any time the board requires to make such reports as the board deems necessary.
(8)
To execute and deliver a good and sufficient bond, payable to the commissioners, to be approved by the board, conditioned upon the faithful performance of such manager's duties, the amount therefor to be determined by the board. This requirement may be satisfied by inclusion of the county manager within the coverage of any blanket bond issued to Troup County as authorized by O.C.G.A. § 45-4-11 with coverage of no less than $100,000.00.
(9)
To make or provide for the purchases of goods and services on behalf of the county as provided in section 2-105.
(10)
To confer with and assist other public agencies, such as the county health department, department of family and children services and other agencies, whose missions are not under the direct control and supervision of the board of commissioners, but who may depend upon appropriations of the board for their continued operation.
(11)
To recommend compensation which is not preempted by state law of county employees lawfully employed by the county government and to implement such compensation as may be approved in the county budget.
(12)
To act as a liaison between the board of commissioners and the department heads or employees of the county.
(13)
To see all legislation, programs, regulations or plans promulgated and required by the state or federal government are duly carried out and complied with in accordance with such legislation, programs, regulations and plans.
(14)
To devote his or her full time to the office and hold no other office or employment while so engaged.
(15)
To perform such other duties as may be required of such manager by the board of commissioners.
(Code 1987, § 2-43; Ord. No. 2014-03 , § 1(6.1(d)), 1-7-2014)
Sec. 2-105. - Purchases and contracts.
(a)
The board of commissioners shall make all purchases on the basis of competitive bids when, in its discretion, it deems it practicable to do so or when required by state law of general application.
(b)
The county manager shall be authorized to execute any contract for the purchase of goods and/or services on behalf of the board; provided, however, that in no event shall the county manager be authorized to make any purchase or enter into any contract, other than an employment contract, expending in excess of $25,000.00 without the prior consent of a majority of the duly qualified members of the board of commissioners.
(Code 1987, § 2-44; Ord. No. 2003-04, § I, 3-4-2003; Ord. No. 2014-03 , § 2(15), 1-7-2014)

Sec. 2-107. - Removal.
The county manager may be removed by a majority vote of the board of commissioners; provided, however, that at least 30 days before such removal shall become effective, the board of commissioners shall, by majority vote, adopt a preliminary resolution stating the reasons for removal. The county manager may, within ten days, reply in writing to the preliminary resolution listing the reasons for removal and also request a hearing before the board. After such hearing, if one is requested, and after full consideration, the board of commissioners may terminate such manager's duties, and, unless otherwise provided by way of written employment agreement, shall cause to be paid to such person, forthwith, any unpaid balance of salary for the next three calendar months following adoption of the preliminary resolution, at such manager's current rate of compensation.
(Code 1987, § 2-46; Ord. No. 2014-03 , § 1(6.1(e)), 1-7-2014)
Sec. 2-108. - Temporary county manager.
The board of commissioners may designate some qualified person to act as temporary or interim county manager in the extended absence (more than 30 calendar days) of the county manager, provided that in the absence of a person acting as county manager, or temporary or interim county manager, the chairman of the board shall be vested with all the rights, duties and authority of the county manager.
(Code 1987, § 2-47; Ord. No. 2014-03 , § 1(6.1(f)), 1-7-2014)

ARTICLE IV. - FINANCE
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-131. - [County issued credit cards].
(a)
Intent and scope. This section is intended to comply with the policy requirements of O.C.G.A. § 36-80-24 regarding the use of county issued government credit cards.
(b)
Definitions.
(1)
Authorized elected official means an elected official designated by public vote of the board of commissioners to receive a county issued government credit card.
(2)
Card administrator means the credit card administrator designated by the Troup County Board of Commissioners.
(3)
County means Troup County and/or the board of commissioners.
(4)
County credit card means a financial transaction card issued by any business organization, financial institution, or any duly authorized agent of such organization or institution, used by a county official to purchase goods, services and other things of value on behalf of the county.
(5)
Financial transaction card means an instrument or device as the term is defined in O.C.G.A. § 16-9-30(5).
(6)
User agreement means the required agreement between the board of commissioners and the authorized elected officials which restricts the use of a county credit card.
(c)
Designated elected officials. The Troup County Board of Commissioners ("county"), in its discretion, may authorize specific county elected officials to use a county credit card by adoption of a resolution in a public meeting.
No authorized elected official may use a county credit card until and unless he or she has executed the county's credit card user agreement.
The county will not make payments to any business organization, financial institution, or any duly authorized agent of such organization or institution, for amounts charged by an elected official to any credit cards that are not issued pursuant to this section or for any purchases that are not authorized by this section.
(d)
Card administrator. The board of commissioners shall designate a county credit card administrator. The responsibilities of the card administrator include:
(1)
Manage county issued credit cards.
(2)
Serve as the main point of contact for all county credit card issues.
(3)
Serve as liaison to the elected officials authorized to use a credit card and their staff, as well as to the issuer of the credit card.
(4)
Provide training on card policies and procedures to the elected officials authorized to use a credit card and their staff.
(5)
Develop internal procedures to ensure timely payment of cards.
(6)
Assist authorized elected officials to dispute transactions when necessary.
(7)
Establish internal procedures to ensure compliance with this section, county procurement ordinances and policies, county credit card user agreements, applicable agreements with the business organization, financial institution, or any duly authorized agent of such organization or institution, issuing card, and state law, specifically, O.C.G.A. §§ 16-9-37 and 36-80-24.
(8)
Document internal controls, audits and other measures to prevent and detect misuse or abuse of the cards.
(9)
Audit and reconcile transactions monthly.
(10)
Maintain records for at least seven years or as otherwise provided by the county's record retention policy.
(e)
Use of cards.
(1)
Authorized purchases. County purchase cards and credit cards may be used to purchase goods and services directly related to the public duties of the authorized elected official only. All purchases are subject to the terms of this section, the county credit card user agreement, county procurement policies and ordinances, and the adopted budget.
Only authorized elected officials may use a county purchase card or credit card for purchases or payments. The cards, and use of the cards, are not transferrable to employees. The authorized elected official shall use care to ensure that others do not have access to the card account number, expiration date and security code.
Unless otherwise approved by the governing authority or established in the county and credit card user agreement, the transaction limits are as follows:
Per day: $500.00
The user may exceed the daily limit must be reported in writing to the card administrator or county manager within three business days.
(2)
Unauthorized purchases. County credit cards shall not be used for goods and services not directly related to the official responsibilities of the authorized elected official. Additionally, cards shall not be used to avoid compliance with the county's purchasing ordinances and procedures, to purchase goods and services that are not approved in the county's budget, to purchase goods and services exceeding the per transaction or per month limit, or to make purchases not in compliance with the county credit card user agreement.
(3)
Receipts and documentation. Receipts, invoices and other supporting documentation of all purchases made with a county credit card shall be obtained and maintained by the authorized county elected official for five years or as otherwise provided by the county's record retention policy. If an original or duplicate cannot be produced, a sworn affidavit of the authorized elected official may be substituted. The documentation must include the supplier or merchant information (i.e., name and location), quantity, description, unit price, total price, price paid without sales tax and an explanation of the purchase sufficient to show that the expense was in the performance of official county duties.
(4)
Public records. All receipts and other documentation of purchases are public records and subject to the requirements of O.C.G.A. § 50-18-70 et seq.
(f)
Review of purchases and audit. Proper documentation of purchases, internal controls and other measures prevent and allow detection to misuse or abuse of county issued purchase cards and credit cards. Authorized elected officials and staff that process payments under this program shall cooperate and comply with the procedures established by the county.
(1)
Review of purchases. All purchases shall be reviewed according to the following procedure:
Monthly statements with attached receipts will be reviewed by the CFO, purchasing director or the county manager.
(2)
Audits. The card administrator shall perform an annual review of the card program to ensure adequacy of internal policies and procedures, cardholder spending limits, monthly reconciliation procedures and documentation for transactions. Elected officials and staff shall cooperate with such review.
(g)
Violations.
(1)
An elected official shall reimburse the county for any purchases made with a county issued credit card in violation of this section or the user agreement.
(2)
In the discretion of the county governing authority, failure to comply with the procedures outlined in this section may result in:
a.
A warning;
b.
Suspension of the elected official's authority to use a county credit card; or
c.
Revocation of the elected official's authority to use a county purchase card or credit card.
(3)
Nothing in this section shall preclude the county governing authority from referring misuse of a purchase card or credit card for prosecution to the appropriate authorities.
( Ord. No. 2016-01 , §§ I—VII, 12-1-2015; Ord. No. 2016-02 , §§ I—VII, 1-19-2016)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 2016-01 , §§ I—VII, adopted Dec. 1, 2015, did not specify manner of inclusion; hence, codification as § 2-131 was at the discretion of the editor.

Sec. 2-151. - Board of commissioners.
The Troup County Board of Commissioners shall adopt rules for regulating purchases by the county and it shall be the duty of all its employees to comply with said rules and be governed thereby.
( Ord. No. 2018-06 , § I, 9-18-2017)
Sec. 2-152. - County manager.
The Troup County Manager shall appoint a purchasing director and shall have the independent authority to make purchases and contracts as provided herein and in article III of chapter 2. Whenever approval of the board of commissioners is required and it has duly approved the purchase, sale, lease or other disposition of real or personal property or a contract for services, either the chairman or the vice-chair of the board of commissioners or the county manager may execute all documents necessary to consummate the approved agreement and transaction on behalf of the county.
( Ord. No. 2018-06 , § I, 9-18-2017)
Sec. 2-153. - Purchasing director.
(a)
There is hereby created the office of purchasing director of the county. The purchasing director shall be appointed by the county manager.
(b)
The purchasing director shall be appointed upon the basis of such person's administrative qualifications, with special reference to such person's actual experience in, or knowledge of, accepted practices in respect to the duties of the office as hereinafter set forth.
(c)
The purchasing director shall be bonded in an amount to be determined by the board of commissioners, payable to the county, and conditioned upon the faithful performance by the purchasing director of all official duties and upon the faithful accounting to the county for all goods and money that may come into said purchasing director's hands. The purpose of said bond shall be to indemnify and hold harmless the county against any losses or claims which may arise as a result of the acts of the purchasing director. The premium on the bond shall be paid by the county. The bonding requirement set forth herein may be satisfied by the procurement of a blanket bond issued in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 45-4-11.
(d)
It shall be the duty of the purchasing director to:
(1)
Review consistent with the authority provided herein and the purchasing rules and regulations adopted by the Troup County Board of Commissioners all purchases of supplies, materials and services over $500.00 on behalf of the county and all contracts. The terms "materials" and "supplies" shall include such items of personal property as are normally used and consumed during the current year, and for which a general appropriation has been made, and shall include, but not be limited to, the purchase of automotive equipment, machinery, items required under construction contracts or other purchases of material of a permanent or semi-permanent nature.
(2)
Exercise sound discretion in the best interest of the county when quality of goods and services as well as price are involved in any purchasing decision.
(3)
Explore opportunities for cooperative purchasing and, in furtherance thereof, shall have the authority to join with other units of government in cooperative purchasing plans as permitted by Georgia law when such plans would be in the best interest of the county.
(4)
Maintain a permanent inventory of all county property having a value of $5,000.00, or more.
( Ord. No. 2018-06 , § I, 9-18-2017)
Sec. 2-154. - Purchasing authority thresholds.
(a)
The purchasing director shall have authority to make purchases of materials, supplies and services on behalf of the county when the purchase price of said materials, supplies and services does not exceed $15,000.00 and when, in such director's sound discretion, it is determined by such director that such purchases are in the best interest of the county. In all purchases where quality of goods and services as well as price are involved, the purchasing director shall exercise sound discretion in making the purchases which are in the best interest of the county.
(b)
Purchases of materials, supplies and services on behalf of the county when the purchase price of said materials, supplies and services is between $15,000.00 and $25,000.00 shall be made by the purchasing director only after obtaining the written approval of the county manager or his designee.
(c)
Department heads or their designee may make purchases of materials, supplies and services on behalf of the county when the purchase price of said materials and supplies does not exceed $5,000.00.
(d)
Except as provided herein or in rules for regulating purchases duly adopted by the board of commissioners, no official, officer or employee of the county shall purchase for or on behalf of the county any material or supplies, goods, wares, or merchandise of any kind or character or services other than through the county manager or purchasing director, and no voucher, check or other methods of payment shall be honored by the county except as provided herein. This subsection does not apply to "emergency purchases" as hereinafter described.
( Ord. No. 2018-06 , § I, 9-18-2017)
Sec. 2-155. - Purchase on competitive bid or proposal.
(a)
Unless otherwise expressly provided for in this section, all purchases greater than $500.00 of materials, supplies and services shall be made on the basis of competitive bids or proposal whenever it is practicable to do so. Unless the rules for regulating purchases adopted by the board of commissioners provide to the contrary, the determination as to the practicality of obtaining competitive bids/proposals and the form and nature of the competitive bid/proposal process shall be in the discretion of the purchasing director for purchases greater than $500.00 that do not exceed $15,000.00 and the county manager for purchases between $15,000.00 and $25,000.00.
(b)
Purchases of materials, supplies and services on behalf of the county when the purchase price of said materials, supplies and services exceeds $25,000.00 shall be made on the basis of competitive bid or proposal after the same have been advertised in the official organ of the county or by electronic means on the county internet website or another internet website identified by the county. Provided, however, that the requirements set forth in this subsection shall not apply to (1) an item which has been identified as a part, part replacement, component, or state or federal surplus property or state and/or federal contract purchases made of items and equipment; (2) where a state and/or federal contract number has been assigned by the state purchasing director; (3) indefinite quantity construction services offered through the Georgia Department of Administrative Services; (4) purchases from other government agencies or at public auction; (5) contracts for professional services, which for purposes of this article are services from an independent contractor or consultant in an occupation which requires exceptional qualifications by education and experience in a particular field or discipline to perform a specialized service; (6) purchases where the board of commissioners has in its sound discretion determined that utilizing a competitive bid or proposal is impractical under the circumstances.
(c)
This section shall not apply to "emergency purchases" as hereinafter described.
( Ord. No. 2018-06 , § I, 9-18-2017)
Sec. 2-156. - Emergency purchases.
In all cases where there is imminent threat to public health, safety and welfare and the need for any material or supplies, goods, merchandise, or services to prevent and/or mitigate harm from the same, the department requesting the emergency purchase shall write on its requisition to the purchasing director the words, "For Emergency Purposes," and when a requisition so marked is received by the purchasing director it shall be the purchasing director's duty to give immediate attention to the same. In the event an emergency should arise after office hours which requires immediate action on the part of the department involved for the protection of the best interest of the county, or should a like situation arise on a Sunday or holiday, the purchasing director or county manager shall be contacted and approval given if the emergency purchase exceeds $25,000.00, and if the emergency purchase exceeds $100,000.00 the county manager and commission chairman must approve the expenditure, and any purchase necessary shall be made by the department head in charge, and such purchase reported to the purchasing director without delay on a purchase requisition.
( Ord. No. 2018-06 , § I, 9-18-2017)
Sec. 2-157. - Sale of real property.
(a)
Public sale required. Disposition of real property owned by the county shall be conducted by public sale in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 36-9-3, or as otherwise provided by state law.
(b)
Exceptions to public sale. The following transactions shall not be governed by subsection (a) of this section:
(1)
A redemption of real property acquired by county under tax deed.
(2)
A grant of easement or license.
(3)
A grant or conveyance of right-of-way or for other transportation purposes.
(4)
A conveyance to any other unit of government.
(5)
A conveyance of recreational set-aside property to a homeowner's association in the manner permitted by state law.
(6)
A lease that constitutes a usufruct under state law.
(7)
Any other conveyance not required to be by public sale pursuant to state law.
(c)
Procedures. Except as otherwise specifically provided by state law, the county shall not dispose of any real property unless the board of commissioners has declared such real property to be unserviceable/surplus. Other than dispositions governed by subsection (a) of this section or by specified provisions of state law, any disposition may be made at public or private sale, upon such terms as the board of commissioners shall deem to be in the county's best interest.
( Ord. No. 2018-06 , § I, 9-18-2017)
Sec. 2-158. - Sale of surplus real property by real estate broker.
Real property, specifically properties which are remnants of land from total takes on county road projects, may be disposed of by sale by real estate broker in accordance with state law, O.C.G.A. §§ 32-7-4 and 32-7-5.
(1)
Requirements for real estate brokers. Real estate brokers must be licensed in accordance with state law, O.C.G.A. § 43-40-1 et seq.
(2)
Declaration of surplus property. Property is declared surplus by the county board of commissioners in a regularly scheduled meeting.
(3)
Notification to former or subsequent owner. Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 32-7-4, the former or subsequent owner is notified in writing of intent to sell and has the first right to purchase the property at the fair market value price. If the former or subsequent owner waives this right and fails to exercise the right of acquisition within 30 days, then adjoining property owners are notified that the property will be sold by a licensed real estate broker who has a place of business located in Troup County or by competitive sealed bid.
(4)
Advertising and listing of property. Commencing at the time of the listing of the property as provided in O.C.G.A. § 32-7-4, the county shall publicly advertise once a week for two weeks in the legal organ of the county the property and the name of the broker, if any, handling the property. If listed with a broker, the property shall be listed for a period of at least three months. Property cannot be sold for less than fair market value excluding any commission fee. If property does not sell during the listing time, the county may renegotiate the commission to a lower fee, extend the exclusive agreement with the real estate broker or recommend to the board of commissioners that disposal be by competitive sealed bid or public auction.
(5)
Approval of sale. All sales of properties shall be approved by the board of commissioners at a regular scheduled public meeting in which public comment is allowed regarding such sale.
( Ord. No. 2018-06 , § I, 9-18-2017)
Sec. 2-159. - Disposition of personal property.
(a)
Declaration of unserviceable or surplus property. The board of commissioners shall determine whether personal property with an original unit purchase cost of $10,000.00 or more can no longer be used advantageously by the county and has therefore become unserviceable or surplus. The purchasing director is delegated the authority to determine whether personal property with an original unit purchase cost of less than $10,000.00 can no longer be used advantageously by the county and has therefore become unserviceable or surplus.
(b)
Disposition.
(1)
The disposition of unserviceable or surplus personal property with an original unit purchase cost of $10,000.00 or more must be approved by the board of commissioners and may be sold by public sale, sealed bidding, spot bidding or any other means deemed most advantageous to the county under the particular circumstances as determined by the board of commissioners.
(2)
The disposition of unserviceable or surplus personal property with an original unit purchase cost of $10,000.00 or less shall be approved by the purchasing director and may be sold by public sale, sealed bidding, spot bidding or any other means deemed most advantageous to the county under the particular circumstances as determined by the purchasing director.
(3)
A sale to a private person shall be for the highest net purchase price reasonably obtainable by the county. A sale to another unit of government shall be for a fair and reasonable purchase price that need not be as high as the purchase price obtainable from a private person.
(4)
If the residual value of an item of personal property is determined by the purchasing director after reasonable inquiry to be $100.00 or less, the property may be disposed of by the purchasing director in such manner and for such consideration, which need not be monetary, as the county manager deems most advantageous to the county under the circumstances.
(5)
If the residual value of an item of personal property is determined by the purchasing director after reasonable inquiry to be $100.00 or less, the property may be provided to a retiring employee by the county manager in recognition of their retirement and years of service to Troup County, Georgia.
( Ord. No. 2018-06 , § I, 9-18-2017)
Sec. 2-160. - Leases of county-owned real property.
(a)
The board of commissioners may offer to lease county-owned real property if:
(1)
The sale of the real property is not in the best interest of the county but the real property is not currently being used to be best advantage of the county.
(2)
The real property has been newly acquired but not immediately put to use by the county, and may reasonably be leased on a month-to-month or short-term basis.
(b)
A lessee's interest under any lease executed under this section shall constitute a usufruct only.
(c)
Where county-owned real estate is to be leased for nonpublic use, public notice of the availability of the real property shall be published once in legal organ and on the county website, soliciting inquiries and offers. The notice shall include as part of the solicitation the requirement that improvements to the real estate be insured for the county's benefit at the lessee's expense. The solicitation shall also describe any restrictions to be placed on the use of the real property. The county manager shall report to the board of commissioners all offers to lease the real estate and shall make recommendations as to which offer is in the county's best interest. The board of commissioners shall approve the offer that it deems to be in the best interest of the county. Such approval shall constitute authority for the chairman to execute a lease and any instruments on behalf of the county necessary to accept and effectuate the offer.
( Ord. No. 2018-06 , § I, 9-18-2017)

Sec. 2-226. - Application for indigent defense services.
Each person lacking the financial ability to pay for legal representation in his or her defense in criminal proceedings in and before the State Court of Troup County, Georgia, and desiring that the county provide such services to him or her, shall submit an application to the county requesting indigent defense representation and setting forth the specifics supporting their need for such indigent representation.
The application shall be completed and submitted to the court administrator upon intake at the jail or at the court administrator's office or at any state court appearance on a form approved by the governing authority of the county.
(Ord. No. 2005-03, § 1, 2-1-2005)
Sec. 2-227. - Application fee.
Each applicant for indigent defense services shall pay an application fee of $50.00 (or the maximum fee allowed by O.C.G.A. § 15-21A-6, as the same may be amended) to the court administrator of the county. Such fee must be paid either at the time the application is submitted or at the time the representation is provided.
(Ord. No. 2005-03, § 2, 2-1-2005)
Sec. 2-228. - Waiver.
The fee required by section 2-227 may be waived only upon order of the presiding judge scheduled to hear the case in which applicant is seeking a waiver of the application fee. The judge may order a waiver of the fee only upon a finding by the court that the defendant is unable to pay the fee or that paying the fee would cause hardship to the defendant.
(Ord. No. 2005-03, § 3, 2-1-2005)
Sec. 2-229. - Transfer of funds.
Within 15 days after the end of each month, the court administrator shall remit the total of all application fees collected pursuant to this division to the board of commissioners.
(Ord. No. 2005-03, § 4, 2-1-2005)
Sec. 2-230. - Separate account.
The board of commissioners shall maintain all funds remitted to it pursuant to this division in a separate "Indigent Defense Services Account" and funds from such account shall be expended only for the purpose of providing legal representation to indigent defendants in and before the State Court of Troup County, Georgia.
(Ord. No. 2005-03, § 5, 2-1-2005)
ARTICLE VI. - RECORDS MANAGEMENT

Sec. 2-231. - Title.
This article shall be known, and referred to, as the "Troup County Records Management Ordinance."
( Ord. No. 2020-06 , § II, 2-18-2020)
Sec. 2-232. - Purpose.
It is declared that the purpose of this article is to prevent the proliferation of records in original form maintained as public records, except to the extent the records may be necessary for permanent or periodic preservation as provided by state and federal laws. Further, it is the purpose of this article to establish and maintain an active and continuing program for the economical and efficient management of public records, and to provide for maximum utilization of county resources in office space and filing equipment through approved records maintenance and retention schedules.
( Ord. No. 2020-06 , § II, 2-18-2020)
Sec. 2-233. - Adoption of Georgia Records Act.
The board of commissioners hereby adopts the Georgia Records Act and such rules and regulations established pursuant to said Act, as amended, as a basis for establishing a records management program in and for Troup County. The terms and provisions of this article shall be construed in accordance with the provisions of the Georgia Records Act, as amended.
( Ord. No. 2020-06 , § II, 2-18-2020)
Sec. 2-234. - Definitions.
For the purpose of this article, the following terms, phrases, words and their derivations shall have the meaning given herein, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning.
Agency means any county office, department, division, board, bureau, commission or other separate unit of county government created or established by law and under the jurisdiction of the Troup County Board of Commissioners. Records of the judiciary, court personnel, and the county attorney's office are expressly excluded from this article.
County means Troup County, a political subdivision of the State of Georgia.
Record(s) means all documents, papers, letters, maps, books (except books in formally organized libraries), microfilm, magnetic tape, or other material, regardless of physical form or characteristics, made or received pursuant to law or ordinance or in performance of functions by any agency. All records created or received in the performance of a public duty or paid for by public funds by county are deemed to be public property and shall constitute a record of public acts.
Records management means the application of management techniques to the creation, utilization, maintenance, retention, preservation, and disposal of records undertaken to reduce costs and improve efficiency of record keeping. "Records management" includes management of filing and microfilming equipment and supplies; filing and information retrieval systems; files, correspondence, reports, and forms management; historical documentation; micrographics; retention programming; and vital records protection.
Retention schedule means a set of disposition instructions prescribing how long, where, and in what form records shall be kept.
Vital records means any record vital to the resumption or continuation of operations, or both; to the recreation of the legal and financial status of government in the state; or to the protection and fulfillment of obligations to citizens of the state.
( Ord. No. 2020-06 , § II, 2-18-2020)
Sec. 2-235. - Administration.
(a)
Designation of records management officer. The board of commissioners designates the director of the Troup County Historical Society or such director's successor in office as the records management officer for the county. Such designation is subject to the execution and existence of an appropriate contract with the Troup County Historical Society. In the absence of such contract, the Troup County Manager, or his designee, shall serve as records management officer.
(b)
Duties of records management officer.
(1)
The records management officer shall act for and in behalf of the county in directing and coordinating the records management matters required by, or appropriate under the terms of, the Georgia Records Act.
(2)
The records management officer shall make provision for and ensure that the maintenance, security, storage and destruction of records shall occur only through the operation of an approved retention schedule.
(3)
The records management officer shall prepare and operate according to written policies and procedures for the performance of the duties established herein, to include provisions for the preservation of records in the event of disaster, whether natural or manmade, or emergency.
(c)
Records committee.
(1)
Created; composition; general duties. There is hereby created a records committee, said committee to consist of the Troup County Clerk, Troup County Court Administrator, Troup County Manager or his/her designee, Troup County Sheriff or his designee, and the records management officer. It shall be the duty of the records committee to review and recommend approval, disapproval or modification of records retention schedules to the board of commissioners. Review of records retention schedules shall be based upon legal, fiscal, administrative and historical retention requirements.
(2)
Chairman; meetings; quorum. The records management officer shall serve as chairman of the records committee and shall schedule meetings of the committee as required. Three members shall constitute a quorum.
(3)
Schedules adopted by reference; authority of committee. The most recent "Retention Schedules for Local Government Records", as compiled by the Georgia Department of Archives and History and as approved by the state records committee, are hereby adopted and by reference incorporated herein and made a part of this Code as completely and to the same extent as if all provisions thereof were fully recited herein. Provided, however, that the records committee herein established and created may make recommendations to revise, amend, delete or add schedules not in conflict with state law and deemed advisable and necessary. Any such recommendations shall first be submitted to the state department of archives and history and the state records committee for their approvals. Upon approval of these two agencies, such recommendations shall be submitted to the board of commissions which shall have final approval authority.
(d)
Duties of county employees and officers. It shall be the duty of all county employees and officers to cooperate fully with the records committee and the records management officer in complying with the provisions of this article, the Georgia Records Act, and the approved record retention standards, policies and schedules.
( Ord. No. 2020-06 , § II, 2-18-2020)
Sec. 2-236. - Archival depository and records center.
Subject to the adoption and execution of an appropriate contract between the Troup County Historical Society and the county:
(1)
The Troup County Historical Society is hereby designated as the archival depository of the county for the purpose of storing records determined, pursuant to the provisions of this section and pursuant to the Georgia Records Act, to have permanent historical value.
(2)
The county premises located at 303 Broad Street LaGrange, Georgia is hereby designated the centralized record center and/or local documents holding area for Troup County. County records shall be transferred to such record center and/or local holding area to be retained until destroyed in accordance with the approved record retention schedule.
(3)
The manner of secure transportation and transitory storage of records shall be at the discretion of the records management officer, who shall be responsible for security of such records during transportation and transitory storage.
(4)
Additional or alternate secure locations for the holding or permanent storage of county records may be approved by the records committee.
( Ord. No. 2020-06 , § II, 2-18-2020)
Sec. 2-237. - Access to records.
(a)
Records shall be maintained and accessible as necessary to comply with the Georgia Records Act and O.C.G.A. § 50-18-70 et seq. The county archival depository and records center shall be open during normal and reasonable hours and, except as otherwise provided by law, ordinance, or policy adopted by the board of commissioners, all county records shall be open to the public or the state or any agency thereof.
(b)
In all cases where an interested member of the public has a right to inspect or take extracts or make copies from public records, instruments or documents, any such person shall have the right of access to the same while in the possession, custody and control of the records management officer, or his authorized deputy. Such work shall be done under the supervision of the records management officer, who shall have the right to adopt and enforce reasonable rules governing the work. Unless otherwise required by law or court order, all inspections, extracts or copies shall be done in the room where the records, documents or instruments are kept by law.
(c)
The records management officer may make copies of any records or any preservation duplicates transferred or deposited in the archival depository or records center or may make reproductions of such records. The records management officer may establish and charge reasonable fees for such services. Nothing contained herein shall authorize the imposition of any charge for public access to records not allowed by state law.
( Ord. No. 2020-06 , § II, 2-18-2020)
Sec. 2-238. - Tampering with records; penalty.
The alienation, alteration, theft or destruction of records by any person or persons in a manner not authorized by the applicable retention schedule is, pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 50-18-102, declared to be a violation of this article and subject to the penalties provided in section 1-19 of the Troup County Code.
( Ord. No. 2020-06 , § II, 2-18-2020)
Sec. 2-239. - Personal liability.
Pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 50-18-102(d), no person acting in compliance with the provisions of the Georgia Records Act shall be held personally liable.
( Ord. No. 2020-06 , § II, 2-18-2020)
