ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 46-1. - Establishment of court.
In accordance with the Charter, there is established the municipal court of the city. This court shall have jurisdiction and authority to try offenses against laws and ordinances of the city and to punish for a violation of such laws and ordinances.
(Code 1980, § 7-1)
Sec. 46-2. - Powers enumerated.
The municipal court shall have the following powers:
(1)
Conduct hearings. To hear and determine all causes of prosecution for violating the penal laws and ordinances of the city, and all other controversies arising under the laws and ordinances of the city;
(2)
Subpoena power. To compel the attendance of parties and witnesses and compel the production of papers;
(3)
Issuance of process. To issue all processes and writs necessary to the exercise of its jurisdiction;
(4)
Punish for contempt. To punish contempts by fine or imprisonment, or both;
(5)
Appoint officers. To appoint such special officers as may be necessary in the absence of the chief of police to execute its writs and processes;
(6)
Prescribe rules. To prescribe rules of practice in cases where there are no rules applicable;
(7)
Prescribe bond. To prescribe amount of appearance bonds for violation of city ordinances;
(8)
Authority as to violations of state law. To bind over or commit to jail offenders against any criminal law of this state, whenever in the course of investigation before such officer a proper case shall be made out by the evidence; and
(9)
Other powers. To exercise such other powers as are necessary in the matters of its jurisdiction, or as may be conferred on it by law or ordinance.
(Code 1980, § 7-2)
Sec. 46-3. - Imposition of sentences.
(a)
The municipal court shall have authority to try offenses against the laws of the city and shall have power to impose fines and/or sentences of imprisonment. A suspended sentence shall be interpreted to mean that the defendant, during the term of the sentence, shall not again violate the law.
(b)
The court shall have the authority to impose alternative forms of punishment allowed by law in case fines are not paid.
(c)
The court shall have the authority to impose a sentence of community service.
(Code 1980, § 7-3)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 36-32-5.
Sec. 46-4. - Appearance of complaining witness; service of notice on.
Any person who shall cause a warrant or summons to be issued against any person for the violation of a city ordinance shall be compelled to appear in the municipal court and to give evidence against such person upon proper notice of the time and place of the hearing, by the issuance of a subpoena by the police department, which subpoena shall be served upon the witness personally or by leaving a copy with a member of his family who is 18 years of age or over; or by posting the subpoena in a conspicuous place on the front door of the dwelling house of the witness; or by serving the witness with a copy of the subpoena by registered mail.
(Code 1980, § 7-4)
Sec. 46-5. - Imposition of cost and fees upon prosecutor of complainant.
The municipal judge may, in his discretion, assess a fee of $100.00 plus ten percent costs, against any individual or other complainant who procures an arrest warrant and subsequently abandons prosecution before trial. This section shall not apply to law enforcement personnel.
(Code 1980, § 7-5)
Sec. 46-6. - Imposition of costs of prosecution upon prosecutor.
Costs of prosecution shall not be chargeable to the prosecutor unless the court trying the case shall find the prosecution malicious and without probable cause, in which event the prosecutor shall pay the cost, under penalty of punishment for contempt.
(Code 1980, § 7-6)
State Law reference— When prosecutor shall pay costs, O.C.G.A. § 17-11-4.
Sec. 46-7. - Execution to collect fine.
On failure of any person upon whom a fine is imposed to pay the fine, execution may issue to collect the fine although such person is imprisoned in default of paying such fine.
(Code 1980, § 7-7)
Sec. 46-8. - Time limitation on prosecution for violation of city ordinances.
All prosecutions for violations of city ordinances shall be commenced within two years after such violations; and any offenses against such ordinances for which no prosecution is begun within two years shall be barred and no person shall be tried therefor unless the party shall have been beyond the limits of the city, in which case the time absence from the city shall not be embraced or computed as within the two years.
(Code 1980, § 7-8)
Sec. 46-9. - Judgments to be recorded.
Upon the trial of any case, the records of the court shall be in the form and manner prescribed by the judge of the municipal court.
(Code 1980, § 7-9)
Sec. 46-10. - Payment of fines and forfeitures into general fund.
All moneys collected in fines and forfeitures in the municipal court shall be deposited to the general fund of the city.
(Code 1980, § 7-10)
Sec. 46-11. - Return of warrants.
Arrest warrants issued for offenses alleged to have been committed in the city which have been issued by any judicial officer of this county (except special warrants issued by a superior court judge) and executed by any police officer of this city shall be returned to the court and entered on the docket of the court.
(Code 1980, § 7-11)
Sec. 46-12. - Proceedings to forfeit recognizances.
Upon the failure of any principal in any bond or recognizance given by a person charged with an ordinance violation to appear, or of a prosecutor to prosecute, or of a witness to appear and testify, the judge shall proceed to forfeit such bond or recognizance, and shall thereupon issue a rule nisi directing the principal and surety to show cause at a date and time more than 30 days thereafter why judgment absolute should not be issued for the amount of the principal sum of the bond together with cost, and directing the clerk of the court to issue a scire facias returnable at the same time as the rule nisi against the principal and the surety.
(Code 1980, § 7-12)
State Law reference— Authority of council to provide by ordinance for bond forfeiture, O.C.G.A. § 36-32-4.
Sec. 46-13. - Judgment against bail.
The clerk shall issue a scire facias on all forfeited bonds, recognizances or other obligations, returnable at the time and date directed by the judge in the rule nisi, against the principal and his sureties, which shall be served by the chief of police or any police officer of the city, along with the rule nisi, or by registered or certified mail, with return receipt requested, at least five days before the return; and if the party resides out of state, scire facias may be served by publication in the official organ of the city once a week for two consecutive weeks prior to the return date, with proof of service entered on the record of the case. If at such return date no sufficient cause shall be shown to the contrary, judgment shall be entered against such principal and sureties or such of them as have been served.
(Code 1980, § 7-13)
Sec. 46-14. - Right to order imprisonment.
(a)
The municipal judge may, in his discretion, punish a person convicted of violating any law or ordinance of the city by imprisoning such person for a period not exceeding six months. The municipal judge may, in his discretion, combine such imprisonment with monetary fines.
(b)
The limitations contained in subsection (a) of this section shall not apply to any powers of imprisonment the municipal judge may have over a person convicted of a violation of state or other laws the municipal judge is empowered to enforce.
(Code 1980, § 7-14)
Sec. 46-15. - Court costs.
Unless otherwise established by ordinance, court costs shall be as determined by the judge of the municipal court.
(Ord. No. 768, 9-6-2001)
Sec. 46-16. - Standard fine/cash bond schedule for code enforcement violations.
The city shall adopt a standard fine/cash bond schedule for code enforcement violations for which civil penalties may be cited. Said standard fine/cash bond schedule shall be kept on file at the court clerk's office and updated from time to time as deemed necessary by the city.
(Ord. No. 995, § 1, 4-1-2010)
Secs. 46-17. - Failure to appear; fee.
The city hereby adopts a standard fee for defendants who fail to appear for their scheduled court dates for arraignment and trial. Said standard fee shall be as determined by the mayor and city council in accordance with O.C.G.A. 40-13-63.
(Ord. No. 1089, § 1, 2-5-2015)
Secs. 46-18—46-45. - Reserved.

Sec. 46-46. - Time and place of court sessions.
There shall be eight regular sessions of court each month commencing at 8:30 a.m. and 1:00 p.m. on Wednesday at city hall, except that the judge may substitute the fifth Wednesday if necessary or may in recognition of holidays not hold court on certain weeks of the year. The judge of the court may establish other court sessions as necessary.
(Code 1980, § 7-20; Ord. No. 895, § 1, 1-18-2007; Ord. No. 1022, § 1, 3-3-2011)
Sec. 46-47. - Officers of court.
(a)
The chief of police or any police officer or bailiff designated by the chief shall be the marshal of the municipal court.
(b)
The clerk of the court shall keep and be the custodian of the records.
(c)
The city solicitor is the prosecutor for the city. In that capacity, the city solicitor may discuss the case with a defendant or a defendant's counsel and the arresting officer and may suggest to the court the most expeditious manner of disposing of the case in order to promote the interests of justice. The manner of disposition of the case, however, is always left to the discretion of the court after hearing in open court as delineated in section 46-46.
(d)
The court will not discuss any case with any defendant, that defendant's representative, the prosecuting officer and the city solicitor without both sides being given the opportunity to be present. A pretrial conference, however, to clarify issues for trial may be appropriate in certain cases so long as this section is not violated.
(Code 1980, § 7-21; Ord. No. 895, § 1, 1-18-2007)
Sec. 46-48. - Rules of evidence.
The rules of evidence which govern the trials in the superior courts of this state shall be in effect in the municipal court.
(Code 1980, § 7-22)
Sec. 46-49. - Continuances.
Continuances will be granted by the court only in open court at the time set for trial on motion of any party or his attorney; except that first continuances may be granted by the court clerk, city solicitor, the arresting officer or the chief of police, prior to the court date on request of a defendant or his counsel, provided all witnesses who have been subpoenaed shall be duly notified. Second and subsequent continuances will only be granted by the court in open session for good cause shown.
(Code 1980, § 7-23; Ord. No. 895, § 1, 1-18-2007)
Sec. 46-50. - Forfeiture of cash bonds and mandatory appearance.
The failure of a defendant, who has posted cash bond for his appearance, to appear at the time of trial, will result in the forfeiture of the cash bond, and such forfeiture will be a final disposition of the case, except for the following charges:
(a)
Any violation of chapter 6 of the Peachtree City Municipal Code (Alcoholic Beverages).
(b)
Disorderly conduct (§ 50-10).
(c)
Violation of Georgia Controlled Substance Act (O.C.G.A. § 16-13-30(a)).
(d)
No insurance (O.C.G.A. § 40-6-10).
(e)
Theft by shoplifting.
(f)
Any traffic offense for which the law makes mandatory the suspension or revocation of a driver's license.
(g)
Any traffic offense resulting in an accident when one or more persons are transported to a hospital.
(h)
Minor in possession of alcoholic beverages.
The forfeiture of cash bond in any of the foregoing enumerated cases will not finally dispose of a case; but the court, in its discretion, may issue a warrant for the defendant; and the forfeited cash will not be applied to the payment of a fine subsequently imposed.
(Code 1980, § 7-24; Ord. No. 895, § 1, 1-18-2007)
State Law reference— Forfeiture of cash bond in traffic cases, O.C.G.A. § 40-13-58.
Sec. 46-51. - Issuance of citation without requiring bail.
Officers may issue citations without requiring bail, and without requiring the surrender of a driver's license in lieu of bail, to any defendant, provided the offense charged is not one listed as a mandatory suspension of license offense in O.C.G.A. § 40-5-54 or 40-5-70.
(Code 1980, § 7-25; Ord. No. 895, § 1, 1-18-2007)
Sec. 46-52. - Setting arraignment date.
The arraignment date of all cases will be fixed by the officer issuing the citation, allowing not less than seven days from the date of the issuance of the citation to the date of trial.
(Ord. No. 895, § 1, 1-18-2007)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 895, § 1, adopted January 18, 2007, amended § 46-52 in its entirety to read as herein set out. Formerly, § 46-52 pertained to setting trial date, and derived from the Code of 1980, § 7-26.
Sec. 46-53. - Record check.
The court clerk shall have available at the time of the trial of all traffic cases the driver's history as furnished by the department of driver services and shall not make the history available to the court until after the defendant enters a plea of guilty or nolo contendere or finding of guilt by the court.
(Code 1980, § 7-27; Ord. No. 895, § 1, 1-18-2007)
Sec. 46-54. - Nolo contendere pleas in driving under the influence cases.
All parties and their attorneys submitting a verified petition requesting a nolo contendere plea be accepted on a charge of driving under the influence, as provided in O.C.G.A. § 40-6-391.1, shall file the petition on or before the call of the case on the calendar unless extended by the court for good cause shown.
(Code 1980, § 7-28)
Sec. 46-55. - Cash bond acceptance.
Cash bonds are not to be accepted after 1:00 p.m. on the date prior to the date the case is set for trial.
(Code 1980, § 7-29; Ord. No. 895, § 1, 1-18-2007)
Sec. 46-56. - Violation of rules.
Any violation of the rules of this article, as now in force or as may be hereafter promulgated, will be dealt with as a contempt of court, and punished as provided in the Charter of this city.
(Code 1980, § 7-30)
Sec. 46-57. - Pre-trial intervention program.
(a)
A defendant may apply in writing before the commencement of trial for participation in the city's Pre-Trial Intervention Program (herein referred to as PTI Program). The prosecutor may consider the circumstances of the case (s) and/or the defendant's criminal history when deciding approval of the defendant's participation in the program. Acceptance in the program is based on but not limited to the following conditions:
(1)
The defendant has no prior record for the same or similar offense(s).
(2)
The defendant is willing to tender a guilty plea to the offense(s), pay a fine, and comply with all conditions recommended by the prosecutor.
(3)
The defendant has never been sentenced under the city PTI Program or similar program.
(4)
The defendant pays an administrative fee of up to $1,000.00.
(b)
Failure of the defendant to comply with conditions will result in adjudication and transmittal of the original sentence. Compliance with all conditions, however, will result in dismissal of the offense(s).
(c)
PTI consideration will not be granted for any offense requiring mandatory suspension of a driver's license pursuant to state law, code enforcement violations, furnishing alcohol to a minor, alcohol permit violations, state traffic offenses, or offenses for which the city does not have jurisdiction.
(Ord. No. 1035, § 1, 9-15-2011; Ord. No. 1063, § 1, 6-6-2013; Ord. No. 1154, § 1, 11-15-2018)
