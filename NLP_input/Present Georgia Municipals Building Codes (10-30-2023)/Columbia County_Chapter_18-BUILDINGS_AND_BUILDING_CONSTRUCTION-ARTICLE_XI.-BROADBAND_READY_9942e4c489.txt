ARTICLE XI. - BROADBAND READY COMMUNITY

Sec. 18-384. - Definitions.
(a)
As used in this article, "broadband network project" means any deployment of broadband services.
(b)
As used in this article, "political subdivision" means a county, municipal corporation, consolidated government, or local authority.
(c)
As used in this article, "applicant" means a person applying for a permit for a broadband network project.
(d)
As used in this article, "permit" means any local permit, license, certificate approval, registration, or similar form of approval required by policy, administrative rule, regulation, ordinance, or resolution with respect to a broadband network project.
( Ord. No. 21-07 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-2-2021)

Sec. 18-386. - Application.
(a)
Application.
(1)
Applications under this chapter shall be submitted to Columbia County and must include the applicant's email address.
(2)
Columbia County shall determine whether an application is incomplete and notify the applicant, by email, of such determination within ten calendar days of receiving an application.
(3)
If the county does not respond to the applicant on whether the application is incomplete, within ten calendar days, the application shall be assumed to be complete on the 11th day.
(b)
Notification of Incomplete Application.
(1)
If Columbia County determines that an application is not complete, the notification by email to the applicant shall specify all mandatory components of the submitted application that were considered 'incomplete';
(2)
If the county's response includes a checklist of sequenced items that resulted in the application being deemed 'incomplete' then the review timeline shall be as follows:
a.
The applicant has up to 40 calendar days from the date of notification of incompleteness to respond back with corrections; and
b.
If the applicant does not respond back within 40 calendar days, the application is deemed canceled.
(3)
If within ten calendar days the county does not respond to the applicant on whether the corrected application is incomplete, the application shall be assumed to be complete on the 11th day; and
(4)
Columbia County shall require a new submission and reset the process and application fees, should an application be deemed incomplete a second time.
( Ord. No. 21-07 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-2-2021)
Sec. 18-387. - Approval or denial notification.
If, on or before the 11th day as described in section 18-386(a)(2), an application is deemed complete, Columbia County shall approve or deny an application within ten calendar days unless Columbia Count deems a joint meeting between the county and the applicant as necessary.
(1)
If a joint meeting is deemed necessary, the joint meeting must occur within 15 calendars days of notice of that an application is complete and the joint meeting shall include:
a.
Where applicant is going to conduct work,
b.
When the work will be conducted,
c.
What type of work will be done,
d.
Who the county can contact for specific details or related questions, and
e.
Any permit seeking approval under application.
Following a joint meeting between the applicant and Columbia County, the county shall deny or approve the application within ten calendar days from the date of the joint meeting.
(2)
Upon final approval, any required permit shall be deemed issued.
( Ord. No. 21-07 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-2-2021)
Sec. 18-388. - Related fees.
(a)
An application under this chapter shall be accompanied by a fee to cover Columbia County's review of the application, issuance of any permit, and/or performance of any other activity related to a broadband network project. The fee shall be reasonable, cost based, and nondiscriminatory to all applicants.
(b)
If an application fee exceeds $100.00, Columbia County shall, upon the request of the applicant, provide documentation justifying such fee based on specific costs.
( Ord. No. 21-07 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-2-2021)
Sec. 18-389. - Other information.
(a)
Double fee: Columbia County shall not require an application, permit(s), or fee when an application or permit has already been approved by an authorized state or federal jurisdiction. Provider shall notify and provide a copy of the approved permit to the single-point-of-contact at the county prior to access of right-of-way within the county jurisdiction.
(b)
Application validity timeline: Any approved application shall be valid for six-months from the date of approval. Should a provider not commence the service request qualified in the approved application within six months, the application shall expire, and it shall require a new application be filed and payment of the application fee.
(c)
Single service drop: Columbia County shall not require a permit for a broadband service provider to perform an installation of broadband service at an individual customer's service address as long as the facility being utilized only transverses a de minimis portion of the public right-of-way to reach the customer's property. The provider must still comply with the provisions of Chapter 9 of Title 25 of the O.C.G.A.
( Ord. No. 21-07 , § 1(Exh. A), 11-2-2021)
