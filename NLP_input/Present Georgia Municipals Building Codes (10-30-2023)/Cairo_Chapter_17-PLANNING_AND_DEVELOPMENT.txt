Sec. 17-1. - City planning commission—Membership.
(a)
Number: The city planning commission shall consist of five (5) members, appointed by the governing body.
(b)
Qualifications: Members of the city planning commission shall be residents of the city, and a majority shall be persons who hold no other public office in the city or county.
(c)
Term of office: The terms of office of the members of the city planning commission shall be for five (5) years.
(d)
Vacancies: Any vacancy in the city planning commission shall be filled for the unexpired term by the governing body.
(e)
Removal: The governing body may remove any member of the city planning commission for cause, on written charges, after a public hearing.
(f)
Compensation; expenses: All members of the city planning commission shall serve without compensation, but may be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in connection with their official duties.
(Code 1965, §§ 2-51—2-56)
Sec. 17-2. - Same—Organization.
(a)
The city planning commission shall elect one of its appointive members as chairman, who shall serve for one (1) year or until such person is re-elected or a successor is elected.
(b)
A second appointive member shall be elected as vice-chairman and shall serve for one (1) year or until re-elected or a successor is elected.
(c)
The city planning commission shall appoint a secretary, who may be an officer or employee of the municipality or of the city planning commission.
(d)
The city planning commission shall promulgate its own rules of procedure.
(e)
The city planning commission shall determine the times of its meetings.
(f)
All meetings of the city planning commission at which official action is taken shall be open to the public and all records of the commission shall be public records.
(Code 1965, §§ 2-57—2-59)
Sec. 17-3. - Same—Technical assistance.
The city planning commission may appoint such employees and staff as it may deem necessary for its work and may contract with the state planning agency and city planners and other consultants for such services as it may require.
(Code 1965, § 2-60)
Sec. 17-4. - Same—Expenditures.
The expenditures of the city planning commission, exclusive of gifts, shall be within the amounts appropriated for the purpose by the governing body.
(Code 1965, § 2-62)
Sec. 17-5. - City-county planning commission—Appointment of representative; term of office.
The five (5) representatives for this city on the Cairo-Whigham-Grady County Planning Commission shall be appointed by the governing body of this city, to hold office for terms of three (3) years.
(Code 1965, § 2-77)
Sec. 17-6. - Same—Vacancies.
If a vacancy shall occur in any of the positions on the Cairo-Whigham-Grady County Planning Commission held by representatives of this city, such vacancy shall be filled by appointment by the governing body of this city for the unexpired term.
(Code 1965, § 2-78)
Sec. 17-7. - Same—Removal.
The five (5) representatives for this city on the Cairo-Whigham-Grady County Planning Commission may be removed at any time for cause, after notice and public hearing.
(Code 1965, § 2-79)
Sec. 17-8. - Same—Service without compensation.
The five (5) representatives for this city on the Cairo-Whigham-Grady County Planning Commission shall serve without compensation.
(Code 1965, § 2-80)
Sec. 17-9. - Same—Reimbursement for expenses.
The five (5) representatives for this city on the Cairo-Whigham-Grady County Planning Commission shall be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in connection with their official duties.
(Code 1965, § 2-81)
Sec. 17-10. - Southwest Georgia Planning and Development Commission—Appointment of representative; term of office.
The representative of this city on the Southwest Georgia Planning and Development Commission shall be appointed by the governing body of this city to hold office for a term of five (5) years, except that the first appointment under this section shall be for three (3) years.
(Code 1965, § 2-68)
Sec. 17-11. - Same—Service without compensation.
The representative of this city shall serve without compensation.
(Code 1965, § 2-69)
Sec. 17-12. - Same—Removal.
The representative of this city may be removed for cause, upon written charges, after a public hearing.
(Code 1965, § 2-70)
Sec. 17-13. - Same—Vacancies.
Vacancies in the office of the representative of this city shall be filled by the governing body of this city for the unexpired term.
(Code 1965, § 2-71)
Sec. 17-14. - Downtown development authority—Activated.
There is hereby activated in the city the public body corporate and politic known as the "Downtown Development Authority of the City of Cairo" which was created upon the adoption and approval of the Downtown Development Authorities Law.
(Res. of 10-11-82)
State Law reference— Downtown Development Authorities Law, OCGA § 36-42-1 et seq.
Sec. 17-15. - Same—Directors.
(a)
The governing body of the city shall appoint the seven (7) members of the board of directors of the downtown development authority, each of whom shall be a taxpayer of the city, and not less than four (4) of whom either have, or represent a party who has, an economic interest in the redevelopment and revitalization of the downtown development area.
(b)
Each of the persons named above as directors shall serve in staggered terms, two (2) for two (2) years, two (2) for four (4) years and three (3) for six (6) years. After expiration of initial terms, the terms of all directors shall be six (6) years. If at the end of the term of office of any director a successive to such director has not been elected the director whose term of office has expired shall continue to hold office until a successor is elected.
(Res. of 10-11-82; Ord. No. 687, § 1, 4-28-08)
Sec. 17-16. - Same—Organization.
The board of directors hereinbefore elected shall organize itself, carry out its duties and responsibilities and exercise its powers and prerogatives in accordance with the terms and provisions of the downtown development authorities law as it now exists and as it might hereafter be amended or modified.
(Res. of 10-11-82)
Sec. 17-17. - Same—Development area.
The geographical area subject to downtown development authority jurisdiction shall be defined as follows: An area bounded on the north by 3 rd Avenue, east by 3 rd Street, south by 3 rd Avenue, and west by 2 nd Street, together with properties fronting on said streets.
(Res. of 10-11-82; Ord. No. 687, § 2, 4-28-08)
