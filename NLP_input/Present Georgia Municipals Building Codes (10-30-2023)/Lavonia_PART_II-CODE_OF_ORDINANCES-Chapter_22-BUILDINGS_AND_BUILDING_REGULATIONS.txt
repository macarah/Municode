ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 22-1. - Permit fees.
Permit fees under this chapter shall be in the amounts referred to in section 2-191.
Sec. 22-2. - Codes adopted.
The city adopts by reference the following most recent publication of the International Codes and National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) 70 National Electrical Code as adopted by the State of Georgia:
International Building Code.
International Fire Code.
International Fuel Gas Code.
International Plumbing Code.
International Mechanical Code.
International Property Maintenance Code.
National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) 70 National Electrical Code.
The adoption of the above codes will include adoption by reference of any amendments or revisions to said codes which has occurred or may occur after the adopted publication date.
(Code 1995, § 15.04.010; Ord. of 1-7-2008, § 1; Ord. of 12-3-12, § I)
Sec. 22-3. - Building permit required.
Any person or business performing any construction, to include new construction or remodeling which exceeds $1,000.00 in cost, must first obtain a building permit and pay the fees provided for in section 22-1.
(Ord. of 5-3-2010(2), § I)

ARTICLE II. - BUILDING DEPARTMENT

FOOTNOTE(S):
--- (2) ---
Cross reference— Administration, ch. 2.
Sec. 22-31. - Established; creation of offices.
(a)
There is established in the city a department to be called the building department, which shall be in charge of a building official.
(b)
There are created the following offices in the city: building inspector, gas inspector, plumbing inspector, housing inspector, electrical inspector and fire inspector, to be appointed by the mayor and council to serve at their pleasure and for such compensation as may be prescribed by them. One person may serve in each and all of the several capacities named in the section. Such official or officials need not devote his entire time to the duties thereof.
(c)
During the temporary absence or disability of such officer or officers, the appointing authority shall designate an acting official to serve.
(Code 1995, § 2.16.010)
Sec. 22-32. - Right of entry.
The building official or any of the inspectors named in section 22-31, in the discharge of his official duties, and upon proper identification, shall have authority to enter any building, structure or premises at any reasonable hour.
(Code 1995, § 2.16.020)

ARTICLE III. - BUILDING NUMBERS

Sec. 22-61. - System established.
A uniform system for numbering all houses, buildings and other structures within the corporate limits of the city is hereby established.
(Ord. of 5-5-2003(2), § 1)
Sec. 22-62. - Assignment of numbers.
Numbers for all houses, businesses, and other structures within the corporate limits of the city shall be numbered and posted in conformity with numbers established and assigned by the county E-9-1-1 emergency numbering system.
(Ord. of 5-5-2003(2), § 2)
Sec. 22-63. - Posting of numbers.
(a)
The owner or occupant or person in charge of any house, building, or other structure to which a number has been assigned and which number has not been posted will post such number in conformity with this article.
(b)
The city, the owner or occupant or person in charge of a house, building, or other structure to which a number has been assigned shall affix the assigned number in a conspicuous manner in a conspicuous place. If the house, building, or other structure receives on-premises mail by a U.S. Postal Service approved box, the number may be affixed to that post office box or to any structure supporting that mailbox. Otherwise, the number shall be displayed on the front of the building or in some other manner in front of the front entrance, provided the number is clearly discernable and visible from the street, private drive, or alley.
(c)
It shall be the duty of such owner or occupant or person in charge thereof upon affixing a number to remove any number which might be mistaken for, or confused with, the number assigned to such structure by the county E-9-1-1 system.
(d)
In case a building or house is occupied by more than one business or family dwelling unit, each separate front entrance may display a separate number. If a building or other structure also has an entrance or exit abutting on an alley or a side street the same number as assigned herein shall be affixed to the rear or side entrance.
(e)
Numerals indicating the official numbers shall be posted in a manner so as to be legible and distinguishable from the street, private drive or alley on which the property is located.
(Ord. of 5-5-2003(2), § 3)
Sec. 22-64. - New structures.
No building permit shall be issued for any house, building, or other structure until the owner or developer has procured from the county E-9-1-1 system the official number of the premises. Final approval for a certificate of occupancy of any house, building, or other structure erected or repaired after May 5, 2003, shall be withheld until permanent and proper numbers have been displayed in accordance with this article.
(Ord. of 5-5-2003(2), § 4)
Sec. 22-65. - Penalties.
If the owner or occupant or person in charge of any house or building refuses to comply with the terms of this article by failing to affix the number assigned within 90 days after notification, or by failing within such 90-day period to remove any old numbers affixed to such house, or house entrance, or elsewhere, which may be confused with the number assigned thereto, he may be punished as provided for in section 1-12.
(Ord. of 5-5-2003(2), § 5)
