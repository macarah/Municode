ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 26-1. - Base map; official system of street and road names established.
There is established an official system of street and road names as shown on the base map entitled Base Map—Wilkes County, Georgia, Dated July 12, 1990, as produced by the Central Savannah River Area Regional Development Center, a copy of which is made a part of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 071290-A, § 1, 7-12-1990)
Sec. 26-2. - Street names in the unincorporated areas of the county.
The names of streets and roads of the unincorporated areas of the county shall remain as shown on said map, unless officially changed by ordinance.
(Ord. No. 071290-A, § 2, 7-12-1990)
Sec. 26-3. - Acceptance of new streets and municipal improvements of the county.
No new streets or roads shall be accepted by the county and no municipal improvements shall be made therein until such street or road has been properly named. If said street or road is an extension of an existing street or road, the existing name shall be continued. If not an extension of an existing street or road, names recorded shall not duplicate or closely approximate street or road names that have already been assigned.
(Ord. No. 071290-A, § 3, 7-12-1990)
Sec. 26-4. - Maintenance of streets.
The county planning commission shall be responsible for maintenance of the system of street and road names. The planning commission shall maintain a copy of the map adopted by this chapter and shall record thereon all changes made by ordinance or new assignments accepted by subdivision platting or other legal means. The planning commission shall cause to be made revisions to the official map to include all official changes made up to date of revision, which is also to be clearly recorded thereon.
(Ord. No. 071290-A, § 4, 7-12-1990)
Secs. 26-5—26-26. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - ROADS AND RIGHT-OF-WAY USE

Sec. 26-27. - Permit required.
No person shall begin to construct, re-construct, repair, alter or grade any right-of-way at any private entrance, exit, ramp, bridge, side road or other vehicular passageway, on the public county roads within the unincorporated areas of the county without first obtaining a permit from the tax assessor's office in the manner specified in this article. No person (contractor or property owner) shall utilize any existing county road right-of-way for ingress or egress to private property within the unincorporated areas of the county for the purpose of doing construction, grading, land improvements, logging, pulpwooding, timber harvesting and other operations without first obtaining a permit from the tax assessor's office in the manner specified in this article. The routine maintenance, grading, graveling of a private road or driveway by the owner or by the contractor for the owner shall not require a permit and shall not be subject to this article.
(Ord. No. 11142013RD, § 1, 11-14-2013; Ord. of 1-9-2014)
Sec. 26-28. - General standards for issuance of special temporary right-of-way entry permit.
Said permit shall apply to both new and existing right-of-way entry. The tax assessor's office shall issue a permit hereunder when the applicant agrees to the following:
(1)
That the work shall be done according to the standard specifications of the county for public work of like character;
(2)
That the operation will not unreasonably interfere with vehicular and pedestrian traffic or the means of ingress to and egress from the affected and adjacent properties;
(3)
That the health, welfare and safety of the public will not be unreasonably impaired; and
(4)
That the roadways will not be damaged and proper water drainage will not be adversely affected.
(Ord. No. 11142013RD, § 2, 11-14-2013; Ord. of 1-9-2014)
Sec. 26-29. - Construction and supervision.
(a)
All work for which a permit is granted hereunder shall be constructed according to the specifications established by the county so that the drainage, access, compaction, materials, dimensions, slope, width and other aspects of its conservation shall not unreasonably interfere with traffic flow and drainage considerations.
(b)
All construction for which a special temporary right-of-way entry permit is granted hereunder shall be under the direction and supervision of the public works director or his designee who shall have the authority to order the applicant to immediately cease all operations when applicant does not construct and maintain the right-of-way entry according to county standards.
(Ord. No. 11142013RD, § 3, 11-14-2013; Ord. of 1-9-2014)
Sec. 26-30. - Application.
(a)
Every person required to procure a special temporary right-of-way entry permit under the provisions of this article shall submit a written application for such permit to the tax assessor's office.
(b)
The written application shall state the following:
(1)
The name and address and telephone number of the applicant.
(2)
The name and address and telephone number of the property owner.
(3)
The location of the right-of-way entry including map and parcel information.
(4)
The purpose for which the application is submitted.
(5)
The nature and location of any work proposed to be done and the name of the person or firm who is to do such work, inclusive of their contact inform.
(6)
The date of commencement and the date of completion of the proposed work and other data or plans as may reasonably be required by the public works director of the county.
(Ord. No. 11142013RD, § 4, 11-14-2013; Ord. of 1-9-2014)
Sec. 26-31. - Plan required.
Prior to issuance of any permit, the applicant shall submit a plan of all right-of-way entries to be used for such operations which are adjacent to any public road, to include graveling when required by weather conditions. The public works director shall have the authority to require suitable materials and installation of a pipe under such right-of-way entries.
(Ord. No. 11142013RD, § 5, 11-14-2013; Ord. of 1-9-2014)
Sec. 26-32. - Issuance of permit.
The tax assessor's office, after a review of the application, shall issue a permit. The permit shall be valid for the permitted site until released or terminated pursuant to the terms of this article. The public works director shall have the authority to revoke a permit when determined that this policy is or has been violated.
(Ord. No. 11142013RD, § 6, 11-14-2013; Ord. of 1-9-2014)
Sec. 26-33. - Appeal of decision on revocation of permit.
(a)
An appeal may be taken to the board of commissioners of the county by any applicant affected by the decisions the public works director of the county within 30 days after the date of the decision of the public works director. The decision of the public works director shall be final upon failure of any aggrieved applicant within 30 days allowed to file an appeal to the board of county commissioners.
(b)
Any applicant aggrieved by the decision of the board of commissioners may take an appeal to the superior court of the county. The appeal shall be filed within 30 days from the date of the decision of the board of county commissioners. The decision of the board of county commissioners shall be final upon failure of any aggrieved applicant to file an appeal within the 30 days allowed to file in the superior court of the county.
(Ord. No. 11142013RD, § 7, 11-14-2013; Ord. of 1-9-2014)
Sec. 26-34. - Display of permit.
It shall be the duty of any applicant hereunder to display the permit issued to him in a conspicuous place at the site of the right-of-way entry. It shall be unlawful for any person to exhibit such permit at or about any location not covered thereby, or to misrepresent the number of the permit or the date of the permit.
(Ord. No. 11142013RD, § 8, 11-14-2013; Ord. of 1-9-2014)
Sec. 26-35. - Limitation on issuance.
Notwithstanding any other provision of this article, no permit shall be issued to any applicant therefor who has failed to satisfy any bonding requirements imposed in section 26-36.
(Ord. No. 11142013RD, § 9, 11-14-2013; Ord. of 1-9-2014)
Sec. 26-36. - Bond.
(a)
An applicant for a permit hereunder shall file a cash bond or letter of credit from a lending institution in the amount of $1,000.00 with his application and shall agree to indemnify and hold harmless the county board of commissioners and any of their agents from all damages and cost of repairs to any county road, county rights-of-way, ditches and bridges. Any person, corporation, business, partnership or any other entity needing to cross the right-of-way of county roads in the unincorporated area of the county shall obtain a permit from the county prior to the start of any operations and disturbances of any county right-of-way and shall covenant and shall guarantee he will maintain the county roads right-of-way, including, but not limited to, its roads, bridges, ditches, pipes, culverts, drainage ditches in accordance with county standards and shall repair any damages to the county right-of-way (property) within 30 days from written notice from county of any damages or problems.
(b)
After completion of the project, the applicant shall remove the right-of-way entry and restore the right-of-way to its original condition. The bond may be released provided all corrections, repairs and improvements have been made by the applicant within the time limits stated herein.
(c)
Failure of the applicant to perform the corrections of any specific problem within 30 days after completion of the project shall result in forfeiture of the bond.
(d)
In the event the applicant fails to correct any damages within 30 days after completion of the project or within 30 days from written notice from county, whichever first occurs, the county may correct all damages and all costs and charges to the county for the corrective work shall be the responsibility of the applicant.
(e)
Said applicant shall reimburse the county within 30 days after he is presented with a written bill for corrective work done by the county. The county shall use the bond as an offset to the costs and if the costs of the corrective work exceeds the value of said bond, the applicant shall be responsible for the deficit balance.
(Ord. No. 11142013RD, § 10, 11-14-2013; Ord. of 1-9-2014)
Sec. 26-37. - Violation.
Any person, corporation, business, partnership or any other entity who performs activity in violation of this article shall be subject to a fine and/or confinement under the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 36-1-20. A violation of this article shall constitute a misdemeanor and shall be tried in the county magistrate court and, upon conviction, shall be punished according to section 1-9, general penalty.
(Ord. No. 11142013RD, § 11, 11-14-2013; Ord. of 1-9-2014)
Secs. 26-38—26-58. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - DELIVERY AND TRANSPORT VEHICLES

Sec. 26-59. - Purpose.
The following sections are adopted for the purpose of promoting the health, safety and general welfare of the present and future inhabitants of the unincorporated county; to establish standards for prohibiting trucks and tractor-trailers from traveling on the rights-of-way in the county; and to restrict prohibiting truck traffic from certain roads in the county.
(Ord. No. 02142019, 2-14-2019)
Sec. 26-60. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning: ;p0; "Truck" means any vehicle used for hauling or carrying a load where the truck (tractor) and trailer has 18 wheels or more and specifically including tractors pulling trailers whether loaded or unloaded.
(Ord. No. 02142019, 2-14-2019)
Sec. 26-61. - Prohibition on through trucks.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any truck to travel any county roads, and excepting all state routes, unless the vehicle trip originates or ends on the Wilkes County portion of such road or is made for the purpose of making a pickup or delivery to a site on the Wilkes County portion of such road.
(b)
This section shall apply to all county roads but excepting state routes.
(Ord. No. 02142019, 2-14-2019)
Sec. 26-62. - Penalties.
Citations for violations of this article shall be heard in the Magistrate Court of Wilkes County. Any person violating this article shall be punished by a fine of no less than $400.00 for the first offense, and not to exceed $1,000.00 for subsequent offenses.
(Ord. No. 02142019, 2-14-2019)
