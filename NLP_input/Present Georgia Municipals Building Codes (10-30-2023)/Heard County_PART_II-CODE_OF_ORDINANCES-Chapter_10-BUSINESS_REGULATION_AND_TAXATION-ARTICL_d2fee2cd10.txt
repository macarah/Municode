ARTICLE VIII. - SPECIAL EVENTS

Sec. 10-350. - Permissible gatherings.
new
The provisions of this article shall not apply to private gatherings, parties, tours, or other similar type entertainments hosted by a property owner in the property owner's own residential building, structure or land for the property owner's own enjoyment and benefit, without compensation, and otherwise permitted under the county's ordinances.
It shall be lawful for a property owner to permit gatherings, parties, tours or other similar type entertainments in any residential building, structure or land, without compensation to the property owner (other than reimbursement for expenses), by a nonprofit organization, school, church, group, association, or individual for fund raising purposes so long as the funds raised are for educational or charitable purposes, or to support a political candidate or party.
(Ord. of 5-22-2018(1))
Sec. 10-351. - Permit required.
new
Unless permitted under section 10-350, no person shall engage in, aid, form or permit a gathering, party, tour or other similar type entertainment in any residential building, structure or land without first obtaining a special events permit from the office of the business license director as set forth in this article. No more than two events shall be permitted annually for the same tax map parcel.
(Ord. of 5-22-2018(1))
Sec. 10-352. - Application.
new
A person seeking issuance of a special events permit shall file application with the office of the business license director on forms provided by the county.
(1)
Filing period. An application for a special events permit shall be filed not less than 45 days or more than 90 days before the date on which it is proposed to conduct the activity.
(2)
Contents. The application for a special events permit shall set forth the following information:
a.
The name, address and telephone number of the person seeking to conduct the activity; and the applicant, if different.
b.
If the activity is proposed to be conducted for, on behalf of, or by an organization, the name, address and telephone number of the headquarters of the organization, and of the authorized and responsible heads of that organization.
c.
The name, address and telephone number of the person who will be the activity leader and who will be responsible for its conduct.
d.
The date when the activity is to be conducted.
e.
If the activity is a tour, a map of the route to be traveled, the starting point and the termination point.
f.
The approximate number of persons who will participate in the activity.
g.
The hours the activity will start and terminate.
h.
A plan that identifies adequate provisions for parking and transportation of participants in the activity.
i.
If the activity requires police protection or traffic control, the number of officers to be employed by applicant to provide such protection and control.
j.
Any additional information which the business license director or his designee shall find reasonably necessary to a fair determination as to whether a special events permit should issue.
(3)
Late applications. The business license director or his designee where good cause is shown therefor, shall have the authority to consider any application under this division which is filed less than 45 days before the date the special event is proposed to be conducted, but in any event no less than five days before the date of the special event.
(4)
Fee. A fee of $50.00 shall be charged for such special events permit.
(Ord. of 5-22-2018(1))
Sec. 10-353. - Standards for issuance.
new
The business license director or his designee shall issue a special events permit under this article when, from a consideration of the application and from any other information as may otherwise be obtained, he finds that:
(1)
The conduct of the special event will not substantially interrupt the safe and orderly movement of other traffic contiguous to the activity.
(2)
The conduct of the special event will not require the diversion of any of the law enforcement officers of the county to police the activity, but that adequate provisions for police protection and traffic control have been made by the applicant.
(3)
The conduct of the special event will not unreasonably interfere with the movement of firefighting equipment en route to a fire.
(4)
The conduct of the special event is not reasonably likely to cause injury to persons or property, to provoke disorderly conduct or create a disturbance.
(5)
The special event is not to be held for the sole purpose of advertising any product, goods or event, and is not designed to be held purely for private profit except as provided in this article.
(6)
The special event will not unreasonably burden the public rights-of-way of the county so as to substantially interfere with the normal use thereof by the public, but that adequate provisions for parking and transportation of participants in the activity have been made by the applicant.
(7)
The special event will not interfere with another special event for which a special events permit has been approved.
(Ord. of 5-22-2018(1))
Sec. 10-354. - Notice of rejection.
new
The business license director or his designee shall act upon the application for a special events permit within ten days after the filing thereof. If the application is disapproved, there shall be mailed to the applicant within two days after the date the application is disapproved, a notice of the action, stating the reasons for the denial of the permit.
(Ord. of 5-22-2018(1))
Sec. 10-355. - Appeal procedure.
new
Any person aggrieved shall have the right to appeal the denial of a special events permit to the county chairman. The appeal shall be taken within two business days after receipt of the notice by the applicant. The county chairman shall act upon the appeal within three business days after its receipt and may grant or deny a special events permit.
(Ord. of 5-22-2018(1))
Sec. 10-356. - Notice to county officials.
new
Immediately upon the application for a special events permit, the business license director or his designee shall send a copy thereof to the following:
(1)
The county chairman and board of commissioners;
(2)
The sheriff;
(3)
The fire chief; and
(4)
The public works director.
(Ord. of 5-22-2018(1))
Sec. 10-357. - Duties of permittee.
new
(a)
A permittee under this article shall comply with all permit directions and conditions and with all applicable laws and ordinances.
(b)
The permittee, leader or other person heading or leading the activity shall carry the special events permit upon his person during the conduct of the activity.
(Ord. of 5-22-2018(1))
Sec. 10-358. - Revocation of permit.
new
The business license director or his designee shall have the authority to revoke a special events permit issued under this division upon a finding that the application filed contains misleading or incomplete information and does not comply with the standards for issuance as set forth under section 10-353.
(Ord. of 5-22-2018(1))
Sec. 10-359. - Violations prohibited; penalties.
new
(a)
No person shall violate any of the provisions of this chapter or assist another in evading the requirements imposed by this chapter.
(b)
Any person knowingly violating any provision of this article shall be guilty of an offense against the county, punishable upon citation before the municipal court. Any person convicted of violating any provision of this article shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00, imprisonment not to exceed 12 months, or both.
(Ord. of 5-22-2018(1))
