ARTICLE XIII. - GRAFFITI

Sec. 5-470. - Purpose.
The Board of Mayor and Commissioners of the City of Avondale Estates, Georgia, finds and determines that graffiti, as defined by this article, is a public nuisance that is destructive of property values, invasive of property rights, and generally deleterious to the public welfare.
(Ord. No. 09-06, § 1, 10-26-09)
Sec. 5-471. - Definitions.
Graffiti, as used in this article, shall have the meaning ascribed by O.C.G.A. § 17-15A-2, to-wit: "any inscriptions, words, figures, paintings, or other defacements that are written, marked, etched, scratched, sprayed, drawn, painted or engraved on or otherwise affixed to any surface of real property or improvements thereon without prior authorization of the owner or occupant of the property by means of any aerosol paint container, broad-tipped marker, gum label, paint stick, graffiti stick, etching equipment, brush or other device capable of scarring or leaving a visible mark on any surface."
Owner, as used in this article, shall include the record owner of the property and also, where the record owner of the property may not be located, any person operating, managing, residing at, or otherwise having control over the property.
(Ord. No. 09-06, § 1, 10-26-09)
Sec. 5-472. - Prohibited acts.
(a)
Defacement. It shall be unlawful for any person within the corporate limits of the city to apply graffiti to any property.
(b)
Failure to remediate. It shall be unlawful for the owner of any property within the city to allow any graffiti that is visible from the street or other public or private property to remain upon any building, structure, appurtenance, equipment, any container used for storage of commercial refuse, recyclables or litter, or any fixed or portable box, container or storage unit, any sign, any surface, paving or similar feature located, installed, used or maintained on such property for more than fifteen (15) days after receipt of a notice to remove graffiti as provided for by this article. A separate violation shall be deemed to occur for each and every day after the expiration of the fifteen-day period that the graffiti remains visible.
(Ord. No. 09-06, § 1, 10-26-09)
Sec. 5-473. - Penalty.
(a)
Any person who is convicted of defacement pursuant to subsection 5-472(a) of this article shall be punished by a fine not to exceed one thousand dollars ($1,000.00), imprisonment not to exceed six (6) months, or both fine and imprisonment in combination. In addition to such punishment, the court may, in imposing sentence, order the defendant to restore the property so defaced, damaged or destroyed, to pay restitution for the actual reasonable cost of removal of the graffiti, and/or may require the defendant to perform up to forty (40) hours of community service as the court deems appropriate.
(b)
When an owner is convicted of violating subsection 5-472(b) of this article, the court may require the owner to pay restitution for the actual reasonable cost of removal of the graffiti.
(Ord. No. 09-06, § 1, 10-26-09)
Sec. 5-474. - Notice to remove graffiti.
Whenever the city becomes aware of the existence of graffiti on any property within the city, the city shall give or cause to be given notice to the property owner to remove such graffiti therefrom. Such notice shall be in writing and have substantially the following form:
NOTICE TO REMOVE GRAFFITI
To the Owner of the property hereinafter described:
Your attention is hereby directed to the provisions of Ordinance Number _____ on file in the office of the City Clerk in the City Hall, Avondale Estates, Georgia.
Pursuant thereto, you are hereby notified that graffiti described as ___________ exists on premises known as ___________, which injures neighboring property and public health, safety and welfare. You are therefore notified to remove such graffiti at once, and in any event within fifteen (15) days from the date of this notice, from the property and thereafter to keep the property free therefrom.
In the event you fail to complete such work within the time set forth herein then you shall be in violation of City Ordinance and may be cited accordingly. In addition, the City may cause the graffiti to be removed from your property and the costs of such removal may be considered by the court in determining the penalty for violation of the City's Graffiti Ordinance upon conviction.
Dated at Avondale Estates, Georgia this _____ day of ________, 20___.
_____
Officer
The notice required by this section shall be served by personal service on the owner, wherever possible. If the owner cannot be located then notice shall be accomplished by sending the same by registered or certified mail addressed to the owner at the last known address of said owner. If the owner's address is unknown, the notice may be tacked to the door of the property.
(Ord. No. 09-06, § 1, 10-26-09)
