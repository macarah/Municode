ARTICLE V. - EMPLOYEE BENEFITS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-211. - Anniversary benefits.
Each year in December, the county will recognize the employees who have attained the following anniversaries representing years of continuous service by the following rewards:
(1)
For those employees who have reached their five-year anniversary of continuous service, the county will:
a.
Present a framed resolution detailing the years of service; and
b.
Award the employee $25.00.
(2)
For those employees who have reached their ten-year anniversary of continuous service, the county will:
a.
Present a framed resolution detailing the years of service; and
b.
Award the employee $50.00.
(3)
For those employees who have reached their fifteen-year anniversary of continuous service, the county will:
a.
Present a framed resolution detailing the years of service;
b.
Award the employee $100.00; and
c.
Allow the employee to take his/her birthday off, with pay, in each year thereafter as long as he/she is employed by the county. If the birthday falls on a scheduled day off, or holiday, the employee shall be able to take the next closest day off for the birthday.
(4)
For those employees who have reached their 20-year anniversary of continuous service, the county will:
a.
Present an engraved plaque detailing the years of service;
b.
Award the employee $150.00; and
c.
Continue to allow the employee to take his/her birthday off, with pay, in each year thereafter as long as he/she is employed by the county. If the birthday falls on a scheduled day off, or holiday, the employee shall be able to take the next closest day off for the birthday; and
(5)
For those employees who have reached their 25-year anniversary of continuous service, the county will:
a.
Present an engraved plaque detailing the years of service;
b.
Award the employee $200.00; and
c.
Continue to allow the employee to take his/her birthday off, with pay, in each year thereafter as long as he/she is employed by the county. If the birthday falls on a scheduled day off, or holiday, the employee shall be able to take the next closest day off for the birthday.
(Res. No. 2001-35, 11-6-01; Res. No. 2006-12, 3-7-06)
Editor's note— Res. No. 2001-35, adopted Nov. 6, 2001, did not specifically amend the Code. Hence, its inclusion herein as section 2-211 was at the discretion of the editor.
Secs. 2-212—2-230. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - WORKERS' COMPENSATION

Sec. 2-231. - Elected officials.
The elected county officials shall be covered under the Association County Commissioners of Georgia Group Self-Insured Workers' Compensation Fund. While performing their elected duties the board of commissioners, sheriff, probate judge, tax commissioner, clerk of court and magistrate judge shall be covered under the workers' compensation laws of the state as stated in O.C.G.A. § 34-9-1.
(Res. No. 93-12, § I, 3-2-93)
Sec. 2-232. - Volunteer firemen.
Volunteer firemen of the county shall be covered under the Association of County Commissioners of Georgia Group Self-Insured Workers' Compensation Fund. While performing their duties volunteer firemen shall be covered under the workers' compensation laws of the state.
(Res. No. 90-12, 12-4-90)
