ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 14-1. - Building official.
The board of commissioners shall have the authority to designate a building inspector for the county. The building inspector has the authority to inspect buildings and structures to ensure they meet the current building codes.
Sec. 14-2. - Permits required for providing utility services.
No person engaged in the business of furnishing utility, water, gas or sewage disposal, referred to in this section as "utility services," shall provide any utility services to a dwelling or other structure for which permits are required to be issued under the terms of this chapter and/or the applicable building, electrical and plumbing codes adopted pursuant to this chapter unless the owner or occupant of such property for which utility services are being furnished shall have first obtained all of the permits required to be issued pursuant to this chapter from the building official prior to the furnishing of utility services or prior to utility services being made available to the dwelling or structure.
(Code 2000, § 14-1; Res. of 7-7-1987, § 17)

Sec. 14-24. - Self-inspection exemption.
The board of commissioners, pursuant to 1996 Ga. Laws (Act No. 1046), page 1632, exempts the county from the alternative inspection procedure as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 8-2-26(d).
(Code 2000, § 14-31; Res. of 7-2-1996)
Sec. 14-25. - Enforcement; penalty.
(a)
This article shall be enforced by the office of the building official. Citations for violations may be issued by the chief building official.
(b)
Any person violating this article shall be tried before the magistrate court of the county. Upon conviction, a violation of this article shall be punished as provided in section 1-11.
(Code 2000, § 14-33)

ARTICLE III. - FLOW RATE RESTRICTIONS ON PLUMBING FIXTURES[3]

State Law reference— Flow rate restrictions on plumbing fixtures, O.C.G.A. § 8-2-3.

Sec. 14-54. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Commercial means any type of building other than residential.
Construction means the erection of a new building or the alteration of an existing building in connection with its repair or renovation or in connection with making an addition to an existing building and shall include the replacement of a malfunctioning, unserviceable or obsolete faucet, showerhead, toilet or urinal in an existing building.
Residential means any building or unit of a building intended for occupancy as a dwelling but shall not include a hotel or motel.
(Code 2000, § 14-66; Ord. No. 91-1, § 1, 1-8-1991)
Sec. 14-55. - Restricted residential construction.
On or after July 1, 1991, no construction may be initiated within the unincorporated area of the county for any residential building of any type that employs a:
(1)
Gravity tank-type, flushometer-valve, or flushometer-tank toilet that uses more than an average of 1.28 gallons of water per flush; however, this subsection shall not be applied to one-piece toilets until July 1, 1992;
(2)
Showerhead that allows a flow of more than an average of 2.5 gallons of water per minute at 60 pounds per square inch of pressure;
(3)
Urinal that uses more than an average of 0.5 gallons of water per flush;
(4)
Lavatory faucet or lavatory replacement aerator that allows a flow of more than 2.0 gallons of water per minute; or
(5)
Kitchen faucet or kitchen replacement aerator that allows a flow of more than 2.0 gallons of water per minute.
(Code 2000, § 14-67; Ord. No. 91-1, § 2, 1-8-1991)
Sec. 14-56. - Restricted commercial construction.
On or after July 1, 1992, there shall be no construction of any commercial building initiated within the unincorporated area of the county for any commercial building of any type that does not meet the requirements of section 14-55(1) through (5).
(Code 2000, § 14-68; Ord. No. 91-1, § 3, 1-8-1991)
Sec. 14-57. - Application of article to repair, renovation.
The requirements of section 14-55 shall apply to any residential construction initiated after July 1, 1991, and to any commercial construction initiated after July 1, 1992, which involves the repair or renovation of or addition to any existing building when such repair or renovation of or addition to such existing building includes replacement of toilets or showers or both.
(Code 2000, § 14-69; Ord. No. 91-1, § 4, 1-8-1991)
Sec. 14-58. - Exemptions.
(a)
New construction and the repair or renovation of an existing building shall be exempt from the requirements of O.C.G.A. § 8-2-3(c) when:
(1)
The repair or renovation of the existing building does not include the replacement of the plumbing or sewerage system servicing toilets, faucets or showerheads within such existing buildings;
(2)
Such plumbing or sewerage system within such existing building, because of its capacity, design or installation, would not function properly if the toilets, faucets or showerheads required by this article were installed;
(3)
Such system is a well or gravity flow from a spring and is owned privately by an individual for use in such individual's personal residence; or
(4)
Units to be installed are:
a.
Specifically designed for use by persons with disabilities;
b.
Specifically designed to withstand unusual abuse or installation in a penal institution or other detention facility; or
c.
Toilets for juveniles.
(b)
The owner of a building undergoing new construction or repair or renovation who is entitled to an exemption as specified in subsection (a)(2), (3) or (4) of this section shall obtain the exemption by applying at the office of the building official. A fee set by the board of county commissioners by ordinance specified in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk shall be charged for the inspection and issuance of such exemption.
(Code 2000, § 14-70; Ord. No. 91-1, § 5, 1-8-1991)
Sec. 14-59. - Enforcement; penalty.
(a)
This article shall be enforced by the office of the building official. Citations for violations may be issued by the building inspector.
(b)
Any person violating this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and upon conviction may be punished as provided in section 1-11. Any violators of this article shall be prosecuted in the magistrate court of the county.
(Code 2000, § 14-71; Ord. No. 91-1, § 6, 1-8-1991)
