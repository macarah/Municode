Chapter 109 - ENVIRONMENT AND NATURAL RESOURCES
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 109-1. - Required criteria for planning and construction of water projects.
It shall be unlawful for any agency of the federal, state, or local government, any instrumentality, agent, or employee thereof, to hereafter erect, build, maintain or otherwise implement any plan to erect a lake, permanent water retention structure, pond, or similar such improvements within the limits of the county, unless all of the following three criteria for the construction of said project are used in connection with the design of any such structure, to-wit:
(1)
The dam and lake project construction shall comply with all minimum requirements of state and federal law concerning its construction, and shall meet all of the provisions of the department of public health of the state and local county department of public health, as to all criteria which might affect the storage of water upon the site; and
(2)
The minimum depth of the permanent water pool area of said site shall in no event and at no location be less than two feet; and
(3)
At least 75 percent of the storage area in the permanent water pool shall have a minimum depth of three feet. "Storage area" shall be defined as the capacity in acres (or parts thereof) of the permanent pool of water which is to be impounded at the site.
(Res. of 11-9-1982, § 1)
Sec. 109-2. - Violation.
Any person, firm or corporation acting on behalf of any federal, state or local government that violates the provisions of this article shall be punished as for a misdemeanor. Any construction or other improvement in violation of the minimum requirements of this article shall also constitute a continuing nuisance and health hazard to the citizens of the county. In addition to any penalties that may be imposed by other state or federal environmental agencies, the punishment imposed for any violation of this article section shall not exceed a fine of $1,000.00 or six months' imprisonment or both. See fines as referenced in Subpart A; Chapter 1, Section 1-18, Fine Schedule.
(Res. of 11-9-1982, § 2; Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 2-5-2019)
Secs. 109-3—109-22. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - ENVIRONMENTAL POLICY

Sec. 109-23. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Environmental affects report means a report on a proposed action, enterprise or venture which may significantly adversely affect the quality of the environment.
Environmental committee means the standing committee composed of not less than two county commissioners as appointed by the chairman of the county board of commissioners, and the purpose of this committee is to review, report on, and make recommendations on environmental matters.
Inert landfill means solid waste consisting of earth, earth-like products, concrete, cured asphalt, rock, bricks, yard trimmings, stumps, limbs and leaves. Demolition and/or construction debris shall not be placed in an inert landfill.
Land disturbing activity means scraping, plowing, clearing, dredging, grading, excavating, transporting, or filling of land or placement of any structure or impervious surface, dam, obstruction, or deposit or placement of or alteration to any structure on or eligible for the state register of historic places; provided, however, that agricultural practices involving the establishment, cultivation, or harvesting of products of the field or orchard; the preparation and planting of pasture land; farm ponds; dairy operations; livestock and poultry management practices; and forestry land management practices, including harvesting of less than five acres of trees over two inches in diameter at breast height, and any clearing or grading of a tract or parcel of land less than 1.1 acres are excluded from the definition of land disturbing activity.
Landfill means any location or facility or site which is permitted by the county and permitted by the state for accepting solid waste.
A proposed action, enterprise or venture which may significantly adversely affect the quality of the environment means a project proposed to be undertaken for which it is probable to expect a significant adverse impact on the natural environment, including the county's air, land, water, plants, animals, historical sites or buildings, or cultural resources. Such actions shall not include emergency measures undertaken in response to an immediate threat to public health or safety.
Solid waste means garbage, rubbish, paper, cardboard, wood, metal, furniture, appliances, glass, demolition waste, construction waste, and other nonhazardous waste as defined by the state EPD.
Superintendent of public works means the superintendent of public works of the county.
Transfer station means any facility used for the temporary storage of solid waste or handling of solid waste or transportation of solid waste between municipalities, counties, or states.
(Ord. of 2-5-2002, art. I)
Sec. 109-24. - Permit required.
(a)
All proposed actions, ventures, or enterprises which may pose an adverse impact on the quality of the environment shall obtain an environmental permit from the county prior to beginning operations or development.
(b)
Environmental permits shall be required for the following proposed or planned actions:
(1)
Industrial operations which produce smoke, vapors or odors;
(2)
Industrial operations which handle, store toxic materials or produce toxic waste;
(3)
Ventures which involve salvage or recycling operations;
(4)
Solid waste disposal operations;
(5)
Solid waste transfer stations/operations;
(6)
Inert landfills; and
(7)
Other proposed actions covered by subsection (a) of this section.
(Ord. of 2-5-2002, art. II)
Sec. 109-25. - Review of applications.
(a)
The environmental committee shall review all applications for an environmental permit. The committee shall have up to 60 days from the date the application is received in the commissioner's office for its review.
(1)
After completing its review, the environmental committee shall make a recommendation to the full board of commissioners as to whether the application should be approved or denied.
(2)
The board of commissioners shall make a final decision as to whether the permit should be approved or the application denied.
(b)
In the review and decision making process, the environmental committee and/or the board of commissioners shall have the right to obtain opinions and comments from any agency, company or person which has jurisdiction by law, or special expertise, or other interest with respect to any environmental resource or matter.
(Ord. of 2-5-2002, art. III)
Sec. 109-26. - Permit applications; fees.
(a)
Permit applications shall be filed with the county commissioner's office on forms provided along with additional data as required by this article.
(b)
Nonrefundable fees as established by the board of commissioners shall be paid by the applicant. Necessary expenses resulting from the review and/or application process shall be paid by the applicant.
(Ord. of 2-5-2002, art. IV)
Sec. 109-27. - Requirements for survey plat and topography, zoning and state permits.
(a)
Survey plat and topography. A boundary survey for each project site shall be submitted. If the proposed project is for a solid waste landfill, insert landfill, or waste transfer station, then the survey plat shall include a topographic survey (aerial or field survey) by contours at vertical intervals of not more than five feet. The entire tract on which the project is located shall be platted. Additionally, the plat shall include the following:
(1)
Graphic scale, north point and date.
(2)
Location sketch map showing the site in relation to the general surrounding area.
(3)
Total acreage of the tract and the project area.
(4)
Deed record names of all adjoining property owners.
(5)
Location of streams, lakes, swamps, wetlands, and the 100 year floodplain.
(6)
Location of buildings on the tract, and proposed buildings and/or facilities.
(7)
Location of drainage areas, culverts, sewers, water mains, or fire hydrants wither within the tract or on rights-of-way adjoining the tract.
(b)
Zoning. All projects, enterprises, actions, or ventures which are subject to this article shall be located within an industrial zone and meet the requirements of that zone.
(c)
State permits. Applicants shall provide evidence that all applicable state permits have been applied for; and, such permits shall include, but not limited to, land disturbing permit, stormwater runoff permit, and applicable EPD permits. The county environmental permit shall not be granted final approval until all state applicable permits have been received.
(Ord. of 2-5-2002, art. V)
Sec. 109-28. - Evaluation.
(a)
In the review, evaluation, and determination of an application the following factors shall be considered:
(1)
The effect of shortterm impact on the environment due to the construction or start-up phase of the project.
(2)
The possible threat of longterm adverse impact on the county's environment.
(3)
Any beneficial aspects of the project.
(4)
Whether the project would have an adverse impact on county projects, operations or services.
(b)
Applications shall be denied if there is a finding of longterm adverse impact to the county's environment, or if the services or operations of the county would be adversely impacted, or if any requirement of this article is not met.
(Ord. of 2-5-2002, art. VI)
Sec. 109-29. - Notice and hearing.
(a)
All applicants shall provide notice to the adjoining property owners. Requirements of the notice shall include the general outline of the project, and the date and time when the county commission will hear the matter.
(b)
The county commissioners shall conduct an advertised public meeting to consider the application and to accept comment on the application from citizens. Notice shall be placed in the newspaper (county legal organ) at least 14 days before the hearing.
(Ord. of 2-5-2002, art. VII)
Sec. 109-30. - Inspections and revocation.
(a)
The county or its appointed agent shall have the right to inspect at any time a project site or enterprise should a permit be granted.
(b)
A permit may be revoked if there is evidence that the health, safety or general welfare of its citizens is being jeopardized; or if any county or state regulations are being violated; or if the permit was fraudulently obtained. A revocation hearing shall be conducted by the board of commissioners in such cases to hear evidence and make a decision. A permit holder shall be given notice of the hearing, and the notice shall state the reasons for the proposed revocation and the date and time of the hearing, and shall be served either personally or by certified mail.
(c)
The decision of the board of commissioners to issue a permit and proceed with the proposed action, enterprise or venture shall not create a cause of action in any person, corporation, association or municipal corporation.
(Ord. of 2-5-2002, art. VIII)
Sec. 109-31. - Enforcement.
(a)
Upon information made known to the chief of police or his authorized representative that any person is in violation of this article, the chief or his authorized agent shall cause a citation to be issued requiring said person to appear before the judge of the magistrate court on a day and time certain, then and there to stand trial for violation of this article.
(b)
Citations issued hereunder shall be pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63, and shall be personally served upon the person accused. Each citation shall state the time and place at which the accused is to appear for trial.
(Ord. of 2-5-2002, art. IX)
Sec. 109-32. - Penalty.
Any person violating the terms of this article, or rules and regulations promulgated pursuant thereto, may be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 or imprisonment in the county jail for 60 days, or both. A separate offense shall be deemed committed on each day during, or on which a violation occurs or continues.
(Ord. of 2-5-2002, art. X)
Secs. 109-33—109-52. - Reserved.

ARTICLE IV. - TIMBER HARVESTING[2]

Footnotes:

Sec. 109-77. - Purpose.
The intent of the county board of commissioners in enacting this article is to enable the county to promote the safety of citizens and to maintain all county roads and rights-of-way in a proper and safe operating condition.
(Ord. of 3-6-2012; Ord. No. 2018-08, § 1, 5-8-2018)
Sec. 109-78. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
County road means any county-owned or maintained highway, road, street, avenue, drive, detour or other way open to the public and intended or used for the passage of motor vehicles.
Operations means those activities contemplated by this article, including the loading or transporting of raw materials such as stone, timber, oil and fill dirt.
Right-of-way means any area occupied by a roadway and the areas and facilities immediately adjacent to a roadway to include the shoulder, front slope, ditch, drain, back slope, and any areas beyond such that have been acquired and/or accepted by the county for the purpose of maintaining such areas and facilities.
Timber harvester means any individual, partnership, corporation, association or private organization of any character, and the officer's agents, and employees thereof carrying out the activities contemplated by this article.
(Ord. of 3-6-2012; Ord. No. 2018-08, § 1, 5-8-2018)
Sec. 109-79. - Notice.
(a)
All persons or firms commercially harvesting standing timber in any unincorporated area of Polk County for delivery as pulpwood, logs, poles, posts, or wood chips to any woodyard or processing plant located inside or outside this state shall provide notice of such harvesting operations to the county public works department prior to harvesting any such timber. Further, such persons shall give notice of cessation of cutting within 24 hours after the job is completed.
(b)
The notice of harvesting operations required by this article shall be provided for each separate tract to be harvested. Such notice shall be made in such form as prescribed by rule or regulation of the director of the Georgia Forestry Commission, and shall include the following information:
(1)
A map of the area which identifies the location of the tract to be harvested and, as to those trucks which will be traveling to and from such tract for purposes of picking up and hauling loads of cut forest products, the main point of ingress to such tract from a public road and, if different, the main point of egress from such tract to a public road;
(2)
A statement as to whether the timber will be removed pursuant to a lump sum sale, per unit sale, or owner harvest for purposes of ad valorem taxation under O.C.G.A § 48-5-7.5;
(3)
The name, address, and daytime telephone number of the timber seller, if the harvest is pursuant to a lump sum or per unit sale, or of the timber owner if the harvest is an owner harvest; and
(4)
The name, business address, business telephone number, and nighttime or emergency telephone number of the person or firm commercially harvesting such timber.
(c)
Notice may be submitted in person, by transmission of an electronic record via e-mail, telefacsimile or by mail.
(d)
Subject to O.C.G.A 12-6-24, persons or firms wishing to harvest timber shall utilize the State of Georgia's timber harvesting notification system. Polk County will accept notifications submitted in this manner.
(Ord. of 3-6-2012; Ord. No. 2018-08, § 1, 5-8-2018; Res. No. 2022-69, § 1, 9-13-2022)
Sec. 109-80. - Haul route.
Any person or firm commercially harvesting timber within the unincorporated areas of Polk County shall provide to the county public works department proposed haul routes prior to harvesting any timber. The public works department shall review and approve all haul routes as proposed or with changes specified by the department. Timber harvesters shall be required to follow the approved haul routes specified and approved by the department.
(Ord. of 3-6-2012; Ord. No. 2018-08, § 1, 5-8-2018)
Sec. 109-81. - Public right-of-way nuisance or hazard.
(a)
No person or firm commercially harvesting timber within the unincorporated areas of Polk County shall allow dirt, mud or other debris resulting from timber operations to accumulate upon the right-of-way of any public road to such an extent that it becomes a nuisance or a hazard to persons traveling upon said roads, or creates an unsightly condition upon the public right-of-way. Gravel or other material shall be used in staging area to reduce mud being tracked onto county roads. Mud removal equipment shall be on site at all times mud may present a problem. The county will not tolerate a buildup of mud on county roads. Skidders shall not be used to remove mud from county roads.
(b)
No person or firm commercially harvesting timber within the unincorporated areas of Polk County shall allow dirt, mud or other debris resulting from timber operations to accumulate in the ditches and drainage areas on public rights-of-way to such an extent that the usual flow of water or runoff is stopped, disturbed, changed or interrupted. Restorative conditions - such conditions as may be necessary for the protection of the road and ditch structure and for the unrestricted flow of stormwater along said ditch. Such conditions may include, but not be limited to, the installation of approved culvert pipe and the restoration of the ditch line, back slopes, and road shoulders to their original condition upon the completion of the logging or timber cutting operation.
(c)
No person or firm commercially harvesting timber within the unincorporated areas of Polk County shall create any other type of public nuisance or hazard within the rights-of-way of the county.
(d)
No person or firm commercially harvesting timber within the unincorporated areas of Polk County shall park or leave unattended a truck or other motor vehicle or trailer upon any county maintained right-of-way for any period of time. Staging of loads shall not be done on county right-of-way.
(e)
All persons or firms commercially engaging in timber harvesting operations within the unincorporated areas of Polk County shall implement and maintain at all times soil erosion and sedimentation control best management practices, as prescribed by the state forestry commission, U.S. EPA, and the state soil and water conservation commission, sufficient to prevent dirt, mud and other debris from accumulating in the county drainage ditches and on the county roads upon which the timber harvester is ingressing and egressing.
(Ord. of 3-6-2012; Ord. No. 2018-08, § 1, 5-8-2018)
Sec. 109-82. - Roadway signage.
(a)
No person or firm commercially harvesting timber within the unincorporated areas of Polk County shall commence timber operations until he has first posted or caused to be posted along the public road onto which the timber harvester will enter from his timber operations at least the following signs: One sign in each direction located 500 feet from the entrance which states "Slow Trucks Entering Highway"; one sign in each direction located 1,000 feet from the entrance stating "Warning: Logging Operation Ahead." Each such sign shall be 36 inches by 36 inches, orange in color and posted at least three feet from the surface of said road.
(Ord. of 3-6-2012; Ord. No. 2018-08, § 1, 5-8-2018)
Sec. 109-83. - Required surety bond or irrevocable letter of credit.
The notice required by this article shall not be or remain effective unless and until the person or firm providing such notice has delivered to the Governing Authority of Polk County or its designated agent a valid surety bond, executed by a surety corporation authorized to transact business in this state, protecting Polk County against any damage caused by such person or firm in the amount of $5,000.00; provided, however, that at the option of the person or firm harvesting timber a valid irrevocable letter of credit issued by a bank or savings and loan association, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 7-1-4, in the amount of $5,000.00 may be provided in lieu of a surety bond. Such bonds or letters of credit shall be subject to the conditions set forth in sections 109-84 and 109-85 of this article. No more than one bond or letter of credit shall be required from each person or firm harvesting timber, regardless of the number of tracts harvested in the county for so long as the bond or letter of credit remains in effect. The bond or letter of credit required herein shall be valid only for the calendar year in which it was delivered. [Note to county attorneys: The maximum bond amount is set by statute at $5,000.00 and may not be increased at the local level.]
(Ord. of 3-6-2012; Ord. No. 2018-08, § 1, 5-8-2018; Res. No. 2022-69, § 1, 9-13-2022)
Sec. 109-84. - Damages subject to repairs from surety bond or letter of credit proceeds.
The bond or letter of credit required by section 109-83 of this article shall protect Polk County against any damage requiring re-ditching or repair of existing ditch structures or the removal of any harvesting residue, including tree tops, debris, logs, pulpwood and other materials, placed in or around the county's rights-of-way caused by such person or firm tendering the bond or letter of credit. The proceeds of such bond or letter of credit shall be available to reimburse the county for any cost incurred to repair such damages or remove such debris in or around the county's rights-of-way. The proceeds of such bond or letter of credit shall also be available to reimburse the county for any costs incurred to maintain or repair county roads damaged by the ingress or egress of motor vehicles engaged in the harvest operations located within 500 feet of any point of ingress or egress of the timber harvesting operation. The right of Polk County to call such bond or letter of credit in accordance with the provisions of section 109-85 of this article shall be in addition to any other remedies available to the county at law or in equity for damage to county roads or rights-of-way.
(Res. No. 2022-69, § 1, 9-13-2022)
Editor's note— Res. No. 2022-69, § 1, adopted September 13, 2022, repealed the former § 109-84, and enacted a new § 109-84 as set out herein. The former § 109-84 pertained to violations and derived from Ord. of 3-6-2012; Ord. No. 2018-08, § 1, 5-8-2018; Ord. No. 2019-01, § 1, 2-5-2019.
Sec. 109-85. - Claims by county under surety bond or letter of credit.
When damage results from a person or firm's harvesting activities, the Governing Authority of Polk County shall make and provide a written claim to the person or firm causing the damage within 30 business days after the Governing Authority becomes aware of the damage. Such claim may be given in person, by telefacsimile, email or mail. The claim shall describe the damage in detail and, in compliance with subsection 109-86 (1) of this article, give the person or firm the opportunity to repair such damage within 30 days of the notification; provided, however, the county shall be authorized to repair the damage immediately if the Governing Authority or its designee determines the conditions present a threat to public safety, health or welfare and, upon making such repairs, shall present to the person or firm and the issuer of the applicable bond or letter of credit an itemized list of expenses incurred as a claim against the responsible party and the issuer of its bond or letter of credit. Upon the issuance of a claim as provided in this section the Governing Authority of Lincoln County or its designee shall notify the issuer of the bond or letter of credit that a claim has been made and will be resolved or adjudicated according to the terms of this article.
(Res. No. 2022-69, § 1, 9-13-2022)
Sec. 109-86. - Response to claim; appeal.
Within 30 days of receipt of the written claim described in section 109-85, the person or firm against whom the claim is submitted may:
(1)
Repair such damage at his or its own expense with the approval and supervision of the Governing Authority of Polk County or its designee. When repairs are completed to the satisfaction of the governing authority or its designee, the governing authority or its designee shall provide a written notification of satisfactory completion within five business days to the responsible person or firm and to the surety issuing the bond or the bank issuing the letter of credit, thereby terminating the claim.
(2)
In the event of inclement weather or other factors preventing repair of the damage, request a 30-day extension to repair the damage from the Governing Authority of Polk County, provided that no extensions shall exceed 90 days from the date the claim was tendered. Approval of any extension shall be at the discretion of the Governing Authority of Polk County or its designee.
(3)
Appeal the claim to the Magistrate Court of Polk County. Any such appeal must name the issuer of the bond or letter of credit as a party, who shall be served with all pleadings in the action and shall have the right to appear. The Magistrate Court will hear evidence and arguments within 30 days of the written appeal and issue a ruling within ten days of such hearing. Any such appeal shall toll the 30-day period, or any extension thereof, required by section 109-85 of this article. If the Magistrate Court rules in favor of the person or firm against whom the claim was made, the county shall have no right to recover any proceeds of the bond or letter of credit, and judgment shall be entered against the county. If the Magistrate Court rules in favor of the county the court shall determine the amount of damages to which the county is entitled to recover and enter judgment accordingly; the Governing Authority of Polk County shall be authorized to call the bond or letter of credit and recover from the proceeds thereof an amount equal to the judgment entered by the court, up to the total amount of the bond or letter of credit. The portion of any judgment entered in favor of the county that exceeds the amount of the bond or letter of credit shall be subject to collection by any additional remedies at law or equity.
(4)
In the event the person or firm against whom the claim has been submitted fails to take any of the actions allowed under subsections (1), (2) or (3) of this section within the time required therein, such person or firm shall be deemed to have waived any and all rights to contest the call of the bond or letter of credit.
(Res. No. 2022-69, § 1, 9-13-2022)
Sec. 109-87. - Calendar year expiration of surety bond or letter of credit.
If the person or firm tendering a bond or letter of credit pursuant to the requirements of this article continues its timber harvesting operation beyond the calendar year in which the bond or letter of credit was issued, the person or firm continuing the timber harvesting operation shall tender a new bond or letter of credit within five business days after the first day of the new calendar year.
(Res. No. 2022-69, § 1, 9-13-2022)
Sec. 109-88. - Revocation of surety bond or letter of credit.
In the event a bond or letter of credit tendered pursuant to the requirements of this article is revoked by the surety or bank, then a valid replacement bond or letter of credit must be delivered to the Governing Authority of Polk County within five business days after the date of revocation in order for timber harvesting operations to continue. In addition, if the person or firm tendering the bond or letter of credit caused its revocation, the amount of the bond or letter of credit required shall be increased to $7,500.00 after the first revocation, and $10,000.00 after a second revocation caused by the person or firm tendering the bond or letter of credit. The maximum amount of the bond or letter of credit shall not exceed $10,000.00.
(Res. No. 2022-69, § 1, 9-13-2022)
Sec. 109-89. - Changes in scope or extent of harvesting operation.
Submission of the notice required by this article shall authorize the person or firm submitting same to undertake the timber harvesting operation described in the notice and shall remain in effect until such time as the person or firm gives notice that the harvesting operation is complete; provided, however, that any change in the facts required to be provided for purposes of such notice, including but not limited to a change in the scope or extent of the operation, must be reported to the Governing Authority of Polk County within three business days after such change.
(Res. No. 2022-69, § 1, 9-13-2022)
Sec. 109-90. - Penalties for violation of notice requirements.
Any person or firm that engages in a timber harvesting operation in the unincorporated portion of Polk County without complying with the notice requirements of this article shall be subject to a citation and trial, and upon conviction shall be fined in an amount not to exceed $1,500.00 for each violation.
(Res. No. 2022-69, § 1, 9-13-2022)
Sec. 109-91. - Applicability of article.
This article applies to activities which qualify as forestry land management practices or agricultural operations under 0.C.G.A. §12-7-17(5) and (6) on land that is zoned for or used for forestry, silvicultural or agricultural purposes. It shall not authorize land disturbing activities incidental to development in conflict with the limitations set forth in 0.C.G.A. §12-7-17(6).
(Res. No. 2022-69, § 1, 9-13-2022)
Sec. 109-92. - Authority of county to take legal action.
Consistent with O.C.G.A. 12-6-24:
(1)
No fee shall be charged to provide and submit the notice required by this article; and
(2)
No permit, including a driveway permit, shall be required of the person or firm engaged in a timber harvesting operation as defined by said Code section. Persons and firms providing the notice required by this article may be asked to consult with county officials responsible for roads and public works for the purpose of minimizing damage to the county's roads, rights-of-way and infrastructure, and are urged to follow recommendations from county officials. Notwithstanding the forgoing, the person or firm conducting the timber harvest operation bears ultimate responsibility for their actions, and nothing in this article shall preclude the Governing Authority of Polk County from taking any and all legal action necessary to protect its property and the health, safety and welfare of its citizens.
(Res. No. 2022-69, § 1, 9-13-2022)
