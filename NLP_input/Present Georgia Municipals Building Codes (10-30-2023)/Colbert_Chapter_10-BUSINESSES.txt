Chapter 10 - BUSINESSES
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 10-1. - Operating or permitting public dance hall, etc.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person or corporation to operate, or permit public dancing, whether for profit, charge, or compensation or gratis, any public dance hall, or place of public assembly for public dancing within the corporate limits of the city from and after the passage of the ordinance from which this section is derived.
(b)
Any person or corporation violating this section shall be punished according to section 1-3.
(Ord. of 2-5-1936)
Secs. 10-2—10-20. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - BUSINESS LICENSES

Sec. 10-21. - License required; exception.
(a)
Every person, firm, or corporation using the streets of the city for the purpose of making sales of any product or merchandise to any firm or any place of business that offer for sale or trade, any product or merchandise, or any person making loans or in any way carrying on a business of description, whether profitable or not, will be required to first obtain a license from the mayor and council before operating such business.
(b)
The above does not include a farmer who is selling or disposing of agricultural, grove, orchard, poultry, dairy, or beef or other produce grown, raised, or produced by him on a farm owned or rented by him. The foregoing exception does not allow a farmer to buy livestock, agricultural or any of the above-named products and resell, unless he certifies to the city clerk in writing that he has owned and has had possession on his farm at least 60 days prior to such sale.
(c)
Any person failing to comply with this section shall be guilty of operating without a license and on conviction shall be punished according to section 1-3.
(Ord. of 8-3-1964(11))

Sec. 10-48. - Insurers license fees.
There is hereby levied an annual license fee upon each insurer doing business within the city in the amount of $15.00. For each separate business location in excess of one not covered by section 10-49, which is operating on behalf of such insurers within the city, there is hereby levied a license fee in the amount of $15.00. For the purposes of this article, the term "insurer" means a company which is authorized to transact business in any of the classes of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5.
(Ord. of 8-7-1995, § 1)
Sec. 10-49. - License fees for insurers insuring certain risks at additional business locations.
For each separate business location, not otherwise subject to a license fee hereunder, operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing and, in connection with such loans or sales offers, solicits or takes application for insurance through a licensed agent of an insurer for insurance, said insurer shall pay an additional license fee of $10.00 per location.
(Ord. of 8-7-1995, § 2)
Sec. 10-50. - Insurers agency license fees; independent insurance agencies, brokers, etc., not otherwise licensed.
There is hereby levied an annual license fee upon independent agencies and brokers for each separate business location from which an insurance business is conducted and which is not subject to the company license fee imposed by section 10-48 in the amount of $15.00 for each such location within the city.
(Ord. of 8-7-1995, § 3)
Sec. 10-51. - Gross premiums tax imposed on life insurers.
There is hereby levied an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer writing life, accident and sickness insurance within the state in an amount equal to one percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.1. The term "gross direct premiums" as used in this section shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 10-48.
(Ord. of 8-7-1995, § 4)
Sec. 10-52. - Gross premiums tax, all other insurers.
There is hereby levied an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer, other than an insurer transacting business in the class of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5(1), doing business within the state in an amount equal to 2½ percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2. The term "gross direct premiums" as used in this section shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 10-48.
(Ord. of 8-7-1995, § 5)
Sec. 10-53. - Due date for license fees.
License fees imposed in sections 10-48 through 10-50 shall be due and payable on the first day of each year.
(Ord. of 8-7-1995, § 6)
