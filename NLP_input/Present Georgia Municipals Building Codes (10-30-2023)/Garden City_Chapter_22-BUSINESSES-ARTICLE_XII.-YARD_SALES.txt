ARTICLE XII. - YARD SALES

Sec. 22-400. - Definitions.
Goods include any goods, merchandise, or other property capable of being the object of a sale regulated hereunder.
Person shall mean and include individuals, partnerships, voluntary associations, and corporations.
Yard sales shall mean and include all sales entitled "garage sales," "lawn sales," "attic sale," "rummage sale," "yard sales" or any similar casual sale of tangible personal property which is advertised by any means whereby the public at large is or can be made aware of said sale.
(Ord. No. 2003-2, § 1, 4-21-03)
Sec. 22-401. - Permits and fees.
It shall be unlawful for any person to conduct a yard sale on residentially zoned property in the city without first filing with the city's code enforcement officer information hereinafter specified and obtaining from such officer a permit to do so, to be known as a "Yard Sale Permit." No fee shall be charged for such permit.
(Ord. No. 2003-2, § 1, 4-21-03)
Sec. 22-402. - Permitting.
Such permit shall be issued to any one person only four times within a 12-month period. No permit shall be issued as to any one property for more than one day during any 90-day period.
Each permit issued under this ordinance must be prominently displayed on the premises upon which the yard sale is conducted throughout the entire period of the permitted sale.
(Ord. No. 2003-2, § 1, 4-21-03)
Sec. 22-403. - Information to be filed.
The information to be filed with the clerk, pursuant to this article, shall be as follows:
(1)
Name of person, firm, group, corporation, association or organization conducting said sale.
(2)
Name of owner of the property on which said sale is to be conducted, and consent of owner if applicant is other than the owner.
(3)
Location at which sale is to be conducted.
(4)
Date of sale.
(5)
Date, nature of any past sale.
(6)
Relationship or connection applicant may have had with any other person, firm, group, organization, association, or corporation conducting said sale and the date or dates of such sale.
(7)
Whether or not applicant has been issued any other vendor's license by any local, state of federal agency.
(8)
Sworn statement or affirmation by the person signing that the information therein given is full and true and known to him to be so.
(Ord. No. 2003-2, § 1, 4-21-03)
Sec. 22-404. - Persons and sales excepted.
The provisions of this article shall not apply to or affect the following persons or sales:
(1)
Persons selling goods pursuant to an order or process of a court of competent jurisdiction.
(2)
Persons acting in accordance with their powers and duties as public officials.
(3)
Any person selling or advertising for sale an item or items of personal property which are specifically named or described in the advertisement and which separate items do not exceed five in number.
(4)
Churches.
(Ord. No. 2003-2, § 1, 4-21-03)
Sec. 22-405. - Enforcement.
The provisions of this article shall be enforced by the city's police department.
(Ord. No. 2003-2, § 1, 4-21-03)
Sec. 22-406. - Penalty.
Any person, association or corporation conducting any such sale without being properly permitted therefor or who shall violate any of the other terms and regulations of this article, shall, upon conviction, be fined not less than $25.00 nor more than $100.00 or be imprisoned for a period not to exceed ten days for each violation.
(Ord. No. 2003-2, § 1, 4-21-03)
