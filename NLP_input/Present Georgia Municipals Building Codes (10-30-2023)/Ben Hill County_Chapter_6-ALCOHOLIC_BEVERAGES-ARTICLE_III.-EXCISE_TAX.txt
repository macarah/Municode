ARTICLE III. - EXCISE TAX

Sec. 6-82. - Levy; schedule.
(a)
Malt beverages. There is hereby levied and imposed upon all wholesale dealers selling malt beverages within the unincorporated area of the county a specific excise tax in an amount set forth by ordinance and published in the schedule of fees on file in the office of the county clerk.
(b)
Distilled spirits and wine. There is hereby levied and imposed upon all wholesale dealers selling distilled spirits and wine within the unincorporated area of the county a specific excise tax in the amount set forth by ordinance and published in the schedule of fees on file in the office of the county clerk.
(Code 2000, § 6-61; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002)
Sec. 6-83. - In addition to other license fees, etc.
The excise tax provided for in this article shall be in addition to any license fee, tax or charge which may now or in the future be imposed upon the business of selling malt beverages, distilled spirits or wine at retail or wholesale, within the unincorporated area of the county.
(Code 2000, § 6-62; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002)
Sec. 6-84. - Payment.
(a)
The excise tax shall be paid as follows: Wholesale dealers of distilled spirits and wine shall file a report, showing, for the preceding calendar month, the exact quantities of beverages by size and container, constituting a beginning and ending inventory of the month, sold within the unincorporated area of the county by the 20th day of each month.
(b)
Each such wholesale dealer shall also remit to the county clerk on the tenth day of each month succeeding the calendar month in which such sales were made the amount of excise tax due by check payable to the board of county commissioners, in accordance with this section, with no discount by state law. All books and records required of each such wholesale dealer by this section and by O.C.G.A. § 3-5-81 shall be subject to inspection and audit by the board of county commissioners to verify compliance with this section.
(Code 2000, § 6-63; Ord. No. 02-001, 3-5-2002)
