Sec. 58-1. - Public drunkenness.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to be and appear in an intoxicated condition in any public place or upon any private property not his own, which condition is made manifest by:
(1)
Boisterousness;
(2)
Indecent condition or act; or
(3)
Vulgar, profane, loud, or unbecoming language.
(b)
The accused may be arrested by law enforcement officers upon being charged with a violation of this section.
(c)
Violation of this section shall, upon conviction, be punishable as provided in section 1-11.
(Ord. No. 76, §§ 1, 3, 4, 4-15-1994)

Sec. 58-2. - Disorderly conduct.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to do any of the following:
(1)
Engage in a fight;
(2)
Act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another person whereby such person is placed in reasonable fear of the safety of such person's life, limb, or health;
(3)
Act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another person whereby the property of such person is placed in danger of being damaged or destroyed;
(4)
Without provocation, uses to or of another person in such other person's presence opprobrious, vulgar, profane, or abusive words which by their very utterance tend to incite to an immediate breach of the peace; words commonly known as fighting words which, as a matter of common knowledge and under ordinary circumstances, will, when used to or of another person in such other person's presence, naturally tend to provoke violent resentment; or
(5)
Without provocation, use obscene, vulgar, or profane language in the presence of or by telephone to a person under the age of 14 years which threatens an immediate breach of the peace.
(b)
A person accused of committing any of the acts prohibited by this section may be arrested by law enforcement officers upon being charged with such violation.
(c)
Violation of this section shall, upon conviction, be punishable as provided in section 1-11.
(Ord. No. 76, § 4, 4-15-1994; Ord. No. 104, §§ 2—4, 6-26-1997)
Sec. 58-3. - Public indecency.
(a)
For the purposes of this section, the term "public place" shall mean any place where the conduct involved may reasonably be expected to be viewed by people other than members of the actor's family or household.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to commit an act of public indecency. A person commits the offense of public indecency when the person performs any of the following acts in a public place:
(1)
An act of sexual intercourse;
(2)
A lewd exposure of the male or female genitals, pubic area, buttocks, or female breasts;
(3)
A lewd appearance in a state of partial or complete nudity; or
(4)
A lewd caress or indecent fondling of oneself or the body of another person.
(c)
Violation of this section shall, upon conviction, be punishable as provided in section 1-11.
(Ord. No. 105, §§ 1—3, 6-26-1997)
Sec. 58-4. - Abandoned personal property.
(a)
When personal property has been placed upon or adjacent to a public right-of-way upon the execution of a writ of possession pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 44-7-50 et seq., such personal property is presumed to be abandoned on the third calendar day following the execution of the writ of possession.
(b)
Any such abandoned personal property remaining on or adjacent to a public right-of-way shall be removed and disposed of by the real property owner and the person executing the affidavit for writ of possession not later than the fifth calendar day following the execution of the writ of possession.
(c)
Failure of the real property owner and the person executing the affidavit for writ of possession to remove any abandoned personal property placed on or adjacent to a public right-of-way, pursuant to writ of possession, as required under this section, shall be a violation and punishable as provided by section 1-11.
(Ord. No. 151, §§ 1—3, 11-8-2001)
