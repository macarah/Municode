Sec. 7-1. - Authority for county to conduct municipal elections.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 21-2-45(c), the city does hereby authorize the county to conduct elections held pursuant to the Georgia Election Code (O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.) and authorizes the county to perform all duties as superintendent of elections as specified under O.C.G.A. Tit. 21, Ch. 3.
(Code 1972, § 3-301; Ord. No. 2007-06, 5-21-07; Ord. No. 2009-001, 1-5-09)
State Law reference— Duties of superintendent, managers and clerks, O.C.G.A. Tit. 21, Ch. 2.
Sec. 7-1.1. - Election officials.
The mayor and council shall by resolution either appoint the county to perform all duties as superintendent of elections pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 21-2-45(c)(1) or appoint a municipal election superintendent, election managers, registrars, absentee ballot clerk and any other officials as are necessary, all of whom shall exercise those powers and duties set forth in the Georgia Election Code (O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.).
(Ord. No. 2007-06, 5-21-07)
Sec. 7-2. - Expenses.
The election superintendent is authorized to incur expenses in the preparation and conduct of an election including the cost of purchasing identification badges for poll officers, the printing of ballots and voters' certificates, and the purchasing of all other supplies necessary for the conduct of an election, except where such supplies are provided by the state. The superintendent is further authorized to incur all necessary expenses to provide voting booths for the proper conduct of all elections.
(Code 1972, § 3-302; Ord. No. 2007-06, 5-21-07)
State Law reference— Funding of elections required, O.C.G.A. Tit. 21, Ch. 2.
Sec. 7-3. - Election districts and polling places.
(a)
The city shall be divided into three (3) election districts or precincts, as now or hereafter described in the City Charter; those electors living within their respective election districts or precincts shall vote at such polling places as shall be designated by the governing authority as provided by law. The elections superintendent shall cause all polling places to be provided with sufficient light and heat.
(b)
Polling places for city elections fo Powder Springs are hereby established as:
(1)
Ward 1: George E. Ford Center, 4818 Atlanta Street, Powder Springs, GA, 30127.
(2)
Ward 2: Ron Anderson Building, 3820 Macedonia Road, Powder Springs, GA, 30127.
(3)
Ward 3: First Baptist Church of Powder Springs, 4330 North Avenue, Powder Springs, GA, 30127.
(Code 1972, § 3-303; Ord. No. 93-12, §§ 1, 2, 6-7-93; Ord. No. 93-21, § 1, 8-2-93; Ord. No. 2005-15, § 1, 6-6-05; Ord. No. 2007-06, 5-21-07; Ord. No. 2007-10, 6-18-07)
Charter reference— Election districts, § 5.11.
State Law reference— Election districts and polling places, O.C.G.A. Tit. 21, Ch. 2.
Sec. 7-4. - Registrars.
The governing body shall appoint registrars, as necessary, who shall serve at the pleasure of the governing body and who shall receive such compensation as provided by the governing body. The registrar shall exercise those powers and duties set forth in the Georgia Municipal Election Code [O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.] The registrar shall provide a list of electors to the chief manager of each election district for each election.
(Code 1972, § 3-305)
State Law reference— Appointment of registrars, O.C.G.A. Tit. 21, Ch. 2.
Sec. 7-5. - Registration of electors; challenge.
(a)
The city hereby elects to use the county registration list for the registration of electors. Persons desiring to vote in city elections shall register with the county registrar no later than 5:00 p.m. on the day thirty (30) days prior to the date of the election in the case of a general election of five (5) days prior to the date of a special election. The applicant must appear in person before the registrar and must have been a resident of the municipality prior to any municipal election in which such person votes. The procedure and form of registration shall conform to the Georgia Election Code [O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.]
(b)
Any elector of the municipality shall be allowed to challenge the right of registration of any person whose name appears upon the elector list or who is applying for registration with the registrar. Any such challenge shall be in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 21-2-229 et seq.
(Code 1972, § 3-304; Ord. No. 2007-06, 5-21-07)
State Law reference— Voter registration, O.C.G.A. § 21-3-120 et seq.
Sec. 7-5.1. - Nomination of candidates.
(a)
Except as otherwise provided by state law, all candidates for municipal office shall file their notice of candidacy and pay the prescribed qualifying fee by the date prescribed in order to be eligible to have their names placed on the election ballot by the election superintendent in the following manner:
Each candidate for municipal office or a designee shall file a notice of candidacy in the office of the election superintendent during the city's qualifying period. The city council shall designate the days of the qualifying period, which shall be no less than three (3) days and no more than five (5) days. The days of the qualifying period shall be consecutive days. Qualifying periods shall commence no earlier than 8:30 a.m. on the last Monday in August immediately preceding the general election and shall end no later than 4:30 p.m. on the following Friday; and, in the case of a special election, the municipal qualifying period shall commence no earlier than the date of the call and shall end no later than twenty-five (25) days prior to the election.
(b)
The hours of qualifying each day shall be from 8:30 a.m. until 4:30 p.m. with one hour allowed for the lunch break; provided, however, that should the city have normal business hours. Except in the case of a special election, notice of the opening and closing dates and the hours for candidates to qualify shall be published at least two (2) weeks prior to the opening of the qualifying period.
(c)
No person shall be eligible to serve as mayor or councilmember unless he or she shall have been a resident of the city for a period of twelve (12) months immediately prior to the date qualifying; and the mayor or councilmember shall continue to reside therein during his or her period of service and to be registered and qualified to vote in municipal elections of this city.
(Code 1972, § 3-306; Ord. No. 2007-06, 5-21-07)
Editor's note— The provision of § 3-306 of the 1972 Code were inadvertently omitted at time of codification of this 1987 Code. The editor has included the provisions as § 7-5.1.
State Law reference— Nomination of candidates, O.C.G.A. § 21-3-91(a)(1); candidates seeking one of two or more public offices, O.C.G.A. § 21-3-93.
Sec. 7-6. - Fixing qualification fees.
The governing body shall fix and publish a qualification fee, to be paid by candidates seeking election in a special or general election, at least two (2) weeks prior to the closing of qualifications. The fee shall be paid to the municipal election superintendent at the time a candidate files notice of candidacy. Said fee shall not exceed three (3) percent of the annual compensation of the office.
(Code 1972, § 3-307; Ord. No. 2007-06, 5-21-07)
State Law reference— Fixing qualification fee, O.C.G.A. § 21-3-90.
Sec. 7-7. - Absentee ballots.
Absentee ballots may be used in all municipal elections. The use of absentee ballots shall be governed by O.C.G.A. §§ 21-2-380 through 21-2-390.
(Code 1972, § 3-308; Ord. No. 2007-06, 5-21-07)
