Chapter 20 - MUNICIPAL COURT[1]

ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 20-1. - Municipal court generally.
The municipal court shall try violations of municipal ordinances and state law as authorized, and shall have the power and authority to impose fines and imprisonment upon persons convicted of said offenses, with the alternative of other punishment allowed by law if such fines are not paid. Said court shall convene at such times and on such occasions as the city council may deem proper and expedient.
Sec. 20-2. - Chief judge and associate judges.
In addition to the qualification and eligibility requirements of Charter section 4.11(b), the chief judge and any associate judge of the municipal court shall be capable of interpreting and applying applicable state law, and the city's Charter and Code of Ordinances in the promulgation of objective and unbiased determinations. The chief judge, and any associate judge deemed necessary, shall be appointed by the mayor and city council and shall serve such terms as they may direct.
Sec. 20-3. - Convening.
The municipal court shall be convened and held at such time and place as established from time to time by the chief judge.
Sec. 20-4. - Municipal court clerk.
The position of municipal court clerk is hereby established. The mayor and city council may appoint an individual to this position, and set compensation therefor. Any such individual appointed shall have all necessary certification required by state and federal law.
(Ord. No. 10-11-001, 12-7-2010)
Secs. 20-5—20-19. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - OFFICE OF SOLICITOR

Sec. 20-20. - Established.
The office of the solicitor shall be established for the purpose of providing representation for the interests of the citizens of the city in matters pertaining to violations of city ordinances and state offenses enforceable within municipal court.
(Ord. No. 11-12-003, § 1, 9-11-2012)
Sec. 20-21. - Appointment.
The mayor and council shall appoint the solicitor. The compensation of the solicitor shall be fixed by the council. No person shall be qualified or eligible to serve as the solicitor unless he shall have attained the age of 21 years and shall be a member of the State Bar of Georgia.
(Ord. No. 11-12-003, § 2, 9-11-2012)
Sec. 20-22. - Jurisdiction and powers.
Pursuant to the city Charter, the solicitor's office shall share the jurisdiction and powers vested therein as well as any powers granted by the laws of the state.
Sec. 20-23. - Serve at pleasure of mayor/council.
The solicitor shall serve at the pleasure of the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. 11-12-003, § 4, 9-11-2012)
