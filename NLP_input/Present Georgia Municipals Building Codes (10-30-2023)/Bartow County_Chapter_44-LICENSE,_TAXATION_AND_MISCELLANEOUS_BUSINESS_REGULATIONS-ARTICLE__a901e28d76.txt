ARTICLE IX. - FAIR BUSINESS PRACTICES ACT

Sec. 44-600. - Title.
This article shall be known as the Bartow County Fair Business Practices Act.
(Ord. of 3-7-2012, Att.)
Sec. 44-601. - Purpose.
The purpose of this article shall be to secure the general health, safety and welfare by ensuring fair dealing between pawnbrokers, secondhand dealers and precious metal dealers and authorizing record keeping for such regulated businesses to discourage illegal activity.
(Ord. of 3-7-2012, Att.)
Sec. 44-602. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning. Definitions from O.C.G.A. § 44-12-130 and O.C.G.A. § 43-37-1 are incorporated herein by reference.
Dealer in precious metals or gems.
(1)
Any person engaged in the business of purchasing precious metals or gems or goods made from precious metals or gems from persons or sources other than manufacturers, manufacturers' representatives, or other dealers in precious metals or gems; or
(2)
A person engaged in any other business if, in conjunction with such business, precious metals or gems or goods made from precious metals or gems are purchased from persons or sources other than manufacturers, manufacturers' representatives, or other dealers in precious metals or gems where such purchase is for resale in its original form or as changed by remounting, melting, re-forming, remolding, or recasting or for resale as scrap or in bulk.
Employee. Any person who works in a regulated business, whether on a part-time or full-time basis, regardless of whether remuneration is received or not.
Exempt items. Clothing, music and video compact discs, furniture, any consignment item, motor vehicles and motorcycles, motorized water craft, waste paper, books, magazines, tires, lumber or wood products and any recycled goods.
Gems. Any precious or semi-precious stone which is cut and polished.
Numismatic coins. Coins whose value as collectors' items exceeds the value of the content of the precious metals in the coins.
Pawnbroker. Any person who works in a pawnshop in the capacity of manager or owner or both.
Pawnshop. Any business that takes or receives by way of pledge, pawn or exchange, any goods, wares, merchandise or personal property of any kind as security for the repayment of money lent thereon.
Pawn or pledge. A bailment of personal property as security for any debt or engagement redeemable upon certain terms and with the implied power of sale or default.
Person. An individual, partnership, corporation, joint venture, trust, association, or any other legal entity however organized.
Precious metals. Gold, silver, or platinum or any alloy containing gold, silver, or platinum.
Regulated business. A pawnshop, a secondhand dealer, or a dealer in precious metals or gems as defined herein.
Regulated secondhand items. Articles or merchandise of any kind, including but not limited to, hand tools, power tools, sporting equipment, electronic equipment, watches, jewelry, precious stones, precious metals, scrap gold, coins, musical instruments, electrical appliances, washers, dryers, refrigerators, and any other item that has been previously sold or put into service and that is not specifically listed as an exempt item under this article.
Secondhand dealer. Any company, sole proprietorship, partnership or other entity whose business is dealing in, purchasing, selling or trading regulated secondhand items. However, secondhand dealers shall not include any persons selling their own household items on an occasional basis (for example, a garage sale or yard sale); sales by a nonprofit charity recognized under IRS Sec. 501(c)(3) which exclusively sells donated items; any person selling at an estate sale; any auctioneer at an auction; or a person whose only business transactions are at a display space or booth at a flea market, festival, fair or swap meet, or similar events.
(Ord. of 3-7-2012, Att.)
Sec. 44-603. - Responsibility for enforcement.
The Bartow County Sheriff shall enforce the provisions of this article.
(Ord. of 3-7-2012, Att.)
Sec. 44-604. - Employees.
No person shall be an employee of a regulated business in any capacity until such person has had a criminal background check run by the sheriff's office and has been issued an annual permit by the sheriff's office authorizing such person to be employed by a regulated business. It shall be the duty of the business owner to assure that there is compliance with the provisions of this section.
(Ord. of 3-7-2012, Att.; Ord. of 4-4-2012, § I)
Sec. 44-605. - Character of person connected with regulated business.
No owner, employee, pawnbroker or any person connected with a regulated business for which a license or permit is sought shall have been convicted of a felony within the last five years. No permit shall be granted to any person with such conviction within five years before the date of application, and any permit holder convicted of a felony while holding a permit shall have his or her permit revoked.
(Ord. of 3-7-2012, Att.; Ord. of 4-4-2012, § II)
Sec. 44-606. - Annual permit.
Before either 1) operating a regulated business or 2) becoming an employee of a regulated business, any person must first file an application with the sheriff for a permit to operate or be employed in the regulated business, pursuant to the following:
(1)
The application shall be made on an annual basis;
(2)
No permit shall be issued until a fee is paid to the sheriff of Bartow County.
• The fee for a permit to operate a regulated business shall be $25.00. This fee shall be in addition to the occupation tax required by chapter 44 of the Code of Bartow County.
• There shall be no fee for an employee permit. The owner of a regulated business must also obtain an employee permit;
(3)
The application shall state the address of the regulated business;
(4)
The application shall contain the full name, address, phone number, date of birth and social security number of any employee or owner or a regulated business;
(5)
The sheriff's office shall investigate each applicant for such license and shall determine whether such applicant is a person of good character and has not been convicted of a criminal offense as indicated in section 44-605. Businesses shall be further investigated for records of past enforcement issues of that business and at the proposed location, as well as whether the business has been and will be in compliance with relevant laws and ordinances.
(6)
Denial of permit. Any person denied a permit shall be sent a written notice letter stating the reasons for the denial. The denied applicant shall have ten days from the date of the notice letter to appeal the decision to the sheriff, and provide any additional information. The notice shall give the permit holder an opportunity for a hearing before the sheriff or his or her designee within ten days of the appeal being requested.
(7)
Revocation of permit. Any person violating the provisions of this article is subject to having his or her permit to operate a regulated business or serve as an employee of a regulated business revoked. The permit holder shall be sent a written notice letter stating the reason for the revocation. The effective date of the revocation shall be five days after the date of the notice letter. The permit holder shall have ten days from the date of the notice letter to appeal the decision to the sheriff, and provide any additional information. The notice shall give the permit holder an opportunity for a hearing before the sheriff or his or her designee within ten days of the appeal being requested. The revocation shall be stayed during the period prior to the hearing.
(Ord. of 3-7-2012, Att.)
Sec. 44-607. - Records of transactions.
(a)
Every regulated business shall maintain permanent electronic records of its transactions containing an accurate description of all property pledged, traded or sold to the business, which records shall be transmitted to the sheriff's office via an electronic automated reporting system. Each of these transactions shall contain an accurate description of all property pledged, traded or sold to the business and shall be made at the time of each transaction, provided that the following information is included:
(1)
The date and time of the purchase, pawn or sale of the property.
(2)
The name, address and telephone number of the customer making the pledge, trade or sale.
(3)
A description of the customer in terms of sex, race, date of birth, height and weight, as well as the driver's license number of the customer or some other identification card which contains a photograph of the customer.
(4)
A description of the pledged or purchased property by serial number, model or other number, if available, and by any identifying marks (e.g., brand name, color, style, etc.)
(5)
The number of the receipt or pawn transaction issued for the property traded, pawned or bought.
(6)
The price paid or the amount loaned, and the number of the check if a check is issued for purchase.
(7)
The maturity date of the transaction, if a pawn.
(8)
A clear and legible photograph of the customer and of the item pawned or bought which photos will be recorded with the electronic automated reporting system at the time of the transaction. The sheriff shall promulgate detailed standards for photographs.
(9)
The signature of the customer.
(10)
Such other information as the sheriff shall require.
(b)
For purposes of this article, "transaction" shall mean any pledge, trade, or sale involving any owner or employee of a regulated business and any other person, occurring on the premises of the business, whether inside or outside the structure. Owners and employees shall not be permitted to evade the recording requirements of this article by asserting such transaction is for personal purposes.
(c)
Every regulated business shall enter each transaction as it occurs into the electronic automated reporting system or may elect to upload electronically via the internet a batch file of all transactions for each business day to the administrator of the electronic automated reporting system immediately at the conclusion of each business day. The administrator of the electronic automated reporting system will electronically transmit all transactions to the sheriff's office.
(d)
In the event that the electronic automated reporting system becomes temporarily or permanently disabled, regulated businesses will be notified as soon as possible. In this event, the businesses will be required to make records of transactions in paper form. Such paper forms must include all information as enumerated in subsection (a) above. Regulated businesses shall maintain a three-day supply of these paper forms.
(e)
Any duly authorized law enforcement officer may, during the ordinary hours of business or any other reasonable time, inspect any regulated businesses' electronic records at the place of business to ensure compliance with this section.
(f)
The sheriff or his designee shall select and designate the required automated reporting system. There will be a fee assessed to the regulated business for each reported transaction; said fee shall be set forth in the fee schedule propagated by the sheriff. This fee will be invoiced to the regulated business and collected by the sheriff's office or by its designee, which may be a third party administrator of the automated reporting system.
The initial automated reporting system shall be the Business Watch International (BWI) Automated Reporting System.
(g)
The sheriff shall be authorized to promulgate other rules and regulations, not inconsistent with this article, to achieve the purposes of this article.
(Ord. of 3-7-2012, Att.)
Sec. 44-608. - Waiting period for disposal of articles.
Any owner or employee of a regulated business who makes a loan on pledged goods, or buys pledged goods on the condition that the seller may repurchase said goods, shall hold said goods for at least 30 days before disposing of them by sale, transfer, shipment or otherwise. Non-pledged goods bought under this section shall be held for at least seven calendar days before disposing of them by sale, transfer, shipment or otherwise.
(Ord. of 3-7-2012, Att.)
Sec. 44-609. - Dealing with minors.
It shall be unlawful for any owner or employee of a regulated business to receive goods in pawn, trade, purchase or sale from a person less than 18 years of age.
(Ord. of 3-7-2012, Att.)
Sec. 44-610. - Lost or stolen items.
(a)
It shall be the duty of every person operating or employed by a regulated business, to report to the sheriff or his duly authorized agent any article or goods sold, traded or pawned to him if he shall have a reason to believe that the article or goods was stolen or lost when presented by seller or customer.
(b)
With respect to any items which would normally have a serial number or other means of identification, if any owner or employee of a regulated business becomes aware that such items have had the identification removed, defaced or destroyed, such fact shall be immediately reported to the sheriff's office.
(c)
If it is determined that an item bought, sold, traded or pawned by a seller or customer to the regulated business is the subject of any reported theft, then the surrender of said item to the sheriff's office shall be done upon demand.
(Ord. of 3-7-2012, Att.)
Sec. 44-611. - Additional regulations.
(a)
Any regulated business shall maintain a fixed location. No person may operate a regulated business without a fixed physical business location (premises).
(b)
Regulated businesses are still subject to the provisions of state law, including O.C.G.A. § 44-12-130 et seq., O.C.G.A. § 43-37-1 et seq., and O.C.G.A. § 43-37-1 et seq.
(c)
Note: Any transaction involving purchase, sale, trade or pawn of a firearm shall also be subject to regulations of the Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco, Firearms and Explosives (ATF). For example, the ATF takes the position that the redemption of a pawned firearm is considered a transfer subject to the permanent Brady law (18 U.S.C. § 922) requiring a NICS (National Instant Criminal Background Check System) check, with certain exceptions. (See Federal Firearms Regulations Reference Guide, 2005).
(Ord. of 3-7-2012, Att.; Ord. of 4-4-2012, § III)
Sec. 44-612. - Violations.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any owner or employee of a regulated business to violate any of the provisions of this article, whether or not such person or employee is the holder of a current valid permit issued according to the terms of this article. Operation of a business without a permit shall be a violation of this article. Working as an employee, including performing any transaction in a regulated business, without a permit shall be violation of this article. Allowing a person without an employee permit to work in the regulated business shall be a violation on the owner. Furthermore, any person failing to comply with any provision of this article or other rules, ordinances and regulations for conduct of the business of a regulated business, shall upon conviction, have the license to conduct business revoked.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any owner or employee of a regulated business to:
(1)
Make any false statement in an application for any permit provided for in this article;
(2)
Make any false entry in any electronic or paper record required by the terms of this article;
(3)
Fail to make an entry of any material matter or required information in any electronic or paper record required by the terms of this article;
(4)
Falsify, obliterate, destroy, or remove from his place of business an electronic or paper record required by the terms of this article;
(5)
Fail to take and record clear and legible photographs of the person or item;
(6)
Refuse to allow any duly authorized law enforcement officer who is certified by the Georgia Peace Officer Standards and Training Council, or who is a federal officer to inspect his records or any goods in his possession during the ordinary hours of business or at any reasonable time;
(7)
Fail to maintain a record of each regulated transaction for at least four years;
(8)
Accept a pledge or purchase property from a person under the age of 18 years or who the pawnbroker knows is not the true owner of such property;
(9)
Make any agreement requiring the personal liability of a pledger or seller or waiving any of the provisions of this part or providing for a maturity date less than one month after the date of the pawn transaction;
(10)
Fail to return or replace pledged goods to a pledger or seller upon payment of the full amount due the pawnbroker unless the pledged goods have been taken into custody by a court or a law enforcement officer or agency; or
(11)
Violate any criminal law of this state while acting in the course of business as an owner or employee of a regulated business.
(c)
Enforcement. Persons who violate this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor. Citation shall be served on the violator and violations shall be heard in the court with appropriate jurisdiction. Minimum fine for a first offense shall be $200.00, and for a second and subsequent offense shall be $350.00. The sheriff shall also be authorized to take other civil action to secure the purposes of this article, including a nuisance action in magistrate or municipal court (as applicable) or an action seeking injunctive relief and compliance in superior court.
(d)
Any person properly identifying himself and presenting a pawn ticket to the pawnbroker shall be presumed to be the pledger or seller and shall be entitled to redeem the pledged goods described in such ticket. In the event such pledged goods are lost or damaged while in the possession of the pawnbroker, it shall be the responsibility of the pawnbroker to replace the lost or damaged goods with like kinds of merchandise and proof of replacement shall be a defense to prosecution. For the purposes of this subsection, "lost" includes destroyed or having disappeared because of any cause, whether known or unknown, that results in the pledged goods being unavailable for return to the pledger.
(Ord. of 3-7-2012, Att.)
Sec. 44-613. - Effective date.
This article shall become effective May 1, 2012.
(Ord. of 3-7-2012, Att.)
