Sec. 9-1. - Codes adopted; enforcement.
(a)
The county fire prevention code, current edition, is hereby incorporated by reference in this section and adopted as part of the general ordinances of the city.
(b)
The life safety code, current edition, is hereby incorporated by reference herein and adopted as part of the general ordinances of the city.
(c)
The county fire department is hereby authorized to inspect any mercantile building, church, school, and any other building of public assembly to assure that the provisions of the fire prevention code and the life safety code are being followed and administered within the city.
(d)
The county fire department may inspect any private dwelling within the city upon the request of the homeowner.
(Code 1972, § 4-110)
Sec. 9-2. - Fire exits—Obstructing.
No person shall at any time place any encumbrances or obstructions of any kind before or upon any fire escape, balcony or ladder intended as a means of escape from fire. It shall be the duty of every member of the police department who shall discover any fire escape encumbered or obstructed in any manner to forthwith report the same through department channels to the police chief, who shall immediately notify the owner or agent or tenant to remove such encumbrance or obstruction and the encumbrance or obstruction shall thereupon be immediately removed.
(Code 1972, § 4-104)
Sec. 9-3. - Same—Signs in theaters.
Every exit in any theater or motion picture house shall be plainly indicated by a sign bearing the word "Exit," which sign shall be kept lit throughout each performance.
(Code 1972, § 4-105)
Sec. 9-4. - Same—In places of public assemblage.
All doors, aisles and passageways within and leading into or out of theaters, churches and all other places of public assemblage shall, during the entire time any show, performance, service, exhibition, lecture, concert, ball or other assemblage may be held therein, be kept adequately lit and free from easels, signs, standards, campstools, chairs, sofas, benches and any other article that might obstruct or delay the exit of the audience, congregation or assemblage; and doors of such buildings while occupied shall not be fastened so that they cannot easily be opened by anyone from within. No person shall sit or stand or remain seated or standing, nor shall the owner or operator of such place allow any person to remain, in any such place of public assemblage in any aisle under any circumstances, or in any exit, or passage required for the safe exit of the assemblage. Clear passage from all exits and on outside sidewalks of all theaters and other places of public assemblage shall be maintained at all times. No aisle, passageway or stairway in any store shall be obstructed with tables, showcases or other obstructions during the hours such store is open to the public.
(Code 1972, § 4-106)
Sec. 9-5. - Fires—Burning trash in public areas.
No person shall burn or cause to be burned any trash, refuse, shavings, paper, leaves, litter or other material of any kind outside any building or upon any street, sidewalk, alley, lot, or other public area.
(Code 1972, § 4-101)
Cross reference— Building fires on streets, § 13-3.
Sec. 9-6. - Same—Burning trash on private property.
Trash or rubbish shall not be burned on any private property except within a safely constructed enclosure made of wiremesh or in a similar safety device.
(Code 1972, § 4-102)
Sec. 9-7. - Same—Permit for bonfire.
No person shall kindle or maintain any bonfire, or knowingly furnish the material for any such fire, or authorize any such fire to be kindled or maintained within the city unless a written permit to do so shall have first been secured from the fire chief. A "bonfire" is defined as any fire outside a building not for the purpose of burning trash.
(Code 1972, § 4-103)
Sec. 9-8. - Same—Guarding.
All persons who shall burn any tar kiln or pit of charcoal, or set fire or burn any brush, grass or other material, whereby any property may be endangered or burned, shall keep and maintain a careful and competent person in charge of such kiln, pit, brush or other material while burning. Fire escaping from such kiln, pit, brush or other material while burning shall be prima facie evidence of neglect of these provisions.
(Code 1972, § 4-108)

Sec. 9-10. - Outdoor burning regulations.
During the period October 1 through April 30 of each calendar year, no person, firm, or entity shall conduct open burning upon any residentially zoned property or properties except in conformity with the following requirements:
(1)
Such burning may only be conducted between 10:00 a.m. and one (1) hour before sunset, and shall not cause air pollution in quantities or characteristics or of a duration which is injurious or which unreasonably interferes with the enjoyment of life or use of property in such area of the city as is affected thereby.
(2)
Only leaves and small limbs which have fallen from trees and are not over two (2) inches in diameter may be burned.
(3)
All fire locations shall be located at least fifty (50) feet from any structure or property line or lines.
(4)
An adult twenty-one (21) years of older must be present the entire time that the fire is burning.
(5)
A functional water hose must be located within twenty-five (25) feet of the burning to control and extinguish the fire.
(6)
The fire must be completely extinguished and out by one (1) hour before sunset, with no smoldering or hot coals remaining.
(7)
No fire shall be ignited or allowed to burn on windy days (average wind speed of ten (10) miles per hour or higher) or upon days when atmospheric conditions (cloudy, overcast, or raining) causes smoke to remain low to the ground.
(8)
Nor fire may be started with petroleum based products.
(9)
No refuse or other combustibles may be burned at any time.
(10)
No leaves or limbs may be transported from one property to another for the purpose of burning.
(11)
Provided however that cooking food for immediate human consumption shall be allowed regardless of the time at which such activities take place.
(Ord. No. 2006-06, 3-20-06)
Sec. 9-11. - Prohibition on the use of fireworks during certain drought conditions.
(a)
When the declared drought response, as determined by the Director of the Georgia Environmental Protection Division, is level 2 or higher, no person, firm, corporation, association or partnership shall, within the city, use, explode, ignite, combust detonate or deflagrate "fireworks," as defined under O.C.G.A. § 25-10-1(a)(1), or cause any such action to occur, except as otherwise state herein.
This prohibition also specifically includes:
(1)
Model rockets and model rocket engines designed, sold, and used for the purpose of propelling recoverable aero models, toy pistol paper caps in which the explosive content averages 0.25 grains or less of explosive mixture per paper cap or toy pistols, toy cannons, toy canes, toy guns, or other devices using such paper caps; and
(2)
Wire or wood sparklers of one hundred (100) grams or less of mixture per item; other sparkling items which are nonexplosive and nonaerial and contain seventy-five (75) grams or less of chemical compound per tube or a total of two hundred (200) grams or less for multiple tubes; snake and glow worms; trick noise makers which include paper streamers, party poppers, string poppers, snappers, and drop pops each consisting of 0.25 grains or less of explosive mixture.
(b)
For purposes of this section, "pyrotechnics" means "fireworks". The term "fireworks" shall not include ammunition consumed by weapons used for sporting and hunting purposes and/or public safety.
(c)
This section shall not apply to:
(1)
Those uses or items specifically exempted at O.C.G.A. §§ 25-10-3 and 25-10-7; and
(2)
Public exhibitions or displays of fireworks by a properly licensed and permitted person, firm, corporation, association or partnership, as governed by O.C.G.A. §§ 25-10-3.2 and 25-10-4, and any other applicable federal, state, county or municipal law, ordinance or regulation.
(Ord. No. 2007-12, 7-16-07)
