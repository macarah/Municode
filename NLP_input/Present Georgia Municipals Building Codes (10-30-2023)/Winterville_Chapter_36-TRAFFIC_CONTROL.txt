ARTICLE II. - UNIFORM RULES OF THE ROAD; TRUCKS

Sec. 36-19. - Uniform rules of the road.
(a)
Adoption by reference. Pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-372 to 40-6-376, the provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-1 to 40-6-395 (except for O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-393 and 40-6-394) and O.C.G.A. §§ 40-2-20 and 40-5-20, known as the Uniform Rules of the Road, and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of this city which like effect as if recited herein.
(b)
Penalties. Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, any person convicted of a violation of any provision of this section shall be punished according to section 1-9.
(Code 1991, § 11-101)
Sec. 36-20. - Prohibiting through trucks on main street.
(a)
Except as stated in subsection (b) of this section, all vehicles with more than six wheels shall be prohibited from travelling along the entirety of Main Street within the corporate limits of the city.
(b)
The prohibitions of this article shall not apply to vehicles on a journey that either begins or ends at a geographic location within the corporate limits of the city.
(c)
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, any person convicted of violating this article shall be punished according to section 1-9.
(Ord. of 12-8-2020(1), §§ 1—3)
Sec. 36-21. - Restriction of truck traffic.
(a)
It shall be illegal for any person to drive a motor vehicle whose weight exceeds 18 tons or whose length exceeds 30 feet or with more than two axles upon Dozier Street or South Church Street within the corporate limits of the city unless the origin or destination of the particular trip lies within the city.
(b)
Any person charged with a violation of the provisions of this section, upon conviction thereof in the municipal court of the city, shall be punished according to section 1-9.
(Ord. of 8-8-1993, § 1)
