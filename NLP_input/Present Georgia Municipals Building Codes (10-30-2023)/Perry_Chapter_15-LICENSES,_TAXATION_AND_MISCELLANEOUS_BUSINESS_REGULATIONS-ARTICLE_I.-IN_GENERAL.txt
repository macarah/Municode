ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 15-1. - Business license tax on insurance companies.
(a)
There is hereby levied for the year 2012 and for each year thereafter an annual license fee upon each insurer doing business within the City of Perry, Georgia, in the amount of seventy-five dollars ($75.00). For each separate business location in excess of one not covered by section 2, which is operating on behalf of such insurers within the City of Perry, Georgia, there is hereby levied a license fee in the amount of seventy-five dollars ($75.00). For the purposes of this section, the term "insurer" means a company which is authorized to transact business in any of the classes of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5.
(b)
For each separate business location, not otherwise subject to a license fee hereunder, operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing and in connection with such loans or sales offers, solicits or takes application for insurance through a licensed agent of an insurer for insurance, said insurer shall pay an additional license fee of twenty-six dollars and 25/100 ($26.25) per location for the year 2012 and for each year thereafter.
(c)
There is hereby levied for the year 2012 and for each year thereafter an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer writing life, accident and sickness insurance within the State of Georgia in an amount equal to one (1) percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.1. Gross direct premiums as used in this section shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by subsection (a) above.
(d)
There is hereby levied for the year 2012 and for each year thereafter an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer, other than an insurer transacting business in the class of insurance designated in Subsection 1 of O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5, doing business within the State of Georgia in an amount equal to 2.5 percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2. Gross direct premiums as used in this subsection shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by subsection (a) above.
(e)
License fees imposed in subsection (a) and (b) above shall be due and payable on the first day of 2012 and on the first date of each subsequent year.
(f)
The city clerk is hereby directed to forward a duly certified copy of this section to the Insurance Commissioner of the State of Georgia within forty-five (45) days of its enactment.
(g)
This section shall become effective November 1, 2011.
(h)
In the event any portion of this section or its subsections shall be declared or adjudged invalid or unconstitutional, it is the intention of the City Council of the City of Perry, Georgia, that such adjudication shall in no manner affect the other subsections, sentences, clauses or phrases of this section which shall remain in full force and effect, as if the invalid or unconstitutional subsection, sentence, clause or phrase were not originally a part of the section.
(Ord. No. 2011-24, 10-18-11)

Sec. 15-2. - Financial institutions business license tax.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93 there is hereby levied an annual business license tax upon state and national banking associations, federal savings and loan associations and state building and loan associations a business license tax at the rate of one-fourth of one (0.25) percent of the gross receipts of the institutions. "Gross receipts" shall mean gross receipts as defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93. Notwithstanding any other provisions of this section, the minimum amount of business license tax due from any depository financial institution pursuant to this section shall be one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) per year.
(b)
Each depository financial institution within the city shall file a return of its gross receipts with the city on March 1 of the year following the year in which such gross receipts were measured. The returns shall be in the manner and in the form prescribed by the commissioner of the department of banking and shall be based upon the allocation method set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(d). The tax levied pursuant to this section shall be assessed and collected based upon the information provided in said return. The due date of taxes levied by this section shall be April 1 of each year.
(Code 1966, § 11-15)
Sec. 15-3. - Revocation of licenses issued under this chapter.
(a)
No license which has been issued or which may hereafter be issued under any article of this chapter shall be revoked, except for due cause as hereinafter defined.
(b)
Failure to pay city taxes, fees or other revenue due to the city within thirty (30) days of the due date shall constitute grounds for revocation.
(c)
Conviction of the licensee or any employee of same of any violation of federal or state law or city ordinance involving the operation of the licensed business shall constitute grounds for revocation.
(d)
An initial license may be denied if the applicant has been convicted of such violations at other locations in the past or in the event the applicant has unpaid city taxes, fees or other revenues due to the city. For the purpose of this section, applicant includes the individual applying for the license or, if the applicant is a partnership, corporation, limited liability company or other legal entity, all persons who have not less than a twenty (20) percent interest in said legal entity.
(e)
At the option of mayor and council, a violation as set forth in subsection (c) hereof, as evidenced by a certified copy of the conviction, shall constitute grounds for refusal to renew said license rather than to revoke the current license. In such event, the licensee may apply, by written request, for a hearing before the mayor and council within thirty (30) days of such refusal to renew.
(f)
In the event mayor and council desire to revoke a current license based on a violation set out herein, they shall cause notice to be served upon the licensee, by registered or certified mail addressed to licensee's establishment, requiring the licensee to appear before the mayor and council and show cause why the license should not be revoked. This hearing shall not be held sooner than five (5) days from the mailing of the notice, and the notice shall specify the date, time and place of the hearing as well as the ground which could, if sustained, result in the revocation of the license.
(g)
The mayor and council shall notify the licensee of their decision within a reasonable time after the hearing in the same manner as provided above for service of notice of hearing. If such decision is to revoke the license, no rebate shall be made on any portion of the license fee paid.
(h)
In lieu of revocation, the mayor and council may, in their discretion, suspend the license for a specified period of time, place the licensee on probation or impose other conditions on the continued holding of the license.
(i)
The licensee may remain open for business after the hearing until a decision is reached and three (3) days have elapsed since the mailing of the decision to licensee.
(Ord. No. 2007-46, § 2, 12-4-07; Ord. No. 2010-20, 8-17-10)
