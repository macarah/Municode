ARTICLE II. - COUNTY ROAD AND RIGHTS-OF-WAY FOR LOGGING PURPOSES[2]

Editor's note— An ordinance adopted June 3, 2002, repealed the former Art. II, §§ 58-31—58-43, and enacted a new Art. II as set out herein. The former Art. II pertained to county roads and rights-of-way and derived from Ord. No. 98-001, 2-2-1998.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 58-31. - Findings.
It is in the best interest of the citizens of the county and the health, welfare and safety of the county and the protection of the county road system, ditches and bridges from damage and excessive maintenance costs, to adopt an ordinance regulating the use of the county rights-of-way for logging purposes and to prohibit loading and unloading of pulp wood and timber within the county rights-of-way.
(Ord. of 6-3-2002(1), § 1)
Sec. 58-32. - Permit required.
No person shall within the unincorporated areas of the county cut, harvest, remove, or haul any trees, pulpwood, logs, poles, posts or wood chips to any wood yard or processing plant located inside or outside of this state without first obtaining a permit from the county road superintendent, in the manner specified in this article.
(Ord. of 6-3-2002(1), § 1)
Sec. 58-33. - Permit issuance.
No such permit shall be issued until the applicant has provided the county road superintendent with prior written notice consisting of: (1) a map of the area which identifies the location of the tract to be harvested, showing the main points of ingress and egress from a public road, to be utilized in such harvest; (2) a statement indicating whether the timber will be removed pursuant to a lump sum sale, unit sale, or owner harvest for purposes of ad valorem taxation as specified in O.C.G.A. § 48-5-7.5; (3) if a lump sum or unit sale, the name, address, and day time telephone number of the timber seller or the timber owner if the harvest is an owner harvest; (3) the name, business address, business telephone number and nighttime or emergency telephone number of the person or firm harvesting such timber; (4) such application may be submitted in person or U.S. mail; (5) a permit fee in the amount of $35.00.
(Ord. of 6-3-2002(1), § 1)
Sec. 58-34. - Bonding requirements.
All persons submitting an application for permit pursuant to this article shall deliver a bond executed by a surety cooperation authorized to transact business in this state or valid irrevocable letter of credit issued by a bank or savings and loan association as defined in O.C.G.A. § 7-1-4, in an amount not less than $5,000.00, said bond for the purpose of protecting the county against any damage caused by such person or firm as the result of logging and hauling operations. Said bond or letter of credit will remain in force until the conclusion of all timber cutting and hauling operations have been completed and the county road superintendent has issued a final certificate of inspection and approval of the operation. If desired, a surety bond may be purchased by the applicant on a yearly basis to cover all access points needed during the calendar year. Forfeiture of a bond for non-performance will require the negligent party to purchase surety bonds on a tract to tract basis.
(Ord. of 6-3-2002(1), § 1; Ord. No. 2014-1, 7-7-2014)
Sec. 58-35. - Supplemental notice required.
Any change in facts and information required to be provided by the applicant to the county road superintendent occurring during the period of the logging and hauling permit shall be communicated to the county road superintendent within three business days after such change.
(Ord. of 6-3-2002(1), § 1)
Sec. 58-36. - Penalty.
This article is adopted pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 12-6-24. Violations of this article shall be punishable by fine not exceeding $500.00.
(Ord. of 6-3-2002(1), § 1)
Secs. 58-37—58-50. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - REGULATING TREE HARVESTING AND HAULING

Sec. 58-51. - Logging and pulp wood loading.
All logging or pulpwood loading operations shall be conducted at a loading site off the county rights-of-way and behind the established ditch line of the county roads.
(Ord. of 6-3-2002(1), § 2)
Sec. 58-52. - Loading or skidding.
Loading or skidding of materials, timber or logs will not be permitted on county roads and county rights-of-way.
(Ord. of 6-3-2002(1), § 2)
Sec. 58-53. - Ditches.
Ditches shall be kept clear of all debris and residue at all times to allow for proper drainage.
(Ord. of 6-3-2002(1), § 2)
Sec. 58-54. - Access site.
County roads at the access site shall be kept in a clean and serviceable condition at all times for the passing of the public, emergency vehicles, school buses, mail carriers, etc.
(Ord. of 6-3-2002(1), § 2)
Sec. 58-55. - Use of crushed stone.
Operators will use crushed stone or some other material as required by the superintendent of roads on access roads to prevent the transport or depositing of excessive amounts of mud onto county roads.
(Ord. of 6-3-2002(1), § 2)
Sec. 58-56. - Suspension of operations.
The superintendent of roads shall have the authority to close down all operations when it is determined that the county roads and access sites are not being maintained. Upon suspension of operations by the superintendent of roads, the firm or company shall have five days to appeal the decision. The appeal shall be made in writing to the county commissioner.
Upon receipt of the written appeal, the commissioner shall have five days to impanel an arbitration board. The arbitration board shall consist of three persons, one person selected by the commissioner, one person selected by the firm or company, and one person mutually agreeable upon by the two persons selected. Once the arbitration board has been selected, the arbitration board shall have no more than 30 days from the receipt of the letter of appeal in which to reach a decision. The decision of the arbitration board shall be final.
(Ord. of 6-3-2002(1), § 2)
Sec. 58-57. - Final inspection.
When the superintendent of roads is advised that the operations have concluded, the superintendent shall inspect the site. If it is determined that the road and access site are not in proper state of repair, repairs shall be made at the expense of the firm or company. The firm or company shall be notified in writing of the decision of the superintendent of roads. Said firm shall have the right of appeal of such decision in the same manner as the appeal of the suspension of logging operations described above.
(Ord. of 6-3-2002(1), § 2)
Sec. 58-58. - Remedies for violation.
The commissioner upon notification of violation by the superintendent of roads shall be authorized to declare the surety bond forfeited for the payment of any repairs or clean up operations to place the county roads or rights-of-way in serviceable condition; however the commissioner shall have all other legal remedies available to him to enforce compliance with this article and the liability of any business enterprises, not limited to the amount of the bond.
(Ord. of 6-3-2002(1), § 2)
Secs. 58-59—58-80. - Reserved.
