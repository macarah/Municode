ARTICLE II. - TAXICABS

Sec. 86-26. - Penalty for violation of article.
Any person violating any of the provisions of this article, upon conviction in the municipal court, shall be punished as provided in section 1-13.
(Code 1976, § 6-1064)
Sec. 86-27. - Registration with police department.
The number assigned a taxicab in accordance with this article, together with the names of the owner and the operator of the taxicab, shall be registered at the police department in a book to be kept by the department for such purpose.
(Code 1976, § 6-1054)
Sec. 86-28. - Identification of vehicles.
(a)
Vehicle signs. Every taxicab shall have a sign plainly painted on the passenger door on each side of the vehicle, in letters not less than four inches high, containing the full name of the company or individual owning the vehicle and the word "Taxicab."
(b)
Illuminated insignia. Every taxicab operated on the streets of the city shall possess, as a part of its equipment, an identification insignia on the top, which shall be electrically lit at night when such taxicab is in use, and which top light shall carry either the name of the company operating the taxicab, the word "Taxi" or the word "Taxicab."
(Code 1976, §§ 6-1051, 6-1053)
Sec. 86-29. - Vehicle numbers.
There shall be painted on the passenger door on each side and on the rear of each taxicab a number at least six inches high, such number to be a separate and distinct number from that on any other public vehicle or taxicab in the city. Such number shall be assigned to such taxicab and the owner thereof by the police department, and shall not be altered or changed without the consent of the department.
(Code 1976, § 6-1052)
Sec. 86-30. - Driver's cap and badge.
All drivers of taxicabs using the streets of the city shall wear, when driving such taxicabs, a uniform cap of the type known as a chauffeur's cap, which shall also carry a badge. Such badge on such cap shall bear the name of the company owning the taxicab, or, if the taxicab is owned by the driver, shall bear an insignia indicating that the driver is a chauffeur.
(Code 1976, § 6-1055)
Sec. 86-31. - Posting of rates.
Every person engaged in the business of operating a taxicab within the corporate limits of the city shall have posted in a conspicuous place in the vehicle operated for the transportation of passengers a printed and detailed schedule of the rate charged by the taxicab for the transportation of passengers. This schedule shall show specifically the rate chargeable per passenger for transportation.
(Code 1976, § 6-1060)
Sec. 86-32. - Driver not to leave vehicle.
It shall be unlawful for any driver of any taxicab to leave such vehicle while waiting to be hired.
(Code 1976, § 6-1056)
Sec. 86-33. - Solicitation of passengers.
It shall be unlawful for any person to solicit passengers, directly or indirectly, at any taxicab stand or upon the streets of the city.
(Code 1976, § 6-1057)
Sec. 86-34. - Cruising.
It shall be unlawful for any taxicab driver to drive such vehicle about the streets of the city while not actually in service of transporting a passenger from one place to a point of destination, except at such times as the vehicle is not available for public use, and such unlawful practice shall be construed as soliciting and a violation of this article.
(Code 1976, § 6-1058)
Sec. 86-35. - Acceptance of additional passengers.
It shall be unlawful for the operator of any taxicab to pick up a second passenger or to permit any other person to occupy or ride in the taxicab when the taxicab is occupied by a fare-paying passenger or by members of the party of the fare-paying passenger, without the consent of such fare-paying passenger.
(Code 1976, § 6-1059)
Sec. 86-36. - Taxicab stands.
(a)
Generally. No parking of taxicabs shall be permitted in the city except at such stands as may be designated by the police department. Such places shall be designated by signs. Whenever the police department shall assign specific spaces as taxicab parking stands, such stands shall be used exclusively by persons operating taxicabs and by no other persons.
(b)
Telephone installations. It shall be unlawful for any person to install or attach any telephone or similar device on any telephone post, telegraph post, tree or post, or on the side of any building, in any street, sidewalk, alley or lane in the city for the purpose of using such telephone in connection with a taxicab business, unless a place in the street adjacent to such telephone has been designated a regular stand for taxicabs. Any person desiring to have a place designated as a regular stand for taxicabs in the city, where a telephone is to be installed adjacent to such stand on a telephone pole, telegraph post, tree, box or side of a building, on the sidewalk or in the street, shall make application in writing to the police department for the establishment of such taxicab stand, setting out where such stand is desired to be set apart. The written permission of the property owner whose property abuts directly on the street where such taxicab stand is to be established shall be first obtained and filed with the application.
(Code 1976, §§ 6-1061, 6-1062)
Sec. 86-37. - Lease of vehicles.
No owner or operator of any public motor vehicle for hire or taxicab shall hire, rent, lease or sublet such vehicle to any other person or shall enter into any similar agreement, scheme or device with any such person unless and until the owner of the vehicle notifies the police department, in writing, of such lease or similar agreement, together with the name and address of the person to whom the vehicle is hired, leased or sublet. The person to whom the vehicle is hired, leased or sublet shall paint his name thereon as required in this article and shall pay a license fee to the city.
(Code 1976, § 6-1063)
Secs. 86-38—86-60. - Reserved.

Sec. 86-61. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Business enterprise means a unit of economic organization or activity in the form of person, firm, partnership, corporation, trust or association.
Property owner means any person in lawful possession of private property, including but not limited to legal title holder, lessee or property manager. In no case shall any owner, operator or agent of a business enterprise engaged in the towing of vehicles from private property be considered a property owner unless he is the legal title holder or in possession of said property.
Removing means the act of changing by towing, the location of a parked vehicle from its location on private property to a storage site.
Storing means to place and to leave a towed vehicle at a site where the business enterprise or another business enterprise exercises control and supervision over the vehicle.
Towing means drawing or pulling a vehicle by means of another vehicle equipped with booms, car carriers, winches or similar equipment.
Wrecker means an automotive vehicle with hoisting apparatus and equipment for towing wrecked or disabled automobiles or other vehicles. The term "wrecker" also includes any vehicle otherwise equipped and used for the purposes of towing wrecked or disabled automobiles or vehicles.
(Ord. of 2-16-98(4), § 1)
Sec. 86-62. - Compliance required.
It shall be unlawful for any person, either as principal, agent or employee, to use or operate upon any of the streets of the city any wrecker or to do any trespass towing (as defined in O.C.G.A. § 44-1-13) without complying with this article.
(Ord. of 2-16-98(4), § 1)
Sec. 86-63. - Licensing and registration.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any business enterprise to engage in the business of towing, removing and storing of motor vehicles without first having secured a license from the city's police department to do so. Such license is required for businesses picking up vehicles at locations in the city, taking vehicles to locations in the city, or both. There is no requirement that employees of such business enterprise have the license provided for by this section. The business enterprise itself shall apply for, obtain and maintain a license.
(b)
Every business enterprise desiring to obtain a license required in this section shall make written application to the city's police department for that privilege upon forms to be prepared and approved by the department. The application shall state the name and address of the applicant; place where the proposed business is to be located; nature and character of the business to be carried on; if a partnership, the names of the partners; if a corporation, the names of the officers; the charges to be imposed for various services; amounts and types of insurance held; such other information as may be required by the police; and shall be sworn by the applicant or agent thereof. All applicants shall furnish all data, information and records requested of them by the police department, and failure to furnish this data, information and records within 30 days from the date of the request shall automatically result in the dismissal, with prejudice, of the application. Applicants, by filing an application, agree to produce for oral interrogation any persons requested by the police department and considered as being important in the ascertainment of facts relative to the license. The failure to produce a person within 30 days after being requested to do so shall result in automatic dismissal of the application. A fee of $_______ shall be charged each applicant to cover the city's administrative cost of processing the application.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any business enterprise to use or to operate upon any of the streets of the city any wrecker without having first registered the vehicle with the police department upon forms to be furnished by the department. The information for registration shall contain the following:
(1)
The make, model and manufacturer's serial number of the vehicle;
(2)
Date the vehicle was put into service as a wrecker;
(3)
The driver's license number of the owner, if an operator, and of all other operators of the wrecker;
(4)
The name of the insurance company with which the owner and all operators have liability insurance coverage with respect to the operation of the vehicle, as required by law;
(5)
The permit number of each person who shall drive or operate the vehicle on behalf of the owner; and
(6)
Such other information as may be reasonably required by the chief of police.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for a person hired by the owner of private property within the city to remove vehicles from any off-street parking facility or to do any trespass towing as defined in O.C.G.A. § 44-1-13 without having first obtained approval of the police department for the signs required to be posted on the property as provided in said section, and as follows:
(1)
Signs shall be located at each designated entrance to a parking lot or parking area where parking prohibitions are to be effective. Where there is no designated entrance, such signs shall be erected so as to be clearly visible from each and every parking space.
(2)
Such signs shall be 7.5 square feet in area (2.5 feet by three feet).
(3)
Such signs located at a designated entrance to a parking lot shall be at least four feet above the site grade. Where there is no designated entrance, such signs shall be six feet above the grade if vehicles are to be parked between the sign and the maneuvering area for the parking space.
(4)
Such signs shall state in letters at least three inches high that "unauthorized vehicles may be towed away at owner's expense to …" Such signs shall also include the following information in letters at least 2½ inches high:
a.
Such vehicles may be removed at a cost of $75.00 and stored at a maximum cost of $7.50 per day to be paid by the owner;
b.
The address of the location to which such vehicles are towed;
c.
Checks and credit cards shall be accepted in lieu of cash and for no additional fee;
d.
No storage fee may be charged if the vehicle has been held less than 24 hours;
e.
Shall state that these conditions are required by state law and city ordinances; and
f.
Shall state that complaints may be made to the police at 966-7770.
No abbreviations shall be used. The lettering on such signs shall be black on a white, reflective grade background, and shall be illuminated if out of headlight range.
(Ord. of 2-16-98(4), § 1; Ord. of 8-17-98(1), § 1)
Sec. 86-64. - Issuance, renewal and denial of license.
(a)
The chief of police or his authorized designee shall approve issuance or renewal of a license hereunder where he finds that:
(1)
The applying business enterprise does not have as an officer or director, if a corporation, as a partner, if a partnership, or as a sole proprietor, if a sole proprietorship, a person who is or was an officer, director, partner or sole proprietor of a business enterprise which has had its towing license revoked by action of the chief of police within five years of the date of application, or who has been convicted of any theft related crime or crime involving violence within the past seven years.
(2)
The tow or storage yard has been inspected and found to be in compliance with the requirements of this article, the fire code, and all other applicable laws and city ordinances.
(b)
A license issued or renewed pursuant to the provisions of this ordinance shall not be assignable or transferable from one business enterprise to another business enterprise.
(c)
All licenses shall expire on December 31 of each year and shall be renewed between December 1 and December 31 of each year.
(Ord. of 2-16-98(4), § 1)
Sec. 86-65. - Regulatory fees.
In accordance with the policy of the city, the costs of the enforcement of laws regulating towing businesses, shall be borne by the applicant, licensee or permittee and current costs shall be reflected in the fees required for such license or permit. Where a fee or sum of money is referred to in this article, the exact amount of such fee or sum of money shall be found either herein or in the code sections setting fees for businesses.
(Ord. of 2-16-98(4), § 1)
Sec. 86-66. - Revocation of license; appeals from order of revocation.
(a)
The chief of police or the chief's designee may revoke the license to operate a wrecker of any business enterprise on any of the following grounds:
(1)
If the license to operate was procured by fraudulent conduct or false statement of a material fact;
(2)
If the licensee proceeds to the scene of an accident in violation of this article;
(3)
If the licensee uses a police radio to obtain information as to the location of the scene of an accident;
(4)
If the licensee shall pay in the form of a gratuity any person not interested in an accident for information as to the location of an accident;
(5)
If the licensee makes any payment to the owner or one in possession of a parking lot or vacant lot in excess of the reasonable and customary fee ordinarily charged by the owner or person in possession of the parking lot or vacant lot for parking thereon, such payment shall be considered a kickback;
(6)
If the licensee charges fees in excess of those set out in section 86-68 or violates section 86-74; or
(7)
If the licensee violates O.C.G.A. § 44-1-13 or any section of this article.
(b)
The violation, within a period of one year, of any three provisions of this article by an owner or by any employee of a wrecker service or company shall result in the automatic revocation of the wrecker service or company's license.
(c)
This revocation shall terminate all authority and permission granted by the license to operate a wrecker service issued to the business enterprise. Any business enterprise whose license has been revoked shall not be eligible to again apply for a license for a period of one year from the date of revocation.
(d)
Any business enterprise whose license to operate has been revoked by the police chief may file an appeal to a review board composed of the chairman of the city's police committee, the city marshall, and the assistant city administrator. An informal administrative hearing shall be held before the panel within 30 days of the submission of the appeal. The chairman of the police committee or his designee shall preside over the hearing. The majority and effective decision of the panel shall be provided to the appellant in writing within five days of the hearing. The panel's decision may be appealed by filing a notice of appeal with the city administrator within ten days of the appellant's receipt of the decision. Within ten days of receiving the appeal, the city administrator shall also conduct an informal hearing, and within ten days after the hearing, he shall issue a ruling which shall be final.
(Ord. of 2-16-98(4), § 1)
Sec. 86-67. - Fingerprints; permits.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to drive, use or operate upon any of the streets of the city any wrecker without first having been fingerprinted by the police department and having been issued a permit by the police department indicating that this person is eligible to use, drive or operate a wrecker.
(b)
The police department shall conduct a complete search relative to any police record of the person fingerprinted. If there is a record of a violation of this article within one year immediately preceding the application for the permit, no permit may be issued and the person may be ineligible to use, drive or operate a wrecker upon any of the streets of the city; however, any person denied a permit under this section shall have the right of appeal to the review board identified in section 86-66(d).
(c)
Any permit issued under this article shall expire as set forth in this subsection unless earlier canceled or revoked. The police department may prescribe regulations for certifying the eligibility of this person to continue to use, drive or operate a wrecker without the necessity of this person being again fingerprinted. The annual permit fee shall be $15.00. This permit shall expire on December 31 of the calendar year for which it was effective and shall be renewed between December 1 and December 31 of each calendar year.
(d)
The business enterprise owning the wrecker shall keep all permits under this section in a place so that they may be inspected by any member of the police department at any time. If the employment of any person possessing this permit is terminated, the permit shall be returned to the person by the business enterprise owning the wrecker.
(e)
No permit shall be issued to any person who has been convicted in this or any other country within three years immediately prior to the application for this permit of soliciting for prostitution, illegally dealing in narcotics, sex offenses or any other crime involving moral turpitude. Further, this permit shall not be issued to any person who, within the three years immediately preceding the application for this permit, has been convicted of three or more moving traffic violations. To be eligible for this permit, the person applying must possess a current driver's license as provided by the laws of the state. Any person denied a permit under this section or any person whose permit may be revoked under this section shall have the right to appeal this denial or revocation to the review board identified in section 86-66(d).
(f)
Any basis set forth in subsection (e) of this section for the denial of a permit shall, if it occurs subsequent to the issuance of a permit, constitute the basis for a revocation of the permit.
(Ord. of 2-16-98(4), § 1)
Sec. 86-68. - Fees charged.
(a)
Any towing and storage firm engaged in the business of trespass towing shall not charge the owner of any towed vehicle or personal property in excess of $75.00 for the removal of the vehicle or personal property or in excess of $7.50 per day for storage fees; however, no storage fee shall be charged for the first 24-hour period from the time the vehicle or personal property is removed from the property. The fee of $75.00 shall not include additional fees which may be charged for using dollies, trailers, lifts, slim jims or any other atypical equipment or service. The maximum fees in this section shall not apply to the trespass towing of vehicles which weigh in excess of two tons.
(b)
Trespass towing and storage fees shall be established annually by the city council after receiving recommendations from the police department based on financial information submitted by the trespass towing firms as to their costs for the removal and storage of vehicles and personal property and on other information. Information shall be submitted by licensees by October 31 of each year.
(Ord. of 2-16-98(4), § 1; Ord. of 8-17-98(3), § 2)
Sec. 86-69. - Lettering on vehicles.
It shall be unlawful for any business enterprise to use or operate upon any street of the city any wrecker unless the vehicle shall have lettered on each side in plain view the name of the business enterprise owning and causing the wrecker to operate on the streets of the city, the address from which the wrecker is operating and the telephone number. This lettering shall be in a contrasting color to the color of the wrecker and shall be no smaller than four inches for the business name and two inches for the street address and telephone number.
(Ord. of 2-16-98(4), § 1)
Sec. 86-70. - Service calls.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for the business enterprise owning any wrecker or the business' agent, representative or employee to go to any place where an accident has occurred unless called by the driver or the owner of the disabled vehicle or by the owner's authorized representative or employee or to go to the place of a wreck because of information received by police radio.
(b)
Prior to towing a vehicle from any site where an accident has occurred, the employee of a wrecker service, both public and private, shall clear the road surface of any broken glass and debris resulting from the accident.
(Ord. of 2-16-98(4), § 1)
Sec. 86-71. - City employees soliciting business.
It shall be unlawful for any city employee to solicit business for any wrecker business enterprise.
(Ord. of 2-16-98(4), § 1)
Sec. 86-72. - Solicitation of towing work.
It shall be unlawful to drive along any street or bridge and solicit towing work. Solicitation of towing work by the operator or the occupant of a wrecker while parked on any street or bridge is also prohibited. A wrecker operator shall not proceed to the scene of a disabled vehicle without having been requested or notified to do so, as provided in section 86-70.
(Ord. of 2-16-98(4), § 1)
Sec. 86-73. - Notification upon removal of vehicle at request other than police officer.
The operator of any wrecker removing a private motor vehicle at the request of any person other than a police officer on duty for the police department shall report to the department the fact that the vehicle was removed and its present storage place, together with a description of the vehicle and the tag number. The report shall be made by the wrecker operator by telephone communication within one hour of the deposit of the vehicle at its storage point.
(Ord. of 2-16-98(4), § 1)
Sec. 86-74. - Impound lot; attendant.
(a)
Every business enterprise operating a wrecker service within the city shall maintain an impound lot within the city limits or within three miles of roadway therefrom, and all vehicles impounded within the city limit shall be delivered to this lot.
(b)
Any business enterprise operating a wrecker upon the streets of the city and maintaining an impound lot within the city as set forth in subsection (a) of this section shall make available an attendant at the impound lot 24 hours of every day, and this attendant shall be clothed with the authority to release any impounded vehicle upon the owner meeting the legal requirements for release.
(c)
There shall be posted at the entrance to the impound lot nearest the impound lot office a sign, the minimum size of which shall be 24 inches by 24 inches, painted white with red lettering, stating the towing charge, the storage rate per day, that checks and credit cards shall be accepted in lieu of cash and for no additional fee, that no storage fee may be charged if the vehicle has been held less than 24 hours, that these conditions are required by state law and city ordinance, and that complaints may be made to the police permits section at 966-7770. The location of such sign must be approved by the police department.
(d)
Lighting shall be provided at a minimum average maintained footcandle value of two on a horizontal plane at the finished surface or grade level.
(e)
Wrecker services must store impounded vehicles within an enclosed building or a fenced and secure outside area which has controlled public access. Said storage area shall comply with standards set forth by the city's zoning ordinance pertaining to storage yards. The storage area for impounded vehicles within the corporate limits of the city must be located within an area zoned for storage yards as set forth in the city zoning ordinance which presently permits storage yards in I-1 and I-2 zoning districts. No sale of vehicles or any parts of vehicles shall be permitted to be stored at any one time. No wrecker service vehicle and no impounded vehicle shall at any time be parked or stored on a street, lane, sidewalk, or other right-of-way or public property.
(f)
Upon impoundment of any vehicle by a wrecker service, the wrecker service shall maintain records which shall include the following information:
(1)
The date and time the call was received by the wrecker service;
(2)
The name of the caller;
(3)
The date and time of initial towing;
(4)
The place of initial towing;
(5)
The date and time of arrival at the impound lot;
(6)
The date and time of release to the owner; and
(7)
The name of the wrecker driver and helper.
(g)
The records required by subsection (f) of this section shall be maintained at a location where affected members of the public may obtain such information by telephone or in person, during regular business hours. Further, all wrecker services, public and private, which impound vehicles shall register with the police department the current telephone number of the person responsible for releasing the vehicles.
(h)
It shall be unlawful for any wrecker service to operate within the city without employing the services of a check approval agency and the major credit card services; and it shall be unlawful to refuse to accept, in lieu of cash, any check which can be insured by a check approval agency, any draft drawn on a credit union which can be insured by a check approval agency or any major credit card for the payment of any and all fees and costs resulting from the towing and storage of the impounded vehicle. For purposes of this subsection, the term "major credit card" means a Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discovery and Diner's Club card. The city administrator shall transmit a copy of this subsection to each wrecker service provider.
(i)
The records required by subsection (f) of this section shall be retained for a period of three years and shall be made available for inspection 24 hours a day at the request of the police department.
(Ord. of 2-16-98(4), § 1; Ord. of 8-17-98(3), § 3)
Sec. 86-75. - Vehicle not towed upon operator returning.
The business enterprise operating any wrecker service summoned to tow away any vehicle, whether on a public street, public property or private property, shall not tow the vehicle away and shall not charge any fee if the operator of the vehicle returns prior to the wrecker service having left the location to which summoned and moves the vehicle immediately and prior to the wrecker vehicle having left the location to which summoned.
(Ord. of 2-16-98(4), § 1)
