ARTICLE XIV. - STOPPING, STANDING OR PARKING PROHIBITED IN SPECIFIED PLACES

Sec. 20-14.1. - Prohibited on private property without invitation.
It shall be unlawful to park, rank, drive or leave an automobile or other vehicle upon private property in the city without invitation or permission from the person or corporation in possession, or entitled to possession, of the premises.
It shall be unlawful for the driver of any vehicle to drive the same upon or through private property in the city for the purpose of cutting through or taking a short-cut without the permission of the person in possession, or entitled to possession, of the premises.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 15, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-14.2. - Where prohibited area designated.
It shall be unlawful to park or rank a vehicle at any point on the streets where limit lines, signs or targets are so placed as to make it clearly appear that parking or ranking at such point is prohibited.
The penalties for violations of this section shall be the same as established by section 20-16.11.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 15, 7-20-71; Ord. No. 00-35, § 2, 4-4-00)
Sec. 20-14.3. - No parking during street paving; authority of traffic engineer.
The traffic engineer is hereby authorized to designate as a "no parking" area and to post "no parking" signs on any street in Columbus, Georgia, on the day when such street is scheduled to be paved or resurfaced and all ordinances allowing parking on such streets shall be of no force and effect while thus designated as a "no parking" area.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 15, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-14.4. - On sidewalks, etc.

No person shall park in such driveway or entrance in such a manner as to block the sidewalk.

Sec. 20-14.5. - In meter zone limits; time in nonparking spaces; position.
In all parking meter zones in the city established by ordinances of the city, it shall be unlawful for the operator of a motor vehicle to stop in the part of a street comprising such zone for a longer period of time than three minutes, except in regularly designated white limit line of parking meter spaces. In a part of a street comprising such parking meter zone, an operator shall remain at the wheel of any vehicle which stops at any place other than in regularly designated white limit lines of parking meter spaces.
The driver of any vehicle permitted to stop in such parking meter zone pursuant to the provisions of this section, after stopping for such three minutes' period shall drive forward and not stop again, unless as required by traffic light or stop sign until he has passed the area within the next street intersection, after which stopping in a parking meter zone shall be again limited to a period of three minutes in accordance with the provisions herein.
In all cases where a vehicle is stopped in a parking meter zone pursuant to the provisions of this section, the driver of such vehicle shall stop on the right side of the street, parallel with the curb, and with the right side of his vehicle not closer than 24 inches from the nearest part of the vehicle next to which the vehicle of such driver has stopped.
Excepted from the provisions of this section is the area on Broadway lying between 9th Street and 14th Street, and the three minute parking privilege shall not apply in said area.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 15, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-14.6. - Near fire plugs, in driveways; time in streets; loading areas.
(a)
Fire hydrants. It shall be unlawful for the operator of a vehicle to park, rank or stop such vehicle within ten feet of a fire hydrant.
(b)
Loading zones. It shall be unlawful for the operator of a vehicle to park, rank or stop such vehicle within an official loading zone unless such person(s) is duly loading or unloading passengers or cargo and in no event shall any vehicle be parked, ranked or stopped in such loading zones for continuous period of time more than 15 minutes.
(c)
Driveways. It shall be unlawful for the operator of a vehicle to park, rank or stop a vehicle in a public or private street so as to obstruct free passage to or from a public or private driveway; or to park, rank or stop such vehicle in a public driveway within street right-of-way so as to obstruct free passage.
(d)
Time in streets. It shall be unlawful for the operator of a vehicle to park or rank a vehicle in a public street for a continuous period of more than 12 hours.
(e)
Penalties. Persons convicted of violating paragraph (a) of this section shall be fined $100.00 for each offense. The penalties for persons convicted of violating paragraphs (b), (c) and (d) of this section shall be the same as established by section 20-16.11.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 15, 7-20-71; Ord. No. 00-35, § 3, 4-4-00; Ord. No. 11-22, § I, 5-31-11; Ord. No. 17-57, § 1, 10-31-17)
Sec. 20-14.7. - Vehicle dealers; restrictions; penalty.
(a)
Vehicles which are kept, offered or exposed for sale, or kept for repair, by automobile dealers or other persons, may be parked or ranked in the part of the street or streets of the city abutting the fronts or sides of the places of business of such automobile dealers or persons only. The total length of the space shall be occupied by such vehicles to be co-extensive with and immediately opposite to the frontage or frontages of such places of business on such street or streets.
(b)
Where a street is divided by a center park into two roadways for vehicular travel, vehicles kept, offered or exposed for sale, or kept for repair, by automobile dealers or other, may be parked or ranked on both sides of that roadway which is nearer to such dealer's or person's place of business. Where a street has only one roadway, such vehicles may be parked or ranked only on one side of such roadway, which shall be the side next to such dealer's or person's place of business.
(c)
This section is not intended to apply to vehicles in transit, when parked or ranked in the streets of the city; the intention hereof is to prohibit an unreasonable use of the streets for business purposes, by automobile dealers and other persons, who, by parking and ranking vehicles which are kept, offered or exposed for sale, or kept for repair, in front of places other than their own, interfere with the proper access to such places of other persons; and this section is intended to be construed and enforced so as to prohibit such practice.
(d)
This section is not intended to extend time limits for parking or ranking fixed by ordinances of the city.
(e)
Any person who parks or ranks a vehicle contrary to the provisions hereof, and any person owning a vehicle who permits it to remain parked or ranked contrary to such provisions, shall, upon conviction before the recorder of the city, be punished as provided in section 1-8 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 15, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-14.8. - Painting or marking; authorization.
No person, unless duly authorized by ordinance of the city, shall paint any curb on any street in the city with yellow or other colored paint or material; or erect or paint signs or lines in any street in the city indicating parking or ranking lines or places for vehicles; or erect or paint signs or lines of any kind indicating hours of times for parking or ranking vehicles.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 15, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-14.9. - Taxi stands, persons entitled to use.
No person shall stop or rank, a vehicle in a taxicab stand space unless such person is duly authorized under the ordinances of the city to operate a taxicab, and is at the time operating a taxicab duly licensed under the tax and taxicab ordinances of the city; or unless such person is authorized by this Code to park in such space.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 15, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-14.10. - Towing—By police department.
When any vehicle is parked, ranked, or stopped in violation of any provision of section 20-14.6 or section 20-14.14 of this article or any law relating to traffic, it shall be the duty of the police department of said city to tow or have towed such vehicle to the impounding lot of said city.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 15, 7-20-71; Ord. No. 73-151, § 2, 11-13-73)
Sec. 20-14.11. - Same—List of vehicles; report.
The police department shall keep a list of all vehicles towed, accessible to public inspection and inquiry, and submit to the city manager monthly a statement of the number of vehicles towed and the name or names of the persons performing the services.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 15, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-14.12. - Same—Release; evidence of ownership; storage fees.
When vehicles are towed to police headquarters or to the public works department pursuant to section 20-14.10 of this chapter, the vehicle shall not be released until the person calling for the vehicle has afforded the police department satisfactory evidence of his ownership and paid the storage fees for removal and storage, such fees not to exceed $5.00 for cost of removal and $0.75 per day after the expiration of 24 hours for storage, which shall be assessed against the vehicle or the owner or other person having the right to possession of the impounded vehicle.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 15, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-14.13. - Loads; extending, restrictions.
A vehicle shall neither rank, park nor stop with any part of it or its load extending beyond limit lines on a street; nor within ten feet of a fire hydrant; nor within 15 feet of an intersecting street line.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 15, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-14.14. - Parking in areas designated for on duty government employees prohibited; parking in vicinity of government center.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for the operator of a vehicle to park or stop such vehicle within off-street parking areas designated and signed as "For On Duty Employee Parking Only," unless such operator is an employee at the government center and such employee shall park only in the space designated and assigned to such employee.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to park or rank a vehicle in the south driveway of the government center from Second Avenue to First Avenue other than for loading and unloading, except for the following:
(1)
Representatives of the news media when such representatives are on news assignments at the government center.
(2)
Service vehicles while servicing the government center.
(c)
It shall be unlawful at all times for any operator of a vehicle to park, rank, or stop such vehicle in the exit and entrance ramps to the government center underground parking area.
(d)
Any person violating any provisions of this section, upon conviction before the recorder, shall be fined in the amount of $5.00.
(Ord. No. 73-151, § 1, 11-13-73)
Sec. 20-14.15. - Handicapped parking areas.
It shall be unlawful and punishable as provided in section 20-19.1 of this Code for any person to park or rank a vehicle in any parking space or area designated by sign as a parking space for handicapped parking, whether on the public streets and ways of Columbus, Georgia, or upon streets, ways and parking areas which are owned by public or quasipublic bodies regulated by Columbus, Georgia, or on privately owned streets, ways and parking areas, without displaying on said vehicle one of the following:
(1)
A license tag issued by the State of Georgia or other state indicating thereon that it is issued to a handicapped veteran or a disabled person; or
(2)
A sticker or decal or other insignia issued by or under the authority of the mayor's committee for employment of the handicapped indicating that the vehicle is used for the transportation of a handicapped person.
(3)
Persons desiring to obtain the certificate of the mayor's committee for employment of the handicapped for use pursuant to this section shall make application to the mayor's committee for employment of the handicapped giving the type of vehicle, the make, model number, year model, manufacturer's number, and Georgia or other state license tag registration number, the name and address of the owner of the vehicle, and the name and address of the handicapped person for whose use the vehicle is intended, if different from the owner. Said application shall be accompanied by the certificate of a doctor so certifying the applicant is handicapped.
The mayor's committee for employment of the handicapped shall issue under its authority such certificate in the form of a sticker or decal or other insignia as it shall determine, that is to be affixed to the vehicle. Such certificate shall be valid for one year from the date of issuance.
(4)
It shall be unlawful and punishable as provided in section 20-19.1 of this Code for any person to park any vehicle in any of the parking places referred to above when said vehicle is not actually being used to transport a handicapped person, notwithstanding that such vehicle may have the required sticker, decal or other evidence attached to it.
(5)
Any person violating this [section] shall pay a fine of $200.00. If an offender proceeds to recorder's court and if convicted by the recorder, offenders shall be subject to imprisonment and court costs and fines not to exceed the statutory maximum set forth at O.C.G.A. § 40-6-226, which is currently established at $500.00.
(Ord. No. 80-26, § 1, 4-1-80; Ord. No. 10-25, § 2, 6-15-10)

Sec. 20-14.17. - City manager authorized to increase parking time limits.
The city manager is hereby authorized to increase parking time limits to a maximum of eight hours for parking spaces in a temporary construction zone for temporary construction projects or for special events and such increased parking time limits are hereby authorized for a period of time not to exceed 12 months. If the construction project or special event is to last more than 12 months, then the city manager and traffic engineer shall initiate an ordinance to regulate such parking spaces during such construction project or special event. The city manager and traffic engineer are hereby authorized to erect appropriate signs delineating such temporary construction or special event zones and parking limits therein.
(Ord. No. 01-83, § 1, 9-4-01)
