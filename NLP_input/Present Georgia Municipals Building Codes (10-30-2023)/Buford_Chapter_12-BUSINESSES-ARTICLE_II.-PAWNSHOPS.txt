Sec. 12-19. - Required records.
Every pawnbroker shall maintain a permanent record book in which shall be entered in legible English at the time of each loan, purchase, or sale:
(1)
The date of the transaction;
(2)
The name of the person conducting the transaction;
(3)
The name, age, and address of the customer; a description of the general appearance of the customer; and the distinctive number from the customer's driver's license or other similar identification card;
(4)
An identification and description of the pledged or purchased goods, including, if reasonably available, the serial, model, or other number, and all identifying marks inscribed thereon;
(5)
The number of the receipt or pawn ticket;
(6)
The price paid or the amount loaned;
(7)
If payment is made by check, the number of the check issued for the purchase price or loan;
(8)
The maturity date of the transaction; and
(9)
The signature of the customer.
(Code 2000, § 22-31; Ord. No. 770-1, § 1)
State Law reference— Pawnbroker records required to be maintained for four years, O.C.G.A. §§ 44-12-132, 44-12-134.
Sec. 12-20. - Inspection of premises by police.
The place of business of every person conducting business coming under the provisions of this article, and any item purchased by him or pledged to him, shall be subject to inspection and examination by members of the police department at any time. Any person purchasing a business license from the city to operate such a business shall thereby grant to the members of the police department permission to enter his premises and place of business and make such inspection or examination without a search warrant.
(Code 2000, § 22-32; Ord. No. 770-1, § 2)
Sec. 12-21. - Age restriction.
No person shall purchase, loan money to, or accept as a pawn or pledge any article from a minor under 18 years of age.
(Code 2000, § 22-33; Ord. No. 770-1, § 3)
