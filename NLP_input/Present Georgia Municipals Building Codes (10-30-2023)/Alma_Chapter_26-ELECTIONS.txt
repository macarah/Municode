Chapter 26 - ELECTIONS[1]

Footnotes:
--- (1) ---
State Law reference— Georgia Election Code, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.

Sec. 26-1. - Registration of electors.
The city shall have and maintain its own system for the registration of electors. The city clerk shall be the chief registrar.
(Code 1969, § 2-71; Code 1981, § 8-1)
Sec. 26-2. - Challenge of registration.
The challenge of listed persons by other electors, shall be as prescribed in O.C.G.A. §§ 21-2-229 and/or 21-2-230.
(Code 1969, § 2-8; Code 1981, § 8-2)
Sec. 26-3. - Election district.
The entire city shall and does comprise a single election district.
(Code 1969, § 2-72; Code 1981, § 8-3)
Sec. 26-4. - Election superintendent.
(a)
Creation of position, appointment. The position of election superintendent is created. The election superintendent shall be an elector in the city and shall be appointed by the council, to serve at the pleasure of the council and until a successor is appointed. In the event of vacancy in such office for any reason, the city manager shall be the election superintendent. The term superintendent or election superintendent when used in this chapter shall have the same meaning as defined in O.C.G.A. § 21-2-2(35)(D).
(b)
Compensation. The election superintendent shall receive compensation for such services, per election or primary. The amount of compensation shall be as established by the city council from time to time.
(c)
Duties and powers. The election superintendent shall have all of the duties and powers prescribed in O.C.G.A. § 21-2-70.1, and such other powers and duties as are fixed from time to time by the council. It shall be the duty of the superintendent to prepare and publish, in the manner provided by the state municipal election code, all notices and advertisements in connection with the conduct of elections; to furnish the proper election officials forms and blanks, including tally and return sheets, paper ballots and other necessary forms, as required by law for the conduct of any such election; and to appoint an election manager and clerks and other poll officers for elections, and fix and pay their compensation for services. All primaries shall be conducted by duly qualified and registered political parties.
(Code 1969, § 2-66; Code 1981, § 8-4)
Sec. 26-5. - Polling place—Designated.
The polling place for all city primaries and elections shall be the city hall.
(Code 1969, §§ 2-6, 2-73; Code 1981, § 8-5)
Sec. 26-6. - Same—Space.
The superintendent of elections, together with the city manager, shall cause such portions of the city hall as they deem appropriate to be converted into a place for casting ballots in conformity with the state municipal election code, section 34A-606.
(Code 1969, § 2-74; Code 1981, § 8-6)
