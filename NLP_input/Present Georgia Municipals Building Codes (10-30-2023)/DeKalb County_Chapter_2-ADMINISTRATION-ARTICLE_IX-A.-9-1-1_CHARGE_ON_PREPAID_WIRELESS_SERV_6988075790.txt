ARTICLE IX-A. - 9-1-1 CHARGE ON PREPAID WIRELESS SERVICE (Effective January 1, 2012)[13]

Editor's note— Ord. No. 11-17, Pt. I, adopted Sept. 27, 2011, added a new Art. IX to Ch. 2. Inasmuch as Art. IX already existed, the new material has been redesignated as Art. IX-A at the county's instruction.

Sec. 2-300. - Prepaid wireless transactions charge.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2, there is hereby imposed a prepaid wireless 9-1-1 charge, as defined by O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(a)(4), upon every prepaid wireless retail transaction occurring within the jurisdiction of the public safety answering point in the amount of seventy-five cents ($0.75).
(Ord. No. 11-17, Pt. I, 9-27-11)
Sec. 2-301. - Collection of 9-1-1 charge on prepaid wireless transactions.
Prepaid wireless 9-1-1 charges collected by sellers shall be remitted to the Commissioner of the Georgia Department of Revenue at the times and in the manner provided by O.C.G.A. § 48-8-1 et seq. with respect to the sales and use tax imposed on prepaid wireless calling service.
(Ord. No. 11-17, Pt. I, 9-27-11)
Sec. 2-302. - Administrative provisions.
The clerk of the board of commissioners and chief executive officer is hereby directed to file with the Commissioner of the Georgia Department of Revenue a certified copy of this ordinance and amendments thereto, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(j)(1), within ten (10) days of approval of this article.
(Ord. No. 11-17, Pt. I, 9-27-11)
Sec. 2-303. - Depositing of funds; use of funds.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(j)(5), funds received by the county from charges imposed by this ordinance shall be deposited in the emergency telephone system fund maintained by the county pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134, and kept separate from general revenue of the jurisdiction; all such funds shall be used exclusively for the purposes authorized by O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134(f).
(Ord. No. 11-17, Pt. I, 9-27-11)
