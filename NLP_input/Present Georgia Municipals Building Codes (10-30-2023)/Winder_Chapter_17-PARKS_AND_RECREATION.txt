Sec. 17-16. - Created.
The Winder-Barrow County Parks and Recreation Commission, referred to in this chapter as the "commission," is hereby created and established by and between the City of Winder, Georgia, hereinafter referred to as the "city," and Barrow County, Georgia, hereinafter referred to as the "county," as authorized by Georgia Laws 1946, pp. 152, 154; Georgia Laws 1964, pp. 319, 320; Georgia Laws 1971, pp. 262, 263 (Ga. Code Ann., 36-64-1 et seq.); Georgia Laws 1972, p. 1552, ratified November 7, 1972, and said commission shall be organized and empowered as set out in this article.
(Code 1968, § 2-11; Ord. of 3-28-75, § 1)
State Law reference— Organization of recreation board, Ga. Code Ann., § 36-64-5; general functions of recreation board, § 36-64-3.
Sec. 17-17. - Purpose.
The purpose of the commission is to carry on a recreation program for the city and the county without duplication of services and expense, and with fairness and equity to both political subdivisions.
(Ord. of 3-28-75, § 3)
Sec. 17-18. - General responsibility.
The commission shall formulate, implement, operate, administer and maintain its recreation program to the best interest of all Barrow County citizens, expending such sums as are appropriated for its purposes by the city and county governing bodies.
Sec. 17-19. - Financial participation; retention of title to land and capital improvements; contribution by city.
(a)
The city and the county shall, and do respectively, retain title to all their respective lands and capital improvements connected with, and use in and by, the recreation program, and the commission shall protect and maintain said properties. Further capital investment shall be made with respect to such property by the commission.
(b)
The financial participation of the city and the county in the recreation program shall begin on the start-up contribution by the city of twenty thousand dollars ($20,000.00) for the period beginning April 1, 1975 and ending December 31, 1975. Thereafter and annually, the city agrees to contribute the sums designated as follows:
1976 fifteen thousand dollars ($15,000.00).
1977 fifteen thousand dollars ($15,000.00).
1978 ten thousand dollars ($10,000.00).
After the year 1979, the city shall determine the amounts of contributions, if any, to be made.
(Ord. of 3-28-75, § 7)
Sec. 17-20. - Composition; terms.
The commission shall consist of eleven (11) appointed members, each (except the original appointees) having terms of four (4) years, commencing on April 1. The city, by and through its mayor and council, shall appoint five (5) members and the board of commissioners shall appoint six (6) members. All terms shall be for four (4) years.
(Ord. of 3-28-75, § 2; Ord. (W-1-95) of 3-7-95)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, Ga. Code Ann., § 36-64-5.
Sec. 17-21. - Initial appointments.
(a)
In order that the terms of members of the commission shall be staggered, the original appointments shall be made by the city and the county as follows:
(1)
The city, by and through its mayor and council, shall appoint two (2) members for a two (2) year term; and two (2) members for a four (4) year term.
(2)
The board of commissioners of Barrow County, by and through its chairman and commissioners, shall appoint three (3) members for a two (2) year term and two (2) members for a four (4) year term, each said county appointment to begin April 1, 1975.
(b)
The anniversary date of all said appointments shall be on April first of each year of an appointment.
(Ord. of 3-28-75, § 2)
Sec. 17-22. - Compensation; expenses.
All members of the commission shall serve without compensation but may be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in connection with their official duties.
(Ord. of 3-28-75, § 2)
Sec. 17-23. - Rules.
The commission shall make its own rules of procedure, consistent with the law of the state and with the authority granted it by this article, the resolution of the county and the agreement between the city and the county hereinafter referred to.
(Ord. of 3-28-75, § 4)
Sec. 17-24. - Organization.
(a)
The commission shall elect its chairman, and such other officers as are necessary, from among its appointed members. The terms of the chairman and such other officers shall be one year, or until their successors are duly elected and qualified.
(b)
The commission shall appoint a secretary, who may be an officer or employee of the city or of the county, or of the commission. The secretary shall keep minutes of the meetings and shall perform such other functions as may be required of him.
(Code 1968, § 2-11; Ord. of 3-28-75, § 4)
Sec. 17-25. - General powers and duties.
The commission shall be vested, except as restricted in this article, with all powers and duties as granted under O.C.G.A. § 36-34-1 et seq., as it now exists, or as it may hereafter be amended.
(Ord. of 3-28-75, § 5)
Sec. 17-26. - Director, other employees; duties.
(a)
The commission shall appoint a recreation director and such other staff and employees as it may deem necessary to properly and adequately carry on the recreation program, but the salaries thereof shall be within and consistent with the budget established and approved.
(b)
The duties of said director shall be to plan, organize, direct and control a countywide recreation program, pursuant to the policy established by the commission and within the budget submitted to and approved by the governing bodies of the city and the county. He shall prepare and present to the commission a suggested budget showing anticipated revenue and required expenditures.
(Ord. of 3-28-75, § 6)
Sec. 17-27. - Facilities generally.
The commission shall provide for expansion of facilities open to all citizens of the city and of the county, but shall consistently maintain the existing facilities of the city, in at least its present good and serviceable condition.
(Ord. of 3-28-75, § 3)
Sec. 17-28. - Budget.
The commission shall, each year, timely submit to the governing body of the county a budget for the administration, operation and maintenance of the recreation program and showing expected expenditures in each of the recreation areas.
(Ord. of 3-28-75, § 5)
Sec. 17-29. - Fees, rentals.
The commission is empowered to establish, revise and collect fees and rentals for the use of recreation areas and to impose conditions and set requirements governing such use. Accurate records of such fees shall be always maintained and open to inspection by the city and county governing bodies. Anticipated fees shall be accounted for in the annual budget.
(Ord. of 3-28-75, § 5)
Sec. 17-30. - Reports, recommendations.
The commission shall periodically report to the city and county governing bodies the condition of the respective lands and capital improvements thereof and shall immediately notify such bodies of any damage thereto or condition requiring repair and make recommendations concerning such condition and/or required repairs.
(Ord. of 3-28-75, § 5)
Sec. 17-31. - Work by city.
Nothing in this article shall prevent the commission from contracting with the city to perform work in the recreation areas, but performance by the city shall be the subject of separate charge and shall not be considered participation by the city.
(Ord. of 3-28-75, § 5)
Sec. 17-32. - Contract.
The mayor and the secretary of the city council are authorized to enter into and sign on behalf of the city a contract with the county to cooperate in the establishment of the Winder-Barrow County Parks and Recreation Commission and a county-wide recreation program, to provide for the financial participation of the city in said program, to provide for assurance that the integrity of the present city recreation system shall be maintained and to contain such other terms as may be necessary to carry out the intentions of the parties.
(Ord. of 3-28-75, § 8)
Sec. 17-33. - Removal; vacancies.
(a)
The mayor and council shall have the authority to remove a city-appointed member of the commission.
(b)
Any vacancy in membership shall be filled for the unexpired term by the governing body whose appointee vacates the position.
(Ord. of 3-28-75, § 2)
