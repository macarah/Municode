ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 6-1. - Bird sanctuary.
The city is hereby declared a bird sanctuary and no person shall intentionally injure or kill any wild bird within the city with the exception of starlings or other nuisance birds.
(Code 1976, § 11-1009; Code 1985, § 4-1)
Sec. 6-2. - Animal and fowl enclosures; size, fencing.
No person shall keep or maintain within the city, any poultry, fowl, rabbits, horses, mules, ponies, cows or cattle, unless the same are kept or maintained on property having a total area of not less than 2½ acres bordered on all sides by a fence not less than five feet in height and which fence at any point shall be at least 50 feet distant from any residence of any adjoining property owner.
(Code 1976, § 9-3015; Code 1985, § 4-2; Ord. of 10-3-1977)
Sec. 6-3. - Swine and goats; poultry kept for commercial purposes.
No person shall keep or maintain within the city any swine or goats. In addition, no person shall keep or maintain, within the city, any poultry of any kind for commercial purposes.
(Code 1976, § 9-3014)
Secs. 6-4—6-24. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - DOGS

Sec. 6-25. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Dog means a canine animal of either sex.
Dog under control means a dog controlled by a leash, at heel, or beside a competent person and obedient to that person's commands, or within a vehicle being driven or parked on the streets, or within the property limits of the owner of the dog.
(Code 1976, § 9-3012; Code 1985, § 4-20)
Sec. 6-26. - State law adopted; enforcement.
The Responsible Dog Ownership Law, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq., and Cobb County Code, Chapter 10, Animals, are adopted by reference as though fully set forth in this section, and the Cobb County animal control officer is as designated the city animal control officer, authorized to enforce this chapter and city animal regulations generally.
(Code 1985, § 4-32; Res. of 8-21-1989)
Sec. 6-27. - Kennel locations restricted.
No person shall keep or maintain, within the corporate limits of the city, any kennel for the boarding, confinement, breeding or keeping of any dogs, except in zones where breeding and boarding kennels are specifically permitted under city zoning regulations. This section shall not prevent a licensed veterinarian from establishing or maintaining a veterinary clinic within the city for the treatment of dogs and other animals if in compliance with city licensing and zoning regulations.
(Code 1976, § 9-3017; Code 1985, § 4-22)
Sec. 6-28. - Conditions for keeping dogs in the city.
(a)
No dogs may be kept or maintained within the city, except on terms and conditions and to the extent provided in this article.
(b)
Each family unit within the city, the same being all the members of a family living in one residential unit, shall be permitted to maintain and keep permanently up to a limit of four dogs. The offspring of such dogs may be kept and maintained for a period of 90 days after their birth and not thereafter in excess of the limit herein provided.
(c)
When any dogs are confined and kept in a pen or enclosure at any time, it shall be thoroughly cleaned at least once a week and kept free from filth and stench at all times.
(Code 1976, § 9-3016; Code 1985, § 4-21)
Sec. 6-29. - Dog control on city property.
(a)
The owners or persons in possession or control of guard dogs, sentry dogs or watch dogs in the city shall immediately clean up any waste, excrement or damage left by or caused by such dog on any city street, sidewalk, parking area, park or other city property. Any dog kept at a place of business is hereby declared to be a sentry dog.
(b)
Any person violating this section shall be liable for a fine of $50.00 per occurrence.
(Code 1985, § 15-13; Ord. of 6-15-1984)
Sec. 6-30. - Tags and collar required; removal.
(a)
It shall be the duty of each dog owner to provide a collar, to which shall be attached an owner identification tag and the vaccination tag, which collar shall be worn by the dog as provided herein, except when such dog is under immediate control of the owner or other person having custody of the dog or when it is participating in a dog show or exhibition.
(b)
It is unlawful for any person to remove the collar without the permission of the owner or lawful custodian.
(Code 1976, §§ 9-3005, 9-3009; Code 1985, § 4-23)
State Law reference— Removal of collars or identifying items or marks, O.C.G.A. § 16-9-71.
Sec. 6-31. - Identification tag required.
Unless exempted herein, every person owning or having custody of a dog shall provide therefor an identification tag showing the name and address of the owner of the dog.
(Code 1976, § 9-3002; Code 1985, § 4-24)
Sec. 6-32. - Vaccination; tag.
It is unlawful for any owner or lawful custodian of a dog to fail to have a valid vaccination tag for such dog as required by the laws of the state. In addition, it is unlawful for any person to attach a vaccination tag to any dog for which it was not issued, or to remove a vaccination tag from any dog without the consent of its owner or lawful custodian. No person shall vaccinate a dog against rabies who is not licensed to practice veterinary medicine in the state.
(Code 1976, §§ 9-3002, 9-3009, 9-3010; Code 1985, § 4-25)
State Law reference— Control of rabies, O.C.G.A. § 31-19-1 et seq.
Sec. 6-33. - Vicious dogs.
It is unlawful for any person to cause, permit or be responsible for the presence of any vicious dog on the streets of the city or in any other public place therein, at any time, unless, and in addition to the other requirements of this article, such dog shall be securely muzzled to effectively prevent it from biting any person or other animal.
(Code 1976, § 9-3006; Code 1985, § 4-27)
Sec. 6-34. - Dogs at large prohibited; impoundment.
(a)
It is unlawful for the owner or other custodian having a dog in possession and control, to permit such dog to be out of control and unattended off the premises of such owner or other custodian, or upon public property, or upon the property of another person without permission of the owner or person in possession thereof.
(b)
All dogs found running at large in violation of this article shall be immediately impounded by the county or the city, or their duly authorized agents. In the event any authorized officer or employee or authorized agent sees a dog which is apparently out of control and unattended, such officer or employee or agent may go upon any property to determine if this article has been violated.
(c)
In the event that the ownership or lawful custodian of a dog is not known, and such dog is upon the streets, alleys, sidewalks, school grounds, public places, or premises of another, any law enforcement officer or agent or employee duly authorized by the county or city shall be authorized to take possession of such dog and impound it in accordance with the rules and regulations provided or adopted in this chapter for the detention, control and disposition of dogs impounded under this article.
(Code 1976, §§ 9-3001, 9-3003, 9-3004; Code 1985, §§ 4-26, 4-28, 4-30)
Sec. 6-35. - Dog bite procedure.
(a)
It is unlawful for any person having knowledge that any person has been bitten by a dog to refuse to notify promptly one or more of the officers, employees or agents mentioned in this section.
(b)
It is unlawful for the owner of the dog to refuse or fail to comply with the written recommendations made by the officer, employee or agent in any particular case involving a biting dog.
(c)
Notwithstanding any other provision of this article, whenever any authorized public officer or employee or agent shall receive information that any person has been bitten by a dog, such officer, employee or agent shall be required to have the dog confined for a period of ten days or whatever period is required by the adopted county animal regulations.
(d)
Any dog confined under this section shall be housed at the county dog pound or at a licensed veterinarian if, in the discretion of the pound master, the latter would be necessary to protect the pound staff and other dogs housed at the pound. Any expense incurred in the handling of any dog, under this section, shall be borne by the owner of the dog.
(Code 1976, § 9-3013; Code 1985, § 4-29; Ord. of 6-1-1987)
Sec. 6-36. - Disposition of impounded dogs; fees.
(a)
Any dog seized or impounded under any provision of this article shall be detained at the county dog pound. The officers, agents, and employees of the county shall notify the owner of such dog by mail, if known or can be reasonably ascertained, that the dog has been impounded. The owner of any dog impounded may, within ten days after the mailing of the notice, reclaim the dog upon the payment of the fees charged by the county for impounding, boarding, rabies inoculation or tagging, and any other expenses due to the county for the impoundment and boarding of the dog.
(b)
In the event the dog is not claimed in the time provided herein, the county may offer for the adoption by any person, any dog unredeemed or unclaimed by the owner upon payment of all costs due to the county for impoundment, boarding and other services provided for the dog.
(c)
If the dog is not claimed by the owner within the time prescribed herein, then the county shall be authorized to dispose of the dog in as humane and painless a manner as possible.
(d)
All dogs, if not claimed or adopted as provided, may be donated to any public or private institution for disposition.
(Code 1976, § 9-3007; Code 1985, § 4-31)
