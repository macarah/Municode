Sec. 12-30. - Financial institution annual business license tax.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93 there is hereby levied an annual business license tax upon depository financial institutions having an office located within the city at a rate of 0.25 percent of the state gross receipts, as defined and allocated in O.C.G.A. §§ 48-6-93 and 48-6-95, of the depository financial institutions. Notwithstanding any other provisions of this section, the minimum annual amount of the business license tax due from any depository financial institution shall be one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) or an amount as may be established by state statute in the future.
(b)
The annual business license tax is due and payable on April 1 of each year, and if not paid by June 1, shall be subject to penalties and interest for delinquency.
(c)
Every depository financial institution shall file a return of its gross receipts with the city permits and licenses coordinator by March 1 of the year following the year in which such gross receipts are measured. The return shall provide the information necessary to determine the portion of the taxpayer's state gross receipts to be allocated to each taxing jurisdiction in which the institution has on office as of December 31 of the year in which gross receipts are measured. A depository financial institution's gross receipts shall be allocated among each taxing jurisdiction pursuant to the method established at O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.
(d)
Should the annual business license tax levied herein remain due and unpaid after June 1 of the year for which the tax is owed, the city shall assess a penalty of ten (10) percent of the taxes due and payable.
(e)
Interest shall accrue on all unpaid taxes levied and assessed under this section from the date the same shall be due and payable at the rate of one and one-half percent per month on the unpaid balance.
(Ord. No. 2008-013, § 1, 7-21-08; Ord. No. 2017-010 , § 1, 5-1-17)
