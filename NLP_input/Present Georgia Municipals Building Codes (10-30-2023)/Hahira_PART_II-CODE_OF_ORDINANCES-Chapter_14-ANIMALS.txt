Sec. 14-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Disposal means the removal of feces by means of a bag, scoop or other device and an eventual disposal in a trash receptacle, by burying or by other means of lawful disposal.
Wild or exotic animal means any animal of a species prohibited by Title 50, Code of Federal Regulations, or otherwise controlled by the State of Georgia. It shall include any animal which is wild, fierce, dangerous, noxious or naturally inclined to do harm. The term "wild animals," however domesticated, shall also include but not be limited to:
(1)
Bears (Ursidea): All bears, including grizzly bears, brown bears, black bears, etc.
(2)
Cat family (Felidea): All except commonly accepted domestic cats, including lions, pumas, panthers, mountain lions, leopards, jaguars, ocelots, margays, tigers, wild cats, etc.
(3)
Crocodillians: All alligators, caimans, crocodiles, gavials, etc.
(4)
Dog family (Canidea): All except domesticated dogs, including wolf, fox, coyote, dingo, wolf hybrids, etc.
(5)
Porcupine (Erethizontidae): All porcupines.
(6)
Raccoons (Procynniddae):
a.
All raccoons and civets.
b.
Skunks.
(7)
Snakes: All venomous and constricting snakes.
(8)
Venomous fish and piranha.
(9)
Venomous lizards.
(10)
Weasels (Mustelidea): All, including weasels, martens, mink, wolverine, ferrets, badgers, otters, ermine, mongoose, etc.
(Code 2006, § 14-1; Ord. No. 08-05, 3-6-2008; Ord. No. 09-03, 6-4-2009)
Sec. 14-2. - Dangerous, vicious, wild or exotic animals.
(a)
Except as provided in section 14-3, it shall be unlawful for any owner to have any dangerous, vicious, wild or exotic animal of any kind within the city.
(b)
The police officers of the city shall be authorized to use such force as is necessary to prevent any such dangerous or vicious animal from causing harm to any person or property.
(c)
Any unregistered dangerous, vicious, wild or exotic animal found within the city limits shall result in a fine to the owner of $250.00 upon discovery of such animal. Each additional day during which the animal remains in the city shall constitute a separate violation of this article.
State Law reference— Injuries or damage done by animal, O.C.G.A. § 51-2-6 et seq.
(Code 2006, § 14-2; Ord. No. 08-05, 3-6-2008)
Sec. 14-3. - Pre-existing ownership or possession of exotic animals.
(a)
Any owner having pre-existing ownership or possession of any exotic animal shall be permitted to continue to possess said animals within the city provided that within 30 calendar days of the effective date of the ordinance from which this article is derived, the owner registers said animals as provided in section 14-4.
(b)
Failure to commence the permit/registration process for a pre-existing exotic animal within 30 calendar days of the effective date of the ordinance from which this article is derived shall be governed by section 14-2.
(Code 2006, § 14-3; Ord. No. 08-05, 3-6-2008)
Sec. 14-4. - Permit/registration process for pre-existing exotic animals.
(a)
Payment of the nonrefundable permit fee as provided in the city fee schedule per exotic animal shall constitute the beginning of the permit/registration process. Any other cost incurred during this permit/registration process shall be the responsibility of the owner or possessor of the exotic animal. All permit fees shall be waived for owners or possessors of pre-existing exotic animals, provided the owner or possessor:
(1)
Commences the permit/registration process within 30 calendar days of the effective date of this article; and
(2)
Completes the list of restrictions associated with the permit/registration process no later than 90 days from the beginning of the permit/registration process, unless extended by the police chief.
(b)
Applicants shall submit a permit application on a form to be provided by the city police department. Applicants beginning the permit/registration process for an exotic animal shall be given a list of restrictions that must be in place before a permit will be issued.
(c)
Restrictions for the issuance of a permit for an exotic animal shall include, but not be limited to the following:
(1)
Proof of insurance coverage with a minimum of $300,000.00 per claim to compensate any person for personal injury or property damage caused by the exotic animal, or in lieu of insurance, a surety bond posted with the city manager in the same amount, conditioned upon payment of such damages.
(2)
At the direction of City of Hahira police chief, installation of permanent signage at all points of entry to the property in question to identify the presence of the exotic animal to any law enforcement or emergency personnel having valid reason to enter the property.
(3)
Required signage shall consist of a minimum size of three square feet and shall at a minimum identify the following in reflective letters of a contrasting color as to be easily read on the background of the sign:
a.
The word "WARNING" in all capitals at least four inches high;
b.
The common name of the exotic animal;
c.
The location on the property of the exotic animal; and
d.
The potential danger posed by the exotic animal (biting, clawing, venom, constriction, kicking, etc.).
(4)
Implantation by a city-approved veterinarian of a microchip under the skin of the exotic animal to aid in identification, or approval by a city-approved veterinarian of a previously implanted microchip. The veterinarian can be chosen by the owner or possessor of the exotic animal if so approved by the city police chief, or can be referred to the owner or possessor by the police chief upon request.
(5)
Obtaining from a city approved veterinarian a health certificate detailing the health and condition of the exotic animal.
(6)
Written emergency response plan, approved by the police chief, in the event of any escape of the exotic animal, including a paragraph detailing owner or possessor's understanding and agreement that if the escaped exotic animal poses any immediate danger whatsoever to life, limb, or property (other than property of the exotic animal owner), then it may be destroyed at that time by law enforcement officers, emergency responders, or any citizen having the means to do so. This emergency response plan shall be signed by the owner and notarized.
(7)
All exotic animals shall be housed in facilities designed with the characteristics and potential dangers of the particular exotic animal in mind.
(d)
Issuance of a permit.
(1)
When the exotic animal owner or possessor has completed the list of restrictions, but no later than 90 days from the beginning of the permit/registration process, unless extended by the police chief, the owner shall contact and arrange for an initial inspection by police chief or his designated representative, which shall not unreasonably delay this initial inspection.
(2)
When the police chief or his representative has conducted his initial inspection and found the list of restrictions to have been completed in a satisfactory manner, it shall issue the permit to the owner or possessor of the exotic animal.
(3)
The initial term of this permit shall be one year.
(4)
This permit shall be renewable every year for the lesser of the life of the exotic animal or the length of its residency in the city, upon payment of the permit renewal fee and satisfactory completion of a follow-up inspection by police chief of the facility housing the exotic animal.
(5)
This permit shall be presented for renewal prior to the expiration of the current term; otherwise, the permit is considered expired.
(6)
Any owner or possessor of an exotic animal and an expired permit for that exotic animal shall be in violation of this article, and the owner or possessor shall face the possibility of immediate seizure of the exotic animal and the imposition of a fine of $250.00 per day for each day the exotic animal is in the custody of the city, plus any additional costs to the county that may arise in connection with seizure of the exotic animal.
(e)
A permit holder shall correct within seven calendar days any defects that may arise in connection with that permit or face seizure of the exotic animal and a fine of $250.00 per day for each day the exotic animal is in the custody of the city, plus any additional costs to the city that may arise in connection with seizure of the exotic animal.

Sec. 14-5. - Animal waste.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for the owner or person having charge of any animal to permit, either willfully or by failure to exercise due care, such animal to commit a public nuisance by defecating in any area of the city other than the premises of the owner or person having charge or control of the animal, unless said owner or person having charge takes immediate steps to remove and properly dispose of said feces.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for the owner or person having charge of any animal to take said animal off of the private property of said person without having in the possession of the owner or person having charge of the animal a proper means of disposal for the feces of the animal.
(Ord. No. 09-03, 6-4-2009)

ARTICLE II. - IMPOUNDMENT

Sec. 14-29. - Authorized.
It shall be the duty of every police officer or animal control officer to seize all animals running at-large in violation of the provisions of this article and to impound such animals in the city pound or other suitable place. Upon receiving any such animal, the county poundkeeper shall enter upon the records of the pound (in a book to be kept by him for such purpose) the date of impounding, a description of the animal impounded and a record as to whether or not such animal has been licensed and tagged.
(Code 1985, § 5-4(a); Code 2006, § 14-31)
Sec. 14-30. - Notice to owner.
Not later than five days after the impounding of any animal as provided in section 14-29, the owner shall be notified or, if the owner of the animal is unknown, written notice shall be posted for five days at two or more conspicuous places in the city, which notice shall describe the animal and indicate the place and time of taking.
(Code 1985, § 5-4(b); Code 2006, § 14-32)
Sec. 14-31. - Redemption of animals.
The owners of any animal impounded by the county poundkeeper may redeem the same by paying all the costs, charges and penalties assessed, if any, that have accrued up to the time of making the redemption, and when the same are paid to said poundkeeper, it shall be his duty to release the animal form said pound and deliver it to the owner thereof.
(Code 1985, § 5-4(c); Code 2006, § 14-33)
Sec. 14-32. - Disposition of unclaimed or infected animals.
If at the expiration of five days from the date of notice to the owner or the posting of notice any animal impounded as herein provided shall not have been redeemed either by the owner or by some other person, such animal may be disposed of in a humane manner. Any animal which appears to be suffering from rabies or affected with hydrophobia, mange or any other infectious or dangerous disease shall not be released but may be destroyed as soon as such infection or disease is discovered.
(Code 1985, § 5-4(d); Code 2006, § 14-34)
Secs. 14-33—14-52. - Reserved.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 14-53. - Rabies control tag required.
It shall be unlawful for any owner to keep any dog in the city unless that dog, at all times and at all places, is wearing a tag indicating that the dog has been inoculated against rabies as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 31-19-6, control of rabies, and the rabies control rules and regulations of the county board of health (Rule II).
(Code 1985, § 5-21; Code 2006, § 14-71)
Sec. 14-54. - Annoyance or nuisance; at-large dogs.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep or harbor a dog within the city which, by loud and frequent or habitual barking, yelping or howling, or by constant threat of attacking and biting persons passing to and fro upon the streets, shall cause continual annoyance to the neighborhood.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for the owner of a dog to permit such dog to run at-large.
(Code 1985, § 5-22(c), (d); Code 2006, § 14-72)
Sec. 14-55. - Rabies check.
(a)
In every case where a dog actually bites a person in the city, the owner of such dog shall immediately chain or confine it so it shall not have access to persons or animals, and shall give immediate notice to the director of the county health department, in order that said dog may be inspected for rabies by the director or his authorized representative, pursuant to statutes of the state on that subject and the rules of the county board of health.
(b)
In all such cases, and in all cases where evidence of rabies appears, the director or his authorized representative may, in his discretion, take charge of such dog for the purpose of full inspection as required by statutes of the state and the rules of the county board of health.
(Code 1985, § 5-23; Code 2006, § 14-73)
State Law reference— Control of rabies, O.C.G.A. § 31-19-1 et seq.; county boards of health, O.C.G.A. § 31-3-1 et seq.; bites by animals, O.C.G.A. § 31-19-4.
Sec. 14-56. - Female dogs in heat.
It shall be unlawful for any owner to allow a female dog, when in breeding season, to go upon or have access to the streets of the city.
(Code 1985, § 5-24; Code 2006, § 14-74)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-6.
Sec. 14-57. - Impounding.
(a)
Any dog not wearing a license tag as prescribed in this article; any vicious dog not confined, chained or muzzled as herein provided; any dog running at-large; any female dog while in breeding season running or having access to the public streets; any dog not wearing the rabies inoculation tag required by the state statutes; and any dog which causes annoyance in accordance with stipulations prescribed in section 14-54(a) shall be subject to be taken up by any police officer of the city, the director of the county health department or his authorized representative or an authorized representative of the city who shall have the dog confined in the dog pound.
(b)
The city shall operate either independently or in cooperation with others; or shall contract with the county health department for the operation of suitable place to be known as the dog pound for the impounding of dogs picked up under the terms of this article.
(Code 1985, § 5-25; Code 2006, § 14-75)
State Law reference— Municipal contracts for government services and facilities, O.C.G.A. § 36-34-2.
Sec. 14-58. - Destruction of animal in attempt to impound.
(a)
In the event any dog subject to being taken up pursuant to the provisions of this article cannot be captured without actual danger to the officer attempting capture, any member of the police department, rabies control officer or authorized representative is authorized and directed to shoot such dog when the shooting can be done without endangering persons or other property.
(b)
Nothing herein shall be construed as requiring any police officer to attempt to capture a dog plainly showing the existence of rabies, but such a dog may be shot by any police officer, rabies control inspector or his authorized representative, on sight, using due care not to injure persons or other property.
(Code 1985, § 5-26; Code 2006, § 14-76)
State Law reference— Authorized killing of dogs, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-5.
Sec. 14-59. - Fees; fines.
(a)
The keeper of the dog pound shall not release any dog impounded until the owner has paid all pound fees, all fines assessed by the recorder of the city for violations of this article for which such dog was impounded and until the owner has secured a license tag and a rabies tag as provided in this article.
(b)
Any dog impounded pursuant to this article which is not suspected of rabies may be reclaimed from the pound upon the payment to the city's authorized representative such fees as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1985, § 5-27; Code 2006, § 14-77)

DIVISION 2. - DANGEROUS DOGS

Sec. 14-77. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Animal control officer means the officer the city contracts from the county animal control office to enforce and house any dogs found to be at-large, or in violation of city and/or state laws.
Dangerous dog means any dog that, according to the records of an appropriate authority:
(1)
Inflicts a severe injury on a human being without provocation on public or private property any time after adoption of the ordinance from which this division is derived; or
(2)
Aggressively bites, attacks, or endangers the safety of humans without provocation after the dog has been classified as a potentially dangerous dog and after the owner has been notified of such classification.
Owner means any natural person or any legal entity, including, but not limited to, a corporation, partnership, firm, or trust owning, possessing, harboring, keeping, or having custody or control of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog within the city.
Potentially dangerous dog means any dog that without provocation bites a human being on public or private property at any time after adoption of the ordinance from which this division is derived.
Proper enclosure means an enclosure for keeping a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog while on the owners property securely confined indoors or in a securely enclosed and locked pen, fence, or structure suitable to prevent the entry of young children and designed to prevent the dog from escaping. Any such pen or structure shall have secure sides and a secure top, and, if the dog is enclosed within a fence, all sides of the fence shall be of sufficient height and the bottom of the fence shall be constructed or secured in such a manner as to prevent the dog's escape either from over or from under the fence. Any such enclosure shall also provide protection from the elements for the dog.
Records of an appropriate authority means records of any state, county, or municipal law enforcement agency; records of any county or municipal animal control agency; records of any county board of health; records of any federal, state, or local court; or records of an animal control officer provided for in this article.
Severe injury means any physical injury that results in broken bones or disfiguring lacerations requiring multiple sutures or cosmetic surgery or a physical injury that results in death.
(Code 2006, § 14-101)
Sec. 14-78. - Investigation of report of dangerous dog.
(a)
Upon receiving a report that a dog has acted in a dangerous, or potentially dangerous manner, the police department shall investigate the allegations and make a determination as to the truthfulness of said allegations. After concluding the investigation, the police department shall:
(1)
Decide the classification of the dog as dangerous or potentially dangerous is not required;
(2)
Classify the dog as potentially dangerous; or
(3)
Classify the dog as dangerous.
(b)
In the event that the police department classifies the dog under investigation as "dangerous" or "potentially dangerous," the police department must notify the owners by certified mail. The notice shall include a summary of the reason for which the animal has been classified and provide the owners with a form to request a hearing to contest the classification within 15 days of the notice date. If no hearing is requested, the dog's classification goes into effect on the 15th day. An owner who requests a hearing shall be provided a hearing before the council within 30 days. The owners must be notified of the hearing date, time, and location by certified mail. The hearing shall be conducted such that owners have the opportunity to present their testimony, cross examine witnesses, and present evidence. The council shall receive such other evidence and hear such other testimony as is reasonably necessary to make a determination to either sustain, returned to its owner upon the owner's compliance with the provisions of this article and upon the payment of the costs to the city for confiscating and boarding the dog. In the event the owner has not complied with the provisions of this article within four days of the date the dog was confiscated, said dog shall be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner. The costs for confiscating, boarding, and disposal of said dog shall remain the responsibility of the owner of the dog.
(Code 2006, § 14-102)
Sec. 14-79. - Limits of city liability.
It is the intent of this article that the owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall be solely liable for any injury to or death of a person caused by such dog. Under no circumstances shall the city or any of its employees or officials which enforce or fail to enforce the provisions of this article be held liable for any damages to any person who suffers an injury inflicted by a dog that has been identified as being a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog, or by a dog that has been reported to the proper authorities as being a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog, or by a dog that a[an] animal control officer has failed to identify as a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog, or by a dog which has been identified as being a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog but has not been kept or restrained in the manner described in this article, or by a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog whose owner has not maintained insurance coverage or a surety bond as required in this article.
(Code 2006, § 14-103)
Sec. 14-80. - Violations of this division.
(a)
The owner of a dangerous dog who violates this article or whose dog is subject to confiscation under this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor. In addition to any confinement that might be imposed for a conviction under this article, for the second conviction a fine of not less than $250.00 shall be imposed and for a third or subsequent conviction a fine of not less than $500.00 shall be imposed.
(b)
The owner of a potentially dangerous dog who violates this article or whose potentially dangerous dog is subject to confiscation under this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor. In addition to any confinement that might be imposed for a conviction under this article, for a second conviction a fine of not less than $150.00 shall be imposed and for a third or subsequent conviction a fine of not less than $300.00 shall be imposed.
(c)
In addition to the penalties for violations as set out in subsections (a) and (b) of this section, the dangerous dog involved shall be immediately confiscated by the animal control officer or by a law enforcement officer and placed in quarantine for the property length of time as determined by the animal control officer, and thereafter, the dangerous dog shall be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner.
(d)
No owner of a dangerous dog shall be held liable under this division for injuries inflicted by said owners dog to any human being while both the dog and victim are on the dog owner's property.
(e)
A dog that inflicts an injury upon a person when the dog is being used by a law enforcement officer's official duties shall not be a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog within the meaning of this article. A dog shall not be a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog within the meaning of this article a person sustained if the injury inflicted by the dog who, at the time, was committing a willful trespass or other tort or was tormenting, abusing, or assaulting the dog or had in the past been observed or reported to have tormented, abused, or assaulted the dog or was committing or attempting to commit a crime.
(Code 2006, § 14-104)
Sec. 14-81. - Requirements for possessing dangerous or potentially dangerous dog.
(a)
It is unlawful for an owner to have or possess within the city a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog without a certificate of registration issued in accordance with the provisions of this section. This certificate shall be in addition to any standard dog license and fees or other requirements of this article.
(b)
Subject to the additional requirements of subsection (c) of this section for dangerous dogs, the council shall issue a certificate of registration to the owner of such dog if the owner presents to the council otherwise or rinds sufficient evidence of:
(1)
A proper enclosure to confine the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog; and
(2)
A surety bond in the amount of $250,000.00 or more issued by the insurer authorized to transact business in this state insuring the owner of the dangerous dog against liability for any personal injuries inflicted by the dangerous dog.
(c)
In addition to the requirements of subsection (b) of this section, the owner of a dangerous dog shall present to the dog control officer evidence of:
(1)
A policy of insurance in the amount of at least $250,000.00 issued by an insurer authorized to transact business in this state insuring the owner of the dangerous dog against liability for any personal injuries inflicted by the dangerous dog; or
(2)
A surety bond in the amount of $250,000.00 or more issued by a surety company authorized to transact business in this state payable to any person injured by the dangerous dog.
(d)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall notify the police department within 24 hours if the dog is on the loose, is unconfined, has attacked a human, has died or has been sold or donated. If the dog has been sold or donated, the owner shall also provide the animal control officer and city police chief with the name, address and telephone number of the new owner of the dog.
(e)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall notify the police chief if the owner is moving from the city. The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog who moves from this city to another jurisdiction within the state shall register the dangerous dog and potentially dangerous dog in the new jurisdiction within ten days after becoming a resident.
(f)
Issuance of a certificate of registration or the renewal of a certificate of registration by the city does not warrant or guarantee that the requirements specified in subsections (b) and (c) of this section are maintained by the owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog on a continuous basis following the date of the issuance of the initial certificate of registration or following the date of any annual renewal of such certificate.
(g)
Any police officer is authorized to make whatever inquiry is deemed necessary to ensure compliance with the provisions of this document.
(h)
The city does hereby levy and charge against the owner of any dangerous dog for the purposes of registration pursuant to this document, and in addition to any other license fees required generally for dogs within the city, the sum set forth in the schedule of fees and charges as a fee to register the dog as a dangerous dog pursuant to the ordinances of the city. All certificates of registration shall be renewed on an annual basis. At the time of any renewal, the police department shall require evidence from the owner or make such investigation as may be necessary to verify that the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog is continuing to be confined in a proper enclosure and that the owner is continuing to comply with all of the provisions of this article which might apply to this situation.
(Code 2006, § 14-105)
