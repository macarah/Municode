Subdivision II. - Motions

Sec. 2-76. - General requirements.
The councilmembers can take action only when a motion to take such action is made and passed by the council. The passage of all ordinances must be accomplished by motion.
(Code 1984, § 1-1-3(a))
Sec. 2-77. - Procedure.
(a)
Before a motion can be discussed or voted upon by the councilmembers, it must be presented to the council by a member of the council. The presentment is made by merely stating the motion.
(b)
Immediately after the motion is presented to the council, the mayor shall ask for a councilmember to second the motion. If the motion is not seconded, it shall fail for lack of a second and cannot be remade until the next meeting of the council, except as otherwise provided herein.
(c)
If the motion is seconded, the mayor shall immediately ask for discussion of the motion. Discussion of the motion shall be limited to the councilmembers and shall relate solely to the motion on hand; however, any councilmember may question a member of the audience or staff provided such question shall pertain strictly to the motion on hand.
(d)
Upon the conclusion of discussion, the mayor shall cause the motion to be restated and shall then call the question.
(e)
A councilmember is not permitted to speak against his own motion. However, if the councilmember changes his mind after hearing the motion discussed, the councilmember may vote against it. The person who seconds the motion can speak against the motion because a second means "Let's discuss it," not "I agree."
(Code 1984, § 1-1-3(b))
Sec. 2-78. - Amendment.
(a)
An amendment may be suggested by any member of the council. The amendment shall be suggested only during the discussion of the original motion.
(b)
A motion can be amended only with the consent of the councilmember making the motion. If the councilmember who seconded the original motion disagrees with the amendment, he shall be allowed to withdraw his second. When a second is withdrawn, the mayor shall immediately request a new second.
(c)
After a motion is amended, the mayor shall call for discussion and proceed with the consideration of the motion in the manner provided in section 2-77.
(Code 1984, § 1-1-3(c))
Sec. 2-79. - Withdrawal.
(a)
Any motion may be withdrawn at the discretion of the councilmember making the motion.
(b)
Motions may be withdrawn at any time prior to but not during nor after the restating of the motion by the mayor.
(Code 1984, § 1-1-3(d))
Sec. 2-80. - Tabling.
(a)
During the discussion of a motion, the motion may be tabled for further discussion by the council upon the motion of any councilmember other than the councilmember who made the original motion.
(b)
The motion to table shall specify the time and place for the continued discussion.
(c)
Discussion of the original motion shall immediately cease when a motion to table is made.
(d)
A motion to table shall not require a second but shall require discussion, restating the motion and a call of the question.
(Code 1984, § 1-1-3(e))
Sec. 2-81. - Reconsideration.
(a)
A motion, which has been passed or defeated, or which fails for lack of a second, may be reconsidered by the councilmembers during the same meeting provided the council passes a motion for reconsideration. Otherwise, the original motion may not be presented to the council again until its next meeting.
(b)
A motion for reconsideration can only be made by a councilmember who voted against the original motion, if defeated, or in favor of the original motion, if passed. Otherwise, any councilmember may make a motion for reconsideration.
(Code 1984, § 1-1-3(f))
Sec. 2-82. - Vote.
(a)
Councilmember in favor of a motion shall say "aye" while holding his right hand.
(b)
A councilmember against a motion shall say "nay" while holding his right hand.
(c)
A councilmember present but refusing to vote shall be recorded as abstaining.
(d)
All votes shall be by simple majority unless as otherwise required by ordinance or state law.
(e)
A unanimous passing vote shall mean a vote in which all members of the council present vote in favor of the motion.
(f)
The preceding procedures apply to all votes taken by the mayor and council except for the annual election of the mayor pro tempore. The annual election of the mayor pro tempore shall be conducted by any legal voting method which shall be agreed upon prior to the election.
(Code 1984, § 1-1-3(g))
Secs. 2-83—2-107. - Reserved.
Subdivision III. - Special Motions

Sec. 2-108. - Call the question.
(a)
During the discussion of any motion, any councilmember may move to call the question in order to end discussion and have the vote on the pending motion.
(b)
When a councilmember has moved to call the question, discussion of the original motion shall cease and the mayor shall immediately restate the motion and then proceed with a vote on whether to call the question. A second of a motion to call the question shall not be necessary.
(c)
If the vote is in favor of the motion to call the question, the mayor shall immediately call the question on the original motion.
(d)
If the vote is against the motion to call the question, discussion of the original motion shall continue.
(Code 1984, § 1-1-3(h)(1))
Sec. 2-109. - Continuance.
(a)
During the discussion of any motion, any councilmember may move for a continuance in order to have any particular item continued to the next meeting. A motion for continuance requires a second; however, no vote shall be necessary for the continuance to take effect.
(b)
When a councilmember has moved for a continuance, discussion of the agenda item shall cease once the motion receives a second. A motion for continuance accompanied by a second shall automatically place the subject item on the agenda for the next scheduled meeting of the council.
(c)
If the motion for a continuance does not receive a second, the motion shall die for the lack of a second.
(d)
There shall be no more than one continuance allowed for any one agenda item.
(Code 1984, § 1-1-3(h)(2))
Sec. 2-110. - Point of order.
(a)
Whenever a councilmember has a question as to the procedure being followed by the mayor, he may raise a point of order. A point of order requires no second.
(b)
Upon the raising of a point of order, the mayor shall stop the meeting and the councilmember shall state his question. The meeting shall continue after a determination of the question has been rendered by the mayor.
(Code 1984, § 1-1-3(h)(3))
Sec. 2-111. - Out of order.
(a)
The mayor shall have the authority to cite any individual out of order when that individual, in the opinion of the mayor, violates these rules of procedure.
(b)
No other action shall be required in citing an individual out of order.
(c)
When an individual has been cited as out of order, the mayor shall state the reasons therefor and shall give the individual directions regarding compliance with these rules.
(Code 1984, § 1-1-3(h)(4))
Sec. 2-112. - Adjournment.
(a)
Adjournment of the meeting must be by motion.
(b)
A motion to adjourn does not require a second but does require discussion.
(c)
When a motion to adjourn is passed, the council can no longer act until another meeting is called to order.
(Code 1984, § 1-1-3(h)(5))
Secs. 2-113—2-137. - Reserved.

DIVISION 2. - SPECIFIC OFFICERS

Sec. 2-166. - Town officers.
(a)
The officers of the town shall be those provided for by the Charter and those positions established by the town council.
(b)
There is hereby created the office of town manager. The town manager shall perform those duties assigned to him by the mayor and council including the closing of roads as necessary.
(c)
There is hereby created the office of chief of police. The chief of police shall have the authority to close roads as necessary.
(Code 1984, § 2-4-1)
Sec. 2-167. - Town clerk.
(a)
Before entering upon the duties of office, the town clerk shall take the prescribed oath and be bonded at the town's expense in the amount of not less than $25,000.00.
(b)
In addition to the duties of the town clerk under the town Charter, the town clerk shall perform the following duties:
(1)
Attendance at council meeting and minutes thereof. The town clerk or his designee shall attend all meetings of the town council and shall take the minutes thereof and shall record the same in suitable books.
(2)
Communication of information to town council. The town clerk shall communicate to the town council, all information he may acquire that may be conducive to the efficiency of the town government and the welfare of the people.
(3)
Preservation, arrangement, indexing, etc. of papers, records and documents. The town clerk or his designee shall have charge of and be responsible for the preservation of all papers, records and documents of every description belonging to the town and shall arrange and file all such papers in such manner as will facilitate access to them.
(4)
Issuing of notices. The town clerk shall issue all notices, personal and general, which the laws, orders and interest of the town require in the administration of its government.
(5)
Attestation of executions. The town clerk shall attest all executions issued by authority of the town council.
(6)
Attestation and sealing of deeds, contracts, etc. The town clerk shall attest and affix the town seal to all deeds to real property sold by the town, including lots in the town cemeteries, and all contracts, agreements and other obligations issued or authorized by the town council.
(7)
Monthly report to town manager. The town clerk shall make out and submit to the town manager, for examination prior to the second regular monthly meeting of the council, a report of transactions as town clerk for the preceding month.
(8)
Additional duties. The town clerk shall perform all other duties that may be imposed upon him by the laws of the state or the provisions of this Code and the ordinances or resolutions of the town or by direction of the town manager.
(Code 1984, §§ 2-4-3, 2-4-4)
Sec. 2-168. - Code enforcement officer.
(a)
The position of code enforcement officer is hereby established for the town.
(b)
The town manager or an individual or individuals designated by the town manager shall serve as the code enforcement officer for the town.
(c)
The code enforcement officer shall have the authority, in addition to other remedies and after due notice to the owner of the property or the permit holder or any other person violating any provision of this Code, the zoning ordinance of the town, the subdivision regulations of the town, the tree and landscape ordinances of the town, and any other ordinance or regulation of the town, to issue a citation to such owner of the property or the permit holder or any other person and require the presence of the violator in the municipal court.
(Code 1984, § 2-4-6; Ord. No. 440, § I, 6-19-2003)

(Code 1984, § 2-5-1)
Sec. 2-225. - Collection of taxes and other revenue and responsibility for sums collected.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the finance manager to collect all taxes levied by the mayor and council according to the provisions of this Code and other ordinances of the town for the support of the town government, all sums paid as occupational taxes imposed by the town council for the privilege of conducting business within the town, or otherwise, and all sums realized under assessment levied by the town council for defraying the expenses of paving or otherwise improving the streets or sidewalks of for any other purpose.

The finance manager or his designee shall deposit, in a timely fashion, to the credit of the town, in the banks designated by the town council as town depositories, all monies received by him and belonging to the town. The finance manager or his designee shall keep records of such deposits made by him, with the dates thereof, which records shall always be open to the inspection of the mayor and council.
(Code 1984, § 2-5-3)
Sec. 2-227. - Checkbooks and receipts; preservation and numbering of original accounts or demands.
The finance manager or his designee shall keep a record of all checks drawn by him on funds on deposit in the banks to the credit of the town, with the names of the persons in whose favor checks are drawn and for what purpose and to which department such checks shall respectively be chargeable. He shall, at the time of drawing such checks, take receipts for all moneys paid and he keep a record of such receipts.
(Code 1984, § 2-5-4)
Sec. 2-228. - Disbursements by check or draft.
All disbursements shall be made by checks signed by the town manager and countersigned by the mayor, or in the absence of the mayor, the mayor pro tem. No money on deposit in any bank to the credit of the town shall be withdrawn from such bank or disbursed otherwise than through checks or drafts thus drawn and signed by the town manager and countersigned by the mayor or, in the absence of the mayor, the mayor pro tem.
(Code 1984, § 2-5-5)
Sec. 2-229. - Duties regarding paid bonds, coupons.
The finance manager shall cancel, mutilate and preserve all town bonds and coupons that have been paid, and shall keep a record of same.
(Code 1984, § 2-5-6)
Sec. 2-230. - Deposit and disbursement of town funds; signing of checks.
Any officer receiving money for the town shall deliver to the finance manager or his designee for deposit all such moneys in the banks designated by the governing body in the name of the town. Such banks shall pay out no funds so deposited except on checks that shall be signed by the town manager and countersigned by the mayor or, in the absence of the mayor, the mayor pro tem and shall specify to whom payable and the purpose for which drawn.
(Code 1984, § 2-5-9)

Sec. 2-256. - Downtown development authority.
(a)
There is created in and for the Town of Tyrone a public body corporate and politic to be known as the Downtown Development Authority of Tyrone. The Downtown Development Authority of Tyrone shall consist of a board of seven directors, which includes a member of the governing body of the Town of Tyrone. Three members of the first board of directors shall be appointed for a term of two years each, and three for a term of four years each. The members initially serving two-year terms are: Ernie Johnson; Jeni Mount; and Adam She. The remaining members will serve four-year terms: John Kaufman; Nathan Reese; and Luci McDuffie. After expiration of the initial terms, except for the director who is also a member of the governing body of the Town of Tyrone, the terms of all directors shall be four years. The term of a director who is also a member of the governing body of the Town of Tyrone shall end when such director is no longer a member of the governing body of the Town of Tyrone. If at the end of any term of office of any director a successor to such director has not been elected, the director whose term of office has expired shall continue to hold office until a successor is elected. A majority of the board of directors shall constitute a quorum.
(b)
The Downtown Development Authority of Tyrone shall operate and have jurisdiction exclusively over that geographic location in the Town of Tyrone known as the downtown development area, more particularly described in Exhibit "A" attached hereto, with said Exhibit "A" being incorporated herein by this reference. A map of the Downtown Development Area is attached as Exhibit "B," with said Exhibit "B" being incorporated herein by this reference.
(c)
The Downtown Development Authority of Tyrone shall have all those powers and duties as spelled out in the Downtown Development Authorities Law for the State of Georgia, i.e., O.C.G.A. § 36-42-1 et seq.
( Ord. No. 2022-01 , § 1, 2-3-2022)
Sec. 2-257. - Qualifications and reimbursement of directors; election of officers; and training.
(a)
Directors shall be:
(1)
Taxpayers residing in the Town of Tyrone;
(2)
Owners or operators of businesses located within the downtown development area and who shall be taxpayers residing in Fayette County; or
(3)
Persons having a combination of the qualifications specified in paragraphs (1) and (2) of this subsection;
provided, however, that one of such directors may be a member of the mayor and council.
(b)
Not less than four of the directors having the qualifications specified in subsection (a) of this Code section shall be persons who, in the judgment of the mayor and council, either have or represent a party who has an economic interest in the redevelopment and revitalization of the downtown development area. Successors to the directors shall be appointed by the mayor and council.
(c)
The directors shall elect one of their members as chairman and another as vice chairman and shall also elect a secretary and a treasurer or a secretary-treasurer, either of whom may but need not be a director. The directors shall receive no compensation for their services but shall be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred by them in the performance of their duties.
(d)
Notwithstanding subsection (a) of this Code section, one director appointed to the board may reside outside the county; provided, however, that such appointed director owns a business within the downtown development area and is a resident of the State of Georgia. If subsequently to his or her appointment to the board pursuant to this subsection, the director ceases to own a business within the downtown development area or reside in the State of Georgia, such director shall relinquish his or her seat on the board.
(e)
Except for a director who is also a member of the mayor and council, each director shall attend and complete at least eight hours of training on downtown development and redevelopment programs within the first 12 months of a director's appointment to the downtown development authority.
( Ord. No. 2022-01 , § 2, 2-3-2022)
Sec. 2-258. - Meetings.
(a)
Meeting schedule. The downtown development authority shall meet monthly. The chairman or members of the downtown development authority may call additional meetings.
(b)
Meeting location. The downtown development authority shall meet in Tyrone Town Hall unless the chairman or a majority of the members designates another location.
(c)
Bylaws. The downtown development authority shall create adequate bylaws to govern the functioning of the downtown development authority.
( Ord. No. 2022-01 , § 3, 2-3-2022)
