ARTICLE II. - DOGS

Sec. 8-17. - Identifying tag required.
It shall be unlawful for any person in the city to keep, own or maintain a dog without the name and address of the owner affixed to such dog by means of a collar or other identification paraphernalia. This identification is in addition to any public health information required by the state department of health for rabies or other inoculation.
(Ord. of 2-3-1986, § 1)
Sec. 8-18. - Tag not required when confined upon owner's premises.
The identifying information provided for in section 8-17 shall not be required where the dog is kept on the premises of the owner or person in charge of such dog and the premises is provided with a secure fence so as to confine the dog upon the premises, or the dog is securely maintained by leash, rope or otherwise restrained.
(Ord. of 2-3-1986, § 2)
Sec. 8-19. - Impounding untagged dogs at large; humane disposal.
Any dog found in the city unrestrained or within the premises securely fenced as provided in section 8-18, or not having on its body the name and address of its owner as provided by section 8-17, shall be seized and confined by the duly authorized agents or employees of the city and shall be confined for a period of three days after which time such agents or employees are authorized to dispose of such dogs in a humane manner.
(Ord. of 2-3-1986, § 3)
Sec. 8-20. - Reclamation.
The owner of any dog confined or impounded as provided in section 8-19 may reclaim such dog by making known his claim at the city hall or any other such place as may be directed by the mayor within the three-day period provided in section 8-19, and shall be entitled to reclaim possession of such dog upon payment to the city the sum of $10.00 plus $5.00 for each day in which the dog was confined by the city and upon affixing to such dog the identifying information provided for in section 8-17.
(Ord. of 2-3-1986, § 4)
Sec. 8-21. - City to care for impounded dogs.
When any dog is caught, impounded and confined under the provisions of this article, it shall be the duty of the city to feed, water and care for such dog until the dog has been claimed by its owner, as provided in section 8-20, or disposed of as provided by section 8-19.
(Ord. of 2-3-1986, § 5)
Sec. 8-22. - City clerk authorized to make regulations.
The city clerk is hereby authorized to make regulations for the implementing of this article consistent with the intention of protecting any dog whose owner seeks to make his ownership known and to rid the city of any dog that apparently has no owner and roams the streets and yards of the city.
(Ord. of 2-3-1986, § 6)
