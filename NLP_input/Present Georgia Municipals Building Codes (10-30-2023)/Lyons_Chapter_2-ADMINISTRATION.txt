ARTICLE II. - CITY COUNCIL

Sec. 2-31. - Time and days of meetings.
The city council shall meet every first Tuesday of each month at 6:00 p.m.
(Code 1977, § 2-1; Ord. No. 1597, § I, 9-1-2020)
Charter reference— Authority to fix time of meetings, § 12.
Sec. 2-32. - Copies of minutes.
A copy of the minutes of the meetings of the city council shall be mailed or delivered by the city clerk to each councilmember in sufficient time for such councilmember to receive the minutes prior to the next regular meeting.
(Code 1977, § 2-3)
Sec. 2-33. - Compensation.
The mayor shall receive a salary of $500.00 per month. Councilmembers shall receive a salary of $150.00 per month.
State Law reference— Authority to determine salary of governing body, O.C.G.A. § 36-35-4.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-181. - Designation of city depository; liability of officers and employees for funds.
(a)
Such bank as is determined by the city council shall be designated as depository for such funds as may come into the hands of the city officers and employees, which funds shall be deposited in such bank.
(b)
City officers and employees shall be held liable for all funds that may come into their hands while acting in their official capacities, until such funds are so deposited and receipts taken therefor.
(Code 1977, § 9-1)
Sec. 2-182. - Writing and signing checks.
The city clerk shall have authority to write and sign all checks, which must also be signed by the mayor.
(Code 1977, § 9-5)
Sec. 2-183. - Approval of checks and accounts.
All checks drawn and accounts paid by the city clerk that are of a legal and binding obligation of the city shall be approved.
(Code 1977, § 9-6)
Sec. 2-184. - Service charge on returned checks.
The city will charge the maximum amount authorized by law as a service charge for handling each returned check.
(Code 1977, § 9-8)
State Law reference— Service charge limitations, O.C.G.A. § 16-9-20(j).

DIVISION 2. - PURCHASES AND EXPENDITURES[4]

State Law reference— Local government public works contracts, O.C.G.A. § 36-91-1 et seq.

Sec. 2-211. - Bill approval and payment procedure.
(a)
All purchases of every nature or kind for and on behalf of the city shall be made by a member of the city council or other officer or employee designated as purchasing agent.
(b)
No bills, charges or debits shall be submitted to the city council for approval unless the bills, charges or debits bear the written approval of the purchasing agent.
(c)
All bills against the city shall be submitted to the city council at its regular meeting and shall not be paid until so approved by the city council at its regular meeting. Only those bills contracted, authorized and approved by the purchasing agent shall be submitted to the city council for its approval.
(Code 1977, § 9-2)
Sec. 2-212. - Purchase order required for merchandise.
A bill to the city will not be honored unless, when the merchandise was purchased, it was accompanied by a purchase order, signed by authorized personnel.
(Code 1977, § 9-3)
Sec. 2-213. - Purchases without council approval.
The limits of purchases without council approval shall be $1,000.00 for the mayor and $100.00 for the city administrator.
(Code 1977, § 9-4)
