Sec. 22-61. - License fee; applicability of state law.
Each person engaged in any business which is regulated by and subject to 1981 Ga. Laws, page 1570, adopted and approved by the general assembly of the state at its 1981 general session, as now adopted or hereafter amended and as codified in O.C.G.A. § 43-37-1 et seq., shall pay a license fee of $500.00 for a continuing fixed business, or a license fee of $100.00 per day, up to a maximum of $500.00, for any one continuous period of temporary business operation. Such business shall be subject to and comply with the provisions of the 1981 Ga. Laws, page 1570, with the provisions of this article applicable to such business in lieu of those set forth in that act.
(Code 1976, § 6-1241(a))
Sec. 22-62. - Retention of records.
The permanent record book required pursuant to subsection 3(b) of the act referred to in section 22-61 [O.C.G.A. § 43-37-3] shall be retained in the city for at least one year after the date of its last entry, at the place of business of the license holder. If the license holder no longer maintains a place of business in the city, the book shall be maintained by a designated custodian for the license holder during such period, and the name, address and telephone number of such designated license holder shall be provided in writing to the chief of police, along with the consent of such designated custodian to retain the book contained on the writing.
(Code 1976, § 6-1241(b))
Sec. 22-63. - Holding period.
The provision in subsection 5(a)(6) of the act referred to in section 22-61 specifying a period of seven calendar days shall, as to any dealer in the city, be extended to a period of ten calendar days.
(Code 1976, § 6-1241(c))
