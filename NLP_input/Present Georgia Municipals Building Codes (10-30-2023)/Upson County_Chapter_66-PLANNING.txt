Sec. 66-31. - Created.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 36-66-1(2)(b)(1), the Upson County Planning Commission, referred to in this article as "the planning commission," is created and established.
(Ord. No. 77, § 103, 6-10-1994)
Sec. 66-32. - Membership and appointments.
(a)
The planning commission shall consist of at least three but no more than five members.
(b)
All members must reside within the unincorporated areas of the county, and they shall be appointed by the board of commissioners.
(c)
None of the members of the planning commission may hold any other public office; however, they may also be members of the Thomaston-Upson County Planning Commission.
(d)
Commission members may be removed by the board of commissioners for cause, upon written charges, and after a public hearing.
(e)
Members of the planning commission will be disqualified to act upon any matter before the planning commission in which they have a personal financial interest or other conflict of interest.
(Ord. No. 77, § 105, 6-10-1994; Ord. No. 252 , § 1, 11-22-2016)
Sec. 66-33. - Terms of office.
(a)
Subject to subsection (b) of this section, the term of each member of the planning commission is for three years. Any vacancy in the membership will be filled for the unexpired term of the vacating member.
(b)
The three-year terms of all previously appointed members of the planning commission which are being served as of January 27, 2000, shall remain unchanged by Ordinance No. 133, except that the terms of Jimmy Robinson and Joel Pitts are extended an additional seven months such that their terms shall expire on January 9, 2004.
(c)
The planning commissioner who is to be appointed to complete county commissioner-Elect James Ellington's unexpired term, set to expire December 31, 2017, shall instead be appointed for a full three-year term to end December 31, 2019, therefore creating a three-year cycle of planning commissioner terms, with no more than two commissioners per year completing their terms.
(Ord. No. 77, § 106, 6-10-1994; Ord. No. 133, § 1, 1-27-2000; Ord. No. 251 , § 1, 11-22-2016)
Sec. 66-34. - Compensation.
modified
(a)
There is no additional compensation for extra meetings. Attendance at extra meetings does not make up for missing the normal and customary monthly meeting.
(b)
If the normal and customary monthly meeting is cancelled due to any reason other than absence/lack of quorum (i.e., lack of agenda items, weather, etc.), members will receive their monthly compensation.
(c)
Only the duly elected, annual chairperson shall receive the chair's compensation. This includes any new chair, permanently replacing a duly elected annual chair who has vacated the office for whatever reason. Temporary/stand-in chairpersons including the duly elected, annual vice chairperson shall receive the regular commissioner compensation.
(Ord. No. 77, § 107, 6-10-1994; Res. of 1-14-2020 )
Sec. 66-35. - Officers; rules and procedures.
(a)
The planning commission will elect one of its members as chairman in January of each year. The chairman will serve for one year. At the end of that term, he must either be reelected or a successor elected.
(b)
The planning commission will appoint a secretary, who may be an officer of the county or the planning commission.
(c)
The planning commission has the authority to adopt rules of procedure which are consistent with the policies and procedures set out in the county zoning ordinance in appendix A to this Code.
(d)
Meetings of the planning commission will be held at the call of the chairman or in his absence the acting chairman.
(e)
The chairman or the acting chairman may administer oaths and compel the attendance of witnesses by subpoena. The planning commission must keep minutes of its proceedings, showing the vote of each member upon each question or, if absent or failing to vote, indicating such a fact. The planning commission must also keep records of its examinations and other official actions, all of which must be immediately filed in the office of the planning commission and will be public record.
(f)
A decision of the planning commission must be recorded in the minutes for the meeting at which the decision was made and must contain a statement of the grounds for its decision or action. The full text of the decision must be sent to the applicant.
(g)
No application requesting the same action for the same property will be received or heard by the planning commission twice within the same 12-month period. However, that limitation does not affect the right of the planning commission to grant a rehearing as provided in the rules and procedures adopted by the planning commission.
(Ord. No. 77, § 108, 6-10-1994)
Sec. 66-36. - Purpose and powers.
(a)
The purpose of the planning commission is as follows:
(1)
Advise the board of commissioners on applications for amendment to the county zoning ordinance in appendix A to this Code by examining amendment applications and providing written recommendations with reasons for the recommendation to the board of commissioners as specified in section 410 of such ordinance.
(2)
Dispense general information about the county zoning ordinance to the public upon request.
(3)
Propose amendments to the county zoning ordinance.
(4)
Maintain and update the county land use plan (where one exists) so that it may provide a current database with which decisions on proposed amendments to the county zoning ordinance may be made that utilize sound planning principles.
(5)
Carry out an ongoing comprehensive planning program which, like the land use plan (where one exists), will provide current data on which decisions regarding the county zoning ordinance may be based that utilize sound planning principles.
(6)
Advise the board of commissioners on matters of zoning and annexation, as appropriate.
(7)
Authorize variances according to procedures specified in section 407 of the county zoning ordinance.
(8)
Accept applications for appeal of an action of the administrative officer and render official decisions on them according to procedures specified in section 406 of the county zoning ordinance.
(9)
Authorize special exceptions under procedures contained in section 409 of the county zoning ordinance.
(10)
Accept applications for appeal of actions of the historic preservation commission and render official decisions on them.
(b)
The planning commission does not have the power to:
(1)
Amend any zoning ordinance;
(2)
Rezone any land;
(3)
Declare the county zoning ordinance or any amendment thereto invalid; or
(4)
Allow any use not permitted by the zoning ordinance.
(Ord. No. 77, § 104, 6-10-1994)
