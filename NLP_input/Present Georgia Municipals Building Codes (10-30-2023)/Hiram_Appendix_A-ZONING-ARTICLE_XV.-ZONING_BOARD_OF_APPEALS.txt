ARTICLE XV. - ZONING BOARD OF APPEALS

Sec. A. - Authorization of the Zoning Board of Appeals, of Paulding County, Georgia.
The Zoning Board of Appeals, of Paulding County, Georgia, is hereby authorized to hear initial appeals and variance requests, on behalf of the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, and make recommendation to the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia. All motions to recommend actions on applications to the Zoning Board of Appeals, of Paulding County, Georgia, shall be forwarded to the Operations Manager, of the City of Hiram, Georgia or the City Manager of the City of Hiram, Georgia or the Temporary City Manager, to be appropriately advertised and presented at the public hearing, before the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, prior to final consideration at their next regularly scheduled meeting.
Sec. B. - Proceedings of the Zoning Board of Appeals.
The Zoning Board of Appeals conduct meetings, in the manner set forth in the Zoning Ordinance of Paulding County, Georgia, Article XV, Section B, with regard to structure of the Zoning Board of Appeals, schedule of meetings, recorded minutes of all meetings, and the public meetings requirement. The City of Hiram, Georgia, understands that the Zoning Board of Appeals, of Paulding County, Georgia, shall adopt such rules and by-laws as they deem appropriate, for their functions and operations.
Sec. C. - Powers and duties of the Zoning Board of Appeals.
The Zoning Board of Appeals shall have the following powers and duties:
(1)
Appeal of Administrative Decision. To hear and gather information, regarding appeals where it is alleged there is error in any order, requirement, decision, or determination made by the Operations Manager, of the City of Hiram or the City Manager of the City of Hiram, Georgia or the Temporary City Manager, or Zoning Administrator, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, in the enforcement of this Ordinance.
(2)
Hearing of Requests for Variances to Terms of this Ordinance. To recommend to the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, upon appeal in specific cases such variance from the terms of this ordinance as will not be contrary to the public interest where, owing to special conditions, a literal enforcement of provisions of this ordinance will, in an individual case, result in unnecessary hardship, so that the spirit of this ordinance shall be observed, public safety and welfare secured, and substantial justice done. Such variance may be recommended to the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, in such individual case of unnecessary hardship upon a finding of the Zoning Board of Appeals, of Paulding County, Georgia that:
(a)
There are extraordinary and exceptional conditions pertaining to the particular piece of property in question because of its size, shape, character, topography, and
(b)
The application of this zoning ordinance to this particular piece of property would create an unnecessary hardship, and
(c)
Such conditions are peculiar to the particular piece of property involved, and
(d)
Relief, if granted, would not cause substantial detriment to the public good or impair the purpose and intent of this ordinance.
(3)
Provided, however, that no variance shall be recommended to the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, for a use of land, or building, or structure that is prohibited by this ordinance.
(4)
In exercising the powers in this section conferred upon it, the Zoning Board of Appeals, of Paulding County, Georgia, may recommend the reversal, or affirmation, wholly, or partly, or may recommend the modification of the orders, requirements, decisions, or determinations appealed from; and to that end shall have all the authority to request information from the officer from whom the appeal is taken and may issue a recommendation regarding the issuance of a permit, to the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia.
Sec. D. - Appeals, hearings, and notice.
(1)
Appeals to the Zoning Board of Appeals, of Paulding County, Georgia, may be taken by any person aggrieved, or by any officer, department, board, or bureau of the City of Hiram, Georgia, affected by any decision of the Operations Manager, of the City of Hiram, Georgia or the City Manager of the City of Hiram, Georgia or the Temporary City Manager, or the Zoning Administrator, of the City of Hiram, Georgia. Such appeal shall be made within thirty (30) days by filing with the Department of Community Development, Planning and Zoning Division, of Paulding County, Georgia, a written notice of appeal specifying the grounds thereof.
(2)
Applications for a variance must be filed on forms provided by the Department of Community Development, Planning and Zoning Division, of Paulding County, Georgia, according to the schedule established by the Division. Applications shall be submitted and the following provided at the date of filing:
(a)
A completed application form signed by the owner and the applicant.
(b)
Warranty deed or other proof of ownership of the property and documentation by the Tax Commissioner, of Paulding County, Georgia that the ad valorem taxes levied against the property have been paid.
(c)
A survey plat of the property (number of copies as specified by the Department of Community Development, Planning and Zoning Division, of Paulding County, Georgia) which demonstrates a true and accurate boundary of the property and illustrates the variance requested, prepared by a Registered Surveyor, licensed to operate by the State of Georgia, drawn to scale, showing north arrow, land lot and district, the dimensions, acreage, and location of the tract. The preparer's seal shall be affixed to the plat. Indicate on the plat structures which are currently located on the property and depict the variance requested on the plat to scale.
(d)
The names and mailing addresses of all adjoining property owners, including the owners across the road, and to the rear.
(e)
The filing fee as set by the Board of Commissioners, of Paulding County, Georgia.
(f)
Such other additional information as may be requested by the Department of Community Development, Planning and Zoning Division, of Paulding County, Georgia, and the Operations Manager, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, in the Procedures for the Filing of Applications.
(3)
Prior to the date of the hearing, a notice of the hearing shall be published within a newspaper of general circulation within the City of Hiram, Georgia, in which are carried the legal advertisements of the City of Hiram, Georgia or Paulding County, Georgia.
Sec. E. - Decisions of the Board of Appeals.
(1)
In exercising its powers, the Zoning Board of Appeals, of Paulding County, Georgia, may, in conformity with the provisions of this Ordinance, recommend reversal or affirmation, wholly or partly, or may recommend modification of the orders, requirements, decisions, or determinations, to the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, and may recommend issuance of a building permit, upon approval by the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia.
(2)
The concurring vote of a majority of the members present of the Zoning Board of Appeals, of Paulding County, Georgia, shall be necessary to recommend the reversal of any order, requirement, decision, or determination of the Operations Manager, of the City of Hiram, Georgia or the City Manager of the City of Hiram, Georgia or the Temporary City Manager, or the Zoning Administrator, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, or to make a recommendation in favor of the applicant and forward such result to the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, for further consideration, with the approved recommendations.
(3)
On all appeals, applications, and other matters brought before the Zoning Board of Appeals, of Paulding County, Georgia, said Board shall inform the applicant of its recommendations, and decisions and the reasons therefore. The Zoning Board of Appeals, of Paulding County, Georgia, will instruct parties with interest in all appeals, applications, and other matters brought before the board that all recommendations will be forward to the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, for the required, and appropriately advertised, legal public hearing, and the final decision on all matters.
Sec. F. - Conduct of hearing.
(1)
The conduct of hearings before the body of the Zoning Board of Appeals, of Paulding County, Georgia, shall be governed by the requirements stipulated in the approved Zoning Ordinance of the City of Hiram, Georgia, as adopted by the Mayor and Council of the City of Hiram, Georgia.
(2)
All motions regarding recommendations for applications pertaining to the City of Hiram, Georgia, shall be forwarded to the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, for the purposes of conducting the required legally advertised public hearing, for zoning matters.
(3)
The recommendations that are forwarded to the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, from the Zoning Board of Appeals, of Paulding County, Georgia, will be heard the day of the next regular session of the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, if there is enough time for the City to meet the advertising requirement for zoning hearings, as stipulated by the laws of the State of Georgia.
(a)
Anyone in attendance at the public hearing wishing to speak on behalf or in opposition to applications that are on the agenda must be recognized and they will be asked to state their name and place of residence, and then asked if they had signed in prior to the start of the meeting on a sheet provided by the staff.
(b)
The person recognized to speak will be allowed to speak on any point relevant to the petition being considered. Applicants and opponents shall be given a maximum of fifteen (15) minutes each to present their case. Any groups which are present are encouraged to choose a spokesperson to present their views. The Mayor, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, may request representatives of each side to speak for the entire group. If more than one (1) speaker represents a group, the fifteen (15) minutes shall be divided among the various speakers. Speakers are encouraged to refrain from presenting information that has previously been stated by others. In no case, however, shall any group be allowed more than the allotted time no matter how many speakers represent the group unless extended by consent of the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia. The Clerk of the City of Hiram, Georgia, will be responsible for keeping time.
(c)
Proposals for each Agenda item should be presented orally to the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, and/or their Representative.
(d)
During and following the Applicant's presentation, the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, may ask any pertinent questions of the Applicant, or their Representative to aid in their understanding of the case.
(e)
After such presentation, and discussion with the Applicant, the Mayor, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, shall ask if there be anyone present who is present in opposition to the granting of such request.
(f)
Those appearing in opposition may offer oral evidence, and any documentation to demonstrate their position.
(g)
During and following the presentation of those in opposition to the application or proposal, the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, may ask any pertinent questions of the Opposition to aid in their understanding of the case. Questions and responses shall not be counted as part of the maximum fifteen (15) minute presentation time limit.
(h)
No further public input will be allowed without consent of the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia.
(i)
At the conclusion of the public hearing, the Mayor, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, will announce the public hearing closed.
(4)
Once the public hearing has taken place, the item may be taken up for consideration by the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, during the same regular session of the Council, unless tabled or postponed by the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia.
If the zoning item is tabled or postponed, the item will be forwarded to the next regular session of the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia. The Mayor will announce this action to the public during the meeting, along with the reasons for this action. No further advertising will be required.
Sec. G. - Appeals from decisions of the board.
Any person, or persons severally, or jointly aggrieved by any final decision of the Zoning Board of Appeals, or any final decision of the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, may file an appeal in writing to the Superior Court, of Paulding County, Georgia. Said written appeal must be filed within thirty (30) days from the date of the final decision of the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia or any final decision of the Zoning Board of Appeals; and upon failure to file said written appeal within thirty (30) days the final decision of the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, or the final decision of the Zoning Board of Appeals shall be final.
Sec. H. - Administrative variances.
The Department of Community Development Director, of Paulding County, Georgia, with the approval of the Mayor and Council of the City of Hiram, Georgia, shall have the power to grant variances (except for density and use variances) from the dimensional standards of the ordinance where, in their opinion, the intent of the ordinance can be achieved and equal performance obtained by granting a variance. The authority to grant such variances shall be limited to variances from the following requirements:
(1)
Front yard or yard adjacent to public street—variance not to exceed five (5) feet.
(2)
Side yard—variance not to exceed three (3) feet.
(3)
Rear yard—variance not to exceed five (5) feet.
(4)
Height—variance not to equal or exceed ten (10) feet, provided that no variance may result in an increase in the number of stories than would otherwise be allowed under the applicable zoning district.
(5)
A reduction of the minimum number of required parking spaces up to a maximum of ten percent (10%).
(6)
A reduction of the minimum distance to any property line for animal quarters (agricultural facilities, kennels, etc.) up to a maximum of fifty (50) feet.
The Department of Community Development Director, of Paulding County, Georgia, shall not have the authority to grant any additional administrative variances after a variance has been approved by the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia.
