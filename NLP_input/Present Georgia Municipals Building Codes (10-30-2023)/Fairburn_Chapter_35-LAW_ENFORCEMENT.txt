ARTICLE II. - POLICE DEPARTMENT
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 35-26. - Composition; appointment of chief.
The police department shall consist of the chief of police and such other members and officers as may be provided for from time to time by the mayor and city council.
(Ord. No. 2009-8, § 8B, 8-28-2009)
Sec. 35-27. - General duties of members.
It shall be the duty of the members of the police department to see to the enforcement of all ordinances of the city and all statutes effective in the city, and to preserve order and prevent infractions of the law and to arrest any person violating such laws.
(Ord. No. 2009-8, § 8B, 8-28-2009)
Sec. 35-28. - Conduct of members.
It shall be the duty of every member of the police department to conduct himself in a proper and law abiding manner, and to avoid the use of unnecessary force.
(Ord. No. 2009-8, § 8B, 8-28-2009)
Sec. 35-29. - Duty of member to appear as witness.
Every member of the department shall appear as a witness whenever this is necessary in a prosecution for a violation of a city ordinance or of a state or federal law.
(Ord. No. 2009-8, § 8B, 8-28-2009)
Sec. 35-30. - Qualifications of officers.
Each person employed as a police officer shall meet the following requirements.
(1)
Be at least 20 years of age;
(2)
Be a citizen of the United States;
(3)
Have a high school diploma or its recognized equivalent;
(4)
Not have been convicted by any state or by the federal government of any crime the punishment for which could have been imprisonment in the federal or state prison or institution nor have been convicted or sufficient misdemeanors to establish a pattern of disregard for the law; provided that, for purposes of this subsection, violations of traffic laws and other offenses involving the operation of motor vehicles when the applicant has received a pardon shall not be considered;
(5)
Be fingerprinted and a search made of local, state and national fingerprint files to disclose any criminal record;
(6)
Possess good moral character as determined by investigation under procedure established by the council;
(7)
Have an oral interview with the hiring authority or its representative to determine the applicant's appearance, background, and ability to communicate;
(8)
Be found, after examination by a licensed physician or surgeon, to be free from any physical, emotional, or mental conditions which might adversely affect his exercising the powers or duties of a peace officer; and
(9)
Within six months after appointment, attend and pass a course at the state police academy to be a certified police officer.
(Ord. No. 2009-8, § 8B, 8-28-2009)
Secs. 35-31—35-45. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - CHIEF OF POLICE

Sec. 35-46. - Appointment.
The chief of police shall be appointed in accordance with charter provisions of the city and shall be the head of the police department.
(Ord. No. 2009-8, § 8B, 8-28-2009)
Sec. 35-47. - Bond.
The chief of police shall be bonded at the city's expense in such amount as may beset by the mayor and city council.
(Ord. No. 2009-8, § 8B, 8-28-2009)
Sec. 35-48. - General powers and duties.
The chief of police shall have jurisdiction over the operations of the department. He shall keep such records and make such reports, concerning the activities of his department as may be required by statute or ordinance or as may be required by the city administrator. The chief shall be responsible for the performance by the police department of all its functions, and all persons who are members of the department shall serve subject to the orders of the chief of police.
(Ord. No. 2009-8, § 8B, 8-28-2009)
Sec. 35-49. - Rules and regulations.
The chief of police may make or prescribe such rules and regulations for the guidance of the members of the department as he shall see fit. Such rules, when approved by the city administrator, shall be binding on such members.
(Ord. No. 2009-8, § 8B, 8-28-2009)
Sec. 35-50. - Custodian of lost, abandoned, stolen property.
The chief of police shall be custodian of all lost, abandoned or recovered stolen property in the city.
(Ord. No. 2009-8, § 8B, 8-28-2009)
Sec. 35-51. - Process serving.
The chief of police shall be authorized to serve writs, summons, and other processes, but no patrolman shall serve any such summons or process except on the order of the chief of police.
(Ord. No. 2009-8, § 8B, 8-28-2009)
