Sec. 4-473. - Revocation of license.
(a)
A special event license issued pursuant to this article may be denied, suspended or revoked by the city council for due cause for a violation of this article in accordance with the procedures provided in section 4-30.
(b)
A special event license may be suspended effective immediately, under the provisions of and subject to the procedures outlined in section 4-30(e).
(c)
A special event license may be suspended or revoked immediately by the chief of police for due cause for a violation of this article when such violation results in an emergency situation in which continued operations of the premises by the licensee endangers the health, safety and/or welfare of the public. A hearing on the suspension or revocation shall be held at the next regularly scheduled meeting of the city council in accordance with the provisions of section 4-30(b).
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(H)(3); Ord. of 10-8-2013)
Sec. 4-474. - Wine and malt beverage tasting license.
Upon filing an application and payment of an annual license fee in the amount established by resolution, the city manager may issue a wine and malt beverage tasting license. The license shall allow the applicant to provide samples of wine or malt beverages (but not both) to the public for consumption on the premises under the following conditions:
(1)
The applicant must already hold a license to sell wine or malt beverages in original packages for off-premises consumption.
(2)
The applicant shall maintain on the premises and offer for sale at all times a variety of wines or malt beverages from a variety of manufacturers of wines or malt beverages. For purposes of this subsection, the term "variety" means more than 25 number of different wines or malt beverages, and ten or more different manufacturers.
(3)
The applicant must hold a valid current wine or malt beverage license in the State of Georgia and City of Porterdale.
(4)
No wine or malt beverage tasting event may be conducted on the premises of any place of business licensed to sell distilled spirits in the unbroken container at retail.
(5)
Wine or malt beverage tasting events may be conducted only in connection with an instructional or educational promotion.
(6)
All wines or malt beverages secured for event purposes must be obtained through a retail or wholesale outlet licensed in the State of Georgia.
(7)
No wine or malt beverage tasting event shall last more than four hours, and no licensee may hold more than one event per week.
(8)
All applicants must comply with all federal and state statutes and sections of this Code of Ordinances and other city ordinances concerning alcoholic beverages, including, but not limited to, those dealing with hours of operation, zoning, and distance requirements, excepting only the provisions of section 4-357(b).
(9)
The licensee shall provide the city clerk/treasurer with written notice of the date and time of each event at least seven days prior to such event.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(H)(1); Ord. of 10-8-2013)
Sec. 4-475. - Special event alcoholic beverage license for qualified nonprofit civic organizations.
Upon filing an application and payment of a license fee per application in the amount established by resolution, and following an investigation and criminal background check by the city manager, the city manager may issue a special event alcoholic beverage license to a qualified nonprofit civic organization. The license shall allow the applicant to serve wine and malt beverages to the public for consumption on the premises only, under the following conditions:
(1)
The applicant shall be a qualified nonprofit civic organization and shall be required to submit with its application a copy of the organization's tax exempt status letter from the IRS, if applicable.
(2)
The applicant shall submit with its application a statement as to the proposed location of the event, provide the name and address of the owner of the real property at which the proposed event is to occur, and shall submit the owner's written permission for the event to occur at the proposed location.
(3)
The applicant must apply to the Georgia Department of Revenue for a State of Georgia One Day or Special Event Alcohol Beverage Permit, and comply with all requirements of the Georgia Department of Revenue regarding such permit.
(4)
No special event may be conducted on the premises of any place of business licensed to sell distilled spirits in the unbroken container at retail.
(5)
All wines or malt beverages secured for event purposes must be obtained through a retail or wholesale outlet licensed in the State of Georgia.
(6)
No event shall last more than eight hours, and no licensee may hold more than two events per year.
(7)
All applicants and permit holders must comply with all state statutes and sections of this Code of Ordinances and other city ordinances concerning alcoholic beverages, including, but not limited to, those dealing with hours of operation, zoning, and distance requirements, excepting only the provisions of section 4-357(b).
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(H)(2); Ord. of 10-8-2013)
Sec. 4-476. - Wine and malt beverages at city-sponsored events at Porter Memorial Gymnasium.
From time-to-time, the city council may, by resolution, allow wine and malt beverages to be brought to city sponsored events held at the Porter Memorial Gymnasium. With respect to any city-sponsored event for which such resolution is adopted, the following rules and regulations shall apply:
(1)
Individuals attending the city sponsored event may bring wine and/or malt beverages to the event in the original sealed containers, to be opened only inside the Porter Memorial Gymnasium. Any individual bringing wine and/or malt beverages to the event shall transport the wine and/or malt beverage in opaque secondary containers (e.g., bags, boxes, baskets), or coolers.
(2)
Individuals leaving the event may not leave the Porter Memorial Gymnasium with unsealed containers of alcohol. Bottles of wine must be securely resealed in the original container, and placed in an opaque secondary container (e.g., bags, boxes, baskets), or cooler before leaving the gymnasium. If transported in a motor vehicle, the container with the resealed bottle of wine shall be placed in a locked glove compartment, a locked trunk, or the area behind the last upright seat of a motor vehicle that is not equipped with a trunk.
(3)
Individuals may not leave the Porter Memorial Gymnasium facility with an open container of malt beverage.
(4)
Individuals may not carry or drink from an open container of wine and/or malt beverage while on any public street or sidewalk, or other public property, including, but not limited to, the areas surrounding the Porter Memorial Gymnasium.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(H)(4); Ord. of 10-8-2013)
