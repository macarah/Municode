ARTICLE VII. - LIFE INSURANCE COMPANIES[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— License fee authorized, O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.

Sec. 13-211. - License fee imposed—Insurers, companies.
There is hereby imposed upon each insurance company doing business within the city an annual license fee in the amount of $75.00 for the year 1989 and for each year thereafter. For each separate business location in excess of one which is operated and maintained by an insurance company within the city, there is hereby imposed an additional annual license fee in the amount of $75.00 for the year 1989 and for each year thereafter.
(Ord. No. 872, § 1, 11-16-1988)
Sec. 13-212. - Same—Money lenders.
For each separate business location, not otherwise subject to a license fee under this article, operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing and which in connection with such loans or sales offers, solicits or takes applications for insurance through a licensed agent of an insurance company, each insurance company shall pay an additional annual license fee of $26.25 per location for the year 1989 and for each year thereafter.
(Ord. No. 872, § 2, 11-16-1988)
Sec. 13-213. - Same—Insurance agents, brokers.
There is hereby imposed upon each independent insurance agency or broker doing an insurance business within the city an annual license fee in the amount of $75.00) for the year 1989 and for each year thereafter. For each separate business location in excess of one which is operated and maintained by an independent insurance agency or broker doing an insurance business within the city, there is hereby imposed an additional annual license fee in the amount of $75.00 for the year 1989 and for each year thereafter.
(Ord. No. 872, § 3, 11-16-1988)
Sec. 13-214. - Gross premium tax—Life insurers.
To the maximum extent permitted by O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.1, there is hereby imposed, for the year 1989 and for each year thereafter, an annual tax upon the gross direct premiums, at a rate of one percent of such gross direct premiums, received during the preceding calendar year from policies insuring persons residing within the city by each life insurance company doing business within the state.
(Ord. No. 872, § 4, 11-16-1988)
Sec. 13-215. - Same—Insurers other than life.
To the maximum extent permitted by O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2, there is hereby imposed, for the year 1989 and for each year thereafter, an annual tax upon the gross direct premiums, at a rate of 2½ percent of such gross direct premiums, received by all foreign, alien and domestic insurance companies doing business in this state other than life insurance companies during the preceding calendar year and derived from policies insuring persons, property or risks in the state.
(Ord. No. 872, § 5, 11-16-1988)
Sec. 13-216. - Same—Collection.
The taxes imposed by sections 13-214 and 13-215 shall be collected by the commissioner of insurance and paid over to the city as provided by O.C.G.A. §§ 33-8-8.1 and 33-8-8.2, respectively.
(Ord. No. 872, § 6, 11-16-1988)
Sec. 13-217. - Same—When due and payable.
License fees imposed in sections 13-211, 13-212 and 13-213 shall be due and payable to the director finance on the first day of January, 1989, and on January 1 of each subsequent year and shall become delinquent after March 1 of each year.
(Ord. No. 872, § 7, 11-16-1988)
Sec. 13-218. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Gross direct premiums means gross direct premiums as defined in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4.
Life insurance company means a company which is authorized to transact only the class of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5 as class (1).
(Ord. No. 872, § 8, 11-16-1988)
Sec. 13-219. - Copy of ordinance to insurance commissioner.
The secretary of the city commission is hereby directed to forward a duly certified copy of the ordinance from which this article is derived to the insurance commissioner of the state.
(Ord. No. 872, § 9, 11-16-1988)
Secs. 13-220—13-240. - Reserved.
