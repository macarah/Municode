DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 22-266. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Licensee means a person or an establishment holding a license to engage in the operation of a pool room or a billiard room or tables used for the purpose of playing the game of pool or billiards.
Pool room and billiard room mean any establishment engaged in permitting games played on a table with balls which are impelled by a cue, including all forms of game known as pocket billiards, carom billiards and English billiards. Pool room or billiard room shall mean any place where a pool or billiard table is permitted to be placed in a public place.
Premises means the locality, whether a room, shop or building, wherein billiard or pool tables are located.
(Code 1976, § 6-1071)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 22-267. - Penalty for violation of article.
(a)
Violation of any of the provisions of this article by the holder of the license under this article shall subject the holder of such license to revocation of the license.
(b)
Any person violating any of the provisions of this article, upon conviction in the municipal court, shall be punished as provided in section 1-13.
(Code 1976, §§ 6-1091, 6-1092)
Sec. 22-268. - Hours of operation.
All pool rooms and billiard rooms shall close at or before 12:00 midnight and shall remain closed from such hour until 6:00 a.m.
(Code 1976, § 6-1074; Ord. of 10-18-93(4), § 1)
Sec. 22-269. - Right of entry of police; view of interior.
At all times when operated, pool rooms shall be open to visitation by the police. At all times, whether operated or not, glass panels shall be maintained in all doors used for entrance to such places, and no such window or panel in such place shall be screened, curtained or otherwise maintained so as to interfere with the clear vision from the outside of the pool room or billiard room.
(Code 1976, § 6-1075)
Sec. 22-270. - Condition of premises.
All pool room or billiard room premises shall be kept clean and in proper sanitary condition and in full compliance with the provisions and regulations governing conditions of premises used in operating a public business.
(Code 1976, § 6-1072)
Sec. 22-271. - Conduct on premises.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to permit any disturbance of the peace or obscenity or lewd, immoral or improper entertainment, conduct or practices in any pool room or billiard room.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for the licensee of any billiard room or pool room to permit riotous, disorderly or noisy conduct within such place of business.
(Code 1976, §§ 6-1073, 6-1076)
Sec. 22-272. - Gambling.
No games of chance shall be permitted or any form of gambling allowed in any billiard room or pool room or any other business operated in connection therewith.
(Code 1976, § 6-1077)
Sec. 22-273. - Sale or consumption of intoxicating liquors.
No alcoholic beverages shall be sold, served or allowed to be served in or on the premises of billiard rooms or any place operating in connection therewith unless such premises or establishment is an establishment which is authorized to sell alcoholic beverages and derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross revenues from the sale of products or services other than alcoholic beverages. If alcoholic beverages are sold by the drink for consumption on the premises of a billiard room, no alcoholic beverages shall be sold, served or used therein unless the establishment derives at least 65 percent of its revenue from the sale of products or services other than alcoholic beverages.
(Code 1976, § 6-1078; Ord. of 10-18-93(4), § 2; Ord. of 6-15-98(1), § 1)
Sec. 22-274. - Possession or use of drugs.
(a)
No controlled substance, dangerous drug or drug-related object as defined in O.C.G.A. §§ 16-13-1 and 16-13-21 of any nature whatsoever shall be sold, served or allowed to be used in or on the premises of any pool room or billiard room.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to permit any person, including a customer, to have in his possession any controlled substance, dangerous drug or drug-related object as defined in O.C.G.A. §§ 16-13-1 and 16-13-21.
(Code 1976, §§ 6-1079, 6-1080)
Sec. 22-275. - Employment of minors.
No person who holds a license to operate a pool room or billiard room shall permit any minor to be employed in the business in any capacity whatsoever.
(Code 1976, § 6-1081)
Sec. 22-276. - Employment of persons convicted of certain crimes.
It shall be unlawful to employ in any premises licensed under this article any person who has been convicted of a felony or of illegally dealing in narcotics or drugs, or of any charge relating to the sale of intoxicating liquors.
(Code 1976, § 6-1083)

DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 22-286. - Filing of application.
The police committee shall have authority to prescribe forms for new or renewal applications for a license for operation of a pool room or billiard room. All applicants shall furnish all data, information and records requested of them by the police committee. Failure to furnish such data, information and records within 30 days from the date of such request shall automatically serve to dismiss, with prejudice, the application.
(Code 1976, § 6-1090)
Sec. 22-287. - Public notice of application.
All applicants for new licenses for operation of a pool room or billiard room shall give notice of the making of such application by advertisement at least three times on different days in the Savannah Evening Press. The notice shall contain a particular description of the location of the proposed business and shall give the name of the applicant and the date and time the council will hear the application.
(Code 1976, § 6-1084)
Sec. 22-288. - Criteria for granting of license.
In determining whether or not any license applied for under this division shall be granted, the following shall be considered in the public interest and welfare:
(1)
The applicant's reputation, character and mental and physical capacity to conduct this business;
(2)
Whether or not the applicant has violated any law, regulation or ordinance other than traffic offenses;
(3)
The location for which the license is sought, as to traffic congestion, general character of the neighborhood and the effect such an establishment would have on the adjacent and surrounding property values; and
(4)
The proximity of the premises to any school or church.
(Code 1976, § 6-1082)
Sec. 22-289. - Action by police committee; approval by council.
(a)
All applications for licenses under this division shall be acted upon by the police committee, and its recommendation shall be reported to the council for action by that body. No license shall be issued until it has been approved by the council.
(b)
Licenses issued under this division shall be mere grants or privileges, and the council shall have the discretion to approve or deny the application for a license to operate a pool room or billiard room.
(Code 1976, §§ 6-1085, 6-1088)
Sec. 22-290. - Transfer.
Licenses issued under this division shall not be transferable. In case of the death of any person holding such license or any interest in a license, the license may, in the discretion of the council, be transferred to the administrator, executor or lawful heirs of the deceased person.
(Code 1976, § 6-1089)
Sec. 22-291. - Suspension or revocation.
(a)
No license which has been issued by the city under this division shall be suspended or revoked except for due cause as defined in this section, and after a hearing and upon three days' prior written notice to the holder of such license of the time, place and purpose of such hearing. Due cause for the suspension or revocation of such license shall be as follows:
(1)
The violation of any laws or ordinances regulating such businesses or violation of regulations made pursuant to authority granted for the purpose of regulating such businesses;
(2)
The violation of any state or federal law; or
(3)
The violation of any city ordinance other than traffic ordinances.
(b)
The council shall conduct the hearings held for the purpose of determining whether a license shall be suspended or revoked.
(Code 1976, §§ 6-1086, 6-1087)
