ARTICLE VIII. - RECORDS MANAGEMENT

Sec. 2-597. - Open records.
All municipal records, except those that by order of a state court or by law are prohibited from being open to public inspection, shall be open for personal inspection by any state during the hours of operation of the administrative service herein below prescribed.
Sec. 2-598. - Fees for records and other administrative services.
Fees for photocopies and other media provided to and facsimile transmissions sent or received by town personnel on behalf of and at the request of town residents, as well as fees for other town administrative services, shall be as provided in the town fee schedule.
(Ord. of 8-7-2000, § 2)
Sec. 2-599. - Records management plan; coordinator; retention schedule.
The town council adopts all applicable state law regarding municipal records management plans and records retention. The town clerk shall be the coordinator of the town's records management plan and authorized to act for and on behalf of the mayor and town council in directing and coordinating all records management matters under the Georgia Records Act, O.C.G.A. § 50-18-90 et seq., and records retention schedules approved by the state. The same records shall be maintained in accordance with approved retention schedules. No record shall be destroyed except as authorized by the state records retention schedule.
State law reference— Inspection and copying of public records, O.C.G.A. § 50-18-70 et seq.; records management programs for local governments, O.C.G.A. § 50-18-99.
