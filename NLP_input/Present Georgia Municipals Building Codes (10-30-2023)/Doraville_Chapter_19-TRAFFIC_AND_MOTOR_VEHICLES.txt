ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 19-1. - Definitions.
The following words and phrases when used in this chapter shall for the purpose of this chapter have the following meanings:
Authorized emergency vehicle means vehicles of the fire department, fire patrol and police department, ambulances and such emergency vehicles of municipal, state and federal departments and of public service corporations as are designated by the City Council.
Bicycle means every device propelled by human power upon which any person may ride, having two (2) tandem wheels either of which is over twenty (20) inches in diameter, and including any device generally recognized as a bicycle though equipped with two (2) front or two (2) rear wheels.
Business district means any street length between intersections where fifty (50) percent or more of the frontage is in use for business purposes, or where so designated as such by ordinance.
Central traffic district means all streets and portions of streets within the area so designated as such by ordinance.
Crosswalk means that portion of a roadway ordinarily included within the prolongation or connection of curb and property lines at intersections, or any portion of a roadway clearly indicated for pedestrian crossing by lines or other markings on the surface.
Curb loading zone means a space adjacent to a curb reserved for the exclusive use of vehicles during the loading or unloading of passengers or materials.
Driver or operator means every person who drives or is in actual physical control of a vehicle.
Freight curb loading zone means a space adjacent to a curb for the exclusive use of vehicles during the loading or unloading of freight or passengers.
Intersection means the area embraced within the prolongation or connection of the lateral curb lines, or if none, then of the lateral boundary lines of two (2) or more streets or highways which join one (1) another at an angle, whether or not one (1) such street or highway crosses the other.
Laned roadway means a roadway which is divided into two (2) or more clearly marked lanes for vehicular traffic.
Limited access highway means every highway, street or roadway in respect to which owners or occupants of abutting property or lands and other persons have no legal right of access to or from the same, except at such points only and in such manner as may be determined by the public authority having jurisdiction over such highway, street or roadway.
Motor vehicle means every vehicle which is self-propelled and every vehicle which is propelled by electric power obtained from overhead trolley wires, but not operated upon rails.
Motorcycle means every motor vehicle having a saddle for the use of the rider and designed to travel on not more than three (3) wheels in contact with the ground, but excluding a tractor.
Official time standard, whenever certain hours are named herein, means standard time as may be in current use in this City.
Official traffic control devices means all signs, signals, markings and devices not inconsistent with this chapter placed or erected by authority of the City Council, for the purpose of regulating, warning or guiding traffic.
Park, when prohibited, means the standing of a vehicle, whether occupied or not, otherwise than temporarily for the purpose of and while actually engaged in loading or unloading passengers.
Passenger curb loading zone means a place adjacent to a curb reserved for the exclusive use of vehicles during the loading or unloading of passengers.
Pedestrian means any person afoot, or in a hand-wheeled vehicle.
Police officer means every officer of the police department or any officer authorized to direct or regulate traffic or to make arrests for violations of traffic regulations.
Private road or driveway means every way or place in private ownership and used for vehicular travel by the owner and those having express or implied permission from the owner, but not by other persons.
Railroad means a carrier of persons or property upon cars, other than streetcars, operated upon stationary rails.
Recreational vehicle means any vehicle or structure that is built on a single chassis; four hundred (400) square feet or less when measured at the largest horizontal projection; designed to be self-propelled or towable by a light duty vehicle; and designed primarily not for use as a permanent dwelling but as temporary living quarters for recreational, camping, or travel. Types of recreational vehicles include campers, fifth-wheel trailers, travel trailers, pop-up campers, transport trailers, mobile offices, and boat trailers. The definition of recreational vehicle explicitly omits any vehicle or structure defined as a utility trailer.
Residence district means any street length between street intersections where more than fifty (50) percent of the frontage is in use for residence purposes, or where so designated as such by ordinance.
Right-of-way means the privilege of the immediate use of the roadway.
Roadway means that portion of a street or highway improved, designed or ordinarily used for vehicular travel. In the event a highway uses two (2) or more separate roadways, the term "roadway" as used herein shall refer to any such roadway separately but not to all such roadways collectively.
Safety zone means the area or space officially set apart within a roadway for the exclusive use of pedestrians and which is protected or is so marked or indicated by adequate signs as to be plainly visible at all times while set apart as a safety zone.
Sidewalk means that portion of a street between the curblines, or the lateral lines of a roadway, and the adjacent property line, intended for the use of pedestrians.
Slow means a speed not in excess of fifteen (15) miles per hour.
Stop, when required, means complete cessation of movement.
Stop, stopping or standing, when prohibited, means any stopping or standing of a vehicle, whether occupied or not, except when necessary to avoid conflict with other traffic or in compliance with the directions of a police officer or traffic control sign or signal.
Street or highway means the entire width between the boundary lines of every way publicly maintained when any part thereof is open to the use of the public for purposes of vehicular traffic.
Through highway means every street or highway or portion thereof at the entrances to which vehicular traffic from intersecting streets or highways is required by law to stop before entering or crossing the same and when stop signs are erected as provided in this chapter.
Traffic means pedestrians, ridden or herded animals, vehicles and other conveyances either singly or together while using any street for purposes of travel.
Traffic control signal means any device, whether manually, electrically or mechanically operated, by which traffic is directed.
Traffic division means the police department of this City or that portion thereof which may be so designated by ordinance.
Trailer means every vehicle without motive power designed for carrying persons or property and for being drawn by a motor vehicle.
Truck means any vehicle designed and used principally for carrying things other than passengers and having a state truck license.
Utility trailer means any wheeled structure, without motive power, designed to be attached and towed by a motor vehicle and which is generally and commonly used to carry and transport personal effects and/or property. The definition of utility trailer includes but is not limited to cargo, carry-on, horse, or storage trailers.
Vehicle means every device in, upon, or by which any person or property is or may be transported or drawn upon a highway, except devices moved by human power or used exclusively upon stationary rails or tracks.
(Code 1969, § 17-1; Ord. No. 2020-040 , § I, 1-20-21)
Sec. 19-2. - Designation of traffic control areas.
The City Council may by a majority vote at any regular or special meeting, by Resolution:
(1)
Determine and designate one-way streets and no parking areas.
(2)
Establish traffic control devices or designations and designate and maintain automated red light enforcement devices.
(3)
Establish through streets.
(4)
Determine and designate intersections where particular hazards exist.
(5)
Determine whether motor vehicles shall stop on one (1) or more streets at any stop intersection. A stop sign shall be erected at every such place where a stop is required.
(Code 1969, § 17-2; Ord. No. 04-20, § 1, 9-20-04)
State Law reference— Powers of local authorities, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-371.
Sec. 19-3. - Abatement of unsafe vehicles as nuisances.
(a)
The use of any vehicle on the streets is a nuisance where, by reason of a defect in such vehicle, the driving thereof on the streets constitutes a menace or a hazard to the life, safety or property of others. The police department and the members thereof are charged with the duty of enforcing this section.
(b)
Where it is shown that any vehicle on the street is dangerous or likely to cause injury to persons or property, the police shall cause notice to be given to the owner or other person to remove such vehicle from the streets or, if the vehicle can be made safe by repair, to repair it. In the event such person refuses to obey such a notice, a summons shall be issued requiring him to be and appear in the municipal court to show cause why such vehicle should not be declared a nuisance and abated.
(c)
If on the hearing the municipal court judge finds from the evidence that such vehicle is a nuisance within the meaning of this section, he shall enter an order so declaring and stating the terms of the abatement and the conditions on which such vehicle may be driven upon the streets.
(Code 1969, § 17-3)
Sec. 19-4. - Vehicles damaging streets prohibited.
No vehicle shall be operated on the streets with cleated wheels, or so constructed in any other way as to damage the street.
(Code 1969, § 17-4)
Sec. 19-5. - Projecting loads.
No vehicle shall be allowed the use of the streets with a load projecting more than four (4) feet in the rear of the body of such vehicle or more than one (1) foot to either side of the vehicle, unless such load has a red flag at least twelve (12) inches square attached to the extreme ends of such projecting load during the daylight hours, and illuminated red light or lantern securely attached to the projecting load during the night hours.
(Code 1969, § 17-5)
Sec. 19-6. - Play streets designated.
The Council may designate by resolution any street or part thereof a play street and place appropriate signs or devices in the roadway indicating and helping to protect the same.
(Code 1969, § 17-6)
Sec. 19-7. - Use of coasters, roller skates and similar devices restricted.
(a)
No person upon roller skates, skateboards or other similar nonmotorized toys, or riding in or by means of any coaster, toy vehicle, or similar device, shall go upon any roadway except while crossing a street on a crosswalk and when so coasting such person shall be granted all of the rights and shall be subject to all of the duties applicable to pedestrians. This section shall not apply upon any street while set aside as a play street as authorized by ordinance of this City.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for persons so skating or riding to gather in crowds or to create noise or be guilty of any disorderly conduct, or to take hold of or hang on to any automobile or other vehicle, or in any way to interfere with the natural progress of automobiles or other vehicles along and in the streets of the City, or for two (2) or more persons to join hands while skating on the sidewalks. Any persons so skating or riding shall give right-of-way to, and in no way interfere with, persons walking on the sidewalks.
(c)
Any persons violating any provisions of this section shall be subject to the penalties herein provided for violation of this Code. Provided, however, that any such offender under the age of sixteen (16) years shall be dealt with as provided by the state law creating juvenile courts as contained in O.C.G.A. § 15-11-1 et seq. The parent of any child and the guardian of the person of any ward shall not authorize or knowingly permit any such child or ward to violate any of the provisions of this section.
(Code 1969, § 17-7)
Sec. 19-8. - Driving on play streets.
Whenever authorized signs are erected indicating any street or part thereof as a play street, no person shall drive a vehicle upon any such street or portion thereof except drivers of vehicles having business or whose residences are within such closed area, and then any such driver shall exercise the highest degree of care in driving upon any such street or portion thereof, and at no time shall such driver's speed exceed five (5) miles per hour.
(Code 1969, § 17-43)
Sec. 19-9. - Obedience to police.
No person shall willfully fail or refuse to comply with any lawful order, signal or direction of a police officer.
(Code 1969, § 17-15)
State Law reference— Obedience to authorized person directing traffic, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-2.
Sec. 19-10. - Public employees to obey traffic regulations.
The provisions of this chapter shall apply to the driver of any vehicle owned by or used in the service of the United States government, any state, county or City. It shall be unlawful for any such driver to violate any of the provisions of this chapter except as otherwise permitted in this chapter or by state statute.
(Code 1969, § 17-17)
Sec. 19-11. - Exemptions for authorized emergency vehicles.
(a)
The provisions of this chapter regulating the operation, parking, and standing of vehicles shall apply to authorized emergency vehicles. A driver when operating any such vehicle in an emergency, except when otherwise directed by a police officer, may:
(1)
Park or stand notwithstanding the provisions of this chapter.
(2)
Proceed past a red or stop signal or stop sign but only after slowing down as may be necessary for safe operation.
(3)
Exceed the speed limits so long as he does not endanger life or property.
(4)
Disregard regulations governing direction of movement or turning in specified directions so long as he does not endanger life or property.
(b)
Those exemptions hereinbefore granted in reference to the movement of an authorized emergency vehicle shall apply only when the driver of such vehicle sounds a horn, siren, bell or exhaust whistle as may be reasonably necessary, and the vehicle displays a lighted red lamp visible as provided in this chapter from the front as a warning to others.
(c)
The foregoing exemptions shall not, however, protect the driver of any such vehicle from the consequences of his reckless disregard of the safety of others.
(Code 1969, § 17-18)
Sec. 19-12. - Truck restriction zones.
(a)
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this section for any person to operate a truck or commercial vehicle over eighteen (18) tons gross weight or thirty (30) feet in length upon Chestnut Drive, Oakcliff Road, Stewart Road, Park Avenue, Central Avenue, Shallowford Road from Buford Highway north of its intersection with New Peachtree Road and Tilly Mill Road from Flowers Road to Woodwin Road at any time, and on Woodwin Road from 6:30 a.m. to 8:30 a.m. and 1:30 p.m. to 4:30 p.m. Truck traffic shall be permitted from the hours of 7:00 a.m. to 7:00 p.m. on the following streets: Winters Chapel Road from the intersection of Woodwin Road northerly to the City limits line, except that such vehicles may be operated thereon for the purpose of delivering or picking up materials or merchandise, and then only entering such street at the intersection nearest the destination of the vehicle and proceeding thereon no further than the nearest intersection thereafter.
(b)
The City shall cause signs to be erected at sufficient distances from the above-referenced truck restriction zones to alert motorists of the zone so that alternate routes can be taken by drivers of the trucks which are prohibited.
(Ord. No. 96-4, § 1, 3-18-96; Ord. No. 2014-10, § 1, 10-6-14)

ARTICLE II. - OPERATION OF VEHICLES

Sec. 19-36. - Uniform rules of the road adopted.
(a)
Adoption by reference. Pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-372 through 40-6-376, the Uniform Rules of the Road, O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-1 through 40-6-395 as amended from time to time, and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 as amended from time to time are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of this City with like effect as if recited herein.
(b)
Penalties. Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this article shall be punished in accordance with section 1-12.
Sec. 19-37. - Signal to leave parking space.
The operator of a parked vehicle, before he starts the vehicle, shall signal his intention to leave the parking space by giving the arm signal for making a left turn.
(Code 1969, § 17-36)
Sec. 19-38. - Towing.
No vehicle shall tow more than one (1) other vehicle, nor shall the connection between vehicles exceed sixteen (16) feet in length.
(Code 1969, § 17-50)
Sec. 19-39. - U-turns.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for an operator of any motor vehicle to turn such vehicle so as to proceed in an opposite direction, or to make a U-turn, at any interchange or intersection formed by the intersection of U.S. 23, known as Buford Highway, and any entrance or exit way into or out of Treasure Island Department Store and the tract of property upon which it is located in the 5600 block of Buford Highway in the City.
(b)
The chief of police is authorized and empowered to erect and maintain "No U-Turn" signs at any intersection or interchange described above to advise motorists of the prohibitions of this section.
(Ord. No. 190-1, §§ 1, 2, 4-18-77)
State Law reference— U-turns, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-121.

Sec. 19-62. - Certain parking prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to park, leave unattended, or abandon any motor vehicle, recreational vehicle, utility trailer, or any vehicle of any type on any public street within the City under such conditions that:
(1)
The vehicle or utility trailer, where parked or left, shall cause a health, safety or traffic hazard, or threat or injury to any person or property.
(2)
The vehicle or utility trailer, where parked, blocks, hinders, or prevents access to any public or private driveway in the City.
(Ord. No. 216, § I, 9-7-82; Ord. No. 2020-040 , § II, 1-20-21)
Sec. 19-63. - On-street parking requirements applying to R-1 and R-2 residential districts.
(a)
These provisions apply only where on-street parking is permitted in R-1 and R-2 residential districts by this chapter.
(b)
Parking on any public street or highway, on streets permitting parking by this chapter, shall be limited to vehicles with a gross vehicle weight rating (GVWR) of six thousand (6,000) pounds or less.
(c)
The on-street parking of utility trailers is not permitted.
(d)
No inoperable vehicle shall be parked or stored on any public street or highway for a period longer than three (3) days, in accordance with section 19-106. This includes anything meeting the definition of recreational vehicle or utility trailer that may not be propelled by power.
(e)
Unregistered vehicles shall be prohibited from being parked or stored on any public street or highway.
(f)
Open outdoor storage, as defined in section 5-29, shall be prohibited.
(g)
Exemptions. Restrictions on vehicles larger than six thousand (6,000) pounds regulated by this section shall not apply when:
(1)
Vehicle is temporarily parked for the purpose of active loading/unloading;
(2)
Vehicle is designed or intended for the transport of the physically impaired;
(3)
Vehicle is being used for active construction, or other professional service being performed on an adjacent property;
(4)
Vehicle is used by law enforcement or other emergency agency;
(5)
During federal or other major holidays; [or]
(6)
When parked for use of a City park during normal park hours.
(h)
Enforcement.
(1)
Warning. For the first violation within a thirty-day period, a warning will be issued.
(2)
Citations issued. For two (2) or more violations in a thirty-day period, a citation will be issued for each subsequent violation beyond the first. Every twenty-four-hour period cars are parked in violation of this section will be considered a separate and distinct violation subject to a separate citation for each violation.
(3)
Accumulation of offenses is reset thirty (30) days from each initial warning of a violation. A maximum of three (3) warnings may be given within a twelve-month period.
(Ord. No. 220, §§ I, II, 9-6-83; Ord. No. 03-02, § 1, 3-3-03; Ord. No. 03-06, § 1, 6-2-03; Ord. No. 05-08, § 1, 3-21-05; Ord. No. 06-05, § 1, 2-6-06; Ord. No. 06-12, § 1, 4-4-06; Ord. No. 2020-040 , § III, 1-20-21)

Sec. 19-64. - Obstructing traffic at shopping center on Longmire Drive, Buford Highway.
(a)
It is hereby declared that the entrance and exit ways into and out of the shopping center currently occupied by Home Depot, T J Maxx, Pharmor, and Pike Nurseries, located on and emptying onto Longmire Drive and Buford Highway respectively, located within the City, are hazardous areas, and the traffic congestion and blockage created by their use pose a serious threat to vehicular users of Longmire Drive and Buford Highway; and it is declared necessary for the protection and benefit of the travelling public to regulate and control traffic in these areas.

(c)
It shall be unlawful for any operator of any motor vehicle operating a vehicle upon Buford Highway or Clearview Drive, in the areas designated above, to stop, stand, or park such vehicle in front of such entrance-exit ways so as to block, impede, or otherwise impair the flow of traffic either into or out of the premises referred to above. It shall further be unlawful for any operator of any motor vehicle using the exit-entrance ways to impede, obstruct, or otherwise impair the flow of traffic on either Buford Highway or Clearview Drive.
(Ord. No. 190, §§ I—III, 9-7-76)

Any agent, servant or employee of a railroad which may be charged with a violation of this article who causes or participates in a violation of this article or allows the same to be done shall be guilty of a violation and subject to punishment therefor.
(Code 1969, § 17-146; Ord. No. 212, § II, 10-19-81)

Service of summons for the offense may be served, either within or without the City, upon an agent, servant or employee of the railroad charged with an offense under this article.

The railroad and any of its agents, servants and employees may be separately guilty of a violation of this article and a charge against one (1) shall not preclude a charge against the others.
(Code 1969, § 17-148; Ord. No. 212, § IV, 10-19-81)
Sec. 19-140. - Standing at grade crossing limited.
No train shall stand at rest upon any grade crossing for a longer period than five (5) minutes at any one (1) time, nor shall any train or part thereof stand at rest at such position as to obstruct any grade crossing for a longer period. This section shall apply to crossings on Flowers Road and Winters Chapel Road as well as other crossings within the City limits.
(Code 1969, § 17-149; Ord. No. 212, § V, 10-19-81)
Sec. 19-141. - Speed limit.
It is unlawful for any person to operate a train within the City limits at a speed of more than fifty (50) miles per hour upon any railroad, track or rail.
(Code 1969, § 17-150; Ord. No. 212, § VI, 10-19-81)

It shall be unlawful to move any train, engine or cars over any of the streets or avenues described in this article unless the signaling devices, when installed, are operating so as to give warning of the approach thereof or unless a flagman is provided to give warning of such movement; provided, however, that this section shall not apply to through trains on the main line of the Southern Railroad.

It shall be unlawful for any train, engine, switch engine, freight car, passenger car or coach, or any part of such train, to be backed over, onto, or across a public street or public road crossing (surface crossing) within the City without a member of the train crew being stationed upon the rear end thereof for the purpose of keeping a lookout and provided with ample means of giving warning to persons upon such crossings and to the person in immediate control of such operation.
(Code 1969, § 17-152; Ord. No. 212, § VIII, 10-19-81)
Sec. 19-144. - Blocking crossings.
It shall be unlawful for any member of a train crew or any other agent or employee of a railway company having control over the stopping, starting, placing or movement of a railway train or trains, railway cars or railway locomotives to permit any part of a public surface crossing over any railway tracks within the City to become and remain blocked by a train at rest, or by any car or locomotive at rest, for longer than five (5) minutes.
(Code 1969, § 17-153; Ord. No. 212, § IX, 10-19-81)

Railway trains, parts of trains, and cars and locomotives shall not be left standing or in motionless position so near to public crossings as to obscure the vision of persons upon such crossings and prevent such persons being able to see approaching trains or parts thereof, or locomotives, for a distance of thirty-five (35) yards in either direction of such crossings.
(Code 1969, § 17-154; Ord. No. 212, § X, 10-19-81)

(Ord. No. 150, § 2, 10-5-70)

ARTICLE VIII. - STREET RACING AND RECKLESS DRIVING EXHIBITIONS

Sec. 19-170. - Definitions.
[The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:]
Illegal street racing and reckless driving exhibition shall mean a motor vehicle speed contest using public highways, streets, or rights-of-way in violation of applicable motor vehicle and traffic laws, including, without limitation, O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-186, 40-6-251 and 40-6-390. Illegal street racing and reckless driving exhibitions may include, but are not limited to, situations in which: (1) a group of motor vehicles or individuals has arrived at a location for the purpose of participating in the event; (2) a group of individuals has gathered on private property open to the general public without the consent of the owner, operator, or agent thereof for the purpose of participating in the event; (3) one (1) or more individuals has impeded the free public use of a public street, sidewalk or highway by actions, words, or physical barriers for the purpose of conducting the event; (4) two (2) or more vehicles have lined up with motors running for an illegal motor vehicle speed contest or exhibition of speed; (5) one (1) or more drivers is revving his engine or spinning his tires in preparation for the event; or (6) an individual is stationed at or near one (1) or more motor vehicles serving as a race starter.
Organizer shall mean any individual who in any manner knowingly takes part in the planning, organization, coordination, facilitation, advertising or sharing of the location for any such illegal street race, or collect moneys in connection with an illegal street racing exhibition, as defined herein.
Participant shall mean any individual who is knowingly present at an illegal street racing exhibition for the purpose of actively taking part in the event, through conduct including riding in a race vehicle as a passenger; assisting the organizers and/or drivers in carrying out or promoting the event; taking video or photographic images for purpose of promoting and/or profiting from the event (also known as a "spectator"); or exchanging money or anything of value with any driver, car owner, organizer or other participant in connection with the event. For the purpose of this definition, a person who is a mere bystander, passerby, or observer not aware of the illegal activity shall not be deemed a participant.
( Ord. No. 2020-032 , § 1, 11-9-20)
Sec. 19-171. - Violations.
(a)
No person shall knowingly act as an organizer of an illegal street racing or reckless driving exhibition, as defined herein.
(b)
Except as provided elsewhere in this article, no person shall knowingly act as a participant in, or spectator of, an illegal street racing or reckless driving exhibition, as defined herein.
(c)
Exemption. Nothing in this article prohibits law enforcement officers or their agents from being participants or spectators at illegal street racing or reckless driving exhibitions in the course of their official duties.
(d)
This article shall not apply to licensed or duly authorized racetracks, drag strips, other designated areas set aside by proper authorities for such purposes.
( Ord. No. 2020-032 , § 1, 11-9-20)
Sec. 19-172. - Penalties.
Violations of this article shall be punished as follows:
(1)
Organizers and those driving motor vehicles shall be punished by a fine of one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) and court costs, or imprisonment for not more than six (6) months,
(2)
All other participants or spectators shall be punished by a fine of up to one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) and court costs, or imprisonment for not more than six (6) months, subject to all limitations contained in the Charter of the City or applicable state law.
( Ord. No. 2020-032 , § 1, 11-9-20)
Sec. 19-173. - Proving a violation.
(a)
Relevant circumstances to prove a violation. Notwithstanding any other provision of law, to prove a violation of this article, admissible evidence may include, but is not limited to, any of the following:
(1)
The time of day;
(2)
The nature and description of the scene;
(3)
The number of people at the scene;
(4)
The location of the individual charged in relation to any individual or group present at the scene;
(5)
The number and description of motor vehicles at the scene;
(6)
That the individual charged drove or was transported to the scene;
(7)
That the individual charged has previously participated in an illegal street racing or reckless driving exhibition;
(8)
That the individual charged has previously aided and abetted an illegal street racing or reckless driving exhibition;
(9)
That the individual charged has previously organized an illegal street racing or reckless driving exhibition; or
(10)
That the individual charged previously was present at a location where preparations were being made for an illegal street racing or reckless driving exhibition or where an illegal street racing or reckless driving exhibition was in progress.
( Ord. No. 2020-032 , § 1, 11-9-20)
