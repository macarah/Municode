ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 5-1. - Bird sanctuary.
(a)
The city is hereby declared to be a bird sanctuary.
(b)
The civic, service and garden clubs of the city are hereby authorized to place at the city limits of the highways leading into the city suitable plaques, markers, or other types of visual publicity mediums of reasonable size designating that the city is a bird sanctuary.
(c)
It shall be unlawful to molest, intimidate, wound or slaughter wild birds by shooting, throwing, trapping or robbing the nests of wild birds in the city.
(Code 1968, §§ 5-25, 5-26)
Sec. 5-2. - Livestock at large—Generally.
Except as may be otherwise provided in this chapter, the running at large within the city of horses, mules, cattle, sheep, goats, hogs and other livestock is hereby declared a nuisance and unlawful, and it shall be unlawful to permit the same to be at large.
(Code 1968, § 5-1)
State Law reference— Livestock at large, O.C.G.A. § 4-3-1 et seq.
Sec. 5-3. - Same—Poultry.
It shall be unlawful for the owner of any poultry to allow such poultry to run at large on another's property or on and in the streets or public ways of the city.
(Code 1968, § 5-17)
Sec. 5-4. - Keeping of horses.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to have, keep or maintain in any manner whatsoever any horses within the city except as specifically set out below:
(1)
All horses housed or quartered within the city as of January 11, 1988 shall be allowed to remain at their present location provided that the horse was properly registered at city hall within sixty (60) days of January 11, 1988. For the purpose of this section, "properly registered" shall be construed to mean that the owner has filed the following documentation with city hall:
a.
A color photograph sufficient to identify the horse;
b.
The name of the owner and the specific location at which the horse is quartered; and
c.
If the horse is a registered horse, the owner shall provide the registration number and a copy of the registration certificate; or,
d.
If the horse is not a registered horse, certification from a veterinarian stating the age and sex of the horse.
(2)
All horses shall be kept and maintained only in areas within the city zoned A-1.
(3)
If any animal was pregnant, then the registration certificate shall also contain certification of such fact by a veterinarian. Otherwise, offspring born after January 11, 1988 shall be prohibited from remaining in any area of the city except A-1.
(b)
For purposes of this section "horse" shall be construed to also mean mule, donkey, burro or any other similar animal.
(Code 1968, § 5-2; Ord. of 1-11-88)
Sec. 5-5. - Keeping of cows.
It shall be unlawful for any person to have, keep and maintain a cow within the city.
(Code 1968, § 5-3)
Sec. 5-6. - Keeping of swine.
The having, keeping or maintaining of any swine in the city is prohibited.
(Code 1968, § 5-4)
Sec. 5-7. - Keeping of poultry.
(a)
The owner of poultry may keep and maintain the same in the city provided such poultry is completely enclosed in a pen or cage on the owner's property, and provided further that said pen or cage is cleaned and sanitized regularly and kept in a sanitary condition in order not to offend adjoining property owners by smell, and not to become a nuisance or a menace to the health and welfare of the residents of the city.
(b)
The term "poultry" shall mean domestic fowl which are propagated and fattened for the table, and for their eggs, feathers, and related items, including, although not excluding any other, chickens, turkeys, ducks, guinea fowl, geese, quail, and pheasants.
(Code 1968, §§ 5-18, 5-21)
Sec. 5-8. - Finding, abatement of nuisance.
In addition to the other remedies provided, any citizen of the city may file a complaint with the council to abate a nuisance, and if the council, after a hearing, decide a nuisance is created, it may abate same.
(Code 1968, § 5-24)
Sec. 5-9. - Impoundment—Authorized sale.
Upon violation of section 5-2 or section 5-3, the chief of police or any police officer of the city shall take possession of livestock or poultry, impound the same, and charge the owner daily an amount sufficient to defray the cost of keeping the same. Upon giving the owner, if the owner may be found, written notice of the impounding, the officer may, after seven (7) days, sell the same at public outcry to the highest bidder if the owner fails to claim same and pay the cost of the impounding, and if the owner cannot be found the sale shall be made without notice.
(Code 1968, §§ 5-20, 5-22)
State Law reference— Impoundment of livestock at large, O.C.G.A. § 4-3-4.
Sec. 5-10. - Same—Fees and charges.
(a)
There shall be levied and collected an impounding fee on cows, horses, mules, hogs and poultry of ten dollars ($10.00) each.
(b)
In addition to the impounding fees prescribed in subsection (a) of this section, the city shall impose and collect daily charges for the care of such impounded animals, sufficient to defray the cost thereof.
(Code 1968, §§ 5-6, 5-22)
Secs. 5-11—5-19. - Reserved.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 5-20. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the following terms shall have the respective meaning ascribed to them:
Animal control officer: An employee of the sanitation department of the city whose duties specifically include enforcement of the provisions of this article.
Dangerous dog: Any dog that, according to the records of an appropriate authority:
(1)
Inflicts a severe injury on a human being without provocation, on public or private property; or
(2)
Aggressively bites, attacks, or endangers the safety of humans without provocation after the dog has been classified as a potentially dangerous dog and after the owner has been notified of such classification.
Dog control officer: The director of the sanitation department, and such employees of the animal control division of the sanitation department acting under the director's supervision and control.
Free-running line: A line which is attached to or is secured by a swivel or other device which will prevent the line from being wrapped around some object and will protect animal from becoming entangled.
Nuisance: Whatever is dangerous or detrimental to human life or health and whatever renders or tends to render the soil, air, water or food impure or unwholesome or unreasonably offends or impairs the senses of smell, sight and hearing.
Owner: Any person owning, possessing, harboring, keeping, or having custody or control of a dog or cat within the territorial boundaries of the city.
Potentially dangerous dog: Any dog which has without provocation, bitten a human being on public or private property.
Proper enclosure: An enclosure for keeping a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog, while on the owner's property, securely confined indoors or in a securely enclosed and locked pen, fence, or structure suitable to prevent the entry of young children and designed to prevent the dog from escaping. Any such pen or structure shall have secure sides and a secure top, and, if the dog is enclosed within a fence, all sides of the fence shall be of sufficient height and the bottom of the fence shall be constructed or secured in such a manner as to prevent the dog's escape either from over or from under the fence. Any such enclosure shall also provide protection from the elements for the dog.
Protection from elements: Keeping animal in a manner which ensures that he/she can seek shelter from rain, cold and inclement conditions without being constantly exposed to those conditions.
Rabies control officer: An employee of the county board of health whose specific duties include enforcement of the rabies control rules and regulations of the county.
Records of an appropriate authority: Records of any state, county, or municipal law enforcement agency, records of any county or municipal animal control agency; records of any county board of health; records of any federal, state, or local court; or records maintained by the dog control officer pursuant to this article.
Severe injury: Any physical injury that results in broken bones or disfiguring lacerations requiring multiple sutures or cosmetic surgery or a physical injury that results in death.
(Code 1968, § 4-30; Ord. of 1-9-89, § 1; Ord. of 8-25-98(2); Ord. of 11-8-10)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 5-21. - Preventing nuisance; restraint of dogs; impounding authorized.
(a)
The owner or keeper of each dog or cat within the city shall keep it from becoming a nuisance and from endangering or injuring any person. Dogs shall at all times:
(1)
Be kept within a proper enclosure from which it cannot escape;
(2)
Be restrained upon the owner's property by a substantial chain, cable, rope or other device from which it cannot escape so as to not reach any adjoining property or public right-of-way; or
(3)
Be restrained on a leash and accompanied by the owner or other responsible person.
(4)
Except in areas authorized for agriculture, the maximum number of dogs that may be kept on any single premise shall not exceed five (5); (exception: licensed veterinary hospitals, kennels and grooming parlors.) Dogs kept in excess of five (5) shall be presumed to create a nuisance in violation of this Code section.
(b)
Whenever a dog or cat is kept in such manner, as constitutes a nuisance, a proceeding to abate the nuisance may be brought by the party aggrieved, pursuant to law. An animal found by the court to constitute a nuisance may be impounded by the city if the owner, after reasonable opportunity to do so, fails to abate the nuisance as ordered by the court.
(c)
Whenever a dog is allowed to roam free, in violation of this section, impoundment of the animal is authorized. Upon identification of the owner, a citation shall be issued for violation of this section.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any owner of a dog not to furnish for such dog adequate food, water, shelter and protection from the elements, all which shall be accessible to the dog at all times.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for any person to treat any cat or dog in a cruel and inhumane manner, to abandon it, or to release it upon public or private property with the intention of abandoning it.
(f)
It shall be unlawful for any person to secure a dog by leash less than ten (10) feet in length which is not free running as herein defined.
(Code 1968, §§ 5-45, 5-46; Ord. of 1-9-89, § 1; Ord. of 8-25-98(2); Ord. of 11-8-10)
State Law reference— Confinement of female dogs in heat, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-6.
Sec. 5-22. - Inoculation; registration; tagging of animals.
(a)
All dogs and cats shall be inoculated against rabies in accordance with the procedures set forth in the rabies control rules and regulations of the county, as promulgated and from time to time amended by the county board of health, which by reference are expressly incorporated as a part of this section as if fully set out herein. Violation of such rules and regulations may be enforced by the rabies control officer in the municipal court, upon citation, as an offense against the city.
(b)
Between January first and March first of each year, the owner of each dog and cat shall present to the city a current certificate of vaccination, and complete the registration form provided by the city, giving therein the full name, address, and telephone number of the owner, breed and sex of the animal, type of animal, and number of the current vaccination tag. Upon payment of a fee in the amount of two dollars ($2.00), and completion of the foregoing requirements, the city shall issue to the owner a certificate of registration. All certificates shall be valid until March first of the following calendar year; provided, however, no certification of registration shall be transferable. It shall be the duty of the owner to notify the city, within twenty-four (24) hours, if the dog or cat dies, is sold or transferred. If sold or transferred to a new owner within the city, the name, address and telephone number of the new owner shall be provided.
(c)
Owners acquiring or bringing animals into the city after March first and before January first of the following year shall immediately cause the animal to be registered with the city in the manner provided by subsection (b) of this section.
(d)
The numbered vaccination tag, issued by a licensed veterinarian upon inoculation, shall be attached to a collar or harness and worn by the animal at all times. Every dog found roaming or running free, whether tagged or untagged, shall be impounded; every untagged cat found roaming or running free shall be impounded. If a tagged animal is impounded, and it is found not to be registered or its current registration has expired, the owner may be cited for violation of this section.
(Code 1968, §§ 5-37—5-44; Ord. of 1-9-89, § 1)
State Law reference— Rabies control, O.C.G.A. § 31-19-1 et seq.
Sec. 5-23. - Impoundment procedure; publication; disposition of unclaimed animals.
(a)
All dogs and cats impounded by the city shall be held in a humane manner. The animal control division shall make a diligent effort to ascertain the owner of the animal; and shall give the owner prompt notice of the fact of impoundment. Notice shall be given by the best means possible to assure actual receipt by the owner. For purposes of this section, written notice to the owner by certified mail, at the address shown on the most current registration form shall be deemed complete upon its mailing. If the owner is personally served, the notice may be left with a person of responsible age residing at the address, or conspicuously tacked on the main entrance thereof. The notice shall advise the owner that the animal will be held for a period of ten (10) days, exclusive of the date of service, and thereafter the animal may be disposed of in accordance with this section. The notice shall state the location and hours of the facility where the animal may be reclaimed, and shall specify the cost for reclaiming the animal.
(b)
The animal control office shall charge and assess such fees for pick-up and impoundment as set forth in a schedule approved by the governing body, as may be from time to time amended. If the owner cannot show proof of current rabies vaccination, the animal shall be vaccinated by a licensed veterinarian before release, and the cost thereof collected from the owner.
(c)
Any impounded animal, except a "dangerous dog" or "potentially dangerous dog" as defined in this article, may be disposed of on or after the fifth day after date of service of notice on the owner if the owner is known or publication if the owner is unknown. Disposal of animals shall be made by adoption or by destruction in an expedient and humane manner. Animals may be held beyond the period stated if circumstances warrant.
(d)
If any dead dog or cat is found upon the public right-of-way, the city shall remove and dispose of the animal and charge the owner, if known, a fee of five dollars ($5.00) for such service.
(Code 1968, §§ 5-47, 5-48; Ord. of 1-9-89, § 1)
Sec. 5-24. - Violations generally.
In addition to the fees and charges imposed in this article, any person owning or possessing any dog or cat within the city who shall be adjudged guilty of violation of this article pertaining to such person's duties with reference to confining such dog or cat and the registration of such dog or cat as provided in this article or guilty of a violation of any other duty imposed upon the owner or possessor of any dog or cat in the city by the terms of this article, shall be punished as provided in section 1-8.
(Code 1968, § 5-55)
Secs. 5-25—5-29. - Reserved.

Sec. 5-30. - Investigations; finding; notice; hearing; registration; procedure.
(a)
Upon receiving a report of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog within the dog control officer's jurisdiction from a law enforcement agency, animal control agency, rabies control officer, or county board of health, the dog control officer shall make such investigations and inquiries with regard to such report as may be necessary to carry out the provisions of this division. Whenever the dog control officer classifies a dog as a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog, or reclassifies a potentially dangerous dog as a dangerous dog, notice shall be given the dog's owner in writing by certified mail to the owner's last known address of such classification or reclassification. Such notice shall be deemed complete upon its mailing.
(b)
The notice to the owner shall meet the following requirements:
(1)
The notice shall be in writing and mailed by certified mail to the owner's last known address;
(2)
The notice shall include a summary of the dog control officer's finding that formed the basis for the dog's classification as a dangerous or potentially dangerous dog;
(3)
The notice shall be dated and shall state that the owner, within fifteen (15) days after the date shown on the notice, has a right to request a hearing on the dog control officer's determination that the dog is a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog before the governing authority;
(4)
The notice shall state that if a hearing is not requested, the dog control officer's determination that the dog is a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog will become effective for all purposes under this section on the day after the last day on which the owner has a right to request a hearing; and
(5)
The notice shall include a form to request a hearing before the governing authority and shall provide specific instructions on mailing or delivering said form to the city.
(c)
When the governing authority receives a request for a hearing it shall schedule such hearing within thirty (30) days after receiving the request. The governing authority shall notify the dog owner in writing by certified mail of the date, time and place of the hearing. At the hearing, the owner of the dog shall be given the opportunity to testify and present evidence and in addition thereto the governing authority shall receive such other evidence and hear such other testimony as the governing authority may find reasonably necessary to make a determination either to sustain, modify, or overrule the dog control officer's classification of the dog. Within ten (10) days after the date of the hearing, the governing authority shall notify the owner in writing by certified mail of its determination on the matter. If such determination is that the dog is a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog, the notice shall specify it is effective on the third day following the date of receipt by the owner.
(d)
Subject to the requirements of this section, the dog control officer shall issue a certificate of registration to the owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog if the owner presents to the dog control officer or if the dog control officer finds sufficient evidence of:
(1)
A proper enclosure to confine the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog;
(2)
The posting of the premises where the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog is located with a clearly visible sign warning that there is a dangerous dog on the premises. The sign required to be posted must substantially conform to the design provided by the state department of natural resources; and
(3)
Payment to the city of a fee in the amount of fifty dollars ($50.00) to defray the cost of administering and enforcing this section.
(e)
The owner of a dangerous dog shall present evidence to the dog control officer of:
(1)
A policy of insurance in the amount of at least fifteen thousand dollars ($15,000.00) issued by an insurer authorized to transact business within the State of Georgia insuring the owner of the dog against liability for any personal injuries inflicted by the dangerous dog; or
(2)
A surety bond in the amount of fifteen thousand dollars ($15,000.00) or more issued by a surety company authorized to transact business in this state payable to any person or persons injured by the dangerous dog.
(f)
Certificates of registration shall be renewed on an annual basis. At the time of an annual renewal of a certificate of registration, a dog control officer shall require evidence from the owner or make such investigation as may be necessary to verify that the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog is continuing to be confined in a proper enclosure and that the owner is continuing to comply with other provisions of this article.
(g)
Issuance of a certificate of registration or the renewal of a certificate of registration by the local government does not warrant or guarantee that the requirements specified in subparagraph (d) of this section are maintained by the owner of a dangerous dog of potentially dangerous dog on a continuous basis following the date of the issuance of the initial certificate of registration or following the date of any annual renewal of such certificate.
(h)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall notify the dog control officer within twenty-four (24) hours if the dog is on the loose, is unconfined, has attacked a human, has died, or has been sold or donated. If the dog has been sold or donated, the owner shall also provide the name, address, and telephone number of the new owner of the dog.
(i)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall notify the dog control officer if the owner is moving from the dog control officer's jurisdiction.
(j)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog who is a new resident of the state shall register the dog in the manner provided by this article within thirty (30) days after becoming a resident.
(k)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog who moves from one jurisdiction to another within the state shall register the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog in the manner provided by this article within ten (10) days after becoming a resident in the new jurisdiction.
(Code 1968, § 4-34; Ord. of 1-9-89, § 1)
Sec. 5-31. - Possession of dangerous or potentially dangerous dog without certification; keeping of dog in violation of conditions of certificate; failure to post clearly visible warning sign; confiscation and impoundment; destruction of animal.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for an owner to have or possess within the city a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog without a certificate of registration issued in accordance with section 5-30.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for an owner of a dangerous dog to permit the dog to be outside a proper enclosure unless the dog is muzzled and restrained by a substantial chain or leash and is under the physical restraint of a responsible person.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for an owner of a potentially dangerous dog to permit the dog to be outside a proper enclosure unless the dog is restrained by a substantial chain or leash and is under the restraint of a responsible person.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for the owner of a dangerous dog to fail to post a warning sign on the premises where a dangerous dog is located in accordance with section 5-30.
(e)
It shall be the duty of the dog control officer, and any person under such officer's authority, to impound the dog if:
(1)
The owner does not secure a certificate of registration in accordance with section 5-30;
(2)
The owner does not secure liability insurance or meet the bond requirement of section 5-30;
(3)
The owner does not keep the dog within a proper enclosure or permits the dog to roam or run free.
(f)
Any dog impounded under the provisions of this section shall be returned to the owner upon the owner's compliance with the provisions of this article and upon payment of reasonable impoundment cost, as fixed by a schedule approved by the governing body, as from time to time amended. Notice of impoundment under this section shall be given the owner, in writing, by certified mail, at such owner's last known address. The notice shall state that if the owner has not complied with the provisions of this article within twenty (20) days from receipt of such notice, the dog shall be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner; provided, however, no dog shall be destroyed under this section unless it has been held a full twenty (20) days, exclusive of the date of giving notice.
(g)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall be solely liable for any injury to or death of a person caused by such dog. Under no circumstances shall the city or any employee or official of the city which enforces or fails to enforce the provisions of this division be held liable for any damages to any person who suffers an injury inflicted by a dog that has been identified as being a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog or by a dog which the city has failed to identify as a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog or by a dog which has been identified as being a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog but has not been kept or restrained in the manner described in this section or by a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog whose owner has not maintained insurance coverage or a surety bond as required in this section.
(Code 1968, § 4-35; Ord. of 1-9-89, § 1)
Sec. 5-32. - Violations; penalties.
(a)
The owner of a dangerous dog who violates the applicable provisions of section 5-30 or section 5-31 or whose dangerous dog is subject to confiscation shall be guilty of a misdemeanor. In addition to any confinement that might be imposed for a conviction under this subsection, for the second conviction a fine of not less than five hundred dollars ($500.00) shall be imposed and for a third or subsequent conviction a fine of not less than seven hundred fifty dollars ($750.00) shall be imposed.
(b)
The owner of a potentially dangerous dog who violates the applicable provisions of section 5-30 or 5-31 or whose potentially dangerous dog is subject to confiscation shall be guilty of a misdemeanor. In addition to any confinement that might be imposed for a conviction under this subsection, for a second conviction a fine of not less than one hundred fifty dollars ($150.00) shall be imposed and for a third or subsequent conviction a fine of not less than three hundred dollars ($300.00) shall be imposed.
(c)
No owner of a dangerous dog shall be held criminally liable under this division for injuries inflicted by said owner's dog to any human being while on the owner's property.
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-28.
