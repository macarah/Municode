ARTICLE II. - UNIFORM RULES OF THE ROAD

Sec. 66-31. - Adopted.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-372—40-6-376, O.C.G.A. Title 40, Chapter 6 (O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-1- et seq.), known as the Uniform Rules of the Road, and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of the city, with like effect as if recited herein.
(b)
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this article shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of section 1-12.
(Code 1982, § 10-1-1; Ord. of 4-4-2006)
Sec. 66-32. - Temporary traffic regulations.
On special occasions when traffic may be extraordinarily heavy, the public safety department may make temporary rules for the regulation of traffic in the congested district, and it shall be unlawful for any person, after being warned of such temporary rules by the police department, to refuse to comply therewith.
(Ord. of 4-4-2006)
State Law reference— Obedience to authorized persons directing traffic, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-2.
Sec. 66-33. - Vehicles not to obstruct traffic.
No vehicle shall occupy any street so as to obstruct traffic.
(Ord. of 4-4-2006)
State Law reference— Stopping, standing or parking prohibited in specified places, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-203; obstructing intersections, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-205.
Sec. 66-34. - Skateboards.
(a)
Where prohibited. Skateboarding shall be prohibited on any roadway or sidewalk:
(1)
Where the volume or speed of the vehicular traffic or the width of the roadway renders the roadway unsafe or unsuitable for skateboarding as determined by the department of public safety.
(2)
In any portion of the city specifically designated by the zoning ordinance of the city as a business district, be it B neighborhood business district, CBD central business district, C highway commercial district or C-PUD commercial planned unit development district, unless the owner of such zoned property authorizes the use thereof for skateboarders and has erected a sign thereon indicating the authorization.
(3)
In any park or other public area which has not specifically been designated as an area where the use of skateboards or similar instruments are allowed.
(b)
Yield right-of-way. Notwithstanding their status as pedestrians, skateboard operators on sidewalks and/or streets shall yield the right-of-way to all vehicular and to all other pedestrian (nonskateboard) traffic upon the sidewalk and/or street.
(c)
Rights and duties. Skateboard operators on roadways and sidewalks in authorized areas shall be granted all of the rights and shall be subject to all the duties applicable to nonskateboard pedestrians.
(d)
Limitation on operation. No more than one person shall operate or ride on a skateboard at one time.
(e)
Authority to establish written regulations. The chief of police or his designee is authorized to establish all written regulations necessary to implement the provisions of this section.
(Ord. of 4-4-2006)
Sec. 66-35. - Designation of streets, parts of streets on which truck traffic prohibited; signs required.
When appropriate signs are erected giving notice thereof, all trucks are hereby prohibited upon the streets or parts of streets so designated; however, such restriction shall not apply to trucks making local deliveries within the areas so posted.
(Ord. of 4-4-2006)
Sec. 66-36. - Truck routes designated; use regulated.
(a)
Truck routes within the city are hereby designated as follows:
Business 441/129 Highway, from city limit to the city limit;
Ga. 16/44, from city limit to the city limit;
Old Godfrey Highway, from N. Jefferson to city limit;
Imperial Mill Road from N. Jefferson Avenue to the 441/129 Bypass;
The U.S 441/129 Eatonton Bypass.
(b)
All trucks other than trucks making local deliveries within the city shall use the streets designated in this section and no others.
(Ord. of 4-4-2006)
Sec. 66-37. - Vehicles weighing more than eight thousand pounds—Authorized routes through city designated.
All motor vehicles which, together with load, weigh in excess of 8,000 pounds, passing through, entering or leaving the city shall be restricted to the following streets and parts of streets, subject to the exceptions set out in section 66-38:
Oak Street/Jefferson Avenue/Madison Highway;
W. Marion Street;
Sumter Street;
Old Godfrey Highway;
Imperial Mill Road;
The U.S. 441/129 Bypass.
(Ord. of 4-4-2006)
Sec. 66-38. - Same—Duties of operator; exceptions.
Each motor vehicle, falling within the provisions of section 66-37, entering the city by any other road or street than those set out in section 66-36 shall proceed by the shortest route to the nearest street or part of street set out in section 66-36 and shall proceed thence along the streets or parts of streets named through the city subject to the following exceptions:
(1)
Vehicles based within city. Motor vehicles having a base within the corporate limits may proceed to such base by the shortest route from the streets and parts of streets set out in section 66-33 and shall likewise return to such streets or parts of streets by the shortest route.
(2)
Servicing vehicle. Motor vehicles may leave the streets and parts of streets set out in section 66-36 for the purpose of purchasing gasoline and oil or securing repairs, but in such case they shall be permitted only upon such streets and parts of streets as are upon the most direct and shortest route from the streets and parts of streets set out in section 66-36.
(3)
Loading, unloading. Motor vehicles may leave the streets and parts of streets set out in section 66-36 for the purpose of making deliveries consigned to points upon other streets, roads and alleys in the city and for the further purpose of receiving goods or other property for transportation from points upon other roads, streets and alleys within the corporate limits, but shall proceed to such points by the shortest direct route from the streets and parts of streets set out in section 66-36 and shall return by the same route.
(Ord. of 4-4-2006)
Sec. 66-39. - Penalties.
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this article shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of section 1-12.
(Ord. of 4-4-2006)

Sec. 66-81. - Parking, stopping so as to obstruct another vehicle.
No vehicle shall be parked or stopped in such a position as to prevent the convenient movement of another vehicle.
(Ord. of 6-6-2006)
Sec. 66-82. - Manner of parking regulated.
All vehicles parking upon the streets of the city shall occupy parking spaces marked out by the public safety department in the manner provided.
(Ord. of 6-6-2006)
State Law reference— How vehicles to be parked, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-200.
Sec. 66-83. - Specific streets where parking is prohibited.
Parking is prohibited at all times upon the following streets or parts of streets, unless otherwise indicated:
State Route 16, from city limit sign on the east to the city limit sign on the west unless otherwise provided for by sign.
U.S. Highway 129, from city limit sign on the south to the city limit sign on the north unless otherwise provided by sign.
U.S. Highway 441, from city limit sign on the south to the city limit sign on the north unless otherwise provided by sign.
(Ord. of 6-6-2006)
Sec. 66-84. - Two-hour parking; designation of locations.
Between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 7:00 p.m. on all days except Sunday, and when appropriate signs are erected giving notice thereof, it shall be unlawful for any person to leave parked an automobile or other vehicle for longer than two hours upon any of the streets or parts of streets so designated by the mayor and city council.
(Ord. of 6-6-2006)
Sec. 66-85. - Parking time limit in alleys.
No vehicle of any description shall be left standing longer than one-half hour in any alley of the city.
(Ord. of 6-6-2006)
Sec. 66-86. - Receipt, discharge of passengers.
No vehicle shall take on or discharge passengers except at the right-hand curb.
(Ord. of 6-6-2006)
Sec. 66-87. - Truck parking restricted.
(a)
Parking in business district. No truck larger than one-half ton shall park in other than parallel parking spaces in the business district. No truck loaded with livestock shall be parked for longer than five minutes in the business district.
(b)
Overnight parking. No truck larger than one-half ton shall be left parked overnight on any street of the city.
(Ord. of 6-6-2006)
Sec. 66-88. - Trucks loading, unloading at business houses; hours.
All trucks loading or unloading at business houses shall use the back entrance to business houses if a back entrance is available and shall not be permitted to unload or load in front between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m.
(Ord. of 6-6-2006)
Sec. 66-89. - Garages parking, working on vehicles.
Garages shall not leave motor vehicles parked in the streets, sidewalks, rights-of-way or easements for the purpose of repairing the vehicles, except in case of emergency repairs, and all work to be done on such vehicles must be carried on upon the premises of the person operating such garage.
(Ord. of 6-6-2006)
Sec. 66-90. - Criminal trespass by motor vehicle.
(a)
A person commits the offense of criminal trespass by motor vehicle when the person, after having been requested not to do so by a law enforcement officer or by the owner or an authorized agent of the owner, parks or stands an occupied or unoccupied motor vehicle in, or repeatedly drives a motor vehicle through or within a parking area which is located on privately owned property and is provided by a merchant, a group of merchants or a shopping center or other similar facility for customers if:
(1)
The parking area is identified by at least one sign as specified in this subsection, and if the parking area contains more than 150 parking spaces, then by at least one such sign for every 150 parking spaces, each such sign containing the following information in easy to read printing:
a.
Notice of the elements of the crime of criminal trespass by motor vehicle;
b.
Identification of the property which is reserved for customers' use only;
c.
Identification of the merchant, group of merchants or shopping center or other similar facility providing the parking area;
d.
Warning that violators will be prosecuted.
(2)
The motor vehicle is parked, is standing or is being operated other than for the purpose of:
a.
Transporting some person to or from the interior of the place of business of a merchant identified by the sign in the parking area or to or from the interior of the shopping center or other facility so identified;
b.
Making use of a telephone, vending machine, automatic teller machine or other similar facility located in the parking area;
c.
Meeting the requirements of an emergency situation in which it has unexpectedly become impossible or impractical for the motor vehicle to continue to travel on the public roads;
d.
Carrying out an activity for which express permission has been given by the owner of the parking area or an authorized representative of the owner.
(b)
A person who commits the offense of criminal trespass by motor vehicle shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction thereof, shall be punished by a fine as provide in section 1-12.
(Ord. of 6-6-2006)

Sec. 66-101. - City not responsible for damage to vehicles on city streets.
No payment, fee, relief of debt or other settlement shall be made to any person for claims of damage to a vehicle arising from driving or parking on city streets or parking lots because of potholes, manholes, valves, curbs, gutters, storm drains, gravel or other objects thrown from the street or dropped from a vehicle or damage from the acts of others including vehicular accidents, vandalism, burglary or any act of God, civil disturbance, civil or military authority, war, court order or acts of public enemy except as provided in a court of competent jurisdiction.
