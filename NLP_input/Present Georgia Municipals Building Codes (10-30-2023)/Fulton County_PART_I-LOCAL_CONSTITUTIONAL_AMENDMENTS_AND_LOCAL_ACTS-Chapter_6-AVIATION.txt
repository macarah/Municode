ARTICLE II. - AIRPORTS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 6-31. - Authority to expand, improve or alter construction or operation for commercial use.
The governing authority of Fulton County shall not be authorized to expand, improve, or otherwise alter the construction or operation of any airport or landing field maintained or operated by such county for noncommercial use as of January 1, 1993, so that such airport or landing field is maintained or operated for any commercial use, unless such change in use is approved by the qualified voters of the county voting in a referendum thereon.
(1993 Ga. Laws (Act No. 179), page 4520, § 1)

DIVISION 2. - AIRPORT AUTHORITY[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
Cross reference— Boards, commissions and authorities, § 2-356 et seq.

Sec. 6-66. - Creation.
There shall be and there is hereby created, in every county in this state having a population of 300,000 or more inhabitants according to the United States census of 1940, or any future United States census, an airport authority for such county.
(1947 Ga. Laws (Act No. 352), page 1446, § 1)
Sec. 6-67. - Composition; terms.
Said airport authority shall be composed of not less than five and not more than nine members, to be appointed by the board of commissioners for such county, or other county authority having charge of the roads and revenues thereof (and any one or more members of said board of commissioners or other county authority may be appointed members of said airport authority) for a term of four years, and until their successors (to be appointed in like manner) shall have been elected and qualified.
(1947 Ga. Laws (Act No. 352), page 1446, § 2)
Sec. 6-68. - Powers.
Every such airport authority, when directed by the board of commissioners or other county authority having charge of the roads and revenues thereof, shall have power to purchase, plan, and erect an airport or airports at any place within the limits of the county outside of incorporate limits of cities in said counties, and upon erection, when directed by the board of commissioners or other county authority having charge of the roads and revenues thereof, shall have power to supervise and manage the operation of same as a revenue producing facility.
(1947 Ga. Laws (Act No. 352), page 1446, § 3)
Sec. 6-69. - Erection of airports on basis of revenue certificate plan.
Every such airport may be erected upon the revenue certificate plan and may be paid for by revenue certificates issued by the county under and by virtue of the authority contained in Ga. Const. (1945) art. VII, § VII, ¶ 5 of the Constitution, and the acts of the general assembly heretofore enacted and hereafter enacted providing for the issuance of revenue certificates for the purchase or construction, in whole or in part, of any revenue producing facility, and no such certificate shall be a general obligation of said county.
(1947 Ga. Laws (Act No. 352), page 1446, § 4)
Sec. 6-70. - Power to extend, repair or improve; funding.
Each such airport authority, when directed by the board of commissioners or other county authority having charge of the roads and revenues thereof, shall have power to extend, repair or improve any airport erected under authority of this division, and may obtain funds therefor under said revenue certificate plan.
(1947 Ga. Laws (Act No. 352), page 1446, § 5)
Sec. 6-71. - Curtailing or limiting power and authority previously conferred on county.
Nothing in this division shall curtail or limit in any way the power and authority now existing in said counties and their officers with respect to airports by reason of laws heretofore enacted; and the power and authority conferred by this division shall be cumulative thereto.
(1947 Ga. Laws (Act No. 352), page 1446, § 6)
