ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 2-1. - Personnel provisions not affected by Code.
Nothing in this Code or in the ordinance adopting this Code shall affect any ordinance relating to the county's personnel classification system, employee pay plan, retirement plan or employee benefits. All such ordinances are hereby recognized as continuing in full force and effect to the same extent as if set out in this Code.
Sec. 2-2. - Safe operation of county vehicles and equipment; use of seat belts.
(a)
Findings. The lack of proper safety procedures has resulted in lost manhours to the county.
(b)
Operation of vehicles and equipment. All county vehicles and equipment shall be operated in a safe and efficient manner in accordance with all applicable state motor vehicle laws.
(c)
Use of seat belts. The county requires the use of seat belts in all county vehicles and equipment.
(Res. of 9-9-2003)
Sec. 2-3. - [9-1-1 charges on telephone services and wireless enhanced 9-1-1 charges other than prepaid wireless services.]
modified
(a)
Definitions. Unless specified otherwise in this resolution, all terms shall be defined in the same manner as specified in O.C.G.A. § 46-5-122.
(b)
9-1-1 charges on monthly services.
(1)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 46-5-133(a), there is imposed a monthly 9-1-1 charge upon each telephone service, subscribed to by a telephone subscriber, whose exchange access lines are in the areas served or which could be served by the 9-1-1 service. Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134(a)(1)(A), the amount of such 9-1-1 charge shall be $1.50 per month per telephone service provided to the telephone subscriber.
(2)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 46-5-133(a), there is imposed a monthly wireless enhanced 9-1-1 charge upon each wireless telecommunications connection, other than a connection for prepaid wireless service, subscribed to by a telephone subscriber whose place of primary use is within the geographic area that is served by Marion County, Georgia, or that would be served by Marion County, Georgia, for the purpose of such an emergency 9-1-1 system. Pursuant to O.C.G.A. 46-5-134(a)(2)(A), the amount of such enhanced wireless 9-1-1 charge shall be $1.50 per month per wireless telecommunications connection provided to the telephone subscriber.
(3)
The 9-1-1 charges under this section shall commence January 1, 2019.
(c)
Collecting and remitting of 9-1-1 charges. All such 9-1-1 charges collected by service suppliers shall be remitted to the Georgia Department of Revenue, as the contracted collection partner of the Georgia Emergency Communications Authority, at the times and in the manner provided by O.C.G.A. § 38-3-185, O.C.G.A. § 38-3-186, and any other Georgia Department of Revenue or Georgia Emergency Communications Authority rule or regulation adopted pursuant to O.C.G.A. tit. 38, ch. 13, art. 12 and O.C.G.A. tit. 50, ch. 13, the "Georgia Administrative Procedures Act."
(d)
Deposit and use of proceeds. In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134, O.C.G.A. § 46-5-125, and the attached Intergovernmental Agreement for the Operation of Middle Flint Regional E-9-1-1 Center (hereafter "IGA"), a copy of which is attached hereto as exhibit "A," all proceeds due to Marion County from 9-1-1 charges imposed by this resolution shall be remitted to the Middle Flint Regional E-9-1-1 Authority (hereafter "authority") and deposited in an emergency telephone system fund maintained by said authority for the benefit of the county, kept separate from any general revenue of the county and authority, and used exclusively for the statutorily authorized purposes.
(Res. of 9-11-2018(1), §§ 1—4)
Editor's note— Sections 1—4 of a resolution adopted Sept. 11, 2018, has been treated as superseding the former provisions of § 2-3, which pertained to charges on telephone subscribers for E-911, and derived from a resolution adopted Nov. 12, 2002. Exhibit "A" as referenced above, has not been set out, but may be reviewed at the county clerk's office.

Sec. 2-5. - [9-1-1 Charge on voice over internet protocol (VoIP) connections.]
new
The board of commissioners of Dooly, Macon, Marion, Schley, Sumter, Taylor and Webster Counties do hereby adopt this resolution to impose a 9-1-1 charge upon each voice over internet protocol telecommunications connection subscribed to by subscribers whose billing address is within the jurisdiction of their respective county at the rate of $1.50 per month per VoIP connection provided to each telephone subscriber, provided however that the increase shall not become effective in any of said counties until every aforementioned county approves this resolution. This resolution shall become effective on January 1, 2008, provided that each respective county commission shall have adopted this resolution on or prior to December 31, 2007. If any of said counties have not approved this resolution on or prior to January 31, 2008, but subsequently approve the resolution following that date, such that all seven counties approve the resolution, the effective date shall be the first day of the first month after which at least 120 days shall have intervened between the adoption of this resolution by the last signatory county.
Upon the adoption of this resolution, each county commission or the clerk thereof shall transmit a copy of the approved resolution to the director of the Middle Flint Regional E-911 Authority, who shall, in turn, provide copies and proper notification to all vendors of wireless telephone services within the jurisdiction of the aforementioned counties. Said director is further instructed to duly inform each of the seven county commissions following the adoption of this resolution by each county.
(Res. of 12-11-2007)
Editor's note— A resolution adopted Dec. 11, 2007, did not specify manner of inclusion; hence, codification as § 2-5 was at the discretion of the editor.
