ARTICLE II. - SUPPLEMENTAL REGULATIONS

Sec. 34-56. - Lots of record.
Any lot of record which is legal on the date of the first published notice of this chapter may be used subject to the following exceptions and modifications.
(1)
Use of substandard lots. Where the owner of a lot at the time of the adoption or amendment of the resolution from which this chapter is derived does not own sufficient area and width to enable him to conform it to the dimensional requirements of this chapter, said lot may be used as a building site for a structure or use permitted in the zone in which it is located, provided the owner has the approval by authorization of the city council.
(2)
Residential use of substandard lots. In addition to subsection (1) of this section, in any residential district, any lot of record existing at the time of adoption or amendment of the resolution from which this chapter is derived which has a width or area less than that required by this chapter may be used as a building site for a single-family dwelling only. In the case of such a lot, when it is not possible to provide the required side yards and at the same time build a minimum width single-family dwelling, the planning commission is empowered to hear the request for a side yard variance provided there is a ten-foot side yard. If a minimum ten-foot side yard is not possible, then a minimum width for single-family dwelling may be requested.
(Res. of 10-12-2004(3), ยง 5.1)
Sec. 34-57. - Area, yard and height regulations.
(a)
Lot area. Hereafter, no lot shall be reduced in size so that lot width, size of yards or lot area per family requirement of this chapter is altered except when a portion of a lot is acquired for a public purpose.
(b)
Yards and open space.
(1)
Whenever a lot abuts a public alley, one-half of the alley width may be considered as a portion of the required yard.
(2)
Every part of a required yard shall be open to the sky so that projections, such as sills, window air conditioning units, chimneys, cornices and ornamental features, may not extend into a required yard.
(3)
Other provisions of this chapter notwithstanding, fences, walls, hedges, driveways and buffer areas may be permitted in any required yard or along the edge of any yard provided that no fence, wall or hedge along the street sides of corner lots shall violate the corner visibility provisions of this chapter.
(c)
Front yards.
(1)
Where an official line has been established for the future widening or opening of a street or major thoroughfare upon which a lot abuts, the depth of a front or side yard shall be measured from such official line to the nearest line of the building.
(2)
On through lots, the required front yard shall be provided on each street.
(3)
Corner lots shall meet the minimum front yard requirements on the side adjacent to both streets and the remaining yards shall be considered side yard requirements.
(4)
Open, unenclosed porches, platforms or paved terraces, which are not covered by a roof or canopy and do not extend above the level of the first floor of the building, may extend or project into the required setback area if it is six feet or less.
(5)
Within the same block and zoning district, when 25 percent or more of the existing buildings have less than the minimum required setback, the required front setback of such lot should not exceed the average of the existing front setbacks.
(d)
Side yards.
(1)
For the purposes of the side yard regulations, a group of commercial buildings separated by common or party walls shall be considered as one building occupying one lot.
(2)
The minimum width of side yards for schools, libraries, churches and other public or semi-public buildings in residential districts shall be 25 feet. Where a side yard is adjacent to a business or commercial district, then the width of that yard shall be as required for the district in which the building is located.
(e)
Fences and walls. No fence or freestanding wall in a required yard other than a retaining wall shall be more than eight feet in height.
(Res. of 10-12-2004(3), ยง 5.2)
Sec. 34-58. - Corner visibility.
No sign, fence, wall, hedge, planting or other obstruction to vision, extending to a height in excess of three feet above the established street grade, shall be erected, planted or maintained within the area of a corner lot that is included between the lines of the intersecting streets and a straight line connecting them at points 25 feet distant from the intersection of the street lines. No sign, fence or planting over two feet in height shall be located in the right-of-way.
(Res. of 10-12-2004(3), ยง 5.3)
Sec. 34-59. - Accessory uses and structures.
(a)
An accessory structure shall not be permitted in any required front yard, except as specified in subsection (f) of this section.
(b)
No accessory building or structure shall be erected beyond a required yard line along any street.
(c)
Residential accessory uses, such as garages, greenhouses or workshops, shall not be rented or occupied for commercial purposes.
(d)
All residential accessory buildings must be located at least ten feet from the principal building.
(e)
Areas in which the accessory storage of a boat, boat trailer or travel trailer is permitted shall not include the required front yard.
(f)
Gasoline station pumps and pump islands where permitted may occupy the required yards, provided that they shall not be less than 15 feet from street lines; canopies, whether attached or detached from the principal building, must be at least 14 feet in height and the outermost edge shall be five feet from any property line.
(g)
All nonresidential accessory buildings shall only be used by the owners, employees, lessee or tenants of the premises, and shall meet the setback requirements of the principal building.
(h)
An open or unenclosed swimming pool may occupy a required rear or side yard, provided that the pool is not located closer than six feet to a rear lot line or ten feet to an interior side lot line. A three-foot-wide walk space shall be provided between pool walls and protective fences or barrier walls.

Sec. 34-60. - Residential home occupations.
(a)
The conduct of business in residential units may be permitted under the provisions of this section. It is the intent of this section to ensure the following:
(1)
Compatibility of home occupations with other uses permitted in residential zoning districts.
(2)
Maintain and preserve the character of residential neighborhoods and provide peace, quiet and domestic tranquility within all residential neighborhoods.
(3)
Within the residential district, guarantee all residents freedom from excessive noise, traffic, nuisance, fire hazard, and other possible effects of commercial uses being conducted in this district.
(b)
Residential home occupations, where permitted, must meet the following special requirements:
(1)
A home occupation is subordinate to the use of a dwelling unit for residential purposes. No more than 25 percent of the floor area of the dwelling unit may be used in connection with a home occupation or for storage purposes in connection with a home occupation.
(2)
No more than one home occupation shall be permitted within a single dwelling unit.
(3)
A home occupation shall be carried on wholly within the principal use. No home occupation, nor any storage of goods, materials, or products connected with a home occupation shall be allowed in accessory buildings or garages, which are attached or detached.
(4)
The residential home occupation is limited to employment of residents of the property and not more than one additional person.
(5)
A home occupation shall produce no noise or obnoxious odors, vibrations, glare, fumes, or electrical interference detectable to normal sensory perceptions outside the principal structure.
(6)
No traffic shall be generated by such home occupations in greater volume than would normally be expected in a residential neighborhood and any need for parking generated by the conduct of such home occupation shall be met by providing off-street space and located in the rear or side yard.
(7)
On the premises, retail sales are prohibited except for the retail sales of products or goods produced or fabricated on the premises as a result of the home occupation.
(8)
There shall be no exterior indication of the home occupation or variation from the residential character of the principal use.
(9)
No on-street parking of business-related vehicles (either marked or commercially equipped) shall be permitted at any home. No business-related vehicle larger than a van, panel truck or pickup truck is permitted to park overnight on the premises. The number of business-related vehicles is one.

Sec. 34-61. - Rural home occupations.
(a)
Rural home occupations in the rural residential district shall be permitted under the provisions of this section. It is the intent of the section to ensure the compatibility of rural home occupations with other uses permitted in the rural residential district; and maintain and preserve the rural character of the area and not create a nuisance for residents in the area by exceeding traffic, smoke, noise or be a fire hazard.
(b)
The purpose of rural home occupations is to provide a means for residents in the larger lot rural residential district to participate in the type of businesses permitted in residential districts, be able to conduct the home occupation in an accessory building where necessary, and to park, on-site, vehicles required for home occupations. Rural home occupations, where permitted, must meet the following special requirements:
(1)
The minimum lot size is five acres. For lots less than five acres, the home occupation is limited to the provisions of section 34-60.
(2)
The rural home occupation shall be clearly subordinate to the principal use of the parcel and shall not change the residential character of the area. No more than 25 percent of the floor area of the principal dwelling shall be used in connection with the home occupation or storage purposes.
(3)
The rural home occupation shall be conducted within a dwelling or within an accessory building, provided all structure uses are harmonious in appearance with the rural residential or single-family district.
(4)
One accessory building not exceeding 1,000 square feet may be used in connection with the rural home occupation.
(5)
The business of selling stocks of merchandise, supplies or products shall not be conducted on-premises except under the following circumstances:
a.
Orders previously made by telephone or at sales parties may be filled on premises; and
b.
Incidental retail sales as may be necessary or in connection with the permitted home occupation.
(6)
The existence of the rural home occupation shall not be apparent outside the dwelling or accessory building in which the rural home occupation is conducted, except that one display sign limited to nine square feet of sign area shall be permitted. The display sign added to all other signs on the parcel shall not exceed the maximum signage allowed in the district.
(7)
No outside storage of equipment or materials used in the conduct of the rural home occupation, other than trade vehicles, is permitted.
(8)
The rural home occupation is limited to residents of the property and not more than two additional persons.
(9)
No additional points of access to any street shall be permitted, unless necessary to provide safe and proper access to the proposed use.
(c)
Permitted rural home occupations include:
(1)
All occupations permitted in section 34-60(b)(10).

(8)

Sec. 34-62. - Farm-based business.
(a)
The uses permitted under this section apply to the Agricultural (A-25) and Rural Residential (RR) zoned districts. Farm occupations may involve any one of a wide range of uses, so long as the use is compatible with the primary agricultural use of the land. The applicant shall demonstrate to the planning commission the farm-based business is compatible with the rural setting and shall not create nuisances for nearby residents.
(b)
No more than the equivalent of two full-time nonresident employees of the farm parcel shall be employed by the farm-based business. However, the number of employees may be increased if it is believed the proposed use is able to sustain increased employees and retain the agricultural character of the area.
(c)
No more than 2,000 square feet of gross floor area in any one building shall be occupied by the farm-based business.
(d)
No more than one acre shall be devoted to the farm-based business, including the structure, parking, storage, and driveway (if separate from the primary residence driveway).
(e)
Where practical, farm-based businesses shall be conducted within an existing building located on the farm parcel. However, any building constructed for use by the farm-based business shall be:
(1)
Located behind a residence; or
(2)
Setback a minimum of 100 feet from any public street right-of-way.
(f)
Any building constructed for the use of the farm-based business shall be of a nature that it can be converted to agricultural use or removed from the property if the farm-based business is discontinued. The structure shall blend with the surrounding agricultural setting.
(g)
A site development sketch shall be submitted to the planning commission. The sketch shall show the location of any building, distance from property lines, parking and location of signage.
(h)
No farm-based business shall be located within 100 feet of any adjoining side or rear property line, nor within 100 feet of any adjacent residential zoning district. Such distances shall be measured as a straight line between the closest points of any structure or any other physical improvement of the farm-based business and the adjoining property line.
(i)
Any outdoor storage of supplies, materials or products shall be in an area least visible to the public, stored in an orderly fashion and adjacent to the building in which the farm-based business is located. Such outdoor storage shall be screened from adjoining roads and properties in accordance with the buffers specified in article III of this chapter.
(j)
An on-premises, freestanding sign is permitted for the farm-based business and shall be in accordance with article VI of this chapter.
(k)
The city planning commission may also require such other reasonable conditions as deemed appropriate.
(l)
Permitted farm-based businesses include:
(1)
All uses listed in sections 34-60(b)(10) and 34-61(c).
(2)
Farm equipment sales and services.
(3)
Greenhouse or nursery sales.
(4)
Flea markets.
(5)
Livestock sales or auction facilities.
(6)
Pet and dog grooming.
(7)
Produce stands.
(8)
Any farm-based business approved by the city council.

Sec. 34-63. - Special density lots for the use of family homesteads.
In order to perpetuate the family homestead in rural areas, each parcel in single ownership may be subdivided for use solely as a homestead by an individual who is the grandparent, parent, stepparent, adopted parent, sibling, child, stepchild, adopted child or grandchild of the person who conveyed the parcel to said individual, at a density not to exceed one unit per acre. This provision supersedes other land use district density requirements, except that it may not be used within a platted subdivision. In order to qualify for the use of the family homestead the following criteria shall be met:
(1)
The property being transferred must be used as the primary residence of the grantee.
(2)
The deed for transferred property must state the relationship of the grantee to the grantor.
(3)
Transferred property may be deeded in the heir's (grantee) name or jointly deeded in the grantor's/grantee's names. The transferred property may not be deeded solely in the grantor name.
(4)
This special density provision may be utilized on only one occasion per eligible individual grantee. The grantor may exercise this special density provision for other eligible individual grantees, so long as the maximum allowable density for the property transferred from the original parcel can be met.
(5)
An individual wishing to obtain a manufactured home or conventional building permit under these special density provisions shall make application to the planning commission. Upon verification of the eligibility of the applicant, a permit may be issued.
(6)
Should the transferred property be subsequently sold or otherwise transferred to another party, including a non-family party, these special density provisions shall remain.

Sec. 34-64. - Medical hardship secondary detached dwelling.
A manufactured home, which meets Type III appearance standards, may be parked and occupied as a residence in a residential area where a medical hardship exists after application of a special exception and approval of same from the city council.
(1)
"Medical hardship" defined. The term "medical hardship" is defined as a health or mental condition necessitating attention and supervision from the occupants of either the primary dwelling or the manufactured home.
(2)
Occupants. Occupants of the primary dwelling shall provide proof to the zoning administrator that a secondary detached dwelling unit is necessary for the physical care and well-being of the infirm person (doctor or medical facility affidavit, nursing home recommendation, etc.). The occupants of the primary dwelling shall also provide approval by the county health department of proper sewerage and water facilities.
(3)
Permit expiration. Once the state of medical hardship is removed, the land use permit for manufactured homes shall expire and the manufactured home shall be removed in a timely manner, but in no case longer than three months.
(4)
Manufactured home secondary detached dwelling permit. A manufactured home permit that is issued under this section shall expire 12 months from the date it is granted. A renewal of the permit must meet all requirements of a new application; however, no advertisement or posting of a sign shall be required. However, this manufactured home shall not be used for any other purpose than to provide assistance in the case of medical hardship. This manufactured home shall not be used for storage, commercial, home occupation or rented for economic gain by the homeowner.

Sec. 34-66. - Property maintenance.
To ensure the health, safety and welfare of the citizens of the city the following regulations shall be established for the maintenance of property in the city:
(1)
All property owners are required to keep their premises in a clean, sanitary, and healthy condition. A complaint of the same general nature by two or more citizens against an occupant or property owner or the observations by city personal are sufficient to warrant action under this chapter.
(2)
No weeds, grasses, vines, etc., shall be permitted to grow on the curtilage of any property over 12 inches in height. Any property owner receiving notice from the city for growth over 12 inches in height upon his premises shall immediately cause the weeds, grasses, and/or growth to be cut down.
(3)
No household garbage, either bagged or unbagged, shall be allowed to accumulate on or around the premises of a residence within the city limits. No rubbish shall be allowed to accumulate on or around the premise of a residence within the city limits.
(4)
Construction materials in the form of lumber, blocks, brick, roofing materials, non-functioning appliances, siding, etc., may not be stored on or around the premises of a residence within the city limits in either a covered or uncovered condition unless the construction of a structure involving these materials at this site is underway or imminent. Materials stored in barns, storage buildings, or out of public view on large acreage lots will not be in violation of this chapter.
(5)
All residences within the city limits, to include their outbuildings, storage sheds, and barns, unoccupied for any reason, to include, but not be limited to, abandonment, foreclosure, storm damage, decay, or vacant, are to be maintained in a safe, sanitary, and secure condition.
(6)
Any business within the city limits, to include its outbuildings, storage sheds, and barns, unoccupied for any reason, to include, but not be limited to, abandonment, foreclosure, storm damage, decay, or vacant, are to be maintained in a safe, sanitary, and secure condition.
(7)
Storm damaged or blown down trees within the yard or curtilage of a residence or business within the city limits will be cleaned up at the earliest opportunity by the owner or occupant. Harvested timber within a yard or curtilage of a residence or business within the city limits shall require cleanup of debris, to include stump removal if visible from roadway.
(8)
All residences and businesses within the city limits employing the use of a septic tank shall maintain the tank and its accompanying lines in good working order. Any observation of black water as a result of a dysfunctional system shall require immediate pumping of the septic tank to stop the overflow of contents onto open ground. Pumping of septic tanks on consecutive days may be required to empty the trunk line of black water until repairs can be made. When efforts of the occupant or owner are not forthcoming to stop the immediate release of black water from the septic tank, emergency measures by the city, to include, but not be limited to, the cutoff of city water to the residence or business, may be performed.
(9)
Abandoned mobile/manufactured homes shall be removed from property at the owner's expense. this subsection and the requirements hereof shall include tractors and trailers, vehicles of any kind, any mobile trailer designed to pull behind vehicles, water craft, aircraft, tires, engines, transmissions, motorcycles, utility vehicles, etc.
(10)
Non-licensed and/or inoperable vehicles shall be stored in an enclosed accessory building. No more than three non-licensed and/or inoperable vehicles shall be stored on property unless application for a junkyard has been issued by the city council.
(11)
Yard sales shall last for a specified duration only. There shall not be any continuous yard sales. Storing of yard sale items shall be in an enclosed accessory or principal building and shall not be on display in any yard except during the hours of the yard sale. Yard sales may last eight hours a day and no more than four days a month at the same residence. All parties having a yard/garage sale must obtain a permit from city hall prior to the event. All signs pertaining to sales must be picked up within 24 hours after sale.
(l2)
Penalties.
a.
The foregoing provisions shall be enforced by the city through the city police or other persons granted such authority by the mayor and council of the city. Enforcement of this section may be through citation to the municipal court for the city. The municipal court shall have the power and authority to impose any and all fines and other penalties allowed by the city Charter and state law. Any fines imposed for violation of this section may be reduced to fi. fa. to serve as a lien against the property in question.
b.
In those instances where the mayor or city council determines that an offender or property owner in violation of subsections (a) through (k) of this section, is unable to make corrective actions necessary to regain compliance, either from physical inability or lack of financial resources, the city may take steps necessary to regain compliance through financial assistance and/or the use of city employees and community service individuals. Under the aforementioned circumstances, the city reserves the right to wave formal prosecution for violation in the municipal court of the city.
c.
In those instances in which the city is unable to identify or locate the owner of property in violation of subsections (a) through (k) of this section, either through foreclosure, abandonment, out of state residence, or other similar reasons, the city may take necessary actions to bring said property into compliance. A minimum fee of $250.00 will be applied against the property in the form of a fi. fa. With payment in full required by new occupants of the property prior to the resumption of city services to that address.

Sec. 34-67. - Certificate of ordinance compliance.
(a)
No building, or part thereof, to include, but not limited to, site built structures, mobile homes, recreational vehicles or trailers, repurposed storage buildings and/or tiny homes, etc., not in compliance with city codes, and lots for the purpose of accommodating any of the above shall not be occupied or used unless the city has issued a certificate of ordinance compliance. This certificate of ordinance compliance shall not apply to structures used as full-time residences by its owners and not used as rental property prior to the enactment of this section until a change of occupancy or intended use occurs. A certificate of ordinance compliance issued by the City of Mount Zion will be in addition to any certificate of occupancy issued by county or state inspections or requirements. The certificate or ordinance compliance, inspection reports, and documentation of any corrective actions shall be maintained by the City of Mount Zion in a manner deemed appropriate by city staff, and a copy of said records provided to the property owner.
(b)
Requirements for certificate of ordinance compliance. Rental property, regardless of the manner of ownership, must have a certificate of compliance on file with the City of Mount Zion reflective of the current tenants. Every time a property is vacated, an inspection and new certificate must be obtained prior to the occupancy by new tenants. Rental property in the form of basement apartments or duplex residences in which the property owner resides in part of the structure while renting out the remaining floor space shall be considered a rental property in whole unless the renter is an immediate family member.
(c)
Inspections.
(1)
All properties subject to certificate of ordinance compliance must be in compliance with section 34-66 and other appropriate codes and ordinances of City of Mount Zion.
(2)
All inspections shall be conducted by the city codes enforcement officer and/or the designee appointed by the City of Mount Zion.
(3)
All inspections are conducted in an attempt to identify and correct dangerous structures or premises as it relates to the health and safety of Mounty Zion citizens. To this end, the City of Mount Zion adopts the following conditions and defects as listed in section (A)108.1.5 of Carroll County Code as grounds for denial of a certificate or ordinance compliance.
a.
Any building or structure, used or intended to be used for dwelling purposes, because of inadequate maintenance, dilapidation, decay, faulty construction, inadequate light, ventilation, mechanical or plumbing system, or otherwise, is determined by the City of Mount Zion to be unsanitary, unfit of human habitation, or in such a condition that is likely to cause sickness or disease.
b.
Any condition constituting a public nuisance, including for example, maintaining stagnant or fetid water on the premises, an accumulation of trash, junk, filth, or other unsanitary and unsafe conditions, the generation of smoke or fumes in sufficient amounts to cause odor or annoyance to the inhabitants of the municipality, and/or maintaining a dangerous or diseased animal or fowl or maintaining such a number of animals in such a foul and unsanitary conditions as to constitute a public nuisance.
(4)
Inspection failures as a result of insect, rodent, or mold infestation must be corrected with appropriate documented actions of a licensed pest control company. A conditional certificate of ordinance compliance may be granted by the City of Mount Zion contingent upon compliance by the property owner with follow up treatments as required by the pest control company.
(5)
Certificate of ordinance compliance inspections performed by the City of Mount Zion shall be billed to the property owner at the rate of $10.00 for the first inspection, and a rate of $5.00 for subsequent inspections necessary for compliance. All cost for corrective actions necessary for compliance shall be the responsibility of the property owner.
(d)
Penalties.
(1)
On the failure of any dwelling, building, structure, or property to pass the appropriate inspection and/or obtain a certificate of ordinance compliance, the owner or renter of the property shall vacate the premises or cause to be vacated from the premises immediately upon notification by the city.
The foregoing provisions shall be enforced by the city through the city police or other persons granted such authority by the mayor and council of the city.
(2)
Enforcement of this section may be through citation to the municipal court for the city. Municipal court shall have the power and authority to impose any and all fines and other penalties allowed by the city Charter and state law. Any fines imposed for violation of this section may be reduced to fi. fa. to serve as a lien against the property in question.
(3)
In those instances where the mayor of city council determines that an offender or property owner in violation of this section is unable to make corrective actions necessary to gain compliance, either from physical inability or lack of financial resources, the city may take steps necessary to gain compliance through financial assistance and/or the use of city employees and community service individuals.
Under the aforementioned circumstances, the city reserves the right to wave formal prosecution for violation in the municipal court of the city.
(4)
In those instances in which the city is unable to identify or locate the owner of property in violation of this section, either through foreclosure, abandonment, or of state residence, or other similar reasons, the city may take necessary actions to bring said property into compliance. A minimum free $250.00 will be applied against the property prior to the resumption of city services to that address.
