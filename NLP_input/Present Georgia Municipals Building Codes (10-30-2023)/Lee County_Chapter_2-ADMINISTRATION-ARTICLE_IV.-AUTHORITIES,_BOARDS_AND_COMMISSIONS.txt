DIVISION 2. - DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY[5]

Footnotes:
--- (5) ---
State Law reference— Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.; Georgia Development Authority Act, O.C.G.A. § 50-10-1 et seq.

Sec. 2-131. - Legislative authority, findings.
The board of commissioners desires to establish and reestablish a development authority pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 36-62-4(a). The board finds there is a need for a development authority to function in the county in accord with O.C.G.A. § 36-62-4(c).
(Res. of 1-22-1998)
Sec. 2-132. - Established.
The Development Authority of Lee County, referred to in this division as the development authority, is established and reestablished effective January 22, 1998, pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 36-62-4(a).
(Res. of 1-22-1998, § 1)
Sec. 2-133. - Prior acts ratified.
All acts of the development authority prior to January 22, 1998, are ratified and approved by the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 1-22-1998, § 2)
Sec. 2-134. - Membership.
(a)
The development authority shall consist of a board of seven directors appointed by resolution of the board of commissioners.
(b)
After the expiration of the initial two-year terms of the directors appointed for two-year terms, their successors shall be appointed for four-year terms such that all board members shall thereafter serve terms of four years on a staggered basis. Nothing shall prevent a director from succeeding himself, and director shall hold office until his successor is appointed by the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 1-22-1998, § 3)
Sec. 2-135. - Directors.
All directors of the development authority shall be taxpayers residing in the county and shall be appointed by the board of commissioners in accord with the terms of this division and state law.
(Res. of 1-22-1998, § 4)
Sec. 2-136. - Officers.
The directors of the development authority shall meet and shall elect one of their number as chairman and another as vice-chairman. The directors shall also elect a secretary and a treasurer, or a secretary/treasurer, either of whom may, but need not be, a director of the authority.
(Res. of 1-22-1998, § 5)
Sec. 2-137. - Compensation.
The directors of the development authority shall receive no compensation for their services as directors, but they shall be reimbursed for their actual expenses incurred in the performance of their duties.
(Res. of 1-22-1998, § 6)
Sec. 2-138. - Bylaws.
The development authority may establish bylaws and regulations for the governing of the authority and may delegate to one or more of its officers, agents and employees such powers and duties as may be deemed necessary and proper. The authority shall have perpetual existence.
(Res. of 1-22-1998, § 7)
Sec. 2-139. - Financial statement.
The development authority shall provide to the board of commissioners an audited financial statement within six months of the end of the previous fiscal year of the authority if such audit is required by the board of commissioners. The fiscal year of the authority shall be established as a fiscal year from July 1 through June 30 of each year.
(Res. of 1-22-1998, § 8)
Sec. 2-140. - Applicability of state law.
The provisions of O.C.G.A. § 36-62-5(e) shall apply to the directors of the development authority.
(Res. of 1-22-1998, § 9)
Sec. 2-141. - Powers.
The development authority shall have all of the powers authorized by law for an authority, including but not limited to the powers set out in O.C.G.A. § 36-62-2 and O.C.G.A. § 36-62-6.
(Res. of 1-22-1998, § 10)
Sec. 2-142. - Ownership of property.
The development authority established and reestablished pursuant to this article shall be deemed to assume ownership and control of any property previously owned by such authority and shall be deemed to have assumed any and all obligations of such previous authority as of the effective date of the resolution from which this article is derived.
(Res. of 1-22-1998, § 11)
Secs. 2-143—2-175. - Reserved.
