ARTICLE IV. - JUNK DEALERS[2]

Sec. 10-119. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Junk dealer means any person who deals in the purchase and sale of old iron, rags, paper, clothes or other similar things recognized as and coming within commodities commonly known as junk.
(Code 1978, § 13-20; Code 1993, pt. II, § 22-146)
Sec. 10-120. - Business hours; holding period.
It shall be unlawful for any junk dealer in the city to purchase any of the articles known as, or coming under the definition of junk, before sunrise or after sundown or during any night; and each junk dealer shall acquire a designated public place somewhere in the city, at which point he shall deposit each day's junk purchased, and let it remain there for at least 24 hours, subject to inspection by any person who might be interested in any article which may have been stolen and sold to such junk dealer.
(Code 1978, § 13-23; Code 1993, pt. II, § 22-149)
Sec. 10-121. - Purchase from minor.
No junk dealer shall purchase anything from any person under 18 years of age, except on inspection thereof by the chief of police and after the chief of police has given written permission so to purchase to the junk dealer.
(Code 1978, § 13-25; Code 1993, pt. II, § 22-151)
Sec. 10-122. - Daily reports.
Daily reports at the end of each business day shall be filed with the chief of police by each junk dealer, and the daily report shall show the goods purchased by the junk dealer on the day which the report is made, and from whom purchased, and shall be accompanied by a report in writing made by the junk dealer, which report shall set forth the statement made by each person from whom any goods were purchased by the junk dealer during that day, as to the source from which the property purchased was acquired by the one selling to the junk dealer. The giving of misinformation in the daily reports required, or a failure to make the daily reports, shall be a violation of this Code.
(Code 1978, § 13-27; Code 1993, pt. II, § 22-153)
Sec. 10-123. - Inspections; prohibited articles.
All houses or places of business where a junk business is conducted shall at all times be subject to visitation and inspection by the police of the city. If any person who conducts such a business shall at any time refuse to submit to such visitation or inspection, the city manager shall immediately close the place of business of such person so refusing. In case any machinery or any part thereof or any appliance of any railroad shop or gas company, or of the waterworks, or sample waste or loose cotton, grates or any part thereof, gas or waterworks fixtures shall be found in any junk shop, without a sufficient explanation of the same, it shall subject the owner, or person conducting such shop, upon conviction, to punishment prescribed in section 1-11; and conviction of a second offense shall be a just and conclusive cause for the revocation of the license of such business.
(Code 1978, § 13-28; Code 1993, pt. II, § 22-154)
