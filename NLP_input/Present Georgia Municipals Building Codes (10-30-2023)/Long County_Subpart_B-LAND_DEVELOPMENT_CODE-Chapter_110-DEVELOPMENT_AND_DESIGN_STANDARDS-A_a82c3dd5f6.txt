ARTICLE VI. - SIGN REGULATIONS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 110-138. - Introduction.
No sign shall be constructed, altered or maintained within the City of Ludowici or unincorporated areas of Long County except in conformance with the requirements of this subpart.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 12.1)
Sec. 110-139. - Purpose.
The right to identify a place must be consistent with the objectives of the community to retain its unique character and economic advantages, which rest largely on the quality of its appearance.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 12.1.1)
Sec. 110-140. - Intent.
It is the intent of these regulations to promote public welfare and to protect private and public property through fair and consistent enforcement of sign control.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 12.1.2)
Sec. 110-141. - State code adopted.
Where incidents of conflict between the state sign code and the county sign code exist, the more stringent regulation shall apply.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 12.1.3)
Sec. 110-142. - Traffic safety.
No sign as regulated by this subpart shall be erected or continued to be displayed at the intersection of any street or any public right-of-way in such a manner as to obstruct free and clear vision; or at any location, by reason of the position, shape, or color, so that it may interfere with, obstruct the view of, or be confused with, any authorized traffic sign, signal or device; or which makes use of the words, "stop," "look," "drive-in," "danger," or any other word, phrase, symbol or character in such manner as to interfere with, mislead, or confuse traffic.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 12.1.4)
Sec. 110-143. - Sign area calculation.
Sign area shall be calculated as follows:
(1)
Two-sided signs. Count the area of the largest side.
(2)
Signs with more than two sides. Multiply the area of the largest side by the number of sides, and then divide by two.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 12.2)
Sec. 110-144. - Unsafe signs.
If a sign displayed is found to be unsafe or unsecured by the building official, the owner, lessee or property owner shall be asked to disassemble, remove or repair the sign. If, in the opinion of the building official, the sign constitutes an immediate peril to persons or property, and the owner cannot be contacted, the governing authority may immediately exercise condemnation proceedings to cause removal of said sign, and the expense thereof shall constitute a lien upon the property upon which said sign is located or attached.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 12.6)
Sec. 110-145. - Exemptions.
(a)
Small signs. All non-illuminated signs smaller than six square feet on each side are considered to be exempt from permits or regulation.
(b)
Dual purpose signs. All single-faced signs which are in any manner attached or fixed flat to an exterior wall of a building or portion of a functional structure are considered to be exempt from permits or regulation. No portion of such sign may project more than eight inches beyond the portion of the structure to which it is attached.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 12.3)
Secs. 110-146—110-173. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - REGULATED SIGNS

Sec. 110-174. - Authorization required.
All regulated signs erected, and existing signs that are altered or repaired at a cost of 50 percent of the replacement value, unless exempted by this subpart, must be permitted and/or licensed by the county.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 12.4)
Sec. 110-175. - Location.
Signs may not be located in residential areas unless required by other laws, and these signs shall not be illuminated.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 12.4.1)
Sec. 110-176. - Area limit.
Total square footage of all regulated signs on a lot shall be no greater than one one-hundredth of the lot size. No one sign shall be larger than 120 square feet in size at the property line.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 12.4.2)
Sec. 110-177. - Height limit.
No sign shall be higher than seven feet at the property line.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 12.4.3)
Sec. 110-178. - Relief from sign design requirements.
A sign may be granted relief from the strict application of the design standards for height and total area by adhering to the following requirements:
(1)
Sign height. The maximum sign height may be increased by one foot for every two feet that the entire sign is placed back from all property lines, including side and rear lines. (See figure 1 below.)

(2)
Sign area. The total sign area for any one sign or all regulated signs on a lot may be increased by two square feet for every one foot that all regulated signs is placed back from all property lines, including side and rear lines. Roof signs are not permitted. (See figure 2 below.)

(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 12.5)
Secs. 110-179—110-209. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - NONCONFORMING SIGNS

Sec. 110-210. - Nonconforming defined.
Any sign which by its height, area, and location does not conform to the requirements of this subpart shall be termed nonconforming.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 12.7)
Sec. 110-211. - Alterations to nonconforming signs.
No nonconforming sign shall be structurally altered or moved, unless brought into conformity with the requirements of this subpart, except that such signs may have normal maintenance, including repainting or changes in copy.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 12.7.1)
Sec. 110-212. - Continuation and removal.
All nonconforming signs that were properly permitted and existing before the effective date of this subpart may continue in use in accordance with the other provisions of this subpart.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 12.7.2)
Sec. 110-213. - Damage.
No nonconforming sign that has been damaged by more than 50 percent of the fair market value of the sign immediately prior to damage shall be restored, except in conformity with the regulations of this subpart.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 12.7.3)
Secs. 110-214—110-234. - Reserved.
DIVISION 4. - RESIDENTIAL ZONING DISTRICTS

Sec. 110-235. - General regulation.
When required by laws of other governments, signs in residential zoning districts shall not be illuminated and must be located adjacent to the main residence or business (if applicable) entrance doorway. They shall be no greater in size than two square feet.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 12.8)
Sec. 110-236. - Signs in manufactured home parks.
Only one manufactured home park sign shall be located within the setback area. Such sign shall not exceed a combined total of 150 square feet and may not be illuminated. Sign height shall not exceed seven feet in height, measured from the ground, to include the base or support, and shall display the 911 address.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 12.8.1)
Sec. 110-237. - Signs in residential subdivisions.
Shall be located on private property and shall be presented at the time of the subdivision plan approval, and shall be subject to the approval of the governing authority.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 12.8.2)
Secs. 110-238—110-267. - Reserved.
