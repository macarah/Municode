ARTICLE II. - LICENSING
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 10-19. - Going-out-of-business, etc., sales.
A going-out-of-business sale shall include a fire sale, bankrupt sale, loss-of-lease sale, or any other sale made in anticipation of the termination of a business at its present location. It shall be unlawful for any person to falsely represent a sale as being a going-out-of-business sale. When any person, after advertising a going-out-of-business sale, adds to his stock or fails to go out of business within 90 days, he shall prima facie be deemed to have violated this section.
(Code 1976, § 5-301)
Sec. 10-20. - Solicitors, peddlers or transient merchants.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any peddler, canvasser, solicitor, or transient merchant to ply his trade within the city limits without first obtaining a permit therefor from the city clerk. Every transient merchant shall give notice to the city clerk prior to his arrival in the city.
(b)
This section shall not be applicable to persons selling at wholesale to dealers, nor to newspaper hawkers nor to bona fide merchants who merely deliver goods in the regular course of business, nor to bona fide charitable, religious or philanthropic organizations.
(c)
Solicitors are required to exhibit their permits at the request of any citizen.
(d)
Any applicant who has not been granted a permit within 30 days after he makes application therefor may appeal to the mayor and council. Any solicitor who has had his permit revoked may appeal to the mayor and council.
(Code 1976, § 5-302)
Sec. 10-21. - Charitable solicitors.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to solicit contributions or anything else of value for any real or alleged charitable or religious purpose without first obtaining a permit therefor from the city clerk.
(b)
This section shall not apply to any locally established organization or church operated exclusively for charitable or religious purposes if the solicitations are conducted exclusively among the members thereof or if the solicitations are in the form of collections or contributions at the regular assemblies of any such organization or church.
(c)
Charitable solicitors are required to exhibit their permits at the request of any citizen.
(d)
Any applicant who has not been granted a permit within 30 days after he makes application therefor may appeal to the mayor and council. Any charitable solicitor who has had his permit revoked may appeal to the mayor and council.
(Code 1976, § 5-303)
Sec. 10-22. - Pawnbrokers.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a pawnbroker business without first obtaining a permit therefor from the city clerk.
(b)
Each pawnbroker shall furnish to the chief of police a list each week of every article taken in pawn or bought by said pawnbroker during the previous week, giving a full and accurate description of the article, from whom it was received, and the hour of the day received.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any pawnbroker to refuse to submit his stock of goods or books of account to inspection by any member of the police force.
(d)
Any applicant who has not been granted a permit within 30 days after he makes application therefor may appeal to the mayor and council. Any pawnbroker who has had his permit revoked may appeal to the mayor and council.
(Code 1976, § 5-304)

DIVISION 2. - INSURERS AND FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS

Sec. 10-48. - License fees for life insurers.
An annual license fee is imposed upon each insurer doing business within the city in the amount as provided in the city fee schedule, pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8. For each separate business location in excess of one not covered by section 10-49 which is operating on behalf of such insurers within the city a license fee in the amount as provided in the city fee schedule is levied. For the purposes of this division, the term "insurer" means a company which is authorized to transact business in any of the classes of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5.
(Ord. of 11-1-1983(1), § 1; Ord. of 5-3-2011, § 1)
Sec. 10-49. - License fees for certain other insurers.
For each separate business location not otherwise subject to a license fee hereunder, operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing, and in connection with such loans or sales offers, solicits or takes applications for insurance through a licensed agent of an insurer for insurance, said insurer shall pay an additional license fee per location as provided in the city fee schedule.
(Ord. of 11-1-1983(1), § 2; Ord. of 5-3-2011, § 2)
Sec. 10-50. - Gross premiums tax imposed on life insurers.
An annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums is imposed upon each insurer writing life, accident and sickness insurance within the state in an amount equal to one percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.1. The term "gross direct premiums," as used in this section, means gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees.
(Ord. of 11-1-1983(1), § 4; Ord. of 5-3-2011, § 3)
Sec. 10-51. - Gross premiums tax imposed on other insurers.
An annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums is imposed upon each insurer, other than an insurer transacting business in the class of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5(1) doing business within the state in an amount equal to 2½ percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2. The term "gross direct premiums," as used in this section, means gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees.
(Ord. of 11-1-1983(1), § 5; Ord. of 5-3-2011, § 4)
Sec. 10-52. - Due date for license fees.
License fees shall be due and payable on the first day of each year. Gross premium tax shall be paid as required by O.C.G.A. § 33-8-6 et seq.
(Ord. of 11-1-1983(1), § 6; Ord. of 5-3-2011, § 5)
Sec. 10-53. - Financial institutions business license tax.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93, there is hereby levied for the year 1984, and for each year thereafter, an annual business license tax upon state and national banking associations, federal savings and loan associations and state building and loan associations a business license tax at the rate of 0.25 percent of the gross receipts of said institutions, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93. Notwithstanding any other provisions of this division, the minimum amount of business license tax due from any depository financial institution pursuant to this division shall be $1,000.00 per year.
(Ord. of 12-6-1983, § 1)
Sec. 10-54. - Filing of return.
Each depository financial institution within the city shall file a return of its gross receipts with the city on March 1 of the year following the year in which such gross receipts were measured. Said returns shall be in the manner and in the form prescribed by the commissioner of the department of banking and shall be based upon the allocation method set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(d). The tax levied pursuant to this division shall be assessed and collected based upon the information provided in said return.
(Ord. of 12-6-1983, § 2)

DIVISION 3. - ARCADE LICENSES AND AMUSEMENT GAMES PERMITS

Sec. 10-82. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Arcade means a location where three or more bona fide coin-operated amusement machines are operated.
Bona fide coin-operated amusement machine means the same as this term is defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-17-1(2) and any applicable state regulations.
License means a license issued by the city to operate an arcade.
Location means a business in the city which has or proposes to have a coin-operated amusement machine.
(Ord. No. 2001-006, § I.a., 5-1-2001)
Sec. 10-83. - Arcade license required.
No person shall operate an arcade, as defined in this division, within the city without first obtaining a license from the city pursuant to the requirements of this division.
Sec. 10-84. - Arcade license requirements.
(a)
Distance requirements. Every application for an arcade in the city shall be verified by a plat of survey prepared by a licensed Georgia surveyor or civil engineer, showing a minimum distance of 1,250 feet between the location of the arcade and the following establishments:
(1)
The property line of a parcel containing a private residential dwelling;
(2)
The property line of a parcel containing a public community/activity center, a public library or a branch of any library;
(3)
The property line of a parcel containing a church, shrine, chapel or a mortuary or other place used exclusively for religious purposes;
(4)
The property line of a parcel containing any school or college campus, including any public, private, or church-supported schools which teach the subjects commonly taught in the common schools and colleges of this state;
(5)
A regular school bus stop where a school bus for the transportation of school children in the public or private schools of the county shall board or discharge passengers;
(6)
Any other (different) zoning classification use regulated under the comprehensive zoning ordinance of the city.
The distance requirements set out above shall apply in any and all directions from the property lines of the proposed location, as measured in a straight line to the closest property line of those properties referenced in subsection (a) of this section.
(b)
Considerations. In determining whether or not a license applied for hereunder shall be granted, renewed, transferred or issued to a new location, the following shall be considered in the public interest and welfare:
(1)
Reputation, character. The applicant's reputation, character, trade and business associates or past business ventures, and his mental and physical capacity to conduct this business.
(2)
Previous violations. If the applicant is a previous holder of an arcade license, whether or not he has violated any law, regulation or ordinance relating to such business.
(3)
Manner of conducting prior arcade business. If the applicant is a previous holder of an arcade license, the manner in which he conducted the business thereunder, especially as to the necessity for unusual police observation and inspection in order to prevent the violation of any law, regulation, or ordinance relating to such business.
(4)
Location. The location for which the license is sought, as to the traffic congestion, general character of the neighborhood, and the effect such an establishment would have on the adjacent and surrounding property values.
(5)
Number of licenses in trading area. The number of licenses already granted for similar businesses in the trading areas of the place for which the license is sought.
(6)
Previous revocation of license. If the applicant is a person and/or other entity whose license, issued under the public powers of any governing authority, has been previously suspended or revoked or who has previously had an arcade license suspended or revoked.
(7)
Payment of taxes and other charges. If the applicant and/or business are delinquent in the payment of any local taxes or other fees or charges.
(8)
Congregation of minors. Any circumstances which may cause minors to congregate in the vicinity of the proposed location, even if the location meets the distance requirements under subsection (a) of this section.
(9)
Prior incidents. Evidence that a substantial number of incidents requiring police intervention have occurred during the 12 months immediately preceding the date of the application.
(10)
Previous denial or revocation. The denial of an application or the revocation of a license occurring within the preceding 12 months which was based on the qualifications of the proposed location.
(c)
License renewal.
(1)
All licenses granted hereunder are privilege licenses and shall expire on December 31 of each year. Licensees who desire to renew their licenses shall file applications, together with the requisite fees, with the city clerk for such renewal, upon forms approved by the city, on or before December 1 of each year.
(2)
The city clerk shall submit the renewal application to the city council for approval no later than December 15 of each year. Any licenses that have been placed on probation, have been suspended, or have been revoked by the city council during the year shall be submitted on a separate list to the city council by the city clerk for review and consideration for approval.
(d)
Non-transferability. No license shall be transferred to any other location.
(e)
Conditional license. Any business with coin-operated amusement machines prior to May 1, 2001, which otherwise would have to comply with the provisions of this division shall obtain a conditional license for that location without having to comply with the distance requirements of this section. This conditional license must be renewed every year as required by this division, but shall automatically expire without the right of renewal whenever the current owner or operator ceases to operate the present location, increases the number of coin-operated amusement machines, or changes the type of machines currently in his business.
(Ord. No. 2001-006, § I.b., 5-1-2001)
Sec. 10-85. - Licenses prohibited.
(a)
No licenses may be granted to any person who:
(1)
Has been convicted of a felony in any jurisdiction. A conviction, for purposes of this subsection, includes a guilty plea or a plea of nolo contendere.
(2)
Has been convicted of a non-felonious crime of moral turpitude, lottery, or illegal possession or sale of narcotics or liquors within the five years preceding the filing of the application. A conviction, for purposes of this subsection, includes a guilty plea, or a plea of nolo contendere.
(3)
Has had his license to operate an arcade revoked for cause in any state or territory of the United States within the ten years preceding the filing of the application.
(4)
Has knowingly falsified information or made any material misrepresentation on the application for the license under this division submitted within the ten years preceding the filing of the application.
(b)
Should any such applicant, partner or officer or director of any applicant entity, after a license has been granted, be convicted or plead guilty or nolo contendere to a crime involving moral turpitude, or to the violation of any laws of the state regulating gambling or the lottery laws, said license shall be subject after hearing to immediate suspension or revocation.
(c)
No license shall be issued for the operation of an arcade unless the license holder's place of business is located in an area of the city that is zoned commercial.
(Ord. No. 2001-006, § I.c., I.d., 5-1-2001)
Sec. 10-86. - Application for license.
All applicants for a license to operate an arcade shall give prior written notice of their intention to make such to the city clerk.
(1)
All applicants for licenses hereunder shall give notice that an application has been filed, and of the purpose of making such application, by the publication of an advertisement once a week for four consecutive weeks prior to the date of consideration of the application by the city council in the newspaper in which legal advertisements are published for the county. The first advertisement shall not appear more than 40 days prior to the date of such consideration. The advertisement shall be of a print type not smaller than ten point, capital and lower case, and shall be at least a one-inch-wide column. The advertisement shall appear on the same days as legal advertisements are regularly published.
(2)
The notice shall contain a particular description of the location of the proposed business, the name of the applicant, the date, time, and place of the hearing, and a statement that any legal objections to the issuance must be made at or prior to the time of the hearing, and, if prior to the time of the hearing, must be in writing and received by the city clerk on or before the date and time of the hearing.
(3)
Applications shall provide proof of such notice by providing the city clerk, prior to the time of the hearing on the license issuance, the affidavit of the publisher of the newspaper in which said notice was published, which affidavit shall reflect the content of the notice and the dates of publishing.
(4)
The applicant shall cause to be placed upon the site of the proposed business a sign stating the following: "City of Warrenton arcade license applied for. A hearing before the city council of the City of Warrenton shall be held on the ___ day of ___________, 20___, at ___:___ (a.m./p.m.) in the City Council Room, City Hall, 309 Main Street, Warrenton, Georgia. All interested persons take notice."
The sign shall be at least 24 inches by 36 inches in size, containing lettering not less than two inches in height with said signs facing toward all public streets, sidewalks, or other public property which adjoins the location so as to be clearly visible by persons using such public area. The sign shall be posted on the property from the date of the first publication of the legal advertisement through the date of consideration by the city council.
(5)
In addition to paying the costs of any advertisement or notice, or other requirements as provided by this division, the applicant shall pay the license fee in an amount to be established by the mayor and council from time to time.
(Ord. No. 2001-006, § I.e., 5-1-2001)
Sec. 10-87. - Suspension of licenses.
In addition to any penalty provided by this Code or other legal remedies available, the city council shall have the right to suspend any license issued under this division whenever a licensee violates any law, or knowingly permits illegal activity upon the premises, or when the health, morals, interest, and convenience of the public demand the suspension of such license. Any licensee whose license is suspended hereunder shall have the right to a hearing before the city council within 30 days of the suspension, with the right to present evidence, after which the license shall be suspended, placed on probation, or permanently revoked, or otherwise it shall be restored and remain in full force.
(Ord. No. 2001-006, § I.f., 5-1-2001)
Sec. 10-88. - Amusement game permit required.
(a)
In order to record where bona fide coin-operated amusement machines have been placed in a business within the city, any machine owner who desires to place one or more bona fide coin-operated amusement machines in a business shall complete and mail a registration for an amusement game permit accompanied by a fee as provided in the city fee schedule for the city clerk, who shall issue a permit to the machine owner for placing the machine in that business location. Failure to obtain the permit required by this section shall result in a fine in the amount of $50.00, which shall be imposed on the machine owner. This section is supplemental to any other applicable regulations imposed by federal, state and local laws.
(b)
For the purposes of this section, the following terms shall have the meanings ascribed in this subsection:
Bona fide coin-operated amusement machine means the same as this term is defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-17-1(2).
Machine owner means any person, firm or corporation which owns a bona fide coin-operated amusement machine.
Permit means an amusement game permit issued pursuant to this division.
(Ord. No. 2001-006, § II, 5-1-2001)

DIVISION 4. - RACETRACKS AND RECREATIONAL VEHICLE TRACKS

Sec. 10-120. - Permit required.
(a)
No person shall operate in the city any race track as defined herein without possessing the necessary permits issued by the city and/or state as required by law.
(b)
It is unlawful for any person to manage, conduct, carry on or own the business of operating a go-kart track unless the location and establishment of such business shall be subject to the city's zoning now or hereafter to be in effect and shall comply therewith in all respects, or said business is considered a nonconforming use thereunder.
(c)
It is unlawful for any person to manage, conduct, carry on or own the business of operating a go-kart track unless such person shall, prior to the conducting, managing, carrying on or establishing of the go-kart track, obtain and maintain during the period of operation of said business public liability insurance covering personal injuries, property damages and medical expenses at proper limits per occurrence. Compliance with this provision shall be deemed complete when said person furnishes the city with proof of insurance coverage by a reputable insurance carrier and with an endorsement to the effect that the city shall receive at least ten days' notice prior to the cancellation of such insurance coverage.
(Ord. No. 2018-07(4), art. I, 7-19-2018)
Sec. 10-121. - Restrictions on operations.
(a)
Days/hours of operation. Days and hours of operations are restricted as follows:
(1)
No person shall operate or permit the operation thereof of said track within the city on any day except Fridays, Saturdays and Sundays.
(2)
No person shall operate or permit the operation thereof of said track within the city on Fridays and Saturdays before the hour of 10:00 a.m. and after the hour of 12:30 a.m., and on Sundays before the hour of 12:00 noon and after the hour of 9:00 p.m.
(b)
Mufflers required. No person shall operate or permit the operation thereof of a go-kart which is not equipped with an adequate muffler in constant operation and properly maintained to prevent excessive or unusual noise.
(c)
Intensity and duration of noise limited. No person shall at any time allow the operation of the go-kart track to be conducted in such a manner as to cause unreasonable loud or disturbing noises of such a character, intensity or duration as to be detrimental to the peace, welfare or good order of the city or in such a manner as to cause disturbing, noisy, riotous or tumultuous conduct.
(d)
Certain devices restricted. Loud speakers, announcing devices, horns and other noise-producing devices shall not at any time be operated in such a manner as to disturb the occupants of the premises in the vicinity of the track and shall be toned down, muffled or subdued so that the sound therefrom shall not carry more than 500 feet from the perimeter of the track in all directions.
(e)
Noxious odors, dust, fumes and smoke prohibited. No person shall at any time allow the operation of the track to be conducted in such a manner as to allow the creation and dispensing through the air to the adjoining areas of the city of noxious odors, fumes, smoke or dust of such density or concentration as to be detrimental to the health, peace, welfare and good order of the city or as to hurt, destroy or deface the property of the inhabitants of the city. If any event is being conducted at any time upon the track for the operation of which a permit has been issued hereunder when the ground or surface of the track is so dry as to cause dirt or dust to be stirred up either by the racing vehicles or by vehicles transporting spectators to or from the track, to be blown or to drift to adjacent areas, the permittee shall sprinkle the track and its approaches with water or other substances so as to settle such dust or dirt.
(Ord. No. 2018-07(4), arts. II, III, 7-19-2018)
Sec. 10-122. - Maintenance required.
Go-kart tracks and all related facilities and equipment shall be maintained in good repair and proper working order. The chief of police or his delegate shall have the right to periodically inspect all go-kart tracks and facilities in order to determine that all persons governed by this division remain in compliance with the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 2018-07(4), art. IV, 7-19-2018)
