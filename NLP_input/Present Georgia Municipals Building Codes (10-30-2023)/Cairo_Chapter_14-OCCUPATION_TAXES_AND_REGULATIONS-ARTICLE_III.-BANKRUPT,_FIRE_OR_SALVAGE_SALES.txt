ARTICLE III. - BANKRUPT, FIRE OR SALVAGE SALES

Sec. 14-40. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the following terms shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them:
Bankrupt goods: Goods, wares or merchandise which have been sold or brought at a bankrupt, trustee or receiver sale.
Shelf-worn goods: Goods which have been unpacked and placed in the general stock of goods, wares or merchandise of any retail merchant.
Strictly bankrupt sale: A sale by one who advertises by placard or otherwise over such person's store or place of business, or in any newspaper or by handbills, or otherwise that a bankrupt sale is being carried on in such store or place of business, or that the stock therein being sold is a bankrupt stock.
(Code 1965, § 5-20)
Sec. 14-41. - License for sale of bankrupt or shelf-worn goods.
There is hereby levied a licensed tax in the sum of fifteen dollars ($15.00) upon each one hundred dollar ($100.00) purchase of bankrupt or shelf-worn goods brought or shipped into the city for the purpose of sale.
(Code 1965, § 5-21)
Sec. 14-42. - Strictly bankrupt sales—Contents of advertisements.
If any bankrupt sale of goods or merchandise is advertised, such advertisement shall state the former location and owner of the stock offered for sale within the city.
(Code 1965, § 5-22)
Sec. 14-43. - Same—Additions to stock prohibited.
No person shall add any additional merchandise to a strictly bankrupt sale conducted as such within the city.
(Code 1965, § 5-23)
Sec. 14-44. - Permit for additions to bankrupt, fire or salvage stock—Required.
When any bankrupt, fire or salvage stock of goods shall be sold or offered for sale, no addition to the stock of goods shall be made, nor shall such stock be offered for sale after any additional merchandise or goods have been added thereto, without first obtaining a permit from the city.
(Code 1965, § 5-24)
Sec. 14-45. - Same—Issuance.
The permit shall be granted only after the licensing officer shall find that additions to bankrupt, fire or salvage stocks are of new and salable merchandise.
(Code 1965, § 5-25)
Sec. 14-46. - Same—When permit prohibited.
No permit shall be granted authorizing additions to bankrupt, fire or salvage stocks of goods, wares and merchandise from any other stock of fire, bankrupt, or salvage goods and merchandise, whether or not located within the city.
(Code 1965, § 5-26)
Sec. 14-47. - Combining stocks of merchandise—Prohibited.
No person shall combine, in whole or in part, one or more bankrupt, fire or salvage stocks of goods, wares and merchandise for the purpose of offering same for sale with any other stock of goods, wares or merchandise at retail within the city.
(Code 1965, § 5-27)
Sec. 14-48. - Same—Interpretation.
It is hereby declared to be the purpose and intention of section 14-47 that any such stock of goods, wares and merchandise shall be sold or offered for sale separately when sold at retail within the city.
(Code 1965, § 5-28)
Sec. 14-49. - Same—Selling or offering for sale.
No person shall sell or offer for sale at retail any stocks of goods, wares or merchandise after they have been so combined contrary to the above provisions.
(Code 1965, § 5-29)
Secs. 14-50—14-59. - Reserved.
