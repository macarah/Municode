Chapter 60 - MANUFACTURED HOMES[1]

Sec. 60-1. - Scope.
These provisions shall be applicable only to manufactured homes and shall apply to the following:
(1)
Construction, alteration, and repair of any foundation system for the installation of a manufactured home.
(2)
Installation, addition, alteration, repair, or maintenance of any new or used manufactured home, or any service equipment necessary to connect the manufactured home to water, fuel, electrical power, and sewage systems.
(3)
Alterations, additions, or repairs to existing manufactured homes.
(4)
Relocation into the city of any manufactured home that is not presently located within the city.
(5)
Relocation within the city of any manufactured home presently located within the city.
(Ord. No. 2000-04, § 8-8-1, 6-19-2000)
Sec. 60-2. - Additions, alterations, and repairs.
Additions, alterations, or repairs to manufactured homes and their service equipment shall comply with all the requirements of the provisions for new construction, except as specifically provided in the section. Non-structural alterations and repairs to an existing manufactured home may be made with materials equivalent to those of which the manufactured home is constructed if it causes no adverse effect on any structural or fire protection component, and subject to the building official's approval. Additions shall not be structurally supported by, or attached to, the manufactured home unless engineering calculations are submitted to substantiate any proposed structural connection. Additions to manufactured homes shall conform to one of the following:
(1)
Be certified under National Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards Act of 1974 (42 U.S.C. Section 401 et seq.).
(2)
Be designed and constructed in conformance with the construction codes adopted by the city.
(3)
Minor alterations, and repairs to existing manufactured homes and their service equipment may be made in accordance with the codes in effect at the time the original installation was made subject to approval of the building official, and provided such alteration or repair will not cause the manufactured home or its service equipment to become unsafe or unsanitary.
(Ord. No. 2000-04, § 8-8-2, 6-19-2000)
Sec. 60-3. - Existing homes.
Manufactured homes lawfully in existence at the time of the adoption of this code may have their use, occupancy, maintenance, and repair continued if the use, maintenance or repair does not create a hazard to life, health or property, as set forth in the code of the city.
(Ord. No. 2000-04, § 8-8-3, 6-19-2000)
Sec. 60-4. - Property maintenance.
All manufactured homes and their service equipment shall be maintained in a safe and sanitary condition. The owner or his/her designated agent shall be responsible for the maintenance of the manufactured home, accessory structures, and the associated service equipment.
(Ord. No. 2000-04, § 8-8-4, 6-19-2000)
Sec. 60-5. - Location.
Manufactured homes which are to be relocated within the city must comply with these provisions:
(1)
No used manufactured home being moved into the city or being relocated within the city shall be allowed to locate for permanent or temporary occupancy if said manufactured home is more than five years old.
Exception: A manufactured home may be moved into, or relocated within the city that is more than five years old provided the home was manufactured after June 15, 1976, and further provided one of the following is met:
a.
A home not presently located within the city must pass an inspection by a professional architect or engineer licensed in the State of Georgia, or by a building inspector certified by the Southern Building Code Congress. This inspection is to verify that the home is structurally sound, that the exterior is free from rot and rust and is uniform in appearance, and that all systems including smoke detectors, plumbing, electrical, heating, ventilation, and gas are in safe working condition. Proof of inspection must be provided as a sworn affidavit on a form provided by the city building official. The city building official reserves the right to perform a review inspection at no charge to the owner prior to the home being moved into the city; or
b.
Said manufactured home, not presently located in the city, and passes an inspection by the city code department prior to being relocated into the city. Manufactured homes requiring an inspection before being moved into the city will be charged a fee as adopted by the mayor and council and kept on file at the city code office and the city clerk's office.
c.
Said manufactured home, that is presently located within the city, passes an inspection by the city code department prior to being relocated within the city. Manufactured homes requiring an inspection before being relocated within the city will be charged a fee as adopted by the mayor and council and kept on file at the city code office and the city clerk's office.
(2)
Manufactured homes and accessory buildings shall be located on the property in accordance with the applicable codes and ordinances of the city.
(Ord. No. 2000-04, § 8-8-5, 6-19-2000)
Sec. 60-6. - Permits.
The issuance of any permit required by this section shall be conditioned upon the owner of said manufactured home submitting proof to the county tax commissioner that all state and county taxes therefore accruing and payable with respect to said home have in fact been paid.
(1)
Manufactured home location permits. No more, hauler, person, firm, corporation, or other entity shall:
a.
Relocate an existing manufactured home to any location within the city without obtaining a manufactured home location permit from the city code department.
b.
Relocate a manufactured into the city without obtaining a manufactured home location permit from the city code department.
c.
Locate any new manufactured home in the city without obtaining a manufactured location permit from the city code department. A manufactured home located on the lot of a licensed dealer authorized to buy and sell manufactured homes is not required to obtain a manufactured home location.
(2)
Other permits required. No person, firm, corporation, or other entity shall:
a.
Alter, remodel, repair or expand any manufactured home without obtaining permits from the city code department. Alteration or expansion to the structure or any system including building, electrical, plumbing, heating, ventilation, air conditioning, and gas require permits from the city code department. Cosmetic repairs are exempted from permits.
b.
Building permit is required for the placement and set-up of any Class "A" manufactured home.
c.
The installation, repair, replacement, or addition to any electrical, plumbing, heating, ventilation, air conditioning, or gas piping system will require permits as described elsewhere in this Code.
(3)
Permit fees.
a.
The fee for manufactured home permits shall be in accordance as adopted by the mayor and council and kept on file at the city code office and the city clerk's office.
b.
Electrical permit fees are established as adopted by the mayor and council and kept on file at the city code office and the city clerk's office.
c.
Plumbing permit fees are established as adopted by the mayor and council and kept on file at the city code office and the city clerk's office.
d.
Heating, ventilation, air conditioning, and gas permit fees are established as adopted by the mayor and council and kept on file at the city code office and the city clerk's office.
(Ord. No. 2000-04, § 8-8-6, 6-19-2000)
Sec. 60-7. - Inspections.
All work for which a permit is required shall be subject to inspection by the building official. It is the responsibility of the permit applicant to cause the work to be accessible and exposed for inspection purposes.
(Ord. No. 2000-04, § 8-8-7, 6-19-2000)
Sec. 60-8. - Final inspection.
When the manufactured home installation is complete, including landings, skirting, service equipment, soil erosion control, and finish grading, and the home is ready for occupancy, a final inspection shall be made. Utility service shall not be provided to any building service equipment that is regulated by this code until a final inspection is performed and approved by the building official.
(Ord. No. 2000-04, § 8-8-8, 6-19-2000)
Sec. 60-9. - Footings and foundations.
Footing and foundation design shall conform to the following:
(1)
Class "A" manufactured homes must be placed on a permanent foundation, either slab or pier, which meets the requirements of the state building code.
(2)
Class "B" manufactured homes are not required to have poured concrete or permanent foundations. Piers must be spaced per manufacturer instructions.
(Ord. No. 2000-04, § 8-8-9, 6-19-2000)
Sec. 60-10. - Skirting.
Each manufactured home requires skirting conforming to the following:
(1)
Class "A" manufactured homes must have a masonry curtain wall, unpierced except for the required ventilation and access, installed so that it encloses the area under the manufactured home to ground level.
(2)
Class "B" manufactured homes shall be completely skirted with either masonry material, or skirting material manufactured for this purpose, and the skirting shall reach to the ground level.
(Ord. No. 2000-04, § 8-8-10, 6-19-2000)
Sec. 60-11. - Landings and steps.
For every manufactured home located in the city, a landing is required for each exterior door. The minimum size of the landing is four [feet] × four [feet]. The structure must include steps that lead to the ground level. Both the steps and landings must be constructed to meet the adopted building code of the city.
(Ord. No. 2000-04, § 8-8-11, 6-19-2000)
Sec. 60-12. - Anchorage.
All manufactured homes must be tied down per manufacturer recommendations, and the requirements listed in Appendix H of the state building code.
(Ord. No. 2000-04, § 8-8-12, 6-19-2000)
