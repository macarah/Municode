DIVISION 2. - COIN-OPERATED AMUSEMENT DEVICES

Sec. 16-101. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Coin-operated amusement device.
(1)
The term "coin-operated amusement device," as used in this division, means any amusement machine or device operated by means of the insertion of a coin, token, or similar object, for the purpose of amusement or skill and for the playing of which a fee is charged.
(2)
The term "coin-operated amusement device" does not include vending machines in which are not incorporated gaming or amusement features, nor does the term include any coin-operated mechanical musical devices.
(3)
The term "coin-operated amusement device" does not include commercial establishments only featuring an array of large mechanical or electronic games, such as pinball machines or video games, that charge players money before each game and which are primarily aimed at doing business for minors.
Operator, as used in this chapter, means any person who sets up for operation by another or leases or distributes for the purpose of operation by another, any device as defined in this section, whether such setting up for operation, leasing or distributing is for a fixed charge or rental, or on the basis of a division of the income derived from such device, or otherwise.
Proprietor means any person or club who, as the owner, lessee, or proprietor, has under his or its control any establishment, place or premises in or at which such device is placed or kept for use or play, or on exhibition for the purpose of use or play.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-102. - License required.
No person shall engage in the business of an operator or proprietor of a coin-operated amusement device, as the term is defined in section 16-101, without first obtaining the proper license therefor.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-103. - Operator's fee.
The license fee for each operator shall be $250.00 per year for the first 50 devices set up for operation, or leased or distributed to any proprietor. If more than 50 devices are set up for operation, or leased or distributed by the same operator, then such operator shall take out another operator's license at the fee of $100.00 for each additional 25 devices, or fraction thereof in excess of the first 50. All operator's license fees shall be payable annually in advance, provided that where an application is made after the expiration of any portion of any license year, a license may be issued for the remainder thereof upon the payment of a proportionate part of the annual fee. In no case shall any portion of the license fee be refunded to the licensee.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-104. - Proprietor's fee.
The license fee for each proprietor as defined in section 16-101 shall be $50.00 per year for each device used or played, or exhibited for use or play. All proprietors' license fees shall be payable annually in advance, provided that where an application is made after the expiration of any portion of any license year, a license may be issued for the remainder thereof upon the payment of a proportionate part of the annual fee. In no case shall any portion of the license fee be repaid to the licensee.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-105. - Application; contents; fee.
(a)
Application for a license under this article shall be filed in writing with the city clerk, on a form to be provided by the city, and shall specify:
(1)
The name and address of the applicant, and if a firm, corporation, partnership or association, the principal officers thereof and their addresses.
(2)
The address of the premises where the licensed devices are to be operated, together with the character of the business as carried on at such place.
(3)
That the applicant is of good character and has never been convicted of a felony.
(4)
The trade name and general description of the devices to be licensed, the name of the manufacturer and the serial number and, if the applicant is a proprietor, the number of devices to be licensed.
(5)
The name and address of the operator of the devices, if other than the proprietor.
(b)
The proper license fee shall accompany such application for a license under this article. It shall be referred by the city clerk to the mayor and council who shall make or cause to be made such investigation as they deem necessary. If the application is approved by the mayor and council, the license shall be issued by the clerk. If the license is denied the fee shall be returned to the applicant. All licenses under this article shall expire on December 31 following their issuance. The license shall be posted in a conspicuous place in the establishment of the licensee. Such license shall be nonassignable and nontransferable and, in the case of a proprietor, shall apply only to the premises for which such license is issued.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-106. - Increasing number of devices.
In case a proprietor licensed under the provisions of this article desires, after the expiration of any portion of any license year, to increase the number of devices to be used or played, or exhibited for use or play in his establishment, he shall surrender his license to the city clerk who shall issue a new license showing the number of devices licensed thereunder, upon the payment of the proper license fee therefor.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-107. - Regulations on amusement devices.
(a)
No coin-operated amusement device therein shall be operated so as to constitute a public nuisance.
(b)
It shall be the responsibility of the licensee to maintain order on the licensed premises at all times.
(c)
It shall be the responsibility of the licensee to see that the licensed premises do not become overcrowded so as to constitute a hazard to the health or safety of persons therein.
(d)
Every premises licensed under this article shall have affixed within the building in plain view evidence of the issuance of its license, however, it is not required that the license be affixed to each machine.
(f)
The license required and described in this article shall be purely a personal privilege and shall not constitute property. It is not transferable in any manner.
(g)
Any violation of the Criminal Code of Georgia (O.C.G.A. Title 26), or of any ordinance, may result in revocation of a license issued under this article.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-108. - Gambling prohibited.
The owner, operator or manager of any establishment with coin-operated amusement devices shall not allow gambling of any kind to occur upon such premises.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Secs. 16-109—16-125. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - AMUSEMENT PARKS, CARNIVALS, AND OTHER EXHIBITIONS

Sec. 16-126. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Amusement park or carnival means an establishment or area offering amusement activities, rides, merry-go-rounds, booths for the conduct of games of skill, goods-dispensing facilities, and sideshows.
Public exhibition means and includes circuses, menageries, sideshows, and other similar itinerant amusement enterprises which are open to the public and for admission to which a fee is charged.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-127. - Prohibited use.
No person, firm, or corporation shall conduct or operate an amusement park, circus, carnival, or public exhibition, as such terms are defined herein, without having first obtained a license from the city clerk in the same manner as provided in chapter 16, article I, including paying the applicable license fee.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-128. - Conditions precedent to issuance of license.
No license under this section shall be issued until the following conditions have been met:
(1)
The operator and sponsor of the amusement park, circus, carnival, or public exhibition have each assumed full responsibility for maintaining order and for keeping the site clean and free of trash, papers, and other debris, and have placed trash containers in adequate number and in convenient locations for the use of the public.
(2)
All rides have been inspected for mechanical, structural, electrical, and other hazards by the appropriate officers and employees of the state, and adequate safeguards have been placed to protect both operators and the general public from inadvertently coming into contact with moving parts, belts, motor gears, electrical switches, and other possible or potential hazards. A copy of the proof of inspection and any required state license must be attached to the application.
(3)
The applicant has placed on file with the city clerk a certificate or certificates of insurance indicating that there is in effect public liability insurance covering any damages arising out of the use and operation of any and all devices and facilities operated in connection with such carnival or exhibition. Such insurance shall be in the minimum amount of $100,000.00 for each person, and $300,000.00 for each accident.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-129. - Inspections.
The chief of police and the fire chief shall see that proper inspections and patrols are made of the premises used for the activities licensed herein.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-130. - Applicability of state law.
Any person who shall operate an amusement park, circus, carnival, or other public exhibition in the city shall at all times be in full compliance with applicable state licensing and regulatory provisions. Any violation of state law shall be grounds for denial of issuance or suspension or revocation of any license issued under this chapter.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))

DIVISION 4. - ADULT ENTERTAINMENT
Subdivision I. - Generally

Sec. 16-151. - Purpose and intent.
The purpose of this article is to regulate certain types of businesses, including, but not limited to, adult entertainment establishments, to the end that the many types of adverse secondary effects frequently engendered by such businesses including criminal activities will be curtailed; provided, however, it is recognized that such regulation cannot de facto approach prohibition because the court has determined that adult entertainment is a protected form of expression. Otherwise a protected form of expression would vanish. As to adult dance establishments, this article represents a balancing of competing interests-reduced criminal activity and the preservation of the quality of urban life through the regulation of adult entertainment establishments and patrons.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-152. - Unlawful operation declared nuisance.
Any adult entertainment establishment operated, conducted or maintained contrary to the provisions of this article shall be and the same is hereby declared to be unlawful and a public nuisance. The city may, in addition to, or in lieu of prosecuting a quasi-criminal action hereunder, commence an action or actions, proceeding or proceedings for abatement, removal or enjoinment thereof, in the manner provided by law. It shall take such other steps and shall apply to such court or courts as may have jurisdiction to grant such relief as will abate or remove such adult entertainment establishment and restrain and enjoin any person from operation, conduction or maintaining an adult entertainment establishment contrary to the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-153. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Adult bookstore means an establishment having a substantial or significant portion of its stock in trade in books, magazines or other periodicals which are distinguished or characterized by their emphasis on matter depicting, describing or relating to specified sexual activities or specified anatomical areas, or an establishment with a segment or section, comprising five percent of its total floor space, devoted to the sale or display of such material, or five percent of its net sales consisting of printed material which are distinguished or characterized by their emphasis on matter depicting, describing or relating to specified sexual activities or specified anatomical areas.
Adult business means:
(1)
Any business which is conducted exclusively for the patronage of adults and as to which minors are specifically excluded from patronage thereat either by law or by the operators of such business; and
(2)
Any business other than those expressly specified in this article, where employees or patrons regularly expose specified anatomical areas or engage in specified sexual activities; or
(3)
Any other business or establishment which regularly offers its patrons services or entertainment characterized by an emphasis on matter depicting, describing, discussing or relating to specified sexual activities or specified anatomical areas.
Adult dancing establishment means a business that features dancers displaying or exposing specified anatomical areas.
Adult mini-motion picture theater means an enclosed building with a capacity for less than 50 persons used for presenting material distinguished or characterized by an emphasis on matter depicting or relating to specified sexual activities or specified anatomical areas for observation by patrons therein.
Adult motion picture arcade means any place to which the public is licensed or invited wherein coin or slug operated or electronically, electrically or mechanically controlled still or motion picture machines, projectors or other image-producing devices are maintained to show images to five or fewer persons per machine at any one time and where the images so displayed are distinguished or characterized by an emphasis on depicting or describing specified sexual activities or specified anatomical areas.
Adult motion picture theater means an enclosed building with a capacity of 50 or more persons regularly used for presenting material distinguished or characterized by an emphasis on matter depicting, describing or relating to specified sexual activities or specified anatomical areas for observation by patrons therein.
Adult video store means an establishment having a substantial or significant portion of its stock in trade in video tapes or movies or other reproductions, for sale or rent, which are distinguished or characterized by their emphasis on matter depicting, describing or relating to specified sexual activities or specified anatomical areas or an establishment with a segment or section, comprising five percent of its total floor space, devoted to the sale or display of such material or which derives more than five percent of its net sales from videos which are characterized or distinguished by their emphasis on matter depicting, describing or relating to specified sexual activities or specified anatomical areas.
City clerk means the person designated from time to time by the city council to administer the provisions of this article.
Encounter center or rap establishment means any business, agency or person who, for any form of consideration or gratuity, provides a place where two or more persons regularly congregate, assemble or associate for the primary purpose of engaging in, describing, or discussing specified sexual activities, or exposing specified anatomical areas.
Erotic dance establishment means a nightclub, theater or other establishment which regularly features live performances by topless and/or bottomless dancers, go-go dancers, strippers or similar entertainers, where such performances are distinguished or characterized by an emphasis on specified sexual activities or specified anatomical areas.
Escort bureau or introduction service means any business, agency or persons whose primary business is to, for a fee, commission, hire, reward or profit furnishes or offers to furnish names of persons, or who introduces, furnishes or arranges for persons who may accompany other persons to or about social affairs, entertainment or places of amusement, or who may consort with others about any place of public resort or within any private quarters.
Good moral character means a person that has not been convicted of a felony or any crime not a felony if it involves moral turpitude in the past five years. Conviction shall include pleas of nolo contendere or bond forfeiture when charged with such crime.
Lingerie modeling studio means an establishment whose primary business activity consists of persons exhibiting or modeling lingerie or similar undergarments and characterized or distinguished by their emphasis on matter depicting, describing or relating to specified sexual activities or specified anatomical areas.
Minor means, for the purposes of this article, any person who has not attained the age of 21 years.
Parcel and parcel of land mean any quantity of land capable of being described by location and boundary, designated and used or to be used as a unit.
Specified anatomical areas shall include less than completely and opaquely covered human genitals or pubic region; completely bare buttock; or female breast showing the areola and human male genitals in a discernibly turgid state, unless completely and opaquely covered.
Specified sexual activities shall include any of the following:
(1)
Actual or simulated sexual intercourse, oral copulation, bestiality, direct physical stimulation of unclothed genitals, flagellation or torture in the context of a sexual relationship, or the use of excretory functions in the context of a sexual relationship, any of the following sexually oriented acts or conduct: anilingus, buggery, coprophasy, coprophilia, piguerism, sapphism, zooerasty;
(2)
Clearly depicted human genitals in a state of sexual stimulation, arousal or tumescence;
(3)
Use of human or animal ejaculation, sodomy, oral copulation, coitus, or masturbation;
(4)
Fondling or touching of nude human genitals, pubic region, buttocks or female breast;
(5)
Torture;
(6)
Erotic or lewd touching, fondling or other sexual contact with an animal by a human being;
(7)
Masochism, erotic or sexually oriented beating or the inflection of pain; or
(8)
Human excretion, urination, menstruation, vaginal or anal irrigation.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-154. - Underage persons.
No adult entertainment establishment licensee shall employ or contract as a dancer with a person under the age of 21 years or a person not licensed as provided herein. No person under the age of 21 years shall be admitted to an adult entertainment establishment.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))

Subdivision II. - Licensing

Sec. 16-176. - License required.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person, association, partnership or corporation to engage in, conduct or carry on, in or upon any premises within the city, an adult entertainment establishment, as defined in this article, without a license. No license so issued shall condone or make legal an activity thereunder if the same is deemed illegal or unlawful under state or federal law.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate an adult bookstore, adult motion picture theater, adult mini-motion picture theater, adult hotel or motel, adult motion picture theater, adult hotel or adult motion picture arcade, cabaret, encounter center, escort bureau, modeling studio, adult business or adult dancing establishment unless such business shall have a currently valid license or shall have made proper application for renewal within the time required thereof under this article, which licensee shall not be under suspension or permanently or conditionally revoked.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-177. - Prohibited licensees.
No license shall be issued under this article to or held by:
(1)
An applicant who has not paid all required fees and taxes for a business at that location or property taxes.
(2)
Any person who is not of good moral character.
(3)
Any corporation, any of whose officers, directors or stockholders holding over five percent of the outstanding issued share of capital stock are not of good moral character.
(4)
Any partnership or association, any of whose officers or members holding more than five percent interest therein are not of good moral character.
(5)
Any person employing, assisted by or financed in whole or in part by any person who is not of good moral character.
(6)
Any applicant who is not qualified to hold and conduct business according to the laws of the federal, state, or local law.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-178. - Limitation on aggregate number of licenses.
Should there be a sufficient number of current licenses to meet the needs and desires of the inhabitants of the city, no new licenses shall issue. In determining the needs and desires of the inhabitants, the standard of review shall be that the market is virtually unrestrained as defined in Young vs. American Mini Theaters. Inc., 427 U.S. 50, 70-71, 96 S.Ct. 2440, 49 L.Ed.2d 310 (1976).
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-179. - Applications for licenses; fees.
(a)
Any person, association, partnership or corporation desiring to obtain a license to operate, engage in, conduct or carry on any adult entertainment establishment shall make application to the city clerk or his designated representative.
(b)
The application for licensure shall be made to the city clerk, on the form provided by the clerk, accompanied by a nonrefundable administrative fee and an annual license fee in the amount established by resolution of the city council. The purpose of the administrative fee is to defray, in part, the costs of investigation and report required by this article. In the event the application for license is denied, the annual license fee shall be refunded to the applicant.
(c)
The mere application for license does not authorize the engaging in, operation of, conduct of, or carrying on of any adult entertainment establishment. No such establishment shall operate unless and until its license has been approved by the mayor and council under this article.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-180. - Contents of application.
Each application for an adult entertainment establishment license shall contain the following information:
(1)
The full, true name and any other names used by the applicant.
(2)
The present address and telephone number of the applicant.
(3)
The previous addresses of applicant, if any, for a period of five years immediately prior to the date of the application and the dates of residence at each.
(4)
Business, occupation or employment history of the applicant for the five years immediately preceding the date of application.
(5)
Business or employment records of the applicant, partners in a partnership, directors and officers of a corporation and, if a corporation, all shareholders holding more than five percent of the share of corporate stock outstanding.
(6)
The business license history of the applicant and whether the applicant, in previous operations in this or any other city, state or territory under license, has had the license or license for an adult entertainment business or similar type of business revoked or suspended, the reason therefor, and the business activity or occupation subsequent to the action of suspension or revocation.
(7)
All convictions, including ordinance violations, exclusive of traffic violations, stating the date and places of any the convictions.
(8)
If the applicant is a corporation, LLC, or other business entity, the name of the entity shall be set forth exactly as shown in its articles of incorporation, operating agreements, or other enabling documents, together with the place and date of formation, together with the names and addresses of each of its current members, officers, or directors, and each stockholder holding more than five percent of any stock in the entity. The applicant entity shall designate one of its members or officers to act as its responsible managing officer. The designated person shall complete and sign all application forms required of an individual applicant under this chapter, but only one application fee shall be charged.
(9)
If the applicant is a partnership, the applicant shall set forth the name, residence address and dates of birth of the partners, including limited partners. If the applicant is a limited partnership, it shall furnish a copy of its certificate of limited partnership filed with the court clerk. If one or more of the partners is a corporation, the provisions of this subsection pertaining to corporations shall apply. The applicant partnership shall designate one of its general partners to act as its responsible managing officer. The designated person shall complete and sign all application forms required of an individual applicant under this article, but only one application fee shall be charged.
(10)
The name and address of the owner and lesser of the real property upon which the business is to be conducted, and a copy of the lease or rental agreement.
(11)
The other identification and information as the police department may require in order to discover the truth of the matters herein specified as required to be set forth in the application.
(12)
The age, date of birth, and social security number of the applicant, of any partners, or of any and all officers, of any stockholders of more than five percent of the share of the corporation stock outstanding, directors of the applicant if the applicant is a corporation.
(13)
If the applicant is a person doing business under a trade name, a copy of the trade name properly recorded. If the applicant is a corporation, a copy of the authority to do business in the state, including articles of incorporation, trade name affidavit, if any, and last annual report, if any.
(14)
At least three character references from individuals who are in no way related to the applicant or individual shareholders, officers or directors of a corporation and who are not or will not benefit financially in any way from the application if the license is granted and who have not been convicted of any felony or violation of any federal, state or local law or ordinance involving moral turpitude in the past five years. The chief of police shall prepare forms consistent with the provisions of this subsection for the applicant who shall submit all character references on the forms.
(15)
Address of the premises to be licensed.
(16)
Whether the premises are owned or rented and if the applicant has a right to legal possession of the premises, and a copy of those documents giving the legal right, documents giving legal title conditioned only on the city's granting of a license under this article.
(17)
A certified plat by a registered surveyor or engineer, licensed by the state, showing the location of the proposed premises in relation to the neighborhood, the surrounding zoning, its proximity to any church, school, public park, governmental building or site, or other business hereunder regulated.
(18)
Each application for an adult entertainment establishment license shall be verified and acknowledged in the presence of a notary to be true and correct by:
a.
If the applicant is an individual, the individual;
b.
If by a partnership, by the manager or general partner;
c.
If a corporation, by the president of the corporation;
d.
If any other organization or association, by the chief administrative official, member of other designated representative.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-181. - Applicant to appear.
The applicant, if an individual, or designated responsible managing officer, if a partnership or corporation, shall personally appear before the city and produce proof that a nonrefundable application fee established by resolution of the city council, has been paid and shall present the application containing the aforementioned and described information.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-182. - Investigation.
The city shall have 45 days to investigate the application and the background of the applicant.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-183. - Grant of license; findings.
Upon completion of the investigation, the mayor and council may grant the license if it finds:
(1)
The required annual license fee has been paid.
(2)
The application conforms in all respects to the provisions of this chapter.
(3)
The applicant has not knowingly made a material misrepresentation in the application.
(4)
The applicant has fully cooperated in the investigation of his application.
(5)
The applicant, if an individual, or any of the stockholders of the corporation, any officers or directors, if the applicant is a corporation, or any members, officers or partners, if the applicant is a limited liability corporation or other business entity, the partners, including limited partners, if the applicant is a partnership, has not been convicted in a court of competent jurisdiction of an offense involving conduct or convicted of an attempt to commit any of the above mentioned offenses, or convicted in any state of any offense which if committed or attempted in this state, would have been punishable as one or more of the above mentioned offenses, or any crime involving dishonesty, fraud, deceit, or moral turpitude.
(6)
The applicant has not had an adult entertainment establishment license or other similar license or license denied or revoked for cause by this city or any other city located in or out of this state prior to the date of application.
(7)
The building, structure, equipment, or location of the business, as proposed by applicant, would comply with all applicable laws, including but not limited to health, zoning, distance, fire and safety requirements and standards.
(8)
The applicant is at least 21 years of age.
(9)
The applicant, his or her employee, agent, partner, director, officer, stockholder or manager has not within five years of the date of the application knowingly allowed or licensed any of the specified sexual activities as defined herein to be committed or allowed in or upon the premises where the adult entertainment establishment is to be located or to be used as a place in which solicitation for the specified sexual activities as defined herein openly occur.
(10)
On the date the business for which a license is required herein commences, and thereafter, there will be a responsible person on the premises to act as manager at all times during which the business is open.
(11)
The proposed premises is not located too close to any church, school, library, government building or site, or any other business restricted hereunder (Section 16-221).
(12)
The grant of the license will not cause a violation of this or any other regulation of the city or any state or federal law.
(13)
Any other inquiry deemed necessary or desirable by the city to ensure the health, safety, and welfare of the citizens of the city or the preservation of its neighborhoods.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-184. - Denial of license; appeal.
If the city, following investigation of the applicant, deems that the applicant does not fulfill the requirements as set forth in this chapter, it shall notify the city clerk and, within 45 days of the date of application, provide copies of the investigation report to the clerk. The clerk shall, within ten days thereafter, notify applicant by certified mail of the denial. Any applicant who is denied a license may appeal the denial to the mayor and council.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-185. - Display of license.
An adult entertainment establishment licensee shall conspicuously display all licenses required by this article.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-186. - License renewal.
Licenses for adult entertainment establishments may be renewed on a year-to-year basis provided that the licensees continue to meet the requirements set out in this article. The renewal fee for the adult entertainment establishment licenses shall be established by resolution of the city council.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-187. - Licenses nontransferable.
(a)
No adult entertainment establishment license may be sold, transferred or assigned by a licensee, or by operation of law, to any other person or persons. Any sale, transfer or assignment, or attempted sale, transfer or assignment, shall be deemed to constitute a voluntary surrender of the license and the license shall thereafter be null and void except as otherwise provided in this section.
(b)
As an exception to subsection (a) of this section, if the licensee is a partnership and one or more of the partners should die, one or more of the surviving partners may acquire, by purchase or otherwise, the interest of the deceased partner or partners without effecting a surrender or termination of the license, and in that case the license, upon notification to the city, shall be placed in the name of the surviving partners.
(c)
An adult entertainment establishment license issued to a corporation shall be deemed terminated and void when either any outstanding stock of the corporation is sold, transferred or assigned after the issuance of a license, or any stock authorized but not issued at the time of the granting of a license is thereafter issued and sold, transferred or assigned.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-188. - Change of location or name.
(a)
No adult entertainment establishment shall move from the location specified on its license until a reasonable change of location fee, established by resolution of the city council, has been deposited with the city, and approval has been obtained from the city clerk and the building inspector. Such approval shall not be given unless all requirements and regulations, as contained in the city's codes, have been met.
(b)
No licensee shall operate, conduct, manage, engage in or carry on an adult entertainment establishment under any name other than his name and the name of the business as specified in his license and at the location specified for that name.
(c)
Any application for any extension or expansion of a building or other place of business where an adult entertainment establishment is located shall require inspection and shall comply with the provisions and regulations of this chapter.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Secs. 16-189—16-200. - Reserved.
Subdivision III. - Appeals

Sec. 16-201. - Notice of appeal.
The licensee shall, within ten days after he has been notified of an adverse determination, submit a notice of appeal to the city clerk. The notice of appeal shall be addressed to the city council and shall specify the subject matter of the appeal, the date of any original and amended application or requests, the date of the adverse decision (or receipt of notice thereof), the basis of the appeal, the action requested of the council, and the name of the applicant. The clerk shall place the appeal on the agenda of the next regular city council meeting occurring not less than five and no more than 30 days after receipt of the application for the city council action.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-202. - Discretion of council regarding appeal procedure.
When an appeal is placed on the city council agenda, the city council may take either of the following actions:
(1)
Set a hearing date and instruct the city clerk to give the notice of hearing as may be required by law.
(2)

Sec. 16-203. - Notice of hearing.
The clerk shall be responsible for the publication of notice of the hearing as may be required by law.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-204. - Conduct of hearing before city council.
Whenever the city clerk has scheduled an appeal before the city council, at the time and date set therefor, the city council shall receive all relevant testimony and evidence from the licensee, from interested parties, and from city staff. The city council may sustain, over-rule or modify the action complained of the action of the city council shall be final.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-205. - Conduct of hearing before hearing officer.
The hearing officer, appointed, pursuant to the procedure set out in this article, may receive and rule on admissibility of evidence, hear testimony under oath and call witnesses as he may deem advisable with respect to the conduct of the hearing.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-206. - Rules of evidence inapplicable.
The city council and the hearing officer shall not be bound by the traditional rules of evidence in hearings conducted under this article. Rules of evidence as applied in an administrative hearing shall apply.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-207. - Report of hearing officer; notice of hearing.
(a)
If a hearing officer has been appointed to hear the appeal, the hearing officer shall, within a reasonable time not to exceed 30 days from the date the hearing is terminated, submit a written report to the city council.
(b)
The report shall contain a brief summary of the evidence considered and state findings, conclusions and recommendation.
(c)
All reports shall be filed with the city clerk, and shall be considered public records. A copy of the report shall be forwarded by certified mail to the licensee the same day as it is filed with the city clerk, with additional copies furnished to city council and the chief of police.
(d)
The city clerk shall place the hearing officer's report on the agenda of the next regular city council meeting occurring not less than ten days after the report is filed and shall notify the licensee of the date of the meeting at least ten days prior to the meeting unless the licensee stipulates to a shorter notice period.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-208. - Review of report by city council; determination.
The city council may adopt or reject the hearing officer's decision in its entirety or may modify the proposed recommendation. If the city council does not adopt the hearing officer's recommendation, it may:
(1)
Refer the matter to the same or another hearing officer for a completely new hearing, or for the taking of additional evidence on specific points; in either of the cases the hearing officer shall proceed as provided in this article.
(2)
Decide the case upon a review of the entire record before the hearing officer with or without taking additional evidence.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))

Subdivision IV. - Location Restrictions

Sec. 16-221. - Proximity to other facilities.
No adult business or use restricted hereunder shall be located:
(1)
Within 600 feet of any parcel of land which is either zoned or used for residential purposes;
(2)
Within 600 feet of any parcel of land upon which a church, school, governmental building, library, civic center, public park, recreational facility, hospital, nursing home or playground is located;
(3)
Within 600 feet of any parcel of land upon which another establishment regulated or defined hereunder is located;
(4)
On a tract of land containing less than 100 feet of road frontage;
(5)
In any residential, neighborhood commercial, or downtown business zoning district. It must be located strictly within an industrial zoning district.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-222. - Measurement of distances.
For the purposes of this article, distance shall be by airline measurement from property line using the closest property lines of the parcels of land involved.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))

Subdivision V. - Maintenance of Premises

Sec. 16-241. - Cleaning of licensed premises.
Each licensed premises shall be maintained in a clean and sanitary condition and shall be cleaned at least once daily and more frequently when necessary. This activity shall be supervised by the person in charge of the licensed premises. There shall be provided adequate facilities, equipment and supplies on the licensed premises to meet this requirement, and adequate ventilation and illumination shall be provided to the licensed [premises for] thorough, complete cleaning of the entire licensed premises. Trash and garbage shall not be allowed to accumulate or to become a nuisance on or in the immediate vicinity of the licensed premises but shall be disposed of daily or as often as collections license.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-242. - Self-inspection of premises.
The licensee of a licensed premises or his designated representative shall make sanitary inspection of the licensed premises at least once a month and shall record his findings on a form supplied by the chief of police. Each licensed premises shall post and maintain in a readily accessible place a schedule for maintaining the sanitation of the premises.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-243. - Sealing for unsanitary or unsafe conditions.
A licensed premises, or any part thereof, may be sealed by order of the chief of police on his finding of a violation of this chapter resulting in an unsanitary or unsafe condition. Prior to sealing, the chief of police shall serve on the licensee, by personal service on him or by posting in a conspicuous place on the licensed premises, a notice of the violation and an order to correct it within 24 hours after service. If the violation is not so corrected, the chief of police may physically seal that portion of the licensed premises causing the violation and order the discontinuance of use thereof until the violation has been corrected and the seal removed by the chief of police. The chief of police shall affix to the sealed premises a conspicuous sign labeled "unclean" or "unsafe," as the case may be.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))

Subdivision VI. - Employees

Sec. 16-281. - Qualifications.
(a)
Employees of an adult business establishment shall be not less than 21 years of age.
(b)
Every employee must be of good moral character as defined in this chapter.
(c)
Any employee who is convicted of a crime constituting a felony or a crime not a felony involving moral turpitude while employed as an adult business establishment employee shall not thereafter work on any licensed premises for a period of five years from the date of such conviction, unless a longer time is ordered by a court of competent jurisdiction.
(d)
The term "convicted" shall include an adjudication of guilt, a plea of guilty or nolo contendere or the forfeiture of a bond when charged with a crime, and the terms employed on the licensed premises and work on any licensed premises shall include as well, work done or services performed while in the scope of employment elsewhere than on the licenses premises.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))

Subdivision VII. - Erotic Dance Establishments

Sec. 16-301. - Applicability.
The regulations of this division shall apply to erotic dance establishments in addition to all other requirements of this chapter.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-302. - Advertising prohibited.
No person, firm, partnership, corporation, or other entity shall advertise, or cause to be advertised, an erotic dance establishment located within the city without a valid adult entertainment establishment license issued pursuant to this chapter.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-303. - Alcoholic beverages prohibited.
No erotic dance establishment licensee shall serve, sell, distribute, or suffer the consumption or possession of any intoxicating liquor or controlled substance upon the premises of the licensee.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-304. - Minimum lighting requirements.
All areas of an establishment licensed hereunder shall be fully lighted at all times that patrons are present. Full lighting shall mean illumination equal to 3.5 footcandles per square foot.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-305. - Performance restrictions.
(a)
All dancing shall occur on a platform intended for the purpose, which is raised at least two feet from the level of the first floor.
(b)
No dancing shall occur closer than six feet from the level off the floor.
(c)
No dancer shall touch, fondle or caress any patron and no patron shall touch, fondle or caress any dancer.
(d)
No patron shall directly pay or give any gratuity to any dancer.
(e)
No dancer shall solicit any pay or gratuity from any patron.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
DIVISION 5. - CLOSING HOURS

Sec. 16-306. - Purpose; applicability.
(a)
The purpose of this article is to discourage criminal activity associated with late-night entertainment venues which remain open past the operating hours of businesses licensed to sell alcohol. Therefore, this article is adopted to protect the public's health, safety and welfare.
(b)
This article shall apply to all public entertainment facilities with the city including, but not limited to, adult entertainment establishments, dance halls, billiard halls, pool rooms, arcade game establishments, and establishments offering live musical performances, including disc jockey performances. Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, a jukebox, tape deck or other means of playing music constitutes entertainment subject to this article.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
Sec. 16-307. - Prohibited hours; penalties.
It shall be unlawful for any public entertainment facility located within the corporate limits of the city to offer or provide public entertainment between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 8:00 a.m. Tuesday through Saturday. In addition, all public entertainment facilities located within the corporate limits of the city shall be closed and all sales and services stopped by 2:00 a.m. on Sunday and shall not resume business any earlier than 8:00 a.m. the following Monday morning, punishable as provided in section 1-8.
(Ord. of 3-2-2017(1))
