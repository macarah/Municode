Sec. 58-1. - Identification of parks and buildings.

East Lakeshore Drive Park, federally leased property that is located on East Lakeshore Drive.
(3)
Frana Brown Park, at the corner of Main Street and Morningside Drive.

Depot Park, located at the corner of Lemon Street and Main Street.

Overlook Park, federally leased property located off of Hwy 92 near the Lake Acworth sub impoundment dam.

Bobby Van Newberry Park, generally bordered by Toccoa Drive, Clarkdale Drive, and Main Street.

South Shore Park, federally leased property that is located on the Southwest portion of Lake Acworth and is bordered by Highway 92 and Ragsdale Road.

Dallas Landing Park, federally leased property that is located at the end of Allatoona Drive on Lake Allatoona.

Proctor Landing Park, federally leased property that is located at the end of Proctor Landing Drive on Lake Allatoona.

The public buildings located within the parks of the city shall be as follows:

Sec. 58-2. - Public park hours.
It shall be unlawful for any person, except the duly employed and authorized employees and agents of the city, or of its commissions or agencies, in the regular and necessary conduct and management of their employment by the city, to be upon or to appear within any of the public parks or the city, in any manner, unless such person has a right to be upon and within such public parks by virtue of a lawful permit, admission, invitation or ticket to an activity or entertainment as authorized, and then being carried on, by the city, or by such subsidiary agency, commission or board of the city as authorized by the mayor and board of aldermen:
(1)
For Dallas Landing Park, Proctor Landing Park, and south Shore Park between the hours of 9:00 p.m. and 8:00 a.m.
(2)
South Shore Park between the hours of 9:00 p.m. to 8:00 a.m. between the first Saturday in April and the second Sunday in October; and 7:00 p.m. to 8:00 a.m. outside of these dates.
(3)
All non-illuminated trails, beaches and swim areas between sunset and sunrise.
(4)
Logan Farm Park from Taylor Street to Tanyard Creek, Depot Park and Frana Brown Park between the hours of 12:00 a.m. and 6:00 a.m.
(5)
All other parks between the hours of 10:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m.
(6)
Director of parks and recreation may temporarily adjust these park hours in the case of construction, safety related issues, special events, or public health emergency for up to 14 days.
(7)
Public building hours of operation shall be set by the mayor and board of aldermen.
(Ord. No. 2021-11, § 1, 5-6-2021)
Sec. 58-3. - Public park rules.
(a)
The following are prohibited acts for all public parks and buildings within the city:
(1)
Sound and noise. Use of any sound-making device or instrument is plainly audible at a distance of 100 feet or more. To use any public address system.
(2)
Alcohol. Possess or consume alcoholic beverages.
(3)
Domestic pets. To have domestic pets on beaches, on playgrounds, or where signage has been specifically posted by the city designating the area as no pets allowed, or to be in violation of Cobb County leash law. For an animal owner or custodian to leave animal waste in a park without its proper removal and disposal. Service animals as defined by the Americans with Disabilities Act are exempt.
(4)
Littering and sanitation. For any person to throw, leave behind, or deposit litter on the grounds, streets, sidewalks, foundations, lakes, ponds, streams, or other bodies of water anywhere else other than a public litter receptacle. To not carry away such litter where receptacles are not provided and properly disposed of elsewhere.
(5)
Damage to government property. Damage, remove, vandalize, deface, or destroy government property or natural resources located on government property.
(6)
Fish and wildlife. To engage in commercial activities related to fish and wildlife, or the buying or selling of fish and wildlife. Fishing in areas recommended for swimming. Fishing without a valid State License at city facilities where is a license is required. To hunt, harm, chase, trap, or frighten wild animals.
(7)
Waterfowl. Feeding of the ducks, geese and other waterfowl.
(8)
Motor vehicles. To operate motorized vehicles, other than city owned, outside of designated parking areas or off of roadways laid out and maintained for vehicular traffic. Driving into the park area to load, unload, or drop off passengers, equipment, or any other items. Exception, personal transportation vehicles as defined in Article IV, section 90-90 are permitted on trails.
(9)
Smoking and tobacco. To smoke any substance or use any tobacco product including smokeless tobacco, e-cigarette, vape pen or any other "vapor products" as defined by O.C.G.A. 16-12-170.
(10)
Bridges, boardwalks, and docks. To jump from, dive from, climb on railings or support beams of bridges or boardwalks.
(11)
Swimming. In ponds at any time, in permitted areas outside of park hours, or when signage is posted that swimming is not permitted. Where swimming is permitted infants and small children to not wear waterproof swim diapers and a swimsuit at all times. To wear thongs or see-through swimsuits. To wear swim attire lower than hip level or for under garments to be visible.
(12)
Grills. Use a gas or charcoal grill.
(13)
Tents. To use tents that have the capability to be fully enclosed and tents over 12 feet by 12 feet. For tents to not be properly secured to the ground.
(14)
Reservations. To exclusively reserve an area of a park, facility, or parking lot without a permit.
(15)
Athletic facilities. To use athletic fields for any other purpose than what the field is designated for. To use a field when said field is marked for approved teams or associations. Conducting organized sports leagues or athletic teams must without approval from the director of parks, recreation, and community resource director to use the athletic fields of a park and recreation facility to conduct organized games or to conduct two or more organized practices.
(16)
Pollution of waters. To throw, discharge or otherwise place, or cause to be placed, in the waters of any fountain, pond, lake, stream, bay or other body of water in or adjacent to any public park, or any tributary, stream, storm sewer or drain flowing into such waters any substances, matter or thing, liquid or solid, that will or may result in the pollution of such waters.
(17)
Glass containers. To possess or use glass containers.
(18)
Bicycles, skateboarding, personal e-bikes, personal scooters, and skates. To ride bicycles, e-bikes, skateboards, scooters, and skates in posted areas. To grind, deface, or cause damage to public property anywhere. To operate anywhere where signage is posted prohibiting use.
(19)
Fireworks. For any person to use, light, shoot off, launch, fly, discharge, detonate or explode explosives or explosive devices, or projectiles of any kind in any park or recreation facility, including fireworks as defined by State law or other pyrotechnics of any kind and rockets.
(20)
Closed areas. To enter an area posted as closed to the public.
(21)
Games of chance. To gamble or participate in or support any game of chance.
(22)
Fires. For any person to build or maintain a fire in a park or recreation facility. Exemption, fires within grills. To leave a permitted fire unattended or to fail to completely extinguished prior to departure. Burning of any materials, even within a grill, that which produces toxic or noxious fumes, including, but not limited to, tires, plastics, floatation materials, and treated wood products.
(23)
Controlled substances. To possess or use any drug or any other controlled substance as defined by state law, except as permitted by the laws of this state, in any park or recreation building.
(24)
Commercial activity, merchandising, advertising and signs. For an individual, corporation, or business to conduct any activities where fees are being collected to participate in an activity. For any person, corporation, of business to sell or offer for sale any merchandise or operate or attempt to operate a concession or engage in any commercial activity; to paste, glue, tack or otherwise post any sign, placard, advertisement, or inspection; any person erect, or cause to be erected, any sign on any public lands, highways, or roads adjacent to the park; and to advertise or call the public attention in any way to any article or service for sale or hire in a park or recreation facility. Exemption, photography.
(25)
Camping. To camp.
(26)
Solicitation of donations. To solicit alms or contributions for any purpose, whether public or private, unless previously approved in writing by the director.
(27)
Interference with permittees. To unreasonably disturb or interfere any person or party occupying any public park areas or participating in any activity under the authority of a permit.
Violations of subsections (1) through (27) shall be punishable by a fine of not less than $250.00.
(Ord. No. 2020-35, § 1, 12-17-2020)
Sec. 58-4. - Use of motorized vehicles in public parks.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Motorized vehicle means any vehicle containing an internal combustion engine that is used or designed for the transportation of persons, and specifically includes any automobile, sport utility vehicle, all-terrain vehicle, motorcycle, minibike, and vehicles commonly referred to as three-wheelers and four-wheelers.
(b)
Prohibited, exception. The operation of unauthorized motorized vehicles in public parks within the city shall be prohibited unless such operation is upon a roadway, driveway or designated parking area in such public park or a permitted personal transportation vehicle.
(Ord. No. 2020-35, § 1, 12-17-2020)
Sec. 58-5. - User fees, parking fees and regulations.
The mayor and board of aldermen shall be authorized to establish by resolution reasonable regulations for public parks and their facilities within the city. User fees and parking fees shall be as fixed by the mayor and board of aldermen and as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file with the office of the city clerk.
(Ord. No. 2021-11, §1, 5-6-2021; Ord. No. 2022-16 , § 1, 6-16-2022)
Sec. 58-6. - Parking restrictions.
(a)
The mayor and board of aldermen shall, if they determine it is in the public interest, be authorized to designate parking areas and streets in parks as parking by permit only and determine the rules and regulations for the permit. Said areas shall be designated by signs which identify the regulations.
(b)
All of the parking areas/lots and designated on street parking areas in Cauble Park, South Shore Park, Overlook Park, Dallas Landing Park, and Proctor Landing Park are hereby designated as parking by permit only per the dates and times annually adopted by the mayor and board.
(c)
Violations of section 58-6(b) shall be punishable by a fine of not less than $50.00.
(Ord. No. 2021-11, §1, 5-6-2021)
Secs. 58-7—58-40. - Reserved.

The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:

Misconduct means:
(1)
Any violation of this article or failure to comply with city park or building rules and policies.
(2)
Entering a permanently or temporarily closed area or facility.
(3)
Behavior that could threaten or endanger the wellbeing of other.
(4)
The offense of disorderly conduct as defined by O.C.G.A. 16-11-39.
(5)
Unsportsmanlike conduct.
(6)
Use of profanity.
(7)
Physical or verbal abuse toward an official, scorekeeper/timer, coach, participant in an event, or an employee of the city or any other person attending or observing the event.
Park means a park, reservation, playground, beach, recreation center or any other area in the city, which is owned or used by the city, and devoted to active or passive recreation, and all the facilities identified in section 58-1.

Sec. 58-42. - Regulated.
(a)
Conduct in park.
(1)
Misconduct. When in a city park no person shall commit any act of misconduct as defined herein.
(2)
Permits. Any individual or group which has a permit to use a city facility in a park is responsible for the conduct for the individuals there while the facility is under their permitted use.
(3)
Authority to eject. City police officers and any park attendant or city official shall have the authority to eject from any public park within the city any person who commits an act of misconduct as defined herein.
(4)
Banned individuals or groups. The city parks and recreation director shall have the authority to ban any individual or group from a city park/s where they have participated in an act of misconduct as defined herein. The director shall notify the party and the police department in writing of said ban and the length of the ban.
(5)
Ban appeals. Any party banned by the city parks and recreation director shall have the ability to appeal the ban to the mayor and board of aldermen by giving written notification to the city clerk within 14 days of being notified by the parks and recreation director and a hearing on such appeal shall be scheduled before the mayor and board within 45 days thereafter. An appeal from the decision of the mayor and board shall be by certiorari to the superior court of the county within 30 days of the decision of the mayor and board.
(6)
Trespassing. Any party banned from a party returning to a park while they are banned from said park shall be guilty of a trespass.
(Ord. No. 2021-11, § 1, 5-6-2021)
Sec. 58-43. - Boating safety zones; creation and restrictions.
(a)
Under the authority provided in O.C.G.A. § 52-7-21, the City of Acworth hereby adopts Code §§ 52-7-1 through 52-7-26, Official Code of Georgia Annotated (Title 52, Chapter 7, Article 1), known as the Georgia Boat Safety Act, as and for the City of Acworth boating regulations as if recited verbatim in this section.
(b)
Under the authority provided in O.C.G.A. § 52-7-21, the usage of gasoline and diesel-powered vessels of any size on Lake Acworth will be limited to permitted events only, city employees performing their job duties, law enforcement or emergency rescue personnel during the course of their duties or by city personnel adhering to lake related work details. Events utilizing a gasoline or diesel-powered vessels must be approved by the board of aldermen.
(c)
Under the authority provided in O.C.G.A. § 52-7-21, the usage of electric powered vessels, that exceed fifteen horsepower or are capable of producing wake that disrupts other watercraft, of any size on Lake Acworth will be limited to permitted events only, city employees performing their job duties, law enforcement or emergency rescue personnel during the course of their duties or by city personnel adhering to lake related work details. Events utilizing gasoline or diesel-powered vessels must be approved by the board of aldermen.
(d)
Violations of section 58-43 shall be punishable by a fine of not less than $250.00.
(Ord. No. 2021-11, §1, 5-6-2021)
Sec. 58-44. - Traffic.
It shall be unlawful for any person in a public park within the city to:
(1)
Applicable state motor vehicle laws. Fail to comply with all applicable provisions of the state motor vehicle traffic laws in regard to equipment and operation of vehicles, together with such regulations as are contained in this article and other ordinances.
(2)
Enforcement of regulations. Fail to obey all traffic officers and park employees who are authorized and instructed to direct traffic whenever and wherever needed in the public parks and on the highways, streets, or roads immediately adjacent to such parks in accordance with the provisions of this article, and such supplementary regulations as may be issued subsequently by the director.
(3)
Observance of signs. Fail to carefully observe all traffic signs which indicate speed, direction, caution, stopping or parking, and all other signs posted for proper control and to safeguard life and property.
(4)
Speed limit. Ride or drive a vehicle at a rate of speed exceeding 15 miles per hour.
(5)
Operation confined to roads. Drive any vehicle on any area, except the paved park roads or parking areas, or such other areas as may be specifically designated as temporary parking areas by the city from time to time.
(6)
Parking.
a.
Designated areas. Park a vehicle in other than an established or designated parking area, and such use shall be in accordance with the directions posted, and with the instructions of an attendant who may be present.
b.
Full parking. Fully park on the road or driveway at any time. In order to enjoy a special natural scenic feature, vehicles may be parked with the two left wheels near the right edge of the paving for a period of time not to exceed five minutes. No stopping or parking is permitted, even briefly, on the left-hand side of any road or driveway.
c.
Overnight parking. Leave a vehicle in a public park overnight unless approval has been given by the city.
d.
Emergency procedure. Fail to immediately notify any attendant or the city police of an emergency, such as a breakdown, requiring the assistance of a tow truck, mechanic, or other person.
e.
Double parking. Double park any vehicle on a road or parkway within a public park, unless directed by a park official, or elsewhere in a parking area at or within a public park.
f.
Muffler required. Fail to use a muffler which is adequate to deaden the sound of the engine in a motor vehicle.
(Ord. No. 2021-11, § 1, 5-6-2021)
Secs. 58-45—58-47. - Reserved.
Sec. 58-48. - Operating policy.
(a)
Hours. Except for unusual and unforeseen emergencies, public parks within the city shall be open to the public every day of the year during designated hours. The opening and closing hours for each individual park shall be posted in the park for public information.
(b)
Closed areas. Any section or part of any public park within the city may be declared to be closed to the public by the board of aldermen at any time, and for any interval of time, either temporarily or at regular and stated intervals (daily or otherwise), and either entirely or merely to certain uses, as the board of aldermen shall find reasonably necessary.
(c)
Lost and found articles. The finding of lost articles by park attendants shall be reported to the Acworth Community Center.
(d)
Permit.
(1)
Activities. A permit shall be obtained from the city before participation in the following park activities shall be allowed:
a.
Reservation of picnic tables.
b.
Special events, including, but not limited, to carnivals, concerts, athletic events, and any other organized events not normally conducted in a passive park.
(2)
Application. Any person seeking the issuance of a permit under subsection (d) of this section shall file an application with the city clerk's office not less than seven days, nor more than 90 days, prior to the proposed activity. The application shall contain the following:
a.
The name and address of the applicant.
b.
The name and address of the person sponsoring the activity, if any.
c.
The day and hours for which the permit is desired.
d.
The park, or portion of the park, for which such permit is desired.
e.
An estimate of the anticipated number of persons attending such event.
f.
Any other information the board of aldermen shall find reasonably necessary for a fair determination as to whether a permit should be issued under subsection (d) of this section.
(3)
Standards for issuance. The city manager shall issue a permit under subsection (d) of this section if he finds that the:
a.
Proposed activity or use of the park will not unreasonably interfere with or detract from the general public's enjoyment of the park.
b.
Proposed activity and use will not unreasonably interfere with or detract from the promotion of public health, welfare, safety, and recreation.
c.
Proposed activity or use is not reasonably anticipated to incite violence, crime, or disorderly conduct.
d.
Proposed activity will not entail unusual, extraordinary, or burdensome expense or police operation by the city.
e.
Facilities desired have not been reserved for other use on the day and hour required in the permit application.
(4)
Appeal. Within 30 days after receipt of a permit application, if the city manager denies the application, he shall apprise the applicant in writing of the denial; and any aggrieved person shall have the right to appeal such decision in writing within seven days to the board of aldermen, which shall consider the application under the standards set forth in subsection (d)(2) of this section, and sustain or overrule the city clerk's or city manager's decision within 60 days. The decision of the board of aldermen shall be final.
(5)
Effect of permit. A permittee under subsection (d) of this section shall be bound by all park rules and regulations and all applicable ordinances as though they were fully set forth in the permit. Each and every permittee acknowledges that such permittee has reviewed this Articles I and II of this chapter and is familiar with the contents hereof.
(6)
Liability of permittee. The person to whom a permit is issued under subsection (d) of this section shall be liable for any loss, damage or injury sustained by any person a guest of the person to whom such permit has been issued.
(7)
Revocation. The city manager or board of aldermen shall have the authority to revoke a permit under subsection (d) of this section upon a finding of a violation of any rule or ordinance, or upon good cause shown.
(8)
Any person or entity which applies for and receives a permit acknowledges the existence of O.C.G.A. § 51-3-20 entitled "The Georgia Recreational Properties" and which limits any and all liability of the City of Acworth.
(9)
Permittee hereby releases the City of Acworth, and agrees to hold the City of Acworth harmless, and hereby indemnifies the City of Acworth from any and all liability whatsoever related to any and all activities, special events, table reservations and all other activities authorized or allowed by any permit issued hereunder.
(Ord. No. 2021-11, § 1, 5-6-2021)
Sec. 58-49. - Enforcement.
(a)
Officials. City police officers, parks and recreation staff, and park attendants shall, in connection with their duties imposed by law, diligently enforce the provisions of this article.
(b)
Ejectment. City police officers, Parks and recreation staff, and any park attendant shall have the authority to eject from any public park within the city any person acting in violation of this article.
(c)
Seizure of property. City police officers, parks and recreation staff, and any park attendant shall have the authority to retain any property, thing, or device in a public park within the city, or which is used in violation of this article.
(Ord. No. 2021-11, § 1, 5-6-2021)
Sec. 58-50. - Violation; penalties.
Any person who violates any provision of this article shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction, shall be punished as provided in section 1-11.
(Ord. No. 2021-11, § 1, 5-6-2021)
Secs. 58-51—58-80. - Reserved.

Editor's note— Ord. No. 2010-23, § 1, adopted Oct. 7, 2010, amended former art. III, §§ 58-81—58-84, in its entirety to read as herein set out. Former art. III pertained to the same subject matter and derived from Ord. No. 2003-34, §§ 1—4, 11-6-2003; Ord. No. 2004-41, § 1, 11-18-2004.

Sec. 58-81. - Creation and establishment of tree board.
There is hereby created and established a city tree commission for the city which shall consist of a minimum of three and a maximum of five members, city citizens, and one position that may not be a citizen of Acworth, but who has a demonstrated background in horticulture, who shall be appointed by the mayor and board of aldermen and shall serve without compensation. One member of the parks and recreation department shall also be a member of the city tree commission.
(Ord. No. 2010-23, § 1, 10-7-2010)
Sec. 58-82. - Appointments.
Initial appointees to the city tree commission shall have staggered terms as follows: Three to five members for four years; and thereafter upon the expiration of such initial terms, successor shall be appointed for a term of four years by the mayor and board of aldermen. Vacancies caused by death, resignation, or otherwise shall be filled for the unexpired term by appointment of the mayor and board of aldermen.
(Ord. No. 2010-23, § 1, 10-7-2010)
Sec. 58-83. - Appointment of officers.
The commission shall choose its own officers, make its own rules and regulations that must be approved by mayor and board of aldermen, establish meeting dates, and keep minutes of its proceedings. The presence of a simple majority of standing members shall constitute a quorum for all purposes.
(Ord. No. 2010-23, § 1, 10-7-2010)
Sec. 58-84. - Powers and duties.
The city tree commission or its authorized agent will have the following powers and duties:
(1)
To study and make recommendations to the mayor and board of aldermen regarding the planting, maintenance, and protection of trees on public property; when requested by the mayor and board of aldermen, shall consider, investigate, make findings, report, and recommend upon any special matter or question coming within the scope of its work.
(2)
To assist the Acworth Parks and Recreation Department in a program to protect and maintain existing trees and green areas and all city owned and recreational lands and properties; and to provide review and comments on the proposed landscaping plans of all recreational lands and properties within the city when requested by mayor and board of aldermen.
(3)
To accept all contributions, donations, fees, exactions, or other funds for the planting, care, and protection of trees throughout the city and recommend expenditure of said monies to the mayor and board of aldermen.
(Ord. No. 2010-23, § 1, 10-7-2010)
Secs. 58-85—58-90. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - FESTIVALS

Sec. 58-91. - Purpose.
The following are general rules for conducting or regulating festivals in the City of Acworth, Georgia.
(Ord. No. 2009-12A, § 1, 8-6-2009)
Sec. 58-92. - Definitions.
Words and terms used in this article are defined as follows:
Alcoholic beverage. Means and includes, but is not limited to, malt beverages, wine and spirituous liquor as defined in this section.
Controlled zone. A zone within the festival area in which special regulations may apply as recommended in each case by the city manager and authorized by the mayor and board of aldermen including wristband identification of persons authorized to purchase and hold in possession alcoholic beverages in a public park or street.
Festival. A program of cultural and/or entertainment events open to the general public and taking place either fully or in part on public streets, thoroughfares, parks, squares, or other public property in connection with a community festival. To be regulated by this article, each such festival must be designated as a festival by the mayor and board of aldermen.
Festival area. The area specifically designated and defined by the mayor and board of aldermen as the location of festival events and activities.
(Ord. No. 2009-12A, § 1, 8-6-2009)
Sec. 58-93. - Designation of a community festival.
On the recommendation of the city manager, the mayor and board of aldermen may designate and name certain community festival days and during such festival days authorize cultural and entertainment events and initiate the regulatory provisions of this article within the festival area.
(Ord. No. 2009-12A, § 1, 8-6-2009)
Sec. 58-94. - Designation of a festival area.
The mayor and board shall designate the festival area in the resolution that designates the event as a festival and have the right to add any regulations per the specific festival
(Ord. No. 2009-12A, § 1, 8-6-2009)
Sec. 58-95. - Designation and management of controlled zone.
(a)
Upon recommendation of the city manager, the mayor and board of aldermen may designate one or more controlled zones within the festival area, and may authorize the city manager to implement regulations and controls which will be specific for each designated controlled zone. Such regulations and controls may include but are not limited to the following:
(1)
Fencing and gating. Fencing designated controlled zone(s) and establishing entrance gates for controlling and limiting access to each controlled zone.
(2)
Identification, entrance criteria. Requiring wristbands or other means of identifying persons who have met entrance criteria. Entrance criteria may include but are not limited to prohibiting persons from entering with weapons, alcoholic beverages, pets, skates, bicycles, and other items which may be dangerous, disruptive, or inconvenient in crowded conditions, and prohibiting entry by persons appearing to be intoxicated.
(3)
Identification, consumption of alcoholic beverages. Requiring wristbands or other means of identifying persons who have displayed proof of attaining the age of 21 years, and who are authorized to purchase or hold in possession alcoholic beverages within the controlled zone, both within and outside of licensed alcoholic beverage establishments. A fee, as fixed by the mayor and board of aldermen as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file with the office of the city clerk, may be charged for such wristband. The transfer of wristband from one person to another person is prohibited and if a violation occurs, the wristband will be forfeited. Displaying such a wristband does not relieve alcoholic beverage establishments of responsibility for determining if a person has attained the age of 21 years before dispensing alcohol to that person.
(4)
Containers, cups. Any establishment serving alcoholic beverages where the individuals purchasing said beverages will be walking around in the containment area shall only serve the alcoholic beverage in a disposable cup. Disposable cups must be composed of a material that is not glass, ceramic, aluminum, or other hard substance. Plastic or Styrofoam cups are allowed. The city also reserves the right to determine additional regulations in regards to the containers/cups when approving the festival.
(5)
Transport of alcoholic beverages into or out of a designated controlled zone. The transport of alcoholic beverages for personal consumption either into or out of any designated controlled zone is prohibited.
(6)
Designation of festival manager and/or city within controlled zone. The mayor and board of aldermen may designate a festival manager and/or the City of Acworth to manage the festival activities to organize and manage festival activities within a controlled zone, as set forth in subsection 58-95(a). Furthermore, the designated festival manager and/or the city will be responsible for approving or not approving the vendors/establishments that seek participation in the festival.
(b)
Insurance. Any organization authorized to organize and manage festival activities within a controlled zone shall take out and keep in force for all activities associated with such event a special events liability policy. Such policy shall provide the following:
(1)
Minimum coverage limits: The city manager will recommend to the mayor and board of aldermen an adequate minimum coverage limit depending on the scope of festival.
(2)
Named as additional insured: Mayor and board of aldermen of the City of Acworth, a Georgia municipal corporation, its elected officials, officers, employees and agents. In some cases other local, state, and federal governments or private individuals or corporations may be required to be named as an additional insured if the property is leased, is under lease, or private property is being used to conduct the festival.
(3)
Certificate of insurance: A certificate of insurance shall be issued to the additional insured at least five days prior to the beginning of the event. Such certificate shall be on a standard form and will provide for notification of the additional insured within ten days of termination of coverage.
(4)
Coverage shall include claims for damages because of bodily injury and for DRAMSHOP liability.
(Ord. No. 2009-12A, § 1, 8-6-2009; Ord. No. 2022-16 , § 1, 6-16-2022)
Sec. 58-96. - Restricting hours of alcohol sales.
Hours of alcohol sales will be set forth in the resolution establishing the festival.
(Ord. No. 2009-12A, § 1, 8-6-2009)
Sec. 58-97. - Qualified vendors.
All qualified vendors must have a City of Acworth pouring license or have completed the application process for a temporary City of Acworth pouring license and a State of Georgia alcohol license prior to the event date. Temporary permits are issued to qualified vendors in accordance with regulations as set forth in section 6-29.
(Ord. No. 2009-12A, § 1, 8-6-2009)
Sec. 58-98. - Permit.
Permit applications shall be obtained from the parks and recreation department or festival manager before requesting approval to organize and/or conduct a festival.
(Ord. No. 2009-12A, § 1, 8-6-2009)
Sec. 58-99. - No waiver.
Nothing contained herein shall waive the sovereign immunity of the City of Acworth as currently existing or as may hereafter amended. Further, this ordinance shall not constitute a waiver or modification of state law, city ordinances or federal law.
(Ord. No. 2009-12A, § 1, 8-6-2009)
