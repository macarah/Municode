Sec. 21-1. - Violations.
For violation of this chapter the engineer and the conductor shall upon conviction be fined for each offense within the limits provided in section 1-8, and the railroad shall be subject to a fine in addition to the operator of its trains for like amounts, to be collected by the issuance of execution and levy thereof against such railroad and any of its properties.
(Code 1968, § 20-7)
Sec. 21-2. - Reserved.
Editor's note— Ord. (W-6-95) of Oct. 3, 1995, repealed § 21-2, pertaining to speed of trains, as derived from the code of 1968, § 20-3.
Sec. 21-3. - Blocking streets.
It shall be unlawful for any railroad company operating trains within the city or any employee thereof operating such trains, including conductors and engineers, to permit a standing railroad car or train of cars to block or close a street crossing in the city for a period of time exceeding ten (10) minutes.
(Code 1968, § 20-2)
State Law reference— Exemption from personal responsibility for train and yard crews blocking crossings under orders, Ga. Code Ann., § 46-8-197.
Sec. 21-4. - Disembarking or boarding moving trains.
It shall be unlawful for any person to board or alight from any train within the city limits while the same is in motion; provided however, that this section shall not apply to such persons who shall have business upon said train, nor to such persons who could not be put off or could not disembark before the train started.
(Code 1968, § 20-6)
Sec. 21-5. - Conduct of persons at depot.
It shall be unlawful for any person to go beyond the rail of the sidetrack next to the depot while waiting for the arrival of a train, or to congregate upon the place where passengers alight from or board a train, or to block the passageways from the depot to the trains, or to interfere with the free passage of persons who are intending to board a train or who are coming from a train into the city; provided however, that this section shall not apply to persons bona fide entering the place where passengers board and alight, for the purpose of taking passage upon the train or for the purpose of assisting a passenger, nor to railroad employees in the performance of their duty.
(Code 1968, § 20-5)
