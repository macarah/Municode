Chapter 4 - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES[1]
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 4-1. - Purpose; business of selling alcoholic beverages declared privilege.
(a)
The purposes of this chapter shall include, without necessarily being limited to, the following:
(1)
Compliance with and effectuation of the general state law;
(2)
Prevention and control of the sale of alcoholic beverages by unfit persons;
(3)
Ensuring that any licenses issued for the consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises are issued only to a legitimate restaurant or private club as defined in this chapter;
(4)
Protection of schools, homes, churches, parks, and other institutions;
(5)
Protection of the public health, safety, and welfare;
(6)
Establishment of a procedure for, and the regulation of, the issuance and regulation of licenses for the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises within the unincorporated area of the county.
(b)
The business of selling alcoholic beverages, to the maximum extent permissible under state and federal law, shall under this chapter be considered to be a privilege to be accorded in conformity with the foregoing and other public policies of the county, rather than a right.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 1)
State Law reference— Alcoholic beverage license as privilege, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-1.
Sec. 4-2. - Definitions.
Unless a contrary intention is clearly apparent from the context, the following terms used in this chapter shall have the following meaning:
Alcohol means ethyl alcohol, hydrated oxide of ethyl, spirits of wine, from whatever source of whatever process produced.
Alcoholic beverage means and includes all alcohol, beer, malt beverages, distilled spirits, wine and fortified wines defined in this section; the term "beer" and "malt beverages" shall be synonymous in meaning for purposes of this chapter.
Beer means malt beverage, as that term is defined in this section.
Bona fide private club means any nonprofit association organized under the laws of this state that:
(1)
Has been in existence at least one year prior to the filing of its application for a license to be issued pursuant to this chapter;
(2)
Has at least 75 regular dues-paying members;
(3)
Owns, hires, or leases a building or space within a building for the reasonable use of its members, which building or space:
a.
Has suitable kitchen and dining room space and equipment; and
b.
Is staffed with a sufficient number of employees for cooking, preparing, and serving meals for its members and guests; and
(4)
Has no member, officer, agent, or employee directly or indirectly receiving, in the form of salary or other compensation, any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages beyond a fixed salary.
Distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume including, but not limited to, all fortified wines.
Establishment means any physical location for the storage or sale either at retail or wholesale of malt beverages and wines in the county.
Fortified wine means any alcoholic beverage containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "fortified wine" includes, but not limited to, brandy.
Interest includes any pecuniary interest and any ownership interest, whether present or future, whole or partial, legal or beneficial, contingent or vested, direct or indirect, and any right, power or authority of control.
Licensed premises includes not only the room wherein alcoholic beverages are sold or served but also the entire building in which such room is located, except that when such a room is located in a hotel, motel or similar facility or in a shopping center only such room and any adjoining storage, office, toilet, and other similar rooms shall constitute the licensed premises. However, no more than one building shall be deemed the licensed premises for a single license, regardless of whether an adjacent building may be connected to the licensed premises by a sidewalk, breezeway, enclosed walkway or any other means of passage from one building to another. Notwithstanding the foregoing, porches, shelters, walkways, and yard area which actually adjoin the licensed building are included as part of the licensed premises as long as such areas are located at and have the same street address as the licensed premises. However streets and driveways are specifically excluded; and parking lots and parking areas are also excluded unless vehicle traffic and parking has been prohibited in such parking lots or areas.
Licensee includes an individual licensee and in the case of a partnership, corporation or limited liability company, includes both the partnership or corporation or limited liability company and the named licensee.
Licensor means the entity authorized to issue a license under this chapter; for purposes of this chapter, the licensor is the county board of commissioners.
Majority stockholder means the person, if any, who owns more than 50 percent of the voting stock of a corporation; if no person owns more than 50 percent of the voting stock of a corporation, the majority stockholder is the person owning more of the voting stock than any other person; and if two or more persons each own the same amount of the voting stock of a corporation and each owns more of the voting stock than any other person, then any one of such persons may act as the majority stockholder.
Malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than 14 percent alcohol by volume and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer and strong beer. The term "malt beverage" does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine. The term "malt beverage" may be used synonymously with beer for purposes of this chapter.
Mixed drink means any distilled spirits served for consumption on the premises, whether or not diluted by water or any other substance.
Named licensee means the person to whom a license is issued under this chapter, including the person acting on behalf of a partnership, limited liability company or corporation.
Private residence means a house or dwelling wherein not less than one or more than two families customarily reside, and does not include a mobile home, apartment building or a residence that has been abandoned or unoccupied for a consecutive period of six months or more immediately prior to the date of the filing of a new application under this chapter.
Sale means the giving or dispensing of an alcoholic beverage in exchange for money or any other valuable consideration regardless of form and regardless of when the money or other consideration is paid or given to the person or entity providing the alcoholic beverage.
Wine.
(1)
The term "wine" means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added.
(2)
The term "wine" includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products.
(3)
The term "wine" does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage.
A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of the term "wine" contained in this section. Unless a contrary intention is clearly apparent from the context, and except as set forth herein, any term used in this chapter shall have the same meaning as when used in comparable provisions of the Georgia Alcoholic Beverage Code, O.C.G.A. § 3-1-1 et seq.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § II; Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 2)
State Law reference— Similar definitions, O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2.
Sec. 4-3. - Chapter applicability.
(a)
Exception for certain private facilities and events. Nothing contained in this chapter shall prohibit the licensing of the sale or distribution of alcoholic beverages by bona fide private clubs, owning their own buildings, subject to licensing under state law. Nothing in this article does, or is intended to, control or prohibit the serving of alcoholic beverages without charge at residences, private parties, or other private functions at any location.
(b)
Exclusion regarding certain properties owned by or located in the City of Hazelhurst.
(1)
County's Big House facility. Notwithstanding any other provision or prohibition in this chapter to the contrary, none of the prohibitions or restrictions set forth in this chapter shall apply to the county's property commonly known as the "big house" facility located off U.S. Highway Number 341 adjoining the lands upon which the Days Inn/Village Inn Motel are currently located, providing such sale or dispensing of alcoholic beverages otherwise complies with, and does not violate, any state law or City of Hazlehurst ordinance or any other county ordinance. Even though the property is owned by the county, it is located within the city limits of the City of Hazlehurst, and the sale and dispensing of alcoholic beverages, and the issuing of licenses for same, at such location shall be governed by the ordinances and resolutions of the City of Hazlehurst rather than the county. The sale and dispensing of alcoholic beverages at such location pursuant to a valid license issued by the City of Hazlehurst, including sale or dispensing of alcoholic beverages at such location during private parties or other functions which might otherwise be prohibited elsewhere in this chapter shall not be violations of this chapter.
(2)
Civic center and golf course. The civic center and golf course property currently owned by the City of Hazlehurst are excluded from this chapter, in regard to any private parties or functions, and are also excluded from the term "recreational facility" and "public park" as used herein. However, should alcoholic beverages be sold at the civic center then the owner or operator would be required to comply with this chapter as long as the property is located in the unincorporated area of this county.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 12(E))
Sec. 4-4. - Waiver of provisions.
All notices and other procedural rights and provisions provided for in this chapter may be waived, and shall be deemed to have been waived unless timely objection has been made thereto. No defect in any notice, statement of grounds or of the proper mailing or service thereof shall be valid unless written objection pointing out the grounds thereof are filed with the office of the board of commissioners at least two days prior to any scheduled hearing or action thereon.
Sec. 4-5. - Excise taxes.
(a)
Mixed drinks. No excise tax is imposed on the sale of mixed drinks poured for consumption on the premises.
(b)
Wholesale dealers. Any wholesale dealer in alcoholic beverages other than beer and wine who has a place of business in this county shall procure a license for distilled spirits under the same provisions applicable to retailed licensees. Licenses for the sale of beer and wine by wholesale dealers are controlled by a separate ordinance.
(c)
Distilled spirits. There is imposed by the county an excise tax on the first sale or use of distilled spirits in the county at the rate of $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter. The excise taxes provided for in this section shall be imposed upon and paid by the licensed wholesale dealer. Such taxes shall be paid on or before the tenth day of the month following the month in which the alcoholic beverages are sold or disposed of by the wholesaler within the county.
(d)
Packaged malt beverages and wines. There is imposed by the county an excise tax on the first sale or use of malt beverages in the county at the rate of $0.05 per 12 ounces and of $0.80 per gallon or $0.22 per liter on wine or proportionately thereof so as to prorate the tax on bottles, cans and containers of various sizes.
(e)
Monthly report. Each licensee responsible for the payment of excise tax under this section shall furnish to the county a monthly report in writing itemizing for the preceding calendar month the exact quantities of alcoholic beverages, by size and type of container, sold within the county. The report may be made in the form of duplicate copies of the wholesaler's invoices. The report required by this section shall be delivered to the county no later than the tenth day of the month following the month of delivery.
(f)
No sale unless excise tax paid. No alcoholic beverages shall be sold unless the excise taxes due under this section have been paid.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, §§ V, VII; Ord. of 2-18-2003, §§ 36, 39, 40)
State Law reference— Municipal and county excise taxes on malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-80; imposition of excise tax on wine may be imposed by local governments provided that no county tax may imposed where municipalities already levy such tax, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-60; imposition of excise tax on distilled spirits by the package may be imposed by local governments provided that no county tax may imposed where municipalities already levy such tax, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-80; imposition by municipalities of excise taxes on distilled spirits sold by the drink, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-130; imposition by counties of excise taxes on distilled spirits for sale by the drink when no tax authorized by O.C.G.A. § 3-4-130 is imposed, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-131.

ARTICLE II. - LICENSES[2]
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-29. - Required.
It is unlawful for any person to sell or offer for sale any alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises within the county except under a valid license issued under this article and in compliance with the provisions of this article.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § III; Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 3(A))
State Law reference— Dealing in malt beverages at wholesale and retail prohibited without local license, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-40; county licensing fees for the sale of malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-41; restriction on county licensing fees for the sale of malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-43; dealing in wine at wholesale and retail prohibited without local license, O.C.G.A. § 3-8-40.
Sec. 4-30. - Separate license required for each establishment.
A separate license under this article shall be required for each separate establishment regardless of ownership.
Sec. 4-31. - Classes of licenses.
(a)
Generally. Classes of licenses issued under this article, and activities permitted and regulated thereunder, are as provided in this section.
(b)
Licenses for retail sales for consumption on the premises. Licenses for retail sales for consumption on the premises include:
(1)
Beer and wine pouring license. Pouring license for retail sale of beer and wine for consumption only on the premises.
(2)
Distilled spirits pouring license. Pouring license for retail sale of distilled spirits for consumption only on the premises.
(3)
Sales of distilled spirits, wine and malt beverages by the drink. Pouring license for retail sale of distilled spirits, wine and malt beverages by the drink for consumption only on the premises.
(c)
Wholesale licenses. Wholesale licenses include:
(1)
Wholesale distribution of distilled spirits. Resident wholesale dealer's licenses for the wholesale distribution and sale of distilled spirits by a wholesale dealer having a place of business in the county.
(2)
Wholesale distribution of beer and wine. Wholesale dealer's licenses for the sale of malt beverages and wine by a wholesale dealer having a place of business in the county.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 3(D))
Sec. 4-32. - Fees.
(a)
Fee schedule. Fees authorized in this chapter include application fees, annual license fees and license transfer fees set by the board from time to time as it deems necessary or appropriate. Such fees are in the amount provided in the county fee schedule, and may be revised and modified from time to time by the board as the board determines to be appropriate; changes to the fees and the fee schedule shall not constitute amendments to this article, but only revisions in the fees authorized by this article.
(b)
Application fees. Application fees shall be charged only for the initial application or for a former licensee who has allowed his license to expire without being timely renewed as provided herein, or where an application is being made by a qualified applicant who is reapplying after his license has terminated. No application fee shall be charged for the renewal or annual re-issuance of a license that has been filed prior to the deadline as provided elsewhere herein. Application fees shall not be refunded under any circumstances.
(c)
Annual license fees. License fees shall be payable in advance for an entire calendar year beginning January 1 and ending December 31 of the same year. If an initial fee is paid after January 1 but on or before June 30 of the year of application, the license fee shall not be prorated but the full license fee shall be payable for the year of application. If an initial fee is paid after June 30 but on or before December 31 of the year of application, the license fee shall be reduced to 50 percent of the fee for the year of application.
(d)
Transfer fees. Each application for the transfer of a license pursuant to this article shall be accompanied by a non-refundable transfer fee. Licensees holding valid licenses by transfer may renew their licenses as any other licensee.
(e)
Time for payment. Application fees and fees for transfer of licenses shall be paid at the time the application is filed. An applicant may pay the annual license fee at the time the application is filed; and in such event the annual license fee shall be refunded if the license applied for is not issued. If the annual license fee is not paid at the time of the application, the annual license fee shall be paid prior to the issuance of the license by the board, but no later than 14 days after notification of approval of the license by the board. If an applicant fails to pay the license fee by the time required, the license will become void, and the applicant must reapply in the same manner as if no previous application had been made or approved.
(f)
Effective date of changes in fee schedule Any increase or decrease in the fees listed in the county fee schedule shall be effective for the applications made for, and licenses issued for, the calendar year immediately following the calendar year in which the change in a fee is approved by the board, provided however, that if the change in a fee is approved after November 1 in any year, the fee change shall only become effective with applications for licenses to be issued the second calendar year after the year in which the change is approved.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § III; Ord. of 9-9-1986, § A; Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 3(B), (C), (E)—(H))
State Law reference— Limitation on wholesale dealer license fees by county other than that in which business is located, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-43.
Sec. 4-33. - Form of application; filing.
Application for a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages in the county shall be filed with the county clerk upon forms prescribed by the county from time to time. The applications shall be subscribed by the applicant under oath and fully completed and executed.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 6(A))
Sec. 4-34. - Applications by corporations and other business entities.
(a)
In the case of a partnership, limited liability company or other legally recognized association, each general partner or managing member shall join as an applicant for the license and each general partner and managing member must meet the qualifications of an individual licensee, as provided herein.
(b)
In the case of a corporation, the majority stockholder or officer or employee who is to be the named licensee shall be the applicant and must meet the qualifications of an individual licensee, as provided herein; provided, however, that the board may require the fingerprinting and investigation of any of the officers and shareholders of the corporation if the board deems it necessary in making its investigation.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § II; Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 4(B), (C))
Sec. 4-35. - Deficient application as grounds for denial.
Failure to fully complete and execute an application for a license or to furnish accurately all data, information and records required by the application form, as well as, failure to accompany the application with the payment of the prescribed fee shall be deemed just cause for denying the application with prejudice.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 6(C))
Sec. 4-36. - Information to be included in application.
(a)
The application submitted shall contain such information as the county deems necessary to evaluate the application, including, but not limited to, the following:
(1)
Full name of the applicant, including any aliases or trade names and, in the case of a partnership, firm, limited liability company, association or corporation, the name of the individual or agent who shall be held responsible for compliance with the provisions of this article. In the event the applicant is a partnership or joint venture, the names and addresses of all members of the partnership or joint venture shall be furnished; in the event the applicant is an association or corporation, the names and addresses of its principal officers, directors and the three stockholders owning the largest amounts of stock shall be furnished; and in the case of a limited liability company, the names and addresses of all members shall be furnished.
(2)
The complete mailing and location address of the applicant and the address of the establishment for the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises.
(3)
The type of establishment to be operated and the type of license being applied for.
(4)
The names and addresses of all employees of the establishment at the time of making the application who will be selling, serving or handling any alcoholic beverage.
(5)
The name and address of, and approximate distance to, the nearest church.
(6)
The name and address of, and approximate distance to, the nearest school.
(7)
The approximate distance of the nearest residence to the establishment to be operated by applicant.
(8)
The name and address of the nearest public park or playground and the estimated distance from the establishment.
(9)
Whether the applicant or any person listed under subsection (a)(1) of this section has previously been refused a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages and, if the answer is in the affirmative, the month and year of such refusal, the jurisdiction refusing the license and the circumstances surrounding such refusal shall be stated.
(10)
Whether the applicant or any person named in subsection (a)(1) of this section, having previously obtained a license, had the same revoked and, if the answer is in the affirmative, the month and year of such revocation, the jurisdiction revoking the license and the circumstances surrounding such revocation shall be stated.
(11)
Whether the applicant or any person identified in subsection (a)(1) of this section has been convicted by any jurisdiction of:
a.
Any misdemeanor or felony offense; or
b.
A violation of any law involving gambling, tax laws, the sale of alcoholic beverages, or the use, sale, possession, manufacturing or distribution of any illegal drugs within the seven years immediately preceding the date of the application.
(12)
Whether any employee identified in subsection (a)(4) of this section has been convicted by any jurisdiction within the last seven years of any felony or any offense involving gambling, the sale of alcoholic beverages, or the use, sale, possession, manufacturing, or distribution of illegal drugs.
(13)
Seating capacity at tables and booths, and the number of parking spaces available on the grounds of the establishment.
(b)
The application shall include or be accompanied by a consent statement by the applicant that all necessary investigation reports on the applicant and any employees in the applicant's establishment including, but not limited to, credit reports and reports from law enforcement agencies, may be obtained; that any information in such reports may be furnished to the licensor; and that the applicant will be responsible for the cost hereof. If so requested, the applicant shall also obtain such consent forms from each employee who will be employed in the applicant's establishment. Licensor may, at its option, require fingerprinting or photographs of the applicant and the applicant's employees for the purpose of conducting its investigation.
(c)
The truth of the information contained in the application shall be sworn to by the applicant before a person authorized to administer oaths.
(d)
Applicant shall notify the county in writing within five days of any changes in the information furnished in the initial application or renewal application.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § III; Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 6(D))
Sec. 4-37. - Qualifications.
(a)
Age, competency and moral character. All individual applicants and all designees must be at least 21 years of age, mentally competent, and of good moral character and a citizen of the United States. A licensee must be a resident of the county, except that the named licensee shall not be required to be a resident of the county if the named licensee designates a resident of the county who shall be responsible for any matter relating to the license (i.e., a designee). All corporations, limited liability companies and other legal entities which apply for a license must either be organized under the laws of this state, or be authorized to do business in this state, and also be in good standing with the office of the secretary of state. Failure to meet and continue to comply with this requirement, or failure to remain in good standing with the secretary of state, will be grounds for the immediate suspension or revocation of a license.
(b)
Criminal history. A licensee or designee shall not have been convicted of any felony or misdemeanor, nor shall a licensee or designee have been convicted of any violations of the laws of this state, or of the United States, or any other state, involving the sale of alcoholic beverages, gambling, tax law violations, or the use, sale, possession, manufacturing or distribution of illegal drugs, within seven years prior to the date of the application, nor shall a licensee or designee currently be on parole or probation for a conviction of any such offense.
(c)
Prior licensing history. A licensee or designee shall not have been denied or had revoked, within the ten years prior to his application, any license to sell alcoholic beverages issued by any governmental entity. No license shall be issued for a location for which the license was revoked within the 12-month period immediately preceding the date of the application.
(d)
Real parties in interest. A licensee may not be a mere surrogate for a person or persons who would not otherwise qualify for a license. The licensee shall be personally responsible for the management and operation of the business and establishment for which the license is granted.
(e)
Premises requirements.
(1)
Ownership of proposed business premises. A licensee shall be the owner of the premises to be licensed or the holder of a written lease thereon for substantially the same period to be covered by the license.
(2)
Private club or restaurant status for consumption on premises. No license for the sale of alcoholic beverages by the drink for consumption on the premises shall be issued to any applicant when the location of the facility for which the licensee is being sought does not meet the requirements of a private club or restaurant.
(3)
Location and safety requirements. Applicant must certify, and provide proof if requested by the county, that the premises to be licensed meets all of the distance and safety regulation requirements for licensed premises under this article, that the proposed location of the licensed premises would not be detrimental to traffic conditions, and that there is sufficient parking area on the proposed premises to be licensed so that it would not be necessary for customers to park in or on the roadways, streets or adjoining property of others. No license shall be granted to any person for the operation of an establishment involving the sale of alcoholic beverages in any area prohibited by law or by county zoning regulations.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, §§ II, III; Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 5; Ord. of 3-24-2011)
Sec. 4-38. - Wholesale dealers to hold state license and sell on to county licensed retailers.
Wholesale licenses shall be issued under this article only to those applicants who are licensed by the state to sell and distribute alcoholic beverages at wholesale. Wholesale licensees shall sell and distribute to persons in the county having a valid retail license issued pursuant to this chapter.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § VI)
Sec. 4-39. - Investigation; fingerprints to be provided.
(a)
Within seven days from the time the application is filed, the county clerk shall notify the sheriff or such other investigative officer appointed by the county board of commissioners, who shall investigate the character of the applicant and other parties listed on the application and the reputation of the location and make a full report to the board within two weeks of the investigation request, unless additional time is requested by the sheriff or other investigative officer.
(b)
As a prerequisite to the issuance of the license, the applicant and any potential designee shall furnish a complete set of fingerprints to be forwarded to the state bureau of investigation which shall search the files and forward the fingerprints to the Federal Bureau of Investigation to determine past criminal activity.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § III; Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 6(B))
Sec. 4-40. - Notice of application for license.
(a)
A notice of each application shall be advertised in a newspaper having general circulation in this county and in which sheriff's sales are advertised once a week for two weeks within the four-week period immediately preceding the meeting of the board at which the application is to be considered and acted upon. The notice shall be in such form as the board may prescribe from time to time. The applicant shall pay the cost of such notice in advance to the newspaper to which the notice is delivered for publication.
(b)
The notice required in this section is only required for new applications, or for applications being made after the applicant's license has been involuntarily terminated or revoked or, when a license expired without being timely renewed. Applicants shall confer with the county clerk or other authorized county official regarding the next available date for the application to be considered by the board after the required notice has been published for two weeks.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § III; Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 4(E))
Sec. 4-41. - Written and oral statements and objections.
(a)
Any county resident or any person owning land in the county may submit a written statement or petition either in support of or opposition to a pending license application regarding the granting of any license under this article.
(b)
A copy of any objection shall be mailed by the person submitting it to the applicant and the person filing the objection shall certify in writing at the time he files the objection that a copy has been mailed or delivered to the applicant at the address indicated on the application. In the event of a written objection filed in petition form by more than one landowner or resident, only such objector need certify that the copy was mailed or delivered to the applicant as required in this section.
(c)
Alternatively, any county resident or landowner may make an oral objection to the granting of license under this chapter at any regular or special meeting of the county board of commissioners providing such landowner or resident, or the representative of any group of such objectors, gives the applicant at least 48 hours' written or oral notice of the intent to make such oral objections. The person giving notice, or the representative of any group of residents or landowners giving notice, shall certify at the time of the making of the objection that proper notice to the applicant has been given as required by this section.
(d)
No objections will be considered unless and until the notice or notices required to be given to the applicant have been given in accordance with this section.
(e)
Applicants may respond to written or oral objections submitted under this section. It is preferred that such responses are in writing, but the response may be made orally at any regular or special meeting of the county board of commissioners at which the resident or landowner makes or submits the objection.
(f)
Each person submitting such a statement or objection shall identify the location of his property in relationship to the proposed location referred to in the license application. This may be done by a map, sketch or narrative statement.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § III; 1986 Ga. Laws (Act No. 537), p. 5710)
Sec. 4-42. - Hearing before board of commissioners; factors considered; determination; rehearing.
(a)
The application for a license to be issued under this article shall be placed on the agenda for action by the board at the first regular monthly meeting of the board which is scheduled after the date the applicant advises the county clerk that he will have run the second weekly notice as required above.
(b)
The county board of commissioners shall consider all circumstances that may reflect favorably and unfavorably upon the applicant, the application, and the proposed location. If in the judgment of the board the circumstances are such that granting of the license would not be in the best interest of the general public the board shall deny the application.
(c)
All objections and statements of support submitted or made by any residents or landowners shall be taken into consideration by the board in determining whether to grant or deny an application filed pursuant to this chapter; however, no such statements or objections shall be controlling or binding upon the board, and the final decision on the application shall always remain within the sole discretion of the board.
(d)
If a majority of people living within one mile of the proposed location of an establishment oppose the grant of the license and file objections to the grant of the license, a license may be withheld if it reasonably appears that issuance of the license would be a hazard or nuisance to the neighborhood.
(e)
If an application is denied, applicant may petition the board for reconsideration, providing applicant has additional relevant information to present that was not presented to the board at the time of its original consideration. Such petition must be filed no later than seven days following the date applicant is notified of denial.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § III; Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 4(F))
Sec. 4-43. - Form of license.
All licenses to be issued under this article shall be on forms prescribed by the board from time to time.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 4(H))
Sec. 4-44. - Name in which license is issued.
(a)
A license issued to an individual shall be issued in the name of the individual. A license issued to a partnership, limited liability company, or other similar legally recognized association which is not a corporation shall be issued:
(1)
In the name of the partnership, limited liability company, or other legally recognized association; and
(2)
In the case of a partnership, in the name of one of the general partners who shall be the named licensee.
(b)
A license issued to a corporation shall be issued:
(1)
In the name of the corporation; and
(2)
In the name of the majority stockholder or a principal officer of the corporation or the officer or employee of the corporation primarily responsible for the operation of the licensed premises, and such majority stockholder or officer or employee shall be the named licensee.
(c)
A license issued to a limited liability company shall be issued:
(1)
In the name of the limited liability company; and
(2)
In the name of the managing member primarily responsible for the operation of the premises, and such managing member shall be the named licensee.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 4(A))
Sec. 4-45. - License term; doing business with expired license prohibited.
Licenses shall be valid only for the calendar year in which they are issued. No licensee may continue to sell alcoholic beverages authorized under this article after his license has expired unless a new or renewed license has been issued.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 4(G))
Sec. 4-46. - Transfer.
(a)
Application and approval required; exception. A license shall be required for each sales location and shall not be transferable from one location to another or from one person to another, except with the express approval of the board after written application for such transfer and the payment of a transfer fee as prescribed by the board; provided, however, that in the case of the death of a named licensee, such license may be transferred to the administrator, executor or adult heir or heirs of the deceased provided such administrator, executor or heirs, meets all of the qualifications of a licensee as prescribed above, and completes an application form for a license and furnishes a complete set of his fingerprints to the Georgia Bureau of Investigation just as an applicant for a new license. There shall be no transfer fee for the transfer of the license of a deceased licensee to his administrator, executor, or adult heir as provided in this section.
(b)
Requirements for transfer upon death of licensee. In the case of the death of a licensee, except for good cause shown, or by prior consent of the board, the administrator, executor or heir must comply with the requirements of this section within seven business days after the death of the licensee, or the license of the deceased licensee shall be automatically suspended until such procedure is complied with, and the license shall be surrendered to the board and no alcoholic beverages may be sold at the licensed location until the license is reinstated or a new license is issued. Once the administrator, executor or heir has complied with the requirements under this section, the administrator, executor or heir may continue to operate under the transferred license for the remainder of the calendar year unless the board determines it would otherwise violate this article. If next a transferee under this article desires to renew the license he must follow the same procedure as any other licensee for renewal. If an administrator, executor or heir is not a resident of this county, he may appoint a designee just as any other licensee. Failure of an administrator, executor or heir to apply for a transfer of the license of a deceased licensee within 30 days after the death of a licensee shall result in the license being automatically terminated; anyone desiring a license at the same location after such termination must reapply as for a new license.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § IV; Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 8)
Sec. 4-47. - Change in business ownership or inability to continue as a licensee.
(a)
If any licensee withdraws from, sells or otherwise transfers the licensee's interest in the licensed business, or if the licensee is determined to be mentally incompetent by a court of competent jurisdiction, or if the licensee becomes mentally incompetent to the extent he can no longer carry out the responsibilities of a licensee as required under this article, or if the licensee violates this article and is no longer able to fully comply with this article, the licensee or his legal representative shall immediately notify the board and surrender the license.
(b)
In the case of such a withdrawal, transfer or sale of business interest, or other event requiring a surrender of a license, anyone desiring to have a license at the same location shall, except when a transfer of license is authorized due to the death of a licensee, make an application for a new license in the same manner as for an original license.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 9)
Sec. 4-48. - Renewal.
(a)
Application; due date. Except as otherwise provided, all alcoholic beverage licensees with licenses issued under this article shall be required to apply for renewal of their licenses annually on forms prescribed by the county, and must file such application for renewal and comply with all provisions of this article no later than December 1 of the year immediately preceding the year for which the renewal license is being sought. However, no application for renewal shall be considered or approved if the licensee is not in full compliance with this article at the time of the filing of the application for renewal, or at any time prior to the renewal license being issued.
(b)
Grace period; late fees. Any application for the renewal of a license filed on or after December 1, but prior to 12:00 noon, on the last day on which the office of the county commissioners is officially opened for business in December of that year, shall pay a late fee in the amount provided in the county fee schedule in addition to all other fees imposed by this article. Such late fee must be paid when the late application for renewal is submitted; no late application for renewal shall be accepted or considered until the late fee is paid.
(c)
Deadline for applying for renewal license. No application for renewal of a license will be accepted or considered if filed after 12:00 noon on the last day on which the office of the county commissioners is officially opened for business in December in any year. Any licensee who desires to renew his license, but fails to file a renewal application prior to the final deadline date in any year, must file an application for a new license in the same manner as an applicant applying for an original license. The board may, but is not required to, notify or remind licensees of the deadlines for filing for a renewal license each year.
(d)
Approval; referral to board of commissioners. The clerk, or in his absence, the deputy clerk, or other county employee or elected official designated by the board, shall review applications for the renewal of a license, and may approve the renewal applications and issue renewal licenses hereunder on such forms as may be prescribed by the board from time to time, providing the application is in proper form, all fees have been paid, and the licensee is in full compliance with this article. If the clerk or other authorized county official has any reservations about issuing a renewal license, the matter shall be presented to the board for its consideration and action at the board's next regular meeting if one is scheduled prior to the end of the calendar year in which the renewal application is filed; otherwise, the board shall call a special meeting prior to the end of that calendar year to consider the questioned renewal application in order that the renewal application can be acted upon prior to the expiration of the licensee's license for that calendar year.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § III; Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 7)
Sec. 4-49. - Suspension and revocation.
(a)
Grounds. Any violation of the provisions of this article shall be grounds for immediate suspension or revocation for cause of the license by the board. Knowingly filing a false application, or providing any false information in any other reports or forms required to be provided to the county or state regarding the licensee's business or sales of alcohol beverages will be grounds for the immediate suspension or revocation of the license by the board.
(b)
Automatic revocation upon revocation of state license. Upon revocation of a state permit or license to sell alcoholic beverages the county license issued under this chapter shall be automatically revoked and forfeited.
(c)
Removal of license by authorized law enforcement officers. The board, by resolution, may direct the sheriff of the county and his lawful deputies to remove the license from any place of business of any licensee upon a determination by the sheriff or his deputies that any provision of this chapter or state law pertaining to the sale of alcoholic beverages, has been violated. Upon removal of the license as provided above, it shall be illegal for the licensee to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages until such time as the license has been reinstated as hereinafter provided.
(d)
Notice to licensee. Upon removal of a license, the board shall give written notice within three days to the licensee of the reasons for the removal, as well as notice of any suspension or revocation of the license.
(e)
Right of licensee to request hearing. The licensee may, within ten days from receipt of the notice from the board, make written application to the board for a hearing on the matter of suspension or revocation, including the right of the licensee to present evidence and cross-examine opposing witnesses. Failure of the licensee to request a hearing within the allowed period shall constitute a waiver of any right of further appeal of the board's decision regarding revocation or suspension.
(f)
Conduct of hearing; determination. If a hearing is requested as provided above, the same will be held within ten days from receipt of the request by the board. The board, after hearing evidence, shall reach its decision and advise the licensee of the same. If the board determines, with or without a hearing, that a license shall be reinstated, the license shall be returned to the licensee on the effective date of the reinstatement as determined by the board.
(g)
Method of service of notices. All notices required to be made under this section shall be in writing, and shall either be delivered personally or sent by certified mail, return receipt requested, to the other party's last known address, or in the case of notices to the licensee, to the address of the licensed premises if the licensee's address is unknown, or if the licensee can not be located.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 10)
Sec. 4-50. - Forfeiture of license.
Any holder of any license issued pursuant to this chapter who, for a period of three consecutive months after his license has been issued, ceases to operate the business and sale of the product or products authorized, shall automatically forfeit his license without the necessity of any further action.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § IX)
Sec. 4-51. - Display of license.
Each license issued under this article shall at all times be kept plainly exposed to view upon the licensed premises.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § V; Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 15)
Sec. 4-52. - Knowledge of licensee and employees of requirements of chapter.
(a)
Every licensee shall, prior to applying for a license, read and familiarize himself with the provisions of this article, and the licensee's application shall constitute a certification by the applicant that he has done so.
(b)
Every licensee shall maintain a copy of this article on the licensed premises and shall instruct each employee engaged in the sale or handling of alcoholic beverages on the relevant provisions of this article prior to the employee's selling or handling of any alcoholic beverages.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 17)
Secs. 4-53—4-77. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - CONSUMPTION ON PREMISES LICENSES

Sec. 4-78. - Applicability.
The provisions of this division apply to licenses authorizing consumption on the premises and are in addition to all other general requirements of this article applicable to such licenses except to the extent that general requirements conflict with the specific regulations in this division.
Sec. 4-79. - Eligibility restricted.
Consumption on the premises license may be granted only to restaurants and private clubs. Licensees who have a license to sell alcohol by the drink for consumption on the premises may not have a license to sell unopened alcoholic beverages by the bottle or package at the same location; nor may a licensee having a license for the sale of alcohol by the drink or consumption on the premises simultaneously have a wholesale license for the sale or distribution of alcoholic beverages.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 25)
Sec. 4-80. - Restaurant qualifications.
To be eligible for a pouring or a consumption-on-the-premises license, a restaurant shall:
(1)
Be used and held out to the public as a place where meals prepared on the premises are regularly served to the public for adequate pay each day the establishment is open for business;
(2)
Contain one or more public dining rooms, with adequate and sanitary kitchen facilities and staff to prepare, cook, and serve suitable food for its guests at all times the establishment is opened for business;
(3)
Have adequate personnel and food supplies available to cook, prepare and serve meals prepared on the premises during any time the establishment is open for business;
(4)
Have a valid the county health department food service permit and any other applicable local, state, and federal permits and licenses required for food service establishments;
(5)
Have at least 60 percent of its total sales comprised of the sale of food prepared and consumed on the premises and nonalcoholic beverages consumed on the premises; and, for this purpose, if a restaurant makes a minimum charge, cover charge or admission charge, or any other nonfood or nonalcoholic beverage charge, the amount so charged shall not be counted in computing total sales and shall not be counted as food or beverage sales;
(6)
Have a permanent seating capacity at tables or booths, not counting bar stools, of at least 25 persons.
(7)
Be in a facility or building which is safe and in good repair, in compliance with all applicable state, federal and local building codes and fire safety codes and regulations at all times, it is opened for business.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 26)
Sec. 4-81. - Private club qualifications.
(a)
To be eligible for a pouring or consumption on the premises licenses, a private club must be a nonprofit association organized under the laws of this state that:
(1)
Has been in existence at least one year prior to the filing of its application for a license;
(2)
Has at least 50 regular dues-paying members;
(3)
Owns, hires, or leases a building or space within a building for the reasonable use of its members, which building or space;
(4)
Has suitable kitchen and dining room space and equipment;
(5)
Is staffed with a sufficient number of employees for cooking, preparing, and serving meals for its members and guests; and
(6)
Has no member, officer, agent, or employee directly or indirectly receiving, in the form of salary or other compensation, any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages beyond a fixed salary.
(b)
For purposes of subsection (a)(4) of this section, the term "fixed salary" means the amount of compensation paid any member, officer, agent, or employee of a private club as may be fixed for him by its members at a prior annual meeting or by the governing body out of the general revenue of the club, and does not include any commission or any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages. For the purposes of this definition, tips, or gratuities which are added to the bill under club regulations shall not be considered profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages.
(c)
No alcoholic beverage license shall be granted to a private club organized or operated primarily for the selling or serving of alcoholic beverages.
(d)
Private clubs licensed under this chapter shall be required to maintain the same percentage sale of food and nonalcoholic beverages as compared to alcoholic beverages as is required of restaurants in the preceding section.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 27)
Sec. 4-82. - Ratio of alcoholic to nonalcoholic sales.
Should the total sales from food and nonalcoholic beverages reported by any licensee for consumption on the premises not equal or exceed 60 percent of the total sales of all food and beverages for any two consecutive reporting periods, the license may be suspended or revoked by the board subject to the right for a hearing as provided in this article.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 28)
Sec. 4-83. - Statement of sales.
In addition to the reporting requirements required elsewhere in this division, the board may require a licensee to furnish a statement from a certified public accountant or a registered public accountant that reports the percentage of the licensee's total sales derived from the sale of food and nonalcoholic beverages sold on the premises compared to sale of all alcoholic beverages sold on the premises in the licensee's most recent reporting period. The failure of a licensee to furnish such a statement within 15 days after being requested to do so shall be grounds for suspending the license of such licensee until the requested statement is received and verified by the board. Any fees charged by the certified public accountant or registered public accountant shall be paid by the licensee.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 29)
Sec. 4-84. - Records and reports requirements for licenses for consumption on the premises.
(a)
Sales records; inspection; retention period. All consumption on the premises licensees shall keep and preserve records of all alcoholic beverages delivered to the licensed premises, and purchased and sold by the licensee, and shall keep and preserve records of all receipts for food and nonalcoholic beverages purchased and sold by them. Such records shall at all times during normal business hours be open for inspection by an authorized agent of the county. Such records shall be maintained for a period of at least three years, provided that the board may authorize the disposal of records prior to the expiration of three years if the maintenance of such records is no longer required by the county.
(b)
Consumption reports.
(1)
All consumption on the premises licensees shall file with the county clerk, at the time and in the form prescribed by the board, semi-annual on-premises consumption report which shall include, but not be limited to, a signed statement by the licensee of the gross revenues (excluding all nonfood items such as cover charges and tips) for:
a.
Food and nonalcoholic beverages; and
b.
Alcoholic beverages, received by the licensee at the licensed premises for the six-month period immediately preceding the report.
(2)
The report must be filed by July 15 for the first six-month period ending June 30 each year, and by January 15th for the last six-month period of each year. If the board is not satisfied with or questions any report filed, the board may require the licensee to furnish the business records for the period covered by the report.
(3)
The actual revenues of a licensee furnished in the reports shall be treated as confidential and shall not be made available to the public unless the county must use them as evidence for any legal or disciplinary action involving the licensee for a violation of this division, or unless it is determined that the county is required to release such information under the open records statute or other applicable laws, or unless the county is required to make information available under a court order or other legal process.
(c)
Other records. All consumption on the premises licensees shall file with the county clerk, at the time and in the form prescribed by the board, any other documents, reports, records, or books of the licensee relating to the licensed premises under the business carried on at that location, as may be required by the board from time to time.
(d)
Suspension or revocation of license for failure to file reports and other records. Failure of a licensee to file the semi-annual reports or other records as required herein without just cause may result in the suspension or revocation of the licensee's license until licensee complies with such requirements.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 16)
Secs. 4-85—4-111. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - PREMISES AND OPERATION REGULATIONS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-112. - Responsibility of named licensee for operation and compliance.
The named licensee shall be active in the operation of the licensed business and shall be personally present on the licensed premises sufficiently to assure compliance with the provisions of this article. The licensee is responsible for all activities at the licensed premises and for any violations of this article regardless of whether the licensee has actual knowledge of or participates in the activity or violation.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 13)
Sec. 4-113. - Responsibility of agents for acts of licensed business entities.
In the case of a partnership, or other legally recognized association, each partner shall be responsible for the actions of the named licensee and the conduct of the licensed business. In the case of a corporation, the corporation shall also be responsible for the actions of the named licensee and the conduct of the licensed business. In the case of a limited liability company, the limited liability company shall also be responsible for the actions of the named licensee and the conduct of the licensed business.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 4(D))
Sec. 4-114. - Employees must meet license qualifications.
Licensee shall not employ in the operation of a licensed establishment any person who can not meet the qualifications for licensure provided in this chapter.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § V)
Sec. 4-115. - Records to be maintained.
Each retail licensee shall maintain for a period of 12 months, records of all deliveries of alcoholic beverages made to the licensed establishment by all wholesalers, including copies of the wholesaler's invoices. Licensee shall make such records available for inspection by authorized county personnel upon request.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § V)
Sec. 4-116. - Products for retail sale must be purchased from licensed county wholesaler.
No alcoholic beverages shall be possessed, sold or held for retail sale unless purchased from a wholesaler licensed to make sales in the county.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § V)
Sec. 4-117. - Premises location restrictions.
(a)
Prohibited proximities. No person knowingly and intentionally may serve, sell, dispense or offer to sell:
(1)
Any distilled spirits in or within 100 yards of any church building or within 200 yards of any school building, educational building, school grounds, or college campus.
(2)
Any wine or malt beverages within 100 yards of any school building, school grounds, church or college campus.
(3)
Any distilled spirits, wine, or malt beverages within 100 yards of an alcoholic treatment center owned and operated by this state or any county or municipal governmental therein.
(4)
Any alcoholic beverage within 200 yards of any public park or recreational facility of any kind owned by the county either in whole or in part, or by the county board of education, or by any other government or agency or department thereof, except the civic center and golf course, currently owned by the City of Hazlehurst, which are specifically excluded from the terms "public park" and "recreational facility" for purposes of this article.
(5)
Any alcoholic beverage within a 300-foot radius of a property line of a private residence, except that if the house structure is not within a 600-foot radius of the establishment which is to be licensed, this restriction and prohibition shall not apply and provided, however, that this restriction may be waived if the owners of all of the private residences within the restricted distance consent in writing to granting of a license for the proposed location.
(6)
Any alcoholic beverage in any building or on any property or grounds owned by the county, except the property commonly known as the "Big House" facility located off the U.S. Highway Number 341 adjoining the lands upon which the Days Inn/Village Inn Motel are currently located, providing such sale or dispensing of alcoholic beverages does not otherwise violate any state law or City of Hazlehurst ordinance.
(b)
Method of measuring distances. For the purposes of this section, all distances shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground from the property line of the church, school, etc., nearest the proposed establishment, to the side or edge of the building housing the establishment nearest the church, school, etc.
(c)
Exceptions for new construction. No licensee seeking a renewal shall be barred or prohibited from obtaining a renewal license if a school, church, residence or other facility referred in subsection (a) of this section is constructed or located within the restricted distance after the license has been originally issued, providing that if a licensee fails to renew his license, or allows his license to be revoked or suspended, and he has to reapply for a license, then, in such event, the former licensee shall be subject to all of the requirements of this article, including the distance requirements, just as for an original license.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § VII; Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 12(A)—(D))
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.
Sec. 4-118. - Interest of landlord in sales of tenant licensee restricted.
It is unlawful for a licensee to enter into any agreement whereby the rental paid for a licensed premises is based in whole or in part on the volume of sales of alcoholic beverages by the licensed business or whereby the lessor otherwise shares in the profits or receipts from the licensed business's sale of alcoholic beverages.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 14)
Sec. 4-119. - Days of sales restricted.
No licensee shall permit the sale of alcoholic beverages on any day or any time when such sales are prohibited by law, during the polling hours of any election being held in the county, on Sunday or on Christmas Day.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § V; Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 19)
State Law reference— Sale of alcoholic beverages on Sundays, election days, and Christmas Day generally, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20; local regulation of Sunday sales, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-7; sale of alcoholic beverage to intoxicated person, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-22; furnishing to, possession of, or purchase of alcoholic beverages by persons under 21 years of age, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23.
Sec. 4-120. - Stock packaging, storage and handling requirements.
(a)
Alcoholic beverages shall be delivered to and received at licensed premises only in the original container and only in a conveyance owned and operated by a licensed wholesale dealer (or licensed common carrier acting for a wholesaler). Alcoholic beverages shall be sold at retail only on the premises.
(b)
A retail licensee shall store alcoholic beverages only on the licensed premises and at no other place. All stock shall be available at all times for inspection by an authorized agent of the county.
(c)
Any alcoholic beverages found in any retail licensee's stock which were not received from a wholesaler licensed to make deliveries in the county shall be subject to immediate confiscation.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 21(A), (B))
Sec. 4-121. - Illumination of premises exterior.
The exterior of each building in which alcoholic beverages are sold for consumption on the premises shall contain sufficient lighting so that all sides of the building, parking areas, and all entrances thereto are clearly visible at all times.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 21(C))
Sec. 4-122. - Cleanliness of premises; inspections.
All licensed premises shall be kept clean and in proper sanitary condition and in full compliance with all regulations governing the condition of premises used for the storage and sale of food for human consumption. All licensed premises shall be open at all times for inspection by authorized agents of the county.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 22)
Sec. 4-123. - Consumption in package sales locations prohibited; posting notice required.
It is unlawful for any person to open or consume any alcoholic beverages on premises licensed for the sale of alcoholic beverages by the package. In each retail package sales location and place of business, where so restricted, there shall be permanently posted and clearly visible to customers, at least two notices in letters at least two inches high, the following language:
EXPAND
"Notice: Unlawful to open malt beverages, wine or any alcoholic beverage containers on premises."
  (Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 24)
State Law reference— Breaking of package on premises prohibited, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-26.
Sec. 4-124. - Sales to underage persons prohibited; employment of underage persons.
(a)
Prohibited sales. Except as otherwise provided in this section:
(1)
No persons knowingly, directly, or through another person, shall furnish, cause to be furnished, or permit any person in such person's employ to furnish any alcoholic beverage to any person under 21 years of age;
(2)
No person under 21 years of age shall purchase, attempt to purchase, or knowingly possess any alcoholic beverage;
(3)
No person under 21 years of age shall misrepresent such person's age in any manner whatever for the purpose of obtaining illegally any alcoholic beverage;
(4)
No person knowingly or intentionally shall act as an agent to purchase or acquire any alcoholic beverage for or on behalf of a person under 21 years of age;
(5)
No person under 21 years of age shall misrepresent his identity or use any false identification for the purpose of purchasing or obtaining any alcoholic beverage.
(6)
No wholesaler, distributor, or consumption-on-the premises licensee shall allow any person under the age of 18 years to dispense, sell, serve, take orders for, or handle alcoholic beverages.
(b)
Exclusions. The prohibitions of subsections (a)(1), (2), and (4) of this section shall not apply with respect to the sale, purchase, or possession of alcoholic beverages for consumption:
(1)
For medical purposes pursuant to a prescription of a physician duly authorized to practice medicine in this state;
(2)
At a religious ceremony;
(3)
When the parent or guardian of the person under 21 years of age gives the alcoholic beverage to the person and when in possession is in the home of the parent or guardian and such parent or guardian is present.
(c)
Effect of proper identification. The prohibition contained in subsection (a)(1) of this section shall not apply with respect to sale of alcoholic beverages by a person when such person has been furnished with proper identification showing that the person to whom the alcoholic beverage is sold to is 21 years of age or older, providing that the person making the sale does not have actual knowledge that the purchaser is under 21 years of age. For purposes of this subsection, the term "proper identification" means any document issued by a governmental agency containing a description of the person, such person's photograph, or both, and giving such person's date of birth and included, without being limited to, a passport, military identification, driver's license, or an identification card authorized under sections 40-5-100 through 40-5-104 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated (O.C.G.A. §§ 40-5-100 through 40-5-104). The term "proper identification" does not include a birth certificate or school I.D.
(d)
Verification of age; request for property identification. In any case where a reasonable or prudent person could reasonably be in doubt as to whether or not the person to whom an alcoholic beverage is to be sold or otherwise furnished is actually 21 years of age or older, it shall be the duty of the person selling or otherwise furnishing such alcoholic beverage to request to see and be furnished with proper identification as provided in subsection of this section in order to verify the age of such person; and the failure to make such request and verification in any case where the person to whom the alcoholic beverage is sold or otherwise furnished is less than 21 years of age may be considered by the trier of fact in determining whether the person selling or otherwise furnishing such alcoholic beverage did so knowingly.
(e)
Section does not prohibit employment of person under 21. This section shall not prohibit employment of a person under 21 years of age in licensed premises if such employment is otherwise not unlawful or the employment of persons under 21 on licensed premises where such persons do not dispense, sell, serve, take orders for, or handle alcoholic beverages. In addition, this section shall not prohibit persons 16 years of age or older from selling or handling packaged or unopened alcoholic beverages for consumption off the premises in grocery stores, convenience stores, or similar establishments.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § V; Ord. of 2-18-2003, §§ 18, 23)
State Law reference— Furnishing alcoholic beverages to, and purchase of alcoholic beverages by, persons under 21 years of age prohibited, possession of alcoholic beverages by persons under 21 years of age prohibited, proper identification, procedure where false identification tendered, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23; unlawful sale of alcoholic beverages to, and unlawful purchase and possession of alcoholic beverages by, any person under 21 years of age, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23.1; persons under 18 years of age not to be allowed or required to serve, sell, or take orders for alcoholic beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-24.
Sec. 4-125. - Sales to intoxicated persons.
No licensee shall permit the sale of alcoholic beverages to any person who is in a state of noticeable intoxication or allow persons who are noticeably intoxicated to congregate on licensed premises.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § V; Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 20(A))
State Law reference— Furnishing alcoholic beverages to intoxicated persons, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-22.
Sec. 4-126. - Certain sales and service prohibited in public places; exception.
(a)
It is unlawful to furnish or dispense alcoholic beverages through kegs, barrels, cylinders or other portable receptacles containing tap or pump-type devices, in which the flow of contents is controlled by the operator, in a public street or in any public or semipublic parking facility or in the common area of an apartment, condominium or multifamily residential building.
(b)
It is further unlawful to furnish or dispense alcoholic beverages using vehicles containing tap or pump-type devices. The term "semipublic parking facility" includes any privately owned area wherein motor vehicles may be parked by the public in conjunction with any business, enterprise, commercial establishment, office building or multiple-family residential facility. When receptacles are found in violation of this section and the person maintaining the receptacle cannot be identified, the receptacle shall be confiscated and held for 30 days by the county sheriff. The receptacle may be claimed by the owner or lessor, but will be disposed of at the end of 30 days if unclaimed.
(c)
Nothing in this section shall prohibit persons holding licenses pursuant to this article from dispensing through kegs, barrels, cylinders or other such receptacles inside the establishments or premises approved by the board for a pouring license.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 11)
Sec. 4-127. - Gambling and disorderly conduct on licensed premises prohibited.
No licensee shall permit any gambling, betting, illegal lottery, or other device for the hazarding of any money or other thing of value on the licensed premises, except that this prohibition shall not apply with respect to a properly licensed bingo game, or any other activities, not prohibited by state or federal laws to be conducted in a facility or business, or at an activity where alcoholic beverages may be sold for consumption on the premises.

DIVISION 2. - CONSUMPTION ON THE PREMISES ESTABLISHMENTS

Sec. 4-153. - Applicability.
The provisions of this division apply to licenses authorizing consumption on the premises and apply to such licenses in addition to all other general requirements of this article except to the extent that general requirements conflict with the specific regulations in this division.
Sec. 4-154. - Illumination of premises.
All restaurants, including all tables, booths, and other areas where customers are served, as well as, all passageways for customers, shall be sufficiently will illuminated so that they may be viewed by those inside the premises.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 30(A))
Sec. 4-155. - Sales in rooms not open to the general public prohibited.
The sale of alcoholic beverages in any back room or side room which is not open to the general public is prohibited, except that this prohibition shall not apply with respect to private parties that have been scheduled in advance, room service to hotel guests in their hotel rooms, provided a properly licensed restaurant is located on the premises and private clubs.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 30(B))
Sec. 4-156. - Hours of sale.
Consumption on the premises licensees shall only engage in the sale and service of alcoholic beverages between the hours of 6:00 a.m. and 12:00 midnight Monday through Saturday on days on which sales are not prohibited by this chapter. No consumption on the premises licensee shall permit the consumption of alcohol by any person in or on the licensed premises on any day or at any time when it would not be lawful for licensee to sell alcoholic beverages for consumption under this chapter.
(Ord. of 2-3-1981, § V; Ord. of 2-18-2003, §§ 31, 34(3))
State Law reference— Sale of alcoholic beverages on Sundays, election days, and Christmas Day generally, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20; local regulation of Sunday sales, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-7; sale of alcoholic beverage to intoxicated person, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-22; furnishing to, possession of, or purchase of alcoholic beverages by persons under 21 years of age, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23.
Sec. 4-157. - Dancing on premises.
Patron dancing shall be permitted at facilities licensed for consumption on the premises sales only where adequate space exists and all fire and safety regulations are met.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 32)
Sec. 4-158. - Sales and consumption on premises only; removal of beverages prohibited.
(a)
No consumption on the premises licensee shall permit the sale, dispensing or serving of any alcoholic beverage to any person except in or on the licensed premises.
(b)
All alcoholic beverages sold by consumption on the premises licensees shall be consumed only on the licensed premises. It is unlawful for any person to remove from the licensed premises, or for any licensee to permit any person to remove from the licensed premises, any alcoholic beverages sold for consumption on the premises. Each licensee shall be responsible for ensuring that no person removes any alcoholic beverages from the premises in any container.
(c)
It is unlawful for any person to drink or have in his possession an open container of any alcoholic beverage on any public street, sidewalk, park, or other public place within the county, or upon or within any motor vehicle on the streets, sidewalks, parks, and public places of the county.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, §§ 33, 34(1))
State Law reference— Permitting persons to remove alcoholic beverages to other premises for certain purposes prohibited, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-44.
Sec. 4-159. - Activities promoting increased consumption prohibited.
Sponsoring, conducting, or allowing contests or promotions that have as their primary purpose the increasing of the consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises is prohibited.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 34(2))
Sec. 4-160. - Employment of persons convicted of certain offenses prohibited.
No consumption on the premises licensee shall employ any person who has been convicted of a felony or a violation of any law of any jurisdiction relating to the sale of alcoholic beverages, gambling, or the manufacture, sale or distribution of any illegal drugs for the ten-year period immediately preceding the beginning of that person's employment, or who is then currently on parole or probation for the conviction of any such felony or stated offense.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 34(4))
Sec. 4-161. - Sexually oriented entertainment.
(a)
No person shall conduct, stage, arrange, or permit upon premises on which alcoholic beverages are sold or consumed any live performance that constitutes or simulates:
(1)
Sexual intercourse, masturbation, sodomy, bestiality, oral copulation, flagellation or any sexual acts which are prohibited by law;
(2)
The touching, caressing, or fondling of the breast, buttocks, anus, or genitals; or
(3)
The displaying of any portion of the female breast below the top of the areola or the displaying any portion of any person's pubic hair, anus, cleft of the buttocks, vulva or genitals.
(b)
No person shall conduct, stage, arrange, or permit upon premises on which alcoholic beverages are sold or consumed the use of artificial devices or inanimate objects to perform, simulate, or depict any of the prohibited activities described in subsection (a) of this section.
(c)
Nothing in this section shall be interpreted to prohibit performances in mainstream performance establishments, museums, or theaters.
(Ord. of 2-18-2003, § 35)
State Law reference— Certain nude and sexual conduct, use of devices or objects to perform or simulate such conduct, and showing and displaying of pictures on licensed premises prohibited, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-41.

ARTICLE IV. - LICENSING, SALE AND REGULATION OF MALT BEVERAGES AND WINE

Sec. 4-181. - Definitions.
new
Applicant shall mean any person or entity applying for a license as contemplated and authorized by this resolution.
Establishment shall mean any physical location for the storage or sale either at retail or wholesale of malt beverages and wines in Jeff Davis County.
His , her , he , she , it , they , them , and their shall, as the context may require, include any other gender and the singular or the plural, for a proper reading.
License shall mean the permit granted to Licensee for the operation of an establishment for the sale and/or distribution of malt beverages or wine in Jeff Davis County.
Licensee means any person engaged in distribution or selling, at retail or wholesale, any malt beverage or wine in Jeff Davis County.
Licensor shall mean the Board of Commissioners of Jeff Davis County, or other authority having control of the fiscal affairs of the county.
Malt beverages shall include "beer" and shall mean those beverages defined by O.C.G.A. § 3-l-2(3).
Person means a natural person, proprietorship, partnership, either general or limited, firm, association or corporation.
Wines shall include those beverages defined by O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2(24).
(Ord. of 1-10-2019, § I)
Sec. 4-182. - Qualifications of applicants and licensees.
new
If the applicant is not a resident of Jeff Davis County, he or she must designate a person who is a resident of Jeff Davis County who shall be responsible for any matter relating to the license.
(Ord. of 1-10-2019, § II)
Sec. 4-183. - Granting of licenses (both wholesale and retail).
new
(a)
No person shall operate an establishment involving the sale of malt beverages or wine in Jeff Davis County without having first complied with the provisions of this article.
(b)
No license shall be granted to any person for the operation of an establishment involving the sale of malt beverages or wine in any area prohibited by law or by valid zoning ordinance.
(c)
No license shall be granted to any person, persons, or officers or directors of any corporation, for the operation of an establishment involving the sale of malt beverages or wine who does not meet the qualifications set forth in section 4-182 above.
(d)
Any person desiring to obtain a license for the operation of an establishment involving the sale of malt beverages or wine in the county shall submit an application in the form to be prescribed by the Licensor and which shall contain the following information, or additional information as the licensor may from time to time require, to wit:
(1)
Type of license for which applicant is applying.
(2)
Full name of the applicant, including any aliases or trade names, and, in the case of a partnership, firm, association or corporation, the name of the individual or agent who shall be held responsible for compliance with the provisions of this resolution. In the event the applicant is a partnership or firm, the names and addresses of all members of the partnership or firm shall be furnished; and in the event the applicant is an association or corporation, the names and addresses of its principal officers, directors and all stockholders The complete mailing and location address of the applicant and the address of the establishment for the sale of malt beverages and wine and the complete mailing address of the person who shall be responsible for any matter relating to the license if that person is not the applicant
(3)
The name and address of the nearest church and the estimated distance from the establishment.
(4)
The name and address of the nearest school building, educational building, or school grounds and the estimated distance from the establishment.
(5)
Whether or not the applicant or any person listed under sub-paragraph (2) above has previously been refused a license. If the answer is in the affirmative, state the month and year of such refusal, the jurisdiction revoking the license and the circumstances surrounding such refusal.
(6)
Has applicant or any person named in sub-paragraph (2) above, having previously obtained a license, had the same revoked? If the answer is in the affirmative, state the month and year of such revocation, the jurisdiction revoking the license and the circumstances surrounding such revocation.
(7)
Has applicant or any person identified in sub-paragraph (b) above been convicted within the last two years of any criminal activity.
The truth of the information contained in said application shall be sworn to by the applicant before a person authorized to administer oaths.
(e)
A licensee shall not have any vested rights in a license and a license shall not be renewable as a matter of right, but must be applied for and approved annually. However, a licensee may apply for a renewal of his license each year prior to the expiration of his then current license either on the same application form as used for a new license, or on an alternate form to be prescribed by licensor. Applications for a renewal license must be submitted to Licensor no later than December 1st of each year. Any licensee who does not submit his application for renewal of his license by such time without just cause will be*required to apply and qualify in all respects as a new applicant A Licensee may not sell or distribute any malt beverages or wine in a new calendar year until his new license is approved.
(f)
In determining the question of the granting of or refusal to grant the license provided for hereunder, Licensor shall consider, among other things the following: The location of the establishment; traffic conditions; the uses of the surrounding property; the conviction of the applicant or his employees for the violation of any criminal statutes; the question of whether or not applicant holds a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages; and other relevant factors deemed by Licensor to have a reasonable relationship to the desirability of the issuance of license to applicant for the establishment provided for in this article.
(g)
Applicant shall notify Licensor in writing within five days of any changes in the information furnished in the initial application or renewal application. The applicant shall attach, with his application, cash, certified check, cashier's check, or money order in the amount of the annual license fee, plus any sum required for an initial license, if applicable.
(h)
Applications for a new license shall not be considered until after notice of the application has been run two times in the newspaper having general circulation in Jeff Davis County in which legal advertisements and notice of sheriffs sales are authorized to run; the first running of the notice must be at least 30 days prior to the application's consideration by licensor. The notice shall be in a form prescribed by Licensor. This provision shall not apply to applications for renewal of a license which are duly submitted by December 1st.
(i)
Applicant must comply with all state requirements for the issuance of license, including the furnishing of a complete set of fingerprints pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2(c) to be forwarded to the Georgia Bureau of Investigation which shall search the files of the Georgia Crime Information Center for any instance of criminal activity during the two years immediately preceding the date of the application The Georgia Bureau of Investigation shall also submit the fingerprints to the Federal Bureau of Investigation under the rules established by the United States Department of Justice for processing and identification of records. The federal record, if any, shall be obtained and returned to the governing authority submitting the fingerprints.
(j)
Applicants for a new license shall pay to licensor, in addition to all other fees and taxes required herein, an initial administrative fee of $50.00 to defray initial administrative and investigative costs. This fee shall be tendered with the application and shall not be refundable.
(k)
After the application has been filed the road superintendent for the county shall go to the business where the applicant proposes to sell the malt beverages and wine and shall determine if the business is located within 100 yards of any church building or within 200 yards of any school building, educational building, school grounds or college campus or within 100 yards of an alcoholic treatment center owned and operated by this state or any county or municipal government. The business cannot be located within 100 yards of any housing authority property unless a license was issued to said business prior to July 1, 2000. The road superintendent shall then submit a written report to the board of commissioners stating his findings.
(l)
The board of commissioners must review the application and vote to issue the application or deny to issue it after collecting all information required by law and after the notice of the application has been published in the local legal organ A denial of an application shall be delivered to the applicant in writing by first class mail to the address shown on the application.
(m)
If an application is denied, applicant may petition the licensor for reconsideration, providing applicant has additional relevant information to present which was not presented to licensor at the time of its original consideration. Such petition must be filed with licensor no later than seven days following the date applicant is notified of such denial.
(Ord. of 1-10-2019, § III)

new

(1)
Licenses granted hereunder shall be prominently displayed in the licensee's establishment, and shall be, together with the establishment, subject to inspection at any time by licensor or any law enforcement agency having jurisdiction of the area of licensee's establishment.

Such report shall be delivered to Licensor no later than the tenth day of each month following the month of delivery of said malt beverages and wines.

Each retail licensee shall maintain for a period of 12 months records of all deliveries made to said licensee's establishment of malt beverages and wines by all wholesalers, which said records shall include copies of the wholesaler's invoice accompanying said delivery. Licensee shall make available to licensor all such records upon request of the licensor.
(5)
No licensee shall permit consumption of any malt beverages or wine on the premises of licensee's establishment at any time, unless otherwise approved by licensor. On-premise consumption shall generally be limited to private clubs.
(6)
No licensee shall sell or make available any beer, malt beverage or wine to minors, habitual drunkards, or intoxicated persons.
(7)
No beer, malt beverages or wine shall be sold on any Sunday, or Christmas.
(8)
No beer or wine shall be sold at retail on which county beer or wine (as the case may be) tax has not been paid.
(9)
No beer or wine shall be possessed or sold or held for sale at retail except beer or wine purchased from a wholesaler licensed to make sales in the county.

Sec. 4-188. - Excise tax.
new
In addition to the license fees enumerated elsewhere in this regulation and ordinance, and the excise tax levied directly by the State of Georgia, there is hereby imposed a county excise tax as follows:
(1)
On beer and malt beverages: Five cents per 12 ounces, or proportionately thereof, so as to prorate the tax on bottles, cans and containers of various sizes pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-5-80(2).
(2)
On wine: Eighty cents per gallon or 22 cents per liter, or proportionately thereof, so as to prorate the tax on bottles and containers of various sizes.
(3)
The excise taxes, as provided above, shall be imposed upon and paid by the licensed wholesale dealers in malt beverages and wine doing business in Jeff Davis County.
Such taxes shall be paid to licensor by such dealer on or before the tenth day of the month following the calendar month in which the beverages and/or wine are sold or disposed of within this county.
(4)
All retail licensees shall furnish to Licensor monthly copies of all invoices from wholesale dealers of malt beverages and/or wine reflecting the amount of county excise taxes paid to the wholesale dealer. Such copies shall be delivered to licensor no later than the tenth day of the month following the month of delivery to the retail licensee's establishment.
(Ord. of 1-10-2019, § IX)
Sec. 4-189. - Revocation, suspension and automatic forfeiture.
new
(a)
Licensee shall have no vested right in his license, and any license may be suspended or revoked for good cause at the discretion of the licensor.
(b)
A license may be suspended or revoked whenever in the judgment of the licensor the suspension or revocation of the license would be in the best interest of the general public.
(c)
Licensor may suspend a violator's license for up to six months for the first violation in lieu of revocation. Suspension of license is an alternative only for a first violation.
(d)
"Good cause" for the suspension or revocation of such license shall consist of the conviction or plea of guilty or nolo contendere to the violation of any laws or ordinances regulating such businesses (including this ordinance and resolution), or the violation of regulations made pursuant to authority granted for the purpose of regulating such businesses, or the violation of any state or federal law or any county ordinance, (other than traffic ordinances) or the deliberate making of irregularities, omissions, or untruths in the application for license.
(e)
Upon information to Licensor that there may be grounds for suspension or revocation, licensor shall serve written notice upon Licensee, either by personal delivery to Licensee or by sending such notice to licensee by registered or certified mail addressed to the address in licensee's application, requiring him to appear before Licensor and show cause why his license should not be suspended or revoked. Such hearing shall not be held sooner than ten days from the giving of such notice, and shall set forth with particularity the date, time and place of the hearing on said matter; such notice shall also state the condition alleged which could, if sustained, result in suspension or revocation of licensee's license.
(f)
Within ten days of the hearing on such matter, Licensor shall render a decision on the matter, notifying licensee, in writing, of its decision, by first class mail In the event of a suspension or revocation no rebate shall be made on any portion of any licensee fee paid.
(g)
After revocation of any license granted hereunder, no person formerly holding such license shall operate an establishment for the sale of malt beverages or wines in the county without first obtaining a valid license.
(h)
The sheriff, upon notice of such suspension or revocation from the Licensor shall take the necessary steps to see that signs are removed and that all malt beverages and wine sales cease.
(i)
Any holder of any license hereunder who, for a period of three consecutive months after his license has been issued, ceases to operate the business and sale of the product or products authorized, shall automatically forfeit his license without the necessity of any further action.
(j)
Whenever the State of Georgia shall revoke any permit or license to sell at wholesale or retail any malt beverages, beer or wine, the county license to sell malt beverages, beer or wine shall thereupon be automatically revoked and forfeited.
(Ord. of 1-10-2019, § IX)

new

No provision of this article shall apply to any current Licensee or currently existing establishment, if the enforcement of such provision upon any such current licensee or existing establishment would be violative of the Constitution or the Laws of the United States or of the State of Georgia.
