ARTICLE VI. - CIRCUSES, CARNIVALS AND PUBLIC EXHIBITIONS

Sec. 13-130. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the following terms shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them:
Carnival: Any amusement activity, ride, merry-go-round, booth for the conduct of games of skill, food dispensing facility or side show.
Public exhibition: Any circus, menagerie, sideshow or other similar itinerant amusement enterprise which is open to the public and for admission to which a fee is charged.
(Code 1974, § 32-207(2))
Sec. 13-131. - License required.
No person shall conduct or operate a circus, carnival or public exhibition, without having first obtained a license from the city clerk in the manner specified in this chapter.
(Code 1974, § 32-207(1))
Cross reference— As to license fee, see § 13-21.
Sec. 13-132. - Regulatory fee; exemptions.
No person shall conduct or operate a circus, carnival or public exhibition without having first paid the regulatory fee provided in appendix A attached to [Ordinance 04-15] and the administrative fee required by section 13-2. All such activities sponsored and conducted in total by nonprofit, charitable, religious or philanthropic organizations shall be exempt from the payment of such fees, but are subject to all other requirements of this section.
(Ord. No. 04-15, § 6, 12-9-04)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 04-15, § 6, adopted Dec. 9, 2004, repealed the former § 13-132 and enacted a new § 13-132 as set out herein. The former § 13-132 pertained to license fee exemption and derived from Code 1974, § 32-207(6).
Sec. 13-133. - Conditions of issuance of license.
No license under this article shall be issued until the following conditions have been met:
(1)
The operator and sponsor of the circus, carnival or public exhibition have each assumed full responsibility for maintaining order and for keeping the site clean and free of trash, papers and other debris, and have placed trash containers in adequate number and in convenient locations for the use of the public;
(2)
All rides have been inspected for mechanical, structural, electrical and other hazards by the appropriate officers and employees of the city, and adequate safeguards have been placed to protect both operators and the general public from inadvertently coming into contact with moving parts, belts, motor gears, electrical switches and other possible or potential hazards; and
(3)
Applicant has placed on file with the city clerk a certificate of insurance indicating that there is in effect public liability insurance covering any damages arising out of the use and operation of any and all devices and facilities operated in connection with such carnival or exhibition. Such insurance shall be in the minimum amount of two hundred fifty thousand dollars ($250,000.00) for each person, and five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000.00) for each accident.
(Code 1974, § 32-207(4))
Sec. 13-134. - Inspections.
It shall be the duty of the chief of police to see that proper inspections and patrols are made of the premises used for the activities licensed herein.
(Code 1974, § 32-207(5))
