Chapter 31 - DEPARTMENTS, COMMISSIONS AND AUTHORITIES
DIVISION 1. - GENERAL PROVISIONS

Sec. 31.01 - Clay County Development Authority.
(A)
There is created a body of corporate and politic in Clay County to be known as the Clay County Development Authority, hereinafter referred to as the Authority, which shall be an instrumentality of Clay County and a public corporation.
(B)
The Authority shall consist of not less than seven nor more than nine members. Members of the Authority shall be appointed by a majority of the Clay County Board of Commissioners. Four shall be appointed for initial terms of two years and the remaining members shall be appointed for initial terms of four years and until their successors are appointed and qualified; thereafter, their successors shall be appointed for terms of four years and until their successors are appointed and qualified. A majority of the members shall constitute a quorum and a majority may act for the Authority in any manner. No vacancy shall impair the power of the Authority to act.
(C)
The property, obligations and the interest on the obligations of the Authority shall have the same immunity from taxation as the property, obligations and interest on the obligations of Clay County. The exemption from taxation herein provided shall not include the exemption from sales and use taxes on property purchased by the Authority or for use by the Authority.
(D)
The powers of the Authority shall include, but not limited to, the power:
(1)
To buy, acquire, develop, improve, own, operate, maintain, sell, lease as lessor and lessee and mortgage land, buildings and property of all kinds within the limits of Clay County;
(2)
To receive and administer gifts, grants and donations and to administer trusts;
(3)
To grant, loan and lease without adequate consideration or without any consideration and with and without security any of its funds and property to private persons and corporations operating or promising to operate any industrial plant or establishment within Clay County which in the judgment of the Authority will be a benefit to the people of the county. The provisions of this clause shall not be construed to limit any other power of the Authority;
(4)
To borrow money, to issue notes, bonds and revenue certificates therefore, to execute trust agreements or indentures and to sell, convey, mortgage, pledge and assign any and all of its funds, property and income as security therefore;
(5)
To contract with Clay County and other political subdivisions of the State of Georgia and with private persons and corporations and to sue and be sued in its corporate name;
(6)
To have and exercise usual powers of private corporation except such as are inconsistent with this section, including the power to appoint and hire officers, agents and employees and to provide their compensation and duties, which officers and agents may or may not be members of the Authority and the power to adopt and amend a corporate seal and bylaws and regulations for the conduct and management of the Authority;
(7)
To encourage and promote the expansion and development of industry, agriculture, trade and commerce and the facilities therefore in Clay County and to make long-range plans therefore, to relieve insofar as possible unemployment within its boundaries and to that end to acquire by purchase or gift any building or structure within the limits of Clay County suitable for and intended for use as a retirement facility, factory, mill shop, processing plant, assembly plant or fabricating plant, including all necessary and appurtenant lands and appurtenances thereto and all necessary or useful furnishings, machinery and equipment. The acquisition may be through the acquisition of land and the construction thereon of a building, including the demolition of existing structures, or through the acquisition of an existing building or building under construction and the remodeling, renovating, reconstructing, completing of construction, furnishing and equipping of such building. No building acquired hereunder shall be operated by the Authority but shall be leased or sold to one or more persons, firms or corporations. If sold, the purchase price may be paid at one time or in installments falling due in not more than 30 years from the date of transfer of possession. The lessee or purchaser shall be required to pay all costs of operating and maintaining the building or buildings and to pay rentals or installments sufficient, together with other revenues which may be pledged for the propose, to retire all bonds, both principal and interest, and to pay all other expenses which the Authority may have incurred in connection with the undertaking;
(8)
To accumulate its funds from year to year and to invest accumulated funds in any manner that public funds of the State of Georgia or any of its political subdivisions may be invested;
(9)
To designate officers to sign and act for the Authority generally or in any specific matter;
(10)
To do any and all acts and things necessary or convenient to accomplish the purpose and powers of the Authority as herein stated.
(E)
The Authority shall not be authorized to create in any manner any debt, liability or obligation against the State of Georgia or Clay County.
(F)
The Authority shall have the same immunity and exemption from liability for torts and negligence as the State of Georgia and the officers, agents and employees of the Authority when in performance of the work of the Authority, shall have the same immunity and exemption from liability for torts and negligence as the officers, agents and employees of the State of Georgia when in performance of their public duties or work of the state. The Authority may be sued the same as private corporations on any contractual obligation of the Authority.
(G)
The members of the Authority shall receive no compensation for their services of the Authority.
(H)
In addition to the purpose for which revenue bonds are now permitted to be issued, the Authority, with the consent of the Board of Commissioners of Clay County, to carry out public purposes is authorized to issue revenue bonds bearing rate or rates of interest not to exceed 8% and maturing at the years and amount determined by the Authority and the procedure of validation, issuance and delivery shall be in all respects in accordance with the Revenue Bond Law (Ga. L. 1937, p. 761), as heretofore or hereafter amended Ga. Code Ann. Supp. Ch. 87-8, as if said obligations had been originally authorized to be issued there under, provided, however, that any property, real or personal of the Authority may be pledged, mortgaged, conveyed, assigned, hypothecated or otherwise encumbered as security for any lawful debt of the Authority. The Authority may execute any trust agreement or indenture not in conflict with the provisions of this section to provide security for any bonds issued as provided herein, and such trust agreement or indenture may provide for foreclosure or forced sale of any property of the Authority upon default on such bonds, either in payment of principle or interest or under any term or conditions under which such bonds are issued.
(I)
The property of the Authority shall not be subject to levy and sale under legal process except such property, income or funds as may be pledged, assigned, mortgaged or conveyed to secure an obligation of the Authority and any such property, funds or income may be sold under legal process or under any power granted by the Authority to enforce payment of the obligation.
(J)
This section is adopted for the purpose of promoting and expanding for the public good and welfare of Clay County and its citizens, industry, agriculture and trade within the County of Clay and making long range plans for such development and expansion and this section and any law enacted with reference to the Authority shall be liberally construed for the accomplishment of this purpose.
(K)
This section shall be effective immediately and the first members of the Authority shall be appointed within 30 days.
(L)
The Clay County Board of Commissioners may further define and prescribe the powers and duties of the Authority and exercise thereof and may enlarge and restrict the same and may likewise further regulate the management and conduct of the Authority. The Authority shall be an instrumentality of Clay County, and the scope of its operations shall be limited to the territory embraced within Clay County.
(M)
There shall be no limitations upon the amount of debt which the Authority may incur, but no debt created by the Authority shall be a debt of Clay County or the state.
(N)
In the event any section, subsection, sentence, clause or phrase of this section shall be declared or adjudged invalid or unconstitutional, such adjudication shall in no manner affect the other divisions, subsections, sentences, clauses or phrases of this section, which shall remain in full force and effect, as if the division, subsection, sentence, clause or phrase so declared or adjudged invalid or unconstitutional was not originally a part hereof.
(O)
A copy of this section shall be filed with the Secretary of State.
(Res. of 6-20-2000)
Sec. 31.02 - Economic Development Council.
(A)
Name and purpose.
(1)
Name. The name of this organization shall be Clay County Economic Development Council.
(2)
Purpose. This Council is organized as a nonprofit organization for the purpose of correlating and strengthening the economic conditions of Clay County and improving the well-being of its citizens by coordinated and harmonious development in the county.
(B)
Membership.
(1)
Members. The Clay County Economic Development Council shall consist of ten members. Members shall be appointed by the Clay County Board of Commissioners upon the recommendation of the EDC. Membership should, to the greatest extent possible, include representation by one or more persons from:
(a)
Clay County Board of Commissioners.
(b)
City Council of the City of Fort Gaines.
(c)
Clay County School System.
(d)
Clay County/Fort Gaines Development Authority.
(e)
The medical community.
(f)
The agricultural community.
(g)
Local businesses.
(h)
The county at large by individuals who have a vital interest in the economic growth and development of Clay County.
The term of the members shall be four years. Vacancies in membership shall be filled by the appointing authority with appointments being made in September for membership beginning October 1. The Economic Development Council shall make recommendations to the appointing authority for candidates to fill such vacancies except for representatives of the City of Fort Gaines, Clay County School System, and Clay County/Fort Gaines Development Authority. Recommendations of candidates for representation of the City of Fort Gaines, Clay County School System, and the Clay County/Fort Gaines Development Authority will be made by the represented body. Members representing the City of Fort Gaines, Clay County School System, Clay County/Fort Gaines Development Authority, the medical community and the agricultural community will be (re)appointed during the even years divisible by four. Members representing the Clay County Board of Commissioners, local business and the county at large will be (re)appointed in even years not evenly divided by four.
(2)
Attendance. If a member is absent from three regular monthly meetings of the Council during a 12-month period, that member shall be removed from the Council. The Chairman shall notify the appointing authority and request that a new appointment be made. Absences for health or business conflicts shall not be counted.
(3)
Meetings. This Council shall meet at 12:30 p.m. the first Monday of each month at a location designated by the Chairman. Special meetings may be called as necessary. The Council may change the time and place of meetings with prior notification to all members and the appointing authority.
(4)
Voting rights. Each member shall have the right of one vote on matters pertaining to the business of this Council.
(5)
Quorum. A quorum shall be one-half of the membership, or five members, whichever is greater. A quorum shall be present at any regular or special meeting in order to transact business.
(6)
Proxies. No member shall be entitled to vote by proxy for another member.
(7)
Voting. Each member shall be entitled to one vote upon each matter submitted to a vote at a meeting. A majority vote by the members present must be received for a matter to be passed. Votes may be received by fax or e-mail from members and shall be addressed to the Secretary. These voting members shall be counted toward a quorum. All elections shall be by ballot unless waived by the unanimous consent of those members present.
(C)
Officers.
(1)
Number. The officers of this Council shall be a Chairman, a Vice-Chairman, Secretary, and Treasurer, each of whom shall be elected by the members.
(2)
Election of officers. Officers will be elected at the regular monthly meeting in September of each year. The Chairman will appoint a nominating committee at the regular monthly meeting in August. The nominating committee will submit nominations for each office at the September meeting. Other nominations may be received from the members present. The term of office shall be October 1 through September 30. In the event an officer can not fulfill their term of office for any reason, a special election will be held to fill the unexpired term.
(3)
Qualifications. The officers shall be members of this Council in good standing.
(4)
Chairman. The Chairman shall be chief executive officer of this Council. He/She shall preside at all meetings of the members, and shall have general charge of the business of this Council, subject to the approval and control of the members.
(5)
Vice-Chairman. The Vice-Chairman shall, in the absence of the Chairman, exercise the powers and perform the duties of the Chairman; otherwise he/she shall have such powers and perform such duties as may be assigned by the Chairman.
(6)
Secretary. The Secretary shall keep minutes of the meetings of the members, shall attend to the giving of all required notices, and shall perform all duties incident to the office of Secretary.
(7)
Treasurer. The Treasurer shall keep a full and accurate account of the receipts and disbursements; deposit all money, checks and other obligations to the credit of this Council in such depository of depositories as may be designated by the members; disburse the funds of this Council as ordered, taking proper vouchers for such disbursements; render a statement of account and transactions whenever required; make a complete annual statement in September of each year at the regular meeting; and generally perform all the duties incident to the position of Treasurer. The Treasurer's books shall be audited annually in conjunction with the audit of the Clay County Board of Commissioners.
(D)
Miscellaneous provisions.
(1)
Fiscal year. The fiscal year of this Council shall begin on the first day of October and terminate on the last day of September in each year.
(2)
Order of business. The order of business at all meetings, insofar as possible, shall be as follows:
(a)
Call to order.
(b)
Roll call.
(c)
Reading of minutes.
(d)
Report of Treasurer.
(e)
Report of committees.
(f)
Unfinished business.
(g)
New business.
(h)
Election and appointments.
(i)
Special programs.
(j)
Adjournment.
(3)
Conduct of business. To insure a coordinated and harmonious effort by the Council, no member shall conduct business in the name of the Council without Council approval.
(4)
Committees. The Chairman shall appoint all standing and special committees. The Chairman and Secretary shall be members, ex-officio, thereof.
(5)
Contributions. This Council may receive, at the discretion of its members, voluntary contributions in the support of its program of work and activities, and for special purposes.
(6)
Investments of funds. Investment of Council funds, within the limitations of law, may be authorized, from time to time by the members.
(7)
Remuneration. Members and officers shall serve without remuneration as such, but may be reimbursed at the discretion of the members to cover necessary expense of travel and subsistence when serving this Council.
(E)
Amendment of by-laws. Recommendations that these by-laws be amended, repealed or altered may be made at any regular meeting of the members of this Council, or at any special meeting of such members called for that purpose, by the affirmative vote of a majority of the members present in quorum, due notice of such meeting having been given, provided such proposed amendment shall be included in the notice of any such special meeting. All amendments to these by-laws must be approved by the Clay County Board of Commissioners.
(Res. of 2-5-1985; Res. No. 08-013, arts. I—V, 1-15-2008)
Sec. 31.03 - Recreational Department and Recreation Commission.
(A)
Under the provisions of the Georgia Recreation Enabling Law, approved February 1, 1946, (Ga. L. 152), and amended in 1955, 1964 and 1971, there is hereby established a City of Fort Gaines-Clay County Recreation Department. This Department shall be governed by a commission established and known as the City of Fort Gaines-Clay County Recreation Commission.
(B)
The City of Fort Gaines-Clay County Recreation Commission shall consist of seven members, three members to be approved by the City Council of the City of Fort Gaines, three members to be approved by the Clay County Board of Commissioners and the last member to be selected by the members of the Recreation Commission themselves. This Commission shall serve without pay.
(C)
The term of office for member's of the City of Fort Gaines-Clay County Recreation Commission shall be for five years or until their successors are appointed and qualified, except that the members first appointed shall be appointed for such terms so that the term of at least one member shall expire annually thereafter. Vacancies occurring other than by expiration of term shall be filled by another appointment by the members of the City of Fort Gaines-Clay County Recreation Commission subject to approval by the City Council or County Commission, depending upon the original appointment of the vacant seat.
(D)
Upon appointment, the City of Fort Gaines-Clay County Recreation Commission shall meet and organize by electing one of its own as Chairperson and such other officers as may be necessary. The Commission shall have power to adopt by-laws and rules and regulations for the proper conduct of recreation for the city and county subject to approval of the city and county.
(E)
The City of Fort Games-Clay County Recreation Commission shall provide, conduct and supervise public playgrounds, playfields, indoor recreation centers and other recreation facilities owned or controlled by the city and the county. It shall have the power to conduct any form of recreation or cultural activity that will employ the leisure time of the people in a wholesome and constructive manner. It may conduct such activities on properties under its own control, on public properties with the consent of the authorities thereof and on private properties with consent of the owners.
(F)
The City of Fort Gaines-Clay County Recreation Commission shall have the power to appoint or designate someone to act as Recreation Director who is trained or properly qualified for the work and such other personnel as the Commission deems proper.
(G)
Annually, the City of Fort Gaines-Clay County Recreation Commission shall submit a budget to the City Council and County Commission for approval. The amount of funding submitted annually by the city and the county shall be negotiated annually and contractually agreed upon by both the city and county. In addition to the funds appropriated by the City Council and County Commission, the Recreation Commission may also solicit or receive any gifts or bequests of money, other personal or real property or any donation to be applied, principal or income, for either temporary or permanent use, for playgrounds or other recreational purposes. However, if the acceptance of any gift or bequest for such purposes shall subject the City Council and/or the County Commission to liability or additional expense for improvements, maintenance or renewal, the acceptance of any grant or devise of any property shall be subject to the approval of the City Council and the County Commission.
(H)
All monies received by appropriation or otherwise shall be deposited to the account of the City of Fort Gaines-Clay County Recreation Commission. The City of Fort Gaines-Clay County Recreation Commission shall make an annual report to the City Council and the County Commission at the conclusion of each budget year and such other reports as deemed necessary from time to time.
(Res. No. 99-02, 3-9-1999)
DIVISION 2. - CLAY COUNTY PLANNING AND ZONING COMMISSION[1]

Sec. 31.20 - Membership.
(A)
(1) The Clay County Planning and Zoning Commission shall consist of seven members as follows:
(a)
Three of the members shall be residents of the City of Fort Gaines appointed by the Fort Gaines City Council; and
(b)
Three of the members shall be residents of Clay County appointed by the Clay County Board of Commissioners.
(c)
The seventh member shall be mutually approved by the Clay County Board of Commissioners and the Fort Gaines City Council and shall serve as the member at large.
(d)
The Clay County Extension Director shall serve as an advisor to the Commission.
(2)
The terms of the members shall be for four years, except that in the appointment of the first Clay County Planning and Zoning Commission under the terms of this section one of the county-appointed members shall be appointed for a term of two years and two for terms of four years.
(B)
Any vacancy in membership shall be filled for the unexpired term by the governing authority making the appointment which has become vacant. The appointing authority shall also have the power to remove any member it may appoint for cause, on written charges, after public hearing. All appointees must reside in and represent the area served by the appointing local government. Inability to serve, or failure to serve the appointive position for a period not in excess of 60 days, may result in the reappointment of an individual capable of maintaining the responsibilities of the office. All members shall serve without compensation.
(Res. of 5-5-1987; Res. of 4-3-2007)
Sec. 31.21 - Organization, rules, staff, finances.
(A)
The Commission shall elect a Chairperson from among its appointive members. The term of the Chairperson shall be for one year with eligibility for re-election. However, the Chairperson shall not be reelected to more than three concurrent terms of office. The Planning Commission shall appoint a Secretary who may be an officer or employee of the municipality or the county.
(B)
The Commission shall meet as deemed necessary. The Commission shall adopt a set of by-laws; however, these by-laws must be approved by the Clay County Commission and the Fort Gaines City Council. All meetings of the Commission at which official action is taken shall be open to the public, and all records of the Commission shall be public record.
(C)
The Commission may appoint such employees and staff as it may deem necessary for its work and may contract with State Planning Agency and city planners and other consultants for such services as it may require. If these employees, staff or contracts with other agencies require financial expenditures, they must first be approved by the Clay County Board of Commissioners and the Fort Gaines City Council. The expenditures of the Commission, exclusive of gifts to the Commission, shall be within the amounts appropriated for the purpose by the governing authority of the city and county.
(Res. of 5-5-1987)
Sec. 31.22 - Powers and duties.
It shall be the function and duty of the Commission to make such careful and comprehensive surveys and studies, or participate with agencies in making said surveys and studies, of existing conditions and probable future development and to prepare such plans for physical, social and economic growth as will best promote the public welfare and prosperity, as well as the efficiency and economy in the development of the entire Clay County, Georgia area.
(Res. of 5-5-1987)
