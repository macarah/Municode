ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 62-1. - Disorderly conduct.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person within the corporate limits of the city to:
(1)
Attempt to commit a violent injury to the person of another.
(2)
Commit an act which places another in reasonable apprehension of immediately receiving a violent injury.
(3)
Intentionally make physical contact of an insulting or provoking nature with the person of another.
(4)
Intentionally cause physical harm to another.
(5)
Intentionally damage any property of another without consent.
(6)
Without provocation, uses to another person or in such other person's presence, opprobrious or abusive words which by their very utterance tend to incite to an immediate breach of the peace, that is to say, words which as a matter of common knowledge and under ordinary circumstances will, when used to another person or in such other person's presence, naturally tend to provoke violent resentment, that is, words commonly called "fighting words".
(7)
Without provocation, uses obscene and vulgar or profane language in a public gathering, school or school function, athletic or sporting event, public meeting, publicly owned or operated building, or in the presence of a person under the age of 14.
(8)
Cause bodily harm to or endanger the bodily safety of another by consciously disregarding a substantial and unjustified risk that the act or omission will cause harm or endanger the safety of the other person and the disregard constitutes a gross deviation from the standard of care which a reasonable person would exercise in the situation.
(9)
Willfully obstruct, hinder, or oppose an employee of the State of Georgia or any political subdivision thereof, including but not limited to the City of Monroe, in the lawful discharge of their official duties.
(10)
Reserved.
(11)
Willfully obstruct, hinder or oppose an emergency medical technician, nurse, or doctor in the lawful discharge of their official duties. This section, however, does not abridge a citizen's right to legally refuse medical treatment.
(12)
Congregate with another or others in or on any public way so as to or significantly impede the flow of vehicular or pedestrian traffic and refuse to clear such public way when requested by a law enforcement officer or other city employee.
(13)
While in a gathering, refuse to obey the reasonable official request or order of a peace officer, firefighter, or emergency medical technician to move for the purpose of promoting the public safety by dispersing those gathered in dangerous proximity to a fire, accident, emergency, or violent situation.
(b)
Any person found guilty of disorderly conduct shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 and applicable state surcharges, or by confinement in the county or other jail or probationary period not to exceed six months.
(Code 1988, § 11-1-3; Ord. No. 2000-05, § 11-1-3, 7-27-2000; Ord. No. 2005-05, 8-2-2005)
Sec. 62-2. - Covering wells required.
Any person who shall allow any old well on his premises to remain uncovered after receiving notice by the chief of police or code officer to cover the same, shall, upon conviction, be punished as prescribed by section 1-11.
(Code 1988, § 11-1-5)
Sec. 62-3. - Possession of marijuana.
It shall be unlawful for any person to possess one ounce or less of marijuana within the corporate limits of the city. Any person found guilty of violating this section shall be subject to the penalty as provided in section 1-11; provided, that any defendant charged with possession of one ounce or less of marijuana shall be entitled on request to have the case against such defendant transferred to the court having general misdemeanor jurisdiction in the county wherein the alleged offense occurred.
(Code 1988, § 11-1-8; Ord. No. 2021-01 , art. I, 3-9-2021)
Sec. 62-4. - Loitering.
(a)
Loitering shall mean remaining idle in essentially one location and shall include the concepts of spending time idly, loafing, walking about aimlessly, or sitting on cars, trucks, or other vehicles, and shall include the colloquial expression "hanging around". It shall be unlawful for any person to loiter within the city on private or public property in such a manner as to:
(1)
Create or cause to be created a danger of a breach of the peace;
(2)
Create or cause to be created any disturbance or annoyance to the comfort and repose of any person;
(3)
Obstruct the free passage of pedestrians or vehicles;
(4)
Obstruct, molest or interfere with any person lawfully in any public place;
(5)
Be at a place, at a time or in a manner, not usual for law-abiding individuals under circumstances which would warrant a justifiable and reasonable alarm or immediate concern for the safety of persons or property within the vicinity; or
(6)
Be at a place in which the owner or lawful occupant of said premises has posted a "no loitering" sign and when such signs are clearly visible.
(b)
Relocating from one position to another and regrouping or generally hanging around shall be prima facie evidence of intent to continue an unlawful act under this section and shall constitute a new offense after having been dispersed or cited for an offense under these provisions.
(Code 1988, § 11-1-11; Ord. No. 2005-06, 8-2-2005)
Sec. 62-5. - Discharge of weapons in city.
It shall be unlawful for any person to discharge a firearm, including pistol, rifle and shotgun, or to shoot an air gun, including BB gun and pellet gun, within the city, except by law enforcement officers in the line of duty, and the military when on drill or parade, in theatrical productions, at a funeral in honor of the dead, or in a commercial indoor firing range; provided that nuisance birds or squirrels may be shot within the city with the prior written permission of the chief of police.
(Code 1988, § 11-1-16; Ord. No. 2010-01, art. I, 6-8-2010)
Sec. 62-6. - Criminal trespass by motor vehicle.
The provisions of O.C.G.A. § 16-7-19 are adopted and incorporated by reference as the ordinance of city governing criminal trespass by motor vehicle, and subsection (a) thereof is hereby made a part of this code, together with the penal provisions contained in subparagraph (c) thereof with reference to fines in subsection (c)(1), (2), and (3).
(Code 1988, § 11-1-17)
Cross reference— Traffic and vehicles, ch. 94.
Sec. 62-7. - Disruption of lawful meeting.
It shall be unlawful for anyone to recklessly or knowingly commit any act which may reasonably be expected to prevent or disrupt a lawful meeting, gathering, or procession either by loud taking, indecent or profane language, or creating any disturbance by any sound or noise whatsoever.
Sec. 62-8. - Loud music or noise from vehicle.
(a)
It is unlawful for any person operating or occupying a motor vehicle on a street, roadway, drive, or highway to operate or amplify the sound produced by a radio, tape player, or other mechanical sound-making device or instrument from within the motor vehicle so that the sound is plainly audible at a distance of 100 feet or more from the motor vehicle.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person operating or occupying a stopped or parked motor vehicle in a parking lot, driveway, or yard to operate or amplify the sound produced by a radio, tape player, or other mechanical sound-making device or instrument from within the motor vehicle so that the sound is plainly audible at a distance of 100 feet or more from the motor vehicle.
(c)
In the event the offense occurs within a residential area, historic district, within 100 feet of a hospital, or within 100 feet of a school zone while school is in session, the judge of the municipal court may, at his discretion, increase the applicable fine and assess all associated state surcharges. For the purposes of this code section, a residential area is a defined as a block or portion of the street, drive, highway or roadway where residential structures comprise more than 50 percent of the structures along that block or portion of the street.
(d)
In the event the offense occurs between the hours of 11:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m., the judge of the municipal court may, at his discretion, increase the applicable fine and assess all associated state surcharges.
(e)
The provisions of this section shall not apply to any law enforcement motor vehicle equipped with any communication device necessary in the performance of law enforcement duties or to any emergency vehicle equipped with any communication device necessary in the performance of any emergency procedures.
(f)
The provisions of this section do not apply to motor vehicles used for government, business or political purposes, which in the normal course of conducting such business use sound-making devices between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 7:00 p.m.
(g)
The provisions of this section do not apply to the noise made by a horn or other warning device required or permitted by the O.C.G.A. § 40-8-70 when such device is used in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 40-7-70.
(h)
Penalties for violation: first offense $150.00 plus applicable state surcharges; second violation for the same offense, within 365 days of the first violation, $300.00 plus applicable state surcharges; third and subsequent offense, within 365 days of the second violation, $500.00 plus applicable state surcharges, or by confinement in the county jail or other jail or probation not to exceed 60 days.
(Ord. No. 2000-06, § 10-1-10, 7-27-2000)
Cross reference— Traffic and vehicles, ch. 94.
Sec. 62-9. - Maintaining standards of neighborhood; maintaining standards of business establishments; prohibition of keeping junk, abandoned appliances, inoperative vehicles, and other equipment on property.
(a)
In order to eliminate unsightly, unhealthy or dangerous situations, and in order to protect property values and to enhance the beauty of the city, it shall be unlawful for any person to own, rent, lease or be in possession of any premises, dwelling, dwelling unit or other structure, place or vacant lot wherein are kept any dilapidated furniture, icebox, refrigerator, stove or other appliance, machinery, equipment, building material, or parts thereof, or other item which is either wholly or partially rusted, wrecked, junked, dismantled, discarded, or inoperative and which is left out-of-doors in an unenclosed structure or so located upon the premises as to be readily visible from any public place or from any surrounding private property.
(b)
An inoperative or junk condition shall include, but not be limited to, any automobile, vehicle, trailer of any kind or type, or contrivance or part thereof, the condition of which is one or more of the following:
(1)
Wrecked.
(2)
Dismantled.
(3)
Partially dismantled.
(4)
Inoperative.
(5)
Abandoned.
(6)
Discarded.
(7)
Does not have a valid license plate attached thereto and is otherwise in bad repair.
(c)
No automobile, vehicle or trailer of any kind or type, which shall be in an inoperative or junk condition, shall be parked or stand on any residentially zoned property or other zoned property unless:
(1)
It shall be in an enclosed building.
(2)
It shall be on the premises of a business enterprise operated in a lawful manner, and necessary to the operation of such business enterprise; however, the automobile, vehicle or trailer shall be kept in an enclosed building or fenced area out of sight.
(3)
It shall be on property occupied and used for repair, reconditioning or remodeling of vehicles in conformance with the zoning ordinance, in which case the automobile, vehicle or trailer may remain on the property for a maximum of two months provided it is enclosed in a building or fenced out of sight.
(d)
No automobile, vehicle or trailer of any kind or type, which shall be in an inoperative or junk condition, shall be parked on any city right-of-way.
(e)
Should the code enforcement officer or any officer of the city authorized to issue citations determine that any person is in violation of this section, he shall give the offending party 15 days notice to eliminate the unsightly, unhealthy, or dangerous situation. This notice shall be in writing and shall be a prerequisite to bringing charges against an offender.
Should the person fail to comply with the request made therein, within the 15-day period, the code enforcement officer or any officer of the city authorized to issue citations may proceed with formal charges as for the violation of any city ordinance. Each day the unsightly, unhealthy, or dangerous situation exists thereafter shall be deemed a separate offense. Upon the municipal court accepting a plea of guilty or finding the person guilty penalties of section 1-11 shall apply and additionally the court may issue an "order to comply".
(f)
An order to comply may be issued by the municipal court to a person who fails to eliminate unsightly, unhealthy, or dangerous situations within the notice given by the code enforcement officer. The order to comply may give the guilty person a period of time to eliminate the situation. If the person charged does not comply within the period of time ordered the property listed in the order shall be deemed abandoned and subject to removal by city officials.
If the code enforcement officer or any officer of the city authorized to issue citations finds the property to have value, he shall have it moved to a storage site where it shall be disposed of in the same manner as city surplus property. If he finds the property to have no value, he shall dispose of the items listed in the order in a safe manner to eliminate the unsightly, unhealthy, or dangerous situation.
(g)
Should the code enforcement officer or any officer of the city authorized to issue citations determine that any person is in violation of this section for a subsequent violation occurring within a 12-month period since the time of the prior violation, 15 days' notice to correct the subsequent violation shall not be required as under subsection (e). The code enforcement officer or any officer of the city authorized to issue citations may proceed immediately with formal charges for the subsequent violation occurring within a 12-month period from the time of the prior violation without the requisite 15 days' notice period to correct the violation.
(Ord. No. 2000-08, art. I, § 5-2-9, 11-7-2000; Ord. No. 2016-08 , art. I, 11-8-2016)
Sec. 62-10. - Unhealthy and unsanitary conditions.
(a)
Prohibition. To protect the health and property of persons in the city and to prevent conditions which might lead to fire or the breeding of reptiles, rodents, and insects, it shall be unlawful for the owner or occupant of any property in the city to allow garbage, trash, refuse, rubbish, stagnant water, filth, carrion, weeds, debris, vines, grass or other vegetation to accumulate thereon.
(b)
Notice to remove. When the code enforcement officer determines that conditions as described in subsection (a) exist in the city, he shall notify the owner and the occupant of the premises of such conditions. Such notice shall be in writing and shall advise the addressee that he has five days within which he must eliminate such condition and shall advise such persons of prosecution under this chapter and correction by the city at the addressee's expense.
(c)
Failure to correct within time specified. If the owner or occupant of the property fails to act in accordance with the notice given him, the city may proceed to charge him with a violation of this chapter and in addition thereto, the city may, at its option, proceed to remedy the situation and accomplish the necessary work. The owner or occupant or both shall be liable to the city for the actual costs of such work.
(d)
Should the code enforcement officer or any officer of the city authorized to issue citations determine that any person is in violation of this section for a subsequent violation occurring within a 12-month period since the time of the prior violation, five days' notice to correct the subsequent violation shall not be required as under subsection (b). The code enforcement officer or any officer of the city authorized to issue citations may proceed immediately with formal charges for the subsequent violation occurring within a 12-month period from the time of the prior violation without the requisite five days' notice period to correct the violation.
(Code 1988, § 5-2-10; Ord. No. 2016-09 , art. I, 11-8-2016)

Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 36-32-10.1, the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 16-7-21 are adopted and incorporated by reference as the ordinance of the city governing criminal trespass and are hereby made a part of this Code, together with the penal provisions contained therein.
(Ord. No. 2005-07, 8-2-2005)

ARTICLE II. - CURFEW OF MINORS

Sec. 62-41. - Imposed.
It shall be unlawful for any child under the age of 18 years to loiter, idle, wander, stroll or play in or upon the public streets, highways, roads, alleys, parks, playgrounds, or other public grounds, public places or public buildings, places of amusement or entertainment, eating places, vacant lot or any other place normally accessible to the general public for public use, whether on foot or in a motor vehicle or by any other means, during the following periods of time:
(1)
For children age 15 and under, between the hours of 10:30 p.m. on any day and 6:00 a.m. of the following day; and
(2)
For children age 16 and 17, between the hours of 12:00 midnight on any day and 6:00 a.m. of the following day.
(Code 1988, § 11-1-7)
Sec. 62-42. - Exceptions.
The provisions of section 62-41 shall not apply when a child is:
(1)
Accompanied by his parent, guardian or other adult person having the lawful care and custody of the child;
(2)
Upon an emergency errand directed by his parent or guardian or other adult person having the lawful care and custody of a child;
(3)
Returning directly home by the most direct and efficient route, from a school activity, entertainment, recreational activity or dance;
(4)
Returning directly home, by the most direct and efficient route, from lawful employment that makes it necessary to be in the places described in section 62-41 during the prescribed period of time;
(5)
On the sidewalk abutting a child's residence or abutting the residence of a next door neighbor if the neighbor did not complain to the police officer about such child's presence;
(6)
Attending or traveling directly to or from an activity involving the exercise of first amendment rights of free speech, freedom of assembly or free exercise of religion; and
(7)
In interstate travel through the city.
Sec. 62-43. - Parental responsibility.
Except in circumstances set out in section 62-42, it shall be unlawful for the parent, guardian or other adult person having the care and custody of a child under the age of 18 years to permit, whether knowingly or through ineffective control or supervision, such child to loiter, idle, wander, stroll or play in or upon the public streets, highways, roads, alleys, parks, playgrounds or other public grounds, public places or public buildings, places of amusement or entertainment, eating places, vacant lots or any other place normally accessible to the general public for public use, whether on foot or in a motor vehicle or by any other means, during the following periods of time:
(1)
For children age 15 and under, between the hours of 10:30 p.m. on any day and 6:00 a.m. of the following day; and
(2)
For children age 16 and 17, between the hours of 12:00 midnight on any day and 6:00 a.m. of the following day.
Sec. 62-44. - Notice of violation.
Any child violating the provisions of this article shall be dealt with in accordance with juvenile court law and procedure. Any police officer finding a child under the age of 18 years violating the provisions of this article shall warn the child to desist from such violation and to immediately return home and shall cause a written notice to be served upon the parent, guardian or person in charge of a child, setting forth the manner in which the provisions of this section have been violated. For the purposes of this article, notice shall be deemed properly served upon the parent, guardian or person in charge of a child if a copy of the notice is served upon him personally or if a copy is sent by certified mail, return receipt requested, to his last known address.
Sec. 62-45. - Penalty for violation of article.
Any parent, guardian, or person having the care and custody of a child who shall permit, whether knowingly or through ineffective control or supervision, a child to violate the provisions of this article after receiving written notice that such child has previously violated this article, shall be subject to punishment as provided in section 1-11.

ARTICLE III. - SEXUALLY EXPLICIT MATERIAL

Sec. 62-66. - Purpose.
The mayor and city council find that the display, loan, rental, sale, dissemination and distribution of certain video movies to minors, and the resulting effect of such on the children of this community, have become a matter of increasingly grave concern to the citizens of the city. The reasonable restriction of such display, loan, rental, sale, dissemination and distribution of certain video movies is in the best interest of the morals, general welfare and good order of the citizens, and especially the children, of the city. The accomplishment of the ends can be achieved by placing reasonable restriction on the display, loan, rental, sale, dissemination and distribution of certain video movies to minors, so that adverse impacts of such video movies on children and the community as a whole may be kept to a minimum.
Sec. 62-67. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Certain video movies means any video movie with an X or XXX rating, as designated by the Classification and Rating Administration of the Motion Picture Association of America.
Commercial establishment means any place of business in which minors are permitted.
Minor means any person under 18 years of age.
Sexually explicit means any behavior that depicts:
(1)
Stimulation of human genitals or exposure of male or female genitalia;
(2)
Acts of human masturbation, sexual intercourse, sodomy, bestiality, buggery, cunnilingus, fellatio, pederasty, homosexuality, sadomasochism, or similar acts; or
(3)
Fondling or other erotic touching of human genitals, pubic regions or female breasts.
Sexually explicit cover means any cover, container, or package which depicts:
(1)
Stimulation of human genitals, or otherwise emphasizing the genitals;
(2)
Acts of human masturbation, sexual intercourse, sodomy, bestiality, buggery, cunnilingus, fellatio, pederasty, homosexuality, sadomasochism, or similar acts; or
(3)
Fondling or other erotic touching of human genitals, pubic regions or female breasts.
Video movie means a videotape, videocassette, video disc, DVD, any prerecorded video display or visual depiction, any prerecorded device that can be converted to a visual depiction, or other reproduction of a motion picture.
X-rated video movie means any video movie with an X or XXX rating as designated by the Classification and Rating Administration of the Motion Picture Association of America.
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 62-68. - X-rated and XXX-rated video movies.
(a)
The display, loan, rental and sale of X-rated and XXX-rated video movies shall be as provided in this subsection (a):
(1)
Display. X-rated or XXX-rated video movies may be displayed in a commercial establishment only in an area set aside and clearly posted for adults only, and minors shall not be allowed entry into such areas. Such areas shall be visible from the cash register or sales center of the commercial establishment. No video movies frequently rented, loaned, or sold to children under 12 years of age shall be located in the vicinity of or adjacent to an adults only area, and any video movie with a sexually explicit cover shall be displayed in such a manner as to not be readily visible to patrons in other areas of the commercial establishment.
(2)
Rental and sale. The rental, loan, sale, dissemination or distribution of X-rated or XXX-rated video movies for monetary consideration to minors is expressly and strictly prohibited.
(b)
The owner of any commercial establishment not engaged in part or in whole in the rental and/or sale of video movies to the general public, together with his employees and agents, shall have the right to require proof of age from any patron so as to ensure compliance with this section.
(c)
Notwithstanding any other section of this Code, no criminal penalty is provided for the violation of this section. The business license of any person may be suspended or revoked by the mayor and city council for violation of any part of this section. Such suspension or revocation shall be only after at least five days notice and an opportunity for hearing afforded to the license holder.
Sec. 62-69. - Display of sexually explicit video in a motor vehicle.
It shall be unlawful for anyone to view or display a sexually explicit video in a motor vehicle, whether the vehicle is moving or stopped, upon private or public property, when the video is available for viewing by any person other than the occupants of the vehicle.
Sec. 62-70. - Same—Penalty.
For the first conviction the offender shall be punished as provided in section 1-11 or otherwise as may be provided by law. For a second or subsequent conviction the offender may be required by the court to remove the video viewing devices from the vehicle.
