ARTICLE VII. - POOLROOMS OR BILLIARD ROOMS[6]

State Law reference— Local governments power to license, regulate, and tax billiard rooms, O.C.G.A. § 43-8-2.

Sec. 12-232. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcoholic beverage, beer and malt beverage, fortified wine, wine and distilled spirits or spirituous liquor as defined in section 6-1 of chapter 6, pertaining to alcoholic beverages, which definitions are incorporated in this article by reference.
Billiard room means any public place where a person is permitted to play the game of billiards and for which a charge is made or any form of consideration is required or tendered for use of equipment. (O.C.G.A. § 43-8-1)
Billiards or pool means any of the several games played on a table surrounded by an elastic ledge of cushions with balls which are impelled by a cue and includes all forms of the game known as "carom billiards," "pocket billiards," and "English billiards." (O.C.G.A. § 43-8-1)
(Ord. No. 2012-3, § 12-1, 7-3-2012)
Sec. 12-233. - Sanitation requirements.
All public billiard rooms shall be kept in a clean, sanitary condition and shall comply with all sanitary rules and regulations of the state, the county, and the city.
(Ord. No. 2012-3, § 12-2, 7-3-2012)
Sec. 12-234. - Use of obstructions and enclosures.
No person who operates a billiard room in the city shall permit any screens, curtains, blinds, partitions or other obstructions forming rooms, stalls or other enclosures. However, this subsection shall not be construed to prohibit the maintenance of washrooms and toilet rooms for proper purposes or the maintenance of closets for storage purposes exclusively.
(Ord. No. 2012-3, § 12-3, 7-3-2012)
Sec. 12-235. - Hours of operation.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a billiard room within the city between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 8:00 a.m. or to permit any person to remain in such billiard room between such prohibited hours except regular employees performing necessary labor on the premises.
(Ord. No. 2012-3, § 12-4, 7-3-2012)
Sec. 12-236. - Admission of persons under 18 years of age to billiard rooms in which alcoholic beverages are sold.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person under the age of 18 years to play billiards in, or for any other purpose to enter or remain in, a billiard room in which beer, wine, malt beverage, distilled spirits, spirituous liquor, fortified wine, or alcoholic beverages are offered for sale. This subsection shall not apply to persons under 18 years of age who are accompanied by a parent or guardian. However, no minor shall be allowed in billiard rooms between the hours of 12:00 midnight and 8:00 a.m.
(b)
In the event that the proprietor of a billiard room is of the opinion that any person desiring admission thereto is under the age of 18 years, he shall require such person to certify his age by producing a legal permit/identification.
(Ord. No. 2012-3, § 12-5, 7-3-2012)
Sec. 12-237. - Posting of placard as to admission of minors.
Every licensee under this chapter shall post in the room where billiard tables are operated a placard with the language of section 12-236 conspicuously written thereon for the information of the patrons.
(Ord. No. 2012-3, § 12-6, 7-3-2012)
Sec. 12-238. - Gambling; posting of sporting results; sale or use of intoxicating liquors/alcoholic beverages.
(a)
No dice, cards, dominoes, or other games of chance shall be permitted, nor any form of gambling allowed, in any billiard room or in any other business place operated in connection therewith; and such games as Kelly pool, keno, pigeon pool, Parker pool, star pool, scrub, and similar gambling devices are prohibited. No racing or other betting pool shall be exhibited or sold in such place of business. The use of baseball tickers and the posting results of sporting events are expressly prohibited in billiard rooms or in any place operated in connection therewith.
(b)
Billiard room operators that are licensed to serve malt beverages, beer, wine, fortified wine, distilled spirits, spirituous liquor, and/or alcoholic beverages shall be subject to all ordinances and restrictions of the state, the city, and/or the county.
(c)
No alcoholic beverages, malt beverage, beer, wine, fortified wine, distilled spirits, and/or spirituous liquor shall be sold, served, or allowed to be used in or on the premises of billiard rooms or any place operated in connection therewith, unless such premises or establishment is authorized to sell alcoholic beverages, malt beverage, beer, wine, fortified wine, distilled spirits and/or spirituous liquor.
(Ord. No. 2012-3, § 12-7, 7-3-2012)
Sec. 12-239. - Applicability of article to billiard tables and billiard room operated by religious orders, charitable institutions and clubs.
This article shall not apply to billiard tables or billiard rooms operated by private industrial concerns or associations, religious orders, charitable institutions, state, county, or city institutions, fraternal orders, or bona fide clubs using such tables for members or employees only.
(Ord. No. 2012-3, § 12-8, 7-3-2012)
Sec. 12-240. - Penalty.
(a)
Any person who has not reached the age of 18 who remains in a billiard room in the city or within prohibited hours or without being accompanied by a parent or guardian in accordance with section 12-236, who makes a false certificate as to age shall be in violation of this article.
(b)
Any licensee, person, firm or corporation who shall knowingly violate any of the provisions of this article shall be guilty of an offense. Each violation thereof shall constitute a separate offense and shall be punishable as such. All violations shall be heard and adjudicated in the municipal court of the city and any final decision of the municipal court of the city may be appealed in writing to the superior court of the county by certiorari within 30 days from the date of any final decision.
(Ord. No. 2012-3, § 12-9, 7-3-2012)
