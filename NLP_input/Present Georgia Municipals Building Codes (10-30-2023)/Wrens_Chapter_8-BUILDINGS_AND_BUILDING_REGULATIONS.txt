ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 8-1. - Office of building inspector created.
(a)
There is hereby created and established the office of building inspector who shall be appointed by the mayor and council.
(b)
The building inspector shall serve at the pleasure of the mayor and council and shall have the responsibility for the enforcement of the standard codes adopted by this chapter and any subsequent amendments thereto and shall perform other duties as may be prescribed by the mayor and council.
(Code 2002, § 6-2-1)
Sec. 8-2. - Right of entry.
The building inspector or his designee in the discharge of his official duties, and upon proper identification, shall have authority to enter any building, structure or premises at any reasonable hour for the purpose of conducting inspections pursuant to this chapter.
(Code 2002, § 6-2-2)
Sec. 8-3. - Applicability of provisions to home owners.
Nothing contained within this chapter shall prevent any home owner from installing or maintaining electrical wiring or plumbing, gas piping or gas appliances within his own property boundaries, provided the electrical, plumbing or gas work is done by himself and is used exclusively by him or his family. This privilege does not convey the right to violate any of the provisions of this chapter; nor is it to be construed as exempting any property owner from obtaining a permit and having the work inspected or from paying the required fees therefore.
(Code 2002, §§ 6-2-22, 6-2-42)
Sec. 8-4. - Permits.
Before any construction, alteration, use, demolition and removal of buildings or other structures, or any appurtenances connected or attached thereto; any electrical wiring, devices or equipment are installed, repaired or altered in any building or structure within the city; any installation or repair of gas work; any installation of all plumbing, including the pipes for distributing the water supply, the fixtures for using water and the drainage pipe for removing waste water and sewage, together with fittings and appurtenances of various kinds, all within or adjacent to the buildings within the city or where connected to the city's water or sewage system, and the whole thereof, the person or company making the installation, repair or alteration shall obtain a permit from the building inspector.
(Code 2002, § 6-2-23)
Sec. 8-5. - Permit fees.
On all buildings, structures, electrical, plumbing, mechanical, and gas systems or alterations requiring a permit, a fee for each permit shall be paid as required at the time of filing application, in accordance with the fee schedules as determined by the mayor and council from time to time.
(Code 2002, § 6-2-24)
Sec. 8-6. - Inspections.
It shall be unlawful for any person to conceal or cause to be concealed, in such manner that the inspector cannot satisfy himself that the provisions of this chapter have been complied with, any work until after the work has been inspected and approved by the inspector.
(Code 2002, § 6-2-25)
Secs. 8-7—8-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - BUILDING CODES

Sec. 8-31. - Adoption.
(a)
The state minimum standard codes enumerated in or promulgated pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 8-2-20(9)(B)(i)(I) through (VIII), as now or hereafter modified or altered pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 8-2-20(9)(B)(ii), are adopted.
(b)
In addition, the following code is adopted: The Uniform Act for the Application of Building and Fire Related Codes to Existing Buildings promulgated pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 8-2-200 through 8-2-222.
(Ord. No. 01-006-2010, § 1.0, 6-8-2010)
Sec. 8-32. - Administration; enforcement official.
The administration of the codes adopted in section 8-31 will be accomplished in accordance with the administration chapter of each code. Wherever in any of the above-stated codes reference is made to the powers, authorities or duties of certain officials named therein, the designated official of the city who has the powers, authorities or duties corresponding to those of the official named in said code shall be deemed to be the official named in said code with said powers, authorities or duties.
(Ord. No. 01-006-2010, § 2.0, 6-8-2010)
Sec. 8-33. - Conflicting provisions.
Any matters in the codes adopted in this article which are contrary to existing ordinances of the city shall prevail over such ordinances to the extent of such conflict.
(Ord. No. 01-006-2010, § 4.0, 6-8-2010)
