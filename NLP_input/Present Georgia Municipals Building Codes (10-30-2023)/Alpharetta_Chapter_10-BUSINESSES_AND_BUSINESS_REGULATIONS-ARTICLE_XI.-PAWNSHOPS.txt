Sec. 10-336. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Employee means any person working for an owner, or pawnbroker, or any owner or, who, in the performance of his duties or the management of the business affairs of a pawnshop, comes into substantial contact with members of the public, or is employed on a part-time or full-time basis, either with or without remuneration, by a pawnshop.
Pawn or pledge means a pledge of personal property as security for any debt or loan, redeemable upon certain terms and with the express or implied power of sale on default.
Pawnbroker means any person, whether an owner or not, who works in a pawnshop on a regular basis and in a managerial capacity whereby he has charge of the business or operations of the pawnshop. The term "pawnbroker" also means any person engaged in whole or in part in the business of lending money on the security of pledged goods, or in the business of purchasing tangible personal property on the condition that it may be redeemed or repurchased by the seller for a fixed price within a fixed period of time, or in the business of purchasing tangible personal property from persons or sources other than manufacturers or licensed dealers as a part of or in conjunction with the business activities described in this definition.
Pawnshop means any business wherein a substantial part thereof is to take or receive, by way of pledge, pawn or exchange, any goods, wares, merchandise or any kind of personal property, as security for the repayment of money lent thereon.
(Code 1989, § 12-71; Ord. No. 273, § 1, 3-26-1990)
Sec. 10-337. - Licensing requirements.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to conduct or transact a pawnbroker business or pawnshop without first obtaining a city license. The fee for such license shall be $150.00 annually. Licenses are not transferable.
(b)
No license shall be issued to any person who has been convicted of a felony or a crime involving moral turpitude as defined by the laws of the state unless a full and complete pardon has been granted.
(c)
No license shall be issued to any person under the terms of this chapter if any false information is furnished upon the application by the applicant to the appropriate authorities of the city.
(d)
No license shall be issued to any person who has been convicted of usury under the laws of this state or of any other state.
(e)
No license shall be issued until the applicant has been investigated by the city police department, and a favorable report is received from the police department as to his general reputation in the business community.
(f)
No license shall be issued to any person who is not a resident of this county, or who does not maintain a registered agent in the county.
(g)
No license shall be issued to any person who does not maintain a blanket bond in an amount not less than $10,000.00. Such bond must be made payable to the city.
(h)
No license shall be issued unless the names and addresses of all persons holding any interest in the business are listed and those persons have signed the application.
(i)
No license shall be issued to any person who is engaged in any finance or lending business.
(Code 1989, § 12-72; Ord. No. 273, § 2, 3-26-1990)
Sec. 10-338. - Entry and examination by police.
(a)
Upon making application for and receiving a license from the city to operate as a pawnbroker, the holder of the license expressly authorizes the city police department to enter upon his premises and to inspect and examine any articles in the business for the purpose of determining whether or not they have been stolen or otherwise illegally obtained by the applicant or the pawnor, and the applicant expressly agrees that no search warrant shall be necessary, but consents to such examination and inspection at any time upon request by any police officer.
(b)
Prior to any police officer removing an article which is suspected to be stolen or lost, the officer will provide the pawnbroker with:
(1)
The name of the jurisdiction taking custody of the item; and
(2)
The case number associated with the item.
(c)
Any item removed shall be tagged with:
(1)
The case number; and
(2)
The name of the pawnbroker from which the item was taken.
(Code 1989, § 12-73; Ord. No. 273, § 3, 3-26-1990)
Sec. 10-339. - Records.
(a)
Each pawnbroker licensed hereunder shall furnish to the director of public safety each week a list of every item sold to or not previously pledged to such pawnbroker. Such list may be a copy of the information required to be kept by O.C.G.A. § 44-12-132, or any other list maintained containing the same information as follows:
(1)
The date of the transaction;
(2)
The name of the person conducting the transaction;
(3)
The name, age and address of the customer; a description of the general appearance of the customer; and the distinctive number from the customer's driver's license with a picture of the license holder;
(4)
An identification and description of the pledged or purchased goods, including, if reasonably available, the serial, model, or other number, and all identifying marks inscribed thereon;
(5)
The number of the receipt or pawn ticket;
(6)
The price paid or the amount loaned;
(7)
If payment is made by check, the number of the check issued for the purchase price or loan;
(8)
The maturity date of the transaction; and
(9)
The signature of the customer.
(b)
Entries shall appear in chronological order, in ink. No blank lines may be left between entries. No obliterations, alterations, or erasures may be made. Corrections shall be made by drawing a line of ink through the entry without destroying its legibility. The book shall be open to the inspection of any duly authorized law enforcement officer during the ordinary hours of business or at any reasonable time.
(Code 1989, § 12-74; Ord. No. 273, § 4, 3-26-1990)

Sec. 10-340. - Thumbprints of pledger or seller.
Any pawnbroker who shall receive as a pledge or purchase any item, goods or wares valued in excess of $10.00 or any revolver, pistol, shotgun, rifle or any other firearm, shall be required to take a thumbprint of such person pledging or selling any of the above items. Such thumbprints must be kept by the pawnbroker as part of the records required by the state, and for the period of time required therein.
(Code 1989, § 12-75; Ord. No. 273, § 5, 3-26-1990)
Sec. 10-341. - Dealing with minors.
No pawnbroker shall have any business dealings as a pawnbroker with a minor, nor shall a pawnbroker's license be issued to a minor, nor shall a pawnbroker employ a minor to assist such pawnbroker in the business. Nothing shall prevent the employment of minors who are otherwise qualified for employment, provided such minors shall not be allowed to take items in pawn or loan money on pledged items.
(Code 1989, § 12-76; Ord. No. 273, § 6, 3-26-1990)
Sec. 10-342. - Stolen goods.
(a)
It shall be the duty of every pawnbroker to report to the police any article pledged with such pawnbroker or which is sought to be pledged with such pawnbroker, if such pawnbroker shall have reason to believe the article was stolen, or lost and found by the person attempting to pledge it, in the case of a lost article. No pawnbroker shall accept any item, goods or wares upon which a manufacturer's identity or serial number has been intentionally removed, obliterated, or in any way caused to become unreadable.
(b)
No pawnbroker shall accept any handgun upon which the manufacturer's identity or serial number has been removed, obliterated or in any way become unreadable.
(Code 1989, § 12-77; Ord. No. 273, § 7, 3-26-1990)
Sec. 10-343. - Certain purchased articles to be held 30 days.
Any pawnbroker or person operating under a pawnbroker's license who purchases goods, other than those purchased through wholesalers or on order in the normal course of business, taking full title thereto (the term "goods" being used in the broadest sense and including all kinds of personal property) shall hold such goods purchased for at least 30 days before disposing of same by sale, transfer, shipment or otherwise.
(Code 1989, § 12-78; Ord. No. 273, § 8, 3-26-1990)
Sec. 10-344. - Suspension or revocation of license.
(a)
Any license issued under this article may be suspended or revoked for any of the following reasons:
(1)
Fraud or misrepresentation in the application for a license;
(2)
Fraud or misrepresentation in the course of conducting business;
(3)
Failure to adhere to the recordkeeping or reporting requirements of sections 10-339 and 10-342;
(4)
Violation of any provision of this article;
(5)
Violation of any provision of O.C.G.A. §§ 44-12-130 through 44-12-137 pertaining to the regulation of pawnbrokers.
(b)
Upon suspension or revocation, the city shall deliver written notice to the license holder stating the action taken and the reason supporting such action. The written notice shall be delivered to the address set forth in the license holder's application.
(c)
The holder of a pawnbroker license shall be responsible for any and all acts of his employees, and for any violation by them of the provisions of this article.
(Code 1989, § 12-79; Ord. No. 273, § 9, 3-26-1990)
Sec. 10-345. - Appeals.
Pawnbrokers who are denied licenses or whose licenses have been suspended or revoked may appeal such denial, suspension or revocation by filing a written notice of appeal with the city clerk. The appeal must be filed within ten days of the date of the notice of denial, suspension or revocation. The city council shall hear and determine the appeal and the decision of the council shall be final.
(Code 1989, § 12-80; Ord. No. 273, § 10, 3-26-1990)
Sec. 10-346. - Penalties.
In addition to the suspension and revocation penalties provided in section 10-344, any pawnbroker or employee who violates any provision of this article shall be punished according to the penalties described in section 1-8. Each day any violation of any provision of this article shall continue shall constitute a separate offense.
(Code 1989, § 12-81; Ord. No. 273, § 11, 3-26-1990)
