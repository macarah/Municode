Chapter 20 - FLOODS

Sec. 20-1. - Appointments.
(a)
The county planning and zoning commission is vested with the responsibility, authority, and means to:
(1)
Delineate or assist the administrator at his request, in delineating the limits of the areas having special flood hazards on available local maps of sufficient scale to identify the location of building sites.
(2)
Provide such information as the administrator may request concerning present uses and occupancy of the floodplain.
(3)
Cooperate with federal, state and local agencies and private firms which undertake to study, survey, map, and identify floodplain or mud slide areas, and cooperate with neighboring communities with respect to management of adjoining floodplain and/or mud slide areas in order to prevent the aggravation of existing hazards.
(4)
Submit on the anniversary date of the community's initial eligibility an annual report to the administrator on the progress made during the past year within the community in the development and implementation of floodplain management measures.
(b)
The county planning and zoning commission appoints the chief tax appraiser of the county to maintain for public inspection and to furnish upon request a record of elevation of the lowest floor of all new or substantially improved structures located in the special flood hazard areas. If the lowest floor is below grade on one or more sides, the elevation of the floor immediately above must also be recorded.
(Res. of 9-3-1975, §§ 1—4)
Sec. 20-2. - Building inspector and zoning board of appeals appointed.
The building and zoning inspector is designated as the administrator of the flood plan. The board of zoning appeals is designated to hear and decide appeals under the flood regulations.
(Res. of 3-20-1984)
