ARTICLE XVIII. - MUSCOGEE COUNTY SCHOOL DISTRICT

Sec. 20-18.1. - Applicability of article.
The provisions of this article shall be applicable, within the city to all grounds owned or held by Muscogee County School District.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 19, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-18.2. - Definition.
The word "vehicle" as used herein shall include both vehicles propelled by both motor power and by muscular power.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 19, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-18.3. - Article cumulative.
All pertinent provisions of this chapter and other traffic ordinances of the city not inconsistent with the provisions hereof shall be applicable to such grounds of such school district.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 19, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-18.4. - Speed limit.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a vehicle on such roadways at a speed greater than is reasonable and safe, which speed shall in no case exceed 15 miles per hour.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 19, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-18.5. - Roadways-using; required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to drive, operate, park, rank or stop a vehicle on such grounds except on the roadways or in spaces set aside for such purposes.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 19, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-18.6. - When permitted.
It shall be unlawful for any person to drive, park, rank or stop a vehicle on such grounds or the roadways therein unless such person is transporting pupils or is on other business connected with school officials, teachers or employees of such schools or where the school authorities permit the use of such grounds or the buildings thereon to be used for other than school purposes, such as lectures, concerts or other cultural purposes.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 19, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-18.7. - Noises; prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to disturb in any manner, any of the schools of such school district by the making of noise or otherwise.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 19, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-18.8. - Signs-obeying.
Where on any of such grounds there is erected a sign reading "Exit", it shall be unlawful for a person to drive a vehicle into such grounds at such point; and, where there is erected on any of such grounds a sign reading "Entrance", it shall be unlawful for any person to drive a vehicle from such grounds at such point; provided, however, in emergencies police officers may direct traffic contrary to the provisions of this section.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 19, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-18.9. - Erecting signs.
The authorities of such school district shall erect on such grounds signs setting forth the place and manner of parking or ranking and the speed limit on such grounds, the places for entrance and exit, and any other signs appropriate to the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 19, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-18.10. - Damaging signs.
It shall be unlawful for any person to remove, deface, damage or interfere with any sign placed on such grounds pursuant to the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 19, 7-20-71)
Sec. 20-18.11. - Maximum loads for school buses.
It shall be unlawful for any person to drive or operate a school bus with a load limit for such school bus in excess of 20 percent above the manufacturer's rated seating capacity of said bus.
(Ord. No. 71-263, §§ 1, 2, 10-12-71; Ord. No. 71-265, §§ 1, 2, 10-19-71)
