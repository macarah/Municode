DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 10-110. - Definitions.
For purposes of this chapter, the following terms shall have the meanings respectfully ascribed to them in this section:
(1)
Building rubbish. Waste building materials resulting from construction, remodeling, repair or demolition operations.
(2)
Bulky waste. Automobile parts, furniture, and other waste materials other than building rubbish, dead animals or hazardous waste, with weights or volumes greater than seventy-five (75) pounds per loading.
(3)
Combustible materials. All flammable waste including, but not limited to, oily rags or oily waste, empty boxes, barrels, flammable liquids, or other like combustible materials.
(4)
Dead animals. Animals or portions thereof equal to or greater than twenty (20) pounds in weight that have died from any cause, except those slaughtered or killed for human consumption.
(5)
Dry-cleaning waste. Inflammable waste generated by dry-cleaning operations.
(6)
Garbage. Waste paper and plastics, normal household disposable items, any or all dead animals of less than twenty (20) pounds in weight except for those slaughtered for human consumption; every accumulation of waste (animal, vegetable and/or other matter) that results from the preparation, processing, consumption, dealing in, handling, packing, canning, storage, transportation, decay or decomposition of meats, fish, fowl, birds, fruits, grains or other animal or vegetable matter including, but not limited to, used tin cans and other food containers; and all putrescible or easily decomposable waste, animal, or vegetable matter.
(7)
Hazardous waste. Any chemical, compound, mixture, substance or article which is designated by the United States Environmental Protection Agency or an appropriate agency of the state to be "hazardous," as that term is defined by federal or state law or regulations.
(8)
Industrial waste. Waste generated by manufacturing or industrial processes or operations including, but not limited to, waste resulting from the following manufacturing processes: electric power generation; fertilizer and agricultural chemicals; food-related products and by-products; inorganic chemicals; iron and steel products; leather and leather products; nonferrous metal and foundry products; organic chemicals; plastics and resin; pulp and paper; rubber and miscellaneous plastic products; stone, glass, clay, and concrete products; textiles; transportation equipment; and water treatment.
(8.1)
Oversized container. Any container for the collection of garbage, recycling materials, trash, yard trimmings and other refuse which has a capacity of not less than ten (10) and not more than fifty (50) cubic yards.
(9)
Recycling materials. Any and all materials which could otherwise become solid waste which, if collected, separated, or processed are capable or being reused or returned to use in the form of raw materials or products. Recycling materials shall include, but are not limited to HDPE and PET plastic; clear, green and brown glass; old newsprint; aluminum and bi-metal cans; and office paper and related paper products.
(10)
Refuse. All ashes, garbage, recycling materials, trash, yard trimmings, or other waste accumulated within the city which is subject to collection by the city through the city collection and disposal system.
(11)
Solid waste. All building rubbish, concrete, rocks, earth, bulky waste, combustible materials, dead animals, dry-cleaning waste, garbage, recycling materials, hazardous waste, industrial waste, trash, yard trimmings, or other waste accumulated within the city which is not subject to collection by the city through the city collection and disposal system.
(12)
Trash. Lumber (excluding building rubbish), sticks, limbs, cans or one-gallon size or larger, discarded toys, and small household items (not including appliances over seventy-five (75) pounds in weight or other large or heavy items over seventy-five (75) pounds in weight), clothing, boxes, bundled paper and similar items. Putrescible food containers which contained such food should not be considered as trash. Dirt or heavy car parts shall not be considered as trash or hauled by the city. Rock and concrete will be hauled by the city from residential property when the work is done by the homeowner. Material from work done by a contractor shall be hauled away by the contractor.
(13)
Yard trimmings. Leaves, brush, grass clippings, shrub and tree limbs (but not including log sections over forty (40) pounds each), and prunings, discarded Christmas trees, nursery and greenhouse vegetation residuals, and vegetative matter resulting from landscape development and maintenance other than mining, agricultural, and silvicultural operations. Provided, however, that yard trimmings shall not include limbs or other materials generated by commercial gardeners, tree services, and the like.
(Code 1963, § 8-1(a), (b); Ord. No. 96-27, § 1, 10-7-96; Ord. No. 98-18, § 1, 9-8-98)
Sec. 10-110.1. - Collection by city required; permit required where service is not offered by city.
(a)
(1)
All garbage, recycling materials, and yard trimmings accumulated in the city shall be collected, conveyed and disposed of by the city or by the city's contractor except as otherwise expressly authorized in this chapter. Owners or, if applicable, non-owners, as that term is defined in section 10-132 of this chapter, of all unites to which the city provides collection and disposal services shall us the city collection and disposal system and shall pay such fees for said services as the city may establish from time to time.
(2)
No person shall collect, convey over any of the streets or alleys of the city or dispose of any refuse accumulated in the city, except as expressly authorized in this chapter or as allowed by pertinent and appropriate actions of the governing body.
(3)
For the purposes of this section, the term "Unit" shall mean a building, structure, or property within the city occupied by a person or group of persons or a business. A unit shall be deemed occupied when water services, either public or private, are being supplied thereto. Each unit of an apartment or condominium dwelling, whether of single-level or multi-level construction, shall be treated as a residential unit; provided, that any multi-family complex provided with dumpster service in lieu of approved containers for each housing unit shall be excluded from this definition.
(b)
To the extent that the city does not collect or provide for the collection of certain types of solid waste accumulated in the city through the city collection and disposal system, a person may obtain a non-exclusive permit from the city to provide such service and shall conduct such activities in full compliance with state laws and regulations and city ordinances regulating the collection, transportation or disposal of such waste, and, upon failure to do so, shall be subject to prosecution for such violations. No person shall collect, convey over any of the streets or alleys of the city, or dispose of any solid waste accumulated in the city, except as expressly authorized in this chapter or as allowed by pertinent actions of the governing body. The annual fee for a non-exclusive permit to collect, transport, or dispose of such solid waste in the city shall be two hundred dollars ($200.00) per year. In the event the city institutes service in any area in which permit holders operate, such permits shall not be renewed for a new period, and the rights of such collectors to continue collecting such items shall cease with expiration of the permit.
(c)
Owners and occupants of commercial, industrial, or residential property serviced by a private collector of solid waste shall maintain the areas immediately surrounding any exterior containers for such waste free of unsightly trash, garbage, or refuse at all times. In addition, any exterior container provided by a private collector of solid waste shall not reduce the number of available parking spaces at the property, nor shall any such container obstruct containers used in the collection of garbage, trash, recycling materials, or other refuse by the city. The owner or manager of the property shall be responsible for instructing all employees and occupants on the proper method for the use of the containers and shall ensure that all materials are placed completely within the container, with all doors and lids to be closed at all times the container is not being loaded or emptied.
(Ord. No. 96-27, § 2, 10-7-96; Ord. No. 98-18, § 2, 9-8-98; Ord. No. 2002-10, § 1, 3-4-02)
Sec. 10-110.2. - Containers required for residents, businesses and public buildings; placement at fence property; separating ashes.
(a)
All owners and occupants of commercial, residential, and industrial property within the city shall provide sufficient containers with tight-fitting covers of a capacity not greater than thirty-five (35) gallons, and shall place garbage and other refuse in same, not to exceed seventy-five (75) pounds loading per container. On all fenced property used to restrain animals, the containers shall be made readily accessible to the collection personnel without their entry into the fenced area. Containers shall be shielded from the sight of the street and shall be within the property building line. This regulation shall not apply to commercial and industrial property served by bulk receptacle boxes.
(b)
Ashes shall be placed in separate containers subject to the same size and weight limitations specified in subsection (a) and placed at the curb.
(c)
Paint cans may be collected by the city so long as the lid is removed, any remaining liquid has been absorbed through use of an absorbent material such as sawdust or fuller's earth, and the can is placed separately at curbside for collection.
(Ord. No. 96-27, § 3, 10-7-96)
Sec. 10-111. - Receptacles for cups, plates, etc.; keeping such business premises clean.
(a)
All business firms dispensing their product in cups, plates, wrappers, sacks, and other similar forms of containers shall provide adequate metal or plastic containers upon the premises for collection of refuse.
(b)
It shall be the express responsibility of all such business firms to collect all cups, plates, wrappers, sacks and other similar forms of containers dispensed by said business that may be discarded upon the premises or neighboring street and sidewalks. It further shall be the responsibility of said business to collect the aforementioned items from the premises of the neighboring property when the owners of the property specifically request and authorize the business personnel to enter upon their property for that purpose.
(c)
Failure to comply with the terms of this section shall be deemed a misdemeanor.
(d)
Any wilful and wanton violation of this section resulting in the unlawful littering of the streets, sidewalks and neighboring property shall be deemed a nuisance and on conviction of the defendant, the governing body may revoke the business license of the defendant.
(Code 1963, § 8-1(j))
Sec. 10-112. - Disposal of trash.
(a)
Trash shall be placed at the street edge behind the curb or where the curb would normally be, but shall not be placed in the street so as to obstruct the sidewalk or an area of public vehicular or pedestrian travel; nor shall it be placed in the gutter, in any ditch or other path of storm water runoff. Where exceptional problems or difficulties are encountered, trash shall be placed as directed by the public works director or the director's designated agent. Where this section is violated, the director of public works of the director's agent may give notice requiring that such trash be moved by the person placing such trash or the occupant or owner within twenty-four (24) hours, or immediately if deemed a sufficient hazard.
(b)
Small items of trans, such as clothes, paper, small household items, and items generally below one foot in length on their maximum dimension, or which can reasonably be bent or compressed to such length, but excluding all yard trimmings, shall be put in receptacle containers, not exceeding seventy-five (75) pounds in weight when full. Such container shall be of sufficient strength, wet or dry, not to burst or rupture when handled or emptied by city collection personnel. Cardboard boxes, when wet, shall not be deemed as acceptable containers. No plastic bags or sacks are to be used as containers for any trash.
(c)
Leaves, grass and pine straw may be placed at the edge of the curb, unbagged, provided that leaves and grass shall not be placed on the paved street surface, but shall be kept behind the curb line. The leaves and grass must be close enough to the curb to enable the city's leaf removal machine to reach them from the paves surface. Yard trimmings shall not be mixed with garbage, building rubbish, waste materials or other disposed materials. Should yard trimmings be mixed with other disposed materials, then the city may charge the resident or business operator for all separation and disposal costs or may refuse to haul the mixed waste until all yard trimmings have been separated and removed into individual piles for disposal.
(d)
Trash shall not be placed at the curb more than forty-eight (48) hours prior to the beginning of the day for which pick-up is assigned in an area. This time limit shall not apply for the placement of leaves at the curb during the months of October through April.
(e)
No item shall be placed in such a manner that it can blow off or wash into the street or away from the area of placement.
(f)
Trash shall be placed only on the owner's property or on the street right-of-way immediately adjacent to the property fro which the trash originated. Trash shall not be placed on vacant lots, parks, across the street from the property on which the trash originated or on another person's property.
(g)
Trash shall not be mixed, stacked, or placed so as to make pick-up or collection difficult or slow. For example, leaves or grass shall not be mixed with limbs, cans, sticks, or other items. Limbs shall not be crossed but shall be placed with the butt or cut end facing the street edge. In general, dissimilar items shall not be mixed so as to slow pick-up or collection.
(Code 1963, § 8-1(c); Ord. No. 92-11A, 5-18-92; Ord. No. 96-27, § 4, 10-76)
Sec. 10-113. - Disposal of old appliances and tires.
Any non-commercial resident who desires to have an old appliance or automobile tires removed from their property may contact the tax office at city hall to arrange for the city to remove the old appliance or automobile tires. Truck tires will not be picked up by the city. All items must be placed by the curb no more than one (1) day prior to the scheduled pick-up. Old or unused appliances or tires may not be placed or stored outside in the open on public or private property. The public works director or the director's agent or other appropriate official designated by the city manager may direct the removal of such appliance or tires at the property owner's expense within forty-eight (48) hours of notification or in a lesser time, if deemed a hazard.
(Code 1963, § 8-1(c); Ord. No. 2000-48, § 1, 12-18-00)
Cross reference— Abandoned iceboxes, etc., § 8-3.
Sec. 10-113.1. - Yard trimmings.
All yard trimmings shall be placed in neat piles in the area on the back side of the curb no more than forty-eight (48) hours prior to the city's scheduled collection. Limbs shall not be in excess of four (4) feet in length and three (3) inches in diameter. All limbs shall be secured in bundles not exceeding forty (40) pounds in total weight and may be placed at any time on the back side of the curb for disposal when the material has been cut, hauled and stacked by the residential property owner.
(Ord. No. 96-27, § 5, 10-7-96)
Sec. 10-113.2. - Items not collectible.
The following items are not collectible by the city:
(1)
Dirt or rocks;
(2)
Tires;
(3)
Batteries (mercury, lead-acid, rechargeable, etc.);
(4)
Hazardous waste (including, but not limited to, HTH, paint, oil, and car batteries);
(5)
Metal drums, thirty-five (35) gallon size or larger;
(6)
Dead animals, or portions thereof greater than twenty (20) pounds in weight;
(7)
All items weighing in excess of seventy-five (75) pounds;
(8)
Bulky waste;
(9)
Automobile parts; and
(10)
Pressurized containers and vessels.
(Ord. No. 96-27, § 6, 10-7-96)
Sec. 10-113.3. - Fee for removal of appliances and tires.
The fee for removal of appliances by the city under section 10-113 shall be seven dollars and fifty cents ($7.50) per appliance. The fee for removal of old tires under section 10-113 shall be one dollar and fifty cents ($1.50) for each tire. This fee must be paid in advance of the scheduled pick-up.
(Ord. No. 2000-48, § 2, 12-18-00)
Sec. 10-114. - Disposal of building rubbish.
No building materials or refuse from building, remodeling or repair operations or landscape work will be handled by city forces. All waste material of this nature shall be removed by the contractor, or in the event of the contractor's failure, by the owner of the property; provided, that the owner may make application to the sanitation superintendent and the superintendent may, if practical, remove the rubbish at the expense of the owner. The charge made for such service shall be determined in each case by the superintendent with relation to the service performed.
(Code 1963, § 8-1(d))
Sec. 10-115. - Disposal of industrial wastes.
Industrial wastes from factories, processing plants, and other manufacturing operations shall be collected, removed and disposed of by the operator of the enterprise.
(Code 1963, § 8-1(e))
Sec. 10-116. - Disposal of dead animals.
Dead animals, excluding horses and cows, will be picked up during working hours upon notice. It shall be the responsibility of the occupant to remove an animal from under any building.
(Code 1963, § 8-1(f))
Sec. 10-117. - Disposal of combustible materials—Generally.
(a)
Placement for garbage collectors. It shall be the duty of the occupant of any building within the city to collect and remove daily to a place convenient for city garbage collectors, empty boxes, barrels, rubbish, trash, waste paper, excelsior or other like combustible materials.
(b)
Ashes, wooden containers prohibited. No person shall place ashes within any building in any box, barrel, or other wooden vessel or upon any wooden vessel or floor.
(c)
Oily waste; metal containers required. No person shall keep or permit to be kept on the premises any oily rags or oily waste, unless at all times when not actually in use such oily rags and oily waste be kept in a metal can with self-closing cover and riveted joints, standing on metal legs which raise the bottom of the container at least four (4) inches above the floor.
(Code 1963, § 8-2)
Cross reference— Separate container required for ashes, § 10-110(b).
Sec. 10-118. - Same—Dry cleaning wastes.
Inflammable wastes from dry cleaning operations shall be deposited in metal containers of a capacity not larger than five (5) gallons, without covers, and placed on the outside of the establishment for collection.
(Code 1963, § 8-1(h))
Sec. 10-119. - Disposal of liquid wastes.
Liquid wastes other than normal garbage juices, shall not be combined with garbage and refuse, and the disposal of same shall be the responsibility of the occupant.
(Code 1963, § 8-1(g))
Sec. 10-120. - Disposal of other waste by city discretionary.
The sanitation superintendent may remove any waste materials for which no provision is made in this division, provided the cost that is determined by the superintendent as being reasonable for the service performed, is paid by the person making application for the removal of such waste material.
(Code 1963, § 8-1(i))
Sec. 10-121. - Frequency of collection.
(a)
Residential garbage and other refuse shall be collected twice per week.
(b)
Commercial garbage and other refuse shall be collected as required, as will yard trimmings and leaves.
(Code 1963, § 8-3)
Sec. 10-122. - Collection outside city limits.
No waste collections shall be made by the city forces outside the city limits.
(Code 1963, § 8-4)
Sec. 10-123. - Containerized garbage pick-up.
(a)
Containerized garbage pick-up may be required where any dwelling house, apartment building or other building or place of dwelling or any commercial establishment discards three (3) or more cubic yards of garbage in a consecutive seven (7)-day period. The city shall have the authority to require the use of containerized garbage pick-up where it deems necessary except where an existing situation is physically impossible to operate city equipment safely. The superintendent of sanitation or public works director shall negotiate the size of the container and the frequency of dumping same in each case, with a mutual understanding in writing with the owner of the property involved. All properties that are required to use containerized garbage pickup shall provide upon the premises an accessible hard-surfaced space of sufficient size to accommodate the required city garbage container. There shall be sufficient driving area to permit reasonable ingress and egress by city trucks. No building permit shall be issued by the building official for new construction unless appropriate plans and specifications are submitted setting forth and meeting the requirements of this section.
(b)
All existing properties shall have complied with this section on or before May 1, 1982.
(Code 1963, § 8-10)
Sec. 10-124. - Dumping in refuse containers regulated.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person other than a resident of the city or a representative of a business located within the city to dump garbage, trash, refuse or waste matter in containers placed about the city for the collection of garbage, trash, refuse or other waste matter of said residents and businesses.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to dump refuse, garbage, trash or any type of waste material in a container placed or located for the collection of refuse, garbage, trash or any other waste material when said container belongs to another person, unless the person dumping such refuse, garbage or waste shall have the written permission of the person or authorized representative of the person owning the container, paying for it or on whose property it is located.
(Code 1963, §§ 8-7, 8-9)
Secs. 10-125—10-129. - Reserved.

Sec. 10-132. - Solid waste disposal deposit.
(a)
For the purposes of this section, the following terms shall have the meanings assigned to them:
(1)
Actual monthly fee shall mean the monthly fee actually invoiced by the city to a non-owner for city solid waste disposal services.
(2)
Anticipated monthly fee shall mean the monthly fee anticipated by the city at the time of application for services by a non-owner for city solid waste disposal services.
(3)
Non-owner shall mean a person or entity applying under Article V of this chapter for city solid waste disposal services, which person or entity does not won the property to which the solid waste disposal services are to be provided.
(b)
Every owner and non-owner shall, at the time of application for services provide to the city a deposit in the amount of twice the anticipated monthly fee.
(c)
Every person or entity who receives city solid waste disposal services of the effective date of Ord. No. 2002-10 shall, within one hundred eighty (180) days of the effective date of this ordinance, provide to the city a deposit in the amount of twice the most recent monthly fee paid to the city by that person or entity for said city solid waste disposal services.
(d)
In the event that the initial actual monthly fee should exceed the anticipated monthly fee, or in the event of any increase in the actual monthly fee, the owner or non-owner shall remit to the city within thirty (30) days of receipt of an invoice reflecting such actual monthly fee or such increase in the actual monthly fee, sufficient funds to raise that owner's or non-owner's deposit amount to twice that owner's or non-owner's then current actual monthly fee.
(e)
In the event that an owner or non-owner fails to make any payment to the city required by this chapter, including but not limited to any payment required by subsection (c) of this section, the city shall deduct the amount of the non-payment and any other applicable penalties, fees and other charges from the deposit. The city, in such event, additionally may terminate the provision of services to the applicable property until such time as the owner or non-owner remits to the city all payment due, including sufficient funds to return the deposit to the appropriate amount.
(Ord. No. 2002-10, § 2, 3-4-02)
