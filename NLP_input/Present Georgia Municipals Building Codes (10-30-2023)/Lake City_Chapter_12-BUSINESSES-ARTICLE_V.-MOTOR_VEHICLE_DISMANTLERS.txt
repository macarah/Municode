ARTICLE V. - MOTOR VEHICLE DISMANTLERS

Sec. 12-128. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Antique vehicle means a motor vehicle manufactured no later than 1948.
Classic vehicle means a motor vehicle manufactured between the years 1925 and 1942.
Historic vehicle means a motor vehicle over 30 years of age and owned solely as a collector's item and for participation in club activities, exhibitions, tours, parades and similar uses, but which shall not be used for general transportation.
Junk vehicle means any motor vehicle, the condition of which is wrecked, dismantled, partially dismantled, inoperative or discarded, in such a manner as to create an unsightly condition or to be rendered unroadworthy according to standards established by O.C.G.A. § 40-8-1 et seq.
Motor vehicle dismantler means any person who, for commission or with the intent to make a profit or gain in money or other thing of value, engages in the business of acquiring, by purchase or other means, wrecked, abandoned or repairable motor vehicles and either removes the usable parts therefrom or allows others to remove the usable parts therefrom for subsequent sale.
Motor vehicle salvage dealer means any person who, for commission or with the intent to make a profit or gain in money or other thing of value, is engaged in the business of acquiring wrecked, abandoned or reparable motor vehicles for the purpose of reselling such vehicles as a unit. Motor vehicle salvage dealers do not remove or allow the removal of usable parts from such wrecked, abandoned or reparable motor vehicles, but sell, store or otherwise dispose of such vehicles in their entirety.
Used motor vehicle means any motor vehicle, other than a motor vehicle which has never previously been the subject of a retail sale by a new motor vehicle dealer, offered for sale by a used motor vehicle dealer to a consumer for such consumer's own use or for resale or to other resellers.
Used motor vehicle dealer means any person who, for commission or with the intent to make a profit or gain in money or other thing of value, sells, exchanges, rents with option to purchase, offers or attempts to negotiate a sale or exchange of an interest in used motor vehicles or who is engaged wholly or in part in the business of selling used motor vehicles, whether or not such motor vehicles are owned by such person. Used motor vehicle dealers includes those persons and excludes those persons included or excluded within the definition of used motor vehicle dealer in O.C.G.A. § 43-47-2(6)(A).
(Code 1994, § 22-111; Ord. No. 93-3, § 1, 6-21-1993)
Sec. 12-129. - Applicability.
Classic, antique and historic vehicles which meet the definition of junk vehicle shall be subject to all restrictions on junk vehicles enacted by the ordinance from which this article is derived, and no special exception to this article shall be established for those vehicles merely by their categorization as a classic, antique or historic vehicle.
(Code 1994, § 22-112; Ord. No. 93-3, § 2, 6-21-1993)
Sec. 12-130. - Inspection authority.
The chief of police or his agent shall have the right to enter onto premises licensed under this article, at reasonable times, to inspect the premises for compliance with this article.
(Code 1994, § 22-113; Ord. No. 93-3, § 10, 6-21-1993)
Sec. 12-131. - Penalties for violation.
(a)
Upon conviction or the entering of a plea of guilty or nolo contendere to any citation under this article, the person cited shall be subject to penalties set out in section 1-8.
(b)
In addition to the criminal penalties prescribed by subsection (a) of this section, upon a third conviction or plea of guilty or nolo contendere within a 12-month period, the business license of the license holder shall be suspended for a period of six months. A fourth conviction or a plea to a similar offense within 12 months shall result in forfeiture of the business license. No new business license shall issue to a person whose license has been forfeited for the same category of business within the city for a period of 12 months. Suspension or forfeiture of licenses shall be accomplished by action of the mayor and council following notice and opportunity to be heard.
(Code 1994, § 22-114; Ord. No. 93-3, § 11, 6-21-1993)
Sec. 12-132. - Business licenses.
All used motor vehicle dealers, motor vehicle dismantlers and motor vehicle salvage dealers operating within the city shall obtain a city business license and pay the appropriate license fees and taxes therefor as a condition of carrying on such business. No business license shall be issued to a used motor vehicle dealer unless the applicant presents proof of a current state license to operate as a used car dealer under O.C.G.A. § 43-47-1 et seq. No business license shall be issued to a motor vehicle dismantler unless the applicant presents proof of a current state license to operate as a dismantler under O.C.G.A. § 43-48-1 et seq.
(Code 1994, § 22-115; Ord. No. 93-3, § 3, 6-21-1993)
Sec. 12-133. - Open storage of used vehicles.
The open storage or display of used motor vehicles, other than junk vehicles, is permitted by used motor vehicle dealers, provided the location of such storage or display areas is limited to the BG General Business District or M Manufacturing District and otherwise complies with the zoning standards in chapter 42.
(Code 1994, § 22-116; Ord. No. 93-3, § 4, 6-21-1993)
Sec. 12-134. - Display of junk vehicles.
Junk vehicles may not be maintained on open lots or be located upon premises of a used motor vehicle dealer so as to be visible from any public right-of-way or from any adjacent private property. All junk vehicles shall be completely enclosed within a building or stored behind a solid opaque fence at least eight feet in height. Such fence shall meet the setback requirements of chapter 42, pertaining to zoning. The use of webbing in chainlink fences to meet the requirements of this section is specifically prohibited.
(Code 1994, § 22-117; Ord. No. 93-3, § 5, 6-21-1993)
Sec. 12-135. - Locations for motor vehicle salvage dealers and dismantlers.
Motor vehicle salvage dealers and motor vehicle dismantlers may be located only within the M Manufacturing District. All storage of vehicles and parts shall be either completely enclosed within a building or behind a solid opaque fence at least eight feet in height so as to screen the operations from any public right-of-way or adjacent private property. Usable parts removed by motor vehicle dismantlers shall be likewise stored within enclosed buildings or behind a solid opaque fence at least eight feet in height so that such parts are not readily visible from any public right-of-way or any adjacent private property.
(Code 1994, § 22-118; Ord. No. 93-3, § 6, 6-21-1993)
Sec. 12-136. - Restrictions on dismantlers.
A motor vehicle dismantler shall conduct his operations in strict compliance with the following standards:
(1)
All fluids shall be drained from every vehicle at the time the vehicle first appears on the dismantler's property. Drainage and storage of fluids shall be performed in compliance with all EPA and EPD rules.
(2)
Dismantling of parts from abandoned, wrecked or reparable motor vehicles shall be accomplished within ten days of the date such vehicles are first moved to the dismantler's facility.
(3)
When usable parts are removed from a vehicle, the hulk of the vehicle shall be removed from the dismantler's premises within 20 days.
(4)
A dismantler shall keep records showing the date each vehicle from which parts are taken was brought onto the dealer's premises, the date parts and fluids removal was completed and the date when the hulk of the vehicle was removed from those premises. Such records shall be open to inspection during reasonable business hours by officials of the city for the purpose of ensuring compliance with this article.
(Code 1994, § 22-119; Ord. No. 93-3, § 7, 6-21-1993)
Sec. 12-137. - Notice to remove.
(a)
Whenever the chief of police or his authorized agent determines that vehicles or dismantled parts are being maintained in violation of this article, he shall provide notice to the owner or operator of the business to correct such violation and to bring the premises into compliance with this article. Such notice shall be in writing, shall describe the offense and shall provide five days within which to comply with this article.
(b)
The notice of violation shall be served by one of the following methods:
(1)
Personal service;
(2)
Certified mail, return receipt requested; and
(3)
Where service has been attempted and cannot be accomplished either by personal service or by certified mail, the notice may be served by mailing a copy of the notice by first class mail, with adequate postage, to the last known address of the owner and by posting a copy of the notice on the business premises.
(Code 1994, § 22-120; Ord. No. 93-3, § 8, 6-21-1993)
Sec. 12-138. - Failure to comply with notice.
Upon receipt of a notice of violation pursuant to section 12-137 and upon failure of the owner or operator of those premises to bring the premises into compliance with this article within the period of time stated in the notice, the chief of police or his agent may proceed to cite the owner or operator of the business for violation of this article. Such notice of violation shall include a summons to appear in the municipal court on a date certain to answer the charge of violation of this article. Each and every vehicle maintained in violation of this article shall be considered as a separate offense, and each day beyond the five-day notice period during which such vehicle remains on the property shall also be considered a separate offense.
(Code 1994, § 22-121; Ord. No. 93-3, § 9, 6-21-1993)
