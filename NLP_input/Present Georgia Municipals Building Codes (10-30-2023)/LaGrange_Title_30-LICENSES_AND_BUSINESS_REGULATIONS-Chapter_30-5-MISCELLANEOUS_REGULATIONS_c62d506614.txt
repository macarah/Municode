Division 1 - GENERALLY

Sec. 30-5-146. - Definition.
For the purpose of this article, a transient merchant, itinerant merchant or itinerant vendor is defined as any person, firm or corporation or any combination thereof, whether as owner, agent, consignee or employee, and whether a resident of the City of LaGrange or not, that engages in any business of selling and delivering goods, wares and merchandise within the corporate limits of the City of LaGrange, and, in furtherance of such purpose, hires, leases, uses or occupies any premises other than a permanent building or structure, including but not limited to a motor vehicle, tent, railroad box car, boat, public room in hotels, lodging places, apartments or alley for the exhibition and sale of such goods, wares and merchandise, either privately or at public auction. There are excepted from and not included in this definition:
(1)
Any person occupying any such temporary location that does not sell or offer to sell from stock but merely exhibits samples only for the purpose of securing orders for future delivery; and
(2)
Any person selling or undertaking to sell agricultural products produced or grown by such person.
No transient merchant, itinerant merchant or itinerant vendor as defined herein shall be relieved from complying with the provisions of this article by reason of any temporary association with any licensed local dealer, trader, merchant or auctioneer, or by conducting such transient business on the premises of, in conjunction with, as a part of or in the name of any such local dealer, trader, merchant or auctioneer.
(Code 1972, § 15-173; Ord. No. 04-33, § 1, 9-28-04)
Sec. 30-5-147. - Findings.
The mayor and council hereby find, determine and declare that the unregulated and uncontrolled operation of businesses in the city by transient merchants, itinerant merchants or itinerant vendors as defined in this division is subject to abuse and is being abused and it is necessary for such business or businesses to be appropriately defined, licensed and regulated by the city.
(Code 1972, § 15-171)
Sec. 30-5-148. - Records of complaints, convictions, violations.
The assistant city manager, together with the police department and any other law enforcement agencies located in the city, shall keep and maintain a record of all complaints received against any person licensed under the provisions of this article and of any records for convictions or violations of this article.
(Code 1972, § 15-179; Ord. No. 07-12, § 7, 4-24-07; Ord. No. 16-12, § 8, 12-13-16)
Sec. 30-5-149. - Limit of operation on any one lot.
(a)
Except for those merchants exclusively selling produce, a transient merchant, itinerant merchant or itinerant vendor may remain and transact business upon any one (1) lot within the City of LaGrange for up to ten (10) consecutive days or consecutive portions of days. Once said transient merchant, itinerant merchant or itinerant vendor has ceased to transact business upon said lot, however, whether after one (1) or up to ten (10) days or portions of days, said merchant or vendor may not conduct business or otherwise remain thereon for sixty (60) days. Merchants participating in events on public property sanctioned and approved by the Downtown LaGrange Development Authority or the Troup County Parks and Recreation Commission shall be exempt from the durational requirements of this section.
(b)
A transient merchant, itinerant merchant or itinerant vendor selling produce may remain and transact business upon any one lot within the City of LaGrange for up to three (3) consecutive days or portions of days per week.
(Ord. No. 04-33, § 2, 9-28-04; Ord. No. 18-17, § 1, 12-20-18; Ord. No. 21-03, 2-9-21)

Division 2 - LICENSE

Sec. 30-5-161. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for a transient merchant, itinerant merchant or itinerant vendor as defined in this article to engage in or operate the business thereof within the city without first obtaining and maintaining in force a license therefor in compliance with the provisions of this article.
(Code 1972, § 15-172)
Sec. 30-5-162. - Application.
Applicants for license required by this article shall file a written application signed by the applicant with the assistant city manager, which application shall contain and show:
(1)
The name or names of the person or persons having management or supervision of the applicant's business during the time the same is proposed to be conducted or carried on in the city; the local address of such person or persons so engaged in the city; the permanent address of such person or persons; the capacity in which such person or persons will act (that is, whether as proprietor, agent or otherwise); and if a corporation, the state in which the same is incorporated, whether the corporation is domesticated or licensed to do business in the State of Georgia, the registered agent of the corporation in the State of Georgia and the designated agent for the service of process in the State of Georgia;
(2)
The place or places in the city where the applicant proposes to carry on the business described in its application; the hours in which the applicant proposes to keep said business open to the public; and the length of time during which the applicant proposes to conduct any such business;
(3)
The place or places, other than the permanent place of business of the applicant, where the applicant within the six-month period next preceding the date of such application conducted a transient business as described in this article, stating the nature of such business and giving the post office and street address of the location in which such business was conducted;
(4)
A statement as to the nature, character and quality of the goods, wares and merchandise to be sold; whether the same are proposed to be sold from stock in possession or by sample; and whether the same are to be sold by direct sale or by taking orders for future delivery only;
(5)
Credentials or other necessary written authorization from the person for whom the applicant proposes to do business, authorizing the applicant to act for and on behalf of such person as his representative; and
(6)
Such other reasonable information pertaining to the identity or character of the person or persons having the management or supervision of the applicant's business or the method or plan of doing such business that the assistant city manager or his agent may deem necessary or proper to fulfill the purpose of this article in the protection of the public good.
(Code 1972, § 15-174; Ord. No. 07-12, § 7, 4-24-07; Ord. No. 16-12, § 8, 12-13-16)
State Law reference— Foreign corporations, O.C.G.A. § 14-2-310 et seq.
Sec. 30-5-163. - Investigation; issuance.
Upon receipt of the application required by this division, the assistant city manager shall cause such investigation into the application and the purposes thereof as he may deem necessary for the protection of the public good and welfare. If the application shall be found to comply with the provisions of this article, a license shall be issued in accordance with the provisions thereof but a complete record of the application, the license and the issuance thereof shall be maintained by the assistant city manager as a part of the permanent records of the city.
(Code 1972, § 15-175; Ord. No. 07-12, § 7, 4-24-07; Ord. No. 16-12, § 8, 12-13-16)
Sec. 30-5-164. - Exhibition of license; separate place of business.
The license issued pursuant to this article shall be posted conspicuously in the place where the business so licensed is located and conducted. In the event that such person or persons applying for said license shall desire to do business in more than one (1) location within the city, separate licenses shall be required for each place of business and a license shall be posted conspicuously in each place of business.
(Code 1972, § 15-176)
Sec. 30-5-165. - Fees.
The applicant for a license under the provisions of this division shall pay at or prior to the time of application, a license fee as specified in section 30-1-24 of this title for each year and for each location for which a license is required in the city.
(Code 1972, § 15-177; Ord. No. 86-23, § 3, 6-26-86; Ord. No. 90-44, § 3, 9-11-90; Ord. No. 95-8, § 3, 3-14-95)
Sec. 30-5-166. - Transfer.
The license issued pursuant to the provisions of this division shall not be transferred or assigned except as authorized by the ordinances of the city.
(Code 1972, § 15-178)
Sec. 30-5-167. - Revocation.
(a)
The license issued pursuant to this article may be revoked by the mayor and council after reasonable notice to the licensee and an opportunity for a hearing thereon for any one (1) or more of the following reasons:
(1)
Fraud, misrepresentation or false statements contained in the application for license;
(2)
Fraud, misrepresentation or false statements made in conjunction with the sale of goods, wares or merchandise;
(3)
Violation of any of the provisions of this article;
(4)
Conviction of the licensee of any felony, or of any misdemeanor or crime involving moral turpitude; or
(5)
Conducting the business licensed pursuant to the provisions of this article in an unlawful manner, in a manner to constitute a public nuisance or in a manner to constitute a breach of peace or to constitute a menace to health, safety or the general welfare of the public.
(b)
Notice of hearing for revocation of a license shall be given in writing to the licensee, setting forth specifically the grounds of the complaint and the time, date and place of the hearing thereon. Such notice shall be served on the licensee by handing the same to the person in charge of the licensed business or by mailing the same postage prepaid to the licensee at his last known address or to the designated agent for the service of process or the registered agent of the licensee, at least five (5) days prior to the date set for the hearing.
(c)
Service of the notice shall suspend the license pending the outcome of the hearing, and any business operation conducted pursuant to the provisions of the license shall promptly cease and terminate.
(Code 1972, § 15-180)
