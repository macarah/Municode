State Law reference— Authority to so provide, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-372 et seq.
Sec. 70-2. - Off-road vehicles.
(a)
Definition. As used in this section, the term "off-road vehicle" means any motorized vehicle designed for or capable of cross-country travel on or immediately over land, water, snow, ice, marsh, swampland, or other natural terrain and not intended for use predominantly on public roads. It includes, but is not limited to, four-wheel drive or low-pressure-tire vehicles, two-wheeled vehicles, amphibious machines, ground-effect or air-cushion vehicles, and any other means of transportation deriving power from any source other than muscle or wind. However, such term shall exclude the following:
(1)
Any motorboat;
(2)
Any military, fire, or law enforcement vehicle;
(3)
Any vehicles used exclusively on airports;
(4)
All farm machinery, farm tractors, and other self-propelled equipment for harvesting and transportation of forest products, clearing of land for planting, utility, service and maintenance, and earth moving, construction, or mining; and
(5)
Self-propelled lawn mowers, snow blowers, garden or lawn tractors, or golf carts, while such vehicles are being used exclusively for their designed purposes.
(b)
Operating instructions. Any person operating an off-road vehicle under any of the following conditions shall be deemed to be in violation of this section and subject to the penalties provided in subsection (c) of this section:
(1)
Without operative brakes or without mufflers or other silencing equipment;
(2)
On any private property without the express written permission of the owner of the property or his agent; or
(3)
On any city street, highway, or alley.
(c)
Enforcement and penalties. All law enforcement officers shall enforce this section within the city. Any person who violates this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction, shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $25.00.
(Code 1977, § 20-12; Ord. No. 1322B, § I, 8-7-2018)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. §§ 40-7-3, 40-7-6; authority to adopt rules consistent with state law for off-road vehicles, O.C.G.A. § 40-7-5.

Sec. 70-67. - Truck routes
new
(a)
Definitions.
(1)
Large truck means every motor vehicle designed, used, or maintained primarily for the transportation of property and having a gross vehicular weight in excess of 36,000 pounds, including the load thereon, or having an overall length in excess of 30 feet, or having in excess of three axles, except recreational vehicles, buses used in transportation of chartered parties, and government owned vehicles.
(2)
Residential zone An area designated on the City of Lyons Zoning Map as R1, R2, R2A, and R3.
(3)
Person means and shall extend and be applied to natural persons, as well as to firms, partnerships, associations, organizations, corporations and bodies politic, or any combination thereof, including, but not limited to and any owners, operators, lessees, or drivers of a large truck that is operated in violation of this section.
(b)
Prohibition on through trucks.
(1)
It shall be unlawful for any large truck to travel those certain city roads set forth on the attached exhibit "A" [to the ordinance from which this section is derived,] as now or hereafter amended, unless the large truck's trip originates or ends on such road or is made for the purpose of making a delivery to a site on such road. All such roads on which large trucks are prohibited as set forth above shall be posted with "No Thru Trucks" signs at each end of such roads, and, at the city council's discretion, such "No Thru Trucks" signs may also be posted at the intersection of any such road with other roads.
(2)
This section shall apply to roads specifically authorized herein by the city council and as set forth on the attached exhibit "A" [to the ordinance from which this section is derived,] as now or hereafter amended by resolution of the board. Upon adoption, this section shall apply immediately to those city roads listed on exhibit "A" attached hereto and incorporated herein by reference.
(c)
Prohibition on travel in residential subdivisions. No large truck shall enter, travel or park on a street in a residential zone in Lyons, except for the purpose of delivering goods to houses or points within such residential subdivision for short periods of time during daylight hours. Operators or owner/operators residing in subdivisions prior to the enactment of this section are exempt from this section of the ordinance until such time that they voluntarily cease operations.
(d)
Limited load streets, bridges and culverts.
(1)
It shall be unlawful to operate any vehicle on any on any street in the city where the weight of such vehicle, with or without loads, is in excess of the weight as limited by ordinance and where signs indicating such limitations are posted. Further, it shall be unlawful to drive any vehicle over a bridge or culvert in the city over the weight capacity shown on said bridge and/or culvert.
(2)
This section shall apply to bridges and/or culverts specifically authorized herein by the city council and as later added to or deleted from exhibit "A" by Resolution of the board. Upon adoption, this section shall apply immediately to those city bridges and/or culverts listed on exhibit "A" attached hereto and incorporated herein by reference.
(e)
Overnight stay. No large truck or tractor-trailer shall enter, travel or park on a street in a residential zone or park on a right-of-way in Lyons for the purpose of an overnight stay by the driver or operator.
(f)
Violations and penalties. Any person violating this section shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 or 60 days imprisonment. Each day that the offense continues shall constitute a separate violation. The imposition of any fine against a person for any violation of this section shall not preclude a civil action against such person to recover for any damages caused by any such person or any such violation of this section, nor shall this section constitute a waiver of any rights the county may have to pursue a civil action against such person or to otherwise seek restitution, and all such rights are expressly reserved.
(g)
Severability. Should a sentence, section, subsection or provision of this section or application thereof be declared unconstitutional or invalid, such declaration shall not affect or impair the validity of the reminder of said ordinance or the ordinance as a whole or any part or application so declared to be unconstitutional or invalid.
(Ord. of 12-7-2021)

ARTICLE IV. - LOW SPEED MOTOR VEHICLES

Sec. 70-81. - Short title.
This article shall be known as the "Lyons Low Speed Motor Vehicle Ordinance."
(Ord. of 9-3-2013)
Sec. 70-82. - Definitions.
[The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:]
All-terrain vehicle means any motorized vehicle designed for off-road use which is equipped with three or more low pressure tires and with a seat to be straddled by the operator and with handlebars for steering control.
Drivers license means any license to operate a motor vehicle issued under the laws of the State of Georgia or any other state.
Low speed motor vehicle (LSMV) means those electric and gasoline-powered pleasure carts, commonly called "golf carts" which when operated, are incapable of being operated at speeds in excess of 20 miles per hour and have at least three or more wheels. Low speed motor vehicle shall also mean any four-wheeled electric vehicle whose top speed attainable in one mile is greater than 20 miles per hour but not greater than 25 miles per hour on a paved level surface and which is manufactured in compliance with those federal motor vehicle safety standards for low-speed vehicles set forth in 49 CFR 571.500 and in effect January 1, 2001.
Operator means any person who drives or is in actual physical control of a motor vehicle.
Owner means a person, other than a lienholder or security interest holder, having the property in or title to a vehicle. The term includes a person entitled to the use and possession of a vehicle subject to a security interest in or lien by another person but excludes a lessee under a lease not intended as a security except as otherwise specifically provided in this title.
Private road or driveway means every way or place in private ownership and used for vehicular traffic by the owner and those having express or implied permission from the owner, but not by other persons.
Sidewalk means that portion of a street between the curb lines, or the lateral lines of a railway, and the adjacent property lines, intended for use by pedestrians.
Street means the entire width between boundary lines of every way publicly maintained when any part thereof is open to the use of the public for purposes of vehicular travel.
(Ord. of 9-3-2013)
Sec. 70-83. - License required.
Those persons who are 15 years of age, but not yet 16 years of age may drive a LSMV on the streets and those areas accessible by the public for vehicular traffic of the city provided he or she has in his or her possession a valid instructional permit issued by the state pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 40-5-4, as may be amended, and has not had his or her instructional permit suspended or revoked, and if he or she is accompanied in the front seat by a person at least 18 years of age who holds a valid motor vehicle driver's license.
(Ord. of 9-3-2013)
Sec. 70-84. - Operation regulations.
(a)
All operators of LSMVs shall abide by all traffic regulations applicable to vehicular traffic when using the streets and those areas accessible by the public for vehicular traffic in the city.
(b)
LSMVs shall not be operated on sidewalks at any time.
(c)
LSMVs may be operated over those streets and those areas accessible by the public for vehicular traffic only during daylight hours unless such motorized carts are equipped with functional headlights, tail lights, and brake lights.
(d)
LSMVs shall not be operated two or more abreast in a single traffic lane.
(e)
LSMVs shall be operated only on any highway or streets where the posted speed limit does not exceed 35 miles per hour. The operator of an LSMV shall not operate such vehicle on any street or highway where the posted speed limit exceeds 35 miles per hour, as required by O.C.G.A. § 40-6-362 except state highways (1, 280, 152 and 292). LSMV may cross state highways perpendicular.
(f)
The operator driving LSMVs shall not engage in hazardous activities on the highway or streets and those areas accessible by the public for vehicular traffic. Such hazardous activities, and the special rules pertaining to them, include but are not limited to the following:
(1)
Racing of LSMV in any form, except for special events approved by the city; and
(2)
Blocking of highway or streets and public access, for vehicular traffic, except for special events approved by the city.
(g)
All laws and ordinances relative to alcohol and its use, including open container laws, which apply to traffic on the streets of the city, also apply to operators of LSMVs.
(h)
All operators and passengers must remain seated at all times during the operation of the LSMV. No person may sit on the operator's lap during the operation of the LSMV.
(i)
Before any LSMV may be operated upon city streets or public areas of the city, it shall have installed thereon:
(1)
An emblem as required by O.C.G.A. § 40-8-4. The emblem shall be in a bright and conspicuous retro-reflective red-orange paint. Such emblem shall be mounted on the rear of such vehicles, in the approximate horizontal geometric center of the vehicle, at the height of three feet to five feet above the roadway, and shall be maintained at all times in a clean and reflective condition.
(2)
An amber strobe light as required by O.C.G.A. § 40-8-35. The light must be functional at all times the vehicle is in operation, and shall be visible within 500 feet from the front and rear of the vehicle.
(Ord. of 9-3-2013)
Sec. 70-85. - Exemption.
The provision of section 70-84 entitled "operating regulations" shall not apply to private streets or cart paths in subdivisions designed as golf course communities if such exemption is requested by the developer of such community or a homeowners association in writing and approved by the city.
(Ord. of 9-3-2013)
Sec. 70-86. - All-terrain vehicle.
All-terrain vehicles shall not be operated on any street or highway, sidewalk within the city.
(Ord. of 9-3-2013)
Sec. 70-87. - Penalties.
Any violation by an operator of a LSMV shall be charged against the operator according to the provisions of O.C.G.A. Title 40 and this article.
(Ord. of 9-3-2013)
