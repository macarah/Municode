ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 10-1. - Hunting with dogs.
(a)
No hunting of any kind with dogs shall be permitted in the city. In addition, if any person should release any dogs at any location for the purpose of hunting, and those dogs should then enter into the boundaries of the city, it shall be understood that the dogs are continuing to hunt, and, therefore, the person who released the dogs shall be defined as hunting with dogs within the city, and therefore in violation of this chapter.
(b)
Any person who violates this chapter or fails to comply with any of its requirements shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-11
(Code 1983, § 3-3; Ord. of 11-5-1992(1))

ARTICLE II. - ANIMAL CONTROL

Sec. 10-31. - County ordinance adopted.
The city does hereby adopt the county animal control ordinance, dated October 28, 1966, as amended.
(Code 1983, § 3-2(1))
Sec. 10-32. - Scope of program.
The county will provide a program of animal control to the city consisting of:
(1)
Administration and control of the sale of dog license tags;
(2)
Impoundment and disposition of stray dogs and dogs found running at large in violation of the county animal control ordinance pursuant to the terms and conditions specified in such ordinance; and
(3)
Issuance of summonses and citations to violators of the county animal control ordinance found in the city.
(Code 1983, § 3-2(5))
Sec. 10-33. - Administration of program.
(a)
All costs of administration of the county animal control ordinance shall be borne by the county and the county will accept for impoundment and disposition, on behalf of the city, all dogs conveyed to the humane shelter by citizens of the city.
(b)
The county will be an agent of the city for administration of dog control. The county will accept responsibility for registration, tagging, and licensing of all dogs pursuant to the county animal control ordinance.
(Code 1983, § 3-2(6))
Sec. 10-34. - Enforcement.
The city names the county as an agent for enforcement and policing of the county animal control ordinance within the city. Certain police officers designated by the county as humane officers shall enforce the county animal control ordinance within the city.
(Code 1983, § 3-2(2))
Sec. 10-35. - Payment to county.
The city will remit to the county, on a monthly basis, an amount equal to all funds collected pursuant to the administration and enforcement of the county animal control ordinance. Such sum shall specifically include:
(1)
All penalties incurred in dog registration and licensing;
(2)
All fines or other sums levied on violators in the appropriate court pursuant to enforcement; and
(3)
All other amounts collected pursuant to the animal control program provided in this article.
(Code 1983, § 3-2(3))
Sec. 10-36. - County to retain certain fees.
The county will retain all impoundment and board fees paid by animal owners upon redemption or adoption of animals impounded pursuant to the county animal control ordinance.
(Code 1983, § 3-2(4))
