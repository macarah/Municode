ARTICLE II. - ANIMAL CONTROL

Sec. 10-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Domesticated animal means any animal raised so as to live in close proximity to man. The term "domesticated animals" includes dogs and cats as well as varieties of poultry, livestock or reptiles, not meant for consumption.
Domesticated animal at large means any domesticated animal not under the restraint of a person capable of controlling it on or off the premises of the owner.
Owner means any person who owns, possesses, harbors, or keeps, or knowingly permits an animal to be harbored or kept or has an animal in his care, or who permits an animal to remain on or about his premises for more than three days.
Public nuisance means any animal that unreasonably annoys humans, endangers the life or health of other animals or persons, or substantially interferes with the rights of citizens, other than their owners, to enjoyment of life or property. The term "public nuisance" means and includes, but is not limited to, any animal that:
(1)
Damages the property of anyone other than its owner on numerous occasions;
(2)
Attacks other domestic animals on numerous occasions;
(3)
Unprovoked, attacks pedestrians or passersby on public property or any property not owned by the animal's owner.
Vaccination means a protection against rabies by inoculation with antirabies vaccine, given in an amount sufficient to provide immunity from rabies for a minimum of one year.
Sec. 10-32. - Vaccinations.
(a)
No person shall own, keep, maintain, harbor, or permit to be kept or harbored, any dog or cat within the unincorporated area of the county unless the dog or cat is vaccinated as herein provided.
(b)
All dogs and cats over three months of age within the unincorporated area of the county shall be vaccinated against rabies. Said rabies vaccination shall be administered by such person as is required by the laws of the state.
(c)
It shall be the duty of each dog owner, possessor, or custodian to have a current rabies tag issued by a person authorized by the laws of the state on such animals.
(1)
Silver rabies tags are current for the year stamped therein and the following year.
(2)
Colored rabies tags are current for the year stamped therein and for the three following years.
(d)
It shall be the duty of each dog owner, possessor or custodian to attach the rabies license tag to a collar, which shall be fastened around the dog's neck. The collar and rabies license shall be worn by the dog at all times. Failure to comply with this section shall be unlawful and shall be punishable as hereinafter provided.
(e)
It shall be the duty of each cat owner, possessor, or custodian to have a current certificate or other evidence of inoculation and to produce such evidence when requested by the law enforcement officer or animal control officer.
(Ord. of 2-14-2006, § 1)
Sec. 10-33. - Animals running at large.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for stray, unclaimed dogs and/or cats, or dogs or cats whose owner, possessor, keeper or harborer cannot be determined, to run loose in the unincorporated area of the county.
(b)
Any dog or cat in violation of subsection (a) of this section may be picked up by the sheriff of the county or the animal control officer and disposed of in accordance with the provisions hereinafter provided for.
(Ord. of 2-14-2006, § 2)
Sec. 10-34. - Female dogs.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to allow a female dog, when in breeding season, to run loose on the public streets, roads, highways and other public places within the incorporated area of the county.
(b)
Failure to comply with this section shall be unlawful and shall be punished as hereinafter provided.
(Ord. of 2-14-2006, § 3)
Sec. 10-35. - Destruction and quarantine of animals.
(a)
Dogs, cats and other animals, whether vaccinated or not, which have bitten a person, and those dogs, cats and other animals suspected of having rabies, or any dog or cat which has been bitten by an animal suspected of having rabies, shall promptly be confined in a cage strong enough to hold a rabid animal under all circumstances. The owner, possessor or custodian of said animal shall immediately notify the health department, the sheriff's office and the clerk of the board of commissioners of the county.
(b)
If a dog or cat is suspected of having rabies or has been bitten by an animal suspected of having rabies, then the dog or cat shall be removed from the owner's premises to a veterinary hospital and there placed under observation for a period of two weeks, and shall only be released to the owner upon authorization of the custodial veterinarian.
(c)
If a dog or cat is quarantined that is suspected of having rabies, and the dog or cat later comes down with rabies, then the rabid animal shall be promptly destroyed, and the dead animal turned over to the proper health officials.
(Ord. of 2-14-2006, § 4)
Sec. 10-36. - Animal control officer.
(a)
The county may contract with an animal shelter or association that operates an animal shelter for said animal shelter or association to provide an animal control officer in enforcing the provisions of section 10-33 and to assist law enforcement officers and/or health officials in the enforcement of the provisions of sections 10-32 and 10-35.
(b)
The animal control officer provided by said animal shelter or association shall serve without compensation from the county.
(c)
The animal control officer shall have no arrest power and at no time restrain or interfere with any person or legal entity, nor enter onto private property except in the pursuit of stray dogs or cats or in assisting a law enforcement in the pursuit of a rabid animal.
(d)
The animal control officer shall have the authority to catch and hold any stray, unclaimed dogs and cats as well as dogs and cats whose owner cannot be ascertained, who are in violation of section 10-33.
(1)
After catching any of said dogs and/or cats as described in subsection (d) of this section and also described in section 10-33, the animal control officer shall make every effort to find the owner, possessor, custodian or harborer of said animal, and return the animal to its owner, possessor, custodian or harborer.
(2)
If, after catching any of said dogs and/or cats as set forth in subsection (d)(1) of this section, and the true owner, possessor, custodian or harborer cannot be determined, then the animal control officer shall quarantine said animal in the animal control shelter for a period of two weeks (14 days), and place an add in the legal organ of the county, with a description of said animal or animals with information sufficient for the public to know where to pick up the dog and/or cat.
(3)
If the owner, possessor or harborer of said dog and/or cat does not come forward and claim said dog after said 14 day period, then, in that event, the animal control officer may put said dog up for adoption by the public, or the animal control officer shall destroy the animal in a humane manner.
(4)
All costs of housing and/or destroying any impounded dogs and cats shall be born by the animal shelter or association contracting with the county, for the impounding of said dogs and cats. However, the animal shelter may charge the owner, possessor, custodian or harborer a daily fee for housing and caring for any impounded animal plus any vaccination and/or veterinarian costs to the owner. The animal shelter may charge an adoption fee plus any vaccination and veterinarian costs to any person wishing to adopt any such impounded animal. All fees must be first approved by the board of commissioners of the county.
(Ord. of 2-14-2006, § 5)
Sec. 10-37. - Punishment for violations.
Any person violating sections 10-32 and/or 10-35 may be charged and summoned by the sheriff of the county, or his lawful deputies, and tried in the magistrate court of the county, and upon conviction, may be fined by the magistrate court no less than $50.00 and no more than $500.00, and/or placed in the county jail for a term not to exceed 60 days. Each day of violation shall constitute a separate and new violation.
(Ord. of 2-14-2006, § 6)

ARTICLE III. - HIGH-DENSITY LIVESTOCK AND POULTRY

Sec. 10-60. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Dwelling means an occupied or unoccupied structure designed to house human occupants. The structure must have a septic tank and either an operational well or public water to qualify. For the purposes of this article, the term "dwelling" includes, but is not limited to, structures such as meetinghouses, churches and commercial businesses. Examples of structures not meeting the definition are storage barns, workshops or sheds for livestock. The term "dwelling" does not include the operator's own house or a house constructed by operator.
High-density livestock and poultry farming means intensive methods for producing animal products which include confinement of large numbers of animals in small containment areas. This would include, but is not exclusive to, poultry and/or rabbits in small cages or houses containing more than 100 animals, swine in small pens, and sheep and cattle in small lots.
Other high-density livestock production means all farm production activities which exceed the minimum herd size requiring compliance with waste treatment standards of the state department of natural resources are high-density development and classified as conditional uses requiring review by the planning commission and approval by the board of commissioners.
Poultry house means a structure designed primarily for the housing of birds for commercial sale of eggs or birds. Poultry houses capable of handling more than 25,500 chickens will be considered as two poultry houses.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(C), § 2.0, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 10-61. - Penalties.
Any violation of this article shall, upon conviction in the magistrate court of the county, be fined an appropriate amount not to exceed the sum of $100.00 for each violation. Each day the violation continues shall be a new offense.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(C), § 9.0, 6-11-1996; Res. of 4-12-2005)
Sec. 10-62. - Substantial completion.
Facilities which have the base completed within 14 days from the effective date of the ordinance from which this article is derived shall be considered existing prior to such effective date, provided at least 50 percent of the framing is complete 90 days after the base has been inspected, and the inspection has taken place within the 14-day period. If framing is not at this 50 percent stage of completion, the permit for existing facilities will be repealed and the operator must meet the criteria for new construction. Compliance with this section will be on a facility-by-facility basis.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(C), § 3.0, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 10-63. - Distance restrictions.
(a)
Generally. No high-density livestock facility or poultry houses shall be erected less than 1,500 feet from an existing dwelling, or from any property located within a municipality zoned residential.
(b)
City limits. No high-density livestock and poultry facility shall be erected or constructed within one mile of the existing corporate city limits of each of the municipalities located in the county.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(C), § 4.0, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 10-64. - Construction.
No more than one high-density livestock facility or poultry house shall be built for each 5.0 acres of land, providing all other distances requirements have been met. Land must be continuous and not separated by country roads, state roads or rivers. For acreage calculation, roadways shall be included, as well as setback distances required under this article. The proposed operator shall be responsible for the preparation of a survey prepared by a registered surveyor and submitted to the administrator.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(C), § 5.0, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 10-65. - Permit.
(a)
Required. All high-density livestock facilities must have a permit obtained from the board of commissioners. Facilities currently under construction will be deemed permitted provided the operator applies within 14 days of the effective date of the ordinance from which this article is derived for a permit and the property is inspected and determined to be in substantial completion by the administrator. The permit shall remain current for the facility for as long as the facility remains in operation. Should the facility cease to operate for more than 12 consecutive months, the permit will no longer be valid. Should the facility be destroyed, the operator can reconstruct the facility no closer to the dwellings than the facility was prior to destruction.
(b)
Permit fee. An operator obtaining a permit after the adoption of the ordinance from which this article is derived shall pay a one-time permit fee of $100.00 to the board of commissioners.
(c)
Soil conservation recommendations. Prior to applying for a permit, an applicant shall contact soil conservation to obtain recommendations regarding soil nutrient content. A permit applicant must have a letter from soil conservation that the applicant has contacted soil conservation.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(C), § 6.0, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 10-66. - Appeals.
Should operators disagree with the findings of the administrator, the operator or landowner shall have the opportunity for an appeal to the board of commissioners or a board established by the board of commissioners to hear such appeal. The appeal must be in writing and received by the county clerk no later than 30 days from the date in which the violation was reported to the operator.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(C), § 7.0, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 10-67. - Inspections.
(a)
Inspections under this article shall be performed by the administrator within three working days after the operator has applied for a permit and requested an initial site inspection. A written report shall be provided to the operator with a copy maintained at the office of the board of commissioners.
(b)
Applicants for permits under this article shall pay all expenses, including, but not limited to, registered surveying, engineering, mapping which may be involved in applying, and certifying compliance with the requirements of this article.
(c)
The administrator shall assist an applicant in determining whether construction is in compliance with this article.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(C), § 8.0, 6-11-1996)
