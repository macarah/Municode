Sec. 62-31. - Created and established.
In order to guide and accomplish a coordinated and harmonious development of the city which will, in accordance with existing and future needs, best promote the public health, safety, morals, order, convenience, prosperity and general welfare, as well as efficiency and economy in the process of development, the City of Pooler Planning and Zoning Commission, referred to in this article as the planning and zoning commission, is created and established.
(Code 1976, § 15-1)
Sec. 62-32. - Membership; term; selection criteria; removal.
(a)
Membership. The planning and zoning commission consists of seven regular members and one alternate member. The regular members shall be comprised of four city employees, three city residents.
The city employees appointed to the planning and zoning commission are: city planner, police department chief, fire department chief, and the department head of public works. The police department chief, fire department chief, and the department head of public works may nominate a designee, within their department, to serve in their place.
City resident regular and alternate members are to be approved by city council. City council may appoint city resident members upon application, on a form to be provided by the city, by any resident who is of voting age and maintained continuous residency within the Pooler city limits for the 12 months preceding the application.
(1)
The city resident alternate member maybe approved and appointed in the same manner as a city resident regular member. Except as otherwise expressly provided in this section, the alternate member may not vote on or participate in planning and zoning commission business unless one or more of the city resident members is/are absent or abstains due to a conflict of interest as explained in section 62-33.
a.
The alternate may participate in workshops and discussions.
b.
The alternate shall be seated with the secretary.
c.
The alternate may be assigned non-voting duties as determined by the chairperson.
(2)
City resident members and the alternate should make every effort to attend all scheduled meetings and participate in a manner that is appropriate to their seat.
a.
Should a member be unable to attend, the member should notify the Chairperson who will activate the city resident alternate.
b.
Failure to attend scheduled meetings may result in removal as outlined in Subsection (d) below.
(3)
All commission members should familiarize themselves with the city's ordinances and zoning laws. Furthermore, all commission members should make a good faith effort to be informed on all issues brought before the commission.
(b)
Term.
(1)
Resident commission members serve at the pleasure of city council for a whole-year term of three years.
(2)
Any city resident regular or alternate member position may be deemed vacant in the same manner as a vacancy in office under section 2.12 of the City Charter.
(3)
In the event of a resident regular member vacancy, the resident alternate may fill the vacancy for the duration of the term. Upon the resident alternate member assuming a resident regular position or a vacancy in the resident alternate position, the alternate seat may then be filled in the same manner as a new applicant for the alternate position would be.
(4)
Resident commission members may serve until their successors are appointed and qualified unless they have been removed by council or are no longer able to meet the requirements to serve.
(5)
Resident commission member terms begin September 1 and end August 30 of each calendar year.
(6)
There are no term limits for resident regular or alternate members.
(7)
Upon conclusion of each term, or vacancy in office, any re-appointment for a subsequent term, appointment of a different city employee designee or city resident, or appointment to fill a vacancy in office, shall be subject to the provisions of subsection (a), (b) and (c) of this section. Notwithstanding, in the event an existing resident or alternate member is being re-appointed for a subsequent term, a new application to the city is not required.
(c)
Selection criteria. City council appoints resident regular and alternate members with consideration of all relevant factors, including without limitation the following:
(1)
Knowledge and experience relevant to the needs of the commission;
(2)
Diverse representation of backgrounds, professions and viewpoints;
(3)
Diverse geographic representation of the city; and
(4)
Potential conflicts of interest as explained in section 62-33 below.
Residents interested in appointment should submit an application to council no less than 60-days prior to start of a term to be considered for the term up for appointment. Applications turned in after this date may still be considered at the sole discretion of the commission. Applicants not appointed must resubmit an application to be considered for appointment in future terms. Existing appointees should notify the city in writing of the desire to be reappointed no less than 60-days prior to the last day of their term. Notification is not a guarantee of reappointment.
(d)
Removal criteria.
(1)
City council has the absolute authority to remove a resident regular or alternate member upon a majority vote. The city council may suspend any city resident or alternate member of the commission while investigating any alleged misconduct of the member(s) and may reinstate member(s), both by a majority vote.
(2)
Any city resident or alternate member may be removed automatically for:
a.
Absence from more than one-third of the commission meetings per annum whether excused or unexcused, unless they are on an approved leave of absence as defined by section 62-32 (e) below;
b.
A leave of absence exceeding 121-days;
c.
Failure to attend any three consecutive regular meetings whether excused or unexcused;
d.
Misconduct while conducting commission business;
e.
Repeated failure to notify the commission chair of an absence in a timely manner;
f.
Violation of section 62-33 below.
(3)
City employee members are subject to the appropriate guidelines for their respective positions with the city.
(e)
Leave of absence. Should the need for extended leave arise; any resident regular member may apply for a leave of absence from the chair not to exceed 120-days. During the duration of the absence, the resident alternate will serve.
(Code 1976, § 15-2; Ord. of 2-15-1999, § I; Ord. of 5-1-2017, § I ; Ord. No. 2020-07.A , § I, 9-15-20)
Sec. 62-33. - Conflict of interest.
(a)
Prohibited. No member of the planning and zoning commission shall benefit monetarily or otherwise, either directly nor indirectly, from a decision of the planning and zoning commission. No member of the planning and zoning commission shall engage in a conflict of interest whereby any member will have an interest in the final decision, determination or judgment of the planning and zoning commission causing a conflict between the personal interest of the member and the best interest of the citizens of the city. All members of the planning and zoning commission are prohibited from benefiting or engaging in a conflict of interest pertaining to any matter which is presented before or decided by the planning and zoning commission.
(1)
All members of the planning and zoning commission are expected to disclose to the chairperson if they believe their participation in the discussion, presentation, and/or vote of a proposal before the commission represents a potential conflict of interest. It is the responsibility of the member to recuse themselves or to abstain from participating in the discussion, presentation, and/or vote of said proposal. The chair may also remove the member from participation in the discussion, presentation, and/or vote of said proposal if he/she determines if a removal is in the interest of ensuring a fair and honest decision. This removal does not constitute any sort of removal of other duties or assignments of the commission nor of membership from the commission.
(2)
In such cases of a temporary removal of a member for conflict of interest, the resident alternate member may be called to participate to achieve quorum as explained in section 62-34(a)(3). If quorum cannot be achieved, with the temporary removal of the member, the proposal must be postponed until such time that quorum can be achieved.
(Code 1976, § 15-2.1; Ord. No. 2020-07.A , § II, 9-15-20)
Sec. 62-34. - Organization; purpose; rules; staff; finances.
(a)
Organization. The chairperson of the planning and zoning commission is the city planner. The chairperson may appoint a secretary, who may be an officer or employee of the city. Should the chairperson be unable to attend a meeting, or the position of city planner is not filled, the city zoning administrator may serve as chairperson pro tem.
(1)
All planning and zoning commission meetings shall comply with the requirements of O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1 et seq. (as amended), including without limitation all meetings being open to the public and minutes taken.
(2)
The planning and zoning commission should hold two meetings per month, with specific meetings dates and times to be set by the commission, provided the planning and zoning meeting is not held in the same calendar week (i.e. Sunday to Saturday) as a regular meeting of the city council.
(3)
Any four members, of the planning and zoning commission constitute a quorum for any meeting, including the chairperson. The resident alternate will not be counted unless they are sitting in place of a resident regular member.
(4)
Any item for consideration by the planning and zoning commission or proposed recommendation to the city council must be acted upon by voice vote and recorded in the minutes.
(5)
Any item for consideration or proposed recommendation requires a minimum of three votes in favor of or against the proposed recommendation. The chairperson shall not cast a vote except in the event of a tie.
The planning and zoning commission shall follow all notice, meeting, and public hearing guidelines set forth in Appendix A, Article V, Sections 5 and 6, of the Code of Ordinances for the City of Pooler.
(b)
Purpose. Except as otherwise expressly provided in the City of Pooler Charter or Code of Ordinances, the planning and zoning commission is as an advisory board only, and any recommendation made is not binding upon the city council. Persons appearing before the commission should not rely on statements made by commission members as being dispositive or approving any item for consideration.
(c)
Rules of procedure. The planning and zoning commission may conduct business according to this order, but may alter the manner of business from time to time as necessary and appropriate:
(1)
Workshop (if applicable)
(2)
Call to order
(3)
Pledge of allegiance
(4)
Review of previous meeting minutes
(5)
Old business
(6)
New business (if applicable), including necessary public hearings
(7)
Other business brought before the commission
(8)
Meeting adjournment
All agenda items may be discussed prior to a motion unless that agenda item is withdrawn by the petitioner. Only seated commission members may comment on a motion after it has been made, unless it is a motion to table or postpone, at which time there is no discussion on the motion.
When any question arises, which is not provided for in the rules applicable to the planning and zoning commission, as far as practicable, it will be controlled by the current edition of Robert's Rules of Order.
(d)
Staff access and finances.
(1)
Subject to approval from the city manager, all members of the planning and zoning commission may have access to staff-to help aid with research and study of items brought to the commission.
(2)
Commission members are not entitled to compensation for their time or efforts spent on commission business but may be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in connection with their official duties, upon approval by the city manager.
(3)
All members of the planning and zoning commission may receive materials and tools, as deemed necessary by the city council, to complete their duties at no cost to the commission members.
(Code 1976, § 15-3; Ord. of 5-1-2017, § II ; Ord. No. 2020-07.A , § III, 9-15-20)
