ARTICLE II. - MOBILE HOME PARKS

Sec. 34-30. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Mobile home means a generic term for a factory-built dwelling unit built on a permanent chassis and made to be readily transportable on its own running gear in one or more sections totaling no less than 576 square feet which is subject to the Georgia Motor Vehicle Title Registration Act (O.C.G.A. § 40-2-1 et seq.) and meeting the construction and safety standards of the national code administered by the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development. The term "mobile home" does not include recreational vehicles. Every mobile home shall:
(1)
Be suitable for yearround occupancy;
(2)
Be placed on a permanent masonry foundation or other substantial foundation that will comply with the state minimum building code for the erection and support of mobile homes and to be substantially anchored to its site also in compliance with the state minimum building code;
(3)
Be connected to a water system approved by either the county health department or the state department of natural resources, environmental protection division, as the case may be, and a letter or certification of such approval shall be presented to the county inspector;
(4)
Be connected to an approved waste disposal system approved by either the county health department or the state department of natural resources, environmental protection division, as the case may be, and a letter or certification of such approval shall be presented to the county inspector;
(5)
Have all electrical supply systems and connections that comply with the current state minimum electric code; and
(6)
Be landscaped with trees, shrubs, and other plan material substantially equal to or better than one-family dwelling within 500 feet. The area between the bottom perimeter of the mobile home and the ground shall be covered with material that is suitable and adequately functional to adequately protect the under side of the mobile home and to keep the mobile home from being unsightly. The owner of the mobile home is responsible for the skirting.
Mobile home park means:
(1)
A parcel of land which has been planned and improved for the placement of two or more mobile homes for nontransient use and occupancy or for rental occupancy. A conditional use permit shall be required for the establishment of a mobile home park. The term "park" includes the term "community."
(2)
The owner of a tract or parcel of land who has placed a mobile home on that tract or parcel of land as his residence may place one additional mobile home on the parcel of land, as long as the second mobile home is not rested, and the two mobile homes located on the tract of land shall not be classified as a mobile home park.
Mobile home space means a plot of ground within a mobile home park designated for the accommodation of not more than one mobile home of single-family occupancy.
Modular home means a factory fabricated transportable building consisting of building units designed to be incorporated at a building site on a permanent foundation as a permanent structure with the appearance of a conventional on site constructed building and to be used for residential purposes. A modular home is not a mobile home. The term "modular home" includes the terms "manufactured home" and "prefabricated home." Every modular home shall:
(1)
Be suitable for yearround occupancy;
(2)
Be placed on a permanent masonry foundation or other substantial foundation that will comply with the state minimum building code for the erection and support of mobile homes and be substantially fixed to its site also in compliance with the state minimum building code;
(3)
Be connected to a water system approved by either the county health department or the state department of natural resources, environmental protection division, as the case may be, and a letter or certification of such approval shall be presented to the county inspector;
(4)
Be connected to an approved waste disposal system approved by either the county health department or the state department of natural resources, environmental protection division, as the case may be, and a letter or certification of such approval shall be presented to the county inspector;
(5)
Have all electrical supply systems and connections that comply with the current state minimum electric code;
(6)
Be landscaped with trees, shrubs, and other plan material substantially equal to or better than one-family dwelling within 500 feet; and
(7)
Meet the general construction and safety standards of the latest edition of the state minimum building codes.
The area between the bottom perimeter of the mobile home and the ground shall be covered with material that is suitable and adequately functional to adequately protect the underside of the mobile home and to keep the mobile home from being unsightly. The owner of the mobile home is responsible for the skirting.
Rent or rental means any enumeration, whether in money, barter, or kind, that is given or received as consideration for the occupancy of a mobile home or the occupancy of a tract, space or parcel of land upon which a mobile home is situated.
Service building means a building that houses facilities such as recreational, maintenance, laundry, and office structures necessary to the successful development and management of a mobile home park.
Travel trailer means a motorized camper, converted bus, tent-trailer or other similar vehicular or portable structure used or designed for temporary portable housing or occupancy while on vacation, recreational, or other trip and provided with sleeping accommodations. The travel trailer shall not be used for permanent housing or a permanent residence.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 1, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-31. - Penalties and incentives.
(a)
Failure to obtain a permit. If any person commences a mobile home park without first obtaining a permit, the person shall be subject to revocation of any permit or other authorization received from the county and associated with the establishment of the mobile home park.
(b)
Stop work orders. Upon notice from the county inspector, work on the mobile home contrary to the provision of this article, or in a dangerous or unsafe manner, shall be immediately stopped. Such notice shall be in writing and shall be given to the owner of the property, his authorized agent, or the person in charge of constructing the mobile home park on the property, and shall state the conditions under which work way be resumed. Where an emergency exists, no written notice shall be required.
(c)
Civil penalties. Any person violating any provision of this article or stop work order shall be liable for a civil penalty of not less than $500.00 per day, a sentence of imprisonment not exceeding 60 days in jail, or both fine and jail, or work alternative. Each day the violation continues shall constitute a separate offence. All violators issued a summons shall be tried in the magistrate court of the county.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 23, 6-11-1996; Res. of 4-12-2005)
Sec. 34-32. - Purpose.
The purpose of this article is to prescribe the manner in which a mobile home park may be created and operated in the county for the health, safety, and welfare of those who reside in the mobile home park and for the preservation of the health, safety, and welfare of all citizens of the county, and to provide certain standards and criteria so that the health, safety and welfare of all citizens may be preserved.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-33. - Conflict with other regulations.
(a)
Whenever the regulations of this article require a greater width or size of yards, courts, or other open space, or an imposed other restrictive standards that are required in or under any other structure or ordinance, the regulations and requirements of this article shall govern.
(b)
Whenever the provisions of any other statute or ordinance require more restrictive standards than are required by this article, the provisions of such statute or ordinance shall govern.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 2, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-34. - Application for permission to develop or maintain.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to develop or maintain a mobile home park in the county unless it is duly permitted and pursuant to the provisions of this section.
(b)
The application for the development of a mobile home park shall have attached to the application proof that all county taxes due the county from the application have been paid in full, and no permit may be issued until all taxes due from the applicant are paid in full.
(c)
The board of commissioners may recommend approval of a planned mobile home park when all conditions precedent to this article have been met.
(d)
An application and all accompanying plans and supporting data shall be filed in triplicate with the county inspector at least two weeks prior to a regular meeting of the board of commissioners. The application shall be in writing, and it shall include the following information:
(1)
The name and address of the applicant;
(2)
The location and description of the boundaries of the property intended for planned mobile home park development;
(3)
A complete mobile home park plan showing all existing conditions and proposed site development as required in this section including preliminary plans of all buildings, improvements and facilities constructed or to be constructed within the mobile home park (on a topographic map). Such plan shall be drawn at a scale of one inch equals not more than 100 feet;
(4)
Any other information reasonably requested by the county inspector to determine the proposed park's compliance with legal requirements, the effect which the proposed park may have on its environment and the compatibility of the adjacent area for park development.
The application shall be verified by the applicant, and the board of commissioners are authorized to revoke the mobile home permit in the event any false statements are made on the application.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 3, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-35. - Dimensional and site development requirements; mobile home park plan.
The mobile home park plan shall show or propose all requirements listed in this article, and in addition, all such requirements shall be complied with before a certificate or occupancy may be issued. The mobile home park plan shall, at a minimum, conform to the following:
(1)
Frontage and area. A mobile home park shall front for at least 100 feet on a major public thoroughfare and shall have a minimum area of at least five buildable acres.
(2)
Entrances and exits. The entrances and exits to the mobile home park shall be from a major public thoroughfare. All entrance and exit streets shall have a right-of-way width of at least 60 feet and a surface width of at least 28 feet and a distance of at least 100 feet from the public thoroughfare extending into the park, and after the first 100 feet shall be deemed an interior street.
(3)
Park boundaries. A mobile home park shall be bounded with a ten-foot buffer strip along all of the exterior property lines of the park not bounding a major public thoroughfare. The buffer strip shall be planted with evergreen trees or shrubs that grow at least eight feet in height and provide a visual screen.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 4, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-36. - Minimum number of mobile home spaces.
Any legal entity that owns and rents two or more spaces shall be required to obtain a permit and will be subject to the terms of this article.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 5, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-37. - Required lot (mobile home space) area.
Each mobile home space shall contain at least 4,500 square feet of area, and will also be of sufficient size to comply with the county and state health laws. There shall not be more than eight spaces per gross acre.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 6, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-38. - Yard requirements.
(a)
Each mobile home space shall be at least 52 feet wide and clearly defined.
(b)
There shall be a 20-foot side yard for each mobile home space, the side yard being the space parallel to the longest dimension of the home.
(c)
There shall be a 40-foot minimum distance between homes, side-to-side, and a 40-foot minimum distance between homes, end-to-end.
(d)
No mobile home shall be located closer than 25 feet to any street and within the park or within 40 feet of any exterior public street.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 7, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-39. - Drainage.
The mobile home park shall be located on a well-drained and properly graded site. The site drainage improvements shall meet the county's soil, erosion, and sedimentation ordinance.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 8, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-40. - Interior streets and driveways.
All mobile home parks shall contain a street system designed to provide convenient circulation within the park, and shall have unobstructed access to a public street or highway in accordance with requirements of the county. The following requirements shall apply to the development of a mobile home park's street system:
(1)
All interior streets shall have a right-of-way width of at least 40 feet unless a greater width for drainage purposes is required. The minimum surface width shall be 22 feet for an interior street;
(2)
All mobile home park streets shall be provided with a smooth, graveled all-weather surface having a minimum width of 22 feet which shall be durable and well drained under normal weather conditions;
(3)
All mobile home park streets shall be maintained in a state of good repair at all times by the park owner;
(4)
Street base and surface construction materials shall be as required by the county's street and read standards. Written approval of the street system by the county inspector shall be required before the first mobile home building permit will be issued.
(5)
Driveways on individual spaces shall be at least eight feet in width;
(6)
All structures located on the space shall observe the required setback and distance requirements.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 9, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-41. - Off-drive parking.
(a)
No automobile parking shall be permitted on that portion of the interior street system designated as the entrance or exit portion and being that portion of the street that is 60 feet in width and 100 feet deep as measured from the major public thoroughfare.
(b)
At least two automobile spaces shall be provided for each mobile home space. The parking spaces shall be provided on the individual spaces or on street parking lanes or on an off-street parking lot that is located within 200 feet of the mobile home spaces the parking lot serves.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 10, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-42. - Signs.
The following nonflashing and nonanimated signs are permitted under the conditions specified:
(1)
Park identification sign. One mobile home park identification sign shall be required, not to exceed 24 square feet in area, for the purpose of denoting the name of the mobile home park.
(2)
Mobile home lot identification sign. One mobile home lot identification sign not exceeding one square foot in area shall be required for each mobile home lot. Such sign shall indicate the name, address, and lot number of the occupant of the mobile home.
(3)
Street signs. All streets in the mobile home park shall be named in conformity with the this article and street signs shall be erected at the park owner's expense. The sign identifying the lot shall be visible from the street.
(4)
Visibility. No sign shall be located so as to impede vehicular visibility at any intersection of street lines with one another or the edge of driveways with street lines.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 11, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-43. - Improvements to mobile homes and mobile home spaces.
(a)
No permanent addition of any kind shall be built onto or become a part of any mobile home, with the exception of awnings and porches, which shall comply with building code requirements in effect in the county.
(b)
Each space shall contain a permanent mobile home stand constructed of a suitable subgrade material to support the mobile home. The chassis of each mobile home shall be supported on an adequate masonry foundation. Such masonry foundation shall meet the minimum requirements of the state minimum building code.
(c)
Each mobile home space shall be given to lot number and accordingly marked to be visible from the street providing access thereto.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 12, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-44. - Special conditions and safeguards.
In recommending approval of any planned mobile home park, the board of commissioners may attach special conditions and safeguards to protect both the occupants of the park and the occupants of surrounding property, including such matters as protection against noise, lights, dust, and fire. Where required to service these ends, walls, plantings, surfacing, or other natural or artificial means for protection may reasonably be required as part of such special conditions on which the recommendation for approval of a mobile home park is based. Failure to meet such conditions shall be grounds for refusal of issuance of a certificate of occupancy.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 13, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-45. - Minimum size and sanitation facilities of mobile homes.
No mobile home which has less than 576 square feet of floor space and which does not contain a built-in bathroom with water closet, lavatory and shower or tub which are in working condition, or manufactured seven years prior to date of permit, shall be placed in a planned mobile home park.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 14, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-46. - Lighting.
All interior drives and walkways within the mobile home park shall be lighted at night with electric lamps spaced at intervals of no more than 150 feet. Such lighting shall meet the minimum requirements of the state mobile home regulations.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 15, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-47. - Refuse collection facilities.
Each mobile home park shall be provided with a sanitary method of solid waste collection and disposal meeting the requirements of the state and the county solid waste ordinance.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 16, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-48. - Service buildings.
Service buildings such as shelters, restrooms, management offices and storage facilities are permitted in each mobile home park and are subject to the following requirements: Such buildings and their parking areas shall not occupy more than ten percent of the gross land area of the park.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 17, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-49. - Existing mobile home parks.
Mobile home parks existing on the effective date of the ordinance from which this article is derived may be continued by the owner who owned the mobile home park as of the date of such ordinance, and maintained except that such mobile home park shall not be enlarged or expanded unless such enlargement or expansion is in full compliance with this article; provided, however, the owner shall comply with the requirements of section 34-53; and provided further, each mobile home park owner, and each owner of a mobile home, shall comply with the skirting requirements of this article within six months of the date of adoption of the ordinance from which this article is derived.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 18, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-50. - Development compliance.
(a)
At least 100 percent of each phase, enlargement or expansion of all planned spaces, as well as all streets, utilities, and other required improvements necessary to serve them pursuant to this article, shall be completely constructed according to the mobile home park plan approved by the board of commissioners before the first mobile home building permit will be issued.
(b)
Failure of the developer or applicant to construct this minimum phase within two years after approval by the board of commissioners shall cause such approval to expire.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 19, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-51. - Inspection.
(a)
The building inspector is authorized and directed to make inspections to determine the condition of mobile home parks located within the county, in order that such inspector may perform the duty of safeguarding the health and safety of occupants of mobile home parks and of the general public.
(b)
The building inspector shall have the power to enter at reasonable times upon any private or public property for the purpose of inspecting and investigating conditions relating to the enforcement of this article.
(c)
The building inspector shall have the power to inspect the register containing a record of all mobile homes and occupants using the mobile home park.
(d)
It shall be the duty of the owners or occupants of mobile home parks, or of the person in charge thereof, to give the building inspector free access to such premises at reasonable times for the purpose of inspection.
(e)
It shall be the duty of every occupant of a mobile home park to give the owner thereof, or the owner's agent or employee, access to any part of such mobile home park or its premises at reasonable times for the purpose of making such repairs or alterations as are necessary to effect compliance with this article or with any lawful order issued pursuant to the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 20, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-52. - Decal required.
(a)
All persons owning a mobile home located in the county on January 1 of each year shall obtain from the tax commissioner a decal before April 1, as provided in O.C.G.A. § 48-5-493. This requirement pertains to all mobile homes, including these exempted from taxation by homestead exemption or other provisions of state law.
(b)
The mobile home decal shall be affixed to each mobile home in such manner as to cause the decal to be easily visible for inspection.
(c)
Owners of mobile homes locating their mobile homes in the county after January 1 of any year shall be required to report the location of their mobile home to the tax commissioner's office and to the building inspector within ten working days.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 21, 6-11-1996)
Sec. 34-53. - Title reports by mobile home park operators.
(a)
Every owner and operator of a mobile home park is required to give to the county inspector an inventory of all mobile homes located in the park as of January 1 of each year, beginning January of 1996. The inventory shall be delivered to the county inspector not later than January 20 of each year.
(b)
Every owner, manager, and operator of a mobile home park is required to report to the county inspector any mobile home moved into the mobile home park after January 1 of each year.
(c)
Every owner, manager, and operator of a mobile home park shall furnish to the county inspector an updated lot map of each mobile home park, and changes to the map shall be reported by January 20 of each year.
(d)
Every owner, manager, and operator of a mobile home park is required to notify the county inspector of any change in ownership or any change in the name of any mobile home park within 30 days of such change.
(e)
The county inspector will notify the tax commissioner of the information that has been furnished to him by the owner and operator of the mobile home park as may be needed by the tax commissioner for the updating of the tax commissioner's records.
(f)
This section applies to all mobile home park owners, including those in existence prior to the adoption of the ordinance from which this article is derived.
(Ord. No. 96-11-06(B), § 22, 6-11-1996)

Sec. 34-71. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, or phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section:
Applicant means any person seeking to install a pre-owned manufactured home in the unincorporated area of Marion County.
Building inspector means the person appointed, employed, or otherwise designated as the director of planning, permits and inspections; the county building official or any of his or her assistants.
Certificate of occupancy means a document issued by the building inspector certifying that a pre-owned manufactured home is in compliance with applicable requirements set forth by this article, and indicating it to be in a condition suitable for residential occupancy.
Guarantee of condition bond means a surety bond to guarantee that the affidavit and photographs required by subsections 34-73(1)a. and b. of this article reasonably portray or represents the existing condition of the pre-owned manufactured home proposed for relocation. In lieu of the bond, a cash deposit may be deposited with the county.
Install means to construct a foundation system and to place or erect a manufactured home on such foundation system. Such term includes, without limitation, supporting, blocking, leveling, securing, or anchoring such manufactured home and connecting multiple or expandable sections of such manufactured home.
Jurisdiction means the unincorporated areas of Marion County, Georgia.
Manufactured home means a structure, transportable in one or more sections, which, in the traveling mode, is eight body feet or more in width or 40 body feet or more in length or, when erected on site, is 320 or more square feet and which is built on a permanent chassis and designed to be used as a dwelling with or without a permanent foundation when connected to the required utilities and includes the plumbing, heating, air conditioning, and electrical systems contained therein; except that such term shall include any structure which meets all the requirements of this paragraph except the size requirements and with respect to which the manufacturer voluntarily files a certification required by the secretary of housing and urban development and complies with the standards established under the National Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards Act of 1974, 42 U.S.C. Section 5401 et seq.
Pre-owned manufactured home means any manufactured home that has been previously used as a residential dwelling and has been titled.
(Ord. of 10-12-2010, § 1)
Sec. 34-72. - Conditions.
All pre-owned manufactured homes located in the jurisdiction shall bear a label certifying it was constructed in compliance with the National Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards Act of 1974, 42 U.S.C. Section 5401 et seq. (the HUD Code) and shall be installed in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 8-2-160 et seq.
(Ord. of 10-12-2010, § 2)
Sec. 34-73. - Permitting, inspection, certificate of occupancy und fees.
A permit shall be required to locate a pre-owned manufactured home in the jurisdiction.
(1)
Permit. To obtain a permit, applicants shall provide to the building inspector:
a.
An affidavit signed by the applicant that the pre-owned manufactured home meets health and safety standards required by this Act;
b.
Photographs of the interior and exterior of the pre-owned manufactured home providing evidence that home meets the minimum health and safety standards of section 34-74 of this article;
c.
A $3,000.00 refundable guarantee of condition bond or $3,000.00 refundable cash deposit; and
d.
The permit and inspection fee required by subsection (4) of this section.
(2)
Inspection. Upon receipt of a permit, applicants may relocate the manufactured home on a residential site for the purposes of inspection. Applicant shall arrange for an inspection to be held once the installation of the manufactured home is complete.
(3)
Certificate of occupancy. A certificate of occupancy shall be issued to the applicant at such time that the building inspector certifies that the requirements of this article have been met.
(4)
Fee. A permit and inspection fee of $100.00 shall be charged to the applicant to cover the cost to the county to process the permit application and inspect the pre-owned manufactured home. Such fee shall cover the initial inspection and one followup inspection. The applicant shall be charged $100.00 for each additional followup inspection that may be necessary,
(5)
Alternative inspection. At the request of the applicant, the building inspector may, at his or her discretion, inspect a pre-owned manufactured home prior to its being relocated if the home is then located at another site within the county.
(Ord. of 10-12-2010, § 3)
Sec. 34-74. - Minimum health and safety standards.
All preowned manufactured homes shall comply with the following before being issued a certificate of occupancy by the building inspector:
(1)
HUD Code. Every pre-owned manufactured home located in the jurisdiction shall be in compliance with the Federal Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards Act, 42 U.S.C. 5401-5445 (the HUD Code) and shall not have been altered in such a way that the home no longer meets the HUD Code.
(2)
Interior condition. Every floor, interior wall, and ceiling of a pre-owned manufactured home shall be in sound condition. Doors and windows shall be operable, watertight and in good working condition. The floor system shall be in sound condition and free of warping, holes, water damage, or deterioration.
(3)
Exterior condition. The exterior of all pre-owned manufactured homes shall be free of loose or rotting boards or timbers and any other conditions that might admit rain or moisture to the interior portions of the walls or to occupied spaces. The exterior siding shall be free of rot and rust. Roofs shall be structurally sound and have no obvious defects that might admit rain or cause moisture to collect on the interior portion of the home.
(4)
Sanitary facilities. Every plumbing fixture, water, and waste pipe of a pre-owned manufactured home shall be in a sanitary working condition when properly connected, and shall be free from leaks and obstructions. Each home shall contain a kitchen sink. Each bathroom shall contain a lavatory and water closet. At least one bathroom shall contain a tub and/or shower facilities. Each of these fixtures shall be checked upon being connected to ensure they are in good working condition.
(5)
Heating systems. Heating shall be safe and in working condition. Un-vented heaters shall be prohibited.
(6)
Electrical systems. Electrical systems (switches, receptacles, fixtures, etc.) shall be properly installed and wired and shall be in working condition. Distribution panels shall be in compliance with the approved listing, complete with required breakers, with all unused openings covered with solid covers approved and listed for that purpose. The home shall be subject to an electrical continuity test to assure that, all metallic parts are properly bonded.
(7)
Hot water supply. Bach home shall contain a water heater in safe and working condition.
(8)
Egress windows. Each bedroom of a manufactured home shall have at least one operable window of sufficient size to allow egress if necessary.
(9)
Ventilation. The kitchen in the home shall have at least one operating window or other ventilation device.
(10)
Smoke detectors. Each pre-owned manufactured home shall contain one operable battery-powered smoke detector in each bedroom and in the kitchen, which must be installed in accordance with the manufacturer's recommendations.
(Ord. of 10-12-2010, § 4)
Sec. 34-75. - Enforcement.
(a)
Permanent connection to utilities shall not be approved until the building inspector has issued a certificate of occupancy.
(b)
Owners of pre-owned manufactured homes that are not in compliance upon a third inspection shall have their permit revoked and shall be required to remove the home front the jurisdiction at their own expense.
(c)
The guarantee of condition bond or cash deposit will be forfeited after 90 days from the date of inspection, unless all conditions and standards are met prior to the end of the 90 days or an extension has been issued in writing by the building inspector.
(Ord. of 10-12-2010, § 5)
Sec. 34-76. - Penalties.
Failure to remove a pre-owned manufactured home from the jurisdiction upon failure to receive a certificate of occupancy shall be punishable by a fine of $500.00. Each day any violation under this article continues shall be considered a separate offense.
(Ord. of 10-12-2010, § 6)
