ARTICLE XII. - BONDSMEN

Sec. 10-200. - Professional bondsmen.
(a)
All professional bondsmen who act as sureties on bail bonds required by the municipal court shall be required to adequately secure the payment of any bail bonds signed by them in the event said bonds are forfeited. As used herein, the term "professional bondsman" means any person who charges a fee for posting bail for another. Fee for bondsmen license two hundred sixteen dollars ($216.00).
(b)
In order to provide adequate security, the professional bondsman must:
(1)
Own property in the county where the equity in such property is sufficient to cover all bonds signed by such professional bondsman, but such equity shall not be less than twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000.00); or
(2)
Post a surety bond to be approved by the chief of police in an amount equal to the amount of outstanding bonds signed by said professional bondsman; but such bond shall be an amount not less than twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000.00); or
(3)
Post a cash bond in the amount of twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000.00), provided that the outstanding bonds signed by said professional bondsman does not exceed twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000.00), a cash bond will be required to cover any amounts in excess of twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000.00).
(c)
The chief of police is authorized to establish such other reasonable rules, regulations and procedures relating to the securing of the payment of forfeited bonds as may be deemed by him to be appropriate.
(d)
Adequate records showing the total amount of bonds signed by each professional bondsman shall be maintained in the office of the chief of police at all times to ensure the proper enforcement of this section.
(e)
Nothing in this section shall prevent an individual from posting his own cash bond or posting a property bond provided any such property bond is approved by the chief of police or any officer to whom he may delegate such authority.
(Code 1977, § 2-3004.1; Ord. No. 53-94, § 1, 11-21-94)
