Sec. 66-31. - Definitions.
modified
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Charitable purpose means any benevolent, philanthropic, patriotic, or eleemosynary purpose for religion, health, education, social welfare, arts and humanities, environment, civic, or public interest.
Contributions means the promise or grant of any money or property of any kind or value.
Itinerant merchant means any person who idles, stops, or stands for more than 30 minutes at any location within the city or on city property or public land, to sell or take orders for any services, goods, wares, or merchandise; and who, in furtherance of such purposes sets up displays or exhibits, distributes samples, or holds instructional demonstrations on the use of those services, goods, wares, or merchandise.
Peddler means any person who travels throughout the city, generally limiting their time to 30 minutes or less per location, to sell or take orders for goods, wares, or merchandise which they carry, convey, or transport as they move from door to door or place to place; and who, in furtherance of such purposes, may distribute handbills or other materials which promote or advertise the sale or provision of goods or services.
Person means an individual, a firm, a corporation, a partnership, a limited liability company, an association, a joint-stock company, a trust, an unincorporated organization, a league, a society, or any religious organization and includes any owner, employee, trustee, receiver, assignee, agent or other similar representative thereof.
Religious organization means an entity which:
(1)
Conducts regular worship services; or
(2)
Is qualified as a religious organization under section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended.
Solicitation activity means the request directly or indirectly of money, credit, property, financial assistance or other things of value from another. These requests may be accomplished by using any of the following methods:
(1)
Any spoken or written request;
(2)
The distribution, circulation, mailing, posting or publishing of any handbill, written advertisement, or publication;
(3)
The making of any announcement to the press over the radio, by telephone or telegraph concerning an appeal, assemblage, athletic or sports event, bazaar, benefit, campaign, contest, dance, drive, entertainment, exhibition, exposition, party, performance, picnic, sale or social gathering, which the public is requested to patronize or to which the public is requested to make a [contribution]; or
(4)
The sale of, offer or attempt to sell, any advertisement, advertising space, book, card, chance, coupon, device, magazine, membership, merchandise, subscription, ticket or other thing.
The term "solicitation" shall be deemed completed when made, whether or not the person making the same received any contribution or makes any sale referred to in this article.
Solicitor means any person who takes or attempts to take orders for goods, wares, or merchandise, including books, periodicals, magazines or personal property of any nature for future delivery; or, who seeks contributions in the form of information, money, donations, financial assistance, or anything else of value for any purpose; or who takes or attempts to take orders for services to be furnished or performed in the future.
The terms "solicitor," "itinerant merchant" and "peddler" shall not include the following classifications of individuals:
(1)
Any person, firm or corporation who solicits orders for the future delivery of goods to merchants for the merchants' resale. Orders solicited from merchants shall be orders for future delivery only.
(2)
Individuals who sell agricultural produce produced by that individual.
(Code 1989, § 9-3-1; Ord. No. 005-2007, 3-12-2007; Ord. No. 4004, § 1, 7-11-2016; Ord. No. 7041-2021 , §§ 2, 3, 4-12-2021)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 66-32. - License required; prohibitions.
(a)
Itinerant merchants prohibited. Itinerant merchants do not qualify for a license to operate within the city limits.
(b)
Solicitors and peddlers. Each solicitor or peddler who contemplates doing or does any business within the city limits shall be required to obtain a license from the city clerk in the manner specified in this article. The applicant must meet the zoning requirements as set forth in chapter 50 pertaining to the land development code. No license shall be issued to the applicant without the approval of the city manager.
(Ord. No. 4004, § 2, 7-11-2016)

Sec. 66-33. - Application.
Application for a license under this article shall be made as provided in chapter 86, article III except that such application shall contain the following additional information.
(1)
Name and address of present place of residence and length of residence at such address; also business address if other than present address;
(2)
Address of place of residence during the past three years if other than present address;
(3)
Age of applicant;
(4)
Name and address of the person firm or corporation or association whom the applicant is employed by or represents and the length of time of such employment or representation;
(5)
Name and address of employer during the past three years if other than the present employer;
(6)
Description sufficient for identification of the subject matter of the soliciting in which the applicant will engage;
(7)
Period of time for which the license is applied;
(8)
Proposed route, including streets to be included on each day, which applicant intends to follow if soliciting from house to house or road to road;
(9)
The date, or approximate date, of the latest previous application for license under this chapter if any;
(10)
Whether the applicant has ever been convicted of a felony, a crime of moral turpitude, or any other violation of any state or federal law;
(11)
Names of magazines, books, or journals to be sold;
(12)
Names of the three most recent communities where the applicant has solicited house to house, if any;
(13)
Proposed method of operation;
(14)
Description and license plate number of vehicle(s) intended to be operated by the applicant for the purpose of engaging in soliciting, if any;
(15)
Signature of applicant; and
(16)
Social Security number of applicant.
All statements made by the applicant upon the application or in connection therewith shall be under oath.
No license shall be issued to any person who has been convicted of a felony or crime of moral turpitude within five years of the date of the application; nor to any person who has been convicted of a violation of any of the provisions of this chapter; nor to any person whose license issued hereunder has previously been revoked as herein provided nor to any person who has been found to have made fraudulent statements on the application.
(Code 1989, § 9-3-3; Ord. No. 005-2007, 3-12-2007)
Sec. 66-34. - Investigation and issuance.
(a)
Upon receipt of an application for a license required by this article, the chief of police shall cause an investigation to be made of those items requested in section 66-33 of this article.
(b)
If, as a result of such investigation, the chief of police has determined that the applicant has been convicted of a felony or crime of moral turpitude within five years of the date of the application; has been convicted of a violation of any of the provisions of this chapter; has previously had his/her license, which was issued hereunder, revoked; or has been found to have made fraudulent statements on the application, then the chief of police shall endorse on such application his disapproval for the license and the reasons for the same, and notify the applicant that his application is disapproved and that no license will be issued. Any person aggrieved by a decision of the chief of police may appeal such decision to the city manager.
(c)
If as a result of such investigation,the chief of police finds the applicant's application to be satisfactory, the chief of police shall, upon receipt of payment of the prescribed license fee, deliver the license to the applicant. The chief of police shall keep in his office a permanent record of all licenses issued, denied, or revoked together with all information and data pertaining thereto.
(Code 1989, § 9-3-4; Ord. No. 005-2007, 3-12-2007)
Sec. 66-35. - Exhibition of license.
All persons obtaining a license under the provisions of this article shall be required to exhibit such license at the request of any police officer or citizen of the City of Woodstock.
(Code 1989, § 9-3-5; Ord. No. 005-2007, 3-12-2007)
Sec. 66-36. - Prohibited acts.
(a)
No licensee under this article, nor anyone in his behalf, shall shout, make any outcry, blow a horn, ring a bell or use any other sound device including any loudspeaking radio or amplifying system upon any of these streets, alleys, parks, or other public places of the city or upon any private premises in the city where sound of sufficient volume is emitted or produced therefrom capable of being plainly heard upon the streets, avenues, alleys, parks or other public places for the purpose of attracting attention to any goods, wares or merchandise which such licensee proposes to sell.
(b)
No licensee shall attempt to do business at any residence, office, or business clearly displaying a sign stating "No Solicitors or Peddlers Invited," "No Solicitation" or other similar language.
(c)
No licensee may engage in door to door soliciting on Sundays or between the hours of 8:00 p.m. and 9:00 a.m. on Mondays through Saturdays.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any licensee to enter any private dwelling without the consent of the owner or occupant, or to remain there after being requested or directed to leave.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for any licensee to fail to register and carry or produce for inspection a license when required under the provisions of this article.
(f)
It is unlawful for any solicitor or peddler to make any false or deceptive statement on any registration hereunder.
(Code 1989, § 9-3-6; Ord. No. 005-2007, 3-12-2007; Ord. No. 005-2007, 3-12-2007; Ord. No. 4004, § 3, 7-11-2016)
State Law reference— Operation of mechanical sound making device within motor vehicle plainly audible at 100 feet prohibited, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-14.
Sec. 66-37. - Revocation of license.
Licenses issued under the provisions of this article may be revoked for any of the following causes:
(1)
Fraud, misrepresentation, or false statement contained in the application for license;
(2)
Fraud, misrepresentation, or false statement made by a solicitor or peddler to another while pursuing any matter or transaction contemplated under this article;
(3)
Any violation of this article;
(4)
Conviction of any felony;
(5)
Conviction of any misdemeanor involving moral turpitude; or
(6)
Conducting any business contemplated under this article, in an unlawful manner or in such a manner as to constitute a breach of the peace or to constitute a breach of the peace or to constitute a menace to the health, safety, or general welfare of the public.
(Code 1989, § 9-3-7; Ord. No. 005-2007, 3-12-2007; Ord. No. 4004, § 4, 7-11-2016)
Sec. 66-38. - Exemptions.
modified
(a)
Any person who solicits or accepts money, donations, or financial assistance of any kind for any religious, charitable, educational, political, or nonprofit organization with a physical office or affiliation in Cherokee County; sells merchandise for a fee on behalf of such an organization; or who leaves leaflets or flyers or other papers related to such an organization shall be exempt from the registration and license requirements of this article, unless solicitation occurs from a public right-of-way, in which case a permit may be required per section 66-39. An individual who resides in Cherokee County, soliciting on behalf of any religious, charitable, educational, political, or nonprofit organization, is also exempt from the registration and license requirements of this article, but must comply with section 66-39.
(b)
Any person who calls upon homes, offices, or businesses at the request of or with the express permission of the occupants is exempt from the requirements of this article.
(c)
Any officer or employee of the city, county, state, or federal government, or any subdivision thereof is not subject to the provisions of this article when such officer or employee is on official business. Such persons who qualify for any exemption outlined in this section shall remain subject to the prohibitions set forth in sections 66-31 through 66-37.
(Ord. No. 005-2007, 3-12-2007; Ord. No. 4004, § 5, 7-11-2016; Ord. No. 7041-2021 , § 4, 4-12-2021)
Sec. 66-39. - Charitable solicitations from a public right-of-way.
(a)
Permit required. Any person soliciting funds on the roads and highways within the incorporated portions of the city shall have a permit issued by the governing authority of the city.
(b)
Eligibility. Permits for soliciting funds on the roads and highways within the incorporated portions of the city shall only be issued to those persons or organizations which have a physical office in Cherokee County. Further, permits for soliciting funds on the roads and highways within the incorporated portions of the city shall only be issued to those persons or organizations which are "charitable organizations" registered in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 43-17-5, or "charitable organizations" exempt from such registration in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 43-17-9. "Charitable organizations" as used herein shall have the meaning as defined in O.C.G.A § 43-17-2. The determination of whether the organizations have a physical office in Cherokee County shall be made by the staff issuing the permit based on documentation provided by the organization and documentation available on the Georgia Secretary of State's website and shall be appealable to the city manager. In the event the city manager upholds the decision to deny the permit, the city manager's decision shall be appealable to the mayor and council in the same manner as is set out in section 10.162 of the Land Development Code of the City of Woodstock.
(c)
Application. Applicants for charitable solicitation permits shall make application with the city development services office, and shall provide the following information at the time of their application:
(1)
Safety plans detailing safety measures to be utilized during solicitation;
(2)
The site of any proposed solicitation and traffic plans which indicate that their proposed solicitation will not hinder the free and orderly flow of traffic on the roads and highways in the city;
(3)
List of names, dates of birth, next of kin, emergency contact numbers, and address of each individual who will participate in the proposed solicitation;
(4)
Insurance information on the individuals and the organization which will participate in the solicitation;
(5)
A statement under oath that the organization is a charitable organization and that all proceeds raised will be used for the benefit of citizens to include those of the city; and
(6)
The dates and times proposed for the solicitation.
(d)
Issuance. Permits shall only be issued by the city development services office if he or she finds that the applicant is qualified and that the proposed solicitation shall not:
(1)
Pose a risk to the health and safety of the citizens of the city;
(2)
Pose a risk to the health and safety of the people participating in the solicitation; and
(3)
Hinder the free flow of traffic on the roads and highways of the city.
(e)
No one under the age of 18 shall solicit funds in any way from the right-of-way or driving surface of any road or highway in the city.
(f)
Solicitation permits must be available for inspection at the site of the solicitation upon the request of any law enforcement officer.
(g)
All persons participating in the solicitation of funds shall in some manner clearly and visibly identify the organization that is permitted to solicit funds and the organization for which they are soliciting funds.
(h)
All persons participating in the solicitation of funds shall wear orange safety vests at all times while located in the roads and rights-of-way of the city.
(i)
Permits are valid for seven calendar days. Solicitation may be between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 5:00 p.m.
(j)
Variances to time limits for charitable solicitation permits shall only be granted by a majority vote of the city council after a showing by the applicant of the necessity of a variance.
(k)
Any references to the Official Code of Georgia used herein shall be deemed to be to the same as they are amended or modified in the future.
(Ord. of 4-22-2013; Ord. of 4-25-2016)

ARTICLE IV. - FARMERS MARKETS

Sec. 66-111. - Definitions.
For the purposes of this section, the following words and phrases shall have the meanings given to them in this section, unless the context otherwise requires:
Employee. Any person employed by a qualified seller at a regular salary, either full- or part-time, but not including any person whose compensation in whole or in part is based, or consists of, a commission on sales.
Family member. A spouse, parent, child, sibling, grandparent, grandchild, or any other person regularly residing in the qualified seller's household.
Market. A farmers' market, a market certified by the city and location, including, where authorized, temporary use of streets and/or parkland.
Qualified seller. A person selling or offering for sale at the market an item or commodity for which she/he has:
(1)
Grown upon land which the person controls, in the case of fruits, nuts, vegetables or other plant products
(2)
Produce obtained from a reputable distributor may be re-sold at the market by a qualified seller.
(Ord. No. 98-05-26, 5-26-1998; Ord. No. 04-08-24, 8-24-2004)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 66-112. - Purpose.
(a)
To establish a legal framework for the operation of a farmers' market within the city;
(b)
To provide a flexible system of management over a farmers' market that is responsive to public input and acts in the interest of all residents of the city; sponsorship to be provided by the Woodstock Downtown Development Authority.
(c)
To meet the need for control over space allocation and enforcement of rules for selling at a farmers' market; and
(d)
To ensure that a farmers' market is successfully maintained primarily as an outlet for farmers to sell their produce directly to consumers on the designated dates set forth by the city.
(Ord. No. 98-05-26, 5-26-1998; Ord. No. 04-08-24, 8-24-2004)
Sec. 66-113. - Permits and licenses.
Proper permits and licenses will be solicited from the county extension and/or Georgia State Department of Agriculture. Permits are listed, but not limited to, the following:
(1)
Processed food permits are available through the Georgia Department of Agriculture, and pertain to any food that has been packaged. Proper labeling must accompany packaging.
(2)
Live plant growers license or live plant dealers license available through the Georgia Department of Agriculture. Pre-cut plants and flowers are exempt from this license, while plants and flowers with root systems must have been in the possession of the dealer for 60 days prior to the sale and proper licensing must be in hand.
(Ord. No. 98-05-26, 5-26-1998; Ord. No. 04-08-24, 8-24-2004)
Sec. 66-114. - Rules and regulations.
(a)
Vendors shall be local farmers and gardeners. Hand crafts shall not be allowed at this time. Entertainment such as local music talent, and special events such as cooking and growing demonstrations will be allowed. Any other vendor that does not fit into the aforementioned categories can contact the manager of the market to discuss their products. Prior written notice must be in hand at the market opening.
(b)
Products offered at market should be of the highest quality within the rules and regulations set forth by the Georgia Department of Agriculture such as, but not limited to: cleanliness, spoilage, and damage to the product.
(c)
Vendors must arrive thirty minutes prior to the opening time of the market. Sign-in, payment, and set-up of the booth must be completed by opening time. First-time vendors will be required to fill out a registration card prior to the market. Booth space is on a first come, first served basis, and top priority will be given to agricultural products.
(d)
The price of a booth will be $5.00 per event. Each vendor is allowed a booth of 12 feet frontage. Multiple booths may be purchased at an additional cost, but cannot exceed more than two spaces unless prior arrangements are made. A lump sum fee may be paid in advance if the vendor wants to take part in multiple markets. The market will be held rain or shine, and refunds of fees will not be given.
(e)
Vendors are responsible for their own messes. Clean-up facilities are not available at the market, so any refuse must be disposed of by the vendor. If refuse is not properly disposed of, the vendor will be subject to a fine of $25.00.
(f)
Pricing of goods will be done by volume or by piece (such as $2.00 for a pumpkin, or $2.00 for a peck of beans), not by weight. These rules are enforced by the Georgia State Department of Agriculture.
(g)
Behavior of vendors to consumers will be in a professional manner that fosters a sense of market community and camaraderie. Verbal or physical threats, foul language or gestures are grounds for immediate ejection from the market and possible permanent expulsion.
(h)
A complete listing of rules and regulations will be made available to vendors, city council, and public entities. Rules and regulations will include a system of warnings and fines for any infractions.
(Ord. No. 98-05-26, 5-26-1998; Ord. No. 04-08-24, 8-24-2004)
Sec. 66-115. - Permits to sell.
No vendor will be allowed to sell their product unless they are properly licensed by supporting governmental entities and are recognized by the city and the Woodstock Downtown Development Authority.
(Ord. No. 98-05-26, 5-26-1998; Ord. No. 04-08-24, 8-24-2004)
