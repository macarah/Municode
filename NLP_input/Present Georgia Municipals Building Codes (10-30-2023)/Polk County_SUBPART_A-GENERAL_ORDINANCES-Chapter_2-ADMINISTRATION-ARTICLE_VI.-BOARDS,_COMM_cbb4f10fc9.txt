DIVISION 2. - COUNTY DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.

Sec. 2-346. - Finding of need.
There is hereby determined and declared a pressing, existing and future need for a development authority (as more fully described and defined in the Development Authorities Law (O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.)) to function in the county for the purpose of developing and promoting for the public good and general welfare trade, commerce, industry and employment opportunities in said county thereby promoting the general welfare of its citizenry.
(Code 1974, § 6-16; Res. of 9-10-1970)
Sec. 2-347. - Created.
There is hereby activated in the county, the public body corporate and politic known as the Development Authority of Polk County which was created upon the adoption and approval of the Development Authorities Law (O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.) and particularly O.C.G.A. § 36-62-4.
(Code 1974, § 6-17; Res. of 9-10-1970)
Sec. 2-348. - Composition.
The development authority shall be composed of seven members, designated as directors, each of whom shall be a taxpayer of the county and none of whom shall be an officer or employee of this county.
(Code 1974, § 6-18; Res. of 9-10-1970)
State Law reference— Development authority composition, O.C.G.A. § 36-63-4.
Sec. 2-349. - Appointment.
(a)
The directors of the county development authority shall be appointed by the board of commissioners as follows: five citizens of the county are appointed as directors by the board of commissioners; one citizen nominated each by the City of Cedartown and the City of Rockmart and such nominations are to be confirmed as directors by the board of commissioners.
(b)
In addition, the following shall serve as ex-officio (non-voting) members of the authority:
(1)
A county commissioner appointed by the BOC chairman.
(2)
A representative appointed by the City of Cedartown.
(3)
A representative appointed by the City of Rockmart.
(4)
A representative appointed by the county water authority.
(5)
A representative appointed by the Georgia Power Company.
(6)
Other ex-officio members as deemed appropriate by the development authority board of directors.
(Code 1974, § 6-19; Res. of 9-10-1970; Ord. of 6-5-2012(2))
State Law reference— Appointment of members of development authority composition, O.C.G.A. § 36-63-4.
Sec. 2-350. - Terms.
Those persons appointed as directors of the development authority shall serve for terms of four years and until their successors have been appointed and qualified.
(Code 1974, § 6-20; Res. of 9-10-1970)
State Law reference— Terms of members of development authority, O.C.G.A. § 36-63-4.
Sec. 2-351. - Organization.
The board of directors of the development authority shall organize itself, carry out its duties and responsibilities and exercise its powers and prerogatives in accordance with the terms and provisions of the Development Authorities Law (O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.).
(Code 1974, § 6-21; Res. of 9-10-1970)
Sec. 2-352. - Exclusion.
Action taken by the board of commissioners as hereinbefore specified is not intended in any way to affect the Cedartown development authority, including, without limitation, its existence, purpose, organization, powers or function.
(Code 1974, § 6-22; Res. of 9-10-1970)

DIVISION 3. - JOINT DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY

Sec. 2-378. - Activation of the authority.
It is hereby declared that there is a need for a joint development authority to function in Polk County, Haralson County, Heard County and Carroll County. Such authority, to be known as the Joint Development Authority of Polk County, Haralson County, Heard County and Carroll County, is hereby created and activated. Said authority, herein called the authority, shall transact business pursuant to, and exercise the powers provided by the provisions of, the Development Authorities Law, codified in the O.C.G.A. title 36, ch. 62 (O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.).
(Res. of 12-6-1994, § 1)
Sec. 2-379. - Joint authority.
The authority is created and activated by this proper resolution of the governing bodies of Polk County, Haralson County, Heard County and Carroll County as a joint authority pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 36-62-5.1.
(Res. of 12-6-1994, § 1)
Sec. 2-380. - Directors.
(a)
The number of members of the authority shall be nine. Two members shall be taxpayers residing in the county and shall be appointed initially, as provided in this resolution and, thereafter, by the board of commissioners of the county as their terms expire. Two members shall be taxpayers residing in Haralson County and shall be appointed initially, as provided in this resolution and, thereafter, by the commissioner of Haralson County as their terms expire. Two members shall be taxpayers residing in Heard County and shall be appointed initially, as provided in this resolution and, thereafter, by the board of commissioners of Heard County as their terms expire. Two members shall be taxpayers residing in Carroll County and shall be appointed initially, as provided in this resolution and, thereafter, by the board of commissioners of Carroll County as their terms expire. The ninth member of the authority shall be appointed for an initial term of two years by the first governing body to approve this joint resolution, and shall be appointed thereafter at the expiration of each two-year term by the governing body next succeeding the governing body previously appointing such director, such succession determined as follows: commissioner of Haralson County shall succeed the board of commissioners of Carroll County, the board of commissioners of Heard County shall succeed the commissioner of Haralson County, the board of commissioners of Polk County shall succeed the board of commissioners of Heard County, and the board of commissioners of Carroll County shall succeed the board of commissioners of Polk County. No director shall be an officer or employee of Carroll County, Haralson County, Heard County or Polk County. The members shall receive no compensation for their services, but shall be reimbursed for their actual expenses incurred in the performance of their duties.
(b)
After expiration of the initial terms, the terms of office of all members shall be terms of two years. Successors to the persons listed above shall be appointed by the governing body of Carroll County, Haralson County, Heard County and Polk County, as applicable, shown opposite such person's name as shown above. If at the end of any term of any member, a successor to such member has not been appointed, the member whose term of office has expired shall continue to hold office until his successor is appointed. A majority of the members of the authority shall constitute a quorum, but no action may be taken by the authority without the affirmative vote of a majority of the full membership of the authority.
(Res. of 12-6-1994, § 3)
Sec. 2-381. - Officers.
The directors shall elect one of their members as chairman and another as vice-chairman and shall also elect a secretary and a treasurer or a secretary-treasurer, either of who may, but need not be, a director.
(Res. of 12-6-1994, § 4)

DIVISION 4. - CEDARTOWN-POLK COUNTY HOSPITAL AUTHORITY

Sec. 2-406. - Finding of need.
There is a need for a hospital authority to function in the county, as contemplated under the Hospital Authorities Law (O.C.G.A. § 31-7-70 et seq.) .
(Code 1974, § 7-16; Res. of 5-3-1946)
Sec. 2-407. - Created.
There is hereby created and constituted a public body corporate and politic to be known as the "Cedartown-Polk County Hospital Authority" with all rights, powers, privileges and immunities provided for in the Hospital Authorities Law (O.C.G.A. § 31-7-70 et seq.).
(Code 1974, § 7-17; Res. of 5-3-1946; Res. of 10-25-1949)
Sec. 2-408. - Composition.
The board of trustees provided for in O.C.G.A. § 31-7-72 shall consist of seven citizens of the county.
(Code 1974, § 7-18; Res. of 5-3-1946)
Sec. 2-409. - Election of trustees.
All trustees shall be elected by the other trustees.
(Code 1974, § 7-20; Res. of 5-3-1946)
Sec. 2-410. - Terms.
Each trustee shall be elected to serve for a term of seven years and until his successor is elected and qualified.
(Code 1974, § 7-21; Res. of 5-3-1946)
Sec. 2-411. - Vacancies.
All vacancies in the membership of said board of trustees, whether by expiration of term or otherwise, shall be filled by the remaining trustees, and any trustee elected to fill an unexpired term shall serve for the remaining portion of such term, and until his successor is elected and qualified.
(Code 1974, § 7-22; Res. of 5-3-1946)

Sec. 2-436. - Finding of need.
There is a need for one regional housing authority to be created for Bartow, Catoosa, Chattooga, Dade, Floyd, Gordon, Haralson, Paulding, Polk and Walker counties to exercise in such counties powers and other functions prescribed for a regional housing authority.

The name of the regional housing authority shall be the Coosa Valley Regional Housing Authority.
(Code 1974, § 8-17; Res. of 11-3-1970, § 3)
Sec. 2-438. - Member appointed.
The board of commissioners shall appoint one member to the regional housing authority. (Code 1974, § 8-18; Res. of 11-3-1970, § 2)
