ARTICLE III. - LICENSE FEES AND GROSS PREMIUM TAXES ON INSURERS[3]

Sec. 12-53. - Insurers license fee.
There is hereby levied an annual license fee upon each insurer doing business within the City of Hiram, Georgia in the amount of $50.00. For each separate business location in excess of one not covered by section 12-54, which is operating on behalf of such insurers within the city there is hereby levied a license fee in the amount of $50.00. For the purposes of this section, the term "insurer" means a company which is authorized to transact business in any of the classes of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5.
(Code 1996, § 18-81; Ord. No. 11-123, § 1, 9-6-2011; Ord. No. 2022-09, 10-4-2022)
Sec. 12-54. - License fee for life insurers insuring certain risks at additional business locations.
For each separate business location, not otherwise subject to a license fee hereunder, operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing and in connection with such loans or sales offers, solicits or takes application for insurance through a licensed agent of an insurer for insurance said insurer shall pay an additional license fee of $14.00 per location.
(Code 1996, § 18-82; Ord. No. 11-123, § 2, 9-6-2011)
Sec. 12-55. - Life insurance agency license fees; independent life insurance agencies and brokers not otherwise licensed.
There is hereby levied an annual license fee upon independent agencies and brokers for each separate business location from which a life insurance business is conducted and which is not subject to the company license fee imposed by section 12-53, in an amount set forth in section 12-20.
(Code 1996, § 18-83; Ord. No. 84-33, § 3, 11-7-1983)
Sec. 12-56. - Gross premiums tax—Imposed on life insurers.
There is hereby levied an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer writing life, accident and sickness insurance within the state in an amount equal to one percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.1. The term "gross direct premiums," as used in this section, means gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is an addition to the license fees imposed by section 12-53.
(Code 1996, § 18-84; Ord. No. 11-123, § 3, 9-6-2011)
Sec. 12-57. - Same—Levied upon all other insurers.
There is hereby levied an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer, other than an insurer transacting business in the class of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5(1), doing business within the State of Georgia in an amount equal to 2½ percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8-2. The term "gross direct premiums," as used in this section, means gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 12-53.
(Code 1996, § 18-85; Ord. No. 11-123, § 4, 9-6-2011)
Sec. 12-58. - Due date for license fees.
License fees imposed in sections 12-53 and 12-54 shall be due and payable on January 1, of each year.
(Code 1996, § 18-86; Ord. No. 11-123, § 5, 9-6-2011)
Sec. 12-59. - Administrative provisions.
The city clerk is hereby directed to forward a duly certified copy of this article to the state insurance commissioner within 45 days of the enactment of the ordinance from which this article is derived.
(Ord. No. 11-123, § 6, 9-6-2011)
