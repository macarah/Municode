DIVISION 4. - INDUSTRIAL DEVELOPMENT COMMISSION

Sec. 2-186. - Created.
There is hereby created within the city an industrial development commission (referred to in this division as the commission), which shall consist of seven members who are residents of the city, each of whom shall be elected by a majority vote of the mayor and council.
(Res. of 6-21-79)
Sec. 2-187. - Members; designated posts, terms, compensation.
Each member of the commission shall be elected to a specific post, the posts to be designated by numbers one through seven. The term of the members shall be for three years, except that in the appointment of the first members of the commission pursuant to this division, the directors chosen to fill post one and post two shall be elected for a term expiring May 31, 1980; the members chosen to fill post three and post four shall be elected for a term expiring May 31, 1981; and the members chosen to fill post five, post six and post seven shall be elected for terms expiring May 31, 1982; and thereafter, the terms of all members shall be three years. Any vacancy in membership shall be filled for the unexpired term by appointment by the mayor and council as hereinbefore provided for full terms. The members of the commission shall serve without compensation, but may be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in the performance of their duties.
(Res. of 6-21-79)
Sec. 2-188. - Organization, meetings.
The members of the commission shall elect a chairman and a secretary, hold meetings at least once per month, and establish such rules and regulations as it shall deem necessary and proper for the carrying on of its function. Any member who fails, without good cause, to attend any two consecutive regular meetings of the commission may be removed by majority vote of the mayor and council. The commission shall keep minutes of each meeting, and shall provide a copy of such minutes to the city clerk.
(Res. of 6-21-79)
Sec. 2-189. - Duties, powers.
It shall be the duty and power of the commission to promote the development of the city; to encourage desirable industries to locate within the city limits of the city; to assist and advise the mayor and council in all matters concerning the promotion of tourism and trade within the city; to further the development of the industrial park area heretofore set aside by the mayor and council; and to develop and promote the public good and general welfare, trade, commerce, industry and employment opportunities available in the city.
(Res. of 6-21-79)
Secs. 2-190—2-210. - Reserved.
DIVISION 5. - PLANNING COMMISSION[7]

Footnotes:
--- (7) ---
Cross reference— Historical preservation, ch. 62; utilities, ch. 98; zoning, ch. 110.
State Law reference— Authority to adopt plans and exercise the power of zoning, Ga. Const. art. 9, sec. 2, par. 4; The Zoning Procedures Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-66-1 et seq.; local government zoning powers, O.C.G.A. § 36-66-2; conflicts of interest in zoning actions, O.C.G.A. § 36-67A-1 et seq.; coordinated and comprehensive planning by counties and municipalities, O.C.G.A. § 36-70-1 et seq.; effect of zoning laws on covenants running with the land, O.C.G.A. § 44-5-60.

Sec. 2-211. - Created.
In order to guide and accomplish a coordinated and harmonious development of the city which will, in accordance with existing and future needs, best promote the public health, safety, morals, order, convenience, prosperity and the general welfare, a city planning commission is hereby created and established as authorized by the Zoning Procedures Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-66-1 et seq. The planning commission shall be organized and empowered as set out in this division.
(Ord. of 1-3-63)
Sec. 2-212. - Membership.
The planning commission shall consist of five members, who shall be residents of the city appointed by the city council. The terms of the members shall be for four years, except that in the appointment of the first planning commission under the terms of this division, the first member shall be appointed for a term of two years, the third member shall be appointed for three years and the remaining members shall be appointed for terms of four years each. Any vacancy in membership shall be filled for the unexpired term by the city council which shall also have the authority to remove any member for cause, or written charges, after a public hearing. All members shall serve without compensation but may be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in connection with their official duties. Effective immediately upon passage, any member of the planning commission who has more than three unexcused absences within a period of 12 consecutive months shall be deemed to have abandoned his duties and relinquished his position. His seat shall be filled in the same manner as any other vacancy. An absence may be excused by a majority vote of the planning commission any time prior to abandonment.
(Ord. of 1-3-63; Ord. of 9-3-81; Ord. No. 2021-6-7-004 , 6-7-21)
Sec. 2-213. - Organization, rules, staff and finances.
(a)
The planning commission shall elect its chairman from among its members. The term of the chairman shall be one year with eligibility for reelection. The planning commission shall appoint a secretary, who may be an officer or employee of the city. The planning commission shall make its own rules of procedure and determine its time of meeting. All meetings of the planning commission at which official action is taken shall be open to the public and all records of the planning commission shall be a public record. No vote shall be taken by the planning commission on any matter unless at least three members are present when the vote is taken.
(b)
The planning commission may appoint such employees and staff as it may deem necessary for its work and may contract with the state planning agency and city planners and other consultants for such services as it may require. The expenditures of the planning commission, exclusive of gifts, shall be within the amounts appropriated for the purpose by the council.
(Ord. of 1-3-63; Ord. of 9-3-81; Ord of 11-24-08(1), § 1)
Sec. 2-214. - Powers and duties.
The planning commission shall have all the powers, duties and responsibilities set forth in the Zoning Procedures Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-66-1 et seq.
(Ord. of 1-3-63)
Secs. 2-215—2-230. - Reserved.
DIVISION 6. - RECREATION COMMISSION[8]

Footnotes:
--- (8) ---
Cross reference— Library, ch. 66.
State Law reference— Power to provide parks, recreation areas, programs and facilities, Ga. Const. art. 9, sec. 2, par. 3(a)(5); authority to construct and operate parks, swimming pools, golf courses, recreation grounds and buildings used for sports, O.C.G.A. § 36-34-3; establishment of recreation systems, O.C.G.A. § 36-64-1 et seq.; state commission on physical fitness created, O.C.G.A. § 50-12-40 et seq.

Sec. 2-231. - Creation.
There is created a commission the title of which shall be the "St. Marys Recreation Commission." The recreation commission shall be considered a part of the administration of the city.
(Ord. of 11-9-87)
Sec. 2-232. - Members; appointment, terms, compensation.
The recreation commission shall consist of seven members appointed as follows: One member shall be appointed by the mayor, and one member shall be appointed by each one of the city councilmembers. The members shall be residents of the city and have a demonstrated interest, expertise or knowledge in public recreation. Members shall be appointed to serve three-year terms. Members may not serve more than two consecutive terms. In order to achieve continuity on the commission, the initial board of commissioners shall be appointed as follows: Two members for one year; two members for two years; and three members for three years. Members shall not receive a salary for their service although they may be reimbursed for out-of-pocket expenses.
(Ord. of 11-9-87)
Sec. 2-233. - Powers.
The recreation commission shall have the power to:
(1)
Recommend to the mayor and council specific plans, programs, expenditures and any other recommendations deemed to be necessary for the promotion of recreational activities within the city.
(2)
Perform any function delegated to it by the mayor and council for the purpose of providing recreation for the city and the citizens of the city.
(Ord. of 11-9-87)
Sec. 2-234. - Rules of procedure, organization.
(a)
The recreation commission shall adopt rules for the transaction of its business and consideration of all matters coming before it; shall provide for the time and place of regular meetings, and for the calling of special meetings.
(b)
The commission shall have the flexibility to adopt rules of procedure without amendment to this division. A quorum shall consist of a majority of the members. The commission shall select a chairman and such officers as it deems appropriate from among its members.
(Ord. of 11-9-87)
Sec. 2-235. - Records of meetings.
A public record shall be kept of the recreation commission's resolutions, proceedings and actions.
(Ord. of 11-9-87)
Sec. 2-236. - Removal of members.
If a member of the recreation commission misses more than three consecutive meetings in one year without written notification to the chairman of the commission, he shall be automatically taken off the commission and the mayor and city council shall appoint a replacement. The mayor or city councilmember whose appointee has been removed shall make the replacement.
(Ord. of 11-9-87)
Secs. 2-237—2-255. - Reserved.
