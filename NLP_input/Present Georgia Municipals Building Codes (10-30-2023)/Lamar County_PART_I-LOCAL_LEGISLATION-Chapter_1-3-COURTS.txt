ARTICLE II. - MAGISTRATE COURT[1]

Footnotes:
--- (1) ---
State Law reference— Magistrate courts, O.C.G.A. § 15-10-1 et seq.

Sec. 1-3-26. - Chief magistrate.
(a)
The chief magistrate shall be elected by the voters of the county in a nonpartisan election at the general election next preceding the expiration of the term of the incumbent chief magistrate.
(b)
The chief magistrate in office on January 1, 1992, shall continue to serve the remainder of the term to which he was appointed, which term shall expire on December 31, 1992, and upon the election and qualification of a successor pursuant to subsection (a) of this section.
(Acts 1984, p. 4561, § 1; Acts 1990, p. 4022, § 1; Acts 1991, p. 3819, § 1)
Sec. 1-3-27. - Appointment of other magistrates.
Any magistrates of Lamar County other than the chief magistrate shall be appointed by the chief magistrate with the approval of the chief judge of superior court of Lamar County.
(Acts 1984, p. 4561, § 2)
Sec. 1-3-28. - Additional court costs for benefit of law library.
(a)
The collection of additional costs in cases before the magistrate court of Lamar County shall be made for the benefit of the Lamar County law library. The amount of such fees shall not exceed three dollars ($3.00). The amount of such fees shall be determined by the senior judge of the superior court of Lamar County.
(b)
The clerk of the magistrate court of Lamar County shall collect the law library fees and remit the same each month to the treasurer of the board of trustees for the law library.
(Acts 1984, p. 4561, § 2A; Acts 1985, p. 4437, § 1)

ARTICLE III. - PROBATE COURT[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Probate court, O.C.G.A. § 15-9-1 et seq.

Sec. 1-3-41. - Fee system of compensation abolished.
The present mode of compensating the probate judge of Lamar County, known as the fee system, is hereby abolished, and, in lieu thereof, an annual salary for the probate judge is prescribed as hereinafter provided.
(Acts 1972, p. 2636, § 1)
Sec. 1-3-42. - Fees, etc., to be property of county.
After the effective date of this Act [January 1, 1973], the probate judge shall diligently and faithfully undertake to collect all fees, fines, forfeitures, commissions, costs, allowances, penalties, funds, moneys and all other emoluments and perquisites formerly allowed as compensation for services in any capacity, and shall receive and hold the same in trust for said county as public moneys, and shall pay the same into the county treasury on or before the fifteenth day of each month next following the month in which they were collected or received. At the time of each monthly payment into the county treasury, said officer shall furnish the governing authority of the county a detailed, itemized statement, under oath, of all such funds received during the preceding month by such officer and paid into the county treasury. The statement shall show the respective amounts of money collected and the source thereof.
(Acts 1972, p. 2636, § 3)
Sec. 1-3-43. - Salary to be in lieu of fees, etc.
It is specifically provided that the salary provided herein for the probate judge shall be in lieu of all fees, commissions, costs, fines, emoluments and perquisites of whatever kind.
(Acts 1972, p. 2636, § 4)
State Law reference— Minimum salary fixed, O.C.G.A. § 15-9-63 et seq.
Sec. 1-3-44. - Personnel generally.
The judge of the probate court [of] Lamar County shall also be clerk of said office but may also employ a deputy clerk and such other personnel to assist him in discharging the official duties of his office as he shall deem proper. The compensation of such deputy clerk and other personnel shall be paid weekly from county funds, as approved by the board of commissioners.
(Acts 1972, p. 2636, § 5; Acts 1975, p. 3365, § 2)
Sec. 1-3-45. - Work assignments of personnel; hiring and firing of personnel.
It shall be within the sole power and authority of said officer [the judge of the probate court], during his term of office, to designate and name the person or persons who shall be employed, and to prescribe their duties and assignments, and to remove or replace any of such employees at will and within his sole discretion.
(Acts 1972, p. 2636, § 6)
Sec. 1-3-46. - Bonds.
The official bonds of said officer [judge of the probate court] and the deputy clerk, assistants and other personnel, as may be required by law, shall be procured by said elected officer, and the premiums and cost thereof shall be paid out of any county funds available for that purpose.
(Acts 1972, p. 2636, § 8)
Sec. 1-3-47. - Expenses.
The necessary operating expenses of said office [of probate judge], expressly including the compensation of all personnel and employees, shall be paid from the funds of the county available for such purpose. All supplies, material, furnishings, furniture, utilities and equipment, and the repair, replacement and maintenance thereof, as may be reasonably required in discharging the official duties of said office, shall be furnished by the county and shall be paid from any funds of the county available for such purpose.
(Acts 1972, p. 2636, § 7)
Secs. 1-3-48—1-3-60. - Reserved.

DIVISION 2. - CLERK[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— Clerk of superior court generally, O.C.G.A. § 15-6-50 et seq.

Sec. 1-3-71. - Fee system abolished.
The present mode of compensating the clerk of the superior court of Lamar County is abolished and in lieu thereof an annual salary for such officer is prescribed as hereinafter provided.
(Acts 1984, p. 3904, § 1)
State Law reference— Minimum salary of clerk of superior court, O.C.G.A. § 15-6-88 et seq.
Sec. 1-3-72. - Fees, etc., to property to county.
After the effective date of this act, said officer [the clerk of the superior court] shall diligently and faithfully undertake to collect all fees, fines, forfeitures, commissions, costs, allowances, penalties, funds, moneys, and all other emoluments and perquisites formerly allowed as compensation for services in any capacity in his office and shall receive and hold the same in trust for said county as public moneys and shall pay the same into the county treasury on or before the fifteenth day of each month next following the month in which they were collected or received. At the time of each such monthly payment into the county treasury, said officer shall furnish the governing authority of the county a detailed, itemized statement, under oath, of such funds received during the preceding month by such officer and paid into the county treasury. The statement shall show the respective amounts of money collected and the source thereof.
(Acts 1984, p. 3904, § 3)
Sec. 1-3-73. - Personnel.
The clerk [of the superior court] shall have the authority to appoint such deputies, clerks, assistants, and other personnel as he shall deem necessary to efficiently and effectively discharge the official duties of his office. The clerk shall, from time to time, recommend to the governing authority of said county the number of such personnel needed by his office, together with the suggested compensation to be paid each employee; however, it shall be within the sole discretion of the governing authority of Lamar County to fix the compensation to be received by each employee in said office. It shall be within the sole power and authority of the clerk to designate and name the person or persons who shall be employed as such deputies, clerks, assistants, or other employees, and to prescribe their duties and assignments and to remove or replace any of such employees at will and within his sole discretion.
(Acts 1984, p. 3904, § 4)
Sec. 1-3-74. - Expenses.
The necessary operating expenses of the clerk's office, expressly including the compensation of all personnel and employees, shall be paid from any funds of the county available for such purpose. All supplies, materials, furnishings, furniture, utilities, and equipment and the repair, replacement, and maintenance thereof as may be reasonably required in discharging the official duties of said office shall be furnished by the county and shall be paid from any fund of the county available for such purpose. The determination of such requirements for said office shall be at the sole discretion of the governing authority of Lamar County.
(Acts 1984, p. 3904, § 5)
