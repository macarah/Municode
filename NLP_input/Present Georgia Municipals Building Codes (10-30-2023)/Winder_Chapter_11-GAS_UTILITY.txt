Sec. 11-1. - Scope of chapter.
The provisions of this chapter shall be incorporated into and be binding upon every applicant for gas service from the city, and it shall be the duty of said applicant to file an agreement with the city incorporating such provisions and agreeing to comply therewith before gas service shall be granted said applicant.
(Ord. (W-11-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 11-2. - Duty to comply with rules and regulations.
An applicant for gas service shall observe all the rules and regulations prescribed by the city relative to gas service, including without limitation, the time, method and manner of installing and maintaining equipment, payment of bills and discontinuance of service.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 11-3. - Rates and charges not affected by Code.
Nothing in this Code or the ordinance adopting this Code shall affect any ordinance relating to rates and charges under this chapter, and all such ordinances are hereby recognized as continuing in full force and effect to the same extent as if set out at length in this Code.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 11-4. - Deposit before service turned on—Required.
Before gas service is turned on and city utility services are provided, utility deposits shall be made with the city according to a schedule of deposits and subject to exceptions and provisions as adopted in policy.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 11-5. - Deposit—Refund.
(a)
If a deposit is required, it shall be refunded without interest, or applied as a credit to an open account, at the end of twelve (12) consecutive months of on-time and in-full payment of the bills for all gas services. Any remainder shall also be refunded without interest upon termination of service by the customer and payment in-full of all outstanding charges.
(b)
Due date. All refunds shall be paid or credited to an open account within thirty (30) days after the finance director or designee determines that such refund is due.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 11-6. - Connection of service; use of gas without authorized service.
(a)
No person except personnel of the City of Winder's natural gas system shall at any time turn on any valve or cock or in any way tamper or make any connection with any natural gas main, meter, meter connection or gas service pipe between said main and meter. The user of such system, however, shall be authorized to turn off gas service at the main jet valve, in the event of an emergency.
(b)
In the event that authorized city employees have discontinued service by placing a lock on the meter of any premises, no person occupying the premises as his primary place of residence (as tenant, owner or otherwise) shall use or knowingly allow another person to use city gas until and unless such use is authorized according to the records kept for such purpose in city hall. Resumption of the use of gas without authorization and payment for such gas in instances when city employees have placed a lock on the meter shall constitute prima facie evidence of intent to use the gas commodity without payment and without authority.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Cross reference— Municipal court, Ch. 15.5.
Sec. 11-7. - Tampering and theft of services.
(a)
Under no circumstances is any customer or individual authorized to turn gas back on or alter services if gas service has been disconnected by the city. Only authorized city staff or contracted employees may connect services or work on city lines.
(b)
All persons are prohibited from interfering with or disturbing in any manner or form, any gas meters, radio equipment, switches, locks, cutoffs or other appliances of the city including fire hydrants, or to appropriate or use any gas after service has been cut off or discontinued for nonpayment or in order to avoid payment.
(c)
The property owner, legal tenant or person(s) noted on the utility account shall be held responsible for the violation of this section. A tampering fine of five hundred dollars ($500.00) shall be assessed where a meter or gas line has been disturbed. A tampering fine of seven hundred and fifty dollars ($750.00) shall be assessed for tampering with a fire hydrant or any fire meter.
(d)
The City of Winder reserves the right to prosecute in the accordance with established laws after any tampering of gas services has been discovered.
(e)
If, after removal of service, an alternative means of obtaining gas service is attempted, then the gas service to that property will be removed until such time as the case can appear and be settled before the city's municipal court.
(f)
If the city determines theft of service has occurred, it reserves the right to adjust the customer's current bill and the bills for the past six (6) months usage. If the approximate amount of service that was stolen cannot be reasonably determined, the customer's usage will be set at three (3) times the average volume.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 11-8. - Damage to meter and radio equipment.
(a)
Customers shall be responsible to ensure that city equipment is not damaged through the routine maintenance adjacent to city equipment/property. The property owner may be deemed responsible for damages to city meters and equipment.
(b)
The city reserves the right to assess the cost of the damaged equipment to the utility account.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 11-9. - Responsibility for damages and maintenance to service lines.
The city shall in no event be responsible for maintaining any service line owned by the consumer, nor for damage done by gas escaping therefrom, nor for defects in lines or fixtures, on the property of the consumer. The consumer shall at all times comply with all regulations of the city and of regulations relating to the service lines and shall make all changes in the line as required.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 11-10. - Leaks; repair of leaks.
Whenever it comes to the attention of the utilities department that any service connection upon the property of any owner is leaking, the utilities department shall immediately disconnect gas service and serve written notice upon the property owner requiring the owner to repair the condition. Gas service will remain disconnected until the owner has corrected the leak and the city has verified that the leak has been corrected.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 11-11. - Suspension of service.
(a)
When gas service is discontinued and all bills paid, the security deposit shall be refunded to the consumer by the city.
(b)
Upon discontinuance of service for nonpayment of bills, the security deposit will be applied by the city toward the settlement of the account. Any balance will be refunded to the consumer; however, if the security deposit is insufficient to cover the bill, the city may proceed to collect the balance in the usual way provided by law for collection of debts.
(c)
The city reserves the right to discontinue its service without notice for the following additional reasons:
(1)
To prevent fraud or abuse;
(2)
Consumer's willful disregard of the city's policy;
(3)
Emergency repairs;
(4)
Insufficiency of gas supply due to circumstances beyond the city's control;
(5)
Legal processes;
(6)
Direction of public authorities;
(7)
Strike, riot, fire, flood or unavoidable accident.
(d)
Service disconnected for nonpayment of bills will be restored only after bills are paid in full, including the service charge (delinquent fee), in an amount set forth in the schedule of fees and charges to be paid for each meter reconnected.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 11-12. - Tap fee.
A nonrefundable fee for connection to the city gas service shall be fixed from time to time by the mayor and council.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 11-13. - Location of meter generally.
No gas meter shall be installed or maintained under the floor of any building or structure unless there is suitable ventilation. No gas meter shall be installed or maintained in a small, unvented or confined space.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 11-14. - Multiple service and rental properties.
When more than one (1) meter is required to serve consumers in one (1) building or structure, the city, at its discretion, may set as many meters as there are separate consumers, connecting such meters to one (1) service line. For any structure or building being used as residential rental property with lease terms of less than one (1) year, the city, in its discretion, may require the building or structure to be served by a master meter in the owner's name, to be installed by the owner's expense, or, the city may require that all accounts for all rental units remain in the property owner's name.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 11-15. - Moving meter to different location.
No gas meter shall be moved from one (1) location of a city gas customer to another such location when the customer makes such a move.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 11-16. - Charges for gas service.
Any person applying for or receiving gas service from the city shall promptly pay the bills and service charges fixed from time to time by the mayor and council.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 11-17. - Right of entry.
The city shall have the right to enter upon premises at any reasonable time for the purpose of servicing its natural gas equipment, reading meters, discontinuing service, removing gas service pipe and meters or for any other reason necessary and appropriate to the conduct of its business as a natural gas distributor.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 11-18. - Ownership of gas meters and service lines.
(a)
The City of Winder retains all ownership of gas meters connected to the city's gas system.
(b)
No applicant named on the utility bill or person associated with the property connected to the gas distribution system, shall sell, transfer, or assign gas rights to the City of Winder's system unless authorized in writing by the City of Winder.
(c)
Only city employees or those directly authorized by the city may work on gas meters, gas lines maintained by the city, and/or tap into city gas lines.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 11-19. - Gas service policies.
In addition to all other provisions of this chapter, gas services shall also be subject to such gas service policies adopted by the mayor and council from time to time.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 11-20. - Customers outside the city limits.
(a)
Gas customers located outside the corporate boundaries of the City of Winder are subject to the rules and regulations contained in this chapter.
(b)
The property owners shall pay all applicable tap fees to make the gas available to the property.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 11-21. - Penalties.
(a)
Civil penalties. Violations of this chapter may be prosecuted upon citations issued by officers of the city police department. The violation of any provision of this chapter shall be punishable by a fine for each offense or violation not to exceed one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) for each offense or violation.
(b)
Criminal penalties. Knowingly violating any provision of this chapter which poses or could pose a threat to public health or safety shall be considered a criminal act and shall be punishable by imprisonment and fines commensurate with the risk to the public as determined by the judiciary.
(c)
Additional penalties. In addition to civil and criminal penalties, any person violating any provision of this chapter may be liable to pay restitution in the discretion of the court for:
(1)
The total cost incurred by the city to repair or replace facilities damaged as a result of the violation.
(2)
Any injury or damage to person or property or loss of services resulting from the violation.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 11-22. - Collection costs for delinquent accounts.
Customers that have a past due balance after disconnection are subject to being referred to collection agencies and are required to pay all fees as permitted by law.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 11-23. - Charges constituting lien.
The charges for gas shall be a lien upon the account holder as provided for in the charter of the city. Whenever a bill for gas service remains unpaid sixty (60) days after it has been disconnected, the city clerk may file with the clerk of the Superior Court of Barrow County, a statement of lien claim. This statement shall contain a legal description of the premises served, the amount of the unpaid bill, and a notice that the city claims a lien for this amount as well as for all charges for gas service subsequent to the period covered by the bill.
(Ord. (W-11-1) of 10-21-13, § 1)
