ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 50-1. - Composition of police department.
The police department shall consist of a chief and such officers and employees of such ranks and positions as approved by the mayor and council.
(Code 1982, § 4-1-1)
Sec. 50-2. - Responsibilities of police department.
The police department shall be responsible for patrol, traffic control, investigation of accidents, investigation of crimes, apprehension of offenders, court appearances, security of business establishments, and any other matters of public safety and law enforcement as directed by the mayor and council.
(Code 1982, § 4-1-2)
Sec. 50-3. - Fees for reproduction of police records, fingerprinting, criminal record checks and other police services.
In all cases where fees are charged to the public for police services, including, but not limited to, reproducing copies of reports, fingerprinting and providing criminal history record checks, said fees shall be charged to such person as fixed from time to time by the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. 364, 7-10-92; Ord. No. 410, 12-19-95; Ord. No. 512, 12-18-01)
Secs. 50-4—50-20. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - PROPERTY IN POSSESSION OF POLICE DEPARTMENT[2]

--- (2) ---

Sec. 50-21. - Sale authorized.
The police department shall have the authority to sell at public auction to the highest bidder for cash any and all articles of personal property that have come into the possession of the police department and which, after a reasonable effort has been made to ascertain the true and lawful owner thereof without success, remain unclaimed for a period of at least three months.
(Ord. No. 384, 11-12-93)
Sec. 50-22. - Record; identification of property.
The police department shall maintain a separate catalog record of all property coming into its possession, such property to be identified in detail by its usual name, make and model, manufacturer's name and serial number, if any, and if no identifying name or numbers, to be fully described on such catalog record as to its physical properties and appearance. Such record shall also show the date such property came into the possession of the department and the disposal thereof.
(Ord. No. 384, 11-12-93)
Sec. 50-23. - Attempt to find owner.
It shall be the duty of the police department to make a reasonable effort to find the true owner of any article of personal property coming into its possession and if such owner can be found to deliver such property to him. If the true owner of any such article cannot be found and the property remains unclaimed for a period of not less than three months next preceding the date of sale as hereinafter provided, the department may proceed to sell such article as hereinafter provided.
(Ord. No. 384, 11-12-93)
Sec. 50-24. - Location, manner of sale.
The police department may hold a public sale of all unclaimed articles of personal property held by it for at least three months immediately preceding the date of sale at the headquarters of the police department or at such other place as designated by the mayor and council. The date, time and location of such sale shall be established by the mayor and city council. All articles sold shall be sold for cash to the highest bidder on an "as is" basis, and no warranties of any nature, either express or implied, either as to merchantability, title, quality, or performance, or fitness for a particular purpose, shall be made by the police department.
(Ord. No. 384, 11-12-93)
Sec. 50-25. - Notice of sale.
The governing authority of the city shall cause notice to be published once in the official legal organ of the county in which the city is located or in a newspaper of general circulation in the community, not less than 15 days nor more than 60 days preceding the day of the auction. The legal notice of sale shall include a general description of the property to be sold. The notice shall also contain the conditions of the proposed sale and shall state the date, time, and place of the proposed sale. The notice shall also state that the sale shall be open to the public and that all sales are to be made to the highest bidder for cash. Such notice shall also state that only unclaimed property which has come into possession of the police department is being sold; that the police department makes no warranties whatsoever either expressly or implied as to merchantability, merchantable title, quality or performance, or fitness for a particular purpose, and all articles are sold "as is."
(Ord. No. 384, 11-12-93)
Sec. 50-26. - Auctioneer.
The police department shall appoint a licensed auctioneer, a member of its own force or any other individual, to conduct the sale authorized by0this article under the guidance of an employee of the police department and more than one such person may act at any sale, either alternately or at the same time as the circumstances may dictate and in the discretion of the employee of the department who is in charge of the sale. The auctioneer or other individual acting shall be the sole arbiter in the case of a disputed sale and he shall not make a sale until all bidding on the article offered has ceased; however, there shall be no required number of bids before a sale is made.
(Ord. No. 384, 11-12-93)
Sec. 50-27. - Sales not warranted.
It shall be the duty of the auctioneer or other person holding a sale under this chapter to make public announcement before opening such sale that the police department does not warrant title to any article, nor is there any other warranty, either as to merchantability or fitness for a particular purpose, either expressed or implied as to any article sold, that all sales are final and all articles are sold "as is." It shall be the duty of such auctioneer or other person in charge to make this announcement at intervals during the progress of the sale.
(Ord. No. 384, 11-12-93)
Sec. 50-28. - Accounting, disposition of receipts.
It shall be the duty of the police department to make a true accounting to the city of all the property sold under this article and money collected from the sale conducted as provided herein, which money shall be paid into the general fund of the city.
(Ord. No. 384, 11-12-93)
