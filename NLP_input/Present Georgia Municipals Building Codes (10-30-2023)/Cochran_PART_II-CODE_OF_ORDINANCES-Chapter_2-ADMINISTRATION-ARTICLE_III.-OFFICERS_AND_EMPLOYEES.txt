DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-107. - Adoption of personnel policies.
(a)
The mayor and council has adopted the city personnel policy and procedure manual that provides rules, regulations, and policies consistent with the city's Charter concerning:
(1)
The method of employee selection and probationary periods of employment;
(2)
The administration of a position classification and pay plan, and methods of promotion and transfer within the classification plan;
(3)
Hours of work, vacation, sick leave and other leaves of absence, overtime pay, and rules governing layoffs or similar reductions in force, and such dismissal hearings as due process may require; and
(4)
Such other personnel policies, regulations, provisions, or notices as were and may be necessary to provide for the adequate and systematic handling of personnel matters.
(b)
A copy of the manual is available for inspection in the office of the city clerk.
(c)
The provisions of this article shall be in addition to regulations provided in the adopted personnel manual. In the event of conflict between this chapter and the adopted manual, the more stringent provision shall prevail.
(Code 2011, § 2-117)
Sec. 2-108. - Appointment and compensation.
All officers and employees who are deemed necessary by the city council and are not otherwise provided for by law or ordinance shall be appointed as provided by said city council for such term and at such compensation as shall be provided thereby.
(Code 1999, § 3-301; Code 2011, § 2-118)
State Law reference— Authority to provide compensation and employee benefits to municipal officers and employees, O.C.G.A. § 36-35-4.
Sec. 2-109. - Bonds.
Except where otherwise provided by law or ordinance, the city council may require any officer or employee, before entering upon his duties, to post bond in an amount specified by the city council. All bond premiums shall be paid from city funds. However, when one person holds two positions, only one bond shall be required.
(Code 1999, § 3-302; Code 2011, § 2-119)
Sec. 2-110. - Joint municipal employees' retirement system.
The city has accepted the retirement plan of the joint municipal employees' retirement system on a non-contributory basis. The mayor shall be empowered to sign any and all necessary documents with such system. Information regarding the current status of the retirement system is available in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1999, § 3-304; Code 2011, § 2-120)
Sec. 2-111. - Acceptance of gratuities.
No municipal officer or employee shall accept any money or other consideration or favor from anyone other than the city for the performance of an act which he would be required or expected to perform in the regular course of his duties; nor shall any officer or employee accept, directly or indirectly, any gift, gratuity or favor of any kind which might reasonably be interpreted as an attempt to influence his actions with respect to city business.
(Code 1999, § 3-305; Code 2011, § 2-121)
Sec. 2-112. - Outside employment.
No full-time officer or employee of the city shall accept any outside employment without a written authorization from the mayor. The mayor shall not grant such authorization if the work is likely to interfere with the satisfactory performance of the officer's or employee's duties, or is incompatible with his municipal employment, or is likely to cast discredit upon or create embarrassment for the city.
(Code 1999, § 3-306; Code 2011, § 2-122)
Sec. 2-113. - Political activity.
Municipal officers and employees may individually exercise their right to vote and privately express their political views as citizens. No municipal officer or employee shall solicit political campaign contributions or engage in or actively participate in any political campaign. These restrictions shall not apply to elective officials.
(Code 1999, § 3-307; Code 2011, § 2-123)
Sec. 2-114. - Use of municipal time, facilities, etc.
No municipal officer or employee shall use or authorize the use of municipal time, facilities, equipment or supplies for private gain or advantage of himself or any other private person or group; provided, however, that this prohibition shall not apply where the governing body has authorized the use of such time, facilities, equipment or supplies, and the city is paid at such rates as are normally charged by private sources for comparable services.
(Code 1999, § 3-308; Code 2011, § 2-124)
Sec. 2-115. - Unethical use of position.
No municipal officer or employee shall make or attempt to make private purchases, for cash or otherwise, in the name of the municipality, nor shall he otherwise use or attempt to use his position to secure unwarranted privileges or exemptions for himself or others.
(Code 1999, § 3-309; Code 2011, § 2-125)
Sec. 2-116. - Strikes and unions.
No municipal officer or employee shall participate in any strike against the municipality, nor shall he join, be a member of, or solicit any other municipal officer or employee to join any labor union which approves or promotes the use of strikes by government employees.
(Code 1999, § 3-310; Code 2011, § 2-126)

DIVISION 2. - CITY CLERK

Sec. 2-146. - Appointment; qualifications; compensation.
The city clerk shall be appointed by the city council at its first regular meeting in each year after the new council has qualified, or as soon thereafter as expedient. To be eligible for the office of city clerk, a person must be at least 21 years of age. The clerk shall receive such salary as the council may determine and fix for his services.
(Code 2011, § 2-175)
Sec. 2-147. - Oath of office; bond.
Before entering upon the discharge of his duties, the city clerk shall take and subscribe the oath required of officers and shall post bond in an amount established by the city council.
(Code 2011, § 2-176)
Sec. 2-148. - General duties.
In addition to such duties as may be from time to time assigned by the city council, it shall be the duty of the city clerk to do the following:
(1)
Keep and preserve the official city seal and all city records;
(2)
Attend meetings of the mayor and council and keep the official minutes of its proceedings, including the names of members and other parties present and absent, and the vote of each councilmember on each resolution, ordinance, motion, or other official action considered;
(3)
Prepare and certify copies of official records when requested, for which fees may be prescribed by ordinance;
(4)
Serve as the chief financial officer of the city and work in coordination with the mayor in budget preparation and review, and in approving and reviewing purchase requests from department heads;
(5)
Maintain a book or record of registration of franchises granted by the city pursuant to section 3.13 of the Charter; and
(6)
Perform such other duties as may be required by the mayor and council or by general law.
(Code 2011, § 2-177)
Secs. 2-149—2-179. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - CITY TREASURER

Sec. 2-180. - Appointment; qualifications; compensation.
The city treasurer shall be appointed by the city manager and approved by the city council at its first regular meeting in each year after the new council has qualified, or as soon thereafter as expedient. To be eligible for the office of city treasurer, a person must be at least 21 years of age. The treasurer shall receive such salary as the council may determine and fix for his services.
(Code 2011, § 2-208)
Sec. 2-181. - Oath of office; bond.
Before entering upon the discharge of his duties, the city treasurer shall take and subscribe the oath required of officers, and shall post bond in an amount established by the city council.
(Code 2011, § 2-209)
Sec. 2-182. - Duties.
The city treasurer shall have such duties as are prescribed by general law and the Charter, and as are designated from time to time by the city council.
(Code 2011, § 2-210)
Secs. 2-183—2-202. - Reserved.
DIVISION 4. - CITY ATTORNEY

Sec. 2-203. - Appointment; qualifications; oath; compensation.
The city attorney shall be appointed by the city manager, by and with confirmation of appointment by majority vote of the city council, and shall serve until a successor is appointed and has qualified. No person shall be so appointed unless he is a member in good standing of the state bar and has been actively engaged in the practice of law for at least three years preceding his appointment. Before entering upon the duties of his office, the city attorney shall take the oath prescribed by this Code for city officers. The city attorney shall receive compensation in the amount established from time to time by the city council.
(Code 2011, § 2-229)
Sec. 2-204. - Powers and duties.
The city attorney shall have such duties as are provided in the Charter and as are assigned to him from time to time, consistent with the Charter and state law, by the city council.
(Code 2011, § 2-230)
