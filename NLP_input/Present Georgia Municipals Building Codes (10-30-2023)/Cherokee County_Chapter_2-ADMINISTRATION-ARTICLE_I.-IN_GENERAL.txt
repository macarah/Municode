ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 2-1. - Time and place of meetings of board of commissioners.
(a)
The board of commissioners shall hold regular meetings on the first and third Tuesday of each month at 6:00 p.m., unless otherwise ordered by the board.
(b)
Special meetings may be held upon call by the chairman of the board of commissioners or any two members. Due notice of such meetings shall be in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(d).
(c)
Meetings shall be held at the Administration Building at 1130 Bluffs Parkway, Canton, Georgia.
Editor's note— Days and time of meetings changed in minutes from the year 2002, per city instruction.
State Law reference— Meetings to be open to public, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1; due notice requirements for other than regular meetings, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(d); excluded proceedings, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-3.
Sec. 2-2. - Agenda for meetings of board of commissioners; addressing board.
There shall be a written agenda prepared prior to each meeting of the board of commissioners. The chairman of the board of commissioners retains the discretion to recognize any person attending a meeting of the board.
Editor's note— Sections 1.08.01 and 1.08.02 of the Rules of Procedure adopted 6-7-16 changed the public comment policy for meetings of the Cherokee County Board of Commissioners. The Rules of Procedure rescined Policy No. 03-P-01 which rescined Policy No. 99-P-1.
Sec. 2-3. - Legal defense for county officials.
The county will provide legal defense for all members of the boards and authorities listed in this section acting in their official and individual capacities. The provision of legal defense shall include indemnification for judgments, legal defenses and expenses incurred after the individual's term of office has expired if the action arises out of an incident occurring while the member held the office to which he was appointed. This section shall not cover actions where a member may be personally liable to one who sustains special damages as a result of any official act of such member if the act is done with the intent to violate the law and if a jury or other finder of fact specifically makes such finding and imposes liability accordingly. This section shall apply to members of the following:
(1)
The zoning board of appeals of the county;
(2)
The industrial development authority;
(3)
The county airport authority;
(4)
The board of commissioners of the county; and
(5)
The planning and zoning commission.
(Res. of 8-23-91; Res. of 10-8-91(1); Res. of 10-8-91(2); Res. of 10-8-91(3); Res. of 10-25-91)
Sec. 2-3.1. - Legal defense for county employees.
The county agrees to provide legal representation to all officers and employees in any civil suit directed against such officer or employee for acts or omissions arising out of the performance or while acting under color of office of said county. it will be a policy of the board of commissioners of the county to reimburse the officer or employee for any of the costs of any such judgment and the judgment itself, where the liability of the employee is based on facts other than his or her own wilful misconduct, occurring in the performance of his or her duty, while he or she is acting under color of office in this county.
(Ord. No. 97-9, 9-26-97)
Sec. 2-4. - Purchase orders and purchasing agent.
(a)
Establishment of position of purchasing agent. The position of purchasing agent is hereby established for the county, which position shall be under the supervision of such officials as may be designated by the board of commissioners.
(b)
Duties and powers.
(1)
The purchasing agent shall purchase all supplies, equipment and services needed by any and all departments, boards, commissions and other agencies of the county regardless of the source of funds. Agencies empowered by state statute to purchase independently may have the county purchasing agent appointed as their agent for the purposes upon relinquishing of such independence by the proper authority.
(2)
The purchasing agent shall keep complete and detailed records of all purchases and disbursement, including purchase orders issued, quotations received, basis for bid award, and any and all documents required for audit of purchase activity.
(3)
Purchases or contracts for goods and services shall not be executed by any officer or employee other than the purchasing agent, or upon his delegation.
(c)
Purchasing procedure.
(1)
The purchasing agent shall prepare or approve purchase order forms or request forms and all standards and written specifications fur supplies, equipment and services. All such specifications shall permit competition wherever practicable.
(2)
All purchases shall be made in accordance with one of the following procedures, except as otherwise provided by resolution or ordinance of the common council:
a.
Public construction contracts shall be bid in accordance with state statutes.
b.
Purchases up to $250.00, may use a field purchase order or regular purchase order provided there is money in the budget to cover the cost.
c.
Purchases from $251.00 to $2,499.00 shall be purchased provided there is money in the budget to cover the cost. There should be a verbal or written quotations of three prices attached to the requisition, when given to the purchasing department or finance department. A purchase order number must be given to the company when the items are ordered. This number must appear on the invoice for the proper payment.
d.
Purchases of $2,500 to $4,999.00 shall be purchased provided there is money in the budget to cover the cost. Purchases must have the written approval from the county manager before purchasing or ordering item. You must have three written quotations attached to the requisition when given to the county manager. On approval he will send the requisition to purchasing for a purchase order number. They will call the department with the purchase order number so the items can be purchased by the department or the purchasing department will place the order. The purchase order number must be given to the company at the time of placing the order. This number must appear on the invoices for proper payment.
e.
Formal sealed bids are required for all purchases over $5,000.00 including professional services. If items are included in the line budget approved by the board of commissioners, the county manager can approve the bid. If the items are not included in the approval line item budget, the board of commissioners must approve the purchase. After the bid is chosen, a copy of the bid is to be attached to the requisition when the order is placed. The purchase order number must be given to the company at the time. This number must appear on the invoice for proper payment.
f.
No purchase order number needed for: medical expenses, utilities, travel advances, personal refunds, postage.
g.
Invoices with no purchase order number. Invoices that come in with no purchase order appearing on the invoice will either be sent back to the company for them to obtain a number or the invoice will be given to the county manager. The county manager will then see what departments aren't completing the correct procedures for ordering items.
(3)
The purchasing agent may reject any or all bids, or waive informalities in the bidding process.
(4)
The purchasing agent may purchase from or in cooperation with other governmental agencies with or without competitive bids.
(5)
The purchasing agent shall be responsible for developing and administering such other policies, rules and regulations not inconsistent with this section or state statutes which he deems necessary for the efficient procurement of goods and services. A copy of such policies, rules and regulations shall be filed with the county clerk.
(d)
Disposal of property. All agencies shall submit reports to the purchasing agent showing stocks or supplies, materials and equipment on hand and not in use, as requested. The purchasing agent shall be responsible for and have the authority for the disposal of obsolete, excess, unused or scrap materials, supplies and equipment. All revenues shall be deposited with the county finance director.
(Ord. No. 94-1, § 1, 2-8-94; Ord. No. 96-10, 4-9-96; Ord. No. 99-O-2, 1-22-99)
Editor's note— Ordinance No. 96-10, adopted April 9, 1996, did not specifically amend the Code. Therefore, such ordinance was treated as replacing § 2-4(c)(2)b.—d. with 2-4(c)(2)b.—g., at the discretion of the editor.
Sec. 2-5. - No smoking and tobacco-free policy within the county facilities.
(a)
E-cigarette(s) means any electronic oral device, such as one consisting of a heating element, battery, and/or electronic circuit, that produces an aerosol mist or vapor, the use or inhalation of which simulates smoking. The term "vaping" shall mean the use of an e-cigarette or inhalation of the vaporized contents of an e-cigarette.
(b)
There shall be no smoking of tobacco products, use of smokeless tobacco products, or vaping of e-cigarettes in county buildings, facilities, vehicles and lease space and equipment, except in those areas as designated by the board of commissioners. In addition, smoking and vaping are prohibited within 100 feet of any flammable substance and outdoor working including fueling stations, three-wall garages and storage areas. However, in other outdoor spaces where fuel and other flammable substances are contained in approved containers, smoking and vaping may be permitted. Smoking and vaping will be permitted in outside work areas which are not within 100 feet of any flammable or combustible substance.
(Ord. No. 94-6, § 1, 3-25-94; Ord. No. 95-O-5, § 1, 2-24-95; Ord. No. 2015-O-002, § 1, 6-16-15)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 2015-O-002, § 1, adopted June 16, 2015, changed the title of § 2-5 from "No smoking policy within the county facilities" to read as set out herein.
Sec. 2-6. - Food and drink in public areas of county justice center prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to possess open containers of food or drink or to consume food or drink in the public areas of the county justice center other than the designated canteen area. The public areas shall not include work areas of employees within the county justice center. However, it shall include the public hallways, courtrooms, deed record room, atrium area elevators, etc.
Any persons in violation of this section shall be subject to citation by either the county sheriff's department or the county marshal returnable to the magistrate's court of the county. Any persons found in violation of this section shall be subject to a fine not to exceed $1,000.00, and/or imprisonment of 60 days.
(Ord. No. 96-05, §§ 1, 2, 2-23-96; Ord. No. 2016-O-005, exh. A, 8-2-16)
Sec. 2-7. - Records management plan.
(a)
The board of commissioners of Cherokee County does hereby adopt the Georgia Records Act (O.C.G.A. § 50-18-90 et. seq.) and the rules and regulations established pursuant to said Act as a basis for establishing a county records management program.
(b)
The Board of Regents of the University System of Georgia publication titled "Local Government Record Retention Schedules," as may be amended and updated from time to time by the Division of Archives and History of the Board of Regents of the University System of Georgia, and an updated copy of which is on file with the Cherokee County Clerk, is hereby ratified and adopted as the Cherokee County record retention schedule. If the county maintains any records not addressed in this publication, the county shall submit a recommended retention schedule for such records to the Division of Archives and History for approval, and upon receiving approval, such retention schedule shall be incorporated into the Cherokee County Clerk's guidelines described in paragraph (e) below.
(c)
No county record shall be destroyed except as authorized by a state records retention schedule approved by the Division of Archives and History of the Board of Regents of the University System of Georgia.
(d)
The Cherokee County Clerk is hereby appointed as the county's records management officer and is responsible for administering the county's records management program and will implement and supervise all components of this section.
(e)
The Cherokee County Clerk shall prepare and maintain guidelines for maintenance and security of all county records covered by this section, and such guidelines shall, upon completion, become a part of this section.
(f)
The county manager of Cherokee County is authorized to take all necessary action to ensure that the records of Cherokee County, Georgia, are preserved, protected, stored, and safeguarded in accord with pertinent statutes and rules and regulations, including but not limited to the aforementioned publication titled "Local Government Record Retention Schedules," O.C.G.A. § 50-18-90, et seq., and O.C.G.A. § 36-9-5.
(Ord. No. 96-09, 4-9-96; Ord. No. 2001-O-21, 11-27-01; Ord. No. 2012-O-001, § 1, 7-10-12; Ord. No. 2018-O-002, § 1, 4-3-18)
Sec. 2-8. - Standard for recovery of expenditures related to providing services.
It is intended that this standard will provide a means for recovering cost of county resources used to respond to and/or provide extraordinary services for situations caused by a person, persons or a company.
(1)
General requirements.
a.
All departments within the county government are hereby authorized to take appropriate steps to generate an invoice for services when, in their judgment, it was necessary to render the services in the best interest of the citizens of the county.
b.
Services provided by the county may be the result of carelessness, willful intent, negligence, or a failure to take actions that will eliminate reoccurring situations which impact on the county's cost of providing services and its ability to provide services for all citizens.
c.
In lieu of monetary reimbursement, the party receiving the services may replace supplies and equipment expended by the county with equal or greater quantities. Any such material must be of equal or better specifications than that expended. This method of reimbursement must be preapproved by the department being reimbursed.
d.
Invoices shall be submitted to the county manager and/or the county commission for approval prior to presenting the invoice to the party responsible for paying the reimbursement.
e.
The cost used to calculate the invoice amounts shall be based on fair market values for the services, labor rates for county employees, advertised cost of goods and equipment, and actual cost. Compensation for overtime hours worked will be based on county policy.
(2)
Preparing and submitting an invoice.
a.
Department instructions, standard operating procedures, and instructions as published by the finance department of the county shall be followed when preparing invoices and obtaining approvals.
b.
The finance department accounts receivable clerk shall be responsible for tracking the invoices and collecting the reimbursements.
(3)
Enforcement.
a.
Department heads shall be responsible for determining when invoicing for reimbursement is appropriate.
b.
Failure to reimburse the county as specified by the invoice shall be processed in accordance with the procedures of the finance department's accounts receivable section.
(Ord. No. 97-8, 8-22-97)
Sec. 2-9 - County seal.
(a)
The Cherokee County Board of Commissioners finds that the seal and logos of the county adopted in this section are a symbol of authority of the county and a valuable asset of its population. The Cherokee County Board of Commissioners desires to ensure that only appropriate uses are made of the County seal and logos.
(b)
The Cherokee County Board of Commissioners adopts the symbols depicted below as its official county seal and county logos as shown on the attached exhibit A.
(c)
Except as otherwise expressly provided by state or federal law, or when done in the course of official county business, it shall be unlawful for any person or entity to use the county seal, emblem or logos approved for county use by the Cherokee County Board of Commissioners, or which is otherwise created by or for the county in connection with or to identify any official county program or activity, by affixing or otherwise depicting the county seal, emblem, or logo, or replica thereof, on any document, thing, item, or material, or in or upon any Internet website, without first having obtained written permission from the Cherokee County Board of Commissioners. Any use approved by the Cherokee County Board of Commissioners or the board's designee may be subject to any reasonable conditions imposed by the board of the board's designee to carry out the purposes of the authorized use.
(d)
Any person who violates this section and uses the county seal, emblem, or logo without prior approval shall be served by certified mail a written notice to cease and desist such behavior within ten days from the date of receipt. Should such violation(s) continues, a hearing shall be set before the magistrate court judge or the state court judge to hear the violations and issue penalties. Each day of any unauthorized use of the county seal, emblem, or logo, shall constitute a separate offense. Any person who violates any provision of this section shall, upon conviction, be guilty of a violation of such ordinance and shall be punished in accordance with section 1-12 and as it may be amended from time to time.
(e)
The county shall retain the right at all times to inspect any document, item, material, or website upon which the county seal, emblem, or logo is used in order to verify that the county seal, emblem, or logo is being used truly and correctly as represented by the authorized party and in an unaltered form. Permission to use the county seal, emblem or logo shall not be assignable.
(f)
Should any part of this ordinance or its application to any person or circumstance be declared unconstitutional or invalid for any reason, or should any portion of this ordinance be pre-empted by state or federal law or regulation, such decision or preemption shall not affect the validity of the remaining portions of this ordinance or its application to other persons or circumstances.
( Ord. No. 2021-O-002 , § 1, 2-16-21)
