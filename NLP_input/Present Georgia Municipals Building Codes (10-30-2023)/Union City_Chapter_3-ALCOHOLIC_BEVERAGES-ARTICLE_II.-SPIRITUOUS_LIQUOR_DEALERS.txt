ARTICLE II. - SPIRITUOUS LIQUOR DEALERS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 3-16. - Purpose of article.
This article has been enacted in accordance with a plan designed for the following purposes, among others: to promote the health and general welfare of the community, to establish reasonable standards for the regulation and control of the licensing and sales of spirituous liquors, to protect and preserve schools and churches, to give effect to existing land use and to preserve certain residential areas, with reasonable considerations, among others, to the character of the areas and their peculiar suitability for particular uses, the congestion in the roads and streets, and with a general view of promoting desirable living conditions and sustaining stability of neighborhood and property values, and to protect against the evils of concentration of the retail outlets for spirituous liquors in one (1) family or to prevent undesirable persons from controlling the retail liquor industry.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-17. - Definitions.
Interest in license. As used in this article, an "interest in a license" shall be deemed to exist if the person involved is the outright owner of the license, a co-owner of the license, a partner in a partnership which owns all or any part of a license, a stockholder in any corporation organized for the pecuniary gain which owns all or any part of a license, an owner, lessor, sublessor or stockholder in any corporation organized for pecuniary gain owning or leasing any real estate which is occupied by a retail spirituous liquor store or shares in any of the income or corpus of any trust or estate having any interest in a retail spirituous liquor store; provided, however, that a stockholder shall not be deemed to have an interest in a retail spirituous liquor store where he owns stock in a motel or hotel having two hundred (200) or more rooms with a retail spirituous liquor store located on the premises of such motel or hotel and owned by such motel or hotel. Notwithstanding any of the other provisions of this section, no one (1) person shall have more than one (1) interest in any one (1) license in the city.
Spirituous liquors. "Spirituous liquors" shall be deemed to include, but not be limited to, whiskey, rum, gin, brandy, vodka, tequila and all alcoholic beverages of similar nature and a similar alcoholic content.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
State Law reference— Similar definitions, O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2.
Sec. 3-18. - Penalties for violation of article or application irregularities.
(a)
Any person who acquires a license or a renewal of same in violation of this article by any misrepresentation or fraudulent statement shall be deemed guilty of an offense and upon conviction thereof shall be punished in accordance with section 1-17.
(b)
Any untrue or misleading information contained in, or material omission left out of, an original, renewal or transfer application for a retail liquor license shall be cause for the denial thereof and if any license has been granted under these circumstances, there shall be cause for the revocation of same.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-19. - Retail licensee prohibited from having first direct financial interest in wholesale liquor business.
No person shall hold a retail license who also has any direct financial interest in any wholesale liquor business.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-20. - Personal restrictions on interest in retail license.
(a)
After the effective date of the ordinance from which this article derives, no person or member of his family shall own, hold or control any interest whatsoever in more than one (1) license to engage in the business of selling spirituous liquors at retail in the city.
(b)
No more than one (1) license to engage in the sale of spirituous liquors at retail in the city shall be issued to any person which shall include all members of such person's family.
(c)
"Family" shall be deemed to include any person related to the holder of such license within the first degree of consanguinity or affinity as computed according to the civil law.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any elected or appointed official or employee of the city, or his spouse or minor children, to have any whole, partial or beneficial interest in any license to operate retail spirituous liquor stores in the city.
(e)
A license may take in partners or additional stockholders where it is determined that the additional capital furnished is to be used exclusively for additional inventory or expanding the facilities of the business or for building new facilities and where it appears that the licensee himself received directly none of the additional capital invested. Under this section an additional partner or stockholder must be approved by the mayor and council and, if approved, the business must obtain a new license.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-21. - Population restrictions on number of licenses issued.
The mayor and council shall not issue any further spirituous liquor licenses when such issuance would cause the number of licenses to exceed one (1) for each five thousand (5,000) persons residing in the city. Such population figures shall be based on the most recent figures issued by the United States Census Bureau.
(Ord. No. 82-9, 12-7-82; Ord. No. 2007-19, § 1, 9-18-07)
Sec. 3-22. - Distance requirements for package sales of spirituous liquors.
At a minimum, the front entrance of any business involved in the package sale of spirituous liquors must be located the distance specified below from the front entrance of any relevant building, unless otherwise specified herein. Using the most direct route of travel on the ground, measure as follows:
(a)
At least three hundred (300) feet from a business engaged in the package sale of spirituous liquors to an alcoholic treatment center;
(b)
At least three hundred (300) feet from a business engaged in the package sale of spirituous liquors to any church building;
(c)
At least six hundred (600) feet from a business engaged in the package sale of spirituous liquors to any school building or school grounds;
(d)
At least six hundred (600) feet from a business engaged in the package sale of spirituous liquors to a college campus;
(e)
At least three hundred (300) feet from an alcoholic treatment center to a business engaged in the package sale of spirituous liquors;
(f)
At least three hundred (300) feet from any church building to a business engaged in the package sale of spirituous liquors;
(g)
At least six hundred (600) feet from any school building or school grounds to a business engaged in the package sale of spirituous liquors; and
(h)
At least six hundred (600) feet from a college campus to a business engaged in the package sale of spirituous liquors.
(Code 1976, § 15-201; Ord. No. 93-11, § 1, 12-21-93; Ord. No 2003-16, § 1, 10-21-03; Ord. No. 2010-14, § 1, 9-21-10)
State Law reference— Sales of alcoholic beverages near churches, school buildings, etc., O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.
Sec. 3-23. - "Schools, colleges" defined.
The schools or colleges referred to in this section shall include only such state, county, city, church or other schools as teach the subjects commonly taught in the common schools and colleges of this state, and shall not include private schools or colleges wherein only specialized subjects such as law, stenography, business, music, art, medicine, dentistry, vocational occupations and other special subjects, are taught. For the purposes of this section, "schools" shall not include day care centers, or facilities which participate in Georgia's Pre-K Program.
(Code 1976, § 15-201; Ord. No. 94-11, § 1, 6-21-94; Ord. No 2003-16, § 2, 10-21-03; Ord. No. 2010-14, § 2, 9-21-10)
Sec. 3-24. - Zoning regulations for a licensed location.
No license to engage in the package sale of spirituous liquors shall be issued within the areas described in this article unless the location has been zoned GC, RSC, M-1, M-2, TCMU or TCMF under the zoning laws of the city.
(Code 1976, § 15-201; Ord. No. 87-8, 2-3-87; Ord. No 2003-16, § 3, 10-21-03; Ord. No. 2007-08, § 1, 4-17-07)
Sec. 3-25. - Annexed areas; continuance of business.
Where a retail liquor store exists on the effective date of the ordinance from which this article derives in an area outside the city limits, upon annexation of such area the same may be continued and shall be subject to all provisions of this article except that part that pertains to residences.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Secs. 3-26—3-35. - Reserved.

Sec. 3-36. - Qualifications of applicant.
(a)
No retail license for spirituous liquors shall be granted to any applicant who is not a citizen of the United States or permanent resident alien thereof and who has not resided continuously in the state for twelve (12) months next preceding the year for which application is made. Where the applicant is a corporation whose primary business is the operation of a liquor store, the majority stockholder must meet the within requirements and the license shall be issued to the corporation and the majority stockholders. Where the applicant is a corporation whose primary business is other than the operation of a liquor store, the license shall be issued to the corporation and an officer or agent of such corporation who meets the above requirements. If the applicant is a partnership, the within requirements shall apply to all partners.
(b)
All applicants for licenses must attach to their application evidence of their good character. No original license shall be issued to any person, partnership or corporation organized for pecuniary gain where any individual having an interest either as owner, partner, stockholder, directly or indirectly beneficial or absolute, shall have been convicted or shall have taken a plea of nolo contendere within ten (10) years immediately prior to the filing of the application for any felony or misdemeanor of any state or of the United States or any municipal ordinance except traffic violations.
(Code 1976, § 15-201; Ord. No. 82-5, 8-17-82; Ord. No. 87-31, § 1, 10-20-87; Ord. No. 2014-07, § 1, 8-19-14)
Sec. 3-37. - Pending applications.
All applications now pending for the year 1972 for the issuance of retail liquor licenses shall be considered under this article without further filing.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-38. - Application.
(a)
Contents. All applications for license under the provisions of this article, both original and renewal, must be accompanied by a full and complete statement under oath of information relative to any and all interest in retail packaged spirituous liquor stores. This shall include the names and addresses of all persons interested in the ownership of the business of selling at retail packaged spirituous liquor, together with any interest each person or any member of his immediate family has in any other retail packaged spirituous liquor store; the ownership of the land and building where such retail business is operated; the amount of rental paid for such land and building and the manner in which the same is determined and to whom and at what intervals it is paid; the names and addresses (by affidavit from the owner, lessor or sublessor of such land and building) of all persons having any whole, partial, beneficial or other interest in and to the land and building on and in which such retail packaged spirituous liquor store is located; and any other information called for by the mayor and council.
(b)
Change in relationship. Any change in any relationship declared must be filed with the police department when such change is made, and failure to so file within a period of thirty (30) days after such change is made shall be grounds for cancellation by the mayor and council.
(c)
Property survey. All applications shall include a certificate from a registered surveyor showing a scale drawing of the location of the proposed or existing premises and the distance from the proposed or existing front entrance of the proposed or existing premises to the front entrance of the buildings of the nearest alcoholic treatment centers, churches, schools, colleges and housing authority properties. The scale drawing shall show measurements of all relevant distances utilizing the most direct route of travel on the ground.
(Code 1976, § 15-201; Ord. No 2003-16, § 4, 10-21-03)
Sec. 3-39. - Physical requirements of applicant's premises.
(a)
No license shall be granted to a retailer, under the provisions of this article, unless the front entrance to the premises is clearly visible from a public street; provided, however, that this restriction shall not apply where the licensee is a hotel, motel, private club, location in a shopping center or multiple-story business building.
(b)
Where a building in which a retailer intends to operate under the provisions of this article is at the time of the application for such license not in existence or not yet completed, license may be issued for such location provided the plans for the proposed building show clearly a compliance with the other provisions of this article.
(c)
No sales shall be made from such establishment until it has been completed in accordance with the plans and is in conformity with all of the other provisions of this article.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-40. - Notice of application for license.
All persons desiring to engage in the sale of spirituous liquors at retail in the city shall give notice of the purpose of making such application by advertisement in at least two (2) consecutive issues of the legal organ of the city plus five (5) times on different days in a daily newspaper of general circulation in the city, which notice shall contain a particular description of the location of the proposed spirituous liquor business and shall give the name of the applicant, and if a partnership the names of the partners, and if a corporation, the names of the officers and all stockholders. The advertisement referred to herein shall be of type not smaller than ten-point capital and lowercase and shall be at least a one-inch, two-column advertisement.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-41. - Investigation of applicant.
(a)
All applications for new licenses for sale of packaged spirituous liquor at retail and all applications for renewal of such new licenses shall be investigated by the police department and no such new or renewal license shall be acted upon by the mayor and council or issued by the city clerk until the city attorney has indicated in writing that, in his opinion, upon the facts as stated and ascertained by the police department, no violation exists as to any ordinance in effect as of the date of the rendering of such opinion. All applications for new or renewal licenses shall furnish to the police department all data, information and records requested of them by the police department and failure to furnish such data, information and records within thirty (30) days from the date of the request by the police department shall automatically serve to dismiss with prejudice the application of any such applicant failing to furnish such data, information and records.
(b)
Applicants, by filing for license to sell packaged spirituous liquor at retail, agree to furnish the data, information and records as called for herein and also agree to submit under oath to interrogation by the police department or the city attorney as to any facts considered pertinent to such application. Applicants, by filing such application, also agree to produce for oral interrogation by the police department or the city attorney, any person or persons requested by the police department and considered as being important in the ascertainment of the facts relative to the license. Failure to produce such persons within thirty (30) days after being requested to do so shall result in the automatic dismissal of any application for license.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-42. - Public hearing on application.
All applications for licenses for the retail sale of spirituous liquors shall be acted upon by the mayor and council after a public hearing after advertisement as required by law, such advertisement to be made as provided in section 3-40, and proof of same furnished to the mayor and council prior to the hearing.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-43. - Action by mayor and council; time limitation.
All applications for new licenses under this article or for transfers of locations shall be passed upon by the mayor and council within sixty (60) days from the date of the filing of such application. Date of filing shall be the time and date that a properly completed and executed application form along with the filing fee as required in section 3-45(2) is received by the city clerk. The city clerk shall not accept an application for consideration by the mayor and council when there is a previously filed application for a place of business for a retail spirituous liquor license within five hundred (500) feet of the applicant's proposed site.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-44. - Grant of application.
All applications to engage in the sale at retail of spirituous liquors meeting the standards of this article shall be granted, unless some specific cause related to location, traffic condition or other environmental condition shall justify refusal. In such case, the applicant shall be entitled to file a new application for a license at an eligible location without loss of any part of his application or license fee.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-45. - Annual license fee schedule.
(a)
The annual license fee for the privilege of engaging in the business of selling spirituous liquors at retail shall be an amount as set by resolution of the city council, which amount shall remain in effect until modified or amended by subsequent resolution adopted by the city council. The sum of five hundred dollars ($500.00) shall be paid at the time an original application is filed to cover investigative costs, which shall be credited against the first annual license fee upon grant of a license. This fee is not refundable.
(b)
All retailer license fees shall be paid between January first and January twentieth each year.
(Code 1976, § 15-201; Ord. No. 2014-10, § 1, 11-18-14)
Sec. 3-46. - Issuance.
(a)
All licenses under this division must be obtained and fees paid not later than two (2) weeks from the date of the approval of the application by the mayor and council and, if not so obtained, the permit granted by the mayor and council shall be void.
(b)
When a license has been approved, and the applicant has deposited with the clerk of the mayor and council the required fee, the fee shall be paid to the municipal revenue collector and a license issued.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-47. - Procedure when license denied by state.
If the applicant is denied a license by the state, upon the proof of such refusal, he shall be entitled to a refund of the license fee less the investigative fee as required in section 3-45(2) plus an additional charge of twenty-five dollars ($25.00) to cover clerical costs of granting the license. The refund may be made by the city clerk without the necessity of any action by the mayor and council.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-48. - Duration.
No license shall be issued for less than a calendar year period and in case of the revocation or surrender of such license before the expiration of such calendar year period, the holder thereof shall not be entitled to receive any refund whatsoever.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-49. - Business to be opened within nine months from license issuance; effect of failure to do so.
(a)
All holders of licenses for the retail sale of spirituous liquors must within nine (9) months after the issuance of the license open for business the establishment referred to in the license and begin the sale of the product or products authorized by the license.
(b)
Failure to open the establishment and begin the sale as referred to in subsection (a) above within the nine-month period shall serve as automatic forfeiture and cancellation of the unused license and no refund of license fees shall be made to the license holder.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-50. - Effect of failure to operate business for nine consecutive months.
Any holder of a license for the retail sale of spirituous liquors who shall begin the operation of the business and sale of the product or products as authorized in the license but who shall for a period of nine (9) consecutive months thereafter cease to operate the business and sale of the product or products authorized in such license, shall upon completion of the nine-month period automatically forfeit his license, which license shall, by virtue of such failure to operate be canceled without the necessity of any further action of the mayor and council.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-51. - Transferability.
(a)
Licenses to engage in the business of selling spirituous liquors at retail shall not be transferable, except as otherwise provided in this section.
(b)
In case of the death of any person holding such a license, or any interest therein, the same may, in the discretion of the city administrator, be transferred to the administrator, executor or the lawful heirs of the deceased person.
(c)
Nothing in this section, however, shall prohibit one (1) or more of the partners in a partnership holding a license to withdraw from the partnership in favor of one (1) or more of the partners who were partners at the time of the issuance of the license. Such withdrawal shall not, however, serve to bring any new ownership into the partnership.
(d)
Should a transfer of a location be approved by the city administrator, there shall be no pro rata return of any license fee and the new location shall be considered as a new license hereunder.
(e)
All applications for transfer of locations shall comply with the provisions herein set forth governing new licenses.
(Code 1976, § 15-201; Ord. No. 91-12, § 1, 11-19-91)
Sec. 3-52. - Suspension or revocation.
(a)
Whenever the state shall revoke any permit or license to sell at retail any spirituous liquors the city license to deal in such products shall thereupon be automatically revoked without any action by the mayor and council or any municipal officer.
(b)
No license which has been issued or which may hereafter be issued by the city to any person as a retail dealer in spirituous liquors shall be suspended or revoked, except for due cause as hereinafter defined, and after a hearing and upon the prior three-day written notice to the holder of such.
(c)
"Due cause" for the suspension or revocation of such license shall consist of the violation of any laws or ordinances regulating such businesses, or violation of regulations made pursuant to authority granted for the purpose of regulating such businesses, or for the violation of any state or federal law, or for the violation of any city ordinances other than traffic ordinances, which in the opinion of the mayor and council deem to render the licensee improper to hold such license.
(d)
Such hearings shall be conducted by the mayor and council.
(e)
Upon a finding of "due cause" for suspension or revocation by the mayor and council against the licensee, the following minimum penalties shall apply:
(1)
First offense—Three-day suspension;
(2)
Second offense—Ten-day suspension and one thousand dollar ($1,000.00) fine;
(3)
Third offense—Thirty-day suspension and one thousand dollar ($1,000.00) fine; and
(4)
Fourth offense—Revocation for minimum of two (2) years.
(f)
In the event of revocation, no refund of any portion of the license shall be paid.
(Code 1976, § 15-201; Ord. No. 94-9, §§ 1, 2, 5-17-94)
Secs. 3-53—3-60. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - OPERATING REGULATIONS

Sec. 3-61. - Front window display.
Each person licensed to sell spirituous liquor at retail shall have printed on the front window of the licensed premises the inscription "City Retail License No. " in uniform letters not less than four (4) inches in height; provided, however, that if a licensee so desires he may have his name displayed on the inside of the window in like manner or in neon or other electric lights. Such sign shall be parallel with the window and so constructed as not to extend more than six (6) inches from the window. A neon sign may also be constructed along the borderline or edge of the front window inside the glass.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-62. - Exterior advertisement of brand and price of liquor prohibited.
No sign of any kind, painted or electric, advertising any brand or price of liquor shall be permitted on the exterior, or in the window, of any licensed premises. No placard or sign of any kind which is visible from the exterior of the licensed premises shall make reference to the price of any liquors sold therein; provided, however, that tags showing the prices of individual bottles or containers may be affixed to each such bottle or container or to the edge of the shelf whereon such bottles or containers are located.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-63. - Interior visibility required; license prohibited in restaurant, etc.
(a)
No screen, blind, curtain, partition, article or thing, which shall prevent a clear view into the interior, shall be permitted in the window or upon the doors of any retail spirituous liquor store, and no booth, screen, partition or other obstruction shall be permitted within the interior of any such store. Each such retail store shall be so lighted that the interior of the store is visible day and night.
(b)
No retail spirituous liquor license shall be allowed in, or in connection with, any restaurant, cafe or eating place, or in the same room where a bar or soda fountain is maintained for the dispensing and sale of soft drinks or other beverages.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-64. - Business hours and days.
Retail dealers in spirituous liquors shall not engage in the sale of such liquors except between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 11:45 p.m. Monday through Saturday and 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m. Sunday. The business hours of wholesale dealers shall be from sunup to sundown.
(Code 1976, § 15-201; Ord. No. 84-1, § 1, 1-17-84; Ord. No. 90-12, §§ 1, 2, 9-4-90; Ord. No. 2011-18, § 1, 11-15-11; Ord. No. 2014-11, § 1, 11-18-14)
State Law reference— Municipal authority to authorize sale of alcoholic beverages on local election days, OCGA § 3-3-20(b)(2)(B); municipal authority to prohibit sales of alcoholic beverages on Christmas Day, OCGA § 3-3-20(c); municipal authority to regulate the sale of alcoholic beverages at stated times, OCGA § 37-8-11(b).
Sec. 3-65. - Deliveries of alcoholic beverages.
(a)
Except as provided in subsection (b) of this section, it shall be unlawful to make deliveries of alcoholic beverages except in the store or department of the store in which such beverages are sold.
(b)
The city adopts and elects to incorporate certain provisions of House Bill 879 (General Assembly 2020) amending O.C.G.A. Title 3. Specifically, local city licensed package goods retailers are permitted to deliver, themselves or through a qualified third party, unbroken package alcohol. The purchaser shall register with the retailer and provide valid identification and signature upon delivery. Retailers shall follow the House Bill and any regulations issued by the Georgia Department of Revenue.
(Code 1976, § 15-201; Ord. No. 2020-11 , § 1, 11-17-20)
Sec. 3-66. - Slot machines, etc., prohibited on premises.
No retail dealer shall permit on his premises any slot machine or mechanical music boxes or pinball machines or any kind or character or any coin-operated machines operated for amusement purposes.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-67. - Price lists or tags.
Each retail licensee of spirituous liquors shall have conspicuously displayed within the interior of the licensed premises not less than four (4) copies of a printed price list of the spirituous liquors offered for sale and one (1) printed copy of the penal sections of this article; provided, however that a licensee, in lieu of having four (4) copies of a printed price list, may have the price placed on the bottles or on the bottom of the shelf where liquors are exhibited for sale.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-68. - Employees; reports to police; investigation; fee.
(a)
No retail licensee of spirituous liquors shall employ any minor in his store, or in the department of a store where alcoholic beverages are sold. No retail licensee shall employ in any premises used for the sale of spirituous liquors any person in any capacity whatsoever who has been convicted in this or any state or by the United States or any other country of soliciting for prostitution, keeping a disorderly place, illegally dealing in narcotics, sex offenses or any charge relating to unlawful manufacture or sale of intoxicating liquors or and other crime involving moral turpitude.
(b)
It shall be the duty of all persons holding retail or wholesale licenses to sell alcoholic beverages to file with the chief of police the names of all employees with their home addresses and home telephone numbers and places of employment. Changes in the list of employees, with the names of new employees, must be filed with the chief of police within three (3) days from the date of such change. All employees shall be subject to such investigative rules and regulations as may be deemed necessary from time to time by the police department of the city. A five-dollar investigative fee shall be paid by all applicants.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Sec. 3-69. - Sales to minors and intoxicated persons prohibited.
No licensee authorized to sell spirituous liquors at retail in Union City, Georgia, nor any agent or employee of any licensee, shall do any of the following upon the licensed premises:
(1)
Sell, give, provide, furnish, or cause to be furnished any spirituous liquor to any person under twenty-one (21) years of age;
(2)
Sell, give, provide, furnish, or cause to be furnished any spirituous liquor to any person who is in a state of noticeable intoxication.
(Code 1976, § 15-201; Ord. No. 94-8, § 1, 5-17-94)
Sec. 3-70. - Sanitary regulations, immoral conduct, etc.; inspection and report by fire department.
(a)
All premises licensed under the provisions of this division shall be kept clean and in proper sanitary condition and in full compliance with the provisions and regulations governing the condition of premises used for the storage and sale of food for human consumption. It shall be unlawful to permit any disturbance of the peace, any obscenity or any lewd, immoral or improper entertainment, conduct or practice on such premises.
(b)
The fire department shall, upon request of the police committee, inspect such premises and report their findings to the committee. All premises licensed under this division shall conform at all times with all fire regulations of the city.
(Code 1976, § 15-201)
Secs. 3-71—3-85. - Reserved.
