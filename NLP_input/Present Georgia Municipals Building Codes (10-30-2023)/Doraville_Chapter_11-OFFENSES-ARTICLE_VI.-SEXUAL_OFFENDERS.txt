ARTICLE VI. - SEXUAL OFFENDERS[6]

Footnotes:
--- (6) ---
Editor's note— Section 1 of Ord. No. 05-36, adopted Oct. 3, 2005, enacted provisions to be designated as §§ 3-180—3-183. In order to maintain the alphanumeric style of the Code, said provisions have been redesignated as §§ 11-180—11-183.

Sec. 11-180. - Prohibited areas.
It is unlawful for any person who is subject to the registration requirements under Georgia law concerning sex offenders to live within one thousand (1,000) feet of any public or private primary and/or secondary school or educational facility, or any public or private park, playground, tot lot, library, recreational area, active and passive park land, child care facility or any church or public housing authority family residence complex.
(Ord. No. 05-36, § 1(3-180), 10-3-05)
Sec. 11-181. - Relocation.
A sex offender who resides within any prohibited area established pursuant to the provisions of this article shall have sixty (60) days from the effective date of this article, or upon the termination of any residential lease entered into prior to the effective date of this article and the terms of which is not longer than one (1) year, whichever is later, to relocate outside the prohibited area. Failure to move to a location which is in compliance with this section within the prescribed time period shall constitute a violation of this article.
(Ord. No. 05-36, § 1(3-181), 10-3-05)
Sec. 11-182. - Previously owned property.
This article shall not apply to any sex offender who purchased property to be used as his/her primary residence which is located within the prohibited area prior to the effective date of this article.
(Ord. No. 05-36, § 1(3-182), 10-3-05)
Sec. 11-183. - Map.
The City Clerk or his or her designee shall maintain a map on file at City Hall which delineates the prohibited areas and, conversely, the areas in the City where convicted sex offenders are permitted to live.
(Ord. No. 05-36, § 1(3-183), 10-3-05)
Secs. 11-184—11-189. - Reserved.
