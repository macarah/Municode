DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 30-31. - Established.
There is established the Brantley County Public Library System pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 20-5-40.
(Res. of 5-11-1993, § A)
Sec. 30-32. - Board of trustees.
(a)
Trustees. There shall exist within the county, the county library board of trustees which shall consist of eight people who are residents of the county.
(b)
Appointment. The members of the library board of trustees shall be appointed by the board of commissioners to serve a term of three years, with the right to reappointment.
(c)
Authority. The library board of trustees is delegated the authority to generally oversee and set policies and practices for the operation of the county library in accord with O.C.G.A. § 20-5-43 and with the constitution and bylaws of the county library board, which are set out in this article, and are approved by the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 5-11-1993, §§ B—D)
Secs. 30-33—30-50. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - CONSTITUTION

Sec. 30-51. - Name.
The name of the library shall be the Brantley County Library. It is located in Nahunta, Georgia.
(Res. of 5-11-1993, § D(att. 1), art. I)
Sec. 30-52. - Purpose.
(a)
The constitution from which this division is derived and the bylaws of the library board are companion documents. They are intended to clarify local practices and the relationship of the local library to the regional system. There should be no conflict between the provisions of the constitution from which this division is derived and bylaws and those of the Brunswick-Glynn Regional Library System. If an inadvertent conflict occurs, the regional constitution and bylaws shall govern unless such conflict exceeds any delegated authority from the board of commissioners.
(b)
The county library shall furnish library service to the people of the county in cooperation with the Brunswick-Glynn Regional Library System, and under the regulations governing public libraries as set forth by the state. The county library shall offer a full program of library service to all citizens of the county to meet their informational, educational, and recreational needs.
(c)
The county shall provide and maintain its own library facilities. It shall staff its library facilities and its staff shall cooperate and coordinate its library services with the Brunswick-Glynn Regional Library System Board.
(d)
In this division, the terms "county library" and "library board" refer to the Brantley County Library and the Brantley County Library Board of Trustees.
(Res. of 5-11-1993, § D(att.1), Art. II)
Sec. 30-53. - Constituency.
The county library shall be a member of the Brunswick-Glynn Regional Library System. Membership in the Brunswick-Glynn Regional Library System provides the citizens of the county with reciprocal borrowing privileges in all libraries in the system. Likewise, the county library shall lend materials to the citizens of all other counties within the regional library system at no charge.
(Res. of 5-11-1993, § D(att. 1), Art. III)
Sec. 30-54. - Library board of trustees:
(a)
Members of the board; appointment. The county library board of trustees shall consist of ten members. The board of commissioners shall appoint all eight members. The county library board may recommend individuals for appointment to the library board.
(b)
Term of office. The term of office shall be three years. Date of appointment is July 1. Members may be appointed to successive terms on the board.
(c)
Residency of board members. Any person appointed to the county library board must be a resident of the county. If he moves his legal residence outside the county, he shall be replaced on the board.
(d)
Vacancies. Vacancies shall be filled in the same manner as appointments are made. If a vacancy occurs prior to the expiration of a board member's term, the new appointee shall complete the unexpired term.
(e)
Compensation; expenses. Board members shall receive no compensation. They may be reimbursed for any reasonable and necessary expenses incurred in the performance of library business, upon approval of the board of commissioners.
(f)
Representatives. The county library board shall appoint two of its members to represent it as members of the Brunswick-Glynn Regional Library System. Such members shall be entrusted with representing and interpreting the interests of the county library board and the entire regional system on the regional board.
(g)
Authority and powers. The library board of trustees shall exercise only such authority and powers as are expressly delegated to it by the board of commissioners, or the laws of the state. The library board shall have no authority to create any debt binding upon the treasury of the county.
(Res. of 5-11-1993, § D(att. 1), art. IV, §§ 1—7; Res. of 12-12-2000)
Sec. 30-55. - Officers.
(a)
Generally. The officers of the library board of trustees shall be a chairman, a vice-chairman, and a secretary elected from those serving on the board. A treasurer may be elected from the board membership, or a nonmember may be appointed to perform the duties of the treasurer. A nonmember appointed to perform the duties of treasurer shall not have a vote and shall be appointed at the same time as election of officers. There shall be no limit on the number of terms he may serve in succession. These officers shall perform the duties as prescribed in this division and by Robert's Rules of Order, Newly Revised, which are not in conflict with this division.
(b)
Officer nominations. Nominations shall be made by a committee of three board members appointed by the chairman of the board. The chairman and/or the library manager may serve as one of the three members on the nominating committee. An officer is elected for a one year term and may succeed himself.
(c)
Term of officers. The officers shall be elected at the January meeting to serve for one year or until their successors are elected, and their terms of office shall begin at the close of the meeting at which they are elected.
(d)
Receiving funds. Should the regional board delegate to the county board the activity of receiving funds, the county board must appoint or elect a treasurer, according to subsection (a) of this section.
(Res. of 5-11-1993, § D(att. 1), art. V, §§ 1—4)
Sec. 30-56. - Committees.
(a)
Executive committee. An executive committee, composed of the duly appointed members who are officers of the board, shall be entrusted to govern in the name of the board of trustees between meetings of the board.
(b)
Standing committee. Standing committees shall be the finance committee, the building and grounds committee, and the committee on constitution and bylaws. Other special committees may be appointed by the chairman as needed.
(Res. of 5-11-1993, § D(att. 1), arts. VI, VII)
Sec. 30-57. - Amendment of constitution.
The constitution from which this division is derived may be amended at any meeting of the board of trustees by a two-thirds vote of the members present, provided that notice is made in writing at least one week prior to the meeting, that a quorum is present, and the amendment is approved by the board of commissioners. All amendments to this division will be filed with the director of the Brunswick-Glynn Regional Library System who will forward them to the division of public library services of the state department of education immediately upon adoption.
(Res. of 5-11-1993, § D(att. 1), art. VIII)

DIVISION 3. - BYLAWS

Sec. 30-81. - Duties and responsibilities of board members.
(a)
Members. The county library board of trustees is one group from which the members of the Brunswick-Glynn Regional Library System board of trustees, the governing body of the Brunswick-Glynn Regional Library System, are drawn.
(b)
Discharge of duties. The county library board of trustees shall discharge those duties delegated to it by the Brunswick-Glynn Regional Library System board of trustees, the laws of the state, and the board of commissioners. All formal actions of the county library board of trustees in discharging those duties shall be reported to the regional board of trustees for approval and recording at the next meeting.
(c)
Policies and practices. The county library board of trustees shall set local policies and practices to include hours of operation, which shall be consistent with the rules and policies of the regional library system.
(d)
Annual budget. The county library board of trustees shall, in consultation with the regional system director and the library manager, prepare and submit the annual county library budget, through its finance committee, together with the fiscal needs of the local and regional libraries, to the board of commissioners.
(e)
Notification of meetings. The regional library system director shall be notified in advance of all meetings of the board or its committees.
(f)
Employment of staff. The county library board shall appoint, with the consent of the board of commissioners, and supervise the library manager and any other necessary staff with the advice of the library manager. The library manager and any other approved staff shall be employed by the board of commissioners, upon consultation with the library board. The library manager and all other library staff shall be employees of the county, subject to and entitled to all rights, privileges and benefits of employees of the county, and the personnel policies and rules as established by the board of commissioners.
(g)
Termination of employees. The library board shall not have the direct authority to terminate any employee, but may recommend any adverse personnel action for a library staff person, including the manager, to the board of commissioners. The library board shall have only such authority in personnel matters as may be expressly delegated to it by the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 5-11-1993, § D(att. 2), art. I, §§ 1—4)
Sec. 30-82. - Duties of the officers.
(a)
Chairman. The chairman shall preside at all regular or called board meetings. He shall appoint all committees and shall be an ex officio (nonvoting) member of all committees.
(b)
Vice-chairman. The vice-chairman shall preside and act for the chairman when the chairman is absent or unavailable. All other duties of the vice-chairman shall be assigned by the chairman.
(c)
Secretary. The secretary shall record the official actions of the board, keep a record of attendance at board meetings, and have the custody of the official books, which shall be housed in the county library. He shall notify the appointing authorities of vacancies which may occur on the county library board. The secretary shall send copies of the minutes of all meetings to the director of the Brunswick-Glynn Regional Library System. He shall also report changes of membership to the director.
(Res. of 5-11-1993, § D(att. 2), art. II, §§ 1—3)
Sec. 30-83. - Funds.
(a)
Authority to receive and administer funds. Should the library board of trustees be delegated authority to receive and administer funds, the library board must appoint a treasurer according to the terms of subsection 30-55(a). The county library board of trustees shall receive and disburse funds in keeping with any regional board authorization, and the budget balanced. A record of all receipts, deposits, and expenditures shall be forwarded to the regional board for its review, approval, and recording at the next meeting. Funds shall be audited annually in accordance with state aid criteria and other state regulations.
(b)
Bonding of employees. The treasurer and any other library employee who handles public funds shall be bonded for an amount commensurate with the amount of funds handled, determined by the county library board and entered in the minutes, with a copy of the bond filed with the division of public library services of the state department of education.
(Res. of 5-11-1993, § D(att. 2), art. II, §§ 4, 5)
Sec. 30-84. - Meetings of the library board.
(a)
Meetings. Meetings of the library board shall be held according to the bylaws of the Brunswick-Glynn Regional Library System, and as to otherwise provided for in this division.
(b)
Schedule and location meetings. The county library board shall hold no less than four regular meetings during each fiscal year, including no less than one each calendar quarter. Meetings shall be held on the second Thursday in the months of January, April, July and October at 3:30 p.m. at the county library, or at some other location as designated by the chairman.
(c)
Notification of meetings. Prior to each regular or called meeting, the chairman or library manager shall notify each member, the regional library director, and the appropriate local media of the date, time, and place of the county board meeting. Called meetings may be convened by the chairman, or upon the request of three members of the board, for the transaction of business stated in the call for the meeting. All meetings shall be held in compliance with the state Open Meetings Act (O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1 et seq.).
(d)
Quorum. Five members of the library board of trustees shall constitute a quorum. No official business may be conducted without a quorum. Except as stated in section 30-87, a simple majority affirmative vote of the quorum present and voting shall be necessary to approve any action before the board.
(Res. of 5-11-1993, § D(att. 2), art. III, §§ 1—4)
Sec. 30-85. - Reports.
The library board shall submit all reports requested by the board of commissioners or the regional library system, and provide the information necessary to complete reports required by state, federal, or local laws or regulations, or to manage the regional library system in an efficient and businesslike manner.
(Res. of 5-11-1993, § D(att. 2), art. IV)
Sec. 30-86. - Attendance.
(a)
A county library board member shall be removed for cause or for failure to attend three consecutive meetings.
(b)
A letter reporting the removal and specifying the cause shall be sent by the chairman to the affected board member and to the authority responsible for his appointment. The local appointing authority shall be asked to appoint another representative to fill that member's unexpired term.
(Res. of 5-11-1993, § D(att. 2), art. IV, §§ 1, 2)
Sec. 30-87. - Amendments.
These bylaws may be amended at any meeting of the board of trustees by a two-thirds vote of the members present, provided that notice is made in writing at least two weeks prior to the meeting, and provided that a quorum is present, and the amendment is approved by the board of commissioners. All amendments to these bylaws shall be filed with the regional director of the Brunswick-Glynn Regional Library System who will forward them to the division of public library services of the state department of education immediately upon adoption.
(Res. of 5-11-1993, § D(att. 2), art. VI)
