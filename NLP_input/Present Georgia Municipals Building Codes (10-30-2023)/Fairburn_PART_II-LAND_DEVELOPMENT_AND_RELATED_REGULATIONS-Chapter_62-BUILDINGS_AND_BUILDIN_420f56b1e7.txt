ARTICLE VIII. - DEMOLITION OF BUILDINGS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 62-285. - Regulations for proceeding with work.
All work of such wrecking or demolition shall be performed in a workmanlike manner and with the least amount of noise possible. Care shall be taken to protect neighboring structures with adequate shoring and whatever else is needful to protect such structures. Signs stating "Wrecking and Demolition Work Going On—No Trespassing" shall be erected on each side of the building that faces on a public street or alley. Adequate protection shall be provided to prevent injury to any city or public utility appurtenances. It shall be the duty of all persons working on or responsible for such wrecking or demolition to see to it that children are warned away from such premises, and are not permitted to play in or on or frequent such structures.
(Code 1966, § 5-36; Ord. No. 69-1, § 6, 8-11-1969)
Sec. 62-286. - Supervision.
The building inspector shall supervise such wrecking or demolition and shall inspect such premises, as he deems necessary.
(Code 1966, § 5-37; Ord. No. 69-1, § 7, 8-11-1969)
Sec. 62-287. - Penalty for violation.
Any person violating any provision of this article shall be fined not less than $10.00 nor more than $100.00 for each offense; and a separate offense shall be deemed committed on each day during or on which a violation occurs or continues.
(Code 1966, § 5-38; Ord. No. 69-1, § 8, 8-11-1969)
Secs. 62-288—62-299. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PERMIT

Sec. 62-300. - Required.
It shall be unlawful to wreck or demolish any building or structure in the city without first securing a permit therefor.
(Code 1966, § 5-31; Ord. No. 69-1, § 1, 8-11-1969)
Sec. 62-301. - Application.
An application for a permit to wreck or demolish any building or structure in the city shall be made in writing to the city clerk, with a copy to the building inspector, and to any utility company serving the premises, and to the owners or agents of adjoining or neighboring premises. Such application shall give the location of the building or structure, the date when wrecking or demolition is to commence and the approximate time which such wrecking or demolition shall take.
(Code 1966, § 5-32; Ord. No. 69-2, § 2, 8-11-1969)
Sec. 62-302. - Inspection of premises.
Before any such permit shall be approved, the building inspector shall inspect the premises where the wrecking and demolition work is to take place, and ascertain that provision for proper care has been made so as not to endanger any sewer or water connections with the city sewer and water systems, or any electrical wires or installations.
(Code 1966, § 5-33; Ord. No. 69-1, § 3, 8-11-1969)
Sec. 62-303. - Approval and issuance of permit.
If the building inspector finds that the terms of this article are being complied with by the applicant he shall approve the application and issue a permit for such wrecking and demolition.
(Code 1966, § 5-34; Ord. No. 69-1, § 4, 8-11-1969)
Sec. 62-304. - Permit fees.
The fee for permits for wrecking or demolition shall be as established by the city council.
(Code 1966, § 5-35; Ord. No. 69-1, § 5, 8-11-1969)
Secs. 62-305—62-321. - Reserved.
