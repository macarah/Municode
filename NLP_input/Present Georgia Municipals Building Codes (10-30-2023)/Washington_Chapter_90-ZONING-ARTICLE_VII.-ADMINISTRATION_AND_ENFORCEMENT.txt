DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 90-801. - Application of chapter.
Except as otherwise provided, no structure or land shall, after June 2, 1997, be used and no structure or part thereof shall be erected, altered or moved unless in conformity with the regulations specified in this chapter for the district in which it is located. In their interpretation and application, the provisions of this chapter shall be considered minimum requirements adopted for the promotion of the public health, safety, morals, convenience, order, prosperity, and general welfare of the community.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-140)
Sec. 90-802. - Enforcing officer.
This chapter shall be administered and enforced by the building official. This official shall have the right to enter upon any premises at reasonable times for the purpose of making inspections of buildings or premises necessary to carry out his duties in the enforcement of this chapter. In addition, he shall issue all building permits and make and maintain records thereof, issue all certificates of occupancy and make and maintain records thereof, and conduct inspections as prescribed by this chapter and other such inspections as are necessary to ensure compliance with this chapter.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-141)
Sec. 90-803. - Building permits.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to commence the excavation for the construction of any building or structure, including accessory structures, or to commence the moving, alteration, or repair of any structure, including accessory structures, until the building official has issued for such work a building permit containing a statement that the plans, specifications and intended use of such structure in all respects conform with this chapter.
(b)
Application for a building permit shall be made in writing to the building official on forms provided for that purpose.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for the building official to approve the plans or issue a building permit for any excavation or construction until he has inspected such plans in detail and found them to be in conformity with this chapter. To this end, the building official shall require that every application for a building permit for excavation, construction, moving, or alteration shall be accompanied by a plan or plat drawn to scale and showing the following in sufficient detail to enable the building official to ascertain whether the proposed excavation, construction, moving or alteration is in conformance with this chapter:
(1)
The actual shape, location, and dimensions of the lot to be built upon.
(2)
The shape, height, use and location on the lot of buildings or structures to be erected or altered.
(3)
The sizes, locations, and use of all existing buildings or structures on the lot.
(4)
The number of dwelling units the building, if residential, is designed to accommodate.
(5)
The setback lines of buildings on adjoining lots.
(6)
The layout of off-street parking and unloading spaces.
(7)
The signature of the county health officer approving the proposed location of the septic tank, and field lines provided public sewerage is not available.
(8)
Such other information concerning the lot or adjoining lots as may be essential for determining whether this chapter is being observed.
(d)
If the proposed excavation, construction, moving or alteration as set forth in the application for a building permit is in conformity with this chapter, the building official shall issue a building permit for such excavation, construction, moving or alteration.
(e)
If the application for a building permit is not approved, the building official shall state in writing on the application the cause for such disapproval. Issuance of a permit shall, in no case, be construed as waiving any section of this chapter, and building permits shall be void after six months from the date of issuance unless substantial progress on the project has been made by that time.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-142)
Sec. 90-804. - Use permit.
The building official shall not issue a building permit for a conditional use until such conditional use is approved by the planning commission and a conditional use permit issued therefor.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-143)
Sec. 90-805. - Certificate of occupancy.
No land or building or other structure or part thereof erected, moved, or altered in its use shall be occupied or used until the building official has issued a certificate of occupancy. A certificate of occupancy shall be issued only if the building or premises is found to conform to this chapter. If the certificate of occupancy is not granted upon request, the building official shall state in writing the reasons the certificate was not granted.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-144)
Sec. 90-806. - Procedure for zoning change.
The procedures in this section shall be required when any person other than the mayor and council or planning commission requests a change in the zoning district boundaries. The mayor and council may from time to time amend, supplement, or change by ordinance the boundaries on the zoning districts established on the zoning map of the city or the regulations set forth in this chapter. Any proposed amendment, supplement, or change shall first be submitted to the planning commission for its recommendation. Before the mayor and council takes action resulting in a zoning decision, a public hearing shall be conducted. The procedures for advertising and holding the public hearing shall be as follows:
(1)
Any person desiring a zoning change in the existing boundaries of any zoning district shall first file a petition requesting such change with the city administrator, together with a plat of the property to be changed and the rezoning fee prescribed by the mayor and council from time to time and filed in the office of the city administrator.
(2)
Within 15 days after receiving the petition, the city clerk shall in writing inform the applicant of the date on which a public hearing will be held by the mayor and council for the zoning change.
(3)
Upon filing of a petition for a zoning change and receiving notice of the date of the public hearing concerning the petitioned request, the applicant shall at his expense erect or cause to be erected in a conspicuous place on the property in question a sign giving public notice of both the existing zoning district in which the property is located and the requested change, the scheduled date for the public hearing, and the name and address of the property owner. Such sign shall be not less than nine square feet in area with not less than two-inch black letters upon a white background stating the required information.
(4)
At least 15 days but not more than 45 days prior to the scheduled public hearing, the city clerk shall advertise such hearing in a newspaper of general circulation in the city and shall notify all property owners within 400 feet of the subject property by mail.
(5)
Prior to the public hearing the planning commission shall prepare an assessment and recommendation on the proposal using the following criteria, and shall advise the mayor and council of its findings:
a.
Existing uses and zoning of nearby property.
b.
The extent to which property values are diminished by the particular zoning restrictions.
c.
The extent to which the destruction of property values of the plaintiff promotes the health, safety, morals, or general welfare of the public.
d.
The relative gain to the public, as compared to the hardship imposed upon the individual property owner.
e.
The suitability of the subject property for the zoned purposes.
f.
The length of time the property has been vacant as zoned considered in the context of land development in the area in the vicinity of the property.
g.
Conformity with or divergence from the land use plan.
(6)
Copies of the draft, zoning ordinance and map, plus the evaluation of the proposed district boundary changes, will be made available for public review during normal working hours between the date of the appearance of the public hearing notice and the time of the public hearing.
(7)
The public hearing shall be conducted by the mayor and council.
(8)
The mayor and council will review for those present procedures and set forth the operating procedures for the hearing as follows:
a.
In order for a person in attendance to speak, the chair must recognize the person. Upon rising to speak, the person recognized will first identify himself. The chair may also request that the person furnish a home or business street address, as appropriate.
b.
The person speaking will be allowed a reasonable time to express opinions and make points on each separate element of the proposed revisions which the person wishes to address, with a maximum total of ten minutes allowed per person.
c.
Additional persons will be recognized per the procedure in this subsection for the purpose of addressing additional elements of the proposed revision or to make additional points with regard to elements already addressed, but not to rehash points already made.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-155)
Sec. 90-807. - Violations, penalties and remedies.
(a)
A violation of any section of this chapter is declared to be a misdemeanor and, upon conviction, shall be punished as provided in section 1-8.
(b)
If any building or structure is or is proposed to be erected, constructed, reconstructed, altered, converted, or maintained or if any building, structure or land is or is proposed to be used in violation of this chapter, the building official, city attorney, or other appropriate authority of the city or any adjacent or neighboring property owner who would be specially damaged by violation may, in addition to other remedies, institute injunction, mandamus, or other appropriate action or proceeding to prevent such unlawful erection, construction, reconstruction, alteration, conversion, maintenance, or use or to correct or abate such violation or to prevent the occupancy of the building, structure, or land.
(c)
Each and every day such unlawful erection, construction, reconstruction, alteration, conversion, maintenance, or use continues shall be deemed a separate offense.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-156)
Sec. 90-808. - Conflict with other laws.
Whenever the provisions of any ordinance, resolution or other statute require more restrictive standards than are required by this chapter, the provisions of such other ordinance, resolution, or statute shall govern.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-158)
Sec. 90-809. - Effective date.
This chapter shall take effect from and after the effective date of its passage and publication as required by law.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-159)

Sec. 90-836. - Established.
(a)
A board of zoning appeals is established to hear and decide appeals and to authorize variances in the terms of this chapter.
(b)
The board of zoning appeals shall consist of at least three members and not more than five members. Members appointed to the board shall hold no other public office except that one may also be a member of the planning commission. The members shall serve for overlapping terms of three years. The chairman shall be elected by the board from its membership. The board shall adopt rules in accordance with this chapter for the conduct of its affairs. Meetings of the board shall be held at the call of the chairman and at such other times as the board may determine.
(c)
There shall be an additional three individuals appointed to the board of zoning appeals whose duties shall be to hear and decide appeals and to authorize variances only as allowed in this chapter in all matters pertaining to signs and other outdoor advertising as set forth in chapter 58. In these matters relating to signs and outdoor advertising, this committee shall act as the board of zoning appeals.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-145)
Sec. 90-837. - Appeals.
An appeal to the board of zoning appeals may be taken by any person aggrieved or by any governmental officer, department, board, or bureau affected by any decision of the building official based in whole or in part upon this chapter. Such appeal shall be taken by filing with the board of zoning appeals a notice of appeal specifying the grounds thereof. The building official shall transmit to the board all papers constituting the record upon which the action appealed was taken. The board shall fix a reasonable time for the hearing of the appeal or other matters referred to it, shall give public notice thereof in a newspaper of general circulation in the city at least 15 days before the hearing, and shall decide the appeal within a reasonable period of time. Any person may appear at the hearing in person, by agent, or by attorney.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-146)
Sec. 90-838. - Powers; appeals from decisions.
(a)
The board of zoning appeals shall have the power to:
(1)
Hear and decide appeals where it is alleged by the appellant that there is error in any order, requirement, permit, decision, determination, or refusal made by the building official or other administrative official in carrying out or enforcing any section of this chapter.
(2)
Hear requests for interpretation of the zoning map, and for the decision of any special questions upon which the board of zoning appeals is authorized to pass.
(3)
Hear and decide applications for variances from the terms of this chapter.
(4)
Hear and decide appeals from the approval or disapproval of the planning commission of the application for a conditional use permit.
(b)
Any person aggrieved by any decision of the board of zoning appeals or any taxpayer or any officer, department or board of the city may present to a court of record a petition, duly verified, setting forth that such decision is illegal, in whole or in part, and specifying the grounds of such illegality. Such petition shall be presented to the court within ten days after the filing of the decision complained of in the offices of the board and not thereafter.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-147)
Sec. 90-839. - Variances.
(a)
After written denial of a building permit, a property owner may make an application for a variance from this chapter, using the form available.
(b)
Upon receipt of an application and payment of the variance fee, as prescribed from time to time by the mayor and council and filed in the office of the city administrator, with the city administrator, the board of zoning appeals shall hold a public hearing, after having first given public notice thereof in a newspaper of general circulation in the city 15 days prior to the date of the hearing, and will notify all property owners within 400 feet of the property in question by mail of the variance request. The board shall consider and decide all applications for variances within 30 days of such public hearing and in accordance with the standards provided in subsection (c) of this section.
(c)
Variances from the terms of this chapter may be granted in such individual hardship cases of practical difficulty or unnecessary hardship upon a finding by the board of zoning appeals based on the following criteria:
(1)
Variances shall be granted only where special circumstances or conditions fully described in the findings of the board do not apply generally to the district.
(2)
Variances shall not be granted to allow a use otherwise excluded from the particular district in which requested.
(3)
For reasons fully set forth in the findings of the board, the special circumstances or conditions are such that the strict application of this chapter would deprive the applicant of any reasonable use of his land. Mere loss in value shall not justify a variance. There must be deprivation of beneficial use of land.
(4)
The granting of any variance shall be in harmony with the general purposes and intent of this chapter and shall not be injurious to the neighborhood, detrimental to the public welfare, or in conflict with the comprehensive plan for development.
(5)
In reviewing an application for a variance, the burden of showing that the variance should be granted shall be upon the person applying therefor.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-148)

DIVISION 3. - PROCEDURES FOR AUTHORIZING CONDITIONAL USES

Sec. 90-866. - Application to planning commission.
(a)
An application requesting a conditional use of land or structure as provided for in the zoning district regulations shall be filed with the planning commission for review. The application shall show the location and intended uses of the site, the name of the property owners and existing land uses within 400 feet, and other material pertinent to the request which the planning commission may require.
(b)
Every applicant for a conditional use permit shall be charged a fee, as prescribed from time to time by the mayor and council and filed in the office of the city administrator.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-149)
Sec. 90-867. - Restrictions.
In the exercise of its approval of a conditional use, the planning commission may impose such conditions regarding the location, character, or other features of the proposed uses or buildings as it may deem advisable and necessary in the furtherance of the general purposes of this chapter.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-150)
Sec. 90-868. - Validity of plans.
All approved plans, conditions, restrictions, and rules made part of the approval of the conditional use by the planning commission shall constitute certification on the part of the applicant that the proposed use shall conform to such regulations.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-151)
Sec. 90-869. - Use permit.
Upon approval of all plans, including any restrictions, as provided in this division, the planning commission shall issue a conditional use permit.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-152)
Sec. 90-870. - Time limit.
Every application for a conditional use under this chapter shall be decided within 45 days of the date of application, and the applicant shall be provided with a written notice of either approval or denial.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-153)
Sec. 90-871. - Public hearing.
Prior to taking action on any request for a conditional use permit, the planning commission shall hold a public hearing. The procedures for advertising and holding the public hearing shall be as provided in subsections 90-806(2), (3) and (4), except that the planning commission shall conduct the hearing.
(Ord. of 6-2-1997, § 19-154)
Secs. 90-872—90-900. - Reserved.
