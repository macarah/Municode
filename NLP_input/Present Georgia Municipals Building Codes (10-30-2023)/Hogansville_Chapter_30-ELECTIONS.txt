Sec. 30-1. - Conduct of municipal elections.
(a)
The mayor and council of the city authorizes the judge of the probate court of the county to conduct any or all elections within the city pursuant to the provisions of the Georgia Election Code (O.C.G.A. tit. 21, ch. 2, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.) and to perform all duties as superintendent of elections as specified therein.
(b)
With reference to any such election, the city shall pay to the county probate judge the actual cost incurred in performing those election functions requested to be performed.
(c)
The city clerk is designated the clerk for handling qualification of candidates.
(Ord. of 9-18-1995, § 2-2-1)
State Law reference— Superintendent, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-70 et seq.; poll officers, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-90 et seq.; duties of superintendent, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-400 et seq.
Sec. 30-2. - Voter registration deadline.
The deadline for registration of voters in city elections shall be pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 21-2-224.
(Code 1988, § 2-2-2)
State Law reference— Registration of voters, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-224.
Sec. 30-3. - Notice of candidacy; filing dates.
Notice of the opening and closing dates for candidates to qualify shall be published in the call for election pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 21-2-132.
(Code 1988, § 2-2-3)
Editor's note— This section of the 1988 Code has been changed to conform to state statute.
State Law reference— Filing notice of candidacy, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-132.
Sec. 30-4. - Qualification fees.
The qualification fee shall be three percent of the total gross salary of the office paid in the preceding calendar year including all supplements authorized by law if a salaried office; provided, however, that for the offices of clerk of the superior court, judge of the probate court, sheriff, tax commissioner, and magistrate, the qualifying fee shall be three percent of the minimum salary provided by general law for the office, exclusive of cost-of-living increases and longevity increases. If not a salaried office, a reasonable fee shall be set by the city, such fee not to exceed three percent of the income derived from such city office by the person holding the office for the preceding year or more than $35.00 for a municipal office.
(Code 1988, § 2-2-4)
State Law reference— Fixing and publishing qualification fees, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-131; filing notice of candidacy, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-132.
Sec. 30-5. - Polling place.
The polling place for all general and special primaries and all general and special elections to be held in the city is hereby designated as the Hogansville Public Library, located at 310 Johnson Street in the city.
(Ord. No. 17-0306 , § 1, 3-6-2017)
State Law reference— Polling places, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-260 et seq.
Sec. 30-6. - Votes required for election.
The number of votes required for election to the office of mayor and council shall be a plurality of the votes cast.
(Ord. of 1-7-2002)
State Law reference— Number of votes required for election, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-501.
