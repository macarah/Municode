Sec. 10-1. - Billiard rooms.
(a)
As used in this section:
(1)
Billiard room means any public place where a person is permitted to play the game of billiards and for which a charge is made for use of equipment.
(2)
Billiards means any of several games played on a table surrounded by an elastic ledge of cushions with balls which are impelled by a cue and shall include all forms of the games known as "carom billiards," "pocket billiards" and "English billiards."
(b)
No billiard room shall operate between the hours of 12:00 midnight and 6:00 a.m.
(c)
Alcoholic beverages may not be sold, served or allowed to be used in or on the premises of a billiard room or any place operated in connection therewith unless such premises or establishment is an establishment which is authorized to sell alcoholic beverages and derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross revenues from the sale of products or services other than alcoholic beverages.
(d)
This section shall not apply to billiard tables or billiard rooms operated by private industrial concerns, Young Men's Christian Associations, religious orders, charitable institutions, state, county or city institutions, fraternal orders or bona fide clubs using such tables for members or employees only.
(Ord. No. 371, § 9-3-1, 3-12-93; Ord. No. 477, § 1, 9-21-99)
State Law reference— Authority to license and regulate billiard rooms, O.C.G.A. § 43-8-2.
Sec. 10-2. - Pinball machines and other coin-operated games.
Generally. There shall be no gambling of any kind permitted, either directly or indirectly, in using pinball machines and other coin-operated game machines or devices, whether mechanical, electronic or video. No such pinball machine or other coin-operated game machine or device shall be permitted where the property line of a place of business containing any such pinball machine or other coin-operated game machine or device is located within 200 yards of the property line of any college or school, measured along the most direct route, with the most direct route being defined as a straight line from property line to property line.
(Code 1982, §§ 9-3-2, 9-3-3; Ord. No. 477, § 1, 9-21-99)
State Law reference— Exchange of free replay for anything of value unlawful, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(c); coin-operated amusement machines, O.C.G.A. § 48-17-1 et seq.
