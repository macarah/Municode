ARTICLE II. - RECREATION COMMITTEE[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
Cross reference— Boards, commissions and authorities, § 2-231 et seq.

Sec. 50-31. - Created.
A committee to be known as the Ringgold Recreation Committee is created and established.
(Res. of 4-9-65)
Sec. 50-32. - Status.
The recreation committee shall be the advisory and planning committee of the recreation department.
(Res. of 4-9-65)
Sec. 50-33. - Ex officio members.
The recreation committee shall have the right to appoint as many ex officio members as it may deem necessary.
(Res. of 4-9-65)
Sec. 50-34. - Duties, responsibilities, rights.
The recreation committee shall have the following duties, responsibilities and rights:
(1)
Submit its recommendations on long-range planning on the recreation program and on the planning and designing of all physical improvements on city property dedicated for parks and recreation and their operation.
(2)
Make such recommendations as it sees fit in hiring such personnel to operate the city's recreation center.
(3)
Acquaint itself with the financial operation of the recreation center by an examination of the books showing the financial condition of the recreation department. The committee shall make available to the city any books that it might keep during the course of its operations.
(4)
Raise moneys, seek donations in any manner as it sees fit as long as it does not obligate the city in any manner for any money or other obligations.
(5)
Spend any funds that might be raised as long as such funds are spent for the benefit of the city's recreation center.
(Res. of 4-9-65)
Sec. 50-35. - Property of city.
All properties purchased by the recreation committee shall become the property of the city.
(Res. of 4-9-65)
