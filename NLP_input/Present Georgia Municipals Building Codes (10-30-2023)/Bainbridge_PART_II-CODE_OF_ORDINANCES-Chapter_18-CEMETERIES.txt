Sec. 18-1. - General Definitions.
modified
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Bronze marker means a memorial bronze or other cast metal projecting above ground level no more than one-half inch.
Burial space or grave space means a space of ground in a cemetery intended to be used for the interment in the ground of human remains.
City cemetery means any tract of land located within the city currently utilized for the burial of the dead and designated as a City of Bainbridge Municipal Cemetery. Any private cemetery located within the city shall be a perpetual care type and it shall comply with the Georgia Cemetery and Funeral Services Act of 2000, if applicable. Any such private cemetery shall file a copy of its annual State of Georgia registration renewal, if applicable, with the clerk's office no later than September 1 of each year along with a filing fee of $50.00 per year payable to the City of Bainbridge. No individual family cemeteries will be allowed within the city limits of Bainbridge.
Committee means the cemetery committee as selected under the terms of this chapter.
Corner marker means a marker buried flush with the ground at the inside corners lots, the maximum dimension of which is six inches by six inches.
Cremation means the reduction of the dead human body to residue by intense heat or any mechanical, chemical, thermal, or other professionally accepted process. Cremation also includes any other mechanical, chemical, thermal, or other professionally accepted process whereby human remains are pulverized, burned, recremated, or otherwise further reduced in size or quantity
Final disposition means the final disposal of a dead human body whether it is by, but not limited to, earth interment, above-ground interment, and cremation, or delivery to a medical institution for lawful dissection if the medical institution assumes responsibility for disposal.
Funeral home means an establishment in which the dead are prepared for burial or cremation and in which wakes and funerals may be held.
Ground cover means the number, in inches, of soil material to be deposited over the burial vault and/or casket at ground level.
Human remains means the bodies of deceased human beings and includes the bodies in any stage of decomposition and the cremated remains.
Interment means the burial of human remains but shall not include the opening and closing of a grave space, crypt, or niche or the installation of a vault.
Inurnment means the disposition of the cremated remains of a deceased human being in any fashion, including without limitation in a columbarium niche, cremorial, cremation bench, cremation rock, urn, or other container but shall not include the opening and closing of a grave space, crypt, or niche or the installation of a vault.
Lot means that tract of land capable of accommodating the burial of two individual outer burial containers.
Mausoleum means an aboveground structure erected for the purpose of the interment of deceased bodies.
Monument means a memorial structure consisting of granite, stone, marble or other designated manmade material marking the lot or individual burial space in which a burial has or will take place.
Niche means a space used, or intended to be used, for the interment of the cremated remains of one or more deceased human beings. This term may be used for cremation burial space.
Notification of burial means that notice requested to be given the city representative by the mortician or other responsible party of date and time of burial.
Outer burial container or vault means an enclosure into which a casket is placed and includes, but is not limited to, containers made of concrete, steel, fiberglass, copper or other metals, polypropylene, sectional concrete enclosures, and crypts.
Permanent marker means a marker made of a permanent material such as concrete, marble, masonry, iron, steel, or metal and identifying the name of the individual with date buried, carved, etched, sandblasted or stamped into the marker.
Permit means that document allowing burial in a city cemetery. The permit shall be obtained prior to burial from a designated city department.
Plantings means any growth of herbaceous, annual and perennial flowers permitted to be planted on a lot or lot within the cemetery as designated by the city's representatives.
Rules means the rules and regulations as may be from time to time adopted by the council.
Trust means the trust created by this chapter.
Trustees means the trustees as selected in accordance with the provisions of this chapter.
Urn means a container in which the cremated remains are placed.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
State Law reference— Definitions relating to cemeteries, O.C.G.A. § 44-3-131.
Sec. 18-2. - Care and maintenance plans.
modified
Perpetual care and maintenance for graves in any municipal cemetery shall be provided by the city, a portion of the fee for which shall be included in the purchase price of a lot.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
Sec. 18-3. - Rules and regulations—Authority to adopt, amend, or repeal.
modified
The city council may from time to time adopt, amend or repeal such rules governing city cemeteries, including, but not limited to, rules governing interment, maintenance, monuments and marking, construction of walks, walls, etc., price of lots and annual and perpetual care and maintenance, planting shrubbery and flowers, and such other rules which may be deemed wise in the judgment of the council. All lots in every city cemetery shall be governed by the rules in force as adopted by the city council.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
Sec. 18-4. - Trust—Creation; composition; authority; funds.
modified
There is hereby created a trust for the Oak City Cemetery to be known as the Oak City Cemetery Trust.
(1)
The trust shall be administered by seven trustees, being the six council members of the city, and the mayor to serve as chair and such members shall serve as trustees during their terms as mayor and council members.
(2)
The trust and the trustees shall have all the rights, powers, privileges, duties and authority accorded to trusts and trustees under the laws of the State of Georgia and the Charter of the city.
(3)
Funds received by the trustees, whether as provided in this chapter or by gifts, donations or otherwise, shall be deposited to the credit of the trust according to the terms of this chapter and the terms of such gifts, donations, etc., and invested in lawful trust investments as prescribed by the laws of the state.
(4)
All funds and properties, including the proceeds therefrom, of such trust shall be used in accordance with the Charter and the provisions of this chapter.
(5)
The trustees may, from time to time, transfer proceeds from the trust to the general funds of the city to be used only for care, maintenance, improvements and land acquisition for the Oak City Cemetery.
(6)
The trustees may designate one or more of the trustees to act for such trust for the purpose of transferring funds and properties by check, draft or otherwise.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
State Law reference— Powers and duties of trustees, O.C.G.A. § 53-13-50 et seq.
Sec. 18-5. - Same—Percentage of payments committed.
modified
A minimum of 50 percent of the purchase price of the lots in Oak City Cemetery shall be committed to the trust.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
Sec. 18-6. - Sale of lots and niches.
modified
(a)
No cemetery lot, burial space, or niche space shall be deeded or contracted to any person under any circumstances without the payment of the price therefor in advance. Any person desiring to purchase a burial space, lot, or niche space within a city cemetery may do so by depositing with the city the full cost of the said space selected. At the time of purchase, the purchaser shall be issued a receipt upon which is noted the burial space, niche space and/or lot number, as well as the cemetery section designation, the purchase price and name and address of purchaser and any other necessary information required by the city.
(b)
In the event that a purchaser of a lot or space in the oak city cemetery desires to resell such lot or space, the purchaser must offer in writing the same to the city at the original selling price of lots or spaces, and the city shall have 30 days from the receipt of such offer in which to purchase the lot or space. In the event the city does not purchase the lot or space in 30 days, the owner shall be free to sell the same to any buyer he may select under the terms, conditions and prices decided between them.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
Sec. 18-7. - Grave site openings—Permit required.
modified
Any person desiring to inter the remains of a dead person within the city limits is hereby required to apply for and obtain a permit from the city prior to the burial. The permit fee required shall be set by the mayor and city council and kept on file in the office of the city manager. In order to obtain such permit it shall be necessary that the person exhibit to the city a deed to the property to be used for such purpose. In no event, however, shall a permit be issued for a burial on any property that has not been approved by the mayor and city council after public hearing for such use. The public hearing shall be advertised and conducted as required in the Bainbridge Zoning Ordinance for special permission request.
(a)
City responsibility. It shall be the responsibility of the city's representative upon receipt of the permit fee to locate the grave site and to mark same in such manner as to be clearly identifiable to the party responsible for opening the grave site. In no event shall any party attempt to open a grave site prior to identification of the site by the city.
(b)
The inurnment of ashes in a city cemetery requires a permit as defined under the regulations set forth in 18-3(a) of this chapter.
(c)
Opening of grave. The opening of a grave in a city cemetery without a permit shall be an offense punishable as provided in section 54-15 of the City of Bainbridge Municipal Code.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
Sec. 18-8. - Grave site openings—Specifications.
modified
(a)
Dimensions. All burial spaces or graves shall have an opening sufficient in size to accommodate the casket and outer burial container.
(b)
Ground cover. All burial spaces or graves shall be dug to a depth sufficient to allow a minimum ground cover of 18 inches.
(c)
Material disposal. All excess soil removed from a grave site shall be deposited by the person authorized to perform such work at a site designated by the city representative.
(d)
Soil removal methods. In no event shall the removal of excess material result in the damage to surrounding lots and/or lots, cause ruts to develop, cause damage to surrounding trees, shrubbery, flowers, monuments, roads, drives, walkways, etc. In the event of such damage the funeral home or other company or person responsible shall pay to the city its cost of repair of such damage.
(e)
Soil compaction. All grave sites opened shall have the soil returned to the site fully compacted to prevent the settling of such soil after completion of the burial. The costs of any settling at the grave site that takes place within one year of the burial which requires city personnel to correct shall be charged to the party responsible for the original grave site opening.
(f)
Outer burial container required. For all burials in any city cemetery, the city will require, in addition to a casket, an outer burial container approved by city officials that is made of such material and of such thickness as to render the grave site impervious to eventual cave-ins (materials such as concrete, one and one-half inches thick, coated metals of 12 gauge or thicker, or manmade materials impervious to decay). Wooden boxes do not comply with this requirement.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
Sec. 18-9. - Lot and/or lot corner markings.
modified
(a)
Permit required. If the owner and/or heirs wish to designate the location of any burial space or lot within any city cemetery with the installation of corner markers, a permit to do so shall be obtained by the monument installer from the community and economic development department. All corner markers shall be a maximum of six inches by six inches in size and shall be set flush with surrounding ground level on the inside corner of the lot or lot so marked.
(b)
Permit fee. A fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges shall be charged the applicant for a corner marking location/installation to assist in defraying the cost to the city for location of corner marking sites on behalf of the applicant.
(c)
Debris removal. It shall be the responsibility of the party installing corner markers to remove from the cemetery all excess soil, etc., resulting from installation of the corner markers. If such debris, soil, etc., is not removed within five working days from the installation of same, the city shall remove such material at the expense of the responsible party.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
Sec. 18-10. - Monuments.
modified
(a)
Installation permit required, fee. There is hereby required a permit for the installation of any monument and/or bronze marker within any city cemetery. The party responsible for installation of such monument and/or marker, prior to such installation, shall make application for a permit to install same to the designated city department and pay the designated department a fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges. Such fee is established for the purpose of helping in defraying cost to the city in locating on behalf of the applicant the lot/lot on which such monument/marker shall be installed.
(b)
Monument base. The base on which all monuments and/or markers are to be located shall consist of poured concrete to a depth and size sufficient to support the monument/marker without cracking. All such bases shall not protrude above surrounding ground level in excess of one inch.
(c)
Material disposal. The removal of all excess material resulting from the installation of a monument and/or marker base shall be the responsibility of the person making such installation. If all such removal is not completed within five working days from date of installation, the city may remove same and charge all costs therefor to the party obtaining the installation permit.
(d)
Damage to property. The party responsible for the installation of the monument/marker base or the monument/marker shall be responsible for the cost of report of any damage to property incurred within the cemetery as a result of such installation.
(e)
Bronze markers. The city may, in its discretion, designate specific locations within a city cemetery wherein only bronze plaques or other similar type markers will be permitted. When so done, the owners of lots within the designated area shall not be permitted to erect monuments/markers, etc., other than those so designated.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
Sec. 18-11. - Number of burials per lot.
modified
There shall be no more than one burial per space within any city cemetery, except in the case of cremated remains where the burial space was purchased prior to April 20, 2021. In the case of cremated remains or infant burials, two burials per grave space will be permitted. Each such burial shall be considered a separate burial unless needed at the same time.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
Sec. 18-12. - Disinterment, removal of human remains; permission required.
modified
No human remains shall be disinterred or removed from any place in the city without permission from the county board of health and a permit issued by the city. A fee in the amount established by resolution and on file with the city clerk shall be paid to the city prior to the issuance of such a permit.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
Sec. 18-13. - Unauthorized entry.
modified
It shall be unlawful for any person to climb, jump or go through or over the fence of any city cemetery, or enter a cemetery in any manner except through a gate.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
Sec. 18-14. - Times of admittance.
new
It shall be unlawful for any person to remain in any of the city cemeteries after sundown, nor shall anyone be allowed to enter therein before sunup except in cases where the city sponsors an event.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
Sec. 18-15. - Deeds.
new
Cemetery deeds issued by the city in any of the city cemeteries shall be nontransferable except upon strict compliance with section 18-2(c), above.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
Sec. 18-16. - Subdivision of lots.
new
The subdivision of lots, burial space or niche spaces by any person shall be prohibited and no person shall be buried in any burial space or niche space not having interest therein except by written consent of all parties interested in the lot, burial space or niche space.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
Sec. 18-17. - Schedule of charges.
new
The cost of a lot, burial space, or niche space in any of the city cemeteries shall be such an amount as determined by the mayor and city council from time to time. A list of said charges shall be kept on file at the office of the city manager.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
Sec. 18-18. - Recording of deeds.
new
It shall be the duty of the city clerk to keep a docket in which shall be recorded the city's title to the city cemeteries and all titles granted. Such titles shall be recorded within three days after they have been issued and the docket shall have a numerical index as to cemetery block and lot number, as well as a name index as to the purchaser of said block and lot.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
Sec. 18-19. - General appearance.
new
Any faux flowers or flags left on grave sites shall be removed when they are/or become torn, faded and/or are in a state unbecoming of their original display purposes. Otherwise, the city shall have the right to enter upon said lots or single grave spaces and remove and properly dispose of said shrubs, fences, or any faux flowers, flags, planted flowers, eliminate grave mounds, and cut high growth or grass that impairs the overall beauty and dignity of the cemetery. When it is necessary for the city to enter said lots or single grave spaces to perform such work, the city shall not be liable for any damage to property or monuments located on said lots or single grave spaces.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
Sec. 18-20. - Plantings.
new
(a)
The owners of a lots or lots within a city cemetery shall be restricted to planting only herbaceous, annual and perennial flowers on such lots.
(b)
No trees or woody plants shall be planted on any lot in any city cemetery except in the case where it has been approved and designated on the master plan for that cemetery.
(c)
The city horticulturist or their designee, with approval by the city manager, shall have the right and power to have removed from any owner's lot any tree, shrub or plant deemed unsafe and likely to injure any of the improvements in the cemetery.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
Sec. 18-21. - Desecration of burial site.
new
The destruction, theft or disturbance of any monument, marker or mausoleum, or their defacement in any manner, shall constitute a desecration of the grave site and shall be punishable by applicable law.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
Sec. 18-22. - Burial of animals prohibited.
new
The burial of animals of any nature shall not be permitted within any city cemetery.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
Sec. 18-23. - Penalty.
new
Violation of the provisions of this article shall be punishable as provided in section 1-7 in the Bainbridge Code of Ordinances.
(Ord. of 6-15-2021(2))
