Sec. 29-31. - Office created; law enforcement unit of state.
(a)
There is created the office of county marshal for the county.
(b)
The county marshal's office shall do all things necessary to be recognized as a law enforcement unit of the state and certified under the standards of the Georgia Peace Officer Standards and Training Act, as provided in O.C.G.A. § 35-8-1 et seq.
(Ord. of 10-18-94(1), §§ 1, 2)
Sec. 29-32. - Powers and duties.
(a)
The county marshal's office shall have full powers of arrest in the enforcement of all county ordinances and the laws of the state.
(b)
The primary duties and responsibilities of the office of county marshal are to:
(1)
Enforce all ordinances enacted by the board of commissioners of the county.
(2)
Issue warnings and citations for court appearances for the violation of county ordinances.
(3)
Investigate and collect evidence of these ordinance violations.
(4)
Respond to complaints of alleged ordinance violations.
(5)
Appear and testify in court.
(6)
Do any follow-up work that may be necessary.
(7)
Ensure proper collection of fines.
(8)
Work in conjunction with the building official/zoning administrator.
(9)
Coordinate enforcement efforts with the Peach County/Fort Valley building and zoning department, the county public works department and any other county department or division as may be necessary.
(10)
Perform such other duties as may be assigned.
(Ord. of 10-18-94(1), §§ 3, 4)
Sec. 29-33. - Chief marshal.
There shall be a chief marshal appointed by the county administrator and approved by the board of commissioners. The chief marshal shall be responsible for developing office policy and organization and for the appointment of such deputy marshals as may be approved by the board of commissioners.
(Ord. of 10-18-94(1), § 7)
Sec. 29-34. - Requirements for officers.
All officers of the county marshal's office shall meet the following requirements:
(1)
All officers of the county marshal's office shall be certified pursuant to the Georgia Peace Officer Standards and Training Act as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 35-8-1 et seq. or certified within a 12-month period after employment.
(2)
All officers shall be experienced in the issuance of citations and warnings, and court appearances may be required as a condition of employment.
(3)
All officers must be at least 19 years of age, have a high school diploma or GED and a valid Georgia driver's license.
(4)
All officers must have a clean criminal history.
(Ord. of 10-18-94(1), § 5)
Sec. 29-35. - Jurisdiction.
The office of county marshal shall be within the operational jurisdiction of the office of the county administrator, under the direct supervision of the building official/zoning administrator.
(Ord. of 10-18-94(1), § 6)
