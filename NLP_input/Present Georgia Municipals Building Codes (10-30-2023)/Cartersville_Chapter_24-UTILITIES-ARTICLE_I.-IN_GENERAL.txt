ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 24-1. - Application for city-provided utility service.
(a)
All applications for water, gas, telecommunications, stormwater, solid waste, electricity or other city utility service shall be made at the city clerk's office, on forms provided by said office, and the location of the premises shall be stated.
(b)
Additionally, prior to city issuing a new account or transferring an account to a new location, to an individual or business for utility service, the applicant must provide the following:
(1)
Individual.
a.
One (1) of the following forms of identification: If a U.S. citizen, a picture identification card, being a valid state issued driver's license, or state issued identification card. In the alternative, if not a U.S. citizen, a valid Visa, employment authorization card, permanent residence card, or other approved immigration document indicating the applicant's legal right to be in the United States.
b.
Social Security Card or a Social Security Number that the city clerk's office has been able to verify as belonging to the applicant either through e-verify or other similar program.
c.
Other documentation as the city clerk may determine is necessary to determine the identity of the applicant.
(2)
Business. The following forms of identification and authorization:
a.
Authorization to create utility account for business; and
b.
Tax ID number or Social Security Number of business.
c.
If an individual, applying for a business account one (1) of the following forms of identification: If a United States citizen, a picture identification card, being a valid state issued driver's license, or state issued identification card. In the alternative, if not a U.S. citizen, a valid Visa, employment authorization card, permanent residence card, visa or other approved immigration document indicating the applicant's legal right to be in the United States.
(c)
In the event an entity or individual is unable to provide the requested information, the city clerk is not authorized to issue a new account or transfer an account to a new location, the city clerk is authorized to not accept said application and to refuse service.
(d)
Additionally, in the event of an applicant's failure to provide the above requested items, the applicant may provide other valid state issued or federal issued documents to assist in determining their identity, and in the event the city clerk's office can verify said documents, the city clerk may authorize said account to be open.
(e)
The city clerk may hold an application pending verification of the supplied documentation, for up to thirty (30) days. After thirty (30) days the city must either accept or reject said application as provided for herein.
(Ord. No. 36-08, § 1, 10-2-08)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 36-08, § 1, adopted Oct. 2, 2008, repealed the former § 24-1 and enacted a new § 24-1 as set out herein. The former § 24-1 pertained to similar subject matter. See the Code Comparative Table for complete derivation.
Sec. 24-2. - Accessibility of property to city employees.
Where meters or meter boxes are placed on the premises of a customer, a suitable place shall be provided by the customer therefor, unobstructed and accessible at all times by the meter readers or other city employees having jurisdiction or authority in such matters.
(Code 1976, § 6-2002)
Sec. 24-3. - Gas department service calls.
The city gas department shall not make service calls to inspect appliances or furnaces, shall not light pilot lights or perform any service from the gas meter into a home or business establishment, other than new service, the responsibility of the gas department not extending beyond the gas meter or city lines. However, the gas superintendent may deem it necessary to relight pilot lights after interruption of service.
(Code 1976, § 6-2003)
Sec. 24-4. - Interference with city property prohibited; responsibility.
(a)
All persons are prohibited from interfering with or disturbing in any manner or form, any electricity, gas or water meters, switches, locks, cutoffs, corporation cocks, above and below ground utility lines and infrastructures which includes fiber, gas, water, sewer, storm sewer, or electricity or other appliances of the city. Additionally, appropriation or use any gas or water or sewer or electricity after service has been cut off or discontinued for the nonpayment of the charges therefore or for other cause. The head of the household, property owner, or if a contractor is responsible they shall be held responsible for the violation of this section. Penalties of up to one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) shall be assessed.
(b)
Tampering with meters includes, but is not limited to, placement of any device that modifies, alters, nullifies or adjust the accuracy of the city meter that is in place. This includes any device that is mechanical, hydraulic, or electric that creates a pulse, harmonic, or interference in any way with city meters.
(c)
Additionally, anyone convicted of tampering with a meter is responsible for reimbursement of all lost charges and all taxes, costs and expenses for the use of said utilities due to said tampering.
(d)
Unauthorized operation of a city owned hydrant will result in a fine of up to five hundred dollars ($500.00).
(Code 1976, § 6-2006; Ord. No. 05-93, 2-18-93; Ord. No. 50-09, § 1, 12-17-09; Ord. No. 04-12, § 1, 1-19-12)
State Law reference— Property damage, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-22 et seq.
Sec. 24-5. - Emergency authority of city manager during water and gas shortages.
(a)
The city manager is authorized at all times when because of drought or low water from any cause, or because of accident or any other cause it becomes necessary for the public welfare to conserve the city water supply, to put into effect such reasonable limitations and regulations of the use of water as may in his judgment be necessary under the circumstances. He may prohibit the use of water for sprinkling streets, yards or gardens or washing of cars, and may establish such other and further regulations and limitations as he may deem necessary. It shall be unlawful for any person to violate any such regulations during the time they are in effect.
(b)
The city manager is hereby given power and authorization at all times during which the city is under a curtailment by the federal power commission with regards to a gas shortage or when any other emergency exists with regards to a shortage of natural gas, as a result of which the city is required to limit the use of natural gas by its customers, to limit, restrict, or terminate if necessary the use of natural gas by industrial and commercial customers of the gas department of the city. In addition:
(1)
If the city council finds that an emergency exists with respect to the quantities of natural gas available to the city and the city manager finds it necessary to limit, restrict or terminate the service of any natural gas customer, reasonable notice shall be afforded to such customer as soon as practicable. Reasonable notice shall be given by publication of the gas shortage and the emergency resulting and of the action therefore required by the gas customers of the city. Such notice shall be given in the local newspaper and on the local radio stations and by personal notice when possible. Such notice shall be given as soon as possible.
(2)
It shall be the responsibility of a gas customer whose service is limited, restricted or terminated to protect his property from the elements by other means.
(3)
Any violation of this subsection shall result in immediate termination of gas service. All gas used in violation of this subsection since the last meter reading shall be billed at ten (10) times the normal rate. The bill must be paid prior to gas service being reconnected.
(Code 1976, § 6-2007)
Sec. 24-6. - Installation of tracer wire in all underground utilities.
(a)
Purpose. The purpose of this section is that more, and practically all utilities are being installed underground, and creating a locating problem for all who have to install or later repair these utilities.
(b)
Requirements of tracer wire to be installed. The owner of the property or the contractor for the owner would be responsible for placing the tracer wire on the customer's side of meters and their own property. The wire would be installed with all utilities that could not be traced and would be installed on the city side of meters and taps by the city. All underground utilities that cannot be traced by mechanical tracing equipment shall have the tracer wire placed above and within twelve (12) of the pipe to be located. The wire should be connected to the wire or pipe supplied by the utility company to make a continuous locatable pipe to the house or structure.
(c)
Inspections. Before any pipeline or conduit is covered with fill material, it shall be inspected by the city for compliance of this section.
(d)
Tracer wire defined. "Tracer wire" shall mean a locatable wire of No. 14 gauge or larger wire (smaller gauge number) and shall be copper or coated copper to prevent rapid deterioration in contact with soils.
(Ord. No. 12-91, 4-4-91)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 12-91, adopted April 4, 1991, did not specifically amend the Code; hence, inclusion herein as § 24-6 was at the discretion of the editor.
Sec. 24-7. - Inspection of change in business.
(a)
All individuals, corporations, businesses or commercial establishments which desire to transfer and/or locate at a previously licensed location under the occupational tax ordinance of the city prior to occupancy of said premises and prior to the transfer or establishment of utility services by the city are required to submit an inspection form as provided by the city to the department of planning and development for fire and building code inspectors to inspect the premises for compliance with all federal, state and local ordinances, rules, regulations, codes and laws.
(b)
The city shall conduct and complete the inspections within two (2) business days of receipt.
(c)
No individual, corporation, business or commercial entities shall be issued an occupational license, certificate of occupancy and/or any license or permit by the city until said inspections have been completed and any violations discovered corrected by the applicant.
(d)
Any individual corporation, business or commercial entities shall be issued temporary utility service upon approval by the building and/or fire official.
(Ord. No. 34-97, 12-18-97; Ord. No. 28-98, 8-13-98; Ord. No. 51-08, § 1, 12-4-08)
Sec. 24-8. - Water ban.
(a)
In the event that the city manager declares a water ban or one (1) is declared by the State of Georgia or Federal Government, the following fine schedule is adopted unless stricter fines are otherwise provided for by the State of Georgia or Federal Governments.
(b)
The fines during the water ban are as follows:
(1)
A first offense (Warning).
(2)
A second offense one hundred dollars ($100.00).
(3)
A third offense two hundred dollars ($200.00).
(4)
A fourth offense two hundred fifty dollars ($250.00) (plus loss of service for three (3) days).
(5)
A fifth offense five hundred dollars ($500.00) (plus loss of meter for thirty (30) days).
(c)
The Water Superintendent of the City of Cartersville may designate employees of the City of Cartersville Water Department to issue citations for violations of this section in addition to city police officers and code enforcement officers.
(d)
All violations of this section shall be prosecuted in the City of Cartersville Municipal Court. In addition, the fines and fees assessed hereunder may be added to the violator's monthly water and sewer bill and collected in the same manner as utility bills are for the City of Cartersville.
(e)
The City of Cartersville hereby adopts by reference for enforcement any restrictions and/or any watering ban imposed for its geographic boundaries by the State of Georgia effective now or as adopted or modified by the State of Georgia from this date forward.
(1)
Cartersville Water Department local option additional exemptions:
(i)
Water leaks on private lines will be considered a violation of the outdoor watering ban if not repaired within seven (7) days of notification by the Cartersville Water Department.

Sec. 24-9. - Procedures for large project locate request.
(a)
If pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 25-9-1 et seq. and Rules of the Georgia Public Service Commission § 515-9-4 the Utility Protection Center designates a request for locates of utility facilities as a Large Project, a written agreement is required to be entered into between the excavator and facility owner(s)/operator(s) or locator(s)/utility(s).
(b)
In the event the City of Cartersville is a necessary party to a large project agreement, the gas superintendent is authorized to negotiate and execute said agreements.
