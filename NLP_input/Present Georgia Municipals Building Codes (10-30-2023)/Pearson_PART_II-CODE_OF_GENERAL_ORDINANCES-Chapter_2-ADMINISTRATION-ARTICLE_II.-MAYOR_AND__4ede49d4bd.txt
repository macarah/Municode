ARTICLE II. - MAYOR AND COUNCIL[2]

Sec. 2-19. - Regular meetings.
The regular meeting date of the mayor and council shall be the second Tuesday in each month at 7:30 p.m.
(Code 1975, § 2-101)
Sec. 2-20. - Special meetings.
(a)
Special meetings of the mayor and council may be held in accordance with the provisions of section 2.05 of the city Charter.
(b)
Notice of a special meeting shall state the date, time, place, and purpose of the meeting.
(Code 1975, § 2-102)
Sec. 2-21. - Order of business.
At each meeting of the mayor and council the following regular order of business shall be observed unless dispensed with by a majority vote of the members present:
(1)
Call to order by the mayor.
(2)
Reading of minutes of the previous meeting by the city clerk for correction and approval.
(3)
Grievances from citizens.
(4)
Communications from the mayor.
(5)
Reports from committees, members of the mayor and council and other offices.
(6)
Old business.
(7)
New business.
(8)
Adjournment.
(Code 1975, § 2-103)
Sec. 2-22. - General rules of order.
Except as otherwise provided in the city Charter or this Code, the rules of order and parliamentary procedure contained in Robert's Rules of Order shall govern the transaction of business by and before the mayor and council at its meetings in all cases to which they are applicable.
(Code 1975, § 2-104)
Sec. 2-23. - Disturbing meetings.
(a)
While the mayor and council are in session, the members must preserve order and decorum, and a member shall neither, by conversation or otherwise, delay or interrupt the proceedings or the peace of the mayor and council nor disturb any member while speaking or refuse to obey the orders of the mayor and council or its presiding officer, except at otherwise herein provided.
(b)
Any person who, with the intent to prevent or disrupt a lawful meeting, substantially obstructs or interferes with said meeting by physical action or verbal utterance while addressing the mayor and council shall be forthwith barred from further audience before the mayor and council, by the presiding officer, unless permission to continue is granted by a majority vote thereof.
(Code 1975, § 2-105)
