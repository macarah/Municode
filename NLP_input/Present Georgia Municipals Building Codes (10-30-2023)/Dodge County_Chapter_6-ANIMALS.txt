ARTICLE II. - ANIMAL CONTROL

Sec. 6-19. - Purpose.
The purpose of this article is to create the position of animal control officer and establish his or her duties; to provide for the classification and registration of dangerous animals and potentially dangerous animals in the county; to provide for requirements for possessing such animals; to provide grounds for the confiscation and disposition of such animals; and to comply with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20.
(Ord. of unknown date(2), § 1)
Sec. 6-20. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Animal control officer means an individual selected by the county board of commissioners pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 4-8-22 to aid in the administration and enforcement of the provisions of this article.
Dangerous animal means any animal that, according to the records of an appropriate authority:
(1)
Inflicts a severe injury on a human being without provocation on public or private property; or
(2)
Aggressively bites, attacks, or endangers the safety of humans without provocation after the dog has been classified as a potentially dangerous dog and after the owner has been notified of such classification.
Owner means any natural person or any legal entity, including, but not limited to, a corporation, partnership, firm or trust, owning, possessing, harboring, keeping, or having custody or control of a dangerous animal or potentially dangerous animal within the county.
Potentially dangerous animal means any animal that without provocation bites a human being on public or private property.
Proper enclosure means an enclosure for keeping a dangerous animal or potentially dangerous animal while on the owner's property securely confined indoors or in a securely enclosed and locked pen, fence, or structure suitable to prevent the entry of young children and designed to prevent the animal from escaping. Any such pen or structure shall have secure sides and a secure top, and, if the animal is enclosed within a fence, all sides of the fence shall be of sufficient height and the bottom of the fence shall be constructed or secured in such a manner as to prevent the animal's escape either from over or from under the fence; any such enclosure shall also provide protection from the elements for the animal.
Records of an appropriate authority means records of any state, county, or municipal law enforcement agency; records of any county or municipal animal control agency; records of any county board of health; records of any federal, state, or local court; or records of an animal control officer as provided in this article and by the laws of the state.
Severe injury means any physical injury that results in broken bones or disfiguring lacerations requiring multiple sutures or cosmetic surgery or a physical injury that results in death.
(Ord. of unknown date(2), § 1-1)
Sec. 6-21. - Exceptions to definitions.
The terms "dangerous animal" and "potentially dangerous animal," as defined in section 6-20, shall not include the following:
(1)
An animal that inflicts an injury upon a person when the animal is being used by a law enforcement officer to carry out the law enforcement officer's official duties; and
(2)
An animal who inflicts an injury within the meaning of this article if the injury was sustained by a person who, at the time, was committing a willful trespass or other tort, or was tormenting, abusing, or assaulting the animal or had in the past been observed or reported to have tormented, abused, or assaulted the animal or was committing or attempting to commit a crime.
(Ord. of unknown date(2), § 1-2)
Sec. 6-22. - Animal control officer.
The county board of commissioners shall appoint an animal control officer who shall serve at the pleasure of the board of commissioners. The animal control officer's compensation shall be established from time to time by the board of commissioners. The county may contract for animal control with a qualified individual, corporation or agency.
(Ord. of unknown date(2), § 1-3)
Sec. 6-23. - Procedures for classifying dangerous animals and potentially dangerous animals; notice; hearing.
(a)
The animal control officer, upon receiving a report of a dangerous or potentially dangerous animal within the county from a law enforcement agency, animal control agency, rabies control officer, or county board of health, shall make such investigations and inquiries with regard to such report as may be necessary to determine the validity of such report and whether the animal should be classified as a dangerous or potentially dangerous animal or reclassified as a dangerous animal if previously classified as a potentially dangerous animal.
(b)
Should the animal control officer determine that an animal is a dangerous or potentially dangerous animal, the animal control officer shall classify such animal accordingly. The animal control officer shall then notify the animal's owner of such classification. The notice to the owner shall meet the following requirements:
(1)
The notice shall be in writing and mailed by certified mail to the owner's last known address;
(2)
The notice shall include a summary of the animal control officer's findings that formed the basis for the animal's classification as a dangerous or potentially dangerous animal;
(3)
The notice shall be dated and shall state that the owner, within 15 days after the date shown on the notice, has a right to request a hearing on the animal control officer's determination that the animal is a dangerous or potentially dangerous animal;
(4)
The notice shall state that the hearing, if requested, shall be before the board;
(5)
The notice shall state that if a hearing is not requested, the animal control officer's determination that the animal is a dangerous or a potentially dangerous animal will become effective for all purposes on a date specified in the notice, which shall be after the last day on which the owner has a right to request a hearing; and
(6)
The notice shall include a form to request a hearing before the board and shall provide specific instructions on mailing or delivering such request to the board.
(c)
When the board receives a request for a hearing as provided in subsection (b) of this section, it shall schedule such hearing within 30 days after receiving the request. The board shall notify the animal owner in writing by certified mail of the date, time, and place of the hearing. Such notice shall be mailed to the animal owner at least ten days prior to the date of the hearing. At the hearing, the owner of the animal shall be given the opportunity to testify and present evidence. In addition, the board shall receive at the hearing such other evidence and hear such other testimony as the board may find reasonably necessary to make a determination either to sustain, modify, or overrule the animal control officer's classification of the animal.
(d)
Within ten days after the date of the hearing, the board shall notify the animal owner in writing by certified mail of its determination on the matter. If such determination is that the animal is a dangerous or a potentially dangerous animal, the notice shall specify the date upon which that determination is effective.
(Ord. of unknown date(2), § 1-4)
Sec. 6-24. - Requirements for possessing a dangerous or potentially dangerous animal.
(a)
No one shall have, own, or possess within the county a dangerous or potentially dangerous animal without a certificate of registration.
(b)
The animal control officer shall issue a certificate of registration to the owner of a dangerous or potentially dangerous animal if the owner presents to the animal control officer or the animal control officer otherwise finds sufficient evidence of:
(1)
A proper enclosure to confine the dangerous or potentially dangerous animal; and
(2)
The posting of the premises where the dangerous or potentially dangerous animal is located with a clearly visible sign warning that there is a dangerous animal on the property. The owner of a dangerous or potentially dangerous animal shall receive such sign from the animal control officer at the time the owner pays the annual registration fee as required in subsection (d) of this section.
(c)
In addition to the requirements of subsection (b) of this section, the owner of a dangerous animal shall present to the animal control officer evidence of:
(1)
A policy of insurance in the amount of at least $15,000.00 issued by an insurer authorized to transact business in the state insuring the owner of the dangerous animal against liability for any personal injuries inflicted by the dangerous animal; or
(2)
A surety bond in the amount of $15,000.00 or more issued by a surety company authorized to transact business in the state payable to any person injured by the dangerous animal.
(d)
The owner of a dangerous or potentially dangerous animal shall pay an annual registration fee at the time the annual certificate of registration is issued. The annual registration fee shall be in the amount of $50.00. Certificates of registration shall be renewed in the month of the initial registration.
(e)
The owner of a dangerous or potentially dangerous animal shall notify the animal control officer if the owner is moving from the county.
(f)
The owner of a dangerous or potentially dangerous animal who is a new resident of the state shall register such animal as required by this article within 30 days after becoming a resident of the state and county.
(g)
The owner of a dangerous or potentially dangerous animal who moves to the county from any other county within the state shall register such animal as required by this article within ten days after becoming a resident of the county.
(h)
The owner of a dangerous or potentially dangerous animal shall notify the animal control officer within 24 hours if the animal is on the loose, is unconfined, has attacked a human, has died, or has been sold or donated. If the animal has been sold or donated, the owner shall also provide the animal control officer with the name, address, and telephone number of the new owner.
(Ord. of unknown date(2), § 1-5)
Sec. 6-25. - Restrictions on permitting dangerous or potentially dangerous animals outside of a proper enclosure.
It shall be unlawful for an owner of a dangerous or potentially dangerous animal to permit the animal to be outside a proper enclosure unless the animal is muzzled and restrained by a substantial chain or leash and is under the physical restraint of a responsible person. The muzzle shall be made in a manner that will prevent it from biting any person but not cause injury to the animal or interfere with its vision or respiration.
(Ord. of unknown date(2), § 1-6)
Sec. 6-26. - Confiscation of animals; grounds; disposition; impoundment.
(a)
An animal shall be immediately confiscated by the animal control officer or by a law enforcement officer or by another person authorized by the animal control officer if:
(1)
The owner of the animal does not secure the liability insurance or surety bond required by section 6-24(c);
(2)
The animal is not validly registered as required by this article;
(3)
The animal is not maintained in a proper enclosure as defined in this chapter; or
(4)
The animal is outside a proper enclosure in violation of this subsection (a).
(b)
Any animal that has been confiscated under the provisions of this section shall be returned to its owner upon the owner's compliance with the provisions of this article as determined by the animal control officer and upon the payment of reasonable confiscation costs. In the event the owner has not complied with the provisions of this section within ten days of the date the animal is confiscated, the animal shall be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner.
(c)
In addition to the foregoing grounds for confiscation under subsection (a) or (b) of this section, a dangerous or potentially dangerous animal which causes severe injury or death of a human being, without provocation, under circumstances constituting a violation of this article, shall be immediately confiscated by the animal control officer or by a law enforcement officer or another person authorized by the animal control officer and placed in quarantine for the proper length of time as determined by the county board of health, and, thereafter, the dangerous or potentially dangerous animal shall be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner.
(d)
Impoundment.
(1)
Any animal seized and impounded under any of the provisions of this section shall be detained for a period of seven working days; and if ownership of the animal is not determined within that period, the animal shall be disposed of in such a manner as the control officer may designate. Any animal seized that is wearing identification tags shall be kept for ten days and the animal control unit shall attempt to notify the owner by writing or telephone if possible.
(2)
If at any time in the required waiting period set forth the owner indicates to the animal control officer his intention not to reclaim the animal, the balance waiting period shall be waived and the animal shall be disposed of.
(3)
If at any time during the required waiting period examination of the animal by the county's designated animal control personnel indicates to be severely sick and/or be a threat to the health of other animals detained in the impoundment, the balance of the waiting period shall be waived and the animal shall be disposed of.
(4)
Nothing herein shall prevent the designated animal control personnel from killing an animal when it is determined that such animal is a menace to public safety or to other animals and cannot be impounded within reasonable effort.
(5)
Any animal euthanized shall be done by a person legally authorized to do so.
(Ord. of unknown date(2), § 1-7)
Sec. 6-27. - General provisions.
(a)
If any provision of this article, or the application of this article to any person or circumstance, shall be held invalid, such invalidity shall not affect the other provisions, or application of such other provisions, of this article which can be given effect without the invalid provision or application. To this end, the provisions of this article are hereby declared to be severable.
(b)
This article is adopted pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq., to implement the duties for which the county is responsible under state law. This article shall not be construed to impose any additional duties or liability on the county or on its officers and employees or to waive any immunity which is currently provided by law.
(c)
All laws and ordinances, or parts thereof, which conflict with the provisions of this article are repealed.
(Ord. of unknown date(2), § 1-8)

ARTICLE III. - DOMESTICATED ANIMALS

Sec. 6-49. - Findings.
The county board of commissioners finds that there are many domesticated animals roaming and running loose in the county, including, but not limited to, dogs and cats, which cause or may cause hurt, inconvenience, damage, or injury to persons, other animals, and public or private property, and that it is in the best interest of the health and welfare of the citizens of the county to reduce such hurt, inconvenience, damage and injury to persons, other animals and to public and private property.
(Ord. of unknown date(2), § 2-1)
Sec. 6-50. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Animal shelter means any facility operated by or participated in by the county under contract for the purpose of impounding or caring for animals held under the authority of this article.
Domesticated animal means any animal raised to live in close proximity to man. Such animals shall include dogs, cats, livestock or reptiles.
Domesticated animal at large means any animal running loose not under restraint or control of the owner or representative of the owner on or off the premises of the owner.
Inhumane treatment of animal means failure, omission, or neglect to provide adequate food, water, shelter, and protection from the elements and the failure to provide an appropriate vaccination for an animal.
Jurisdiction means Dodge County, Georgia.
Owner means any person who owns, or possesses, harbors or keeps, or knowingly permits an animal to be harbored or kept or has an animal in his care, or who permits an animal to remain on or about his premises for more than three days.
Public nuisance means any animal that unreasonably annoys humans, causes hurt, inconvenience or damage to another, endangers the life or health of other animals or persons, or substantially interferes with the rights of citizens, other than their owners, to the enjoyment of life or property. The term "public nuisance" shall include, but not be limited to, any animal that:
(1)
Is found at large or is otherwise without a home or identifiable owner;
(2)
Damages the property of anyone other than its owner;
(3)
Chases vehicles or human-propelled transportation;
(4)
Attacks other domestic animals;
(5)
Threatens to do harm to or attacks pedestrians or passersby; or
(6)
Has been found to be a public nuisance animal by virtue of being a menace to the public health, welfare or safety.
Vaccination means a protection against rabies by inoculation with anti-rabies vaccine given in an amount sufficient to provide immunity from rabies for a minimum period of one year.
(Ord. of unknown date(2), § 2-2)
Sec. 6-51. - Vaccinations.
(a)
The owners and keepers of all dogs are hereby required to have such dogs vaccinated against rabies by a licensed veterinarian. No person shall own, keep or harbor any dog over three months of age unless the dog is vaccinated.
(b)
All dogs shall be vaccinated against rabies by a licensed veterinarian in accordance with the latest recommendations published by the National Association of State Public Health Veterinarians.
(c)
A certificate of vaccination shall be issued to the owner of each animal vaccinated.
(d)
Each owner shall affix a durable vaccination tag indicating the year for which the certificate was issued. The tag shall be securely affixed to the collar of each animal and such collar and tag shall be worn continuously by any dog for which the certificate has been issued. Tags are not transferable.
(Ord. of unknown date(2), § 2-3)
Sec. 6-52. - Prohibited acts in the unincorporated areas of the county.
(a)
Prohibited acts. The following shall be prohibited and the owner or person in control of any animal shall be subject, at a minimum, to prosecution and fine for the following:
(1)
Animals running at large. It is unlawful for any owner or person in possession of an animal to cause or permit an animal to run at large in or upon, across or through any public or private property without the consent of the owner thereof.
(2)
Dog control.
a.
No person owning or otherwise having a dog in his or her care, charge, control, custody or possession shall cause or permit or allow such dog to be in or upon any public property or on the private property of another without permission to be on such property unless such dog is under complete control and restraint of the owner. Dogs are prohibited in those areas so designated and posted by the county.
b.
A hunting dog while in the company of its owner, hunter, or active custodian is considered "in control."
(3)
Keeping of sick or diseased animals. It is unlawful for any person to own, possess, control, or be in charge of any animal which is seriously sick, diseased, or injured without providing proper treatment in care of such animal. This subsection shall not be construed to include animals under the care of veterinarians or veterinary hospitals.
(4)
Putting out poison. It shall be unlawful for any person to scatter or put out or otherwise distribute any poison in any form whatsoever which is or may likely be sufficiently lethal or of such a strength to cause death or harm to any animal without obtaining a permit from the county manager. The county manager may, in his or her discretion, refuse to issue such permits.
(5)
Inhumane treatment. It shall be unlawful for any owner of a dog to fail, omit, or neglect to provide for such dog adequate food, water, shelter, and protection from the elements. It shall be unlawful for any person to treat any dog in a cruel and inhumane manner, or abandon it, or release it upon public or private property with the intention of abandoning it.
(6)
Animal fights. It is unlawful for any person to promote, stage, hold, conduct, manage, carry on, pay for, attend or own any animal for the purpose of participating in any game, exhibition, contest or fight in which one or more animals are engaged for the purpose of injuring, killing, maiming, or destroying another animal, themselves or any other thing.
(7)
Female dogs in heat. It shall be unlawful for the owner of any female dog to allow the animal to run at large on any public or private property and/or fail to restrain or confine the animal to the property of the owner while such animal is in heat.
(8)
Vaccination requirement. It shall be unlawful for the owner of any dog to fail, omit or neglect vaccinating such animal and attaching the property tags for and where evidence of vaccination is provided in section 6-51.
(9)
Destruction of property. It shall be unlawful for any animal to destroy or damage private or public property.
(b)
Penalty for violation. A violator of subsection (a) of this section shall be fined no more than $500.00, imprisoned for a period not greater than two months, probation for a period no greater than one year, such hours of community services as may be imposed by the presiding judge, court costs as set by the magistrate court of the county and assessed by the presiding judge, or such other punishment as may be deemed appropriate by the presiding judge.
(Ord. of unknown date(2), § 2-4)
Sec. 6-53. - Fees.
The following fees shall be charged for the impoundment of any animal under the provisions of this chapter:
(1)
First impoundment within calendar year: .....$30.00
(2)
Second impoundment within calendar year: ..... 60.00
(3)
An additional fee of $5.00 shall be charged for each day, or fraction thereof, of the impoundment for the feeding and caring for such impounded animal.
(Ord. of unknown date(2), § 2-5)
Sec. 6-54. - Unlawful interference by third parties.
(a)
No person shall, without the knowledge or consent of the owner, hold or retain possession of any animal which he or she does not own for more than 48 hours without first reporting the possession of such animal to the animal control officer. This section shall not apply to those nonprofit organizations whose purpose is the protection of animals.
(b)
No person shall willfully cause the animal to be in violation of any portion of this article.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to attack, assault, or in any way physically or verbally threaten or interfere with animal control personnel in the performance of those duties required to enforce this article.
(d)
It shall be unlawful to tamper with, destroy or take any trap, snare or other device which has been set by the animal control officer, his employees, agents or representatives.
(e)
No person shall perform any cruel act on, upon or against any animal, nor shall any person harm, maim or kill any animal except:
(1)
Those persons appointed by the county for animal control;
(2)
Those persons acting in defense of his or her person or the person of another, or the property of such person, from injury or damage being caused by an animal; or
(3)
Any person killing an animal causing injury or damage to any livestock, wild game or poultry.
Penalties for violation under this provision shall be a fine not less than $100.00 and such other punishment as may be provided for other offenses of this section or as may be imposed by the presiding judge.
(Ord. of unknown date(2), § 2-6)
Sec. 6-55. - Jurisdiction.
The magistrate court of the county shall have jurisdiction over all alleged violations of any portion of this article and shall have all powers granted to it which are necessary and incident to the hearing and disposition of matters brought before any such court, such powers to include the authority to issue citations and summons for witnesses. The following persons shall file complaints for violations herein:
(1)
Animal control personnel, their agents, representatives or employees;
(2)
Law enforcement officers;
(3)
Public health officials;
(4)
Individuals with personal knowledge of any violation upon the filing of the sworn complaint with the magistrate court of the county.
(Ord. of unknown date(2), § 2-7)
Sec. 6-56. - Additional provision.
(a)
The provisions of this article are applicable only to the unincorporated areas of the county except in those municipalities that have adopted the same ordinance from which this article is derived and have entered into an agreement with the county for mutual enforcement of the same.
(b)
The county may enter into contracts with one or more agencies or individuals for the operation of animal shelters. Said agencies or individuals shall be licensed by the state.
(c)
The county manager may appoint one or more animal control officers and/or one or more deputy sheriffs as animal control officers and shall have the right to terminate any such animal control officer with or without cause.
(Ord. of unknown date(2), § 2-8)
