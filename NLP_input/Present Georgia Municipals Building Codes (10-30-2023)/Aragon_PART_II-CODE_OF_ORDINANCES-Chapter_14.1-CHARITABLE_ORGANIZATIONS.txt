Chapter 14.1 - CHARITABLE ORGANIZATIONS

Sec. 14.1-1. - Authorization for solicitation.
Charitable organizations registered in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 43-17-5 and such organizations exempt from registration in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 43-17-9 are authorized to solicit for contributions on the streets and highways within the city, subject to compliance with the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. of 9-18-2003, § 3-2)
Sec. 14.1-2. - Application; liability insurance.
Charitable organizations seeking a permit pursuant to this chapter shall file an application, in the form provided by the city, with the city clerk. Such application shall include, the name, address and telephone number of the organization, the officers of the organization, the date(s), time(s) and place(s) of the proposed solicitation and must include an agreement by the organization to hold harmless and indemnify the city from liability for damage or injury arising out of the permitted solicitation. Such application must be executed by a duly authorized officer of said organization. Such organization must provide a copy of the declaration page of a liability insurance policy which provides liability insurance coverage, with limits of at least $1,000,000.00 per occurrence, for the permitted solicitation. Such policy shall include an endorsement listing the city as a named insured.
(Ord. of 9-18-2003, § 3-3)
Sec. 14.1-3. - Cost.
The cost of the permit for solicitation shall be $25.00. In the event the solicitation will continue more than one day, an additional fee in the amount of $25.00 per day shall be imposed.
(Ord. of 9-18-2003, § 3-4)
Sec. 14.1-4. - Violation; penalty.
Solicitation for contribution on the streets and highways within the city without a permit shall be punished as provided in section 26-51.
(Ord. of 9-18-2003, § 3-5)
