ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 4-1. - Keeping domestic animals generally.
It shall be unlawful for any person to house or keep any livestock or fowl, or other domestic animal, within the city limits of Winder, except as otherwise provided in the Comprehensive Zoning Ordinance of the City of Winder and also except for household pets such as dogs and cats subject to article II and article III of this chapter.
(Code 1968, § 4-1; Ord. of 9-5-78; Ord. (7-W-88) of 2-2-88; Ord. (W-4-11) of 6-7-11)
Charter reference— Power of city to regulate or prohibit the keeping or running at large of animals and fowl, § 1-3(8).
State Law reference— Estrays, Ga. Code Ann., § 4-5-6.
Sec. 4-2. - Police authorized to impound animals.
The chief of police or any member of the police force shall be authorized to impound any animal or fowl at large in violation of this chapter, until the owner pays the impoundment charge and the cost of keeping which shall be prescribed by the mayor and council.
(Code 1968, § 4-2)
Charter reference— Power of city to provide for the impoundment of animals and fowl, § 1-3(8).
Sec. 4-3. - Reserved.
Editor's note— At the direction of the city, former § 4-3, which pertained to sale of impounded animals and derived from the 1968 Code, § 4-2, has been deleted as being superseded by the provisions of an ordinance (7-W-88) adopted Feb. 2, 1988, included herein as §§ 4-41—4-57.
Sec. 4-4. - Reserved.
Editor's note— Ord. No. W-4-11, adopted June 7, 2011, repealed § 4-4, which pertained to enclosures—location and derived from Code 1968, § 4-5.
Sec. 4-5. - Same—Maintenance.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to permit or allow any pen or enclosure for the keeping of animals in the city to become offensive or filthy or liable to endanger the public health.
(b)
All such enclosures in the city shall be subject to inspection by the mayor and council or by their authorized agents.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to fail or refuse to put such enclosure in a sanitary condition after being ordered to do so by said mayor and council or by their authorized agents.
(Code 1968, § 4-6)
Sec. 4-6. - Same—Inspection.
The mayor and city council or their authorized agents shall be authorized to enter and inspect any and all premises in the city at any reasonable hour, to determine compliance with sections 4-4 and 4-5.
(Code 1968, § 4-7)
Sec. 4-7. - Same—Violations.
Failure to comply with the provisions of sections 4-4—4-6 after being duly notified by the mayor and city council, or by their authorized representative, shall constitute a violation of this Code.
(Code 1968, § 4-8)
Sec. 4-8. - Cruelty to animals.
It shall be unlawful for any person in the city to work any animal which is in a lame, starved or famished condition, or in any condition unfitting the same for work, or to overload or cruelly whip, beat, maim, bruise, deprive of necessary sustenance, ill use or in any manner whatsoever, torture or abuse any animal in the city.
(Code 1968, § 4-11)
Cross reference— Offenses, ch. 16.
State Law reference— Cruelty to dogs, Ga. Code Ann., § 4-8-5; cruelty to animals, Ga. Code Ann., § 16-12-4.
Sec. 4-9. - Animals fights.
It shall be unlawful for any person to fight or make fight any animal for the purpose of gain or amusement in the city.
(Code 1968, § 4-12)
Cross reference— Parks and recreation, ch. 17.
Sec. 4-10. - City declared bird sanctuary.
The entire area embraced within the corporate limits of the city shall be, and the same is hereby designated, a bird sanctuary.
(Code 1968, § 4-13)
Cross reference— Parks and recreation, ch. 17.
Sec. 4-11. - Trapping, hunting, molesting birds, wildfowl—Prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to trap, hunt, shoot or attempt to shoot or molest in any manner any bird or wildfowl, or to rob birds' nests or wildfowl nests.
(Code 1968, § 4-14)
Sec. 4-12. - Same—Exception.
(a)
If starlings or similar birds are found to be congregating in such numbers in a particular locality that they constitute a nuisance or a menace to health or property in the opinion of the proper health authorities of the county, then in such event such health authorities shall meet with representatives of the Audubon Society, bird club, garden club or humane society, or as many of such clubs as are found to exist in the city, after having given at least three (3) days' actual notice of the time and place of the meeting to the representatives of the clubs.
(b)
If as a result of such meeting no satisfactory alternative is found to abate such nuisance, then the birds may be destroyed in such numbers and in such manner as is deemed advisable by the health authorities under the supervision of the chief of police.
(Code 1968, § 4-14)
Charter reference— Power of city to provide for the abatement of nuisances, § 1-3(4).
State Law reference— Nuisances, Ga. Code Ann., title 41; nuisances in cities, § 41-2-5.
Secs. 4-13—4-28. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - DOGS AND CATS[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Dogs generally, Ga. Code Ann., § 4-8-1 et seq; control of rabies, Ga. Code Ann., § 31-19-1 et seq.

Sec. 4-29. - Inoculation of dogs and cats newly acquired.
Any person acquiring a dog or cat to be kept in the city shall have said animal inoculated against rabies within ten (10) days from the date such person acquires same or brings the same into the city, or within ten (10) days after the animal attains the age designated by the county board of health. Such person shall also secure the certificate and tag evidencing such inoculation and shall be responsible for affixing the tag to dogs as provided for in this article.
(Code 1968, § 4-30)
Sec. 4-30. - Owner, custodian to procure rabies inoculation.
It shall be the duty of the owner or custodian of any dog or cat kept within the city or allowed to run at large within the city, to cause said dog or cat to be inoculated for rabies by a competent veterinarian.
(Code 1968, §§ 4-26, 4-27)
State Law reference— Municipal regulations of animals for the control of rabies, Ga. Code Ann., § 31-19-3.
Sec. 4-31. - Certificate to evidence inoculation—Duty of veterinarian.
A veterinarian inoculating a dog or cat for rabies shall give to the owner or custodian of the same, immediately after the animal is inoculated, a certificate evidencing the fact that such animal has been inoculated.
(Code 1968, §§ 4-28, 4-31)
State Law reference— Distribution of forms for use in certifying to inoculation, Ga., Code Ann., § 31-19-6.
Sec. 4-32. - Same—Contents.
The certificate evidencing inoculation shall be numbered and shall indicate the date the dog or cat was inoculated and the expiration date of the inoculation.
(Code 1968, § 4-28)
Sec. 4-33. - Tag to evidence inoculation—Duty of veterinarian.
A veterinarian inoculating a dog or cat for rabies shall give to the owner or custodian thereof, immediately after animal is inoculated, a tag evidencing the fact that such animal has been inoculated.
(Code 1968, §§ 4-29, 4-31)
State Law reference— Distribution of numbered tags, Ga. Code Ann., § 31-19-6.
Sec. 4-34. - Same—Contents.
The tag evidencing inoculation shall be numbered with the same number as the certificate provided for in sections 4-31 and 4-32, and shall indicate the date the animal was inoculated and the expiration date of the inoculation.
(Code 1968, § 4-29)
Sec. 4-35. - Same—Duty of owner, custodian.
The owner or custodian of a dog inoculated as provided in this article, and receiving a tag therefor, shall affix said tag to a collar or harness to be worn by the dog, in such manner that the tag will be conspicuously displayed.
(Code 1968, §§ 4-26, 4-29)
State Law reference— Removal of collar or tag without permission of owner, Ga. Code Ann., § 16-9-71.
Sec. 4-36. - Failure to have tag.
Any dog will be presumed not to have been inoculated as provided in this article, that does not have attached to a collar or harness worn by it a current inoculation tag, provided for in section 4-33.
(Code 1968, § 4-29)
Sec. 4-37. - Unauthorized tag.
It shall be unlawful for any person to tag a dog as evidence of inoculation, when such dog in fact has not been inoculated as provided in this article.
(Code 1968, § 4-32)
Sec. 4-38. - Inability to seize dog.
If it is impossible or impractical for the police to seize any dog that is not wearing an inoculation tag as provided in this article, then said police shall be authorized to destroy said animal.
(Code 1968, § 4-38)
Sec. 4-39. - Restraint of dogs.
It shall be unlawful for the owner or custodian of a dog to permit the same to be on any streets, alleys or any other place in the city off the premises of the owner or custodian, or the premises of a person authorized by said owner or custodian, unless the animal shall wear the inoculation tag prescribed in this article and is restrained by a chain or leash not more than six (6) feet in length, held by a person capable of restraining the movement of such animal by means of said chain or leash.
(Code 1968, §§ 4-33—4-35)
Sec. 4-40. - Female dogs in heat.
It shall be the duty of the police of the city to remove and destroy any female dog in heat found running at large on the streets of the city.
(Code 1968, § 4-37)
State Law reference— Permitting female dog in heat to roam free, Ga. Code Ann., § 4-8-6.
