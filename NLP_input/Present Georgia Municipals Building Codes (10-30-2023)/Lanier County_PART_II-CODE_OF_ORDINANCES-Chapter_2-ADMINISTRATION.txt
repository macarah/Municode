ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 2-1. - Audio recordings.
All audio recordings of the county meetings are required to be retained by the county clerk for a period of 30 days from the date of approval by the board of the minutes and that after such date, the clerk is authorized to destroy any audio recordings taken by the board.
(Res. of 1-11-2010)
Sec. 2-2. - Authorize the tax commissioner to accept checks or money orders for payment of taxes and license fees.
The county board of commissioners does hereby authorize the receipt of personal, company, certified, treasurer's, or cashier's checks, or bank, postal, or express money orders in payment of taxes and license fees by the tax commissioner.
(Res. of 6-13-2011(1))

ARTICLE II. - FINANCE

Sec. 2-23. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
County officer means the people elected to serve in the position of sheriff, tax commissioner, clerk of superior court and judge of the probate court.
Procurement rules means the rules of how purchases are funneled through the county administration and what forms the procurement requests must take.
(Ord. of 11-9-2009)
Sec. 2-24. - Authority.
Under article IX, § 11, paragraph 1(a) of the constitution of the state, the governing authority is granted the legislative power to adopt clearly reasonable ordinances relating to its property, affairs and local government for which no provision has been made by general law and which is not inconsistent with the constitution. Under article I, § II, ¶ I of the state constitution, commissioners are vested with the fiduciary responsibilities of a trustee to the people of the county. O.C.G.A. § 36-5-22.1 provides that the governing authority has original and exclusive jurisdiction over directing and controlling all the property of the county and further authorizes the governing authority to examine and audit the accounts of all officers having the care, collection and disbursement of money belonging to the county appropriated to its use.
(Ord. of 11-9-2009)
Sec. 2-25. - Procurement rules.
In order to meet its constitutional fiduciary obligations to the citizens of the county, the governing authority has to monitor the budget and account for expenditures of county funds. As such, it is necessary to establish procurement rules designed to unify, monitor, record and provide oversight regarding county government spending and purchasing requests relative to the approved county budget.
(Ord. of 11-9-2009)
Sec. 2-26. - Purchase orders.
(a)
Every county officer and all other departments shall be required to submit a purchase order to the governing authority prior to any purchases by that office or department. Said purchase orders shall contain the name of the vendor, the department name or county officer's division, the budget classification and line item code to which the purchase is to be expensed, a list or description of the items or services about what is to be purchased, and the total amount of the purchase. Said purchase orders shall be on forms which are approved and provided by the governing authority.
(b)
The county administrator is authorized to approve purchase order requests from county officers in amounts consistent with funds budgeted for said officers by the governing authority. The county administrator shall not approve purchase order requests from county officers that would, if granted, exceed budgeted amounts.
(c)
The county administrator is authorized to approve purchase order requests from all other departments, in amounts of up to $10,000.00, provided such expenditures are consistent with budgeted funds for said departments. The county administrator shall not approve purchase order requests from departments that would, if granted, exceed budgeted amounts. All purchase order requests over $10,000.00 shall be brought before the board of commissioners for consideration at the next regular scheduled meeting.
(d)
No item shall be purchased by a county officer or any other department until said purchase order has been approved by the county administrator or the governing authority. The governing authority reserves the right to refuse payment for purchases unless a purchase order was obtained and approved prior to the purchase of said item or services.
(e)
Neither the county administrator nor the governing authority shall refuse to approve any expenditure that is consistent with the legal level of control enacted by the governing authority and is not in conflict with the budget for the county officer approved by the governing authority.
(Ord. of 11-9-2009)
Sec. 2-27. - Continuing education, classes and meetings.
All county officers and employees shall submit to the governing authority notice of all continuing education classes, conferences or meetings they are required to attend. Said notice shall state whether classes and/or meetings are a requirement of the office or job and what costs, if any, is county expense. Said notices shall be given at least ten days prior to the scheduled event.
(Ord. of 11-9-2009)
Sec. 2-28. - Mileage.
All county officers and employees of the county officers shall be required to submit all requests for mileage reimbursement to the county board of commissioners on forms which are approved and provided by the county board of commissioners. Said requests shall state destination and reason for said trip.
(Ord. of 11-9-2009)
Sec. 2-29. - Taxes, fees, fines and forfeitures.
(a)
All taxes, fees, fines and forfeitures and other revenues collected by county officers and which are payable to the governing authority by law, shall be remitted to the clerk of the county board of commissioners in accordance with applicable statutes or regulations. If no statute or regulation specifies a schedule for remittance of said funds to the governing authority, county officers shall remit the same no less than once each calendar month. Said funds shall be deposited in the county general funds account.
(b)
All county officers shall submit to the governing authority an accounting of all taxes, fees, fines and forfeitures received in each calendar month, noting that portion due and payable to the governing authority. The accounting shall be submitted no more than 30 days after the end of each calendar quarter and shall contain a true statement of the amount and source of money paid or payable to the governing authority. If a county officer fails to submit the accounting as required, the governing authority may audit the accounts and records of any such county officer.
(Ord. of 11-9-2009)
Sec. 2-30. - Resolution.
The board, pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 36-81-1 et seq., makes the following findings:
(1)
That the county administrator be appointed the budget officer to perform the duties of the budget officer set forth in O.C.G.A. § 36-81-5.
(2)
That each county department, office and agency and each constitutional officer located within the confines of the county and receiving funds from the board submit an annual proposed budget for their respective department, agency or office to the budget officer in the form provided by such officer which at a minimum shall reflect all anticipated revenues, expenditures and capital outlays on a line item basis consistent with the chart of accounts adopted by the board.
(3)
That said departmental, agency or office or constitutional officer budget be submitted at a time chosen by the budget officer but no later than April 1 of each year.
(4)
After organization and presentation of the proposed budget to the board, and to the extent the board desires additional information, the budget officer shall require each department, office or agency or constitutional officer to submit additional information as required by the board within its reasonable discretion.
(5)
That after the adoption of the budget by the board, resources within each line item of the budget of any department, agency or office or constitution officer may only be reassigned or reallocated to other line items within said budget with the consent of either the budget officer or the board.
(Res. of 3-8-2010)

DIVISION 2. - DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY

Sec. 2-70. - South Georgia Joint Development Authority.
(a)
Pursuant to the authority vested in the governing authority of the county, it is hereby declared that there is a need for a joint development authority encompassing the counties of Ben Hill, Berrien, Brooks, Cook, Echols, Irwin, Lanier, Lowndes, Tift, and Turner, and in conjunction with said counties, the county hereby creates and activates the South Georgia Joint Development Authority.
(b)
The board of directors of the authority shall have ten members. Each participating county shall have the right to appoint one member of the board of directors, said person to be appointed by the governing authority of said county. Each member of the board of directors shall be a resident of the county which he represents on the board. Directors shall serve terms of six years and may be reappointed for successive terms of office.
(c)
In the event that any of the above designated counties do not participate in the creation and activation of the authority, the municipal corporations within each such county or counties shall be eligible to participate in the authority by passage of an appropriate resolution. The governing authority of each such municipal corporation shall have the right to appoint one member to the board of directors who shall be a resident of the municipal corporation which he represents on the board. In the event the provisions of this section become operative, the number of members of the board of directors shall equal the number of counties and municipal corporations participating in the authority.
(d)
A copy of this chapter shall be filed with the secretary of the state.
(Res. of 9-8-1981)
Sec. 2-71. - Lowndes County, Lanier County, Berrien County, Echols County, Clinch and Brooks County Development Authority.
(a)
Whereas, two or more counties located in the state may, pursuant to the Development Authorities Law (Ga. L. 1963, p. 531, § 1; Ga. L. 1969, p. 137, § 1), create a joint authority.
(b)
Whereas, on March 31, 1995, the board of commissioners of Lowndes County, Georgia and the board of commissioners of Lanier County, Georgia each determined that it was in the best interests of the residents of the counties governed by each to create a joint authority for the purpose of developing, promoting and enhancing economic development and employment in both counties.
(c)
Thereafter, the board of commissioners of Lowndes County and Lanier County, Georgia signed a joint resolution creating a joint authority known as the Lowndes County and Lanier County Development Authority which was activated on the March 31, 1995, and was to be governed by ch. 62 of title 36 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated in the same manner as other authorities created pursuant thereto, except as specifically provided in O.C.G.A. § 36-62-5.1.
(d)
Whereas, the board of commissioners of Lowndes County, Georgia, the board of commissioners of Lanier County, Georgia, the board of commissioners of Berrien County, Georgia, the board of commissioners of Echols County, Georgia, the board of commissioners of Clinch County, Georgia, and the board of commissioners of Brooks County, Georgia, have each determined that it is in the best interests of the residents of each county governed by these commissions to expand the joint authority formerly created by Lowndes County and Lanier County to also include Brooks County, Georgia.
(e)
It is further resolved that, the authority created, activated and amended by the former action of Lowndes County, Georgia, Lanier County, Georgia, Berrien County, Georgia, Echols County, Georgia and Clinch County, Georgia is, by this resolution, hereby amended and supplemented to include Brooks County, Georgia. The joint authority shall be known as the Lowndes County, Lanier County, Berrien County, Echols County, Clinch County and Brooks County Development Authority. The amended joint authority shall consist of 13 members who are residents of either Lowndes County, Georgia; Lanier County, Georgia; Berrien County, Georgia, Echols County, Georgia, Clinch County, Georgia or Brooks County, Georgia as follows: Two members shall be appointed by the board of commissioners of Lowndes County, Georgia; two members shall be appointed by the board of commissioners of Lanier County, Georgia; two members shall be appointed by the board of commissioners of Berrien County, Georgia; two members shall be appointed by the board of commissioners of Echols County, Georgia, two members shall be appointed by the board of commissioners of Clinch County, Georgia, and, two members shall be appointed by the board of commissioners of Brooks County, Georgia, the 13th member shall be appointed by the concurrent action of the board of commissioners of Lowndes County, Georgia, the board of commissioners of Lanier County, Georgia, the board of commissioners of Berrien County, Georgia, the board of commissioners of Echols County, Georgia, the board of commissioners of Clinch County, Georgia and the board of commissioners of Brooks County, Georgia, the respective terms of the members shall begin as follows:
(1)
The first member appointed by the board of commissioners of each county shall be appointed for a term of two years;
(2)
The second member appointed by the board of commissioners of each county shall be appointed for a term of three years;
(3)
The member appointed by the joint action of the board of commissioners from each county shall be appointed for a term of three years; and thereafter upon the expiration of such terms, successors shall be appointed for a term of years by the political body who appointed the member whose term is expiring. All members who have served on the authority shall be eligible for reappointment to succeed themselves if they are reappointed by said political subdivision. Should any member resign, or be unable to serve, or move beyond the boundaries of the counties of Lowndes, Lanier, Berrien, Echols, Clinch or Brooks of the State of Georgia, his successor shall be appointed to serve his remaining term by the political subdivision who originally appointed the member being removed.
(f)
Subsection (e) of this section was adopted by the joint action of the board of commissioners of Lowndes County, Georgia; the board of commissioners of Lanier County, Georgia; the board of commissioners of Berrien County, Georgia, the board of commissioners of Echols County, Georgia; the board of commissioners of Clinch County, Georgia and the board of commissioners of Brooks County, Georgia during their respective monthly meeting as is set out by the chairperson's signature for each respective county.
(Res. of 3-31-1995; Res. of 3-10-2005)

DIVISION 3. - LAKELAND-LANIER COUNTY RECREATION AND PARKS BOARD

Sec. 2-101. - Established; composition; powers and duties.
(a)
The Lakeland-Lanier County Recreation and Parks Board shall consist of five people who will be appointed by the board of commissioners. The terms of office shall be five years or until their successors are appointed and qualified, except that the members first appointed shall be appointed for such terms that the term of one member shall expire annually thereafter. Vacancies occurring other than by expiration of term shall be filled by another appointment for the unexpired term.
(b)
The chairperson of the board of commissioners and the superintendent of the county school district shall serve as the advisory board to work with the board for the parks and recreation department.
(c)
The board shall have the following powers and duties:
(1)
Immediately after their appointment, the board shall meet and organize by electing a chairperson and such other officers as may be necessary. The board shall have power to adopt bylaws, rules, and regulations for the proper conduct of the parks and recreation department for the county.
(2)
The board shall provide, conduct and supervise public playgrounds, playfields, indoor recreation centers, and other recreation facilities owned or controlled by the county.
(3)
The board shall monitor and evaluate the effectiveness of park and recreation programs.
(4)
The board shall have the power to hire a park director who is trained or properly qualified for the work, and such other personnel as the board deems proper.
(5)
The board will set all fees for the variety of activities carried on by the parks and recreation department.
(6)
All monies collected shall be deposited to the account of the Lakeland-Lanier County recreation and parks department. The board shall make an accounting to the board of commissioners at each monthly meeting.
(7)
Annually, the board shall submit a budget request to the county commission for their approval.
(8)
The board shall file an annual report to the county, and such other reports as from time to time may be requested, relative to programs accomplishments for the preceding fiscal year.
(Ord. of 4-9-2012)
