Sec. 22-5. - Occupation tax required; business dealings in city.
For the year 1996 and succeeding years thereafter, each person engaged in any business, trade, profession, or occupation in the city, whether with a location in the city or in the case of an out-of-state business with no location in Georgia exerting substantial efforts within the state pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7, shall pay an occupation tax for such business, trade, profession, or occupation; which tax and any applicable registration shall be displayed in a conspicuous place in the place of business, if the taxpayer has a permanent business location in the city. If the taxpayer has no permanent business location in the city, such business tax registration shall be shown to the city clerk or the city clerk's designees or to any police officer or codes enforcement officer of the city upon request.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95; Ord. No. O-22-11 , § 2(Exh. A), 10-17-22)
Sec. 22-6. - Construction of terms; definitions.
(a)
Generally. Wherever the term Decatur is used herein, such term shall be construed to mean the City of Decatur, Georgia; wherever the term city is used herein, it shall be construed to mean the City of Decatur, Georgia.
(b)
Specific terms. As used in this article, the following terms shall have the meanings respectively ascribed to them as follows:
Administrative fee means a component of an occupation tax which approximates the reasonable cost of handling and processing the occupation tax.
City clerk means the appointed city clerk of the City of Decatur, Georgia, or the city clerk's designee.
Dominant line means the type of business, within a multiple-line business, that the greatest amount of income is derived from.
Location or office means a fixed place located within the city from which a person conducts business, but shall not include a work site located in the city for less than four months in any calendar year for the purpose of serving a single customer or project.
Occupation tax certificate means a document issued by the city acknowledging payment of the occupation tax and administrative fee.
Occupation tax means a tax levied on persons, partnerships, corporations, or other entities for engaging in an occupation, profession, or business for revenue raising purposes.
Person shall be held to include sole proprietors, corporations, partnerships, nonprofits, or any other form of business organization.
Practitioner of profession or occupation is one who, by state law requires state licensure regulating such profession or occupation.
Practitioners of professions are:
(1)
Lawyers.
(2)
Physicians licensed under O.C.G.A. title 43, chapter 34.
(3)
Osteopaths licensed under O.C.G.A. title 43, chapter 34.
(4)
Chiropractors.
(5)
Podiatrists.
(6)
Dentists.
(7)
Optometrists.
(8)
Psychologists.
(9)
Veterinarians.
(10)
Landscape architects.
(11)
Land surveyors.
(12)
Practitioners of physiotherapy.
(13)
Public accountants.
(14)
Embalmers.
(15)
Funeral directors.
(16)
Civil, mechanical, hydraulic, or electrical engineers.
(17)
Architects.
(18)
Marriage and family therapists, social workers, and professional counselors.
Practitioners of professions and occupations shall not include a practitioner who is an employee of a business, if the business pays an occupation tax.
Regulatory fee means payments, whether designated as license fees, permit fees, or by another name, which are required by a local government as an exercise of its police power and as a part of or an aid to regulation of an occupation, profession, or business. A regulatory fee may not include an administrative fee. Development impact fees as defined by O.C.G.A. § 36-71-2, paragraph 8 or other costs or conditions of zoning or land development are not regulatory fees for the purposes of this article.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95; Ord. No. O-22-11 , § 2(Exh. A), 10-17-22)
Sec. 22-7. - Administrative and regulatory fee structure.
(a)
A nonprorated, nonrefundable administrative fee of $25.00 shall be required on all business and occupation tax accounts for the initial startup, renewal, or reopening of those accounts.
(b)
A regulatory fee will be imposed as provided under O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9 on those applicable businesses. A regulatory fee may not include an administrative fee.
(c)
The regulatory fee schedule shall be as described elsewhere in the Decatur City Code.
(d)
The administrative fee of $25.00 shall be waived for certificate renewals paid within 30 days of invoicing.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95; Ord. No. O-15-12, § I, 10-5-15)
Sec. 22-8. - Occupation tax levied; restrictions.
(a)
An occupation tax shall be levied upon those businesses and practitioners of professions and occupations with one or more locations or offices in the corporate limits of the city and/or upon the applicable out-of-state businesses with no location or office in the state pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7, based on the dominant line of business, based upon the following criteria:
(1)
Profitability ratio for the type of business, profession, or occupation as measured by nationwide averages derived from statistics, classifications, or other information published by the U.S. Internal Revenue Service, or successor agencies of the United States. The city manager or the city manager's designee shall review the assignment of businesses to profitability classes on an annual basis and administratively reassign businesses as necessary to the accurate profitability class in accordance with the most recent such nationwide averages. A copy of the annual administrative assignment of businesses to profitability classes shall be kept on file in the office of the city clerk.
(2)
The particular type of business, profession, or occupation as classified by the North American Industrial Classification System (NAICS). A copy of the NAICS manual shall be kept on file in the office of the city clerk.
(b)
Occupation tax schedule:
Profitability ratios: The tax rate for each business, trade, profession, or occupation is determined by ranking profitability ratios according to federal statistics as follows:

(c)
(1)
No business or practitioner shall be required to pay more than one occupation tax for each of its locations.
(2)
No occupation tax shall be required from those real estate brokers, real estate agents, or real estate companies whose offices are located outside the jurisdiction and who sell property inside the jurisdiction.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95; Ord. No. O-15-12, §§ II, III, 10-5-15; Ord. No. O-22-11 , § 2(Exh. A), 10-17-22)
Sec. 22-9. - Paying occupation tax of business with no location in state.
Registration and assessment of an occupation tax is hereby imposed on those businesses and practitioners of professions with no location or office in the state if the business's largest dollar volume of business in the state is in the city and the business or practitioner:
(1)
Has one or more employees or agents who exert substantial efforts within the jurisdiction of the city for the purpose of soliciting business or serving customers or clients; or
(2)
Owns personal or real property which generates income and which is located within the jurisdiction of the city and which generates income or is used to generate income.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95)
Sec. 22-10. - Each line of business to be identified.
The registration of each business operated in the local government's jurisdiction shall identify the line or lines of business that the business conducts. No business shall conduct any line of business without first having that line of business registered with the city clerk's office and that line of business being noted by the city clerk's office upon the occupation tax certificate which is to be displayed by the business owner.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95)
Sec. 22-11. - Number of businesses considered to be operating in city.
Where a person conducts business at more than one fixed location, each location or place shall be considered a separate business for the purpose of occupation taxes, administrative fees, and regulatory fees.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95)
Sec. 22-12. - Professionals as classified in state law.
Practitioners of professions as described in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9(c)(1) through (18) shall elect as their entire occupation tax one of the following:
(1)
The occupation tax based on profitability ratio;
(2)
A fee of $400.00 per practitioner who is licensed to provide the service, such tax to be paid at the practitioner's office or location; provided, however, that a practitioner paying according to this paragraph shall not be required to provide information to the local government relating to the gross receipts of the business or practitioner. The per-practitioner fee applies to each person in the business who qualifies as a practitioner under the state's regulatory guidelines and framework;
(3)
This election is to be made on an annual basis and must be done on or before January 31, 1996. Such election shall be changed for subsequent calendar years only by a written request filed by the professional on or before January 31 of the year in which the election is to be changed.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95)
Sec. 22-13. - Practitioners exclusively practicing for a government.
Any practitioner whose office is maintained by and who is employed in practice exclusively by the United States, the state, a municipality or county of the state, instrumentalities of the United States, the state, or a municipality or county of the state shall not be required to obtain a license or pay an occupation tax for that practice.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95)
Sec. 22-14. - Purpose and scope of tax.
The occupation tax levied herein is for revenue purposes only and is not for regulatory purposes, nor is the payment of the tax made a condition precedent to the practice of any such profession, trade, or calling. The occupation tax only applies to those businesses and occupations which are covered by the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-5 to 48-13-26. All other applicable businesses and occupations are taxed by the local government pursuant to the pertinent general and/or local law and ordinance.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95)
Sec. 22-15. - When tax due and payable; effect of transacting business when tax or fee delinquent.
(a)
Except for lawyers, each such occupation tax or administrative fee, as applicable, shall be for the calendar year 1996 and succeeding calendar years thereafter unless otherwise specifically provided. Any occupation tax or administrative fee on businesses continuing from the preceding year shall be due and payable annually to the city by January 31 of each year and shall, if not paid by April 30 of each year, be subject to a ten percent penalty of the amount of tax or fees due and interest at the rate of one percent per month or any portion thereof. Any occupation tax or administrative fee for lawyers shall be payable pursuant to the provisions of section 22-26.
(b)
On any new profession, trade or calling begun in the city in 1996 or succeeding years thereafter, except for the practice of law, the occupation tax shall be delinquent if not paid immediately upon beginning business and a penalty of ten percent and interest at the rate of one percent per month or any portion thereof shall be imposed if not paid within 90 days after commencing business.
(c)
The occupation tax certificate herein provided for shall be issued by the city.
(d)
In addition to the above remedies, the city may proceed to collect in the same manner as provided by law for tax executions.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95; Ord. No. O-97-13, 4-7-97; Ord. No. O-03-10, §§ II—IV, 8-19-03; Ord. No. O-15-12, §§ IV—VIII, 10-5-15)
Sec. 22-16. - Proration of tax.
If a person commences business in the city on or after July 1 in any calendar year, the occupation tax for the remaining portion of the year shall be 50 percent of the tax imposed for the entire year. The administrative fee shall not be reduced. This provision is not intended to benefit delinquent persons who did operate a business prior to July 1 of the calendar year.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95)
Sec. 22-17. - Exemption on grounds that business is operated for charitable purpose.
No business on which an occupation tax, regulatory fee, or administrative fee is levied by this article shall be exempt from such fee or tax on the ground that such business is operated for a charitable purpose, unless 100 percent or more of the entire proceeds from such business are devoted to such purpose; except that places of worship and schools shall be exempt.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95)
Sec. 22-18. - Evidence of state registration required if applicable.
Each person who is required to be licensed by the secretary of state pursuant to O.C.G.A. title 43, excluding aviation and aeronautical engineers, shall provide evidence of proper and current state licensure before the city occupation tax certificate and/or regulatory fee certificate may be issued.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95; Ord. No. O-97-13, 4-7-97; Ord. No. O-15-12, § VIII, 10-5-15)
Sec. 22-19. - Evidence of qualification required if applicable.
Any business required to obtain health permits, bonds, certificates of qualification, certificates of competency, or any other regulatory matter shall first, before the issuance of a city occupation tax certificate or regulatory fee certificate, show evidence that such requirements have been met.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95)
Sec. 22-20. - Liability of officers and agents; registration required; failure to obtain.
All persons subject to the occupation tax levy pursuant to this article shall be required to obtain the necessary city occupation tax certificate and/or regulatory fee certificate for such business as described in this article, and in default thereof the officer or agent soliciting for or representing such persons shall be subject to the same penalty as other persons who fail to obtain a city occupation tax certificate and/or regulatory fee certificate. Every person commencing business in the city after January 1 of each year shall likewise obtain the city occupation tax certificate and/or regulatory fee certificate herein provided for upon or before commencing the same.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95; Ord. No. O-97-13, 4-7-97)
Sec. 22-21. - When occupation tax due and payable.
(a)
Each such occupation tax shall be for the calendar year in which the occupation tax certificate was obtained unless otherwise specifically provided. Every person commencing business in the city after January 1 of each year shall obtain the occupation tax certificate required upon or before commencing such business.
(b)
The occupation tax certificate herein provided for shall be issued by the city clerk.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95; Ord. No. O-97-13, 4-7-97)

Sec. 22-24. - Businesses not covered by this article.
(a)
The following businesses are not covered by the provisions of this article but may be assessed an occupation tax or other type of tax pursuant to the provisions of other general laws of the state or by local law:
(1)
Those businesses regulated by the Georgia Public Service Commission.
(2)
Those electrical service businesses organized under O.C.G.A. title 46, chapter 3.
(3)
Any farm operation for the production from or on the land of agricultural products, but not including agribusiness.
(4)
Nonprofit agricultural product cooperative marketing associations governed by O.C.G.A. § 2-10-105.
(5)
Insurance companies governed by O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8 et seq.
(6)
Motor common carriers governed by O.C.G.A. § 46-7-15.
(7)
Those businesses governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-5-355. (Businesses that purchase carload lots of guano, meats, meal, flour, bran, cottonseed, or cottonseed meal and hulls.)
(8)
Agricultural products and livestock raised in the state governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-5-356.
(9)
Depository financial institutions governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.
(10)
Any business where the levy of such occupation tax is prohibited by the laws of the state or the United States.
(b)
The exemptions and limitations contained in this article shall not be construed to repeal or otherwise affect in any way franchise fees, business taxes, or other fees or taxes otherwise permitted by law.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95)
Sec. 22-25. - More than one place or line of business.
Where a business is operated at more than one place or where the business includes more than one line, said business shall be required to obtain the necessary registration for each location and line and pay an occupation tax in accordance with the prevailing taxing method and tax rate for the dominant line of business.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95)
Sec. 22-26. - Exemption of lawyers from registration and certificate requirements; when occupation tax due and payable for lawyers.
(a)
Lawyers are expressly exempted from the registration and occupation tax certificate requirements, including posting of the certificate, of this article. Such an exemption shall not affect the occupation tax payment requirement for lawyers under this article.
(b)
Any occupation tax or administrative fee for lawyers shall be due and payable annually to the city by December 31 of each year and shall, if not paid by March 31 of the following year, be subject to a ten percent penalty of the amount of tax or fees due and interest at the rate of one percent per month. Such occupation tax or administrative fee for lawyers shall be for the calendar year concluding on December 31.
(Ord. No. O-03-10, § I, 8-19-03; Ord. No. O-15-12, §§ IX, X, 10-5-15)
Sec. 22-27. - Lien taken for delinquent occupation tax.
In addition to the other remedies herein provided for the collection of the occupation tax herein levied, the city clerk's office of the city, upon any tax or installment of such tax becoming delinquent and remaining unpaid, shall issue execution for the correct amount of such tax against the persons, partnership, or corporation liable for the tax, which execution shall bear interest at the rate of 18 percent per annum from the date when such tax or installment becomes delinquent, and the lien shall cover the property (in the city) of the person, partnership, or corporation liable for the tax, all as provided by the laws, ordinances, and charter of the city and the laws of the state. The lien of such occupation tax shall become fixed on and date from the time when such tax or any installment thereof becomes delinquent. The execution shall be levied by the city clerk or marshal of the city upon the property of the defendant located in the jurisdiction, and sufficient property shall be advertised and sold to pay the amount of the execution, with interest and costs. All other proceedings in relation thereto shall be had as is provided by the laws, ordinances, and charter of the city and the laws of the state, and the defendant in the execution shall have rights of defense, by affidavit of illegality and otherwise, which are provided by the applicable laws in regard to tax executions.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95; Ord. No. O-97-13, 4-7-97)
Sec. 22-28. - Amendment, repeal of provision.
This article shall be subject to amendment or repeal, in whole or in part, at any time, and no such amendment or repeal shall be construed to deny the right of the city commission to assess and collect any of the taxes or other charges prescribed. The amendment may increase or lower the amounts and tax rates of any occupation and may change the classification thereof. The payment of any occupation tax provided for shall not be construed as prohibiting the levy or collection by the jurisdiction of additional occupation taxes upon the same person, property, or business.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95)
Sec. 22-29. - Applications of provisions to prior ordinance.
This article does not repeal or affect the force of any part of any ordinance heretofore passed where taxes levied under such prior ordinance have not been paid in full. So much and such parts of ordinances heretofore and hereinafter passed as provided for the issuing and enforcing of execution for any tax or assessment required by such ordinances, or that imposed civil penalties for the nonpayment of such tax, or for failure to pay regulatory fees provided for in such ordinance or ordinances, or failure to comply with any other provisions hereof, shall continue and remain in force until such tax, regulatory fee, or assessment shall be fully paid.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95; Ord. No. O-97-13, 4-7-97)
Sec. 22-30. - Enforcement of provisions.
(a)
It is hereby made the duty of city officers designated by the city manager to see that the provisions of this article relating to occupation taxes are observed. City police officers are hereby authorized to issue citations for violations of this article. Each citation shall state the specific provision allegedly violated. It is made the further duty of such designated officers to inspect all occupation tax certificates issued by the city as often as in their judgment it may seem necessary to determine whether the occupation tax certificate held is proper for the business sought to be transacted hereunder.
(b)
Except for lawyers, a person or business violating any provisions of this article shall, upon order of the municipal court, be subject to a civil penalty in an amount not to exceed $500.00. Such civil penalty may be enforced by the contempt power of the municipal court.
(c)
Lawyers who fail to pay the occupation tax by the payment deadline may be reported by the city to the State Bar of Georgia.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95; Ord. No. O-97-13, 4-7-97; Ord. No. O-15-12, §§ XI, XII, 10-5-15)
Sec. 22-31. - Provisions to remain in full force and effect until changed by governing body.
This article shall remain in full force and effect until changed by amendment adopted by the city commission. All provisions hereto relating to any form of tax herein levied shall remain in full force and effect until such taxes have been paid in full.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95)
Sec. 22-32. - Public hearing requirements.
(a)
After January 1, 1996, the city commission shall conduct at least one public hearing before adopting any ordinance or resolution which will increase the rate of occupation tax as set forth in this article.
(b)
In any year when revenue from occupation taxes is greater than revenue from occupation taxes for the preceding year for the city, the city commission shall hold one or more public hearings as a part of the process of determining how to use the additional revenue.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95)
Sec. 22-33. - Option to establish exemption or reduction in occupation tax.
The city commission may by subsequent ordinance or resolution provide for an exemption or reduction in occupation tax to one or more types of businesses or practitioners of occupations or professions as part of a written plan for economic development or attracting or encouraging selected types of businesses or practitioners of selected occupations or professions. Such exemptions or reductions in occupation tax shall not be arbitrary or capricious, and the reasons shall be set forth in a written economic development plan approved by the city commission.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95)
Sec. 22-34. - Peddlers.
Any person in the city offering for sale merchandise in a door-to-door fashion, shall be deemed a peddler and every such peddler shall be required to pay an occupation tax, based on the dominant line of business as classified by the appropriate NAICS code.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95; Ord. No. O-15-12, §§ XIII, XIV, 10-5-15)
Sec. 22-35. - Stands.
No occupation tax certificate, regulatory fee certificate, or clearance to operate shall be issued to any shoe shine, newsstand, popcorn, food/beverage vendor, or any other retail stand, where such stand or stands propose to locate on any street, sidewalk, alley, or public area of the city, unless written permission is granted by the city commission.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95)
Sec. 22-36. - Businesses not listed.
Any person, firm, or corporation desiring to conduct any other business than those specified in the following schedule shall make application to the city manager or the city manager's designee, who shall determine the most appropriate NAICS code and amount of such tax based on profitability ratios, and shall instruct the Revenue Division as to the classification and amount of the tax.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95; Ord. No. O-15-12, § XV, 10-5-15; Ord. No. O-22-11 , § 2(Exh. A), 10-17-22)
Sec. 22-37. - Reduction in tax for persons with a business activity located in their principal residence.
Other provisions of this chapter notwithstanding, a person required to obtain an occupation tax certificate for a business activity located in said person's primary residence with no more than one non-resident employed in the business activity for no more than ten hours per week shall pay the occupation tax normally required as specified in section 22-7 and section 22-8 for profitability ratio/tax class 1. No reduction in the administrative fee or regulatory fee(s) is intended for these persons. If these persons begin business operations prior to July 1 of the calendar year, they must pay the normal occupation tax. If these persons begin business operations after July 1 of the calendar year, and have not operated any portion of their business prior to July 1, then they shall pay one-half of the normal occupation tax.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95; Ord. No. O-01-05, 3-5-01; Ord. No. O-22-11 , § 2(Exh. A), 10-17-22)
Sec. 22-38. - Denial.
(a)
The city manager or the city manager's designee shall be authorized to deny an application for an occupation tax certificate, for any of the following reasons:
(1)
The applicant fails to provide evidence required by section 2-18.
(2)
The applicant furnishes fraudulent or untruthful information or omits required information in the application for an occupation tax certificate.
(3)
The applicant is not a citizen of the United States or lawfully present in the United States, as evidenced by an affidavit complying with O.C.G.A. § 50-36-1.
(b)
The city manager or the city manager's designee shall give written notification to the applicant of such denial, including a specification of the reasons for denial.
(c)
An applicant may appeal a denial through the appeals procedure identified in section 22-39.
(d)
An applicant shall not furnish fraudulent or untruthful information in the application.
(Ord. No. O-15-12, § XVI, 10-5-15; Ord. No. O-22-11 , § 2(Exh. A), 10-17-22)
Sec. 22-39. - Procedure for appeals.
Any person taxed under this article shall have the right to appeal the tax classification to the city commission, provided however that the appeal be in writing stating the grounds for the appeal and provided that the petition of appeal be first presented to the city manager.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95)
Sec. 22-40. - Conflicts between specific and general provisions.
Where there is an apparent conflict in this article between specific and general provisions, it is the intention hereof that the specific shall control.
(Ord. No. O-95-16, 12-20-95)
Sec. 22-41. - Alteration of certificates.
The holder of an occupation tax certificate shall not alter or modify the information contained on any occupation tax certificate issued by the city.
(Ord. No. O-15-12, § XVII, 10-5-15)
Secs. 22-42—22-45. - Reserved.
