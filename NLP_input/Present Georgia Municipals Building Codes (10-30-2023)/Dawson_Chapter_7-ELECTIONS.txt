Sec. 7-1. - Applicability of charter and state law.
Except to the extent that the same are modified by court order no. 77-27-AMER, future elections in the city shall be governed by the charter of the city and the state municipal election code.
(Code 1974, § 2-101)
State Law reference— Authority to adopt state rules and regulations in this area is conferred on municipalities by the municipal election code, O.C.G.A. § 21-3-8.
Sec. 7-2. - Elective officers.
The elective officers of the city shall consist of a mayor and six (6) councilmembers who shall be elected as provided by law.
(Code 1974, § 2-402)
Charter reference— Elective officers, § 2-1.
Sec. 7-3. - Expenses.
Such funds as are necessary for the conduct of elections and for the performance of the duties that are specified by this chapter shall be budgeted and appropriated annually, and from time to time.
(Code 1974, § 2-102)
State Law reference— Municipalities are required by law to budget funds for the conduct of municipal elections, O.C.G.A. § 21-3-8.
Sec. 7-4. - Election officials.
(a)
Appointment: The following election officials may be appointed by the mayor and council and shall receive such compensation as is provided by same:
(1)
Municipal election superintendent;
(2)
Chief manager;
(3)
Two (2) assistant managers;
(4)
Clerks, as necessary.
State Law reference— Appointment, O.C.G.A. §§ 21-3-32, 21-3-33.
(b)
Qualifications and powers: The municipal election superintendent and all poll officers shall meet such qualifications and exercise such powers and duties as are provided in O.C.G.A. § 21-3-30 et seq.
(Code 1974, § 2-401)
Sec. 7-5. - Election date.
Every two (2) years a regular election shall be held on the date specified in section 8-4 of the charter for the selection of three (3) councilmembers, one (1) each from wards two, five and six to serve two-year terms, and likewise there shall be such an election on the following year from wards one, three and four.
(Code 1974, § 2-403)
Sec. 7-6. - Candidate qualification fees.
The qualification fee to be paid by any candidates seeking election in any special or general election in the city is hereby set at three (3) percent of the annual salary of the office for which the candidate seeks election. If a candidate shall file for election to an office for which there is no salary, the qualification fee for qualifying for such office shall be thirty-five dollars ($35.00). This fee shall be paid to the municipal superintendent at the time a candidate files his notice of candidacy.
(Code 1974, § 2-302; Ord. of 2-11-93(2))
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 21-3-90.
Sec. 7-7. - Campaign financing disclosure.
All candidates for municipal public office shall keep all such reports as are required by the state campaign and financial disclosure act, O.C.G.A. § 21-5-50 et seq.
(Code 1974, § 2-303)
Sec. 7-8. - Polling place.
The polling place within the city shall be that designated by the council pursuant to charter section 8-6.
(Code 1974, § 2-405)
Sec. 7-9. - Voter registration.
If any person whose name is not on the municipal registration list desires to vote at any primary or special or general election, he shall, at least thirty (30) days prior to the primary, special, or general election at which he desires to vote, apply to be registered as an elector. The registrars shall thirty (30) days prior to such primary, general, or special election cease taking applications to qualify persons to vote in such election.
(Ord. of 2-11-93(3))
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 21-3-126(b)(1).
