DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 12-318. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
City means the city commission, a municipal corporation, to include all areas within the corporate limits of the city.
City manager or his designee means the individual employee or organizational unit of the city charged with the responsibility for administering and enforcing this article, except for specific responsibilities otherwise provided herein.
Driver means any person who drives or operates a taxicab on the streets of the city.
Stand means a public place alongside the curb of a street or elsewhere which has been designated by the city as reserved exclusively for the use of taxicabs.
Street means and includes any street, alley, lane, avenue, court, or public place in the city.
Taxicab means a motor vehicle engaged in the business of carrying passengers for hire or offering to carry passengers for hire. The term "taxicab" does not include motor vehicles used as ambulances, sight-seeing cars or buses, or limousines and buses operating on a fixed route.
Taxicab company means the holder of one or more taxicab regulatory permits to operate a taxicab under the provisions of this article, whether a person, firm, partnership, or corporation.
Taxicab regulatory permit means the right and privilege granted by the city for the operation of a taxicab within the corporate limits of the city issued upon the receipt of a regulatory fee established in the revenue ordinance.
Taximeter means a meter instrument or device attached to a taxicab which measures the distance driven and the waiting time upon which the fare is based.
Telephone stand means a place alongside a street or elsewhere where a taxicab company has been authorized by the city to install a telephone or call box for the taking of calls and the dispatching of taxicabs.
Zoning board of appeals means a citizen group to review appeals of citations and suspension pursuant to this article.
(Code 2000, § 22-301; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-319. - Taxicab regulatory permit required.
No person, firm, or corporation shall operate a taxicab on the streets of the city unless a taxicab regulatory permit has been issued by the city for such taxicab. All taxicabs shall display a current taxicab regulatory permit issued by the city to signify compliance with this permit requirement in each vehicle. A taxicab regulatory permit shall be issued by the city manager or his designee only after the taxicab has been inspected and found to comply with all requirements of this article, including the payment of the taxicab regulatory permit fee. The taxicab regulatory permit fee is established in the amount set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 2000, § 22-302; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-320. - Fixed business address required.
Each taxicab company shall, as a condition of receiving one or more taxicab regulatory permits to operate a taxicab in the city, provide a physical address for the taxicab company. If the company headquarters is to be moved, the owner of the taxicab company shall notify the city manager or his designee of the new location prior to relocation. If the taxicab company is located within the corporate limits of the city, the taxicab company shall conform with all zoning, business tax certificate, and other applicable laws of the city.
(Code 2000, § 22-303; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-321. - Indemnity for benefit of city.
Any taxicab company operating under this article shall hold the city harmless against any and all liability, loss, costs, damages, or expense which may accrue to the city by reason of the negligence, default, or misconduct of the company in connection with the rights granted to such company hereunder. Nothing in this article shall be considered to make the city liable for damages because of any negligent act or omission or commission by any taxicab company, its servants, agents, drivers, or other employees, during the operation by the company of a taxicab business or service, either in respect to injuries to persons or with respect to damage to property which may be sustained.
(Code 2000, § 22-304; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-322. - Insurance for benefit of passenger.
Any taxicab company desiring to do business in the city shall give and maintain a policy of indemnity insurance with an indemnity insurance company authorized to do business in the state for each vehicle in use as a taxicab, or a self-insurance certificate issued under state guidelines and regulated by the insurance commissioner of the state. Proof of insurance shall be kept in the vehicle and on file at the office of the city clerk and with the city manager or his designee. The minimum insurance coverage shall be in accordance with state law. Such indemnity insurance shall insure to the benefit of any person who is injured or who sustains damage to property cause by the negligence of a taxicab company or the taxicab driver, whether as servant, agent, or independent contractor.
(Code 2000, § 22-305; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
State Law reference— Insurance required of taxicab drivers, O.C.G.A. § 36-60-25(f).
Sec. 12-323. - Blanket policy.
Any company or person operating a taxicab in the city shall give a separate policy of indemnity insurance or certificate of self-insurance for each separate taxicab for hire, except where such company or person actually owns or holds legal title to more than one taxicab, in which event such company or person may give one policy of indemnity insurance or certificate or self-insurance covering all the taxicabs actually owned. This latter provision, however, shall not apply to any group of persons separately owning taxicabs who may be jointly operating or doing business under a licensed taxicab company name. The taxicab company is responsible for ensuring that individual persons operating taxicabs under said license shall comply with all state laws and local ordinances governing insurance coverage.
(Code 2000, § 22-306; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-324. - Notice when voided.
Each certificate of insurance submitted to the city manager, or his designee, prior to the insurance of a taxicab regulatory permit shall include, by endorsement to the policy, a statement that written notice shall be given to the city by certified mail 30 days prior to cancellation or upon any material change in coverage.
(Code 2000, § 22-307; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-325. - Photographs of applicant.
Before any permit is granted under this article, the city manager or his designee shall take two photographs of the applicant. One photograph shall be attached permanently to the regulatory permit for display in the vehicle which the applicant is to drive, and the second photograph shall be filed for record by the city manager, or his designee.
(Code 2000, § 22-308; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-326. - State driver's license required.
Any person applying for a taxicab regulatory permit under this article must show that a current motor vehicle operator's license has been issued or approved by the state and that the license is not under suspension or revocation.
(Code 2000, § 22-309; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-327. - Permit and notice to be displayed.
(a)
The permit issued under the provisions of this article shall be placed on display in the taxicab to be operated by the holder of the permit in a display case or holder designed for that purpose on the dashboard facing the passenger compartment, in clear view of the passengers at all times when the taxicab is for hire.
(b)
A notice provided by the city manager, or his designee, shall be on display on the front dashboard facing the passenger compartment. The notice shall contain sufficient information to allow the customer to contact the city manager or his designee.
(Code 2000, § 22-310; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Secs. 12-328—12-357. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - OPERATING REGULATIONS

Sec. 12-358. - Taxicab identification and marking.
Every taxicab shall have a sign plainly indicating on the door on each side of the vehicle, in letters not less than four inches high, the full name of the taxicab company operating the vehicle, including words such as "taxicab," "taxi," or "cab." Such sign shall be professionally applied and shall be either painted or permanently affixed to each side of the vehicle. There shall be no outside advertising of any kind on taxicabs.
(Code 2000, § 22-311; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-359. - Assignment of number.
There shall be shown on the door on each side and on the rear of each taxicab a number at least four inches high, such number to be separate and distinct from that on any other public vehicle or taxicab in the city. Such number shall be assigned to such taxicab and the owner thereof by the city manager, or his designee. Such number shall be professionally applied and shall be either painted or permanently affixed thereon.
(Code 2000, § 22-312; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-360. - Identification lights.
Every taxicab operated on the streets of the city shall display, as a part of its equipment, an identification insignia on top, which shall be electrically lit at night, and which top light shall carry either the name of the company operating the taxicab, the word "taxi," "taxicab," or "cab"; provided, however, that during the period in which a taxicab is occupied and under hire by a passenger, the identification insignia on the top of the vehicle shall not be lit.
(Code 2000, § 22-313; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-361. - Registration of number and names of owner and operator.
The number assigned a taxicab in accordance with this division, together with the names of the owner and operator of the taxicab, shall be registered with the city manager or his designee in a book to be kept for such purposes.
(Code 2000, § 22-314; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-362. - Distinctive color scheme.
No two taxicab companies or taxicab operators may operate taxicabs of the same color scheme. Color schemes shall be recorded and controlled by the city manager or his designee.
(Code 2000, § 22-315; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-363. - Safe mechanical condition of taxicabs required.
Every taxicab operated on the streets of the city shall be maintained in a safe mechanical condition, with all safety and pollution control equipment remaining intact and operative at all times when the taxicab is in service.
(Code 2000, § 22-316; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-364. - Cleanliness of taxicabs required.
Each vehicle operating under this division shall be kept painted, and in a clean sanitary condition, free of litter and debris and at all times suitable for public transportation of passengers.
(Code 2000, § 22-317; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-365. - State license tag for taxicabs requires.
Prior to the use and operation of any vehicle as a taxicab under the provisions of this division, the owner of said vehicle shall secure and display on the vehicle a current state license registration tag.
(Code 2000, § 22-318; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-366. - Manifest.
Each taxicab driver, operator, or company shall maintain a daily manifest upon which shall be recorded all trips made by each vehicle each day, showing time and place of origin, destination of each trip, and amount of fare. All such completed manifests shall be returned to the taxicab company by the driver at least weekly or shall be maintained by the company at its offices at the conclusion of each driver's daily tour of duty. Each taxicab company shall retain and preserve all drivers' manifests for at least 90 days and said manifests shall be made available to the city manager, or his designee, any official of the finance department, or to any officer of the police department at all reasonable times. The manifest shall be kept in a legible condition.
(Code 2000, § 22-319; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-367. - Taximeter required.
All taxicabs operated under the authority of this division shall be equipped with a taximeter fastened in front of the passengers, visible to them at all times, day and night; and, after sundown, the face of the taximeter shall be illuminated. The taximeter shall be operated by a mechanism of standard design and construction. Any such taximeter shall be sealed at all points and connections by the city manager, or his designee, which, if manipulated, would affect its correct reading and recording. Each taximeter shall have thereon a signal to denote when the vehicle is employed and when it is not employed, and it shall be the duty of the driver to activate the signal of such taximeter into a nonrecording position at the termination of each trip. Any taximeter shall be subject to inspection at any time by the city manager, or his designee.
(Code 2000, § 22-320; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-368. - Posting rates required.
(a)
No owner or driver of a taxicab shall charge a greater amount for the use of a taxicab than the published and printed rates which shall be displayed within and on each vehicle, as provided in subsection (b) of this section.
(b)
Each taxicab operating within the city shall have a rate card setting forth its rates for mileage, waiting time, extra passengers, excess baggage, and airport flat rates. Said rate card shall be printed in standard block letters showing the taxicab company name not less than three-quarters-inch in height, and the rates not less than three-eighths-inch in height. The rate card shall be displayed within the vehicle in such place as to be conspicuous and in view of all passengers, and subject to the approval of the city manager or his designee.
(Code 2000, § 22-320.5; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-369. - Receipts.
The driver of any taxicab shall, upon demand by the passenger, render to such passenger a receipt for the amount charged, on which shall be the name of the taxicab company, the taxicab number, name and permit number of the driver, trip origin and destination, amount of charges, and date of transaction.
(Code 2000, § 22-321; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-370. - Refusal of passenger to pay legal fare.
It shall be unlawful for any person to refuse to pay the legal fare of any vehicle having a valid taxicab regulatory permit under this division after having hired the same, and it shall be unlawful for any person to hire any taxicab with intent to defraud the person from whom it is hired of the value of such service.
(Code 2000, § 22-322; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-371. - Taxicab stands.
No parking shall be permitted in the corporate limits of the city except at such stands as may be established and approved by the city commission, which taxicab stands shall be designated by signs. The parking of such vehicles is expressly prohibited in public parking places and residences. Whenever any such stand is established, such stand may be used by taxicabs upon a rotation basis of first-come, first-served. Taxicab drivers shall pull into stands from the rear and shall advance forward as the taxicabs ahead depart. Drivers shall stay within five feet of their cabs and shall not solicit passengers or engage in loud or boisterous talk while at the stand. Nothing in this section shall be construed as preventing a passenger from boarding any taxicab of choice that is parked at a taxicab stand.
(Code 2000, § 22-323; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-372. - Telephone stands.
It shall be unlawful for any person to install or attach any telephone or similar device on any telephone post, telegraph post, tree post, or on the side of any building in any street, sidewalk, alley, or lane in the city for the purpose of using such telephone in connection with the taxicab business, unless a place in the street adjacent to such telephone has been designated a regular stand for taxicabs. The operator or driver of taxicabs holding a valid taxicab regulatory permit under this division shall not use taxicab stands as base stations to dispatch other units.
(Code 2000, § 22-324; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-373. - Application for taxicab stands.
Any person desiring to have a place designated as a regular stand for taxicabs in the city or as a telephone stand shall make application by written petition to the mayor and city commission for the establishment of such taxicab stand, setting out where such stand is desired to be set apart. The written permission of the property owner whose property abuts shall be first obtained and filed with the application.
(Code 2000, § 22-325; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-374. - Taxicab parking prohibited in certain areas.
It shall be unlawful for taxicabs to use or park in any area or stand designated for the exclusive use of airline limousines and no parking shall be permitted at the Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, except at such stands as may be designated by the mayor and commission upon the recommendation of the airport commission for the exclusive use of designated companies and by no other company or persons.
(Code 2000, § 22-326; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-375. - Proper dress required.
While operating a taxicab for hire on the streets of the city, a taxicab driver shall be properly attired in accordance with the following standards:
(1)
A driver's clothing and shoes shall be neat and in good repair, free of holes, tears, fading, stains, or soil.
(2)
Proper hygiene is required.
(Code 2000, § 22-327; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-376. - Soliciting passengers prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to solicit passengers verbally or by gesture, directly or indirectly, at any taxicab stand or upon the streets of the city.
(Code 2000, § 22-328; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-377. - Cruising.
Taxicab drivers shall be permitted to drive such taxicabs about the streets of the city for the purposes of picking up and transporting passengers.
(Code 2000, § 22-329; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-378. - Use of designated bus stops prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any driver of any taxicab to park or stand at any bus stop designated for use by the Gwinnett County transit authority.
(Code 2000, § 22-330; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-379. - Carrying more than one fare-paying passenger.
It shall be unlawful for the operator of any taxicab to pick up a second passenger or to permit any other person to occupy or ride in the taxicab when the same is occupied by a fare-paying passenger or by members of the party of the fare-paying passenger.
(Code 2000, § 22-331; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-380. - Restriction on number of passengers.
No driver shall permit more persons to be carried in a taxicab as passengers than the rated seating capacity of his taxicab. A child in arms shall not be counted as a passenger.
(Code 2000, § 22-332; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-381. - Refusal to carry orderly passengers prohibited.
No driver shall refuse or neglect to convey any orderly person, upon request, unless previously engaged or unable or forbidden by the provisions of this division to do so.
(Code 2000, § 22-333; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-382. - Prohibitions of drivers.
It shall be unlawful for any driver of a taxicab to solicit business for any hotel or other business, or to attempt to divert patronage from one hotel or business to another. It shall be unlawful for a driver to solicit business from any employee of any hotel or other such business. Neither shall such driver engage in selling or soliciting for the sale of intoxicating beverages or illegal drugs, or solicit for prostitution, or use his vehicle for any purpose other than the transportation of passengers.
(Code 2000, § 22-334; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-383. - Smoking prohibited when taxicab is occupied by a passenger.
It shall be unlawful for the driver of any taxicab operated upon the streets of the city to smoke in such taxicab while the taxicab is occupied by a passenger.
(Code 2000, § 22-335; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-384. - Taxicab movement prohibited under certain circumstances.
No driver shall collect fares, make change, or take on or discharge passengers while his taxicab is in motion.
(Code 2000, § 22-336; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-385. - Property left in taxicab by a passenger.
Any taxicab driver or operator discovering in any taxicab under such driver's or operator's control personal property, which was lost or left therein by a passenger of such taxicab, shall report such loss and deliver all such property to the office of the taxicab company within 12 hours after the discovery of such property. The driver's report shall include brief particulars to enable the company to identify the owner of the property. The company shall retain the property on behalf of the owner for at least 60 days.
(Code 2000, § 22-337; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-386. - Sleeping, lounging, or lying in taxicabs prohibited.
While any taxicab is parked at a stand or is otherwise in service, the driver will remain awake and alert at all times. Sleeping, lounging in a reclining posture, or lying in the taxicab is prohibited.
(Code 2000, § 22-338; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-387. - Radio scanners prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any taxicab driver, owner, or operator to use a radio frequency scanner or to be in possession of a radio frequency scanner in a taxicab or in company headquarters, and no such scanner shall be in the possession of any taxicab driver, owner, or transmissions of other taxicab companies.
(Code 2000, § 22-339; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-388. - Firearms in taxicabs.
(a)
Any driver possessing a firearm in a taxicab must have a current, valid state firearms permit and written permission of the taxicab company. Such driver shall furnish the city manager or his designee a copy of the firearms permit, the brand name, serial number, type, and caliber of the firearm, and the original written permission statement of the taxicab company, which statement shall be signed by the owner or chief operating officer of the company.
(b)
Possession of a firearm on Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport property is prohibited at all times.
(Code 2000, § 22-340; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-389. - Age limit of taxicabs.
Every taxicab operating on the streets of the city shall be no more than ten model years older than the current calendar year.
(Code 2000, § 22-341; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))

DIVISION 3. - ENFORCEMENT

Sec. 12-407. - Vehicle inspections required.
Each taxicab shall be inspected by the city manager, or his designee, for compliance with the provisions of this article and shall pass such inspection before the vehicle may be used as a taxicab in the city. Each taxicab involved in an accident shall be inspected by the city manager or his designee before it may be returned to service transporting passengers for hire. Each taxicab shall be inspected by the city manager, or his designee, at least once annually to ensure continued maintenance of safe operating condition and continued compliance with the provisions of this article.
(Code 2000, § 22-342; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-408. - Additional vehicle inspection.
Any taxicab shall be subject to inspection at any time and, upon discovery of any violation of this or other applicable laws, the city manager, or his designee, shall take appropriate remedial action as authorized under this article. The city manager, or his designee, shall establish a systematic procedure to perform random, unannounced inspections of taxicabs to enforce compliance with this article and all other applicable laws. By accepting a taxicab regulatory permit and/or a taxicab driver's license to operate taxicab service in the city, each taxicab owner/operator consents to unannounced, randomly selected inspections to ensure compliance with this article and other applicable ordinances.
(Code 2000, § 22-343; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-409. - Taximeter inspection.
Any taximeter shall be subject to inspection at any time and upon discovery of any violation or any inaccuracy, the city manager or his designee shall notify the operator of said taxicab to cease operation immediately, which action shall take the taxicab out of service and keep it out of service until the taximeter is repaired, the required working condition is restored, and the taximeter is resealed by the city manager, or his designee.
(Code 2000, § 22-344; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-410. - Citation for violation of article.
The city manager or his designee, or other agents authorized by the city commission, shall have the authority to cite or subpoena taxicab operators and/or taxicab company owners for violations of this article. The fees for violations of this article shall not exceed the maximum as established and levied by the city's municipal court.
(Code 2000, § 22-345; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-411. - Failure to pay fee.
(a)
When any taxicab company owner or taxicab operator fails to pay a fine in accordance with section 12-410 within ten calendar days after the notice was issued, a late payment penalty shall be added.
(b)
A taxicab operator or taxicab company owner may appeal citations to the zoning board of appeals. In the case that a taxicab operator or taxicab company owner submits a written appeal to the city manager and city commission before the end of the tenth calendar day from the date of the citation, no late payment penalty fee shall be applied.
(Code 2000, § 22-346; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-412. - Authority for removal of taxicabs from streets.
The city manager, or his designee, shall have the authority to remove from operation on the streets of the city any vehicle used as a taxicab which is in violation of this article and to prohibit operation of such taxicab until all deficiencies have been corrected. An order of the city manager, or his designee, to remove a vehicle from the city streets may be appealed to the zoning board of appeals as provided herein. Until the appeal has been heard and the zoning board of appeals has ruled otherwise, the taxicab ordered to be removed from service shall not be used for operations.
(Code 2000, § 22-347(1); Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-413. - Driving after suspension or revocation prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a motor vehicle for hire or taxicab for the carriage of passengers during any period in which his permit to do so is suspended or revoked in accord with the provisions of this article.
(Code 2000, § 22-347(2); Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Sec. 12-414. - Zoning board of appeals.
(a)
The zoning board of appeals shall meet as needed to review unresolved appeals of permit denials, citations, suspensions, and taxicab removal orders pursuant to this article.
(b)
Upon the receipt of a written appeal of the city manager's ruling, the zoning board of appeals shall convene with reasonable notice to hear evidence from both the city manager or his designee and the taxicab driver or his/her representative. By a majority vote of the entire body, the zoning board of appeals may:
(1)
Uphold the ruling of the city manager, or his designee;
(2)
Overturn the ruling of the city manager, or his designee;
(3)
Reduce the ruling of the city manager, or his designee, and impose a lesser penalty authorized under this article.
(c)
Decisions of the zoning board of appeals may be appealed to the city commission, whose decision shall be final.
(Code 2000, § 22-348; Ord. of 10-2-2006(1))
Secs. 12-415—12-441. - Reserved.
