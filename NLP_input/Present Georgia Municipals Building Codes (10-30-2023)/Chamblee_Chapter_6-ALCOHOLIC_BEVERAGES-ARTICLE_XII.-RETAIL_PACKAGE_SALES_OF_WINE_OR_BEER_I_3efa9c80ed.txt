ARTICLE XII. - RETAIL PACKAGE SALES OF WINE OR BEER IN SPECIALTY SHOPS[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
Editor's note— Ord. No. 692 , pt. V, adopted Nov. 17, 2015, amended art. XII in its entirety to read as herein set out. Former art. XII, §§ 6-253—6-257, was entitled "Retail Package Sales of Wine in Wine Specialty Shops", and derived from Ord. No. 631, pt. I, adopted Feb. 15, 2011.

Sec. 6-253. - Permitted locations.
No wine or malt beverages shall be sold at retail pursuant to this article except in establishments whose total interior floor area, inclusive of storage area, shall not exceed 3,500 square feet and are located in zoning districts in which these establishments are permitted as a conforming use.
(Ord. No. 692 , pt. V, 11-17-15)
Sec. 6-254. - License required; compliance with applicable regulations.
It shall be unlawful to operate an establishment governed under the provisions of this article without first obtaining a license from the finance director and paying such fees and taxes as may be required by the ordinances, rules and regulations of the city.
(Ord. No. 692 , pt. V, 11-17-15; Ord. No. 801 , pt. XXXVII, 9-21-21)
Sec. 6-255. - Investigative and administrative fee.
Each application for a license under this article shall be accompanied by payment for the full amount of the license fee, together with payment in an amount as may be required by the ordinances, rules and regulations of the city to defray investigative and administrative costs. If the application is denied and the license refused, or if the applicant withdraws the application prior to its being issued, the license fee shall be refunded, but the investigative and administrative fees shall be retained. Notwithstanding the foregoing sentence, the license fee shall not be refunded to any applicant that sold, or offered to sell, alcoholic beverages during the license year at issue.
(Ord. No. 692 , pts. V, VIII, 11-17-15; Ord. No. 801 , pt. XXXVIII, 9-21-21)
Sec. 6-256. - Hours of sale.
Retail package licensees may engage in the sale of wine and beer between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 11:45 p.m. Monday through Saturday and on Sundays between the hours of 11:00 a.m. and 11:45 p.m. The hours within which business may be carried on shall be determined by the standard time in force at the time of the sale.
(Ord. No. 692 , pt. V, 11-17-15; Ord. No. 794 , pt. XI, 3-16-21)
Sec. 6-257. - Marking of prices.
Retailers shall indicate plainly, by tags or labels on the bottles or containers or on the shelf immediately below where the containers are placed, the prices of all wine and beer exposed or offered for sale.
(Ord. No. 692 , pt. V, 11-17-15)
Sec. 6-258. - Wine and malt beverage tasting.
(a)
The holder of a package wine license, with or without a package malt beverage license, but in no event with a package distilled spirits license, shall be allowed to provide samples of wine offered for sale to customers under the conditions set forth in this section.
(b)
The holder of a package malt beverage license, with or without a package wine license, but in no event with a package distilled spirits license, shall be allowed to provide samples of draft malt beverages offered for sale to customers under the conditions set forth in this section.
(c)
Wine and malt beverage sampling shall be on limited occasions when a customer requests a sample of a wine or malt beverage offered for sale within the premises, or in conjunction with wine or malt beverage education classes and sampling designed to promote wine or malt beverage appreciation and education.
(d)
Wine or malt beverage tasting for customers shall only be conducted at a counter area constituting no more than ten percent of the entire floor area of the premises.
(e)
Samples shall not exceed two ounces, and no customer shall consume more than 12 ounces in any two-hour period.
(f)
Only the licensee or an employee shall open, handle or serve samples.
(g)
No open wine or malt beverage containers shall be removed from the licensed premises.
(h)
Not more than two times per week for a period of not to exceed two consecutive hours, the holder of a wine or malt beverage package license in a wine or malt beverage specialty shop may conduct educational classes and sampling for class participants. All conditions of sampling set forth in this section shall apply to such classes, except for the limitation on floor areas where the classes can be conducted.
(i)
Licensees shall not charge for samples or tastings.
(Ord. No. 692 , pt. V, 11-17-15)
Secs. 6-259—6-260. - Reserved.
