ARTICLE VIII. - E 9-1-1 ADVISORY COMMITTEE[11]

Cross reference— Civil emergencies, ch. 30.

Sec. 2-326. - Established.
An E 9-1-1 advisory committee is hereby established.
(Code 1977, § 2-186)
Sec. 2-327. - Members.
The E 9-1-1 advisory committee shall be composed of seven members as follows:
(1)
A member of the board of commissioners of county to be named by the committee.
(2)
The mayor or city councilmember of the City of Douglasville, Georgia, to be named by the mayor and approved by resolution by the council.
(3)
The chief of the county fire department or a representative appointed by the chief of the fire department.
(4)
The chief of the police department or a representative appointed by the chief of police.
(5)
The county sheriff or a representative appointed by the sheriff.
(6)
A private citizen who resides in the county and who is elected to a two-year term by the E 9-1-1 advisory committee at the first meeting of every other year.
(7)
A private citizen who resides in the City of Douglasville and who is elected to a two-year term by the E 9-1-1 advisory committee at the first meeting of every other year.
(Code 1977, § 2-186)
Sec. 2-328. - Terms.
The members of the E 9-1-1 advisory committee shall serve in two-year staggered terms with the board of commissioner's representative, city council representative and the fire department representative initially serving a three-year term to expire on December 31, 1991, and two-year terms thereafter to expire on odd numbered years. The city police department representative, the sheriff's department representative and the two private citizens will serve two-year terms with the first term to expire on December 31, 1990, and two-year terms thereafter that expire on even numbered years.
(Code 1977, § 2-186)
Sec. 2-329. - Removal.
All members of the E 9-1-1 advisory committee shall serve at the pleasure of the mayor and city council and the board of commissioners and may be removed with cause by a joint resolution by the city council and the county board of commissioners.
(Code 1977, § 2-186)
Sec. 2-330. - Meetings; reports; compensation.
The E 9-1-1 advisory committee shall meet at least once every 60 days and shall make written reports of each meeting to the city council and the county board of commissioners. The E 9-1-1 advisory committee shall serve without compensation.
(Code 1977, § 2-187)
