ARTICLE IV. - DEPARTMENTS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-126. - Organization of all divisions.
(a)
The municipal government workforce shall be organized into divisions and departments reporting to the city manager's office as follows:
(1)
The executive services division, headed by the city clerk, shall be responsible for the public information and city clerk functions.
(2)
The planning and development division, headed by the planning and development director, shall be responsible for planning, zoning, protective/building inspection, and code enforcement functions.
(3)
The financial and administrative services division, headed by the financial and administrative services director, shall be responsible for the financial, accounting, treasury, information technology, geographic information systems, municipal court, city hall customer service, purchasing, payroll, accounts receivable, accounts payable, grant administration, and budgeting functions.
(4)
The human resources and risk management division, headed by the human resources and risk management director, shall be responsible for human resources and risk management, including workers' compensation and safety functions.
(5)
The public safety division shall be divided into two separate departments, each of which shall be regarded as a division:
a.
The police department, headed by the police chief, shall be responsible for law enforcement, security, and safety education functions.
b.
The fire department, headed by the fire chief, shall be responsible for fire suppression, fire prevention, fire inspections, fire investigations, public fire and life safety education, technical rescue, hazardous materials response and mitigation, emergency planning and management, and emergency medical services functions.
(6)
The public works division, headed by the public works director, shall be responsible for the public works functions including the maintenance of city infrastructure including but not limited to streets, rights-of-way, paths, signs, facility grounds, recycling functions, vehicles, and equipment.
(7)
The engineering services division, headed by the city engineer, shall be responsible for city engineering services, building maintenance, and stormwater.
(8)
The recreation and special events division, headed by the recreation and special events director, shall be responsible for recreation and recreation programming, sports association management, special events, amphitheater, grounds maintenance landscaping, and maintenance functions for city recreation facilities.
(9)
The library services department, headed by the director of library services, shall be responsible for library programming and all other library services.
(b)
Each director or chief oversees the operations of the respective division and supervises the department heads within the division. The directors/chiefs report directly to the city manager or assistant city manager, serving in the capacity of chief administrative officer and assistant chief administrative officer, and, along with the city manager, assistant city manager, and their department head designees, comprise the city's executive leadership team.
(Ord. No. 763, 9-6-2001; Ord. No. 978, § 1, 7-16-2009; Ord. No. 1068, § 1, 10-3-2013; Ord. No. 1132, § 1, 9-7-2017; Ord. No. 1200 , 9-15-2022; Ord. No. 1208 , § 1, 4-20-2023)

The fire department shall consist of a chief and such other authorized positions as shall be deemed necessary by the council for the effective operation of the department; the only employees receiving a salary for service rendered shall be the chief and such other authorized positions as may be appointed by the council, which likewise shall fix the compensation of such employees.

The chief of the fire department shall be technically qualified by training and experience and shall have the ability to command men and hold their respect and confidence. He shall be removed from office as prescribed by the Charter.
(Code 1980, § 2-53; Ord. No. 1208 , § 1, 4-20-2023)

(a)
The chief of the fire department shall be appointed by the city manager. The chief of the fire department shall serve without reappointment until terminated according to city policies or otherwise resigns.
(b)
All other fire department personnel shall be accountable to the chief of the fire department and to the city manager's office as assigned.

Sec. 2-176. - Duties of the chief.
(a)
Formulation of rules and regulations. The chief of the fire department shall formulate a set of rules and regulations to govern the department and shall be responsible to the chief administrative officer and council for the personnel, morale and the general efficiency of the department.
(b)
Determining department organization and responsibility. The chief shall determine the number and kind of companies of which the department is to be composed and shall determine the responsibility of such companies to alarms.
(c)
Conduct of drills and instructions. The chief shall, at least once a month, conduct suitable drills or instructions in the operation and handling of equipment, first aid and rescue work, salvage, a study of the buildings in the city, fire prevention, water supply, and all other matters generally considered essential to good firemanship and safety of life and property.
(d)
Investigation of fires. The chief is required to assist the proper authorities in suppressing the crime of arson by investigating or causing to be investigated the cause, origin and circumstances of all fires.
(e)
Right of entry; issuing abatement notices. The chief is empowered to enter any buildings or premises at reasonable times for the purpose of making inspections and to serve written notice upon the owner or occupant to abate within a fixed time any fire hazards that may be found.
(f)
Keeping of records. The chief shall see that complete records are kept of all fires, EMS runs, apparatus and equipment, personnel, and other information about the work of the department.
(g)
Making monthly reports. The chief shall report on the operations of the department in a format approved by the chief administrative officer.
(h)
Making annual reports and recommendations. The chief shall make a complete annual report to the chief administrative officer within one month after the close of each calendar year, such report to include information specified in subsection (f) of this section, together with comparative data for previous years and recommendations for improving the effectiveness of the department.
(i)
Submission of budget. The chief shall, at the direction of the chief administrative officer, submit for approval to the chief administrative officer and city council a budget for the operation of the fire department and the purchase of capital equipment required for the efficient operation of the department. Further, he shall provide with the council's approval, sufficient funds for educational training, (books, seminars, schooling, etc.) that will improve the efficiency of all personnel as it relates to current fire and rescue practices.
(Code 1980, § 2-55; Ord. No. 1208 , § 1, 4-20-2023)
Sec. 2-177. - Emergency medical services.
The fire department, under the direction of the chief and such persons as may be appointed by the chief, shall operate the emergency medical services. All such personnel shall possess the required training and experience.
(Code 1980, § 2-56; Ord. No. 1208 , § 1, 4-20-2023)
Sec. 2-178. - Volunteers—Membership; employee status.
The membership of the fire department, other than the chief and permanent personnel employed by the council, shall consist of unpaid volunteers who shall be considered employees of the city only while actively engaged as members of the fire department of the city.
(Code 1980, § 2-57; Ord. No. 1208 , § 1, 4-20-2023)
Sec. 2-179. - Same—Appointment and removal of members.
The volunteer members of the fire department shall be appointed in a manner prescribed by the chief and chief administrative officer, and such appointment shall be terminated by the chief and the chief administrative officer.
(Code 1980, § 2-58; Ord. No. 1208 , § 1, 4-20-2023)
Sec. 2-180. - Same—Autonomy; qualifications.
The volunteer body shall be recognized by the city as self-regulating and financially autonomous. The persons approved as volunteer members of the fire department by the chief shall be citizens residing within the city or within the area designated by the chief, preferably property owners whose business activities are normally within the confines of the city and who have telephones in their homes. Determination of whether a candidate for appointment is acceptable shall be made by the chief.
(Code 1980, § 2-59; Ord. No. 1200 , 9-15-2022; Ord. No. 1208 , § 1, 4-20-2023)
Sec. 2-181. - Same—Cause for suspension or discharge.

(Code 1980, § 2-60; Ord. No. 1208 , § 1, 4-20-2023)
Sec. 2-182. - Insurance.
The council shall authorize the chief administrative officer to secure personal, property and public liability and workers compensation insurance for each paid or volunteer member of the fire department, which shall be paid from the general fund of the city. The amount and type of insurance secured for members of the department shall have the approval of the council.
(Code 1980, § 2-61; Ord. No. 1208 , § 1, 4-20-2023)
Sec. 2-183. - Badge.

(Code 1980, § 2-62; Ord. No. 1208 , § 1, 4-20-2023)
Sec. 2-184. - Police powers.

(Code 1980, § 2-63; Ord. No. 1208 , § 1, 4-20-2023)
