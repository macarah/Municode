Sec. 2-469. - Open records.
All municipal records, except those that, by order of a state court or by law, are prohibited from being open to public inspection, shall be open for personal inspection by any state during the hours of operation of the administrative service herein prescribed.
(Code 2011, § 2-480)
Sec. 2-470. - Fees for records and other administrative services.
Fees for photocopies and other media provided to and facsimile transmissions sent or received by city personnel on behalf of and at the request of city residents, as well as fees for other city administrative services, shall be as provided in the city fee schedule.
(Code 2011, § 2-481)
Sec. 2-471. - Records management plan adopted by state law; coordinator; retention schedule.
The city council adopts all applicable state law regarding municipal records management plans and records retention. The city clerk shall be the coordinator of the city's records management plan and authorized to act for and on behalf of the city council in directing and coordinating all records management matters under the Georgia Records Act, O.C.G.A. § 50-18-90 et seq., and records retention schedules approved by the state. The same records shall be maintained in accordance with approved retention schedules. No record shall be destroyed except as authorized by the state records retention schedule.
(Code 2011, § 2-482)
