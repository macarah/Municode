Sec. 4-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
License means the authorization by the county to engage in the sale of malt beverages and wine. Licenses shall permit the sale of beer, wine, and malt beverages only and shall not permit the sale of other spirits, liquors, or fortified wines.
License for sale means the retail sale of malt beverages and wine packaged to go and not to be consumed on the premises where sold.
License for sale and consumption means the retail sale of malt beverages and wine to be consumed on the definite closed or partitioned premises.
Malt beverages means fermented beverages made in whole or in part from malt, or any similar fermented beverages. However, no such malt beverages shall be sold under the provisions of this section which contain more than six percent of alcohol by volume.
Wine means all alcoholic beverages containing not more than 21 percent of alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes, either by natural fermentation or natural fermentation with brandy added, including all sparkling wines, champagne, combinations of those beverages, vermouth, special natural wines, rectified wines and like products. The term "wine" shall not include cooking wines and like products which are mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render them unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at the point of the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this subsection.
Sec. 4-2. - License required.
(a)
Every person, firm or corporation engaged in the sale of malt beverages and wine, within the unincorporated areas of the county, for consumption on or off the premises, shall be required to obtain the appropriate license or licenses before business may be commenced. This license shall be issued on an annual basis set forth in this chapter.
(b)
Licenses shall be issued on an annual basis beginning January 1 of each year. However, any and all fees for continuation from year to year must be paid on or before December 15 for the upcoming year. Every licensee shall be required to display this license in a prominent place. He shall be required to obtain a license for each place of business within the county. For a new applicant all fees must be paid prior to application being issued.
(c)
A license is not transferable to any person, firm or corporation.
(d)
The business premises of the holder of a license issued under this chapter shall be open to inspection at any time during business hours by any member of the sheriff's department, by the code enforcement officer, by the county administrator/clerk, and by the board of commissioners or any county employee designated by them.
(e)
No person engaged in the sale of alcoholic beverages shall sell, serve or permit the sale of malt beverages and wine to any person under 21 years of age.

Sec. 4-4. - Qualifications for license.
(a)
Any applicant for a malt beverage and wine license must show, to the satisfaction of the board of commissioners:
(1)
Financial responsibility.
(2)
Good character, of the applicant and all managers, clerks or other employees. Corporation applicants shall be of good business reputation.
(3)
No license shall be granted to an applicant who has been convicted under any federal, state or local law for a criminal offense involving moral turpitude, or who has been convicted under any federal, state, or local law of any criminal offense involving alcoholic beverages, gambling, or tax violations, if such conviction tends to indicate that the applicant would not maintain the operation for which he is seeking a license in conformity with federal, state, or local law.
(b)
No location for a malt beverage and wine license shall be approved when such location is within 100 yards of a church, school or college. The distance shall be measured in a straight line from the nearest property line of the school or college to the nearest wall of the building of the proposed license location. Licensees who hold a valid license at the time a school, college, or church locates within the prohibited distance of the licensee shall be eligible for continuance of and renewal of that license notwithstanding this provision.
Sec. 4-5. - Application required; investigation and report; recommendation; action by board of commissioners.
modified
(a)
Persons desiring to sell malt beverages and wine shall file an application for a new license with the county board of commissioners office, and shall provide any documentation required by the county, including immigration compliance documents.
(b)
All approved applications for new licenses under this article shall be accompanied by a payment in cash or certified check for all the minimum fees and business taxes provided for in this Code for the classification into which the applicant falls. A schedule of license fees is on file at the county board of commissioners office and will be made available to all applicants. The annual license fees may be updated from time to time by the county board of commissioners. The period of time during which the license is in effect will be the calendar year between January 1 and December 31, after which time it must be renewed.
(c)
Any new applicant requesting a license for less than one year will be required to pay a prorated amount for the portion of the year remaining. In subsequent years, the applicant will be required to pay the full license fee.
(d)
Attached to the county application shall be a photostatic copy of all appropriate state applications and forms, along with the following:
(1)
A legal description of the property upon which the premises are located;
(2)
A current identification document that includes a photograph of the applicant;
(3)
Fingerprints of the applicant, to be taken by the county sheriffs office;
(4)
Completed background check of the applicant, to be performed by the county sheriffs office;
(5)
An affidavit from the county sheriffs office verifying that the establishment complies with the distance requirement contained in this chapter;
(6)
A cashier's check, or cash, in the appropriate amount for costs of the investigative portion of the application process.
(e)
Upon a favorable report from the investigative portion of the application procedure, a notice shall be advertised in the official gazette of the county for two weeks immediately preceding the next available meeting of the county board of commissioners. The applicant will remit payment to the county board of commissioners office for the cost of the notice and administrative expenses.
(f)
The county board of commissioners shall vote on the application at the next available meeting. Should the license application be denied, a written notice of setting forth the reasons for denial shall be mailed to the applicant. The applicant shall have the right to a full hearing before the board of commissioners, with an opportunity to present evidence and to cross-examine opposing witnesses. The right to a hearing must be exercised by the applicant, in writing, within five business days of the denial.
(g)
Any false statement or material misrepresentation in any application pursuant to this section shall be grounds for the revocation of any license granted pursuant to this section.

Sec. 4-7. - List of managers to be filed with sheriff.
All persons licensed hereunder shall file with the sheriff a list of the names of all managers of the licensed business, with home addresses, within three days of the effective date of the license. Changes in the managers with the names of new managers, shall be filed with the sheriff within three days of any such notice.
Sec. 4-8. - Compliance with state law.
The applicant for the license for the sale of malt beverages and wine shall and will always comply with each and every provisions of state law as well as any local laws and codes.
Sec. 4-9. - Required closure of coolers and lockers.
Licensees holding a license for the sale of malt beverages shall lock their coolers and lockers at 12:00 midnight. They shall not open for the sale of said malt beverages and wine again until 6:00 a.m. the following day.
(Ord. No. WC-49, § 1, 2-10-1983)
Sec. 4-10. - Brown-bagging restricted.
Licensees shall allow no brown-bagging and no malt beverages, wine or liquor shall be brought into said establishment during or after the operating hours.
(Ord. No. WC-49, § 2, 2-10-1983)
Sec. 4-11. - Licensed establishment—Hours of operation.
Establishments holding a license for the sale of malt beverages and wine must stop the sale of malt beverages and wine at 12:00 midnight on all nights that they are open for business. Said establishments may remain open until 1:00 a.m. All customers must be gone from premises no later than 1:30 a.m.
(Ord. No. WC-49, § 3, 2-10-1983)
Sec. 4-12. - Same—Parking lot conduct restrictions.
There shall be no drinking or loitering in parking lots of said establishments.
(Ord. No. WC-49, § 4, 2-10-1983)
Sec. 4-13. - Sunday Sales.
No licensee holding a license for the sale of malt beverages and wine shall sell alcohol on Sundays.
(Ord. No. WC-49, § 5, 2-10-1983)
Sec. 4-14. - Prohibited sale to minors.
Alcoholic beverages shall not be sold to minors. Further, minors shall not be permitted on or in the premises of any establishment holding a license for the sale of malt beverages and wine with consumption on the premises which exclusively sells alcohol and does not sell other consumables including food. The term "minor" means a person under the legal age for purchase of alcoholic beverages at the time of the enactment of the ordinance from which this chapter is derived; said age being 21 in state law.
(Ord. No. WC-49, § 6, 2-10-1983)
Sec. 4-15. - Prohibited attire, entertainment and conduct.
(a)
The employment or use of any person, in any capacity, in the sale or service of alcoholic beverages while such person is unclothed or in such attire, costume or clothing as to expose to view any portion of the female breast below the top of areola or of any portion of the male or female pubic hair, anus, cleft of the buttocks, vulva or genitals.
(b)
Live entertainment where any person appears in the manner described in subsection (a) of this section or where such person performs acts of, or acts which simulate any of the following:
(1)
Sexual intercourse, masturbation, sodomy, bestiality, oral copulation, flagellation of any sexual act which is prohibited by law.
(2)
The caressing or fondling of the breasts, buttocks, anus or genitals.
(3)
The displaying of the male or female pubic hair, anus, vulva or genitals.
(c)
The holding, promotion, sponsoring or allowance of any contest, promotion, special night, event or any other activity where patrons of the licensed establishment are encouraged or allowed to engage in any of the conduct described in subsections (a) or (b) of this section, provided, however, that nothing contained in this section shall apply to the premises of any mainstream performance house, museum or theater which derives less than 20 percent of its gross annual from the sale of alcohol beverages.
(d)
Violation of any provision of this amendment shall constitute an offense under this article with maximum punishment which shall not exceed a fine of $1,000.00 or imprisonment for 60 days or both.
(e)
Jurisdiction over violations of this amendment shall be in the county magistrate court, and procedures for enforcement shall be as provided in O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60.
(Ord. of 9-9-1999)
