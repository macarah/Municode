Sec. 30-1. - Magistrate court's jurisdiction; citations; penalty.
Violations of ordinances and resolutions of the county shall be tried in the magistrate court of the county upon citations issued by the county or by any agent from time to time designated by the county to issue citations on its behalf for ordinance and resolution violations.
(Res. No. 97-3, § 1, 4-15-1997)
Sec. 30-2. - Truancy.
The county commissioners and county board of education hereby authorize the magistrate court of the county to handle truant cases and levy fines against parents who keep their children out of school without valid reason.
(Res. No. 94-2, 1-12-1994)
Sec. 30-3. - Agents for the county for issuance of citations.
(a)
The county marshal, sheriff, sheriffs deputy, zoning administrative officer, and building inspection officer for the county are hereby designated as agents of the county for the purpose of issuing citations for the violation of any ordinance or resolution of the county, except as set forth in [subsection] (b) below; and
(b)
The county fire chief, county deputy fire chief, county fire marshal, or deputy county fire marshal, are hereby designated as agents of the county for the purpose of issuing citations for the violation of any ordinance or resolution of the county relating to fire or fire safety or burning or fire prevention and protection.
(Res. No. 97-3, § 2, 4-15-1997; Res. No. 2014-05 , § 1, 4-8-2014)
Sec. 30-4. - Penalty.
Violations of county ordinances and resolutions may be punished as provided in section 1-11.
(Res. No. 97-3, § 3, 4-15-1997)
