Sec. 42-31. - Persistent and excessive noises.
(a)
Purpose. This section is enacted to protect, preserve and promote the health, safety and welfare of the citizens of the City of Valdosta, Georgia through the control of noise. It is the intent of this article to establish standards that will reduce excessive community noises, which are harmful and otherwise detrimental to individuals, businesses and the community as a whole through loss of enjoyment of life and property and disruption to the conduct of business. It is further the purpose to realize that acceptable noise standards vary throughout the city and are impacted by the type of primary use in those areas.
(b)
Definitions.
Plainly audible shall mean any sound emanating from the specific sound-producing source which can be can be heard by the auditory senses of a person at a distance no less than the required minimum distance from the specific sound-producing source.
Residential dwelling unit shall mean buildings or portions thereof such as homes, townhouses, mobile homes, duplexes, condominiums, apartments, hotel and motel rooms, bed and breakfast rooms and other similar structures which contain habitable rooms for occupancy and which are designed primarily for living, sleeping, cooking or eating therein.
Outdoor entertainment shall mean any live act, including but not limited to, vocalists, actors, dancers, floor shows, instrumentalists, and reproduced music played by disc jockeys occurring outside the main structure housing the business.
Zoning districts shall mean the applicable zoning district reflected by the official zoning map of the City of Valdosta, as amended.
(c)
Sound measurement standards. For the purposes of this section "plainly audible" shall mean any sound emanating from the specific sound-producing source which can be heard by the auditory senses of a person at a distance no less than the required minimum distance from the specific sound-producing source. If the sound-producing source is located on private property, the distance shall be measured from the applicable property line from which the sounds are emitting. If the sound-producing source is located on public property, the distance shall be measured from the sound-producing source itself. For music and other noise, words and phrases need not be discernible and bass reverberations are included.
(d)
General prohibited conduct.
(1)
Unless otherwise provided herein, no person, business, company or entity shall produce, create or amplify any noise or sound, either vocally or by any device, instrument, equipment or machine, any sound or noise that is plainly audible at a distance greater than those specified in subsection (e) below.
(2)
Unless otherwise provided herein no person, business, company or entity shall allow their property, or property under their control, to be used by others to produce, create or amplify any noise or sound, either vocally or by any device, instrument, equipment or machine, any sound or noise that is plainly audible at a distance greater than those specified in subsection (e) below.
(e)

  (f)
Multi-family dwellings, apartments, condominiums, townhouses, duplexes, hotels, motels or other similar residential dwelling units. In addition to the general prohibition stated in subsections (d) and (e), it also shall be unlawful for any person to produce, create or amplify any noise or sound in such a manner as to be plainly audible to any other person at a distance of five feet beyond the adjoining party wall or structural boundary between any residential dwelling units with adjoining points of contact. When a residential dwellings unit has an adjoining property line wall, party wall or structural boundary that adjoins another structure that is not considered a residential dwelling unit, this subsection shall not apply.
(g)
Downtown Commercial (C-D) zoning district. In addition the foregoing, the following activity is prohibited in C-D zoning:
(1)
Outdoor entertainment on the premises of private businesses shall only be allowed to occur between the hours of 11:00 a.m. to 11:00 p.m. on Fridays and Saturdays, and 12:00 noon to 10:00 p.m. on Sunday, Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday and Thursday.
(2)
No individuals shall play music in any automobile on public or private parking lots accessible to the public which is plainly audible for more than 100 feet.
(3)
Businesses allowing music shall be required to keep all doors closed during said times while music is playing except for the ingress and egress of patrons. Doors shall not be propped open or be allowed to remain open.
(h)
Exclusions. The prohibitions of this section shall not apply to the following:
(1)
Noises or sounds made by domestic animals;
(2)
Sound volumes produced by radio, tape player, or other mechanical sound-making device or instrument from within a motor vehicle on a public street or highway, which sound is controlled by the O.C.G.A. § 40-6-14;
(3)
Noises or sounds made by law enforcement and other public safety officials performing their public functions;
(4)
Trains, trucks, aircraft, construction equipment, and site development or maintenance activities operating during approved hours;
(5)
Organized school and college sporting events or practice including marching bands;
(6)
Burglar alarms;
(7)
Church chimes or church bells; or
(8)
Outdoor concerts, special events or festivals permitted by the office of the city manager.
(Ord. No. 2005-72, 11-21-2005; Ord. No. 2014-12, 7-24-2014)
Sec. 42-32. - Enumeration.
The acts contained in this article, among others, shall constitute a violation of this article; but such enumeration shall not be deemed to be exclusive.
Sec. 42-33. - Horns; signaling devices.
The following shall be a violation of this article:
(1)
The sounding of any horn or signaling device on any automobile, motorcycle or other vehicle on any street or public place in the city except as a danger warning.
(2)
The creation of any unreasonably loud or harsh sound by means of any such signaling device and the sounding of any such device for an unreasonable period of time.
(3)
The use of any signaling device, except a police whistle or one operated by hand or electricity.
(4)
The use of any horn, whistle or other device operated by engine exhaust.
(5)
The use of any signaling device when traffic is held up for any reason.
Sec. 42-34. - Radios, phonographs, other machines or devices that produce or reproduce sound.
The using, operating or permitting to be played, used or operated of any radio receiving set, musical instrument, phonograph or other machine or device for the producing or reproducing of sound in such manner as to disturb the peace, quiet and comfort of the neighboring inhabitants, or at any time with louder volume than is necessary for convenient hearing for the person who is in the room, vehicle or chamber in which such machine or device is operated and who is a voluntary listener shall be a violation of this article. The operation of any such set, instrument, phonograph, machine or device between the hours of 9:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m. in such a manner as to be plainly audible at a distance of 50 feet from the building, structure or vehicle in which it is located shall be prima facie evidence of a violation of this section.
(Code 1976, § 11-2010)
Sec. 42-35. - Loudspeakers; amplifiers; other devices for production or reproduction of sound for advertising.
The using, operating or permitting to be played, used or operated of any radio receiving set, musical instrument, phonograph, loudspeaker, sound amplifier or other machine or device for the producing or reproducing of sound which is cast upon the public streets for the purpose of commercial advertising or attracting the attention of the public to any building or structure without a special permit from the city clerk or city council shall be a violation of this article. Announcements over loudspeakers can only be made by the announcer, in person, and without the aid of any mechanical device.
(Code 1976, §§ 11-2008, 11-2009)
Sec. 42-36. - Yelling; shouting; hooting; whistling; singing.
Yelling, shouting, hooting, whistling or singing on the public streets, particularly between the hours of 9:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m. or at any time or place so as to annoy or disturb the quiet, comfort or repose of any persons in the vicinity shall be a violation of this article.
Sec. 42-37. - Animals and birds.
The keeping of any animal or bird that will disturb the comfort or repose of any persons in the vicinity by making long, continual or frequent noise shall be a violation of this article.
Cross reference— Animals, ch. 14.
Sec. 42-38. - Steam whistle.
The blowing of any train whistle or steam whistle attached to any stationary boiler except to give notice of the time to begin or stop work or as a warning of fire or danger or upon request of proper city authorities shall be a violation of this article.
Sec. 42-39. - Exhausts.
The discharge into the open air of the exhaust of any steam engine, stationary internal combustion engine, motorboat or motor vehicle except through a muffler or other device that will effectively prevent loud or explosive noises shall be a violation of this article.
Sec. 42-40. - Defect in vehicle or load.
The use of any automobile, motorcycle or vehicle so out of repair, so loaded or in such manner as to create loud and unnecessary grating, grinding, rattling or other noise shall be a violation of this article.
Cross reference— Traffic and vehicles, ch. 94.
Sec. 42-41. - Loading; unloading; opening boxes.
The creation of a loud and excessive noise in connection with loading or unloading any vehicle or the opening and destruction of bales, boxes, crates and containers shall be a violation of this article.
Sec. 42-42. - Construction or repairing of buildings.
The erection, including excavation, demolition, alteration or repair, of any building other than between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and sunset on weekdays, except on urgent necessity in the interest of public health and safety, and then only with a permit from the city building official, shall be a violation of this article. Such permit may be granted for a period not to exceed three days or less while the emergency continues and may be renewed by the building inspecting department for periods of three days or less while the emergency continues. If the building inspector determines that the public health and safety will not be impaired by the erection, demolition, alteration or repair of any building or the excavation of streets and highways from sunset to 7:00 a.m., and further determines that loss or inconvenience would result to any party in interest, the building inspector may grant permission for such work to be done from sunset to 7:00 a.m., upon application made at the time the permit for the work is applied for or during the progress of the work.
Cross reference— Buildings and building regulations, ch. 18.
Sec. 42-43. - Schools; courts; churches; hospitals.
The creation of any excessive noise on any street adjacent to any school, institution of learning, church or court while they are in session or adjacent to any hospital, and which unreasonably interferes with the work of such institution, or which disturbs or unduly annoys patients in the hospital, provided that conspicuous signs are displayed about such institutions indicating the presence of such institutions, shall be a violation of this article.
(Code 1976, § 11-2007)
Sec. 42-44. - Hawkers; peddlers; vendors.
The shouting and crying of hawkers, peddlers and vendors which disturb the peace and quiet of the neighborhood shall be a violation of this article.
Cross reference— Peddlers and solicitors, § 22-191 et seq.
Sec. 42-45. - Noises to attract attention.
The use of any drum or other instrument or device for the purpose of attracting attention to any performance, show or sale by creation of noise shall be a violation of this article.
Sec. 42-46. - Transportation of metal rails, pillars or columns.
The transportation of rails, pillars or columns of iron, steel or other material over and along streets and other public places upon carts, drays, cars, trucks or in any other manner so loaded as to cause loud noises or as to disturb the peace and quiet of such streets or other public places shall be a violation of this article.
Cross reference— Traffic and vehicles, ch. 94.
Sec. 42-47. - Piledrivers, hammers, other such appliances.
The operation from 6:00 p.m. to 7:00 a.m. of any piledriver, steam shovel, pneumatic hammer, derrick, steam or electric hoist or other appliance the use of which is attended by loud or unusual noise without a special permit from the building inspector shall be a violation of this article.
Sec. 42-48. - Blowers.
The operation of any noise-creating blower or power fan or any internal combustion engine, the operation of which causes noise due to the explosion of operating gases of fluids, unless the noise from such blower or fan is muffled and such engine is equipped with a muffler device sufficient to deaden such noise, shall be a violation of this article.
Sec. 42-49. - Sound trucks.
The use of mechanical loudspeakers or amplifiers on trucks or other moving or standing vehicles for advertising or other commercial purposes and the use of sound trucks for noncommercial purposes during such hours or in such places or with such volume as would constitute such use a public nuisance shall be a violation of this article.
Cross reference— Traffic and vehicles, ch. 94.
