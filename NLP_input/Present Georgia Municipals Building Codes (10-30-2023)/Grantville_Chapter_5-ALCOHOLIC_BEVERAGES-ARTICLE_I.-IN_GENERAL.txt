ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 5-1. - Election day sales.
In accordance with the power and authority granted to the governing bodies of municipalities in the state by the Constitution of the state, and pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20, the sale of alcoholic beverages shall be allowed on all election days pursuant to the regulation and prohibitions placed on the sale of alcoholic beverages by the statutes of the state and ordinances of this city currently governing such sales on other days of the year.
(Prior Code, § 5.32.010; Ord. No. 589, 9-10-1990)
Sec. 5-2. - Reserved.
Editor's note— Ord. No. 2023-02 , § 1, adopted February 27, 2023, repealed § 5-2, which pertained to possession of alcholic beverages on city property and derived from Ord. No. 520, adopted March 9, 1976.
Sec. 5-3. - Storage of alcoholic beverages by a public business.
It shall be unlawful for the proprietor of any business open to the public to allow any beer, wine, or distilled spirits to be kept or stored upon the premises of the business; provided, however, that any business which is duly licensed to sell beer and wine may store same on premises.
(Ord. No. 523)
Sec. 5-4. - Minimum drinking age.
(a)
No person knowingly, directly or through another person, shall furnish, cause to be furnished, or permit any person in such person's employ to furnish any alcoholic beverage to any person under 21 years of age.
(b)
No person under 21 years of age shall purchase or knowingly possess any alcoholic beverage.
(c)
No person under 21 years of age shall misrepresent such person's age in any manner whatever for the purpose of obtaining illegally any alcoholic beverage.
(d)
No person knowingly or intentionally shall act as an agent to purchase or acquire any alcoholic beverage for or on behalf of a person under 21 years of age.
(e)
No person under 21 years of age shall misrepresent his identity or use any false identification for the purpose of purchasing or obtaining any alcoholic beverage.
(f)
Any provision of any city ordinance dealing with licensing, sale, possession, or use of alcoholic beverages which establishes a minimum age for sale, use, possession, and/or consumption of alcoholic beverages is hereby amended to comply herewith.
(Ord. No. 572, §§ I—VI, 1986)
Sec. 5-5. - Home delivery of alcohol.
(a)
For all purposes of this section, all terms defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-10 shall have the same meaning when used herein.
(b)
The delivery of any alcoholic beverages by any packaged goods retailer or licensed retail package liquor store is prohibited.
( Ord. No. 2020-06 , § 1, 12-21-2020)
