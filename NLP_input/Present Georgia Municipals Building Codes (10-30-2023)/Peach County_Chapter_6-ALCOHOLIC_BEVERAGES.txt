ARTICLE II. - SALE AND DISTRIBUTION OF MALT BEVERAGES AND WINE[2]

Sec. 6-20. - Application.
The following rules, requirements, licenses and standards shall be effective from September 12, 1995, and shall remain in effect until amended and/or modified thereafter.
(Ord. of 9-12-95)
Sec. 6-21. - Definitions.
As used in this article:
Governing authority means the Board of Commissioners of Peach County.
Licensee means the person whose name appears upon a retail dealer's license and shall include the business entity operating pursuant thereto.
Licensed premises means the business location of a licensed retail dealer existing on a single contiguous tract of real estate and operating under one name.
Malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than six percent alcohol by volume, and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, malt beer, and strong beer, but not including sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Package means a bottle, can, keg, barrel, or other original consumer container.
Person means any individual, firm, partnership, cooperative, non-profit membership corporation, joint venture, association, company, corporation, agency, syndicate, estate, trust, business trust, receiver, fiduciary, or other group or combination acting as a unit, body politic, or political subdivision, whether public, private, or quasi-public.
Retail dealer means any person who sells malt beverages or wine, either in unbroken packages or for consumption on the premises, at retail only to consumers and not for resale.
Retail dealer's license or license means a license to sell malt beverages or wine at retail either in unbroken packages or for consumption on the premises, issued by the governing authority pursuant to and subject to the terms of this article.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "wine" does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 1; Mo. of 11-14-95; Ord. of 9-10-96)
State Law reference— Similiar provisions, O.G.C.A. 3-1-2.
Sec. 6-22. - Construction of definitions.
As used in this article, defined words shall have the meanings specified unless the context in which the word or term is used clearly requires that a different meaning be used.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 2)
Sec. 6-23. - Retail dealer licenses; applications; notice; fingerprints; bonds; restrictions; standards; hearing.
(a)
No malt beverages or wine shall be sold in Peach County except as authorized by a retail dealer's license issued pursuant to this article. Licenses issued hereunder shall authorize the sale of malt beverages and wine, as defined herein, only. Separate licenses are required for the sale of malt beverages and for the sale of wine.
(b)
Application for a retail dealer's license shall be made on forms furnished by the governing authority. A notice of each application shall be advertised in the official gazette of Peach County, Georgia once a week for two weeks immediately preceding the meeting of the governing authority in which the application is to be acted upon.
(c)
Each applicant shall furnish a complete set of fingerprints which shall be forwarded to the Peach County Sheriff's Department who shall search the files of the Georgia Crime Information Center for a period of five years immediately preceding the date of such application for any instance of criminal activity. The Peach County Sheriff's Department shall also submit such fingerprints to the Federal Bureau of Investigation under rules established by the United States Department of Justice for processing and identification records. The federal record, if any, shall be obtained and returned to the governing authority considering such application.
Where it appears to the governing authority that an application is complete except for the F.B.I. fingerprint report, and that the applicant otherwise qualifies for a retail dealer's license, the governing authority may grant the license contingent on receiving a satisfactory F.B.I. report. Should the report provide grounds for denying the license, the license may be revoked immediately as though the application had been denied, and the licensee shall have the right to a hearing.
(d)
Along with their application, applicants must post with the governing authority a personal performance bond, said bond to be conditioned upon the faithful observance and performance by the licensee of the rules and regulations contained in this article. Upon the violation of this article, or any part thereof, the amount of the bond to be forfeited will be determined by the governing authority in accordance with the nature of the violation after a hearing is held by the said governing authority. Written notice of such hearing shall be given to the alleged violator not less than 15 days prior thereto. The determination by the governing authority to forfeit such bond may be in addition to any action taken by the governing authority under section 6-40. Said bond shall be payable to Peach County, Georgia in the amount of $1,000.00, said bond to be approved by the governing authority and shall be executed in the form specified by the governing authority. However, nothing herein shall preclude the governing authority from waiving said bond requirement whenever a licensee has established sufficient financial responsibility and/or a record of faithful compliance with the laws and regulations of the United States of America, the State of Georgia and the County of Peach.
(e)
No retail dealer's license shall be granted to any person or corporation unless such person is of legal age and such person or corporation is a resident of Peach County, Georgia or has its principal place of business located in peach County, Georgia.
(f)
No employee of Peach County, Georgia shall be eligible to receive a retail dealer's license.
(g)
The following standards shall be applied to all decisions pertaining to the issuance or denial of licenses hereunder:
(1)
All applicants for a retail dealer's license must show financial responsibility. The governing authority may require all applicants to provide financial statements and other evidence of financial responsibility in conjunction with the application.
(2)
All applicants for a retail dealer's license must be of good character, and all operators, managers, clerks, or other employees shall be of like character. Corporate or firm applicants shall be of good business reputation.
(3)
No license shall be granted to an applicant who has been convicted under any federal, state, or local law for a criminal offense involving alcoholic beverages, gambling, or tax law violations if such conviction tends to indicate that the applicant would not maintain the operation for which a license is being sought in conformity with Federal, State or the laws of Peach County, Georgia. Furthermore, no license shall be granted if any operators, managers, clerks, or other employees employed by the applicant has been convicted under any Federal, State, or local law for a criminal offense involving alcoholic beverages, gambling, or tax law violations if such conviction tends to indicate that the applicant would not maintain the operation for which a license is being sought in conformity with Federal, State or the laws of Peach County, Georgia.
(4)
A license application may be denied to any applicant for a retail dealer's license where it appears that the applicant would not have adequate financial participation in the proposed business to direct and manage its affairs, or where it appears that the applicant is intended to be a mere surrogate for a person or persons who would not otherwise qualify for a license for any reason whatsoever.
(5)
The governing authority, in their discretion, may consider any extenuating circumstances which may reflect favorably or unfavorably on the applicant, application or the proposed location of the business. If, in the governing authority's sole judgment, circumstances are such that the granting of the license would not be in the best interest of the general public, such circumstances may be grounds for denying the application.
(h)
All decisions of the governing authority either approving or denying a license application shall be stated in writing and a copy of such statement shall be provided to the applicant either personally or by certified mail within ten days of the decision.
(i)
Whenever the governing authority shall deny an application for a retail dealer's license, the applicant shall have 15 days following notification of denial to request a hearing before the governing authority. The applicant shall be entitled to present evidence and cross-examine opposing witnesses at such hearing either with or without legal counsel.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 3; Ord. of 4-6-96; Ord. of 9-10-96; Mo. of 11-6-96; Ord. of 8-12-14)
Sec. 6-24. - Premises.
No retail dealer's license shall be issued to any person unless the building in which the business will be located is complete and detailed plans of said building and outside premises are attached to the application, or unless proposed plans and specifications and a building permit for a building to be built or renovated are attached to the application. The completed building or the proposed building shall comply with ordinances of Peach County, Georgia, regulations of the State Revenue Commissioner and the laws of the State of Georgia. The proposed building shall also be subject to final inspection and approval when completed by the Building Inspector for Peach County. Each such building shall contain sufficient lighting so that the building itself and the premises on all sides of the building are readily visible at all times from the street on which the building is located so as to reveal the inside retail area of the building and so as to reveal all of the outside premises of said building. Each applicant for a retail dealer's license shall attach to his application evidence of ownership of the building or proposed building or a copy of the lease if the applicant is leasing the building. A separate retail dealer's license shall be required for each place of business.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 4)
Sec. 6-25. - Distance requirements.
No retail dealer's license shall be granted to any person unless the entire business premises of the proposed location, including parking lot, is situated not less than 100 yards from any church building, not less than 200 yards from the property line of any school ground or college campus, and not less than 100 yards from any building used as an alcohol rehabilitation center, the same to be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground. Each application for a retail dealer's license shall include a scale drawing of the location of the proposed premises showing the distance to the nearest church building, school ground or campus, or alcohol rehabilitation center, or a certificate of a registered surveyor that such location complies with this article with reference to this section.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 5)
Sec. 6-26. - Term of license; renewal.
A retail dealer's license shall be valid only for the 12-month period from the date of issuance and no license may be renewed except upon approval of the governing authority. A licensee who desires to continue to operate during a subsequent year must apply for renewal prior to the expiration of his or her current license. An application for renewal is subject to the requirements of §§ 6-44—6-46.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 6; Ord. of 9-10-96)
Sec. 6-27. - License fees.
The fee for each retail dealer's license shall be (as established by the county commission) per annum and shall be paid prior to the issuance of such license, said fee to accompany the application, and shall be either a bank certified check or money order.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 7; Mo. of 11-6-96; Ord. of 8-12-14)
Sec. 6-28. - Place of sale or delivery of goods.
(a)
No licensee shall sell or deliver any malt beverages or wine to any person except in the licensed premises except as exempted under paragraph (b) of this section. A licensee shall be permitted to load only unopened packaged malt beverages and wine into a customer's vehicle when the sale physically takes place and monies have been exchanged inside the licensed premises. A licensee hereunder shall be permitted to have a drive-in window and it shall be permissible for the licensee to deliver unopened packaged malt beverages and wines through such window. "Curb service" type sales and sales to customers in vehicles parked in the parking area of licensed premises are prohibited. All other sales shall be for consumption on the licenses premises only.
(b)
Open area sales and outdoor special events.
(1)
Malt beverage and wine sales can be made by a licensed consumption on-premises establishment in an open area type environment or by a licensed caterer with a catering off-premises license at an outdoor special event if the establishment or event has been approved by the county marshal.
(2)
The open area must be enclosed by some structure providing for public ingress/egress only through the main licensed premises.
a.
The height of such structure shall be a minimum of four feet above the floor or ground. It must be approved by the county building inspector and the county fire department as required by governing regulations and codes.
b.
The only exit from the area is to be through the licensed establishment's main premises and through an approved fire exit.
(3)
An outdoor special event must be enclosed by some structure or fencing providing for public ingress/egress only through designated entrances and exits.
a.
The outdoor special event must be approved by the county marshal and the Peach County Sheriff on crowd control, traffic, and security measures.
b.
The event location must be properly zoned and approved by the Peach County Zoning Department.
(c)
Promotion and sales.
(1)
No licensee or employee or agent of a licensee, in connection with the sale or other disposition of malt beverages and wine for consumption on the premises shall:
a.
Offer or deliver any free malt beverage or wine to any person or group of persons; however, nothing herein shall prohibit a brewery, winery, or distillery from distributing free samples in accordance with state law provisions;
b.
Deliver more than one malt beverage or wine to one person at a time; however, nothing herein shall prohibit a brew pub from offering a sampler of malt beverages in containers not exceeding four ounces. Each sampler shall not exceed four different types of beverages.
c.
The county marshal and the sheriff shall have responsibility for the enforcement of this article.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 8; Ord. of 9-10-96; Ord. of 8-12-14)
Sec. 6-29. - Hours of operation.
No person, knowingly and intentionally, shall sell or offer to sell malt beverages and wine on:
(1)
Sundays except between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m.; or
(2)
Election days. Pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20, the sale of distilled spirits for consumption on the premises shall be permitted on election days as the same are defined in the Official Code of Georgia Annotated; provided, however, that it shall nonetheless be unlawful for any person to sell distilled spirits for consumption on the premises within 250 feet of any polling place or of the outer edge of any building within which such polling place is established on primary or election days.
(3)
Christmas Day.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 9; Mo. of 11-14-95; Ord. of 9-10-96; Mo. of 12-10-96; Ord. of 8-12-14)
Sec. 6-30. - Minimum age of consumption; exceptions.
(a)
No retail dealer or employee of a retail dealer shall knowingly furnish, sell or offer for sale any malt beverages or wine to a person prohibited to purchase malt beverages or wine under state law. This prohibition shall not apply with respect to the sale of malt beverages or wine to a person when such person has furnished proper identification showing that the person to whom the malt beverages or wine are being sold is 21 years of age or older.
(b)
The above prohibition shall not apply with respect to the following:
(1)
Whenever malt beverages or wine are purchased for medical purposes pursuant to a prescription of a physician duly authorized to practice medicine in this state;
(2)
Whenever malt beverages or wine are purchased for consumption at a religious ceremony; or
(3)
Whenever malt beverages or wine are possessed for consumption by a person under 21 years of age when the parent or guardian of the person under 21 years of age gives the alcoholic beverage to the person and when possession is in the home of the parent or guardian and such parent or guardian is present.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 10; Mo. of 11-14-95; Ord. of 8-12-14)
Sec. 6-31. - Employment of minors prohibited.
No licensee shall employ, require, or permit a person under 21 years of age to sell or take orders for any malt beverage or wine.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 11; Ord. of 9-10-96)
Sec. 6-32. - Furnishing malt beverages or wine to certain persons prohibited.
No licensee shall furnish, sell, or offer to sell any malt beverages or wine to any person who the licensee knows or reasonably should know is intoxicated, or is of unsound mind, or is a habitual drunkard.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 12; Ord. of 9-10-96)
Sec. 6-33. - Retail dealers to buy only from licensed wholesalers.
No licensee shall buy or arrange to buy or in any way effect the transfer of any malt beverages or wine except from a licensed wholesaler.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 13; Ord. of 9-10-96)
Sec. 6-34. - Retail dealers to store inventory only on premises.
A licensee shall keep no inventory or stock of malt beverages or wine at any place except the licensed premises, and within the licensed premises the storage area for malt beverages or wine shall be immediately adjacent to the retail area. No licensee shall enter into any type of arrangement whereby malt beverages or wine ordered by the licensee are stored by a licensed wholesaler.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 14; Ord. of 9-10-96)
Sec. 6-35. - Licenses not transferable.
No retail dealer's license issued hereunder shall be transferable or assignable to any other person or location, and in the event that a licensed business is sold or closed, it shall be the duty of the licensee to immediately surrender the license to the governing authority.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 15; Ord. of 9-10-96)
Sec. 6-36. - Display of license.
Licenses issued under this article shall be displayed prominently at all times on the premises for which same was issued.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 16; Ord. of 9-10-96)
Sec. 6-37. - Licensees to maintain a copy of the article; employees to be familiar with terms; responsibility of licensee for violations.
Each licensee shall keep a copy of this article in the licensed premises and shall instruct any person working there with respect to the terms hereof and each licensee and the licensee's agents and employees selling malt beverages or wine shall at all times be familiar with the terms hereof. The acts of any person working in the licensed premises which occur on the licensed premises and are in violation of this article or of any federal, state or local law relating to the sale, regulation or control of alcoholic beverages or to drugs or gambling shall be attributable to the licensee for the purposes of enforcement of this article.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 17; Ord. of 9-10-96)
Sec. 6-38. - Premises open for inspection.
The licensed premises shall be open to inspection at any and all times by officers or officials representing the governing authority authorized to conduct such inspections.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 18; Ord. of 9-10-96)
Sec. 6-39. - Gambling on premises prohibited.
There shall be no gambling, betting, games of chance, punch-board, slot machines, pin-ball machines, or the operation of any scheme for hazarding money or any other thing of value in any place of business licensed under this article, or in any room adjoining the same, owned, leased or controlled by a licensee. Any violation of this section shall be cause for suspension or revocation of a license.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 19)
Sec. 6-40. - Grounds for suspension or revocation of license.
Any of the following shall be grounds for suspension or revocation, as the governing authority sees fit, of a license:
(a)
The making of any statement on an application for a license issued hereunder which shall be later found to be false;
(b)
Violation of any of the terms of this article;
(c)
With respect to the licensee or any person with an ownership interest in the licensed premises, the violation of any federal, state or local law relating to the sale, control or regulation of alcoholic beverages, drugs, gambling, or taxation.
(d)
Failure to maintain adequate financial responsibility upon which issuance of the license was conditioned, or default on any obligation, of any kind whatsoever, lawfully owing to Peach County, Georgia;
(e)
Suspension or revocation of a state retail dealer's license, which shall automatically operate to suspend or revoke any license issued hereunder.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 20; Ord. of 9-10-96)
Sec. 6-40.1. - Suspension and revocation—Procedure; hearing.
Any action taken by the governing authority to suspend or revoke a license issued hereunder shall not preclude and may be in addition to any criminal prosecution by a proper authority as provided by the laws and ordinances of Peach County, the State of Georgia, or the United States. Whenever any action is taken by the governing authority to suspend or revoke any license issued hereunder, the governing authority shall provide written notice to the licensee within 10 days of such action, said notice stating the action taken and the reasons therefor. The licensee shall have 15 days following notification of such action to request a hearing before the governing authority, which hearing shall be held within 30 days of such request. The licensee shall be entitled at such hearing to present evidence and to examine opposing witnesses either with or without counsel.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 20; Ord. of 9-10-96)
Sec. 6-41. - Repeal of conflicting ordinances.
All ordinances and parts of ordinances in conflict herewith are hereby expressly repealed.
(Ord. of 9-12-95, § 21)
Sec. 6-42. - Multiple locations; separate license required.
Each license issued hereunder shall authorize the sale of malt beverages or wine at one licensed premises only. A person selling alcoholic beverages at more than one licensed premises must obtain a separate license for each location.
(Ord. of 9-10-96; Mo. of 11-6-96)
Sec. 6-43. - Operation in accordance with law.
Licensees shall operate in accordance with the terms of this article and with all applicable federal and state law related to the regulation of alcoholic beverages, including but not limited to the Georgia Alcoholic Beverage Code, O.C.G.A. 3-3-1 et seq. The term "licensee" as used in this part includes agents and employees of the licensee.
(Ord. of 9-10-96)

A license revocation becomes final (1) upon the expiration of any applicable period, as provided by this Ordinance or as allowed by law, to request a hearing or appeal with regard to the revocation decision, if no hearing or appeal is requested; or (2) by a decision of the Superior Court of Peach County which has the effect of affirming a decision of the governing authority revoking a license.

Each applicant for a retail dealer's license shall attach to his application evidence of ownership of the building or proposed building or a copy of the lease if the applicant is leasing the building. A separate retail dealer's license shall be required for each place of business.
(g)
Location. No retail dealer's license shall be granted to any person unless the entire business premises of the proposed location, including parking lot, is situated not less than 100 yards from any church building, not less than 200 yards from the property line of any school ground or college campus, not less than 100 yards from any building used as an alcohol rehabilitation center, and not less than 1,000 feet from any licenced adult entertainment establishment. Distance shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground. Each application for a retail dealer's license shall include a scale drawing of the location of the proposed premises showing the distance to the nearest church building, school ground or campus, alcohol rehabilitation center, or adult entertainment establishment, or a certificate of a registered surveyor that such location complies with this ordinance with reference to this section.

ARTICLE III. - RETAIL SALES OF DISTILLED SPIRITS FOR CONSUMPTION ON THE PREMISES

Sec. 6-50. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcohol means ethyl alcohol, hydrated oxide of ethyl or spirits of wine, from whatever source or by whatever process produced.
Alcoholic beverage means and includes all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, malt, beverage, wine or fortified wine.
Distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume, including, but not limited to, all fortified wines.
Fortified wine means any alcoholic beverage containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term includes, but is not limited to, brandy.
Governing authority means the Board of Commissioners of Peach County.
Licensee means the person whose name appears upon a retail dealer's license and shall include the business entity operating pursuant under one name.
Licensed premises means the business location of a licensed retail dealer existing on a single contiguous tract of real estate and operating under one name.
Package means a bottle, can, keg, barrel, or other original consumer container.
Person means any individual, firm, partnership, cooperative, non-profit membership corporation, joint venture, association, company, corporation, agency syndicate, estate, trust, business trust, receiver, fiduciary, or other group or combination acting as a unit, body politic, or political subdivision, whether public, private or quasi- public.
Retail dealer means any person who sells distilled spirits for consumption on the premises, at retail only to consumers and not for resale.
Retail dealer's license or license means a license to sell distilled spirits at retail for consumption on the premises, issued by the governing authority pursuant to and subject to the terms of this article.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, wines and like products. The term does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this section.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-51. - Familiarity with article.
It shall be the duty of each licensee to maintain a copy of this article on the premises, and to instruct each employee as to the terms thereof; and each licensee and employee shall at all times be familiar with this article.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-52. - Responsibility for acts of employees and others.
Licensees are charged with the responsibility hereunder for compliance with this article by their officers, agents, servants and employees.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-53. - Removal of alcoholic beverage served for consumption on the promises prohibited.
No alcoholic beverage sold pursuant to a license for sales of distilled spirits for consumption on the premises may be removed from the premises on which such alcoholic beverage is sold.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-54. - Construction of definitions.
As used in this article, defined words shall have the meanings specified unless the context in which the word or term is used clearly requires that a different meaning be used.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-55. - Retail dealer licenses; applications; notice; fingerprints; bonds; restrictions; standards; hearing.
(a)
No distilled spirits for consumption on the premises shall be sold in Peach County except as authorized by a retail dealer's license issued pursuant to this article. Licenses issued hereunder shall authorize the sale of distilled spirits for consumption on the premises as defined herein, only.
(b)
Application for a retail dealer's license shall be made on forms furnished by the governing authority. A notice of each application shall be advertised in the official gazette of Peach County, Georgia once a week for two weeks immediately preceding the meeting of the governing authority in which the application is to be acted upon.
(c)
Each applicant shall furnish a complete set of fingerprints which shall be forwarded to the Peach County Sheriff's Department who shall search the files of the Georgia Crime Information Center for a period of five years immediately preceding the date of such application for any instance of criminal activity. The Peach County Sheriff's Department shall also submit such fingerprints to the Federal Bureau of Investigation under rules established by the United States Department of Justice for processing and identification records. The federal record, if any, shall be obtained and returned to the governing authority considering such application. Where it appears to the governing authority that an application is complete except for the F.B.I. fingerprint report, and that the applicant otherwise qualifies for a retail dealer's license, the governing authority may grant the license contingent on receiving a satisfactory F.B.I. report. Should the report provide grounds for denying the license, the license may be revoked immediately as though the application had been denied, and the licensee shall have the right to a hearing.
(d)
Along with their application, applicants must post with the governing authority a personal performance bond, said bond to be conditioned upon the faithful observance and performance by the licensee of the rules and regulations contained in this article. Upon the violation of this article, or any part thereof, the amount of the bond to be forfeited will be determined by the governing authority in accordance with the nature of the violation after a hearing is held by the said governing authority. Written notice of such hearing shall be given to the alleged violator not less than 15 days prior thereto. The determination by the governing authority to forfeit such bond may be in addition to an action taken by the governing authority under sections 6-72 through 6-72.1. Said bond shall be payable to Peach County, Georgia in the amount of $1,000.00, said bond to be approved by the governing authority and shall be executed in the form specified by the governing authority. However, nothing herein shall preclude the governing authority waiving said bond requirement whenever a licensee has established sufficient financial responsibility and/or a record of faithful compliance with the laws and regulations of the United States of America, the State of Georgia and the County of Peach.
(e)
No retail dealer's license shall be granted to any person or corporation unless such person is of legal age and such person or corporation is a resident of Peach County, Georgia or has its principal place of business located in Peach County, Georgia.
(f)
No employee of Peach County, Georgia shall be eligible to receive a retail dealer's license.
(g)
The following standards shall be applied to all decisions pertaining to the issuance or denial of licenses hereunder:
(1)
All applicants for a retail dealer's license must show financial responsibility. The governing authority may require all applicants to provide financial statements and other evidence of financial responsibility in conjunction with the application.
(2)
All applicants for a retail dealer's license must be of good character, and all operators, managers, clerks, or other employees shall be of like character. Corporate or firm applicants shall be of good business reputation.
(3)
No license shall be granted to an applicant who has been convicted under any federal, state, or local law for a criminal offense involving alcoholic beverages, gambling, or tax law violations if such conviction tends to indicate that the applicant would not maintain the operation for which a license is being sought in conformity with federal, state of [or] the laws of Peach County, Georgia. Furthermore, no license shall be granted if any operators, managers, clerks, or other employees employed by the applicant has been convicted under any federal, state, or local law for a criminal offense involving alcoholic beverages, gambling, or tax law violators if such conviction tends to indicate that the applicant would not maintain the operation for which a license is being sought in conformity with federal, state or the laws of Peach County, Georgia.
(4)
A license application may be denied to any applicant for a retail dealer's license where it appears that the applicant would not have adequate financial participation in the proposed business to direct and manage its affairs, or where it appears that the applicant is intended to be a mere surrogate for a person or persons who would not otherwise qualify for a license for any reason whatsoever.
(5)
The governing authority, in their discretion, may consider any extenuating circumstances which may reflect favorably or unfavorably on the applicant, application or the proposed location of the business. If, in the governing authority's sole judgment, circumstances are such that the granting of the license would not be in the best interest of the general public, such circumstances may be grounds for denying the application.
(h)
All decisions of the governing authority either approving or denying a license application shall be stated in writing and a copy of such statement shall be provided to the applicant either personally or by certified mail within ten days of the decision.
(i)
Whenever the governing authority shall deny an application for a retail dealer's license, the applicant shall have 15 days following notification of denial to request a hearing before the governing authority. The applicant shall be entitled to present evidence and cross-examine opposing witnesses at such hearing either with or without legal counsel.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-56. - Premises.
No retail dealer's license for consumption of distilled spirits on the premises shall be issued to any person unless the building in which the business will be located is complete and detailed plans of said building and outside premises are attached to the application, or unless proposed plans and specifications and a building permit for a building to be built or renovated are attached to the application. The completed building or the proposed building shall comply with ordinances of Peach County, Georgia, regulations of the State Revenue Commissioner and the laws of the State of Georgia. The proposed building shall also be subject to final inspection and approval when completed by the Building Inspector for Peach County. Each such building shall contain sufficient lighting so that the building itself and the premises on all sides of the building are readily visible at all times from the street on which the building is located so as to reveal the inside retail area of the building and so as to reveal all of the outside premises of said building. Each applicant for a retail dealer's license shall attach to his application evidence of ownership of the building or proposed building or a copy of the lease if the applicant is leasing the building. A separate retail dealer's license shall be required for each place of business.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-57. - Distance requirements.
(a)
No retail dealer's license shall be granted to any person unless the entire business premises of the proposed location, including parking lot, is situated not less than 100 yards from any church building, not less than 200 yards from the property line of any school ground or college campus, and not less than 100 yards from any building used as an alcohol rehabilitation center, the same to be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground. Each application for a retail dealer's license shall include a scale drawing of the location of the proposed premises showing the distance to the nearest church building, school ground or campus, or alcohol rehabilitation center, or a certificate of a registered surveyor that such location complies with this article with reference to this section.
(b)
Nevertheless, a license may be issued to any hotel, motel, or similar establishment; to any bona fide private club generally recognized as such and having bona fide membership requirements; or to any bona fide restaurant or other bona fide food service establishment, the distance between any such place and a church, library, branch, school ground or college campus notwithstanding.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-58. - Term of license; renewal.
(a)
A retail dealer's license for sale of distilled spirits on the premises shall be valid only for the 12-month period from the date of issuance and no license may be renewed except upon approval of the governing authority. A licensee who desires to continue to operate during a subsequent year must apply for renewal prior to the expiration of his or her current license. An application for renewal is subject to the requirements of [sections] 6-76 through 6-78.
(b)
The issuance of a license under this article for any calendar year or any portion thereof shall not be deemed to vest the licensee or any person having an interest in the business with any right to renewal thereof.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-59. - License fees.
The fee for each retail dealer's license for consumption of distilled spirits on the premises shall be as established by the county commission per annum and shall be paid prior to the issuance of such license, said fee to accompany the application, and shall be either a bank certified check or money order.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012; Ord. of 8-12-14)
Sec. 6-60. - Place of sale of delivery of goods.
(a)
No licensee shall sell or deliver any distilled spirits for consumption on the premises to any person except in the licensed premises except as exempted under paragraph (b) of this section.
(b)
Open area sales and outdoor special events.
(1)
Sales of distilled spirits can be made by a licensed consumption on-premises establishment in an open area type environment or by a licensed caterer with a catering off-premises license at an outdoor special event if the establishment or event has been approved by the county marshal.
(2)
The open area must be enclosed by some structure providing for public ingress/egress only through the main licensed premises.
a.
The height of such structure shall be a minimum of four feet above the floor or ground. It must be approved by the county building inspector and the county fire department as required by governing regulations and codes.
b.
The only exit from the area is to be through the licensed establishment's main premises and through an approved fire exit.
(3)
An outdoor special event must be enclosed by some structure or fencing providing for public ingress/egress only through designated entrances and exits.
a.
The outdoor special event must be approved by the county marshal and the Peach County Sheriff on crowd control, traffic, and security measures.
b.
The event location must be properly zoned and approved by the Peach County Zoning Department.
(c)
Promotion and sales.
(1)
No licensee or employee or agent of a licensee, in connection with the sale or other disposition of distilled spirits for consumption on the premises shall:
a.
Offer or deliver any free alcoholic beverage to any person or group of persons; however, nothing herein shall prohibit a brewery, winery, or distillery from distributing free samples in accordance with state law provisions;
b.
Deliver more than one alcoholic beverage to one person at a time; however, nothing herein shall prohibit a brew pub from offering a sampler of malt beverages in containers not exceeding four ounces. Each sampler shall not exceed four different types of beverages.
c.
The county marshal and the sheriff shall have responsibility for the enforcement of this article.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012; Ord. of 8-12-14)
Sec. 6-61. - Hours of operation.
No person, knowingly and intentionally, shall sell or offer to sell alcoholic beverages on:
(1)
Sundays except between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 12:00 midnight; or
(2)
Election days. Pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20, the sale of distilled spirits for consumption on the premises shall be permitted on election days as the same are defined in the Official Code of Georgia Annotated; provided, however, that it shall nonetheless be unlawful for any person to sell distilled spirits for consumption on the premises within 250 feet of any polling place or of the outer edge of any building within which such polling place is established on primary or election days.
(3)
Christmas Day.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012; Ord. of 8-12-14)
Sec. 6-62. - Minimum age of consumption; exceptions.
No retail dealer or employee of a retail dealer shall knowingly furnish, sell or offer for sale any distilled spirits to a person prohibited to purchase distilled spirits under state law.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-63. - Employment of persons under 21 years of age prohibited.
No licensee shall employ, require, or permit a person under 21 years of age to sell or take orders for any distilled spirits.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-64. - Furnishing distilled spirits to certain persons prohibited.
No licensee shall furnish, sell, or offer to sell any distilled spirits to any person who the licensee knows or reasonably should know is intoxicated, or is of unsound mine, or is a habitual drunkard.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-65. - Retail dealers to buy only from licensed wholesalers.
No licensee shall buy or arrange to buy or in any way effect the transfer of any distilled spirits except from a licensed wholesaler.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-66. - Retail dealers to store inventory only on premises.
A licensee shall keep no inventory or stock of distilled spirits at any place except the licensed premises, and within the licensed premises the storage area for distilled spirits shall be immediately adjacent to the retail area. No licensee shall enter into any type of arrangement whereby distilled spirits ordered by the licensee are stored by a licensed wholesaler.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-67. - Transfers and assignments.
Alcoholic beverage licenses issued under this article shall not be transferable or assignable to new owners, but where there is a change in the ownership of a business, the new owner or owners shall file an application as for a new license as provided by this article. Changes of business interests from one party at interest named in the original application to another party at interest named in the original application and changes from one employee or manager of a social club to his successor shall not be deemed a transfer of a license. In each instance, however, the licensee shall notify the board of commissioners in writing of such change and make a complete disclosure of all of the facts in connection therewith, such notification to be made within three days from the date of such change.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-68. - Display of license.
Licenses issued under this article shall be displayed prominently at all times on the premises for which same was issued.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-69. - Licensees to maintain a copy of the article; employees to be familiar with terms; responsibility of licensee for violations.
Each licensee shall keep a copy of this article in the licensed premises and shall instruct any person working there with respect to the terms hereof and each licensee and the licensee's agents and employees selling distilled spirits or wine shall at all times be familiar with the terms hereof. The acts of any person working in the licensed premises which occur on the licensed premises and are in violation of this article or of any federal, state or local law relating to the sale, regulation or control of alcoholic beverages or to drugs or gambling shall be attributable to the licensee for the purposes of enforcement of this article.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-70. - Premises open for inspection.
The licensed premises shall be open to inspection at any and all times by officers or officials representing the governing authority authorized to conduct such inspections.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-71. - Gambling on premises prohibited.
There shall be no gambling, betting, games or chance, punch-board, slot machines, pin-ball machines, or the operation of any scheme for hazarding money or any other thing of value in any place of business licensed under this article, or in any room adjoining the same, owned, leased or controlled by an licensee. Any violation of this section shall be cause for suspension or revocation of a license.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-72. - Grounds for suspension or revocation of license.
Any of the following shall be grounds for suspension or revocation, as the governing authority sees fit, of a license:
(1)
The making of any statement on an application for a license issued hereunder which shall be later found to be false;
(2)
Violation of any of the terms of this article;
(3)
With respect to the licensee or any person with an ownership interest in the licensed premises, the violation of any federal, state or local law relating to the sale, control or regulation of alcoholic beverages, drugs, gambling, or taxation;
(4)
Failure to maintain adequate financial responsibility upon which issuance of the license was conditioned, or default on any obligation, of any kind whatsoever, lawfully owing to Peach County, Georgia;
(5)
Suspension or revocation of a state retail dealer's license, which shall automatically operate to suspend or revoke any license issued hereunder.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-72.1. - Suspension and revocation—Procedure; hearing.
Any action taken by the governing authority to suspend or revoke a license issued hereunder shall not preclude and may be in addition to any criminal prosecution by a proper authority as provided by the laws and ordinances of Peach County, the State of Georgia, or the United States. Whenever any action is taken by the governing authority to suspend or revoke any license issued hereunder, the governing authority shall provide written notice to the licensee within ten days of such action, said notice stating the action taken and the reasons therefor. The licensee shall have 15 days following notification of such action to request a hearing before the governing authority, which hearing shall be held within 30 days of such request. The licensee shall be entitled at such hearing to present evidence and to examine opposing witnesses either with or without counsel.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-73. - Repeal of conflicting ordinances.
All ordinances and parts of ordinances in conflict herewith are hereby expressly repealed.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-74. - Multiple locations; separate license required.
Each license issued hereunder shall authorize the sale of distilled spirits for consumption on the premises at one licensed premises only. A person selling alcoholic beverages at more than one licensed premises must obtain a separate license for each location.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-75. - Operation in accordance with law.
Licensees shall operate in accordance with the terms of this article and with all applicable federal and state law related to the regulation of alcoholic beverages, including but not limited to the Georgia Alcoholic Beverage Code, O.C.G.A. 3-3-1 et seq. The term "licensee" as used in this part includes agents and employees of the licensee.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-76. - License issuance; application procedure.
(a)
Application. Application for a retail dealer's license shall be made on forms furnished by the governing authority. Applications shall be made in the name of a natural person and shall include the name of the business entity which will operate pursuant to the license, and the names of all individuals holding an ownership interest in the business entity.
(b)
Fee. The fee for each retail dealer's license for consumption of distilled spirits on the premises shall be as established by the county commission per annum and shall be paid prior to the issuance of such license, said fee to accompany the application, and shall be either a bank certified check or money order.
(c)
Background investigation. Each applicant shall furnish a complete set of fingerprints which shall be forwarded to the Peach County Sheriff's Department who shall search the files of the Georgia Crime Information Center for a period of two years immediately preceding the date of such application for any instance of criminal activity. The Peach County Sheriff's Department shall also submit such fingerprints to the Federal Bureau of Investigation under rules established by the United States Department of Justice for processing and identification records. The federal record, if any, shall be obtained and returned to the governing authority considering such application. When considering an application for renewal of a license, the governing authority may waive compliance with all or any part of this paragraph.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012; Ord. of 8-12-14)
Sec. 6-77. - Same—Standards for granting and renewing licenses.
(a)
Financial responsibility. All applicants for a retail dealer's license must show financial responsibility. The governing authority may require all applicants to provide financial statements and other evidence of financial responsibility in conjunction with the application. An application may be denied to any applicant where it appears that the applicant would not have adequate financial resources to direct and manage the affairs of the business.
(b)
Legal age. The applicant must be at least 21 years of age.
(c)
County employees ineligible. No employee of Peach County, Georgia shall be eligible to receive a retail dealer's license.
(d)
Violation of laws. No retail dealer's license shall be granted or renewed where the individual applicant, or any individual with an ownership interest in the business entity which will operate pursuant to the license, has been convicted or, or has entered a plea of guilty or nolo contendere to, any crime related to the sale or regulation of alcoholic beverages, gambling, drugs, or tax law violations, within the two-year period preceding the application date. This section shall not apply to a person who has previously had his or her license revoked by the governing authority.
(e)
Prior revocation. No person whose retail dealer's license has been revoked by the governing authority shall be granted a license for a period of 12 months from the date the revocation becomes final, or for such lesser period as the governing authority may determine in its discretion, except that no person whose license is revoked by the governing authority due to conviction of, or a plea of guilty or nolo contendere to, a crime related to drugs, gambling, or tax law violations shall be granted a license for a period of two years from the date the revocation becomes final. No business in which any such person has an ownership interest shall be entitled to operate pursuant to a license for 12 months following the revocation of such person's license. A license revocation becomes final:
(1)
Upon the expiration of any applicable period, as provided by this article or as allowed by law, to request a hearing or appeal with regard to the revocation decision, if no hearing or appeal is requested; or
(2)
By a decision of the Superior Court of Peach County which has the effect of affirming a decision of the governing authority revoking a license.
(f)
Condition of premises. No retail dealer's license shall be issued to any person unless the building in which the business will be located is complete and detailed plans of said building and outside premises are attached to the application, or unless proposed plans and specifications and a building permit for a building to be built or renovated are attached to the application. The completed building or the proposed building shall comply with ordinances of Peach County, Georgia, regulations of the State Revenue Commissioner and the laws of the State of Georgia. The proposed building shall also be subject to final inspection and approval when completed by the Building Inspector for Peach County. Each such building shall contain sufficient lighting so that the building itself and the premises on all sides of the building are readily visible at all times from the street on which the building is located so as to reveal the inside retail area of the building and so as to reveal all of the outside premises of said building. Each applicant for a retail dealer's license shall attach to his application evidence of ownership of the building or proposed building or a copy of the lease if the applicant is leasing the building. A separate retail dealer's license shall be required for each place of business.
(g)
Location. No retail dealer's license shall be granted to any person unless the entire business premises of the proposed location, including parking lot, is situated not less than 100 yards from any building used as an alcohol rehabilitation center, and not less than 1,000 feet from any licensed adult entertainment establishment. Distance shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground. Each application for a retail dealer's license shall include a scale drawing of the location of the proposed premises showing the distance to the nearest church building, school ground or campus, alcohol rehabilitation center, or adult entertainment establishment, or a certificate of a registered surveyor that such location complies with this article with reference to this section.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
Sec. 6-78. - Same—Temporary issuance.
Where it appears to the governing authority that an application is complete except for the F.B.I. report as described in paragraph 6-76(c) above, and that the applicant otherwise qualifies for a retail dealer's license, the governing authority may grant the license contingent on receiving a satisfactory F.B.I. report. Should the report provide grounds for denying the license, the license may be revoked immediately as though the application had been denied, and the license shall have the right to a hearing as provided in section 6-55.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)

All decisions of the governing authority either approving or denying a license application shall be stated in writing and a copy of such statement shall be provided to the applicant either personally or by certified mail within ten days of the decision. Whenever the governing authority shall deny an application for a retail dealer's license, the applicant shall have 15 days following notification of denial to request a hearing before the governing authority. The applicant shall be entitled to present evidence and cross-examine opposing witnesses at such hearing either with or without legal counsel.
(Ord. of 5-8-2012)
