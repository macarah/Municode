ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 58-1. - Property of transient persons.
(a)
Whenever at any time during any year any transient person shall temporarily locate in the city for the purpose of selling or disposing of goods, wares, merchandise or any other article of value, it shall be the duty of the city finance director or the director of the code enforcement department or the director of the code enforcement department's designee to call on such person and make a return for taxation of all of such person's stock in trade at its fair market value to the county tax director as in other cases, and in case such person shall neglect or refuse to return the same within 24 hours after such demand, it shall be the duty of the city finance director or the code enforcement director, as the case may be, to report the same to the county tax director who shall immediately assess and determine the same, as in other cases, and the tax shall be computed thereon at the rate of assessment of the city taxes.
(b)
If a city rate has not been fixed for the current year, then the rate for the previous year shall prevail. In case such person shows that such property has been returned and the taxes assessed thereon paid, then no further tax shall be assessed for such year.
(Code 1975, § 31-5; Code 1985, § 26-3)

ARTICLE II. - AD VALOREM TAXES

Sec. 58-25. - Exemption of certain tangible personal property from ad valorem taxation.
There is hereby exempted from ad valorem taxation all three types of tangible personal property as described under the section commonly known as the Freeport Exemption (O.C.G.A. § 48-5-48.2(b)(1)—(3)), including all such taxes levied for educational purposes and for state purposes, at the rate of 80 percent of the value of said property as defined in Ga. Const. art. 7, § 2, ¶ III and O.C.G.A. § 48-5-48.2.
(Code 1985, § 26-4; Ord. No. 87-208, 12-8-1987, Ord. No. 89-175, 11-14-1989; Ord. No. 90-103, 1-23-1990; Ord. No. 91-109, 1-22-1991)
Sec. 58-26. - Homestead exemptions.
(a)
An applicant seeking one or both of the homestead exemptions provided in Act No. 117, Georgia Laws 1989, p. 4062 et seq. (1989 Ga. Laws (Act No. 117), p. 4062), shall, on or before April 1 of the year in which the exemption from taxation is sought file a written application with the tax director of Dougherty County, Georgia, on forms provided by that official, specifying the exemptions sought. Said application shall be accompanied by the affidavit of the owner that said owner was the owner of an interest in the real property for which exemption is sought and resided therein as such owner's residence, on January 1 of the year in which the exemption is sought; and if the additional exemption allowed for such owners who are 65 years or older is sought, said affidavit shall likewise state that said owner was 65 years of age or older on January 1 of said year.
(b)
Except as provided in subsections (c) and (d) of this section, the failure to file timely and properly the application and the affidavit herein required shall constitute a waiver of the homestead exemption on the part of the owner failing to make application for such exemption for that year.
(c)
The owner of a homestead which is actually occupied by the owner as a residence and homestead shall not have to apply for the exemption more than once so long as the owner remains in continuous occupation of the residence as a homestead. The homestead shall automatically be renewed from year to year so long as the owner continuously occupies the residence as a homestead. If the original application did not include an application for the exemption granted to persons 65 years or older, such owner shall not be entitled to that exemption until such owner supplements his or her application by an affidavit establishing that said owner was on January 1 of the year in which that exemption is sought.
(d)
All persons now qualified for the $2,000.00 homestead exemption granted to property owners in the county residing on the property as a residence, and for the special homestead exemption from ad valorem taxes granted to certain residents of the county who have reached the age 65 years and are below a certain level of income, are hereby declared to be qualified for the equivalent city tax exemptions without having to file any additional application or affidavit.
(e)
In accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-5-50.1, the determination of eligibility of an applicant to claim the exemptions shall be made by the municipal governing authority subject to appeal to the superior court of Dougherty County, Georgia. Any such appeal must be filed within 30 days after the final determination by the municipal governing authority and shall be a de novo proceeding.
(Code 1985, § 26-5; Ord. No. 89-159, §§ 1—5, 8-22-1989)

ARTICLE III. - HOTEL OCCUPANCY TAX[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Excise tax on rooms, lodgings and accommodations, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-50 et seq.

Sec. 58-56. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Due date means the 20th day after the close of the monthly period for which tax is to be computed.
Guestroom means a room occupied, or intended, arranged, or designed for occupancy, by one or more occupants for the purpose of living quarters or residential use.
Hotel means any structure or portion of a structure, including a hotel, motel, inn, lodge, tourist camp, tourist cabin or any other place in which rooms, lodgings or accommodations are regularly furnished for value.
Monthly period means the calendar months of any year.
Occupancy means the use or possession, or the right to the use or possession of any room or apartment in a hotel, or the right to the use or possession of the furnishings or to the services and accommodations accompanying the use and possession of the room, except when such room is being used as a meeting room.
Occupant means any person who, for a consideration, uses, possesses or has the right to use or possess any room in a hotel under any lease, concession, permit, right of access, license to use or other agreement, or otherwise.
Operator means any person operating a hotel in the city, including, but not limited to, the owner or proprietor of such premises, lessee, sub-lessee, lender in possession, licensee or any other person otherwise operating such hotel.
Permanent resident means any occupant as of a given date who has or shall have occupied, or has or shall have the right of occupancy of any guestroom in a hotel for more than ten consecutive days next preceding such date.
Person means an individual, firm, partnership, joint adventure, association, social club, fraternal organization, joint stock company, corporation, nonprofit corporation or co-operative non-profit membership, estate, trust, receiver, trustee, syndicate, or any other group or combination acting as a unit, the plural as well as the singular number, excepting the United States of America, the state, and any political subdivision of either thereof upon which the city is without power to impose the tax provided in this article.
Rent means the consideration received for occupancy valued in money, whether received in money or otherwise, including all receipts, cash, credits, and property or services of any kind or nature, and also the amount for which credit is allowed by the operator to the occupant, without any deduction therefrom whatsoever.
Return means any return filed or required to be filed as provided in this article.
(Code 1975, § 31-81; Code 1985, § 26-36)
Sec. 58-57. - Imposition and rate of tax.
There shall be paid a tax of eight per centum of the rent for every occupancy of a guest room in a hotel, motel, inn, lodge, tourist cabin, campground or any other place in which rooms, lodging or accommodations are regularly or periodically furnished for value in the City of Albany, Georgia all in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(b)(1) through (6). The tax imposed by this article shall be paid upon any occupancy on or after the effective date of this article, although such occupancy is pursuant to a contract, lease or other arrangement made prior to such date.
(Code 1975, § 31-82; Code 1985, § 26-37; Ord. No. 87-181, § 1, 9-22-1987; Ord. No. 06-100, § 1, 1-24-2006; Ord. No. 12-122, § 1, 12-18-2012)
Sec. 58-58. - Collection of tax by operator; receipt to occupant; rules for collection schedules.
Every operator maintaining a place of business in this city, as provided in the next preceding section, and renting guestrooms in this city, not exempted under section 58-59, shall collect a hotel occupancy tax from each occupant.
(Code 1975, § 31-83; Code 1985, § 26-38; Ord. No. 87-181, § 1, 9-22-1987)
Sec. 58-59. - Exemptions.
This tax shall be subject to all exemptions required under state law, and shall not be imposed upon the class of guests exempted from its application under O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(a)(1)(C).
(Code 1975, § 31-84; Code 1985, § 26-39)
Sec. 58-60. - Registration of operator; certificate of authority.
(a)
Every person engaging or about to engage in business as an operator of a hotel in the city shall immediately register with the finance director or the finance director's designee of the city, on a form provided by the finance director or the finance director's designee. Such registration shall set forth the name under which such person transacts business or intends to transact business, the location of such person's place of business, and such other information which would facilitate the collection of the tax as the finance director or the finance director's designee may require. The registration shall be signed by the owner if a natural person; in case of ownership by an association or partnership, by a member or partner; in the case of ownership by a corporation, by an officer.
(b)
The finance director or the finance director's designee shall, after such registration, issue without charge a certificate of authority to each operator to collect the tax from the occupant.
(c)
A separate registration shall be required for each place of business of an operator.
(d)
Each certificate shall state the name and location of the business to which it is applicable.
(Code 1975, § 31-85; Code 1985, § 26-40)
Sec. 58-61. - Determination, returns and payments.
(a)
Due date of taxes. All amounts of taxes due under this article shall be due and payable to the finance director or the finance director's designee monthly on or before the 20th day of every month next succeeding each respective monthly period.
(b)
Return; time of filing; persons required to file; contents. On or before the 20th day of the month following each monthly period, a return for the preceding monthly period shall be filed with the finance director or the finance director's designee showing the gross rent, rent from permanent residents, taxable rent, amount of tax collected or otherwise due for the related period, and such other information as may be required by the finance director or the finance director's designee.
(c)
Collection fee allowed operators. Operators collecting the tax authorized by this article shall be allowed a percentage of the tax due and accounted for, and shall be reimbursed in the form of a deduction in submitting, reporting and paying the amount due, if such amount is not delinquent at the time of payment. The rate of the deduction shall be the same rate as provided under state law.
(Code 1975, § 31-86; Code 1985, § 26-41)
State Law reference— Each person collecting the tax allowed a three percent rate of deduction in submitting, reporting, and paying the amount due if the amount due is not delinquent at the time of payment, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-52.
Sec. 58-62. - Deficiency determinations.
(a)
Recomputation of tax; authority to make; basis of recomputation. If the finance director or the finance director's designee is not satisfied with the return of the tax or the amount of the tax required by this article to be paid to the city by any person, the finance director or the finance director's designee may compute and determine the amount required to be paid upon the basis of any information within his or her possession or that may come into his or her possession. One or more deficiency determinations may be made of the amount due for one or more monthly periods.
(b)
Interest on deficiency. The amount of the determination shall bear interest at the rate of three-fourths of one percent per month, or fraction thereof from the due date of taxes.
(c)
Notice of determination; service of. The finance director or the finance director's designee shall give to the operator written notice of his or her determination. The notice may be served personally or by mail; if by mail, such service shall be addressed to the operator at the operator's address as it appears in the records of the finance director or the finance director's designee. Service by mail is complete when delivered by certified mail with a receipt signed by addressee.
(d)
Time within which notice of deficiency determination is to be mailed. Except in the case of failure to make a return, every notice of a deficiency determination shall be mailed within three years after the 20th day of the calendar month following the monthly period for which the amount is proposed to be determined, or within three years after the return is filed, whichever period should last expire.
(Code 1975, § 31-87; Code 1985, § 26-42)
Sec. 58-63. - Determination if no return made.
(a)
Estimate of gross receipts. If any person fails to make a return, the finance director or the finance director's designee shall make an estimate of the amount of the gross receipts of the person, or as the case may be, of the amount of the total rentals in this city which are subject to the tax required by this article. The estimate shall be made for the period in respect to which the person failed to make the return, and shall be based upon any information which is or may come into the possession of the finance director or the finance director's designee. Written notice shall be given in the manner prescribed in section 58-62(c).
(b)
Interest on amount found due. The amount of the determination shall bear interest at the rate of three-fourths of one percent per month, or fraction thereof, from the 20th day of the month following the monthly period, for which the amount or any portion thereof should have been returned, until the date of payment.
(Code 1975, § 31-88; Code 1985, § 26-43)
Sec. 58-64. - Administration of article.
(a)
Authority of finance director or the finance director's designee. The finance director or the finance director's designee shall administer and enforce the provisions of this article for the collection of the tax imposed by this article.
(b)
Records required from operators, etc.; form. Every operator renting guestrooms in this city to a person shall keep such records, receipts, invoices and other pertinent papers in such form as the finance director or the finance director's designee may require.
(c)
Examination of records; audits. The finance director or the finance director's designee or any person authorized in writing by the finance director or the finance director's designee may examine the books, papers, records, financial reports, equipment and other facilities of any operator renting guestrooms to a person and any operator liable for the tax, in order to verify the accuracy of any return made, or if no return is made by the operator, to ascertain and determine the amount required to be paid.
(d)
Authority to require reports; contents. In the administration of the provisions of this article, the finance director or the finance director's designee may require the filing of reports by any persons or class of persons having in such person's or persons' possession or custody information relating to rentals of guestrooms which are subject to the tax. The reports shall be filed with the finance director or the finance director's designee when required by the finance director or the finance director's designee, and shall set forth the rental charged for each occupancy, the dates of occupancy, and such other information as the finance director or the finance director's designee may require.
(Code 1975, § 31-89; Code 1985, § 26-44)
Sec. 58-65. - Collection of delinquent taxes.
(a)
Action for tax; time for. At any time within three years after any tax or any amount of tax required by this article to be collected becomes due and payable, and at any time within three years after the delinquency of any such tax or any amount of tax required to be collected, the finance director or the finance director's designee may bring an action in a court of competent jurisdiction in the name of the city to collect the amount delinquent together with interest, court fees, filing fees, attorney's fees and other legal fees incident thereto.
(b)
Duty of successors or assignees of operator to withhold tax from purchase money. If any operator liable for any amount under this article sells out such owner's business or quits the business, such owner's successors or assigns shall withhold a sufficient sum from the purchase price to cover such amount until the former owner produces a receipt from the finance director or the finance director's designee showing that such owner's has been paid or a certificate stating that no amount is due.
(c)
Liability for failure to withhold; certificate of notice of amount due; time to enforce successor's liability. If the purchaser of a business fails to withhold the purchase price as required, such purchaser shall be personally liable for the payment of the amount required to be withheld by him or her to the extent of the purchase price.
(d)
Tax credit, or interest paid more than once or erroneously or illegally collected. Whenever the amount of any tax or interest has been paid more than once, or has been erroneously or illegally collected or received by the city under this article, it may be offset by the finance director or the finance director's designee. If the operator or person determines that he or she has overpaid or paid more than once, which fact has not been determined by the finance director or the finance director's designee, such operator or person will have three years from date of payment to file a claim in writing stating the specific ground upon which the claim is founded. The claim shall be audited. If the claim is approved by the finance director or the finance director's designee, the excess amount paid to the city may be credited on any amounts then due and payable from the person by whom it was paid, or such person's administrators or executors.
(Code 1975, § 31-91; Code 1985, § 26-45)
Sec. 58-66. - Proceeds of the tax.
The proceeds of the tax shall be used in accordance with state law.
(Code 1985, § 26-46; Ord. No. 05-107, § 1, 4-26-2005)

Sec. 58-109. - Time of issuance.
For all taxes unpaid on December 20 in each year, execution shall immediately be issued therefor.
(Code 1975, § 31-41; Code 1985, § 26-61)
Sec. 58-110. - Issuance by clerk, etc.; form.
All tax executions shall be issued by the city clerk and directed to the code enforcement director and bear test in the name of the mayor and shall follow substantially the form of executions for state and county taxes.
(Code 1975, § 31-42; Code 1985, § 26-62)
Sec. 58-111. - Levy of executions.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the code enforcement director to immediately levy all tax executions placed in his or her hands on the goods, chattels, lands and tenements in the city of the person or property in default and to sell the same in accordance with the provisions of the law and this Code.
(b)
All executions for taxes shall be by the code enforcement director in the same manner as pointed out for the levy of state and county tax executions and all entries thereon shall be made in accordance therewith.
(Code 1975, § 31-43; Code 1985, § 26-63)
Sec. 58-112. - Execution to be collected within 90 days.
It shall be the duty of the code enforcement director within 90 days after the delivery of tax executions to the code enforcement director to collect the same or either return the same with an entry of "nulla bona" entered thereon.
(Code 1975, § 31-44; Code 1985, § 26-64)
Sec. 58-113. - Cost of executions to be collected.
It shall be the duty of the code enforcement director to collect, in addition to the principal and interest, the costs allowed by law on all tax executions issued by the city clerk and placed in his or her hands for collection or levy and sale, such cost to be turned into the city treasury.
(Code 1975, § 31-45; Code 1985, § 26-65)
Sec. 58-114. - Insolvent list.
It shall be the duty of the code enforcement director to make a complete list of insolvent persons against whom he or she holds executions with an entry thereon of "nulla bona" and file same with the city finance director. When such list has been allowed or approved by the board of city commissioners, the clerk shall then receipt the code enforcement director for the amount of executions with such nulla bona entries and such receipt shall be his or her voucher to be credited on his or her account with the city.
(Code 1975, § 31-21; Code 1985, § 26-66)
Sec. 58-115. - Record of tax defaulters.
The city finance director shall keep a record containing, in alphabetical order, the names of all persons who have not paid their taxes, placing opposite the name of each person the amount due for such taxes and the year due. Such record shall be made in a well-bound book. When any tax shall be collected after such record is made, it shall be applied to the oldest tax payment against the person paying the same.
(Code 1975, § 31-22; Code 1985, § 26-67)

ARTICLE V. - ELECTRIC SUPPLIERS

Sec. 58-180. - Gross receipts business tax established; verified statement of revenues to be filed.
Each electric supplier (including specifically electric membership corporations whether or not operated for profit), within the meaning of O.C.G.A. § 46-3-1 et seq., distributing and selling electric power within the city, which is not otherwise paying a franchise fee pursuant to a franchise agreement, and whether or not said electric supplier has, operates and maintains offices within the city shall pay a gross receipts tax at the rate of four percent on any and all gross revenue derived from distribution and sale of electric power to their customers, members, or purchasers within the city, according to the verified statement of said gross revenues which shall be submitted by said electric supplier at the time said taxes are paid. This gross receipts business tax is levied pursuant to authority granted the city in Georgia Laws 1923, page 370 of the Acts of the General Assembly of Georgia (1923 Ga. Laws (Act No. 303), p. 370, § 27(2)).
(Code 1985, § 26-91; Ord. No. 95-106, § 1, 2-28-1995)
Sec. 58-181. - Taxes payable monthly; taxes to be in lieu of certain other taxes.
The taxes hereinbefore set out in this article shall be due and payable to the city on the 20th day of each month, calculated on gross revenues billed for the immediately preceding month; also, the percentage of said gross revenues hereinbefore prescribed to be collected shall be in lieu of all other license and business or occupation taxes, but shall not at any time be considered to interfere with, or in any way prevent the collection of ad valorem taxes upon the property of said electric supplier in the same manner as all other property, real and personal, in the city is taxed.
(Code 1985, § 26-92; Ord. No. 95-106, § 2, 2-28-1995)
Sec. 58-182. - Electric service within the city.
Electric suppliers distributing and selling electric power within the city, and which do not have a franchise agreement with the city, do not acquire any additional rights by the payment of the tax herein levied under this article other than those rights granted under the Georgia Electric Territorial Service Act.
(Code 1985, § 26-93; Ord. No. 95-106, § 3, 2-28-1995)
Sec. 58-183. - Enforcement and administration.
(a)
The finance director shall be responsible for the administration and enforcement of this article and shall have the power to prepare and provide the necessary forms and for the collection of the tax herein levied.
(b)
The finance director shall issue executions for the collection of all outstanding and unpaid taxes imposed and assessed by this article. The unpaid taxes and executions shall be collected in the manner provided by law for the collection of other taxes due the city.
(Code 1985, § 26-94; Ord. No. 95-106, § 4, 2-28-1995)

ARTICLE VI. - LICENSE FEES AND GROSS PREMIUMS TAX ON INSURERS

Sec. 58-205. - Insurers license fees.
There is hereby levied an annual license fee upon each insurer doing business within the City of Albany, Georgia, in an amount as established by ordinance or resolution of the board of city commissioners, from time to time, and made available for public examination in the office of the city clerk. For each separate business location in excess of one not covered by section 58-206, which is operating on behalf of such insurers within the city, there is hereby levied a license fee in an amount as established by ordinance or resolution of the board of city commissioners, from time to time, and made available for public examination in the office of the city clerk. For the purpose of this article, the term "insurer" means a company which is authorized to transact business in any of the classes of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5.
(Code 1985, § 26-106; Ord. No. 95-158, § 1, 11-21-1995)
Sec. 58-206. - License fees for insurers insuring certain risks at additional business locations.
For each separate business location not otherwise subject to a license fee hereunder, operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing and in connection with such loans or sales offers, solicits or takes applications for insurance through a licensed agent of an insurer for insurance, said insurer shall pay an additional annual license fee per location. The amount of the additional annual license fee shall be established by ordinance or resolution of the board of city commissioners, from time to time, and made available for public examination in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1985, § 26-107; Ord. No. 95-158, § 2, 11-21-1995)
Sec. 58-207. - Gross premiums tax imposed on life insurance.
There is hereby levied an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer writing life, accident and sickness insurance within the state in an amount equal to one percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.1. The term "gross direct premiums," as used in this section, means gross direct premiums as used in the O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fee imposed by section 58-205.
(Code 1985, § 26-108; Ord. No. 95-158, § 3, 11-21-1995)
Sec. 58-208. - Gross premiums tax, all other insurers.
There is hereby levied an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer, other than an insurer transacting business in the class of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5(1), doing business within the state in an amount equal to 2½ percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with the O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2. The term "gross direct premiums," as used in this section, means gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 58-205.
(Code 1985, § 26-109; Ord. No. 95-158, § 4, 11-21-1995)
Sec. 58-209. - Due date for license fee.
License fees imposed in sections 58-205 and 58-206 shall be due and payable on January 1 of each year.
(Code 1985, § 26-110; Ord. No. 95-158, § 5, 11-21-1995)

Sec. 58-240. - Deficiency determinations.
(a)
Recomputation of tax; authority to make; basis of recomputation. If the finance director or the finance director's designee is not satisfied with the return of the tax or the amount of the tax required by this article to be paid to the city by any person, the finance director or the finance director's designee may compute and determine the amount required to be paid upon the basis of any information within his or her possession or that may come into his or her possession. One or more deficiency determinations may be made of the amount due for one or more monthly periods.
(b)
Interest on deficiency. The amount of the determination shall bear interest at the rate of three-fourths of one percent per month, or fraction thereof from the due date of taxes.
(c)
Notice of determination; service of. The finance director or the finance director's designee shall give to the operator written notice of his or her determination. The notice may be served personally or by mail; if by mail, such service shall be addressed to the operator at the operator's address as it appears in the records of the finance director or the finance director's designee. Service by mail is complete when delivered by certified mail with a receipt signed by addressee.
(d)
Time within which notice of deficiency determination is to be mailed. Except in the case of failure to make a return, every notice of a deficiency determination shall be mailed within three years after the 20th day of the calendar month following the monthly period for which the amount is proposed to be determined, or within three years after the return is filed, whichever period should last expire.
(Ord. No. 13-117, § 6, 5-28-2013)
Sec. 58-241. - Determination if no return made.
(a)
Estimate of gross receipts. If any rental motor vehicle concern fails to make a return, the finance director or the finance director's designee shall make an estimate of the amount of the gross receipts of the person, or as the case may be, of the amount of the total rentals in this city which are subject to the tax required by this article. The estimate shall be made for the period in respect to which the person failed to make the return, and shall be based upon any information which is or may come into the possession of the finance director or the finance director's designee. Written notice of the finance director's determination, or the finance director's designee's determination, shall be given to the motor vehicle rental concern via certified mail, return receipt requested addressed to the motor vehicle rental concern's address as it appears in the records of the finance director or finance director's designee.
(b)
Interest on amount found due. The amount of the determination shall bear interest at the rate of three-fourths of one percent per month, or fraction thereof, from the 20th day of the month following the monthly period, for which the amount or any portion thereof should have been returned, until the date of payment.
(Ord. No. 13-117, § 6, 5-28-2013)
Sec. 58-242. - Administration of article.
(a)
Authority of finance director or the finance director's designee. The finance director or the finance director's designee shall administer and enforce the provisions of this article for the collection of the tax imposed by this article.
(b)
Records required from operators, etc.; form. Every rental motor vehicle concern renting vehicles in this city to a person shall keep such records, receipts, invoices and other pertinent papers in such form as the finance director or the finance director's designee may require.
(c)
Examination of records; audits. The finance director or the finance director's designee or any person authorized in writing by the finance director or the finance director's designee may examine the books, papers, records, financial reports, equipment and other facilities of any operator renting guestrooms to a person and any operator liable for the tax, in order to verify the accuracy of any return made, or if no return is made by the operator, to ascertain and determine the amount required to be paid.
(d)
Authority to require reports; contents. In the administration of the provisions of this article, the finance director or the finance director's designee may require the filing of reports by any persons or class of persons having in such person's or persons' possession or custody information relating to rentals of vehicles which are subject to the tax. The reports shall be filed with the finance director or the finance director's designee when required by the finance director or the finance director's designee, and shall set forth the rental charged for each occupancy, the dates of occupancy, and such other information as the finance director or the finance director's designee may require.
(Ord. No. 13-117, § 6, 5-28-2013)
