ARTICLE IV. - AUTHORITIES, BOARDS AND COMMISSIONS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-141. - County appointees.
(a)
All persons appointed by the board of commissioners of Tift County to serve on its various authorities, boards and commissions shall take an oath of office before the commission to attend all meetings and to well and truly perform the duties attendant to such position to the best of their skill and ability.
(b)
Appointees of the board of commissioners shall be removed for cause or for failure to attend three consecutive meetings or 60 percent of the meetings of the authority, board or commission [as applicable] without medical excuse.
(c)
Any appointees removed under the provisions of this section shall be entitled to petition the board of commissioners for reinstatement for good and valid cause.
(Res. No. 2004-14, 10-11-04)

Sec. 2-156. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Applicant means any individual or business entity applying for rezoning action.
Business entity means any corporation, partnership, limited partnership firm, enterprise, franchise, association or trust.
Commission means the Greater Tift County Planning and Zoning Commission.
Financial interest means all direct or indirect ownership interest of the total assets or capital stock of a business entity where such ownership interest is ten percent or more.
Governing authorities means collectively the board of commissioners, the city commission of the City of Tifton and the city commission of the City of Omega.
Governing authority of the City of Tifton means the city commission of the City of Tifton, Georgia.
Governing authority of the City of Omega means the mayor and council of the City of Omega, Georgia.
Governing authority of Tift County means the board of commissioners.
Governing authority for the City of Ty Ty means the mayor and council of the City of Ty Ty, Georgia.
Member means a member of the commission.
Member of the family means the spouse, mother, father, brother, sister, son or daughter of a member.
Property interest means the direct or indirect ownership of real property and includes any percentage of ownership less than total ownership as well as both legal and equitable interest.
Real property means any tract or parcel of land and, if developed, any buildings or structures located on the land.
Rezoning action means action by one of the governmental entities adopting or rejecting a proposed amendment to the zoning ordinance or zoning map of the governmental entity which would have the effect of rezoning real property from one zoning classification to another.
Zoning Ordinance of Omega means the zoning ordinance of the City of Omega as hereafter adopted and as thereafter amended.
Zoning Ordinance of Tift County means the zoning ordinance of the county adopted February 27, 1990, as now and hereafter amended.
Zoning Ordinance of Tifton means the zoning ordinance of the City of Tifton adopted October 6, 1986, as now and hereafter amended.
Zoning Ordinance of Ty Ty means the zoning ordinance of the city as adopted and thereafter amended.
(Ord. of 12-10-90, § 1.2; Ord. No. 2000-01, § 1, 3-13-00)

Sec. 2-157. - Establishment.
There is hereby established the Greater Tift County Planning Commission which shall function in accordance with the provisions of this division.

Sec. 2-158. - Membership; selection procedure.
(a)
The commission shall consist of 12 members who shall be selected from time to time as follows:
(1)
One member shall be selected from time to time by the governing authority of Tift County for an initial term expiring December 31, 1991, and five-year terms thereafter with such position to be designated as county post number one.
(2)
One member shall be selected from time to time by the governing authority of Tift County for an initial term expiring December 31, 1992, and five-year terms thereafter with such position to be designated as county post number two.
(3)
One member shall be selected from time to time by the governing authority of Tift County for an initial term expiring December 31, 1993, and with five-year terms thereafter with such position to be designated as county post number three.
(4)
One member shall be selected from time to time by the governing authority of Tift County for an initial term expiring December 31, 1994, and five-year terms thereafter with such position to be designated as county post number four.
(5)
One member shall be selected from time to time by the governing authority of Tift County for an initial term expiring December 31, 1995, and five-year terms thereafter with such position to be designated as county post number five.
(6)
One member shall be selected from time to time by the governing authority of the City of Tifton for an initial term expiring December 31, 1991, and five-year terms thereafter with such position to be designated as Tifton post number one.
(7)
One member shall be selected from time to time by the governing authority of the City of Tifton for an initial term expiring December 31, 1992, and five-year terms thereafter with such position to be designated as Tifton post number two.
(8)
One member shall be selected from time to time by the governing authority of the City of Tifton for an initial term expiring December 31, 1993, and five-year terms thereafter with such position to be designated as Tifton post number three.
(9)
One member shall be selected from time to time by the governing authority of the City of Tifton for an initial term expiring December 31, 1994, and five-year terms thereafter with such position to be designated as Tifton post number four.
(10)
One member shall be selected from time to time by the governing authority of the City of Tifton for an initial term expiring December 31, 1995, and five-year terms thereafter with such position to be designated as Tifton post number five.
(11)
One member shall be selected from time to time by the governing authority of the City of Omega for an initial term expiring December 31, 1995, and five-year terms thereafter with such position to be designated as Omega post number one.

(Ord. of 12-10-90, § 1.4; Ord. No. 2000-01, § 2, 3-13-00)
Sec. 2-159. - Compensation.
Members shall receive no compensation for their services except that they may be reimbursed for any out-of-pocket expenses incurred by them in direct connection with the performances of their services and duties, subject to approval thereof by the governing authorities or the governing authority of the governmental entity on behalf of which the member shall have function in accordance with such requirements as shall be imposed in connection therewith from time to time by resolutions of the governing authorities or of the governing authority of the governmental entity on behalf of which the member shall function.
(Ord. of 12-10-90, § 1.5)
Sec. 2-160. - Meetings.
Meetings of the commission shall be held from time to time as necessary for the commission to perform its duties and responsibilities, with meetings to be held at least once per month on a regularly scheduled day and at a regularly scheduled time and place, as determined from time to time by majority vote of the members of the commission. Meetings shall also be held upon call by the chairperson, or by a majority of the members. All meetings of the commission shall be open to the public and shall be held in accordance with the requirements of state law relative to open public meetings.
(Ord. of 12-10-90, § 1.6)
Sec. 2-161. - Disclosure of financial interest.
A member of the commission who:
(1)
Has a property interest in any real property affected by any proposed rezoning action pending before the commission;
(2)
Has a financial interest in any business entity which has a property interest in any real property affected by any proposed rezoning action pending before the commission; or
(3)
Has a member of the family who has any interest described in either subsection (1) or subsection (2) of this section shall, at or prior to the initial meeting of the commission at which the matter is to be considered, disclose the nature and extent of such interest in writing to the applicant and to the commission. All such disclosures shall be entered into the minutes of meetings of the commission, but in no event later than immediately following the announcement of the matter for consideration by the commission, and shall be matters of public record of both the commission and the governmental entity involved and shall be available for public inspection at any time during normal working hours.
(Ord. of 12-10-90, § 1.7)
Sec. 2-162. - Disclosure of contributions.
(a)
When any applicant for any proposed rezoning action has made, within two years immediately preceding the filing of that applicant's application for the rezoning action, gifts having in the aggregate a value of $250.00 or more to any member, it shall be the duty of the applicant or the attorney representing the applicant to disclose in writing to the commission:
(1)
The name of members to whom the gifts were made; and
(2)
An enumeration and description of each gift having a value of $250.00 or more made by the applicant to each such member during the two years immediately preceding the filing of the application for the zoning change.
(b)
The disclosures required by subsection (a) of this section shall be filed within ten days after the application for the proposed rezoning action is first filed.
(Ord. of 12-10-90, § 1.8)
Sec. 2-163. - Disclosure of relationships.
Each member who shall have any business or financial relationship with any applicant or with any owner of any property proposed for rezoning action and who, due to the nature or extent of such relationship or relationships, may not be able to objectively act with respect to the proposed rezoning action shall, at or prior to the initial meeting of the commission at which the matter is to be considered, but in no event later than immediately following the announcement of the matter for consideration by the commission, disclose the nature and extent of such relationships to the applicant and to the commission. All such disclosures shall be entered into the minutes of the commission, shall be matters of public record of both the commission and the governmental entity involved and shall be available for public inspection at any time during normal working hours.
(Ord. of 12-10-90, § 1.9)
Sec. 2-164. - Disqualification of members.
(a)
Any member who shall have a property interest, a financial interest, or a member of the family who has either a property interest or financial interest which is subject to disclosure pursuant to the provisions of this division; shall have received any contribution which is subject to disclosure pursuant to the provisions of this division; or shall have any relationship which is subject to disclosure pursuant to the provisions of this division may disqualify himself from participation in the consideration of the proposed rezoning action in issue or may be disqualified by vote of the commission, as provided in subsection (b) of this section.
(b)
Any other member, the applicant and any other interested person shall each be entitled at the time of the call for consideration of the matter or at any time prior thereto to raise any issue of possible disqualification of any member whereupon, following such consideration as the commission shall deem appropriate, the commission shall determine by majority vote of those members who are not subject to potential disqualification in connection with the proposed rezoning action whether the member shall be disqualified.
(Ord. of 12-10-90, § 1.10)
Sec. 2-165. - Duties and responsibilities.
The commission shall function only in an advisory capacity to each of the respective governing authorities. In connection therewith, the commission shall, subject to the direction, control and instructions of the respective governing authority of each governmental entity, conduct surveys and studies of existing conditions and probable future developments and shall develop plans for physical, social and economic growth which will best promote the public health, safety, morals, convenience, prosperity, general welfare, efficiency and economy in the development of the governmental entity involved and submit the same to the governing authority of each governmental entity, and shall also, subject to the direction, control and instructions of the respective governing authority of each governmental entity:
(1)
Develop and submit to the governing authority of each governmental entity for its consideration of a master plan, and amendments thereto, for the development of each governmental entity.
(2)
Develop and submit to the governing authority of each governmental entity for its consideration recommendations relative to amendments to or revisions of the zoning ordinances and zoning maps of the governmental entity.
(3)
Review proposed amendments to or revisions of the zoning ordinances and zoning maps of each of the respective governmental entities which shall be submitted by others, make findings with respect thereto, and make recommendations to the governing authority of the governmental entity involved with respect to the adoption, rejection, modification or conditional adoption of each such proposed amendment or revision.
(4)
Develop and submit to the governing authority of each governmental entity for its consideration regulations and amendments thereto for the subdivision of land.
(5)
Develop and submit to the governing authority of each governmental entity for its consideration official maps or plats, and amendments thereto, which designate the exact location of the boundary lines of existing, proposed, extended, widened and narrowed streets, public ways, public open spaces and public buildings and sites together with regulations and amendments thereto for the erection of buildings and other structures to be located therein.
(6)
Perform such additional functions and services for and on behalf of each governmental entity as shall be authorized or directed from time to time by the governing authority thereof.
(Ord. of 12-10-90, § 1.11)
Sec. 2-166. - Adoption of rules and regulations.
The commission shall adopt such rules, policies, procedures and regulations as shall not be inconsistent with the provisions of this division, the zoning ordinances of the governmental entities and applicable law.
(Ord. of 12-10-90, § 1.12)
Sec. 2-167. - Chairperson and vice-chairperson.
The commission shall annually select from among its members a chairperson who shall preside at meetings of the commission and a vice-chairperson who shall preside at meetings of the commission in the absence of the chairperson or the disqualification of the chairperson.
(Ord. of 12-10-90, § 1.13)
Sec. 2-168. - Secretaries of commission—Generally.
(a)
The secretary of the commission with respect to matters relative to Tift County and the zoning ordinance of Tift County shall be the zoning administrator of Tift County or his designee or such other person or official as shall from time to time be so designated by the county administrator of Tift County to perform the functions of secretary of the commission on behalf of Tift County or his designee.
(b)
The secretary of the commission with respect to matters relative to the City of Tifton and the zoning ordinance of the City of Tifton shall be the city clerk of the City of Tifton or his designee or such other person or official as shall from time to time be so designated by the city manager of the City of Tifton to perform the functions of secretary of the commission on behalf of the City of Tifton or his designee.
(c)
The secretary of the commission with respect to matters relative to the City of Omega and the zoning ordinance of the City of Omega shall be the zoning administrator of the City of Omega or his designee or such other person or official as shall from time to time be so designated by the mayor of the City of Omega to perform the functions of secretary of the commission on behalf of the City of Omega or his designee.
(d)
The secretary of the commission with respect to matters relative to the City of Ty Ty and the zoning ordinance of the City of Ty Ty (or his/her designee or such other person or official as shall from time to time be so designated by the mayor of the city to perform the functions of secretary of the commission on behalf of the city or his/her designee) shall be the zoning administrator of the city.
(Ord. of 12-10-90, § 1.14; Ord. No. 2000-01, § 3, 3-13-00)
Sec. 2-169. - Same—Duties.
The respective secretaries of the commission shall provide administrative assistance to the commission relative to matters relating to each of their respective governmental entities and specifically shall, with respect to matters which are relative to their respective governmental entities:
(1)
Prepare and conduct all correspondence of the commission.
(2)
Receive and file all correspondence to the commission.
(3)
Receive and file all applications and filings to and with the commission.
(4)
Receive and file all transmittals to the commission from the zoning administrator of the governmental entity and other public officials.
(5)
Review and evaluate all applications and filings to and with the commission for compliance with the rules and regulations of the commission and notify those filing the same of any noncompliance therewith.
(6)
Following consultation with the chairperson, and to the extent necessary with the other members, schedule meetings of the commission in accordance with applicable law, ordinances, resolutions and the rules and regulations of the commission.
(7)
Prepare and maintain a calendar and docket of proposed rezoning actions before the commission.
(8)
Prepare and submit to the members at least 24 hours prior to a scheduled hearing an agenda therefor as well as copies of all applications and other filings made in connection with each such proceeding.
(9)
Determine all notices to be given in connection with proceedings before the commission and cause such notices to be given to those entitled thereto.
(10)
Attend all meetings and hearings of the commission.
(11)
Cause a mechanical record to be made of all meetings and hearings of the commission relative to proposed rezoning actions by use of tape recorder or other appropriate device.
(12)
Cause to be retained and maintained a copy or a facsimile of all physical evidence presented in connection with proposed rezoning actions.
(13)
Prepare and maintain minutes of proceedings of the commission indicating therein all significant information, matters and facts pertaining thereto including specifically all motions, votes and decisions of the commission together with the vote of each member upon each such motion, vote and decision including those absent, disqualified or failing to vote thereon together with such other matters as shall be pertinent to the activities of the commission.
(14)
Cause to be retained and maintained all minutes, records, files and notices of the commission.
(15)
Attempt, at hearings of the commission with respect to proposed rezoning actions, to obtain the name and address of all persons who shall present any evidence, testimony or opinions, and make any statement to the commission or present any argument in connection with the matter before the commission.
(16)
Perform such other duties as shall be directed from time to time by the commission which is approved by the chief administrative official of the governmental entity.
(Ord. of 12-10-90, § 1.15)
Sec. 2-170. - Authority.
The commission shall have no authority or power other than as specifically provided from time to time by ordinance or resolution of the governing authorities and, without limiting the generality of the foregoing, shall have no authority, right or power to expend any funds; to direct the activities of any employee or official of any of the governmental entities, except solely the secretaries of the commission and then only to the extent specifically authorized in this division; or to contract for or on behalf of either itself or any of the governmental entities.
(Ord. of 12-10-90, § 1.16)
Sec. 2-171. - Legal assistance.
(a)
With respect to the zoning ordinance of the City of Tifton and matters relative to the City of Tifton, the city attorney of the City of Tifton shall act as the legal advisor and representative of the commission and shall render such legal assistance to the commission as shall be necessary.
(b)
With respect to the zoning ordinance of the City of Omega and matters relative to the City of Omega, the city attorney of the City of Omega shall act as the legal advisor and representative of the commission and shall render such legal assistance to the commission as shall be necessary.
(c)
With respect to the zoning ordinance of Tift County and matters relative to Tift County, the county attorney of Tift County shall act as the legal advisor and representative of the commission and shall render such legal assistance to the commission as shall be necessary.
(d)
With respect to the zoning ordinance of the City of Ty Ty and matters relative to the City of Ty Ty, the city attorney of the City of Ty Ty shall act as the legal advisor and representative of the council and shall render such legal assistance to the council as shall be necessary.
(Ord. of 12-10-90, § 1.17; Ord. No. 2000-01, § 4, 3-13-00)
Sec. 2-172. - Decisions.
Decisions of the commission as to its recommendations on applications for rezoning action shall be rendered by majority vote of members who are not disqualified to vote thereon which shall be made either at the time of the consideration thereof or at a later public meeting of the commission as to which notice has been given to those entitled thereto.
(Ord. of 12-10-90, § 1.18)
Sec. 2-173. - Minutes and records.
All minutes, upon approval thereof by the commission, and all files, dockets, calendars, physical evidence, notices and records of the commission which relate to matters with respect to each governmental entity shall be maintained in the office of the clerk or zoning administrator of each governmental entity, as determined from time to time by each governmental entity, subject to court orders relative thereto and subject to public inspection.
(Ord. of 12-10-90, § 1.19)
Sec. 2-174. - Transitional provisions.
(a)
The adoption of this division and the provisions contained in this section shall not affect or alter the status or validity of any decision or recommendation made by the Tifton-Tift County Planning and Zoning Commission either prior to or subsequent to the effective date of the ordinance from which this division derives.
(b)
All applications for rezoning action and all applications and other requests for amendments to or revisions of the zoning ordinance or zoning map of Tift County or the zoning ordinance or zoning map of the City of Tifton which shall have been filed and heard by the Tifton-Tift County Planning and Zoning Commission prior to the effective date of the ordinance from which this section derives shall continue to be considered by the Tifton-Tift County Planning and Zoning Commission in accordance with applicable current ordinances and resolutions following the effective date of the ordinance from which this section derives until such matters are finally disposed of by such body.
(c)
All applications for rezoning action and all applications and other requests for amendments to or revisions of the zoning ordinance or zoning map of Tift County or the zoning ordinance or zoning map of the City of Tifton which have been filed but not heard by the Tifton-Tift County Planning and Zoning Commission prior to the effective date of the ordinance from which this section derives as well as all other matters, other than those specified in this section, then under consideration by the Tifton-Tift County Planning and Zoning Commission shall stand by virtue hereof transferred to the commission effective as of the effective date of the ordinance from which this section derives.
(d)
All applications for rezoning action and all applications and other requests for amendments to or revisions of the zoning ordinance or zoning map of Tift County or the zoning ordinance or zoning map of the City of Tifton which are filed on or subsequent to the effective date of the ordinance from which this section derives shall be, and for all purposes shall be deemed to have been, filed with, and shall be considered by the commission.
(Ord. of 12-10-90, § 1.20)

The county, after diligent study and review, hereby joins with the other participants in finding and declaring that there is a need for an authority to function in the area of solid waste management. In conjunction with the other participants, the county hereby activates the South Georgia Regional Solid Waste Management Authority (the authority), a public corporation created as an institution of purely public charity, to perform an essential governmental function in the exercise of the power conferred upon it by this division and the Georgia Comprehensive Solid Waste Management Act as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-20 et seq.

Sec. 2-243. - Authorization of agreement among participants.
Under the authority of art. IX, sec. III, par. I of the state constitution and the Georgia Comprehensive Solid Waste Management Act as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-20 et seq., the execution, delivery and performance by the county of an agreement, dated June 13, 1994, (the agreement), between and among the county and each of the participants and the authority is hereby authorized. The execution of the agreement by the chairperson of the board of commissioners, which is hereby authorized, shall be conclusive evidence of such approval. The agreement shall provide for the activation of the authority and relationship between and among the participants, including the county, and establish on a preliminary basis the powers and duties of the authority relating to research, study, and planning for projects for the management of solid waste on behalf of the participants. Sec. 2-286. - Need established; purpose.
There is hereby determined and declared a pressing, existing and future need for a development authority to function in the county for the purpose of developing and promoting for the public good and general welfare trade, commerce, industry and employment opportunities in the county, thereby promoting the general welfare of the citizenry and in furtherance of that purpose, to issue revenue bonds which bear interest at rates fixed by the board of directors of such development authority in order to provide capital for the development of industrial facilities.
(Res. of 6-2-75)
Sec. 2-287. - Creation on authority of state law.
There is hereby activated in the county the public body, corporate and politic, known as the Development Authority of Tift County, referred to in this division as the development authority, which was created upon the adoption and approval of the development authorities law as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.
(Res. of 6-2-75)
Sec. 2-288. - Terms of board members.
The terms of the initial seven members of the initial board of directors of the development authority shall be staggered as follows: two members shall serve terms of two years, two members shall serve terms of four years, and the remaining three members shall serve terms of six years. Thereafter, their successors shall serve terms of six years. If at the end of any term of office of any director, a successor thereto shall not have been elected, then the director whose term of office shall have expired shall continue to hold this office until his successor shall be so elected.
(Res. of 6-2-75)
Sec. 2-289. - Power and authority.
The development authority is authorized to exercise any and all power and authority conferred upon it by the development authorities law as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq. as it now exists and as it might hereafter be amended or modified.
(Res. of 6-2-75)
Sec. 2-290. - Organization; execution of duties and responsibilities according to state law.
The board of directors once elected shall organize itself, carry out its duties and responsibilities and exercise its powers and prerogatives in accordance with the terms and provisions of the development authorities law, as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq. as it now exists and as it might hereafter be amended or modified.
(Res. of 6-2-75)
Sec. 2-291. - Effect of action by county upon the development authority.
The action taken by the board of commissioners of the county, as specified in this division, is not intended in any way to affect the Tift County Development Authority, including, without limitation, its existence, purpose, organization, powers or functions.
(Res. of 6-2-75)

DIVISION 12. - CODE ENFORCEMENT BOARD

Sec. 2-339 - Code enforcement board.
new
(a)
Purpose. The purpose of this article is to promote, protect, and improve the health, safety, and welfare of the citizens of the county by imposing administrative fines and noncriminal penalties for violations of the Code.
(b)
Definitions. The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Board of commissioners means the governing body of the county.
Code means zoning ordinances and resolutions, ordinances and resolutions enacting subdivision regulations, environmental ordinances and resolutions, state minimum standard codes provided for by state law, ordinances and resolutions enacted pursuant to other ordinances and resolutions regulating the development of real property, and ordinances and regulations providing for control of litter and debris, control of junked or abandoned vehicles, and control of overgrown vegetation. Notwithstanding the above, the term "code" shall not include:
(1)
Those codes and ordinances requiring a permit, unless the alleged violator has failed to secure all necessary valid permits under said codes and ordinances; or
(2)
Any local amendments to the state minimum standard codes provided for by state law that have not been adopted in conformity with state law.
Code enforcement officer means any authorized agent or employee of the county whose duty it is to ensure code compliance.
County means the County of Tift, Georgia.
County attorney means the county attorney and/or his designee.
Enforcement board means the code enforcement board of the county.
Repeat violator means someone who has a previous violation for both the same property and same ordinance violation as currently before the enforcement board.
Violation involving the health or safety of a third party means a violation that creates a legitimate concern for the health and safety of a third-party occupant of a dwelling place or that creates an immediate and substantial danger to the environment.
(c)
Board.
(1)
The enforcement board shall be composed of three members who shall serve at the pleasure of the board of commissioners. The initial appointments to the enforcement board shall be as follows: one member appointed for a term of two years and two members appointed for a term of four years each. A member may be suspended or removed for cause by a majority vote of a quorum of the board of commissioners. The board of commissioners may, with or without cause, refuse to reappoint any member of the enforcement board at the expiration of his or her term of office.
(2)
The enforcement board members shall be residents of the county.
(3)
Upon the expiration of the initial terms specified in this section, the members of the enforcement board shall serve three-year terms. There shall be no limit on number of terms served by any member on the enforcement board.
(4)
Any member of the board missing more than two consecutive meetings or failing to attend at least 75 percent of meetings in a calendar year, without good cause shown, may be removed by the board of commissioners upon recommendation of the county manager. An appointment to fill any vacancy on the enforcement board shall be for the remainder of the unexpired term of office.
(5)
The members of the enforcement board shall elect one member to serve as chairperson, one member to serve as vice-chairperson, and one member to serve as secretary. The duties of secretary may be delegated to staff.
(6)
The members shall not receive compensation for service on the enforcement board.
(d)
Powers. The enforcement board shall have the following powers and duties under the provisions of this article:
(1)
To hear and decide cases alleging violations of the Code, to the maximum extent allowed by O.C.G.A. § 36-74-1 et seq.;
(2)
To adopt rules for the conduct of its hearings, which rules shall, at a minimum, ensure that each side has an equal opportunity to present evidence and argument in support of its case:
(3)
To subpoena alleged violators and witnesses to its hearings, with the approval of the Tift County Magistrate Court or other court with jurisdiction over violations of the ordinances and Code of the County of Tift, Georgia. Subpoenas may be served by the sheriff, marshal, or constable of Tift County or by any other individual authorized by O.C.G.A. § 24-13-24 to serve subpoenas;
(4)
To subpoena evidence to its hearings in the same way as provided in subsection (c) of this section;
(5)
To take testimony under oath;
(6)
To issue orders having the force of law to command whatever steps are necessary to bring a violation into compliance;
(7)
To impose fines upon violators and the real property of violators; and
(8)
To exercise any and all other powers set forth in O.C.G.A. § 36-74-1 et seq. and this article.
(e)
Code enforcement officer.
(1)
The community development director, or his or her designee, shall be the code enforcement officer for the purpose of performing investigations and ensuring code compliance.
(2)
The code enforcement officer may not perform investigations or inspections of rental property within the county unless there is a probable cause that a violation of applicable codes exists or did exist.
a.
Conditions which may appear in plain view may form the necessary basis for probable cause.
b.
If such probable cause exists and a violation is found, the code enforcement officer is required to notify, in writing, the violator and allow a minimum of 30 days to correct the violation.
c.
If the violation is not corrected within the prescribed period of time, the code enforcement officer shall proceed by calling a hearing before the enforcement board. Repeat violators shall not be afforded additional time to correct a continuing violation.
(3)
If the code enforcement officer has substantial reason to believe a violation presents a serious threat to the public health, safety, and welfare or if the violation is irreparable or irreversible in nature, the code enforcement officer shall make a reasonable effort to notify the violator and immediately notify the enforcement board and request a hearing.
(4)
The code enforcement officer shall give violators 30 days to correct the violation before bringing the violation before the enforcement board. Repeat violators are not required to be afforded 30 days to correct a continuing violation.
(f)
Meetings and hearings.
(1)
The enforcement board shall meet on the fourth Monday of each month at a time and place determined by the enforcement board. Additional meetings may be called by the community development director or the chairman of the enforcement board. The enforcement board shall not be required to meet in any month where there is no business to conduct. A quorum of the enforcement board shall be two members. The chairperson shall preside over hearings before the enforcement board.
(2)
Notice of a scheduled hearing shall be made via hand delivery by the sheriff, code enforcement officer, or other person designated by the community development director, certified mail, or statutory overnight delivery, or by leaving the notice at the violator's usual place of residence with someone over 15 years old.
(3)
Hearings shall be open to the public and minutes shall be kept of all hearings. All testimony before the enforcement board shall be under oath and recorded. The enforcement board shall have the authority to issue subpoenas of witnesses and evidence.
a.
The community development director, his or her designated staff, or the county solicitor shall present each case before the enforcement board.
b.
Alleged violators may be represented by an attorney and provide witnesses. All costs associated with these rights will be borne by the alleged violators.
(4)
The state rules of civil procedure (The Civil Practice Act O.C.G.A. Title 9, Chapter 11) and formal rules of evidence (Evidence, O.C.G.A. Title 24) shall not apply; however, the fundamental right of due process shall be observed by the enforcement board.
(5)
At the conclusion of a hearing, the enforcement board shall issue findings of fact and conclusions of law and an order with its ruling and any proper relief as provided by law. The findings and conclusions shall be made by motion and approved by of a majority of present members. The secretary of the enforcement board shall certify the order. The certified copy of an order of the enforcement board may include notice of a specific date to perfect compliance and any applicable fine imposed. If the order is not complied with in the time designated or if the violator is a repeat violator, then the order shall be recorded with the Clerk of Superior Court of Tift County so as to constitute notice to subsequent purchasers.
(6)
All meetings and hearings are subject to the Georgia Open Meetings Act (O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1 et seq.) and shall be noticed accordingly.
(g)
Administrative fines.
(1)
The enforcement board may impose a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 per day if the violation involves the health or safety of a third party.
(2)
The enforcement board may impose a fine not to exceed a total of $1,000.00 if the violation does not involve the health or safety of a third party.
(3)
A portion of the fines levied above by the enforcement board may consist of administrative fees associated with the violation.
(4)
The enforcement board may order the violation corrected by a date certain and waive or suspend all or a portion of the fine if compliance is obtained by the specified date.
(5)
In determining the amount of administrative fine, the enforcement board shall consider:
a.
The gravity of the violation;
b.
Any actions taken by the violator to correct the violation; and
c.
Any previous violations committed by the violator.
(6)
The enforcement board may record a certified copy of an order imposing an administrative fine with the Clerk of Superior Court of Tift County. Such order shall constitute a lien against the land on which the violation exists and upon any real or personal property owned by the violator. At least three months from the filing of an order constituting a lien, the enforcement board may request that the board of commissioners foreclose on the lien. No liens shall continue for more than 20 years after the recording of the order with the clerk of court. If an order is recorded in the public records and the order is complied with by the date specified in the order, the enforcement board shall issue an order acknowledging compliance which shall be recorded in the public records. A hearing is not required to issue such an order acknowledging compliance.
(h)
Appeal. An aggrieved party, including the county, may appeal an order of the enforcement board within 30 days following the issuance of the order to the Superior Court of Tift County. Such an appeal shall be de novo.
(Ord. No. 2022-04, 12-12-22)
Sec. 2-340. - Code enforcement board hearing procedures.
new
(a)
The chairman should call the hearing to order and determine if a quorum is present. A quorum of the enforcement board shall be three members.
(b)
The chairman should clearly explain the procedures to be followed throughout the hearing.
(c)
The chairman of the enforcement board shall call the proceeding to order and for each case give a statement which includes the following:
(1)
Names of the participants and witnesses of the hearing;
(2)
Date of hearing; and
(3)
Violations/issues to be considered at the hearing.
(d)
Each party (code enforcement officer and violator) shall be allowed to give a brief opening statement. A party's opening statement is not subject to cross-examination. The enforcement board may limit the opening statement to a reasonable time depending on the complexity of the case and number of issues involved.
(e)
The enforcement board shall receive relevant evidence, and may exclude all evidence that is irrelevant, immaterial, unduly repetitious, or unduly prejudicial.
(f)
The enforcement board shall attempt to obtain the reasonably available, competent evidence necessary to resolve the issue(s) in the case.
(g)
The secretary of the enforcement board shall ensure that all exhibits are clearly marked, allow parties to review the exhibits and offer objections. The enforcement board shall ensure all exhibits are authenticated (to the extent possible) where questionable or challenged, receive all competent, relevant and reasonably available exhibits, and shall give an explanation for the denial of any of the proposed exhibits.
(h)
Parties and witnesses should be called and sworn, and the evidence developed, in a logical and efficient manner. The code enforcement officer has the burden of proof and should present their case first.
(i)
Each witness may be cross-examined, on relevant issues, by the opposing party after the witness has been questioned on direct examination.
(j)
The enforcement board must provide parties or their representatives with a timely opportunity to question their own witnesses and to cross-examine the other party's witnesses.
(k)
The parties should not ask improper leading questions on material issues during direct examination. A leading question is one which suggests the answer. Exceptions are made when a party's representative asks background questions relating to name, address, etc.
(l)
After the hearing has begun, the enforcement board should use good judgment as to allowing continuances. The enforcement board shall only grant those continuances requested by either party which are necessary, and should deny unwarranted continuances that unreasonably delay the disposition of the case.
(m)
The enforcement board should provide the parties ample opportunity to present all of the information pertinent to their case, keeping in mind the need to prevent unnecessary repetition of evidence and testimony.
(n)
The enforcement board must conduct the hearing within the scope of the issues raised by the notice, and within the issues as finally developed at the hearing, giving proper notice of new issues.
(o)
In instances where an interpreter is necessary, the enforcement board should swear in the interpreter so as to affirm that the interpreter will truthfully interpret the questions and answers supplied. The enforcement board shall require that the interpretation be word for word to the extent possible as it would be spoken in the foreign language.
(p)
The enforcement board shall verbally notify all parties of the decision at the hearing and formalize a written order within five days of the hearing. A copy of the written order shall be provided to all parties and/or their representatives by hand delivery or through certified mail.
(q)
The order should contain findings of facts, conclusions of law, and any relief granted.
(r)
The enforcement board shall conduct the hearing in a fair and impartial manner.
(s)
The enforcement board shall ensure that due process is afforded to all parties.
(Ord. No. 2022-04, 12-12-22)
