Sec. 15-15-11. - Short title; applicability.
This article shall be known and may be cited as the "Public Park Ordinance for the City of LaGrange" and shall be applicable to and enforced in all parks, playgrounds, recreation centers and any other area in the city owned or used by the city and devoted to active or passive recreation, including but in no way limited to the following:
(1)
Granger Park located on the west side of North Greenwood Street;
(2)
East Side Park located on the east side of Daniel and the west side of Niles Street;
(3)
West Side Park located at Jones Street and Boatwright Street;
(4)
Edgewood and Guinn Street Park located at the intersection of Edgewood Street and Guinn Street;
(5)
Easy Street Park located on Easy Street;
(6)
George Harris Baseball Complex located on Ragland Street;
(7)
Shuford Softball Complex located on Calumet Drive;
(8)
Lindsey Street Park located behind 217 Lindsey Street;
(9)
Dunson Field located on Hogansville Road;
(10)
Calumet Park located on the south side of Hines Street;
(11)
Jackson Street Park located on Jackson Street;
(12)
McCluskey Tennis Center located on Hunnicutt Place;
(13)
Mike Daniel Recreation Center located on Lafayette Parkway;
(14)
Union Street Park located on the corner of Union and Hamilton Street;
(15)
William J. Griggs Recreation Center located on Glenn Robertson Drive;
(16)
Whitesville Road Soccer Complex located on Whitesville Road; and
(17)
Chris Joseph Track and Field and Soccer Complex located on Fort Drive.
(Ord. No. 17-05, § 1, 5-9-17)
Sec. 15-15-12. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
City means the City of LaGrange.
Department means the Troup County Parks and Recreation Department
Recreation facility(ies) means any active or passive recreation area, nature area or amenity owned by the city.
(Ord. No. 17-05, § 1, 5-9-17)
Sec. 15-15-13. - Hours of operation; rules of operation.
All parks in the city shall close at 9:00 p.m. and shall be closed and remain closed until 6:00 a.m. on the succeeding morning. Only those activities authorized in writing by an authorized agent of the Troup County Recreation Commission, or the city manager or designated representative, shall be authorized beyond 9:00 p.m. No person shall be in, on, or around a park after dusk until the opening hour of 6:00 a.m., except in attendance at a scheduled activity authorized by this section.
(1)
Notwithstanding the park operating hours established herein, the city manager or department representative may establish different opening and/or closing times for any recreation facility or area owned or maintained by the city, which times shall be posted in a conspicuous location(s) in or around the facility or area regulated.
(2)
There shall be no use of the recreation facility or area affected after the posted closing time. No one shall be allowed on the subject property and there shall be no parking or congregating on the public streets, or parking areas around the property.
(3)
Rules and regulations specific to each recreation facility shall be posted by the city and/or the department in a conspicuous location. Users of each recreation facility shall comply with said rules and regulations as adopted by the city council, the department or as identified in facility use agreements with specific user groups.
(Ord. No. 17-05, § 1, 5-9-17)
Sec. 15-15-14. - Prohibited acts.
(a)
No person in a park shall:
(1)
Willfully mark, deface, disfigure, injure, tamper with or deflate or remove any building, bridges, tables, benches, railings, water lines or other public utilities or parts or appurtenances thereto, signs, notices, whether temporary or permanent, or other structures or equipment, facilities or park property or appurtenances thereto, whether real or personal;
(2)
Damage, cut, carve, transplant or remove any tree, plant or shrubbery;
(3)
Bring in or dump, deposit or leave any bottles, broken glass, ashes, paper, boxes, cans, dirt, rubbish, trash, garbage, refuse or other waste. If any receptacles are provided, rubbish and other waste shall be deposited in the proper receptacles, but if receptacles are not provided, all such rubbish and other waste shall be carried away from the park by the person responsible for its presence and properly disposed of elsewhere.
(4)
Aggressive horseplay and/or inappropriate behavior will not be tolerated and is cause for removal from said venue.
(5)
Glass containers of any kind are prohibited.
(6)
Outside cooking equipment is prohibited from being brought into public parks.
(7)
Open flames, other than those produced by grills or similar cooking devices specifically mounted and provided as part of the design of these parks, are prohibited.
(8)
The use of tobacco products is specifically prohibited.
(9)
The use of motorcycles, dirt bikes, E-bikes, ATV's or motorized vehicles of any kind or similar vehicles designed for off-road use are prohibited within public parks. Motorized wheelchairs are permitted.
(10)
No bounce houses are permitted within public parks. No tents or other structures are permitted within public parks unless specifically authorized in advance by the city manager.
(11)
No organized event having more than fifty (50) attendees will be permitted to take place within a public park unless a certified peace officer is hired to provide security for the duration of said event and an official permit has been issued to the user.
(b)
Each person using a park shall:
(1)
Comply with the applicable provisions of the state motor vehicle code in regard to the equipment required and the operation of motor vehicles, together with such regulations as are contained in the ordinances of the city;
(2)
Obey all police officers, traffic officers and park employees, who are specifically by this article authorized to direct traffic whenever and wherever needed in the park and on the streets adjacent thereto;
(3)
Carefully observe all traffic signs indicating speed, direction, caution, stopping or parking; and
(4)
Not drive any vehicle on any paved recreational surface or any other park area except the paved park roads, parking areas, or such other areas as may on occasion be specifically designated as temporary parking areas by the person in charge of such park.
(Ord. No. 17-05, § 1, 5-9-17; Ord. No. 22-08, §§ 1, 2, 5-24-22)
Sec. 15-15-15. - Reserved.
Sec. 15-15-16. - Swimming.
No person in a park shall bathe or swim except in areas designated specifically for that purpose and then only within the hours when such activity is authorized.
(Ord. No. 17-05, § 1, 5-9-17)
Sec. 15-15-17. - Alcoholic beverages.
The use, possession, purchase or sale within a park in the city of alcoholic beverages or malt beverages is prohibited at any time. No person shall enter or be in a park in the city while under the influence of intoxicating liquors or malt beverages.
(Ord. No. 17-05, § 1, 5-9-17)
Sec. 15-15-18. - Pollution or disturbance of springs, ponds.
It shall be unlawful for any person to pollute or disturb any spring, branch, pond or other water within the confines of any of the recreation facilities belonging to the city by throwing sticks, stones, refuse or trash in or by washing the person or objects in such waters.
(Ord. No. 17-05, § 1, 5-9-17)
Sec. 15-15-19. - Unreasonable disturbances.
It shall be unlawful for any person to handle or discharge any firecracker, airgun, slingshot or other explosive, noisy or destructive article or substance, or throw any stone or other missile in, about or across any of the recreation facilities of the city.
No person at any time shall disturb or interfere unreasonably with any person or party occupying any area or participating in any activity authorized by the city or by the Troup County Recreation Commission.
(Ord. No. 17-05, § 1, 5-9-17)
Sec. 15-15-20. - Sales from stands, carts, vehicles.
No person in a park shall expose or offer for sale any article or thing nor shall he station or place any stand, cart or vehicle for the transportation, sale or display of any such article or thing except and unless such person is operating as a licensed concessionaire, licensed by the city and/or the Troup County Recreation Commission.
(Ord. No. 17-05, § 1, 5-9-17)
Sec. 15-15-21. - Animals in the parks.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person who harbors, possesses or is in charge of a domestic, restrained or unrestrained animal (such as dogs, cats, etc.) to bring said animal onto parks, tennis courts, playgrounds and outdoor basketball courts. The only exception to this will be animal training classes that are approved by and scheduled through the department as pre-approved areas.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to ride, graze or walk a horse or other type hoofed animal within a recreation facility without obtaining permission from the department.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person who possesses, harbors, or is in charge of any animal not to immediately remove excrement deposited by the animal while in the recreation facility. Animal excrement shall be removed from the facility and disposed from the recreation facility and disposed of in a sanitary means.
(Ord. No. 17-05, § 1, 5-9-17)

ARTICLE III. - RULES AND REGULATIONS FOR THE THREAD

Sec. 15-15-50. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
The Thread means that certain trail or path, or any portion thereof, designated by the mayor and city council for pleasure or for use as an alternative mode of transportation within the city and includes uses for walking, jogging, running and bicycling.
(Ord. No. 17-04, § 1, 5-9-17)
Sec. 15-15-51. - Motorized vehicles prohibited.
With the exception of motorized wheelchairs, no person shall operate a motorized vehicle in, over, through or upon The Thread or any portion thereof, except when specifically authorized by law.
(Ord. No. 17-04, § 1, 5-9-17)
Sec. 15-15-52. - Horseback riding prohibited, except in designated areas.
No person shall ride a horse in, over, through or upon The Thread, except in areas designated for such use, and where permitted, no person shall ride a horse in a reckless manner or in any other manner which endangers other users of The Thread.
(Ord. No. 17-04, § 1, 5-9-17)
Sec. 15-15-53. - Operation of bicycles.
No person shall operate a bicycle on The Thread in a reckless manner or in any other manner which endangers other users of The Thread.
(Ord. No. 17-04, § 1, 5-9-17)
Sec. 15-15-54. - Animals.
No person shall bring any animal on The Thread, except when such animal is under the direction and control of its owner and is secured by a leash, chain, or lead of sufficient strength to prevent it from escaping from its owner. As used in this section, the term "animal" shall include any type of animal, including, but not limited to, dogs, cats, and horses.
(Ord. No. 17-04, § 1, 5-9-17)
Sec. 15-15-55. - Climbing bridges, trestles, etc.
No person shall climb the exterior of, repel from, or jump from any bridge, trestle, overpass, wall, cliff, embankment, building, or monument which is located on or adjacent to The Thread.
(Ord. No. 17-04, § 1, 5-9-17)
Sec. 15-15-56. - Hours of operation.
The Thread is open from dawn until dusk each day, and it shall be unlawful for any person to be on The Thread before sunrise or after sunset, unless otherwise and specifically authorized by law.
(Ord. No. 17-04, § 1, 5-9-17)
Sec. 15-15-57. - Alcoholic beverages and drugs prohibited.
It shall be unlawful to possess any alcoholic beverages or illegal drugs on The Thread.
Sec. 15-15-58. - Vandalism or theft of trail amenities.
It shall be unlawful to vandalize, mutilate, deface, or steal any portion of The Thread or any amenities thereto.
(Ord. No. 17-04, § 1, 5-9-17)
Sec. 15-15-59. - Penalty for violations.
Any person violating any provision of this chapter shall, upon conviction thereof, be punished as provided in section 1-1-6.
(Ord. No. 17-04, § 1, 5-9-17)
