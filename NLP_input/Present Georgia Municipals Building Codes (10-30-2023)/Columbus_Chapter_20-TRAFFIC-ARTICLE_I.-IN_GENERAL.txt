Sec. 20-01. - Adoption by reference—Title 68A, "The Uniform Rules of the Road."

Sec. 20-1. - Definitions.
Commercial construction equipment. Any wheeled or tracked construction equipment designed primarily for off road (non-licensed) use and designed, altered, maintained and used to handle, load/unload or manipulate earthwork, construction materials/debris and raw timber materials for hire and meeting any of the following conditions:
(1)
Having a gross vehicle weight rating (GVWR) of 8,000 pounds or more,
(2)
Having an overall length of 20 feet or more,
(3)
Having an overall height of eight feet six inches or more.
Curb loading zone. A space adjacent to a curb reserved for the exclusive use of vehicles during the loading or unloading of passengers or materials.
Curb. The edge of a public street, whether marked or understood.
Large commercial trailers. Any trailer that is designed, altered, maintained and used to transport or handle materials, supplies, cargo, vehicles or people for hire and meeting any of the following conditions:
(1)
Having a gross vehicle weight rating (GVWR) of 10,000 pounds or more.
(2)
Having an overall length of 25 feet or more.
(3)
Having an overall height of eight feet six inches or more.
(4)
Having two or more axles with more than two wheels per axle.
Large commercial vehicles. Any vehicle that is designed, altered, maintained and used to transport or handle materials, supplies, cargo, vehicles or people for hire and meeting any of the following conditions:
(1)
Having a gross vehicle weight rating (GVWR) of 14,000 pounds or more,
(2)
Having an overall length of 25 feet or more,
(3)
Having an overall height of eight feet six inches or more.
Limit lines. Lines on pavement defining ranking or parking spaces, safety, danger, directing, neutral zones and crosswalks. Where a certain hour is specified herein for the performance or nonperformance of an act, eastern standard time or other "time" then in use in the city, is intended.
Parking pad. An area having a minimum parking space dimension of ten feet by 20 feet established for the purpose of parking a vehicle on private property constructed of a material capable of supporting the weight of said vehicle. Examples of suitable material are a well established grass turf, compacted soil, man-made matting, gravel, pavers, asphalt, concrete, etc.
Passenger curb loading zone. A place adjacent to a curb reserved for the exclusive use of vehicles during the loading or unloading of passengers.
Private road or driveway. Every way or place in private ownership and used for vehicular travel by the owner and those having express or implied permission from the owner, but not by other persons.
Rank. To place vehicles parallel to the curb.
Residential driveway. A driving area/roadway having a minimum width of nine feet beginning at the edge of a public roadway extending across public right-of-way onto private property terminating at a parking pad having minimum dimension of 10 feet by 20 feet. The portion of said driveway located on public right-of-way shall be paved in accordance with City Code sections 18-35 through 18-43. The portion of said driveway located on private property is not required to be paved within residential districts.
Small commercial trailers. Any trailer that is designed, altered, maintained and used to transport or handle materials, supplies, cargo, vehicles or people for hire and meeting any of the following conditions:
(1)
Having a gross vehicle weight rating (GVWR) of less than 10,000 pounds,
(2)
Having an overall length of less than 25 feet,
(3)
Having an overall height of less than eight feet six inches,
(4)
Having one or two axles with only two wheels per axle.
Small commercial vehicles. Any vehicle or wheeled/tracked construction equipment that is designed, altered, maintained and used to transport or handle materials, supplies, cargo, vehicles or people for hire and meeting any of the following conditions:
(1)
Having a gross vehicle weight rating (GVWR) of less than 14,000 pounds,
(2)
Having an overall length of less than 25 feet,
(3)
Having an overall height of less than eight feet six inches.
Traffic division. The traffic division of the police department of this city, or in the event a traffic division is not established, then said term whenever used herein shall be deemed to refer to the police department of this city.
For other definitions, refer to Georgia Code Annotated, Section 68A-101 of the Uniform Rules of the Road.
(Ord. No. 71-198, § 1, 7-20-71; Ord. No. 73-165, § 1, 12-4-73; Ord. No. 75-48, § 1, 6-10-75; Ord. No. 78-65, § 5, 5-23-78; Ord. No. 87-126, § 1, 9-8-87; Ord. No. 99-52, § I, 8-10-99)
Sec. 20-1.1. - Driving past police traffic control vehicles.
It shall be unlawful, for the mere purpose of driving and without having any immediate destination, to drive a motor vehicle on the streets of Columbus, Georgia, past an identifiable law enforcement vehicle, placed for traffic control purposes, more than two times per hour traveling in the same direction; the violation shall occur when the same motor vehicle passes by the police control vehicle for the third time, and the driver of the motor vehicle at that time will be subject to citation.
(Ord. No. 93-40, 6-1-93)
Editor's note— Ordinance No. 93-40, adopted June 1, 1993, did not specifically amend this Code; hence, inclusion as § 20-1.1 was at the discretion of the editor.
