ARTICLE IV. - BILLIARDS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 22-256. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Billiard room means any public place where a person is permitted to play the game of billiards and for which a charge is made for use of equipment.
Billiards means any of the several games played on a table surrounded by an elastic ledge of cushions with balls which are impelled by a cue and shall include all forms of the game known as "carom billiards," "pocket billiards" and "English billiards."
(Code 1988, § 9-7-1)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 22-257. - Alcoholic beverages prohibited.
Alcoholic beverages shall be prohibited from being sold, served or allowed to be used in or on the premises of billiard rooms or any place operated in connection therewith unless such premises or establishment is an establishment which is authorized by the city to sell alcoholic beverages and derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross revenues from the sale of products or services other than alcoholic beverages. Alcoholic beverages shall be prohibited from being sold, served or consumed on the premises of a billiard room or establishment connected therewith in any establishment authorized by the city to sell alcoholic beverages by the drink for consumption on the premises unless the establishment derives at least 75 percent of its revenue from the sale of products or services other than alcoholic beverages.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-3)
Sec. 22-258. - Exemptions.
This article shall not apply to billiard tables or billiard rooms operated by private industrial concerns, Young Men's Christian Associations, religious orders, charitable institutions, state, county or city institutions, fraternal orders or bona fide clubs using such tables for members or employees only.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-4)
Sec. 22-259. - Health and sanitation.
All billiard rooms shall be kept in a clean, sanitary condition and shall comply with all sanitary rules and regulations of the board of human resources and of the county health department.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-7)
Sec. 22-260. - Hours of operation.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a billiard room between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 6:00 a.m. or to permit any person to remain in such billiard room between such prohibited hours, except regular employees performing necessary labor on the premises.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-8)
Sec. 22-261. - Obstruction of vision.
No person who operates a billiard room shall permit any screens, curtains, blinds, partitions or other obstructions to be used to prevent a clear view of the interior, which clear view must be maintained at all times. No partitions forming rooms, stalls or other enclosures shall be permitted; however, this section shall not be construed to prohibit the maintenance of washrooms and toilet rooms for proper purposes nor the maintenance of closets for storage purposes exclusively.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-9)
Sec. 22-262. - Gambling.
No dice, cards, dominoes or other games of chance shall be permitted, nor any form of gambling be allowed, in any billiard room or in any other business place operated in connection therewith.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-10)

DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 22-286. - Required.
No person shall operate a billiard room within the city unless the business establishment wherein the billiard room is located has a valid occupational tax certificate.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-2)
Sec. 22-287. - Minimum qualifications.
No license to operate a business which operates or contains a billiard room shall be issued, renewed or remain valid for any person who is not 21 years of age and a citizen of the United States or who has been convicted of a felony.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-5)
Sec. 22-288. - Required affidavit.
Every applicant for a license under this division or licensee requesting renewal of an existing license to operate a business wherein a billiard room will be operated shall provide to the city clerk an affidavit, sworn to before an officer authorized by law to administer oaths, stating that he:
(1)
Is a citizen of the United States;
(2)
Has not been convicted of a felony;
(3)
Will not permit vagrants or persons under the influence of intoxicating liquors or controlled substances to enter or play billiards in his business establishment;
(4)
Will have personal charge and management of the business;
(5)
Will not permit gambling nor permit the billiard tables to be used in any manner other than as provided by law.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-6)
Secs. 22-289—22-320. - Reserved.
