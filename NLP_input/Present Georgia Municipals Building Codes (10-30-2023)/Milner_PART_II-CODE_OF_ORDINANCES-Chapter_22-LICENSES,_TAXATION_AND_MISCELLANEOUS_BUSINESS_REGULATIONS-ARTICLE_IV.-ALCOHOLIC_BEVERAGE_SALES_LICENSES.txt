ARTICLE IV. - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGE SALES LICENSES

Sec. 22-70. - Definitions.
The following terms and words are hereby defined and shall be interpreted as follows:
Beer shall mean any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than six percent alcohol by volume and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer, and strong beer. The word does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine. The term "beer" shall be synonymous with "malt beverage."
City shall mean the City of Milner, Georgia.
Commercial district shall mean any zoning district in the city starting with the letter "C".
Corporation shall include both "S" corporation and "C" corporations.
Licensee shall mean the individual to whom a license is issued or, in the case of a partnership or corporation, all partners of the partnership and all officers and directors of the corporation.
Partnership shall include a general partnership, limited partnership, limited liability partnership, and limited liability limited partnership.
Premises shall mean the building described in the application for a license issued hereunder to any licensee; provided, however, this definition shall not include the property or grounds upon which said building is located.
Retail beer dealer shall mean and include every person, firm, corporation or association that engages in selling or handling of beer, as hereinabove defined, at retail within the city.
Retail establishment shall mean a convenience store, grocery store, pharmacy.
Retail wine dealer shall mean and include every person, firm, corporation or association that engages in selling or handling wines, as hereinabove defined, at retail within the city.
Wholesale beer dealer shall mean and include every person, firm, corporation or association that sells and/or deals in beer, as hereinabove defined, to a retail beer dealer, as defined above, to or within the city.
Wholesale wine dealer shall mean and include every person, firm, corporation or association that sells and/or deals in wine, as hereinabove defined, to a retail wine dealer, as defined above, to or within the city.
Wine shall mean any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products. The term does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in hereinabove.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-71. - Requirement of license.
Beer and wine may be sold or distributed in the city only under a license granted by the city, upon the terms and conditions provided in this article. The city is empowered to grant the following classes of licenses:
(1)
License for the retail sale of beer only;
(2)
License for the retail sale of wine only;
(3)
Combination license for the retail sale of beer and wine;
(4)
License for the wholesale sale and distribution of beer only;
(5)
License for the wholesale sale and distribution of wine only; and
(6)
Combination license for the wholesale sale and distribution of beer and wine.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-72. - Application for sale or distribution.
All persons desiring to sell or distribute beer and/or wine shall make application on the forms prescribed by the city. The content of all applications shall be sworn to by the applicant before a notary public or other officer qualified to administer oaths.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-73. - Location requirements.
No license shall be granted hereunder to any person unless the premises of the proposed location is situated:
(1)
300 feet or more from the property line of any church, synagogue, shrine, or other place used exclusively for religious services, including the chapel of a mortuary or cemetery; or
(2)
300 feet or more from the property line of any school ground or college campus. The schools and colleges referred to herein shall include only such state, county, city, church, or other schools as teach the subjects commonly taught in the common schools and colleges of this state, and shall not include schools or colleges wherein only specialized subjects such as law, stenography, business, music, art, medicine, dentistry, vocational occupations, karate and other martial arts, performing arts, and other special subjects are taught; or
(3)
100 feet or more from the property line of any private residence, unless such residence is itself in a commercial district; or
(4)
300 feet or more from the property line of an alcoholic treatment center.
(5)
100 feet or more from the property line of any branch of any public library.
(6)
For purposes of this section, distances shall be measured by the most direct route which provides the closest and most reasonable means of travel on the ground to the front door entrance of the licensee.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008; Res. of 8-20-2008, §§ 1, 2)
Sec. 22-74. - Location limited to commercial district.
No license shall be granted hereunder to any person unless the premises of the proposed location is situated in a commercial zoning district.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-75. - Visibility of entrance.
No license shall be granted hereunder to a retail beer dealer or retail wine dealer or retail beer and wine dealer unless the front entrance to the premises is clearly visible from a public street.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-76. - Off-premises consumption required.
No license shall be granted hereunder to any person except for the sale or distribution of beer and/or wine in sealed containers and for consumption off the premises.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-77. - Retail sales required.
No license shall be granted hereunder to any person for the retail sale of beer and/or wine unless the premises of the proposed location is a retail establishment which derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross sales from the sale of staple foodstuffs, household supplies, meats, produce and dairy products.
It shall be the responsibility of the licensee to ensure that this requirement is met on an annual basis and to maintain all records necessary for a validation thereof by designated city officials. A licensee's failure to maintain the required sales ratio shall constitute grounds for the revocation of such license. A licensee's failure to maintain sales and financial records sufficient to show compliance with the sales ratio required by this section shall also constitute grounds for the revocation of such license.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-78. - Age/criminal record of applicant.
Any applicant for a license under this article shall be 25 years of age or older, an American citizen or an alien lawfully admitted for permanent residence, and have resided in the State of Georgia for a period of one year. No applicant shall have been convicted of, pled guilty to or pled nolo contendere to any felony or misdemeanor charge, other than traffic offenses, within ten years of the date of submission of the application, nor shall any such applicant have been incarcerated, on probation or on parole within five years preceding the date of submission of the application as a result of any felony conviction, plea of guilty or plea of nolo contendere. Where the applicant is a partnership or corporation, the requirements of this section shall apply to all of the applicant's partners, officers, directors, and majority shareholders.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-79. - Application contents.
The license application shall include, but not be limited to, the name and address of the applicant; the proposed business location; the type of business to be conducted; if a partnership, the names and residence addresses of the partners; and if a corporation, the names of the directors, officers, and shareholders, the name and address of the corporation's agent for service of process, and the name and address of any other person or entity having a financial interest in the entity which is to own or operate the establishment for which a license is sought.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-80. - Surveyor certification.
All original applications for new licenses shall include a certificate from a registered surveyor showing a scale drawing of the location of the proposed or existing premises, and stating both the straight line distance and the distance measured along the closest and most reasonable public access from the front door of the proposed or existing premises of the licensee to the building and property line of the nearest church, school, library, alcoholic treatment center, and private residence.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-81. - Compliance of new commercial structure required.
Where a building intended for the sale of beer and/or wine is at the time of the application for such license not in existence or not yet completed, a license may be issued for such location, provided the plans for the proposed building show clear compliance with all provisions of this article and applicable provisions of the City Code. No sales shall be allowed in such establishment, however, until it has been completed in accordance with such plans.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-82. - Application fee.
Each application for a retail beer dealer license or retail wine dealer license or combination retail beer dealer and retail wine dealer license shall be accompanied by a nonrefundable application fee in the amount of $250.00.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-83. - State license required.
(a)
A wholesale beer dealer license or wholesale wine dealer license or combination wholesale beer dealer and wholesale wine dealer license shall be issued only to those persons who are licensed by the State of Georgia to sell and distribute beer or wine or both, as a wholesale dealer of such products.
(b)
A retail beer dealer license or retail wine dealer license or combination retail beer dealer and retail wine dealer license shall be issued only to those persons who are licensed by the State of Georgia to sell and distribute beer or wine or both, as a retail dealer of such products.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-84. - Fingerprints.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2(c), each license application shall be accompanied by a complete set of the applicant's fingerprints.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-85. - Investigation of applicant/applicant consent thereto.
After receipt of an application in proper form, the city shall direct an investigation to verify the contents of the application and ensure conformance with the provisions of this article. The applicant shall be given written notice of any additional data which may be required to facilitate or complete the verification process. The city manager shall prepare or direct the preparation of a report and recommendation and deliver same to the city council for its consideration.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-86. - Procedure for approval.
After all procedures called for under this ordinance have been completed, the application shall be administratively reviewed by the city council. If it is determined that the applicant meets all standards and requirements of this article, then the license applied for shall be granted upon payment of the applicable license fee(s), unless some specific cause related to location, traffic condition, or other environmental condition shall justify refusal, and the city shall deliver by mail to the applicant a writing evidencing such approval. If it is determined that the application fails to meet all standards and requirements of this article, it shall be rejected and the applicant shall be notified by mail of the rejection stating the reasons therefore.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-87. - Issuance of license to corporation.
Where an applicant is a corporation, the license shall be issued jointly to the corporation and to the majority shareholder, if an individual. Where the majority shareholder is not an individual, the license shall be issued jointly to the corporation and its president, as evidenced in the corporation's articles of incorporation. Where an applicant is a limited liability company, the license shall be issued to one of the company's members or managers. Where an applicant is a partnership, the license shall be issued to one of the partners, if an individual.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-88. - Corporation registered agent in city required.
All licensees shall have and continuously maintain in the city a registered agent upon whom any required process and/or notice may be served. The licensee shall file the name of such agent along with the written consent of such agent with the city manager.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-89. - Request for hearing.
Within 30 days of the city council's written decision to deny an application under this article, the applicant may file a request for a hearing before the city council to present evidence and cross-examine opposing witnesses.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-90. - Restriction on submittal of subsequent applications.
An unsuccessful applicant for a license under this article may not submit a new application for the same location for 12 months after the date of the original application. An unsuccessful applicant may submit a new application for a different location at any time after final rejection of the prior application. No applicant shall have more than one application outstanding at the same time.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-91. - Annual license fees.
All licenses issued shall be for the calendar year and shall be paid in accordance with the following provisions:
(1)
The fee for a retail beer dealer license issued prior to or on July 1 shall be $500.00. The prorated fee for a retail beer dealer license issued after July 1 shall be $300.00.
(2)
The fee for a wholesale beer dealer license issued prior to or on July 1 shall be $500.00. The prorated fee for a wholesale beer dealer license issued after July 1 shall be $300.00.
(3)
The fee for a retail wine dealer license issued prior to or on July 1 shall be $500.00. The prorated fee for a retail wine deal license issued after July 1 shall be $300.00.
(4)
The fee for a wholesale wine dealer license issued prior to or on July 1 shall be $500.00. The prorated fee for a wholesale wine dealer license issued after July 1 shall be $300.00.
(5)
The fee for a combination retail beer dealer and retail wine dealer license issued prior to or on July 1 shall be $750.00. The prorated fee for a combination retail beer dealer and retail wine dealer license issued after July 1 shall be $450.00.
(6)
The fee for a combination wholesale beer dealer and wholesale wine dealer license issued prior to or on July 1 shall be $750.00. The prorated fee for a combination wholesale beer dealer and wholesale wine dealer license issued after July 1 shall be $450.00.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-92. - License expiration.
All licenses issued hereunder shall expire on December 31 of each year unless renewed as hereinafter provided.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-93. - Renewal application.
(a)
Licensees who desire to renew their licenses shall file an application and submit the requisite license fees to the city manager on the form prescribed by the city manager. Applications for renewal shall be filed before December 1 of each year. Renewal applications shall be reviewed by the city manager and upon investigation if there is no change in the ownership of licensee and no adverse reports from those agencies or departments required to inspect the premises of the licensee, the city manager shall issue a renewal license for the ensuing year, upon payment of the applicable license fee(s); and
(b)
No new or renewal licenses shall be issued to any applicant who is delinquent in the payment of city taxes or fees, including, but not limited to, property taxes, alcoholic beverage excise taxes as hereinafter set forth, business license fees, or any other taxes or fees which are owned to the city by the applicant.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-94. - Excise tax.
(a)
There is hereby imposed upon and levied against every licensed wholesale beer dealer an excise tax, in addition to the excise taxes levied by the state, as follows:
(1)
Where malt beverages, commonly known as tap or draft beer, are sold in or from a barrel or bulk container, a tax of $6.00 on each container sold containing not more than 15½ gallons, and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 15½ gallons;
(2)
Where malt beverages are sold in bottles, cans, or other containers, except barrel or bulk containers, a tax of $0.05 per 12 ounces, and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 12 ounces.
(b)
The excise tax levied and imposed in this section with respect to malt beverages sold or distributed by such wholesalers in the city to retailers doing business in the city shall be paid by such licensed wholesale dealers to the city on or before the tenth day of the month following the calendar month in which the beverages are sold or disposed of by such licensed wholesale dealer. Every licensee responsible for the payment of the excise tax shall file a report with the city itemizing for the preceding calendar month the exact quantities of malt beverages, by size and type of container, sold during the month within the city.
(c)
There is hereby imposed upon and levied against every licensed wholesale wine dealer an excise tax, in addition to the excise taxes levied by the state, a tax of $0.22 per liter, and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter.
(d)
The excise tax levied and imposed in this section with respect to wine sold or distributed by such wholesalers in the city to retailers doing business in the city shall be paid by such licensed wholesale dealers to the city on or before the tenth day of the month following the calendar month in which the wine is sold or disposed of by such licensed wholesale dealer. Every licensee responsible for the payment of the excise tax imposed by Section 23 shall file a report with the city manager itemizing for the preceding calendar month the exact quantities of wine, by size and type of container, sold during the month within the city.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-95. - Hours of sale and/or delivery.
(a)
Retail beer and/or retail wine dealers shall not engage in the sale of beer and/or wine except between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 11:45 p.m., Monday through Saturday;
(b)
It shall be unlawful to make wholesale deliveries of beer and/or wine except between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 7:00 p.m., Monday through Saturday; and
(c)
The retail sale of beer and/or wine is authorized on any election days.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-96. - Limitation on signage.
No sign of any kind, painted or electronic, advertising the sale of beer or wine shall be permitted on the exterior of any licensed premises. No placard or sign of any kind which is visible from the exterior of the licensed premises shall make reference to the sale of beer or wine.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-97. - Limitation on license transfer.
(a)
All licenses issued hereunder shall be personal to the licensee. No license shall be transferable, except as otherwise provided in this article.
(b)
In case of the death of a licensee, the establishment shall be allowed to continue to sell alcoholic beverages for a period of 45 days from the date of death or until expiration of the license or until approval of a new licensee, whichever shall first occur.
(c)
If a licensee severs his association with a licensed establishment, the establishment may continue to sell alcoholic beverages for a period of 45 days from the date determined to be the date of severance or until expiration of the license or until approval of a new licensee, which shall first occur. This section shall not apply, however, to the withdrawal of one or more partners licensees from a partnership in favor of one or more partners who were partners at the time of the issuance of the license.
(d)
A license issued hereunder shall be revoked by operation of law whenever partners are added to a partnership who were not partners of the partnership at the time of the issuance of the license, members or managers of a limited liability company ("LLC") are added to the LLC who were not members or managers of the LLC at the time of the issuance of the license, or officers, directors, or majority shareholders are added to a corporation who were not officers, directors, or majority shareholders of the corporation at the time of the issuance of the license.
(e)
No transfer of a license to a new location shall be permitted where such move is made other than as a result of the exercise of eminent domain. Any change which is a result of the exercise of eminent domain shall authorize the transfer of a license to a new location, provided that such location meets all the qualifying criteria for a location in an initial application.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-98. - License as privilege.
Any license issued hereunder shall be a mere grant of the privilege, and not a right, to carry on such licensed business during the term of such license or permit, subject to compliance with all the terms and conditions imposed by this article, or imposed by any other ordinance, resolution, or code provision of the City of Milner, Georgia; and any such license shall be subject to revocation or suspension at any time or times by the governing authority of the city without the refund of any license fee paid by the licensee; and the violation of any provision of this article shall be grounds for immediate revocation or suspension of any license or permit issued hereunder.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-99. - Revocation by state.
Whenever the state shall revoke any license of any licensee to sell at wholesale or retail any alcoholic beverages, the city license to sell alcoholic beverages shall thereupon be automatically revoked without any action by the city council or any city officer.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-100. - Revocation by city.
The city council may suspend or revoke a license issued hereunder upon a finding after a hearing before the city council that the licensee, or the licensee's agent or employee, acting within the scope of his employment, violated any provision of this article or violated any law, state or federal, pertaining to the conduct of the licensee's business. The city shall give the licensee at least three days' written notice of a hearing conducted pursuant to this section. Hearings under this section shall be transcribed by a court reporter, whose services shall be paid for by the licensee. The licensee shall have the right to cross-examine witnesses. After hearing the evidence, the city council shall decide by majority vote whether a license should be suspended or revoked.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Sec. 22-101. - No refund of fee upon revocation.
Where a license issued hereunder is revoked pursuant to any provision of this article, the licensee shall not be entitled to any refund of license fees.
(Ord. No. 08-5, § 2, 6-11-2008)
Secs. 22-102—22-129. - Reserved.
