ARTICLE XIX. - NEPOTISM[5]

Footnotes:
--- (5) ---
Editor's note— Section 1 of Ord. No. 86-16, enacted Feb. 25, 1986, amended Ch. 16B by adding thereto a new article pertaining to nepotism. Designation of such provisions as Art. XIX, §§ 16B-19-1—16B-19-6, is at the discretion of the editor.

Sec. 16B-19-1. - Purpose.
The purpose of this article is to establish policy and guidelines for department heads and the personnel division to be followed during the hiring process for the consolidated government.
(Ord. No. 86-16, § 1(1), 2-25-86)
Sec. 16B-19-2. - Definitions.
For the purpose of this article, the following words and terms shall have the meanings respectively ascribed.
Immediate family. For purposes of this policy, immediate family includes the employee's spouse, child, stepchild, parents, stepmother, mother-in-law, stepfather, father-in-law, brother, stepbrother, sister, stepsister, brother-in-law, sister-in-law, grandparent, uncle, aunt, niece, nephew, and first cousin.
Supervisor. To supervise implies the routine assignment of work and an evaluation of the results; involvement in the approval process for overtime, vacation and sick leave, and other decisions and recommendations related to the work environment.
(Ord. No. 86-16, § 1(2), 2-25-86)
Sec. 16B-19-3. - Policy.
(a)
Department heads will not permit the employment of members of their immediate family within their respective departments.
(b)
Immediate family members of division managers will not he employed in roles directly subordinate to the division manager.
(c)
Those employees who become in contravention of the provisions of paragraphs (a) and (b) above, and those employees who become so affected because of the assignment of a department or division head, will be given a reasonable time (not to exceed six months) in which to find another position outside the relatives' span of control.
(d)
Those employees who become in contravention of this policy because of marriage will be handled in accordance with paragraph (c) above.
(Ord. No. 86-16, § 1(3), 2-25-86)
Sec. 16B-19-4. - Exceptions.
Conditions and requirements as outlined in section 16B-19-3 which are in existence prior to the effective date of this policy will not apply.
(Ord. No. 86-16, § 1(4), 2-25-86; Ord. No. 88-75, 7-26-88)
Sec. 16B-19-5. - Responsibility for enforcement.
Department and division heads are responsible for enforcement of this policy within their span of control.
(Ord. No. 86-16, § 1(5), 2-25-86)
Sec. 16B-19-6. - Counsel and audit.
A personnel technician I, employment, is responsible for keeping this article current, and recommending changes when necessary. If it becomes known that this article of the personnel regulations is being abused, it is the responsibility of the personnel technician I to inform the personnel officer.
(Ord. No. 86-16, § 1(6), 2-25-86)
