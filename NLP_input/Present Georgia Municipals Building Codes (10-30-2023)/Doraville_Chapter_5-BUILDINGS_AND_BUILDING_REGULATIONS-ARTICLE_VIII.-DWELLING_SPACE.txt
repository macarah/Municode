Sec. 5-260. - Living space minimums.
(a)
Minimum living space: Excluding bathrooms, hallways and rooms used for cooking, every dwelling unit shall contain at least three hundred (300) square feet each for the first two (2) occupants and two hundred (200) additional square feet of floor area per additional occupant.
(b)
Minimum ceiling height: At least one-half (½) of the floor area of every habitable room, foyer, hall or corridor shall have a ceiling height of at least seven (7) feet. The floor area of that part of any room where the ceiling height is less than seven (7) feet shall not be considered as part of the floor area in computing the total floor areas of the room for the purpose of determining the maximum permissible occupancy thereof.
(c)
Conditions to inhabiting basement: No cellar shall be used as a habitable room. No basement shall be used as a habitable room or dwelling unit unless:
(1)
The floor and walls are impervious to leakage of underground and surface runoff water and are adequately protected against dampness.
(2)
All habitable rooms shall be provided with aggregate glazing area of no less than eight (8) percent of the floor area of the room. Such openings shall be provided with ready access or shall otherwise be readily controllable by the building occupants. The minimum open area to the outdoors shall be four (4) percent of the floor area.
(3)
With regard to bathrooms, glazing areas need not be provided where approved mechanical ventilation and artificial light is provided.
(4)
Such required minimum window area is located entirely above the grade of the ground adjoining such window area or is equipped with an adequate window well providing light and ventilation.
(5)
All sleeping rooms have an emergency escape window or door leading directly to the exterior.
(d)
Responsibility for compliance with provision: Both the owner and occupants of each dwelling unit shall be responsible for compliance with requirements of this section.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § 1, 4-20-09; Ord. No. 2009-32, § 1, 11-16-09)
