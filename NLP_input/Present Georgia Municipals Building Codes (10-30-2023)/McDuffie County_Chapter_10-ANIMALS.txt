ARTICLE II. - DANGEROUS DOG CONTROL

Sec. 10-26. - Enforcement of article.
Pursuant to the provisions of the dangerous dog control law of the state (O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq.), the county director of animal control is hereby designated dog control officer and shall administer and enforce the provisions of the dangerous dog control law.
(Ord. of 12-7-88, § 1)
Sec. 10-27. - Creation of animal control board.
There is hereby created an animal control board of three members to be appointed for initial terms of one, two and three years, and three years thereafter, to conduct hearings as required by O.C.G.A. § 4-8-24.
(Ord. of 12-7-88, § 2)
Cross reference— Authorities, boards and commissions, § 2-201 et seq.
Sec. 10-28. - Composition and purpose of animal control board.
The animal control board shall comprise one representative each from the board of commissioners, city council, and board of health to conduct periodic hearings at the call of the dog control officers, for those dog owners who desire to contest the classification of their dog or dogs as dangerous or potentially dangerous.
Sec. 10-29. - Annual fee for dangerous dogs.
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall pay an annual fee to the county in the amount of $100.00.
(Ord. of 12-7-88, § 3)
