ARTICLE II. - BUILDING INSPECTOR

Sec. 10-29. - Office created.
The office of building inspector is hereby created and the executive official in charge shall be known as the building inspector.
(Code 1962, § 5-5; Code 1986, § 5-16)
Sec. 10-30. - Appointment; qualifications.
The building inspector shall be appointed by the board of commissioners as provided in this Code. To be eligible for such appointment, the candidate shall have had experience as an architect, structural engineer, building inspector or superintendent of building construction. He or she shall be in good health and physically capable of making the necessary examinations and inspections. He or she shall not have any interest whatever, directly or indirectly, in the sale or manufacture of any material, process or device entering into or used in, or in connection with, building construction, alteration, removal or demolition.
(Code 1962, §§ 5-5, 5-6; Code 1986, § 5-17)
Sec. 10-31. - Term; dismissal.
The building inspector shall hold office at the pleasure of the board of commissioners and he or she may be dismissed by the board at any time, without notice and without cause.
(Code 1962, § 5-5; Code 1986, § 5-18)
Sec. 10-32. - Designation of acting inspector.
During temporary absence or disability of the building inspector, the board of commissioners shall designate an acting building inspector.
(Code 1962, § 5-5; Code 1986, § 5-19)
Sec. 10-33. - General duties.
(a)
The building inspector shall receive applications, issue permits and furnish certificates required by the building code. He or she shall examine premises for which such permits have been issued and shall make necessary inspections to see that the provisions of such code and other ordinances are complied with and that construction is prosecuted safely.
(b)
The building inspector shall enforce all laws relating to the construction, alteration, removal, demolition, raising or lowering, equipment, use and occupancy, location and maintenance of buildings and structures, except as may be otherwise provided for in other ordinances or regulatory measures. He or she shall, when requested by proper authority, or when the interests of the city so require, make investigations in connection with matters referred to in this section and render written reports on the same.
(c)
To enforce compliance with law, to remove illegal or unsafe conditions, to secure the necessary safeguards during construction or to require adequate exit facilities in buildings and structures, the building inspector shall issue such notices or orders as may be necessary.
(Code 1962, § 5-7; Code 1986, § 5-20)
Sec. 10-34. - Records.
(a)
The building inspector shall keep comprehensive records of applications received, permits and certificates issued, inspections made, reports rendered and notices or orders issued by him or her. He or she shall retain on file copies of required plans and all documents relating to building work, so long as any part of the building or structure to which they relate is in existence.
(b)
All records referred to in this section shall be open to public inspection for good and sufficient reasons, during office hours observed by the city, but shall not be removed from the office of the building inspector.
(Code 1962, § 5-8; Code 1986, § 5-21)
Sec. 10-35. - Reports.
The building inspector shall make written reports to the city manager once each month, or more often if requested, including statements of permits and certificates issued and orders promulgated.
(Code 1962, § 5-9; Code 1986, § 5-22)
Sec. 10-36. - Assistance from other officers.
The building inspector may request and shall receive, so far as may be necessary to the discharge of his or her duties, the assistance and cooperation of the officers of the city.
(Code 1962, § 5-10; Code 1986, § 5-23)
Sec. 10-37. - Right of entry.
The building inspector, in the discharge of his or her duties and upon proper identification, shall have authority to enter any building, structure or premises at any reasonable hour.
(Code 1962, § 5-11; Code 1986, § 5-24)
Sec. 10-38. - Intergovernmental agreement.
The board of commissioners of the city shall be authorized from time to time to enter into agreements between the city and any other governmental authority for the providing of consolidated building inspection services for the city and another governmental unit. In the event that the board of commissioners of the city authorizes the execution of a contract between the city and another governmental unit for the providing of building inspection services, the building inspector or enforcement official provided for in said intergovernmental agreement shall be deemed the building inspector for the city and shall have all responsibilities and powers set forth in this article.
(Code 1986, § 5-25; Ord. No. 415, § 1-5-1993)
Secs. 10-39—10-64. - Reserved.
