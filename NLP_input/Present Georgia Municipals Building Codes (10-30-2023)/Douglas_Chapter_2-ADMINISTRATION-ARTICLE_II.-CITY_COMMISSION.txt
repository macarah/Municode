ARTICLE II. - CITY COMMISSION

Sec. 2-19. - Meetings.
(a)
The city commission shall hold regular meetings on the second and fourth Mondays of each month at 7:00 p.m., unless otherwise ordered by the city commission.
(b)
Meetings shall be at city hall, 224 East Bryan Street, Douglas, Georgia.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-26)
State Law reference— Meetings to be open to public, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1 et seq.
Sec. 2-20. - Appointment of departmental advisors; duties.
At the first meeting in January of each year of the board of commissioners, the mayor shall appoint a commissioner as advisor to each of the city departments. It shall be the duty of the commissioner appointed as advisor to each department to discuss matters of concern from time to time that may arise; discuss such problems and matters that may concern that department with the city manager; and report from time to time to the board of commissioners on the operation of that department and make recommendations in regard to that department.
(Code 1978, § 2-27; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-27)
Sec. 2-21. - Parliamentary procedure.
The proceedings of the city commission shall be generally governed by Robert's Rules of Order, Newly Revised, so far as applicable and not inconsistent with the special rules of the city commission, the Charter and this Code.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-28)
Sec. 2-22. - Presiding officer.
(a)
The mayor shall preside at all commission meetings, preserve order and decorum, and shall appoint all committees, unless the commission shall otherwise direct, in which case they shall be appointed by ballot.
(b)
In the absence of the mayor, the mayor pro tem shall preside; and in the absence of both, a chairperson shall be appointed by the board of commissioners and he shall be vested with all the powers of the mayor or mayor pro tem during their absence.
(Code 1978, §§ 2-31, 2-32; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-29)
Sec. 2-23. - Reading, correction and approval of minutes.
At the commencement of each regular meeting, the clerk shall present the minutes of the last regular meeting and of any special meetings held since the last regular meeting; and when the same have been corrected of any errors, deletions or omissions, they shall be approved by vote of the board of commissioners.
(Code 1978, § 2-33; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-30)
Sec. 2-24. - Voting on questions.
All questions shall be put by the mayor, or by the member presiding. The members shall vote by voice, the majority determining all questions, unless otherwise provided. The mayor may, at his discretion, call any member to take the chair, to allow him to address the board or make a motion, and the mayor shall have equal vote with the other commissioners.
(Code 1978, § 2-34; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-31)
Sec. 2-25. - Submitting of questions and measures.
It shall not be necessary to have a second to any motion, resolution, ordinance or question to be voted on, and all such motions, resolutions, ordinances or questions shall be submitted to the board of commissioners by the mayor or presiding officer, when made or submitted by any member of the board of commissioners.
(Code 1978, § 2-35; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-32)
Sec. 2-26. - Recording vote.
Whenever it shall be required by any one or more commission members, the yeas and nays shall be recorded.
(Code 1978, § 2-36; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-33)
Sec. 2-27. - Methods of electing.
All elections shall be by ballots upon request of any member of the board of commissioners; but, unless such request is made, elections may be by voice vote.
(Code 1978, § 2-37; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-34)
Sec. 2-28. - Reports and resolutions in writing.
All reports and resolutions of the board of commissioners, unless for adjournment, must be in writing.
(Code 1978, § 2-38; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-35)
Sec. 2-29. - Interruptions not permitted.
No interruptions shall be permitted while a member is speaking on such matters as may be before the board of commissioners.
(Code 1978, § 2-39; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-36)
Sec. 2-30. - Decision on questions of order; appeal.
The mayor, or member presiding, shall decide all questions of order, but any member dissatisfied with a decision may appeal to the board of commissioners.
(Code 1978, § 2-40; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-37)
Sec. 2-31. - Communications to be in writing, referred to committee, advisor, etc.
Applications, petitions or other communications to the board must be in writing; and in all cases the same must be referred to the proper committee or advisor to the department head before any action is taken thereon, unless the board shall otherwise direct.
(Code 1978, § 2-42; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-38)
Sec. 2-32. - Motion to reconsider matters in minutes of previous meeting.
Before the minutes of any preceding meeting of the board of commissioners are confirmed, any member may move to reconsider any question, motion, resolution or other matter or thing contained in the minutes of such previous meeting; and if such reconsideration is voted by a majority of the members present, the subject matter of such reconsideration shall be first business in order of disposition.
(Code 1978, § 2-44; Code 1993, pt. II, § 2-39)
Secs. 2-33—2-52. - Reserved.
