ARTICLE VIII. - FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS

Sec. 22-611. - Business license tax levied.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93, there is hereby levied an annual business license tax upon state and national banking associations, federal savings and loan associations and state building and loan associations at the rate of 0.25 percent of the gross receipts of such institutions. As used in this section, the term "gross receipts" shall mean gross receipts as defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93. Notwithstanding any other provisions of this article, the minimum amount of business license tax due from any depository financial institution pursuant to this article shall be $1,000.00 per year.
(Code 1988, § 12-180)
State Law reference— Local business license tax, O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93; gross receipts taxed, O.C.G.A. § 48-6-95.
Sec. 22-612. - Returns.
Each depository financial institution within the city shall file a return of its gross receipts with the city on March 1 of each year in the manner and in the form prescribed by the commissioner of the state department of banking, which shall be based upon the allocated method set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(d). The tax levied pursuant to this article shall be assessed and collected based upon the information provided in such return.
(Code 1988, § 12-181)
