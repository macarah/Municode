Sec. 22-31. - Title.
This article shall be known as the "Lincoln County Occupational Tax and Regulatory Fee Ordinance."
(Ord. No. 18, § 201, 12-10-1998)
Sec. 22-32. - Adoption.
This article is hereby adopted pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-2 et seq.
(Ord. No. 18, art. 1, 12-10-1998)
Sec. 22-33. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Director means the director of planning and zoning.
(Ord. No. 18, § 202, 12-10-1998)
Sec. 22-34. - Administration and enforcement.
The director of planning and zoning shall administer and enforce the levy, assessment, and collection of occupational taxes and regulatory fees under the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 18, § 202, 12-10-1998)
Cross reference— Administration, ch. 2.

Sec. 22-37. - Professionals as classified in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9(c)(1)—(18).
Practitioners of professions or occupations who by state law requires state license regulating such professions or occupations shall elect as their entire occupation tax one of the following:
(1)
The occupation tax based on number of employees as set forth in section 22-35(b).
(2)
A fee of $100.00 per practitioner who is licensed to provide the service, such tax to be paid at the practitioner's office or location.
(Ord. No. 18, § 303, 12-10-1998)
Sec. 22-38. - Taxes levied based on business locations.
(a)
Taxes are levied on each business or practitioner location in the county. Location includes any structure or vehicle where a business, profession, or occupation is conducted.
(b)
Temporary or construction work sites are not included as business locations as well as vehicles used for sales or delivery by a business or practitioner which has a location or office.
(c)
An occupation tax is hereby levied, and shall be assessed and collected, on those businesses and practitioners of professions and occupations with no location or office in the state in accordance with this article if the business or practitioner:
(1)
Has one or more employees or agents who exert substantial efforts within the unincorporated part of the county for the purpose of soliciting business or serving customers or clients; or
(2)
Owns personal or real property which generates income and which is located in the unincorporated part of the county; or
If no other state taxes Georgia receipts for tax purposes in that state; in such cases, proof of such payment may be required.
(Ord. No. 18, § 304, 12-10-1998)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7.
Sec. 22-39. - Exclusions and exemptions.
(a)
Businesses and practitioners subject to this article shall be required to pay occupation tax to only one local government in this state, the local government for the municipal corporation or county in which the largest dollar volume of business is done or service is performed by the individual business or practitioner. Proof of payment in another jurisdiction must be provided when requested.
(b)
The county shall not require an occupation tax on those real estate brokers, agents, or companies, whose offices are located outside the county and sell property in the county.
(c)
Only one occupation tax shall be imposed on a business where several activities are done at the same location under one ownership or sole proprietorship.
(d)
Businesses or professions regulated by the state public service commission; electric membership cooperatives; and agricultural operations, excluding agribusinesses, are exempt from local taxes and regulatory fees.
(e)
Governmental agencies, federal, state, and local, and all nonprofit organizations are exempt from local taxes and regulatory fees.
(f)
The board of commissioners may by subsequent ordinance or resolution provide for an exemption or reduction in occupation tax to one or more types of businesses or practitioners of occupations or professions as part of the plan for economic development. Such exemptions or reductions shall not be arbitrary or capricious and the reasons shall be set forth in the minutes of the board.
(Ord. No. 18, § 305, 12-10-1998)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7.
Sec. 22-40. - Purpose and scope of taxes and fees.
(a)
The occupation tax levied in this article is for revenue purposes only and is not for regulatory purposes, nor is the payment of the tax made a condition precedent to the practice of any such profession, trade or calling.
(b)
Regulatory fees paid by businesses or practitioners are to defray cost for investigations or inspections of such businesses or practitioners as a protection to the public health, safety or welfare or in the course of enforcing, a state or local building, health or safety code.
(Ord. No. 18, § 308, 12-10-1998)
Sec. 22-41. - When tax due and payable.
(a)
All occupational taxes and regulatory fees levied by this article shall be due and payable annually on January 1.
(b)
Should any occupation tax or regulatory fee imposed by this article remain due and unpaid for 90 days from the due date of the tax or fees, the person liable for the tax or fee shall be subject to and shall pay a penalty of 1.5 percent per month (18 percent per year) interest on unpaid taxes or fees.
(c)
When any person commences business on or after July 1, in any year, the occupation tax for the remaining portion of the year shall be 50 percent of the tax imposed for the entire year. This provision does not apply to regulatory fees.
(d)
A practitioner of a profession or occupation who elects as his occupation tax the amount described in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-10(g)(2) shall receive no reduction provided for in subsection (c) of this section.
(Ord. No. 18, § 401, 12-10-1998)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-22.
Sec. 22-42. - Evidence of qualification required, if applicable.
(a)
Any business required to obtain health permits, bonds, certificates of qualifications, certificates of competency or any other regulatory matter shall first, before the issuance of a county registration, show evidence of such qualification.
(b)
Each person who is licensed by the state shall post the state license in a conspicuous place in the licensee's place of business and shall keep the license there at all times while the license remains valid.
(c)
Each person who is licensed by the secretary of state, pursuant to O.C.G.A. title 43, shall provide evidence of proper and current state license before the county registration may be issued.
(Ord. No. 18, § 402, 12-10-1998)
Sec. 22-43. - Adoption, amendments and hearings.
(a)
This article and all future amendments concerning occupation taxes and regulatory fees may only be adopted after giving notice and conducting a public hearing.
(b)
A public hearing will be held, in any year in which revenues are anticipated to be higher than the previous year from business taxation, to determine how the additional revenue is to be used. This will be considered as part of the normal budgetary process on each year's budget and addressed independently to show the disposition of these revenues in paying for county government services.
(Ord. No. 18, § 403, 12-10-1998)
Sec. 22-44. - Enforcement and penalties.
(a)
Violations.Violations of this article or any other ordinance or regulation of the county shall constitute grounds for suspension or revocation of a business registration. The provisions and requirements of this article shall be enforced by injunctions.
(b)
Penalties. Violations of this article shall be tried in the magistrate court of the county as provided in O.C.G.A. § 15-10-62 and shall be punishable as provided in section 1-9. The county attorney is designated as prosecuting attorney on behalf of the county. Citations for violations of this article shall be issued by any agent of the county authorized for that purpose by resolution of the board of commissioners.
(Ord. No. 18, § 501, 12-10-1998)
Sec. 22-45. - Proof of solid waste collection required prior to issuance.
(a) Any businesses or practitioners of professions and occupations which are required to pay the occupational tax levied under this article shall also be required to provide direct evidence to the county, or its designated authority, that the business or practitioner has adequately provided for solid waste collection services that are sufficient to dispose of the amount of solid waste generated by the business or practitioner prior to issuance of the occupational tax certificate.
(b) In the event that the business or practitioner is unable to produce direct evidence of solid waste collection, then and in that event the department of planning and zoning shall not be authorized to issue an occupational tax certificate to the business or practitioner.
(c) Any violation of this section shall be punishable as specified in section 22-44 of this article.
(Ord. of 12-8-2005)
