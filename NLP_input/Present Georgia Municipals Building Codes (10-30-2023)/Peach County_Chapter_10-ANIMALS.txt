ARTICLE II. - DANGEROUS DOGS

Sec. 10-26. - Appointment of dog control officer; duties.
The individual holding the title of county zoning enforcement officer is hereby appointed to the position of dog control officer to carry out the duties as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq., entitled "Dangerous Dog Control Law."
(Ord. of 11-5-88)
Sec. 10-27. - Hearings; animal registration fee.
The county board of health is hereby designated to conduct all hearings required by O.C.G.A. § 4-8-24, and that a fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges shall be collected as an annual registration fee by the county dog control officer to register each potentially dangerous dog so classified under the dangerous dog control law.
(Ord. of 11-5-88)
Sec. 10-28. - Arrangements for housing; apprehension; destruction.
The county dog control officer shall be further authorized to contract or cooperate with other governmental agencies and private veterinarians to provide for the housing, apprehension, and destruction of dangerous dogs or potentially dangerous dogs as may be required or permitted by the dangerous dog control law. Any expenditure of funds on behalf of the county by the county dog control officer for such services from other governments or private veterinarians for the housing, apprehension, and destruction of such animals shall first be approved by the clerk of the board of commissioners.
(Ord. of 11-15-88)
