It shall be unlawful for any person to drink any whiskey, wine, beer or any other intoxicating beverage in any street, alley, park or other public place or business house, except a beer can be consumed in such business houses which are licensed by the city to sell beer or other malt beverages, and such license provides that it can be consumed in the building covered by such license.

ARTICLE III. - TAXATION AND REGULATION
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-42. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Adequate parking means one parking space for each 100 square feet of customer service area within the premises of the applicant, but not less than five parking spaces. Such off-street parking shall have vehicular access to streets and shall be constructed of all-weather surface.
Church means a permanent building in which persons regularly assemble for religious worship.
City clerk means the duly appointed city clerk for the City of Fairmount.
College means only such state, county, city, church, or other colleges as teach the subjects commonly taught in the common colleges of this state, and shall not include private colleges where only specified subjects such as law, stenography, business, music, art, medicine, dentistry, vocational occupations, and other special subjects are taught.
Distance means the measurement in linear feet from the nearest wall enclosing the proposed premises of a licensee under this article to the nearest wall of any church, or to the nearest wall enclosing the premises of any other licensee under this article, or to the nearest property line of any school or residence.
Family means any person related to the holder of a license issued under the provisions of this article within the first degree of consanguinity or affinity as determined according to the law of this state.
License means the authorization by the city council to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages by the package or for the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises, or both, as may be permitted by the terms of this article.
Licensee means a person, real or artificial, holding any class of license issued under the terms of this article.
Outlet means a store for the sale of alcoholic beverages.
Package store means a specific building or facility within the city wherein a license may be issued for the sale of alcoholic beverages in their respective consumer containers, and not for consumption on the premises. Such package store must be operated as a distinct business.
Premises means the definite closed or partitioned-in locality, whether a room, shop or building, wherein alcoholic beverages are sold under the terms of this article.
Private residence means a house or dwelling wherein not less than one or more than three families customarily reside and shall not include an apartment house having facilities for housing four or more families, and also shall not include any residence which has been unoccupied for a period of at least six consecutive months immediately prior to the filing of an application hereunder.
Retail sales means selling or offering for sale alcoholic beverages to any member of the public.
School means only such state, county, city, church or other schools as teach the subjects commonly taught in the common schools of this state, and shall not include private schools where only specialized subjects such as law, stenography, business, music, art, medicine, dentistry, vocational occupations and other special subjects are taught.
Store manager means that person who does or will actually manage or operate the package store on a day-to-day basis.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 1)
State law reference— Similar definitions, O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2.
Sec. 4-43. - Purpose.
This article has been enacted for the purpose of:
(1)
Promoting the health and general welfare of the city;
(2)
Establishing reasonable and ascertainable standards for the regulation and control of the licensing and sale of alcoholic beverages;
(3)
Protecting and preserving schools and churches giving to the character of the area and their peculiar suitability for particular uses, the congestion in roads and streets, and with a general view of promoting desirable living conditions;
(4)
Sustaining the stability of neighborhoods and property values;
(5)
Protecting against the evils of concentration of retail licenses for alcoholic beverages in one family and/or corporation, and preventing an undesirable person from engaging or having an interest in alcoholic beverages or the sale thereof in the city;
(6)
Considering the proximity to the proposed location of other establishments selling alcoholic beverages;
(7)
Considering adequate parking availability; and
(8)
Considering the feelings and attitudes of citizens residing in the area adjacent to the proposed location.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 2)
Sec. 4-44. - Compliance.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or offer for sale at wholesale or retail any alcoholic beverages within the city without having first complied with the provisions of this article.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 3)
Sec. 4-45. - Hours of operation.
(a)
No sale of alcoholic beverages by the package shall be allowed except between the hours of 6:00 a.m. and 11:50 p.m. on weekdays and on Saturdays. No sale of alcoholic beverages by the package shall be allowed between the hours of 11:59 p.m. on Saturday through 6:00 a.m. on the following Monday.
(b)
No sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises shall be allowed.
(c)
The sale of alcoholic beverages shall not be permitted on Thanksgiving Day, Christmas Day, and Sundays. Alcoholic beverages can be sold on election day, except as may not be authorized by state law.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 4; Ord. of 3-4-1996)
State law reference— Sunday sales, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-7; sale on Sunday and election days, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20.
Sec. 4-46. - Distance from schools, churches, and polling places.
(a)
No person knowingly and intentionally may sell or offer to sell any alcoholic beverage in or within 100 yards of any church building, any school building, educational building, school ground, or college campus.
(b)
No person shall knowingly and intentionally sell or offer to sell any alcoholic beverage within 250 feet of any polling place or the outer edge of any building within which such polling place is established for any state or local election days, including primary, special, run-off, or general elections.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 5)
Sec. 4-47. - Minimum age of employees.
No person shall allow or require a person in his employment under 18 years of age to dispense, serve, sell or take orders for any alcoholic beverages.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 6)
State law reference— Restrictions on employment of underage persons, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-24.
Sec. 4-48. - Drinking of alcoholic beverages by employees at outlets.
It shall be unlawful for the licensee or any employee of a licensee for the sale of alcoholic beverages to drink any alcoholic beverages while at such licensed location or to be present at such location in an intoxicated condition. Permit violations of this section by the licensee, or repeated violations, shall constitute grounds for suspension, revocation and refusal to renew the license or other action with regard to said license.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 7)
Sec. 4-49. - Prohibited persons.
It shall be unlawful to grant a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages to any elected official, city department head, or his immediate family. It shall be unlawful for any elected official, city department head, or his immediate family to own and hold partial or beneficial interest in any license to sell alcoholic beverages in the city.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 8)
Sec. 4-50. - Limitation on ownership of alcoholic beverage licenses; definitions.
(a)
For the purposes of this section, the following shall apply:
(1)
The term "person" shall include all members of a retail dealer licensee's family.
(2)
The beneficiaries of a trust shall be considered to have a beneficial interest in any business forming a part of the trust estate.
(b)
No person shall be issued more than one license for the retail package sale of beer or other malt beverages, nor shall any person be permitted to have a beneficial interest in more than one such retail malt beverage license issued under this article, regardless of the degree of such interest.
(c)
No person shall be issued more than one license for the retail package sale of wine, nor shall any person be permitted to have a beneficial interest in more than one such retail wine license issued under this article, regardless of the degree of such interest.
(d)
It shall be permissible for one person to simultaneously own or have a beneficial interest in a retail package malt beverage license and a retail package wine license.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 9)
Sec. 4-51. - Grounds for suspension or revocation; notice; hearing; refund.
(a)
Any license which has been issued or which may be hereafter issued by the city to any licensee for the sale of alcoholic beverages may be suspended for a time certain or revoked entirely for due cause as hereinafter defined, with such suspension or revocation to be effective only after a hearing held before the mayor and city council for the purpose of considering any such suspension or revocation. At least five days' prior written notice of a time, place and purpose of such hearing, including a specific statement of the charge upon which the suspension or revocation is being sought, shall be given to the holder of such license which is sought to be suspended or revoked.
(b)
A hearing held before the mayor and council pursuant to this section shall be conducted informally and the technical rules of evidence shall not apply. All testimony shall be under oath and shall be recorded. The holder of the license sought to be suspended or revoked shall be entitled to be represented by legal counsel, if desired, shall be able to cross examine any witnesses produced by the city, and shall be able to present witnesses and evidence in his own behalf. Pursuant to such hearing, a decision to suspend or revoke the license under consideration shall be upon a majority vote of the mayor and council hearing the evidence. The license holder shall be promptly notified in writing of the mayor and council's finding and decision affecting his license.
(c)
In the event the holder of a license which is suspended or revoked by the city seeks to appeal said suspension or revocation to the appropriate tribunal, the cost of preparing a transcript of the hearing proceedings shall be borne by the license holder and must be paid in full at the delivery of such transcript.
(d)
Due cause for the suspension or revocation of any license shall include, but not be limited to, a violation of any laws or ordinances, both state and municipal, regulating the sale of alcoholic beverages, or a violation of regulations made pursuant to authority granted for the purpose of regulating such businesses, or for the violation of any state or federal law, or for the violation of any city ordinances other than traffic ordinances, or for the failure of the licensee or any employee of the licensee to promptly report to the city police department any violation of the law or ordinance, breach of the peace, disturbance or altercation resulting in violence on the premises, or for the violation of any validly promulgated law, ordinance or regulations pertaining to the sale of alcoholic beverages by any employee of the license holder involved in the sale of alcoholic beverages.
(e)
The mayor is hereby authorized to suspend any license issued hereunder for any emergency situation such as civil disorders or natural disasters or in any situation that the mayor deems such immediate suspension necessary for the protection of the health and welfare of the citizens of the city, and such suspension may be made effective immediately and shall remain in force until the mayor determines the emergency is over or until the next regular meeting of the mayor and council, or a meeting called prior to the next regular meeting, at which time the mayor and council, by majority vote, may determine that the emergency is ended.
(f)
When a license is suspended or revoked, the city shall not be required to refund any portion of the license fee to the holder of such suspended or revoked license.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 10)
Sec. 4-52. - Automatic revocation of city license upon revocation of state license.
Whenever the state shall revoke any permit or license to sell at wholesale or retail any alcoholic beverages, the city license held by the same licensee to sell at wholesale or retail any alcoholic beverages within the city shall thereupon be automatically revoked without any additional action by the mayor and council or any other officer of the city.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 11)
Sec. 4-53. - False information on application.
Any material omission from or untrue or misleading information which is contained in an original, renewal or transfer application for a license hereunder shall be cause for the denial or refusal of a license, and if any license has previously been granted under such circumstances, the same shall constitute due cause for revocation of such license.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 12)
Sec. 4-54. - Production of records.
In conjunction with any application for a license, or in conjunction with any license which has been issued under the terms hereof, or in conjunction with the revocation, suspension or cancellation of any license, or in conjunction with any of the hearings contemplated by this article, or in conjunction with the payment or nonpayment of any excise tax levied or to be collected hereunder, the mayor and council shall have the right to subpoena all or any part of the records, books, documents, reports or invoices of any licensee for the purpose of auditing the records of such licensee, securing compliance by such licensee with the provisions of this article, proving or disproving violation of any part of this article by any licensee, or to show payment or nonpayment of any taxes, fees, charges, or the like due hereunder.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 13)
Sec. 4-55. - Factors for council consideration.
The mayor and council, in passing upon an initial application for an alcoholic beverage license at the final meeting thereon, shall be guided by the following factors as to whether to grant or to deny such application:
(1)
The proximity of the proposed location to any other establishment selling alcoholic beverages of any nature;
(2)
The character of the neighborhood immediately adjacent to the proposed location;
(3)
The proximity of churches, schools, and playgrounds to the proposed location;
(4)
Whether the proposed location has adequate off-street parking facilities or other parking available for its patrons;
(5)
Whether the location would tend to increase and promote traffic congestion and resulting hazards therefrom;
(6)
The feelings and attitudes of those residing in the area adjacent to the proposed location;
(7)
Whether any malt or alcoholic beverage license previously issued for such location was ever revoked for cause by the city council;
(8)
Whether the applicant has ever sold alcoholic beverages illegally in the municipality;
(9)
The criminal record, if any, of the applicant and the outlet manager, their general good character and reputation, and their financial responsibility; provided, that nonpayment of federal, state, county or city taxes shall be prima facie evidence of lack of financial responsibility;
(10)
The information required in the application or statement and whether all requirements as to notice and advertisements have been complied with; and
(11)
The history or reputation of the building or establishment proposed for the sale of alcoholic beverages for fighting, shooting, stabbing, or other acts of violence; gambling; illegal dealing in the alcoholic beverages or drugs; or other violations of the law.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 14)
State law reference— Location restrictions regarding sale of alcoholic beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21; authority of municipalities to regulate and determine business locations, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-49.
Sec. 4-56. - Store manager.
(a)
When a license for the sale of any alcoholic beverage by package is applied for by a partnership or corporation, the applicant shall also name an individual manager who shall be responsible for managing and overseeing the proposed business of selling alcoholic beverages. Such manager must meet and maintain all the requirements of any individual licensee as prescribed in this article, such as age and residence, as if such manager were individually applying for and/or holding such a license.
(b)
In the event such a partnership or corporation holding a license for the retail sale of any alcoholic beverage should change managers for whatever reason, such licensee shall immediately notify the city through the city clerk or police chief of such change, and the new manager must meet all the qualifications and requirements of this article for an individual holding a similar alcoholic beverage license.
(c)
Failure of a partnership or corporation holding a retail alcoholic beverage license to immediately notify the city clerk or police chief of such a change in managers shall be grounds for suspension or revocation of such licensee's license.
(d)
In the event that such a named manager should cease to possess or maintain all of the qualifications and/or requirements as are required of an individual license holder for the sale of similar alcoholic beverages, the license of the partnership or corporation responsible for such manager may be suspended until the partnership or corporation names a manager who does meet and possess all such qualifications and/or requirements.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 15)
Sec. 4-57. - Prohibited persons.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to grant a license for the sale of any alcoholic beverages to:
(1)
A person who has been convicted of a felony, or of a crime opposed to decency and morality, or of a crime involving a violation of the ordinances of the city relating to the use, sale, taxability or possession of alcoholic beverages or a violation of the laws of the state and/or of the United States pertaining to the manufacture, possession, transportation or sale of any alcoholic beverages, or taxability thereof; or
(2)
A person whose license under this article has been revoked for cause or who has had a license under this article revoked for cause.
(b)
Any licensee who moves his primary residence from the county or otherwise becomes a prohibited person as defined in subsection (a)(1) of this section shall, within ten days of such event, make said fact known to the mayor and city council and shall surrender his license upon demand by the mayor and council.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 16)
Sec. 4-58. - Consumption on premises.
Except as provided herein for premises licensed for the retail sale of malt beverages for consumption on the premises, it shall be unlawful for anyone to open or consume any alcoholic beverage upon any premises for which a license to sell any retail package alcoholic beverage is issued.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 17)
Sec. 4-59. - Restrictions on licenses.
(a)
No retail or wholesale license for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall be issued to an applicant unless such applicant shall either own the premises for which the license is sought or have a written lease for the premises for which the license is sought for the full period for which the license is to be issued.
(b)
If such applicant shall merely lease the premises for which a license is sought, the applicant shall attach a copy of the lease to the application for the issuance of a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 18)
Sec. 4-60. - Police supervision.
Anyone holding a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall, at all times, permit the police department to have complete supervision over the conduct of those people patronizing the establishment, and any dealer or licensee refusing to permit the city police department to have full supervision, or interfering with such activities or hindering the proper policing of such establishments, will be subject to having his license suspended or revoked in the manner provided herein.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 19)
Sec. 4-61. - Application for relocation of retail malt beverage business.
(a)
In the event that the holder of any retail alcoholic beverage license for a prescribed location in the city desires to move his retail alcoholic beverage business to another location in the city, said license holder shall make application to the mayor and city council for permission to relocate the business. Such application shall be in the same form and contain the same information as is required for an initial application for a retail alcoholic beverage license.
(b)
No fee shall be required for the processing of an application for relocation of a retail alcoholic beverage business.
(c)
The applicant for relocation of a retail alcoholic beverage business shall comply with the provisions of this article pertaining to newspaper advertisement and the posting of a sign upon the proposed new location of the business as if the applicant for relocation of the business were making an initial application for such retail alcoholic beverage license.
(d)
In reviewing and acting upon an application for the relocation of a retail alcoholic beverage business and license, the mayor and council shall be guided by the same factors and considerations as if said application were an application for a new license.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 20)
Sec. 4-62. - Nontransferability.
Except as provided herein, no license for the sale of any alcoholic beverage may be transferred from one person to another. Further, no license for the sale of any alcoholic beverage may be transferred from one location or premises to another, without the prior approval thereof by the mayor and city council in the manner described herein.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 21)
Sec. 4-63. - Prohibition against coin-operated devices.
No holder of a retail license for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall permit on the licensed premises any slot machine, mechanical music box or jukebox, pinball machine, video arcade machine, or any other coin-operated or token-operated machine for amusement purposes.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 22)
Sec. 4-64. - Criminal provisions.
Any person who shall violate any of the provisions of this article or assist or aid another in violating this article shall, upon conviction thereof by the mayor's court, be guilty of a misdemeanor.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 23)
Secs. 4-65—4-86. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - BEER AND MALT BEVERAGES

FOOTNOTE(S):
(4) State Law reference— Local licensing of the sale of malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-42; local licensing of the sale of wine, O.C.G.A. § 3-10-40.
Sec. 4-87. - Sale of malt beverages; charges imposed.
It shall be unlawful to manufacture, sell or offer to sell at wholesale or retail within the corporate limits of the city any malt beverage without having the appropriate license for such manufacture or sale, or to carry on such activity in violation of the terms of such license or of this division.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 24)
Sec. 4-88. - Fee established.
The annual business license fee for malt beverage retail package dealers, whose place of business is located within the corporate limits of the city, shall be set by the city council and maintained by the clerk.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 25)
State law reference— License fee for sale of malt beverages authorized, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-42; maximum fees for municipal alcoholic beverage licenses, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-50.
Sec. 4-89. - Malt beverage excise tax.
(a)
There is hereby set and levied on the sale of malt beverages within the city an excise tax in the following amounts:
(1)
$1.60 per case of 24 containers of 16 fluid ounces each.
(2)
$1.20 per case of 24 containers of 12 fluid ounces each.
(3)
$1.20 per case of 36 containers of eight fluid ounces each.
(4)
$1.60 per case of containers of 32 fluid ounces each.
(5)
$1.40 per case of containers of 14 fluid ounces each.
(6)
$1.40 per case of containers of seven fluid ounces each.
(7)
$6.00 per keg.
(b)
The malt beverage excise tax herein levied is levied against and shall be paid by each licensed wholesale dealer in malt beverages in the municipality.
(c)
The malt beverage excise tax shall be paid on or before the tenth day of the month following the calendar month in which the beverages are sold or disposed, and payment shall be accompanied by a report itemizing the exact quantities of malt beverages sold for the preceding calendar month by size and type of container. Any tax remaining unpaid at the expiration of 15 days from the due date shall be delinquent.
(d)
The tax levied by this section may be enforced by execution in the same manner as other taxes of the city and, in addition, any failure of payment of such tax shall be grounds for revocation or refusal of the business license of the delinquent taxpayer.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 26)
State law reference— Local excise taxes on distilled spirits generally, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-80; due dates for excise taxes on wholesale sales of malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-81; local excise taxes on wine, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-60; municipal excise taxes on distilled spirits by the drink, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-130.
Sec. 4-90. - Application, issuance and revocation of retail malt beverage license.
(a)
Application and financial statement.
(1)
Applicants for retail malt beverage licenses shall execute and complete an application for a retail malt beverage license on a form to be prescribed by the city clerk, such application to contain all of the necessary information required by this division.
(2)
There shall be attached to and made a part of the application referenced in subsection (a)(1) of this section a financial statement detailing the amount of investment by the applicant, the amount and sources of loans, if any, to be used for construction and/or operation of the business, and the collateral used to secure such loan, if any. This statement and the information therein shall be treated confidentially and shall be examined by the mayor, council and city clerk only.
(b)
Public notice of application and proposed business.
(1)
All applicants for licenses hereunder shall give public notice of making such application by advertisement at least once a week for two consecutive weeks prior to the date of the public hearing on such application as provided herein in the Calhoun Times, the newspaper in which the legal advertisements of the city are published.
(2)
Such notice shall sufficiently identify the proposed location of the proposed business, the name of the applicant, the type of license applied for, and the date and time of the public hearing on such application. If the applicant is a partnership such notice shall give the names of all partners, whether general or limited, and, if the applicant is a corporation, the notice shall name the officers of the corporation.
(3)
The advertisement referred to herein shall be of a type not smaller than ten point Capital in lowercase and shall be at least a two-inch by two-inch column advertisement.
(c)
Processing fee. Upon the filing of the application with the city clerk, the applicant shall deposit in cash or by certified check a processing fee in an amount set by the council and in a schedule maintained by the clerk to cover the expense of the investigation and processing the application, which fee shall not be refundable and shall not apply against the license fee granted hereunder.
(d)
Hearing date. The mayor and council shall set a date for a hearing on an application for a retail malt beverage license or an application for the relocation of a retail malt beverage license business within 45 days from the date of the filing of such application.
(e)
Issuance. If such application is approved by the mayor and council, the city clerk shall forthwith issue a license and permit to the applicant to engage in such business for the ensuing calendar year, or remaining portion thereof, subject to all terms and conditions of this division.
(f)
Revocation/suspension. When such license and permit is suspended or revoked, as herein provided, the holder of same shall no longer be licensed or have the right to engage in the business of selling malt beverages either at retail or at wholesale until such license shall be reinstated or until such suspension expires.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 27)
Sec. 4-91. - Age and residence requirements of licenses.
It shall be unlawful to grant a license for the sale of malt beverages at retail to anyone other than:
(1)
A person at least 21 years of age or older who is currently a resident of the county;
(2)
A partnership all of whose partners are at least 21 years of age or older and who are residents of the county; or
(3)
A corporation incorporated under the laws of the state or authorized to do business in the state.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 28)
Sec. 4-92. - Licensing of wholesale dealers.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to grant a license for the sale of malt beverages at wholesale to anyone other than a:
(1)
Person at least 21 years of age or older who is currently a resident of the state, and who has been a resident of the state for at least one year immediately preceding the filing of the application;
(2)
Partnership, all of whose partners are at least 21 years of age or older, and who are residents of the state and have been such residents for at least one year immediately preceding the filing of the application; or
(3)
Corporation incorporated under the laws of the state, authorized to do business in the state.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any wholesale dealer in malt beverages to sell or deliver malt beverages to any establishment in the city properly licensed for the sale of malt beverages without having first received a license from the city for the wholesale sale of malt beverages in the city.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any wholesale dealer properly licensed to sell malt beverages at wholesale in the city to either sell or deliver malt beverages to anyone other than a holder of a license for the sale of malt beverages at retail in the city. Deliveries shall be made in a conveyance owned and operated by such wholesale dealer licensed to sell malt beverages at wholesale in the city.
(d)
In the event any wholesale malt beverage dealer shall be exempted or excused for any reason from paying a local business license fee for the privilege of selling and delivering malt beverages within the corporate limits of the city, such wholesale malt beverage dealer must still apply for and receive a wholesale malt beverage license and comply with all applicable provisions of this division prior to selling and/or delivering any malt beverages within the corporate limits of the city.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 29)
Sec. 4-93. - Compliance bond.
(a)
In addition to the license fee required herein, each wholesale and retail licensee for the sale of malt beverages shall post a cash bond in favor of the city to secure compliance with the requirements and provisions of this division. In lieu of posting such cash bond, each wholesale or retail licensee may file a surety bond in favor of the city to ensure compliance with the requirements and prohibitions of this division by the licensee. Such surety bond shall be with an insurance company authorized to do business in the state. The bond must provide that the insurance company issuing such bond will notify the city through the city clerk if the bond is cancelled for any reason, including cancellation for failure to pay premiums. The amount of such bond shall be set by the council and contained in a schedule maintained by the clerk.
(b)
Each year upon issuance of a new wholesale or retail malt beverage license, each wholesale or retail licensee shall offer to the city satisfactory proof that the surety bond for such licensee is currently still in force and effect, provided the licensee shall not have posted a cash bond to the city under the terms of this section. Such proof of satisfactory bond shall be a condition preceding the issuance of the license.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 30)
Sec. 4-94. - Storage and inspection on premises.
(a)
Storage on premises. All holders of licenses for the retail sale of malt beverages shall store all malt beverages in their possession on the premises for which the license was issued and at no other place except upon the written permission from the mayor upon written application therefor.
(b)
Inspection. All such malt beverage stock stored on the premises for which the license was issued shall be available at all times for inspection by authorized city officials.
(c)
Duty of retailer; penalty for violation. Any brand or type of malt beverages found in any retailer's stock that is handled by a wholesale malt beverage dealer who is not licensed to make sales or deliveries in the city under the terms of this division shall be subject to immediate confiscation.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 31)
Sec. 4-95. - Sale on premises.
All malt beverages in the possession of a holder of a license to sell malt beverages at retail shall be sold in and upon the premises for which such license was issued and at no other place. It shall be unlawful for the holder of the retail malt beverage license to sell malt beverages in anything other than the original package of the malt beverage. Provided, however, that the holder of a license for the retail sale of malt beverages by package may sell draft beer or other malt beverages in gallon or half-gallon plastic containers.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 32)
Sec. 4-96. - Delivery of malt beverages to retail dealers.
Retail dealers in malt beverages licensed under this division shall not buy or accept deliveries of malt beverages from wholesalers or other persons offering same for sale except from wholesalers duly licensed to sell malt beverages at wholesale in the city. Such retailers shall not accept deliveries of malt beverages except directly to the premises for which their license and permit was issued, and by no other means other than in a conveyance owned and operated by a wholesaler licensed under the provisions of this division to make deliveries of malt beverages in the city. Provided, however, that on written request to the mayor and upon the granting of permission by the mayor in writing, deliveries may, in special instances, be made otherwise, upon terms and conditions prescribed by the mayor as to each such delivery.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 33)
Sec. 4-97. - Invoices for purchases by retailer to wholesaler.
Upon each and every delivery by a licensed wholesaler to a licensed retailer, an invoice in triplicate shall be prepared showing the quantities and brands of malt beverages delivered, together with the price thereof and the tax collected thereon, and the original copy of such invoice shall be delivered by the licensed wholesaler to the licensed retailer simultaneously with each such delivery. The licensed wholesaler shall retain the second copy of the invoice and shall keep the same for a period of 12 months and shall have same available for inspection at any and all times by authorized representatives of the city. The licensed wholesaler shall attach the third copy of such invoice to a weekly report to be made to the city clerk as provided in section 4-99.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 34)
Sec. 4-98. - Monthly report by licensed wholesaler.
(a)
Every wholesaler licensed hereunder shall make a monthly report to the city clerk on forms prescribed and furnished by such clerk showing as to each delivery made during the preceding month:
(1)
Date of same;
(2)
Name of retailer to whom made;
(3)
Method of delivery;
(4)
Amount, number and size of containers; and
(5)
Tax collected.
(b)
Such report, together with a copy of each invoice delivered during the week as provided herein, shall be mailed to the clerk not later than the tenth day succeeding the month for which it is made.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 35)
Sec. 4-99. - Weekly report by licensed retailer.
(a)
Every retailer licensed hereunder shall make a weekly report to the city clerk on forms furnished by such clerk showing as to each delivery received during the week, among other things:
(1)
Date;
(2)
Name of wholesaler from whom purchased;
(3)
Amount, number and size of containers; and
(4)
Tax paid.
(b)
Such report shall be mailed to the clerk not later than the Tuesday night succeeding the week for which it is made.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 36)
Secs. 4-100—4-126. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - WINE

Sec. 4-127. - Selling by the package and at wholesale, retail.
The sale of wine by the package in the city is hereby made lawful. It shall be unlawful to sell, store or offer to sell wine at wholesale or retail within the corporate limits of the city without having the appropriate license for such sale, or to carry on such activity in violation of the terms of such license or this division.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 37)
Sec. 4-128. - Retail licensee qualifications.
The holder of any retail license to sell package wine in the city must be a person of good, moral character, a citizen of the United States, and a resident of the county for a period of at least two years immediately preceding the date said license is applied for.
(1)
Any licensee hereunder shall not have been convicted within a period of ten years of the date of his application of a felony or any violation of the laws of this state or any other state, or the laws of this city or any other city in the state relating to the sale of alcoholic beverages.
(2)
The licensee shall be responsible for the management and operation of the business for which the license is granted.
(3)
The applicant shall make a sworn statement to his qualifications in accordance with the provisions of this division and shall place the same on file with the city clerk for any licenses issued.
(4)
It shall be unlawful for any elected official or city department head or his spouse, or children, to have any whole, partial or beneficial interest in any license to sell wines.
(5)
No person shall be granted a license to engage in the sale of wine at retail unless such shall appear to the satisfaction of the mayor and council that such person is of good and moral character.
(6)
Any material misstatement of fact or omission or concealment of fact in the application shall be grounds for the revocation of the license issued, and the applicant shall be liable for prosecution for perjury under the laws of the state.
(7)
The city shall make such investigation of the qualifications of each applicant as it may deem necessary.
(8)
No license shall be granted to an applicant for a license for the sale of wine at retail unless such applicant is:
a.
A person at least 21 years of age or older who is currently a resident of the county;
b.
A partnership, all of whose partners are at least 21 years of age or older;
c.
A corporation incorporated under the laws of the state or authorized to do business in the state.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 38)
Sec. 4-129. - Wholesale licenses.
Licenses to sell wine at wholesale shall be issued only to those parties who are licensed by the state to sell and distribute wine at wholesale. Application for such license shall be made with the city clerk.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 39)
Sec. 4-130. - Sale on premises.
No holder of a license authorizing the sale of package wine at retail in the city, or any agent or employee thereof, shall sell wine upon the licensed premises to be consumed on such premises or permit wine to be consumed thereon.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 40)
Sec. 4-131. - Delivery of wine.
Wine shall be received at or delivered to the premises of a retail licensee for the sale of wine at retail by no means other than by a conveyance owned or leased and operated by a wholesale dealer with a permit from the state to sell wine at wholesale.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 41)
Sec. 4-132. - License fee.
(a)
Wholesale wine license. The applicant for a wholesale wine license shall pay the city clerk at the time of such license being issued an amount set by the council and retained in a schedule by the clerk as an annual license fee for operation.
(b)
Retail wine license. The applicant for a retail wine license shall pay the city clerk at the time of such license being issued an amount set by the council and retained in a schedule by the clerk as an annual license fee for operation for the calendar year. The fee for the sale of a beer license and a wine license for the same location and by the same person shall be set by the council and retained in a schedule by the clerk.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 42)
Sec. 4-133. - Bond.
(a)
No bond shall be required if the applicant for a wine license currently holds a license for the sale of malt beverages with the city. Should such applicant not have a malt beverage license, in order to ensure payment of the excise tax levied hereby and prior to the issuance of any retail wine license in the city, an applicant must post and file with the governing authority, along with his application, a cash performance bond or a surety bond with an approved insurance company authorized to do business in the state, such bond being conditioned upon the faithful observance and performance by the applicant of the rules and regulations contained in this division, and upon the payment of any taxes due the city.
(b)
Upon the violation of this division, or any portion thereof, such bond may be declared forfeited by a decision of the mayor and council, upon a hearing for that purpose, upon three days' written notice given to the licensee concerned.
(c)
Such bond shall be payable to the city clerk.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 43)
Sec. 4-134. - Taxation.
In addition to the license fee imposed in section 4-132, there is hereby assessed an excise fee, based upon sales, in an amount equal to the maximum amount allowed by the laws of the state, such amount being presently $0.22 per liter of wine sold, which shall be collected and paid in the following manner:
(1)
Tax on each wine liter shall be collected by the wholesale supplier of such wine at the time of delivery to the retail licensee, and the same shall be remitted on or before the tenth day of the month following the date of such sale.
(2)
Each wholesale supplier of wine doing business in the city shall maintain a copy of each invoice showing the date, place and license number of the retail seller of such wine. Such records shall be maintained for a period of at least one year and the same shall be open for inspection at any time by any authorized representative of the city.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 44)
State law reference— Local excise taxes on distilled spirits generally, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-80; due dates for excise taxes on wholesale sales of malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-81; local excise taxes on wine, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-60; municipal excise taxes on distilled spirits by the drink, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-130.
Sec. 4-135. - Hours of operation.
Wholesale suppliers of wine to retail licensees for the sale of wine in the city shall distribute and deliver such wine during daylight hours and prior to sunset on any given day.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 45)
Sec. 4-136. - Authority to issue licenses.
All applications for licenses hereunder shall be presented to the mayor and city council for approval and, if approved, the licenses shall be issued upon the payment of the required license fee and bond outlined herein.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 46)
Sec. 4-137. - Rules and regulations.
Licensees hereunder shall at all times comply with the laws of the state and the rules and regulations, promulgated from time to time, regulating the sale of wine by the commissioner of revenue of the state, and any violation of those regulations shall subject the licensee to revocation of his license.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 47)
Sec. 4-138. - Annual renewal.
All licensees hereunder shall be required to renew their licenses annually with the city clerk, on the forms provided by the city.
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 48)
Sec. 4-139. - License constitutes grant or privilege.
(a)
All licenses issued under this division shall constitute a mere grant or privilege to carry on or conduct the business covered by such license and shall be subject to the terms and conditions imposed by the city's Charter, this article, and any other applicable ordinances of the city, the Constitution, laws and regulations of the state, and the Constitution, laws, and regulations of the United States applicable thereto.
(b)
All licenses issued under this division shall have printed on the face the following:
"THIS LICENSE IS A MERE PRIVILEGE AND IS SUBJECT TO BEING REVOKED AND ANNULLED BY THE MAYOR AND CITY COUNCIL OF FAIRMOUNT AND IS SUBJECT TO LAWS, ORDINANCES AND REGULATIONS HEREAFTER ADOPTED."
(Ord. of 4-18-1991, § 49)
