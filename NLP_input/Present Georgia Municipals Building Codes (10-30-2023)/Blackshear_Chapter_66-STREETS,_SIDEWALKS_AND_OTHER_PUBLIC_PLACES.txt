ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 66-1. - Street names—Authority of council generally.
The city council by resolution may change, rename, or name an existing or newly established street within the city at any time, upon recommendation of the planning commission.
(Code 1987, § 16-1)
Sec. 66-2. - Same—Naming unnamed streets; changing names to avoid confusion.
(a)
In case of streets which bear no names the city council shall be empowered to choose names which they feel are appropriate and which are suitable to the residents of such street.
(b)
In the case of streets which bear a name in common with another street or if the name is similar enough so as to cause confusion, the city council shall be empowered to rename one of the streets.
(Code 1987, § 16-2)
Sec. 66-3. - Streets in subdivisions.
Every subdivision plat submitted to the council for approval shall bear upon its face the proper names of all streets, avenues, and other public ways dedicated to public use within the jurisdiction of the council, which shall first have been checked and approved by the planning commission.
(Code 1987, § 16-3)
Sec. 66-4. - Inspections of projections over public streets.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the building inspector to inspect semi-annually all awnings, sheds, coverings, and other projections extending over the public streets, sidewalks and roads within the corporate limits of the city; and to make a written report of such inspections to the city council.
(b)
Any awning, shed, covering, and other projection extending over the public streets, sidewalks and roads found to be in a defective or dangerous condition, shall be condemned and shall be immediately reported to the owner of such property and if the owner cannot be found, then to the occupant of such property.
(c)
If such condemned awning, shed, covering, and other projection extending over the public streets, sidewalks and roads are not repaired and placed in a safe condition within 30 days, it shall be the duty of the building inspector, with the aid and assistance of the police department, to remove such condemned awning, shed, covering and other projection.
(Code 1987, § 16-4)
Sec. 66-5. - Permit for construction of paved driveway.
(a)
An application for a permit to construct paved driveways on city rights-of-way shall be obtained from the city superintendent of public works, and that no such construction shall begin until such permit and pertinent specifications have been provided.
(b)
The fee for the permit shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges.
(c)
Driveways constructed on city rights-of-way, which are out of compliance with city specifications, shall be removed at the owner's expense.
(Code 1987, § 16-5; Ord. of 2-9-93, §§ 1—3)
Sec. 66-6. - Driveway construction permits.
(a)
An application for a permit to construct paved driveways on city rights-of-way shall be obtained from the city superintendent of public works. No such construction shall begin until said permit and pertinent specifications have been provided.
(b)
The fee for the permit shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges.
(c)
Driveways constructed on city rights-of-way, after the adoption of the ordinance from which this section derives which are out of compliance with city specifications shall be removed at the owner's expense.
(Ord. of 2-9-93, §§ 1—3)
Secs. 66-7—66-35. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - BUILDING NUMBERING SYSTEM

Sec. 66-36. - Uniform system established.
There is hereby established a uniform system for numbering buildings fronting on all streets, avenues, and other public ways in the city. All houses and other buildings shall be numbered in accordance with the provisions of this article.
(Code 1987, § 16-20)
Sec. 66-37. - Base lines; quadrants; diagonal streets.
(a)
Ware Street to the west of the intersection of Ware Street and U.S. 84 and U.S. 84 to the east of such intersection, shall constitute the base line for numbering buildings along all streets running northerly and southerly and shall be referred to as the east-west base line. Gordon Street, Main Street, (north of College Street) and College Street shall constitute the base line for numbering buildings along all streets running easterly and westerly and shall be referred to as the north-south base line.
(1)
Each building to the north of the east-west base line and facing a street running in a northerly direction shall carry a number and address indicating its location north of such base line.
(2)
Each building to the south of the east-west base line and facing a street running in a southerly direction shall carry a number and address indicating its location south of such base line.
(3)
Each building to the west of the north-south base line and facing a street running in a westerly direction shall carry a number and address indicating its location west of such base line.
(4)
Each building to the east of the north-south base line and facing a street running in an easterly direction shall carry a number and address indicating its location east of such base line.
(b)
All buildings on diagonal streets shall be numbered the same as buildings on northerly and southerly streets if the diagonal runs more from the north to the south, the same rule shall apply on easterly and westerly streets if the diagonal runs more from east to west. All buildings on diagonal streets having a deviation of exactly 45 degrees shall be numbered the same as buildings on northerly and southerly streets.
(Code 1987, § 16-21)
Sec. 66-38. - Commencement and succession of numbers.
The numbering of buildings on each street shall begin at the base line. All numbers shall be assigned on the basis of one number for each 25 feet except those fronting on the following streets: All buildings fronting on Main Street between U.S. 84 and Park Street or on Taylor Street between Highway Avenue and Central Avenue or on Repparde Street between Highway Avenue and Central Avenue or on Highway Avenue between Main Street and Repparde Street or on Central Avenue between Main Street and Repparde Street shall be assigned numbers on the basis of one number for each 15 feet of frontage.
(Code 1987, § 16-22)
Sec. 66-39. - Even-odd number assignment.
(a)
All buildings on the right side of each street running from the base line bear even numbers. All buildings on the left side of each street running from the base line shall bear odd numbers.
(b)
All buildings facing streets not extending through the base lines shall be assigned the same relative numbers as if the street extended to the base line.
(Code 1987, § 16-23)
Sec. 66-40. - Separate numbers for each entrance.
Where any building has more than one entrance serving separate occupants, a separate number shall be assigned to each entrance serving a separate occupant. If the building is not located on a lot, parcel or tract which would permit the assignment of one number to each such entrance, letters shall be used, as set forth in section 66-43.
(Code 1987, § 16-24)
Sec. 66-41. - Directional prefixes.
All streets perpendicular to and crossing the east-west base line shall be given a prefix of "north" for the section which lies to the north of the base line and a prefix of "south" for the section which lies to the south of the base line. All streets perpendicular to and crossing the north-south base line shall be given a prefix of "east" for the section which lies to the east of the base line and a prefix of "west" for the section which lies to the west of the base line.
(Code 1987, § 16-25)
Sec. 66-42. - Display of numbers.
(a)
The assigned number shall be displayed within 30 days after receiving the assignment.
(b)
The numbers shall be conspicuously placed immediately above, on or at the side of the main entrance so that the number can be seen plainly from the street line.
(c)
Whenever any building is situated such that the main entrance is more than 50 feet from the street line or such entrance is obscured, then the numbers may be placed upon a gatepost, fence, tree, post or other appropriate place so as to be easily discernible from the street.
(d)
The numerals shall be no less than three inches in height and be made out of a durable and highly visible material. Such numerals shall be placed on a contrasting background.
(e)
There shall be a penalty incurred of $1.00 for each week which the owner of a building fails to display the assigned number after the assignment of the number.
(Code 1987, § 16-26)
Sec. 66-43. - Letter suffixes.
Where only one number can be assigned to a building, the owner, occupant, or agent of such building who shall desire distinctive numbers for different parts of such building shall use the suffix A, B, C, etc., as may be required.
(Code 1987, § 16-27)
Sec. 66-44. - Supplying information of numbers; resolution in case of conflict.
It shall be the duty of the superintendent of public works to inform any party applying therefor, of the numbers assigned to or embraced within the limits of any lot or property as provided in this article. In case of conflict as to the proper number to be assigned to any building, the superintendent of public works shall determine the number of such building.
(Code 1987, § 16-28)
Sec. 66-45. - Obtaining for, and affixing numbers to, new structures.
(a)
Whenever any house, building, or other structure shall be erected or located in the city, in order to preserve the continuity and uniformity of numbers of the houses, buildings, and other structures, it shall be the duty of the owner to procure the correct numbers as designated from the superintendent of public works for the property and to immediately fasten the numbers so assigned upon such structure or building as provided by this article.
(b)
No building permit shall be issued for any house, building, or other structure until the owner has procured from the superintendent of public works the official number of the premises. Final approval of any structure shall be withheld by the city building inspector until permanent and proper numbers have been affixed to such structure.
(Code 1987, § 16-29)
Secs. 66-46—66-70. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - PARADES AND DEMONSTRATIONS[2]

Sec. 66-71. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Assembly means any group of persons who assemble or congregate on any public street, alley or sidewalk in the city for the purpose of displaying, advertising, promoting, exhibiting, advocating or otherwise attracting public attention or for any cause or thing.
Parade means any group of persons marching or promenading on and along any public street, alley or sidewalk in the city for the purpose of displaying, advertising, promoting, exhibiting, advocating or otherwise attracting public attention to or for any cause or thing.

Sec. 66-72. - Approval required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to parade, assemble or congregate on any public street, alley or sidewalk in the city for any purpose specified in section 66-71, unless the city council shall have previously approved and authorized such parade or assembly. Any person participating in or attending any parade or assembly which has not been authorized and approved by the city council shall be considered as a part of the group constituting such parade or assembly, and upon conviction thereof shall be punished as provided in section 1-12.
(Code 1987, § 17-12(b))
Sec. 66-73. - Application.
Any person desiring to parade or assemble on any of the public streets, alleys or sidewalks of the city for the purposes in this article shall file an application with the mayor, setting forth the time, place and purpose of such parade or assembly, and obtain a permit before conducting such parade or assembly.
(Code 1987, § 17-12(c))

ARTICLE IV. - PUBLIC RIGHTS-OF-WAY CLOSING AND/OR ABANDONMENT

Sec. 66-100. - Intent.
[The intent of this article is] to establish regulations for the closing and/or abandoning of roads, streets, alleys or public easements in the city; to provide for the harmonious, orderly and progressive development of streets/transportation facilities; to provide for application procedures and data required for consideration; to provide for the enforcement of this article; to provide for fees payable under this article; and for other purposes.
(Ord. of 7-10-07(2), § 1)
Sec. 66-101. - Application.
Application for city road, right-of-way or easement closing or abandonment shall be made to the city clerk's office on an application provided by the city. The applicant shall outline the nature of the request and specify the anticipated follow-on use of the road, right-of-way, or easement for which the request is made. The applicant shall provide a plat that shows the right-of-way or easement for which the application is being filed. The plat shall be prepared by a land surveyor registered by the state in accordance with the Georgia Plat Act, O.C.G.A. § 15-6-67.
(Ord. of 7-10-07(2), § 1)
Sec. 66-102. - Review.
(a)
The city public works superintendent shall coordinate the review of the request with the community development director, city administrator, city attorney, police chief, fire chief, and utility providers (power, telephone, cable, and gas) and other departments as necessary. All comments made by reviewers shall be written and returned to the city public works superintendent within 30 days of the application filing date.
(b)
The city public works superintendent shall prepare staff report based upon the written findings that shall be forwarded to the city council.
(c)
The public works superintendent shall contact the city council clerk to schedule a date for a public hearing with the city council on their next regular meeting date.
(d)
The city clerk or his/her designee shall advertise a public hearing, giving the nature of the request, the place, date, and time of hearing in the local newspaper at least fifteen days before the hearing. The beginning and ending of the road or easement closing/abandoning shall be posted with the same information by the city clerk. The advertisement shall state the proposed closing/abandonment has "ceased to be used by the public and no longer serves any useful public purpose."
(e)
The city administrator will place the abandonment request item on the city council official public hearing and meeting agenda as advertised and posted.
(Ord. of 7-10-07(2), § 1)
Sec. 66-103. - Determination by the city council.
(a)
If the city council approves the closing and abandonment, the city attorney shall prepare a quitclaim deed and file it for recording with the Pierce County Superior Court if the identity of the grantee for such a deed is clear. The submitted plat may require a revision if the city council placed conditions on the approved abandonment. The applicant shall bear the cost of any required plat modification. The action is final only after the deed is recorded by the city attorney. The original deed (if any) shall be mailed to the applicant(s) by the city clerk.
(b)
No application for abandonment shall be made, or considered, if any portion of the property sought to be abandoned has been the subject of an abandonment application filed, or considered by the city council, within the immediate preceding one year.
(Ord. of 7-10-07(2), § 1)
Sec. 66-104. - Fee.
(a)
Unopened or dirt street or alley. Any request of an unopened, dirt street or alley submitted under the provisions of this article shall be accompanied by a $450.00 non-refundable application fee. The check shall be made payable to the "City of Blackshear." The applicant may withdraw the application or request a postponement of the hearing date once, by submitting a written request to the city administrator, at least seven calendar days, prior to the date set for the public hearing. If a postponement is requested, the applicant will bear the additional administrative charges (advertising and administrative costs) as determined by the city administrator.
(b)
Paved street. Any request to close and abandon a paved or otherwise improved open street/road under the provisions of this article shall be accompanied by a non-refundable fee of $450.00 and the current department of transportation item mean value of improvements in the right-of-way to reimburse the city for the funds spent on placing improvements in the street. The cost/value shall be determined by the public works superintendent or another office designated by the city council and submitted by written report to the city administrator prior to the public hearing by the city.
(c)
Cul-de sac required. Any abandonment that results in creating a dead-end road/street shall require the applicant to construct a cul-de-sac at the dead-end road/street. The newly constructed cul-de-sac shall be constructed of the same material as the dead-end road/street. Applicant, in addition to the application fee, shall incur all cost of the cul-de-sac construction, including but not limited to, construction, drainage improvements, right-of-way dedication, etc.
(Ord. of 7-10-07(2), § 1)
Sec. 66-105. - Definitions.
The law of the state recognizes three types of roadways:
(1)
Fee-simple roadway under which complete title is vested in the city. The city actually has a deed to the roadbed and the adjoining right-of-way. If the road is closed, the city has to dispose of the real property at public sale.
(2)
Easement-type roadway is where the public has no title vested in the city but merely an easement. The adjacent landowners own the ground underneath the road right-of-way.
(3)
Conditionally owned roadway is where the city owns the road right-of-way, but the right-of-way reverts to the owner if the city ceases using the right-of-way for road purposes.
(Ord. of 7-10-07(2), § 1)
Sec. 66-106. - Closing and abandoning policies and factors.
(a)
Policies. In order for a road to be closed, the city council shall determine that all or part of the road has ceased to be used by public so as to no longer serve any substantial useful purpose. This decision is made by the adoption of an ordinance, which is then recorded on the minutes of the city council. Owners of property located on the road shall first be notified of the impending ordinance prior to its passage by the city council.
(b)
Cul-de-sac. A street/road approved by the city council to be closed/abandoned with a dead-end shall be provided with a cul-de-sac. The cul-de-sac construction should be funded and completed entirely by the applicant before the quit-claim deed(s) are signed by the mayor. The construction shall meet city standards and receive city approval.
(c)
Considered factors. There are several factors, which should be considered by the city council in deciding whether or not to close a road or street. These factors are hereinafter set out.
(1)
Source of title. Does the city own fee simple title to the road/street or merely an easement for use by public for road purposes?
(2)
Present use of road, street or alley. How is the road, street, or alley currently being used?
a.
General public;
b.
Limited use by a small group;
c.
Location of public utilities; or
d.
Unopened right-of-way.
(3)
Past history. What use has been made of the road/street in the last 20 years?
(4)
Potential need for road in the future. What is the potential for development along the proposed closing/abandoning?
a.
To serve future growth area.
b.
Transportation plan projects future right-of-way needed.
c.
Volume to capacity ration shows need to retain street.
(5)
Cost involved in closing/abandoning or opening road/street.
a.
Obstructions in or along right-of-way or at ends of road/street.
b.
Attorneys fees and court costs projected to exceed value of road/street.
c.
Other.
(6)
Availability of other means of travel to take place of vehicular traffic using roadway.
a.
Public transportation.
b.
Adjoining landowners' assistance.
c.
Neighborhood.
d.
Emergency vehicles.
e.
Garbage pickup.
f.
Other.
(7)
Benefit to public in continuing public usage of roadway.
a.
Serves adjoining landowners.
b.
Serves neighborhood traffic needs.
c.
Provides access for emergency vehicles.
d.
Allows garbage pick-up and other services.
e.
Other.
(8)
Benefit to public in closing road.
a.
Eliminates road grading expense.
b.
Eliminates traffic movement hazards.
c.
Allows better land use plan for development of adjoining land.
d.
Other.
(9)
Adverse impact to public in closing road.
a.
Prevents landowner(s) from accessing public road.
b.
Interrupts comprehensive transportation plan project.
c.
Interrupts neighborhood to neighborhood transportation connection.
d.
Other.
(Ord. of 7-10-07(2), § 1)
Sec. 66-107. - Enforcement.
The enforcement of requirements of this article shall be through city attorney or other official designated by the city council.
(1)
The owner, or agent of the owner, of any land adjoining a road proposed to be closed and/or abandoned and located within the jurisdiction of this article or any other person who closes any road or records in Pierce County Superior Court clerk's office any deed or plat or map abandoning a road before such road closing and/or abandoning proposal has been approved by the city council shall be guilty of a violation of this article.
(2)
Any person, firm, corporation or agent of the owner of any land who shall violate a provision of this article, shall be guilty of a violation of this article. Each such person, firm or corporation shall be guilty of a separate offense for each and every day or portion thereof during which any violation of any of the provisions of this article is committed, or continued and upon conviction of any such violation such person shall be punished by sentence of not less than five days nor more than ten days in jail and/or fine of not less than $500.00 nor more than $1,000.00 for each and every day or portion thereof in violation.
(3)
No plat, map, or plan of road closing or abandoning, adjoining land owned by any person, firm, corporation or agent of the owner of any land within jurisdiction of this article shall be filed in the office of the clerk of the Superior Court of Pierce County until it has been approved by the city council and such approval entered in writing on the plat/map by the city clerk. Any such person who files or records a plat of road closing or abandoning without such approval is guilty of violating this article and upon conviction, shall be punished as provided in subsection (2).
(4)
The clerk of superior court shall not file or record a plat of a road closing or abandoning which does not have the written approval of the city council, thereon.
(Ord. of 7-10-07(2), § 1)
