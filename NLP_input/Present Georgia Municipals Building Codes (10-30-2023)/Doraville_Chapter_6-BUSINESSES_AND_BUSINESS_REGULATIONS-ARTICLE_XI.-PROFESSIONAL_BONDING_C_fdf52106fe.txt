ARTICLE XI. - PROFESSIONAL BONDING COMPANIES[11]

Editor's note— Ordinance No. 00-09, adopted Oct. 2, 2000, deleted Article XI in its entirety and enacted new provisions to read as herein set out. Former Article XI pertained to similar subject matter and derived from Ord. No. 93-7, adopted Oct. 4, 1993; Ord. No. 97-12, adopted Nov. 3, 1997.

DIVISION 1. - LICENSING

Sec. 6-500. - Definition.
For the purposes of this article, a bonding or professional surety company shall mean any person or entity conducting a business of arranging, posting and acting as surety on bonds for accused in any state law or City ordinance violation coming within the jurisdiction of the City.
(Ord. No. 00-09, 10-2-00)
Sec. 6-501. - Application for license.
(a)
All persons desiring to obtain a occupation tax certificate to operate a bonding or professional surety company in the City shall file an application with the City Clerk on forms approved by the chief of police. The applicant must be an owner, partner, limited liability company member or majority stockholder in the enterprise.
(b)
Each applicant shall submit the following information at a minimum:
(1)
Trade name and business address;
(2)
Applicant's name and residence address;
(3)
Name and residence address of all interested persons, to include owners, partners, stockholders, limited liability company members, officers and directors together with financial and background information on these individuals;
(4)
Manager's name and residence address;
(5)
Employee's names, ages, and residence addresses, including all representatives who will function within the City on behalf of applicant; and
(6)
Such other information as may be required by the City Clerk or chief of police.
(c)
All applicants for a license to operate a bonding or professional surety company within the City and all employees of the business must be fingerprinted so as to conduct a background investigation of the applicant and shall furnish such further information as may be required by the City Clerk or the City Police Department, including the completion of an application and all accompanying documents.
(d)
A fee in an amount approved by the Mayor and City Council shall be submitted with each application as the cost of conducting the investigation of applicant. The application forms and the current approved fee shall be kept on file in the office of the City Clerk.
(e)
The license fee for the conduct of a bonding business shall be determined in accordance with section 6-602. The license shall be posted in a conspicuous place at the office of the bonding company, visible to any individual lawfully on the premises.
(Ord No. 00-09, 10-2-00; Ord. No. 04-17, § 1(A), 7-6-04)
Sec. 6-502. - Qualifications of applicants.
(a)
All interested persons, and any applicant shall at a minimum meet all requirements of a professional bondsman under state law.
(b)
No applicant, owner, partner, stockholder, officer, director, limited liability company member, or any other interested party connected with the business for which a license is applied, nor any employee, shall have been convicted of a felony, other crime involving moral turpitude or any illegal sale or possession of a controlled substance or dangerous drug under federal or state law. Any subsequent convictions, including nolo contendre pleas, of the above nature shall automatically act to void any such license and permits held, and any applicant shall be responsible for immediately notifying the City Clerk and the City Police Department, in writing, of such disposition of such case.
(c)
Each license holder shall submit an affidavit on a form supplied by the City Clerk each year at the time of renewing the license. The affidavit shall set out any changes in the information furnished in the original applications or any prior affidavits.
(d)
In the event the right of a bonding or professional surety company to conduct business is suspended or revoked in another jurisdiction, said suspension or revocation in another jurisdiction shall constitute grounds to suspend or revoke said license in the City of Doraville.
(Ord. No. 00-09, 10-2-00; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 11, 6-17-13)
Sec. 6-503. - Requirements of employees.
A personal identification card authorizing a person to act as a representative of the bonding or professional surety company will be issued by the City which shall expire on December 31 each year. Such identification must be renewed annually by submitting, not less than thirty (30) days prior to January 1 of each year, an application to the City Police Department for a renewal thereof. It shall be the responsibility of the occupation tax certificate applicant to ensure that the provisions of this section are complied with and that no employee possesses an expired identification card or permit while in the business's employment.
(Ord. No. 00-09, 10-2-00)
Sec. 6-504. - Engaging in bail bond business.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any elected official, officer of the court, law enforcement officer, or attorney in this state to engage either directly or indirectly in the bail bond business.
(b)
Any person who violates this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor as set in the laws of the State of Georgia.
(Ord. No. 00-09, 10-2-00)
Sec. 6-505. - Maximum number of bonding or professional surety companies.
There shall be no more than four (4) bonding or professional surety companies within the City.
(Ord. No. 00-09, 10-2-00)
DIVISION 2. - CONDUCT OF BUSINESS

Sec. 6-506. - Requirements of contract and security.
Any bonding company, prior to the conduct of any business in the City, shall post, or cause to be posted, with the City Clerk a surety bond and collateral account in favor of the City in the total amount of seventy-five thousand ($75,000.00) dollars. The surety bond shall be approved by the Mayor and City Council. The collateral account in the form of a certificate of deposit, endorsed over to the City, shall be in the minimum amount of twenty-five thousand ($25,000.00) dollars. The amount required to be posted, in the sole discretion of the Mayor and City Council, may be increased or decreased, but any increase or decrease shall only be effective after notice to any licensed bonding company and by appropriate amendment to this section. The surety bond and collateral account sum shall be given as additional security to the City for the faithful performance and payment of all obligations owed to the City as a bonding or professional surety company. Applicant shall enter into a written contract in such form as is deemed appropriate by the City setting forth the applicant's obligations as a bonding or professional surety company and shall, otherwise, comply with all reasonable rules and regulations now in effect or which may hereafter be established by the City. The posting of such bond and collateral account shall be a condition precedent to the issuance of any occupation tax certificate to any bonding company and shall be in such form as may be required by the City and all bonds shall be on forms acceptable to the City. In the event any claim is made by the City against the surety bond or the collateral account, the license of the defaulting company shall be suspended until the company shall restore the surety bond and/or collateral account to the original amounts. Failure to restore the surety bond or collateral account within fifteen (15) days shall result in the automatic termination of the licensed of the defaulting company to transact business in the City.
(Ord. No. 00-09, 10-2-00)
Sec. 6-507. - Payment for bail bonds; prenumbered receipt as evidence of payment by client.
Any bonding company engaged in the bail bond business, who accepts money or any other consideration for any bail bond which it executes, must for each payment received give to the person paying the money or giving the consideration a prenumbered receipt as evidence of payment, which receipt shall state the date, name of the principal, amount of money or consideration received and purpose for which received, number of the power of attorney form attached to the bond, penal sum of the bond, and the name of the person making payment or giving compensation. A bonding company must retain a duplicate copy of each receipt issued as part of its records.
(Ord. No. 00-09, 10-2-00)
Sec. 6-508. - Employee termination.
Any bonding company which terminates the appointment of any employee authorized to sign bonds shall immediately file written notice thereof with the chief of police and the City Clerk, together with a statement that notice has been given or mailed to the employee. Such notice filed shall state the reasons, if any, for such termination. Information so furnished shall be privileged and shall not be used as evidence in any action against the bonding company.
(Ord. No. 00-09, 10-2-00)
Sec. 6-509. - Display of signs.
(a)
Each bonding company duly licensed by the City shall be allowed to display a sign of its own making in a place designated by the chief of police, at or near the book-in section of the City jail. All signs shall be uniform and shall comply with the remaining provisions of this section.
(b)
One (1) sign per licensee shall be permitted and shall be limited to lettering and numbering of no more than three (3) inches high and one and one-half (1½) in width for each letter or numeral.
(c)
Signs shall be limited to two (2) lines to allow the name of the business at the top and the phone number at the bottom.
(d)
Signs shall be consistent in coloring and hung in the place designated by the chief of police. The placement of the signs shall be rotated by the chief of police on a regular basis.
(Ord. No. 00-09, 10-2-00)
DIVISION 3. - BONDS

Sec. 6-510. - Bond requirements.
All bonds posted by any bonding company as surety for the accused in offenses set forth in the bonding schedule referred to herein shall be in accordance with the current schedule of the City, and further shall be in accordance with any future bond schedules which may be promulgated by the City any time hereafter. All bonds shall be made returnable to the appearance date in the municipal court of the City.
(Ord. No. 00-09, 10-2-00)
Sec. 6-511. - Power of attorney.
A bonding company must attach to each bail bond a duly executed power of attorney in an amount of at least the penal sum of the bond.
(Ord. No. 00-09, 10-2-00)
Sec. 6-512. - Forfeiture.
Should any individual accused of violating any of the ordinances or laws referred to in the bonding schedule, as referred to above, and for whom a bonding company has acted as surety or posted bond, fail to appear on his appearance date, including continuances, then the bond of that individual failing to appear shall be declared forfeited, and the bonding company, which acted as surety thereon, shall have a period of one hundred twenty (120) days from such appearance date to deliver the individual up before the municipal court. At the time of the nonappearance, the judge shall order an execution hearing not sooner than one hundred twenty (120) days, but not later than one hundred fifty (150) days after such failure to appear. Notice of the execution hearing shall be served within ten (10) days of such failure to appear by certified mail to the surety at the address listed on the bond or by personal service to the surety within ten (10) days of such failure to appear at its home office or to its designated registered agent. Service shall be considered complete upon the mailing of such certified notice. If at the execution hearing, it is determined that the judgment should be entered, the judge shall so order and a writ fieri facias shall be filed in the office of the clerk of the municipal court and City Clerk. The bonding company or other surety shall then and there, upon demand, pay to the municipal court the penal sum of the bond as established by the cash bond schedule plus any court costs assessed by the judge. If payment is not made within ten (10) days in addition to any other remedy against such bonding company, the occupation tax certificates of the bonding company shall be suspended until the judgment absolute is satisfied. Payment of the judgment absolute becomes a final disposition and relieves the principal and surety of any further obligation to the City on the bond.
(Ord. No. 00-09, 10-2-00)
Sec. 6-513. - Termination.
In the event that the license of a bonding or professional surety company to transact business in the City is terminated for any reason, such company shall remain liable on all bonds which it has issued.
(Ord. No. 00-09, 10-2-00)
