Sec. 6-100. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, and phrases shall, for the purposes of this article, and except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning, be defined as follows:
(a)
Chief Financial Officer (CFO). The Chief Financial Officer (CFO) or such other person designated by the board to administer this article.
(b)
Due date. The 20th day after the close of the quarterly period for which tax is to be computed.
(c)
Guest room. A room occupied, or intended, arrayed or designed for occupancy, by one or more occupants for the purpose of living quarters or residential use.
(d)
Harris County. The County Government of Harris County, Georgia.
(e)
Hotel/motel. Any structure or any portion of a structure, including any lodging house, roominghouse, bed and breakfast inn, dormitory, Turkish bath, bachelor hotel, studio hotel, motel, motor hotel, auto court, inn, cabins or manufactured housing units, within or without a state park facility, public club, or private club, containing guest rooms and which is occupied, or is intended or designed for occupancy, by guests, whether rent is paid in money, goods, labor or otherwise. It does not include any jail, hospital, asylum, sanitarium, orphanage, prison, detention, or other buildings in which human beings are housed and detailed under legal restraint.
(f)
Occupancy. The use or possession, or the right to the use or possess any room or apartment in a hotel/motel or the right to the use or possession of the furnishings or to the services and accommodations accompanying the use and possession of the room.
(g)
Occupant/guest. Any person, who, for a consideration, uses, possesses, or has the right to use or possess any room in a hotel/motel under any lease, concession, permit, right or access, license to use or other agreement, or otherwise.
(h)
Operator. Any person operating a hotel/motel in Harris County, including, but not limited to, the owner or proprietor or such premises, lessee, sublessee, lender in possession, licensee or any other person otherwise operating such hotel/motel.
(i)
Permanent resident. Any occupant as of a given date who has or shall have occupied or has or shall have the right of occupancy of any guest room in a hotel/motel for more than 30 consecutive days next preceding such date.
(j)
Person. An individual, firm, partnership, joint adventure, association, social club, fraternal organization, joint stock company, corporation, nonprofit corporation or cooperative nonprofit membership, estate, trust, business trust, receiver, trustee, syndicate, or any other group or combination acting as a unit, the plural as well as the singular number, excepting the United States of America, the State of Georgia, and any political subdivision of either thereof upon which Harris County is without power to impose the tax herein provided.
(k)
Quarterly period. Any period consisting of three successive months of any year, beginning respectively with the months of January, April, July or October.
(l)
Rent. The consideration received for occupancy valued in money, whether receive in money or otherwise, including all receipts, case, credits, and property or services of any kind or nature, and also the amount for which credit is allowed by the operator to the occupant, without any deduction therefrom whatsoever.
(m)
Return. Any return filed or required to be filed as herein provided.
(n)
Tax. The tax imposed by this article.
(Ord. No. 3-91, intro ¶, 7-29-91; Ord. No. 02-18 , § 1, 9-18-2018)
Sec. 6-101. - Imposition and rate of tax.
There is hereby imposed an excise tax in the amount of five percent of the charge to the public, upon the furnishing for value to the public of any room or rooms, lodgings or accommodations furnished by any person licensed by or required to pay business or occupation taxes to Harris County for operating a hotel/motel within the meaning of this article. The tax imposed by this article shall be paid upon any occupancy had on and after the 1st day of January, 2019, although such occupancy is had pursuant to a contract, lease or other arrangement or agreement made prior to such date. The proceeds from this tax shall be expended pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51 et. seq., which provides for the imposition of this tax.
(Ord. No. 3-91, § I, 7-29-91; Ord. No. 02-18 , § 1, 9-18-2018)
Sec. 6-102. - Collection of tax by operator.
Every operator maintaining a place of business in Harris County, as provided in the preceding section, and renting guest rooms in Harris County, not exempted under section 6-103 of this article shall collect a tax of five percent on the amount of rent from the occupant.
(Ord. No. 3-91, § II, 7-29-91; Ord. No. 02-18 , § 1, 9-18-2018)
Sec. 6-103. - Exemptions.
No tax shall be imposed hereunder upon the fees or charges for any rooms, lodgings or accommodations furnished for use by a permanent resident. No tax shall be imposed hereunder upon the fees or charges for any rooms, lodgings or accommodations furnished for use by Georgia state or local officials or employees while traveling on official business. No tax shall be imposed hereunder upon the fees or charges for any rooms, lodgings or accommodations furnished to victims displaced by a natural disaster.
(Ord. No. 3-91, § III, 7-29-91; Ord. No. 02-18 , § 1, 9-18-2018)
Sec. 6-104. - Registration of operator; form and contents; execution; certificate of authority.
Every person engaging or about to engage in business as an operator of a hotel/motel in Harris County shall immediately register with the CFO of the Board of Commissioners of Harris County on a form provided by said CFO. Persons engaged in such business must so register no later than 30 days after the date of this article becomes effective, and the tax is imposed as set forth in section 6-101, but such privilege or registration after the imposition of such tax shall not relieve any person from the obligation of payment or collection of tax on and after the date of imposition thereof, regardless of registration. Such registration shall set forth the name under which such person transacts business or intends to transact business, the location of his place or places of business and such other information which would facilitate the collection of the tax as the CFO may require. The registration shall be signed by the owner if a natural person; in case of ownership by an association or partnership, by a member or partner; in the case of ownership by a corporation, by an officer. The CFO shall, after each registration issue without charge a certificate of authority to each operator to collect the tax from the occupant, provided, however, that such certificate shall not relieve the operator from obtaining any other required licenses, certificates or permits. A separate registration shall be required for each place of business of an operator. Each certificate shall state the name and location of the business to which it is applicable.
(Ord. No. 3-91, § IV, 7-29-91; Ord. No. 02-18 , § 1, 9-18-2018)
Sec. 6-105. - Determination, returns and payments.
(a)
Due date of taxes. All amount of such taxes shall be due and payable to the Board of Commissioners on a quarterly basis no later than 20 days after the quarterly period.
(b)
Return; time of filing; persons required to file; contents. No later than 20 days after the quarterly period, a return for the preceding quarterly period shall be filed with the CFO showing the gross rent, rent from permanent residents, rent from Georgia local or state officials, or rent from victims displaced by a natural disaster, taxable rent, amount tax collected or otherwise due for the related period, and such other information as may be required by the CFO.
(c)
Collection fee allowed operators. Operators collecting the tax shall be allowed a percentage of the tax due and accounted for and shall be reimbursed in the form of a deduction in submitting, reporting and payment of the amount due, if said amount is not delinquent at the time of payment. The rate of the deduction shall be the same rate authorized for deductions from state tax as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-52, as now or hereafter amended.
(Ord. No. 3-91, § V, 7-29-91; Ord. No. 02-18 , § 1, 9-18-2018)
State Law reference— Deductions from transient occupancy tax, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-52.
Sec. 6-106. - Deficiency determinations.
(a)
Recomputation of tax; authority to make; basis of recomputation. If the CFO is not satisfied with the return or returns of the tax or the amount of the tax required to be paid to Harris County by any person, the CFO may compute and determine the amount required to be paid upon the basis of any information within his/her possession or that may come into his/her possession. One or more deficiency determinations may be made of the amount due for one or more quarterly period.
(b)
Interest on deficiency. The amount of the determination shall bear interest at the rate of one percent per month, or fraction thereof, from the due date of taxes.
(c)
Notice of determination; service of. The CFO shall give to the operator written notice of his/her determination. The notice may be served personally or by mail; if by mail such service shall be addressed to the operator at his address as it appears in the records of the CFO. Service by mail is complete when delivered by certified mail with a receipt signed by addressee or his agent.
(d)
Time within which notice of deficiency determination to be mailed. Except in the case of failure to make a return, every notice of a deficiency determination shall be mailed within three years after the 20th day of the calendar month following the quarterly period for which the amount is proposed to be determined, or within three years after the return is filed, whichever period should last expire.
(Ord. No. 3-91, § VI, 7-29-91; Ord. No. 02-18 , § 1, 9-18-2018)
Sec. 6-107. - Determination if no return made.
(a)
Estimate of gross receipts. If any person fails to make a return, the CFO shall make an estimate of the amount of the gross receipts of the person, or as the case may be, of the amount of the total rentals in Harris County which are subject to tax. The estimate shall be made for the period or periods in respect to which the person failed to make the return and shall be based upon any information which is or may come into the possession of the CFO. Written notice shall be given in the manner prescribed in section 6-106(c).
(b)
Interest on amount found due. The amount of the determination shall bear interest at the rate of one percent per month, or fraction thereof, from the 20th day of the month following the quarterly period for which the amount or any portion thereof should have been returned, until the day of payment.
(Ord. No. 3-91, § VII, 7-29-91; Ord. No. 02-18 , § 1, 9-18-2018)
Sec. 6-108. - Administration of article.
(a)
Authority of CFO. The CFO shall administer and enforce the provisions of this article for the collection of the tax imposed by this article.
(b)
Records required from operators, etc.; form. Every operator providing rooms, lodgings or accommodations shall keep such records, receipts, invoices, and other pertinent papers in such form as the CFO may require for a period of five years.
(c)
Examination of records; audits. The CFO or any person authorized in writing by the Board of Commissioners of Harris County may examine the books, papers, records, financial reports, equipment and other facilities of any operator and any operator liable for the tax, in order to verify the accuracy of any return made, or if no return is made by the operator, to ascertain and determine the amount required to be paid.
(d)
Authority to require reports; contents. In administration of the provisions of this article, the board of commissioners may require the filing of the reports by any persons or class of persons having in such person's or person's possession or custody information relating to rentals of rooms, lodgings or accommodations which are subject to the tax. The reports shall be filed with the board of commissioners when required by the board of commissioners and shall set forth the rental charged for each occupancy, the date or dates of occupancy, and such other information as the board of commissioners may require.
(Ord. No. 3-91, § VIII, 7-29-91; Ord. No. 02-18 , § 1, 9-18-2018)
Sec. 6-109. - Violations.
Any person violating any of the provisions of this article shall be deemed guilty of an offense and upon conviction thereof shall be punished as provided in section 1-7 of the Code of Ordinances of Harris County. Each such person shall be guilty of a separate offense for each and every day during any portion of which any violation of any provision of the article is committed, continued, or permitted by such person, and shall be punished accordingly. Any operator or any other person who fails to register as required herein, or to furnish any return required to be made, or who fails or refuses to furnish a supplemental return or other data required by the board of commissioners who renders a false or fraudulent return shall be deemed guilty of an offense and upon conviction thereof shall be punished as aforesaid.
(Ord. No. 3-91, § IX, 7-29-91; Ord. No. 02-18 , § 1, 9-18-2018)
Sec. 6-110. - Collection of tax.
(a)
Action for tax; time for. At any time within three years after any tax or any amount of tax required to be collected becomes due and payable and at any time within three years after the delinquency of any tax or amount of tax required to be collected, the board of commissioners may bring an action in a court of competent jurisdiction in the name of Harris County to collect the amount delinquent together with interest, court fees, filing fees, attorney's fees and other legal fees incident thereto.
(b)
Duty of successors or assignees of operator to withhold tax from purchase money. If any operator liable for any amount under this article sells or transfers his business or quits the business, his successors or assigns shall withhold sufficient of the purchase price to cover such amount until the former owner produces a receipt from the CFO showing that it has been paid or a certificate stating that no amount is due.
(c)
The CFO is hereby authorized to issue a fi.fa. for execution and levy to satisfy the amount of any tax, penalty or interest due but not paid under the provisions of this section.
(d)
Liability for failure to withhold; certificate of notice of amount due; time to enforce successor's liability. If the purchaser of a business fails to withhold purchase price as required, he shall be personally liable for the payment of the amount required to be withheld by him to the extent of the purchase price.
(e)
Tax credit, or interest paid more than once or erroneously or illegally collected. Whenever the amount of any tax or interest has been paid more than once, or has been erroneously or illegally collected or received by Harris County under this article, it may be offset by the board of commissioners. If the operator or person determines that he has overpaid or paid more than once, which fact has not been determined by the CFO, he will have three years from date of payment to file a claim in writing stating the specific ground upon which claim is founded. The claim shall be audited. If the claim is approved by the board of commissioners, the excess amount paid Harris County may be credited on any amounts then due and payable from the person by whom it was paid, or his administrators or executors.
(Ord. No. 3-91, § X, 7-29-91; Ord. No. 02-18 , § 1, 9-18-2018)
