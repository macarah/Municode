DIVISION 3. - DOWNTOWN DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— The Downtown Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-42-1 et seq.

Sec. 74-81. - Declaration of need.
There is determined and declared to be a present and future need for a downtown development authority (as more fully described and defined in the Downtown Development Authorities Law) to function in the city.
(Code 1989, § 8-1-41)
State Law reference— Creation of authority, O.C.G.A. § 36-42-4.
Sec. 74-82. - Activation.
There is activated in the city the public body corporate and politic known as the "Woodstock Downtown Development Authority," which was created upon the adoption and approval of the Downtown Development Authorities Law.
(Code 1989, § 8-1-42)
Sec. 74-83. - Members.
The downtown development authority shall consist of seven directors, each of whom is a taxpayer of the city, residing in the city, and not less than four of whom either have or represent a party who has an economic interest in the redevelopment and revitalization of the downtown development area. Directors shall serve for six-year staggered terms.
(Code 1989, § 8-1-43)
Charter reference— Boards, commissions and authorities, § 3.13.
Sec. 74-84. - Organization.
The board of directors elected under this division shall organize itself, carry out its duties and responsibilities and exercise its powers and prerogatives in accordance with the terms and provisions of the Downtown Development Authorities Law as it now exists and as it might hereafter be amended or modified.
(Code 1989, § 8-1-44)
Sec. 74-85. - Downtown development area defined.
The downtown development area shall be that geographical area described in an appropriate map on file in the city clerk's office where it is available for public inspection. The area, in the judgment of the city council, constitutes the central business district of the city as contemplated by the Downtown Development Authorities Law.
(Code 1989, § 8-1-45)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 74-86. - Effect.
The action taken by the city council as specified in this division is not intended in any way to affect any public corporation, industrial development, downtown development or payroll authority previously created by legislative act or constitutional amendment including, without limitation, its existence, purpose, organization, powers or function.
(Code 1989, § 8-1-46)
