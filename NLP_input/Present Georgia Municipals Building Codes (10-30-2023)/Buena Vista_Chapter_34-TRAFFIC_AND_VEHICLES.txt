ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 34-1. - Authority of Mayor and Council to regulate traffic.
The authority to make and enforce all rules and regulations governing traffic and the use of the streets of the City by vehicles and pedestrians shall be vested in the Mayor and Council, and the Mayor and Council shall have full power to designate directions of traffic, time limits, and locations for parking, reservation of parking places, maximum and minimum speeds insofar as same shall not conflict with the laws of the state, to establish through streets, truck routes, and stop crossings, and to install such street signs and indicators and markings as shall be necessary or advisable for the operation and enforcement of such rules and regulations. No such rule or regulation shall be effective unless and until appropriate signs, indicators or markings are installed.
(Prior Code, § 18-1; Code 2004, § 74-1)
Sec. 34-2. - Uniform Rules of the Road.
(a)
Adoption by reference. The provisions of O.C.G.A. § 40-6-1 et seq., known as the Uniform Rules of the Road, save and except such portions as are hereafter deleted, modified, or amended, are adopted as and for the traffic regulations of the City with like effect as if recited in this section.
(b)
Penalties. Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this chapter shall be punished by a fine of not more than $1,000.00 or by imprisonment for not more than 365 days, or by both such fine and imprisonment.
(Prior Code, §§ 18-2, 18-3; Code 2004, § 74-2)
Sec. 34-3. - Permits for parades and processions.
No funeral, procession, or parade containing 50 or more persons or ten or more vehicles, excepting the Armed Forces of the United States, the military forces of the state, and the forces of the Police and Fire Departments, shall occupy, march, or proceed along any street except in accordance with a permit issued by the City Clerk with the approval of the Mayor and Council and such other regulations as are set forth in this chapter which may apply.
(Prior Code, § 18-4; Code 2004, § 74-3)
Sec. 34-4. - Funeral processions to be identified.
A funeral composed of a procession of vehicles shall be identified as such by having the headlights of each vehicle turned on. Such funeral, unless under the supervision and control of a law enforcement agency, shall allow sufficient space between each vehicle or combination of vehicles in the procession to enable any other vehicle to enter and occupy such space without danger.
(Prior Code, § 18-5; Code 2004, § 74-4)
Secs. 34-5—34-26. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - TRAFFIC CONTROL DEVICES

Sec. 34-27. - Authority to place and maintain electric traffic control devices.
As an aid in facilitating the movement of traffic on the streets of the City and in reducing accidents, the City's authorized representative is hereby authorized and directed to place and maintain electric traffic control devices and signals at such locations as may be designated from time to time by resolution of the Mayor and Council.
(Prior Code, § 18-8; Code 2004, § 74-31)
Sec. 34-28. - Other devices.
The City's authorized representative shall place and maintain other than electrically operated traffic control devices when and as required under this chapter and other traffic ordinances of the City to make effective the provisions of this chapter and such ordinances and, with the approval of the Mayor and Council, may place and maintain such additional traffic control devices as he may deem necessary to regulate traffic under this chapter and other traffic ordinances of the City or under state law, or to guide or warn traffic.
(Prior Code, § 18-9; Code 2004, § 74-32)
Secs. 34-29—34-59. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - OPERATION AND PARKING OF VEHICLES

Sec. 34-60. - Speed restrictions.
(a)
No person shall drive a vehicle on a highway at a speed greater than is reasonable and prudent under the conditions then existing.
(b)
Where no special hazard exists, the following speeds shall be lawful, but any speed in excess of such a limit shall be prima facie evidence that the speed is not reasonable or prudent and is unlawful:
(1)
25 miles per hour within the corporate limits, except as otherwise provided in this section.
(2)
25 miles per hour within the zones designated, and when signs are posted at either end of a speed zone.
(c)
The fact that the speed of a vehicle is lower than the foregoing prima facie limits shall not relieve the driver from the duty to decrease speed when approaching and crossing an intersection, when approaching a hill crest, when traveling upon any narrow or winding roadway, or when a special hazard exists with respect to pedestrians or other traffic or by reason of weather or highway conditions, and speed shall be decreased as may be necessary to avoid colliding with any person, vehicle, or other conveyance on or entering the highway in compliance with legal requirements and the duty of all persons to use due care.
(Prior Code, § 18-14; Code 2004, § 74-61)
Sec. 34-61. - Parking lines angle, and parallel parking.
On streets where white lines are shown for guidance, parking must conform with such lines. The driver must park his vehicle entirely within the boundaries designated by such lines. No person shall park and leave unattended a vehicle in a roadway other than parallel with the edge of the roadway, headed in the direction of traffic, and with the curbside wheels of the vehicle within 12 inches of the edge of the roadway, except upon those streets which have been marked or signed for angle parking and on such streets vehicles shall be parked at the angle to the edge of the roadway indicated by such marks or signs.
(Prior Code, § 18-15; Code 2004, § 74-62)
Sec. 34-62. - Speed signs.
Speed signs shall be erected by the City. Any person convicted of a violation of the City speed limits shall be punished as provided for by law.
(Code 2004, § 74-64; Ord. No. 110)
