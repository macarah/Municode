ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 2-1. - Fiscal year.
All fiscal years shall begin on July first (1st) and shall end on June thirtieth (30th) of each year.
Sec. 2-2. - Macon-Bibb County vehicle decal.
All motor vehicles owned or leased by Macon-Bibb County with the exception of the motor vehicle furnished the mayor, unmarked motor vehicles utilized by the sheriff, the arson investigator's car, the county manager's car, and the fire chief's car, shall display in a prominent location, a decal replica of the seal of Macon-Bibb County not less than eight (8) inches in diameter on each side of the motor vehicle. All vehicles required to display a decal shall also display a vehicle identification number below each decal. Such number shall be at least three (3) inches high. Motor vehicles requiring a decal may bear the decal of the City of Macon or Bibb County until such time as they are repainted or replaced at which time they shall be made to bear the decal of Macon-Bibb County.
Sec. 2-3. - Private use of Macon-Bibb County-owned vehicles; bid process.
(a)
Except as specifically excepted in this section, no Macon-Bibb County-owned vehicle furnished to a Macon-Bibb County employee shall be driven by such employee from his place of work to his residence or from his residence to his place of work. At all times when the vehicle is not being used on Macon-Bibb County business, it shall be parked in a designated space in proximity to the place of work of the person to whom the vehicle is furnished. The provisions of this section, however, shall not apply to the vehicles utilized by the sheriff, the chief of the fire department, the assistant chief of the fire department, the director of emergency management, the director of business development services, the director of recreation, the county manager, the mayor, and to other employees who may be excepted from time to time by resolution of the commission when it is found to be in the public interest, health, safety and welfare.
(b)
Any such vehicle furnished to an employee of Macon-Bibb County is furnished as a matter of privilege and not of right, and is not a part of any employment contract, and the vehicle may be withdrawn from the employee at any time.
(c)
Before bids for any passenger vehicles are invited, the proposed bid invitation, including specifications, shall be filed with the county clerk, and submitted to the commission for their approval. If commission does not disapprove the proposed bid invitation and specifications within thirty (30) days after filing, the invitation for bid may be issued.
(d)
The mayor and commission shall limit the number of vehicles furnished to employees for use on Macon-Bibb County business to a specified number of vehicles for each department.
( Ord. No. O-15-0029, § 1, 7-7-2015 )
Editor's note— Ord. No. O-15-0029, § 1, adopted July 7, 2015 , amended the title of § 2-3 to read as set out herein. Previously § 2-3 was titled "Private use of Macon-Bibb County-owned vehicles; wheelbase limitation; bid process."
Sec. 2-4. - Scholarships.
Any person residing within Macon-Bibb County may make application to the commission for an undergraduate scholarship to Mercer University, without being connected to any school. Applications for the fifteen (15) undergraduate scholarships to be awarded by the commission are to be made to the mayor on blanks prepared for the purpose, giving name, age and residence of applicant, name of parent or guardian, applicant's ability or inability to pay for the tuition, and the like, and the names of three (3) citizens as references.
Sec. 2-5. - Formal action by governing body to bind Macon-Bibb County.
Without formal action by the governing body no act or omission by a Macon-Bibb County employee with regard to private property will be an act of dominion or acceptance on the part of the Macon-Bibb County to repair, maintain or acquire the property, or for any other purpose, except that Macon-Bibb County is not prohibited from ascertaining existing easements, encroachments or other such rights.
Sec. 2-6. - Appointments.
(a)
No person or entity shall be appointed to any board of position of trust requiring the confirmation of the commission unless such person or entity complies with the certificate of good standing requirements set forth in Division 1 of Article II of Chapter 26 of this Code prior to consideration of such appointment by the commission.
(b)
A criminal background check, which shall be submitted to the commission for review, is required on all persons nominated for appointment to such bodies prior to such appointment being approved.
(Ord. No. O-22-0011 , § 5, 3-1-2022)
Sec. 2-7. - Macon-Bibb County buildings.
Except in cases where imminent danger to the public health as determined by the appropriate authorities requires such action, no Macon-Bibb County building shall be demolished, destroyed or otherwise disposed of except upon approval by the governing authority of Macon-Bibb County.
Sec. 2-8. - Bonds subject to appropriation process.
The expenditure of any bond funds administered by Macon-Bibb County shall be pursuant to Macon-Bibb County appropriations process, as provided for in the Charter, this Code and any other regulations of Macon-Bibb County.
Sec. 2-9. - Balanced budgets.
Macon-Bibb County shall adopt and operate under balanced budgets as to all Macon-Bibb County funds (other than fiduciary funds) and such budgets shall reflect any and all transfers between funds anticipated to be necessary in order to balance all such funds.
Sec. 2-10. - Reserve accounts and fund balance of the general fund.
(a)
Working capital reserve. The finance director shall maintain at least a five million six hundred thousand dollars ($5,600,000.00) working capital reserve in a separate bank account and such account shall be accessed only by resolution of mayor and commission, provided that, in the event there are not adequate funds to maintain such a reserve, the county manager shall work to build such reserves. Any and all interest earned in the working capital reserve account shall accumulate in said account and shall not be transferred or expended except by resolution of mayor and commission.
(b)
In addition, it shall be the policy of Macon-Bibb County that the fund balance of the general fund shall be greater than the average quarterly operating expenses of the prior fiscal year.
(1)
The fund balance shall be defined as the net current assets of the general fund, or the difference in current assets and current liabilities of the general fund.
(2)
Current assets shall be defined as all cash, cash equivalents, investments, accounts receivable, and funds due from other funds and governments. The working capital reserve account is included in the scope of this definition.
(3)
Current liabilities shall include accounts payable, retainage payable, accrued liabilities, due to other funds, deferred revenues, and advances from other funds.
(c)
The county manager shall work to increase the fund balance of the general fund at a rate of at least five hundred thousand dollars ($500,000.00) each fiscal year until the minimum amounts referenced in subsections (a) and (b), are reached.
(d)
Until such minimum fund balance of the general fund is attained, it shall be the policy of Macon-Bibb County that no year end operating surplus shall be budgeted to meet the next year's operating expenses, unless and except to the extent such funds are needed in the event of an emergency, defined as:
(1)
A fiscal financial crisis acknowledged by resolution of mayor and commission that would otherwise require Macon-Bibb County to pursue the issuance of a tax anticipation note ("TAN"); and/or
(2)
Any situation where Macon-Bibb County, or any portion thereof, has been officially declared to be in a state of emergency by the governor of Georgia.
(e)
Where working capital reserve account funds have been accessed, the finance director shall repay the funds to such account as follows:
(1)
All grant or reimbursement funds related to repayment of funds expended by Macon-Bibb County for the emergency shall be deposited into the working capital reserve account immediately upon receipt; and/or
(2)
Funds used in lieu of obtaining a TAN shall be repaid to the working capital reserve account as determined by the commission or as would be required by the laws of the state for repaying a TAN.
(3)
The goal of repayment is to replenish the working capital reserve account as soon as possible upon receipt of the respective funds by Macon-Bibb County, and to maintain at least the minimum required fund balance of the Macon-Bibb County's general fund.
(f)
Landfill closure capital reserve. The finance director shall maintain a four million five hundred thousand dollars ($4,500,000.00) landfill closure capital reserve in a separate bank account and such account shall be accessed only by resolution of mayor and commission, provided that, in the event there are not adequate funds to maintain such a reserve, the finance director shall present to mayor and commission a plan to build such reserves in a separate bank account, in as expeditious a manner as feasible. Such plan shall be approved by mayor and commission as part of the budgetary process. Once the plan is approved, funds shall be accumulated in such account pursuant to the approved plan and shall not be expended except by resolution of mayor and commission. Any and all interest earned in the landfill closure capital reserve account shall accumulate in said account and shall not be transferred or expended except by resolution of mayor and commission.
Sec. 2-11. - Travel expenditure restrictions.
(a)
No funds of Macon-Bibb County shall be expended to fund or reimburse any travel to any country certified by the Secretary of State of the United States of America or her designee as not fully cooperating with United States counter-terrorism efforts or designated by the United States Department of State as a state sponsor of terrorism.
(b)
Further, no funds shall be expended to pay for any expenditure or reimburse any expenditure made by any person when the expenditure was incurred in any country so certified by the Secretary of State or her designee or designated a state sponsor of terrorism.
(c)
This section shall apply to all expenditures as listed in subsections (a) and (b), and any such funds expended in violation of this section shall be reimbursed by the person incurring the expenditures. Any person who violates this paragraph shall be personally liable to Macon-Bibb County for any charges associated with the improper expenditure.
Sec. 2-12. - Designating buildings which constitute the courthouse.
(a)
The structure which stands at the corner of Second and Mulberry Streets and which has historically been known and used as the county courthouse, together with the entrances to and appurtenances thereto, is hereby designated as the Macon-Bibb County Courthouse together with such other properties as are hereinafter specified.
(b)
The grand building and the grand theater building, together with the entrances to and appurtenances thereof, which are now or may hereafter be used by Macon-Bibb County or by any other public agency, are hereby declared to be a part of the courthouse for all purposes.
(c)
The building commonly known as the courthouse annex which has been constructed between and connects with the original county courthouse and the grand building and the grand theater buildings referred to in the preceding paragraph is hereby designated as a part of the courthouse of Macon-Bibb County for all purposes.
(d)
The grounds owned by Macon-Bibb County in Macon-Bibb County, Georgia, bounded by First, Oglethorpe, Second and Hawthorne Streets, together with the improvements thereon owned by Macon-Bibb County and specifically, but not limited to, the building known as the law enforcement center, together with its entrances and appurtenances, are hereby declared to be a part of the Macon-Bibb County Courthouse for all purposes.
Sec. 2-13. - Smoking prohibited in all Macon-Bibb County-owned buildings.
(a)
Prohibited activity. The smoking of tobacco in all buildings owned or independently operated by Macon-Bibb County, Georgia, is hereby prohibited. Designated smoking areas outside of the buildings may be provided for employees and the public.
(b)
Enforcement. Any law enforcement officer shall be authorized to issue a summons for any violation of this section except that they may arrest for instances when:
(1)
The alleged violator refuses to provide the officer with such person's name and address and any proof thereof as may be reasonably available to the alleged violator; or
(2)
The alleged violator refuses to cease such person's illegal activity after being issued a citation.
(c)
Penalty. The first and second violations shall be treated as warnings. The third and each subsequent violation thereafter shall constitute an offense punishable under this code.
(d)
Other remedies. No provision of this section shall be construed to impair or replace any common law or statutory cause of action or any other legal remedy of any person for injury or damage arising from any violation of this section or from the privacy rights of such individuals.
Sec. 2-14. - Destruction of paid general obligation bonds and interest coupons.
From time to time, as that officer shall determine, the finance director is hereby authorized and empowered to select paid general obligation bonds of Macon-Bibb County, Bibb County, or the City of Macon and paid interest coupons originally issued in connection with general obligation bonds and to provide for their destruction. Destruction shall be accomplished by cremation or burning in the presence of the finance director who shall thereafter execute a memorandum in writing containing a general description of the bonds and beginning and ending numbers thereof and a general description of any interest coupons so destroyed. Any paying agent for the Macon-Bibb County general obligation bonds who shall have custody of paid bonds or coupons issued in connection therewith shall likewise have authority to destroy such paid bonds and coupons from time to time. In such case, destruction shall be accompanied by cremation or burning the paid bonds and coupons, and two officers of the paying agent shall attest to the fact, and execute a written certificate describing in general the bonds cremated together with the beginning and ending numbers thereof and a description of the interest coupons so destroyed. Such certificate shall be forwarded to the finance director.
Sec. 2-15. - Destruction of revenue bonds and coupons.
Revenue bonds and coupons issued in connection therewith may be destroyed in like manner as prescribed for general obligation bonds.
Sec. 2-16. - Audits of agencies disbursing tax funds.
(a)
Required annually. Every agency receiving at least $5,000.00 in tax funds derived from Macon-Bibb County shall have an audit made at least annually by a certified public accountant who shall also prepare and furnish a management letter as an adjunct thereto. Those agencies receiving less than $5,000.00 in tax funds derived from Macon-Bibb County shall submit a compilation made at least annually by a certified public accountant.
(b)
Time frame. Such audit shall be accomplished as soon as practicable after the end of each fiscal year of each agency receiving funds from Macon-Bibb County and a copy thereof furnished to the mayor and commission along with a copy of the management letter not later than 120 days following the end of the fiscal year for reporting agencies having expenditures of less than $3,000,000.00 and 180 days following the end of the fiscal year for reporting agencies having expenditures in excess of $3,000,000.00.
(c)
Extension of time. For good cause shown to the finance director, time for filing the audit report and management letter with the mayor and commission be extended by 30 days, but his or her authority shall go no farther.
(d)
Review and report. Macon-Bibb County will cause a review of the audit to be made and will report to the agency involved any exceptions which it finds to the management of public funds entrusted to it.
(e)
Penalty for failure to file. Macon-Bibb County reserves the right to suspend further payments in the event an agency fails to file a timely audit report and management letter prepared by a certified public accountant.
Sec. 2-17. - Code of ethics.
(a)
Principal policies. The public trust requires public servants to fulfill their duties faithfully and honestly and to subordinate any personal interest which conflicts with the public interest. A public servant is a trustee of the people and should strive to further the general welfare and not use public office or position to unethically improve his or her own private standing. The principal policies that form the foundation of this code of ethics are as follows:
(1)
The trust of citizens in their government is cultivated when individual public servants act with integrity and when the public is aware that its servants act with integrity.
(2)
The constitutions, laws and regulations of the United States and the State of Georgia and ordinances of Macon-Bibb County should be upheld as a minimum standard of conduct.
(3)
The most effective way to eradicate unethical practices is consistently to act with the highest moral principles and react appropriately to the ethical decisions of others.
(4)
Macon-Bibb County officials should exercise sound judgment and apply ethical principles in making decisions that in any manner reflect upon their elected office.
(5)
All citizens should be treated with courtesy, impartiality and equality.
(b)
Intent. It is the intent of this code of ethics that Macon-Bibb County officials shall not knowingly engage in any activity that is incompatible with the proper discharge of their official duties or which would tend to impair their judgment or actions in the performance of their official duties. Furthermore, Macon-Bibb County officials should avoid any action that might result in or create the appearance of:
(1)
Using public office or position in an unethical manner for private gain,
(2)
Impeding Macon-Bibb County efficiency or economy, or
(3)
Affecting adversely the confidence of the public in the integrity of those who conduct the affairs and business of Macon-Bibb County.
(c)
Definitions. As used in this Section, the following words shall have the meaning ascribed to them below [unless otherwise required by context]:
(1)
Benefit means anything of monetary value that a reasonably prudent person would recognize as being likely to be intended to influence a Macon-Bibb County official or employee in the performance or non-performance of an official action.
(2)
Immediate family means spouse, child, parent or sibling of a Macon-Bibb County official.
(3)
Financial interest means any potential direct or indirect financial gain, payment or benefit accruing to a Macon-Bibb County official or their immediate family as a result of a contract or transaction that is, or may be, the subject of an official act or action by, or with, Macon-Bibb County. Macon-Bibb County officials shall be deemed to have a financial interest in any business in which the Macon-Bibb County official or their immediate family member, individually or in the aggregate, owns five percent or more of the business or is a director, officer, employee, or agent of the business.
(4)
Macon-Bibb County official means the mayor, commission members.
(5)
Third degree of consanguinity or affinity shall include the following familial relationships whether by blood or marriage: parents, children, grandparents, grandchildren, great-grandparents, great-grandchildren, siblings, aunts and uncles, nieces and nephews, and spouse.
(d)
Acceptance of gifts. Macon-Bibb County officials shall not solicit or accept, directly or indirectly, any benefit or payment from any person, corporation or group that has, or is seeking to obtain, a contractual or other business or financial relationship with the Macon-Bibb County.
(e)
Financial interests.
(1)
Macon-Bibb County officials may not:
a.
Have a financial interest that conflicts with their responsibilities and duties as trustees of the public good, or
b.
Directly or indirectly engage in financial transactions as a result of, or primarily relying upon, confidential information obtained in the course of their office or received due to their position.
(f)
Use of public property. A Macon-Bibb County official shall not use Macon-Bibb County property of any kind for other than officially approved activities, nor shall he or she direct a Macon-Bibb County employee to use such property for other than official purposes.
(g)
Use of confidential information. A Macon-Bibb County official shall not directly make use of, or permit others to make use of, for the purpose of furthering a private interest, Macon-Bibb County information not made available to the general public.
(h)
Coercion. A Macon-Bibb County official shall not use his or her position in any way to coerce, or give the appearance of coercing:
(1)
Another person to provide any benefits to him or her or to his or her immediate family as defined herein, or
(2)
A Macon-Bibb County employee, an appointed official of the Macon-Bibb County, or a contract employee to provide any benefit to him or her or to his or her immediate family as defined herein, or
(3)
Any judge in the outcome of matters before the court.
(i)
Purchases. A Macon-Bibb County official shall not order any goods or services for the Macon-Bibb County or on behalf of Macon-Bibb County without proper authorization in compliance with the Macon-Bibb County Code.
(j)
Reserved.
(k)
Conflicts of interest. In accordance with the Charter, no elected official, appointed officer, or employee of Macon-Bibb County, or any authority or agency to which the Charter applies shall represent private interests, other than his or her own, in any action or proceeding against Macon-Bibb County or any portion of its government; or vote or otherwise actively participate in the negotiation or the making of any contract between the Macon-Bibb County government and any business or entity in which he or she has a substantial financial interest.
(l)
Macon-Bibb County employees. A Macon-Bibb County official shall not use a Macon-Bibb County employee for personal or private business during regular business hours or during the employee's scheduled shift.
(m)
Travel expenses. A Macon-Bibb County official shall not draw per diem or expense money from Macon-Bibb County to attend a seminar, convention, conference or similar meeting and then fail to attend the seminar, conference, convention or similar meeting.
(n)
Commitments. A Macon-Bibb County official shall not act or create the appearance of acting on behalf of the Macon-Bibb County by promising to authorize or prevent any future official action of any nature, without proper authorization.
(o)
False statements. No Macon-Bibb County official shall make a false or materially misleading statement or in any manner commit fraud in relation to any Macon-Bibb County or public business.
(p)
Disclosure. A Macon-Bibb County official or employee shall disclose the nature of any financial interest he or she has in a matter at the time such matter is presented to the mayor and commission for discussion and/or action. Such disclosure shall be recorded into the minutes of the meeting and become part of the public record.
(q)
Deliberation and vote prohibited. No Macon-Bibb County official shall participate in a Macon-Bibb County Commission Committee or Commission meeting during the discussion, debate, deliberation or vote, or otherwise take part in the decision-making process on any agenda item, any official act or action in which he or she has a financial interest. Provided, however, that such elected official shall not be precluded from addressing a commission committee or the commission during the discussion of any agenda item or any official act or action in the same manner as a member of the general public.
(r)
Duty to leave meeting. To avoid the appearance of impropriety, after any member of the commission is determined to have a financial interest or a potential financial interest in any matter, they shall leave their regular seat as a member of the commission or commission committee and not return to it until deliberation and action on the matter is completed. Provided, however, that such elected official shall not be precluded from addressing a commission committee or the commission during the discussion of any agenda item or any official act or action in the same manner as a member of the general public.
(s)
Ethics hearing officer. The chief judge of municipal court, or his or her designee who shall be another municipal court judge, is hereby designated as the ethics hearing officer. Any person serving as the ethics hearing officer shall receive his or her ordinary salary and benefits for the time spent in such service, and may be removed for cause by a majority vote of the commission. If any ethics hearing officer is removed from cause, recused from a matter, or is otherwise unavailable to preside over a matter brought under this Section, then a substitute ethics hearing officer shall be appointed according to the procedures for reassigning a case upon the disqualification of a judge, as provided in the Uniform Rules for the Municipal Courts.
(t)
Receipt of complaints. All complaints against a Macon-Bibb County official shall be filed in writing with the county manager to be referred to the ethics hearing officer in such form as may be prescribed by the ethics hearing officer. Any person making a complaint against a Macon-Bibb County official shall be responsible for the prosecution of his or her complaint, and the failure to diligently prosecute any complaint shall be cause for dismissal. The municipal court may, at its discretion, provide general forms or instructions to facilitate the prosecution or defense of any ethics matter. Upon receipt of a complaint in proper form, the ethics hearing officer shall:
(1)
Review it to determine whether the complaint is unjustified, frivolous, patently unfounded or fails to state facts sufficient to invoke disciplinary action or is to be considered for further investigation. All allegations in any complaint received shall automatically stand denied by any Macon-Bibb County official named in the complaint, The ethics hearing officer shall enter a written record of findings as to the reasons why a complaint is or is not subject to dismissal under subsections (t)(1) or (t)(2) of this Section prior to scheduling any hearing or allowing any discovery on any ethics complaint.
(2)
Be empowered and required to dismiss those complaints that are unjustified, frivolous, patently unfounded or that fail to state facts sufficient to constitute a violation of this Section; provided, however, that a rejection of such complaint by the ethics hearing officer shall not deprive the complaining party of any action he or she might otherwise have at law or in equity against the Macon-Bibb County official.
(3)
For purposes of this Section, only allegations of acts made or attempted in the Macon-Bibb County official's capacity as a Macon-Bibb County official, or under color of his or her official capacity, where such acts constitute:
(i)
a violation of Article 1 of Chapter 10 of Title 16 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to abuse of governmental office;
(ii)
a violation of Chapter 5 of Title 21 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, relating to ethics in government, or of any regulation promulgated under the authority of such Chapter;
(iii)
a violation of O.C.G.A. § 50-14-6, relating to open meetings;
(iv)
a violation of O.C.G.A. § 50-18-74, relating to open records;
(v)
any felony under the laws of the State of Georgia;
(vi)
any misdemeanor crime under the laws of the State of Georgia involving moral turpitude;
(vii)
a violation of Section 20 of the Charter of Macon-Bibb County, relating to ethical conduct in government;
(viii)
any act which would, if completed, create a substantial likelihood of providing some unethical material benefit or detriment to any private person or entity;
(ix)
any act which would, if completed, create a substantial likelihood of providing some unethical material detriment to the financial or operational interests of Macon-Bibb County or any public employees, retirees, board, agency, office, department, or governmental entity operating in Macon-Bibb County; or
(x)
any act which creates a reasonable appearance that one of the acts described in subsections (t)(3)(i) through (t)(3)(ix) has occurred or been attempted shall be deemed to state facts sufficient to constitute a violation of this Section.
(4)
Be empowered to provide for the complainant and official to conduct reasonable discovery with regard to any claim raised in the complaint, issue subpoenas for evidence or witnesses at the request of the complaining party or the official named in any complaint, to set appropriate limitations on the conduct and completion of discovery and to add evidence produced and admitted into evidence at a hearing to the file containing such complaint;
(5)
Be empowered to take evidence and hold hearings. The ethics hearing officer shall give the Macon-Bibb County official notice and an opportunity for a hearing;
(6)
Be empowered to recommend to the commission that any Macon-Bibb County official found to have violated any provision of this Section by a preponderance of the evidence receive a reprimand, censure, or be requested to resign his or her office;
(7)
Be empowered to refer a complaint to the sheriff of Macon-Bibb County or other law enforcement agency for investigation and possible prosecution;
(8)
The decision by the ethics hearing officer shall be reduced to writing and served upon all parties at interest within five days of reaching a decision.
(u)
Penalties. Upon majority vote of the commission, any violation of this code of ethics shall subject the offender to disciplinary action including reprimand, censure, or a request to the offending Macon-Bibb County official that he or she resign their office.
(v)
Right to appeal. Any Macon-Bibb County official adversely affected by a disciplinary action taken by the commission may appeal the decision to the Bibb County Superior Court in accordance with the laws of the State of Georgia. Provided, however, that no action of a decision maker refusing or failing to take action pursuant to this code of ethics shall be reviewable by the superior court.
(w)
No limitation on powers of commission. Nothing in this Section shall be construed as limiting in any way the powers of the Macon-Bibb County Commission to reprimand, censure, or request the resignation of any public official directly and without first requiring any complaint to be filed in accordance with this Section.

Sec. 2-18. - Nepotism and self-dealing.
(a)
Pursuant to Section 20(h) of the Macon-Bibb County Charter, the County shall at all times comply with the following provisions concerning nepotism. Any appointment or offer of employment made in violation of these provisions, whether known or unknown, shall be invalid upon discovery. If any employees are discovered to be working in a position which violates this Section, then one or more of such employees shall be transferred to new positions to return to compliance with this Section within sixty days, or else the unelected employee(s) with the least amount of tenure working for Macon-Bibb County (from among each group of those related employees working in violation of this Section) shall be terminated from employment until such conflict is resolved. The human resources department shall make reasonable efforts to assist affected employees in finding a suitable position to transfer into, but such employee shall not be entitled to any favorable consideration or advantage when applying for such transfer position. For purposes of this Section, all references to relatives shall include step-relatives, half-relatives, and adoptive relatives, as well as relatives of spouses.
(1)
No person shall be appointed by the mayor, commission, or any member thereof, to any office, agency, or employment who is related within the third degree of consanguinity or affinity to the mayor or any member of the commission. For purposes of this subparagraph, persons are considered to be "appointed" to a position only if federal, state, or local laws require the affirmative vote of the commission as a precursor to the person assuming such position. This subparagraph shall not operate to prohibit the employment by Macon-Bibb County of any relatives of the mayor or any commissioner to any position which does not require an affirmative vote of the commission as a precursor to assuming such position.
(2)
No board created under the authority of Macon-Bibb County shall be authorized to appoint or employ any person who is related within the third degree of consanguinity or affinity to any member of such board.
(3)
No head of any County department shall be authorized to appoint or employ any person who is related within the third degree of consanguinity or affinity to such department head in any capacity within their department;
(4)
No person shall be appointed or employed in any capacity on behalf of the County if that person is related within the third degree of consanguinity or affinity to any of the following people:
(A)
any person who is or would be in the chain of command for such person within that person's department, according to the official County organizational chart, if they were hired to or employed in a given position; or
(B)
any person who is making hiring, termination, salary, assignment, promotion, or disciplinary decisions regarding a given position or employee, provided that this rule shall not prohibit relatives of the mayor, commissioners, or county manager from being employed with the County;
(C)
notwithstanding the foregoing, this subparagraph (a)(4) of this Section shall not apply to any person who works for any County department solely in a volunteer capacity, or as part of any non-competitive or unpaid internship or externship program, or in any paid non-permanent position not to exceed twenty weeks per calendar year.
(5)
No person shall be transferred or promoted into any position of employment for the County if doing so would cause either that employee or another employee to be in violation of this Section.
(6)
If two unrelated employees become related within the third degree of consanguinity or affinity during the term of their employment, and as a result, fall out of compliance with this Section then one or more of such employees shall be transferred to new positions to return to compliance with this Section within sixty days, or else the unelected employee(s) with the least amount of tenure working for Macon-Bibb County (from among each group of those related employees working in violation of this Section) shall be terminated from employment until such conflict is resolved.
(7)
Notwithstanding any of the foregoing provisions, any employees who were working for the County as of January 1, 2014, in positions which would otherwise be in violation of this Section shall be authorized to remain in such positions or promote up from such positions provided that all other restrictions of this Section, including restrictions on transfers, are met.
(8)
During deliberations of the Macon-Bibb County Commission, it shall not be considered a conflict of interest for the mayor or any commissioner to debate or vote on any matter affecting the salary or employment status of a relative as an incidental part of considering the salary or employment status of an entire department or office, sub-department or sub-office division, or class of job positions, of which such relative is a part, provided however, that if the salary or employment status of a relative of the mayor or any commissioner is being considered in isolation, then such mayor or commissioner would have a conflict of interest and would have to disclose the conflict and abstain from voting in accordance with this Code.
(9)
In addition to the foregoing, all elected officials shall be subject to state laws regarding nepotism, including O.C.G.A. § 45-10-80, as amended.
(b)
Pursuant to Section 20(d) of the Macon-Bibb County Charter,
(1)
No elected member of the governing authority of Macon-Bibb County nor any company or business in which such elected member or a member of his or her immediate family presently has an interest, direct or indirect, which is incompatible with the proper discharge of his or her official duties or which would tend to impair his or her independence of judgment or action in the performance of his or her official duties, shall do business with an authority associated with Macon-Bibb County nor an authority whose members in whole or in part are appointed by the governing authority of Macon-Bibb County; and
(2)
No member of an authority of the restructured government shall do business with an authority associated with Macon-Bibb County or an authority whose members in whole or in part are appointed by the governing authority of Macon-Bibb County.
