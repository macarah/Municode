DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-648. - Contracts.
No contract for public work, amounting to $100,000.00 or more, shall be let except to the lowest and best bidder, and then, only after the contractor shall give the city sufficient performance and payment bonds, as provided in O.C.G.A. § 13-10-1 et seq. If the contract for public works amounts to $100,000.00 or more, opportunity for competitive bidding shall be given after reasonable advertisement for at least five days prior to the date set for receiving such bids.
(Code 1975, § 2-7; Code 1985, § 2-286; Ord. No. 14-124, § 1, 9-23-2014; Ord. No. 21-111, § 1, 5-4-2021)
Sec. 2-649. - City manager as purchasing and contracting agent; duties specified.
(a)
(1)
The city manager shall be the purchasing and contracting agent of the city. All purchases and contracts made by the city manager requiring the expenditure of money in the amount of $40,000.00 or more shall be approved by the board of city commissioners.
(2)
In the capacity of purchasing agent, the city manager shall conduct all sales of personal property which the board may authorize to be sold and which have become unnecessary or unfit for the city's use. Such sales involving personalty of $40,000.00 or less may be made without approval by the board of city commissioners.
(3)
As contracting agent, the city manager is authorized, to the extent of $40,000.00, to execute in the name of and on behalf of the city all contracts for the purchase and sale of personal property and services of every kind and description, and contracts for construction. If sales or purchases or other contracts involve amounts in excess of $40,000.00, opportunity for competitive bids shall be given after reasonable advertisement thereof, and in the case of contracts for public work, the requirements of section 2-648 shall be complied with; provided, however, that the requirements contained herein and in section 2-648 respecting competitive bids after reasonable advertisement shall not apply in cases of contracts for professional services such as consultants, architects, engineers, accountants, attorneys, and other professional advisors, and no advertisement or solicitation for competitive bids or proposals shall be necessary in such cases. Prior to execution of any such contract, same shall be reviewed by the appropriate department head or such department head's designee.
(4)
Contracts involving the purchase or sale of personal property or services or construction involving a maximum of $40,000.00 may be executed by the city manager without the prior approval of the board of city commissioners for emergency maintenance or repairs.
(5)
The city clerk is hereby authorized and directed to affix the seal of the city to any contract executed in the name of the city by the city manager.
(b)
Notwithstanding the provisions of this section, and the provisions of section 2-648, the city manager shall have the authority to make and execute all contracts, franchises and leases in connection with the operation of the Albany Civic Center. The city manager may delegate all or any portion of her authority in this connection, but shall remain responsible for the proper use of such authority.
(c)
Notwithstanding the provisions of this section, and the provisions of section 2-648, the city manager shall have authority to make emergency purchases of parts and materials and to contract for labor necessary for the repair and maintenance of all mechanical systems and equipment, up to an amount not to exceed $40,000.00, without first obtaining approval of the board of city commissioners and without advertising and obtaining competitive bids.
(d)
The city manager may recommend to the city commission approval of a contract involving the procurement of goods, services or equipment/construction items where a procurement unit of a public entity has established such a contract through a process that substantially meets the requirements of Georgia law. Any such agreement is not binding on the city until the matter has been submitted to and formally approved by the mayor and board of commissioners.
(Code 1975, § 2-8; Code 1985, § 2-287; Ord. No. 82-194, § 1, 11-9-1982; Ord. No. 86-184, § 1, 7-8-1986; Ord. No. 89-154, § 1, 8-8-1989; Ord. No. 14-124, § 1, 9-23-2014; Ord. No. 15-109, § 1, 3-24-2015)
Sec. 2-650. - Requests for donations or special appropriations.
All requests for donations or special appropriations shall be in writing, addressed to the board of city commissioners, setting forth the amount and purposes to be used. No such donation or special appropriation shall be made unless such proposed donation or special appropriation be first referred to the finance and administration committee and reported back to the board of city commissioners.
(Code 1975, § 2-9; Code 1985, § 2-288)
State Law reference— Limitation on contributions of municipalities, Ga. Const. 1983, art. IX, § II, ¶ VIII.
Sec. 2-651. - City manager and indefinite quantity contracting.
Notwithstanding section 2-648, the city manager may enter into contracts utilizing indefinite quantity contracting with the following limitations: (a) each contract must not exceed $100,000.00; (b) the city manager's total contracting authority shall not exceed $2,500,000.00.
(Ord. No. 15-124, § 1, 10-20-2015)
Sec. 2-652. - City manager and state of emergency.
Notwithstanding section 2-648, the city manager or whomever she designates in writing may, subject to the provisions of this section, enter into contracts as well as approve invoices for services rendered on behalf of city. This section is effective only during times when (i) the emergency operations center has been activated and (ii) a state of emergency has been declared and is still in effect and (iii) such contracts or invoices were essential to the city being able to deliver services or promote public health, safety and welfare.
(Ord. No. 17-100, 1-10-2017)
Secs. 2-653—2-673. - Reserved.

Sec. 2-674. - Preliminary proposal; adoption or rejection.
Preliminary to any issue of bonds by the city, the proposal for the issue shall be referred to the public administration and finance committee or committee of the whole which committee may give diligent consideration of the proposal, making a thorough investigation touching the same and as early as practicable shall report to the board of city commissioners with their approval or disapproval of the proposal, giving their reasons for their action. No ordinance providing for a bond issue shall be in order until the action of the finance committee on the preliminary proposal has been adopted or rejected by the board of city commissioners.
(Code 1975, § 2-131; Code 1985, § 2-301)
Sec. 2-675. - Records information to be shown.
The director of administrative and financial services, shall procure and provide a suitable record to be kept on file in her office in which, upon written application and presentation of the bond by the holder thereof, she shall enter a description of each bond so presented, stating:
(1)
The number;
(2)
Of what issue;
(3)
The amount;
(4)
The date issued;
(5)
The date of maturity;
(6)
The rate and maturity of interest;
(7)
The date and number of ordinance authorizing;
(8)
How, and where payable, both as to principal and interest;
(9)
By whom signed, and such other data as may be necessary for a ready identification of the bond registered;
(10)
The name of the person registering the bond;
(11)
The capacity in which such person holds the bond;
(12)
For whose benefit the bond is registered;
(13)
To what extent the bond is registered, or whether as to principal or principal and interest.
(Code 1975, § 2-142; Code 1985, § 2-302)
Sec. 2-676. - Written application required for registration; negotiability generally.
All bonds evidencing the indebtedness of the city may, upon written application or request of the holders of the bonds or any of them, be registered both as to principal and interest or as to principal only, as set out in section 2-677, and to the extent so registered, shall become non-negotiable.
(Code 1975, § 2-141; Code 1985, § 2-303)
Sec. 2-677. - Registration application; information required.
(a)
In order that bonds may be registered as provided in section 2-676, the holder shall first make written application for such registration and shall present therewith the bond desired to be registered, which application shall be made to the city clerk, and shall state:
(1)
The number of bonds presented;
(2)
Of what issue;
(3)
The date;
(4)
The amount;
(5)
When due;
(6)
To what extent the bonds shall be registered.
(b)
An application for registration of bonds shall be signed by the applicant who, if holding bonds in any capacity other than for himself, shall sign the application in the name of the party for whose benefit the applicant holds, by himself, stating the capacity in which the applicant signs and attaching proof of the capacity in which the applicant signs, the sufficiency of which proof to be determined by the city attorney.
(Code 1975, § 2-143; Code 1985, § 2-304)
Sec. 2-678. - Registration statement; form.
After any bonds shall have been registered as provided by this division, it shall be the duty of the chief finance officer to stamp, print or write upon each bond a statement of the registration in substantially the following form, inserting in the blanks the matter applicable to each transaction, to wit:
Registered by _______. Nonnegotiable. As to principal and interest only to ______ of ______ at ______ or his legal representative at ______. This ___ day of ________, 20___.
CITY OF ALBANY,
___________
Chief Finance Officer
(Code 1975, § 2-144; Code 1985, § 2-305)
Sec. 2-679. - Disposition of coupons.
In the event any bond is registered both as to principal and interest, the chief finance officer shall tear off and destroy the coupons and stamp, write or print upon each bond a statement, properly dated, the amount and value of such coupons and that the interest, at the rate and on the date as provided by the coupons, will be paid only to the holder or the holder's legal representative or the chief finance officer shall make the entry as provided for in section 2-675 or a similar entry to the same effect upon each coupon and leave them attached or either, as may be desired by the holder.
(Code 1975, § 2-145; Code 1985, § 2-306)
Sec. 2-680. - Procedure for transfer after registration.
(a)
In order to transfer any bond which may have been registered under the provisions of this division, the holder thereof shall present the same to the chief finance officer and shall authorize such transfer, in writing, giving the name of the transferee, the number of the bond, of what issue and the date of issue and maturity. Such authority shall be signed and acknowledged in the presence of a notary public or some other officer authorized by law to administer oaths and such notary public or other officer shall certify, in writing and under seal of her office, that such authority was signed and acknowledged in her presence.
(b)
In addition to giving written authority, the holder of a bond shall enter a statement of the transfer on the face of each bond, properly dated and signed. Thereupon the chief finance officer shall enter the transfer of each bond opposite the original entry of registration in the book, under the provision of section 2-675, giving the name of the transferee and date of transfer and shall always enter the same on each bond over her official signature. The transferee may thereafter, in the manner prescribed in this section, also transfer such bond.
(Code 1975, § 2-146; Code 1985, § 2-307)
Sec. 2-681. - Negotiability after registration.
When any bond is registered as to principal only, the same may be made fully negotiable by transfer in the manner prescribed in section 2-680 in the general term "to bearer." When so done, the chief finance officer shall cancel all former entries on the face of the bond.
(Code 1975, § 2-147; Code 1985, § 2-308)
Sec. 2-682. - Annual tax for retirement.
For all bonds issued by the city there is hereby levied and assessed, in addition to all other taxes and for each year during the term for which such bonds run, an ad valorem tax upon all the taxable property in the city, in such amount as will be sufficient to pay such bonds, principal and interest at their respective dates of maturity. Such tax shall be promptly collected in each year in the same manner as other city taxes and when collected, such amounts shall be turned into the sinking fund and appropriated as provided in this Code.
(Code 1975, § 2-133; Code 1985, § 2-309)
Sec. 2-683. - Sinking fund—Established; withdrawals, etc.
All taxes collected for the payment of the principal and interest of city bonds shall be kept by the chief finance officer as a separate fund and shall be known as the sinking fund. Under no circumstances shall such fund be paid out by the chief finance officer for any other purpose than for the payment of the interest and principal on the bonds for which it was collected or for the purpose of investment as provided by law and city ordinance. If the chief finance officer or other officer honors or pays any warrant on such fund for any other purpose than those named in this section, they shall be guilty of malfeasance and liable to removal.
(Code 1975, § 2-161; Code 1985, § 2-310)
Sec. 2-684. - Same—Investment.
It shall be the duty of the chief finance officer to promptly make arrangements for the investment of and keeping invested the principal of the sinking fund.
(Code 1975, § 2-164; Code 1985, § 2-311)
Sec. 2-685. - Records to be kept.
The chief finance officer shall keep a suitable record with a complete and detailed statement and description of each bond issued and of force and the same shall at all times show the status of each bond.
(Code 1975, § 2-132; Code 1985, § 2-312)
Secs. 2-686—2-713. - Reserved.
