ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 28-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Building regulations means and includes the building regulations and standard codes heretofore adopted by the county, building regulations in effect in the county prior to such date, and fire regulations in effect in the county.
Closing means securing and causing a dwelling, building, or structure to be vacated.
County health officer or official means the official representatives of the county health department charged with the responsibility of inspecting and reviewing health and environmental matters throughout the county, which includes, but is not limited to, issues deemed nuisances under state law, the provisions of this chapter, or the ordinances of the county.
Dwelling, building, or structure means any building or structure or part thereof used and occupied for human habitation, commercial, industrial, or business uses or intended to be so used and includes any outhouses and appurtenances belonging thereto or usually enjoyed therewith and also includes any building or structure of any design.
Nuisance means anything that causes hurt, inconvenience, or damage to another, and the fact that the act done may otherwise be lawful shall not keep it from being a nuisance. The inconvenience complained of shall not be fanciful, or such as would affect only one of fastidious taste, but it shall be such as would affect an ordinary, reasonable person.
Owner means the holder of the title in fee simple and every mortgagee of record.
Parties in interest means persons in possession of a dwelling, building or structure and all individuals, association, and corporations who have interest of record in such dwelling, building or structure, including executors, administrators, guardians, and trustees.
Public officer means the building inspector, code enforcement officer, county manager, or any other county official who is authorized by law and this Code to exercise the powers prescribed by this chapter, or any agent of such officer or officers, or any other officer or officers designated by the county manager for purposes of this chapter.
Repair means closing a dwelling, building, or structure or the cleaning or removal of debris, trash, and other materials present and accumulated which create a health or safety hazard in or about any dwelling, building, or structure.
Unfit building means any dwelling, building, or structure described as follows:
(1)
Any building which is in violation of any of the provisions of the building, fire, health, sanitation or life safety code, regulation, or ordinance;
(2)
Any building in which a loadbearing wall or other vertical structural member lists, leans or buckles to such extent as to weaken the structural support of such wall or member below the minimum required by the building regulations;
(3)
Any building having an improperly distributed load on the floor or roof or some portion thereof;
(4)
Any building in which there is damage or deterioration of 33 percent or more of the supporting members or 50 percent or more of the nonsupporting enclosing or outside walls, exclusive of the foundation;
(5)
Any building with any part thereof so attached as to create a danger that it will fall or cause injury to person or property;
(6)
Any building which is so dilapidated, decayed, deteriorated, or damaged from any cause or in any manner as to create a danger of injury to person or property;
(7)
Any building likely to cause injury to person or property because it contains a weakened, defective, or deteriorated footing, foundation, flooring, floor support, ceiling, ceiling support, roof, or chimney;
(8)
Any building which has visible soil erosion adjacent to or under any structural support;
(9)
Any building in which any means of egress or portion thereof, including, for example, but not limited to, fire doors, closing devices, stairways, or fire escapes, is in disrepair or is in a dilapidated or nonworking condition or not of adequate size or is not arranged to provide a safe path of travel in case of fire or panic;
(10)
Any building in which there exists any of the general conditions itemized in O.C.G.A. § 41-2-10; or
(11)
Any building where there is the improper keeping of pets and animals on the premises, and/or the improper disposal of human and/or animal wastes.
Sec. 28-2. - Penalties.
(a)
Any person who violates any provision of this chapter shall be punished according to the general penalties described in section 1-9.
(b)
Action pursuant to subsection (a) of this section shall not be a bar to enforcement of this chapter by injunction or other appropriate remedy, and the code enforcement officer shall have the power to institute and maintain in the name of the county any and all such enforcement proceedings.
(c)
Nothing in this chapter shall be construed to abridge, limit, or otherwise impair the right of any person to maintain any action or other appropriate proceeding for damages or other relief on account of injuries to persons or property.
Sec. 28-3. - Working with other officers.
The county code enforcement officer shall have the authority to work in conjunction with and rely upon the advice, expertise or recommendation of the county health officer, regarding the inspection and review of issues throughout the county deemed nuisances under state law, the provisions of this chapter, or the ordinances of the county. This authority shall include the ability to issue summons or citations for violations of and to enforce the provisions of this chapter, based upon a complaint received from the county health official.

ARTICLE III. - PROCEDURES FOR ABATEMENT

Sec. 28-54. - Abatement of nuisances.
The magistrate court shall have jurisdiction to hear and determine the question of the existence of nuisances alleged to exist in the county, and if a nuisance is found to exist, to order its abatement.
Sec. 28-55. - Procedure for abatement of public nuisances.
(a)
Investigation and notice of complaint. Upon recommendation of the board of health or the health officer, police officer or employee of the county charged with enforcing the ordinances of the county, or the complaint of any resident of the county in writing, as to the existence of anything in the county constituting a public nuisance, the public officer charged with enforcing the provisions of this chapter shall make an investigation or inspection of the specific nuisance. If the public officer's investigation or inspection identifies circumstances constituting a nuisance, then the public officer or county attorney shall cause a written notice of the complaint setting out the alleged nuisance to be drawn up and served on the person alleged to be responsible for the nuisance and the owner of the property where the nuisance is alleged to exist, in accordance with the further provisions hereof. For the purpose of this chapter, the person returning such lot for county taxes for the year in which notice is given shall be taken to be the owner thereof.
(b)
Time-frame for response. The notice shall be directed to the person alleged to be maintaining, erecting, creating or keeping the alleged nuisance and to the owner of the property where the nuisance is alleged to exist; shall set out facts sufficient to show the nature of the alleged nuisance; identify the subject real property where the nuisance is located by appropriate street address and official tax map reference; contain a statement of the action sought by the public officer to abate the alleged nuisance, and shall give the person to whom the notice is directed notice to abate the nuisance within not more than 30 days from the date of service of the notice or to show cause before the county court at a date and time stated, which date shall be not less than 30 days nor more than 45 days after the filing of said complaint in the magistrate court, why said alleged nuisance should not be abated. The notice here provided for shall bear teste in the name of the magistrate or his designee and be signed by the county clerk. A copy of the notice shall be served instanter upon each person to whom it is directed.
(c)
Service of notice. The method of service of the notice above provided for shall be as follows:
(1)
If the person to be served is a resident of the county, by personal service by any constable of the county.
(2)
If the person to be served is a nonresident of the county but is a resident of the state, by personal service by any officer of the state authorized to serve process in civil cases under the Georgia Civil Practice Act (O.C.G.A. § 9-11-1 et seq.).
(3)
If the person to be served is a nonresident of the state, by publication of the notice once a week for two consecutive weeks in a newspaper in which the sheriff of the county publishes its legal notices. If the address of the person to be served is known, the clerk of the county shall mail a copy of the notice, as given to the publisher, to the person to be served at his known address within three days of the first publication of the notice. In the case of service by publication, the date of the newspaper in which the last publication appears shall be considered the date of service and the date required for abatement or appearance shall be not less than five days from the date of such newspaper.
Sec. 28-56. - Proceedings in the magistrate court.
(a)
Judicial hearing. On the date and at the time set for the hearing before the county court, or as soon thereafter as the case can be reached for trial, the judge of the county court shall hear and determine the question of whether or not a nuisance exists and whether or not the same has been abated as required by the notice and shall make such further order as may be required by the circumstances under the further provisions hereof.
(b)
Presentation of witnesses and evidence. The code enforcement officer or county attorney shall be responsible for presenting the case on behalf of the citizens by proper witnesses and evidence. The person charged with the nuisance shall be entitled to present witnesses and other evidence as allowed by law.
(c)
Judicial order. If after hearing the evidence the acts complained of shall be adjudged a nuisance, the judge of the magistrate court shall issue an order upon the interested parties that have answered the complaint or appeared at the hearing, stating either of the following:
(1)
If the repair, alteration, or improvement of the said dwelling, building, or structure can be made at a reasonable cost in relation to the present value of the dwelling, building, or structure, requiring the owner, within the time specified in the order, to repair, alter, or improve such dwelling, building, or structure so as to bring it into full compliance with the applicable codes relevant to the cited violation and, if applicable, to secure the structure so that it cannot be used in connection with the commission of drug crimes;
(2)
If the repair, alteration, or improvement of the said dwelling, building, or structure in order to bring it into full compliance with applicable codes relevant to the cited violations cannot be made at a reasonable cost in relation to the present value of the dwelling, building, or structure, requiring the owner, within the time specified in the order, to demolish and remove such dwelling, building, or structure and all debris from the property; or
(3)
If abatement of the adjudged nuisance does not involve the above circumstances, then abatement of the same within a reasonable time as set by the court.
(d)
Failure to comply. If the owner or parties in interest fail to comply with an order to abate the nuisance, or vacate and close or demolish the dwelling, building or structure, and do not seek an injunction under O.C.G.A. § 41-2-13, the code enforcement officer may cause such nuisance to be removed or such dwelling, building or structure to be repaired, altered or improved or to be vacated and closed or demolished; and the public officer may cause to be posted on the main entrance of any building, dwelling or structure so closed a placard with the following words:
"This building is unfit for human habitation or commercial, industrial, or business use; the use or occupation of this building for human habitation or for commercial, industrial or business use is prohibited and unlawful."
The amount of the cost of removing the nuisance or demolition in general, including all court costs, appraisal fees, and all other costs necessarily associated with the abatement action, including restoration to grade of the real property after demolition, shall be a lien against the real property upon which such cost was incurred.
Sec. 28-57. - Other procedure for abatement of nuisances.
Notwithstanding the provisions of sections 28-1, 28-2, 28-25, 28-54, and 28-55, where a nuisance complained of consists of acts expressly declared to be unlawful and to constitute nuisances under any other ordinance of the county, proceedings in the magistrate court may be by summons as for the violation of any county ordinance, and upon conviction in the magistrate court, the person convicted shall be punished according to the general penalties described in section 1-9.

ARTICLE IV. - ABANDONMENT OF MOTOR VEHICLES[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— County authorized to enact ordinances for removal of vehicles, O.C.G.A. § 36-60-4; abandoned motor vehicles, O.C.G.A. § 40-11-1 et seq.

Sec. 28-90. - Definition.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Disabled, wrecked, junked, or abandoned motor vehicle means any motor vehicle which does not have lawfully affixed thereto an unexpired license plate or plates, and the condition of which is wrecked, dismantled, partially dismantled, inoperative, or discarded.
Sec. 28-91. - Prohibited conduct.
No person shall park, store, place, or allow to remain on the public streets, alleys, or other public places in the county, or in the open upon private property, unless necessary for operation of a business enterprise lawfully situated upon such private property, any disabled, wrecked, junked, or abandoned motor vehicle or parts of motor vehicles, or other major appliances, machinery or other junked objects, and the parking, storing, placing, or allowing to remain upon such designated places of such disabled, wrecked, junked, or abandoned property is hereby declared to be a nuisance.
Sec. 28-92. - Enforcement; notification and hearing; impoundment.
(a)
Removal and impoundment of vehicles, etc. The county manager or code enforcement officer is authorized to tow away or cause to be towed away or removed any disabled, wrecked, junked, or abandoned motor vehicle or parts of motor vehicles or other major appliances, machinery, or other junked objects found on the public streets, alleys, or other public places within the county in violation of section 28-91, and to store and impound the same until the expenses of towing and storage shall be paid by the owner of such property. The expense of such towing and storage shall be paid by the owner of such property and such expenses may be assessed against such owner and collected as county taxes are assessed and collected.
(b)
Notification to remove. Upon notice of the presence in the open on any private property within the county of any disabled, wrecked, junked, or abandoned motor vehicle or parts of motor vehicles or other major appliances, machinery, or other junked objects in violation of section 28-91, except where necessary in the operation of a business enterprise lawfully situated upon such property, the county manager or code enforcement officer shall at once notify the owner of such premises in writing to relocate such property to an enclosed garage or other building or to remove same from the premises.
(c)
Failure to comply. Upon the failure of any person to relocate or remove such disabled, wrecked, junked, or abandoned property after notice as provided under state law, the code enforcement officer shall report such failure to the county manager. Upon knowledge of the failure of the person to correct the nuisance within the given period of time, the county manager may then issue a summons or direct the county attorney to issue a summons to the offending property owner requiring him to be and appear before the magistrate court to show cause why the existence of such condition on his property should not be abated as a nuisance, as provided under the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 41-2-5.
(d)
Abatement by authorities; expenses assessed against owner. After such notice and hearing thereon, the county manager may properly order the abatement of such nuisance, which order shall be directed to and executed by the county manager, constable or sheriff, and such order shall include the right to impound and tow away or otherwise remove or destroy the offending property. All of the expenses of the abatement of such nuisance may be assessed against the owner of the property upon which the nuisance was located and may be collected as county taxes are assessed and collected.
Sec. 28-93. - Sale and proceeds.
(a)
Sale of impounded property. The owner of any property impounded under the provisions of this article shall have 30 days from the date the same was impounded to redeem the same by paying the cost of removal and storage. After 30 days from the date of the impounding of any property under the terms hereof, the constable, or his designee, may sell or otherwise dispose of the same in accordance with the procedures in O.C.G.A. § 9-13-140 et seq. and after giving opportunity for competitive bidding.
(b)
Advance notice to be given. Notice by mail shall be sent at least ten days prior to such disposition to the registered owner of the vehicle, if known, and also to the owner or occupant of the private property from which the vehicle was removed.
(c)
Details of notice. The notice of the sale shall state:
(1)
The sale is of abandoned property in the possession of the county;
(2)
A description of the vehicle, including make, model, license number and any other information which will accurately identify that vehicle;
(3)
The terms of the sale;
(4)
The date, time, and place of the sale; and
(5)
Any other information required by state law.
(d)
Disposition of proceeds. The proceeds of such sale shall be applied first to pay the expenses of sale, towing, removal, and storage, and the balance, if any, paid to the owner of the property, if known. If the owner of such property is not known, then the balance of such proceeds, if any, shall be paid into the county treasury.

Sec. 28-115. - Emissions of gases, vapors, and odors.
(a)
No person shall cause, suffer, or allow any emissions of gases, vapors, or odors beyond the property line from which such emissions occur to be in sufficient quantities and of such characteristics and duration as is or is likely to be injurious to the public welfare; to the health of human, plant, or animal life; or to property; or which interfere with the enjoyment of life and property.
(b)
Detectable odors emitted from the following sources of emission are hereby declared to be objectionable, provided, however, that this listing shall not be deemed as exclusive:

Ammonia, bleaching powder, or chlorine manufacture;
(2)

Sec. 28-116. - Open burning.
(a)
Generally. Except as provided in subsection (b) of this section, no person shall kindle an open fire in any public or private place outside any building. Fires started in violation of this section shall be promptly extinguished by the person responsible for same upon notice by the fire chief or his duly designated agent. During the existence of an air pollution alert, as may be declared by the county manager, all exceptions are void and no open fires shall be kindled.
(b)
Exceptions.
(1)
Open burning may be done under permit as follows:
a.
Application for burning permits shall be on forms provided by the fire chief;
b.
No permit shall be issued unless the issuing officer is satisfied that:
1.
There is no practical available alternate method for the disposal of the material to be burned;
2.
No hazardous condition will be created by such burning;
3.
No salvage operation by open burning will be conducted; and
4.
No leaves will be burned in those areas where provision is made for public collection thereof;
c.
Any permit issued may be limited by the imposition of conditions to:
1.
Prevent the creation of excessive smoke; or
2.
Protect property and the health, safety, and comfort of persons from the effects of the burning;
d.
If it becomes apparent at any time to the fire chief that limitations need to be imposed for any of the reasons stated in subsection (b)(1)c of this section, the fire chief or his duly designated agent shall notify the permittee in writing and any limitations so imposed shall be treated as conditions under which the permit is issued.
(2)
Open burning may be done without permit as follows:
a.
In those areas where provision for public collection of leaves is not made, the opening burning of leaves is permitted;
b.
In those areas where regular refuse collection is not provided, open burning of ordinary household trash by householders is permitted, provided that:
1.
The fires are located no closer than 500 feet to any neighboring habitable dwelling or place where people work or congregate;
2.
Garbage, dead animals, and animal waste are not burned; and
3.
Materials which create dense or excessive smoke or emissions injurious or noxious to people or property are not burned.
(c)
Open fires may be set in performance of an official duty of any public officer if the fire is necessary for one or more of the following reasons or purposes:
(1)
For the prevention of a fire hazard which cannot be abated by other means;
(2)
For the instruction of public firefighters or industrial employees under supervision of the fire chief; or
(3)
For the protection of public health.
(d)
Fires may be used for the cooking of food, provided no smoke violation or other nuisance is created.
(e)
Salamanders or other devices may be used for heating by construction or other workers, provided no smoke violation or other nuisance is created.
(f)
Fires may be set in the course of agricultural operations in growing crops or raising fowl or animals, provided no nuisance is created.
(g)
Open fires may be set for recreational purposes, such as campfires, provided no smoke violation or nuisance is created.

ARTICLE VI. - NOISE REGULATION

Sec. 28-148. - Anti-noise regulation.
Subject to the provisions of this section, the creating of any unreasonably loud, disturbing and unnecessary noise is prohibited. Noise of such character, intensity, or duration as to be detrimental to the life or health of any individual, or in disturbance of the public peace and welfare is prohibited.
Sec. 28-149. - Miscellaneous prohibited noises enumerated.
The following acts, among others, are declared to be loud, disturbing and unnecessary noises in violation of this section, but this enumeration shall not be deemed to be exclusive, namely:
(1)
Blowing horns. The sounding of any horn or signal device on any automobile, motorcycle, bus, streetcar, or other vehicle while not in motion, except as a danger signal if another vehicle is approaching, apparently out of control, or if in motion, only as a danger signal after or as brakes are being applied and deceleration of the vehicle is intended; the creation by means of any such signal device of any unreasonably loud or harsh sound; and the sounding of such device for an unnecessary and unreasonable period of time.
(2)
Radios, phonographs, etc. The playing of any radio, phonograph, or any musical instrument or sound device, including but not limited to loudspeakers or other devices for reproduction or amplification of sound, either independently of or in connection with motion pictures, radio or television, in such a manner or with such volume, particularly during the hours between 11:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m., as to annoy or disturb the quiet, comfort, or repose of persons in any office or hospital, or in any dwelling, hotel, or other type of residence, or of any person in the vicinity.
(3)
Yelling, shouting, hooting, etc. Yelling, shouting, hooting, whistling, or singing on the public streets, particularly between the hours of 11:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m., or at any time or place so as to annoy or disturb the quiet, comfort, or repose of any person in any hospital, dwelling, hotel, or other type of residence, or of any person in the vicinity.
(4)
Pets. The keeping of any animal, bird, or fowl which, by causing frequent or long continued noise shall disturb the comfort or repose of any person in the vicinity.
(5)
Use of vehicle. The use of any automobile, motorcycle, streetcar, or vehicle so out of repair, so loaded, or in such a manner as to cause loud and unnecessary grating, grinding, rattling, or other noise.
(6)
Blowing whistles. The blowing of any steam whistle attached to any stationary boiler, except to give notice of the time to begin or stop work or as a warning of fire or danger, or upon request of proper county authorities.
(7)
Exhaust discharge. To discharge into the open air the exhaust of any steam engine, stationary internal combustion engine, motor vehicle, or boat engine, except through a muffler or other device which will effectively prevent loud or explosive noises therefrom.
(8)
Noises near schools, hospitals, churches, etc. The creation of any excessive noise on any street adjacent to any hospital or adjacent to any school, institution of learning, church, or court while the same is in session.
(9)
Noises to attract attention. The use of any drum, loudspeaker, or other instrument or device emitting noise for the purpose of attracting attention to any performance, show, or sale, or display or merchandise, unless a permit for the use of such device shall have been obtained under the regulations hereinafter provided.
(10)
Loudspeakers or amplifiers on vehicles. The use of mechanical loudspeakers or amplifiers on trucks or other moving or standing vehicles for advertising or other purposes, unless a permit for the use of such device shall have been obtained under the regulations hereinafter provided.
Sec. 28-150. - Exemptions.
(a)
Noise permit. The reasonable use of amplifiers, loudspeakers, drums, or other instrument or device emitting noise for the purpose of any public address or for the purpose of attracting attention to any performance, show, sale, or display of merchandise shall be permitted providing a permit therefor is secured from the county manager.
(b)
Application and terms for permit. An application for the use desired shall be submitted in writing to the county clerk, which application shall set out the nature of the noise-making activity, the purpose thereof, the place where the activity is to be carried on, and the dates and hours during which the use is to be made. The county manager shall act upon each application so submitted, and shall, before granting a permit, place such restrictions upon the use as to the extent, hours, and location as to prevent undue annoyance to the public or any resident. Any denial of a permit by the county manager may be appealed to the board of commissioners at the next regularly scheduled board meeting.
(c)
Violation of terms. It shall be unlawful to use any device or to create any undue noise in violation of the terms of any permit issued under this section.

ARTICLE VII. - TRASH, WEEDS, AND LITTER

Sec. 28-169. - Duties of property owners.
(a)
It shall be the duty of each and every owner of a lot of land whether occupied or unoccupied within the county to remove therefrom any trash, rubbish or filth that may be thereon. Allowing any lot of land to have thereon trash, rubbish or filth shall constitute the maintaining of a nuisance per se.
(b)
Every owner or person in charge of any lot of land in the county located within 200 feet of any human habitation or place of business who shall allow such lot to be in any of the conditions described under subsection (a) of this section, who shall fail or refuse to remedy the condition after notice of such shall be guilty of a violation of this chapter.
Sec. 28-170. - Prohibited storage of property.
All nonfunctioning motor vehicles, appliances, machinery or equipment stored, kept or maintained out of doors is declared to be a public health hazard. Any trash, rubbish, garbage or putrescible matter stored out of doors for a period of more than six months, whether in a container or not, is declared to be a public health hazard. The placing, keeping or maintaining of any such items on any property out of doors within the county shall be a violation of this chapter.
Sec. 28-171. - Exceptions.
This article shall not apply to any motor vehicle, kept, stored or maintained upon private property, which has a current up-to-date motor vehicle license tag thereon, nor shall it apply to any vehicles, appliances or equipment kept and maintained in connection with or as a part of any duly licensed appliance, machinery or automobile repair shop or junkyard.
Sec. 28-172. - Administration; notice of violation; appeal.
(a)
This article shall be administered by the county manager or his designee, and if and whenever it shall appear to the county manager or his designee that it is being violated, he shall issue a citation returnable to the county court charging the owner or occupant of the property with a violation of this section.
(b)
If the owner or occupant of property of which or upon which material is stored has been found in violation of this section two or more times, the county manager may notify, in writing, the owner of the property or the tenant in possession of the land on which the violation exists to abate such violation by removing the material or materials to a conforming location or into some enclosed garage or other storage house, within ten days from the date of such notice. Such notice shall be mailed to the owner or occupant of the property, if known, and placed on or adjacent to the door of any structure on the property in question.
(c)
Any person disagreeing with the decision of the county manager or his designee under subsection (b) of this section shall have ten days from the date of the notice in which to appeal the decision of the county manager or his designee to the magistrate court, requesting that the county court make a determination as to whether or not a violation actually exists, which appeal shall be heard after due notice and as soon as practical, and the decision of the county court shall be final.
(d)
Any person who fails to comply with the notice given by the county manager or his designee in compliance with this section within the ten days after the notice is given or within ten days after the final decision is made by the magistrate court (in cases where appeals are filed), shall be in violation of this section.
Sec. 28-173. - Removal of nonfunctioning vehicles, appliances, machinery or equipment.
(a)
After the time provided in section 28-172 for the removal of nonfunctioning motor vehicles, appliances, machinery or equipment has expired, the county manager or his designee may seek an order of the magistrate court to permit entry onto the property for the purpose of removing and disposing of the items stored in violation of this section. If the magistrate court judge finds that nonfunctioning motor vehicles, appliances, machinery or equipment is stored, kept or maintained out of doors in violation of this section and the requirements of this section have been fulfilled, he shall issue an order permitting entry onto the property for the purpose of removing and disposing of the items stored in violation of this section. If the magistrate court judge finds that any trash, rubbish, garbage or putrescible matter has been stored out of doors for a period of more than six months in violation of this section and the requirements of this section have been fulfilled, he shall issue an order permitting entry onto the property for the purpose of removing and disposing of the items stored in violation of this section. No order shall be necessary to remove and dispose of items stored in violation of this section when that property is located on any public street.
(b)
The county manager or his designee is also directed to remove any abandoned or nonfunctioning motor vehicles, appliances, machinery or equipment from any public street in the county, the expense of which shall likewise be charged to the owner.
Sec. 28-174. - Penalties.
Any person who violates any provision of this article may be charged in the magistrate court of the county and punished according to the general penalties described in section 1-9 in addition to or instead of any other action set forth in this article.
