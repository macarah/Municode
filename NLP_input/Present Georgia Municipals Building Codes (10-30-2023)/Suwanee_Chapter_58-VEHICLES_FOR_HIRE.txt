ARTICLE II. - TAXICABS

Sec. 58-31. - Business occupation tax license required.
Any person coming under the provisions of this article shall obtain a business occupation tax license for any activity coming under the provisions of section 50-161 et seq.
Sec. 58-32. - Operation restrictions.
No person shall be permitted to operate a taxicab in the city, who has:
(1)
In the past 24 months been convicted of a crime involving moral turpitude or dealing in intoxicating beverages;
(2)
Previously had a permit to operate a taxi in the city suspended or revoked.
(Ord. No. 780, § 1)
Sec. 58-33. - Maintenance of vehicle; required head wear of driver.
Every person operating a taxi in the city shall maintain the vehicle used in good mechanical condition, washed and clean and clearly marked as a taxicab, and the operator thereof shall wear an appropriate cap.
(Ord. No. 780, § 2)
Sec. 58-34. - Insurance.
No person shall be permitted or licensed to operate a taxicab company or business within the city unless such person shall first file with the city clerk a policy of indemnity insurance with an indemnity insurance company authorized to do business in the state, in an amount of not less than $20,000.00 for any one accident, nor less than $10,000.00 for any one injured or damaged party or claimant, for the protection of passengers and baggage carried by such taxicab, and of the public against injury proximately caused by the negligence of such taxicab operators, his servants or agents, provided, that any person who shall submit satisfactory proof to the mayor and city council that they own in their own right unencumbered real estate located in the county of a net value of not less than $50,000.00, such person shall be permitted to become a self-insurer in lieu of depositing the policy of indemnity insurance.
(Ord. No. 790, § 1)
