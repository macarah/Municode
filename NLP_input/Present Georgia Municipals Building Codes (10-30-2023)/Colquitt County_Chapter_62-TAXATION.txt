ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 62-1. - Special service district.
(a)
Creation of district and boundaries. The unincorporated and incorporated areas of the county, exclusive of the corporate limits of Moultrie, is hereby declared to be a special service district and the boundaries of said district shall be coincident with those boundaries defining all of the areas of the county, exclusive of the corporate limits of the City of Moultrie, Georgia.
(b)
Power of taxation, assessment, fees, and charges. The board of commissioners of the county, in the exercise of the authority granted in Article IX, Section II, Paragraph VI of the 1982 Constitution of the state as amended, and by virtue of the Statutes of Georgia, shall have the power to levy ad valorem taxes and such other forms of taxation as may be legal for the provision of the services to be rendered in the special service district. Said taxes shall be used to pay the cost of providing specified enumerated services in the special service district and shall not be used for service provided outside said district. The cost of the services provided shall be placed and designated in the annual county budget and voted upon by the board of commissioners as other expenditures are voted. Said taxes shall be levied by the county board of commissioners and shall be collected by the county tax commissioner in the manner provided for the collection of ad valorem taxes.
(c)
Types of service. The types of services to which this section shall apply shall include, but not necessarily be limited to, the following:
(1)
Recreation services.
(2)
Fire protection services.
(3)
Civic auditorium operating and maintenance expenses.
(Ord. No. 99-6, §§ I—III, 7-19-99)

ARTICLE II. - PROPERTY TAXES

Sec. 62-41. - Due date.
The board of commissioners, pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-5-23(B)(1), hereby adopts the date of December 10 as the property tax due date in the county.
(Res. No. 95-R-11, 12-18-95)
Sec. 62-42. - Judicial in rem tax foreclosures.
As provided by state law effective July 1, 1995, and in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-4-76(a), the county board of commissioners does hereby declare that it will follow the requirements of S.B. 338 as set forth in O.C.G.A. Article 5 of Chapter 4 of Title 48 relating to tax sales in order to undertake judicial in rem tax foreclosures for delinquent taxes.
The duly elected tax commissioner for the county shall have the authority to commence judicial in rem tax foreclosures and to file petition with the county superior court in accordance with O.C.G.A. Article 5 of Chapter 4 of Title 48 relating to tax sales.
(Ord. No. 2017-2, 10-24-17)
Secs. 62-43—62-65. - Reserved.

Sec. 62-66. - Gross premiums tax on insurance other than life insurance.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2, there is hereby levied for the year 1984, and for each year thereafter, an annual tax upon each insurer doing insurance business within the unincorporated area of the county, in an amount equal to 2½ percent of the gross direct premiums received during the calendar year on all insurance, other than life insurance, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5(2), (3), (4), and (5). Gross direct premiums shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2(a).
(Code 1981, § 6-31)

ARTICLE IV. - FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— Local depository financial institutions business license tax, O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.

Sec. 62-96. - Business license tax levied.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93, there is hereby levied for the year 1984, and for each year thereafter, an annual business license tax upon all depository financial institutions located within the county at a rate of 0.25 percent of the gross receipts of such depository financial institutions. "Gross receipts" shall mean gross receipts as defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93. "Depository financial institutions" shall mean state and national banks, state building and loan associations, and federal savings and loan associations.
(Code 1981, § 6-41)
State Law reference— Local depository financial institutions business license tax, O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.
Sec. 62-97. - Minimum business license tax.
The minimum annual amount of business license tax due from any depository financial institution pursuant to section 62-96 shall be $1,000.00.
(Code 1981, § 6-42)
Sec. 62-98. - Filing of return.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(c), each depository financial institution subject to this tax shall file a return of its gross receipts with the board of commissioners on March 1 of the year following the year in which such gross receipts are measured. Such return shall be in the manner and in the form prescribed by the commissioner of the department of revenue based on the allocation method set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(d). The board of commissioners shall assess and collect the tax levied pursuant to this article based upon the information provided in such return.
(Code 1981, § 6-43)
Sec. 62-99. - Due date of taxes.
Taxes levied pursuant to this article shall be due no later than 30 days after filing of the return prescribed by section 62-98 unless extended by the board of commissioners.
(Code 1981, § 6-44)
