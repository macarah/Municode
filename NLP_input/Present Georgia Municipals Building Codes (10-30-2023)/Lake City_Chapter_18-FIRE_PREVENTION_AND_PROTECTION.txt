Chapter 18 - FIRE PREVENTION AND PROTECTION[1]

ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 18-1. - Contracts for fire protection.
The mayor and council shall be authorized to enter into contracts on a year-to-year basis with the county or any adjoining municipality which has adequate equipment and personnel to provide the city with fire protection. When such a contract is in existence, the term "chief of the fire department" shall mean the chief of the fire department protecting the city and the term "fire department" shall mean the protecting department.
(Code 1972, § 11-4; Code 1994, § 42-1; Ord. of 10-14-1991, § 3)
Sec. 18-2. - Reciprocal agreements for fire protection.
The mayor and council shall be authorized to enter into reciprocal agreements for fire protection with the United States, the state, the county and municipalities of the state when such agreements are in the best interests of the city and will serve to protect the lives and properties of its citizens.
(Code 1972, § 11-5; Code 1994, § 42-2)
Sec. 18-3. - Unlawful acts in connection with fires.
It shall be unlawful for any person to:
(1)
Set fire to any motor vehicle without first obtaining the permission of the chief of the fire department.
(2)
Set fire to any structure or building without first obtaining the permission of the chief of the fire department.
(3)
Set any fire on or which spreads to the property of another without the owner's permission.
(4)
Leave any fire unattended.
(5)
Set any fire that requires the response of the fire department.
(6)
Burn any substance or material that emits noxious or poisonous fumes or gases.
(7)
Set any fire on or which spreads to public property.
(8)
Obstruct in any manner any fire hydrant in the city.
(9)
Refuse to obey the orders and directions of fire department personnel in the vicinity of a fire.
(10)
Obstruct a fire hose or drive a motor vehicle across or over such hose.
(11)
Obstruct fire department vehicles or apparatus.
(12)
Interfere with or hinder fire department personnel from performing their duties.
(13)
Give or turn in a false alarm.
(14)
Turn on a fire hydrant unless authorized to do so.
(15)
Damage any fire hydrant, fire alarm box or device or fire extinguisher or equipment designed for use in fighting fires.
(Code 1972, § 11-8; Code 1994, § 42-3)

ARTICLE II. - FIRE CODES

Sec. 18-25. - Adopted.
(a)
The following codes are adopted by the city for the purpose of regulating conditions that may increase the risk of damage to life and property from fire or explosion:
(1)
Chapter 42 of the Code of Clayton County, Georgia.
(b)
The code adopted in subsection (a) of this section is adopted in its entirety and incorporated by reference in this section as fully as though set out at length in this section. In any conflict a provision of the code adopted in subsection (a) and a provision of this Code, that provision which is the most stringent shall prevail and be controlling.
(c)
Whenever the term "board of commissioners" is used in any provision of the code adopted by this section, it shall mean the mayor and city council. Whenever the term "Clayton County" is used in any provision of the code adopted by this section, it shall mean the City. Whenever the term "County Code of Ordinances" or "County Code" is used in any provision of the code adopted by this section, it shall mean the Code of Ordinances, City of Lake City, Georgia.
(d)
Current copies of each code adopted by this section shall be maintained on file in the office of the city clerk where they shall be available for public inspection.
(Code 1972, § 11-1; Code 1994, § 42-26; Ord. of 10-14-1991, § 1; Ord. No. 99-03, § 1(42-26), 12-13-1999; Ord. No. 2016-1, § 1, 2-8-2016)
