ARTICLE V. - CITY CLERK
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-150. - Election; term.
The city clerk shall be appointed annually by the mayor and council, as provided by the Charter.
(Code 1969, § 2-251; Code 1981, § 2-70; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-151. - Bond.
Before entering upon the duties of office, the city clerk shall execute a bond in an amount not less than $100,000.00, conditioned upon the faithful performance of such officer's duties.
(Code 1969, § 2-252; Code 1981, § 2-71; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-152. - Sealing and attesting documents.
The city clerk shall seal and attest all contracts of the city and all licenses, permits and such other documents as shall require this formality.
(Code 1969, § 2-253; Code 1981, § 2-72; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-153. - Keeping accounts.
The clerk shall keep accounts showing all money received by such officer and the source and disposition thereof; and such other accounts as may be required by the Charter, statute or ordinance.
(Code 1969, § 2-255; Code 1981, § 2-74; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-154. - Records.
(a)
In addition to the record of the proceedings of the council, the record of ordinances and other records which the clerk is required by law to keep, such officer shall keep:
(1)
A register of all licenses and permits issued and the payments thereon;
(2)
A record showing all of the officers and regular employees of the city; and
(3)
Such other records as may be required by the city council.
(b)
The city clerk is responsible for records management activities, acting for and in behalf of the city in directing and coordinating all records management matters, and records shall be maintained in accordance with approved retention schedules as adopted by the state records committee on March 7, 1983, and as on file with the secretary of state's office.
(Code 1969, § 2-256; Code 1981, § 2-75; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1; Res. No. 1983-8, 11-7-1983)
Sec. 2-155. - Seal.
The clerk shall be the custodian of the city seal, and shall affix its impression on documents whenever this is required.
(Code 1969, § 2-257; Code 1981, § 2-76; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-156. - Custodian of documents.
The clerk shall be the custodian of all documents belonging to the city which are not assigned to the custody of some other officer.
(Code 1969, § 2-258; Code 1981, § 2-77; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-157. - Indexing documents and records.
The clerk shall keep and maintain a proper index to all documents and records kept by such officer, so that ready access thereto and use thereof may be had.
(Code 1969, § 2-259; Code 1981, § 2-78; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-158. - Additional duties.
In addition to the duties herein provided the clerk shall perform such other duties and functions as may be required by the Charter, statute or ordinance.
(Code 1969, § 2-260; Code 1981, § 2-79; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Secs. 2-159—2-184. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - DEPUTY CLERK

Sec. 2-185. - Office created; execution of documents.
There is hereby created the office of deputy clerk. The city clerk is authorized to appoint a deputy clerk, subject to confirmation by the mayor and city council, who shall have the power and duty to execute all documents required by law or ordinance to be executed by the clerk, and affix the seal of the city thereto whenever required.
(Code 1969, § 2-262; Code 1981, § 2-80; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-186. - Signing documents.
When signing any documents the deputy clerk shall sign the name of the city clerk followed by the term "by" and the deputy clerk's own name and the term "deputy clerk."
(Code 1969, § 2-263; Code 1981, § 2-81; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Sec. 2-187. - Time of authority.
(a)
The powers and duties herein described shall be exercised by such deputy clerk only in the absence of the city clerk from such city clerk's office in the city hall, and only when either written direction has been given by the city clerk to exercise such power, or the city council has determined by resolution that the city clerk is temporarily or permanently incapacitated to perform such function.
(b)
Such deputy clerk shall have the authority and power herein described, and such further power and authority as may be provided by law.
(Code 1969, § 2-264; Code 1981, § 2-82; Ord. of 5-16-1977, § 1)
Secs. 2-188—2-212. - Reserved.
