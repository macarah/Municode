ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 14-1. - Definitions adopted.
Unless otherwise indicated, words and phrases used in this chapter, which are defined in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 shall have the meanings ascribed to them in such section.
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
State Law reference— Municipal authority to adopt O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 by reference, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-372; future amendments or repeal of adopted provisions of state law effective in city without action of board of city commissioners, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-373.
Sec. 14-2. - Enforcement of chapter.
It shall be the duty of the officers of the police department or such officers as are assigned by the marshal to enforce this chapter and all other traffic laws of the city and all of the state vehicle laws applicable to street traffic in the city.
(Code 1953, § 22.2)
Sec. 14-3. - Emergency and experimental regulations.
The marshal, by and with the approval of the city manager, is hereby empowered to make regulations necessary to make effective the provisions of this chapter and other traffic ordinances of the city and to make and enforce temporary or experimental regulations to cover emergencies or special conditions. No such temporary or experimental regulation shall remain in effect for more than 90 days.
(Code 1953, § 22.3)
Sec. 14-4. - Direction of traffic by police and firefighters.
Officers of the police department, or such officers as are assigned by the marshal, are hereby authorized to direct all traffic by voice, hand or signal in conformity with traffic laws; provided, that in the event of a fire or other emergency or to expedite traffic or to safeguard pedestrians, officers of the police department may direct traffic as conditions may require notwithstanding the provisions of the traffic laws. Officers of the fire department, when at the scene of a fire, may direct or assist the police in directing traffic thereat or in the immediate vicinity.
(Code 1953, § 22.4)
Sec. 14-5. - Obedience to police.
No person shall willfully fail or refuse to comply with any lawful order, signal or direction of a police officer.
(Code 1953, § 22.5)
Sec. 14-6. - Persons propelling pushcarts, riding bicycles or animals or driving animals subject to chapter.
Every person propelling any pushcart or riding a bicycle or an animal upon a roadway, and every person driving any animal shall be subject to the provisions of this chapter applicable to the driver of any vehicle, except those provisions of this chapter which by their very nature can have no application.
(Code 1953, § 22.6)
Sec. 14-7. - Public employees to obey traffic regulations.
The provisions of this chapter shall apply to the driver of any vehicle owned by or used in the service of the United States government, or any state, county or city, and it shall be unlawful for any such driver to violate any of the provisions of this chapter, except as otherwise permitted in this chapter or by state statute.
(Code 1953, § 22.7)
Sec. 14-8. - Authorized emergency vehicles.
(a)
The provisions of this chapter regulating the movement, parking and standing of vehicles shall not apply to any authorized emergency vehicle while the operator of such vehicle is operating the same in an emergency in the necessary performance of public duties.
(b)
When responding to an emergency call, the operator of any authorized emergency vehicle, upon approaching a red or stop signal or any stop sign, shall slow down as necessary for safety, but he may proceed cautiously past such red or stop sign or signal. At all other times operators of authorized emergency vehicles shall stop in obedience to a stop sign or signal.
(c)
No operator of any authorized emergency vehicle shall assume any special privilege under this section except when such vehicle is operated in response to an emergency call, and this exemption shall not protect the operator of any such vehicle from the consequences of a reckless disregard of the safety of others.
(Code 1953, § 22.8)
Cross reference— Ambulances, § 13-71 et seq.

Sec. 14-10. - Use of skateboards, skates, coasters, and similar devices restricted.
This section shall apply to use of roller skates, skateboards, inline skates, roller blades, bicycles, and any other human-powered vehicle, whether intended for recreation, transportation or otherwise (referred to in this section as "non-motorized devices") and to any go-cart, motorcycle, moped, scooter, mini-bike, golf cart, or other vehicle powered by any motor or engine ("motorized device"). It shall not apply to use of shopping carts, hand trucks and similar devices when intended for (and actually used for) the purpose of moving groceries, furniture, appliances, containers and similar items over a short distance (not exceeding two city blocks). It shall not apply to use of a motorized wheelchair or similar device by any disabled or handicapped person. It shall not apply to any baby stroller or other similar device designed for (and actually used for) transporting infants five years of age or younger if propelled by and under the control of a parent or legal guardian or other person authorized by a parent or legal guardian to do so.
(a)
No person shall use or operate any non-motorized device on or upon any sidewalk in any general commercial core (GC Core) zoning district, or at any place where signage is posted to prohibit such use or operation.
(b)
No person shall use or operate any motorized device on or upon any sidewalk.
(c)
No person shall use or operate any motorized or non-motorized device upon any publicly-owned stairway, handrail, handicapped access ramp, fence, bench, monument or any concrete or brick coping, retaining wall or similar architectural feature.
(d)
No person shall use or operate a motorized or non-motorized device in a reckless, disruptive or unsafe manner so as to impede, obstruct or endanger any person making use of a sidewalk or other pubic property, or so as to damage any public property or landscaping.
(e)
No person shall, while riding or using any motorized or non-motorized device covered under this section, grab hold of, hang onto, or cling to any other such device or to any motor vehicle, or to any rope, line or handle attached in any way to such other device or vehicle so long as either device or vehicle is in motion.
(f)
Use of any non-motorized device on any sidewalk where such use is permitted under this section shall be subject to the requirement that the operator of the non-motorized device shall yield and give way to pedestrians using the sidewalk.
(g)
In the event a person seventeen years of age or younger commits an act in violation of this section, any officer of the Brunswick Police Department may take possession of and secure the device used in the violation until such time as the minor's parent or legal guardian shall appear at the Brunswick Police Department headquarters to obtain possession of the device. In the event of a second or subsequent violation of this section by a person under seventeen, the device shall be retained by the police until the violation is resolved by court appearance or otherwise.
(Code 1953, § 22.10; Ord. No. 977, § 1, 3-1-2006)
Sec. 14-11. - Riding on portion of vehicles not intended for passengers.
No person shall ride on any vehicle upon any portion thereof not designed or intended for the use of passengers. This provision shall not apply to an employee engaged in the necessary discharge of a duty, or to persons riding within truck bodies in space intended for merchandise.
(Code 1953, § 22.13)
Sec. 14-12. - Discarded, dismantled, wrecked, scrapped, ruined, inoperable or junked motor vehicles.
(a)
The city manager shall be authorized in his discretion to cause the removal and disposal of any discarded, dismantled, wrecked, scrapped, ruined, inoperable or junked motor vehicles or parts thereof without further authorization when such motor vehicles are located on any property owned or leased by the city and when such motor vehicles or parts thereof are in such condition that they constitute a health hazard or unsightly nuisance.
(1)
"Discarded, dismantled, wrecked, scrapped, ruined, inoperable or junked motor vehicle or parts thereof" means any motor vehicle and/or parts to or from any motor vehicle which are no longer operative as a motorized vehicle and/or has no current license tag, no current state certificate of registration, or no current city certificate of restoration permit; is missing some critical part such as, but not limited to, wheels, engine, transmission, chassis, windshields or headlights; has flat tires; overgrown with vegetation; unknown ownership.
(2)
For the purposes of this section "inoperable" shall mean any motor vehicle from which the engine, wheels or other parts have been removed, or on which the engine, wheels or other parts have been altered, damaged or otherwise so treated that the vehicle is incapable of being driven under its own motor power. "Inoperable motor vehicle" shall not include: (i) a motor vehicle which has been rendered temporarily incapable of being driven under its own motor power in order to perform ordinary service or repair operations not in violation of other provisions of this section; (ii) any motor vehicle that is kept within a building when not in use; (iii) to operate historic vehicles over 25 years of age and have a valid certificate of restoration permit under subsection (h); (iv) to a motor vehicle on the premises of a place of business engaged in the wrecking or junking of motor vehicles; (v) any motor vehicle that is exempted from status as inoperable by this section.
(b)
A motor vehicle may be deemed to be discarded, dismantled, wrecked, scrapped, ruined, inoperable or junked within the meaning of subsection (a) of this section whenever the city has sent notice to the owner of record at his last-known address via certified or registered mail or via hand delivery indicating that the city is requiring the owner to remove such motor vehicle and the owner fails to remove it within a period of 15 business days, whether or not such notice was actually received by the owner.
(c)
In order to carry out and effectuate the purposes of subsection (a) of this section, the city manager shall be authorized to sell such motor vehicles or parts thereof and to enter into such contracts as he may deem necessary with private individuals and firms for the removal and disposal of the vehicles or parts.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person owning or having proper custody of any discarded, dismantled, wrecked, scrapped, ruined, inoperable or junked motor vehicle to leave or store such vehicle upon any street, other city property, or any property that is not owned, leased, or otherwise legally occupied by said person owning or having proper custody of said motor vehicle.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for any person owning or having custody of any discarded, dismantled, wrecked, scrapped, ruined, inoperable or junked motor vehicle or parts thereof to store or permit any such vehicle or parts to remain on any private property including, but not limited to, private property owned, leased, or otherwise legally occupied by said person within the city for a period of more than 15 business days after the receipt of a notice requiring removal of such vehicles or parts. This paragraph shall apply to automobile service stations and accessory sales stores but shall not apply to motor vehicles or parts stored in an enclosed building or to auto repair or body shops or licensed motor vehicle salvage dealers.
(f)
It shall be unlawful for any person, after notification to remove any discarded, dismantled, wrecked, scrapped, ruined, inoperable or junked motor vehicle or parts thereof has been given, to move such vehicle or parts to any other private property including, but not limited to, private properties other than the private property for which notification was issued owned, leased, or otherwise legally occupied by said person within the city upon which storage is not permitted.
(g)
The open storage of discarded, dismantled, wrecked, scrapped, ruined, inoperable, or junked motor vehicles or parts thereof on private property as prohibited in this section is hereby declared to be a public nuisance which may be abated pursuant to chapter 12, article IV of this Code.
(1)
"Open storage" shall be defined as the storage of any discarded, dismantled, wrecked, scrapped, ruined, inoperable or junked motor vehicle or parts thereof not within an enclosed building or garage, or other similar enclosure, or not covered by a tarpaulin (tarp) or other similar large cloth like/plastic material which would prevent the aforementioned motor vehicle and parts thereof from being viewed and seen from the general public.
(h)
Certificate of restoration permit.
(1)
Any person owning or having proper custody of any discarded, dismantled, wrecked, scrapped, ruined, inoperable or junked motor vehicles or parts thereof may apply with the city manager or his designee for a certificate of restoration permit for a fee of $40.00. Any person who has received notice requiring removal of said vehicle or parts may file for a certificate of restoration permit within a 15 business day period of receiving notice if they are otherwise qualified, but there is no guarantee that a permit will be issued to said person.
(2)
In order to qualify for said restoration permit said person must agree in writing to keep the restoration limited to one motor vehicle per property, to not openly store the motor vehicle during the restoration process, to provide a vehicle identification number (VIN) for the vehicle to the city manager or his designee, and to allow the city manager or his designee to enter the property to inspect the restoration process with proper notice.
(3)
The certificate of restoration permit shall be valid for a period of six months from the date of issue and may be renewed one time for a period of three months for an additional fee of $25.00. After the allowed time period has expired the motor vehicle and/or parts will be deemed junked within the meaning of the ordinance if the restoration is incomplete or the motor vehicle or parts are not stored in an enclosed building, at a licensed auto repair or body shop, or at a licensed motor vehicle salvage dealer. There is no guarantee that the three-month renewal period will be granted and the city manager or his designee will have the ability to inspect the work progress during any application period.
(4)
If the person owning or having proper custody of the motor vehicle fails to abide by the certificate of restoration permit the city manager or his designee reserves the right to revoke the restoration permit with no return of the permit fee.
(Ord. No. 910, § 1, 2-3-1993; Ord. No. 997, § 1, 1-2-2008)

Sec. 14-33. - Driving in excess of altered speed limit.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a motor vehicle on any portion of street or highway specified in section 14-31 of this Code at a speed in excess of the maximum vehicle speed limit established in said section for such portion of street or highway, when appropriate signs giving notice of the speed limit are erected upon the street or highway. Speed-limit signs on streets and highways on the state system shall be erected by the state department of transportation, and speed-limit signs on off-system streets and highways shall be erected by the city.

This limitation shall not prohibit operation of any police, fire, emergency medical or other public safety vehicle on any street or way within the city.
(3)
This section shall not prohibit operation of any vehicle owned or operated by any federal, state, local or other governmental entity.

This section shall not prohibit operation of any truck operated by any private person or entity which is using the vehicle while in the course of performing any work or service under contract with any governmental entity.

This section shall not prohibit operation of any vehicle in a parade for which a written permit has been issued by the city manager.

Sec. 14-35. - Mobile telephone as a contributing factor to a collision.
If a driver improperly uses a mobile telephone while he or she operates a moving motor vehicle and it is proven that the mobile telephone's improper usage distracted the driver from safe operation of such a vehicle and was a contributing factor in any collision resulting in death, physical injury, and/or property damage; then the court is authorized to impose an additional fine not exceeding $500.00 in addition to any other penalty or punishment imposed by the court for the violation of the underlying traffic offense. The total fine amount shall not exceed the maximum fine amount allowed by section 15-8 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 986, § 1, 3-21-2007)

Sec. 14-61. - Manner of parking generally.
In parking any vehicle at the curb the right hand front wheel must be nearest the curb, and at an angle of 45 degrees with the curb, in areas marked for this purpose. Areas where parking is parallel, the right hand side of the vehicle shall be not more than one foot from the curb and parallel thereto. Parking on one-way streets shall be allowed with the flow of traffic in spaces as provided for parking.
(Code 1953, § 22.77; Ord. No. 715, § 1, 6-15-1977)
Sec. 14-62. - Parking not to obstruct traffic.
No person shall park any vehicle upon a street, other than an alley, in such manner or under such conditions as to leave available less than ten feet of the width of the roadway for free movement of vehicular traffic.
(Code 1953, § 22.78)
Sec. 14-63. - Permit for loading or unloading at an angle to the curb.
The city manager and marshal are authorized to issue special permits to permit the backing of a vehicle to the curb for the purpose of loading or unloading merchandise or materials subject to the terms and conditions of such permit. Such permits may be issued either to the owner or lessee of real property or to the owner of the vehicle and shall grant to such person the privilege as therein stated and authorized herein, and it shall be unlawful for any permittee or other person to violate any of the special terms or conditions of any such permit.
(Code 1953, § 22.79)
Sec. 14-64. - Motor vehicles left unattended; brakes to be set and engine stopped.
No person having control or charge of a motor vehicle shall allow such vehicle to stand on any street unattended without first setting the brakes thereon and stopping the motor of such vehicle and, when standing upon a perceptible grade, without turning the wheels of such vehicle to the curb or the side of the street or highway.
(Code 1953, § 22.81)
Sec. 14-65. - Stopping, standing or parking near hazardous or congested places.
(a)
The city manager and marshal are hereby authorized to determine and designate by proper signs places not exceeding 100 feet in length in which the stopping, standing or parking of vehicles would create an especially hazardous condition or would cause unusual delay to traffic.
(b)
When official signs are erected at hazardous or congested places, as authorized herein, no person shall stop, stand or park a vehicle in any such designated place.
(Code 1953, § 22.83)
Sec. 14-66. - Multi-wheeled vehicles, buses, and trailers on-street parking prohibited.
(a)
With the exception of commercial vehicles parking temporarily in conjunction with a service, sale, delivery, or pick-up, any motor vehicle, or combination of motor vehicle and trailer, which either weighs more than 6,000 pounds, or has more than six wheels, or has a height greater than nine feet measured from the road surface is prohibited from parking on residential streets or within the public right-of-way of a residentially zoned district.
(b)
No truck camper, camper trailer, nonmotorized trailer, motor home, boat, boat trailer, or other recreational vehicle shall be parked on any city street or public place that is not attached to a motorized vehicle.
(c)
Such vehicles are declared a nuisance and a hazard to traffic and law enforcement officials of the city are authorized and empowered to impound any vehicle found to be in violation of this section. The owner of such vehicle shall be responsible for all expenses and charges incurred in the towing and storage of the vehicle. At the discretion of the law enforcement officer, a vehicle parked in violation of this section may be released to the owner or driver if the vehicle is then immediately removed.
(d)
The vehicle owner, or driver if different from the owner, may be punished as provided in section 1-6 of the City of Brunswick Code for a violation of this section.
(Ord. No. 1061, § 2, 9-18-2019)
Sec. 14-67. - Parking prohibited on narrow streets.
(a)
The city manager and marshal are hereby authorized to erect signs indicating no parking upon any street when the width of the roadway does not exceed 20 feet, or upon one side of a street as indicated by such signs when the width of the roadway does not exceed 30 feet.
(b)
When official signs prohibiting parking are erected upon narrow streets, as authorized herein, no person shall park a vehicle upon any such street in violation of any such sign.
(Code 1953, § 22.84; Ord. No. 1061, § 3, 9-18-2019)
Sec. 14-68. - Standing or parking on one-way streets.
(a)
The city manager and marshal are authorized to erect signs upon either side of any one-way street to prohibit the standing or parking of vehicles, and when such signs are in place, no person shall stand or park a vehicle upon such street in violation of any such signs.
(b)
Unless prohibitive signs are erected on one-way streets, vehicles may also park on the left-hand side headed in direction of traffic, with the left-hand wheels of the vehicle within six inches of the curb or edge of the roadway.
(Code 1953, § 22.85; Ord. No. 1061, § 3, 9-18-2019)
Sec. 14-69. - Parking adjacent to schools.
(a)
The city manager and marshal are hereby authorized to erect signs indicating no parking upon that side of any street adjacent to any school property when such parking would, in their opinion, interfere with traffic or create a hazardous situation.
(b)
When official signs are erected indicating no parking upon that side of a street adjacent to any school property, no person shall park a vehicle in any such designated place.
(Code 1953, § 22.86; Ord. No. 1061, § 3, 9-18-2019)
Sec. 14-70. - Stopping and parking prohibited on certain streets.
When not inconsistent with this chapter or any ordinance, the city manger and marshal are authorized to prohibit parking or stopping of vehicles on any street or portion of a street when traffic and engineering surveys indicate that such stopping or parking may impede the free flow of traffic on such street.
(Code 1953, § 22.87; Ord. No. 1061, § 3, 9-18-2019)
Sec. 14-71. - Parking prohibited during certain hours.
When prohibited by ordinance or by order of the city manager and marshal, no person shall park a vehicle during the hours prohibited at places so indicated by official sign.
(Code 1953, § 22.88; Ord. No. 1061, § 3, 9-18-2019)
Sec. 14-72. - Parking time limited on certain streets.
The city manager and the marshal are hereby authorized to designate the time limit for parking on any street or portion of a street. They shall cause to be placed the time limit signs in these areas. It shall be unlawful for any person to park a vehicle of any description for a period longer than the time designated by the city manager and the marshal.
(Code 1953, § 22.89; Ord. No. 1061, § 3, 9-18-2019)
Sec. 14-73. - Signs indicating prohibited or limited parking to be erected.
When, by any ordinance or order of the city manager and marshal, any parking time limit is imposed or parking is prohibited on designated streets, it shall be the duty of the city manager and marshal to erect appropriate signs giving notice thereof, and no such regulations shall be effective unless such signs are erected and in place at the time of any alleged offense. At least one sign shall be erected in each block on each side of the street where parking is either prohibited or restricted.
(Code 1953, § 22.91; Ord. No. 1061, § 3, 9-18-2019)
Sec. 14-74. - Parking in squares, parks, etc.
It shall be unlawful for any person to park any vehicle, team or animal in or on any of the squares, public parks or places in the city except upon the driveways.
(Code 1953, § 22.92; Ord. No. 1061, § 3, 9-18-2019)
Sec. 14-75. - Parking for certain purposes prohibited.
No person shall park a vehicle upon any roadway for:
(1)
Displaying such vehicle for sale.
(2)
Washing, greasing, storing or repairing such vehicle except repairs necessitated by a sudden emergency, and in such emergency such vehicle shall be moved or towed away with all due haste.

Sec. 14-76. - Stopping within intersection or on crosswalk.
It shall be unlawful for any vehicle to stop within an intersection or on a crosswalk for the purpose of receiving or discharging passengers.
(Code 1953, § 22.94; Ord. No. 1061, § 3, 9-18-2019)
Sec. 14-77. - Continuous parking in same location.
Any vehicle that shall remain parked continuously in the same location on any street in the city for the period of 72 hours or more shall be presumed to be abandoned and may be impounded by the police department.
(Code 1953, § 22.95; Ord. No. 1061, § 3, 9-18-2019)
Sec. 14-78. - Loading zones—Designation; erection of signs.
The city manager and marshal are hereby authorized to determine the location of loading zones and shall place and maintain appropriate signs indicating the same and stating the hours during which the provisions of section 14-78 are applicable.
(Code 1953, § 22.96; Ord. No. 1061, § 3, 9-18-2019)
Sec. 14-79. - Same—Use of zones by trucks; other vehicles prohibited.
No person shall stop, stand or park a truck for any purpose or length of time other than for the expeditious unloading and delivery or pick-up and loading of materials in any place marked as a loading zone during hours when provisions applicable to such zones are in effect; nor shall any person stop, stand or park any other vehicle for such unloading of materials.
(Code 1953, § 22.97; Ord. No. 1061, § 3, 9-18-2019)
Sec. 14-80. - Taxicab stands—Designation; erection of signs.
The city manager and marshal are hereby authorized to create and abolish the creation of taxicab stands on such public streets, in such places and in such number as they shall determine to be of the greatest benefit and convenience to the public. Every such taxicab stand shall be designated by appropriate signs.
(Code 1953, § 22.98; Ord. No. 1061, § 3, 9-18-2019)
Cross reference— Taxicabs, ch. 21.
Sec. 14-81. - Same—Other vehicles prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to stop, stand or park a vehicle other than a taxicab in a taxicab stand.
(Code 1953, § 22.99; Ord. No. 1061, § 3, 9-18-2019)
Sec. 14-82. - Bus stops—Designation; erection of signs.
The city manager and marshal are hereby authorized to create and abolish the location of bus stops on such public streets, in such places and in such number as they shall determine to be of the greatest benefit and convenience to the public. Every such bus stop shall be designated by appropriate signs.
(Code 1953, § 22.100; Ord. No. 1061, § 3, 9-18-2019)
Sec. 14-83. - Same—Use by busses; other vehicles prohibited.
The driver of a bus shall not park upon any street in any business district at any place other than at a bus stop, except for making emergency repairs. No person shall stop, stand or park a vehicle other than a bus in a bus stop.
(Code 1953, § 22.101; Ord. No. 1061, § 3, 9-18-2019)
