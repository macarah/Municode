ARTICLE II. - CEMETERY REGULATIONS

Sec. 17-19. - Penalty for violation.
The violation of sections 17-20 through 17-22 or any part thereof shall constitute a criminal offense, for which the offender shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $100.00, or confinement in the city jail not to exceed 30 days. Such offender shall be tried in the municipal court of the city.
(Code 1966, § 6-7; Ord. of 8-14-1967)
Sec. 17-20. - Permit to open grave; issuance; fee.
Any and all undertakers in charge of any interment in the Fairburn City Cemetery are hereby required to obtain a permit to open any grave in said cemetery, and to pay a permit fee in the amount established by the city council before said grave shall be opened. The permit required shall be in writing and issued by the city clerk.
(Code 1966, § 6-4; Ord. of 8-14-1967, § 1)
Sec. 17-21. - Filling and leveling of graves.
The undertaker or anyone else opening a grave in the city cemetery is hereby made responsible for the filling and leveling of the grave, and for the leveling of the area surrounding the same; including the opening of the grave for the purpose of removing a body therefrom, all of which shall be accomplished within 30 days after the interment or the removal of a body.
(Code 1966, § 6-5; Ord. of 8-14-1967, § 2)
Sec. 17-22. - Permission required to place, etc., monuments, grave markers, coping or wall.
No monuments, markers of graves, coping or walls of any kind or character shall be placed or erected on or around any lot in the city cemetery without first obtaining from the city council written permission for such monument, grave markers, coping or wall.
(Code 1966, § 6-6; Ord. of 8-14-1967, § 3)
Sec. 17-23. - Disposition of fines, fees and forfeitures.
All fines, fees and forfeitures collected by the city under section 17-19 or for the violation of this article are hereby appropriated to the exclusive use in the annual upkeep of the city cemetery, whether said upkeep is performed by the city itself or any of its authorized agents.
(Code 1966, § 6-8; Ord. of 8-14-1967, § 4)
