ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 42-1. - Penalty for violation of chapter.
Any person found in violation of any of the provisions of this chapter shall be subject to a fine not to exceed $50.00 per day. A separate offense shall be deemed to be committed each day during or upon which a violation occurs or is permitted to continue.
(Ord. of 10-6-1997, § 12-106)
Sec. 42-2. - Maintenance and repair of public streets.
All maintenance and repair of public streets, alleys, curbs, sidewalks, and other public ways shall be under the supervision of the city manager, which officer shall have the responsibility of enforcing all provisions of this chapter and all ordinances of the city relating to such public ways.
(Code 1982, § 12-101(2))
Sec. 42-3. - Maintenance of sidewalks.
It shall be the duty of all owners of property in the city upon which sidewalks have been laid to keep such walkways in good repair at all times.
(Code 1982, § 12-101(2))
Sec. 42-4. - Maintenance of property between sidewalks and streets.
All persons who own real estate in the city which abuts upon any sidewalk which is paved shall be required to keep that portion of such sidewalk which lies between the property line and the curbline of the adjoining street, and upon which his real estate abuts, in good and smooth condition and free from growing weeds and other obnoxious and unsightly vegetation or other things which would mar or detract from the beauty and cleanliness of the street upon which their property abuts. Any property owner who shall place or allow grass to grow upon the portion of the sidewalk lying between the property line and the curbline of the street as aforesaid, and upon which his real estate abuts, shall keep such grass properly mowed and free from rubbish of all kinds. If such owners are not in the possession of their property, then this section shall apply to their tenants or those who have possession or control of the same.
(Code 1982, § 12-101(3))
Sec. 42-5. - Defacing sidewalks, streets, and curbs.
It shall be unlawful for any person to deface any public sidewalk, street or curb in the city by painting any signs thereon, whether for commercial advertising purposes or not, or to walk or drive any vehicle upon or injure in any way any newly laid street, sidewalk or curbing pavement while the same is guarded by a warning sign or barricade or is soft or newly laid.
(Code 1982, § 12-101(4))
Sec. 42-6. - Obstructions.
It shall be unlawful for any person to cause, create or maintain any obstruction on any street, street right-of-way, alley, sidewalk, or other public way, except as may be provided in this chapter or in the ordinance and laws of the city.
(Code 1982, § 12-101(5); Ord. No. 08-23, 10-2-2007)
Sec. 42-7. - Deposits and discharges onto streets and sidewalks.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to deposit on any street or sidewalk any material which may be harmful to the pavement thereof, or any waste material, or any glass or other article which may do injury to any person, animal or property.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to discharge or allow to be discharged onto any public street or sidewalk any water or other fluid material containing objectionable material such as sewage, waste milk or other organic material.
(Code 1982, § 12-101(6))
Sec. 42-8. - Report of defects, obstructions, deposits and discharges.
It shall be the duty of every city officer and employee who becomes aware of any defect or obstruction, or of any unlawful deposit or discharge, in or on any public way of the city to report the same to the city manager as soon as possible.
(Code 1982, § 12-101(7))
Sec. 42-9. - Private use.
It shall be unlawful for any person, firm, or corporation to use any street, sidewalk, or other public place as space for the display of goods or merchandise for sale; or to write or make any sign or advertisement on any such pavement if, in the opinion of the mayor and council, it would be dangerous to the public.
(Code 1982, § 12-101(8))
Sec. 42-10. - Encroachments.
It shall be unlawful for any person to erect or maintain any building or structure which encroaches upon any public street or property, or to erect any poles or wires or maintain any poles or wires over any public place, street, alley or other public way, without having first secured a permit from the clerk in the manner specified in this chapter.
(Code 1982, § 12-101(9))
Sec. 42-11. - Openings.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to construct or maintain any opening or stairway in any public street, sidewalk or alley without first obtaining a permit from the clerk in the manner specified in this chapter.
(b)
All lawfully maintained openings shall be guarded by a suitable strong cover or railing approved by the city manager.
(Code 1982, § 12-101(10))
Sec. 42-12. - Trees and shrubs.
(a)
Planting and removal. It shall be unlawful for any person to plant, remove, injure or cut any tree, bush or shrub in or from any public street, parkway or other public place without first obtaining a permit therefor from the clerk in the manner specified in this chapter.
(b)
Advertisements. It shall be unlawful for any person to attach any sign, advertisement or notice to any tree or shrub in any public place.
(c)
Dangerous trees. Any tree or shrub which overhangs any sidewalk, street or other public place in the city in such a way as to impede or interfere with traffic or travel shall be trimmed by the owner of the premises on which such tree or shrub grows so that the obstruction shall cease. Any tree or limb of a tree which is likely to fall on or across any public way or place shall be removed by the owner of the premises on which such tree grows or stands. The city manager may cause to be trimmed or removed any and all such trees or shrubs so that the obstruction or danger to traffic or passage shall be removed.
(d)
Poles and wires. Any person or company which maintains poles and wires in the streets, alleys or other public places of the city shall keep such wires and poles free and away from any nearby trees or shrubs in such places, and keep all such trees and shrubs properly trimmed, subject to the supervision of the city manager, so that no injuries befall either the poles and wires or the shrubs and trees by their contact.
(Code 1982, § 12-101(11))
Sec. 42-13. - Burning of leaves and rubbish.
It shall be unlawful for any person, firm, or corporation to burn any leaves, paper, rubbish, or other substances upon any of the public streets, sidewalks or alleys of the city.
(Code 1982, § 12-101(12))
Sec. 42-14. - Deposit of limbs, shrubbery trimmings and debris, street right-of-way.
It shall be unlawful for any person, firm, or corporation to place limbs, shrubbery trimmings or other generated debris on street right-of-way for an extended period of time. This debris must be removed within seven days after placement on the right-of-way. Burning of any type of debris is prohibited on street right-of-ways.
Enforcement; penalty. This section shall be enforced by the office of the city building inspector. Citations for noncompliance may be issued by the chief building inspector of the city and subject to provisions of city ordinance section 1-11.
(Ord. No. 08-30, 11-13-2007)
Sec. 42-15. - Placement of curbside mailboxes.
All mailboxes shall be placed at curbside in accordance with the following dimensions: Front of mailbox to be six to eight inches from the curb or edge of pavement; height of mailbox to be 41 to 45 inches off the ground.
(Ord. No. 08-27, 11-13-2007)
Sec. 42-16. - Speed control devices.
The construction of speed control devices (speed bumps) are prohibited on all city controlled streets and alleys.
(Ord. No. 08-31, 3-11-2008)
Secs. 42-17—42-40. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - STREET CONSTRUCTION AND IMPROVEMENTS

Sec. 42-41. - Council resolution.
Whenever the improvement of any street or part thereof shall be deemed necessary by the mayor and council, a resolution stating the nature and location of the proposed improvement shall be declared and published once a week for three consecutive weeks in the newspaper in which the county sheriff's advertisements are published. If the owners of a majority of the lineal feet of frontage of the lands abutting on the proposed improvement do not within 15 days after the last day of publication of said resolution file with the clerk a written protest against such improvement, contracts for the work to be done shall be entered into and assessments and liens shall be fixed in accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-5-358.
(Code 1982, § 12-102(1))
Sec. 42-42. - Permit required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to construct or lay any pavement for or on any public street, sidewalk, curb, alley, or other public way, or to repair the same, without having first obtained a permit from the clerk in the manner specified in this chapter.
(Code 1982, § 12-102(2))
Sec. 42-43. - Bond.
Each applicant for a permit to construct or improve any pavement on any public street, sidewalk, curb, alley, or other public way shall be required to file a bond in the amount with surety to be approved by the city council, conditioned to indemnify the city for any loss or damage caused by, or resulting from, the work undertaken.
(Code 1982, § 12-102(3))
Sec. 42-44. - Specifications.
(a)
Generally. All street and sidewalk pavements and all curbing shall be laid in conformity with the specifications established or approved from time to time by the mayor and council.
(b)
Curb ramping. The standard for construction of curbs on each side of any city street, or any connecting street or road for which curbs have been prescribed, shall be not less than one ramp per lineal block on the crosswalks at intersections. Such ramps shall be at least 40 inches wide and constructed to allow reasonable access to the crosswalk for physically handicapped persons.
(Code 1982, § 12-102(4))
Sec. 42-45. - Barricades.
Any person, firm, or corporation laying or preparing any pavement on a street, sidewalk, or other public place shall maintain suitable barricades to prevent injury to any person or vehicle by reason of the work, which barricades shall be protected by a light at nighttime.
(Code 1982, § 12-102(5))
Secs. 42-46—42-80. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - EXCAVATIONS

Sec. 42-81. - Permit required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to dig up, break, excavate, tunnel, undermine, or in any manner break up any street, or to make or cause to be made any excavation in or under the surface of any street for any purpose, or to place, deposit, or leave upon any street any earth or other excavated material obstructing or tending to interfere with the free use of the street, unless such person shall first have obtained an excavation permit from the clerk in the manner specified in this chapter.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(1))
Sec. 42-82. - Excavation permit fee.
The excavation permit fee to be collected by the clerk shall be in an amount varying with the type of surface to be opened, dug, or excavated under the permit issued, on file in the clerk's office.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(2))
Sec. 42-83. - Surety bond.
Each applicant for an excavation permit shall be required to file a surety bond with the clerk in the amount approved by the council. The required surety bond must be conditioned upon the permittee's agreement to secure and hold harmless the city and its officers against and all claims, judgments, or other costs arising from the excavation and other work covered by the excavation permit or for which the city, the city council, or any city officer may be made liable by reason of any accident or injury to persons or property through the fault of the permittee resulting from the negligence of the permittee, and further conditioned on the permittee's agreement to fill up, restore, and place in good and safe condition as near as may be to its original condition, and to the satisfaction of the city manager, all openings and excavations made in streets, and to maintain any street where an excavation is made in as good condition for the period of 24 months after said work shall have been done, usual wear and tear excepted, as it was in before said work shall have been done. Any settlement of the surface within said two-year period shall be deemed conclusive evidence of defective backfilling by the permittee, except that nothing herein contained shall be construed to require the permittee to maintain any repairs to pavement made by the city if such repairs should prove defective. Recovery on such bond for any injury or accident shall not exhaust the bond, but it shall in its entirety cover any or all future accidents or injuries during the excavation work for which it is given. In the event of any suit or claim against the city by reason of the negligence or default of the permittee, upon the city's given written notice to the permittee of such suit or claim, any final judgment again the city requiring it to pay for such damage shall be conclusive upon the permittee and its surety.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(3))
Sec. 42-84. - Routing of traffic.
The permittee shall take appropriate measures to ensure that during the performance of the excavation work, traffic conditions shall be maintained at all times as nearly normal as practicable so as to cause as little inconvenience as possible to the occupants to the abutting property and to the general public, provided that the city manager may recommend the closing of streets to all traffic for a period of time if in his opinion it is necessary. The permittee shall route and control traffic, including its own vehicles, as directed by the police department. The following steps shall be taken before any highway may be closed or restricted to traffic:
(1)
The permittee must receive the approval of the city manager and the police department.
(2)
The permittee must notify the chief of the fire department of any street so closed.
(3)
Upon completion of construction work the permittee shall notify the city manager and city police department before traffic is moved back to its normal flow so that any necessary adjustments may be made.
(4)
Where flagmen are deemed necessary by the chief of police, they shall be furnished by the permittee at its own expense. Through traffic shall be maintained without the aid of detours, if possible. In instances in which this would not be feasible, the city manager will designate detours. The city shall maintain roadway surfaces of existing highways designated as detours without expense to the permittee, but in case there are not existing highways the permittee shall construct all detours at its expense and in conformity with the specifications of the city manager. The permittee will be responsible for any unnecessary damage caused to any highways by the operation of its equipment.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(4))
Sec. 42-85. - Clearance for fire equipment.
The excavation work shall be performed and conducted so as not to interfere with access to fire stations and fire hydrants. Materials or obstructions shall not be placed within 15 feet of fire plugs. Passageways leading to fire escapes or firefighting equipment shall be kept free of piles of material or other obstructions.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(5))
Sec. 42-86. - Protection of traffic.
The permittee shall construct and maintain adequate and safe crossings over excavations and across highways under improvement to accommodate vehicular and pedestrian traffic at all street intersections. Vehicular crossings shall be constructed and maintained of plank, timbers, and blocking of adequate size to accommodate vehicular traffic safely; pedestrian crossings shall be of adequate thickness, width, and length, together with necessary blocking, to ensure safe passage by pedestrians.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(6))
Sec. 42-87. - Interference with utilities.
The permittee shall not interfere with any existing utility without the written consent of the city manager and the utility company or person owning the utility. If it becomes necessary to remove an existing utility, this shall be done by its owner. No utility owned by the city shall be moved to accommodate the permittee, unless the costs of such work shall be borne by the permittee. The costs of moving privately owned utilities shall similarly be borne by the permittee unless it makes other arrangements with the person owning the utility. The permittee shall support and protect by timbers or otherwise all pipes, conduits, poles, wires, or other apparatus which may be in any way affected by the excavation work, and do everything necessary to support, sustain, and protect them under, over, along, or across said work. In case any of said pipes, conduits, poles, wires, or apparatus should be damaged, they shall be repaired by the agency or person owning them, and the expense of such repairs shall be charged to the permittee, and the bond of same shall be liable therefor. The permittee shall be responsible for any damage done to any public or private property by reason of the breaking of any water pipes, sewers, gas pipes, electric conduits, or other utilities, and its bond shall be liable therefor. The permittee shall inform itself as to the existence and location of all underground utilities and protect the same against damage.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(7))
Sec. 42-88. - Protection of adjoining property.
The permittee shall at all times, and at its own expense, preserve and protect from injury adjoining property by providing proper foundations and taking other measures suitable for the purpose. Where in the protection of such property it is necessary to enter upon private property for the purpose of taking appropriate protective measures, the permittee shall obtain permission from the owner of such private property for such purpose. The permittee shall, at its own expense, shore up and protect all buildings, walls, fences or other property likely to be damaged during the progress of the excavation work and shall be responsible for all damage to public or private property or highways resulting from its failure properly to protect and carry out said work. Whenever it may be necessary for the permittee to trench through lawn areas, the sod shall be carefully cut and rolled and replaced after ditches have been backfilled. All construction and maintenance work shall be done in a manner calculated to leave the lawn area clean of earth and debris and in a condition as near as possible to that which existed before such work began. The permittee shall not remove even temporarily any trees or shrubs which exist in parking strip areas or easements across private property without first having notified and obtained the consent of the property owner, or in the case of public property, the city manager.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(8))
Sec. 42-89. - Sidewalk excavations.
Any excavation made in or under any sidewalk shall be provided with a substantial and adequate footbridge over said excavation on the line of the sidewalk, which bridge shall be at least three feet wide and securely railed on each side so that pedestrians can pass over safely at all times.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(9))
Sec. 42-90. - Protective measures.
The permittee shall erect such fence, railing or barriers about the site of the excavation work as shall prevent danger to persons using the street or sidewalks, and such protective barriers shall be maintained until the work shall be completed or the danger removed. At twilight there shall be placed upon each place of excavation, and upon any excavated materials, structures or other obstructions to streets, suitable and sufficient lights which shall be kept burning throughout the night for the duration of such obstructions.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(10))
Sec. 42-91. - Attractive nuisances.
It shall be unlawful for the permittee to allow to remain unguarded at the place of excavation any machinery, equipment or other device having the characteristics of an attractive nuisance likely to attract children and be hazardous to their safety or health.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(11))
Sec. 42-92. - Care of excavated material.
All material excavated from trenches and piled adjacent to the trench or in any street shall be piled and maintained in such manner as not to endanger those working in the trench, pedestrians or users of the streets, and so that as little inconvenience as possible is caused to those using streets and adjoining property.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(12))
Sec. 42-93. - Damage to existing improvements.
All damage done to existing improvements during the progress of the excavation work shall be repaired by the permittee. Materials for such repair shall conform to the requirements of any applicable code provision or ordinance. If upon being ordered, the permittee fails to furnish the necessary labor and materials for such repairs, the city manager shall have the authority to cause said necessary labor and materials to be furnished by the city, and the costs shall be charged against the permittee, and the permittee shall also be liable on its bond therefor.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(13))
Sec. 42-94. - Cleanup.
As the excavation work progresses, all streets and private properties shall be thoroughly cleaned of all rubbish, excess earth, rock and other debris resulting from such work. All cleanup operations at the location of such excavation shall be accomplished at the expense of the permittee and shall be completed to the satisfaction of the city manager. From time to time, as may be ordered by said city manager, and in any event immediately after completion of said work, the permittee shall, at its own expense, clean up and remove all refuse and unused materials of any kind resulting from said work, and upon failure to do so within 24 hours after having been notified to do so by the city manager, said work may be done by the city and the costs thereof charged to the permittee, and the permittee shall also be liable for the costs thereof under the surety bond provided for hereunder.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(14))
Sec. 42-95. - Backfilling.
Backfilling in any street opened or excavated pursuant to an excavation permit issued hereunder shall be compacted to a degree equivalent to that of the undisturbed ground in which the trench was dug and in a manner consistent with sound engineering practices generally recognized in the construction industry.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(15))
Sec. 42-96. - Restoration of surface.
(a)
The permittee shall restore the surface of all streets broken into or damaged as a result of the excavation work to its original condition in accordance with the specifications of the city manager. The permittee may be required to place a temporary surface over openings made in paved traffic lanes and shall be required to exercise special care in making and maintaining such temporary restorations to assure safe traveling conditions until such time as permanent restorations are made.
(b)
Permanent restoration of streets shall be made by the permittee in strict accordance with the specifications prescribed by the city manager.
(c)
Acceptance or approval of any excavation work by the city manager shall not prevent the city from asserting a claim against the permittee and its surety under the surety bond required hereunder for incomplete or defective work if such defects are discovered within 24 months from the completion of the excavation work.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(16))
Sec. 42-97. - City's right to restore surface.
(a)
If the permittee shall have failed to restore the surface of the street to its original and proper condition upon the expiration of the time fixed by the permit, or shall otherwise have failed to complete the excavation work covered by such permit, the city manager, if he deems it advisable, shall have the right to do all work and things necessary to restore the street and to complete the excavation work. The permittee shall be liable for the actual cost thereof and 25 percent of such cost, in addition, for general overhead and administrative expenses.
(b)
It shall be the duty of the permittee to guarantee and maintain the site of the excavation work in the same condition it was prior to the excavation for two years after restoring it to its original condition.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(17))
Sec. 42-98. - Prompt completion of work.
The permittee shall prosecute with diligence and expediency all excavation work covered by the excavation permit and shall promptly complete such work and restore the street to its original condition, or as near as may be, as soon as practicable and in any event not later than the date specified in the excavation permit.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(18))
Sec. 42-99. - Emergency work.
In the event of any emergency in which a sewer, main, conduit, or utility in or under any street breaks, bursts, or otherwise is in such condition as to immediately endanger the property, life, health, or safety of any individual, the person owning or controlling such sewer, main, conduit, or utility, without first applying for and obtaining an excavation permit hereunder, shall immediately take proper emergency measures to cure or remedy the dangerous conditions for the protection of the property, life, health, and safety of individuals. However, such person owning or controlling such facility shall apply for an excavation permit not later than the end of the next succeeding day during which the clerk's office is open for business, and shall not proceed with permanent repairs without first obtaining an excavation permit as herein provided.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(19))
Sec. 42-100. - Noise, dust and debris.
Each permittee shall conduct and carry out the excavation work in such manner as to avoid unnecessary inconvenience and annoyance to the general public and occupants of neighboring property. The permittee shall take appropriate measures to reduce to the fullest extent practicable noise, dust, and unsightly debris and during the hours of 10:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m. shall not use, except with the express written permission of the city manager or in case of an emergency as herein provided, any tool, appliance or equipment producing noise of sufficient volume to disturb the sleep or repose of occupants of the neighboring property.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(20))
Sec. 42-101. - Inspections.
The city manager shall make such inspections as reasonably necessary in the enforcement of the provisions of this article, and shall have the authority to promulgate and cause to be enforced such rules and regulations as may be required to enforce and carry out the intent of this article.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(21))
Sec. 42-102. - Drawings.
Users of subsurface street space shall maintain accurate drawings, plans, and profiles showing the locations and character of all underground structures including abandoned installations, and shall provide the city manager with a copy of the same. Corrected maps shall be filed with such officer within 60 days after new installations, changes or replacements are made.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(22))
Sec. 42-103. - Exemption.
The provisions of this article shall not be applicable to any excavation work carried out under the direction of competent city authorities by employees of the city.
(Code 1982, § 12-103(23))
Secs. 42-104—42-130. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - DRIVEWAYS AND CURBS

Sec. 42-131. - Permit required.
No person shall begin to construct, reconstruct, repair, alter, or grade any driveway, curb or curb cut on the public streets without first obtaining a permit from the clerk in the manner specified in this article.
(Code 1982, § 12-104(1))
Sec. 42-132. - Bond.
The city manager shall have the authority to require an applicant for a permit hereunder to file a bond with his application conditioned to protect and save harmless the city from all claims for damages or injury to any persons by reason of the proposed alteration work.
(Code 1982, § 12-104(3))
Sec. 42-133. - Standards for issuance of permit.
The clerk shall issue a permit hereunder when he finds that:
(1)
The plans for the proposed operation have been approved by the city manager, to whom such plans shall be forwarded by the clerk within a reasonable time after receipt thereof.
(2)
The work shall be done according to the standard specifications of the city for public work of like character.
(3)
The operation will not unreasonably interfere with vehicular and pedestrian traffic and the means of ingress to and egress from the affected and adjacent properties.
(4)
The health, welfare and safety of the public will not be unreasonably impaired.
(Code 1982, § 12-104(4))
Sec. 42-134. - Supervision.
All work for which a permit is granted hereunder shall be under the direction and supervision of the city manager or designated persons.
Secs. 42-135—42-170. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - PROCEDURE FOR ISSUANCE OF PERMITS

Sec. 42-171. - Application.
Every person required to procure a permit under the provisions of this chapter or any ordinance or law relating to the subject matter of this chapter shall submit a written application for such permit to the clerk. The written application shall state the following:
(1)
The name and address of the applicant.
(2)
The purpose for which the application is submitted.
(3)
The nature and location of any work proposed to be done and the name of the person or firm who is to do such work.
(4)
The estimated cost of the proposed work.
(5)
The date of commencement and date of completion of the proposed work, and other data or plans as may reasonably be required by the clerk or the city manager.
(Code 1982, § 12-105(1))
Sec. 42-172. - Permit fee.
Unless otherwise provided, each application for a permit under this article shall be accompanied by a permit fee on file in the clerk's office.
(1)
Issuance of receipts. The clerk shall issue a receipt to the applicant for the amount of the fee tendered with the application for a permit, provided that such receipt shall not be construed as approval of the application.
(2)
Rebate of fee. Upon the disapproval of any application for which a fee has been submitted under the provisions of this article, the clerk shall refund such fee, provided that the applicant is not otherwise indebted to the city.
(Code 1982, § 12-105(2))
Sec. 42-173. - Review of application.
Upon receipt of the application for a permit under the provisions of this article, the clerk shall forward such application to the city manager, who shall approve or disapprove such application within seven days after receipt thereof. After indicating approval or disapproval on the application, the city manager shall return the same to the clerk.
(Code 1982, § 12-105(3))
Sec. 42-174. - Issuance of permit.
Upon receipt of an approved application from the city manager, the clerk shall issue a permit to the applicant therefor, which shall state the nature of the work to be done or activity to be carried out and bear the date of issuance and the signature of the clerk.
(Code 1982, § 12-105(4))
Sec. 42-175. - Display of permit.
It shall be the duty of any permittee hereunder to keep the permit issued to him in a conspicuous place at the site of the work or activity to be engaged in. It shall be unlawful for any person to exhibit such permit at or about any location not covered thereby, or to misrepresent the number of the permit or the date of expiration of the permit.
(Code 1982, § 12-105(5))
Sec. 42-176. - Limitation on issuance.
Notwithstanding any other provision of this article, no permit shall be issued to any applicant therefor who has failed to satisfy any bonding requirements imposed upon persons engaged in the kind of work or activity proposed to be done or carried out by such applicant.
(Code 1982, § 12-105(6))
Secs. 42-177—42-199. - Reserved.
ARTICLE VI. - DOWNTOWN BUSINESS DISTRICT GUIDELINES

Sec. 42-200. - In general.
The purpose of this document is to provide guidelines for property owners, merchants and event sponsors within the City of Glennville Downtown Business Area. However, all state and federal regulations to include American Disabilities Act (ADA) supersede these regulations as set forth in this article.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-201. - Definitions.
For purposes of this article, the following terms shall have the meanings respectively ascribed to them by this section:
Downtown business area: The area of Barnard Street from U.S. 301(Veterans Boulevard) to Tillman Street to include alleys,(one block) to the north and south of said area excluding U.S. 301.
Downtown development authority: A committee appointed by the mayor and council, for the purpose of revitalization and redevelopment of the central business district of the city, to develop and promote for the public good and general welfare trade, commerce, industry and employment opportunities, and to promote the general welfare of the State of Georgia by creating a climate favorable to the location of new industry, trade and commerce and the development of existing industry, trade and commerce within the city.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-202. - Regulation of use of streets and sidewalks for business purposes.
(a)
The mayor and council shall have full power and authority to regulate the use of the streets and sidewalks of the City of Glennville for business purposes and no person, firm or corporation shall have the right to use the streets and sidewalks of the City of Glennville for business purposes without first being permitted by the City of Glennville.
(b)
If at any time, it shall appear that any person is encroaching upon or maintaining any obstruction in any part of a street, sidewalk, alley or other thoroughfare, it shall be the duty of the city manager, chief of police or code enforcement officer or their designees to give notice to such party, ordering the removal of such encroachment or obstruction; and if such encroachment or obstruction is not removed within 24 hours after receipt of such notice, or such other reasonable time as may be designated by the city manager, chief of police or code enforcement officer, it shall be the duty of the chief of police to cause any such encroachment or obstruction to be summarily removed, and the cost and expense thereof shall be charged to the person maintaining the encroachment or obstruction.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-203. - Street closing.
The complete or partial closure of any street, sidewalk or other public way as a part of a special event shall be approved by the city manager in consultation with the chief of police.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-204. - Banners and signs across streets and alleys restricted.
It shall be unlawful for any person to place or have placed any banner or sign across any public street or alley except when expressly authorized by the mayor and council.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-205. - Obstructing view at intersections prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any property owner or occupant to have or maintain on his property any billboard, merchandise or other obstruction which prevents persons driving vehicles on public streets or alleys from obtaining a clear view of traffic when approaching an intersection.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-206. - Encroachment by street coverings, awnings or other shelters.
It shall be unlawful for any person to build or maintain within the fire limits of the city over or upon the sidewalks or streets of the city, within said limits, any shelter or sidewalk or street covering, which shall encroach upon said street or sidewalks, or decorative street lights and extending arms, which said shelter or sidewalk or street covering shall be supported from the ground by columns or otherwise.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-207. - Riding on sidewalks.
It shall be unlawful for any person to ride any horse, bicycle, motorcycle, or roller skate, roller blade or skateboard on any sidewalk within the area designated as downtown business area.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-208. - Deposits and discharges.
(a)
No person shall sweep or deposit in any gutter, street, storm drainage system or other public place within downtown business area the accumulation of litter from any building or lot from any public or private sidewalk or driveway. Persons owning or occupying property shall keep the sidewalk in front of their premises free of litter.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to deposit on any street, sidewalk or storm drainage system any material which may be harmful to the pavement thereof, or any waste materials, or any glass or other article which may do injury to any person, animal or property.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to discharge or to allow to be discharged onto any city street, sidewalk or storm drainage system any water or other fluid material containing objectionable material such as sewage, waste, milk or other organic materials.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-209. - Maintenance of property between street and sidewalk.
The city requests and encourages that all persons who owns real estate in the city which abuts upon any sidewalk keep that portion of such sidewalk which lies between the property and the curb line of the adjoining street, and upon which his real estate abuts, free from growing weeds and other obnoxious and unsightly vegetation or other things which would mar or detract from the beauty and cleanliness of the street upon which their property abuts. If the owners are not in possession of their property, then this section shall apply to their tenants or those who have possession or control of the same.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-210. - Interference with traffic and conduct of business.
No number of persons shall block any street, sidewalk, alley or entrance to any place of business in the city by congregating on any street, alley sidewalk, or entrance to any place of business in the city in such number as to interfere with traffic or the conduct of business thereon and therein.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-211. - Defacing sidewalks, streets, and curbs.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to deface any public sidewalk, street or curb in the city by painting any signs thereon, whether for commercial advertising purposes or not, or to walk or to drive any vehicle upon or injure in any way any street, sidewalk or curbing pavement while the same is guarded by a warning sign or barricade or is soft or newly laid.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any unauthorized person to move, disturb or take any earth, stone or other material from any street, sidewalks or other public property.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-212. - Trees and shrubs.
(a)
Planting and removal. It shall be unlawful for any person to plant, remove, injure, or cut any tree, bush or shrub in or from any public street, or other public place without first obtaining a permit from the city.
(b)
Advertisement. It shall be unlawful for any person to attach any sign, advertisement or notice to any tree or shrub in any public place.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-213. - Street numbers.
The owner or owners of any real estate upon which there is situated a business or other building or structure to which there is assigned a street number by the city shall post the street number of such business or other building, in one of the following manners:
(a)
By affixing the street number to the front of the business or other building in a prominent manner such that the same can be easily viewed from the street or road upon which such business or other building fronts.
(b)
By incorporating the street number into or onto a sign located on the property in such a manner that the same can be easily viewed from the street upon business or other building fronts.
(c)
All street numbering shall be a minimum of three inches in height and a maximum of five inches.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-214. - Encroachments, obstructions.
(a)
It is unlawful for any person to erect or maintain any building or structure which encroaches upon any public street or property, or to erect any poles or wires or maintain any poles or wires over any public place, street, alley or other public place.
(b)
No person shall erect, place or maintain in, upon or over any sidewalk, street, alley or other public place in the city, any fruit stand, shoe shining stand, flower stand, vegetable stand, lunch wagon, table box bin or other arrangement or structure for the display or sale of goods, wares or merchandise, or for the pursuit of any occupation whatsoever.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-215. - Smoking prohibited.
Smoking is prohibited on the Barnard Street of the Glennville Downtown Business Area. Smoking is allowed only in the alleys located behind the downtown business area.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-216. - Use of public spaces and facilities.
No person shall, without first submitting and receiving a permit from the city manager or his/her designee made according to the requirements set out hereinafter:
(a)
Conduct a public assembly ,meeting, march, parade, demonstration, or other like event fixed or processional.
(b)
Circulate or distribute any leaflets, handbills, notices, pamphlets, book, documents or papers of any kind on any public street, sidewalk or public place.
(c)
Bring any non-domestic (e.g., any of the following would be considered non-domestic: poisonous, exotic, dangerous, feral, or farm animals) on, over or upon the public properties of the city, or facilities thereon.
(d)
Station or erect any building, stand, bandstand, stage, tower, scaffold, sound stage, platform, rostrum or other structure on, over, or upon the public properties of the city.
(e)
Create or emit any electronically amplified sound, on, over, or upon the public properties of the city, except from a radio, recorder or other device, which is possessed and used by an individual for his own enjoyment and is operated in such a manner so as to not interfere with the and enjoyment by any other person; or
(f)
Conduct any exhibit, music, or dramatic performance, fair, circus, concert, play, radio or television broadcast, other than a news transmission on, over, or upon the public properties of the city; or station or use any electrical or electronic device or equipment that would require outdoor auxiliary power.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-217. - Event application.
No less than 30 days prior to an event, the person associated in fact, whether or not a legally recognized entity, who wish to conduct an event requiring a permit, shall apply to the City of Glennville for an event permit.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-218. - Exhibition shows in street regulated.
(a)
Defined. For the purposes of this section "exhibition show" shall mean an exhibition or show where merchandise, wares or other tangible items are displayed for show and/or sale either out of doors or in a building occupied in whole or part for a period not to exceed two days. It shall not include a sidewalk sale conducted by a merchant having a valid business license, on the sidewalk immediately adjoining said merchants' store. However, all sidewalk sales must be conducted so that reasonable use of the sidewalk is maintained for the normal pedestrian traffic.
(b)
Permit required. No person shall engage in, participate in, aid, form or commence an exhibition show as defined above, in or upon any street, sidewalk or other public place in the city unless an event permit has been obtained from the city.
(c)
The producer of a special event shall be required to provide temporary outdoor toilet facilities for any event at which 200 or more persons are expected to be in attendance. The number of toilets and their locations shall be determined by the city code enforcement section.
(d)
Persons wishing to use the street or sidewalks to conduct an event must present to the city a writing signed by an agent of his insurance carrier stating the person currently carries liability insurance in the amount equal to or exceeding $500,000.00, and that any accident or mishap occurring during the event is covered under the primary liability coverage.
(e)
The producer of any special event shall hold harmless the city, its officers, employees and agents from liability of damages arising from any acts or omissions emanating from a special event. Proof of such wavier shall be filed with, and made a part of the application form. The producer shall be responsible for providing any and all other insurance that may be necessary for any special event. The amount and kind of insurance required shall be determined by the city manager, or his/her designee, after considering the nature of the special event, and the potential for harm and the degree of harm to persons and property. The producer shall provide proof to the city manager or his/her designee, that such insurance is in effect prior to the issue of a permit.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-219. - Cessation of activities.
The safety, health, welfare and good order of all citizens and the community shall be first and foremost and every special event shall be conducted in such manner. The city manager, chief of police or code enforcement officer or their designee may terminate any activity, whether a part of or the entire event, which does not meet this community standard. Upon notification of the event producer in person by the city manager, chief of police or code enforcement officer to cease any activity, it shall be unlawful for such producer and/or any participants to permit such activity to continue.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-220. - Display of merchandise on sidewalks.
It shall be permissible and lawful for merchants and dealers in various and sundry types of merchandise to display their merchandise on the sidewalk in front of their place of business provided such merchandise does not extend more than 27 inches from the front wall of the building occupied by the merchant or dealer (this area can be generally defined as the area of concrete located between the front wall of the building and the edge of the brick pavers), and provided the placing of such merchandise or articles for sale on the sidewalk does not interfere with the free and uninterrupted passage by the public along the sidewalk, and meets all state and federal regulations to include American Disabilities Act (ADA). No merchandise, articles for sale or other items shall be placed on or among any of the streets, alleys, sidewalks or passageways within the city if the placing of same will interfere with the free use and passage of the public along said street, alley, sidewalk or passageway.
(a)
The merchant wishing to use the sidewalk for display of goods must present to the city a writing signed by an agent of his insurance carrier stating the merchant currently carries premises liability insurance in the amount equal to or exceeding $500,000.00 and that any accident or mishap occurring as a result of merchant displaying any wares, goods or merchandise of any kind upon any part of a city sidewalk is covered under the primary liability coverage.
(b)
The merchant must execute an indemnity contract between himself and the city in which he agrees to indemnify and save harmless the city from any and all liability resulting from merchant's displaying any wares, goods, or merchandise of any kind upon any part of a city sidewalk.
(c)
A merchant using city sidewalk pursuant to this section may use only that portion of the sidewalk directly in front of his own store, and may not in any way encroach upon the sidewalk frontage directly in front of any other store or business.
(d)
Any items placed must be free of any lewd, racist or derogatory materials or images.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-221. - Posting.
No person shall stick or post any handbill or placard of any description upon any public street light, electrical power poles, street signs, on vehicles, or any other structure without the permission of the property owner.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-222. - Signage.
(a)
Permit required. Sign permits are required for all signs affixed to or hung from building, awning or structure. Permit applications are available in Glennville City Hall.
(b)
Sidewalk signs in front of store fronts are authorized, provided the signs meet the follow conditions:
(1)
Overall height not to exceed 60 inches tall and width not to exceed 27 inches wide.
(2)
Contains no posters or print materials.
(3)
Features no logos other than that of the business it promotes.
(4)
Contain a chalkboard writing area (no dry-erase boards).
(5)
Does not extend more than 27 inches from the front wall of the building (this area can be generally defined as the area of concrete located between the front wall of the building and the edge of the brick pavers), occupied by the merchant or dealer, and provided the placing of such signage on the sidewalk does not interfere with the free and uninterrupted passage by the public along the sidewalk, and meets all state and federal regulations to include American Disabilities Act (ADA).
(6)
Items placed must be free of any lewd, racist or derogatory materials or images.
(c)
All planned signage for downtown area must be reviewed and recommended by the downtown development authority prior to approval of the city.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)
Sec. 42-223. - Storefront awnings, painting and renovations.
Prior to installing any awning, painting of exterior or renovation of any storefront, a permit must be obtained from city hall. All permits must be reviewed by the Glennville Downtown Development Authority which will make recommendations to the mayor and council for approval/disapproval.
(Res. No. 14-17, 11-18-2013)

ARTICLE VII. - STREET NAMING/RENAMING

Sec. 42-300. - Purpose of article.
The city council may consider to accept applications for the naming or renaming of streets or for placement of signage or plaques on streets in honor or memory of individual(s) or group(s).
(Res. No. 20-02, 7-2-2019)
Sec. 43-301. - Procedures for street naming, name change or placement of plaques on streets in honor or memory of individual(s) or group(s).
Any person desiring to submit a petition requesting to name a street or change in street name or placement of signage or plaques in honor of individual(s) or group(s) shall file such petition or request together with a payment in the amount on file in the clerk's office to cover all administrative, advertising and signage costs.
(Res. No. 20-02, 7-2-2019)
Sec. 42-302. - Public hearing notification.
The building inspector or his designee shall then prepare and cause to be published at least once in the area newspaper, a newspaper of general circulation within the territorial boundaries of the city, a notice of the public hearing(s) which will be held by the city council stating the time, place and purpose of such hearings, the location of the street, the present street name and the proposed name change. The publication of said notice shall be published at least 15 days and no more than 45 days prior to the date of the hearing(s).
(Res. No. 20-02, 7-2-2019)
Sec. 42-304. - Notification of property owners.
Upon request of any street naming or renaming or plaque placement in honor or memory of individual(s) or group(s), the building inspector or his designee shall notify by First Class United States Postal Mail or personal delivery all persons owning property that has boundaries along such street of the time, place and purpose of the hearing before the city council in such proceedings. It being the intention of this section to notify, insofar as reasonably possible, those persons substantially interested or affected in the proposed change in street renaming/or in honor or memory of, that a public hearing is pending. Depositing such notices in the United States Postal Mail is properly addressed envelopes with adequate postage affixed of personal delivery at least 15 days prior to the date of the hearing of which notice is given shall be deemed full compliance with the requirements of this section.
(Res. No. 20-02, 7-2-2019)
Sec. 42-305 - City council meeting procedures.
The hearings before the city council shall be public hearings as aforesaid and all discussions and deliberations shall be conducted in open meeting provided, however, that nothing contained herein shall prohibit the city council from going into executive session for the purpose of discussing legal questions arising due to the matter under consideration with the city attorney in an attorney-client relationship. At the commencement of the hearing, the presiding officer, whether the mayor or mayor pro tem or someone designated by the presiding officer, shall give a brief explanation of the matter under consideration and the issue(s) to be determined. Following such explanation, the petitioner and proponents of the petition seeking to name a street or change in street name or placement of plaques or signage in honor or memory of individual(s) along said street, or as the case may be, shall be afforded the first opportunity to present their case to the city council. After this presentment, all parties in opposition shall be afforded an opportunity to present their opposition and evidence, and, after all parties have been so heard by the city council, the city council may, but shall not be required to permit one person to summarize the evidence of the proponents, and one person to summarize the evidence of the parties in opposition, limiting the time for the presentation of such summaries to the extent the presiding officer deems appropriate. After all parties have been heard and such summaries, if any, have been completed, the city council shall close the floor to open debate and further evidence, whereupon the city council shall consider the issue to be determined and the evidence and arguments presented to it during the hearing, and make its decision by open ballot. Once deliberation begins by the city council on the issue before it, no further evidence may be presented or arguments made either for or against the proposed request without the express consent of the city council.
Sec. 42-306. - Placement of signage/plaques.
(a)
All signs shall comply with U.S. Department of Transportation, Federal Highway Administration, current edition, [and] recreational and cultural interest area signs.
(b)
The cost of all signs shall be borne by the petitioner.
(Res. No. 20-02, 7-2-2019)
