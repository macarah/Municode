Chapter 2 - ADMINISTRATION
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Secs. 2-1—2-18. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - MAYOR AND CITY COUNCIL

Sec. 2-19. - Compensation.
(a)
The mayor shall be compensated for services to the city in an amount as determined by ordinance from time to time.
(b)
Each council member shall be compensated for services as a council member in an amount as determined by ordinance from time to time.
(Code 1985, § 2-1-1; Code 2004, § 30.01)
Sec. 2-20. - Regular meetings.
(a)
The mayor and city council shall hold the regular public meeting at 7:00 p.m. on the fourth Monday of each month with the work session at 7:00 p.m. on the Thursday immediately preceding the fourth Monday of each month at the public safety building; provided, however, the mayor and city council may, by majority vote reschedule, with two weeks' notice, any city council meeting of any given month for another specified time, date and place.
(b)
Notice of any change in any given regular city council meeting shall be posted in the public safety building at least two weeks in advance of the meeting time, and the notice shall further be given in a newspaper in general circulation in the city.
(Code 1985, § 2-1-11; Code 2004, § 30.02; Ord. No. 262, 4-12-1982; Am. Ord. No. 356, 4-14-2003; Am. Ord. No. 358, 4-14-2003; Am. Ord. No. 434, 1-11-2010; Am. Ord. No. 462, 3-17-2014; Am. Ord. No. 1704, 5-22-2017)
Sec. 2-21. - Adjourned meetings.
If a quorum shall fail to attend any regular or special meeting of the mayor and city council or if for any reason any meeting shall fail to complete transaction of the business during the meeting, the meeting may be adjourned to any date prior to the next regular meeting agreed upon by a majority of the members present.
(Code 1985, § 2-1-12; Code 2004, § 30.03)
Sec. 2-22. - Order of business.
(a)
The order of business at all regular meetings shall be as follows:
(1)
Approval of minutes of last meeting;
(2)
Corrections, if any, to be made in the minutes;
(3)
Unfinished business;
(4)
New business;
(5)
Other business.
(b)
If the mayor and city council directs any matter to be the special business of a future meeting, that matter shall have precedence over all other business at that future meeting.
(c)
No proposition shall be entertained by the mayor and city council until it has been seconded, and every proposition shall, when required by the mayor or any member, be reduced to writing.
(Code 1985, § 2-1-13; Code 2004, § 30.04)
Sec. 2-23. - Rules of procedure.
Except as otherwise provided by ordinance, the procedures of the mayor and city council shall be governed by Robert's Rules of Order.
(Code 1985, § 2-1-14; Code 2004, § 30.05)
Sec. 2-24. - Previous questions.
The previous questions may be called at any time by a majority of the members present. The ayes and nays may be called for by any member.
(Code 1985, § 2-1-15; Code 2004, § 30.06)
Sec. 2-25. - Motions having precedence.
(a)
When a question is under consideration no motion shall be received except as follows:
(1)
To lay on the table;
(2)
To postpone to a time certain;
(3)
To postpone indefinitely;
(4)
To refer to a committee;
(5)
To amend;
(6)
To strike out or insert; or
(7)
To divide.
(b)
Motions for any of these purposes shall have precedence in the order named.
(Code 1985, § 2-1-16; Code 2004, § 30.07)
Sec. 2-26. - Motion to adjourn.
A motion to adjourn shall always be in order and shall be decided without debate.
(Code 1985, § 2-1-17; Code 2004, § 30.08)
Sec. 2-27. - Standing committees.
(a)
There are established the following standing committees of the city council, which each shall consist of not less than two members of the council.
(1)
Committee on government operations.
(2)
Committee on finance.
(3)
Committee on legal affairs.
(b)
Appointment; term; vacancy.
(1)
The mayor shall annually at the first council meeting in January appoint the standing committee members.
(2)
All such appointments shall be for one year.
(3)
The mayor shall serve as an ex-officio, non-voting member of each of the standing committees.
(4)
The mayor shall have the authority to appoint members to the standing committees, however the council shall have the authority to reject any appointment by majority vote.
(5)
Should a vacancy occur in any committee by the withdrawal for whatever reason of any committee member, the appointment may be made at any time by the mayor to fill the vacancy.
(6)
The standing committees shall meet whenever necessary and may be called upon by the chair of each committee, the mayor or the city manager.
(7)
The committees shall present their findings to the full city council during regular meetings for consideration when necessary.
Code 2004, § 30.09; Ord. No. 1706, 6-27-2017)
Sec. 2-28. - Oliver Hardy steering committee.
(a)
Steering committee. The committee shall be comprised as follows:
(1)
The steering committee shall be comprised of five regular members to be appointed by the city council and shall have terms of three years. The members are not required to be residents of the city but shall have an interest in the event.
(2)
In addition, the council shall have the ability but are not required to appoint four ex-officio members to the steering committee. One each from the following: Harlem Arts Council, Harlem Merchants Association, and two representative of the mayor and city council with the consent of the city manager (if representative is an employee) to serve as public safety coordinators; said representatives should be active police officers or fire fighters. Ex-officio members shall have the right to participate in discussions but are not entitled to vote on matters under consideration by the committee.
(3)
The committee shall from time to time report to the mayor and city council on the annual Oliver Hardy Festival, and shall be required to present a recap of the festival to the mayor and city council within 60 days after the event at a regular meeting of the city council.
(b)
Procedures. The committee shall:
(1)
Appoint one of their members to act as chair of the committee and another member to act as vice-chair of the committee on an annual basis;
(2)
Meet a minimum of once every month;
(3)
Operate in accordance with Robert's Rules of Order and allow public input during their regular meetings to anyone who attends; and
(4)
The committee shall establish such ad hoc committees as may be required from time to time to provide the committee with advice on specific events, proposals and initiatives.
(c)
Duties and powers. The committee shall:
(1)
Recommend suggestions to the mayor and city council for ways to improve the festival.
(2)
Have authority to spend money within an annual budget approved by the mayor and city council.
(3)
Hold events to raise funds for the event, provided that all funds are presented to the city to be held in a restricted account for the sole purpose of supporting the festival.
(4)
Establish the festival venue areas, vendor list, entertainment schedule, and dates for the annual festival and all other matters necessary and reasonable for planning and executing the annual festival.
(5)
Coordinate all recommendations and festival details with the representative of the mayor and city council.
(Code 2004, § 30.10; Ord. No. 1721, 1-22-2018)
Secs. 2-29—2-59. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - OFFICERS AND EMPLOYEES

Sec. 2-60. - City clerk; duties.
In addition to the duties of the city clerk under the city Charter, the city clerk shall perform the following duties:
(1)
Be the custodian of the city seal and affix its impression on documents whenever required;
(2)
Preserve the codes, records and documents belonging to the city and maintain a proper index to all records and documents;
(3)
Receive all money due the city, including taxes, licenses and fees, and pay out the same only upon orders of the mayor and city council; and
(4)
Perform any other duties as may be required by the mayor and city council.
(Code 1985, § 2-4-1; Code 2004, § 31.01)
Sec. 2-61. - City attorney; duties.
The city attorney shall be the legal advisor and representative of the city and in that capacity shall perform the duties and responsibilities set out in the city Charter, in addition to those duties designated by the mayor and city council.
(Code 1985, § 2-4-2; Code 2004, § 31.02)
Sec. 2-62. - City manager; duties.
(a)
Purpose. To establish responsibilities, powers, and duties of city manager pursuant to section 2.30 of the Charter of the city and to provide for a job description and duties of the position.
(b)
Duties. The city manager is charged with the responsibility of acting as the chief executive and administrative officer of the city of ensuring that all administrative functions of the city are carried out in accordance with the policies and procedures of the city as determined by the mayor and city council. The city manager's duties shall include, but are not limited to, the following:
(1)
Faithful execution and enforcement of the laws of the United States, this state and city, including ordinances, resolutions, and rules and regulations of the city;
(2)
Preparation of the agenda and attendance at meetings of the mayor and city council;
(3)
Preparation and recommendations for adoption of any measures deemed proper and suitable for the city;
(4)
The monitoring of the city's overall financial condition and its future needs and reporting of the condition and the needs to the mayor and city council as needed;
(5)
Preparation of correspondence to state and federal agencies and departments regarding state or federal qualifications and rules for the city, grants, or available loans, and reporting the correspondence to the mayor and city council;
(6)
Appointment, suspension, or removal of any city employee, except the city recorder, recorder pro temp, city attorney, city treasurer, city clerk or in accordance with the general rules, regulations, policies or ordinances of the city;
(7)
Preparation and submission of an annual proposed budget and capital program to the mayor and city council as directed;
(8)
Administration of all departments, properties, and operations of the city, subject to the general direction and control of the mayor and city council;
(9)
Coordination of the investigation of the affairs, records, accounts, and expenditures of the various commissions and boards created by the city and report the findings of same to the mayor and city council at least once per year;
(10)
Communication with the city attorney concerning any matter effecting the legal interest of the city;
(11)
At the request of the mayor and city council, act as liaison with boards, offices, agencies, and commissions of the city;
(12)
The exercise of any other powers and the performance of any other duties required or authorized by the mayor and city council not inconsistent with the Charter, ordinances, and resolutions of the city.
(c)
Residence requirements. During his tenure of office, the city manager shall reside in the city normal moving and transfer of residence time excepted.
(d)
Restrictions. The city manager shall not engage in any other business or profession during his tenure of office without the permission and approval of the mayor and city council.
Code 2004, § 31.03; Ord. No. 354-A, 1-14-2002)
Sec. 2-63. - Code enforcement officer; duties.
(a)
Office created. The city hereby creates the position of code enforcement officer to oversee the enforcement of this Code.
(b)
Oversight. The code enforcement officer shall report directly to the city manager or his/her designee, and shall be appointed or removed directly by the city manager or designee subject to the Harlem Personnel Policies and Procedures Manual.
(c)
Requirements. The code enforcement officer shall not be required to be a sworn police officer by the state. The city manager at his or her discretion shall appoint anyone as code enforcement officer as he or she sees fit whether on a part-time or full-time basis. Educational and training opportunities will be made available and internal policies and procedures shall be developed within community development department subject to the city manager's approval for all matters involving the code enforcement officer.
(d)
Goals. The city sets forth that the goal of the code enforcement officer shall be gaining compliance with all provisions of this Code.. However, it is understood that from time to time, the code enforcement officer may have to issue citations which would fall under the jurisdiction of the Harlem Municipal Court as provided by law. No citation shall be issued without direct approval by the city manager.
(Ord. No. 1814, 9-24-2018)
Sec. 2-64. - Purpose of personnel policies.
As deemed necessary and appropriate, the city council may establish certain personnel policies, manuals or other controlling documents (including organizational charts and pay scales) for the purpose of providing for an orderly process for administering the personnel system of the city and to provide the fundamental features for an effective system of personnel administration. These personnel policies and procedures are not and do not form an express or implied contract between the city and its employees.
(Code 2004, § 33.01; Ord. No. 459, 12-16-2013)
Secs. 2-65—2-84. - Reserved.

DIVISION 3. - FIRE DEPARTMENT[1]

Sec. 2-142. - Fire chief and members.
The fire department shall be headed by a fire chief and shall be composed of the other paid and volunteer members and employees as may be determined necessary by the mayor and city council.
(Code 1985, § 3-2-1; Code 2004, § 32.20)
Sec. 2-143. - Interfering with equipment.
No person other than a duly enrolled member of the fire department shall ride upon the fire trucks of the city at any time, nor use, borrow or interfere with any fire department equipment, or attempt to use the equipment at the scene of a fire unless authorized to do so by the fire chief.
(Code 1985, § 3-2-2; Code 2004, § 32.21)
Sec. 2-144. - Enforcement.
Whenever the fire department is answering an alarm or operating at the scene of a fire or other emergency every enrolled member of the department is empowered and authorized to control and direct motor vehicle traffic, stop or move vehicles, and enforce all provisions of this chapter and any other code provisions the enforcement of which is deemed necessary to assist in the control of the fire or other emergency.
(Code 1985, § 3-2-3; Code 2004, § 32.22)
Sec. 2-145. - Tampering with alarm system.
It shall be unlawful for any person to willfully, maliciously or mischievously interfere or tamper with any fire alarm box or any of the appliances or apparatus connected therewith located within the corporate limits.
(Code 1985, § 3-2-4; Code 2004, § 32.23)
Sec. 2-146. - Testing of siren.
The fire chief of the city shall regularly sound the fire siren for the purpose of testing the same at the time on a regular basis to be selected by the fire chief. For the purpose of implementing the test, the fire chief is authorized and directed to install an automatic clock device which can be calibrated automatically to conduct the test on a regular basis.
(Code 1985, § 3-2-5; Code 2004, § 32.24; Ord. No. 225, 6-12-1972)
Sec. 2-147. - Service beyond city limits.
(a)
The owners of all single-family residential structures located outside the city limits, but within the area to be served by the city volunteer fire department, as established by mutual agreement among all fire departments of the county (the unincorporated service area), shall pay a fee to the city for fire protection services computed on the basis, as established by the mayor and city council and provided in the city fee schedule, of the fair market value of the improvements as valued by the county board of tax assessors; provided, however, there is established a minimum annual fee, as established by the mayor and city council and provided in the city fee schedule, for all the structures located outside the city limits, but within the area served by the fire department and a maximum fee, as established by the mayor and city council and provided in the city fee schedule, for all the structures.
(b)
The owners of all commercial, business, or industrial structures located in the unincorporated service area shall pay an annual fee of an amount, as established by the mayor and city council and provided in the city fee schedule, of the fair market value of all improvements to real estate located at one location within the fire protection area as valued by the county board of tax assessors; provided, however, there shall be a minimum fee, as established by the mayor and city council and provided in the city fee schedule, for all the structures and a maximum fee, as established by the mayor and city council and provided in the city fee schedule, for the structures located at one location.
(c)
The owners of all structures, commercial and residential, located within the unincorporated service area shall receive the same fire protection services afforded by the fire department to owners of structures within the city and to those paying the subscription fee without the necessity of paying an annual fee to the city, provided the owners register annually with the fire department and agree to pay to the city the sum, as established by the mayor and city council and provided in the city fee schedule, for each and every call to a residential structure that the fire department shall receive to go to the structure and a fee, as established by the mayor and city council and provided in the city fee schedule, for each and every call to a business, commercial, or industrial structure that the fire department shall receive to go to the structure.
(d)
In the event the annual fees established herein are not paid within 30 days of the date billed, there shall be added a ten percent penalty for payment during the following 30 days. If payment has not been made within 60 days, the owners will be deemed to have declined fire protection services and the fire department shall be under no obligation to furnish the service. Should the fire department elect to respond to the call even though the owner is not a subscriber as provided herein, the city shall charge a fee as mutually agreed, or if none, a fee not less than the limits stated in subsection (c) of this section.
(Code 1985, § 3-2-6; Code 2004, § 32.25; Ord. No. 290, 4-13-1987)

DIVISION 4. - CITY PLANNING COMMISSION

Sec. 2-178. - Establishment.
The city planning commission, hereinafter referred to as the planning commission, is created and established.
(Code 1985, § 6-1-1; Code 2004, § 32.40; Ord. No. 205, 5-17-1960; Ord. No. 215, 6-15-1962)
Sec. 2-179. - Membership.
The planning commission shall consist of five members, who shall be residents of the city, appointed by the mayor and city council. The terms of the members shall be for four years. Any vacancy in membership shall be filled for the unexpired term by appointment of a qualified member by the mayor and city council who shall also have the authority to remove any member for cause, on written charges, after a public hearing. All members shall serve without compensation but may be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in connection with their official duties.
(Code 1985, § 6-1-2; Code 2004, § 32.41; Ord. No. 205, 5-17-1960; Ord. No. 215, 6-15-1962)
Sec. 2-180. - Organization, rules, staff and finances.
(a)
The planning commission shall elect its chairperson from among its members. The term of the chairperson shall be one year with eligibility for re-election. The planning commission shall appoint a secretary who may be an officer or employee of the municipality. The planning commission shall make its own rules of procedure and determine its time of meeting. All meetings of the planning commission shall be open to the public except where otherwise permitted by state law, and all records of the planning commission shall be a public record.
(b)
The planning commission may appoint the employees and staff as it may deem necessary for its work and may contract with city planners and other consultants for the services as it may require. The expenditures of the planning commission, shall be within the amounts appropriated for the purpose by the mayor and city council.
(Code 1985, § 6-1-3; Code 2004, § 32.42; Ord. No. 205, 5-17-1960; Ord. No. 215, 6-15-1962; Ord. No. 2112, 5-24-2021)
Sec. 2-181. - Powers and duties.
(a)
It shall be the function and the duty of the planning commission to make sure careful and comprehensive surveys and studies of existing conditions and probable future developments and to prepare the plans for physical, social, and economic growth as will best promote the public health, safety, morals, convenience, prosperity, or the general welfare as well as efficiency and economy in the development of the municipal subdivision.
(b)
In particular, the commission shall have the power and duty to:
(1)
Prepare a master plan or parts thereof for the development of the town;
(2)
Prepare and recommend for adoption to the mayor and city council a zoning ordinance and map for the town;
(3)
Prepare and recommend for adoption by the governing authority regulations for the subdivision of land and administer the regulations that may be adopted; and
(4)
Prepare and recommend for adoption to the governing authority a planning or official map showing the exact location of the boundary lines of existing, proposed, extended, widened or narrowed streets, public open spaces or public building sites, together with regulations to control the erection of buildings or other structures within the lines within the town.
(Code 1985, § 6-1-4; Code 2004, § 32.43)
Secs. 2-182—2-200. - Reserved.
DIVISION 5. - AREA PLANNING COMMISSION

Sec. 2-201. - Establishment.
The Central Savannah River Area Planning and Development Commission, hereinafter referred to as the area planning commission, is created and established as authorized by O.C.G.A. § 50-8-30 et seq., as amended. The area planning commission shall be organized and empowered as set out in this division.
(Code 1985, §6-1-5; Code 2004, § 32.55; Ord. No. 211, 9-19-1961)
Sec. 2-202. - Membership.
The Central Savannah River Area Planning and Development Commission shall consist of two members from each of the counties participating in the Area planning commission. Of the two members from each county one shall be appointed by the governing authority municipality wherein the county seat is located and the other member shall be appointed by the governing authority of the county. Where the county seat is unincorporated, then one member shall be appointed by the governing authority of the largest unincorporated town in the county in lieu of the county seat.
(Code 1985, § 6-1-12; Code 2004, § 32.56; Ord. No. 211, 9-19-1961)
Sec. 2-203. - Term of office.
The term of office for all members shall be five years. Any vacancy in the membership shall be filled for the unexpired term by the governing authority making the appointment which has become vacant. The appointing authority shall also have the power to remove any member it may appoint for cause, on written charges, after public hearing. All members shall serve without compensation.
(Code 1985, § 6-1-13; Code 2004, § 32.57; Ord. No. 211, 9-19-1961)
Sec. 2-204. - Organization, rules, staff and finances.
The commission shall elect a chairperson and vice-chairperson from among its appointive members. The term of office of the chairperson and vice-chairperson shall be for one year or until re-elected or their successors are elected. The commission shall appoint a secretary who may be an employee of the commission. The commission shall meet at least once each month at the call of the chairperson and at the other times as the chairperson or board may determine, shall adopt rules for the transaction of business and shall keep a record of its resolutions, transactions, findings, and determinations, which record shall be a public record. The commission may appoint the employees and staff as it may deem necessary for its work. In the performance of its duties, the planning commission may cooperate with, contract with, or accept funds from federal, state, or local public or semi-public agencies or private individuals or corporations, may spend the funds, and may carry out the cooperative undertakings and contracts. It may make expenditures of the commission, exclusive of gifts to the commission and contract receipts, which shall be within the amounts appropriated for the purpose by the participating governing authorities.
(Code 1985, § 6-1-14; Code 2004, § 32.58; Ord. No. 211, 9-19-1961)
Sec. 2-205. - Powers and duties.
It shall be the function and duty of the commission to make the careful and comprehensive surveys and studies of existing conditions and probable future developments and to prepare the plans for physical, social, and economic growth as will best promote the public health, safety, morals, convenience, prosperity, or the general welfare as well as efficiency and economy in the development of the entire Central Savannah River Area and each political subdivision therein. In general, the commission shall have the powers and duties and responsibilities as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 50-8-30 et seq.
(Code 1985, § 6-1-15; Code 2004, § 32.59)

ARTICLE V. - MUNICIPAL COURT

Sec. 2-270. - Sessions.
The municipal court of the city shall convene monthly at a time and date determined by the chief judge of municipal court. However, the addition of permanent scheduled court dates occurring monthly shall be authorized by the mayor and city council.
(Code 1985, § 2-3-2; Ord. No. 257, 9-8-1980; Am. Ord. No. 300, 3-14-1988; Am. Ord. No. 328, 12-14-1994; Ord. No. 1813, § 34.01, 7-23-2018)
Sec. 2-271. - Terms and compensation of municipal court chief and deputy judges.
(a)
The term of judges shall be for a period of four years, and shall expire upon the judge being removed from office by the mayor and city council or until another judge is appointed at the end of the current judge's term.
(b)
The compensation for the chief judge of the municipal court and the deputy judge shall be at a rate determined from time to time by the mayor and city council.
(Code 1985, § 2-3-1; Ord. No. 257, 9-8-1980; Am. Ord. No. 300, 3-14-1988; Am. Ord. No. 328, 12-14-1994; Ord. No. 1813, § 34.02, 7-23-2018)
Sec. 2-272. - Service of summons.
(a)
Any person charged with violating any city ordinance shall receive notice by service of a summons as herein provided. Such summons may be issued by any city law enforcement officer, code enforcement officer, or other city official designated by the city manager or judge. The summons shall be directed to the accused and shall distinctly state the offense charged, and the day, hour, and place of trial, requiring the accused to appear before the judge of the municipal court to answer the accusation made. Service of the summons shall be made by any city law enforcement officer, code enforcement officer, or other city official when so designated by the city manager or judge, either by serving the accused personally or by leaving a copy at his dwelling, house, or usual place of abode with some person of suitable age and discretion therein, or by delivering a copy of the summons and complaint to an agent designated by appointment or by law to receive service of process.
(b)
In the case of a summons issued for violation of laws or ordinances relating to the parking of motor vehicles, such summons may be directed to an unknown person as owner of an automobile designated in the summons and may be served upon such person by leaving a copy in or attached to such automobile.
(Ord. No. 1813, § 34.03, 7-23-2018)
Sec. 2-273. - Failure to obey summons or subpoena.
Any person who fails to appear at the time and place set out in any summons or subpoena served upon him shall be guilty of contempt of court and upon conviction thereof shall be punished for same.
(Ord. No. 1813, § 34.04, 7-23-2018)
Sec. 2-274. - Fines.
Any person against whom a fine is imposed by the municipal court of the city, for the violation of any of the laws or ordinances of the city or the laws of the state, shall be subject to an additional fee in the amount, as established by the mayor and city council and provided in the city fee schedule, per violation to be used for the enhancement of the information technology of the public safety department and other court costs, as established by the mayor and city council and provided in the city fee schedule, is authorized by council and may be imposed by a judge in the event of a conviction.
(Ord. No. 380, § 34.05, 2-13-2006)
Sec. 2-275. - Municipal court solicitor.
The office of solicitor of the municipal court is hereby established for the purpose of providing representation of the city in matters pertaining to ordinance violations of the city and state offenses enforceable in the municipal court as allowed by state law. The prosecuting attorney for the municipal court shall be a part-time contract position.
(1)
Any person appointed as the solicitor for the municipal court of the city shall be a member in good standing of the state bar and admitted to practice before the trial and appellate courts of this state. Nothing in this section shall prevent the city from appointing the city attorney to be the solicitor for the municipal court, so long as all other requirements under the law are met.
(2)
The city manager shall select and appoint on behalf of the city a qualified attorney to serve as solicitor in the municipal court of the city. The duties of the solicitor are:
a.
To attend each session of the municipal court unless excused by the judge thereof and to remain until the business of the city is disposed of;
b.
To aid the municipal court judge in organizing the court as he may require;

ARTICLE VII. - CODE OF ETHICS

Sec. 2-345. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Appointee means any person appointed to a city board, committee or commission by the city council or mayor.
City staff means any person who is a full-time or part-time employee of the city, as well as any full-time or part-time employee of any independent contractor contracted to perform specific duties for, and on behalf of the city.
Family member means the spouse, mother, father, brother, sister, son or daughter of a city councilmember or an appointee or the mother-in-law, father-in-law, brother-in-law, sister-in-law, son-in-law or daughter-in-law of a city councilmember or an appointee.
Financial interest means all direct ownership interests of the total assets or capital stock of a business entity where such ownership interest is ten percent or more.
Member means and includes the mayor or any councilmember of the city and any person appointed to any board or commission of the city.
Substantial interest means the following:
(1)
Funds received by the member from the other person or entity during the previous 12 months either equal or exceed:
a.
$5,000.00 in salary, bonuses, commissions or professional fees, or $5,000.00 in payment for goods, products or services; or
b.
Ten percent of the recipient's gross income during that period, whichever is less; or
(2)
The member is a creditor, debtor, or guarantor of the other person or entity in an amount of $5,000.00 or more.
(Code 2004, § 30.20; Ord. No. 1711, 8-28-2017)
Sec. 2-346. - Prohibitions.
All members shall meet the following standards:
(1)
Compliance with the law. Members shall comply with all laws of the United States, the state, and the city in the performance of their public duties. These laws include, but are not limited to: the United States and Georgia Constitutions; laws pertaining to conflicts of interest, elections, campaigns, financial disclosures, employer responsibilities, and open processes of government; and city ordinances and written policies.
(2)
Policy role of members. Members shall respect and adhere to the structure of government of the city as outlined in the city's charter and policies and procedures. In this structure, the city council determines the policies of the city with the advice, information and analysis provided by the public, boards, committees, commissions, and city staff.
(3)
Acceptance of gifts, benefits or remuneration.
a.
Members shall not solicit or accept directly or indirectly anything of value from any person, corporation, or group which:
1.
Has, or is seeking to obtain, contractual or other business or financial relationships with the city, unless: a member's contractual relationship with such person, corporation, or group existed prior to the city's contractual relationship or prior to the member's election or appointment to office; the member, if in office at the time the contractual, business or financial relationship came before the city for consideration, disclosed such relationship or, if not in office at such time, has immediately disclosed the relationship to the city after being elected or appointed to office and becoming aware of the city's relationship with such person, corporation or group; the member abstained from discussion of the city's consideration of entering a contract with such person, corporation, or group or competing vendor; the member abstained from voting on any matter related to the relationship between such person, corporation, or group or the subject contract or services; and the member did not make personal use of any official non-public information, as prohibited by subsection (9) of this section;
2.
In exchange for the thing of value, seeks to have a member exercise a matter of discretion in his favor; or
3.
In exchange for the thing of value, seeks to have interests which may be affected by the performance or nonperformance of the official duty of the member.
b.
Members shall not directly or indirectly request, exact, receive, or agree to receive a gift, loan, favor, promise, benefit or thing of value for himself or another person if:
1.
It could reasonably be considered to influence the member in the future, and the member is involved in any official act or action which results in a pecuniary benefit for the donor or lender which is not available to the public at large; or
2.
It could reasonably be considered to influence, benefit or reward the member, and the member recently has been, or is now or within six months in the future, involved in any official act or action which results in a pecuniary benefit for the donor or lender which is not available to the public at large.
c.
The above prohibitions shall not apply in the case of:
1.
Occasional non-pecuniary receipt of trinkets or gifts with a value of less than $100.00 or admission to and/or consumption of food and beverages at a breakfast, lunch, dinner, function or other event;
2.
Award publicly presented in recognition of public service;
3.
Transaction authorized by and performed in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 16-10-6 as now or hereafter amended;
4.
A commercially reasonable loan or other financial transaction made in the ordinary course of business by an institution or individual authorized by the laws of Georgia to engage in the making of such loan or financial transaction;
5.
Campaign contributions made and reported in accordance with state law; or
6.
Any gift, loan, favor, promise or thing of value from a family member.
(4)
Conflict of interest.
a.
A member may not participate in a vote or decision on a matter affecting an immediate family member or any person, entity, or property in which the member has a substantial interest.
b.
A member who serves as a corporate officer or member of the board of directors of a nonprofit entity must disclose their interest in said entity to the mayor and city council prior to participating in a vote or decision regarding funding of the entity by or through the city.
c.
Where the interest of a member in the subject matter of a vote or decision is remote or incidental, the member may participate in the vote or decision and need not disclose the interest.
(5)
Use of public property. A member shall not use city property of any kind for other than officially approved activities, nor shall he direct city staff to use such property for these purposes.
(6)
Coercion by members. A member shall not use his position in any way to coerce, or give the appearance of coercing, another person to provide any financial benefit to him or a family member, or those with whom a member has a financial interest.
(7)
Unauthorized use of city staff. A member shall not use his superior position to unduly pressure or request or otherwise require a member of the city staff to:
a.
Do clerical work on behalf of a family member, business, social, church or fraternal interest;
b.
Purchase goods and services to be used for personal, business or political purposes; and
c.
Work for him personally without offering him just compensation.
(8)
Restrictions on contracts with former members. The city shall not enter into any contract with any person or business represented by such person, who has been within the preceding 12-month period a member, unless the contract is awarded by a competitive bid or a committee selection process.
(9)
Improper use of official non-public information. Members shall not directly or indirectly make use of, or permit others to make use of, official information, which at the time of its disclosure is not subject to being made available to the general public, for the purpose of furthering a private interest regardless of whether the private interest belongs to the member or a third party.
(10)
Unauthorized attempts to bind the city. Members shall not order any goods and services for the city without prior official authorization for such an expenditure, nor shall members attempt to obligate the city nor give the impression of obligating the city without proper prior authorization to purchase or otherwise be liable for any goods, services or property.
(11)
Improper influence in city judicial matters. No member shall attempt to unduly influence the outcome of a case before the municipal court of the city.
(Code 2004, § 30.21; Ord. No. 1711, 8-28-2017)
Sec. 2-347. - Receipt of complaints; service; response.
(a)
All complaints against members shall be filed with the city manager; provided, however, to discourage the filing of ethics complaints solely for political purposes, complaints will not be accepted against a person seeking election as a member, whether currently serving as a member or not, from the date qualifying opens for the elected office at issue through the date the election results for that office are certified. The time for filing complaints will not run during this period. Properly filed complaints will be accepted and processed after the election results have been certified.
(b)
No action may be taken on any complaint which is filed later than one year after a violation of this code of ethics is alleged to have occurred, and a complaint alleging a violation must be filed within six months from the date the complainant knew or should have known of the action alleged to be a violation; such limitation periods to be measured from the date of the last act occurring in furtherance of such violation. No proceedings under this article shall be instituted or prosecuted after the earlier of:
(1)
The expiration of the term of office of the person complained against; or
(2)
The resignation, death, vacancy, disqualification or withdrawal from office of the person against whom a complaint is filed.
(c)
No action may be taken on any complaint which arises out of substantially the same facts or circumstances which have previously served as the basis for a complaint pursuant to this article.
(d)
Separate complaints.

A separate complaint shall be filed for each person alleged to have engaged in any activity violating this article even if the allegations arise from the same factual basis. Each complaint shall state:

b.
The specific section of state law, the city Charter, or this code of ethics alleged to be violated for each count;
c.
With specificity, the facts which are alleged to constitute the violation; and
d.
The documentary evidence which the charging party possesses.
(2)
Copies of said documentary evidence shall be attached to the complaint as exhibits.

Upon receipt of a complaint, the city manager will deliver a copy of the complaint to the city manager.
(g)
Original pleadings shall be filed with the city manager and the city manager shall cause the complaint to be served on the member charged as soon as practicable but in no event later than seven calendar days after receipt of a verified complaint. Service may be by personal service, by e-mail, by certified mail, return receipt requested or by statutory overnight delivery.

(Code 2004, § 30.22; Ord. No. 1711, 8-28-2017)
Sec. 2-348. - Appointment of arbiter; burden of proof.
(a)
Upon receipt of complaint, the city manager or his designee, shall transmit the same to the city attorney, who shall review the complaint to ensure compliance with the procedure for filing established by this code of ethics and to determine whether the allegations therein constitute a statement upon which action under this article may be granted.
(b)
In the event the city attorney finds the complainant has alleged a prima facie case for relief under this article, the city attorney shall appoint an arbiter who:
(1)
Shall be a competent attorney-at-law of good standing in his profession;
(2)
Shall have at least five years' experience in the practice of law; and
(3)
Shall not maintain an office within a ten-mile radius of the city.
(c)
Once an arbiter is appointed, no member shall communicate with or otherwise contact the arbiter, except as authorized herein, unless such member is the complainant or the member charged in the complaint; however, no party to a complaint shall engage in ex parte communications with the arbiter.
(d)
Within 15 days of receipt of a complaint, the arbiter shall make an initial investigation into the complaint and may contact the complainant and member as deemed necessary. Upon conclusion of his investigation, the arbiter may dismiss the complaint for lack of cause or may call for appointment of a committee of two councilmembers and the city attorney to further investigate the claim. The councilmembers comprising the committee shall be appointed by the mayor, or the mayor pro tem in the event the complaint is filed against the mayor.
(e)
In all proceedings under this article, the burden of proof shall be on the complaining party. Further, the quantum of proof required to establish a violation under this article shall be beyond a reasonable doubt.
(Code 2004, § 30.23; Ord. No. 1711, 8-28-2017)
Sec. 2-349. - Investigation and hearing by committee.
(a)
The committee shall determine whether the complaint against a member sets forth sufficient facts and circumstances so as to warrant a hearing. The committee shall have 60 days to complete the investigation. Any complaint deemed non-frivolous by the committee shall warrant a hearing. If the committee determines a hearing is warranted, said hearing shall be held within 60 days of the conclusion of the investigation. In the event the complaint does not set forth sufficient facts to constitute an alleged violation and is found unjustified, frivolous or patently unfounded, it shall be dismissed and the complainant notified immediately.
(b)
Upon a determination that the complaint should not be dismissed, the committee shall be empowered to collect evidence and information concerning any complaint and to add the findings and results of its investigations to the file containing such complaint. In furtherance of this investigation, the committee may:
(1)
First, seek such further information from the complainant or the member charged through inquiry or written questions, provided, however the member charged shall have no obligation to answer any inquiries. If it is determined that the complaint should not be dismissed pursuant to this division, then the provisions below shall apply;
(2)
Conduct a hearing regarding the allegations set forth in the complaint. At any hearing, the member who is the subject of inquiry shall have the right:
a.
To representation by counsel at all stages of these proceedings;
b.
To written notice of the hearing at least ten calendar days before the first hearing;
c.
To hear and examine the evidence and witnesses;
d.
To not testify; and
e.
To submit evidence and call witnesses to oppose or mitigate the allegations. In all hearings held under this section, the rules of evidence applicable in civil cases shall apply.
(c)
All investigations under this section shall be completed within 60 business days of the filing of the complaint. Should the investigation not be completed in said period, the complaint will be deemed dismissed as a failure to state facts sufficient to invoke the disciplinary jurisdiction of the city council. Within seven business days of the completion of the investigation, the committee shall make a recommendation to the city council to:
(1)
Dismiss the complaint on the grounds that it is unjustified, frivolous, patently unfounded, or that it fails to state facts sufficient to invoke the disciplinary jurisdiction of the city council; or
(2)
Prepare a report of findings and recommendations to the mayor and city council.
(d)
Should the committee determine to submit a report in the matter, the report shall consist of:
(1)
A written finding of facts;
(2)
A determination that the complaint establishes beyond a reasonable doubt that a violation has been committed, and if so, the specific violation and evidence supporting the same; and
(3)
A recommendation regarding the punishment for such violation.
(e)
Any person violating any provision of this article is subject to:
(1)
Public or private reprimand or censure by the city council; or
(2)
Request for resignation by the city council.
(f)
The committee's written determination of findings and recommendations shall be delivered to the city manager who shall provide a copy to the city manager and the mayor and city council and serve a copy on the complainant and member charged by personal service, by e-mail, by certified mail, return receipt requested or by statutory overnight delivery. Such findings shall not be final until approved by vote of the city council, as provided in section 2-350(a).
(Code 2004, § 30.24; Ord. No. 1711, 8-28-2017)
Sec. 2-350. - Report to mayor and city council.
(a)
Upon receipt of findings and recommendations from the committee, the mayor and city council may:
(1)
By simple majority accept the findings and recommendations of the committee;
(2)
By simple majority accept the findings of fact and reject the recommended discipline, instead substituting its own discipline; or
(3)
By a supermajority consisting of a majority of those present forming a quorum, plus one, reject the findings and recommendations and either: dismiss the complaint, or conduct its own hearing in accordance with section 2-349(b)(2). Upon the completion of such hearing, the findings and recommendations of the mayor and city council shall be binding.
(b)
If the subject of the complaint is the mayor or any city councilmember, he will not be allowed to vote pursuant to this section, serve on the committee, or participate in any hearing held pursuant to this section other than as set forth and allowable by the member charged, nor shall such position be counted for the purposes of establishing a quorum.
(c)
Upon a final judgment and certification of the minutes of the meeting disposing of the matter, the city manager shall serve the respondent with a copy of the certified minutes and findings and recommendations by personal service, e-mail, certified mail (return receipt requested) or by Federal Express or other overnight delivery service.
(Code 2004, § 30.25; Ord. No. 1711, 8-28-2017)
Sec. 2-351. - Right to appeal.
(a)
Any member or complainant adversely affected by the findings or recommendations of the city council may obtain judicial review of such decision as provided in this section.
(b)
An action for judicial review may be commenced by filing an application for a writ of certiorari in the superior court of the county within 30 business days after the final action on a complaint pursuant to this article.
(Code 2004, § 30.26; Ord. No. 1711, 8-28-2017)

The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Bid, quote or proposal most advantageous to the city means a bid, quote, or proposal chosen on the basis of a variety of considerations including price, quality of merchandise, suitability of merchandise, and service reputation of the vendor, and therefore may not necessarily mean the lowest bid received.
Field purchases means an informal purchase of supplies needed urgently for an ongoing or in-process city operation in an amount less than $200.00.
Formal bid or proposal process means a process whereby the city develops a set of specifications that is made available to potential vendors through the city website and the state local government access website.
Informal quotes, bids or proposals means quotes, bids or proposals obtained by contacting experienced reputable vendors directly. Informal quotes, bids or proposals may be either oral or written depending upon the purchase amount.

(b)
The city council must approve and authorize the purchase of any supplies or services which are not a part of or exceed the annual operating budget of the city or a specified department's operating budget.

(a)
The following are exceptions to the normal quote, bid, and proposal requirements although the appropriate approvals must still be obtained as outlined in § 2-379(b) above:

Once a contract is bid and awarded by city officials in accordance with this article, the city reserves the right to further negotiate all terms of the contract if the city determines that it is in the city's best interest to do so without the necessity of rebidding any contract; provided, however, that any negotiations permitted hereunder shall not result in a variance of price terms exceeding 20 percent of the original price.
(Code 2004, § 36.56; Ord. No. 484, 9-28-2015)
Sec. 2-383. - Procedure for informal bidding.
(a)
When informal quotes, bids or proposals are a permitted method of procurement, department heads or their designee may procure goods and services by contacting vendors directly.

The number of quotes, bids or proposals required is governed by the purchase amount as outlined in section 2-379(b).
(c)
The city reserves the right to determine whether quotes, bids or proposals are the appropriate means of procuring a good or service.
(d)
In all cases, the quote, bid or proposal most advantageous to the city shall be selected.
(e)
The city reserves the right to reject any or all bids and to waive technicalities.
(Code 2004, § 36.57; Ord. No. 484, 9-28-2015)
Sec. 2-384. - Procedure for formal bidding.
(a)
When formal bids or quotes are the required method of procurement, the city manager or his designee shall prepare an invitation for bids or proposals that clearly describes the needed goods or services and specifies the date and time by which the bid or proposal must be submitted.

The invitation for bids or proposals shall be publicized through the city's website and through the state local government access website for a minimum of ten calendar days. At the discretion of the department head or city manager, the invitation may also be sent directly to known interested vendors.
(c)
Unless specified in the bid or proposal invitation, a formal bid or proposal opening is not required.
(d)
The city reserves the right to determine whether bids or proposals are the appropriate means of procuring a good or service.
(e)
In all cases, the quote, bid or proposal most advantageous to the city shall be selected.
(f)
The city reserves the right to reject any or all bids or proposals and to waive technicalities.
(Code 2004, § 36.58; Ord. No. 484, 9-28-2015)
Sec. 2-385. - Administration of competitive bids or proposals.
(a)
The city shall keep a record of all bids, quotes or proposals submitted as required by applicable open records laws and the records shall be opened to proper inspection by any person.
(b)
Tied quotes, bids or proposals shall be resolved by the city manager except that if the price exceeds $25,000.00, the city council shall resolve the tie.
(c)
The city reserves the right to accept or reject any or all bids, to investigate the qualifications of any bidder, and to waive or not waive any and all technicalities or informalities in the bids or proposals when making an award.
(d)
The city retains custody of all quotes, bids and proposals submitted to the city.
(Code 2004, § 36.59; Ord. No. 484, 9-28-2015)
