Chapter 101 - ADMINISTRATIVE AND GENERAL PROVISIONS
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 101-1. - Building, zoning and subdivision fees.
The fees to be paid to the county for building and other permits required by the state construction code, for plat review and approval, and for appeals, certificates, variance requests and amendments (text and map) to the zoning ordinance, shall be as established by resolution. See fees as referenced in Subpart A, Chapter 1, Section 1-17 - Fee Schedule.
(Ord. of 9-2-2003; Ord. No. 2019-04, § 1, 2-5-2019)
Secs. 101-2—101-20. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - COUNTY PLANNING AND ZONING COMMISSION[1]

Editor's note— An ordinance adopted Jan. 7, 2014, in effect repealed Art. II and renumbered former App. A, § 722.06 as §§ 101-21—101-29, as set out herein. The historical notation has been retained with the amended provisions for reference purposes. The former Art. II, §§ 101-21—101-25, pertained to the county planning commission and derived from §§ 13-16—13-19 and 13-21 of the 1974 Code; §§ 1 and 2 of a resolution adopted May 10, 1967; a resolution adopted Nov. 13, 1968; and the minutes of May 11, 1971.

Sec. 101-21. - Creation and membership.
There is hereby established the planning and zoning commission of the county which shall consist of six members appointed by the board of county commissioners, consisting of two representatives from each of the three commission districts. Also, the board of commissioners shall appoint one alternate at large to serve when a member is absent.
(Ord. of 5-5-2009; Ord. of 1-7-2014)
Sec. 101-22. - Compensation for service.
Pay for service on the commission may be authorized by the board of commissioners providing the following conditions are met:
1.
Pay per meeting may be authorized for regular members and the alternate providing the meeting is duly advertised, a quorum is present, official business is conducted, and minutes are duly recorded; and providing the regular member or alternate member attends such meeting.
(Ord. of 5-5-2009; Ord. of 1-7-2014)
Sec. 101-23. - Restrictions.
A regular member or alternate members shall be prohibited from holding any public office, local government position, or be a member of any other county board or committee.
(Ord. of 5-5-2009; Ord. of 1-7-2014)
Sec. 101-24. - Conflict of interest.
A regular member or alternate member shall be prohibited from taking part in the discussion or vote on any matter in which the member has a vested interest or which a relative of the member has a vested interest or which in any way might be viewed as a conflict of interest.
(Ord. of 5-5-2009; Ord. of 1-7-2014)
Sec. 101-25. - Motion, minutes, and voting.
(a)
All motions, seconds, and votes of the commission shall be recorded in the minutes.
(b)
The chairman shall have the right to discuss all matters before the board, but shall vote only in case of a tie, and only the regular members or alternate sitting in place of an absent regular member shall make motions, second motions, and vote.
(c)
The planning and zoning commission shall meet all open meeting and public notice requirements. Not less that four members and/or alternate member shall be present in order to conduct business. If there are three voting members (regular or alternate) present plus the chairman, an action shall only be valid if the three voting members are unanimous in a decision.
(Ord. of 5-5-2009; Ord. of 1-7-2014)
Sec. 101-26. - Removable for cause.
Members shall be removable for cause by the board of county commissioners upon written charges and after a public hearing and full inquiry is conducted; providing however, that any member who fails to attend three meetings within a 12-month period, without cause, may be removed without a public hearing.
(Ord. of 5-5-2009; Ord. of 1-7-2014)
Sec. 101-27. - Appointments; terms of office; and rules of procedure.
(a)
Appointments for vacancies shall be made by the board of commissioners.
(b)
Each member shall serve three-year terms. A member shall serve no more than four consecutive one-year terms as chairperson.
(c)
The planning and zoning commission may adopt by-laws providing such is not in conflict with county policy, procedures or ordinances.
(d)
The planning and zoning commission shall conduct all business matters and public hearings as required by the County Code and state law.
(Ord. of 5-5-2009; Ord. of 12-3-2013; Ord. of 1-7-2014)
Sec. 101-28. - Conflicting policies.
Previous actions or existing policies in conflict with provisions of this amendment are null and void.
(Ord. of 5-5-2009; Ord. of 1-7-2014)
Sec. 101-29. - Amendments.
These procedures may be amended by majority vote of the county commissioners.
(Ord. of 5-5-2009; Ord. of 1-7-2014)
