Sec. 10-19. - Business license tax.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93, there is hereby levied for each year, an annual business license tax upon state and national banking associations, federal savings and loan associations, state building and loan associations the rate of 0.25 percent of the gross receipts of said institutions as defined in this article. Notwithstanding any other provision of this article, the minimum amount of business license tax due from any depository financial institutions pursuant to this article shall be $1,000.00 per year.
(Ord. of 12-28-1983, § 1)
Sec. 10-20. - Gross receipts defined.
For purposes of this article, the term "gross receipts" shall have the meaning specified in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-95(b)(2).
(Ord. of 12-28-1983, § 2)
Sec. 10-21. - Due date; filing of return.
Each depository financial institution within the city shall file a return of its gross receipts with the city measured. Said returns shall be in the manner and in the form prescribed by the commissioner of the department of banking and shall be based upon the allocation method set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(d). The tax levied pursuant to this article shall be assessed and collected based upon the information provided in said return. The due date of taxes levied by this article shall be April 1 of each year.
(Ord. of 12-28-1983, § 3)

ARTICLE III. - SOLICITORS AND CANVASSERS[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— Municipal and county occupation taxes for certain traveling salespersons, merchants, and dealers, O.C.G.A. § 48-5-354.

Sec. 10-46. - What constitutes solicitors and canvassers.
Taking orders for anything whatsoever, for future delivery or shipment, distributing literature, distributing goods or information is considered canvassing or soliciting. If deliveries are made, the license for peddlers will apply as set forth in the city business license code.
(Ord. of 3-22-1982, § 1-1)
Sec. 10-47. - Registration required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in business as a canvasser or solicitor calling at or telephoning residences, offices or businesses without the previous consent of the occupant for the purpose of soliciting orders, sales, subscriptions or business of any kind, or seeking for information or donations without first having registered at the city hall with the city manager or his designated authority. Any organization or individual desiring a permit to do so must submit a written request to the city at least seven days prior to the date of such soliciting, sales or canvassing. Each registrant shall be required to fill out a registration form giving complete identification, signature, name of employer, products sold, manufacturer of products, or name of organization he is representing, and the dates of and method of soliciting.
(Ord. of 3-22-1982, § 1-2)
Sec. 10-48. - Identification.
Upon completion of investigation, a personal identification card will be issued to each individual. This card is not transferable.
(Ord. of 3-22-1982, § 1-4)
Sec. 10-49. - Exemptions.
Nothing in this article shall apply to officers or employees of the city, county, state or federal governments or the school system or any subdivisions thereof, when on official business.
(Ord. of 3-22-1982, § 1-5)
Sec. 10-50. - Revocation.
An identification card or permit issued under this article may be revoked by the city manager or the chief of police or their designated representative because of any violation by the registrant or any one of a group of registrants of these regulations, or of any ordinance of the city, or of any state or federal law, or whenever the registrant shall cease to possess the qualifications and good character required herein for the original registration.
(Ord. of 3-22-1982, § 1-6)
Sec. 10-51. - Soliciting, canvassing or peddling within fire zones.
No person shall be allowed to solicit, canvass, or peddle within the fire zones of the city.
(Ord. of 3-22-1982, § 2-1)
Sec. 10-52. - Limitations.
Nothing herein shall be construed as authorizing the use of any vehicular traveled portion of the public streets for solicitation, canvassing, sales or distributions of any item. All authorized solicitations, canvassing, sale or distribution shall be limited to no more than two individuals within any city block, and no more than two occasions in any calendar year by the same person, firm or organization.
(Ord. of 3-22-1982, § 2-2)
Sec. 10-53. - Prohibitions.
No person, firm or organization shall distribute any pamphlet, brochure, circular, notices or literature within the city unless the same is delivered directly to the person for whom the same is intended. This prohibition shall not apply to any item which is distributed regularly to subscribers or delivered by prearrangement between the parties.
(Ord. of 3-22-1982, § 2-3)
Sec. 10-54. - Penalties.
Persons violating any section of this article shall be issued a citation by a law enforcement officer to appear before the judge of the municipal court and shall be punished according to section 1-14. The provisions of the city Charter are hereby incorporated herein and made a part of said article.
(Ord. of 3-22-1982, § 2-6)

There is hereby levied an annual license fee upon each insurer doing business within the city in an amount as established by the city council from time to time. There is hereby levied an annual license fee upon independent agencies and brokers for each separate business location from which an insurance business is conducted and which is not subject to the company license fee imposed by section 10-82 hereof. Sec. 10-85. - Gross premiums tax imposed on life insurers.
There is hereby levied for each year an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer writing life, accident and sickness insurance within the city in an amount equal to one percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.1. The term "gross direct premiums," as used in this section, means gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 10-82.
(Ord. of 12-3-1990(1), § 4)
Sec. 10-86. - Gross premiums tax imposed on all other insurers.
There is hereby levied an annual tax upon each insurer, other than an insurer transacting business in the class of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5(1), doing business within the city in an amount equal to 2.5 percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2. ARTICLE V. - VEHICLES FOR HIRE

Sec. 10-118. - Conditions prerequisite to furnishing wrecker/towing service at the request of the city.
No person engaged in the wrecker/towing service business within the city shall be called upon by city police officers or other authorized employees of the city to furnish the service of towing any vehicle upon the city streets unless the person's firm or corporation shall have been placed on the approved scheduled rotation call list after having complied with the following conditions:
(1)
Complete in full an on-call service wrecker information/registration form.
(2)
Possess a valid/current city business license.
(3)
Possess a valid state public service commission certificate and meet all Federal Motor Carrier Safety Regulations and other applicable state public service requirements for operating a wrecker/towing service.
(4)
Have an established business location, as established in section 10-125, within the city limits.
(5)
Be available 24 hours a day, seven days per week to provide service, and respond within 20 minutes on at least 90 percent of calls made by the city police department as determined by response times recorded by dispatch and the department.
(6)
The wrecker/towing service shall have qualified employees, skilled in performing their duties and possessing a valid state driver's license available at all times to avoid delay.
(7)
The wrecker/towing service shall have available at least two wreckers in good operating condition, meeting all requirements of local rules/regulations, local ordinances, Federal Motor Carrier Safety Regulations, state law and the state public service commission.
(8)
All wreckers shall be equipped with an amber flashing light meeting the most current regulatory requirements of the state patrol, and also sufficient red flares, lanterns or warning lights and reflectors (as required by 49 CFR 393.95 of the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Regulations) so as to be able to mark the scene of a wreck on both sides of the same, and so as to warn all traffic on the highway of the emergency existing while the wreckers are in operation. The wreckers shall also carry brooms, shovels and fire extinguishers, suitable for extinguishing fires in wrecked vehicles and for cleaning up the debris and glass from the area where the wreck has occurred. The fire extinguisher shall meet the requirements of 49 CFR 393.95 of the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Regulations.
(9)
There shall be displayed in a conspicuous place, on both the right and left side of a wrecker, a sign showing the name of the company, or the owner and operator of the wrecker.
(10)
All persons, corporations and firms engaged in the wrecker/towing business shall carry insurance, including liability insurance in the form of comprehensive, general and automobile liability, and the policy shall protect it from claims for bodily injury, including death, to its employees and all others, and from claims for property damage, all of which may arise out of the result of the wrecker's operation under this article. This insurance shall be sufficient to reasonably assure the contractor's solvency, in the event of any injuries, deaths or property damage, with the following minimum limits:
a.
Bodily injury: A minimum of $100,000.00 per person; $300,000.00 per accident.
b.
Property damage: A minimum of $50,000.00.
(11)
The wrecker/towing service shall provide a storage area located with the city limits for the storage and safekeeping of vehicles towed under this article and enclosed with a solid wall or substantial wire fence not less than six feet in height. Said storage area shall be staffed weekly during regular business hours. Garage liability insurance in the same amounts as required in subsection (10) of this section shall be required.
(12)
All wrecker/towing services shall agree to permit regular inspection of wrecker/towing vehicles and storage premises by representatives of the city police department. These inspections may be conducted by the state public service commission.
(Ord. of 7-6-1993, § I)
Sec. 10-119. - Responsibility of wrecker/towing firms.
(a)
Wrecker/towing service shall be responsible to the owner for all damage done during towing and storage of any vehicles entrusted to them under this article.
(b)
Wrecker/towing service shall cooperate with all duly authorized law enforcement officers in the protection of any evidence or in any investigation arising out of a wreck or seizure of a vehicle by any officials.
(c)
It shall be the responsibility of the wrecker/towing service, prior to leaving the scene of any collision, to clean the pavement of all debris of the collision, including all glass.
(d)
The wrecker/towing service shall not release any vehicle placed on hold from storage unless approved by an authorized representative of the police department with the issuance of a signed release form.
(e)
The wrecker/towing service shall maintain wrecker units and all related equipment adequately to perform towing service in a reasonably workmanlike manner.
(Ord. of 7-6-1993, § II)
Sec. 10-120. - Fees for services rendered.
Fees for services rendered under this article shall be established by the respective wrecker/towing service. A listing of such fees shall be clearly posted at the garage/storage site and a written copy available for distribution on each wrecker unit.
(Ord. of 7-6-1993, § III)
Sec. 10-121. - Procedures for city police officers and employees.
(a)
A schedule shall be set up on a two-week rotating basis of all qualified wrecker/towing services under this article, which service shall be used as hereinafter provided except in extreme emergency, where persons are trapped or pinned in or under vehicles, or in burning vehicles, or in danger of drowning by submersion, or where traffic is blocked to prevent the normal flow of vehicles, in which event, and only in which event, said police officer shall be authorized to call the nearest, most qualified service, to prevent serious injury or death.
(b)
The police officer or lawfully authorized officer shall determine whether or not a wrecker is needed, and if a wrecker is needed, shall ascertain if the owner or operator is in condition to make a preference known, what wrecker/towing service the owner or operator desires called, and shall have dispatch call the preferred service desired by the owner or operator of the vehicle. No police or other authorized officer when on official duty shall recommend to any person the wrecker/towing service company to be called or employed.
(c)
In the event the owner or operator is unable, due to injury or due to physical or mental impairment, to make a choice, or does not have any preference dispatch shall call the wrecker/towing service on scheduled call.
(d)
In the event the dispatcher is unable to reach or if for any reason the wrecker/towing service on call is unable to furnish the service, then the backup wrecker/towing service, which shall be the service next on call, shall be called, to provide the needed wrecker/towing service.
(e)
Except in extreme emergencies, the police officer or other authorized officer shall await the arrival of the wrecker and shall in the meantime try to make the scene of the accident safe for other vehicles, using the road in the vicinity thereof, giving warnings and directing traffic.
(f)
The police officer or other authorized officer shall make a tow-in report, which shall contain the information and details prescribed by the city.
(g)
If an officer requests that a vehicle be towed to the police department, the wrecker operator shall have the shift personnel sign the tow-in bill. At this point, the shift personnel or a designee will assume the responsibility for said vehicle and indicate where said vehicle is to be placed.
(h)
Additional wrecker/towing services meeting the requirements of these provisions may be added to the rotation list. The first scheduled on call rotation for any additional service shall occur after all services currently on the list have completed one regular two-week rotation.
(Ord. of 7-6-1993, § IV)
Sec. 10-122. - Wreckers while in use designated as authorized emergency vehicles.
All wreckers used by a wrecker/towing service company, when used in an emergency while carrying on the business of the wrecker company, are hereby declared to be authorized emergency vehicles under purview of applicable laws of the state.
(Ord. of 7-6-1993, § V)
Sec. 10-123. - Suspension removal from scheduled rotation call list.
Upon written notice and the opportunity for a hearing, the mayor and city council may suspend or remove any wrecker/towing service from the scheduled rotation call list for continued violation of this article, local ordinances, state law and state public service commission regulations, or other just causes affecting the health and safety of the general public.
(Ord. of 7-6-1993, § VI)
Sec. 10-124. - Continuation of present services.
This article requiring all wrecker/towing services on the call list to maintain a place of business in the city and a storage area within the city are waived as to all such services who held a valid business license in the city on July 7, 1993, for so long as they shall continue to be a licensed wrecker/towing service of the city until January 1, 1994, and thereafter all temporary provisions of this article shall thereafter apply. Any transfer or change of ownership of such service shall terminate this waiver.
(Ord. of 7-6-1993, § VII)
Sec. 10-125. - Requirements for an established business location.
The city shall require the following of each wrecker/towing service eligible for consideration to provide service at the request of the city:
(1)
Each service must have a separate garage/storage area located within the city limits. The garage/storage area cannot be shared with any other service on the approved scheduled call list and shall be staffed as provided in section 10-118(11).
(2)
Each service must have a separate telephone listing, with 24-hour coverage.
(3)
Each service must have a separate valid city business license issued in the name of the service.
(4)
Wrecker/towing units and equipment required under these guidelines must be assigned and allocated to a specific service. Wreckers/towing units may not be assigned or allocated to more than one service or shared between or among services for purposes of meeting these guidelines.
(5)
Each service must have separate insurance coverage issued in the name of the service and meeting all limits required herein.
(6)
Each service must have separate public service commission certification, as required by law or regulation.
(Ord. of 7-6-1993, § VIII)
Secs. 10-126—10-148. - Reserved.
ARTICLE VI. - GAME ROOMS

Sec. 10-149. - Purpose.
The purpose of this article is to provide for the regulation of businesses which include game rooms to ensure the health, safety, welfare and moral character of the city.
(Prior Code, app. D, art. 16(16.1))
Sec. 10-150. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Bona fide coin-operated amusement machine.
(1)
The term "bona fide coin-operated amusement machine" means a machine of any kind or character used by the public to provide amusement or entertainment whose operation requires the payment of or the insertion of a coin, bill, other money, token, ticket, card, or similar object and the result of whose operation depends in whole or in part upon the skill of the player, whether or not it affords an award to a successful player pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(b)—(g), and which can be legally shipped interstate according to federal law. Examples of bona fide coin-operated amusement machines include, but are expressly not limited to, the following:
a.
Pinball machines;
b.
Console machines;
c.
Video games;
d.
Crane machines;
e.
Claw machines;
f.
Pusher machines;
g.
Bowling machines;
h.
Novelty arcade games;
i.
Foosball or table soccer machines;
j.
Miniature racetrack, football, or golf machines;
k.
Target or shooting gallery machines;
l.
Basketball machines;
m.
Shuffleboard games;
n.
Kiddie ride games;
o.
Skeeball machines;
p.
Air hockey machines;
q.
Roll down machines;
r.
Trivia machines;
s.
Laser games;
t.
Simulator games;
u.
Virtual reality machines;
v.
Maze games;
w.
Racing games;
x.
Coin-operated pool tables or coin-operated billiard tables as defined in O.C.G.A. § 43-8-1(3); and
y.
Any other similar amusement machine which can be legally operated in the state.
(2)
The term "bona fide coin-operated amusement machine" also means a machine of any kind or character used by the public to provide music whose operation requires the payment of or the insertion of a coin, bill, other money, token, ticket, card, or similar object such as jukeboxes or other similar types of music machines. The term "bona fide coin-operated amusement machine" does not include the following:
a.
Coin-operated washing machines or dryers;
b.
Vending machines which for payment of money dispense products or services;
c.
Gas and electric meters;
d.
Pay telephones;
e.
Pay toilets;
f.
Cigarette vending machines;
g.
Coin-operated scales;
h.
Coin-operated gumball machines;
i.
Coin-operated parking meters;
j.
Coin-operated television sets which provide cable or network programming;
k.
Coin-operated massage beds; and
l.
Machines which are not legally permitted to be operated in the state.
Class A machine means a bona fide coin-operated amusement machine that is not a Class B machine, does not allow a successful player to carry over points won on one play to a subsequent play, and:
(1)
Provides no reward to a successful player;
(2)
Rewards a successful player only with free replays or additional time to play;
(3)
Rewards a successful player with noncash merchandise, prizes, toys, gift certificates, or novelties in compliance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(c) or (d)(1), and does not reward a successful player with any item prohibited as a reward in O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(i) or any reward redeemable as an item prohibited as a reward in O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(i);
(4)
Rewards a successful player with points, tokens, tickets, or other evidence of winnings that may be exchanged only for items listed in subsection (3) of this definition; or
(5)
Rewards a successful player with any combination of items listed in subsections (2) through (4) of this definition.
Class B machine means a bona fide coin-operated amusement machine that allows a successful player to accrue points on the machine and carry over points won on one play to a subsequent play in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(d)(2) and:
(1)
Rewards a successful player in compliance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(d)(1) and (2); and
(2)
Does not reward a successful player with any item prohibited as a reward in O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(i) or any reward redeemable as an item prohibited as a reward in O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(i).
Game room means any establishment which operates solely on the proceeds from any amusement machine or device operated by means of insertion of a coin, token, or similar object for the purpose of amusement or skill and for playing where a fee is charged.
Mechanical amusement device means a machine, which, upon the insertion of a coin, trade token, debit card or slug, operates or may be operated as a game of contest or skill or amusement of any kind or description and which contains no automatic pay-off device for the return of money, trade tokens or slugs, and which makes no provision whatever for the return of money. The term "mechanical amusement device" also means any machine, apparatus or contrivance which is used or may be used as a game with skill and amusement wherein or whereby the player initiates, employs or directs any force generated by the machine.
(Prior Code, app. D, art. 16(16.2))
Sec. 10-151. - License required.
No person, firm or corporation shall engage in the business of an operator or proprietor of coin-operated amusement devices, as the term is defined in this article, without first having obtained the proper license. For the purposes of this article, any person, firm, partnership or corporation having four or more devices shall be defined as engaging in the business of operating a game room and shall be required to obtain proper license for the operation of such business and be governed under the provisions of this article.
(Prior Code, app. D, art. 16(16.3))
Sec. 10-152. - Application for license, nontransferable.
(a)
Application for a license in accordance with this article shall be filed in writing with the city clerk, on a form to be provided by the city and shall specify the following:
(1)
The name and address of the applicant, and if a firm, corporation, partnership or association, the principal officers and their addresses.
(2)
The address of the premises where the licensed device is to be operated.
(3)
The trade name and general description of the device to be licensed, the number of devices to be placed in the business and whether the devices will be the primary or secondary service of the business.
(4)
The name and address of the operator of the business, if other than the proprietor.
(b)
A license fee shall accompany such application for license as set forth in fee schedule posted in the city hall.
(c)
The license application shall be investigated as to accuracy and any background checks as necessary are prepared in writing by the code enforcement officer with the assistance of any city agency as is required.
(d)
Approved applications which have paid the license fee shall be issued a license by the city clerk, after approval of the code enforcement officer.
(e)
The approved license shall be posted in a conspicuous place in the licensed business.
(f)
Applicants which are denied shall be notified by mail within 30 days after the conclusion of the appropriate investigation. Any license fee paid shall be used for expenses and not returned to the applicant.
(g)
Approved licenses shall be non-transferable.
(h)
Approved licenses shall expire on January 1 of each year.
(Prior Code, app. D, art. 16(16.4))
Sec. 10-153. - Increasing the number of devices.
In case a licensed proprietor under the provisions of this article desires to increase the number of devices used, played or exhibited for use or play, there shall be an additional application required to be filed with the city clerk. An additional fee shall be assessed according to the schedule posted in the city hall.
(Prior Code, app. D, art. 16(16.5))
Sec. 10-154. - Regulations of coin-operated amusement devices.
(a)
No coin-operated amusement device shall be operated in unsanitary conditions. Periodic code enforcement checks shall be made to determine the sanitary conditions of the establishment.
(b)
It shall be the responsibility of the licensee to see the premises licensed do not become overcrowded in violation of the county occupancy regulations.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person, firm, partnership or corporation engaged solely in the business of operating a game room to sell, offer for sale, knowingly permit to sold, offered for sale, dispensed, consumed or brought on the licensed premises any alcoholic beverage or illegal drugs.
(d)
Any violation of the Criminal Code of Georgia (O.C.G.A. title 16), or of any ordinance pertaining to obscene materials as defined and regulated under state law, shall result in immediate revocation of a licensed issued under this article.
(e)
It shall be a reason for revocation of an approved license for any license holder to offer a reward, prize, or award in the form of free games or money in connection with the use or operation of said coin-operated amusement machines.
(f)
It shall be a reason for revocation of an approved license and the violation of applicable laws for any license holder to allow or permit gambling of any type to occur in connection with the use or operation of said coin-operated amusement machines.
(g)
Licensed holders shall not permit machines to be in operation after 11:00 p.m. on weekdays or after 12:00 midnight on Saturdays and Sundays. No machine shall be in operation prior to 8:00 a.m. Monday through Saturday or prior to 12:01 p.m. on Sundays.
(h)
Violations of these provisions shall be deemed a misdemeanor and punishable according to the city Charter and revocation of license.
(Prior Code, app. D, art. 16(16.6))
Sec. 10-155. - Revocation of license.
The mayor and city council shall have the right to revoke any license issued by the city for the violation of the provisions of this article after a written notice to the licensee of such violation. The licensee may appeal to the city council on the first city council meeting after the receipt of written notice of violation.
(Prior Code, app. D, art. 16(16.7))
Secs. 10-156—10-160. - Reserved.
ARTICLE VII. - FLEA MARKETS[5]

Footnotes:
--- (5) ---
Editor's note— Ord. No. 01-2017 , adopted Oct. 2, 2017, added provisions to the Code, but did not specify manner of inclusion. Therefore, at the discretion of the editor, said provisions have been designated as art. VII, §§ 10-161—10-168, as set out herein.

Sec. 10-161. - Purpose.
The purpose of this article is to preserve the health and safety of the citizens of the city through reasonable regulation. The regulations contained in this article are minimum requirements needed to attempt to rectify identified problems.
(Ord. No. 01-2017 , 10-2-2017)
Sec. 10-162. - Scope.
This article shall apply to all businesses and locations in the city which meet the definition of flea market and all flea market vendors, promoters, owners and operators as defined in this article. This article shall not apply to developed and operating shopping centers, antique shops, antique malls, jewelry stores, coin shops, salvage operations, clothing stores or special sales events not exceeding 14 days in duration, or other businesses dealing in merchandise common to that dealt in by flea markets, where vendors therein are not operating among a collection of vendors renting or securing individual spaces within an overall operation.
(Ord. No. 01-2017 , 10-2-2017)
Sec. 10-163. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Antique mall means a fully-enclosed building in which operates a single business, with regular operating hours, selling antique or antique replica merchandise of one or more consignees and where the vast majority of merchandise is not "new" or "modern."
Flea market means
(1)
Any event:
a.
At which two or more persons offer personal property for sale or exchange; and
b.
At which a fee is charged for the privilege of offering or displaying personal property for sale or exchange; or
c.
At which a fee is charged to prospective buyers for admission to the area where personal property is offered or displayed for sale or exchange; or
d.
Regardless of the number of persons offering or displaying personal property or the absence of fees, at which used personal property is offered or displayed for sale or exchange if the event is held more than six times in any 12-month period.
(2)
The term "flea market" is interchangeable with and applicable to "swap meet," "indoor swap meet," or other similar terms regardless of whether these events are held inside a building or outside in the open. The primary characteristic is that these activities involve a series of sales sufficient in number, scope, and character to constitute a regular course of business.
(3)
The term "flea market" shall not mean and shall not apply to:
a.
An event which is organized for the exclusive benefit of any community chest, fund, foundation, association, or corporation organized and operated for religious, educational, or charitable purposes, provided that no part of any admission fee or parking fee charged vendors or prospective purchasers or the gross receipts or net earnings from the sale or exchange of personal property, whether in the form of a percentage of the receipts or earnings, as salary, or otherwise, inures to the benefit of any private shareholder or person participating in the organization or conduct of the event; or
b.
Any event at which all of the personal property offered for sale or displayed is new, and all persons selling, exchanging, or offering or displaying personal property for sale or exchange are manufacturers or licensed retail or wholesale merchants; or
c.
Any city-sponsored event.
Flea market promoter, operator or owner means all persons or other forms of business entities operating, maintaining, managing and promoting flea markets.
Vendor or flea market vendor means all persons or other forms of business entities of every type and character operating among collections of other flea market vendors for sales to the public of new and used merchandise in collected marketing centers for sales from stalls, booths, tables, benches, rooms and other similar displays or marketing configurations and arrangements, excluding those operations defined under this section.
(Ord. No. 01-2017 , 10-2-2017)
Sec. 10-164. - Violations and penalties.
(a)
Any person violating any of the provisions of this article shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided by the Whitesburg City Code.
(b)
In addition to the penalty provided in subsection (a) of this section, every violation of the terms of this article by any person shall be termed a nuisance and a continuing nuisance so long as such violation may be continued; and such violation is subject to abatement as a nuisance as provided by the laws of this state.
(c)
Any violation of this article shall be subject to injunction. Violations shall also be punished by revocation, suspension or probation of the license or permit.
(d)
The remedies provided for in this section are cumulative. Pursuance of any one remedy shall not be deemed an election of remedies and shall not prohibit pursuance of more than one remedy simultaneously.
(Ord. No. 01-2017 , 10-2-2017)
Sec. 10-165. - Business certificate requirements.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person or entity to conduct a flea market or for any flea market promoter, operator or owner to operate or allow to be operated such a business, or for any flea market vendor to display or sell wares of any kind at a flea market without an occupation tax certificate from the city. Occupation tax certificate issued for a flea market or any flea market promoter, operator or owner shall be valid only for the licensed location. An occupation tax certificate issued for a flea market vendor shall be valid when the vendor is operating from any duly licensed flea market in the city. It shall be unlawful for any flea market promoter, owner, operator or agent providing or renting space for flea market vendors to rent any sales area to any vendor or to provide space free of rent or to allow any vendor to use any sales area within a flea market unless the vendor has a valid occupation tax certificate from the city. It shall be the duty of the flea market promoter, operator, owner or agent renting or providing space to verify that such certificate is in effect for each vendor. Failure to verify that each vendor has a valid occupation tax certificate, or the renting or provision of space for any unlicensed vendor to display or sell wares, shall subject the occupation tax certificate of the flea market operator, owner or promoter to revocation.
(b)
All persons desiring to obtain an occupation tax certificate as a vendor or for a flea market as a flea market promoter, operator or owner shall make written application to the city through City Hall on forms to be prepared and approved by the city. Applicants shall supply such reasonable information as may be required by the city. If the license is for a flea market operation, the information shall include the square footage of sales area within the flea market. An application fee, which is nonrefundable if a permit is not issued, shall be submitted with each application to operate a flea market.
(c)
All applicants for an occupation tax certificate under this article shall furnish all data, information and records reasonably necessary for the evaluation of an application, as may be requested by the city.
(d)
Subject to the review of the city, any application for any occupation tax certificate under this article shall presumptively not be granted where the application or investigation shows any of the following to exist:
(1)
The applicant has had any license issued under the police power of any city or other governmental entity previously suspended or revoked.
(2)
The location, if applicable, is not properly zoned.
(3)
The applicant, as a previous holder of a license as a flea market promoter, operator, owner or vendor, has violated a law, including this article, relating to such business within a ten-year period immediately preceding the date of application.
(4)
No flea market special use permit is in effect for the location, if applicable.
(5)
The applicant has supplied false information either upon his application or in any communication with any official of the city relating to his application.
(6)
The applicant has failed to pay any fee required under this article or has otherwise failed to comply with the provisions of this article.
(7)
Any individual having an interest either as owner, partner, stockholder owning at least 25 percent of the stock of the corporation, such interest being direct or indirect, beneficial or absolute, shall have been convicted or shall have taken a plea of nolo contendere within ten years immediately prior to the filing of such application for any felony or misdemeanor of any state or of the United States. The term "conviction" shall include an adjudication of guilt or plea of guilty or nolo contendere, or the forfeiture of a bond when charged with a crime.
(8)
In addition to the provisions of subsections (d)(l) through (d)(7) of this section and in determining whether or not any occupation tax certificate applied for under this article shall be granted, the following shall be considered in the public interest and welfare:
a.
If the applicant is a previous holder of a license, the manner in which he conducted business thereunder, and particularly as to the necessity for unusual police observation and inspection in order to prevent the violation of any law, regulation or ordinance relating to such business.
b.
The location for which the license is sought, if applicable, as to traffic congestion, general character of the neighborhood and the effect such an establishment would have on the adjacent and surrounding property values.
(e)
All applications for occupation tax certificate for a flea market owner or operator required by this article may be investigated by the city police department. The police department shall report any recommendations to the city. The applicant for an occupation tax certificate to operate a flea market shall be fingerprinted and shall supply such other information as the police department deems reasonably necessary to complete its investigation.
(f)
Fees for flea market owners, promoters, or operators and for flea market vendors shall be as established by the city council as a schedule of fees and charges which will be publicly available upon request.
(g)
Any occupation tax certificate issued under this article shall be posted conspicuously at the place of business.
(h)
No occupation tax certificate issued under this article will be transferable.
(i)
Each occupation tax certificate for a flea market or flea market promoter, operator or owner shall be for one location only. Flea markets or flea market promoters, operators or owners shall obtain a separate occupation tax certificate, paying a separate fee, for each non-adjacent place of business.
(j)
Any vendors operating in the city a maximum of three consecutive days selling merchandise as a part of a show, festival, fair, or promotion shall be required to obtain an occupation tax certificate that shall be valid only for the period of time that special show, fair, or promotion is being held. This temporary occupation tax certificate shall not apply to flea market vendors, special tent sales, peddlers or any business not connected with a show, festival, fair, or promotion.
(Ord. No. 01-2017 , 10-2-2017)
Sec. 10-166. - Use regulations.
The following regulations shall apply to all flea markets licensed under this article:
(1)
There shall be no on-street parking at any flea market. Each flea market shall provide on-site parking at the rate of one space per 100 square feet of sales area. No parking or parking spaces shall be located on or within any road right-of-way. It shall be the responsibility of the flea market promoter, operator or owner to assure at all times that no vehicle of any vendor, customer, patron, visitor, guest, invitee, or other person within the flea market premises is parked on any street or road or street or road right-of-way or off the premises of the flea market. The flea market promoter, operator or owner will be responsible to make sure that any person within the flea market premises who has a vehicle parked off the premises is parked in a space acceptable under the city parking regulations and this article.
(2)
The flea market promoter, owner, or operator shall ensure as well as he or she is reasonably able based upon the circumstances, that ingress and egress of vehicles occurs only at curb cuts and locations approved by the city's public works department.
(3)
There shall be no overnight outdoor storage of any kind. There shall be no sales of any kind whatsoever from any space, stall, shed or area which is not permanently covered by a roof and completely floored by a concrete floor of a minimum thickness of four inches, except at locations designated as such and approved at the time of the granting of the use permit. Regardless, no sales or displays shall occur within parking areas required by this ordinance. Any and all sales shall not interfere with city or the flea market's traffic flow.
(4)
No sales of any kind whatsoever shall be made from any shed, structure or building unless such structure meets or exceeds the minimum requirements of all applicable city codes (including, but not limited to, the building code, fire code, plumbing code, and electrical code). All structures are required to have building permits and inspections in the same manner as prescribed by the city code for other constructions. Flea markets will be classified as Group M, mercantile occupancy, under the city building code and shall meet all such requirements; provided that for applications for permits for all open structures, plans shall be submitted only once signed and sealed by an architect or engineer and they shall include design calculations to substantiate compliance with code wind uplift requirements.
(5)
All plumbing shall meet minimum city and state code requirements. Additionally, each flea market shall provide, at a minimum, facilities for use by the public based on occupant loads as follows:
a.
For flea markets located within enclosed structures, the occupant load shall be determined in the same manner as mercantile occupancy and exit capacity according to all applicable laws.
b.
For flea market areas located in the open air, the occupant load shall be determined as two people for each table, counter, or booth of no more than 20 square feet.
c.
After the occupant load is identified, toilet facilities will be provided in the same manner as for commercial buildings of multiple tenants.
d.
No temporary bathroom facilities will be permitted.
(6)
Notwithstanding any other provisions of this Code, no person, including, but not limited to, a flea market promoter, operator, owner, employee, manager, vendor or invitee, shall be allowed to camp or reside overnight with or without a recreational vehicle, tent, sleeping bag, vehicle or other structure at a flea market. The flea market owner may, however, allow a reasonable number of overnight security personnel who may be either the flea market owner or agents or employees of the flea market owner, to patrol the premises.
(Ord. No. 01-2017 , 10-2-2017)
Sec. 10-167. - Record keeping.
Every person who engages in the retail sale of personal property at a flea market shall maintain, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 10-1-360, a permanent record book in which shall be entered in ink and in legible English at the time any property is acquired for the purpose of retail sale:
(1)
The date of the transaction;
(2)
The name, age, and address of the person, corporation, or entity from whom the property was acquired, a description of the general appearance of any such person, and the distinctive number from such person's driver's license or other similar identification card;
(3)
An identification and description of the property acquired including, if reasonably available, the serial, model, or other number and all identifying marks inscribed thereon;
(4)
The price paid for such property; and
(5)
The signature of the seller.
All entries shall appear in ink and shall be in chronological order. No blank lines may be left between entries. No obliterations, alterations, or erasures may be made. Corrections shall be made by drawing a line of ink through the entry without destroying the legibility. Such record book shall be open to the inspection of any law enforcement officer during the ordinary hours of business or at any reasonable time.
(c)
The record of each purchase transaction provided for in this section shall be maintained for a period of not less than two years and shall be kept by the nonregistered vendor or vendor and made available during any period at which such person is open for business or is offering property for sale.
(d)
Any person required to maintain a record book under the provisions of this section who shall:
(1)
Fail to make an entry of any material matter in his or her permanent record book;
(2)
Make any false entry therein;
(3)
Falsify, obliterate, destroy, or remove such record book from his or her place of business during any time such record book is required to be present;
(4)
Refuse to allow any law enforcement officer to inspect his or her permanent record book or any goods or property in his or her possession during the ordinary hours of business or at any reasonable time; or
(5)
Fail to maintain the records required by this Code section for at least two years shall be guilty of a misdemeanor.
(Ord. No. 01-2017 , 10-2-2017)

ARTICLE VIII. - YARD SALES[6]

Editor's note— Ord. No. 02-2017 , adopted Oct. 2, 2017, added provisions to the Code, but did not specify manner of inclusion. Therefore, at the discretion of the editor, said provisions have been designated as art. VIII, §§ 10-181—10-187, as set out herein.

Sec. 10-181. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Applicant/sponsor means the person sponsoring or promoting the yard sale.
Permit means the written authorization to operate or conduct the yard sale.
Person shall mean and include individuals, partnerships, associations, corporation or any other legal entity.
Yard sale means the sale, exchange, trade or display of goods outside of an enclosed permanent structure including, but not limited to, areas commonly know as the carport, garage or the area commonly known as the yard of a residence.
(1)
Goods include, but are not limited to, clothing, furniture, household items, food, dishes, antiques or similar goods or merchandise, not regularly sold in the normal course of business, or the sale or trading of such goods, as outlined in this definition, by a person not regularly engaged in such business.
(2)
The term yard sale shall include garage sales, carport sales, lawn sales, attic sales, rummage sales and similar types of sales events.
(3)
The term yard sale includes neighborhood yard sales that are conducted by two or more persons where such persons have joined together to offer goods for sale at the property of one of the participants in the yard sale.
(4)
Such goods or merchandise do not need to be attended by a person for a sale to be deemed to be in existence.
(Ord. No. 02-2017 , 10-2-2017)
Sec. 10-182. - Location and manner.
(a)
Yard sales may be held on a lot or parcel of land upon which there is situated a house, apartment or other structure which is utilized primarily for residential purposes. Yard sales are prohibited upon vacant lots and upon lots upon which there is situated a structure that is utilized primarily for any other use other than residential purposes.
(b)
All displays of yard sale merchandise shall be limited to that portion of the front yard area which is at least five feet back from the property line on all sides, and at least ten feet back from any curb or road edge.
(c)
No retail of new products is permitted.
(d)
No sales may run more than four consecutive days.
(e)
All yard sale signs shall be removed within 24 hours after the conclusion of the yard sale. The failure to remove any yard sale sign shall be in violation of the Code of Ordinances. All signage must comply according with City of Whitesburg Ordinances.
(f)
Only non-illuminated signs, not to exceed four square feet, may be posted to advertise a yard sale. The signs cannot interfere with traffic, businesses, or adjacent property owners.
(g)
The applicant/sponsor listed on the permit is in charge of the premises upon which the sale is conducted. Applicant/sponsor shall supervise the parking of vehicles of patrons so [as] to avoid congestion and hazardous conditions.
(Ord. No. 02-2017 , 10-2-2017)
Sec. 10-183. - Exemptions.
(a)
Religious and charitable organizations exempt from federal taxation pursuant to section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, may conduct yard sales on the property of the organization or at other locations within the city with the expressed written consent of the owner of the premises where the yard sale is being conducted.
(b)
Any item of personal property which is specifically named and described in an advertisement. This exemption is limited to no more than five advertised items.
(Ord. No. 02-2017 , 10-2-2017)
Sec. 10-184. - Permit required.
(a)
Applicant/sponsor owning the property upon which the yard sale will take place, or conducting the yard sale or the seller in the yard must obtain a permit from City Hall or its designee at least 24 hours prior to conducting any yard sale and no later than 4:00 p.m. Thursday for weekend sales.
(b)
No person shall obtain more than two permits in any calendar year for any premises.
(c)
No property shall host more than two yard sales in any calendar year.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person to allow, conduct or participate as a seller in a yard sale in the city without being properly permitted.
(e)
The application for a permit shall require the following information:
(l)
Name and address of person conducting the yard sale.
(2)
Name and address of property owner where sale will be held.
(3)
Number of days of sale.
(4)
Date(s) of sale.
(5)
Date(s) and location of any previous sales within the past year.
(f)
The applicant shall pay a fee of $5.00 for processing the application.
(g)
Permits shall be displayed in public view each day of the sale. Whitesburg Police Officers may inspect each yard sale and issue the proper penalty including, but not limited to, closing it down for any sale that does not include the proper permit.
(h)
If for any reason the city clerk or his/her designee denies a yard sale permit for any of the reasons listed in this article, the applicant shall have the right to appeal the decision to the city council at its next regularly scheduled meeting.
(Ord. No. 02-2017 , 10-2-2017)
Sec. 10-185. - Prohibited acts.
(a)
No individual, firm, corporation or other entity shall be permitted to engage in the business of promoting or conducting yard sales for others for a fee or other consideration. No merchandise shall be purchased in anticipation of resale at a yard sale.
(b)
No yard sale shall be held on premises other than the premises of at least one of the persons conducting the yard sale except with the express written consent of the owner of the premises where the yard sale is being conducted.
(c)
Yard sales must be operational only during daylight hours. Goods shall be brought inside at dusk and the surrounding area must be properly cleaned to ensure no trash or debris is left behind.
(Ord. No. 02-2017 , 10-2-2017)
Sec. 10-186. - Penalty for violation.
Violations of this article shall be deemed a misdemeanor. Each violation and each day of violation shall be considered a separate offense. Upon conviction of a person in violation of this article, said person shall be punished as provided by the city Charter and city ordinances.
(Ord. No. 02-2017 , 10-2-2017)
