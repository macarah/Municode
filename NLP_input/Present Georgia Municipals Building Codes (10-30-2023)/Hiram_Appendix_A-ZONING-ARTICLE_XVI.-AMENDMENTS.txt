ARTICLE XVI. - AMENDMENTS

Sec. A. - Planning Commission to propose or review amendments.
This Ordinance, including the comprehensive land use map, of Paulding County, Georgia, and the official zoning map(s), of the City of Hiram, Georgia, may be amended by the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, on its own motion, or on recommendation of the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, or the Operations Manager, of the City of Hiram, Georgia or the City Manager of the City of Hiram, Georgia or the Temporary City Manager, but no amendment shall become effective, unless it has been proposed by, or has first been submitted to the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, for review and recommendation. Before enacting an amendment, public notice shall be given and a public hearing held as required herein.
Sec. B. - Initiation of amendments.
(1)
Applications to amend this Ordinance may be in the form of proposals to amend the text or the official zoning maps.
(a)
An application to amend the text of this Ordinance may be initiated by the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, or be submitted to the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, by the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, or by any person having an interest in the City of Hiram, Georgia.
(b)
An application to amend the official zoning map may be initiated by the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, or may be submitted to the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, by the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, or may be submitted by any person having an interest in the City of Hiram, Georgia.
(2)
Unless initiated by the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, or the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, all applications (rezoning, land use, special use, or medical hardships) must be submitted by the owner of the affected property, or the authorized agent of the owner. If submitted by an agent of the owner, such authorization shall be notarized and attached to the application.
An application may be withdrawn without prejudice at any time prior to the date scheduled for the hearing by the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia. Unless withdrawn at the hearing, the withdrawal must be in writing, signed and dated by the applicant. If an application is withdrawn by the applicant after the legal advertising as required by this Article shall have first appeared, the cost of the advertising and any other expenses incurred may be deducted from any refund of fees.
The applicant, or his/her representative, must be present at the hearing, or the application may not be heard.
Sec. C. - Applications for amendments.
Applications for text amendments, rezonings, land use permits, special use permits, and/or medical hardships shall be filed with all accompanying documents with the Department of Community Development, Planning and Zoning Division, of Paulding County, Georgia, in accordance with the filing deadline established by the Department of Community Development, Planning and Zoning Division, of Paulding County, Georgia. Application fees are non-refundable and are established by the Board of Commissioners, of Paulding County, Georgia, and made available by the Department of Community Development, Planning and Zoning Division, of Paulding County, Georgia. A fee shall not be charged for applications initiated by the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, Board of Commissioners, of Paulding County, Georgia, or the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia. Applications shall be submitted and the following provided at the date of filing:
(1)
Text amendment applications shall include the following:
(a)
Name and current address of the applicant;
(b)
Current provisions of the text to be affected by the amendment;
(c)
Proposed wording of text change; and
(d)
Reason for the amendment request.
(2)
Applications for rezoning, special use permits, and land use permits shall contain the following:
(a)
A completed application form signed by the owner and the applicant (if applicable).
(b)
A warranty deed or other proof of ownership of the property.
(c)
Documentation by the Tax Commissioner, of Paulding County, Georgia that the ad valorem taxes, levied against the property, have been paid.
(d)
A legal description of the tract of property.
(e)
A survey plat of the property which demonstrates a true and accurate boundary of the property, prepared by a Surveyor, Registered with the State of Georgia, drawn to scale, showing north arrow, land lot and district, the dimensions, acreage and location of the tract. The preparer's seal shall be affixed to the plat.
(f)
A site development plan prepared by a Surveyor, Registered with the State of Georgia, Engineer, Land Planner, or Architect (with preparer's seal affixed) drawn to scale of the subject property, including proposed building locations, proposed use of the property within the requested zoning classification, parking areas, access points, creeks, streams, flood plain, etc., and any buffers that may be required (number of copies specified by the Department of Community Development, Planning and Zoning Division, of Paulding County, Georgia, in addition to the one required by the Operations Manager, of the City of Hiram). The applicant may also furnish additional material which may aid in the understanding of the request.
(g)
The names and mailing addresses of all adjoining property owners, including the owners across the road and to the rear.
(h)
The filing fee as set by the Board of Commissioners, of Paulding County, Georgia or the Mayor and Council of the City of Hiram, Georgia.
(i)
Such other additional information as may be requested by the Department of Community Development, Planning and Zoning Division, of Paulding County, Georgia, in the Procedures for the Filing of Applications, located in the Department of Community Development, of Paulding County, Georgia.
(3)
Applications for medical hardships shall contain the following:
(a)
A completed application form signed by the owner, the applicant, and the person with the medical hardship.
(b)
A warranty deed or other proof of ownership of the property.
(c)
Documentation by the Tax Commissioner, of Paulding County, Georgia that the ad valorem taxes, levied against the property, have been paid.
(d)
A legal description of the tract of property.
(e)
A current survey plat of the property (number of copies specified by the Department of Community Development, Planning and Zoning Division, of Paulding County, Georgia, in addition to the one required by the Operations Manager, of the City of Hiram, Georgia or the City Manager of the City of Hiram, Georgia or the Temporary City Manager) prepared by a Surveyor, Registered with the State of Georgia, whose state registration is current and valid, drawn to scale, showing north arrow, land lot and district, the dimensions, acreage and location of the tract. The preparer's seal shall be affixed to the plat. Indicate on the plat structures which are currently located on the property (such as house or storage building) and indicate on the plat where the mobile home will be positioned.
(f)
The names and mailing addresses of all adjoining property owners, including the owners across the road and to the rear.
(g)
The filing fee as set by the Board of Commissioners, of Paulding County, Georgia or the Mayor and Council of the City of Hiram, Georgia.
(h)
An original, notarized doctor's certificate stating the name of the person with the medical hardship, a description of the physical condition, and explanation of why the person needs to live in close proximity to receive care, and any other evidence to support the medical hardship application.
(i)
Such other additional information as may be requested by the Department of Community Development, Planning and Zoning Division, of Paulding County, Georgia, and/or the Operations Manager, of the City of Hiram, Georgia or the City Manager of the City of Hiram, Georgia or the Temporary City Manager, in the Procedures for the Filing of Applications, located in the Department of Community Development, of Paulding County, Georgia.
(4)
If an application is withdrawn by the applicant after the legal advertising as required by this Article shall have first appeared, the cost of advertisement and any other expenses incurred may be deducted from any refund of fees.
(5)
With respect to amendments to the official zoning map, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, an applicant may file renderings, construction specifications, written development restrictions, and other conditions which the applicant proposes as binding conditions upon the development and use of the property involved in the application; provided, however, that such conditions, or alterations, or changes thereto shall be filed with the Department of Community Development, Planning and Zoning Division, Zoning Administrator, of Paulding County, Georgia, at least seven (7) days prior to the public hearing before the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia. If such conditions, or alterations, or changes thereto are proposed by an applicant and have not been filed as required by this paragraph, the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, at the time of the public hearing on the application, may defer any action on such application to a specific meeting date which will permit the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, to conduct another hearing to consider the applicant's proposal prior to consideration of the application by the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia.
At the hearing in which the deferral is granted, the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, shall specify the month of the published scheduled hearing before the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, and the subsequent hearing before the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, and this action shall constitute public notice of such hearings and no additional notices shall be required prior to the hearings so scheduled by the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia. The date designated for action on the application shall be set at a time which will allow the applicant to comply with the filing requirements of this paragraph.
(6)
An application for rezoning, special use permit, land use permit, or medical hardship may be forwarded by the applicant from its scheduled public hearing date to the next available public hearing date with the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, a maximum of two (2) times. The request to forward an application must be written with the applicant's (or representative's) signature and submitted to the Department of Community Development, Planning and Zoning Division, of Paulding County, Georgia, prior to the hearing date. Each request must include the application number, explanation for the request to forward the application, and date of the next scheduled public hearing, at which the application will be considered by the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia. Payment of an additional application fee in the full amount of the original application fee is required upon written request to forward the application in order to pay for the additional costs associated therewith.
Applications which must be forwarded as a result of the actions, or omissions of the Applicant, resulting in an incomplete or deficient application will also be subject to the payment of the additional application fee in the full amount of the original application. Payment is due immediately upon the forwarding of said deficient application.
Applications that are forwarded by the Department of Community Development, Planning and Zoning Division, of Paulding County, Georgia, Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, or by the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, or at the request of the Operations Manager, of the City of Hiram, Georgia or the City Manager of the City of Hiram, Georgia or the Temporary City Manager, or Department of Community Development, Planning and Zoning Division staff, of Paulding County, Georgia, will not be considered as a applicant's request to forward an application and no additional fees will be due.
Sec. D. - Planning Commission action.
(1)
This Zoning Ordinance, including the zoning map, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, may be amended from time to time by the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, but no amendment shall become effective unless it shall have been proposed by, or shall first have been submitted to, the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, for review and recommendation.
(2)
The Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, shall be allowed to hold its public hearing for an application and to table its recommendation until the next scheduled meeting for the purpose of considering more information. If the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, fails to submit a recommendation at its next scheduled meeting, it shall be deemed to have approved the proposed amendment. The amendment will then be forwarded to the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, for their public hearing, and final consideration.
Sec. E. - Public notification.
(1)
Legal Notice. Before the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, takes action on any proposed amendment, the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, and the Mayor and Council of the City of Hiram, Georgia shall hold a public hearing thereon. At least fifteen (15) days, but not more than forty-five (45) days, prior to the date of the hearing, a notice of the hearing shall be published within a newspaper of general circulation within the City of Hiram, Georgia, in which are carried the legal advertisements of the City of Hiram, Georgia. This notice shall state the time, date, place, and purpose of the hearing. If a rezoning application is initiated by a party other than the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, the notice of hearing as provided herein shall also include the location of the property, the present zoning classification of the property, and the proposed zoning classification of the property.
(2)
Sign(s) Posted. A sign shall be placed in a conspicuous location on the property not less than fifteen (15) days prior to the date of the hearing. The sign shall state the time, date, and place of the hearing, the present zoning classification of the property, and the proposed zoning classification of the property. Such signs shall be visible from each public right-of-way fronting the property. The sign will include the time, date, and place of the public hearing on the application, by the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia.
(3)
Letters to Property Owners. The Department of Community Development, Planning and Zoning Division, of Paulding County, Georgia, must notify in writing all owners of property abutting any tract or lot for which a rezoning has been filed in accordance with the list of owners provided by the applicant of the nature of the request and the date of the hearing before the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia. This notification is in addition to and not in lieu of, any other notice or publication required under this Ordinance.
Sec. F. - Conduct of hearing.
(1)
The public hearing of the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, or the Mayor and Council of the City of Hiram, Georgia shall be held at regularly scheduled meetings, such meetings to be held at a time, place, and date as prescribed according to a schedule which shall be approved and adopted as provided in subsection 5 of Section 16 of the Code of Ordinances, of Paulding County, Georgia. The public hearing shall be presided over by the Chairman of the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, or another officer of the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, in the absence of the Chairman. A secretary shall be present at all public hearings to take minutes.
(2)
The Chairman, or any other Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, member or the Mayor and Council of the City of Hiram, Georgia shall review the following procedures that will be adhered to during the public hearing:
(a)
Anyone in attendance at the public hearing wishing to speak on behalf or in opposition to petitions that are on the agenda must be recognized and they will be asked to state their name and place of residence, and then to sign his or her name on a sheet provided by the staff.
(b)
The person recognized to speak will be allowed to speak on any point relevant to the petition being considered. Applicants and opponents shall be given a maximum of fifteen (15) minutes each to present their case. Any groups which are present are encouraged to choose a spokesperson to present their views. The Chairman, of the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, may request representatives of each side to speak for the entire group. If more than one (1) speaker represents a group, the fifteen (15) minutes shall be divided among the various speakers. Speakers are encouraged to refrain from presenting information that has previously been stated by others. In no case, however, shall any group be allowed more than the allotted times, no matter how many speakers represent the group, unless extended by the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia. A designated staff member will be responsible for keeping time.
(c)
Proposals for each Agenda item should be presented orally to the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, and to a representative, if available, of the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, by Applicant(s), and/or their Representative, along with any documentation necessary to demonstrate the justification for such request.
(d)
During and following the Applicant's presentation, the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, and the representative of the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, may ask any pertinent questions of the Applicant, or their Representative to aid in their understanding of the case.
(e)
After such presentation, and discussion with the Applicant, the Chairman of the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, shall ask if there be anyone present who is present in opposition to the granting of such request.
(f)
Those appearing in opposition may offer oral evidence and any documentation to demonstrate their position.
(g)
During and following the presentation of those in opposition to the application or proposal, the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, and the representative, if available, of the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, may ask any pertinent questions of the Opposition to aid in their understanding of the case. Questions and responses shall not be counted as part of the maximum fifteen (15) minute presentation time limit.
(h)
No further public input will be allowed without consent of the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia.
(i)
At the conclusion of the public hearing, the Chairman of the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, will cause to be forwarded to the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, recommendation of the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, concerning each application, pertaining to the City of Hiram, Georgia. The Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, shall hold its public hearing, and then make its final determination at a future meeting of the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia. The public hearing, of the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, is concluded at the time the Planning Commission, of Paulding County, Georgia, makes its recommendation to the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia.
Sec. G. - Zoning review standards.
Any proposed rezoning will be evaluated using the following zoning review standards:
(1)
Existing land use and zoning classification of nearby property.
(2)
Whether the zoning proposal will permit a use that is suitable in view of the use and development of adjacent and nearby property.
(3)
Whether the zoning proposal will adversely affect the existing use or usability of adjacent or nearby property.
(4)
Whether the zoning proposal will result in a use which will or could cause an excessive or burdensome use of existing streets, transportation facilities, utilities, or schools.
(5)
Whether the proposed use is supported by new or changing conditions not anticipated by the Comprehensive Plan, of Paulding County, Georgia, or reflected in the existing zoning on the property or surrounding properties; and
(6)
Whether the zoning proposal is in conformity with the policies and intent of the Comprehensive Plan, of Paulding County, Georgia.
Sec. H. - Decision by Hiram City Council.
The Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, shall also hold a public hearing at the next regularly scheduled meeting of the body, with notice as required by Section E of Article XVI once the application/amendment has been forwarded to the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia. Once the public hearing has been held the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, shall address the applications and may approve, deny, or table the applications using the same standards and criteria as set forth herein. An action by the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, to defer the application shall include a statement of the date and time of the next meeting at which the application(s) will be considered, which shall constitute public notice of the hearing on the application(s) and no further notice shall be required. If an application for a rezoning is denied, no re-application shall be allowed until twelve (12) months have passed from the date of final decision by the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia.
Sec. I. - Site plan amendment.
(1)
At the time the site is developed, if the submitted site plan reflects a proposed use which is an allowable use within the specified zoning district, but differs from that approved by the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, at the time of the rezoning action, then the site plan shall be reviewed again by all applicable City of Hiram, Georgia/Paulding County, Georgia, departments, as well as, applicable State of Georgia, and/or Federal agencies.
(2)
At the time the site is developed, if the submitted site plan deviates significantly, as determined by City of Hiram, Georgia/Paulding County, Georgia, staff, from the site plan which was approved by the Mayor and Council, of the City of Hiram, Georgia, at the time of the rezoning action, the site plan shall be reviewed by all applicable City of Hiram, Georgia/Paulding County, Georgia, departments, as well as, applicable State of Georgia, and/or Federal agencies.
(3)
The Mayor and Council of the City of Hiram, Georgia is authorized to make final approval or disapproval/denial of the amended site plan referred to above. If an amended site plan is disapproved/denied, an appeal may be made in writing within thirty (30) days of the final denial/disapproval by the Mayor and Council of the City of Hiram, Georgia of such site plan amendment to the Superior Court of Paulding County, Georgia.
(4)
All site plans are subject to the plan review standards established by the City of Hiram, Georgia/Paulding County, Georgia.
