ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 86-1. - State law—Adopted.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-372—40-6-376, the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1, and O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-1—40-6-346, the Uniform Rules of the Road, are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of this city with like effect as if recited in this section.
(Code 1986, § 13-1)
Sec. 86-2. - Same—Violations.
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of section 86-1 shall be punished as provided in section 1-8.
(Code 1986, § 13-2)
Sec. 86-3. - Signs.
The council shall designate and install signs and other traffic control devices in the city except at locations where the state department of transportation has jurisdiction.
(Code 1986, § 13-3)
Sec. 86-4. - Temporary traffic rules.
In cases where the traffic upon the streets of the city may become congested, and upon the occasion of parades, theatrical entertainments and other public assemblages, when a large number of vehicles are assembled, the police may make temporary rules regulating the traffic in such congested districts, and any person who, after being warned of the temporary traffic regulation by a police officer, shall refuse to comply with such regulation, shall be liable to the punishment prescribed in section 1-8.
(Code 1986, § 13-4)
Sec. 86-5. - Trespass on college square.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to drive a vehicle or trespass on foot on the square known as the Old Capitol Square occupied by the Georgia Military College and bounded on the north by Green Street, on the east by Elbert Street, on the south by Franklin Street and on the west by Wayne Street, unless the occupant of the vehicle or the pedestrian shall be on such square on business with the college, or attending some function taking place on such square to which the public is invited.
(b)
This section shall not apply to the walk leading from the south gate of the Old Capitol Square to Wayne Street near the Episcopal church.
(Code 1986, § 13-5)
Sec. 86-6. - Speed breakers on streets.
(a)
No speed breakers, risers, rumble strips or other such structures shall be placed or maintained on any streets within the limits of the city unless authorized by proper resolution of the council. Said resolution shall identify the particular street or streets upon which the speed breakers, risers, rumble strips or other such structures shall be placed, and shall identify the specific type of structure that is being authorized for installation upon said street or streets.
(b)
Upon the installation of speed breakers, risers, rumble strips or other such structures in accordance with this section, the city shall cause to be erected proper signage and other street markings that are adequate to warn motorists of the installation of the particular speed control device authorized by this section.
(Code 1986, § 13-6; Ord. No. O-0608-023, 9-12-2006)
Sec. 86-7. - Criminal trespass by motor vehicle.
(a)
In accordance with the authority granted in O.C.G.A. § 40-6-252(c), the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 40-6-252(a) are hereby adopted.
(b)
Any person violating this section shall be subject to a monetary fine not to exceed:
(1)
$50.00 for the first such violation;
(2)
$100.00 for the second such violation;
(3)
$150.00 for the third or any subsequent violation.
(Code 1986, § 13-7; Ord. of 6-20-1989)
Secs. 86-8—86-40. - Reserved.

Sec. 86-41. - Driving vehicle without consent of owner.
No person shall operate an automobile, motorcycle or similar vehicle upon any of the streets, lanes or alleys of the city without the consent of the owner.
(Code 1986, § 13-41)
State Law reference— Offenses involving theft, O.C.G.A. § 16-8-1 et seq.
Sec. 86-42. - Driving through processions.
(a)
No vehicle shall cut or run through any parade or funeral procession, except as directed by a police officer. Drivers of all vehicles shall observe and obey the signals of police officers at the intersections of streets.
(b)
The first sentence of subsection (a) of this section shall not apply to authorized emergency vehicles.
(Code 1986, § 13-42)
Sec. 86-43. - Driving over fire department equipment.
It shall be unlawful for any person to drive any vehicle on, over or against any fire engine, fire pump or other firefighting apparatus belonging to the city.
(Code 1986, § 13-43)
Sec. 86-44. - Distance from fire.
It shall be unlawful for the driver of any vehicle on the streets or alleys of the city, who may be within a distance of 500 feet of a fire at the time of its outbreak, to permit such vehicle to remain in such street or alley, but shall immediately drive the vehicle to a point more than 500 feet from such fire.
(Code 1986, § 13-44)

(2)
Liberty Street, between Greene Street and Hancock Street.
(3)
Wilkinson Street, between Greene Street and Montgomery Street.
(4)
Wayne Street, between Greene Street and Montgomery Street.
(5)
Jefferson Street, between Greene Street and Montgomery Street.
(6)
Greene Street, between Jefferson Street and Clark Street.
(7)
Hancock Street, between Jefferson Street and Clark Street.
(8)
McIntosh Street, between Jefferson Street and Wilkinson Street.
(9)
Montgomery Street, between Jefferson Street and Clark Street.
(b)
It shall be the duty of the police department to enforce the provisions of this section.
(c)
Any person violating this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall, on conviction in the municipal court, be punished in the discretion of the municipal court judge but not in excess of the provisions as provided in the Charter of the city and the acts amending the Charter and the ordinances passed thereunder.
(Code 1986, § 13-46; Ord. No. O-0704-002, 4-24-2007)

ARTICLE III. - STOPPING, STANDING AND PARKING[3]

State Law reference— Stopping, standing and parking, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-200 et seq.; powers of local authorities, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-371.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 86-81. - Enforcement.
It shall be the duty of the police department to enforce the provisions of this article.
(Code 1986, § 13-61)
Sec. 86-82. - General restriction.
(a)
Vehicles of any type, including but not limited to buses, trucks, cars, automobiles, lawn mowers, tractors, farm machinery, boat trailers, utility type trailers or any type trailer to be drawn by any type motorized or nonmotorized vehicle or vehicles, or any and all parts and appurtenances to any type vehicle whether farm, commercial or domestic, shall not be left stopped, standing, parked or stored upon any right-of-way of any streets, alleyways, lanes, cul-de-sacs or any land designated as public property at any time, unless properly zoned, marked, posted and time permitted. All other times they are strictly prohibited.
(b)
Items listed in this section shall be moved within eight hours after notification; otherwise they shall be impounded in accordance with this article.
(Code 1986, § 13-62)
Sec. 86-83. - Loading and unloading.
It shall be unlawful for any person to back any vehicle to the street curb unless for the purpose of actually loading or unloading, or to keep such vehicle backed to such curb longer than is necessary to actually load or unload. When loading or unloading, all vehicles shall stand diagonally and near the righthand curb, unless it is possible for such vehicle to stand parallel to the righthand curb. No vehicle shall stand abreast of another.
(Code 1986, § 13-63)
Sec. 86-84. - Loading zones designated by signs.
When appropriate signs are erected giving notice thereof, the areas so marked shall be loading zones.
(Code 1986, § 13-64)
Sec. 86-85. - Obstructing traffic; egress of previously parked vehicles.
(a)
No vehicle shall be parked so as to obstruct traffic.
(b)
No vehicle shall be parked or stopped in such a position as to prevent the convenient movement of another vehicle already parked.
(Code 1986, § 13-65)
Charter reference— Power of city to prohibit the obstruction of streets, § 7; removal of obstructions, § 8.
Sec. 86-86. - Parking time in alleys.
No vehicle of any description shall be left standing for a longer time than 30 minutes in any of the alleys of the city.
(Code 1986, § 13-66)
Sec. 86-87. - Parking beyond curb line.
The parking of vehicles of any type where any of the wheels of such vehicle, when parked, extend onto the established curb lines of the streets of the city, or onto that area between the established curb lines and the extremities of the street right-of-way, is hereby prohibited.
(Code 1986, § 13-67)
Sec. 86-88. - No parking areas to be designated by signs.
Where signs are erected prohibiting parking upon certain sections of the streets and/or the curbs are painted yellow, parking upon the streets so marked yellow or appropriate signs erected, parking at all times shall be prohibited unless otherwise indicated.
(Code 1986, § 13-68; Ord. No. O-0508-029, 8-9-2005)
Sec. 86-89. - Limitation on use of parking spaces.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to park on the streets, alleys or public parks of the city, any automobile, truck or motor vehicle, except such as are being used in vehicular traffic in the city, or to park any automobile, truck or motor vehicle in the streets, alleys or public parks of the city, there to be exhibited, offered for sale, held for sale, held for rent, held for repair, held for storage, repaired or sold.
(b)
This section shall not apply to taxicabs operating in the city while in the charge of the driver thereof.
(Code 1986, § 13-69)
Sec. 86-90. - Controlled vehicle parking in residential areas.
(a)
Definitions. Whenever in this section the following words are used, they shall, unless the context requires otherwise, be deemed to have the following meanings:
Controlled parking residential zones means a contiguous area containing streets or parts thereof upon which there may be designated restricted residential parking by the city.
Guest means one who is a recipient of hospitality at the home of another.
Resident means one who makes his home in a particular place with ownership.
Tenant means one who pays rent to occupy property owned by another for a stated period of time.
(b)
Privileges and restrictions on controlled parking residential zones.
(1)
Where parking prohibited. A residential parking zone shall not authorize the resident, tenant or guest to stop, stand or park a motor vehicle in such places where the stopping, standing or parking of a motor vehicle is prohibited or set aside for specific types of vehicles, nor exempt the resident, tenant or guest from the observance of any traffic regulation within the controlled residential area.
(2)
Use of on-street parking; nonresidents. No person other than residents, tenants or authorized guests of the controlled parking residential area shall park on streets properly marked as a residential parking zone.
(3)
The following streets have been designated as controlled vehicle parking:
a.
Liberty Street from Greene Street to Franklin Street.
b.
Greene Street from Columbia Street to Tattnall Street (north side only).
c.
Greene Street from Clarke Street to Wilkinson Street (south side only).
d.
Jackson Street from Hancock Street to Greene Street.
e.
North Columbia Street from Montgomery Street to Hancock Street.
f.
North Clarke Street from Montgomery Street to Thomas Street.
g.
Washington Street from Wayne Street to Clarke Street.
h.
South Wilkinson Street from Greene Street to Franklin Street.
i.
North Jefferson Street from Hancock Street to Montgomery Street.
(4)
Any person violating this section shall be subject to a monetary fine not to exceed:
a.
Fifty dollars for the first such violation;
b.
One hundred dollars for the second such violation;
c.
One hundred fifty dollars for the third or any subsequent violation.
(5)
Administrative regulations. The police department shall have the authority to develop and implement administrative regulations for the enforcement of this chapter.
(Ord. No. O-0507-019, 7-26-2005)
Sec. 86-91. - Residential permitted parking.
(a)
Definitions. Whenever in this section the following words are used, they shall, unless the context requires otherwise, be deemed to have the following meanings:
Controlled parking residential zones means a contiguous area containing streets or parts thereof upon which there may be designated restricted residential parking by the city.
Guest means one who is a recipient of hospitality at the home of another.
Resident means one who makes his home in a particular place with ownership.
Residential parking permit means a permit issued hereunder for the privilege of parking on a street designated as a "controlled parking residential area."
Residential permitted parking only means a special area for the restricted parking of residents and guests on a street designated as being within a controlled parking residential area.
Tenant means one who pays rent to occupy property owned by another for a stated period of time.
(b)
Privileges and restrictions on residential permitted parking only.
(1)
Permit bestows privileges and restrictions on residential parking permits. The holder of a residential parking permit shall be permitted to stand or park a motor vehicle displaying the permit and operated by him or her in a designated residential controlled parking area. While a vehicle for which a residential parking permit has been issued is so parked, such a permit shall be prominently displayed in the bottom drivers side corner of the front windshield of the vehicle.
(2)
Where parking prohibited. A residential parking permit shall not authorize the holder thereof to stand or park a motor vehicle in such places where the stopping, standing or parking of a motor vehicle is prohibited or set aside for specific types of vehicles, nor exempt the holder from the observance of any traffic regulation within the controlled residential area.
(3)
Where parking permitted. A residential parking permit shall permit such residents/tenants to park in a designated street for such permit.
(4)
Use by persons other than permitted regulated. No person other than the permittee named thereon shall use the residential parking permit or display it on a vehicle operated or parked, and any such use or display by a person other than the permittee shall constitute a violation of this chapter by the permittee and by the person who so used or displayed such parking permit. Bona fide visitors of permittee shall be issued a guest permit with permission of the permittee.
(5)
False representation. It shall constitute a violation of this chapter for any person to falsely represent himself as eligible for a residential parking permit and to furnish any false information in an application in order to obtain a residential parking permit.
(6)
Revocation of permit. The city is authorized to revoke the residential parking permit for any permittee found to be in violation of this chapter and upon written notification thereof, the permittee shall surrender such to the city. Failure, when requested, to surrender a residential parking permit so revoked shall constitute a violation of this chapter.
(7)
Administrative regulations. The police department shall have the authority to develop and implement administrative regulations for the enforcement of this chapter.
(Ord. No. O-0507-020, 7-26-2005)

DIVISION 2. - TIME LIMITS

Sec. 86-111. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Operator means every individual who shall operate a vehicle as the owner thereof, or as the agent, employee or permittee of the owner, or is in actual physical control of a vehicle.
Park or parking means the standing of a vehicle, whether occupied or not, upon a street otherwise than temporarily for the purpose of, and while actually engaged in, receiving or discharging passengers or loading or unloading merchandise or in obedience to traffic regulations, signs or signals, or an involuntary stopping of the vehicle by reason of causes beyond the control of the operator of the vehicle.
Parking space means any space within the parking zone which is duly designated for the parking of a single vehicle by lines painted or otherwise durably marked on the curb or on the surface of the street adjacent to or adjoining the parking space.
Vehicle means any device, upon or by which any person or property is or may be transported upon a highway, except a device which is operated upon rails or tracks.
(Code 1986, § 13-86)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.

(a)

(1)
Cause, allow, permit or suffer any vehicle registered in the name of, or operated by such person to be parked overtime, or beyond the period of legal parking time established for any parking zone.
(2)
Park any vehicle across any line or marking of a parking space or in such position that the vehicle shall not be entirely within the area designated by such lines or markings.
(3)
Park any vehicle on the right-of-way of State Route 29 (US 441) except in the case of an emergency.
(b)
Any person who shall violate or fail to comply with any of the provisions of this division, or who shall counsel, aid or abet any such violation or failure to comply, shall be deemed guilty of an offense and shall be punished as provided for in section 1-8.

The chief of police may mark off in such zones additional parking spaces and may alter any parking space now existing or marked off, in such manner as he deems advisable to accomplish the purposes of this article. Whenever any vehicle shall be found parked in any place within the city where parking is not permitted at that time, or whenever any vehicle is found parked in violation of the terms of this chapter or any other ordinance relating to traffic, such vehicle may be wheel locked or removed, and conveyed by any member of the police department by means of towing the vehicle, or otherwise, to a vehicle pound.

Sec. 86-144. - Records.
(a)
The officer impounding a vehicle under this division shall immediately report the fact of such impounding, together with any other information which will definitely identify the vehicle, to the chief of police, who shall cause a permanent record to be made thereof, a copy of which will be filed with the clerk and treasurer.

The record kept by the chief of police of all vehicles committed to the pound shall include the names and addresses of the owners of the vehicles, the numbers of their state license tags and the nature and circumstances of each violation, as well as the disposition of each case, and shall account for all fees collected under this division. Sec. 86-145. - Release of impounded vehicle.
Any person claiming a vehicle impounded under this division shall produce evidence of his identity and ownership or right of possession to the chief of police and pay to such officer the fees and cost for removal and storage, for which a receipt shall be given and an order issued to the officer in charge of the pound to discharge the vehicle and to deliver the vehicle to such person. Thereupon, the impounded vehicle shall be surrendered by the officer in charge thereof, who shall take a receipt for such impounded vehicle. Such receipt shall definitely identify the vehicle and shall become a permanent record of the police department. A copy of such receipt shall be delivered to the clerk and treasurer.
(Code 1986, § 13-110)
Sec. 86-146. - Protest and bond.
(a)
In case a protest is made against the payment of any impounding or storage fee under this division, the chief of police may authorize the person in charge of the impounded vehicle to discharge the vehicle.
(b)
The person making the protest shall be charged with violation of this chapter or other traffic ordinances and shall make bond for his appearance and trial before the municipal court judge. Thereupon, the chief of police shall authorize the person in charge of the impounded vehicle to deliver it to such person. If such person is convicted of violating this chapter or other traffic ordinances by the judge, in addition to other costs the judge shall assess as cost the fees for removal and storage which have accumulated in connection with the impounding of such vehicle.
(Code 1986, § 13-111)
Sec. 86-147. - Sale of impounded vehicle.
When any vehicle is left on the streets and it becomes necessary for the police department to take charge of it in order to preserve the safety of travel on the streets, and the police department does take charge of it and removes it and places it in storage, and it is not claimed for as long as 30 days, the chief of police shall sell it. He shall place an advertisement in the newspaper in which the city's advertisements are published, describing the vehicle and giving such information about it as will put the owner or other person having knowledge thereof in possession of the facts, stating that the vehicle is in his possession or control and that, at the expiration of ten days, it will be exposed for sale, at the place where the vehicle is kept or stored, and the proceeds turned in to the city treasury. At the time named, the chief shall proceed to the place where the vehicle is stored and expose it for sale and sell it to the highest bidder for cash. A record shall be kept of such sale showing each vehicle sold, with a description corresponding to the advertisement, the name of the purchaser and the amount received therefor. The chief of police shall turn the proceeds of the sale in to the clerk and treasurer, keeping such record thereof as may serve to identify the vehicle with the proceedings enumerated in this section required.
(Code 1986, § 13-112)

ARTICLE IV. - ROTATIONAL WRECKER SERVICE[5]

Footnotes:
--- (5) ---
Editor's note— Ord. No. O-1009-013, adopted September 28, 2010, did not specify manner of inclusion; hence, inclusion as article IV, sections 86-181—86-184 is at the discretion of the editor.

Sec. 86-181. - Purpose.
The purpose of this general order is to establish policy, procedures, and guidelines for providing a rotational wrecker service for towing vehicles under direction of the city police department.
(Ord. No. O-1009-013, 9-28-2010)
Sec. 86-182. - Policy.
It shall be the policy of the city police department to utilize only those wrecker services whose equipment, procedures, and services to the herein stated rules and regulations.
(Ord. No. O-1009-013, 9-28-2010)
Sec. 86-183. - Discussion.
In the course of providing traffic law enforcement related services to the city, it is frequently necessary to obtain the services of a wrecker due to incidents such as traffic accidents, criminal activity, abandoned vehicles, etc. Occasionally, the owner/operator of a vehicle to be towed is not present to request a particular wrecker service; therefore the bureau rotation wrecker shall be utilized. It shall be the responsibility of officers to adhere to policy set forth in this general order and for participating wrecker services to adhere to the following standards and regulations. It shall be the responsibility of the administrative services division commander for administration and maintenance of this general order. Any modifications, alterations, or changes shall be approved by the chief of police. All members requiring the assistance of a wrecker service shall abide by the procedures and guidelines set forth in this general order.
(Ord. No. O-1009-013, 9-28-2010)
Sec. 86-184. - Procedures.
Rotation wrecker service policy and standards:
(1)
Police department call list.
a.
The police department will maintain a call list of each wrecker service participating in the rotation wrecker service. (All classes—A, B, and C.)
b.
The bureau will normally follow a set rotation list.
Exceptions to this rule would include: if a specialized service is needed, or if the owner/operator chooses to request his/her own service. The officer will request the communication office to call the wrecker of the subject's choice. A reasonable length of time will be given to an owner/operator to obtain the wrecker of his/her choice.
c.
Communications shall keep a record of each time a wrecker is called. The following shall be documented on the record:
1.
Date of call;
2.
Time of call;
3.
Wrecker service called;
4.
If the call is requested wrecker or a rotation wrecker call;
5.
The officer making the request for service;
6.
Location/destination of the wrecker call;
7.
If wrecker is responding or not;
8.
If not, why; and
9.
Operator making the call.
d.
The wrecker service agrees to pay an administrative fee of $30.00 per month. This fee may be paid yearly at the time of purchase of a business license.
(2)
Original applications.
a.
Wrecker service proprietors wishing to have their service included on the police call list shall submit a request to the chief of police.
b.
Any wrecker service utilized by the department shall be properly licensed and insured.
c.
All wrecker and storage facilities shall be inspected by the department and proof of insurance filed prior to being placed on the department call list.
d.
Insurance must be sufficient to compensate for any loss of, or damage to, property entrusted to the wrecker service.
1.
Vehicle liability—Minimum requirements as set by state law.
2.
Garage keeper's legal liability—To cover loss by fire, theft, etc.
e.
Wrecker service operators will cause insurance agents to submit verification of coverage to the chief of police.
f.
Wrecker service will sign a contract with the Milledgeville Police Department to be placed on the list.
(3)
Annual inspection and renewal.
a.
In December, a letter of renewal will be mailed to the wrecker services. The wrecker services will have 30 days to respond to the letter.
b.
All wreckers and storage facilities shall be inspected annually in January.
c.
The annual inspection of wrecker services shall include the checking of equipment, insurance, tow and storage rates, etc.
d.
Certificates of insurance must be submitted at this time.
e.
Insurance carriers shall notify the chief of police within five days if a policy is cancelled.
f.
Any changes of insurance coverage, i.e., changing companies, vehicles, etc., will be submitted to the chief of police within ten days.
g.
The chief of police may have a wrecker service inspected at any time if the circumstances warrant.
h.
After the completion of each inspection, the chief of police shall inform the administrative services division commander whether the wrecker service will be added to or taken off the call list.
i.
The public service commission must certify the wrecker service. Proof of certification will be updated at the same time insurance is validated. Any company suspended by the PSC for any reason will notify the city police department immediately. The company will also be suspended from the rotation list until proof of reinstatement by the PSC is provided.
j.
The wrecker service will provide a copy of current vehicle registration for each truck in operation. This will be updated at the time insurance is validated.
k.
The wrecker service will provide a truck inspection form filled out by a certified mechanic according to DOT regulations for each truck.
The wrecker service must have a current occupation tax.
(4)
Equipment.
a.
It is essential that the wrecker service have a unit capable of highly efficient performance at the incident scene.
b.
Emergency equipment.
1.
At least one amber-colored flashing type light shall be mounted on the top of the wrecker. The department will approve no other color. All emergency flashers and directional lights showing to the front must be amber in color.
2.
Sirens on wreckers or service trucks are prohibited.
c.
The following additional equipment is required:
1.
At least one heavy-duty push broom.
2.
Flood lights on hoist to illuminate the scene at night.
3.
One shovel.
4.
Minimum of one five-pound Class ABC undercover laboratory approved fire extinguisher.
d.
Appearance of wreckers shall be reasonably good.
e.
All tow trucks shall display the firm's name, address, and phone number. Such information shall be painted on or permanently affixed to both sides and such lettering shall be at least three inches high.
Magnetic signs will not be permitted.
f.
It shall be the responsibility of the wrecker service to have equipment for removing glass and other debris from the highway accident scene. It is the responsibility of the wrecker operator to remove such debris from the highway (T.C.A. 55-8-170).
(5)
Storage facilities.
a.
Wrecker services must be equipped to provide a fenced-in lighted lot or building for proper and safe storage.
1.
It must be located on the property of the wrecker service.
2.
The wrecker service will be responsible for the storage, safekeeping, and prevention of vandalism of vehicles and contents.
b.
Records of vehicles towed and charges of calls given from the city police department of police rotation service shall be open to the chief of police.
(6)
Services and procedures.
a.
Tow trucks shall be available for immediate response 24 hours a day.
1.
They must respond in a reasonable length of time or the next scheduled wrecker will be called and the first one will lose their turn. (Reasonable length of time shall be 30 minutes from the time called). If and when the initial wrecker service arrives, the officer will advise the operator that they have lost their turn due to delayed response.
2.
A responding wrecker(s) must be one displaying the firm's name as called for by the department. Tag, title, and insurance must be in the wrecker service named.
3.
Wrecker services cannot refer a call to another wrecker company or substitute another company's wrecker service to avoid losing a turn on the call list.
b.
Wrecker service operators agree not to perform repair work on a "tow-in" without the owner's permission.
c.
Hold orders placed by the department on vehicles stored for any reason shall be honored by the wrecker establishment.
d.
The police department shall give a written release when a hold is lifted.
e.
All other "tow-ins" will be released at the owners request after proof of ownership is established and the necessary financial transactions between vehicle owner and wrecker service are completed.
f.
No wrecker shall remove a wrecked vehicle without it being investigated by a law enforcement agency.
g.
Soliciting at the scene is prohibited by the owner, operator, or representative of any wrecker concern.
h.
All wrecker services shall be prohibited from "chasing" or running wrecks without a bona fide call from the department, dispatcher or request from the owner. Wreckers arriving or stopping at the scene of accidents without being called may assist in clearing the scene, but are prohibited from towing any cars involved in the accident.
i.
If a wrecker operator desires to be off-duty for any length of time, he/she will not lose his/her place on the call list if they advise the department. When returning, they shall be placed back on the rotation list.
j.
A wrecker operator who fails to answer his/her call will lost the call. If they miss three consecutive calls in a 30-day period, an investigation shall be made by the department and suspension or removal will be considered.
k.
An operator who fails to respond promptly to calls may be removed from the call list.
l.
An operator who refuses a call without just reason shall be removed from the call list.
m.
A wrecker service shall not be allowed to pull more than one vehicle from an accident scene unless no other wreckers are available.
n.
When two vehicles are involved and two wreckers are called:
1.
The first wrecker arriving at the scene will tow the vehicle causing the greatest traffic hazard, which shall be determined by the investigating officer.
2.
Wrecker drivers are responsible for cleaning all glass and debris from the streets left as a result of the accident.
Drivers shall check with an officer prior to beginning clean-up activities to ensure evidence is not disturbed.
o.
When a wrecker service must be called and a specific request is not made by the owner/operator of the vehicle, the wrecker rotational schedule maintained by the communication center shall be utilized.
p.
The wrecker service on-call at that particular time shall be called to pull in the vehicle in question.
q.
When a wrecker service is called, that service shall be placed at the bottom of the call list unless the requesting officer advises the communication officer to place that wrecker service back on top of the list.
r.
The following shall be cause for a wrecker service to be placed back on top of the rotation list:
1.
The vehicle is released to the owner or for some reason not towed.
2.
A wrecker company is requested and also the rotation wrecker in the same incident, the service will be used as per request and then placed on top of the rotation list so as not to lose a turn.
3.
When a vehicle that appears to have been abandoned and an officer requests a rotation wrecker and the owner/operator or wrecker service summoned by the owner/operator arrives prior to the rotation wrecker taking the said vehicle, then the vehicle shall be released to the owner/operator, or request wrecker service.
s.
Owners shall not be permitted to operate wrecker equipment under more than one company name, out of one location.
t.
All wrecker service operators are expected to be familiar with and abide by all traffic laws of the state.
(7)
Complaints and grievance process.
a.
The department shall investigate all complaints of service and/or charges.
b.
The administrative division commander shall meet with the parties involved to resolve the complaints.
c.
If the complaint is not resolved during discussions with the administrative division commander, he shall forward a report to the chief of police. The written report shall contain the following principle elements.
1.
What the complaint is about and the facts on which it is based.
2.
The alleged violation of the rule, order, regulation, procedure, right, or harm done.
3.
The remedy or adjustment sought.
4.
Date and time submitted to chief of police.
d.
The chief of police will meet with the parties involved to try to resolve the complaint. If the chief of police cannot resolve the conflict he will forward the report to the public safety committee.
e.
The mayor and council resolution shall be final and the grievance shall be considered resolved.
f.
Any valid complaints of over charges by any wrecker service shall result in the following suspension of towing privileges:
First violation—30 days.
Second violation—Six months.
Third violation—Two years.
Fourth violation—Permanent.
g.
Failure to comply with any rule and regulation may result in a wrecker service being removed from the rotation list by the mayor and council.
Owner/operators of wrecker services shall be notified in writing by the chief of police or his/her designated representative of suspension or removal and a copy forwarded to the mayor and council.
h.
Wrecker rotation grievance procedures.
1.
Only matters involving interpretation, application, or enforcement of wrecker rotation rules and regulations, as they apply to wrecker service owners and all enforcers of the policy set forth for wrecker rotation, shall constitute a grievance under these provisions. Grievances may be pursued/appealed through this process as high as the mayor and council.
A.
A grievance may be brought by administrative division commander or wrecker service representative.
B.
Time limitations are outlined in the grievance process steps. Failure by the aggrieved person to follow time limits will void the grievance. Failure of a superior to follow time limits will be reported in writing, to the chief of police by the aggrieved party. The chief of police shall take steps to ascertain the validity of the time violation and may go ahead and hear the complaint personally.
C.
Responsibility for coordination of the procedures shall rest with the administrative division commander. Once grievances are completed, they will be placed in the individual's/wrecker service's file.
2.
Steps:
A.
Grievances shall be resolved in a timely and orderly manner as outlined in the steps specified by this order.
B.
Step one (nothing in writing needed at this step).
i.
The aggrieved party shall meet with the administrative division commander within seven calendar days of the grievance occurrence or within seven calendar days of his/her knowledge of the occurrence and orally discuss the grievance.
ii.
The commander shall make a decision and orally communicate this decision to the aggrieved party within five calendar days of the presentation of the grievance. Every effort shall be made by the aggrieved and the commander to resolved the grievance at this level.
C.
Step two.
i.
If the grievance is not resolved during discussion with the commander, the aggrieved shall submit a written grievance form within five calendar days following the commanders oral response. The written grievance at this and all subsequent steps shall contain the following principle elements:
(A)
What the grievance is about and the facts on which it is based;
(B)
The alleged violation of the rule, order, regulation, procedure or right and the harm done:
(C)
The remedy or adjustment sought; and
(D)
The signature of the aggrieved and the date of submission.
ii.
The chief of police shall respond in writing to this grievance within five calendar days. Receipts of the grievance shall be acknowledged by the date, time, and signature of the person receiving the grievance. The written response at this and subsequent steps shall contain the following information:
(A)
An analysis of the alleged violation:
(B)
A statement of affirming or denying the allegations in the grievance;
(C)
The remedy or adjustment to be made, if any; and
(D)
Signature of the chief of police and date completed.
iii.
At this and subsequent steps the chief of police may meet with the aggrieved and others to resolve the grievance. The aggrieved will have a second interview with the chief of police upon his/her completion of the grievance.
D.
Step three. The mayor and council shall review the grievance and agree or disagree with the chief of police findings. The mayor and councils resolution shall be final and the grievance shall be considered resolved.
E.
Analysis. An annual review of grievances will be done to determine if a trend exist in filed grievances. The review will be used determine if any changes are needed in this policy.
(8)
Towing charges.
a.
Towing and storage rates shall be determined and established on an annual basis by mayor and council.
b.
The towing rate shall be set per rate schedule.
c.
The storage rate shall be set per rate schedule.
d.
Wrecker services agree to tow city owned vehicles for $35.00.
