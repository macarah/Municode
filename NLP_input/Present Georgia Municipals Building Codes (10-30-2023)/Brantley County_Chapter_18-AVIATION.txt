ARTICLE II. - AIRPORT ADVISORY COMMITTEE[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
Cross reference— Authorities, boards and commissions, § 2-61 et seq.

Sec. 18-31. - Establishment of an airport advisory committee.
(a)
There is established and created for the county a committee to be known as the county airport advisory committee (referred to in this article as the committee).
(b)
The committee shall consist of five members, Posts 1 through 5, who shall be appointed by the board of commissioners from citizens of the county who have knowledge and interest in aviation, and/or property maintenance and/or development.
(c)
The term for a member of the committee shall be for a period of three years, except for the initial term, which shall be as follows: Posts 1 and 2 shall be for periods of one year; Posts 3 and 4 shall be for periods of two years; and Post 5 shall be for a period of three years. Any member may be reappointed for an unlimited number of successive terms.
(d)
The committee shall organize itself at its initial meeting by the election of a chairman, vice chairman and secretary. The committee shall meet in a facility provided for it by the county, at times as set by the members of the committee, or upon the call of the chair; the committee shall hold it meetings in compliance with the state Open Meetings Act (O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1 et seq.). The committee shall hold its first meeting within 15 days of the final appointment of all of its initial members.
(e)
Any issue coming before the committee for a vote must receive the votes of three members to pass, with the chair having the authority to vote on all matters. Three members of the committee shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of any business. The committee shall function under the Robert's Rules of Order.
(f)
The members of the committee shall serve without compensation, but shall be entitled to reimbursement for actual expenses that may be incurred in carrying out his duties as a member of the committee. Such expenses shall be paid from the general funds of the county on the filing of a claim for such expenses in the manner as required by county policy, and upon approval of the board of commissioners.
(g)
Neither the committee nor any member thereof shall have the authority or power to make any purchases or otherwise bind the county to any financial obligation or undertaking, such authority being hereby expressly prohibited and reserved unto the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 11-13-2001, § I)
Sec. 18-32. - Powers, duties and responsibilities.
(a)
The members of the committee may adopt bylaws for the committee.
(b)
The committee shall investigate, review, consider and recommend to the board of commissioners any use, operation, maintenance, development, regulations, plans and/or other matters directly relating to, concerning or affecting the county airport.
(c)
The committee shall recommend an annual budget to the board of commissioners relating to the county's airport.
(d)
The committee may do such other things as are necessary and proper to effectuate the purpose of this article and is not expressly prohibited herein.
(Res. of 11-13-2001, § II)

ARTICLE III. - CROP DUSTING

Sec. 18-61. - Permit required for certain uses of county airport.
No person may use any of the facilities, runway or any other part or portion of the county airport for any crop duster services, to include the landing, takeoff, fueling, refueling, or the loading or unloading of any materials used in crop dusting or other aerial fertilizer application, without first obtaining a permit for any of the activities specified in this section in accordance with the provisions of this article. A permit shall not be issued for a period of time greater than 14 days.
(Ord. of 11-12-2002, § 1)
Sec. 18-62. - Bond required for certain uses of county airport.
No person may be granted a permit to use any of the facilities, runway or any other part or portion of the county airport for any crop duster services, to include the landing, takeoff, fueling, refueling, or the loading or unloading of any materials used in crop dusting or other aerial fertilizer application, without first posting a bond in the amount of $500.00 to cover any required cleanup or repairs of the airport as a result of the use of the county's airport for any of the uses set forth in this section or in section 18-61. The bond must be in a form approved by the county attorney, and secured by the deposit of cash or a bank's cashier's check payable to the county in the amount of $500.00.
(Ord. of 11-12-2002, § 2)
Sec. 18-63. - Permit application, application fee and issuance of permit.
(a)
Any person desiring to obtain a permit for any of the activities specified in sections 18-61 and 18-62 in accordance with the provisions of this article shall make application to the board of commissioners in the form as established by the clerk of the board and pay a permit fee of $15.00.
(b)
The application shall contain, at a minimum, the name, address and telephone number of the applicant; an identification of the aircraft to be used, including the FAA's (Federal Aviation Administration) identification number; the name and address of the owner of the aircraft to be used in the aerial application service; the name and address of the owner of the property upon which an aerial application of any material is to be applied; an identification of the product to be applied by aerial application; and the date and time the airport will be used under the permit.
(c)
The application shall be reviewed by the chairman of the board of commissioners, or the clerk of the board if the chairman is unavailable to consider and respond to the application within 48 hours, who shall, if the application is complete and the bond has been given and secured as required, issue the permit listing thereon the effective date of the permit.
(Ord. of 11-12-2002, § 3)
Sec. 18-64. - Penalties.
(a)
Any person violating this article, or any provision hereof, shall, upon conviction, be punished by a fine of not less than $250.00 and not more than $1,000.00, and/or a sentence of one to 60 days in the county jail. The magistrate court shall have authority to permit the sentenced party to perform community service in lieu of payment of that portion of a fine imposed above $250.00 at the rate of $6.00 per hour; however, this would not include any statutory imposed fine add-ons. The performance of such community service shall be in compliance with such conditions as the magistrate may order.
(b)
No person accused of violating this article shall be arrested prior to trial, but any person who fails to appear for trial shall be arrested thereafter on the warrant of the magistrate, and required to post bond for his future appearance.
(c)
Violations of this article shall be tried upon citations which shall be issued to an alleged violator of any provisions of this article. Such citation shall state the time and place at which the accused is to appear for trial, shall identify the offense with which the accused is charged, shall have an identifying number by which it shall be filed with the court, shall indicate the identity of the accused and the date of service, and shall be signed by the representative of the county who completes and serves it. The citation may be served by certified mail, return receipt requested.
(d)
Citations issued under this article may be tried without a prosecuting attorney; however, if the prosecuting attorney is needed, the county attorney shall serve as the prosecuting attorney.
(e)
Violations of this chapter shall be tried in the magistrate court of the county, and shall be tried in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq.
(f)
Nothing in this article shall prevent the board of commissioners from bringing any civil action for injunction, or other proceedings to abate any violation of this article or other state or federal law. No sanction, penalty or remedy prescribed in this section shall be considered exclusive of any other remedy, but shall be available in addition to any other sanction, penalty or remedy provided by law.
(g)
Each violation of this article shall constitute a separate offense.
(Ord. of 11-12-2002, § 4)

ARTICLE IV. - AIRPORT RULES AND REGULATIONS

Sec. 18-91. - Provisions saved from repeal.
The rules and regulations governing the county airport as adopted November 7, 1972, and amended by resolution on May 9, 1995, are not repealed and shall continue in full force and effect pending a revision of the subject matter by the county.
