ARTICLE II. - AUXILIARY POLICE FORCE

Sec. 58-31. - Composition.
The auxiliary police force will be composed of a chief, assistant chief and three sergeants. Such officers will be appointed by regulations set forth by the governing board of the auxiliary police.
(Code 1976, § 25-16)
Sec. 58-32. - Rules and regulations.
The auxiliary police force will be governed by the rules and regulations of the city police department, with the exception of ownership of specific businesses or employment of such force. Any change or exceptions to the rules and regulations will be by approval of the chief of police.
(Code 1976, § 25-18)
Sec. 58-33. - Personnel qualifications.
To be eligible for membership in the auxiliary police force a person shall:
(1)
Be at least 21 years of age.
(2)
Be a citizen of the United States and a resident of the city.
(3)
Have a high school diploma or its recognized equivalent.
(4)
Not have been convicted by any state court or the federal government of any crime, the punishment for which could have been imprisonment in the federal or state prison; nor shall he have been convicted of sufficient offenses to establish a pattern of disregard for the law.
(5)
Be fingerprinted and a search made of local, state and national fingerprint files to disclose any criminal record.
(6)
Possess good moral character as determined by investigation under the procedure established by a three-member board of the auxiliary police force and the chief of police.
(Code 1976, § 25-19)
Sec. 58-34. - Compensation; uniforms and equipment; expenditures and expenses.
(a)
All members of the auxiliary police force will work without compensation in any manner.
(b)
All uniforms and any other equipment needed by members of the auxiliary police will be furnished by the members and at no cost to the police department or the city.
(c)
The auxiliary police force shall not make any expenditures or incur any expenses whatsoever on behalf of the city.
(Code 1976, § 25-21)
