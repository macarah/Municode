Sec. 31-1. - Scope of chapter.
The rules and regulations in this chapter shall govern payment by consumers for water furnished such consumers by the water system of the city and its water service territory.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-2. - Water rates and charges not affected by Code.
Nothing in this Code or the ordinance adopting this Code shall affect any ordinance relating to rates and charges under this chapter, and all such ordinances are hereby recognized as continuing in full force and effect to the same extent as if set out at length in this Code.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-3. - Water charges.
The schedule of charges for water service effective in the city shall be fixed from time to time by the mayor and council.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-4. - Deposit before service turned on—Required.
Before water service is turned on and city utility services are provided, utility deposits shall be made with the city according to a schedule of deposits and subject to exceptions and provisions as adopted in policy.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-5. - Deposit—Refund.
(a)
If a deposit is required, it shall be refunded without interest, or applied as a credit to an open account, at the end of twelve (12) consecutive months of on-time and in-full payment of the bills for all water services. Any remainder shall also be refunded without interest upon termination of service by the customer and payment in-full of all outstanding charges.
(b)
Due date. All refunds shall be paid or credited to an open account within 30 days after the finance director or designee determines that such refund is due.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-6. - Use of mails for billing and payment.
All bills and accounts for the amounts charged for water service and water used from the city's water system shall be mailed to the respective consumers, with proper postage affixed and addressed to the consumer at the address where such water service may be furnished; however, neither the city nor its employees shall be responsible for such bills and water accounts not reaching customers, nor for such remittance made through the mails therefor not reaching the city in due time.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-7. - Tampering and theft of services.
(a)
Under no circumstances is any customer or individual authorized to turn water back on or alter services if water service has been disconnected by the city. Only authorized city staff or contracted employees may connect services or work on city lines.
(b)
All persons are prohibited from interfering with or disturbing in any manner or form, any water meters, radio equipment, switches, locks, cutoffs or other appliances of the city, including fire hydrants, or to appropriate or use any water after service has been cut off or discontinued for nonpayment or in order to avoid payment.
(c)
The property owner, legal tenant, or person(s) noted on the utility account shall be held responsible for the violation of this section. A tampering fine of five hundred dollars ($500.00) shall be assessed where a meter or water line has been disturbed. A tampering fine of seven hundred and fifty dollars ($750.00) shall be assessed for tampering with a fire hydrant or any fire meter.
(d)
The City of Winder reserves the right to prosecute in the accordance with established laws after any tampering of water services has been discovered.
(e)
If, after removal of service, an alternative means of obtaining water service is attempted (such as a "jumper" or straight piping), then the water service to that property will be removed until such time as the case can appear and be settled before the city's municipal court.
(f)
If the city determines theft of service has occurred, it reserves the right to adjust the customer's current bill and the bills for the past six (6) months usage. If the approximate amount of service that was stolen cannot be reasonably determined, the customer's usage will be set at three (3) times the average volume.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-8. - Damage to meter and radio equipment.
(a)
Customers shall be responsible to ensure that city equipment is not damaged through the routine maintenance adjacent to city equipment/property. The property owner may be deemed responsible for damages to city meters and equipment.
(b)
The city reserves the right to assess the cost of the damaged equipment to the utility account.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-9. - Responsibility for damages and maintenance to service lines.
The city shall in no event be responsible for maintaining any service line owned by the consumer, nor for damage done by water escaping therefrom, nor for defects in lines or fixtures, on the property of the consumer. The consumer shall at all times comply with all regulations of the city and of regulations relating to the service lines, and shall make all changes in the line as required on account of change in grade or otherwise.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-10. - Wasting water; repair of leaks.
(a)
Whenever it comes to the attention of the utilities department that any service connection upon the property of any owner is leaking, the utilities department shall serve written notice upon the property owner requiring the owner to repair the condition. If the property owner does not repair the leak within five (5) days after receiving the notice, the city shall be authorized to disconnect water service until notification that the leak has been corrected and has been verified.
(b)
The city administrator or designee, upon investigation of any leaks or waste that may jeopardize the city's water system or deemed a nuisance, will be disconnected immediately.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-11. - Suspension of service.
(a)
When water service is discontinued and all bills paid, the security deposit shall be refunded to the consumer by the city.
(b)
Upon discontinuance of service for nonpayment of bills, the security deposit will be applied by the city toward the settlement of the account. Any balance will be refunded to the consumer; however, if the security deposit is insufficient to cover the bill, the city may proceed to collect the balance in the usual way provided by law for collection of debts.
(c)
The city reserves the right to discontinue its service without notice for the following additional reasons:
(1)
To prevent fraud or abuse;
(2)
Consumer's willful disregard of the city's policy;
(3)
Emergency repairs;
(4)
Insufficiency of water supply due to circumstances beyond the city's control;
(5)
Legal processes;
(6)
Direction of public authorities;
(7)
Strike, riot, fire, flood or unavoidable accident.
(d)
Service disconnected for nonpayment of bills will be restored only after bills are paid in full, including the service charge (delinquent fee) in an amount set forth in the schedule of fees and charges to be paid for each meter reconnected.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-12. - Cross connection and backflow prevention.
(a)
Employees of the city water system are authorized to discontinue water service when necessary to prevent contamination of the public water supply or public water system due to possible cross connections or when it is necessary to protect the water system or individual properties from emergency cross connection or backflow situations.
(b)
The city water system and utilities department is further authorized to install or require to be installed suitable backflow prevention devices at the customer's service connection or other areas as needed to prevent contamination or the risk of contamination of the public water system.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-13. - Installation of meters.
(a)
Upon payment of all associated fees, the city shall install water meters on the premises or property occupied by all users of water furnished by the city, both within and without the corporate limits of the city. All users of water shall pay the entire expense of such installation in advance.
(b)
The city administrator is authorized to allow a customer of an existing residence who is currently on a well and requesting to switch to city water to enter into payment arrangements not to exceed six (6) months.
(c)
In all multi-unit residential developments, each unit shall be individually metered.
(d)
Location of installation. Meters shall be installed as close to the property line of the customer as possible. All connections shall be inspected by a designated city employee from the utilities department. Only those properties where meters have been installed will be furnished water from the city's water system.
(e)
Access to premises. An authorized employee of the city shall have during times of normal operations noted between 7:00 a.m. and 7:00 p.m. free access to the premises for the purpose of reading the meter or removing the same for the purpose of testing its accuracy.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-14. - Cost of water meters and installations thereof.
(a)
The city shall charge water customers for making taps in the city water mains according to the fees established by the mayor and council.
(b)
When the size of the meter is upgraded for any reason, the cost associated with the new meter size must be paid in full prior to any work being started.
(c)
When a customer requests a reduction in meter size, there will be no charge to the customer for the reduction of meters two (2) inches and below. Meters larger than two (2) inches will be handled according to a fee established by the mayor and council.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-15. - Service to fire suppression systems.
(a)
All new customers who request fire suppression service shall install a separate line.
(b)
The customer shall not be permitted to take water from the fire suppression system except for fire suppression purposes.
(c)
Monthly fees for fire suppression service will be charged in accordance with a schedule of fees established by the mayor and council.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-16. - Water construction standards.
(a)
General. All extensions of the water system shall be designed and built in accordance with current city standards. The standards shall be those stated in the latest edition of the City of Winder's Minimum Standards, which is available at the utilities department. No installation of pipe or other materials for water extensions shall be allowed until the required information is received and the design is approved by the City of Winder. Inspection and acceptance procedures shall be specified in the city's minimum standards.
(b)
Other regulations. Design and construction of water system extensions shall comply with all local, state, and federal rules and regulations pertaining to water line installation, including but not limited to rules and regulations for safety, erosion and sediment control, stream buffer protection, wetlands and water quality standards.
(c)
Authority. The mayor and city council are hereby authorized to issue and from time to time amend the water installation regulations and specifications.
(d)
Enforcement. Failure to comply with the provisions of this section may result in enforcement and fines as described in this chapter.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-17. - Ownership of water meters and service lines.
(a)
The City of Winder retains all ownership of water meters connected to the city's water system.
(b)
No applicant named on the utility bill or person associated with the property connected to the water distribution system shall sell, transfer or assign water rights to the City of Winder's system unless authorized in writing by the City of Winder.
(c)
Only city employees or those directly authorized by the city may work on water meters, water lines maintained by the city and/or tap into city water lines.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-18. - Restriction on outdoor watering of landscape.
(a)
Outdoor watering for purposes of planting, growing, managing, or maintaining ground cover, trees, shrubs, or other plants in the City of Winder water service area may occur only between the hours of 4:00 p.m. and 10:00 a.m.; provided, however, that this limitation shall not create any limitation upon the following outdoor water uses:
(1)
Commercial raising, harvesting, or storing of crops; feeding, breeding, or managing livestock or poultry; the commercial production or storing of feed for use in the production of livestock, including, but not limited to, cattle, calves, swine, hogs, goats, sheep, and rabbits, or for use in the production of poultry, including, but not limited to, chickens, hens, ratites, and turkeys; producing plants, trees, fowl, or animals; or the commercial production of aquacultural, horticultural, dairy, livestock, poultry, eggs, and apiarian products or as otherwise defined in O.C.G.A. § 1-3-3;
(2)
Capture and reuse of cooling system condensate or stormwater in compliance with applicable City of Winder ordinances and state guidelines;
(3)
Reuse of gray water in compliance with O.C.G.A. § 31-3-5.2 and applicable local board of health regulations;
(4)
Use of reclaimed wastewater by a designated user from a system permitted by the Environmental Protection Division of the Georgia Department of Natural Resources to provide reclaimed wastewater;
(5)
Watering personal food gardens;
(6)
Watering new and replanted plant, seed, or turf in landscapes, golf courses, or sports turf fields during installation and for a period of thirty (30) days immediately following the date of installation;
(7)
Drip irrigation or irrigation using soaker hoses;
(8)
Hand watering with a hose with automatic cutoff or handheld container;
(9)
Use of water withdrawn from private water wells or surface water by an owner or operator of property is such well or surface water is on said property;
(10)
Watering horticultural crops held for sale, resale, or installation;
(11)
Watering athletic fields, golf courses, or public turf grass recreational areas;
(12)
Installation, maintenance, or calibration of irrigation systems;
(13)
Hydroseeding.
(b)
No person shall use or allow the use of water in violation of the restrictions on outdoor water use contained in this section. Violations of this section shall be punishable in accordance with section 31-22 of this Code.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-19. - Emergency restrictions of service.
The utilities director is authorized and empowered to implement water conservation measures during emergency conditions including implementation of such outdoor water use restrictions as may be recommended by state officials during such times and days as he deems necessary in any area served by the water system. When such water use restrictions are imposed, notice shall be given through the public media and published in the legal organ of the city.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-20. - Water service policies.
In addition to all other provisions of this chapter, water services shall also be subject to such water service policies adopted by the mayor and council from time to time.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-21. - Customers outside the city limits.
(a)
Water customers located outside the corporate boundaries of the City of Winder are subject to the rules and regulations contained in this chapter.
(b)
The property owners shall pay all applicable tap fees to make the water available to the property.
(c)
When a property owner requests to connect onto the city's water system, and the property to be served is located outside of the corporate boundaries the city, the property owner shall meet one (1) of the following requirements before construction of water lines and connection to the city water system shall begin, and such construction and connection shall begin only with the authorization of the city administrator and approval in advance by a formal vote of the mayor and city council:
(1)
If the property is contiguous to the existing limits of the city, the property owner shall petition the city for annexation of the property to be served by the city prior to commencement of construction or connection of water lines; or
(2)
If the property is not contiguous with the existing limits of the city, the property owner shall execute a restrictive covenant consenting to annexation of such property into the city when the property becomes contiguous. The property owner shall also be required to submit a certificate of title to the city attorney's office to verify ownership of the property. The restrictive covenant will be recorded in the property records at the county courthouse and such covenant shall stipulate that if the property becomes contiguous with the existing limits of the city at a future date and the property owner refuses to petition for annexation, the city will have the authority to terminate water service.
The requirement for annexation may be waived by the mayor and council at the city's option.
(d)
Should a property owner whose property has been connected to the city's water system pursuant to the requirements of section 31-21(c)(2) refuse to petition for annexation by the city when their property becomes contiguous with the city's corporate limits, or meet any other requirement of section 31-21(c)(2), the city may terminate water service and disconnect that property from the city water system until such time as a petition for annexation is filed.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1; Ord. (W-3-21) of 11-18-21, §§ 1, 2)
Sec. 31-22. - Penalties.
(a)
Civil penalties. Violations of this chapter may be prosecuted upon citations issued by officers of the city police department. The violation of any provision of this chapter shall be punishable by a fine for each offense or violation not to exceed one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) for each offense or violation.
(b)
Criminal penalties. Knowingly violating any provision of this chapter which poses or could pose a threat to public health or safety shall be considered a criminal act and shall be punishable by imprisonment and fines commensurate with the risk to the public as determined by the judiciary.
(c)
Additional penalties. In addition to civil and criminal penalties, any person violating any provision of this chapter may be liable to pay restitution in the discretion of the court for:
(1)
The total cost incurred by the city to repair or replace facilities damaged as a result of the violation;
(2)
Any injury or damage to person or property or loss of services resulting from the violation.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-23. - Collection costs for delinquent accounts.
Customers that have a past due balance after disconnection are subject to being referred to collection agencies and are required to pay all fees as permitted by law.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
Sec. 31-24. - Charges constituting lien.
The charges for water shall be a lien upon the account holder as provided for in the charter of the city. Whenever a bill for water service remains unpaid sixty (60) days after it has been disconnected, the city clerk may file with the clerk of the Superior Court of Barrow County, a statement of lien claim. This statement shall contain a legal description of the premises served, the amount of the unpaid bill, and a notice that the city claims a lien for this amount as well as for all charges for water service subsequent to the period covered by the bill.
(Ord. (W-12-13) of 10-21-13, § 1)
