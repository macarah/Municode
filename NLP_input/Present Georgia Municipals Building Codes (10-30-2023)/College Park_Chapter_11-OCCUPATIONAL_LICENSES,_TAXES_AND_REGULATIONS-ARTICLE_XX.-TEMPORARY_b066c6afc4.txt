ARTICLE XX. - TEMPORARY AGENCIES

Sec. 11-325. - Definitions.
For the purposes of this article, the term:
(1)
Labor pool means the business entity which operates by:
a.
Contracting with other entities or persons to supply them with temporary employees for short-term assignments of labor:
b.
Hiring persons to fulfill these contracts for short-term assignments of labor, and requiring such persons to come to the labor pool business location to receive an assignment; and
c.
Employing each individual employee no longer than the time period required to complete the assignment for which that individual employee was hired, although an individual may be eligible for rehire when additional assignments are available.
(2)
Short term assignment of labor means a work assignment for a term of forty (40) hours or less where an individual is assigned to work for a business entity by a labor pool or temporary agency.
(3)
Temporary agency means a business entity which operates by:
a.
Contracting with other entities or persons to supply them with temporary employees for short-term assignments of labor;
b.
Hiring persons to fulfill these contracts, but not requiring such persons to come to the temporary agency business location to receive an assignment; and
c.
Employing each individual employee for the time period required to complete the assignment for which that individual was hired, although an individual may be eligible for rehire when additional temporary assignments are available.
(4)
Temporary employees means individuals who are assigned by a labor pool or a temporary agency to complete a work assignment with another entity or person.
(Ord. No. 97-03, § 1, 2-3-97)
Sec. 11-326. - License requirement; procedure.
(a)
Any person desiring to operate a labor pool within the city shall apply for and obtain a license from the city clerk. The annual fee for such license shall be two hundred dollars ($200.00).
(b)
The application shall include a scale drawing of the premises, including interior walls, and also any drop-off areas and parking areas reserved for the licensed establishment.
(Ord. No. 97-03, § 1, 2-3-97)
Sec. 11-327. - Locational and structural requirements.
Labor Pools shall be subject to the provisions of the zoning code, this article and the following standards of use.
(1)
No labor pool shall be located within one thousand (1,000) feet of an existing labor pool.
(2)
No labor pool shall be located within one thousand five hundred (1,500) feet of the property boundaries of residentially-zoned property or schools.
(3)
Labor pools shall be located only on property which is zoned C-2, C-3 or Industrial under the City Ordinance.
(4)
The number of licenses issued for labor pools within the city shall not exceed one (1) license for each five thousand (5,000) citizens, or fraction thereof, according to the United States Census of 1990, or any future such census.
(Ord. No. 97-03, § 1, 2-3-97)
Sec. 11-328. - Operating standards; conduct; exceptions.
(a)
The labor pool must have sufficient seating inside the establishment for all temporary employees who report to the business for job assignments, and each temporary employee shall remain inside the establishment while he or she is waiting for an assignment. In no event shall temporary employees congregate outside of a labor pool while they are waiting for an assignment.
(b)
Labor pools shall have separate restroom facilities for men and women of sufficient size to accommodate all persons awaiting employment. Such restrooms shall be handicapped accessible in accordance with state and federal standards.
(c)
Labor pools shall have sufficient dressing rooms or locker rooms for both men and women for all temporary employees to change clothes before reporting to or returning from work. The manager of such labor pool will be responsible for ensuring such facilities are used by temporary employees in connection with all work assignments.
(d)
Each labor pool must maintain general liability insurance and premises liability insurance in the minimum amount of one hundred thousand dollars ($100,000.00) per occurrence.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a labor pool between 8:00 p.m. and 5:00 a.m. of the following day or to permit a temporary employee to remain in such establishment between such prohibited hours, except regular employees performing necessary labor on the premises.
(f)
Each labor pool shall have a properly configured area on the property where temporary employees can be safely picked up and dropped off. Any such area shall not include any right-of-way immediately adjacent to the property or any adjoining property which is not owned or leased by the agency.
(Ord. No. 97-03, § 1, 2-3-97)
Sec. 11-329. - Violation; penalties.
In the event that any owner or operator of a labor pool is found to be in violation of the provisions of this article, he shall be cited for such violation and, upon conviction, shall be punished by a fine or imprisonment, or both, as established by section 1-8 of the Code of Ordinances.
(Ord. No. 97-03, § 1, 2-3-97)
