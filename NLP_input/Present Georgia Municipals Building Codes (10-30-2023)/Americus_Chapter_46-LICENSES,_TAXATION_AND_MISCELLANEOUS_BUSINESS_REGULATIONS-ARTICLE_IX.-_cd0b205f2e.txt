ARTICLE IX. - TRAVELING AND ITINERANT DEALERS OF GOLD AND SILVER[6]

Footnotes:
--- (6) ---
State Law reference— Dealers in precious metals and gems, O.C.G.A. § 47-37-1 et seq.

Sec. 46-330. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Itinerant or traveling dealer, salesperson or buyer means a person who does not have a business license in the city or shall enter the city for any period of time and advertise by any form of media communication to the public that such person will buy, sell, trade, or otherwise trade in and receive precious metals, gold, silver, coins and jewelry.
(Code 1962, § 13-72; Code 1986, § 14-60)
Sec. 46-331. - Applicability.
This article shall be applicable to all traveling and itinerant dealers, salespersons, and buyers of gold, silver, jewelry, coins and other precious metals, whether said sales, trades and purchases are retail, wholesale, or by any form of pawn. Specifically excluded are wholesalers; buyers and sellers, who sell, trade and deal only with mercantile establishments and buyers; and sellers who have permanent mercantile establishments.
(Code 1962, § 13-71; Code 1986, § 14-61)
Sec. 46-332. - Notice of intent; required information.
(a)
Prior to advertising any buying, selling, trading, or otherwise dealing in precious metals, gold, silver, coins, or jewelry, the person shall, at least 72 hours prior to the initial advertising, file with the chief of police a written notice of intent to conduct business in the city.
(b)
Such notice shall include the following:
(1)
Full name of the person and such person's address.
(2)
The name of the person who will be in the city and such person's address.
(3)
The length of the stay in the city, including the dates of opening and closing.
(4)
Where business will be conducted and during what hours.
(Code 1962, § 13-73; Code 1986, § 14-62)
Sec. 46-333. - Records and information to be maintained.
All traveling and itinerant dealers, salespersons, and buyers of gold, silver, coins, jewelry, and other precious metals shall keep books wherein shall be entered an accurate description of all property pledged, traded, or sold by them. Such description shall include, to the extent possible, the name or the make of the article, any identifying mark or number and a statement of the kind of material of which it is made. In such books there shall be entered also the full name and address of the person by whom same was deposited or sold, and the time this was done. Such entries shall be made as soon after the transaction as is possible, and in no event more than four hours thereafter.
(Code 1962, § 13-74; Code 1986, § 14-63)
Sec. 46-334. - Report to police; contents, submission.
(a)
Every traveling and itinerant dealer, salesperson, or buyer shall make a written report to the police department no less than 24 hours after completion of its business in the city. The report shall be in such form as may be prescribed by the chief of police, and of all property pledged, traded, bought, or sold during the period of operation in the city. In addition to any other information required by the chief of police, they shall show:
(1)
The name and address of any person who bought, sold, traded, or otherwise disposed of or received any item;
(2)
The date and time of the transaction;
(3)
The amount paid or advanced, or the item traded;
(4)
The full description of articles, including kind, style, material, color, design, kind and number of stones in jewelry, and all identifying names, ranks, and numbers; and
(5)
A description of persons trading, buying, selling or pawning, including the name, address, race, weight and height.
(b)
Such report shall be delivered to the police department no less than 24 hours after completion of business. It shall be neat and legible.
(Code 1962, § 13-75; Code 1986, § 14-64)
Sec. 46-335. - Dealing with minors.
It shall be unlawful for any traveling or itinerant dealer, salesperson, or buyer to receive in pawn, trade, or to buy from minors or to sell to minors, goods of any character or description. A minor, for the purposes of this section, is an individual 17 years of age or under.
(Code 1962, § 13-77; Code 1986, § 14-65)
Sec. 46-336. - Receiving or possessing prohibited items.
No traveling or itinerant dealer, salesperson, or buyer shall take in pawn, or receive, sell, trade, or exchange any of the following named articles: knucks, dirk, sword-cane, blackjack, bowie knife, spring-back knife, switchblade knife, or any knife with a blade exceeding four inches in length, but it is not the intention thereof to prohibit case knives, butcher knives or other knives customarily used for domestic or industrial purposes only; nor shall they own or keep on hand any of the weapons named or referred to in this section.
(Code 1962, § 13-78; Code 1986, § 14-66)
Sec. 46-337. - Articles not to be disposed of seven days after acquisition.
All traveling and itinerant dealers, salespersons, and buyers of gold, silver, jewelry, coins and other precious metals shall hold such goods so pledged, traded, or purchased by them for at least seven days before disposing of them in any way.
(Code 1962, § 13-79; Code 1986, § 14-67)
Sec. 46-338. - Surrender of stolen property.
In the event it is determined that any item bought, sold, or traded to, or received by the traveling or itinerant dealer, salesperson or buyer is the subject of any theft or crime, the dealer, salesperson, or itinerant buyer shall, upon demand of the chief of police, or the chief's designee, surrender the same to the police department.
(Code 1962, § 13-76; Code 1986, § 14-68)
Sec. 46-339. - Penalties; suspension or revocation of authority to do business.
Any person who violates any provision of this article shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-8. Further, any person failing to comply with any provision of this article, or such other laws or ordinances as may be passed by the city council shall, by vote of the city council, be barred from conducting business in the city until such time as they fully comply with this article.
(Code 1962, § 13-82; Code 1986, § 14-69)
