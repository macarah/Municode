ARTICLE II. - ENFORCEMENT

Sec. 7-26. - Applicability.
Pursuant to authority granted to municipalities under O.C.G.A. Title 48, the city council declares that the provisions of this article shall govern the prosecution of individuals who fail to pay special taxes, occupation taxes or license fees, pursuant to the various license, occupation tax, or business regulation ordinances, to the city.
(Ord. of 6-18-90, § 1; Ord. of 8-8-11)
Sec. 7-27. - Issuance of citations.
To ensure compliance with the provisions of this Code and the ordinances of the city that require the payment of special taxes, occupational taxes, and license fees, the city police department and/or the city clerk and/or a duly authorized agent of the city clerk are hereby empowered to cite violators for the various ordinances of the city providing for license fees, occupation taxes and special taxes related to certain businesses to the municipal court. Such citations shall state the provision of the specific ordinance allegedly violated by the individual cited.
(Ord. of 6-18-90, § 2(a); Ord. of 3-8-93; Ord. of 7-12-10; Ord. of 8-8-11)
Sec. 7-28. - Prosecution of violators.
The municipal court of the city is empowered to prosecute persons violating the provisions of this Code and ordinances of the city by failing to pay the special taxes, occupation taxes or license fees or in any other way violating the license, occupation tax, or business regulation ordinances of the city. This remedy shall be in addition to any other remedies provided for by local law or by state law.
(Ord. of 6-18-90, § 2(b); Ord. of 8-8-11)
Sec. 7-29. - Penalties.
Any person who violates any provisions of the license, occupation tax, or business regulation ordinances of the city may, upon conviction, be punished as provided in such ordinance or in the absence of such provision, by a fine not to exceed one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) for each offense and/or imprisonment for a period not to exceed sixty (60) days.
(Ord. of 6-18-90, § 3; Ord. of 8-8-11)
