Sec. 58-19. - Adopted; penalty.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-372 through 40-6-376, the provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-1 through 40-6-395, known as the Uniform Rules of the Road, and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 are adopted as and for the traffic regulations of this county, with like effect as if recited in this section.
(b)
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this section shall be punished in accordance with section 1-11.
(Code 2000, § 58-31)

ARTICLE III. - TRUCK ROUTES

Sec. 58-42. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
City means the City of Fitzgerald.
Deviating truck means a truck that leaves and departs from a truck route while traveling within the city as permitted in this article.
Truck means any vehicle designed or operated for the transportation of property, including raw materials, and having more than six wheels.
Truck route means any street or road within the county designated as a truck route by resolution of the board of commissioners.
Vehicle, for the purposes of determination of weight or number of axles, includes any tractor-trailer, which together shall comprise one vehicle.
(Code 2000, § 58-66; Ord. No. 88-2, § I, 9-6-1988)
Sec. 58-43. - Purpose.
It is the purpose of this article to:
(1)
Protect the public safety and the use of the streets and roads of the county;
(2)
Facilitate and expedite traffic within the county; and
(3)
Protect the streets and roads of the county from unusual wear and damage.
(Code 2000, § 58-67; Ord. No. 88-2, § V, 9-6-1988)
Sec. 58-44. - Application of article.
(a)
Restricted operation. Except as specifically set out in this article, no truck shall be operated over any public street or road in the county other than a truck route.
(b)
Exceptions.
(1)
Any truck may deviate from a truck route and operate over a prohibited street or road where necessary to reach a destination point in the county or the city for the conduct of business, provided truck routes and state or federal highways and Merrimac, Monitor, Sultana and Roanoke Drives (hereinafter referred to as "drive" or "drives") are used until reaching the intersection of the prohibited street or road nearest such destination.
(2)
Any truck may be operated over a prohibited street or road where necessary to leave a point of origin within the county or the city for the conduct of business, provided it leaves such prohibited street or road at the nearest intersection of a truck route, state or federal highway, or drive, to such point of origin.
(3)
Any truck may be operated over a prohibited street or road if owned or operated by the city or county, or if operated by any public utility, contractor or materialman while engaged in repair, maintenance or construction of streets, street improvements or utilities within the county or city.
(Code 2000, § 58-68; Ord. No. 88-2, § II, 9-6-1988)
Sec. 58-45. - Enforcement.
(a)
The county clerk shall keep and maintain accurate maps setting out truck routes. The map shall be kept on file in the office of the county clerk and shall be available to the public.
(b)
The sheriff shall cause those roads upon which truck traffic is prohibited to be clearly signed and posted to give notice that this article is in effect.
(Code 2000, § 58-69; Ord. No. 88-2, § III, 9-6-1988)
Sec. 58-46. - Violations.
Any person violating a provision of this article shall be tried in the probate court of the county and upon conviction shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be punished as provided in section 1-11.
(Code 2000, § 58-70; Ord. No. 88-2, § IV, 9-6-1988)
