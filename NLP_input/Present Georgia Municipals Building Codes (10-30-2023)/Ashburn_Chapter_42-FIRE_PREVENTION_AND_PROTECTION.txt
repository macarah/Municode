ARTICLE III. - BURNING ORDINANCE[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
Editor's note— Ord. No. 06-02, §§ 1—5, adopted July 6, 2006, did not specifically amend this Code but is treated as amending art. III, §§ 42-61—42-66, in its entirety. Formerly, said article pertained to similar subject matter.

Sec. 42-61. - Burning permitted; prohibited.
Subject to the procedures, rules and regulations hereinafter set forth, burning of natural vegetation (trees, brush, stumps, grass, shrubs, etc.) for land clearing can be allowed. However, it is illegal to burn household garbage, plastics, tires, asphalt materials, construction materials, processed lumber, petroleum products, other manmade materials, or hazardous materials/waste products.
(Ord. No. 06-02, § 1, 7-6-2006)
Sec. 42-62. - Permit procedures.
On the form supplied by the city, persons desiring to burn natural vegetation will submit a completed application for a burn permit to Ashburn Fire and Emergency Services.
(Ord. No. 06-02, § 2, 7-6-2006)
Sec. 43-63. - Burn requirements and conditions.
(a)
The location of the burn must be a minimum of 500 feet from structures or public streets unless otherwise varied/allowed by the city fire chief.
(b)
Only natural vegetation (trees, bush, stumps, grass, shrubs, etc.) that contains small amounts of soil shall be burned.
(c)
Open land burning if permitted will require a plowed fire break, and the burn permit recipient must take the necessary measures to ensure that the fire does not spread onto the property of others.
(d)
Permitted burning must not be left unattended at any time.
(e)
Burn permits may only be issued to property owner or tenant. However, tenants must have written permission from the property's owner before a permit will be issued.
(f)
Burn permits will not be issued to demolish a standing structure.
(g)
Pit and blower may be required for large scale burning.
(h)
Contractors will be required to be adequately licensed and bonded, and provide a certificate of insurance in the amount of $100,000.00 or more prior to being issued a burn permit.
(i)
Property owners and/or tenants must have adequate insurance to cover damage to the property of others in case of fire spread.
(j)
Tires or other rubber products, plastics, paper goods, petroleum products, or asphalt based or impregnated materials may not be used to start or maintain the operation of the burn.
(k)
Construction materials or manmade materials can not be burned.
(l)
Prevailing winds must be in a direction away from any residential areas near the proposed burn area.
(m)
Humidity must be such that residual smoke will not settle to surrounding ground areas.
(n)
Burning will not commence prior to one hour after sunrise local time and must be extinguished by one hour prior to sundown local time.
(o)
Burn permits will not be issued during times of drought or "statewide no burn" advisories from the Georgia Forestry Commission.
(p)
Ashburn Fire and Emergency Services will have the authority to stop the burn at any time should any of the above conditions not be met.
(Ord. No. 06-02, § 3, 7-6-2006)
Sec. 42-64. - Fees and charges.
(a)
A burn permit will be issued at no charge.
(b)
If the burn permit recipient desires or the Ashburn Fire Chief deems it necessary for the Ashburn Fire and Emergency Services to be present during burning times, the burn permit recipient will be charged a standby fee of $50.00 per hour.
(c)
A suppression fee of $500.00 will be charged for unauthorized burning.
(Ord. No. 06-02, § 4, 7-6-2006)
Sec. 42-65. - Trial of violators.
The presiding officer of the Ashburn Municipal Court shall have the power and authority to try all offenses and violations against the laws and ordinance of the city and, upon conviction, to punish offenders by a fine not exceeding $1,000.00, by imprisonment for any period not exceeding six months, or by compulsory work on the streets or upon any of the public works of the city for any period not exceeding six months, or by any combination of such penalties, as the presiding officer of the court may decide.
(Ord. No. 06-02, § 5, 7-6-2006)
