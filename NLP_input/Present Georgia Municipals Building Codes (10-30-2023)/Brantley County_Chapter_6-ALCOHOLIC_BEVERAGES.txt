Sec. 6-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this chapter shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcoholic beverages, distilled spirits, malt beverages, wine, retailer, retail dealer, wholesaler and wholesale dealer mean the same as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2, as amended.
Sheriff means the sheriff or any deputy sheriff of the county.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. I, §§ 1, 3)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 6-2. - Applicant criteria.
An individual applicant must be the person who will own or manage the premises (business) where the sales of alcoholic beverages will occur.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. II, § 1)
Sec. 6-3. - Application for license.
All applications for licenses for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall be made in person at the office of the clerk of the board of commissioners and shall be accompanied by a completed form or forms furnished for this purpose by the clerk, which application shall contain the following information:
(1)
Full name, address and telephone number of the applicant; if the owner of the subject business is a partnership or corporation, the name and addresses of each partner or the name and address of each principle stockholder must be shown;
(2)
Location or address of the place of business at which the applicant will engage in the retail sale of alcoholic beverages if and when permission to do so is granted by the board of commissioners and a license is issued;
(3)
All applicants shall list in their application the name, home address, home telephone number and present place of employment of the person who will be employed to be the manager of the place of business for which the license is requested, if other than the applicant. No one shall be allowed to serve in a supervisory and/or managerial position who has, within five years immediately prior to the filing of the application, entered a plea of guilty, a plea of nolo contendere, or shall have suffered any conviction of a felony or any crime involving moral turpitude or involving alcohol control laws of the state or the United States; or of any other state. Thereafter, if any person other than the person listed in the application as manager shall be employed as manager, the name, address, and telephone number of the new manager shall be furnished to the clerk of the board of commissioners within five days from the date of employment of the new manager; and
(4)
Such other information as the clerk of the board of commissioners, under the direction of the board of commissioners, may from time to time require.
The applicant shall set forth in detail, under oath, such information as may be required.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. II, § 2-A)
Sec. 6-4. - Prerequisite to issuance of license; fingerprinting.
(a)
Issuance to individuals. At the time any application for a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages under this chapter is filed at the office of the clerk of the board of commissioners and as a prerequisite to the issuance of any license hereunder, the applicant shall have a complete set of his fingerprints made by the sheriff or his designee, which shall be forwarded to the state crime information center who shall search his files for a period of five years immediately preceding the date of such application for any instance of criminal activity, and the state crime information center shall submit the results of such search to the county board of commissioners.
(b)
Issuance to firm or corporation. If the license is to be issued to a firm or corporation, fingerprints shall be made by the sheriff or his designee and forward for investigation as set forth in subsection (a) of this section for all persons who will serve in a supervisory and/or managerial position with respect to the sale of malt beverages. If the firm or corporation changes the person in such supervisory and/or managerial positions during any year, and designate such functions to a person who has not previously been responsible for such functions referred to as new person, then such firm or corporation shall notify the clerk of the board of commissioners, in writing, of the change in such supervisory and/or managerial personnel, giving the name, address of the new person, his address, the effective date of the change and shall furnish the clerk with the fingerprints of the new person. The notification and furnishing of the fingerprints shall be within five regular business days of the office of the board of commissioners. Should the fingerprints or investigation of the new person reveal that he is objectionable, the clerk of the board of commissioners shall notify the licensee immediately, upon the receipt of such notice, the new person shall not serve in such capacity with the licensee until authorized by the commissioners. The licensee may appeal such objections to the commissioners within three days of the receipt of notice, by delivering written notice of the appeal of the clerk of the board of commissioners. As soon as possible, the commissioners will hear evidence on the appeal, but the new person shall not serve in such capacity until and unless authorized by the commissioners.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. II, § 2-B)
Sec. 6-5. - Publication of notice.
The applicant for a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall cause a notice to appear in the official organ of the county, for two weeks, once each week, that an application for a license has been made. The notice shall contain the name of the applicant, the address, location, of the premises from where sale will occur, and the date and time when the application will be considered by the board of commissioners. The clerk of the board of commissioners shall prepare the notice for the publication; the applicant shall bear all cost of publication. The notice shall not be required for the renewal of any existing license.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. II, § 3)
Sec. 6-6. - Moral character of applicants.
All applicants for licenses for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall be of good moral character or such persons' applications shall not be considered; and in no event shall any license be granted to any person where any of the individuals, or any of the corporate officers, directors or majority stockholder, have, within five years immediately prior to the filing of the applications, entered a plea of guilty, a plea of nolo contendere, or shall have suffered any conviction of a felony or crime involving moral turpitude or involving alcohol control laws of the state, or the United States; or any other state. Should any licensee, partner, officer, director or a majority stockholder of a licensed corporation, after a license has been granted, be convicted or plead guilty or nolo contendere to felony or to a crime involving moral turpitude or to a violation of any laws of the United States or of the state relating to alcohol, the licenses issued to such licensee shall stand revoked and canceled.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. II, § 4)
Sec. 6-7. - Considerations of extenuating circumstances.
The board of commissioners shall consider any extenuating circumstances which may reflect favorably or unfavorably on the applicant, application, or the proposed location of the business. If, in the board's judgment, circumstances are such that the granting of the license would not be in the best interest of the general public, such circumstances may be grounds for denying the application. In considering an application, the commissioners shall consider the following factors before making any decision on an application:
(1)
Is there any statutory prohibition of the law of the state against the issuance of such a license to such person at such location?
(2)
Is the proposed location primarily a residential neighborhood or business neighborhood?
(3)
Are there schools, churches and numerous dwelling houses in the immediate vicinity of the proposed license site?
(4)
Are there other alcoholic beverages retail sales licensed premises in the immediate neighborhood of the proposed site?
(5)
Would the issuance of such a license be compatible to the neighborhood as it is now constituted?
(6)
Would the issuance of such a license in such location tend to diminish the property values of the adjoining lands?
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. II, § 5)
Sec. 6-8. - Proposed location; prohibitions.
(a)
Sales near churches and schools prohibited. No application for a license to sell alcoholic beverages shall be approved where the proposed location is within 1,000 feet of a church, school or college campus, or school grounds teaching subjects commonly taught in the public schools of the state. In determining compliance with this section, the distance shall be determined by measuring in the most direct route of travel on the ground and shall be measured in the following manner:
(1)
From the front door of the structure from which beverage alcohol is sold or offered for sale;
(2)
In a straight line to the nearest public sidewalk, walkway, street, road or highway;
(3)
Along such public sidewalk, walkway, street road or highway by the nearest route;
(4)
To the front door of the building, or to the nearest portion of the grounds, whichever is applicable under the appropriate statute.
(b)
Consumption on premises of sale prohibited. No application for a license to sell alcoholic beverages which may be consumed on the premises of the sale shall be approved where the proposed location is within 600 feet of any house which is occupied or regularly occupied.
(c)
Sales near existing retail package liquor stores prohibited. No application for a license for a new retail package liquor store, application for a license for the sale of distilled spirits, or the relocation of an existing retail package liquor store shall be approved if there is located within 500 yards of the proposed new location of an existing licensed retail package liquor store (licensed dealer of distilled spirits).
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. II, §§ 6-A—6-C; Ord. of 1-7-2016)
Sec. 6-9. - Licenses and duties of licensees.
(a)
No alcoholic beverages may be sold in the county except under a license issued in accordance with this chapter.
(b)
Licenses for the retail sale of alcoholic beverages shall be of the following types:
(1)
For the sale of malt beverages and wine only; or
(2)
For the sale of distilled spirits, malt beverages and wine.
(c)
No license for the sale of distilled spirits, malt beverages and/or wine shall be transferable, and in the event a licensed business is sold or closed, the licensee must immediately surrender his license to the sheriff. All licenses shall be issued to specific persons and shall be applicable to and valid with respect only to the business location referred to in such license.
(d)
All licenses shall expire upon and be of no validity after December 31 next after their issuance.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. III, §§ 1—4)
Sec. 6-10. - Authorization of approved licenses.
(a)
All licenses for the sale of alcoholic beverages approved, authorized and issued under this chapter shall authorize the retail sale of distilled spirits, malt beverages and/or wines either for off-premises consumption only or for on-premises consumption, provided, however, there shall be no license issued for the sale of on-premises consumption of distilled spirits.
(b)
All licenses approved, authorized and issued on and after the effective date of the ordinance from which this chapter is derived shall be plainly and conspicuously marked either for off-premises consumption only, or for on-premises consumption.
(c)
All licenses approved, authorized and issued on and after the effective date of the ordinance from which this chapter is derived for retail supermarkets, grocery stores, quick food outlets or other similar retail stores and for a package/liquor store shall be plainly and conspicuously marked for off-premises consumption only.
(d)
All licenses approved, authorized and issued on and after the effective date of the ordinance from which this chapter is derived for beer parlors, lounges or other similar establishments shall be plainly and conspicuously marked for on-premises consumption.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. III, §§ 5—8)
Sec. 6-11. - Intention of licensing authority.
It is the intention of the licensing authority in the adoption of the ordinance from which this chapter is derived to prohibit the consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises of any retail outlet under the authority of any license for the sale of alcoholic beverages held under subsection 6-10(c) and to permit the consumption of any malt beverages or wines on the premises of any retail outlet under the authority of any license held under subsection 6-10(d).
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. III, § 9)
Sec. 6-12. - Compliance of licensee with rules and regulations.
(a)
Each licensee for the sale of alcoholic beverages under this chapter shall comply with all applicable rules, regulations, laws and statutes of the state, and shall in addition thereto or in furtherance thereof:
(1)
Prohibit the sale, serving or consumption of alcoholic beverages in automobiles or other vehicles, or anywhere outside the main building or accessory thereto in which the business of such licensee is operated, except drive-in windows; and
(2)
Strictly adhere to the laws of the state, respecting the sale of alcoholic beverages to persons under the age of 21 years, intoxicated persons or habitual abusers of alcoholic beverages.
(b)
A licensee for on-premises consumption shall not permit a minor, a person under the legal age of majority as defined by the general assembly of the state, presently being the age of 18 years, to be on or about the licensed premises.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any licensee to allow or require a person in his employment under 18 years of age to dispense, serve, sell or take orders for alcoholic beverages; provided, however, that the provisions of this section shall not prohibit persons under 18 years of age, who are employed in supermarkets, convenience stores, or drugstores, from selling or handling malt beverages or wine which are sold for consumption off the premises.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. III, § 10)
Sec. 6-13. - License fees.
(a)
For the license year commencing January 1, 1998, and ending December 31, 1998, and for each license year thereafter until changed by action of the board of commissioners, there is hereby levied and imposed a special privilege and license fee, and an excise tax in the amount equal to the total of sections 6-14—6-15.
(b)
The license fees provided for in sections 6-13—6-15 shall be paid in full prior to the issuance of any license authorized by this chapter.
(c)
No license shall be issued unless the applicant therefor shall have fully paid all license fees and ad valorem taxes due the county.
(d)
All licenses granted shall expire on December 31 each year. All licenses granted shall be for the calendar year and a full license fee shall be paid for any license issued. There shall be no proration of any license fee. There shall be no refund made upon the revocation or suspension of any license.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. III, §§ 11, 14, 19, 20)
Sec. 6-14. - Annual license fee upon each retail distilled spirits, malt beverages and/or wine dealer.
modified
(a)
License fee upon each retail malt beverages and wine dealer for on-premises consumption of $350.00 payable at the time the license is issued.
(b)
License fee upon each retail malt beverage and wine dealer for off-premises consumption only of $350.00 payable at the time the license is issued.
(c)
License fee upon each retail dealer of distilled spirits for off-premises consumption only of $2,000.00 payable at the time the license is issued.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. III, § 11; Amend. of 3-3-2022, §§ 1, 2 )
Sec. 6-15. - License fee and tax upon each wholesale dealer.
(a)
There is hereby imposed a license fee of $100.00 for any wholesale dealer with a place of business located in the county, and an excise tax in the following amounts:
(1)
For malt beverages, an amount as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 3-5-80, or as such section may be from time to time amended by the general assembly of the state.
(2)
For distilled spirits, excluding fortified wine, and/or wine by the package, an amount of $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter.
All wholesalers and distributors of distilled spirits, malt beverages and/or wine wholesaling and/or distributing such commodity in the county shall add the amount of tax to be collected thereon, computed as provided in this section, to the invoice of sale to any and all retailers in the county, outside any municipality located therein, and remit the amount of such tax so computed to be due to the county directly to the office of the board of commissioners, less the sum of five percent of such tax which is hereby allowed for the collection of such tax. Such wholesalers and/or distributors shall furnish the clerk of the board of commissioners copies of all such invoices together with the amount of tax so collected at the time of payment of such taxes which shall be made and filed with the clerk on or before the tenth day of each and every month. The records of wholesalers and/or distributors shall be made available periodically to a duly authorized agent of the county for inspection and confirmation of the taxes collected as provided in this section.
(b)
No wholesale dealer of distilled spirits, malt beverages and/or wine shall sell, ship or deliver any distilled spirits, malt beverages and/or wine to a retail dealer in the county out of the corporate limits of any municipality located therein without having first complied with all procedures, acts and doings as prescribed and required by this chapter.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. III, § 13)
Sec. 6-16. - Sales outside corporate limits prohibited.
It shall be a violation of this section for any retail dealer or any wholesale dealer of distilled spirits, malt beverages and/or wine to own, hold, possess, sell or distribute any distilled spirits, malt beverages and/or wine in the county out of the corporate limits of any municipality located therein, without being in compliance with all the terms of this chapter.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. III, § 15)
Sec. 6-17. - Engage in business.
The privilege to engage in the business of a retail dealer or wholesale dealer of distilled spirits, malt beverages and/or wine shall, in no event, be granted to any person except by the issuance of a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages as provided in this chapter and such privilege shall be held and enjoyed by the licensee only so long as such licensee shall comply with the requirements of this chapter and promptly pay all fees and/or taxes which are provided in this chapter.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. III, § 16)
Sec. 6-18. - Violations.
Any violation of this chapter by a retail or wholesale distilled spirits, malt beverages and/or wine dealer shall be deemed as sufficient grounds for the revocation of the license issued for sale of alcoholic beverage to the violator by the board of commissioners and for the refusal thereafter to issue again any license to such violator under this section.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. III, § 17)
Sec. 6-19. - False statements; revocation of license.
The making of any statement on an application for a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages required by this chapter which shall be later found to be false shall constitute grounds for revocation of such license.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. III, § 18)
Sec. 6-20. - Prohibition of sales.
(a)
No alcoholic beverages shall be sold between the hours of 12:00 midnight on Saturday night until the hour of 6:00 a.m. on Monday morning.
(b)
No alcoholic beverages shall be sold between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 8:00 a.m. on any day by a holder of a for on-premises consumption license.
(c)
No alcoholic beverages shall be sold on Christmas day.
(d)
Alcoholic beverages may be sold at any time not specifically prohibited in this section.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. IV, §§ 1—4)
Sec. 6-21. - Inspections and licensee's responsibility.
(a)
Inspection of premises. The business premises of a holder of a license for the sale of any alcoholic beverages, including outbuildings, shall be opened to inspection at any and all times by the sheriff.
(b)
Retail dealer responsible for on-premises consumption. No retail dealer for the sale of malt beverages and/or wines shall allow such malt beverages and/or wines to be consumed on his premises unless such dealer has a license for on-premises consumption. Any retail dealer with a license for off-premises consumption only shall be held responsible for such consumption by his customers or other parties.
(c)
Copy of ordinance on premises. All holders of a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall keep a copy of the ordinance from which this chapter is derived on his premises and instruct any person working there with respect to the terms in this chapter and each licensee and person selling such beverages shall at all times be familiar with the terms of such ordinance.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. V, §§ 1—3)
Sec. 6-22. - Revocations and suspensions.
(a)
The performance of any act prohibited in this chapter or the failure to perform any act required hereby, as well as the violation of any law, state or federal, relating to the sale of alcoholic beverages shall constitute grounds for revocation of a license.
(b)
A license for the sale of malt beverages and wine may be temporarily suspended, and the place of business closed if it has a for on-premises consumption license, upon receipt of evidence by the commissioners from the sheriff of any violation of this chapter.
(c)
The sheriff or his lawful deputies shall be authorized, if at any time, the licensee shall be found in violation of this chapter in violation of any of the state and federal laws or regulations, to pick up the license and present to the clerk of the board of commissioners with a written report stating the violation.
(1)
The licensee shall have the right to call for a hearing within ten days or such other time as the commissioners shall designate. Should a hearing be so called for, the hearing must be held within 72 hours of requesting it, exclusive of Sundays. If a hearing is not held within such time, then the license shall be returned until further action by the commissioners.
(2)
The owner or employees shall cease to sell malt beverages and /or wine until the matter is concluded.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. VI, §§ 1—2-B)
Sec. 6-23. - Length of suspension.
Any license for the sale of any alcoholic beverages may be suspended for a definite length of time.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. VI, § 3)
Sec. 6-24. - Revocation of license.
(a)
Notice of revocation. No license shall be revoked unless the licensee has been given at least three days written notice, in person or by registered mail, of the intention of the board of commissioners to take such action. Such notice shall specify the time, place and purposes of the hearing and a statement of the charges upon which the commissioners propose to take action. At such hearings conducted by the commissioners, the licensee shall have the right to appear in person and with an attorney, and the sheriff, county attorney or the commissioners, as well as the licensee shall have the right to present evidence relating to the question as to whether or not a violation, as set forth in section 6-18, has occurred, as well as evidence relating to any extenuating or mitigating circumstances.
(b)
Consideration of evidence. After consideration of such evidence, the board of commissioners shall, by a majority, determine whether the license shall be revoked; or whether the license shall be suspended for a definite length of time not to exceed one-half of the remaining license year.
(c)
Hearing. If the licensee does not desire a hearing before the board of commissioners or fails to appear, the sheriff shall be authorized to then permit the owner to dispose of the merchandise within a ten-day period under the supervision of the sheriff. The merchandise may not be sold in bulk only.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. VI, §§ 4—6)
Sec. 6-25. - Signs.
(a)
Display of notice. The holder of a license for the retail sale of distilled spirits, malt beverages and/or wine shall post in a conspicuous place in his place of business a notice with letters not less than four inches in height which shall be clearly visible to all customers entering the establishment as follows: "Sale of Alcoholic Beverages to Persons under the age of 21 is Strictly Prohibited".
(b)
Advertising by use of signs. Retail dealers of alcoholic beverages may advertise the sell of such beverages by use of signs only under the following conditions:
(1)
There shall be only one sign at a business establishment, which sign may be doubled-faced;
(2)
The maximum dimensions for any sign shall be four feet in height and six feet in width;
(3)
A sign may be lighted;
(4)
All signs advertising alcoholic beverages must be located on premises of the establishment selling such beverages; and
(5)
No sign or display or advertisement or information regarding the prices of any alcoholic beverages may be located in any window or other place visible from outside the licensed premises.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. VII, §§ 1, 2)
Sec. 6-26. - Violations prosecuted in the magistrate court.
Any person who shall violate any of the sections of this chapter or who shall assist another in evading or avoiding payment of the fees and/or taxes imposed by this chapter shall be cited for evading or avoiding payment and tried in the magistrate court of the county in accordance with the provisions of state law for violations of county ordinances. Upon conviction, a sentence may be imposed by the magistrate of a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 and/or incarceration in the county jail of not more than 60 days.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. VII, § 5)
Sec. 6-27. - Grandfathering in of certain premises.
No application for a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages or for renewal shall be denied by reason of closeness to a church, school, residence or other business licensed to sell packaged liquor at retail as required under subsection 6-8(a), if a license for the subject business premises had been granted prior to the adoption of the ordinance from which this chapter is derived in reliance on the prior alcoholic beverages licensing ordinance of the county, and the subject premises remained licensed without interruption. Further, no application for a license or for renewal shall be denied by reason that a house, school or church has been erected within the prohibited distance after the licensed establishment was opened, and it continued in operation without interruption.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. VII, § 5)
Sec. 6-28. - Display of license.
Each person holding a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages issued pursuant to this chapter shall display the license prominently at all times on the premises for which the license is issued.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. VII, § 6)
Sec. 6-29. - Denial of renewal for adverse conditions.
Should a licensee for the sale of alcoholic beverages engage in acts or conduct or permit such acts or conduct to occur on his business that creates a continuing public disturbance, traffic congestion and/or obnoxious loud noise, from or on his business premises, then the board of commissioners shall give special attention to any renewal application and make a careful review of any complaints relating to the operation of the business. Should the board of commissioners find there has been just and substantial complaints made with or by the sheriff, the licensee shall be warned and informed to eliminate the adverse conditions existing on, at or around the licensed premises. After receiving a warning, should the licensee fail to eliminate the adverse conditions, the board of commissioners shall be authorized to refuse to renew a license to such licensee or to such licensed premises.
(Ord. of 4-14-1998, pt. VII, § 7)
