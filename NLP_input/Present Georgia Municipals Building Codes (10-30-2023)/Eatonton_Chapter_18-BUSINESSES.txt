Sec. 18-2. - Garage sales, yard sales, and rummage sales.
Sales commonly known as garage sales, yard sales, or rummage sales, conducted on residential property, shall be subject to the following rules and regulations:
(1)
Such sales shall be conducted only by the owner or tenant of the residential property used or with the express written permission of the property owner.
(2)
Such sales shall be allowed a maximum of three times during a calendar year on one residential lot. No individual sale shall be conducted for a longer period than 72 hours consecutively.
(3)
Any and all items related to such sales shall be promptly removed from the sale site and from areas that are viewable by the public within 24 hours of the conclusion of a sale.
(4)
No wares, goods, or merchandise shall be advertised or offered for sale, or swap, or given away, except goods, wares, and merchandise previously owned by the sellers.
(5)
No goods, wares, or merchandise shall be purchased by the owner or tenant for the purpose of selling, swapping, or giving away same at such a sale.
(6)
Any person desiring to conduct such a sale shall notify the city clerk no later than 12:00 noon of the day preceding the sale. The city clerk, or designated agent, shall issue a permit to the person to conduct a sale upon the payment of a permit fee as fixed from time to time by the city council if the applicant otherwise meets the requirements of this section. This fee shall be in lieu of any other fees or business taxes in this Code.
(7)
It shall be unlawful for any person to conduct a garage sale, yard sale, or rummage sale without first obtaining a permit as described herein, or in such a manner as to violate any of the provisions of this section, and the city administrator, or his/her designee, is authorized and directed to enforce the provisions of this section.
(8)
Religious and charitable organizations exempt from federal taxation pursuant to section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code may conduct yard sales on the property of the organization or at other locations within the city with the express written permission of the property owner without obtaining a permit required by paragraph (7) of this section.
(9)
Any violation of the provisions of this section shall be punished as prescribed by section 1-12.
(10)
Each day's violation of this section shall be considered a separate offense.
(Res. of 9-5-2017)

Sec. 18-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases shall, for the purposes of this chapter, have the following meaning:

Sec. 18-39. - Evidence of qualification required if applicable.
(a)
Before an occupational tax certificate can be issued to a new business, or a change in type of business or a change in the ownership of a business located within the city, a building must have a current inspection and approval from the Putnam County Planning and Development Department. All buildings must meet SBCCI standards. Business owners or operators are responsible for making the inspection request to the Putnam County Planning and Development Department and submitting the Putnam County Planning and Development Certificate of Occupancy along with this application to the city clerk. For the purposes of this section, an inspection will only be required if a building has been vacant for longer than 180 days. Vacant means that there is no provable use of utilities other than those necessary for reasonable security, no occupational tax certificate has been issued to such site for 180 days, no verifiable inventory sale or purchase has occurred during the time or other verifiable business activity has occurred during the 180 days prior to the application for the occupational tax certificate.
(b)
Any business required to obtain health permits, bonds, certificates of qualification, certificates of competency or any other regulatory matter shall first, before the issuance of an occupation tax certificate or a regulatory fee certificate, show evidence of such qualification.
(c)

Sec. 18-41. - Date due: penalty and interest.
(a)
Any occupation tax due pursuant to this article shall be due and payable annually within 30 days after January 1. In the event that any person commences business or initially engages in a regulated activity in the city after January 1 in any year, 100 percent of the occupational tax shall be due prior to the commencing or initially engaging said business unless it commences on July 1 or later that year whereon 50 percent of said tax shall be due in said business.
(b)
Any regulatory fee due pursuant to this article shall be due and payable annually within 30 days of January 1 for the current calendar year. In the event that any person commences business or initially engages in a regulated activity in the city after January 1 in any year, 100 percent of the regulatory fee shall be due prior to the commencing or initially engaging said business unless it commences on July 1 or later that year whereon 50 percent of said tax shall be due in said business.
(c)
Regulatory fees authorized by this article shall be paid before commencing business or the practice of a profession as a condition precedent for transacting business, or practicing a profession.
(d)
Regulatory fees may be paid after commencing business or the practice of a profession when:
(1)
The work done or services provided are necessary for the health and safety of one or more individuals:
(2)
The work done or services provided have no adverse effect on any other person; and
(3)
Regulatory fees are tendered to the local government within two business days after commencing business or the practice of a profession.
(e)
Any individual, business or practitioner subject to any occupation tax or regulatory fee imposed by this article which is unpaid for 60 days after the date on which payment was due shall be subject to the penalties provided in section 1-12 (general penalties) of this Code.
(Ord. of 10-21-2002)
Sec. 18-42. - Enforcement; violations.
(a)
It is the duty of the city clerk, Eatonton Police Department, code enforcement officer and building inspector to administer and enforce the provisions of this article, to perform all functions necessary to administer and enforce this article and to summon violators of this article to appear before the municipal court. The city clerk, Eatonton Police Department, code enforcement officer and building inspector may issue executions against individuals, businesses and practitioners for taxes or fees which are due and owing; any penalty imposed by section 1-12.
(b)
Individuals, businesses and practitioners who fail or refuse to make a timely or truthful tax return or make available truthful and accurate information the city requests or requires for determining applicability or amount of occupation tax or regulatory fee, or for levying or collecting such occupation tax or regulatory fee shall be subject to provisions of 1-12 or may be referred to the district attorneys' office for violating O.C.G.A. § 16-10-20.
(c)
All persons subject to the occupation tax or regulatory fee imposed by this article shall be required to file for and pay such tax or fee. For failure to do so, any officers or agents soliciting for or obtaining business for such person, business or practitioner shall be subject to the same penalty as other persons, businesses or practitioners who fail to obtain the required certificate or make a return for or pay the applicable occupation tax or regulatory fee.
(Ord. of 10-21-2002)
Sec. 18-43. - Public hearings.
(a)
After January 1, 2003, the city shall conduct at least one public hearing before adopting any ordinance or resolution which will increase the occupation tax rate specified in this chapter.
(b)
In any year when revenue from occupation taxes is greater than revenue from occupation taxes for the preceding year, the city may hold one (1) public hearing(s) as part of the process of determining how to use the additional revenue.
(c)
The city clerk, city police department, code enforcement officer and building inspector shall issue executions against individuals, businesses and practitioners for taxes or fees which are due and owing, penalties, or interest. Such executions shall bear interest at the rate authorized by O.C.G.A. § 48-4-40 or, if such statute should be repealed, one percent per month. The lien shall cover the property of the individual, business or practitioner liable for payment of the delinquent occupation tax or regulatory fee and become fixed as of the date and time of the occupation tax or regulatory fee became delinquent. The execution shall be levied by city clerk, city police department, code enforcement officer and building inspector of the city upon property of the delinquent tax or fee payer located in the city and sufficient property shall be advertised and sold to pay the amount of the execution, including penalty, interest and costs. All other proceedings in relation thereto shall be as provided by the Code and Charter of the city and the laws of Georgia. The defendants at execution shall have the rights of defense, by affidavit of illegality of the tax or otherwise as provided by the Charter of the city and laws of Georgia in regard to tax executions.
(d)
Individuals, businesses and practitioners who fail or refuse to pay any occupation tax or regulatory fee charged pursuant to this ordinance shall be subject to, in addition to the penalty provisions of 1-12, a fine not to exceed $500.00 pursuant to the contempt powers of the recorder's court.

ARTICLE IV. - ADULT ENTERTAINMENT ESTABLISHMENTS

Sec. 18-100. - Purpose.
The purpose of this chapter is to regulate certain types of businesses, including but not limited to, adult entertainment establishments to the end that the many types of criminal activities frequently engendered by such businesses will be curtailed. However, it is recognized that such regulation cannot defacto approach prohibition. Otherwise a protected form of expression would vanish. As to adult dance establishments, this article represents a balancing of competing interest: reduced criminal activity and protection of the neighborhoods through the regulation of adult entertainment establishments versus the protected rights of adult entertainment establishments and patrons.
(Ord. of 5-01-01)
Sec. 18-101. - Definition.
The following terms, [phrases, words when] used in this article, defining adult entertainment establishments shall have the meanings indicated below:
Adult bookstore means an establishment having a substantial or significant portion of its stock in trade, books, magazines or other periodicals which are distinguished or characterized by their emphasis on matter depicting, describing or relating to specified sexual activities or specified anatomical areas or an establishment with a segment or section, comprising 20 percent of its net sales consisting of printed material which are distinguished or characterized by their emphasis on matter depicting, describing or relating to specified sexual activities or specified anatomical areas.
Adult business means:
(1)
Any business other than those expressly specified in this article, where employees or patrons expose specified anatomical areas or engage in specified sexual activities; and
(2)
Any other business or establishment which offers its patrons services or entertainment characterized by an emphasis on matter depicting, describing, discussing or relating to specified sexual activities or specified anatomical areas, and
(3)
Any other business or establishment which offers for sale or rental for any form of consideration instruments, devices or paraphernalia that are designed for use in connection with specified sexual activities.
Adult dancing establishment means a business that features dancers displaying or exposing specified anatomical areas.
Adult motion picture theater means an enclosed building with a capacity of 50 or more persons, used for presenting material distinguished or characterized by an emphasis on matter depicting, describing or relating to specified sexual activities or specified anatomical areas for observation by patrons therein.
Adult mini-motion picture theater means an enclosed building with a capacity for less than 50 persons used for presenting material distinguished or characterized by an emphasis on matter depicting or relating to specified sexual activities or specified anatomical areas.
Adult hotel or motel means a hotel or motel wherein material is presented which is distinguished or characterized by an emphasis on matter depicting or relating to specified sexual activities or specified anatomical areas.
Adult motion picture arcade means any place to which the public is permitted to invited wherein coin or slug-operated or electronically, electrically or mechanically controlled still or motion picture machines, projectors or other image producing devises are maintained to show images to five or fewer persons per machine at any one time and where the images so displayed are distinguished or characterized by an emphasis on matter depicting or relating to specified sexual activities or specified anatomical areas.
Adult video store means any establishment having a substantial or significant portion of its stock in trade, video tapes or motives to other reproductions, whether for sale or rent, which are distinguished or characterized by their emphasis on matter depicting, distinguished or characterized by an emphasis on matter depicting or relating to specified sexual activities or specified anatomical areas or an establishment with a segment or section, comprising five percent its net sales from videos which are characterized or distinguished or relating to specified activities or specified anatomical areas.
Church means a temple or building consecrated to the honor of God or other supreme being and religion, or an assembly or person united by the profession of the same religious faith, meeting together routinely for religious worship.
Erotic dance establishment means a nightclub, theater or other establishment which features live performances by topless and/or bottomless dancers, go-go dancers, strippers or similar entertainers, where such performances are distinguished or characterized by an emphasis on matter depicting or relating to specified sexual activities or specified anatomical areas.
Encounter center or rap establishment means any business, agency or person who, for any form of consideration or gratuity, provides a place where two or more persons may congregate, assemble or associate for the primary purpose of engaging in, describing or discussing specified sexual activities, or exposing specified anatomical areas.

ARTICLE V. - NON-CONSENSUAL TOWING PERMIT

Sec. 18-150. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Abandoned motor vehicle means a motor vehicle or trailer defined by O.C.G.A. § 40-11-1 or as may be thereafter amended.
Secure impoundment facility means an impoundment facility with a minimum fencing of six feet high encompassing the area to store vehicles which is lighted and equipped with a lock or is otherwise an enclosed building equipped with a lock.
Wrecker service means any business authorized by the State of Georgia to engage in the practice of towing for a business and in fact has applied for and obtained a business permit from the city.
(Res. of 11-15-2004)
Sec. 18-151. - Permit application.
In lieu of obtaining a non-consensual towing permit from the Georgia Department of Motor Vehicle Safety, a wrecker service may obtain on an annual basis a non-consensual towing permit from the city by completing the application provided by the city. A non-consensual towing permit shall be valid through December 31 of the year it is issued. A new permit shall be required beginning January 1 for that calendar year.
(Res. of 11-15-2004)
Sec. 18-152. - Annual fee.
Any wrecker service applying for a non-consensual towing permit from the city shall pay an annual fee of no less than $150.00 in order to obtain said license. The city council is authorized to reduce this rate prior to December 31, 2004. Thereafter, the city council may raise this fee at any time.
(Res. of 11-15-2004)
Sec. 18-153. - Rules and regulations.
Any wrecker service licensed in the city to hold a non-consensual towing permit must also abide by the following rules and regulations, or the same is subject to being revoked by the city council:
(1)
No storage fees may be charged for the first 24-hour period beginning at the time the vehicle is removed from the property.
(2)
Wrecker services that perform non-consensual towing must have a secure impoundment facility.
(3)
Wrecker services shall not remove any vehicle from a property, except residential property having no more than four residential units, unless said property has signage conspicuously posted stating that trespassing vehicles will be removed at the owner's expense.
(4)
Rates charged by wrecker services for towing and storage must be reasonable as compared with other wrecker services of the same size and are subject by the city council.
(5)
Wrecker services may only remove vehicles at the express consent of the property owner or the owner's authorized agent.
(Res. of 11-15-2004)
