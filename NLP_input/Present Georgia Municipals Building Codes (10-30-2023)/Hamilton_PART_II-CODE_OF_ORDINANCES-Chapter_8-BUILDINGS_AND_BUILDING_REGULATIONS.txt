Sec. 8-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this chapter shall have the meaning ascribed to them in this section except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Building official or building inspector means the qualified building official or qualified building inspector as designated by the city.
Dwelling, as used in this chapter, shall mean a structural dwelling house built of wood, rock, or ceramic materials or of any other accepted materials used in construction of buildings, modular homes, trailers, and pre-fabricated houses.
(Ord. No. 12, § 1, 9-10-1973)
Sec. 8-2. - State minimum standard codes.
Unless otherwise provided for by subsequent ordinance or another provision of this Code, the city hereby adopts and incorporates by reference, as if set out at length herein, the latest editions of the state minimum standard codes enumerated in O.C.G.A. § 8-2-20(9)(B), as adopted and amended by the department of community affairs.
(1)
International Building Code, 2012 Edition, with 2014 and 2015 Georgia amendments.
(2)
International Residential Code, 2012 Edition, with 2014 and 2015 Georgia Amendments.
(3)
Fire Code, 2012 Edition, with 2014 Georgia Amendments.
(4)
International Plumbing Code, 2012 Edition, with 2014 and 2015 Georgia Amendments.
(5)
International Mechanical Code, 2012 Edition, with 2014 and 2015 Georgia Amendments.
(6)
International Fuel Gas Code, 2012 Edition, with 2014 and 2015 Georgia Amendments.
(7)
National Electrical Code, 2011 Edition.
(8)
International Energy Conservation Code, 2009 Edition, with Georgia Supplements and Amendments 2011 and 2012.
(Ord. No. 12, § 2, 9-10-1973)
Sec. 8-3. - Withholding of permit or water service for noncompliance.
City officials shall not issue a building permit nor furnish water service for any dwelling house failing to qualify in accordance with section 8-2.
(Ord. No. 12, § 3, 9-10-1973)
Sec. 8-4. - Conflicts.
(a)
If any of the publications listed in section 8-2 are adopted as a state minimum code pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 8-2-20 et seq., then the edition adopted by the state shall control. If any of the publications listed in section 8-2 are less stringent than the state minimum codes promulgated pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 8-2-20 et seq., the state minimum code shall control.
(b)
A copy of each of the publications listed in section 8-2, as adopted by the state, is filed in the city clerk's office and available for review by the public. The same is adopted and incorporated as fully as if set out at length herein, and from the date on which this section shall take effect, the provisions thereof shall be controlling within the corporate limits of the city.
Sec. 8-5. - References to officials in adopted technical codes.
Where reference is made to the duties of certain officials named within the technical codes which are adopted by reference in this chapter, then that official of the city as designated by the mayor who has duties corresponding to those of the named official in said technical code shall be deemed to be the responsible official insofar as enforcing the provisions of that technical code are concerned.
Sec. 8-6. - Tests.
Required tests are to be made at the expense of the owner, or his agent, by an approved testing laboratory or other approved agency.
