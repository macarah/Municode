Chapter 38 - VEHICLES FOR HIRE
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 38-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Business includes any and all persons which own, lease, have license to, or otherwise control any amount of real property within the geographical boundaries of the city for the express purpose of operating, maintaining or facilitating the operation or maintenance of taxicabs, limousines and other passenger-carrying vehicles.
Driver means any person who is employed by a person or entity who has or is required to have a business license issued by the city who, in the course of this employment, operates a taxicab, limousine or other passenger-carrying vehicle, upon the streets of the city. This definition includes persons who are self-employed.
Vehicle for hire means any taxicab, limousine, or other passenger-carrying vehicle operated for hire and designed or used for the purpose of transporting passengers for consideration or charges which are determined by agreement, contract, mileage, or by the length of time the vehicle is used. The vehicles regulated by the state public service commission are excluded from this definition.
(Code 1979, § 9-5-2; Code 1998, § 78-1)
Sec. 38-2. - Compliance with chapter.
No person shall conduct the business of operating vehicles for hire in the incorporated area of the city without first meeting the requirements of this chapter and having been issued a business license for operation in the geographical boundaries of this city. No business licensed for operating vehicles for hire shall employ any driver that has not first met the requirements of this chapter and been issued a driver's permit.
(Code 1979, § 9-5-1; Code 1998, § 78-2; Ord. No. 23-95, § III, 12-4-1995)

ARTICLE II. - BUSINESS LICENSE

Sec. 38-23. - Required.
No person shall authorize or permit a vehicle for hire to be operated within the geographical boundaries of the city without first obtaining a license from the city.
(Code 1979, § 9-5-3; Code 1998, § 78-36)
Sec. 38-24. - Application.
An application for a business license under this article shall be filed with the General Government Administration Department. Such application shall be verified under oath and shall contain the following information:
(1)
The dispatch location or dispatch terminus from which the business will be conducted.
(2)
Proof of automobile liability insurance covering public liability and property damage issued by an insurance company approved by the state on all vehicles licensed for use in the city. Such insurance shall insure passengers and third persons against personal injury and property damage in the amounts complying with state law. Such proof of insurance must evidence that all motor vehicles used for passenger conveyance operated by the applicant and/or licensee are insured as required by state law.
(3)
A list of all drivers who will be scheduled for licensing under this section. The list must indicate whether the drivers are company employees or independent contractors.
(4)
A copy of the rate schedule.
(5)
A statement of the daily hours of operation.
(6)
The name, address and telephone number of a responsible individual who will be the registered agent for the purpose of service of process.
(Code 1979, § 9-5-4(a); Code 1998, § 78-37)
Sec. 38-25. - Fee.
Each application for a business license under this article shall be accompanied by a fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges for each vehicle to be operated in the city. Fees are nonrefundable, are not prorated, and are not transferable.
(Code 1979, § 9-5-4(b); Code 1998, § 78-38)
Sec. 38-26. - Suspension, revocation.
A business permit issued under this article may be suspended or revoked for the following reasons:
(1)
The applicant furnishes fraudulent or untruthful information or omits information required in the application for a license or permit.
(2)
Failure to pay all fees, taxes or other charges imposed by the provisions of this article.
(3)
Violations of any part of this article.
(4)
Charging a fare in excess of those fares on file with the General Government Administration Department.
(5)
Allowing the required insurance coverage to lapse.
(Code 1979, § 9-5-18(a); Code 1998, § 78-39)
Secs. 38-27—38-55. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - DRIVER'S PERMIT

Sec. 38-56. - Required.
No driver shall operate a vehicle for hire upon the streets of the city, and no person who owns or controls a vehicle for hire shall permit it to be so driven, and no taxicab licensed by the city shall be driven at any time, unless the driver of such taxicab shall have first obtained and shall have then in force a driver's permit issued under the provisions of this article.
(Code 1979, § 9-5-5; Code 1998, § 78-71)
Sec. 38-57. - Application.
(a)
An application for a vehicle for hire driver's permit shall be filed with the General Government Administration Office and/or the Police Department on forms provided by the city. Such application shall be verified under oath and shall contain the following information:
(1)
The names and addresses of four residents of the county who have known the applicant for a period of six months and who will vouch for the sobriety, honesty and general good character of the applicant.
(2)
The experience of the applicant in the transportation of passengers.
(3)
The educational background of the applicant.
(4)
A concise history of his employment.
(5)
Disclosure of all crimes or infractions, including traffic violations, which the applicant has been convicted of, on probation or parole for or or imprisoned for during the past five years.
(b)
Each application shall be accompanied by a certificate from a reputable physician of the county certifying, that in his opinion, the applicant is not inflicted with any disease or infirmity which might make him an unsafe or unsatisfactory driver. At the time the application is filed, the applicant shall pay to the General Government Administration Office and/or the Police Department a regulatory fee set forth in the schedule of regulatory fees which is maintained on file in the Office of the General Government Administration Department where it shall be available for inspection by the public. All permits are renewable on the anniversary date of issue on proper forms as directed by the City Manager's office.
(Code 1979, § 9-5-6; Code 1998, § 78-72; Ord. No. 23-95, § III, 12-4-1995; Ord. No. 9-98, 5-4-1998)
Sec. 38-58. - Current state operator's permit required.
Before any applicant under this article is finally passed upon by the City Manager's office, the applicant shall show that he has a current class I motor vehicle operator's permit issued by the state.
(Code 1979, § 9-5-7; Code 1998, § 78-73)
Sec. 38-59. - Fees.
The fee for a permit required by this article, and for a renewal thereof, shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges.
(Code 1979, §§ 9-5-10(c), 9-5-14; Code 1998, § 78-74; Ord. No. 23-95, § III, 12-4-1995)
Sec. 38-60. - police investigation.
The City Manager's office shall conduct an investigation of each applicant for a driver's permit and a report of such investigation and a copy of the traffic and police record of the applicant, if any, shall be attached to the application for the consideration of the General Government Administration Department Head.
(Code 1979, § 9-5-8; Code 1998, § 78-75)
Sec. 38-61. - Consideration.
The City Manager shall, upon consideration of the application for a permit under this article and the reports and certificate required to be attached thereto, approve or reject the application. If the application is rejected, the applicant may request a personal appearance before the City Manager to offer evidence why his application should be reconsidered.
(Code 1979, § 9-5-9; Code 1998, § 78-76)
Sec. 38-62. - Issuance.
(a)
Upon approval of the application for a driver's permit under this article, the City Manager's office shall issue a permit to the applicant which bears his name, address, age, signature and photograph.
(b)
Such permit shall remain the property of the city. The permittee is solely responsible for returning such permit to the city if he ceases to operate a vehicle for hire within the city.
(Code 1979, § 9-5-10(a), (b); Code 1998, § 78-77)
Sec. 38-63. - Duration.
Every permit issued under the provisions of this article shall expire on December 31 of the year in which the same was issued.
(Code 1979, §§ 9-5-10(c), 9-5-14; Code 1998, § 78-78; Ord. No. 23-95, § III, 12-4-1995)
Sec. 38-64. - Renewal.
The permittee shall contact the City Manager's office on or before December 15 of each year for renewal scheduling. In order to have his permit renewed, a driver must show that he is currently permitted and in good standing with the city. A new annual permit shall be issued upon such showing and the payment of a renewal fee.
(Code 1979, § 9-5-14; Code 1998, § 78-79; Ord. No. 23-95, § III, 12-4-1995)
Sec. 38-65. - Display.
Every driver of a vehicle for hire shall post his driver's permit in such a place to be in full view of all passengers while such driver is operating a taxicab.
(Code 1979, § 9-5-11; Code 1998, § 78-80)
Sec. 38-66. - Loss.
Loss of a driver's permit issued under this article shall result in automatic revocation of such permit.
(Code 1979, § 9-5-13; Code 1998, § 78-81)
Sec. 38-67. - Failure to comply with laws.
Every driver permitted under this article shall comply with all city, state and federal laws, including all Uniform Rules of the Road (O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-1—40-6-395) as set out in state law and adopted by the city. Failure to do so will justify the City Manager's office suspending or revoking a taxicab driver's permit issued by the city.
(Code 1979, § 9-5-16; Code 1998, § 78-82)
Sec. 38-68. - Suspension, revocation—Grounds.
A driver's permit issued under this article may be suspended or revoked for the following reasons:
(1)
The applicant furnishes fraudulent or untruthful information or omits information required in the application for a license or permit.
(2)
Failure to pay all fees, taxes or other charges imposed by the provisions of this article.
(3)
Violations of any part of this article.
(4)
Charging a fare in excess of those fares on file with the General Government Administration Department.
(5)
Having a conviction of any felony during the time which the permit is issued for.
(6)
Having four or more traffic violations, while operating any taxicab, limousine or passenger-carrying vehicle during any 12-month period.
(7)
Refusing to accept a passenger solely on the basis of race, color, national origin, religious belief, or gender.
(8)
Failure to have the driver's permit posted in the taxicab in a place and manner clearly visible from the passenger compartment.
(Code 1979, §§ 9-5-12, 9-5-18(b); Code 1998, § 78-83)
Sec. 38-69. - Same—Operation of vehicle.
Any person who operates a vehicle for hire within the city at the time when his privilege to do so has been suspended or revoked shall be guilty of a misdemeanor.
(Code 1979, § 9-15-18(c); Code 1998, § 78-84)
Sec. 38-70. - Same—Duty of City Manager.
(a)
The City Manager's office, upon receiving the record of conviction of any person upon a charge of driving a vehicle for hire while the permit of such person to do so was suspended, shall revoke such person's permit.
(b)
The City Manager's office, upon receiving the record of conviction of any person upon a charge of driving a vehicle for hire while the permit of such person was revoked, shall extend the period of revocation for an additional one year.
(Code 1979, § 9-5-18(d), (e); Code 1998, § 78-85)
Sec. 38-71. - Same—Reapplication.
Any driver who has had a permit revoked for any reason must reapply for and be issued a new driver's permit before such driver may operate a taxicab, limousine or other passenger-carrying vehicle upon the streets of the city.
(1)
If the permit was revoked due to the driver's losing the permit, the driver may reapply for a permit immediately.
(2)
If the permit was revoked for any other reason, the driver may not apply for a new permit for a period of six months.
(Code 1979, § 9-5-17; Code 1998, § 78-86)
Sec. 38-72. - Liability of vehicle owner.
Any person who knowingly authorizes or permits a taxicab, limousine or other passenger-carrying vehicle, owned by him or under his control, to be operated for hire within the city by any person who is not authorized under this section, or who is not permitted under this section, or is in violation of any of the provisions of this article, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor. Each day that a person knowingly authorizes or permits a taxicab, limousine or other passenger-carrying vehicle, owned or operated by him or under his control, to be driven within the city by any person who is not authorized under this article or is in violation of any of the provisions of this article shall be considered a separate violation.
(Code 1979, § 9-5-18(f); Code 1998, § 78-87)
