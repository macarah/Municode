ARTICLE II. - INSURERS LICENSE FEES[1]

Sec. 18-19. - Insurers license fees.
There is hereby levied for each year an annual license fee upon each insurer doing business within the city in the amount of $50.00. For each separate business location in excess of one not covered by section 18-20, which is operating on behalf of such insurers within the city, there is hereby levied a license fee in the amount of $50.00. For the purposes of this article, the term "insurer" means a company which is authorized to transact business in any of the classes of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5.
(Ord. No. O-003-15, § 1, 1-1-2015)
Sec. 18-20. - License fees for insurers insuring certain risks at additional business locations.
For each separate business location not otherwise subject to a license fee hereunder, operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing, and in connection with such loans or sales offers, solicits or takes application for insurance through a licensed agent of an insurer for insurance, said insurer shall pay an additional license fee of $17.50 per location for each year.
(Ord. No. O-003-15, § 2, 1-1-2015)
Sec. 18-21. - Gross premiums tax imposed on life insurers.
There is hereby levied for each year an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer writing life, accident and sickness insurance within the state in an amount equal to one percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 3-8-8.1. The term "gross direct premiums," as used in this section, shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 18-19.
(Ord. No. O-003-15, § 3, 1-1-2015)
Sec. 18-22. - Gross premiums tax, all other insurers.
There is hereby levied for each year an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer, other than an insurer transacting business in the class of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5(1), doing business within the state in an amount equal to 2½ percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2. The term "gross direct premiums," as used in this section, shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 18-19.
(Ord. No. O-003-15, § 4, 1-1-2015)
Sec. 18-23. - Due date for license fees.
License fees imposed in sections 18-19 and 18-20 shall be due and payable on the first date of each year.
(Ord. No. O-003-15, § 5, 1-1-2015)
Secs. 18-24—18-46. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - HOTEL AND MOTEL EXCISE TAX[2]

Sec. 18-47. - Intent and statutory authority.
The intent hereof is to establish and impose an excise tax pursuant to the authority and terms of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(a)(3), the funds or revenues from which taxation shall be used and expended for all lawful public purposes, including the promotion, attraction, and development of tourism, conventions, trade shows, and industry in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51.
(Ord. No. O-006-15, § 1, 1-22-2015)
Sec. 18-48. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Due date means the 20th day after the close of the monthly period for which tax is to be computed.
Guest room means a room occupied, and/or intended, arranged, and/or designed for occupancy, by one or more person for the purported and/or actual and/or designated purpose of residence, living quarters.
Hotel or motel means any structure or portion thereof, including any lodginghouse, roominghouse, dormitory, Turkish bath, bachelor hotel, studio hotel, motor hotel, auto court, inn, public club, or private club, containing guest rooms and occupied, or intended or designed for occupancy, by guests, whether rent is paid in money, goods, labor, or otherwise. Excluded herefrom are all jails, hospitals, asylums, sanitariums, orphanages, prisons, detention centers, rehabilitation centers or other buildings in which human beings are housed and detained under legal restraint.
Monthly period means a calendar month.
Occupancy means the use, possession, and/or the right to such use or possession, of any rooms, apartments, parts of rooms, and/or space located within, or under the control of, a hotel, motel, or travel trailer park, and the right to the use or possession of the furnishings or to the services and accommodations accompanying the use and possession of the room or travel trailer space.
Occupant means any person who, for a consideration, uses, possesses, or has the right to use or possess any room or travel trailer space in a hotel, motel, or travel trailer park under any lease, concession, permit, right of access, license to use or other agreement, or otherwise.
Operator means any person operating a hotel, motel or travel trailer park in the city, including, but not limited to, the owner, proprietor, lessee, sublessee, lender in possession, licensee, or any other person otherwise operating such hotel or travel trailer park.
Permanent resident means any occupant who occupies any guest room in a hotel or motel or a travel trailer space for not less than 30 continuous days next preceding such date.
Person means an individual, firm, partnership, joint venture, association, social club, fraternal organization, joint stock company, corporation, nonprofit corporation or cooperative nonprofit membership, estate, trust, business trust, receiver, trustee, syndicate, or any other group or combination acting as a unit, the plural as well as the singular number, excepting the United States of America, the state, and any political subdivision of either thereof upon which the city is without power to impose the tax provided for in this article.
Rent means the consideration received for occupancy, valued in money whether received in money or otherwise, including all receipts, cash, credits, and property or services of any kind or nature, and also the amount for which credit is allowed by the operator to the occupant, without any deduction therefrom whatsoever.
Return means any return filed or required to be filed as provided in this article.
Tax means the tax imposed by this article.
Travel trailer means any moveable vehicle or structure, including trailers, campers, vans, buses, motor homes, and other recreation vehicles.
Travel trailer park means a facility providing space for the parking of trailers, campers, vans, buses, or motor homes.
(Ord. No. O-006-15, § 2, 1-22-2015)
Sec. 18-49. - Imposition and rate of tax.
(a)
A tax is hereby imposed in an amount equal to five percent of the total sum paid for the rent for every occupancy of a guest room in a hotel or motel, or occupancy of accommodations for value, including, but not limited to, travel trailer spaces in the city. The tax imposed by this article shall be paid upon any occupancy by any entity subject hereto as defined hereinabove, beginning on the first day of the month immediately succeeding the month in which this article was passed and adopted, and shall be due and payable the 20th day of said month and each month thereafter pursuant to the terms contain herein; and, regardless of when the terms of said rent were negotiated and regardless if such occupancy and consideration therefor is or was of a contract, lease or other arrangement made or entered prior to said date.
(b)
All proceeds of this tax shall be used in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(a)(3).
(Ord. No. O-006-15, § 3, 1-22-2015)
Sec. 18-50. - Operators obligated to collect tax.
Every operator maintaining or operating a hotel or motel or business meeting said definition as provided herein above, or otherwise renting guest rooms or travel trailer spaces within the jurisdictional boundaries of the city, and who is not exempted, shall collect a tax of five percent on the amount of rent from each occupant.
(Ord. No. O-006-15, § 4, 1-22-2015)
Sec. 18-51. - Exemptions.
No tax shall be imposed under this article upon:
(1)
A permanent resident as defined hereinabove.
(2)
Any state or local government officials or employees traveling on official business. Notwithstanding the availability of any other means of identifying the person as a state or local government official or employee, whenever a person pays for any rooms, lodgings, or accommodations with a state or local government credit or debit card, such rooms, lodgings, or accommodations shall be deemed to have been furnished for use by a state or local government official or employee traveling on official business for purposes of the exemption provided by this subsection. For the purpose of the exemption provided under this subsection, a local government official or employee shall include officials or employees of counties, municipalities, consolidated governments, or county or independent school districts.
(3)
Any person to whom rooms, lodging or accommodations are provided by virtue of the destruction of their home or dwelling by fire or other casualty.
(4)
The use of meeting rooms and other such facilities or any rooms, lodgings, or accommodations provided without charge.
(Ord. No. O-006-15, § 5, 1-22-2015)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(h).
Sec. 18-52. - Operator registration and certificate of authority.
All persons engaged or about to engage in the business of operating a hotel, motel, or travel trailer park within the jurisdictional boundaries of the city shall immediately register with the city clerk. Said clerk shall provide the appropriate registration form. Such registration shall state the name under which said person transacts or intends to transact business, the location of his place of business, and such other information requested by said clerk and/or within said form and which said clerk deems necessary and proper for the collection of the tax. Said registration shall be signed by the owner, if a natural person; or, in case of ownership by an association or partnership, by a member or partner; and in case of ownership by a corporation, by a corporate officer duly authorized by said entity to sign the same. The city clerk shall, after such registration, issue without charge a certificate of authority to each operator to collect the tax from the occupant. A new and separate registration and/or form shall be required for each business, regardless of unity of ownership thereof in a single operator. Each certificate shall state the name and location of the business to which it is applicable.
(Ord. No. O-006-15, § 6, 1-22-2015)
Sec. 18-53. - Deficiency determinations.
(a)
Recomputation of tax; authority to make basis of recomputation. If the city clerk is not satisfied with the return filed by any person pursuant to this article, he may compute and determine the amount required to be paid upon the basis of any information available to him. One or more deficiency determinations may be made of the amount due for one or more monthly periods.
(b)
Interest on deficiency. The amount of any deficiency determination shall bear interest at the rate of three-fourths of one percent per month or fraction thereof from the due date of the taxes.
(c)
Service of notice of determination. The city clerk or a designated representative thereof shall give to the operator written notice of any deficiency determination. The notice may be served personally or by certified mail; if by certified mail, such service shall be addressed to the operator at his address as it appears in the records of the city clerk. Service by certified mail is complete upon the signing by the addressee of the return receipt acknowledging delivery. Except in the case of failure to make a return, every notice of a deficiency determination shall be mailed within three years after the 20th day of the calendar month following the monthly period for which the amount is proposed to be determined, or within three years after the return is filed, whichever is later.
(Ord. No. O-006-15, § 8, 1-22-2015)
Sec. 18-54. - Failure to make or file return.
(a)
Estimate of gross receipts. If any person fails to make a return, the city clerk shall make an estimate of the amount of the gross receipts of the person, or as the case may be, of the amount of the total rentals of such person that are subject to the tax. The estimate shall be made for the periods in respect to which the person failed to make the return and shall be based upon any information which is or may come into the possession of the city clerk. Written notice shall be given in the manner prescribed in section 18-53(c).
(b)
Interest on amount found due. The amount of such determination shall bear interest at the rate of three-fourths of one percent per month or fraction thereof, from the 20th day of the month following the monthly period for which the amount or any portion thereof should have been returned, until the date of payment.
(Ord. No. O-006-15, § 9, 1-22-2015)
Sec. 18-55. - Miscellaneous administrative provisions.
(a)
Clerk's authority. The city clerk shall administer and enforce the provisions of this article relating to the collection of the tax imposed by this article.
(b)
Recordkeeping requirement. Every operator renting guest rooms or travel trailer space in the city shall keep such records, receipts, invoices, and other papers or documents related to rents billed, collected, due, and/or contracts therefor in such form as the city clerk may require.
(c)
Examination of records. The city clerk or any person authorized thereby may examine the books, papers, records, financial reports, equipment and other facilities of any operator renting guest rooms or travel trailer space to persons and any operator liable for the tax, in order to verify the accuracy of any return made, or if no return is made by the operator, to ascertain and determine the amount required to be paid.
(d)
Authority to require reports. In the administration of the provisions of this article, the city clerk may require the filing of reports by any person having in his possession or custody information relating to rentals of guest rooms or travel trailer space subject to the tax. The reports shall be filed with the city clerk when required by the city clerk and shall set forth the rental charged for each occupancy, the dates of occupancy, and such other information as the city clerk may require.
(Ord. No. O-006-15, § 10, 1-22-2015)
Sec. 18-56. - Violations.
Any person violating any of the provisions of this article shall be guilty of violating this article and, upon conviction thereof, shall be punished as generally provided in section 1-8. Such person shall be guilty of a separate offense for each day during which any portion or provision hereof is violated or each day in which an act is committed, continued, or permitted by such person to be committed or continued, and shall be punished accordingly. Any operator or any other person who fails to register as required in this article or who fails or refuses to furnish other data required by the city clerk shall be deemed guilty of an offense.
(Ord. No. O-006-15, § 11, 1-22-2015)
Sec. 18-57. - Collection of taxes.
(a)
Action for tax. At any time within three years after any tax or any amount of tax required to be collected under this article becomes due and payable and at any time within three years after the delinquency of any tax or any amount of tax required to be collected, the city clerk may bring an action in a court of competent jurisdiction in the name of the city to collect the amount delinquent, together with interest, court fees, filing fees, attorneys' fees and other legal fees incident thereto.
(b)
Overpayments. Whenever the amount of any tax or interest has been paid more than once, or has been erroneously or illegally collected or received by the city under this section, it may be offset by the city clerk. If the operator or person determines that he has overpaid or paid more than once, which fact has not been determined by the city clerk, he shall have three years from the date of payment to file a claim in writing, stating the specific ground upon which claim is founded. The claim shall be audited. If the claim is approved by the city clerk, the excess amount paid the city may be credited on any amounts then due and payable from the person by whom it was paid, or his administrators or executors.
(Ord. No. O-006-15, § 12, 1-22-2015)

ARTICLE IV. - BUSINESS LICENSE AND OCCUPATIONAL TAX[3]

Sec. 18-85. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Administrative fee means the component of an occupation tax which approximates the reasonable cost of handling and processing the registration of the business from whom the occupation tax is collected.
Dominant line means the type of business within a multiple service or product business from which the greatest amount of revenue is derived.
Eleemosynary business, institution or corporation means any business, institution or corporation created for educational, charitable and benevolent purposes; and holding an exemption as a charitable, religious, educational or other exempt organization under the Internal Revenue Code.
Employee means an individual whose work is performed under the direction and supervision of the employer and whose employer withholds FICA, federal income tax, or state income tax from such individual's compensation or whose employer issues to such individual for purposes of documenting compensation a form IRS W-2 but not a form IRS 1099. An individual who performs work under the direction and supervision of one business or practitioner in accordance with the terms of a contract or agreement with another business which recruits such individual is an employee of the business or practitioner which issues to such individual for purposes of documenting compensation a form IRS W-2.
Gross receipts.
(1)
The term "gross receipts" means the total revenue of the business or practitioner for the period, including, without limitation, the following:
a.
Total income without deduction for the cost of goods or expenses incurred;
b.
Gain from trading in stocks, bonds, capital assets, or instruments of indebtedness;
c.
Proceeds from commissions on the sale of property, goods, or services;
d.
Proceeds from rent, interest, royalty, or dividend income.
(2)
The term "gross receipts" shall not include the following:
a.
Sales, use, or excise tax;
b.
Sales returns, allowances, and discounts;
c.
Interorganizational sales or transfers between or among the units of a parent-subsidiary controlled group of corporations, as defined by 26 USC 1563(a)(1), between or among the units of a brother-sister controlled group of corporations, as defined by 26 USC 1563(a)(2), between or among a parent corporation, wholly owned subsidiaries of such parent corporation, and any corporation in which such parent corporation or one or more of its wholly owned subsidiaries owns stock possessing at least 30 percent of the total value of shares of all classes of stock of such partially owned corporation, or between or among wholly owned partnerships or other wholly owned entities;
d.
Payments made to a subcontractor or an independent agent for services which contributed to the gross receipts in issue;
e.
Governmental and foundation grants, charitable contributions, or the interest income derived from such funds received by a nonprofit organization which employs salaried practitioners otherwise covered by this article, if such funds constitute 80 percent or more of the organization's receipts; and
f.
Proceeds from sales of goods or services which are delivered to or received by customers who are outside the state at the time of delivery or receipt.
Location or office includes any structure or vehicle where a business, profession, or occupation is conducted, but shall not include a temporary or construction work site which serves a single customer or project or a vehicle used for sales or delivery by a business or practitioner of a profession or occupation which has a location or office. The renter's or lessee's location, which is the site of personal property which is rented or leased from another, does not constitute a location or office for the personal property's owner, lessor, or the agent of the owner or lessor. The site of real property which is rented or leased to another does not constitute a location or office for the real property's owner, lessor, or the agent of the owner or lessor unless the real property's owner, lessor, or the agent of the owner or lessor, in addition to showing the property to prospective lessees or tenants and performing maintenance or repair of the property, otherwise conducts the business of renting or leasing the real property at such site or otherwise conducts any other business, profession, or occupation at such site.
Medical establishments means hospitals, nursing homes and visiting nurses associations or corporations.
Occupation tax means a tax levied on persons, partnerships, corporations or other entities for engaging in an occupation, profession or business for revenue purposes.
Occupation tax certificate means a document issued by the city acknowledging payment of the occupation tax and administrative fee.
Person includes sole proprietors, practitioners of professions, corporations, partnerships, or any other form of organization but specifically excludes nonprofit organizations and state and local authorities.
Practitioners of professions are ones who, by state law, require state licensure regulating such profession or occupation, such as:
(1)
Lawyers;
(2)
Physicians licensed under O.C.G.A. title 43, ch. 34 (O.C.G.A. § 43-34-1 et seq.);
(3)
Osteopaths licensed under O.C.G.A. title 43, ch. 34 (O.C.G.A. § 43-34-1 et seq.);
(4)
Chiropractors;
(5)
Podiatrists;
(6)
Dentists;
(7)
Optometrists;
(8)
Psychologists;
(9)
Veterinarians;
(10)
Landscape architects;
(11)
Land surveyors;
(12)
Practitioners of physiotherapy;
(13)
Public accountants;
(14)
Embalmers;
(15)
Funeral directors;
(16)
Civil, mechanical, hydraulic, or electrical engineers;
(17)
Architects; and
(18)
Marriage and family therapists, social workers and professional counselors.
Such term shall not include a practitioner who is an employee of a business, if the business pays an occupation tax.
Profitability class means the category into which an occupation is placed for occupation tax purposes in accordance with this article.
Regulatory fee means the payment, whether designated as license fees, permit fees, or by another name, required by the city as an exercise of its police power as part of or as an aid to regulation of an occupation, profession or business, the amount of which shall approximate the reasonable cost of the actual regulatory activity performed by the city. (Regulatory fees may not include an administrative fee. Also, development impact fees or other costs imposed as conditions of zoning or land development are not regulatory fees for purposes of this article. See O.C.G.A. §§ 48-13-5(6) and 36-71-2(8).) Nothing herein shall be construed to apply a regulatory fee to a profession or to any other occupation whose qualification to practice or perform their profession or occupation is determined by the general laws of the state.
Regulatory fee certificate means a document issued by the city acknowledging payment of regulatory fees.
Standard industrial classification means categories of occupations and industries established by and promulgated by the Office of Management and Budget of the United States of America and found in the Standard Industrial Classification Manual of 1987.
(Ord. No. O-008-15, § 2, 1-22-2015)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-5.
Sec. 18-86. - Purpose.
The occupation tax levied herein is for revenue purposes only and is not for regulatory purposes, nor is the payment of the tax made a condition precedent to the practice of any such profession, trade, or calling. The occupation tax only applies to those businesses and occupations which are covered by the provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 48-13-5 to 48-13-26. All other applicable businesses and occupations are taxed by the local government pursuant to the pertinent general and/or local law and ordinance.
(Ord. No. O-008-15, § 1, 1-22-2015)

(Ord. No. O-008-15, § 3, 1-22-2015)

Sec. 18-89. - Occupation tax limitations.
(a)
The city shall not require the payment of more than one occupation tax for each location of a person.
(1)
If a person with no location or office in the city provides to the city proof of payment of a local business or occupation tax in another state which purports to tax the person's gross receipts in the state, then the person shall be exempt from the occupation tax levied by this article.
(2)
A person who has locations or offices elsewhere in the state who is subject to occupation tax by more than one local government in the state shall be subject to occupation tax by the city, unless the person provides documentation to the city of current payment of an occupation tax, measured by the gross receipts of the person, to the other state local government.
(b)
Persons from out of state with no office or location in the state subject to an occupation tax under this article shall be required to pay an occupation tax to only one local government in the state. Such local government shall be the government of the municipal corporation or county in which the largest dollar volume of business is done or services are performed by the person. Such persons submitting documentation to the city showing payment of a local business or occupation tax to another local government in the state shall be exempt from the occupation tax levied by this article.
(c)
If a person commences business in the city on or after July 1 in any year, the occupation tax for the remaining portion of the year shall be 50 percent of the tax imposed for the entire year. The administrative fee shall not be reduced.
(1)
Real estate brokers shall be subject to the occupation tax imposed by this article only if they maintain a principal or branch in the city. (Licensing and taxation of insurers is governed by O.C.G.A. § 38-8-8 et seq., and is covered by a separate ordinance. Likewise, alcoholic beverage businesses are subject to special provisions in O.C.G.A. title 3 (O.C.G.A. § 3-3-1 et seq.) and are covered by a separate ordinance.)
(2)
Real estate brokers whose offices are located outside the city and who sell property inside the city shall be subject to the occupation tax based upon the gross receipts from sales of property located within the city.
(d)
(1)
No person shall be required to pay more than one occupation tax for each of its locations.
(2)
No occupation tax will be required upon more than 100 percent of a business' gross receipts.
(3)
No occupation tax will be required on receipts on which such tax has been levied in other localities or states.
(4)
An occupation tax shall not be levied in any other manner except as described in this section.
(5)
Out-of-state businesses with no location in the state shall be assessed occupation taxes based on the gross receipts of the business as defined in O.C.G.A. § 14-13-7 which are reasonably attributed to sales or services in the state.
(Ord. No. O-008-15, § 6, 1-22-2015)
Sec. 18-90. - Registration of line of business.
The registration by each person shall identify the dominant line of business, profession, or occupation conducted.
(Ord. No. O-008-15, § 7, 1-22-2015)
Sec. 18-91. - Multiple locations within city.
Wherever a business or occupation is conducted at more than one location or place within the city, each such location shall be considered a separate business for the purpose of the occupation tax imposed by this article. (A business shall be required to obtain the necessary registration for each location and line and pay an occupation tax in accordance with the prevailing taxing method and tax rate for each location and line.)
(Ord. No. O-008-15, § 8, 1-22-2015)
Sec. 18-92. - Multiple locations inside and outside city.
(a)
For those businesses that have multiple locations inside and outside of the city where the gross receipts can be allocated to each location, the gross receipts used to determine the occupation tax assessed will be those gross receipts generated by each city location. Where the dollar amount of gross receipts cannot be allocated among multiple locations, the total gross receipts will be divided by the total number of locations in the city and elsewhere and an equal percentage of such gross receipts shall be allotted to each location. Upon request, the business or practitioner with a location or office situated in more than one jurisdiction shall provide to the city the following:
(1)
Financial information necessary to allocate the gross receipts of the business or practitioner; and
(2)
Information relating to the allocation of the business' or practitioners' gross receipts by other local governments.
(b)
Where the business has locations outside of the city and taxation is levied for criteria other than gross receipts in the other local governments, the city shall not assess more than the allotted share of gross receipts for the local operation.
(Ord. No. O-008-15, § 9, 1-22-2015)
Sec. 18-93. - Taxation of professionals.
(a)
Practitioners of professions, as defined in section 18-85, shall elect for their occupation tax either:
(1)
The occupation tax as set forth in section 18-88; or
(2)
A tax of $400.00 for each practitioner who is licensed to provide the service, such tax to be paid at the practitioner's office or location.
(b)
Such election shall be made or changed on or before February 1 of the year in which the election is to be changed and must be a written request.
(Ord. No. O-008-15, § 10, 1-22-2015)
State Law reference— Taxation of professionals, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-10(g).
Sec. 18-94. - Financial institutions.
An occupation tax of 0.25 percent of gross receipts of each financial institution or the sum of $1,000.00, whichever is greater, is imposed against each financial institution operating within the corporate limits of the city.
(Ord. No. O-008-15, § 11, 1-22-2015)
State Law reference— Authority for above fee, O.C.G.A. §§ 48-13-9(c), 48-6-94.
Sec. 18-95. - Time for payment of fees, tax, certificates.
Each administrative fee, regulatory fee and occupation tax, as applicable, shall be for the calendar year. Any administrative fee, regulatory fee and occupation tax, as applicable, shall be payable January 1 of each year and shall be delinquent if not paid by April 1 of each year. Any amounts due which are not paid by April 1 of that year shall be subject to the penalties for delinquency as described in this article. For any new profession, occupation or business begun in the city after January 1 of any given year, the administrative fee, regulatory fee and occupation tax, as applicable, shall be due immediately upon beginning business and, if not paid immediately, or by April 1 in the year of commencement of the business, whichever shall occur later, it shall be delinquent. A regulatory fee certificate and/or an occupation tax certificate, indicating that the profession, occupation, or business has paid the fees and/or taxes owed, as applicable, shall be issued by the city clerk. Any person practicing an occupation or operating a business without valid certificates issued to him shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 18-102; provided, however, that those practicing professions or any other occupation whose qualification to practice or perform their profession or occupation is determined by the general laws of the state shall not be subject to this requirement.
(Ord. No. O-008-15, § 13, 1-22-2015)
Sec. 18-96. - Occupation tax certificate.
Every person subject to payment of the occupation tax amended by this article shall display a current occupation tax certificate in a conspicuous place at the location for which such certificate was issued; if the taxpayer does not have a permanent location within the city, the occupation tax certificate shall be shown to any police officer (or any other person charged with enforcing this article) upon request.
(Ord. No. O-008-15, § 14, 1-22-2015)
Sec. 18-97. - Exemptions.
(a)
No occupation tax shall be levied upon the following:

Any practitioner whose office is maintained by and who is employed in practice exclusively by the United States, the state, a municipality or county of the state, or instrumentality of the United States, of the state or a municipality or county of the state;
(2)
Those businesses regulated by the state public service commission;
(3)
Those electrical service businesses organized under O.C.G.A. title 46, ch. 3 (O.C.G.A. § 46-3-1 et seq.);
(4)
Any farm operation for the production from or on the land of agricultural products, but not including agribusiness;
(5)
Nonprofit agricultural product cooperative marketing associations pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 2-10-105;
(6)
Motor common carriers pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 46-7-15;
(7)
Persons purchasing guano, meats, meal, flour, bran, cotton seed or cotton seed meal or hulls in carload lots for distribution among the purchasers for use and not sale pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-5-355;
(8)
Persons selling or introducing into the city agricultural products or livestock, including animal products, raised in the state when the sale and introduction are made by the producer of the product and the sale is made within 90 days of the introduction of the product into the city; (See O.C.G.A. § 48-5-356)
(9)
Depository institutions pursuant to O.C.G.A.§ 48-6-93; (See this statute regarding the collection on occupation tax from banks and other financial institutions and section 18-94)
(10)
All purely eleemosynary businesses, institutions or corporations which have been created for charitable and benevolent purposes; such exemptions being granted as part of a plan for attraction and encouraging eleemosynary businesses, institutions or corporations under O.C.G.A. § 48-13-10(e); the term "eleemosynary business, institution or corporation" being defined in section 18-85;
(11)
Insurance companies governed by O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8 et seq.;
(12)
Any business where the levy of such occupation tax is prohibited by the laws of the state or the United States; or
(13)
Any state of local authority or nonprofit corporation.
(b)
No business license or occupation tax fees shall be assessed against vendors at any special events sponsored by McRae/Helena Crossroads Cities, Inc., and/or the Telfair County Chamber of Commerce for the purpose of, and as part of a plan for, economic development, pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-10(f)(1).
(c)
The exemptions and limitations contained in this article shall not be construed to repeal or otherwise affect in any way franchise fees, business taxes or other fees or taxes otherwise permitted by law.
(Ord. No. O-008-15, § 15, 1-22-2015)
State Law reference— Exemptions generally, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-13.
Sec. 18-98. - Occupation taxes levied on business to be transacted during current calendar year; filing of returns showing gross receipts during preceding calendar year.
(a)
All occupation taxes levied under this article are levied on the amount of business to be transacted during the current calendar year. However, for convenience of both city and the taxpayer, each business subject to the occupation tax shall, on or before the dates hereinafter set forth, file with the city the return hereinafter specifically provided for, showing the gross receipts of that business during the preceding calendar year. This return shall be used as the basis for making estimated payments on the occupation tax for the current calendar year. The actual and final amount of tax levied for business transacted in the current calendar year shall be paid in accordance with a final return to be made after the end of the year, in accordance with the procedure set forth here.
(b)
The person subject to said occupation tax on the current calendar year shall, at the end of the preceding year, and on or before April 1 of the current calendar year, file with the city, on a form furnished by the city, a signed return setting forth the amount of gross receipts of such business for the entire preceding calendar year. This return will be used to determine the final tax for the calendar year just completed and as an estimate of the gross receipts and occupation tax for the current calendar year.
(c)
Where a business subject to the occupation tax for the calendar year has been conducted for only a part of the preceding year, the amount of gross receipts for such part shall be reported in said return. Said return shall also show a figure putting the receipts for such part of a year on an annual basis with the part-year receipts bearing the same ratio to the whole-year gross receipts as the part year bears to the whole year. Said figure shall be used as the estimate of the gross receipts of the business for the current calendar year in establishing the business tax liability.
(Ord. No. O-008-15, § 15A, 1-22-2015)
Sec. 18-99. - Filing of return showing actual gross receipts during calendar year; procedure where taxes overpaid or underpaid.
(a)
On or before April 1 of each year, the owner, proprietor, manager, or executive officer of the business liable for said occupation tax levied for the year shall file with the city, on a form furnished by the city, a signed return setting forth the actual amount of the gross receipts of such business during the preceding calendar year.
(b)
If the amount of the occupation tax for the preceding year, based on the return provided for in this section and on the rate of said tax provided for in this article, is less than the amount of occupation tax theretofore paid by said business based on the estimate filed by the business, the difference in said amount shall be due and payable by the taxpayer to the city on April 1 of the current year and is delinquent if not paid on or before said date.
(c)
If the amount of the occupation tax for the preceding year, based on the return provided for in this section and on the rate of said tax provided for in this article, exceeds the amount of occupation tax theretofore paid by said business based on the estimate filed by the business, the difference in said amount shall be refunded by the city to the taxpayer; or if said business continues to be conducted in the city during the current year, such difference in amount may be credited by the city of the amount of occupation tax to be paid to the city by said business for the current year.
(Ord. No. O-008-15, § 16, 1-22-2015)
Sec. 18-100. - Evidence of state registration; other qualifications.
(a)
Each person who is licensed under O.C.G.A. title 43 by the examining boards of the Secretary of State's Office shall provide evidence of proper and current state licensure before any city regulatory fee certificate may be issued.
(b)
Any person required to obtain health permits, bonds, certificates of qualification, certificates of competency or any other regulatory matter shall first, before the issuance of a city registration, show evidence of such qualification.
(c)
Any person required to submit an annual application for continuance of that business shall do so before the registration is issued.
(d)
Each person who is licensed by the state shall post the state license in a conspicuous place in the licensee's place of business and shall keep the license there at all times while the license remains valid.
(Ord. No. O-008-15, § 18, 1-22-2015)
Sec. 18-101. - Liability of officers and agents, registration required, failure to obtain.
All persons subject to the occupation tax required in this article shall be required to take out the necessary registration for said business prescribed in this article. In default thereof, the officer or agents soliciting for or obtaining business shall be subject to the same penalty as other persons who fail to obtain a certificate. Each person commencing business in the city after January 1 of each year shall likewise obtain the registration provided herein before commencing such business. Any person transacting or offering to transact any of the kinds of business without having first obtained such registration shall be subject to the penalties provided herein and, upon conviction, shall be punished as provided in section 18-102; provided, however, that obtaining the registration shall not be a prerequisite for commencement of practicing a profession or any other occupation whose qualification to practice or perform their profession or occupation is determined by the general laws of the state.
(Ord. No. O-008-15, § 19, 1-22-2015)
Sec. 18-102. - Due date, court penalty and interest.
A penalty is imposed on each business and practitioner of an occupation which or who fails to apply for and obtain appropriate registration and to pay any and all regulatory and administrative fees and occupation taxes required by this article prior to April 1 of each year. Said penalty shall be in the amount of ten percent of the regulatory and administrative fee and occupational tax determined to be due and owing under the provisions of this article. Such delinquency shall also bear interest at the rate of 1½ percent each month or partial month of such delinquency. Penalties provided herein shall be in addition to any other penalties, civil or criminal, which may be applicable.
(Ord. No. O-008-15, § 20, 1-22-2015)

Sec. 18-103. - Inspection of records.
The city, through its employees, officers, or agents, may inspect the books of any person or business subject to an occupation tax to determine the accuracy of the documents and information submitted by a business or practitioner to the city. Failure to submit such books or records within 30 days from the date of a written request by the city shall be grounds for revocation of the regulatory fee certificate and/or occupation tax certificate, and to the extent applicable, shall revoke the right of the person or business to do business within the city. Adequate records shall be kept in the city for examination by the designated city agents. If, after examination of the books or records, it is determined that a deficiency exists as a result of improper designation of a dominant line of a business, profession or occupation, a penalty of ten percent of the deficiency and an additional interest of one percent per month of the amount of the deficiency shall be added.
(Ord. No. O-008-15, § 22, 1-22-2015)
Sec. 18-104. - Revocation.
Failure of any person to pay an applicable administrative fee, regulatory fee or occupation tax or any part thereof before it becomes delinquent or upon failure to permit inspection of books as required, shall result in any business tax certificate or regulatory fee certificate granted by the city under this article permitting such person to do business in the city for the current year being automatically revoked without any further action required of the city. No new occupation tax certificate or regulatory fee certificate shall be granted by the city for the operation of a business for which any part of the occupation tax or fees herein required are unpaid, nor shall such certificates be issued to a person who has failed to submit adequate records for inspection as required by section 18-103.
(Ord. No. O-008-15, § 23, 1-22-2015)
Sec. 18-105. - Execution, liens.
(a)
The mayor, or his designee, upon any tax or installment of any tax becoming delinquent and remaining unpaid, shall issue an execution for the amount of said tax against the person liable for said tax, including interest at the rate of one percent per month accruing thereon. The lien shall cover the property of the person liable for said tax as provided by this article, the Charter of the city and the laws of the state. The lien for said occupation tax shall become fixed on and dated from the time that such tax or installment thereof becomes delinquent. The execution shall be levied by the appropriate city agent upon the property of the person liable for said tax, and sufficient property shall be advertised and sold to pay the amount of said execution, with interest and cost allowed by law. All other proceedings in relation thereto shall be provided by this article and other applicable ordinances of the city, the Charter of the city and the laws of the state. The defendants at execution shall have the rights of defense, by affidavit of illegality of the tax or otherwise as provided by the Charter of the city and the laws of the state in regard to tax executions.
(b)
When a nulla bona entry has been entered by the proper authority upon execution issued by the city against any person or business defaulting on an occupation tax, or regulatory fee, the person or business against whom the entry was made shall not be allowed or entitled to have or collect any fees or charges whatsoever for services rendered or goods provided after the entry of the nulla bona. If, at any time after the entry of the nulla bona has been made, the person or business against whom the execution has been issued pays the tax in full, together with all interest, penalties and costs accrued on the tax, the person may collect any fees and charges for services and goods due the person as though the person had never defaulted in the payment of taxes; provided, however, that nothing in this subsection shall be construed to restrict in any way the ability of those practicing a profession or any other occupation whose qualification to practice or perform their profession or occupation is determined by the general laws of the state to perform that profession or occupation and to collect fees or charges therefor.

(Ord. No. O-008-15, § 25, 1-22-2015)

This article shall remain in full force and effect until changed by amendment adopted by the council. All provisions hereto relating to any form of tax herein levied shall remain in full force and effect until such taxes have been paid in full.
(Ord. No. O-008-15, § 26, 1-22-2015)

This article shall not apply to those businesses subject to separate ordinance of the city; those businesses or practitioners excluded under the terms of O.C.G.A. §§ 48-13-16 and 48-13-19(c); as well as those business or practitioners excluded or exempted under any other section of the Official Code of Georgia or by the constitution of the state, or by the laws or Constitution of the United States.

ARTICLE VI. - PEDDLERS AND OTHER TRANSIENT MERCHANTS

Sec. 18-126. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Charitable organization means any benevolent, philanthropic, patriotic, or eleemosynary (of, relating to, or supported by charity or alms) corporation, partnership, limited liability company, association, joint-stock company, trust, unincorporated organization or individual who solicits or obtains contributions solicited from the general public for charitable purposes.
Charitable purpose means any charitable, benevolent, philanthropic, patriotic, or eleemosynary purpose for religion, health, education, social welfare, arts and humanities, environment, civic, or public interest.
Door-to-door salesperson means any person who goes from house to house, from place to place, or from street to street, soliciting or taking or attempting to take orders for any goods, wares, or merchandise, including books, periodicals, magazines or personal property of any nature whatsoever for future delivery, or seeking information, money, donations or financial assistance for any purpose whatsoever, or soliciting or taking or attempting to take orders for services to be furnished or performed in the future.
Goods means merchandise, wares, fabric, material, furniture, clothing, shoes, or any other articles of commerce; provided, however, that "goods" shall not include the sale of motorized vehicles or boats.
Peddler means any person who goes from house to house, from place to place, or from street to street, carrying or transporting goods, wares, or merchandise and offering or exposing the same for sale, or making sales and deliveries to purchasers, or giving or leaving leaflets, pamphlets or other items that promote or advertise the sale of goods or the provision of services for profit.
Red light fundraiser means the solicitation of monetary donations or other donations of things of value made by a charitable organization or religious organization for charitable purposes at a traffic light intersection. For the purposes of a red light fundraiser, individuals shall not be included in the definition of charitable organization or religious organization.
Religious organization means an entity which is qualified as a religious organization under section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended.
Solicit and solicitation mean the request directly or indirectly of money, credit, property, financial assistance or other things of value from another. These requests may be accomplished by using any of the following methods:
(1)
Any oral or written request; or
(2)
The sale of, offer or attempt to sell, any advertisement, advertising space, book, card, chance, coupon, device, magazine, membership, merchandise, subscription, ticket or other thing.
The term "solicitation" shall be deemed completed when made, whether or not the person making the same received any contribution or makes any sale referred to in this article.
Transient merchant means any person, firm or corporation, as principal or agent, or both, which is not a regular retail or wholesale merchant with a permanent place of business in this state, but rather one who displays samples, goods, wares, or merchandise in any lot, building, structure, or vehicle for the purpose of securing orders for the sale of such item or items of like kind of quality for future or immediate delivery, or one who sells food, drink, produce, or like products from a temporary site or structure.
The terms "door-to-door salesperson," "transient merchant" and "peddler" shall not include any person, firm or corporation who solicits orders for future delivery of goods to merchants for the merchant's resale. The orders solicited from merchants shall be orders for future delivery only.
(Ord. No. O-034-18, 7-12-2018; Ord. No. O-036-18, 9-13-2018)
Sec. 18-127. - Exclusions.
This article shall not apply to:
(1)
Any person, firm, or corporation which solicits sales for charitable organizations, charitable purposes, or on behalf of religious organizations, political organizations, or political candidates.
(2)
Any person, firm, or corporation which conducts business at any industry or association trade show or at any craft show or festival of not more than three days in duration
(3)
Any person, firm, or corporation which sells items at a flea market, antique mall or similar place of business where such place has a business license.
(4)
Sales made to or by dealers, traders, manufacturers, merchants, or businesses at their established places of business within the incorporated limits of the city.
(5)
Annual sidewalk sale (otherwise known as "Peaches to Beaches") which is part of economic development promotional activities by the Telfair County Chamber of Commerce
(6)
Residential yard sales conducted in accordance to the provisions of [section] 18-145 of this Code.
Such persons who qualify for any exemption outlined in this section shall remain subject to the prohibitions set forth in sections 18-128 through 18-146.
(Ord. No. O-034-18, 7-12-2018)
Sec. 18-128. - Registration required; duration of registration.
Each peddler, transient merchant, and door-to-door sales person who does business within the city shall be required to obtain a registration from the city, in the manner specified in this article. Every such registration shall be valid for such continuous period as the merchant applies for as stated in section 18-131.
(Ord. No. O-034-18, 7-12-2018)
Sec. 18-129. - Requirements for corporations; registered agents.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any peddler or transient merchant or door-to-door salesperson which is a corporation to engage in business within the city unless such corporation shall comply with all applicable provisions of O.C.G.A. § 14-2-101 et seq., relating to corporations and obtain a registration.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any peddler or transient merchant or door-to-door salesperson which is not a corporation engage in business within the city unless such person shall have and maintain a registered agent(s) and shall obtain a permit.
(Ord. No. O-034-18, 7-12-2018)
Sec. 18-130. - Applications.
(a)
All peddlers and transient merchants and door-to-door salespersons shall file an application for a registration with the office of the city manager. Such application shall include, but not be limited to, the following:
(1)
The name, permanent address, and telephone number of the individual peddler or transient merchant or door-to-door salesperson, and if applicable the name and address of the corporate registered agent or office.
(2)
Type of business to be conducted and nature of goods to be sold.
(3)
The date and place of incorporation, when such peddler or transient merchant is a corporation
(4)
The address, location, or general area where business is to be conducted.
(5)
Written consent by property owner if the intended location is not the property of the applicant for the conduct of business thereon.
(6)
A copy of photographic identification.
(7)
If employed, the name and address of the employer.
(8)
The length of time for which the right to do business is desired.
(9)
If the goods are not available for receipt at time of purchase, the place where the goods or property to be sold are manufactured or produced, where such goods or products are located at the time the application is filed, and the proposed method of delivery.
(10)
A statement as to whether or not the applicant has been convicted of any crime, offense, or violation of any municipal ordinance, the nature of the offense, and the punishment or penalty assessed therefore.
(11)
Whether the applicant, upon any sale or order, shall demand, accept, or receive payment or deposit money in advance of final delivery.
(12)
The last three municipalities wherein the applicant has worked before coming to this city.
(13)
Such other relevant information as may be required by the investigation of this applicant.
(b)
As part of the application process, each applicant and each representative for a corporation shall submit to a criminal background check by the police department.
(c)
Applications in which the location of a transient merchant will unduly impede traffic or compromise public safety will not be approved.
(d)
As part of the registration process, each transient merchant applicant designating any one or more stationary addresses or locations owned by another where business is to be conducted, shall submit with such application a written statement showing the consent of the property owner for the applicant to transact business thereon.
(e)
At the time of filing this application, the applicant shall present his driver's license, if in possession of one, to the city clerk or her representative.
(Ord. No. O-034-18, 7-12-2018)
Sec. 18-131. - Fee.
Before any permit shall be issued under the provisions of this article, the applicant shall pay a non-refundable fee, based upon the duration in which he intends to do business in the city, as follows:
Per day: $50.00
Per week: $200.00
Such fee schedule shall be adopted by resolution of the McRae-Helena Mayor and Council and may be amended from time to time by resolution.
(Ord. No. O-034-18, 7-12-2018)
Sec. 18-132. - Display.
Every peddler, transient merchant, or door-to-door salesperson issued a permit under the provisions of this article and doing business within the city shall display his permit upon the request by any person and failure to do so shall be deemed an offense. If a stand, vehicle, or room is used in the conduct of business, the permit shall be displayed so as to be easily visible to the public.
(Ord. No. O-034-18, 7-12-2018)
Sec. 18-133. - Loud noise and amplification devices prohibited.
No registration holder under the provisions of this article shall shout, ring bells, blow horns, make loud noises or use any radio or amplifying system upon any of the streets, alleys, parks, or other public places within the city for the purpose of attracting attention to the goods, wares, or merchandise which such registrant intends to sell.
(Ord. No. O-034-18, 7-12-2018)
Sec. 18-134. - Additional regulations for transient merchants.
(a)
Transient merchants shall conduct business only in locations zoned commercial and in accordance with appendix A, zoning of the city Code.
(b)
All vehicles must be removed from the transient merchant's location at the end of each workday.
(c)
All vehicles associated with the business shall be parked on asphalt or concrete and outside of the right-of-way.
(d)
No transient merchant shall conduct business in between the hours of 9:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m.
(Ord. No. O-034-18, 7-12-2018)
Sec. 18-135. - Additional regulations for door-to-door salespersons.
(a)
The police department shall provide each registered door-to-door salesperson with an information card, and the door-to-door salesperson shall present this card to each household he visits. It is the responsibility of the salesperson to pick up this card and return it to the police department after expiration of the permit.
(b)
At any home or business where a "no soliciting" sign is posted, no soliciting shall be made.
(c)
No door-to-door salesperson shall enter a home except at the express invitation of the occupant. Any residential occupant may refuse entry, or to ask the door-to-door salesperson to leave the premises after allowing entry, and if the door-to-door salesperson should fail to leave the premises after such instructions, he shall be in violation of the laws of this state and ordinances of the city.
(d)
At each house where a door-to-door salesperson calls for sales, the door-to-door salesperson shall announce to the owner or occupant of the home his name and that he has been issued a permit by or has been registered with the City of McRae-Helena as a door-to-door salesperson and shall announce the purpose of his call and present the card provided by the police department.
(e)
A door-to-door salesperson shall at all times maintain a courteous decorum and shall not use opprobrious words to any homeowner or occupant. No door-to-door salesperson shall remain upon the premises of an occupant or owner after such occupant or owner has requested the door-to-door salesperson to leave or has indicated that he is not interested in purchasing the product.
(f)
It shall be unlawful for any door-to-door salesperson to represent by word, action, or deed that the issuance of a permit by the city in any way represents approval of either the action of the salesperson or his product or organization.
(g)
If a door-to-door salesperson shall secure an order from an owner or occupant, such salesperson shall inform the owner or occupant as to his rights under the home solicitation act of the state. This may be done by the door-to-door salesperson leaving a written card or document specifying the rights of a homeowner or occupant under the Retail Installment and Home Solicitation Sales Act (O.C.G.A. § 10-1-1 et seq.)
(h)
It shall be unlawful for any person to make any more than one solicitation call at the same premises for identical goods or services within any consecutive two-week period, without receiving prior invitation by the occupant of the premises.
(i)
It shall be unlawful for any person to solicit or attempt to solicit at a place of residence at any entrance or part of the building other than the main entrance.
(Ord. No. O-034-18, 7-12-2018)
Sec. 18-136. - Denial of applications; revocation of registration.
(a)
Applications for registration for individual peddlers and transient merchants and door-to-door salesperson shall be denied for:
(1)
Any person who has been convicted within the past seven years of any felony offense of moral turpitude;
(2)
Any person who has been convicted within the past seven years of any misdemeanor involving any crime of moral turpitude or physical violence
(3)
Any person who has, within the past seven years, had revoked his business license or transient merchant's license by a local government.
(b)
Denial of registration may be appealed by the applicant to the mayor and council, by filing of a written appeal, including the legal and factual basis for appeal, with the city clerk not later than seven business days after dispatch of written notice of denial to applicant. On appeal, the mayor and council may correct any errors of law or fact, but may not grant a registration to any individual whose background is in violation of the requirements of this article. The mayor and council will convene within 45 days of receiving an appeal to render a decision.
(c)
Registrations may be revoked or otherwise sanctioned in the same manner and upon the same basis as for the revocation of business licenses and occupation tax registrations.
(Ord. No. O-034-18, 7-12-2018)
Sec. 18-137. - Refusing to leave.
Any peddler, transient merchant, or door-to-door salesperson who enters upon premises owned, leased, or rented by another and refuses to leave such premises after having been notified by the owner or occupant of such premises, or his agent, to leave the same and not return to such premises, shall be deemed guilty of an offense.
(Ord. No. O-034-18, 7-12-2018)
Sec. 18-138. - Misrepresentation.
It shall be unlawful for any peddler to make false or fraudulent statements concerning the quality of his goods, wares, merchandise or services for the purpose of inducing another to purchase the same.
(Ord. No. O-034-18, 7-12-2018)
Sec. 18-139. - Hours of operation.
It shall be unlawful for any transient merchant or door-to-door salesperson to operate business within the city between the hours of 9:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m. the following morning. Additionally, door-to-door salespersons and peddlers shall not operate business at any time on Sundays, except by specific appointment with or invitation from the prospective customer.
(Ord. No. O-034-18, 7-12-2018)
Sec. 18-140. - False information.
It shall be unlawful for any person to give any false or misleading information in connection with his application for a permit required by this article.
(Ord. No. O-034-18, 7-12-2018)
Sec. 18-141. - Investigation.
Upon receipt of an application for a permit required by this article, the original shall be referred to the chief of police, who shall cause such investigation of the applicant's business and moral character as he deems necessary for the protection of the public good. The chief of police shall complete his investigation within one week of receiving the application.
(Ord. No. O-034-18, 7-12-2018)
Sec. 18-142. - Issuance.
If, as a result of the investigation, the character and business responsibility of the applicant are found to be satisfactory, the chief of police shall endorse on the application his approval and return the application to the city clerk, who shall deliver the permit to the applicant.
(Ord. No. O-034-18, 7-12-2018)
Sec. 18-143. - Contents of permit.
Each permit issued under this article shall contain the signature and seal of the issuing officer and shall show the name and address of the permittee, the class of the permit issued and the kind of goods to be sold thereunder, the amount of fee paid, the date of issuance, and the length of time and same shall be operative, as well as the permit number.
(Ord. No. O-034-18, 7-12-2018)
Sec. 18-144. - Duration.
Every permit issued under the provisions of this article shall be valid for the period of time stated therein, but in no event shall any such permit be issued for a period of time in excess of one month.
(Ord. No. O-034-18, 7-12-2018)
Sec. 18-145. - Residential yard sales.
Residents of the City of McRae-Helena may have up to four yard sales per year. A permit fee of $3.00 per day and an application shall be submitted to city hall. Yard sale duration shall not exceed two consecutive days. Yard sales authorized by this provision shall be limited to residential property in McRae-Helena and shall not occur in commercial districts.
(Ord. No. O-034-18, 7-12-2018)
Sec. 18-146. - Enforcement and penalty for violation.
(a)
The city manager or her designee shall be the enforcement officer for the provisions of this article and shall insure that the provisions are complied with prior to the issuing of any license.
(b)
Any person, firm, partnership, or corporation that violates the terms of this division shall upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-8 of this Code.
(Ord. No. O-034-18, 7-12-2018)
Sec. 18-147. - Solicitation banned within fifteen feet of the right-of-way of city intersections.
It shall be unlawful for any person, corporation, partnership, limited liability company, or organization of any type to solicit within 15 feet of the right-of-way of any road intersection in the city unless otherwise allowed by this article.
(Ord. No. O-036-18, 9-13-2018)
Sec. 18-148. - Red light fundraisers allowed in certain locations at certain times.
(a)
Notwithstanding the foregoing section, red light fundraisers, as defined in this article, shall be permitted at the intersections of Third Avenue and Oak Street and Third Avenue and Martin Luther King, Jr. Boulevard at certain times and upon certain conditions as provided by this article.
(b)
Red light fundraisers shall only be held on Saturdays and will begin no earlier than 9:00 a.m. and will end no later than 12:00 p.m.
(Ord. No. O-036-18, 9-13-2018)
Sec. 18-149. - Conduct of solicitation at red light fundraiser.
(a)
It shall be unlawful with respect to any person, organization, or entity participating in a red light fundraiser on or within 15 feet of any public right-of-way, to conduct its business in such a manner that the flow of vehicular traffic is impeded. Solicitations from travel lanes shall not be permitted; provided however, that walking alongside a line of cars may be permitted if the person stays out of the travel lanes and the solicitation occurs when the traffic light is red and cars are stopped. No sales or gifts to vehicle occupants shall be permitted.
(b)
No person under the age of 15 shall be allowed to set foot on the roadway at any red light fundraiser.
(c)
For every six people participating at a red light fundraiser who are age 18 or under, there shall be at least one person designated to the city as a supervisor of them. Such supervisors shall be 19 years of age or older.
(d)
Every person participating at a red light fundraiser shall at all times wear an orange or green safety vest satisfactory to the McRae-Helena Police Department. In no event shall a brightly colored shirt be allowed to substitute for a safety vest.
(e)
The city shall not be liable for any accidents, injuries, or damages incurred relating to any red light fundraisers.
(Ord. No. O-036-18, 9-13-2018)
Sec. 18-150. - Application for red light fundraiser permit.
(a)
Each organization desiring to conduct a red light fundraiser shall file an application for a permit to do so with the office of the city manager. Such application shall include, but not be limited to, the following:
(1)
The name, permanent address, and telephone number of the corporate registered agent or office of the organization or other such person authorized to legally contract on behalf of the organization.
(2)
The intersection at which the fundraiser is to be conducted.
(3)
A copy of photographic identification.
(4)
The date and length of time for which the fundraiser is to be conducted.
(5)
The designation of any supervisors for those participants whom supervision is required for by this article.
(6)
The execution of a hold harmless and release agreement with the city.
(b)
Organizations shall make an application to the city for a permit to conduct a red light fundraiser no later than two weeks prior to the requested date of the fundraiser.
(c)
Only one application for a red light fundraiser shall be approved for any organization during the calendar year.
(d)
Any application for a requested fundraiser date shall be received by the city at least two weeks in advance of the requested date.
(e)
There shall be no more than one organization holding a red light fundraiser at any intersection at a given time. Application approvals shall be made on a first come, first served basis.
(f)
All applicants for a permit to conduct a red light fundraiser shall provide proof of general liability insurance including products liability and property damage in the amount of $1,000,000.00 combined single limit. A certificate of insurance and a blanket or specific endorsement shall be delivered to the city clerk prior to the issuance of a permit and shall name as additional insureds the city and its employees regarding any liabilities that may arise in connection with the operations of the applicant.
(Ord. No. O-036-18, 9-13-2018)
Sec. 18-151. - Red light fundraiser permit fee.
There shall initially be no fee for the permit required to conduct a red light fundraiser. However, the city reserves the right to require an applicant to pay a non-refundable fee for obtaining such permit, which shall be set by the city from time to time.
(Ord. No. O-036-18, 9-13-2018)
Sec. 18-152. - Issuance and contents of red light fundraiser permit.
Upon the approval of any application for a permit to conduct a red light fundraiser, the city shall cause to be issued a written permit therefore. Such permit shall contain the signature of the city clerk or city manager and shall show the name and address of the permittee, the date of issuance, the approved date for the fundraiser, the duration of the fundraiser, and a permit number.
(Ord. No. O-036-18, 9-13-2018)
Sec. 18-153. - Applicability of other ordinances to red light fundraisers.
All other ordinances within the McRae-Helena City Code not specifically addressed by this article shall remain applicable to organizations conducting red light fundraisers.
(Ord. No. O-036-18, 9-13-2018)
Sec. 18-154. - Violations of red light fundraiser ordinance and penalties.
Any organization conducting a red light fundraiser that violates any provision of this article or any other provision of the McRae-Helena City Code or who makes any misrepresentation to the city in conjunction with its application for or conduct of a red light fundraiser shall be subject to the immediate revocation of its permit. Any such violations may also serve as grounds for the denial of future applications for a duration to be determined by the McRae-Helena Police Chief according to the severity of the violation or misrepresentation. Any objection to the validity of such denial may be appealed directly to the city council.
(Ord. No. O-036-18, 9-13-2018)
