ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 62-1. - Cash bonds.
(a)
The chief of police, the captains of police and the desk sergeants of the police department of the city are hereby designated to accept cash bonds under the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 17-6-10, whereby designated officials of municipalities are authorized to accept cash bonds and to issue a receipt to the applicant for same.
(b)
All cash bonds so accepted shall be posted or accepted at the office of the police department in the city hall and at no other place, and likewise at the time such bond is so posted or accepted a receipt shall be issued at such office and at no other place.
(Code 1967, § 2-10)
Sec. 62-2. - Charge for furnishing information relative to police records.
The chief of police is authorized to make a charge for furnishing information as to arrest records created by and in the custody of the police department. Such charges shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges.
(Code 1967, § 17½-1)
Secs. 62-3—62-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - POLICE DEPARTMENT, IDENTIFICATION DIVISION

Sec. 62-31. - Penalty for violation of article.
Any person who violates any provision of this article may, upon conviction, be punished by the provisions of section 1-12.
(Code 1967, § 17½-30)
Sec. 62-32. - Maintenance of identification and fingerprint division for certain persons.
(a)
The chief of police or the chief of police's representative shall maintain an identification and fingerprint division within the police department of the city on the following persons:
(1)
Those persons arrested by the city police department and charged with a violation of a city ordinance or state or federal law.
(2)
Those persons engaged in the operation of cars for hire, and owners of same.
(3)
Those persons engaged in the operation or owners of a health club or reducing salon.
(4)
Those persons including, but not limited to, managers, shift managers and/or persons responsible for on-site supervision of the premises, who control the sale or service of any alcoholic beverage within the city limits in any store, restaurant or club or any other type of establishment.
(b)
A correct identification system shall be maintained with the fingerprints, photographs and personal history of those persons named in subsection (a) of this section. The records maintained in this section will be classified as "Temporary-Long term" as defined by the Georgia Secretary of State's Office — Archive Division and will be retained for at least the minimum time period required by the retention schedule published by the Georgia Secretary of State's Office — Archive Division.
(Code 1967, §§ 17½-21, 17½-22; Ord. No. O-07-02, 1-17-07; Ord. No. O-22-11 , § 2(Exh. A), 10-17-22)
Sec. 62-33. - Application by persons specified in section 62-32(4) to be fingerprinted and photographed; work permit required; contents, cost and renewal.
Those persons in any way mentioned in section 62-32(4) shall, before engaging in any of the services mentioned, make application to the chief of police, for the purpose of being fingerprinted and photographed and shall have received a work permit which shall be valid for 12 months from the date of issue. This permit shall bear the name, date of birth, date of issue, person issuing the permit and a photograph of the applicant. The cost of this permit shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges for the first year and for each additional year thereafter. The chief of police may deem it not necessary to refingerprint each applicant at the end of a 12-month period after a check of records, but a renewal permit will be required.
(Code 1967, § 17½-23)
Sec. 62-34. - Provisions of section 62-33 applicable to persons specified in section 62-32(2), (3).
Those persons engaged in any way with section 62-32(2), (3) shall be governed by and issued work permits as provided in section 62-33.
(Code 1967, § 17½-24)
Sec. 62-35. - Permit to be kept on premises or in vehicle for hire; permits required under section 62-32(2) nontransferable.
(a)
Any permit issued by the chief of police for persons under section 62-32(2), (3) or (4) shall be kept on the premises where the applicant is employed or in the vehicle for hire, and shall be the property of the applicant unless revoked or expired.
(b)
Any permit issued under section 62-32(2) shall not be transferable to any other company for car or cars for hire.
(Code 1967, § 17½-25)
Sec. 62-36. - Revocation of permit; appeal from denial of permit.
(a)
Any and all permits issued by the chief of police shall be revoked by the chief of police, the mayor, or the city commission upon proof of any violations of this article, or any laws or ordinances of the city or any state or federal laws which are pertinent to the business covered by such permit.
(b)
Any person denied a permit for any of the purposes mentioned in this article may have the right to appeal to the city commission.
(Code 1967, § 17½-26)
Sec. 62-37. - Records to be used for purpose of law enforcement and licensing only.
Any and all records maintained in the identification and fingerprint division shall be of use for the purpose of law enforcement and licensing only and shall not be a matter of public record.
(Code 1967, § 17½-27)
Sec. 62-38. - Right to review and challenge records.
Any person having a record in the identification division of the city police department shall have the right to review any and all records maintained on the person, upon making written request to the chief of police, and then being fingerprinted and identifying themselves to challenge the accuracy of such records.
(Code 1967, § 17½-28; Ord. No. O-22-11 , § 2(Exh. A), 10-17-22)
