Sec. 28-1. - Financial institutions.
The following type of business shall be exempted from the general provisions of classes V and VI, and shall be treated as follows:
(1)
Gross receipts tax imposed on banks. There is hereby levied for each year an annual business license tax upon each banking institution doing business within the city, based solely upon gross receipts, in an amount equal to twenty-five-hundredths percent of the gross receipts of such bank during the calendar year, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(a). Gross receipts as used in this subsection shall mean the total amount of revenue generated from sources, as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-95.
(2)
Gross receipts tax imposed on savings and loan associations. There is hereby levied for each year an annual business license tax upon each savings and loan association doing business within the city, based solely upon gross receipts, in an amount equal to twenty-five-hundredths percent of the gross receipts of such savings and loan association during the calendar year, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(a). Gross receipts as used in this subsection shall mean the total amount of revenue generated from sources as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-95.
(3)
Filing of gross receipts return by depositing financial institutions; due date. Every depository financial institution doing business in this city and subject to this tax as authorized by O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93 shall file a return of its gross receipts with the city tax office on or before March 1 of the year following the year in which such gross receipts are measured. Such return shall be in the manner and form as provided by O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(d). In the event fees go unpaid after the due date, a ten percent penalty will be charged based upon the past due amount, and interest at the rate of one percent per month shall accrue until the fees are paid in full.
(4)
Assessment and collection. The city tax department shall assess and collect the tax provided for by this section based upon the information provided in the returns as filed by each depository financial institution annually.
(Code 1989, § 12-17; Ord. No. 193, §§ 11.1—11.4, 12-15-1983; Ord. No. 506, 9-23-2002)
