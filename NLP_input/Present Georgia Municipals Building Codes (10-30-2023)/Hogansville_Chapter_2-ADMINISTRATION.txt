ARTICLE II. - MAYOR AND COUNCIL[2]
Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
Charter reference— City council and mayor, art. II.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-31. - Compensation.
The salary of the mayor and council members shall be as established from time to time by the city council. Each member of the governing authority shall be reimbursed for expenses actually and necessarily incurred in carrying out their official duties. Reimbursement shall be made upon submission of an expense voucher to the city manager, drawn in accordance with terms and procedures of the city and supported by proper documentation.
(Code 1988, § 2-1-1; Ord. No. 17-0403 , § 1, 4-3-2017; Ord. No. 18-0305A , § 1, 3-5-2018)
State Law reference— Compensation, Ga. Const. art. IX, § I, ¶ III; compensation of governing authority, O.C.G.A. § 36-35-4.
Sec. 2-32. - Mayor pro tem.
During the absence or disability of the mayor, including during a vacancy in the office of mayor, the mayor pro tem, as chosen by the council, shall be clothed with all the rights and privileges of the mayor and shall perform the duties of the office of mayor so long as such absence or disability shall continue.
(Ord. No. 12-11-19, § 1, 11-19-2012)

DIVISION 2. - MEETINGS[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
Charter reference— Meetings, § 2.05.
State Law reference— Meetings of governing bodies open to public, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1 et seq.

Sec. 2-51. - Regular meetings.
Regular meetings of the city council are held at the city hall or at such other place as may be designated, on the first and third Monday of each month at 7:00 p.m.
(Code 1988, § 2-1-11)
Sec. 2-52. - Meeting days falling on holidays.
In the event a regular meeting date for the city council falls on a holiday, the regular meeting shall be canceled and shall be held the next day at 7:00 p.m. at city hall in the council room or at such other place designated by the mayor.
(Code 1988, § 2-1-12)
Sec. 2-53. - Adjourned meetings.
If a quorum shall fail to attend any regular or special meeting of the city council or if for any reason any meeting of the council shall fail to complete transaction of the business before it, the meeting may be adjourned to any date prior to the next regular meeting agreed upon by a majority of the members present.
(Code 1988, § 2-1-13)
Sec. 2-54. - Order of business.
(a)
The order of business at all regular meetings shall be as follows:
(1)
Invocation;
(2)
Approval of minutes of last meeting, with corrections, if any, to be made in the minutes;
(3)
Old business;
(4)
New business;
(5)
Reports:
a.
City manager;
b.
Committees; and
(6)
Appearances.
(b)
If the city council directs any matter to be the special business of a future meeting, that matter shall have precedence over all other business at that future meeting.
(c)
No proposition shall be entertained by the city council until it has been seconded, and every proposition shall, when required by the mayor or any member, be reduced to writing.
(Code 1988, § 2-1-14)
Sec. 2-55. - Rules of procedure.
Except as otherwise provided by ordinance, the procedures of the city council shall be governed by Robert's Rules of Order.
(Code 1988, § 2-1-15)
Sec. 2-56. - Reading and adoption of ordinances.
(a)
An ordinance may be amended, corrected or revised or a substitute ordinance introduced and amended, corrected or revised at any time after the first reading but prior to the vote on its adoption or rejection.
(b)
Reading of the ordinance shall consist of the reading of the title or caption of the ordinance at the meeting at which the ordinance is introduced and reading of the title or caption of the ordinance at the meeting at which the vote to adopt or reject the ordinance is taken. At either such meeting, the reading of the entire ordinance shall be required when ordered by the mayor or by a majority of the members voting therefor.
(Ord. No. 07-04-02-A, § 1, 4-2-2007)

DIVISION 2. - DEPARTMENTS

Sec. 2-96. - Established.
The following departments of the city are established:
(1)
Legislative and administrative;
(2)
Personnel;
(3)
Clerk/treasurer;
(4)
Police;
(5)
Fire;
(6)
Ambulance;
(7)
Public works; and
(8)
Recreation.
(Code 1988, § 2-4-1)
Sec. 2-97. - Records management.
(a)
The clerk/treasurer department is responsible for records management activities and shall designate a person or officer to act for and in behalf of the city council in directing and coordinating all records management matters including maintenance and security of records.
(b)
No record shall be destroyed except in accordance with approved retention schedules.
(c)
Whenever any records are destroyed, a certificate of destruction shall be prepared and forwarded to the city council which shall show the type of records destroyed, the approved retention schedule number, the dates covered, and the volume destroyed.
(Code 1988, § 2-4-2)
State Law reference— Municipal governments required to adopt records management plans, O.C.G.A. § 50-18-90 et seq.

DIVISION 2. - PURCHASING

Sec. 2-141. - Purchasing agent; duties.
The city manager shall be the purchasing agent of the city. Within the limitations imposed by section 6.06 of the Charter, the purchasing agent shall:
(1)
Arrange and negotiate the purchase of all equipment, supplies and contractual services for the city, and sell or otherwise dispose of all surplus city equipment and supplies; and
(2)
Maintain an inventory of all materials, supplies or equipment owned by the city.
(Code 1988, § 3-1-2)
