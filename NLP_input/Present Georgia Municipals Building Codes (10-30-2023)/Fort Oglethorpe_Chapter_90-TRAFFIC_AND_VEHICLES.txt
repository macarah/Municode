ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 90-1. - Vehicle operation without insurance.
The provisions of O.C.G.A. § 33-34-12 are adopted as an ordinance of the city; and any person who operates or authorizes another to operate a vehicle without effective insurance on the vehicle or without an approved self-insurance plan shall, on conviction, be subject to the penalties of Charter section 4.13.
(Code 1986, § 10-1-2)
Sec. 90-2. - Use of skateboards, roller skates, go-carts, homemade vehicles on streets or right-of-way prohibited.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to ride or operate any skateboard, roller skates, go-carts, whether motorized or nonmotorized, or "soap box" type homemade vehicle on any street or right-of-way within the city.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to erect, place or use any ramp or other device designed to be used for the riding of skateboards on any street or right-of-way within the city.
(c)
Anyone intentionally failing to comply with this section shall be subject to the maximum fine and penalties which can be levied under the provision of the Charter.
(Code 1986, § 10-1-11)
Sec. 90-3. - Provisions saved from repeal.
Section 10-1-10 of the 1986 Code, on the subject of impounding vehicles, and section 10-1-4 of the 1986 Code, relating to truck traffic, are not repealed and shall continue in full force and effect pending a revision of the subject matter by the city.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 90-101. - On-street parking in residential areas.
(a)
Prohibited. At no time shall there be on-street parking of motor vehicles on public streets within the residential areas of the city that would hamper the efforts of the city in protecting the lives and property of the citizens of the city. The intent of this section is to use constructed driveways for the parking of motor vehicles in lieu of public streets.
(b)
Temporary on-street parking.
(1)
When no room is available for the parking of motor vehicles off the public streets of the city, individuals may temporarily park on the public streets provided that half the vehicle's width is off the shoulder of the road under the following conditions:
a.
When all driveway space on the occupant's lot is taken by motor vehicles;
b.
When unloading a motor vehicle and the occupant has no parking space available;
c.
When entertaining visitors and numerous vehicles are expected.
(2)
This section shall also include Hedekin Street, when no driveway is available, and that portion of Heys Street east of Cross Street as exceptions to subsection (a).
(c)
Enforcement. It shall be the duty of all law enforcement officers of the city to enforce the provisions of this section. Individuals violating the provisions of this section may be punished as provided in Charter section 4.13.
(Code 1986, § 10-1-8)
Secs. 90-102—90-125. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PARKING, STANDING OR DRIVING IN PRIVATE PARKING AREAS

Sec. 90-126. - Findings.
(a)
The city council has determined that there is a problem in the community with the parking, standing or driving of vehicles in private parking areas.
(b)
The city, pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 40-6-252, is authorized to adopt certain provisions of state law into its own municipal ordinance.
(c)
No person shall, after having been requested not to do so by a law enforcement officer or an authorized agent of the owner, park or stand in an occupied or unoccupied motor vehicle and repeatedly drive a motor vehicle through or within a parking area located on privately owned property and provided by a merchant, group of merchants, or shopping center or other facility for customers if:
(1)
The parking area is identified by at least one sign as specified in this subsection, and if the parking area contains more than 150 parking spaces by at least one such sign for every 150 parking spaces, each sign containing the following information in easy-to-read printing:
a.
Notice of this section and O.C.G.A. § 40-6-252;
b.
Identification of the property which is reserved for customers' use only;
c.
Identification of the merchant, group of merchants, or shopping center or other similar facility providing the parking areas;
d.
Warning that violators will be prosecuted; and
(2)
The motor vehicle is parked, is standing, or is being operated other than for the purpose of:
a.
Transporting some person to or from the interior of the place of business of the merchant identified by the sign in the parking area or to or from the interior of the shopping center or other facility so identified;
b.
Making use of a telephone, vending machine, automatic teller machine, or other similar facility located in the parking area;
c.
Meeting the requirements of the situation in which it has unexpectedly become impossible or impractical to continue to travel on the public roads; or
d.
Carrying out an activity for which expressed permission has been given by the owner of the parking area or an authorized representative of the owner.
(d)
Any person who violates subsection (c) of this section shall, upon conviction, be punished by a fine not to exceed:
(1)
For the first such offense, $50.00;
(2)
For the second such offense, $100.00; and
(3)
For the third or subsequent such offense, $150.00.
(Ord. No. 96-19, § I, 1-13-1997)
