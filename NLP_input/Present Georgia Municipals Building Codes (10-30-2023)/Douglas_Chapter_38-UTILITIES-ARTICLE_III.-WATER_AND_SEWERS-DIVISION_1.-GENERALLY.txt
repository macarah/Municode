DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 38-59. - Conditions for extending water mains outside city.
The city will make no extension of any water line or main outside the city unless the person desiring such line or main to be constructed shall agree to pay the total cost of the project, including labor and material, and provided, further, that such person agrees to use electrical service through the electrical department of the city.
(Code 1978, § 23-5; Code 1993, pt. II, § 90-5)
Sec. 38-60. - Maintenance of pipes connected to city sewer.
Each customer, wherever residing and however defined herein, is hereby required to maintain and keep clean the sewer pipes leading to and connecting from his plumbing system to the city street line or city sewer main, whichever is nearer.
(Code 1978, § 23-6; Code 1993, pt. II, § 90-6)
Sec. 38-61. - Fluoridation of water.
The city approves the fluoridation of the water supply in the city and approves the application by the city manager to the state department of health to fluoridate the water supply in the city.
(Code 1978, § 23-9; Code 1993, pt. II, § 90-7)
Sec. 38-62. - Water tap prerequisites.
The applicant for a water tap shall have his service pipe extended to the property line, at a point designated by the water department and terminating in a water-gate valve. In consideration of the tapping fee, which shall be paid to the water department in advance, the city will tap the main and extend service to the customer's valve at the property line.
(Code 1978, § 23-10; Code 1993, pt. II, § 90-8)
Sec. 38-63. - Exemption from certain self-inspection regulations.
Pursuant to the authority contained in section 3 of Act 1046 Georgia Laws, 1996, page 1633, O.C.G.A. § 8-2-26(d), relating to self-inspections of water and sewer projects by master plumbers or utility contractors, shall not be applicable within the city.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 90-9; Ord. No. 07-08.1-96, § 1, 7-8-1996)
Sec. 38-64. - Connection to publicly-owned water system; private wells.
Any residential, commercial or industrial facility within the city limits shall be required to connect to a publicly-owned water system provided the publicly-owned water system is within 100 feet of the property line of the facility. No private wells shall be installed within the city limits for any purpose if the city has a water line within 100 feet of the property line of the person attempting to install the private well.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 90-10; Ord. No. 08242015(C) , 8-24-2015; Ord. No. 08242015(D) , 8-24-2015)
