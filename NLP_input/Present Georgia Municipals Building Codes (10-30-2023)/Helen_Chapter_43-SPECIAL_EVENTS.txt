Sec. 43-1. - Purpose and definitions.
Preamble: The City of Helen encourages special events. The intent of this chapter is to set procedures for approval by the regulating authorities of the city for the special event and the apparatus required to efficiently bring special events to a successful conclusion, while encouraging special events which are compatible with the tourism industry, as well as the public safety and welfare of the city's residents, visitors, and merchants. The following definitions shall apply to the interpretation and enforcement of this chapter.
Special event: The term "special event" shall mean any organized activity having as its purpose, entertainment, recreation and/or education, such as a festival or celebration, foot or vehicle race, parade or march, rally or assembly which takes place on a public street, sidewalk, right-of-way, city property, or occurs on private property and impacts government services.
City Manager: The city manager of the government of the City of Helen, or his designee for the administration of this chapter.
Producer: Any person, group of persons, organization, association, club, or other entity responsible for planning, producing and conducting a special event.
Vendor: Any person or persons who engage in the sale to the public of any food or food products, goods, services, or merchandise of whatever nature from any location, either mobile or stationary, on a temporary itinerant basis on any public street, sidewalk, right-of-way or other city owned property.
( Ord. No. 15-02-01, 3-17-15 )
Sec. 43-2. - Permits required.
It shall be unlawful for any person, group, organization, association, club or other entity to conduct or cause to be conducted any special event within the city limits of Helen without having obtained a valid permit as required prior to such special event. All permit and application fees are non-refundable.
( Ord. No. 15-02-01, 3-17-15 )
Sec. 43-3. - Application requirements and fees.
(a)
Application. An application package shall be submitted to the city manager, a minimum of two months prior to the intended date of the special event (but no more than one year in advance thereof) and shall include the following:
(1)
Request letter on organization letterhead providing a description of the special event and listing all persons in charge of the special event along with a principal designated agent for the special event. A photo identification card of the designated agent must be provided, and the designated agent shall be fully responsible for the compliance with all provisions, including all financial requirements of this chapter, and other applicable laws.
(2)
Photos or renderings of all apparatus required to hold the special event such as signs, banners, inflatables, tents, offices and structures.
(3)
The time frame of the proposed special event should be clearly specified. Except for clearing of the area. no special event shall be allowed to continue beyond the time approved on the permit for the special event.
(4)
Identify what is needed from the city related to safety, law enforcement and clean up. A special event shall be limited to the specific geographic area(s) within which the special event is to take place and the producer of a special event shall be responsible for the provision of police and sanitation services within the boundaries of the special event for a period of two hours past the conclusion of the special event or until the area is cleared of all activities and structures related to such special event.
(5)
Identify by sketch or map all locations in the city which will be occupied by the special event. The outermost boundaries of the special event shall be fully and clearly delineated on a map, which shall be no smaller than eight and one-half by 11 inches in size, and attached to the application for a permit. All public streets and/or sidewalks within and adjacent to such area(s) shall be clearly identified. The manager, or designee, shall require, as part of the approval for the special event, that areas no less than five feet in width shall be designated and preserved for public circulation and access to adjoining business establishments outside the area(s) delineated for the special event. The producer of a special event such as a walk or run, as to which the above provisions would not be practicable, shall submit with the application for a permit, a map, which shall be no smaller than eight and one-half by 11 inches in size, showing the exact route of the course for the walk or run.
(6)
Identify any amplification of sound and apparatus to be used for amplification of sound.
(7)
A list of anticipated vendors sponsored by the special event with a description of the anticipated vendors and how their products and or services relate to the special event. Only vendors selling goods or services specific to the special event will be allowed.
(b)
Fees. An application processing and permitting fee of up to $225.00 based on the approvals necessary will be required at the time of the initial application. The schedule of fees for approvals, processing and permitting are as follows:
(1)
Standard application fees.
Application .....$50.00
Approval by city commission [if required] .....50.00
Parade permit for city regulated streets .....25.00
Parade permit for Department of Transportation regulated streets .....75.00
Signage approval and permitting .....25.00
A deposit of $500.00 will be required at the time of application approval, all or a portion of which may refunded at the conclusion of the special event depending on the condition of city property used for the special event.
(2)
Additional fees required: Additional fees to conduct a special event may be imposed which shall be based on all services to be provided by the government for such special event, and which fees shall be equal to the estimated actual cost to the government to provide such services. If, at the conclusion of the special event, the cost of government services is greater than the initial payment, the producer of the special event shall be billed for the difference. Failure to pay the outstanding amount within 30 days of the billing date shall be a violation of this chapter. Fees for the estimated costs of government services must be paid no later than 14 days prior to the date of the special event.
( Ord. No. 15-02-01, 3-17-15 )
Sec. 43-4. - Approval process and requirements at time of approval.
(a)
Administrative review. The city manager shall cause the application to be circulated to each government department and/or other agency whose services the manager determines would be affected by the nature and activities of the proposed special event. Each such department or agency shall review the application and note the services which it will be required to perform, the number of personnel to perform such services, the length of time to perform such services, the cost to perform such services, and any other information which would assist the manager in facilitating the special event and services required. A minimum of 30 days shall be allowed for the administrative review of the application.
(b)
Special conditions. Each department of the government reviewing an application may recommend in writing certain conditions or restrictions as deemed necessary to facilitate the special event, to comply with other laws or regulations, and/or to ensure the safety, health and welfare of the community. In reviewing the recommendations of the departments, the city manager, planning design and review board, or city commission may impose such conditions or restrictions as a part of any permit issued and such special conditions so imposed shall be construed to have the full force and effect of law as a provision of this chapter. A violation of such conditions or restrictions shall be deemed a violation of this chapter.
(c)
Review by planning design and review board. The application package will be reviewed by the city planning design and review board. The board will approve or disapprove the application if city commission approval is not required under this chapter: or shall recommend approval or disapproval is city commission approval is required under this chapter. The review of the planning design and review board shall be based upon the administrative review of the city manager and the city departments as described above: the compatibility and compliance of the application package with the requirements of this chapter: and the compatibility of the proposed special event with the city's tourism industry. Ordinances related to land development, as contained in the city code, are not applicable, and will not be the basis for the planning design and review board decision. However, all local and state regulations for public health and safety must be complied with by the special event producers.
(d)
City commission approval required in certain circumstances. If a public right-of-way will be required to be closed for two hours or longer; or if the special event will require the use of city property for two hours or longer; or in the event that because of the nature of the special event, additional law enforcement personnel or public works personnel will be required to be on duty; or if the city manager determines that additional commission approval should be required because of potential impact of the special event on the public safety or infrastructure of the city; then, in those circumstances. City commission approval will be required. If city commission approval is required, the planning design and review board will recommend approval or disapproval of the application to the city commission following their review pursuant to subsection 43-4(c) supra, and the city commission shall make the final decision regarding approval or disapproval of the application.
(e)
Final approval of permit. If the application is approved in accordance with the above, the city manager is authorized to issue a permit for a special event after the payment of all applicable fees and costs: together with such special conditions as are required by the city manager. City planning design and review board, and/or city commission. If the application is denied, the producer shall be notified in writing of the denial and the reasons for the denial.
( Ord. No. 15-02-01, 3-17-15 )
Sec. 43-5. - Liability.
The producer of any special event shall hold harmless the City of Helen, its officers, employees and agents from liability of damages arising from any acts or omissions emanating from a special event. Proof of such waiver shall be filed with, and made a part of, the application form. The producer shall be responsible for providing any and all other insurance that may be necessary for any special event and proof of such insurance may be required by the City of Helen prior to permit issuance. A City of Helen may require such insurance as a condition of the approval of the special event.
( Ord. No. 15-02-01, 3-17-15 )
Sec. 43-6. - Vendors.
(a)
Sales permitted. The sale of food or any merchandise or services of any type by a vendor shall be allowed as a component of a special event provided such vendor is approved and authorized in writing by the producer of the special event and shall be conducted in accordance with such conditions and limitations as shall be imposed in writing by the producer and submitted as part of the application for a permit. All vendors must be identified by the producer of the special event to the city prior to the special event. Vendors not identified to the city as part of the application process shall not be allowed to vend.
(b)
Authorization of vendors. Upon meeting the requirements of subsection 43-6(a), the producer of a special event shall have sole responsibility and authority to allow or disallow vending as a component of an special event and to designate the location and activities of such vendors. It shall be unlawful for any vendor to engage in such business at any location or in any manner not authorized by the producer of the special event, or in violation of any local, state or federal law or regulation.
(c)
Permit not required. Vendors authorized by the producer of a special event as a component of the special event shall not be required to obtain any separate vendors permit to operate during the period of the special event.
(d)
Food sales. The vendor of any food, whether hot or cold, as a part of a special event shall be subject to all rules and regulations of the county health department. It shall be the responsibility of the producer of a special event to assure compliance with this section by any such vendors.
( Ord. No. 15-02-01, 3-17-15 )
Sec. 43-7. - Additional rules and regulations for special events.
(a)
Glass containers prohibited. The use of glass containers within the area of any special event shall be prohibited.
(b)
Police services. Traffic and crowd control shall be provided by the Helen Police Department and the number of officers and/or special arrangements shall be determined by the chief of police. Costs shall be paid by the Special event permittee as determined above.
(c)
Toilet facilities. The producer of a special event shall be required to provide temporary outdoor toilet facilities at any special event at which 100 or more persons are expected to be in attendance. The number of toilets and their locations shall be determined by the director of the building inspection department.
(d)
Musical entertainment. Any musical entertainment shall be provided in a manner consistent with the peace and good order of the community. The producer of the special event shall be responsible for full compliance with the noise ordinance.
(e)
Temporary facilities. Tents, arbors, stages, grandstands or other temporary facilities constructed or erected as a part of a special event shall be approved by the director of the building inspection department and the fire marshal at the time of review.
(f)
Street closing. The complete or partial closure of any city street, sidewalk or other public way as a part of a special event shall be approved by the city commission in consultation with the chief of police. A request to close the state highway will also require the approval of the Georgia Department of Transportation.
(g)
Banners, signs, etc. Temporary, noncommercial banners or signs may be permitted overhead across city public streets for the purpose of designating "starting" and "finish" lines for a race. Small banners, pennants, signs or other decorations which do not interfere with vehicular or pedestrian traffic, and which do not damage or deface any tree or public structure, may be allowed as part of a special event with approval by the planning design and review board. The producer of the special event shall be responsible for the removal of such banners, pennants or decorations within 24 hours after the close of the special event.
(h)
Duration of permit. No permit issued under this chapter shall be valid prior to or past the date of the special event for which it is issued.
(i)
Cessation of activities. The safety, health, welfare and good order of all citizens and the community shall be first and foremost and every special event shall be conducted in such manner. The chief of police may terminate any activity, whether a part of or the entire special event, which does not meet this community standard. Upon notification of the special event producer in person by the chief of police to cease any activity, it shall be unlawful for such producer and/or any participants to permit such activity to continue.
( Ord. No. 15-02-01, 3-17-15 )
