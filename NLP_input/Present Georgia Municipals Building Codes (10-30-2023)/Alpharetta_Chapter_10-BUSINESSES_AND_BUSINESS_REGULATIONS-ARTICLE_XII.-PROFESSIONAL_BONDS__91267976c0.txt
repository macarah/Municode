ARTICLE XII. - PROFESSIONAL BONDS PERSONS[7]

Sec. 10-366. - Definition of professional bonds person.
(a)
Bondsmen or persons who hold themselves out as signers or sureties of bonds for compensation are declared to be professional bonds persons.
(b)
A professional bondsperson is one who holds himself out as a signer or surety of bonds for compensation who must meet the following qualifications:
(1)
Is 18 years of age or over;
(2)
Is a resident of the state for at least one year before making application to write bonds;
(3)
Is a person of good moral character and has not been convicted of a felony or any crime involving moral turpitude; and
(4)
Is approved by the sheriff and remains in good standing with respect to all applicable federal, state, and local laws and all rules and regulations established by the sheriff in the county where the bonding business is conducted.
(c)
The sheriff of the county in which the bonding business is conducting business or is seeking approval to conduct business shall initiate a criminal background investigation to ensure that a professional bonds person has not been convicted of a felony or a crime involving moral turpitude in this state or any other jurisdiction. The sheriff shall require the professional bonds person to furnish two full sets of fingerprints which the sheriff shall submit to the Georgia Crime Information Center. The center shall submit a full set of fingerprints to the Federal Bureau of Investigation for a national criminal history record check.
(d)
It shall be the duty of each professional bonds person approved by the sheriff in accordance with this section to provide the clerk of each court before which one or more of such professional bonds person's principals are required to appear with the business name, complete address, telephone number, and e-mail address of the chief operating officer or his designee of such professional bonds person for the purpose of receiving any notices that may be sent pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 17-6-71. Each professional bonds person shall have the duty to keep such information current and accurate. It shall be the duty of each clerk of court to keep, maintain, and update such information as provided by a professional bonds person.
(Code 1989, § 12-30; Ord. No. 139, § 2, 10-28-1974)
Sec. 10-367. - Permit—Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to be engaged in the business of professional bonds person until authorized so to do by a permit granted by the city council upon recommendation of the police chief and/or police committee. No business license shall be issued to a professional bonds person until such permit has been granted.
(Code 1989, § 12-31; Ord. No. 139, § 1, 10-28-1974)
Sec. 10-368. - Same—Suspension or revocation authorized.
The chief of police and/or police committee shall have the right at any time to suspend the permit of any person, or agent thereof, engaged in the business of acting as a professional bonds person for noncompliance with this article. The police committee and/or chief of police shall have the right to suspend, for a definite period of time, or to permanently revoke, any such permit.
(Code 1989, § 12-32; Ord. No. 139, § 6, 10-28-1974)
Sec. 10-369. - Same—Hearing on suspension.
The person, or agent thereof, whose permit is suspended shall be notified by the chief of police and/or police committee of the reason for such suspension and shall be given the right, within 30 days from the effective date of such suspension, to appear before the police committee and to show cause why the suspension should not be made permanent. Failure to appear within the specified time or any extension thereof shall, of course, automatically forfeit the permit.
(Code 1989, § 12-33; Ord. No. 139, § 7, 10-28-1974)
Sec. 10-370. - Exemptions.
Nothing contained in this article shall be construed as affecting the right of a citizen to sign a bond not for compensation, or the right of surety companies who have complied with the laws of the state regulating such companies to carry on their regular business.
(Code 1989, § 12-34; Ord. No. 139, § 3, 10-28-1974)
Sec. 10-371. - Contents of bond.
(a)
Each bond signed by any professional bonds person shall, on a separate affidavit, contain:
(1)
A description of all real property in the county owned in fee simple by such person, and the clear market value of each item of real property so scheduled shall be set forth;
(2)
A description of each mortgage, lien or other encumbrance affecting such real property, showing the maturity date and amount thereof;
(3)
A statement of the total amount of all bonds upon which the petitioner may, at that time, appear as surety; and
(4)
A sub-schedule of all bonds upon which the petitioner may, at that time, appear as surety which have been forfeited and not paid.
(b)
All schedules and sub-schedules required by this article shall be made under oath by the petitioner, provided that, if the petitioner is a firm, each member of the firm shall sign the same, and if a corporation, two principal officers thereof authorized by the corporation's bylaws shall sign the same, and the corporate seal shall be affixed.
(Code 1989, § 12-35; Ord. No. 139, § 4, 10-28-1974)
Sec. 10-372. - Submitting schedules.
The chief of police and/or police committee shall have the right and authority at all times to require professional bonds persons to submit schedules of property and of bonds upon which they appear as surety. In the event such schedules disclose the insolvency or inability of a bonds person to sign bonds because of an excess of liability over assets, then the committee shall have the power to bar such bonds person from signing further bonds in the municipal court for so long as such insolvency or inability exists. Any professional bonds person who shall fail to furnish such schedules on demand, or who shall furnish a false schedule, shall thereafter be barred from signing bonds in connection with cases in the municipal court.
(Code 1989, § 12-36; Ord. No. 139, § 5, 10-28-1974)
Sec. 10-373. - Violations.
Any professional bonds person violating this article shall, on conviction in the municipal court, be punished as provided by law. In addition thereto, any bonds person convicted of any violation of this article shall automatically be barred from signing bonds in the city.
(Code 1989, § 12-37; Ord. No. 139, § 8, 10-28-1974)
