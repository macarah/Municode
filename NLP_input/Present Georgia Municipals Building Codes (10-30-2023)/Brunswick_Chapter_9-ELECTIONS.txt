ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 9-1. - Conduct of municipal elections generally.
The county board of elections is hereby authorized to conduct all elections held pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 21-3-1 et seq. in and for the city and to perform all functions imposed by such law upon the city commission with reference to any municipal election. The county board of elections is hereby requested to conduct all municipal elections in and for the city and to perform such functions, including, but not limited to, voter registration, qualification of candidates, determining challenges to the qualification of candidates, designation of polling places, hearing of election contests, and every other function essential to the election process.
(Ord. No. 820, § 4, 7-11-1985)
Sec. 9-2. - Political party primaries prohibited.
No political party shall conduct primaries for the purpose of nominating candidates for election to the city commission. Candidates for election to the commission shall be nominated by petition. The nomination petition of a candidate shall be signed by at least one percent of the registered voters eligible to vote in the last municipal election for the particular office for which the candidate is qualifying.
(Ord. No. 820, § 6, 7-11-1985; Ord. No. 923, § 1, 9-6-1995)
Secs. 9-3—9-20. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - VOTER REGISTRATION

Sec. 9-21. - Use of county list.
The voter registration list of the county shall be used in all municipal elections held in and for the city.
(Ord. No. 820, § 1, 7-11-1985)
Sec. 9-22. - Supplementation of county list for use by city.
All persons who are lawfully registered to vote in municipal elections in the city, but who are not registered to vote in county elections shall be added to the voters registration list of the county prior to the use of the county registration list in a municipal election.
(Ord. No. 820, § 2, 7-11-1985)
Sec. 9-23. - Maintenance by city not permitted.
The city shall not maintain its own voter registration list for use in municipal elections.
(Ord. No. 820, § 3, 7-11-1985)
