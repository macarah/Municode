ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 8-1. - Driveway culvert installation on public roadways.
(a)
Prior to installing or having installed driveway culverts on public roadways of the county, the property owner of such property shall obtain a permit from the road superintendent of the county or its [his] designated agent. The driveway and culvert site shall be inspected and approved by the road superintendent of the county or its [his] designated agent prior to installation of any culvert on the right-of-way of county public roads.
(b)
Culverts shall have a minimum diameter of fifteen (15) inches and a minimum length of twenty-four (24) feet. Any exceptions in such measurements must be approved by the road superintendent or its [his] designated agent.
(c)
Driveways must be designed to prevent excess water from entering the public roads of the county, and, if required by the road superintendent or its [his] designated agent, the property owner must take whatever action necessary to satisfy this requirement before a permit is issued.
(d)
All persons obtaining a driveway culvert installation permit for culverts which are not to be installed by the county shall pay a fee of twenty dollars ($20.00); provided, however, should a person knowingly and willfully fail to obtain such permit prior to the installation of such culvert, the fee shall be sixty dollars ($60.00), and the failure to pay such fee within thirty (30) days of notification shall institute [constitute] a violation of this section and be punishable as a misdemeanor.
(Ord. of 12-6-94)
Sec. 8-2. - Weight and load limits.
The county shall inspect and determine the maximum load and weight which may be transported over each road in the county road system and shall post a legible notice showing such maximum load limit, each sign to conform to the regulations of the department of transportation, promulgated under authority of O.C.G.A. § 32-6-50. It shall be unlawful for any person to haul, drive, move or transport on any road in the county road system any vehicle, load, or weight which in any manner exceeds the maximum limits so ascertained and posted on each such road. On any county road in the county road system which does not have a posted load limit, the maximum total gross weight or load shall not exceed fifty-six thousand (56,000) pounds. Violators may be punished by fine not to exceed the sum of one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) or twelve (12) months imprisonment or both and any violator of this section shall be issued a uniform traffic citation and may be tried upon said citation with or without a prosecuting attorney. Any uniform traffic citation may be issued by any certified law enforcement officer of the state or county and heard in the probate court of the county.
(Res. of 7-17-01)
Sec. 8-3. - Policy for acceptance of certain private roads.
The county, acting through its board of commissioners, adopts the following nonbinding policy for the consideration for acceptance as public roads certain private roads presently located within the confines of the county, subject to the following terms and conditions:
(1)
In order for any private road to be considered for acceptance as a public road by the county, such roads shall meet the following minimum standards and conditions:
a.
A minimum right of way of fifty (50) feet in width, surveyed by a registered surveyor, and shown on a plat prepared in recordable form, with three (3) copies of the plat furnished to the county and the plat duly recorded in the public records of the county.
b.
Provide access to a minimum of five (5) individually owned tracts of real property in the county on which is located a minimum of four (4) separate inhabited full time residences for a period of time of at least one (1) year prior to the enactment of the county subdivision ordinance, which ordinance was enacted in September, 1986.
c.
Connect to a public road in the county.
d.
A minimum roadbed as approved by the county road superintendent of eighteen (18) feet, not including ditches, and if necessary the addition of an amount of gravel as stipulated by the road superintendent.
(2)
In order to be considered for acceptance as a public road by the county, such private road must be conveyed to the county by a general warranty deed conveying fee simple title, and a certificate of title furnished to the county certifying that such warranty deed conveys fee simple title to the county.
(3)
This policy does not mandate or require the acceptance of any particular private road as a public road by the county, and the county board of commissioners may exercise their discretion in the acceptance of any private road as a public road, except that no private road shall be considered for acceptance as a public road that does not meet these minimum standards and criteria.
(4)
This policy shall be amendable at any time by the board of commissioners of the county.
(5)
Any repairs or improvements to any private road accepted as a public road under this policy shall be at the sole discretion of the county.
(6)
This policy applies only to private roads which were constructed or built between January 1, 1975 and September 1, 1985.
(Ord. of 5-7-96, § 1; Mo. of 8-5-97)

ARTICLE II. - RIGHTS-OF-WAY ENCROACHMENT
modified

Sec. 8-10. - Title and jurisdiction.
The rights-of-way encroachment ordinance [this article] shall apply to the unincorporated area of Monroe County, Georgia and to all public and private utilities, and to any person who desires to locate a structure within any right-of-way within the county.
(Ord. of 10-16-07)
Sec. 8-11. - Definitions.
Administrator means the staff person designated to administer and implement the provisions of this ordinance. The county road superintendent is hereby designated as the administrator.
Owner means a person having sufficient proprietary interest in the utility or other entity seeking approval for a right-of-way encroachment.
Permittee means the person or agency to whom the permit is issued.
Rights-of-way means property owned or controlled by the county including but not limited to roads, easements, streets, alleys, bridges and pedestrian walkways within the unincorporated areas of the county.
Structure means a combination of materials to form a construction for use, occupancy or ornamentation whether installed on, above, or below the surface of land or water.
(Ord. of 10-16-07)
Sec. 8-12. - Establishment of encroachment districts.
An encroachment permit shall be required in conformance with the provisions of this article prior to the commencement of any construction activity within a public right-of-way such as but not limited to the following:
(1)
The most common utility facilities that are affected by this article involve the installation or repair of electrical lines, telephone lines, gas lines, water lines, sanitary sewer lines, fuel line, steam lines, television cables, overhead lines of the same;
(2)
Storm water drainage ditches and facilities, and storm sewer lines;
(3)
Landscaping within the public right-of-way.
(4)
Any other existing or future structure, public or private that desires to encroach on any right-of-way.
(Ord. of 10-16-07)
Sec. 8-13. - Compliance.
No utility company, public utility agency, public agency, or any person may hereafter construct, install or pave any structure within the public right-of-way, or physically enter or alter any public right-of-way without being in full compliance with the terms and requirements of this article and other applicable regulations, codes and ordinances of the county.
(Ord. of 10-16-07)
Sec. 8-14. - Work standards/specifications.
(a)
Quality of work. The work shall be performed in a workmanlike manner and all installations shall be done by the permittee/owner in such a way to leave free flows in drainage ditches, pipes, culverts or other surface water drainage facilities of the roadway or its connections. No part of any installation shall be attached to any portion of a bridge, culvert or other structure of the roadway without special authorization of the county road superintendent after detailed information is furnished as he may require.
(b)
Cutting of pavement. Where cutting of the pavement has been approved, an eight-inch concrete slab shall be poured over the ninety-five (95) percent compacted backfill trench and will rest on undisturbed soil (see attached detail). If the pavement is asphalt, the surface of the eight-inch concrete slab must be two (2) inches below the surface of the abutting pavement. The finished surface must be smooth and flush with abutting pavement using an asphaltic top, type "F", two (2) inches minimum. No pavement shall be cut unless specifically authorized by the permit.
(c)
Underground facilities.
(1)
Service and other small diameter pipes shall be jacked, driven, or otherwise forced underneath the pavements on any based or surfaced road without disturbing said pavement. No pavement shall be cut unless specifically authorized by the permit.
(2)
The facility trench shall be backfilled carefully after the facility has been installed in accordance with the standard practice for installing culverts and minor structures. In crossing roadways, the backfill must be made in six-inch layers and each layer firmly compacted.
Where roadway grass is disturbed, satisfactory replacement will be accomplished including adequate seeding of new grass. No driveway cuts will be allowed.
(Ord. of 10-16-07)

The attachment referenced in this section is not set out at length herein, but is on file in the county offices.
Sec. 8-15. - Minimum conditions for a permit.
(a)
County to be held harmless. The full and entire expense and cost of the facility installation and maintenance shall be borne by the permittee/owner and the permittee shall make necessary arrangements for traffic over said point during such work as may be directed by the administrator.
The permittee agrees by the acceptance of the encroachment to indemnify and hold harmless the county, its commissioners, officers, agents and employees from all suits or claims from any and all damages done by, or on the part of, the permittee, its agents, servants, officers or employees, or contractors engaged in doing said work, or any injuries or damages suffered by any person as a result of said work.
(b)
Liability for future relocation lies with owner. In case the county should, in the future, decide to widen and/or relocate an existing road, the county reserves the right to require the owner to take up and relay those sections of the facility within the right-of-way as may be necessary to accommodate such widening and/or relocation; the taking up and relaying to be the expense of the owner. This work shall be completed within sixty (60) working days after notification by the county, or within such other time as may be approved in writing by the county.
(c)
Notification of start work required. The permittee shall notify the county road superintendent of the location of the permit and the proposed date of execution, the name and telephone number of the company, utility, or agency that is performing the work.
(d)
Emergency repairs. In the event of an emergency, the developer, his agents, and/or contractors are authorized to make repairs provided application for a right-of-way encroachment permit is made within forty-eight (48) hours (or two (2) working days) of the emergency event.
(e)
Restoration of road facilities upon moving or removing encroachment. If and when the encroachment contemplated herein shall be moved or removed, either on the demand of the county or at the option of the permittee, the roadway and facilities shall be restored to the original condition at the expense of the permittee.
(f)
Permission of abutting property owners. It is understood that this permit does not in any manner grant to the permittee or release from the owners any rights lawfully possessed by the abutting property owners. Any such rights necessary from the completion of the work shall be secured from said abutting property owners by the permittee.
(g)
Public convenience and safety.
(1)
The permittee, his agents, and/or contractors shall at all times conduct the permitted work within the right-of-way according to the "GUCC Manual On Traffic Control Procedures For Utilities" and in such manner as to provide for and ensure the safety and convenience of the traveling public and the residents along and adjacent to the road and to offer the least practicable obstruction to the flow of traffic.
(2)
The permittee, his agents, and/or contractor shall not close to traffic any bridge, culvert, road, street, or highway until permission has been granted by the administrator or his designee and notice published with a map (if required by the administrator) in the local newspaper for the week prior to the closing. The permittee shall notify each abutting occupant at least twenty-four (24) hours prior to when a driveway will be cut or blocked for any length of time. Notice to law enforcement and fire protection is required at least twenty-four (24) hours prior to closing.
(3)
The permittee, his agents and/or contractor will maintain the roadway and adjacent driveways in a safe and passable condition at all times and will take corrective action to remedy problems.
(4)
Should the permittee/owner not respond to a notice, oral or written, that corrective action is needed in a timely manner, it shall be deemed grounds for revocation of the permit. In a timely manner shall mean the next day (within twenty-four (24) hours) unless an extension is granted to the permittee where circumstances beyond his control prohibit correction but a temporary solution can be agreed upon.
(5)
Should it be deemed necessary by the administrator, in a situation where permittee/owner, his agent and/or contractor are not capable of performing temporary repairs within the required time limits, the county road department may perform the temporary repairs. The county will in no way be responsible for any damage to utilities which might occur as a result of these repairs. The permittee will be billed for the cost of repairs at a rate of cost plus twenty (20) percent.
(h)
Penalty. Failure to comply with the provisions of this article shall be grounds for revocation of the permit and reason for not issuing future permits to the permittee or owner concerned.
(Ord. of 10-16-07)
Editor's note— An ordinance of October 16, 2007, enacted provisions intended for use as subsections 1.—8. To preserve the style of this Code, and at the discretion of the editor, said provisions have been redesignated as subsections (a)—(h).
Sec. 8-16. - Permit approval process.
(a)
Application. Application for a right-of-way encroachment permit shall be made to the county road superintendent who shall review the application for compliance with the requirements of this article. The administrator shall effect coordination with other county departments and agencies plus affected outside organizations and persons as needed and appropriate to accomplish a full review of all aspects of the application. At a minimum, the application shall include the following:
(1)
Complete application in full;
(2)
Sketch of engineering quality with dimensions showing proposed work;
(3)
Name and telephone number for 24-hour contact.
(b)
Variance procedure. Any applicant that is aggrieved by the decision of the administrator may file a request for an appeal for an interpretation, or a variance to the terms of this article with the county board of commissioners. The applicant shall be allowed to present the proposed use the board at a regular meeting of the commission. The board my uphold the decision of the administrator or modify the terms of the permit as it deems appropriate to accomplish the intent of this article.
(Ord. of 10-16-07)
Editor's note— An ordinance adopted October 16, 2007, enacted provisions intended for use as subsections 1. and 2. To preserve the style of this Code, and at the discretion of the editor, said provisions have been redesignated as subsections (a) and (b).

Sec. 8-51. - Notice required.
modified
All persons or firms harvesting standing timber in any unincorporated area of Monroe County for delivery as pulpwood, logs, poles, posts, or woodyard or processing plant located inside or outside this state shall provide notice of such harvesting operations to the Monroe County Governmental Authority or the designated agent thereof prior to entering onto the property if possible, but no later than twenty-four (24) hours after the job is completed.
( Ord. of 02-02-2021 , § 1)
Sec. 8-52. - Notice requirements.
modified
The notice of harvesting operations required by this article shall be provided for each separate tract to be harvested. Such notice shall be made in such form as prescribed by rule or regulation of the Director of the Georgia Forestry Commission, and shall include the following information:
(a)
A map of the area which identifies the location of the tract to be harvested and, as to those trucks which will be traveling to and from such tract for purposes of picking up and hauling loads of cut forest products, the main point of ingress to such tract from a public road and, if different, the main point of egress from such tract to a public road. If multiple points of ingress and/or egress will be used, all such points shall be identified;
(b)
A statement as to whether the timber will be removed pursuant to a lump sum sale, per unit sale, or owner harvest for purposes of ad valorem taxation under O.C.G.A. §48-5-7.5;
(c)
The name, address, and daytime telephone number of the timber seller if the harvest is pursuant to a lump sum or per unit sale of the timber owner if the harvest is an owner harvest; and
(d)
The name, business address, business telephone number, and nighttime or emergency telephone number of the person or firm harvesting such timber.
( Ord. of 02-02-2021 , § 1)
Sec. 8-53. - Notice delivery.
modified
Subject to the provisions of section 8-52 of this chapter, the notice required by this article may be submitted in person, by transmission of an electronic record via telefacsimile or email, or by mail.
( Ord. of 02-02-2021 , § 1)
Sec. 8-54. - Statewide notification system.
modified
(a)
Subject to the provisions of section 8-52 of this chapter, upon notification published by the Director of the Georgia Forestry Commission that a state-wide notification website or platform is available for public use, persons or firms wishing to utilize said website or platform to provide the notice required by this article may do so at their option, Monroe County will accept notifications submitted in this manner.
(b)
On and after a date specified and published by the Director of the Georgia Forestry Commission, use of the state-wide notification website or platform shall be mandatory and shall be the sole means of providing the notice required by this article; on and after said date submission of the notice by any of the means listed in section 8-53 above shall cease and will no longer be deemed acceptable or in compliance with this article.
( Ord. of 02-02-2021 , § 1)
Sec. 8-55. - Effective and amount of bond or surety.
modified
The notice required by this article shall not be or remain effective unless and until the person or firm providing such notice has delivered to the Governing Authority of Monroe County or its designated agent a valid surety bond, executed by a surety corporation authorized to transact business in this state, protecting Monroe County against any damage caused by such person or firm harvesting timber a valid irrevocable letter of credit issued by a bank or savings and loan association, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 7-1-4, in the amount of $5,000.00 may be provided in lieu of a surety bond. Such bonds or letter of credit shall be subject to the conditions set forth in sections 8-56 and 8-57 of this chapter. No more than one bond or letter of credit shall be required from each person or firm harvesting timber, regardless of the number of tracts harvested in the county for so long as the bond or letter of credit remains in effect. The bond or letter of credit required herein shall be valid only for the calendar year in which it was delivered.
( Ord. of 02-02-2021 , § 1)
Sec. 8-56. - Damage.
modified
The bond or letter of credit required by section 8-55 of this article shall protect Monroe County against any damage requiring re-ditching or repair of existing ditch structure or the removal of any harvesting residue, including treetops, debris, logs, pulpwood and other materials, placed in or around the county's rights of way caused by such person or firm tendering the bond or letter of credit. The proceeds of such bond or letter of credit shall be available to reimburse the county for any cost incurred to repair such damages or remove such debris in or around the county's rights of way. The proceeds of such bond or letter of credit shall also be available to reimburse the county for any costs incurred to maintain or repair county roads damaged by the ingress or egress of motor vehicles engaged in the harvest operations located within five hundred (500) feet of any point of ingress or egress of the timber harvesting operation. The right of Monroe County to call such bond or letter of credit in accordance with the provisions of section 8-57 of this article shall be in addition to any other remedies available to the county at law or in equity for damage to county roads or rights of way.
( Ord. of 02-02-2021 , § 1)
Sec. 8-57. - Claims.
modified
When damage results from a person or firm's harvesting activities, the Governing Authority of Monroe County shall make and provide a written claim to the person or firm causing the damage within thirty (30) business days after the governing authority becomes aware of the damage. Such claim may be given in person, by telefacsimile, email or mail. The claim shall describe the damage in detail and, in compliance with section 8-59 of this article, give the person or firm the opportunity to repair such damage within thirty (30) days of the notification; provided, however, the county shall be authorized to repair the damage immediately if the governing authority or its designee determines the conditions present a threat to public safety, health or welfare and, upon making such repairs, shall present to the person or firm and the issuer of the application bond or letter of credit an itemized list of expenses incurred as a claim as provided in this section the Governing Authority of Monroe County or its designee shall notify the issuer of the bond or letter of credit that a claim has been made and will be resolved or adjudicated to the term of this article.
( Ord. of 02-02-2021 , § 1)
Sec. 8-58. - Reserved.
modified
Sec. 8-59. - Procedure following claims.
modified
Within 30 days of receipt of the written claim described in section 8-57, the person or firm against which the claim is submitted may:
(a)
Repair such damage at his or its own expense with the approval and supervision of the Governing Authority of Monroe County or its designee. When repairs are completed to the satisfaction of the governing authority or its designee, the governing authority or its designee shall provide a written notification of satisfactory completion within five (5) business days to the responsible person or firm and to the surety issuing the bond or the bank issuing the letter of credit, thereby terminating the claim.
(b)
In the event of inclement weather or other factors preventing repair of the damage, request a thirty (30) day extension to repair the damage from the Governing Authority of Monroe County, provided that no extensions shall exceed ninety (90) days from the date the claim was tendered. Approval of any extension shall be at the discretion of the Governing Authority of Monroe County or its designee.
(c)
Appeal the claim to the Magistrate Court of Monroe County. Any such appeal must name the issuer of the bond or letter of credit as a party, who shall be served with all pleadings in the action and shall have the right to appear. The Magistrate Court will hear evidence and the action and shall have the right to appear. The Magistrate Court will hear evidence and arguments within thirty (30) days of the written appeal and issue a ruling within ten (10) days of such hearing. Any such appeal shall toll the thirty (30) day period, or any extension thereof, required by section 5-87 of this article. If the magistrate court rules in favor of the person or firm against whom the claim was made, the county shall have no right to recover any proceeds of the bond or letter of credit, and judgement shall be entered against the county. If the magistrate court rules in favor of the county the court shall determine the amount of damages to which the county is entitled to recover and enter judgment; accordingly, the Governing Authority of Monroe County shall be authorized to call the bond or letter of credit and recover from the proceeds thereof an amount equal to the judgement entered by the court, up to the total amount of the bond or letter of credit. The portion of any judgement entered in favor of the county that exceeds the amount of the bond or letter of credit shall be subject to collection by any additional remedies at law or equity.
(d)
In the event that person or firm against whom the claim has been submitted fails to take any of the actions allowed under subsections (a), (b) or (c) of this section within the time required therein, such person or firm shall be deemed to have waived any and all rights to contest the call of the bond or letter of credit.
( Ord. of 02-02-2021 , § 1)
Sec. 8-60. - Bonds required annually.
modified
If the person or firm tendering a bond or letter of credit pursuant to the requirements of the article continues its timber harvesting operation beyond the calendar year in which the bond or letter of credit was issued, the person or firm continuing the timber harvesting operation shall tender a new bond or letter of credit within five (5) business days after the first day of the new calendar year.
( Ord. of 02-02-2021 , § 1)
Sec. 8-61. - Surety revocation.
modified
In the event a bond or letter of credit tendered pursuant to the requirements of this article is revoked by the surety or bank, then a valid replacement bond or letter of credit must be delivered to the Governing Authority of Monroe County within five (5) business days after the date of revocation in order for timber harvesting operations to continue. In addition, if the person or firm tendering the bond or letter of credit caused its revocation, the amount of the bond or letter of credit required shall increase to seven thousand five hundred dollars ($7,500.00) after the first revocation, and ten thousand dollars ($10,000.00) after the second revocation caused by the person or firm tendering the bond or letter of credit. The maximum amount of the bond or letter of credit shall not exceed ten thousand dollars ($10,000.00).
( Ord. of 02-02-2021 , § 1)
Sec. 8-62. - Duration.
new
Submission of the notice required by this article shall authorize the person or firm submitting same to undertake the timber harvesting operation described in the notice and shall remain in effect until such time as the person or firm gives notice that the harvesting operation is complete; provided, however, that any change in the facts required to be provided for purposes of such notice, including but not limited to a change in the scope or extent of the operation, must be reported to the Governing Authority of Monroe County within three (3) business days after such change.
( Ord. of 02-02-2021 , § 1)
Sec. 8-63. - Violation.
new
Any person or firm that engages in a timber harvesting operation in the unincorporated portion of Monroe County without complying with the notice requirements of this article shall be subject to a citation and trial, and upon conviction shall be fined in an amount not to exceed one thousand five hundred dollars ($1,500.00) for each violation.
( Ord. of 02-02-2021 , § 1)
Sec. 8-64. - Applicability.
new
This article applies to activities which qualify as forestry land management practices or agricultural operations under O.C.G.A. §12-7-17(5) and (6) on land that is zoned for or used for forestry, silvicultural or agricultural purposes. It shall not authorize land disturbing activities incidental to development in conflict with the limitations set forth in O.C.G.A. §12-7-17(6).
( Ord. of 02-02-2021 , § 1)
Sec. 8-65. - No permit or fees.
new
Consistent with O.C.G.A §12-6-24:
(a)
No fee shall be charged to provide and submit the notice required by this article; and
(b)
No permit, including a driveway permit, shall be required of the person or firm engaged in a timber harvesting operation as defined by said code section. Persons and firms providing the notice required by this article are asked to consult with county officials responsible for roads and public works for the purpose of minimizing damage to the county's roads, rights of way and infrastructure, and are urged to follow recommendations from county officials. Notwithstanding the forging, the person or firm conducting the timber harvest operation bears ultimate responsibility for their actions, and nothing in this article shall preclude the Governing Authority of Monroe County from taking any and all legal action necessary to protect its property and the health, safety and welfare of its citizens.
( Ord. of 02-02-2021 , § 1)
