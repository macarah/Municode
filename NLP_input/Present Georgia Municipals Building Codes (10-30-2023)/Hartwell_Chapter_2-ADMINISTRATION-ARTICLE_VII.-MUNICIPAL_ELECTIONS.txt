ARTICLE VII. - MUNICIPAL ELECTIONS[8]

Editor's note— Ord. No. 2011-01, §§ 1—7, adopted July 5, 2011, enacted provisions which did not specify manner of inclusion. To facilitate indexing, said provisions have been included herein as Art. VII, §§ 2-461—2-467.

Sec. 2-461. - County conduction of municipal elections.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 21-2-45(c), and subject to the conditions set forth in section 2-463 of this article, the mayor and council of the City of Hartwell, Georgia hereby find and determine that it would be in the best interest of the citizens of Hartwell, Georgia, for the city to pay Hart County, Georgia to utilize the judge of the probate court of Hart County, Georgia, who serves as Ex-Officio Hart County Election Superintendent, and his staff, equipment and expertise in conducting city elections in the future.
(Ord. No. 2011-01, § 1, 7-5-2011)
Sec. 2-462. - County election superintendent duties.
Subject to the conditions set forth in section 2-463 of this article, the mayor and council of the City of Hartwell hereby request that, as of, from and after the effective date of this article, the Hart County Election Superintendent and his staff conduct any and all future municipal elections in the City of Hartwell, Georgia, and perform all duties as superintendent of elections as specified under O.C.G.A. tit. 21, ch. 2, except for calling municipal elections and qualifying candidates for such elections, which shall be performed by the current municipal election superintendent (the Hartwell City Clerk).
(Ord. No. 2011-01, § 2, 7-5-2011)
Sec. 2-463. - County conduction of municipal elections conditional upon intergovernmental agreement.
The findings and action of the mayor and city council, as set forth in this article, are all expressly conditioned upon, and shall not be effective unless, the city, Hart County, Georgia, and the Hart County Election Superintendent enter into an intergovernmental agreement regarding the Hart County Election Superintendent's conducting of municipal elections and performing of all duties of superintendent of elections, and outlining specific duties, costs and other details of such matters, and such intergovernmental agreement is in form and substance satisfactory to the Hartwell City Council. The approval of such intergovernmental agreement by the Hartwell City Council shall be effective upon approval by resolution of the Hartwell City Council, and it shall not be necessary for such agreement to be approved by ordinance.
(Ord. No. 2011-01, § 3, 7-5-2011)
Sec. 2-464. - Negotiation of terms of intergovernmental agreement.
The city manager of the City of Hartwell, Georgia, and the city clerk of the City of Hartwell, Georgia, are herby authorized to negotiate with Hart County, Georgia, and the Hart County Election Superintendent to determine the terms of the intergovernmental agreement described in section 2-463 of this article.
(Ord. No. 2011-01, § 4, 7-5-2011)
Sec. 2-465. - Effective date.
This article shall take effect upon its adoption, without any further action by the city council.
(Ord. No. 2011-01, § 5, 7-5-2011)
Sec. 2-466. - Repeal of inconsistent ordinances or resolutions.
Any and all ordinances or resolutions, or parts of ordinances or resolutions, which are inconsistent with this article shall be and are hereby repealed.
(Ord. No. 2011-01, § 6, 7-5-2011)
Sec. 2-467. - Severability of provisions.
The sections, paragraphs, sentences, clauses and phrases of this article are severable. If any section, provision or clause of this article shall be declared invalid or unconstitutional, or if the provisions of any part of this article as applied to any particular situation or set of circumstances shall be declared invalid or unconstitutional, such invalidity shall not be construed to affect portions of this article not so held to be invalid, or to affect the application of this article to other circumstances not so held to be invalid. It is hereby declared as the intent as the city council of Hartwell that this article would have been adopted had such invalid portion not been included herein.
(Ord. No. 2011-01, § 7, 7-5-2011)
