ARTICLE II. - PARADES, DEMONSTRATIONS AND PICKETING
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 58-26. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Assembly means a group of two or more persons who are together for a common cause or purpose, who stand, walk, congregate or assemble upon any of the sidewalks, streets or public property of the city, who evidence their cause by signs, slogans, common dress, solicitation of money or membership, public speech, or the distribution of written materials. The solicitation by one or more persons for money, or membership in any organization or group, or the distribution of written materials for the solicitation of money or membership in any organization, or an advertising or sales promotion upon any of the sidewalks, streets, or public property of the city, is prohibited.
Demonstration and picketing shall be given their usual meaning, but in any event, any such activity must first be defined as an "assembly" to be included in the context herein.
Parade means any procession on a public street, excepting funerals and military activities, containing ten or more persons or five or more vehicles.
(Code 1987, § 31-105)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 58-27. - Penalty.
Anyone found guilty of violating any provision of this article shall be punished as provided in section 1-7.
(Code 1987, § 31-109(1))
Sec. 58-28. - Appendixes adopted by reference.
All appendixes referenced in this article are on file in the office of the city clerk and are hereby adopted by reference as if set out at length in this article.
Sec. 58-29. - Conduct of activities.
(a)
All activities under a permit issued pursuant to this article shall be conducted under the supervision of the person or persons making the application, and shall be conducted in a peaceful and orderly manner in compliance with the applicable laws and ordinances.
(b)
In the conduct of such activities, no signs, shields or banners shall be placed or carried by any person upon the sidewalks of the city that extend beyond the width of the person's body, or that extend more than 24 inches above the person's head. Persons carrying any such signs, shields or banners shall be at least ten feet apart; shall not stand or loiter in front of any doorway, driveway or intersection; and shall not interfere with the general flow of traffic, whether pedestrian or vehicular.
(Code 1987, § 31-104)
Secs. 58-30—58-40. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PERMIT

Sec. 58-41. - Required; compliance with terms.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to promote, organize or hold, or to assist in promoting, organizing, or holding, or to take part or to participate in any parade, assembly, picketing or other public demonstration in the streets, on the sidewalks or other public areas of the city, unless a permit for it has been secured from the city clerk.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to conduct a parade, assembly or demonstration or picketing at any time other than the time specified in the permit therefor.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to conduct a parade, assembly or demonstration, or picketing along, upon or over any street, sidewalk or public area other than those set out in the permit issued as provided in this division.
(Code 1987, § 31-107)
Sec. 58-42. - Application; review and approval procedure.
Any person desiring to stage a parade, demonstration or assembly, or engage in picketing, on the public streets, sidewalks or public area of the city, shall make application for a permit to the city clerk not later than 4:00 p.m. ten days prior to the day that such proposed activity is intended to be staged or carried out. Such application shall be filed with the city clerk in writing except that any application for activity on any of the state highways of the city must be filed 30 days prior to the planned activity. Such permit shall be granted only after review and approval by the city manager following consultation with the chief of police and the city attorney. The city manager shall be empowered to determine and reasonably restrict the time and duration of the proposed activity, the number of units or automobiles to be involved, the number of persons to participate in such activity, and the streets or locations where such activity shall be allowed, so as not to impair the health, safety, and convenience of the public in general (see Appendix A, on file in the office of the city clerk, application form).
(Code 1987, § 31-101)
Sec. 58-43. - Payment for additional expenses.
After a review of the application for a permit to conduct a parade, assembly, demonstration or picketing activity, and as a condition precedent to the issuance of a permit for such activity, the applicant may be required to pay to the city any and all additional expenses incurred by the city for additional police protection, traffic control, and the expense of cleaning up the area in which the parade, assembly, demonstration or picketing activity is permitted to take place, if such extra expense is reasonably foreseeable. The city manager may require a cash deposit to be paid by the applicant in advance to defray such additional municipal expenses as herein set out. If no additional expenses are incurred by the city as a result of the parade, assembly, demonstration or picketing activity, or if only a portion of the deposit is utilized therefor, the city will refund to the applicant any excess funds held by the city. If the cash deposit is insufficient to defray such additional expenses, the applicant will be liable for the additional expenses as a debt owed to the city, and subject to payment. The applicant, upon accepting the permit from the city for the conduct of a parade, assembly, demonstration or picketing activity, expressly waives questions regarding jurisdiction and venue, and any civil action brought to collect any indebtedness owed for excess municipal expenses may be brought in the appropriate court in the county.
(Code 1987, § 31-108)
Sec. 58-44. - Considerations for receiving permit.
The city manager shall consider at least the following factors before making a decision under this division (see Appendix B, approval form):
(1)
Traffic densities involved on the streets or sidewalks during the time for which the permit is requested.
(2)
Police and fire protection required and available at the time and place requested.
(3)
Street and sidewalk condition in the area requested.
(4)
Other public or private activities known to occur during the same time and place which would have an impact upon public safety and convenience.
(5)
Sanitary facilities available in the area requested.
(6)
Noise impact analysis for the time and area requested.
(7)
Any other matter which is likely to affect the public order, safety, health or welfare of the citizens, including those people participating in the activity.
(Code 1987, § 31-102)
Sec. 58-45. - Number of permits.
An application and permit under this division shall be required for each such activity; provided, however, only one application and permit shall be required for each proposed parade, demonstration, assembly or picketing site and the picketing (see Appendix C, receipt of permit form).
(Code 1987, § 31-103)
Sec. 58-46. - Permit from DOT.
Parades which are scheduled to pass over or cross any of the state highways which pass through the city must also be given permits by the department of transportation of the state. It shall be the applicant's responsibility to obtain the correct permit from the state department of transportation. No city permit will be issued without a state department of transportation permit having been first obtained.
(Code 1987, § 31-106)
Sec. 58-47. - Revocation.
If any participants, during the course of the permitted activity, breach any law of the United States, the state or any ordinance of the city, and an arrest is made or citation is issued, the permit may be immediately revoked by the chief of police or senior police officer on duty, and the activity shall cease immediately.
(Code 1987, § 31-109(2))
Secs. 58-48—58-100. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - STREET NAMING AND STRUCTURE NUMBERING SYSTEM[2]

Footnotes:

Sec. 58-101. - Short title.
This article shall be further referred to as the "Street Naming and Structure Numbering System."
(Ord. No. 94-02-01, Art. I, 3-15-94)
Sec. 58-102. - Designation of street and road names and numbers.
Streets and roads now being maintained by the City of Helen and other public agencies within incorporated Helen shall in the future be named and numbered as now designated on the Official Map and Official Index of the City of Helen as amended by each implementing ordinance. Every other street and road within incorporated Helen shown on the Official Index shall hereafter be referred to as designated on the Official Index for structure numbering purposes.
(Ord. No. 94-02-01, Art. II, 3-15-94)
Sec. 58-103. - Designation of structure numbers.
(a)
Structure numbers for dwelling units, places of business, industrial locations and all other structures and uses requiring same shall be assigned by:
(1)
The Planning Department of White County and the Helen Public Works Department in conjunction with the United States Postal Service during the implementation stage of the system; and
(2)
The Planning Department of White County and the Helen Public Works Department following the implementation phase of the system in each area of the incorporated area of the city.
(b)
The planning department shall keep a record of all numbers assigned under this article. Such records may be maintained in data processing storage systems if so desired.
(Ord. No. 94-02-01, Art. III, 3-15-94)
Sec. 58-104. - Posting of designated structure numbers.
modified
The owner or occupant or person in charge of any dwelling unit, structure or use to which a number has been assigned, shall be notified in writing by the White County Planning Department in conjunction with the City of Helen Public Works Department of the number assigned to the same at any time after adoption of this article.
(Ord. No. 94-02-01, Art. IV, 3-15-94; Ord. No. 19-12-01 , 1-14-20)
Sec. 58-105. - Types of numbers.
Two types of numbers are used under this article:
(1)
A primary number to be assigned to each street frontage of each parcel of land, whether or not the parcel is occupied. The primary number is required to be posted only if the parcel is occupied by a dwelling unit, structure or active use and the owner, occupant or person in charge is notified under section 58-104(a) of this article. Other primary numbers are reserved for future development of the numbered parcels and will be assigned at the time of development.
(2)
Secondary numbers may be used when a number of units, structures and uses co-exist on the same parcel of land. Examples of parcels requiring secondary numbers include apartment projects, condominium projects, mobile home parks, office parks, planned unit developments, recreational vehicle parks, recreational areas, shopping centers, and other uses where the use of secondary numbers would clarify the location of a unit or be used for public safety purposes. To provide secondary numbers, the White County Planning Department and the city public works director shall work with the owner, manager, or person in charge of the project to determine a logical numbering system under the following guidelines:
a.
Existing numbered units and uses shall retain the present set of addresses with only the primary number being changed if the primary number is not in sequence with the overall system.
b.
Buildings on a single parcel with more than one and less than five units may be given either letter (preferred) or number designations as requested by the owner.
c.
A single building on a single parcel of land and with five or more units may be given numerical designations such as Suite 5, Apartment 5, etc., if so desired.
d.
Multiple buildings on the same parcel of land may be given secondary numbers consisting of number designations if the buildings are accessed from a main entrance to the project. Generally the number designations should increase in a clockwise direction from the main entrance.
e.
Recreational vehicle parks, and similar uses shall be given letter designations for lots or sections and number designations for individual lots or sites.
(Ord. No. 94-02-01, Art. V, 3-15-94)
Sec. 58-106. - Exempt and excluded structures.
(a)
The intent of this article is to exclude from this article those structures that do not present a significant danger to human life if destroyed by a fire or other events.
(b)
The following structures shall be excluded from the provisions:
(1)
Agricultural buildings not requiring a separate mailing address such as a barn, poultry house, outbuildings, or equipment storage buildings. Buildings used as dwelling units, offices, or the normal work station of an employee shall not be exempt.
(2)
Storage and accessory buildings for the use of the occupant of another building on the property. Buildings used as dwelling units, offices, or the normal work station of an employee, or requiring a separate mailing address, shall not be exempt.
(Ord. No. 94-02-01, Art. VI, 3-15-94)
Sec. 58-107. - New structure and lots.
(a)
Structure numbers will be assigned to each new lot, tract or building site on the original drawings of a final subdivision plat, recreational vehicle park plan, planned unit development, or other plan requiring the approval of the city public works director.
(b)
No building, electrical, plumbing or mechanical permit for any new or remodeled or repaired structure will be issued by the public works director of the city, following the implementation of this article in the affected area of the city until the owner, developer, or builder has procured from the city public works director the official structure number or numbers. Final approval for a certificate of occupancy of any principal building erected or repaired, or use requiring said number, shall be withheld until permanent and proper numbers have been displayed in accordance with the standards of this article.
(Ord. No. 94-02-01, Art. VII, 3-15-94)
Sec. 58-108. - Utility company compliance.
(a)
Following the implementation of this article in any area of the city, all utility services regulated by the state public service commission or any utility cooperative service organization, shall withhold service from any building until the owner or other requesting party has furnished the utility with a valid structure number.
(b)
It shall be the responsibility of the city public works director to notify each affected utility service of each implementation area by issuing maps and lists of official numbers assigned each structure or use.
(Ord. No. 94-02-01, Art. VIII, 3-15-94)
Sec. 58-109. - Implementation by city departments.
Every department and official under the jurisdiction of the city manager shall not render any non-emergency service until the occupant, owner, or requesting individual has provided the department with a valid structure number. Non-emergency service includes any service provided by the city manager except fire, rescue, law enforcement, or emergency medical service.
(Ord. No. 94-02-01, Art. IX, 3-15-94)
Sec. 58-110. - Penalties.
Any firm, person, or corporation who shall do anything prohibited by this article as the same exists or as it may hereafter be amended or who shall fail to do anything required by this article as the same exists or as it may hereafter be amended shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, amenable to the process of the municipal court of the city, and, upon conviction, shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 or by confinement in the county jail or correctional institution not to exceed 60 days, either fine or confinement, or both, in the discretion of the court. Each and every day that such violation exists shall be deemed a separate offense.
(Ord. No. 94-02-01, Art. X, 3-15-94; Ord. No. 11-3-03, 4-5-2011)
Sec. 58-111. - Effective date and repealer.
(a)
This article shall be implemented upon the date of adoption, and shall be in full force and effect.
(b)
All ordinances and parts of ordinances in conflict with this article are repealed upon enactment of this article.
(Ord. No. 94-02-01, Art. XI, 3-15-94)
Sec. 58-112—58-400. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - CONDITIONS FOR USE OF MARKET PLATZ, CITY PARKS, AND BAND SHELL[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
Editor's note— Ord. No. 10-03-01, adopted Apr. 6, 2010, repealed former Art. IV, §§ 58-401—58-409, and enacted a new article as set out herein. The former article pertained to similar subject matter and derived from Ord. No. 05-08-01, 8-16-05; Ord. No. 06-07-01, 8-1-06.

Sec. 58-401. - Short title and purpose.
This article shall be further referred to as the "Conditions for Use of Market Platz, City Parks, and Band Shell." The City of Helen recognizes that the City Band Shell and Market Platz area benefits the city by providing a place for musical entertainment and special events; contributing to the enjoyment of visitors to the city and to the economy of the area. At the same time, the city is aware of the need to insure the peace and privacy for residents and businesses in the area surrounding the band shell.
(Ord. No. 10-03-01, 4-6-10)
Sec. 58-402. - Market Platz defined.
The Market Platz is that triangular shaped pedestrian area of the downtown city lying on the southerly side of State Highway 75, bounded by State Highway 75, River Street, and a row of privately owned shops, and containing a structures commonly referred to as the "band shell," and fountain.
(Ord. No. 10-03-01, 4-6-10)
Sec. 58-403. - Market Platz performance area defined.
The Market Platz performance area is that area of the Market Platz bounded: easterly by the rear of the band shell; southerly by the northerly rock face, and rock face extended, of a planter; westerly by the easterly rock face, and rock face extended, of a planter located directly behind the benches placed for band shell seating; and northerly by the southerly rock face, and rock face extended through a walkway, of a wall near Main Street. Less and except from those boundaries is the walkway lying immediately northerly of the band shell and the planter lying immediately southerly of the band shell.
(Ord. No. 10-03-01, 4-6-10)
Sec. 58-404. - Reservation of band shell; permits required for performance in band shell; use of band shell; reservation of performance periods; conditions; exceptions; limitations.
(a)
Reservations for events or performances shall be on a first-come, first-serve basis; however, events or performers reserved by the City of Helen, Alpine Helen/White County Convention and Visitors Bureau or the Helen Chamber of Commerce receive priority in reservation of the Market Platz performance area and band shell, until reservations are opened to the public.
(b)
Reservation of the band shell includes uses of the band shell, but does not include the remainder of the Market Platz area. This area remains open to the public.
(c)
Permits are required in order to provide performers with the exclusive right to perform, during designated periods, within the Market Platz performance area and to use the band shell itself;
(d)
Performance times shall be scheduled on a daily basis beginning with the first performance. The period for performances shall be between 10:00 a.m., and 9:30 p.m.; with the last performance of the day concluding no later than 9:30 p.m.
(e)
Each performance period shall begin on the hour and end upon the half hour or shall begin on the half hour and end on the hour; other than events or performers reserved by the City of Helen, Alpine Helen/White County Convention and Visitors Bureau, or the Helen Chamber of Commerce which may be of longer duration.
(f)
Performers must be present at least 15 minutes prior to, and begin set up no earlier than 15 minutes prior to, the applicable performance period, and must complete removal within 15 minutes following the applicable performance time, unless the same performer is permitted for consecutive performance times, in which event no removal shall be required between consecutively scheduled performance times.
(g)
During set up and removal, no audible testing of equipment, voice, amplification, or tuning shall be allowed.
(h)
For the calendar period beginning Thanksgiving Day and continuing through Christmas Day, performers may reserve, at any time during the 12 months prior to the requested date, a maximum of two performance periods each day.
(i)
For times other than the calendar period beginning Thanksgiving Day and continuing through Christmas Day, performers may reserve, at any time during the six months prior to the requested date, a maximum of four performance periods each day with a maximum of 12 performance periods in any one month.
(j)
The permit may not be transferred or assigned.
(k)
Each performer shall be conclusively presumed to have inspected the entirety of the Market Platz performance area, and found same to be acceptable for their purpose, "as is," without any warranty whatsoever, and without any representation, warranty, or covenant as to fitness for any purpose.
(l)
No performer shall use any public utility prior to the first daily performance period and following the last daily performance period.
(m)
Each performer, and any and all persons associated in any manner with the performer, shall be conclusively presumed to have received, acknowledged, executed, and agreed to hold harmless and indemnify the city, their commissioners, agents, employees, officers, directors, and any person or persons acting in concert with them, from and against any and all liability, cost, expense, and damages, direct or indirect, foreseen or unforeseen, related to, or in any manner arising from, the performance by the performer.
(n)
If a performer or performers desire an amplified performance, the city amplification system must be used. No outside speakers or amplification is permitted.
(Ord. No. 10-03-01, 4-6-10)
Sec. 58-405. - By whom permits are to be issued.
The entity charged with the responsibility of issuing permits for the use of the Market Platz performance area and band shell shall be that entity with whom the city has contracted for the promotion of tourism within the city.
(Ord. No. 10-03-01, 4-6-10)
Editor's note— At the time of the passage of Ordinance No. 10-03-01, that entity is the Alpine Helen/White County Convention and Visitors Bureau.
Sec. 58-406. - Prohibited activities within the Market Platz and city parks.
The following activities are prohibited within the Market Platz and city parks:
(1)
Any activity in violation of federal, state, county or city laws, ordinances, rules, and regulations, or of these conditions. Any performers, by use of the Market Platz, Market Platz performance area, or city parks shall be conclusively presumed to have actual knowledge of all such laws, ordinances, rules and regulations, and of these conditions, including, in particular, and without limitation, noise ordinances; and
(2)
Any performance other than a solely gratuitous performance. However, so long as it is not violative of these conditions, performers may accept voluntary contributions or donations, provided also that no amount of contribution or donation may be specified, urged, suggested or commented on.
(Ord. No. 10-03-01, 4-6-10)
Sec. 58-407. - Prohibited activities within the Market Platz.
The following activities within the Market Platz are prohibited:
(1)
Open flames;
(2)
Dispersing of any liquids;
(3)
Unreasonable obstruction of pedestrian traffic to businesses;
(4)
Any electronically amplified sound except when originating from the band shell or speakers provided by the city;
(5)
Use of profanity; and
(6)
Consumption of alcohol or display of alcoholic beverages.
(7)
Use of any animal or bird to perform in the Market Platz area which may leave excrement causing unsanitary conditions, littering of the sidewalks or causing damage to the landscaping or other city property; or the use of any animal or bird in a performance which can create a biting or scratching hazard to the public.
(8)
Use of the city planters at the Market Platz area for any type of display or signage.
(Ord. No. 10-03-01, 4-6-10; Ord. No. 13-06-02, 7-16-13)
Sec. 58-408. - Rules and regulations.
The city manager may adopt and publish such additional rules and regulations for use of the Market Platz and city parks as may be consistent with this article, and such shall have the effect of law. However, no such rules or regulations shall be effective until reviewed for comment by the city commission, and published and made available to the public at city hall. All such rules and regulations shall be published in a single volume, in a reasonable organizational manner, and revised not later than 60 days following any change thereto. The city commission may, by vote, abolish any or all of such rules and regulations.
(Ord. No. 10-03-01, 4-6-10)
Editor's note— At the time of the passage of Ordinance No. 10-03-01, a City of Helen Band Shell Usage Policy and Application for use pursuant to section 58-405 was reviewed for comment by the city commission, published and made available to the public at City Hall.
Sec. 58-409. - Penalties.
Any firm, person, or corporation who shall do anything prohibited by this article as the same exists or as it may hereafter be amended or who shall fail to do anything required by this article as the same exists or as it may hereafter be amended shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, amenable to the process of the municipal court of the city, and, upon conviction, shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 or by confinement in the county jail or correctional institution not to exceed 60 days, either fine or confinement, or both, in the discretion of the court. Each and every day that such violation exists shall be deemed a separate offense.
(Ord. No. 10-03-01, 4-6-10)
Sec. 58-410. - Security deposit for use of band shell
A refundable security deposit of $100.00 shall be paid with the reservation for any event, performance or performances in the band shell. Upon request by applicant, the deposit may be retained by the Alpine Helen/White County Convention and Visitors Bureau as the required deposit for future reservations, or it will be refunded within two weeks of a written request following the event, performance or performances except under the following circumstances.
(1)
There is damage to the facility or grounds of the band shell. Applicant shall be held liable for all damages that occur during the reservation period. Applicant will be billed for all damages in excess of the damage deposit.
(2)
The band shell and surrounding area is not left in a clean condition at the conclusion of the performance.
(Ord. No. 10-03-01, 4-6-10)
Sec. 58-411. - Revocation of permit.
The city shall have the authority to revoke any permit upon violation of any conditions in this section or where staging of the event would have an immediate and adverse effect on the welfare and safety of persons or property. The chief of police shall have the authority to close any event immediately upon violation of conditions set forth herein or to preserve the safety of persons or property.
(Ord. No. 10-03-01, 4-6-10)
Secs. 58-412—58-500. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - PROTECTION OF SIDEWALKS, STREETS, PUBLIC PLACES, PUBLIC PROPERTY, AND PUBLIC RIGHTS-OF-WAY

Sec. 58-501. - Throwing, depositing, or disposing of garbage, trash, yard trimmings, leaves, or other solid waste upon the sidewalks, streets, public places, public property and public rights-of-way.
It shall be unlawful for any person to throw, deposit, sweep, blow or discard debris, garbage, litter, trash, yard trimmings, leaves or other solid waste upon the streets, sidewalks, public places, public property and public rights-of-way within the city.
(Ord. No. 05-12-01, 12-20-05)
Sec. 58-502. - Transporting garbage, trash, solid waste, or other material in open vehicles; spills from motor vehicles.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate or cause to be operated upon any public street in the city any open truck, trailer, wagon or other motor vehicle in and upon which garbage, trash, manure, solid waste material, other debris, or materials are transported, unless the vehicle shall be equipped with a cover or other securing device that will prevent the garbage, trash, waste material, other debris or other material from falling from the truck onto the streets of the city.
(b)
In the event that spillage does occur in spite of the appropriate use of said devices, the person responsible for the operation of the vehicle shall take immediate action to remove the material from the street or sidewalk. Shoveling, sweeping, or vacuuming the material and removing it from the roadway is an appropriate response.
(Ord. No. 05-12-01, 12-20-05)
Sec. 58-503. - Spills from vehicles; concrete, cement, gravel, sand, dirt, and asphalt hauling.
(a)
Any person engaged in hauling concrete, cement, gravel, sand, dirt, asphalt or any other material on any public streets within the corporate limits of the city shall load or fill vehicles so as not to allow spillage of any material onto the city streets or sidewalks. All vehicles hauling wet concrete, cement or loose material, are required to use suitable covers, spill prevention and capture devices to prevent materials from blowing, spilling, or dripping from the vehicle.
(b)
In the event that spillage does occur in spite of the appropriate use of said devises, the person responsible for the operation of the vehicle shall remove the material from the street or sidewalk. Shoveling, sweeping, or vacuuming the material and removing it from the roadway is an appropriate response.
(Ord. No. 05-12-01, 12-20-05)
Sec. 58-504. - Defacing city sidewalks, streets, public places, public property and public rights-of-way.
It shall be unlawful for any person to perform the act of nailing, tacking, posting, gluing, painting, stapling, marking or writing upon the surface of any street, sidewalk, public place, public property and public rights-of-way.
(Ord. No. 05-12-01, 12-20-05)
Sec. 58-505. - Penalties.
Anyone found guilty of violating any provision of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, amenable to the process of the municipal court of the city, and, upon conviction, shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 or by confinement in the county jail or correctional institution not to exceed 60 days, either fine or confinement, or both, in the discretion of the court. Each and every day that such violation exists shall be deemed a separate offense.
(Ord. No. 05-12-01, 12-20-05; Ord. No. 11-3-01, 3-15-2011)
Sec. 58-506. - Types of alcohol possession and consumption prohibited above city sidewalks, public places, public property and public rights-of-way.
(a)
Purpose. The purpose and intent of this section is to protect the health, safety and welfare of Helen's citizens and visitors through the prohibition of the possession and consumption of alcoholic beverages in those second story open air locations in which the possession and consumption of such those alcoholic beverages, by virtue of being possessed and consumed on a second story deck, pavilion or other such open air facility, and that are located in direct proximity to city sidewalks, public places, public property, or public rights-of-way creates safety hazards to the citizens and visitors of Helen, and problems regarding trash, spillage, and other issues detrimental to the city's image and overall economic well being.
(b)
Definitions.
(1)
Second story decks, pavilions and open air facilities are defined as decks, pavilions or other open air gathering places, which are located on the second story (or higher) above ground level.
(2)
Adjacent to a city sidewalk is defined as a location in which the closest structural side of a second story deck, pavilion, or other open air structure is within ten linear yards of the city sidewalk at its closest point.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to possess or consume alcoholic beverages contained in either a bottle, can, or any other type of container other than a styrofoam or flexible plastic cup no larger than 18 ounces; upon any second story deck, pavilion, or other open air facility that is adjacent to a city sidewalk, or is directly located over any area in which either the public may be located or which is the public entryway into the first floor of a building.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any business within the City of Helen, Georgia to permit patrons to possess or consume alcoholic beverages other than in a flexible plastic or styrofoam cup no larger than 18 ounces; or to serve patrons alcoholic beverages other than in a plastic cup; for the consumption upon any second story deck, pavilion or other open air facility that is adjacent to a city sidewalk, or directly located over any area in which either the public may be located or which is the public entryway into the first floor of a building.
(e)

Sec. 58-507. - Shareable dockless mobility devices.
new
(a)
Purpose. The purpose of this section is to prohibit shareable dockless mobility devices from being placed in the public right-of-way or on public property, operated in the public right-of-way or on public property, or offered for use anywhere in the city.
(b)
Definitions. For purposes of this section, the following terms, phrases, words, and their derivatives shall have the meanings set forth in this section:
Dockless means that the device does not require the individual user to return and lock the device to an authorized fixed station once the user has completed their use of the device.
Shareable dockless mobility device means any dockless wheeled device, whether it be human-powered, electric, or otherwise motorized, that permits an individual to move or be moved freely, including but not limited to a bicycle, scooter, or skateboard; is accessed via an on-demand portal, whether a smartphone application, membership card, or similar method; is operated by a private entity that owns, manages, and maintains devices for shared use by members of the public; and is available to members of the public in unstaffed, self-service locations.
(c)
General requirements. It is unlawful to park, leave standing, leave lying, abandon, or otherwise place a shareable dockless mobility device in a public right-of-way or on public property anywhere within the city. It is unlawful to operate a shareable dockless mobility device in a public right-of-way or on public property anywhere within the city. It is unlawful to provide or offer for use a shareable dockless mobility device anywhere within the city.
(d)
Impoundment. Violations of this section shall be punishable as provided for all other violations of the City Code. The city police department is further authorized to specifically to impound any shareable dockless mobility device that has been offered for use, placed in a public right-of-way or on public property, or operated in a public right-of-way or on public property in violation of this section.
(e)
Any device so impounded under subsection (d) may be released from such impoundment only upon payment in full of all impound and storage fees accrued.
