Sec. 34-1. - Age restricted.
Any manufactured homes moved into the city and manufactured in a year more than ten years prior to the year in which the manufactured home is to be located in the city shall be presumed to be in a condition or state of disrepair making such home unsuitable for use in the city and shall not be located in the city.
(Ord. of 10-5-1993, § 1)
Sec. 34-2. - Skirting.
All manufactured homes must be fitted with skirting and/or underpinning that completely enclose the undercarriage. Such skirting and/or other underpinning shall be of brick, masonry, concrete or siding of like or similar character to the exterior of the manufactured home which shall meet minimum requirements of the state building code.
(Ord. of 10-5-1993, § 2)
Sec. 34-3. - Entry required.
All manufactured homes shall provide an adequate means of entry (steps) at every door opening.
(Ord. of 10-5-1993, § 2)
Sec. 34-4. - Additions.
Permanent additions may be built onto or become a part of any manufactured home unit and include porches, carports, decks, and additional living space.
(Ord. of 10-5-1993, § 2)
Sec. 34-5. - Minimum setbacks.
No manufactured home shall be located within 25 feet of any road right-of-way and 15 feet of any property line.
(Ord. of 10-5-1993, § 2)
Sec. 34-6. - Tiedown requirements.
All manufactured homes shall be secured in accordance with the tiedown requirements approved by the Mobile Home Manufacturers Association.
(Ord. of 10-5-1993, § 2)
