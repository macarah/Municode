ARTICLE II. - RULES AND REGULATIONS FOR LAKE GRACE

Sec. 26-19. - Hours of operation.
The hours of operation for Lake Grace shall be 24 hours a day, seven days a week, except for shelter reservations, which last until 11:00 p.m., and except as hereinafter provided for skiing, race boats, etc.
(Code 1992, § 24-26; Ord. of 1-5-1995(2), § 1)
Sec. 26-20. - Fishing.
Fishing in Lake Grace is allowed at all times.
(Code 1992, § 24-27; Ord. of 1-5-1995(2), § 1)
Sec. 26-21. - Boating regulations.
(a)
All fishing boats, both motorized and non-motorized, are permitted at any time on the lake.
(b)
Ski boats, jet skis (also known as personal water vehicles or PWVs), runabouts, pleasure boats, pontoon boats, kneeboard skiing, tubes pulled by boats, and water skiing will be permitted every day from 10:00 a.m. to one hour before sunset. No boats or watercraft longer than 18 feet will be permitted at any time on the lake, except pontoon boats and sailboats.
(c)
Ski boats, pleasure boats, and jet skis must travel in a counter-clockwise direction around the center open portion of the lake, from the island up to the lake to the no-wake zone 250 yards downstream from the fence posts that cross the lake. In other areas of the lake, these boats must travel at no-wake speed, five miles per hour or less.
(d)
Racing boats, either professional or amateur, will not be permitted at any time, except when prior arrangements are made for a specific date for a race, plus one day for test runs prior to the race. Arrangements must be made through the county commission at least two weeks in advance.
(e)
There shall be no restriction on motor size at Lake Grace.
(f)
All boats shall be launched only at paved ramps.
(g)
Boat trailers, vehicles and boats may not be left on the ramp for a longer time than necessary to launch or load a boat. No one will be permitted to block a ramp at any time.
(h)
Children under 18 years of age will not be permitted to operate boats unless under the direct supervision of a responsible adult in the boat, including a jet ski.
(i)
The area between the island and the nearest shoreline near the picnic area is a no-wake zone. All boats traveling in this designated area shall travel at no-wake speed, five miles per hour or less.
(j)
All boats are required to meet all state laws and regulations, including the following:
(1)
Each boat must carry one approved life preserver (personal flotation device or PFD) for each person on board, including persons being towed by the boat on skis, tubes, knee boards, etc.;
(2)
Proper running lights are required for night operation; and
(3)
All other safety equipment required by state regulations are required on each boat.
(Code 1992, § 24-28; Ord. of 1-5-1995(2), § 1; Ord. of 1-8-2001, § 1)
Sec. 26-22. - Vehicular regulation in park area.
(a)
Speed limits on the roads at Lake Grace are as posted, 25 miles per hour.
(b)
Four-wheel or three-wheel all-terrain vehicles are prohibited at Lake Grace, except for farming uses where Lake Grace is being used for irrigation purposes.
(Code 1992, § 24-30; Ord. of 1-5-1995(2), § 1)
Sec. 26-23. - Reservation fees.
Reservation fees at Lake Grace for the use of large shelters, small shelters and/or electricity shall be as set out in the schedule of fees.
(Code 1992, § 24-31; Ord. of 1-5-1995(2), § 1)
Sec. 26-24. - Swimming.
Swimming at Lake Grace is at a person's own risk; no life guard shall be on duty. No swimming is allowed within 50 yards of any boat ramp.
(Code 1992, § 24-32; Ord. of 1-5-1995(2), § 1)
Sec. 26-25. - Alcoholic beverages.
No alcoholic beverages are allowed in the park at Lake Grace.
(Code 1992, § 24-33; Ord. of 1-5-1995(2), § 1)
Sec. 26-26. - Pets.
Except as provided in O.C.G.A. § 30-4-2, no pets are allowed in the park at Lake Grace.
(Code 1992, § 24-34; Ord. of 1-5-1995(2), § 1)
Sec. 26-27. - Fishing creel limits.
At Lake Grace the daily fishing creel limit shall be 50 bluegills or other sunfish species (bream), 30 crappies (white perch), five bass (14-inch minimum), and catfish as per state regulations.
(Code 1992, § 24-35; Ord. of 1-5-1995(2), § 1)
Sec. 26-28. - Use of trotlines, bush hooks.
No trotlines, bush hooks, bank lines, baskets or nets shall be used at Lake Grace. Fishing shall be done with rod and reel or pole only.
(Code 1992, § 24-36; Ord. of 1-5-1995(2), § 1)
Sec. 26-29. - Full-time attendant.
The county administrator shall be given the authority to hire an attendant for Lake Grace on a full-time basis.
(Code 1992, § 24-37; Ord. of 1-5-1995(2), § 1)
Sec. 26-30. - Hunting.
It shall be unlawful to hunt on any county land at Lake Grace.
(Code 1992, § 24-38; Ord. of 1-5-1995(2), § 1)
Secs. 26-31—26-48. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - RULES AND REGULATIONS FOR THE JAYCEE RIVER PARK LANDING

Sec. 26-49. - Houseboats.
The parking of houseboats on county property at the Jaycee River Park Landing is prohibited.
(Code 1992, § 24-61; Mo. of 8-2-1977, § 8; Mo. of 4-7-1978(2))
Sec. 26-50. - Posting of regulations for lagoon area at Jaycee Landing.
Signs shall be installed at Jaycee Landing, and it shall be included in the county ordinance that there will be no swimming, no skiing and no wake in the lagoon area at Jaycee Landing.
(Code 1992, § 24-62; Mo. of 7-3-1979)
Secs. 26-51—26-73. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - RULES AND REGULATIONS FOR THE WILLIAMSBURG LANDING

Sec. 26-74. - Camping prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to camp or otherwise remain or stay overnight in the Williamsburg Landing and picnic area.
(Code 1992, § 24-80; Ord. of 12-20-1994, § 1)
