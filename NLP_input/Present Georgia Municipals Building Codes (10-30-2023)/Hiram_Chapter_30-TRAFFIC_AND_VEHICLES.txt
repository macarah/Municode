ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 30-1. - Uniform rules of the road adopted; penalty for violation of section.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-372 through 40-6-376, O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-2 through 40-6-395, known as the Uniform Rules of the Road, and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of the city with like effect as if recited in this section.
(b)
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-12.
(Code 1996, § 62-1; Ord. No. 84-60, § 1, 11-5-1984; Ord. No. 88-37, 8-1-1988)
State Law reference— Uniform Rules of the Road, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-1 et seq.; power of local authorities generally, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-371; adoption of uniform rules of the road by local authorities, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-372.
Sec. 30-2. - Prohibition of certain truck traffic; penalty for violation of section.
(a)
No truck having more than a total of six wheels or more than two axles shall be operated on any portion of any city street, except that such trucks may be so operated to reach a bona fide destination on or adjacent to one of such streets.
(b)
No person shall stop, stand or park such a truck except while actually engaged in loading or unloading of merchandise.
(c)
At night. The parking of trucks having a capacity of more than one-half ton on any street shall be prohibited for more than one hour between the hours of 6:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m. at night, unless the truck is in charge of an attendant who is awake during all hours; and such parked truck shall be properly lighted.
(d)
In the daytime. It shall be unlawful to park trucks in excess of one-half ton for more than one hour from 6:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m. on city streets.
(e)
Residential areas at night. It shall be unlawful to park or stand any truck of a weight in excess of 8,000 pounds gross weight or any other freight conveyance of a weight in excess of 8,000 pounds gross weight except authorized emergency vehicles on any city street within the residential area of the city between the hours of 7:00 p.m. Eastern Standard Time and 7:00 a.m. Eastern Standard Time for a period longer than one-half hour.
(f)
No commercial vehicle, tractor, tractor-trailer, 18-wheeler or bus exceeding 8,000 pounds gross weight may be parked for overnight or weekend storage visible to the general public on properties zoned residential. The term "storage" shall not include short-term loading or unloading of such vehicles during daylight hours or otherwise in accordance with normally accepted commercial practices. Daylight hours shall mean the period between 30 minutes after dawn and 30 minutes after sunset. This section shall not apply to privately owned recreational vehicles which may be parked at a person's own residence. However, such vehicles must also comply with the other provisions of this Code.
(g)
Areas designated for parking of trucks associated with or serving commercial establishments shall be paved and installed with proper drainage.
(Code 1996, § 62-2; Ord. No. 91-4, § 2, 1-7-1991)

Sec. 30-23. - Minimum, maximum speed limit; penalty for violation of section.
(a)
The minimum and maximum speed limits on city streets shall be established by the governing authority, from time to time, and as designated by a traffic control device or a traffic sign posted on or at the entrance of that roadway giving notice of the speed limit.
(b)
Speed limits and locations approved by the Georgia Department of Transportation within the City of Hiram are adopted as the List of Roads for the City of Hiram Number 04-15-15. These approved and adopted speed limits are enforceable within the City of Hiram pursuant to the speed detection device permit issued by the department of public safety using the list of roads as the controlling document.
(c)
Erecting and maintaining the speed limit signs in all state routes and U.S. highways, referred to as on-system, is the responsibility of the Georgia Department of Transportation. The City of Hiram will be responsible for installing and maintaining the appropriate speed limit signs for city roads and streets, referred to as off-system. All signs should be in accordance with the Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices.
(d)
Any person convicted of a violation of this section shall be punished, upon conviction, as provided for by section 1-12.
(Code 1996, § 62-31; Ord. No. 91-12, 3-4-1991; Ord. No. 5-10-00, 7-3-2000; Ord. No. 2015-06, §§ 1—3, 8-4-2015)
