ARTICLE XI. - DEALERS IN PRECIOUS METALS OR GEMS

Sec. 12-260. - Definitions.
For the purposes of this article, the following words and phrases when used herein shall be construed as follows:
Dealer in precious metals or gems means:
(1)
Any person engaged in the business of purchasing precious metals or gems or goods made from precious metals or gems from persons other than manufacturers, manufacturers' representatives, or other dealers in precious metals or gems; or
(2)
A person engaged in any other business if, in conjunction with such business, precious metals or gems or goods made from precious metals or gems are purchased from persons or sources other than manufacturers, manufacturers' representatives, or other dealers in precious metals or gems where such purchase is for resale in its original form or as changed by remounting, melting, re-forming, remolding, or recasting or for resale as scrap or in bulk.
Gems means any precious or semiprecious stone which is cut and polished.
Nonpermanent location means any location designed to be used to conduct the aforementioned business, in any movable vehicle, and temporary and movable structure, including, but not limited to, vans, mobile homes, trailers, hotels and motels, lodges, or any similar nonpermanent structure designed by applicant to be used to conduct said business for a limited or specific time. Applications for businesses under this article at such locations shall not be accepted.
Numismatic coins means coins whose value as collectors' items exceeds the value of the content of the precious metals in the coins.
Permanent location means a business domiciled within a properly constructed building which must be located within a properly zoned area, according to the city's zoning ordinance.
Person means an individual, partnership, corporation, joint venture, trust, association, or any other legal entity however organized.
Precious metals means gold, silver, or platinum or any alloy containing gold, silver, or platinum.
( Ord. No. 2015-02, § 1, 2-2-2015 )
Sec. 12-261. - Registration required; application contents; application approval fees.
(a)
No person shall engage in business as a dealer in precious metals or gems within the municipal limits of the City of Palmetto without first obtaining from the city an occupation tax certificate and registering with the city to operate as a dealer in precious metals or gems.
(b)
The application for registration to operate a business as a dealer in precious metals or gems shall be promulgated by the city administrator.
(c)
The application for said registration shall be in writing and shall be sworn to or affirmed by the applicant. The application shall contain, but not be limited to:
(1)
The precise location from which the proposes business is to operate;
(2)
The full name, address, phone number, date of birth and Social Security number of the applicant and any other persons having an ownership interest in the proposed business;
(3)
The names, addresses, and ages of all officers of any publicly held corporation holding an interest in the proposed business and their titles;
(4)
The full name, address, phone number, date of birth and Social Security number of all employees to be employed in the proposed business;
(5)
Photographs, fingerprints and any other information deemed necessary for a background investigation by the Police Department of the City of Palmetto; and
(6)
Any such other information as deemed necessary by the mayor and council for the City of Palmetto or the city administrator.
(d)
All applications for registration under this article shall be accompanied by the tendering of a regulatory fee as set out in a schedule of fees adopted by the mayor and council. Said regulatory fee shall approximate the reasonable cost of the actual regulatory activity performed by the city.
( Ord. No. 2015-02, § 1, 2-2-2015 )
Sec. 12-262. - Processing of application.
Review of an application to register as a dealer in precious metals or gems shall be conducted by the city administrator. The Police Chief for the City of Palmetto shall determine whether the applicant or any of their employees has ever been convicted of any felony, any crime involving larceny, burglary or theft of any description, as defined in the Official Code of Georgia, or any misdemeanor involving moral turpitude.
( Ord. No. 2015-02, § 1, 2-2-2015 )
Sec. 12-263. - Method of maintaining records.
(a)
Every dealer in precious metals or gems shall maintain a book, in permanent form, in which shall be entered in legible English at the time of each purchase of precious metals or gems or goods containing precious metals or gems, the following information:
(1)
The date and time of the purchase transaction;
(2)
The name of the person making the purchase from the seller;
(3)
The name, age and address of the seller of the item(s) purchases and the distinctive number from such seller's driver's license or other similar identification card containing a photo of the seller;
(4)
A clear and accurate identification and description of the purchased goods, including the serial numbers, model numbers or other numbers, and any identifying marks inscribed thereon;
(5)
The price paid for the goods purchased;
(6)
The number of the check issued for the purchase price, if the payment is made by check; and
(5)
The signature of the seller.
(b)
The permanent record book required herein shall be maintained for each purchase of precious metals or gems or goods made from precious metals or gems for a period of four (4) years. Entries shall appear in chronological order in ink. No blank lines may be left between entries. No obliteration, alterations, or erasures may be made. Corrections shall be made by drawing a line of ink through the entry without destroying its legibility. The book and all precious metals or gems and goods containing precious metals or gems shall be open to the inspection of any duly authorized law enforcement officer at all times.
( Ord. No. 2015-02, § 1, 2-2-2015 )
Sec. 12-264. - Required daily written report.
Every dealer in precious metals or gems shall make a daily report in writing to the Police Department of the City of Palmetto of all precious metals or gems or goods containing precious metals or gems purchased during the twenty-four (24) hours ending at 9:00 p.m. on the date of the report. The reports shall be on forms or on duplicate invoices as may be prescribed or approved the police department. The reports shall be legible and mailed or delivered to the police department prior to 10:00 a.m. on the day following the date of the report. In addition to any other information required by the police department, the reports shall include the name and address of the registered dealer, the date of transaction, a description and amount of the precious metals or gems or goods containing precious metals or gems purchased, and the name and address of the person from whom purchased. The police department may promulgate rules and regulations consistent with this section to require the proper enforcement thereof.
( Ord. No. 2015-02, § 1, 2-2-2015 )
Sec. 12-265. - Holding period.
Any dealer who acquires used valuables in the course of business shall hold these items for at least ten (10) days before disposing of them by sale, transfer, shipment, grinding down, melting, or otherwise changing the original appearance of the item. The provisions of this section shall not be deemed as to prevent any dealer from storing such items off the business premises, or placing such items in the hands of any bank or security company for the purpose of safekeeping. However, any dealer storing such items off the premises shall produce such items for inspection within twelve (12) hours following the demand of any officer of the police department.
( Ord. No. 2015-02, § 1, 2-2-2015 )
Sec. 12-266. - Hours of operation.
The hours within which dealers in precious metals or gems may keep open their place of business shall be from 7:00 a.m. until 9:00 p.m.
( Ord. No. 2015-02, § 1, 2-2-2015 )
Sec. 2-267. - Unlawful activities.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any dealer in precious metals or gems or any agent or employee of a dealer in precious metals or gems who makes purchases of precious metals or gems or goods containing precious metals or gems to:
(1)
Make any false statement in the registration provided for by this article;
(2)
Fail to maintain and make entries in the permanent record book as required by section 12-263 of this article;
(3)
Make any false entries therein;
(4)
Falsify, obliterate, destroy or remove from the place of business such permanent record book;
(5)
Fail to submit daily reports as required by section 12-264 of this article;
(6)
Refuse to allow a duly authorized law enforcement officer to inspect such permanent book or any precious metals or goods containing precious metals in his or her possession;
(7)
Sell, exchange, or remove from the legal possession of the buyer, or to alter the form of, any precious metals or gems or goods containing precious metals or gems purchased by remounting, melting, cutting up, or otherwise altering the original form until at least seven (7) calendar days have elapsed from the time of purchase or acquisition; or
(8)
Purchase any precious metals or gems from any person under seventeen (17) years of age.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to advertise or transact business as a dealer in precious metals or gems without first registering as required by this article.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any dealer in precious metals or gems to purchase precious metals in a melted or smelted state unless the purchase is from a registered dealer in precious metals or gems.
(d)
If a law enforcement officer has probable cause to believe that precious metals or gems have been stolen, he or she may give notice in writing to the dealer to retain the precious metals or gems for an additional fifteen (15) days; and it shall be unlawful for the dealer to dispose of the property unless the notice is revoked in writing within the fifteen-day period.
( Ord. No. 2015-02, § 1, 2-2-2015 )
Sec. 12-268. - Suspension or revocation of registration.
Any registration issued pursuant to this article may be suspended or revoked upon the following grounds:
(1)
Misrepresentation of any information in the original or renewal application for any registration authorized by this article;
(2)
The conviction of the registered dealer for a felony, the violation of any criminal law involving larceny, burglary or theft of any description as defined in the Official Code of Georgia, or any misdemeanor involving moral turpitude; and
(3)
The conviction of the registered dealer for a violation of any part of section 12-267(a) of this article.
( Ord. No. 2015-02, § 1, 2-2-2015 )
Sec. 12-269. - Notice and hearing.
(a)
No registration which has been issued to any person pursuant to this article shall be suspended or revoked except upon one (1) or more of the grounds stated in section 12-268 and until after the registered dealer has been notified by the city administrator.
(b)
Where it is reported to the city administrator that a registered dealer is engaged in unlawful activities, a preliminary investigation shall be conducted by the city administrator in order to attempt to determine whether there is a basis for the reports. If the city administrator's preliminary investigation reveals that there may be a basis for revocation and suspension of the registration, the registered dealer shall be notified to appear before the city administrator and show cause on a date certain why his or her registration should not be revoked or suspended. The registered dealer may appear in person at the hearing or be represented by counsel. At the conclusion of the hearing, the city administrator, based upon evidence submitted at the hearing, shall enter an order making a finding of fact and then:
(1)
Find that the evidence does not authorize revocation or suspension;
(2)
Issue a warning to the registered dealer;
(3)
Suspend the registration and probate suspension;
(4)
Revoke the registration and probate revocation;
(5)
Suspend the registration; or
(6)
Revoke the registration.
(c)
Within five (5) days from the date of the order from the city administrator, the registered dealer may appeal the decision by filing a notice of appeal with the mayor and council. The city administrator shall schedule a hearing before the mayor and council and notify the registered dealer of the time and date of the hearing. At the conclusion of the hearing, the mayor and council shall enter an order which contains therein a finding of fact and a decision to:
(1)
Find in favor of the registered dealer;
(2)
Issue a warning to registered dealer;
(3)
Suspend the registration and probate suspension;
(4)
Revoke the registration and probate revocation;
(5)
Suspend the registration;
(6)
Revoke the registration; and/or
(7)
Take any other appropriate action regarding the registered dealer.
( Ord. No. 2015-02, § 1, 2-2-2015 )
