ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 28-1. - Levy and scope.
There is hereby levied a license fee on each person, partnership, or corporation listed in this chapter, in accordance with the provisions appearing in the same.
(Code 1992, § 32-101)
Sec. 28-2. - Form of applications.
Each application shall be a written statement upon forms provided by the city clerk.
(1)
An application fee will be assessed based on the cost to process the application. Said fee shall be established by the city council from time to time.
(2)
Each applicant shall sign a consent form authorizing the city police department to process a criminal background check.
(Code 1992, § 32-102; Ord. of 3-9-1998, § 32-102)
Sec. 28-3. - Penalties.
(a)
Any person, firm, company. or corporation required by this chapter to obtain a license to engage in any trade, business, or profession within the city limits, who shall fail to make application therefor within the time limits prescribed, shall be subject and required to pay a penalty of 50 percent of the regular license fee, provided that the city clerk-treasurer, when in his judgment circumstances may seem to warrant, may grant an extension of time (in no case exceeding 60 days) within which time to file application and pay the license fee. No penalty will be charged on any license on which an extension of time has been granted.
(b)
Any person violating any other provision of this chapter shall, upon conviction before the city council, be fined an amount to be established by the city council from time to time, or shall be imprisoned up to 60 days, as determined by the judge.
(Code 1992, § 32-114)
Secs. 28-4—28-24. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - LICENSING

Sec. 28-25. - Procedure for issuance.
The granting of a business license under the provisions of this chapter shall be deemed a privilege only, and nothing herein contained shall be construed as granting any person whose business is subject to municipal regulation any legal right to engage in such business. The city council has the right to not grant an application for any reason that it deems fit and proper for the health, safety and well being of city and its citizens.
(Code 1992, § 32-103; Ord. of 3-9-1998, § 32-103)
Sec. 28-26. - Display of license.
It shall be the duty of any person conducting a licensed business in the city to keep his license posted in a conspicuous place on the premises used for such business at all times.
(Code 1992, § 32-104)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-3.
Sec. 28-27. - Inspections.
(a)
Search of premises. Whenever inspections are a reasonable precedent to the licensing of a business or to the detection of violations of public peace, order, or morality that would normally be cause to revoke a license for any period of time, it shall be the duty of the licensee, or the person in charge of the premises to be inspected, to admit thereto, for the purpose of making the inspection, any city officer or employee who is authorized or directed to make such inspection at any reasonable time that admission is requested.
(b)
Testing of material. Whenever an analysis of any commodity or material is a reasonable precedent to the licensing of a business or to the detection of violations of the public welfare that would normally be cause to revoke a license for any period of time, it shall be the duty of the licensee whose business is governed by such provision to give to any authorized city officer or employee requesting the same, sufficient samples of such material or commodity for such analysis.
(c)
Refusal to allow inspection. In addition to any other penalty which may be provided, the city council may revoke the license of any licensed proprietor of a licensed business in the city who refuses to permit any officer or employee who is authorized to make such inspection or take such sample to make the inspection, or take an adequate sample of the said commodity, or who interferes with such officer or employee while in the performance of his duty in making such inspection; provided that no license shall be revoked for such cause unless written demand is made upon the licensee or person in charge of the premises, in the name of the city, stating that such inspection or sample is desired at the time it is sought to make the inspection or obtain the sample.
(Code 1992, § 32-105)
Sec. 28-28. - Termination and renewal of business licenses.
(a)
All annual licenses shall terminate on December 31 of the calendar year in which they are issued.
(b)
Each licensee shall make a written application for renewal on forms supplied by the city clerk, on or before November 15 of each calendar year, which application shall contain substantially the same information as the initial application and be accompanied by all required fees.
(c)
The licensee of each restaurant shall furnish at the time of any renewal application an affidavit that reports the percentage of the licensee's total annual gross sales derived from the sale of food products or prepared meals in the licensee's most recently ended fiscal year.
(d)
An applicant for renewal of a license shall be entitled to a refund of fees tendered if he withdraws his application for renewal prior to final action on the same by the city.
(e)
All license fees shall be prorated over the calendar year in which the license is to have effect.
(Code 1992, § 32-106; Ord. of 6-11-2001, exh. A)
Sec. 28-29. - Revocation, suspension, etc.
The city council, after affording the licensee notice of the charges and opportunity to be heard with respect to any revocation proceedings may, on the bases of its findings, revoke such license in its entirety, suspend the same for a specified period of time, place the licensee on probation, or place other conditions thereon as the city council may deem necessary.
(Code 1992, § 32-107)
Sec. 28-30. - Change of location.
In the absence of any provision to the contrary, the location of any licensed business or occupation may be changed, provided ten days' notice thereof is given to the city clerk, and provided that all building and zoning requirements are complied with.
(Code 1992, § 32-108)
Sec. 28-31. - Transfer of license.
All licenses shall be personal to the licensee to whom it is issued and no transfer of said license shall occur.
(Code 1992, § 32-109; Ord. of 3-9-1998, § 32-109)
Sec. 28-32. - Duplicate licenses.
A duplicate license shall be issued by the city clerk to replace a previously issued license which has been lost, stolen, defaced, or destroyed without any willful conduct on the part of the licensee, upon the filing of a sworn affidavit attesting to such fact and the payment of a fee as established by the city council from time to time.
(Code 1992, § 32-110)
Sec. 28-33. - Branch offices.
For the purpose of this chapter, each branch establishment or location wherein a representative of the owner is employed, and is authorized to transact business for such owner, shall be deemed a separate place of business, for which a separate license shall be required; provided that warehouses and distributing plants used in connection with and incidental to a business licensed under the provisions of this chapter shall not be deemed to be separate places of business or branch offices.
(Code 1992, § 32-111)
Sec. 28-34. - Joint license.
A person engaged in two or more businesses at the same location shall be required to obtain separate licenses for conducting each of such businesses for which a license is required.
(Code 1992, § 32-112)
Sec. 28-35. - False statements.
Any misstatement or concealment of fact in the application herein required, or in response to any inquiries made by the city clerk or an authorized city official in carrying out his duties under this chapter, shall make the applicant or licensee liable to prosecution for perjury under the laws of the state.
(Code 1992, § 32-113)
Sec. 28-36. - Schedule of license fees.
The fee schedule for licenses shall be established from year to year by the city council.
(Code 1992, § 32-201)
Sec. 28-37. - Each line of business to be identified on business registration.
The business registration of each business operated in the city shall identify the major lines of business that the business conducts. No business shall conduct any line of business without first having that line of business registered with the city clerk's office, and that line of business being noted by the city clerk upon the business registration form which is to be displayed by the business owner, and making payment of the applicable fee for that activity.
(Code 1992, § 32-202)
Sec. 28-38. - Number of businesses considered to be operating in the city.
Where a person conducts business at more than one fixed location, each location or place shall be considered a separate business for the purpose of occupation tax and shall require a separate occupation tax certificate and payment of applicable occupation tax at each location.
(Code 1992, § 32-203)
Secs. 28-39—28-66. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - GAMING DEVICES

Sec. 28-67. - Issuance of game and/or entertainment permit.
A written permit, in addition to a business license, will be required for each and every jukebox, pool table, video game, pinball machine and all similar devices operated in any business. Annual fees for each game shall be established by the city council from time to time. The fee will be applicable for the whole year or any part thereof. A permit for a business will reflect the total number of games permitted. The presence of more games in any business than the permit calls for will result in an automatic fine to be established by the city council from time to time, per excess game.
(Code 1992, § 32-303)

ARTICLE V. - PAWNBROKERS[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— Pawnbrokers, O.C.G.A. § 44-12-130 et seq.; local governments, limitation on imposition and calculation of regulatory fees, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9; local laws not superseded, O.C.G.A. § 44-12-135; municipal authority to license and regulate pawnbrokers, O.C.G.A. § 44-12-136.

Sec. 28-111. - Definitions.
For purpose of this article, a "pawnbroker" is any person with whom property is deposited as security for the payment of a debt. The definitions set forth in O.C.G.A. § 44-12-130 shall be effective as definitions of the words, terms, and phrases used in this article. All words, terms, and phrases used herein, other than those specifically defined elsewhere in this chapter, shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them in O.C.G.A. § 44-12-130, and shall have the same scope and effect that the same words, terms, and phrases have where used in O.C.G.A. § 44-12-130.
(Code 1992, § 32-302(1))
Sec. 28-112. - License fee.
The annual business license fee for operating a pawnbroker shop shall be determined by the city council. If a new license is issued July 1, or thereafter, the fee shall be one-half the annual fee.
(Code 1992, § 32-302(2))
Sec. 28-113. - Shop operated with other businesses.
When operating a pawnbroker business in conjunction with another business, the opening and closing hours shall be the same as with the other business; provided, however, that such closing hour shall in any event be no later than 10:00 p.m. However, no pawnbroker may open his place of business or transact any business therein on Sunday.
(Code 1992, § 32-302(3))
Sec. 28-114. - Weekly report; false statements.
All pawnbrokers shall make a correct and truthful weekly report, in writing, on Monday of each week, to the police chief at his office in the police department, wherein he shall supply the information required by section 28-120 pertaining to all property sold or pledged to them during the previous week. Such report shall be signed by the pawnbroker making such report. Any pawnbroker making any willfully false or misleading statements in such reports shall be punished as provided in section 28-116.
(Code 1992, § 32-302(4))
Sec. 28-115. - Transactions with minors.
No pawnbroker shall receive any article in pawn or purchase any article for resale from any person under 18 years of age.
(Code 1992, § 32-302(5))
Sec. 28-116. - Penalties.
Any pawnbroker or person running a pawnshop or employee thereof violating the provisions of this article shall be punished as the municipal court may direct, and in addition the city council may also, on proper proof, revoke the license of any pawnbroker violating the provisions of any law or ordinance pertaining to pawnbrokers.
(Code 1992, § 32-302(6))
Sec. 28-117. - Premises open to police.
It shall be the duty of every pawnbroker to admit to his premises the police chief or any other police officer who may desire at any time to search for articles missing or stolen, or to make any inspection authorized by this article, without the formality of a search warrant.
(Code 1992, § 32-302(7))
Sec. 28-118. - Inspection of pledges and purchases.
All articles pledged or sold to pawnbrokers shall, at all times, be subject to inspection and examination by the police.
(Code 1992, § 32-302(8))
Sec. 28-119. - Display of pledges.
Every pawnbroker shall have his pledges openly displayed so that the inspection authorized by this article may be easily made.
(Code 1992, § 32-302(9))
Sec. 28-120. - Records—Generally.
Every pawnbroker shall maintain a book, in permanent form, in which shall be entered in legible English at the time of each loan, purchase, or sale:
(1)
The date of the transaction;
(2)
The name of the person conducting the transaction;
(3)
The name, age and address of the customer, a description of the general appearance of the customer, and the distinctive number from the customer's driver's license or other similar identification card;
(4)
An identification and description of the pledged or purchased goods, including, if reasonably available, the serial number, model number, or other number, and all identifying marks inscribed thereon;
(5)
The number of the receipt or pawn ticket;
(6)
The price paid or the amount loaned;
(7)
The number of the check issued for the purchase price or loan, if payment is made by check;
(8)
The maturity date of the transaction;
(9)
The signature of the customer.
(Code 1992, § 32-302(10))
Sec. 28-121. - Same—Receipt.
A receipt providing all of the information specified in section 28-120 shall be signed and a copy given to the person pledging or selling property.
(Code 1992, § 32-302(11))
Sec. 28-122. - Same—Inspection.
The records required to be kept by section 28-120 shall be subject to inspection and examination by the police during the business hours when the proprietor or any of his agents or employees are present.
(Code 1992, § 32-302(12))
Sec. 28-123. - Same—When entries made.
The entries required by this article shall be made as soon after the transaction as is possible and, in no event, more than one hour thereafter.
(Code 1992, § 32-302(13))
Sec. 28-124. - Same—False entries.
It shall be unlawful for any person engaged in the business of a pawnbroker, or his clerk, agent, or employee to make any false entry in any books kept as required by section 28-120.
(Code 1992, § 32-302(14))
Sec. 28-125. - Goods to be held ten days.
Any pawnbroker or person operating under a pawnbroker's license who takes goods on pawn or buys goods, taking full title thereto, the "goods" being used in the broadest sense and including all kinds of personal property, shall hold such goods so taken in pawn or purchased for at least ten days before disposing of the same by sale, transfer, shipment or otherwise. This section cannot be waived by contract, but it does not apply and is not intended to cover purchases from reputable wholesale supply houses and manufacturers.
(Code 1992, § 32-302(15))
Sec. 28-126. - Maximum interest chargeable by pawnbroker.
Any pawnbroker may contract for and receive interest up to the rate of two percent per month, 24 percent annually, with a minimum charge of $5.00 per month on any loan. Any charge directly or indirectly made, contracted for, or received in excess of the amounts permitted by this section shall be uncollectible and the pawn or pledge shall be void. In addition, a 25 percent storage fee may be charged. Pawn shops may charge a storage fee for motor vehicles not to exceed $30.00 per day.
(Code 1992, § 32-302(16))
Sec. 28-127. - Public posting of interest charge.
The pawnbroker is required to post, in a location that is clearly in view of the public, the maximum interest rate the pawn shop may charge.
(Code 1992, § 32-302(17))
Secs. 28-128—28-143. - Reserved.
ARTICLE VI. - LANDFILLS

Sec. 28-144. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning.
Landfill means an area of land on which, or an excavation in which, solid waste is placed for permanent disposal, and which is not a land application unit, surface impoundment, injection well, or compost pile.
Recyclable means those materials which have known use, reuse or recycling potential; can be feasibly used, reused or recycled; and have been diverted or removed from the solid waste stream for sale, use, reuse or recycling.
Recycling means any process by which materials which would otherwise become solid waste are collected, separated, or processed and reused or returned to use in the form of raw materials or products.
Refuse means solid waste, discarded putrescible and nonputrescible waste, including garbage and rubbish, industrial waste, household waste, demolition waste, construction waste, hazardous waste and any other waste material in a solid, semisolid, or liquid state not otherwise defined in this section, but does not include recyclable materials.
(Ord. of 12-14-1998(1))
Sec. 28-145. - Approval to operate landfills.
No public or private landfill shall be operated within the city without prior written approval. No business license shall be granted for private solid waste handling where the applicant or a representative of the applicant has been judicially determined to have made concealments and/or misrepresentations concerning any application for solid waste handling or to operate a landfill according to any federal, state, or local laws.
(Ord. of 12-14-1998(2))
Sec. 28-146. - Transportation of refuse.
It shall be unlawful for any person to haul, convey or cause to be conveyed any refuse upon or along the public streets and roadways except when the material transported is adequately secured in such a manner as to prevent it from falling, leaking fluids or being blown from the transporting vehicle. The operator or owner of the offending vehicle shall be personally responsible for any violation of this section.
(Ord. of 12-14-1998(4))
Sec. 28-147. - Landfill hours of operation.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a landfill in the city outside the hours of 9:00 a.m. to 3:30 p.m. No operation of any transportation, collection or disposal activities shall be conducted at a landfill in the city between the hours of 3:30 p.m. and 9:00 a.m.
(Ord. of 12-14-1998(6))
Sec. 28-148. - Testing before landfill refuse.
It shall be unlawful for any person to haul, convey, or cause to be conveyed any refuse or to dispose of any refuse in a landfill in the city without providing written proof in report form from an EPA-approved laboratory of scientific testing showing the constituents of any and all materials in each load of such refuse.
(Ord. of 12-14-1998(7))
Sec. 28-149. - Refuse from areas outside the city.
It shall be unlawful for any person to haul, convey or cause to be conveyed any refuse to a landfill within the city from outside the city limits without prior written permission of the city.
(Ord. of 12-14-1998(8))
Sec. 28-150. - Penalties.
(a)
In the event that any person violates any provision of this article, the city may, in addition to other remedies, institute injunction, mandamus, or other appropriate action or proceeding to prevent such unlawful act or to correct or abate such violation including removal of illegally disposed of solid waste and restoration of the disposal site to that characterized by the presence of natural slopes and vegetation in all appearances to be of a pristine nature.
(b)
Any person who shall do anything or fail to do anything prohibited by any provision of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, and, upon conviction, shall be assessed with a fine to be determined by the city council, from time to time and may be liable to pay all costs and expenses involved in such case. Each day any violation continues shall be considered a separate offense.
(Ord. of 12-14-1998(12))
