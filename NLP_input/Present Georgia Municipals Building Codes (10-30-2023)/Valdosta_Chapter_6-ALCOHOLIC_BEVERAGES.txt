ARTICLE I. - GENERAL

Sec. 6-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcoholic beverage means and includes all beverages containing alcohol, including but not limited to, distilled spirits, beer, malt beverages, wine or fortified wine.
Brown bagging means the bringing of alcoholic beverages into business establishments licensed for the retail consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises.
City means the City of Valdosta, Georgia.
City manager means the Manager of the City of Valdosta, Georgia or his designee for implementation of this chapter.
Container means a receptacle in which food is served.
Distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverages obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume, including, but not limited to, all fortified wines.
Entertainment establishment means any business licensed by the city as a retail consumption dealer and in which also provides live music or provides pre-recorded music for the purpose of dancing, or provides entertainment such as, but not limited to, dancers, comedians, karaoke, performers or other like or similar entertainment. A business establishment which has its primary business as the operation of a restaurant shall not be a considered an entertainment establishment.
Food caterer means any person who prepares food for consumption off the premises.
Fortified wines means any alcoholic beverage containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "fortified wine" includes, but is not limited to brandy.
Growler means a bottle made of glass or other material customary to the industry, provided that the bottle is capable of being sealed with a cap and then sealed with a plastic collar for the purpose of complying with open container law; and further provided, that the bottle does not exceed 68 ounces and is filled by a licensee or employee of a licensed establishment from a keg or serving tank.
Improved outdoor location means a privately owned area attached or immediately adjacent to the premises of a retail consumption dealer which meets all applicable building code requirements, including seating and tables in adequate and sufficient numbers to accommodate any customers allowed into the location and specifically designated for the consumption of food and alcoholic beverages. A varied and complete menu of food items must be available in the designated improved outdoor location during all hours in which alcohol is consumed therein for any such specifically designated location to qualify for this exemption of this subsection. For similar areas on public rights-of-way, please see the definition of outdoor public seating area.
Legal age means the age established by O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23, as amended from time to time, as the age below which alcoholic beverages may not lawfully be sold to, furnished to, purchased by or possessed by a person.
License means the authorization granted by the City of Valdosta to engage in the sale of specified alcoholic beverages for a calendar year, or the remaining portion of a calendar year.
License year means the 12-month period for and with respect to which licenses under this chapter shall be issued. License years shall begin on January 1 of each year and end on December 31.
Licensed alcoholic beverage caterer means any retail consumption dealer who has been licensed pursuant to this article.
Licensee means a person holding a license to sell alcoholic beverages issued by the city pursuant the terms of this chapter.
Lounge means a separate room connected with a part of and adjacent to the premises of a restaurant or its adjacent lobby or reception area, with a seating capacity of at least 20 persons, and with all access and all exits to and from the lounge opening into such restaurant or the lobby or reception area adjoining such restaurant except for emergency exits or exits used solely as service doors.
Malt beverages means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt hops or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than 14 percent alcohol by volume and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer and strong beer, provided that the term "malt beverage" does not include sake, also known as Japanese rice wine.
Manager means the individual who supervises the regular operations of a business licensed under this chapter and who is physically present on-site at such business: (a) at least 35 hours per week; or (b) at least 90 percent of the hours such business is open to the public, whichever is less.
Manufacturer means any maker, producer, or bottler of an alcoholic beverage. "Manufacturer" also means:
(1)
In the case of distilled spirits, any person engaged in distilling, rectifying, or blending any distilled spirits.
(2)
In the case of malt beverages, any brewer.
(3)
In the case of wine, any vintner.
Outdoor public seating area means the location and use of tables and chairs on the public sidewalk or public right-of-way and for which an outdoor public seating area permit has been obtained in accordance with the section 6-84.
Package means a bottle, can, keg, barrel or other original consumer container.
Person means any individual, firm, partnership, cooperative, nonprofit membership corporation, joint venture, association, company, corporation, agency, syndicate, estate, trust, business trust, receiver, fiduciary, or other group or combination acting as a unit, body politic, or political subdivision, whether public, private, or quasi-public.
Premises means one physically identifiable place of business consisting of one room or two or more contiguous rooms operating under the same trade name, business license and ownership where alcoholic beverages are sold or otherwise dispensed to the public, such premises to be within the confines of the licensed building structure; provided, however that any outside area whether defined as an improved outdoor location or outdoor public seating area may be considered premises for the purpose of serving alcoholic beverages from a bar or other dispensing facility located within the confines of the building structure; except that in the case of hotels and motels, the term "premises" shall include only that portion of the property where alcoholic beverages are sold, delivered or served.
Private club means a non-profit organization which is organized under state law which meets each of the following requirements:
(1)
Has either a tax exempt status under Section 501 of the United States Internal Revenue Code or operates pursuant to a charter from a state or national parent organization which has such tax exempt status;
(2)
Has been in existence at least one year prior to the filing of its application for a license to be issued pursuant to this chapter;
(3)
Has at least 50 members paying dues on a regular periodic basis;
(4)
Is organized and operated exclusively for the pleasure and recreation of its members and their guests or for other nonprofit purposes;
(5)
Has facilities which are available for use only by the membership thereof and their guests and not by the general public;
(6)
Owns, rents or leases a building or space within a building for facilities for the use of its members and their guests;
(7)
Maintains kitchen and dining room space, equipment and items necessary to appropriately provide food service of full course meals for its members and their guests;
(8)
Is staffed with a sufficient number of employees for the preparation and service of meals for its members and their guests;
(9)
Has no member, officer, agent or employee directly or indirectly receiving, in the form of salary or other compensation, any profits, beyond a fixed salary, from the sale of alcoholic beverages to the club or to its members or their guests; and
(10)
Have such licenses as are required for the operation thereof.
Restaurant means a business kept, used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public as a place where meals are actually and regularly prepared and served for a period of at least five hours per day on at least five days per week with the exception of: (i) holidays, (ii) vacations not exceeding two weeks duration, and (iii) periods of redecorating not exceeding 30 calendar days duration except to the extent extended by the city manager in its discretion upon request therefore; and which meets all of the following requirements:
(1)
Meets all health and sanitation requirements for food service establishments required under applicable federal, state and local laws, codes, ordinances, rules and regulations;
(2)
Maintains upon the premises thereof, in operative condition, cooking and kitchen facilities and equipment appropriate to accommodate the preparation of meals for at least 30 food service customers at one time;
(3)
Maintains upon the premises thereof seating facilities, at booths or tables designed for food service, sufficient for at least 30 food service customers at one time, not including seating provided by counters and stools, with booths and tables for purposes hereof being large enough to adequately accommodate at least two place settings of cutlery and dishes;
(4)
Maintains at all times such additional facilities, equipment and items as well as employees as shall be necessary for the cooking, preparation and service of meals for consumption at tables or booths located upon the premises for at least 30 food service customers at one time; and
(5)
Is at all times during the hours of operation thereof prepared to serve full course meals with substantial entrees from a substantial variety of culinary choices, or substantial substitutes thereof, chosen by food service customers from a printed menu available to all food service customers at prices which are competitive with other restaurants in the community whether or not such other restaurants engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages.
Retail consumption dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises at retail only to consumers and not for resale.
Retail sale means the sale or offer of sale of any alcoholic beverage to any member of the public.
Retail tobacco store means a retail store utilized primarily for the sale and consumption of tobacco products and accessories and in which the sale of other products, including alcohol, does not exceed 50 percent of gross revenue.
Retailer or retail dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages to consumers and not for resale.
Special event privilege permit may be issued by the City of Valdosta for special events that: (1) are considered to be of a community nature; (2) that are advertised and open to the entire community; and (3) for which the city or Central Valdosta Development Authority is a sponsor or co-sponsor. Such events must be submitted to and approved by the office of the city manager under the terms and conditions as determined by the city, which may be changed from time to time. Such conditions may include certain notification requirements, security, sanitation and clean-up, beginning and ending times, insurance, parking requirements, and more. At the city's sole discretion, the special event privilege permit may be issued on a one-time basis, or for ongoing events, on a weekly, monthly, or annual basis; provided that said ongoing event permits shall not exceed 12 months. Any permit issued is subject to revocation for cause at any time. The city reserves the right to review the history of previous events to determine future decisions in regards to the special event privilege permit and to approve or deny any and all requests.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, and combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines and like products. The term does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this section.
Wine tasting means the dispensing of wine in small quantities to customers for consumption on the premises at no charge as an instructional or educational promotion of wine products in accordance with subsection 6-36(b). Wine tastings may be allowed in establishments operating under a retail wine package license or in a facility approved through a special event privilege permit at the discretion of the city manager or his designee and per the terms of that permit.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2015-4, § 1, 2-5-2015; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 6-2. - Activities are privileges.
The manufacture, distribution, sale and possession of alcoholic beverages within the city are declared to be privileges and not rights. Such privileges shall not be exercised except in accordance with this chapter.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-3. - Compliance with chapter, license and applicable law.
No person shall:
(1)
Distribute, sell, or offer to sell, either at retail or wholesale, any alcoholic beverage in the city except to the extent permitted pursuant to a current license issued by the city;
(2)
Distribute, handle, possess, sell, or offer to sell any alcoholic beverage in the city in violation of any provisions of this article or any provision of any applicable federal or state law regulating the distribution, handling, possession, selling or offering to sell any alcoholic beverage in this state; and/or
(3)
Assist or aid another in violating any provision of this article, or any federal or state law regulating the distribution, handling, possession, selling or offering to sell any alcoholic beverage in this state.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-4. - Knowledge of chapter.
The holder of any license as well as all employees, agents and servants of any business in which such license is utilized shall be conclusively presumed, for purposes of this chapter, to have at all times complete and full knowledge of all provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Secs. 6-5—6-30. - Reserved.

Sec. 6-33. - Activities authorized pursuant to licenses.
The holder of a license issued by the city, as well as the employees of such holder, shall be authorized subject to the provisions of this article, the laws of the State of Georgia and the rules, regulations and requirements of the Georgia Department of Revenue relative thereto to engage in the following respective activities but none other than those which are specifically authorized with respect to each such license:
(1)
Wholesale malt beverage license: wholesale sale only of malt beverages in original unbroken and sealed containers to the holders of effective retail malt beverage licenses issued by the georgia department of revenue for the purpose of resale only.
(2)
Retail malt beverage package license: Retail sale only of malt beverages in original unbroken and sealed containers to consumers for take-out only and not for consumption upon the licensed premises.
(3)
Retail malt beverage consumption license: Retail sale only of malt beverages to consumers for consumption only upon the licensed premises.
(4)
Wholesale wine license: Wholesale sale only of wine in original unbroken and sealed containers to the holders of current retail wine licenses issued by the Georgia Department of Revenue for the purpose of resale only.
(5)
Retail wine package license: Retail sale only of wine in original unbroken and sealed containers to consumers for take-out only and not for consumption upon the licensed premises.
(6)
Retail wine consumption license: Retail sale only of wine to consumers for consumption only upon the licensed premises.
(7)
Wholesale distilled spirits license: Wholesale sale of distilled spirits in original unbroken and sealed containers to the holders of current retail distilled spirits licenses issued by the Georgia Department of Revenue for the purpose of resale only.
(8)
Retail distilled spirits package license: Retail sale only of distilled spirits in original unbroken and sealed containers to consumers for take-out only and not for consumption upon the licensed premises.
(9)
Retail distilled spirits consumption license: Retail sale only of distilled spirits to consumers for consumption only upon the licensed premises.
(10)
Sunday sales consumption license: A license which allows the selling and dispensing of alcoholic beverages on Sunday for consumption on the premises as set forth in section 6-81.
(11)
Sunday package sales license: A license which allows the packages selling of alcoholic beverages on Sunday as set forth in section 6-81.
(12)
Manufacture of malt beverages license: A license which allows the manufacture of malt beverages and making of sales of such malt beverages, including sales to licensed wholesalers and direct-to-consumers sales of such malt beverages in accordance with state law. This license allows only the sale of malt beverages manufactured by the licensee on the licensed premises.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-34. - Qua;ifications for license.
No license for the sale or distribution of alcoholic beverages required by the provisions of this article shall be issued to any person or for any location unless and until it has been successfully demonstrated to the satisfaction of the city manager that:
(1)
Such person, or the person who shall actually and actively be in charge and manages the day to day operation of the business in which such license shall be utilized, is a citizen of the United States;
(2)
Such person by reason of such person's business experience, financial standing, moral character, mental capacity, physical capacity, trade associations, lack of criminal history, and reputation in communities in which he has lived is, in the opinion of the city manager, likely to operate the business in which the license will be utilized in conformity with federal, state and local laws, ordinances, rules, regulations, and requirements, including particularly the provisions of this chapter and the laws, rules and regulations of the state and the United States relative to the manufacture, sale, and distribution of alcoholic beverages. In making such determination, the city manager shall, in addition to the other and further provisions of this section, consider all information that would be considered by the city manager if this person was being denied a renewal license pursuant to the requirements of section 6-49 or, if this person was having his license suspended or revoked pursuant to the requirements of section 6-52; provided further that no license shall be issued to or for:
a.
Any person whose business in which the license will be utilized is conducted by a manager or agent unless such manager or agent possesses and meets all of the qualifications required hereunder for the holder of a license and would not be prohibited from being the holder of such a license;
b.
A partnership unless all partners, members and managers of such partnership, as well as the manager of the business in which the license will be utilized, possess and meet all of the qualifications for the holder of a license and would not be prohibited from being the holder of such a license;
c.
A corporation unless all officers thereof, all directors thereof and all shareholders thereof who shall either directly or beneficially as such word is defined and utilized in the Internal Revenue Code of the United States and regulations issued pursuant thereto own more than 20 percent of the outstanding shares of stock thereof, as well as the manager of the business in which the license will be utilized, possess and meet all of the qualifications for the holder of a license and would not be prohibited from being the holder of such a license;
d.
Any person who has been convicted, has entered a plea of nolo contendere or has forfeited a bond with respect to any felony within ten years prior to the filing of any such application or with respect to any misdemeanor within five years prior to the filing of any such application including particularly but not being limited to those offenses involving force or violence, prostitution, gambling, tax law, or alcohol-related violations in the event that such conviction, plea or bond forfeiture tends to indicate, in the opinion of the city manager, that the applicant may not maintain or operate the business in which the license sought by the applicant would be utilized in conformity with federal, state or local laws, rules and regulations. The terms "felony" and "misdemeanor" as used in this subsection shall include the violation of any criminal law of the United States of America, the State of Georgia, or any other state of the United States of America; provided that the term "misdemeanor" shall not include those violations which are commonly referred to as "traffic violations". The term "conviction" as used in this subsection shall include an adjudication of guilt, plea of guilty, a plea of nolo contendere or the forfeiture of a bond by a person charged with a crime. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the city manager, in his sole discretion, may decide to authorize the issuance of a license to a person who has in the past been convicted of, plead guilty to, entered a plea of nolo contendere to, or forfeited a bond on any crime when, after a thorough investigation of all the facts including parole or probation officer's reports, judge's recommendations, and any other evidence bearing on the character of the applicant, it has determined, in the sole discretion of the city manager, that such action is in keeping with the ends of justice and the public interest and welfare;
e.
Any person who as either the previous holder of any license issued by any authority, or as a party interested in any business in which any license issued by any authority was utilized, either conducted, or allowed the conduct of, the business with respect to which such license was issued in such a manner as to cause, in the opinion of the city manager, greater than normal police activity, observation or inspection in order to prevent the violation of any law or regulation either relating to such license or the business associated therewith or in order to maintain public order;
f.
Any location that does not satisfy the conditions of section 6-37;
g.
Any location that does not have adequate parking relative to the potential occupancy load of the premises. For purposes of this section, whenever there is a difference in the allowed occupancy approved by the inspections department through the certificate of occupancy issued for the establishment and the occupancy approved by the Valdosta Fire Department, the more restrictive occupancy level shall prevail except where provisions of the Valdosta/Lowndes County Land Development Regulations govern shared parking;
h.
Any location for which there is an unpaid or unsatisfied tax or other financial obligation due the city which is not then the subject of appeal or litigation;
i.
Any person who has not attained the age of 21 years;
j.
Any person who either held any license issued by any authority which was revoked by such authority within a one-year period immediately prior to the date of the filing of an application or was a party interested in any business in which any license issued by any authority was revoked by such authority within the one-year period prior to the date of the filing of an application;
k.
Any location at which a license has previously been suspended or revoked by either the city or the Georgia Department of Revenue and where, in the opinion of the city manager, there exists the likelihood that the utilization of such license at such location would cause or necessitate greater than normal police activity, observation or inspection in order to either prevent violation of laws and regulations relating to such location or to maintain public order, due to the history of either: (i) violations of the provisions of this chapter or of the laws, rules and regulations of the state relative to alcoholic beverages by the holder of the previous license at such location or agents, servants or employees thereof; or (ii) activities or conduct of patrons of the business in which such previous license was utilized at such location, all as compared to the locations of other licensed premises, or would cause or tend to cause, in the opinion of the city manager, greater than normal community problems or conflict as compared to the locations of other licensed premises; and
l.
Any location at which a license has previously been suspended or revoked by either the city or the Georgia Department of Revenue and where, in the opinion of the city manager, problems associated with the utilization of a prior license at such location were such that it would not be in the best interest of the public health, safety or welfare to grant any other license for such location.
Notwithstanding the above, any facility which has been utilized and permitted under this article during the previous six months prior to the application as a legally permitted and nonconforming facility, regardless of distance from churches or schools, shall be eligible for application and approval of license under this article, provided the business is in good standing per the requirements of this chapter and its history of operation.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-35. - Additional qualifications for retail malt beverage consumption license and distilled spirits consumption license.
(a)
In addition to the requirements of section 6-34, retail malt beverage consumption licenses and distilled spirits consumption licenses shall only be issued for individual locations that comply with all of the following:
(1)
Meet all health and sanitation requirements set for food service establishments by the city, state and federal government;
(2)
Have installed an operative cooking facility and kitchen equipment on the premises for accommodating the demand for 30 food customers in C-C zoning, 60 customers in C-H zoning and 30 customers in C-D zoning;
(3)
Have a complete place setting of utensils and durable and reusable serving containers in regular use for 30 food customers in C-C zoning, 60 customers in C-H zoning and 30 customers in C-D zoning;
(4)
Have seating facilities, at booths or tables designed for food service, sufficient for 60 food customers in C-C zoning, 60 customers in C-H zoning and 30 customers in C-D zoning, and such booths or tables shall each be large enough to accommodate at least two place settings of utensils and serving containers;
(5)
For locations within C-D zoning, the applicant licensee, excluding private clubs as defined in this chapter, must derive at least 50 percent of its total annual gross food and beverage sales from the sale of prepared meals or food;
(6)
At all times during the hours of operation are prepared to serve full course meals with a substantial variety of culinary choices, or substantial substitutes therefore, which must be prepared on-site;
(7)
Shall print and maintain a complete menu and furnish such printed menu to each food customer, with prices which are competitive with other restaurants within the city, whether or not those restaurants engage in the business of serving alcoholic beverages;
(8)
Requirements in subsections (2), (3), (4) and (5) above, shall all be provided within the same premises as the licensed establishment, and shall not be shared with any other business establishment unless such establishment is adjacent, under the same ownership, and internally connected to allow the free flow of patrons;
(9)
Alcoholic beverages shall be served only during the hours in which all printed menu items, or substantial substitutes therefore, are available to the customers. The hours of service for food may be prolonged, but in no event shall alcoholic beverages be sold at any time contrary to the hours established for the sale of alcoholic beverages by other ordinances of the City of Valdosta.
(b)
Additional qualifications and restrictions on growler license:
(1)
The filling of growlers by means of a tapped keg shall not constitute the breaking of the package as contemplated by O.C.G.A. § 3-3-26 or other provisions of this chapter.
(2)
Growlers may only be filled from kegs procured by the licensee from a duly licensed wholesaler.
(3)
Only professionally and sanitized and sealed growlers may be filled and made available for retail sale. Each growler must be securely sealed and removed from the premises in the original sealed condition.
(4)
Consumption on the premises is strictly prohibited, however, samples may be made available but shall not exceed three ounces in volume, nor shall any one individual be offered more than four samples within a 24-hour period.
(5)
Growlers may not be sold at any establishment engaged in the sale of distilled spirits.
(6)
The sale of growlers shall require the payment of an annual license fee of $250.00 from the local license holder and the payment of all excise tax.
(c)
The additional requirements for acquiring a retail malt beverage consumption licenses and distilled spirits consumption licenses in subsection (a) shall not apply if the premises from which the alcoholic beverages are to be sold and consumed is located in the following:
(1)
Hotels of 50 rooms or more;
(2)
Hotels of less than 50 rooms for the retail sale of alcoholic beverages solely to occupants of the hotel and their guests by the drink for consumption only on the premises; or
(3)
A private club.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2014-29, 11-6-2014; Ord. No. 2015-4, § 2, 2-5-2015; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-36. - Wine only establishments, wine tastings and retail tobacco stores.
(a)
If a license holder only sells wine, it shall not be required to meet the conditions of section 6-35 of this chapter. To demonstrate compliance with this section, the license holder shall allow the city manager, or his designee, full permission to inspect the premises of said location and produce upon demand, any and all information that may be relevant to same.
(b)
Upon filing an application and payment of a license fee of $50.00 per year, the city manager may issue a permit to hold wine tastings. Wine tasting may be allowed in conjunction with an event through a special event privilege permit at the discretion of the city manager or his designee and per the terms of that permit. Additionally, wine tastings shall be allowed in premises operating under a retail wine package license provided the following conditions are met:
(1)
The applicant shall be a licensee authorized to sell wine within the city pursuant to and in accordance with a retail wine package license;
(2)
The applicant shall maintain on the premises and offer for sale at all times a variety of wines from not less than 30 manufacturers of wine; and
(3)
The applicant's establishment shall have a maximum interior floor area of 1,850 square feet devoted to the storage, display and sale of wine.
(c)
Retail tobacco establishments may be issued a retail malt consumption license, provided the retail tobacco establishment has a maximum interior floor area of 1,850 square feet devoted to the storage, retail sale and consumption of tobacco products and accessories. The occupancy of the establishment may not exceed more than 40 customers as set forth on the certificate of occupancy issued by the City of Valdosta for the business.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-37. - Location of alcoholic beverage businesses.
(a)
Retail malt beverage, distilled spirits and wine package licenses.
(1)
The provisions of any other ordinance of the city notwithstanding, retail malt beverage, distilled spirits or wine package licenses shall be issued only for locations in areas classified at the time of the issuance by the Valdosta Land Development Regulations (LDR), as amended, as zoned C-D, C-H, C-C, M-1 or M-2;
(2)
No retail package licenses, whether it be for distilled spirits, malt beverages or wine, shall be issued for a premises unless it is in accordance with the provisions contained in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.
(b)
Manufacturer of malt beverage license, retail malt beverage, distilled spirits and wine consumption licenses.
(1)
The provisions of any other ordinance of the city notwithstanding, retail malt beverage, distilled spirits and wine package licenses shall be issued only for locations in areas classified at the time of issuance by the LDR, as amended, as zoned C-D, C-H, or C-C.
(2)
In all areas classified by the Valdosta Land Development Regulations, as amended, as C-D, a retail malt beverage, distilled spirits and wine consumption license shall only be issued if the building from which the business will operate is more than 50 feet from any church sanctuary, school building or education building. In all areas classified by the LDR, as amended, as zoned C-C or C-H, the retail malt beverage, distilled spirits and wine consumption license shall only be issued if the premises from which the business will operate is more than 50 feet from any private residence, church building, school building or educational building. The terms "school building" and "educational building" shall apply only to state, county, city, or church school buildings and to such buildings at such other schools in which are taught subjects commonly taught in the common schools and colleges of this state and which are public schools or private schools as defined in O.C.G.A. § 20-2-690(b). The terms "church sanctuary" and "church building" shall mean a stand-alone building owned in fee simple by the religious organization where regular worship services are performed and shall not mean a commonly platted area which has common or shared parking such as shopping centers, strip malls, etc.
(c)
Measurement of distances. Unless otherwise provided by law, all measurements to determine distances required by this chapter shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground in the following manner: (1) from the front door of the structure from which alcoholic beverages are sold or offered for sale; (2) to the front door of the building of a church, government-owned treatment center or other retail package store; or (3) the nearest property line of the real property being used for school or educational purposes.

Sec. 6-38. - Applications.
Prior to the issuance of any license, the applicant shall file a written application with the city, in such form and substance as shall from time to time be determined by the city manager, together with a check payable to the City of Valdosta or cash for the application fee provided for in this article. In the case of an individual applicant who will actually and actively be in charge and manages the day to day operation of the business in which the license applied for will be utilized, then the name, age, sex, height, weight, date of birth, Social Security number, current residence address, business addresses, and all previous such addresses of the applicant within the last ten years;

(4)

(5)
A statement with respect to whether the applicant or any person with respect to whom information is required under subsections (1), (2), (3) or (4) of this section has made at any prior time any application to any governmental entity for any license, and, if so, the circumstances thereof, including the disposition and current status thereof, all in sufficient detail to evaluate such application;

(9)

(11)
A statement that both the applicant and all other persons with respect to whom information is required under subsections (1), (2), (3) or (4) of this section are fully qualified in all respects under this chapter to be the holder of a license issued by the city and that all such persons are entitled to have the license requested issued in accordance with the provisions of this chapter;
(12)
A head and shoulder photograph, such as is contained on a state driver's license, taken within the past three years of the applicant and all persons with respect to whom information is required under subsections (1), (2), (3) or (4) of this section; and
(13)
Written consents of the applicant and all persons with respect to whom information is required under subsections (1), (2), (3) or (4) of this section that the city has continuing permission and authority of all such persons to monitor the public records of the city, county, the state and of the United States to ensure on-going compliance with the statements set forth in subsections (7) and (8) of this section.

ARTICLE III. - OPERATIONS

Sec. 6-81. - Hours of operation.
(a)
Retail consumption dealers shall not sell, give away or otherwise dispense any alcoholic beverages during the following time periods:
(1)
Monday: 12:00 midnight - 10:00 a.m.;
(2)
Tuesday—Saturday: 2:00 a.m. - 10:00 a.m.; and
(3)
Sunday: 2:00 a.m. - 11:00 a.m. Additional alcohol sales on Sunday shall be governed by sections 6-82 and 6-83.
(b)
Retail package dealers shall not sell, give away or otherwise dispense any alcoholic beverages as follows:
(1)
Monday: 12:00 midnight - 6:00 a.m.;
(2)
Tuesday—Saturday: 2:00 a.m. - 6:00 a.m.; and
(3)
Sunday: 2:00 a.m. - 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m. - 12:00 midnight. Alcohol sales on Sunday shall be governed by sections 6-82 and 6-83.
(c)
Entertainment establishments and retail consumption dealers shall close between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 10:00 a.m. This prohibition applies notwithstanding the fact that said entertainment establishment may also qualify as a restaurant under this article.
(d)
Manufacturers shall not sell, give away or otherwise dispense any alcoholic beverages as follows:
(1)
Monday: 12:00 midnight - 6:00 a.m.;
(2)
Tuesday—Saturday: 2:00 a.m. - 6:00 a.m.; and
(3)
Sunday: 2:00 a.m. - 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m. - 12:00 midnight.
(e)
Except as provided by subsection 6-83(c), no customer or other person not employed by the entertainment establishment or retail consumption dealer shall be permitted to remain on the premises later than 2:30 a.m. Employees of these establishments must depart the establishment building by 3:30 a.m. If an "entertainment establishment" has unique circumstances as a result of the load-in or load-out of entertainers, they may request additional time, specifically for this purpose only, by directly contacting the Valdosta Police Department, who may not unreasonably refuse such a request if conditions are verified and warranted. This section shall not apply, however, to restaurants, motels and hotels that are not designated as an entertainment establishment, as defined in this chapter, and whose primary sales and the majority of its business income is generated by products or services other than alcoholic beverages sales. Any business licensed to provide alcoholic beverages by the drink and designated as an entertainment establishment shall comply with the closing times established in the ordinance for entertainment establishments regardless of any other business product or service they may provide, including service as a restaurant or food services.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018; Ord. No. 2019-22, 12-5-2019)
Sec. 6-82. - Sunday sales licenses.
(a)
A retail consumption dealer may obtain a Sunday sales consumption license which allows the selling and dispensing of alcoholic beverages on Sunday from 11:00 a.m. to 12:00 midnight provided the retail consumption dealer: (1) pays Sunday sales license fee; and (2) demonstrates to the satisfaction of the city manager, or his designee, that he derives at least 50 percent of the total annual gross sales from the sale of prepared meals or food in all of the combined retail outlets of the individual establishment where food is served or in any licensed establishment which derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross income from the rental of rooms for overnight lodging.
(b)
A retail package dealer may obtain a Sunday package sales license which allows the selling of packaged sale of alcoholic beverages between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m. upon paying the applicable Sunday package sales license fee.
(c)
A manufacture of malt beverage license allows the selling and dispensing of malt beverages by the drink on Sundays from 12:30 p.m. to 12:00 midnight and the selling of packaged malt beverages from 12:30 p.m. to 11:30 p.m.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018; Ord. No. 2019-22, 12-5-2019)
Sec. 6-83. - Sunday sales permitted; elections; New Year's Day.
(a)
Alcoholic beverages may be sold on Sunday from 12:00 midnight until 2:00 a.m. No further alcohol sales may take place on Sunday unless a Sunday sale license has been obtained in accordance with section 6-81.
(b)
Alcoholic beverages may be sold on election days provided no selling, dispensing or giving away takes place within 200 feet of a polling place during such time as the polls are open.
(c)
Licensees may sell alcoholic beverages between the hours of 12:00 midnight and 4:00 a.m. on New Year's Day on any day of the week, including Sunday. All non-employees shall be off the premises no later than 4:30 a.m. and all employees no later than 5:30 a.m.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-84. - Consumption off-premises.
Except as otherwise provided in this subsection, it shall be unlawful to consume any alcoholic beverage or to be in possession of an open container of an alcoholic beverage within 500 feet of the premises of any retail consumption dealer or retail dealer. This provision shall not apply:
(1)
To a geographical area covered by a special event permit;
(2)
To a geographical area designated in a catering event permit; and
(3)
To the consumption or possession of alcoholic beverages in an improved outdoor location or outdoor public seating area.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-85. - Off-premises catering.
(a)
A retail consumption dealer that is also a food caterer may become a licensed alcoholic beverage caterer by meeting all of the following requirements:
(1)
Complying with all provisions of O.C.G.A. tit. 3, ch. 11 (sales off-premises for catered functions); and by
(2)
Meeting the requirement that at least 50 percent of the gross income for such business comes from the sale of prepared foods as set forth in section 6-35 of this article; and by
(3)
Applying for an off-premises catering license on the form prescribed by the city; and by
(4)
Paying an annual fee, not to exceed $1,000.00, to the city for such a license, with such fee being shown upon a schedule of fees maintained in the office of the city manager, provided that the combined license fees shall not exceed $5,000.00 as prescribed in section 6-179; and by
(5)
Obtaining a catering event permit, as hereinafter defined and provided, for each and every such authorized catered function to which such alcoholic beverage caterer license shall be applicable.
(b)
A licensed alcoholic beverage caterer is eligible to sell or furnish only those types of alcoholic beverages as he can sell or furnish pursuant to his underlying license or any other license issued by the State of Georgia and another Georgia municipality.
(c)
A catering event permit, issued by the city manager or his designee, is required before an otherwise licensed alcoholic beverage caterer can sell or furnish alcoholic beverages off-premises, i.e., at any location other than the licensed premises utilizing the underlying license. In order to obtain a catering event permit, the licensed alcoholic beverage caterer shall be required to submit a fully accurate and completed application on the form prescribed by the city 14 days prior to the event to be catered and shall include the following information:
(1)
Name of caterer;
(2)
Date of event;
(3)
Time (hours) of event;
(4)
Location (address) of event;
(5)
Host or sponsor of event;
(6)
Estimated number of persons to attend the event that are of legal age;
(7)
Estimated number of persons to attend the event that are not of legal age;
(8)
Whether "open bar" or "cash bar" of alcoholic beverages at event;
(9)
Estimated quantities of malt beverages, wine and/or distilled spirits (to the extent authorized by license) to be served at event;
(10)
Whether facility is private or public;
(11)
Whether location is within 300 feet of a church or school building;
(12)
A plat or sketch showing the service area where alcoholic beverages will be served indicating whether said area is within the building or in an open area;
(13)
An event fee in the amount of $50.00 shall be submitted along with the application;
(14)
Comply with the reporting provisions of O.C.G.A. § 3-11-3;
(15)
Provide security and parking enforcement plans and personnel as required by the city.
(d)
A catering event permit cannot be obtained unless the event permit application referenced in subsection (b) above is accurately completed in full and submitted to the city manager or his designee at least 14 calendar days prior to the scheduled date of the event or function. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the city manager in his sole discretion may issue a catering event permit within a shorter timeframe if the applicant has been previously approved for a catering event permit and if the proposed event location has been previously approved.
(e)
A catering event permit may be refused by the city manager or his designee for any of the following reasons:
(1)
Inadequate parking or enforcement personnel at the location designated;
(2)
Criminal record of host or applicant;
(3)
Previous complaints on location or of applicant;
(4)
Previous damage to facility by applicant or permit holder.
(f)
The city reserves the right to define and/or restrict the service area for serving of alcoholic beverages.
(g)
The city shall control the dispensing of alcohol in all of its facilities by requiring the use of a licensed alcoholic beverage caterer if alcohol is furnished at any such facility. Consistent with this article, alcohol may also be dispensed by a licensed alcoholic beverage caterer who has been issued an event permit at facilities of any local, state or federal public entity.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-86. - Brown bagging.
No person shall be permitted to carry or otherwise take an alcoholic beverage of any kind into a business or establishment licensed under this chapter to sell or dispense beer, wine, liquor, or other spirited beverages by the drink nor shall any business licensee of the city allow patrons to carry or otherwise take beer, liquor, or spirited beverages of any kind into a business in the city. However, at the sole discretion of the licensee, wine may be brought onto the premises for consumption subject to the following:
(1)
Only one bottle per patron of legal drinking age at a table or booth shall be allowed;
(2)
Before opening, the wine must be sealed in the original seal;
(3)
The bottle must be commercially manufactured;
(4)
The bottle may not exceed 1,000 ML in volume;
(5)
The bottle must be given to the licensee or his staff designee before the patron is seated;
(6)
No bottle may be returned to the patron unless the staff has re-corked the bottle to make the cork flush with the top of the bottle. The original seal must be set as close to the original condition as possible and secured to the bottle.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-87. - Manufacturing.
(a)
Notwithstanding any other provision of this chapter 6, tastings and direct-to-consumer sales, including both on-premises and off-premises sales, from the licensed premises shall be permitted under the manufacturer license in accordance with state law, as amended from time to time.
(b)
Notwithstanding any other provision of this chapter 6, and in addition to on-premises sales and package sales, a licensed brewer or brewpub may furnish or sell growlers directly to consumers in accordance with state law as amended from time to time. Furthermore, such growlers are not required to be filled from kegs obtained from wholesalers.
(c)
Notwithstanding any other provision of this chapter 6, licensed manufacturers may sell or furnish alcohol directly to consumers on Sundays between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 2:00 a.m. without having to satisfy a food sales requirement.
(d)
Notwithstanding any other provision of this chapter 6, alcoholic beverage sales can be made outdoors in the open on the premises of the manufacturer, provided that such alcoholic beverages must be consumed on the premises of the manufacturer and may not be carried off of such premises once having been opened.
(e)
Notwithstanding any other provision of this chapter 6, a manufacturer license shall not be required to obtain any additional alcohol license to take advantage of the privileges afforded to it above.
(Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)

ARTICLE IV. - MINIMUM AGE

Sec. 6-111. - Consumption by persons not of legal age.
No person, who is the holder of any alcoholic beverage license issued by the city nor any agent, servant or employee of the business in which any such license is utilized, shall in connection with the operation of the business in which such license is utilized, permit or allow any person who has not attained legal age to consume any alcoholic beverage upon the licensed premises.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-112. - Possession by persons not of legal age.
No person, who is the holder of any alcoholic beverage license issued by the city nor any agent, servant, or employee of any business in which any such license is utilized, shall in connection with the operations of the business in which such license is utilized, permit or allow any person who has not attained legal age to possess any alcoholic beverage upon the premises licensed. Notwithstanding the above, employees of alcoholic beverage license holders who have not obtained the legal age may engage in such activities described in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23(e) or O.C.G.A. § 3-3-24(b) without violating this section.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-113. - Verification of age.
All entertainment establishments are required to verify the age of all persons prior to entry with a government issued photo identification such as a driver's license. Any person under the age of 21 years old who is admitted into an entertainment establishment holding a license shall be identified by a red wrist band or strap which shall be attached around the wrist of the underage person. Such wrist band must be approved by the chief of police or his designee. All persons age 21 and over entering an entertainment establishment after 8:00 p.m. must be identified with a green wrist band or strap. Such wristband or strap must be approved by the chief of police or his designee.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-114. - Presence of a minor in grandfathered establishments.
No individual who has not attained the age of 18 shall be allowed into/onto the premises operated by a retail consumption dealer where said retail consumption dealer, because of continuity of operation, does not have to meet the additional qualifications specified in section 6-35.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)

ARTICLE V. - EMPLOYMENT

Sec. 6-136. - Employment of persons by retail and retail consumption licensees.
(a)
No holder of a license nor any agent, servant or employee of any business in which such license is utilized shall permit or allow any person who has not attained 18 years of age and who is employed by any business operated upon premises for which a license is issued by the city to engage in the sale, distribution, or acceptance of orders for alcoholic beverages.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person licensed for the retail sale of beer or liquor in the city to employ any person upon the premises where beer is sold at retail or is consumed any part of whose wages or compensation is computed on the basis of a percentage of moneys deposited upon such premises and music playing machines, pinball machines or other coin-operated devices.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any such licensee to employ any person upon such premises whose duties are to encourage or induce customers to operate coin machines upon the premises.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any such licensee to employ any person who has been convicted of a felony within the previous five years, unless approval is granted in written form by an appropriate judicial officer or as approved terms of any probation requirements. For the purposes of this section, transient or temporary personnel, such as musicians, dancers, magicians, or similar entertainers who are contracted to, or actually perform for a period of no more than 15 continuous business days, as determined by the establishment's regular and routine schedule, shall not be considered employees of the business.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)

ARTICLE VI. - SECURITY

Sec. 6-151. - Security cameras required in certain retail establishments.
Any retail business establishment licensed by the city to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages, beer or malt beverages by the package for off-premises consumption is required to install security cameras in accordance with this article.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-152. - Security camera requirements; responsibility; exception.
(a)
Cameras meeting the requirements of this section must be capable of producing a retrievable image on film, tape, or digital media that can be made a permanent record and can be enlarged through projection or other means. Cameras meeting the requirements of this section shall be maintained in proper working order at all times and shall be subject to periodic inspection by the chief of police or his designee. In addition, the location of all such cameras shall be approved or determined by the chief of police or his designee.
(b)
If the system camera used is a still frame camera, it shall contain the following equipment capabilities:
(1)
Automatic still frame.
(2)
Capable of being concealed.
(3)
Remote activation.
(4)
Quiet activation.
(5)
Must be digital format of at least four megapixels quality.
(6)
Produce good-quality color photographs.
(7)
Must have automatic focus.
(8)
Easily accessible for maintenance checks, film loading, etc.
(9)
Capable of showing signs or signals of activation covertly.
(10)
Must have wide-angle capacity.
(11)
If the system camera is a still video camera, it must be capable of following:
a.
All components must be compatible to produce sharp, detailed, still frame video images in color.
b.
The recording equipment shall be able to transfer images to a CD or other digital media within 24 hours of activation.
c.
Remote activation.
d.
Quiet activation.
e.
Color images.
(c)
Retail establishments required to install the camera security system shall be responsible for the maintenance, supplies and periodic check of such systems.
(d)
In the event of an actual robbery or other crime in any establishment containing a security camera, the police department shall be given, upon demand, the recording media that captured the incident. Under such circumstances, the recorded media shall become the property of the police department and shall be retained by it for use in any criminal proceedings.
(e)
The city manager may, at his discretion, require a security camera system in the parking lot of any business licensed for the sale of alcohol and consumption on-premises based upon any history of fighting, disorderly conduct, crime or evidence of criminal acts, loitering, or a demand on public safety resources greater than such demand from other licensed alcohol establishments. The police department will have access to such security cameras at all times. Such security camera equipment will comply with subsection (b)(11)b. of this section, or as determined by the city manager or his designee.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-153. - Additional security for entertainment establishments only.
For those businesses defined as entertainment establishments or any business operating as an entertainment establishment, the city manager may, at his discretion, based upon documented incidents and demand on public safety resources, require any such establishment to provide security or parking lot attendants in its parking lot(s). Such attendants will be required during the hours of operation as determined by the city and in a number determined by the city. Based on the size of the parking lot(s) and their proximity to the business, a minimum of one and a maximum of four may be required.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)

ARTICLE VII. - TAXES

Sec. 6-176. - Distilled spirits.
There is imposed and levied upon each retail distilled spirits dealer a privilege tax for and with respect to each license year, graduating in amount and measured and computed in accordance with the following:
(1)
An initial tax upon each dealer of $1,075.00 payable with the filing of the application for license; however, with respect to dealers who are clubs or organizations making sales to members only and having a charitable purpose, the initial tax shall be $750.00; and with respect to veterans organizations making sales to members only and having a charitable purpose, the initial tax shall be $500.00. If the initial license of the applicant is applied for after the beginning of the license year, the initial tax provided shall be prorated with the remainder of the initial year.
(2)
For a bottle or container of distilled spirits purchased, acquired or received for resale during the license year, the city imposes an excise tax of $0.22 per liter on same.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-177. - Wines.
There is imposed and levied upon each retail wine dealer who is a retailer of wines a privilege tax for and with respect to each license year, graduating in an amount and measured and computed in accordance with the total of the following:
(1)
An initial tax upon each such dealer of $500.00, payable with the filing of the application for license; however, should the application for license be made after the beginning of the license year, the initial tax shall be prorated for the remainder of the initial tax year.
(2)
For a bottle or container of wine purchased, acquired or received for resale during the license year, the city imposes an excise tax of $0.22 per liter on same.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-178. - Malt beverages.
(a)
There is hereby levied and imposed upon each retail beer dealer a privilege tax in an amount equaling the total of the following:
(1)
An initial tax upon each such dealer of $500.00, payable with the filing of the application for a license for the consumption of beer on the premises and an initial tax upon each such dealer of $500.00, payable with the filing of the application for a license for the package beer sales; however, should the application for licenses be made after the beginning of the license year, the initial taxes provided for in this section shall be prorated for the remainder of the initial tax year.
(b)
There is levied an excise tax on all beer sold by wholesalers to retail consumption dealers and retail package dealers as well as by manufacturers directly to consumers in the city at the highest rates allowed by the state, but not less than the following:
(1)
Where beer is sold in or from a barrel or bulk container, $6.00 on each container sold containing not more than 15½ gallons, and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional part of 15½ gallons; and
(2)
Where beer is sold in bottles, cans or other containers, except barrel or bulk containers, a tax of $0.05 per 12 ounces, and a proportionate rate on all fractional parts of 12 ounces.
Beer which contains less than one-half of one percent alcohol by volume shall not be subject to such tax. Such tax shall be paid to the city by the wholesaler selling the beer to any retail consumption dealer or retail package dealer or by the manufacture selling the beer directly to any consumer on or before the 20th day of the following calendar month, along with a report of the wholesaler's gross sales of beer in the county during the reporting period on forms prescribed by the director.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-179. - Consumption licenses fees.
In addition to the initial taxes provided for in sections 6-176, 6-177 and 6-178, retail dealers desiring to obtain a consumption licenses, shall pay an additional privilege tax in the following amounts:
Distilled spirits consumption license .....$2,475.00
Wine consumption license .....$165.00
Malt beverage consumption license .....$165.00
Manufacturer of malt beverage license .....$1,500.00
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-180. - Sunday sales license fees.
(a)
There is imposed and levied upon each retail consumption dealer obtaining a Sunday sales consumption license pursuant to subsection 6-82(a) an additional privilege and license tax in the amount of $450.00, payable with the filing of the application for such license and payable in full on each renewal on each license. License years shall be the same as provided for all retail consumption licenses. Calculation of the amount of the fee pursuant to this section shall be made in accordance with section 6-181.
(b)
There is imposed and levied upon each retail package dealer obtaining a Sunday package sales license pursuant to subsection 6-82(b) an additional privilege and license tax in the amount of $1,000.00, payable with the filing of the application for such license and payable in full on each renewal on each license. License years shall be the same as provided for all retail consumption licenses. Calculation of the amount of the fee pursuant to this section shall be made in accordance with section 6-181.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-181. - Total amount of fees for all licenses.
Notwithstanding the above, the total amount of fees for all licenses allowing the sale, distribution or disbursement of alcoholic beverages in the City of Valdosta shall not exceed an aggregate total of $5,000.00.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-182. - Method of collection.
The taxes imposed by this article shall be collected and paid in the following manner:
(1)
Initial tax. The initial taxes provided for in sections 6-176, 6-177 and 6-178 applicable to any specific license shall be paid by check attached to the application for license as required by section 6-38.
(2)
Retail dealer of distilled spirits and wine. Each retail dealer shall pay the taxes imposed by this article and measured and computed with respect to a container of distilled spirits and wine purchased or received by him after December 31 of each calendar year to the wholesale liquor dealer from whom such container of liquor is purchased or received, and the taxes shall be paid by such wholesaler to the city in the following manner:
a.
Each wholesale dealer who ships or delivers any distilled spirits, or wine by rail, truck or otherwise to a retail dealer licensed under this article, whether the distilled spirits or wine is delivered to the place of business of that retailer or elsewhere, shall, prior to such shipment or delivery, prepare for the city legible copies of the invoices or other records of the shipment or delivery and before the tenth day of the month file with the city such legible copies of all invoices or other records made with respect to such shipments or deliveries made during the periods subsequent to December 31 of each calendar year and up to the time of filing such invoice copies or other records excepting only such information which have been previously filed with the city; and at the time of such filing, the wholesale dealer shall remit to the city the sum of taxes imposed by this article and measured by the containers of liquor or beer shipped or delivered and reflected by such invoices.
b.
All invoices or copies of invoices furnished to the city by wholesalers shall have written thereon the acknowledgement of the retailer for whom delivery has been made, or the acknowledgment of such dealer's agent or employee. Each retailer licensed under this chapter shall furnish to the city monthly statements showing the amounts of total purchases such retailer has made of each wholesaler from whom such retailer has made purchases during the period covered by such statements. Statements shall be due on July 10, and on the tenth day of every succeeding month and shall cover the period of the proceeding calendar month.
(3)
Wholesale malt beverage dealer and manufacturer of malt beverages.
a.
The local excise taxes imposed pursuant section 6-178 is imposed upon and shall be collected by each wholesale malt beverage dealer or manufacturer selling malt beverages within the city. The wholesale malt beverage dealer or manufacturer must file a monthly report on or before the tenth day of each month with the city, indicating sales in the city for the preceding month. the monthly report shall itemize for the preceding calendar month the exact quantities of malt beverages, by size and type of container, sold during the month within the city. The licensee shall file the report with each municipality or county wherein the beverages are sold by the licensee. In the case of manufacturers, qualifying sales include only sales made directly to consumers on the premises of the manufacturer, and not sales made by manufacturers to wholesalers.
b.
The wholesale malt beverage dealer or manufacturer shall remit to the city, along with the monthly report specified in the preceding subsection, the taxes imposed by section 6-178.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-183. - Unlawful possession by retail dealers.
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this article for any retail alcoholic beverage package licensee and after the commencement of business to own, hold, possess or sell any liquor or beer within the corporate limits of the city, with respect to which licensed taxes have not been paid as prescribed and required by this article.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Sec. 6-184. - Unlawful possession by individuals.
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this article for any person other than a wholesale alcoholic beverage licensee to keep or possess any liquor within the corporate limits of the city unless the license tax imposed by this article, measured by each bottle or container of such liquor, has been paid; however, it shall not be a violation of this article to keep or possess not more than eight half pints or four pints, or one case of fifths, quarts or half-gallon containers of liquor within the city with respect to which a similar tax imposed by the county or by any other county or city of the state has been paid.
(Ord. No. 2011-41, 12-8-2011; Ord. No. 2018-22, 9-6-2018)
Secs. 6-185—6-210. - Reserved.
