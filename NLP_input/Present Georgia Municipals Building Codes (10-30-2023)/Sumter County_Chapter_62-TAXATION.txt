ARTICLE III. - SPECIAL COUNTY ONE PERCENT SALES AND USE TAX[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— Special county one percent sales and use tax, O.C.G.A. § 48-8-110 et seq.

Sec. 62-66. - Imposed.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-8-110 et seq., there is hereby authorized to be levied and collected within the county a sales and use tax in the amount of one percent on all sales and uses in the county for a period of time not to exceed five years from the date of adoption of the resolution from which this section derives to raise not more than $10,000,000.00. The proceeds of such tax are to be used to fund the following capital outlay projects:
(1)
The acquisition, construction and equipping of a new county jail;
(2)
The acquisition, construction and equipping of recreational facilities;
(3)
The acquisition, construction and equipping of a transfer station for transporting solid waste to a sanitary landfill; and
(4)
Capitalization of interest and costs with respect to the issuance of general obligation debt in the maximum principal amount of $8,890,000.00 to finance the projects enumerated in subsections (1)—(3) of this section.
(Res. of 6-14-94)

ARTICLE IV. - DEPOSITORY FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS BUSINESS LICENSE TAX[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— Local depository financial institutions business license tax, O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.

Sec. 62-101. - Levied.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-6-90 et seq., and more particularly, O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93, all depository financial institutions with an office located within Sumter County shall pay an annual business license tax at a rate of 0.25 percent of the depository financial institution's Georgia gross receipts. The terms "gross receipts" and "depository financial institutions" shall be defined in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-6-90 et seq.
(Res. of 10-11-83, § 1; Res. of 2-15-84; Ord. of 6-9-15(1))
Sec. 62-102. - Minimum business license tax.
The minimum annual amount of business license tax due from any depository financial institution pursuant to section 62-101 shall be $1,000.00.
(Res. of 10-11-83, § 2)
Sec. 62-103. - Filing of return.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(c), each depository financial institution subject to this tax shall file a return of its gross receipts with the board of commissioners on March 1 of the year following the year in which such gross receipts are measured. This return shall be in the manner and in the form prescribed by the commissioner of the department of revenue based on the allocation method set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(d). The board of commissioners shall assess and collect the tax levied pursuant to this section based upon the information provided in the return.
(Res. of 10-11-83, § 3)
Sec. 62-104. - Due date of taxes.
Taxes levied pursuant to this article shall be due no later than 30 days after filing of the return prescribed by section 62-103 unless extended by the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 10-11-83, § 4)
Secs. 62-105—62-135. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - GROSS PREMIUMS TAX ON INSURERS[5]

Footnotes:
--- (5) ---
State Law reference— Local tax on life insurance companies, O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.1; local tax on other than life insurance companies, O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2; general assembly authorized to provide for taxation of insurance companies based on gross direct premiums received from insurance policies within the unincorporated areas of counties, Ga. Sec. 62-136. - Gross premiums tax on insurance other than life insurance.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2, there is hereby levied an annual tax upon each insurer doing insurance business within the unincorporated area of the county, in an amount equal to 2.5 percent of the gross direct premiums received during the calendar year on all insurance, other than life insurance, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5(2)—(5). The term "gross direct premiums" shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2(a).
(Res. of 8-9-83, § 1)

ARTICLE VI. - AD VALOREM TAXES

Sec. 62-170. - Judicial in rem tax foreclosures for delinquent ad valorem taxes.
(a)
Sumter County shall be authorized to proceed with judicial in rem tax foreclosures for delinquent taxes in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-4-5.
(b)
The right to proceed with judicial in rem tax foreclosures shall be in addition to any other rights and remedies provided under state or local law for the enforcement of tax liens, and nothing in this section shall prohibit the collection of delinquent ad valorem taxes in any other manner as allowed by state or local law.
(c)
Proceedings in accordance with this section are designed solely to enforce a lien for ad valorem taxes against the property subject to such taxation and shall not constitute an action for personal liability for such taxes of the owner or owners of such property.
(Ord. of 2-17-15)
