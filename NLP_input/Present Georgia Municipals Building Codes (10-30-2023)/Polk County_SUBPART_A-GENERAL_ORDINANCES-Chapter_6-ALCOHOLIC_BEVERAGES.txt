Sec. 6-19. - Definitions.
Unless the context or the specific provisions of this article demand otherwise, the words and terms used in this article shall have the same definition and meaning as set forth in this section and in O.C.G.A. title 3 (O.C.G.A. § 3-1-1 et seq.).
Alcohol or alcoholic beverage means malt beverage and/or wine.
Applicant means the actual owner of a business establishment for which an alcoholic beverage license is requested.
Authorized catered function means a scheduled special social event, contest, reception, party, or similar activity for which a food caterer or event venue has prepared substantial food items to be served with appropriate serving facilities and personnel.
Building inspector means the Building Inspector for Polk County.
Business location means the location of a retail or wholesale outlet.
Central loudspeaker. A long throw speaker horn, permanently fixed in location to a structure, such as a pole, fence, or building.
Drink means any bottle, can, or glass of malt beverage and/or wine served for consumption on the premises, which may or may not be diluted by any other non-alcoholic liquid.
Event venue means a site, place, or location satisfying all of the requirements of this article, upon which authorized catered functions are hosted. In addition, an event venue shall be equipped with a sanitary kitchen suitable for catered food or if food is prepared on premises, a sanitary kitchen approved for food service by the county health department. Section 6-41 shall not apply to event venues. Event venues shall also comply with section 6-28(c) prohibited locations, of this Code.
Farm winery. A domestic winery located on premises that is licensed as a farm winery by the state, and which makes at least 40 percent of its annual production from agricultural produce grown in the state where the winery is located and; is located on the premises, a substantial portion of which is used for agricultural purposes, including the cultivation of grapes, berries. Or fruits to be utilized in the manufacture or production of wine by the winery; or is owned and operated by persons who are engaged in the production of a substantial portion of the agricultural produce and used in its annual production.
Farm winery tasting room. An outlet for the promotion of a farm winery's wine by providing samples of such wine to the public and for the sale of such wine produced on premises or from other farm wineries at retail for consumption on the premises and for sale in closed packages for consumption off the premises. Samples of wine can be given free of charge or for a fee.
Food caterer means an individual or business possessing a valid food service permit issued by a Georgia County Health Department and any other license or certificate as may be required, that prepares food for consumption off the premises.
Full service kitchen. A kitchen designed, intended and equipped to produce meals for sale to the general public as a major function of the business.
Immediate family means spouse, mother, father, brother or sister.
License means the authorization by the board of commissioners to engage in the sale or consumption on the premises of alcoholic beverages, and for the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption off of the premises.
Licensed alcoholic beverage caterer means any person, firm, or corporation who has been licensed pursuant to the provisions of this article and O.C.G.A. § 3-11-2 to sell alcoholic beverages by the bottle, glass, or other open container for consumption on the premises of an event venue or special event venue.
Licensee means the person to whom an alcoholic beverage license has been issued, or in the case of a partnership, corporation or other legal entity, all partners, officers, and directors of such entity.
Loudspeaker. A portable or non-portable device which converts electrical signals into sound as a part of a public address or sound system capable of producing or reproducing sound, such as music or a person's voice, from an amplified or combined sound source and is projected out to an audience.
Malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than 14 percent alcohol by volume and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer, and strong beer. The term does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine. O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2.
Outdoor. Not within the confines of an enclosed building that substantially blocks the transmittal of sound.
Outlet means a store or business where malt beverages and/or wine are sold.
Outlet manager means that person who does or will actually manage or operate the outlet on a day-to-day basis.
Package means a bottle, can, keg, barrel, or other original consumer container.
Portable. Able to be easily transported, carried, and arranged by a person of average physical capabilities.
Purchase price means the consideration received for the sale of alcoholic beverages by the drink, valued in money, whether received in cash or otherwise, including all receipts, cash credits, and property of services of any kind or nature, and also the amount for which credit is allowed by the licensee to the purchaser, without any deduction therefrom whatsoever.
Purchaser means any person who orders and gives present or future consideration for any alcoholic beverage by the package or drink.
Qualified business owner means the actual owner of a business establishment who holds an occupational certificate issued by the county.
Restaurant means any public place kept, used, maintained, advertised, and held out to the public as a place where meals are served and where meals are actually regularly prepared and served, and without sleeping accommodations, and holds a valid food service permit from the county health department, and has seating capacity for at least 24 people, and has adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining facilities, and serves at least one full course meal for not less than three days a week for not less than three months a calendar year, and serving of meals is the principal business conducted, and the serving of malt beverages to be consumed on premises as only incidental thereto, and malt beverages shall only be served during such times full course meals are actually being served.
Speaker. Any electric powered device designed for the amplification of sound.
Special event means an event conducted by or for the benefit of a certified nonprofit or civic organization at an approved venue.
Special event organizer(s) means any person, firm, or corporation who is responsible for promoting and/or conducting a special event.
Special event venue means a site, place, or location satisfying all of the requirements of this article upon which a special event may be hosted. Section 6-41 shall not apply to special event venues. Special event venues shall also comply with section 6-28(c), prohibited locations, of this Code.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products. The term does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this Code section.
(Res. of 1-16-1966, § 1; Res. of 6-14-2004, § 1; Ord. of 4-5-11; Res. of 8-6-2013; Ord. No. 2016-03, § 1; Ord. No. 2017-04, § 1, 10-9-2017; Ord. No. 2017-04, § 1, 10-9-2017; Res. No. 2019-08A, § 1, 4-9-2019; Res. No. 2019-16, § 1, 8-6-2019)
Sec. 6-20. - Necessity for license; unlawful activity.
It shall be unlawful to manufacture, sell, offer to sell, or possess for the purpose of sale, at wholesale or retail, within the unincorporated areas of the county, any malt beverages or wine, without having the appropriate license for such manufacture or sale, or to carry on such activity in violation of the terms of such license or of the resolution from which this article is derived.
(Res. of 1-16-1966, § 2; Res. No. 2019-08A, § 1, 4-9-2019)

Sec. 6-21. - Classification of licenses.
The following types or classes of licenses will be available within this county:
(1)
Retail package outlet. The retail package outlet license shall permit only the sale at retail of malt beverages and/or wine in packages. On-premises consumption shall be prohibited.
(2)
Pouring outlet. The pouring outlet license shall permit the sale of malt beverages and/or wine only by the bottle, glass, or other open container for consumption inside a restaurant as defined in section 6-19, Definitions, and section 6-41, Supplemental requirements.
(3)
Wholesale distributor. The wholesale distributor license shall permit only the sale of malt beverages and/or wine at wholesale.
(4)
Alcoholic beverage caterer. The alcoholic beverage caterer license shall permit the sale or distribution of malt beverages and/or wine by the bottle, glass or other open container for consumption on the premises of an event venue hosting authorized catered functions. This license is not transferable.
(5)
Special event permit. The special event permit shall permit the sale or distribution of malt beverages and/or wine by the bottle, glass, or other open container for consumption, out of the view of any public right-of-way or adjacent properties, on the premises of an approved special event venue. This permit is not transferable.
(6)
Farm winery. The farm winery license shall be limited to the operation of a farm winery licensed by the state to manufacture wine in Georgia under the state farm winery laws. See section 6-47.
(7)
Farm winery tasting room. The farm winery tasting room license shall be limited to an on premises farm winery tasting room licensed by the State of Georgia in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 3-6-21.1, et seq., and the licensee shall be permitted to perform only acts allowed in accord with such statutes. See section 6-47.
(Res. of 1-16-1966, § 3; Ord. of 3-8-11; Res. of 8-6-2013; Res. No. 2019-08A, § 1, 4-9-2019; Res. No. 2019-16, § 1, 8-6-2019)
Sec. 6-22. - Separate license for each malt beverage and/or wine outlet; terms of transfer.
(a)
A separate license shall be required for each malt beverage and/or wine outlet and a separate application shall be made for each such outlet and business location.
(b)
Transfer of licenses between business locations is prohibited.
(c)
The transfer of malt beverage and/or wine license from one individual or entity to another shall be prohibited.
(d)
Malt beverage and/or wine licenses shall only be issued to qualified business owners.
(Res. of 1-16-1966, § 4; Res. of 8-6-2013; Res. No. 2019-08A, § 1, 4-9-2019)
Sec. 6-23. - Initial application for license.
(a)
Form and contents. Each initial application for a malt beverage and/or wine license shall be made under oath, in writing, on forms supplied by the county manager or designee, and shall contain the following information:
(1)
The type of license for which application is made.
(2)
The full name, age, home address, business address, name of spouse and minor children, and complete disclosure of all convictions and pleas of guilty and nolo contendere for violations of city, state and federal laws of each applicant and spouse.
(3)
The full name, age, home address, business address, name of spouse and all minor children, and complete disclosure of all convictions and pleas of guilty and nolo contendere for violations of city, state and federal laws of the proposed outlet manager.
(4)
Exact location of the proposed outlet.
(5)
Name and address of owner, landlord and resident manager of location for proposed outlet.
(6)
Whether or not the building for the proposed outlet has been erected as the proposed location.
(7)
Trade name or proposed trade name of business.
(8)
Names and addresses of all persons having any financial interest whatsoever in the proposed outlet including but not limited to ownership of building or property, ownership of stock in business or receipt of income from business.
(9)
Whether or not the applicant has ever held a license for the sale of malt beverages and/or wine and, if so, the dates held, the authority issuing such license, the current status of such license, whether or not such license was revoked, and the reasons for any such revocation.
(10)
Such other information as may be required by the board of commissioners.
(11)
A copy of the applicant's application or proposed application for license issued by the state shall be attached to the application form.
(b)
Fee. Each initial application shall be accompanied by a nonrefundable application fee in the amount established by the resolution for investigation of the applicant and proposed business location and for processing of the application. See fees as referenced in Subpart A, Chapter 1, Section 1-17 - Fee Schedule.
(c)
Fingerprints. Each initial application shall be accompanied by a complete set of fingerprints of the applicant and/or the proposed outlet manager to be forwarded to the Georgia Bureau of Investigation for search and investigation as provided in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2.
(d)
Proof of advertisement. Proof of compliance with the advertisement requirements as set forth in section 6-25 shall be filed by the applicant prior to consideration of the application.
(Res. of 1-16-1966, § 5; Res. of 6-14-2004(1), § 5; Res. of 6-14-2004(2), § 2; Ord. No. 2019-04, § 1, 2-5-2019; Res. No. 2019-08A, § 1, 4-9-2019)
Sec. 6-24. - Waiting list.
Any application for a retail package store or pouring outlet license filed at a time when no such licenses are available shall be placed on a waiting list and shall be considered in its proper order at such time as a vacancy occurs. For an application to be placed on the waiting list, the applicant must comply with all requirements for initial application except proof of advertisement. Upon notification by the county manager or designee that a vacancy exists, the applicant shall comply with the advertisement requirements set forth in section 6-25 and file proof of such compliance within 30 days following such notification. If no proof of advertisement is filed within the 30-day period, the application shall be removed from the waiting list and the applicant shall be ineligible to reapply for a period of one year following such removal.
(Res. of 1-16-1966, § 6)
Sec. 6-25. - Proof of advertisement.
(a)
Publication of notice. Upon filing an application for a malt beverage license, the applicant shall publish a notice of his application in The Cedartown Standard and The Rockmart Journal once a week for two weeks. Such notice shall contain the name of the applicant, the type of license for which application is made, the proposed business location, and the name of the proposed outlet manager.
(b)
Posting of notice. Within three days after filing an application for a malt beverage license, the applicant shall post a sign on the proposed business location. The sign shall be not less than 24 inches in height and 36 inches in width, with letters of at least 2½ inches in height and shall be captioned, "malt beverage license application pending." It shall designate the name and address of the applicant, the type of license, and the date of application and shall contain no other advertisement. The sign shall be posted on the front door of the building to be used if said door faces the right-of-way of the street upon which the building fronts, otherwise, the sign shall be posted facing the street and within three feet of said right-of-way. If a building is to be constructed on the location, the sign shall be posted facing the street and within three feet of the right-of-way of the street upon which the building will front.
(c)
Cost. Cost of advertisement as provided herein shall be borne by the applicant.
(d)
Proof of advertisement. The applicant shall file the following with the county manager or designee within 30 days following the filing of the initial application:
(1)
A copy of the newspaper advertisements, together with the publisher's affidavits stating the dates of publication.
(2)
A photograph of the sign posted on the proposed business location together with an affidavit stating that the sign was erected as required herein.
(Res. of 1-16-1966, § 7)
Sec. 6-26. - Procedure for grant or denial of license; subsequent applicant.
(a)
Within seven days after the filing of the proof of advertisement, the county manager or designee shall refer the application to the chief of the county police department for investigation. The board of commissioners shall consider the application, the investigation, the report of the chief of the county police department and any objections filed.
(b)
Upon written request of the applicant, the board of commissioners shall conduct a public hearing prior to making a decision. No decision shall be made except at a regular meeting of the board of commissioners. Should the application be granted, a written notice shall be given to the applicant and a license shall be issued upon payment of the appropriate licensing fee as referenced in Subpart A, Chapter 1, Section 1-17 - Fee Schedule. Should the application be denied, written notice setting forth the reasons for denial shall be given to the applicant.
(c)
In the event an application is denied, the applicant shall be ineligible to reapply for a period of one year from the date of denial.
(Res. of 1-16-1966, § 8; Ord. No. 2019-04, § 1, 2-5-2019)
Sec. 6-27. - Guidelines for the consideration of applications for malt beverage and/or wine licenses.
The following factors shall be considered in determining whether or not to grant a malt beverage and/or wine license:
(1)
The proximity of other licensed malt beverage and/or wine sales outlets to the proposed business location;
(2)
The nature of the neighborhood immediately adjacent to the proposed business location; that is, whether the same is predominantly residential, commercial or industrial;
(3)
The proximity of churches, hospitals, schools, college campuses, public parks, playgrounds and private residences;
(4)
The availability of adequate off street parking facilities for patrons;
(5)
The possibility of increased traffic congestion and traffic hazards;
(6)
The criminal record, financial responsibility, character and reputation of the applicant, owners or stockholders of the applicant, and proposed outlet manager;
(7)
The compliance with all application and advertisement requirements;
(8)
The need for additional business facilities selling malt beverages within the county;
(9)
The revocation for cause of any previous license for the sale of malt beverages and/or wine issued to the applicant or proposed outlet manager; or for the proposed business location;
(10)
The history or reputation of the proposed business location for prostitution or other sex offenses, fighting, shooting, stabbing or violence, gambling, illegal drug dealing in alcoholic beverages or drugs, or other violations of the law.
(Res. of 1-16-1966, § 9; Res. No. 2019-08A, § 1, 4-9-2019)
Sec. 6-28. - Grounds for denial of license.
(a)
Denial of license. An application for a malt beverage and/or wine license may be denied if it is determined based upon the factors set forth in section 6-27 that the granting of such license would be detrimental to the public safety, health or welfare of the residents of the county.
(b)
Prohibited persons. An application for a malt beverage and/or wine license shall be denied to the following:
(1)
An individual who is an illegal alien;
(2)
A corporation which is not duly qualified to do business in the state or whose proposed manager is a prohibited person under the terms of this section;
(3)
A person who has been convicted of a felony, of a crime opposed to decency and morality or of a crime involving the violation of any county, state or federal laws or regulations pertaining to the manufacture, possession, transportation or sale of malt beverages, wine or intoxicating liquors and served any sentence imposed following such a conviction within a period of five years prior to the date of application;
(4)
A person whose license has been revoked for cause or whose license for the sale of malt beverages and/or wine issued by any other licensing authority has been revoked within a period of five years prior to the date of application;
(5)
An individual who is under the age of 21 or who is not of sound mind and memory;
(6)
An officer or employee of the county, or members of their immediate families; provided that the subsequent election, appointment, or employment shall not result in the revocation or prevent the renewal of a malt beverage and/or wine license previously granted.
(c)
Prohibited locations.
(1)
No original retail or wholesale malt beverage and/or wine license, including consumption on premises or retail package license, shall be issued under this article for any location which is within 500 feet of a church, public or private school or college campus, or a private residence on the same street unless such residence is itself in a commercial district.
(2)
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Church building means the main structure used by any religious organization for purposes of worship.
Private residence means a house, dwelling, condominium or duplex wherein not less than one nor more than two families reside and shall not include a mobile home park or court, an apartment house having facilities for housing more than two families, nor a boardinghouse or roominghouse where there are five or more boarders or roomers. Any building occupied as a residence located within an area zoned for business shall not be construed as a "private residence."
School building or educational building means and shall apply only to state, county, city, or church school buildings and to such buildings at such other schools in which are taught subjects commonly taught in the common schools and colleges of this state and which are public schools and private schools as defined in O.C.G.A. § 20-2-690(b).
(3)
For purposes of this section, distance shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground and shall be measured in the following manner:
a.
From the main entrance of the establishment from which malt beverages are sold or offered for sale;
b.
In a straight line to the nearest public sidewalk, street, road or highway right-of-way by the nearest route;
c.
Along such public sidewalk, street, road or highway right-of-way by the nearest route;
d.
To the nearest property line of a church building, private residence, or school grounds;
e.
To a point at the front door of the main structure of the church building, school, or residential building, other than the dwelling of the licensee.
(4)
No location which is licensed to sell alcoholic beverages on the effective date of the ordinance from which this section is derived shall be denied continued operation under an existing license, or denied any renewal of such license, nor shall any new owner of the location be denied a new license based upon the measurements set forth in this section.
(5)
As to any location licensed in the future, if the distance requirements in this section are met at the time of issuance of any license, the subsequent opening and operation of a church or school, or construction of a private residence within the distance prohibited in this section shall not prevent the continuance of an existing license or the renewal thereof or the issuance of a new license to any subsequent owner of such property. Provided, however, that the distance requirements shall not apply at any location for which a new license is applied if the sale of alcoholic beverages was lawful at such location at any time during the 12 months immediately preceding such application.
(6)
Where a license is issued to a grocery or convenience store, where the sale of malt beverages and/or wine is incidental to the sale of groceries and other like items, such store shall be exempt from the provisions relating to private residences.

Sec. 6-29. - Renewal of license; denial of renewal application; hearing.
(a)
All malt beverage and/or wine licenses shall be issued on a calendar year basis. Each such license holder shall make written application for renewal of such license on or before December 20 of each year on forms provided by the county manager or designee.
(b)
All applications for renewal shall be referred to the chief of the county police who shall file a report on the activity of the licensee and the activity on the licensed premises.
(c)
Upon receipt of the report of the chief of the county police, the board of commissioners shall either grant or deny the renewal application. In the event the renewal application is approved, written notice thereof shall be given to the applicant and a license shall be issued upon payment of the appropriate license fee. See fees as referenced in Subpart A, Chapter 1, Section 1-17 - Fee Schedule.
(d)
In passing upon a renewal application, the board of commissioners shall be guided by the provisions of sections 6-27 and 6-28.
(Res. of 1-16-1966, § 11; Ord. No. 2019-04, § 1, 2-5-2019; Res. No. 2019-08A, § 1, 4-9-2019)

Sec. 6-33. - Suspension and revocation of licenses; grounds; notice of proposed action; hearing.
(a)
The board of commissioners may suspend, revoke, or place on probation any license issued under this article upon the following grounds:
(1)
The violation by the license holder or any employee at the licensed business location of any provision of this article, of any laws or ordinances regulating the sale and taxation of malt beverages and/or wine, of any regulations promulgated pursuant to such laws or ordinances, or of any state, federal or local law or ordinance relating to the use and sale of drugs or alcoholic beverages.
(2)
The making of a false statement on the license application.
(3)
The license holder or business manager becoming a prohibited person as set forth in section 6-28.
(4)
Discontinuance of or closure of an outlet or business location for a continuous period of time in excess of 15 days.
(5)
Failure of the license holder to file timely reports, to remit proper tax, or to maintain records as required by sections 6-35 and 6-36.
(6)
The continued operation of the licensed business is detrimental to the health, safety or welfare of the citizens of the county.
(b)
Should the county manager or designee determine that probable cause exists for the suspension, revocation or placement on probation of a malt beverage and/or wine license, he shall schedule a hearing thereon before the board of commissioners and cause the license holder to be served personally or by registered mail or by certified mail, return receipt requested, with notice of the time and place of hearing on the proposed action, together with a statement of the reasons therefor. Such notice shall be served at least ten days prior to the hearing. The license holder shall be allowed the opportunity to present evidence and to cross-examine opposing witnesses at the hearing. Should either party desire the hearing to be recorded, then the costs therefor shall be borne equally between the parties. The party desiring the hearing to be recorded shall secure the services of a certified court reporter and failure to do so shall not delay the hearing. After such hearing, the board of commissioners may suspend, revoke or place on probation, any malt beverage and/or wine license. Its decision shall be made in writing, setting forth the reasons for such decision and shall be served upon the license holder, personally or by registered mail or by certified mail. Upon the suspension or revocation of a malt beverage and/or wine license, the chief of the county police or an officer designated by him shall immediately remove the malt beverage license from the licensed premises and deliver it to the county manager or designee.
(Res. of 1-16-1966, § 15; Res. No. 2019-08A, § 1, 4-9-2019)

Sec. 6-35. - Taxation rate.
In addition to any license fee imposed and required, there is hereby levied and imposed upon each wholesale dealer selling, shipping, delivering or otherwise disposing of malt beverages and/or wine within this county, whether or not such wholesale dealer is licensed as a wholesale distributor in this county, an excise tax on malt beverages and/or wine sold, shipped, delivered, or otherwise disposed of within this county, as follows:
(1)
Where malt beverages, commonly known as tap or draft beer, are sold in or from a barrel or bulk container, a tax of $6.00 on each container sold containing not more than 15½ gallons and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 15½ gallons.
(2)
Where malt beverages are sold in bottles, cans, or other containers, except barrel or bulk containers, a tax of $.05 per 12 ounces and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 12 ounces.
(3)
Where wine is sold in bottles or other containers, a tax of $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter. Tax shall only be imposed within the unincorporated areas of the County. Reference O.C.G.A §3-6-60.
(Res. of 1-16-1966, § 17; Res. No. 2019-08A, § 1, 4-9-2019)
Sec. 6-36. - Records; availability for inspection.
(a)
An invoice showing the quantity of malt beverages and/or wine, by size, type of container and brand name, shall be prepared in triplicate for each delivery of malt beverages and/or wine to a retail package store or pouring outlet in this county by a wholesale distributor, whether such distributor is licensed in this county or another county of the state. The original invoice shall be retained by the retail package store or pouring outlet licensee and a copy retained by the wholesale distributor for a period of at least three years. Such invoices and all records maintained pursuant to state law shall be available for inspection at any time by officials authorized by the board of commissioners of the county, acting by and through the county manager or designee.
(b)
The holder of a license for malt beverages and/or wine for consumption on premises shall produce satisfactory evidence to the county manager on a monthly basis that the business meets the percentage of food requirement as required herein. Failure to produce such evidence shall be cause for denial or revocation of the license.
(Res. of 1-16-1966, § 19; ; Res. of 6-14-2004(1), § 19; Res. of 6-14-2004(2), § 7; Res. No. 2019-08A, § 1, 4-9-2019)
Sec. 6-37. - Right of audit.
The county shall have the right to audit, and to require production of records from each wholesale distributor supplying retailers in this county and each retail package store and pouring outlet licensed in the county.
(Res. of 1-16-1966, § 20)
Sec. 6-38. - Storage of inventory.
Retail package store and pouring outlet licensees shall store all malt beverages and/or wine in their possession only on the premises for which the license was issued.
(Res. of 1-16-1966, §§ 21—23; Res. No. 2019-08A, § 1, 4-9-2019)
Sec. 6-39. - Employee identification cards.
(a)
Condition precedent to employment. No licensee shall permit any person to work or assist in a retail package store, pouring outlet, or alcoholic beverage caterer until such person has procured an alcoholic beverage employee identification card as provided in this section.
(b)
Application. Any person who intends to work for a retail package store, pouring outlet, or alcoholic beverage caterer shall apply to the county police department for an alcoholic beverage employee identification card on such forms as approved by the board of commissioners. Such application shall contain the following information:
(1)
Full name, address, and age.
(2)
Driver's license number.
(3)
Social Security number.
(4)
A complete disclosure of any convictions or pleas of guilt or nolo contendere for violations of any state or federal law or city or county ordinance.
(5)
Such other information as the board of commissioners deems necessary for investigation of the applicant.
(c)
Fingerprints and photographing. Applicants for alcoholic beverage employee identification cards shall be photographed and fingerprinted by the county police department as a part of their application. Applicants shall make themselves available for such photographing and fingerprinting as requested by the county police department.
(d)
Fee. Each application shall be accompanied by a nonrefundable application fee as referenced in Subpart A, Chapter 1, Section 1-17 - Fee Schedule.
(e)
Procedure for approval. The county police department shall investigate the applicant and shall file its report with the board of commissioners. The board of commissioners may either approve or deny the application for an alcoholic beverage employee identification card. The board of commissioners may deny the application if the record indicates that approval of the application would adversely affect the public health, safety or welfare and shall deny the application if the applicant has made a material false statement on his application or is a prohibited person as set forth in section 6-28.
(f)
Temporary identification card. The county police may issue a temporary identification card pending final decision by the board of commissioners. In the event the application is denied, the temporary identification card shall be automatically revoked and shall be surrendered upon demand by the county police department.
(1)
Display of identification card on licensed premises. All alcoholic beverage employee identification cards and temporary identification cards shall be displayed prominently at all times on the licensed premises where the holder is employed.
(2)
Revocation of identification card. Grounds for revocation of an alcoholic beverage employee identification card and the procedure for revocation of such identification cards shall be the same as that set forth in section 6-33 for revocation of alcoholic beverages licenses.
(3)
Surrender of identification card. A holder of an alcoholic beverage employee identification card shall surrender it to the county police department immediately upon discontinuance of his employment at a licensed retail package store or pouring outlet and upon demand after the same has been revoked as provided in this article. It shall be unlawful for a holder to refuse to so surrender or to alter, conceal, deface or destroy such card.
(Res. of 1-16-1966, § 25; Res. of 8-6-2013; Ord. No. 2019-04, § 1, 2-5-2019; Res. No. 2019-08A, § 1, 4-9-2019)
Sec. 6-40. - Regulations.
(a)
No holder of a retail package store, pouring outlet, or alcoholic beverage caterer license, nor any agent or employee of said license holder, shall do any of the following upon the outlet premises:
(1)
Knowingly sell malt beverages and/or wine to any underage person as provided in (O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23);
(2)
Knowingly sell malt beverages and/or wine to any person who is in a state of noticeable intoxication;
(3)
Sell malt beverages and/or wine or permit the consumption of malt beverages and/or wine on any day or at any time when such sale or consumption is prohibited;
(4)
Allow any disorderly conduct, breach of the peace, or any lewd, immoral or illegal conduct, entertainment or practices on the licensed premises;
(5)
Allow "curb service" or the consumption of malt beverages and/or wine on any parking lot adjacent to the premises.
(b)
Malt beverages and/or wine shall be received at and delivered to the premises of a retail package store or pouring outlet licensee only by conveyance owned and operated by a wholesale distributor licensed in the county, unless said wholesale distributor is licensed in another county of this state and only delivers and receives payment in the county for malt beverages and/or wine previously ordered through a representative of said wholesale distributor.
(c)
Each holder of a license issued pursuant to this article shall display the license prominently at all times on the premises for which the license is issued.
(d)
Every retail package store and pouring outlet shall post in a conspicuous place on the premises a sign in letters at least two inches high, reading, "Sale of malt beverages and/or wine to minors strictly prohibited."
(e)
No screen, partition or other object which prevents a clear view into the interior of a retail package store from the street shall be permitted.
(f)
Regulations of the state department of revenue relating to the sale and distribution of malt beverages and/or wine are hereby incorporated into and made a part of this article as if fully set out herein.
(Res. of 1-16-1966, § 26; Res. of 8-6-2013; Res. No. 2019-08A, § 1, 4-9-2019)
Sec. 6-41. - Supplemental requirements for sale of malt beverages and/or wine for on-premises consumption.
The area (in square feet) of a bar or lounge facility in a restaurant shall not be greater than 25 percent of the square footage of the dining area of the establishment where tables are located, excluding kitchen area, hall, restrooms, waiting area, and areas where games or other activities are located.
(Res. of 6-14-2004(2), § 7; Res. No. 2019-08A, § 1, 4-9-2019)
Sec. 6-42. - Hours of operation.
(a)
Retail package store, pouring outlets, and alcoholic beverage caterer. Malt beverages and/or wine may be sold at retail package stores, pouring outlets, and alcoholic beverage caterers only between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 2:00 a.m., Monday through Friday, between the hours of 6:00a.m. and 12:00 midnight on Saturday. Malt beverages only, may be sold between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m. on Sunday. No malt beverages and/or wine shall be sold on Christmas Day or any other day prohibited by state law.
(b)
Limitation on pouring outlets. All pouring outlets shall be closed for business of any kind between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 8:00 a.m., Monday through Saturday, between the hours of 12:00 midnight Saturday and 12:30 p.m. Sunday, and between 11:30 p.m. Sunday and 8:00 a.m. Monday.
(c)
Wholesale distributors. Wholesale distributors, whether licensed in this county or any other county in this state, may distribute malt beverages and/or wine within this county only between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m., Monday through Saturday.
(d)
Farm winery tasting rooms. Wine may be sold at farm winery tasting rooms only between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 12:00 midnight, Monday through Saturday.
Sunday Sales shall be as set forth in O.C.G.A. 3-6-21.2
No wine shall be sold on Christmas Day or any other day prohibited by state law.
(Res. of 1-16-1966, § 27; Res. of 6-14-2004(1), § 28; Ord. of 12-4-2012(2); Res. of 8-6-2013; Res. No. 2019-08A, § 1, 4-9-2019; Res. No. 2019-16, § 1, 8-6-2019)
Sec. 6-43. - Advertising; signs.
There shall be no more than three signs advertising the sale of malt beverages and/or wine on the premises of a licensee. Such signs shall be no more than eight inches in height and 24 inches in length.
(Res. of 1-16-1966, § 28; Res. of 6-14-2004(1), § 29; Res. No. 2019-08A, § 1, 4-9-2019)
Sec. 6-44. - Limitation of number of licenses.
Notwithstanding any other provision of this article, the total number of licenses issued in the county for the retail sale of malt beverages and/or wine, either retail package store or pouring outlet licenses, shall not exceed one license per 1,000 population according to the latest national census.
(Res. of 1-16-1966, § 29; Res. of 6-14-2004(1), § 30; Res. No. 2019-08A, § 1, 4-9-2019)
Sec. 6-45. - Licensed alcoholic beverage caterer.
(a)
Alcoholic beverage caterers may only operate within the unincorporated portions of Polk County, under an alcoholic beverage caterers license granted by the county, based upon the terms and conditions provided in this article. Malt beverages and/or wine shall not be distributed, sold, or possessed for the purposed of sale or distribution by an alcoholic beverage caterer without a license granted by the county. All licenses issued hereunder shall be subject to all regulations, ordinances, and requirements contained in this chapter regarding alcoholic beverages generally. This license is not transferrable.
(b)
Eligibility for license. Any person, firm, or corporation applying for an alcoholic beverage caterer license shall be subject to the same licensing requirements applicable to an establishment licensed for consumption on the premises. Application shall be made on forms provided by the county clerk and must be accompanied by a certified check for the appropriate license fee as referenced in Subpart A, Chapter 1, Section 1-17 - Fee Schedule.
(c)
Duration and renewal of license. Any alcoholic beverage caterer's license issued hereunder shall automatically expire on December 31 of the year of its issuance. Each licensee shall make a written application for renewal on or before December 20 of each year.
(Res. of 8-6-2013; Ord. No. 2019-04, § 1, 2-5-2019; Res. No. 2019-08A, § 1, 4-9-2019)
Sec. 6-46. - Special event permit.
(a)
A special event permit shall only be issued to special event organizers. The permit shall be valid for one day of a special event conducted by or for the benefit of certified nonprofit or civic organizations. Event organizers must specify, at the time of application, a properly licensed person, firm, or corporation that will be selling or otherwise distributing malt beverages and/or wine for an event. Only persons, firms, or corporations properly licensed by the state department of revenue for the sale of malt beverages and/or wine for consumption on the premises shall be permitted to provide malt beverages and/or wine at a special event. Event organizers shall be limited to six days of special events in any calendar year for which a special event permit is required. Special event organizers shall make application for the permit on forms provided by the county clerk and must be accompanied by a certified check for the appropriate permit fee as referenced in Subpart A, Chapter 1, Section 1-17. - Fee Schedule. Special event permits shall not be issued for special events taking place on public property. This permit is not transferrable.
(b)
Following each special event, the event organizer(s) shall provide to the county clerk, documented proof of the actual amount of proceeds from the event that were distributed to certified nonprofit or civic organizations. Failure to provide the required documentation shall be grounds for denying future permit applications.
(c)
Revocation of permit. The chief of the county police department, or his designee, may immediately revoke any special event permit if continued alcohol sales may endanger the health, welfare, or safety of the public. There will be no refunds for revoked permit.
(d)
Indemnification. As a condition precedent to the issuance of a special event permit, event organizers shall indemnify, in writing, and hold the county and all its departments, agencies, officials, officers, and employees forever harmless from claims, demands, or cause of actions that may arise from activities associated with any special event.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for any person, entity, or organization to sell, dispense, or distribute alcoholic beverages at any special event, except in accordance with this article.
(Res. of 8-6-2013; Ord. No. 2019-04, § 1, 2-5-2019; Res. No. 2019-08A, § 1, 4-9-2019)
Sec. 6-47. - Farm winery.
No entity shall engage in the business of a farm winery of any kind in the unincorporated area of the county without first being qualified and properly licensed by the state and the Polk County Board of Commissioners. Farm wineries shall be allowed to manufacture wine and to distribute such wine, as specified by state law. Farm wineries may only be located in unincorporated areas of the county zoned A-1 as specified in Section 700.17 A-1 Agriculture District.
Farm wineries may obtain a license for the sale of wine produced on premises or the wine of other farm wineries, at retail for consumption on the premises or in closed packages for consumption off the premises at its properly licensed tasting room.
The farm winery tasting room license created by this section is limited to farm wineries licensed by the state and allows the licensee to deal in its farm winery products pursuant to state law. No license is created by this section authorizing any other person to deal in any other alcoholic beverage.
Food service shall only be permitted in the tasting room in conjunction with, and as a compliment to, wine tasting, and shall be limited to pre-prepared and/or pre-packaged finger foods, appetizers, cheese trays, and charcuterie boards, commonly served as an accompaniment to wine service. No ovens, fryers, grills, burners, or other commercial kitchen equipment shall be utilized in the preparation of such food.
Hosting of special events at the winery shall be governed by this section, rather than by section 6-46 of this chapter. Special events may be held at the winery, including but not limited to, personal celebrations such as weddings, birthday parties, corporate parties, fundraisers, and similar events, provided that the following additional requirements are met:
(1)
The special event shall be limited to a maximum of 200 guests.
(2)
The special event is limited to specific, scheduled event-based operations, not ongoing, repetitive events that are advertised as open to the public.
(3)
A traffic control plan approved by the Polk County Police Department shall be followed during the hours of any special event upon the winery property.
(4)
The operation of Outdoor loudspeakers shall be governed by the requirements of Polk County Code Section 38-65 "Loud Music, Excessive Noise".
(5)
Food caterers may be utilized to provide food and beverages for special events.
(Res. No. 2019-16, § 1, 8-6-2019)
Sec. 6-48. - Violations.
Any person violating any provision of this chapter shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be subject to a fine of up to $1,000.00 and/or six months in jail per violation. In addition, any person violating any provision of this chapter may also have their license suspended or revoked and/or be subject to other penalties provided for in connection with violations of said license.
(Res. of 8-6-2013; Res. No. 2019-16, § 1, 8-6-2019)
Editor's note— Res. No. 2019-16, § 1, adopted August 6, 2019, added provisions for § 6-47, which pertains to farm winery, as herein set out. Said resolution renumbered the former § 6-47, which pertained to violations, as § 6-48 as herein set out.
