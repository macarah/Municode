Chapter 54 - PERSONNEL[1]
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 54-1. - Provisions saved from repeal.
Nothing in this Code or in the ordinance adopting this Code shall affect any ordinance or resolution:
(1)
Adopting or amending the personnel policy and merit system of the county.
(2)
Adopting or amending the flexible benefit plan, which became effective April 1, 1995.
(3)
Adopting or amending the county's substance abuse policy.
All such ordinances or resolutions are hereby recognized as continuing in full force and effect to the same extent as if set out at length in this Code.
Sec. 54-2. - County automobile/truck use policy.
(a)
No county-owned automobile or truck shall be used by any county employee to drive from the employee's place of work to home except the employees occupying the following positions may use a county vehicle to drive from work to home and from home to work: the county manager, the assistant county manager, the department head of road department, the assistant department head of road department-overtime supervision, the road department head of equipment maintenance, the supervisor of ditching, stormwater and drainage, the liaison official for stormwater, landfill and sewer, the department head of sewer department, the zoning administrator, the department head of animal control, all Transaid Drivers, the department head of building maintenance, the department head of recreation department, and Transaid Meals-on-Wheels drivers. In addition, the animal control officer on-call shall be permitted to drive his/her vehicle home from work and the building maintenance employee on-call shall also be permitted to drive his/her vehicle home from work.
(b)
Except as specifically provided in subsection (a) above, all county employees who drive a county automobile/truck in the course of their employment with the county shall park the automobile/truck at the conclusion of their work day at the county department where the employee reports to work and shall not drive the vehicle home from work. If a county employee is required to return to work after his/her shift for county business and use the vehicle, the employee shall pick up the county automobile/truck at the employee's place of duty.
(c)
County employees are prohibited from using a county vehicle for personal business unless the personal business is incidental to the employment duties of the county employee. This provision shall not apply to use of the county vehicle by the county manager.
(d)
If the county manager determines that it is necessary for a county employee to drive a county vehicle home in order to conduct specific county business, the county manager may authorize a county employee to use a county vehicle for this purpose. The county manager may not authorize the use of a county vehicle under this provision for more than 15 days unless the use of the county vehicle is specifically approved by the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 8-17-99, §§ 1—4)
Sec. 54-3. - E-mail/internet/information technology resources employee usage policy.
(a)
Purpose:
(1)
The purpose of this addendum to the employee handbook is to establish the policy for employee use of information technology resources in Catoosa County Government. This policy applies to all Catoosa County Information Technology resources regardless of location (i.e., office, home, field locations, etc.). This policy applies to all employees, contractors, part-time employees, volunteers or other individuals provided with access to the county's email system as necessary for business within the county.
(2)
Computer systems that belong to Catoosa County are not to be used for employee personal gain or to support or advocate non-county-related business or purposes. All use of Catoosa County internet or e-mail system is subject to monitoring and nothing placed on the computer by the employee, contractor, volunteer or other individuals provided with access to the county's information technology resources can be considered private. Do not put any information on the computer you consider private or personal. The computer you use at work is subject to unannounced public access.
(3)
As a public record, Catoosa County management retains the right to inspect, copy, or retrieve any data on the county information technology system, without prior approval of the employee. Contents of county owned computers are subject to monitoring from time to time and at any time that the supervisor, department head, the county manager or even a member of the public desires to review the contents of the computer. A member of the public would be required to make an open records request.
(b)
Policy:
(1)
The county information technology resources are county property. You have no expectation of privacy. Your supervisor, department head, the computer systems manager, the county manager, their designees and even the general public can access the data on your county computer.
(2)
Employees who are permitted use of e-mail, intranet, internet and other technology resources and have received approval from the county manager may use the information technology resources to support the conduct of official programmatic and administrative duties.
(3)
Use of these technology systems is intended for official purposes.
(4)
Use of the Catoosa County Information Technology Resources are subject to federal, state, and county laws and regulations governing such, including, but not limited to:
•
Copyright Act.
•
State Open Records Act.
(5)
All internet/intranet data that is composed, transmitted, or received via the information technology system of the county is considered part of the official records system and as such, is subject to disclosure law and the Georgia Open Records Act.
(6)
Prohibited uses:
•
Erasing, deleting or damaging any information on the computer hard drive.
•
Promoting or conducting political activities.
•
Making personal use of e-mail, intranet, internet or other information technology resources to advertise, trade (including buying or selling stocks), give away, solicit, or provide goods or services, except under circumstances and conditions that are specifically authorized by Catoosa County Manager.
•
Violating copyrights or software licensing agreements.
•
Engaging in unauthorized transactions that may incur a cost to the county.
•
Disseminating, intentionally accessing, or storing offensive or disparaging information, including hate literature, pornographic or sexually explicit images, harassing emails or discriminatory/racist literature.
•
Use of the email system for messages that could reasonably be considered as injurious or offensive to others on the basis of race, sex, age, sexual orientation, religious or political beliefs, national origin, or disability are strictly prohibited.
•
Distributing e-mail to multi users (e.g. all employees) outside your department without the specific approval of the county manager.
•
Sending, or contriving to send, or re-transmitting anonymous messages.
•
Revealing or publicizing proprietary or confidential information.
•
Making or posting improper remarks, proposals, or sending/posting message that defame or slander other individuals.
•
Overriding or avoiding security and integrity procedures and devices.
•
Subscribing to mail lists or list servers that are not related to official Catoosa County business.
•
The loading of any software onto a Catoosa County Desktop Computer System from any source without prior approval from both the county manager, head of the department and the county computer systems manager, is strictly prohibited. This includes, but is not limited to:
1.
Screen savers.
2.
Images.
3.
Video or audio clips not intended for business purposes.
•
Attaching or installing any piece of hardware to a Catoosa County Desktop System without prior approval from both the county manager, the head of the department and the county computer systems manager is also strictly prohibited. This includes, but is not limited to:
1.
External modems.
2.
Zip drives.
3.
Non-standard audio or video devices.
•
Removal of any piece of Catoosa County Hardware or Software from its intended location without prior approval from the county manager, the head of the department and the Catoosa County Computer Systems Manager is strictly prohibited. This includes, but is not limited to:
1.
Desktop computers.
2.
Printers.
3.
Scanners.
4.
Terminals.
5.
All software packages.
•
Intentionally intercepting, eavesdropping, recording, reading, altering or receiving another person's email message without proper authorization in accordance with this policy.
•
Listening to the radio or viewing video clips through the Catoosa County Internet connection for county business. The system is not to be used for employee entertainment, personal gain or to support or advocate non-county-related business or purposes.
(Amend. of 1-3-02)

ARTICLE II. - KICKBACKS

Sec. 54-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Employee means any person employed by the board of commissioners specifically including all persons receiving payroll checks from the office of commissioner.
Give (or any derivative phraseology or tense thereof, such as "giving," "given," etc.) means the delivery, directly or indirectly, by way of gift, payment, deposit, mail or parcel delivery, furnishing, supplying or other transfer of an item of value to an employee.
Item of value means tangible or intangible objects, moneys, remunerations, gifts, rewards, gratuities, fees, benefits, supplies, equipment, appliances, materials, commissions, kickback payments, deposits of moneys or checks, or other consideration.
Natural person means to one human being.
Receive (or any derivative phraseology or tense thereof, such as "receiving," "received," etc.) means the receipt, directly or indirectly, by way of gift, transfer payment, deposit, mail or parcel receipt, delivery, furnishing, supplying or other acceptance of an item of value by an employee.
(Ord. of 1-3-89, § 1)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 54-32. - Purpose.
The purpose of this article shall be to promote an open, honest and efficient method of handling the transactions involving the employees of the county in their capacity of performing the duties for which they are employed by the county. The further purpose of this article is to provide for the resolution of any improper conduct that may occur and to provide for penalties, forfeitures, employment actions and other methods of discouraging and prohibiting improper conduct.
(Ord. of 1-3-89, § 2)
Sec. 54-33. - Receiving items of value restricted.
(a)
All employees are prohibited from receiving or accepting any item of value from any person to influence any act related to the functions of his employment. All employees are further prohibited from soliciting any such item of value for such purpose.
(b)
Any employee who violates any of the provisions of this article will be subject to the immediate termination or suspension for the violation.
(Ord. of 1-3-89, § 3)
Sec. 54-34. - Giving away items of value restricted.
All persons are prohibited from giving or offering to give any employee any item of value for the purpose of influencing such employee in the performance of any act related to the functions of his employment.
(Ord. of 1-3-89, § 4)
Sec. 54-35. - Determination of costs.
(a)
Upon finding by the commissioner upon investigation that any person paid fees, commissions or moneys, or granted gifts or gratuities, or received or gave any item of value in violation of this article, it shall be conclusively presumed that the cost of such expense or item was included in the price of the contract or order of purchase, and ultimately borne by the county. The cost or expense of such item shall be conclusively presumed to be the fair market value of the payment or item of value as of the date of the receipt or transfer to the employee.
(b)
To the extent that the county has not received reimbursement for the fair market value of the item of value from the person that gave such item (as provided for in this article upon a violation of this article), all employees who so violate the provisions of this article shall be required to account to the county for the fair market value of all items of value so received, regardless of whether the county government, or any agency or division thereof, has suffered any actual loss. The county shall have the authority to withhold the fair market value of the item of value or moneys as of the date of receipt of such item or moneys.
(c)
Upon violation of this article by any person who is not an employee, including but not limited to any contractor or supplier, who is a party to a contract, order or purchase with the county or any agency thereof, the county shall have the option to void any such order, contract or purchase and to refrain from accepting any future bids, purchases, orders or contracts with such person. This subsection shall be deemed to be a part of all contracts entered into between the county and any person regardless of whether the terms hereof are included in the contract. The county commissioner shall have the authority to withhold from sums otherwise due any such person any amount found to have been paid or the fair market value of any item of value given in violation of this article; the amount that may be withheld shall be the fair market value of the item of value or moneys as of the date of delivery of such item or moneys.
(d)
This article is supplemental to any and all rights of action, claims or other actions that may be taken by the county or by the state to otherwise collect any amounts improperly paid or received or to punish any person for conduct that is in violation of this article or in violation of some other state or federal law.
(Ord. of 1-3-89, § 5)
Sec. 54-36. - Items of food, beverage.
Any items of food or beverage are specifically excluded from the prohibition of this article, but any such gift of food or beverage shall be open for partaking in by the taxpayers and residents of the county.
(Ord. of 1-3-89, § 6)
Sec. 54-37. - Limitation of costs.
The cost of any plaques, gifts or other awards of recognition given to any natural person by the county or any agency thereof in recognition for the service of such person shall not exceed the sum of $100.00 per item of county or county agency funds spent; that is, any cost in excess of $100.00 shall be borne by funds derived from sources other than the county or any agency of the county.
(Ord. of 1-3-89, § 7)
Sec. 54-38. - County's receipt of gifts.
Nothing in this article shall prohibit the county from receiving donations, gifts or other moneys from any source whatsoever, so long as the same does not inure solely to or for the benefit of one or a few individuals, but rather inures as a public benefit.
(Ord. of 1-3-89, § 8)
