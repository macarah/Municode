ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 70-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Ashes means the waste products of coal and other fuel used for heating and cooking from all public and private establishments and from all residences.
Commercial container means a solid waste container supplied for continuing use to a commercial or industrial establishment or to an apartment complex, or a container supplied for temporary use at a construction site, including but not limited to all dumpsters, commercial compactor units, and commercial roll-off containers.
Garbage means all putrescible wastes, except sewage and body wastes, including vegetable and animal offal and carcasses of dead animals, but excluding recognized industrial by-products, and includes all refuse or accumulation of animal, fruit or vegetable matter, liquid or otherwise, that attends the preparation, use, cooking, consumption, merchandising or storing of meat, fish, fowl, fruits or vegetables.
Health officer means a representative of the county health department.
Refuse means garbage, rubbish, ashes and other putrescible and nonputrescible wastes, except sewage, from all public and private establishments and residences.
Residential container means a solid waste container supplied for continuing use to premises used primarily for residential purposes.
Trash means all nonputrescible wastes, except ashes, from all public and private establishments and from all residences.
(Code 1977, § 12-1; Ord. No. O-95-20, § 1, 7-17-95)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Secs. 70-2—70-35. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - COLLECTION REGULATIONS

Sec. 70-36. - Authority of city.
The mayor and council may provide for the collection and disposal of garbage, trash and refuse, regulate the manner and time of such collection and disposal, establish the rates to be charged therefor, and otherwise make such rules and regulations pertaining to garbage, trash and refuse as may be desirable to assure the public health and welfare.
(Code 1977, § 12-17)
Sec. 70-37. - Disposal generally.
All garbage accumulating in private houses or on private premises, in hotels, restaurants, boardinghouses, fruit stands, stores, markets or elsewhere in the city shall be carried to a place designated and approved by the mayor and council. Such garbage as melon rinds, fruit parings, vegetables, leavings and liquid waste used as food or drink for fowl or animals may be kept on the premises temporarily, from one hauling period to the next, in such a manner as not to cause a nuisance or become a fly-attracting agent.
(Code 1977, § 12-18)
Sec. 70-38. - Accumulation prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any owner, occupant or lessee of any building, yard or lot of ground within the city to allow garbage, trash or refuse of any kind to accumulate or remain in the building, or upon the yard or lot, unless placed in a container as required by this article.
(Code 1977, § 12-19)
Sec. 70-39. - Containers—Required.
Every person and every business or commercial enterprise in the corporate limits of the city having vegetable or animal waste or other garbage shall keep the same on the premises in one or more containers at places where it may be conveniently reached and collected by the sanitary department of the city. Additionally every person shall affix a sign in a conspicuous location on each container (other than those exclusively for single-family residential use) prohibiting private dumping.
(Code 1977, § 12-20; Ord. No. O-05-50, § 4, 9-19-05)
Sec. 70-40. - Same—Specifications.
Containers for the accumulation of garbage and refuse shall meet such specifications therefor as promulgated by the mayor and council and on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1977, § 12-21)
Sec. 70-41. - Fees.
The fees for the collection and disposal of garbage and refuse shall be as determined by the mayor and council from time to time and on file in the office of the finance director. Except as specifically exempted under this section, the owner, occupant, tenant or lessee of any home, building or commercial structure shall pay at least the minimum charge for garbage pick-up and disposal, as adopted by the mayor and council and on file with the finance director. Any single-family household in which any adult resident is over the age of 64 years shall be eligible for exemption from payment of the minimum charge for residential collection and disposal of garbage and refuse, upon written certification of the same to the finance director by the owner, occupant, tenant or lessee thereof. Fees for solid waste containers and services shall be incurred exclusively in the name(s) or the owner(s) and/or lessee(s) of the subject property, and not in the name(s) of any contractor(s) performing work upon the property.
(Code 1977, § 12-23; Ord. No. O-96-26, § 1, 5-6-96; Ord. No. O-01-81, § 8, 12-3-01; Ord. No. O-05-29, § 1, 6-20-05; Ord. No. O-07-32, § 1, 6-4-07)
Cross reference— Failure to pay utility charges, § 86-46.
Sec. 70-42. - Maintenance of containers.
Every garbage and trash container required by this article shall be maintained in as sanitary a condition as possible in view of the use to which it is put, and shall be thoroughly cleansed as needed by washing, sterilizing or otherwise.
(Code 1977, § 12-24)
Sec. 70-43. - Notice of damage to commercial containers.
It shall be the duty of every person owning, controlling, managing, operating, policing, renting, or occupying any premises to which a commercial container is supplied to inspect the container regularly and to give notice to the city of any container which has jagged edges capable of causing injuries or which has deteriorated or been damaged to such an extent.
(Code 1977, § 12-25; Ord. No. O-95-20, § 2, 7-17-95)
Sec. 70-44. - Commercial containers furnished by city.
(a)
The city will determine the size of each container needed by the user and such containers shall be placed in a manner as to be easily accessible to the city equipment making the pick-up. It shall be the responsibility of the user to remove all or any vehicles or objects blocking the container.
(b)
The containers provided the user shall not be damaged or abused by the user. If such containers are damaged or abused by the user or its employees, the user shall be responsible for the expenses incurred by the city in repairing and/or replacing such container.
(c)
No person shall ignite or set fire to the contents of any waste container provided by the city. Any person violating this provision of this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-3 of this Code.
(d)
No person shall dispose of residential waste or refuse in a commercial container. No person shall place into any commercial container any waste or refuse not generated by the established to which the container is supplied.
(e)
No person shall place into any commercial container any material which is highly acidic, radioactive, volatile, highly flammable, explosive toxic, hazardous or any material which is hazardous, damaged or dangerous waste as defined by federal, state or local law, regulation or ordinance.
(Code 1977, § 12-26; Ord. No. O-91-24, § 2, 8-5-91; Ord. No. O-95-20, § 3, 7-17-95)
Sec. 70-45. - Cover required for vehicles hauling garbage, etc.
All vehicles used in hauling garbage, trash or refuse of any kind whatsoever on any street or highway within the city shall have beds of tight construction to prevent the contents from falling or sifting onto the street, highway or ground, and shall at all times, except when loading or unloading, be covered with a canvas cover or some comparable cover.
(Code 1977, § 12-27)
Sec. 70-46. - Duty of customer to bag leaves and other common yard wastes.
(a)
All leaves, small limbs, twigs, clippings, straw, bark, pine cones, grass and other such materials associated with common yard waste shall be properly bagged in an appropriate container in order for it to be disposed of by the city's trash disposal service.
(b)
Plastic containers of sufficient size and strength of the generic term lawn bags or yard bags shall be utilized for the purpose of disposal of such waste. Bags shall be of such size as to allow the openings to be securely fastened so that no spillage shall occur upon loading or transporting by city employees. Bags shall be of sufficient strength not to split, open or tear under the load of the contents while being picked up, carried by the slack in the top of the bag or loaded onto the truck. Spillages due to improper bagging techniques or materials shall not be the responsibility of the city or its employees.
(c)
No container shall be placed upon the paved or traveled portion of a city street or sidewalk while awaiting collection.
(Code 1977, § 12-28)
Sec. 70-47. - Residential containers furnished by city.
(a)
The city shall furnish residential containers to all residential properties in the City of Douglasville.
(b)
The containers shall be the property of the City of Douglasville and shall not be used for any purposes not approved by the city.
(c)
The container shall not be damaged or abused by the user. If a container is damaged or destroyed, the property owner and tenant shall be jointly and severably liable to the city for the cost of repair or replacement of the container.
(d)
It shall be the responsibility of the user to place the container at the curb at the time specified by the city for garbage pick-up and to remove the container promptly after the city has emptied the container.
(e)
The container lid shall be securely closed at all times except when the container is being filled or emptied to keep out animals and insects.
(f)
No person shall ignite or set fire to the contents of any residential container provided by the city. Any person violating this provision of this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-3 of this Code.
(Code 1977, § 12-29)
Sec. 70-48. - Reserved.
Sec. 70-49. - Authorized residential containers; required.
(a)
Every person disposing of household trash or garbage at any residence (other than apartment residences) within the corporate limits of the City of Douglasville shall do so by enclosing all such household trash or garbage within one or more authorized residential containers supplied by the city or its franchisee, with the lid(s) fully and securely closed. The following described household waste shall be exempt from this requirement:
(1)
All leaves, small limbs, twigs, plant clippings, straw, bark, pine cones, grass, and other common yard waste, which may be disposed of in non-authorized bags without city stickers and in non-authorized containers as provided in section 70-46; and
(2)
Recyclable materials as authorized by the mayor and council and on file with the city clerk; and
(3)
Household trash or garbage when disposed of in a bag upon which an authorized city sticker has been affixed, provided that such bag is strong enough to contain the contents throughout the disposal process; and
(4)
Any solid waste disposed pursuant to an exempt "community clean-up" program authorized by city resolution.
(b)
The city sanitation department shall assign an appropriate sized residential container for each household, considering, among other factors, the following: the request of the resident(s), the usual volume of waste generated by the household, the frequency with which waste is disposed of in bags which have or should have city stickers affixed, any history of non-compliance with this article, and availability of city containers in specific sizes. Where the city sanitation department determines that any assigned container size is inadequate for any household, the department may assign a different size container more appropriate to the needs of the residents, and the resident shall pay the lawful rate for the newly assigned container.
(Ord. No. O-95-12, § 1, 5-15-95; Ord. No. O-97-36, § 1, 4-21-97; Ord. No. O-98-42, § 1, 5-18-98; Ord. No. O-05-29, § 2, 6-20-05)
Sec. 70-50. - Uncollected residential waste.
The city sanitation department or its authorized franchisee may decline to collect household waste which is left at the curb for disposal not in compliance with section 70-49. Failure of the resident(s) to remove from the curb any rejected household waste within 48 hours of the regular collection time shall constitute unlawful accumulation.
(Ord. No. O-95-12, § 1, 5-15-95; Ord. No. O-05-29, § 3, 6-20-05)
Sec. 70-51. - City stickers not for resale or counterfeit.
It shall be unlawful for any person or business enterprise, other than the City of Douglasville or its authorized franchisees, to transfer or offer to transfer, within the corporate limits of the City of Douglasville, any specially marked solid waste bag sticker authorized by the city for use in connection with disposal of household trash or garbage. It shall be unlawful for any person or business enterprise other than the City of Douglasville or its authorized franchisees to fabricate or to transfer within the corporate limits of the City of Douglasville any sticker substantially similar in appearance to any specially marked solid waste bag sticker authorized by the city for use in connection with disposal of household trash or garbage.
(Ord. No. O-05-29, § 4, 6-20-05)
Secs. 70-52—70-75. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - PERMIT FOR PRIVATE COLLECTION AND DISPOSAL[2]

Sec. 70-76. - Required.
No person, firm or corporation except the duly authorized franchisee or duly authorized agents or employees of the city, shall collect garbage or trash within the city or convey or transport garbage or trash on the streets, alleys, and public thoroughfares of the city for profit.
(Ord. No. O-97-59, § 1, 9-2-97)
Sec. 70-77. - Filing application; investigation.
Any person, firm or corporation desiring a permit for the collection, removal and disposal of garbage, trash and rubbish shall make application for such permit to the city council, which shall make or cause to be made such investigation as it may consider necessary in order to determine whether or not the public convenience and necessity require the granting of such permit, and whether the applicant is a fit and proper person to conduct such business or activity for profit.
(Ord. No. O-97-59, § 1, 9-2-97)
Sec. 70-78. - Contents of application.
The application for a private collection and disposal permit shall set forth:
(1)
The name and address of the applicant;
(2)
Whether the applicant is an individual, a corporation or a partnership;
(3)
The trade name under which the applicant does or proposes to do business;
(4)
The number of vehicles the applicant desires to operate;
(5)
The class, size and design of each vehicle;
(6)
The names, addresses, and drivers license numbers of all drivers and other information sufficient to obtain driving record reports on each driver;
(7)
Whether or not the applicant has been convicted of any crime involving moral turpitude;
(8)
Sufficient information for the city to obtain a commercial credit report;
(9)
Proof of public liability insurance to cover the operation of his vehicles for damage to property of others while operating such vehicles on the streets of this city;
(10)
The nature and character of the service the applicant proposes to render;
(11)
The experience he or it has had in rendering such service;
(12)
The patrons for whom he proposes to render this service; and
(13)
Such other information as the mayor and city council may require.
(Ord. No. O-97-59, § 1, 9-2-97)
Sec. 70-79. - Fees.
(a)
Application. The mayor and city council shall determine by resolution the amount to be charged as an application fee to cover the cost of the investigation. Such fee shall not be refundable.
(b)
Permit. The mayor and city council shall determine by resolution the amount to be charged as a permit fee. Such permit fee shall be in addition to, and not in lieu of, applicable business license fees and taxes.
(Ord. No. O-97-59, § 1, 9-2-97)
Sec. 70-80. - Insurance required.
Any applicant making application for a permit under this section shall, for each and every vehicle to be used in collection, have issued to him and maintained by him by a company duly licensed to operate and do business in the state, a policy of indemnity insurance in the amount of at least $500,000.00 for bodily injury to any one person, in the amount of $1,000,000.00 for injuries to more than one person sustained in the same accident, and in the amount of $300,000.00 for property damage resulting from any one accident. Such policy of indemnity may cover all of the vehicles operated by the same owner. A copy of such policy shall be furnished to the city upon application for a permit, and a copy shall be thereafter maintained with the city clerk should a permit be granted.
(Ord. No. O-97-59, § 1, 9-2-97)
Sec. 70-81. - Nontransferable; revocation.
All permits granted by the mayor and city council for collection, removal and disposal of garbage, trash and rubbish shall be nontransferable. Such permits may be revoked for a violation of this article by the city council at any time when, in its judgment, such action is deemed to be in the best interest of the public. The permit holder shall receive five days advance notice of the revocation and may request a hearing before the mayor and council to protest the revocation.
(Ord. No. O-97-59, § 1, 9-2-97)
Sec. 70-82. - Display; inspection.
In all cases where permits have been issued for the collection, removal and disposal of garbage, trash and rubbish, each such permit shall be in the possession of the person rendering such service, and shall be subject to inspection at all times, and the permit numbers shall be painted on both sides of the vehicle used for such purpose.
(Ord. No. O-97-59, § 1, 9-2-97)
Sec. 70-83. - Penalty for violation; parties to a violation.
Any person violating any provision of this chapter shall be held liable and punished as provided in Section 4.13 of the Douglasville City Charter. Any person who hires another to collect, remove or dispose of garbage, trash or rubbish within the city shall first inspect the hauler's permit and verify that the same has been duly issued by the city clerk and that the same is current and effective; failure to comply with this provision shall constitute a violation of this chapter. Any person who intentionally aids, abets, advises, encourages, hires, counsels or procures another to commit a violation of this chapter is a party to said violation, notwithstanding that the person alleged to have directly committed the violation has not been prosecuted or convicted, has been convicted of a different violation, or is not amenable to justice or has been acquitted.
(Ord. No. O-97-59, § 1, 9-2-97; Ord. No. O-01-41, § 9, 6-18-01)
Secs. 70-84—70-100. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - LITTER CONTROL[3]

Sec. 70-101. - Short title.
This article shall be known and may be cited as the Douglasville Anti-Litter Ordinance.
(Code 1977, § 14-51)
Sec. 70-102. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Aircraft means any contrivance used or designated for navigation or for flight in the air. The word aircraft shall include helicopters and lighter-than-air dirigibles and balloons.
Authorized private receptacle means a litter storage and collection receptacle as required and authorized in the ordinances of the city.
City means the City of Douglasville.
Commercial handbill means any printed or written matter, any sample or device, dodger, circular, leaflet, pamphlet, paper, booklet, or any other printed or otherwise reproduced original or copies of any matter of literature:
(1)
Which advertises for sale any merchandise, product, commodity or thing;
(2)
Which directs attention to any business or mercantile or commercial establishment, or other activity, for the purpose of either directly or indirectly promoting the interest thereof by sales;
(3)
Which directs attention to or advertises any meeting, theatrical performance, exhibition, or event of any kind, for which an admission fee is charged for the purpose of private gain or profit; but the terms of this clause shall not apply where an admission fee is charged or a collection is taken up for the purpose of defraying the expenses incident to such meeting, theatrical performance, exhibition, or event of any kind, when either of the same is held, given or takes place in connection with the dissemination of information which is not restricted under the ordinary rules of decency, good morals, public peace, safety and good order; provided, that nothing contained in this clause shall be deemed to authorize the holding, giving or taking place of any meeting, theatrical performance, exhibition, or event of any kind, without a license, where such license is or may be required by any law of this state, or under any ordinance of this city; or
(4)
Which, while containing reading matter other than advertising matter, is predominantly and essentially an advertisement, and is distributed or circulated for advertising purposes, or for the private benefit and gain of any person so engaged as advertiser or distributor.
Garbage means putrescible animal and vegetable wastes resulting from the handling, preparation, cooking and consumption of food.
Litter means garbage, refuse, and rubbish and all other waste material which, if thrown or deposited tends to create a danger to public health, safety and welfare, and the term also includes materials of whatever nature which, although not waste or discarded material, have been placed on or scattered onto the property of another without permission and are not promptly removed.
Newspaper means any newspaper of general circulation as defined by general law, any newspaper duly entered with the United States Postal Service, in accordance with federal statute or regulation, and any newspaper filed and recorded with any recording officer as provided by general law; and, in addition thereto, means and includes any periodical or current magazine regularly published with not less than four issues per year, and sold to the public.
Noncommercial handbill means any printed or written matter, any sample or device, dodger, circular, leaflet, pamphlet, newspaper, magazine, paper, booklet, or any other printed or otherwise reproduced original or copies of any matter of literature not included in a commercial handbill or newspaper.
Park means a park, reservation, playground, beach, recreation center or any other public area in the city, owned or used by the city and devoted to active or passive recreation.
Person means any person, firm, partnership, association, corporation, company or organization of any kind.
Private premises means any dwelling, house, building or other structure, designed or used either wholly or in part for residential or commercial purposes, whether inhabited or temporarily or continuously uninhabited or vacant, and shall include any yard, grounds, walk, driveway, porch, steps, vestibule or mailbox belonging or appurtenant to such dwelling, house, building or other structure.
Public place means any and all streets, sidewalks, boulevards, alleys or other public ways and any and all public parks, squares, spaces, grounds and buildings.
Refuse means all putrescible and nonputrescible solid wastes, except body wastes, including garbage, rubbish, ashes, street cleanings, dead animals, abandoned automobiles, and solid market and industrial wastes.
Rubbish means nonputrescible solid wastes consisting of both combustible and noncombustible wastes, such as paper, wrappings, cigarettes, cardboard, tin cans, yard clippings, leaves, wood, glass, bedding, crockery, natural fibers, man-made materials and similar materials.
Vehicle means every device in, upon, or by which any person or property is or may be transported or drawn upon a highway, including devices used exclusively upon stationary rails or tracks.
(Code 1977, § 14-52)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 70-103. - Penalties.
Any person violating any of the provisions of this article shall be punished in accordance with section 1-3. Each day such violation is committed or permitted to continue shall constitute a separate offense and shall be punishable as such hereunder.
(Code 1977, § 14-72)
Sec. 70-104. - Duty of business owners, occupants to prevent littering.
(a)
Generally. The owner or occupant of any store or other place of business situated within the city shall exercise reasonable diligence at all times to keep his premises clean of wastepaper, wrapping paper, paper napkins, cartons, package containers and other used or waste materials thrown or left on the premises by customers of such business, and to take reasonable measures to prevent the same from drifting or blowing to adjoining premises.
(b)
Receptacles. Receptacles of sufficient size and number shall be placed on the premises accessible to the customers of any business where the articles of waste referred to in subsection (a) of this section may be disposed of.
(c)
Signs. Each and every business establishment shall place upon its premises in conspicuous places in close proximity to the receptacles required by subsection (b) of this section signs which shall, in essence, convey to its customers a request that they use such receptacles for the disposal of waste material.
(Code 1977, § 12-4)
Sec. 70-105. - Duty of customer to use litter receptacles.
It shall be unlawful for any customer going upon the premises of another in any manner to dispose of wastepaper, wrapping paper, paper napkins, cartons, package containers and other used or waste materials, except in receptacles provided for such purposes.
(Code 1977, § 12-5)
Sec. 70-106. - Placement of litter in receptacles so as to prevent scattering.
Persons placing litter in public receptacles or in authorized private receptacles shall do so in such a manner as to prevent it from being carried or deposited by the elements upon any street, sidewalk or other public place or upon private property.
(Code 1977, § 14-54)
Sec. 70-107. - Sweeping litter into gutters prohibited.
No person shall sweep into or deposit in any gutter, street or other public place within the city the accumulation of litter from any building or lot or from any public or private sidewalk or driveway. Persons owning or occupying property shall keep the sidewalk in front of their premises free of litter.
(Code 1977, § 14-55)
Sec. 70-108. - Reserved.

Sec. 70-109. - Truck loads causing litter.
No person shall drive or move any truck or other vehicle within the city unless such vehicle is so constructed, loaded or covered as to prevent any load, contents or litter from being blown or deposited upon any street, alley or other public place. Nor shall any person drive or move any vehicle or truck within the city, the wheels or tires of which carry onto or deposit in any street, alley or other public place, mud, dirt, sticky substances, litter or foreign matter of any kind.
(Code 1977, § 14-58)
Sec. 70-110. - Dropping litter from aircraft.
No person in an aircraft shall throw out, drop or deposit within the city any litter, handbill or any other object.
(Code 1977, § 14-66)
Sec. 70-111. - Posting notices prohibited.
No person shall post or affix any notice, poster or other paper or device, calculated to attract the attention of the public, to any lamp post, public utility pole or shade tree within the right-of-way of any street, or upon any private property without the permission of the owner or occupant, or upon any public structure, building or property, except as may be authorized or required by law.
(Code 1977, § 14-67)
Sec. 70-112. - Owner to maintain premises free of litter.
The owner and/or person in control of any private property shall at all times maintain the premises free of litter. Provided, however, that this section shall not prohibit the storage of litter in authorized private receptacles for collection.
(Code 1977, § 14-69)
Sec. 70-113. - Clearing of litter from open private property by city.
(a)
Notice to remove. The building official, health officer, city clerk, or chief of police, or their deputies, are hereby authorized and empowered to notify the owner of property within the city or the agent of such owner to properly dispose of litter located on such owner's property which is dangerous to public health, safety or welfare. Such notice shall be by certified mail addressed to such owner at his last known address as indicated on the tax records of the city.
(b)
Action upon noncompliance. Upon the failure, neglect or refusal of any owner or agent so notified, to properly dispose of litter dangerous to the public health, safety or welfare within seven days after receipt of written notice provided for in subsection (a) of this section, or within ten days after the date of such notice if the same is returned to the city because of the inability of the United States Postal Service to make delivery thereof, provided the same was properly addressed to the last known address of such owner or agent, the officers authorized above are hereby authorized and empowered to order disposal of the litter by the city or private removal agencies designated by the city.
(c)
Charge included in tax bill. When the city has effected the removal of such litter or has paid for its removal, the actual cost thereof, plus accrued interest at the rate of six percent per annum from the date of the completion of the work shall be paid by the owner within 15 days of the completion of the work. If such is not paid by such owner prior to the next regular tax billing date then such charges shall be charged to the owner of such property on the next regular tax bill forwarded to such owner by the city, and such charges shall be due and payable by the owner at the time of payment of such bill.
(d)
Recorded statement constitutes lien. Where the full amount due the city is not paid by such owner within 15 days after the disposal of such litter, as provided for in subsections (a) and (b) of this section, the city clerk shall cause to be recorded in the office of the clerk of the superior court, a sworn statement showing the cost and expense incurred for the work, the date the work was done and the location of the property on which such work was done. The recordation of such sworn statement shall constitute a lien and privilege on the property, and shall remain in full force and effect for the amount due in principal and interest, plus costs of court, if any, for collection, until final payment has been made. Such costs and expenses shall be collected in the manner fixed by law for the collection of taxes and further, shall be subject to a delinquent penalty of ten percent if the same is not paid in full on or before the date the tax bill upon which such charge appears becomes delinquent. Sworn statements recorded in accordance with the provisions of this section shall be prima facie evidence that all legal formalities have been complied with and that the work has been done properly and satisfactorily, and shall be full notice to every person concerned that the amount of the statement, plus interest, constitutes a charge against the property designated or described in the statement and that the same is due and collectible as provided by law.
(Code 1977, § 14-71)
Sec. 70-114. - Dumping, depositing, etc., litter on public or private property or waters.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to dump, deposit or throw, or leave or cause or permit the dumping, depositing, placing, throwing, or leaving of litter on any public or private property in the city or any waters of this city, unless:
(1)
The property is designated by the state or by one of its agencies or political subdivisions for the disposal of litter and the person is authorized by the proper public authority to use such property;
(2)
The litter is placed into a litter receptacle or container installed on the property for the purpose of limited use by guests and members of the public; or
(3)
The person is the owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property or has first obtained consent of the owner or tenant in lawful possession or unless the act is done under the personal direction of the owner or tenant, all in a manner consistent with the public welfare.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to dispose of household trash or garbage in any container provided by the city, except as provided in section 70-49 of this chapter.
(c)
Any person who violates subsection (a) of this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction thereof, shall be punished as follows:
(1)
By a fine of not more than $500.00 nor more than 90 days in jail.
(2)
In the sound discretion of the court, the person may be directed to pick up and remove from any public street or highway, public right-of-way, public park, or with the permission of the legal owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property, any private property upon which it can be established by competent evidence that the person has deposited litter, any and all litter deposited thereon by anyone prior to the date of execution of sentence.
(d)
The court may publish the names of persons convicted of violating subsection (a) of this section.
(Ord. No. O-92-5, § 7(14-73), 4-6-92; Ord. No. O-97-39, § 1, 5-19-97)
Sec. 70-115. - Prima facie evidence; rebuttable presumption.
(a)
Whenever litter is thrown, deposited, dropped, or dumped from any motor vehicle, boat, airplane or other conveyance in violation of section 70-114, it shall be prima facie evidence that the operator of the conveyance has violated section 70-114.
(b)
Except as provided in subsection (a) of this section, whenever any litter which is dumped, deposited, thrown, or left on public or private property in violation of section 70-114 is discovered to contain any article or articles, including but not limited to letters, bills, publications, or other writings which display the name of a person thereon in such a manner as to indicate that the article belongs or belonged to such person, it shall be a rebuttable presumption that such person has violated section 70-114.
(Ord. No. O-92-5, § 8(14-74), 4-6-92)

ARTICLE V. - RESTAURANTS AND SHOPS ON THE DOWNTOWN PLAZA[4]

Sec. 70-130. - Intent.
It is the intent of this article to create and maintain a clean, safe, healthy and pleasant environment for the public within the Downtown Plaza, including public places at the rear, front and sides of all buildings within this area, while encouraging and supporting restaurants and other businesses within the Downtown Plaza area.
(Ord. No. O-08-015, 4-7-08)
Sec. 70-131. - Applicability.
The provisions of this article shall apply to restaurants, eating establishments and other businesses (for purposes of this article, hereinafter called "restaurants") generating grease, vegetable waste, food waste, garbage, other waste with an obnoxious smell, or containers having a residue of such material (for purposes of this article, hereinafter collectively called "food waste") located in the Downtown Plaza as defined herein. The provisions of this article shall also apply to all other business establishments (for purposes of this article, hereinafter called "shops") in the Downtown Plaza area from the southern boundary of the Norfolk Southern Railroad right-of-way southward to the northern boundary of the Church Street right-of-way; from the eastern boundary of the Courthouse Square East right-of-way westward to the western boundary of the Campbellton Street right-of-way. Where any provision of this article is in conflict with any other provision of this chapter, the more restrictive provision of the two shall apply.
(Ord. No. O-08-015, 4-7-08)
Sec. 70-132. - Responsible party.
It shall be the duty of every person owning, controlling, managing, operating, policing, renting or occupying any restaurant on the Downtown Plaza to assure compliance of such restaurant with the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. O-08-015, 4-7-08)
Sec. 70-133. - Accumulation of waste prohibited.
(a)
Outdoor disposal of food waste prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any restaurant or shop within the Downtown Plaza to deposit or to allow any food waste to accumulate or remain outdoors on the premises of such restaurant or shop, whether or not such food waste or other solid waste is enclosed within any bag, cart or container.
(b)
Outdoor disposal of non-food waste prohibited for restaurants. It shall be unlawful for any restaurant within the Downtown Plaza to deposit or allow any non-food waste to accumulate or remain outdoors on the premises of such restaurant, whether or not such non-food waste or other solid waste is enclosed within any bag, cart or container, except for non-food recyclables as provided in section 70-134.
(c)
Outdoor disposal of non-food waste for shops. It shall be unlawful for any shop within the Downtown Plaza to deposit or to allow any non-food waste to accumulate or remain outdoors on the premises of such shop, except in accordance with this article.
(Ord. No. O-08-015, 4-7-08)
Sec. 70-134. - Proper disposal of solid waste.
(a)
Shops. Shops shall dispose of bagged non-food waste by placing such bags at the rear entrance to the building, not sooner than one and one-half hours prior to any normal scheduled time for collection by the city or its collection franchisee. Exceptionally, non-food recyclables may be disposed of in approved containers, rather than in bags, but the times for collection shall be the same as for other non-food waste.
(b)
Restaurants. Restaurants shall dispose of food waste and non-food waste by bagging the same and keeping it indoors and accessible for regular backdoor collection by the city or its collection franchisee. Exceptionally, non-food recyclables may be disposed of in approved containers, rather than in bags, but the times for collection shall be the same as for other non-food waste, and recyclable grease may be disposed of through the use of private recycling establishments, but grease shall not be left outdoors for collection nor discharged or leaked onto the ground or pavement incident to recycling.
(Ord. No. O-08-015, 4-7-08)
Sec. 70-135. - Fees for disposal.
The fees for disposal of solid waste by restaurants and shops shall be as determined by the mayor and council from time to time and on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Ord. No. O-08-015, 4-7-08)
Sec. 70-136. - Outdoor transporting of food waste prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any restaurant or shop to transport or allow to be transported any food waste, except in accordance with section 70-134.
(Ord. No. O-08-015, 4-7-08)
Sec. 70-137. - Outdoor cleaning prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any restaurant or shop to clean or wash any food waste cart, solid waste container, grease container, utensil, mat or equipment used in the course of its business at any outdoor location within the Downtown Plaza.
(Ord. No. O-08-015, 4-7-08)

ARTICLE VI. - SCRAP TIRE DISPOSAL

Sec. 70-151. - Intent and purpose.
The city council finds that protection of the environment is vital to the health, safety, welfare and economic progress of the city and its citizens. Therefore, it is the intent of this article to develop regulations and procedures that govern scrap tires from the point of generation to the point of disposal.
( Ord. No. O-2020-38 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-17-20)
Sec. 70-152. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Dump means to throw, discard, place, deposit, discharge, bury, burn, or dispose of a substance.
End user means the last person who uses the scrap tires, chips, crumb rubber, or similar materials to make a product with economic value, or in the case of energy recovery, the person who utilizes the heat content or other forms of energy from the incineration or pyrolysis of waste tires, chips or similar materials.
Environmental code enforcement officer means an officer of the city authorized by this Code to write official warnings and citations to ensure compliance with this article and the solid waste code. This definition shall include police officers and code compliance officers.
Manifest means a document used to identify the quantity and composition and the origin, routing, and destination of scrap tires during transportation from the point of generation, through any intermediate points, to an end user, processor or disposer approved by the state department of natural resources, environmental protection division, hereinafter referred to as EPD.
Mixed tires means a heterogeneous group of tires consisting of used, retreadable casings and scrap tires.
Recycle means any process by which materials which would otherwise become solid waste are collected, separated, or processed and reused or returned to use in the form of raw materials or products.
Retreadable casing means a tire suitable for retreading.
Reused tire means a tire used for purposes, approved by the EPD, other than intended, such as playground equipment, offshore reefs, and erosion control.
Scrap tire means a tire that is no longer suitable for its originally intended purpose because of wear, damage, or defect.
Scrap tire carrier means any person engaged in picking up or transporting scrap tires, not otherwise exempted, for the purpose of removal to a scrap tire processor, end use, or disposal facility.
Scrap tire generator means any person who generates scrap tires. Generators may include, but are not limited to, retail tire dealers, retreaders, scrap tire processors, automobile dealers, private company vehicle maintenance shops, garages and service stations.
Scrap tire processing means any method, system, or other treatment designed to change the physical form, size, or chemical content of scrap tires and includes all aspects of its management (administration, personnel, land, equipment, building, and other elements) and includes processing by: shredding, chopping, chipping, baling, splitting, recycling or sorting of scrap tires.
Scrap tire processor means any person who is approved by the EPD to receive tires from scrap tire generators, scrap tire carriers or the general public for the purpose of scrap tire processing.
Scrap tire sorter means any person, other than the original scrap tire generators, who handles mixed tires by separating used tires and retreadable casing from scrap tires.
Solid waste means any garbage or refuse; sludge from a wastewater treatment plant, water supply treatment plant, or air pollution control facility; and other discarded material including solid, liquid, semisolid, or contained gaseous material resulting from industrial, commercial, mining, and agricultural operation materials; solid or dissolved matters in domestic sewage; solid or dissolved materials in irrigation return flows or industrial discharges that are point sources subject to permit under 33 USC 1342; or source, special nuclear, or by-product material as defined by the Federal Atomic Energy Act of 1954, as amended and as defined by O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(33).
Tire means a continuous, solid or pneumatic covering designed for encircling the wheel of a vehicle that is not permanently attached to the vehicle or a part of the vehicle as original equipment.
Tire handling business means any person that derives 15 percent or more of its gross income from the sale, processing, transporting or disposal of tires.
Tire retailer means any person engaged in the business of selling new replacement tires.
Tire retreader means any person actively engaged in the business of retreading scrap tires by scarifying the surface to remove the old surface tread and attaching a new tread to make a usable tire.
Ultimate consumer means the last person who receives and uses a new replacement tire. Ultimate consumers may be, but are not limited to: individual, leasing and private companies purchasing tires from retail dealers for their vehicle fleets and government agencies.
Used tire means a tire which has a minimum of 2/32 foot of road tread and which is still suitable for its original purpose. It must be inventoried and marketed in substantially the same fashion as a new tire, and the dealer must be able to provide satisfactory evidence to the county that a market exists, and that the used tires are in fact being marketed.
( Ord. No. O-2020-38 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-17-20)
Sec. 70-153. - Dumping of scrap tires.
It shall be unlawful to dump scrap tires or cause, suffer or allow the dumping of scrap tires at any place in the city including the following:
(1)
Any public highway, road, street, alley, or thoroughfare, including any portion of the right-of-way thereof, or on any other public lands except in containers or areas lawfully provided for such dumping; and
(2)
Any river, canal, stream, creek, or fresh water lake; and
(3)
Any public property or private property not owned or maintained by the alleged violator.
( Ord. No. O-2020-38 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-17-20)
Sec. 70-154. - Scrap tire storage, handling, and disposal.
(a)
The provisions of O.C.G.A. § 12-8-20 et seq., as amended, are hereby incorporated in this section as if fully set forth herein.
(b)
It shall be the responsibility of the owner or occupant of property to store all scrap tires in a manner that will not adversely affect the public health and safety.
(c)
It shall be the responsibility of the owner or occupant of property to store all scrap tires in a manner that is consistent with federal, state and local law.
( Ord. No. O-2020-38 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-17-20)
Sec. 70-155. - Accumulation.
(a)
No person may store more than 100 scrap tires anywhere in the city. Any person storing in excess of 100 scrap tires shall be deemed to be in violation of this part. The following exceptions shall apply:
(1)
A solid waste disposal site permitted by the division if permitted by the EPD if the permit authorizes the storage of scrap tires prior to their disposal;
(2)
A tire retailer with not more than 3,000 scrap tires in storage;
(3)
A tire retreader with not more than 1,500 scrap tires in storage so long as the scrap tires are of the type that the retreader is actively retreading;
(4)
An auto salvage yard with not more than 500 scrap tires in storage; and
(5)
A scrap tire processor approved by the division so long as the number of scrap tires in storage do no exceed the quantity approved by the EPD.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to store scrap tires except in a container, located outside of an authorized business, enclosed on all four sides by a solid screen and having a secure top cover to prevent the accumulation of water in the container.
( Ord. No. O-2020-38 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-17-20)
Sec. 70-156. - Compliance.
(a)
Scrap tire generator. Any person who generates scrap tires shall obtain a scrap tire generator identification number issued by EPD and shall maintain copies of manifests for any scrap tires that have been shipped or removed from the business location within the past 36 months. Separate identification numbers are required for each generator having multiple generation locations.
(1)
The manifests shall include the following information:
a.
Name and identification number of the generator;
b.
Number and total tonnage (accurate to within ten percent of actual number) of scrap tires to be transported;
c.
Name and permit number of the scrap tire carrier;
d.
Date of transport;
e.
Destination of scrap tires; and
f.
Signatures of the scrap tire generator, scrap tire carrier and scrap tire processor.
(2)
The completed manifest and the scrap tire generator identification number shall be made available to the environmental code enforcement officer upon request.
(3)
Scrap tire generator identification numbers are not transferable; and each generator shall ensure that the scrap tire section of the EPD approves the carrier being used to transport scrap tires.
(b)
Scrap tire carrier. It shall be the responsibility of the carrier to return a completed copy of the manifest to the scrap tire generator within 30 days from the date on which the scrap tire carrier takes possession of the scrap tires. The date the scrap tire carrier takes possession of the scrap tires shall be indicated on the manifest.
(c)
Tire handling businesses. All tire handling businesses shall maintain an accurate inventory of all new and used tires received at the place of business, sold to a consumer, sent to another tire handling businesses, or shipped to an approved processor. Inventory and quarterly transport reports shall be kept on site and made available for inspection by environmental code enforcement officers.
Quarterly reports shall be sent to the city director of public works and shall include the following information:
(1)
Name of company that transported tires;
(2)
Name of owner of company and driver of transport vehicle;
(3)
Telephone number of transport company;
(4)
Valid environmental protection division scrap tire program transportation identification number; and
(5)
Type of vehicle used to transport tires.
( Ord. No. O-2020-38 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-17-20)
Sec. 70-157. - Enforcement.
(a)
Enforcement of this article shall be the responsibility of the city community development director or his designee.
(b)
The environmental code enforcement officers are authorized to enforce this article on behalf of the city, and shall be empowered to enter private property, with the owner's consent, at reasonable times in order to inspect the property for violation of this article. If the owner does not consent to such entry on private property for inspection, such officer shall obtain a warrant to enter upon the property.
( Ord. No. O-2020-38 , § 1(Exh. A), 8-17-20)
