Sec. 16-1. - Smoking in public buildings.
(a)
The board of commissioners has determined the following:
(1)
The inhalation of smoke is hazardous to human health;
(2)
Most public buildings are now considered smoke-free zones;
(3)
There are areas outside the public buildings in the county for smokers; and
(4)
It would be in the best interest of county employees, county citizens and others using county public buildings to have a no smoking policy inside of such buildings.
(b)
The board of commissioners resolves that all public buildings in the county shall be considered smoke-free zones.
(c)
The board of commissioners further resolves that "no smoking" signs or notices shall be posted inside all county offices and public buildings.
(Code 2000, § 38-1; Res. of 2-1-1999)
