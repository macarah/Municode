DIVISION 5. - ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT COUNCIL

Sec. 2-161. - Findings.
(a)
The chamber of commerce and the development authority of the county recognize the need for improving the economic well-being of the county through the attraction and expansion of business, commercial and industrial development.
(b)
There is a need for an economic development council to undertake the responsibility and assist the development authority, the chamber of commerce and local governmental entities in seeking new industry and assisting existing industry to expand.
(c)
Representatives of the development authority, chamber of commerce, and local governments mentioned in this section have met and deliberated at length on these matters.
(Res. of 7-20-1993)
Sec. 2-162. - Created; board of directors.
The chamber of commerce hereby creates an economic development council. The council shall function as an autonomous body under the direction of a nine member board of directors composed of the following members: the commissioner, the Mayor of the City of Jasper, one representative from the development authority, one representative from the chamber of commerce, and five private sector representatives to be selected by the first four named representatives to serve two year terms with no limitation upon reappointment.
(Res. of 7-20-1993)
Sec. 2-163. - Powers and duties.
The council shall have the following powers and duties:
(1)
All authority, powers, and duties as set forth in that certain service contract of even date between the development authority and the chamber of commerce, such contract being attached hereto and made a part hereof; and
(2)
The authority to do what is necessary, proper and convenient to carry out its purpose as stated in this division and in such service contract.
(Res. of 7-20-1993)
Editor's note— An official copy of the service contract dated July 20, 1993, referred to in this section is on file in the office of the county clerk where it is available for reference and review during normal business hours.

DIVISION 6. - JOINT DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY[6]
Footnotes:
--- (6) ---
State Law reference— Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.

Subdivision 1. - Bartow County

Sec. 2-186. - Activation of the authority.
It is hereby declared that there is a need for a joint development authority to function in Bartow County and Pickens County. Such authority, to be known as the "Joint Development Authority of Bartow County and Pickens County," is hereby created and activated. Such authority, called the authority in this division, shall transact business pursuant to and exercise the powers provided by the provisions of, the Development Authorities Law, as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq.
(Res. of 12-22-1994, § 1)
Sec. 2-187. - Joint authority.
The authority is created and activated by this proper resolution of the governing bodies of Bartow County and Pickens County as a joint authority pursuant to provisions of 1981 Ga. Laws, page 1419 and O.C.G.A. § 36-62-5.1.
(Res. of 12-22-1994, § 2)
Sec. 2-188. - Directors.
(a)
The authority shall consist of seven directors. Three directors shall be taxpayers residing in Bartow County and shall be appointed by the Commissioner of Bartow County initially, as provided in this resolution and, thereafter, as their terms expire. Four directors shall be taxpayers residing in Pickens County and shall be appointed by the Commissioner of Pickens County initially, as provided in this resolution and, thereafter, as their terms expire. No director shall be an officer or employee of Bartow County or Pickens County. The directors shall receive no compensation for their services, but shall be reimbursed for their actual expenses incurred in the performance of their duties. The initial directors shall serve for terms of two and four years.
(b)
After expiration of the initial terms, the terms of office of all members shall be terms of six years. Successors to persons listed in subsection (a) of this section shall be named by the governing body of the county making the original appointment. If at the end of any term of any member a successor to such member has not been appointed, the member whose term of office has expired shall continue to hold office until his successor is appointed. A majority of the members of the authority shall constitute a quorum, but no action may be taken by the authority without the affirmative vote of a majority of the full membership of the authority.
(Res. of 12-22-1994, § 3)
Sec. 2-189. - Officers.
The directors shall elect one of their members as chairperson and another as vice-chairperson and shall also elect a secretary and a treasurer or a secretary-treasurer, either of whom may, but need not be, a director.
(Res. of 12-22-1994, § 4)
Sec. 2-190. - Articles of organization.
The commissioner of each such county is hereby authorized to execute "articles of organization" for such joint authority, such articles of organization to be entered upon the minutes of the respective commissioners.
(Res. of 12-22-1994, § 7)
Sec. 2-191. - Reserved.

(a)
The county currently owns and operates a county water system.

DIVISION 9. - AREA PLANNING AND DEVELOPMENT COMMISSION[8]

Footnotes:
--- (8) ---
Cross reference— Planning, ch. 50.
State Law reference— Automatic membership of counties and municipalities in regional development centers, O.C.G.A. § 50-8-33; area planning and development commissions succeeded by regional development centers, O.C.G.A. § 50-8-41.

Sec. 2-261. - Findings.
(a)
It appears to be advantageous to the welfare of this county that a comprehensive and continuous planning program be undertaken.
(b)
The commissioner needs an area planning commission to assist in guiding and accomplishing a coordinated and harmonious development of the county and North Georgia which will, in accordance with existing and future needs, best promote public health, safety, morals, order, convenience, prosperity, and the general welfare, as well as efficiency and economy in the process of development.
(Res. of 1-5-1970)
Sec. 2-262. - Established.
The commissioner in cooperation with the governing authorities of other area counties and municipalities, hereby establishes the North Georgia Area Planning and Development Commission, in this section called the commission to be governed by the provisions enumerated in this division.
(Res. of 1-5-1970)
Sec. 2-263. - Membership.
The North Georgia Area Planning and Development Commission shall consist of four members from each of the counties participating in the area planning commission. Of the four members from each county, two shall be appointed by the governing authority of the municipality wherein the county seat is located and the other two to be appointed by the governing authority of such county.
(Res. of 1-5-1970, § 1)
Sec. 2-264. - Term of office.
The term of office for all members shall be for five years. Any vacancy in membership shall be filled for the unexpired term by the governing authority making the appointment which has become vacant. The appointing authority shall also have the power to remove any member it may appoint for cause, on written charges, after public hearing. All members shall serve without compensation.
(Res. of 1-5-1970, § 2)
Sec. 2-265. - Organization, rules, staff and finances.
The commission shall elect a chairperson and a vice-chairperson from among its appointive members. The term of office of the chairperson and vice-chairperson shall be for one year or until reelected or their successors are elected. The commission shall appoint a secretary who may be an employee of the commission. The commission shall meet at least once each month at the call of the chairperson and at such other times as the chairperson or board may determine, shall adopt rules for the transaction of business and shall keep a record of its resolutions, transactions, findings, and determinations, which record shall be a public record.
(Res. of 1-5-1970, § 3)
Sec. 2-266. - Powers and duties.
(a)
The commission may appoint such employees and staff as it may deem necessary for its work. In the performance of its duties, the planning commission may cooperate with, contract with, or accept funds from federal, state, or local public or semipublic agencies or private individuals or corporations, may spend such funds, and may carry out such cooperative undertakings and contracts. It may make expenditures for the purchase of required equipment and supplies. The expenditures of the commission, exclusive of gifts to the commission and contract receipts, shall be within the amounts appropriated for the purpose by the participating governing authorities.
(b)
It shall be the function and duty of the commission to make such careful and comprehensive surveys and studies of existing conditions and probable future developments and to prepare such plans for physical, social, and economic growth as will best promote the public health, safety, morals, convenience, prosperity, or the general welfare as well as efficiency and economy in the development of the entire North Georgia area and each political subdivision in the area. In general, the commission shall have such powers and duties and responsibilities as set forth in the General Planning Enabling Act of 1957, as amended (Act No. 358).
(Res. of 1-5-1970, §§ 3, 4)
Secs. 2-267—2-270. - Reserved.
DIVISION 10. - PICKENS COUNTY PLANNING COMMISSION

Sec. 2-271. - Membership.
The planning commission shall consist of seven members. The terms of the appointed members shall be for four years, except that in the appointment of the first planning commission under the terms of this division, one member shall be appointed for four years; one member shall be appointed for three years; two members shall be appointed for two years; and the remaining members shall be appointed for one year. Members of the planning commission may be reappointed for succeeding terms. Any vacancy in the planning commission membership shall be filled for the unexpired term by the Pickens County Commissioner, which shall also have the authority to remove any members for cause upon written charges.
All members may be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in connection with their official duties and may also receive a per diem upon approval by the Pickens County Commissioner.
All planning commission members are encouraged to attend planning related training opportunities available from such organizations as the Georgia Planning Association, the Regional Development Center, Georgia Department of Community Affairs, universities and similar organizations whenever it is appropriate to the activities of the planning commission.
(Ord. of 12-10-99, § 1)
Sec. 2-272. - Organization, rules, staff and finances.
The planning commission shall elect its chairman and vice-chairman from among its appointive members. The term of the chairman and vice chairman shall be one year or until they are reelected or their successors are elected. The planning commission shall appoint a secretary who may be an officer or employee of the county or the planning commission. The planning commission shall make its own bylaws, rules of procedure, and shall determine its time of meeting. All meetings and records of the planning commission shall be subject to Georgia laws regarding open meetings and public records.
The planning commission may appoint such employees and staff as it may deem necessary for its work and may cooperate with, contract with, or accept funds from Federal. State, or local, public or semipublic agencies or private individuals or corporations; may expend such funds; and may carry out such cooperative undertakings and contracts. The expenditures of the planning commission, exclusive of gifts or contract receipts, shall be within the amounts appropriated for the purpose by the Pickens County Commissioner.
(Ord. of 12-10-99, § 2)
Sec. 2-273. - Powers and duties.
From and after the time when the planning commission shall have organized and selected its officers, and shall have adopted its rules of procedure, then said planning commission shall have the following powers, duties, and responsibilities:
(1)
To make careful and comprehensive surveys and studies of existing conditions and probable future developments and to prepare amendments or updates to the Joint Comprehensive Plan for Pickens County, which was adopted on July 30, 1993, also known as the official plan, and a short term work program update adopted December 23, 1998; for physical, social, environmental, and economic growth as will promote the development of Pickens County; such plan(s), with accompanying maps, plats, charts, and descriptive materials, shall show the planning commission's recommendations for the physical development and uses of land, shall meet the minimum requirements of the Georgia Planning Act of 1989 and related minimum planning standards and procedures promulgated by the state, and may include other matters of special interest to Pickens County;
(2)
To prepare and recommend to the Pickens County Commissioner from time to time, such changes in the plan or any part thereof as may be deemed necessary by the commissioner or by the planning commission; to prepare and recommend to the Pickens County Commissioner from time to time plans and/or recommendations for specific improvements in pursuance of the official plan;
(3)
To cooperate with other municipal or regional planning commissions and other agencies or groups to further the local planning program and to assure harmonious and integrated planning for the area;
(4)
To prepare and recommend for adoption by the Pickens County Commissioner amendments to the Pickens County Land Development Ordinance, which may deal with such matters as establishing land use districts and regulations, regulations for the subdivision of land, sign controls, flood protection, environmental protection, soil erosion and sediment control, manufactured home development standards, and other regulations that would be for the benefit of managing growth in Pickens County; and to oversee the administration of the regulations that may be adopted or amended;
(5)
To prepare and recommend for adoption by the Pickens County Commissioner an official map showing the location of existing and proposed streets, public open spaces, and public building sites within Pickens County;
(6)
To delegate to employees authority to perform ministerial acts, in all cases except where final action of the planning commission is necessary;
(7)
If deemed advisable, to establish an advisory committee or committees; and,
(8)
To arrange or conduct any form of publicity relative to planning commission activities for the general purpose of public understanding,
(Ord. of 12-10-99, § 3)

ARTICLE V. - COUNTY MARSHALS OFFICE

Sec. 2-321. - County marshal's office.
modified
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 15-10-100(c)(1), power is given to the Pickens County Board of Commissioners to establish and maintain a county marshal's office. The primary duty of the county marshal's office is the enforcement of all ordinances of Pickens County for proper prosecution and sentencing of violations of such ordinances in the county magistrate court. Other lawful duties may be imposed on the county marshal's office at the discretion of the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 1-10-2003; Res. of 10-6-2022(1))
Sec. 2-322. - County marshal's office personnel.
new
With the concurrence of the chairman of the board of commissioners and within the allowable confines of the applicable annual budget of the county as approved by the board of commissioners, the county marshal's office may employ a chief county marshal, deputy county marshals and code enforcement officers, including animal control officers.
(Res. of 10-6-2022(1))
Sec. 2-323. - Chief county marshal.
new
(a)
The chief county marshal shall be responsible for the day-to-day operations of the county marshal's office.
(b)
The chief county marshal shall report to the designee of the board of commissioners.
(c)
The chief county marshal will be subject to standards of the O.C.G.A. tit. 35, ch. 8 and shall be POST certified.
(d)
The chief county marshal must be at least 21 years of age, have a high school diploma or GED and possess a valid Georgia state driver's license.
(e)
The chief county marshal must be of good moral character and standing in the community and not have been convicted of a felony.
(f)
At his or her discretion, the sitting sheriff of Pickens County may administer the oath of office to the chief county marshal, thereby lawfully bestowing such powers as specified by the by applicable laws of the State of Georgia.
(g)
The powers of the chief county marshal shall be as follows in accordance with applicable provisions of the Constitution of the State of Georgia and other applicable laws of the State of Georgia:
(1)
To enforce all ordinances enacted by the board of commissioners, including the provisions of chapter 14, animals.
(2)
To issue and serve citations for court appearances for the violation of county ordinances or, at the discretion of the chief county marshal, to issue warnings regarding the violation of county ordinances in lieu of citations.
(3)
To investigate and collect evidence of ordinance violations.
(4)
To respond to complaints of alleged ordinance violations.
(5)
To appear and give testimony in any court having jurisdiction of any cases involving the prosecution of ordinance violations.
(6)
To coordinate enforcement efforts with the planning and development department of the county and other applicable county departments.
(7)
To perform any other duties, including arrests for state law violations, which are permissible and allowed under O.C.G.A. § 36-8-5 as may be lawful and applicable in conjunction with the primary purpose of the county marshal's office.
(8)
To assist as needed in the county's response to disasters and other large-scale emergency response efforts.
(9)
To assist the Pickens County Sheriff's Office as requested by the Pickens County Sheriff.
(10)
To perform any other lawful duties required by the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 10-6-2022(1))
Sec. 2-324. - Deputy county marshals.
new
(a)
With the concurrence of the chairman of the board of commissioners and within the allowable confines of the applicable annual budget of the county as approved by the board of commissioners, the chief county marshal may employ deputy county marshals.
(b)
Deputy county marshals will be subject to standards of the O.C.G.A. tit. 35, ch. 8 and shall be POST certified.
(c)
Deputy county marshals must be at least 21 years of age, have a high school diploma or GED and possess a valid Georgia state driver's license.
(d)
Deputy county marshals must be of good moral character and standing in the community and not have been convicted of a felony.
(e)
At his or her discretion, the sitting Sheriff of Pickens County may administer the oath of office to deputy county marshals, thereby lawfully bestowing such powers as specified by the by applicable of the State of Georgia.
(f)
Solely at the direction of the chief county marshal, deputy county marshals shall assist the chief county marshal in the conduct of the operations of the county marshal's office and shall therefore have the following powers in accordance with applicable provisions of the Constitution of the State of Georgia and other applicable laws of the State of Georgia when acting at the direction of the chief county marshal:
(1)
To enforce all ordinances enacted by the board of commissioners, including the provisions of chapter 14, animals.
(2)
To issue and serve citations for court appearances for the violation of county ordinances or, at the discretion of the deputy county marshal, to issue warnings regarding the violation of county ordinances in lieu of citations.
(3)
To investigate and collect evidence of ordinance violations.
(4)
To respond to complaints of alleged ordinance violations.
(5)
To appear and give testimony in any court having jurisdiction of any cases involving the prosecution of ordinance violations.
(6)
To coordinate enforcement efforts with the planning and development department of the county and other applicable county departments.
(7)
To perform any other duties, including arrests for state law violations, which are permissible and allowed under O.C.G.A. § 36-8-5 as may be lawful and applicable in conjunction with the primary purpose of the county marshal's office.
(8)
To perform any other duties as constables as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 15-10-102.
(9)
To assist as needed in the county's response to disasters and other large-scale emergency response efforts.
(10)
To assist the Pickens County Sheriff's Office as requested by the Pickens County Sheriff or the chief county marshal.
(11)
To perform any other lawful duties required by the chief county marshal.
(Res. of 10-6-2022(1))
Sec. 2-325. - Code enforcement officers, including animal control officers.
new
(a)
With the concurrence of the chairman of the board of commissioners and within the allowable confines of the applicable annual budget of the county as approved by the board of commissioners, the chief county marshal may employ code enforcement officers, including animal control officers.
(b)
Code enforcement officers shall not be subject to standards of the O.C.G.A. tit. 35, ch. 8.
(c)
Code enforcement officers must be at least 18 years of age, have a high school diploma or GED and possess a valid Georgia state driver's license.
(d)
Code enforcement officers must be of good moral character and standing in the community and not have been convicted of a felony.
(e)
Solely at the direction of the chief county marshal and deputy county marshals, code enforcement officers shall assist the chief county marshal and deputy county marshals in the conduct of the operations of the county marshal's office and shall therefore have the following authorization and responsibilities in accordance with this article:
(1)
To enforce all ordinances enacted by the board of commissioners, including the provisions of chapter 14, animals
(2)
To issue and serve citations for court appearances for the violation of county ordinances or, at the discretion of the code enforcement officer, to issue warnings regarding the violation of county ordinances in lieu of citations.
(3)
To investigate and collect evidence of ordinance violations.
(4)
To respond to complaints of alleged ordinance violations.
(5)
To appear and give testimony in any court having jurisdiction of any cases involving the prosecution of ordinance violations.
(6)
