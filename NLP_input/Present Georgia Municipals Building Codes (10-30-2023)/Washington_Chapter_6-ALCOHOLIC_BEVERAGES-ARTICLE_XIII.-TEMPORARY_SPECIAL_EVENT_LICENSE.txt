ARTICLE XIII. - TEMPORARY SPECIAL EVENT LICENSE

Sec. 6-160. - Responsible party designation.
A bona fide nonprofit civic organization wishing to apply for and receive a temporary special event license shall designate a responsible party who shall be a member in good standing of said bona fide nonprofit civic organization. The responsible party shall make the application for said temporary special event license, and the temporary special event license shall be issued in the responsible party's name on behalf of the bona fide nonprofit civic organization. The responsible party shall be individually responsible for any and all claims, losses or liability arising out of or connected with the temporary special event, whether in contract or in tort (including negligence), law or equity.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-161. - Temporary special event license application forms.
(a)
All bona fide nonprofit civic organizations desiring to receive a temporary special event license shall make application on a form prescribed by the city clerk or his/her designee. No application will be processed until the responsible party has completed the required interview with the city clerk or his/her designee.
(b)
The application shall include, but shall not be limited to:
(1)
The name and address of the responsible party;
(2)
The date, address, beginning time and ending time of the temporary special event;
(3)
A description of the premises at which said temporary special event shall occur (said description shall be an aerial map, a description of the metes and bounds, or other such specific descriptions as will allow the city to determine the boundaries of said premises).
(4)
A copy of the 501(c)(3) letter issued by the IRS for the bona fide nonprofit civic organization;
(5)
A copy of the by-laws and corporate charter from the secretary of state's office for the bona fide nonprofit civic organization;
(6)
A copy of the special event non-profit permit issued by the state for said temporary special event;
(7)
A resolution issued by the bona fide nonprofit civic organization authorizing the temporary special event; and
(8)
Written documentation evidencing a partnership between the bona fide nonprofit civic organization and an alcoholic beverage license holder who has been granted an off-premises consumption license.
(c)
The responsible party shall furnish data, information and records as required by the city clerk or his/her designee and to ensure compliance with the provisions of this chapter. Failure to furnish data as requested shall cause the application to be denied.
(d)
A complete set of fingerprints of the responsible party for a new temporary special event license shall be required.
(1)
Authority. This subsection is enacted pursuant to O.C.G.A. ยง 35-3-35(a)(1.2), the Georgia Umbrella Statute entitled "Dissemination of information to public agencies, political subdivisions, authorities, and instrumentalities."
(2)
Fingerprinting required. An individual seeking to receive a temporary special event license shall be fingerprinted as a condition of submitting an application for a temporary special event license. The responsible party shall be required to provide fingerprints each time an application is submitted, unless that same responsible party has already provided fingerprints to the city within the same calendar year. By filing such application, the applicant consents to the city obtaining their criminal history record information (CHRI) from the Georgia Crime Information Center (GCIC) and the Federal Bureau of Investigation (F.B.I.).
(3)
Access authorized; usage regulations.
a.
The city is hereby authorized to access national criminal history record information pursuant to O.C.G.A. ยง 35-3-35(a)(1.2).
b.
Information obtained and handled for any purposes pursuant to this subsection shall comply with all Georgia laws and the Federal Privacy Act.
c.
No information given as part of the request for a criminal history and no record obtained pursuant to this section may be entered on any database.
d.
No information given or obtained pursuant to this section shall be subject to the provisions of the Georgia Open Records Act.
(d)
Administration; fees; procedures.
(1)
The applicant shall provide his/her fingerprints, which may be taken by the county sheriff's department or may be taken at any Georgia applicant processing services location, so long as the applicant registers in such a manner that allows the county sheriff's department to receive the criminal history results.
(2)
The applicant shall bear the cost of the electronic fingerprinting submission services set by the Georgia applicant processing services and an administrative fee allowed by statute to be charged by the city, which shall be the maximum allowed by statute, which fee shall include fingerprinting when carried out by the county sheriff's department.
(3)
Upon receipt of the fingerprints and the appropriate fees, the city will transmit said fingerprints and appropriate fees (unless a satisfactory billing arrangement has been entered into between the sheriff's department and the GCIC and/or FBI) to the GCIC. As provided by law, the GCIC will compare the subject's fingerprints against its criminal file and submit the fingerprints to the F.B.I. for a comparison with nation-wide records, unless submission to the F.B.I. is automatic pursuant to the use of live-scan. The results of the F.B.I. check will be returned based on its current procedure, presently being directly to the sheriff's department if submissions are made manually to the GCIC, or electronically from both the OBI and F.B.I. where submissions are made electronically to the GCIC.
(4)
In compliance with federal law 95-544, which provides for the rendering of a "fitness determination," the city will decide whether the applicant has been convicted of, or is under pending indictment for enumerated disqualifiers, as set forth within this chapter.
(5)
A person who has consented to the city for a criminal history based on fingerprinting record may request and receive a copy of the criminal history record report from the city at no additional charge. Should the person seek to amend or correct the record, he or she shall be responsible for contacting the GCIC as to Georgia records and/or the F.B.I. concerning records from other jurisdictions maintained in its file.
(e)
All applications shall be sworn to by the applicant before a notary public or other officer authorized to administer oaths.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-162. - Temporary special event license.
(a)
In order to distribute or sell alcoholic beverages at a temporary special event, a bona fide nonprofit civic organization shall be required to:
(1)
Apply to the city for an alcoholic beverage temporary special event license; and
(2)
Pay an alcoholic beverage temporary special event license fee according to the schedule approved from time to time by the city council.
(b)
The city clerk or his/her designee shall review the responsible party's application and supporting documentation and shall evaluate the application based upon the following factors:
(1)
The city clerk or his/her designee's perception of whether the event can be conducted safely;
(2)
Whether the county sheriff's department can adequately provide security for said temporary special event;
(3)
The number of people anticipated to be attending the temporary special event;
(4)
The geographic location of the premises upon which the temporary special event will be held;
(5)
The impact of the temporary special event on surrounding businesses or residents; and
(6)
Whether the responsible party has failed to meet any other criteria specified by this chapter or state law.
(c)
The city clerk or his/her designee shall have discretion when evaluating the aforementioned factors and shall either approve or deny, in writing, said temporary special event license application.
(d)
All temporary special event license applications must be submitted at least 30 calendar days prior to the planned special event.
(e)
No more than two temporary special event licenses may be issued to a bona fide nonprofit civic organization in any one calendar year.
(f)
A list of all donated alcoholic beverages from the wholesaler must be maintained by the temporary special event license holder at all times.
(g)
The restrictions set forth in the section 6-13, hours of operation, shall apply to all sales of alcoholic beverages at authorized temporary special events.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
