ARTICLE II. - CITY COUNCIL
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-19. - Members; authority.
The city is governed by the city council consisting of the mayor and six councilmembers who are vested with all corporate, legislative and other powers of the city as permitted by the city Charter and by law. The council shall be the final judge of the election and qualifications of its members; also of the amount and manner of compensating councilmembers for their services, to the extent permitted by the city Charter and state law.
(Code 2011, § 2-19)
Sec. 2-20. - Election; term; vacancies.
Election of the mayor and councilmembers shall be as provided in the city Charter or by general law. Vacancies in office shall be filled as provided in the city Charter or by general law.
(Code 2011, § 2-20)
Sec. 2-21. - Compensation; reimbursement of expenses.
The mayor and councilmembers shall receive such compensation as may be established from time to time by ordinance of the city council, together with reimbursement of their actual and necessary expenses incurred in the performance of their duties. Expense reimbursements shall be subject to approval by the city council.
(Code 2011, § 2-21)
Sec. 2-22. - Organizational meeting; election of mayor pro tem.
At their first regular meeting in each year, the mayor and council shall elect a mayor pro tem who shall have full authority to discharge all the duties of the mayor, whenever from physical or mental disability or absence from the city, or any other cause, the mayor is unable to act.
(Code 2011, § 2-22)
Sec. 2-23. - Oath of office.
Each councilmember, before entering upon the discharge of his duties, shall take an oath in the form prescribed by the city council.
(Code 2011, § 2-23)
Sec. 2-24. - Duties of councilmembers.
It shall be the duty of each councilmember to faithfully discharge his duties as chair or member of such committees as he shall be appointed on, and to perform such other and all duties as may be required of him by the Charter, state law, and this Code.
(Code 2011, § 2-24)
Secs. 2-25—2-51. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - AUTHORITY AND DUTIES

Sec. 2-52. - General authority of council.
The legislative authority of the city is vested by the Charter and general law in the city council, and the council is authorized to exercise those powers by duly established ordinance.
(Code 2011, § 2-54)
Sec. 2-53. - Authority to organize and reorganize city administrative structure.
The city council may by ordinance establish, abolish, merge, or consolidate offices, positions of employment, departments, and agencies of the city, provide that the same person shall fill a number of offices and positions of employment, and transfer or change the functions and duties of various offices, positions of employment, and departments and agencies of the city.
(Code 2011, § 2-55)

Sec. 2-80. - Quorum; tie votes.
Four councilmembers shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of any business before the city council, and the majority of the votes cast shall determine all questions and elections before the council. On all questions before the city council, the mayor, or the mayor pro tem, if he is presiding, shall be entitled to vote only in case of a tie.
(Code 1999, § 2-101; Code 2011, § 2-85)
Sec. 2-81. - Regular meetings.
The council shall hold regular meetings at least once in every month at stated times at the council chamber in the city, and shall hold such called meetings as may be ordered by the mayor, or, in his absence or sickness, by the mayor pro tem, for special purposes, or when requested to by a majority of the councilmembers. All meetings by the council shall be public, and the public shall be allowed at all times to witness its deliberations, except when the city council resolves itself into executive session, as authorized by law, when the public shall be excluded.
(Code 1999, § 2-101; Code 2011, § 2-86)
Sec. 2-82. - Order of business.
At each meeting of the mayor and council, the following regular order of business shall be observed, unless dispensed with by a majority vote of the members present:
(1)
Call to order by the mayor.
(2)
Reading of minutes of the previous meeting by the city clerk for correction and approval.
(3)
Grievances from citizens.
(4)
Approval of bills for payment.
(5)
Review of financial statements.
(6)
Communications from the mayor.
(7)
Reports from committees, members of the mayor and council, and other offices.
(8)
Old business.
(9)
New business.
(10)
Adjournment.
(Code 1999, § 2-103; Code 2011, § 2-87)
Sec. 2-83. - General rules of order.
Except as otherwise provided in the Charter or this Code, the rules of order and parliamentary procedure contained in Robert's Rules of Order, newly revised, shall govern the transaction of business by and before the mayor and council at its meetings in all cases to which they are applicable.
(Code 1999, § 2-104; Code 2011, § 2-88)
Sec. 2-84. - Disturbing meetings.
It shall be unlawful to engage in disorderly or contemptuous behavior which intentionally disturbs any meeting of the mayor and council.
(Code 1999, § 2-105; Code 2011, § 2-89)
Sec. 2-85. - Minutes of meetings.
(a)
Book One. All minutes recorded in the traditional journal shall stand as the official record of the meetings of the council through the date of the last entry therein posted. A final entry shall be made in the traditional journal to identify the location of all subsequent meeting minutes. This journal shall be designated as Book One.
(b)
Book Two. All minutes recorded by the prior city clerk but not posted to the journal for approximately the last 13 years shall be placed in a distinguished folder or folders appropriately marked as "Official Minutes of Council, City of Cochran, Georgia." These minutes shall stand as the official record of city council from the date of the last entry into the traditional journal and shall include the last record of the council's meeting in 1999. These records shall be known as Book Two, containing minutes from the last date in Book One through December 31, 1999. Book Two shall have as a final comment the location of all subsequent records.
(c)
Book Three. All records of council meetings beginning January 1, 2000, shall be filed chronologically in an appropriate folder which shall be marked "Official Minutes of Council, City of Cochran, Georgia." Such folders shall have the inclusive dates of the council meeting minutes which it contains appropriately marked on the cover of the folder used. Such folders will have a method of binding the minutes therein to secure and retain the official record of actions by the mayor and council. These records shall be known as Book Three. Other books will sequentially follow as each folder reaches capacity, and each book shall include as a final comment the location of all subsequent minutes.
(d)
Seal and certification. After approval by the council, each record of minutes shall be embossed with the seal of the city and be certified by signature of the city clerk as an accurate account of such meetings.
(Code 2011, § 2-90; Ord. No. 1010-2, 12-21-1999)
Sec. 2-86. - Passage of legislation.
(a)
Every official act of the city council having the force and effect of law shall be by ordinance; all other actions may take such form as determined by rules or resolutions of the mayor and council.
(b)
Any member of the city council shall have the right to require the yeas and nays to be recorded on the minutes in all cases.
(Code 1999, § 2-106; Code 2011, § 2-91)
Sec. 2-87. - Effective date of legislation.
Except as otherwise provided by any ordinance or state law:
(1)
All ordinances and resolutions not vetoed by the mayor shall be effective five days after passage thereof.
(2)
All ordinances and resolutions vetoed by the mayor shall be effective immediately upon passage by a vote of five councilmembers in the alternative not later than the second regular meeting after such veto.
(Code 1999, § 2-107; Code 2011, § 2-92)
Sec. 2-88. - Official copy of ordinance.
A true copy of an ordinance which has been duly enacted by the city council, attested to by the city clerk, and signed by the city councilmembers shall be known as an official copy of an ordinance of the city.
(Code 1999, § 2-108; Code 2011, § 2-93)
Sec. 2-89. - Ordinances appropriating money.
No appropriation ordinance or ordinance to alter or repeal an appropriation ordinance shall be enacted at any meeting other than a regular meeting or at a meeting called and announced at a regular meeting except by a unanimous vote of the full membership of the council.
(Code 1999, § 2-109; Code 2011, § 2-94)
Secs. 2-90—2-106. - Reserved.
