Sec. 24-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Applicable codes means:
(1)
Any optional housing or abatement standard provided in O.C.G.A. § 8-2-1 et seq., as adopted by ordinance or operation of law, or general nuisance law, relative to the safe use of real property;
(2)
Any fire or life safety code as provided for in O.C.G.A. § 25-2-1 et seq.;
(3)
Any minimum standard codes provided in O.C.G.A. § 8-2-1 et seq., provided that such building or minimum standard codes for real property improvements shall be deemed to mean those building or minimum standard codes in existence at the time such real property improvements were constructed unless otherwise provided by law;
(4)
The 2012 Edition of the International Property Maintenance Code and its amendments, as set forth by the state department of community affairs; and
(5)
The 2012 Edition of the International Existing Building Code is adopted by reference as though copied fully herein.
Building means any structure that encloses a space used for sheltering any occupancy.
Debris includes residue from the burning of wood, coal, or other combustible material, as well as paper, rags, cartons, boxes, wood, rubber, leather, fallen, broken or destroyed trees or tree limbs or other material or objects not a part of the land and constituting a health or safety hazard.
Inoperable motor vehicle means any motor vehicle as defined herein which is incapable of being moved under its own power without additional work or repairs being completed to the motor vehicle, or without any additional components being added to or placed upon said motor vehicle.
Junked motor vehicle is any motor vehicle, as defined in this section, which does not have lawfully affixed thereto both an unexpired license plate and a current license decal, or the condition of such motor vehicle is in a state of disrepair, wrecked, dismantled, partially dismantled, inoperative, abandoned or discarded. The absence of a current car tag or decal from any vehicle shall be presumptive evidence that such vehicle is a junked motor vehicle.
Lot means one or any one or more of several contiguous parcels of land making up a block, measured and set apart for use or occupancy.
Motor vehicle means any vehicle which is or is intended to be self-propelled and designed to travel on the ground and shall include, but shall not be limited to, automobiles, buses, motorbikes, motorcycles, motor scooters, trucks, tractors, go-carts, golf carts, four-wheelers, campers and trailers, as well as any part thereof.
Private property means any real property within the city which is privately-owned and which is not public property as defined in this section.
Public property means any street or highway which shall include the entire width between the boundary lines of every publicly-maintained way and dedicated to the purposes of vehicular traffic, and shall also mean any other publicly-owned property or facility.
Unlicensed vehicle means any car, truck van, motor home, camper, trailer, motorcycle or other vehicle not displaying a proper, valid/current proof of licensing from the state of license plate issuance or any car, truck, van, motor home, camper, trailer, motorcycle or other vehicle that is not bearing a valid/current license plate.
Weeds means all objectionable growth, including weeds and grasses which serve as a breeding place for mosquitoes and other unhealthy or undesirable insects, or as a refuge for snakes, rats or other rodents, or that create a fire or traffic hazard or provide a hiding place for persons; provided, however, that flowers, shrubbery, trees and other growth used as a part of landscaping shall not be included in this definition.
Wooded areas means those portions of lots consisting of a dense growth of trees, which exceed three inches in diameter.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 38-26; Ord. No. 05082017(1), 5-8-2017 )
Sec. 24-2. - Specific nuisances.
The various nuisances described and enumerated in this section shall not be deemed to be exclusive, but shall be in addition to all other nuisances described and prohibited in this Code. The term "nuisance" means anything within the city or its police jurisdiction that causes hurt, inconvenience or damage to others or is manifestly injurious to the public health or safety or tends to corrupt the manners or morals of the people. The following are hereby declared to be nuisances:
(1)
Things interfering with peace or comfort. Sounds, animals or things which interfere with the peace or comfort or disturb the quiet of the community.
(2)
Obnoxious, offensive odors. The emission of obnoxious and offensive odors; the tainting of the air rendering it offensive or unwholesome so as to affect the health or comfort of reasonable persons residing in the neighborhood thereof.
(3)
Discharging of offensive matter. The placing or throwing or discharging from any house or premises and flow from or out of any house or premises, of any filthy, foul or offensive matter or liquid of any kind, into any street, alley or public place, or upon any adjacent lot or ground.
(4)
Water pollution. The obstruction or pollution of any watercourse or source of water supply in the city.
(5)
Stagnant water. Any stagnant pool of water (excluding city-owned holding ponds) in the city.
(6)
Vacant lots. Any vacant lot whereon debris is permitted to accumulate and remain in such a manner as to create a fire hazard or other hazard to the public health, safety and welfare.
(7)
Nonconforming structures, machines, etc. Any building, business, thing, machine or machinery, erected, repaired, conducted, maintained, operated or used within the city contrary to or in violation of any of the fire and safety regulations of this Code, state law or city ordinance.
(8)
Animal enclosures. Any enclosure in which any animals are kept, dog kennels or runs and other animal or fowl pens wherein manure, dung, filth or litter is allowed to accumulate.
(9)
Privies. The construction or maintenance of any surface closet, privy or privy vault on any premises within the city.
(10)
Dead animals. The carcass of any dead animal of any kind on any premises within the city.
(11)
Depositing trash, garbage, refuse, furniture, or other household goods, etc., on private or public property. The depositing and leaving on private or public property of trash, garbage, refuse, furniture, household goods, scrap building materials, paper, cardboard containers, brick, cement rubbish, tree residue, cans, containers, or any other rubbish and trash which is a menace to public health and safety in the city.
(12)
Accumulation of junk. The retention, storage or accumulation of any automobile, motor vehicle, mechanized equipment, machinery, appliance, scrap metal, or other scrap material on property for a period of ten days or longer in a condition that prevents its use for the purpose for which it was originally manufactured so as to affect the public health, safety and welfare of reasonable persons residing in the neighborhood thereof.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 38-27; Ord. No. 05082017(1), 5-8-2017 )
Sec. 24-3. - Weeds, debris, parking or storage of unlicensed, inoperable vehicles, trailers or equipment, junked motor vehicles; prohibition exception.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for the occupant of any lot of land or premises in the city, or the owner of any lot of land or premises in the city, or any agent or representative of such owner or occupant, to permit or maintain on such lot any nuisance as enumerated herein, the growth thereon of objectionable weeds, the accumulation of debris or the harborage of inoperable motor vehicles, as defined in this article, which distracts from the community aesthetics, and endangers the public health, safety or welfare. The following conditions shall constitute a nuisance as it relates to the growth of objectionable weeds:
(1)
Any lot on which a residential structure is built shall be deemed a nuisance if the growth of weeds as defined herein is 12 inches or more in height.
(2)
Any vacant lot located within a residential district or area shall be deemed a nuisance if the growth of weeds as defined herein exceeds 18 inches or more in height.
(3)
Any lot or property other than those described above shall be deemed a nuisance if the growth of weeds as defined herein exceeds 18 inches in height.
Exception: For the purpose of this article, wooded areas as defined herein shall be exempt from the above requirements.
(b)
This article defines and establishes procedures for abating inoperable, unlicensed or junked motor vehicles. It shall be unlawful for any person, firm, corporation or partnership to allow any motor vehicle of any kind without valid/current license plate and decals, or any vehicles, trailers or equipment to remain in an inoperable or junk vehicle for more than 30 days within any residential district, gateway district, town center district, commercial district, or planned development district. Those vehicles which are exempted from the provisions of this article are as follows:
(1)
Any motor vehicle or parts thereof which are located on the premises of licensed automobile dealers;
(2)
Any motor vehicle or parts thereof which are located on the premises of any licensed junkyard operated in compliance with state and local laws relating to licensing, regulating and zoning of such junkyards;
(3)
Any motor vehicle enclosed within a building on private property which is not a hazard to public health;
(4)
Any motor vehicle in operable condition specifically adapted or designed for operation on drag strips or raceways; and
(5)
Any motor vehicles in the process of being restored to an operable condition provided they are:
a.
Limited to no more than two in number;
b.
Located in the rear yard and behind the house of the owner of such vehicles; and
c.
Not visible from any road.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 38-28; Ord. No. 05082017(1), 5-8-2017 )
Sec. 24-4. - Penalty for violations.
(a)
When any objectionable condition as defined in section 24-1 or 24-2 or violations of any applicable codes is being permitted or maintained on any lot of land or premises in the city, it shall be punished as a misdemeanor in accordance to section 1-11. If the court determines that the conditions do in fact constitute a nuisance, the court shall issue an order specifying the time within which such nuisance must be abated. Failure to abate the nuisance within the ordered the time period may also be punished as listed in section 24-6(b).
(b)
In any proceedings brought under this chapter, each day that a violation exists shall constitute a separate offense unless the applicable ordinance explicitly states otherwise.
(c)
This chapter shall be enforced by the chief of police or his designee, certified peace officer employed with the city police department, city marshal or building inspector, where applicable.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 38-29; Ord. No. 05082017(1), 5-8-2017 )
Sec. 24-5. - Responsibility for removal.
Upon giving written notice, citation or summons, the owner of the unlicensed or inoperable vehicle, trailer, equipment or junked motor vehicle, and the owner or occupant of the private property upon which the same is located, either, or all of them, shall be responsible for its removal. In the event that such owners fail to remove such vehicle and thereby abate such nuisance within the 15-day time period provided in the notice, the official officer shall be authorized to institute proceedings against such owners in the municipal court of the city. Such service shall be as provided in O.C.G.A. § 41-2-12.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 38-30; Ord. No. 05082017(1), 5-8-2017 )
Sec. 24-6. - Complaint, trial, order, failure to comply, assessment, collection.
(a)
When it becomes necessary to summons an owner or occupant of lands subject to the provisions of this chapter, said summons shall be in the form of a written complaint setting forth, particularly, the nature and location of the nuisance and the identity of the person complained against. If the court determines that the conditions do in fact constitute a nuisance, the court shall issue an order specifying the time within which such nuisance must be abated. In addition, the court may impose a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 for the offense of failure to abate the nuisance within the period provided for in section 24-3, if the court determines that the owner or occupant unreasonably failed to abate the nuisance during that time period or prior to the court hearing.
(b)
The failure of the owner or occupant to comply with the court's order to abate the nuisance shall constitute an offense and shall subject the person to prosecution and penalties as provided in section 1-11, including:
(1)
Fines not to exceed $1,000.00 per violation.
(2)
Imprisonment, not to exceed six months.
(3)
Community service to be performed with any private, nonprofit community service organization or any public agency willing to participate and approved by municipal court. Violators shall not be used for any purpose resulting in private gain to any person, except in accordance with state law. The role of the city in the supervision of violators performing community service shall be a governmental function.
(4)
Any or all of these penalties in the discretion of the judge.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 38-31; Ord. No. 05082017(1), 5-8-2017 )
Sec. 24-7. - Summary abatement.
(a)
Enforcement; notice to abate. In the event that the nuisance is abated by the city pursuant to the authority of the order of the court authorized by this chapter, it shall be lawful for the city to abate the same and remove the objectionable conditions from the property. In such cases, the official shall notify the public works director, who shall cause the nuisance to be abated and the objectionable conditions to be removed from the property and shall assess the costs associated with said removal and charge all costs thereof to the owner or representative. Said costs shall be a lien against the property upon which the condition existed, ranking equally with the lien for county taxes; provided, however, that this shall not preclude other remedies available to the official for abating said nuisance or the city's use of other legal means of collecting the cost therefor.
(b)
Unlicensed inoperable or junked motor vehicle. In the event that the nuisance is abated by the city pursuant to the authority of the order of the court authorized by this chapter, the city shall abate the nuisance in the following manner:
(1)
The city shall hire a wrecker service, or such other service as may be required, to remove the nuisance, and direct that the nuisance be removed to a local licensed junkyard or other similar facility.
(2)
The wrecker service hired to remove the nuisance shall notify the owner of the property and the owner of the motor vehicle within ten days of removal of the property of the location of the junked motor vehicle and of the cost incurred by the owner in connection with the abatement service. Such cost may include the actual cost of removal of the motor vehicle and storage charges, if applicable, up to and including the date of payment by the owner.
(3)
In the event that the owner does not pay the charges or otherwise recover the motor vehicle within such time as may be established by the wrecker service or by state law, the wrecker service may be authorized to recover its costs and expenses and dispose of the property in accordance with general provisions of state law.
(Code 1993, pt. II, § 38-32; Ord. No. 05082017(1), 5-8-2017 )
Sec. 24-8. - Blowing debris upon sidewalk prohibited.
(a)
No person shall sweep, rake, blow or deposit any trash, garbage, debris, leaves, grass, clippings, brush, soil or other foreign matter onto any sidewalk or public way.
(b)
Any person violating subsection (a) of this section shall be issued an accusation to appear in municipal court and shall be punished as provided in section 1-11. Each day of the violation shall constitute a separate offense.
(Code 1993, pt. II, §§ 38-33, 38-34; Ord. No. 09142017(1) , 9-14-2017)
