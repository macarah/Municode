4.04.010 - Police department.
The police department shall be composed of the chief of police and any police officers or other employees as may be necessary. The police shall have arrest powers in accordance with local, State, and Federal law. Its purpose is to enforce City of Stockbridge ordinances, and county, State, and Federal law within its jurisdiction so as to maintain the peace, good order and tranquility of the city. Enforcement powers shall include the arrest and prosecution for any violation of such laws. The police department may be assisted by others as may be authorized by mayor and council, or as provided in any duly executed intergovernmental contract or mutual aid agreement.
(Ord. No. 22-499 , § 1, 5-31-2022)
4.04.020 - Functions and duties of the department.
The functions and duties of the department of police are as follows:
1.
Preserve the public peace and good order of the city by the enforcement of applicable laws and ordinances.
2.
Conduct community relations programs.
3.
Conduct crime prevention programs.
4.
Protect the rights of persons and property.
5.
Administer licensing procedures for all licenses issued under the police powers of the city.
6.
Enforce all ordinances applicable to licenses and permits issued under the police powers of the city.
(Ord. No. 22-499 , § 1, 5-31-2022)
4.04.030 - Functions and duties of the police chief.
A.
It shall be the duty of the police chief to formulate and implement rules and regulations for the operations of the police department. The police chief shall have the authority to promulgate departmental rules and regulations that are consistent with the terms or intent of the laws and ordinances relating to the department. Any changes in the rules and regulations for the operation of the department of police, including the employee work rules, shall be executed by the police chief in writing. The chief of police shall provide the city manager or designee with a copy of the police department work rules from time to time whenever the same are amended.
B.
It is also the duty of the police chief to:
1.
Administer corrective and disciplinary action for violation of rules and regulations of the police department. Such action may include, but is not limited to, formal counseling, oral or written reprimands, retraining and suspension with or without pay, or termination.
2.
Suspend any employee with or without pay pending an investigation in which the employee is accused of misconduct if the police chief or the police chief's designee determines that the suspension is in the best interest of the department, the public welfare or accused employee.
3.
Cause an investigation into a police department employee's conduct when the employee's conduct indicates probable cause for disciplinary action.
4.
Develop other necessary procedures for the implementation of the rules and regulations.
(Ord. No. 22-499 , § 1, 5-31-2022)
4.04.040 - Authorization for the chief of police to administer oath of office for sworn police officers.
The police chief or the chief's designee is authorized, in accordance with Georgia law, to administer the oath of office to all sworn police officers employed by the City of Stockbridge. When taken, the officer shall sign the official oath and copies thereof shall be filed with the city clerk and in the personnel records of the officer so sworn.
(Ord. No. 22-499 , § 1, 5-31-2022)
4.04.050 - Assignments to certain positions without competitive examination.
The police chief or the chief's designee is authorized, in that official's discretion, to make assignments to the positions of detective, senior police officer, major or assistant chief, without reference to competitive examination or eligible lists. Any detective, senior police officer, major or assistant chief so designated may be removed or transferred at the pleasure of the police chief. Such person so removed or transferred shall thereupon resume the nondiscretionary rank or title in the department of police held immediately prior to such person's discretionary assignment as a detective, senior police officer, major or assistant chief. Such person shall be notified in writing of the removal or transfer.
(Ord. No. 22-499 , § 1, 5-31-2022)
4.04.060 - Probationary period.
A.
Any newly hired, or promoted, sworn member of the police department shall serve a twelve-month probationary period.
B.
A sworn member filling a discretionary rank at the time of promotion to a nondiscretionary rank, in accordance with this division, shall have the time successfully served in the discretionary rank count toward the required twelve-month probationary period, provided such service was immediately prior to promotion to the nondiscretionary rank. Time successfully served in the discretionary rank after the date of promotion to the nondiscretionary rank shall also be credited as part of the twelve-month probationary period required to attain status in the nondiscretionary rank.
C.
The probationary period shall be utilized for closely observing how well a probationary employee performs the work and responsibility of the position, and for securing the most accurate judgments as to whether or not the probationary employee's performance meets the required work standards of their position. It shall be the duty of the police chief to issue administrative regulations governing the probationary evaluation process.
D.
At least thirty (30) days prior to the completion of the twelve-month probationary period, the police chief shall determine whether or not the probationary employee should be released from probation or receive permanent status in the promoted rank. If the probationary employee is deemed to have passed the probationary period and is to be given status in the rank, the employee shall be notified of such decision no later than fifteen (15) days before the expiration of the twelve-month probationary period.
E.
At any time during the probationary period, the police chief may remove a promoted sworn member whose performance does not meet the required work standards and/or return that member to the rank in which the member has retained status. If such occurs, the police chief shall notify the member in writing that the probationary evaluation part of the examination has been failed and the reason for such failure. Such notification shall be given at least fifteen (15) days in advance of the date the action is to be official, and in no case later than fifteen (15) days before the twelve-month probationary period is to expire. Thereafter, the name of a sworn member who failed the probationary evaluation part of the examination shall be removed from the department or promotional eligibility list.
(Ord. No. 22-499 , § 1, 5-31-2022)
4.04.070 - Authority to claim compensation or property due because of information provided to Federal or State agencies.
A.
The police chief shall be authorized to act on behalf of the city to claim compensation or property due to the city by virtue of information provided to the Internal Revenue Service, the State Department of Revenue or similar agencies, by agents of the department of police, pursuant to 26 U.S.C. § 7623 or any similar provisions under State law or due to the city pursuant to State and Federal forfeiture statutes or any similar State or Federal statute.
B.
Any such information or evidence provided or secured through investigations conducted by agents or officers of the department of police shall remain the property of the State or city or the United States in accordance with State and United States law.
C.
Any such compensation paid to the city by virtue of information provided or secured through investigations conducted by agents of the department of police shall be paid directly into such account maintained for the benefit of law enforcement activities by the department of police, and all property so received shall be entrusted to disposal or utilization by the police chief or the chief's designee.
(Ord. No. 22-499 , § 1, 5-31-2022)
Chapter 4.06 - ALARM SYSTEMS

4.06.010 - Definitions.
As used in this chapter:
"Alarm signal" means a detectible signal, either audible or visual, generated by an alarm system, to which the Stockbridge Police Department is expected to respond.
"Alarm system" means any device or assembly of equipment designed to signal the occurrence of an illegal entry or other event requiring immediate attention and to which the Stockbridge Police Department is expected to respond, but does not include alarms installed in motor vehicles or fire alarms.
"Alarm systems coordinator" means an employee of the city designated by the city council of Stockbridge, Georgia to administer this chapter.
"Alarm user" means any person, corporation, partnership, proprietorship, governmental or educational entity or any other entity owning or leasing an alarm system or on whose premises an alarm system is maintained for the protection of such premises.
"Automatic dial protection device" means an automatic dialing device or an automatic telephone dialing alarm system and shall include any system which, upon being activated, automatically initiates to the Henry County "911" emergency communications center a recorded message or code signal indicating a need for emergency response.
"False alarm" means the activation of an alarm system through mechanical or electrical failure, malfunction, improper installation, or the negligence or improper activation by the alarm user, his employees or agents, and false signals activated to summon law enforcement or emergency personnel unless response was canceled by the alarm user or his agent before personnel are dispatched to the alarm location. A false alarm shall not include an alarm which can reasonably be determined to have been caused or activated for the first time by unusual conditions of nature nor does it include other extraordinary circumstances not reasonably subject to the control of the alarm user. In addition, an alarm activated during an alarm system testing procedure shall not be considered a false alarm if the alarm user first notifies and receives permission from the alarm systems coordinator to test the system. An alarm is false within the meaning of this chapter when, upon investigation or inspection by city employees, evidence indicates that no unauthorized entry, burglary, robbery, or such other crime was committed or attempted and no fire or other emergency existed in or on the premises which would have activated a properly functioning alarm system.
(Ord. 98-4 (part), 1998)
(Ord. No. 22-499 , § 2(Exh. A), 5-31-2022)
4.06.020 - Alarm installations.
The installation and alteration of alarm systems shall be in accordance with Article 760 "Fire Protective Signaling Systems" of the National Electrical Code, NFPA Number 70-1975 and the following NFPA standards:
A.
Standard 71 - Standard for the installation, maintenance, and use of central station signaling systems;
B.
Standard 72-B - Standard for the installation, maintenance and use of auxiliary protective signaling systems for fire alarm service;
C.
Standard 72-C - Standard for the installation, maintenance, and use of remote station protective signaling system;
D.
Standards 72-D - Standard for the installation, maintenance, and use of remote station protective signaling systems for watchman, fire alarm and supervisory service;
E.
Standards 72-E - Standard on automatic fire detectors;
F.
Standard 74 - Standard for the installation, maintenance and use of household fire warning equipment.
(Ord. 98-4 (part), 1998)
4.06.030 - False alarm determinations.
A.
Response. Whenever an alarm is activated in the incorporated area of the city and the Stockbridge Police Department personnel respond, a city official on the scene of the activated alarm system shall inspect the area and determine whether the response was in fact necessary as indicated by the alarm system or whether the alarm was a false alarm.
B.
Notification. If the city official at the scene of the activated alarm system determines the alarm to be false, he shall make a report of the false alarm. The alarm systems coordinator shall be supplied with a copy of each false alarm determination report. The alarm systems coordinator shall in turn, notify the alarm user of the false alarm, and any such civil penalties if appropriate.
(Ord. 98-4 (part), 1998)
(Ord. No. 22-499 , § 2(Exh. A), 5-31-2022)
4.06.040 - Review of false alarm determinations.
A.
Request for Review. The alarm systems coordinator shall, when requested by an alarm user, review the determination that an alarm was false. Such review shall be conducted by the alarm systems coordinator only if the alarm user requests in writing such a review within ten (10) days of the date the alarm user was notified of the false alarm determination. The written request for review of a false alarm determination by the alarm systems coordinator shall include the following information:
A.
Alarm user name;
B.
Address at which alarm is installed;
C.
Date of false alarm that is being contested; and
D.
Facts upon which the request for review of the false alarm determination is made.
(Ord. 98-4 (part), 1998)
4.06.050 - Penalties.
A.
Excessive False Alarms. It is found and determined that three (3) or more false alarms within any fiscal year of the city are excessive and constitute a public nuisance.
B.
Civil Penalties.
1.
The fourth false alarm within a fiscal year will result in the alarm user being assessed a twenty-five dollar ($25.00) civil penalty. Each such penalty shall be paid within thirty (30) days from date of receipt of the assessment notice.
2.
The fifth false alarm within a fiscal year shall result in the alarm user being assessed a fifty dollar ($50.00) civil penalty, which shall be paid within thirty (30) days from receipt of the assessment notice.
3.
The sixth false alarm within a fiscal year and all future alarms thereafter within the thirty-day period shall result in the alarm user cited to appear before the municipal court of the city to be assessed a civil penalty not to exceed five hundred dollars ($500.00).
C.
Remedy for Refusal to Pay Assessments or to Take Remedial Action. If an alarm user is assessed civil penalties for excessive false alarms and refuses either: (1) to pay the same; or (2) to make immediate remedial action so that excessive false alarms cease, then the city shall be authorized to bring an action in the municipal court of the city to abate the nuisance pursuant to O.C.G.A. Section 41-2-5.
(Ord. 98-4 (part), 1998)
4.06.060 - Preexisting alarm systems.
Alarm systems upon which substantial installation work has already begun on the effective date of the ordinance codified in this chapter shall not be required to comply with the construction standards required by Section 4.06.020, yet such systems shall in all respects be subject to the false alarm, penalty, and other provisions set forth in this chapter.
(Ord. 98-4 (part), 1998)

Article III. - Fireworks

4.08.050 - Fireworks—Possessing—Dealing in.
It shall be unlawful for any person, firm, corporation, association or partnership to offer for sale at retail or wholesale, or to use or explode or cause to be exploded, or to possess, manufacture, transport or store any fireworks or other composition used or usable to obtain visible or audible pyrotechnic display within the city's jurisdiction, except as otherwise permitted in this article and except as may be permitted by O.C.G.A. Sections 25-10-3, 25-10-3.1, 25-10-3.2, 25-10-4, and 25-10-5.
(Ord. 06-145 § 1, 2006: Ord. 05-124 § 3 (part), 2005)
4.08.060 - Definitions.
"Fireworks" means any combustible or explosive composition or any substance or combination of substances or article prepared for the purpose of producing a visual or audible effect by combustion, explosion, deflagration or detonation, including blank cartridges, balloons requiring fire underneath to propel them, firecrackers, torpedoes, skyrockets, Roman candles, bombs or other combustibles and explosives of like construction, as well as articles containing any explosive or flammable compound and tablets and other devices containing an explosive substance.
"Pyrotechnics" means fireworks.
"Sparklers" are defined as being constructed of wire or wood containing one hundred (100) grams or less of mixture per item; other sparkling items which are non-explosive and non-aerial and contain seventy-five (75) grams or less of chemical compound per tube or a total of two hundred (200) grams or less for multiple tubes; snake and glow worms; trick noise makers which include paper streamers, party poppers, string poppers, snappers and drop pops each consisting of twenty-five hundredths (0.25) grains or less of explosive mixture.
(Ord. 06-145 § 2, 2006: Ord. 05-124 § 3 (part), 2005)
4.08.070 - Exceptions.
As used in this article, the term "fireworks" shall not include the following:
A.
Model rockets and model rocket engines, designed, sold and used for the purpose of propelling recoverable aero models;
B.
Toy pistol paper caps in which the explosive content does not average more than twenty-five hundredths (0.25) grains of explosive mixture per paper cap;
C.
Ammunition consumed by weapons used for sporting and hunting purposes;
D.
Toy pistols, toy cannons, toy canes, toy guns or other devices using such paper caps;
E.
"Sparklers" as defined in Section 4.08.060 of this article.
(Ord. 06-145 § 3, 2006; Ord. 05-124 § 3 (part), 2005)
4.08.080 - Permit required.
A.
The Henry County fire department shall be permitted to adopt reasonable rules and regulations for the licensing of individuals and corporations or granting of permits for supervised displays of fireworks by corporations, fair associations, amusement parks and other organizations or individuals or groups of individuals. Such permits shall be permitted to be granted upon application to the Henry County fire department's fire marshal's office and approval in accordance with the regulations for the display and filing of a bond by the individual filing the permit application. An inspection and/or determination by the fire department will be performed after application and before issuance of permit. (See NFPA 1126, Code for Fireworks Display and NFPA 1126, Standard for the Use of Pyrotechnics before a Proximate Audience.)
B.
A competent operator, licensed and certified as to competency by the Henry County fire department, shall handle every such display. Every such display shall be of such composition and character and shall be located, discharged or fired so as, in the opinion of the city, after proper site inspection, not to be hazardous to any person or property. After such privileges have been granted, sales, possession, use and distribution of fireworks for such displays shall be lawful for that purpose only. No permit granted hereunder shall be transferable.
(Ord. 05-124 § 3 (part), 2005)
4.08.090 - Proof of financial responsibility required.
Before any permit for a pyrotechnic display shall be issued, the person, firm or corporation making application therefore shall furnish proof of financial responsibility to satisfy claims for damages to property or personal injuries arising out of any act or omission on the part of such person, firm or corporation or any agent or employee thereof, in such amount, character and form as the fire department determines to be necessary for the protection of the public.
(Ord. 05-124 § 3 (part), 2005)
4.08.100 - Permitted uses.
Nothing in this law shall be construed to prohibit any of the following:
1.
The sale, at wholesale, of any fireworks for supervised displays by any approved resident manufacturer, wholesaler, dealer or jobber, in accordance with regulations of the U.S. Bureau of Alcohol, Tobacco and Firearms (see Title 27, Code of Federal Regulations, Part 181) and the U.S. Department of Transportation;
2.
The manufacture, transportation or storage of fireworks at a manufacturing facility. The testing of fireworks under the direction of its manufacturer provided permission for such testing has been obtained from the authority having jurisdiction;
3.
The sale, transportation, handling or use of industrial pyrotechnic devices or fireworks, such as railroad torpedoes, fuses, automotive, aeronautical and marine flares and smoke signals;
4.
The sale and use of blank cartridges for use in a show or theater, for signal or ceremonial purposes, in athletics or sports or legal power tools;
5.
The transportation, handling or use of any pyrotechnic devices by the armed forces of the United States;
6.
The use of pyrotechnics in training by the fire service, law enforcement or similar government agencies;
7.
The use of fireworks for agricultural purposes under the direct supervision of the U.S. Department of the Interior or an equivalent state or local agency. [See Title 16, Code of Federal Regulations, Part 1500.17 (a) (8).]
(Ord. 05-124 § 3 (part), 2005)

Chapter 4.10 - STORAGE OF WOOD PRODUCTS BY SUBDIVISION DEVELOPERS

4.10.010 - Definitions.
"Impermissible materials" as defined and used in this chapter means and includes but is not limited to concrete, roofing materials, sheetrock, insulation, buckets, cans, boxes, styrofoam, pipes, plumbing or electrical fixtures and wires, or any other building materials used for construction of residences not made from wood.
"Open Burning Ban" as defined and used in this chapter means the ban of open burning of leaves, tree limbs, other yard wastes, forest land and vegetative waste from land clearing in the Atlanta Ozone Nonattainment Area in effect from May 1st through September 30th of each year, beginning in 1996 as set forth in the Air Quality Control Chapter 391-3-1 of the Rules of Georgia Department of Natural Resources, Environmental Protection Division.
"Permissible materials" as defined and used in this chapter means leaves, trees, tree stumps, limbs, branches, prunings, brush, wood building materials, and other such wood wastes.
"Receipt of notice" as defined and used in this chapter means hand delivery or the depositing of the notice in the United States mail or personal delivery. The notice may but is not required to be sent via certified mail.
"Storage area" as defined and used in this chapter means a portion of the subdivision designated by the developer of the subdivision for collecting and maintaining the permissible materials.
(Ord. 96-6 § 1, 1996)
4.10.020 - Designation of storage area.
Developers of subdivisions within the city limits shall designate a portion of the subdivision property to be used as a storage area for the purpose of storing permissible materials.
(Ord. 96-6 § 2, 1996)
4.10.030 - Usage of storage area.
The storage area may be used by the developer from May 1st through September 30th for each year that the Open Burning Ban is in effect, unless notice to the contrary is issued by the city.
(Ord. 96-6 § 3, 1996)
4.10.040 - Removal of permissible materials.
Permissible materials in the storage area must be removed within sixty (60) days of the occurrence of any of the following:
A.
Expiration of the Open Burning Ban each year; or
B.
Upon receipt of a notice to remove the materials issued by the city.
(Ord. 96-6 § 4, 1996)
4.10.050 - Bond requirement.
A.
Developers designating and using a storage area shall be required to post a cash or surety bond with the city in an amount to be set by the city manager in order to ensure the cleanup of the stored materials.
B.
The bond shall be refunded upon the city manager's inspection and determination that all materials have been removed.
C.
Failure to properly and timely remove the permissible materials or the storage of impermissible materials shall result in a forfeiture of the entire bond.
(Ord. 96-6 § 5, 1996)

Chapter 4.18 - MOSQUITO CONTROL

4.18.010 - Purpose.
It is the intent of this chapter to control and reduce the mosquito population in the city of Stockbridge by removing, draining, treating, altering or otherwise eliminating all breeding sources for mosquitoes. The city shall establish an effective program of mosquito control including, but not limited to, elimination of or treatment of breeding sources for mosquitoes, elimination of identified mosquito populations and alleviation of all other such conditions found to be conducive to the reproduction or continued existence of mosquitoes. This shall be accomplished through the processes of education and public information, conference, conciliation, persuasion and these having failed, by administrative and judicial proceedings for the explicit purpose of controlling the breeding of mosquitoes, the presence of which constitute a danger to the public health, impair the comfort and convenience of the residents, and jeopardize the enjoyment of life and property within the city.
(Ord. 04-104 (part), 2004)
4.18.020 - Definitions.
"Artificial container" means any bucket, barrel, tire, bottle, tub, tank, gutter, bird bath, swimming pool, ornamental pond, flower pot, jar, retention pond or any other such manmade items capable of collecting water.
"Breeding source" means any area capable of sustaining the reproduction of mosquitoes.
"City council" means the Stockbridge city council.
"Director" means the person designated by the city council to administer and enforce this chapter or his or her authorized designee.
"Evidence of mosquito breeding" means the natural presence of mosquito larva, pupa or their remains.
"Insecticide" means a chemical agent which kills or prevents the reproduction of insects.
"Larva" means the immature, fully aquatic stage of mosquito development in which the insect appears as a small, wingless, worm-like form.
"Larvicide" means a chemical agent which kills or prevents the reproduction of mosquito larvae.
"Mosquito" means a small, long-legged, two-winged insect of the family Culicidae, in which the female of the species is distinguished by a long proboscis for sucking blood.
"Occupant" means the person who has the use of or occupies any building or any part thereof, or who has the use or possession, actual or constructive, of the premises whether the owner or tenant. In the case of vacant buildings or vacant portions of a building, or in case of occupancy in whole or in part by the owner, the owner shall be deemed to be and shall have the responsibilities of an occupant of such building.
"Owner" means the person owning the building or premises.
"Person" means individuals, firms, partnerships, associations, public or private institutions, municipalities, political subdivisions of the state, governmental agencies or public or private corporations.
"Premises" means a parcel of real property, including all buildings and structures located thereon.
"Pupa" means the immature, fully aquatic stage of mosquito development immediately following the larval stage and preceding the adult form in which the insect appears as a small, wingless, worm-like shape with a greatly enlarged head.
(Ord. 04-104 (part), 2004)
4.18.030 - General provisions.
All premises within the city shall be maintained in such a manner as to prevent the breeding of mosquitoes on the premises. Artificial containers, ditches, streams, flooded areas, retention ponds and all other such sources of standing water or other liquid where mosquitoes are breeding, must be removed, drained, treated, altered, maintained or otherwise eliminated by the person occupying the premises or, in the absence of an occupant, by the owner, in such a manner as to prevent the breeding of mosquitoes. Where there is evidence of mosquito breeding, effective methods of eliminating and treating mosquito breeding sources shall be instigated by the person occupying the premises, or in the absence of an occupant, by the owner, within forty-eight (48) hours after discovering or being informed of the evidence of mosquito breeding on the premises.
(Ord. 04-104 (part), 2004)
4.18.040 - Methods of eliminating and treating mosquito breeding sources.
Breeding sources for mosquitoes shall be eliminated or treated by one or more of the following methods as shall be approved by the director.
A.
Draining, removing or otherwise eliminating the breeding source;
B.
Completely emptying the breeding source of all water at least every seven (7) days, or as approved by the director. Where an artificial container is a water basin (e.g. swimming pool) designed to hold water, such containers shall be maintained or altered to prevent mosquito breeding;
C.
Treating the breeding source with an effective insecticide and/or larvicide approved by the director;
D.
Completely emptying artificial containers of all liquid and storing in an enclosed structure which is constructed in such a manner as to prevent the permanent collection of liquid in said containers;
E.
Maintain all natural or manmade storm or surface water drain ways in a manner as to prevent the ponding of water sufficient to provide breeding for mosquitoes;
F.
Other methods proven to be effective in controlling mosquitoes and as approved by the director.
(Ord. 04-104 (part), 2004)
4.18.050 - Right of entry—Inspection.
The director shall have the right of entry upon any premises where entry is necessary to carry out the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. 04-104 (part), 2004)
4.18.060 - Penalties and remedies.
A.
Any person who violates any provision of this chapter shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction, punished by a fine not to exceed one thousand dollars ($1,000.00).
B.
The city may bring a civil proceeding in any court of competent jurisdiction to enforce the provisions of this chapter in accordance with the general nuisance law of the state.
C.
Any person who violates any provision of this chapter and is convicted shall be liable to the city of all costs and fees incurred by it in the enforcement of this chapter including, but not limited to, attorneys' fees. The city shall be entitled to a lien against the property of the violator that was the subject of the enforcement proceedings for its costs incurred.
(Ord. 04-104 (part), 2004)
Chapter 4.20 - SMOKE-FREE AIR

4.20.010 - Title.
This chapter shall be known, cited and referred to as "City of Stockbridge Srnoke-Free Air Ordinance."
(Ord. 05-143 § 2 (part), 2005)
4.20.020 - Findings and purpose.
A.
The city of Stockbridge finds that:
1.
Numerous studies have found that tobacco smoke is a major contributor to indoor air pollution, and that breathing secondhand smoke is a cause of disease in healthy non-smokers, including heart disease, stroke, respiratory disease and lung cancer.
2.
Secondhand smoke is particularly hazardous to elderly people, individuals with cardiovascular disease and individuals with impaired respiratory function, including asthmatics and those with obstructive disease. Children exposed to secondhand smoke have an increased risk of asthma, respiratory infections, sudden infant death syndrome, developmental abnormalities and cancer.
B.
Accordingly, the city council finds and declares that the purpose of this chapter is:
1.
To protect the public health and welfare by prohibiting smoking in public places and public and private places of employment;
2.
To guarantee the right of non-smokers to breathe smoke-free air; and
3.
To recognize that the need to breathe smoke-free air shall have priority over the desire to smoke.
(Ord. 05-143 § 2 (part), 2005)
4.20.030 - Definitions.
As used in this chapter, the following definitions shall apply:
"Bar" means an establishment that is devoted to the serving of alcoholic beverages for consumption by guests on the premises and in which the serving of food is only incidental to the consumption of those beverages, including but not limited to, taverns, nightclubs, cocktail lounges and cabarets.
"Business" means any corporation, sole proprietorship, partnership, limited partnership, limited liability corporation, limited liability partnership, professional corporation, enterprise, franchise, association, trust, joint venture or other entity, whether for profit or nonprofit.
"City" means the city of Stockbridge, Georgia.
"Employee" means an individual who is employed by a business in consideration for direct or indirect monetary wages or profit.
"Employer" means an individual or a business that employs one (1) or more individuals.
"Enclosed area" means all space between a. floor and ceiling that is enclosed on all sides by solid walls or windows, exclusive of doorways, which extend from the floor to the ceiling.
"Health care facility" means an office or institution providing care or treatment of diseases, whether physical, mental or emotional, or other medical, physiological, or psychological conditions, including, but not limited to, hospitals, rehabilitation hospitals or other clinics, including weight control clinics, homes for the chronically ill, laboratories, and offices of surgeons, chiropractors, physical therapists, physicians, dentists and all specialists within these professions. This definition shall include all waiting rooms, hallways, private rooms, semiprivate rooms and wards within health care facilities. This definition shall not include long-term care facilities as defined in paragraph (3) of the Official Code of Georgia section 31-8-81, as amended.
"Infiltrate" means to permeate an enclosed area by passing through its walls, ceilings, floors, windows or ventilation systems to the extent that an individual can smell secondhand smoke.
"Local governing authority" means the municipal corporation of the city.
"Place of employment" means an enclosed area under the control of a public or private employer that employees utilize during the course of employment, including, but not limited to, work areas, employee lounges, restrooms, conference rooms, meeting rooms, classrooms, employee cafeterias and hallways. A private residence is not a place of employment unless it is used as a licensed family day care, adult day care or health care facility. This term shall not include vehicles used in the course of employment.
"Public place" means an enclosed area to which the public is invited or in which the public is permitted, including, but not limited to, banks, bars, educational facilities, health care facilities, Laundromats, public transportation facilities, reception areas, restaurants, retail food production and marketing establishments, retail service establishments, retail stores, shopping malls, sports arenas, theaters and waiting rooms. A private residence is not a public place unless it is used as a licensed family day care, adult day care or health care facility.
"Restaurant" means an eating establishment, including, but not limited to, coffee shops, cafeterias, sandwich stands and private and public school cafeterias, which gives or offers for sale food to the public, guests or employees, as well as kitchens and catering facilities in which food is prepared on the premises for serving elsewhere. The term shall include a bar area within any restaurant.
"Retail tobacco store" means a retail store utilized primarily for the sale of tobacco products and accessories and in which the sale of other products is merely incidental.
"Secondhand smoke" means smoke emitted from lighted, smoldering or burning tobacco when the person smoking is not inhaling, smoke emitted at the mouthpiece during puff drawing and smoke exhaled by the person smoking.
"Service line" means an indoor line in which one (1) or more persons are waiting for or receiving service of any kind, whether or not the service involves the exchange of money.
"Shopping mall" means an enclosed public walkway or hall area that serves to connect retail or professional establishments.
"Smoking" means inhaling, exhaling, burning or carrying any lighted tobacco product including cigarettes, cigars and pipe tobacco.
"Smoking area" means a separately designated enclosed room which need not be entered by an employee in order to conduct business that is designated as a smoking area and, when so designated as a smoking area, shall not be construed as to deprive employees of a nonsmoking lounge, waiting area or break room.
"Sports arena" means enclosed stadiums and enclosed sports pavilions, gymnasiums, health spas, boxing arenas, swimming pools, roller and ice rinks, bowling alleys and other similar places where members of the general public assemble to engage in physical exercise, participate in athletic competition or witness sports or other events.
(Ord. 05-143 § 2 (part), 2005)
4.20.040 - Smoking prohibited in state and government buildings and certain privately owned facilities and buildings.
A.
Smoking shall be prohibited in all enclosed facilities of, and buildings owned, leased or operated by, the state of Georgia, its agencies and authorities, and any political subdivision of the state that are located within the city.
B.
Smoking shall be prohibited in all enclosed facilities of and buildings owned, leased or operated by the city, its agencies and authorities.
C.
Smoking shall also be prohibited in enclosed facilities and buildings owned, leased or operated by any local board or authority created by general, local or special act of the General Assembly or by ordinance or resolution of the governing body of a county or municipal corporation individually or jointly with other political subdivisions or municipalities of the state that are located within the city.
D.
Subject to certain limited exceptions as set out at Section 4.20.060 of this chapter, smoking shall also be prohibited in privately owned public buildings and facilities that are located in the city.
4.20.050 - Smoking prohibited in all enclosed areas within places of employment.
A.
Except as otherwise specifically provided in Section 4.20.060 of this chapter, smoking shall be prohibited in all enclosed areas within places of employment, including, but not limited to, common work areas, auditoriums, classrooms, conference and meeting rooms, private offices, elevators, hallways, medical facilities, cafeterias, employee lounges, stairs, restrooms and all other enclosed facilities that are located within the city.
B.
Such prohibition on smoking shall be communicated to all current employees and to each prospective employee upon their application for employment.
(Ord. 05-143 § 2 (part), 2005)
4.20.060 - Exemptions.
A.
Notwithstanding any other provision of this chapter, the following areas located within the city of Stockbridge shall be exempt from the provisions of Sections 4.20.040 and 4.20.050, if one (1) or more of the following requirements have been met and if the requirements of subsection B of this section have also been met:
1.
Private residences, except when used as a licensed family day care, adult day care, or health care facility;
2.
Any private room or rooms designated for smoking use only ("smoking room") which are located within privately owned, freestanding buildings, so long as any such smoking room or rooms are enclosed and have an air handling system independent from the main air handling system that serves all other areas of the building and all air within any such smoking room is exhausted directly to the outside of the building by an exhaust fan of sufficient size and no air from such smoking room is recirculated through or infiltrates other parts of the building. This exemption is limited to buildings that do not operate a bar, restaurant or private club therein. No person under the age of eighteen (18) shall be permitted to enter any such smoking room;
3.
Hotel and motel rooms that are rented to guests and are designated as smoking rooms; provided, however, that not more than twenty percent (20%) of rooms rented to guests in a hotel or motel may be so designated;
4.
Retail tobacco stores, provided that secondhand smoke from such stores does not infiltrate into areas where smoking is prohibited under the provisions of this chapter;
5.
Long-term care facilities as defined in Official Code of Georgia Annotated Section 31-8-81(3), as amended;
6.
Outdoor areas of places of employment;
7.
All workplaces of any manufacturer, importer, or wholesaler of tobacco products, of any tobacco leaf dealer or processor, all tobacco storage facilities, and any other entity set forth in Official Code of Georgia Annotated Section l0-13A-2, as amended;
8.
Private and semiprivate rooms in health care facilities licensed under Title 31 of the Official Code of Georgia that are occupied by one (1) or more persons, all of whom have written authorization by their treating physician to smoke;
9.
Smoking areas designated by an employer, in an establishment other than a restaurant, bar or private club, which shall meet the following requirements:
a.
The smoking area shall be located in a nonwork area where no employee, as part of his or her work responsibilities, shall be required to enter, except such work responsibilities shall not include custodial or maintenance work carried out in the smoking area when it is unoccupied,
b.
The smoking area shall have an air handling system that is independent from the main air handling system that serves all other areas of the building and all air within the smoking area shall be exhausted directly to the outside by an exhaust fan of sufficient size and capacity for the smoking area and no air from the smoking area shall be recirculated through or infiltrate other parts of the building, and
c.
The smoking area shall be for the use of employees only;
10.
Private room or rooms designated for smoking use only ("smoking room") that are located in buildings that allow for the operation of a bar, restaurant or private club therein so long as such smoking room or rooms are enclosed and have an air handling system independent from the main air handling system that serves all other areas of the building and all air within such smoking room is exhausted directly to the outside by an exhaust fan of sufficient size and no air from such smoking room is recirculated through or infiltrates other parts of the building. Any such smoking room cannot be part of the servicing area of a restaurant, bar or private club and cannot be located in any dressing room, restroom, locker room or office area of a building that houses a bar, restaurant or private club. No person under the age of eighteen (18) shall be permitted to enter any such smoking room; and
11.
Private offices in private places of employment occupied exclusively by smokers over the age of eighteen (18) and which are not subject to public access in the ordinary course of business, if such private offices are enclosed and have an air handling system independent from the main air handling system that serves all other areas of the building and all air within any such private office is exhausted directly to the outside by an exhaust fan of sufficient size, and no air from such private office is recirculated through or infiltrates other parts of the building.
B.
Any area described in subsection A of this section (hereinafter "exempt area") shall not become exempt from the provisions of Sections 4.20.040 and 4.20.050 of this chapter unless and until signs have been conspicuously posted in the manner described below:
1.
At every entrance to any exempt area, a sign shall be conspicuously posted indicating that smoking is permitted,
2.
At every other entrance to an area that is not an exempt area, a sign indicating that smoking is not permitted shall be conspicuously posted, and
3.
Notwithstanding anything to the contrary contained herein, there shall be no duty to post any signs at any entrance of those areas described in subsection (A)(1) of this section.
(Ord. 06-152 § 1, 2006)
4.20.070 - Right to declare entire establishment, facility or outdoor area as nonsmoking place.
Notwithstanding any other provision of this chapter, an owner, operator, manager or other person in control of an establishment, facility or outdoor area may declare that entire establishment, facility or outdoor area as a nonsmoking place. Smoking shall be prohibited in any place in which a sign conforming to the requirements of subsection A of Section 4.20.080 of this chapter is posted.
(Ord. 05-143 § 2 (part), 2005)
4.20.080 - Posting of signs and removal of ashtrays.
A.
"No Smoking" signs or the international "No Smoking" symbol consisting of a pictorial representation of a burning cigarette enclosed in a red circle with a red bar across it may be clearly and conspicuously posted by the owner, operator, manager or other person in control in every public place and place of employment where smoking is prohibited by this chapter.
B.
All ashtrays shall be removed from any area where smoking is prohibited by this chapter by the owner, operator, manager or other person in control of the area, unless such ashtray is permanently affixed to an existing structure.
(Ord. 05-143 § 2 (part), 2005)
4.20.090 - Enforcement.
A.
Any officer employed or contracted by the city with the police department, city code enforcement, or with the Henry County Police Department, the Henry County Sheriff and Deputies, the Henry County Code Enforcement Office or any such officer or agency employed or contracted by the city to perform such services shall be responsible for enforcement of this chapter. Those parties shall be authorized and empowered to enforce compliance with this chapter and the rules and regulations adopted and promulgated under this chapter and in connection therewith, to enter upon and inspect the premises of any establishment or business at any reasonable time and in any reasonable manner as provided at Article 2, Chapter 5 of Title. 31 of the Official Code of Georgia
B.
Any citizen who desires to register a complaint under this chapter may initiate the complaint by filing the same with the city or any of the officers identified in subsection A of this section.
C.
Any owner, operator or manager of any establishment regulated by this chapter shall inform persons whom they witness violating this chapter of the appropriate provisions and request compliance. In the event persons violating this chapter refuse to comply with this chapter after being informed by such owner, operator, or manager, the person smoking and not the owner, operator or manager, shall be subject to an action for violation of this chapter.
(Ord. 05-143 § 2 (part), 2005)
4.20.100 - Violations and penalties.
Any person, business, owner or operator who violates any provision of this chapter shall be subject to the following penalties:
1.
Upon first offense both the violating person and the location of violation shall be subject to a warning;
2.
Upon second offense the location and/or person shall be fined in an amount not less than twenty-five dollars ($25.00) but not to exceed one hundred dollars ($100.00) for a second; and
3.
Upon third offense the location and/or person shall be fined in an amount not less than one hundred dollars ($100.00) but not to exceed five hundred dollars ($500.00).
4.
Upon fourth offense the location and/or person shall be fined in an amount not less than five hundred dollars ($500.00) but not to exceed one thousand ($1000.00).
5.
All offenses shall be cumulative as to the location and the individual offender over a period of five (5) years.
6.
A hearing shall be held for review of all violations and fines by the municipal court of the city of Stockbridge, unless such hearing is expressly waived in writing by the violator.
7.
Appeals of violations and fines shall proceed by application for writ of certiorari to the Superior Court of Henry County in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 3-4-3.
(Ord. 05-143 § 2 (part), 2005)
4.20.110 - Other applicable laws and disclaimer.
This chapter shall not be interpreted or construed to allow smoking where it is otherwise restricted by other applicable laws. By regulating smoking, the city is assuming an undertaking only to promote the general health and welfare of its citizens. By this enactment, the city, its officers and its employees are not liable in money damages to any person who claims that any breach of this chapter caused injury.
(Ord. 05-143 § 2 (part), 2005)
Chapter 4.30 - REGULATION OF DISCHARGE OF WEAPONS
Article I. - General Provisions

4.30.010 - Applicability of chapter.
This chapter shall apply to the regulation of the discharge of weapons within the corporate limits of the city of Stockbridge.
(Ord. 07-187 § 1 (part), 2007)
4.30.020 - Regulatory purpose of chapter.
State law provides that the governing authority of each municipal corporation shall have legislative power to adopt clearly reasonable ordinances, resolutions, or regulations relating to its property, affairs, and local government for which no provision has been made by general law.
State law further provides that municipalities, by ordinance, resolution, or other enactment, may reasonably limit or prohibit the discharge of firearms within the boundaries of the municipal corporation.
The city council and mayor of the city of Stockbridge are charged with the protection of the public health, safety and welfare.
(Ord. 07-187 § 1 (part), 2007)
4.30.030 - Regulations subject to change.
All licenses or permits granted under this chapter shall be subject on the part of the city to make or adopt any further ordinances, resolutions and regulations further regulating and governing the operation of vehicles for hire, or prescribing additional and other regulations, or changing, modifying or withdrawing, in whole or in part, the approval of any right for the operation of such vehicles, as may, in the judgment of the mayor and council, be consistent and in the best interest of the public welfare.
(Ord. 07-187 § 1 (part), 2007)
4.30.040 - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
"Bow" means a bowed shaft of material such as metal, wood or plastic, the ends of which are pulled into a bow formation by a string, cord, wire or any other type of material and used for the purpose of propelling an arrow by means of the power developed in pulling the material and used for the purpose of propelling an arrow or other similar device by means of the power developed in pulling the string against the tension of the bow.
"Discharge" means to shoot or fire a weapon or cause a projective to be released from a weapon.
"Projectile" means a missile or missiles projected or thrown by force from weapon.
"Weapon" means firearm, rifle, pistol, revolver, BB gun, paintball gun, cannon, or any weapon designed or intended to propel a shot, bullet or other missile of any kind, or any device capable of discharging a projectile by air, spirit, gas or explosive, or any kind of explosive substance or harmful solid, liquid and gaseous substance, or any spear, spear gun, arrow, bow and arrow, slingshot, crossbow, compound bow, longbow, recurve bow, dirk, Bowie knife, switchblade, knife, ballistic knife, or any other knife, straight-edged razor, spring stick, metal knuckles, blackjack, bat, club or other bludgeon-type weapon, any flailing instrument or any disk which is designed to be thrown or propelled and which may be known as a throwing star or oriental dart, or any weapon of like kin, and any stun gun, taster or similar device.
(Ord. 07-187 § 1 (part), 2007)
4.30.050 - Unlawful discharge of weapon.
A.
It is unlawful for any person to discharge, shoot or fire a firearm, rifle, pistol, revolver, cannon, air rifle, pellet rifle, BB gun, or similar weapon within the city except as allowed under Section 4.30.060.
B.
It is unlawful for any person to discharge or shoot a slingshot or bow of any type within the city except as allowed under Section 4.30.060.
(Ord. 07-187 § 1 (part), 2007)
4.30.060 - Permissible discharge of weapon.
A.
It is permitted to discharge a weapon within the city limits under the following conditions:
1.
Law enforcement officers in the discharge of their official duties and the military at a funeral in honor of the deceased;
2.
Those persons acting in defense of person or property and otherwise in conformity with state law;
3.
Those persons in possession of a permit for turkey shoots, licensed firing ranges, and those performing in theatrical productions using blanks;
4.
City or county animal control officers for the specific intended purpose of sedation and capture of animals; or
5.
Those persons in possession of a pest control permit issued by the city for the specific intended purpose of sedation and capture of animals.
(Ord. 07-187 § 1 (part), 2007)
