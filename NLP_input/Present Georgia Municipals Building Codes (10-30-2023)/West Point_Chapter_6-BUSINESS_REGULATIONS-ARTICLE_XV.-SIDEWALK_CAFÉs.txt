ARTICLE XV. - SIDEWALK CAFÉs[15]

Editor's note— Ord. No. 2010-04, Exh. A, adopted May 10, 2010, set out provisions intended for use as Art. XIII. For purposes of classification, and at the editor's discretion, these provisions have been included as Art. XV.

Sec. 6-401. - Purpose.
This article shall apply to the establishment, operation, and maintenance of all outdoor cafés within the City of West Point Central Business District—CBD (Downtown) on public sidewalks. The purpose of this article is to promote the general economic development and atmosphere of the City of West Point for the benefit of businesses and citizens, and no right of individuals or individual businesses are created therein, and to create an aesthetic ambiance which will attract tourists to the West Point area. Further, this article is designed to maintain the efficiency of the pedestrian path.
(Ord. No. 2010-04, Exh. A, 5-10-10)
Sec. 6-402. - Definitions.
Sidewalk café shall mean the location and use of tables and chairs on the public sidewalk and operated as an extension of a licensed food or beverage establishment in downtown West Point and which sidewalk café shall be an incidental activity of the establishment.
Central business district—CBD (Downtown) shall mean all property zoned CBD between West First Avenue and West Fourth Avenue and West Seventh Street and West Twelfth Street.
Planning and development director shall mean the planning and development director of the City of West Point, Georgia, or his designee.
Permittee shall mean the recipient of a sidewalk café permit under the terms and provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 2010-04, Exh. A, 5-10-10)
Sec. 6-403. - Permit.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a sidewalk café in the CBD District—(Downtown) without a permit issued by the planning and development director, or to fail to comply with all sections of the sidewalk café ordinance. Such permit shall be renewed annually at a time to be designated by the director. The issuance of a sidewalk café permit shall not be construed or interpreted to convey any property rights or any leaseholder rights to any person or business. The provisions of this article are subject to change and amendment by the mayor and council from time to time. Sidewalk café permits are nontransferable. Permits will expire upon transfer of ownership, change in use of facility, and December 31 of each year.
(Ord. No. 2010-04, Exh. A, 5-10-10)
Sec. 6-404. - Application.
Any person desiring to operate a sidewalk café shall submit an application, on a form prescribed by the planning and development director, to the planning department. The application shall include, but not be limited to, the following:
(1)
Name of applicant;
(2)
Name, address, and telephone number of business;
(3)
A copy of a valid City of West Point business license to operate a business in front of which the sidewalk café will be located;
(4)
A copy of a current certificate of insurance in the amounts and categories required by subsection 6-408(b)(1) of this article;
(5)
A detail sketch drawn to scale identifying the perimeter area, the dimensions of area, the dimensions from perimeter to curb or nearest obstacle, and the design and materials of the required café railing system.
(Ord. No. 2010-04, Exh. A, 5-10-10)
Sec. 6-405. - Issuance of permit.
Applicant shall be notified within two (2) weeks of application submission of approval/denial of permit. If permit is denied, applicant shall be provided an explanation of the denial.
(Ord. No. 2010-04, Exh. A, 5-10-10)
Sec. 6-406. - Permit fee.
The annual permit fee to operate a sidewalk café shall be one hundred dollars ($100.00). Prior to issuance or renewal of a sidewalk café permit, the city shall verify that there are no outstanding business license or alcohol license fees owed to the city by the person or entity requesting a sidewalk café permit. A sidewalk café permit will not be issued until all outstanding debts to the City of West Point are paid in full.
(Ord. No. 2010-04, Exh. A, 5-10-10)
Sec. 6-407. - Regulations generally.
A sidewalk café operating in the CBD District (Downtown) shall comply with the following regulations:
(1)
Limitations on area. The area in which a sidewalk café is operated shall abut the outside wall of the restaurant of which it is an extension and shall not extend parallel in either direction beyond the outside wall of the restaurant. The area permitted for a sidewalk café shall not extend perpendicular no more than one-half (½) the width of the sidewalk from the outside wall of the restaurant; however, a minimum of five (5) feet shall be provided at all times for continuous unobstructed pedestrian traffic on the sidewalk between the sidewalk café area and the curb or nearest obstacle.
(2)
Divider required. The area permitted for a sidewalk café shall be separated from the remaining sidewalk area by a system of connected metal rails and posts that would serve to contain crowds and maintain the boundaries of the café. The system shall be designed and constructed such that it resists movement and can be disassembled and removed if necessary. The design must be included in the application and must be approved by the director prior to issuance of a permit. Such divider shall not be less than three (3) feet nor more than four (4) feet in height. All tables and chairs shall be located totally within the limits of the divider. It shall be the responsibility of the permittee to maintain the divider in its exact/approved location at all times.
(3)
Hours of operation. Sidewalk cafés shall be permitted to operate only from 7:00 a.m. to 11:00 p.m.
(4)
The following are strictly prohibited in the café area:
a.
The cooking or preparation of any food.
b.
The storage of any food, beer and alcoholic or nonalcoholic beverage containers, including but not limited to vending machines, carts, cartons, kegs, boxes, bottles, or other such containers.
c.
The placement or drilling of any stakes, rods, support poles, or holes for whatever purpose.
d.
The placement of any permanent fixtures.
e.
The lighting of fires.
f.
Propane or natural gas-powered heaters.
g.
Anything else that could cause physical or structural damage to the sidewalk use area, or cause unreasonable disturbance to the neighborhood, as determined by the city.
(5)
Alcoholic beverages. Alcoholic beverages are prohibited outside the café area on public sidewalk.
(6)
Smoking. There shall be no smoking in the sidewalk café area.
(7)
Noise and lighting. Noise shall be kept at such a level as to not disturb adjacent commercial or residential properties. Lighting shall be directed on the sidewalk café and shall not intrude on public or other private property.
(8)
Sanitation. It shall be the responsibility and duty of the restaurant to which the sidewalk café permit is issued to maintain the area covered by the permit in a clean, neat, and orderly manner at all times. The area shall be cleared of all debris at all times. It shall also be the responsibility of the permit holder to wash the sidewalk surface on which the sidewalk café is located no less often than once every three (3) months. Permittee shall sweep the sidewalk and collect the debris prior to washing. The permittee shall maintain records of the date sidewalk was washed, and such records shall be open to inspection by the City of West Point Street Department. Pressure washing of the sidewalk is not permitted due to the brick pavers and sand filled base. The use of cleaning agents is prohibited. Warm soapy water is all that is allowed for cleaning. All tables and chairs are to be kept clean, sanitary, safe, and in structurally sound condition at all times.
(9)
Removal of furnishings. All tables, chairs, and dividers of a sidewalk café shall be removed from the public sidewalk area as notified by the street department for sidewalk cleaning or other reasons determined by the city.
(10)
Suspension or modification of operation. The director shall have the authority to require any sidewalk café operating in an area created by this section to suspend operation and clear such area, or to move or modify the location or operation of the sidewalk café, for things such as, but not limited to:
a.
Any permitted special event;
b.
Any street, sidewalk, or utility construction;
c.
Any emergency situations; or
d.
The protection of the health, safety, and welfare of the public.
(Ord. No. 2010-04, Exh. A, 5-10-10)
Sec. 6-408. - Liability and insurance.
(a)
The permittee agrees to indemnify, defend, save, and hold harmless the City of West Point, its officers and employees, from any and all claims, liability, damages, and causes of action which may arise out of the permit or the permittee's activity on the premises.
(b)
The permittee agrees to meet and maintain for the entire permit period, at its own expense, the following requirements:
(1)
Commercial general liability in the amount of one million dollars ($1,000,000.00) per occurrence for bodily injury and property damage. The City of West Point must be named as an additional insured on this policy, and an endorsement must be issued as part of the policy reflecting compliance with this requirement. Proof of insurance shall be presented prior to issuance of permit.
(2)
The City of West Point must receive thirty (30) days written notice prior to any cancellation, nonrenewal, or material change in the coverage provided.
(3)
The permittee must provide an original certificate of insurance as evidence that the above requirements have been met prior to issuance of a permit.
(Ord. No. 2010-04, Exh. A, 5-10-10)
Sec. 6-409. - Revocation or suspension of permit.
The approval of a sidewalk café permit is conditional at all times. A sidewalk café permit may be revoked or suspended by the director if it is found that:
(1)
Any necessary business or health permit has been suspended, revoked, or cancelled.
(2)
The permittee does not have insurance which is correct and effective in the minimum amounts described in subsection 6-408(b)(1).
(3)
The permittee has failed to correct violations of this article or any other ordinance within forty-eight (48) hours of receipt of the director's notice of same delivered in writing to the permittee.
(4)
The permittee has a history of violations of this chapter of three (3) or more within a two-year period, or immediately upon any violation depending upon severity of the violation.
(5)
Permits may be suspended for a period up to twelve (12) months depending upon history and severity of violations.
(Ord. No. 2010-04, Exh. A, 5-10-10)
Sec. 6-410. - Fines for violators.
The following minimum fines shall be imposed for violations of this article:
(1)
First citation: One hundred dollars ($100.00) fine.
(2)
Second citation (within one-year period): Two hundred fifty dollars ($250.00) fine.
(3)
Third citation (within one-year period): Five hundred dollars ($500.00) fine.
(Ord. No. 2010-04, Exh. A, 5-10-10)
Sec. 6-411. - Americans with Disabilities Act.
Any person or entity receiving a permit hereunder agrees to fully comply with all requirements of the Americans with Disabilities Act as currently existing or as may be hereafter amended and all other federal, state and local laws.
(Ord. No. 2010-04, Exh. A, 5-10-10)

(Ord. No. 2010-04, Exh. A, 5-10-10)
