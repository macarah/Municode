ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL[2]

Sec. 19-1. - Due date for payment of taxes.
All taxes due the city shall be payable on and after the first day of October and until the twentieth day of December in each year. The city clerk-treasurer shall publish once, ten (10) days before the time of payment of taxes, a notice thereof in some newspaper circulated in the city.
(Code 1967, § 22-11)
Sec. 19-2. - Executions for delinquent taxes.
After the twentieth day of December in each year the clerk shall issue executions on all assessments upon which taxes have not been paid.
(Code 1967, § 22-12)
Sec. 19-3. - Rate of taxation to be fixed.
Not later than the second Monday in September of each year the city council shall determine the rate of taxation for that year.
(Code 1967, § 22-14)
Sec. 19-4. - Collection of back taxes.
(a)
When any property subject to taxation has not been returned for taxation assessed in any year or years prior to the then present year and no taxes paid thereon for such prior year or years, or when any person shall fail to return property within the time prescribed, the tax assessors shall assess a valuation for the year and double tax it. The assessors shall give to the owner notice of the time and place when they will fix such valuation. If the owner is unknown, no notice need be given. Should the amount of such double tax not be paid within five (5) days and application for a release or decrease has not been filed, then execution shall issue therefor, as for other taxes, against the owner of such property, if known, against the particular property.
(b)
In case returns on assessments have been made for any prior years and no taxes paid and no executions issued thereon, the clerk-treasurer shall give the owner of the property five (5) days' notice that execution will be issued and, if the amount of the taxes thereon is not paid in said five (5) days, then execution shall issue and be collected as provided for other taxes.
(Code 1967, § 22-16)
Sec. 19-5. - Executions and sales.
All executions of the city for taxes shall be made returnable to the second regular meeting of the council after the issuing of the execution. Whenever any property is levied on under any execution issued by authority of the city, it shall be advertised in the same manner and the time, place and method of conducting the sale and making titles shall be the same as sheriff's sales for state and county taxes, except that the officer making the levy may advertise the sale in any one of the newspapers published in the city, and the sale may be conducted by the chief of police; and provided, further, where the property levied on is personalty, the sale shall be advertised by posting a notice at the council chamber door and at two (2) other places in the city ten (10) days before the sale.
(Code 1967, § 22-17)
Sec. 19-6. - Sales and use tax imposed.
A combination of city-county local sales and use tax at the rate of one (1) percent is hereby levied by the city council.
(Code 1967, § 22-22)

Sec. 19-7. - Hotel-motel and accommodations excise tax.
(a)
Levied; amount; exception. There is hereby levied an excise tax in the city upon the furnishing for value to the public of any room or rooms, lodging, or accommodations furnished by any person or legal entity licensed by, or required to pay business licenses or occupation taxes to the city for operating a hotel, motel, inn, lodge, tourist camp, tourist cabin, or any other place in which rooms, lodgings, or accommodations are regularly furnished for value in the amount of five (5) percent of the charge to the public for the furnishing of such accommodations; provided that such tax shall not be levied upon the fees or charges for any rooms, lodgings, or accommodations for a period of more than ten (10) consecutive days or for use as meeting rooms or upon the fees or charges for any rooms, lodgings, or accommodations furnished for a period of one (1) or more consecutive days for use by state or local government officials or employees when traveling on official business.
(b)
Collection; payment. The excise tax levied hereby shall be collected by the person or legal entity furnishing such accommodations at the time the same are furnished and shall be reported to the city quarterly on the first day of January, the first day of April, the first day of July and the first day of October of each year upon forms made available by the clerk-treasurer of the city and payment of the excise tax levied hereby shall be made to the city at the time each quarterly return is filed; provided, however, that the excise tax adopted shall be effective on February 1, 1991, with the first quarterly return and payment to be due on April 1, 1991, as authorized by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(a)(12).
(c)
When due; late penalty. The quarterly return required in O.C.G.A. § 30-5-302 shall be filed and the payment of the excise tax levied hereby shall be due and payable on the first day of each quarter. A penalty of ten (10) percent of the tax due shall be assessed against those persons or legal entities who shall fail to file the return and to pay the tax on or before the first day of the month following the due date and in addition to the penalty, interest as provided by law shall thereafter accrue and be due and payable.
(d)
Use of funds. An amount equal to the amount by which the total taxes collected under this article exceed three (3) percent shall be used for the purposes specified in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(a)(3) and as required therein such funds shall be expended only through a contract or contracts with the state, a department of state government, a state authority, a convention and visitors bureau authority created by a local act of the general assembly for the city, or a private sector nonprofit organization selected by the city or through a contract or contracts with some combination of such entities or organizations. Any such private sector nonprofit organization shall be an organization as defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(a)(8)(A).
(e)
Budget required. Prior to the imposition of the additional excise tax imposed pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. 30-5-301 as amended, and prior to each fiscal year thereafter in which this tax is imposed, the city shall adopt a budget plan specifying how the expenditure requirements of this section will be met and prior to the adoption of such budget plan, the city shall receive from the entity with which it proposes to contract to meet the expenditure requirements of this section, a budget for expenditures to be made by such organization and such budget shall be made a part of the municipal budget plan as required by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(a)(9)(A).
(f)
Report required. As contemplated by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-56, the city, as a condition of continuing authorization to impose the tax authorized by this section, shall annually file with the state department of community affairs a report specifying the rate of taxation and the amounts collected and expended pursuant to O.C.G.A. tit. 48, ch. 13, art. III.
(Ord. of 1-21-91)

ARTICLE III. - EXCISE TAX ON ENERGY USED IN MANUFACTURING

Sec. 19-20. - Short title.
This article shall be known as the "West Point Energy Excise Tax Ordinance."
(Ord. No. 12-06, § 3, 11-12-12)
Sec. 19-21. - Findings and intent.
Georgia law authorizes municipalities to impose an excise tax on the sale or use of energy. The City of West Point has accomplished the imposition of such excise tax through intergovernmental agreement with Troup County, Georgia, as to those portions of West Point which are within Troup County. The purpose of this article is to apply the authorized excise tax on the sale or use of energy within those portions of the corporate limits of the City of West Point, as may be modified from time to time, which are within Harris County, Georgia.
(Ord. No. 12-06, § 3, 11-12-12)
Sec. 19-22. - Definitions.
[The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:]
Dealer shall have the same meaning as provided for in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-110.
Energy shall have the same meaning as provided for in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-110.
Local sales and use tax shall have the same meaning as provided for in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-110.
Purchaser shall have the same meaning as provided for in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-110.
(Ord. No. 12-06, § 3, 11-12-12)
Sec. 19-23. - Energy excise tax imposed.
(a)
In accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. Title 48, Chapter 13, Article 6, there is imposed within the territorial limits of West Point which are in Harris County, Georgia, an excise tax on the sale or use of energy when such sale or use would have constituted a taxable event for purposes of sales and use tax under O.C.G.A. Title 48, Chapter 8, Article 1 but for the exemption found in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2.
(b)
The sales or use of energy when such sale or use would have constituted a taxable event for purposes of sales and use tax under O.C.G.A. Title 48, Chapter 8, Article 1 but for the exemption found in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2 includes, but is not limited to, the items listed in subsection (e) of the same Code section.
(Ord. No. 12-06, § 3, 11-12-12)
Sec. 19-24. - Energy excise tax rate and phase-in.
(a)
The rate of the excise tax levied and collected pursuant to this article shall be phased in over a four (4) year period as directed by O.C.G.A. §§ 48-13-112(a)(3) and 48-13-112(b) and as set forth below:
(1)
For the period commencing January 1, 2013, and concluding at the last moment of December 31, 2013, such excise tax shall be at a rate equivalent to twenty-five (25) percent of the total amount of local sales and use tax in effect in such special district that would be collected on the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy but for the exemption in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2;
(2)
For the period commencing January 1, 2014, and concluding at the last moment of December 31, 2014, such excise tax shall be at a rate equivalent to fifty (50) percent of the total amount of local sales and use tax in effect in such special district that would be collected on the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy but for the exemption in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2;
(3)
For the period commencing January 1, 2015, and concluding at the last moment of December 31, 2015, such excise tax shall be at a rate equivalent to seventy-five (75) percent of the total amount of local sales and use tax in effect in such special district that would be collected on the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy but for the exemption in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2; and
(4)
On or after January 1, 2016, such excise tax shall be at a rate equivalent to one hundred (100) percent of the total amount of local sales and use tax in effect in such special district that would be collected on the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy but for the exemption in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2.
(b)
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2(c)(4), if a project of regional significance under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3(92) is started in the special district, it shall not be subject to the phase-in period contained in subsections (a)(1), (2) and (3) of this section, and the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy in connection therewith shall be subject to the rate specified in subsection (a)(4) of this section notwithstanding the January 1, 2016, limitation in that subsection.
(c)
Following such initial imposition during the phase-in time period, on or after January 1, 2016, the rate of the excise tax levied and collected by the governing authority of the city shall be directed by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-112(d). Such rate shall be controlled by the maximum amount of local sales and use tax in effect in the special district, but in no event more than two (2) percent.
(d)
In the event the total rate of local sales and use taxes in effect in the special district decreases from two (2) percent to one (1) percent, the rate of the excise tax under this article shall likewise be reduced at the same time such local sales and use tax rate reduction becomes effective.
(e)
In the event the total rate of local sales and use taxes in effect in the special district increases from one (1) percent to two (2) percent, the rate of the excise tax under this article shall likewise be increased at the same time such local sales and use tax rate increase becomes effective.
(Ord. No. 12-06, § 3, 11-12-12)
Sec. 19-25. - Energy excise tax imposition, remittance and recovery.
(a)
The excise tax levied and collected pursuant to this article shall be imposed at the time and be recoverable as governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-112(c),
(b)
The excise tax shall be a debt of the purchaser of energy until it is paid and shall be recoverable at law in the same manner as authorized for the recovery of other debts as governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-112(c).
(c)
The dealer collecting the excise tax shall remit the excise tax to the city if the city is the governing authority imposing the excise tax as governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-112(c).
(d)
Every dealer subject to an excise tax under this article shall be liable for the excise tax at an applicable rate on the charges actually collected or the amount of the excise taxes collected from the purchasers, whichever is greater as governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-112(c).
(Ord. No. 12-06, § 3, 11-12-12)
Sec. 19-26. - Exemption certificates.
(a)
The governing authority of the city, or the collecting officer appointed under this article, shall make diligent efforts to identify all energy providers and purchasers in the city and to ascertain whether such purchasers have completed a uniform exemption certificate provided by the Georgia Department of Revenue seeking to qualify such purchaser under the exemption found in O.C.G.A. § [48-8-3.2]. Such certificate shall be utilized by such governing authority or collecting officer for the purpose of determining the applicability of the excise tax under this article.
(b)
An energy provider shall be authorized to rely upon such uniform exemption certificates provided by the Georgia Department of Revenue. The energy provider shall not be liable for failing to collect and remit the excise tax imposed under this article if a purchaser has failed to submit the uniform exemption certificate provided by the Georgia Department of Revenue to such energy provider.
(Ord. No. 12-06, § 3, 11-12-12)
Sec. 19-27. - Commencement of collections and due date.
(a)
The excise tax imposed pursuant to this article shall become effective on January 1, 2013.
(b)
The excise tax levied pursuant to this article shall be imposed only at the time that sales and use tax on the sale and use of such energy would have been due and payable under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-30 but for the exemption in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2.
(c)
The excise tax shall be due and payable in the same manner as would otherwise be required under O.C.G.A. Title 48, Chapter 8, Article 1.
(d)
All sales and purchases taxable under this article during the preceding calendar month shall be due and payable to the City of West Point monthly on or before the 20th day of every month and each respective month in which such taxes are collected, and payment shall be accompanied by a return for the preceding monthly period showing the gross sales and purchases arising from all sales and purchases taxable under this article during the preceding calendar month.
(Ord. No. 12-06, § 3, 11-12-12)
Sec. 19-28. - Expenditure of proceeds.
The proceeds of the excise tax levied and collected pursuant to this article shall not be subject to any use or expenditure requirements provided for under any of the local sales and use taxes but shall be authorized to be expended in the same manner as otherwise would have been required under such local sales and use taxes or may be expended for any lawful purpose as governed by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-112(c). Any proceeds received by the city pursuant to this article shall be deposited in the general fund of the city.
(Ord. No. 12-06, § 3, 11-12-12)
Sec. 19-29. - Termination of municipal excise tax.
(a)
If the city has levied and collected an excise tax within the corporate limits of municipality under this article because the county in which the city is located has refused or failed to enter into an intergovernmental agreement with each municipality wishing to participate in such excise tax as allowed by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-115(a)(1) and then the county determines to commence proceedings for the imposition of the excise tax as allowed by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-115(b)(2), then the excise tax levied and collected by the municipality shall cease to the extent as provided for by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-115(b)(2).
(b)
If the county has commenced proceedings for the imposition of the excise tax as allowed by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-115(b)(2) and the excise tax levied and collected by the municipality has terminated and the municipality has chosen to participate in the county excise tax, then this article shall remain effective for the purposes of O.C.G.A. Title 48, Chapter 13, Article 6 and particularly for the rates required by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-112.
(Ord. No. 12-06, § 3, 11-12-12)
Sec. 19-30. - Administrative provisions and audit.
(a)
The city governing authority shall be authorized to designate a collecting officer for collection and administration of the excise tax under this article.
(b)
The collecting officer shall be authorized to provide for procedures necessary to the proper implementation of this article, including, but not limited to, periodic auditing of dealers collecting and remitting the excise tax.
(c)
The collecting officer, or designee, may examine the books, papers, records, financial reports, equipment and other facilities of and locations within the city of any purchaser which is required to remit the excise tax to the energy dealer under this article.
(d)
As part by the audit report required by O.C.G.A. § 36-81-7, the auditor shall include, in a separate schedule, a report of the revenues pertaining to the excise tax under this article.
(Ord. No. 12-06, § 3, 11-12-12)
