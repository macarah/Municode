ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 4-1. - Definitions.
Unless otherwise defined herein, the definitions contained within O.C.G.A. § 3-1-1 et seq., shall be applicable to this chapter. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning.
Alcoholic beverage means and includes all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, malt beverage, wine or fortified wine.
Calendar year means the period of time from the date of issuance of a license to December 31 of the year of issuance of said license.
Distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume, including, but not limited to, all fortified wines.
Election day means any general election, primary election, runoff election, special election, or referendum within the incorporated areas of the city.
First public notice means the date of the posting of the sign required in this article on the premises.
Malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than six percent alcohol by volume, and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer, and strong beer. The term "malt beverage" does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Private club means a private club affiliated with a nationally chartered organization which has at least 50 regular dues-paying members and suitable kitchen and dining room space for the serving of meals to their members and guests.
Restaurant means any establishment which is licensed to sell alcoholic beverages and which derives at least 70 percent of its total annual gross income from the sale of prepared meals or food and which has a seating capacity of not less than 40 persons.
Retailer or retail dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages, either in unbroken packages or for consumption on the premises, at retail only to consumers and not for resale.
School means any state or county school or such other schools as teach the subjects commonly taught in the common schools and colleges of the state.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes, either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added.
(1)
The term "wine" includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products.
(2)
The term "wine" does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage.
(3)
A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this section.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-2; Res. of 8-6-2012, § 1; Res. of 7-7-2015)
State Law reference— Similar definitions, O.C.G.A. § 3-1-1 et seq.
Sec. 4-2. - Compliance with applicable law required.
The requirements of this chapter shall be in addition to any other requirements for business licenses under this Code and, if other provisions of this Code conflict with this chapter, then this chapter shall control. In addition, all licensees shall at all times comply with federal and state laws, municipal ordinances and the rules and regulations of the state revenue commissioner, including licensing qualifications.
Sec. 4-3. - Penalties.
(a)
Violation of any provision of this chapter for failure to comply with any of its requirements shall constitute a misdemeanor. Any person who violates this chapter or fails to comply with any of its requirements shall, upon conviction thereof, be fined the maximum allowable by law or imprisoned for not more than the maximum allowable by law, or both, and, in addition, shall pay all costs and expenses involved in such case. As each such violation continues it shall be considered a separate offense.
(b)
In addition thereto and totally and separately distinct therefrom, any violation of any of the provisions of this chapter or failure to comply with any of its requirements shall be grounds for immediate suspension or revocation of any and all licenses, regardless of the number of locations involved, and regardless of whether or not the holder of the license knew or contributed to such violation. It is the intent of this subsection without limiting the generality of the foregoing to grant to the council the right to suspend or revoke any or all licenses held by a licensee when any violation of this chapter occurs at any place of business where alcoholic beverages are sold pursuant, to a license issued hereunder.
(c)
Any violation of state statute pertaining to alcoholic beverages shall constitute a violation of this chapter.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-23)

ARTICLE II. - LICENSES[2]

Sec. 4-25. - License declared privilege.
Notwithstanding anything in this article to the contrary, the distribution, sale, handling, and other dealing in or possession of alcoholic beverages in the city is a privilege and not a right, and the issuance of a license under this chapter shall not create any property rights in the license holder. Furthermore, such privilege shall not be exercised in the city except as licensed under the terms of this chapter.
State Law reference— Similar provision, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-1.
Sec. 4-26. - Classification of licenses.
The city shall issue five classes of alcoholic beverage licenses:
(1)
Class A, permitting the sale of malt beverages and wine for consumption off the premises;
(2)
Class B, permitting the sale and distribution of malt beverages and wine at wholesale;
(3)
Class C, permitting the manufacture of malt beverages and wine;
(4)
Class D, permitting the sale of malt beverages and wine for consumption on the premises of qualified restaurants; and
(5)
Class E, permitting the sale of malt beverages, wine and distilled spirits by the drink for consumption only on the premises.
(Res. of 1-9-2018(1))
Sec. 4-27. - Required.
(a)
No person, firm or corporation shall offer for sale or sell any alcoholic beverage within the incorporated areas of the city without first obtaining the appropriate license herein required.
(b)
The possession of more than one standard case of malt beverages of any kind by any person in a place of business operated by him, or on the premises used by him for business purposes shall be presumed to be a keeper of such beverage for the purpose of sale, and unless such person shall have complied with the terms of this chapter he shall be guilty of dealing in such beverages without a license and subject to the penalties as provided in this chapter.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-3)
State Law reference— Dealing in malt beverages at wholesale and retail prohibited without local license, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-40; county licensing fees for the sale of malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-41; restriction on county licensing fees for the sale of malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-43; dealing in wine at wholesale and retail prohibited without local license, O.C.G.A. § 3-8-40.
Sec. 4-28. - Separate license required for each business location.
A separate alcoholic beverage license shall be required for each retail place of business licensed under this chapter.
Sec. 4-29. - Payment of other taxes required for license issuance.
No license shall be issued under this chapter unless the applicant has fully paid all license fees and taxes due under this chapter as well as all other business taxes and ad valorem taxes, and any utility billings due to the city for the business location.
Sec. 4-30. - Qualifications for licensure.
No alcoholic beverage license shall be granted to any applicant, unless that applicant meets the following qualifications, in addition to all other requirements of this chapter:
(1)
All applicants must be at least 21 years of age at the time of filing an application for license under this chapter.
(2)
All applicants for retail licenses must provide with the application for license proof of financial responsibility. The city council may require all applicants to provide financial statements and other evidence of financial responsibility in conjunction with any application.
(3)
The applicant must be a citizen of the United States or a resident alien, and a resident of the city for a period of one year preceding the date of filing the application, except as otherwise specifically stated in this section; provided, however, that residency requirements may, at the discretion of the city council, be waived if the applicant designates a resident of the city or county who shall be responsible for any matter relating to the license.
(4)
No applicant who has a criminal record (except minor traffic offenses) shall be granted a license. The plea of nolo contendere and the forfeiture of bond or collateral shall be considered a conviction for the purposes of this subsection. Additionally, no applicant who has been convicted of violating any ordinance of the city or any law or regulation of the state or any political subdivision thereof, governing the sale of any alcoholic beverage shall be granted a license.
(5)
No license shall be issued to an applicant who has previously had revoked, within five years next preceding his application, any license issued to him by any city or county in this or any other state to sell alcoholic beverages of any kind.
Sec. 4-31. - Moral character of applicant and employees.
(a)
All applicants for alcoholic beverage licenses must be of good character, and all managers, clerks or other employees shall be of like character or the application will not be considered.
(b)
Ascertainable standards of the good moral character of the applicant that may be considered by the mayor and council in deciding if a license will be issued include, but are not limited to, the following:
(1)
Present and prior compliance with city ordinances.
(2)
Prior supervision over employees and customers.
(3)
Truthfulness in submitted application.
(4)
Criminal and civil court records.
(5)
Employment history.
(6)
Credit reports.
Sec. 4-32. - Active involvement in business required.
(a)
Applicants for license and licensees under this chapter shall be either the owner of the premises for which the license is held or the owner of a lease thereon, and a copy of any applicable premises lease shall be provided with the license application. If the applicant is a franchisee, then such applicant shall attach a copy of the franchise agreement or contract with the application.
(b)
The applicant for a license under this chapter must be active in and solely responsible for the management and operation of the business for which the license is granted.
Sec. 4-33. - Partnership applicants.
If the applicant is a partnership, all members of the partnership must be qualified to obtain a license, one member of the partnership must be a resident of the county, and all members of the partnership must make sworn statements of those qualifications.
Sec. 4-34. - Corporate applicants.
A license may be granted to a corporation existing under the law of the state or to a foreign corporation authorized to do business in the state; provided, however, that any such corporation shall be required to designate a resident of the county as its local agent responsible for compliance with all the requirements of this chapter and authorize to accept all services of notice.
Sec. 4-35. - Application; filing period; when and by whom granted.
(a)
Each applicant shall apply to the clerk for a license by written application on forms approved by the council. All questions and information requested on the application form shall be filled in and subscribed to by all applicants under oath.
(b)
All applications must be filed at least 30 days prior to the date considered by the council.
(c)
Applications will be considered and license granted only at a regular meeting of the council.
(d)
If the owner of the business is an individual, the application shall be made by such individual, unless he is not a citizen of this state. In such event, the application shall be made by a person who is a citizen of this state and who is actively engaged in the management of the business for which such license is sought.
(e)
If the owner of the business for which a license is sought is a partnership or corporation, the application shall be made by one of the partners or an officer of the corporation who is actively engaged in the management of the business. In the event that no partner or officer is a citizen of this state, then the application shall be made by a designated agent of the business who is actively engaged in management of the business.
(f)
In the event that a firm, partnership or corporation owns or operates more than one place of business in the city, all applications for licenses for each place of business made pursuant to this chapter shall be made by the same person.
(g)
If the application is being made on behalf of a private club, the application shall be made by an officer of such private club.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-4; Ord. No. 2, § IV(2), 8-7-1989)
Sec. 4-36. - Advertisement.
(a)
Each licensee presenting an application for licensure shall publish in the legal gazette of the county a notice of the fact that application has been made by the licensee and that the matter will be heard by the council at its next regular scheduled meeting immediately following the notice. The notice shall be published once a week for three consecutive weeks.
(b)
The applicant is required to show proof of three newspaper advertisements by affidavit of publisher or his designee and is responsible for expenses incurred for such advertising.
(c)
No application will be considered until proof of full compliance with the requirements of this section has been provided to the council.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-5)
Sec. 4-37. - Nonrefundable application fee.
Every application, excluding renewals, shall be accompanied by a nonrefundable application fee in the established by the city council. Said fee shall be paid by certified check, cashier's cheek or money order made payable to the city and shall be credited toward the license fee if license is granted.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-6)
Sec. 4-38. - Investigations; recommendations.
The application shall be sworn to and directed to the clerk who shall inspect the application. The clerk or the designee of the clerk shall cause the character and reputation of the applicant and suitability of the location of the establishment for which the license is sought to be fully investigated. After all of the foregoing requirements have been fulfilled, applications for original licenses shall be presented to the council.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-7)
Sec. 4-39. - Hearing; consideration; and denial of license.
All applications presented shall be noted as to the date and exact time of filing and, after filing, such applications shall be presented and considered by the council in the order of their filing at the next regularly scheduled meeting of the council, provided 30 days have elapsed after the date of filing.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-8)
Sec. 4-40. - Factors considered in determining whether application should be granted.
(a)
In determining whether any license applied for hereunder shall be granted, in addition to all the other provisions of this chapter, in the public interest and welfare, the following will be considered:
(1)
The applicant's reputation, character, mental, and physical capacity to conduct this business;
(2)
If the applicant is a previous holder of a license to sell any alcoholic beverage, whether or not he has violated any law, regulation or ordinance relating to such business;
(3)
If the applicant is a previous holder of a license to sell any alcoholic beverage, the manner in which he conducted the business thereunder as to the necessity for unusual police observation and inspection in order to prevent the violation of any law, regulation, or ordinance relating to such business; and
(4)
Whether the person has previously had an alcoholic beverage license suspended or revoked;
(5)
No license shall be granted to any existing building or establishment having a history of or reputation for prostitution or other sex offenses; fighting, shooting, stabbing, or other violence; gambling; illegal dealing in alcoholic beverages or drugs; or other violations of the law. By history or reputation is meant within the 12 months next preceding the filing of said application and provided said business is now owned, operated, managed, or in any way connected to the person operating said business during the times these offenses occurred.
(b)
No license shall be granted for the sale of malt beverages or wine at retail where the proposed location does not have a front entrance to the premises which is clearly visible from a public street, road, or highway, except as to a private club.
(c)
No license shall be granted to a retailer under any provision or section of this chapter unless the front entrance to the building is clearly visible from a street, road, or highway, and the front of said building shall have a window of a standard size and no screen, blind, curtain, partition, article, or thing which shall prevent a clear view into the interior of the building shall be permitted in the window or upon the doors of any building where retail sales are permitted. Each building shall be so lighted that the interior of the premises is visible day or night and all parking areas adjacent to or used in connection with said business shall be fully lighted and unobstructed.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-8)
Sec. 4-41. - Location.
No license shall be issued hereunder where the place of business is located within 200 yards of any school or within 100 yards of any church. All distances herein referred to shall be measured at the closest point between the entrance to such place of business and the property line of such school or church.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-19)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.
Sec. 4-42. - Annual license fees.
Annual license fees for licenses issued under this chapter shall be in the amount provided in the city fee schedule. When a license has been approved and granted, the fee for same shall be paid to the council by certified check, cashier's check, or money order in the full amount of the license fee.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-9)
Sec. 4-43. - License period; lapse for nonuse.
All licenses shall be issued for a calendar year. In case of the revocation or surrender of a license before the expiration of such calendar year, the holder thereof shall not be entitled to receive a refund whatsoever. All licenses must be obtained no later than 90 days from the date of the approval of the application, and, if not so obtained, said license shall be void.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-10)
Sec. 4-44. - Renewal permitted; conditions.
All alcoholic beverage licenses in this article may be renewed by payment of the fees set forth in the city fee schedule, provided that:
(1)
Applications for renewal of existing licenses are made in person by the applicants at the office of the city clerk between December 1 through November 30 of each year for the succeeding year and the prescribed license fee paid at time of filing.
(2)
The licensee presents with the application for renewal proof of publication placed in the legal gazette of the county a notice of the fact that application for renewal has been made by the licensee and that the matter will be heard by the council at its next regular scheduled meeting immediately following the notice.
(3)
The licensee and location remain the same.
(4)
The licensee's circumstances would enable him to qualify for a license if the renewal was a new application.
(5)
The licensee is still in compliance with the requirements of state and municipal laws and regulations pertaining to the original issuance of the license and pertaining to the operation of the business under the license at the time of application for renewal.
(6)
The city has received no complaints regarding the licensee's operation of the licensed premises.
Sec. 4-45. - Effect of complaint on license renewal; complaint procedure.
If a complaint is received as to the licensee's alleged violation of state or municipal law or regulation before November 30 of the licensed year or 30 days after the application for renewal is received, whichever date is later, the license shall not be renewed but shall be referred to the mayor and council for decision as to whether renewal shall be granted as follows:
(1)
The mayor and council shall conduct a hearing after giving the licensee reasonable notice of the time and place of such hearing, as well as the purpose thereof.
(2)
The licensee may remain open for business until a decision by the mayor and council is made and shall have a period of three days following that decision in which to pay the required fees if the decision of mayor and council is to permit renewal of the license.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-11)
Sec. 4-46. - Sale of business.
(a)
If an individual, corporation, firm or partnership sells the business at which a license issued hereunder authorizes the sale of malt beverages and wine, such license or licenses shall automatically terminate unless the new owner shall file an application for the continuance of the license or licenses within 30 days from the date of the purchase of said business and pay the processing fee established by the city council, which fee shall not be refundable.
(b)
When an application for continuance has been filed and the processing fee has been paid, the council shall cause an investigation to be made in accordance with the provisions of this chapter, and, if the application is approved by the mayor and council, the clerk is hereby authorized to issue a temporary permit allowing such new owner to continue the sale of malt beverages and wine until the end of the license year for which the business is licensed.
(c)
Any person who has been granted a temporary license to finish out the license as provided in this section shall make application for a license as a new applicant prior to the expiration of such temporary license, and all provisions of this chapter shall apply as if no license had ever been granted to the applicant.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-12)
Sec. 4-47. - Transfers.
(a)
No license granted hereunder shall be transferable from one person to another. No license shall be transferred from one location to another.
(b)
In the event that the holder of a license on behalf of a corporation or private club ceases to be an officer of such corporation or is no longer associated with such private club, such corporation or private club shall apply within 15 days after such event has occurred for a new license to be issued to the person holding the same office as the original applicant and pay a transfer fee in the amount established by the city council.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-13)
Sec. 4-48. - Investigations incident to disciplinary action.
The city council shall have the right to make such inquiry or investigation as it may find to be reasonably necessary to determine the licensee's compliance or noncompliance with this chapter. Such investigation may consist of, among other actions, calling licensees for examination under oath, obtaining evidence under oath from other persons, the procurement of documents and records including records of the licensee, and inspection and examination of records and documents from whatever source obtainable.
Sec. 4-49. - Suspension or revocation.
(a)
The council shall have the full power and authority to revoke, refuse to renew, suspend, or place on probation any license to sell malt beverages and wine for violation of this chapter or violation of any state or federal laws.
(b)
Any untrue or misleading information contained or material omissions in the application for a license shall be cause for the denial thereof, and, if any license has been granted under such circumstances, the same shall be subject to revocation.
(c)
Should any licensee be convicted or plead guilty or plead nolo contendere to any felony, such license shall be immediately and automatically revoked and cancelled.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-14)
Sec. 4-50. - Display of license.
The city license shall at all times be posted in a conspicuous place within the building, and, if said license is not so posted, said business shall not be authorized to sell alcoholic beverages until same shall be posted.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-15)
State Law reference— Similar provision, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-3.
Sec. 4-51. - Due process rights regarding denial, suspension and revocation of license.
(a)
Under this chapter, the council has the discretionary powers within the guidelines of due process as hereinafter defined to grant, refuse, and to suspend or revoke any licenses in accordance with the standards set forth in this chapter.
(b)
All decisions by the council denying, suspending, or revoking such licenses shall be in writing with the reasons therefor stated and mailed or delivered to the applicant personally within ten days from the date of the decision by the council.
(c)
Upon application within ten days of the receipt of the notice of denial, suspension, or revocation, the applicant shall be afforded a hearing with an opportunity to present evidence and cross examine opposing witnesses.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-24)

ARTICLE III. - EXCISE TAXES[3]

State Law reference— Imposition of excise tax on malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-80; imposition of excise tax on wine, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-60; imposition by municipalities of excise tax on distilled spirits package sales, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-80; imposition by municipalities of excess taxes on distilled spirits sold by the drink, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-130.

Sec. 4-74. - Levy; rate.
There is imposed and levied a specific tax upon all retail dealers in malt beverages and wine within the city limits at the highest rate permitted by state law.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 2-5-41)
Sec. 4-75. - Collection.
(a)
The excise taxes shall be paid to the city by each wholesale dealer on all malt beverages and wine sold within the incorporated areas of the city not later than the tenth day of each month, based upon the units of beer and wine sold during the previous calendar month by the wholesalers to retailers in the city.
(b)
The wholesale dealer shall keep true and correct records of all sales and shipments and shall render a sworn statement of the same accompanying the monthly report to the city and the report shall show the exact quantities of malt beverages or wine, by size and type of container, and the amount of excise tax collected.
(c)
The city shall have the right to audit and to require production of records from each wholesaler supplying retailers in the incorporated areas of the city and also from each retailer so supplied.
(d)
Failure to make a timely report and remittance of the aforesaid taxes shall render a wholesale dealer liable for a penalty equal to ten percent of the total amount due, in addition to the amount due; additionally, if the report is not filed or if the taxes are not remitted and paid within 30 days from the date that the same are due, the council shall have the right to suspend or revoke any wholesale license, and further to prohibit the wholesaler from making any deliveries of any type whatsoever within the incorporated areas of the city.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell at retail or otherwise within the incorporated areas of the city any malt beverages or wine on which the tax required in this article has not been paid to the wholesaler or distributor or to the city.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 2-5-42)

ARTICLE IV. - CONDUCT OF BUSINESS

Sec. 4-95. - Posting of chapter regulations.
At all times all retail licensees under this chapter must post a copy of this chapter in a conspicuous place within the place of business.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-16)
Sec. 4-96. - Advertising signs.
(a)
No signs or advertisement of alcoholic beverages shall be erected outside the place of business licensed under this chapter, except as expressly permitted by state law.
(b)
No posters or signs, lighted or unlighted, shall be affixed to any window or so placed that they will be predominately seen from the outside of the premises, except that each establishment licensed herein shall be allowed to erect or affix one lighted sign or advertisement of alcoholic beverages on an interior wall of the establishment visible from the exterior of the building. The sign must be affixed or erected on the rear wall of the establishment or at a location more than ten feet from the nearest exterior window. This sign shall only be lighted during the legal hours of sale of alcoholic beverages as provided in this chapter.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-17)
Sec. 4-97. - Hours and days of sale.
(a)
Weekdays and Saturday. No retail dealers shall engage in the sale of malt beverages, wine or distilled spirits on weekdays except between the hours of 6:00 a.m. and 2:00 a.m. and on Saturdays, between the hours of 6:00 a.m. and 12:00 midnight. All persons shall be off the premises of any package store by 2:00 a.m. on weekdays and by 12:00 midnight on Saturdays, at which time said package store shall close for business. Grocery or convenience stores where the principal business is the sale of groceries and other like items and the sale of malt beverages and wine is incidental thereto may remain open during other hours but shall not sell malt beverages or wine except during the times and hours specified herein, and, at all times when the sale of malt beverages or wine is prohibited, all malt beverages or wine shall be out of sight and under lock and key.
(b)
Sundays.
(1)
Package sales. No retail dealers shall engage in the sale of malt beverages or wine by the package on Sundays except between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m. All persons shall be off the premises of the package store by 11:30 p.m., at which time said package store shall close for business. Grocery or convenience stores where the principal business is the sale of groceries and other like items and the sale of malt beverages and wine is incidental thereto may remain open during other hours but shall not sell malt beverages or wine except during the times and hours specified herein, and, at all times when the sale of malt beverages or wine is prohibited, all malt beverages or wine shall be out of sight and under lock and key.
(2)
Consumption on the premises. No retail dealers shall engage in the sale of malt beverages, wine, or distilled spirits for consumption on the premises on Sundays except between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 12:00 midnight in any licensed establishments which derives at least 70 percent of its total annual gross sales from the sale of prepared meals or food in all of the combined retail outlets of the individual establishment where food is served.
(c)
Christmas Day. Retail dealers shall not permit the sale of malt beverages or wine on Christmas Day or any other day on which state law prohibits the sale of malt beverages and wine.
(d)
Election days. On any county, state, or national election day, unless prohibited by state law, retail dealers may engage in the sale of malt beverages and wine between the hours of 6:00 a.m. and 2:00 a.m.
(e)
Wholesalers. The business hours of wholesale dealers shall be the same hours and days as retail dealers are allowed to engage in the sale of malt beverages and wine by the terms of subsections (a) and (b) of this section.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-18; Res. of 8-6-2012, § 1; Res. of 7-7-2015)
State Law reference— Sale of alcoholic beverages on Sunday, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-7; sale of alcoholic beverages on Sundays and election days generally, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20; Sunday sales by farm wineries, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-21.2; Sunday sales of distilled spirits by private clubs, O.C.G.A. § 3-7-2.
Sec. 4-98. - Sale to minors, incompetents, and inebriates.
(a)
No malt beverages or wine shall be sold to a minor under any conditions whatsoever. A minor for the purposes of this chapter shall be considered to be a person under the age of 21 years or as provided by state law.
(b)
No malt beverages or wines shall be sold to any intoxicated person, nor shall any intoxicated person be allowed to remain upon the premises of any place of business selling malt beverages or wines.
(c)
No employees of any business selling malt beverages or wines shall work in said place of business while intoxicated and no employees shall drink malt beverages or wine while working and employed in such business.
(d)
No malt beverages or wines shall be sold to any person who is mentally incompetent.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-20)
Sec. 4-99. - Display of price.
Retailers shall indicate plainly by tags or labels on the bottles or containers or on the shelf immediately below where the containers are placed, or on an exposed sign prominently placed, the price of all malt beverages or wine exposed or offered for sale.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-21)
Sec. 4-100. - Compliance with health rules.
All retail licensees under this chapter are hereby required to comply with all rules and regulations and requirements of the county board of health, state board of health and any and all other state, federal local public health authorities.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-22)
Sec. 4-101. - Drinking in specified places prohibited.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person within the corporate limits of the city to:
(1)
Publicly engage in the drinking of alcoholic beverages.
(2)
Engage in the drinking of alcoholic beverages upon any premises upon which an athletic contest is being conducted.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person within the corporate limits of the city to drink alcoholic beverages on the premises of any retail, wholesale, or manufacturing alcoholic beverage business or business establishment.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person within the corporate limits of the city to possess an open container or to drink any alcoholic beverage on any street, highway, park, parking lot, alleyway, or public place not lawfully licensed to sell such beverages for consumption on the premises.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person to discard in any manner whatsoever containers of alcoholic beverages upon the street, road, highways, alleyway, parks, parking, lots, or other public places except in proper collection receptacles provided or upon any private property.
(Comp. Ords. 1998, § 7-3-25)
Sec. 4-102. - Prohibited products.
No license holder under this chapter shall sell, offer for sale, display, lend, rent, lease, give, exchange, keep in stock, possess with intent to sell, or otherwise distribute to any person, at any place of business licensed hereunder any of the following products:
(a)
Any "drug paraphernalia" or "drug related object" as defined by O.C.G.A. § 16-13-32(a)(1), which states:
"(1)
'Drug related object' means any instrument, devise, or object which is designed, marketed as useful primarily for one or more of the following purposes:
(A)
To inject, ingest, inhale or otherwise introduce marijuana or a controlled substance into the human body;
(B)
To enhance the effect of marijuana or a controlled substance on the human body;
(C)
To test the strength, effectiveness, or purity of marijuana or a controlled substance;
(D)
To process or prepare marijuana or a controlled substance for introduction into the human body;
(E)
To conceal any quantity of marijuana or a controlled substance; or
(F)
To contain or hold marijuana or a controlled substance while it is being introduced into the human body."
(b)
Any "drug paraphernalia" or "drug related object" which are further defined as including, but not limited to, objects used or intended for use in ingesting, inhaling or otherwise introducing marijuana or a controlled substance into the human body, such as:
(1)
Metal, wooden, acrylic, glass, stone, plastic or ceramic pipes with or without screens, permanent screens, hashish heads or punctured metal bowls;
(2)
Water pipes;
(3)
Carburetion tubes and devices;
(4)
Smoking and carburetion masks;
(5)
Roach clip; meaning objects used to hold burning materials, such as a marijuana cigarette, that has become too small or too short to be held in the hand;
(6)
Miniature cocaine spoons and cocaine vials;
(7)
Chamber pipes;
(8)
Carburetor pipes;
(9)
Electric pipes;
(10)
Air-driven pipes;
(11)
Chillums;
(12)
Bongs; and
(13)
Ice pipes or chillers.
