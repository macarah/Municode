Sec. 16-71. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Litter means all sand; gravel; slag; brickbats; rubbish; waste material; tin cans; refuse; garbage; trash; weeds, to include unkempt grass; debris; dead animals; or discarded materials of every kind and description.
Road and street shall be mutually inclusive and shall likewise be deemed to include any alley, land, court and other thoroughfare, however described or designated.
(Code 1987, § 9-31; Ord. of 8-3-1987; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 16-72. - Violation and penalty.
All persons failing to comply with the mandatory provisions of this article or doing any act prohibited by this article shall be punished as provided by section 1-8, provided that any fine imposed for a violation of this article shall not be less than $25.00.
(Code 1987, § 9-52; Ord. of 8-3-1987; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 16-73. - Deposit of garbage, trash, brush and debris on property prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sweep, throw or deposit any garbage, trash, brush, dirt, concrete, rocks, stagnant water, dead animals, discarded items or other debris into, upon or along any drain, gutter, alley, lane, sidewalk, street or vacant lot, or upon any public or private premises within the corporate city limits.
(Code 1987, § 9-32; Ord. of 8-3-1987; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 16-74. - Deposits of rubbish and junk on public places.
It shall be unlawful for any person to place, store, dump, park or broadcast any material, such as old junk, metal, brickbats, stones, plaster, lumber, trash, garbage, litter, paper or similar rubbish and junk, on any public street, sidewalk, land, alley, park or reserve in the city. Every person found in the municipal court to be in violation of this section shall be punished as provided by the Charter and city ordinances for violations of city ordinances as provided in section 1-8, provided that any fine levied for violation of this section shall not be less than $100.00, nor shall it exceed $1,000.00.
(Code 1987, § 9-33; Ord. of 8-3-1987; Ord. of 4-1-1996; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Cross reference— Streets, sidewalks and other public places, ch. 34.
Sec. 16-75. - Containers on streets or sidewalks.
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep on the public streets or sidewalks of the city any chicken coops, bread boxes, ice cream churns, soft drink or soda water crates or any similar containers, except authorized trash receptacles.
(Code 1987, § 9-34; Ord. of 8-3-1987; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Cross reference— Streets, sidewalks and other public places, ch. 34.
Sec. 16-76. - Spitting or throwing litter on sidewalks or in churches, halls, buses or other public places.
It shall be unlawful for any person to spit or throw hulls, peelings or other litter upon the sidewalks or in or upon the floors of churches, public halls, theaters, buses or other public places.
(Code 1987, § 9-35; Ord. of 8-3-1987; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Cross reference— Streets, sidewalks and other public places, ch. 34.
Sec. 16-77. - Objects injurious to persons or tires.
It shall be unlawful for any person to place or throw or to leave or abandon on any street, sidewalk or alley in the city any glass bottle or can or the fragments thereof; any broken glass or crockery of any kind; or nails, tacks, bricks or any article or object liable to injure barefoot persons walking on the streets, sidewalks or alleys or to injure or puncture the tires of automobiles, motorcycles, bicycles or the rubber tires of other vehicles.
(Code 1987, § 9-36; Ord. of 8-3-1987; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 16-78. - Handbills and advertising matter.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to place in automobiles in the city or to otherwise distribute anywhere in the city any handbill, circular, pamphlet, poster, postcard or literature of any kind except with the permission, express or implied, of the owner or occupant of such automobile.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to place on private property any handbill, circular, pamphlet, poster, postcard or literature of any kind, except with the permission, express or implied, of the owner of the property or intended recipient.
(Code 1987, § 9-37; Ord. of 8-3-1987; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 16-79. - Cargo of vehicles.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate any vehicle upon any public street, lane, alley, park, or reserve in the city, unless the cargo is in containers or covered by a tarpaulin in such a manner as to prevent any part of its contents from falling or being scattered upon any such public street, lane, alley, park or reserve in the city.
(Code 1987, § 9-38; Ord. of 8-3-1987; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 16-80. - Cleanliness of area surrounding business.
The proprietor or person in charge of every business and commercial establishment in the city is hereby required to keep the sidewalks abutting the business, driveways, yards, parking lots and areas clean at all times with sweepings placed in a container to prevent a violation of section 16-73.
(Code 1987, § 9-39; Ord. of 8-3-1987; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 16-81. - Providing containers.
Every owner, occupant, tenant or lessee using or occupying any commercial, institutional or industrial building or property shall provide adequate litter containers of sizes, numbers and types as specified by the utility department superintendent for shopping centers, supermarkets, convenience stores, fast-food restaurants, service stations and similar business establishments, including residences, churches, schools, colleges, lodges, commercial businesses, etc.
(Code 1987, § 9-40; Ord. of 8-3-1987; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 16-82. - Containers provided by parking lot owners.
Every owner or operator of a private or commercial parking lot shall provide litter containers, at a location and of a type as specified by the utility department superintendent.
(Code 1987, § 9-41; Ord. of 8-3-1987; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 16-83. - Construction sites and construction site stormwater runoff control.
Property owners and prime contractors in charge of a construction site are required to furnish litter containers for the litter from construction and workers. All litter from construction activities or any related activities shall be containerized and shall be picked up and placed in containers at the end of each workday.
Construction site operators must control waste such as discarded building materials, concrete truck washout, chemicals, litter, and sanitary waste at the construction site that may have adverse impacts to water quality. (See 40 CFR Part 122.34(b)(4)(5)(c)).
(Code 1987, § 9-42; Ord. of 8-3-1987; Ord. No. 2020-2 , 4-13-2020; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 2021-14 , adopted Feb. 14, 2022, amended the title of § 16-83 to read as herein set out. The former § 16-83 title pertained to construction sites.
Sec. 16-84. - Excessive litter from special occasions.
Excessive litter resulting from special occasions as holidays, moving or cleanup will be placed in bundles weighing not more than 50 pounds each and securely tied so that the bundles are not more than four feet long and two feet by two feet. All empty cartons will be broken down and placed in bundles in accordance with the size requirement in this section and placed at the curb for pickup.
(Code 1987, § 9-43; Ord. of 8-3-1987; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 16-85. - Cleanliness of garbage storage areas.
Every owner, occupant and lessee of a house or building used for a residence, business or commercial purpose is required to maintain garbage storage areas in a clean condition and ensure that all garbage is properly containerized. Failure to clean garbage storage areas will constitute a violation of this article.
(Code 1987, § 9-44; Ord. of 8-3-1987; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 16-86. - Loading and unloading operations.
All loading and unloading operations shall provide containers for deposit of litter created by packing or repacking operations, and the owner or occupant shall maintain surveillance to ensure that all litter is placed in a proper container and the area is kept clean.
(Code 1987, § 9-45; Ord. of 8-3-1987; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 16-87. - Cleanliness of residential sidewalks.
The owner or occupant of residential property is hereby required to keep the sidewalk in front of the property free of litter. All litter is to be placed in containers for collection by the utility department.
(Code 1987, § 9-46; Ord. of 8-3-1987; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 16-88. - Removal of trapped litter.
The owner, occupant or lessee of all property is required to remove all trapped litter and place the litter in a proper container for collection. Vacant lots, borders, parking lots, embankments, fences, walls and sidewalks are to be kept free of litter. Parking lots, shopping centers, convenience stores, drive-in restaurants and all commercial and industrial enterprises will ensure that no litter remains on the property.
(Code 1987, § 9-47; Ord. of 8-3-1987; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 16-89. - Removal of tree trunks, logs, limbs and tops.
The owner of property within the corporate city limits on which trees are cut down, whether by the owner or by another, is required to promptly, following such cutting, remove or have removed from the property at his or her own expense all tree trunks, logs, limbs and tops and other debris resulting therefrom. Such materials shall not be destroyed or removed by burning. Such materials shall not be placed in, on, or about any drainage easements, easements, streets, common areas, or rights-of-way, whether publicly or privately owned.
(Code 1987, § 9-48; Ord. of 8-3-1987; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)

Sec. 16-91. - Cleanup costs.
When litter has remained on private property for a period of at least ten days following the giving by the city to the owner of such property of written notice to remove such litter, the city is authorized, but not required, to petition a court of competent jurisdiction to enforce this section. After notice and an opportunity for a hearing and upon a finding of a violation by the court, the city may abate the nuisance and seek recovery for costs of abatement from the owner. The charges for such cleanup service shall become delinquent if not paid within 30 days from the date of billing. At the expiration of such 30-day period, execution may be issued by the city clerk-treasurer against the property upon which such service was rendered for the amount of the cleanup service charge. From and after the recording of such execution on the general execution docket in the office of the clerk of the superior court of the county, such execution shall constitute a lien on the property upon which the service was rendered until the charges have been fully paid and satisfied. Such executions may be enforced in the same manner and with the addition of interest and costs as provided by law for enforcement of executions for ad valorem taxes of the city.
(Code 1987, § 9-50; Ord. of 8-3-1987; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
Sec. 16-92. - Weed growth limited.
The growth of grass, weeds or undergrowth to a height in excess of 18 inches above the ground shall be deemed in violation of section 16-88.
(Code 1987, § 9-51; Ord. of 9-3-1991; Ord. No. 2021-14 , 2-14-2022)
