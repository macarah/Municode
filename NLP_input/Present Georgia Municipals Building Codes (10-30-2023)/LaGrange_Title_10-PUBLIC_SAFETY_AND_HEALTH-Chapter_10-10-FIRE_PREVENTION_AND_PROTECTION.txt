Article I - IN GENERAL

Sec. 10-10-1. - Division designated; chief; appointment.
There is hereby created the fire prevention and protection department of the city, the head of which shall be the chief. The chief shall be appointed by the city manager.
(Ord. No. 18-13, § 4, 11-27-18)
Sec. 10-10-2. - Powers and duties of chief.
The chief shall be responsible for the planning, directing and supervising of all the activities of the fire prevention and protection department, and to that end shall:
(1)
Exercise general control and supervision of the equipment, apparatus, and personnel of the department;
(2)
Investigate or cause to be investigated the cause, origin and circumstances of all fires within the city; maintain a system of records; prepare monthly and annual reports of fire department activities and assist in the preparation of the annual budget;
(3)
Develop, approve and conduct training programs for fire department employees and attend conferences, schools and conventions to keep abreast of the latest fire-fighting methods and administration;
(4)
Enforce all laws, ordinances and resolutions of the mayor and council pertaining to fire prevention, fire control and fire safety.
(5)
Establish procedures, rules and regulations for the conduct of the department within the framework of the general policies approved by the mayor and council.
(6)
Serve as local fire marshal deputized by the state fire marshal and as such perform the duties imposed by the state laws; and
(7)
Perform related duties as may be directed by the city manager or prescribed by ordinance or resolution of the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. 18-13, § 4, 11-27-18)
Sec. 10-10-3. - Persons allowed near fire; obedience to orders.
It shall be unlawful for any person, except firemen, the mayor, members of council, the police force, employees of the city in performance of their duties, the owners of the property, their agents and the agents of insurance companies, to be within the immediate vicinity of any fire, without being ordered there by the officer in command of the fire department; and every person shall obey the orders and directions of such officer.
(Code 1972, § 11-1; Ord. No. 06-01, § 4, 2-28-06; Ord. No. 18-13, § 3, 11-27-18)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 18-13, § 3, adopted November 27, 2018, renumbered § 10-10-1 as § 10-10-3.
Sec. 10-10-4. - Fire alarms.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to injure, destroy or in any manner interfere with the electric fire alarm system of the city, or with the batteries, boxes, stations, wires or other apparatus or appurtenances belonging thereto or used in connection therewith.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to give or cause to be given, with intent to deceive, a false alarm of fire, or to pull the slide of any station or signal box, except in case of fire.
(Code 1972, §§ 11-2, 11-3; Ord. No. 93-37, § 2, 7-13-93; Ord. No. 04-14, § 2, 4-13-04; Ord. No. 06-01, § 4, 2-28-06; Ord. No. 18-13, § 3, 11-27-18)

Sec. 10-10-50. - Fire protection systems.
All fire protection systems will be installed in accordance with the most current applicable codes. Such system must be installed and served by certified personnel of the manufacturer of the particular system, and shall be subject to the approval of the fire prevention bureau.
(Code 1972, § 11-30)
Sec. 10-10-51. - Group homes.
(a)
Generally. This section applies to group homes providing sleeping accommodations for eighteen (18) or fewer persons. In addition to the provisions hereof, every group home shall comply with the minimum requirements for one-and two-family dwellings. A group home with sleeping accommodations for more than eighteen (18) persons shall meet the requirements for dormitories as set forth in the National Fire Protection Association 101 Life Safety Code (or other title thereof), as is currently in force in the city.
(b)
Means of egress:
(1)
Every sleeping room shall have access to two (2) separate means of exit if more than three (3) persons are occupying said sleeping room;
(2)
Exit access shall have flame spread ratings class A finish or class B;
(3)
Measurement of travel distance to exit shall not exceed one hundred (100) feet.
(c)
Bedding. Bedding (mattresses) will be one hundred (100) percent cotton boric acid treated with the ticking approved under D.O.C. ff 4-72.
(d)
Alarm system:
(1)
Buildings will be equipped with automatic smoke detection.
(2)
Smoke detectors shall be installed on each floor level and in all sleeping areas.
(3)
Two (2) telephones are required.
(4)
Fire drills shall be conducted monthly. A report shall be submitted to the fire prevention bureau no later than the fifth of each month.
(e)
Occupant load. The occupant load for any floor shall be the maximum number of persons intended to occupy that floor but not less than one (1) person for each one hundred twenty (120) square feet gross floor area, pending approval of the fire department.
(Code 1972, § 11-29)
Sec. 10-10-52. - Application of building and fire related codes to existing buildings.
(a)
In accordance with the provisions of Section 8-2-203 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, there is hereby adopted and designated for enforcement thereof in the City of LaGrange the Uniform Act for the Application of Building and Fire Related Codes to Existing Buildings. In so doing, there shall be incorporated into the Code of the City of LaGrange, by reference, the applicable provisions of O.C.G.A. section 8-2-200 through section 8-2-222.
(b)
The right to appeal enforcement decisions rendered pursuant to this Code section shall be in accordance with the appeal procedure set forth in Section 10-10-46 above.
(Ord. No. 95-33, § 1, 7-25-95)

Article IV - FIRE LANES

Sec. 10-10-61. - Short title.
This article shall be known as the City of LaGrange Fire Lane Ordinance.
(Ord. No. 93-41, § 1, 8-10-93)
Sec. 10-10-62. - Purpose and scope.
The purpose of this article is to provide for the designation and identification of fire lanes; the manner, method and language for the posting of signs for fire lanes; to provide for enforcement of this article and the authority therefor; to prohibit parking in fire lanes; to identify the property subject to the provisions of this article; and to provide a penalty for violation of the provisions hereof.
(Ord. No. 93-41, § 1, 8-10-93)
Sec. 10-10-63. - Definitions.
The words in this article shall have their normal accepted meanings except as set forth below:
Authorized emergency vehicle shall include a vehicle designated as an emergency vehicle by the department of public safety; an authorized emergency service vehicle; a vehicle of the LaGrange Police Department or LaGrange Fire Department; a motor vehicle of a federal, state, local law enforcement agency; a vehicle of any utility of the city or of any other public utility corporation; provided any such vehicle is at the time being used as an emergency vehicle by a person authorized to use the same for that purpose.
City of LaGrange means the corporate limits of the City of LaGrange, Georgia.
Enforcement officer(s) shall refer to any duly authorized law enforcement officer or fire official employed by the City of LaGrange, Georgia.
Fire lane(s) are areas designated by the fire official providing access for fire department vehicles to buildings, fire department connections and fire hydrants. This includes all alleys, driveways or lanes, on public or private property available or devoted for use by the general public, where the parking of motor vehicles or other obstructions may interfere with or obstruct the ingress or egress of fire department vehicles for the protection of persons and properties and includes but is not limited to shopping centers, theatres, hospitals, bowling lanes, churches, multi-family housing, and high-rise buildings.
Fire official means the LaGrange Fire Chief, or other designated authority, or their duly authorized representative, charged with the administration and enforcement of the fire prevention code.
New building(s) means any commercial structure or facility whose certificate of occupancy was issued after the effective date of this article.
Property owner(s) shall refer to each person, firm, corporation, or entity possessing any estate, usufruct or leasehold right in the property being designated as fire lane(s).
Return mail parking citation shall mean an order issued out of Court, by an enforcement officer of the City of LaGrange, directing a violator of this article to comply with all penalties as set forth.
(Ord. No. 93-41, § 1, 8-10-93)
Sec. 10-10-64. - Identification.
(a)
All existing fire lanes shall be recognized as meeting the requirements of this article and shall not be obstructed in any manner, including the parking of vehicles, and shall be accessible to fire department vehicles or apparatus. All new buildings shall be accessible to fire department apparatus by way of fire lanes constructed in accordance with the SBCCI Standard Fire Prevention code, 1991 edition. Designated fire lanes shall be provided with an all weather driving surface of not less than twenty (20) feet, of unobstructed width. There shall be adequate roadway turning radius capable of supporting the imposed loads of fire apparatus and having a vertical clearance of thirteen (13) feet, six (6) inches. During construction, when combustibles are brought onto the site in such quantities as may be deemed hazardous by the fire official, access roads and a suitable temporary supply of water acceptable to the fire department shall be provided and maintained.
(1)
Where fire protection systems approved by the fire official are provided, the above required clearances may be modified by the fire official.
(2)
The fire official shall have the authority to require an increase in the minimum access widths where they are deemed inadequate for fire or rescue operations.
(3)
The creation of a fire lane at existing locations shall be done solely at the discretion of the fire official.
(b)
The areas designated as fire lanes shall be marked and shall have signs erected thereon at the sole expense of the property owner meeting the following criteria:
(1)
Signs shall be aluminum reflective, standard size of eighteen (18) inches by twenty-four (24) inches, with red letters on a white background, and shall be placed seven (7) feet from ground level.
(2)
Signs shall read "NO PARKING FIRE LANE" or "FIRE LANE NO PARKING AT ANY TIME".
(3)
Letters on signs shall not be less than two (2) inches in height.
(4)
One (1) sign shall be posted at the beginning of the fire lane and one (1) at the end of the fire lane. Additional signs may be required within the fire lane and each sign erected shall not be more than fifty (50) feet apart.
(5)
Signs posted shall not be more than four (4) feet from the edge of the curb, and shall be visible from both directions of the driving surface.
(6)
The fire lane shall also have the words "FIRE LANE" painted on the paved surface at distances of not more than fifty (50) feet apart.
(Ord. No. 93-41, § 1, 8-10-93)
Sec. 10-10-65. - Approval; subsequent maintenance.
All fire lanes shall be approved by the fire official pursuant to these regulations, and thereafter shall be maintained by the property owner. Designated fire lanes or roads deemed necessary for fire department access by the fire official shall be maintained in a passable condition.
(1)
Private property owners or their representative(s) shall be responsible for keeping all fire lanes on their property free from obstructions.
(2)
Public property owners or their representative(s) shall be responsible for keeping all fire lanes within the corporate limits of the city free of obstructions.
(Ord. No. 93-41, § 1, 8-10-93)
Sec. 10-10-66. - Erection of fire lane signs.
No person or property owner shall place, erect or maintain any sign for control of parking or traffic which includes the words "fire lanes", unless such location has been designated a fire lane by the fire official. Any person failing to meet the applicable sign requirements specified in section 10-10-64 shall, upon conviction, be subject to a fine of one hundred fifty dollars ($150.00) for each violation; provided however, that such fine may be waived if the required specifications are complied with within fourteen (14) days of the date of citation. If the property owner fails or refuse to meet said requirements on his property within such fourteen (14) days he shall, on the fifteenth (15) day after receiving the citation, be subject to the one hundred fifty dollar ($150.00) fine for each violation and an additional five dollars ($5.00) fine for each violation for each day that the owner fails to comply with the provisions of this subsection until the area is properly designated and constructed.
(Ord. No. 93-41, § 1, 8-10-93)
Sec. 10-10-67. - Violations.
(a)
The parking of any vehicle in violation of the provisions hereof, will authorize the issuance of a citation therefore in accordance with the provisions of the code which shall be left with the vehicle or delivered to the person in charge or in possession thereof.
(b)
Except as otherwise provided in section 10-10-66, any person, firm or corporation who shall violate the provisions of this article, shall upon conviction, be fined as provided in this article, but not less than twenty-five dollars ($25.00), or otherwise punished as provided in section 1-1-6 of this Code as such provision may now exist or hereafter be amended.
(Ord. No. 93-41, § 1, 8-10-93)
Sec. 10-10-68. - Enforcement authority.
The provisions of this article shall be enforced by any duly authorized law enforcement officer or fire official employed by the city.
(Ord. No. 93-41, § 1, 8-10-93)
Sec. 10-10-69. - Liability of owner and/or operator of vehicle.
For the purposes of the enforcement of the provisions of this article and for the assessment of any fines or penalties for violation thereof, it shall be presumed that the owner of a vehicle is in control and/or is in possession of the same. The lessee or owner of any vehicle shall not be liable for any fine or penalty assessed if notification is given to the appropriate court in which any violation is pending, certifying the name and address of the person or persons who, with permission of the lessee or owner, was operating the vehicle on the date and at the time the violation occurred. Failure of the lessee or owner to submit such notice while such proceeding is pending and prior to disposition thereof shall render the lessee/owner liable for such parking violation.
(Ord. No. 93-41, § 1, 8-10-93)
Sec. 10-10-70. - Liability from enforcement of Fire Lane Ordinance pertaining to maintenance and clearing of fire lanes.
The city assumes no liability for any damages, injuries, or deaths resulting from enforcement, or lack of enforcement, of the laws pertaining to maintenance and clearing of the fire lanes. The city has the authority to issue individual citations for fire lane violations, but the property owner shall have the ultimate and final responsibility to maintain clear fire lanes on his property.
(Ord. No. 93-41, § 1, 8-10-93)
Sec. 10-10-71. - Enforceability of properly placed signs.
The disregard or disobedience of the instruction of any sign placed in accordance with the provisions of this section by the driver of a vehicle shall be deemed prima-facie evidence of a violation of law, without requiring proof by whom and by what authority such sign has been erected.
(Ord. No. 93-41, § 1, 8-10-93)
Sec. 10-10-72. - Enforcement—Suspended.
Enforcement of the provisions of this article at a specific location may be suspended only pursuant to written agreement between the appropriate fire official of the city and the property owner involved. Such agreement shall be in writing and will make the property owner involved solely responsible for any damages, injuries or deaths resulting from lack of enforcement of the Fire Lane Ordinance. The property owner shall also be required by the agreement to indemnify, release and hold harmless the city and any other person, board, commission, or agent from any cause of action which relates either directly or indirectly to the suspension of enforcement of the Fire Lane Ordinance.
(Ord. No. 93-41, § 1, 8-10-93)
Sec. 10-10-73. - Identification and marking of fire lanes.
The chief of the fire department of the city shall have the right and is hereby authorized and directed to enter upon private property for the purpose of designating those areas on private, residential, commercial or industrial property which in the exercise of fire safety shall require the identification, marking and existence of fire lanes for the protection of the occupants thereof and the general public. Any such fire lanes shall be designated by the fire chief or his designated representative in writing and it shall thereupon be the responsibility of the owner of such property to identify the fire lanes and to mark the same and to prohibit parking thereon or therein in violation of the provisions of this article. Any parking lanes when so identified by the fire chief or his designated representative shall become fire lanes of the city and shall in all instances be subject to the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 93-41, § 1, 8-10-93; Ord. No. 06-01, § 7, 2-28-06; Ord. No. 18-13, § 5, 11-27-18)
