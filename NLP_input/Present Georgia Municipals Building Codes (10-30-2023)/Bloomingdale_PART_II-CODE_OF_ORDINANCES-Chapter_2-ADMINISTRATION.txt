ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 2-1. - Personnel rules and regulations authorized.
There is granted to the personnel committee of the council authority to promulgate personnel rules, regulations and programs, which shall be in effect after approval by the city council.
(Code 1983, § 16-1)
Sec. 2-2. - Administrative costs assessed on fines imposed by municipal court.
(a)
Any person against whom a fine is imposed by the municipal court of the city for the violation of any of the laws or ordinances of the city or of the laws of the state shall be subject to a minimum administrative cost in the amount of ten percent of the base fine and any additional administrative costs that the judge deems necessary.
(b)
Any person against whom a fine is imposed by the municipal court of the city for the violation of any of the laws or ordinances of the city or of the laws of the state shall be subject to additional costs in the amount of $10.00 per violation to be used for the improvement or enhancement of information technology of the court and department of police.
(Ord. of 6-21-2007(1))
Secs. 2-3, 2-4. - Reserved.
Sec. 2-5. - City administrator.
(a)
Created. The position of city administrator is hereby created and the powers, duties and functions of the city administrator are to be as provided in this section.
(b)
Job function. The city administrator manages the day-to-day operations of the city, all city finances and supervises all department heads and employees. The city administrator shall be the chief administrative officer of the city and shall be responsible to the mayor and council for the administration of all city affairs.
(c)
Powers. The city administrator shall have the following powers:
(1)
To recommend to the mayor and city council the appointment, suspension or removal of any city employee or administrative officer. With the mayor and city council's approval, the city administrator may authorize any administrative officer who is subject to direction and supervision of the city administrator to exercise these powers with respect to subordinates in that officer's department, office or agency. In the event of a situation where the city administrator feels it would be in the best interest of the city to discipline an employee, a temporary suspension of up to three days may be initiated by the city administrator without approval of the mayor and city council, but prior to any additional disciplinary action in regard to that employee, the city administrator must first obtain approval from the mayor and city council;
(2)
To direct and supervise the administration of all departments, offices, and agencies of the city, except as otherwise provided by law;
(3)
To attend all meetings of the mayor and council and to have the right to take part in discussion, but not to vote;
(4)
To see that all laws, provisions of this charter, and acts of the mayor and council, subject to enforcement by the city administrator or by officers subject to directions and supervision, are faithfully executed;
(5)
To prepare and submit the annual operating budget and capital budget to the mayor and council;
(6)
To submit to the mayor and council and make available to the public a complete report on the finances and administrative activities of the city as of the end of each fiscal year;
(7)
To make such other reports, as the mayor and council may require, concerning the operations of the city's departments, offices and agencies subject to direction and supervision;
(8)
To keep the mayor and council fully advised as to the financial condition and future needs of the city, and make such recommendations to the mayor and council concerning the affairs of the city as deems desirable;
(9)
To have the authorization to issue payment for the city's financial obligations up to the amount of $5,000.00;
(10)
To perform other such duties as are specified in the charter as may be delegated by the mayor and council; and
(11)
To keep the mayor and council informed on a daily basis of city business.
(d)
Duties. The city administrator is responsible for administering all municipal functions at the professional level. Duties include establishing and maintaining budgetary, financial and personnel programs; planning and monitoring the activities of city departments; and coordinating with state, federal, and local government officials, attorneys, auditors, engineers, and the general public. Work to be performed under the general direction and policy guidance of mayor and council.
(e)
Description of work. The following examples are illustrative of the duties/responsibilities assigned to this position. No attempt is made to be exhaustive in the listing:
(1)
Recommends and implements approved annual budget:
a.
Reports projected income and expenditures to mayor and council members.
b.
Prepares in conjunction with department heads, submits budget for each operating department and fund account, and combines them to achieve a single annual balanced budget.
c.
Coordinates grant and revenue sharing proposals and awards.
d.
Establishes and implements budget monitoring and reporting procedures .
(2)
Coordinates financial management programs:
a.
Establishes accounting and reporting procedures for each fund.
b.
Identifies investment alternatives for city funds.
c.
Coordinates the preparation of annual financial report with auditor.
d.
Maintains annual inventory report.
e.
Assists mayor and council members in establishing millage, utility, license, permit, and other fees.
(3)
Supervises municipal purchasing activities:
a.
The city administrator shall serve as the purchasing agent for the city.
b.
Establishes the information for the requirements for project on a bid.
c.
Hosts the bid opening and reviews bids and accepts or recommends lowest responsible bid to mayor and council members.
d.
Administers contracts awarded to providers of supplies, equipment and services.
(4)
Assists in municipal planning and development activities:
a.
Meets with engineers, developers, contractors, investment advisors, governmental officials, boards and utility companies.
b.
Reviews/supervises schematic drawings and recommendations for modifying development projects.
(5)
Coordinates activities of city operating departments:
a.
Establishes goals, budgets and work procedure in coordination with department heads.
b.
Monitors performance of operations at designated intervals.
c.
Adjusts procedures to improve effectiveness and efficiency.
d.
Investigates and resolves complaints regarding city services and activities.
e.
Administers and monitors compliance with city personnel policies and procedures.
(6)
Coordinates with federal, state and local government officials and the public:
a.
Makes recommendations to mayor and council on the appointment and removal of department heads.
(f)
Acting city administrator. By letter filed with the city clerk, the city administrator shall designate, subject to approval of the mayor and council, a qualified city administrator officer to exercise the powers and perform the duties of city administrator during temporary absence or disability. During such absence or disability, the mayor and council may revoke such designation at any time and appoint another of the city to serve until the city administrator shall return of the disability shall cease.
(Ord. of 4-3-2008)

Sec. 2-31. - Regular meetings; study sessions.
(a)
Regular meetings of the city council shall be held on the first and third Thursdays of each month at the city hall at the hour of 7:00 p.m. Before each such regular meeting of the city council, at 6:30 p.m., the city council shall meet at the city hall in a study session which shall be open to the general public to informally review and discuss, but not decide, the items on the agenda for its forthcoming meeting.
(b)
During the months of June, July, and August, the city council may choose to meet only one Thursday of each month. Other city council meetings may be cancelled with approval of the council or at the discretion of the mayor. Notice of said cancellations shall be given to the public as soon as possible prior to the regular meeting date.
(Code 1983, § 2-1; Ord. of 4-1-1993, § 2-1; Ord. of 6-18-1998(1))
Sec. 2-32. - Meetings open; exceptions.
All meetings of the city council shall be held in accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1 et seq. The public shall at all times be afforded access to all meetings other than executive sessions.
(Code 1983, § 2-5)
Sec. 2-33. - Acting mayor.
(a)
In the absence of both the mayor and vice-mayor, the council shall elect an acting mayor who shall exercise all the powers of the mayor during a city council meeting.
(b)
In case of vacancy in office of both the mayor and vice-mayor from any cause, the council shall elect an acting mayor, who shall hold such office for the remainder of the term.
(Code 1983, § 2-2)
Sec. 2-34. - Record of voting.
The yeas and nays on any question during a city council meeting shall be entered on the record, and the clerk shall also enter on the record the names of those councilmembers, other than the mayor, not voting and those councilmembers voting.
(Code 1983, § 2-3)
Sec. 2-35. - Parliamentary rules.
When any question arises which is not provided for in the rules contained in this article, the question, as far as practicable, shall be controlled by Robert's Rules of Order, Newly Revised.
(Code 1983, § 2-6)
Sec. 2-36. - Reading of ordinances before passage.
For a city ordinance to be valid and binding it must be read twice before the city council, on two different dates before passage. However, by the majority vote of the city council, the second reading may be dispensed with, and an ordinance may be passed after one reading at the council meeting.
(Code 1983, § 2-4)

ARTICLE III. - FINANCE

Sec. 2-71. - Sale of surplus personal property.
(a)
The city shall have the power to dispose of surplus personal property in the following manner: Any property deemed to be of a greater value than $500.00 by the mayor shall be sold by receiving bids from the general public. The buyer willing to pay the highest price shall be awarded the right to purchase the property in question provided such purchaser can satisfy the city council that such person is in a position to produce the money, check or similar negotiable instrument only at the discretion of the council. The council may delegate the above-mentioned power to the mayor to be exercised in the mayor's sole judgment.
(b)
Such sale, as described in subsection (a) of this section, shall be advertised for a period of two weeks, once each week. On the 14th day after first being advertised or any day thereafter, the city council shall decide which bid to accept. Such sale may be advertised in any paper of general circulation in the city or such sale may be advertised by placing notice of such sale in any public building in the city. Potential purchasers shall give their bids to the city clerk at the city hall on or before the closing date (the closing date shall be mentioned in the advertisement). Bids presented to the clerk should be in a sealed container. The mayor shall open such bids in the presence of at least one other person. The council may refuse any or all bids in its discretion for good and proper reason.
(c)
The city's surplus personal property may also be sold at public auction. Notice of such auction shall be given by advertisement in a newspaper of general circulation at least once no less than 15 nor more than 60 days before the day of the auction. When appropriate, an experienced auctioneer should be used to cry the sale and assist in the preparation of the sale. The notice shall contain a general description of the property to be sold, all the terms and conditions of the sale, that a deposit may be required in order to participate in the bidding, that the purchaser must remove within a stated time all surplus property purchased, and that the city retains the right to reject any and all bids.
(d)
Surplus personal property which has an estimated value of $500.00 or less may be sold in established markets where such personal property is regularly sold in wholesale lots and prices are set by open competition. Surplus personal property may be sold at posted prices as determined by the city clerk, provided that such prices are based on fair market value. Surplus personal property may be traded in only when the city clerk determines that the trade-in value is expected to exceed the value estimated to be obtained through the sale or other disposition of such personal property.
(e)
Only United States postal money orders, certified checks, cashier's checks, or cash shall be accepted for sales of personal property.
(Code 1983, § 8-6; Ord. of 2-16-1993, § 1; Ord. of 12-18-1997, § 1)

ARTICLE IV. - CODE OF ETHICS

Sec. 2-101. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Complaint means a written sworn statement filed with the city's ethics committee containing specific allegations of misconduct by a member of the governing authority; provided, however, such allegations must be filed within six months of the discovery of the alleged misconduct.
Employee means any person who is a full-time or part-time employee of the city.
Governing authority and member of governing authority mean any person who is an elected city official, including the mayor of the city, a member of the city council, or any member of an appointed board or commission of the city.
Government and city mean the city government.
Interest means any direct pecuniary benefit which is not a remote interest held by or accruing to a member of the governing authority as a result of a contract or transaction that is or may be subject of an official act or action by or with the city. A member of the governing authority shall be deemed to have an interest in transactions involving:
(1)
Any person in the member's immediate family;
(2)
Any person with whom a contractual relationship exists whereby the member may receive any payment or other benefit unless the member is receiving a benefit for goods or services in the normal course of business for which the member has been paid a commercially reasonable rate;
(3)
Any business in which the member is a director, officer, employee, agent, or shareholder, except as otherwise provided herein; or
(4)
Any person of whom the member is a creditor, whether secured or unsecured.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-26))
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 2-102. - Intent.
It is the intent of this article that governing authority members avoid any action whether or not specifically prohibited herein which might result in, or create the appearance of, the following:
(1)
Using public office for private gain;
(2)
Impeding government efficiency or economy; or
(3)
Affecting adversely the confidence of the public in the integrity of the government.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-27))
Sec. 2-103. - Acceptance of gifts.
(a)
Members of the governing authority shall not solicit or accept directly or indirectly anything of value from any person, corporation, or group which:
(1)
Has, or is seeking to obtain contractual or other business or financial relationships with the city;
(2)
In exchange for an item of value, seeks to have a member exercise a matter of discretion in his or her favor;
(3)
In exchange for an item of value, seeks to have interests which may be affected by the performance or nonperformance of the official duty of the member.
(b)
This section does not apply in the case of:
(1)
Campaign contributions required to be reported in accordance with state law;
(2)
An occasional nonpecuniary gift of insignificant value;
(3)
An award publicly presented in recognition of public service; and
(4)
A commercially reasonable loan or other financial transaction made in the ordinary course of business by an institution or individual by the laws of the state to engage in the making of such a loan or financial transaction.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-28))
Sec. 2-104. - Conflict of interest.
(a)
No elected official, appointed officer, or employee of the city or any agency or political entity to which the city's Code applies shall knowingly engage in any business or transaction or have a financial or other personal interest, direct or indirect, which is incompatible with the proper discharge of his official duties or which would tend to impair his independence of judgment or action in the performance of his official duties. No such individual shall also engage in, directly or indirectly, financial transactions as a result of, or primarily relying upon, information obtained from his office.
(b)
If an employee of the city is an officer, director, agent or member of, or owns interest in any entity which is subject to the regulation of, or which has financial commitments with the city, he shall file a sworn statement to that effect with the city clerk.
(c)
An employee shall not transact any business in their official capacity with any business entity of which he is an officer, director, agent, member, or in which he owns a controlling interest, excluding civic, charitable or religious organizations.
(d)
Employees shall not have personal investment in any enterprise which will create a conflict or a perceived conflict between their private interest and the public interest.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-29))
Sec. 2-105. - Employment restrictions.
No member of the governing authority shall engage in, accept employment with, or render services for any private business or professional activity when such employment or rendering of services is adverse and incompatible with the proper discharge of official duties. This section shall not apply to a member of the governing authority who is a licensed professional and appears on behalf of any applicant for official action in such professional capacity so long as disclosures required by Section 2-106 are made to city council 30 days prior to any action being taken, and the member is associated with a project at the time the initial application is filed.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-30))
Sec. 2-106. - Disclosure of conflicts of interest.
An appointed member of the governing authority who has an interest that he has reason to believe may be affected by his official acts or actions or by the official acts or actions of the governing authority shall disclose the precise nature of such interest by written or verbal statement 30 days prior to the governing authority's taking official action on a matter affecting such interest and abstain from discussion and voting. An elected member of the governing authority shall disclose the nature of any interest he has at the time such matter is presented to mayor and council for discussion. Such written or verbal statements shall be recorded into the minutes of the meeting and thus become part of the public record. Following any disclosure made pursuant to this section, the member shall refrain from all ex-parte communications with other members regarding the application in which he has an interest.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-31))
Sec. 2-107. - Disqualification.
A member of the governing authority shall disqualify himself from participating in any official act or action of the city which results in a pecuniary benefit to the member or a business or activity in which he has an interest, when such benefit is not available to the public at large.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-32))
Sec. 2-108. - Prohibited contracts.
The city shall not enter into any contract involving services or property with a member of the governing authority or with a business in which a member of the governing authority has an interest. This section shall not apply in the case of:
(1)
The designation of a bank or trust company as a depository for city funds;
(2)
The borrowing of funds from any bank or lending institution which offers the lowest available rate of interest in the community for such loan;
(3)
Contracts entered into in accordance with the O.C.G.A. § 16-10-6;
(4)
Contracts entered into under circumstances that constitute an emergency situation, provided that the mayor prepares a written record explaining the emergency; and
(5)
Contracts entered into with a member of the governing authority, or with a business in which a member of the governing authority has an interest, provided that such contract is the result of a competitive bid, disclosure of the nature of such member's interest is made prior to the time any bid is submitted, and a waiver of the prohibition contemplated by this section is issued by the city clerk following disclosure.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-33))
Sec. 2-109. - Restrictions on contracts with former members of the governing authority.
The city shall not enter into any contract with any person or business represented by such person, who has been within the preceding 12-month period a member of the governing authority, unless the contract is awarded by a competitive bid or a committee selection process.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-34))
Sec. 2-110. - Restrictions on use of public property.
(a)
A governing authority member shall not use government property of any kind for other than officially approved activities, nor shall he direct employees to use such property for these purposes.
(b)
All plans, designs, reports, specifications, drawings, devices, inventions, and other items produced or created by employees during working hours or through the use of city facilities or equipment or at the request of the city shall be or become the sole property of the city.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-35))
Sec. 2-111. - Use of confidential information.
A governing authority member shall not directly or indirectly, make use of, or permit others to make use of, for the purpose of furthering a private interest, official information not made available to the general public.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-36))
Sec. 2-112. - Coercion by governing authority members.
A governing authority member shall not use his position in any way to coerce, or give the appearance of coercing, another person to provide any financial benefit to him or persons within his immediate family, or those with whom he has business or financial ties.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-37))
Sec. 2-113. - Voting in matters of personal interest.
A governing authority member shall not vote on an ordinance or amendment in a governing authority meeting that would directly affect his private business.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-38))
Sec. 2-114. - Improper payments to city employees from outside sources.
City employees shall not accept payment from outside sources for professional services (i.e., teaching, instructing, speaking engagements, consulting, honorariums) when such activities are done on city time or when such services pertain to the purchase or sale of city property.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-39))
Sec. 2-115. - Unauthorized purchases.
A governing authority member shall not order any goods and services for the city without prior official authorization for such an expenditure.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-40))
Sec. 2-116. - City attorney used for private business.
A governing authority member shall not use the attorney or attorneys who are under retainer by the city for personal or private business without paying just compensation.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-41))
Sec. 2-117. - Unauthorized use of public employees.
A governing authority member shall not use his superior position to request or require an employee to:
(1)
Do clerical work on behalf of his family, business, social, church or fraternal interests;
(2)
Purchase goods and services to be used for personal, business, or political purposes; and
(3)
Work for him personally without offering just compensation.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-42))
Sec. 2-118. - Travel expenses.
A governing authority member shall not draw per diem or expense monies from the city to attend a seminar, convention, or conference and then fail to attend the seminar, convention, or conference without refunding all expense monies to the city.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-43))
Sec. 2-119. - Political activity.
(a)
No person employed by the city shall either publicly or otherwise hold himself out as a candidate in any city election while holding employment with the city.
(b)
City employees shall not take part in any political management or political campaigns in the election of mayor or any member of the governing authority for the city during any period of time for which he is expected to perform work or receive compensation from the city.
(c)
No employee, official or other person shall solicit, orally or by letter, or be in any other manner concerned in obtaining any assessments, contributions, or services for any political party from any employee during his hours of duty, service, or work with the city.
(d)
Employees shall not represent the city by wearing any uniform or portion thereof that is issued by the city while he is participating in any campaign at any time.
(e)
The city in no way seeks to influence employees in their choice of party affiliations or candidates, recognizing that this is a matter for each person to decide. Therefore, nothing contained herein shall be construed to restrict the right of the employee to hold membership in and support a political party, to vote as he chooses, to express opinions on political subjects or candidates, to maintain political neutrality, to attend political parties after work hours, or to campaign actively during off duty hours in all areas of political activity.
(f)
Employees shall not utilize any city equipment or vehicles in support of any political campaign.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-44))
Sec. 2-120. - Limitations and approval requirement for outside employment.
(a)
City employment shall be considered to be the primary employment of all full-time employees. No employee may engage in outside employment which will interfere with the interests of city service. The standard by which some employment is not acceptable includes, but is not limited to:
(1)
Illegal activity;
(2)
Employment that by virtue of association will reflect upon the reputation of the city; and
(3)
High hazard or fatiguing work which might detract from or reduce city job performance.
(b)
Prior to beginning any regularly scheduled outside employment, employees will obtain specific written approval from the city clerk or his designee on request forms which indicate the name of the outside employer, the nature of the work, hours of work, address and telephone number where the employee can be reached. Such information will become a part of the employee's official personnel record. Employees are required to obtain approval from the city clerk or designee for any change in a previously approved outside employment request.
(c)
Any employee accepting or engaging in outside employment under the terms of this rule shall make arrangements with the outside employer to be available to respond immediately to any emergency call of duty whenever the city clerk or his designee shall determine that the employee's services are necessary.
(d)
Employees sustaining injuries while engaged in outside employment are normally ineligible to receive benefits under the city's workers' compensation program. If an employee sustains an injury while engaged in outside employment, but is performing duties within the scope of his city responsibilities (i.e., a police arrest), he may be eligible for partial city workers' compensation benefits.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-45))
Sec. 2-121. - Administration; ethics committee.
(a)
The ethics committee shall consist of three persons, one city resident appointed by the mayor, one city resident appointed by the council, and the third appointed by the two above named subject to approval by a majority of the mayor and council. All members shall be residents of the city and shall serve a two-year term. No person shall serve as a member of the ethics committee if the person has, or has had within the preceding one-year period, any interest in any contract, transaction, or official action of the city.
(b)
Members of the ethics committee shall serve without compensation. The city shall provide public meeting space for the committee as requested. Subject to budgetary procedures and limitations, the city shall provide the ethics committee with such supplies, equipment and materials as may be necessary for the proper performance of duties and responsibilities.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-46))
Sec. 2-122. - Receipt and processing of complaints.
(a)
All complaints against governing authority members or city employees shall be filed with the ethics committee within six months of the discovery of conduct prohibited herein. The ethics committee may require that oral complaints, and complaints illegibly or informally drawn, be reduced to a memorandum of complaint in such form as it may prescribe.
(b)
Upon receipt of a complaint in proper form, the ethics committee shall commence a review to determine whether the complaint is justified, unjustified, or fails to state facts sufficient to invoke the disciplinary jurisdiction of the governing authority. Complaints must be reviewed within 60 days from time of receipt. Reviews not initiated within 60 days shall become null and result in dismissal. Dismissed complaints may not be re-filed for a period of six months. The ethics committee shall be empowered to collect evidence and information concerning any complaint and to add the findings and results of its investigations to the file containing such complaint.
(c)
The ethics committee as appointed herein shall cause the complaint to be served on the member of the governing authority or city employee charged without unreasonable delay. Service may be by personal service or certified mail, return receipt requested. A hearing shall be held within 90 days after filing of the complaint. The committee shall conduct the hearing in accordance with the procedures and regulations it establishes, but in all circumstances, the hearing shall include the taking of testimony and the cross-examination of witnesses.
(d)
Upon completion of its investigation of a complaint, the ethics committee shall be empowered to dismiss those complaints which are unjustified, frivolous, patently unfounded or which fail to state facts sufficient to invoke the disciplinary jurisdiction of the governing authority; provided, however, that a rejection of such complaint by the ethics committee shall not deprive the complaining party of any action he might otherwise have at law or in equity against the respondent government servant.
(e)
The ethics committee shall be empowered to adopt forms for formal complaints, subpoenas, notices, applications for reinstatement and any other written instruments necessary or desirable under its rules.
(f)
The ethics committee shall report its findings to the mayor and council for such action as the mayor and council deem appropriate.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-47))
Sec. 2-123. - Penalty and member rights.
(a)
Any member of the governing authority who knowingly violates any provision of the code of ethics provided in this article shall be subject to public reprimand or censure by the mayor and council of the city. A city employee's failure to comply with the provisions of this article may result in disciplinary action. Power to administer a greater punishment shall include power to administer the lesser.
(b)
At any hearing held by the ethics committee, the member of the governing authority or employee who is the subject of inquiry shall have the right to written notice of the allegations at least ten business days before a hearing, to be represented by counsel, to hear and examine the evidence and witnesses and to present evidence and witnesses in opposition or in extenuation.
(Ord. of 4-4-2002(1), § 1(2-48))
Sec. 2-124. - Right to appeal.
Any final decision by the mayor and council pursuant to this code of ethics for governing authority members and employees may be reviewed by the superior court of the county. The review by the superior court shall be limited to an inquiry of whether there was any evidence before the mayor and council which supported the decision of the mayor and council. Provided, however, no action of the mayor and council refusing or failing to take action pursuant to this code of ethics may be reviewed by the superior court.
