Chapter 24 - ROADS AND OTHER PUBLIC WAYS AND PLACES
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 24-1. - Reward for road sign damage.
The board of commissioners authorizes the reward of $250.00 to an individual for information that leads to the arrest and conviction of persons for damaging or defacing road signs in the county. The board of commissioners shall make the final decision as to whether an individual is entitled to the reward.
(Code 2008, § 22-301)
Secs. 24-2—24-20. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - SUBDIVISION ROAD CONSTRUCTION AND MAINTENANCE
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 24-21. - Construction standards and details applicable.
The county standards and details are made a part of this article and shall be used to construct the structures and items referenced therein. Copies of all or individual standards and details are available from the county commissioner's office. Items and structures not covered by the county standards and details shall be constructed in accordance with the current state department of transportation specifications, standards and details, to the extent applicable. The developer may submit special design structures accompanied by a detailed drawing and explanation of the use and purpose of the structure. The board of commissioners or its representative must approve the structure before construction begins.
(Code 2008, § 22-101(1))
Sec. 24-22. - Road inspection required.
In order to ensure compliance with state and federal regulations as well as these road provisions, road inspections are required as follows:
(1)
When clearing and grubbing is complete on a section of roadway and before grading is begun to ensure stumps, trees, boulders and other debris have been properly removed;
(2)
During the grading process whenever a compaction test is required according to the current county road ordinance and when cross-drain pipe is being laid or as deemed appropriate by the inspector;
(3)
When a section of grading is complete and the sub-grade is being processed according to the current road ordinance, the inspector may request a test roll by a loaded truck of sufficient weight to satisfy the inspector that proper construction and compaction have been achieved;
(4)
When a section of base has been placed on the sub-grade and when required compaction tests are being performed. In addition to or in lieu of compaction tests as required, the inspector may request a test roll by a loaded truck of sufficient weight to satisfy the inspector that proper construction and compaction has been achieved;
(5)
During the paving operation to ensure proper placement of the paving material according to the current road ordinance;
(6)
During the same time the inspections of this section are being made, the inspector shall make measurements and determinations that the roadway is being built to grade and cross section. The inspector has the authority to have the developer or its contractor remove and replace unsatisfactory materials and workmanship and replace such until satisfactory results are obtained.
(Code 2008, § 22-201)
Sec. 24-23. - Right-of-way widths.
Rights-of-way for roads and streets shall conform to the minimum widths set forth in the current subdivision regulations of the county. However, rights-of-way shall also be increased to or may be decreased to the following widths for roadways that are constructed as derived in this section:
(1)
24 feet of pavement with five-foot shoulders: 80 feet of right-of-way.
(2)
20 feet of pavement with curb and gutter: 50 feet of right-of-way. (Yields 24 feet of driving surface.)
(3)
20 feet of pavement with five-foot shoulders: 70 feet of right-of-way.
(4)
18 feet of pavement with curb and gutter: 40 feet off right-of-way. (Yields 20 feet of driving surface.)
(Code 2008, § 22-101(2))
Sec. 24-24. - Easements.
Easements are required and shall be shown on the plat for slopes, drainage structures and utilities for the following conditions:
(1)
Cut or fill slopes which by nature of the topography extend beyond the right-of-way. Slopes that are flattened at the developer's discretion for aesthetic purposes are not considered criteria for an easement.
(2)
An easement shall be shown on the plat for all drainage structures 24 inches and larger and extending a minimum of 20 feet left and right and 50 feet above and below the structure. Easements for large structures or difficult topographical features may require larger limits and should be discussed with the board of commissioners or its representative in advance. The purpose of a drainage easement is to allow the county to adequately maintain these structures and the free drainage of the inlet and outlet ends. Utilities, not including service taps, installed beyond the right-of-way due to topographical conditions shall include a utility easement on the lot.
(3)
The existence of an easement on a lot does not reduce the acreage of the lot.
(Code 2008, § 22-101(3))
Sec. 24-25. - Inspection schedule.
(a)
It is the responsibility of the developer to adhere to the requirements for subdivision road inspections and notify the roadway inspector when inspections are needed.
(b)
Materials and roadway items found to be in noncompliance and brought to the attention of the developer shall be re-inspected when the work has been corrected. The cost of repeated inspections of noncompliance or punch list items that were not corrected will be charged to the developer.
(Code 2008, § 22-101(4))
Sec. 24-26. - Utility installation.
All utilities shall be installed a minimum of 1½ feet from the edge of the pavement or the back of the curb. Utilities, not including service taps, installed beyond the right-of-way due to topographical conditions shall include a utility easement on the lot. All utilities and service taps shall be installed three feet below the working surface while within the right-of-way or easement.
(Code 2008, § 22-103)
Sec. 24-27. - Culverts and pipe.
(a)
Pipe size shall be determined by standard engineering practice. The developer will provide drainage areas on the plat. All pipe shall be galvanized bituminous coated, aluminized, galvanized or concrete and shall be obtained from a DOT-approved supplier. Cross drain pipe smaller than 18 inches in diameter shall not be used, including entrances to subdivisions on existing roadways and side streets. One of the following materials shall be used at the ends of cross drain pipe: Flared end sections (except on pipe 30 inches or larger), stone dumped rip-rap, sand bag rip-rap or concrete headwalls according to optical carrier standards and details. Safety flared end sections or safety grates on headwalls shall be used on cross drain pipes at subdivision entrances and other roads or streets along roadways with speed limit of 45 miles per hour or greater.
(b)
A flared end section shall be attached to the inlet and outlet of side drain pipes. Pipe less than 15 inches in diameter shall not be used. Plastic pipe (15 inches or larger) may be used for driveways only. Driveways should be constructed to divert water into drainage ditches or away from the road according to the county driveway regulations.
(Code 2008, § 22-104)
Sec. 24-28. - Grassing.
(a)
Topsoil, which may be stripped and stockpiled during clearing and grubbing, shall be spread over the shoulders and front slopes of the roadway a minimum depth of four inches in preparation for a seed bed. The soil shall be thoroughly broken, well pulverized, smoothed and firmed before planting. Ditches and back slopes may also be treated with soil before planting as described above. Otherwise, ditches and back slopes shall be harrowed, well pulverized and smoothed in preparation for a seed bed. Shoulders, front slopes, ditches and back slopes shall be seeded with Bermuda grass applied at the rate of two pounds per 1,000 square feet or fescue applied at the rate of five pounds per 1,000 square feet. The entire seeded area shall be mulched.
(b)
Centipede grass will not be used in development with sidewalks or those graded in such a way that the lawns are contiguous with the back slope.
(Code 2008, § 22-105)
Sec. 24-29. - Road signs.
The subdivider shall deposit with the county, at the time of final subdivision approval, the sum as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges for each street sign and for traffic control signs required by the road department, at all intersections. The county shall install all road signs before the issuance of certificate of occupancy for any residence on the roads approved. Road signs are to be placed at all intersections within or abutting the subdivision; the type and location of such are to be approved by the road department.
(Code 2008, § 22-106)
Sec. 24-30. - Acceptance of streets and roads.
Oglethorpe County shall not accept the dedication as a public street/road of any private street/road unless such street/road is built in accordance with the requirements of this county road ordinance and is shown on an approved and recorded final plat. Notwithstanding the foregoing, private streets/roads in existence prior to April 14, 1997 may be accepted for dedication as public streets/roads. In determining whether to accept the dedication of a private street/road in existence prior to April 14, 1997, the board of commissioners shall consider the condition of the road, including the nature of its right-of-way, the quality of its grading and drainage, the presence of utilities on or about the right-of-way of the road and their distance from the road itself, and any applicable staff recommendations regarding the same. Oglethorpe County shall only maintain streets/roads that are public streets/roads.
(Code 2008, § 22-107; Ord. of 9-5-2017)

DIVISION 2. - PAVING

Sec. 24-49. - Clearing and grubbing right-of-way.
This work shall consist of removal of all trees and vegetation within the right-of-way to an approved site for disposal. Stumps, boulders or large stones may be left in place three feet below sub-grade, provided stumps are cut off at ground level. Stump holes, removed boulders or large stones shall be backfilled with the most suitable material available and compacted to not less than 95 percent of the maximum density.
Sec. 24-50. - Grading.
This work shall consist of grading the roadway to line, grade and cross section as approved or stated herein. Compaction shall not be less than 95 percent of the maximum density, except that the top one foot, including the sub-grade, shall be compacted to not less than 100 percent of the maximum density.
Sec. 24-51. - Sub-grade.
(a)
Sub-grade work consists of obtaining and placing suitable sub-grade material on the top six inches of the roadway, mixing, compacting and shaping to grade and cross section. This work also includes sub-grade stabilization. The entire surface of the roadway (width of pavement plus one foot left and right) shall be plowed, harrowed and mixed to a depth of at least six inches. After the material has been thoroughly mixed, the sub-grade shall be compacted to 100 percent of the maximum density. Sheep foot rollers are required.
(b)
Sub-grade material shall be of a suitable soil as defined by the current state department of transportation specifications. If such material is not available on site, it shall be furnished by the developer. On site materials not meeting the above criteria may be stabilized with crushed stone as defined in the current state department of transportation specifications.
Sec. 24-52. - Base.
(a)
Graded aggregate and asphaltic concrete base. All roads and streets shall have a base placed upon the sub-grade consisting of six inches of graded aggregate base or four inches of asphaltic concrete Type "B." Graded aggregate base shall be compacted to 100 percent for the maximum density. Asphaltic concrete base shall be compacted to at least 98 percent of the maximum density.
(b)
Soil cement base. Existing sub-grade shall be harrowed to a depth of one foot and compacted to 100 percent of the maximum density with sheep foot rollers and bladed to cross-section and profile, then the top six inches shall be thoroughly mixed with an eight percent mixture of Portland cement with a rotary mixer and compacted to 100 percent of the maximum density with sheep foot rollers or vibratory smooth rollers. Adequate moisture should be added as necessary to ensure bonding of the soil and cement.
Sec. 24-53. - Paving.
All streets shall be paved with two inches of asphaltic concrete Type "E" or Type "F" onto a properly prepared base. Asphaltic concrete surface course shall be compacted to at least 98 percent of the maximum density.
Sec. 24-54. - Compaction.
(a)
Testing performed. Compaction tests shall be performed on the following materials at the following intervals:
(1)
Grading. One compaction test shall be required in each fill section two feet in depth or greater regardless of length and one test is required every 500 feet in fill sections longer than 500 feet. A compaction test shall be required within every four feet of fill or portion thereof for fills greater than four feet. Fill material shall be compacted to at least 95 percent of the maximum density.
(2)
Sub-grade. One compaction test shall be required every 500 feet of sub-grade. The sub-grade shall be compacted to 100 percent of the maximum density.
(3)
Base. For aggregate base material, a compaction test shall be required every 500 feet. Aggregate base shall be compacted to 100 percent of the maximum density. Asphaltic concrete base shall be compacted to at least 98 percent of the maximum density.
(4)
Paving. For asphaltic concrete paving, a compaction test shall be required every 500 feet. Asphaltic concrete paving shall be compacted to at least 98 percent of the maximum density.
(b)
Areas not meeting compaction test specifications. Any areas that do not meet with the specified compaction shall be excavated and replaced with suitable material. Test results shall be submitted to the chairman of the board of commissioners or the chairman's designee, as appropriate. The developer shall incur all costs for compaction tests.
(Code 2008, § 22-102)

ARTICLE III. - DRIVEWAY CONSTRUCTION

Sec. 24-82. - Permit required.
Before installing any driveway or means of ingress and egress to any property abutting a county road or county-maintained road, the owner of the subject property, or the person installing the driveway acting as the owner's designee, shall make application to the county board of commissioners through its building inspection department for a permit and receive the permit authorizing the installation. The permit must be posted so it is visible from the county road. If existing curb and gutter is to be removed to facilitate the driveway, then a permit authorizing the removal shall also be required.
(Code 2008, § 39-601)
Sec. 24-83. - Construction standards and specifications.
(a)
Permits shall specify the location of the driveway, the width of same, the size and material for the culvert to be used and other matters required for adequate drainage of the driveway in accordance with accepted engineering and road management practices. In issuing the permit, the county may impose any reasonable conditions that the circumstances may warrant. Conditions include, but are not necessarily limited to, drive location, elevation, pipe size, surface treatment, drainage easements, and other requirements to facilitate safe maintenance and operation of county roads.
(b)
A minimum pipe size of 15 inches and minimum 20 feet length of pipe with flared ends must be used for all driveway construction. Pipe shall extend a minimum of four feet on each side of the area serviced. A minimum pipe size of 18 inches must be used for all cross drains under public roads.
(c)
Driveway construction shall be graded so water does not run from the driveway into the county road or collect along the road right-of-way. Driveways are required to have a down slope away from the county road. If this is not possible, the driveway is required to be paved with concrete or asphalt to the top of the slope to prevent dirt or gravel from washing into the road. Driveway pipes are required to be installed with the grade of the road to ensure proper drainage and be covered with a minimum of one foot of fill.
(d)
Pipe for driveways shall be either 16 gauge corrugated steel or aluminum in accordance with county standards. Reinforced concrete or smooth lined high density polyethylene pipe may be used. PVC or other corrugated plastics are not permitted within county rights-of-way. Driveways requiring curb and gutter removal shall be installed in accordance with instructions and diagrams issued by the road superintendent at the time of the application of permit.
(e)
A permit authorizing driveway construction is to be strictly constructed and no work other than that specifically authorized by the permit may be performed in county rights-of-way. This permit must be renewed if the work described is not completed within one year from the date the permit is issued.
(f)
No provision of this article shall be construed as a responsibility of the county to provide future maintenance of new or currently installed driveways or drainage structures. By application and issuance of the permit, the owner and successors agree to bear all responsibility and cost for repair and future maintenance of the driveway and associated drainage structures. If the owner or successors fails to maintain the driveway or associated drainage structures to the extent that the board of commissioners or an authorized representative of the board of commissioners determines that the driveway or associated drainage structures has a detrimental impact on the safe operation and maintenance of county roads, notice shall he given to the owner. The owner shall perform the necessary actions to correct the detrimental impacts at the sole expense of the owner. Failure of the owner to perform the necessary work within the time prescribed in the notice shall be cause for the county to perform the work. Expenses incurred by the county to perform the work shall be the responsibility of the owner.
(Code 2008, § 39-602)
Sec. 24-84. - Inspection of work.
Driveway construction and material shall be the responsibility of the owner/applicant for the permit. All work shall be in conformity with the permit as granted and shall be subject to inspection by authorized representatives of the board of commissioners. No use of the driveway or issuance of permits for uses of the property served thereby shall be permitted until such compliance is met.
(Code 2008, § 39-603)
Sec. 24-85. - Penalty.
Violation of this article shall be punishable by a fine not to exceed $500.00. Each day that the violation continues shall constitute a separate offense.
(Code 2008, § 39-604)

ARTICLE IV. - FESTIVALS, SPORTING EVENTS AND OTHER RECREATIONAL USES

Sec. 24-114. - Applicability.
This article shall apply to any recreational or sporting event conducted wholly or partly within the unincorporated area of the county. If the event is conducted wholly within the unincorporated area of the county, this article shall apply to the entire event and its full length. If the event is conducted partly within the unincorporated area and partly outside the unincorporated area, this chapter shall apply to the portion of the event conducted in the unincorporated area.
Sec. 24-115. - Sports and recreation events generally not permitted in public rights-of-way.
Public rights-of-way of the county shall not be used for sporting or recreational contests or competitions except as provided in this article.
Sec. 24-116. - Exceptions.
Any events in public rights-of-way involving competitions on foot, bicycle, vehicle, or any other method of locomotion must comply with the requirements of this article.
(1)
Sponsorship. No such event shall be conducted on the public rights-of-way of the county unless the event is sponsored by a nonprofit charitable institution such as a local school, religious institution, youth organization, fraternal organization, or chamber of commerce and is approved by the board of commissioners. The sponsoring organization must be actively involved in the planning, scheduling, and staging of the event and is subject to approval under the conditions of this section.
(2)
Application. The sponsor of the event shall, at least 30 days prior to the scheduled date of the event, make application to the board of commissioners or its designee to conduct the event. Application shall be in such form as the board of commissioners prescribes and shall at least provide that the name and address of the local sponsor or event organizer (or in the event of an organization that is not local, the name and address of a responsible county resident who is involved in planning the event); the date and times of the proposed events; the expected number of participants; names and qualifications of medical or paramedical personnel that will be available; the location of such event, including all public rights-of-way which would be used or otherwise impacted; the duration of disruption of any vehicular traffic; specific arrangements for crowd control and accommodation for security, traffic, safety, restroom facilities, and waste control; certification that the event will be handled as part of a festival or other ongoing event; and that the length of the competition or race shall then be no more than one-half mile from the start to the finish line.
(3)
Grounds for rejection. The board of commissioners reserves the right to reject any and all applications for such events based on the public health, safety, and welfare, including the failure to provide for adequate measures to ensure a safe event. Special consideration shall be given to the impact of the event on more heavily traveled roads and on citizens not involved in the event.
(4)
Alteration of route. If the board of commissioners determines, upon investigation, that the proposed route is hazardous to participants or others, the board or commissioners or its designee may alter the proposed route and specify another or different route in the interest of public safety.
(5)
Punishment. Any person violating any provisions of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be punished, upon conviction, by the county magistrate court by a fine or imprisonment as prescribed by section 1-9.
(Code 2008, § 31-104)

ARTICLE V. - TIMBER HARVESTING[1]

Sec. 24-145. - Title.
This article shall be known as the Oglethorpe County Timber Harvesting Ordinance.
(Ord. No. 2022-01 , § 1, 3-7-2022)
Sec. 24-146. - Notice required.
All persons or firms harvesting standing timber in any unincorporated area of Oglethorpe County for delivery as pulpwood, logs, poles, posts, or wood chips to any woodyard or processing plant located inside or outside this state shall provide notice of such harvesting operations to the Board of Commissioners of Oglethorpe County or the designated agent thereof prior to entering onto the property if possible, but in no event later than 24 hours after entering onto the property. Further, such persons shall give notice of cessation of cutting within 24 hours after the job is completed.
(Ord. No. 2022-01 , § 1, 3-7-2022)
Sec. 24-147. - Form of notice.
The notice of harvesting operations required by this article shall be provided for each separate tract to be harvested. Such notice shall be made in such form as prescribed by rule or regulation of the Director of the Georgia Forestry Commission, and shall include the following information:
(1)
A map of the area which identifies the location of the tract to be harvested and, as to those trucks which will be traveling to and from such tract for purposes of picking up and hauling loads of cut forest products, the main point of ingress to such tract from a public road and, if different, the main point of egress from such tract to a public road. If multiple points of ingress and/or ingress will be used, all such points shall be identified;
(2)
A statement as to whether the timber will be removed pursuant to a lump sum sale, per unit sale, or owner harvest for purposes of ad valorem taxation under O.C.G.A. § 48-5-7.5;
(3)
The name, address, and daytime telephone number of the timber seller if the harvest is pursuant to a lump sum or per unit sale or of the timber owner if the harvest is an owner harvest; and
(4)
The name, business address, business telephone number, and nighttime or emergency telephone number of the person or firm harvesting such timber.
(Ord. No. 2022-01 , § 1, 3-7-2022)
Sec. 24-148. - Submission of notice.
Subject to the provisions of section 24-150 of this article, the notice required by this article may be submitted in person, by transmission of an electronic record via telefacsimile or e-mail, or by mail.
(Ord. No. 2022-01 , § 1, 3-7-2022)
Sec. 24-149. - Permissive online notice after certain date.
Subject to the provisions of section 24-150 of this article, upon notification published by the Director of the Georgia Forestry Commission that a state-wide notification website or platform is available for public use, persons or firms wishing to utilize said website or platform to provide the notice required by this article may do so at their option, and Oglethorpe County will accept notifications submitted in this manner.
(Ord. No. 2022-01 , § 1, 3-7-2022)
Sec. 24-150. - Mandatory online notice after certain date.
On and after a date specified and published by the Director of the Georgia Forestry Commission, use of the state-wide notification website or platform shall be mandatory and shall be the sole means of providing the notice required by this article; on and after said date submission of the notice by any of the means listed in section 24-148 above shall cease and will no longer be deemed acceptable or in compliance with this article.
(Ord. No. 2022-01 , § 1, 3-7-2022)
Sec. 24-151. - Surety required.
The notice required by this article shall not be or remain effective unless and until the person or firm providing such notice has delivered to the Board of Commissioners of Oglethorpe County or its designated agent a valid surety bond, executed by a surety corporation authorized to transact business in this state, protecting Oglethorpe County against any damage caused by such person or firm in the amount of $5,000.00; provided, however, that at the option of the person or firm harvesting timber a valid irrevocable letter of credit issued by a bank or savings and loan association, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 7-1-4, in the amount of $5,000.00 may be provided in lieu of a surety bond. Such bonds or letters of credit shall be subject to the conditions set forth in sections 24-152 and 24-153 of this article. No more than one bond or letter of credit shall be required from each person or firm harvesting timber, regardless of the number of tracts harvested in the county for so long as the bond or letter of credit remains in effect. The bond or letter of credit required herein shall be valid only for the calendar year in which it was delivered.
(Ord. No. 2022-01 , § 1, 3-7-2022)
Sec. 24-152. - Purpose of surety; right of county to call bond.
The bond or letter of credit required by section 24-151 of this article shall protect Oglethorpe County against any damage requiring re-ditching or repair of existing ditch structures or the removal of any harvesting residue, including tree tops, debris, logs, pulpwood and other materials, placed in or around the county's rights-of-way caused by such person or firm tendering the bond or letter of credit. The proceeds of such bond or letter of credit shall be available to reimburse the county for any cost incurred to repair such damages or remove such debris in or around the county's rights-of-way. The proceeds of such bond or letter of credit shall also be available to reimburse the county for any costs incurred to maintain or repair county roads damaged by the ingress or egress of motor vehicles engaged in the harvest operations located within 500 feet of any point of ingress or egress of the timber harvesting operation. The right of Oglethorpe County to call such bond or letter of credit in accordance with the provisions of section 24-153 of this article shall be in addition to any other remedies available to the county at law or in equity for damage to county roads or rights-of-way.
(Ord. No. 2022-01 , § 1, 3-7-2022)
Sec. 24-153. - Damage; claim on surety.
When damage results from a person or firm's harvesting activities, the Board of Commissioners of Oglethorpe County shall make and provide a written claim to the person or firm causing the damage within 30 business days after the board of commissioners becomes aware of the damage. Such claim may be given in person, by telefacsimile, email or mail. The claim shall describe the damage in detail and, in compliance with subsection 24-154(1) of this article, give the person or firm the opportunity to repair such damage within 30 days of the notification; provided, however, the county shall be authorized to repair the damage immediately if the board of commissioners or its designee determines the conditions present a threat to public safety, health or welfare and, upon making such repairs, shall present to the person or firm and the issuer of the applicable bond or letter of credit an itemized list of expenses incurred as a claim against the responsible party and the issuer of its bond or letter of credit. Upon the issuance of a claim as provided in this section the Board of Commissioners of Oglethorpe County or its designee shall notify the issuer of the bond or letter of credit that a claim has been made and will be resolved or adjudicated according to the terms of this article.
(Ord. No. 2022-01 , § 1, 3-7-2022)
Sec. 24-154. - Response; repair of damage or appeal of claim.
Within 30 days of receipt of the written claim described in section 24-153, the person or firm against whom the claim is submitted may:
(1)
Repair such damage at his or its own expense with the approval and supervision of the Board of Commissioners of Oglethorpe County or its designee. When repairs are completed to the satisfaction of the board of commissioners or its designee, the board of commissioners or its designee shall provide a written notification of satisfactory completion within five business days to the responsible person or firm and to the surety issuing the bond or the bank issuing the letter of credit, thereby terminating the claim.
(2)
In the event of inclement weather or other factors preventing repair of the damage, request a 30-day extension to repair the damage from the Board of Commissioners of Oglethorpe County, provided that no extensions shall exceed 90 days from the date the claim was tendered. Approval of any extension shall be at the discretion of the Board of Commissioners of Oglethorpe County or its designee.
(3)
Appeal the claim to the Magistrate Court of Oglethorpe County. Any such appeal must name the issuer of the bond or letter of credit as a party, who shall be served with all pleadings in the action and shall have the right to appear. The magistrate court will hear evidence and arguments within 30 days of the written appeal and issue a ruling within ten days of such hearing. Any such appeal shall toll the 30-day period, or any extension thereof, required by section 24-153 of this article. If the magistrate court rules in favor of the person or firm against whom the claim was made, the county shall have no right to recover any proceeds of the bond or letter of credit, and judgment shall be entered against the county. If the magistrate court rules in favor of the county the court shall determine the amount of damages to which the county is entitled to recover and enter judgment accordingly; the Board of Commissioners of Oglethorpe County shall be authorized to call the bond or letter of credit and recover from the proceeds thereof an amount equal to the judgment entered by the court, up to the total amount of the bond or letter of credit. The portion of any judgment entered in favor of the county that exceeds the amount of the bond or letter of credit shall be subject to collection by any additional remedies at law or equity.
(4)
In the event the person or firm against whom the claim has been submitted fails to take any of the actions allowed under subsections (1), (2) or (3) of this section within the time required therein, such person or firm shall be deemed to have waived any and all rights to contest the call of the bond or letter of credit.
(Ord. No. 2022-01 , § 1, 3-7-2022)
Sec. 24-155. - Harvesting operation beyond calendar year.
If the person or firm tendering a bond or letter of credit pursuant to the requirements of this article continues its timber harvesting operation beyond the calendar year in which the bond or letter of credit was issued, the person or firm continuing the timber harvesting operation shall tender a new bond or letter of credit within five business days after the first day of the new calendar year.
(Ord. No. 2022-01 , § 1, 3-7-2022)
Sec. 24-156. - Revocation of surety.
In the event a bond or letter of credit tendered pursuant to the requirements of this article is revoked by the surety or bank, then a valid replacement bond or letter of credit must be delivered to the Board of Commissioners of Oglethorpe County within five business days after the date of revocation in order for timber harvesting operations to continue. In addition, if the person or firm tendering the bond or letter of credit caused its revocation, the amount of the bond or letter of credit required shall be increased to $7,500.00 after the first revocation, and $10,000.00 after a second revocation caused by the person or firm tendering the bond or letter of credit. The maximum amount of the bond or letter of credit shall not exceed $10,000.00.
(Ord. No. 2022-01 , § 1, 3-7-2022)
Sec. 24-157. - Duration and change of operation.
Submission of the notice required by this article shall authorize the person or firm submitting same to undertake the timber harvesting operation described in the notice and shall remain in effect until such time as the person or firm gives notice that the harvesting operation is complete; provided, however, that any change in the facts required to be provided for purposes of such notice, including but not limited to, a change in the scope or extent of the operation, must be reported to the Board of Commissioners of Oglethorpe County within three business days after such change.

Sec. 24-158. - Operation without providing notice; fine.
Any person or firm that engages in a timber harvesting operation in the unincorporated portion of Oglethorpe County without complying with the notice requirements of this article shall be subject to a citation and trial, and upon conviction shall be fined in an amount not to exceed $1,500.00 for each violation.
(Ord. No. 2022-01 , § 1, 3-7-2022)
Sec. 24-159. - Zoning requirements.
This article applies to activities which qualify as forestry land management practices or agricultural operations under O.C.G.A. §§ 12-7-17(5) and (6) on land that is zoned for or used for forestry, silvicultural or agricultural purposes. It shall not authorize land disturbing activities incidental to development in conflict with the limitations set forth in O.C.G.A. § 12-7-17(6).
(Ord. No. 2022-01 , § 1, 3-7-2022)
Sec. 24-160. - No fee for notice; no other permit required.
Consistent with O.C.G.A. § 12-6-24:
(1)
No fee shall be charged to provide and submit the notice required by this article; and
(2)
No permit, including a driveway permit, shall be required of the person or firm engaged in a timber harvesting operation as defined by said code section. Persons and firms providing the notice required by this article may be asked to consult with county officials responsible for roads and public works for the purpose of minimizing damage to the county's roads, rights-of-way and infrastructure, and are urged to follow recommendations from county officials. Notwithstanding the forgoing, the person or firm conducting the timber harvest operation bears ultimate responsibility for their actions, and nothing in this article shall preclude the Board of Commissioners of Oglethorpe County from taking any and all legal action necessary to protect its property and the health, safety and welfare of its citizens.
(Ord. No. 2022-01 , § 1, 3-7-2022)
