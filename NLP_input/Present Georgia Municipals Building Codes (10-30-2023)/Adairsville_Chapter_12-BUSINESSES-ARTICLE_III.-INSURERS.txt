DIVISION 2. - LIFE INSURERS

Sec. 12-70. - Purpose.
The purpose of this division is to impose license fees on life insurers conducting business within the city; to impose a gross premiums tax on insurers operating within the city; to provide an effective date; to repeal conflicting ordinances; and for other purposes.
(Code 1986, ch. V, § 5.1; Code 2004, § 58-51)
Sec. 12-71. - License fees.
All insurance license fees will be based on city population as dictated and authorized by the state municipal association. Fees will fluctuate as population changes. There is levied an annual license fee upon each life insurer doing business within the city as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk. For each separate business location in excess of one not covered by section 12-72, which is operating on behalf of such insurers within the city, there is levied a license fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk. For the purposes of this division, the term "insurer" means a company which is authorized to transact business in the class of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5(1).
(Code 1986, ch. V, § 5.2; Code 2004, § 58-52)
Sec. 12-72. - License fee for life insurers insuring certain risks at additional business locations.
For each separate business location, not otherwise subject to a license fee under this division, operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing, and in connection with such loans or sales offers, solicits or takes applications for insurance through a licensed agent of a life insurer for life insurance, such insurer shall pay an additional license fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1986, ch. V, § 5.3; Code 2004, § 58-53)
Sec. 12-73. - Life insurance agency license fees; independent life insurance agencies, brokers, etc., not otherwise licensed.
There is levied an annual license fee upon independent agencies and brokers for each separate business location from which a life insurance business is conducted and which is not subject to the company license fee imposed by section 12-70, as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1986, ch. V, § 5.4; Code 2004, § 58-54)
Sec. 12-74. - Gross premiums tax imposed on life insurers.
There is levied an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer writing life, accident and sickness insurance within the city in an amount equal to one percent of the gross direct premiums received during the calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.1. Gross direct premiums shall have the meaning provided in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 12-71.
(Code 1986, ch. V, § 5.5; Code 2004, § 58-55)
Sec. 12-75. - Gross premiums tax, all other insurers.
There is levied an annual tax upon each insurer, other than an insurer transacting business in the class of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5(1), doing business within the city in an amount equal to 2½ of the gross direct premiums received during the calendar year, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2. The term "gross direct premiums" as used in this section shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2(A).
(Code 1986, ch. V, § 5.6; Code 2004, § 58-56)

DIVISION 3. - NONLIFE INSURERS

Sec. 12-94. - Insurer license fees.
All insurance license fees will be based on city population as dictated and authorized by the state municipal association. Fees will fluctuate as population changes. There is levied an annual license fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk upon each insurer doing business within the city. For each separate business location in excess of one not covered by section 12-95, which is operating on behalf of insurers within the city there is levied a license fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk. For the purposes of this division, the term "insurer" means a company which is authorized to transact business in any of the classes of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5.
(Code 1986, ch. V, § 6.1; Code 2004, § 58-81)
Sec. 12-95. - License fee for insurers insuring certain risks at additional business locations.
For each separate business location, not otherwise subject to a license fee under this division, operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing, and in connection with such loans or sales solicits or takes application for insurance through a licensed agent of an insurer for insurance, such insurer shall pay an additional license fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1986, ch. V, § 6.2; Code 2004, § 58-82)
Sec. 12-96. - Insurance agency license fees; independent insurance agencies, brokers, etc., not otherwise licensed.
There is levied an annual license fee upon independent agencies and brokers for each separate business location from which an insurance business is conducted and which is not subject to the company license fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1986, ch. V, § 6.3; Code 2004, § 58-83)
Sec. 12-97. - Due date for license fees.
License fees imposed in this article shall be due and payable on the January 1 of each year.
(Code 1986, ch. V, § 6.4; Code 2004, § 58-84)
