Sec. 50-1. - Failure of library patron to return borrowed property; violation; penalties.
(a)
Any person who borrows from any county public library any book, newspaper, magazine, manuscript, pamphlet, publication, recording, video product, microform, computer software, film or other article or equipment necessary to its display or use belonging to or in the care of such public library under any agreement to return such article, and fails to return such article or equipment necessary to its display or use, shall be given written notice, mailed to such person's last known address or delivered in person, to return such article or equipment within 15 days after the date of such notification. Such notice shall contain a copy of this section and O.C.G.A. § 20-5-53. If such person shall thereafter willfully and knowingly fail to return such article or equipment within such 15-day period, such person shall be guilty of a misdemeanor.
(b)
Any person who violates the provisions of this section shall be subject to penalties and fines as set forth in section 1-9, and shall be required to return such borrowed article or equipment or provide reimbursement for the replacement cost of such borrowed article or equipment.
(Code 1979, § 2-8.5-1)
