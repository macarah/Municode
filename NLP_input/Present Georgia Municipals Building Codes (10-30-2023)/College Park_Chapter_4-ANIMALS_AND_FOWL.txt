ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL[2]

Sec. 4-1. - Bird sanctuary—City designated.
The entire area embraced within the corporate limits of the city shall be, and the same is hereby designated as a bird sanctuary.
(Code 1963, § 4-1)
Sec. 4-2. - Same—Trapping, hunting, molesting birds.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to trap, hunt, shoot or attempt to shoot or molest in any manner any bird or wild fowl or to rob bird nests or wild fowl nests; provided, however, that if starlings or similar birds are found to be congregating in such numbers in a particular locality that they constitute a nuisance or a menace to health or property in the opinion of the proper health authorities, then said health authorities shall meet with representatives of the Audubon Society, Bird Club, Garden Club or Humane Society, or as many of said clubs as are found to exist in the city after having given at least three (3) days' actual notice of the time and place of said meeting to the representatives of said clubs.
(b)
If as a result of said meeting no satisfactory alternative is found to abate such nuisance, said birds may be destroyed in such numbers and in such manner as is deemed advisable by said health authorities under the supervision of the chief of police.
(Code 1963, § 4-2)
State Law reference— Municipal authority to abate nuisances, O.C.G.A. § 41-2-5.
Sec. 4-3. - Cruelty to animals.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to overload, poison, cruelly treat, maim, tease, bruise, deprive of necessary substance or medical attention, improperly use, deprive of shade and shelter, or in any manner whatsoever torture, kill, or abuse any animal.
(b)
No person shall abandon any animal on any property, public or private, or keep an animal under unsanitary conditions.
(c)
It shall be unlawful to transport any dog in an open bed truck, except when safely confined in a portable kennel, or when safely restrained by a harness to prevent the animal from escaping out of the vehicle or having the ability to extend over the end of the vehicle while it is in motion.
(d)
No animal shall be transported in the trunk of any vehicle or under a tonneau or hard cover of a pickup truck.
(e)
Law enforcement officers may use any reasonable force necessary to remove an animal locked in a closed vehicle when the ambient temperature is above seventy-five (75) degrees Fahrenheit outside the vehicle.
(f)
No animal may be given away as a prize, auctioned, raffled, or sold on any public street within the city limits.
(g)
All breeders operating within the city must obtain all required permits from, and abide by all regulations made or issued by, the United States Department of Agriculture and the Georgia Department of Agriculture. Such permits shall be presented to city officials upon demand.
(Code 1963, § 4-9; Ord. No. 2011-04, § 1, 4-4-11)
State Law reference— Cruelty to animals, O.C.G.A. §§ 4-8-5, 16-12-4.
Sec. 4-4. - Poisoning animals.
It shall be unlawful for any person to put out or cause to be put out any poison within the city for the killing of any dog or other domestic animal.
(Code 1963, § 4-10)
Sec. 4-5. - Keeping of livestock, fowl and rabbits—Horses, mules, goats.
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep or maintain any barn, space or stable for any horse, mule or goat, or to permit any horse, mule or goat to be kept, maintained or grazed within the city for any purpose or for any length of time except in compliance with the following:
(1)
Whenever any person desires to keep or maintain any horse, mule, or goat or to permit any horse, mule or goat to be kept, maintained or grazed within five hundred (500) feet of any house or building where people reside or work, such person shall file an application for permit therefor with the city. Before being entitled to the issuance of a permit the affiant must obtain the written consent of the occupants of such house or building and file the same with the city. The governing body may approve or reject any application.
(2)
For each horse, mule or goat kept there shall be provided a space of at least five thousand (5,000) square feet; not more than one (1) horse, mule or goat shall be kept in such space, it being the purpose and intent of this section to require an area for the keeping of horses, mules or goats based upon five thousand (5,000) square feet per horse, mule or goat multiplied by the number of horses, mules or goats kept in the event more than one horse, mule or goat shall be kept.
(3)
No horse, mule or goat shall be kept, maintained or grazed within seventy-five (75) feet of any residence in the city.
(Code 1963, § 4-4)
Cross reference— Nuisance defined, abatement, § 8-2.
Sec. 4-6. - Same—Cows.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep any cow within the city for any purpose or for any length of time except in compliance with the following:
(1)
For each cow kept there shall be provided a space of at least twenty thousand (20,000) square feet; not more than one cow shall be kept in such space, it being the purpose and intent of this section to require an area for the keeping of cows based upon twenty thousand (20,000) square feet per cow multiplied by the number of cows kept, in the event more than one cow shall be kept.
(2)
No cow shall be kept in a barn, space or stable within seventy-five (75) feet of any residence.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep within the city any cow for the purpose of barter and sale.
(Code 1963, § 4-5)
Sec. 4-7. - Same—Ducks, chickens, turkeys, geese and rabbits.
See appendix A - zoning, article 6 - development standards, section 6.16 - keeping of farm animals standards (FA).
(Code 1963, § 4-5.1; Ord. No. 2021-01, § 1, 3-15-21)
Sec. 4-8. - Livestock or fowl at large.
It shall be unlawful for any person to wilfully permit any livestock or any duck, goose, chicken or other domestic fowl belonging to such person to run or be at large or upon the premises of any other person within the city, without the consent of the owner or tenant in possession of such premises.
(Code 1963, § 4-3)
State Law reference— Livestock at large, O.C.G.A. § 4-3-1 et seq.
Sec. 4-9. - Confinement of animals; impounding, disposal, redemption.
(a)
Confinement required. It shall be unlawful for the owner, custodian, harborer, or any person having custody of any animals, including but not limited to a dog, cat, pig, bird, rodent, or reptile (hereinafter a "pet") within the city to fail to confine said pet at all times by one (1) of the following means:
(1)
A leash of not more than six (6) feet in length and in the care of a competent person;
(2)
Inside an area completely enclosed by a fence sufficient to keep the pet contained therein;
(3)
Inside a carrier appropriate for the particular pet; or
(4)
Inside a housing or completely enclosed structure.
(b)
Tethering.
(1)
It shall be unlawful for the owner, custodian, or harborer of any pet to restrain, or anchor, a pet by means of a tether to any fixed or inanimate object.
(2)
Notwithstanding subsection (1) above, a dog may be temporarily restrained by means of a tether while the dog is attended by its owner, custodian, or harborer who is physically present with the dog.
(3)
Any tether used to temporarily confine a dog, pursuant to subsection (2) above, while attended by its owner, custodian, or harborer, must be properly attached to a properly fitted collar, or harness. Tethers shall not be wrapped directly around the dogs neck; nor shall tethers be excessively heavy or weighted so as to inhibit the dogs movement.
(4)
No vicious, dangerous, or potentially dangerous dog shall be chained, tethered, or otherwise tied while unattended by its owner, custodian, or harborer to any inanimate object outside of its proper enclosure, as provided for in this chapter.
(c)
Electronic confinement.
(1)
Electronic confinement systems shall be acceptable enclosures when, and if, the equipment is properly maintained, and it is in continuous working order, and the pet to be contained wears the appropriate electronic collar within the system perimeter. A sign, or signs, shall be posted advising that an electronic confinement system is in use on properties using such a system.
(2)
Electronic confinement systems cannot be used as either a primary or secondary enclosure, as required by this section, for the following pets:
a.
Guard dogs;
b.
Animals that have been deemed or declared to be dangerous by a court of competent jurisdiction, or pursuant to the operation of the laws of the state;
c.
Vicious, dangerous, or potentially dangerous dogs, as provided for in this chapter.
(d)
Impounding. All pets not confined as required by this section, or otherwise in violation of any of the provision of this chapter, shall be impounded by a duly authorized officer or agent of the city. The owner, custodian, or harborer of a pet impounded pursuant to this chapter shall be charged and brought before the city court, and if found guilty of violating this chapter, fined not less than thirty-five dollars ($35.00), for the first violation of this chapter. Subsequent violations of this chapter by the same person shall be considered by the city court, and progressively higher fines may be levied as circumstances warrant.
(e)
Redemption, disposal.
(1)
Whenever the name and address of any person appears on the collar of, or the information is found on an imbedded identification microchip in, an impounded pet, such person shall be notified of such seizure in writing by a postcard, addressed to such person, and deposited in the United States Mail with appropriate postage.
(2)
Any pet seized and impounded by the city under any of the provisions of this section shall be detained for a period of forty-eight (48) hours, or in the case of animals identified in subsection (1), three (3) days from the mailing of notification, and if not claimed, or redeemed, by the owner, or the person harboring said pet within such period, shall be disposed of in such manner as the officers or agents having custody of said pet may designate.
(3)
The animal control officer shall, from time to time, set a fee schedule detailing the redemption fee(s) that must be paid before such pet(s) are returned to its owner. The redemption fee for vicious, dangerous, or potentially dangerous dogs, as established and identified pursuant to the terms of this chapter, shall be double the fee set in the fee schedule provided for herein. No pet shall be returned to the owner, or the person harboring said pet, until proof of rabies inoculation has been submitted to the city's duly authorized officer or agent, or the owner has paid the inoculation fee and caused the pet to be inoculated.
(f)
The city, its officers, employees, or agents, shall not be responsible for any accidents, diseases, injuries, or death of any animal that may occur while said animal is being impounded, or boarded, by the city, at an animal control facility either owned by or approved by the city.
(Code 1963, §§ 4-6—4-8; Ord. No. 2002-35, § 1, 8-5-02; Ord. No. 2011-04, § 2, 4-4-11)
State Law reference— Female dogs in heat not to be at large, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-6; inoculation required, O.C.G.A. § 31-19-5.
Sec. 4-10. - Vicious animals.
(a)
The words "vicious animal" shall mean any animal which attacks, bites, or injures humans, other animals without adequate provocation; or which, because of temperament, conditioning, or training, has a known propensity to attack, bite, or injure other living creatures; or an animal which has on one or more occasions caused injury to other living creatures.
(b)
No person owning or having custody or control of any dog or other animal known by such person to be vicious or dangerous shall permit it to run at large or permit it to run loose on or within the premises of such person in such a manner as to endanger the life or limb of any person lawfully entering such premises.
(c)
A vicious animal shall be confined by the owner, custodian, or harborer within a building or secure enclosure and shall not be taken out of such building or secured enclosure unless securely restrained.
(d)
A vicious animal, dangerous, or potentially dangerous dog shall not be upon any street, public place, or outside its enclosure on the owner's property unless said animal is securely restrained by a leash in the charge of a competent person, or is humanely muzzled, when appropriate, as determined by the animal control officer defined in this chapter.
(Ord. No. 87-15, § 3, 6-1-87; Ord. No. 2011-04, § 3, 4-4-11)
Sec. 4-11. - Confinement of female dogs in heat required.
It shall be the duty of any owner, custodian, or harborer of any female dog in heat to securely confine such dog in a building or other enclosure to prevent such dog from coming into contact with another dog except for planned breeding. Confinement should be sufficient to prevent the female dog in heat from enticing, tempting, or attracting other animals off their property.
(Ord. No. 87-15, § 4, 6-1-87)
State Law reference— Female dogs in heat not to be at large, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-6.
Sec. 4-12. - Vaccination of dogs and cats; display of tag required.
(a)
Any person having custody of a dog or cat over four (4) months of age shall be required to maintain a current rabies vaccination on such dog or cat. The dog or cat shall be vaccinated upon its reaching the age of four (4) months, and any dog or cat over the age of four (4) months shall be vaccinated within fourteen (14) days of its entry into the city. Vaccinations must be performed by a veterinarian licensed in the State of Georgia.
(b)
Any person having custody of a dog or cat over four (4) months of age shall be required to affix a rabies vaccination tag to a collar worn by the dog or cat at all times; provided, however, that this section shall not apply to licensed kennels.
(Ord. No. 96-23, § 1, 8-19-95)
Sec. 4-13. - Kennel registration.
(a)
For this section, a kennel shall be a premises where there are kept, maintained, or harbored, for a period of fourteen (14) days or longer, four (4) or more dogs, each dog being over the age of four (4) months.
(b)
The owner or person in possession of a premises where a kennel is located shall be required to apply to the city inspections department for a kennel permit.
(c)
A kennel permit will be issued upon presentation of proof that the premises and dogs kept at the kennel meet the requirements set out in this chapter and upon payment of the annual fee, set in a fee schedule adopted from time to time by the mayor and council.
(d)
Kennel permits will be valid for one (1) year from the date of issuance; provided that the permit is not revoked during the year for violations of this article. Applications to renew a kennel permit must be made at least fourteen (14) days prior to the expiration of the existing permit.
(e)
The animal control officers shall issue individual license tags for each dog located in a kennel; additional fees for each such tag shall not be charged beyond the annual kennel permit fee.
(f)
If the owner or operator of a kennel presents a city occupation tax certificate for the current year to the inspections department, the annual kennel permit fee shall be waived.
(Ord. No. 2011-04, § 4, 4-4-11)
Sec. 4-14. - Removal of excrement.
It shall be unlawful for any person owning, possessing, harboring, or having care, charge, control or custody of a pet to fail to remove any excrement left by that pet on any public sidewalk, gutter, street, lot, public park or other area or right-of-way traversed by the public. Such excrement shall be immediately removed from areas traversed by the public by placing said matter in a sealed container and thereafter disposing of it in a trash receptacle or refuse container. This section shall not apply to visually impaired persons who have the charge, control or use of a certified guide dog.
(Ord. No. 2021-13, § 1, 11-1-21)

ARTICLE II. - DANGEROUS DOGS

Sec. 4-21. - Construction.
(a)
This article is enacted pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq. Except as otherwise provided in this section, all terms shall be defined and construed in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq.
(b)
"Potentially dangerous dog" means any dog that without provocation bites a human being on public or private property at any time after March 31, 1989, or any dog which is classified by the dog control officer substantially or in whole as being of one of the following breeds: Pitbull, American Pitbull Terrier, American Staffordshire Terrier, Staffordshire Bull Terrier, Rottweiler, Doberman, German Shepherd.
(c)
Proper enclosure means an enclosure for keeping a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog while on the owner's property securely confined indoors or any securely enclosed and locked pen, fence, or structure suitable to prevent the entry of young children and designed to prevent dogs from escaping. Any such pen or structure shall have secure sides and a secure top, and if the dog is enclosed within a fence, all sides of the fence shall be of a height of no less than six (6) feet, but in any event of a sufficient height to prevent a dog's escape over the fence. Additionally, such fence shall have the bottom of the fence constructed or secured in such manner as to prevent the dog's escaping under the fence by burying an additional two (2) feet of fencing underground, or by placing a concrete slab under the perimeter of the enclosure. Any such enclosure shall also provide the dog with protection from the elements. No such enclosure shall be located in the front or side yard of a residence.
(d)
A warning sign or signs, such as "Beware of Dog," shall be conspicuously posted denoting a vicious, dangerous, or potentially dangerous dog on the premises. These signs shall be exempt from building permit requirements.
(Ord. No. 89-2, § 3, 1-3-89; Ord. No. 97-22, § 1, 9-2-97; Ord. No. 2011-04, § 5, 4-4-11)
Sec. 4-22. - Appointment of a dog control officer and animal control board.
(a)
The mayor and council shall designate a dog control officer who shall perform the duties and exercise the powers assigned to that position by the Dangerous Dog Act.
(b)
The board of appeals shall serve as the animal control board. Members shall receive such compensation as is established by the mayor and council. The animal control board will conduct hearings as provided for by O.C.G.A. § 4-8-24 and section 4-24 of this Code.
(c)
The dog control officer is authorized to go upon any premises within the city limits to seize, for impounding, any dog or other animal which the officer is in immediate pursuit, provided that the dog control officer shall not enter an occupied building without the permission of the owner or occupant of such building.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person, in any manner, to interfere with, hinder, resist, obstruct, or molest an animal control officer in the performance of his/her duties or for any person to remove any animal from the animal control vehicle, animal control shelter, or animal control trap without the permission of the animal control officer.
(Ord. No. 89-2, § 3, 1-3-89; Ord. No. 2011-04, § 6, 4-4-11)
Sec. 4-23. - Investigation.
The dog control officer may make such investigations and inquiries as may be necessary to identify dangerous dogs and potentially dangerous dogs and their respective owners within the city. The dog control officer shall have the authority to investigate complaints as part of the process of identifying dangerous dogs and potentially dangerous dogs. The dog control officer's authority to investigate shall include the power to enter onto public and private property for the purpose of periodic inspection.
(Ord. No. 89-2, § 3, 1-3-89)
Sec. 4-24. - Classification; notice and hearing.
When the dog control officer, based on his investigation, classifies a dog as a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog, the dog control officer shall follow the procedure set out in O.C.G.A. section 4-8-24 provided that:
(1)
In addition to the certified notice provided by O.C.G.A. section 4-8-24, a second identical notice shall be sent by regular first class mail. Notice shall be deemed received three (3) days after the notice was mailed, in the event the certified notice is not accepted;
(2)
Appeals from the dog control officer's initial classification of a dog as a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog shall be heard before a panel of the animal control board;
(3)
Appeals from any subsequent classification of a dog as a dangerous dog shall be heard before the panel of the animal control board which has not heard the matter previously.
(Ord. No. 89-2, § 3, 1-3-89)
Sec. 4-25. - Registration.
(a)
The owner of a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog must secure a certificate of registration in accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 4-8-25, and obtain from the city, and place upon the dog a special identification tag.
(b)
The dog control officer shall, from time to time, set a fee schedule detailing the annual fee for the certificate of registration.
(c)
Owners, custodians, or harborers of dogs classified by the dog control officers as Pitbull, American Pitbull Terrier, American Staffordshire Terrier, Staffordshire Bull Terrier, Rottweiler, Doberman, and German Shepherd, must notify the dog control officer and secure a certificate of registration, city issued identification tag, and pay registration fees with the city inspection department within seven (7) days of moving the dog(s) into the city. City issued identification tags must be placed upon the dog at all times.
(d)
Nonpayment of the registration fee shall be an offense and shall be punishable under section 1-8 of this Code.
(e)
The dog control officer shall maintain a copy of all state and local laws applicable to dangerous dogs and potentially dangerous dogs which shall be available for inspection by the public during regular business hours. The dog control officer shall not be authorized to interpret these laws nor to give advice to owners.
(f)
Owners, custodians, or harborers of dogs classified by the dog control officers as Pitbull, American Pitbull Terrier, American Staffordshire Terrier, Staffordshire Bull Terrier, Rottweiler, Doberman, and German Shepherd must update the dog control officer if the animal is moved to another premises for a period of more than seven (7) days.
(g)
Prior to the issuance of the certificate of registration required herein, owners, custodians, or harborers of dogs classified by the dog control officers as a dangerous dog, potentially dangerous dog, Pitbull, American Pitbull Terrier, American Staffordshire Terrier, Staffordshire Bull Terrier, Rottweiler, Doberman, and German Shepherd must present evidence to the dog control officer that the dog has been implanted with an identification microchip.
(h)
Prior to the issuance of the certificate of registration required herein, owners, custodians, or harborers of dogs classified by the dog control officers as a dangerous dog, potentially dangerous dog, Pitbull, American Pitbull Terrier, American Staffordshire Terrier, Staffordshire Bull Terrier, Rottweiler, Doberman, and German Shepherd must present evidence to the dog control officer that such owner, custodian, or harborer has obtained either:
(1)
A policy of insurance in the amount of at least one hundred thousand dollars ($100,000.00) issued by an insurer authorized to transact business in this state insuring the owner, custodian, or harborer of the dog against liability for any personal injuries inflicted by the dog; or
(2)
A surety bond in the amount of at least one hundred thousand dollars ($100,000.00) issued by a surety company authorized to transact business in this state payable to any person or persons injured by the dangerous dog.
(Ord. No. 89-2, § 3, 1-3-89; Ord. No. 2011-04, § 7, 4-4-11)
Sec. 4-26. - Confiscation.
(a)
Dangerous dogs and potentially dangerous dogs are subject to immediate confiscation pursuant to the grounds for confiscation in O.C.G.A. section 4-8-27.
(b)
Dangerous dogs and potentially dangerous dogs that have been confiscated shall be taken to such impound facility as is designated from time to time by the mayor and council.
(c)
Dog owners shall pay such confiscation fees and daily boarding fees as established from time to time for the impound facility. Nonpayment of either or both of these fees shall be an offense and shall be punishable under section 1-8 of this Code.
(d)
Dangerous dogs and potentially dangerous dogs shall be returned to their owners pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. section 4-8-27 and after all fees have been paid.
(Ord. No. 89-2, § 3, 1-3-89)
