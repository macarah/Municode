Chapter 70 - EMERGENCY SERVICES
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 70-1. - 9-1-1 charge on prepaid wireless transactions.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2, there is hereby imposed a prepaid wireless 9-1-1 charge as defined by O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(a)(4) upon every prepaid wireless retail transaction occurring within the jurisdiction of the public service answering point in the amount of $0.75.
(Ord. No. 2011-49(11-O-1323), § 1, 11-16-11)
Sec. 70-2. - Collection of 9-1-1 charge on prepaid wireless transactions.
Prepaid wireless 9-1-1 charges collected by sellers shall be remitted to the city's chief financial officer at the times and in the manner provided by Chapter 8 of Title 48 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated with respect to the sales and use tax imposed on prepaid wireless calling service.
(Ord. No. 2011-49(11-O-1323), § 2, 11-16-11)
Sec. 70-3. - Administrative provisions.
The clerk of the city is hereby directed to file with the state revenue commissioner a certified copy of this ordinance and amendments thereto in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(j)(1), by December 31, 2011.
(Ord. No. 2011-49(11-O-1323), § 3, 11-16-11)
Sec. 70-4. - Depositing of funds; use of funds.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(j)(5), funds received by the city from charges imposed by this ordinance shall be deposited in the emergency telephone system fund maintained by the city pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134(d)(2) and kept separate from general revenue of the jurisdiction. All such funds shall be used exclusively for the purposes authorized by O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134(f).
(Ord. No. 2011-49(11-O-1323), § 4, 11-16-11)
Sec. 70-5. - Effective date.
As required by O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(j)(1), this ordinance shall become effective January 1, 2012.
(Ord. No. 2011-49(11-O-1323), § 5, 11-16-11)
Secs. 70-6—70-25. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - BURGLAR OR ROBBERY ALARM SYSTEMS

Sec. 70-26. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alarm companies mean any company or entity that installs systems and/or monitors and/or responds to alarm systems for remuneration.
Automatic dialing device means any device connected to an alarm system which automatically sends a prerecorded message or coded signal indicating the activation of the alarm system to a predetermined location.
Automatic response alarm system means equipment and devices designed to detect and signal an unauthorized intrusion, an attempted burglary, robbery or fire at a protected premises and signals an alarm system business, monitoring station or other entity with respect to such signal city police or fire personnel are expected to respond.
False alarm means the activation of an emergency alarm by other than a forced entry, attempted forced entry or fire at the premises and at a time when no burglary or robbery is being committed and no fire is occurring on the premises.
Independent alarm system means the equipment and devices designed to detect and signal an unauthorized intrusion, an attempted burglary, a robbery, or a fire at a protected premises and upon activation does not automatically signal an alarm company, monitoring station or other entity for emergency response.
Multiple alarm sites mean more than one alarm system on the premises.
Response means the dispatch of a police officer or fire personnel to the premises where an alarm system has been activated indicating a burglary, robbery or fire is presently in progress on those premises.
(Ord. No. 2009-03(08-O-2137), § 1, 1-27-09)
Sec. 70-27. - Permit for business of installation, operation or maintenance.
(a)
Prior to doing business within the city, any person or entity who installs, operates or maintains burglar or robbery alarm devices shall obtain a business license from the city.
(b)
Any person or entity who installs an alarm system shall obtain building permit from the city.
(c)
No alarm system business, monitoring station or other entity shall operate or cause to be operated or install any device or combination of devices that will, upon action either mechanically, electronically or by any other automatic means, initiate a call and deliver a recorded message to any telephone number assigned to the city.
(Ord. No. 2009-03(08-O-2137), § 1, 1-27-09)
Sec. 70-28. - Training.
The person in control of the property on which an alarm system is installed shall train all persons who may activate the alarm system in the proper operation of the alarm system.
(Ord. No. 2009-03(08-O-2137), § 1, 1-27-09)
Sec. 70-29. - Enforcement.
Enforcement of this article, including the collection of civil penalties for false alarms, shall be the responsibility of the City of Atlanta Solicitor or his/her designee and the municipal court.
(Ord. No. 2009-03(08-O-2137), § 1, 1-27-09)
Sec. 70-30. - False alarms of automatic response alarm systems; civil penalty.
(a)
The City of Atlanta shall be exempt from such penalties for responses to false alarms of automatic response alarm systems installed in city-owned facilities within the boundaries of the city.
(b)
The City of Atlanta 911 call center receives the call for assistance, dispatches emergency personnel and gets a report from the responding members of the Atlanta Police Department or the Atlanta Fire Rescue Department of the results of the alarm.
(c)
The City of Atlanta 911 Call Center shall keep a record of the number of false alarms at a specific address, and shall notify the city solicitor of same. The city solicitor shall file a warning or citation with the municipal court and shall send copy of the warning or citation to the person having or maintaining the property on which the alarm system is installed.
(d)
The person having or maintaining the property on which the alarm system is installed shall be liable for a civil monetary penalty as set forth in this subsection if it is found as evidenced by the report of the Atlanta Police Department or the Atlanta Fire Rescue Department, that a false alarm, as defined in section 70-26, occurred on said property:
(1)
For the first false alarm in a calendar year, a warning shall be given, but no civil penalty shall be imposed.
(2)
For the second false alarm in a calendar year, the person or entity shall be charged a $100.00 civil penalty.
(3)
For the third false alarm in a calendar year, the person or entity shall be charged a $200.00 civil penalty.
(4)
For the fourth false alarm in a calendar year, the person or entity shall be charged a $300.00 civil penalty.
(5)
For the fifth and sixth false alarms in a calendar year, the person or entity shall be charged a $750.00 civil penalty.
(6)
For all successive false alarms in a calendar year, the person or entity shall be charged a $1,000.00 civil penalty.
(Ord. No. 2009-03(08-O-2137), § 1, 1-27-09)
Sec. 70-31. - Notice.
(a)
The city solicitor shall send by first-class mail addressed to the person having or maintaining the property on which the alarm system is installed postmarked no later than ten days after the date of the alleged false alarm:
(1)
A warning, or citation for the false alarm, which shall include the date and time of the false alarm, the location of the property where the alarm system at issue was installed, the number of false alarms within the calendar year, the amount of civil monetary penalty imposed, and the date by which the civil monetary penalty shall be paid;
(2)
A copy of a certificate sworn to by the member of the Atlanta Police Department or the Atlanta Fire Rescue Department who responded to the property at the date and time of the alleged false alarm setting forth that there was a false alarm at the property, as defined in section 70-26
(3)
A statement of the inference provided by subsection (c) of this section and of the means specified therein by which such inference may be rebutted;
(4)
Information advising the person having or maintaining the property on which the alarm system is installed of the manner and time in which liability as alleged in the citation may be contested in court; and
(5)
Warning that failure to pay the civil monetary penalty or to contest liability in a timely manner shall waive any right to contest liability and result in a civil monetary penalty.
(Ord. No. 2009-03(08-O-2137), § 1, 1-27-09)
Sec. 70-32. - Proof and liability
(a)
Proof that a false alarm, as defined in section 70-26, occurred shall be evidenced by the sworn statement of the member of the Atlanta Police Department or the Atlanta Fire Rescue Department who responded to the property at the date and time of the false alarm at the property as defined in section 70-26. A copy of a certificate of this sworn statement shall be prima-facie evidence of the facts contained therein.
(b)
Liability under this chapter shall be determined based upon preponderance of the evidence. Prima-facie evidence that there was a false alarm on the property on which the alarm system is installed, together with proof that the person to whom the citation was issued is the person having or maintaining the property on which the alarm system is installed shall permit the trier of fact in its discretion to infer that such person or entity is liable for the false alarm. Such an inference may be rebutted in the person or entity:
(1)
Testifies under oath in open court that he or she was not the person having or maintaining the property on which the alarm system was installed at the time of the alleged false alarm.
(2)
Testifies under oath and in open court that the alarm was activated due to a forced entry, attempted forced entry or fire at the premises.
(Ord. No. 2009-03(08-O-2137), § 1, 1-27-09)
Sec. 70-33. - False alarm violations shall be noncriminal.
A false alarm violation in accordance with this chapter shall be deemed non-criminal, and imposition of a civil penalty pursuant to this chapter shall not be deemed a conviction and shall not be made a part of the criminal record of the person upon whom such liability is imposed.
(Ord. No. 2009-03(08-O-2137), § 1, 1-27-09)
Sec. 70-34. - Liability in absentia.
If a person summoned by first-class mail fails to appear on the date of return set out in the citation and has not paid the penalty for the false alarm violation, the person summoned shall have waived the right to contest the false alarm violation and shall be liable for the civil monetary penalty as set forth in the citation.
(Ord. No. 2009-03(08-O-2137), § 1, 1-27-09)
Sec. 70-35. - Jurisdiction shall lie in the municipal court.
The City of Atlanta Municipal Court shall have jurisdiction over cases arising under this chapter and shall be authorized to impose the civil monetary penalty provided by this chapter. The provisions of law governing jurisdiction, procedure, defenses, adjudication, appeal, and payment and distribution of penalties otherwise applicable to City of Atlanta Code violations adjudicated in the municipal court shall apply to enforcement under this chapter.
(Ord. No. 2009-03(08-O-2137), § 1, 1-27-09)
