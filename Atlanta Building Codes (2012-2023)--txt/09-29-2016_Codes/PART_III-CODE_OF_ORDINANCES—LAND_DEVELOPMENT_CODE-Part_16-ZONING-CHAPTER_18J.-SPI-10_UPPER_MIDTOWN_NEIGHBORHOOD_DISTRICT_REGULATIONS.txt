CHAPTER 18J. - SPI-10 UPPER MIDTOWN NEIGHBORHOOD DISTRICT REGULATIONS

Sec. 16-18J.001. - Scope of provisions.
The regulations set forth in this chapter, or set forth elsewhere in this part, when referred to in this chapter, are the regulations for the SPI-10 Upper Midtown Neighborhood District.
(Code 1977, § 16-18J.001)
Sec. 16-18J.002. - Statement of intent.
The intent of this chapter in establishing the SPI-10 Upper Midtown Neighborhood District is as follows:
It is within the public interest to:
(1)
Maximize the advantages of the presence of Piedmont Park for Upper Midtown neighborhood residents and other citizens of Atlanta.
(2)
Encourage opportunities for high- and very-high-density residential development with views into Piedmont Park.
(3)
Encourage the development of buildings with orientations that allow park views from other buildings.
(4)
Preserve and protect front and side yard green spaces.
(5)
Preserve and protect the Upper Midtown neighborhood character from the development of exposed parking structures.
(6)
Facilitate safe and convenient pedestrian circulation and minimize pedestrian/vehicular conflicts through establishment of wide sidewalks and implementation of the pedestrian space plan.
(Code 1977, § 16-18J.002)
Sec. 16-18J.003. - Effect of SPI-10 district designation.
The SPI-10 district designation shall modify existing district regulations by superseding existing regulations which are not in accordance with SPI-10 district regulations.
(Code 1977, § 16-18J.003)
Sec. 16-18J.004. - General regulations.
(1)
Minimum Yard Requirements:
(a)
For all residential districts the following restrictions shall apply:
1.
There shall be setbacks along all street frontage of not less than 20 feet.
2.
There shall be side and rear yards, each having a depth of not less than 20 feet or as determined by the distance set forth in section 16-28.011, whichever is greater.
(b)
In commercial districts the following restrictions shall apply:
1.
There shall be setbacks along all street frontage of not less than 20 feet from the sidewalk.
2.
No side or rear yards shall be required unless the property adjoins a residential district, in which case the side or rear yard shall have a depth of not less than 20 feet.
(c)
Side and rear yards not along street frontage may have a reduction in depth to not less than eight feet if the total square feet of setback area around all building sides is equivalent to a 20-foot setback along all sides. Such reduction is subject to approval by the director, bureau of planning.
(2)
Setbacks: In multi-building developments, setbacks between buildings shall be determined by the formula set forth in section 16-28.011(5)(e)a. or a minimum of 35 feet, whichever is greater.
(3)
Site design:
(a)
All buildings with street frontage shall have a pedestrian entrance directly fronting the sidewalk.
(b)
All street frontage shall have a yard that is landscaped with grass, trees, ground cover or shrubs for a minimum of two-thirds of the yard area.
(c)
There shall be a minimum number of trees within the sidewalk and setback areas that shall equal to one tree per 25 linear feet of property line that is adjacent to setback areas. Such trees shall have a minimum of 36 square feet of planting area and shall have a minimum mature height of 60 feet, as approved by the city arborist.
(d)
Ground floor uses with street frontage shall only be retail, office, institutional, or residential and entrances shall be directly accessible, visible and adjacent to the public sidewalk. Said uses shall be provided for a minimum depth of 20 feet from any building façade along the public sidewalk, unless existing topographical considerations render this requirement unreasonable.
(4)
Limitations on parking structures:
(a)
Building faces shall be no more than four stories above grade.
(b)
All street frontage on the street level shall have continuous residential uses, or accessory commercial retail uses in accordance with section 16-08.004(7), excluding driveway access to parking.
(c)
All vehicles shall be screened from view from surrounding buildings with permanent screening.
(d)
All building faces shall be screened with trees that have a minimum ten-foot height and three-inch caliper, planted a maximum of 25 feet on center.
(5)
Pedestrian routes: Sidewalks shall have a dedicated minimum 15-foot width from the existing curb and shall include street trees spaced at a maximum of 30 feet on the center.
(Code 1977, § 16-18J.004; Ord. No. 2002-32, § 11, 2-13-02)
Sec. 16-18J.005. - Special permits.
(1)
Special use permits:
(a)
Broadcasting towers and line-of-site relay devices for telephonic, radio or television communications that are 70 feet or less in height and alternative design mounting structures as contemplated by section 16-25.002(3)h(iv)(i).
(2)
Special administrative permits:
(a)
Broadcasting towers and line-of-site relay devices for telephonic, radio or television communications that are 70 feet or less in height and alternative design mounting structures as contemplated by section 16-25.002(3)h(iv)(i).
(Ord. No. 1997-06, § 5, 2-10-97)
Sec. 16-18J.006. - Districts exempt from SPI-10 district regulations.
These SPI-10 district regulations shall not apply to planned development districts created prior to the effective date of this chapter which include PD-H-5 (Planned-Development-Housing-Sector 5), PD-H-6 (Planned-Development-Housing-Sector 6), and PD-MU-6 (Planned-Development-Mixed Use-Sector 6).
(Code 1977, § 16-18J.006)
Sec. 16-18J.007. - Permitted principal uses and structures.
Digital industry switchboards, power generators and other relay equipment and rooms housing such equipment when located on subterranean levels or the second floor above sidewalk level and higher, or on ground floors provided that retail, office, institutional, or residential uses are provided for a minimum depth of 20 feet from any building façade along the public sidewalk and entrances to such uses are directly accessible, visible and adjacent to the public sidewalk.
(Ord. No. 2002-32, § 10, 2-13-02)
