Sec. 106-266. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Assault weapon.
(1)
Assault weapon means and includes:
a.
Any semiautomatic action, center fire rifle or carbine which accepts a detachable magazine with a capacity of 20 rounds or more, including but not limited to the following firearms or their copies: AR 15 semiautomatic assault carbines, Ingram Mac-10 semiautomatic assault carbines, Ingram Mac-11 semiautomatic assault carbines, Heckler and Koch 93 semiautomatic assault rifles, Heckler and Koch 91 semiautomatic assault rifles, AK-47 semiautomatic assault rifles, AKM-47 semiautomatic assault rifles, all Avatomate Kalashnikov weapons, M1-A semiautomatic assault rifles, Thompson semiautomatic carbines and any other semiautomatic carbines manufactured.
b.
Any shotgun with a barrel of less than 18 inches and a folding stock or magazine capacity of more than six rounds.
c.
Any weapons which may be readily restored to an operable assault weapon, as defined in this section.
d.
Any part, or combination of parts, designed or intended to convert a weapon into an assault weapon, as defined in this section or any combination of parts from which an assault weapon may be readily assembled, if those parts are in the possession or under the control of the same person.
(2)
Assault weapon does not include any of the following:
a.
Any of the generally and specifically described weapons in this section which is a machine gun.
b.
Any pistol, revolver or other firearm which is capable of being concealed upon one's person.
c.
Any of the following: weapons which do not use fixed ammunition, weapons which were manufactured prior to 1898, manually operated bolt action weapons, lever action weapons, slide action weapons, single-shot weapons, multiple-barrel weapons, revolving cylinder weapons, semiautomatic weapons which use exclusively Mannlicher-style clips, semiautomatic weapons manufactured prior to 1954, rim-fire weapons that employ a tubular magazine.
d.
Any assault weapon which is an antique or relic firearm.
e.
Any assault weapon which has been modified either to render it permanently inoperable or to permanently make it a device no longer defined as an assault weapon.
Dealer means any person regularly engaged in the business of selling firearms as defined in this section.
Firearms means any pistol, revolver, derringer or assault weapon, by whatever name known, which is designed to expel a projectile by the action of an explosive, and a firearm muffler or a firearm silencer, or any part or parts of such weapon.
Identification means any driver's license, birth certificate, U.S. military identification card, baptismal or christening certificate, employee's identification card provided same shows the age and home address of employee and U.S. passport.
Semiautomatic means a weapon which fires a single projectile for each single pull of the trigger and which employs a magazine.
(Code 1977, § 17-4021)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 106-267. - Identification of purchaser.
It shall be unlawful for any dealer in firearms, as defined in this division, or for any employee, servant or agent of such dealer, to sell, transfer, deliver or dispose of, in any way, any firearms unless the person obtaining the firearm shall furnish identification to the dealer setting forth the person's name, present address and age. Provided further, that no dealer in firearms, nor any employee, servant or agent of such dealer, shall sell, transfer, deliver or dispose of, in any way, an assault weapon, a pistol, revolver or short-barrelled firearm, the barrel of which is less than 15 inches in length, to any person within the city unless there shall first have been compliance with each of the provisions of this section. For the purpose of this section, a dealer in firearms shall include any person engaged in the business of operating a pawnshop, or other place where money is advanced on goods or other effects, or merchandise of any kind is taken in pawn.
(Code 1977, § 17-4022)
Sec. 106-268. - Certificate of approval.
(a)
Generally. No dealer in firearms, or any employee, servant or agent of such dealer, shall sell, transfer, deliver or dispose of in any way an assault weapon, a pistol, revolver or short-barrelled firearm less than 15 inches in length as provided in this section, unless the prospective purchaser shall have first obtained a certificate of approval from the police chief and submitted such to the dealer.
(b)
Application. The applicant shall file with the department of police an application furnished by the department stating the applicant's name, any and all names by which the applicant may have previously been known; sex; race; age; height; color of hair; color of eyes; present address, any and all addresses for the applicant within the past five years; whether the applicant has been convicted of any crime of violence within the past five years; whether the applicant has been convicted of any felony related to firearms, sex, alcohol or drug offenses within the past five years; whether the applicant has been found guilty of the violation of any law governing the use of firearms within the past five years; whether the applicant is presently a fugitive from justice from any police jurisdiction within the United States; whether the applicant is presently suffering from any mental illness, whether the applicant has been treated for any mental illness within the past ten years, and if so, the location of the hospital or mental institution providing such treatment; and whether the applicant presently has a valid permit issued pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 16-11-129, and, if so, the date of issuance and the county of issuance of such permit. The application shall also provide the name and address of the dealer from whom the purchaser will purchase the firearm and the date such application is completed by the applicant.
(c)
Fingerprints; background history. The applicant shall provide a full set of fingerprints as may be required by the department of police; furthermore, the applicant shall authorize the bureau to conduct a background history of any criminal record which the applicant may have related to the applicant's qualifications to purchase a firearm pursuant to this section. The applicant shall further authorize the department of police to obtain such hospital or mental institution records as may be required to determine the applicant's qualifications for purposes of this section.
(d)
Investigation. After receipt of the application, the police chief or designee shall conduct an investigation of the applicant, including a search of the applicant's fingerprint records, and hospital or mental institution records, if any.
(e)
Fee. The applicant shall pay to the department of police a fee of $5.00 for processing of the application, and such fee shall be paid at the time the application is delivered to the department of police.
(f)
Approval or disapproval. Within 15 days for a state resident of not less than one year and 60 days for a nonresident from the delivery of the application provided in this section to the department of police, the chief of police or designee shall notify the dealer, indicated on the subject application in writing, of the approval or disapproval of the applicant; provided, however, no dealer shall sell, transfer, deliver or dispose of, in any way, any firearms, as provided in this section, until and unless such dealer is notified of the approval by the chief of police or designee. Such approval shall remain in effect for a period of 90 days after the effective date thereof; and if approved, such approval shall only permit the applicant to obtain a firearm or firearms from the dealer whose name shall appear upon the application. If disapproved, the dealer shall not sell, transfer, deliver or dispose of, in any way, any firearm to such applicant; and the applicant shall not make any further application as provided in this until the expiration of six months after the effective date of such disapproval.
(g)
Grounds for disapproval. The chief of police or designee shall disapprove the disposition if the chief of police or designee shall determine that any of the following conditions apply to the applicant:
(1)
Such applicant has been convicted of a crime of violence within the past five years. For the purposes of this subsection, a crime of violence shall mean murder, manslaughter, rape, mayhem, assault, aggravated assault, assault with intent to murder, assault with intent to rape, assault with intent to rob, simple assault, simple battery, aggravated battery, robbery, burglary, kidnapping or the crime of attempting to commit any of the crimes set out in this subsection.
(2)
Such applicant has been convicted of any felony related to firearms, sex, alcohol or drug offenses within the past five years.
(3)
Such applicant has been found guilty of the violation of any law governing the use of firearms within the past five years.
(4)
Such applicant is presently a fugitive from justice from any police jurisdiction within the United States.
(5)
Such applicant is presently suffering from any mental illness.
(6)
Such applicant fails or refuses to complete the application or fails or refuses to complete any part of the application process.
(7)
Such applicant is under 21 years of age at the time the application is submitted to the department of police.
(h)
Pistol permit. The issuance of a pistol permit to the applicant under the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 16-11-129 shall be prima facie evidence of good cause for the approval of the application by the chief of police.
(i)
Change of dealer. If the application is approved as provided in this section and the applicant elects to obtain a firearm from a dealer other than the dealer whose name appears upon the application, the applicant shall notify the department of police of the name and the address of such dealer and the bureau shall promptly notify such dealer, in writing, of the applicant's eligibility to obtain a firearm as provided in this section.
(j)
Revocation. If at any time during the period for which the applicant retains eligibility to obtain a firearm, the chief of police or designee shall learn or have brought to such person's attention in any manner any reasonable ground to believe the applicant is not entitled to retain eligibility to obtain a firearm, the chief of police or designee may revoke the applicant's eligibility and shall promptly notify the dealer whose name appears upon the application of such revocation. The chief of police or designee shall further confiscate from the applicant any firearm obtained in contravention of the provisions of this section.
(k)
Appeal. If an applicant's eligibility is revoked, or if the applicant's application is disapproved, both as provided in this section, the chief of police or designee shall promptly notify the applicant of such disapproval or revocation and shall further notify the applicant that the applicant shall have the right to appeal the disapproval or revocation to the license review board. After a hearing, the license review board shall thereupon report its findings and recommendations to the mayor that the action of the chief of police or designee be either affirmed or overruled. The mayor, upon receiving the recommendation of the license review board, may, within 60 calendar days of receipt of said recommendation, deny or grant an application for a new certificate of approval and may revoke any existing certificate of approval. In addition, within said 60-day period, if the mayor determines there is a need to correct a clear error or prevent a manifest injustice, the mayor may remand the application or matter back to the license review board for further hearing. If the mayor fails to take action within the 60-day period, the recommendation of the license review board shall become the final decision of the mayor.
(l)
Exceptions. This section shall not be construed to apply to toy pistols, toy revolvers or toy weapons of any kind, or to antique weapons which are used only for display. This section shall further not apply to casual sales between bona fide gun collectors; nor shall this section apply to any marshal, deputy marshal, sheriff, prison or jail warden, deputy prison or jail warden, correctional officer, police officer or other authorized law officer. No person regularly engaged in the manufacture, repair or sale of firearms shall be required to make application to obtain firearms as provided by this section. It shall be unlawful for any person to knowingly provide false information on an application to obtain firearms as provided in this section.
(m)
Violation. Any person who shall violate any of the provisions of this section shall be subject to punishment as provided in section 1-8
(Code 1977, § 17-4022(a)—(m); Ord. No. 2008-79(08-O-1677), § 23, 11-6-08)
Sec. 106-269. - Permanent records to be maintained.
Each dealer within the city shall maintain complete and adequate records reflecting the disposition, at wholesale or retail, of all firearms as defined in this division physically or constructively disposed of in the course of such dealer's business. Entries of such records shall be posted at the time of each transaction. The records required by this section shall be in permanently form and shall be retained on the business premises of the dealer for a period of not less than ten years from the date the transaction occurs or until discontinuance of business by the dealer. Where there is a discontinuance of business by the dealer, all records of sales of firearms shall be delivered to the department of police within 90 days of the discontinuance. Where there is a transfer of the business of a dealer to a new owner, the records will reflect those facts and will be delivered to the successor dealer. The records will show and include:
(1)
A full and adequate description of each firearm, including:
a.
The manufacturer thereof;
b.
The manufacturer's serial number stamped thereon;
c.
The caliber of the firearm; and
d.
The model and type of firearm;
(2)
The disposition made of each firearm, including the name, address and age of the person to whom sold and the date of disposition;
(3)
The evidence of identification furnished to the dealer by the purchaser; and
(4)
A statement signed by purchaser setting forth:
a.
The purchaser's age;
b.
That the purchaser is not a fugitive from justice;
c.
That the purchaser is not under indictment or has not been convicted of a crime punishable by imprisonment for a term exceeding one year by or in any court; and
d.
That purchase is made for the sole use of purchaser or members of the purchaser's immediate family.
(Code 1977, § 17-4023)
Sec. 106-270. - Records subject to inspection by bureau of police services.
All records required to be kept under the provisions of section 106-269 and all firearms held for resale by any dealer shall be subject to inspection by officers and agents of the department of police during regular business hours or, if regular business hours are not maintained, upon demand during the daytime.
(Code 1977, § 17-4025)
Sec. 106-271. - Fingerprint record to be maintained.
(a)
In addition to the other records and information called for in this division, each dealer shall obtain from each person purchasing any firearm the fingerprint of the right hand index finger, unless that finger is missing, in which event the print of the next finger in existence on the right hand of the person purchasing the firearm shall be obtained, with a notation as to the exact finger printed. If the purchaser has no right hand, the dealer shall require the fingerprint of the left hand index finger, unless that finger is missing, in which event the print of the next finger in existence on the left hand of the person purchasing the firearm shall be obtained, with a notation as to the exact finger printed. Fingerprints and the information as required in this section shall be obtained from all persons each time that person purchases any firearm regardless of whether or not that person may have previously purchased a firearm from the dealer and been fingerprinted.
(b)
Failure to fingerprint the person purchasing a firearm shall constitute an offense under this Code.
(Code 1977, § 17-4024)
Sec. 106-272. - Compliance by dealer and purchaser.
Any failure to comply with the terms and provisions of this division by any dealer, or the furnishing of any false identification or statement by any purchaser under this division, shall constitute an offense by the dealer or purchaser under this Code.
(Code 1977, § 17-4026)
Sec. 106-273. - Sale, distribution of replica firearms prohibited.
(a)
Except as provided in this division, no person shall market for sale, distribute or sell a replica or facsimile of a firearm which is a toy or imitation firearm which substantially duplicates an actual pistol or revolver.
(b)
As used in this section, a "replica or facsimile of a firearm" shall include any device or object made of plastic, wood or metal, or any other material, which can reasonably be perceived as an actual firearm. Such term does not include nonfiring replica of an antique firearm, nor shall it include starter pistols used in athletic events.
(Code 1977, § 17-4027)

DIVISION 3. - POSSESSION AND USE

Sec. 106-301. - Discharging firearms.
Shooting any firearms in the city, unless in a licensed shooting gallery or by permission of the council on military parades, is prohibited.
(Code 1977, § 17-4005)
Sec. 106-302. - Firing of cannons, artillery.
It shall be unlawful for any person within the city to fire a cannon or other piece of artillery without special permission of the council.
(Code 1977, § 17-4006)
Sec. 106-303. - Air guns, slingshots, similar weapons.
It shall be unlawful for any person to shoot any air gun, air pistol, slingshot or like instrument or weapon within the limits of the city.
(Code 1977, § 17-4031)
Sec. 106-304. - Arrows.
It shall be unlawful for any person, within the corporate limits of the city, to discharge any arrow of any kind or character from a bow; provided, however, nothing in this section shall be construed to prohibit the use of bows and arrows indoors on any established archery range under the personal and direct supervision of a person at least 18 years of age.
(Code 1977, § 17-4032)
Sec. 106-305. - Sale or display in show windows visible from streets.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or offer for sale any kind of metal knucks, dirks, sword-in-canes, spears, Bowie knives, switchblade knives or any blackjack or similar weapons. Any person convicted of violating the terms of this section shall not only be subject to the penalties provided in section 1-8, but shall likewise have any license issued by the city revoked, in the discretion of the public safety committee of the council.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to expose for sale by displaying in any open show window visible from the street any pistol, revolver, automatic, derringer, metal knucks, dirk, sword-in-cane, spear, Bowie knife, switchblade knife or any blackjack or other similar weapon. Violations of this section shall be considered as an offense under this Code and punishable as such.
(Code 1977, § 17-4033)
Sec. 106-306. - Knives, razors, similar weapons.
It shall be unlawful for any person to have in such person's possession, except within such person's own residence or place of business, any of the following implements unless securely wrapped so as not to be readily available for use:
(1)
Any knife which has a blade which opens automatically by hand pressure applied to a button, spring or other device in the handle, the blade of which when open projects from the handle more than two inches;
(2)
Any knife which has any obstruction or other device of any kind which holds the blade partly open, the blade of which when open projects from the handle more than two inches;
(3)
Any knife of any kind, the blade of which when open projects from the handle more than three inches; this is intended to include all knives such as butcher knives or other knives which do not close;
(4)
Any razor; or
(5)
Any ice pick or similar device having a sharp point.
(Code 1977, § 17-4034)
Sec. 106-307. - City employees prohibited from carrying weapons.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any employee of the city to have on such employee's person, either concealed or unconcealed, or in such employee's immediate possession, or to bring onto any premises of the city any of the following implements:
(1)
Any firearms, guns, pistols or other instruments of like kind or similar character;
(2)
Any sword or dirk, spear or sword-in-a-cane;
(3)
Any knife which has a blade which opens automatically by hand pressure applied to a button, spring or other device in the handle, the blade of which when open projects from the handle more than two inches;
(4)
Any knife which has any obstruction or other device of any kind which holds the blade partly open, the blade of which when open projects from the handle more than two inches;
(5)
Any knife of any kind, the blade of which when open projects from the handle more than three inches; this is intended to include all knives such as butcher knives or other knives which do not close;
(6)
Any straight razor;
(7)
Any ice pick or similar device having a sharp point;
(8)
Any knucks;
(9)
Any explosives;
(10)
Any article or combination of articles likely to be made into so as to produce an explosive, including but not limited to gas, fire or water;
(11)
Slingshots, bows and arrows, cannon, artillery or fireworks;
(12)
Gas bombs, fire bombs, water bombs or similar type bomb or like in kind likely to produce harm either to the person or property of the city; or
(13)
Any or all other instruments, implements, objects or devices that would or could produce harm or injury to any person or property of the city or of another.
(b)
The following employees or special employees of the city are authorized to carry pistols or other weapons or equipment when such is lawfully issued to them:
(1)
Police.
(2)
Park police.
(3)
Airport police.
(4)
Security guards employed by the city.
(5)
Court bailiffs.
(6)
Probation officers.
(7)
Specially deputized citizens or employees of the city.
(8)
Personnel employed by the office of corrections.
(c)
Any violation of this section or any subsection thereof shall be considered an offense and any person convicted of violating the terms of this section or any subsection thereof shall be subject to the penalties provided in section 1-8
(Code 1977, § 17-4035; Ord. No. 2006-69, § 1, 10-11-06)

DIVISION 4. - ALIENS

Sec. 106-326. - Registration required by aliens possessing firearms.
Every foreign-born alien living within or coming into the city, having or gaining possession of any firearms, such as pistols, rifles, shotguns, machine guns or any other such instruments of any kind and character, shall register same with the police chief, giving such information as may be required by the mayor or those working under the mayor's orders. Aliens coming into the city and bearing or possessing any of the firearms described in this section shall register same within 24 hours after arrival within the city.
(Code 1977, § 17-4011)
Sec. 106-327. - Disposition; permit required.
After any firearm, as is described in section 98-281, has been registered with the director, no person shall dispose of same without a permit from the police chief.
(Code 1977, § 17-4012)
Sec. 106-328. - Application for permit to dispose of firearm.
The application for the permit required by section 98-282 shall give such information as may be required by the mayor or designee.
(Code 1977, § 17-4013)
Sec. 106-329. - Notice of purchase, possession to be given to mayor or designee.
No person shall purchase or come into possession of firearms from any alien without notifying the mayor or designee of such person's intention a reasonable time in advance and securing permission from such officer to obtain possession of same.
(Code 1977, § 17-4014)
Sec. 106-330. - Enforcement of division.
The mayor or designee is authorized to set up any reasonable rules and regulations for the enforcement of the provisions of this division respecting possession and disposition of firearms by aliens.
(Code 1977, § 17-4015)

DIVISION 5. - PISTOLS

Sec. 106-351. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Pistol means any small firearm fired by hand, capable of being concealed upon the person or being strapped around some portion of the body. The term shall include all small firearms having one or more barrels, such as revolvers, automatics, derringers and the like, discharging ball or similar ammunition and usually called pistols.
(Code 1977, § 17-4001)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 106-352. - Dealer's license—Required.
No license shall be issued to dealers in pistols, wholesale or retail, until the party applying for such license shall have received a special permit from the council to deal in pistols.
(Code 1977, § 17-4002)
State law reference— Pistol dealers, O.C.G.A. § 45-16-1 et seq.
Sec. 106-353. - Same—Application.
Any person desiring a license to deal in pistols under the provisions of section 106-352 shall first apply to the council, setting out such person's name, address and the business (other than dealing in pistols) intended to be carried on, and stating a request for a permit to deal in pistols at such place.
(Code 1977, § 17-4003)
Sec. 106-354. - Same—Approval, disapproval of application.
The council, after considering the application, under the provisions of section 106-353, in any manner that may appear to them proper, may approve or disapprove such application. If disapproved, then no license shall be issued such applicant for the sale of pistols. If approved, and on payment of the license fee provided in chapter 30, the municipal clerk is authorized to issue a license to such person to sell pistols at the place named.
(Code 1977, § 17-4004)

Sec. 106-377. - Sale or display prohibited.
Except as specified in section 106-377, no person, including wholesale and retail gun dealers, shall sell, offer or display for sale, any assault weapon.
(Code 1977, § 17-4036(d))
Sec. 106-378. - Exceptions.
(a)
Law enforcement. Nothing in this division shall prohibit the acquisition, possession or disposition by police departments, sheriff's offices, marshal's offices, other local, state and federal law enforcement agencies, or the military and naval forces of this state or of the United States for use in the discharge of their official duties, nor shall anything in this section prohibit the possession of assault weapons or magazines therefor by regular, salaried full-time officers, employees or agents thereof when on duty and the use of assault weapons is within the scope of their duties.
(b)
Weapons prohibited by state law. The provisions of this division shall not apply to the possession of any assault weapon when such possession is prohibited by the provisions of any controlling provision of state law.
(Code 1977, § 17-4036(e), (f))
