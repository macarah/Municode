ARTICLE IV. - OFFENSES AGAINST PUBLIC MORALS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 106-126. - House of ill fame.
(a)
A person, having or exercising control over the use of any place or conveyance within the city which would offer seclusion or shelter for the practice of prostitution, commits the offense of keeping a place of prostitution when such person knowingly grants or permits the use of such place for the purpose of prostitution.
(b)
Those places or conveyances which have been adjudged to be places of prostitution as provided in this Code may be abated as also provided in this Code.
(Code 1977, § 17-2001)
State law reference— Keeping a place of prostitution, O.C.G.A. § 16-6-10.
Sec. 106-127. - Solicitation for an illicit sexual act.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person, while in any place open to the public and whether as a pedestrian or in a motor vehicle, to offer or consent to perform any illicit sexual act for money. Furthermore, it shall be unlawful for any person, while in any place open to the public and whether as a pedestrian or in a motor vehicle, to attempt to induce, entice, solicit, pander, purchase or procure another to commit any illicit sexual act for money. An illicit sexual act is defined as one or more of the following:
(1)
Prostitution;
(2)
Sodomy for hire; or
(3)
Masturbation for hire.
Any person in violation of this Code section shall be guilty of the offense of idling and loitering for the purpose of committing an illicit sexual act.
(b)
For the purpose of subsection (a) any person shall include solicitors of illicit sexual acts or panderers, who solicit, procure or purchase an other to perform any illicit sexual act in exchange for money, and who are commonly referred to as "johns" or "tricks".
(c)
The following penalties shall be imposed upon the conviction of the offense of idling and loitering for the purpose of committing an illicit sexual act.
(1)
First conviction. Punishment as provided in section 1-8
(2)
Second conviction. Sixty days incarceration in the city jail or stockade without a reduction in time.
(3)
Third and subsequent convictions. Six months in the city jail or stockade without a reduction in time.
(d)
The following penalties shall be imposed upon the conviction of subsection (a), (b) or (c) of this section.
(1)
First conviction. A mandatory minimum term of imprisonment of 15 days in the city jail and a minimum mandatory fine of $250.00.
(2)
Second conviction.
a.
A mandatory term of 180 days imprisonment with a minimum of 60 days of said sentence of imprisonment to be served in confinement in the city jail. The remaining 120 days may be served on probation as prescribed in subsection 2(b);
b.
The sentencing court may, in its discretion, require any person convicted of a second violation of subsection (a), (b) or (c) of this Code section, as a special condition of probation, to leave the corporate limits of the city and not return during the probationary period;
c.
Any person convicted of a second violation of subsection (a), (b) or (c) of this Code section shall also pay a fine of $1,000.00.
(3)
Third and subsequent convictions. A mandatory term of six months of imprisonment in the city jail.
(4)
None of the mandatory sentences referred to in this Code section shall be suspended, probated, deferred or withheld, except as provided herein.
(5)
Nothing in this Code section shall affect the sentencing court's authority to impose any other condition of probation as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 42-8-5.
(6)
The city solicitor may move the sentencing court to impose a reduced or suspended sentence upon any person who is convicted of a violation of this Code section and provides substantial assistance in the identification, arrest, or conviction of any of his accomplices, accessories, co-conspirators, or principals. Upon good cause shown, the motion may be filed and heard in camera. The judge hearing the motion may impose a reduced or suspended sentence if he finds that the defendant has rendered such substantial assistance.
(7)
Diversion program
a.
Upon a verdict or plea of guilty or a plea of nolo contendre, but before an adjudication of guilt, the sentencing court may, in its discretion, without entering a judgment of guilt and with the consent of the defendant, defer further proceeding and enroll the defendant in an educational program administered by the City Solicitor's Office in accordance with Official Code of Georgia Annotated 15-18-80, and designed to address issues related to idling and loitering for the purpose of committing an illegal sexual act and/or prostitution.
b.
Upon fulfillment of the terms of the educational program, including a showing that the defendant has not violated this Code Section for a period of one year, the defendant shall be discharged without court adjudication of guilt.
c.
The City Solicitor's Office is authorized to assess and collect from each defendant who enters the program a fee as set forth in O.C.G.A. 15-18-80 for the administration of the program. Any such fee collected shall be deposited into the City of Atlanta General Fund.
(8)
With the exception of those fees collected in accordance with 106-127(d)(7), all fines paid to and collected by the city's courts pursuant to conviction under this section shall be deposited to the Inmate Welfare Program Trust Fund, 3PO2494008121CL9999, to defray the cost of inmate programs and services in the department of corrections.
(Code 1977, § 17-2004; Ord. No. 1996-76, 11-12-96; Ord. No. 1998-76, § 1, 10-22-98; Ord. No. 1998-89, § 1, 12-14-98; Ord. No. 2005-03, § 13, 1-10-05; Ord. No. 2008-81(08-O-1485), §§ 1, 2, 11-6-08)
State law reference— Keeping a place of prostitution, O.C.G.A. § 16-6-10.
Sec. 106-128. - Criminal impersonation.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Intent to defraud means the use of deception with the intention to injure another's interest which has economic value.
(b)
Intent. A person is guilty of criminal impersonation if such person:
(1)
Assumes a false identity and does not act in such person's assumed character with the intent to defraud another; or
(2)
Pretends to be a representative of some person or organization and does an act in such person's pretended capacity with the intent to defraud another.
(Code 1977, § 17-2002)
Sec. 106-129. - Indecency.
It shall be unlawful for any person to perform any of the following acts in a public place:
(1)
An act, or simulated act, of sexual intercourse;
(2)
An exposure of one's genitals, or of one's breasts, if female; or
(3)
The touching, caressing or fondling of the genitals, or the breast of a female.
(Code 1977, § 17-2003)
State law reference— Public indecency, O.C.G.A. § 16-6-8.
Sec. 106-130. - Defecating or urinating on public property or in areas.
It shall be unlawful for any person to defecate or urinate on the streets or sidewalks, or in the halls or elevators of public or commercial buildings, or on any property open to public view in the city.
(Code 1977, § 17-3011)
State law reference— Public indecency, O.C.G.A. § 16-6-8.
Sec. 106-131. - Spitting.
It shall be unlawful for any person to spit upon the sidewalks, or upon the floors of places of worship, buses, public halls, theaters or other public places.
(Code 1977, § 17-3012)
Sec. 106-132. - Hazing.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Haze means to subject a student to an activity which endangers the physical health of a student regardless of a student's willingness to participate in such activity.
School means any school, college or university in the city.
School organization means any club, society, fraternity, sorority or a group living together which has students as its principal members.
Student means any person enrolled in a school in the city.
(b)
Prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any person to haze any student in connection with or as a condition or precondition of gaining acceptance, membership, office or other status in a school organization.
(c)
Violation. Any person who violates this section shall be guilty and penalized as provided in section 1-8
(Code 1977, § 17-3021)
State law reference— Hazing, O.C.G.A. § 16-5-61.

DIVISION 2. - GAMBLING AND RELATED OFFENSES

Sec. 106-151. - Playing or betting at game of chance in public place.
It shall be unlawful for any person to play or bet in any street or public place at any game or pretended game of chance.
(Code 1977, § 17-5003)
State law reference— Gambling, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-21.
Sec. 106-152. - Maintenance of place for betting on races.
It shall be unlawful for any person to maintain or carry on any office or place of business, or to have a space in, or portion of, the office, store or place of business of another, or to maintain a place or point of meeting, in which persons are allowed to bet or offer to bet, or place an order for a bet, or telegraph or take a bet, on horse races, boat races, bicycle races or any other kind or description of race, whether such race is to be run in the city or at any place outside the city.
(Code 1977, § 17-5004)
State law reference— Keeping a gambling place, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-23.
Sec. 106-153. - Pinball and other coin-operated machines.
(a)
Prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any person to own, maintain or operate any pinball machine except as provided in this section.
(b)
Applicability. Nothing contained in this section shall prohibit the operation of any pinball machine or other machine for the playing of a game or engaging in a contest of skill or for recreational purposes, and only which may, by application of skill, entitle the player to replay the game at no additional cost, if the machine can react to no more than 15 free replays or can be discharged of accumulated free replays only by reactivating the machine for one additional play for each accumulated free replay, nor shall anything contained in this section prohibit the operation of any machine used for the purpose of vending merchandise. Such machines, as provided in this section, may be operated whether operated by depositing a coin therein or otherwise.
(c)
Slot machines. It shall be unlawful for any person to have on hand for the purpose of offering to the public for service or use or pay any slot machine or similar device involving any element of chance, either singly or in connection with purchases.
(Code 1977, § 17-5001)
State law reference— Possession of gambling device, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-24; permitted amusement devices, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35(b).
Sec. 106-154. - Use of wheel or other device.
It shall be unlawful for any person conducting or carrying on any business in the city to use or operate in connection therewith, for the purpose of attracting trade or for any other purpose, any wheel or other device or contrivance in which the element of chance is involved.
(Code 1977, § 17-5002)
State law reference— Possession of gambling device, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-24.
Sec. 106-155. - Turf exchanges and pool selling.
No turf exchange license shall be issued, and no permit shall be granted, for the sale of pools of any kind. Any person running a turf exchange or selling pools of any kind shall be guilty of an offense, and on conviction thereof, shall be punished as provided in section 1-8.
(Code 1977, § 17-5005)
Sec. 106-156. - Buying or selling pools or making or taking books on horse races.
It shall be unlawful for any person to buy or sell a pool, or any interest or share in such pool, or to make or to take a book, upon the running, pacing or trotting, or what purports to be the running, pacing or trotting, either within or without the city, of any mare, horse or gelding, or upon any horse race, or upon what purports to be any horse race.
(Code 1977, § 17-5006)
State law reference— Gambling, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-21; commercial gambling, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-22.
Sec. 106-157. - Giving or taking money to bet on horse races.
It shall be unlawful for any person to give to, or take or receive from, another person any money or other thing of value to be bet or hazarded, or to be used in buying any pool, or any interest in any pool, upon the event of any horse race, either within or without the city.
(Code 1977, § 17-5007)
State law reference— Gambling, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-21; commercial gambling, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-22.
Sec. 106-158. - Bookmaking, pool selling, loitering to give or receive racing news, to bet.
It shall be unlawful for any person to carry on or conduct the practices commonly known as bookmaking or pool selling, or either of them, or to loiter or assemble on any of the streets or within any place within the city, to give or receive racing news, or to give or receive money on any horse race, or to buy or to sell any pool or interest in any pool on any horse race within or without the city.
(Code 1977, § 17-5008)
State law reference— Gambling, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-21; commercial gambling, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-22; communicating gambling information, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-28.
Sec. 106-159. - Operation of tickers.
It shall be unlawful to operate tickers or other instruments recording the results of any horse race or football game in any public place or building where any kind of business is operated, where same is used for the purpose of inducing or gaining trade.
(Code 1977, § 17-5009)
State law reference— Communicating gambling information, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-28.
Sec. 106-160. - Conducting numbers game.
(a)
Based on stock, bond sales. It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or handle, or be concerned in the sale or the handling, or to have on hand for the purpose of sale or handling, tickets or forms, under which the purchaser thereof is entitled to receive more than such person pays for same, if the numbers shown on such tickets or forms appear in the numbers or in some of the numbers of the total bond sales or total stock sales, as published in one or more of the newspapers of the city.
(b)
Based on other means. It shall be unlawful to furnish, sell or to be concerned in the furnishing or sale, or to have on hand or be concerned in the having on hand for the purpose of furnishing or selling, tickets or forms under which the purchaser thereof is entitled to receive more than such person pays for same, if the numbers or other symbols shown thereon, by reference to some other matter, publication or by any other means are determined to be the lucky numbers.
(c)
Applicability of section. Nothing contained in this section shall be construed as prohibiting question contests, spelling bees or any other contests, the outcome of which is determined by the skill or ability of the contestant rather than by uncontrolled and uncontrollable chance; and provided further that such contests shall not be conducted in any manner which would be in violation of the laws of the state or ordinances of the city relating to lotteries.
(Code 1977, § 17-5010)
State law reference— Commercial gambling, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-22.
Sec. 106-161. - Possession of tickets of numbers game.
It shall be unlawful for any person to hold or have in such person's possession any ticket or form under which the purchaser thereof is entitled to more than such person pays for same, if the number thereon, by reference to any publication, or by any other means, is determined to be the lucky number.
(Code 1977, § 17-5011)
State law reference— Possession of gambling device, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-24.
Sec. 106-162. - Purchase or possession of lottery tickets.
It shall be unlawful to wilfully purchase or possess any ticket or other device representing a chance in a lottery. The possession of a ticket or other device representing a chance in a lottery shall be prima facie evidence of guilt, but such presumption shall be rebuttable.
(Code 1977, § 17-5012)
State law reference— Possession of gambling device, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-24; legally authorized lotteries, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-35.
Sec. 106-163. - Seizure of properties used in connection with lottery or gambling house.
Any money, property or paraphernalia of any kind seized by officers of the law which is used, or has been used, in connection with the operation of a lottery or gambling house shall be held by the officer seizing such property for not exceeding 15 days for the purpose of checking the purchase, ownership and registration thereof.
(Code 1977, § 17-5013)
State law reference— Seizure of property used in gambling, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-32.
Sec. 106-164. - Vehicles used in connection with lottery or gambling house.
(a)
Seizure. If any vehicle operated by motor or muscular power used in connection with a lottery or gambling house shall be seized by any officer, such vehicle, if registered under a fictitious name or from a fictitious address, or if a fictitious bill of sale thereof has been made, or if the operator of such vehicle has given false information of any form or character in connection with its registration, shall be held by the officer seizing the same for not exceeding 15 days for the purpose of making a full and complete investigation and discovery of all persons who have aided or abetted the person operating such vehicle, and for the purpose of determining the true ownership, purchase and registration of such vehicle; and it shall be the duty of the officer seizing such vehicle to prosecute any and all persons and corporations having any connection with, or aiding and abetting the commission of a crime, where such vehicle is used in the operation of such unlawful enterprise involving the operation of a lottery or gambling house.
(b)
Unlawful sale. It shall be unlawful for any person knowingly to sell such an automobile in a fictitious name.
(c)
Obliteration of numbers. It shall be unlawful for any person to obscure or obliterate any of the numbers on the license tag of such vehicle, with intent to make it difficult to identify it.
(d)
False address. It shall be unlawful for any person to give a false address in registering or purchasing such vehicle, or to have it delivered at such a false address.
(e)
Foreign tag. It shall be unlawful for any person knowingly to use any foreign tag, or other tag than the one issued for any such vehicle.
(f)
False representation of ownership. It shall be unlawful for any person to represent himself to be the true owner of any such vehicle when in fact such person is not the true owner thereof but has allowed the use of such person's name for the purpose of concealing the true ownership.
(g)
Report of seizure. It shall be the duty of any officer of the law seizing that vehicle to report it to the director of the bureau of police services within not less than ten days from the date of such seizure.
(Code 1977, § 17-5014)
State law reference— Seizure of property used in gambling, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-32.

DIVISION 3. - DRUGS AND OTHER CONTROLLED SUBSTANCES

Sec. 106-181. - Model glue.
(a)
Sale to retail dealers. It shall be unlawful for any wholesaler or merchandiser to sell or transfer any quantity of model glue to any person whatsoever who is not or does not represent a recognized bona fide retail dealer in merchandise customarily handling such product in the ordinary course of such person's business at a fixed location.
(b)
Retail sale. It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in the retail sale of model glue unless such person is a recognized bona fide retail dealer at a fixed location in merchandise, customarily handling such product in the ordinary course of such person's business.
(c)
Sale of more than one tube. It shall be unlawful for any bona fide recognized retail dealer, as defined in subsection (b) of this section, to sell more than one tube, or other minimum-size container, of model glue to any one customer within any 24-hour period; provided, that the provisions of this subsection do not apply to the sale of model glue together with a hobby or model kit.
(d)
Keeping or displaying. It shall be unlawful for any bona fide recognized retail dealer, as defined in subsection (b) of this section, to keep, maintain or prominently display in such dealer's business establishment any stock or supply of model glue in such manner or place as to make the same accessible to customers or other members of the public, without actually receiving the same directly from the retailer, or such dealer's bona fide authorized representative.
(Code 1977, §§ 17-8001, 17-8002)
State law reference— Model glue, O.C.G.A. § 16-13-90 et seq.
Sec. 106-182. - Marijuana.
It shall be unlawful for any person to possess one ounce or less of marijuana within the corporate limits of the city. Any person found guilty of violating this section shall be subject to the penalty provided in section 1-8; provided that any defendant charged with possession of one ounce or less of marijuana shall be entitled on request to have the case against such defendant transferred to the court having general misdemeanor jurisdiction in the county wherein the alleged offense occurred.
(Code 1977, § 17-8003)
Possession of marijuana, O.C.G.A. § 16-13-30.1.
Sec. 106-183. - Aftershave lotion.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in the retail sale of any aftershave lotion unless such person is a recognized bona fide retail merchant or dealer at a fixed location in merchandise, customarily handling such product in the ordinary course of business.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any bona fide recognized retail merchant or dealer, as defined in subsection (a) of this section, to sell any aftershave lotion containing an alcoholic content of 35 percent or more by volume, to any customer, when the merchant or dealer has actual knowledge of or has reason to believe that the aftershave lotion is being purchased to be consumed for the purpose of causing a condition of intoxication, stupefaction, euphoria, excitement, exhilaration or dulling of the senses or nervous system.
(c)
Among the circumstances to be considered in determining whether the aftershave lotion is being purchased to be consumed for the purpose of intoxication are:
(1)
The merchant's or dealer's premises or the grounds or public areas immediately adjacent to those premises are habitually littered with empty containers of aftershave lotion; and
(2)
The customer appears to be in a continuous state of intoxication or has purchased more than eight ounces of such aftershave lotion within any 24-hour period.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any bona fide recognized retail merchant or dealer to maintain or display in such merchant's business establishment, any stock or supply of aftershave lotion commingled with or immediately adjacent to such merchant's stock or supply of food or beverages.
(Code 1977, § 17-8004)
Sec. 106-184. - Spray paint.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to intentionally smell or inhale spray paint containing halogenated hydrocarbons for the purpose of intoxication.
(b)
Any person guilty of violating this section shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $250.00 or imprisonment for not more than 25 days for such violation.
(Code 1977, § 17-8005)
