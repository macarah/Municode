ARTICLE V. - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGE CATERING[4]

State Law reference— Local government has authority to issue an off-premises license allowing alcoholic beverage caterer to sell malt beverages or wine by the drink off premises and in connection with an authorized catered function, O.C.G.A. § 3-11-1 et seq.

Sec. 6-391. - Off-premises alcoholic beverage catering license.
(a)
Any licensed alcoholic beverage caterer who holds a valid license from the county to sell:
(1)
At a fixed location within the county, malt beverages or wine by the drink for consumption on the premises; or
(2)
By the package for consumption off the premises;
may be issued an off-premises license which authorizes such licensed alcoholic beverage caterer to distribute malt beverages and/or wine at an authorized catered function.
(b)
Application for an off-premises license as provided herein shall be made to the county clerk upon forms to be prepared and approved by the board of commissioners. Applications will be decided according to the same rules and guidelines set forth in this chapter.
(c)
The annual fee for an alcoholic beverage caterer's license shall be $100.00 annually for each licensed location.
(Code 2000, § 32-205A(2); Ord. of 11-2-2004, § 32-205A(2))
Sec. 6-392. - Event permits.
(a)
Alcoholic beverage caterer licensed by the county. In order to distribute malt beverages and/or wine at an authorized catered function, an alcoholic beverage caterer licensed by the county shall be required to:
(1)
Apply for an event permit. The application shall include:
a.
The name of the alcoholic beverage caterer, including the name of the specific representative of the caterer who will be responsible for, and present at, the catered function;
b.
The date of the event;
c.
The address of the event;
d.
The time of the event;
e.
The licensed alcoholic beverage caterer's license number; and
f.
The name, address and phone number of the host of the catered function.
No event permit fee shall be charged.
(2)
Provide satisfactory reports to the state revenue commissioner on forms provided by that department, stating the quantity of any and all alcoholic beverages transported from the licensee's primary premises to the location of the authorized catered function and such other information as required by the commissioner.
(3)
Maintain original local event permits and documents required by the state revenue department in the vehicle transporting the alcoholic beverages to the catered function at all times.
(4)
Comply with all laws and ordinances regulating the sale and distribution of alcoholic beverages, and no permit will be issued to any alcohol beverage caterer on probation or with a suspended license.
(b)
Alcoholic beverage caterer licensed by other jurisdictions. In order to distribute malt beverages and/or wine at an authorized catered function, an alcoholic beverage caterer licensed by a subdivision of the state other than the county shall be required to:
(1)
Apply for an event permit. The application shall include:
a.
The name of the alcoholic beverage caterer, including the name of the specific representative of the caterer who will be responsible for, and present at, the catered function;
b.
The date of the event;
c.
The address of the event;
d.
The time of the event;
e.
The jurisdiction issuing the alcoholic beverage caterer's license;
f.
The licensed alcoholic beverage caterer's license number;
g.
A photocopy of the alcoholic beverage caterer's license; and
h.
The name, address and phone number of the host of the catered function.
(2)
Pay an event permit fee of $10.00;
(3)
Pay local excise taxes that will be levied on the total quantity of alcoholic beverages brought into the county by the caterer, as calculated under this article, within 15 days of the conclusion of the catered event;
(4)
Provide satisfactory reports to the state revenue commissioner on forms provided by that department, stating the quantity of any and all alcoholic beverages transported from the licensee's primary premises to the location of the authorized catered function and such other information as required by the commissioner; and
(5)
Maintain original local event permits and documents required by the revenue department in the vehicle transporting the alcoholic beverages to the catered function at all times.
(c)
Charitable organizations.
(1)
Charitable organizations, as defined in this chapter, may serve malt beverages and/or wine at authorized catered functions without an alcoholic beverage catering license, so long as the qualified charitable organizations apply for an event permit. The application shall include:
a.
The name of the charitable organization;
b.
The name of the caterer;
c.
The date of the event;
d.
The address of the event;
e.
The time of the event; and
f.
The name, address and phone number of the host of the catered function.
(2)
A charitable organization serving alcoholic beverages under this subsection is limited to 12 event permits per calendar year.
(d)
Issuance of event permits. The issuance of event permits is an administrative function of the county, and the county is permitted under this article to request additional information from the licensed alcoholic beverage caterer and/or host of the catered event in deciding whether to issue an event permit. However, no event permit will be issued:
(1)
Where the requirements set forth under subsection (a), (b) or (c) of this section have not been met;
(2)
Where the host or the alcoholic beverage caterer has violated any of the prohibitions set forth in section 6-393; or
(3)
To licensed alcoholic beverage caterers, where the total cost of alcohol at a catered event will exceed the total cost of food at an event. This subsection does not apply to qualified charitable organizations.
(Code 2000, § 32-205A(3); Ord. of 11-2-2004, § 32-205A(3))
Sec. 6-393. - Violations.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for a food caterer to distribute or sell malt beverages and/or wine off the premises of the food caterer's business without a license issued pursuant to this section. This subsection shall not affect any other provisions of this chapter which may require a food caterer who has a license to sell alcoholic beverages on the premises of the food caterer's business.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for a licensed alcoholic beverage caterer licensed under this article to distribute malt beverages and/or wine off premises except in connection with an authorized catered function within the scope of the event permit for that function.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for anyone, including licensed alcoholic beverage caterers, to distribute distilled spirits in the unincorporated areas of the county.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for a licensed alcoholic beverage caterer to distribute malt beverages and/or wine during any of the following times:
(1)
At any time on Sundays;
(2)
At any time on Christmas Day; or
(3)
At any time in violation of state law, state rules or regulations, local ordinances or regulations.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for a licensed alcoholic beverage caterer to employ any person under 21 years of age who, in the course of such employment, would dispense, serve, sell, or handle alcoholic beverages. It is the intent of this subsection to prevent any person employed by such caterer, or any other employee, to knowingly violate any prohibitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23, relating to furnishing alcoholic beverages to, and the purchase and possession of alcoholic beverages by, a person under 21 years of age.
(f)
It shall be unlawful for anyone, including, but not limited to, hosts of catered functions and licensed alcoholic beverage caterers, to charge admission to a catered function.
(g)
It shall be unlawful for anyone, including, but not limited to, hosts of catered functions and licensed alcoholic beverage caterers, to charge attendees at catered events for alcoholic beverages, directly or indirectly.
(h)
Any person violating the provisions of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, punishable by a fine of up to $1,000.00 and/or imprisonment up to one year.
(Code 2000, § 32-205A(4); Ord. of 11-2-2004, § 32-205A(4))
Sec. 6-394. - Exemptions.
(a)
Charitable organizations (as defined in section 6-2) holding authorized catered functions may charge admission to catered alcohol events and may charge attendees for alcoholic beverages, so long as the money collected is used primarily for charitable purposes and not solely to defray the costs of the alcohol provided.
(b)
Fundraisers for candidates for political office may accept contributions from attendees at authorized catered functions, so long as the contributions are primarily used for the candidates' political campaigns and not solely to defray the costs of the alcohol provided.
(c)
Nothing contained in this section is intended to prohibit anyone from hosting private functions within the county where the host provides alcohol to guests free of charge.
(Code 2000, § 32-205A(5); Ord. of 11-2-2004, § 32-205A(5))
Sec. 6-395. - Compliance with this chapter.
In addition to the requirements set forth herein, all licensed alcoholic beverage caterers performing alcoholic beverage catering services in the county are required to comply with all of the provisions of article III of this chapter that are not inconsistent with this article.
(Code 2000, § 32-205A(6); Ord. of 11-2-2004, § 32-205A(6))
Sec. 6-396. - State law.
All licensed alcoholic beverage caterers performing alcoholic beverage catering services in the county are required to comply with all state laws, including but not limited to O.C.G.A. §§ 3-4-20 et seq., 3-5-20 et seq., 3-6-20 et seq., and 3-11-1 et seq., and all regulations promulgated by the state department of revenue, including but not limited to Ga. Admin. Code § 560-2-10 et seq.
(Code 2000, § 32-205A(7); Ord. of 11-2-2004, § 32-205A(7))
