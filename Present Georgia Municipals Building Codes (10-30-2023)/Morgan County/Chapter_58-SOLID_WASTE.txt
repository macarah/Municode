ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 58-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Hazardous materials or hazardous waste means waste, in any amount, which is defined, characterized or designated as hazardous by the United States Environmental Protection Agency or appropriate state agency by or pursuant to federal or state law. The term "hazardous waste" shall include motor oil, gasoline, paint, paint cans and items containing Freon, chlorofluorocarbons or other form that may pose an unreasonable risk to health, safety, property and the environment.
Household garbage means all refuse or trash which is of a size no larger than one foot wide by two feet long and is refuse or trash of a type that is not an appliance or furniture.
Junked motor vehicle means any motor vehicle, as defined in this section, which does not have lawfully affixed thereto an unexpired license plate or plates, and the condition of which is wrecked, dismantled, partially dismantled, inoperative, abandoned or discarded.
Litter means all discarded sand, gravel, slag, brickbats, rubbish, waste material, tin cans, refuse, garbage, trash, debris, dead animals or other discarded material of every kind and description which are not waste as such term is defined in this section.
Motor vehicle means any vehicle which is self-propelled and designed to travel along the ground and shall include, but not be limited to, automobiles, buses, motorbikes, motorcycles, ATVs, trucks, tractors, go-carts, campers and trailers.
Person means any entity, person, firm, partnership, corporation or unincorporated association.
Private property means any real property within the county limits, which is privately owned and which is not public property as defined in this section.
Public property means the right-of-way of any street or highway, which shall include the entire width between the boundary lines of every way publicly maintained for the purposes of travel; any body of water or watercourse or the shores or beaches thereof; any publicly owned park, playground, building, refuge, conservation or recreation area.
Road orstreet shall be mutually inclusive and shall likewise be deemed to include any alley, lane, court, and other thoroughfare, however described or designated.
Scrap tires means all tires that are no longer suitable for their original intended purposes because of wear, damage or defect.
Tire means a hoop of iron, rubber or plastic which is generally designed for encircling the wheel of a motor vehicle and which is neither attached to the motor vehicle nor a part of the motor vehicle as original equipment.
Waste means all discarded substances and materials whatsoever exceeding ten pounds in weight or 15 cubic feet in volume, or any such substance in any weight or volume, biomedical waste, hazardous waste as defined in this section, a hazardous substance as defined by O.C.G.A. § 12-8-92, or any such substance or material dumped for commercial purposes. The term "waste" includes, without limitation: bottles; boxes; containers; papers; tobacco products; tires; appliances; mechanical equipment or parts; building or construction material; tools; machinery; wood; motor vehicles and motor vehicle parts; vessels; aircraft equipment; waste oil; batteries; antifreeze; sludge from wastewater treatment facility, water supply treatment plant, or air pollution control facility; and air contaminants from any source from any source or facility, and any other discarded material or substance of every kind and description resulting from domestic, industrial, commercial, mining, or governmental operations, including household, commercial, construction and demolition waste, or industrial solid waste which is nonhazardous, nonmedical, and in a form classified as solid waste by the state department of natural resources.
Yard trimmings means leaves, brush, grass clippings, shrub and tree prunings, discarded Christmas trees, nursery and greenhouse vegetative residuals, and vegetative matter resulting from landscaping development and maintenance other than mining, agricultural, and silvicultural operations.
(Code 2000, § 12-101(1); Ord. of 9-10-2002, § 34-101)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. §§ 12-8-22, 16-7-42.
Sec. 58-2. - Evidence of violations.
(a)
Whenever litter is thrown, deposited, dropped, or dumped from any motor vehicle, boat, airplane, or other conveyance in violation of this chapter, it shall be prima facie evidence that the operator of the conveyance has violated this chapter.
(b)
Whenever any litter which is dumped, deposited, thrown, or left on public or private property in violation of this chapter is discovered to contain any article or articles, including but not limited to letters, bills, publications, or other writings which display the name of a person thereon in such a manner as to indicate that the article belongs or belonged to such person, it shall be a rebuttable presumption that such person has violated this chapter.
(Code 2000, § 12-101(8))

ARTICLE II. - DISPOSAL AND TRANSPORTING

Sec. 58-23. - Waste disposal site.
(a)
Residents of the county may dispose of household garbage at one of the numerous waste disposal sites located throughout the county for the purpose of collecting such.
(b)
All items of refuse or trash other than household garbage must be taken to the county transfer station during the hours it is open to the public.
(c)
No item of garbage of any kind may be placed on top of, adjacent to, or near said waste disposal sites. All items of household garbage must be placed wholly inside said waste disposal sites.
(d)
Only residents of the county are permitted to make use of waste disposal sites, or dump or place garbage or debris of any kind within the county.
(e)
Any person charged with violating this section shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction thereof, shall be punished according to the penalties described in section 1-9.
(Code 2000, § 12-102)
Sec. 58-24. - Entry prohibited.
(a)
Unless so employed or authorized by the county manager, no person shall place any part of his or her person or use any tool to remove or attempt to remove any object or item from any waste disposal site in the county.
(b)
Any person charged with violating this section shall be charged a fine of at least $25.00 if convicted.
(Code 2000, § 12-103)
Sec. 58-25. - Garbage storage areas to be kept clean.
(a)
The proprietary owner, tenant, or occupant of any premises, and the proprietor and person in charge of every business, commercial, institutional, and industrial establishment, shall be responsible for the sanitary handling and disposition of garbage, litter, waste, scrap tires and refuse on the premises used, occupied or under the control of such person and shall be required to keep the driveways, yards, parking areas, loading or unloading areas and lots adjacent thereto or under the control thereof reasonably clean at all times.
(b)
Every proprietary owner, occupant, and lessee of a house, business, or building shall be jointly and severally required to maintain garbage storage areas in a clean condition and to ensure that all garbage is properly containerized.
(Code 2000, § 12-101(2)(a); Ord. of 9-10-2002, §§ 34-106, 34-108)
Sec. 58-26. - Containers required.
Every proprietary owner, occupant, tenant and person using or occupying any commercial, institutional or industrial building or property in the county shall be obligated, jointly and severally, to provide litter containers of such size, number, and type as may be specified by the county manager or his or her designee to be reasonably required to hold litter generated by operations on the premises. Specifically, and without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the requirement for such containers shall apply to shopping centers, supermarkets, convenience stores, fast food and other type restaurants, service stations and similar establishments and shall likewise apply to commercial establishments, garages, schools, and churches.
(Code 2000, § 12-101(2)(b); Ord. of 9-10-2002, § 34-107)
Sec. 58-27. - Construction sites.
Within the county, all property owners and prime contractors in charge of a construction site, jointly and severally, are required to furnish litter containers for construction litter. All litter from construction and related activities shall be containerized and shall be picked up and placed in containers at the end of each workday.
(Code 2000, § 12-101(2)(c); Ord. of 9-10-2002, § 34-110)
Sec. 58-28. - Litter and waste disposal.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to sweep, dump, deposit, place, throw, or leave or to cause or permit the sweeping, dumping, depositing, placing, throwing or leaving of household garbage, litter, waste, scrap tires, dead animals, discarded items or other debris at any place in the county, including, without limitation, any public or private property or in any waters in this county unless such litter or waste originates in this county and:
(1)
The property is designated and permitted by the state and/or by the county board of commissioners or its duly designated agent for disposal of litter and waste, and the person is authorized to use said property; and
(2)
The litter and waste is placed into a receptacle or container installed on such property; however, any litter or waste placed into such a receptacle or container installed on such property must not be allowed to accumulate for any unreasonable period of time, and in no event shall it be allowed to accumulate to the point of overflowing the container or receptacle or to a point where the container or receptacle may not be closed.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to dump, place, store, park or broadcast any waste, junk, metal, brickbats, stones, plaster, lumber, trash, garbage, litter, paper or similar rubbish or junk, unless authorized to do so by a duly issued permit, in or on any:
(1)
Public highway, road, street, alley, sidewalk, or thoroughfare, including any portion of the right-of-way thereof, or on any other public lands except in containers of areas lawfully provided for such dumpings;
(2)
Freshwater lake, river, canal, or stream or creek; or
(3)
Public or private property unless such dumping will not adversely affect the public health and is not in violation of any other local, state, or federal law, rule, or regulation.
(Code 2000, §§ 12-101(2)(d), (e); Ord. of 9-10-2002, §§ 34-102, 34-103)
Sec. 58-29. - Tire disposal.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to deposit, place or put any tire or tires within the confines of the county landfill with the intent of disposing of or abandoning the same.
(b)
It shall be lawful for persons to store a reasonable number of tires on their property. Said number shall not exceed ten tires in addition to tires attached to or located inside the trunks of vehicles.
(c)
Tires shall be disposed of at either a state recycling facility, state tire carriers, or the county transfer station.
(d)
Any person found guilty of violating any of the provisions of this section shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction thereof, shall be punished according to the penalties described in section 1-9. Each tire so disposed, and each day such violation is committed or permitted to continue shall constitute a separate offense and shall be punishable as such hereunder.
(Code 2000, §§ 12-101(13), 12-104, 31-103)
Sec. 58-30. - Packing, repacking, and loading areas to be kept clean.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep on the streets, roads, or sidewalks in the county any crates, boxes or any similar containers other than when loading, unloading, packing or unpacking merchandise.
(b)
Work areas, including, but not limited to, loading and unloading areas, shall be provided with containers for the deposit of litter created by packing or repacking operations and the owner of the premises and the occupant thereof shall, jointly and severally, maintain surveillance to ensure that all litter is placed in the proper container and the area is kept clean.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to burn without first obtaining a permit from the state forestry commission.
(d)
Nothing in this article shall be interpreted to unreasonably interfere with or make illegal any bona fide agricultural operations.
(Code 2000, §§ 12-101(2)(f), (g); Ord. of 9-10-2002, §§ 34-104, 34-109)
Sec. 58-31. - Transportation.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to transport any garbage, trash, refuse, or other cargo upon the public thoroughfares in the county if the same is of a character or substance which is likely to leak, freely flow or spill, blow or escape from the motor vehicle onto the public rights-of-way.
(b)
Any litter or waste hauled on a moving vehicle in the county shall be covered or secured by containers, tarpaulin, or other device in such fashion as to effectively prevent it from leaking, freely flowing or spilling, blowing or escaping onto the public rights-of-way.
(c)
This section shall not prohibit the necessary spreading of any substance in public road maintenance or unreasonably interfere with bona fide agricultural operations.
(d)
The duties and responsibilities herein imposed shall be applicable alike to the owner of the motor vehicle and operator thereof.
(Code 2000, § 12-101(3); Ord. of 9-10-2002, § 34-105)
Secs. 58-32—58-50. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - BOX DUMPSTERS, COMPACTORS, AND RECYCLING

Sec. 58-51. - Authorized waste; site maintenance; permitted use.
(a)
All box dumpster sites or compactor sites shall be maintained in as sanitary a manner as is reasonably possible consistent with its use for garbage and litter disposal.
(b)
Persons using box dumpster or compactor sites shall deposit all authorized garbage and refuse in the container or receptacle.
(c)
Only household garbage may be deposited in the container or receptacle.
(d)
All other refuse or trash must be taken to the county transfer station.
(e)
Only residents of the county are permitted to make use of dumpsters or dump or place garbage or debris of any kind within the county.
(Code 2000, § 12-101(4))
Sec. 58-52. - Prohibited waste; prohibited acts.
(a)
No person shall deposit any burning or smoldering material in any such container or receptacle.
(b)
No person shall set fire to the contents of any such container or receptacle.
(c)
No dead animals shall be deposited in any such container or receptacle.
(d)
No person shall deposit large, noncompactable articles in such containers or receptacles such as appliances, bedsprings, mattresses, tires, air conditioning units, construction material, tree limbs, lawn trimmings, furniture, or similar items.
(e)
No person shall deposit any flammable or explosive materials in any such container or receptacles.
(f)
No person shall place or scatter litter or waste in or around such containers or receptacles.
(g)
No one shall deposit any hazardous materials in or around such containers or sites.
(h)
No person shall enter into said receptacle or container, place any part of his or her person within such receptacle or container or use any tool to remove or attempt to remove any object or item from any such receptacle within the county.
(Code 2000, § 12-101(4))
Sec. 58-53. - Use of recycling container.
It shall be unlawful for any individual to located or place, or cause to be located or placed, a non-paper product or substance or any kind of nonpaper waste or unauthorized waste into a recycling container which is so labeled or marked.
(Code 2000, § 12-101(14))
Secs. 58-54—58-79. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - LITTER AND WASTE

Sec. 58-80. - Prohibited accumulation.
(a)
No owner or occupant of any premises in the county shall allow litter or waste to accumulate thereon. The owner, occupant and lessee of all property, jointly and severally, are required to remove all litter and place the same in proper containers. Vacant lots, borders, parking lots, embankments, fences, walls and sidewalks shall be kept free from litter. Parking lots, shopping centers, convenience stores, drive-in restaurants and all other commercial and industrial enterprises shall see to it that their respective properties are kept litter-free.
(b)
No owner or occupant of any property shall allow the accumulation on his or her premises of garbage or waste where such material creates or causes a health hazard to neighbors or other citizens, or which is unsightly or emits foul or obnoxious odors.
(Code 2000, § 12-101(5); Ord. of 9-10-2002, § 34-111)
Sec. 58-81. - Wrecked, dilapidated, and inoperative items.
It shall be unlawful for any owner or occupant of land to have on private property litter, dilapidated furniture, appliances, machinery, equipment, building materials, or other items, including but not limited to automobiles, which are either in a wholly or partially rusted, wrecked, junked, dismantled, or inoperative condition, unless such items are wholly contained in buildings that lawfully exist on the property. A motor vehicle without a valid and current tag shall be presumed to be junk.
(Ord. of 9-10-2002, § 34-112)
Sec. 58-82. - Prohibited burning.
(a)
No owner or occupant of any such property shall bury or burn litter or waste without prior authorization and permission from the applicable regulatory agency, including but not limited to the Environmental Protection Agency, environmental protection division and/or the state forestry service.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to set fire to the contents of, indiscriminately scatter or disperse the contents of, or otherwise vandalize any containers used for the storage or dumping of litter or waste.
(c)
Nothing in this section shall authorize or be construed to permit the burial or burning of any material which is otherwise prohibited by state law.
(Code 2000, § 12-101(5)(a), (c))
Sec. 58-83. - Separate violation.
The conduct described in this article shall constitute a separate violation of this article for each day the garbage or waste material remains on such premises.
(Code 2000, § 12-101(5)(d))

ARTICLE V. - ADVERTISEMENTS

Sec. 58-110. - Prohibited conduct.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to place in or on any vehicle in the county any commercially oriented handbill, circular, pamphlet, poster, postcard, or other literature except with the permission, express or implied, of the owner or occupant of said vehicle.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to place on public property within the county any handbill, circular, pamphlet, postcard, poster, or other literature except with the permission, express or implied, of the board.
(Code 2000, § 12-101(6)(a), (b))
Sec. 58-111. - Bond required.
Any person desiring to place any handbill, circular, pamphlet, poster, postcard or other literature on public property within the county shall first post a $100.00 cash bond with the clerk of the county along with obtaining permission from the board to distribute said materials. The bond shall be returned upon request, provided said literature and materials are removed within a reasonable time after the event which it promotes. This section shall not prohibit a merchant from placing such literature within his or her commercial establishment or on his or her storefront.
(Code 2000, § 12-101(6)(c))

ARTICLE VI. - YARD WASTES

Sec. 58-136. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Commercial solid waste means all types of solid waste generated by stores, offices, restaurants, warehouses and other nonmanufacturing activities, excluding residential and industrial wastes.
Composting means the controlled biological decomposition of organic matter into a stable, odor-free humus.
County solid waste means any solid waste derived from households, including garbage, trash and sanitary waste in septic tanks and includes solid waste from single-family and multifamily residences, hotels and motels, bunkhouses, campgrounds, picnic grounds, and day use recreation areas. The term includes yard trimmings and commercial solid waste but does not include solid waste from mining, agricultural or silvicultural operations or industrial processes or operations.
County solid waste disposal facility means any facility or location where the final deposition of any amount of county solid waste occurs, whether or not mixed with or including commercial or industrial solid waste, including but not limited to county solid waste landfills.
County solid waste landfill means a disposal facility where any amount of county solid waste, whether or not mixed with or including commercial waste, industrial waste, nonhazardous sludge or small quantity generator hazardous waste, is disposed of by means of placing an approved cover thereon.
Leachate collection system means a system at a landfill for collection of the leachate which may percolate through the waste and into the soils surrounding the landfill or transfer stations.
Yard trimmings means leaves, brush, grass clippings, shrub and tree prunings, discarded Christmas trees, nursery and greenhouse vegetative residuals, and vegetative matter resulting from landscaping development and maintenance, other than mining, agricultural and silvicultural operations.
(Ord. of 9-10-2002, § 34-115(1))
Sec. 58-137. - Excess weeds and grasses prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any owner or occupant of land to have on private property weeds and grasses in excess of 20 inches in height. Heavily wooded lots where equipment cannot maneuver on the lot because of density are exempt from this section.
(Ord. of 9-10-2002, § 34-113)
Sec. 58-138. - Placement and disposal.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to place or mix yard trimmings with other county solid waste within the unincorporated area of the county.
(b)
Yard trimmings shall not be disposed of at any county solid waste disposal facility having a liner and leachate collection system or requiring vertical expansion located within the unincorporated areas of the county.
(Ord. of 9-10-2002, § 34-115(2))
Sec. 58-139. - Sorting; storing; composting; collecting.
(a)
Yard trimmings shall be sorted and stockpiled, or chipped, or composted, or used as mulch or otherwise beneficially reused or recycled to the maximum extent feasible.
(b)
Any yard trimmings to be collected by any other entity other than the property owner shall be sorted and stored in such a manner as to facilitate collection, composting, or other handling.
(c)
The board of commissioners hereby adopts and recommends the following hierarchy for handling yard trimmings:
(1)
Naturalized, low-maintenance landscaping requiring little or no cutting;
(2)
Grass cycling by mowing it high and letting it lie;
(3)
Stacking branches into small brush piles for use as wildlife habitats and for gradual decomposition into the soil;
(4)
Composting on the site where the material was grown, followed by incorporation of the finished compost into the soil at that site;
(5)
Chipping woody material on the site where such material was generated;
(6)
Collecting yard trimmings and transporting them to another site to be chipped or composted for later use; and
(7)
Chipping woody material for later use as fiber fuel.
(Code 2000, § 12-101(9); Ord. of 9-10-2002, § 34-115(3))

ARTICLE VII. - ENFORCEMENT

Sec. 58-162. - Responsibility; right of entry; power of summons.
Enforcement of this chapter shall be the responsibility of any sworn officer of the court, including, but not limited to, the sheriff, deputy sheriff, the code enforcement officer, building inspector, or assistant building inspector. Any person or persons authorized to enforce this chapter shall be empowered to enter any property, upon reasonable cause, at reasonable times in order to inspect the property for violations of this chapter, subject to the conditions that to allow entry on private property for inspection, the alleged violation of this chapter must be visible from a public road or right-of-way, or upon such officer having received a written complaint alleging a violation of this chapter. The magistrate court shall also be authorized to issue summons for hearings on alleged violations of this chapter upon written accusation of any person sui juris alleging such violation.
(Code 2000, § 12-101(16))
Sec. 58-163. - Cleaning property at expense of owner or occupant.
(a)
Upon the first violation of any section of this chapter, the county manager, or a designated county official, shall issue a warning citation to the owner and/or occupant of the offending property, instructing the person cited to remedy the unlawful condition within 30 days from the date of the warning citation.
(b)
If the person cited fails to so remedy the offending property within that 30-day time period, the county is authorized but not required to clean the offending property to bring it into compliance with the provisions of this chapter. In order to do so, the county, through its agents, may:
(1)
Remove any unlawful litter, junk, automobiles, weeds and/or grasses that exist thereon and bill the owner for the cost thereof. If the owner and/or occupant fails or refuses to pay bills for cleanup service within 30 days from the date of billing, then execution may be issued by the county against the property upon which such service is rendered for the amount of the cleanup service charge; and from and after the recording of such execution on the general execution docket in the office of the clerk of the superior court of the county, such execution shall constitute a lien on the property which the service is rendered until the same shall have been fully paid and satisfied. Such executions may be enforced in the same manner and with the addition of interest and costs as provided by law for the enforcement of executions for ad valorem taxes for the county; or
(2)
Contract with private individuals or entities to clean the offending property by removing the unlawful litter, junk, automobiles, weeds and/or grasses that exist thereon, allowing the private individuals or entities to collect their costs and expenses from either the cited owner and/or occupant directly or through the sale of any junk or scrap removed from the offending property.
(c)
After an owner and/or occupant of property has been issued a warning citation instructing the person to remedy an unlawful condition under this chapter, and the owner and/or occupant has allowed or caused an unlawful condition to occur on the same property again, the county is authorized but not required to clean the offending property to bring it into compliance within the provisions of this chapter in the manner set forth in subsections (b)(1) and (2) of this section, but the county shall not be required to issue any warning citation, nor will the offending owner or occupant of property entitled to 30 days to remedy the violation.
(d)
Action by the county under this section shall not preclude criminal prosecution for a violation of any section of this chapter, and the remedies authorized in this section are exclusive of any other criminal penalties and fines that may be assessed under this chapter.
(Code 2000, §§ 12-101(10)(b), (15); Ord. of 9-10-2002, § 34-116)
Sec. 58-164. - Civil remedies; abatement.
In the event that any person, firm, or corporation violates any provision of this chapter, the county attorney or other appropriate authority of the county may, in addition to other remedies, institute an action for injunction, mandamus, or other appropriate action or proceeding to prevent such unlawful act or to correct or abate such violation.
(Code 2000, § 12-101(7))
Sec. 58-165. - Violations; penalties.
(a)
Any person, firm, or corporation violating any portion of this chapter shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction thereof, shall be punished according to the general penalty described in section 1-9.
(b)
In addition, in the sound discretion of a court in which conviction is obtained:
(1)
The violator may be directed to pick up and remove from any public street or highway, or public right-of-way for a distance not to exceed one mile, any litter said person has deposited and any and all litter deposited thereon by anyone else prior to the date of the execution of sentence;
(2)
The violator may be directed to pick up and remove from a public park, private right-of-way, or with the prior permission of the legal owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property, any private property upon which it can be established by competent evidence that the violator has deposited thereon by anyone prior the date of execution of sentence; and/or
(3)
The court may publish the names of offenders convicted of violating this chapter.
(c)
In the case of an improper garbage or waste disposal site, the property owner, contractor, developer, builder or other person responsible for the property shall cause the property to be cleaned and to come into full compliance with this chapter.
(d)
The county shall not be responsible for any costs of cleanup or remediation.
(Code 2000, § 12-101(17))
