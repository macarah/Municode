Sec. 22-1. - Title.
This chapter shall be known and may be cited as the "Bulloch County Rural Telephone Cooperative Ordinance."
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-2. - Definitions.
For the purposes of this chapter, the following terms, phrases, words, and their derivations shall have the meaning given herein. When not inconsistent with the context, words used in the present tense including the future, words in the plural number include the singular number, and words in the singular number include the plural number. The word "shall" is always mandatory and not merely directory.
Cable system means a system of coaxial cable or other electrical conductors and equipment used or to be used to originate, receive and/or transmit television signals, radio signals, or any other audio, audio-visual, radio frequency, data or digital signals, or any other signals or frequencies, including, but not limited to signals obtained off-air, or via satellite, video tape, video discs, microwave, pay cable services, pay-per-view cable services, data processing, digital signals, local origination signals, telemetry, and any other type of transmission by means of electrical impulses, which may be distributed to subscribers for a fee by means of its system of coaxial cable and/or other electronic conductors and equipment.
City is the City of Statesboro, Georgia.
Council is the City Council of Statesboro, Georgia.
FCC is the Federal Communications Commission.
Franchise shall mean and include any authorization granted hereunder in terms of a franchise, right, privilege, and nonexclusive authority to construct, erect, operate, and maintain in, upon, along, across, above, over, and under the streets, alleys, public ways, public places now laid out of dedicated and all extensions thereof, and additions thereto in the city, a system of poles, wires, cables, underground conduits, manholes, and other conductors and fixtures necessary or convenient to maintain and operate a cable system in the city.
Grantee is Bulloch County Rural Telephone Cooperative, Inc. or anyone who succeeds Bulloch County Rural Telephone Cooperative, Inc. in accordance with the provisions of this chapter.
Person is any person, firm, partnership, association, corporation, company, or organization of any kind.
Subscriber shall mean any person who purchases or receives service from the grantee.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-3. - Grant of nonexclusive franchise.
(a)
There is hereby granted by the city to the grantee a nonexclusive franchise, right, privilege, authority, easement, and license to construct, erect, suspend, install, renew, repair, maintain, and open throughout the entire city in, upon, along, across, above, over, and under the streets, alleys, easements, public ways, and public places as now laid out or dedicated, and all extensions thereof, and additions thereof in the city, a system of poles, wires, cables, underground conduit, ducts, trenches, conductors, amplifying equipment, manhole fittings, and any and all other fixtures, appliances, and appurtenances necessary for the installation, maintenance, and operation in the city of a cable system either separately or in conjunction with any public utility maintaining the same in the city. This franchise shall further include the franchise, right, privilege, easement, and authority to construct, erect, suspend, install, lay, renew, repair, maintain, and operate such poles, wires, cables, underground conduits, manholes, ducts, trenches, fixtures, appliances, and appurtenances for the purpose of distribution to inhabitants both within and beyond the limits of the city. Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, this nonexclusive franchise shall and does hereby include the right, in, over and upon the streets, sidewalks, alleys, easements (including public utility easements) where such easements have been granted by the city, public grounds and places in the city for the purpose of installing, erecting, operating, or in any other way acquiring the use of, as by leasing or licensing, all poles, lines, and equipment necessary to operate a cable system and the right to make connections to subscribers, the right to repair, replace, and enlarge and extend said lines, equipment, and connections, and to maintain and use the same for the purpose of originating or receiving television signals, radio signals, or any other audio, audio-visual, radio frequency, data or digital signals, or any other signals or frequencies, including, but not limited to signals obtained off-air, or via satellite, video tape, video discs, microwave, pay cable services, pay-per-view cable services, data processing, digital signals, local origination signals, telemetry, and any other type of transmission by means of electrical impulses, which may be distributed to subscribers for a fee by means of its system of coaxial cable and/or other electronic conductors and equipment.
(b)
The right to use and occupy said streets, alleys, public ways, and places for the purposes herein set forth shall not be exclusive, and the city reserves the right to grant a similar use of said streets, alleys, public ways, and places, to any person at any time during the period of this franchise, however, that the following apply:
(1)
No cable system shall be allowed to operate without a duly authorized and granted franchise issued by city ordinance; and
(2)
No franchise ordinance shall be granted by the city to any person on terms or conditions less restrictive than those contained herein.
(c)
This franchise is for the corporate limits of the city and for any areas henceforth added thereto during the term of this franchise, which hereinafter shall be known as the "franchise area."
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-4. - Compliance.
The grantee shall, at all times during the life of this franchise, be subject to all lawful exercise of the police power by the city and to such reasonable regulation as the city shall hereafter provide.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-5. - Territorial area involved.
This franchise relates to the present territorial limits of the city and to any area henceforth added thereto during the term of this franchise.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-6. - Liability and indemnification.
(a)
The grantee shall pay and by its acceptance of this franchise the grantee specifically agrees that it will pay all reasonable damages and penalties which the city may legally be required to pay as a result of the negligent or intentionally wrongful acts or omissions of the grantee. These damages or penalties shall include, but shall not be limited to, damages arising out of copyright infringements and all other damages arising out of the installation, operation, or maintenance of the cable system authorized herein, whether or not any act or omission complained of is authorized, allowed, or prohibited by this franchise.
(b)
The grantee shall pay and by its acceptance of this franchise specifically agrees that it will pay all reasonable expenses incurred by the city in defending itself with regard to all damages and penalties mentioned in subsection (a) above. These expenses shall include all out-of-pocket expenses, such as attorney fees, and shall also include the reasonable value of any services rendered by the city attorney or his assistants or any employees of the city.
(c)
The city shall notify grantee, in writing, within ten days after the presentation of any claim or demand, either by suit or otherwise, made against the city on account of any negligent or intentionally wrongful acts or omissions as aforesaid on the part of grantee. Where any such claim or demand against the city is made by suit or other legal action, written notice thereof shall be given by the city to grantee not less than five days prior to the date upon which an answer to such legal action is due or within ten days after the claim or demand is made upon the city, whichever notice period yields grantee the greater amount of time within which to prepare an answer. Failure by the city to notify grantee properly in accordance with the foregoing of any such claim, suit, or demand against the city shall release grantee from its obligation to indemnify the city as provided herein.
(d)
The grantee shall maintain, and by its acceptance of this franchise specifically agrees that it will maintain throughout the term of this franchise liability insurance insuring the grantee, and naming the city as an additional insured, with regard to all damages mentioned in subparagraph (a) above, in the minimum amounts of:
(1)
One million dollars for bodily injury or death to any one person, within the limit, however, of $1,000,000.00 for bodily injury or death resulting from any one accident;
(2)
Five hundred thousand dollars for property damage resulting from any one accident;
(3)
Twenty-five thousand dollars for the infringement of copyrights; and
(4)
One million dollars for all other types of liability.
(e)
The grantee shall maintain, and by its acceptance of this franchise specifically agrees that it will maintain throughout the term of this franchise a faithful performance bond running to the city, with a good and sufficient surety reasonably acceptable to the city, in the penal sum of $10,000.00 conditioned that the grantee shall well and truly observe, fulfill, and perform each term and condition of this franchise and that in case of any breach of condition of the bond, the amount thereof shall be recoverable from the principal and sureties thereof by the city for all damages proximately resulting from the failure of the grantee to well and faithfully observe and perform any provision of this franchise.
(f)
The insurance policy and bond obtained by the grantee in compliance with this section must be approved by the council, which approval shall not be withheld unreasonably, and evidence of such insurance policy and bond, along with evidence of the renewals of same, shall be filed and maintained with the city clerk of the city during the term of this franchise.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-7. - Color television.
The facilities used by the grantee shall be capable of distributing color television signals, and when the signals the grantee distributes are received in color they shall be distributed in color where technically feasible.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-8. - Signal quality requirements.
The grantee shall:
(1)
Produce a picture, whether in black and white or in color, that is undistorted, free from ghost images, and accompanied with proper sound on typical standard production television sets in good repair, and as good as the state of the art allows;
(2)
Transmit signals of adequate strength to produce good pictures with good sound at all outlets without causing cross-modulation in the cables or interfering with other electrical or electronic systems;
(3)
Limit normal cable system failures to a minimum by locating and correcting malfunctions promptly and with due diligence; and
(4)
Be capable of demonstrating, by instruments and otherwise, to subscribers that a signal of adequate strength and quality is being delivered.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-9. - Operating and maintenance of cable system.
(a)
The grantee shall render efficient service, make repairs promptly, and interrupt service only for good cause and for the shortest time possible. Such interruptions, insofar as possible, shall be preceded by notice and shall occur during period of minimum use of the cable system.
(b)
The grantee shall maintain an office in the city, which shall be open during all usual business hours, have a listed telephone, and be so operated that complaints and requests for repairs or adjustments may be received at any time.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-10. - Carriage of signals.
The grantee shall have the right to receive and distribute television and radio signals which are disseminated to the general public without charge by broadcasting stations licensed by the FCC, and may distribute originated or received television signals, radio signals, or any other audio, audio-visual, radio frequency, data or digital signals, or any other signals or frequencies, directly or indirectly off air, including, but not limited to satellite, video tape, video discs, or microwave distributed signals, pay cable services, pay-per-view cable services, data processing, digital signals, origination signals, telemetry, and any other type of transmission by means of electrical impulses, whether or not directed to originating signals or receiving signals off-the-air, which may be distributed to subscribers by means of its cable system for a fee.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-11. - Program alteration.
All programs of broadcasting stations carried by the grantee shall be carried in their entirety as received, except the announcements and advertisements may be changed according to FCC regulations.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-12. - Service to schools.
The grantee shall provide basic cable service (consisting generally of a group of off-air broadcast signals and other signals that are sold to the largest group of grantee's subscribers) free of charge to any public school locations and teaching stations within the city for educational purposes upon request by the city. The service to be provided hereunder shall consist of a single outlet and any further distribution within any school building shall be the responsibility of the owner of such building. The grantee may, at its election, provide similar services without cost to private schools, including parochial or other religious schools, and other public buildings.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-13. - Emergency use of facilities.
In the case of any emergency or disaster, the grantee shall, upon request of the council, make available its facilities to the city for emergency use during the emergency or disaster period.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-14. - Other business activities.
(a)
The grantee hereunder shall not engage in the business of selling or installing television receivers, radio receivers, or accessories for such receivers within the city during the term of this franchise, except to the extend the same is authorized or required by the FCC.
(b)
This franchise authorizes only the operation of a cable system as provided for herein, and does not take the place of any other franchise, license, or permit which might be required by law of the grantee.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-15. - Safety requirements.
(a)
The grantee shall at all times employ ordinary care and shall install and maintain in use commonly accepted methods and devices for preventing failures and accidents which are likely to cause damage, injuries, or nuisances to the public.
(b)
The grantee shall install and maintain its wires, cables, fixtures, and other equipment in accordance with the requirements of the various codes of the city and in such manner that they will not unreasonably interfere with any installations of the city or of a public utility serving the city.
(c)
All structures and all lines, equipment, and connections in, over, under, and upon the streets, sidewalks, alleys, and public ways or places of the city, wherever situated or located, shall at all times be kept and maintained in a safe, suitable, substantial condition, and in good order and repair.
(d)
The grantee shall maintain a force of one or more resident agents or employees at all times and shall have sufficient employees to provide safe, adequate, and prompt service for its facilities.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-16. - New developments.
It shall be the policy of the city liberally to amend this franchise, upon application of the grantee, when necessary to enable the grantee to take advantage of any developments in this field of cable systems which will afford it any opportunity more effectively, efficiently, or economically to serve its customers. Provided, however, that this section shall not be construed to require the city to make any amendment.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-17. - Conditions on street occupancy.
(a)
All transmissions and distribution structures, lines, and equipment erected by the grantee within the city shall be so located as to cause minimum interference with the proper use of streets, alleys, and other public ways and places, and to cause minimum interference with the rights and reasonable convenience of property owners who adjoin any of the said streets, alleys, or other public ways and places.
(b)
In case of disturbance of any street, sidewalk, alley, public way, or paved area, the grantee shall, at its own cost and expense and in manner approved by the city engineer replace and restore such street, sidewalk, alley, public way, or paved area in a reasonably good a condition as before the work involving such disturbances was done.
(c)
If at any time during the period of this franchise the city shall lawfully elect to alter or change the grade of any street, sidewalk, alley, or other public way, the grantee, upon reasonable notice by the city, shall remove, relay, and relocate its poles, wires, cable, underground conduits, manholes, and other fixtures at its own expense.
(d)
Any poles or other fixtures placed in any public way by the grantee shall be placed in such a manner as not to unreasonably interfere with the usual travel on such public way.
(e)
The grantee shall, on request of any person holding a building moving permit issued by the city, temporarily raise or lower its wires to permit the moving of the buildings. The expense of such temporary removal or raising or lowering of wires shall be paid by the person requesting the same, and the grantee shall have the authority to require such payment in advance. The grantee shall be given not less than 48 hours advance notice to arrange for such temporary wire changes.
(f)
The grantee shall have, subject to approval of the city engineer, the authority (but not the obligation) to trim the trees upon and overhanging streets, alleys, sidewalks, and public ways and places of the city so as to prevent the branches of such trees from coming in contact with the wires and cables of the grantee.
(g)
In all sections of the city where the cables, wires, or other like facilities of public utilities are placed underground, the grantee shall place its cables, wires, or other like facilities underground to the maximum extent that existing technology reasonably permits the grantee to do so.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-18. - Transfer of franchise.
(a)
The grantee shall not transfer or sell its cable system and the franchise granted herein without first securing the approval of the city for sale or transfer, which such approval shall not be unreasonably withheld. The grantee may mortgage or assign its assets to secure a loan or loans without approval of the city; provided, however, such mortgage, deed to secure debt or assignment to secure said loan or loans, shall be subject to the terms and conditions of this franchise or any amendments thereto, and upon foreclosure, said lenders or purchasers at sale shall be bound by said terms and conditions.
(b)
Such new loan shall be procured from one of the following classes of institutions: bank; federal savings and loan association; state building and loan association; trust company; insurance company; educational institution; pension, retirement or welfare fund; and the loan instruments shall be in the usual form and content of such lender.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-19. - Filings and communications with regulatory agencies.
Copies of all petitions, applications, and communications submitted by the grantee to the FCC, Securities and Exchange Commission, or any other federal or state regulatory commission or agency having jurisdiction in respect to any matters affecting cable system operations authorized pursuant to this franchise, shall also be submitted simultaneously to the council if so requested in writing by the council.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-20. - City rights in franchise.
(a)
The right is hereby reserved to the city or the council to adopt, in addition to the provisions contained herein and in existing applicable ordinances, such additional regulation as it shall find necessary in the exercise of the police power; provided that such regulations, by ordinance or otherwise, shall be reasonable and not in conflict with the rights herein granted.
(b)
The city clerk, city attorney, or a certified public accountant retained by the city, shall have the reasonable right to inspect the books, records, maps, plans, income tax returns, and other like materials of the grantee at any time during normal business hours; provided, however, that any information so obtained shall be kept confidential.
(c)
The city shall have the right, during the life of this franchise, to install and maintain free of charge upon the poles of the grantee any wire and pole fixtures necessary for a police alarm system, on the condition that such wire and pole fixtures do not interfere with the cable system operations of the grantee.
(d)
The city shall have the right to supervise, at its own expense, all construction or installation work performed subject to the provisions of this franchise and make such inspections as it shall find necessary to insure compliance with the terms of this franchise and other pertinent provisions of law.
(e)
At the expiration of the term for which this franchise is granted, or upon its termination and cancellation, as provided for herein, the city shall have the right to require the grantee to remove at its own expense all portions of the cable system from all public ways within the city.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-21. - Maps, plats, and reports.
(a)
The grantee shall file with the city clerk true and accurate maps or plats of all existing and proposed installations.
(b)
The grantee shall submit to the city clerk an annual report, due on March 31st of each year this franchise is in effect, certified by an officer of the grantee, showing how it calculated the fees paid for the year ending December 31st of the previous year.
(c)
The city shall have the right to examine any of grantee's officers under oath with respect to the accuracy of any of grantee's reports or records upon 30 days' notification.
(d)
The grantee shall keep on file with the city clerk a current list of its shareholders and bondholders.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-22. - Payment to the city.
(a)
The grantee shall pay to the city annually and within 90 days after the end of grantee's fiscal year, an amount equal to five percent of the annual "gross revenues" received from grantee from the operation of its cable system within the corporate limits of the city as now or hereafter constituted. Such payments by grantee to the city shall be in lieu of payment by the grantee of any license tax or similar levy.
(b)
"Gross revenues" shall not include monies received by grantee as compensation for the following:
(1)
Installation charges;
(2)
Charges and fees for reconnections, relocations, and modifications of any installation;
(3)
Expenses incurred by grantee for program fees or other charges paid by grantee to any entity distributing pay cable services, pay-per-view cable services, program costs for satellite distributed or microwave broadcast stations (either radio or television), or any direct costs or expenses incurred by grantee for any such auxiliary services, including, but not limited to the fees paid directly to Home Box Office, WTBS, Cable News Network, WGN, WOR, USA Network, and ESPN ("direct costs" as used herein means costs paid to the entity furnishing the applicable services and does not include "indirect costs" such as overhead and salaries paid to employees of the cable system); and
(4)
The amount of any federal, state, or local sales taxes now or hereafter levied, assessed or imposed.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-23. - Forfeiture of franchise.
(a)
In addition to all other rights and powers pertaining to the city by virtue of this franchise or otherwise, the city reserves the right to terminate and cancel this franchise and all rights and privileges of the grantee hereunder in the event that the grantee:
(1)
Violates any provision of this franchise or any rule, order, or determination of the city or council made pursuant to this franchise, except where such violation, other than of section 14-50 or subsection (a)(2) below, is without fault or through excusable neglect;
(2)
Fails to provide proper and adequate television pictures and services to the citizens of the city;
(3)
Becomes insolvent, unable, or unwilling to pay its debts, or is adjudged a bankrupt;
(4)
Attempts to dispose of any of the facilities or property of its cable system business to prevent the city from purchasing same, as provided for herein; or
(5)
Attempts to evade any of the provisions of this franchise or practices any fraud or deceit upon the city.
(b)
Such termination and cancellation shall be by ordinance duly adopted only after providing the grantee with the following:
(1)
Written notice of my alleged defaults or violations of the franchise; and
(2)
A period of not less than 90 days in which to cure, or provide the city with a reasonable proposal to effect a cure, of the defaults or violations set forth in the city's written notice.
(c)
Such termination and cancellation shall in no way affect any of the city's rights under this franchise or any provision of law. In the event that such termination and cancellation depends upon a finding of fact, such finding of fact as made by the council or its representative shall be conclusive. Provided, however, that before this franchise may be terminated and cancelled under this section, the grantee must be provided with an opportunity to be heard in a proceeding affording due process before the council.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-24. - City's right of intervention.
The grantee agrees not to oppose intervention by the city in any suit or proceeding to which the grantee is a party.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-25. - Duration and acceptance of franchise.
(a)
This franchise and the rights, privileges, and authority hereby granted shall take effect and be in force from and after final passage hereof, as provided by law, and shall continue in force and effect until June 1, 2012.
(b)
Grantee is hereby given an option to renew this franchise for an additional ten years commencing at midnight on the day on which the original term of this franchise terminates.
(c)
The parties agree that said franchise extensions shall be automatic so that unless the grantee shall give to the city notice that it does not intend to renew this franchise, which notice must be given not later than 90 days prior to the expiration of the term specified above then, and in that event the parties hereto shall be bound each to the other, to the obligations, covenants, and agreements of this franchise for such additional term of years as set forth in said extensions.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-26. - Erection, removal, and common use of poles.
(a)
No poles or other aerial wire-holding structures shall be erected by the grantee without prior approval of the city engineer with regard to location, height, type, and any other pertinent aspect. However, no location of any pole or aerial wire-holding structure of the grantee shall be a vested interest and such poles or structures shall be removed or modified by the grantee at its own expense whenever the city engineer determines that the public convenience would be enhanced thereby.
(b)
Where poles or other wire-holding structures already existing for use in serving the city are available for use by the grantee, but it does not make arrangements for such use, the council may require the grantee to use such poles and structures if it determines that the public convenience would be enhanced thereby and the terms of the use available to the grantee are just and reasonable.
(c)
Where the city or a public utility serving the city desires to make use of the poles or other wireholding structures of the grantee but agreement therefore with the grantee cannot be reached, the council may require the grantee to permit such use for such consideration and upon such terms as the council shall determines to be just and reasonable, if the council determines that the use would enhance the public convenience and would not unduly interfere with the grantee's operations.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-27. - Number of channels.
The grantee's cable distribution system shall be capable of carrying at least 12 television channels. Further, the grantee shall make available upon the request of the council one channel for educational television other than normal state operated educational television and one channel for any other noncommercial service for the benefit of the inhabitants of the city.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-28. - Publication costs.
The grantee shall assume the cost of publication of this franchise as such publication is required by law and such is payable upon the grantee's filing of acceptance of this franchise.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-29. - Acceptance by grantee.
The grantee shall, within 90 days from the approval of this chapter, file its written acceptance of the same with the city clerk of the city, so as to form a contract between the parties.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-30. - No waiver of city rights.
City and grantee understand this franchise is subject to the provisions of the Cable Communications Policy Act of 1984 and other federal regulations currently in place. In particular, city and grantee understand that regulation of rates is governed by Section 543 of said Act, which as of date of the execution of this franchise precludes the city's involvement. However, the city does not by execution of this franchise abrogate or waive any future involvement or control over rates to subscribers should the current system of federal regulations by altered by Congress. In the event current federal regulation is changed, the city reserves the right to renegotiate provisions of this franchise concerning regulation of rates and other areas that are placed within the city's ability to control by subsequent acts of Congress or the FCC.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-31. - Compliance with FCC rate regulations.
The city will follow the FCC rate regulations in its regulation of the basic service rates and charges of the company and any other cable television system operating in the city, notwithstanding any different or inconsistent provisions in the franchise.
In connection with such regulation, the city will ensure a reasonable opportunity for consideration of the views of interested parties.
The mayor or his designee, is authorized to execute on behalf of the city and file with the FCC such certification forms or other instruments as are now or may hereafter be required by the FCC rate regulations in order to enable the city to regulate basic service rates and charges.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
Sec. 22-32. - Severability.
If any section, subsection, sentence, clause, phrase, or portion of this chapter is for any reason held invalid or unconstitutional by any court of competent jurisdiction, such portion shall be deemed a separate, distinct, and independent provision and such holding shall not affect the validity of the remaining portions hereof.
(Ord. No. 2010-16, 9-21-10)
