Sec. 22-221. - Authority.
This article is adopted pursuant to the authority granted to the city by the General Assembly, for the purpose of maintaining the health and safety of the city; and the provisions of this article are declared to be necessary for reasons of public health, safety and welfare.
(Ord. of 12-12-1994(2), § 2(A))
Sec. 22-222. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Peddler means any person, whether or not a resident of the city, traveling by foot, by wagon, automotive vehicle, or any other type of conveyance, from place to place, from house to house, or from street to street, carrying, conveying or transporting goods, wares, merchandise, products or provisions, offering or exposing such items for sale, or making sales and delivering articles to purchase or who, without traveling from place to place, shall sell or offer such items from a wagon, automotive vehicle, a railroad car or other vehicle or conveyance; and further provided that one who solicits orders and has a separate transaction, makes deliveries or helps cause to be delivered to purchasers as part of a scheme or design to evade the provisions of this article shall be deemed a peddler subject to the provisions of this article. The word "peddler" shall include the words "hawker" and "huckster."
(Ord. of 12-12-1994(2), § 2(B)II)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 22-223. - Application form.
Applicants for a permit and license under this article must file with the city clerk a sworn application in writing on a form to be furnished by the city clerk which shall give the following information:
(1)
Name of the applicant.
(2)
Legal and local address of the applicant.
(3)
A brief description of the nature of the business and the goods to be sold.
(4)
The name and address of the employer of the applicant.
(5)
The length of time in which the right to do business is desired.
(6)
If a vehicle is to be used, a description of the vehicle, together with its license number.
(Ord. of 12-12-1994(2), § 2(C))
Sec. 22-224. - Issuance of license; fee.
Upon receipt of a fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges, the city clerk shall issue a peddlers license. This license shall be carried by the person at all times while peddling within the city and may not be used or transferred at any time by or to any person other than the one to whom it was issued.
(Ord. of 12-12-1994(2), § 2(D))
Sec. 22-225. - Use of the streets.
No peddler shall have an exclusive right to any location or public streets, nor shall any be permitted a stationary location, nor shall he be permitted to operate in any congested area where his operations may impede or inconvenience the public. For purposes of this article, the judgment of a police officer exercised in good faith shall be deemed conclusive as to whether the area is congested or the public impeded or inconvenienced.
(Ord. of 12-12-1994(2), § 2(E))
Sec. 22-226. - Suspension and revocation of license.
(a)
Licenses issued under the provisions of this article may for good cause be suspended for a period of not more than five days by the chief of police.
(b)
Licenses issued under the provisions of this article may be revoked by the city manager after notice and hearing for any of the following causes:
(1)
Fraud, misrepresentation or false statement contained in the application for license.
(2)
Fraud, misrepresentation or false statement made in the course of carrying on his business as a peddler.
(3)
Any violation of this article.
(4)
Conviction of any crime or misdemeanor involving moral turpitude.
(5)
Conducting the business of peddling in an unlawful manner or in such a manner as to constitute a breach of the peace or to constitute a menace to the health, safety or general welfare of the public.
(c)
Notice of a hearing for revocation of a license shall be in writing setting forth specifically the grounds of complaint and giving the time and place of hearing. Such notice shall be mailed to the licensee at his last known address at least five days prior to the date set for a hearing.
(Ord. of 12-12-1994(2), § 2(F))
Sec. 22-227. - Appeal.
Any person aggrieved by the action of any city employee concerning his license shall have the right to appeal to the city council if such appeal is taken within 14 days after notice of the action complained of has been received by that person.
(Ord. of 12-12-1994(2), § 2(G))
Sec. 22-228. - Expiration of license.
All licenses issued under the provisions of this article shall expire on December 31 of the year when issued.
(Ord. of 12-12-1994(2), § 2(H))
Sec. 22-229. - Penalty.
Any person violating the provisions of this article shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-12.
(Ord. of 12-12-1994(2), § 2(I))
