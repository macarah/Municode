Chapter 23 - FILM AND MUSIC PROJECT PERMITS

Sec. 23-1. - Purpose and findings.
(a)
This chapter sets forth rules and regulations to govern and authorize individuals and film companies to conduct filming activities in the City of Oakwood without unreasonably impacting the peace and comfort of the residents of the community; to assure that such activities are consistent with considerations of the public health, safety and general welfare and to ensure the protection of property; and to provide a streamlined process for issuing film production permits. The requirements provided in this chapter shall be separate and in addition to those provisions of the City Business License Code and/or other codes adopted by reference, regarding business regulation and licensing.
(b)
The purpose of this chapter is to regulate filming and recording activity within the City of Oakwood in order to protect the public health, safety, and welfare.
(Ord. No. 2017-CO-07-751, § 1(Exh. A), 8-14-2017)
Sec. 23-2. - Definitions.
Business license office shall mean the Oakwood city clerk and/or the city manager.
Camera ready liaison shall mean the designated staff person of the Lake Lanier Convention & Visitors Bureau, Inc. who serves as the Camera Ready Liaison for the Georgia Film Office.
Chief of police shall mean the serving City of Oakwood Police Chief or his designee.
Fire marshall shall mean the serving Fire Marshall of Hall County or his designee.
Engineering shall mean the designated staff person from the City of Oakwood who shall be charged with handling any and all engineering issues.
Planning and zoning shall mean the designated staff person from the City of Oakwood Planning and Zoning department.
News purposes shall mean filming, videotaping or otherwise recording news events for television, radio or Internet news casting by reporters, photographers, or audio or camera persons in the employ of a newspaper, news service or similar entity where the event is not preplanned and the film or tape is originally broadcast within 24 hours of the event, not including magazine or documentary programs.
Filming activity shall mean the staging, shooting, filming, videotaping, photographing, or other similar process conducted for the making of still photographs, motion pictures, television programs, music videos, commercials, and other commercial and non-theatrical productions.
Studio shall mean a fixed place of business where filming activities are regularly conducted upon the premises.
Film permit shall mean written authorization from the City of Oakwood business license clerk to conduct the filming activity outlined in the permit.
(Ord. No. 2017-CO-07-751, § 1(Exh. A), 8-14-2017)
Sec. 23-3. - Permit required.
Except as otherwise provided in section 23-4, it shall be unlawful for any person to engage in filming activity as defined in section 23-2 any place within the City of Oakwood, other than an established studio as defined in section 23-2 or within an enclosed structure or building, without a film permit from the City of Oakwood.
(Ord. No. 2017-CO-07-751, § 1(Exh. A), 8-14-2017)
Sec. 23-4. - Exceptions.
The provisions of this chapter shall not apply to:
(1)
Noncommercial filming or videotaping activities conducted solely for private or family use;
(2)
Film activity conducted for use in a criminal investigation or civil or criminal court proceeding;
(3)
Film activity conducted for news purposes as defined in section 23-2;
(4)
Film activity conducted for education, government, and public access and local origination for public television or public cable or public Internet channels within the State of Georgia; or
(5)
Non-profit 50l(c)(3) tax exempt organizations and other governmental agencies if their film project is not for commercial use;
(6)
Any film activity deemed by the City of Oakwood to be in the public interest.
Nothing in this chapter shall limit the right of the city or any of its departments, the chief of police or the fire marshall to suspend film activity otherwise accepted herein when deemed necessary for the protection of the public health, safety or general welfare or when such film activity poses an immediate hazard to persons and/or property.
(Ord. No. 2017-CO-07-751, § 1(Exh. A), 8-14-2017)
Sec. 23-5. - Permit application.
The city clerk shall provide an application form and make it available for anyone who desires a film permit. A completed application must be provided to the city clerk at least two weeks prior to the date on which the film activity is proposed. Upon a showing of good cause, applications filed after the deadline may be considered by the business license office. The business license office shall determine in its sole discretion, not to be exercised in a discriminatory manner, whether there is time to ensure all requirements to protect the public health, safety and welfare can be timely met before the film activity is to commence. After filing and approval, permit applications can be amended only with the approval of the business license office and only after the business license office determines that requested modifications will not negatively impact the public health, safety and welfare.
(Ord. No. 2017-CO-07-751, § 1(Exh. A), 8-14-2017)
Sec. 23-6. - Application review and permit conditions.
(a)
Before granting a film permit, the business license office shall review the application and consult with the chief of police, fire marshall, planning and zoning and the camera ready liaison as appropriate and determine that the proposed film activity will not unreasonably interfere with the public health, safety and general welfare or unreasonably endanger any property. Appropriate city officials (and county) shall be consulted to review the application as deemed necessary by the business license office. In granting a film permit, any reasonable conditions may be imposed as necessary to protect public health, safety, general welfare and property. Factors which may be taken into consideration in granting and conditioning a permit include but are not limited to: noise, traffic congestion, traffic hazards, environmental problems, and interference with street maintenance work, unreasonable interference with normal business operations or residential peace and quiet and whether the proposed activity would violate federal, state or local law.
(b)
Conditions which may be attached to the film permit may include but are not limited to the following:
(1)
Requirements regarding the presence of governmental employees, such as police officers or firemen, at the permittee's expense;
(2)
Requirements regarding traffic control, such as the placement of traffic control devices and signs at the permittee's expense;
(3)
Requirements regarding cleanup and restoration of property used in the film activity;
(4)
Restrictions regarding the use of firearms and noise-creating or hazardous devices;
(5)
Restrictions regarding stunts involving pyrotechnics, vehicle crashes and other hazardous materials and/or activity;
(6)
Requirements relating to partial or full nudity in public places;
(7)
Restrictions regarding the time of the film activity; and
(8)
Requirements regarding notifications to nearby businesses and/or residences.
(Ord. No. 2017-CO-07-751, § 1(Exh. A), 8-14-2017)
Sec. 23-7. - Fees.
The fee for permits issued pursuant to this chapter shall be an application fee and a per day permitting fee as set forth in chapter 22 of the Oakwood City Code or as may be adjusted from time to time considering the duration and scope of any particular project. The applicant shall pay such fee upon delivery of the application to the business license office. Any amounts representing the estimated cost of reimbursement of governmental personnel and equipment shall be paid at the time of permit issuance. Should the actual costs of governmental personnel and equipment exceed the estimated costs, the permit holder may be invoiced for the difference by the business license office.
(Ord. No. 2017-CO-07-751, § 1(Exh. A), 8-14-2017)
Sec. 23-8. - Insurance.
The applicant shall provide the business license office with evidence of insurance for general liability and covering any liability of the permittee, permittee's lessees, and any state or local governmental authorities within whose jurisdiction the film activity is to occur, including their /its officers, employees and agents arising out of the film activity. The insurance policy shall be in an amount not less than $1,000,000.00 or such higher amount as determined by the business license office to be appropriate to protect against foreseeable risks for bodily and personal injury liability and property damage arising from or in connection with the film activity. The applicant shall furnish the business license office with proof of such insurance coverage in a form and manner that is satisfactory in all respects to the business license office prior to the start of the film activity.
(Ord. No. 2017-CO-07-751, § 1(Exh. A), 8-14-2017)
Sec. 23-9. - Indemnification and liability.
The permittee shall hold harmless, indemnify and defend all state and local governments and their officers, employees and agents from and against any and all losses, claims or judgments for damages to any person or property arising from the film activity or issuance of the film permit.
(Ord. No. 2017-CO-07-751, § 1(Exh. A), 8-14-2017)
Sec. 23-10. - Permit revocation.
The business license office may revoke the film permit if the permittee, or any agents, employees, volunteers or contractors of the permittee fail to comply with the permit conditions and requirements or if it is determined after issuance that the permit application is materially false. The business license office shall provide the permittee with grounds for revocation in writing within a reasonable time of determining the basis for the revocation.
(Ord. No. 2017-CO-07-751, § 1(Exh. A), 8-14-2017)
Sec. 23-11. - Permit suspension.
Any permit issued pursuant to the terms of this chapter shall be subject to immediate suspension by the business license office if it is found necessary for the protection of the public health, safety or general welfare. Any employee of a local governmental entity within whose jurisdiction film activity is occurring and who has public safety responsibilities for that local government may suspend the film permit upon a determination by that employee that the film activity poses an immediate hazard to persons and/or property and the permit holder's representative in charge will not or cannot prevent the hazard after being instructed to do so by the employee.
(Ord. No. 2017-CO-07-751, § 1(Exh. A), 8-14-2017)
Sec. 23-12. - Appeals.
The permit applicant or permittee may appeal a permit denial, revocation, suspension or refusal to waive a deadline set forth in this chapter. The appeal shall be filed in the city clerk at City Hall no later than three business days after the date notice of the decision is given. The appeal shall be heard by the city manager or his or her designee no later than three days after the appeal is filed. A decision shall be rendered no later than two business days after the appeal hearing. The decision of the city manager or his or her designee shall be final.
(Ord. No. 2017-CO-07-751, § 1(Exh. A), 8-14-2017)
Sec. 23-13. - Unlawful conduct not sanctioned.
The granting of a permit does not absolve the permittee or its agents from complying with all federal, state and local laws, other than for conduct expressly described in the application and not subject to a condition or requirement of the permit. (For example, the permit might allow stunts that include speeding on a roadway that would otherwise be prescribed by law.)
(Ord. No. 2017-CO-07-751, § 1(Exh. A), 8-14-2017)
Sec. 23-14. - Violation.
(a)
Violations of any of the provisions of this chapter or any of the terms and conditions of a Film and Music Project Permit are a misdemeanor punishable under the provisions of this Code.
(b)
It shall also be a misdemeanor to:
(1)
Provide false or fraudulent information to the city during the permit application process;
(2)
Provide any false or fraudulent information to the city after the permit has been issued including, but not limited to, the possession or existence of consent or permission from property owners when required; or
(3)
Failure or refusal to cease any film activities when ordered to do so by the city manager due to violations of any provision of the Oakwood Code of Ordinances including this chapter, state law or federal law.
(c)
If an applicant violates any provisions of this chapter or a permit issued pursuant thereto, the city may provide the applicant with verbal or written notice of such violation. If the applicant fails to correct the violation, the city may immediately revoke the permit and all activity must cease.
(d)
Any filming or related activity conducted contrary to the provisions of this chapter shall be and is hereby declared to be an unlawful action and a public nuisance. The city may authorize the commencement of an action at law or in equity in the name of the city in any court of competent jurisdiction against the applicant to ensure compliance with the terms of this chapter. All remedies prescribed herein will be cumulative and the use of any one or more remedies by the city shall not preclude the use of any other remedy for the purpose of enforcing the provisions of this chapter, nor shall the city's delay or failure to exercise any remedy result in a waiver of that remedy.
(Ord. No. 2017-CO-07-751, § 1(Exh. A), 8-14-2017)
