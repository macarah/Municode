ARTICLE II. - REFUSE COLLECTION AND DISPOSAL

Sec. 38-19. - Penalties.
(a)
Any person charged with a violation of any section or provision of this article shall be given a written notice and instructions from the city manager as to the required remedy for such violation and shall be given 30 days to comply with the provisions of this article. Failure to comply with the requirements of such written notice and instructions of the city manager shall result in the termination of services by the city and shall, upon conviction in the municipal court be punished as provided heretofore.
(b)
Reinstatement of city trash and garbage collection shall only be provided after the person has fully complied with all the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013)
Sec. 38-20. - City responsible for sanitation services generally.
(a)
All residential garbage, trash and refuse accumulated in the city shall be collected, conveyed and disposed by the city except as expressly provided in this article. Commercial garbage, trash and refuse collection, conveyance, and disposal in the city shall be performed by private service providers.
(b)
The city will collect refuse in accordance with a schedule as established from time to time by the city council and kept on file in the city manager.
(c)
The following items, products or materials will not be collected and it shall be a violation of this article for any person to place such items, products or materials in any refuse can or container for collection: rubber tires, acid, explosive material, inflammable liquids and dangerous or corrosive material of any kind.
(d)
The city council may grant a contract to persons to collect, convey and sell residential recyclables and solid waste with the following requirements:
(1)
Such person shall pay an annual permit fee as fixed from time to time by the city council to operate within the city.
(2)
The city shall bill and collect a fee from residential customers.
(3)
The fee shall cover the operating cost of such persons as specified in the contract and the cost of management.
(4)
The operation cost as specified in the contract shall be paid to such persons on a monthly basis upon receipt of an invoice and record of recyclables collected.
(5)
Such person shall provide the city manager or his designee with evidence of insurance coverage as to vehicle liability and public liability in an amount as established from time to time by the city council prior to securing a permit.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013)
Sec. 38-21. - Residential collection days designated.
Residential garbage, trash, and refuse shall be collected from the various premises in the city by the proper departments of the city as follows:
(1)
Collections from residential premises for garbage, trash and refuse will normally be made once a week in accordance with the schedule established by the City Council.
(2)
In the event of inclement weather, collection will be on the next business day when weather permits.
(3)
Residents with a temporary or permanent disability may apply for an exemption of the curbside requirement on forms to be provided by the city clerk.
(4)
Special collections for trees or tree limbs shall be on the last Friday of each month. Limbs are to be no more than six inches in diameter and not less than three feet in length. Limbs in excess of the required size will not be collected. Limbs should be placed curbside in such a manner as to not obstruct passage. This placement shall be no more than two days prior to pickup. All limbs not collected shall be removed by the resident within 24 hours.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013)
Sec. 38-22. - Household item collections.
The following household items may be collected and deposed of by the city upon payment of a special pickup fee established by the City Council:
(1)
Barbeque grills;
(2)
Appliances;
(3)
Electronics;
(4)
Furniture; and
(5)
Other like items.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013)
Sec. 38-23. - Separation of refuse for pickup.
(a)
Each classification of refuse must be kept separate and will be picked up separately. Recycling is mandatory and should be placed in a separate container from general refuse, garbage and trash.
(b)
The city shall not be responsible for collecting or hauling discarded building materials, dirt, broken concrete, bricks, rock or debris resulting from repairs, remodeling or construction. Such materials must be disposed of by the contractor or owner of the property. The city shall not be responsible for collecting or hauling trees, bushes or other vegetation from commercial tree trimmers, landscapers, grading contractors or building contractors except on a special fee basis by agreement with the city manager or his designee.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013)
Sec. 38-24. - Preparation and storage of residential garbage and refuse for collection.
(a)
With the exception of multifamily dwellings, residential garbage and refuse shall be placed in securely tied plastic bags and stored in roll-cart containers owned and provided by the city. Any excess refuse shall be placed in securely tied plastic trash bags and placed on top of the containers. The containers shall be placed curbside no later than 8:00 a.m. on pickup day and shall be removed from the right of way no later than 8:00 a.m. the next day.
(b)
Garbage and refuse placed in containers not provided by the city will not be collected and the customer will be notified of noncompliance with this article.
(c)
Garbage and refuse services for multifamily dwellings shall be provided in accordance with a site-specific storage and collection plan approved by the city manager. The city will not collect garbage and refuse from multifamily dwellings that fail to comply with the city approved storage and collection plan.
(d)
Residents are required to maintain roll cart containers in a sanitary condition, tightly covered at all times except for filling. The container shall be further maintained so as to prevent the overflow or scattering of trash, garbage or refuse.
(e)
Grass trimmings, small shrubbery trimmings, pine needles, etc., are not accepted and should be composted.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013)
Sec. 38-25. - Provision of approved city-owned garbage and refuse containers.
The city shall provide, or arrange the provision of, city-owned containers of sufficient size for households to deposit their garbage, trash and refuse. However, the following costs shall apply:
(1)
Single-family residences. Property owners shall be required to execute an acknowledgement receipt of said approved city-owned container. The first container shall be provided at no cost. Additional cans shall be provided upon receipt of deposit. If said container(s) are lost, stolen, damaged or destroyed, the property owner shall be responsible for a replacement fee as set forth in the fee schedule.
(2)
Townhouses, two-family residences (duplexes), multifamily dwellings, rental single-family residences. Managers/owners shall be required to execute an acknowledgement receipt of said approved city-owned container. The first container shall be provided at no cost. Additional cans shall be provided upon receipt of deposit. If said container(s) are lost, stolen, damaged or destroyed, the managers/owners shall be responsible for a replacement fee as set forth in the fee schedule.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013)
Sec. 38-26. - Depositing in street or public place.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to litter, throw, deposit, scatter or otherwise cause to be placed or left upon any vacant or unoccupied lot or lots along any street or sidewalk or other public place or private property within the city without the consent of the owner or tenant in lawful possession, any paper, wastes, rubbish, trimmings, limbs or garbage of any kind.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person utilizing any vehicle, moving or parked, to cause any paper, wastes, cartons, rubbish, feathers, gravel, earth, trimmings, limbs or garbage of any kind to be littered, thrown, deposited or scattered upon any street, sidewalk or public place or any private property, occupied or unoccupied. Such precautions as are necessary to contain transported materials including covering of open trucks, shall be utilized to prevent such littering and scattering.
(c)
The city shall not be responsible for the disposal of dead animals.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013)
Sec. 38-27. - Unauthorized use of solid waste containers and prohibiting scavenging.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to throw, deposit or otherwise leave trash in solid waste containers that are leased, owned or paid for private use except by permission of the owner. This prohibition includes dumpsters on government property. Only dumpsters marked "For Public Use" shall be used and it shall be unlawful to import household trash to these dumpsters.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to scavenge or remove solid waste from solid waste containers or dumpsters. Trash in these containers is the property of the entity providing the service.
(c)
Any person found guilty of violating the provisions of this section shall on conviction thereof be punished as set forth in section 38-19.
Secs. 38-28—38-54. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - RECYCLING

Sec. 38-55. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Administrator means the city manager or his authorized representative.
Aluminum cans means containers fabricated primarily of aluminum, usually beverage cans (soda, beer, etc.), including tin foil, pie pans, TV dinner trays, emptied of all contents both liquid and solid, and rinsed.
Approved recycling container means the receptacle provided by the city or its authorized representative for placement of certain recyclable materials.
Cardboard means wood pulp based material which is usually smooth on both sides with or without corrugated center (wavy middle layer), commonly used for boxes such as for storage, shoes, cereals, crackers, etc. Specifically excluded are wax coated cardboards.
Corrugated cardboard means wood pulp based material which is usually smooth on both sides with a corrugated center (wavy middle layer), commonly used for boxes, that has been dismantled and flattened, and including clean, brown paper grocery bags. The term "corrugated cardboard" specifically excludes waxed-coating cardboard and single-layer boxes such as for shoes, cereals, crackers, etc.
Glass means transparent or translucent jars, bottles and containers which are used for packaging and bottling food and beverages only, but only those that are green, blue, brown or clear in color, emptied of all contents, both liquid and solid and rinsed, and not including lids. Specifically excluded are plate and window glass, light bulbs, ceramic or heated, treated glass (i.e. Pyrex). Glass may contain printed paper labels and still be acceptable for recycling. Glass must be placed in separate containers and not comingled with other recyclables.
Magazines means glossy ground wood containing paper, including magazines, catalogs and periodicals having stapled or glued bindings.
Metal cans means food and beverage containers fabricated primarily of tin or steel, emptied of all contents both liquid and solid and rinsed, and not including lids. Cans may contain printed paper labels and still be acceptable for recycling. Metal paint cans are included if empty and paint residue is dried. Aerosol spray cans are also included if empty and without plastic caps.
Newspaper means newsprint, including printed inserts that come with newsprint, but not including magazines and catalogs printed on "glossy" or "slick" paper.
Newsprint means common, inexpensive machine finished paper made chiefly from wood pulp and used for newspapers.
Nonrecyclable refuse means any solid waste not listed as recyclable materials as defined in this section.
Plastic means beverage containers (soda, milk, etc.), including high density polyethylene (HDPE No. 2), both natural (cloudy white, such as bleach and laundry detergent bottles), and polyethylene terephthalate (PETE No. 1), such as green and clear one-liter and two-liter soda bottles, all materials being rinsed, without caps or lids, and flattened. Specifically excluded any plastics that contain hazardous materials (such as motor oil, transmission fluid and pesticides), rigid plastics (such as buckets, toys and plastic furniture) and styrofoam.
Recyclable materials means aluminum cans, cardboard, glass, newspaper, magazines, metal cans and plastic, all as more specifically defined in this section.
Source separation means the segregation of recyclable materials from solid waste at the point of generation for separate collection, sale or other disposition.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013; Ord. No. 2018-CO-05-769, § 1, 6-11-2018)
Sec. 38-56. - Violations and penalties.
Any failure to comply with the provisions of this article shall be deemed a violation. The administrator is authorized to provide a written warning notice to first time offenders, at his discretion, depending upon the extent of the violation. Any person violating this article shall be liable for a civil penalty of not less than $50.00 for first offenses and $200.00 for subsequent violations, as determined by the court of proper jurisdiction.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013)
Sec. 38-57. - Purpose.
(a)
The ordinance from which this article is derived is enacted for the purpose of implementing state, county and city policies for encouraging waste stream reduction through recycling, as more fully described in the Georgia Comprehensive Solid Waste Management Act, the Georgia Solid Waste Management Plan, and the Hall County-Municipal Comprehensive Solid Waste Management Plan.
(b)
This article is intended to facilitate the disposal of solid waste generated within the city in the most environmentally acceptable manner possible and to facilitate and encourage the recovery of all recyclable materials which can be marketed or used for secondary purposes.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013)
Sec. 38-58. - Administrative authority.
The administrator is hereby empowered by the governing body to administer and enforce the provisions of this article and to promulgate, publish, revise and amend administrative rules, regulations and orders necessary to implement and enforce the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013)
Sec. 38-59. - Applicability.
The provisions of this article shall apply to all households within all single-family residences, townhouses, two-family residences (duplexes) and all multiple family dwellings within the city limits, whom on the effective date of the ordinance from which this article is derived, are receiving solid waste collection services, or whom hereafter receive solid waste collection services provided or arranged by the city. This article shall not apply to institutions and commercial or industrial establishments.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013)
Sec. 38-60. - Exemptions.
Any household unable to comply with this article for good cause shown, for example, handicapped residents, may apply to the administrator for an exemption from this article.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013)
Sec. 38-61. - Residential recyclable materials collection days designated.
Collections from residential premises for recyclable materials will normally be made once a week in accordance with the schedule established by the City Council.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013)
Sec. 38-62. - Source separation of recyclable materials required.
All households to which this article applies shall be required to separate all recyclable materials, as defined by this article, from nonrecyclable refuse, and place said recyclable materials in an approved recycling container provided by the city, and to place the approved container near the curb, or streetside for collection by the city or as arranged by the city. Recyclable materials shall not be commingled with nonrecyclable refuse.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013)
Sec. 38-63. - Provisions for multiple-family residential complexes.
(a)
The owner, manager, or superintendent of every multiple-family residential complex receiving refuse collection by the city or as arranged by the city, shall provide and maintain, in a neat and sanitary condition, recycling collection areas to receive the recyclable materials generated by households of the complex. This provision merely requires the source separation of recyclable materials from nonrecyclable refuse, although further separation of recyclable materials by type of material is encouraged.
(b)
In cases where a cooperative or similar association exists, the association shall be responsible for the provision and maintenance of the recycling collection areas.
(c)
All households of multiple-family residential complexes shall provide source separation of recyclable materials and nonrecyclable refuse and shall deposit all recyclables in the appropriate containers.
(d)
Multiple-family residential complexes shall have 60 days from the effective date of the ordinance from which this article is derived to comply with this section. Failure to provide for the recycling collection area within the specified period of time shall be considered a violation of this article.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013; Ord. No. 2021-CO-09-819, § 4, 10-11-2021)
Sec. 38-64. - Soiled or unclean recyclable materials.
In limited instances, recyclable materials may become soiled, unclean, or used for a purpose (i.e., newspaper as a bird cage liner) that renders them unable to be cleaned and therefore unsuitable for recycling. In such limited cases, soiled recyclable materials shall not be included in the approved recycling container and shall be considered nonrecyclable refuse.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013)
Sec. 38-65. - Other recyclable materials.
There are several materials that do not meet the definition of recyclable materials as provided in this article, but which are indeed recyclable materials collectable by the resource recovery division of the county department of public works at various compactor sites in the county. Such materials include used motor oil, cooking grease, electronics and clothing. Households are encouraged, but not required, to source separate and deposit for recycling any recyclable materials not collected by the city.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013; Ord. No. 2018-CO-05-769, § 1, 6-11-2018)
Sec. 38-66. - Provision of approved recycling containers by city.
The city shall provide, or arrange the provision of, containers of sufficient size for households to deposit their recyclable materials. The city shall not be required to provide approved containers for households within multiple-family residential complexes which are required by section 38-63 to provide recycling collection areas. Approved city-owned recycling containers shall be provided at no cost to households or multiple-family residential complex owners. However, the following costs shall apply:
(1)
Single-family residences. Property owners shall be required to execute an acknowledgement receipt of said approved city-owned recycling container. The first container shall be provided at no cost. Additional cans shall be provided upon receipt of deposit. If said container(s) are lost, stolen, damaged or destroyed, the property owner shall be responsible for a replacement fee as set forth in the fee schedule.
(2)
Townhouses, two-family residences (duplexes), multifamily dwellings, rental single-family residences. Managers/owners shall be required to execute an acknowledgement receipt of said approved city-owned container. The first container shall be provided at no cost. Additional cans shall be provided upon receipt of deposit. If said container(s) are lost, stolen, damaged or destroyed, the managers/owners shall be responsible for a replacement fee as set forth in the fee schedule.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013)
Sec. 38-67. - Notification of program by city.
The administrator shall be responsible for providing written notification and explanation, by individual letter, brochure or through the city's newsletter or website to all households concerning the mandatory residential recycling program.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013)
Sec. 38-68. - Amendment.
This article may be amended by the city council subject to any applicable procedures required by the City Charter.
(Ord. No. 2013-CO-04-709, § 1(Exh. A), 5-13-2013)
