ARTICLE VII. - CITY FARMERS' MARKET LICENSES

Sec. 8-300. - City farmers' market license.
All sellers of produce, fruits, vegetables and other goods at the city farmers' market shall obtain a city farmers' market license which shall be valid for a calendar year and permits a person to commence business at the city farmers' market at any time that it is open at such hours as are set by the city manager or his/her designee. Applications for a city farmers' market license and the license form shall be created by the city manager or his/her designee. Only one city farmers' market license may be issued per year to any individual or entity. A fee as set forth in section 2-110 of this Code shall be required for the purpose of regulating such businesses which shall be due and payable prior to selling any products at the city farmers' market.
(Ord. of 10-02-2017(1), § 3)
Sec. 8-301. - Compliance with other laws and regulations.
All applicants for a city farmers' market license shall be required to obtain any applicable health department, state department of agriculture or federal approval/permit needed for the sale of the produce, fruits, vegetables or other products as a precondition to the issuance of a city farmers' market license.
(Ord. of 10-02-2017(1), § 3)
Sec. 8-302. - Enforcement and revocation.
The provisions of this article may be enforced through the city's code enforcement procedures or as otherwise authorized in this Code. The remedies that the city may pursue for a violation of this article include:
(1)
As provided in section 1-8 of the Code of Ordinances of Dawsonville; and/or
(2)
Revocation or refusal to renew the city farmers' market license.
(Ord. of 10-02-2017(1), § 3)
