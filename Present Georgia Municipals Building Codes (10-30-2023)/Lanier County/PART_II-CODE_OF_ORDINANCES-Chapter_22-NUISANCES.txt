Chapter 22 - NUISANCES[1]

Sec. 22-1. - Junk vehicles; junk scraps; household appliances.
(a)
It shall be illegal for any owner or occupant to store in public view for a period of more than 30 days a junk motor vehicle, junk scrap parts; abandoned, dilapidated mobile homes; or household appliances on property within the county that can be viewed from a state, county, or other public road.
(b)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Junk motor vehicle means:
(1)
Any motor vehicle which is not capable of locomotion under its own power due to missing components such as motors, wheels, tires or any other parts necessary to make the vehicle operable; wrecked, scrapped, ruined or dismantled motor vehicles.
(2)
Any motor vehicle that does not comply with Georgia Traffic Law, O.C.G.A. ยง 40-8-1 et seq., equipment and inspection of motor vehicles, in its entirety.
Junk scraps mean old or scrap copper, wood furniture, brass, rope, rags, batteries, paper, trash, rubber, debris, or waste; junked or dismantled automobile parts; or iron, steel, inoperable household appliances and other old scrap ferrous or nonferrous material.
(c)
The occupant of any lot that stores a junk motor vehicle, junk scrap parts or household appliances, shall be given 30 days' notice to remove the property or build a privacy fence at least six feet high prior to issuance of a written summons. If the occupant has not complied with the request, the matter will be turned over to the magistrate court of the county.
(d)
Any person violating any provision of this chapter shall, upon conviction, be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be punished according to the general penalties described in section 1-14, per occurrence, as provided by O.C.G.A. ยง 36-1-20.
