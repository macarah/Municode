ARTICLE III. - TEMPORARY USES

Sec. 110-56. - Permit conditions.
A permit for a temporary structure or land use such as a carnival, revival meeting, construction facility, seasonal sale, or use of a similar nature may be issued by the building official provided the following conditions are met:
(1)
The use is, in fact, temporary, and will terminate at a specific time, as determined by the building official;
(2)
The proposed site is of adequate size to accommodate the use without creating congestion in the streets or inadequate circulation for fire or other emergency vehicles;
(3)
Adequate access and parking facilities shall be provided and shall not interfere with traffic movements on adjacent streets;
(4)
No banners, pennants, or noise-producing devices of a disruptive nature shall be permitted in a residential district;
(5)
Outdoor lighting shall be shielded or directed away from adjoining residential property and streets;
(6)
Neighboring uses shall not be adversely affected; and
(7)
The property shall be returned to its original condition, devoid of temporary use remnants, upon termination of the temporary use period.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 8.5)
Sec. 110-57. - Parking lots designed for special events.
Parking lots designed for a special event in any zoning district may be approved for a maximum of 30 days.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 8.6.1)
Sec. 110-58. - Yard sales.
Yard sales for the purpose of relieving a household of goods and wares, operated from a residential property, are considered permitted accessory uses to a residence in any zoning district, provided the sale is held no more than three times in any calendar year, per address, nor more than one time in any one month, with a duration of no more than three consecutive days. No permit is required.
(Ord. of 2-3-2015, § 8.6.2)
