DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 94-26. - Scope.
This article shall define location, placement, installation, maintenance and relocation requirements of all public and private utilities placed within county right-of-way and along county-maintained roads within the unincorporated area of the county.
(Res. of 5-13-86)
Sec. 94-27. - Installation standards.
(a)
Aerial facilities.
(1)
No utility may occupy county rights-of-way unless sufficient space is available such that the free flow and safety of roadway traffic is not unduly impaired and the installation does not prevent the reasonable maintenance of the roadway.
(2)
Longitudinal installation shall be limited to single pole construction.
(3)
Joint use shall be encouraged.
(4)
Vertical clearance shall conform to the National Electrical Safety Code.
(5)
The location of poles, guys, etc., shall be located as close to the right-of-way line as possible.
(6)
Disturbed areas of right-of-way shall be properly regraded and regrassed with common Bermuda grass.
(b)
Underground facilities.
(1)
No open cuts in pavement shall be allowed except in unusual circumstances and with the specific approval and stipulation of the county manager.
(2)
Installation under pavement and paved drives shall be bored and jacked at least 30 inches under pavement.
(3)
Utilities shall be located as close to the right-of-way line as possible.
(4)
Underground installation shall be placed 30 inches below the surface.
(5)
Backfilling shall be accomplished in layers in order to reach a density of 95 percent. The top 12 inches shall be compacted to 100 percent of specific density.
(6)
The disturbed areas shall be graded, then sodded, sprigged or seeded with common Bermuda grass in rural areas. In residential areas, grasses shall conform to the adjacent property owners as established within the right-of-way.
(Res. of 5-13-86)
Sec. 94-28. - Cost allocation.
The entire cost of installing, maintaining, repairing, operating, or using poles, buried cable or pipelines or other facility shall be borne by the permitted applicant. Damage to county structures, pipes, culverts, bridges, slopes or improvements shall be replaced or repaired at the discretion of the county. Should the county select to perform repairs or replacements all costs associated with the damage shall be the responsibility of the applicant.
(Res. of 5-13-86)
Sec. 94-29. - Workmanship.
All work shall be done in a workmanlike manner as set out in this article.
(Res. of 5-13-86)
Sec. 94-30. - Safety.
The permittee under this article shall employ any and all methods in performing the operations authorized by the permit in order to protect the public from injury and the roadway from damage.
(Res. of 5-13-86)
Sec. 94-31. - Relocation of utilities.
If the county should decide to widen or relocate the existing roadway, it will be the responsibility of the utility owner to absorb all costs associated with the relocation.
(Res. of 5-13-86)
Sec. 94-32. - Protection of utilities.
In addition to the requirements of Chapter 94, each person responsible for any excavation or demolition operation shall:
(1)
Plan the excavation or demolition to avoid damage to or minimize interference with underground utilities in and near the construction area;
(2)
Taking into account the known limit of control of any cutting edge or point on mechanized equipment to be used in the excavation or demolition, maintain sufficient clearance between any underground utility and the cutting edge or point of the mechanized equipment as may be reasonably necessary for the protection of the utility;
(3)
Provide such support for underground utilities in and near the construction area, including during backfill operations, as may be reasonably necessary for the protection of such utilities; and
(4)
Prior to construction of underground facilities, mark the proposed location with white flags and white paint. The proposed location shall be marked before all other existing underground utilities have been marked in the work area covered by the proposed construction.
(Res. of 11-9-04(2))

DIVISION 2. - PERMIT

Sec. 94-51. - Required.
No person, partnership, corporation or public utility shall place any power line, telephone line, toll line, pipeline, water or sewer line or cable television line either above or below ground without the written approval of the board of commissioners or its designate. A permit application along with a detailed plan of installation shall be submitted to the board and approved prior to commencement of work.
(Res. of 5-13-86)
Sec. 94-52. - Application; review procedure.
Submittal of a permit application under this division shall be made to the director of public works who shall review the plans and route for conformity with this article. Upon completion of review of the permit request, the public works director shall recommend approval as submitted or stipulate within conformity with this article. The county manager shall have final approval of the installation application and shall return one copy to the requesting party.
(Res. of 5-13-86)
Sec. 94-53. - Permit fee.
There shall be no charge for utility placements which serve the citizens of the county.
(Res. of 5-13-86)
Sec. 94-54. - Revocation.
Any approved permit under this division shall be revocable at the discretion of the county manager for cause. If facilities either during or after construction become dangerous, unsafe or a nuisance to the motoring public or county maintenance personnel the facility permit shall be considered for revocation. The utility owner shall have ten days to correct dangerous or unsafe facilities. Roadway widening or relocation shall be considered a nuisance.
(Res. of 5-13-86)
