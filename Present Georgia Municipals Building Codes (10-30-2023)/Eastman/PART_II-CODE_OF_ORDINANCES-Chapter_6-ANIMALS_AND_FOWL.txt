ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 6-1. - Bird sanctuary—City designated.
The entire area embraced within the corporate limits of the city is hereby designated as a bird sanctuary.
(Code 1982, § 4-1; Ord. of 3-23-1959)
Sec. 6-2. - Same—Prohibited activities; abatement of nuisance.
It shall be unlawful to trap, hunt, shoot or attempt to shoot or molest in any manner any bird or wild fowl or to rob bird nests or wild fowl nests; provided, however, if starlings or other birds are found to be congregating in such numbers in a particular locality that they constitute a nuisance or a menace to health or property in the opinion of the proper authorities of the city, such authorities shall meet with representatives of the Audubon Society, Bird Club, Garden Club or Humane Society, or as many of said organizations as are found to exist in the city, after having given at least three days actual notice of the time and place of said meeting to the representatives of said organizations. If as a result of said meeting no satisfactory alternative is found to abate such nuisance, said birds may be destroyed in such numbers and in such manner as is deemed advisable by said city authorities under the supervision of the chief of police.
(Code 1982, § 4-2; Ord. of 3-23-1959)
State Law reference— Cruelty to animals, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-4.
Sec. 6-3. - Livestock at large.
It shall not be lawful for any owner or person in charge of any horse, mule, hog, sheep, goat or other livestock to permit or allow the same to be at large within the city at any time.
(Code 1911, §§ 149, 186; Code 1982, § 4-3)
State Law reference— Livestock running at large or straying, O.C.G.A. § 4-3-1 et seq.
Sec. 6-4. - Livestock—Turning out.
It shall be unlawful for any person to turn out onto the streets or other public places of the city, any livestock.
(Code 1911, §§ 151, 194; Code 1982, § 4-4)
Sec. 6-5. - Same—Impounding and sale.
The city police department shall impound all livestock found at large in the city and notify the owner (if found) as early as practicable. The owners of such impounded animals may, on proof of ownership, redeem the same by paying to the city $10.00 per head and all expenses of impounding, feeding and keeping the same, and not otherwise. The chief of police shall at once advertise in writing, by posting at three public places in the city, or by advertising one time in a newspaper of general circulation in the city, offering, on not less than three days' notice, for sale to the highest bidder, for cash, all livestock thus impounded and unredeemed, such notice to give description, marks and brand (if any), and if the same remains unredeemed at day of sale it shall be by the chief of police sold to the highest bidder for cash, and, after deducting the charges and expenses aforesaid, the remainder of purchase price shall be turned over to the owner of the livestock, if known; if no owner is found, the surplus to be turned over to the city treasurer for the use of the city.
(Code 1911, §§ 150, 151; Code 1982, § 4-5)
Sec. 6-6. - Poultry at large.
It shall be unlawful for any person to permit or allow any chicken or other poultry belonging to such person to go upon the streets or other public places in the city, or to permit or allow any chicken or other poultry belonging to such person to go upon lands of others in which such person has no ownership or right of possession.
(Code 1982, § 4-7; Ord. of 12-31-1945, §§ 1, 2)
Sec. 6-7. - Keeping hogs.
It shall be unlawful for any person to have or keep any hog at any place within the city.
(Code 1911, § 184; Code 1982, § 4-8)
Sec. 6-8. - Impoundment—Records.
The animal control officer designated by the city shall keep a record of all animals impounded and an accurate description of the same, when impounded, when released, when sold, to whoever sold and the price paid.
(Ord. of 7-22-1996, § 4-20)
Sec. 6-9. - Same—Fees.
The animal control officer designated by the city shall be entitled to impounding fees for equine animals, bovine animals, hogs, sheep or goats in an amount determined by the city council from time to time and listed in the schedule of fees and charges maintained in the city clerk's office.
(Ord. of 7-22-1996, § 4-21)
Secs. 6-10—6-38. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - DOGS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 6-39. - Definitions.
(a)
As used in this article, the following terms shall have the following meanings, unless the context clearly indicates that a different meaning is intended:
Board means the animal control board.
City governing authority means the city council.
Dangerous dog means any dog that, according to the records of an appropriate authority:
(1)
Inflicts a severe injury on a human being without provocation on public or private property at any time after March 31, 1989;
(2)
Aggressively bites, attacks, or endangers the safety of humans without provocation after the dog has been classified as a potentially dangerous dog and after the owner has been notified of such classification; or
(3)
That has been determined to be a dangerous or potentially dangerous dog under the authority of section 6-72.
Dog control officer means an individual selected by the city governing authority pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 4-8-22 to aid in the administration and enforcement of the provisions of this article.
Owner means any natural person or any legal entity, including, but not limited to, a corporation, partnership, firm or trust owning, possessing, harboring, keeping, or having custody or control of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog within this city.
Potentially dangerous dog means any dog that without provocation bites a human being on public or private property at any time after March 31, 1989, or has been determined to be such under the authority of section 6-72.
Property enclosure means an enclosure for keeping a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog while on the owner's property securely confined indoors or in a securely enclosed and locked pen, fence, or structure designed to prevent the dog from escaping. Any such pen or structure shall have secure sides and a secure top, and, if the dog is enclosed within a fence, all sides of the fence shall be of sufficient height and the bottom of the fence shall be constructed or secured in such a manner as to prevent the dog's escape either from over or from under the fence. Any such enclosure shall also provide protection from the elements for the dog.
Records of an appropriate authority means records of any state, county, or municipal law enforcement agency; records of any county or municipal animal control agency; records of any county board of health; records of any federal, state or local court; or records of a dog control officer as provided in this article and by the laws of the state.
Severe injury means any physical injury that results in broken bones or disfiguring lacerations requiring multiple sutures or cosmetic surgery or a physical injury that results in death.
(b)
The terms "dangerous dog" and "potentially dangerous dog" shall not include the following:
(1)
A dog that inflicts an injury upon a person when the dog is being used by a law enforcement officer to carry out the law enforcement officer's official duties; and
(2)
A dog who inflicts an injury within the meaning of this article if the injury was sustained by a person who, at the time, was committing a willful trespass or other tort, or was tormenting, abusing, or assaulting the dog or had in the past been observed or reported to have tormented, abused, or assaulted the dog or was committing or attempting to commit a crime.
(Ord. of 10-8-1990, § 4-27)
Sec. 6-40. - Rabies inoculation, tag and leashing required.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to cause, permit or allow any dog owned by such person to leave or be off of the premises of the owner or custodian unless the dog shall be wearing at such time a collar with a current rabies inoculation tag affixed thereto in such a manner as to be plainly visible to an observer. The dog shall be firmly held by a chain or leash (or under control), which chain or leash shall at all times be held by some person strong enough to control the movements of the dog.
(b)
The inoculation referred to in subsection (a) of this section shall be done each year.
(Ord. of 7-22-1996, § 4-22)
State Law reference— Requirement to regulate or license animals in the control of rabies, O.C.G.A. § 31-19-3.
Sec. 6-41. - Running at large; impoundment.
(a)
The fine shall be $50.00 for the first violation; $100.00 for the second violation and $300.00 for the third violation of this section.
(b)
Any dog found running at large within the city, and found without a collar and rabies inoculation tag affixed thereto, and not firmly held with a chain or leash, which chain or leash is held by a person strong enough to control the movements of such dog, shall be subject to be impounded by the city for a period not to exceed five days. Any dog found running at large within the city, and found with a collar and rabies inoculation tag affixed thereto, shall be subject to be impounded by the city for a period not to exceed five days. Such dogs, whether collared and tagged as directed by city ordinance or without collar and tag shall be subject to seizure by the enforcement officer and impounded by the city. During such period, the lawful owner may redeem such dog by paying the city $5.00 per day for the impounding. If such dog is not redeemed within the period, it may be destroyed by a licensed veterinarian.
(Ord. of 7-22-1996, § 4-23; Ord. of 2-10-1997)
Sec. 6-42. - Payment to veterinarian for keeping impounded dogs.
The city is authorized to pay the veterinarian keeping dogs impounded, as provided in this article, for the period not exceeding five days, at a rate in an amount to be determined by the city council from time to time and listed in the schedule of fees and charges maintained in the city clerk's office, should such dogs not be redeemed by their owners or be destroyed as provided in this article.
(Ord. of 7-22-1996, § 4-24; Ord. of 2-10-1997)
Sec. 6-43. - Preventing nuisance; restraint of dogs; impounding authorized.
(a)
The owner or keeper of each dog or cat within the city shall keep it from becoming a nuisance and from endangering or injuring any person.
(1)
Dogs shall at all times:
a.
Be kept within a proper enclosure from which it cannot escape;
b.
Be restrained upon the owner's property by a substantial chain, cable, rope or other device from which it cannot escape so as to not reach any adjoining property or public right-of-way; or
c.
Be restrained on a leash and accompanied by the owner or other responsible person.
(2)
Except in areas authorized for agriculture, the maximum number of dogs and cats that may be kept on a single premises shall not exceed three (exception: licensed veterinary hospitals, kennels and grooming parlors); A litter of cats or dogs will be permitted to remain on the premises for a period of eight weeks after their birth.
(3)
Animals committing the following acts shall be deemed a nuisance:
a.
Molests passersby or passing vehicles.
b.
Attacks other animals.
c.
Trespasses on private or public property.
d.
Is repeatedly at large.
e.
Damages private or public property.
f.
Barks, whines, or howls in an excessive, continual or untimely fashion.
(b)
Whenever a dog or cat is kept in such manner, as constitutes a nuisance, a proceeding to abate the nuisance may be brought by the party aggrieved, pursuant to law. An animal found by the court to constitute a nuisance may be impounded by the city if the owner, after reasonable opportunity to do so, fails to abate the nuisance as ordered by the court.
(c)
Whenever a dog is allowed to roam free, in violation of this section, impoundment of the animal is authorized. Upon identification of the owner, a citation shall be issued for violation of this section.
(Ord. of 9-9-2002)
Sec. 6-44. - Cleaning up after pets.
(a)
Any person owning, keeping, possessing or harboring any dog or cat shall promptly remove and dispose of all feces left by the dog or cat on any public property and on any private property not owned by such person or lawfully occupied by such person.
(b)
Any person, firm or corporation violating any provision of this section shall be fined not less than $25.00 or more than $100.00 for each offense.
(Ord. of 2-28-2000, § 4-25B, C)
Sec. 6-45. - Dogs in heat.
(a)
Any dog under any circumstances shall not be permitted to run at large on any public property or on any private property while in heat. Such animals shall be confined to an enclosed area at all times and, while in the process of breeding, shall be in a concealed area out of view of the public.
(b)
Any person, firm or corporation violating any provision of this section shall be fined not less than $50.00 or more than $500.00 for each offense, and a separate offense shall be deemed committed on each occasion on which a violation occurs.
(Ord. of 2-28-2000, § 4-25D, E)
Sec. 6-46. - Animal control board.
(a)
There is created and established an animal control board. The purpose of the board shall be to conduct the hearings provided for in section 6-71.
(b)
The board shall consist of three members. All members shall be appointed by the city governing authority. The initial terms of members shall be as follows: one member to serve for one year; one member to serve for two years; and one member to serve for three years. Thereafter, all members appointed to the board by the city governing authority shall be appointed for terms of three years and until their successors are appointed.
(c)
No member of the board shall participate in a hearing on any matter in which such member previously participated in the classification of the dog at issue. In the event that a member of the board is disqualified to participate in a hearing by reason of the foregoing provision, the city governing authority shall appoint an individual, pro tempore, to participate as a member of the board in any matter creating such disqualification of a member of the board. In the event of a vacancy during the term of any member by reason of death, resignation, or otherwise, the appointment of a successor by the city governing authority shall be for the remainder of the unexpired term of such member.
(Ord. of 10-8-1990, § 4-28)
Sec. 6-47. - Dog control officer.
(a)
The city governing authority shall appoint a dog control officer who shall serve at the pleasure of the city governing authority. The dog control officer's compensation shall be established from time to time by the city governing authority.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to knowingly hinder, interfere, harass or otherwise obstruct the performance of the dog control officer in the official performance of his duties and shall upon conviction thereof in the municipal court be punished as provided for in section 1-8.
(Ord. of 10-8-1990, § 4-29)
Sec. 6-48. - Article not to impose additional duties or waive immunity.
This article is adopted pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq., to implement the duties for which the city is responsible under state law. This article shall not be construed to impose any additional duties or liability on the city or its officers and employees or to waive any immunity which is currently provided by law.
(Ord. of 10-8-1990, § 4-34)

DIVISION 2. - DANGEROUS DOGS, POTENTIALLY DANGEROUS DOGS

Sec. 6-71. - Procedures for classifying dangerous dogs and potentially dangerous dogs; notice; hearing.
(a)
The dog control officer, upon receiving a report of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog within the city from a law enforcement agency, animal control agency, rabies control officer, or county board of health, shall make such investigations and inquiries with regard to such report as may be necessary to determine the validity of such report and whether the dog should be classified as a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog or reclassified as a dangerous dog if previously classified as a potentially dangerous dog.
(b)
Should the dog control officer determine that a dog is a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog, the dog control officer shall classify such dog accordingly. The dog control officer shall then notify the dog's owner of such classification. The notice to the owner shall meet the following requirements:
(1)
The notice shall be in writing and mailed by certified mail to the owner's last known address;
(2)
The notice shall include a summary of the dog control officer's findings that formed the basis for the dog's classification as a dangerous or potentially dangerous dog;
(3)
The notice shall be dated and shall state that the owner, within 15 days after the date shown on the notice, has a right to request a hearing on the dog control officer's determination that the dog is a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog;
(4)
The notice shall state that the hearing, if requested, shall be before the animal control board;
(5)
The notice shall state that if a hearing is not requested, the dog control officer's determination that the dog is a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog will become effective for all purposes on a date specified in the notice, which shall be after the last day on which the owner has a right to request a hearing; and
(6)
The notice shall include a form to request a hearing before the animal control board and shall provide specific instructions on mailing or delivering such request to the board.
(c)
When the animal control board receives a request for a hearing as provided in subsection (b) of this section, it shall schedule such hearing within 30 days after receiving the request. The board shall notify the dog owner in writing by certified mail of the date, time, and place of the hearing. Such notice shall be mailed to the dog owner at least ten days prior to the date of the hearing. At the hearing, the owner of the dog shall be given the opportunity to testify and present evidence. In addition, the board shall receive at the hearing such other evidence and hear such other testimony as the board may find reasonably necessary to make a determination either to sustain, modify, or overrule the dog control officer's classification of the dog.
(d)
Within ten days after the date of the hearing, the animal control board shall notify the dog owner in writing by certified mail of its determination on the matter. If such determination is that the dog is a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog, the notice shall specify the date upon which that determination is effective.
(Ord. of 10-8-1990, § 4-30)
Sec. 6-72. - Requirements for possessing a dangerous or potentially dangerous dog.
(a)
No one shall have, own, or possess within the city a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog without a certificate of registration.
(b)
The dog control officer shall issue a certificate of registration to the owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog if the owner presents to the dog control officer or the dog control officer otherwise finds sufficient evidence of:
(1)
A proper enclosure to confine the dangerous or potentially dangerous dog; and
(2)
The posting of the premises where the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog is located with a clearly visible sign warning that there is a dangerous dog on the property. The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall receive such sign from the dog control officer at the time the owner pays the annual registration fee as required in subsection (d) of this section.
(c)
In addition to the requirements of subsection (b) of this section, the owner of a dangerous dog shall present to the dog control officer evidence of:
(1)
A policy of insurance in the amount of at least $15,000.00 issued by an insurer authorized to transact business in this state insuring the owner of the dangerous dog against liability for any personal injuries inflicted by the dangerous dog; or
(2)
A surety bond in the amount of $15,000.00 or more issued by a surety company authorized to transact business in this state payable to any person injured by the dangerous dog.
(d)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall pay an annual registration fee at the time the annual certificate of registration is issued. The annual registration fee shall be in the amount of $100.00. Certificates of registration shall be renewed in the month of the initial registration.
(e)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall notify the dog control officer if the owner is moving from the city.
(f)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog who is a new resident of the state shall register such dog as required by this article within 30 days after becoming a resident of the state and the city.
(g)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog who moves to the city from any other municipality or county within the state shall register such dog as required by this article within ten days after becoming a resident of the city.
(h)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall notify the dog control officer within 24 hours if the dog is on the loose, is unconfined, has attacked a human, has died, or has been sold or donated. If the dog has been sold or donated, the owner shall also provide the dog control officer with the name, address, and telephone number of the new owner of the dog.
(Ord. of 10-8-1990, § 4-31)
Sec. 6-73. - Restrictions on permitting dangerous or potentially dangerous dogs outside of a proper enclosure.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for an owner of a dangerous dog to permit the dog to be outside a proper enclosure unless the dog is muzzled and restrained by a substantial chain or leash and is under the physical restraint of a responsible person. The muzzle shall be made in a manner that will prevent it from biting any person but not cause injury to the dog or interfere with its vision or respiration.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for the owner of a potentially dangerous dog to permit the dog to be outside a proper enclosure unless the dog is restrained by a substantial chain or leash and is under the restraint of a responsible person.
(Ord. of 10-8-1990, § 4-32)
Sec. 6-74. - Confiscation; grounds; disposition.
(a)
A dangerous dog shall be immediately confiscated by the dog control officer or by a law enforcement officer or by another person authorized by the dog control officer if:
(1)
The owner of the dog does not secure the liability insurance or surety bond required by section 6-72(c);
(2)
The dog is not validly registered as required by this article;
(3)
The dog is not maintained in a proper enclosure as defined in this article; or
(4)
The dog is outside a proper enclosure in violation of section 6-73(a).
(b)
A potentially dangerous dog shall be confiscated in the same manner as a dangerous dog if:
(1)
The dog is not validly registered as required by this article;
(2)
The dog is not maintained in a proper enclosure as defined in this article; or
(3)
The dog is outside a proper enclosure in violation of section 6-73(b).
(c)
Any dog that has been confiscated under the provisions of this section shall be returned to its owner upon the owner's compliance with the provisions of this article as determined by the dog control officer and upon the payment of reasonable confiscation costs. In the event the owner has not complied with the provisions of this section within 20 days of the date the dog is confiscated, the dog shall be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner.
(d)
In addition to the foregoing grounds for confiscation under subsection (a) or (b) of this section, a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog which aggressively attacks and causes severe injury or death of a human being, without provocation, under circumstances constituting a violation of this article, shall be immediately confiscated by the dog control officer or by a law enforcement officer or another person authorized by the dog control officer and placed in quarantine for the proper length of time as determined by the county board of health, and, thereafter, the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner.
(Ord. of 10-8-1990, § 4-33)
