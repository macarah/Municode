DIVISION 2. - MAYOR

Sec. 2-91. - Election and term.
(a)
The mayor shall be elected by a plurality vote for a four-year term and shall serve until his successor is elected and qualified as provided by statute.
(b)
The election for the mayor shall be from the city at large without restriction as to district.
(Code 1972, § 2-1)
Charter reference— Elections and term of mayor, §§ 5.1 et seq., 5.7.
State Law reference— Municipal office terms to be for four years, O.C.G.A. § 21-3-63.
Sec. 2-92. - Chief executive officer.
The mayor shall be the chief executive officer of the city.
(Code 1972, § 2-2)
Charter reference— Duties of mayor as chief executive officer, § 2.23.
Sec. 2-93. - Execution of deeds, checks, etc.
The mayor shall sign all deeds to real property sold by the city, including lots in the city cemeteries, and all checks and other obligations issued or authorized by the city council.
(Code 1972, § 2-3)
Charter reference— Mayor to sign contracts, ordinances and other instruments, § 2.23(4).
Sec. 2-94. - Absence from city.
It shall be the duty of the mayor to notify the mayor pro tem when he expects or intends to be absent from the city, and the mayor pro tem shall perform all of the duties of the mayor in his absence.
(Code 1972, § 2-4)
Charter reference— Mayor pro tempore, § 2.22(a); duties of mayor pro tempore, § 2.24.
Sec. 2-95. - Compensation of mayor; reimbursement of expenses.
(a)
The mayor shall receive $10,000.00 annually which shall be paid to the mayor in 12 equal monthly installments. This compensation is subject to all federal, state, and local taxes.
(b)
The mayor is entitled to reimbursement for all expenses incurred in the exercise of their responsibilities on behalf of the city. The mayor shall follow the procedures established by the city for all city employees for reimbursements.
(Code 1972, § 2-5; Ord. No. 03-17, § 2, 6-10-03)
Charter reference— Compensation and expenses, § 2.3.
Sec. 2-96. - Presiding officer; right to vote.
The mayor shall preside at all meetings of the city council and shall have the right to vote in all elections and upon all questions which may come before such body, except upon questions where disqualified by reason of interest or otherwise.
(Code 1972, § 2-6)
Charter reference— Similar provisions, § 2.23(1).
Sec. 2-97. - Emergency powers.
In addition to any and all powers enumerated in the City Code, the mayor, as the chief executive of the city, shall have such other powers as enumerated by state law and as delegated to the mayor in section 9-2 of the Code of Ordinances.
(Code 1972, § 2-7; Ord. No. 12-14, § 1, 8-28-12)
Charter reference— Emergencies, § 2.14.
State Law reference— Georgia Mutual Aid Act, O.C.G.A. § 36-69-2 et seq.; Georgia Emergency Management Act of 1981, O.C.G.A. § 38-3-1 et seq.; Interstate Civil Defense and Disaster Compact, O.C.G.A. § 38-3-70; authority to adopt temporary or experimental regulations to cover emergencies or special conditions, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-371(a)(19).
Sec. 2-98. - Mayor pro tempore.
There shall be elected from the city council a mayor pro tempore who shall be elected at the first regular meeting in January and shall hold his office for one year. He shall be clothed with the powers, privileges and duties of the mayor, when the mayor is absent from the city, is sick or for any reason is legally disqualified from acting.
(Code 1972, § 2-8)
Charter reference— Election and term of mayor pro tem, § 2.22.

DIVISION 3. - CITY ATTORNEY[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
Charter reference— Appointment, compensation, duties and responsibilities of city attorney, § 3.3.

Sec. 2-116. - Office created; appointment.
There is hereby created the office of city attorney, an executive office of the city. The attorney shall be appointed by the mayor, by and with the advice and consent of the city council.
(Code 1972, § 2-40)
Sec. 2-117. - Duty to prosecute and defend suits and actions.
The attorney shall prosecute or defend any and all suits or actions at law or equity to which the city may be a party, or in which it may be interested, or which may be brought against or by any officer of the city on behalf of the city, or in the capacity of such person as an officer of the city.
(Code 1972, § 2-41)
Sec. 2-118. - Enforcement of judgments, decrees, etc.
It shall be the duty of the attorney to see to the full enforcement of all judgments or decrees rendered or entered in favor of the city, and of all similar interlocutory orders.
(Code 1972, § 2-42)
Sec. 2-119. - Duty to render advice; written opinions.
The attorney shall be the legal advisor of the city, and shall render advice on all legal questions affecting the city, whenever requested to do so by any city official. Upon request by the mayor or by the council, he shall reduce any such opinion to writing.
(Code 1972, § 2-43)
Sec. 2-120. - Duty regarding special assessment, condemnation.
It shall be the duty of the attorney to see to the completion of all special assessment proceedings and condemnation proceedings.
(Code 1972, § 2-44)
Sec. 2-121. - Drafting of contracts, ordinances, etc.
It shall be the duty of the attorney to draft or supervise the phraseology of any contract, lease or other document or instrument, to which the city may be a party; and upon request of the council, to draft ordinances covering any subject within the power of the city.
(Code 1972, § 2-45)
Sec. 2-122. - Compensation.
The city attorney shall, for his retainers and advice, receive such compensation as may be determined by the city council each year. In all litigation or work not contemplated in fixing the retainer, or for which no adequate compensation is provided, he shall be paid such reasonable compensation as the city council may allow.
(Code 1972, § 2-46)

DIVISION 4. - CITY CLERK AND TREASURER[5]

Footnotes:
--- (5) ---
Charter reference— Appointment of clerk/treasurer, § 3.4.
Cross reference— City clerk designated as purchaser for city at tax sales, § 19-39.

Sec. 2-136. - Merger of offices; appointment.
The offices of city clerk and city treasurer shall be merged into one office and the respective duties of such offices shall devolve upon and be performed by one officer, bearing the title city clerk and treasurer who shall be appointed in accordance with provisions of the city charter.
(Code 1972, § 2-50)
Sec. 2-137. - Oath and bond.
Before entering upon the duties of his office, the city clerk and treasurer shall take the prescribed oath and be bonded at the city's expense in the amount of not less than $25,000.00.
(Code 1972, § 2-51)
Sec. 2-138. - Attendance at council meetings; council minutes and records generally.
The city clerk and treasurer shall attend all meetings of the city council, shall take the minutes thereof and shall record the same in suitable books. He shall keep, in connection with the book recording the proceedings of the council, an index book, in which shall be indexed the subject matter of the important proceedings by the council, with reference to the pages of the record book, where such matters appear. He shall read at each meeting of the council the minutes of the previous meeting, unless otherwise ordered, and shall preserve the original memoranda thereof for reference in case of need.
(Code 1972, § 2-52)
Sec. 2-139. - Communication of information to city council.
The city clerk and treasurer shall communicate to the city council, through the city manager, all information he may acquire which may be conducive to the efficiency of the city government and the welfare of the people.
(Code 1972, § 2-53)
Sec. 2-140. - Preservation, arrangement, indexing, etc., of papers, records and documents.
The city clerk and treasurer shall have charge of and be responsible for the preservation of all papers, records and documents of every description belonging to the city and shall arrange and file all such papers in such manner as will facilitate access to them, arranging and filing the same according to their subject matter in chronological order, numbering all files or cases in consecutive order. The city clerk and treasurer shall also keep an index book, in which shall be entered alphabetically the names or subject matter of such papers and documents and the number of the files or cases in which the same are to be found.
(Code 1972, § 2-54)
Sec. 2-141. - Issuance of notices.
The city clerk and treasurer shall issue all notices, personal and general, which the laws, orders and interest of the city require in the administration of its government.
(Code 1972, § 2-56)
Sec. 2-142. - Attestation of executions.
The city clerk and treasurer shall attest all executions issued by authority of the city council.
(Code 1972, § 2-57)
Sec. 2-143. - Attestation and sealing of deeds, contracts, etc.
The city clerk and treasurer shall attest and affix the city seal to all deeds to real property sold by the city, including lots in the city cemeteries, and all contracts, agreements and other obligations issued or authorized by the city council.
(Code 1972, § 2-58)
Sec. 2-144. - Official books relating to money and fiscal transactions.
The city clerk and treasurer shall keep a regular set of official books in which he shall enter all of the city's money and fiscal transactions of every description in plain detail without complexity of system. Among such official books shall be kept a book in which shall be set forth the form, the condition and the amounts of the debts owed by the city, the dates when such debts were contracted and the purposes for which they were incurred, the various rates of interest borne thereby and the respective times at which such debts shall become due.
(Code 1972, § 2-59)
Sec. 2-145. - Collection of taxes and other revenue and responsibility for sums collected.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the city clerk and treasurer to collect all taxes levied by the mayor and council according to the provisions of this Code and other ordinances of the city for the support of the city government, all sums paid as license taxes imposed by the city council for the privilege of conducting business within the city, or otherwise, and all sums realized under assessment levied by the city council for defraying the expenses of paving or otherwise improving the streets or sidewalks or for any other purpose.
(b)
In general, the city clerk and treasurer shall collect and receive the entire revenue of the city derived from any sources whatsoever. After such collection, the city clerk and treasurer shall be responsible to the city for all sums coming into his possession.
(Code 1972, § 2-60)
Charter reference— Collection of delinquent taxes and fees, § 6.9.
Sec. 2-146. - Deposit of funds; bank books.
(a)
The city clerk and treasurer shall deposit, to the credit of the city, in the banks designated by the city council as city depositories, all moneys received by him and belonging to the city on the same day he shall receive such moneys or on the following business day.
(b)
The city clerk and treasurer shall keep official bank books, wherein shall be entered, by the proper officers of the banks in which such deposits shall be made, acknowledgments of such deposits made by him, with the dates thereof, which bank books shall always be open to the inspection of the mayor and council.
(Code 1972, § 2-61)
Sec. 2-147. - Checkbooks and receipts; preservation and numbering of original accounts or demands.
The city clerk and treasurer shall keep checkbooks, with marginal entries on the stubs thereof showing the dates, numbers and amounts of all checks drawn by him on funds on deposit in the banks referred to in section 2-146 to the credit of the city, with the names of the persons in whose favor checks are drawn and for what purpose and to which department such checks shall respectively be chargeable. He shall, at the time of drawing such checks, take receipts for all moneys paid and he shall number such receipts in regular consecutive order, preserving and numbering the original accounts or written demands corresponding with such checks and receipts.
(Code 1972, § 2-62)
Sec. 2-148. - Disbursements by check or draft.
The city clerk and treasurer, the mayor and the city manager or any two of them acting together shall draw and sign all checks, drafts and/or wire transfers upon the funds of the city deposited in any bank to its credit. No money on deposit in any bank to the credit of the city shall be withdrawn from such bank or disbursed otherwise than through checks or drafts thus drawn and signed by the clerk and treasurer, the mayor and city manager.
(Code 1972, § 2-63)
Sec. 2-149. - Duties regarding paid bonds, coupons.
The city clerk and treasurer shall cancel, mutilate and preserve all city bonds and coupons which have been paid, and shall file, arrange, classify and index the same in such a manner as to render them readily available for future reference.
Sec. 2-150. - Monthly report to city manager.
The city clerk and treasurer shall make out and submit to the city manager for examination prior to the first regular monthly meeting of the council a report of his transactions as city clerk and treasurer for the preceding month.
(Code 1972, § 2-64)
Sec. 2-151. - Additional duties.
The city clerk and treasurer shall perform all other duties which may be imposed upon him by the laws of the state or the provisions of this Code and the ordinances or resolutions of the city or by the direction of the city manager.
(Code 1972, § 2-65)
