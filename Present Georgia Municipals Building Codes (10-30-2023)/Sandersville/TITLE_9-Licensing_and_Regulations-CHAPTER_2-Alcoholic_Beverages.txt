CHAPTER 2 - Alcoholic Beverages
State Law reference— Alcoholic beverage code, O.C.G.A., Title 3.
Cross reference— Excise taxes on alcoholic beverages, Sec. 3-2-1 et seq.
ARTICLE A - Malt Beverages And Wine
DIVISION 1 - General Provisions

Sec. 9-2-1 - Definitions.
The meaning of the terms used in this article shall be:
(1)
License. The authorization by the mayor and council to engage in the sale of malt beverages and wine.
(2)
Off-premises. Retail sale of malt beverages and wine packaged to go and not to be consumed on the premises where sold.
(3)
On premises. Retail sale of malt beverages and wine to be consumed on the definite closed or partitioned premises meeting the criteria of a restaurant as defined in paragraph (6).
(4)
Malt beverages. Fermented beverages made in whole or in part from malt, or any similar fermented beverages. However, no such malt beverages shall be sold under the provisions of this section which contain more than six percent (6%) of alcohol by volume.
(5)
Wine. All alcoholic beverages containing not more than twenty-one (21) percent of alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes, either by natural fermentation or natural fermentation with brandy added, including all sparkling wines, champagne, combinations of those beverages, vermouth, special natural wines, rectified wines and like products. The term wine shall not include cooking wines and like products which are mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render them unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at the point of the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this subsection.
(6)
Restaurant. Any public place, kept, maintained, advertised and held out to the public as a place where meals are served and where meals are actually and regularly served. Restaurant will further be defined as being an establishment in which fifty (50) percent of the money taken in by the establishment is from the sale of meals. Any such place must be provided with adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining room facilities and equipment and have a seating capacity of at least seventy-five (75) people. It shall also employ a sufficient number and kind of employees to prepare, cook and serve suitable good for its guests. At least one (1) meal a day shall be served at least four (4) days a week with the exception of holidays, vacations and periods of redecorating, and the serving of such meals shall be the principal business conducted, with the serving of malt beverages and wine to be consumed on the premises as only incidental thereto.
(Ord. of 6/18/68; 6/3/85; 7/2/90; Ord. of 10/15/01)
Sec. 9-2-2 - License required.
Every person, firm or corporation engaged in the sale of malt beverages and/or wine within the city, for consumption on or off the premises, shall be required to obtain a license before business may be commenced. This license shall be issued on an annual basis set forth in this chapter.
Sec. 9-2-3 - Display of license; multiple locations.
Every licensee shall be required to display his license in a prominent place. He shall be required to obtain a license for each place of business within the city.
(Ords. of 6/18/68 and 6/3/85)
Sec. 9-2-4 - License non-transferable.
A license issued under this chapter is not transferable to any new person, firm or corporation, unless the transfer is authorized by the mayor and council.
(Ords. of 6/18/68 and 6/3/85)
Sec. 9-2-5 - Inspection of licensed premises.
The business premises of the holder of a license issued under this article shall be open to inspection at any time during business hours by any member of the Police Department, Mayor and council, City Administrator or other city employee designated by the mayor.
(Ord. of 6/18/68; Ord. of 6/3/85; Ord. of 6/21/04)
Sec. 9-2-6 - Sale to minors.
(a)
No person engaged in the sale of malt beverages or wine shall sell or permit the sale of any malt beverages or wine to any person under twenty-one (21) years of age.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person under twenty-one (21) years of age to purchase malt beverages or wine. It shall also be unlawful for any person to purchase any malt beverages or wine for any other person who is under twenty-one (21) years of age.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person under twenty-one (21) years of age to exhibit any faked or borrowed credentials of any kind in an effort to purchase or for the purpose of purchasing any malt beverages or wine.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person under twenty-one (21) years of age to loiter in any establishment which sells malt beverages or wine.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for any person under twenty-one (21) years of age to have any malt beverages or wine in his or her possession.
(Ord. of 4/28/68)
Sec. 9-2-7 - Suspension; revocation of license.
(a)
Any license may be revoked or suspended by the Mayor and council for good cause after hearing upon three (3) days' written notice to the holder of such license of the time, place and purpose of the hearing and a general statement of the charges to be considered.
(b)
"Good cause" for the suspension or revocation of any such license shall consist of the violation of any laws or ordinances related to alcoholic beverages, violation of these regulations or other regulations made pursuant to authority granted for the licensing of such business, or for any change in a licensee's status which would preclude issuance of a license initially.
State Law reference— Due process requirements for revocation, O.C.G.A., Sec. 3-3-2.
Secs. 9-2-8—9-2-20 - Reserved.
DIVISION 2 - Off-premises Consumption

Sec. 9-2-21 - License fees.
(a)
The annual fees for the off-premises license required by section 9-2-2 shall be as fixed from time to time by the mayor and council. The fee shall accompany the application. The license fee shall be for the calendar year and shall not be prorated.
(b)
Wholesale licenses shall be issued only to those parties who are licensed by the State of Georgia to sell and distribute malt beverages and wine. Applications shall be made with the city clerk. The wholesale business license fees shall be as fixed from time to time by the mayor and council.
(Ord. of 6/18/68)
Sec. 9-2-22 - Qualifications of applicant.
(a)
Any applicant for a malt beverage or wine license must show, to the satisfaction of the mayor and council, financial responsibility.
(b)
Any applicant for a malt beverage or wine license must be of good character; and all managers, clerks or other employees shall be of like character. Corporation applicants shall be of good business reputation.
(c)
No license shall be granted to an applicant who has been convicted under any federal, state, or local law for a criminal offense involving moral turpitude, or who has been convicted under any federal, state, or local law of any criminal offense involving alcoholic beverages, gambling, or tax law violations, if such conviction tends to indicate that the applicant would not maintain the operation for which he is seeking a license in conformity with federal, state, or city laws.
(d)
No license shall be issued to an applicant who has not been a resident of the State of Georgia for more than 12 months preceding the filing of the application.
State Law reference— Due process requirements for grant or denial of license, O.C.G.A., Sec. 3-3-2.
Sec. 9-2-23 - Application required; investigation and report; action by mayor and council.
(a)
Persons desiring to sell malt beverages or wine shall file an application with the city clerk, who shall refer the same to the chief of police for investigation. The chief of police shall investigate: (i) the moral fitness and reputation of the applicant; (ii) his reputation as a peaceful citizen or law violator; (iii) the general character and reputation of his place of business; and (iv) any other facts that the mayor and council should know in considering the granting of a permit and license to the applicant.
(b)
It shall be the duty of the chief of police to report his findings to the mayor and council at the next meeting or as soon thereafter as possible, which body shall either grant or refuse the permit or license.
(c)
A notice of each new application for a new location shall be advertised in the official gazette of Washington County once a week for two (2) weeks immediately preceding the meeting of the Mayor and Council at which the application is to be acted upon. The cost of the notice shall be paid by the applicant.
(d)
Should the mayor and council deny the license application, a written notice of the decision, setting forth the reasons for denial, shall be mailed to the applicant. The applicant shall have the right to a full hearing before the mayor and council, with an opportunity to present evidence and to cross-examine opposing witnesses. The right to a hearing must be exercised by the applicant, in writing, within five (5) days of his receipt of the denial.
(e)
If the application is granted, the city clerk shall issue the license.
(Ord. of 10/15/01)
State Law reference— Due process requirements for grant or denial of license, O.C.G.A., Sec. 3-3-2.
Sec. 9-2-24 - Location of business.
No application for a malt beverage or wine license shall be approved for a location in violation of any regulation of the zoning ordinance of the city or in violation of any statutes of the State of Georgia. No location for a malt beverage or wine license shall be approved when such location is within 100 yards of a school or college. The distance shall be measured in a straight line from the nearest property line of the school or college to the nearest wall of the building of the proposed license location.
State Law reference— Additional distance requirements under state law, O.C.G.A., Sec. 3-3-21.
Sec. 9-2-25 - Size of locations.
Each store shall have a minimum of 400 square feet of store space and each location shall have two (2) mercury vapor lamps of sufficient voltage lighting the premises to be located in the front and rear of the store so as to adequately light the premises.
Sec. 9-2-26 - Employees.
No person shall be employed, as operator, manager, or clerk of a licensed location under this article who is not of good character or has a record of violations of alcohol control regulations.
Sec. 9-2-27 - Days and hours of operation.
(a)
Malt beverages or wine shall not be sold on Sundays or on Christmas Day.
(b)
Sales of malt beverages or wine shall be lawful during the polling hours of any election if the sale takes place at least 250 feet from open polling places.
(c)
Malt beverages and wine may be sold at any time except between the hours of 12:00 midnight Saturday and 8:00 a.m. the following Monday and on the days specifically prohibited in subsection (a).
Editor's note— Authority to permit election day sales, O.C.G.A., Sec. 3-3-20.
Sec. 9-2-28 - Advertising.
No malt beverage or wine licensee shall display in any form or in any manner whatsoever any malt beverage or wine price on the exterior of the premises at which the license is being exercised. No store location shall display signs or advertisement other than the name of the store in letters not over eight (8) inches high. Only two (2) signs shall be permitted.
(Ord. of 4/28/68)
Sec. 9-2-29 - Wholesale deliveries.
(a)
Malt beverages or wine shall be received at or delivered to the premises of a retail licensee only by a conveyance owned and operated by a wholesale dealer with a state permit.
(b)
Transportation of malt beverages and wine by any other means or the delivery by the wholesaler shall be grounds for revocation of the retail license or wholesale permit.
Cross reference— Excise tax reports and remittance by wholesale dealers, Sec. 3-2-2.
Sec. 9-2-30 - Reports by chief of police.
The Chief of Police shall promptly report, in writing to the Mayor and council, all cases made by the Police Department involving violations of City ordinances and state laws relating to the licensing, sale, purchase, and possession of malt beverages and wine, and all instances, known to the Police Department, in which disturbances have taken place in establishments where malt beverages or wine is sold under a license issued by the City.
(Ord. of 6/18/68)
Sec. 9-2-31 - Penalties.
(a)
Each violation of any provision of this article shall constitute an offense and every person violating any provision of this article shall be tried in the Municipal Court and, upon conviction, shall be punished as provided in Section 1-1-8 of this Code.
(b)
Licensees violating any provision of this article may have their license revoked or suspended pursuant to Section 9-2-7.
Secs. 9-2-32—9-2-40 - Reserved.

Alcoholic beverage shall include any distilled or fermented liquid containing consumable alcohol, mixed with water or other substance in solution, and includes brandy, rum, whiskey, gin, vodka, cordials or other spirituous liquors by whatever name called, and including fortified wines as defined by the federal administration.
License shall mean the authorization by the council to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages.
License for the sale of distilled spirits by the drink on the premises shall mean any person duly licensed to sell by the drink and for the consumption only on the premises.
Premises shall mean the definite or partitioned-in locality, whether a room, shop or building, wherein distilled spirits shall be sold and/or consumed.
Restaurant Any public place, kept, maintained, advertised and held out to the public as a place where meals are served and where meals are actually and regularly served. Restaurant will further be defined as being an establishment in which fifty (50) percent of the money taken in by the establishment is from the sale of meals. Any such place must be provided with adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining room facilities and equipment and have a seating capacity of at least seventy-five (75) people. It shall also employ a sufficient number and kind of employees to prepare, cook and serve suitable good for its guests. At least one (1) meal a day shall be served at least four (4) days a week with the exception of holidays, vacations and periods of redecorating, and the serving of such meals shall be the principal business conducted, with the serving of malt beverages and wine to be consumed on the premises as only incidental thereto.

The business premises of the holder of a license issued under this article shall be open to inspection at any time during business hours by any member of the police department, Mayor and Council, City Administrator or designee or other City employees designated by the Mayor.
No person engaged in the sale of alcoholic beverages shall sell, serve or permit the sale of alcoholic beverages to any person under twenty-one (21) years of age.

ARTICLE B Distilled Spirits
Cross reference— Excise tax on distilled spirits, Sec. 3-2-3.

Sec. 9-2-61 - Definitions.
For purposes of this chapter, the following terms shall have the meanings respectively ascribed to them:
(1)
Distilled spirits mean any alcoholic beverage containing alcohol obtained by distillation, mixed with water or other substance in solution, and includes brandy, rum, whiskey, gin, cordials, or other spirituous liquors by whatever name called, to include fortified wines as defined by Federal Alcohol Administration, but nothing in this article shall prohibit the sale of wines from natural fermentation of fruits, berries, and other products.
(2)
Liquor is used throughout this article and means distilled spirits as defined in paragraph (1) of this section.
(3)
Alcohol means the product of distillation of any liquid, whether rectified or diluted, whatever may be the origin thereof, and shall include synthetic ethyl alcohol.
(4)
Person means and includes any individual, partnership, corporation, or association.
(5)
Wholesaler or wholesale distributor mean any person, firm, or corporation engaged in distribution or selling to retailers for the purpose of resale any of the distilled spirits as defined in this article.
(6)
Retailer or retail distributor mean any person, firm, or corporation engaged in selling, at retail, any distilled spirits, for beverage purposes, in unbroken packages.
(Ord. of 12/4/78)
Sec. 9-2-62 - License required.
No person, firm or corporation shall engage in the sale of liquor within the City without first having obtained a license from the governing authority.
Sec. 9-2-63 - Qualifications of applicant.
(a)
No retail liquor license shall be granted to any person unless such person is of legal age and has been a resident of Sandersville, Georgia, for a minimum of six (6) months prior to filing an application for such license; the same requirements shall apply to a corporation.
(b)
Reserved.
(c)
No employee of the City shall be eligible to receive a retail liquor license.
(d)
Any applicant for a retail liquor license must be of good character, and all managers, clerks or other employees shall be of like character or the application will not be considered. Corporation applicants shall be of good business reputation.
(e)
No license shall be granted to an applicant who has been convicted under any federal, state, or local law for a criminal offense involving moral turpitude, or has been convicted under any federal, state, or local law of any criminal offense involving alcoholic beverages, gambling, or tax law violations, if such conviction tends to indicate that the applicant would not maintain the operation for which he is seeking a license in conformity with federal or state laws, or the laws of the City.
(f)
The governing authority, in their discretion, may consider any extenuating circumstances which may reflect favorably or unfavorably on the applicant, application or the proposed location of the business. If in their judgment circumstances are such that the granting of the license would not be in the best interest of the general public, such circumstances may be grounds for denying the application.

No person shall engage in the sale of liquor in the City of Sandersville without first complying with the rules and regulations set out in this article. No retail liquor license shall be granted to any person unless the entire business premises of the proposed location is in a portion of the City which is zoned commercial (C-1 or C-2) under the City zoning regulations, and is situated beyond one hundred (100) yards from the property line of any church or cemetery and beyond two hundred (200) yards from the property line of any school ground or college campus, the same to be measured by way of the nearest traveled road, street or highway, as provided by law. Each application for a retail liquor license shall include a scale drawing of the location of the proposed premises showing the distance to the nearest church, cemetery, or school or a certificate of a registered surveyor that such location complies with this article with reference to this section.
(Ord. of 12/4/78)
Sec. 9-2-73 - Each location requires separate license.
A separate retail liquor license shall be required for each place of business.
(Ord. of 12/4/78)
Sec. 9-2-74 - Building standards.
No retail liquor license shall be issued to any person unless the building in which the business will be located is complete and detailed plans of the building and outside premises are attached to the application, or unless proposed plans and specifications and a building permit of a proposed building to be built are attached to the application. The completed building or the proposed building shall comply with the ordinances of the City, regulations of the State Revenue Commissioner, and the laws of the State of Georgia. The proposed building shall also be subject to final inspection and approval when completed by the building inspector. Each building in which the business will be located shall contain sufficient lighting so that the building itself and the premises on all sides of the building are readily visible at all times from the front of the street on which the building is located so as to reveal the inside retail area of the building and so as to reveal all of the outside premises of said building. Each applicant for a retail liquor license shall attach to his application evidence of ownership of the building or proposed building or a copy of the lease if the applicant is leasing the building.
(Ord. of 12/4/78)

Sec. 9-2-77 - Deliveries off premises prohibited.
No retailer shall sell or deliver any liquor to any person except in the retailer's place of business.
(Ord. of 12/4/78)
Sec. 9-2-78 - Sales prohibited on certain days and during certain hours.
modified
(a)
No retailer shall be in his place of business, or open his place of business, or furnish, sell or offer for sale any liquor at any of the following times:
(1)
At any time on Christmas Day or on Sundays except between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m.
(2)
At any time in violation of a local ordinance, or regulation of a special order of the governing authority; or
(3)
On any day before 8:00 a.m. or after 11:45 p.m.
(b)
Sales of liquor shall be lawful during the polling hours of any election if the sale takes place at least two hundred fifty (250) feet from open polling places.
(Ord. of 10/19/99; Ord. No. 2021-02 , § 1, 4/5/21)
Sec. 9-2-79 - Sales to certain persons prohibited.
No retailer shall give, sell or offer to sell any liquor to any person who is under the age at which such beverages may be legally purchased in Georgia, or who is noticeably intoxicated, or who is of unsound mind, or who is a habitual drunkard whose intemperate habits are known to said retailer. At any time a state driver's license shall be considered the only legal proof of age which would relieve a person making a sale from responsibility therefor.
(Ord. of 12/4/78)
Sec. 9-2-80 - Posting of signs.
The holder of a license to sell liquor shall post in the most conspicuous place in his establishment a sign printed in letters at least four (4) inches high reading as follows: "SALE OF LIQUOR TO MINORS STRICTLY PROHIBITED."
(Ord. of 12/4/78)
Sec. 9-2-81 - Storage of inventory.
No licensed retailer shall keep any liquor stored in any bonded or other type warehouse in the City, nor shall he enter into any type of arrangement whereby liquor ordered by him is stored for him by any licensed wholesaler. A retailer shall keep no inventory or stock of liquor at any place except his licensed place of business, and within his licensed place of business his storage space for liquor shall be immediately adjacent to the room in which he is licensed to do business.
(Ord. of 12/4/78)
Sec. 9-2-82 - Age of employees.
No person under twenty-one (21) years of age shall be employed in any capacity in any place of business licensed under this article.
(Ord. of 12/4/78; Ord. of 10/19/99)
Sec. 9-2-83 - Gambling prohibited on premises.
There shall be no gambling, betting, games of chance, punch boards, vending machines, slot machines, pinball machines, lotteries, tickets or chances therein or the operation of any schemes for hazarding money or any other thing of value in licensed retailers place of business or in any room adjoining the same, owned, leased or controlled by the licensee with the exception of any state licensed games of chance and state lotteries which are specifically allowed by the State of Georgia. Any violation of this section shall be cause for suspension or revocation of the license.
(Ord. of 10/19/99)
Sec. 9-2-84 - Regulations kept on premises.
All holders of a license for the sale of liquor shall keep a copy of this article in his premises and shall instruct any person working there with respect to the terms hereof and each licensee or his agents selling liquor shall at all times be familiar with the terms hereof. The licensee shall be held responsible for any acts of his employees in any violation of this article or of the laws of the State of Georgia or the rules and regulations of the Georgia Revenue Commissioner.
(Ord. of 12/4/78)
Sec. 9-2-85 - Inspections by City; minimum inventory.
The business premises of the holder of a retail liquor license for the sale of liquor shall be open to inspection at any and all times by officers or officials authorized to conduct such inspections. Every applicant who is licensed must maintain a minimum of twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000.00) stock of goods, subject to inventory audit at the end of each month while the license is enjoyed by the licensee.
(Ord. of 12/4/78)
