CHAPTER 6 - Taxicabs

Sec. 9-6-1 - Definition.
The term "taxicab" as used in this chapter shall mean and include any vehicle used to carry passengers for hire but not operating on a fixed route.
(Ord. of 2/4/63)
Sec. 9-6-2 - License required; application procedure.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to engage in the business of operating a taxicab without first having secured a City license. Applications for such licenses shall be made in writing to the City Administrator or designee, and shall state the name of the applicant, the intended place of business, and the number of cabs to be operated. If the applicant is a corporation, the names and addresses of the corporate president and secretary shall be given.
(b)
No person shall drive a taxicab unless he shall have secured a license therefor as herein provided.
(Ord. of 2/4/63; Ord. of 6/21/04)
Sec. 9-6-3 - License fee.
(a)
The annual fee, as fixed from time to time by the Mayor and council, shall be payable in advance. Each taxicab must have a license.
(b)
The annual fee for a taxicab driver's license shall be as fixed from time to time by the Mayor and council.
(Ord. of 2/4/63)
Sec. 9-6-4 - Qualifications of operators.
No operator's license shall be issued to or held by any person who is not a person of good character or one who has been convicted of a felony. A license shall not be issued to or held by any corporation if any officer thereof would be ineligible for a license for the foregoing reasons.
(Ord. of 2/4/63)
Sec. 9-6-5 - Qualifications of drivers.
A license shall not be issued to any person who is not competent to operate a motor vehicle or who is not familiar with the traffic laws and ordinances. The chief of police shall examine each applicant for a taxicab driver's license to determine competency of the applicant, and no such license shall be issued except on certification of the Chief of Police that the applicant has demonstrated his ability to operate a motor vehicle as required.
Sec. 9-6-6 - Condition of taxicabs; state tag required.
(a)
No taxicab shall be operated unless it bears a state license duly issued.
(b)
No taxicab shall be operated unless it is equipped with proper brakes, lights, tires, horn, muffler, rear vision mirror, and windshield wipers in good condition.
(c)
It shall be the duty of the Chief of Police to inspect every taxicab so often as may be necessary to see to the enforcement of this section.
(d)
Each taxicab, while operating, shall have displayed either on the side or top, the name of the licensee operating it.
(Ord. of 2/4/63)
Sec. 9-6-7 - Insurance required.
No taxicab shall be operated unless it is covered by a public liability insurance policy in an amount as established as minimum coverage by state law for operation of motor vehicles.

Sec. 9-6-8 - Conduct of drivers.
(a)
A driver shall obey all traffic rules established by statute or ordinance.
(b)
No driver shall knowingly permit any taxicab to be used in violation of state or federal laws or local ordinances.
(c)
A driver shall accept as a passenger any person who seeks to so use the taxicab, provided such person conducts himself in an orderly manner. No person shall be admitted to a taxicab occupied by a passenger without the consent of the passenger.
(d)
The driver shall take his passenger to his destination by the most direct route available from the place where the passenger enters the cab.
(e)
A driver of a taxicab, while on duty, shall not drink any intoxicating liquor, or use any profane or obscene language, or shout or call to prospective passengers, or disturb the peace in any way.
(Ord. of 2/4/63)
Sec. 9-6-9 - Suspension and revocation of taxicab driver's license.
(a)
The Chief of Police shall have the right to suspend for a given number of days, or to revoke entirely, following ten (10) days' notice and hearing, any license issued under this chapter when in the opinion of the chief such suspension or revocation is needed to protect the health and welfare of persons and property. Sufficient reasons for suspension or revocation shall include, but not be limited to, the giving of false information on the application to obtain the license, or a renewal thereof; a failure to comply with the Code of Ordinances of the City; or a failure to maintain a record of safe and lawful driving.
(b)
In addition to other penalties provided in the Charter of the City, this Code of Ordinances, and laws of the state, the Mayor and Council may revoke a taxicab driver's license when the holder thereof is convicted of a violation of the Code of Ordinances of the City relating to traffic or operation of the taxicab or conduct of the business.
Sec. 9-6-10 - Penalty.
Any person, firm, or corporation violating any provision of this chapter shall be punished as provided in Section 1-1-8. A separate offense shall be deemed committed on each day during or on which a violation occurs or continues.
(Ord. of 2/4/63)
