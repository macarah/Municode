ARTICLE II. - LIVESTOCK RUNNING AT LARGE OR STRAYING

Sec. 10-21. - Legislative intent; county livestock pounds.
(a)
The county board of commissioners has found and declared a necessity for a uniform county livestock law embracing all public roads and property in the county.
(b)
The county commissioners shall provide, or contract for the provision of, truck transportation, establish and maintain suitable places for the keeping of impounded livestock until sold, redeemed or otherwise disposed.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-22. - Permitting livestock to run at large or stray; releasing impounded livestock; penalty.
(a)
No owner shall permit livestock to run at large on public roads or any property not belonging to the owner of the livestock except by permission of the owner of such property.
(b)
Any owner of livestock who intentionally or knowingly permits livestock to run at large on public roads or property not belonging to the owner, unless by permission of the owner of such property, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor. Upon conviction thereof, the owner of the livestock shall be punished as hereinafter set out in section 10-101 (penalties).
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-23. - Impoundment of livestock running at large.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the animal control officer to impound livestock found to be running at large.
(b)
Owners or operators of farms may also impound livestock. If an owner or operator of a farm impounds livestock, it shall be their duty to notify the owner of such livestock immediately. If the owner of the livestock is unknown and is not determined within three days, the person who impounds the livestock shall notify the animal control officer who shall transport the livestock to a county facility as provided for in section 10-21. The animal control officer shall then follow the procedure set out in this chapter as if the animal control officer had originally impounded such livestock. Owners or operators shall receive the feed and care fee allowed in section 10-30.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-24. - Caring for an impounded animal.
(a)
It shall be the duty of any person impounding an animal under section 10-23(b) to make reasonable and proper arrangements to provide the impounded animal with humane care and adequate and necessary veterinary services.
(b)
Any person impounding an animal under this article or providing care for an impounded animal shall have a lien for the reasonable costs of caring for such animal. Such lien may be foreclosed in any court that is competent to hear civil cases.
(c)
Any person impounding an animal under this article is authorized to return the animal to its owner:
(1)
Upon payment by the owner of all costs of impoundment and care;
(2)
Upon the entry of a consent order;
(3)
If the impounding entity fails to provide humane care to an animal, commits cruelty to animals, or engages in dog fighting.
(d)
A consent order shall provide conditions relating to the care and treatment of impounded animals, including, but not limited to:
(1)
The animal being given humane care and adequate and necessary veterinary services.
(2)
The animal will not be subjected to cruelty.
(3)
The owner complying with this article.
(e)
The provisions of subsection (c) of this section shall not apply to an animal that was an object or instrumentality of a crime nor shall any such animal be returned to the owner without the approval of the prosecuting attorney. An agency having custody of an animal that was seized as an object or instrumentality of a crime may, with the consent of the prosecuting attorney, apply to the court having jurisdiction over the offense for an order authorizing such agency to dispose of the animal prior to trial of the criminal case.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)

Sec. 10-26. - Redemption of livestock before sale.
(a)
The owner of impounded livestock shall have the right at any time before the sale to redeem the livestock by paying the impounding expenses, including fees, keeping charges, advertising or other costs incurred to the clerk of the court who shall pay all fees and costs allowed in section 10-30.
(b)
In the event there is a dispute as to the amount of such costs and expenses, the owner may provide bond, with sureties in an amount to be determined by the animal control officer, but not exceeding the fair cash value of such livestock, conditioned upon the payment of costs and damages. Within ten days after providing bond, the owner shall institute an action to have the dispute adjudicated by the court or referred to a jury if requested by either party to the action.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-27. - Disposition of livestock not sold at auction.
If there is no bidder for the livestock at the sale provided for in section 10-24, the animal control officer shall have the livestock slaughtered and shall dispose of the carcass. If there are funds received from the disposal, it shall be disbursed in the manner provided for in section 10-28. If there is no ready sale for the carcass, the animal control officer shall deliver the carcass to a public institution of the county or municipality within the county or to any private charitable institution, in this order, based on need.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-28. - Return and disposition of proceeds of sale.
(a)
The animal control officer, upon making a sale or other disposal, shall make a written report to the clerk of the court including an accurate description of the livestock sold or disposed of and if sold, to whom the livestock was sold and the sale price.
(b)
At the time of making his report, the animal control officer shall remit to the clerk of the court the entire proceeds of the sale. The clerk shall pay all costs and fees allowed in section 10-30. If there is a balance remaining, it shall be paid to the owner of such livestock subject to the owner providing satisfactory proof of ownership within 90 days from the date the animal control officer reports the sale.
(c)
If proof of ownership is not made within 90 days from the date the animal control officer reports the sale, the clerk of the court shall pay such proceeds into the fine and forfeiture fund of the county. The clerk shall keep a permanent record of all sales, disbursements and distributions made under this chapter. If the amount realized from the sale or other disposition of the livestock is insufficient to pay all fees, costs and expenses as provided for in section 10-30, the clerk shall pay the deficit from its fine and forfeiture fund.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-29. - Care of impounded animals; employment of guards.
The animal control officer shall ensure that animals have food and water provided not less than twice a day and shall see that all milk cows and milk goats are milked twice a day. The animal control officer shall employ persons as necessary to protect, feed and care for the impounded animals.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-30. - Fees for impounding, serving notice, care and feeding, advertising and disposing of impounded animals.
The allowable fees for impounding, serving notice, care and feeding, advertising and disposing of impounded animals shall be as follows:
(1)
Impounding - $10.00 per animal and mileage as provided by law for the arrest and commitment of prisoners.
(2)
Serving any notice and making report - $7.50 and mileage provided by law for executing writs in actions at law and filing a report upon the same.
(3)
Feed and care - $5.00 per day per animal.
(4)
Advertising or posting Notices of Sale - the same as provided by law for advertising property for sale under process.
(5)
Sale or other disposition - $5.00 per animal.
(6)
Report of sale - $2.50 per animal.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
