DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 34-1001. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Abandoned sign means a permitted sign which was erected on property in conjunction with a particular use which use has been discontinued for a period of 30 days or more, or a permitted temporary sign for which the permit has expired.
Administrator means that person designated by the city manager to administer and enforce the provisions of this article.
Banner means a flexible substance (such as cloth, plastic, or paper), typically square or rectangular in shape, on which copy or graphic may be displayed.
Changeable copy sign means a sign on which message copy is changed manually in the field through the utilization of attachable letters, numbers, symbols and other similar characters or changeable pictorial panels.
Commercial or industrial center means two or more separate occupancies located within the same or adjacent building or buildings on the same commercial or industrial plat of record.
Commercial sign or message means any sign containing a message that promotes a business or attempts to generate good will for a business; any message that advertises a product or service for sale; and any message that proposes a commercial transaction. Any sign containing any commercial message shall be regulated as a commercial sign. The content of commercial signs is not regulated by this article, but examples of commercial sign usage might be the establishment name, services or good offered by the establishment, logos or trademarks of the business; or information about the business such as whether an establishment is open or closed, its hours of operation, or specific services available. However, these examples are illustrative, rather than an imposition of any required content, and neither the contents nor the messages contained within a commercial sign are regulated by this article.
Dilapidated sign means any sign which is structurally unsound, has defective parts or is in need of painting or maintenance.
Directional sign means:
(1)
A sign, permanently erected and permitted, in the public right-of-way by the city or other governmental agency, or upon private property by the appropriate enterprise, to denote the name of any thoroughfare; the route to any city, town, village, educational institution, public building, historic place, shrine or hospital; to direct and regulate vehicular or pedestrian traffic; to denote any railroad crossing, bridge or other transportation company for the direction or safety of the public.
(2)
Signs erected and maintained by the city upon city property or right-of-way, upon which the city, for an annual fee, provides, attaches and maintains, uniform, multiple directional arrows, imprinted with minimal information consisting of the various businesses names.
(3)
A sign, notice or symbol for the information of the Federal Aviation Agency as to locations, directions, landings and conditions affecting safety in aviation.
Directory sign means a sign utilized by more than one business, activity or professional office for activity conducted within a building, group of buildings, or a commercial center.
Double-faced sign means a single sign, with two faces, having identical information of each, with no angle separating the two faces.
Driving area means those areas within the city not designated as "walking area" and are most commonly accessed through vehicular means, rather than pedestrian. The city commission finds that differing permitted signage size requirements are permissible in the driving area from the walking area, as areas most conducive to vehicular traffic, rather than the pedestrian traffic common to the walking area.
Electrical sign means a self-illuminated sign or sign structure in which electrical wiring, connections and/or fixtures are used as part of the sign proper.
Existing sign means any sign that was erected, mounted or displayed prior to the adoption of the ordinance from which this article is derived.
Facade means the entire building wall, including main street wall face, and parapet, fascia, windows, doors, canopy and roof on any complete elevation.
Flag means any unframed fabric or bunting, usually rectangular in shape, that is used as a symbol for a non-commercial message (ie. as of a nation, a signaling device, or as decoration).
Flashing sign means any lighted or electrical sign which emits light in sudden transitory bursts.
Freestanding sign means a sign supported by a sign structure secured in the ground and which is wholly independent of any building, fence, vehicle or other support.
Frontage means the length of the property line of any one premises parcel serving as public right-of-way line.
Frontage wall face means the building facade, excluding parapet, fascia, soffit, mansard and roof, which faces a frontage of the premises.
Height of sign means the vertical distance measured from the average elevation of the nearest road centerline to the top of the sign face or sign structure, whichever is greater.
Illuminated sign, external means any sign which is directly lighted by an external source.
Illuminated sign, internal means any sign which transmits light through its face or any part thereof.
Inflatable sign means a sign that is either expanded to its full dimensions or supported by gasses contained within the sign or sign parts at a pressure greater than atmospheric pressure.
Ingress/egress sign means a sign which designates only the direction of ingress or egress of a parking area or driveway or business, such as "Enter," "Exit," "One Way," "Do Not Enter," etc.
Moving message board means any electrical sign having a continuous message flowing across its face by utilization of lights or other electrical impulses forming various words or designs.
Noncommercial message means any message that is not a commercial message.
Nonconforming sign means any sign which has a valid permit, was erected or displayed prior to the effective date of the ordinance from which this article is derived, or any subsequent amendments thereto and does not conform with any provisions of this article.
Occupancy means any use of or activity upon the premises.
Off-premises sign means any sign located or proposed to be located any place other than within the same platted parcel of land on which the specific business or activity being promoted on such sign is itself located or conducted. For purposes of this article, easements and other appurtenances shall be considered to be outside such platted parcel of land and any sign located or proposed to be located in such easement or other appurtenance shall be considered an off-premises sign.
On-premises sign means any sign located or proposed to be located any place, if otherwise permitted by this article, within the plat of record for the business or other activity identified on such sign.
Painted wall sign means a sign painted directly on any exterior building wall or door surface.
Panel means the primary surface of a sign upon which the message of the sign is carried.
Pennants and balloons means any animated, rotating, fluttering or non-stationary device made of flexible materials designed to attract attention.
Permitted sign means a sign for which a valid permit has been issued.
Portable sign means any sign which is not permanently affixed to a building, structure or the ground, or which is attached to a motor vehicle.
Premises means the plat of record which is affected either directly or indirectly by the contents of this article.
Project sign means any sign erected and maintained on the premises temporarily while undergoing construction by an architect, contractor, developer, finance organization, subcontractor or materials vendor upon which property such individual is furnishing labor, services or material.
Public right-of-way line means the line where the property meets the public right-of-way at a public street or public waterway, provided that this definition shall not include unimproved alleys, easements or other similar dedicated uses.
Public way means any street, highway, road, path or right-of-way, whether privately or publicly owned, which is designed or used for vehicular or pedestrian traffic, either by public right or custom or by invitation of two or more common owners.
Pylon sign means a sign advertising multiple businesses, with each individual business sign stacked one upon the other.
Real estate sign means a temporary sign erected by the owner, or his agent, advertising the real property upon which the sign is located for rent, lease or sale.
Roof means the exterior upper covering of the top of a building, or a mansard.
Roof sign means any sign erected on or over, and wholly or partially dependent upon the roof of any building for support, or attached to the roof in any way.
Rotating sign means any sign which revolves around one or more fixed areas.
Shadow box means a sign utilized for menus and specials by establishments offering food and beverage as their primary business purpose.
Sidewalk or sandwich sign means a movable sign not secured or attached to the ground or any building or structure.
Sign means any device or presentation for visual communication used for the purpose of bringing the subject to the attention of others which is located on or attached to premises, real property, structures on real property or a vehicle.
Sign, area of means the square-foot area enclosed by the perimeter of the sign face. On signs which are composed of individual symbols, letters, figures, illustrations, messages, forms or panels, the sign area shall include all lettering, wording and accompanying designs and symbols, together with the background on which they are displayed, and frame around the sign and cutouts or extensions, but shall not include any supporting structure or bracing.
Sign face means the part of the sign that is used, or can be used, to identify or communicate information or for visual representation which attracts the attention of the public for any purpose. Sign face includes any background material, panel, trim or color that differentiates the sign from the building, structure, backdrop surface or object upon which or against which it is placed. The sign structure shall not be included as a portion of the sign face, provided no message, symbol or any of the aforementioned sign face criteria are displayed on or designed as part of the sign structure.
Sign structure means a supporting structure erected, used or intended for the purpose of identification or attracting attention, with or without a sign thereon, situated upon or attached to the premises, upon which any sign may be fastened, affixed, displayed or applied. This definition shall not include a building, fence, wall or earthen berm.
Snipe sign means a sign which is tacked, nailed, posted, pasted, glued or otherwise attached to trees, poles, stakes or fences, or to other objects, and the advertising matter appearing thereon is not applicable to the present use of the premises upon which such sign is located.
Special event sign means a sign which carries a message regarding a special event or function which is of general interest to the community.
Swinging sign means a sign installed on an arm, mast or spar that is not, in addition, permanently fastened to an adjacent wall or upright pole.
Temporary sign means any sign or information transmitting devise intended to be erected or displayed for a limited period of time.
Traffic direction/Safety sign means a sign which is on the premises which is designed, sized, and erected for the purpose of vehicular or pedestrian traffic or safety.
Vehicle sign means a permanent or temporary sign affixed, painted on or placed in or upon any vehicle legally and physically capable of being driven upon the public ways of this state, or in or upon a trailer or other device capable of being legally and physically towed upon the public ways of this state. Specifically excluded from this definition is a sign upon or in a vehicle, trailer, or other device which is no longer physically or legally capable of being driven or towed upon the public ways of this state, and has, as its principal function or primary purpose, the display or attraction of the attention of the public rather than to serve the business or the owner thereof in the manner which is customary for the vehicle, trailer, or device.
Walking area as referenced in this article, means those commercial areas within the city most conducive to pedestrian traffic by the visitors to the city and its residents, and as such, subject to differing signage size requirements than the "driving area" of the city for aesthetic purposes in the promotion of the city's tourism industry. The walking area is further defined as follows:
(1)
Main Street: Bounded by Munich Strasse to Spring Street;
(2)
River Street: Bounded by Main Street to Unicoi Strasse;
(3)
Dye Street: Bounded by Main Street to Unicoi Strasse;
(4)
Spring Street: Bounded by Main Street to Unicoi Strasse;
(5)
East side of Munich Strasse: Bounded by Main Street to The Helendorf Inn (33 Munich Strasse); and
(6)
Chattahoochee Strasse: Bounded by Main Street to Schloss Weg.
Wall graphics means artwork, excluding lettering except for the identification of the artist, applied upon a wall.
Window graphics means artwork, excluding lettering except for the identification of the artist, applied upon a window.
Window sign means any sign which is painted on, applied to, attached to or projected upon or within the exterior of a building glass area, including doors, whose identification, message, symbol, insignia, visual, representation, logotype or any other form which communicated information can be read in such a manner within the building that it can be seen from the exterior of the structure through a window or door and is designed to attract attention from the exterior as such. This definition is specifically intended to include illuminated signs located within the interior of premises, but which signs are designed to be seen from and attract attention from the exterior of the structure through either a window or door.
Window sign, temporary means a window sign of a temporary nature used to direct attention to the sale of merchandise, or a change in the status of the business, including, but not limited to, sign for sales, special and grand openings.
(Ord. No. 05-01-01, 2-15-05; Ord. No. 11-06-02, 7-19-11; Ord. No. 12-09-02, 9-18-12; Ord. No. 13-06-03, 7-16-13; Ord. No. 14-01-01, 2-18-14 ; Ord. No. 16-06-01 , 6-21-16)
Sec. 34-1002. - Penalties.
(1)
The city manager, code enforcement officer, or other personnel authorized by the governing authority, are authorized to issue a citation for violation of this article, and/or institute an injunctive proceeding and/or remove the sign if within a right-of-way pursuant to section 34-1032 of this chapter or if said sign does not have a proper permit and is violation of this chapter, and/or proceed with other appropriate action or proceedings to prevent the violation of this article.
(2)
Any citation issued for violation of this article shall be taken to the municipal court of the city and prosecuted as any other criminal citation within the municipal court.
(3)
The maximum punishment imposed for a violation of this article shall not exceed a fine of $1,000.00 or six months imprisonment or both.
(4)
Any suit for injunction filed on behalf of the governing authority as authorized above shall be filed in a court of competent jurisdiction and prosecuted as any other suit for injunction within that court as allowed by law.
(5)
Any citation, injunction, or other appropriate action or proceeding to prevent the violation of this article may be taken against the owner or erector of the sign, the owner of the property on which the sign is located, and/or any other person or entity that has an ownership interest in the sign or property.
(6)
Each day such violation is committed, or permitted to continue, shall constitute a separate offense and shall be punishable as such.
(7)
All remedies and penalties specified in this article are cumulative.
(Ord. No. 05-01-01, 2-15-05; Ord. No. 11-06-02, 7-19-11)
Sec. 34-1003. - Interpretation.
(a)
The standards and provisions of this article shall be interpreted as being the minimum requirements necessary to uphold the purposes of this article.
(b)
Whenever this article imposes a higher standard than required by any other city ordinance or requirement, the provisions of this article shall govern. Whenever any other city ordinance or requirement imposes a higher standard than required by this article, the provisions of such city ordinance or requirement shall govern.
(c)
Whenever this article imposes a higher standard than required by easements, covenants or agreements, the provisions of this article shall govern.
(d)
When the provisions of any applicable state or federal statute imposes a higher standard than required by this article, the provisions of such statute shall govern.
(Ord. No. 05-01-01, 2-15-05)
Sec. 34-1004. - Purpose.
The City Commission finds that the purpose of this article is for:
(1)
Safety.
a.
Do not create traffic hazards by distracting or confusing motorists, impairing motorists' ability to see pedestrians, other vehicles, obstacles, or to read traffic signs;
b.
Do not create a hazard due to collapse, fire, collision, decay or abandonment; and
c.
Do promote the aesthetics, safety, health, morals and general welfare and the assurance of protection of adequate light and air within the city by regulating the posting, displaying, erection, use and maintenance of signs.
(2)
Information. Promote the efficient transfer of general public and commercial information through the use of signs.
(3)
Public welfare. Protect the public welfare and enhance the overall appearance and economic value of the landscape and preserve the unique natural environment that distinguishes the city. It is also acknowledged that the city's economic wellbeing is heavily dependent upon the resort and tourism industry. This dependence makes the preservation of the environment from unreasonable signage a matter of critical importance to everyone.
(4)
Aesthetics. The designation of the city as Georgia's Alpine Village is well known, and the presentation to visitors of the Bavarian theme is a vital economic component of the city; such that the proliferation of signs without regulation would have an adverse economic impact on the city.
Accordingly, the city commission finds that by minimizing the proliferation of signs through the restrictions contained in this article; that the rights of individuals to convey their message through signs is preserved, while simultaneously protecting residents and visitors to the city to be protected from adverse effects of an unrestricted proliferation of signs.
(Ord. No. 05-01-01, 2-15-05; Ord. No. 16-06-01 , 6-21-16)
Sec. 34-1005. - Prohibited signs.
Except as may be specifically permitted hereinafter, it shall be unlawful for any person to erect, place or use within the city, when visible from any public way, any of the following signs:
(1)
Snipe signs;
(2)
A sign which contains any moving, flashing, animated lights, visible moving or moveable parts, or giving the appearance of animation;
(3)
Roof signs;
(4)
Any sign which emits a sound, odor or visible matter;
(5)
Any sign which obstructs free ingress to or egress from a required door, window, fire escape or other required exit way;
(6)
Any sign and/or sign structure which obstructs the view of, may be confused with, or purposed to be a governmental or traffic directional/safety sign;
(7)
Any sign or sign structure, other than freestanding, which extends above the parapet or building roofline against which the sign is located;
(8)
Signs using the words "stop," "danger" or any other word, phrase, symbol or character in a manner that misleads, confuses or distracts a vehicle driver;
(9)
Moving message signs;
(10)
Except as otherwise provided, no sign, whether temporary or permanent, except by a public agency, is permitted within any street or highway right-of-way;
(11)
Signs painted on or attached to trees, fence posts, rocks or other natural features, telephone or utility poles or painted on the roofs of buildings visible from any public way;
(12)
Abandoned or dilapidated signs;
(13)
Any sign which exhibits statements, words or pictures of obscene or pornographic subjects or material;
(14)
Portable signs;
(15)
Inflatable signs;
(16)
Finger or pennant streamers;
(17)
Paper signs or paper advertising on the exterior of building or on the interior of a window or door, or within three feet of a window or door, so as to be visible from the outside; or
(18)
Reserved.
(19)
A vehicle sign, when the vehicle is (a) not regularly used in the conduct of the business or activity advertised on the vehicle, and (b) is visible from a public right-of-way. A vehicle shall not be considered as "regularly used in the conduct of the business or activity" if the vehicle is used primarily (i) for advertising, or (ii) for the purpose of advertising.

Sec. 34-1007. - City sponsored directional fee signs.
(a)
The city may, but shall not be required to, erect, replace, repair, and maintain one or more structures upon which the city may affix, for a fee, signs identifying private commercial business within the city, and directing traffic in the direction of such business.
(b)
There shall be multiple signs affixed to the same structure, on a first come, first served basis.
(c)
All such signs shall be of a uniform size, material, coloring, and lettering.
(d)
No business owner or operator may affix, remove, or modify any sign upon any such structure, and the entirety of the structures, and any signs affixed thereto, shall be the sole property of the city.
(e)
The city shall establish, and revise from time to time as necessary:
(1)
An initial fee to cover the cost of manufacture, installation, affixation, and related expenses, of signs and sign structures; and
(2)
An annual renewal fee to cover the cost of repair, maintenance, and replacement, of signs and sign structures.
(f)
No business owner or operator shall have a sign affixed to such sign structure, unless and until, such owner or operator shall have paid the initial fee, any renewal fee, is current on payment of all business license fees, occupation tax, and any other tax or fees owed to the city, and completed an application for affixation of such sign upon forms provided by the city manager.
(g)
Upon implementation of this section, all previous and similar city structures and signs shall be permanently removed.

DIVISION 2. - ADMINISTRATION AND ENFORCEMENT
Subdivision I. - General Provisions

Sec. 34-1026. - Authority to issue notices—Sign maintenance requirements.
(a)
Compliance. It is unlawful for any person to place, erect, locate, or allow any sign within the city that does not comply with the provisions of this chapter.
(b)
Permit required. Except for those signs exempted from permitting under this chapter, it shall be unlawful for any person to erect, repair, alter, replace, or relocate within the city any sign without first following the permit process in sections 34-1051 through 34-1100 of this chapter.
(c)
Interpretation and authority. The city manager and duly authorized officers shall have the authority to issue sign violation notices and citations, identifying a sign which is not in compliance as to the requirements, standards, definitions or any other provisions of this chapter.
(d)
Sign maintenance. Any signs not meeting the following provisions shall be repaired or removed in accordance with the specifications of this chapter.
(1)
The area around the sign on private property on which the sign is erected shall be maintained clear of brush, trees, or other obstacles such that the sign is readily visible.
(2)
All burned out bulbs (if the sign falls within the permitted category of illuminated signs which are permitted under section 34-1102 of this chapter) must be replaced.
(3)
All panels or portions of any sign which are damaged must be replaced.
(4)
All sign copy shall be maintained securely to the face of the sign, and all missing copy must be replaced.
(5)
All existing signs must be maintained in good repair, and will be subject to annual examination in accordance with subsection 34-1034(a) of this chapter.
(6)
All signs shall be designed, constructed, installed and maintained in compliance with the applicable building and electrical codes and in accordance with any applicable state law governing sign placement and sign characteristics.

Sec. 34-1028. - Nonconforming signs.
(a)
Any sign erected or existing as of the date of the first reading of the ordinance from which this article is derived, and which has a valid permit from the city, but which does not conform to the provisions of this article is hereby deemed to be a non-conforming sign. Any alteration, relocation, replacement, or redesign of a sign other than for the permitted maintenance purposes described above will render the prior permit void, and such sign shall be reclassified as an illegal sign pursuant to section 34-1029. (b)
Any nonconforming sign may become a legal sign if, after compliance with the provisions of this article, it is brought into conformity.
(Ord. No. 05-01-01, 2-15-05; Ord. No. 16-06-01 , 6-21-16)

Subdivision II. - Permit

Sec. 34-1051. - Required; exceptions.
(a)
Unless specifically exempted by this section, no sign or sign structure shall be erected, displayed, moved, relocated or altered until a permit fee has been paid and a sign permit issued by the planning and design review board.
(b)
A permit is not required for the following types of signs:
(1)
An official sign or notice issued by any court, public agency or office.
(2)
A traffic directional, warning or information sign authorized by any public agency.
(3)
A private street or road name sign or a traffic directional sign which does not exceed one square foot per sign face.
(4)
Current real estate signs, not more than one on each road frontage, advertising property for rent, lease, or sale, not to exceed nine square feet per sign face, provided such sign(s) are removed within 30 days of the completion of the advertised transaction.
(5)
An ingress/egress sign which does not exceed one square foot per sign face.
(6)
Temporary holiday decorations, which do not contain advertising, provided such decorations are installed no more than 45 days prior to the holiday, and removed not more than ten days following the holiday.
(7)
Flags which are not utilized for a commercial message and which are maintained in good repair, provided that the flag is either attached to a flagpole mounted in the ground or attached to a structure, and the flagpoles shall conform to the applicable setback restrictions of the zoning district. The location and flagpole must be placed in an area that does not create any traffic safety issue, and shall not exceed the height of the primary structure on the lot.
(8)
Banners, maintained in good repair, flown for holidays; citywide special events which involve the city as a whole, such as Fasching, Mayfest, and Oktoberfest; or permitted banners for permitted special events.
(9)
Vehicle signs, if such vehicle is regularly used in the conduct of the business or activity advertised on the vehicle. A vehicle shall not be considered as "regularly used in the conduct of the business or activity" and this permit exemption shall not apply if the vehicle is used primarily, (i) for advertising, or (ii) for the purpose of advertising.
(10)
Patio Umbrellas.
(11)
All signs containing non-commercial messages only. However, any temporary sign which does not require a permit because it contains a non-commercial message and which pertains to an event to be concluded on a date certain shall be removed within 14 days after the conclusion of the event.
(c)
A permit is not required prior to engaging in sign alterations if such alteration involves only:
(1)
The changing of copy on a permitted changeable copy sign; or
(2)
The painting or refinishing of the surface of a sign face or sign structure of a permitted sign to keep the appearance of such sign as it existed on the date such sign received a permit.

Sec. 34-1052. - Application.
(a)
Where to apply. Any application for a sign permit shall be submitted to the city manager on a form supplied by the city.
(b)
Information required. The following information shall be submitted with the application for a sign permit:

Name, address, telephone number and city occupation tax license number of sign permittee;

Upon compliance with the provisions of this article, the planning and design review board shall issue a permit for such sign or sign structure within 45 days of receipt of a completed permit application. Failure to comply with any provision of this article shall result in a denial of the application.
(Ord. No. 05-01-01, 2-15-05)
Sec. 34-1054. - Expiration.
A sign permit shall be valid for a period of 90 days from the date of its issuance and noted on the permit, unless a 30-day extension is granted for good cause shown by the applicant. Failure to complete approved work related to the permitted sign within such 90-day period shall result in the expiration of the permit.
(Ord. No. 05-01-01, 2-15-05)
Sec. 34-1055. - Appeal and variance.
(a)
Denial of application. In the event of denial by the planning design and review board of a sign permit application, an appeal may be made to the city commission. The applicant may appeal to the city commission for relief based on:
(1)
Grounds that his sign application met the criteria of the Helen Municipal Code as presented.
(2)
To request a variance from the Helen Municipal Code.
(b)
If a variance is requested, the planning design and review board shall deny the application, but make a recommendation to the city commission, based on information submitted by the applicant and review by the city building and zoning inspector, as to whether the city commission should approve a variance request. Variance requests should only be approved in such circumstances where:
(1)
The property itself, or a building being constructed on said property, results in extraordinary conditions in which a variance is justified because of the size, shape, location, or topography of the property or building.
(2)
The application of this article to this particular piece of property or building would create an unusual hardship on the applicant.
(3)
If the size, shape, or location of the building being constructed (or a modification of an existing building) will cause the hardship, the request for a sign application variance must be made as a part of the building permitting process.
(4)
Variances should not be granted where the extraordinary conditions the applicant relies on are the result of actions of the applicant or the owner of the property.
The decision of the city commission with regard to variance requests will be a final determination from the city. The city commission shall make a decision on a variance request within 30 days of the recommendation of the planning design and review board.
(Ord. No. 16-06-01 , 6-21-16)

DIVISION 3. - STANDARDS AND REQUIREMENTS

Sec. 34-1101. - Setback from right-of-way, and visual restrictions.
No portion of a sign or sign structure shall be allowed on any city right-of-way without special approval of the city commission, and not withstanding any other size restrictions, and in addition thereto, neither the sign structure nor sign face may obstruct or impair safe vehicular or pedestrian traffic.
(Ord. No. 05-01-01, 2-15-05)
Sec. 34-1102. - Sign illumination.
(a)
In the non-residential zoning districts, up to one lighted commercial sign which is internally lit or is composed of lighted elements may be permitted and approved. The dimension of the sign face shall not exceed four square feet in the "walking area" of the city; or six square feet in the "driving area" of the city, as those terms are defined in Code section 34-1001. Additionally, hotels, motels, or other rentals providing overnight accommodations may also display one additional illuminated commercial sign which does not exceed seven inches in height.
(b)
Light sources to illuminate signs shall be shielded from all adjacent residential buildings and streets and shall not be of such brightness to cause glare hazardous to pedestrians or auto drivers or to create a nuisance to adjacent property owners or businesses.
(Ord. No. 05-01-01, 2-15-05; Ord. No. 14-01-01, 2-18-14 ; Ord. No. 16-06-01 , 6-21-16)
Sec. 34-1103. - Materials and style.
(a)
All signs will be constructed of wood or a suitable substitute approved by the planning and design review board. An internally illuminated sign may also be approved by the planning and design review board as authorized in the code.
(b)
Signs shall not be light-reflecting.
(c)
Any double-faced sign shall be identical on both faces.
(d)
Colors shall comply with the applicable city color chart.
(e)
Business signs shall utilize those fonts approved on the chart shown on City of Helen Font Chart. A copy of this font chart is maintained in Helen City Hall. Due to the physical nature of internally illuminated signage, this font requirement shall not be imposed thereon.
(Ord. No. 05-01-01, 2-15-05; Ord. No. 12-08-01, 9-18-12; Ord. No. 14-01-01, 2-18-14 )
Sec. 34-1104. - Dimensions and quantity.
The dimensions and quantity of any sign face shall conform with the following:
(1)
Each individual business in the non-residential zoning districts of the city will be authorized two non-illuminated commercial signs in the following dimensions: Signs in the walking area of the city (as defined in Code section 34-1001) shall not exceed six square feet. Signs in the driving area shall not exceed 24 square feet.
(2)
Residential developments shall be permitted one freestanding sign, of not more than 32 square feet, at primary vehicle entrance plus one additional sign, of not more than 16 square feet, at any secondary vehicle entrance.
(3)
Commercial developments, which contain at least 2,500 square feet of heated floor space shall be permitted one freestanding sign per vehicle entrance, with not more than 24 square feet of sign face per sign.
(4)
Regardless of the permitted square footage for a sign, no sign shall be permitted which has a ratio of height to width; or a ratio of width to height; on the sign face that is greater than 4:1.
(5)
The area of the "sign face" shall be computed as the area within the smallest rectangle which would enclose the limits of the sign face, regardless of the shape of the sign, and specifically including any material or portions of the sign which form an integral component of the sign face.
(Ord. 05-01-01, 2-15-05; Ord. No. 09-09-01, 9-15-09; Ord. No. 10-01-01, 1-19-10; Ord. No. 12-05-01, 6-5-12; Ord. No. 14-01-01, 2-18-14 ; Ord. No. 16-06-01 , 6-21-16)

The maximum height of any freestanding sign shall not exceed ten feet above the average elevation, (measured along the distance of the road frontage), of the nearest public right-of-way.
(Ord. No. 05-01-01, 2-15-05)
Sec. 34-1106. - Width.
The maximum width of the entire sign structure shall not exceed ten feet.
(Ord. No. 05-01-01, 2-15-05)

The size of the support structure for any freestanding sign shall not exceed the sign face by more than 100 percent.
(Ord. No. 05-01-01, 2-15-05)

Prior to permitting, the location for a new freestanding sign shall be clearly identified by a white stake extending at least six inches above ground level at each location at which a support footing will be embedded in the ground.
(Ord. No. 05-01-01, 2-15-05)

Window signs are considered signs for the purposes of this article and must be approved by the planning design and review board. Sec. 34-1110. - Off-premises and on-premises directional signs.
(a)
Off-premises signs, limited to two, not to exceed nine square feet each, are permitted for direction to a business or development where the on-premises signage is not clearly visible to the general public from Main Street or Edelweiss Strasse.

On-premises directional signs are permitted where necessary and prudent for reasonable business operations. Shall not exceed one square foot in size;
(2)
Lettering not to exceed two inches in height;
(3)

All sign color shall be compatible with building colors.

Sec. 34-1111. - Temporary commercial signs.
(a)
Temporary commercial signs as regulated herein means any sign or information transmitting device intended to be erected or displayed for a limited period of time for conveyance of a commercial message. The intent of this section is not to limit or regulate the content of a permitted temporary commercial signs, however, by way of illustrative example, temporary commercial signs could be those related to the initial opening or final closing of a business, signs related to a to a sale or markdown of merchandise or services, or signs related to services or goods offered, as examples thereof. However, these examples are illustrative, rather than required, and the contents and messages contained therein are in no way regulated in this article. All temporary commercial signs will be subject to the permit requirements of this article, unless specifically exempted by Code section 34-1051; and may be permitted in accordance with the following standards.
(1)
Size requirements. Temporary commercial signs in the "walking area" of the city, as that term is defined in Code section 34-1001, shall not exceed four square feet in size, with the calculation of the size of said sign calculated in the same matter as provided for in Code section 34-1104(5). Temporary signs in the "driving area" of the city, as that term is defined in Code section 34-1001, shall not exceed six square feet in size, with the calculation of the size of said sign calculated in the same matter as provided for in Code section 34-1104(5).
(2)
Location requirements. Temporary commercial signs shall only be permitted to be located only in the interior of premises and affixed either to the windows or doors of those premises.
(3)
Time Limits and number of signs. Temporary commercial signs shall be authorized for a maximum of seven days, with no more than two temporary commercial signs permitted at any given time. Such authorized signs shall be removed upon expiration of the approved time. A business must have an interval of 21 days between the uses of temporary commercial signs. No business shall receive more than four permits in any one calendar year under this subsection.
(b)
Temporary real estate development signs are signs related to the commercial development of, or for the sale of real property. Unless specifically exempted by section 34-1051, a temporary sign is subject to the permit requirements of this article.
(1)
Temporary Project signs. Signs for work under construction may be erected upon the issuance of a building permit for the construction site and remain during construction. It shall be removed within seven days following the issuance of the certificate of occupancy.
(2)
Temporary signs announcing the subdivision of land. Temporary signs announcing the subdivision of land may be erected on the land being developed and shall be removed when 100 percent of the development lots are conveyed, or for a term not to exceed six months, whichever comes first. A single equal extension may be permitted by the planning and design review board upon application.
(3)
Temporary signs advertising sale, rental or lease of property exceeding in size those exempted by Code section 34-1051. Signs permitted and exceeding nine square feet, advertising the sale, rental, or lease of property shall be removed within 30 days following the completion of the transaction advertised, or upon the expiration of six months, whichever comes first. Equal extensions, if necessary, may be permitted by the planning and design review board upon application.
(Ord. No. 16-06-01 , 6-21-16)

Sec. 34-1118. - Standards and requirements for shadow box signs.
(a)
Purpose. As defined in section 34-1001 of the code, shadow box means a sign utilized for menus by restaurants who offer prepared food on site and beverage as their primary business purpose. Shadow box signage under section 34-1118 is limited to these establishments. This type of shadow box signage is in addition to other types of signage permitted under this chapter.
(b)
Shadow box structure. The shadow box structure must be approved in the same manner proscribed in this chapter for all other signage. The maximum size of the shadow box shall have a maximum content area of 24 inches wide by 24-inch height and constructed of a material approved by the planning design and review board. Decorative trim with a maximum width of four inches shall be required. A roof shall be required for the shadow box with a 12/12 pitch. The face of the shadow box shall have an impact resistant (Plexiglass; lexan, or other approved material); with a hinged and lockable door. The shadow box must be located on the property in such a manner that it does not impede foot or vehicular traffic. Once the structure is approved, the menu contained within the shadow box may be changed as the establishment may desire. However, the menu displayed within the shadow box shall be of white or a neutral color, and the content therein shall be maintained by the establishment.
(c)
Quantity. Each business shall be allowed one shadow box sign per entrance to the licensed restaurant, with a maximum of two.
(Ord. No. 13-06-03, 7-16-13; Ord. No. 14-07-01, 7-15-14 )
