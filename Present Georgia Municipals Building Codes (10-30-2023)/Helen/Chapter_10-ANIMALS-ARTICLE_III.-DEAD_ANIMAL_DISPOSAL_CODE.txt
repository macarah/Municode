ARTICLE III. - DEAD ANIMAL DISPOSAL CODE

Section 10-31. - Abandonment of dead animals; requirements as to disposal generally; disposal in wells, open pits, or surface waters or on private or public property; disposal in city or county landfill.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person who owns or is caring for an animal, which has died or has been killed, to dispose of the carcass in any fashion other than provided for in this chapter. Dead animals shall not be abandoned in wells, open pits or surface waters of any kind on private or public land.
(b)
No person shall dispose of a dead animal on the land of another without the permission of the owner of the land.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-32. - Removal and disposition of dead animals within highway rights-of-way generally.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the state department of transportation to remove and dispose of the carcasses of all dead animals found within the rights-of-way of all state maintained highways within the state.
(b)
It shall be the duty of the county public works department to remove and dispose of the carcasses of all dead animals found within the rights-of-way of all county maintained roads in the unincorporated areas of the county.
(c)
Such carcasses or parts of carcasses shall be disposed of in a manner consistent with this chapter.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-33. - Methods of disposal of dead animals.
Methods which can be used for disposal of dead animals are burning, incineration, burial, rendering or any method using appropriate disposal technology which has been approved by the state department of agriculture. Disposal of dead animals must be completed within 24 hours after death or discovery. Dead animals that are buried must have not less than three feet of earth over the carcass and must not contaminate ground or surface water.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-34. - Destruction of diseased and disabled animals.
(a)
For purposes of ending the suffering of an abandoned domestic animal, which is hopelessly diseased or injured past recovery, a person may apply to the animal control officer for a decision regarding whether such animal should be destroyed.
(b)
The order authorizing the destruction of such animal shall defeat an owner's claim for damages against the person destroying or procuring the destruction of such animal.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-35. - Disposal of dead animals and waste material.
Public livestock sales markets, livestock slaughter establishments, poultry dealers, poultry sales establishments, pet dealers, kennels, bird dealers, animal shelters and stables licensed by the state department of agriculture shall have an approved method and place for the disposal of dead animals and accessory waste material which die within the premises.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-36. - Penalty for violations of chapter or rules or regulations promulgated hereunder.
Any person, firm, partnership or corporation, which violates any provision of this article, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor punishable as hereinafter set out in section 10-101 (penalties).
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
