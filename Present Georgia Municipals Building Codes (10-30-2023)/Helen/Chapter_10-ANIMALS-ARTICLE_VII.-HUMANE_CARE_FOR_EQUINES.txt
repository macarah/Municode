ARTICLE VII. - HUMANE CARE FOR EQUINES

Sec. 10-71. - Prohibited acts.
It shall be unlawful for the owner or custodian of any equine to:
(1)
Commit a violation of section 10-47 (cruelty to animals) which involves an equine owned by, possessed by, or in the custody or control of such person.
(2)
Fail to provide adequate food and water.
(3)
Fail to provide humane care.
(4)
Unnecessarily overload, overdrive, or beat an equine or to cause the death of an equine in a cruel or inhumane manner.
(5)
Interfere with or hinder the animal control officer in carrying out his/her duties under this article.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05; Res. No. 2006-35, 6-29-06)
Sec. 10-72. - Inspection warrants; impoundment authorized; examination.
(a)
If there is cause to believe that a violation of section 10-71 (prohibited acts) has occurred, the animal control officer may apply to the appropriate court for an inspection warrant for inspecting any equine found on such property to determine if a violation of section 10-71 (prohibited acts) has occurred.
(b)
The animal control officer is authorized to impound an equine which has not been furnished with adequate food and water, which has not received humane care or which has been subjected to cruelty in violation of section 10-71 (prohibited acts). A licensed veterinarian following an examination shall make a determination as to the condition or treatment of the equine.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05; Res. No. 2006-35, 6-29-06)
Sec. 10-73. - Duty to care for impounded equines; lien; return to owner.
(a)
It shall be the duty the animal control officer to make reasonable and proper arrangements to provide an impounded equine with adequate and necessary shelter, food, water, veterinary services and humane care and to take such actions as to ensure the survival of the equine. If reasonable and proper arrangements cannot be made, the humane euthanasia of the equine and disposal thereof shall be necessary.
(b)
Such arrangements may include:
(1)
Providing shelter and care for the equine at any state, federal, county, municipal or governmental facility or shelter;
(2)
Contracting with a private individual, partnership, corporation, association, or other entity to provide shelter, food, water, veterinary services and humane care for a reasonable fee; or
(3)
Allowing a private individual, partnership, corporation, association or other entity to provide shelter, food, water, veterinary services, and humane care as a volunteer and at no cost.
(c)
Any person impounding an equine or providing care for an impounded equine shall have a lien on such equine for the reasonable costs of caring for such equine.
(d)
The lien acquired under subsection (a) of this section may be foreclosed in any court, which is competent to hear civil cases. Liens shall be foreclosed in magistrate courts only when the amount of the lien does not exceed the jurisdictional limits established by law for such courts.
(e)
Any person impounding an equine is authorized to return the equine to its owner upon payment by the owner of all costs of impoundment and care and upon the entry of a consent order or receiving written assurances that:
(1)
The equine will be given humane care, adequate food and water, adequate shelter and veterinary services.
(2)
That such equine will not be subjected to cruelty.
(3)
That the owner will comply with the requirements of this article.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-74. - Notice of impoundment.
It shall be the duty of any person impounding an equine to notify the owner, if known, of such equine immediately upon impoundment. Such notice of impoundment shall state the name and address of the person impounding the equine, the location where the equine is being held and a description of the equine. If the owner of such equine is unknown or cannot be found, service of the notice on the owner shall be obtained by publishing a notice once in a newspaper of general circulation.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-75. - Disposal of equine by sale or euthanasia.
(a)
The animal control officer may dispose of the equine through sale at a public auction or by sealed bids. If the physical condition of the equine is such that euthanasia is the only reasonable course of action, the equine may humanely disposed of if the owner:
(1)
Cannot be found;
(2)
Refuses to enter into a consent order;
(3)
Fails to provide written assurance that such equine will be given humane care and adequate food, water, shelter and veterinary care.
(b)
Before disposing of an equine through sale or euthanasia, the animal control officer shall make a reasonable effort to locate the owner and, if the owner cannot be located after reasonable effort, the sale or euthanasia shall proceed.
(c)
Any proceeds from the sale of such equine shall be used first to pay the costs of care given the equine. Funds remaining shall be paid into the animal control revenue account.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-76. - Injunctive relief.
In addition to the remedies provided in this section or elsewhere in the laws of the state and notwithstanding the existence of an adequate remedy at law, the owner of an equine is authorized to apply to the superior court for an injunction or restraining order. Such court shall have jurisdiction and for good cause shown shall grant a temporary or permanent injunction or an ex parte or restraining order enjoining any person, partnership, firm, corporation or other entity from violating and continuing to violate this section or any rules and regulations promulgated under this section. Such injunction or restraining order shall be issued without bond and may be granted notwithstanding the fact that the violation constitutes a criminal act and notwithstanding the pendency of any criminal prosecution for the same violation.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-77. - Penalty for violation of chapter.
A person, partnership, firm, corporation or other entity violating any of the provisions of this chapter shall be punished as hereinafter set out in section 10-101 (penalties). In the alternative, and in the discretion of the animal control officer, the person may be cited with a violation of state law, and be guilty of a misdemeanor if convicted.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05; Res. No. 2007-33, 10-2-07)
