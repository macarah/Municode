ARTICLE III. - DISTRIBUTORS OF HANDBILLS, PAMPHLETS AND CIRCULARS

Sec. 10-40. - License—Required.
No person shall distribute handbills, circulars or pamphlets upon the public streets or other public property or upon private property in the city without first obtaining a license from the city.
(Code 1968, § 10-41)
Sec. 10-41. - Same—Application.
(a)
A license hereunder shall be issued by the city clerk upon application to the city clerk. Such application shall show the name, age, sex and criminal record of the applicant for five (5) years preceding the date of application and also whether the applicant proposes to distribute circulars, pamphlets or handbills for such person's or others and if for others a list of clients for whom the applicant will make distribution.
(b)
A licensee hereunder may at any time amend the application to include additional clients for distribution, without any additional charge therefor.
(Code 1968, §§ 10-42, 10-43)
Sec. 10-42. - Same—Duration; fee.
Licenses hereunder shall be granted for six (6) months, for which the applicant shall pay the license fee prescribed by ordinance.
(Code 1968, § 10-43)
Sec. 10-43. - Same—Disqualification of applicant.
Licenses under this article shall be denied to any person who has a record of conviction of a felony or a crime involving moral turpitude within five (5) years from the date of application, but shall otherwise be granted as a matter of right.
(Code 1968, § 10-45)
Sec. 10-44. - Same—Forfeiture.
Any person granted a license under this article and thereafter convicted of a felony or a crime involving moral turpitude shall by such conviction forfeit the license.
(Code 1968, § 10-47)
Sec. 10-45. - Scattering; littering.
No person shall broadcast or scatter circulars, handbills or pamphlets upon the public streets or upon any property in the city, and all persons distributing handbills, circulars or pamphlets within the city shall hand such pamphlets, circulars or handbills to persons for whom intended, or leave the same secured in some way in order not to become litter and trash upon the public streets or other property.
(Code 1968, § 10-46)
Cross reference— Littering, § 9-20 et seq.; attaching handbills to poles, § 17-23.
Sec. 10-46. - Violations and penalties.
In addition to the forfeiture of license provided for in section 10-44, any person violating the provisions of this article shall be punished as provided in section 1-8.
(Code 1968, § 10-48(a))
