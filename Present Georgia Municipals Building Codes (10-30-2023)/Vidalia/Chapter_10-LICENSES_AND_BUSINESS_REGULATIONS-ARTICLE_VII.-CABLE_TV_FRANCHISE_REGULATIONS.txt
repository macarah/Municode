ARTICLE VII. - CABLE TV FRANCHISE REGULATIONS

Sec. 10-100. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the following terms shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them:
Cable service:
(1)
The one-way transmission to subscribers of:
a.
Video programming, or
b.
Other programming service, and
(2)
Subscriber interaction, if any, which is required for the selection of such video programming or other programming service.
Cable television distributor or cable television program supplier: Any person who leases, licenses, or sells program material or program services used or useful in the operation of a cable television system by a cable television exhibitor.
Cable television exhibitor or cable television operator or cable operator: Any person:
(1)
Who provides cable service over a cable system and directly or through one (1) or more affiliates owns a significant interest in such cable system, or
(2)
Who otherwise controls or is responsible for, through any arrangement, the management and operation of such a cable system.
Cable television system or cable system: A system of coaxial cables or other electrical conductors and equipment used or to be used to originate or receive television or radio signals directly or indirectly off the air and to transmit them via cable to subscribers for a fixed or variable fee, including the origination, receipt, transmission, and distribution of voices, sound signals, pictures, visual images, digital signals, telemetry and any other type of closed circuit transmission by means of electrical impulses, whether or not directed to originating signals or receiving signals off the air.
Combine, contract, is a party to, and assist: Shall include affiliation with a cable television program supplier.
Franchise: An initial authorization or renewal thereof that authorizes the construction or operation of a cable system.
Franchisee: The entity to whom the franchise is awarded and shall include all persons having any rights, powers, privileges, duties, liabilities or obligations under the franchise agreement, and also all persons having or claiming any title or interest in or to the system, whether by reason of the franchise itself or any subcontract, transfer, assignment, mortgage, pledge, hypothecation, security agreement, management or operating agreement, or otherwise arising or created.
Subscriber: A person whose premises are physically wired to receive, and/or who is receiving by any manner, any transmission from the system in exchange for a fee.
(Ord. of 8-14-89, § 20-73)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 10-101. - Purpose.
This article is designed to implement the interest of the city in facilitating the opportunity for free and open competition among cable television operators franchised to provide cable television services to the citizens of the city.
(Ord. of 8-14-89, § 20-72)
Sec. 10-102. - Contracts or combinations in restraint of cable TV.
It is an unlawful restraint of trade for a cable television exhibitor providing or intending to provide cable services within the city to contract, combine, or conspire with any other person to restrain trade with respect to any of the goods or services necessary or useful for the provision of cable television services by a cable operator within the city.
(Ord. of 8-14-89, § 20-74)
Sec. 10-103. - Monopolizing cable TV.
It is an unlawful restraint of trade for a cable television exhibitor providing or intending to provide cable services within the city to monopolize, attempt to monopolize, or combine or conspire with any other person to monopolize or attempt to monopolize, any part of the trade in goods and services necessary or useful for the provision of cable television services within the city.
(Ord. of 8-14-89, § 20-75)
Sec. 10-104. - Contracting or combining to discriminate in acquiring goods and services.
It is an unlawful restraint of trade for a cable television exhibitor providing or intending to provide cable services within the city to combine or contract with any other person, covering the provision of credit, or the lease, sale or exchange of goods or services necessary or useful for the provision of cable services within the city, to require or provide for such other person to discriminate against another cable operator providing or intending to provide cable services within the city with respect to the availability of services or products, or with respect to the price for such services or products, where the effect of such discrimination may be to lessen competition substantially, or to tend to create a monopoly or to injure, destroy, or prevent or hinder competition with respect to the provision of cable television services within the city.
(Ord. of 8-14-89, § 20-76)
Sec. 10-105. - Party to or assisting in discrimination in acquiring goods and services.
It is an unlawful restraint of trade for a cable television exhibitor providing or intending to provide cable television services within the city to be a party to, or to assist in any transaction to lease, sell or purchase, or contract to lease or sell or purchase, which transaction discriminates against competitors of the cable exhibitor, by way of any discount, rebate, allowance, service charge, or price with respect to goods or services necessary or useful for the provision of cable television services within the city, where the purpose or effect of such discrimination is to diminish, eliminate or prevent competition in the provision of cable television service.
(Ord. of 8-14-89, § 20-77)
Sec. 10-106. - Discrimination by cable television distributor in marketing of programs and program services.
It is an unlawful restraint of trade for a cable television distributor or cable television program supplier to engage in any transaction to lease, license, or sell or to contract to lease, license, or sell, which transaction discriminates between cable television exhibitors or franchisees by way of availability of product, differential pricing, discounts, rebates, allowances, service charges, or the like with respect to program material and program services used or useful for the provision of cable television services within the city, where the purpose or effect of such discrimination is to diminish, eliminate, or prevent competition in the provision of cable television service.
(Ord. of 8-14-89, § 20-78)
Sec. 10-107. - Pricing to impede or destroy competition.
It is an unlawful restraint of trade for a cable television operator providing or intending to provide cable television services within the city, to reduce rates for service, below cost or otherwise, or to offer rebates, discounts or refunds for its cable services, for the purpose or to achieve the effect of diminishing, eliminating or hindering competition, or which makes it impossible or impracticable for a present or potential competitor to offer cable services within the city or within any area of the city.
(Ord. of 8-14-89, § 20-79)
Sec. 10-108. - Boycotting, blacklisting, or contributing to withholding of goods and services.
It is an unlawful restraint of trade for a cable operator providing or intending to provide cable services within the city to be a party to, or to assist in, any transaction that discriminates against, boycotts or blacklists, or prevents the lease or sale by a distributor of products or services necessary or useful to the construction and operation of a cable television system to another cable operator intending to provide or providing cable services within the city, when the transaction is for the purpose of diminishing, eliminating or preventing competition in the provision of cable television services within the city.
(Ord. of 8-14-89, § 20-80)
Sec. 10-109. - Cancellation of subscription contracts without penalty.
All subscription contracts for the provision of cable services to a resident of the city may be cancelled by the subscriber within three (3) business days after execution without cause and without imposition of any penalty. No cable operator providing cable services within the city shall impose any penalty for cancellation of a contract for such services by the subscriber after a period of sixty (60) calendar days from the date of its execution. A copy of all forms of contracts for the provision of cable services offered by a cable operator within the city shall be supplied every subscriber, and such forms shall include a notice, in a size equal to at least ten-point boldface type, of these rights to cancel.
(Ord. of 8-14-89, § 20-81)
Sec. 10-110. - Violation grounds for franchise termination.
A violation by a franchisee of any of the provisions of this article shall be grounds for terminating the franchise of the violator. Prior to exercising its right to terminate, the city shall give notice to the franchisee of its intention to do so and shall in all other respects follow the procedures set out in section 20-69 of the cable TV ordinance of the city adopted on March 14, 1988.
(Ord. of 8-14-89, § 20-82)
Sec. 10-111. - Enforcement by judicial action.
The provisions of this article may be enforced in an action brought by the city, an affected cable operator providing or desiring to provide cable services within the city, or by an affected subscriber of cable services within the city, in a court of competent jurisdiction seeking declarative, injunctive or monetary relief, including punitive or exemplary damages, as is found appropriate, plus reasonable attorneys fees when the plaintiff secures affirmative relief, whether by way of damages or declaratory or injunctive relief.
(Ord. of 8-14-89, § 20-83)
Sec. 10-112. - Presumption of intent in action to enforce.
In all actions brought under this article, proof of one or more proscribed acts, together with proof of the injurious effect of such acts, is presumptive evidence of the purpose or intent to injure competitors or to diminish, eliminate or prevent competition.
(Ord. of 8-14-89, § 20-84)
Sec. 10-113. - Revocation of franchise following adverse judicial finding.
A final judgment in an action under this article based on a finding that a cable operator providing cable services within the city has violated any of its provisions is grounds for revocation of that cable operator's franchise by the city.
(Ord. of 8-14-89, § 20-85)
Sec. 10-114. - Franchise fee for state-issued cable or video franchise.
The city hereby requires a franchise fee of five (5) percent of gross revenues generated within the city for any cable or video state franchise issued in its corporate boundaries by the State of Georgia.
(Ord. of 1-7-08, § 1)
Sec. 10-115. - Authorized designee.
The city hereby authorizes the mayor of the City of Vidalia, upon receipt of notice to the city of its right to designate a franchise fee for an applicant for or holder of an existing state franchise, to provide written notice to the Secretary of State and each applicant for or holder of a state franchise within a service area that is wholly or partially located within the city limits of the five (5) percent franchise fee rate applicable to such applicant or holder of a state franchise.
(Ord. of 1-7-08, § 2)
