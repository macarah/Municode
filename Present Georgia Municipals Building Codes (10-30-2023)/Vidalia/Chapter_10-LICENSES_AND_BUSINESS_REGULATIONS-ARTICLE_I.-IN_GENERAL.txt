Sec. 10-16. - Flea market; garage, stall or yard sale.
(a)
Garage sale permit required; number of permits limited: Any person desiring to sell used merchandise or personal property of such person's own or of another person on such person's own premises or at other locations within the city, with written permission from the owner of said property, shall obtain a free permit from the city for such sale. Upon request, any one (1) family unit shall be issued a permit for a period of three (3) days; however, no family unit will receive more than two (2) permits per year. Any signage used in conjunction with this activity must be removed within twenty-four (24) hours of the activity and no signage is permissible on any right-of-way.
(b)
Nonprofit organizations: Any nonprofit or charitable organization shall be issued a free yard sale, garage sale or similar sale permit on a quarterly basis upon adequate proof of nonprofit or charitable status.
(c)
Commercial property owners: Commercial property owners shall be allowed two (2) permits yearly at no charge, after two (2) permits have been issued, including those issued in section (a), within one (1) calendar year, the owner shall purchase the required business occupational tax permit.
(d)
Flea markets: The owner of a flea market shall be charged a regulatory fee of ten dollars ($10.00) per permanent stall and ten dollars ($10.00) per area allocated for a vehicle stall plus the seventy dollars ($70.00) administrative fee. The definition of a vehicle stall equals a minimum area of 144 square feet and a maximum area of five hundred seventy-six (576) square feet. (Flea market vendors will not be required to purchase an occupational tax certificate.)
(e)
Display of goods: Merchandise to be displayed or stored on premises for which a license or permit is required by this article shall be stored behind closed doors between 11:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m.
(f)
Casual sales: The owner of land who desires to rent or permit any part or all of said land to use for the purpose of casual sales of other personal property by third party or parties shall be charged a regulatory fee of ten dollars ($10.00) per area allocated for such casual sales, plus the seventy dollars ($70.00) administrative fee. The definition of land for casual sales shall mean a minimum area of 144 square feet.
(Code 1968, § 10-18; Ord. of 12-29-82, §§ 2—4; Ord. of 12-20-94; Ord. of 8-14-95; Ord. of 8-27-02(1); Ord. of 3-25-03(1); Ord. of 8-12-13(1) )
Sec. 10-17. - Due compensation for telephone companies with end user retail customers within the city.
(a)
The city hereby requires due compensation of three (3) percent of actual recurring local service revenues, as defined in paragraphs (8) and (9) of O.C.G.A. 46-5-1(b); provided, however, that any company which pays in excess of three (3) percent of actual recurring local service revenues pursuant to an existing franchise agreement shall continue to pay in accordance with the agreement until the expiration of the franchise agreement or December 31, 2012, whichever occurs first, and any company which pays in excess of three (3) percent of actual recurring local service revenues in accordance with an occupational license tax arrangement shall continue to pay in accordance with each payment schedule until December 31, 2012.
(b)
Regarding any telephone company that does not have retail end user customers located within the city's municipal boundaries, the payment by such company to a municipal authority in accordance with the rates set by regulations promulgated by the Department of Transportation for the use of its rights-of-way shall be considered the payment of due compensation.
(Ord. of 6-8-09, § 1)
Sec. 10-18. - Authorized designee.
The city manager or his designee, shall, on behalf of the city, exercise day-to-day administrative duties necessary to fulfill the regulatory authority of the city under O.C.G.A. 46-5-1, et seq., and shall perform the following duties:
(a)
Review application information submitted by a telephone company to the city and, if an application is incomplete, notify the telegraph or telephone company within fifteen (15) business days of the receipt of such application, identifying in such notice all application deficiencies.
(b)
Report the receipt of a completed application to the council within sixty (60) calendar days of the receipt of such completed application.
(c)
Review payments of due compensation submitted by the company to ensure compliance with the provisions of the amended law, section 10-17 and this section.
(d)
Provide a coordination function between a telephone company and all city departments on any matter relating to the amended law, section 10-17 and this section.
(e)
Arrange and evaluate, no more than once a year, a comprehensive review of the records of a company which is reasonably related to the calculation and payment of due compensation.
(f)
Provide to all telegraph and telephone companies located in its rights-of-way written notice of annexations and changes in municipal corporate boundaries.
(Ord. of 6-8-09, § 2)
