ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 20-1. - Definitions.
Unless otherwise expressly stated, whenever used in this article the following words shall have the meanings given them by this section:
Cruising means the operation of a taxicab upon public streets, when not employed by a passenger and when it is by words or actions seeking patronage.
Driver's permit means permission granted by the city to any person to drive any licensed taxicab upon the streets of the city.
Operator means any person owning or having control of the use of one or more taxicabs used for hire on the streets of the city, or engaged in the business of operating a taxicab.
Police or police department mean the police or police department of the city under the supervision and authority of the chief of police or his designated representative, who may be any employee of the police department or the license inspector.
Private livery cab means any vehicle carrying persons at rates at special instance and request, per unit of time, by the hour, day, week or fraction thereof.
Street means any street, alley, avenue, court, bridge, lane, or public right-of-way in the city.
Taxicab means any motor vehicle engaged in the business of carrying persons for hire except vehicles under control of the state public service commission. No vehicle less than an intermediate size four-door sedan or station wagon shall be licensed as a taxicab.
Taxicab driver means any person who drives a taxicab, whether such person is the owner of such taxicab or employed by a taxicab owner or operator.
Taxicab license means permission granted by the city to any person to operate or keep for hire any taxicab in the city.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-2. - Compliance and fee payment prior to permit issuance; term of permit; proration of fees, no refund.
(a)
Before a permit shall be granted, the applicant shall comply with all city rules and regulations pertaining to the operation of a taxicab, and each applicant shall pay a permit fee in the amount of one hundred dollars ($100.00) per vehicle.
(b)
All permits shall be granted for the full calendar year or for the number of months remaining in the calendar year. Any applicant granted a permit before July first shall pay the full permit fee without proration. Permit fees for permits granted on or after July first shall be prorated based on the number of months remaining in the calendar year; partial months shall be counted as a full month.
(c)
Permit fees are not refundable.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Secs. 20-3—20-19. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - TAXICABS/VEHICLES FOR HIRE[2]

Sec. 20-20. - Compliance with provisions.
It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in the business of operating taxicabs on the highways of the city until the provisions of this article shall have been complied with as herein set forth.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-21. - Operator's permit—Required; application.
(a)
Any person desiring to engage in the business of operating taxicabs as an operator within the city shall first obtain a permit therefor.
(b)
Such person shall make written application to the council for a permit to operate taxicabs on the highways of the city, which application shall be in writing, addressed to the council and shall be filed with the city clerk. The written application shall contain the full name and address of the person and the principal location from which the business is to be operated, (if a corporation, the application shall set forth the names of the officers of
such
corporation), the number of taxicabs to be operated under the permit if granted, and such other information as may be required by the council. All applications shall be accompanied by a certified check for the full amount of the permit fee. If the application is denied, or if the applicant withdraws the application prior to its approval, the permit fee (without interest) shall be refunded.
(c)
Every person applying for a taxicab driver's permit must be the possessor of a state driver's license in full force and effect under the motor vehicle laws of this state.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-22. - Same—Action upon application.
Upon the filing of an application for an operator's permit, the council shall make or cause to be made such investigation thereon as it may deem advisable and may in its discretion fix a time and place for the holding of a public hearing upon such application. Such application may be acted upon either at a regular or special meeting of the council.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-23. - Same—Indemnity bond; insurance.
(a)
Bond: No permit to an operator or owner shall be issued or continued in operation and force unless there is on file with the city clerk, and in full force and effect, an indemnity bond for each vehicle authorized in the amount of twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000.00) for bodily injury to any one person; fifty thousand dollars ($50,000.00) for injuries to more than one person which are sustained in the same accident, and ten thousand dollars ($10,000.00) for property damage resulting from any one accident. The bonds shall insure to the benefit of any person who shall be injured or who shall sustain damage to property proximately caused by the negligence of an operator, the operator's servants, agents or employees. The bonds shall be kept on file in the office of the city clerk and shall have as surety thereon a surety company authorized to do business in the state.
(b)
Insurance: The city clerk may in such clerk's discretion allow an operator to file, in lieu of a bond, an automobile liability insurance policy issued by an insurance company authorized to do business in the state. The policy shall conform to the provisions of this section pertaining to bonds.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-24. - Same—Not construed as franchise or vested right; revocation.
Nothing contained herein shall be construed as a franchise or to give any vested right to any operator or to anyone else, and the right is reserved in the council to revoke any permit to an operator under this article.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-25. - Same—Transferability.
Permits to engage in the taxicab business as an operator, when issued, shall not be transferable without the consent of the council; provided, however, that holders of permits may make replacements and substitutions of taxicabs, provided the number of taxicabs operated does not exceed the number provided for in the permit already issued to such holder.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-26. - Operator to maintain regular office or place of business.
In addition to all other qualifications specified in this article, an operator shall maintain a regular office or place of business satisfactory to the council, as headquarters for such operations.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-27. - Driver's permit—Required; application.
(a)
Any person desiring to operate taxicabs as a driver shall first obtain a driver's permit from the Vidalia Police Department.
(b)
Such person shall make written application for a driver's permit to the Vidalia Police Department, which shall maintain a file on all permitted drivers.
(c)
Any conviction or plea as referenced in code section 20-28 shall mean automatic revocation of a driver's permit under this code section.
(d)
Upon receipt of written application for a driver's permit, the issuing authority may take a minimum of seventy-two (72) hours for investigation and response.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-28. - Same—Action upon application.
The chief of police shall cause to be conducted such investigation of an applicant for a taxicab driver's permit as deemed necessary, and if the applicant is approved the chief of police shall issue to the applicant an original permit and one copy thereof. There shall be no such approval if the applicant has been convicted of or plead nolo contendere to driving under the influence or a crime involving moral turpitude or a violation of a controlled substances law within the last five (5) years of application.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-39. - Same—Annual fee.
The annual fee for a taxicab driver's permit shall be two dollars and fifty cents ($2.50).
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-30. - Same—License and photograph to be posted in vehicle.
A taxicab driver shall keep posted inside the taxicab, in a place that is visible to all passengers, such driver's operator's license, original permit and photograph.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-31. - Temporary replacement vehicles.
In the event a licensed vehicle shall become temporarily unusable due to a wreck, needed repairs or emergency breakdown, the licensee may contact the police department and obtain permission to use a substitute vehicle for a period not to exceed thirty (30) days, provided signs showing it is a temporary taxi are posted on the front door panel on each side of the vehicle. The sign for putting on each side shall be furnished by the police department at actual cost and shall have entered thereon the expiration date; and provided further, that written notice is submitted by the insurance company through the local agent that such temporary vehicle is fully covered with all insurance as specified and required for the regularly licensed taxi vehicle. In extreme emergency the chief of police may approve extension of the substitute vehicle for more than thirty (30) days.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-32. - Disapproval of license.
The chief of police and the city manager may disapprove an application for a taxicab license if, in their discretion, they determine that:
(1)
The applicant has made a material false statement or concealed a material fact in connection with his application.
(2)
The applicant was the former holder, or was an officer, or partner in a corporation or partnership which was the former holder of a taxicab license which has been revoked or is in suspension.
(3)
The applicant has been convicted of a felony, or any crime involving violence, dishonesty, deceit, indecency, degeneracy, gambling or moral turpitude.
(4)
The applicant is not the true owner of the vehicle or of the taxicab business.
(5)
If the applicant is also to be the driver and has been convicted of:
a.
Drunk driving within the last twenty-four (24) months.
b.
Public drunkenness or under the influence two or more times within the last twelve (12) months, or one time within the last six months.
c.
Quarreling and fighting, disorderly conduct or any misdemeanor within the last six months.
d.
Three or more traffic violations within the last twelve (12) months.
(6)
No license or driver permit for any licensed owner will be issued to anyone who owes any taxes, licenses or fees of any kind to the city or county. Character, general reputation and physical condition may also be grounds for disapproval of a license applicant.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-33. - Limit on number of taxicabs licensed.
No person shall be granted a license to operate a taxicab without it being determined by the police department approving authority that public convenience and necessity requires further taxicab service. Public convenience and necessity shall be limited based on ratio of number of cabs per population of the county. The maximum number of taxicabs which shall be licensed to operate within the city shall be limited to one taxicab for each one thousand (1,000) persons living in the county according to the decennial census of 2000 or any future census plus any estimated increase in population which the city council may from time to time determine to have occurred. This estimated increase shall be taken from the state department of labor or other reliable source.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-34. - Suspension or revocation of license, permit.
(a)
Where it is not specifically stipulated elsewhere in this article, suspension or revocation of a taxicab license or a driver's permit shall be as follows for the various charges:
(1)
Drunk driving or driving under the influence in taxicab, private car or any other vehicle: Automatic suspension of driver's permit only, unless driver is also the licensee, pending the outcome of any charges in court. If convicted and it is the first offense since passage of this article, suspension of driver's permit or license (if driver is licensee) for a period of six (6) months.
(2)
Any disorderly conduct (including drunk or under the influence) charge, no action pending outcome in court. If convicted and it is the first offense since passage of this article, suspension of driver's permit or license (if driver is the licensee) for a period of thirty (30) days.
(3)
Any offense other than first offense under subsections (a)(1) and (a)(2) of this section, to be immediately suspended by the police chief pending any court action. If convicted, suspension to remain in effect and holder of driver's permit or taxicab license (if license is the driver) shall be notified of a hearing before the city council as covered in subsection (b) of this section.
(4)
Conviction of three (3) traffic violations within a six-month period shall mean automatic suspension of driver's permit for a period of thirty (30) days. If suspended twice in a twelve-consecutive-month period, the driver's permit only shall be revoked unless the driver is also the licensee.
(5)
Felony charge: The driver's permit only, unless the driver is also the licensee, suspended immediately pending outcome in court. If convicted the driver's permit or taxicab license (if driver is the licensee) shall be revoked.
(6)
Charge involving transporting, possessing, selling or delivering any beer, wine or other alcoholic beverage: Immediate suspension pending outcome of case in court. If convicted and is first offense since passage of this article, suspension of driver's permit and taxicab license for a period of four (4) months; second offense after passage of this article, regardless of the time lapse, suspension of driver's permit and license for twelve (12) months; third offense, regardless of time lapse, permanent revocation of driver's permit and license, even if licensee is not the driver.
(7)
Any violation of this taxicab article (not covered above and not stipulated elsewhere in this article), or any other violations of the laws of this state or the city: Up to thirty (30) days suspension by the chief of police. If longer than thirty (30) days is recommended by the chief of police, a hearing shall be held before the city council as provided in subsection (b) of this section.
(b)
Wherever a hearing is provided in this section before the city council, the license holder or the driver permit holder shall be given written notice of the hearing. Notwithstanding the requirement for a hearing, the chief of police, with approval of the city manager, may suspend any license pending a hearing and, after the hearing the license or driver's permit may be revoked, remain in suspension or be reinstated.
(1)
The hearing shall be held as soon as a quorum of the city council is available.
(2)
At the hearing, the police and other witnesses and the accused shall present any evidence or facts they desire. The appearance of the accused shall be voluntary. Without any attempt to determine a verdict of guilty or not guilty on any pending court action, the board shall decide what to do or not do regarding the status of the taxicab license or the driver's permit. The action of the board to suspend, revoke or reinstate such license or driver's permit is independent of the outcome of any possible court action.
(3)
Immediately upon any conviction in court, if necessary, the city council shall again convene to decide if the license or driver's permit involved should be restored, revoked or remain in suspension; and if suspension is continued, for what length of time.
a.
If a not guilty verdict should result in court, any suspension shall be automatically and immediately lifted by the chief of police.
b.
Any bond forfeiture or nolo contendere plea, for the purpose of this article, shall be construed in the same light as a conviction by the court.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-35. - Character of service to be rendered.
(a)
All operators and drivers engaged in the taxicab business in the city, operating under the provisions of this article, shall render an overall service to the public desiring to use taxicabs. They shall answer all calls received by them for service inside the city as soon as they can do so, and if such service cannot be rendered within a reasonable time, they shall notify the prospective passengers how long it will be before the call can be answered, and give the reason therefor.
(b)
Any operator or driver who shall refuse to accept a call anywhere in the city at any time when such person has a taxicab available, or who shall fail or refuse to give overall service, shall be deemed a violator of this article and the permit or license granted such holder shall be revoked at the discretion of the chief of police.
(c)
No license or permit shall be issued to an operator or to a driver who is not in a position to render fulltime service to the exclusion of any other business, except with the approval of the council.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-36. - Requirements as to vehicles.
(a)
Be painted in one uniform color or combination of colors, adopted by the owner and reported to the marshal;
(b)
Have four (4) doors, unless such type vehicle cannot be obtained by the owner because of a national emergency or because of other circumstances beyond his control and this requirement waived by resolution of the city council.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-37. - Identification of vehicles.
(a)
Every taxicabs shall have a sign plainly indicating on the rear door on each side of the vehicle, in letters not less than four inches high, the full name of the taxicab company operating the vehicle, including words such as "taxicab," "taxi," or "cab." Such sign shall be professionally applied and shall be either painted or permanently affixed to each side of the vehicle. There shall be no outside advertising of any kind on taxicabs.
(b)
The taxicab telephone number may also be used if desired.
(c)
Each taxicab shall have a taxicab style dome light installed on top of the vehicle. Such light shall be lighted whenever the taxicab is in use at night. The telephone number or the word taxi or taxicab may be printed on the light globe.
(d)
No taxicab shall be operated without being properly marked as required by the provisions of this article.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-38. - Maintenance of vehicles; inspections.
(a)
No vehicle shall be licensed as a taxicab until it has been found to be in a thoroughly safe condition, clean, fit, of good appearance, safe mechanical condition and well painted. The police department shall make, or cause to be made, such examination and inspection before a license is issued.
(b)
The operator shall have each taxicab thoroughly inspected annually by an authorized mechanic permitted to do business in the city using the form provided by the city and shall present proof of such inspection showing the vehicle to be in a safe condition to the city clerk or his/her designee prior to the issuance or renewal of the license or permit.
(c)
A list of authorized mechanics shall be maintained by the police department and said list is incorporated herein and made a part of this section. Said list may be updated annually by the mayor and city council.
(d)
Prior to issuance of permit, the operator under this section shall also provide proof of a tag receipt verifying purchase of a motor vehicle tag issued in Toombs or Montgomery County with tag attached to vehicle.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-39. - Monthly inspection of vehicles; certificates of inspection.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the owner of any taxicab operated in the city to present such vehicle to the chief of police at the police station of the city, on or before the fifth day of each month, for the purpose of permitting the chief of police or some person designated or approved by him to make a full and complete inspection of such vehicle, especially of the brakes thereof, for the purpose of determining whether such may safely be permitted to operate upon the streets of the city.
(b)
The chief of police, or person designated or approved by him, shall inspect the vehicles so presented and test the brakes thereof. If the vehicle is found to be in such condition that it can safely be operated upon the streets of the city, the chief of police, or person designated or approved by him, shall issue to the owner thereof a certificate of inspection allowing the operation of such vehicle for one month. It shall be unlawful for any owner to operate any taxicab upon the streets of the city without a currently valid certificate of inspection.
(c)
It shall be the duty of the person making the inspection to refuse to issue a certificate of inspection to any owner of a taxicab when he shall find the vehicle to be unsafe for operation upon the streets of the city.
(d)
During the inspection herein provided for, an inspection shall also be made with reference to the requirements of section 20-38 and an entry made on the certificate of inspection as to compliance or noncompliance with such requirements.
(e)
The driver of any taxicab shall at all times carry in such vehicle the certificate of inspection issued for that vehicle, which certificate he shall exhibit to any police officer of the city upon demand.
The city specifically disclaims any and all liability for any claims or damages that may be connected to these inspections.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-40. - Inspection fee.
The owner of any taxicab operated within the city shall pay an annual fee of twenty-five dollars ($25.00) for each vehicle for all required inspections.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-41. - Carrying additional passengers.
No taxicab driver shall permit any other person to occupy or ride in the taxicab, unless the person first employing the taxicab shall consent to the acceptance of the additional passengers.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-42. - Receipt for fare.
Whenever a taxicab passenger asks for a receipt, it shall be given to such passenger by the driver. Such receipt shall show the amount of fare collected, the name of the driver, the name of the owner of the taxicab, and the beginning point and ending point of the trip of such passenger.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-43. - Charging excessive fare.
No taxicab driver or operator shall charge or attempt to charge any passenger a rate of fare greater than that to which the taxicab driver or operator is entitled under the ordinances or regulations of the council.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-44. - Transport of passengers for unlawful purposes.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any licensed driver of a taxicab to misrepresent the name of such driver's employer, or knowingly to receive or transport any person who intends to commit an unlawful act in such vehicle, during the voyage or at the termination thereof, whether within such vehicle or not.
(b)
It shall also be unlawful for any driver to solicit or procure or to aid or assist in soliciting or procuring any person to ride in a licensed taxicab with the intent to commit an unlawful act therein at any time during the voyage or at the termination thereof, whether within the vehicle or not.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-45. - Alcoholic beverage licensees, alcoholic beverage establishment employees not to be licensed or permitted.
No alcoholic beverage license or any employee of an alcoholic beverage establishment shall be a concurrent holder of a taxicab license or a taxicab driver's permit or a dispatcher of taxicabs.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-46. - Driver's permit required; state driver's license.
(a)
No person shall operate a taxicab upon the streets of the city and no person who owns or controls a taxicab shall permit a taxicab to be driven at any time for hire unless the driver of such taxicab shall have first obtained and be the holder of a taxicab driver's permit, which is then in full force and effect, from the police department; and provided, further, that such driver shall be an employee or agent of the licensee or the holder of the taxicab license.
(b)
Every person applying for a taxicab driver's permit must be the possessor of a state driver's license in full force and effect under the motor vehicle laws of this state.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-47. - Qualifications of applicants for drivers' permits.
Each applicant for a taxicab driver's permit under the terms of this article must conform to the following regulations:
(1)
Must be twenty-one (21) years of age or over.
(2)
Must be of sound physique with good eyesight and not subject to epilepsy, vertigo, heart trouble or any other infirmity of body or mind, which might render him unfit for the safe operation of a public vehicle.
(3)
Must be able to read and write. Reading of this article and copying of any parts of it may be required.
(4)
Must be clean in dress, neat in personal appearance, and not addicted to the use of intoxicating liquor or drugs.
(5)
Must not owe the city any delinquent taxes, licenses or fees of any kind whatsoever.
(6)
Must fill out, upon a blank form provided by the city, giving full and truthful information as required. Any false statement by the applicant shall be considered cause for denial of driver's permit. Application shall be accompanied by the appropriate fee.
(7)
Must not have any conviction of a felony, or any crime involving violence, dishonest, deceit, indecency, degeneracy or moral turpitude.
(8)
Must not have been convicted of:
a.
Drunk driving within the last twenty-four (24) months.
b.
Public drunkenness or being under the influence two (2) or more times within the last twelve (12) months, or one time within the last six (6) months.
c.
Quarreling and fighting, disorderly conduct or any misdemeanor within the last six months.
d.
Three (3) or more traffic violations within the last twelve (12) months.
(9)
Character, general reputation or physical condition may reflect grounds for disapproval of a driver's permit.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-48. - Application for driver's permit required.
An application for a taxicab driver's permit shall be filed with the police department on forms provided by the city. All information requested on the form must be full and truthful. False or omitted information by the applicant may be considered cause for denial of driver's permit.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-49. - Photograph of applicant for driver's permit.
Each applicant for a driver's permit must file with his application three (3) unmounted photographs of himself, taken within thirty (30) days preceding the filing of his application and of such size as may be easily attached to his permit and application. If approved, each driver must display his permit and photograph in the taxicab he drives. If permit is denied, two (2) copies of the photograph shall be returned to the applicant.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-50. - Fingerprinting of driver's permit applicant.
After filling out the application, the applicant for a driver's permit shall be fingerprinted. A set of the fingerprints with one photograph shall be kept on file at the police headquarters.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-51. - Conviction of felony excludes person from driver's permit.
No permit shall be issued to any person who has been convicted of a felony, except by the majority vote of the city council of the city.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-52. - Renewal of driver's permit.
A driver's permit shall be filed annually for renewal during the month of January. Any driver's permit not renewed by February 1 shall be considered revoked.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-53. - Examination of applicant for driver's permit by police.
Each applicant for a driver's permit shall be examined by some police personnel, designated by the chief of police, as to the applicant's knowledge of the provisions of this article, the traffic regulations of the city, the vehicle and traffic laws of the state, and the geography of the city. If the results of any of the examinations are unsatisfactory in the opinion of the examiner, the applicant shall be refused the permit by the chief of police or his designated representative.
Sec. 20-54. - Issuance of driver's permit; photograph and signature required thereon.
An approved applicant for a driver's permit shall be issued a permit of such form as to contain a photograph and signature of the applicant.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-55. - Fee for driver's permit.
A fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges shall be paid for a driver's permit or renewal thereof. Provided, however, that this permit fee is included in the license fee paid by an individual who is the holder of a taxicab license; but the licensee, if he is also the driver, must meet all requirements for a driver's permit as called for in this article.
Sec. 20-56. - Defacing driver's permit.
Any driver who defaces, removes or obliterates any official entry made on his permit shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and the permit shall be suspended or revoked.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-57. - Hearing on appeal.
Except where a refusal to approve an application for a driver's permit or a taxicab license or an application for a renewal thereof or revocation thereof or suspension of such permit or license is based solely on a judicial conviction or convictions, a licensee, permit holder, or applicant shall have an opportunity to be heard. Such hearing shall be held by the city council of the city at such time and place as the chairman shall prescribe. A driver's permit or a taxicab license, however, may be temporarily suspended by the chief of police or the city manager without notice pending any criminal proceeding, investigation or hearing.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-58. - Taxicab rates; maximum rates of fare; posting required.
(a)
Maximum meter rates. As a condition of licensing by the city and operating within the city, all taxicab companies and drivers shall charge no more than the following schedule of fares for all trips originating or terminating within said city, except as otherwise provided in subsection (b):
(1)
The maximum meter rate shall be as follows:
a.
A rate of two dollars ($2.00) for the first one-sixth ( 1/6 ) of a mile;
b.
A rate of thirty cents ($0.30) per one-sixth ( 1/6 ) of a mile thereafter;
c.
Should a taximeter charge for increments of a mile differently from those in subsections a. and b., the maximum rates shall nevertheless not exceed three dollars and fifty cents ($3.50) for the first mile and one dollar and eighty cents ($1.80) per mile thereafter.
(2)
One dollar ($1.00) for each passenger in excess of one riding in the vehicle on the same trip.
(3)
Twenty-one dollars ($21.00) per hour waiting time; thirty-five cents ($0.35) per minute for traffic delays.
(4)
There shall be no charge for baggage which can be transported within the interior and/or cargo area of any taxicab.
(5)
Taxi fuel surcharge of one dollar ($1.00) surcharge for each trip with a fare equal to or less than five dollars ($5.00), a one dollar and fifty cent ($1.50) surcharge for each trip with a fare of more than five dollars ($5.00) and less than ten dollars ($10.00), and a two dollar ($2.00) surcharge for each trip with a fare of more than ten dollars ($10.00). The surcharge must be posted in all taxicabs.
(b)
Travel to passenger location. No charge shall be made for time and/or mileage for traveling to the location of a prospective passenger.
(c)
Posting rates required. No owner or driver of a taxicab shall charge a greater amount for the use of a taxicab than the published and printed rates which shall be displayed within and on each vehicle, as provided below:
(1)
Each taxicab operating within Savannah shall have a rate card setting forth its rates for mileage, waiting time, extra passengers, excess baggage, and airport flat rates. Said rate card shall be printed in standard block letters showing the taxicab company name not less than three-quarters (¾) inch in height, and the rates not less than three-eighths ( 3/8 ) inch in height. The rate card shall be displayed within the vehicle in such place as to be conspicuous and in view of all passengers, subject to the approval of the transportation services coordinator.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-59. - Payment of fares.
(a)
Prepayment. Every driver of a taxicab shall have a right to demand payment of a legal fare in advance, and may refuse employment unless so prepaid, but no driver shall otherwise refuse or neglect to convey any orderly person upon request anywhere in the city, unless previously engaged or unable to do so. Sec. 20-60. - Cruising.
No driver of a vehicle for hire shall cruise in search of passengers or other business at any time, but shall proceed from an origin by the most convenient and direct route to the required destination. When a vehicle for hire has completed a trip it shall, if awaiting a call for another trip, remain parked at a convenient location on the general area of the last destination, or if not awaiting a call it shall proceed directly to its headquarters or to a stand designated for such vehicles, without soliciting passengers or other business en route.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-61. - Double-parking, waiting in loading zone.
No taxicab is authorized to double-park or to wait in a loading zone.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-62. - Allowing passengers in front seat.
No person shall be allowed to ride in the front seat of a taxicab with the driver except a paying customer, unless such passenger is a member of the driver's or operator's immediate family. Any driver who violates this section shall have his permit revoked.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-63. - Delivering alcoholic beverages.
It shall be unlawful for the driver of any taxicab to deliver alcoholic beverages in such taxicab.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-64. - Misplaced articles in taxicabs.
Every driver of a taxicab must make a diligent effort to return to the rightful owner any property lost or left in his taxicab. If such owner is not found and properly returned within twenty-four (24) hours, the driver of such taxicab shall report such finding to the police department with a description of the article found.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-65. - License and permit not assignable.
Every person to whom a license, or permit has been issued, shall return the license or permit to the police department upon discontinuing or abandoning the operation or driving of a taxicab. Such licenses, or permit shall not be assigned or transferred to any other person or be applicable to any other vehicle than the one specified on original application. Any person who allows his permit or license to be used by any other person, or any person who uses such permit or license, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-66. - Use for unlawful purposes.
It shall be unlawful for any driver of any taxicab to knowingly receive or transport any person or persons who intend to commit any unlawful act during the voyage or at the destination or termination thereof, whether within such vehicle or not. No driver of any taxicab shall use such vehicle himself in the commission of any act that violates any local ordinances or state laws.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-67. - Cleanliness of vehicle.
Every holder of a taxicab license shall at all times keep the inside and outside of the cab operated by him in a clean and sanitary condition.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-68. - What constitutes operation in city.
The taking on or discharging a passenger within the city shall be deemed operating a taxicab within the city.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-69. - Visibility from vehicle.
All windows of taxicabs shall be kept in condition for clear vision. Nothing shall be put on or over windows, including the back glass, to mar any vision at any time. This also includes tinting or glazing that can be attached to the windows.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-70. - Licensees, permittees to give notice of change of address.
Changes of address of owners of licenses or drivers' permits shall be reported to the police department within three (3) days.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-71. - Notice of drivers changing employers.
Persons holding drivers' permits who change employment from one company to another company shall notify the police department before starting to drive for the new employer.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-72. - Political or business advertising prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person owning or operating a taxicab licensed by the city to place, display, affix or allow to be placed, displayed or affixed upon either the exterior or interior of any such licensed taxicab any political or business advertising signs or devices, including bumper strips.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-73. - Waiting list for license applications; procedure.
(a)
Any applicant to be placed on the waiting list for a taxicab license shall file a regular application form with the police department, together with a deposit in amount of the required license fee.
(b)
The application will be processed and:
(1)
If turned down, the deposit will be immediately returned.
(2)
If approved, the deposit will be held to pay the license fee when the applicant becomes eligible for a license.
(3)
If an applicant should become disqualified while on the waiting list due to misconduct, law violations, or any other cause, the deposit shall be immediately returned and the applicant removed from the waiting list.
(4)
An applicant may withdraw from the waiting list and request refund of his deposit at any time.
(c)
Applicants shall appear on the waiting list in order of priority in which they are filed.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
Sec. 20-74. - Fee for driver's permit application; fee for renewal photographs.
A fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges is to be paid with each initial applicant for a taxi driver's permit. This fee is to cover the cost of processing, investigating, fingerprinting, etc., and will also include furnishing the photographs required. If the applicant is turned down, the fee is forfeited. If the applicant is approved, he will be issued a permit upon payment of the prescribed permit fee. A fee as set out in the schedule of fees and charges will be charged for renewal photographs when necessary.
(Ord. of 4-9-07)
