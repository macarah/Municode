ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 19-1. - Adoption of state law by reference.
(a)
O.C.G.A. § 40-6-372, and O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1, containing definitions, and O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-1—40-6-395, known as the "Uniform Rules of the Road," are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of this municipality with like effect as if recited herein.
(b)
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-8.
(Code 1968, § 12-88; Ord. of 12-13-82)
Sec. 19-2. - Scope, purpose of provisions.
This chapter is adopted in the interest of public safety and convenience and is designed to promote the general peace, health, safety, welfare and good government of the city.
(Code 1968, § 12-2)
Sec. 19-3. - Authority of police.
The police shall enforce the provisions of this chapter and the rules adopted hereunder. Subject to the provisions hereof, it shall be the duty of the police department to direct and control traffic of vehicles and pedestrians.
(Code 1968, § 12-6)
Sec. 19-4. - Direction of traffic by voice, hand or mechanical signal.
Traffic shall be directed by officers of the police department by voice, hand or mechanical signal or shall be directed by automatic signal; but such officers or signal shall not prevent anyone from proceeding or standing in the manner required or permitted by law. In case of a fire or other emergency, or in case of a parade or other concourse of people, traffic may be directed as conditions may require, notwithstanding the provisions of any ordinance.
(Code 1968, § 12-7)
Sec. 19-5. - Temporary regulations authorized.
In cases where the traffic upon the streets of the city may become congested, or upon occasions of parades, or at theaters, churches, or schools, or any public gathering or assemblage at any park, building or public place, the police may make temporary rules regulating the traffic, and any person who violates such regulations, after having been warned of such temporary traffic regulations, shall be guilty of a violation of this Code.
(Code 1968, § 12-13)
Sec. 19-6. - Authority to close streets, divert traffic.
The police shall have authority, in the interest of public safety or convenience, temporarily to close any street, alley or portion of the same to vehicular or foot traffic or divert such traffic therefrom.
(Code 1968, § 12-16)
Cross reference— Driving on streets being paved, § 17-16.
Sec. 19-7. - Parades and processions.
(a)
Permission for all parades and processions must be obtained as provided in section 17-20.
(b)
No vehicle shall be driven through a parade or procession except with the permission of a police officer in attendance.
(Code 1968, § 12-14)
Sec. 19-8. - Using closed streets.
Whenever any street, alley, or sidewalk, or any portion thereof, is closed by order of the city authorities, whether the closing thereof is for repairs, improvements, protection against danger, or for any other purpose, it shall be unlawful for any person to enter, cross or travel upon the portion so closed except by permission of the authorities closing the same.
(Code 1968, § 12-17)
Cross reference— Driving on streets being paved, § 17-16.
Sec. 19-9. - Spillage from vehicles—Generally.
It shall be unlawful for the operator of a vehicle to haul trash, refuse, ashes, dirt, sand, gravel, stone, coal, brick, steel shavings or other material, in such a manner as to cause such material to be scattered over any street of the city.
(Code 1968, § 12-138)
Cross reference— Littering, § 9-20 et seq.; collection of refuse, § 12-70 et seq.
Sec. 19-10. - Same—Remedial requirements.
(a)
Every operator of an automobile, truck or trailer, containing or hauling any property including trash of any type or description within the city shall secure such property in such a manner as to keep such property or trash from falling from the vehicle.
(b)
Every covered-body vehicle shall have its load secured by closing the rear doors to such vehicle. All operators of open-bodied vehicles, which are vehicles designed in such a manner that the cargo area is not completely enclosed (to include the top, sides, front, rear and floor), shall cover and secure the load with a tarpaulin or other similar cover that will secure the load in such a manner that the load will not fall or be blown from the vehicle.
(c)
All vehicles used to haul equipment or property which will not fall or be blown from such vehicle while such vehicle is under way are hereby excluded. Examples of excluded loads are building supplies such as lumber and bricks, automobiles and earth-moving equipment, as long as such loads are secured and will not fall or be blown from the hauling vehicle.
(d)
Vehicles hauling gravel, sand and trash are included.
(Code 1968, § 12-80)
Cross reference— Littering, § 9-20 et seq.; collection of refuse, § 12-70 et seq.
Sec. 19-11. - Investigation of accidents.
In case of an accident the police department, if it deems the public interest so requires, shall cause an investigation to be made and shall give proper and sufficient notice of the time and place of holding such investigation. The department shall tabulate all data, statistics, and other pertinent information obtained through such investigation and make reports of the same to the city council with its recommendation as to means and methods whereby such accidents may be averted or minimized.
(Code 1968, § 12-36)
Sec. 19-12. - Ratification of existing signs, signals, markings and devices.
All of the existing traffic control signs, signals, markings and devices erected and maintained throughout the city pursuant to lawful directions of the council from time to time, are hereby ratified and confirmed as fully as if they were erected, installed and maintained pursuant to the provisions of this chapter.
(Code 1968, § 12-49)
Sec. 19-13. - Vehicle equipment generally.
It shall be unlawful to operate any vehicle on the streets of the city which does not comply in all respects with O.C.G.A. § 40-8-1 et seq., relating to equipment of vehicles.
(Code 1968, § 12-71)
Sec. 19-14. - Speed limit—Generally.
Except as otherwise provided in section 12-15 et seq., and in the absence of signs indicating a greater or lesser permissible speed, within a business district as such district is fixed and defined by the zoning regulations of the city now in effect or hereafter adopted, it shall be unlawful to operate any vehicle in the city at a greater rate of speed than twenty-five (25) miles per hour; or in a residential district fixed and defined as provided above, at a greater rate of speed than thirty-five (35) miles per hour.
(Code 1968, § 12-91)
State Law reference— State speed limit in urban and residence districts, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-181.

Sec. 19-17. - "U" turns.
It shall be unlawful to turn a vehicle on a street, so as to proceed in the opposite direction, commonly known as making a "U" turn, at any intersection at which a traffic-control signal is in place or at any other intersection or place where signs have been erected by the police department prohibiting such turns.
(Code 1968, § 12-114)
State Law reference— U-turns, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-121.
Sec. 19-18. - No thru trucks.
It shall be unlawful for any truck and/or truck and trailer with over six (6) wheels to drive thru the following designated area except for the purpose of delivering or receiving product or materials within the area defined as being bordered on the north from South Main Street to the north side of Highway 280 on the south and from the west side of Leader Street on the west to the east side of Jackson Street on the east. All said trucks otherwise passing through this designated area shall be in violation of said section and may be fined up to one hundred ten dollars and thirty-six cents ($110.36), plus the cost of any and all repairs for damages caused by said truck passing through said area.
(Ord. of 8-28-01; Ord. of 9-10-09(2))

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 19-30. - Duty to remove disabled or stalled vehicle.
Whenever a vehicle becomes stalled upon a street or when, because of damage or other reason, it cannot be moved by its own power and consequently blocks the street or interferes with the use of same by other vehicles, it shall be the duty of its owner or operator to remove the same, by towing or otherwise within a reasonable time.
(Code 1968, § 12-154)
Sec. 19-31. - Vehicles left on city parking lots to be towed away at owner's expense.
Any vehicle not in operating condition and without proper license and stickers left on city parking lots over twenty-four (24) hours will be towed away at the owner's expense.
(Code 1968, § 12-154.1)
Sec. 19-32. - Authority of police to remove illegally stopped vehicles.
(a)
Whenever any police officer finds a vehicle standing upon a street in violation of any of the provisions of this article such officer is hereby authorized to move such vehicle, or require the driver or other person in charge of the vehicle to move the same, to a position off the paved or main-traveled part of such highway.
(b)
Whenever any police officer finds a vehicle unattended upon any street, where such vehicle constitutes an obstruction to traffic, such officer is hereby authorized to provide for the removal of such vehicle to the nearest garage or other place of safety.
(Code 1968, § 12-155)
Cross reference— Abandoned vehicles, § 9-40 et seq.
State Law reference— Authority to remove illegally stopped vehicle, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-206.
Sec. 19-33. - Regulations at specific locations.
The city council may from time to time regulate, restrict or prohibit the standing or parking of vehicles on specified streets or parts of streets or in specified areas. Signs or markings shall be installed and maintained giving notice of such regulations, restrictions and prohibitions, and it shall be unlawful to violate or fail to comply with the directions of any such sign or marking.
(Code 1968, § 12-157)
Sec. 19-34. - Standing, parking in front of public buildings.
When appropriate signs giving notice thereof are erected, no vehicle shall be permitted to park or stand in front of the entrance of any theater, church, office building, post office or other building in which a large number of people are accustomed to gather, except for the purpose of stopping to take on or discharge passengers or merchandise.
(Code 1968, § 12-158)
Sec. 19-35. - Parking in alleys.
Vehicles shall not park in public alleys; provided however, that vehicles may park in public alleys not longer than necessary to load and unload, and when so parked, such vehicles shall stand as close as possible to one side of such alley in order to permit other vehicles to traverse such alley. Alley shall not be blocked while unloading for more than two (2) hours between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 5:00 p.m.
(Code 1968, § 12-159; Ord. of 3-27-02)
Sec. 19-36. - Diagonal parking restrictions.
For the purpose of this section, any motor vehicle allowed to park diagonally in the city, shall not have an overall length of more than two hundred twenty-two (222) inches.
(Code 1968, § 12-161)
Sec. 19-37. - Maximum parking time limit.
It shall be unlawful for any person to park any motor or other vehicle on any street in the city and leave it so parked for a period of time longer than twelve (12) hours.
(Code 1968, § 12-162)
Sec. 19-38. - Manner of loading, unloading freight, merchandise.
When practicable, merchandise and freight shall be discharged and loaded from the side of the vehicle nearest the curb. When such handling is impracticable because of the size or weight of the merchandise or freight, the vehicle may back to the curb but it shall not stand at any angle which will interfere with the operation of other vehicles.
(Code 1968, § 12-163)
Sec. 19-39. - Loading, unloading at safety zones.
Nothing contained in this chapter nor any rule of the chief of police shall be deemed to prohibit a vehicle from parking at the curb of any street while taking on or discharging passengers, freight or merchandise, but no vehicle shall be parked, nor shall take on or discharge merchandise or passengers, between a safety zone and the curb nearest thereto at any time.
(Code 1968, § 12-164)
Sec. 19-40. - Parking for sale of wares, merchandise from vehicles.
There shall not be conducted from any truck, motor vehicle, wagon, trailer or other similar type of conveyances, any merchandise business on the streets of the city within a business district, as defined herein, except with special permission from the council, in writing.
(Code 1968, § 12-165)
Sec. 19-41. - Using streets, sidewalks to repair or store vehicles.
It shall be unlawful for any person operating and maintaining a garage in the city for the purpose of repairing or storing motor vehicles to use any of the streets, alleys or sidewalks for the purpose of parking such motor vehicles or for the purpose of washing, painting or repairing them.
(Code 1968, § 12-166)
Sec. 19-42. - Boarding, alighting from vehicles.
So far as practicable, all persons shall alight from vehicles and board the same upon the side nearest to the curb.
(Code 1968, § 12-167)
Sec. 19-43. - Truck tractors in residence district.
It shall be unlawful to operate or park any truck tractor in any residence district, except for the purposes of making delivery or picking up a load, in which case such vehicle may be driven on such street or parked on such street for not more than the minimum distance or time necessary the purpose.
(Code 1968, § 12-156(c); Ord. of 10-12-87(3))
Secs. 19-44—19-49. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PARKING ZONES

Sec. 19-50. - Definitions.
For the purposes of this division, the following terms shall have the meanings respectively ascribed herein:
Holiday: The Fourth of July, Christmas Day, Thanksgiving, and any other day declared a holiday by the council.
Operator: Any individual who shall operate a vehicle as the owner thereof, or as the agent, employee or permittee of the owner, or in actual physical control of a vehicle.
Parking space: Any space laid out, marked off and established under the provisions of this ordinance for individual vehicle parking in any parking zone.
Parking zone: The parking zones named, designated and established pursuant to this ordinance.
(Code 1968, § 12-178; Ord. of 10-21-85; Ord. of 3-27-02(2))
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 19-51. - Designation of parking zones.
The council shall have the authority to designate streets or parts of streets in the city as parking zones and to erect and maintain appropriate signs or other devices giving notice of such designation. When such signs or other devices have been erected or installed, it shall be unlawful for the operator of any vehicle to disobey the directions thereof.
(Code 1968, § 12-179; Ord. of 10-21-85)
Sec. 19-52. - Reserved.
Editor's note— An ordinance of March 27, 2002, repealed section 19-52 in its entirety. Formerly, said section pertained to designation of two hour parking zones as adopted by Code 1968, § 12-180, as amended. See the Code Comparative Table.
Sec. 19-53. - Designation of parking spaces.
The chief of police is hereby directed and authorized to mark off individual parking spaces in the two-hour parking zones designated as provided in section 19-51, such parking spaces to be designated as described in section 19-51.
(Code 1968, § 12-181; Ord. of 10-21-85; Ord. of 6-2-03(2))
Sec. 19-54. - Parking to be diagonal unless otherwise provided.
Parking spaces shall be diagonal to the curb in all parking areas, unless such areas have been designated and appropriately signposted or otherwise marked for diagonal parking.
(Code 1968, § 12-182; Ord. of 10-21-85; Ord. of 3-27-02(2))
Sec. 19-55. - Days, hours of two hour parking zones.
Two (2) hour parking shall be in effect every day between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m., except Sundays and holidays.
(Ord. of 6-2-03(2))
Sec. 19-56. - Violations enumerated.
In addition to the other violations of this division specified herein, it shall be unlawful and a violation of the provisions of this division for any person to do any of the following:
(1)
Overtime parking: To cause, allow, permit or suffer any vehicle registered in the name of, or operated by, such person, to be parked in any parking space beyond the period of lawful parking time established for such space in this division;
(2)
Parking across lines, markings: To park any vehicle across any line or marking of a parking space or in such position that the vehicle shall not be entirely within the area designated such lines of marking;
(3)
Defacing, tampering with: To deface, damage, tamper with or destroy, or impair the usefulness of, any parking sign installed under the provisions of this division.
(Code 1968, § 12-184; Ord. of 10-21-85; Ord. of 3-27-02(2))
Sec. 19-57. - Penalties.
(a)
Generally: Any person guilty of any violation of this division shall be fined ten dollars ($10.00) for the first offense, twenty-five dollars ($25.00) for the second offense, and fifty dollars ($50.00) for the third or additional offense in a twelve-month period.
(b)
Summons; issuance, effect: When any police officer finds any vehicle parked in any parking space in any parking zone in violation of the provisions of this division, such officer shall issue a summons to the operator of the vehicle requiring the operator to appear at the next session of the court to answer the charge of violating this division, which summons shall be served personally upon the operator of the vehicle if such operator is present. If the operator is not present, the summons shall be attached to and left on the vehicle. Such summons shall also contain notice to the operator that such operator may be relieved of the charge of violation of this division by payment of a fine as defined in section (a) above. Nothing in this section would limit the officer's ability to issue a warning where deemed appropriate.
(c)
Effect of multiple violations: In the event any person shall be guilty of three (3) or more violations of this division within any thirty (30) day period of time, then such person for such third or later violation shall be punished as provided in section 1-8.
(Code 1968, § 12-185; Ord. of 10-21-85, § 12-185; Ord. of 3-27-02(2); Res. of 6-2-03(2))
Sec. 19-58. - Enforcement of provisions.
It shall be the duty of the police department to enforce the provisions of this division.
(Code 1968, § 12-186; Ord. of 10-21-85)
Sec. 19-59. - Reservation of powers to city.
Nothing in this division shall be construed as prohibiting the council from providing for bus stops, taxicab stands, loading and unloading spaces, limited or restricted parking spaces, or no-parking spaces within any parking zones.
(Code 1968, § 12-187; Ord. of 10-21-85)
Sec. 19-60. - Erasing marking; penalty fixed.
(a)
Any person who shall attempt to evade or evades the parking regulations of the city by erasing any marking placed on any portion of any motor vehicle for the purpose of hindering or preventing city authorities from ascertaining the length of time such vehicle has been parked shall be guilty of a violation of this section and upon conviction shall be fined the sum of fifty dollars ($50.00) and/or imprisoned for fifteen (15) days.
(b)
Any person who shall remove such person's vehicle from one public parking place to another for the purpose of hindering or preventing the proper city authorities from enforcing the city's parking regulations shall likewise be guilty of a violation of this section and upon conviction shall be fined fifty dollars ($50.00) and/or imprisoned for fifteen (15) days.
(Code 1968, § 12-188; Ord. of 10-21-85; Res. of 6-2-03(2))

ARTICLE III. - AUTOMATED TRAFFIC ENFORCEMENT SAFETY SYSTEM
DIVISION 1. - SPEED MONITORING SYSTEMS

Sec. 19-70. - Definitions.
For the purposes of this article, the following terms, phrases and their derivatives shall have the meanings given herein:
Automated Traffic Enforcement Safety Device means a speed detection device that: (A) Is capable of producing photographically recorded still or video images, or both, of the rear of a motor vehicle or of the rear of a motor vehicle being towed by another vehicle, including an image of such vehicle's rear license plate; (B) Is capable of monitoring the speed of a vehicle as photographically recorded pursuant to subparagraph (A) of this paragraph; and (C) Indicates on each photographically recorded still or video image produced the date, time, location, and speed of a photographically recorded vehicle traveling at a speed above the posted speed limit within a marked School Zone.
School zone means the area within 1,000 feet of the boundary of any public or private elementary or secondary school.
( Ord. of 2-11-2019(2) , § 1)
Sec. 19.71. - Authorized.
Speed camera enforcement, using automated traffic enforcement safety device that is fixed is authorized within School Zones in the City between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 5:00 p.m., Monday through Friday.
( Ord. of 2-11-2019(2) , § 1)
Sec. 19-72. - Administration.
(a)
The law enforcement agency, or agent on behalf of the law enforcement agency, operating an automated traffic enforcement safety device provided for under O.C.G.A. § 40-14-18 shall maintain a log for the automated traffic enforcement safety device attesting to the performance of such device's self-test at least once every thirty (30) days and the results of such self-test pertaining to the accuracy of the automated traffic enforcement safety device. Such log shall be admissible in any court proceeding for a violation issued pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 40-14-18.
The law enforcement agency, or agent on behalf of the law enforcement agency, operating an automated traffic enforcement safety device shall perform an independent calibration test on the automated traffic enforcement safety device at least once every twelve (12) months. The results of such calibration test shall be admissible in any court proceeding for a violation issued pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 40-14-18.
(b)
Prior to the placement of a device within a School Zone, each school within whose School Zone such automated traffic enforcement safety device is to be placed shall first apply for and secure a permit from the Department of Transportation for the use of such automated traffic enforcement safety device. Such permit shall be awarded based upon need. The Department of Transportation shall promulgate rules and regulations for the implementation of this paragraph.
(c)
If an automated traffic enforcement safety device is moved to or placed in a location where an automated traffic enforcement safety device had not previously been moved to or placed in, no citation shall be issued for a violation recorded by that automated traffic enforcement safety device until:
(1)
The City shall erect signs warning of the use of a stationary speed detection device within the approaching School Zone. Such signs shall be at least twenty-four (24) by thirty (30) inches in area, shall be visible plainly from every lane of traffic, shall be viewable in all traffic conditions, and shall not be placed in such a manner that the view of such sign is subject to being obstructed by any other vehicle on such highway. Such signs shall be placed within five hundred (500) feet prior to the warning sign announcing the reduction of the speed limit for the school speed zone. There shall be a rebuttable presumption that such signs are properly installed pursuant to this subsection at the time of any alleged violation under this article; and
(2)
That no citation shall be issued for the first thirty (30) days after such signage is installed for the initial automated traffic enforcement safety device authorized by this article.
( Ord. of 2-11-2019(2) , § 1)
Sec. 19-73. - Violations and penalties.
Any person who shall violate any provision of this article shall be subject to the penalties set forth in O.C.G.A. § 40-14-18(b)(1), as amended, including a fine in the amount of seventy-five dollars ($75.00) for a first violation and one hundred twenty-five dollars ($125.00) for a second or any subsequent violation, in addition to fees associated with the electronic processing of such civil monetary penalty which shall not exceed twenty-five dollars ($25.00).
( Ord. of 2-11-2019(2) , § 1)
