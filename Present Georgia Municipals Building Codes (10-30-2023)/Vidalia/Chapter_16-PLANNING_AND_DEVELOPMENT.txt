Sec. 16-20. - Creation, purpose.
In order to guide and accomplish a coordinated and harmonious development of the municipality which will, in accordance with existing and future needs, best promote the public health, safety, morals, order, convenience, prosperity, and the general welfare, as well as efficiency and economy in the process of development, the city planning commission, hereinafter referred to as "the planning commission," is hereby created and established as authorized by law.
(Code 1968, § 2-95)
Sec. 16-21. - Scope of article.
The planning commission provided for herein shall be organized and empowered as provided in this article.
(Code 1968, § 2-96)
Sec. 16-22. - Appointment, term of members; filling vacancies.
The planning commission shall consist of seven (7) members, who shall be residents of the city, appointed by the council. The terms of the members shall be for four (4) years, except that in the appointment of the first planning commission under the terms of this article, two (2) members shall be appointed for a term of one year each, the third and fourth members shall be appointed for a term of two (2) years each, the fifth member shall be appointed for a term of three (3) years, and the sixth and seventh members shall be appointed for terms of four (4) years each. Any vacancy in membership shall be filled for the unexpired term by the council.
(Code 1968, § 2-97)
Sec. 16-23. - Removal of members.
The council shall have the authority to remove any member of the planning commission for cause, upon written charges, after a public hearing.
(Code 1968, § 2-98)
Sec. 16-24. - Compensation, expenses of members.
All members of the planning commission shall serve without compensation, but may be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in connection with their official duties.
(Code 1968, § 2-99)
Sec. 16-25. - Organization.
The planning commission shall elect its chairman from among its members, whose term of office shall be one year, with eligibility for reelection; and shall appoint a secretary, who may be an officer or employee of the municipality. The planning commission shall make its own rules of procedure and determine its time of meeting.
(Code 1968, § 2-100)
Sec. 16-26. - Public nature of meetings, records.
All meetings of the planning commission at which official action is taken shall be open to the public, and all records of the planning commission shall be public.
(Code 1968, § 2-101)
Sec. 16-27. - Expenditures.
Expenditures of the planning commission, exclusive of gifts, shall be within the amounts appropriated for the purpose by the council.
(Code 1968, § 2-103)
Sec. 16-28. - Powers, duties and responsibilities.
The planning commission shall have all the powers, duties and responsibilities set forth in the law.
(Code 1968, § 2-104)
