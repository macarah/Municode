ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 4-1. - Keeping, grazing near buildings.
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep or maintain any pen, coop or enclosure for animals or fowl or to permit any animal or fowl to be kept, maintained or grazed within two hundred and fifty (250) feet of any house or building where people reside or work. This section shall not apply to any house or building on the same premises where such animal or fowl is kept or maintained, nor shall it apply to dogs or cats as household pets, but it shall apply to dog kennels where more than three (3) dogs are kept.
Sec. 4-2. - Vicious animals.
It shall be unlawful for any owner or other person to maintain or harbor within the city a manifestly vicious animal unless the same is securely, but humanely, confined or restrained in such manner as to prevent such animal from attacking or biting a person or another animal. It shall be prima facie evidence of viciousness if an animal, without provocation, fiercely attacks or bites persons or animals not on the owner's premises.
(Code 1967, § 4-5)
State Law reference— Liability for damages caused by vicious animals, Ga. Code Ann., § 105-110.
Sec. 4-3. - Keeping so as to constitute nuisance or be offensive.
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep, maintain or permit any animal or fowl in the city in such manner as to create an unsanitary or obnoxious condition, or to become noisy, offensive or a nuisance to neighbors or citizens of the city, or to disturb the peace of the city.
(Code 1967, § 4-7)
Sec. 4-4. - Notice to correct offensive conditions.
Should a complaint to the police department arise from the causes referred to in section 4-3, the person involved shall be properly notified by a member of the police department and given an adequate time to either dispose of the animal or fowl or eliminate the cause of the complaint.
(Code 1967, § 4-8)
Charter reference— Authority of mayor and city council to provide for the abatement of nuisances, § 2-9(rr).
Sec. 4-5. - Animals running at large prohibited; pen regulations.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep chickens, turkeys, pigeons, goats, cows, horses, mules, hogs or pigs in the city unless such party or parties provide suitable coops, pens, lots and enclosures for such fowl or animals, and keep said fowl and animals enclosed and confined therein so that they cannot escape therefrom, and provide further that the following conditions are complied with, as set out below.
(b)
Wherever fowl are kept as above described, the pens, lots or such other enclosures must not have any part or section thereof located within fifty (50) feet of a residence, hotel, hospital, church or other building where people congregate or reside.
(c)
Whenever cows, horses, mules, goats, hogs or pigs are kept, the pen, stable, lot, barn or enclosure must not have any part or section thereof located within one hundred fifty (150) feet of a residence, hotel, church, restaurant or other building where people reside or congregate.
(d)
Whenever fowl and animals are kept as above-mentioned, the pen, lot, stable, coop and/or other enclosure must be kept in a clean and sanitary condition at all times so as not to create a fly breeding place or offensive odors, thereby causing a nuisance and menace to public health. The sanitary safety and nuisance factors of such areas, coops, pens or stables shall be judged by the health authority of the city, whose decision shall be final.
(e)
Whenever cows, horses, mules or such animals including hogs and pigs are kept, the pen, lot and stables must be graded to drain, or drainage ways must be provided in order to keep said area free of standing water.
(f)
Wherever cows, horses, mules, hogs, pigs or goats are kept, the manure must be picked up daily and stored, but never allowed to be stored more than four (4) continuous or consecutive days. It shall then be hauled away from the premises and spread in such a manner as not to cause fly breeding.
(Code 1967, § 4-1)
Sec. 4-6. - Removal of dead animals.
It shall be the duty of the owner or persons responsible for any dead animal in the city to remove or dispose of the same in a sanitary and inoffensive manner within twenty-four (24) hours of learning of such dead animal. If not removed within said time, the city may remove the animal and charge the costs thereof against the owner or person responsible.
(Code 1967, § 4-2)
State Law reference— Dead Animal Disposal Act, O.C.G.A., § 62-21.
Sec. 4-7. - City declared bird sanctuary.
The entire area within the corporate limits of the city shall be, and the same is hereby designated and established, as a bird sanctuary.
(Code 1967, § 4-9)
State Law reference— Protection of birds, nests and eggs, O.C.G.A., § 45-530.
Secs. 4-8—4-23. - Reserved.

Sec. 4-24. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Abandoned animal means any domesticated animal which has been placed upon public property or within a public building unattended or uncared for, or upon or within the private property of another without the express permission of the owner, custodian or tenant of the private property and is unattended or uncared for. A domesticated animal shall also be considered as abandoned when it has been upon or within the property of the owner or custodian of such animal for a period of time in excess of thirty-six (36) hours unattended and without proper food and water.
Abused animal means any animal which is any of the following:
(1)
Mistreated, beaten, tormented or teased.
(2)
Deprived of water, food or shelter.
(3)
Kept under unsanitary conditions.
(4)
Abandoned.
(5)
Trained for fighting other animals.
(6)
Transported in the trunk of any vehicle.
(7)
Left unattended in a vehicle in extreme heat without proper ventilation.
Altered animal shall mean a female animal which has been spayed or a male animal which has been castrated (neutered) by a licensed veterinarian in order to render the animal incapable of reproducing.
Animal at large means any animal not under restraint.
Animal control center means the facility designated by city council for the detention of animals.
Animal control officer means any person so designated by the city to perform the duties prescribed by this chapter.
Animal under restraint means any animal humanely secured by a leash or lead or enclosed by way of fence or other enclosure or under the control of a responsible and competent person and obedient to that person's commands, and the person being present with the animal or the animal confined within a vehicle parked or in motion.
Companion animal means a domesticated animal kept for pleasure rather than utility. Such pets include, but are not limited to birds, cats, dogs, hamsters, horses, mice, reptiles, domesticated wild animals, exotic animals and other animals associated with human environment.
Dangerous animal means any animal which constitutes a physical threat to human beings or other domestic animals by virtue of a known propensity to endanger life by an unprovoked assault or by biting so as to cause serious bodily harm or an animal trained, owned or harbored for the purpose, primarily or in part, of animal fighting. An animal shall not be deemed dangerous if:
(1)
It bites, attacks or menaces:
a.
Anyone assaulting the owner;
b.
A trespasser on the property of the owner; or
c.
Any person or other animal who has tormented or abused it;
(2)
It is otherwise acting in defense of an attack from a person or other animal upon the owner or other person; or
(3)
It is protecting or defending its young or other animal.
Department means the police department.
Director means the animal control officer.
Domesticated animal means an animal that is accustomed to living in or about the habitation of people, including, but not limited to cats, cows, dogs, fowl, horses, swine, domesticated wild animals and/or exotic animals.
Electronic animal confinement system shall mean a commercially produced and professionally installed electronic fence which requires an electrically generated perimeter which is not visible, and that the animal within the perimeter wear an electronic collar which produces an electric shock to the animal when the perimeter is approached.
Guard dog means any dog which has been trained to attack persons independently or upon oral command and any dog which, while not so trained, is reasonably expected to perform as a guardian of its owner and/or the property upon and within which the dog is located.
Owner means any person owning, keeping, harboring or acting as custodian of a domesticated animal.
Public nuisance or public nuisance animal means any animal which:
(1)
Is found at large in violation of section 4-31 or 4-34;
(2)
Is unlicensed (unregistered) in violation of section 4-35;
(3)
Is trained, owned or harbored for the purpose, primarily or in part, of dogfighting;
(4)
Is dangerous, as defined, and is not restrained or confined, as provided for in sections 4-33 and 4-34;
(5)
Produces, because of quantity, manner or method in which the animals are domesticated or maintained, unsanitary conditions in the city; or
(6)
Is inimical to the public health, welfare or safety according to the rules and regulations promulgated by the board of health, which rules and regulations are incorporated herein and made a part hereof as if fully set out in this chapter.
(Ord. of 9-8-08)

Sec. 4-26. - Enforcement generally.
(a)
The primary responsibility for the enforcement of this chapter shall be vested in the director who may call upon the city police from time to time to assist in the enforcement of this chapter. The director may also be designated by the mayor and council as the official rabies control officer for the city. The director may, in the exercise of authority as the director and as the chief rabies control officer for the city, delegate the enforcement responsibilities of this chapter to city employees.
(b)
Upon information made known to or complaint lodged with the director or the director's authorized representative that any owner, possessor or custodian of any animal is in violation of this chapter, the director or the director's authorized representative shall cause a summons to be issued requiring the owner, possessor or custodian of the animal to appear before a judge of the municipal court on a day and time certain, then and there to stand trial for the violation of this chapter. If a violation of this chapter has not been personally witnessed by the director or the director's authorized representative or other employee of the city, a subpoena shall be issued to the person making the complaint to be and appear on the day and time set for trial, then and there to testify on behalf of the city. The director will refuse to respond to anonymous complaints.
(c)
If the owner, possessor or custodian of any dog or animal is not known and such dog or animal is upon the public streets, alleys, sidewalks, school grounds or other public places or premises or the property of another without that property owner's permission as prohibited by this chapter, upon complaint made to or information made known to the animal control center, it shall be the duty of the director or the director's authorized representative to immediately take possession of such dog or animal and impound it in a facility designated for the detention of animals. Once impounded, an animal shall be kept for a period of time as defined in section 4-30; thereafter, if the animal is not claimed by anyone after a reasonable effort has been made to locate the owner, possessor or custodian of the animal as hereinafter provided, the animal may be disposed of in a humane fashion or in accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. tit. 4 (§ 4-1-1 et seq.).
(Ord. of 9-8-08)

Sec. 4-28. - Public nuisances prohibited.
It is unlawful to knowingly keep, own, harbor or act as custodian of a public nuisance animal.
(Ord. of 9-8-08)
Sec. 4-29. - Dangerous animals.
(a)
If a law enforcement agent of the city has probable cause to believe that a dangerous animal is being harbored in violation of this chapter, such agent may do one (1) of the following:
(1)
Order the violation immediately corrected and cite the owner or keeper to appear in court for the violation.
(2)
If the violation cannot be immediately corrected and the animal is posing an imminent, serious threat to human beings or other domestic animals, the animal may be seized and impounded, in which case the owner or keeper will be cited to appear in court for the violation. At the owner's request and expense, such impoundment may be at a veterinarian or licensed kennel of the owner's choosing. If the court rules that the animal is not dangerous, it will be released to the owner. If the court rules that it is dangerous, the animal will be released to the owner after payment of any fees and penalties, and upon presentation of proof that the animal will now be kept restrained or confined as specified in this chapter.
(b)
If the owner or keeper of an alleged dangerous animal impounded for violation of this chapter presents proof that the animal will now be kept in compliance with this chapter, the animal will be released upon payment of any fees and penalties due.
(c)
If, within fifteen (15) days after impoundment or such later time as specified by the court, the owner or keeper of an alleged dangerous animal fails to either provide proof that the animal will now be kept restrained or confined in compliance with the provisions of this chapter or fails to reclaim it from animal control after impoundment, and if it cannot be adopted by someone providing proof that it will be kept restrained or confined as specified in this chapter, it will be humanely euthanized.
(d)
If the owner or keeper of an animal previously deemed a dangerous animal by the court fails to keep the dangerous animal restrained or confined in compliance with the provisions of this chapter and if it cannot be adopted by someone who does not reside in the same household as the owner or keeper providing proof that it will be kept restrained or confined as specified in this chapter, it shall be humanely euthanized.
(Ord. of 9-8-08)
Sec. 4-30. - Notice to owner of impounded animal.
(a)
Upon impounding any dog or other animal, the city shall cause to be made a prompt and reasonable effort to locate the animal's owner, possessor or custodian and, upon location thereof, shall send to such owner, possessor or custodian notification of impoundment. For the purpose of this section, it shall be deemed sufficient notice if a notification of impoundment is sent by certified mail, return receipt requested, to the owner named on the most recent rabies certificate on file at the address indicated thereon. If the dog or animal is not claimed by the owner, possessor or custodian thereof within three (3) days from the receipt of the certified letter giving notice of impoundment or returning of letter by the post office, then it shall be the duty of the city to dispose of the dog or other animal. If the impounded dog or animal is not wearing a rabies tag or a tag identifying the name, address and telephone number of the dog's or animal's owner, possessor or custodian, the city may dispose of the dog or animal following five (5) days after it is impounded.
(b)
In addition, if the dog or animal is not claimed as provided for herein, the city, after being satisfied that the dog or animal is in good health, may offer the dog or animal for adoption to any person after the required notification of impoundment has been given.
(c)
In no event shall any animal be redeemed by its lawful owner, possessor or custodian until the required fees established for boarding the animal are paid.
(d)
If for any reason as hereinabove described any animal is impounded, or if any owner, possessor or custodian of any animal violates any of the duties imposed by this chapter, in addition to penalties imposed, such owner, possessor or custodian shall be conclusively presumed to have given consent to the adoption of the impounded animal or to have given consent to dispose of the animal in a humane fashion.
(Ord. of 9-8-08)
Sec. 4-31. - Running at large.
It shall be unlawful for the owner of an animal, or anyone having custody of any animal, to allow it to run at large unattended on or about the streets and highways of the City of Nashville, or on the property of another person without permission of the owner of that property or of the person in possession of that property.
(Ord. of 9-8-08)
Sec. 4-32. - General responsibilities of owners.
(a)
It shall be the duty of every owner of any animal, or anyone having possession or custody of any animal, to exercise reasonable care and to take all necessary steps and precautions to protect other people, property and animals from injuries or damages which might result from such animal's behavior, regardless of whether such behavior is motivated by mischievousness, playfulness or ferocity.
(b)
If the owner or keeper of any animal is a minor, the parent or guardian of such minor shall be responsible to ensure that there is compliance provisions of this section.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person who possesses, harbors or is in charge of any animal not to immediately remove excrement deposited by the animal on any street right-of-way, public or private property. It shall be the duty of any person having custody and control of any animal, while not on their own property, to have in their possession a device, equipment, or other means for the picking up and removal of animal excrement. The provisions of this section shall not apply to any animal aiding the handicapped (e.g., guide dog) or to an animal when in police or rescue activities.
(Ord. of 9-8-08)
Sec. 4-33. - Keeping animal under restraint—While on owner's property.
(a)
It shall be the duty of every owner of any animal, or anyone having possession or custody of any animal, to ensure that the animal is kept under restraint and that reasonable care and precautions are taken to prevent the animal from leaving, while unattended, the real property limits of its owner, possessor or custodian, and to ensure that:
(1)
The animal is securely and humanely enclosed within a house, building, fence, pen or other enclosure out of which it cannot climb, dig, jump or otherwise escape on its own volition. Such enclosure must be securely locked at any time the animal is left unattended.
(2)
The animal is on a leash and under the control of a competent person; or is off leash and obedient to that person's command and that person is present with the animal any time it is not restrained as provided for in paragraphs (1) and (3) of this subsection while on the owner's property.
(3)
It shall be unlawful for any animal to be confined upon the premises of the owner or custodian of such animal by a leash or chain of less than ten (10) feet in length and equipped with swivels at both ends, except by a leash which is a free running line. Any animal confined by leash or chain shall be restrained at least six (6) feet from all entrance and exit locations to the property or building within which the animal is located. Animals shall not be restrained by leash or chain in a manner and location that precludes unrestricted and safe ingress and egress by animal control or law enforcement personnel.
(b)
Additional requirements for precautions to be taken by owners of dangerous animals are:
(1)
Whenever outside of its enclosure as provided for in paragraph (a)(1) of this section, but on the owner's property, a dangerous dog must be attended by the owner and restrained by a secure collar and leash of sufficient strength to prevent escape.
(2)
No dangerous animal shall be chained, tethered or otherwise tied to any inanimate object such as a tree, post or building, outside of its own enclosure as provided for in paragraph (a)(1) of this section.
(3)
In addition to the requirements in paragraph (a)(1) of this section for owners of dangerous animals who maintain their animals out-of-doors, a portion of their property should be fenced with a perimeter or area fence. Within this perimeter fence, the dangerous animal must be humanely confined inside a pen or kennel of adequate size. The pen or kennel may not share common fencing with the area or perimeter fence. The kennel or pen must have secure sides and a secure top attached to all sides; the sides must be sunken into a concrete pad of at least two-inch thickness or securely attached to a wire bottom. The gate to the kennel must be locked.
(c)
An electronic animal confinement system shall be considered an acceptable enclosure when:
(1)
The equipment is maintained and in continuous working order, and the animal to be contained within wears the appropriate electronic collar when within the fence perimeters.
(2)
Permanent and prominently displayed signs are posted at twenty-five-foot intervals around the entire perimeter of the electronic animal confinement system. The signs shall be no smaller than six (6) inches square, and shall read: "Caution—Animal(s) on this property contained by an electronic animal confinement system."
(d)
In cases where an animal has been deemed dangerous by the court, or has been trained to be a guard dog, an electronic animal confinement system may not be used as either the primary or secondary fence.
(e)
Individuals who contain their animal(s) by means of an electronic animal confinement system and are found in violation of this section or have been deemed as restraining a dangerous animal(s) pursuant to section 4-29 shall thereafter restrain the animal(s) in the manner provided in subsections (a) and (b) above.
(f)
Failure to keep any animal confined or under restraint as provided for in this section shall be unlawful.
(Ord. of 9-8-08)
Sec. 4-34. - Same—While off owner's property.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the owner of any animal or anyone having possession of any animal to keep the animal under restraint and control at all times while the animal is off the real property limits of the owner, possessor or custodian.
(b)
For the purposes of this section, an animal is deemed under control when:
(1)
It is securely confined within a vehicle, parked or in motion;
(2)
It is properly confined with a secure enclosure with the permission of the owner of the property where the enclosure is located; or
(3)
It is securely restrained by a leash or other device held by a competent person.
(c)
Additional requirements for precautions to be taken by owners of dangerous animals are that, except when being transported in and securely confined within a vehicle, no dangerous animal shall be permitted off the property of its owner, except when it is:
(1)
Attended by its owner;
(2)
Restrained by secure collar and leash (not to exceed six (6) feet in length), both collar and leash of sufficient strength to prevent escape; and
(3)
Muzzled by any means sufficient to prevent biting other persons or domestic animals.
(d)
Failure to keep any animal under restraint or control as provided for in this section shall be unlawful.
(Ord. of 9-8-08)
Sec. 4-35. - Rabies vaccination; registration fee; issuance of tag.
(a)
It shall be the duty of any owner or possessor or custodian of any dog or cat three (3) months old or older, when such animal is being kept in the City of Nashville, to procure a current rabies vaccination from a duly licensed veterinarian, and pay the required registration fee. If a dog or cat is being transported from outside the city limits into the City of Nashville where it will reside on a permanent basis with its owner, possessor or custodian, it shall be the duty of the owner, possessor or custodian to have a current rabies tag on the animal transported into the City of Nashville unless the animal has not reached the age of three (3) months. Such rabies tag shall be obtained from the animal control center, after proof of a current vaccination by a recognized licensed veterinarian has been presented.
(b)
Any owner, custodian or possessor of any dog or cat which has reached the age of three (3) months or older, or who is obligated under this section to obtain the rabies tag therefore, who fails to obtain same shall be obligated to pay a late charge in the amount established by city council, a copy of which is on file in the office of the city clerk, for any rabies tag required under the terms of this section.
(c)
The directors and designees thereof shall exercise the powers and duties of the rabies control officer pursuant to the provisions of section and shall be responsible for enforcing the provisions.
(Ord. of 9-8-08)
Sec. 4-36. - Collars, tags worn by dogs and cats.
It shall be the duty of each dog or cat owner, possessor or custodian to provide a collar for such dog or cat to which the rabies inoculation tag shall be attached. The collar, together with the tag, shall be worn by the dog or cat if the animal is required to be inoculated against rabies as provided in section 4-35. Failure to comply with this section shall be unlawful.
(Ord. of 9-8-08)
Sec. 4-37. - Rabies inoculation mandatory for dogs and cats.
All dogs and cats in the incorporated limits of the city shall be inoculated for the prevention of rabies by a licensed veterinarian.
(Ord. of 9-8-08)
Sec. 4-38. - Guard dogs, dangerous dogs; warning signs on-premises.
All owners, keepers or harborers of any guard dog or dangerous dog shall display in a prominent place on their premises, and at each entrance or exit to the area where such dog is confined, a sign easily readable by the public using the words "Beware of Dog."
(Ord. of 9-8-08)
Sec. 4-39. - Abandonment or abuse.
It shall be unlawful for anyone to knowingly abandon or abuse any domesticated animal within the City of Nashville. Each person who knowingly abandons or abuses, or willingly permits such abandonment or abuse, or aids in the abandonment or abuse of any domesticated animal shall be guilty of an offense.
(Ord. of 9-8-08)
Sec. 4-40. - Training for fighting prohibited.
It shall be prohibited for any person to keep, buy, sell, trade, exchange, give, transport, or deliver any animal:
(1)
For the purpose of training the animal, primarily or in part, for animal fighting.
(2)
Which has been trained, primarily or in part, for animal fighting.
(3)
Which has been or is intended to be engaged in any manner in animal fighting.
(Ord. of 9-8-08)
Sec. 4-41. - Change of ownership of animals.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to abandon, sell, trade, swap or give away animals within the real property limits of the animal control facility, except by the established services provided by the animal control division inside the facility itself.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to give away or sell any animal in front of any business or store or on the side of any public roadway in the city.
(Ord. of 9-8-08)
Sec. 4-42. - Neutering of animal(s).
It shall be the duty of any owner or possessor of any dog or cat adopted from the animal control center to have the dog or cat altered. Puppies and kittens must be altered by a licensed veterinarian within six (6) months from their date of adoption from the animal control center, and adult dogs and cats must be altered by a licensed veterinarian within thirty (30) days of their adoption. Failure to comply with this section may result in a fine not to exceed five hundred dollars ($500.00).
(Ord. of 9-8-08)

DIVISION 3. - IMPOUNDMENT

Sec. 4-58. - Dogs subject to impoundment.
Any dog not wearing a license tag as prescribed herein; any vicious dog not confined, chained or muzzled as herein provided; any dog running at-large; any female dog while in breeding season running or having access to the public streets; any dog not wearing the rabies inoculation tag required by the statutes of Georgia; and any dog which causes annoyance shall be subject to be taken up by any police officer of the city, or any designated representative of the city, the director of the county health department, or his authorized representative, who shall have the dog confined in the dog pound.
(Ord. No. 5-77, § F, 8-15-77)
Charter reference— Authority of mayor and city council to impound and dispose of animals running at large, § 2-9(qq).
Sec. 4-59. - Destruction of animal in attempt to impound.
(a)
In the event any dog subject to being taken up pursuant to the provisions of this article cannot be captured without actual danger to the officer, or the representative of the city or health department attempting capture, any member of the police department, rabies control officer, or authorized representative, is authorized and directed to shoot such dog when the shooting can be done without endangering persons or other property.
(b)
Nothing herein shall be construed as requiring any police officer to attempt to capture a dog plainly showing the existence of rabies, but such a dog may be shot by any police officer, rabies control inspector, or his authorized representative on sight, using due care not to injure persons or other property.
(Ord. No. 5-77, § G, 8-15-77)
Sec. 4-60. - Operation of dog pound.
The city shall operate either independently or in cooperation with others, or shall contract with the Berrien County Humane Society, for the operation of a suitable place for the impounding of dogs picked up under the terms of this article. If the city shall operate independently, then said facility shall be known as the "dog pound."
(Ord. No. 5-77, § F, 8-15-77; Ord. of 5-26-98, § 1)
Sec. 4-61. - Notice to owner.
Not later than two (2) days after the impounding of any dog, the owner, if known, shall be notified of the time allowed for redemption of the dog.
Sec. 4-62. - Fees and fines.
(a)
The keeper of the dog pound shall not release any dog impounded until the owner has paid all pound fees, all fines assessed by the judge for violations of this article for which such dog was impounded, and until the owner has secured license tag and rabies tag as provided in this article.
(b)
Any dog impounded pursuant to this article which is not suspected of rabies may be reclaimed from the pound upon the payment to the city's authorized representative the sum of twenty-five dollars ($25.00) for first release. The release fee shall double for each subsequent infraction occurring within six (6) months of the initial impoundment. In addition to any release fee assessed, there shall be paid the sum of ten dollars ($10.00) per day, or any portion thereof, for room and board.
(Ord. No. 5-77, § H, 8-15-77; Ord. of 11-10-97, § 1; Ord. of 5-23-05)
Sec. 4-63. - Failure to claim.
Any dog which has been impounded and is not taken up by the owner, within five (5) days of the date impounded, shall be subject to destruction by the keeper of the pound, or other disposition.
(Ord. No. 5-77, § I, 8-15-77)
State Law reference— Methods of disposal of dead animals, Ga. Code Ann., § 62-2105.
Sec. 4-64. - Precedence of rabies inspector over pound keeper where rabies is suspected.
In the case of any vicious dog or any dog giving signs of rabies, the authority of the rabies inspector under the statutes of the state shall take precedence over the keeper of the pound; but the handling of a dog by the rabies control officer shall not relieve any person violating this article from the penalties provided.
(Ord. No. 5-77, § J, 8-15-77)
State Law reference— County rabies control officer, Ga. Code Ann., § 88-1506.1.
