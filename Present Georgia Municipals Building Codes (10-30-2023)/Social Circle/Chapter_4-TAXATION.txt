Sec. 4-26. - Ad valorem tax.
(a)
Rate of levy. The council may set and levy to meet the ordinary current expenses of the city an annual ad valorem tax upon all real and personal property within the city.
(b)
Assessment and fair market value. All property subject to municipal ad valorem taxation shall be assessed at 40 percent of its fair market value. The basis for fair market value shall be 100 percent of the fair market value determined for the property for city ad valorem tax purposes.
(c)
Appeal of assessment. Any taxpayer may appeal from an assessment by the board of tax assessors to the board of equalization as to matters of taxability, uniformity of assessment, and value, and if still dissatisfied may appeal to the Superior Court of Walton County from a decision of the county board of equalization. All such appeals shall be made in the manner provided by law.
(d)
When taxes are due and payable. Ad valorem taxes shall become due on the 20th day of October each year and shall be deemed delinquent if not paid within 60 days after said due date. Tax bills showing the assessed valuations, amount of taxes due, tax due dates, and information as to delinquency dates and penalties shall be sent to all taxpayers at least 30 days prior to the due date, but failure to send a tax bill shall not invalidate any tax. Delinquent taxes shall bear interest at 12 percent per annum from due date.
(e)
Failure to pay tax. The city shall issue an execution against any person who has defaulted in the payment of any ad valorem tax. The execution thus issued shall be a lien on all the property of such person, both real and personal, and shall be placed in the hands of the city officer responsible for collection by levy and sale.
(f)
Against whom charged. Taxes are to be charged against the owner of the property if known, or against the specific property itself if the owner is not known. Life tenants and those who enjoy the use of the property are chargeable with the tax.
(g)
Exempted property. The property exempt from ad valorem taxation under law shall be exempt from city ad valorem taxation.

Sec. 4-27. - Malt beverage excise tax levied under Georgia law.
(a)
Rate of levy. There is hereby set and levied on the sale of malt beverages within the city an excise tax in the sum of $0.05 per 12 ounces and in similar proportion of bottles, cans, and containers of various sizes.
(b)
Against whom levied. The malt beverage excise tax is levied against and shall be paid by each licensed wholesale dealer in malt beverages in the municipality.
(c)
Due date and required report. The malt beverage excise tax shall be paid on or before the tenth day of the month following the calendar month in which the beverages are sold or disposed of, and payment shall be accompanied by a report itemizing the exact quantities of malt beverages sold for the preceding calendar month by size and type of container. Any tax remaining unpaid at the expiration of 15 days from the due date shall be delinquent.
(d)
Enforcement. The tax levied by this section may be enforced by execution in the same manner as other taxes of the city and failure of payment of such tax shall be grounds for revocation or refusal of the business license of the delinquent taxpayer.

(a)
Rate of levy. There is hereby set and levied on the sale of alcoholic beverages, excluding fortified wine, an excise tax in the sum of $0.80 per wine gallon and in similar proportion for bottled containers of various sizes.
(b)

(c)
Due date and required report. Sec. 4-29. - Insurers license fees assessed under Georgia law.
modified
(a)
General. The purpose of this section is to impose license fees on insurers conducting business within the city; to impose a gross premiums tax on insurers operating within the state; and other purposes.
(b)
Insurers license fees.
(1)
Definition. For the purposes of this section, the term insurer means a company which is authorized to transact business in any of the classes of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5.
(2)
Fee levied. There is hereby levied an annual license fee upon each insurer doing business within the city in the amount of $50.00 and an additional annual license fee in the same amount for each separate business location in excess of one operated and maintained by such company within the city.
(3)
License fees for insurers insuring certain risks at additional business locations. For each separate business location, not otherwise subject to a license fee hereunder, operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing and in connection with such loans or sales offers, solicits or takes application through a licensed agent of an insurer for insurance, said insurer shall pay an additional license fee of $17.50 per location.
(4)
Insurance agency license fees; independent insurance agencies, brokers, etc. not otherwise licensed. There is hereby levied an annual license fee upon independent agencies and brokers for each separate business location from which an insurance business is conducted and which is not subject to the company license fee imposed by subsection (b)(2) hereof in the amount of $100.00 for each such location within the city.
(c)
Gross premiums tax.
(1)
Gross premiums tax imposed on life insurers. There is hereby levied an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer doing business within the state writing life, accident and sickness insurance in an amount equal to one percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.1. "Gross direct premiums" as used in this paragraph shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this paragraph is in addition to the license fees imposed by this section.
(2)
Gross premiums tax, all other insurers. There is hereby levied an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer doing business within the state, other than an insurer transacting business in the class of insurance designated in Subsection 1 of O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5, in an amount equal to two and one-half percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2. "Gross direct premiums" as used in this subsection (c)(2) shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this subsection is in addition to the license fees imposed by this section.
(d)
Due date for license fees. License fees imposed in subsection (b) shall be due March 1 each year.

Sec. 4-30. - Public utility franchise taxes.
(a)
Electric utility franchises. The authority, right, permission and consent are hereby granted to each electric supplier within the meaning of O.C.G.A. §§ 46-3-1 et seq., ("company") their successors, lessees and assigns, subject to the terms and conditions set forth hereinafter, for a period of 35 years, to occupy and use the streets, alleys and public places of the city as from time to time each such company may deem proper or necessary for the overhead or underground construction, maintenance, operation and extension of poles, towers, lines, wires, cables, conduits, insulators, transformers, appliances, equipment, connections and other apparatus for the business and purpose of transmitting, conveying, conducting, using, supplying and distributing electricity for public or private use, and to re-enter upon such streets, alleys and public places from time to time as each such company may deem proper or necessary to perform these functions, and to cut and trim trees and shrubbery when and where necessary, in the judgment of each such company, to ensure safe and efficient service. The foregoing notwithstanding, the definition of company shall not include any entity with whom the city has a written franchise agreement.
The use by any company of the streets, alleys, public places and other property of the city for the operation of any of its electric distribution systems, and the grant of requisite street franchise rights, is expressly conditioned upon payment by such company of franchise fees pursuant to this section, and continued use and occupancy of such city property for said purpose without payment of such franchise fees shall be unlawful, and the city shall be entitled to enforce compliance with this section by appropriate proceeding at law or in equity.
Provided further, that the rights, permission and consents herein contained are made for the following considerations and upon the following terms and conditions, to wit:
(1)
Each company shall pay to the city on or before the 20th day of each month, a sum of money equal to five percent of the gross sales of electric energy to all of such company's customers served within the corporate limits of the City during the preceding month. The five percent rate herein specified shall be reviewed at the first council meeting in January of each year and may be changed by majority vote of the council to such other levels as are allowed or mandated by law. Such a rate change shall take effect on February 1st of the year in which such rate change is approved. The city should provide the company with written notice of any such rate change.
(2)
The percentage of gross revenues prescribed to be collected shall be in lieu of all other license and business or occupation taxes, but shall not at any time be considered to interfere with, or in any way prevent the collection of, ad valorem taxes upon the property of the company in the same manner as all other property, real and personal, in the city is taxed.
(3)
In the event the city shall grant to any other electric utility the right to use and occupy its streets for like purposes, such use and occupancy shall be upon the same terms and conditions as those herein contained, including the payment provisions.
(4)
Each company shall fully protect, indemnify and hold harmless (including payment of the city's reasonable attorney's fees and court costs) the city from all damages to person or property caused by the construction, maintenance, operation or extension of poles, wires or other apparatus, by such company, or conditions of streets, alleys or public places resulting therefrom, for which the city would otherwise be liable. Provided however, the city shall not be indemnified for damages arising from the willful misconduct of the city itself.
(5)
Each company shall, in constructing, maintaining, operating and extending its poles, wires and other apparatus, submit and be subject to all reasonable exercises of the police power of the city.
(6)
The grant of rights, permission and consents by the city in this section to any company is specifically conditioned upon the payment of all sums due the city from such company in accordance with the rate, conditions and payment dates set forth in subsection (1) above, and failure by any company to timely pay said franchise fees shall constitute a forfeiture of all rights granted to such company by this section. From and after the original implementation of this public utility franchise tax, each company's continued use and occupancy of the streets, alleys and public places of the city for the aforesaid purposes shall evidence that company's acceptance of the franchise granted hereby and shall render that company liable for payment of all fees required by subsection (1). In the event of forfeiture for failure to comply with such requirements, the forfeiting company shall nevertheless remain liable for all sums accrued until such time as the company removes all of its property from the city following such forfeiture.
(7)
The city manager shall be responsible for the administration and enforcement of this section, and shall have the power to prepare and provide for the collection of the fees herein required.
(8)
All work done by any company upon the streets, alleys and public places of the city shall be done under the supervision of the city, and any sidewalk, street pavement or other city improvement which may be displaced, obstructed or destroyed by reason of such work shall be properly replaced, reopened or repaired by such company to the satisfaction of the mayor and council of the city.
(b)
Franchise tax on telephone and telegraph companies. The authority, right, permission and consent are hereby granted to each telephone and/or telecommunication company ( "company") providing telephone and/or telecommunication service within the city their successors, lessees and assigns, subject to the terms and conditions set forth hereinafter for a period of 35 years, to occupy and use the streets, alleys and public places of the city as the company may deem proper or necessary for the overhead or underground construction, maintenance, operation and extension of poles, towers, lines, wires, cables, conduits, appliances, equipment, connections and other apparatus for the business and purpose of transmitting, conveying, conducting, using, supplying and distributing telephone communications service for public or private use, and to re-enter upon such streets, alleys and public places as such company may deem proper or necessary to perform these functions, and to cut and trim trees and shrubbery when and where necessary, in the judgment of such company, to ensure safe and efficient service.
The use by the company of the streets, alleys, public places and other property of the city for the operation of any of its telephone communication systems, and the grant of requisite street franchise rights, is expressly conditioned upon payment by company of franchise fees pursuant to this section, and continued use and occupancy of such city property for said purpose without payment of such franchise fees shall be unlawful, and the city shall be entitled to enforce compliance with this section by appropriate proceeding at law or in equity.
The rights, permission and consents herein contained are made for the following considerations and upon the following terms and conditions, to wit:
(1)
The company shall pay to the city on or before the 20th day of each month, a sum of money equal to three percent of the gross recurring local service revenues received from all of such company's customers served within the city during the preceding month. For the purposes of this section, the phrase "gross recurring local service revenues" shall include, but not be limited to, revenues from the following services, all as defined in company's tariff in effect on the date of passage hereof as applicable to the city:
Basic Local Exchange Service, Directory Assistance Service, Operator Assisted Local Calls, ESSX Service, Touch Tone Calling Service, Interphone Services, Special Billing Services, Arrangement for Night, Sunday and Holiday Services, Custom Calling Services, Touch Tone Service, RingMaster Service, TicketTaker Service, Simplified Message Desk Interface, Message Waiting Indication-Audible, Surrogate Client Number, Telecommunications Service Priority (TSP) System, Electronic White Pages (EWP), Multiline Hunt Queuing, Hot Line Service, Warn Line Service, Automatic Number Identification, Custom Service Area (CSA), Answer Supervision, Caller Detail Information, Data Transport Service, Integration Plus Management Services (IPMS), Frame Relay Service, Switched Multi Megabit Data Service (SMDS), Native Mode LAN Interconnection (NMLI) Service.
The three percent rate herein specified shall be reviewed at the first council meeting in January of each year and may be changed by majority vote of the council to such other levels as are allowed or mandated by law. Such a rate change shall take effect on February 1st of the year in which such rate change is approved. The city should provide the company with written notice of any such rate change.
(2)
The percentage of gross recurring local service revenues prescribed to be collected shall be in lieu of all other license and business or occupation taxes, but shall not at any time be considered to interfere with, or in any way prevent the collection of ad valorem taxes upon the property of the company in the same manner as all other property, real and personal, in the city is taxed.
(3)
In the event the city shall grant to any other telephone and/or telecommunication company the right to use and occupy its streets for like purposes, such use and occupancy shall be upon the same terms and conditions as those herein contained, including the payment provisions hereof.
(4)
The company shall fully protect, indemnify and hold harmless (including payment of the city's reasonable attorney's fees and court costs) the city from all damages to person or property caused by the construction, maintenance, operation or extension of poles, wires or other apparatus, by such company, or conditions of streets, alleys or public places resulting therefrom, for which the city would otherwise be liable. The city shall not be indemnified for damages arising from the willful misconduct of the city itself.
(5)
The company shall, in constructing, maintaining, operating and extending its poles, wires and other apparatus, submit and be subject to all reasonable exercises of the police power of the city.
(6)
The grant of rights, permission and consents contained in this section are specifically conditioned upon the payment of all sums due the city from the company in accordance with the rate, conditions and payment dates set forth in subsection (1) above, and failure by the company to timely pay said franchise fees shall constitute a forfeiture of all rights granted to such company by this section. In the event of forfeiture for failure to comply with such requirements, the forfeiting company shall nevertheless remain liable for all sums accrued until such time as the company has removed all of its property from the city after such forfeiture.
(7)
The city manager shall be responsible for the administration and enforcement of this section, and shall have the power to prepare and provide for the collection of the fees herein required.
(8)
All work done by the company upon the streets, alleys and public places of the city shall be done under the supervision of the city, and any sidewalk, street pavement or other city improvement which may be displaced, obstructed or destroyed by reason of such work shall be properly replaced, reopened or repaired by such company to the satisfaction of the council.
(9)
Due compensation for telephone companies with end user retail customers within the city.
a.
The city hereby requires due compensation of three percent of actual recurring local service revenues, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 46-5-1(b)(8) and (9); provided, however, that any company which pays in excess of three percent of actual recurring local service revenues pursuant to an existing franchise agreement shall continue to pay in accordance with the agreement until the expiration of the franchise agreement or December 31, 2012, whichever occurs first, and any company which pays in excess of three percent of actual recurring local service revenues in accordance with an occupational license tax arrangement shall continue to pay in accordance with such payment schedule until December 31, 2012.

Sec. 4-35. - Occupation tax and business regulations.
(a)
Occupation tax required for business dealings in Social Circle. For the year 2004 and thereafter, each person engaged in any business, trade, profession, or occupation in the incorporated area of Social Circle, Georgia, including an out-of-state business with no location in Georgia exerting substantial efforts within the state pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7, shall pay an occupation tax for said business, trade, profession, or occupation. Registration of payment of such tax shall be displayed conspicuously in the taxpayer's place of business. If the taxpayer has no permanent business location in Social Circle, such business tax registration shall be shown to the city clerk or to any officer of the Walton County Sheriff's Department or a representative of the city department of public safety, upon request. Payment of occupation tax shall not be a precondition to the operation of any business or practice of any profession in the city, said tax being for raising revenue.
(b)
Construction of terms; definitions.
Administrative fee means the approximate reasonable cost of handling and processing the occupation tax.
Employee means an individual whose work is performed under the direction and supervision of the employer and whose employer withholds FICA, federal income tax, or state income tax from such individual's compensation or whose employer issues to such individual for purposes of documenting compensation a form I.R.S. W-2 but not a form I.R.S. 1099.
Location or office shall include any structure or vehicle where a business, profession, or occupation is conducted, but shall not include a temporary or construction work site which serves a single customer or project or a vehicle used for sales or delivery by a business or practitioner of a profession.
Occupation tax means a tax levied on persons, partnerships, corporations, or other entities engaging in an occupation, profession, or business, for revenue raising purposes.
Regulatory fees means payments, whether designated as license fees, permit fees, or by another name, which are required by Social Circle as an exercise of its police power and as a part of or an aid to regulation of an occupation, profession, or business. The amount of a regulatory fee shall approximate the reasonable cost of the actual regulatory activity performed by Social Circle. A regulatory fee may not include an administrative fee.
Person includes sole proprietors, corporations, partnerships, or any other form of business organization, but excludes nonprofit organizations and state, federal or local governments or instrumentalities thereof.
Practitioner of profession or occupation is one who by law requires state licensure regulating such profession or occupation.
Practitioners of professions and occupations shall not include a practitioner who is an employee of a business, if the business pays an occupation tax.
(c)
Administrative and regulatory fees.
(1)
A non-prorated, non-refundable administrative fee of $100.00 shall be required on all business and occupation tax accounts for the initial start-up, renewal, or reopening of those accounts.
(2)
Regulatory fees may only be imposed in compliance with O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9.
(d)
Occupation tax levied; restrictions.
(1)
An occupation tax shall be levied upon those businesses and practitioners of professions and occupations with one or more locations or offices in the incorporated area of Social Circle, based upon the following criteria:
a.
The number of employees of the business or practitioner who are employed in the business or practitioner's dominant line of business in Social Circle. An employee who works for the same business or practitioner in more than one county or municipal corporation shall be counted as being employed in Social Circle if Social Circle is the local government entity in which the employee works for the longest period of time within the calendar year.
b.
The number of employees of a business or practitioner includes all full-time positions and all full-time position equivalents as follows: An employee who works 40 hours or more weekly shall be considered a full-time position. The average weekly hours of employees who work less than 40 hours weekly shall be added and such sum shall be divided by 40 to determine the number of full-time position equivalents.
(2)
Occupation tax schedule. The tax rate determined by number of employees for each business, trade, profession, or occupation is as follows and will be developed and updated from time to time by the mayor and council of the city: All businesses—$4.50 per employee.
(3)
Exemptions.
a.
No business or practitioner shall be required to pay more than one occupation tax for each of its locations.
b.
No occupation tax shall be required from those real estate brokers, real estate agents, or real estate companies whose offices are located outside the jurisdiction and who sell property inside the jurisdiction.
c.
No occupation tax shall be required from the following:
1.
Any disabled veteran of any war or armed conflict in which any branch of the armed forces of the United States engaged, whether under United States command or otherwise;
2.
Any blind person; or
3.
Any veteran of peace-time service in the United States armed forces who has a physical disability incurred during the period of such service.
d.
No occupation tax shall be levied on any state or local authority, nonprofit organization, or vendor operating under a contract with a tax-exempt agricultural fair, as that term is defined in O.C.G.A. § 2-2-8.
(e)
Occupation tax on businesses with no location in Georgia.
(1)
An occupation tax shall be levied on those businesses and practitioners of professions with no location or office in the state of Georgia if the business's largest dollar volume of business in Georgia is in the city and the business or practitioner: has one or more employees or agents who exert substantial efforts within Social Circle for the purpose of soliciting business or serving customers or clients; or owns personal or real property which generates income and which is located in Social Circle.
(2)
The rate of taxation for businesses taxed under this section is the same as in subsection (d) above. For the purposes of computing the occupation tax on a business described in subsection (1) of this section, the number of employees of the business employed in Social Circle shall be computed based on the number of employees or agents who exert substantial efforts within the incorporated area of Social Circle for the purpose of soliciting business or serving customers or clients.
(f)
Reduction for businesses commenced after July 1. When any person commences business on or after July 1 in any year, the occupation tax for the remaining portion of the year shall be 50 percent of the tax that would have been imposed for the entire year. A practitioner of a profession or occupation who elects as his or her occupation tax the amount described in subsection (h)(2) below shall receive no reduction in such amount.
(g)
More than one place or line of business. Where a business is operated at more than one fixed location in Social Circle, each location shall be considered a separate business for the purpose of occupation tax. Such business is required to obtain the necessary registration and pay an occupation tax for each location.
(h)
Professionals classified in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9(c). Practitioners of professions as described in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9(c)(1—18) shall elect as their occupation tax one of the following:
(1)
The occupation tax based on number of employees as set forth in subsection (d) above.
(2)
A fee of $100.00 per practitioner who is licensed to provide the service, such tax to be paid at the practitioner's office or location; provided, however, that a practitioner paying according to this paragraph shall not be required to provide information to the local government relating to the gross receipts of the business or practitioner. The per-practitioner fee applies to each person in the business who qualifies as a practitioner under the state's regulatory guidelines and framework.
(3)
This election is to be made on an annual basis and must be done by January 1 each year. If no election is timely made, the occupation tax shall be imposed as set forth in subsection (d) above.
(i)
Practitioners exclusively practicing for a government. Any practitioner whose office is maintained by and who is employed in practice exclusively by the US, Georgia, a city or county of Georgia or instrumentalities of the US, shall not be required to obtain a license or pay an occupation tax for that practice.
(j)
Purpose and scope of tax. The occupation tax levied herein is for revenue, not for regulatory purposes, nor is the payment of the tax a condition precedent to the practice of any such profession, trade, or calling. The occupation tax only applies to those businesses and occupations covered by O.C.G.A. §§ 48-13-5—48-13-28. All other businesses and occupations are taxed by the local government pursuant to the pertinent general and/or local law and ordinance. This section does not apply to state or local authorities or non-profit organizations.
(k)
Return required. The owner, proprietor, manager, or secretary of any business subject to the occupation tax for the upcoming calendar year shall, on or before December 31 of the preceding year, file with the city clerk, on a form furnished by the city clerk, a signed return setting forth each location at which business is conducted in Social Circle, the line or lines of business that the business conducts at each such location, the business tax identification number, the employees' social security numbers, and the number of employees of the business or practitioner, computed according to the above guidelines, for each line of business at each location. Any omission of federal tax identification number or social security number shall be reported to the state revenue commissioner. Where a business or practitioner has commenced operation after January 1, the return shall be filed no later than 30 days after commencement of such business.
(l)
Failure to file return. Any person required to file an occupation tax return who fails to file by the date required shall be subject to a civil fine by a court of competent jurisdiction. The fine shall not exceed $200.00 and may be enforced by the contempt power of the court.
(m)
Inspections of books and records; penalty for under-reporting. Social Circle, through its officers, agents, employees, or representatives, may inspect the books of the business for which the returns are made for the sole purpose of determining the number of employees of the business, and upon demand relevant books and records shall be made immediately available for such inspection. Failure to comply shall be punishable by a fine by a court of competent jurisdiction for the enforcement of city ordinances. The fine shall not exceed $200.00 and may be enforced by the contempt power of the court. Adequate records relevant to the number of employees of the business shall be kept in Social Circle for examination. If, after examination of the books or records, it is determined that a deficiency occurs as a result of under-reporting, the amount of the deficiency shall be treated as delinquent and shall be subject to the penalties and procedures for delinquent occupation tax amounts provided in subsections (o) through (r) below. This section shall not be construed as requiring any professional or business to divulge information protected under any privilege recognized by the laws of Georgia, including, but not limited to attorney-client privilege.
(n)
Returns confidential. Except in judicial proceedings or other proceedings necessary to collect the occupation tax hereby levied, or as otherwise required by law, it shall be unlawful for any person to divulge or make known in any manner gross receipts set forth or disclosed in any occupation tax return required hereunder. Information revealing gross receipts shall be confidential and open only to the officials, employees, agents, or clerks of Social Circle using said returns for the purpose of this occupation tax levy and the collection of the tax. Independent auditors or bookkeepers retained by the city shall be classed as "employees". Nothing herein shall be construed to prohibit the publication by officials of statistics, so classified as to prevent the identification of gross receipts, or the inspection of information revealing gross receipts by duly qualified employees of the tax departments of the State of Georgia, the United States, and other local governments.
(o)
When tax due; tax registration.
(1)
Each occupation tax for 2004 and succeeding calendar years thereafter shall be payable January 31 of each year and shall, if not paid by May 1 of each year, be subject to penalties for delinquency as prescribed in subsection (p) below. If any person commences business for the first time in Social Circle after January 1, the tax shall be due and payable 30 days after the date of the commencement of the business, and delinquency shall occur upon nonpayment after 90 days from the due date.
(2)
The tax registration herein provided for shall be issued by the city clerk upon receipt of all amounts due for the occupation tax. Each such registration shall be for the calendar year the registration was obtained unless otherwise specifically provided.
(p)
Penalty for delinquency; interest on delinquent tax; other remedies.
(1)
Should any occupation tax imposed by this chapter be delinquent, the person liable for the tax shall pay a penalty of ten percent of the tax due.
(2)
Any occupation tax which becomes delinquent shall bear interest from the date of delinquency on the unpaid amount at 18 percent per annum.
(3)
The city clerk may collect delinquent occupation taxes in the same manner as provided by law for tax executions.
(4)
A court of competent jurisdiction for the enforcement of ordinances of Social Circle may impose a civil fine for failure to pay the occupation tax (or regulatory fee), not to exceed $500.00, which may be enforced by the contempt power of the court. Per City of Atlanta v. Barnes, this subsection (p)(4) shall not apply to attorneys.
(q)
Registration to be revoked for failure to pay tax, file returns, permit inspection of books. No new business tax registration shall be granted for a business for which any part of the occupation tax is unpaid, or to a person who has failed to submit adequate records as requested by Social Circle in accordance with subsection (m). However, nothing in this section shall be construed as prohibiting the operation of any business or practice of any profession in Social Circle, regardless of status of the occupation tax registration for such business or professional.
(r)

Sec. 4-36. - Pawnbrokers and dealers in second-hand merchandise.
(a)
Definitions.
Dealer in second-hand merchandise means any private entity which holds itself or its place of business out as a buyer, seller or trader of used or distressed merchandise.
Licensee is deemed to include all persons, agents or employees operating under said license.
Pawnbroker means any private entity with which property is deposited as security for the payment of a debt.
Specific entities included in this section. Specifically included in the above defined categories shall be:
(1)
Any dealer, salesperson, or buyer of gold, silver, jewelry, coins and/or other precious metals, whether said sales, trades or purchases are retail, wholesale or by any form of pawn;
(2)
Any dealer, salesperson, or buyer of semi-precious metals to include, but not being limited to, iron, steel, copper, aluminum, brass, and wet-cell batteries, commonly referred to as "junk";
(3)
Any dealer, salesperson, or buyer of used parts designed for use with automobiles, trucks or other vehicles; and
(4)
Any private entity providing selling space, singularly or in multiple units, indoors or outdoors, for the purpose of what is commonly referred to or known as a flea market operation shall, for the purpose of this section, be considered a primary license applicant, and as such, shall be held responsible for the provisions of this section concerning transactions made by lessees of said selling space in the same manner as if said lessees were agents or employees.
Specific entities excluded in this section. Specifically excluded from the above defined categories shall be:
(1)
Wholesalers, buyers and sellers, who sell, trade or deal only with retail mercantile establishments;
(2)
Private sales, commonly referred to as garage sales, when held on the seller's residential property for a period not exceeding 24 hours;
(3)
Organizational sales by non-profit groups holding fund raisers with all funds going directly to the sponsoring organization; and
(4)
State licensed used car or truck dealers or repair garages as to the dealing in used vehicle parts.
(b)
Licensing requirements.
(1)
Application to be submitted. Any private entity desiring to do business as a pawnbroker or dealer in second-hand merchandise shall make application and notice to the city, at least 72 hours prior to the opening or advertising of the business operation.
(2)
Application requirements. All notices/applications shall include the following: 1) full name and address of the person, company or corporation; and 2) submission of standard fingerprint cards of principals, agents and employees to the clerk within 24 hours of the application of agents or employees operating in the city. The cost of processing the fingerprinting shall be deposited.
(3)
Fee required. At the time of submission of an application to operate as a pawnbroker or dealer in second-hand merchandise the applicant shall pay the annual license fee as specified in section 4-35 of this Code).
(c)
Traveling or itinerant dealers.
(1)
Provisions applicable to traveling dealers. All of the provisions of this section shall also apply to any traveling or itinerant dealer, his agent, or employees intending to establish a temporary business of a type addressed by this section.
(2)
Exceptions and specifications. The license fee for an itinerant dealer shall be $25.00, and the license issued shall be valid for a period not to exceed seven calendar days. No itinerant dealer, his agent, or employee may apply for or receive a temporary license more than once during any calendar quarter. Any dealer, agent or employee not previously furnishing fingerprint cards as required by this section must do so within 24 hours of any subsequent application.
(d)
General regulations.
(1)
Business hours. No licensee shall open his place of business or conduct any business between 10:00 p.m. and 8:00 a.m.; provided, however, that such a place of business may remain open until 10:30 p.m. on Fridays and until 11:00 p.m. on Saturdays.
(2)
Premises to be open to police. It shall be the duty of every licensee to admit to his premises the sheriff or any deputy or police officer or a representative of the city department of public safety, or agent of the city who may desire at any time to search for articles missing or stolen, or to make any inspection authorized by this section, without a search warrant.
(e)
Records requirements.
(1)
Forms to be used, distributed. Each licensee shall have an adequate supply of pre-numbered three-part forms which, in addition to any records deemed necessary by the licensee, must be made, maintained and distributed by the licensee daily. Specific retention and distribution of forms shall occur as follows:
a.
The original shall be maintained by the licensee as a permanent record to be kept for a minimum of ten years;
b.
The second copy of the form shall be kept in the Walton County Sheriff's files or the city department of public safety's files, for comparison with items reported stolen; and
c.
The third copy will be forwarded by the Walton County Sheriff's Department or the city department of public safety, to the communications record center for G.C.I.C. comparison as computer time availability permits.
(2)
Licensees to provide accurate descriptions of merchandise. The licensees shall enter an accurate description of all property pledged or sold to them. The description shall include as a minimum: The name of the maker of the article; any identifying mark or number; and a statement of the kind and color of material of which it is made.
(3)
Licensees to provide accurate descriptions of seller or depositor. The licensees shall also enter positive identifying information of the person who sold or deposited the article, including name, address, date of birth, race, estimated weight and height, and signature. Licensees may take such information from a valid driver's license or other such official form bearing a photograph of the person when completing the form.
(4)
Entries to be timely made. The entries required in subsection (e)(1) above shall be made at the time of the transaction. Other records maintained by the licensee shall be kept current to reflect every transaction and they shall be made as soon as possible following each transaction.
(5)
False entries. It is unlawful for any licensee to make any false entry in any records required by this section.
(6)
Transactions with minors prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any licensee to receive any article in pawn, or purchase any article for resale, from any person under 18 years of age.
(f)
Holding of goods.
(1)
Goods to be held for ten days. Any licensee operating under this section who takes any kind of personal property, on pawn or buys such, taking full title thereto, shall hold the property so taken in pawn or sale for at least ten days before disposing of the goods by sale, transfer, shipment, or otherwise.
(2)
Exception. This aforesaid provision cannot be waived by contract, but it does not apply nor is it intended to cover purchasers from reputable wholesale supply houses and manufacturers, or goods of a perishable nature.
(3)
Surrender of stolen property. If is reasonably suspected that any item bought, sold or traded to, or received in any manner by licensees, is the subject of any crime, the licensees shall, upon demand of the sheriff or his designee, or a representative of the department of public safety, surrender the same to the Walton County Sheriff's Department or the city department of public safety.
(g)
General operating requirements for junk dealers and junk yards.
(1)
Application of this subsection. The general operating requirements as specified herein shall apply to all junk dealers.
(2)
Appearance of junk yards. The following appearance requirements shall apply to all junk dealers in Social Circle:
a.
No weeds in a junk yard shall be permitted to attain a height of more than eight inches;
b.
Junk shall be stored in piles not exceeding ten feet in height, and shall be arranged to permit easy access to all such junk for firefighting purposes;
c.
The area on the premises where junk is kept shall be enclosed, except for entrances and exits, with a solid, vertical wall or fence of a minimum height of eight feet measured from ground level;
d.
The fence or wall shall not contain any poster or advertising of any kind except one sign of the licensee not exceeding nine square feet in size; and
e.
Entrances and exits shall not be wider or more numerous than reasonably necessary for conduct of the business.
(3)
Sanitary conditions to be maintained. The following sanitary conditions shall be maintained at all junk dealers:
a.
No junk yard shall be allowed to become a nuisance; nor shall any junk yard be operated in such manner as to become injurious to the health, safety, or welfare of the community or of any residents close by;
b.
No water shall be allowed to stand in any place in such manner as to afford a breeding place for mosquitoes;
c.
No garbage or other waste liable to give off a foul odor or attract vermin shall be kept on the premises; nor shall any refuse of any kind be kept on the premises, unless such refuse is saleable junk;
d.
No junk shall be allowed become scattered or blown off the business premises;
e.
No combustible material of any kind not necessary or beneficial to the business shall be kept on the premises; nor shall the premises be allowed to become a fire hazard;
f.
Fuel and oil shall be removed from any scrapped engines or vehicles on the premise;
g.
No junk or other material shall be burned on the premises in any incinerator not meeting the requirements of the city building codes; and no junk or other material shall be burned on the premises in the open; and
h.
No noisy processing of junk or other noisy activity shall be carried on in connection with the licensed business on any Sunday holiday, or on any day between the hours of 6:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m.
(4)
Administrative requirements. The licensee shall permit inspection of the premises by law enforcement at any reasonable time.
(h)
Penalties. Any licensee or agent or employee thereof violating this section shall be subject to punishment as a misdemeanor by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 or by confinement in the Walton County Jail or other detention facility for a maximum term not to exceed six calendar months, or both, at the discretion of the presiding judge.

Sec. 4-38. - Hotel-motel excise tax.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Due date means from the 20th day after the close of the monthly period for which tax is to be computed.
Guestroom means a room occupied or intended, arranged or designed for occupancy, by one or more occupants for the purpose of living quarters or residential use.
Hotel means any structure or any portion of a structure, including but not limited to any bed and breakfast house, lodging-house, rooming-house, dormitory, Turkish bath, bachelor hotel, studio hotel, motel, motor hotel, auto court, inn, public club or private club, containing guestrooms and which is occupied or is intended or designed for occupancy, by guests, whether rent is paid in money, goods, labor or otherwise. Such term does not include any jail, hospital, asylum, sanitarium, orphanage, prison, detention center or other building in which human beings are housed and detained under legal restraint.
Monthly period means the calendar month of any year.
Occupancy means use or possession or the right to the use or possession of any room or apartment in a hotel, or the right to the use or possession of the furnishings or to the services and accommodations accompanying the use and possession of the room.
Occupant means any person who, for a consideration, uses, possesses or has the right to use or possess any room in a hotel under any lease, concession, permit, right of access, license or use or other agreement or otherwise.
Operator means any person operating a hotel in this city, including but not limited to the owner or proprietor of such premises, lessee, sub-lessee, lender in possession, licensee or any other person otherwise operating such hotel.
Permanent resident means any occupant as of a given date who has or shall have occupied or has or shall have right of occupancy of any guestroom in a hotel for at least 30 consecutive days.
Person means an individual, firm, partnership, joint venture, association, social club, fraternal organization, joint stock company, corporation, nonprofit corporation or cooperative nonprofit membership, estate, trust, business trust, receiver, trustee, syndicate, or any other group or combination acting as a unit, the plural as well as the singular number, excepting the United States of America, the state, and any political subdivision of either upon which the city is without power to impose the tax provided in this article.
Rent means the consideration received for occupancy valued in money, whether received in money or otherwise, including all receipts, cash, credits, and property or services of any kind or nature, and also the amount for which credit is allowed by the operator to the occupant, without any deduction whatsoever.
Return means any return filed or required to be filed as provided in this article.
(b)
Rate of levy. There is set and levied on each occupant of a guestroom of any hotel located within the corporate limits of this city a tax in the amount of five percent of rent for such occupancy. This tax is imposed per O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(a)(1)(A)(3).
(c)
Collection of tax by operator. It shall be the duty of every operator of a hotel located within the city to collect the tax on occupants imposed herein.
(d)
Exemption. Notwithstanding any other provision of this article, no tax shall be imposed upon a permanent resident, or upon rooms, lodgings or accommodations for a period of one or more days for use by state or local government officials or employees traveling on official business.
(e)
Registration of operator. Every person engaging in or about to engage in business as an operator of a hotel in this city shall immediately register the business with the city clerk, on a form provided by the city clerk for such purpose. The required registration shall set forth the name which the operator transacts business or intends to transact business, the location of his place of business, and such other information as would facilitate the collection of the tax by the city clerk. The registration shall be signed by the owner if a natural person, by a member or partner in case of ownership by association or partnership, or by an officer in the case of ownership by a corporation. A separate registration shall be required for each place of business of an operator.
(f)
Certificate of taxing authority. Upon registration of an operator, the city clerk shall issue to such operator without charge a certificate of authority to collect the tax on occupants. Each certificate shall state the name and location of the business to which it relates.
(g)
Due date and required report. All taxes levied by this article shall be due and payable to the city clerk monthly on or before the 20th day succeeding each respective month in which such taxes are collected, and payment shall be accompanied by a return for the preceding monthly period showing the gross rent, rent from permanent residents, taxable rent, and amount of tax collected or otherwise due for the period, and such other information as may be required by the city clerk.
(h)
Collection fee allowed operators. Operators collecting the tax levied under this article shall be allowed a percentage of the tax due and accounted for and shall be reimbursed in the form of a deduction in submitting, reporting and payment of the amount due if the amount is not delinquent at the time of payment. The rate of the deduction shall be the same rate authorized for deductions from state tax under the "Georgia Retailers" and "Consumer's Sales and Use Tax Act".
(i)
Determination if no return made; unsatisfactory return.
(1)
Estimate authorized. If any hotel operator fails to file a return as required under the provisions of this article, the city clerk shall make an estimate of the amount of gross rentals of such operator for the period for which the operator failed to file the return and such estimate shall be based upon any information which is or may come into the possession of the city clerk. One or more deficiency determinations may be made of the amount due for one or more monthly periods.
(2)
Notice of determination. The city clerk or his designated representative shall give to the hotel operator written notice of his determination as provided in this section. The notice may be served personally or by mail; if by mail, such service shall be addressed to the operator at his address as it appears in the records of the city clerk. Service by mail is complete when mailed by certified mail.
(3)
Interest on amount found due. The amount of the determination made under this section shall bear interest at the rate of three-fourths of one percent per month, or fraction of a month, from the 20th day of the month following the monthly period for which the amount or any portion of the amount should have been returned until the date of payment.
(4)
Time within which notice of deficiency determination to be mailed. Except in the case of failure to make a return, every notice of a deficiency determination shall be mailed within three years after the 20th day of the month following the monthly period in which the amount is proposed to be determined, or within three years after the return is filed, whichever period should expire last.
(5)
Unsatisfactory return. The same rules shall apply if a return is made but is determined to be unsatisfactory by the city clerk.
(j)
Required records. Each hotel operator collecting a tax under the provisions of this article shall keep all records, receipts, invoices and other pertinent papers for a period of at least three years, setting forth dates of occupancy and such other information as the city clerk may require.
(k)
Administration and enforcement.
(1)
Authority of the clerk. The city clerk shall administer and enforce the provisions of this article for the collection of the tax imposed by this article.
(2)
Records required from operators; form. Every operator renting guestrooms in this city to a person shall keep such records, receipts, invoices and other pertinent papers in such form as the mayor and council may require.
(3)
Examination of authorized records; audits. The city clerk or any person authorized in writing by the city clerk may examine the books, papers, records, financial reports equipment and other facilities of any operator renting guestrooms to a person and any operator liable for the tax, in order to verify the accuracy of any return made, or if no return is made by the operator, to ascertain and determine the amount required to be paid.
(4)
Authority to require reports; contents. In administration of the provisions of this article, the city clerk may require the filing of reports by any persons or class of persons having in such persons' possession or custody information relating to rentals of guestrooms which are subject to the tax. The reports shall be filed with the city clerk when required by the city clerk and shall set forth the rental charged for each occupancy; the date of occupancy and such other information as the city clerk may require.
(l)
Action for collection of tax.
(1)
Action for tax; time for. At any time within three years after any tax or any portion of such tax required to be collected becomes due and payable, the mayor and council may bring an action in a court of competent jurisdiction in the name of the city to collect such amount due, together with interest, court fees, filing fees, attorney's fees and other incidental legal fees.
(2)
Duty of successors or assignees of operator to withhold tax from purchase money. If any operator liable for any amount under this article sells his business or quits the business, his successors or assigns shall withhold sufficient of the purchase price to cover such amount until the former owner produces a receipt from the city clerk showing that he has been paid or a certificate showing no amount is due.
(3)
Liability for failure to withhold, certificate of notice of amount due, time to enforce successor's liability. If the purchaser of a business fails to withhold the purchase price as required, he shall be personally liable for the payment of the amount required to be withheld by him to the extent of the purchase price.
(4)
Tax credit, or interest paid more than once or erroneously or illegally collected. Whenever the amount of any tax or interest has been paid more than once, or has been erroneously or illegally collected or received by the city under this section, it may be offset by the city clerk.
(m)
Penalties. Any operator or other person who fails to register as required in this articles or who fails to furnish any return required to be made, or who fails or refuses to furnish a supplemental return or other data required by the city clerk, or who renders a false or fraudulent return shall be deemed guilty of an offense and upon conviction shall be punished as provided herein.
