ARTICLE II. - COLLECTION AND DISPOSAL

Sec. 28-19. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Container means any receptacle used to receive and confine solid waste to be placed in any public landfill. The term "container" specifically includes all solid waste receptacles provided by the city.
Contractor means any person engaged individually or in the trade or business of construction, including, but not limited to, general contractors, carpenters, electricians, plumbers, roofing contractors, paving and asphalt contractors, and grading contractors.
Permit means written authorization granted to a person by the county board of health to operate a waste collection or waste disposal system, or both, for the collection or disposal of solid waste; to operate a waste collection or waste disposal system, or both, to dispose of putrescible waste; to operate a waste collection or waste disposal system, or both, to dispose of hazardous wastes; or to maintain and operate a disposal area.
Putrescible wastes means wastes that are capable of being decomposed by microorganisms to cause nuisances. Kitchen wastes, offal, hatchery and poultry processing plant wastes and dead animals are examples of putrescible wastes.
Resident means a person who resides within the city.
Resident business means a commercial enterprise or office whose principal location is inside the city limits.
Solid waste means putrescible and nonputrescible wastes, except water-carried body waste and materials destined for recycling, and shall include garbage, rubbish (paper, cartons, boxes, wood, tree branches, yard trimmings, furniture and appliances, metal, tin cans, glass, crockery, or dunnage), ashes, street refuse, dead animals, sewage sludges, animal manures, industrial wastes (waste materials generated in industrial operations), residue from incineration, food processing wastes, demolition wastes, abandoned automobiles, dredging wastes, construction wastes, and any other waste material in a solid or semisolid state not otherwise defined in this article.
(Code 1974, § 9-13; Code 1991, § 8-26; Res. of 8-10-1992, § 1)
State Law reference— Definitions relating to solid waste management, O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22.
Sec. 28-20. - Solid waste containers.
All containers used by any person for the storage and/or collection of solid waste must have lids which will keep the contents free of rodents, flies or other insects. All solid waste not placed or stored in containers shall be kept free of rodents, flies and other insects. No waste may be stored, collected or disposed of in a condition or manner which creates a health hazard, a public nuisance, or is conducive to the breeding and/or existence of rodents or insects. No person except residents, resident businesses and contractors who are working inside the city limits shall place solid waste within containers located in the city. The penalty upon citation and conviction for unauthorized use of containers in the city is $500.00 for a first offense and $1,000.00 for all subsequent offenses.
(Code 1974, § 9-14; Code 1991, § 8-27; Res. of 8-10-1992, § 2)
Sec. 28-21. - Dumping and disposal.
No solid or human waste shall be dumped or disposed of on a landfill, site or dump which does not have a permit from, or does not meet the approval and requirements of, the county board of health.
(Code 1974, § 9-15; Code 1991, § 8-28)
Sec. 28-22. - Unauthorized items.
(a)
No person shall place the following items in any container in the city:
(1)
Tires.
(2)
Batteries.
(3)
Stumps.
(4)
Trees and limbs in excess of three to four inches in diameter and three feet in length.
(5)
Liquids.
(6)
Bio-medical waste.
(7)
Asbestos.
(8)
Demolition materials.
(9)
Construction materials.
(10)
All metal (especially, old stoves, refrigerators, freezers, hot water heaters, etc.).
(11)
Hazardous materials.
(12)
Containers, five gallons or larger that previously contained hazardous material.
(b)
Upon citation and conviction for the unauthorized placement of the foregoing items in a container in the city the following penalties shall be imposed:
(1)
Residents.
a.
First offense: Warning.
b.
Second offense: $150.00 fine.
c.
Third and all subsequent offenses: $300.00 fine.
(2)
Resident business or contractors.
a.
First offense: $250.00 fine.
b.
Second and all subsequent offenses: $500.00 fine.
(Code 1991, § 8-29; Res. of 8-10-1992, § 3)
Sec. 28-23. - Collection of solid waste.
The city shall, except under unavoidable circumstances, provide solid waste collection a minimum of once weekly.
(Code 1974, § 9-16; Code 1991, § 8-30; Res. of 8-10-1992, § 4)
Sec. 28-24. - Enforcement.
The provisions of this article shall be enforced by the city manager or designee.
(Code 1974, § 9-17; Code 1991, § 8-31; Res. of 8-10-1992, § 4)
