Sec. 30-1. - Adoption of state law relative to street improvements.
The provisions of O.C.G.A. title 36, ch. 39 (O.C.G.A. § 36-39-1 et seq.) are hereby adopted as a part hereof to the same extent as if set out at length herein, and the city shall make improvements under such sections as provided therein.
(Code 1974, § 17-2; Code 1991, § 9-1)
Editor's note— The adoption of the law referred to above was approved by the voters at an election held pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 36-39-2.
Sec. 30-2. - Obstructions.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to place or maintain in or on any street or sidewalk any obstruction whatsoever tending to prevent free passage.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person, for any purpose whatever, to obstruct the sidewalks, streets or alleys of this city, by crowds or the conduct of any business or otherwise. Whenever any obstruction occurs, the city police shall notify the persons causing such obstruction to disperse, and on failure to do so, the police shall arrest every person violating this section.
(Code 1974, § 17-3; Code 1991, § 9-2)
Sec. 30-3. - Unauthorized alterations, etc.
It shall be unlawful for any person to alter any public street or sidewalk, or cut any ditch across the same, or make any new bridge necessary by his act, without first obtaining an order therefor from the mayor and council.
(Code 1974, § 17-4; Code 1991, § 9-3)
Sec. 30-4. - Street sales.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any business establishment to store or display any merchandise on public sidewalks that do not leave at least four feet of clear passageway between the merchandise and the curb edge for public passage.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any business establishment to place any vending machines of any type on the public sidewalks.
(c)
Any business storing and/or displaying merchandise or any other articles on the store front sidewalk shall be responsible for maintaining such merchandise or articles in a neat, orderly manner.
(d)
Such business shall remove all merchandise and other articles from the sidewalks at the end of each day's storage or display thereof.
(Code 1991, § 9-4; Ord. of 11-14-1988(3))
Sec. 30-5. - Building fire on street or alley.
It shall be unlawful for any person to build a fire in the open air in or on any street or alley, or the public square of the city, without a permit from the mayor and council.
(Code 1974, § 17-6; Code 1991, § 9-5)
Sec. 30-6. - Fence or barricade for excavations adjoining streets.
Any person making an excavation adjoining a street in the city shall place and maintain a fence, railing or barricade along the street sufficient to protect the excavation and to prevent persons and animals from falling therein.
(Code 1974, § 17-7; Code 1991, § 9-6)
