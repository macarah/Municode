ARTICLE III. - GAME ROOMS[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Coin operated amusement machines, O.C.G.A. § 50-27-70 et seq.

Sec. 20-49. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Game room means a location, place or premises where four or more mechanical, electrical and amusement devices are installed, operated and made available for invitees on the premises of such game room.
Mechanical amusement devices means each machine which, upon the insertion of a coin, trade token or slug, operates or may be operated as a game of contest or skill or amusement of any kind or description and which contains no automatic pay-off device for the return of money, trade tokens or slugs, and which makes no provision whatever for the return of money. The term "mechanical amusement devices" also means any machine, apparatus or contrivance which is used or may be used as a game with skill and amusement wherein or whereby the player initiates, employs or directs any force generated by the machine or by human skill. The term "mechanical amusement devices" includes, within its perimeter, each machine vending recorded music in return for the insertion or deposit therein of a coin or token.
(Code 1974, §§ 12-25, 12-26; Code 1991, § 5-51)
Sec. 20-50. - License required; fee.
(a)
No person shall operate a game room or any device therein until such person shall have applied for and obtained a license from the mayor and council. No person shall install, operate or maintain any such mechanical amusement or music device without having first obtained such license, which application for license shall be made to the city clerk pursuant to this article.
(b)
Accompanying the application shall be a fee for the first year or any fraction thereof in the amount established by resolution. In the event of the installation of pool tables, whether coin operated or not, the application shall state the number of such pool tables and the fee for each pool table shall be in the amount established by resolution for each year or fraction thereof that such tables are in such establishment. The fee shall be paid for each subsequent year or portion thereof that such establishment operates same or has such devices placed therein.
(Code 1974, § 12-26; Code 1991, § 5-52)
Sec. 20-51. - Issuance of license; duration.
No license shall be issued under this article except to a person of good character approved by the mayor and council. One license for all machines shall be issued and shall be placed in a conspicuous place within the licensed establishment. The license period shall be the calendar year.
(Code 1974, § 12-27; Code 1991, § 5-53)
Sec. 20-52. - Public view to be made available.
All devices to be kept shall be kept in plain view from the street level of all persons passing along or by the entrance of the licensed establishment.
(Code 1974, § 12-28; Code 1991, § 5-54)
Sec. 20-53. - Gambling prohibited.
No device for the hazarding of money, gambling, lottery, game of chance or similar gaming device shall be placed therein.
(Code 1974, § 12-29; Code 1991, § 5-55)
Sec. 20-54. - Hours of operation.
Game rooms may be operated from 8:00 a.m. to 12:00 midnight Monday through Saturday with Sunday operations from 1:00 p.m. to 6:00 p.m.
(Code 1974, § 12-30; Code 1991, § 5-56)
Sec. 20-55. - Inspection.
The police department shall inspect or cause to be inspected any place or building in which any such device is set up for operation and shall inspect, investigate and test such devices at least twice in every month.
(Code 1974, § 12-31; Code 1991, § 5-57)
Sec. 20-56. - Intoxicating beverages prohibited.
No licensee shall permit to be used upon the premises any intoxicating beverage of any classification whatever, nor shall the licensee permit intoxicated persons to frequent and continue to frequent the licensed establishment.
(Code 1974, § 12-32; Code 1991, § 5-58)
Sec. 20-57. - Punishment for violation and revocation.
In addition to the penalties in section 1-6, the mayor and council may revoke such license for any violation of this article or any other ordinance pertaining to conduct of like business or game room ordinances. The license is a privilege and not a contract or ordinance which may not be withdrawn.
(Code 1974, § 12-33; Code 1991, § 5-59)
