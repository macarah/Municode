Chapter 50 - STREETS, SIDEWALKS, AND OTHER PUBLIC PLACES
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 50-1. - Barbed wire fences prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to erect or maintain any barbed wire fence along or adjacent to any sidewalk in the city.
(Ord. No. 2002-3, § 1(20-3), 4-8-2002)
Sec. 50-2. - Gates opening on streets and sidewalks.
All gates shall be hung so as to swing inside and on the property of the person erecting the same or occupying such property. It shall be unlawful for any person owning or occupying any property in the city to permit any gate to swing over or across any sidewalk in the city or into any street or alley.
(Ord. No. 2002-3, § 1(20-4), 4-8-2002)
Sec. 50-3. - Draining fluids onto streets.
It shall be unlawful for the owner or occupant of any premises in the city to allow any wastewater, or other fluid, except natural rainfall, to flow or be emptied from said premises onto streets or sidewalks, without a special permit from the city council.
(Ord. No. 2002-3, § 1(20-5), 4-8-2002)
Sec. 50-4. - Obstructions prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to obstruct any public right-of-way within the city.
(Ord. No. 2002-3, § 1(20-6), 4-8-2002)
Sec. 50-5. - Street name changes.
(a)
The city clerk shall notify in writing all abutting property owners and occupants of any proposed street name changes, giving all pertinent information, including the date of the public hearing to be held as required in this section. The notice shall be mailed to the addresses shown on the tax records of the city and to the managing authority if such street address contains more than one unit. These notices shall be mailed at least 14 days prior to the date of the public hearing.
(b)
The mayor and council shall conduct a public hearing on any proposed street name changed prior to its final adoption and after notification to all abutting property owners and occupants.
(c)
The city clerk shall advertise the public hearing in a newspaper of general circulation in the city at least once during the ten days immediately preceding the public hearing.
(d)
Whenever applicable, the city clerk shall make a good faith effort to contact and receive comment from at least one member of the family for whom the original street was named.
(e)
The effective date of each street name change shall be 30 days after the enabling ordinance becomes effective.
(Ord. No. 2002-3, § 1(20-7), 4-8-2002)
Sec. 50-6. - Construction standards.
Public streets and roads shall be constructed in accordance with the subdivision regulations and development standards of the city. Each street shall be inspected for compliance with city ordinances by the city engineer or his designee. The city engineer or his designee shall notify, in writing, the owner, developer and/or contractor of the objections to the acceptance of said street by the city as a public street, and provide a reasonable time for compliance with the existing ordinances.
(Ord. No. 2002-3, § 1(20-31, 20-32), 4-8-2002)
Secs. 50-7—50-9. - Reserved.
ARTICLE I-A. - COMPLETE STREETS

Sec. 50-10. - Title.
This article shall be known and referred to as the complete streets ordinance of 2016.
(Ord. No. 2016-12 , § 1, 11-14-2016)
Sec. 50-11. - Purpose.
The purpose of this complete streets ordinance is to ensure that the current and future needs of all users of the City of Fairburn's transportation system—including motorists, bicyclists, public transportation users, and pedestrians of all ages and abilities—are considered in all transportation projects and project phases. These "complete streets" principles shall be integral to the transportation policy of the City of Fairburn. All involved departments and agencies of the City of Fairburn, shall:
(1)
Make the practice of creating complete streets a routine part of everyday operations;
(2)
Approach every transportation project, program, and practice as an opportunity to improve streets and the transportation network for all users; and
(3)
Work in coordination with other departments, agencies, and jurisdictions to maximize opportunities for complete streets, connectivity, and cooperation.
(Ord. No. 2016-12 , § 1, 11-14-2016)
Sec. 50-12. - Standards and implementation.
(a)
All transportation projects in the city shall create complete streets that allow safe and convenient travel along and across streets for users of all ages and abilities and for all modes of transportation, including motorists, bicyclists, public transportation vehicles and their passengers, and pedestrians, rail, and truck freight.
(b)
Except as may otherwise be provided below, subsection (a) shall apply to all transportation projects in the City of Fairburn. This shall include new construction, reconstruction, restoration, and rehabilitation of highways, roads, and streets.
(c)
The City of Fairburn Department of Community Development shall develop and implement procedures, documents, training programs, and performance measures within one year of the effective date of the ordinance from which this article is derived to ensure that the needs of all users of the highways, roads, and streets in the City of Fairburn are substantively considered during all phases of all transportation projects, including funding, planning, designing, operating, and maintaining transportation infrastructure.
(d)
The City of Fairburn Department of Community Development shall develop and/or adopt design manuals, standards, and guidelines based on the latest and best practices of street design, construction, operations, and maintenance as these apply to bicycle, pedestrian, transit, and highway facilities. All manuals, standards, and guidelines must be made publicly available online. Recommended design guidelines include, but are not limited to:
•
A Policy on Geometric Design of Highways and Streets (American Association of State Highway and Transportation Officials);
•
Guide for the Development of Bicycle Facilities (American Association of State Highway and Transportation Officials);
•
Guide for the Planning, Design, and Operation of Pedestrian Facilities (American Association of State Highway and Transportation Officials);
•
Separated Bike Lane Planning and Design Guide (Federal Highway Administration);
•
Urban Bikeway Design Guide (National Association of City Transportation Officials);
•
Creating Walkable Communities (Federal Highway Administration Office of Safety);
•
Pedestrian & Streetscape Guide (Georgia Department of Transportation).
(Ord. No. 2016-12 , § 1, 11-14-2016)
Sec. 50-13. - Exceptions.
(a)
A transportation project shall not be required to accommodate the needs of a particular user group if the city administrator, in consultation with the department of community development, determines in writing that:
(1)
Such accommodation would constitute an unlawful act or result in an unlawful condition, or be contrary to terms and conditions applicable to the use of non-City of Fairburn transportation funds; or
(2)
The cost of accommodating the needs of the particular user group for the transportation project would be excessively disproportionate to the current or future need or probable use of the involved facility by the particular user group. "Excessively disproportionate" means to exceed 20 percent of the total project cost, excluding fees; provided, however, where accommodations provide public safety benefits to address a history of demonstrated bicycle or pedestrian crash history, these benefits of the proposed accommodations must be considered.
(b)
The determination shall be supported by data and written documentation. The determination shall identify the applicability of an exception for each user group whose needs will not be accommodated by the transportation project.
(Ord. No. 2016-12 , § 1, 11-14-2016)
Sec. 50-14. - Evaluation and reporting.
(a)
The department of community development shall establish benchmarks reflecting the ability of all users to travel safely and conveniently along highways, roads, and streets within the city's jurisdiction.
(b)
The City of Fairburn shall develop plans and set goals to ensure the successful implementation of the complete streets ordinance throughout the City of Fairburn. On or before the end of each fiscal year, each involved city department shall prepare an initial report to identify barriers, and propose solutions, to successful implementation of the Complete Streets Act in low- and moderate-income communities.
(c)
The City of Fairburn shall collect and monitor data to determine compliance with such benchmarks, including, but not limited to:
(1)
New miles of bicycle lanes and sidewalks; new street trees or plantings; the number of new curb ramps and improved street crossings; and improved signage;
(2)
Measurements regarding existing levels of service for different modes of transport and categories of users, including public transportation ridership;
(3)
Collision statistics by neighborhood and mode of transportation, as well as bicycle and pedestrian injuries and fatalities;
(4)
Other benchmarks to track the safety, functionality, and actual use of the neighborhoods and areas within the City of Fairburn by each category of users.
(d)
Within 60 days following the end of each fiscal year, the department of community development shall prepare an annual report to evaluate implementation of the complete streets ordinance. Each annual report shall include the data collected pursuant to this article, as well as a list of transportation projects initiated or completed during the most recent fiscal year. The report also shall include a list of ongoing transportation projects. If any exceptions are applied to transportation projects pursuant to section 50-13, such projects and the relevant exceptions shall be identified in the annual report.
(e)
All benchmarks and reports shall be made publicly available online.
(f)
For the purpose of this section, "low income community" refers to any census tract that meets one of the following criteria (as reported in the most recently completed decennial census published by the U.S. Bureau of the Census):
(1)
The poverty rate for the census tract is at least 20 percent; or
(2)
For a community located outside of a metropolitan area, the median family income (MFI) for a census tract does not exceed 80 percent of statewide MFI; or
(3)
For a community within a metropolitan area, the MFI for a census tract does not exceed 80 percent of the greater of statewide MFI or metropolitan area MFI; or
(4)
Within a possession of the United States, the MFI does not exceed 80 percent of the possession-wide MFI.
(g)
For the purpose of this section, "moderate income community" refers to any population whose income is between 81 and 95 percent of the MFI for the area.
(Ord. No. 2016-12 , § 1, 11-14-2016)
Sec. 50-15. - Effective date.
The requirements and provisions of this complete streets ordinance shall apply to every City of Fairburn transportation project for which preliminary design work is completed after December 1, 2016.
(Ord. No. 2016-12 , § 1, 11-14-2016)

ARTICLE II. - EXCAVATIONS AND OBSTRUCTIONS

Sec. 50-31. - Permit required.
It shall be unlawful for any person under any pretext whatever to make or place an excavation, embankment or obstruction in any street, alley, sidewalk, or other right-of-way in the city until written application has been made for a permit from the city administrator authorizing the same; except that in the event an emergency exists where the health and safety of the citizens of the city are in danger, or for the immediate preservation of property, an excavation, embankment or obstruction may be made to the extent of abating the dangerous condition or situation; provided that, upon relieving the existing emergency, the person making the excavation shall notify the city administrator and otherwise comply with the requirements of this article regulating same. The issuance of a permit is not an affirmative assumption by the city to supervise or ensure the permittee complies with all requirements. Failure of the permittee to comply will not be grounds for any actions against the city for negligence in issuing the permit.
(Ord. No. 2002-3, § 1(20-61), 4-8-2002)
Sec. 50-32. - Application for permit; issuance.
Each application for a permit under this article shall describe the character of the work to be done, a full description of the premises where such work is to be done and shall be signed by the person applying for the permit. The permit shall be issued in duplicate, the original to be delivered to the party applying therefor, and one copy to be filed in the office of the city administrator together with the application.
(Ord. No. 2002-3, § 1(20-62), 4-8-2002)
Sec. 50-33. - Permit conditions.
The conditions of the permit required herein are as follows:
(1)
That the permittee will perform all work in a good and workmanlike manner in strict accordance with the provisions of this article;
(2)
That the permittee will backfill any excavations in any streets, alleys, sidewalks, or other public ways in the city, so as to return the same to their original condition;
(3)
That the permittee will place and maintain lights and barricades and/or guardrails around all excavations, materials or equipment left in any street, alley, sidewalk, or other public place of the city; and
(4)
That the permittee will indemnify, enter, and defend the city from all damages, real or asserted, that may occur by reason of the operation.
(Ord. No. 2002-3, § 1(20-63), 4-8-2002)
Sec. 50-34. - Restoration of surface.
When any part of any street, avenue, alley or public easement in the city shall be excavated, torn, dug or taken up for any purpose or when an embankment or obstruction is placed thereon, the person doing the same shall, immediately upon the completion of such purpose, and within at least ten days after the completion of such purpose, backfill and compact the same to a firm and solid bearing and in such a manner as will entirely prevent the settling of such earth, and shall also relay the paving in a skillful and permanent manner, and in every case, to the satisfaction of the city administrator. Failure to comply with the requirements of this section shall constitute an offense, and each day such failure continues shall constitute a separate offense.
(Ord. No. 2002-3, § 1(20-64), 4-8-2002)
Sec. 50-35. - Lights and barricades.
It shall be unlawful for any person to make any excavation, embankment or obstruction of whatsoever kind in the city, whether the same be in, along or near any street or sidewalk, alley or other public way of any kind, without placing proper barricades and signal lights or other warnings, at, in or around the same, sufficient to warn the public of such excavation, embankment or obstruction and to protect all persons using reasonable care from an accident on account of the same.
(Ord. No. 2002-3, § 1(20-65), 4-8-2002)
Sec. 50-36. - Damaging streets, etc.; removing dirt.
No person shall cut down or destroy any embankment or other improvement of the streets, alleys, sidewalks or other public ways. No person shall be allowed to dig or haul any dirt from any of the streets or alleys of the city without written permission from the city.
(Ord. No. 2002-3, § 1(20-66), 4-8-2002)

ARTICLE III. - STRUCTURE NUMBERING

Sec. 50-61. - Official house and building numbering map.
Every residence, building, and business structure in the city shall be numbered in accordance with the general plan of numbering established for the city and shown on a map set up for that purpose on file in the office of the city clerk. The number appearing on said residence, building or business structure shall be that number assigned to it by the city or its authority.
(Ord. No. 2002-3, § 1(20-76), 4-8-2002)
Sec. 50-62. - Duty of owner; location of numbers.
(a)
The responsibility for placing or replacing the numbers on residences, buildings and structures is hereby placed upon the owner, and it shall be the owner's duty to see that such residence, building or structure has affixed to it the number assigned thereto. The actual numbers shall be furnished by said city at a cost established by the council and paid by the property owner.
(b)
The numbers shall be so placed as to be plainly visible from the street and sidewalk, and if the residence, building or structure is too far from the street and sidewalk for the numbers to be plainly visible on said building, then the number shall be placed on a permanent post or elsewhere near the street and sidewalk.
(Ord. No. 2002-3, § 1(20-77), 4-8-2002)

ARTICLE IV. - PUBLIC ASSEMBLY

Sec. 50-110. - Erection of tents for events, gatherings.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to place, locate, install or erect any tent within the corporate limits of the city except as hereinafter provided. For the purpose of this section, the term "tent" shall be construed to mean any temporary or portable structure or shelter made of canvas or other fabric including plastic stretched over poles or supported by air.
(b)
Any person, company or corporation desiring to locate a tent within the corporate limits of the city shall first make application to the city administrator setting forth the site location, identifying the location of a permanent structure and restroom facilities, the number of attendees anticipated at the event for which the tent is to be used, the location of parking areas and number of parking spaces to be utilized for the event, the size and type of tent to be used including the interior contents and components, and the length of time the tent will be on site. In addition, there shall be a permit fee in the amount established by the city council paid by the applicant on filling.
(c)
Any tent permitted for installation or erection shall be subject to the requirements set forth in the city's building and zoning regulations.
(d)
A tent may be installed or erected on:
(1)
Lots for which there are permanent restroom facilities readily available. No portable restroom facilities will be allowed.
(2)
Lots for which adequate off-street parking is available to accommodate the anticipated attendees.
(3)
Lots for which adequate permanent facilities are readily available to provide shelter during a threat of dangerous or inclement weather.
(e)
The provisions of this section shall not apply to the following tents:
(1)
Camping tents designed for sleeping.
(2)
Funeral home tents used for temporary cover from the elements during a grave side funeral service.
(f)
The maximum length of time a tent may be installed on any lot shall be one week per calendar year.
(Ord. No. 98-19, § 1, 8-8-1998)
Sec. 50-111. - Passing through streets; permit required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to cause to assemble in or upon any street in the city, any congregation of people of 25 or more in number, or cause to induce such congregation of people to pass along, upon, or through any street of the city to a common place called destination, without first obtaining from the city administrator a written permit for such purpose. Application for such permit shall be made in writing, plainly setting forth the character and purpose of the assembly or congregation of people, the time and place at which they desire to assemble, the streets upon which they desire to pass, and their destination. When such application is made, it shall be the duty of the city administrator to inquire into the character and purpose of the assembly or congregation of people, the character of those who desire to meet in, or to pass upon the streets of said city, as herein referred to; and if those desiring to meet or pass upon the streets in the manner herein referred to be of good character and the purpose of the meeting and the use of the streets to be orderly, the city administrator is authorized to grant such permit. Any and all persons who shall assemble in or congregate upon or pass along or upon any public street of said city, as herein referred to, without a permit having been granted for such purpose, shall be deemed to be in violation of this section.
(Code 1941, § 1223; Code 1966, § 16-2)
