ARTICLE II. - MOBILE AND MODULAR STRUCTURES

Sec. 38-19. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Modular structure means any structure to be used for housing or business purposes that is designed to be transported after fabrication to its site location. This definition shall apply to all structures regardless of whether the same shall be transported in its completed stage or in divisible component parts.
(Code 1966, § 5-5(a); Ord. No. 71-4, 6-28-1971)
Sec. 38-20. - Placement and installation unlawful.
It shall be unlawful to place, locate, install or erect any modular structure within the corporate limits of the city except as hereinafter provided.
(Code 1966, § 5-5(b); Ord. No. 71-4, 6-28-1971)
Sec. 38-21. - Application to locate; contents of application; fee.
Any person desiring to locate a modular structure within the corporate limits of the city shall first make application to the city council setting forth the site location, the type structure to be used including the interior contents and components. The application shall be accompanied by a permit fee in the amount established by the city council.
(Code 1966, § 5-6; Ord. No. 71-4, 6-28-1971)
Sec. 38-22. - Consideration of application.
The mayor and council, after considering an application as set forth in section 38-21 and finding that the same meets all of the requirements of the building, electrical and plumbing codes, zoning ordinance, set forth in chapter 80, and other applicable ordinances, may permit the installation or erection of the modular structure if, in their opinion, such installation would not be detrimental to the community and the city.
(Code 1966, § 5-7; Ord. No. 71-4, 6-28-1971)
Sec. 38-23. - Installation subject to building, electrical and plumbing fees.
All structures permitted for installation or erection shall be subject to all building, electrical and plumbing fees established by the city.
(Code 1966, § 5-5; Ord. No. 71-4, 6-28-1971)
