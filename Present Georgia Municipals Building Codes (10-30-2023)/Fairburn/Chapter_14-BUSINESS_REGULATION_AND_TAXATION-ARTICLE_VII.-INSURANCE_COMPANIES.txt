ARTICLE VII. - INSURANCE COMPANIES

Sec. 14-200. - Insurers' license fees.
The city has levied an annual license fee upon each insurer doing business within the city in the amount of $75.00. For each separate business location in excess of one not covered by section 14-201, which is operating on behalf of such insurers within the city, there is hereby levied a license fee in the amount of $75.00. For the purposes of this article, the term "insurer" means a company that is authorized to transact business in any of the classes of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5.
(Code 1966, § 14-51; Ord. No. 94-08, § 3, 10-24-1994; Ord. No. 2001-17, § 1(14-51), 10-28-2001; Ord. No. 2003-15, § 1, 11-10-2003; Ord. No. 2011-09, § 1, 10-12-2011)
Sec. 14-201. - License fees for insurers insuring certain risks at additional business locations.
For each separate business location, not otherwise subject to a license fee hereunder, operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing and in connection with such loans or sales offers, solicits or takes application for insurance through a licensed agent of an insurer for insurance, said insurer shall pay an additional license fee of $26.25 per location for the year 2012 and for each year thereafter.
(Code 1966, § 14-52; Ord. No. 94-08, § 3, 10-24-1994; Ord. No. 2001-17, § 1(14-52), 10-28-2001; Ord. No. 2003-15, § 2, 11-10-2003; Ord. No. 2011-09, § 2, 10-12-2011)
Sec. 14-202. - Insurers agency license fees; independent insurance agencies, brokers, etc., not otherwise licensed.
The city has levied an annual license fee upon independent agencies and brokers for each separate business location from which an insurance business is conducted and which is not subject to the company license fee imposed by section 14-200 in the amount of $75.00 for each such location with the city.
(Code 1966, § 14-53; Ord. No. 94-08, § 3, 10-24-1994; Ord. No. 2001-17, § 1(14-53), 10-28-2001; Ord. No. 2011-09, § 3, 10-12-2011)
Sec. 14-203. - Gross premiums tax imposed on insurers.
The city has levied an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer writing life, accident and sickness insurance within the state in an amount equal to one percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.1. The term "gross direct premiums," as used in this section, shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 14-200.
(Code 1966, § 14-54; Ord. No. 94-08, § 3, 10-24-1994; Ord. No. 2001-17, § 1(14-54), 10-28-2001; Ord. No. 2003-15, § 3, 11-10-2003)
Sec. 14-204. - Gross premiums tax, all other insurers.
The city has levied an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer, other than an insurer transacting business in the class of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5(1), doing business within the state in an amount equal to 2.5 percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2. The term "gross direct premiums," as used in this section, shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 14-200.
(Ord. No. 2003-15, § 4, 11-10-2003)
Sec. 14-205. - Due date for license fees.
License fees imposed in sections 14-200 and 14-201 shall be due and payable on January 1, 2012, and on January 1 of each subsequent year.
(Code 1966, § 14-56; Ord. No. 94-08, § 3, 10-24-1994; Ord. No. 2001-17, § 1(14-56), 10-28-2001; Ord. No. 2003-15, § 5, 11-10-2003; Ord. No. 2011-09, § 4, 10-12-2011)
Sec. 14-206. - Lien taken for delinquent fees and taxes.
In addition to other remedies provided by law for the collection of the license fees and taxes herein levied, the city administrator, upon any fee or tax or installment of said fee or tax becoming delinquent and remaining unpaid, shall issue execution for the correct amount of said fee or tax against the persons, partnership or corporations liable for said fee or tax, which said execution shall bear interest at the highest rate allowable under law from the date when such fee, tax or installment becomes delinquent, and the lien shall cover any property in the county of the person, partnership or corporation liable for said fee or tax, all as provided by the city's Charter and the laws of the state. The lien shall become fixed on and date from the time when such fee, tax or installment thereof becomes delinquent. The execution shall be levied by the city administrator upon the property of the defendant in execution located in said jurisdiction, and sufficient property shall be advertised and sold to pay the amount of said execution, with interest and costs. All other related proceedings shall be as provided by the city's Charter and ordinances and the laws of the state. The defendant in said execution shall have rights of defense by affidavit of illegality and otherwise, as provided by the applicable laws in regard to tax executions. When a nulla bona entry has been entered by the proper authority upon an execution issued by the city administrator against any person defaulting on the fees and taxes levied under this article, the person against whom the entry was made shall not be allowed or entitled to have or collect any fees or charges whatsoever for services rendered after the entry of the nulla bona. If at any time after the entry of nulla bona has been made, the person against whom the execution issues pays the fee or tax in full together with all interest and costs accrued on the fee or tax, the person may collect any fees and charges due him as though he had never defaulted in payment of the fees or taxes.
(Code 1966, § 14-57; Ord. No. 94-08, § 3, 10-24-1994)
