ARTICLE VII. - MOVING EXISTING STRUCTURES

Sec. 62-251. - Procedure for review by city council.
It shall be unlawful for any used or secondhand building to be brought into the city from any point outside the city or for any such building already within the city to be removed from its present location to any other location without special written permission from the city council, which permission shall not be given in any case unless and until there shall have been a public hearing on written application of which hearing notice shall have been published once a week for two weeks at the expense of the applicant in the official newspaper of the city, and also that a notice eight inches by 12 inches in size be placed on the property for two weeks prior to the hearing, which notice of such public hearing shall consist of a copy of such application for permission to remove such used or secondhand buildings, and such other information as may be required by the city council.
(Code 1966, § 5-12; Ord. of 8-28-1967, § 1; Mo. of 10-9-1967)
Sec. 62-252. - Moving of unfit buildings prohibited.
In no event will permission be granted to move into the city any building which any government or governmental agency has found unfit for human habitation or which has been constructed of defective materials, or which has become defective from age, fire, abuse, or other cause.
(Code 1966, § 5-13; Ord. of 8-28-1967, § 4)
Sec. 62-253. - Notice of intent to move house on lot.
It shall be unlawful for any house to be moved from a location outside the city to a lot within the city unless there shall have been posted on the lot to which such house is to be moved a notice of the intention to move such house to such location, which notice shall be posted and shall conform to all rules, regulations, and provisions relating to signs posted on lots to which houses are moved.
(Code 1966, § 5-14; Ord. of 8-28-1967, § 5)
Sec. 62-254. - Application for council approval; advertising costs.
The application for council approval shall be in writing addressed to the city council, and delivered to the city clerk at least five days before the next regular meeting of the city council, together with a deposit of the amount of money necessary to pay for the advertising cost, at which meeting of the city council such application shall be presented by the city clerk to the city council for its consideration. If such application is found in order, and the city council determines that sufficient funds have been deposited with the city treasurer to pay the cost of advertising the notice as hereinbefore provided, it shall fix a time and place for a hearing of such application and order publication of such notice stating the time and place when such application will be considered by the city council.
(Code 1966, § 5-15; Ord. of 8-28-1967, § 2)
Sec. 62-255. - Contents of application for council approval.
The application shall include the name of the applicant, the location and type of building the applicant desires permission to move, and the size and age of such building, the use to which it has been put, and the location to which the applicant desires to move it, the purpose for which such building is to be moved, and whether the applicant is the owner of such building and the lot to which he desires to move it, and such other information as the city council may require.
(Code 1966, § 5-16; Ord. of 8-28-1967, § 3)
Sec. 62-256. - Moving permit required.
It shall be unlawful to move any house in, on, along or across any public street, alley, lane, road or way in the city, unless a special moving permit, in writing, has been issued for this purpose within the previous 24 hours by the police department. The moving shall be in strict compliance with the provisions of this article.
(Code 1966, § 5-17; Ord. of 8-28-1967, § 6)
Sec. 62-257. - Application for moving permit.
Request for the moving permit required by section 62-256 shall be in writing, filed with the chief of police at least 24 hours before it is desired to move such house, and shall state the correct name and post office and street address of such mover, and the time when and the streets along or across which the house is to be moved.
(Code 1966, § 5-18; Ord. of 8-28-1967, § 8)
Sec. 62-258. - Issuance of permit.
It shall be the duty of the police department to grant the permit upon approval thereof by the fire department, to which the police department shall submit the same approval by the fire department, unless it appears that the moving of such house cannot or will not be done within 24 hours from the time the permit would be issued, or that such moving will expose other persons or their property to great danger or injury or damage, or unless it appears that such moving will unreasonably congest traffic or work, or be an unnecessary inconvenience, delay, injury, damage or expense to others.
(Code 1966, § 5-19; Ord. of 8-28-1967, § 7; Ord. No. 69-2, 10-13-1969)
Sec. 62-259. - Deposits against loss or damage.
If it appears that there is some reasonable prospect of injury or damage to others, but that refusal of such permit would work a more serious loss or injury to the owner or mover of such house, such permit may be issued upon deposit of an amount of money with the chief of police by the owner of the house or the applicant for the permit to indemnify and save anyone from loss or damage resulting from the moving, such amount of money shall be fixed by the chief of police. In lieu of a money deposit, the applicant may file proof of a valid liability insurance policy.
(Code 1966, § 5-19; Ord. of 8-28-1967, § 7; Ord. No. 69-2, 10-13-1969)
Sec. 62-260. - Form of permit.
The moving permit shall be signed by the chief of police or his duly authorized agent and shall set forth the time when and streets over and across which the house is to be moved.
(Code 1966, § 5-20; Ord. of 8-28-1967, § 9)
Sec. 62-261. - Permit and inspection fees.
(a)
Any person, owner or mover, who shall move a house or other building as a unit on, across or along any public street or highway, within the city, that due to its size or shape blocks or protrudes into the opposing traffic lanes; or requires the removal, lifting or relocating of the city electrical distribution wires; or requires the removal, lifting, or realignment of traffic signal devices shall pay a permit fee in the amount established by the city council; provided, however, that said fee shall not be required of a church, hospital or other charitable or eleemosynary institution. It shall be unlawful to move any house or building requiring any of the aforesaid without first obtaining said permit.
(b)
No permit shall be issued to move a house, building or other structure into the city until the same has been inspected on the site prior to removal by the city superintendent to ascertain that said house, building or other structure meets the minimum building code requirements of the city. An inspection fee shall first be paid in the amount established by the city council to cover the expenses of said inspection. The inspection fee is nonrefundable.
(Code 1966, § 5-21; Ord. of 8-28-1967, § 17; Ord. No. 69-2, 10-13-1969; Ord. No. 70-1, 5-25-1970)
Sec. 62-262. - Moving prohibited under certain circumstances.
No house shall be moved nor shall any permit be granted for the removal of any house in the city at any time when it is not likely to be reasonably safe, or when it is reasonable to suppose there will be a street congestion for an unreasonable length of time on account of weather conditions or any other unusual condition or circumstances, except upon such reasonable terms and conditions as may be prescribed by the joint chiefs of the police and fire departments of the city.
(Code 1966, § 5-22; Ord. of 8-28-1967, § 16)
Sec. 62-263. - Hours when moving permitted.
No house shall be moved over or across any heavily traveled street or street crossing, except between the hours of 1:00 a.m. and 6:00 a.m. in the morning and then only so as to interfere with the orderly and regular flow of traffic as little as possible.
(Code 1966, § 5-23; Ord. of 8-28-1967, § 10)
Sec. 62-264. - Notice to fire department.
No house shall be moved on or across any public street, lane, road, alley or way in the city without first giving the fire department of the city a written notice of the time of the moving and the names of the streets over, through, in or across which such house is to be moved in order that the fire department of the city may not be retarded or unnecessarily delayed in discharging its duties by an obstructed or congested street resulting from moving a house into, in or out of the city.
(Code 1966, § 5-24; Ord. of 8-28-1967, § 15)
Sec. 62-265. - House mover's equipment.
No house mover shall commence or undertake to move any house in the city without adequate equipment in good working order.
(Code 1966, § 5-25; Ord. of 8-28-1967, § 13)
Sec. 62-266. - Required lighting.
No house shall be moved without at least one red light at the top and one at the bottom of each back corner to indicate the outside area of the street occupied by such house and likewise at each front corner of the house for the same purpose. Such lights shall be so located that the operator of any vehicle approaching such house from any direction can readily see how much of the street is being taken up by the moving of the house. All of the lights shall be well-lighted at all times.
(Code 1966, § 5-26; Ord. of 8-28-1967, § 11)
Sec. 62-267. - Guards required during moving.
No house shall be moved in the city on any vehicle without a guard attending on either side and in front of and behind such moving house as the same moves along or across any street to give warning of its presence. The guards shall remain at a sufficient distance from the house to give ample warning of the presence of such house in the street in ample time to avoid any collision with any moving vehicle, train, locomotive or other moving object.
(Code 1966, § 5-27; Ord. of 8-28-1967, § 12)
Sec. 62-268. - House not to be left unattended.
No house shall be left unattended on or partly on or across any public street, lane, alley or way in the city at any time of the day or night.
(Code 1966, § 5-28; Ord. of 8-28-1967, § 14)
Sec. 62-269. - Special redevelopment moving permits.
Any person who shall desire to move any house or building which shall be subject to destruction or displacement because of urban renewal or the construction of roads, streets, highways, hospitals or other public buildings in the city may do so by first obtaining a special permit to move such house or building from the city council, and by paying any license fee which may be required by the city.
(Code 1966, § 5-29; Ord. of 8-28-1967, § 18)
Secs. 62-270—62-284. - Reserved.
