ARTICLE II. - PARKING

Sec. 56-19. - When lights required; reflectors.
Whenever a vehicle is parked on a street during the time between one-half hour after sunset and one-half hour before sunrise, or at any other time when there is not sufficient light to render clearly any vehicle on the street from a distance of 200 feet, there shall be displayed at least one light on each end of such vehicle. Where red glass reflectors are installed on the rear of vehicles, the same may be used without turning on red taillights. Reflectors must be of not less than two inches in diameter.
(Code 1941, § 1119; Code 1966, § 24-1)
Sec. 56-20. - Parallel parking; exceptions.
Except when necessary in obedience to traffic regulations or traffic signs, or traffic signals or orders of a police officer, the driver of a vehicle shall not stop, stand or park such vehicle in any street or roadway other than parallel with the edge of such street or roadway headed in the direction of traffic, and with the curbside wheels of said vehicle within six inches of the edge of the roadway.
(Code 1941, § 1106; Code 1966, § 24-2)
Sec. 56-21. - No parking signs.
It shall be unlawful for any person to park an automobile or truck or any vehicle propelled by gasoline at any place on any street in the city where there is a yellow curb or a "No Parking" sign.
(Code 1941, § 1121; Code 1966, § 24-3)
Sec. 56-22. - Prohibited parking areas.
When prohibited by this article or other ordinance, no person shall park a vehicle during the hours prohibited at places so indicated by official signs.
(1)
There shall be no parking on a residential street directly across from another vehicle already parked or within 50 feet of that vehicle;
(2)
There shall be no on-street parking within any residential cul-de-sac;
(3)
There shall be no on-street parking within 50 feet of a residential street intersection; and
(4)
There shall be no parking on a residential street facing in the direction of oncoming traffic.
(5)
There shall be no parking on a residential street opposite a driveway.
(Code 1966, § 24-4; Ord. of 4-27-1964; Ord. of 2-13-1967; Ord. No. 68-10, 6-24-1968; Ord. No. 68-14, 9-9-1968; Ord. No. 75-5, 8-25-1975; Ord. No. 88-2, § 2, 7-11-1988; Ord. No. 2004-5, 4-11-2004; Ord. No. 2010-10, § 1, 10-25-2010)
Sec. 56-23. - Time limitations for parking.
Where time limitations for parking are imposed by this article or other ordinance, no person shall park a vehicle longer than the hours indicated on official signs posted at such places.
(Code 1966, § 24-5; Ord. of 4-27-1964; Ord. No. 88-2, § 3, 7-11-1988)
Sec. 56-24. - Restrictions on parking of vehicles with three or more axles.
(a)
No motorized vehicle in excess of 7,000 pounds (empty weight), trailer, boat, vehicle not in operating condition or currently licensed, vehicle with more than four wheels, or RV shall be permitted to be parked in front of the main dwelling on a lot in a residential area or neighborhood.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to park or caused to be parked on any street within the city a truck or trailer which has three or more axles for a period of more than one hour in any one place. The provisions of this subsection shall not apply to vehicles making deliveries of goods or merchandise to residents or businesses located in the city or to vehicles temporarily parked for the purpose of loading and unloading household or business goods and furnishings (i.e., moving van type vehicles).
(Ord. No. 98-08, § 1, 3-10-1998)
Sec. 56-25. - Impounding of vehicles.
(a)
Any vehicle parked in the restricted area described in this article for a period of time in excess of two hours, shall be impounded, moved from the street and stored, subject to being claimed by the owner who may obtain said vehicle by the payment of the towing and storage charges. Any officer of the city authorized to enforce traffic regulations and ordinances in the city is hereby authorized to impound said vehicles remaining in said parking area for more than two hours, and to store the same in some off-street parking area, either indoors or out-of-doors.
(b)
The charges for towing and storage provided for in subsection (a) of this section shall constitute a lien upon the vehicle so impounded in favor of the city, and shall be enforced in the same manner as the collection of taxes.
(Code 1966, § 24-7; Ord. of 4-27-1964; Ord. No. 93-21, § 2, 11-22-1993)
Sec. 56-26. - Temporary and emergency parking restrictions.
Temporary or emergency no parking restrictions may be established and posted by order of the city engineer, fire chief, or police chief. Violations of such restrictions shall be subject to the enforcement and impoundment provisions of section 56-25.
(Ord. No. 2010-03, 5-10-2010)
Sec. 56-27. - Parking on narrow streets.
(a)
Placement of signs. The city engineer, at the direction of the city council, shall erect signs prohibiting parking upon any street where it is determined that the width of the roadway may not permit free and unobstructed passage of a vehicle unless such parking is prohibited.
(b)
Effect of signs. When official signs prohibiting parking are erected upon narrow streets as authorized in this section, no person shall park a vehicle upon any such street in violation of any such sign.
(c)
[Specific streets.] Pursuant to the provisions of this code section, parking on the following streets shall be prohibited:
Belmont Terrace;
Bluegrass View;
Horseshoe Lane;
Trotters Circle;
Apian Way;
Horseman Run;
Jules Trace;
Seneca Road.
Fairview Drive, west side, between Estes Street and Barton Street.
(Ord. No. 2010-10, § 2, 10-25-2010; Ord. No. 2010-11, § 1, 11-8-2010; Ord. No. 2013-32, § 1, 11-25-2013)
Sec. 56-28. - Commercial vehicles.
(a)
No more than one commercial vehicle may be parked on a residential lot overnight (11:00 p.m.—7:00 a.m. eastern standard time or eastern daylight savings time, as appropriate) except in furtherance of a temporary and city permitted construction project upon the residential lot.
(b)
For the purpose of subsection (a), commercial vehicle shall mean a vehicle that carries or contains commercial or industrial equipment or trade tools on the exterior of the vehicle, including, but not limited to, ladders, power equipment, earth moving equipment, and lawn equipment; or a vehicle on which is painted, drawn, or attached a sign advertising a commercial business or trade, excluding license plates and bumper stickers.
(c)
For purposes of this section, vehicle means motorized equipment, or nonmotorized equipment designed to be pulled by motorized equipment, designed to travel with persons or cargo upon land, air, or water. Vehicle shall include, without being limited to, cars, automobiles, trucks, trailers, boats, airplanes, motorcycles, golf carts, campers, recreation vehicles and buses. A trailer designed to carry one boat, or up to two motorcycles, or up to two personal watercraft, when loaded, as designed, with one boat, or up to two motorcycles, or up to two personal watercraft, shall be counted as one vehicle for the purpose of this section. Whenever a trailer, designed to be pulled by a car, automobile, or truck, is hitched to a car, automobile, or truck for travel on public roads, the hitched trailer and the car, automobile, or truck shall be considered one vehicle.
(d)
Except as otherwise provided at subsections (a) and (e), or except when being utilized in a temporary, city permitted construction project on the lot in question, it shall be unlawful for the driver of a vehicle defined below to park overnight as defined at subsection (a) above, and it shall be unlawful for the owner or occupant of the residential zone lot in question to knowingly allow to be parked overnight, upon a residential zoned lot, any vehicle meeting any one of the following classifications:
(1)
A commercial vehicle exceeding 30 feet in length (for purposes of this length restriction, a trailer hitched to a car, motor vehicle, or truck shall be measured as a separate vehicle);
(2)
A commercial vehicle whose gross vehicle weight rating exceeds 18,000 pounds;
(3)
A commercial vehicle which sits upon more than two axles;
(4)
A commercial vehicle which sits, or is designed to sit, upon more than six wheels;
(5)
A trailer of a tractor trailer or a semi-trailer;
(6)
Dump truck, wrecker truck, tow truck, auto transportation truck, freight truck, logging truck, dirt-sand-gravel hauling truck, bull dozer, bucket truck, cement truck, crane, forklift, backhoe, track hoe, articulated truck, compactor roller, excavator, front-end loader, motor grader, buses, including school buses.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for the owner or driver of a commercial vehicle to park said vehicle overnight (between 11:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m. eastern standard time or eastern daylight savings time, as appropriate) on any public street within the city unless the street is posted with a sign, authorized by the city's police chief, which sign allows or authorizes parking of vehicles overnight.
(Ord. No. 2010-10, § 2, 10-25-2010)
Sec. 56-29. - Application for permit.
(a)
Where parking has been prohibited under subsection 56-27(c) and it is determined that exceptional circumstances exist warranting consideration of special, temporary parking needs, application for a special use parking permit shall be made to the chief of police, or his designee.
(b)
Said applications may be submitted only by residents of properties that have addresses on the streets specified in subsection 56-27(c).
(Ord. No. 2010-12, § 1, 11-22-2010)
Sec. 56-30. - Limitations on special permit parking.
(a)
A motor vehicle which displays a valid residential parking permit issued under section 56-29 shall be allowed to stand or park in the residential permit parking area for which the permit has been issued without being limited by the restrictions established in subsection 56-27(c).
(b)
Such permit does not authorize a motor vehicle to stand or park in any place where or during any time when the stopping, standing, or parking of a motor vehicle is otherwise prohibited by this article, is designated for other uses, or inhibits movement of emergency vehicles.
(c)
Permit shall be limited to not more than a 24-hour period.
(d)
Not more than four permits may be issued to the same address within a calendar month.
(Ord. No. 2010-12, 11-22-2010)

ARTICLE III. - TRAFFIC SIGNS AND SIGNALS

Sec. 56-54. - Traffic control devices.
(a)
The police chief, or his designee, upon approval by resolution of the mayor and council, is authorized to designate and maintain with appropriate control signs, markings and devices after engineering and traffic investigations:
(1)
Speed zones;
(2)
Truck routes and streets to be designated specifically to prohibit various classes of trucks;
(3)
One-way streets and other directional control devices;
(4)
Freight loading zones and regulations;
(5)
Parking and no parking zones and regulations thereon;
(6)
Stop, yield and other right-of-way signs; and
(7)
Stop signals and other traffic signals.
(b)
The police chief, or his designee, is authorized to issue written orders designating appropriate traffic control signs, markings and devices after engineering and traffic investigations:
(1)
Pedestrian crosswalks;
(2)
Other safety zones for pedestrians;
(3)
Traffic lanes; and
(4)
Any other sign, marking or zone necessary for orderly and safe conditions on the streets of the city.
(Code 1966, § 24-10; Ord. No. 88-2, § 1, 7-11-1988)
Sec. 56-55. - Schedule of rules and regulations.
The police chief shall maintain or cause to be recorded a current schedule of all traffic rules, regulations and orders under this article, which records shall be available for inspection by the public. For items listed in section 56-54(a), this schedule, and any amendments thereto, shall become effective only upon approval thereof by ordinance of the mayor and council; and this schedule is hereby incorporated herein and copies thereof shall be available for public inspection in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1966, § 24-10; Ord. No. 88-2, § 1, 7-11-1988)
Sec. 56-56. - Specifications for traffic control devices.
All traffic control signs, signals, devices and markings shall conform to specifications in the Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices adopted by the state transportation board. All signs and signals required hereunder for a particular purpose shall, so far as practicable, be uniform as to type and location throughout the city. All traffic control devices so erected and not inconsistent with the provisions of state law or this Code shall be official traffic control devices of the city.
(Code 1966, § 24-10; Ord. No. 88-2, § 1, 7-11-1988)
Sec. 56-57. - Violation by operators of vehicles or pedestrians prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for the operator of any vehicle or any pedestrian to violate or disobey the instructions of any official traffic sign or signal placed in accordance with the provisions of this article, unless otherwise directed by a police officer.
(Code 1966, § 24-19)

Sec. 56-94. - Truck travel restricted to truck routes; exceptions.
It shall be unlawful for a person or business entity to operate or park a truck on any city street, road, drive, or avenue which is not designated as a truck route pursuant to section 56-93, except under the following circumstances:
(1)
Any truck may travel via the most direct route possible from a designated truck route to a location in the city not on a designated truck route for the exclusive purpose of local pick up, delivery, repair, or other necessary business, subject to verification of such purpose by written invoices, orders, delivery instructions, or such other written documents in possession of the truck operator; provided, however, this exception shall not apply in the case of a business located in a zoning district designated for residential use.
(2)
Travel within the city pursuant to a permit issued under section 56-95.
(3)
Truck travel authorized by a contract with the city for municipal services, including, without being limited to, solid waste and yard waste pick up.
(Ord. No. 2014-07, § 3, 4-14-2014; Ord. of 4-22-2019(2) , § 3)
Sec. 56-95. - Travel off truck routes; special permit required.
The city police chief, or police chiefs designee, may, upon application in writing and for good cause shown, issue a special permit, in writing, authorizing the owner or operator of a truck to operate and/or park such specified vehicle on any street, road, drive, or avenue in the city other than those routes listed in section 56-93, provided, further, that said permit may contain such conditions, restrictions, and limitations as are deemed necessary by the police chief or police chiefs designee to protect the public health, safety, and welfare. The fee for such permit shall be $50.00 per truck. Permits may be issued for any period of time not to exceed 30 days. The fee shall be collected by the city as a condition of the issuance of the permit.
(Ord. No. 2014-07, § 3, 4-14-2014)
Sec. 56-96. - Special permit; grounds for revocation; notice.
The police chief, or police chiefs designee, may revoke any special permit granted pursuant to this article upon reasonable cause being shown that the holder of the permit has violated the provisions of the permit, or that the public health, safety or welfare is endangered by the continuation of the permit. The holder of the permit shall be given notice of such revocation in writing by personal service or by certified mail, return receipt requested.
(Ord. No. 2014-07, § 3, 4-14-2014)
Sec. 56-97. - Appeal from administrative decision.
Any applicant for or holder of a special permit pursuant to this article, or any resident of the city who is personally and specially affected by the granting, denial, or revocation of such a permit, may appeal the administrative decision of the police chief, or police chiefs designee, to the city administrator. Such appeal must be made in writing within ten days of the administrative decision. All parties to the appeal will be given not less than ten days advance written notice of the date, time and place of the hearing of such appeal. The city administrator's decision on such an appeal shall be in writing and shall be the final decision of the city. Appeals from the city administrator's decision shall be by writ of certiorari to the superior court.
(Ord. No. 2014-07, § 3, 4-14-2014)
Sec. 56-98. - Penalties.
The operator, owner or lessee of any truck in violation of section 56-94 shall be subject to a minimum fine of $350.00 for the first offense and a minimum fine of $450.00 for any second or subsequent violation; provided, further, that all violations of any provision of this article shall be subject to a fine not to exceed $1,000.00, or up to 60 days in jail, or both.
(Ord. No. 2014-07, § 3, 4-14-2014; Ord. of 4-22-2019(2) , § 4)

ARTICLE V. - VEHICLE EQUIPMENT

Sec. 56-111. - Overloading; seating of passengers.
It shall be unlawful for the driver of any vehicle to drive the same when such vehicle is so loaded, or when there are in the front seat of such vehicle such number of persons as to interfere with the operator's control over the driving mechanism of the vehicle or obstruct the view of the driver to the front or sides of the vehicle.
(Code 1941, § 1117; Code 1966, § 24-69)
Sec. 56-112. - Interfering with view of driver.
It shall be unlawful for any passenger to ride in any vehicle in such a position as to interfere with the driver's view ahead or to the sides or to interfere with the operator's control over the driving mechanism of the vehicle.
(Code 1941, § 1118; Code 1966, § 24-70)

ARTICLE VI. - BICYCLES, ROLLER SKATES AND SKATEBOARDS

Sec. 56-138. - Traffic ordinances apply to persons riding bicycles.
Every person riding a bicycle upon a roadway shall be granted all of the rights and shall be subject to all the duties applicable to the driver of a vehicle by this chapter, except as to special regulations in this Code and except as to those provisions of this chapter which by their very nature can have no application.
(Ord. No. 2002-3, § 1(20-86), 4-8-2002)
Sec. 56-139. - Riding on sidewalks.
(a)
No person shall ride a bicycle upon a sidewalk within a district zoned as business.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to ride a bicycle on any sidewalk located within the area designated as the downtown tax district.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to roller skate or ride a skateboard on the public streets or on any sidewalk located within the area designated as the downtown tax district.
(Ord. No. 2002-3, § 1(20-88), 4-8-2002)
