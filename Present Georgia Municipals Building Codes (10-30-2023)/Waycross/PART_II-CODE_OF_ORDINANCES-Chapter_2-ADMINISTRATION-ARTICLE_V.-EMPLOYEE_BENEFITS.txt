ARTICLE V. - EMPLOYEE BENEFITS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-215. - Declared disaster overtime pay for exempt employees.
(a)
In the event that a disaster or a state of emergency is proclaimed or declared in accordance with chapter 14, article III, sections 14-99 and 14-100 of the Code and/or a disaster or a state of emergency that affects the city is proclaimed by either the county, the state, or declared by the president, exempt employees shall be compensated for the overtime hours that they work as disaster service workers.
(b)
Exempt employees, including the fire chief, the police chief and the exempt administration sworn personnel, working as disaster service workers shall be paid only for those hours actually worked in excess of 40 hours during their normal work week and the hourly rate of pay shall be calculated by dividing the exempt employers current annual salary by 2080 (time and one-half pay). The following exceptions in public safety shall apply:
(1)
Fire exempt employees working as disaster service workers shall be paid only for those hours actually worked in excess of 53 hours during their normal work week and the hourly rate of pay shall be calculated by dividing the exempt employers current annual salary by 2756 (time and one-half pay).
(2)
Police exempt employees working as disaster service workers shall be paid only for those hours actually worked in excess of 43 hours during their normal work week and the hourly rate of pay shall be calculated by dividing the exempt employers current annual salary by 2236 (time and one-half pay).
(c)
Exempt employees working as disaster service workers may, at their option, elect to take either overtime pay as described in this section or compensatory time off at the equivalent rate of pay. Accrual of such compensatory time off shall not be counted against any other limits for compensatory time off that may otherwise exist in policy or memorandum of understanding.
(Ord. No. 16-25 , § 1, 12-20-2016)

DIVISION 2. - PERSONNEL

Sec. 2-237. - Personnel policies adopted by reference.
The personnel policies of the city are hereby adopted by reference as fully as if set out at length herein.
(Code 1977, § 2-171; Ord. No. 91-5, § 1, 4-16-1991)
Sec. 2-238. - Purpose.
The purpose of this division is to establish a system of personnel administration for the city. The system shall consist of the following merit principles:
(1)
Recruiting, selecting and promoting or advancing employees on the basis of their knowledge, skills and abilities.
(2)
Establishing pay rates and schedules consistent with the principles of comparable pay for comparable work.
(3)
Retaining employees on the basis of the competency of their job performance, correcting inadequate or incompetent job performance and separating or, where possible, reassigning employees whose inadequate or incompetent job performance cannot be corrected.
(4)
Establishing a uniform system of handling grievances and appeal rights based on the principles of due process.
(5)
Assuring equal opportunity to applicants and employees of the city in all aspects without regard to race, color, creed, national origin or ancestry, age, sex, political or religious affiliation.
(Code 1977, § 2-178; Ord. No. 79-36, § 1, 12-17-1979)
Sec. 2-239. - Classified and unclassified service.
(a)
All offices and positions of the city shall be and are hereby divided into those of classified service and those of unclassified service.
(1)
The classified service of employment with the city shall include all full-time positions of employment with the city, which are not specifically placed in the unclassified service to the city. Unless specifically designated otherwise, the merit system of personnel administration established herein shall apply only to employees of the classified service of employment to the city.
(2)
The unclassified service shall include the following:
a.
The mayor and members of the city commission and other elected positions.
b.
Members of boards and commissions, city manager, municipal court judge and judge pro tempore, city attorney, city physician, city auditor, and the following department heads and staff: city finance director, personnel director, human resources director, purchasing and property control director, garage superintendent, public safety director/police chief, fire chief, fire marshal, public works director, park and tree superintendent, streets and drainage superintendent, solid waste superintendent, community improvement director, code enforcement director, city engineer, water and sewer director, water/sewer construction and maintenance superintendent, administrative assistant to the city manager and the secretary to the city manager, and any other position for which the city commission shall approve a transfer into the unclassified service. The city manager shall establish with the city commission's approval administrative guidelines for the employment and retention of department heads and staff of the unclassified service. These guidelines shall not be a part of the personnel system or policies established herein.
c.
Persons employed to make or conduct a temporary and special inquiry, investigation, or examination on behalf of the city commission, a committee thereof, or the city manager.
d.
Temporary or part-time positions unless specifically covered by action of the city commission.
(b)
Nothing herein shall be construed as precluding the appointing authority from filling any position in the manner in which positions in the classified service are filled.
(Code 1977, § 2-179; Ord. No. 79-36, § 1, 12-17-1979; Ord. No. 05-19, § 1, 7-5-2005)
Sec. 2-240. - Administration officers; effect of policies.
(a)
Administration of personnel by city manager. The city manager shall be responsible for a personnel system conforming with the policies and principles established by this division. The city manager shall specifically be responsible for:
(1)
Administration of the personnel system;
(2)
Selection of a personnel director responsible for the technical direction and operation of the personnel system;
(3)
Appointment, removal, suspension and discipline of all employees of the city subject to the policies set forth in this division, the City Charter and state and federal law. This authority may be delegated, at the discretion of the city manager, to the personnel director or other department heads;
(4)
The establishment of the number of city employees in the various city departments and offices and determination of duties and compensation of employees in accordance with the policies set forth in this division and subject to budget limitations and approval of the city commission; and
(5)
Performance of other duties in personnel administration as may be prescribed by law, this division and policies established hereunder.
The city manager or his authorized agent shall be responsible for certification that the persons employed in the classified service have been appointed and employed in accordance with the provisions of this division and the policies hereunder. No city disbursing or auditing officer shall make or approve or take any part in making or approving any payment for personal service to any person holding a position in the Waycross city government unless said payroll voucher or account of such pay bears the certification of the city manager or his authorized agent.
(b)
Administration by personnel director. The city manager shall appoint a personnel director, whose appointment shall be confirmed by the city commission. The personnel director shall:
(1)
Administer, under the direction of the city manager, the personnel system as set forth in this division, and the personnel policies;
(2)
Recommend to the city manager personnel rules and regulations. Recommend changes to the personnel policies and to this division from time to time for the effective administration of the personnel system;
(3)
Maintain the administration of the classification and pay plan;
(4)
Develop, coordinate and administer recruiting and training programs for the city and its employees in order to have the best suited employee for a particular job;
(5)
Foster and develop programs for the improvement of employee effectiveness including training, safety, health and counseling;
(6)
Administer benefits;
(7)
Oversee and administer the maintenance of comprehensive personnel records of all the employees of the city;
(8)
Direct and administer the affirmative action program of the city;
(9)
Make an annual report to the city manager and city commission regarding the work of the department.
(c)
Administration of personnel policies, generally.
(1)
The personnel policies shall have the force and effect of law. The city manager shall present to the city commission personnel policies, rules and regulations for review and approval by the commission. The policies shall be effective following approval by the commission and after posting the notice and effective date of the proposed personnel policies, additions or changes thereto, for four weeks on the bulletin board or other posting place of every employee work site of the city; and issuance of a memorandum to all employees giving notice of a change in the policies. This memorandum shall be distributed one time only with the employees' paycheck approximately four weeks prior to the effective date of the policy change. During the four-week period, employees may voice any comments regarding the changes in the personnel policies.
(2)
The policies will cover specific procedures and policies governing the following phases of the personnel system:
a.
Administration of a position classification plan.
b.
Administration of a pay plan.
c.
Announcement of employee vacancies, acceptance of applications and selection methods.
d.
Establishment of promotional policies.
e.
Evaluation of the work of employees, including those serving probationary periods.
f.
Separation from service of employees by resignation, layoff, suspension and/or dismissal.
g.
Establishment of work schedules, attendance and leave regulations.
h.
Development of employee training and safety programs.
i.
Uniform system of handling all grievances and appeals.
j.
Rules governing the outside employment of city employees.
k.
Maintenance and use of necessary personnel forms and records.
l.
Such other matters as may be necessary to carry out the intent and purpose of this division.
(Code 1977, § 2-180; Ord. No. 79-36, § 1, 12-17-1979)
Sec. 2-241. - Discrimination prohibited.
Illegal discrimination against any employee in recruitment, examination, appointment, training, promotion, retention, discipline or any other aspects of personnel administration shall be prohibited.
(Code 1977, § 2-181; Ord. No. 79-36, § 1, 12-17-1979)
Sec. 2-242. - Severability.
If any policy or order hereunder, or application of such provision to any person or circumstances, shall be held invalid, the remainder of and the applications of such provision of such policy or order to persons or circumstances other than those to which it is held invalid, shall not be affected thereby. It is hereby declared as the intent that the policy or order would have been adopted had such invalid portion not been included therein.
(Code 1977, § 2-182; Ord. No. 79-36, § 1, 12-17-1979)
