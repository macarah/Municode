ARTICLE II. - FIRE DEPARTMENT[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Powers of local fire departments generally, O.C.G.A. § 25-3-1 et seq.; firefighter standards and training act, O.C.G.A., § 25-4-1 et seq.

Sec. 42-31. - Composition; rank.
(a)
The fire department shall consist of a chief and such subordinate officers and firefighters as may from time to time be provided by the city council.
(b)
Following the fire chief in rank shall be the assistant chief, captains, lieutenants, sergeants and the firefighters.
(Ord. of 6-8-2015, § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 42-32. - Fire chief;appointment.
The fire chief shall be appointed by the city manager, and shall be the department director of the fire department, reporting directly to the city manager.
(Ord. of 6-8-2015, § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 42-33. - Duties of fire chief.
The fire chief shall be responsible for and have control over the fire department and all fire apparatus belonging to the city, and shall have the following duties:
(1)
To properly extinguish all accidental or intentionally caused fires;
(2)
To enforce the fire prevention code, article Ill of this chapter, adopted by the city council;
(3)
To keep adequate records of all fires, inspections, equipment, fire prevention efforts, and other activities of the department;
(4)
To establish rules and regulations concerning the discipline, good order, proper conduct, care, and management of the fire department, subject to approval by the city council; and
(5)
To oversee the investigation as to the cause and origin of all extended or destructive fires occurring within the city limits.
(Ord. of 6-8-2015, § 1(Exh. A))
State Law reference— Powers and duties of department, O.C.G.A. §§ 25-3-1 et seq., 25-4-1 et seq.
Sec. 42-34. - Disability of fire chief.
In the event of resignation, removal, death, or disability of the fire chief, the officer next in rank shall perform the duties of the chief until removal of such disability or until a successor has been appointed.
(Ord. of 6-8-2015, § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 42-35. - Firefighters; qualifications; training.
(a)
Applicants shall have the following qualifications to become a member of the fire department:
(1)
Be at least 18 years of age;
(2)
Not have been convicted of a felony within ten years prior to employment; and
(3)
Have good moral character as determined by an investigation under the procedure approved by the Georgia Firefighters Standards and Training Council.
(b)
Upon employment, firefighters shall:
(1)
Complete all training required in the Woodstock Fire Department's Standard Operating Procedures and attain state certification as a firefighter within the first 12 months after being appointed a full-time paid member of the department.
(2)
Train, drill, or study as required by ISO and Georgia Firefighter Standards and Training Council and any other appropriate accrediting agency following the first year of employment.
(Ord. of 6-8-2015, § 1(Exh. A))
State Law reference— Qualifications of firefighters, O.C.G.A. § 25-4-8.
Sec. 42-36. - Duties of firefighters.
All firefighters shall become familiar with and proficient in the handling of all of the apparatus of the fire department, shall be prepared to respond to all alarms of fire, shall observe all rules and regulations of the fire department established by the fire chief, and shall obey all orders of the commanding officer at all fires.
(Ord. of 6-8-2015, § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 42-37. - Fire station inspections.
Each fire station and its equipment shall be examined regularly. Such inspections shall be rigidly made and shall ensure that all apparatus is maintained in excellent working condition at all times, is ready for immediate service, and that all fire stations and surroundings are in a clean and sanitary condition.
(Ord. of 6-8-2015, § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 42-38. - Command at fires.
In case of fire, the officer of the highest rank or designee at the fire shall take command of the fire department and direct the suppression of the fire in the best manner possible. Such commanding officer may cause buildings to be removed, torn down, or destroyed when such acts are necessary for the protection of other property and for the prevention of the spread of the conflagration.
(Ord. of 6-8-2015, § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 42-39. - Use of equipment.
(a)
No one is to take any city fire truck outside the city limits except as approved by the fire chief or for purposes described in the automatic aid contract with the county fire department or the state mutual aid group, as stated in section 42-42.
(b)
No persons other than those firefighters on official business shall tamper with firefighting equipment either on public or private property.
(Ord. of 6-8-2015, § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 42-40. - Persons in vicinity of fire; obstructing firefighting apparatus.
(a)
No persons other than firefighters, city officials, police officers, and the property owner or his agent shall be allowed within the immediate vicinity of a fire, unless such persons are given permission to do so by the fire chief.
(b)
No person shall obstruct in any manner any firefighting apparatus or any of the officers or members of the fire department in the performance of their duties.
(Ord. of 6-8-2015, § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 42-41. - Riding upon firefighting apparatus.
No persons other than members of the fire department or personnel covered under this article shall be permitted to ride upon the firefighting apparatus in going to or returning from emergency calls unless approved by the fire chief or designee.
(Ord. of 6-8-2015, § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 42-42. - County and state, automatic aid.
The city has an automatic aid agreement with the county and a mutual aid agreement with the state.
(Ord. of 6-8-2015, § 1(Exh. A))

Sec. 42-71. - Adoption of fire prevention codes.
(a)
The City of Woodstock adopts and enforces codes and standards contained in the local ordinances. The adoption of these codes and ordinances is by the consent of the city council in accordance with its charter. These local fire codes may be more restrictive than the codes that are adopted by the state but not less restrictive.
(b)
The city adopts and enforces the State Minimum Fire Safety Standards, as now or hereafter amended, as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 25-2-12, and appoints the fire chief or his designee as the fire marshal for the city.
(c)
For amendments enacted by the State Fire Marshal's Office, contact the Office at 404-656-7087.
A current copy of the fire prevention code and the life safety code shall be maintained on file in the office of the fire marshal where they are available for public inspection.
(d)
Exemptions from local fire code requirements. Individuals who are seeking relief from a local fire ordinance may request an exemption from said requirements by:
(1)
When requesting an exemption from a local fire ordinance through the DPC staff it must be submitted in writing in order to get a recommendation prior to presenting the request to the city council.
(2)
The request for an exemption to the local fire ordinances shall be made to the city council for approval.
(3)
The following conditions must be considered before an exemption from the requirements of the city's fire ordinances can be considered by the DPC and granted by the council:
a.
There are exceptional and extraordinary conditions pertaining to the building or a particular piece of property in question due to its size, shape or topography; and,
b.
The application of these regulations to this particular piece of property would create a practical difficulty or unnecessary hardship; and,
c.
Such conditions are peculiar to the particular piece of property involved; and,
d.
A literal interpretation of this ordinance would deprive the applicant of any rights that others in the city are allowed; and,
e.
Relief, if granted, would not cause substantial detriment of the public good, or impair the purposes and intent of the ordinance; and,
f.
That the authorizing of the exemption will not unreasonably increase the congestion of public streets, increase the danger of fire, imperil the public safety, or in any other respect impair the health, safety, comfort, morals or general welfare of the inhabitants of the city.
(4)
In granting an exemption, the council may attach thereto such conditions regarding the location, character and other features of the proposed building, structure, or use as it may deem advisable so that the purpose of this ordinance will be served.
(5)
The council shall not be authorized to grant an exemption to "Rules and Regulations for the State Minimum Fire Safety Standards" (O.C.G.A.) Section 25-2-4.
(6)
Following the review and consideration of an exemption application by the DPC recommendation shall be made by the DPC to the mayor and council to approve, approve with conditions or deny.
(Ord. of 6-8-2015, § 1(Exh. A))
State Law reference— Statewide application of minimum standard codes, O.C.G.A. § 8-2-25; definitions of standard codes for adoption, O.C.G.A. § 8-2-20; adoption of state fire safety standards, O.C.G.A. § 25-2-12.
Sec. 42-72. - Open burning.
(a)
Open burning for reasons other than the exceptions listed in this section is prohibited. Exceptions to the burning prohibition shall require a permit from the fire department's fire prevention bureau. A particular exception may be disallowed by the fire marshal to safeguard the public health, safety, and welfare of the citizens of the city, or because of other extenuating circumstances. No burn permits are required if the guidelines of subsection (b) of this section are met.
(b)
In accordance with EPD regulations and guidelines:
(1)
Burning shall not be within 50 feet of a street, building or place where people congregate.
(2)
A rake and/or shovel and waterhose shall be on hand at the burning site.
(3)
The fire must be attended at all times until it is extinguished.
(4)
No burning on overcast and/or windy days.
(5)
No burning on Sunday.
(6)
No burning at dusk and/or a night.
(c)
Small fires using only untreated wood are allowed to keep warm for outside working. Burning manmade materials or painted, stained, or impregnated wood is not allowed.
(d)
Operating devices using open flames such as tar kettles, blowtorches, welding torches, or other flame-making equipment are allowed.
(e)
Fires set for fire department training are allowed.
(f)
Burning for the cooking of food for immediate consumption and recreational activities is allowed.
(g)
No commercial burning is allowed in the city. The term "commercial burning" means any burning by any party other than a person who resides in a residential dwelling located on the property on which the burning is taking place.
(h)
It shall be unlawful to have an outdoor fire such as leaves, limbs, etc., upon any of the public streets, sidewalks, or alleys of the city.
(i)
Violations incur the liability of a criminal offense and, upon conviction, subject the offender to the maximum penalties provided by section 1-7.
(Ord. of 6-8-2015, § 1(Exh. A))
State Law reference— Limitations on open burning, Georgia Department of Natural Resources, Air Quality Control Regulations, Georgia Administrative Code, ch. 391-3-1.
Sec. 42-73. - Fire extinguishers.
Fire extinguishers shall be required in accordance with NFPA 10.
(Ord. of 6-8-2015, § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 42-74. - Fire prevention bureau established; duties.
(a)
The fire prevention code and all other fire service related ordinances shall be enforced by the bureau of fire prevention in the fire department of the city, which is hereby established and which shall be operated under the supervision of the chief of the fire department.
(b)
The assistant chief for fire prevention shall be the city fire marshal who shall be appointed by the chief of the department.
(c)
The fire prevention bureau shall be responsible for conducting investigations of fires in the city. The fire prevention bureau shall also be responsible for public fire safety education.
(d)
The chief of the department may detail such members of the fire department as inspectors/investigators or fire safety education specialists as shall, from time to time, are necessary. Each member assigned as fire inspector/investigator or fire safety education specialist may be reassigned to the fire suppression force at the discretion of the chief of the department.
(e)
A report of the bureau of fire prevention shall be made monthly and transmitted to the chief of the fire department. It shall contain all proceedings under the fire prevention code, the life safety code, fire investigations, and public fire safety education, with such statistics as the chief of the fire department may wish to include therein. The chief of the fire department shall also recommend any amendments to the code that, in his judgement, shall be desirable.
(Ord. of 6-8-2015, § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 42-75. - Fees.
Fees for plan review, inspections, reinspections and certificates of occupancy shall be as established from time to time by the city council.
(Ord. of 6-8-2015, § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 42-76. - Suppression company fire inspection program.
(a)
The bureau of fire prevention will be responsible for training the fire suppression personnel in familiarity of the standard fire prevention code. After being familiarized with the code, the suppression companies will coordinate with the fire prevention bureau and conduct scheduled fire prevention inspections throughout the city.
(b)
The bureau of fire prevention will intend to schedule the fire inspections in a way that all buildings affected by the code will be inspected.
(c)
Enforcement of the fire prevention code by the prevention and suppression personnel shall be carried out in the following manner:
(1)
Suppression companies shall perform inspections on all buildings in the city.
(2)
If violations are found the suppression companies will give each building a specified time period to have all violations corrected.
(3)
If the time period elapses and the violations have not been corrected, a citation may be issued by the fire marshal to the building owner or occupant and a new time period will be assigned.
(4)
After the second assigned time period has elapsed and it is determined the violations have still not been corrected, a second citation may be issued and a determination made by the fire marshal and the chief of the department whether or not to close the building until all violations are corrected.
(5)
All persons issued citations will appear before the municipal judge for determination of penalties.
(Ord. of 6-8-2015, § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 42-77. - Chronological order of inspections of a new building.
The order of plan reviews for new buildings shall be as follows:
(1)
Site plan;
(2)
Plat review (division of land, where applicable);
(3)
Building plan review;
(4)
Review of sprinkler plans and other fire protection systems:
a.
Plans for sprinkler systems and other fire protection systems, new or modifications of existing systems, shall be submitted to the Bureau of Fire Prevention for approval before construction on said systems commences. This section may be revised at any time deemed necessary by the city.
The order of inspections for new buildings shall be as follows:
(1)
Fifty percent inspection as needed for two-hour fire walls and other fire protection systems that will not be visible at the 80 percent inspection.
(2)
Eighty percent inspection to inspect fire walls, sprinkler piping, insulation and other fire protection system not visible at final inspection.
(3)
Final fire inspection: results are forwarded to the building official for the issuance of the certificate of occupancy pending the completion of all other required inspections.
(Ord. of 6-8-2015, § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 42-78. - Penalties.
(a)
Any person in a position of authority who shall violate any of the provisions of the fire prevention code, or fail to comply therewith, or who shall violate or fail to comply with any order made thereunder, or who shall build, in violation of any detailed statement, specifications or plans submitted and approved thereunder, or any certificate or permit thereunder and from which no appeal has been taken, or who shall fail to comply with such an order as affirmed or modified by the chief of the fire department, the city manager, or by a court of competent jurisdiction, within the time fixed herein, shall severally for each and every such violation and noncompliance respectively be punished, upon conviction, as provided by section 1-7. The imposition of one penalty for any such violation shall not excuse the violation or permit it to continue; and all such persons shall be required to correct or remedy the violations or defects within a reasonable time. When not otherwise specified, each five days that prohibited conditions are maintained shall constitute a separate offense.
(b)
The application of the penalty in subsection (a) of this section shall not be held to prevent the enforced removal of prohibited conditions.
(Ord. of 6-8-2015, § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 42-79. - Blasting.
Procedures for the issuance of blasting permits within the City of Woodstock shall be held on file with the Fire Department and are available upon request.
(Ord. of 6-8-2015, § 1(Exh. A))

Sec. 42-111. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context indicates a different meaning:
Attached dwelling means any residential structure attached [to] another.
Bureau of fire prevention means the city fire department, bureau of fire prevention for the city.
(Ord. No. 6712-2020 , § 1, 10-26-2020)
Sec. 42-112. - Use of sprinkler system in multifamily structures, attached dwellings.
(a)
All attached dwellings (excluding duplexes) and multifamily residential structures in the city shall be constructed with an approved sprinkler system.
(b)
An N.F.P.A. 13D fire sprinkler system is required on all attached dwellings not required to be sprinklered under other codes.
(c)
This section shall not apply to detached freestanding single-family residences with three or fewer stories.
(d)
The installer of any sprinkler system shall be a licensed Georgia fire sprinkler installer contractor.
(Ord. No. 6712-2020 , § 1, 10-26-2020)
Sec. 42-113. - Additions to the National Electric Code.
All commercial structures will be equipped with an outside electrical disconnect.
(Ord. No. 6712-2020 , § 1, 10-26-2020)
Sec. 42-114. - Knox Rapid Entry System.
Use of the Knox Rapid Entry System is authorized by Section 506 of the International Fire Code. The Code specifies that the fire department may require a key box containing access keys be installed at the entrance to structures or areas where access for the purpose of life safety or fire-fighting is restricted. The Code authorizes the fire department to determine the type of key box which is approved for use.
All new commercial construction equipped with a sprinkler system and/or monitored alarm system shall be equipped with a Knox key entry system.
(Ord. No. 6712-2020 , § 1, 10-26-2020)
