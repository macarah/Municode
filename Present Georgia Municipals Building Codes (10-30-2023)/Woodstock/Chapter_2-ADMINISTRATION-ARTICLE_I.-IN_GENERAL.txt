ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 2-1. - Departments established.
(a)
The following departments of the city are established:
(1)
Administration;
(2)
City clerk;
(3)
Community development;
(4)
Economic development;
(5)
Finance;
(6)
Fire;
(7)
Parks and recreation;
(8)
Police;
(9)
Public works.
(b)
Each department may be headed by a director who shall exercise such powers and perform such duties as provided by law or as prescribed from time to time by the city manager. Department directors shall report to the city manager.
(Code 1989, § 2-3-1; Ord. No. 04-03-23, 3-23-2004; Ord. No. 05-9-13, 9-13-2005; Ord. No. 2008-0019, 9-8-2009)
Charter reference— Organization and general provisions, § 3.10.
Sec. 2-2. - City manager.
(a)
The position of city manager is created. The city manager shall be responsible for the preparation and monitoring of the city budget and implementation of the council policy.
(b)
The financial and budget duties of the city manager shall be to:
(1)
Prepare and present an annual budget to the city council.
(2)
Monitor expenditures on authorized budgets for all departments.
(3)
Prepare quarterly financial reports to the city council.
(4)
Establish centralized purchasing guidelines.
(5)
Prepare requests for review on all public works projects, equipment purchases and service contracts.
(6)
Review and approve expenditures by all departments.
(c)
The personnel duties of the city manager shall be to:
(1)
Administer policies for managing city operations;
(2)
Administer personnel policy;
(3)
Approve hiring of personnel along with department heads;
(4)
Supervise department heads;
(5)
Plan with departments for longterm growth and objectives; and
(6)
Dismiss for cause any city personnel, other than department heads, and suspend department heads for up to three days. All employees retain the right of appeal to the city council.
(d)
Administrative polices and personnel decisions made by the city manager may be vetoed by the city council only. Personnel decisions may be appealed to the city council.
(Code 1989, § 2-3-2)
Charter reference— City manager, § 3.20.
Sec. 2-3. - City attorney.
(a)
The city attorney shall be appointed by the mayor, by and with the advice and consent of the city council, and shall serve until a successor is appointed and has qualified. No person shall be so appointed unless he is a member in good standing of the state bar and has been actively engaged in the practice of law for at least three years preceding his appointment.
(b)
Before entering upon the duties of his office, the city attorney shall take the oath prescribed by this Code for city officers.
(c)
The city attorney shall be the legal advisor and representative of the city and in such capacity shall:
(1)
Advise the council or its committees or any city officer, when thereto requested, upon all legal questions arising in the conduct of city business;
(2)
Prepare or revise ordinances when so requested by the council or any committee thereof;
(3)
Give his opinion upon any legal matter or question submitted to him by the council, or any of its committees, or by any city officer;
(4)
Prepare for execution or approve as to form all contracts and instruments to which the city is a party and approve, as to form, all bonds required to be submitted to the city;
(5)
Defend any and all suits and actions at law or equity brought against the city, unless otherwise directed by the city council;
(6)
Make immediate report to the city council of the outcome of any litigation in which the city has an interest;
(7)
Make an annual report to the city council of all pending litigation in which the city has an interest and the status thereof; and
(8)
Keep complete records and accurate records of the following, which records shall forever remain the property of the city:
a.
All suits in which the city had or has an interest, giving the names of the parties, the nature of the action, the disposition of the case or its status, if pending, and the briefs of counsel; and
b.
All written opinions prepared by the city attorney and all certificates or abstracts of titles furnished by him to the city, or any department official thereof.
(d)
The city attorney shall be compensated at an hourly rate to be set from time to time by the city council.
(Code 1989, § 2-3-3)
Charter reference— City attorney, § 3.30.
Sec. 2-4. - Hours of operation.
All administrative units shall be open between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 5:00 p.m. on weekdays. The units shall be closed on weekends and legal holidays.
(Code 1989, § 2-3-4; Ord. No. 04-03-23, 3-23-2004)
Sec. 2-5. - Daily deposit required.
All administrative units shall turn over monies received each day to such persons as designated from time to time by the city council to make deposits of city funds.
(Code 1989, § 2-3-5)
