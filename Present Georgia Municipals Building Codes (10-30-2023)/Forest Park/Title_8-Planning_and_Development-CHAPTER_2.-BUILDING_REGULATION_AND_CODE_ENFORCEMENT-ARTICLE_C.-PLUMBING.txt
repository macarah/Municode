ARTICLE C. - PLUMBING

Sec. 8-2-21. - Plumbing code adopted.
The International Code Council (ICC) Plumbing Code, specifically the 2003, edition thereof, together with any subsequent amendments, additions, or deletions thereto and future updated editions thereof, including any fee schedule included therein, as published by the International Code Council is hereby adopted as the code of the city relating to the installation of all plumbing, including the pipes for distributing the water supply, the fixtures for using water and drainage pipe for removing waste water and sewage, together with fittings and appurtenances of various kinds, all within or adjacent to the buildings within the city or where connected to the city's water or sewerage system, and the whole thereof, except the portions as may be hereafter deleted, altered or amended, is incorporated herein as fully as if set out at length, and all plumbers shall be required to make installations in conformity with the code and the regulations contained therein, a copy of which code is on file in the office of the city clerk. In the event of any conflict between the provisions of the plumbing code and the provisions of this Code, state law or ordinances, rules or regulations, the provisions.
(Ord. No. 92-006, 3-16-92, § 1; Ord. No. 04-75, § 1, 12-20-2004)
Sec. 8-2-22. - Metallic water system for replacement construction.
Every structure in the city built prior to January 1, 1984, shall be required to maintain metallic water systems on all repairs and replacement parts of the water lines and fixtures.
Sec. 8-2-23. - Purpose.
It is the purpose of sections 8-2-23 through 8-2-30 to require the use of ultra low-flow plumbing fixtures in all new construction, or when replacing plumbing fixtures during renovation or remodeling of existing buildings for the purpose of conserving water, to maintain the integrity of drinking water supplies and to reduce waste water flows.
(Ord. No. 91-014, 6-17-91, § 1, as amended by Ord. No. 92-018, 8-31-92, § 1)
Sec. 8-2-24. - Definitions.
For the purpose of sections 8-2-23 through 8-2-30, the following terms shall have the meanings respectively ascribed to them:
(1)
Commercial building means any type of building other than residential.
(2)
Construction means the erection of a new building or the alteration of an existing building in connection with its repair or renovation or in connection with making an addition to an existing building and shall include the replacement of a malfunctioning, unserviceable, or obsolete faucet, shower head, toilet, or urinal in an existing building.
(3)
Plumbing fixture means any toilet, urinal, shower head, bathroom, lavatory or kitchen faucet, and replacement aerators.
(4)
Residential building means any building or unit of a building intended for occupancy as a dwelling unit, but does not include a hotel or motel.
(5)
Toilet means any fixture consisting of a water-flushed bowl with a seat, used for the disposal of human waste.
(6)
Urinal means any fixture consisting of a water-flushed bowl but not provided with a seat, used for the disposal of human waste.
(Ord. No. 91-014, 6-17-91, § 1, as amended by Ord. No. 92-018, 8-31-92, § 1)
Sec. 8-2-25. - Standards for plumbing fixtures.
(a)
No plumbing fixture shall be installed within the city which:
(1)
Employs a gravity tank type, flush-a-meter valve, or flush-a-meter tank toilet that uses more than an average of 1.6 gallons of water per flush; provided, however, this paragraph shall not be applicable to one-piece toilets until July 1, 1992;
(2)
Employs a shower head that allows a flow more than 2.5 gallons of water per minute at sixty (60) pounds per square inch of pressure;
(3)
Employs a urinal that uses more than an average of one (1) gallon of water per flush;
(4)
Employs a lavatory faucet or lavatory replacement aerator that allows a flow of more than two (2) gallons of water per minute; or
(5)
Employs a kitchen faucet or kitchen replacement aerator that allows a flow of more than 2.5 gallons of water per minute.
(b)
The flow restriction device in a shower head must be a permanent and integral part of the shower head and must not be removable to allow flow rates in excess of that stated in subsection (a)(2) of this section.
(c)
Lavatory faucets located in restrooms intended for use by the general public shall be of the metering or self-closing type, in addition to the flow requirements listed in subsection (a) (4) of this section.
(Ord. No. 91-014, 6-17-91, § 1, as amended by Ord. No. 92-018, 8-31-92, § 1)
Sec. 8-2-26. - Applicability of restrictions.
(a)
The requirements of section 8-2-25 shall apply to any residential construction initiated after April 1, 1992, and to any commercial construction initiated after July 1, 1992, which involves the repair or renovation of or addition to any existing building when such repair or renovation of or addition to such existing building includes replacement toilets or showers or both.
(b)
Sections 8-2-23 through 8-2-30 shall not apply to any construction of a residential building, the contract for which was entered into prior to April 1, 1992, nor shall it apply to any construction of a commercial building, the contract for which was entered into prior to July 1, 1992.
(Ord. No. 91-014, 6-17-91, § 1, as amended by Ord. No. 92-018, 8-31-92, § 1)
Sec. 8-2-27. - Exemptions.
New construction and the repair or renovation of existing buildings shall be exempt from the requirements of section 8-2-25 when:
(1)
The repair or renovation of the existing building does not include the replacement of the plumbing or sewerage system servicing toilets, faucets or showerheads within such existing building;
(2)
When such plumbing or sewerage system within such existing building, because of its capacity, design or installation would not function properly if the toilets, faucets or showerheads required by sections 8-2-23 through 8-2-30 were installed. This exemption includes, but is not limited to, showers and faucets installed for safety purposes, such as an emergency eye-wash station;
(3)
Such system is a well or gravity flow system from a spring and is owned privately by an individual for use in such individual's personal residence;
(4)
Units to be installed are:
a.
Specifically designed for use by the handicapped;
b.
Specifically designed to withstand unusual abuse or installation in a penal institution, or
c.
Toilets for juveniles.
(Ord. No. 91-014, 6-17-91, § 1, as amended by Ord. No. 92-018, 8-31-92, § 1)
Sec. 8-2-28. - Procedure of obtaining exemption.
(a)
No owner shall be entitled to an exemption from the requirements of section 8-2-25 unless he shall first have obtained an exemption in writing from the office of the building inspector. Application for exemption shall be made on a form provided by the building inspector and shall include a listing of the specific exemption sought; the basis for such exemption; and, where such exemption is based on technological unfeasibility, shall provide the affidavit of a registered professional engineer or architect verifying the unfeasibility of the water conservation device for the particular existing structure.
(b)
A fee, as fixed from time to time by the mayor and council of the city, shall be charged for issuance of such an exemption.
(c)
The building inspector shall review the plans for the facility and may make inspections of existing premises where exemptions from the requirements of sections 8-2-23 through 8-2-30 are sought.
(d)
If, upon the basis of such investigation and any inspections, the building inspector determines that the requested exemption falls within those parameters provided by section 8-2-27, he shall issue such exemption in written form, which form shall bear his signature and date.
(Ord. No. 91-014, 6-17-91, § 1, as amended by Ord. No. 92-018, 8-31-92, § 1)
Sec. 8-2-29. - Enforcement responsibility.
(a)
Sections 8-2-23 through 8-2-30 shall be enforced by the building inspector who shall have full authority to issue citations for violations of such sections.
(b)
Failure to comply with any of the terms and conditions of Sections 8-2-23 through 8-2-30 shall be deemed an offense and shall be punished upon conviction as provided in section 1-1-8 of this Code. Each day's continuance of a violation shall constitute a separate offense.
(Ord. No. 91-014, § 1, 6-17-91; Ord. No. 92-018, § 1, 8-31-92; Ord. No. 96-005, 3-18-96)
Sec. 8-2-30. - Remedies for violations.
(a)
In addition to the penalties provided in section 8-2-29, any building or plumbing contractor, developer, owner or other person who violates the provisions of sections 8-2-23 through 8-2-30 shall be subject to suspension of his business license for a period of up to ninety (90) days.
(b)
In addition to the penalties provided in section 8-2-29, the city may take such other actions as authorized by law to compel compliance with the requirements of sections 8-2-23 through 8-2-30 and may specifically maintain an action or proceeding in any court of competent jurisdiction to comply with or restrain any violation of sections 8-2-23 through 8-2-30.
(Ord. No. 91-014, § 1, 6-17-91; Ord. No. 92-018, § 1, 8-31-92)
