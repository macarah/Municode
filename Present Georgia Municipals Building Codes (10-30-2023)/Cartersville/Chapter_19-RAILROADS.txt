Chapter 19 - RAILROADS[1]

State Law reference— Municipal home rule, Ga. Const. art. IX, § II, O.C.G.A. § 36-35-3; railroads generally, O.C.G.A. § 46-8-1 et seq.

Sec. 19-1. - Definition.
The term "company" or "railroad company," as used in this chapter shall be construed to include any owner or lessee of any railroad and any person operating any railroad through the city.
(Code 1976, § 7-5001)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 19-2. - Repair of crossings.
It shall be the duty of all railroad companies operating a line of railroads through the city, over or across any street or avenue in the city, now or hereafter established, to construct and maintain the track bed as nearly uniform with the level of the street surface on both sides as practicable, and to so construct and maintain the rails as to be as nearly level with the surface of the track bed as practicable, or to provide a system of false rails or steel bars parallel with the track, the width of the street, spaced so as to avoid unnecessary jolting of automobiles, trucks, wagons and other vehicles crossing the track.
(Code 1976, § 7-5002)
State Law reference— Maintenance of grade crossings by railroads, O.C.G.A. § 32-6-190.
Sec. 19-3. - Blocking crossings.
It shall be unlawful to obstruct any street crossing within the corporate limits with any freight cars or train for a longer time than ten (10) minutes with the train stopped, unless a longer time shall be necessary to enable it to perform its duties as a common carrier.
(Code 1976, § 7-5006; Ord. No. 51-95, 12-6-95)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, OCGA § 46-8-197.
Sec. 19-4. - Obstruction of street by automatic signals or crossbars.
It shall be unlawful to obstruct any street crossing within the city with automatic signals or crossbars when a moving train is not approaching.
(Code 1976, § 7-5003)
Sec. 19-5. - Speed limits.
It shall be unlawful for any engineer or other person in charge of a locomotive engine to run an engine over any grade crossing in the city at a speed greater than thirty-five (35) miles per hour. Engines may resume normal speed after crossing Carter Street going north or Cook Street going south.
(Code 1976, § 7-5004)
Sec. 19-6. - Watchman to warn public of switching or backing.
It shall be unlawful for any engineer or other person in charge of a locomotive engine attached to a car or train of cars and otherwise to make running switches, or to back an engine, car or train of cars on any railroad track in the city across any street unless such movement is preceded by a watchman, to give warning to the traveling public, except when such warning is given by properly operating automatic signals.
(Code 1976, § 7-5005)
