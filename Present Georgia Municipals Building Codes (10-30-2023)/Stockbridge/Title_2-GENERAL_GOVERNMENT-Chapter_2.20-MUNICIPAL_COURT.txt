Chapter 2.20 - MUNICIPAL COURT
Article I. - General Provisions

2.20.010 - Time for court sessions.
The municipal court shall convene at the discretion of the judge, depending on case load.
(Amended during 1991 codification: Mo. of 6/11/79)
Article II. - Bail, Appearance and Supersedeas Bonds and Recognizances

2.20.020 - Scope of Article II provisions.
Bail, appearance and supersedeas bonds and similar obligations, shall be forfeited in the municipal court in the manner provided in this article.
(Amended during 1991 codification; Code 1971, Sec. 13-40)
2.20.030 - Proceeding to forfeit upon nonappearance.
Upon the failure to appear of any principal in any bond or recognizance given by a person charged with or convicted of the violation of a municipal ordinance, or by a witness to appear and testify, the presiding officer of the municipal court shall proceed to forfeit such bond or recognizance in the manner practiced in the courts of this state.
(Amended during 1991 codification; Code 1971, Sec. 13-41)
2.20.040 - Scire facias to issueâ€”Appropriating cash obligations.
The court clerk shall issue or cause to be issued scire facias on all forfeited bonds, recognizances or other obligations, returnable to the next term of such court at the usual place of holding said court, against the principal and his sureties, which shall be served by the police at least five (5) days before the return thereof; or, if the party resides out of the state or county, scire facias may be served by publication, as in cases of scire facias to revive judgments, and if said party resides in said county, outside of the corporate limits of the city, the same shall be served by the sheriff or his deputy. If, at such return term, no sufficient cause is shown to the contrary, judgment shall be entered up against such principal and sureties or such of them as have been served. Provided however, if such bond or recognizance given is cash or its equivalent, upon failure of the defendant to appear at the court at the appointed time, judgment shall be entered up against such defendant, and the bond so given shall be forfeited.
(Amended during 1991 codification; Code 1971, Sec. 13-42)
2.20.050 - Execution of judgments.
Final judgments in all cases of forfeitures shall be enforced by execution issued or cause to be issued by the clerk, bearing teste in the name of the presiding officer of the court, and shall be in favor of the city, and against the principal and his sureties on the bond, which execution shall be turned over to the police for levy and sale.
(Amended during 1991 codification; Code 1971, Sec. 13-43)
2.20.060 - Rearresting defendant and carrying out judgment.
The principal or defendant then under conviction shall be rearrested by the police under proper order of the court, and the sentence or judgment of conviction carried out.
(Code 1971, Sec. 13-44)
2.20.070 - Defense of illegality.
Any defense to such execution shall be by affidavit of illegality, returnable to the next term of the superior court of this county, and the affidavit shall not be accepted by such officer unless the party offering the affidavit shall give bond with good security for the eventual condemnation money or make affidavit of his inability so to do.
(Code 1971, Sec. 13-45)
2.20.080 - Bond amounts for various offenses.
The schedule of cash bonds and property bonds for violations shall be as established from time to time by ordinance of the mayor and council. Any of the scheduled offenses may be added on to a DUI.
(Amended during 1991 codification: Code 1971, Sec. 13-46)
