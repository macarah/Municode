DIVISION 16. - RESIGNATION

Sec. 2-486. - Resignation defined.
Resignation is the separation of any employee from the county through the submittal of a notice by an employee that he wishes to resign.
(Mo. of 12-4-2007, § 18.01)
Sec. 2-487. - Resignation in good standing.
Employees are expected to provide a minimum of two weeks' notice in order to resign in good standing; provided however, department heads are expected to provide a minimum of four weeks notice in order to resign in good standing. This may be in written form or given verbally. Failure to provide such notice may adversely affect the employee's chances of re-employment with the county.
(Mo. of 12-4-2007, § 18.02)
Sec. 2-488. - Refusal to accept resignation.
Under certain circumstances, the appointing authority may choose not to accept an employee's resignation and may instead choose to dismiss the employee.
(Mo. of 12-4-2007, § 18.03)
