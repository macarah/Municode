ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 8-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning.
Abandonment means releasing or leaving an animal on any property, public or private, with the intention of deserting the animal.
Adequate food and water means food and water which is sufficient in amount and appropriate for the particular type of animal to prevent starvation, dehydration, or a significant risk to the animal's health from a lack of food or water.
(1)
Adequate food means palatable, noncontaminated and nutritionally adequate food that is appropriate for the species. Adequate food does not include garbage.
(2)
Adequate water means clean, fresh, potable water.
Adequate shelter means a structure having three sides, a top and bottom which is suitable for the animal's size to protect it from cold, wind and rain and other elements. During cold weather, sufficient bedding inside the shelter shall be present.
Animal means any living thing not a plant, other than man and excluding rodents, insects and fish.
Animal control officer means the person authorized by the city council to enforce the provisions of this chapter and may include any or all of the following: designated county personnel, city marshal, city code enforcement officer, and state, county and city law enforcement officers.
Cruel means causing unnecessary and excessive pain or suffering, or unjustifiable injury or death.
Dangerous animal means any wild mammal, reptile, or fowl which is not naturally tame or gentle but is of a wild nature or disposition and which, because of its size, vicious nature, or other characteristics, would constitute a danger to human life or property if not kept or maintained in a safe manner or in secure quarters; and any domestic mammal, reptile, or fowl which, because of its size, vicious propensity, or other characteristic, would constitute a danger to human life or property if not kept or maintained in a safe manner or in secure quarters.
Dog means any canine, regardless of age or sex.
Humane care of animals means, but is not limited to, the provision of adequate shelter and wholesome and adequate food and water consistent with the normal requirements and feeding habits of the breed.
Inhumane care means any act, omission or neglect which causes unjustifiable physical pain, suffering or death to any living animal.
Inoculation against rabies means the injection, subcutaneously or otherwise, of rabies vaccine approved by the state department of public health, and administered under the supervision of a licensed veterinarian and a certificate of vaccination or inoculation issued by the veterinarian.
Livestock means cattle, horses, donkeys, swine, sheep, goats, llamas, emus, ostriches, alpacas or farm raised deer and other such animals susceptible to use for commercial purposes, including domesticated fowl, such as chickens, turkeys, geese, ducks, guineas or other poultry.
Owner means any person, partnership or corporation owning, possessing, keeping or harboring one or more animals. An animal shall be deemed to be harbored if it is fed or sheltered for seven or more consecutive days.
Restraint means any animal that is on a leash or at heel or beside a competent person and obedient to that person's commands, or within a vehicle being driven or parked on the streets and roads of the city, or in a fenced area within the real property limits of the owner, or invisible fence systems maintained in good working order.
Running at large means any animal that is off the property of its owner and not under the control of a competent person.
State Law reference— Similar definitions, O.C.G.A. §§ 4-3-2, 4-8-21, 4-8-41, 4-11-1, 4-11-12.

ARTICLE II. - ANIMAL PROTECTION AND CONTROL[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Authority of municipalities to enact ordinances for the protection of animals, O.C.G.A. § 4-11-18.

Sec. 8-21. - Adequate food, water and medical care required.
It is unlawful for any person, whether owner, person in possession or any person in charge of any animal, to fail, refuse or neglect to provide such animal with the necessary food and water as well as necessary medical treatment for such animal.
Sec. 8-22. - Cruelty to animals prohibited.
(a)
It is unlawful for any person to overload, poison, cruelly treat, maim, tease, bruise, deprive of necessary sustenance or medical attention, ill use, deprive of shade and shelter or in any manner whatsoever, torture, kill or abuse any animal.
(b)
No person shall abandon any animal, on any property, public or private or keep an animal under unsanitary conditions.
State Law reference— Animal protection, O.C.G.A. § 4-11-1 et seq.; performing cruel acts on, harming, maiming or killing dogs, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-1.
Sec. 8-23. - Authority to remove abused or neglected animals.
Animal control officers shall be authorized to remove or cause to have removed to a safe location any animal that appears to be abused or suffering undue pain from abuse or neglect, as may be necessary to prevent further suffering. The owner or possessor of the animal may be charged according to O.C.G.A. § 16-12-4 concerning cruelty to animals.
Sec. 8-24. - Authority to remove animals from vehicles.
Animal control and law enforcement officers may use any force reasonably necessary to remove any animal locked in a closed vehicle if the owner cannot be immediately located. The operator of the vehicle may be charged with cruelty to animals as defined in this chapter. No injunction or claim for damages may be placed against the animal control department or its officers for this action.
Sec. 8-25. - Motorist hitting domestic animals to make report.
Any person who, while operating a motor vehicle, strikes or causes injury to any domestic animal shall notify the owner if known or ascertainable, as well as the police department, of the injury to and location of animal.
Sec. 8-26. - Registration of nondomestic exotic, venomous and wild animals; fee.
It is unlawful for anyone to own, harbor, or permit at large any nondomestic exotic, venomous or wild animal within the areas of the city without the written permission of the city marshal, who may impose upon the owner reasonable conditions of confinement to ensure the protection and safety of the general public. Written permission under this section shall be in the form of registration of the animal with both county animal control personnel and the city marshal. The fee for registration of such animals in the city shall be in the amount provided in the city fee schedule.
Sec. 8-27. - Owner responsible for damage to property.
It is unlawful for any person's animal to cause damage to another person's property. The owners of such animals shall continue to be civilly liable for any damage occasioned by the actions of their animals. Property shall include, but not be limited to, pets, livestock, vehicles, etc.
Sec. 8-28. - Bird sanctuary.
(a)
The entire area embraced within the corporate limits of the city is hereby designated as a bird sanctuary.
(b)
It is unlawful for any person to hunt, shoot or molest any bird or to destroy any bird's nest, with the exception and proviso that when birds congregate in such numbers that they become a menace to health or destroy property, then such birds shall not be protected by this section.
(Code 1999, § 14-2; Ord. of 3-12-1957)
Sec. 8-29. - Keeping livestock.
(a)
It is unlawful for any person to have or maintain within the city any stable in which are kept one or more horses, mules or cows unless the stable is not less than 150 feet from any dwelling, place of business or other house belonging to another than the owner of such stable.
(b)
When any stable for a horse, mule or cow is kept or maintained in any location within the city, the stable shall be kept in a clean and sanitary condition.
(Code 1999, § 14-3)
State Law reference— Livestock running at large, O.C.G.A. § 4-3-1 et seq.; disposal of impounded livestock, O.C.G.A. § 4-3-7.
Sec. 8-30. - Keeping swine.
(a)
It is unlawful for any person to have within the city any pen for the keeping of hogs in which hogs are kept unless and except the same shall be not less than 150 feet from any dwelling, place of business or other house belonging to another than the owner of such pen. Hogs shall not be kept in pens within the city from April 1 until and including September 30 of each and every year. Any pen used for pigs or hogs shall be kept clean and in a sanitary condition.
(b)
If any hog or pig shall be kept in a lot within the city, such lot shall be at least 50 square feet for each hog or pig.
(Code 1999, § 14-4)
State Law reference— Livestock running at large, O.C.G.A. § 4-3-1 et seq.; disposal of impounded livestock, O.C.G.A. § 4-3-7.
Sec. 8-31. - Keeping fowl; fowl running at large.
(a)
It is unlawful for any person to have or maintain within the city any pen or lot for the keeping of chickens and in which chickens are kept, except that the same shall be sufficient to allow not less than ten square feet per chicken.
(b)
Any housing facilities for chickens shall be not less than 150 feet from any dwelling, place of business or other house belonging to another than the owner of such housing for chickens.
(c)
Any housing facilities for chickens shall be kept and maintained in a clean and sanitary condition.
(d)
It is unlawful for the owner or custodian of any chickens or other domestic fowl within the city limits to allow or permit chickens or other domestic fowl to run at large off the premises of the owner or custodian.
(Code 1999, §§ 14-1, 14-5; Ord. of 4-11-1950; Res. of 7-24-1980)
State Law reference— Livestock running at large, O.C.G.A. § 4-3-1 et seq.; disposal of impounded livestock, O.C.G.A. § 4-3-7.
Sec. 8-32. - Animals running at large.
It is unlawful for any person intentionally or otherwise to permit any livestock, fowl, or other animal to go at large.
State Law reference— Livestock running at large, O.C.G.A. § 4-3-1 et seq.; disposal of impounded livestock, O.C.G.A. § 4-3-7.

ARTICLE III. - DOGS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 8-53. - Confinement of female dogs in heat required.
It shall be the duty of any owner, custodian or harborer of any female dog in heat within the city, when she is left unattended, to securely confine such dog inside a building or other enclosure to prevent such dog from coming into contact with another dog except for planned breeding.
State Law reference— Permitting dogs in heat to run free prohibited, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-6.
Sec. 8-54. - Removal of dog feces from certain areas.
The owner, keeper, walker or person in charge of any dog shall not permit solid fecal matter of such animal to deposit on any street, alley, or other public or private property, unless such matter is immediately removed therefrom by said owner or person in charge. This section shall not apply to a person who is visually or physically handicapped.
Sec. 8-55. - Dog control ordinance—Statement of purpose.
Sections 8-55 through 8-58 shall be commonly referred to as the City of Clayton's Dog Control Ordinance. The intended purposes of these sections is to promote responsible ownership, management and control of dogs; and to help protect the health, safety and welfare of the public from and against damage and nuisance caused by uncontrolled dogs.
( Ord. of 3-15-2016(1) )
Sec. 8-56. - Same—Definitions.
The following terms, as used in these sections, shall have the definitions stated below:
Leash. A line, not exceeding six feet in length, constructed of material sufficient in strength and suitable to securely restrain and control a dog.
Owner. A person determined to be the lawful owner of a dog.
Owner's agent. A person authorized by an owner to have possession and control of a dog.
Private property. A parcel of land that is privately owned and not open and available for access by the general public.
Public property. All areas of land open and available for access by the general public, including but not limited to public streets, rights-of-way, sidewalks, parks and open green space.
( Ord. of 3-15-2016(1) )
Sec. 8-57. - Same—Prohibition.
No dog shall be loose and not physically restrained and controlled by the owner or owner's agent using a leash securely attached to the dog at any time while on public property or private property, except in the following specific situations:
(1)
The dog is upon and within the lawful boundaries of private property which is owned or lawfully possessed and controlled by the dog owner; or
(2)
The dog is upon and within the lawful boundaries of private property with the knowledge and consent of a person who either owns or holds lawful possession and control of that private property; or
(3)
The dog is within a fenced area designated by the city as a dog park while in the presence of the dog owner or owner's agent; or
(4)
The dog is securely contained within an enclosures; or
(5)
The dog is involved in a specific event which shall have been authorized and made to be subject to terms and provisions by resolution duly adopted by city council.
( Ord. of 3-15-2016(1) )
Sec. 8-58. - Same—Dog owner responsibility.
The dog owner shall be responsible at all times for management and control of the dog in accordance and compliance with the requirements of these sections. The city shall have authority to charge or impose upon and against the dog owner found to be in violation of section 8-57 of this article a warning for the first occurrence of violation, a monetary fine in the amount of $50.00 for the second, and a monetary fine in the amount of $100.00 for and each subsequent occurrence of violation. the provisions of this section 8-58 shall not be construed to limit the city's rights and remedies under any other provision of the ordinances of the city or laws of the State of Georgia pertaining to animal control.
( Ord. of 3-15-2016(1) )

DIVISION 2. - DANGEROUS AND VICIOUS DOGS[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— Dangerous Dog Control Law, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq.; vicious dog control (Mercedes' Law), O.C.G.A. § 4-8-40 et seq.; duties of cities with regard to dangerous dogs, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-22.

Sec. 8-75. - Investigation required.
Upon receiving a report of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog within the city, the animal control officer shall make such investigations and inquiries with regard to such report as may be necessary to carry out the provisions of this division.
Sec. 8-76. - Procedure when owner is not known.
(a)
Where the owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog is unknown, and cannot be determined after reasonable effort, the dog shall be impounded. Notice of the impoundment shall be given by publication in the official city newspaper once per week for two consecutive weeks. The notice shall contain a general description of the dog, with color and sex thereof, the general vicinity where the dog was found and a number to call to claim the dog.
(b)
Any impounded dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog may be disposed of on or after the 20th day after publication of the notice of impoundment in the newspaper. Disposal of animals shall be made in an expeditious and humane manner.
Sec. 8-77. - Investigation of reports; notice to owner.
(a)
Upon receiving a report of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog within the animal control officer's jurisdiction from a law enforcement agency, animal control agency, rabies control officer, or county board of health, the animal control officer shall make such investigations and inquiries with regard to such report as may be necessary to carry out the provisions of this article.
(b)
Whenever the animal control officer classifies a dog as a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog, or re-classifies a potentially dangerous dog as a dangerous dog, notice shall be given the dog's owner in writing by certified mail to the owner's last known address of such classification or re-classification. Such notice shall be deemed complete upon its mailing.
Sec. 8-78. - Contents of notice.
The notice to the owner shall meet the following requirements:
(1)
The notice shall be in writing and mailed by certified mail to the owner's last known address;
(2)
The notice shall include a summary of the animal control officer's finding that formed the basis for the dog's classification as a dangerous or potentially dangerous dog;
(3)
The notice shall be dated and shall state that the owner, within 15 days after the date shown on the notice, has a right to request a hearing on the animal control officer's determination that the dog is a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog before the city;
(4)
The notice shall state that if a hearing is not requested, the animal control officer's determination that the dog is a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog will become effective for all purposes under this section on the day after the last day on which the owner has a right to request a hearing; and
(5)
The notice shall include a form to request a hearing before the city and shall provide specific instructions on mailing or delivering said form to the city.
Sec. 8-79. - Scheduling and conduct of hearing; notice to owner of determination.
(a)
When the city receives a request for a hearing, it shall schedule such hearing within 30 days after receiving the request. The city shall notify the dog owner in writing by certified mail of the date, time and place of the hearing.
(b)
At the hearing, the owner of the dog shall be given the opportunity to testify and present evidence and in addition thereto the city shall receive such other evidence and hear such other testimony as the city may find reasonably necessary to make a determination to sustain, modify, or overrule the animal control officer's classification of the dog.
(c)
Within ten days after the date of the hearing, the city shall notify the dog owner in writing by certified mail, of its determination on the matter. If such determination is that the dog is a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog, the notice shall specify it is effective on the third day following the date of receipt by the owner.
Sec. 8-80. - Requirements for and issuance of certificates of registration.
Subject to the requirements of this section, the animal control officer shall issue a certificate of registration to the owner of a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog if the owner presents to the animal control officer or if the animal control officer finds sufficient evidence of:
(1)
A proper enclosure to confine the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog;
(2)
The posting of the premises where the dangerous dog or the potentially dangerous dog is located with a clearly visible sign warning that there is a dangerous dog on the premises. The sign required to be posted must substantially conform to the design provided by the department of natural resources;
(3)
Payment to the city of a registration fee in the amount established by the city council to defray the costs of administering and enforcing this section; and
(4)
Proof of a policy of insurance in the amount of at least $100,000.00 issued by an insurer authorized to transact business within the state insuring the owner of the dog against liability for any personal injuries inflicted by the dangerous dog or a surety bond in the amount of $100,000.00 or more, issued by a surety company authorized to transact business in this state, payable to any person or persons injured by the dangerous dog.
Sec. 8-81. - Term of certificate; renewal; investigation regarding continued compliance.
Certificates of registration shall be renewed on an annual basis. At the time of an annual renewal of a certificate of registration, an animal control officer shall require evidence from the owner or make such investigation as may be necessary to verify that the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog is continuing to be confined in a proper enclosure and that the owner is continuing to comply with other provisions of this article.
Sec. 8-82. - Certificate not construed as guarantee of compliance.
Issuance of a certificate of registration or the renewal of a certificate of registration by the city does not warrant or guarantee that the requirements specified in this division are maintained by the owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog on a continuous basis following the date of the issuance of the initial certificate of registration or following the date of any annual renewal of such certificate.
Sec. 8-83. - Owner's duty to notify city of change in status.
(a)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall notify the animal control officer within 24 hours if the dog is on the loose, is unconfined, has attacked a human, has died, or has been sold or donated. If the dog has been sold or donated, the owner shall also provide the name, address, and telephone number of the new owner of the dog.
(b)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall notify the animal control officer if the owner is moving from the animal control officer's jurisdiction.
Sec. 8-84. - Registration requirements for new residents.
The owner of a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog who is a new resident of the city shall register the dog in the manner provided in this article, within 30 days after becoming a resident.
State Law reference— Investigation of report of dangerous or potentially dangerous dog and notice to owner, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-23; requirements of notice and hearing, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-24.
Sec. 8-85. - Prohibited acts; violations.
(a)
It is unlawful for an owner to have or possess within the city a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog without a certificate of registration.
(b)
It is unlawful for an owner of a dangerous dog to permit the dog to be outside a proper enclosure unless the dog is muzzled and restrained by a substantial chain or leash and is under the physical restraint of a responsible person.
(c)
It is unlawful for an owner of a potentially dangerous dog to permit the dog to be outside a proper enclosure unless the dog is restrained by a substantial chain or leash and is under the restraint of a responsible person.
(d)
It is unlawful for the owner of a dangerous dog to fail to post a warning sign on the premises where a dangerous dog is located.
Sec. 8-86. - Impoundment for certain violations.
It shall be the duty of the animal control officer, and any person under his employ, to impound the dog if:
(1)
The owner does not secure a certificate of registration;
(2)
The owner does not secure liability insurance or meet the bond requirements of this article; or
(3)
The owner does not keep the dog within a proper enclosure or permits to dog to roam or run free.
Sec. 8-87. - Redemption of impounded dog.
(a)
Any dog impounded under the provisions of this section shall be returned to the owner upon the owner's compliance with the provisions of this article and upon payment of reasonable impoundment costs, as fixed by a schedule approved by the city, as from time to time amended.
(b)
Notice of impoundment under this section shall be given to the owner, in writing, by certified mail, at his last known address.
(c)
The notice shall state that if the owner has not complied with the provisions of this article within 20 days from receipt of such notice, the dog shall be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner; provided, however, that no dog shall be destroyed under this section unless it has been held a full 20 days, exclusive of the date of giving notice.
Sec. 8-88. - Liability of owner for damages, injuries and death.
(a)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall be solely liable for any injury to or death of a person caused by such dog.
(b)
Under no circumstances shall the city or any employee or official of the city which enforces or fails to enforce the provisions of this article be held liable for any damages to any person who suffers an injury inflicted by a dog that has been identified as being a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog or by a dog which the city has failed to identify as a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog or by a dog which has been identified as being a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog, but has not been kept or restrained in the manner described in this section or by a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog whose owner has not maintained insurance coverage or a surety bond as required by this section.
State Law reference— Registration of dangerous dogs, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-25; muzzling and restraint, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-26; confiscation and destruction, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-27.
Sec. 8-89. - Penalties.
Violations of this article shall be punished as provided in O.C.G.A. § 4-8-28.

DIVISION 3. - NUISANCES

Sec. 8-107. - Determination as public nuisance.
Maintaining a dangerous dog, a dog that has demonstrated vicious behavior, a dog that is not tagged, a dog that is running loose or a dog that is generally classified as a disturbance is declared to be a public nuisance.
(Code 1999, § 14-66; Ord. of 4-16-1986, § 10-2-4)
Sec. 8-108. - Complaint; hearing.
Any official or inhabitant of the city may direct a complaint of nuisance to the city police department, who shall investigate and may place the complaint before the municipal judge for a hearing upon the basis of the investigation. The municipal judge, after a ten-day notice to the party involved, shall hold a hearing thereon and upon finding that a nuisance does exist, shall issue an order to the owner, agent in control of, or tenant in possession, stating that a nuisance has been found to exist and that the nuisance must be abated within so many hours or days as the municipal judge shall deem reasonable, having consideration for the nature of the nuisance and its effect on the public.
(Code 1999, § 14-67; Ord. of 4-16-1986, § 10-2-2)
Sec. 8-109. - Failure of owner to abate; abatement by city.
(a)
In any case where the owner or tenant fails to abate the nuisance in the time specified, or where the owner, agent or tenant cannot be served with notice, or where the nature of the nuisance is such, in the opinion of the municipal judge, that it must be immediately abated, the municipal judge may issue an order to the city police department directing the nuisance to be abated. The city police department, in such case, shall keep record of the expenses and cost of abating such nuisance and the costs shall be billed against the owner, agent or tenant for collection as for city revenues.
(b)
Other city departments shall assist the code enforcement officers as is necessary in abating nuisances under this division.
(Code 1999, § 14-68; Ord. of 4-16-1986, § 10-2-3)

DIVISION 4. - RABIES CONTROL[4]

State Law reference— Responsibility for control of rabies, O.C.G.A. § 31-19-1.

Sec. 8-127. - Registration fee for at-large dogs required.
The owner of each dog in the city and the owner of any dog running loose within the city shall first, before allowing such dog to run loose within the city limits, register the dog at the city hall and pay a registration fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1999, § 14-91; Ord. of 3-11-1975, § 2)
Sec. 8-128. - Prerequisite to registration; exhibition of current rabies inoculation tag.
As a prerequisite to registering any dog, the owner shall exhibit to the city clerk a current (within the preceding 24 months) rabies inoculation tag or certificate.
(Code 1999, § 14-92; Ord. of 3-11-1975, § 3)
Sec. 8-129. - Collar or chain and evidence of compliance required.
(a)
The owner of each dog running loose within the city shall have a collar or chain with the owner's identification thereon and the receipt for registration and evidence of a current rabies inoculation (within the preceding 24 months) exhibited on the chain. Each of the provisions of this section shall be referred to as evidence of compliance.
(b)
Any dog within the city running loose and off of the property of the owner that does not have attached thereto the evidence of compliance as described in this section shall be deemed a stray dog, and the owner shall be in violation of this division.
(Code 1999, §§ 14-93, 14-94; Ord. of 3-11-1975, §§ 4, 5)
Sec. 8-130. - Impoundment of strays; posting of notice; disposal.
(a)
The city's humane patrol officer is hereby directed to pick up and impound any and all stray dogs as defined in section 8-129 within the city limits, and to keep such dogs impounded for seven days during which seven days' notice of impoundment and description of such dog shall be posted on the dog impoundment bulletin board plainly visible at all times to the public at the city hall. Reasonable effort shall be made to contact the owner of such stray dog upon any information received by the humane patrol officer.
(b)
After seven days, such stray dog may be disposed of first to any person who will comply with the requirements of registration, inoculation and identification of this division and promise to provide a good home for such dog. In addition thereto, such person shall pay the cost of pickup and housing and feeding such dog in an amount not to exceed an amount per day set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(c)
Upon failure to find a person to accept the responsibility of the stray dog the city humane patrol officer is hereby empowered and directed to dispose of the dog in a humane manner after the expiration of seven days from the date the stray dog was picked up or impounded.
(Code 1999, § 14-95; Ord. of 3-11-1975, § 6)
