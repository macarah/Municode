ARTICLE IV. - ANIMAL HUSBANDRY FACILITIES

Sec. 10-320. - Definition.
For the purposes of this article, the term "animal husbandry use" shall mean the commercial care, breeding, confinement (excluding grazing) of domestic animals such as dogs, cattle, sheep, goats, swine, reptile, fowl and/or the storage and composting of organic wastes. The term "animal husbandry use" includes wholesale and retail feed lots and animal waste lagoons.
(Code 1996, § 14-26; Res. of 8-6-1996, § 1)
Sec. 10-321. - Application required.
No person shall erect, construct or enlarge any agricultural structure for an animal husbandry use without first submitting an application to the county administrator or other designated official. The application shall include a general location map and a sketch of the proposed development and site plan. The site plan shall depict the location of the right-of-way of any adjacent roadways and the required setbacks at a scale of no less than one inch equals 200 feet. The relative location to any habitable residential or commercial structure, any church, cemetery, school or historic structure eligible for the National Register shall be shown on the site plan. The placement of exhaust fans shall be shown on the sketch. The distances between the agricultural structures and other structures or places listed above shall be clearly labeled.
(Code 1996, § 14-27; Res. of 8-6-1996, § 2)
Sec. 10-322. - Review procedure.
The application shall be reviewed by the county administrator and the county planning commission at its next regular meeting following submission of the application for the compliance to the standards of this article. The planning commission and the county administrator shall recommend approval or disapproval of the application to the county board of commissioners. The county board of commissioners shall consider the application at its next regularly scheduled meeting following the planning commission meeting. If the application is denied, the county board of commissioners shall state in writing for the applicant the reason for denial. If approved, the applicant shall be sent a letter confirming approval of his application and he may begin construction.
(Code 1996, § 14-28; Res. of 8-6-1996, § 3)
Sec. 10-323. - New construction development standards.
The construction of buildings or structures involving animal husbandry uses shall be located no closer than 150 feet from any adjacent property line. The building setback line shall also be 150 feet from the property line. No animal husbandry use shall be located within 1,500 feet from any habitable structure as listed in section 10-321, excluding any structure owned by the developer of the site. The exhaust fans associated with any animal husbandry operation shall be directed away from any existing structure or place listed in section 10-321. The tract on which poultry houses are developed shall not be less than five acres per house in size.
(Code 1996, § 14-29; Res. of 8-6-1996, § 4)
Sec. 10-324. - Expansions.
The expansion of any animal husbandry operation which was in place prior to the adoption of this article shall be allowed provided that the expansion or addition shall not encroach within 1,500 feet of an inhabited dwelling or place as outlined in section 10-321 except those already existing within that range. In addition, any expansion or addition shall not diminish the distance between any existing animal husbandry uses and any structures or places listed in section 10-321 already within the 1,500 feet range. In no case may an expansion or addition of such use infringe upon the setback required from an adjacent property line without submittal of an agricultural adjacency waiver form and application.
(Code 1996, § 14-30; Res. of 8-6-1996, § 5)
Sec. 10-325. - Agricultural adjacency waiver.
The county board of commissioners may waive the standard requiring the distance of 1,500 feet from any habitable residential dwelling, church, cemetery, commercial business, school or historic property as listed in section 10-321 and the requirement of 150 feet from the adjacent property line providing that the developer/property owner secure a notarized agricultural adjacency waiver form from the property owners of such nearby properties. The notarized agricultural adjacency form (as attached to this article) must be submitted at the time of application for every applicable property.
(Code 1996, § 14-31; Res. of 8-6-1996, § 6)
Sec. 10-326. - Variances from setback requirements.
Requests for variances from the setback requirements shall be considered and must be requested on the application. Variances shall only be granted due to unusual topographic conditions of the proposed site and consideration of the impact the request might have on safety and traffic management in the area. All variances shall be logged as to request and reason for approval or disapproval. The board of commissioners shall make the decision regarding variance requests after recommendations are submitted by the planning commission following the procedure outlined in section 10-322.
(Code 1996, § 14-32; Res. of 8-6-1996, § 7)
Sec. 10-327. - Violations.
Violations of this article shall be treated as a misdemeanor, punishable as provided in section 1-14.
(Code 1996, § 14-33; Res. of 8-6-1996, § 8)
Secs. 10-328—10-357. - Reserved.
