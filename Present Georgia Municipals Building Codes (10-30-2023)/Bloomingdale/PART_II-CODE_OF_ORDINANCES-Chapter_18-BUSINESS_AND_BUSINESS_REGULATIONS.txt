ARTICLE II. - GARAGE SALES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 18-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Garage sales means and includes all sales entitled "garage sales," "lawn sales," "attic sale," "rummage sale," "flea market sale," or any similar casual sale of tangible personal property which is advertised by any means whereby the public at large is or can be made aware of such sale.
Goods means and includes any goods, warehouse merchandise or other property capable of being the object of a sale regulated under this article.
(Ord. of 10-3-1996(1), § 5-100)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 18-32. - Persons and sale excepted.
The provisions of this article shall not apply to or affect the following persons or sales:
(1)
Persons selling goods pursuant to an order or process of a court of competent jurisdiction.
(2)
Persons acting in accordance with their powers and duties as public officials.
(3)
Any person selling or advertising for sale an item or items of personal property which are specifically named or described in the advertisement and which separate items do not exceed five in number.
(Ord. of 10-3-1996(1), § 5-104)
Secs. 18-33—18-50. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 18-51. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to conduct a garage sale in the city without first filing with the clerk the information specified in this division and obtaining from such clerk a license to do so, to be known as a "Garage Sale License."
(Ord. of 10-3-1996(1), § 5-101)
Sec. 18-52. - Application.
The application for a license required by this division shall be filed with the city clerk and contain the following information:
(1)
Name of person conducting such sale.
(2)
Name of owner of the property on which such sale is to be conducted, and consent of owner if applicant is other than the owner.
(3)
Location at which sale is to be conducted.
(4)
Number of days of sale.
(5)
Date, nature of any past sale.
(6)
Relationship or connection the applicant may have had with any other person conducting such sale and the date or dates of such sale.
(7)
Whether or not the applicant has been issued any other vendor's license by any local, state of federal agency.
(8)
Sworn statement or affirmation by the person signing that the information therein given is full and true and known to him to be so.
(Ord. of 10-3-1996(1), § 5-103)
Sec. 18-53. - Fee.
The fee for a license required by this division shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Ord. of 10-3-1996(1), § 5-101)
Sec. 18-54. - Frequency of issuance.
A license shall be issued under this division to any one person only six times within a 12-month period.
(Ord. of 10-3-1996(1), § 5-102)
Sec. 18-55. - Duration.
No license shall be issued under this division for more than four consecutive calendar days.
(Ord. of 10-3-1996(1), § 5-102)
Sec. 18-56. - Display.
Each license issued under this division must be prominently displayed on the premises upon which the garage sale is conducted throughout the entire period of the licensed sale.
(Ord. of 10-3-1996(1), § 5-102)
Secs. 18-57—18-80. - Reserved.

Sec. 18-81. - Definitions.

Insurer means a company which is authorized to transact business in the classes of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5.
(Ord. of 11-5-1992(2), § 1)

Sec. 18-82. - Fees imposed.
The license fees imposed by this article shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
Sec. 18-83. - Life insurers.
There is levied an annual license fee upon each insurer doing business within the city. For each separate business location in excess of one not covered by section 18-84, which is operating on behalf of such insurers within the city, there is levied a separate license fee.
(Ord. of 11-5-1992(2), § 1(5-30))
Sec. 18-84. - Insurers of certain risks at additional business locations.
For each separate business location, not otherwise subject to a license fee under this article, operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing and in connection with such loans or sales, offers, solicits or takes application for insurance through a licensed agent of an insurer for insurance such insurer shall pay an additional license fee.

Sec. 18-85. - Independent agencies, brokers, etc., not otherwise licensed.
There is levied an annual license fee upon independent agencies and brokers for each separate business location from which an insurance business is conducted and which is not subject to the company license fee imposed by section 18-83 for each such location within the city.
(Ord. of 11-5-1992(2), § 3(5-30))

(a)
There is levied an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer writing life, accident and sickness insurance within the city in an amount equal to one percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8-1.
(b)
The term "gross premiums tax" as used in this section means gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4.
(c)
The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 18-83
(Ord. of 11-5-1992(2), § 4(5-30))
Sec. 18-87. - All other insurers.
(a)
There is levied an annual tax upon each insurer, other than an insurer transacting business in the class of insurance designated in subsection 1 of O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5, doing business within the city in an amount equal to 2½ percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8-2.
(b)
The term "gross premiums tax" as used in this section shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8-2(a).
(Ord. of 11-5-1992(2), § 5(5-30))
Sec. 18-88. - Due date for fees.
License fees imposed in this article shall be due and payable on the first day of each year.
(Ord. of 11-5-1992(2), § 6(5-34))
Sec. 18-89. - Levy on business location.
Each insurance business conducted within the city limits which is not subject to the company license fees imposed in sections 18-82 through 18-87 shall be subject to an occupational tax pursuant to the terms of the city's occupation tax ordinance, as set forth in chapter 70, article II of this Code.
(Ord. of 4-6-1995, § 1(5-31))

ARTICLE IV. - JUNKYARDS AND MOTOR VEHICLE SALVAGE YARDS

FOOTNOTE(S):
--- (3) ---
Cross reference— Traffic, ch. 74.
State Law reference— Junk dealers, O.C.G.A. § 43-22-1 et seq.
Sec. 18-121. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Junk means and includes old rope, chains, iron, copper, brass, lead, parts of machinery, old car parts, old rubber, old plumbing fixtures, bagging, burlap, old pipe and all other articles and things ordinarily gathered and bought up by junk dealers.
Junk dealer means any person who buys or receives used property which is no longer usable for the purpose for which it was originally intended.
Junkyard means any business or place of business where secondhand materials are kept and stored not in a building, but in any open area within the city, for the purpose of resale, whether retail or wholesale, or whether in small quantities or in carload lots.
Motor vehicle salvage business means and includes any business of buying, gathering or storing of motor vehicles unfit to be reconditioned for use on the public highways and shall include the selling, delivering or storing of used parts of motor vehicles or old iron, metal, glass, paper, cordage or other waste or discarded secondhand material, which has been a part of or is intended to be a part of any motor vehicle.
Secondhand dealer means any person who buys or receives, for the purpose of resale, used property which is still capable of being used for the purpose for which it was originally intended.
Vehicle means any wagon, dray, automobile, truck, motor truck, motorcycle, pushcart and any other like conveyance.
(Code 1983, § 5-18)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 18-122. - Approval of location.
No junkyard or motor vehicle salvage business shall be located anywhere in the corporate or jurisdictional limits of the city, unless the operators thereof file with the city council a petition asking approval of the location thereof, and receive such approval.
(Code 1983, § 5-19)
Sec. 18-123. - Fencing.
Any junkyard or motor vehicle salvage business visible from a primary street or main thoroughfare must be fenced in the following manner: Such fence or enclosure shall be of either wood, metal or other suitable material which must be painted and through which there must be no visibility. The fence shall be in height sufficient that no junk contents therein are visible. The necessity and sufficiency of such fence shall be within the judgment of the city council. Highway 80 and Cherry Street Extension (Bloomingdale Road and/or Pine Barren Road), and such other streets as may be designated in the future, are designated as primary streets or main thoroughfares.
(Code 1983, § 5-20)
Sec. 18-124. - Retention of salvage records; submission of vehicle identification numbers.
The city adopts as law under this article O.C.G.A. §§ 40-4-5, 40-4-6, 40-4-40—40-4-43. In addition, the operator of any motor vehicle salvage business, or dealer in used motor vehicles, motor vehicle components, or motor vehicle parts, will submit to the police department, on a weekly basis, a complete listing of the vehicle identification numbers, and/or part identification numbers on all motor vehicles, motor vehicle components, and motor vehicle parts that are purchased, acquired, sold, or given away in the operation of that business. The list will differentiate as to the transaction involving that item (purchased, acquired, sold, or given away), the date of the transaction, and the parties involved in the transaction (including name, address, and telephone number). The format of the list will be submitted to the police department, and approved by the police department before issuance of the business license. Failure to comply with the requirements of this section will result in suspension and/or revocation of the business license, and/or criminal charges against the owner and/or operator of the business.
(Ord. of 12-20-1990, § 5-21)

ARTICLE V. - SOLICITORS, PEDDLERS, CANVASSERS
FOOTNOTE(S):
--- (4) ---
Cross reference— Streets and sidewalks, ch. 66.
State Law reference— Georgia Charitable Solicitations Act of 1988, O.C.G.A. § 43-17-1 et seq.; peddlers and itinerant traders, O.C.G.A. § 43-32-1 et seq.; exception from local peddler or itinerant vendor licensing requirements for persons selling agricultural products, O.C.G.A. § 43-32-6; Transient Merchant Act of Georgia, O.C.G.A. § 43-46-1 et seq.
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 18-161. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Charitable means and includes the words patriotic, philanthropic, social service, health, welfare, benevolent, educational, civic, cultural or fraternal, either actual or purported.
Contributions means and includes the words alms, money, subscription, property or any donations under the guise of a loan of money or property.
Department means the police department.
Peddler means any person who goes upon the premises of any private residence in the city, not having been invited by the occupant thereof, carrying or transporting goods, wares, merchandise or personal property of any nature and offering the same for sale. This definition also includes any person who solicits orders and as a separate transaction makes deliveries to purchasers as part of the scheme to evade the provisions of this article.
Peddling means and includes all activities ordinarily performed by a peddler as indicated under the definition of peddler.
Solicitation means and includes all activities ordinarily performed by a solicitor as indicated under the definition of solicitor.
Solicitor means any person who goes upon the premises of any private residence in the city, not having been invited by the occupant thereof, for the purpose of taking or attempting to take orders for the sale of goods, merchandise, wares, or other personal property of any nature for future delivery, or for services to be performed in the future. This definition also includes any person who, without invitation, goes upon private property, to request contribution of funds or anything of value, or sell goods or services for political, charitable, religious, or other noncommercial purposes.
(Ord. of 5-4-1995, § 5-50(1))
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 18-162. - Exemptions authorized.
The following are exempt from the provisions of this article:
(1)
Any solicitation made upon premises owned or occupied by an organization upon whose behalf the solicitation is made;
(2)
Any communication by an organization soliciting contributions solely from persons who are members of the organization at the time of such solicitation;
(3)
Any solicitation in the form of a collection at a regular meeting, assembly or service of a charitable person; or
(4)
Any solicitation for the relief of any individual specified by name at the time of the solicitation where the solicitor represents in each case that the entire amount collected shall be turned over to the named beneficiary.
(Ord. of 5-4-1995, § 5-50(2))
Sec. 18-163. - Claims of exemption.
Any person claiming to be legally exempt from the regulations set forth in this article, or from the payment of a permit fee, shall cite to the city clerk the statute or other legal authority under which exemption is claimed and shall present to the city clerk proof of qualification for such exemption.
(Ord. of 5-4-1995, § 5-50(16))
Sec. 18-164. - Violations and penalty.
(a)
Violations of any of the provisions of this article shall be unlawful, and shall, upon conviction, be punishable as set forth in section 1-11 of this Code.
(b)
In addition to any criminal enforcement, the city or any individual may pursue any available civil remedies deemed appropriate and necessary.
(Ord. of 5-4-1995, § 5-50(17))
Sec. 18-165. - Entry upon signed premises unlawful.
It shall be unlawful for any person, whether licensed or unlicensed, while conducting the business of a peddler or solicitor, to enter upon any residential premises in the city where the owner, occupant or person legally in charge of the premises has posted, at the entry to the premises, or at the entry to the principal building on the premises, a sign bearing the words "No Peddlers," "No Solicitors," or words of similar import.
(Ord. of 5-4-1995, § 5-50(12))
Sec. 18-166. - Hours of solicitation.
No person, while conducting the activities of a peddler or solicitor, whether licensed or unlicensed, shall enter upon any private property, knock on doors, or otherwise disturb persons in their residences, between sundown and 8:00 a.m.
(Ord. of 5-4-1995, § 5-50(13); Ord. of 3-6-1997)
Secs. 18-167—18-190. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PERMIT

Sec. 18-191. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person 18 years of age or older to engage in peddling or solicitation activities within the city without first obtaining a permit issued by the police department.
(Ord. of 5-4-1995, § 5-50(2))
Sec. 18-192. - Permit for sponsoring juvenile peddlers.
(a)
No person under the age of 18 shall be permitted to engage in peddling except as provided in this section and in accordance with state law.
(b)
A permit shall be obtained by a sponsoring person, company or organization for the conduct of any peddling or solicitation activities involving, in whole or in part, a sales force of one or more persons under 18 years of age.
(c)
The sponsor shall be responsible for supervising and controlling the conduct of all persons, including juveniles, peddling under the sponsor's permit.
(d)
The sponsor shall provide to each individual in its sales force a badge or other easy readable form of identification which identifies the name of the sponsor and the name of the individual. The sponsor shall require all individuals in its sales force to wear such identification so that it is clearly visible at all times when the individuals are peddling or soliciting.
(Ord. of 5-4-1995, § 5-50(3))
Sec. 18-193. - Application.
Every person subject to the provisions of this division shall file with the city clerk an application in writing on a form to be furnished by the city, which shall provide the following information:
(1)
Proof of age, address and identification of the applicant, to be provided through the applicant's driver's license, articles of incorporation (for sponsors), or other legally recognized form of identification;
(2)
A brief description of the business or activity to be conducted;
(3)
The hours and location for which the right to peddle or solicit is desired;
(4)
If employed, the name, address, and telephone number of the employer; or if acting as an agent, the name, address and telephone number of the principal who is being represented, with credentials in written form establishing the relationship and the authority of the employee or agent to act for the employer or principal, as the case may be;
(5)
A statement as to whether or not the applicant has been convicted of a felony, misdemeanor or ordinance violation (other than traffic violations), the nature of the offense or violation, the penalty or punishment imposed, the date when and place where such offense occurred, and other pertinent details thereof;
(6)
Proof of possession of any license or permit which, under federal, state or local laws or regulations, the applicant is required to have in order to conduct the proposed business, or which, under any such law or regulation, would exempt the applicant from the licensing requirements of this division; and
(7)
Two photographs of the applicant which shall have been taken within 60 days immediately prior to the date of filing of the application. The photographs shall measure four inches by four inches and show the head and shoulders of the applicant in a clear and distinguishing manner.
(Ord. of 5-4-1995, § 5-50(4))
Sec. 18-194. - Fees.
At the time an application for a permit under this division is filed with the city clerk, the applicant shall pay a fee, as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk, to cover the cost to the city of processing the application, investigating the facts stated therein, and regulating the applicant's solicitation. Those organizations and individuals able to prove a nonprofit status as recognized by revenue service agencies of the state and federal government are exempt from the fee payment as outlined in this section but must comply with all other provisions of this article as detailed. Any business regularly engaging in solicitation and peddling within the city throughout the year shall be required to pay at the beginning of the calendar year, a flat regulatory fee instead of a fee based on the number of solicitors acting on its behalf and the number of days it will be conducting such activities within the city. Such business will be required to submit, for approval by the chief of police, permit applications pursuant to section 18-193(4) on all persons who will be conducting business on its behalf within the city and will be required to notify the city clerk concerning any changes in the identity of such persons.
(Ord. of 5-4-1995, § 5-50(5); Ord. of 10-3-1996(2))
Sec. 18-195. - Application review.
Upon receipt of an application for a permit under this division, the chief of police, or his authorized representative, shall review the application as deemed necessary to ensure the protection of the public health, safety and general welfare.
(Ord. of 5-4-1995, § 5-50(6)(a))
Sec. 18-196. - Issuance.
(a)
If the chief of police finds an application for a permit under this division to be satisfactory, the chief of police shall endorse his approval on the application, whereupon the city clerk shall, upon payment of the prescribed fee, deliver the required permit to the applicant.
(b)
The permit shall show the name, address and photograph of the permittee, the class of permit issued, the kind of goods or services to be sold or delivered, the date of issuance, and the length of time that the permit shall be in effect. The permit shall also show the permit number and identifying description of any vehicle to be used in carrying on the business for which the permit is issued.
(c)
A record of all permits issued shall be maintained by the city clerk for a period of two years.
(Ord. of 5-4-1995, § 5-50(6)(b)—(d))
Sec. 18-197. - Denial.
(a)
Upon the chief of police's review of an application for a permit under this division, the chief may disapprove the issuance of a permit to the applicant under this division for any of the following reasons:
(1)
The location and time of solicitation or peddling would endanger the safety and welfare of the solicitors, peddlers or their customers;
(2)
An investigation reveals that the applicant falsified information on the application;
(3)
The applicant has been convicted of a felony, misdemeanor or ordinance violation involving a sex offense, trafficking in controlled substances, or any violent acts against persons or property, such conviction being entered within the five years preceding the date of application;
(4)
The applicant is a person against whom a judgment based upon, or conviction for, fraud, deceit or misrepresentation has been entered within the five years immediately preceding the date of application;
(5)
There is no proof as to the authority of the applicant to serve as an agent to the principal; or
(6)
The applicant has been denied a permit under this division within the immediate past year, unless the applicant can and does show to the satisfaction of the chief of police that the reasons for such earlier denial no longer exist.
(b)
The chief of police's disapproval and the reasons for disapproval shall be noted on the application, and the applicant shall be notified that his application is disapproved and that no permit will be issued. Notice shall be mailed to the applicant at the address shown on the application form, or at the applicant's last known address.
(Ord. of 5-4-1995, § 5-50(7))
Sec. 18-198. - Expiration.
All permits issued under the provisions of this division shall expire five days from the date of issuance, unless an earlier expiration date is noted on the permit.
(Ord. of 5-4-1995, § 5-50(8))
Sec. 18-199. - Identification badges.
At the same time a permit is issued under the provisions of this division, the city clerk shall issue to each permittee a badge, which shall be worn by the permittee in such a way as to be conspicuous at all times while the permittee is soliciting or peddling in the city.
(Ord. of 5-4-1995, § 5-50(9))
Sec. 18-200. - Exhibition.
Every person required to obtain a permit under the provisions of this division shall exhibit the permit when requested to do so by any prospective customer or police officer.
(Ord. of 5-4-1995, § 5-50(10))
Sec. 18-201. - Transfer prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person other than the permittee to use or wear any permit or badge issued under the provisions of this division.
(Ord. of 5-4-1995, § 5-50(11))
Sec. 18-202. - Revocation.
Any permit issued under this division may be revoked or suspended by the mayor and council, after notice and hearing, for any of the following reasons:
(1)
Fraud, misrepresentation or false statement contained in the application for a permit;
(2)
Fraud, misrepresentation or false statement made by the permittee in the course of conducting solicitation or peddling activities;
(3)
Conducting peddling or solicitation activities contrary to the provisions contained in the permit;
(4)
Conviction for any crime involving moral turpitude; or
(5)
Conducting peddling or solicitation activities in such a manner as to create a public nuisance, constitute a breach of the peace or endanger the health, safety or general welfare of the public.
(Ord. of 5-4-1995, § 5-50(14))
Sec. 18-203. - Notice and hearing.
Notice of a hearing for revocation of a permit issued under this division shall be provided in writing and shall set forth specifically the grounds for the proposed revocation and the time and place of the hearing. Notice shall be mailed, postage prepaid, to the permittee at the address shown on the permit application or at the last known address of the permittee.
(Ord. of 5-4-1995, § 5-50(15))
Secs. 18-204—18-220. - Reserved.
ARTICLE VI. - TOW TRUCKS

Sec. 18-221. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Tow truck means any wrecker, tow truck, rollback, or other motor vehicle equipped with an apparatus used for the purpose of moving or transporting other motor vehicles. This definition will include tractor-trailer combinations used to transport heavy equipment, or any other motor vehicle.
(Ord. of 12-20-1990, § 5-60)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 18-222. - Inspection.
On an annual basis, prior to the issuance of a business license to any business which utilizes tow trucks, the tow trucks must be submitted for inspection to the police department. The initial application for business license will be submitted to the police department. Within one week from the date of submission of this application, an officer from the police department will, at the applicant's business site, inspect every vehicle designated for the moving or transportation of other vehicles. This inspection will consist of, but not be limited to, the inspection:
(1)
Of all lights, tires, towing gear, and safety equipment to ensure that it is in proper working order.
(2)
And recording of all vehicle identification numbers on the body, frame, motor, and transmission.
(3)
And recording of the license, registration, and proper emergency light permits.
(4)
Any recording of the insurance information on each vehicle.
(Ord. of 12-20-1990, § 5-61)
Sec. 18-223. - Minimum equipment.
All tow trucks will be equipped with the proper lighting and safety equipment, as well as the proper state permits, before it will be approved for use in the city.
(Ord. of 12-20-1990, § 5-61)
Sec. 18-224. - Denial of use.
Should any tow truck not contain the equipment required by this article, or should that equipment not operate properly, that vehicle will be marked as restricted for use, until such time as it is brought into compliance. In addition, should the inspecting officer determine that a tow truck is deficient, he may deny approval of its use in the city. At any time that the police department determines that a tow truck is not granted approval for use, a report will be issued to the business operator and the city clerk, stating the reason for denial, and the measures which must be taken by the business operator to bring the tow truck into compliance.
(Ord. of 12-20-1990, § 5-61)
Sec. 18-225. - Approval.
After approval of the inspected tow truck is obtained, the application for its use will be forwarded from the police department to the office of the city clerk. Should this business acquire any uninspected tow trucks during the following year, the business operator will notify the police department prior to using the tow truck within the city, and request an inspection. After the tow truck has passed inspection and been granted approval, it may be utilized by the business. The police department will retain the authority to inspect any tow truck utilized by a business within the city at any time, and deny further use of that tow truck by the business if it is found to be outside of the boundaries of compliance.
(Ord. of 12-20-1990, § 5-61)
Sec. 18-226. - List of operators.
In addition to the provisions of this article, a list of all employees of the applying business that will be operating the tow trucks will be submitted to the police department. This list will contain the employees full name, address, date of birth, driver's license number, and social security number. A check of these employees will be conducted by the police department to ensure that they are properly licensed by the state department of public safety to operate a motor vehicle. If any new tow truck operators are utilized and/or hired by the business during the following year, the aforementioned information on the new operator will be submitted to the police department within five business days of their hiring.
(Ord. of 12-20-1990, § 5-61)
Sec. 18-227. - Effect of noncompliance.
Failure to meet any of the requirements of this article will result in the suspension and/or revocation of the business license.
(Ord. of 12-20-1990, § 5-61)
Secs. 18-228—18-260. - Reserved.
ARTICLE VII. - WRECKER SERVICES

Sec. 18-261. - Approval by committee.
In order for a wrecking service to be approved by the city, the police committee shall determine that such wrecking service has a secure, suitable place to store vehicles. Such wrecking service must satisfy the police committee that it is in a position to be responsible for the handling and storage of vehicles by suitable insurance or other suitable means.
(Code 1983, § 18-6)
Sec. 18-262. - Calling for service.
(a)
Should the police department find it necessary to move a vehicle, motor or otherwise, the police officer shall give the owner of such vehicle a choice as to what wrecking service such owner desires to move the vehicle, provided that such wrecking service is within a reasonable distance from the vehicle.
(b)
If the owner or driver of such vehicle has no choice of a wrecking service the police department may call any wrecking service that has been approved by the council upon the recommendation of the police committee.
(Code 1983, § 18-7)
