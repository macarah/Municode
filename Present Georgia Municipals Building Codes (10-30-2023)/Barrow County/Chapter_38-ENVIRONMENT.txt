ARTICLE II. - PROHIBITED DISPOSALS

Sec. 38-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section:
Dispose means to discharge, deposit, inject, dump, spill, leak, or place anything into or on any land or water such that any constituent thereof may enter the environment or be emitted into the air or discharged into any waters, including ground waters.
Coal combustion residuals (CCRs) means fly ash, bottom ash, boiler slag, and flue gas desulfurization materials generated from burning coal for the purpose of generating electricity by electric utilities and independent power producers.
Person means, but is not limited to, any natural person, corporation, partnership, association, firm, trust, estate, receiver, guardian, trustee, executor, administrator, fiduciary, representative, agent, landfill and/or solid waste handling facility.
(Ord. of 3-10-2020, § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 38-32. - Disposal of CCRs prohibited.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to dispose of any coal combustion residuals within Barrow County.
(b)
It shall be and hereby is prohibited for any landfill or solid waste handling facility located in Barrow County, to include any facility accepting construction and demolition waste, or any person or entity that owns or is in operational control of such landfill or solid waste handling facility to accept, dispose of, bury, deposit, dump, process, store, or manage coal combustion residuals.
(Ord. of 3-10-2020, § 1(Exh. A))
Sec. 38-33. - Violation; penalty.
Any violation of this article shall result in the issuance of a citation for prosecution in the Barrow County Magistrate Court, with the penalty being up to the maximum identified in O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60, as the same may be amended from time to time, to specifically include up to six months in jail. In addition to a citation, any violation of this article may result in the Board of Commissioners authorizing the County Attorney or his/her designee to initiate injunctive and other appropriate civil proceedings to abate a nuisance and otherwise compel compliance with this article. It is the declaration of Barrow County that the matters prohibited by this article, if occurring in violation of this article, shall constitute a per se nuisance.
(Ord. of 3-10-2020, § 1(Exh. A))
