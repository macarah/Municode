Sec. 50-1. - Health department fees.
Charges for health department services shall be in amounts as established and amended from time to time by the director of the health department, upon the written approval of the chairman of the board of commissioners and the chief of operations; provided, however, that the amount of such fees shall be presented within the annual budget approved by the board of commissioners. In the event that the position of director of the health department is vacant, the amount of the fees may be established and amended from time to time by the chief of operations, upon the written approval of the chairman of the board of commissioners; provided, however, that the amount of such fees shall be presented within the annual budget approved by the board of commissioners.
(Code 1993, § 11-1; Mo. of 6-23-1998; Mo. of 11-23-1999; Mo. of 6-26-2001; Mo. of 3-25-2003; Ord. of 1-25-2005; Ord. of 5-10-2005)
Sec. 50-2. - Emergency medical services fees.
Charges for emergency medical services shall be in amounts as established and amended from time to time by the director of the emergency medical services department, upon the written approval of the chairman of the board of commissioners and the chief of operations; provided, however, that the amount of such fees shall be presented within the annual budget approved by the board of commissioners. In the event that the position of director of the emergency medical services department is vacant, the amount of the fees may be established and amended from time to time by the chief of operations, upon the written approval of the chairman of the board of commissioners; provided, however, that the amount of such fees shall be presented within the annual budget approved by the board of commissioners.

Sec. 50-3. - Emergency Communications—Telephone charge.
(a)
The county has an enhanced emergency telephone number 911 (E-911) system countywide.
(b)
In accord with O.C.G.A. §§ 46-5-133(a) and 46-5-134(a)(1)(A) and (a)(2)(B), the necessity for the "9-1-1" and the wireless enhanced "9-1-1" charges is hereby affirmed, and the maximum allowable fee rates are set at a rate not to exceed $1.50 per month: 1) per telephone service subscribed to by telephone subscribers whose exchange access lines are in the areas served or which would be served by the county's emergency communications service; and 2) per each wireless telecommunications connection, other than a connection for prepaid wireless service, subscribed to by telephone subscribers whose place of primary use is within the geographic area that is served by the county or that would be served by the county for the purpose of the county's emergency communications system.
(c)
All acts and doings of the officers of the county which are in conformity with the purposes and intents of this section and in the furtherance of the implementation of the emergency communications system shall be, and the same hereby are, in all respects approved and confirmed.
(Code 1993, § 8-8; Res. of 5-25-1999; Res. of 2-24-2009; Ord. of 3-22-2011)

Sec. 50-4. - Same—Advisory board.
(a)
Creation. The government of Barrow County hereby creates the Barrow County 911 Advisory Board in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 45-5-136.
(b)
Membership. The membership shall consist of individuals knowledgeable of the 911 communications system, representatives from other public safety agencies which respond to emergency communications calls under the system, and other individuals knowledgeable of emergency communications systems and the emergency needs of the citizens of Barrow County, provided that the advisory board not exceed 13 members.
(c)
Composition. The following public safety/support agencies shall be represented on the advisory board:
(1)
Barrow County Board of Commissioners Chairperson/or appointee.
(2)
City of Auburn Mayor.
(3)
Town of Carl Mayor.
(4)
Town of Bethlehem Mayor.
(5)
Town of Braselton Mayor.
(6)
City of Stratham Mayor.
(7)
City of Winder Mayor.
(8)
Barrow County Sheriff.
(9)
Barrow County Chief of Emergency Services.
(10)
City of Statham Chief of Police.
(11)
City of Auburn Chief of Police.
(12)
City of Winder Chief of Police.
(13)
City of Winder Fire Chief.
(d)
Meetings. The 911 advisory board shall meet on a quarterly basis, or more frequently as needed. The chairperson of the board of commissioners or their appointee shall be chairperson of the emergency communications advisory board.
(e)
Duties. In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-236, the governing authority of Barrow County does hereby create by resolution the Barrow County 911 Advisory Board and authorizes this board to assist the local government in the following duties:
(1)
Review and analyze the progress of public safety agencies in developing their emergency communications system requirements as needed for an effective public safety communications system.
(2)
Recommend steps of action to effect the necessary coordination, regulation and development of an emergency communications system.
(3)
Identify mutual aid agreements and standard operating procedures necessary for an efficient communication system to serve the local government.
(4)
Assist in the promulgation of necessary rules, regulations, operating procedures, schedules, and other such policies and administrative devices as shall be deemed necessary and appropriate; and
(5)
Provide other services as may be deemed appropriate by the local government.
(f)
Compensation. The members of the advisory board shall not be compensated from moneys deposited into the "emergency telephone system fund."
(g)
Funding. Funding shall be appropriated from the general fund of Barrow County on an annual basis.
(h)
Amendments. The duties of the advisory board may be amended, as needed, by Barrow County authorities and subject to change by O.C.G.A. section as amended.
(Ord. of 5-24-2005; Ord. of 3-22-2011; Ord. of 4-12-2011)
Sec. 50-5. - Mutual aid resource pact.
(a)
Barrow County Department of Emergency Services may render aid and assistance during disastrous incidents and other immediate response emergencies outside its jurisdiction.
(b)
Department of emergency services may form a mutual aid resource pact, known as the Georgia Mutual Aid Group, with other jurisdictions, as defined on O.C.G.A. §§ 25-6-1 et seq., the Georgia Mutual Aid Group being a public corporation.
(c)
The Georgia Mutual Aid Group shall be governed by O.C.G.A. §§ 25-6-1 et seq.
(d)
The department of emergency services chief or his/her designee shall be authorized to act on behalf of the fire and emergency services all matters relating to the activities and functions of the mutual aid resource pact once it has been established.
(e)
Department of emergency services may withdraw from the Georgia Mutual Aid Group by resolution and vote of the Barrow County Board of Commissioners pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 25-6-1 et seq.
(f)
Department of emergency services liabilities, privileges, and immunities, including those of its firefighters and other persons acting on its behalf, shall be governed by O.C.G.A. §§ 25-6-1 et seq.
(Ord. of 12-13-2005; Ord. of 3-22-2011)

Sec. 50-7. - Safety coordinator.
The safety coordinator will be responsible for insuring compliance with certain safety rules and regulations that protect the well-being of county employees and the general public.
The safety coordinator will be responsible for:
(1)
Establishing and coordinating safety meetings and self inspections to identify safety related hazards;
(2)
Coordinating and implementing safety rules and training for supervisors and employees and/or making recommendations to management regarding such items;
(3)
Reviewing and/or investigating all employee accidents for causes and making recommendations for improvements and corrections;
(4)
Conducting periodic safety self inspections of all vehicles, buildings, grounds, equipment and machinery, and work practices and conditions to determine potential injury exposures;
(5)
Documenting and maintaining inspections records and implementing any follow-up actions.
The safety coordinator will ensure that qualifications have been met that will enable the county to receive substantial discounts on their insurance costs.
(Res. of 5-14-2013)
