ARTICLE XII. - BEER, WINE, AND DISTILLED SPIRIT AMENITY LICENSES

Sec. 4-525. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the following words shall have the following meanings:
Barbering shall have the same meaning as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 43-10-1(3).
Beauty shop, beauty salon, and barber shop mean any premises where one or more individuals engage in barbering or in the occupation of a cosmetologist.
Cosmetologist shall have the same meaning as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 43-10-1(7).
( Ord. of 11-05-2018(1) , § 2)
Sec. 4-526. - Beer, wine, and distilled spirit amenity license; businesses allowed.
(a)
A beauty shop, beauty salon, barber shop, or businesses located in and offering tours within the City of Porterdale may be issued a beer, wine and distilled spirits amenity license (hereinafter "amenity license") to serve beer, wine and/or distilled spirits as an act of hospitality and ancillary the primary business conducted. The service of an alcoholic beverage must clearly be a secondary function of the business, provided solely as an act of hospitality, and no purchase of any good or service shall be required to receive the provided beer, wine, or distilled spirits.
(b)
To be eligible to apply for an amenity license, the business may not be a brewpub, hotel, lounge, private club, restaurant, special event facility or wine café, as defined in section 4-1 of the Code, respectively.
( Ord. of 11-05-2018(1) , § 2; Ord. of 10-15-2020(1) , § 1)
Sec. 4-527. - License constitutes grant or privilege.
(a)
All licenses issued under this article shall constitute a mere grant or privilege to carry on or conduct the activities covered by such license and shall be subject to the terms and conditions imposed by the Charter of the City, these ordinances, the Constitution, laws and regulations of the State of Georgia, and the Constitution, laws and regulations of the United States as may be applicable thereto.
(b)
All licenses issued under this article shall have printed on the face the following:
"This License is a Mere Privilege and is Subject to Being Revoked and Annulled by the Council of the City of Porterdale and is Subject to Laws, Ordinances and Regulations Hereinafter Adopted."
( Ord. of 11-05-2018(1) , § 2)
Sec. 4-528. - Applications.
(a)
Application for license.
(1)
Applicants for an amenity license shall execute and complete an application in such form as may be prescribed by the city clerk/treasurer from time to time containing all necessary information and representations required by this article.
(2)
All applicants for licenses hereunder shall give notice of the making of an application by advertisement at least once a week for two consecutive weeks prior to the date of a public hearing on said application as provided herein in the Covington News, or other newspaper in which legal advertisements of the city are published, which notice shall contain a complete description of the location of the proposed business and shall give the name of the applicant, and, if a partnership, the names of the partners, whether limited or general, and, if a corporation, the names of the officers, and the date and time the council will hear such application. The advertisement referred to herein shall be of type not smaller than ten point capital and lower case and shall be at least a two inch by two column advertisement.
(3)
Upon the filing of the application with the city clerk/treasurer, the applicant shall deposit in cash or by certified check a process fee in the amount established by resolution, which fee shall not be refundable and shall not apply against the license fee granted hereunder. This processing fee shall be in addition to the deposit of the license fee more particularly delineated in section 4-531(a).
(4)
The applicant shall cause to be placed on the location of the proposed business for at least two weeks prior to the date set for a hearing on the application for a license hereunder one or more signs stating the following:
"Beer, Wine and/or Distilled Spirit Amenity License Applied For. Hearing Before the Council of the City of Porterdale, Georgia, on the ___ Day of ___, 20 ___ at ___ o'clock p.m."
The signs required by this subsection shall each not be less than 24 inches by 26 inches and shall face toward all public streets, alleys, sidewalks, or other public property adjoining the proposed location. Such signs shall be placed where they can easily be seen from all public properties adjoining the proposed location.
(5)
The council shall set a date for a hearing on said application within 45 days from the date of the filing of said application.
(b)
Separate application and separate license required for each location. A separate application must be made for a license and a separate license must be obtained for each location where beer, wine and/or distilled spirits may be offered as a hospitality.
( Ord. of 11-05-2018(1) , § 2)
Sec. 4-529. - Licensee qualifications.
(a)
Occupation tax. The applicant must be a business licensed by the city, and must have paid or, if a new business, register for and pay any applicable occupation tax required under the provisions of chapter 24, article III of this Code.
(b)
Age and residency requirements for licenses. No amenity license shall be issued to an applicant unless said applicant shall be:
(1)
A person at least 21 years of age, who is a citizen of the United States, or an alien lawfully admitted for permanent residence, for a period of at least one year immediately preceding the date said license is applied for, and a resident of Butts, Henry, Jasper, Morgan, Newton, Rockdale, or Walton County;
(2)
A partnership, all of whose partners are at least 21 years of age and residents of the state; or
(3)
A corporation incorporated under the laws of the state or authorized by the office of the Secretary of State for the state to do business in the state. In the event an application for license is applied for by a partnership or corporation, then said applicant must name a manager, as required by section 4-26 of this Code, who shall meet the age and residency requirements of subsection (b)(1) of this section, and the requirements of section 4-26.
(c)
Any licensee or person named as manager hereunder shall not have been convicted of a crime involving a violation of the ordinances of the city or any other city relating to the use, sale, taxability or possession of alcoholic beverages, or a violation of the laws of the state or of the United States pertaining to the manufacture, possession, transportation or sale of any alcoholic beverages, or taxability thereof.
(d)
The licensee shall be responsible for the management and operation of the business for which the license is granted.
(e)
The applicant shall make a sworn statement to such applicant's qualifications in accordance with the provisions of this Code and shall place the same on file with the city clerk/treasurer for any license issued.
(f)
No person shall be granted an amenity license unless it shall appear to the satisfaction of the council that such person is of good moral character. The term "good moral character" means that a person has not been convicted of a felony, or any crime not a felony if it involves moral turpitude, within the past ten years. The term "conviction," as used herein, includes pleas of nolo contendere or bond forfeiture when charged with such crime.
(g)
The city shall make such investigation of the qualifications of each applicant as it may deem necessary. Said investigation shall include a background check with the Georgia Crime Information Center, and submission of fingerprint identification.
( Ord. of 11-05-2018(1) , § 2)
Sec. 4-530. - Authorities to issue license; authority to revoke or suspend license.
All applications for licenses hereunder and consideration of the issuance of licenses shall be subject to the provisions of articles I and II of this chapter 4 of this Code, and shall be presented to the council for approval. If approved, the licenses shall be issued upon the payment of the required license fee outlined hereinafter. The failure of a licensee hereunder to comply with the laws of the State of Georgia and the rules and regulations promulgated from time to time regulating the service of alcoholic beverages on the premises by the Georgia Commissioner of Revenue, any violation of those regulations, and any violations of this Code shall subject the licensee to revocation or suspension of the license as provided for in section 4-30 of this Code.
( Ord. of 11-05-2018(1) , § 2)
Sec. 4-531. - Fee.
(a)
License fee.
(1)
Annual license fee.
a.
The annual fee for a beer, wine and/or distilled spirits amenity license shall be as established by resolution. The fee for such license shall be paid in one lump sum payment at the time of original or renewal application. In the case of a new license application, one-half of the license fee shall be paid at the time of application, and the other half shall be paid at the time of license issuance. In the case of a renewal license application, the full license fee shall be paid at the time of application.
b.
No license shall be issued for less than a calendar year.
(b)
Renewal application. On or before January 1 of each year, all holders of an amenity license shall make application to the council for renewal of the license, said application to be in such form and contain such information as shall from time to time be prescribed by the council and shall be accompanied by the application fee as provided herein.
(c)
Issuance. If such renewal application is approved by the council, the city clerk/treasurer shall forthwith issue a license to the applicant to engage in said business for the ensuing calendar year, subject to all terms and conditions of this chapter.
( Ord. of 11-05-2018(1) , § 2)
Sec. 4-532. - Revocation/suspension of license.
When such license is suspended or revoked, as provided in this article, the holder of same shall no longer be licensed or have the right to engage in the service of alcoholic beverages until such license shall be reinstated or until such suspension expires.
( Ord. of 11-05-2018(1) , § 2)
Sec. 4-533. - Regulations on hospitality service.
(a)
Serving limitations. The licensee shall permit the service of no more than one six-ounce serving of wine, or no more than one 12-ounce serving of beer, or no more than one two-ounce serving of distilled spirits to a person within a single business day, and an individual shall not be served more than one type of alcoholic beverage.
(b)
Limitation on who may serve. Service and handling of alcoholic beverages shall be made solely by the owner of the business or the manager, if any, named in the application for an amenity license. In the event a partnership or corporation holding an amenity license should change managers, said licensee immediately shall notify the city through the city clerk/treasurer or police chief of such change, and said new manager must meet all qualifications and requirements of this chapter for an individual holding an alcoholic beverage license.
(c)
Availability of non-alcoholic beverages. As part of the hospitality service permitted to a licensee, the licensee shall also have available for serving and offer to serve non-alcoholic beverages.
(d)
Limitation on alcoholic beverages stored on premises. A licensee shall have and store on the premises no more than three bottles of wine, one case of beer or its equivalent (i.e., 24 separate containers), or two bottles of distilled spirits at any one time.
(e)
The provision of beer, wine, or distilled spirits shall be subject to the provisions of this Code and to all applicable state laws which address the use, handling and serving of alcoholic beverages, including but not limited to the limitations on the handling and serving (but not sale) of alcoholic beverages set forth in articles I and II of this chapter 4 of this Code. No one who is under 21 years of age or intoxicated may be served or consume alcoholic beverages on the premises of the licensee. It shall be the duty of the license holder or manager to enforce the limitations of this subsection (e).
(f)
The licensee shall not permit the removal from the premises by a customer or guest of any opened or unopened container of alcoholic beverage.
(g)
The licensee may provide the hospitality service to customers during normal business hours, and have an established closing time of no later than 10:00 p.m. The licensee shall not serve alcohol on Sundays.
(h)
The licensee shall not allow any activities that would cause the business to be deemed a sexually oriented business under the provisions of article XVI of chapter 10 of this Code.
(i)
Record keeping. A licensee shall maintain a record noting the date and time when any alcoholic beverage is served and type of alcoholic beverage served. The record shall be made available to the city for inspection upon request.
( Ord. of 11-05-2018(1) , § 2)
