DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-388. - Consumption sales only.
Persons holding a license to sell distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises where sold shall not be permitted to sell distilled spirits by the package or bottle.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(F)(9); Ord. of 5-5-2008; Ord. of 8-3-2009; Ord. of 10-8-2013)
Sec. 4-389. - Locations where permitted.
(a)
In addition to any other applicable limitations contained in this Code of Ordinances, distilled spirits may be sold by the drink for consumption on the premises where sold in brewpubs, as defined in section 4-1, in zoning districts permitting brewpub uses, in private clubs, as defined in section 4-1, in restaurants that regularly serve food prepared on the premises, with a full service kitchen in operation consistently, in zoning districts permitting restaurant uses, as defined in section 4-1, in hotels, as defined in section 4-1, in zoning districts permitting hotel uses, in lounges, as defined in section 4-1, and in special events facilities, as defined in 4-1.
(b)
A licensee for the sale of distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises where sold shall have the right to allow the consumption of distilled spirits by the drink by patrons seated in an outdoor dining area as part of the operation of a sidewalk cafe. Drinks shall only be transported into outdoor dining areas by a licensee's working employees as part of their work duties. A licensee operating a sidewalk cafe shall not have the right to allow the consumption of alcoholic beverages by any person who is not seated, at a table, in a chair, provided by the licensee.
(c)
For the purpose of ensuring compliance with this article, and in addition to all other applicable provisions of this Code of Ordinances relating to production of records and accounting for compliance with this Code of Ordinances, the city manager may require that a licensee furnish a statement from a certified public accountant that reports the percentage of the licensee's total annual gross sales derived from the sale of groceries, food products, or prepared meals in the licensee's most recently ended fiscal year.
(d)
The licensee of each such establishment shall furnish at time of any renewal application an affidavit that reports the percentage of the licensee's total annual gross sales derived from the sale of food products or prepared meals in the licensee's most recently ended fiscal year.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(F)(7); Ord. of 5-5-2008; Ord. of 8-3-2009; Ord. of 10-8-2013)
Sec. 4-390. - Entertainment.
(a)
Bands or orchestras and patron dancing shall be permitted at pouring outlets:
(1)
Where adequate space exists;
(2)
Where all fire and safety regulations are met; and
(3)
Where the appropriate pouring license fee has been fully paid.
(b)
The licensee may provide electronically amplified music or live entertainment on any patio, deck, or in any other outdoor or open air dining area immediately adjacent to the licensed premises; provided, however:
(1)
Application for issuance of a temporary entertainment permit. An application for a temporary entertainment permit shall be submitted to the city clerk/treasurer with a nonrefundable payment of an administrative fee in the amount established by resolution no later than 15 days prior to the date of the event. No temporary entertainment permit shall conflict with any city sponsored special event, but may be approved upon coordination and consideration of dates, times, and schedules of activities. The following information shall be provided in the application:
a.
The name, address, and telephone number of the applicant.
b.
The applicant shall attach a map no smaller than 8.5 inches by 11 inches in size, clearly identifying the secured area proposed to be the location of the activity under the temporary entertainment permit. The proposed secured area shall take into account and provide for areas for public circulation around the secured area. The applicant shall describe in detail the size of the secured area proposed, and the manner and materials by which the applicant proposes to secure the area within which the activity under the temporary entertainment permit will be conducted. This description shall include the type and number of tables and seating arrangements to be located within the secured area, and describe the service area from which the provision of food and beverage services will be made.
c.
The applicant shall provide such additional information as the city manager may require from time to time on application forms to be provided by the city.
(2)
Administrative review and approval of a temporary entertainment permit.
a.
Upon receipt of an application hereunder, the city clerk/treasurer or his designee shall note on the application the day and time of receipt.
b.
The application shall be delivered to the city manager for review. In the event the city manager determines that the application is incomplete, the city manager shall return the application to the applicant, noting the nature of the deficiency. The city manager shall determine the completeness of the application within three business days of the date entered on the application by the city clerk/treasurer. In the event the applicant resubmits the application, the city clerk/treasurer or his designee shall note on the resubmitted application the day and time of receipt.
c.
The city manager is authorized to approve and issue a temporary entertainment permit.
(3)
Appeal of denial. Any applicant whose permit application has been denied may request a review by the city council by filing a written notice of such appeal within three business days of the notice of denial of the applicant's permit. The mayor and council shall set a hearing date within five business days of receiving such appeal request. At such a hearing, the applicant is entitled to be heard and present evidence in his behalf. The mayor and city council shall determine at the hearing whether the denial of the permit is justified. The determination by the council shall be binding upon the applicant.
(4)
Time and noise limits. No licensee shall provide any electronically amplified music or live entertainment on any patio, deck, or in any other outdoor or open air dining area after 11:00 p.m., nor exceeding a volume that would be disruptive or disturbing to any neighboring residences; provided, however, that the city manager may declare that the time provided in this subsection may be extended to 1:00 a.m., of the following day on certain days, such as New Year's Eve.
(5)
Compliance. Section 16-94 is complied with.
(6)
Removal of tents, stages, fencing, seating, tables, and other items from secured area. The applicant shall be required to remove all tents, stages, fencing, seating, tables, and all other items provided by or belonging to the applicant from the secured area within 24 hours of the end of the event for which the permit is issued.
(c)
Failure to obtain a temporary entertainment permit or abide by the regulations for the temporary entertainment permit may result in a fine of not less than $1,000.00 per offense and possible jail time of six months. In addition, the chief of police shall be entitled to demand that a violation cease and desist, and shall be authorized to take such action as may be necessary to obtain compliance with a cease and desist order.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(F)(10); Ord. of 5-5-2008; Ord. of 8-3-2009; Ord. of 10-8-2013)
State Law reference— Limitation on penalties, O.C.G.A. § 36-35-6(a)(2).
Secs. 4-391—4-408. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - LICENSE[9]

Footnotes:
--- (9) ---
State Law reference— Local license to sell distilled spirits by the drink, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-110.

Sec. 4-409. - Authority to sell; license required.
Distilled spirits may be sold by the drink for consumption on the premises where sold within the city only under a license granted by the city council upon terms and conditions provided in this Code of Ordinances.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(F)(1); Ord. of 5-5-2008; Ord. of 8-3-2009; Ord. of 10-8-2013)
Sec. 4-410. - License constitutes grant or privilege.
(a)
All licenses issued under this division shall constitute a mere grant or privilege to carry on or conduct the business covered by such license and shall be subject to the terms and conditions imposed by the Charter of the city, these ordinances, and any other applicable ordinances of the city, the Constitution, laws and regulations of the State of Georgia, and the Constitution, laws and regulations of the United States as may applicable thereto.
(b)
All licenses issued under this division shall have printed on the face the following:
"This License is a Mere Privilege and is Subject to Being Revoked and Annulled by the Council of the City of Porterdale and is Subject to Laws, Ordinances and Regulations Hereafter Adopted."
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(F)(6); Ord. of 5-5-2008; Ord. of 8-3-2009; Ord. of 1-25-2011(2); Ord. of 10-8-2013)
Sec. 4-411. - Applications.
(a)
Application for license.
(1)
Applicants for a license to sale distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises where sold shall execute and complete an application in such form as may be prescribed by the city clerk/treasurer from time to time containing all necessary information and representations required by this division. There shall be attached to and made a part of the application a financial statement detailing the amount of investment by the applicant, the amount and source of loans, if any, and collateral used to secure said loans, if any. This statement and the information therein shall be treated confidentially and shall be examined by the mayor, members of the city council, city clerk/treasurer, city manager, and city attorney only.
(2)
All applicants for licenses hereunder shall give notice of the making of an application by advertisement at least once a week for two consecutive weeks prior to the date of a public hearing on said application as provided herein in the Covington News, or other newspaper in which legal advertisements of the city are published, which notice shall contain a complete description of the location of the proposed business and shall give the name of the applicant, and, if a partnership, the names of the partners, whether limited or general, and, if a corporation, the names of the officers, and the date and time the council will hear such application. The advertisement referred to herein shall be of type not smaller than ten point capital and lower case and shall be at least a two inch by two column advertisement.
(3)
Upon the filing of the application with the city clerk/treasurer, the applicant shall deposit in cash or by certified check a process fee in the amount established by resolution, which fee shall not be refundable and shall not apply against the license fee granted hereunder. This processing fee shall be in addition to the deposit of the license fee more particularly delineated in section 4-414(a).
(4)
The applicant shall cause to be placed on the location of the proposed business for at least two weeks prior to the date set for a hearing on the application for a license hereunder one or more signs stating the following:
"License for the Sale of Distilled Spirits by the Drink for Consumption on the Premises Where Sold Applied For. Hearing Before the Council of the City of Porterdale, Georgia, on the _____ Day of _____, 20___ at ___ o'clock p.m."
The signs required by this subsection shall each not be less than 24 inches by 26 inches and shall face toward all public streets, alleys, sidewalks, or other public property adjoining the proposed location. Such signs shall be placed where they can easily be seen from all public properties adjoining the proposed location.
(5)
The council shall set a date for a hearing on said application within 45 days from the date of the filing of said petition.
(b)
Separate application and separate license required for each location.
(1)
A separate application must be made for a license and a separate license to sell distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises where sold must be obtained for each location where the same is sold.
(2)
Additional licenses shall be required for all additional facilities located within the same building; provided, however, that all brewpub, special event facility, or restaurant patios or open air eating areas, which are immediately adjacent to a brewpub, special event facility, restaurant, or wine cafe, shall be exempt from the additional facility license requirement.
(3)
A lounge in a hotel shall be required to obtain a license separate from any license obtained to permit the sale of distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises of a restaurant operated by the hotel.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(F)(4), (8); Ord. of 5-5-2008; Ord. of 8-3-2009; Ord. of 10-8-2013)
Sec. 4-412. - Licensee qualifications.
(a)
Age and residency requirements for licenses. No license for the sale of distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises where sold shall be issued to an applicant unless said applicant shall be:
(1)
A person at least 21 years of age, who is a citizen of the United States, or an alien lawfully admitted for permanent residence, for a period of at least one year immediately preceding the date said license is applied for, and a resident of Butts, Henry, Jasper, Morgan, Newton, Rockdale, or Walton County;
(2)
A partnership, all of whose partners are at least 21 years of age and residents of the state; or
(3)
A corporation incorporated under the laws of the state or authorized by the office of the Secretary of State for the state to do business in the state.
In the event an application for license is applied for by a partnership or corporation, then said applicant must name a manager, as required by section 4-26, who shall meet the age and residency requirements of subsection (a)(1) of this section, and the requirements of section 4-26.
(b)
Any licensee or person named as manager hereunder shall not have been convicted of a crime involving a violation of the ordinances of the city or any other city relating to the use, sale, taxability or possession of alcoholic beverages, or a violation of the laws of the state or of the United States pertaining to the manufacture, possession, transportation or sale of any alcoholic beverages, or taxability thereof.
(c)
The licensee shall be responsible for the management and operation of the business for which the license is granted.
(d)
The applicant shall make a sworn statement to such applicant's qualifications in accordance with the provisions of this Code of Ordinances and shall place the same on file with the city clerk/treasurer for any license issued.
(e)
No person shall be granted a license to engage in the sale distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises where sold unless it shall appear to the satisfaction of the council that such person is of good moral character. The term "good moral character" means that a person has not been convicted of a felony, or any crime not a felony if it involves moral turpitude, within the past ten years. The term "conviction," as used herein, includes pleas of nolo contendere or bond forfeiture when charged with such crime.
(f)
The city shall make such investigation of the qualifications of each applicant as it may deem necessary. Said investigation shall include a background check with the Georgia Crime Information Center, and submission of fingerprint identification.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(F)(2); Ord. of 5-5-2008; Ord. of 8-3-2009; Ord. of 10-8-2013)
Sec. 4-413. - Authorities to issue license.
All applications for licenses hereunder shall be presented to the council for approval and, if approved, the licenses shall be issued upon the payment of the required license fee and bond outlined hereinafter. The failure of a licensee hereunder to comply with the laws of the State of Georgia and the rules and regulations promulgated from time to time regulating the sale of distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises where sold by the Georgia Commissioner of Revenue and any violation of those regulations shall subject the licensee to revocation of the license.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(F)(3); Ord. of 5-5-2008; Ord. of 8-3-2009; Ord. of 10-8-2013)
Sec. 4-414. - Fee.
(a)
License fee.
(1)
Annual license fee.
a.
The annual fee for a license to sell distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises where sold only shall be as established by resolution. The fee for such license may be paid in two installments, rather than in one lump sum payment at the time of original or renewal application. In the case of a new license application, one-half of the license fee shall be paid at the time of application, and the other half shall be paid at the time of license issuance. In the case of a renewal license application, one-half of the license fee shall be paid at the time of application, and the other half shall be paid no later than March 1 immediately following the date of application.
b.
If a licensee or applicant is denied a license by the state, upon the proof of that refusal, the licensee or applicant shall be entitled to a refund of the license fee paid to the city, without interest, less a charge of $100.00 to cover clerical cost of granting the license. The refund may be made by the city clerk/treasurer without the necessity of any action by the council.
c.
No license shall be issued for less than a calendar year, or the remainder thereof; provided, however, that if an application is made for a license after June 30 in any calendar year, the license fee shall be 50 percent of the annual license fee for the remainder of the calendar year. If a license is revoked or surrendered before the expiration of a calendar year, the holder thereof shall not be entitled to receive any refund of the license fee paid.
d.
A licensee or applicant may be entitled to a refund of the license fee paid to the city if the licensed business cannot continue to be operated due to:
1.
Destruction by fire or an act of God;
2.
Death of the licensee; or
3.
Any action taken by a governmental entity beyond the licensee's control, but unmixed with the fault or negligence of the licensee or applicant.
e.
A refund shall not be granted if the license is revoked or suspended. Any refund under this subsection must first be approved by the council. If a refund is approved by the council, the refund shall be calculated as follows:
1.
Three-fourths of the license fee if the licensee goes out of business (but only for those reasons delineated in subsection (a)(1)d of this section during the first quarter of the calendar year.
2.
One-half of the license fee if the licensee goes out of business (but only for those reasons delineated in subsection (a)(1)d of this section during the second quarter of the calendar year.
3.
One-fourth of the license fee if the licensee goes out of business (but only for those reasons delineated in subsection (a)(1)d of this section during the third quarter of the calendar year.
4.
No refund if the licensee goes out of business (but only for those reasons delineated in subsection (a)(1)d of this section during the fourth quarter of the calendar year.
(2)
If, for any reason, the issuance of a license is delayed so as to entitle the applicant to pay a prorated portion of the license fee for the year when the applicant has already prepaid with submission of the application the full year's license fee, the city shall refund any overpayment of license fees to the licensee within 30 days after the license is issued.
(3)
In addition to the license fee in subsection (a)(1) of this section, there is imposed an excise tax upon all distilled spirits sold by the drink for consumption on the premises as more particularly delineated in division 3 of this article.
(b)
Renewal application. On or before January 1 of each year, all holders of a license to sell distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises where sold shall make application to the council for renewal of the license to engage in their businesses in the city, said application to be in such form and contain such information as shall from time to time be prescribed by the council and shall be accompanied by the application fee as provided herein.
(c)
Issuance. If such renewal application is approved by the council, the city clerk/treasurer shall forthwith issue a license to the applicant to engage in said business for the ensuing calendar year, subject to all terms and conditions of this chapter.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(F)(11), (5)(a), (5)(b); Ord. of 5-5-2008; Ord. of 8-3-2009; Ord. of 10-8-2013)
Sec. 4-415. - Revocation/suspension of license and permit.
When such license is suspended or revoked, as provided in this division, the holder of same shall no longer be licensed or have the right to engage in the business of selling distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises where sold until such license shall be reinstated or until such suspension expires.
(Code 2003, § 32-201(1)(F)(5)(c); Ord. of 5-5-2008; Ord. of 8-3-2009; Ord. of 10-8-2013)
Secs. 4-416—4-443. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - EXCISE TAX[10]

Sec. 4-444. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Drink means any alcoholic beverage served for consumption on the premises which may or may not be diluted by any other liquid.
Due date means the tenth day after the close of the monthly period for which the tax is to be computed.
Licensee means any person who holds a license or permit from the City of Porterdale to dispense liquor by the drink for consumption on premises.
Liquor or distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume, including, but not limited to, all fortified wines.
Monthly period means the calendar months of the year.
Person means an individual, firm, partnership, joint adventure, social club, fraternal organization, joint stock company, corporation, cooperative, estate, trust, receiver, trustee, nonprofit corporation, syndicate, or any other group or combination acting as a unit, the plural as well as the singular number, excepting the United States of America, the State of Georgia, and any political subdivision of either thereof upon which the city is without power to impose the tax herein provided.
Purchase price means the consideration received for the sale of liquor or distilled spirits by the drink valued in money, whether received in cash or otherwise, including all receipts, cash, credits and property or services of any kind or nature and also the amount for which credit is allowed by the licensee to the purchaser, without any deduction therefrom whatsoever.
Purchaser means any person who orders and gives present or future consideration for any liquor or distilled spirits dispensed by the drink.
Return means any return filed or required to be filed by this division.
Tax means any tax imposed by this division.
(Code 2003, § 4-106(1); Ord. of 5-5-2008)
Sec. 4-445. - Imposition; rate of tax.
There is hereby imposed, and there shall be paid, a tax of three percent on the sale of liquor by the drink in the city.
(Code 2003, § 4-106(2); Ord. of 5-5-2008)
State Law reference— Tax authorized, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-131.
Sec. 4-446. - Collection of tax.
Every licensee holding a license to dispense liquor or distilled spirits for consumption on premises is authorized and required to collect the tax imposed under this article from the purchasers of liquor sold by the drink within his licensed premises.
(Code 2003, § 4-106(3); Ord. of 5-5-2008)
Sec. 4-447. - Determination generally; returns; payments.
(a)
Due date of taxes. All amounts of such taxes collected by the licensee shall be due and payable to the City of Porterdale on or before the tenth day of every month next succeeding each respective monthly period.
(b)
Return; persons required to file; content. The remittance of the taxes collected under this division shall be made and a return shall be filed by every licensee with the city clerk/treasurer or his designee showing the gross sales of liquor by the drink and the amount of tax collected or otherwise due for the period, along with such other information as may be required by the city clerk/treasurer.
(c)
Collection fee allowed licensees. Licensees collecting the tax shall be allowed a percentage of the tax due and accounted for and shall be reimbursed in the form of a deduction in submitting, reporting and paying the amount due, if the amount is not delinquent at the time of payment. The rate of the deduction shall be the same rate authorized for deductions from state sales and use tax as provided for in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-50.
(d)
Penalty and interest for failure to pay tax by due date. A penalty of 15 percent is imposed for failure to pay any amount of tax when due and payable to the city, with a minimum amount of penalty due of $25.00. Delinquent amounts shall bear interest at the rate of one percent per month, or fraction thereof, until paid.
(Code 2003, § 4-106(4); Ord. of 5-5-2008)
State Law reference— Dealer collection fee required, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-133.
Sec. 4-448. - Deficiency determinations.
(a)
Recomputation of tax; authority to make; basis of recomputation. If the city clerk/treasurer is not satisfied with the return of the tax or the amount of the tax required to be paid to the city by any person, he may compute and determine the amount required to be paid upon the basis of any information within his possession or that may come into his possession. One or more deficiency determinations may be made of the amount due for one or more monthly periods.
(b)
Penalty and interest for failure to pay tax. A specific penalty of 15 percent is imposed upon the amount of any determination. Additionally, the amount of any determination shall bear interest at the rate of one percent per month, or fraction thereof, from the due date of taxes until the date of payment.
(c)
Notice of determination; service of. The city clerk/treasurer shall give to the licensee written notice of his determination. The notice may be served personally or by mail; if by mail such service shall be addressed to the licensee at his business address as it appears in the records of the city. Service by mail is complete when it is delivered by certified mail with a receipt signed by addressee.
(d)
Time within which notice of deficiency determination to be mailed. Except in case of failure to make a return, every notice of a deficiency determination shall be mailed within three years after the 20th day of the calendar month following the monthly period for which the amount is proposed to be determined or within three years after the return is filed, whichever shall last expire.
(Code 2003, § 4-106(5); Ord. of 5-5-2008)
Sec. 4-449. - Determination if no return is made.
(a)
Estimate of gross receipts. If any person fails to make a return, the city clerk/treasurer shall make an estimate of the amount of the gross receipts of the licensee from the sale of liquor by the drink, or, as the case may be, of the amount of total such receipts in this city which are subject to the tax. The estimate shall be made for the period in respect to which the person failed to make the return and shall be based upon any information which is or may come into the possession of the city clerk/treasurer. Written notice shall be given in the manner prescribed in section 4-448(c).
(b)
Penalty and interest for failure to pay tax. Penalty and interest shall be applied to the estimated taxes due in the manner prescribed in section 4-448(c).
(Code 2003, § 4-106(6); Ord. of 5-5-2008)
Sec. 4-450. - Collection of unpaid tax.
(a)
Alcohol license to dispense by the drink conditioned upon payment and remittance of tax. As a condition for holding a license to dispense liquor by the drink, a licensee must comply with this division and collect and remit the tax imposed in the manner prescribed. Failure to comply will subject the license to suspension of alcohol dispensing privileges as provided in section 4-30.
(b)
Action for delinquent tax. At any time within three years after any tax or any amount of tax required to be collected becomes due and payable and at any time within three years after the delinquency of any tax or any amount of tax required to be collected, the city clerk/treasurer may bring an action in a court of competent jurisdiction in the name of the city to collect the amount delinquent, together with penalty, interest, court fees, filing fees, and other legal fees incident thereto.
(c)
Duty of successors or assignees of licensee to withhold tax from purchase price. If any licensee liable for any amount under this division sells out his business or quits the business, his successors or assigns shall withhold sufficiently from the purchase price to cover such amount until the former owner produces a receipt issued by the city clerk/treasurer reflecting payment in full of the amount due or a certificate stating that no amount is due.
(d)
Liability for failure to withhold. If the purchaser of a business fails to withhold purchase price as required, he shall be personally liable for the payment of the amount required to be withheld by him to the extent of the purchase price.
(Code 2003, § 4-106(7); Ord. of 5-5-2008)
Sec. 4-451. - Credits for overpayments.
If it is determined that a licensee has overpaid the tax imposed under this division, overpaid penalties or interest, or has paid the tax more than once, the city clerk/treasurer may, upon filing of a claim by the licensee and audit of the claim, authorize credit of the overpayment toward the current amount of tax due and payable from the licensee. All claims for overpayments must be made by the licensee, his administrators or executors, and must be made in writing within three years from the date of payment stating the specific grounds upon which the claim is founded.
(Code 2003, § 4-106(8); Ord. of 5-5-2008)
Sec. 4-452. - Administration generally; record keeping.
(a)
Authority of the city clerk/treasurer. The city clerk/treasurer or his authorized representative shall administer and enforce the provisions of this division for the levy and collection of the tax herein imposed. The city clerk/treasurer shall have the authority to make and publish reasonable rules and regulations not inconsistent with this division or other laws of the City of Porterdale and the State of Georgia, for the administration and enforcement of the provisions of this division and the collection of the taxes provided herein.
(b)
Records required. Every licensee for the sale of liquor by the drink in the City of Porterdale shall preserve, for a minimum of three years, all records, receipts, invoices and other pertinent papers as the city clerk/treasurer may require and in such form as the city clerk/treasurer may require.
(c)
Examination of records; audits. The city clerk/treasurer, or any person authorized in writing by him, may examine the books, papers, records, financial reports, inventory, equipment and other facilities of any licensee liable for the tax, in order to verify the accuracy of any return made, or, if no return is made, to ascertain and determine the amount required to be paid.
(d)
Authority to require reports; contents. In administering the provisions of this article, the city clerk/treasurer may require the filing of reports by any person or class of persons having in their possession or custody information relating to the sale of liquor by the drink. The reports shall be filed with the city clerk/treasurer when required and shall set forth the gross sales from the sale of liquor by the drink, the amount of tax collected thereon, or such other information as the city clerk/treasurer may require.
(Code 2003, § 4-106(9); Ord. of 5-5-2008)
Secs. 4-453—4-472. - Reserved.
