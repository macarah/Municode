ARTICLE II. - COLLECTION AND DISPOSAL
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 20-19. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Commercial wastes means all waste by-products of manufacturing or commercial establishments, cinders and ashes for commercial boilers, and cardboard and wooden boxes, crates and barrels.
Garbage means domestic waste composed of meat, vegetable and fruit scraps, cans, bottles, paper, cardboard, rags, ashes and other such waste matter normally to be disposed of from residences, churches, schools, small business establishments. Waste from establishments such as slaughterhouses and meat and fish markets shall not be considered as garbage but as "other wastes."
Other wastes means animal, fowl and fish excrement, entrails, bones, carcasses, in whole or in part, and dead animals.
Sanitary superintendent means the public works director, or his authorized representative.
Scavenger means any person who salvages or collects, for a fee for resale or use, any garbage, paper, cardboard, boxes, crates or other waste which are being or are to be disposed of from any residence or establishment where people reside, congregate or are employed.
Trash means yard wastes such as weeds, grass and hedge trimmings, leaves, brush, tree limbs and similar items.
(Code 2003, § 12-101)
Sec. 20-20. - Permit required for private collectors of garbage.
No person shall engage in the commercial collection or transportation of garbage and combustible refuse matter without having first secured a permit therefor from the city. Every person obtaining such permission shall conform to the rules deemed necessary by the city.
(Code 2003, § 12-111)

DIVISION 2. - RESIDENTIAL COLLECTION

Sec. 20-44. - Compliance by each family.
Each family in a residence building must comply with this division.
(Code 2003, § 12-110)
Sec. 20-45. - Garbage container: placing for collection.
(a)
Container. The city provides a 95 gallon container to each residential unit served. Each container will be numbered and assigned to a specific residential address. Containers are for household garbage only. All garbage must be bagged.
(b)
Other containers. You may not use any container for household garbage other than the one provided for you by the city. Additional containers may be obtained from the city upon payment of $10.00 per month for each such additional container.
(c)
Stolen containers. Report stolen containers to the Porterdale Police Department at 786-2217 and city hall at 786-2217.
(d)
Damage. If your container breaks through normal use, the city will replace it at no charge. Please call city hall at 786-2217 to report a damaged container.
(e)
Moving. The rollout container is the property of the City of Porterdale. Each container is numbered and assigned to a specific address. If you move, the container must remain at the current address.
(Code 2003, § 12-102; Ord. of 5-15-2007(2))
Sec. 20-46. - Placing trash for collection.
(a)
Trash shall be collected by the city trucks on application or call to city hall, by persons having such trash or upon days established therefor. Collections shall be at a time and in a manner prescribed by the mayor and city council, provided that at no time shall trash be placed in such a manner as to obstruct gutters, drains, walkways or streets.
(b)
Your container should be placed at the curb or within ten feet of the back alley no later than 7:00 a.m. on the day of your rollout service and removed by 12:00 midnight that same day.
(c)
A rollout exemption form is available by calling city hall at 786-2217. A special collection will be provided for disabled residents after completing this process.
(Code 2003, § 12-103)
Sec. 20-47. - Yard waste.
Yard waste will be collected on Tuesdays, Wednesday and Friday. Place your yard waste loose by the curb, but separate from other materials. It will be chipped into mulch at Newton County Land Field and offered back to the public for landscaping projects at $10.00 per load. Call city hall for more information.
(Code 2003, § 12-104)
Sec. 20-48. - Other wastes.
(a)
Bulk waste. Items such as furniture, mattresses, televisions, etc., will also be collected from residential dwellings. These items should be piled on the curb separate from yard waste. Bulk waste from nonresidential structures is not picked up by the city.
(b)
Scrap metal applications. White goods such as refrigerators, dishwashers, stoves, water heaters and washing machines, will be picked up from residential dwellings on the same day as bulk waste. Residents must call city hall to make arrangements for pickup of these items. There is no charge for this service, except refrigerators, freezers and air conditioners carry a $10.00 charge for the removal of Freon. Bulk waste is not picked up from nonresidential structures by the city.
(Code 2003, § 12-105)
Sec. 20-49. - Collection.
Containers will be picked up once a week. Due to landfill restrictions, yard waste and bulk waste will not be picked up at the same time.
(Code 2003, § 12-106; Ord. of 5-15-2007(2))
Sec. 20-50. - New service.
If you move to an address that does not have a rollout container or has not previously received sanitation services, call the city hall at 786-2217.
(Code 2003, § 12-107)
Sec. 20-51. - Fees as determined.
(a)
The collection fees for residences, commercial establishments, factories and other business places shall be such as may be set from time to time by resolution.
(b)
Bills for garbage pickups are included in the water bill. Payment is due the fifth day of the month. Late fees are as established by resolution.
(Code 2003, § 12-108)
Sec. 20-52. - Burning leaves or trash on streets.
No person shall burn leaves or other trash in the streets of the city.
(Code 2003, § 12-109)
Secs. 20-53—20-77. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - COMMERCIAL COLLECTION

Sec. 20-78. - When commercial garbage receptacles required.
The owners and/or operators of commercial and industrial establishments and apartment houses, including public housing, consisting of six or more living units, shall, subject to the determination of the director of public works, rent from the city commercial containers in sufficient number to adequately contain the garbage and refuse disposal at the location. The type, size, number and location of commercial containers required by this section shall be subject to the approval of the director of public works.
(Code 2003, § 12-201)
Sec. 20-79. - Placement of commercial containers.
Commercial containers required by this division shall be placed as required by the director of public works. If a commercial container is placed at any place other than that directed by the director of public works, the container will not be picked up. The failure of the city to pick up garbage or trash under such circumstances shall not affect the garbage container rental or rate for this service.
(Code 2003, § 12-202)
Sec. 20-80. - Commercial containers not to be left on streets; exception.
Commercial containers containing garbage, ashes or other refuse material shall not be left for collection upon any street, sidewalk, avenue or other public place, except as specifically authorized by the director of public works.
(Code 2003, § 12-203)
Sec. 20-81. - Night collections.
(a)
Whenever the mayor and the council shall decide that the best interests of the public will be served by collection of garbage from certain areas in the city at night, the director of public works shall be directed to make such collection.
(b)
The director of public works shall notify the occupants of the places of business within the designated area that the garbage will be picked up during certain hours and that the containers must be placed in the place designated for the pickup. All persons so notified shall comply with such order and shall place the containers as designated within such hours.
(c)
Residents shall be permitted to place up to 35 cubic feet (e.g., a small pick up truck load) per month of yard waste for pick up, without additional charge. If more than 35 cubic feet per month of yard waste is placed for pick up at a residence, an additional charge will be incurred, according to the City of Porterdale Solid Waste Disposal Fee Schedule.
(Code 2003, § 12-204; Ord. of 7-12-2010(1))
Sec. 20-82. - Bulk waste.
In the event a property owner, resident, and/or tenant places bulk waste for collection without first having applied for and obtained approval as required by section 20-81(c), the city will place a hang tag on the residence informing the owner or occupant that he shall have 48 hours to remove the bulk waste. In the event the bulk waste is not removed within the 48 hour period, the city may pick up the bulk waste and bill the cost of collection to the sanitation account or the owner of the property if there is no currently valid sanitation account covering the property.
(Ord. of 7-12-2010(1))
Sec. 20-83. - Other waste.
(a)
It shall be the responsibility of property owners, residents, and/or tenants to arrange for the disposal of all other waste other than those solid wastes collected by the city in accordance with the provisions of this division.
(b)
In the event a property owner, resident, and/or tenant places other waste for collection, the city will place a hang tag on the residence informing the owner or occupant that he shall have 48 hours to remove the other waste. In the event the other waste is not removed within the 48 hour period, the city may pick up the other waste and bill the cost of collection to the sanitation account or the owner of the property if there is no currently valid sanitation account covering the property.
(Ord. of 7-12-2010(1))
Secs. 20-84—20-109. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - SOLID WASTE COLLECTORS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 20-110. - Duty of licensees.
It shall be the duty of all persons issued a license under this article to conform to the requirements of this chapter and the Georgia Comprehensive Solid Waste Management Act (O.C.G.A. § 12-8-20 et seq.). Failure to so conform shall be grounds for revocation of said license.
(Code 2003, § 32-206(6))
Secs. 20-111—20-133. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 20-134. - License required.
Each person engaged in the business of solid waste collection and disposal in this municipality shall be required to obtain a license from the city clerk/treasurer in the manner specified in this division.
(Code 2003, § 32-206(1))
Sec. 20-135. - Application.
Application for a license to engage in the business of solid waste collection and disposal shall be made as provided in section 20-134, except that such application shall contain the following additional information:
(1)
Number, type, and size of waste collection vehicles to be used;
(2)
The number of employees to be assigned to each waste collection vehicle;
(3)
Name and location of commercial and industrial establishments and/or the number of residences to be served;
(4)
The types of waste to be collected; and
(5)
The state permit number of each disposal site to be used.
(Code 2003, § 32-206(2))
Sec. 20-136. - Review of application.
No action on any application for a license to engage in the business of solid waste collection and disposal shall be taken by the city council until the director of public works has reviewed such application and forwarded his recommendation thereon to the city clerk/treasurer, in the manner specified in this division.
(Code 2003, § 32-206(4))
Sec. 20-137. - Limitation on issuance.
No license shall be issued to any person whose solid waste collection vehicles do not meet the standards established for such vehicles in this division.
(Code 2003, § 32-206(5))
Sec. 20-138. - Fee established.
The annual business license fee for solid waste collectors doing business within this municipality shall be set by resolution of the council.
(Code 2003, § 32-206(3))
