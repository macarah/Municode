ARTICLE III. - BED AND BREAKFAST INNS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 22-146. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Bed and breakfast means a structure occupied by all of its owners, wherein lodging or lodging and breakfast only are provided to guests for compensation. The dwelling unit in which the bed and breakfast takes place shall be the principal residence of the operator, and such operator shall live in the structure when the bed and breakfast operation is in operation.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-2, 12-18-1995)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 22-147. - Exceptions.
This article shall not apply to hotels, inns, motels, motor lodges, boardinghouses, roominghouses or other establishments providing rooms to guests for compensation. None of such establishments shall be permitted in the city except as otherwise allowed by ordinances of the city.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-3, 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-148. - Temporary operations.
This article shall not apply to bed and breakfast establishments being operated on a temporary basis for charitable or civic organizations. Such establishments shall be permitted at the discretion of the mayor and city council upon application for no more than four consecutive days and for no more than eight days per calendar year.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-4, 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-149. - Compliance with article.
No bed and breakfast establishment shall be operated in the city except as provided in this article.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-3, 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-150. - Penalty for violations.
Any violation of the provisions of this ordinance shall be a misdemeanor and shall subject the offender to punishment as provided in section 1-11. In addition thereto, any such violation shall be cause for revocation, suspension or nonrenewal of the offender's bed and breakfast license by the city.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-25, 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-151. - Location restricted.
No bed and breakfast establishment shall be allowed except in areas zoned R1 residential district, R-1A residential district, P professional district, according to the official zoning district maps for the city, as amended, which are hereby incorporated in this article by reference.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-13, 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-152. - Other taxes applicable.
All bed and breakfast operations shall be subject to the hotel/motel tax of the city.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-19, 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-153. - Periodic inspections.
The code enforcement officer shall be granted access to any bed and breakfast establishment, upon 24 hours prior written notice delivered to the operator or licensee of the establishment, for purposes of inspecting the premises to ensure compliance with this article.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-23, 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-154. - Inspection of records.
The code enforcement officer shall be provided access to any guest registration records maintained by the operator or licensee for the purpose of determining the number of rooms being rented to the public.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-24, 12-18-1995)
Secs. 22-155—22-175. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 22-176. - Occupation tax required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a bed and breakfast establishment without having first paid an occupation tax. The annual occupation tax for such license shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk for the whole or any part of one year, and shall be paid at city hall, no later than January 15th or upon the filing of an application for such business.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-5, 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-177. - Application.
All applicants for a license to operate a bed and breakfast shall submit the following materials for review:
(1)
A floor plan of the single-family dwelling unit, illustrating that the proposed operation will comply with all ordinances and regulations of the city, and all other governmental agencies or bodies.
(2)
A completed and signed application form.
(3)
A plat of survey of the subject real estate by a state registered surveyor which shows all improvements located on the real estate and the distance from the bed and breakfast dwelling to the nearest bed and breakfast dwelling.
(4)
A nonrefundable application fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk shall be paid at the time the application is submitted. Such fee is in addition to the annual occupation tax which must be paid at the same time and is refundable if the license application is not approved.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-8, 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-178. - Inspection.
No bed and breakfast license shall be granted to any applicant until the bed and breakfast premises have been inspected by the code enforcement officer who finds that the electrical and plumbing systems of the premises are in safe and satisfactory condition.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-9, 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-179. - Factors considered for issuance, denial.
In deciding whether or not to grant, renew, suspend or revoke a license under this division, the mayor and council shall consider the following:
(1)
Compliance by the applicant with the provisions of this article and all other relevant laws or ordinances, regulations and resolutions of the city and any other governmental body or agency having jurisdiction of such activity;
(2)
The character and reputation of the applicant or license holder;
(3)
The conduct of the business of the applicant;
(4)
The actual or proposed effect of the bed and breakfast on nearby properties, including but not limited to noise, pollution, dirt, property values and all other relevant considerations;
(5)
The actual or proposed effect of the bed and breakfast on the city, including but not limited to the services and utilities of the city;
(6)
The profit and loss of the bed and breakfast establishments and the reason for operation of same by the applicant; and
(7)
All other relevant issues, including, but not limited to, the public health, safety, morals, interest and convenience.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-12, 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-180. - Business records.
All license holders under this division shall maintain separate records which show all activities of their bed and breakfast operation and shall keep a profit and loss statement. All such documents and related papers shall be available to the city or its agents at all reasonable times.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-11, 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-181. - Reserved.
Editor's note— Art. I, of Ord. No. 2014-04 deleted § 22-181, maximum number of licensed operations, which derived from Ord. No. 95-08, adopted Dec. 18, 1995, in its entirety.
Sec. 22-182. - Voidance upon sale.
A bed and breakfast license shall be immediately voided upon the sale or transfer of the subject real estate.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-17, 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-183. - Duration.
All licenses granted under this division shall be issued for a period of one year or until December 31 of the year the application is made, whichever date is earlier.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-10, 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-184. - Expiration.
All licenses issued under this division shall expire December 31 of each year after issuance, unless revoked or suspended prior to expiration.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-22, 12-18-1995)
Secs. 22-185—22-205. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - OPERATION REQUIREMENTS

Sec. 22-206. - Compliance.
No premises shall be utilized for a bed and breakfast operation unless all the provisions of this division are met.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-15, 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-207. - Guest register.
Each operator shall keep a register of all guests of the bed and breakfast operation. Such list shall be available for inspection by the city or its agents at all reasonable times.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-6, 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-208. - Maximum length of stay per guest.
The maximum stay for any occupant of bed and breakfast operations shall be seven consecutive days.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-7, 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-209. - Number of units, guests.
There shall be no more than four bedrooms per dwelling unit used for bed and breakfast operation. The maximum number of guests per day shall be eight. Establishments with more than four guest rooms shall be considered a motel and must therefore be located in a commercial district.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-15(a), 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-210. - Bath facilities.
At least one indoor lavatory and bathing facility must be available to all guests using any bed and breakfast operation for every two bedrooms in such operation.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-15(b), 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-211. - Signs.
No signs indicating the structure is being used as a bed and breakfast shall be allowed on the subject real property, except for existing signs and one sign made out of wood or woodlike material measuring no larger than two feet by one foot affixed to the bed and breakfast structure and the background color of the sign being the same color as the exterior of the bed and breakfast structure.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-15(d), 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-212. - Parking.
One off-street parking space per bedroom must be available for the bed and breakfast operation which is in addition to the parking to be available for the operator and/or his staff. No on-street parking for guests or operators of the bed and breakfast shall be permitted.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-15(d), 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-213. - Smoking.
No smoking by guests shall be allowed.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-15(e), 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-214. - Appearance of structure.
There shall be no outside evidence of bed and breakfast operation, and no changes within six months of the date of application by the applicant shall be made in the bed and breakfast structure that are visible from the exterior.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-15(f), 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-215. - Operation restricted.
The bed and breakfast shall be totally operated in the principal existing structure.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-15(g), 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-216. - Minimum size of structure.
The structure used in a bed and breakfast operation shall contain at least 2,500 square feet of heated living area, and not to any degree located in any accessory structure.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-15(g), 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-217. - Workers restricted.
No more than one nonfamily worker shall be allowed outside or inside the premises exclusive of maintenance personnel. All family workers must permanently reside inside the principle existing structure used as the bed and breakfast operation.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-15(h), 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-218. - Alcoholic beverages.
Alcoholic beverages, beer and/or wine shall not be sold to any guest at the premises.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-15(i), 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-219. - Meals restricted.
No meals except breakfast may be served to any guest.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-15, 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-220. - Commercial use restricted.
All bed and breakfasts located in areas zoned R1 residential district or R-1A residential district according to the official zoning district maps for the city shall be used exclusively for residential and for bed and breakfast purposes. No other type of home occupation or commercial activity shall be conducted on any portion of the subject real estate.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-16, 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-221. - Discrimination.
No owner of a bed and breakfast establishment shall discriminate regarding his guests on the basis of race, color, sex, religion, creed or natural origin.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-18, 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-222. - Food handling.
Food shall be prepared in a sanitary manner. The county health officer shall provide instruction and written information to all persons handling food in each bed and breakfast establishment. Storage of food, and the temperature at which food must be stored, shall be in accordance with applicable laws, rules and regulations of the state, county and city.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-20, 12-18-1995)
Sec. 22-223. - Vermin control.
Each bed and breakfast establishment shall be kept free of insects and rodents.
(Ord. No. 95-08, § 8-8-21, 12-18-1995)
Secs. 22-224—22-255. - Reserved.
