Chapter 38 - ELECTIONS[1]

Cross reference— Mayor and council, § 2-36 et seq.

Sec. 38-1. - Adoption of state rules, regulations.
The rules and regulations promulgated by the state election board which pertain to municipal elections, together with the provisions of Title 21 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated as the same may be amended from time to time are hereby adopted as the rules, regulations and provisions that shall govern the conduct of municipal general and special elections in the city.
(Ord. No. 2012-02, art. I, 1-10-2012)
Sec. 38-2. - Election officials.
Pursuant to the authority granted under Georgia Municipal Election Code, the city hereby authorizes Walton County, through the Walton County Board of Elections and Registration, to conduct all municipal elections in the city and further requests that the county perform all duties of conducting elections as specified under the Georgia Municipal Election Code and the Code of the City of Monroe, Georgia.
(Ord. No. 97-02, § 2-2-1, 3-4-1997; Ord. No. 2003-01, 5-14-2003)
Sec. 38-3. - Expenses.
The city shall pay the county all costs incurred in performing those functions which the city has requested the county to perform in handling and conducting the municipal elections in the city. The city and county shall enter into an intergovernmental contract to establish the expenses associated with the election.
(Ord. No. 97-02, § 2-2-2, 3-4-1997)
Sec. 38-4. - Election districts and polling places.
The city is divided in election districts pursuant to state law and city charter. Those persons living in the city shall vote at an election precinct according to the district in which they reside and as directed by the election superintendent.
(Ord. No. 97-02, § 2-2-3, 3-4-1997)
Sec. 38-5. - Nomination of candidates.
Qualifications of candidates for municipal office shall be at such times and dates as required by the Georgia Municipal Election Code.
(Code 1988, § 2-2-6)
State Law reference— Filing notice of candidacy, O.C.G.A. § 21-3-91.
Sec. 38-6. - Qualification fees.
Each candidate seeking election in a special or general election for the office of mayor or council member shall pay a qualification fee. The fee will be paid to the municipal election superintendent, as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk, at the time a candidate files his notice of candidacy.
(Code 1988, § 2-2-7)
State Law reference— Qualification fees, O.C.G.A. § 21-3-90.
Sec. 38-7. - Nonpartisan elections.
All elections held for the purpose of filling municipal offices in the city shall be nonpartisan, and no political party shall conduct primaries for the purpose of nominating candidates for such municipal elections.
(Code 1988, § 2-2-8)
Sec. 38-8. - Vote required for election.
A candidate must receive a majority of the votes cast as provided in the Georgia Municipal Election Code as amended from time to time.
(Code 1988, § 2-2-9; Ord. No. 2012-01, art. I, 1-10-2012)
