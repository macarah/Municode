Sec. 18-111. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Peddler means any person who purchases or otherwise acquires within or without the county any goods, wares, merchandise, produce or other things of value and goes about from place to place within the county selling or offering for sale any of such things to either merchants or customers.
Solicitor means any person who solicits orders on behalf of a firm, corporation, company, association, partnership or individual for any goods, wares or merchandise or other things of value, from house to house. Any person who obtains orders for merchandise or other things of value shall be deemed a solicitor.
(Res. No. 92-17, § 1.70, 2-28-92)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 18-112. - Peddlers to be licensed.
Every peddler shall be required to obtain a license. All licenses to peddle shall be paid for at the price fixed by article II of this chapter, or otherwise.
(Res. No. 92-17, § 1.71, 2-28-92)
Sec. 18-113. - Conduct of business by peddlers.
(a)
No trader or peddler shall offer for sale any goods, wares or merchandise in the unincorporated area of the county without first obtaining a permit from the license bureau of the county.
(b)
Peddlers shall conduct business from locations zoned GC only.
(c)
Peddlers shall maintain all merchandise on a vehicle. The peddler's business license shall be void if the peddler does not have in his possession at the location a business license and a written notarized affidavit from the property owner authorizing the peddler to be engaged in business at the property owner's location.
(d)
Any violations of the provisions of this division pertaining to peddlers shall subject the license to revocation.
(Res. No. 92-17, § 1.72, 2-28-92)
Sec. 18-114. - Solicitor's permit.
(a)
Permit required; applicability of division.
(1)
Resident solicitors. A resident solicitor shall obtain a solicitor's permit and be subject to all rules and regulations of this division.
(2)
Nonresident solicitors. Any solicitor who solicits orders as described in this division and whose employer does not maintain a place for doing business in the county so as to be subject to the government, rules and regulations of this chapter shall be deemed a nonresident solicitor and shall comply with the regulations of this division.
(3)
Persons holding business license. Any applicant who has paid a business license fee or tax shall receive a credit of the amount so paid toward the payment of the solicitor's registration and permit fee. However, such applicant shall in all manners and all respects be subject to all other provisions of this division.
(b)
Application; investigation of applicant; grounds for denial.
(1)
No solicitor shall be authorized to solicit orders until he files an application with the license bureau and obtains a permit and registers as a solicitor. The license bureau shall make an investigation, including but not limited to an investigation of the criminal record and moral character of the applicant. The license bureau shall furnish the county marshal a duplicate of the application for the solicitor's permit. The county marshal, within ten days, shall furnish a report to the license bureau of the criminal record, if any, of the applicant. No permit shall be given to any applicant who shall have either a pending charge or a conviction for a crime of moral turpitude or an offense involving the elements of assault and battery, or any civil judgment involving unethical and improper business actions, including but not limited to actions which would constitute fraud and deceit under the laws of the state.
(2)
No application for a permit under this section shall be received or acted upon if the applicant has submitted the same or a similar application for a permit within the preceding 12 months, which prior application has been dismissed, denied or abandoned. No permit shall be granted if the applicant is in violation of this division or has, within 12 months preceding the application, been in violation of this division or any other law or ordinance regulating the activities for which such permit is sought.
(c)
Fee. The applicant for a solicitor's permit shall pay a fee as set out in the schedule of fees, as cost for investigation and regulation under this division. This fee shall be used for payment of the cost of such registration, investigation and regulation of persons subject to this division.
(d)
Term; renewal. A permit issued under this division shall be valid for 90 days from the date the permit is issued. The permit may be renewed by filing a renewal application with the business license office upon forms prescribed by the office, and paying a renewal fee as set out in the schedule of fees, to cover the expense of updating the investigative report.
(e)
House-to-house solicitation. All solicitors, both nonresident and resident, who solicit orders from house to house shall first obtain a permit for such solicitation from the supervisor of the license office.
(Res. No. 92-17, §§ 1.73—1.77, 2-28-92; Ord. No. 2016-O-005, exh. A, 8-2-16)
Sec. 18-115. - Badge for solicitors.
All solicitors shall wear a badge, the design of which shall be in the discretion of the license office, but which shall be uniform as to all solicitors. The badge shall provide the following information: the name of the solicitor, the name of the company, and an identification number, in large enough type to be read and seen by persons with normal vision at a distance of ten feet. At all times, the solicitor shall wear on either the left or right shoulder of his garment the badge prescribed by the license office, so that such badge can be readily seen by the owner or occupant of the premises.
(Res. No. 92-17, § 1.78, 2-28-92)
Sec. 18-116. - Conduct of business by solicitors; information to be provided to customers.
(a)
Information card required; contents. The supervisor of the license office shall provide to each solicitor an information card, and the solicitor shall present this card to each household he visits. It is the responsibility of the solicitor to make sufficient copies of the information card to furnish a copy to each household he visits. Such card shall contain such information as required by the business license supervisor. The following statements may appear on the information card:
The solicitor is not to enter your home except at your express invitation.
You have the right to refuse entry or to ask the solicitor to leave the premises, and if he/she should fail to leave the premises after your instructions, he/she shall be in violation of the laws of this state and/or ordinances of Cherokee County.
Report any discourteous conduct to the Business License Office of Cherokee County.
In the event you purchase the product of the solicitor, you have the right to cancel a Home Solicitation Service Agreement any time until midnight on the business day after the day on which you sign the agreement, provided you give notice of the cancellation to the seller at the place of business as set forth in the agreement or by Certified Mail, Return Receipt Requested, which shall be posted not later than midnight on the day following execution of your agreement.
A solicitor shall place his/her name and permit number in the blank provided on the card and shall present it to the occupant of each home that he visits.
(b)
Failure to present information card. It shall be unlawful for a solicitor to fail to present the card to the resident or occupant of a home at which the solicitor solicits business.
(c)
Announcement to occupant. At each house, the solicitor shall announce to the owner or occupant of the home his name and that he has been issued a permit by the county license bureau as a home solicitor, and shall announce the purpose of his call and present the card provided for by this section and shall allow time for the occupant or owner to read the card.
(d)
Entry on private property. No solicitor shall enter a home without the express invitation of the occupant or owner. If a "no soliciting" sign is posted, no soliciting shall be made.
(e)
Courteous conduct required; compliance with request to leave premises. A solicitor shall at all times maintain a courteous decorum and shall not use opprobrious, vulgar or slang words to any homeowner or occupant. No solicitor shall remain upon the premises of an occupant or owner after such occupant or owner has requested the solicitor to leave or indicated that he is not interested in purchasing the solicitor's product.
(f)
Misrepresentation of effect of permit. It shall be unlawful for any solicitor to represent by word, action or deed that the issuance of a permit by the county license bureau in any way represents approval or condonation of either the actions of the solicitor or his product.
(g)
Information to be provided to persons placing orders. If a solicitor secures an order from an owner or occupant, the solicitor shall inform the owner or occupant as to his rights under the home solicitation act of the state. This may be done by the solicitor leaving a written card or document specifying the rights of a homeowner or occupant under the Retail Installment and Home Solicitation Sales Act.
(Res. No. 92-17, §§ 1.79—1.85, 2-28-92)
Sec. 18-117. - County records of solicitors; complaints against solicitors.
(a)
The business license department shall maintain true and accurate records of the name and identification number of each solicitor, together with other information required by this division, and shall maintain a log of all complaints for each solicitor or organization represented by solicitors.
(b)
If the license office receives any ordinance violation complaints concerning a particular solicitor, or three violation complaints concerning an organization, the business license office shall notify the solicitor and the organization the solicitor represents of the complaints, and the business license department may suspend the permit until such time as the solicitor or organization can show cause that he or it is in compliance with the rules and regulations of this division.
(c)
The business license department shall maintain a true and accurate log of each complaint, showing the name of the complainant and the substance of the complaint. Such records shall be available to the solicitor or his company or organization, and shall be deemed a public record. A hearing shall be set before the supervisor of the license office within five business days after notification of the solicitor, at which time the license office shall investigate the nature of the complaints and the solicitor may use witnesses or other evidence to show his compliance with this chapter. If the supervisor of the license office determines that the solicitor has violated the ordinances of the county, he shall revoke the permit of the solicitor.
(Res. No. 92-17, § 1.86, 2-28-92)
Sec. 18-118. - Appeals by solicitors.
(a)
A solicitor shall have the right to file an appeal from the ruling of the business license department under this division to the board of commissioners.
(b)
If any solicitor or his company is dissatisfied with the decision of the board of commissioners, he shall have the right to appeal as provided by the laws of the state.
(Res. No. 92-17, §§ 1.87, 1.88, 2-28-92)
Sec. 18-119. - Charitable solicitors, religious solicitors and evangelists.
(a)
Registration required. No person may conduct charitable solicitation or religious evangelism or solicitation on a door-to-door basis in any residential area unless he has registered as a charitable solicitor or as a colporteur, solicitor or evangelist with the county license bureau.
(b)
Hours of operation. No person may conduct charitable solicitation or religious evangelism or solicitation on a door-to-door basis in any residential area except during the period from 9:00 a.m. through 9:00 p.m. of any day.
(c)
Badge. Any person conducting charitable solicitation or religious evangelism or solicitation on a door-to-door basis in any residential area shall wear a badge on either the left or right shoulder printed in type normally visible from a distance of ten feet, which badge must contain the following information:
(1)
The person's name; and
(2)
The name, address and telephone number of the organization he represents.
(d)
Information to be provided to license bureau. Each person registering with the county business license department as a charitable solicitor or as a religious colporteur, solicitor or evangelist shall give the following information to the license bureau:
(1)
His full name.
(2)
His residence address.
(3)
His residence phone number.
(4)
His social security number.
(5)
The name, local address and phone number of the organization the individual represents.
(e)
Registration fee. Each individual registering under this section shall pay to the business license department a fee as shown on the schedule of fees in the business license department. Such fee is not a revenue-raising measure, but is intended only to cover the cost to the county of registration.
(Res. No. 92-17, § 1.89, 2-28-92)
