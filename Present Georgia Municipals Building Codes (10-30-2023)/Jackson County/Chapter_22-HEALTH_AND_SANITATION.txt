ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 22-1. - Rules and regulations promulgated for the protection of environmental health and public health.
The following rules and regulations of the Georgia Department of Public Health ("DPH"), including all subsequent amendments, revisions or modifications thereto, and which are expressly incorporated herein, are hereby adopted and approved for enforcement as set forth herein:
(1)
Tourist Accommodations, Comp. R. and Regs. § 511-6-2.
(2)
On-Site Sewage Management Systems, Comp. R. and Regs. § 511-3-1.
(3)
Portable Sanitation Contractors, Comp. R. and Regs. § 511-3-6.
Copies of those DPH rules and regulations are on file in the office of the clerk of the board of commissioners, where the same are available for inspection and copying by the public during regular county operating hours.
( Ord. No. 15-001, § I, 1-19-2015 )

ARTICLE II. - SMOKING REGULATIONS FOR COUNTY BUILDINGS[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Smoking in public places, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-2.

Sec. 22-19. - Statement of intent.
The surgeon general of the United States has identified smoking as "public health enemy No. 1" in light of its role as the leading cause of premature death and disability in our country. The board of commissioners recognizes that secondhand cigarette smoke causes lung cancer in nonsmokers and is a major source of indoor air pollution and toxic chemical exposure for county employees. The board of commissioners, therefore, is dedicated to providing a healthy, comfortable and productive work environment for county employees.
(Code 2005, § 42-31)
Sec. 22-20. - Applicability.
This article is applicable to all county employees, employees of other agencies, and the general public. The success of this policy will depend upon the thoughtfulness, consideration and cooperation of smokers and nonsmokers. All officers and employees of the county share the responsibility for adhering to and enforcing this article.
(Code 2005, § 42-32)
Sec. 22-21. - Distribution.
Each elected officer and department head will be responsible for distributing a copy of this article to all employees and a copy of this article shall be posted on the primary employee bulletin board in each department or agency. The department head shall be responsible for providing a copy of such ordinance to each potential employee prior to employment.
(Code 2005, § 42-33)
Sec. 22-22. - Penalty.
(a)
A violation of this article shall be treated just as a violation of any other ordinance of the county and shall be adjudicated in the magistrate's court of the county.
(b)
The penalty for violation of this article shall be not less than $25.00 nor more than $100.00. In addition to such fine, or in lieu thereof, the judge of the magistrate's court may impose the performance of community service of not less than ten hours nor more than 50 hours for each such violation.
(c)
The penalty imposed by the magistrate's court, notwithstanding any employee of the county who violates this article may be disciplined as for a violation of any other county policy as provided for in the county personnel system.
(Code 2005, § 42-34)
Sec. 22-23. - Prohibition.
Smoking is prohibited in the county courthouse and the county administrative building, and all other county buildings.
(Code 2005, § 42-35)
Sec. 22-24. - Designation of smoking permitted areas.
Outdoor areas only shall be designated as "smoking permitted" areas.
(Code 2005, § 42-36)
Sec. 22-25. - Signs.
(a)
No smoking signs shall be placed throughout the buildings as deemed necessary.
(b)
Signs displaying "Smoking Prohibited" shall be posted at the entrances of the buildings. Appropriate receptacles shall be placed at the entrances of all buildings for the disposal of smoking materials.
(Code 2005, § 42-37)
Secs. 22-26—22-53. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - FOOD SERVICE

Sec. 22-54. - The rules and regulations for food services.
The rules and regulations for food services adopted by the state department of human resources known as rules and regulations of food services, O.C.G.A. § 290-5-14 et seq., are hereby incorporated by reference, attached to the ordinance from which this article is derived, and adopted as this section, the rules and regulations for food services.
(Ord. of 4-21-2008, § 2)
Sec. 22-55. - Penalties.
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this article shall be punished by a fine of not more than $1,000.00 or by imprisonment for not more than six months, or by both such fine and imprisonment, or up to the limits of any penalty provided by state law for the violation of this article. Each day any violation of a section or subsection of this article shall continue shall constitute a separate offense.
(Ord. of 4-21-2008, § 3)
