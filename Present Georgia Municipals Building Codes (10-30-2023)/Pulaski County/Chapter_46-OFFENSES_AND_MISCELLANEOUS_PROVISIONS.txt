Sec. 46-1. - Limits on sound.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate or amplify the sound produced by any radio, tape player, amplifier, musical instrument or other mechanical sound making device or instrument so that the sound generated thereby is plainly audible at a distance of 100 feet or more from such amplifying device or instrument. The provisions of this section shall not apply to:
(1)
Any law enforcement motor vehicle equipped with any communication device necessary in the performance of law enforcement duties or to any emergency vehicle equipped with any communication device necessary in the performance of any emergency procedures;
(2)
The discharge of firearms associated with lawful hunting or marksmanship training;
(3)
The operation, landing or takeoff of any airplane or helicopter;
(4)
The sound made by any horn or other warning device required or permitted by O.C.G.A. § 40-8-70 nor any burglar, fire or security alarm;
(5)
The lawful operation of any manufacturing process, lawfully equipped motor vehicle, motorized earth moving or grading equipment, motorized farm equipment, power tools or chainsaws;
(6)
Public address systems utilized at athletic contests and events sponsored by Pulaski County, Pulaski County Schools or Board of Education or the City of Hawkinsville.
(b)
Violation of this section shall be punishable as set out in section 1-11 of the Pulaski County Code of Ordinances.
