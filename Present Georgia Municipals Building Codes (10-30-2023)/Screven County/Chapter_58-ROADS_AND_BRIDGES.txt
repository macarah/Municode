ARTICLE II. - TIMBER HARVESTING

Sec. 58-31. - Notification of public works director; inspection to determine suitability of access site; culverts.
All persons, prior to engaging in the harvesting of any timber, either logs or pulpwood, in the county, are required to notify the public works director, or his designee, in person or by telephone, and provide the following information:
(1)
The name of the person about to engage in the harvesting operation;
(2)
The mailing address and telephone number of the harvesting entity;
(3)
The name of the foreman or supervisor personally in charge of the harvesting operation;
(4)
The name of the landowner where the harvesting operation is to take place;
(5)
The access points (within a reasonable area of operation) to a public county road from the lands on which the harvesting is to take place; and
(6)
The date on which the harvesting operation will commence and the anticipated duration of the operation.
The public works director, or his designee, shall inspect the access site to the public county road and shall determine its suitability and if a culvert is required at the point of access to the county right-of-way, together with the size and length thereof, if needed. The culvert must be installed by the harvesting person under the supervision of the public works director or his designee.
(Ord. No. 92-02, § I, 11-12-1991)
Sec. 58-32. - Harvesting operations.
(a)
All harvesting operations shall be conducted at the selected loading site and off the county right-of-way.
(b)
Loading or skidding of logs on county roads and county rights-of-way is strictly prohibited.
(c)
Ditches shall be kept clear of all debris and residue at all times so as to permit proper drainage.
(d)
There shall be no blockage of county roads at the access site, and roads must remain open at all times for utilization by the traveling public, emergency vehicles, school buses and mail carriers.
(e)
Persons engaged in the harvest and transportation of timber shall take all necessary steps to prevent accumulations of mud and debris on the county roads and will be required to use crushed stone or other material to prevent such accumulation at sites, where necessary.
(Ord. No. 92-02, § II, 11-12-1991)
Sec. 58-33. - Termination of access site.
(a)
At the conclusion of the harvesting operation, the foreman or supervisor in charge thereof shall notify the public works director, or his designee, that the harvesting operation is complete and request an inspection of the access site to determine if there has been any damage to the road or right-of-way as a result of the harvesting operation. Damages caused by the harvesting operation shall be repaired by the harvesting person within 15 days or, if necessary, by the county or a contractor of the county's choice, at the expense of the person responsible therefor.
(b)
Culverts installed and utilized in the harvesting operation may be removed, provided that the road right-of-way shall be restored to the same condition existing prior to the installation of the culvert.
(Ord. No. 92-02, § III, 11-12-1991)
Sec. 58-34. - Penalty for violation of article.
(a)
The public works director, or his designee, shall have the authority, with the specific approval of the county manager, to close down any harvesting operation when conducted in a manner in violation hereof or for failure to provide the information and notices required.
(b)
A violation of the provisions of this article, including failure to reimburse the county for necessary repairs, shall subject the offending person to a penalty as provided in section 1-11. The payment of such penalty does not relieve the offending person from the obligation to pay for all repairs as stated.
(Ord. No. 92-02, § IV, 11-12-1991)
Secs. 58-35—58-50. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - UTILITIES ON COUNTY RIGHTS-OF-WAY

Sec. 58-51. - Permit required.
Before any utility, public or private, may install, construct, remove or relocate any equipment facility or appliance of any kind in, on, under or over the public roads and/or roads' rights-of-way of the county, it must apply for and obtain a permit from the county roads department, pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 32-4-42(6).
(Ord. of 1-22-2008, § 2)
Sec. 58-52. - County manager to promulgate rules and regulations.
The county manager shall promulgate such rules and regulations as he deems necessary to carry out the state law provisions referenced in section 58-51.
(Ord. of 1-22-2008, § 2)
Sec. 58-53. - Penalties for violations.
Any violation of this article shall be punishable as provided in section 1-11.
(Ord. of 1-22-2008, § 2)

Sec. 58-60. - Declaration of policy and purpose.
The board of commissioners does hereby declare that it shall promote the health, safety and general public welfare of the citizens of Screven County by providing for regulations of the use of roads contained in the Screven County road system pursuant to the authority granted in the Georgia Constitution, Article IX, Section II Paragraphs I and III and the Official Code of Georgia §§ 40-6-371 and 32-4-42, which provide that, in addition to the power specifically delegated to it in that title, a county shall have the authority to adopt and enforce rules and regulations or ordinances to require permits and to perform all other acts which are necessary, proper or incidental to the efficient operation and development of the county road system.
(Ord. of 5-12-2015, § 2)
Sec. 58-61. - Definitions.
The following terms, when used in this article shall, for the purpose of this article, have the meanings respectively ascribed to them:
Commercial truck means any truck with more than six wheels.
County means the unincorporated area of Screven County, Georgia.
Designated road means any road listed in section 58-44 of this article.
Local delivery means, with respect to a designated road, a delivery made by a commercial truck to a business or residence located on and with access to a designated road. Provided, however, that a delivery shall not under any circumstances be deemed a "local delivery" if an undesignated road also provides a means of access to the business or residence to which the delivery is being made.
Person means any individual, partnership, corporation, association or private organization of an character.
(Ord. of 5-12-2015, § 2)
Sec. 58-62. - Determination and posting of load limits.
(a)
The county director of public works shall direct a study of the roads of the county, and in such director's discretion, without impeding the flow of necessary commercial traffic, make and determine proper load limits for those roads which are being damaged and destroyed by overweight traffic, and having determined a proper load limit, shall report the same to the board of commissioners for its approval. The approved load limits shall be recorded in the official minutes of the board, and the director shall post both ends of each such road with the standard state-designed signs notifying the traveling public of the established load limits. After the signs have been in place for a period of one week, the sheriff shall be instructed to enforce the same, pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 32-1-10, and this Code.
(b)
Operation on a county road of a motor vehicle exceeding the load limit established for such road is prohibited.
(Ord. of 5-12-2015, § 2)
Sec. 58-63. - Application and permit for single use exceptions.
In the event it is necessary for trucks carrying a weight greater than the weight established for a road to use that road for any purpose, the county director of public works shall have discretion, upon receipt of an appropriate application, to permit the usage for the specific purpose requested, for a single use, and on the terms and conditions which will protect the road, as determined by the director.
(Ord. of 5-12-2015, § 2)
Sec. 58-64. - Commercial truck traffic prohibited.
(a)
Commercial trucks (as defined in this article) are prohibited from traveling upon or utilizing the following roads with the county:
(1)
Ogeechee Road.
(2)
Rocky Ford Road.
(3)
Kildare Road.
(4)
Milhaven Road.
(5)
Pryor Road.
(6)
Oglethorpe Trail.
(7)
Hiltonia Creek Road.
(8)
Plantation Road.
(b)
The prohibition set forth in subsection (a) above shall not apply to commercial trucks when making a local delivery on any of the designated roads.
(Ord. of 5-12-2015, § 2)
Sec. 58-65. - Publication and dissemination.
A copy of this article of the code shall be disseminated by the director of public works in any reasonable manner.
(Ord. of 5-12-2015, § 2)
Sec. 58-66. - Penalties.
Any person violating any provision of this article shall be subject to criminal penalties as provided in section 1-11. In addition, any owner or operator of a truck who is prosecuted for violation of this ordinance shall also be liable for any court costs incurred by the county in prosecuting such violation. Any fine or other penalty imposed pursuant to this article shall be in addition to any recovery by the county through civil remedy for damages caused to county roads by trucks operating on such roads.
(Ord. of 5-12-2015, § 2)
