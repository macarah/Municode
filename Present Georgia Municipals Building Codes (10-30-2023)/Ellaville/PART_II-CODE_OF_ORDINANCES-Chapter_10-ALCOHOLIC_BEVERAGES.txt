Chapter 10 - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES[1]
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 10-1. - Definitions.
modified
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcoholic beverage means any beverage containing alcohol, however sold, and shall include, but shall not be limited to, malt beverages, wines, and spirituous liquors.
Malt beverage means any fermented beverage made in whole or in part from malt, or any similar fermented beverage, containing any amount of alcohol.
Manager means any person who supervises the regular operations of a business licensed under this chapter.
Mixed drink means any distilled spirits or spirituous liquors served for consumption on the premises by the drink that may or may not be diluted by any other liquid. Any sales of mixed drinks in the city must be approved by referendum as required by state law.
Nuisance means anything that causes hurt, inconvenience or damage to another, and the fact that the act done may otherwise be lawful shall not keep it from being a nuisance. The inconvenience complained of shall not be fanciful, or such as would affect only one of fastidious taste, but it shall be such as would affect an ordinary reasonable person.
Owner means any person, partner or stockholder owning any percentage of a business.
Premises means the space or area owned, leased or controlled by the licensee and used by the licensee for the purpose of operating under the license.
Restaurant means any establishment which is licensed under this chapter, which establishment derives at least 30 percent of its total annual gross sales from the sale of prepared meals and which is publicly held out as a place where hot meals are prepared and served at least five days a week, excluding holidays.
Spirituous liquors or distilled spirits means all beverages containing alcohol obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume, including fortified wines.
Wine means any wine, fortified or unfortified, produced either within or without the state and containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-1)
Sec. 10-2. - Applicability of and conflict with state law.
modified
(a)
Governing law. The Georgia Alcoholic Beverage Code, O.C.G.A. § 3-1-1 et seq., wherever applicable to municipalities, is hereby adopted and made a part of this chapter.
(b)
Conflict of laws. Any provisions of this chapter in conflict with the cited state code, shall be invalid and the state code section dealing with the same subjects shall control.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-2)
Sec. 10-3. - Activity declared a privilege.
Notwithstanding anything in this chapter to the contrary, the sale of alcoholic beverages in the city is a privilege, and not a right, and the issuance of a license hereunder shall not create any property rights in the license holder.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-3)
State Law reference— Similar provision, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-1.
Sec. 10-4. - Responsibility for enforcement.
It shall be the responsibility of the chief of police or the chief's agents to enforce the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-4)
Sec. 10-5. - Familiarity and compliance with chapter.
Any licensee hereunder shall ensure that the licensee and all employees of the licensee are advised of and comply with the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-53)
Sec. 10-6. - Availability of telephone.
Every premises licensed hereunder must have a telephone and have such telephone available during the hours of operation. Failure to have a telephone shall constitute grounds for suspension or revocation of all licenses.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-57(d))
Sec. 10-7. - Illegal drugs or narcotics.
For every license issued pursuant to this chapter, it shall be the duty and responsibility of the licensee, or licensee's agent or employee to report to the police department any usage or possession of illegal drugs or narcotics on a licensed premises.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 10-57(e))
Secs. 10-8—10-32. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - LICENSING[2]

Sec. 10-33. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to manufacture or sell or offer for sale at wholesale or retail in the city any alcoholic beverage without having the required alcoholic beverage license.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-30)
State Law reference— Authority for local licensure generally, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2.
Sec. 10-34. - Licenses for nonprofit organizations.
Any patriotic organization, charitable organization, or fraternal organization that has been in operation in the city for a period of five years under a national or state charter and that is nonprofit, in that the individual members do not receive any dividends or share in the profits, shall be licensed in order to make retail sales of spirituous liquors to its members. No license shall be issued unless the organization is approved by the city for the issuance of such license.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-31(a))
Sec. 10-35. - Bona fide private clubs.
modified
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Fixed salary means the amount of compensation paid any member, officer, agent or employee of a bona fide club as may be fixed for the person by its members at a prior annual meeting or by the city council of the organization out of the general revenue of the club and shall not include any commission on any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages. For the purpose of this definition, tips or gratuities that are added to charges under club regulation are not considered to be from the sale of alcoholic beverages.
(b)
License and city approval required. Any bona fide private club that is nonprofit, in that the individual members do not receive any dividends or share in the profits, shall be licensed in order to make retail sales of spirituous liquors to its members. No license shall be issued unless the club is approved by the city for the issuance of the license.
(c)
Qualifications for licensure. To qualify for licensure, the bona fide private club must be a nonprofit corporation or association, organized under the laws of this state, that:
(1)
Has been in existence at least one year prior to the filing of its application for a license to be issued pursuant to this chapter;
(2)
Has at least 50 regular dues-paying members;
(3)
Owns, hires or leases a building or space within a building for the reasonable use of its members with suitable kitchen and dining room space and equipment and sufficient number of employees for cooking, preparing and serving meals for its members and guests;
(4)
Has no member, officer, agent or employee directly or indirectly receiving, in the form of salary or other compensation, any profit from the sale of alcoholic beverages, beyond a fixed salary.
(d)
Preexisting clubs. However, any private club previously holding any type of alcoholic beverage license issued by the city at the time of the effective date of the ordinance from which this section is derived shall not be required to meet all of the criteria set out for a bona fide private club.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-31(b)-(c))
State Law reference— Authority for local regulation and licensing of private clubs for sale of alcoholic beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-7-40.

Criminal convictions related to alcohol. To a person found by the city council to have been guilty of the violation of any federal, state or local law or regulation respecting the manufacture, possession or sale of any alcoholic beverage, or who has forfeited a bond to appear in court to answer charges for any such violation.

Other criminal convictions. Previously licensed persons. Unsuitable location. For a location:

Family relationship. Managers of former licensees. To a person who was a manager of a previous license holder, whose license was denied, revoked or suspended, if the person was the manager of the license holder at the time that any act occurred which formed the basis for the denial, revocation or suspension.

License previously denied. To a person who has previously had a license denied, revoked or suspended or was a partner, officer, director or stockholder of a previous license holder, whose license was denied, revoked or suspended.
(17)

To an owner, lessor or lessee of the premises where the business is to be located, where an owner, lessor or lessee of the premises or any partner, officer, stockholder or director of an owner, lessor or lessee of the premises where the business is to be located would not be eligible to receive a license hereunder for any reason other than nonresidency in this county.
(18)
Sanitary facilities. For premises not containing sanitary facilities as required by city code.
(19)
Prior application denied. To any person or entity for any location with respect to which a prior application has been submitted and denied, withdrawn or otherwise disposed of within the preceding 30 days.

Any applicant for a license to be issued pursuant to this article shall accompany the application with two complete sets of fingerprints of the applicant and applicant's manager, if the regular operations of the business will be conducted by one other than the applicant; if the applicant is a partnership, of all partners of the partnership; if the applicant is a corporation, of all officers and directors of the corporation. The foregoing provisions shall apply to transfers of licenses, to changes in partners, officers or directors of corporations. The fingerprints shall be taken by the police department on forms to be provided by the department and the applicant shall pay a fee in the amount established by the city council for each individual fingerprinted.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-34)
State Law reference— Fingerprints of applicants to be furnished, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2.

All applications for alcoholic beverage licenses required by this article shall name in the application one or more persons resident in the state and county as the agent and representative of the corporation to receive all communications, notices, service of process or other papers or documents on behalf of the corporation in connection with the issuance, holding, suspension, revocation or other action with respect to any such license. The application shall give the mailing address of the person and the mailing to any such person at the address of any notice required to be given under this article shall be sufficient notice to the corporation. If any such person shall cease to be a resident of the state and county, another person shall immediately be appointed in writing in such person's place, and written notice shall be given to the city stating the name and address of the new agent.

Sec. 10-40. - When applications due.
All applications for alcoholic beverage licenses shall be filed with the city clerk prior to December 1 of the particular year of filing. Applications for alcoholic beverage licenses submitted to the city clerk between December 1 and December 31 shall not be considered by the council until the first regularly scheduled meeting in the following January.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-36)
Sec. 10-41. - False statements.
The making of any untrue or misleading statement in the application for an alcoholic beverage license shall be sufficient cause of the refusal, suspension, revocation or cancellation of the license by the city council.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-37)
Sec. 10-42. - Notice for distilled spirits license.
No application for a distilled spirits license shall be acted upon by the city council until the applicant shall have run a notice of the application in a newspaper published in the county, at least ten days prior to the meeting of the city council at which the application is to be presented and considered. The notice shall contain the names of all persons named in the application, and the location of the proposed business. The notice shall not be required from an existing license holder making application for the renewal of an existing distilled spirits license at the same location.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-38)

The chief of police shall forward the application with the chief's recommendation to the city council. The city council shall approve or disapprove the issuance of the license. An investigation fee in the amount determined by the city council shall accompany the application; it shall not be refundable.

A manufacturer's license in any category issued under the provisions of this article will entitle the licensee to manufacture only the alcoholic beverage for which the license is issued, at one location only.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-45)
Sec. 10-49. - Wholesaler's activities restricted.
A wholesale dealer's license in any category issued under the provisions of this article will entitle the licensee to sell only the alcoholic beverage for which the license is issued, and only in the original unbroken container, to persons holding a retail license for the sale of that alcoholic beverage, for the purpose of resale only.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-46)
Sec. 10-50. - Package dealers.
modified
(a)
Permitted sales; unopened containers. A retail dealer (sale by package only) license in any category issued under the provisions of this article will entitle the licensee to sell only the alcoholic beverage for which the license is issued, at one location only, and only in the original and unopened container. Entrances and exits. No retailer's place of business shall have more than one entrance, which must open on a public street, and the place of business shall have no other entrance except as approved by state laws, and shall contain no vestibule, closet, booth, room or other place where persons may consume alcoholic beverages.

Vicinity to other businesses. The place of business operated under this license shall not be operated in conjunction with or open into or have access to or from any lunchroom, dining room, dance hall or store. A malt beverage retail dealer license (package and for consumption on premises) issued under the provisions of this article will entitle licensee to sell at one location malt beverages to consumers to take out or to consume on the premises.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-48)

A malt beverage retail dealer license (package only) issued under the provisions of this article will entitle the licensee to sell at one location malt beverages only in the unopened container, and will not authorize or permit the drinking of the contents on the premises where sold.

Sec. 10-53. - Transferability.
All alcoholic beverage licenses shall be personal to the licensee to whom issued. Upon proper application, the city council may permit the license to be transferred upon payment of a transfer fee equal to one-half the original license fee, provided that the transferee meets all of the qualifications which would have been required had the transferee been an applicant for an alcoholic beverage license.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-50)
Sec. 10-54. - Death of license holder.
Upon the death of any license holder, the person's personal representatives or heirs may continue to operate under the license for the balance of the year without the payment of any fee, if otherwise qualified as license holders, or the license may be transferred to a qualified transferee within 30 days after the date of death of the license holder upon payment of the transfer fee referenced in section 10-53.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-50)
Sec. 10-55. - Sale of interest by licensee.
Where the license holder is a partnership or corporation, one or more partners, or one or more stockholders, as the case may be, it or they may withdraw from the partnership or the corporation in favor of one or more of the partners or stockholders who were partners or stockholders at the time of the issuance of the license. The sale by a partner or a stockholder of the person's interest in the partnership or the corporation to a person who is not at the time of the sale already a partner or a stockholder shall constitute a ground for revocation of the license unless the new partner or stockholder is a person qualified to hold a license, and unless the transaction is approved by the city council.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-51)
Sec. 10-56. - Change of location.
The location named in the licensee's application shall not be changed without permission and approval of the city council upon written application made therefor. Approvals as to change of location shall be at the discretion of the city council.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-52)
Sec. 10-57. - Duration of license; fee refunds.
No license shall be issued under this article for less than a calendar year, or the remainder thereof. In case of the revocation, suspension or surrender of the license before the expiration of the calendar year, the holder thereof shall not be entitled to receive any refund.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-54)
Sec. 10-58. - Expiration of license.
All alcoholic beverage licenses shall expire at 12:00 a.m. on December 31 of the year in which issued. All applications for a renewal of the license for the ensuing year shall be treated as applications for new licenses, except that they need not be advertised as required by this article.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-55)

Additionally, it shall be the responsibility of licensee's agent or employee to see that all patrons are out of the premises within 30 minutes after the time prescribed for closing; provided, however, in the case of licensed restaurants, motels and bona fide private clubs, the establishments may be kept open for business after times provided in this section, but no mixed drinks, malt beverages or wine may be sold to the public after times stated herein.

Sec. 10-60. - Grounds for revocation.
The following shall be grounds for the suspension or revocation of an alcoholic beverage license. Whether the grounds exist shall be determined by the city council at its sole discretion.
(1)
The violation by the license holder or license holder's employee of any federal, state or local law or ordinance relating to the use and sale of drugs or alcoholic beverage or involving the use of firearms, theft, violence or a crime of moral turpitude. The determination of whether any such violation has occurred shall be made by the city council, and actual conviction in a court for the offense shall not be necessary in order to suspend or revoke the license.
(2)
The failure of the license holder or license holder's employee to report promptly to the police department any violation of law or municipal ordinance, breach of the peace, disturbance or altercation, occurring inside the premises, or the removal by the license holder or license holder's employees of any customer from inside the premises.
(3)
Any conduct on the part of the license holder or license holder's employee contrary to the public welfare, safety, health or morals of the citizens of the city.
(4)
Operating or conducting the business on the licensed premises in a manner contrary to the public welfare, safety, health or morals of the citizens of the city, or in the manner as to constitute a nuisance.
(5)
All licenses are issued subject to revocation or suspension if the fingerprint records accompanying the application reveal past criminal activity on the part of any applicant or other person whose fingerprints are required by this article to be furnished with the application.
(6)
Upon the business or the operation or location thereof, or the owner or any person connected therewith, ceasing to meet any of the qualifications for the issuance of a license as stated in this article.
(7)
The failure of the license holder or license holder's employees to keep the licensed premises free and clear of any disturbances of the peace, loud or boisterous conduct, lewd, obscene or immoral entertainment, violation of any federal, state or local law or ordinance relating to the use and sale of drugs or alcoholic beverages or involving the use of firearms, theft, violence or a crime of moral turpitude. The determination of whether the license holder or license holder's employee has violated this section shall be made by the city council; actual conviction in a court for any offense shall not be necessary in order to suspend or revoke the license.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-58)
Sec. 10-61. - Procedure for denial, suspension or revocation of license.
modified
(a)
Notice to applicant or license holder. After the city council shall deny any application for a license or for the transfer of any license, or shall suspend or revoke any existing license, the applicant or license holder, as the case may be, shall be given notice in writing with the reasons therefor stated.
(b)
Request for hearing. Any applicant or license holder aggrieved by the decision of the city council shall have the right to request, within ten days after receipt of notice, a hearing on the decision.
(c)
Scheduling and notice of hearing. Upon receipt by the city council of a request for the hearing, a hearing shall be scheduled within 20 working days and the applicant or license holder, as the case may be, shall be notified of the date, time and place of the hearing.
(d)
Conduct of hearing; evidence. At the hearing, the licensee or applicant shall have the right to appear in person and by attorney, and to have witnesses appear in the appellant's behalf, and to cross examine opposing witnesses. The witnesses shall testify under oath and technical rules of evidence shall not apply.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-59)

Sec. 10-87. - Penalties for late payment.
The failure to make a timely report and remittance shall render a licensee liable for a penalty equal to 20 percent of the total amount due during the first 30-day period following the date the report and remittance were due and a further penalty of ten percent of the amount of the remittance for each successive 30-day period or any portion thereof during which the report and remittance are not filed.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-16(d))
Sec. 10-88. - Penalty for false or fraudulent reports.
The filing of a false or fraudulent report shall render the licensee making the report liable for a penalty equal to 50 percent of the amount of the remittance that would be required under an accurate and truthful report.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-16(d))

ARTICLE IV. - PROHIBITED ACTS

Sec. 10-112. - Public nuisance; disturbing the peace.
It shall be unlawful for the holder of any license issued under the provisions of this chapter to permit any disturbance of the peace or obscenity or any lewd, immoral or improper entertainment, conduct or practices in the licensed premises or to operate the business carried on in the licensed premises in such a manner as to constitute a nuisance. It shall be unlawful for any licensee, or licensee's agent or employee to fail to report to the police department any disturbance that occurs immediately outside the licensed premises or in the parking facilities, of which any of them have knowledge.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-57(a)-(b))
Sec. 10-113. - Hiring persons with criminal convictions.
It shall be unlawful for any licensed establishment to employ any person who has been convicted of, entered a plea to, or has otherwise been found guilty of a felony, a misdemeanor involving moral turpitude or alcoholic beverage violation.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-57(c))
Sec. 10-114. - Failing to assist law enforcement officers.
It shall be unlawful for any licensee, or licensee's agent or employee to fail to assist any law enforcement officer in the performance of the person's duty while the officer is on a licensed premises.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-57(f))
Sec. 10-115. - Refusal to admit law enforcement officers.
It shall be unlawful to refuse to grant law enforcement officers admission to any licensed premises at any time upon the verbal request of any law enforcement officer who displays proper identification for the purpose of inspecting the premises to ensure compliance with this chapter. It shall be unlawful to refuse to open any cabinet, storage room or any other area within the licensed premises at the request of such officers.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-57(g))
Sec. 10-116. - Advertising restricted.
It shall be unlawful for the holder of any alcoholic beverage license to advertise in any news media, or by any other means, the fact that alcoholic beverages may be purchased at the establishment except as permitted by state law and by regulations duly promulgated thereunder.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-56)
Sec. 10-117. - Proximity to school or church.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or offer to sell any distilled spirits, wine or malt beverage within the prohibited distances to a church building, school building, educational building, school grounds or college campus as set out in the Georgia Alcoholic Beverage Code, O.C.G.A. § 3-1-1 et seq.
(Ord. of 7-10-1995, § 3-5)
