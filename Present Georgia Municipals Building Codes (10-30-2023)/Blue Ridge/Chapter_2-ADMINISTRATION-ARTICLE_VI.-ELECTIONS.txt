ARTICLE VI. - ELECTIONS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-438. - Adoption of state rules and regulations.
All matters relating to elections within the city shall be governed by the city Charter and state law.
(Code 1979, § 2-101; Code 2003, § 34.01)
Sec. 2-439. - Expenses.
Such funds as are necessary for the conduct of elections and for the performance of the duties that are specified by this article shall be budgeted and appropriated annually and from time to time.
(Code 1979, § 2-102; Code 2003, § 34.02)
Sec. 2-440. - Qualification fees.
The qualifying fee for the position of councilmembers posts 1 through 5 shall be as established by the mayor and city council from time to time and set out in the city fee schedule. The qualifying fee for the position of mayor shall be as established by the mayor and city council from time to time and set out in the city fee schedule. An appropriate notice shall be published as provided by law.
(Res. No. BR2021-01, 1-20-2021)
Secs. 2-441—2-461. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - REGISTRATION

Sec. 2-462. - Registrars and deputy registrars.
(a)
Appointment and duties.
(1)
The governing body of the city shall appoint three registrars, one of whom shall be designated as chief registrar and who shall be the chief administrative officer of the board. The registrars shall receive such compensation as provided by the governing body and shall exercise those powers and duties and shall be subject to such regulations as set forth in the Georgia Municipal Election Code (O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.) and any regulations established by the governing authority. All appointments shall be entered on the minutes of the council meeting at which they are made.
(2)
The registrar shall provide a list of electors to the chief manager of each election district for each election.
(3)
The registrar shall purge the list of electors as provided in O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.
(b)
Term. The registrars shall serve for a term of two years (at the pleasure of the governing authority) and until their successors are appointed and qualified, except in the event of resignation or removal as hereinafter provided. The governing authority shall have the right to remove one or more of such registrars at any time for cause after notice and hearing. Any registrar shall have the right to resign at any time by submitting a resignation to such governing authority. In the event of any such removal or resignation of a registrar, his duties and authority as such shall terminate instantly. In the case of the removal, death or resignation of a registrar, the governing authority shall appoint a successor who shall serve the unexpired term if such term is specified. Each such appointment or change in designation shall be entered on the minutes of the governing authority.
(c)
Oath. Prior to entering upon his duties, each registrar and deputy registrar shall swear to faithfully perform his duties, such oath to be administered by an officer authorized by law to administer the oath.
(d)
Qualifications. Registrars and deputy registrars shall be electors of the city and shall be able to read, write and speak the English language. The appointees, while serving as registrars or deputy registrars or within a period of six months after so serving, shall not be eligible to any nomination or office to be voted for at a primary or election.
(Code 1979, § 2-201; Code 2003, § 34.15)

DIVISION 3. - VOTING

Sec. 2-493. - Election officials.
The following offices are created in connection with the conduct of elections for the city:
(1)
Superintendent.
a.
The governing body of the city shall appoint a city election superintendent who shall receive such compensation as provided by the governing body of the city. The superintendent shall exercise those powers and duties as set forth in the Georgia Municipal Election Code (O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.).
b.
The election superintendent is authorized to incur expenses in the preparation and conduct of an election, including the cost of purchasing identification badges for poll officers, printing of ballots and voters certificates and purchasing of all other supplies necessary for the conduct of an election. The superintendent is further authorized to incur all necessary expenses to provide voting booths for the proper conduct of all elections.
(2)
Poll officers. All poll officers shall, before entering upon their duties at any primary or election, be duly sworn in before each other.
a.
Chief manager. A chief manager for each election district shall be appointed by the governing body of the city for each election. The chief manager shall receive such compensation as provided by the governing body and shall exercise those powers and duties as set forth under state law.
b.
Assistant manager. Two assistant managers for each election district shall be appointed by the governing body of the city for each election. The assistant managers shall receive such compensation as provided by the governing body and shall exercise those powers and duties as set forth under state law.
c.
Clerks. Prior to the opening of the polls in each district of each election, the governing body of the city shall appoint a sufficient number of clerks to serve at such election. The clerks shall receive such compensation as provided by the governing body and shall exercise those powers and duties as set forth under state law.
(Code 1979, § 2-401; Code 2003, § 34.50)
Sec. 2-494. - Election districts.
The area comprising the corporate limits of the city, as the same is now or shall hereafter exist, shall constitute the sole election district of the city.
(Code 1979, § 2-402; Code 2003, § 34.51)
Sec. 2-495. - Polling places.
Those persons living within the designated election district shall vote at the county courthouse. The superintendent shall cause all polling places to be provided with sufficient light and heat.
(Code 1979, § 2-403; Code 2003, § 34.52)
Sec. 2-496. - Absentee ballots.
Absentee ballots shall be used in all municipal elections, and such use shall be governed by the provisions of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated.
(Code 1979, § 2-406; Code 2003, § 34.55)
Sec. 2-497. - Vote required for election.
Candidates for nomination for any public municipal office in any primary and candidates for any public municipal office in any general or special election shall be nominated or elected by a plurality of the votes cast to fill such nomination or public office.
(Code 1979, § 2-407; Code 2003, § 34.56)
Sec. 2-498. - Ground for removal.
Shall the mayor or any member of the council fail or refuse to perform the duties of his office for the term of three consecutive months, the office may be declared vacant by the remaining members.
(Code 1979, § 2-IIB; Code 2003, § 34.59)
