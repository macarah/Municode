Chapter 30 - MANUFACTURED HOMES AND PREOWNED HOUSES[1]
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 30-1. - Permit required.
Prior to the location or relocation of any house or mobile home within the county, a building permit must be acquired from the county commissioners' office.
(Mo. of 2-2-1976)
Secs. 30-2—30-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - DECAL

Sec. 30-31. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any mobile home dealer or mover to locate or relocate a house or mobile home on any land in the county whose owner has not made application for a building permit from the county commissioners' office, as required in section 30-1, and obtained a decal from the county tax commissioners' office and have the decal affixed thereto.
(Mo. of 2-2-1976)
Sec. 30-32. - Application.
The application for a building permit, as required in section 30-1, shall be filed with the county commissioners' office and shall disclose the following information:
(1)
The name and address of the applicant.
(2)
The location and address of the house or mobile home upon which land such house or mobile home is to be placed.
(3)
The manufacturer, size, year, model and serial number of the mobile home to be located or relocated within the county.
(4)
Sewage certification with verification of an adequate sewage disposal system.
Sec. 30-33. - Fee.
The fee for a permit required by this article shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk.
(Mo. of 2-2-1976)
Sec. 30-34. - Issuance.
It shall be the duty of the county commissioners' office to issue to an applicant under this article a permit for the location or relocation of a house or mobile home.
(Mo. of 2-2-1976)
Sec. 30-35. - Renewal.
Renewal of decals under this article shall be granted by the tax commissioner each year thereafter, upon payment of taxes due, if applicable. In such cases where the mobile home is a homestead there shall be no fee.
Sec. 30-36. - Display.
All decals issued pursuant to this article shall be affixed on the mobile home in such manner as to be clearly visible from the front or from the street or roadway.
Sec. 30-37. - Utility connections.
It shall be unlawful for any electric, gas or water utility company to connect electricity, gas or water to any house or mobile home to be located in the county unless a building permit has been issued by the county commissioners' office, and a decal attached thereto.
Secs. 30-38—30-70. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - USED, PREOWNED HOMES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 30-71. - Inspection required; exception.
No used or preowned house, mobile home or manufactured home being moved into the county shall be allowed to locate for permanent or temporary occupancy in this county without an inspection if such house, mobile home or manufactured home is over 12 years old. However, mobile home or manufactured home that is located in the county as of February 10, 2003, shall not be affected by passage of the resolution from which this section was derived if such existing mobile home or manufactured home was manufactured prior to U.S. Housing and Urban Development Department construction standards.
(Res. of 2-9-2003)
Sec. 30-72. - Permit requirements.
No permit shall be issued under this division unless a septic tank permit has been obtained from the health department. All houses, mobile homes or manufactured homes being moved into the county shall conform to all permitting requirements. No house, mobile home or manufactured home permit shall be issued unless proof of payment of the current year's taxes is provided.
(Res. of 2-9-2003)
Sec. 30-73. - Inspections outside the county.
Mobile homes or manufactured homes 12 years old or older can be moved into the county only after passing an inspection on all requirements set out in this article. These inspections outside the county shall be:
(1)
Limited to a 100-mile radius of the county, with none accepted outside this radius;
(2)
Inspections to be conducted by the county building inspection department;
(3)
Scheduled by the building inspector;
(4)
Subject to the fees as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk.
(Res. of 2-9-2003)
Sec. 30-74. - Violations.
Any person violating the provisions of this article shall be brought before the magistrate court for violation of this article and, upon conviction thereof, shall be fined as provided in section 1-19. Such person shall be guilty of a separate offense for each and every day during which any violation of any provision of this article is committed, continued or permitted by that person and shall be punished by a fine per day as provided in section 1-19 for the violation.
(Res. of 2-9-2003)
Secs. 30-75—30-100. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - MINIMUM STANDARDS

Sec. 30-101. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Assignee means any bank financing institution or any person purchasing a retail installment contract that has as security a manufactured home.
Preowned manufactured home means any manufactured home as defined in O.C.G.A. § 8-2-131(3) that has been previously utilized as a residential dwelling.
Retailer is synonymous with dealer as defined in O.C.G.A. § 8-2-131(2).
(Res. of 2-9-2003)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-4.
Sec. 30-102. - Sanitary facilities.
Every preowned house or manufactured home shall contain not less than a kitchen sink, lavatory, tub or shower, and a water closet all in working condition when properly connected to an approved water and sewer system. Every plumbing fixture and water and waste pipe shall be in a sanitary, working condition free from leaks and obstructions.
(Res. of 2-9-2003)
Sec. 30-103. - Hot and cold water supply.
Every kitchen sink, lavatory and tub or shower in a preowned house or manufactured home must be connected to a supply of both cold and hot water.
(Res. of 2-9-2003)
Sec. 30-104. - Water heating facilities.
Every preowned house or manufactured home shall have water heating facilities in a safe, working condition.
(Res. of 2-9-2003)
Sec. 30-105. - Heating facilities.
(a)
Every preowned house or manufactured home shall have heating facilities in safe, working condition. Where a central heating system is not provided, each house or manufactured home shall be provided with facilities whereby heating appliances may be connected.
(b)
Unvented fuel-burning heaters shall be prohibited except for gas heaters listed for unvented use and the total input rating of the unvented heaters is less than 30 BTUs per hour per cubic foot of room content.
(c)
Unvented fuel-burning heaters shall be prohibited in bedrooms.
(d)
In lieu of inspecting the heating facilities of a preowned house or manufactured home, the retailer may request a heating inspection by a qualified third party. Approval by such person will be accepted as compliance with those portions of the safety standards established in this division which pertain to heating systems.
(Res. of 2-9-2003)
Sec. 30-106. - Smoke detector.
Every preowned house or manufactured home shall be provided with a state-approved smoke detector, installed in accordance with the manufacturer's recommendations and listing.
(Res. of 2-9-2003)
Sec. 30-107. - Windows.
Every habitable room in every preowned house or manufactured home, excluding bathrooms, kitchens and hallways, shall have at least one window that can be opened, facing directly to the outdoors. Glass must be in the windows.
(Res. of 2-9-2003)
Sec. 30-108. - Ventilation.
Every habitable room in every preowned house or manufactured home shall have at least one window or skylight which can be opened, or such other device that will ventilate the room.
(Res. of 2-9-2003)
Sec. 30-109. - Electrical.
(a)
Distribution panels. Distribution panels in every preowned house or manufactured home shall be in compliance with the approved listing, complete with required breakers or fuses, with all unused openings covered with black covers approved and listed for that purpose.
(b)
Electrical system. The electrical system (switches, receptacles, fixtures, etc.) shall be properly installed and wired and shall be in a working condition. The preowned house or manufactured home shall be subject to an electrical continuity test to ensure that all metallic parts are properly bonded.
(c)
Third party inspection. The retailer may, in lieu of inspecting the electrical and heating systems of a preowned house or manufactured home, request an electrical inspection by a qualified third party. Approval by such a person will be accepted as compliance with those portions of the safety standards established in this division which pertain to electrical systems.
(Res. of 2-9-2003)
Sec. 30-110. - Exterior walls.
The exterior of the preowned house or manufactured home shall be free of loose or rotting boards or timbers and any other conditions that might admit rain or moisture to the interior portions of the walls or to the occupied spaces of the preowned house or manufactured home.
(Res. of 2-9-2003)
Sec. 30-111. - Exterior siding.
The exterior siding of the preowned house or manufactured home shall be free of rot and rust and must be uniform in appearance.
(Res. of 2-9-2003)
Sec. 30-112. - Roofs.
Roofs of every preowned house or manufactured home shall be structurally sound and have no obvious defects, which might admit rain or cause moisture to collect on the interior portion of the home.
(Res. of 2-9-2003)
Sec. 30-113. - Interior floors, walls and ceiling.
Every floor, interior wall and ceiling of every preowned house or manufactured home shall be kept in sound condition to prevent the admittance of rain or moisture.
(Res. of 2-9-2003)
Sec. 30-114. - Exception for homes purchased for repair.
(a)
If a preowned house or manufactured home is to be sold as is for purposes of repair prior to occupancy, the sales agreement and/or contract shall specifically state that the purchaser understands that the home is not habitable in its present condition and that the purchaser must bring the home into compliance with the specified standards.
(b)
No home may be placed for occupancy until the standards in this division are met.
(Res. of 2-9-2003)
