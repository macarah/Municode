Chapter 3 - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES[1]
ARTICLE I. - GENERAL PROVISIONS

Sec. 3-1. - Definitions.
In this chapter, in addition to the definitions provided in specific articles hereof in this Code, the following words shall have the indicated meanings:
(1)
Alcoholic beverage means and includes all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, malt beverage, wine or fortified wine.
(2)
Building code means and include all building, plumbing and electrical codes and any other similar technical code of the city.
(3)
Church means any permanent building where persons regularly assemble for religious worship and shall not include any church land or property not regularly used as an assembly area for religious worship services.
(4)
College means those state, county, city, church or other colleges that teach the subjects commonly taught in the common colleges of this state.
(5)
Distance shall be measured from the property line of the tract on which is located the business regulated under this chapter to the property line of the tract on which is located the church, residence, school, corporate boundaries etc.
(6)
Distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume, including, but not limited to, all fortified wines.
(7)
Election day shall be deemed to refer to any day upon which any municipal, county, statewide, or national primary, special, or general election is held and to any day upon which any runoff is held for such election.
(8)
Liquor store or retail liquor means any establishment that is licensed for the retail sale of alcoholic beverages.
(9)
License year means that 12-month period for and with respect to which licenses under this chapter shall be issued; such license year shall begin on the first day of January of each year and end on the 31st day of December next following.
(10)
Malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than six percent alcohol by volume and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer, and strong beer. The term does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
(11)
Premises means the definite closed or partitioned-in locality, whether room, shop or building, wherein alcoholic beverages are sold or consumed. Premises also include any area or patio immediately adjacent to the main licensed facility and located on property owned or leased by such licensee. The area or patio need not be covered.
(12)
Private club means a corporation or association organized and existing under the laws of the state, actively in operation within the city immediately prior to the application for a license hereunder, having at least 25 members regularly paying dues, organized and operated exclusively for pleasure, recreation and other nonprofitable purposes, no part of the net earnings of which inure to the benefit of any shareholder or member and owning, hiring or leasing a building or space therein for the reasonable use of its members with suitable kitchen and dining room space and equipment and maintaining and using a sufficient number of servants and employees for cooking, preparing and serving meals for its members and guests; provided that no member or officer, agent or employee, is paid or directly or indirectly receives in the form of salary or other compensation any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages beyond the amount of such salary as may be fixed by its members at an annual meeting, or by its governing body, out of the general revenue of the club. For the purpose of this subsection, tips which are added to the bill under club regulations shall not be considered as profits hereunder. A private club organized or operated primarily for the selling or serving of alcoholic beverages by the drink shall not be licensed under this article nor permitted to sell or serve such beverages at all.
(13)
Private residence means a house or dwelling located in any residential zoning classification including all single-family or multi-family zoning classifications under zoning ordinances of the City of Lake Park.
(14)
Retail means retail sales packaged to go and not for consumption on the premises.
(15)
School means those state, county, city, church or other schools that teach the subjects commonly taught in the common schools of this state and shall include the adjacent open land used for school purposes.
(16)
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term includes, but is not limited to all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products. The term does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at the point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this subsection.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-2. - License required; scope of chapter.
(a)
No malt beverage, wines, distilled spirits or any other alcoholic beverage shall be stored, delivered, sold or manufactured in the city except under a license issued pursuant to this chapter and then only for the specific beverage and manner of sale provided in the license.
(b)
The requirements of this chapter shall be in addition to any other requirements for business licenses under this Code; and if other provisions of this Code conflict with this chapter, then this chapter shall control.
(c)
Licenses which may be issued under this chapter include:
(1)
Retail:
a.
Malt beverages;
b.
Wine;
c.
Distilled spirits;
(2)
Consumption on the premises:
a.
Malt beverages;
b.
Wine;
c.
Distilled spirits;
d.
Sunday sales; or
(3)
Any combination of the above at the same location provided no retail or wholesale licensee shall hold any consumption-on-the-premises license for the same location.
(d)
Licenses hereunder shall be mere grants or privileges, and the City Council of Lake Park shall have the discretion to approve, or deny any application for a license to sell alcoholic beverages by the drink, or to revoke the same.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-3. - Time limits on opening and continuing business.
(a)
All licenses issued under this chapter must, within 90 days after the issuance of a city license, open for business. Failure to open for business shall constitute a forfeiture and cancellation of the issued licenses and no refund of license fees or business taxes shall be made. Any applicant unable to comply with the time limit of this section may make written request to the city council for an extension of time not to exceed 90 days; and the city council, at its discretion, may grant or deny the request.
(b)
Any license issued under this chapter shall automatically be null and void where the licensed business has not operated or been open to the public for six consecutive months. Said license shall automatically become null and void and be of no further force or effect without the necessity of any action of the clerk, city manager or city council.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-4. - Prohibited interests.
(a)
No person who has any direct financial interest in a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages at wholesale shall hold any other license or an interest in any other license under the terms of this chapter.
(b)
No person, related within the first degree of consanguinity or affinity under civil law to any wholesale licensee or employee thereof or any person with any interest therein shall hold any other license or an interest in any other license issued under this chapter.
(c)
No elected or full-time appointed official of the city or any person related within the first degree of consanguinity or affinity under civil law to such an official of the city shall own any interest in any license issued under this chapter unless the interest was obtained prior to election or appointment to office.
(d)
No applicant herein, nor any member of the immediate family of any applicant herein, shall own, control, or have a financial interest in more than two liquor stores, liquor store licenses, or liquor businesses in Lowndes County, Georgia or in any other county adjoining Lowndes County, and shall not have any interest in any liquor distillery, or wholesale liquor distributorship or business anywhere.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-5. - Applications for new license.
(a)
The provisions of this Code on general business licensing shall apply in addition to the following:
(1)
All applications for new licenses under this chapter shall be accompanied by a payment in the form of certified funds, for all the minimum fees and business taxes provided for in this Code for the classification into which the applicant falls. If the initial alcoholic beverage license applications are filed after January 1, the applicable licensing fee shall be prorated for the remainder of the initial year.
(2)
The city application shall be a photostatic or other copy of all the appropriate state applications and forms along with a city supplemental application form provided by the city along with the following:
a.
A legal description of the property upon which the premises are located;
b.
A current photograph of the applicant;
c.
Fingerprints of the applicant to be taken by the City of Lake Park Police Department;
d.
A signed consent on forms prescribed by the Georgia Crime Information Center authorizing the release of the applicant's criminal records to the City of Lake Park.
e.
A sketch or plat of the proposed location showing the surrounding area for 500 feet and noting the location of any church, school, public library or child care facility or residence within 500 feet of the proposed location.
(b)
The application shall also contain a form of oath providing that the information disclosed in the application is true and correct and that the proposed location meets all the standards necessary for issuance of a similar license by the state and providing further that the applicant will abide by, observe and conduct his or her business according to the rules and regulations prescribed by the city, Georgia Alcoholic Beverage Code, OCGA § 3-1-1 et seq., as now or hereafter amended, and the rules and regulations of the state department of revenue in respect thereto. The oath shall be taken by the applicant and the agent in charge of the establishment if different from or additional to the applicant, and the oath shall be taken by the true owners.
(c)
Sunday sales license. The application for a Sunday alcohol sales license shall be filed on a form provided by the city and shall be accompanied by a letter from a certified public accountant certifying that the financial records, including tax returns, daily sales receipts and sales records have been audited by the accountant and that the applicant derives at least 50 percent of the establishment's total annual gross sales from the sale of prepared meals or food in all of the combined retail outlets of the individual establishment where food is served or, if applicable, derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross income from the rental of rooms for overnight lodging.
(d)
Any false statement or material misrepresentation in any application hereunder shall be grounds for denial of the application and where a license has been issued, the revocation of any license granted hereunder.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-6. - Residency required; agents.
All applications for licenses under this chapter by any person, firm or corporation shall name in the application one or more residents of the State of Georgia as the agent and representative of the person, firm or corporation to receive all communications, notices, service of process or other papers or documents, on behalf of the corporation in connection with any matter arising out of or connected with the issuance, holding, suspension, revocation or other action with respect to any city license. The application shall give the mailing address of the person or persons, and the mailing to any person at that address of any notice required to be given under this chapter or any other law shall be sufficient notice to the corporation. If any person shall cease to be a resident of the State of Georgia, another person shall immediately be appointed in writing in his or her place, and written notice shall be given the city clerk stating the name and address of the new agent within 30 days.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-7. - Preliminary approval required; taxes; police.
(a)
The clerk shall cause an inquiry to be made into the city tax records to determine if the applicant or other parties with interest in the application has any outstanding taxes or special assessments that are delinquent against his or her property or any other monies owing to the city. No license shall be issued or renewed until such debts are paid in full.
(b)
All applications shall be approved or disapproved by the city council or a designee as a condition to action thereon and issuance of a license by the city clerk. In the event that the city council determines that the application is not in order, in view of the requirements of federal or state law or this Code and any police department investigation, the city council or its designee shall then notify the applicant of the reasons for not issuing the license to the applicant.
(c)
All applicants shall furnish all reasonable data, information and records requested of them by the city council, city clerk or police department and failure to furnish such data, information, contracts, documents, financial statements and other records within 30 days of the request shall automatically serve to dismiss the application.
(d)
The city council or designee may require in addition to the licensee, fingerprinting and background investigation of any person interested in the application, including any person with a financial interest, such as stockholders, partners or employees, and any spouse, brother, child or parent of any person with a financial interest. Should the applicant fail to produce such persons within 30 days of the request, the application may be denied by the city council.
(e)
Applicants by filing an application agree to produce for oral interrogation any person or persons requested by the police department and considered as being important in the ascertainment of the facts relative to such license. The failure to produce those persons within 30 days after being requested to do so may result in a denial of the application.
(f)
The city may require 60 days for processing any application for a license required under this chapter. For investigative purposes, the city council may extend the 60 days processing period according to the licensee's compliance should the chief of police deem it necessary.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-8. - Notice of application to be published; signs.
(a)
At least once a week for two weeks immediately preceding the hearing before council on any license application under this chapter, the applicant, at its sole expense shall cause advertisements to be placed in any newspaper used for sheriff's advertisements in this county. The advertisements shall be in a form prescribed by the city council or city clerk and shall include the name and address of the applicant, type of license applied for, business location applied for, and the time, date and location of the council meeting for which the appeal of the application shall be heard.
(b)
The applicant shall also cause to be placed upon the location of the proposed premises to be licensed, signs of a size, number and form prescribed by the clerk stating the time, date and location of the council meeting for which the appeal of the application shall be heard.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-9. - Completion of premises proposed to be licensed.
Where a building in which any person intends to operate under the provisions of this chapter is, at the time of the application for the license, not in existence or not yet completed, a license may be issued for the location provided the plans for the proposed building show clearly a compliance with the other provisions of this chapter and all applicable building and zoning codes and regulations. No sales or consumption shall be allowed in the establishment until it has been completed in accordance with the plans and is in conformity with all of the other provisions of this chapter and the City Code.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-10. - Issuance of license to persons with prior convictions prohibited.
(a)
No license under this chapter shall be issued, renewed or transferred to any person, partnership or corporation where any individual having an interest either as owner, partners, stockholder, directly or indirectly, beneficial or absolute, shall have been convicted within five years immediately prior to the filing of the application for any felony or convicted within two years immediately prior to the filing of the application for any misdemeanor relating to any alcoholic beverage business or any state, county or municipal ordinance violation relating to any alcoholic beverage business.
(b)
The term "conviction" shall include an adjudication of guilty, a plea of guilty or nolo contendere or the forfeiture of a bond in part or in whole when charged with a crime or the suspension or revocation of any alcoholic beverage license owned in whole or in part by persons listed in subsection (a) above.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-11. - Denial of license application; granting; appeal.
(a)
The city council shall deny a city license under this chapter on any of the following grounds:
(1)
Failure to meet state requirements for state license;
(2)
Failure to pay required fees and taxes;
(3)
Failure to provide valid information, documents and the like required by this chapter;
(4)
False information in the application or attached documents;
(5)
Improper residency of applicant, owner, or registered agents;
(6)
Failure to pass review by the city council or designee;
(7)
Failure to be in compliance with zoning laws. Compliance will be determined by the zoning administrator and city council. The city council's and zoning administrator's determination will be delivered in writing to the applicant and the licensing administrator;
(8)
Failure to meet the distance requirements as set forth in Code section 3-21 of this chapter;
(9)
Prior convictions as herein provided; or
(10)
Failure to meet any other requirements in this chapter for a license of the class applied for.
(11)
Previous revocation of license. If the applicant is person or entity whose alcohol license, issued under the police power of any county or municipality in Georgia, has been previously revoked for violation of any ordinance, statute or regulation with the exception of distance violations or failure to renew.
(b)
An application which has been denied shall not be resubmitted for twelve months after the denial unless there is a substantial change therein which would affect the reason for denial originally.
(c)
The clerk shall issue the license once it is determined that the property and applicant has met all of the requirements of this chapter. Any applicant that is denied a license by the clerk for any reason under this chapter will be notified in writing as required by state law.
(d)
Any license that is denied by the city council for any reason under this section or failure to comply with any other section in this chapter may at the request of the applicant be appealed to the city council and, upon hearing, the determination of whether to uphold the denial or grant the license shall be made by said city council. Said hearing shall be held in accordance with Code section 3-28 of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-12. - State regulations.
All licenses issued under this chapter shall be subject to the rules and regulations set forth in OCGA Title 3, as now or hereafter amended, and those prescribed by the state department of revenue and violation of these statutes and regulations shall be violations of this Code. In addition, all businesses, the applicant for the license, the agent in charge of the business and the owner and owners thereof shall observe and obey the regulations provided in this article and any other applicable provisions of this chapter.
State Law reference— Alcoholic beverages, Ga. Code Ann., Tit. 3.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-13. - Open to inspection.
Any establishment holding a license issued under this chapter shall at all times, during the period allowable by law for operation of the business, be made available and open to inspection without a warrant by any license inspector of the city or to any person designated by the city council. In addition, if the premises are being used after hours by employees or the owners or their agents, the premises may be inspected at this time by the designated persons in this section.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-14. - Open containers prohibited; exception.
No bottle or other container of alcoholic beverages shall be opened or consumed by any person on the premises or in any adjacent parking area, upon which the place of business is conducted and licensed under this chapter, whether the bottle or other container so opened or consumed was bought or obtained at that place of business or elsewhere unless the premises is licensed for consumption on the premises under this chapter.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-15. - Transfer of license.
(a)
Scope of section. Licenses hereunder shall not be transferable, except as otherwise provided herein. All transfers will require the applicant to pay the same fee as new applicants and go through the same process as a new applicant.
(b)
Death of licensee. In case of the death of any person holding such license, or any interest therein, the same may, in the discretion of the City Council of Lake Park, be transferred to the administrator, executor or the lawful heirs of the deceased person.
(c)
Withdrawal of partner. Nothing in this section, however, shall prohibit one or more of the partners in a partnership holding a license from withdrawing from the partnership in favor of one or more of the partners who were partners at the time of the issuance of such license. Such a withdrawal shall not, however, serve to bring any new ownership into the partnership.
(d)
New ownership. New ownership of an existing business will occur when an existing business is purchased or transferred in its entirety to a new person, persons or entity. The new owner must follow the same procedures as a new applicant.
(e)
Addition of partners, stockholders. However, a licensee may take in partners or additional stockholders where it is determined by the city that the additional capital furnished is to be used exclusively for additional inventory or expanding the facilities of the business, or for building new facilities and where it appears that neither the licensee himself, nor any partner or stockholder therein receives directly or indirectly any of the additional capital invested. Under this section an additional partner or new principal stockholder must be approved by the city council.
(f)
Transfer from agent to agent; name change. Where licenses are held by agents, upon the resignation or removal of an agent holding the liquor license for a particular location, the partners, owners or stockholders of the business entity shall immediately appoint a new agent to hold said liquor license, which agent shall apply to the city council for the reissuance of the liquor license for the stated location in his name. Upon completion of the police background check and investigation as required herein, the police department shall make a recommendation to the city council and the licensing administrator for the approval or disapproval of the transfer of agent. In such a transfer, it will be required that a new investigation fee be paid for the investigation of the new agent but no new fees for the issuance of the liquor license will be collected until the normal expiration date of the existing license. In those instances in which only the name of the establishment holding a liquor license is to change, with no change in the ownership or agent holding the license, the partners, owners or stockholders shall notify the city council of the change of name, which notation shall be made on the records of the city clerk and forwarded to the license inspector's office for a change on the records of that office. No charge will be made for this name change.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-16. - Legal hours of sale; separate from package sale rooms; adequate seating.
(a)
Sales by the drink. No licensee shall sell, give away or otherwise dispense distilled spirits, wines, champagnes or malt beverages or other alcoholic beverages by the drink for consumption on the premises except between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 2:00 a.m., Monday through Saturday, and between the hours of 12:30 p.m. to 12:00 midnight on Sunday, according to standard time in effect at the time the sale is made. To clarify, Saturday sales by the drink shall be allowed to continue until 2:00 a.m. Sunday morning. Sunday alcohol sales of alcoholic beverages by the drink for consumption on the premises shall be permitted only in licensed liquor or beer and wine by the drink establishments which: (1) obtain a Sunday sales license; and (2) derive at least 50 percent of their annual gross sales from the sale of prepared meals or food in all of the combined retail outlets of the individual establishments where food is served and in any licensed establishment which derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross income from the rental of rooms for overnight lodging. Alcoholic beverages shall not be sold or dispensed in the same room used for the sale of packaged alcoholic beverages except where adequate seating arrangements are provided for the licensee's patrons.
(b)
Package sales. Package sales of distilled spirits, malt beverages, wine and other alcoholic beverages by licensees shall be permitted between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 12:00 midnight, Monday through Saturday, and between the hours of 12:30 p.m. to 11:30 p.m. on Sunday, according to standard time in effect at the time the sale is made. Sunday package sales of alcoholic beverages shall be permitted only in licensed package sale establishments which obtain a separate Sunday sales license.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011; Ord. No. 104 Amd 1, § I, 5-5-2015)
Sec. 3-17. - Reserved.
Sec. 3-18. - Minors; prohibited practices.
(a)
Except as otherwise authorized by law:
(1)
No person knowingly, directly or through another person, shall furnish, cause to be furnished, or permit any person in such person's employ to furnish any alcoholic beverage to any person under 21 years of age;
(2)
No person under 21 years of age shall purchase, attempt to purchase, or knowingly possess any alcoholic beverage;
(3)
No person under 21 years of age shall misrepresent such person's age in any manner whatever for the purpose of obtaining illegally any alcoholic beverage;
(4)
No person knowingly or intentionally shall act as an agent to purchase or acquire any alcoholic beverage for or on behalf of a person under 21 years of age; or
(5)
No person under 21 years of age shall misrepresent his or her identity or use any false identification for the purpose of purchasing or obtaining any alcoholic beverage.
(b)
The prohibitions contained in paragraphs (1), (2), and (4) of subsection (a) of this Code section shall not apply with respect to the sale, purchase, or possession of alcoholic beverages for consumption:
(1)
For medical purposes pursuant to a prescription of a physician duly authorized to practice medicine in this state; or
(2)
At a religious ceremony.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person, licensee, or employee or agent to sell, furnish or serve any alcoholic beverage to any person without first requesting and being furnished with proper evidence of age and identification. Proper evidence of age and identification shall mean any document properly issued by a governmental agency containing a description of such person, such person's photograph, or both, and giving such person's name, and date of birth, a passport, valid driver's license, military identification card, or an identification card authorized under OCGA §§ 40-5-100 through 40-5-104 shall be deemed sufficient evidence. Traffic citations and complaint forms and birth certificates shall not be deemed sufficient evidence.
(d)
The prohibition contained in paragraph (1) of subsection (a) of this Code section shall not apply with respect to sale of alcoholic beverages by a person when such person has been furnished with proper identification showing that the person to whom the alcoholic beverage is sold is 21 years of age or older. For purposes of this subsection, the term "proper identification" means any document issued by a governmental agency containing a description of the person, such person's photograph, or both, and giving such person's date of birth and includes, without being limited to, a passport, military identification card, driver's license, or an identification card authorized under OCGA §§ 40-5-100 through 40-5-104, as amended. "Proper identification" shall not include a birth certificate and shall not include any traffic citation and complaint form.
(e)
If such conduct is not otherwise prohibited pursuant to subsection (f) nothing contained in this Code section shall be construed to prohibit any person under 21 years of age from:
(1)
Dispensing, serving, selling or handling alcoholic beverages as a part of employment in any licensed establishment;
(2)
Being employed in any establishment in which alcoholic beverages are distilled or manufactured; or
(3)
Taking orders for and having possession of alcoholic beverages as a part of employment in a licensed establishment.
(f)
No person shall allow or require a person in his employment under 18 years of age to dispense, serve, sell, or take orders for any alcoholic beverages. This subsection shall not prohibit persons under 18 years of age who are employed in supermarkets, convenience stores, breweries or drugstores from selling or handling alcoholic beverages which are sold for consumption off the premises.
(g)
Testimony by any person under 21 years of age, when given in an administrative or judicial proceeding against another person for violation of any provision of this Code section, shall not be used in any administrative or judicial proceedings brought against such testifying person less than 21 years of age.
(h)
In any case where a reasonable or prudent person could reasonably be in doubt as to whether or not the person to whom an alcoholic beverage is to be sold or otherwise furnished is actually 21 years of age or older, it shall be the duty of the person selling or otherwise furnishing, such alcoholic beverage to request to see and to be furnished with proper identification as provided for in subsection (d) of this section in order to verify the age of such person; and the failure to make such request and verification in any case where, the person to whom the alcoholic beverage is sold or otherwise furnished is less than 21 years of age may be considered by the trier of fact in determining whether the person selling or otherwise furnishing such alcoholic beverage did so knowingly.
(i)
Any retailer or retail consumption dealer, or any person acting on behalf of such retailer or retail consumption dealer, who upon requesting proper identification from a person attempting to purchase alcoholic beverages from such retailer or retail consumption dealer pursuant to subsection (h) of this section is tendered a driver's license which indicates that such driver's license is falsified, is not the driver's license of the person presenting it, or that such person is under the age of 21 years, the person to whom said license is tendered shall be authorized to either write down the name, address, and license number or to seize and retain such driver's license and in either event shall immediately thereafter summon a law enforcement officer who shall be authorized to seize the license either at the scene or at such time as the license can be located. The procedures and rules connected with the retention of such license by the officer shall be the same as those provided for the acceptance of a driver's license as bail on arrest for traffic offenses pursuant to OCGA § 17-6-11.
(j)
Violation of any provision of this section shall constitute an offense hereunder and shall be punishable in the municipal court; provided, violation of this section by a child shall be handled as provided by OCGA § 15-1-1 et seq.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)

Sec. 3-20. - Duration and renewal of license.
(a)
Generally. Except as otherwise provided, duration and renewal of licenses under this chapter shall be as for business licenses generally; and no licensee shall have any vested right to the renewal of any city license. No licenses under this chapter may be renewed if the licensee could be denied a new license under this chapter. In the case of the revocation or surrender of such license before the expiration of such license year, the holder thereof shall not be entitled to receive any refund whatsoever.
(b)
All annual licenses renewal fees shall be paid on or before December 31 of each year or be charged a late penalty as set out hereafter. All fees will be accompanied by an application form as prescribed by the City Council of Lake Park which will be reviewed by the city clerk for any new (within the last year) infractions that would cast doubt as to whether the applicant should hold a license. Any licensee filing an application for renewal from January 1 until January 31 shall be charged a late penalty of 25 percent of the license fee to be paid in addition to the regular renewal fee. Any licensee filing an application for renewal from February 1 until February 28 shall be charged a late penalty of 50 percent of the license fee to be paid in addition to the regular renewal fee. An application for renewal filed after February 28 shall be treated as an original application must comply with all rules and regulation then in existence for new licenses.
(c)
Expiration; renewal of distilled spirits licenses. Applicants in any given year for renewal of their licenses hereunder for the sale of distilled spirits for the next year shall file their application on or before the tenth day of December with the license inspector for the City of Lake Park. Upon payment of such fees and license taxes as are applicable on or before the 31st day of December, to the city, the license inspector shall issue the renewal license for the applicant. All licenses shall expire on the 31st day of December of the year for which they are issued, except when extended as provided in this chapter.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-21. - Distance and area requirements.
(a)
No person shall be licensed by the city under this chapter for the retail sale of distilled spirits, malt beverages or wine where the parcel is located within 500 feet of any parcel upon which is located any of the following:
(1)
Any public or private school;
(2)
Any public library;
(3)
Any church or similar place of worship;
(4)
Any child care or day care facility;
(5)
Any residence.
The distance will be measured as provided in section 3-1(5) of this chapter.
(b)
No license shall be issued by the city under this chapter for consumption on the premises of distilled spirits, malt beverages, wine where the use of the property is a private club, as defined in section 3-101(5) or a supper club as defined in section 3-101(7) or a lounge as defined in section 3-101(3) and either use is located on a parcel within 300 feet of any parcel upon which is located any of the following:
(1)
Any public or private school;
(2)
Any public library;
(3)
Any church or similar place of worship;
(4)
Any child care or day care facility;
(5)
Any residence.
The distance will be measured as provided in section 3-1(5) of this chapter.
(c)
Except as is herein provided, no license shall be issued by the city under this chapter for consumption on the premises of distilled spirits, malt beverages or wine where the use of the property is a restaurant as a defined in section 3-101(6) or a hotel as defined in section 3-101(1) and either use is located on a parcel within 100 feet of a parcel upon which is located any of the following:
(1)
Any public or private school;
(2)
Any public library;

Sec. 3-22. - Penalties; revocation of license.
Any license under this chapter shall be subject to penalty and revocation for any conviction of the licensee or any agent, employee or officer thereof for violation of this chapter or any state law or regulation involving alcoholic beverages or sale of any illegal substance or article under the following procedure:
(1)
Upon the conviction or entry of a plea of guilty or nolo contendere of a licensee or any agent, employee, or licensee thereof for a violation described in this section, the city clerk shall propose a penalty to be assessed against the licensee according to the following schedule:
a.
For the first violation within the preceding 24 months, a fine of up to $5,000.00 and suspension of the license for up to seven days, or revocation.
b.
For the second violation within the preceding 24 months, a fine of up to $10,000.00 and suspension of the license for up to 30 days, or revocation.
c.
For the third violation within the preceding 24 months, a fine of up to $20,000.00 and/or revocation of the license.
(2)
The city clerk shall cause to be served upon the licensee or any license agent thereof a written notice of the violation described in this section and the penalty proposed therefor. The licensee shall have five days from the date of service to request a hearing before the city council. Upon receipt of a request for a hearing, the city council shall provide by certified mail a notice to the licensee of the date, time and place of hearing, which date shall be not less than five days subsequent the date of such notice. At the hearing the licensee may present evidence and cross-examine opposing witnesses.
(3)
Within five days after the hearing, or, if no hearing has been requested, after review of the proposed penalty, the city council shall issue a written decision either affirming, modifying or denying the proposed penalty. A copy of the written decision shall be provided to the licensee by certified mail and shall state the date and time of the next regular meeting of the city council at which the decision of the city council shall be presented for confirmation.
(4)
The city council shall by resolution confirm, modify or deny the decision of the city clerk, or shall remand the action to the city clerk for further proceedings as may be directed. Following confirmation by the city council, the city clerk may cause the penalty to be imposed.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)

ARTICLE V. - LOUNGES, HOTELS AND RESTAURANTS

Sec. 3-101. - Definitions.
For the purposes of this article only the following terms shall have the meanings respectively ascribed to them, to wit:
(1)
Hotel shall mean every building or other structure kept, used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public to be a place where food is actually served and consumed and sleeping accommodations are offered for adequate pay to travelers and guests, whether transient, permanent or residential, in which 50 or more rooms are used for the sleeping accommodations of such guests and having one or more public dining rooms, where meals are regularly served to such guests, such sleeping accommodations and dining rooms being conducted in the same building or in separate buildings or structures used in connection therewith that are on the same premises and are a part of the hotel operation. Motels meeting the qualifications set out herein for hotel shall be classified in the same category as hotels. Hotels shall have the privilege of granting franchises for the operation of a lounge, restaurant or supper club in their premises and the holder of such franchise shall be included in the definition of hotel hereunder. The public dining room connected herewith shall not be confused or defined with the definition of separate lounges, restaurants, private clubs or supper clubs as may be contained further herein, but in this instance, must be a dining room conducted in the same building or in separate buildings or structures used in connection therewith that are on the same premises and are a part of the hotel operation. The seating capacity for these hotel-connected facilities shall not be fixed as may be contained in further definitions herein. Further, the fee for the operation of any sale of alcoholic beverages shall be that fee as contained in section 3-146 of this chapter, and as relates to restaurants in connection with hotels, shall be determined by the seating capacity of 149 and below and 150 and above for the fee as designated in said section 3-146.
(2)
Licensee shall mean a person, firm, corporation, association or establishment holding a license to engage in the retail sale of any and all alcoholic beverages by the drink for consumption on the licensed premises, and the annual license fees prescribed by this chapter includes any and all of such beverages sold by the drink by such licensee for consumption on the licensed premises.
(3)
Lounge shall mean a separate room connected with, a part of, and adjacent to a restaurant or located in hotels or motels as defined herein, with all booths, stools and tables being open and unobstructed to the view of any other customers of, and in such lounge or the manager thereof. All lounges shall be air conditioned, and having a seating capacity of at least 50. A lounge, as defined herein, which is operated on a different floor in the premises, or in a separate building, or is not connected to or adjacent to a restaurant, shall be considered a separate establishment and shall pay an additional license fee therefor.
(4)
Premises shall mean the lot and building thereon, closed or partitioned-in locality, whether room, shop or building wherein alcoholic beverages are sold by the drink, except in case of a hotel or motel premises.
(5)
Private club shall be defined as contained in the definition under section 3-1, specifically subparagraph (10), as contained in this chapter.
(6)
Restaurant shall mean any public place kept, used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public as a place where meals are served and where meals are actually and regularly served, without sleeping accommodations, such place being provided with adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining room equipment and seating capacity of at least 50 people, having employed therein a sufficient number and kind of employees to prepare, cook and serve suitable meals for consumption at tables in a dining room or dining rooms on the premises. Dining room(s) shall be staffed by a full service wait staff that will be responsible for the taking of the order at the table, delivering the order to the table, clearing the table and cashing out the bill. At least one meal per day shall be served at least six days a week, with the exception of holidays, vacations and periods of redecorating, and the serving of such meals shall be the principal business conducted, with the serving distilled spirits to be consumed on the premises as only incidental thereto. Said business must derive at least 50 percent of its total annual gross income from food and non-alcoholic beverage sales.
(7)
Supper club shall mean those establishments having a seating capacity of at least 150 persons with all booths and tables being open to unobstructed view, and providing a band and/or other professional entertainment, a minimum of 20 days in each calendar month, each and every calendar month of a calendar year, with the exception of holidays, vacations and periods of redecorating. All such supper clubs shall be equipped with air conditioning, and shall maintain an adequate kitchen with a sufficient number of servants and employees for cooking, preparing, and serving food and meals for their patrons, and shall regularly serve at least one meal each day for six days each calendar week; provided, that supper clubs located in hotels or motels as defined herein having dining room and kitchen facilities may be relieved from maintaining a kitchen in connection with such supper club. The principal business of such supper clubs shall be entertainment, and any serving of alcoholic beverages shall be incidental thereto.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)

Sec. 3-137. - Sale to minors or incapacitated persons; loitering about premises.
No licensee hereunder shall sell alcoholic beverages to or allow any persons under 21 years of age or incapacitated persons to be in, frequent or loiter about the licensed premises of a supper club or lounge; provided, however, persons under 21 years of age may frequent or be in such supper clubs or lounges if they are accompanied by a parent or legal guardian.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-138. - Misrepresentation of age, possession, consumption by minors.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person under 21 years of age to misrepresent his age in any manner whatsoever to a licensee hereunder.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person under 21 years of age to drink or possess any alcoholic beverage as defined herein on any licensed premises.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-139. - Package sales by licensees.
Licenses hereunder shall not permit the sale of distilled spirits by the bottle or package. Such persons holding such licenses shall have the right to sell wines, champagnes or malt beverages to the public by the bottle or package for consumption on the premises without the issuance of a separate retail license therefor.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-140. - Opened original packages on licensed premises prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person except a licensee, his manager or agent in charge of any licensed premises hereunder to carry into or have in his possession on any licensed premises, any alcoholic beverage in the original package the seal of which has been broken or the original package opened.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-141. - Misrepresenting quantity, quality of bottle.
It shall be illegal for licensees hereunder to add to the contents of a bottle, or to refill empty bottles, or in any other manner to misrepresent the quantity, quality or brand name of any alcoholic beverages.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-142. - Service in back rooms prohibited.
The sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption by persons in any back room or side room which is not open to general public use is prohibited, except that private parties or conventions, which have been scheduled in advance, may be served in public or private dining rooms or meeting rooms; and provided further, that this prohibition shall not apply to private clubs hereunder, nor to the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption hereunder to the registered guest of any hotel or motel in their designated and private rooms.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-143. - Sales outside premises.
It shall be unlawful for any sales to be made outside of the premises on the lot on which such business is licensed for such sale, except as permitted herein.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-144. - Acts prohibited unless licensed.
It shall be illegal and an offense against the ordinances of the city for any person, firm, corporation, association, society, union, institution, private club, or other form of organization, or any combination thereof, without having applied for, and obtained a annual license to sell at retail alcoholic beverages by the drink for consumption on the premises where such sale occurs, to engage or continue in any of the following activities or conduct:
(1)
Selling alcoholic beverages at retail by the drink for consumption on the premises where sold for such consumption;
(2)
Selling at retail alcoholic beverages for consumption by the drink on the premises where such retail sale occurs;
(3)
Controlling or possessing alcoholic beverages for retail sale by the drink for consumption on the premises where controlled or possessed for such retail sale;
(4)
Offering alcoholic beverages for sale at retail by the drink for consumption on the premises where offered;
(5)
Furnishing alcoholic beverages, to be served by the drink, at retail for consumption on the premises where so furnished;
(6)
Storing or keeping, or permitting the storage or keeping of broken or unsealed packages of alcoholic beverages of members for consumption thereof by the owners of such liquors on the premises where so stored or kept;
(7)
Acquiring, delivering, receiving or accepting alcoholic beverages by any one at or on any premises other than his or her bona fide home for consumption thereat or therein, if no city license for the retail sale of such beverage at such place by the drink is in effect;
(8)
Providing a room, place, or premises for keeping or storage, of sealed and unopened, or opened or unsealed containers or bottles of alcoholic beverages for sale at retail, or for consumption thereof on such premises when no valid city license to retail alcoholic beverages by the drink for consumption on such premises shall be in force;
(9)
Evading any provision of this or any other valid ordinance of this city relating to the retail sale of alcoholic beverages by the drink for consumption on the premises where sold, served, or furnished for consumption on such premises, by whatever false scheme, trick or device may be employed;
(10)
Making gratuitous gifts or donations of spurious memberships in an alleged private, regular, dues-paying members club, or including enough not specified in the price charged for purchases of merchandise or privileges, other than distilled spirits, to cover membership dues, shall be held an evasion, and shall not be treated as a bona fide donation or gift;
(11)
Aiding, abetting, counseling, advising, or assisting in the violation of this or any other applicable valid city ordinance relating to the sale at retail of any alcoholic beverage by the drink for consumption on the premises where sold.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
Sec. 3-145. - Private clubs defined.
(a)
In addition to the definition as provided in section 3-1(10) of this chapter, a private membership club, within the meaning of this section, shall be and is hereby defined as only such organizations as:
(1)
Cannot limit or restrict the authority, control, or power of one of its members within three months from the date he becomes such member; or
(2)
Shall make and keep a correct and true printed or typewritten list of all of its members open to inspection of the city license inspector, mayor, all members of the city council, the city manager, and any member of the police department of the city, at all times of day and night when the club is open to its members; and
(3)
Confine its membership to persons over 21 years of age; and
(4)
Restricts their patronage to their bona fide regular dues-paying members; and
(5)
Derive the most of their income from their bona fide regular membership dues, sales and service of meals, and private dances by such members and their immediate families, or other lawful activities not related to or involving the sale or use of distilled spirits; and
(6)
Do not derive their chief income from the sale of alcoholic beverages.
(b)
The prohibitions contained in section 3-144 shall specifically apply herein.
(Ord. No. 104, 1-4-2011)
ARTICLE VI. - LICENSE FEES AND EXCISE TAXES

Sec. 3-146. - Schedule of license fees and excise taxes.
There shall be an application fee of $100.00 for all applications for a new alcoholic beverage license. Applicants applying for more than one type of license at the same time shall only be required to pay one application fee. The application fee shall not apply to applications to renew existing licenses. In addition to any other business license fees that may be applicable under this Code, the following annual license taxes shall be paid for the type of license being acquired as follows:
(1)

a.
Malt beverages: .....$165.00
b.
Wine: .....$165.00
c.
Distilled spirits: .....$2,920.00
d.
Sunday sales of alcoholic beverages by the drink for consumption on the premises (in addition to all licenses to sell alcoholic beverages by the drink): .....$500.00
(2)
License fee for package sale (no on-premises consumption) of malt beverages: .....$165.00
(3)
License fee for package sale (no on-premises consumption) of wine: .....$165.00
(4)
License fee for package sale (no on premises consumption) of distilled spirits: .....$2,920.00
(4.1)
License fee for Sunday package sales of beer, wine and/or distilled spirits (no on premises consumption, in addition to all regular licenses for package sales): .....$500.00
(5)
License fee for on-premises consumption of malt beverages and wine at private membership club: .....$400.00
a.
Sunday sales of alcoholic beverages by the drink for consumption on the premises at private membership club (in addition to all licenses to sell alcoholic beverages by the drink): .....$500.00
(6)
Excise tax upon wholesalers selling malt beverages within the city per 12-ounce container or fraction thereof: .....0.05
Or, per ounce of malt beverage sold by each wholesale dealer within the corporate limits of the city: .....0.0041

Excise tax upon distilled spirits wholesalers: $0.22/liter, excluding fortified wines, and a proportionate tax at the same rate for all fractional parts of a liter.

Wine sold to persons outside this state for resale or consumption outside this state; and

Wine containing less than one-half of one percent alcohol by volume.
