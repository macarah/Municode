ARTICLE VI. - BUDGETARY POLICIES AND ADMINISTRATION

Sec. 2-130. - Departmental responsibilities.
Prior to the end of each fiscal year, each department head shall submit to the mayor and city manager that department's requested budget to be submitted on a line item budget method of budgeting.
(Ord. of 9-7-93)
Sec. 2-131. - Review by the mayor and city manager.
Upon receipt of the proposed budget for each department, the mayor as chief executive of the city shall review the requested departmental budget with the city manager, shall review the goals and objectives, both short term and long term, of each department, and shall make recommendations regarding the departmental budgets to the city council. The mayor's recommended budget as established in this section shall be the proposed budget which is reviewed and acted upon by the city council.
(Ord. of 9-7-93)
Sec. 2-132. - Public hearings and council review.
The city council shall hold a public hearing on the mayor's recommended budget. The city council shall have the right to accept the mayor's recommended budget or to accept the mayor's recommended budget with certain amendments and changes thereto as the city council may deem appropriate in its judgment.
(Ord. of 9-7-93)
Sec. 2-133. - Budget set by ordinance.
Prior to the commencement of the fiscal year, the city council, by ordinance, shall adopt and approve a budget for the ensuing fiscal year. This ordinance shall be subject to veto by the mayor in the same manner as any other ordinance.
(Ord. of 9-7-93)
Sec. 2-134. - Capital projects and cemetery funds.
The mayor and city council shall have the right to establish one or more capital projects funds for capital projects that may not be funded within a single budget year. They may also establish a fund for the maintenance of the historic cemeteries within the city to be funded from cemetery lot sales and to provide that monies not utilized for maintenance and operating expenses in the city cemetery in any given year shall be carried forward to the ensuing fiscal year. No funds may be appropriated from the cemetery fund except for maintenance or other use within the city cemetery.
(Ord. of 9-7-93)
Sec. 2-135. - Requirement of a balanced budget.
In preparing the budget, the mayor, city manager and city council must provide for a balanced budget in that anticipated revenues plus that portion of the beginning fund balance in excess of the required fund balance reserve as established under section 2-137 must equal the total estimated expenditures from the general fund. In any capital projects fund, total anticipated revenues must equal total estimated expenditures.
(Ord. of 9-7-93)
Sec. 2-136. - Contingency fund.
To the extent fiscally possible, the city shall, as part of its annual budget, establish an unreserved fund balance equal to no less than three (3) and no more the five (5) percent of the general fund budget to pay expenditures caused by unforeseen emergencies, cash short falls caused by revenue declines, and to eliminate any need for short term borrowing.
(Ord. of 9-7-93)
Sec. 2-137. - Appropriations to be annual.
All unencumbered appropriations lapse at the end of the fiscal year. Any encumbered appropriations at year end automatically carry forward to the subsequent year, but are reserved for the purposes for which they were encumbered and are charged as an expenditure to the budget appropriation in the year initially encumbered.
(Ord. of 9-7-93)
Sec. 2-138. - Accounting standards.
(a)
The city shall maintain a system of financial monitoring, control, and reporting for all operations and funds in order to provide an effective means of ensuring that financial integrity is not compromised. In addition, such practices shall provide city officials with the necessary resources in order to make sound financial decisions. This system of control shall be in accordance with the prescribed accounting policies of the city. These policies should be available in written form to any employee who may perform duties within the financial administration, control, monitoring, reporting, and operational functions of the city.
(b)
The budget adopted shall be based upon generally accepted accounting principles applied on a consistent basis. Revenues shall be budgeted when they become measurable and available and expenditures shall be charged against the budget when they become measurable, a fund liability has been incurred, and the liability will be liquidated with current resources.
(Ord. of 9-7-93; Ord. No. 2017-03, pt. I, §§ 1, 2, 6-6-17)
Sec. 2-139. - Departmental budgets.
Each department's budget, as approved by the city council, shall be deemed to be its appropriation for the fiscal year. Within each department's budget, there shall be such line items as may be established by the mayor, city manager and department head. The total of such line items shall equal the department's annual appropriation. The city manager, upon request of the department head, may adjust and amend amounts budgeted for any line item within that department by transferring funds among budgeted line items subject to the following conditions:
(1)
No transfers to or from a capital acquisition account shall be made without the prior consent of the city council;
(2)
Any amendments to the line item budget of any department shall be reported by the city manager to the city council no less than quarterly;
(3)
There may be no change in the total amount of funds appropriated to any department of the city without an amendment to the budget ordinance made by the city council at any regular or special meeting thereof as provided in section 2-140.
(4)
No transfer or change in any budgeted line item dealing with salaries shall be made without the prior written consent of the city council.
(Ord. of 9-7-93)
Sec. 2-140. - Amended appropriations.
A department's appropriation for the fiscal year may be changed by an amendment to the budget ordinance by the city council at a regular meeting or special meeting thereof. It shall be the responsibility of the city manager and the finance officer of the city to insure that no department or department head exceeds the amount of funds appropriated for that department in any fiscal year.
(Ord. of 9-7-93)
Sec. 2-141. - Violations.
A violation of these provisions shall subject the person responsible to disciplinary action as described in section 2-52 of the Code of Ordinances of the City of Stone Mountain.
(Ord. of 9-7-93)
Sec. 2-142. - Adoption of current budget.
(a)
The fiscal year shall be January 1, 2020 through December 31, 2020.
(b)
The budget of revenues and appropriations for the year 2020, a copy of which is attached to this Appropriations Ordinance as Exhibit 1, is hereby adopted and the city clerk is directed to publish and print such budget in accordance with those provisions set forth in section 3.20 of the Charter of the City of Stone Mountain. The aforesaid copy of the budget of revenues and appropriations for the year 2020, labeled Exhibit 1, is incorporated by reference into this ordinance and expressly made a part hereof.
