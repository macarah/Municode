ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 4-1. - Definitions.
Except as otherwise provided in this chapter, the definitions of words and phrases in O.C.G.A. tit. 3 are hereby adopted for all purposes of this chapter. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Licensee means the actual license holder, as well as any person in the employment of the licensee or the establishment for which the license was obtained. It shall also include any person in an informal relationship with the licensee, who takes any action which furthers the conduct of the business for which the license was obtained.
Restaurant means any public place kept, used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public as a place where meals are served and where meals are actually and regularly served, without sleeping accommodations, such place being provided with an adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining room equipment and seating capacity for at least 50 people, having employed therein a sufficient number and kind of employees to prepare, cook and serve suitable food for its guests. At least two meals per day shall be served at least five days a week, with the exception of holidays, vacations and periods of redecorating, and the serving of such meals and food shall be the principal business conducted, with at least 60 percent of restaurant's revenue being generated from the sale of food, with the serving of malt beverages, wine and distilled spirits to be consumed on the premises as only incidental thereto. The sale of alcoholic beverages in accordance with the provisions of this chapter in an amount in excess of food sales for any period of 90 days shall be prima facie evidence that the principal business of the licensee is not that of serving food.
Retail beer dealer means every person, firm, corporation or association that engages in selling or handling malt beverages of any kind or character, at retail, within the corporate limits of the city.
Retail wine dealer and wholesale wine dealer shall have a like meaning and import, as related to the sale and handling of wine as is respectively defined in the definitions of retail and wholesale beer dealers.
Wholesale beer dealer means every person, firm, corporation or association that sells and/or delivers malt beverages of any kind or character to a retail beer dealer as defined in this section, to or within the corporate limits of the city.
Sec. 4-2. - Penalty.
The opening or operation for the doing of any business or vocation described in this chapter without first obtaining a written license for the doing of the business or vocation; or the violation of any rule or regulation for the control of alcoholic beverages promulgated in and by this chapter; or the assistance of another to evade or to avoid the payment of any fee or tax imposed and provided in and by this chapter; shall be an offense against the city, and upon conviction in the municipal court, shall be subject to the penalties provided by law. In addition thereto, the license of such offender may be suspended or revoked in the discretion of the council.
Sec. 4-3. - Possession—Fully tax-paid beverages.
Possession of fully tax-paid alcoholic beverages, in quantities not exceeding those provided by state law, shall be permitted within the limits of the city.
Sec. 4-4. - Possession—Nontax-paid beverages.
Except as otherwise provided by state law, it shall be unlawful for any person, whether holding a license under this chapter or not, to have in his possession or custody or control, regardless of the use of the same by him intended, any quantity, however small, of any alcoholic beverage on which all required federal and state taxes thereon have not been paid in full, and on which proper and sufficient governmental stamps or decals are not affixed to the package or container.
State Law reference— Possession and transportation of lawfully purchased alcoholic beverages upon which taxes have not been paid in this state, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-8.
Sec. 4-5. - Public consumption of alcoholic beverages.
No person shall in the city drink alcoholic beverages or possess alcoholic beverages in any open container while upon or within any parked vehicle, upon or in any public street, alley, parking lot, park or city owned and operated recreation center, public building or any parking area adjacent to any place of business conducted and licensed under this chapter.
Sec. 4-6. - Carrying, drinking or possessing open container, bottle, cup, glass or can.
It shall be unlawful for any person to carry, drink or possess any alcoholic beverage in any type of open container, bottle, cup, glass or can, while upon or in any public street, sidewalk, alley, parking lot, city owned park or any parking area adjacent to any place of business conducted and licensed under this chapter.
Sec. 4-7. - License; refusal; revocation.
In addition to other provisions of this chapter, the council may refuse to grant, or to review, or may suspend or revoke, in accordance with due process, any license under this chapter if the applicant has violated, or if the license holder violates, any of the ordinances, rules and regulations prescribed by the city for the conduct of the business dealing in alcoholic beverages, or the law of the state concerning such business, or should the licensee fail to preserve order at the place of business. The licensee shall be entitled to a hearing before the revocation of his license on the ground of fault committed by him. The business licensed under this chapter shall be subject to such further rules and regulations as the council shall establish.
State Law reference— Local powers as to licenses, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2.
Sec. 4-8. - Display and advertisement; application of state and federal law.
All laws and regulations of the United States of America, of force, in respect to the display and advertisement for sale of alcoholic beverages, are hereby adopted and made effective and operative within the limits of the city, and a violation of the laws and regulations is made an offense against the city.
State Law reference— Display of prices in show window, etc., O.C.G.A. § 3-4-26.
Sec. 4-9. - Sales on election days.
Pursuant to the delegation of authority granted to the council by O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20(b)(2)(B), the sale by wholesale and retail of alcoholic beverages: distilled spirits, wine and malt beverages for consumption both on and off the premises shall be lawful during the polling tours of any election; provided, however, nothing in this section shall authorize the sale of alcoholic beverages within 250 feet of a polling place or the outer edge of any building within which such polling place is established on primary or election days during such time as the polls are open.
Sec. 4-10. - Sunday package sales.
The sale of package malt beverages and wine is prohibited on Sundays.
Secs. 4-11—4-40. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - DEALERS OF MALT BEVERAGES AND WINE
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-41. - Excise tax—Malt beverages.
(a)
There is hereby imposed and levied a specific excise tax upon malt beverages as provided in the city's most recently adopted fee schedule.
(b)
The excise tax shall be imposed upon and shall be paid by the licensed wholesale beer dealer.
(c)
The tax shall be paid on or before the tenth day of the month following the calendar month in which the beverages are sold or disposed of within the city by the wholesale beer dealer.
(d)
Each licensee responsible for the payment of the excise tax shall file a report itemizing for the preceding calendar month the exact quantities of malt beverages, by size and type of container, sold during the month within the city.
(e)
The wholesale beer dealer shall remit to the city on the tenth day of the month following the calendar month in which the sales were made the tax imposed by the city.
(f)
This section shall not apply to malt beverages that contain less than one-half of one percent alcohol by volume.
(Code 1976, §§ 3-426—3-428; Ord. of 5-1-1973, §§ 1—3)
Sec. 4-42. - Excise tax—Wine.
(a)
There is hereby imposed and levied a specific excise tax upon all retail wine dealers in foreign and domestic wine, operating and doing business within the limits of the city as provided in the city's most recently adopted fee schedule.
(b)
The sales described in O.C.G.A. § 3-6-70 and the wines described in O.C.G.A. § 3-6-71 shall be exempt from this section.
(Code 1976, §§ 3-429—3-431; Ord. of 9-4-1973, §§ 1—3)
Sec. 4-43. - Excise tax—Effect.
Excise taxes levied under this article shall be in addition to all other taxes or licenses imposed by the city.
Secs. 4-44—4-60. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - CONDUCT OF BUSINESS

Sec. 4-61. - Rules.
The following rules shall apply to the conduct of the business of dealing in malt beverages and wine in the city:
(1)
The beverages shall not be sold or served in private rooms;
(2)
The beverages shall not be sold or served behind screens, blinds, curtains or other hiding places, but only in the open where the public is admitted;
(3)
The beverages shall not be sold or served to any persons in violation of any law relating to the age of such persons;
(4)
No malt beverages or wine shall be dealt in at retail by the licensee except with some other business conducted in the city for which the owner pays a license tax of not less than $25.00;
(5)
The place of business of the licensee and all places and rooms in connection with the place of business over which he has control shall be subject to inspection by the county, state or city officers charged with the preservation of the peace and by their deputies.
Sec. 4-62. - Unlawful sales.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell malt beverages or wine in the city without obtaining a license so to do under this article or to sell such malt beverages or wine after the revocation of such license, or to violate any of the rules and regulations governing the business of dealing in malt beverages and wine.
State Law reference— Sale of alcoholic beverages on Sundays, election days and Christmas Day, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20; furnishing alcoholic beverages to persons under 21 years of age, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23.
Sec. 4-63. - Regulation of stacking, keeping of stock.
The clerk, chief of police or other officer authorized and directed by the council to make examinations and inspections to determine whether or not the terms and provisions of this article are complied with may promulgate and enforce such reasonable regulations and requirements for the stacking, placing and keeping of retail stocks of malt beverages and wine as will most effectually facilitate such examinations and inspections; and a willful and continued violation of such reasonable regulation or regulations, independent of any or all other causes or reasons, shall be sufficient ground for the revocation of the retail license of the offending retail dealer, by the city.
Sec. 4-64. - Receiving from unlicensed persons.
It shall be unlawful for any retail beer dealer and/or any retail wine dealer to buy or receive, directly or otherwise, for possession or sale within the corporate limits of the city, from any person who does not at the time of such purchase and delivery have and hold in his name a wholesale license regularly issued by the city for the wholesale sale of such product; and a conviction of any retail dealer of such offense shall, in addition to all other penalties imposed by the city, authorize the summary revocation by the city of the license of the retail dealer thus offending.
Sec. 4-65. - Delivery to unlicensed persons.
It shall be unlawful for any wholesale beer dealer and/or any wholesale wine dealer to sell, ship or deliver, directly or otherwise, for possession or sale within the corporate limits of the city, to any person who does not at the time of such sale and delivery have and hold in his name a retail license regularly issued by the city for the sale of such product; and a conviction of any wholesale dealer of such offense shall, in addition to all other penalties imposed by the city, authorize the summary revocation by the city of the license of the wholesale dealer thus offending.
Secs. 4-66—4-90. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - DEALER LICENSE[2]

Sec. 4-91. - Required.
(a)
Serving malt beverages, wine. No person shall serve to his patrons or customers malt beverages or wine in any hotel, cafe, restaurant, depot, storeroom, place of business or other public place in the city unless such person has received a license from the city to deal in malt beverages or wine at such cafe, hotel, restaurant, depot, storeroom or place of business or other public place.
(b)
Dealing at wholesale or retail. The privilege of engaging in the business of a wholesale or retail beer dealer, and the business of a wholesale or retail wine dealer, respectively, shall in no event be granted by the city to any person, except by the issuance of a license in the manner provided in, and after compliance with all of the terms and conditions imposed by, this article.
Sec. 4-92. - Application.
(a)
Licenses may be issued to persons desiring to deal in malt beverages or wine in the city as provided in this section.
(b)
The person who desires to engage in the business of dealing in malt beverages or wine shall file an application in writing for a license to conduct such business, which application shall give the name of the applicant, the location of the business he proposes to establish and the kind of business he proposes to conduct, whether the same is the business of a wholesale or retail dealer. He shall also designate the character of business which he proposes to conduct at the place of his malt beverage or wine business and the amount of the license fee for conducting such business.
(c)
The applicant shall accompany his application with the amount of the license fee assessed upon the business proposed to be conducted.
Sec. 4-93. - Right of entry for inspection purposes; conformity with laws.
The applicant for a license under this article shall, in his application, agree that his place of business and all places and rooms in connection with his place of business, over which he has control, shall be subject to inspection during business hours by the county, state and city officers charged with the preservation of the peace, and by their deputies; and he shall agree to conduct the business for which the license is applied in conformity with the provisions of O.C.G.A. tit. 3, and in conformity with the rules and regulations governing such business passed by the council.
Sec. 4-94. - Fee; duration of license.
(a)
A license fee shall be levied upon all dealers in malt beverages and wine as set out in the city's most recently adopted fee and excise tax schedule.
(b)
A beer and wine license shall be valid only for the calendar year indicated thereon and no such license may be renewed. A licensee who desires to continue in business during the next or subsequent calendar year must make a new application for such year on or before December 10 of the preceding year.
(c)
Licensees planning to operate during the subsequent license year who fail to file on or before December 10 as provided by the regulations may be cited by the council to show cause why their current license should not be suspended or revoked or why their application for a license to operate the subsequent year should not be denied.
(d)
Any licensee making proper application, with all supporting documents, for a license to operate during the following calendar year and making such application prior to December 10 shall be permitted to continue to operate pending final approval of such licensee's application for the following year if final approval is not granted prior to January 1; provided, however, that any person holding any license issued pursuant to this article who shall fail to file a proper application for a similar license for the following year, with the proper fee accompanying the application, on or before December 10 who files such an application after January 1 shall be required to pay, in addition to the license taxes imposed by the city's most recently adopted fee and excise tax schedule an additional amount equal to one-half the amount required for the license for which application is made.
Sec. 4-45. - Wineries.
Any person may establish a winery for the manufacture, storage and sale of wines made from Georgia crops, after having first obtained a permit from the council.
State Law reference— Local license for farm winery, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-40.
Sec. 4-96. - Ancillary wine tasting license.
(a)
The holder of a wine license shall be eligible for an ancillary wine tasting license to provide samples of wine offered for sale to customers under the conditions set forth in this section.
(b)
Wine sampling shall be on limited occasions when a customer requests a sample of a wine offered for sale within the premises, or in conjunction with wine education classes and sampling designed to promote wine appreciation and education.
(c)
Wine tasting for customers shall only be conducted at a wine counter area constituting no more than ten percent of the entire floor area of the premises.
(d)
Wine sampling for customers shall be limited to no more than one time per day for a period of not to exceed two consecutive hours. Samples shall not exceed two ounces, and no customer shall consume more than eight ounces in any two-hour period.
(e)
Wine bottles shall be opened by the licensee or an employee, and samples shall only be poured by the licensee and/or an employee.
(f)
No open containers of wine shall be removed from the licensed premises.
(g)
Not more than two times per week for a period not to exceed two consecutive hours, the holder of an ancillary wine tasting license may conduct educational classes and sampling for classes. All conditions of sampling set forth in this section shall apply to such classes, except for the limitation on floor areas where the classes can be conducted.
(h)
Holders of an ancillary wine tasting permit shall not charge for samples or tastings, but may accept donations for a charitable organization of their choice.
(i)
Wine sampling and tasting is only permitted within the enclosed portion of the premises.
(j)
The annual fee for an ancillary wine tasting license shall be set as stated in the city's most recently adopted fee and excise tax schedule.
Sec. 4-97. - Ancillary growler malt beverage tasting license.
(a)
The holder of a package malt beverage license, with or without a package wine license, but in no event with a package distilled spirits license, shall be eligible for an ancillary growler malt beverage tasting license to provide samples of growler malt beverages offered for sale to customers under the conditions set forth in this section.
(b)
Growler malt beverage sampling shall be on limited occasions when a customer requests a sample of a growler malt beverage offered for sale within the premises, or in conjunction with growler malt beverage education classes and sampling designed to promote growler malt beverage appreciation and education.
(c)
Growler malt beverage tasting for customers shall only be conducted at a counter area constituting no more than ten percent of the entire floor area of the premises.
(d)
Growler malt beverage sampling for customers shall be limited to no more than one time per day per customer. Samples shall not exceed two ounces, and no customer shall consume more than eight ounces in any two-hour period.
(e)
Only the licensee or an employee shall open, handle, and serve, and samples shall only be poured by the licensee and/or an employee.
(f)
No open growler containers shall be removed from the licensed premises.
(g)
Not more than two times per week for a period of not to exceed two consecutive hours, the holder of an ancillary growler malt beverage tasting license may conduct educational classes and sampling for class participants. All conditions of sampling set forth in this section shall apply to such classes, except for the limitation on floor areas where the classes can be conducted.
(h)
Holders of an ancillary growler malt beverage tasting license shall not charge for samples or tastings, but may accept donations for a charitable organization of their choice.
(i)
Growler malt beverage sampling and tasting is only permitted within the designated interior portion of the premises.
(j)
The annual fee for an ancillary growler malt beverage tasting license shall be set as stated in the city's most recently adopted fee and excise tax schedule.

ARTICLE III. - SALES BY THE DRINK[3]
Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— Sales by the drink, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-90 et seq.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-181. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Distilled spirits, malt beverage, beer, and wine shall be synonymous and will be intended to mean any alcoholic beverage for the purposes of sections 4-184 through 4-186, 44-181-189, 4-191 and 4-222 through 4-232.
License means the authorization by the council to engage in the sale of distilled spirits for consumption on the premises.
Licensee for the sale of distilled spirits by the drink on the premises means any person duly licensed to sell by the drink and for consumption only on the premises.
Premises means the definite closed or partitioned in locality, whether a room, shop or building, wherein distilled spirits shall be sold and consumed.
Wholesaler or wholesale distributor means any person engaged in distribution or selling to licensees for the sale of spirituous liquors by the drink on the premises.
Sec. 4-182. - Violations; state and federal requirements.
(a)
Any person violating the rules and regulations set out in this article shall be subject to revocation of his license and punishment as prescribed in the city's municipal fine schedule.
(b)
In addition to the rules and regulations set out in this article, each licensee doing business in the city under this article shall comply with all laws of the state, federal laws, and rules and regulations of the state revenue commissioner relating to the sale and distribution of distilled spirits in the state, and any violation of the rules and regulations shall subject such licensee to the immediate suspension or revocation of his license and shall subject such licensee to criminal prosecution by the proper authority as provided by law and the ordinances of the city.
Sec. 4-183. - General compliance.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or offer to sell any alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises without first complying with the rules and regulations set out in this article.
Sec. 4-184. - Place of sale or delivery.
No licensee shall sell or deliver any distilled spirits to any person except in the licensee's place of business.
Sec. 4-185. - Time of sales.
(a)
No licensee shall sell, furnish or offer for sale any alcoholic beverages at any of the following times:
(1)
At any time on Christmas Day;
(2)
On Monday between the hours of 12:00 midnight and 8:00 a.m.;
(3)
On Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday and Saturday between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 8:00 a.m.; or
(4)
At any time on Sunday.
(b)
All glasses and containers in which alcoholic beverages were sold during legal hours of sale shall be removed from the customers' table and all customers shall be required by the licensee to vacate the premises within 30 minutes after the end of the legal hours of sale as set forth in this section.
(c)
If the licensee is open at times other than when alcoholic beverages can legally be sold, it shall be the duty of such licensee to secure its inventory during such hours or on such days and to notify its customers and invitees that alcoholic beverages and products are not available for sale.
State Law reference— Sale of alcoholic beverages on Sundays, election days and Christmas Day, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20; regulation of conduct of licensees, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-110.
Sec. 4-186. - Providing beverages to certain persons.
No licensee shall give, sell or offer to sell any distilled spirits to any person as provided in state law on distilled spirits, who is of unsound mind, or who is a habitual drunkard whose intemperate habits are known to such retailer.
State Law reference— Furnishing alcoholic beverages to intoxicated persons, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-22; furnishing alcoholic beverages to persons under 21 years of age, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23; identification cards for persons without driver's licenses, O.C.G.A. § 40-5-100 et seq.
Sec. 4-187. - Storage spaces.
No licensee shall keep any distilled spirits stored in any bonded or other type warehouse in the city nor shall he enter into any type of arrangement whereby distilled spirits ordered by him are stored for him by any licensed wholesaler. A licensee shall keep no inventory or stock of distilled spirits at any place except his licensed place of business. Within his licensed place of business, his storage space for distilled spirits shall be immediately adjacent to the room in which he is licensed to do business.
Sec. 4-188. - Containers; manner of sale.
No consumption-on-premises licensee may purchase distilled spirits in containers smaller than one-fifth gallon. The sale of distilled spirits by any consumption-on-premises licensee in unbroken packages or in any quantity for other than consumption on the premises is prohibited.
Sec. 4-189. - Duties of licensee and agents.
(a)
All holders of a consumption-on-premises license shall keep a copy of this article in his premises and shall instruct any person working there with respect to the terms. Each licensee or his agents selling distilled spirits shall at all times be familiar with the terms. The licensee shall be held responsible for any acts of his employees in any violation of this article or of the laws of the state or the rules and regulations of the state revenue commissioner.
(b)
It shall be the duty of the licensee to train all employees whose duties shall include the sale, dispensing, serving, or taking orders for alcoholic beverages in the requirements of this article and applicable state laws and regulations. Each licensee shall establish written policies, a copy of which shall be posted within the licensed premises, governing the sale and dispensing of alcoholic beverages in accordance with the state law and this article. Licensees are encouraged to adopt disciplinary sanctions for employees who fail to meet the standards of law as violations by employees can constitute grounds for revocation, suspension or denial of a license under this article. The degree of training, supervision, and discipline of employees by the licensee may be considered by the city council in determining action on any licensee.
Sec. 4-190. - Inspection of premises.
The business premises of the holder of a consumption-on-premises license for the sale of distilled spirits shall be open to inspection during business hours by officers or officials authorized to conduct such inspection.
Sec. 4-191. - Gambling.
There shall be no gambling, betting, games of chance, punchboards, slot machines, gambling machines, lotteries or tickets or chances therein or the operation of any schemes for hazarding money or any other thing of value in any licensee's place of business or in any room adjoining the place of business, owned, leased or controlled by a licensee. Any violation of this section shall be cause for suspension or revocation of the license.
State Law reference— Gambling and related offenses, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-20 et seq.
Sec. 4-192. - Malt beverages; wine.
A licensee for the sale of distilled spirits for consumption on the premises shall also have the right to serve malt beverages or wine, provided he shall secure a license therefor.
Sec. 4-193. - Disturbance of the peace, obscenity or public indecency prohibited.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to permit any disturbance of the peace, obscenity or public indecency on such premises licensed to sell alcohol by the drink or otherwise licensed to sell alcoholic beverages.
(b)
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
(1)
Alcoholic beverages establishment means any restaurant holding a license under this chapter for the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on premises.
(2)
Substantially nude means dressed or undressed in a manner so as to plainly expose to view any portion of a male's or female's pubic hair, anus, cleft of the buttocks, vulva, or genitals, or any portion of the breasts below the top of the areola.
(c)
No person shall appear substantially nude in any alcoholic beverage establishment, and no owner or manager of an alcoholic beverage establishment shall permit any person to appear substantially nude on the licensed premises.
(d)
No owner or manager of an alcoholic beverage establishment shall permit any person to perform acts of or acts which constitute or simulate:
(1)
Sexual intercourse, masturbation, sodomy, bestiality, oral copulation or any sexual acts which are prohibited by law.
(2)
The touching, caressing or fondling of the breasts, buttocks, anus or genitals, provided that random acts of patrons or employees, whose actions do not constitute actions taken pursuant to encouragement or acquiescence of the management of the establishment and are not for the purposes of entertainment, promotion, publicity, or notoriety shall not constitute violations of this section.
(e)
No person shall engage in any of the acts identified in subsection (d)(2) of this section on the premises of an alcoholic beverage establishment.
(f)
It shall be unlawful to permit on the premises so licensed any contest or form of entertainment which consists of wetting or soaking of the upper torso of a female or the pelvic areas of a male or female.
(g)
The restrictions of subsection (d)(2) of this section shall apply to all persons physically present on the licensed premises regardless of whether such persons are categorized as employees, patrons, independent contractors or otherwise.
(h)
In addition to prosecution of any person for violation of this section, the business license of any premises upon which a violation of this section occurs shall be subject to suspension or revocation in the manner provided in this Code. Any conviction or plea of guilty or nolo contendere in the municipal court or any other court having jurisdiction of such charges to a charge of violating this section shall be admissible in a license suspension or revocation proceeding.
(i)
In addition to other provisions of this section, violation of the provisions of this section shall be punishable as for a misdemeanor in the municipal court or any other court having jurisdiction of violations of city ordinances and it shall be in the discretion of the court in which a violation is found to suspend the license of the violator. The failure of the municipal court to suspend the license of a violator shall not preclude the mayor and council from doing so.
Sec. 4-194. - Public consumption of alcoholic beverages during community festivals.
It shall be unlawful for any licensed establishment to dispense any alcoholic beverage in an open container for removal from the premises, and it shall be unlawful for any person to remove from an alcoholic beverage establishment any open container of alcoholic beverage or to drink or attempt to drink any alcoholic beverage from any open container or to possess in any open container any alcoholic beverage on the streets, sidewalks, rights-of-way, and parking lots, whether public or private, within the corporate limits of the city.
Sec. 4-195. - Possession of alcohol prohibited by persons under the age of 21 years; other prohibited practices; sales to same; proof of identification.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person under the age of 21 years to have in his possession, custody and control, or to have in any vehicle in his possession, custody and control, any malt or alcoholic beverages of any type, kind or description whatsoever within the city.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person under the age of 21 years to either purchase or attempt to purchase from any person any malt or alcoholic beverages of any type, kind or description whatsoever within the city.
(c)
It shall be unlawful to sell, offer to sell or give away to any person under 21 years of age any malt or alcoholic beverages of any type whatsoever within the city.
(d)
Proof of identification needed to purchase alcoholic beverages and/or tobacco products. It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or offer to sell any alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises and for a retail establishment to sell or for any person to purchase alcoholic beverages of any kind whatsoever not for consumption on premises or tobacco products in the city unless the purchaser exhibits proper identification to the seller so that the seller may verify the age of such person. For purposes of this chapter, the term "proper identification" means any document issued by a governmental agency containing a description of the person, such person's photograph, or both and giving such person's date of birth and includes without being limited to a passport, military identification card, driver's license, or an identification card authorized by O.C.G.A. §§ 40-5-100—40-5-104. The term "proper identification" shall not include a birth certificate, or any traffic citation or complaint form. This section applies to all persons regardless of age, and regardless of whether or not such person appears to be of proper age to purchase alcoholic beverages or tobacco products.
Secs. 4-196—4-221. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - CONSUMPTION-ON-PREMISES LICENSE

Sec. 4-222. - Required; application.
No distilled spirits shall be sold by the drink for consumption on the premises except under a license granted by the council. An application for a consumption-on-premises license shall be made on forms provided by the council as follows:
(1)
A consumption-on-premises license shall be valid only for the calendar year indicated thereon and no such license may be renewed. A licensee who desires to continue in business during the next calendar year must make a new application for such year on or before November 15 of the preceding year.
(2)
Licensees planning to operate during the subsequent license year who fail to file on or before November 15, as provided by the regulations, may be cited by the council to show cause why their current license should not be suspended or revoked or why their application for a license to operate during the subsequent year should not be denied.
(3)
Notwithstanding, any current owner of the property or licensee of a business who does not now comply with this section and who fails to maintain a business under this article for a period of one year shall not be authorized to operate in noncompliance of this section.
Sec. 4-223. - Presentation of application.
All applications for a license for the sale of distilled spirits by the drink for consumption in the premises shall be presented in person to the clerk.
Sec. 4-224. - Requirements for approval.
(a)
Any applicant for a consumption-on-premises license must be of good character, and all managers, clerks or other employees shall be of like character or the application will not be considered. Corporate applicants shall be of good business reputation.
(b)
No applicant for a license who has a criminal record (except minor traffic offenses) shall be granted a license.
(c)
A consumption-on-premises license shall not be issued without first being approved by the chief of police, the building official, and the fire chief.
(d)
The council may consider any circumstances which may reflect favorably or unfavorably on the applicant, the application or the proposed location of the business. If in the council's judgement circumstances are such that the granting of a license would not be in the best interest of the general public, such circumstances may be grounds for granting or denying the application, provided they are disclosed to the applicant.
(e)
No building permit shall be issued to an applicant for construction or remodeling of a building for consumption on-premises until the applicant is approved under this section.
State Law reference— Powers of local governing authorities as to granting or refusal of licenses, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2.
Sec. 4-225. - Age of licensee.
No consumption-on-premises license shall be granted to any individual unless he is 18 years of age of older.
State Law reference— Age of legal majority, O.C.G.A. § 39-1-1.
Sec. 4-226. - Proximity to churches and schools.
(a)
No consumption-on-premises license shall be granted to any person unless the entire business premises of the proposed location is situated beyond 100 yards from the property line of any church, school ground or college campus, with the exception of a consumption-on-premises license to restaurants.
(b)
The distances between a business and churches, schools or colleges as provided for in this section shall be measured as follows:
(1)
From the property line of the tract on which is located the business regulated under this section;
(2)
To the property line of the tract on which is located the church, school ground or college campus;
(3)
Along a straight line which describes the shortest distance or most direct route of legal travel on the ground property lines.
(c)
Each application for a consumption-on-premises license shall include a scale drawing of the location of the proposed premises showing the distance to the nearest church or school or a certificate of a registered surveyor that such location complies with the distance set forth in this section.
State Law reference— Sales of alcoholic beverages near churches and schools, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.
Sec. 4-227. - Separate locations.
A separate consumption-on-premises license shall be required by each place of business.
Sec. 4-228. - Building plans and requirements.
No consumption-on-premises license shall be issued to any person unless complete and detailed floor plans of the building in which the business will be located, and of the outside premises, are attached to the application, or unless proposed plans and specifications and a building permit of a proposed building to be built are attached to the application. The completed building or the proposed building shall comply with ordinances of the city, regulations of the state revenue commissioner and the laws of the state. The proposed building shall also be subject to final inspection and approval when completed by the building official. Each building in which the business will be located shall contain sufficient lighting. Each applicant for a consumption-on-premises license shall attach to his application evidence of ownership of the building or proposed building or a copy of the lease if the applicant is leasing the building. All premises for which a consumption-on-premises license shall be issued shall afford therein adequate sanitary toilet facilities and shall be adequately illuminated so that all hallways, passageways and open areas may be clearly seen by customers therein.
Sec. 4-229. - Duration; fee.
(a)
The annual fee for a consumption-on-premises license shall be the amount stated in the city's most recently adopted fee and excise tax schedule and shall be paid prior to the issuance of any license. Such fee shall accompany the application and shall be either in cash or a bank certified check.
(b)
Such license shall be for the calendar year. No refund will be made, except if the license is denied by the state, a pro rata refund will be made.
Sec. 4-230. - False statements.
The making of any statement on an application for a license to sell distilled spirits for consumption on the premises which shall be later found to be false shall constitute grounds for revocation of such license.
Sec. 4-231. - Transferability or assignability.
(a)
Except as provided in this article, no consumption-on-premises license shall be transferable or assignable to any person or other location. If a licensed business is sold or closed, it shall be the duty of the licensee to surrender his license immediately to the council.
(b)
Should a transfer of a location be approved by the council, there shall be no pro rata return of any license fee under the new location; the new location shall be considered as a new license.
Sec. 4-232. - Death of licensee; partnerships; corporations.
(a)
In case of the death of any person holding a license under this article, or any interest in the license, the license may, in the discretion of the council, be transferred. Nothing in this section, however, shall prohibit one or more of the partners in a partnership holding a license to withdraw from the partnership in favor of one or more of the partners who were partners at the time of the issuance of the license. Such a withdrawal shall not, however, serve to bring any new ownership into the partnership.
(b)
A licensee may take in partners or additional stockholders where it is determined that the additional capital furnished is to be used exclusively for additional inventory or expanding the facilities of the business or for building new facilities and where it appears that the licensee himself received directly none of the additional capital invested. Under this section an additional partner or new principal stockholder must be approved by the council pursuant to the criteria of this chapter.
Sec. 4-233. - One-day special events license and fees.
(a)
Applicants who are desirous of conducting a one-day event within the corporate limits of the city must first make application to the license department.
(b)
Applicant must allow 30 days for processing.
(c)
Applications are categorized by nonprofit and for-profit.
(d)
Qualified nonprofit organizations must submit a current 501c(3) letter from the Internal Revenue Service.
(e)
No more than six one-day special events licenses may be held by a nonprofit organization in any one calendar year at the established fees. If the nonprofit applicant desires a seventh event in any one calendar year, the established for-profit fee schedule will apply.
(f)
One-day special events licenses which are approved and granted shall comply and conform with all other existing requirements set forth by the current zoning ordinances and this Code.
(g)
Third party vendors including but not limited to catering companies, whether selling alcoholic beverages directly or indirectly, must obtain the proper alcohol license and comply with all local, state and federal regulations regarding the purchase of alcohol from suppliers.
(h)
No one-day special events license will be approved by the city council unless it bears written approval of the office of the chief of police, and the city clerk of the city.
(i)
Applicants for a one-day special events license who will serve or sell alcoholic beverages must:
(1)
Comply with the requirements, including, but not limited to fingerprinting and a background check conducted by the city police department.
(2)
Comply with all local, state and federal laws and regulations relating to the sale of alcoholic beverages with the person named on the application being held solely responsible for all compliance.
(3)
Provide evidence of current liability insurance coverage.
(4)
Submit written permission from the property owner detailing date, time and specifics of the event.
(5)
Provide any other material requested by any city personnel.
(j)
Evidence of licensure shall be posted and remain posted at the site during the event.
(k)
Fees for one-day special events licenses are as stated in the city's most recently adopted fee and excise tax schedule.
