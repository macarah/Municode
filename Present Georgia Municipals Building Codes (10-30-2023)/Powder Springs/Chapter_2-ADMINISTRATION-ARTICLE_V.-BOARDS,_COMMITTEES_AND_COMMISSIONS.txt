ARTICLE V. - BOARDS, COMMITTEES AND COMMISSIONS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-81. - Oaths.
(a)
All appointed members of boards, commissions, and authorities of the city shall, prior to assuming office, execute and file with the city clerk the prescribed oath. Such oath shall be in the following form, unless otherwise provided herein or by law:
I do solemnly (swear) (affirm) that I will faithfully and impartially perform the duties of my office and that I will support and defend the charter of the City of Powder Springs as well as the Constitution and laws of the State of Georgia and of the United States of America.
(b)
The prescribed oath for members of the planning and zoning commission shall be as follows:
I swear (or affirm) that I will faithfully and impartially perform the duties of my office, charter and ordinances of this city, and conduct myself with integrity as a commission member during my term. I have not in order to influence my appointment to this position, directly or indirectly promised my vote or support to any person or officer in city government, nor for any other position. I will not knowingly permit my vote on zoning matters to be influenced by fear, favor or the hope of reward, but in all things pertaining to my position I will be governed by what in my judgment is for the public good and for the best interest of Powder Springs, so help me God.
(c)
The official acts of a member of a board, commission, or authority of the city shall be valid regardless of his or her omission to take the oath, except in cases so specially declared.
(Ord. No. 2003-32, § 1, 12-1-03)
Sec. 2-82. - Appointment and reappointment process.
(a)
All persons desiring appointment or reappointment to a board, commission, or authority shall complete and file with the city clerk an application setting forth the applicant's educational and work background, any special skills or training which the applicant believes will aid in the performance of the duties required for service on a particular board, commission, or authority, and the applicant's past history of service on boards, commissions, or authorities. Those seeking reappointment shall provide details of his or her prior service.
(b)
As part of the application, the applicant will be required to state that he or she has read and agrees to abide by the City's Code of Ethics.
(c)
As a further part of the application, the applicant will be expected to pledge that he or she will devote substantial time to the work required of the particular board, commission, or authority, both in terms of attendance at regular meetings, scheduled training, and as otherwise needed to be an effective member of such board, commission, or authority.
(d)
The city clerk shall provide to the City Council any and all applications received in advance of the filling of a vacancy so that the council may evaluate the credentials of the applicants in advance of making appointments. In making reappointments, the City Council shall consider the applicant's attendance record and the effectiveness of such applicant's prior service.
(e)
Further, each board, commission, or authority shall keep an attendance record of its members and report such attendance to the City Council on an annual basis, so that the City Council may consider such record when considering appointments and reappointments.
(f)
Except as otherwise provided by law, appointments and reappointments shall be made in such a fashion that the mayor and each member of the council will at all times have at least one (1) appointee of his or her choosing on each board, commission, or authority for which the mayor and council make appointments.
( Ord. No. 2016-009, § 1, 3-7-16)
Secs. 2-83—2-90. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - GEORGE E. FORD CENTER BOARD

Sec. 2-91. - Name.
The public buildings and grounds of the former Powder Springs Elementary School shall be named the "Coach" George E. Ford Center.
(Ord. No. 97-20, § I, 2-18-97)
Sec. 2-92. - Board of directors.
The governing body of the city affirmatively finds that there is a necessity to establish a board of directors to oversee the operations of the "Coach" George E. Ford Center, and therefore, pursuant to City Charter, sections 1.13(17) and 3.11, there is hereby established a board of directors to be known as the "Coach" George E. Ford Center Board of Directors.
The governing body of the "Coach" George E. Ford Center shall be known as the board of directors and shall consist of seven (7) directors to be appointed by the governing body of the city for staggered terms of two (2) years. All directors shall serve at the pleasure of the mayor and council. At least four (4) directors shall be taxpayers residing in the city or persons owning or operating businesses in the city; no director shall be an officer or an employee of the city. Each councilmember shall be a afforded the opportunity to appoint a director of his or her choosing, and the mayor shall be afforded the opportunity to appoint two (2) directors of his or her choosing. The mayor shall designate one (1) of his initial two (2) appointees in the year 2016 to serve as chairman for his or her initial term. Thereafter, the directors shall elect one (1) of their members as chairman. The directors shall also elect one (1) of their members as vice-chairman, and shall also elect members as secretary and treasurer or a secretary/treasurer. The directors shall receive no compensation for their services but shall be reimbursed for their actual expenses incurred in the performance of their duties, subject to budgeted availability, as determined by the finance director, approval by a majority of the members prior to incurring the expense, and submittal of receipts within fourteen (14) days of incurring the expense.
(Ord. No. 97-20, § II, 2-18-97; Ord. No. 98-28, 12-7-98; Ord. No. 2016-009, § 2, 3-7-16; Ord. No. > 2016-026 , § 1, 10-17-16; Ord. No. 2017-033 , § 1(Exh. A), 12-4-17)
Sec. 2-93. - Duties of the board.
The board shall, among other duties and responsibilities, establish rules, regulations and procedures regarding usage, rental, hours of operation, fees and deposits for use of the facility; oversee maintenance of the facility and property; prepare annual operating and capital budgets for maintenance and renovation of the facility; receive rental and other funds; plan for future improvement and development of the facility via a five-year capital plan, long-term improvement plan and a vision for the use of the facility, including marketing and use of the facility; and assist with the marketing and use of the Seven Springs Museum at the Bodiford House as a cultural facility.
(Ord. No. 97-20, § III, 2-18-97; Ord. No. 2016-009, § 2, 3-7-16)
Sec. 2-94. - Authority of board; quorum.
The board may make by-laws and regulations for its government and may delegate to one or more of its officers, agents or employees, such powers and duties that may be deemed necessary and proper. The board created hereunder shall have perpetual existence as hereinafter provided. A majority of the directors shall constitute a quorum, but no action shall be taken by the board without the affirmative vote of a majority of the full membership of the board.
The board shall have all of the powers necessary or convenient to carry out and effectuate its purposes and the appropriate provisions of this chapter, including, but without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the following powers:
(1)
To sue and be sued;
(2)
To adopt and amend a corporate seal;
(3)
To make and execute contracts and other instruments necessary to exercise the powers of the board;
(4)
To receive and administer gifts, grants and [devices] of any property and to administer trusts;
(5)
To acquire by purchase, gift, or construction any real or personal property desired to be acquired as part of any project for the purpose of improving, extending, adding to, reconstructing, renovating or remodeling any project or part thereof already acquired, or for the purpose of demolition to make room for such project or any part thereof;
(6)
To lease, assign, or grant use of the property to members of the community and other groups;
(7)
To appoint officers and retain agents, engineers, attorneys, physical agents, accountants and employees and to provide for their compensation and duties;
(8)
To construct, acquire, own, repair, remodel, maintain, extend, improve and equip projects located on the property;
(9)
To do all things necessary or convenient to carry out the powers expressly conferred above.
(Ord. No. 97-20, § IV, 2-18-97; Ord. No. 2016-009, § 2, 3-7-16)
Secs. 2-95—2-100. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - CODE ENFORCEMENT BOARD[3]

Editor's note— Ord. No. 2003-31, § 1, adopted Nov. 17, 2003, supplied provisions for use as §§ 2-130—2-140. In order to maintain the continuity of the Code, and the editor's discretion, these provisions have been included as Div. 3, §§ 2-101—2-112, as set out herein.

Sec. 2-101. - Intent.
It is the intent of this article to promote, protect, and improve the health, safety, and welfare of the citizens of the city by authorizing the creation of an administrative board with authority to impose administrative fines and other noncriminal penalties to provide an equitable, expeditious, effective, and inexpensive method of enforcing any codes and ordinances in force in the city, where a pending or repeated violation continues to exist.
(Ord. No. 2003-31, § 1(2-130), 11-17-03)
Sec. 2-102. - Creation of code enforcement board.
There is hereby created, pursuant to the Local Governmental Code Enforcement Boards Act, O.C.G.A. § 36-74-1, et seq., a local government code enforcement board in the city to be known as the Powder Springs Code Enforcement Board.
(Ord. No. 2003-31, § 1(2-131), 11-17-03)
Sec. 2-103. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the term:
Code inspector means any authorized agent or employee of the city whose duty it is to assure code compliance.
Codes and ordinances mean City of Powder Springs zoning ordinances and resolutions, ordinances and resolutions enacting subdivision regulations, environmental ordinances and resolutions, state minimum standard codes provided for in O.C.G.A. § 8-2-25, ordinances and resolutions enacted pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 8-2-25, other ordinances and resolutions regulating the development of real property, and ordinances and regulations providing for control of litter and debris, control of junked or abandoned vehicles, and control of overgrown vegetation. Notwithstanding the above, the term "City of Powder Springs codes and ordinances" shall not include:
(1)
Those codes and ordinances requiring a permit, unless the alleged violator has failed to secure all necessary valid permits under said codes and ordinances; or
(2)
Any local amendments to the state minimum standard codes provided for in O.C.G.A. § 8-2-25 that have not been adopted in conformity with the requirements of subsection (c) of O.C.G.A. § 8-2-25.
Enforcement board means the Powder Springs Code Enforcement Board.
Violation involving the health or safety of a third party means a violation that creates a legitimate concern for the health and safety of a third party occupant of a dwelling place or that creates an immediate and substantial danger to the environment.
(Ord. No. 2003-31, § 1(2-132), 11-17-03)
Sec. 2-104. - Organization.
(a)
The code enforcement board shall consist of seven (7) members appointed by the mayor and City Council. The mayor and City Council may appoint up to two (2) alternate members to serve on the board in the absence of board members.
(b)
Members of the enforcement board shall be residents of the City of Powder Springs. In making appointments to the enforcement board, the mayor and City Council shall make good faith efforts to appoint one (1) or more individuals who have experience or expertise relevant to one (1) or more of the municipal codes that are within the subject matter jurisdiction of the enforcement board, including individuals with property management and litter control experience; provided, however, that the authority and jurisdiction of an enforcement board shall not in any way be limited due to the absence from its membership of one (1) or more individuals with such experience or expertise.
(c)
The initial appointments to the enforcement board shall be as follows:
(1)
Three (3) members appointed for a term of two (2) years each; and
(2)
Four (4) members appointed for a term of four (4) years each.
(d)
Upon the expiration of the initial terms specified in paragraphs (c) (1) and (2) of this section, all terms shall be for three (3) years.
(e)
A member may be reappointed upon approval of the mayor and City Council.
(f)
An appointment to fill any vacancy on an enforcement board shall be for the remainder of the unexpired term of office. If any member fails to attend two (2) of three (3) successive meetings without cause and without prior approval of the chairperson, the enforcement board shall declare the member's office vacant, and the mayor and City Council shall promptly fill such vacancy.
(g)
The members shall serve in accordance with ordinances of the local governing body and may be suspended and removed for cause as provided in such ordinances for removal of members of boards. A local governing body may, with or without cause, refuse to reappoint any member of an enforcement board at the expiration of his or her term of office.
(h)
The members of the enforcement board shall elect a chairperson, who shall be a voting member, from among the members of the board. The presence of four (4) or more members shall constitute a quorum. Members shall serve without compensation, but may be reimbursed for such travel, mileage, and per diem expenses as may be authorized by the mayor and City Council or as otherwise provided by law.
(i)
The city attorney shall either be counsel to an enforcement board or shall represent the municipality or county by presenting cases before the enforcement board, but in no case shall the city attorney serve in both capacities.
(Ord. No. 2003-31, § 1(2-133), 11-17-03)
Sec. 2-105. - Enforcement procedures.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the code inspector to initiate enforcement proceedings pursuant to the various codes and ordinances; however, no member of the enforcement board shall have the power to initiate such enforcement proceedings.
(b)
Except as provided in subsections (c) and (d) below, if a violation of any code or ordinance is found, the code inspector shall notify the violator and give him or her a reasonable time to correct the violation. Should the violation continue beyond the time specified for correction, the code inspector shall proceed with enforcement through the appropriate court or shall proceed with enforcement through the enforcement board. If the code inspector proceeds through the enforcement board, the code inspector shall notify an enforcement board and request a hearing. The enforcement board shall schedule a hearing, and written notice of such hearing shall be hand delivered or made as provided in section 2-111 to said violator. At the option of the enforcement board, notice may additionally be served by publication or posting as provided in section 2-111. If the violation is corrected and then recurs or if the violation is not corrected by the time specified for correction by the code inspector, the case may be presented to the enforcement board even if the violation has been corrected prior to the board hearing, and the notice shall so state.
(c)
If a repeat violation is found, the code inspector shall notify the violator but is not required to give the violator a reasonable time to correct the violation. The code inspector, upon notifying the violator of a repeat violation, shall notify an enforcement board and request a hearing. The enforcement board shall schedule a hearing and shall provide written notice pursuant to section 2-111. The case may be presented to the enforcement board even if the repeat violation has been corrected prior to the board hearing, and the notice shall so state.
(d)
If the code inspector has substantial reason to believe a violation presents a serious threat to the public health, safety, and welfare or if the violation is irreparable or irreversible in nature, the code inspector shall make a reasonable effort to notify the violator and may immediately notify the enforcement board and request a hearing.
(Ord. No. 2003-31, § 1(2-134), 11-17-03)
Sec. 2-106. - Conduct of hearings.
(a)
Upon request of the code inspector, or at such other times as may be necessary, the chairperson of the enforcement board may call a hearing of the board; a hearing also may be called by written notice signed by at least three members of the board. Minutes shall be kept of all hearings by the enforcement board, and all hearings and proceedings shall be open to the public. The mayor and City Council may provide or assign clerical and administrative personnel to assist the enforcement board in the proper performance of its duties.
(b)
Each case before the enforcement board shall be presented by the city attorney or by a code inspector or other member of the administrative staff of the mayor and City Council.
(c)
The enforcement board shall proceed to hear the cases on the agenda for that day. All testimony shall be under oath and shall be recorded. The enforcement board shall take testimony from the code inspector and alleged violator. Formal rules of evidence shall not apply, but fundamental due process shall be observed and shall govern the proceedings.
(d)
At the conclusion of the hearing, the enforcement board shall issue findings of fact, based on evidence of record and conclusions of law, and shall issue an order affording the proper relief consistent with powers granted in this division. The findings and conclusions shall be by motion approved by a majority of those members present and voting, except that at least four members must vote in order for the action to be official. The order may include a notice that it must be complied with by a specified date and that a fine may be imposed if the order is not complied with by said date. A certified copy of such order may be recorded in the public records of the city and Cobb County and shall constitute notice to any subsequent purchasers, successors in interest, or assigns if the violation concerns real property, and the findings therein shall be binding upon the violator and, if the violation concerns real property, any subsequent purchasers, successors in interest, or assigns. If an order is recorded in the public records pursuant to this subsection and the order is complied with by the date specified in the order, the enforcement board shall issue an order acknowledging compliance that shall be recorded in the public records. A hearing is not required to issue such an order acknowledging compliance.
(Ord. No. 2003-31, § 1(2-135), 11-17-03)
Sec. 2-107. - Powers.
The enforcement board shall have the power to:
(1)
Adopt rules for the conduct of its hearings, which rules shall, at a minimum, ensure that each side has an equal opportunity to present evidence and argument in support of its case;
(2)
Subpoena alleged violators and witnesses to its hearings, with the approval of the court with jurisdiction over a criminal violator of the municipal code or ordinance. Subpoenas may be served by the police department or by any other individual authorized by O.C.G.A. § 24-10-23 to serve subpoenas;
(3)
Subpoena evidence to its hearings in the same way as provided in subsection (2) above, with the approval of the court with jurisdiction over a criminal violator of the municipal code or ordinance;
(4)
Take testimony under oath; and
(5)
Issue orders having the force of law to command whatever steps are necessary to bring a violation into compliance.
(Ord. No. 2003-31, § 1(2-136), 11-17-03)
Sec. 2-108. - Administrative fines and liens.
(a)
The enforcement board, upon notification by the code inspector that an order of the enforcement board has not been complied with by the set time may order the violator to pay an administrative fine in an amount specified in this section.
(b)
(1)
An administrative fine imposed pursuant to this section for a violation involving the health or safety of a third party shall not exceed one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) per day.
(2)
An administrative fine imposed pursuant to this section for a violation that is not a violation involving the health or safety of a third party shall not exceed a total of one thousand dollars ($1,000.00).
(3)
In determining the amount of the fine, if any, the enforcement board shall consider the following factors:
a.
The gravity of the violation;
b.
Any actions taken by the violator to correct the violation; and
c.
Any previous violations committed by the violator.
(4)
The enforcement board may reduce a fine imposed pursuant to this section.
(c)
A certified copy of an order imposing an administrative fine may be recorded in the public records of any county and thereafter shall constitute a lien against the land on which the violation exists and upon any real or personal property owned by the violator. Upon petition to the superior court, such order may be enforced in the same manner as a court judgment by the sheriffs of this state, including levy against the personal property, but such order shall not be deemed to be a court judgment except for enforcement purposes. After three (3) months from the filing of any such lien which remains unpaid, the enforcement board may request the city attorney to foreclose on the lien.
(Ord. No. 2003-31, § 1(2-136), 11-17-03)
Sec. 2-109. - Foreclosure of liens.
No lien imposed under this chapter shall continue for a period longer than twenty (20) years after the certified copy of an order imposing a fine has been recorded, unless within that time an action to foreclose on the lien is commenced in a court of competent jurisdiction. In an action to foreclose on a lien, the prevailing party is entitled to recover all costs, including a reasonable attorney's fee, that it incurs in the foreclosure. The continuation of the lien effected by the commencement of the action shall not be good against creditors or subsequent purchasers for valuable consideration without notice, unless a notice of lis pendens is recorded.
(Ord. No. 2003-31, § 1(2-137), 11-17-03)
Sec. 2-110. - Appeals.
An aggrieved party, including the mayor and City Council, may appeal a final administrative order of an enforcement board to the superior court. Such an appeal shall be a hearing de novo. An appeal shall be filed within thirty (30) days of the execution of the order to be appealed.
(Ord. No. 2003-31, § 1(2-138), 11-17-03)
Sec. 2-111. - Notices.
(a)
All notices required by this chapter shall be provided to the alleged violator by certified mail or statutory overnight delivery, return receipt requested; by hand delivery by the sheriff or other law enforcement officer, code inspector, or other person designated by the mayor and City Council; or by leaving the notice at the violator's usual place of residence with any person residing therein who is above fifteen (15) years of age and informing such person of the contents of the notice.
(b)
In addition to providing notice as set forth in subsection (a) of this section, at the option of the code enforcement board, notice may also be served by as follows:
(1)
Notice may be published once during each week for four (4) consecutive weeks (four (4) publications being sufficient) in the newspaper in which the sheriff's advertisements are printed in Cobb County. Proof of publication shall be made by affidavit of a duly authorized representative of the newspaper;
(2)
Notice by publication may run concurrently with, or may follow, an attempt or attempts to provide notice by hand delivery, mail, or statutory overnight delivery as required under subsection (a) above. Evidence that an attempt has been made to deliver notice by hand, mail, or statutory overnight delivery as provided in subsection (a) above, together with proof of publication as provided in this subsection, shall be sufficient to show that the notice requirements of this section have been met, without regard to whether or not the alleged violator actually received such notice.
(Ord. No. 2003-31, § 1(2-139), 11-17-03)
Sec. 2-112. - Provisions supplemental.
It is the intent of this division to provide an additional or supplemental means of obtaining compliance with local codes. Nothing contained in this chapter shall prohibit the city through its code inspector from enforcing its codes by any other lawful means including criminal and civil proceedings; provided, however, that the city shall not pursue a specific instance of an alleged violation of an ordinance against one violator before both the code enforcement board and a magistrate, municipal, or other court authorized to hear ordinance violations.
(Ord. No. 2003-31, § 1(2-140), 11-17-03)
DIVISION 4. - ARTS AND CULTURAL AFFAIRS COMMISSION

Sec. 2-113. - Commission created.
There is hereby created the arts and cultural affairs advisory commission.
(Ord. No. 2017-021 , § 1, 9-18-17)
Sec. 2-114. - Purpose.
The purpose of the commission is to build the community through arts and cultural programs by developing visibility, funding, audiences, information, and partnerships. The commission plans, promotes, and encourages programs to further public awareness of, accessibility to, participation in, and support for the artistic and cultural development of the city and makes recommendations to the City Council regarding the establishment of arts and cultural policies and programs, the expenditure of budgeted funds on arts and cultural activities, and the implementation of programs.
(Ord. No. 2017-021 , § 1, 9-18-17)
Sec. 2-115. - Operation.
The commission may adopt rules and regulations to govern its organizational procedures as may be necessary and which are not in conflict with this Code. All administrative, personnel, accounting, budgetary, and procurement policies of the city govern the commission in all its operations.
(Ord. No. 2017-021 , § 1, 9-18-17)
Sec. 2-116. - Membership.
(a)
Number of members. The commission comprises seven (7) members, appointed by the City Council.
(b)
Special qualifications:
(1)
Two (2) members must have significant knowledge and demonstrate interest in one (1) or more areas of the arts including music, dance, literature, visual arts, theater, folk arts, media arts, design, or arts education.
(2)
Two (2) members must be from the business and professional community.
(3)
Four (4) members must be city residents or owners of city businesses.
(c)
Oath. Each person, upon appointment or reappointment to the commission, must execute an oath of office at the first meeting of the commission following the appointment or reappointment or at the city clerk's office any time prior to the first meeting of the commission.
(d)
Terms. The term of office for commissioners is two (2) years or until such commissioner's successor is appointed and qualified.
(e)
Vacancies. Vacancies must be filled in the same manner as original appointments.
(f)
Officers. The commissioners must choose annually a chairperson and vice chairperson, each to serve a term of one (1) year. The chairperson must appoint a secretary, who need not be a member of the commission. The commissioners must fill a vacancy among its officers for the remainder of the officer's unexpired term.
(g)
Compensation. Commissioners serve without compensation, provided that they may receive reimbursement for necessary travel and other expenses while on official commission business within the limits established in the city administrative policies and budget, as determined by the finance director and subject to approval of a majority of the members prior to incurring expenses and submittal of receipts within fourteen (14) days of incurring the expenses.
(Ord. No. 2017-021 , § 1, 9-18-17; Ord. No. 2017-033 , § 1(Exh. A), 12-4-17)
Sec. 2-117. - Meetings.
(a)
Regular meetings. The commission must meet on each month.
(b)
Special meetings. Special meetings may be called by the chairperson or at the written request of a majority of the commissioners.
(c)
Open meetings. All meetings must be called and held in conformance with the open meetings law.
(d)
Attendance.
(1)
In the event a commissioner has been absent for three (3) or more consecutive meetings of the commission, such absence will be grounds for the commission to recommend to the City Council that the position be declared vacant and a replacement appointed.
(2)
Attendance must be entered upon the minutes of all meetings.
(e)
Minutes. A copy of the minutes of all regular and special meetings of the commission must be filed with the City Council within ten (10) working days after each meeting, or by the next regularly scheduled City Council meeting, whichever is later.
(f)
Quorum. Four (4) commissioners constitute a quorum for the transaction of business. The affirmative vote of a majority of the commissioners present and voting is necessary for the adoption of any motion or resolution.
(g)
Removal. The City Council may remove any commissioner for cause.
(Ord. No. 2017-021 , § 1, 9-18-17)
Sec. 2-118. - Powers.
The commission has the following powers, duties, and responsibilities:
(1)
To promote, educate, and create awareness for arts and cultural activities;
(2)
To develop and increase the market for arts and cultural activities;
(3)
To facilitate and convene partnerships for arts and cultural activities;
(4)
To develop funding options and allocate budgeted funding for arts and cultural activities;
(5)
To act in an advisory capacity to the City Council in all matters pertaining to the arts and cultural environment;
(6)
To make recommendations to the City Council on the allocation of budgeted city funding for arts programs and services;
(7)
To recommend applications and solicitations for grants, gifts, and donations from individuals, groups, organizations, and agencies, both private and public, for any purpose consistent with the purposes of the commission;
(8)
To make recommendations regarding city sponsorship of public programs to further the development of public awareness of and accessibility to arts and culture programs and activities;
(9)
To work with area organizations on broad community issues in which a cultural perspective is relevant and desirable; and
(10)
To engage in comprehensive planning for the cultural development of the city.
(Ord. No. 2017-021 , § 1, 9-18-17)
Sec. 2-119. - Cooperation with other boards and commissions.
The commission cooperates with other arts and culture groups interested in the promotion and implementation of arts and cultural activities which are in the best interest of the city.
(Ord. No. 2017-021 , § 1, 9-18-17)
Sec. 2-120. - Solicitation and acceptance of donations.
The commission may solicit and otherwise accept donations and gifts of money, personal and real property, and personal services on behalf of the city. All such donations and gifts received become the property of the city for the general use or support of arts and cultural programs as determined by the City Council unless such items have been designated by the donor for specific purposes.
(Ord. No. 2017-021 , § 1, 9-18-17)
