ARTICLE IX. - PROFESSIONAL BONDSMEN[9]

Footnotes:
--- (9) ---
Editor's note— Ord. No. 96-17, adopted Aug. 19, 1996, purported to amend ch. 12 of the Code by the inclusion of a new Art. VIII, §§ 12-160—12-163. To avoid duplication of article and section numbers, such provisions have been redesignated as Art. IX, §§ 12-211—12-214, at the discretion of the editor.

Sec. 12-211. - Definitions.
Professional bondsman. Any person who, for compensation, holds himself out as a signer or surety of criminal or other bonds.
(Ord. No. 96-17, 8-19-96)
Sec. 12-212. - License required.
No person shall operate as a professional bondsman without first obtaining a business license as such in the manner set out by this section.
(Ord. No. 96-17, 8-19-96)
Sec. 12-213. - Written approval of police chief required.
An application for a business license to operate as a professional bondsman in the city shall be accompanied by written approval of the chief of police, who shall investigate the applicant and issue such approval when he finds that the applicant is:
(1)
Eighteen (18) years of age or over;
(2)
A resident of the State of Georgia for at least one (1) year before making application to write bonds;
(3)
A person of good moral character and has not been convicted of a felony or any crime involving moral turpitude; and
(4)
Approved by the sheriff of Cobb County, Georgia, and remains in good standing with respect to all applicable federal, state and local laws and all rules and regulations established by the city and the Cobb County, Georgia, sheriff.
(Ord. No. 96-17, 8-19-96)
Sec. 12-214. - Grounds for refusal and revocation of license.
Any violation of O.C.G.A. Title 17, Chapter 6 (section 17-6-1 et seq.) shall constitute grounds for refusal to issue and/or revocation of the business license.
(Ord. No. 96-17, 8-19-96)
Secs. 12-215—12-300. - Reserved.
