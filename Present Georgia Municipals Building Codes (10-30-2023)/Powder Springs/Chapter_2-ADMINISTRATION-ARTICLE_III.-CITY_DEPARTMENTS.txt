Sec. 2-40. - Departmental organization.
The administrative and operating departments of the city shall be the administrative services department, utilities and public works department, police department, finance department, community development department, economic development department, and parks and recreation department.
(Code 1972, § 3-101; Ord. No. 2014-023, § 1, 11-3-14 ; Ord. No. 2017-009 , § 1, 4-17-17)
Sec. 2-41. - Responsibility of department heads.
Every department head is immediately responsible to the city manager for the effective administration of such department head's respective department unless prohibited by an ordinance, rule, regulation or policy of the council.
(Code 1972, § 3-104)
Sec. 2-42. - Administrative services department—Functions.
The administrative services department shall perform all administrative duties for the council and for the city which are not expressly assigned by law or this Code to another department or officer. All such duties of the administrative services department shall be performed under the direction of the city manager with the assistance of the assistant city manager, the city clerk, and such other employees as the city manager may designate. The duties of the administrative services department shall include overseeing municipal court functions and personnel human resources functions and personnel, information technology functions and personnel, the city manager's executive assistant, and the city hall receptionist.
(Code 1972, § 3-15; Ord. No. 2014-023, § 2, 11-3-14 )
Sec. 2-42.1. - Same—Assistant city manager's duties.
The assistant city manager shall:
(1)
Assist and consult with the city manager as required, and when so directed in writing by the city manager, shall supervise the administrative services department; and
(2)
Supervise special projects and perform such other duties and responsibilities as may be required.
(Ord. No. 97-15, 6-16-97; Ord. No. 2009-006, 2-2-09; Ord. No. 2014-023, § 3, 11-3-14 )
Sec. 2-43. - Same—City clerk's duties generally.
The city clerk shall:
(1)
Perform the duties conferred upon such officer by section 3.13 and any other applicable sections of the charter.
(2)
Keep true and accurate books of accounts of the dealings and transactions of the city, which books shall show at all times the true condition of the city, its resources and liabilities and the disposition and use of the monies coming under the control of the city.
(3)
Keep or cause to be kept in a safe place all monies, records and accounts.
(4)
Disburse funds for the various purposes of the city only when an appropriation for such purpose has been made in the annual budget and the disbursement is authorized by the council.
(5)
Perform such other duties as the council may from time to time require.
(Code 1972, § 3-106)
Sec. 2-44. - Same—Records retention and disposition.
(a)
City records of taxes, departmental dockets and logs, police permanent records, and contracts, and minutes of City Council meetings shall be retained indefinitely.
(b)
All records of city payables, including all bills and invoices of every type, shall be retained for a period of three (3) calendar years, and then the same shall be destroyed within one (1) year thereafter.
(c)
All other records, correspondence and documents not otherwise hereinabove set forth shall be retained for a period of ten (10) calendar years from the date of their receipt by the city, and the same shall be destroyed within one (1) year thereafter.
(d)
It shall be the responsibility of the city clerk to monitor and enforce the records retention required by this section.
(Code 1972, § 3-107; Ord. No. 83-7, § 1, 12-6-83)
Sec. 2-45. - Utilities and public works department—Functions.
The utilities and public works department shall be responsible for the proper construction and maintenance of streets, sidewalks, parks, buildings, public utilities, and other movable and immovable property of the city.
(Code 1972, § 3-107)
Sec. 2-46. - Same—Director's duties.
The director of the utilities and public works department shall:
(1)
Make regular inspections of the streets, alleys and sidewalks within the city; make a written memorandum of same; and cause all defects and obstructions to be remedied at the earliest opportunity.
(2)
Ensure that all highways, streets and sidewalks of the city are maintained in a clean condition; and enforce and carry out all city ordinances and regulations concerning city streets and sidewalks.
(3)
Enforce the provisions of the building code and all other technical codes adopted by the council.
(4)
Supervise and inspect the operations of all public utilities provided by the city.
(Code 1972, § 3-108)
Sec. 2-47. - Police department—Composition; compensation as fixed; direction and control.
(a)
Composition; compensation as fixed. The police department shall consist of a chief of police, and such officers and members as may be authorized from time to time by the council. The chief, other officers and the members of the department shall receive such compensation as the council may from time to time fix.
(b)
Direction and control. The direction and control of the police department shall be under the chief of police and it shall be the duty of all members of the department to obey the orders of the chief.
(Code 1972, §§ 3-109, 3-110, 3-116)
Sec. 2-48. - Same—Authority and general duties of chief.
(a)
Obedience to regulations. The chief shall receive such officer's orders from the city manager, and shall see that these orders are enforced, these requests and orders and ordinances having first been approved by the council.
(b)
Duties generally. The chief of police shall report all offenses against the city ordinances, serve all processes, notices, executions, attachments and warrants issued by mayor or municipal judge, any member of the council, the city manager or the city clerk and obey and execute all orders of the council in relation to any matter or thing affecting the interest of the city.
(c)
Attending meetings; collection and disposition of fines. The chief of police shall attend all meetings of the council and the municipal court and preserve order therein; collect all fines imposed by the council or municipal judge and all executions and demands that may be placed in such officer's hands by the council and pay the money over to the city clerk, and taking the clerk's receipt therefor, and making a detailed report of all such collections to the city manager as required. It shall be the duty of the chief to keep a docket, furnished by the city, in which such officer shall enter all executions issued by authority of the city, and when the same are paid, such officer shall enter it on the docket.
(d)
Preservation of order. The chief of police and officers shall preserve order in the city, enforce every public ordinance of the city and make cases against violators of the same; keep the streets and sidewalks open and prevent persons from congregating in crowds in the streets or sidewalks of the city and make cases against those who refuse or fail to remove or disperse at such officer's request; arrest all disorderly, drunken or riotous persons and commit them to the city jail to be tried by the municipal judge.
(e)
Absence from duty. When a police officer from sickness or other cause is unable to perform official duties the officer shall promptly notify the chief of police. When the chief of police cannot perform such duties, the chief shall notify the city manager.
(Code 1972, §§ 3-111—3-115)
Sec. 2-49. - Same—General powers, duties, authority and restrictions.
(a)
Complying with Code. The police officers shall see that any person who violates this Code is arrested and brought to jail. There the defendant will await trial, post bond or put up security for appearance in municipal court.
(b)
Authority to break and enter. The police officers shall have the right and authority, as provided by law upon proper warrant to break and enter any house or place in the city where they may have reason to suspect that any of the laws and ordinances of the city are being violated and to arrest the persons therein and imprison or admit them to bail as provided for in cases of arrest.
(c)
General regulations.
(1)
No member of the police department shall leave the city while on duty without permission from the chief.
(2)
Police officers will call in a "10-7" when getting out of their car, and "10-8" when resuming service.
(3)
There are to be no passengers in any police car, unless on official police business.
(4)
Officers will conduct themselves as gentlemen or ladies, as the case may be, in any situation.
(5)
No officer will discuss such officer's personal feelings about another officer with anyone except the chief. Any officer who violates this subsection shall be suspended for five (5) days without pay.
(6)
When an officer is given an order, such officer will discuss it with no one other than such officer's superior.
(7)
Any item taken from a prisoner must be receipted for, tagged with prisoner's name and date, and inventoried by two (2) persons.
(8)
Any alcoholic beverage or other property seized from a person, or out of a house or vehicle is the property of the city and is to be brought directly to the police station, where it shall remain until it is properly disposed of by the city manager, chief or judge. The contraband is to be tagged with the name and date. Also, the amount is to be written on the bottom of the ticket. This is also to apply to any contraband found in the city.
(9)
Every item of property brought to the police station by anyone, regardless of whether it is stolen property, lost and found, or evidence, is to be tagged or labeled with a complete history noted on the tag or label. The information on the tag or label is to show the number of pieces, where picked up and by whom picked up or brought to the station.
(10)
A complete daily report is to be filled out and signed by each officer.
(11)
No officer shall break any traffic ordinance of the city, except in an emergency.
(12)
During a council meeting or court session, all officers on duty who are not involved shall continue to patrol the streets.
(13)
Each officer on duty will keep any person, including minors, from loitering around city hall.
(14)
Police vehicles are to be used only for city business.
(15)
A memorandum pad is to be kept in every police car for keeping a record of all descriptions of wanted and missing persons, stolen vehicles and all available information of all serious emergencies.
(16)
Each officer in command, or such officer's delegate, will check the jail for knives, bottles, garbage, etc., and will make a note on the daily report.
(17)
When possible, police officers will check abandoned vehicles, tags, etc., by phone instead of by radio.
(18)
No police officer will call for assistance unless it is actually needed, as in an emergency. If a call for assistance is made, a notation will be made on the daily report.
(19)
Officers, while on the streets or in public places, shall be armed at all times and shall have with them their badges. Any officer appearing on the streets or in a public place in uniform will be considered on duty.
(20)
Every officer shall carefully inspect every part of such officer's beat as often as possible and by such vigilance and activity render it difficult for anyone to commit crime therein. The officer must, to the utmost of such officer's power, prevent the commission of assaults, breaches of the peace, and all other offenses about to be committed. When offenses occur frequently in any beat without detection or arrest of the offenders, the officer of this shift will be presumed guilty of neglect of duty or inefficiency and treated accordingly.
(21)
Sleeping, idling or loafing while on duty is a serious violation and any member of the department guilty of such misconduct will be subject to dismissal.
(22)
Members of the force who obtain credit by virtue of being employed by the city as police officers, and contract debts for clothing, house rent, food and the other necessary things for themselves and families, and who fail to pay for same within a reasonable time, will be considered guilty of unofficer-like conduct and may be dismissed from the force.
(23)
It is the duty of every member of the force to be thoroughly familiar with the rules and regulations governing the department. Ignorance of the rules shall not be an excuse when broken.
(24)
The chief of police is authorized to suspend any officer at the chief's discretion, and such suspension shall be reviewed by the council no later than the next regular meeting thereof.
(Code 1972, §§ 3-117—3-119)
Sec 2-50. - Community development department—Functions.
The community development department shall be responsible for the comprehensive plan, long range and current planning, greenspace protection, intergovernmental coordination, infill and redevelopment, analyzing and providing advice on land use regulations and other zoning matters, administering and enforcing the unified development code, assisting public works in enforcement of technical codes, creating and maintaining, to the extent of available resources, geographic information systems (GIS) in collaboration with the county's GIS that will support the functions of the department, registering and collecting business registration certificate fees and occupational taxes, issuing alcoholic beverage and other licenses and collecting regulatory license fees, administering and managing the building and land development permitting processes, and managing special projects related to capital enhancement projects.
( Ord. No. 2014-023, § 4, 11-3-14 )
Sec. 2-51. - Same—Director's duties.
The director of the community development department shall:
(1)
Periodically review city codes and ordinances and make recommendations for updates and amendments;
(2)
Enforce the provisions of the unified development code and assist public works in the enforcement of technical codes adopted by the city;
(3)
Prepare annual reports required by the state relating to administration and implementation of ordinances relating to the functions of the department;
(4)
Supervise and manage permitting, licensing, planning, zoning, GIS departmental functions, building and development and special projects related to enhancement capital projects;
(5)
Perform such other duties and responsibilities related to the community development department as may be required.
( Ord. No. 2014-023, § 4, 11-3-14 )
Sec. 2-52. - Economic development department—Functions.
The economic development department shall support and coordinate economic development activities to promote and enhance the city's functions, as provided for in section 2-121 of this Code.
( Ord. No. 2014-023, § 4, 11-3-14 )
Sec. 2-52.1. - Same—Director's duties.
The director of the economic development department shall:
(1)
Be responsible for the economic development activities of the city, to include recruitment of new business/industries and work with existing business on expansions;
(2)
Serve as city staff facilitator and advisor to the Downtown Development Authority (DDA) and Development Authority of Powder Springs (DAPS);
(3)
Develop, in connection with the city's Comprehensive Plan, the city manager, city staff, elected officials, DDA and DAPS boards, strategies for economic development utilizing the community's human and economic resources. The director shall become familiar with all persons and groups directly or indirectly involved in downtown and citywide development, property owners, businesses and related organizations; is mindful of the roles of various interest groups, and assists the DDA and DAPS in developing and implementing annual action plans;
(4)
Attend all DDA and DAPS meetings, activities, and events; prepare and participate in DDA and DAPS meetings by arranging for space, preparing and sending notices, posting notices, preparing agendas, taking notes, drafting minutes, arranging for refreshments, coordinate public meeting notices, press releases, and special advertisements as it pertains to the DDA, DAPS and their activities;
(5)
Prepare reports and agenda items for the DDA and DAPS, maintain files on the implementation of DDA and DAPS activities, maintain a file on the minutes of the meetings of the DDA and DAPS for public inspection and historical purposes, be responsible for preparation of budgets, purchase orders, check requests, and periodic financial reports for the DDA, DAPS and the Economic development department;
(6)
Research and apply for grants and funding for the enhancement of Downtown Powder Springs, the DDA-defined areas, as well as other areas of potential development within the corporate limits of Powder Springs;
(7)
Promote tourism programs and maintain files on tourism, including but not limited to, demographic data, community statistics, businesses and key contacts; answer telephone, email, mail, and in-person inquiries related to economic development, tourism, community information, and historic preservation;
(8)
Provide appropriate information as requested or direct the requests to the appropriate department or agency; if the inquiry is identified as a potential new or expanding business within the city, coordinate with the city manager and city staff as required and notify the DDA and/or DAPS of such activity;
(9)
Develop promotional materials and advertising strategies to attract business development, trade and tourism. Promotional material may include: information packets, booklets and brochures for prospective businesses, tourists and new residents;
(10)
Attend various meetings of the Cobb Chamber of Commerce, South Cobb Council of the Cobb Chamber, South Cobb Business Association, West Cobb Business Association, and other civic and community-minded organizations as deemed necessary by the city manager;
(11)
Attend seminars, conventions, and economic development-related meetings as approved by the city manager;
(12)
Interface with the city finance director and city auditor as needed to insure compliance with reporting requirements;
(13)
Develop and maintain a comprehensive inventory of available buildings and sites for economic development purposes;
(14)
Work to resolve complaints or concerns of business/property owners within the city limits and act as liaison between city officials and the business community;
(15)
Prepare press releases involving economic development activities;
(16)
Serve as a member of various employee and inter-governmental committees as assigned; and
(17)
Provide backup to related positions.
( Ord. No. 2014-023, § 4, 11-3-14 )
Sec. 2-52.2. - Same—Director's qualifications.
The director of the economic development department shall have the following education and experience, and knowledge, skills and abilities:
(1)
Education and experience:
a.
The director shall be a graduate from a college or university with a degree in Public Administration, Planning, Economic Development or related field, with preference given to applicants with a master's degree in a related field; and
b.
Two (2) years of responsible related experience in planning, small business administration, historic preservation, public relations, design marketing, "Main Street" administration, public administration or community organization; or
c.
Any equivalent combination of education and experience.
(2)
Knowledge, skills, and abilities:
a.
The director shall have knowledge of commercial revitalization from the point of merchants, property owners, public agencies, and community organizations, knowledge of computer software, and a working knowledge of modern office practices and procedures;
b.
Skill in organization and coordinating events and meetings and in remaining professional and politically neutral;
c.
The ability to meet and deal with the public, the ability to work evenings and weekends, the ability to work one on one with individuals or with large groups of people, the ability to communicate effectively verbally and in writing, the ability to handle stressful situations, and the ability to function effectively in an independent situation;
d.
Skill in using a personal computer and experience with MS Office Suite applications and similar software to compose business letters and promotional material, and skill in creating, interpreting, presenting and explaining statistical information using said applications and software; and
e.
Knowledge and skill in the application of business math and interpreting spreadsheets with financial and statistical data.
( Ord. No. 2014-023, § 4, 11-3-14 )
Sec. 2-53. - Finance department—Functions.
The finance department and its director shall be responsible for all activities relating to financial planning, accounting, revenue administration, and treasury management, and shall have the functions, duties, and obligations provided for in section 8-01 et seq. of this Code.
( Ord. No. 2014-023, § 4, 11-3-14 )
Sec. 2-53.1. - Same—Director's duties.
The duties of the director of the finance department, also known as the "financial director" or "city finance director," are provided for in section 8-01 et seq. of this Code.
( Ord. No. 2014-023, § 4, 11-3-14 )
Sec. 2-54. - Parks and recreation department—Functions.
The parks and recreation department shall be responsible for planning and organizing recreational events, park planning and programming, beautification and waste reduction on public properties such as rights of way, tree and landscape maintenance oversight, and general oversight of the maintenance of city owned and city maintained parks, recreational and cultural properties.
(Ord. No. 2017-009 , § 2, 4-17-17)
Sec. 2-54.1 - Same—Director's duties.
The director of the parks and recreation department shall:
(1)
Periodically review city codes and ordinances and make recommendations for updates and amendments;
(2)
Supervise and manage the department to effectuate its purposes;
(3)
Perform such other duties and responsibilities related to parks and recreation and cultural affairs as may be required.
(Ord. No. 2017-009 , § 2, 4-17-17)
