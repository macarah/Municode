Editor's note— Ord. No. 2006-39, §§ 1—3, adopted Aug. 15, 2006, repealed article XII, sections 15-200—15-208, in its entirety and replaced it with a new article XII, sections 15-200, 15-201. Former article XII pertained to similar material and derived from Ord. No. 2005-18, adopted June 21, 2005.
State Law reference— Regulation of fireworks, O.C.G.A. § 25-10-1 et seq.

Sec. 15-200. - Definitions.
"Fireworks" shall have the meaning as set out in O.C.G.A. § 25-10-1.
(Ord. No. 2006-39, §§ 1—3, 8-15-06)
Sec. 15-201. - Fireworks displays.
Firework displays shall be subject to the following requirements:
(1)
Any person desiring to conduct a public exhibition or display of fireworks shall first comply with the requirements of O.C.G.A. § 25-10-4 and show proof of such compliance by submitting to the public safety director for the city a copy of the fireworks exhibition permit issued by the judge of the probate court of the county.
(2)
Written notification must be given to the public safety director for the city for a fireworks exhibition within the city at least ten (10) days prior to the scheduled event. Accompanying said notification shall be a copy of the permit issued by the judge of the county probate court; and proof of liability insurance as required under state law.
(3)
The site of the fireworks and the equipment to be used shall be inspected by the fire department of the city prior to the time of the fireworks display.
(Ord. No. 2006-39, §§ 1—3, 8-15-06)
