ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 3-3-1. - Board of tax assessors.
The board of tax assessors is established as required by statute. Such board shall have the functions provided by statute.
State Law reference— Board of tax assessors created, Official Code of Georgia Annotated, § 48-5-290; functions of board, Official Code of Georgia Annotated, § 48-5-297 et seq.
Sec. 3-3-2. - Board of equalization.
The board of equalization is established as required by statute. Such board shall have the functions provided by statute.
State Law reference— Establishment and functions of board of equalization, Official Code of Georgia Annotated, § 48-5-311.
Sec. 3-3-3. - Due date for taxes; interest, etc.
(a)
All ad valorem taxes due the state, county, county board of education or any other entity or agency created by law which are levied upon tangible property, other than motor vehicles, shall become due and payable in a single tax billing.
(b)
All taxes due the state or the county shall be due and payable on November 15 in each year.
(c)
Taxes which are not paid by the due date shall be delinquent; provided however, after notices of taxes due are mailed out, each taxpayer shall be afforded sixty (60) days from date of postmark to make full payment of taxes due before the taxes shall bear interest as provided in this section.
(d)
The rate of interest and penalty to be charged by the county shall be the rate of interest and penalty specified by general law. All taxes due the state or the county remaining unpaid on November 15, the tax commissioner shall collect the interest on the unpaid taxes.
(Res. No. 82-22, 5-4-82; Res. No. 83-07, 4-19-83)
State Law reference— Authority of board of commissioners to so provide, Official Code of Georgia Annotated §§ 48-5-148—48-5-152.
Sec. 3-3-3.1. - Tax refunds due to clerical error.
(a)
Findings of fact. The board of commissioners of Henry County has determined that a need exists, from time to time, to refund ad valorem taxes to property owners when obvious clerical errors have been made.
(b)
Investigation; determination. It will first be investigated by the chief tax appraiser and then the tax commissioner, and a unanimous joint determination made and signed by both on the appropriate written form that clerical errors have indeed been made in specific cases.
(c)
Statutory authority. In accordance with state law as found in Official Code of Georgia Annotated, Section 48-5-380(e), the authorization to refund these monies, including approving or disapproving of claims, may be delegated by the board of commissioners to an appropriate department in local government, and this authority shall be delegated only in undisputed cases of obvious clerical errors.
(d)
Inapplicability. In disputed cases where there is no obvious error, the approval or disapproval of claims shall not be delegated by the Henry County governing authority.
(e)
Refund authority delegated; written report. The chief appraiser of Henry County and the Henry County Tax Commissioner are delegated with the hereinabove stated joint authority, provided that said refund not exceed two hundred dollars ($200.00) from the board of commissioners portion of the refund; and provided further that, each time a refund is made, the board of commissioners shall be notified in writing.
(Res. No. 85-01, 1-8-85)
Editor's note— Resolution No. 85-01, enacted Jan. 8, 1985, did not specifically amend this Code; hence, designation of the substantive provisions of such resolution as § 3-3-3.1 is at the discretion of the editor.
Sec. 3-3-4. - Purchase of automobile tags with checks; bad checks.
(a)
The tax commissioner of the county is authorized to accept checks from the public in payment for automobile tags.
(b)
The board of commissioners will be responsible for any checks which are returned and are deemed uncollectible.
(c)
The tax commissioner shall collect all returned checks and shall charge a fee of five dollars ($5.00) for all returned checks which are later collected. The fee charged for returned checks shall be paid into the general fund of the county treasury.
(d)
All returned checks which are deemed uncollectible by the tax commissioner after having exhausted the legal remedies available shall be paid for by the county out of the county treasury.
(e)
Upon notice to the board of commissioners that a check is uncollectible, the board of commissioners shall be authorized to issue a notice to the county sheriff and his lawful deputies, and to all sheriffs and lawful deputies of the state, to pick up the tag purchased with the bad check issued and deliver the tag to the tax commissioner.
(Res. No. 79-1, §§ 1—6, 1-2-79)
State Law reference— Payment of taxes by check, penalties for bad checks, etc., Official Code of Georgia Annotated § 48-5-146.
Sec. 3-3-5. - Local option sales tax.
The local option sales tax is hereby imposed pursuant to Official Code of Georgia Annotated section 48-8-83.
(Res. of 1-1-80)
State Law reference— Local option sales tax, Official Code of Georgia Annotated § 48-8-81 et seq.
Sec. 3-3-6. - Financial institutions business license tax.
(a)
In accordance with OCGA Section 48-6-93, there is hereby levied an annual business license tax upon all depository financial institutions located within the county at a rate of 0.25 percent of the gross receipts of said depository financial institutions. Gross receipts shall mean gross receipts as defined in OCGA Section 48-5-93. Depository financial institutions shall mean state and national banks, state building and loan associations, and federal savings and loan associations.
(b)
The minimum annual amount of business license tax due from any depository financial institution pursuant to subsection (a) shall be one thousand dollars ($1,000.00).
(c)
Pursuant to OCGA Section 48-6-93(c), each depository financial institution subject to this tax shall file a return of its gross receipts with the board of commissioners on March 1 of the year following the year in which such gross receipts are measured. Said return shall be in the manner and in the form prescribed by the commissioner of the department of revenue based on the allocation method set forth in subsection (d) of OCGA Section 48-6-93. The board of commissioners shall assess and collect the tax levied pursuant to this section based upon the information provided in said return.
(d)
Taxes levied pursuant to this section shall be due no later than thirty (30) days after filing the return prescribed by subsection (c).
(Res. No. 83-22, §§ 1—4, 10-4-83)
Sec. 3-3-7. - Gross premiums tax on insurance other than life insurance.
In accordance with OCGA Section 33-8-8.2, there is hereby levied an annual tax upon each insurer doing insurance business within the unincorporated area of the county, in an amount equal to two and one-half (2½) percent of the gross direct premiums received during the calendar year on all insurance, other than life insurance, as defined in OCGA Section 33-3-5(2), (3), (4), and (5). "Gross direct premiums" shall mean gross direct premiums as used in OCGA Section 33-8-3.2(a).
(Res. No. 83-17, § 1, 9-6-83)
Secs. 3-3-8—3-3-20. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - BUSINESS LICENSES[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
Cross reference— Authority of general assembly to authorize county to levy license tax or regulatory fees, § 1-4-4; local acts relating to business licenses and regulation, § 2-24-26 et seq.; penalty for violations of business regulations, § 2-4-26.
State Law reference— County occupation taxes, Official Code of Georgia Annotated, § 48-13-1 et seq.

Sec. 3-3-21. - Purpose.
The purpose of this article is to regulate establishments which do business in the county and to protect the general public from unfair and unscrupulous business practices.
(Ord. No. 81-1021, § I, 10-21-81)
Sec. 3-3-22. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to offer, advertise, begin or carry on any of the occupations, uses or businesses mentioned for which a license fee is required in the unincorporated area of the county, without first applying annually to the board of commissioners through the business license department and obtaining a license for carrying on such business, use or occupation.
(Ord. No. 81-1021, §§ I, II, 10-21-81)
Sec. 3-3-23. - License fee schedule.
(a)
Generally. Business license fees shall be as established and published by the board of commissioners.
(b)
Unlisted categories. Any person, firm or corporation engaged in business of any kind, which is not covered in this Code or the schedule of fees established as provided in paragraph (a) above, shall be required to purchase a business license. The fee for such license shall be determined by similar or related business as listed in the schedule of fees. Such fee shall remain in force until such time as the county commissioners may fix or assess the same.
(Ord. No. 81-1021, §§ V, XV, 10-21-81)
Cross reference— License fees required to be paid prior to issuance of a building permit, § 3-9-3.
Sec. 3-3-24. - Term, payment and proration.
All business licenses shall be issued for the calendar year and the required fees shall be paid in full by each person upon the issuance of the license. No pro rata license shall be issued to any person commencing business in the county after July 1 of each year where the business shall be considered as temporary. No license shall be issued at anytime for less than one-half of the annual fee.
(Ord. No. 81-1021, § III, 10-21-81)
Sec. 3-3-25. - Penalty for late renewal.
A penalty of fifty (50) percent of the required license fee is imposed upon any licensee who does not renew his license before February 1 of each year.
(Ord. No. 81-1021, § X, 10-21-81; Ord. No. 83-02, § 1, 1-5-83; Ord. No. 87-08, 6-16-87)
Sec. 3-3-26. - Posting.
All licenses issued pursuant to the provisions of this article shall be posted conspicuously in the place of business of person to whom the license is issued. Upon failure to do so, the board of commissioners may revoke the license.
(Ord. No. 81-1021, § VI, 10-21-81)
Sec. 3-3-27. - Transfer.
A license issued pursuant to the provisions of this article shall not be transferable as to licensee or place of business.
(Ord. No. 81-1021, § VIII, 10-21-81)
Sec. 3-3-28. - Exemption for veterans.
Veterans of the armed forces of the United States may apply for free license; providing, they are drawing a permanent disability pension of one hundred (100) percent and have a certificate of eligibility from the Georgia Department of Veterans Services. This shall be verified by the office of the probate court of the county. Only one free permit shall be issued to each veteran and it shall authorize the operation of only one business. The veteran must be the sole owner of the business for which the permit is issued and meet all prescribed codes and regulations as set forth by the board of commissioners.
(Ord. No. 81-1021, § IV, 10-21-81)
Sec. 3-3-29. - Permit, etc., for exempt businesses.
A person who is exempt from paying a license fee to the county shall obtain a permit from the county license department. Such a permit shall be issued without charge upon the applicant submitting proper and lawful credentials which show that the applicant is exempt from paying the license fee. Such a person shall comply with all ordinances governing his business or occupation.
(Ord. No. 81-1021, § XIII, 10-21-81)
Sec. 3-3-30. - Home occupations.
Any business being operated in a private home and in a residential area, will be known as home occupation. No license shall be prorated for a home occupation. Such a license may be suspended by the business license department when it is proven that the health, welfare or convenience of the residents in the immediate area is adversely affected.
(Ord. No. 81-1021, § XIV, 10-21-81)
Sec. 3-3-31. - Compliance with zoning and other ordinances.
No license shall be issued if the proposed location of the business to be licensed does not comply with the county's zoning ordinance and all other applicable ordinances.
Sec. 3-3-32. - Suspension or revocation.
(a)
Home occupation licenses. Any business being operated in a private home and in a residential area, will be known as home occupation. No license shall be prorated for a home occupation. Such a license may be suspended by the business license department when it is proven that the health, welfare or convenience of the residents in the immediate area is adversely affected.
(b)
Suspension authority; causes. The license department is authorized to suspend any license issued pursuant to the provisions of this article whenever a person doing business deviates from the normal operation for which the license was purchased, or fails in performance to meet the required regulations and codes set forth by the building inspection, police, fire or health departments, or violates any law or ordinance of the United States, of the state or the county in pursuance of such business conducted under such license, or when it is proven before the license department that there is a violation of the nuisance law.
(c)
Notice; hearing. The license department shall not suspend a license prior to notice to the license holder and a hearing before the director of the building and inspection department unless the holder of the license is endangering or harming the public through the operation of that business. In the event that a license is suspended under the "harm" to the public [provision] of this section, a hearing shall be held before the director of the building and inspection within three (3) days from the date the license is suspended. All other hearings shall be held within ten (10) days from the date of notice of hearing.
(d)
Appeals. A licensee may appeal the decision of the director to the board of commissioners within five (5) days of the date of his decision by written notice of appeal filed with the clerk of the board of commissioners.
(Ord. No. 81-1021, § VII, 10-21-81; Ord. No. 87-11)
Sec. 3-3-33. - Reserved.

Sec. 3-3-35. - Portable eating establishments.
(a)
Portable eating establishment is defined as any structure which is capable of being relocated in its entirety on wheels with axles or in any other manner and which does not sit on a foundation designed for the establishment as a permanent structure.
(b)
License conditions. Licenses may be issued in accordance with the following provisions:
(1)
The licensee shall be a person of good moral character or, in the case of other business entities, the individuals of the business entities who have any ownership interest shall be persons of good moral character.
(2)
Licenses shall be issued to individual applicants, corporations or other business entities. Ownership interest must be disclosed on the application. Failure to disclose ownership interest at any time during the term of the license shall be cause for revocation.
(3)
The establishment must be an accessory to an existing business. The establishment shall be allowed only in C-1, C-2 or C-3 zoning districts.
(4)
The applicant must have written permission from the property owner where the establishment is to be located.
(5)
The establishment shall be located on a paved surface and shall be located in the front of an existing business or on the side of the existing business and shall be plainly visible to all traffic. The establishment must be placed at least twenty (20) feet from any other structure.
(6)
The establishment shall be at least ten (10) feet from existing rights-of-way and shall be located so that it will not impede traffic flow or obscure vision of oncoming motorists. The establishment shall provide off-street parking.
(7)
Electrical service shall be from existing business.
(8)
No outside storage shall be allowed for the establishment which shall include but not be limited to picnic tables.
(9)
The establishment must provide health department approval to Henry County officials within ten (10) days from the issuance of a temporary license. The license shall be immediately revoked if health department approval is not received within ten (10) days.
(10)
Portable signs shall not be allowed in connection with the establishment business.
(11)
The establishment must be skirted completely to conceal the undercarriage.
(c)
Fee. The fee for the license shall be two hundred fifty dollars ($250.00), which shall be paid at the time the application is made and the fee shall be returned if the license is not issued to applicant.
(Ord. No. 85-18, 10-15-85)

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---

Sec. 3-3-46. - Door to door canvassing and soliciting prohibited; exceptions.
It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in business as a canvasser or solicitor, calling on the residences within the unincorporated areas of the county for the purpose of soliciting orders, sales, subscriptions, or business of any kind, without first registering with the revenue collection officer and paying the appropriate regulatory fee. Each registrant shall furnish his/her name and permanent address, his/her signature, the name and address of his/her employer, the nature of products sold or displayed, and the proposed method of operation within the county. Each registrant shall be fingerprinted and photographed by an employee of the county.
(1)
Regulatory fee; identity cards.
(a)
The revenue collection officer shall collect a fee of one hundred dollars ($100.00) for each registration. Upon approval, a registration card will be issued showing the name of the firm or corporation and the name of the representative. Such registration shall be valid for ninety (90) days from the date of issuance. The registration may be renewed during the same calendar year for an additional ninety-day period without another investigation or additional fees.
(b)
Each registrant shall be issued an identity card bearing his/her name and photograph, the company name, and the expiration date of the registration. Each solicitor must carry such identity card at all times while soliciting or canvassing within the county and shall display such card to each customer and upon appearance at each residence and/or business establishment canvassed or solicited.
(2)
Hours of operation; other requirements.
(a)
Soliciting or canvassing on the public streets, areas, or parks of the county shall be conducted only between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 7:00 p.m.
(b)
Soliciting or canvassing or calling from house to house within the unincorporated areas of the county shall be conducted only between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m.
(c)
The number of solicitors or canvassers in the county for any single firm, corporation, or organization shall not exceed ten (10) in number at any one (1) time.
(d)
Prior to any solicitation of funds within the county, each canvasser or solicitor shall identify the organization which he represents. Additionally, each canvasser or solicitor must inform each person solicited of any minimum payment, deposit, or donation required for the acceptance of any merchandise, wares, goods, or any similar items provided by each canvasser or solicitor prior to such acceptance by each person solicited.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for a canvasser or solicitor to perform any of the following acts:
1.
Falsely represent, either directly or by implication, that funds being solicited are on behalf of any person other than the person registered with the business license division;
2.
Without the express prior permission of an occupant or property owner, to solicit at any residence, apartment complex, or shopping center, other than areas open to public parking, where a sign has been posted prohibiting such solicitation;
3.
To remain on private premises after being asked to leave the premises or to continue solicitation after being refused upon the public streets, areas, or parks; such action shall constitute harassment; or
4.
To solicit or canvass on any private premises upon which is displayed a sign, plaque or other posting declaring" "No Soliciting" or other similar prohibition.
(3)
Special requirements for religious or charitable soliciting.
(a)
Any person desiring to solicit or canvass upon the public streets, areas, or parks, or call from house to house within the unincorporated areas of the county for the purpose of raising funds or seeking donations for any religious, charitable, or eleemosynary organization shall register with and obtain a license from the county. Such person shall furnish the following information to the business license division:
1.
Applicant's name and permanent address;
2.
Name, address, and telephone number of the firm, corporation, or organization represented;
3.
Names and addresses of all persons canvassing or soliciting within the county;
4.
The nature of any merchandise or goods to be sold or offered for sale in conjunction with such solicitation; and
5.
Proof of tax-exempt status shall be required when registering with the business license division.
a.
The business license division shall collect a fee of one dollar ($1.00) from each organization to cover costs of processing the license. Each organization shall be issued an identity card bearing the name of each individual who shall engage in solicitation or canvassing on behalf of the organization, the organization's name, and the expiration date of the license. Each applicant does not have to be fingerprinted or photographed.
b.
The license shall be valid for ninety (90) days from the date of issuance.
(4)
License revocation. Any license issued under this article may be suspended and/or revoked by the board of commissioners, revenue collection officer, business license director, sheriff, due to any violation of any ordinance or resolution of the county, or of any state or federal law, or whenever the license holder shall cease to possess the qualifications and character required in this article for the original application.
(5)
Activity not regulated hereby. This section is not intended to, nor shall it operate to, regulate door-to-door visitation for the following purposes: political canvassing or religious canvassing, provided that such canvassing does not include the soliciting of orders, sales, subscriptions or business of any kind.
(6)
Penalty.
(a)
Any person violating any of the provisions of this article shall, upon conviction or entering a plea of guilty or nolo contendere in the Henry County Magistrate Court, shall be punished by a fine of not more than five hundred dollars ($500.00) or by confinement in the common jail of the county for a period of not more than sixty (60) days or by both fine and confinement.
(b)
Continued violation of the provisions of this article may be enjoined by instituting appropriate proceedings for injunction in a court of competent jurisdiction of this state. Such actions may be maintained notwithstanding that other adequate remedies at law may exist. Remedies contained in this article are meant to be cumulative in nature.
(Ord. No. 84-01, § 1, 1-17-84; Ord. No. 87-18, 10-20-87; Ord. No. 04-09, § 1, 8-18-2004)

ARTICLE IV. - EXCISE TAX ON ROOMS, LODGINGS AND ACCOMMODATIONS[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— County excise tax on rooms, lodgings or accommodations, Official Code of Georgia Annotated § 48-13-50 et seq.

Sec. 3-3-66. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Due date: From the twentieth day after the close of the monthly period for which tax is to be computed.
Guest room: A room occupied, or intended, arranged, or designed for occupancy, by one or more occupants for the purpose of living quarters or residential use.
Hotel: Any structure or any portion of a structure, including any lodging house, rooming house, dormitory, turkish bath, bachelor hotel, studio hotel, motel, motor hotel, auto court, inn, public club, or private club, containing guest rooms and which is occupied, or is intended or designed for occupancy, by guests, whether rent is paid in money, goods, labor, or otherwise. The term does not include any jail, hospital, asylum, sanitarium, orphanage, prison, detention, or other buildings in which human beings are housed and detained under legal restraint.
Monthly period: The calendar months of any year.
Occupancy: The use or possession, or the right to the use or possession of the furnishings or to the services and accommodations accompanying the use and possession of the room.
Occupant: Any person who, for a consideration, uses, possesses, or has the right to use or possess any room in a hotel under any lease, concession, permit, right of access, license to use or other agreement, or otherwise.
Operator: Any person operating a hotel in the county, including, but not limited to, the owner or proprietor of such premises, lessee, sublessee, lender in possession, licensee or any other person otherwise operating such hotel.
Permanent resident: Any occupant as of a given date who has or shall have occupied or has or shall have the right of occupancy of any guest room in a hotel for at least ten (10) consecutive days next preceding such date.
Person: An individual, firm, partnership, joint association, social club, fraternal organization, joint stock company, corporation, nonprofit corporation or cooperative nonprofit membership, estate, trust, business trust, receiver, trustee, syndicate, or any other group or combination acting as a unit, the plural as well as the singular number, excepting the United States of America, the state, and any political subdivision of either, upon which the county is without power to impose the tax herein provided.
Rent: The consideration received for occupancy valued in money, whether received in money or otherwise, including all receipts, cash, credits, and property or services of any kind or nature, and also the amount for which credit is allowed by the operator to the occupant, without any deduction therefrom whatsoever.
Return: Any return filed or required to be filed as provided in this article.
Tax: The tax imposed by this article.
(Ord. No. 82-2, § 1, 2-16-82)
Sec. 3-3-67. - Scope.
This article shall apply only to the unincorporated areas of the county.
(Ord. No. 82-2, § 1, 2-16-82)
Sec. 3-3-68. - Levied; amount.
There is hereby levied and there shall be paid to the governing authority a tax of five (5) percent of the rental paid for occupancy of every guest room in a hotel or motel in the county.
(Ord. No. 82-2, § 2, 2-16-82; Ord. No. 92-01, 1-7-92)
Sec. 3-3-69. - Collection of tax by operator.
(a)
Every operator maintaining a hotel or motel in the county and renting guest rooms, not exempted under this article, shall collect a tax of five (5) percent of the amount of the rental paid by each occupant.
(b)
Proceeds of the tax. The proceeds of this tax shall be used in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(a)(3).
(Ord. No. 82-2, § 3, 2-16-82; Ord. No. 92-01, 1-7-92; Ord. No. 07-08, § I, 11-5-07)
Sec. 3-3-70. - Exemptions.
No tax shall be imposed pursuant to this article with respect to occupancy by a permanent resident.
(Ord. No. 82-2, § 4, 2-16-82)
Sec. 3-3-71. - Registration of operator; form and contents; execution; certificate of authority.
(a)
Every person engaging in or about to engage in business as an operator of a hotel in this county shall immediately register with the tax commissioner, on a form provided by the tax commissioner. Such registration shall set forth the name under which such person transacts business or intends to transact business, the location of this place or places of business and such other information which would facilitate the collection of the tax as the tax commissioner may require. The registration shall be signed by the owner if a natural person; in case of ownership by an association or partnership, by a member or a partner; in the case of ownership by a corporation, by an officer thereof.
(b)
The tax commissioner shall, after such registration, issue without charge a certificate of authority to each operator to collect the tax from the occupant. A separate registration shall be required for each place of business of an operator. Each certificate shall state the name and location of the business to which it is applicable.
(Ord. No. 82-2, § 5, 2-16-82)
Sec. 3-3-72. - Determination, returns and payments.
(a)
All amounts of taxes shall be due and payable to the tax commissioner monthly on or before the twentieth day of every month next succeeding each respective monthly period.
(b)
On or before the twentieth day of the month following each monthly period, a return for the preceding monthly period shall be filed with the tax commissioner showing the gross rent, rent from permanent residents, taxable rent, amount of tax collected or otherwise due for the related period, and such other information as may be required by said officer.
(c)
Operators collecting the tax shall be allowed a percentage of the tax due and accounted for and shall be reimbursed in the form of a deduction in submitting, reporting and payment of the amount due, if said amount is not delinquent at the time of payment. The rate of the deduction shall be the same rate authorized for deductions from state tax under the "Georgia Retailers' and `Consumers' Sales and Use Tax Act", approved February 20, 1951 (Ga. Laws, 1951, p. 360), as now or hereafter amended.
(Ord. No. 82-2, § 6, 2-16-82)
State Law reference— Authorized deduction, Official Code of Georgia Annotated § 48-8-50.
Sec. 3-3-73. - Deficiency determinations.
(a)
If the tax commissioner of the county is not satisfied with the return of the tax or the amount of the tax required to be paid to the board of commissioners by any person, said officer may compute and determine the amount required to be paid upon the basis of any information within his possession or that may come into his possession. One or more deficiency determinations may be made of the account due for one or more monthly periods.
(b)
There shall be imposed a specific penalty to be added to the amount of the determination of the taxes due in the amount of five (5) percent or five dollars ($5.00), whichever is greater, for each ten (10) day period or fraction thereof from the date the taxes were due.
(c)
The tax commissioner, or his designated representatives, shall give to the operator written notice of his determination. The notice may be served personally or by mail; if by mail, such service shall be addressed to the operator at the address as it appears in the records of the tax commissioner. Service by mail is complete when delivered by certified mail with a receipt signed by addressee.
(d)
Except in the case of failure to make a return, every notice of a deficiency determination shall be mailed within three (3) years after the twentieth day of the calendar month following the monthly period for which the amount is proposed to be determined, or within three (3) years after the return is filed, whichever period should last expire.
(Ord. No. 82-2, § 7, 2-16-82; Ord. No. 93-09, 11-16-93)
Sec. 3-3-74. - Determination if no return made.
(a)
If any person fails to make a return, the tax commissioner shall make an estimate of the amount of the gross receipts of the person, or as the case may be, of the amount of the total rentals in this county which are subject to the tax. There shall be imposed a specific penalty to be added to the amount of the determination of the taxes due in the amount of five (5) percent or five dollars ($5.00) whichever is greater, for each ten (10) day period or fraction thereof from the date the taxes were due.

Sec. 3-3-75. - Administration.
(a)
The county administrator shall administer and enforce the provisions of this article for the collection of the tax imposed by this article.
(b)
Every operator renting guest rooms in this county to a person shall keep such records, receipts, invoices, and other pertinent papers in such form as the tax commissioner may require.
(c)

In administration of the provisions of this article, the tax commissioner may require the filing of reports by any persons or class of persons having in their possession or custody information relating to rentals of guest rooms which are subject to the tax. Upon a reasonable belief that an operator has made no return or a deficient return, or a determination of deficiency has been made by the tax commissioner, the county administrator, upon notice to the operator, may request a hearing before the board of commissioners of Henry County requiring the operator to show cause why its business license should not be revoked or suspended. The notice may be served personally or by mail; if by mail, such service shall be addressed to the operator at the address as it appears in the records of the tax commissioner. Service by mail is complete when delivered by certified mail with a receipt signed by addressee. Notice shall be given at least ten (10) days prior to the date of the hearing. The operator may appear at the hearing personally or through an attorney, may cross examine the county's witnesses, may present evidence or otherwise be heard. The board of commissioners of Henry County may revoke or suspend the operator's business license upon a finding to the preponderance of the evidence that the operator has made a deficient return of the tax, has failed to pay the full amount due, or has failed to make a return.

At any time within three (3) years after any tax or any amount of tax required to be collected becomes due and payable and at any time within three (3) years after the delinquency of any tax or any amount of tax required to be collected, the tax commissioner may bring an action in a court of competent jurisdiction in the name of the board of commissioners to collect the amount delinquent together with interest, court fees, filing fees, attorney's fees and other legal fees incident thereto.
(b)
If any operator liable for any amount under this article sells out his business or quits the business, his successors or assigns shall withhold sufficient of the purchase price to cover such amount until the former owner produces a receipt from the tax commissioner showing that he has been paid or a certificate stating that no amount is due.
(c)
If the purchase[r] of a business fails to withhold purchase price as required, he shall be personally liable for the payment of the amount required to be withheld by him to the extent of the purchase price.
(d)
Whenever the amount of any tax or interest has been paid more than once, or has been erroneously or illegally collected or received by the board of commissioners under this article, it may be offset by the tax commissioner. If the operator or person determines that he has overpaid or paid more than once, which fact has not been determined by the tax commissioner, he will have three (3) years from the date of payment to file claim in writing stating the specific ground upon which claim is founded. Sec. 3-3-101. - Application.
(a)
Before any cable television system does any work in the county for the purpose of operating a cable television system, the owner and/or operators of the cable television system are required to make an application for a franchise for the nonexclusive right to erect, construct, maintain and operate a community antenna television system. The owners and/or operator shall make an application in writing to the licensing department of the county.
(b)
The franchise may be issued in accordance with the following provisions:
(1)
The applicant shall pay a six thousand five hundred dollar ($6,500.00) nonrefundable application fee which shall be payable and submitted at the time the application is submitted.
(2)
The franchisee shall be a person of good moral character. The franchisee shall provide to the county, at the time of the application, the nature of the legal entity of the franchisee, character references, a complete financial disclosure form and financial statement regarding the assets and liabilities of the franchisee entity, a resume of technical skills and knowledge in the field of community antenna television systems and feasibility study of the proposed cable television system including engineer's drawings providing specifications for construction and erection of the system and estimated costs of the system.
(3)
Franchises may be issued to individual applicants, corporations or other business entities. All ownership interest must be disclosed on the application. Failure to disclose any ownership interest at any time during the term of the franchise shall be cause for revocation.
(4)
The franchise, if granted, shall be for a period of fifteen (15) years. A renewal option for successive fifteen-year periods shall be included in the franchise agreement; however, the option shall be renewed by the county only after public hearing.
(5)
The franchise, if granted after public hearing, shall specify that the franchisee shall accomplish significant construction within one year from date of agreement and the franchisee shall thereafter reasonably make cable service available to a substantial percentage of its franchise area each year. The board of commissioners shall determine percentage based upon population, number of houses and any factors relevant to the area to be served. If the franchise granted does not require the complete wiring of the franchise areas, this provision shall be adopted only after full public hearings, which includes specific notice of the consideration of this policy. The board of commissioners may require the posting of a bond or certified check in an amount and with surety conditions satisfactory to the board of commissioners, providing for and securing to the governing body an amount to provide for the cost of significant construction.
(6)
The franchise fee shall be determined by the board of commissioners at the time of the public hearing and shall be reviewed by the board of commissioners on an annual basis thereafter and is subject to change upon notice to the franchisee and public hearing. However, beginning January 1, 2008, the franchise fee rate applicable to all cable operators doing business in Henry County under a local franchise agreement shall be five (5) percent of the cable operators' "gross revenues" as that term is defined by Georgia law.
(7)
The county board of commissioners shall have the right to impose any tax, fee or assessment of general applicability, on a cable operator or its services. Such fee is in addition to the regular franchise fee. A written notice will be given to the franchisees of the proposal of such tax, fee or assessment, and a hearing may be held at the request of the franchisees.
(Ord. No. 85-21, § 1, 12-3-85; Ord. No. 07-10, 12-3-07)

For the purpose of this article, the following terms, phrases, words, abbreviations and their derivations shall have the same meanings given herein. Collecting and amplifying local and distant broadcast television or radio signals and distributing and transmitting them;
(2)
Transmitting original cablecast programming not received from television broadcast signals;

Transmitting television pictures; film and video tape programs, not received through broadcast television channels, whether or not encoded or processed to permit reception by only selected receivers; and

Transmitting and receiving all other signals, including digital, voice and audio-visual.
Provided, however, that any of the services permitted hereunder to be performed, described in this paragraph, shall be those performed by or through the franchisee for subscribers, as herein defined, in the operation of a cable television system as franchised by Henry County.
Converter means an electronic device which converts signals from a frequency not susceptible to reception by the television receiver of the subscriber to a frequency which is susceptible of reception, and by an appropriate channel selector also permits the subscriber to view all signals delivered at designated dial locations.
County means Henry County, Georgia.

Sec. 3-3-104. - Modification.
(a)
Cable operators shall have the right to request modification of franchise requirements. A replacement or alternative to the existing requirement must be submitted, and it must be shown that such replacement or alternative is appropriate, because of the same commercial impracticability. All requests to modify, eliminate or substitute services must be in writing, and must be accompanied by the required showing of commercial impracticability and proposed alternatives in the case of a request for modification or by a demonstration of the mix, quality and level of services to be rendered after an elimination or substitution of services. Sec. 3-3-130. - Same—Notice and hearing.
(a)
No license issued under this article shall be suspended, revoked or placed on probation with or without conditions, except for due cause as defined in subsection (b) hereof and only after a hearing before the board of commissioners upon a prior written notice to the licensee of the time, place and purpose of such hearing and a statement of the reason why the license may be suspended or revoked. Three (3) days' notice shall be deemed reasonable, but shorter or longer periods of notice shall be authorized as the board of commissioners may deem the circumstances justify.
(b)
"Due cause" for the suspension or revocation of such license shall consist of any of the following:
(1)
A licensee commits a deceptive business practice when he: (i) uses or possesses a false weight, measure or any other device for falsely determining or recording any quality or quantity in connection with any sale or service; (ii) sells, offers, exposes for sale or delivers less than the represented quality or quantity; (iii) takes or attempts to take more than the represented quality or quantity of any commodity when, as buyer or broker, he furnishes the weight or measure; (iv) provides a service which is of an unreasonably lesser quality than the service offered or represented; (v) engages in any other fraudulent business transaction which is made punishable by the laws of the State of Georgia;
(2)

(3)
The licensee has supplied false information to the licenses and permits office or board of commissioners regardless of when the county shall have become aware of same;

The licensee has failed to obtain or has suffered the expiration, suspension or revocation of any paper or document necessary to pursue its business as may be required by any office, agency, authority or department of the county, state or the United States under authority of any law, ordinance or resolution of the county, state or the United States;
(5)
The licensee has failed to comply with any provisions of this article;
(6)
A licensee makes any misrepresentation of fact, whether through advertisement or through any form of direct communication oral or written, which is intended to mislead the public or to mislead any party with whom the licensee deals. The term "misrepresentation of fact" as used herein shall embrace not only express misrepresentations; but, also misrepresentations arising by virtue of the licensee's conduct, including acts and omissions. By way of illustration only and without limiting the scope of this subsection, "due cause" as herein defined shall consist of any act or practice designated as unlawful in sections 3 (b)(1) through 3 (b)(11) of the Fair Business Practices Act of 1975 (OCGA 10-1-390 et seq.) or declared by the Administrator of said Act to be unlawful pursuant to regulations made under section 4 of said Act, subject to the exemptions contained in Section 6 of said Act; or

Any licensee who has been charged with the offense of theft by receiving stolen property as contemplated under the provisions of OCGA section 16-8-7. It is not necessary for the licensee to be convicted under the statute.

The annual license fee for licenses hereunder shall be set by the board of commissioners.
(b)
No license shall be issued for less than a calendar year period and in case of the revocation or surrender of such license before the expiration of such calendar year period the licensee shall not be entitled to receive any refund.
(c)
All licenses shall be issued for the calendar year beginning January 1 and the fees prescribed by the board of commissioners shall be paid in full by each person prior to issuance of a license.
(d)
Any license issued under this article shall expire on December 31 of the calendar year for which it is issued.
(e)
No license fee shall be prorated for any business commenced after January 1 of any calendar year and full amount of the annual license fee as prescribed by the board of commissioners shall be payable for any business commenced after January 1 of any calendar year.
(Ord. No. 92-07, 9-1-92)
Sec. 3-3-134. - License to authorize operation of one place of business.
Each license shall authorize the operation of one place of business only and persons doing business in more than one place within unincorporated Henry County shall obtain a separate license, paying a separate fee, for each such place of business.
(Ord. No. 92-07, 9-1-92)
Sec. 3-3-135. - Change of location.
Any person moving from one location to another shall notify the licenses and permits office of such move and of the new address in writing, on a form provided by the county, no later than thirty (30) days before the day of moving. A new license shall be issued upon payment of a fee in an amount to be set by the board of commissioners if the new location conforms to the county's zoning regulations.
(Ord. No. 92-07, 9-1-92)
Sec. 3-3-136. - License to be displayed.
All licenses issued under this article shall be posted conspicuously in the place of business of the person to whom the license is issued.
(Ord. No. 92-07, 9-1-92)
Sec. 3-3-137. - Time limit for commencement of business in licensed establishment; forfeiture for nonuse.
(a)
All holders of licenses hereunder must, within three (3) months after the issuance of said license, open for business the establishment referred to in the license unless the time is extended by the board of commissioners. Failure to open the licensed establishment within three (3) months shall operate as an automatic forfeiture and cancellation of the unused license and no refund of license fees shall be made to the licensee.
(b)
Any licensee who begins the operation of business and then ceases to operate the business for a period of three (3) consecutive months shall constitute a forfeiture of his license to operate the business. Said license shall, by virtue of said failure to operate, be canceled without the necessity for any further action of the board of commissioners. However, an extension, for good cause, may be granted.
(Ord. No. 92-07, 9-1-92)
Sec. 3-3-138. - Renewal.
Any person subject to this article shall register with the licenses and permits office and shall apply for renewal of any existing license on or before December 15 of each calendar year prior to the year in which he intends to do business.
(Ord. No. 92-07, 9-1-92)
Sec. 3-3-139. - Licenses constitute grant or privilege.
(a)
All licenses issued hereunder shall be a mere grant of a privilege to carry on a pawnshop business during the term authorized in the license subject to all the terms and conditions imposed by this article and any other ordinance, resolution or law of the county, state or United States relating to such business.
(b)
All licenses issued hereunder shall have printed on the front these words: "This license is a mere privilege subject to be revoked and annulled by the Board of Commissioners of Henry County and is subject to any future ordinances affecting the operation of this business."
(Ord. No. 92-07, 9-1-92)
Sec. 3-3-140. - Name of licensee and license number to be displayed.
Each licensee shall have printed on the front window of the licensed premises the inscription, "Henry County Pawnshop License Number _______," in uniform letters not less than three (3) inches in height.
(Ord. No. 92-07, 9-1-92)
Sec. 3-3-141. - Daily report to police.
(a)
Every licensee shall prepare a daily report in writing for the Henry County Police Department, in such form as may be prescribed by the Police Department, of all property pledged to or bought by the licensee during the preceding twenty-four (24) hours ending at 8:00 p.m. on the date of the report. Such report shall be typewritten or printed legibly. In addition to any other information required by the police department, the report shall disclose:
(1)
The name and address of the licensee;
(2)
Time of transaction;
(3)
Serial numbers of pawn tickets;
(4)
Amount paid or advanced;
(5)
Full description of articles, including kind, style, material, color, design, kind and number of stones in jewelry, and all identifying names, marks and numbers; and
(6)
A description of persons pledging, selling or pawning, including name, address, driver's license number, weight, height, and eye color.
Serial numbers on items must be included in the report. Insufficient or incomplete reports shall be rejected. Any licensee or employee who fails or refuses to prepare and keep reports as provided in this section or makes false entries concerning a transaction or who fails or refuses to permit an inspection or examination of the report by any police officer shall constitute a criminal offense, punishable as provided in section 3-3-148 hereof.
(b)
Each licensee shall require any person pawning, pledging or selling an article or articles to display a photo identification such as a duly issued driver's license or other similar identification. The licensee shall record the driver's license number or other number or feature of such identification.
(c)
The failure of any licensee or employee to comply with the provisions of this section shall constitute a criminal offense, punishable as provided in section 3-3-148 hereof.
(Ord. No. 92-07, 9-1-92)
Sec. 3-3-142. - Regulation as to employees.
No person shall be employed by a pawnshop in any capacity until such person has been finger printed by the police department and has been issued an annual personal identification card. A reasonable fee for the issuance of the annual personal identification shall be charged by Henry County Police Department. It shall be the duty of the licensee to assure the provisions of this section are strictly followed.
(Ord. No. 92-07, 9-1-92)
Sec. 3-3-143. - Adding merchandise to stock.
A licensee selling goods other than those pledged may add new merchandise to his stock of the same type as that on which loans are made, up to value of five thousand dollars ($5,000.00), without having to pay an additional license fee as may otherwise be imposed according to the terms of the business license ordinance adopted by the board of commissioners.
(Ord. No. 92-07, 9-1-92)
Sec. 3-3-144. - Articles not to be disposed of for thirty (30) days after acquisition.
Any licensee who takes goods on pawn or buys goods, taking full title thereto, the word "goods" being here used in the broadest sense and including all kinds of personal property, shall hold such goods so taken in pawn or purchased for at least thirty (30) days before disposing of same by sale, transfer, shipment or otherwise. Failure to comply with this section shall constitute a criminal offense, punishable according to section 3-3-148 hereof.
(Ord. No. 92-07, 9-1-92)
Sec. 3-3-145. - Dealing with minors.
It shall be unlawful for any licensee, his agents or employees, to receive in pawn, pledge or sale goods of any character or description from a minor. For the purposes of this article, a minor is any individual seventeen (17) years of age or under.
(Ord. No. 92-07, 9-1-92)
Sec. 3-3-146. - Sale of knives, blackjacks, other weapons.
It shall be unlawful for any licensee to sell, offer for sale or expose for sale any kinds of metal knucks, dirks, sword-in-cains, spears, bowie knives, switch blade knives, blackjacks or similar weapons. Any licensee or employee thereof violating this section shall constitute a criminal offense punishable as provided in section 3-3-148 hereof.
(Ord. No. 92-07, 9-1-92)
Sec. 3-3-147. - Failure to obtain license, permission for transfer; penalty.
Any person who fails to apply for and obtain a license as required in this article or fails to obtain permission for transfer of an existing license, shall pay a penalty of ten (10) percent of the license fee. Said penalty shall be imposed for each thirty-day period or portion thereof, which the person conducts business while failing to comply with the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 92-07, 9-1-92)
Sec. 3-3-148. - Violation; penalties.
(a)
Any person violating any of the provisions of this article shall, upon conviction or entering a plea of guilty or nolo contendere in the Henry County Magistrate Court, shall be punished by a fine of not more than five hundred dollars ($500.00) or by confinement in the common jail of the county for a period of not more than sixty (60) days or by both fine and confinement.
(b)
Continued violation of the provisions of this article may be enjoined by instituting appropriate proceedings for injunction in a court of competent jurisdiction of this state. Such actions may be maintained notwithstanding that other adequate remedies at law may exist. Remedies contained in this article are meant to be cumulative in nature.
(Ord. No. 92-07, 9-1-92)
Sec. 3-3-149. - Collection of unpaid fees.
Any person who fails or neglects to pay any license fee as required by this article is subject to the collection in the same manner as provided for the collection of taxes, regardless of the provisions herein for penalty.
(Ord. No. 92-07, 9-1-92)
Secs. 3-3-150—3-3-200. - Reserved.
ARTICLE VII. - SHORT-TERM RENTALS

Sec. 3-3-201. - Purpose and applicability.
The purpose of this article is to establish regulations for the use of short-term rentals for residential living units. The requirements of this article apply only to short-term rental, as defined herein, located in residential zoning districts established as per the Henry County Unified Land Development Code. Nothing in this article, however, shall be construed to be a waiver of the requirement to assess and collect hotel occupancy taxes for any residential rental for no more than thirty (30) consecutive days, or any other applicable provision of the Henry County Code of Ordinances.
( Ord. No. 22-10, § I, 10-4-22)
Sec. 3-3-202. - Definitions.
Advertise means the written, audio, oral or other methods of drawing the public's attention whether by brochure, written literature or online posting to a short-term rental in order to promote the availability of the short-term rental.
Hotel occupancy tax means the tax as defined in chapter 3-3, article IV of the Henry County Code of Ordinances.
Local emergency contact means an individual other than the applicant, who resides within twenty (20) miles of the subject property, and who is designated by the owner/applicant to act as the owner's authorized agent if the owner has traveled outside of the immediate area or is otherwise unavailable. The local emergency contact should be reachable on a twenty-four-hour basis, have access to the short-term rental property, and be authorized by the owner to act in the owner's absence to address any complaints, disturbances, and emergencies.
Owner means any person having a legal or equitable interest in the property; or recorded in the official records of the state, county or municipality as holding title to the property; or otherwise having control of the property, including the guardian of the estate of any such person, and the executor or code official of the estate of such person if ordered to take possession of real property by a court. The term "owner" does not include the holder of a nonpossessory security interest in the property.
Party means a social gathering of people, including those who have not rented the residence, for the primary purpose of eating, drinking or entertaining in a manner that constitutes a nuisance to surrounding property owners in accordance with chapter 3-16, nuisances.
Primary residence means the usual dwelling place of the applicant's residential dwelling and is documented as such by the applicant's driver's license and one (1) of the following: Motor vehicle registration, Georgia State Identification card, voter registration, property tax documents, or utility bill. For purposes of this article, a person may have only one (1) primary residence.
Short-term rental (STR) means the rental of any residence or residential structure, or a portion of a residence or residential structure for a period of no more than thirty (30) days. The term does not include:
(1)
A unit that is used for a nonresidential purpose, including, but not limited to, institutional care, educational uses, health care, retail, restaurant, banquet space, event center purpose, or other similar use;
(2)
A bed and breakfast;
(3)
A boarding and rooming house; or
(4)
A hotel/residence hotel or motel.
( Ord. No. 22-10, § I, 10-4-22)
Sec. 3-3-203. - Short-term rental application requirements.
No person shall hereafter advertise, offer to rent, lease, license or sublicense a residential property within unincorporated Henry County as a short-term rental for which an occupational tax certificate has not been properly filed for and obtained through the Henry County Occupational Tax department.
Short-term rental occupational tax certificate shall be made upon forms furnished by the occupational tax department for such purpose and shall specifically require the following minimum information:
(1)
Name, address, phone number and e-mail address of the property owner of the short-term rental property;
(2)
Verification that the short-term rental application is for property within unincorporated Henry County;
a.
A short-term rental business license may be applied for up to three (3) dwelling units without additional requirements, fees, permits, or related restrictions.
(3)
Name, address, phone number and e-mail address of the designated local emergency contact.
(4)
The number of bedrooms and square footage of each.
(5)
A signed affidavit from a licensed building inspector, attesting that the property meets all applicable building and life safety codes.
(6)
Informational brochure.
(7)
Short-term rental properties located within a subdivision with an active HOA shall provide written approval from the HOA.
( Ord. No. 22-10, § I, 10-4-22)
Sec. 3-3-204. - Right to inspect premises.
The county reserves the right, with reasonable notice to the owner, to inspect the residential premises to determine compliance with this section as well as with the current adopted version of the International Property Maintenance Code (IPMC). The Henry County Building, Code Enforcement, Fire and Police Departments are authorized to enforce the provisions of this article, including inspection of the residential premises.
(1)
If only a portion of the premises is offered for rent, then that portion and any shared amenities, areas, and points of access may be inspected.
(2)
If, upon completion of an inspection, the premises are found to be in violation of one (1) or more provisions of applicable Codes and County Ordinances, the county shall provide written notice of such violation and shall set a re-inspection date for a violation to be corrected prior to its occupancy.
( Ord. No. 22-10, § I, 10-4-22)
Sec. 3-3-205. - Standards.
The following standards shall apply to short-term rental:
(1)
Rentals shall be no more than thirty (30) days.
(2)
The business owner shall post emergency contact information (police, fire, hospital) and show renters the location of fire extinguishers in the short-term rental.
(3)
A business owner must provide the name, address, and phone number for the managing agent or local contact to all property owners within three hundred (300) feet of the property boundary. The business owner shall provide certified mailed notice to all property owners within one hundred (100) feet of the property boundary within ten (10) days of a change in the managing agent or local contact's contact information.
(4)
A business owner must disclose in writing to their renters the following information:
a.
The managing agent or local contact's name, address, and phone number.
b.
The maximum number of guests allowed at the property as determined by county officials.
c.
The maximum number of vehicles, recreational vehicles, and trailers allowed at the property and where they are to be parked.
d.
Property rules related to use of exterior features of the property, such as decks, patios, grills, recreational fires, pools, hot tubs, saunas and other outdoor recreational facilities.
e.
Applicable sections of county ordinances governing noise and parking.
(5)
Additional occupancy by use of recreational vehicles, tents, or accessory structures is not permitted, except legally permitted accessory dwelling units.
(6)
The business owner shall keep a report; detailing use of the short-term rental by recording the full name, address, phone number and vehicle license number of guests using the rental. A copy of the report shall be provided to county upon request.
(7)
A business owner must post their license number on all print, poster or web advertisements.
(8)
Shall comply with chapter 3-16, nuisances and chapter 3-17, article IV, noise control of the Henry County Code of Ordinances.
( Ord. No. 22-10, § I, 10-4-22)
Sec. 3-3-206. - Restrictions on short-term rentals.
(a)
External signage. There shall be no external on-site or off-site advertising signs or displays indicating the property is a short-term rental.
(b)
Limits on number of vehicles. There shall be a maximum of one (1) car per bedroom, or the maximum number of cars that can be accommodated within any garage(s), carport(s), and driveway. Vehicles shall be located on a hard surface or gravel and outside of any and all easements, and shall not encroach on any public or private streets, right-of-way, sidewalks, drainage areas, or similar.
(c)
Sublease. No property shall be subleased for the purpose of a short-term rental.
(d)
Other restrictions. It shall be unlawful:
(1)
To operate or allow to be operated a short-term rental without first receiving an approved occupational tax certificate, in accordance with this article, the property in which the rental is to occur;
(2)
To advertise or offer a short-term rental without first receiving an approved occupational tax certificate, in accordance with this article, the property in which the rental is to occur; documented advertisement of the subject property as a short-term rental, online or offline, shall be considered evidence of a violation of this article;
(3)
To operate a short-term rental that does not comply with all applicable county and state laws and codes;
(4)
To operate a short-term rental without paying the required hotel occupancy taxes;
(5)
To offer or allow the use of a short-term rental for the sole or primary purpose of having a party venue;
(6)
To operate the residence in a manner that constitutes a nuisance to surrounding property owners in accordance with chapter 3-16, nuisances;
(7)
To fail to include a written prohibition against the use of a short-term rental for having a party venue in every advertisement, listing, or other publication offering the premises for rent;
(8)
Permit the use of short-term rental for the purpose of: operating a structured sober, recovery or other purpose living home or similar enterprise; selling illegal drugs; selling alcohol or another activity that requires a permit or license, or operating as a sexually oriented business; and
(9)
To operate or allow the property to be operated or serve as a pick-up or drop-off location for a commercial vehicle transportation service.
( Ord. No. 22-10, § I, 10-4-22)
Sec. 3-3-207. - Brochure and safety features.
(a)
Informational brochure. Each business owner operating a short-term rental shall provide to guests a brochure that includes:
(1)
The business owner's twenty-four-hour contact information;
(2)
A local responsible party's twenty-four-hour contract information if the owner is not within the county limits when guests are renting the premises;
(3)
Pertinent neighborhood information including, but not limited to, parking restrictions, restrictions on noise and amplified sound, and trash collection schedules; and
(4)
Information to assist guests in the case of emergencies posing threats to personal safety or damage to property, including emergency and non-emergency telephone numbers for police, fire and emergency medical services providers and instructions for obtaining severe weather, natural or manmade disaster alerts and updates.
( Ord. No. 22-10, § I, 10-4-22)
Sec. 3-3-208. - License term, fees, and renewal.
(a)
All occupational tax certificates approved under this article shall be valid for a period of one (1) year from January 1 to December 31 or July 1 to June 31.
(b)
The fee for occupational tax certificate of a short-term rental shall be determined by using gross receipts with a sixty-dollar ($60.00) administration fee and the rate class fee in addition to any inspection fees.
(c)
Upon receipt of an application for renewal of the occupational tax certificate, the tax commissioner or their designee may deny the renewal if there is reasonable cause to believe that the applicant has plead no contest to or been convicted of a violation of any ordinance of the county, or any state, or fee law on the premises or has permitted such a violation on the premises by any other person.
( Ord. No. 22-10, § I, 10-4-22)
Sec. 3-3-209. - Violations and penalties.
(a)
Unless otherwise specified within a particular chapter of the Code of Henry County, Georgia, any person violating any of the provisions of this article or failure to comply with any of its requirements, shall, upon conviction or plea of guilty thereof, be punishable by a fine not to exceed one thousand dollars ($1,000.00), or by imprisonment of not more than sixty (60) days, or both, such fine and imprisonment as the facts of the case may justify. Upon the third violation for the same property, the county shall revoke any pending licenses and reject all applications for that particular property for a period of twenty-four (24) consecutive months. Each day that such condition continues shall be regarded as a new and separate offense.
(b)
All costs, attorney's fees, expert witness fees and other expenses incurred by the county in connection with the enforcement of this article shall be recovered from the violator.
( Ord. No. 22-10, § I, 10-4-22)
