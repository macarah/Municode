ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 24-1. - Title.
This chapter shall be entitled and known as the "2005 Hiawassee Police Powers Ordinance."
(Ord. No. 0162, art. I, § 1, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-2. - Patrol powers.
(a)
The police officers of the city shall have the authority to patrol over any street, alley, path, sidewalk, or park, in and around any stores, cafes, taverns, restaurants, dance halls, service stations or any other establishment in the city.
(b)
An officer, while on duty for the city, shall have authority to enter any type of business while open to the public and check to see if everything is being carried on in a lawful manner. After business hours, and while closed to the public, an officer for the city has authority to check places of business, to see if windows and doors are safely locked.
(c)
An officer for the city shall have the authority to check and investigate, enter and patrol any park, business, residential section or public place within the city limits if he has reason to believe some act is being committed which would involve the violation of a law or city ordinance, a personal injury or damage to property, or if any of the above would appear to be imminent.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. II, § 2, 5-3-2011)
Secs. 24-3—24-22. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - DRUG AND ALCOHOL OFFENSES

Sec. 24-23. - Possession of marijuana in quantity less than one ounce.
Pursuant to the authority granted in O.C.G.A. §§ 16-13-2 and 36-32-6, such sections are referred to and incorporated herein by reference. Any person convicted of a violation of this section shall be punished in accordance with section 1-13.
Sec. 24-24. - Open container.
(a)
Vehicles. It shall be unlawful for any person to possess in unsealed containers any alcoholic beverage on any street, road, alleyway, or other right-of-way within the city limits.
(b)
Other. It shall be unlawful for any person to possess in unsealed containers any alcoholic beverage upon any shopping center parking lot, business, or residential parking area, or any public place or way within the city limits.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. IV, §§ 3, 4, 5-3-2011)

ARTICLE III. - OFFENSES AGAINST PROPERTY

Sec. 24-52. - Littering on public and private property.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Litter means all sand, gravel, slag, brickbats, rubbish, waste, material, tin cans, refuse, garbage, trash, debris, dead animals or discarded materials of every kind and description.
Public or private property means the right-of-way of any road or highway; any body of water or watercourse or the shores or beaches thereof, any park, playground, building refuge, or conservation or recreation area; and residential, commercial, governmental, farm, properties, timberlands or forests.
(b)
Prohibitions. It shall be unlawful for any person to dump, deposit, throw, or leave or to cause or permit the dumping, deposition, placing, throwing, or leaving of litter on any public or private property or waters in the incorporated areas of the city, unless:
(1)
The property is designated by the city for the disposal of litter and the person is authorized by the proper public authority to use such property;
(2)
The litter is placed into a litter receptacle or container install on such property; or
(3)
The person is the owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property or has first obtained consent of the owner or tenant in lawful possession or unless the act is done under the personal direction of the owner or tenant, all in a manner consistent with the public welfare.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. III, § 1, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-53. - Criminal trespass.
(a)
A person commits the offense of criminal trespass when he intentionally damages any property of another without consent of that other person and the damage thereto is $500.00 or less or knowingly and maliciously interferes with the possession or use of the property of another without consent of that person.
(b)
A person commits the offense of criminal trespass when he knowingly and without authority:
(1)
Enters upon the land or premises of another person or into any part of any vehicle, railroad car, aircraft, or watercraft of another person for an unlawful purpose;
(2)
Enters upon the land or premises of another person or within the vehicle, railroad car, aircraft, or watercraft of another person after receiving, prior to such entry, notice from the owner, rightful occupant, or, upon proper identification, an authorized representative of the owner or rightful occupant such entry is forbidden; or
(3)
Remains upon the land or premises of another person or within the vehicle, railroad car, aircraft, or watercraft of another person after receiving notice from the owner, rightful occupant, or, upon proper identification, an authorized representative of the owner or rightful occupant to depart.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. III, § 2, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-54. - Destruction of mailboxes and mail.
It shall be unlawful for any person willfully or maliciously to injure, tear down, or destroy any mailbox or receptacle intended or used for the receipt or delivery of mail or willingly of maliciously to injure, deface, or destroy any mail deposited therein.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. III, § 3, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-55. - Criminal trespass by motor vehicle.
A person commits the offense of criminal trespass by motor vehicle when the person, after having been requested not to do so by a law enforcement officer or an authorized agent of the owner, parks or stands an occupied or unoccupied motor vehicle in, or repeatedly drives a motor vehicle through or within a parking area that is located on privately-owned property and is provided by a merchant, a group of merchants, or a shopping center or other similar facility for customers if:
(1)
The parking area is identified by at least one sign, as specified in this subsection, and if the parking area contains more than 150 parking spaces then by at least one such sign for every 150 parking spaces, each sigh containing the following information in easy to read printing:
a.
Notice of the elements of the crime of criminal trespass by motor vehicle;
b.
Identification of the property which is reserved for customer use;
c.
Identification of the merchants or shopping center or similar facility so identified;
d.
Warning that violators will be prosecuted.
(2)
A defense to an alleged violation of this section shall include:
a.
The motor vehicle is parked, or standing, or is being operated other than for the purpose of transporting some person to or from the interior of the place of business or a merchant identified by the sign or signs in the parking area or to or from the interior of the shopping center or other facility so identified;
b.
Making or use of a telephone, vending machine, automatic teller machine, or other similar facility located in the parking area; or
c.
Carrying out an activity for which express permission has been given by the owner of the parking area or an authorized representative of the owner.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. IV, § 8, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-56. - Damaging, injuring or interfering with municipal property, utilities or services.
(a)
A person commits the offense of damaging, injuring, or interfering with municipal property as follows:
(1)
It shall be unlawful for any person, intentionally and without authority, to injure or destroy any property or utility owned by the city, including, but not limited to, any sidewalk, roadway, meter, pipe, conduit, wire, line, post, lamp, or other apparatus belonging to the municipality, or used in the manufacture or sale of electricity, gas, water, telephone, sewer or other public service;
(2)
It shall be unlawful for any person, intentionally and without authority, to prevent a meter or measuring device from properly registering the quantity of such service supplied;
(3)
It shall be unlawful for any person, in any way to interfere with the proper action of the municipality and its employees and representatives in the operation, delivery, manufacture or sale of electricity, gas, water, telephone, sewer or municipal services;
(4)
It shall be unlawful for any person intentionally to divert any services of the municipality; or to intentionally and without authority use, or cause to be used, without the consent of the municipality, any service manufactured, sold, or distributed by the municipality.
(b)
Where there is no evidence to the contrary, the person performing any of the illegal acts set forth in subsection (a) of this section and any person who with knowledge of such violation receives the benefit of such service without proper charge as a result of the improper action shall be presumed to be responsible for the act of tampering or diversion.
(c)
Any person who commits an offense hereunder shall be guilty of a misdemeanor.
(Ord. No. 0092, art. 1, 7-5-2005)
Sec. 24-57. - Improper distribution of printed material prohibited.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to distribute, or cause to be distributed, within the city any handbill, phone book, advertisement or printed or written material by placing, or causing such handbill or printed or written material to be placed in any yards, driveways, walkways or porches of any structure within the city.
(b)
The provisions of this section shall not apply to any newspaper, or to any publication containing printed or written material, to which the occupants of the subject premises pay money (United States currency) in return for receiving such material.
(c)
This section is not intended, nor shall it be construed to prohibit distribution of printed or written material utilizing any means of distribution which does not cause such materials to be placed or deposited in any yards, driveways, walkways or porches of any structure within the city which means of distribution shall include, but shall not be limited to, the following:
(1)
The U.S. mail;
(2)
Personally handing such material to persons willing to accept the same on streets, sidewalks, parks or at their residences; or
(3)
Utilizing doorknobs or mailbox hanging devices in a manner which reasonably ensures that such material shall not be placed or caused to become placed or deposited upon any yards, driveways or porches of any structure within the city.
(Ord. of 7-1-2014, § 1)
Sec. 24-58. - Violations and penalty.
Violations of this section shall be punishable upon conviction, as follows:
(1)
Penalties for the first violation of this article shall be a minimum fine of $25.00.
(2)
The penalty for second violation of the same provisions of this article by the same person shall be a minimum fine of $50.00.
(3)
The penalty for a third or more violations of the same provisions of this article by the same person or legal entity shall be a minimum fine of $100.00.
For the purposes of enforcing a penalty under this article, each separate action at a different property or lot shall constitute a separate violation of the article.
(Ord. of 7-1-2014, § 2)
Secs. 24-59—24-89. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - OFFENSES AGAINST PUBLIC PEACE AND ORDER

Sec. 24-90. - Public intoxication.
It shall be unlawful for any person to be and appear on the streets or roads of the city or in any public place or place of business patronized by the public in an intoxicated condition.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. IV, § 2, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-91. - Crowding; pushing other persons.
No person shall jostle or roughly crowd or push any person in any public place within the city.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. IV, § 1, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-92. - Loitering.
(a)
Generally.
(1)
It shall be unlawful for any person, after having been directed by an officer or member of the police department to away therefrom, to remain or loiter in front of any church or other place of public worship during services therein or in front of any theater, concert hall, ballroom, coffeehouse, restaurant, tavern or other public place, or to loiter or idle away time around the hotels or on the sidewalks or public streets or parking areas of the city.
(2)
A person commits the offense of loitering when the person is in a place at a time or in a manner not usual for law-abiding individuals under circumstances that warrant a justifiable and reasonable alarm or immediate concern for the safety of persons or property in the vicinity.
(3)
Among the circumstances which may be considered in determining whether alarm is warranted is the fact that the person takes flight upon the appearance of a law enforcement officer, refuses to identify himself, or manifestly endeavors to conceal himself or any object. Unless flight by the person or other person or other circumstances make it impracticable, a law enforcement officer shall, prior to any arrest for an offense under this section, afford the person an opportunity to dispel any alarm or immediate concern which would otherwise be warranted by requesting the person to identify himself and explain his presence and conduct. No person shall be convicted of an offense under this section if the law enforcement officer failed to comply with the foregoing procedure or if it appears at trial that the explanation given by the person was true and would have dispelled the alarm or immediate concern.
(b)
Minors. It shall be unlawful for any parent, guardian or other person having the custody or control of any minor to permit, allow or encourage such minor to violate this section.
(c)
Responsibility of proprietors, managers, etc. It shall be unlawful for the proprietor, manager or other person having charge or control of any public or other place to permit, allow or encourage any minor to violate this section.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. IV, §§ 5—7, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-93. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Adult means an individual who has attained the age of 18.
Curfew hours means the period between the hours of 11:00 p.m. on any Sunday, Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday or Thursday and 6:00 a.m. of the following day; and between the hours of 12:00 midnight and 6:00 a.m. on any Friday or Saturday.
Emergency means an unforeseen combination of circumstances or the resulting state that calls for immediate action. The term "emergency" shall include but not be limited to, a fire, natural disaster, vehicular accident, or a serious medical condition of sudden onset.
Guardian means the person who, under a court order, is the guardian of the person of a minor or the public or private agency with whom a minor has been placed by a court.
Minor means any person under 18 years of age.
Parent means the natural mother or father or adoptive mother or father of a minor.
Public place means any place to which the public or a substantial group of the public has access and includes, but is not limited to, streets, highways, and the common areas of schools, hospitals, apartments, office buildings, transport facilities, restaurants, theaters, game rooms, shops and shopping centers.
(Ord. No. 0201, art. I, § 1, 8-6-2013)
Sec. 24-94. - Offenses.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any minor to knowingly remain, walk, run, stand, drive or ride about, in or upon any public place in the city during curfew hours.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for the parent or guardian having legal custody of a minor to knowingly allow or permit the minor to be in violation of the curfew imposed in section 24-93.
(Ord. No. 0201, art. I, § 2, 8-6-2013)
Sec. 24-95. - Defenses.
It is a defense to prosecution under section 24-94 that:
(1)
The minor was accompanied by his parent or guardian;
(2)
The minor was accompanied by an adult designated by his parent or guardian;
(3)
The minor was on an errand made necessary by an emergency;
(4)
The minor was attending a school, religious, or government-sponsored activity or was traveling to or from a school, religious, or government-sponsored activity;
(5)
The minor was engaged in a lawful employment activity or was going directly to or coming directly from lawful employment;
(6)
The minor was on the sidewalk directly in front of the place where he resides;
(7)
The minor was on an errand directed by his parent or guardian;
(8)
The minor was in a motor vehicle involved in intrastate or interstate transportation;
(9)
The minor was engaged in, participating in or traveling to or from any event, function or activity for which the application of the above defenses apply;
(10)
The minor was married, emancipated or had been married or had disabilities of minority removed in accordance with state law;
(11)
The minor was attending or was traveling to or from a recreational activity supervised by adults and sponsored by a school, education facility, church, a unit of local government, a civic organization, or another similar entity that takes responsibility for the minor.
(Ord. No. 0201, art. I, § 3, 8-6-2013)
Sec. 24-96. - Enforcement and penalties.
(a)
Violation of this article may result in a petition being filed in the county juvenile courts for a delinquent child under the age of 17 or for a deprived or unruly child under the age of 18 at the election of law enforcement.
(b)
A first offense by a minor will result in a verbal and documented written warning to the child, parent or guardian.
(c)
For second or subsequent violations, a citation may issue to the city municipal court and a violator may be fined up to $500.00 in the discretion of the municipal judge.
(Ord. No. 0201, art. I, § 5, 8-6-2013)
Sec. 24-97. - Smoking, vaping and tobacco use prohibited in specified areas.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to smoke or to use any tobacco product, or to vape using an electronic or e-cigarette product, as follows:
(1)
Except as otherwise specifically authorized in O.C.G.A. § 31-12A-6, smoking and the use of tobacco products, and vaping and the use of electronic or e-cigarettes, are prohibited in all enclosed areas of the city hall and in all city parks including the Hiawassee City Square.
(2)
All city parks and the Hiawassee City Square shall be posted to reflect the prohibition contained in subsection (a)(1) of this section.
(3)
Nicotine patches, nicotine gum or prescribed medications are exempt hereunder.
(b)
Violations and penalty. Any person violating this section shall be asked to discontinue the prohibited behavior. If such prohibited behavior is not discontinued, the violater will be subject to prosecution in municipal court and, upon conviction, punished in accordance with the section 1-13.
Sec. 24-98. - Preventing or disrupting lawful meetings, gatherings or processions.
It shall be unlawful for any person to recklessly or knowingly commit an act that may reasonably be expected to prevent or disrupt a lawful meeting, gathering or procession.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. IV, § 10, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-99. - Throwing objects.
No person shall recklessly or knowingly throw or propel any stone, brick, piece of wood or other object at any person, vehicle, house, building or other structure or object, or across or upon any private or public property.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. IV, § 11, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-100. - Defecating or urinating on public property or in certain areas.
It shall be unlawful for any person to defecate or urinate on or adjacent to any street or sidewalk, or in the halls, elevators, stairways, or any other area designated for public passage within any public or commercial buildings, or on any property open to public view.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. IV, § 12, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-101. - False police alarms.
No person shall knowingly or willfully harass, or attempt to harass, or mislead any police officer by false alarms or unauthorized use of any device of whatever nature to summon police aid without reasonable cause.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. IV, § 13, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-102. - Schools.
(a)
Unauthorized persons entering school buildings. No person shall enter or remain in any public, private or parochial school building between the hours of 7:30 a.m. and 6:00 p.m. on days that school is in session (or until 10:00 p.m. at those schools which have extended sessions), who is not a regularly enrolled student, teacher or employee at that school, unless the person shall have first and immediately proceeded to the administrative offices and identified himself to the principal or principal's agent and receives permission to remain on the premises.
(b)
Unauthorized persons not to remain in school buildings or on school grounds after requested to leave. It shall be unlawful for any person to enter and remain in any public, private, or parochial school or on the surrounding school grounds after being directed to leave by the principal of the school or by someone with lawful authority.
(c)
Creating a disturbance. It shall be unlawful for any person to create a disturbance in any public, private or parochial school or on the surrounding school grounds lawfully used for school activities while such recreational areas are in use or other activities are in process thereon. For purposes of this section, the term "disturbance" means any act that may be reasonably expected to interfere with the school or school activities on the school grounds or fields where such activities are in progress thereon.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. IV, §§ 14—16, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-103. - Disorderly conduct; affray.
(a)
Disorderly conduct. It shall be unlawful for any person to act in a loud and boisterous, reckless, unruly or violent manner for the purpose of insulting, degrading, or inciting another or a group of individuals in a public place.
(b)
Affray. Fighting by two or more persons in some public place to the disturbance of public tranquility is prohibited.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. IV, §§ 17, 18, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-104. - Willfully obstructing or hindering law enforcement officers.
Knowingly and willfully obstructing or hindering any law enforcement officer in the lawful discharge of his official duties is prohibited.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. IV, § 20, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-105. - Giving of false identity information to law enforcement officer.
Giving a false name, address, or date of birth to a law enforcement officer in the lawful discharge of his official duties with the intent of misleading the officer as to his identity or birth date is prohibited.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. IV, § 21, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-106. - Unauthorized persons entering vacant building.
It shall be unlawful for any person to enter or to remain in a vacant or unoccupied building or on any portion of vacant land upon which such vacant building is located unless with permission of an authorized agent of said property; provided, such building or vacant property is prominently marked by a posted notice which is easily seen from a distance of at least 50 feet that informs the public such property is vacant or unoccupied and unauthorized persons are prohibited from entering.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. IV, § 22, 5-3-2011)
Secs. 24-107—24-129. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - OFFENSES AGAINST PUBLIC SAFETY

Sec. 24-130. - Reckless operation of a motor vehicle.
No person shall operate a motor vehicle upon any parking facility, public or private, vehicle-access or pedestrian walkway of any parking facility by sudden starting, stopping or turning so as to endanger the person or property of another.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. V, § 1, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-131. - Creating hazardous or offensive conditions.
No person shall create a hazardous or physically offensive condition by an act that serves no legitimate purpose.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. V, § 2, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-132. - Aiding; encouraging minor to commit unlawful act.
No person shall aid, abet or encourage a minor to do any act that constitutes disorderly conduct or a breach of the peace.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. V, § 3, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-133. - Discharge of weapons.
It shall be unlawful for any person to discharge an air rifle or pistol, or firearm, or weapon of any type, or to shoot a slingshot or any form of weapon within any area of the city within 1,500 feet of any residence, commercial building, business or public meeting place.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. V, § 4, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-134. - Halting or impeding flow of traffic.
No person shall congregate with another or others in or on any public right-of-way or place so as to halt or impede the flow of vehicle or pedestrian traffic after having been directed to clear such public right-of-way or place by a police officer or any other authorized law enforcement officer.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. V, § 5, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-135. - Soliciting or begging on city property.
Without the express written consent of the mayor or appointed designee, it shall be unlawful for any person to beg, solicit, or sell goods, wares or other objects or services within any building, or on any grounds, sidewalks or rights-of-way or property owned by or under the control of the city, its agencies, departments, authorities, commissions, boards, bureaus or other city entities.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. V, § 6, 5-3-2011)
Secs. 24-136—24-153. - Reserved.
ARTICLE VI. - PARENTAL RESPONSIBILITY

Sec. 24-154. - Responsibility of a parent.
It shall be unlawful for any parent, guardian, or any other person having the custody or control of any minor to permit, allow, or encourage such minor to violate any law or ordinance, or to disturb the good order, morals or dignity of the city.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. VII, § 1, 5-3-2011)

ARTICLE VII. - PENALTIES

Sec. 24-177. - Penalties for violation of this chapter.
Unless otherwise stated, any person who is convicted of, or who shall plead guilty to a violation of this chapter, shall be punished in accordance with section 1-13.
(Ord. No. 0162, art. VI, § 1, 5-3-2011)
Sec. 24-178. - Technology fee.
(a)
Information technology fee. There shall be imposed against any person, firm, entity, group, association, corporation, partnership, or company against whom the municipal court of the city imposes a monetary fine or sentence of incarceration a mandatory technology fee in the sum and amount of $20.00. Said mandatory technology fee shall be in addition to, and not in lieu of, any other fines, fees, or penalties mandated by state law, local ordinance or otherwise. Said technology fee, as imposed by this section, shall supersede, replace and be in lieu of any previous technology fee imposed by the city in such matters. Notwithstanding the foregoing, such technology fee shall not be imposed where the imposition of such technology fee would cause the total amount of the fines and fees imposed to exceed the maximum amount of such fines or fees that the city or the municipal court of the city is authorized by law to impose.
(b)
Multiple citations and convictions. In the event of multiple violations the fee shall be charged per violation of any of the laws or ordinances of the city or any laws of the state.
(c)
Deposits and use. The technology fee shall be deposited in the city's general fund, but shall be tracked and only be used for expenditures related to the technology needs of the city's law enforcement and municipal court. Funds collected pursuant to this chapter shall be accounted for by the city and shall be used only for the purposes authorized in this section at the direction of the city mayor and chief of police.
( Ord. No. 2020-08-01 , § 1, 9-1-2020)
