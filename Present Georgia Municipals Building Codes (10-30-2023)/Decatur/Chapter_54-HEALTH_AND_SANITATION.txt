ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 54-1. - Violation of county health department rules and regulations for food service establishments; penalty for violation.
Violations of the rules and regulations for food service establishments adopted October 11, 1977, by the county health department, occurring within the city, are declared to be unlawful and the municipal court shall have jurisdiction over persons charged with any such violation. The penalty for violation of any such rule or regulation shall be as provided by section 1-12.

Editor's note— Ord. No. O-04-13, adopted July 1, 2004, repealed the former art. II, §§ 54-31—54-37, and enacted a new art. II as set out herein. The former art. II pertained to smoking policy and derived from Ord. No. O-93-05, §§ 16-63—16-69, adopted July 6, 1993.

Sec. 54-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Child care facility means any institution, society, agency, or facility, whether incorporated or not, which either primarily or incidentally provides full-time care for children under 17 years of age outside their own homes, subject to such exceptions as may be provided in rules and regulations of the State Board of Human Resources, as defined by O.C.G.A. § 49-5-3, as amended.
Dining area means an enclosed interior or exterior area containing a counter or tables upon which food is served.
Employer means any person who employs the services of another person.
Employee means any person who is employed by any employer in consideration for direct or indirect monetary wages of profit.
Enclosed means closed in by a roof and at least three sides with appropriate openings for ingress and egress. It includes areas commonly described as public lobbies or lobbies when those are in an area that is enclosed as defined herein.
Establishment means any business, store, office or other place where goods or services are sold or provided as part of a commercial venture. The term establishment includes but is not limited to the following: (1) automobile dealerships, furniture or other showrooms for the display of merchandise offered for sale; (2) grocery, pharmacy, specialty, department and other stores which sell goods or merchandise; (3) service stations, stores or shops for the repair or maintenance of appliances, shoes, motor vehicles or other items or products; (4) barbershops, beauty shops, cleaners, laundromats and other establishments offering services to the general public; (5) video arcade, pool hall and other amusement centers; (6) offices providing professional services such as legal, medical, dental, engineering and architectural services; (7) banks, savings and loan offices, and other financial establishments; (8) hotels and motels and other places that provide accommodations to the public; and, (9) restaurants and cafeterias.
Health care facility means any licensed general or specialized hospital, institutional infirmary, public health center or diagnostic and treatment center, as defined by O.C.G.A. § 31-7-1(2), as amended.
Intermediate care (nursing) home means any long-term custodial care facility that provides for the physical and mental welfare of the aged.
Personal care home means a residential facility having at least 25 beds and providing, for compensation, protective care and oversight of ambulatory, nonrelated persons, including the elderly, who need a monitored environment but who do not have injuries or disabilities which require chronic or convalescent care, including medial, nursing, or intermediate care, personal care homes include those facilities which monitor daily residents' functioning and location, have the capability for crises intervention, and provide supervision in areas of nutrition, medication, and provision of transient medical care. Such term does not include residential facilities which are devoted to independent living units with kitchen facilities in which residents have the option of preparing and serving some or all of their own meals, or boarding facilities which do not provide personal care.
Place of employment means any enclosed area under the control of a public or private employer which employees frequent during the course of employment including, but not limited to, work areas, restrooms, hallways, employee lounges, cafeterias and snack bars, conference and meeting rooms, lobbies and reception areas.
(1)
A private residence is not a place of employment unless it is used as a childcare facility, an adult day care facility, or a health care facility.
(2)
The dining area of a restaurant shall be treated as a place of employment under this article.
Private enclosed offices means any enclosed area within a place of employment. Private enclosed offices must have walls that extend from floor to ceiling and a closable door.
Public park means any public park, playground, greenspace, plaza or outdoor recreation area maintained and operated by the city, including but not limited to, all city parks, the MARTA Plaza, the Decatur Cemetery and the Old Courthouse lawn.
Public place means any enclosed area to which the public is invited or in which the public is permitted including, but not limited to, restaurants; stores; waiting rooms; lobbies; reception areas, hallways, public transit; restrooms; enclosed shopping malls; elevators, service lines, service stations, offices providing professional services, banks and other financial institutions, educational, recreational and health care facilities; child care facilities; auditoriums; theaters; arenas; meeting rooms, repair shops, automobile dealerships, conventional halls, bowling facilities, polling places and bingo games. A private residence is not a public place unless it is used as a childcare facility, and adult day care facility or a health care facility.
Restaurant means any establishment or enclosed area which is primarily devoted to the serving of food by which more than 50 percent of its total annual gross sales are derived from food sales to the public or guests and which contains a dining area; or, an establishment by which at least 40 percent of its total annual gross sales are from the sale of prepared meals or food and provides chairs, tables and counters to seat and serve 50 or more persons. The term restaurant shall include any dining area located within a health care, educational, or childcare facility. Food courts within enclosed shopping malls shall be treated as restaurants under this article.
Retail tobacco store means a retail store utilized primarily for the sale of tobacco products and accessories and in which the sale of other products is merely incidental.
Service line means any indoor line at which one or more persons are waiting for or receiving service of any kind, whether or not such service involved the exchange of money.
Smoking means inhaling, exhaling, burning or carrying any lighted cigar, cigarette, e-cigarette, oral smoking device, or pipe containing any weed, plant, nicotine based liquid or other combustible substance in any manner or in any form.
(Ord. No. O-04-13, 7-1-04; Ord. No. O-16-09, § 1, 5-16-16)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 54-32. - Public places, places of employment, and public parks; generally.
(a)
Except as otherwise provided in this article, smoking is prohibited in all public places within the city, all places of employment within the city, and all public parks. All ashtrays and similar smoking paraphernalia shall be removed from any place where smoking is prohibited.
(b)
Except as otherwise provided in this article, smoking is prohibited:
(1)
Within 20 feet of any outside entrance, operable window or ventilation system of any public place within the city and of any place of employment within the city;
(2)
Within 20 feet of any exterior seating or service area of any restaurant within the city.
(Ord. No. O-04-13, 7-1-04; Ord. No. O-16-09, § 2, 5-16-16)
Sec. 54-33. - Exceptions.
(a)
The regulation of smoking pursuant to this article shall not apply in the following areas:
(1)
Designated smoking rooms in hotels and motels rented to guests, provided that such designated smoking rooms shall not comprise more than 25 percent of the total number of rooms available for rent.
(2)
Retail tobacco stores.
(3)
Private clubs when associated with and incidental to any apartment or office development.
(4)
Private residences, including those, which may serve as an office workplace, except if, used as a childcare, an adult day care or a health care facility.
(5)
Any property owned or leased by the State of Georgia or the federal government.
(b)
Notwithstanding any other provision of this article, any owner, operator, manager or other person who controls any establishment described in this article may declare that the entire establishment is a non-smoking establishment.
(Ord. No. O-04-13, 7-1-04; Ord. No. O-16-09, § 3, 5-16-16)
Sec. 54-34. - Posting of signs.
(a)
"No Smoking" signs or the international "No Smoking" symbol (consisting of a pictorial representation of a burning cigarette enclosed in a red circle with a red bar across it) shall be clearly and conspicuously posted in every building where smoking is prohibited by this article, by the owner, operator manager or other person having control of such building or area.
(b)
Every public place and every place of employment where smoking is prohibited by this article shall have posted at every entrance a conspicuous sign clearly stating that smoking is prohibited.
(c)
Notice of the provisions set forth in this article shall be given to all applicants obtaining an occupational tax certificate or professional license in the city.
(Ord. No. O-04-13, 7-1-04)
Sec. 54-35. - Employers' responsibility.
It is the responsibility of employers to provide a smoke-free workplace for all employees of public places and places of employment.
(Ord. No. O-04-13, 7-1-04)
Sec. 54-36. - Penalty for violation of article.
(a)
Any person who violates any provision of this article shall be punished, upon conviction, as provided in section 1-12.
(b)
Any person who owns, manages, operates or otherwise controls a public place or a place of employment and who upon notice from any person authorized to enforce this article, knowingly violates any provision of this article shall be punished, upon conviction, as provided in section 1-12.
(Ord. No. O-04-13, 7-1-04)
Sec. 54-37. - Enforcement of article.
(a)
Any police officer, as defined by Georgia law, may issue a citation for any violation of this article.
(b)
The designated fire marshal of the city may issue a citation for any violation of this article.
(c)
Any designated codes enforcement officer, building inspector, building official or fire inspector of the city may issue a citation for any violation of this article.
(Ord. No. O-04-13, 7-1-04)
Secs. 54-38—54-55. - Reserved.

Sec. 54-56. - Prohibited conditions.
It shall be unlawful for the owner or occupant of any property within the city to maintain such property with weeds, briars, vines, dead limbs, and branches from trees, underbrush, garbage, trash or other things thereon that will constitute an unsanitary or dangerous condition, health menace or fire hazard.
(Code 1967, § 16-35(a); Ord. No. O-91-21, 11-4-91; Ord. No. O-92-06, 3-16-92)
Sec. 54-57. - Storage of abandoned vehicles and junk.
It shall be unlawful for the owner or occupant of any property within the city to utilize the premises of such property for the open storage of any abandoned motor vehicle, ice box, refrigerator, stove, appliance, glass, building material, building rubbish, or similar items that will constitute an unsanitary or dangerous condition, health menace or fire hazard. For the purposes of this section, an abandoned motor vehicle is defined as one that is in a state of disrepair and incapable of being moved under its own power.
(Code 1967, § 16-35(b); Ord. No. O-91-21, 11-4-91; Ord. No. O-92-06, 3-16-92)
Sec. 54-57.1. - Maintenance of graffiti.
(a)
"Graffiti" is defined according to O.C.G.A. § 17-15A-2 as "any inscriptions, words, figures, paintings, or other defacements that are written, marked, etched, scratched, sprayed, drawn, painted or engraved on or otherwise affixed to any surface of real property or improvements thereon without prior authorization of the owner or occupant of the property by means of any aerosol paint container, broad-tipped marker, gum label, paint stick, graffiti stick, etching equipment, brush or other device capable of scarring or leaving a visible mark on any surface."
(b)
It shall be unlawful for the owner or occupant of any property within the city to allow any graffiti that is visible from the street or other public or private property to remain upon any building, structure, appurtenance, equipment, any container used for storage of commercial refuse, recyclables or litter, or any fixed or portable box, container or storage unit, any sign, any surface, paving or similar feature located, installed, used or maintained on such property after the 15-day compliance period provided below.
(Ord. No. O-08-03, 2-19-08)
Sec. 54-58. - Unsecured vacant buildings.
It shall be unlawful for the owner or person entitled to possession of any dwelling house, commercial or institutional building within the city to allow a vacant building to remain unsecured and open to the public for a period of ten or more days. Any such vacant building having broken, unlocked or missing doors or windows shall be deemed to be open to the public and to constitute a dangerous condition, health menace or fire hazard.
(Code 1967, § 16-35(c); Ord. No. O-91-21, 11-4-91; Ord. No. O-92-06, 3-16-92)
Sec. 54-59. - Failure to abate unlawful conditions after notice; violation of article.
If the owner or occupant of any such property shall so maintain such property and shall fail to clean the premises or remove such items and/or graffiti as described in sections 54-56, 54-57 and 54-57.1, or if the owner or person entitled to possession of such vacant building as described in section 54-58 shall not close and secure such vacant building within 15 days after having been notified in writing by an official of the city public works department, fire department, or development department, such action on the part of the owner or occupant of the property shall constitute a violation of this article. A separate violation shall be deemed to occur for each and every day or portion thereof during which any violation of the requirements of this article is committed or continued.
(Code 1967, § 16-35(d); Ord. No. O-91-21, 11-4-91; Ord. No. O-92-06, 3-16-92; Ord. No. O-08-03, 2-19-08)
Sec. 54-60. - Notice contents; service.
(a)
Such written notice shall state what items or conditions constitute the graffiti, unsanitary or dangerous condition, health menace or fire hazard that must be cleared, removed or secured.
(b)
Service of such notice shall be as follows:
(1)
By delivery to the owner and occupant personally;
(2)
By registered or certified mail with postage prepaid deposited in the United States mail addressed to the owner's and occupant's last known address.
(3)
If service of notice cannot be accomplished by using the methods stated in subsections (b)(1) and (b)(2) of this section, by posting a copy of the notice in a conspicuous place on the premises.
(Code 1967, § 16-35(e), (f); Ord. No. O-91-21, 11-4-91; Ord. No. O-92-06, 3-16-92; Ord. No. O-08-03, 2-19-08)
Sec. 54-61. - Appeal of notice.
Any person receiving written notice from an official designated in section 54-59 of deficiencies in said person's property under this article may, within 15 days following the date of such notice, enter an appeal in writing to the housing board of adjustments and appeals at city hall. Such appeal shall state the location of the property and the date of the notice of violations. The appeal must state the reasons upon which the appeal is made. Such appeal shall be heard and determined according to the provisions of section 106, housing board of adjustments and appeals, of the Southern Standard Housing Code, 1973 edition, as adopted by the city.
(Code 1967, § 16-35(g); Ord. No. O-91-21, 11-4-91; Ord. No. O-92-06, 3-16-92; Ord. No. O-22-11 , § 2(Exh. A), 10-17-22)
Sec. 54-62. - Citation for violation of article without further notice; conditions.
Any person shall be considered to be in violation of this article and shall be subject to immediate citation without further notice if such person:
(1)
Has been found guilty of one violation of this article within the previous 12 months; and
(2)
Thereafter again, after the prior violation was cured, maintains the property on which the prior violation occurred in a manner described by sections 54-56, 54-57 and/or 54-58.
(Code 1967, § 16-35(h); Ord. No. O-91-21, 11-4-91; Ord. No. O-92-06, 3-16-92)
Sec. 54-63. - Report of violation; abatement procedure.
In the event of a violation of this article, the city public works department, fire department or development department, may make a written report of its findings to the city commission with recommendation that the city have the premises cleaned and have removed therefrom such items as described in this article, that constitute an unsanitary or dangerous condition, health menace or fire hazard or, in the case of a building which is vacant, unsecured and open to the public, that the city close and secure the building. When such report with recommendations has been filed:
(1)
Placement on city commission agenda for hearing. The city commissioners shall have full power and authority, in their discretion, to place the matter upon their agenda for hearing at such time and place as the city commissioners may fix.
(2)
Service of report and notice to owner or occupant prior to hearing. A copy of the written report aforesaid and a written notice of the time and place fixed for hearing the matter shall be served upon the owner or occupant of such property at least 15 days prior to the date fixed for the hearing, such service to be made by the building official or other designated officer of the city in the manner specified in section 56-60. The hearing may be continued upon request of either party for reasonable cause.
(3)
Hearing; finding. The city commissioners shall hear the matter, including the response of such owner or occupant and witnesses introduced, and make their finding.
(4)
Abatement by city; lien; collection of lien. The city commissioners shall have the full power and authority to order that the offending premises be cleaned and/or secured and to authorize entry upon such premises for such purpose; to have graffiti removed or obliterated; to have the premises cleaned; and to have removed therefrom weeds, briars, vines, dead limbs, and branches from trees, underbrush, garbage, trash or other things thereon that will constitute an unsanitary or dangerous condition, health menace or fire hazard. In the case of a building which is vacant, unsecured and open to the public, the city commission shall have the building closed and secured, and assess the property for the cost of the cleaning, removal and/or closing and securing. Such assessment shall constitute a lien on the property until paid. An execution in favor of the city in the amount of such assessment shall be issued by the city clerk and bear attest in the name of the mayor or mayor pro-tem and be collected in accordance with the provisions of section 85 of the city Charter as set forth in this Code.
(Code 1967, § 16-35(i); Ord. No. O-91-21, 11-4-91; Ord. No. O-92-06, 3-16-92; Ord. No. O-08-03, 2-19-08)
