Sec. 26-1. - Penalty for violation of chapter.
Any person guilty of a violation of this chapter shall be punished as provided in section 1-12.
(Ord. No. O-13-02, 2-4-13)
Sec. 26-2. - Control and supervision of cemetery.
(a)
The city manager is hereby vested with the power and authority to control and supervise any improvements, changes in contour of lots or any other change of any kind or character to any of the lots located in the Decatur Cemetery. For purposes of this chapter, "city manager" means the person designated by the city commission as the city manager or the city manager's designee.
(b)
No such changes or improvements shall be made to any of such lots without first securing the written approval of the city manager and then only under direct supervision and direction of the city manager.
(c)
Except as permitted by section 26-5, all improvements or alterations to individual lots in the cemetery shall be undertaken in compliance with the direction of and subject to the consent, satisfaction and approval of the city manager; and should such improvements or alterations be made without the city manager written consent, the city shall have the right to remove, alter or change such improvements or alterations and to assess the costs of such work against the lot or the owner of the lot.
(d)
All work on lots within the cemetery, including all grading, landscape work, improvements, and care of any kind, all planting, trimming, cutting and removing of trees and shrubs of any kind, all opening and closing of graves, and all interments, disinterments and removals, shall be made in compliance with the authority of the city manager, including all rules that may from time to time be adopted for the cemetery.
(e)
Employees of the City of Decatur shall have the authority to remove all floral designs, flowers, weeds, trees, shrubs, and plants of any kind from the cemetery, both in public areas and on privately owned lots, when, in the judgment of the city manager such objects become dangerous, detrimental, diseased or in a state of disrepair.
(f)
All marker restoration and repair of individual lots in the 1828 historic section of the cemetery shall be under the direction of and subject to the written approval of the city manager or the city manager's designee.
(Ord. No. O-13-02, 2-4-13; Ord. No. O-22-11 , § 2(Exh. A), 10-17-22)
Sec. 26-3. - Prices of lots, charges and fees.
Prices of lots, charges and fees for services offered in Decatur Cemetery shall be established by the city commission and set forth in the schedule of fees and charges.
(Ord. No. O-13-02, 2-4-13)
Sec. 26-4. - Purchase of lots by residents; resale to nonresidents.
The prices of lots in Decatur Cemetery purchased by residents shall apply to bona fide and legal residents of the city. Any such resident, before purchasing any lots in the cemetery, shall provide the city manager with sufficient proof of legal residency within the city, and such purchaser shall agree that they will not sell or dispose of any such lot or part thereof to a nonresident of the city without first obtaining written consent from the city manager. If such consent is obtained and such resale is made, then such new purchaser or transferee shall pay to the city an amount equal to the difference between the current resident graves or lot price and the current nonresident grave or lot price for each lot or grave that is resold. In the event of a violation of this section, the lot shall revert to the city, and the owner thereof shall be reimbursed in the amount originally paid therefor.
(Ord. No. O-13-02, 2-4-13)
Sec. 26-5. - Placement of gravestone or markers.
(a)
When a gravestone or marker is placed above a grave by any firm or person licensed to perform such work in the Decatur Cemetery, the same shall be set in a concrete foundation of a size and type approved by the city manager, such work to be performed by the city and paid for by the person or firm requesting such foundation work, based on the current fee schedule of the cemetery.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any lot owner, or any agent or employee of such lot owner, to place any marker in a manner otherwise than as provided by this section, including all rules that may from time to time be adopted for the cemetery.
(Ord. No. O-13-02, 2-4-13)
Sec. 26-6. - Mausoleums, tombs, or other edifices; removal.
It shall be unlawful for any person in Decatur Cemetery, to erect, construct or build on any lot in the cemetery any mausoleum, tomb or other edifice of like kind or character or any edifice other than what is commonly termed a monument or marker and in keeping with monuments and markers now erected in the cemetery without first obtaining the written permission of the city manager. Upon the violation of this section, the city reserves the right to remove any such edifice from any such lot and to assess the costs of such removal against the lot, the owner of the lot, or the person erecting the edifice.
(Ord. No. O-13-02, 2-4-13)
Sec. 26-7. - Trees.
It shall be unlawful for any person to plant any tree in Decatur Cemetery without first obtaining the written permission of the city manager.
(Ord. No. O-13-02, 2-4-13)
Sec. 26-8. - Shrubbery over three feet in height.
It shall be unlawful for any person to plant upon any lot in Decatur Cemetery any shrubbery that exceeds three feet in height at the time of such planting, without first obtaining written permission of the city manager.
(Ord. No. O-13-02, 2-4-13)
Sec. 26-9. - Removal of trees and shrubbery.
(a)
The city reserves the right to remove any trees or shrubbery prohibited by sections 26-7 and 26-8 from any lot in Decatur Cemetery and to assess the costs of such removal against the owner of the lot or against the person planting such trees or shrubbery.
(b)
If any tree or shrub, situated in any lot, shall, by means of its roots or branches, become detrimental in any way to the adjoining lot, drive or walk, the city shall have the authority to enter upon the lot and remove the tree or shrub, or any part thereof. However, no tree growing in any lot shall be pruned or removed without the written consent of the city manager.
(Ord. No. O-13-02, 2-4-13)
Sec. 26-10. - Animals.
(a)
All animals in the Decatur Cemetery must be kept under restraint as defined in sections 14-31 and 14-34.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person who possesses, harbors or is in charge of any domestic restrained animal not to immediately remove excrement deposited by the animal while in the Decatur Cemetery. Animal waste must be removed and deposited in suitable sanitary containers.
(Ord. No. O-13-02, 2-4-13)
Sec. 26-11. - Erection of walls, coping and curbing.
It shall be unlawful for any person to build or erect any wall, coping or curbing in, around or on any lot in Decatur Cemetery lying outside of the 1828 historic section of the cemetery, without first obtaining written permission of the city manager.
(Ord. No. O-13-02, 2-4-13)
Sec. 26-12. - Obstruction of walks or drives.
It shall be unlawful for any person to block or obstruct any of the walks or drives in Decatur Cemetery without the permission of the city manager.
(Ord. No. O-13-02, 2-4-13)
Sec. 26-13. - Hours for cemetery.
The Decatur Cemetery shall open at 7:00 a.m. and shall close at one-half hour after sunset; provided, however, that the closing time may be extended if at the regularly scheduled closing time there is an ongoing activity supervised by the city or having a permit issued by the city, in which event the closing time shall be ten minutes after the end of such supervised activity or permitted event.
(Ord. No. O-13-02, 2-4-13)
