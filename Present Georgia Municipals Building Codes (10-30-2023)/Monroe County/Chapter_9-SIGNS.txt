ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Secs. 9-1—9-25. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - INTERSTATE HIGHWAYS 75 AND 475
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 9-26. - Application.
This article shall apply to all outdoor advertising signs abutting or visible from Interstates 75 and 475 rights-of-way in this county.
(Ord. of 1-8-85, § I(1); Ord. of 10-20-98 § I(1))
Sec. 9-27. - Compliance.
No sign shall be erected or maintained unless it is in compliance with the regulations of this article.
(Ord. of 1-8-85, § I(2); Ord. of 10-20-98, § I(2))
Sec. 9-28. - Construction, maintenance generally.
Signs must be constructed of durable materials, maintained in good condition and not permitted to become dilapidated.
(Ord. of 1-8-85, § I(3); Ord. of 10-20-98, § I(3))
Sec. 9-29. - Other provisions.
All signs located on sites abutting or visible from Interstates 75 and 475 rights-of-way shall conform with O.C.G.A. 32-6-70 et seq. (The Georgia Outdoor Advertising Code) and shall meet all federal and state requirements necessary to obtain a permit under said code. In instances where the sign control of this article are more strict, these regulations shall apply.
(Ord. of 1-8-85, § I(4); Ord. of 10-20-98, § I(4))
Sec. 9-30. - Removal.
Any sign which advertises an activity, business, product or service which has ceased operation or production shall be removed within six (6) months of the discontinuance of said activity, business, product or service which has ceased operation or production.
(Ord. of 1-8-85, § I(5); Ord. of 10-20-98, § I(5))
Sec. 9-31. - Prohibited signs.
(1)
Signs imitating warning signals are prohibited. No sign shall display lights resembling the flashing lights customarily used in traffic signals or in police, fire, ambulance, or rescue vehicles; nor shall any signs use the words, slogans, dimensional shape and size, or colors of the governmental traffic signs.
(2)
No signs, except traffic signs and signals and informational signs erected by a public agency, are permitted within any street or highway rights-of-way.
(3)
Signs painted or attached to natural features such as trees or rocks, telephone poles, utility poles, or fence posts are prohibited.
(4)
Fluttering ribbons and banners are prohibited.
(5)
Signs within three hundred (300) feet of any officially designated historical site or monument, except signs pertaining to that particular site or monument, are prohibited.
(Ord. of 1-8-85, § II; Ord. of 10-20-98, § II)
Secs. 9-32—9-40. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - SPECIFIC SIGN REGULATIONS

Sec. 9-41. - Home occupation signs.
One (1) nonilluminated professional or business nameplate not exceeding two (2) square feet in area is permitted.
(Ord. of 1-8-85, § III(1); Ord. of 10-20-98, § III(1))
Sec. 9-42. - Work under construction.
One (1) nonilluminated sign not exceeding forty (40) square feet in area displaying the name of the building, the contractors, the architects, the engineers, the owners, the financial selling and development agencies is permitted upon the premises of any work under the construction, alteration, or removal. Such signs shall be removed from the site within thirty (30) days after the completion of the project.
(Ord. of 1-8-85, § III(2); Ord. of 10-20-98, § III(2))
Sec. 9-43. - Temporary subdivision signs.
Temporary signs, not exceeding forty (40) square feet in area announcing a land subdivision development are permitted on the premises of the land subdivision. Such signs shall be removed when seventy-five (75) percent of the lots are conveyed.
(Ord. of 1-8-85, § III(3); Ord. of 10-20-98, § III(3))
Sec. 9-44. - Roof signs.
(a)
Not more than one (1) roof sign structure may be erected on the roof of any one (1) building.
(b)
No roof sign structure shall extend more than twenty-five (25) feet above the elevation of the roof.
(Ord. of 1-8-85, § III(4); Ord. of 10-20-98, § III(4))
Sec. 9-45. - Wall signs.
Signs on the walls of a building (including signs attached flat against the wall, painted wall signs and projecting signs) shall meet the following requirements:
(1)
Signs on the front surface of a building. The total area of signs on the exterior front surface of a building shall not exceed twenty-five (25) percent of the front surface of the building.
(2)
Signs on the side and rear surface of a building. The total area of signs on a side or rear surface of building shall not exceed fifty (50) percent of the exterior side or rear surface of the building respectively.
(3)
Projecting signs.
a.
Wall signs attached flat against a wall may extend not more than twenty-four (24) inches from the wall.
b.
Signs attached at an angle to a wall may extend outward from the wall of a building not more than five (5) feet.
(Ord. of 1-8-85, § III(5); Ord. of 10-20-98, § III(5))
Sec. 9-46. - Illuminated signs.
Illumination devices shall be so placed and shielded that light from the sign itself will not be directly cast into any residential district, sleeping rooms in any district, or the eyes of any automobile or vehicular driver.
(Ord. of 1-8-85, § III(6); Ord. of 10-20-98, § III(6))
Sec. 9-47. - Outdoor advertising signs.
Outdoor advertising signs shall meet the following requirements:
(1)
Uniform size. The outside measurements of all such signs shall be twelve (12) feet in height and fifty (50) feet in length, or fourteen (14) feet in height and forty-eight (48) feet in length, with or without trim.
(2)
Illumination. All illuminated signs shall use base mounted fluorescent or mercury vapor lights and shall be activated by photoelectric cells. Additional lighting including, but not limited to, neon, animation, and running lights is prohibited.
(3)
Height above interstate grade. All signs shall be a minimum of ten (10) feet above adjacent interstate pavement, measuring from the lower portion of the sign face. The height of signs shall be as determined by the regulations of the Georgia Department of Transportation. Two (2) signs in the same location (back to back) or "V" formation shall be the same height above the interstate surface. In the event that any sign exceeds a height of seventy-five (75) feet above interstate grade, a general liability insurance policy in a form acceptable to the county shall be maintained with adequate proof thereof to the county insuring against all liability resulting from any damage to persons or property caused by said sign.
(4)
Extrusions prohibited. Extrusions beyond the face of the sign, excluding aprons or cutouts, are prohibited.
(5)
Number of signs per location. Only one (1) sign shall be allowed to face the same direction per location. This allows back to back or "V" formation signs but prohibits two (2) signs (side by side) facing the same direction.
(6)
Spacing. Sign locations shall be no less than five hundred (500) feet apart measuring from the two (2) closest points.
(7)
Sign structures shall be no less than ten (10) feet from any property or right of way line.
(8)
Allow three (3) outdoor advertising signs per quadrant of an interchange adjacent to the interstate highway. These signs must be located on property which is zoned commercial or industrial. These signs are restricted to an area twelve hundred (1200) feet long beginning five hundred (500) feet from the point where the pavement widens on the main traveled way to accommodate the longest entrance or exit ramp.
(9)
No structure shall be closer than seventy-five (75) feet to an occupied residence.
(10)
Historic area locations prohibited. No sign shall be placed in or obstruct the view of an area of designated historic interest.
(11)
Advertising limitations. Outdoor advertising shall be limited to the following:
a.
Travel service facilities including lodgings, gas, food, camping;
b.
Areas of scenic beauty;
c.
Public attractions including historic, natural scientific or recreational amenities; and
d.
Any combination of the above listed facilities. Further, the content of all advertising shall be directional in nature.
(Ord. of 1-8-85, § III(7); Ord. of 10-20-98, § III(7); Ord. of 7-25-00, § III(7)(c))
Sec. 9-48. - On-premise signs.
On-premise signs located within one hundred (100) feet of a regularly used part of the activity being advertised and located on the same property and for-sale or for-lease signs located on the property being advertised would not be prohibited.
(Ord. of 1-8-85, § III; Ord. of 10-20-98, § III(8))
Sec. 9-49. - Nonconformance.
Any legally erected sign which would become nonconforming as a result of this article would be allowed to remain until purchased by the department of transportation or the county, provided that the sign owner meets the requirements of state laws and rules and regulations governing such signs.
(Ord. of 1-8-85, § III; Ord. of 10-20-98, § III(9))
