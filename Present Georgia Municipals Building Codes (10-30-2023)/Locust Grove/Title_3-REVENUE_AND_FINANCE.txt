Title 3 - REVENUE AND FINANCE
Chapters:
Chapter 3.04 - FISCAL PROVISIONS GENERALLY
Sections:

3.04.010 - Fiscal year.
The fiscal year of the city shall commence on January 1st and end on December 31st of each year.
(Prior code § 2-5-1)
3.04.020 - Annual budget—Appropriations.
An annual budget and an appropriations ordinance shall be adopted by the mayor and council prior to the first day of the fiscal year. However, if for good and sufficient reasons the budget cannot be adopted by the first day of the fiscal year, the budget shall be adopted not later than forty-five days subsequent to the beginning of the fiscal year. If the budget and the appropriations ordinance are not adopted prior to the beginning of the fiscal year, this section automatically authorizes the continuation of necessary and essential expenditures to operate the city. The budget as adopted shall be a balanced budget with anticipated revenues (including appropriated unencumbered surplus) equal to appropriated expenditures. All funds within the budget shall also be balanced.
(Prior code § 2-5-2)
3.04.030 - Purchasing.
A.
Each department head shall be responsible for purchases for his department involving expenditures of less than one thousand dollars, and shall:
1.
Arrange and negotiate the purchase or contract for all equipment, supplies and contractual services for his department, and sell or otherwise dispose of surplus equipment and supplies; and
2.
Maintain an inventory of all materials, supplies or equipment owned by his department.
B.
Expenditures in excess of one thousand dollars and sales of surplus property valued at one thousand dollars or more shall require the approval of the mayor and council.
(Prior code § 2-5-3)
Chapter 3.08 - PROPERTY TAXES
Sections:

3.08.010 - Date due.
All property subject to property tax levy by the city shall be due and payable on or before December 20th of each year.
(Ord. 5/20/80 § 1)
3.08.020 - Failure to pay—Issuance of tax fi. fa.
In the event the property tax due the city is not paid on or before December 20th of each year, then a tax fi. fa. shall be issued on said property.
(Ord. 5/20/80 § 2)

The form of the tax fi. fa. to be issued shall be as follows:

(Ord. 5/20/80 § 3)
3.08.040 - City clerk authorized to issue fi. fa.
The clerk of the city is authorized and directed to issue said fi. fa.s on the 21st day of December of each year for the city property taxes that have not been paid.
(Ord. 5/20/80 § 4)
3.08.050 - Certain persons authorized to execute fi. fa.
The chief of police of the city, or any of his authorized officers, is authorized and directed to execute said fi. fa. under the same manner and procedure as provided by the laws of the state of Georgia governing executions for such process from the Superior Court.
(Ord. 5/20/80 § 5)
3.08.060 - Inspection of fi. fa. to clerk of Superior Court.
The clerk of the city shall issue to the clerk of the Superior Court of Henry County, Georgia, said fi. fa. with the following notice attached thereto:

You are hereby authorized and directed to file the foregoing tax fi. fa. on the general execution document of the official records of Henry County, Georgia.

_____
Clerk, City of Locust Grove,
Georgia
(Ord. 5/20/80 § 6)
3.08.070 - Issuance of fi. fa.s for outstanding bills?Recordation where.
The clerk of the city is directed to immediately issue fi. fa.s for all outstanding property tax bills that are due to the city, and forward the same to the attorney for the city for recording in the office of the clerk of Superior Court of Henry County, Georgia.
(Ord. 5/20/80 § 7)
3.08.080 - Levy on property?Collection of delinquent taxes.
Once the fi. fa. has been issued, the chief of police of the city is directed to make the necessary levy on the property for the unpaid taxes and notify the city attorney. Said attorney is then authorized and directed to commence collection of all delinquent taxes as provided by law.
(Ord. 5/20/80 § 8)
Chapter 3.10 - IMPROVEMENTS AND SERVICES FINANCING
Sections:
Article 1. - Generally

3.10.010 - Constitutional authority for establishing special tax districts within the city.
The mayor and city council of the City of Locust Grove, Georgia is authorized by Article 9, Section 2, Paragraph VI of the Constitution of the State of Georgia, to create special districts for the provision of local government services within such districts and to levy and collect fees, assessments and taxes within such districts to pay, wholly or partially, the cost of providing such services therein.
(Ord. No. 10-06-035, § 2, 6-7-10)
3.10.020 - Purpose and scope.
The purpose of this article is to provide for the services and improvements set forth in this section, and to provide for the manner and method of assessing the costs of such services and improvements, or the creation of special tax districts to pay for such services and improvements. The following services and improvements are covered by this article:
(1)
Garbage and solid waste collection and disposal;
(2)
Public health facilities and services, including hospitals, ambulances, emergency rescue services and animal control;
(3)
Street and road construction and maintenance, including curbs, sidewalks, street lights and devices to control the flow of traffic on streets and roads; and,
(4)
Parks, recreational areas, programs and facilities.
(Ord. No. 10-06-035, § 2, 6-7-10)
3.10.030 - Preliminary survey and recommendation.
Whenever a proposal to adopt or provide any of the improvements or services as set forth in Section 3.10.020 has been filed with the mayor and city council, or the mayor and council on their own motion have considered a proposal to furnish such improvement or to provide such utility, the mayor and council shall cause the appropriate department head to make a preliminary survey and recommendation to the mayor and city council.
(Ord. No. 10-06-035, § 2, 6-7-10)
3.10.040 - Hearing; notice.
Whenever the city council shall determine from the preliminary survey that such project has merit and will serve a public need, and that the costs thereof shall be borne in whole or in part by the owners of the property affected, the city council shall set a time and place for a public hearing before the city council on such preliminary survey. Notice of such hearing shall be given to the owners of the property affected, by regular mail, if practicable, at least ten days in advance of such hearing, and by publication of such notice, one time, not less than ten days in advance of such hearing, in the legal organ of Henry County. Such notice shall generally describe the improvement or service to be constructed or furnished, the area affected and the manner in which the costs therefore shall be assessed or taxed against the owners of the affected property.
(Ord. No. 10-06-035, § 2, 6-7-10)
3.10.050 through 3.10.100 - Reserved.
Article 2. - Street Light Tax Districts

3.10.110 - Declaration of policy and purpose.
The purpose of this article is to provide the citizens of the city with a procedure for the installation, maintenance and operation of street lights in certain public rights-of-way in the city of Locust Grove. To that end, this article is adopted in order to effect the creation of street light tax districts to pay for services rendered by such street lights.

3.10.130 - Street light tax districts created; register; responsibility for costs.
(a)
New special tax districts which shall be known as street light tax districts may be created upon submission of proper petition and resolution of the mayor and city council as provided hereinafter. Each new street light tax district so created shall be assigned a number and shall be designated by recorded plats showing each lot therein.
(b)
A register of street light tax districts shall be maintained in the office of the city manager or his designee and the office of the county tax commissioner. Such registers shall be available to the public for inspection upon reasonable notice to either of these offices.
(c)
The cost of the street lights and poles, the cost of installing the street lights, and any costs for the subsequent maintenance of said street lights shall be the responsibility of the developer, the property owners, the utility company contracting to provide electrical services for the street lights in that district, or such other entities having an interest in such service. The city shall not be responsible for any such costs, except as provided herein.
(d)
The cost of electricity and maintenance for street light services, as provided by this article, shall be the responsibility of the owners of the property within the special tax district. The pro rata cost of the electricity and maintenance shall be assigned to each platted lot within the special tax district.
(e)
Administrative costs incurred by the city in the collection of the street light tax shall be the responsibility of the property owners in the district for which said costs were incurred. This administrative charge assessed each year shall be set by the mayor and city council, but shall not exceed fifteen percent of the total charges of the utility company for that district for the preceding year; provided. Provided further, that the mayor and city council shall be authorized to establish different administrative charges for different special tax districts and to adjust the respective rates from time to time by resolution, after taking into consideration all of the cost factors involved in collecting the charges contemplated by this article, including, but not limited to, the delinquency rate within a particular special tax district. The pro rata portion of administrative charges for the tax district for each lot shall be charged to the owners of the property. Notwithstanding anything herein, the minimum annual administrative charge for any special purpose street lighting tax district shall be fifty dollars.
(f)
The total charge to property owners for street light services in a new residential subdivision shall be on a per lot basis as is determined by resolution of the mayor and city council, giving consideration for the electrical service and maintenance charges from the utility company for street light services provided within the district; the administrative costs incurred by the city in the collection of the street light tax; and the number of lots irrespective of size or improvements on such lots.
(g)
The total charge to property owners for street light services in a commercial development shall be based either on a per lot basis, lot frontage basis, total square footage of improvements on said lots, or by some combination of assessment as is determined by resolution of the mayor and city council, giving consideration for the electrical service and maintenance charges from the utility company for street light services provided within the district; the administrative costs incurred by the city in the collection of the street light tax; and the number, size, frontage, or amount of improvements on said lots in said development.
(Ord. No. 10-06-035, § 2, 6-7-10)
3.10.140 - Collection and responsibility for administration.
(a)
The charges for street lighting in each street light district shall be paid as a street light district fee, and shall be collected concurrently with the ad valorem tax billed to each lot owner within each street light tax district. The date for determining ownership of each lot shall be January 1st of each year.
(b)
The administration payment and accounting of the services herein authorized shall be the responsibility of the mayor and city council or some department of the county government to which that responsibility shall be assigned by the mayor and city council. Any proration of such charges between owners of lots to which title is transferred during any year shall be the responsibility of the sellers and buyers of said lots.
(c)
The office of the tax commissioner of the county shall be responsible for the timely collection of the taxes, fees and assessments assessed to each street light tax district and shall remit said sums to the mayor and city council in the same manner as other taxes, fees, and/or assessments are remitted.
(Ord. No. 10-06-035, § 2, 6-7-10)
3.10.150 - Lien created.
In addition to any other rights of collection for late or unpaid charges, the city shall have all rights available under the laws of the state for assessment, the creation of a lien upon the property of the owner receiving the service provided, together with all rights of execution, levy, foreclosure and sale.
(Ord. No. 10-06-035, § 2, 6-7-10)
3.10.160 - Deposit required; forfeiture.
In addition to other charges authorized in this article, the mayor and council shall have the right to require deposits from the lot owners receiving the street light service in any or all such street light tax districts created hereunder in an amount sufficient to cover the estimated cost of such service for the respective lot until the due date for the tax bill containing the lot owners' share of expenses for the lighting service. This deposit shall be held by the city as security for payment for services rendered pursuant to this article, and the city shall have the authority to setoff against the deposit for nonpayment for services at such times and on such occasions as shall be determined by the city. Notice of such setoff shall be given to the lot owner by mail at the address of the property serviced by the street lights, although advance notice of such setoff shall not be required. In the event of a reduction in the deposit of a property owner because of failure to pay charges assessed pursuant to this article, the lot owner shall be required to restore the deposit to its required level upon request by the city. Any required deposit as contemplated herein shall be retained by the city, so long as the street light tax district is in existence. Upon termination of the existence of such district, any refund of such deposits will be made to the record owner of said lot on the date of termination.
(Ord. No. 10-06-035, § 2, 6-7-10)
3.10.170 - Lighting in existing residential areas and subdivisions; creation of street light districts; constructive knowledge of this article and the street light districts created pursuant to said article.
(a)
Any property owner in an area of the city may present a request for the creation of a street light district to the city manager or his designee. The request shall designate boundaries for a street lighting district which shall serve the lot owner presenting the request and other property owners within said geographical area. The request shall include a plat or plats showing the proposed street lighting district. The office of the city manager shall then provide a petition for the creation of the proposed street lighting district to be circulated by the presenter of the request among the lot owners in the proposed district. If the proposed street lighting district extends beyond the bounds of an existing subdivision or a new subdivision and includes lots or parcels outside the existing or new subdivision, then equally seventy-five percent of the owners of the lots within the existing or new subdivision and seventy-five percent of the owners outside the existing or new subdivision and within the proposed district must sign the petition in order to be presented to the mayor and city council. In all other instances, seventy-five percent of the owners of lots within the proposed street lighting district must sign the petition for the creation of a street lighting district for the petition to be presented to the mayor and city council for consideration. Unless one hundred percent of the lot owners have signed the petition, a public hearing shall be held by the mayor and city council after having been advertised one time in the official organ of the county not less than fifteen days before the public hearing; and a sign or signs shall be posted in the proposed street lighting district giving notice of the hearing, at least fifteen days before the public hearing.
(b)
The petition for creation of the proposed street light district must be returned to the city manager within ninety days after it is obtained from such manager's office. The city manager or his designee shall verify the signatures on the petition and that the requisite numbers of property owners have signed said petition.
(c)
By signing the petition for the creation of a new street light district, all petitioners within a proposed street light district shall be deemed to have actual or constructive notice of this article and all the provisions contained herein. In consideration of the street light services provided pursuant to this article, said petitioners shall agree to abide by and comply with all the provisions of this article.
(d)
All successors-in-interest to the lot owners within a certain district at the time of the creation of said street light tax district shall be deemed to have constructive notice of this article and the existing street light district wherein the lot is located due to the existence of public records containing such information.
(e)
Upon receipt of a petition as provided herein and after a public hearing, if one is required, the mayor and city council shall take under consideration the establishment of the requested special tax district. The mayor and city council, in its sole discretion, may vote to create one or more of such districts, but nothing contained herein shall be construed as creating an obligation on the mayor and city council to create any one or more of such districts. Likewise, nothing contained herein shall create any absolute right by any property owner or owners to the creation of any such district. The discretion as to whether to create any such district shall be that solely of the mayor and city council, based upon the evidence available at the time of determination. Further, the creation by the mayor and city council of one or more such districts shall not create an absolute right by petitioners for another district or districts to the creation of such other district, nor shall the creation of one or more districts generate a precedent or obligation of the mayor and city council regarding the creation of additional special tax districts. The petition for each such special tax district shall be considered by the mayor and city council based upon the totality of the evidence regarding each respective proposed special tax district. The decision of the mayor and city council, in the exercise of its discretion, shall be final.
(f)
The decision of the mayor and city council to create special street lighting district shall be evidenced by resolution adopted at a regular or special meeting of the mayor and city council. The form of said resolution shall be substantially in the form attached hereto as appendix "A" [attached to Ordinance No. 10-06-035].
(Ord. No. 10-06-035, § 2, 6-7-10)
3.10.180 - Street light construction by property developers for new residential subdivisions; constructive knowledge of street light districts created under this article.
(a)
All developers of new residential subdivisions, at the time of the submission of the preliminary plats or drawings of the development to the community development department, shall present a proposed street light service for the development and the establishment of a special street light tax district for the subdivision. The proposal presented by the developer must be approved by the mayor and city council prior to installation of street lights in the development. All fixtures and poles to be used shall be approved not only by the mayor and city council but also by the utility company which will provide electricity to the street lights and which will be responsible for the maintenance of the facilities. Upon approval by the mayor and city council and receipt of written approval from the utility company regarding the fixtures and poles, the mayor and city council may, upon petition, consider the creation of a special street light tax district for said subdivision. All rights and obligations shall be the same as set forth in Section 3.10.170(e) above.
(b)
The developer shall be billed by the city or utility provider for those charges contemplated in Section 3.10.130(f) above for the calendar year of creation of the special tax district and the calendar year thereafter. For subsequent years owners shall be billed their pro rata share of said costs in the same manner as existing subdivisions which are designated as special tax districts. In addition, all purchases of lots located in a new subdivision which has been designated as a special tax district as provided herein shall be deemed to have constructive notice of said district and the obligation imposed thereby due to the public records containing such information.
(c)
All successors in-interest to a developer in a new residential subdivision wherein a street light district has been approved under this section shall have constructive notice of said district due to the existence of public records containing such information.
(Ord. No. 10-06-035, § 2, 6-7-10)
3.10.190 - Street light construction by property developers for commercial developments; constructive knowledge of street light districts created under this article.
(a)
All developers of commercial developments, either at the time of the submission of the preliminary plats or drawings of the development to the community development department but before any public rights-of-way have been accepted by the city, may present a proposed street light service for the development and the establishment of a special street light tax district for the commercial development. The proposal presented by the developer must be approved by the mayor and city council prior to installation of street lights in the development. All fixtures and poles to be used shall be approved not only by the mayor and city council but also by the utility company which will provide electricity to the street lights and which will be responsible for the maintenance of the facilities. Upon approval by the mayor and city council and receipt of written approval from the utility company regarding the fixtures and poles, the mayor and city council may, upon petition, consider the creation of a special street light tax district for said development, including any dedication plat and final plat designating the district boundaries and method of assessment (per lot, by property frontage, by lot size, by the size of improvements on the lot, or by a combination of methods). All rights and obligations shall be the same as set forth in Section 3.10.170(e) above.
(b)
The developer shall be billed by the city or utility provider for those charges contemplated in Section 3.10.130(f) above for the calendar year of creation of the special tax district and the calendar year thereafter. For subsequent years owners shall be billed their pro rata share of said costs in the same manner as existing subdivisions which are designated as special tax districts. In addition, all purchases of lots located in a commercial development which has been designated as a special tax district as provided herein shall be deemed to have constructive notice of said district and the obligation imposed thereby due to the public records containing such information.
(c)
All successors in-interest to a developer in a commercial development wherein a street light district has been approved under this section shall have constructive notice of said district due to the existence of public records containing such information.
(Ord. No. 10-06-035, § 2, 6-7-10)
3.10.200 - Pre-payment for services.
(a)
Upon submission of the petition as contemplated by Section 3.10.170 above, or request for consideration after mayor and city council approval as contemplated by Section 3.10.180 or Section 3.10.190 above, there shall be delivered to the mayor and city council by the party or parties seeking tax district creation under this chapter, sufficient certified funds to pay for the estimated total costs, including administration fees, of providing the services for the special street lighting tax district from the time of inception of said tax district until the end of the calendar year in which the tax district is created, said sum to be deposited by the city and drawn upon to pay the expenses of street light services until the ad valorem tax bills for the year in which the tax district is created are due, which said tax bills shall include the charges to the respective property owners for the next calendar year as hereinafter provided. The total cost based on any previous year's actual usage multiplied by the current cost of the respective utility provider for electricity and maintenance, including administrative costs, for the calendar year following the year of creation, shall be billed on the ad valorem tax notices due on December 20th of the year of creation and likewise for each year thereafter.
(b)
The requirements of this section are supplemental to, and not a substitute for, any requirements imposed pursuant to Section 3.10.160 above.
(c)
A special fund shall be established to provide for certain capital expenditures for lighting of street intersections or in other areas of the city of Locust Grove where there is substantial engineering evidence, traffic accidents, or incidents of crime presented to the mayor and city council. Said funding amount shall be established by the mayor and council in the annual budget and shall cover the amounts for poles, fixtures, bulbs, and installation of said lighting appurtenances.
(Ord. No. 10-06-035, § 2, 6-7-10)
3.10.210 - Authority to contract with public utilities.
The mayor and city council may contract with the public utilities for the purpose of carrying out the terms of this article. Annually, prior to the first day of October, the city manager, or his designee, shall secure a guaranteed rate from the applicable public utilities for the forthcoming year of service in such street light tax districts, as tax assessment for the service is calculated and billed in advance. Should the public utilities fail or refuse to guarantee the rate for the forthcoming year, the mayor and city council may resolve to terminate or eliminate the applicable street light tax districts according to Section 3.10.220.
(Ord. No. 10-06-035, § 2, 6-7-10)
3.10.220 - Termination/elimination of street light tax district by city; by lot owners within district.
(a)
Termination by city. The mayor and city council may terminate or abolish any and all street light tax districts within the city by resolution. Any such termination or abolition shall be effective January 1st of the year following the adoption of said resolution. Upon said termination, the county shall not be responsible for the collection of fees, assessments, and taxes for electrical services rendered for street lights after the date of termination and shall not be responsible for the charges of the utility company for electricity for said street lights after termination.
(b)
Termination by lot owners within the district. At any time, the property owners within a street light district may request the mayor and city council to terminate said district by submission of a petition containing the signatures of the owners of seventy-five percent of the lots within the district. Based upon all available information, the mayor and city council shall be authorized but not required to grant the property owners' request to terminate the street light tax district. Any such termination shall be effective January 1st of the year following the adoption by the mayor and city council of the termination resolution.
(Ord. No. 10-06-035, § 2, 6-7-10)
3.10.230 - Federal, state laws to prevail in conflicts.
If any provision of this article is in conflict with any state or federal law, or with any rule, regulation or order of any agency of this state or federal agency having jurisdiction of the subject matter of this article, it is hereby deemed to be the intention of the mayor and city council that the state or federal law or rule, regulation or order, as the case may be, shall prevail so that the remaining portion of this article shall be deemed to be of full force and effect.
(Ord. No. 10-06-035, § 2, 6-7-10)
3.10.240 through 3.10.300 - Reserved.
Chapter 3.12 - CLAIMS AGAINST THE CITY
Sections:

3.12.010 - All claims to be in writing—Form of claims.
A.
All claims for damage or loss against the city are to be in writing, attested to under oath, and signed and dated by the claimant; if a claim is submitted by a claimant's representative, the claimant must nevertheless attest to and sign the claim.
B.
The claim is to be addressed to the mayor and council, and shall be entitled "Notice of Claim."
(Ord. 7/9/90 § 1)
3.12.020 - Delivery of notice of claim.
All notices of claims are to be hand delivered to the city clerk, who shall date stamp the receipt on the notice of claim itself; alternatively, the claim may be mailed to the attention of the city clerk, by registered mail, return receipt requested, and the city clerk shall date stamp the receipt of each notice of claim on the notice itself.
(Ord. 7/9/90 § 2)
3.12.030 - Content of notice of claim.
All notices of claims shall describe in detail:
A.
The date, time and place of the incident or loss in question;
B.
The identity of any city employees or officials involved or connected in any way with the incident or loss;
C.
The nature of the incident allegedly resulting in the damage or loss;
D.
The dollar amount of the claimant's alleged injury or loss; and
E.
A description of all meetings or discussions between the claimant and any city employee or official wherein the claim was discussed or investigated, including:
1.
The date and place of the meeting or discussion;
2.
The identity of each and every city employee or official involved in the meeting or discussion, and
3.
A description of any statements or representations made by any city employee or official.
(Ord. 7/9/90 § 3)
3.12.040 - Accompanying documents in support of claim.
Each notice of claim shall be accompanied by any and all receipts, estimates, appraisals, bills, invoices or other documents upon which the claimant relies in support of his/her claim for damage or loss.
(Ord. 7/9/90 § 4)
3.12.050 - Official custodian of notices of claims—Notice of claim file.
A.
The city clerk is designated as the official custodian of all notices of claims. The city clerk shall maintain a notice of claim file, which shall include an index of each notice of claim received by the city; the index shall be maintained in two separate forms:
1.
By the claimant's last name (including the date received); and
2.
By chronological order received (i.e., by date received).
B.
The original of each notice of claim and all accompanying documents thereto shall be kept in the notice of claim file.
(Ord. 7/9/90 § 5)
3.12.060 - Procedure upon receipt of notice of claim.
Upon receipt of a notice of claim, the city clerk shall apprise the claimant of any deficiencies in the form or content thereof; the city clerk may return any notice of claim not in substantial compliance with this chapter, and shall maintain a written record of all notices of claims so returned. Upon receipt of each notice of claim in compliance with this chapter, and after indexing same in the notice of claim file referred to in Section 3.12.050, the city clerk shall immediately transmit a copy of the notice of claim (together with any accompanying documents) to:
A.
The mayor and council;
B.
The city attorney; and
C.
The agent for each insurance company that may possibly have coverage for the loss or claim (by certified mail, return receipt requested).
(Ord. 7/9/90 § 6)
Chapter 3.16 - CREDIT CARD PAYMENTS
Sections:

3.16.010 - Payment by credit card.
All sums due the city, including but not limited to, ad valorem taxes, occupation taxes, fines, forfeitures, penalties, license fees, permit fees and registration fees, may be paid by the use of credit cards. The mayor shall establish the procedures for acceptance of credit card payments for amounts due the city. The mayor is authorized to enter into agreements with credit card issuers to facilitate the acceptance of credit card payments by the city.
(Ord. 96-7.8.C § 1 (part))
3.16.020 - Credit card agreement.
A credit card agreement may provide for the acceptance of the credit card payments from the credit card company at a discount from the face amount of the payment or for the withholding of administrative fees from the face amount of the payment. Such discounted fee is granted in exchange for the improved governmental cash flow, reduction of administrative expenses and increased public convenience as a result of credit card payments.
Any agreement with a credit card issuer for the acceptance of payments via credit shall contain a provision that the agreement may be canceled at any time by the city.
(Ord. 96-7.8.C § 1 (part))
3.16.030 - Surcharge.
A surcharge equal to the administrative fees charged by the credit card issuer to the city shall be imposed upon any person making a payment by credit card, when such surcharges are permitted by the operating rules and regulations of the credit card issuer.
(Ord. 96-7.8.C § 1 (part))
3.16.040 - Liability for payment of obligation.
No person making payment by credit card to the city is relieved from liability for the underlying obligation except to the extent that the city actually receives final payment of the obligation in cash or its equivalent. If final payment is not made by the credit card issuer or other guarantor of payment in the credit card transaction, the underlying obligation for the money due is not extinguished. All applicable penalties, interest and collection fees on the unpaid amount shall be due from the person making the payment by credit card.
Such penalties, interest and fees on an unpaid amount shall accrue from the date the unpaid obligation was first due under the applicable statute or ordinance. The city shall follow the collection procedures for the unpaid amount that would have otherwise been applicable had the credit card payment not been attempted.
(Ord. 96-7.8.C § 1 (part))
Chapter 3.20 - CHECK PAYMENTS
Sections:

3.20.010 - Payment by check.
All sums due the city, including but not limited to ad valorem taxes, occupation taxes, fines, forfeitures, penalties, license fees, permit fees and registration fees, may be paid by the use of personal checks issued by the payor. The mayor shall establish the procedures for acceptance of check payments for amounts due the city.
(Ord. 01-05.03A § 2)
3.20.020 - Charge for returned checks.
Upon notification by a financial institution that a check presented to the city will be returned for insufficient funds ("NSF"), because it is not properly payable, or for any other reason, the city shall be authorized to charge the payor or individual issuing said check a penalty equal to twenty-five dollars for each returned check or instrument. The city may, but shall not be required to, post a notice to this effect at one or more places where payments are delivered to the city.
(Ord. 01-05.03A § 3)
3.20.030 - Liability for payment of obligation.
No person making payment by check to the city is released from liability for the underlying obligation except to the extent that this city actually receives final payment of the obligation in cash or its equivalent. If final payment is not paid by the payor, a financial institution, or any other guarantor of payment in the transaction, the underlying obligation for money due is not extinguished. All applicable penalties, interest and collection fees on the unpaid amount shall be due from the person making the payment by check.
Such penalties, interest and fees on any unpaid amount shall accrue from the date the unpaid obligation is first due under the applicable statute or ordinance. The city shall follow the collection procedures for the unpaid amount that would have otherwise been applicable had the check payment not been attempted.
(Ord. 01-05.03A § 4)
