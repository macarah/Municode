CHAPTER 5.28 - SECONDARY ELECTRIC SUPPLIERS

5.28.010 - Gross receipts business tax established—Verified statement of revenues to be filed.
Each secondary electric supplier (including specifically electric membership corporations whether or not operated for profit), within the meaning of O.C.G.A. § 46-3-1 et seq., distributing and selling electric power within the City which is not otherwise paying a franchise fee pursuant to a franchise agreement, and whether or not the secondary electric supplier has, operates and maintains offices within the City, shall pay a gross receipts tax at the rate of four percent on any and all gross revenue derived from distribution and sale of electric power to their customers, members or purchasers within the City, according to the verified statement of the gross revenues which shall be submitted by the secondary electric supplier at the time the taxes are paid.
(Prior Code § 8-5-1; Ord. of 11-20-1995, (part))
5.28.020 - Taxes payable monthly—Taxes to be in lieu of certain other taxes.
The taxes set out in section 5.28.010 shall be due and payable to the City on the twentieth day of each month, calculated on gross revenues billed for the immediately preceding month; also, the percentage of the gross revenues prescribed in this Chapter to be collected shall be in lieu of all other license and business or occupation taxes, but shall not at any time be considered to interfere with, or in any way prevent, the collection of ad valorem taxes upon the property of the secondary electric supplier in the same manner as all other property, real and personal, in the City is taxed.
(Prior Code § 8-5-2; Ord. of 11-20-1995, (part))
5.28.030 - Electric service within the City.
Secondary electric suppliers distributing and selling electric power within the City, and which do not have a franchise agreement with the City, do not acquire any additional rights by the payment of the tax levied under this Chapter other than those rights granted under the Georgia Electric Territorial Service Act.
(Prior Code § 8-5-3; Ord. of 11-20-1995, (part))
5.28.040 - Enforcement and administration.
A.
The City Clerk shall be responsible for the administration and enforcement of this Chapter, and shall have the power to prepare and provide the necessary forms and for the collection of the tax levied.
B.
The City Clerk shall issue executions for the collection of all outstanding and unpaid taxes imposed and assessed by this Chapter. The unpaid taxes and executions shall be collected in the manner provided by law for the collection of other taxes due the City.
(Prior Code § 8-5-4; Ord. of 11-20-1995, (part))
