CHAPTER 17.28 - R-100 SINGLE-FAMILY RESIDENTIAL DISTRICT

Sections:
17.28.010 - Purpose and permitted uses.
A.
The R-100 single-family residential district is intended to provide suitable open space and residential areas for single-family detached dwellings at low to moderate densities with access to public water and sewerage.
B.
Within any R-100 Single-Family Residential District, the following uses shall be permitted:
1.
Single-family detached dwellings.
2.
Customary residential accessory buildings or uses, provided such shall be permitted only in a rear yard and shall be situated not less than three (3) feet from any property line; and further provided that in the case of corner lots, such buildings or structures shall be set back at least forty (40) feet from any side street right-of-way line.
3.
Parks, playgrounds, community centers, swimming pools, and other recreational facilities, operated on a nonprofit basis, provided no building or structure is located less than fifty (50) feet from any property line, and further provided that provisions are made for a buffer strip that is twenty-five (25) feet wide along all side and rear lot lines and a planted landscape strip of at least twenty (20) feet is provided at the front lot line.
4.
Golf courses.
5.
Residential group projects subject to provisions of Sections 17.88.070 and 17.88.080.
6.
Public works and public utility facilities such as distribution lines, transmission lines and towers, electric substations, water tanks and towers, pumping stations, water treatment plants, telephone exchanges, and fire stations, provided:
a.
Such facilities are essential to the service of the immediate area;
b.
No vehicles or materials shall be permitted, except in the case of fire stations;
c.
All buildings and facilities shall be set back at least fifty (50) feet from all property lines and shall be designed and landscaped in such a way as to blend in with the surrounding areas;
d.
All dangerous apparatus shall be enclosed by a black, vinyl coated chain link fence at least eight (8) feet in height;
e.
There shall be a buffer strip which is twenty-five (25) feet wide along all lot lines, except openings for necessary driveways. No such buffer shall, however, extend nearer to a street right-of-way line than the established building set back line of adjoining lots.
7.
Home occupations, subject to the provisions of Section 17.20.060.
8.
Residential subdivision amenity centers provided by developers or homeowner's associations.
(Zoning Ord. § 805, 1979; Ord. No. 93-002.0; Ord. No. 04-12-06-B, § VIII, 12-6-2004; Ord. No. 08-01-22(C), § X, 1-22-2008)
17.28.020 - Special Uses.
Within any R-100 Single-Family Residential District, the following uses may be allowed upon obtaining a Special Use Permit from the City Council:
A.
Senior Condominium Center.
B.
Churches.
C.
Cemeteries.
D.
Schools.
E.
Public buildings.
(Ord. No. 04-12-06-B, § IX, 12-6-2004; Ord. No. 08-01-22(C), § XI, 1-22-2008)
