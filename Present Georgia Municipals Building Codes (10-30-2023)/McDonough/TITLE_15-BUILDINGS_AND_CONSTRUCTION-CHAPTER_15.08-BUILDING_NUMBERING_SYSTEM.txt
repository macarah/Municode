CHAPTER 15.08 - BUILDING NUMBERING SYSTEM

Sections:
15.08.010 - Numbering of buildings—Assignment.
Street numbers for dwelling units and places of business on all public streets and street numbers and/or building numbers for dwelling units and places of business within apartment projects and nonresidential developments located on private streets shall be assigned by the city planning department in accordance with administrative procedures.
(Prior Code, § 9-8(part); Ord. No. 90-8.1, (part))
15.08.020 - Posting.
Each dwelling unit and place of business shall have light reflective numbers posted and maintained in a prominent place on the property, visible from the street providing public access, the address of the dwelling unit or place of business:
A.
For residences using mailboxes: two-inch high light reflective numbers with contrasting background;
B.
For residences which do not use mailboxes and within one hundred (100) feet of the roadway: three-inch light reflective numbers, with contrasting background, visible from the roadway, and placed on the dwelling unit;
C.
For residences which do not use mailboxes and are more than one hundred (100) feet from the roadway: three-inch high light reflective numbers visible from the roadway;
D.
For all commercial establishments: three-inch high light reflective numbers with contrasting backgrounds;
E.
All existing buildings shall be made to conform to the above requirements within six months from date of adoption of the ordinance codified in this chapter.
(Prior Code, § 9-8(part); Ord. No. 90-8.1, (part))
15.08.030 - Violations—Penalties.
Any owner or occupant of a building which in the opinion of the community development department director has failed to comply with the regulations of this title shall be notified personally or by registered or certified mail. If after 15 calendar days from receipt of notification the requirements of this title have not been fulfilled, the mayor and council may authorize the purchase and placement of building numbers as directed by this title by the community development department director or public safety director or his designee and charge the expenses of the same to the owner.
(Prior Code, § 9-8(part); Ord. of 10-3-1995, § 2(part); Ord. No. 90-8.1, (part); Ord. No. 05-02-07A, § 17, 2-7-2005; Ord. No. 06-05-01(A), § 19, 5-1-2006)
