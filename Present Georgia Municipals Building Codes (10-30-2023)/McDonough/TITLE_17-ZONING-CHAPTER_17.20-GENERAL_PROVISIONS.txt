CHAPTER 17.20 - GENERAL PROVISIONS

17.20.010 - Nonconforming use.
A.
Any building, structure or use existing at the time of the enactment or subsequent amendment of this Title 17, but not in conformity with its regulations and provisions, may be continued except that the use of a principal nonconforming building, structure or land shall not be:
1.
Changed to another non-conforming use;
2.
Re-established after discontinuance for one (1) year;
3.
Enlarged, altered and extended, except in conformity with this Title 17;
4.
Rebuilt, altered or repaired after damage exceeding sixty (60) percent of its replacement cost, as determined by the community development director or his/her designee, except in conformity with this Title and provided such rehabilitation, alteration or repair is completed within twelve (12) months of such damage.
B.
All nonconforming buildings, structures and uses shall be discontinued and made to conform within the time periods set forth below:
1.
All nonconforming uses of land where no buildings are involved: six (6) months from date of adoption of this title;
2.
All nonconforming uses of land on which are located demountable or portable buildings or structures: twelve (12) months from date of adoption of this title.
C.
Nothing in this Title 17 shall require any change in the plan, construction or intended use of a building which is under construction at the effective date of this regulation and upon which actual building construction has been carried on diligently.
(Zoning Ord. § 700, 1979; Ord. of 10-3-1995, § 2(part); Ord. No. 04-12-06-B, § IV, 12-6-2004; Ord. No. 08-01-22(C), § IV, 1-22-2008)

17.20.030 - Restricted parking areas for business and industrial uses.
The establishment and operation of a restricted parking area shall be permitted in such parts of any residential district except RA-200 and R-100 districts as abut, either directly or across an alley or street, a commercial or industrial district subject to the following conditions and requirements:
A.
The parking lot shall be accessory to and for use in connection with one or more business or industrial establishments located in an adjoining commercial or industrial district.
B.
Such parking lot shall be situated on premises which have an area of not less than six thousand (6,000) square feet, which shall be at least fifty (50) feet either contiguous to or across an alley or street from a commercial or industrial district.
C.
Such parking lot shall be used solely for the parking of passenger vehicles.
D.
No commercial repair work or service of any kind other than emergency repairs shall be conducted on such parking lot.
E.
Reserved.
F.
A buffer strip shall be provided along all side and rear lot lines.
G.
No charge shall be made for parking except directly to the business or industrial establishments in the adjoining district who are using the lot to meet the parking space requirements of this Title 17.
H.
Each entrance and exit to and from such parking lot shall be at least twenty (20) feet distant from any adjacent property located in any residential district.
I.
The location and design of entrances, exits, surfacing, marking and lighting shall be subject to the approval of the community development director or his/her designee and planning commission.
J.
Such parking lot shall have all spaces adequately marked with paint lines, curb stones or other similar designations.
K.
Such parking lot shall be permanently maintained by the owners or the tenants for their invitees and/or licensees so long as the use exists.
L.
After giving thirty (30) days written notice of any violation of the provisions of this section, the community development director or his/her designee may revoke a permit granted for a restricted parking lot.
(Zoning Ord. § 720, 1979; Ord. of 10-3-1995, § 2(part); Ord. No. 01-08-20R, § 5, 8-20-2001)
17.20.040 - Off-street loading and unloading space.
Every building or structure used for business, trade or industry, hereafter erected, shall provide space as indicated in this section for the loading and unloading of vehicles off the street or public alley. Such space shall have access to an alley, or if there is no alley, to a street. For the purposes of this section, an off-street loading space shall have an overhead clearance of fourteen (14) feet in height above the alley or street grade.
A.
Retail Business. One space, ten feet by forty (40) feet, for each ten thousand (10,000) square feet of floor area, or fraction thereof.
B.
Wholesale and Industry. One (1) space, ten (10) feet by fifty (50) feet, for each ten thousand (10,000) square feet of floor area, or fraction thereof.
C.
Bus and Truck Terminals. Sufficient space to accommodate the maximum number of trucks and buses loading, unloading or stored at any one time.
(Zoning Ord. § 730, 1979)
17.20.050 - Curb cuts.
Curb cuts for service drives, entrances, exits and other similar facilities or public streets in other than R-100 and R-85 districts shall not be located within thirty (30) feet of any intersection or within forty (40) feet of another curb cut. The latter requirement shall not be applicable for the C-1 and C-2 districts. A curb cut shall be no greater than forty (40) feet in width and no closer than five feet to any property line unless approved by the planning commission. Service drives that serve commercial, office-professional or industrial zoning districts shall not be allowed to pass through residential zoning districts.
(Zoning Ord. § 740, 1979)
17.20.060 - Home occupations.
A.
Home occupations, as defined in Section 17.08.020, may be established in a dwelling unit in any single-family dwelling provided that the following requirements shall apply in addition to all other applicable requirements of this title for the residential district in which such uses are located.
1.
No accessory buildings or outside storage shall be used in connection with the home occupation.
2.
No internal or external alterations inconsistent with the residential use of the building may be permitted.
3.
Only vehicles used primarily as passenger vehicles shall be permitted in connection with the conduct of the customary home occupation.
4.
No equipment that interferes with radio and/or television reception shall be allowed.
5.
No chemical, electrical or mechanical equipment that is not normally a part of domestic or household equipment shall be used primarily for commercial purposes.
6.
Instruction in music, dance, arts and crafts, and similar subjects shall be limited to two (2) students at one time.
B.
Home occupations, as defined in Section 17.08.020, may be established in a dwelling unit in any multi-family dwelling provided that the following requirements shall apply in addition to all other applicable requirements of this title for the residential district in which such uses are located:
1.
No accessory buildings or outside storage shall be used in connection with the home occupation.
2.
No internal or external alterations inconsistent with the residential use of the building may be permitted.
3.
Only vehicles used primarily as passenger vehicles shall be permitted in connection with the conduct of the customary home occupation.
4.
No equipment that interferes with radio and/or television reception shall be allowed.
5.
No chemical, electrical or mechanical equipment that is not normally a part of domestic or household equipment shall be used primarily for commercial purposes.
6.
No home occupation shall be allowed in which customers, suppliers, or any other third parties associated with the home occupation are present on the premises.
7.
Approval of the property owner or its representative of the home occupation must be provided in writing which includes the name, address and phone number of the property owner or its representative.
C.
No vehicle whose gross vehicle weight rating (GVWR) is over twenty-six thousand (26,000) pounds, or a trailer with a gross vehicle weight rating (GVWR) of over ten thousand (10,000) pounds, including commercial tractor-trailers, dump trucks, wreckers, or earth moving equipment shall be parked in any residential district in connection with a home occupation conducted on the premises.
(Zoning Ord. § 750, 1979; Ord. No. 04-12-06-B, § VI, 12-6-2004; Ord. No. 05-11-21(D), § 1, 11-21-2005; Ord. No. 08-01-22(C), § V, 1-22-2008; Ord. No. 08-12-29, § IV, 12-29-2008)
17.20.070. - Accessory buildings and uses.
The following restrictions apply to accessory buildings or structures:
A.
Accessory buildings shall be located on the same lot as the principal building to which it is accessory.
B.
Accessory uses and structures are permitted within all districts subject to provisions of this title.
C.
Accessory buildings shall only be located in a required rear yard.
D.
No accessory building shall be located closer than three (3) feet to a side or rear lot line.
E.
When an accessory building is attached to the principal building by a breeze way, passageway or similar means, it shall comply with the yard requirements of the principal building to which it is accessory.
F.
No accessory building shall be located closer than twenty (20) feet to the principal building or to any other accessory building in a residential district.
G.
Accessory buildings in residentially zoned districts shall be no larger than fifty (50) percent of the principal structure subject to the following exceptions:
1.
The property is zoned RA-200 or R-100.
2.
The historic preservation committee grants a certificate of appropriateness for the reconstruction of a historic building.
(Zoning Ord. § 760, 1979; Ord. No. 04-12-06-B, § VI, 12-6-2004; Ord. No. 08-01-22(C), § VI, 1-22-2008)

17.20.090 - Building design criteria.
A.
All new buildings or existing buildings involving substantial alterations during exterior renovations (other than single-family detached structures and accessory structures) in the city shall be architecturally compatible with building(s) of architectural influence. Compatibility shall be achieved through architectural style, similar material or texture choice, similar color choice, similar building height and mass, and similar roof shape and pitch. Multi-family structures shall be treated with materials or textures and similar architectural treatments comparable with residential structures of influence.
B.

C.
Industrial Buildings. Metal buildings zoned for industrial uses shall only be required to have the exterior treatment of brick, stone, masonry, or other materials compatible with the buildings of influence on any part of the building facing a public right-of-way and on side walls. The exterior treatment on side walls not facing public right-of-way shall cover the front half of the wall or shall cover half the height of the wall for its entire length. No exterior treatment shall be required on the rear of metal industrial buildings.
D.
Rooftop Equipment. All rooftop equipment shall be completely screened from public areas. Public areas generally include streets, driveways, sidewalks, and parking areas.
E.
Buildings Adjacent to Subdivision Entrances. Buildings adjacent to the entrance of proposed or established subdivisions shall be designed so as to be comparable in design to the design of homes in the subdivision or to buildings of influence. Minimum architectural features required to achieve compatibility include, but are not limited to: roof pitch; shingles; exterior treatment of brick, stone, masonry, wood or siding comparable with the homes nearest to the entrance of the subdivision or to buildings of influence; and exterior colors that are comparable to the homes nearest the entrance to the subdivision. Elevation drawings shall be submitted to the planning commission for review and approval.
F.
Lighting. Neon accent lighting around windows, building facades, roof lines and outdoor signs is prohibited. Site lighting shall be limited to that necessary to provide security and shall be designed to prevent being a nuisance to adjoining residential properties.
G.
Service Station Canopies. Service station canopies shall be architecturally compatible with the service station building and their color schemes shall be coordinated.
H.
Accessory Buildings and Structures. Accessory buildings and structures shall be of the same material and shall be designed to be compatible with the main building or structure.
I.
Alternates Allowed. When specifically requested by the owner/developer, the planning commission may consider other buildings as buildings of architectural influence for a particular project. The planning commission shall have the authority to approve alternate designs when, in their opinion, the alternate plan meets the intent of this section.
(Ord. No. 98-06-01A, 6-1-1998; Ord. No. 08-12-29, § V, 12-29-2008)
17.20.100 - Buffer strip requirements.
A.
All buffer strips shall consist of a planting strip at least twenty-five (25) feet in width, six (6) feet in height, composed of deciduous or evergreen trees or a mixture of each, spaced not more than twenty (20) feet apart and not less than one (1) row of dense shrubs, spaced not more than five (5) feet apart.
B.
All buffer strips shall be planted and maintained in a healthy, growing condition by the property owner.
C.
No buffer strip shall extend nearer to a street right-of-way line than the established building setback line of the adjoining lot.
D.
The area within the buffer strip shall not be counted as part of the lot area.
(Ord. No. 04-12-06-B, § VII, 12-6-2004; Ord. No. 08-01-22(C), § VII, 1-22-2008)
