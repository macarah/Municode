Sec. 26-201. - Title.
This article shall be known as the "Brantley County Junkyard Ordinance" and is hereby enacted by the board of commissioners.
(Ord. of 6-8-2010, § 1)
Sec. 26-202. - Purpose.
This article prescribes rules and regulations relating to the exercise of effective control of junkyards in the unincorporated areas of Brantley County, Georgia, adjacent to or within 1,000 feet of a residential area, church or public school, or located on primary residential roads or streets.
(Ord. of 6-8-2010, § 2)
Sec. 26-203. - Definitions.
The following terms as used in this article, shall have the following meaning unless the context requires otherwise:
Automobile graveyard means any establishment which is maintained or used for storing, buying, or selling wrecked, scrapped, ruined or dismantled motor vehicles or motor vehicle parts.
Board of commissioners shall mean the Brantley County Board of Commissioners.
County road means any road, street or highway which is a portion of a county road or secondary state road system.
Establish means to construct, build, raise, assemble, place, affix, create or in any way bring into existence.
Junk means old or scrap copper, brass, batteries, paper, trash, rubber, debris, waste; junked, dismantled, or wrecked automobiles, or parts thereof; or iron, steel, and other old scrap ferrous or nonferrous material.
Junkyard means any establishment which is maintained or used for storing, buying, or selling junk or for an automobile graveyard; and the term shall include scrap processing establishments.
Maintain means to allow to exist.
Nonconforming junkyard means any junkyard which was lawfully established and in existence for _______ (_____) years, holds a valid Brantley County Business License and any part of junkyard located within 1,000 feet of any residence, church or public school, except:
(1)
Those which are screened by natural objects, plantings, fences, or other appropriate means so as not to be visible to a residence, church or public school or otherwise removed from sight; and
(2)
Those located within an unzoned industrial area, which area shall be determined from actual land use as defined by the board of commissioners; and
(3)
Those not visible from the main traveled way of well traveled county roads or from secondary state highways; and
(4)
Those not located within close proximity to an established residential subdivision, and does not use residential roads or streets to access their property.
Person means any individual, firm, agency, company, association, partnership, business trust, joint stock company, body politic or corporation.
Screening means the use of any vegetative planting, fencing, ornamental wall of masonry, or other architectural treatment or device, earthen embankment, or a combination of any of these which will effectively hide from view any deposit of junk from a residence, church or public school, within a distance of 1,000 feet, on a yearround basis. Screening does not include the use of junk.
Unlawful junkyards means any junkyard which comes into existence after the adoption of this article.
(1)
Which is located within 1,000 feet of the nearest residence, church or public school and is not screened by natural objects, plantings, fences, or other appropriate means so as to be visible from the residence, church or public school; or
(2)
Is located within an unzoned industrial area of the unincorporated area of the county; and
(3)
Is visible from the well-traveled county roads or secondary state highways in the unincorporated area of the county; and
(4)
Is located on a primarily residential road in the unincorporated area of the county.
Visible means capable of being seen without visual or optical aid by a person of normal visual acuity.
Zone of control means all areas within the boundaries of this county which are adjacent to and within 1,000 feet a residential area, church or public school within Brantley County.
(Ord. of 6-8-2010, § 3; Amd. of 4-7-2016)
Sec. 26-204. - Policy.
In carrying out the purpose of this article:
(1)
Every effort shall be made to screen nonconforming junkyards which are to be continued as on-going businesses.
(2)
Nonconforming junkyards are to be relocated only as a last resort.
(Ord. of 6-8-2010, § 4)
Sec. 26-205. - Measurement of distance.
Distance from the edge of a residential property, church or public school line shall be measured horizontally along a straight line.
(Ord. of 6-8-2010, § 5)
Sec. 26-206. - Screening.
(a)
Responsibility for screening.
(1)
Any junkyard lawfully in existence [on] effective date of this article, which is within 1,000 feet of the nearest residence, church or public school.
(2)
Junkyards established subsequent to adoption of this article by Brantley County Board of Commissioners in non-industrial areas within 100 feet of a residence, church or public school must provide for adequate screening located between the junkyard and residences, churches or public schools and at the expense of the person owning the junkyard. Screening provided by such owner must be in compliance with this article and of a type approved by the board of commissioners. Those junkyards which are established subsequent to the adoption of this article and located within zones of control and which cannot be adequately screened shall be relocated at the owner's expense.
(b)
Every effort shall be made to screen nonconforming junkyards where they are to be continued as on-going businesses. Screening may be accomplished by use of natural objects, landscaping, plantings, fences, and other appropriate means, including relocating inventory on site to utilize an existing natural screen or a screenable portion of the site.
(c)
Where screening is used, it must, upon completion of the screening project, effectively screen the junkyard from the residential areas, churches or public schools on a yearround basis, and be compatible with the surroundings.
(Ord. of 6-8-2010, § 6)
Sec. 26-207. - Nonconforming junkyards.
For the purposes of this article, a discontinued junkyard is any junkyard which was operated as a business enterprise in the past and where the proprietor is retaining the inventory for the present but not actively engaged in buying or selling junk.
(1)
The nonconforming junkyard may continue as long as it is not extended, or changed in use. Once a junkyard has been made conforming, the subsequent placement of junk so that it may be seen, above or beyond a screen, or if the junk otherwise becomes visible, said junkyard shall then be treated the same as the establishment of a new junkyard.
(2)
A nonconforming junkyard must have been in lawful existence and operation on adoption of this article, and may continue to exist and operate (1) as long as it continues to be lawfully maintained, (2) is not extended or enlarged, (3) is not substantially changed in use, or (4) is not located on a primary residential street or road.
(3)
In order for a junkyard to continue as a nonconforming junkyard, there must be existing property rights in the junkyard and junk affected by this article. A junkyard may not continue to exist if it has been abandoned, destroyed, or voluntarily discontinued. Brantley County shall consider the following criteria as the minimum criteria which must exist and must be applied before any determination can be made by the board of commissioners that a junkyard or junk has been abandoned:
a.
The junkyard has not operated or conducted business for one year or more; or
b.
The junkyard is in need of substantial repair or maintenance; or
c.
The junkyard owner cannot be found or determined after a diligent search has been made by a building inspector as verified by his affidavit which shall set forth all the steps taken and attempts made to locate and determine said owner.
(Ord. of 6-8-2010, § 7)
Sec. 26-208. - Unlawful junkyards.
The Brantley County Code Enforcement Department, through its designated representatives shall inspect junkyards periodically to insure that they are being operated in accordance with the applicable laws, rules and regulations pertaining to junkyard control, and shall require that all screening materials be maintained in such a manner as to accomplish the objective of this article. If the inspection discloses that a junkyard is being operated in violation of this article, the owner shall be informed in writing, sent by registered mail, stating the violation and instructing him to make the corrections within 60 days from the date of the letter.
(Ord. of 6-8-2010, § 8)
Sec. 26-209. - Screening criteria.
(a)
[Plan for screening.] Plan for screening of junkyards established after the adoption of this article.
(1)
The screening shall be located on the owner's land and not on any part of the public land.
(2)
The screen shall be in place prior to the time the junk is deposited.
(3)
At no time after the screen is established shall the junk be stacked high enough to be visible above the screen. No junk shall be placed outside of the screened areas.
(4)
Fences must be located in such a manner as to not be hazardous to the traveling public.
(5)
The construction of fences shall be uniform and no patchwork type of construction shall be permitted.
(6)
When fences are painted, the paint used shall be of a color so as to blend into the environs of the area.
(b)
Acceptable fencing materials. Acceptable fencing shall include fences of steel or other metals, durable woods such as heart cypress, redwood, or other wood treated with a preservative, or walls of masonry. Some of the types acceptable are:
(1)
Chain link type with aluminum, steel, plastic, or wooden slat inserts.
(2)
Wooden types of basket weave, palisade, louver, or other suitable design.
(3)
Walls of masonry including plain or ornamental concrete block, block, brick, stone, or other suitable masonry material.
(4)
An earth wall, if properly designed, grassed, and landscaped.
Note: The above types of fencing and walls appearing in subparagraphs (b)(1), (2), (3), and (4) above are given for examples only. Any other design of fencing constructed of other materials may be submitted to the board of commissioners for consideration.
(c)
Plant material.
(1)
All plant material shall be predominantly evergreen and approved by the board of commissioners.
(2)
The minimum size of plant material at the time of planting shall be as follows:
a.
Large trees deciduous: 1″—1½″ caliper (Maples, Sycamore, Sweetgum).
b.
Small trees: 4′—5′ in height (Hawthorn, Crepe Myrtle, etc.).
c.
Evergreen trees: 4′—5′ in height (Pinus, Magnolia, etc.).
d.
Evergreen shrubs: 2½′—3′ in height (including Elaeaqnus, Abelia, etc.).
(d)
Maintenance. The owner or owners of any junkyard shall maintain the screening established initially, doing such painting and repairs to keep any fences, walls or other structural material in good appearance. Plant material shall be watered, cultivated, or mulched, and given any required maintenance including spraying for insect control, to keep the planting in a good healthy condition. Dead or destroyed plant material will be removed immediately and shall be replaced during the next spring or fall planting season.
The replacement plants shall be at least as large as the initial planting.
(e)
Junkyards screened. The owner or owners of any junkyard lawfully in existence on the adoption of this article, and which has been screened from view may not extend the deposit of junk beyond such screening so as to be visible from the main traveled way of the highway or a residence, church or public school. Any such extension shall be screened by the owner at his own expense and such screening shall be subject to these rules and regulations. Piling of junk above planned height of screening established by the department is prohibited.
(Ord. of 6-8-2010, § 9)
Sec. 26-210. - Violations and penalties.
(a)
Any person, firm, corporation or agent who shall violate a provision of this article, or failure to comply therewith, or with any requirements thereof, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor. Each such person shall be considered guilty of a separate offense for each and every day or portion thereof during which any violation of any of the provisions of the junkyard article is committed or is continued.
(b)
Any violation of this article shall be governed by O.C.G.A. § 15-10-62 and O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63. The Brantley County Building Inspector may file accusations pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 15-10-62 upon the approval of such actions by the chairman of the Brantley County Board of Commissioners.
(Ord. of 6-8-2010, § 11)
