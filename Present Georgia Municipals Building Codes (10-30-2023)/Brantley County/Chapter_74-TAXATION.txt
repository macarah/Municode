ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 74-1. - [Waiver of penalties and interest—Ad valorem taxes.]
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-5-242, the board of commissioners may waive in whole or in part, the collection of any amount due to Brantley County, for penalties or interest assessed for failure to comply with the law governing assessment and collection of ad valorem taxes. The tax commissioner shall determine whether or not the default giving rise to the penalty or interest was due to a reasonable cause and not due to gross or willful neglect of the law or of regulations or instructions issued pursuant to law. The tax commissioner may recommend to the board of commissioners the waiver of penalties and interest, in whole or in part.
(b)
The waiver of penalties or interest in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-5-242 and under the provision of this section shall be subject to the following conditions:
(1)
Only interest that accrues on or after July 1, 2002 may be waived;
(2)
The taxpayer shall provide a claim in writing, under oath, stating the specific reason for the taxpayer's failure to pay the ad valorem taxes in a timely manner;
(3)
The tax commissioner advises the board of commissioners that the default giving rise to penalty or interest was not a result of:
a.
Gross or willful negligence;
b.
A disregard of law or regulations or instructions issued pursuant to law;
c.
That the taxpayer has not been delinquent in the payment of other taxes owed to Brantley County or other counties; and
d.
Taxes and penalties will impose a substantial hardship upon the taxpayer.
(c)
The board of commissioners will make the final decision as to whether to waive the penalties and interest in whole or in part.
(Ord. of 9-8-2009, §§ 1—3)

ARTICLE III. - FINANCIAL INSTITUTION BUSINESS LICENSE TAX

Sec. 74-61. - Annual business license tax.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93, there is hereby levied for the year 1984, and for each year thereafter, an annual business license tax upon all depository financial institutions located within the county at a rate of 0.25 percent of the gross receipts of such depository financial institutions. The term "gross receipts" means the same as defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93. The term "depository financial institutions" means state and national banks, state building and loan associations, and federal savings and loan associations.
(Res. of 1-1-1984(1), § 1)
Sec. 74-62. - Minimum business license tax.
The minimum annual amount of business license tax due from any depository financial institution pursuant to section 74-61 shall be $1,000.00.
(Res. of 1-1-1984(1), § 2)
Sec. 74-63. - Filing of return.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(c), each depository financial institution subject to an annual business license tax shall file a return of its gross receipts with the board of commissioners on March 1 of the year following the year in which such gross receipts are measured. Such return shall be in the manner and in the form prescribed by the commissioner of the department on the allocation method set forth in O.C.G.A § 48-6-93(d). The board of commissioners shall assess and collect the tax levied pursuant to this article based upon the information provided in such return.
(Res. of 1-1-1984(1), § 3)
Sec. 74-64. - Due date of taxes.
Annual business license taxes levied pursuant to this article shall be due no later than 30 days after filing of the return prescribed by section 74-63 unless extended by the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 1-1-1984(1), § 4)

ARTICLE IV. - GROSS PREMIUMS TAX ON INSURERS

Sec. 74-91. - Gross premiums tax on insurance other than life insurance.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2, there is hereby levied for the year 1984, and for each year thereafter, an annual tax upon each insurer doing insurance business within the unincorporated area of the county, in an amount equal to 2.5 percent of the gross direct premiums received during the calendar year on all insurance, other than life insurance, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5(2), (3), (4) and (5). The term "gross direct premiums" means the same as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2(a).
(Res. of 1-1-1984(2), §1)

DIVISION 2. - WAYNESVILLE SPECIAL FIRE PROTECTION DISTRICT

Sec. 74-141. - Tax district created.
(a)
There is hereby created a special fire tax district within Brantley County which shall be known and cited as the "Waynesville Special Fire Protection District", hereinafter sometimes referred to as "the district".
(b)
Waynesville Special Fire Protection District shall be as follows:
Beginning at the Northeast corner of Brantley County; then in a Southerly direction along the Easternmost line of Brantley County where it abuts Glynn County and later Camden County to the point where it intersects the Satilla River; thence in a Northerly direction along the Satilla River to the intersection with a straight line extrapolated from the Coffee County Road; thence Eastward along that line and on through the Coffee County Road to State Highway 110 North and the intersection with the Western boundary of Georgia Militia District #334; thence in a Northeasterly direction along the Western boundary of the Georgia Militia District #334 to the point where it abuts the Wayne County line; thence in a Westerly direction along the Northern line of the Georgia Militia District #334 where is abuts the Wayne county line back to the point of origin at the Northeast corner of Brantley County.
(Res. of 5-8-1990, § 2; Res. of 9-14-2010, §§ 1, 2)
Sec. 74-142. - Additional ad valorem tax.
All properties, both real and personal, located and situated within the boundaries of the district shall be subject to an additional ad valorem tax levy as set annually hereafter by the Board of Commissioners of Brantley County.
(Res. of 5-8-1990, § 3; Res. of 9-14-2010, § 3)
Sec. 74-143. - Use of taxes.
(a)
All ad valorem taxes collected from the additional levy within the district shall be used and appropriated only, to pay the cost of providing additional fire protection services within the district; to construct and maintain facilities for housing fire equipment and personnel within the district; to purchase equipment to be located and used within the district; to pay the cost of training for persons who provide fire protection services within the district; to pay the cost of materials and supplies used for training firefighters assigned to the district; to pay the cost of insurance provided on real and/or personal property located within the district; to pay for such other costs and expenses as may be deemed appropriate and needed for the fire protection services within the district.
(b)
All properties, both real and personal, purchased from ad valorem tax revenue within the district shall be acquired in the name of "Brantley County, for the benefit of the Waynesville Fire Protection District". All proceeds derived from the sale of any such acquired property, shall be used in the same manner as the taxes levied within the district.
(Res. of 5-8-1990, §§ 4, 6; Res. of 9-14-2010, §§ 4, 6)
Sec. 74-144. - Additional tax levy.
Nothing herein shall require an additional tax levy within the district, as the resolution is permissive and not mandatory. However, should a tax be levied by the board of commissioners within the district, funds collected must be used only for providing fire protection services within the district. Same must be used only for providing fire protection services in addition to such services as then being provided by the board of commissioners in the county which lies outside the district and not in any other special district.
(Res. of 5-9-1990, § 7; Res. of 9-14-2010, § 7)
Sec. 74-145. - Fire protection services.
The providing of the fire protection services as herein contemplated within the district may at the discretion of the board of commissioners, be performed by the Waynesville Volunteer Fire Department Inc., a nonprofit Georgia Corporation, or such other party or parties as may be selected by the board of commissioners from time to time, under such terms and conditions as established by the board of commissioners. Any such services arrangement shall be set forth in a contractual agreement and approved by the board of commissioners. The board shall review the manner in which any such contracted services are being performed on an annual basis when considering the levy to be made within the district. No contract shall be continued with any party not providing the required service which meets the expectation of the Brantley County Commission.
(Res. of 5-8-1990, § 5; Res. of 9-14-2010, § 5)
Secs. 74-146—74-170. - Reserved.

DIVISION 5. - SOLID WASTE DISPOSAL TAX DISTRICT

Sec. 74-231. - Tax district created.
(a)
Brantley County Solid Waste Disposal Tax District. There is hereby created a special tax district within the county, which shall be known and cited as the "Brantley County Solid Waste Disposal Tax District," sometimes referred to in this division as "the district."
(b)
Boundaries. The boundaries of the Brantley County Solid Waste Disposal Tax District shall be as follows:
All of the real property and nonhomestead mobile homes situated, located, lying and being within the unincorporated areas of the county.
(Res. of 11-19-1991, §§ 1, 2; Amend. of 10-8-2002, § 2)
Sec. 74-232. - Solid waste disposal fee.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Residential unit capable of being occupied by humans means a structure that has electrical hookup for any part of the year and is enclosed on four sides and has sewage disposal facilities. Provided, however, a structure being exclusively used for agricultural storage (hay storage, etc.), that is without sewage disposal facilities, shall be an exempt residential or commercial unit.
(b)
Fee imposed. All real property and nonhomestead mobile homes located and situated within the boundaries of the district shall be subject to the imposition of a solid waste disposal fee. The fee shall be set annually by the board of commissioners and shall be a part of the annual county tax notice when possible or otherwise a supplement thereto. The solid waste disposal fee for the year 2002 on each residential unit capable of being occupied by humans on real property in the district, including each homestead mobile home, shall be in the amount of $85.00; and for the year 2003 on nonhomestead mobile homes located in the district which are capable of being occupied by humans, in the amount of $85.00; and in the amount of $85.00 for each commercial unit (a structure presently being used for or capable of being used for commercial/business operations) located on real property within the district, that has not contracted with some permitted solid waste disposal company for a period of 12 months covering the succeeding year (2003), for the disposal of solid waste from such commercial establishment (upon providing proof of such contracted disposal service, the fee shall be removed from such commercial unit).
(c)
Exemption. A mobile home park (a defined area of land that is owned by an individual, husband and wife, partnership or corporation where at least 12 mobile homes are located and the park owner has obtained a business license from the county for a mobile home park business) shall be considered a commercial unit for exemption purposes as to all mobile homes located in the park, if such mobile home park has provided a solid waste collection container (dumpster) of at least six cubic yards for 12 mobile homes and an additional yard capacity for each additional two mobile homes.
(Res. of 11-19-1991, § 3; Amend. of 12-3-2002, § 3)
Sec. 74-233. - Use of fees.
All fees collected from the solid waste disposal fee within the district shall be used and appropriated only, to pay the cost of disposal of solid waste generated within the county, and the necessary incidental related matters associated therewith, to include but not limited to the following:
(1)
The purchase of garbage disposal dumpsters;
(2)
The purchase, maintenance and operation of equipment for the collection of solid waste from solid waste dumpsters (this specifically does not include door-to-door collection);
(3)
Transportation cost to the disposal site or transfer site;
(4)
Any charges by way of a tipping fee for the disposal of solid waste; and
(5)
For such other cost and expenses as may be deemed needed and appropriate from time to time by the board of commissioners in order to collect and dispose of the solid waste generated within the county.
(Res. of 11-19-1991, § 4)
Sec. 74-234. - Fee levy.
Nothing in this division shall require a fee to be levied within the district as this division is permissive and not mandatory. However, should a fee be levied by the board of commissioners within the district, the fee must be used only for providing sanitation disposal services within the district as specifically set forth in this division, and none of the fees collected may be appropriated for any other purposes.
(Res. of 11-19-1991, § 6)
Sec. 74-235. - Solid waste disposal services.
In order to provide the solid waste disposal service, as contemplated in this division, within the district, the board of commissioners may, at the discretion of the board of commissioners, enter into a contract with other parties who will receive the solid waste generated within the county and dispose of the solid waste in accordance with the appropriate laws and regulations, and charge the county for such disposal. Any such solid waste disposal arrangements shall be set forth in a contractual agreement and approved by the board of commissioners. The board shall review the expenses incurred for the disposal of solid waste within the county on an annual basis when considering the annual fee to be imposed within the district.
(Res. of 11-19-1991, § 5)
Sec. 74-236. - Fees due.
All fees imposed within the district, as contemplated by the terms of this division, shall be due and payable on the same date as ad valorem taxes assessed in the county become due, and such fees shall be a lien against the nonhomestead mobile home and/or the real property upon which the residential or commercial unit is physically located, as provided for by O.C.G.A. §§ 48-2-56 and 12-8-39.3. Further, it is provided that the tax commissioner of the county shall be the officer charged with the enforcement and collection of any and all fees imposed under this division.
(Res. of 11-19-1991, § 7; Amend. of 10-8-2002, § 7)
Secs. 74-237—74-240. - Reserved.

DIVISION 10. - EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICE OPERATION AND MAINTENANCE SPECIAL FEE DISTRICT

Sec. 74-281. - Creation.
(a)
There is hereby created a special fee district within the county, which shall be known as the "county emergency medical service operation and maintenance special fee district", hereafter sometimes referred to as "the District".
(b)
The boundaries of the "county emergency medical service operation and maintenance special fee district" shall be as follows:
All of the real property lying, being and situate within the county wide geographical boundaries of Brantley County, Georgia, which shall include the incorporated and the unincorporated areas.
(Ord. of 12-6-2012; Amend. of 8-8-2013, § 1, 2)
Sec. 74-282. - Fees.
All parcels of real property (excluding cemeteries) located and situated within the boundaries of the district, every residential structure being used as a residence or capable of being used as a residence which would include utilities, every commercial structure being used as a business or capable of being used as a business, or being used for storage or capable of being used for storage, every mobile home or mobile device or structure being used as a residence or capable of being used as a residence which would include utilities, and every parcel of 25 acres or more with no improvements thereon, shall be subject to the levying of an emergency medical operation and maintenance fee. The fee shall be set annually by the board of commissioners of the county and shall be a part of the annual the county real property and personal property tax notice from the office of the county tax commissioner, when possible or otherwise a supplement thereto. The initial emergency medical service operation and maintenance fee shall be as follows: for the year 2012 shall be $20.53 for every parcel in the district (excluding cemeteries); a fee of $75.70 for every commercial structure; a fee of $75.70 for every mobile home or mobile device or structure being used as a residence; and a fee of $75.70 for every parcel of 25 acres or more without any improvements thereon.
All fees collected from the county emergency medical service operation and maintenance fee within the district shall be used and appropriated only, to pay for county operation and maintenance needs relating to the emergency medical service to the county's citizens within the unincorporated area of the county.
(Ord. of 12-6-2012; Amend. of 8-8-2013, §§ 3, 4)
Sec. 74-283. - Contracts.
The board of commissioners may, at the discretion of the board, upon a majority vote, enter into valid contracts with such entities that may provide technical assistance and/or services relating to the operation and maintenance of the emergency medical service in accordance with appropriate laws and regulations, and shall be able to charge the county for said technical assistance and/or services. Any such contract shall be set forth in a contractual written agreement, approved by a majority vote of the board of commissioners in a public meeting of the said board. The board of commissioners shall review the emergency medical service operation and maintenance needs on an annual basis when considering the annual fee to be imposed within the district.
(Ord. of 12-6-2012; Amend. of 8-8-2013, § 5)
Sec. 74-284. - Additional fees.
Nothing herein shall require a fee to be levied within the district as this article allows the board of commissioners to set a fee on an annual basis for the categories of properties set out in section 74-283. However, should a fee be levied by the board on an annual basis within the district, the same must be used only for the operation and maintenance of the emergency medical service within the district as specifically set forth herein, and none of the fees collected may be appropriated for any other purposes.
(Ord. of 12-6-2012; Amend. of 8-8-2013, § 6)
Sec. 74-285. - Additional ad valorem tax.
Any fees imposed within the district as contemplated by the terms of this article shall be due and payable on the same date as ad valorem taxes on real property and personal property taxes assessed in the county become due. If said fee or fees as may be applicable to a residential taxpayer, is not paid as to any of the particular categories heretofore set forth, the following shall be applicable:
(1)
In the event that a taxpayer contends that the taxpayer's property does not meet the classification assignment of the respective fee at the time the taxpayer pays the real property taxes and/or personal property taxes to the office of the county tax commissioner, then at that time, the office of the county tax commissioner shall inform the taxpayer by a written notice that the taxpayer shall have 15 days from the date of the receipt of the written notice from the office of the county tax commissioner as provided in this paragraph, to file a written appeal to the county manager setting forth the particular basis of the taxpayer's objection to the payment of the fee or fees as the case may be. The notice form shall provide for written receipt signed by the taxpayer of the notice of the right to appeal the taxpayer's contention with regard to the property classification to the county manager.
(2)
The county manager, upon receipt of a timely written appeal, shall review the written appeal, and within 15 days after the receipt of the said written appeal as measured from the county's date stamp reflecting the date of receipt of the written appeal, notify in writing the taxpayer by first class mail at the taxpayer's last known address, as to whether the appeal is allowed or denied.
(3)
If the appeal is allowed, the taxpayer shall be relieved of the payment of the fee or fees as the case may be.
(4)
If the appeal is denied, the written notice to the taxpayer as set out in paragraph (2) shall notify the taxpayer that the taxpayer shall have the right to request to meet with the county manager to review the taxpayer's appeal, which meeting shall be recorded for the record. At the end of the meeting with the county manager, the county manager shall, within five days of the said meeting, advise the taxpayer in writing by first class mail to the taxpayer's last known address of the county manager's decision as to the appeal.
(5)
In the event the county manager shall determine, either initially upon the receipt of a written appeal or after the hearing as heretofore provided, that the taxpayer's appeal is denied and the fee or fees is payable, and if the taxpayer refuses to pay the fee or fees as are applicable, the county manager shall notify the county magistrate court of the taxpayer's refusal to pay and the taxpayer shall be fined the sum of $200.00 plus the payment of the court costs. The magistrate court shall notify the taxpayer in writing by first class mail at the taxpayer's last known address that if the fee or fees as may be applicable are not paid within ten days of the date of the notice, that the taxpayer shall be summoned to the magistrate court and subject to being fined the sum of $200.00) plus the court costs for the failure to pay the fee or applicable fees as provided pursuant to this article.
(Ord. of 12-6-2012; Amend. of 8-8-2013, § 7)

ARTICLE VI. - ENERGY EXCISE TAX

Sec. 74-301. - Definitions.
As used in this ordinance, the terms: "dealer;" "energy;" "local sales and use tax;" and "purchaser" shall have the same meanings as provided in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-110.
(Ord. of 11-8-2012)
Sec. 74-302. - Imposition; rate; remitting; recovery; liabilities.
In accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-110 et seq., there is imposed within the territorial limits of the special district created pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-111, an excise tax on the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy when such sale, use, storage, or consumption would have constituted a taxable event for purposes of sales and use tax under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-1 et seq. but for the exemption in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2.
(Ord. of 11-8-2012)
Sec. 74-303. - Excise tax rate; phase-in.
(a)
Such excise tax shall be levied and collected by the governing authority of Brantley County pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 48-13-112 and 48-13-114 and as provided in this section.
(b)
Except as otherwise provided in this section, the rate of such excise tax shall be phased in over a four-year period as follows:
(1)
For the period commencing January 1, 2013, and concluding at the last moment of December 31, 2013, such excise tax shall be at a rate equivalent to 25 percent of the total amount of local sales and use tax in effect in such special district that would be collected on the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy but for the exemption in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2;
(2)
For the period commencing January 1, 2014, and concluding at the last moment of December 31, 2014, such excise tax shall be at a rate equivalent to 50 percent of the total amount of local sales and use tax in effect in such special district that would be collected on the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy but for the exemption in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2;
(3)
For the period commencing January 1, 2015, and concluding at the last moment of December 31, 2015, such excise tax shall be at a rate equivalent to 75 percent of the total amount of local sales and use tax in effect in such special district that would be collected on the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy but for the exemption in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2; and
(4)
On or after January 1, 2016, such excise tax shall be at a rate equivalent to 100 percent of the total amount of local sales and use tax in effect in such special district that would be collected on the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy but for the exemption in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2.
(c)
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2 (c)(4), if a project of regional significance under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3(92) is started in the special district, it shall not be subject to the phase-in period contained in paragraphs (1), (2), and (3) of subsection (b) of this section, and the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy in connection therewith shall be subject to the rate specified in paragraph (4) of subsection (b) of this section notwithstanding the January 1, 2016 limitation in that paragraph (4).
(d)
(1)
Following such initial imposition during the phase-in time period, on or after January 1, 2016, the rate of the excise tax levied and collected by the governing authority of Brantley County shall be directed by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-112(d). Such rate shall be controlled by the maximum amount of local sales and use tax in effect in the special district, but in no event more than two percent.
(2)
In the event the total rate of local sales and use taxes in effect in the special district decreases from two percent to one percent, the rate of the excise tax under this ordinance shall likewise be reduced at the same time such local sales and use tax rate reduction becomes effective.
(3)
In the event the total rate of local sales and use taxes in effect in the special district increases from one percent to two percent, the rate of the excise tax under this ordinance shall likewise be increased at the same time such local sales and use tax rate increase becomes effective.
(Ord. of 11-8-2012)
Sec. 74-304. - Imposition; remittance; recovery.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. §48-13-112(c), such excise tax shall be:
(1)
Imposed only at the time sales and use tax on the sale or use of such energy would have been due and payable under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-30 but for the exemption under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2;
(2)
Due and payable in the same manner as would be required under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-1 et seq., except as other provided in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-110 et seq.;
(3)
A debt of the purchaser of energy until it is paid and shall be recoverable at law in the same manner as authorized for the recovery of other debts;
(4)
Remitted to the governing authority of Brantley County by the dealer collecting such excise tax; and
(5)
A liability of the dealer at the applicable rate on the charges actually collected or the amount of excise taxes collected from purchasers, whichever is greater.
(Ord. of 11-8-2012)
Sec. 74-305. - Exemption certificates.
(a)
The governing authority of Brantley County, or the collecting officer appointed under section 74-308 of this article, shall make diligent efforts to identify all energy providers and purchasers in the special district and to ascertain whether such a purchaser has completed a uniform exemption certificate provided from the website of the Georgia Department of Revenue seeking to qualify such purchaser for the exemption under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2. Such certificate shall be utilized by such governing authority or collecting officer for the purpose of determining the applicability of the excise tax under this ordinance.
(b)
An energy provider shall be authorized to rely upon such uniform exemption certificates. No penalty shall attach to such provider for failure to collect the excise tax under this ordinance when it has properly collected and remitted local sales and use taxes on such energy where the purchaser would have qualified for the uniform exemption certificate but failed to apply for such certificate or where the purchaser received such certificate but failed to file it with the energy provider.
(Ord. of 11-8-2012)
Sec. 74-306. - Commencement of collections.
The excise tax imposed pursuant to this ordinance shall become effective on January 1, 2013, and shall be due and payable to the governing authority of Brantley County monthly on or before the twentieth day of every month and each respective month in which such taxes are collected, and payment shall be accompanied by a return for the preceding monthly period showing the gross sales and purchases arising from all sales and purchases taxable under this ordinance during the preceding calendar month.
(Ord. of 11-8-2012)
Sec. 74-307. - Allocation, distribution, and expenditure of proceeds.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-114 (b), the excise tax proceeds shall be allocated and distributed by the governing authority of Brantley County at the end of each calendar month. An amount equal to one percent of such proceeds collected by Brantley County shall be paid into the general fund of Brantley County to defray the costs of collection and administration.
(b)
The remainder shall be distributed in accordance with the intergovernmental agreement as required under O.C.G.A. § 48-13-114 (b) and (c). Within 30 days following the end of each calendar month, the remainder shall be distributed to the general fund of Brantley County and the general fund of each participating municipality in accordance with the applicable provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-114 (c)(1) or (2) as follows:
(1)
If two local sales and use taxes are in effect in the special district, an amount equal to one-half of the proceeds shall be distributed to the general fund of Brantley County and the general fund of each participating municipality located in the county according to the same proportionate share as specified under the distribution provisions of the first local sales and use tax and an amount equal to one-half of the proceeds of the excise tax shall be distributed to the general fund of Brantley County and the general fund of each participating municipality located in such county according to the same proportionate share as specified under the distribution provisions of the second local sales and use tax; or
(2)
If only one such local sales and use tax is in effect in the special district, then the proceeds of the excise tax shall be distributed to the general fund of Brantley County and the general fund of each participating municipality located in the county according to the same proportionate share as specified under the distribution provisions of the local sales and use tax.
(c)
Except as provided in subsection (a) of this section, proceeds of the excise tax deposited in the general fund of Brantley County may be expended for any lawful purpose as determined by the governing authority of Brantley County.
(Ord. of 11-8-2012)
Sec. 74-308. - Administrative provisions; audits; examinations; collecting officer.
(a)
The governing authority of Brantley County shall be authorized to designate a collecting officer for collection and administration of the excise tax under this ordinance.
(b)
The collecting officer shall be authorized to provide for procedures necessary to the proper implementation of this ordinance, including, but not limited to, periodic auditing of dealers collecting and remitting the excise tax. The collecting officer, or the designee of the collecting officer, is specifically authorized to examine the books, papers, records, financial reports, equipment,, or other facilities of any purchaser which is required to remit the excise tax under this ordinance.
(c)
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-13-118, as part of the audit report required under O.C.G.A. § 36-81-7, the auditor shall include, in a separate schedule, a report of the revenues pertaining to the excise tax under this ordinance.
(Ord. of 11-8-2012)
