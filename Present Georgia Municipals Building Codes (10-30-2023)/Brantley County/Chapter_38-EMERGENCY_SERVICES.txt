ARTICLE II. - E 911 SYSTEM

Sec. 38-31. - Advisory board.
(a)
Created. There is created the county 911 system advisory board, which board shall not exceed 13 members.
(b)
Membership. The members of the advisory board shall consist of the following persons:
(1)
The sheriff of the county;
(2)
The chief of police of the City of Nahunta;
(3)
The chief of police of the City of Hoboken;
(4)
The emergency medical services director of the county;
(5)
Two members of the board of commissioners;
(6)
The mayor or councilmember as designated by the City of Nahunta;
(7)
The mayor or councilmember as designated by the City of Hoboken;
(8)
Two representatives designated by and from the firefighter's association of the county;
(9)
The county forest ranger (state forestry commission);
(10)
The county civil defense director; and
(11)
A citizen designated by the board of commissioners, initial appointment being Jack Crawford (retired from GSP).
(c)
Functions and duties. The advisory board shall have the functions and duties as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 46-5-136 (b).
(Res. of 2-9-1993)
Cross reference— Authorities, boards and commissions, § 2-61 et seq.
Sec. 38-32. - Charge imposed.
In compliance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134(d)(2), a 911 system charge in the amount of $1.50 per month is imposed upon each exchange access facility and same shall be billed to each subscriber of any exchange from which E-911 service is available from the county. This section shall be retro-active to January 1, 2013, and will remain in full force and effect until further action by the county board of commissioners.
(Res. of 1-14-1992; Res. of 2-8-1994; Res. of 2-14-1995; Res. of 12-13-2011; Res. of 5-9-2013)

ARTICLE III. - 911 CHARGE ON WIRELESS SERVICE[2]

Sec. 38-51. - 9-1-1 charge on prepaid wireless transactions.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2, there is hereby imposed a prepaid wireless 911 charge as defined by O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(a)(4) upon every prepaid wireless retail transaction occurring within the jurisdiction of the public service answering point in the amount of $0.75.
(Ord. of 6-14-2011, § 1)
Sec. 38-52. - Collection of 9-1-1 charge on prepaid wireless transactions.
Prepaid wireless 911 charges collected by sellers shall be remitted to the commissioner of the Department of Revenue at the times and in the manner provided by Chapter 8 of Title 48 of the O.C.G.A. with respect to the sales and use tax imposed on prepaid wireless calling service.
(Ord. of 6-14-2011, § 2)
Sec. 38-53. - Administrative provisions.
The clerk of the county is hereby directed to file with the state revenue commissioner a certified copy of this article and amendments thereto, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(j)(1), within ten days of enactment of this article.
(Ord. of 6-14-2011, § 3)
Sec. 38-54. - Depositing of funds; use of funds.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(j)(5), funds received by this county from charges imposed by this article shall be deposited in the emergency telephone system fund maintained by this county pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134 and kept separate from general revenue of the jurisdiction; all such funds shall be used exclusively for the purposes authorized by O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134(f).
(Ord. of 6-14-2011, § 4)
Sec. 38-55. - Wireless enhanced 911 charge.
A E-911 system charge in the amount of $1.50 per month is imposed on each wireless telecommunications with a billing address within the county and said charge shall continue for the following 12 months hereafter.
(Res. of 12-13-2011)

Sec. 38-61. - Definitions.
Unless specified otherwise in this resolution, all terms shall be defined in the same manner as specified in O.C.G.A. § 46-5-122.
(Res. of 9-6-2018, § 1; Res. of 12-6-2018; § 1)
Sec. 38-62. - 911 charges on monthly services.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 46-5-133(3), there is imposed a monthly 911 charge upon each telephone service, subscribed to by a telephone subscriber, whose exchange access lines are in the areas served or which could be served by the 911 service. Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134(a)(i)(A), the amount of such 911 charge shall be $1.50 per month per telephone service provided to the telephone subscriber.
(b)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 46-5-133(3), there is imposed a monthly wireless enhanced 911 charge upon each wireless telecommunications connection, other than a connection for prepaid wireless service, subscribed to by a telephone subscriber whose place of primary use is within the geographic area that is served by Brantley County, Georgia, or that would be served by Brantley County, Georgia, for the purpose of such an emergency 911 system. Pursuant to O.C.G.A. 46-5-134(a)(2)(A), the amount of such enhanced wireless 911 charge shall be $1.50 per month per wireless telecommunications connection provided to the telephone subscriber.
(c)
The 911 charges under this section shall commence January 1, 2019.
(Res. of 9-6-2018, § 2; Res. of 12-6-2018; § 2)
Sec. 38-63. - Collecting and remitting of 911 charges.
All such 911 charges collected by service suppliers shall be remitted to the Georgia Department of Revenue, as the contracted collection partner of the Georgia Emergency Communications Authority, at the times and in the manner provided by O.C.G.A. § 38-3-185, O.C.G.A. § 38-3-186, and any other Georgia Department of Revenue or Georgia Emergency Communications Authority rule or regulation adopted pursuant to Article 12 of Chapter 13 of Title 38 of the O.C.G.A. and Chapter 13 of Title 50 of the O.C.G.A., the "Georgia Administrative Procedures Act."
(Res. of 9-6-2018, § 3; Res. of 12-6-2018; § 3)
Sec. 38-64. - Deposit and use of proceeds.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134, all proceeds received by Brantley County from 911 charges imposed by this resolution shall be deposited in the Emergency Telephone System Fund maintained by the county; kept separate from general revenue of the county; and used exclusively for the statutorily authorized purposes.
(Res. of 9-6-2018, § 4; Res. of 12-6-2018; § 4)
Sec. 38-65. - Resolution filing requirements.
The clerk of the county shall file with the state revenue commissioner a certified copy of this resolution within ten days of the adoption thereof. Any subsequent amendment to this resolution shall likewise be so filed by the clerk within ten days of the adoption thereof.
(Res. of 9-6-2018, § 5; Res. of 12-6-2018; § 5)
Sec. 38-66. - Effective date and applicability.
This resolution shall become effective on April 12, 2019. Any prior resolution establishing such 911 charges shall remain in effect until April 12, 2019. On such date, such 911 charges shall be governed by this resolution.
(Res. of 9-6-2018, § 6; Res. of 12-6-2018; § 6)
