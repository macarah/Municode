Sec. 14-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Access channels means services provided by a cable television system on its public, educational, local government or leased channels, whether separate or composite.
Additional service or additional subscriber service means any communications service other than a basic service provided over its cable television system by the franchisee directly or as a carrier for its subsidiaries, affiliates or any other person engaged in communications services including, by way of illustration but not limited to, pay or subscription television, burglar alarm, data or other electronic intelligence transmission, facsimile reproduction, meter reading and home shopping.
Basic service or basic subscriber service means the delivery by the franchisee to television receivers, or any other suitable type of audiovideo communication receivers, to all subscribers to locations in the county of signals of over-the-air television broadcasters and channels required by the FCC; and other service at the option of the company.
Channel means a signaling path provided by a cable television system to relay to subscriber terminals television broadcast programs or any other programs or signals from whatever source which may legally and technically be delivered to such terminal.
Community antenna television system, cable television system and CATV are synonymous terms describing a system employing antennas, microwave, wires, waveguides, coaxial cables, fiber optics or other conductors, equipment or facilities, designed, constructed or used for the purpose of:
(1)
Collecting and amplifying local and distant broadcast television or radio signals and distributing and transmitting them;
(2)
Transmitting original cablecast programming not received from television broadcast signals;
(3)
Transmitting television pictures, film and video tape programs not received through broadcast television channels, whether or not encoded or processed to permit reception by only selected receivers; and
(4)
Transmitting and receiving all other signals, including digital, voice and audiovisual.
Provided, however, that any of the services permitted to be performed, described in this definition, shall be those performed by or through the franchisee for subscribers in the operation of a cable television system franchised by the county.
Converter means an electronic device which converts signals from a frequency not susceptible to reception by the television receiver of the subscriber to a frequency which is susceptible of reception, and by an appropriate channel selector also permits the subscriber to view all signals delivered at designated dial locations.
County clerk means the county clerk or any other person selected by the board of commissioners with regard to the administration of this chapter or any lawfully designated designee of such official.
FCC means the Federal Communications Commission.
Franchise means and includes any authorization granted under this chapter in terms of a franchise, privilege, permit, license or authority to construct, operate and maintain a cable television system or components thereof within the county. Such authorization means and includes any license or permit required for the privilege of transacting and carrying on the business within the county as required by other ordinances and related state laws of the county.
Franchisee means Westinghouse Broadcasting and Cable, Inc., d/b/a/ Group W Cable, being the individual, company, corporation or firm granted the franchise by the board of commissioners under this chapter, and its lawful successors, transferees or assigns.
Gross annual receipts includes those revenues derived from the supplying of basic subscriber service, that is, the installation fees, disconnect and reconnect fees, and fees from regular cable benefits, including the transmission of broadcast signals, and access and local origination, if any. However, it does not include revenues derived from per program or per channel charges, lease channel revenue, advertising revenue or any other income from the system.
Road means the surface, the air space above the surface, and the area below the surface, of all public streets, roads, highways, alleys, easements, boulevards, bridges, tunnels, public utility easements and all public grounds within or belonging to the county.
Subscriber or customer means an authorized recipient legally receiving basic service or additional service, or both.
User means a person utilizing a cable television system channel for purposes of transmission of material to subscribers, as contrasted with receipt thereof in a subscriber capacity.
(Code 1992, § 12-1)

Sec. 14-2. - Franchise granted; duration.
In consideration of the faithful performance and observance of the conditions and reservations specified in this chapter, the nonexclusive right is hereby granted to Westinghouse Broadcasting and Cable, Inc. d/b/a/ Group W Cable, its successors and assigns, hereinafter referred to as the grantee, to erect, construct, maintain and operate a community antenna television system in the county for the purpose of transmission and distribution by cable of television impulses and television energy to the residents of the county in all forms, in accordance with the laws and regulations of the United States and the state, and the ordinances and regulations of the county, for a period of 15 years.
(Code 1992, § 12-2)
Sec. 14-3. - Scope of franchise.
(a)
There is hereby granted by the county to the franchisee, the right and privilege to construct, erect, operate and maintain, in, upon, along, across, above, over and under the streets, roads and public places now laid out or dedicated, and all extensions thereof, and additions thereto, in the county, poles, wires, cables, underground conduits, manholes and other television conductors and fixtures necessary or convenient for the maintenance and operation in the county of a cable television system for the reception, sale and distribution of television and radio signals, or any other services which may legally and technically be provided.
(b)
The rules, regulations and standards as legally adopted by the FCC from time to time shall apply to and shall govern the operations of the franchisee of the franchise and its operations under this chapter, and are hereby expressly declared to be a part of the franchise.
(Code 1992, § 12-3)
Sec. 14-4. - Franchise territory.
The franchise granted under this chapter and all the terms and conditions thereof relate to the unincorporated areas of the county.
(Code 1992, § 12-4)
Sec. 14-5. - Rights reserved to the county.
(a)
Nothing contained in this chapter shall be construed to contract away or to modify or abridge, in any way, the county's right and power of eminent domain.
(b)
There is hereby reserved to the county every right and power which it is required by law to reserve, and the county is granting only those rights it has the power and authority to grant.
(c)
Neither the granting of any franchise nor any provision hereof shall constitute a waiver or bar to the exercise of any governmental right or power of the county.
(d)
The board of commissioners may do all things which are necessary and convenient in the exercise of its jurisdiction under this chapter. The parties may agree upon a referee to determine any disputed questions of fact which may arise under this franchise. The board of commissioners, with the concurrence of the county attorney, is hereby authorized and empowered to adjust, settle or compromise with the franchisee any controversy or charge arising from the operations of the franchise under this chapter on behalf of the county, in the best interest of the public, and fairness to the franchisee.
(Code 1992, § 12-5)
Sec. 14-6. - Franchise fee.
(a)
In consideration of the granting and exercise of a franchise under this chapter, for the operation of a cable television system, the franchisee shall pay to the county during the life of the franchise a fee in the amount of three percent of its gross annual receipts from all cable services in the county, including all forms of consideration, and such other receipts and revenues if and when permitted by FCC regulations.
(b)
All payments shall be made semiannually on July 31 and January 31 and shall be accompanied by a statement of gross receipts derived by the grantee from services located within the areas of the county, such statement to be certified annually to coincide with bookkeeping systems, as to correctness by a certified public accountant licensed to practice in the state.
(c)
The franchise payments provided for in this section shall be in addition to and not in lieu of ad valorem taxes assessed with respect to real or personal property of the grantee by the county.
(Code 1992, § 12-6)
Sec. 14-7. - Insurance required; indemnification of county.
(a)
The grantee shall indemnify and hold harmless the county at all times during the term of this franchise from and against all claims for injury or death to person or physical damage to property, both real and personal, which result from the negligence of the grantee, his employees, agents or assigns in the construction, erection, operation or maintenance of any structure, equipment, appurtenances or product authorized or used pursuant to authority of this chapter. The grantee shall carry liability insurance in such form and with such companies as shall be approved by the county attorney so as to protect the county and the grantee from and against any and all such claims resulting from injury or death to persons or damages to property. The amount of such liability insurance shall be not less than $100,000.00 as to any one person and $300,000.00 as to any one accident, and property damage insurance for not less than $50,000.00 for any one accident.
(b)
The grantee, and/or its insurance carrier, upon due notice in writing from the county, shall defend at its own expense any action or proceedings against the county in which it is claimed that the injury, death or damages was due to the grantee's negligence in the operation of its television system, and in the event of a determination of liability shall indemnify the county.
(Code 1992, § 12-7)
Sec. 14-8. - Technical standards.
(a)
Service standards. The franchisee shall maintain and operate the system and render efficient service in accordance with the rules and regulations as are, or may be, set forth by the FCC. The franchisee shall furnish to its subscribers and customers, for all services, with no discriminatory or unreasonable delay in the franchisee's installation of full service within the various neighborhoods in the serviceable franchise area, the best signals practicable under the circumstances existing at the time, and shall provide quality reception of its CATV service to each subscriber so that both sound and picture are produced free from visible and audible noise and distortion on standard television receivers in good repair.
(b)
Maintenance of and service to the system. The franchisee shall put, and keep and maintain, all parts of the CATV system in good condition throughout the term of this franchise.
(Code 1992, § 12-8)
Sec. 14-9. - Street occupancy conditions.
(a)
All transmission and distribution structures, lines and equipment erected by the franchisee within the county shall be so located as to cause minimum interference with the proper use of streets, roads, alleys and other public ways and places, and to cause minimum interference with the rights and reasonable convenience of property owners who adjoin any of the streets, roads, alleys or other public ways and places.
(b)
In case of disturbance of any street, road, sidewalk, alley, public way or paved area, the franchisee shall, at its own cost and expense and in a manner approved by the board of commissioners, acting through its authorized officers or employees, replace and restore such street, road, sidewalk, alley, public way or paved area in as good a condition as existed prior to the commencement of the work involving the disturbance.
(c)
If at any time during the period of this franchise the county shall lawfully elect to alter or change the grade of any street, road, sidewalk, alley or other public way, the franchisee, upon reasonable notice by the county, shall remove, relay and relocate its poles, wires, cables, underground conduits, manholes and other fixtures at its own expense.
(d)
Any poles or other fixtures placed in any public way by the franchises shall be placed in such manner as not to interfere with the usual travel upon such public way.
(e)
The franchisee shall, on the request of any person holding a building moving permit issued by the county, temporarily raise or lower its wires to permit the moving of buildings. The expense of such temporary removal, raising or lowering of wires shall be paid by the person requesting the temporary wire changes, and the franchisee shall have the authority to require such payment in advance. The franchisee shall be given not less than five working days' advance notice to arrange for such temporary wire changes.
(f)
The franchisee shall have the authority to trim trees upon or overhanging streets, roads, alleys, sidewalks and public ways and places of the county so as to prevent the branches of such trees from coming in contact with the wires and cables of the franchisee.
(g)
In all sections of the county where the cables, wires or other like facilities of county utilities are all placed underground, the franchisee shall place its cables, wires or other like facilities underground to the maximum extent that existing technology reasonably permits the franchisee to do so. As to depth, such installation shall comply with county standards.
(Code 1992, § 12-9)
Sec. 14-10. - Permits and construction.
(a)
The franchisee shall utilize existing poles, conduits and other facilities whenever reasonably possible, and shall not construct or install any new, different or additional poles, conduits or other facilities on public property unless and until the necessary permits have been secured. Whenever the franchisee shall not utilize existing poles, conduits and other facilities, or whenever existing conduits and other facilities to be located beneath the surface of the streets or roads in any area or throughout the county, in the exercise of its police power or pursuant to the terms hereof, upon reasonable notice to the franchisee, any such conduits or other facilities shall be constructed, installed, placed or replaced beneath the surface of the streets or roads. Any construction, installation, placement, replacement or changes which may be so required shall be entirely at the expense of the franchisee.
(b)
The county shall have the right to make additional use, for any public or county purpose, whether governmental or proprietary, of any poles, conduits or other similar facilities erected, controlled or maintained exclusively by or for the franchisee in any street, provided such use by the county does not interfere with the use by the franchisee; provided, further, the county shall pay a reasonable and fair price for such use.
(c)
The franchisee, at its expense, shall protect, support, temporarily disconnect, relocate or remove any property of the franchisee when, in the opinion of the county, such property is required by reason of traffic, conditions, public safety, street vacation, street or highway construction, change or establishment of street grade, installation of sewers, drains, water pipes, power lines, signal lines, transportation facilities, tracts or any type or manner of structure or improvements made by governmental agencies whether acting in a governmental or a proprietary capacity, or any other structure of public improvement, including, but not limited to, movement of buildings, urban renewal and redevelopment, and any general program under which the county shall cause all such properties to be located beneath the surface of the ground. The franchisee shall in all cases have the privilege, subject to the corresponding obligations, to abandon any of its property in place, as provided in this chapter. Nothing under this section shall be deemed a taking of the property of the franchisee, and the franchisee shall be entitled to no surcharge or extra compensation, rates excepted, by reason of anything under this section.
(d)
Upon the failure, refusal or neglect of the franchisee to cause any work or other act required under this section to be properly completed in, on, over or under any street within any time prescribed therefor, or upon reasonable notice given, where no time is prescribed, the clerk may cause such work or other act to be completed in whole or in part, and upon so doing shall submit to the franchisee an itemized statement of the costs thereof. The franchisee shall, within 30 days after receipt of such statement, pay to the county the entire amount thereof.
(Code 1992, § 12-10)
Sec. 14-11. - Rules, regulations by franchisee; connections for schools.
(a)
The grantee shall have the right to prescribe reasonable service rules and regulations for the conduct of its business not inconsistent with the provisions of this chapter, and a copy of such rules and regulations shall be kept on file at all times with the county clerk. Prior to the commencement of service to any of its subscribers, the grantee shall file with the county clerk a copy of its proposed rate schedule governing charges for service to its subscribers. Any change in such schedule shall become effective 30 days subsequent to the filing of due notice of change with the county clerk.
(b)
One connection to the television receiver in each public school in the county school system shall be provided if requested by the school, provided such school is within the existing service area of the franchisee. If such school is outside the franchisee's service area, the franchisee will extend service to such school at actual cost to the franchisee if it is technically feasible to do so. Service to additional receivers in the public schools shall be charged for at the same rate as that charged to other subscribers for comparable service in accordance with the terms and conditions prescribed by the grantee pursuant to this section.
(Code 1992, § 12-11)
Sec. 14-12. - Service complaint procedure.
(a)
System maintenance. The franchisee shall keep, repair and maintain all parts of the cable television system in such a manner that the entire system complies with section 14-8 throughout the entire franchise period.
(b)
Customer service. The franchisee shall render efficient service, make repairs promptly and interrupt service only for good cause and for the shortest reasonable time practical.
(c)
Complaint office. The franchisee shall maintain an office within the area of the county which shall be open during all usual business hours, have a publicly listed telephone, and be so operated that complaints and request for repairs and adjustments may be received on a daily basis during each day of the year.
(d)
Complaint response. The franchisee shall maintain a capability for responding to subscriber complaints or requests for service within 24 hours after receipt of the complaint or request under normal circumstances. Nothing contained in this section shall prohibit the franchisee from seeking and receiving recompense for damages to its equipment.
(e)
Subscriber notice of complaint procedures. The franchisee has established procedures for receiving, acting upon and resolving subscriber complaints in accordance with FCC regulations. These procedures shall be filed with the county clerk. The franchisee shall furnish a notice of such procedures to each subscriber at the time of initial subscription to the system. The franchisee shall advise its subscribers when a change or alteration in complaint procedures occurs.
(f)
County official responsible. In accordance with FCC regulations, section 76.31(a)(5)(iii), the county clerk is hereby designated by the county as having primary responsibility for the continuing administration of the franchise and implementation of complaint procedures.
(Code 1992, § 12-12)
Sec. 14-13. - Sale or lease of franchise.
(a)
This franchise shall be deemed a privilege to be held in personal trust by the grantee. It may not be sold, transferred, leased, assigned or disposed of in whole or in part either by forced or voluntary sale, merger, consolidation or otherwise without the prior consent of the board of commissioners, expressed by resolution, and then only under such conditions as may be prescribed. The board of commissioners shall not unreasonably withhold its consent to an assignment.
(b)
The grantee shall not have such property rights in this franchise that the same may be effected, transferred or disposed of voluntarily or involuntarily without the consent of the board of commissioners. Any transfer or assignment or other distribution of any of the rights under this franchise shall be made only by an instrument in writing, a duly executed copy of which shall be filed in the office of the county clerk at least 30 days after such transfer or assignment shall have been executed or effected and shall reflect therein the consent of the board thereto. The grantee, however, shall have the right at any time, to mortgage the whole of its system or any part thereof subject to the rights of the county under the terms of this franchise.
(Code 1992, § 12-13)
Sec. 14-14. - Forfeiture.
In addition to all other rights and powers reserved or pertaining to the county, the county reserves as an additional and as a separate and distinct remedy the right to revoke this franchise and all rights and privileges of the grantee in any of the following events or for any of the following reasons:
(1)
The grantee fails after 30 days' prior written notice to comply with any of the provisions of this chapter or has, by act or omission violated any term or condition thereof;
(2)
Any provision of this chapter shall be finally adjudged by a court of law invalid or unenforceable and the board of commissioners further finds that such provision constitutes at that time a consideration material to the continuance of the franchise granted under this chapter;
(3)
The grantee becomes insolvent, unable or unwilling to pay its debts, or is adjudged a bankrupt;
(4)
All or part of the grantee's facilities should be sold under an instrument to secure a debt and are not redeemed by the grantee within 30 days from such sale;
(5)
The grantee attempts to or does practice any fraud or deceit in its conduct or relations with the county under this franchise; or
(6)
The county condemns all of the property of the grantee within the county by the lawful exercise of eminent domain.
(Code 1992, § 12-14)
Sec. 14-15. - Removal of poles, wires and appurtenances upon termination.
(a)
The television system of the grantee to be installed shall not be abandoned, in whole or in part, without the written consent of the board of commissioners. Upon termination or forfeiture of this franchise, in accordance with any of its terms, the grantee shall, within a reasonable time, remove its poles, cable, wires and appurtenances from the county streets, roads, lanes, avenues, sidewalks, alleys, bridges, highways and other public places within the county.
(b)
Upon failure of the grantee to perform such removal, the county shall have the right to make a written demand on the grantee to proceed to carry out the removal of such equipment within 60 days from the date of such demand and in the event that the grantee fails to carry out the terms and conditions of such demand, the county shall have the right to remove or maintain in its then status all or part of the grantee's equipment and to retain such equipment as the county's property. Such removal is to be accomplished without accounting therefor to the grantee, and the expense of such removal shall be charged to and paid by the grantee without credit for the value, if any, for the equipment removed by the county.
(Code 1992, § 12-15)
Sec. 14-16. - Dealing in receivers restricted.
The rights and franchises granted and extended under this chapter are so granted and extended upon the express stipulation that neither the grantee, nor any assignee or successor in interest of such grantee, will, within 15 miles of the areas of the county described in this chapter at any time during the primary or any renewed or extended term hereof, engage in the business of selling or servicing radio or television receivers either directly or indirectly, or through the medium of any person in whose activities such grantee, assignee or successor has any business or financial interest, except that nothing in this section shall prohibit such grantee, assignee or successor from maintaining such receivers as are used exclusively by them in the operation of the television system with respect to which this franchise is granted.
(Code 1992, § 12-16)
Sec. 14-17. - Connection fee authorized.
The franchise granted to provide service within the areas of the county shall authorize and permit the franchisee to solicit, sell, distribute and make a charge to subscribers within the county for connection to the cable television system of the franchisee.
(Code 1992, § 12-17)
Sec. 14-18. - Prohibited activities.
The franchisee shall be prohibited from directly doing any of the following:
(1)
Imposing a fee or charge for the determination by the franchisee of the quality of its signal to the subscriber or for any service or repair to a subscriber-owned receiving devices; provided, however, that charges may be made for damage or destruction of the franchisee's equipment, including converters.
(2)
Soliciting, referring or causing or permitting the solicitation or referral of any subscriber to any specific person or firm engaged in any business in this chapter prohibited to be engaged in by franchisee.
(Code 1992, § 12-18)
