Sec. 6-16. - Adopted.
There is hereby adopted by the mayor and city council, for the purpose of prescribing regulations governing conditions hazardous to life and property from fire or explosion, that certain code known as the Standard Fire Prevention Code, 1991 Edition, except for such portions as may be deleted, modified or amended in this article, of which code not less than one copy has been and now is filed in the office of the city clerk. The same is adopted and incorporated as fully as if set out at length herein, and from the date on which this section shall take effect, the provisions thereof shall be controlling within the corporate limits of the city.
(Code 1976, § 5-101; Ord. No. 91-10, § 1, 10-15-91)
State Law reference— Municipal authority to adopt fire codes, OCGA § 25-3-4.
Sec. 6-17. - Definitions.
(a)
Wherever the words "chief of the bureau of fire prevention" are used in the fire prevention code, they shall be held to mean the chief of the fire department of the city, and whenever the words "bureau of fire prevention" are used in such code, they shall be held to mean the fire department of the city.
(b)
Wherever the term "corporation counsel" is used in the fire prevention code, it shall be held to mean the city attorney.
(c)
Wherever the word "municipality" is used in the fire prevention code, it shall be held to mean this city.
(Code 1976, § 5-102)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 6-18. - Enforcement of code.
This code shall be administered and enforced, as provided within the Standard Fire Prevention Code, by the fire prevention department. The City of Union City Fire Department shall be designated as such fire prevention department. The fire chief shall hereby be appointed as the fire official with such duties and responsibilities as provided for by said code.
(Code 1976, § 5-103; Ord. No. 91-10, § 2, 10-15-91)
Sec. 6-19. - Power to make modifications.
(a)
The chief of the fire department shall be authorized to modify any of the provisions of the fire prevention code upon application in writing by the owner or lessee of any property, or his duly authorized agent, when there are practical difficulties in the ways of carrying out the strict letter of the code, provided that the spirit of the code shall be observed and the public safety secured.
(b)
The particulars of such modifications when granted or allowed, and the decision of the fire chief thereon, shall be entered upon the records of the department and a signed copy shall be forwarded to the applicant.
(Code 1976, § 5-104)
Sec. 6-20. - Reserved.
Sec. 6-21. - Knox boxes.
(a)
Where required. Where access to or within a structure or an area is restricted because of secured openings or where immediate access is necessary for life-saving or fire-fighting purposes, the fire chief or fire marshal is authorized to require a knox box to be installed in an approved location. The knox box shall be of an approved type and shall contain keys to gain necessary access as required by the fire chief or fire marshal.
(b)
Locks. An approved knox lock shall be installed on gates or similar barrier when required by the fire chief or fire marshal.
(c)
Knox box maintenance. The operator of the building shall immediately notify the fire chief or fire marshal and provide the new key when a lock is changed or rekeyed. The key to such lock shall be secured in the knox box.
(Ord. No. 2011-07, § 1, 5-17-11; Ord. No. 2011-12, § 1, 8-16-11)
Sec. 6-22. - Use and storage of grills.
No grill may be used or stored in breezeways, balconies/patios, stairways, or exit access areas.
(Ord. No. 2011-07, § 2, 5-17-11; Ord. No. 2011-12, § 2, 8-16-11)
Sec. 6-23. - Electric coil cooking devices.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used inthis section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection:
Apartment building shall mean a building or portion thereof containing three (3) or more dwelling units with independent cooking and bathroom facilities.
Electric coil cooking device shall mean a cooking device that utilizes a coil for the cooking heat source. This term shall include freestanding and built-in electric ranges "drop in" type counter-top cook tops, and hot plates.
High end heat limiting technology shall mean a device that is hard-wired into the electric coil range (tamper proof) that limits the high end cooking temperature to a safe level so as to prevent auto-ignition of common cooking and household materials.
Nursing home shall mean a building or portion of a building used on a twenty-four-hour basis for the housing and nursing care of four (4) or more persons who, because of mental or physical incapacity, might be unable to provide for their own needs and safety without the assistance of another person.
Residential board and care occupancy shall mean a building or portion thereof that is used for lodging and boarding of four (4) or more residents, not related by blood or marriage to the owners or operators, for the purpose of providing personal care services. This term shall include, but shall not be limited to, the following types of facilities:
(1)
Facilities containing group housing arrangements for physically or mentally handicapped persons who normally attend school in the community, attend worship in the community, or otherwise use community facilities.
(2)
Facilities containing group housing arrangements for physically or mentally handicapped persons who are undergoing training in preparation for independent living, for paid employment, or for other normal community activities.
(3)
Facilities containing group housing arrangements for the elderly that provide personal care services but that do not provide nursing care.
(4)
Facilities for social rehabilitation, alcoholism, drug abuse, or mental health problems that contain a group housing arrangement and that provide personal care services but do not provide acute care.
(5)
Assisted living facilities.
Tax supported housing shall mean any housing that is initially constructed by, and/or rent-subsidized by the taxpayers for the purpose of providing affordable housing for the at-need citizens within the jurisdiction of the city. This term shall also include privately-owned rental properties for which the rent is subsidized by the taxpayers.
(b)
As of the effective date of this section, all electric coil cooking devices purchased for the purpose of replacement in existing apartment buildings, nursing homes, tax supported housing, or facilities used for residential board and care occupancy or purchased as standard equipment in new apartment buildings, nursing homes, tax supported housing, or facilities used for residential board and care occupancy shall be equipped with listed and approved high end heat limiting technology.
(Ord. No. 2012-20, § 1, 9-18-12; Ord. No. 2017-02, § 1, 3-21-17)

ARTICLE III. - FIRE HYDRANT CODE[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
Charter reference— Authority of mayor and council to adopt codes of technical regulations, § 2-406.

Sec. 6-31. - Adopted.
There is hereby adopted for the purpose of establishing standards and procedures affecting the design, erection and construction of fire hydrants the AWWA Standard for Dry-Barrel Fire Hydrants, C 502, and 1973 Edition, and the foreword thereunto, as promulgated by the American Water Works Association, Inc., of which sketches labeled "A" and "B" are attached thereto, and of which not less than one (1) copy has been and is on file in the office of the city clerk, and save and except such portions as are hereinafter stricken or modified, is adopted and incorporated as fully as if set out at length herein.
(Ord. No. 78-5, 8-15-78)
State Law reference— Municipal authority to adopt fire codes, O.C.G.A. § 25-3-4.
Sec. 6-32. - Spacing.
(a)
Single-family residential areas. Maximum distance between hydrants shall be not more than five hundred (500) feet. Maximum distance from hydrant to any building shall be not more than four hundred (400) feet from nearest hydrant. Distance shall be measured along the necessary route of travel.
(b)
Multifamily dwelling units, commercial areas, industrial areas, other high value areas. Maximum distance between hydrants shall be not more than four hundred (400) feet. Maximum distance from hydrant to the furthest part of any building shall be not more than four hundred (400) feet from nearest hydrant. Distance shall be measured along the necessary route of travel.
(Ord. No. 78-5, 8-15-78)
Sec. 6-33. - Connecting hydrants to water mains.
All fire hydrants and valves need to be rodded to the water main. No pipe less than six (6) inches inside diameter shall be used for connection of hydrants to water mains and in high value areas it is recommended that not less than eight-inch pipe be used. All pipes shall be looped in a gridiron so that no leg is more than six hundred (600) feet long unless both ends are tied into the water main.
(Ord. No. 78-5, 8-15-78)
Sec. 6-34. - Valves.
Valves shall be installed at every hydrant to make it possible to shut off parts of the piping system for repairs and extensions without putting large sections out of service. These valves shall be installed at intervals so that it is not necessary to cut off more than five hundred (500) feet in high value districts and not more than eight hundred (800) feet in other areas, and arranged so that flow may be maintained through arterial mains.
(Ord. No. 78-5, 8-15-78)
Sec. 6-35. - Location.
(a)
Hydrants when installed at intersections of streets shall be located a minimum of six (6) feet from the corner of such intersection in order to prevent damage from vehicles.
(b)
Hydrants shall be installed so that the maximum distance from the curb or street is six (6) feet and the minimum distance is three (3) feet.
(c)
All hydrants shall be set in such a manner that the lowest nozzle opening is a minimum of eighteen (18) inches above the finish grade level and a maximum of twenty-four (24) inches above the grade level.
(d)
All hydrants shall be set with a four-and-one-half-inch outlet facing the street.
(e)
The base of all hydrants shall be set in a minimum of four (4) square feet of crushed rock in order to provide access for the hydrant barrel to drain.
(Ord. No. 78-5, 8-15-78)
Sec. 6-36. - Parking.
All hydrants shall be located so as to discourage parking in front of and near them. When it is necessary to locate hydrants in or near parking areas, curbing or other means shall be used to prevent the blocking of hydrants by vehicles.
(Ord. No. 78-5, 8-15-78)
Cross reference— Parking, stopping and standing, § 14-31 et seq.
Sec. 6-37. - Bury length.
The bury length of fire hydrants shall be a minimum of two and five-tenths (2.5) feet.
(Ord. No. 78-5, 8-15-78)
Sec. 6-38. - Number of hose and pumper outlet nozzles.
Each fire hydrant shall have two (2), two-and-one-half-inch hose nozzles and one (1) four-and-one-half-inch steamer nozzles.
(Ord. No. 78-5, 8-15-78)
Sec. 6-39. - Type of outlet nozzle threads.
All fire hydrant outlet nozzle threads must be in conformity with NFPA No. 194 for national (American) standard fire coupling screw threads.
(Ord. No. 78-5, 8-15-78)
Sec. 6-40. - Direction of rotation of operating nut.
There must be counterclockwise rotation of the square operating nut on fire hydrants.
(Ord. No. 78-5, 8-15-78)
Sec. 6-41. - Size.
The nominal diameter of the main valve of all fire hydrants shall be six (6) inches.
(Ord. No. 78-5, 8-15-78)
Sec. 6-42. - Size of joints.
The inlet connection of all fire hydrants shall be six (6) inches in nominal diameter.
(Ord. No. 78-5, 8-15-78)
Sec. 6-43. - Color.
All fire hydrants must be of aluminum color.
(Ord. No. 78-5, 8-15-78)
Secs. 6-44—6-60. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - FIRE DEPARTMENT[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
Cross reference— Departments generally, § 2-121 et seq.
State Law reference— Local fire departments generally, OCGA Title 25, Ch. 3.

Sec. 6-61. - Composition and rank.
(a)
Composition. The fire department shall consist of a chief and such subordinate officers and firemen as may from time to time be provided for by the mayor and city council, provided that the mayor and council may contract with the Union City Volunteer Fire Department, Inc., a nonprofit corporation of the state, to provide for personnel including a chief and assistant chief and firemen.
(b)
Rank. Following the fire chief in rank shall be the captain, the lieutenants, the sergeants and the firemen, in order of seniority in each group.
(Code 1976, § 3-603)
Sec. 6-62. - Fire chief.
(a)
Appointment. The fire chief shall be appointed by the mayor, by and with the advice and consent of city council, and shall be the chief executive officer of the fire department, subject always to the orders and regulations of the mayor and council.
(b)
Powers and duties. The fire chief shall be responsible for and have control over the fire department and all fire apparatus belonging to the city, and he shall have the following additional duties:
(1)
To see to the proper and speedy extinguishment of all accidental or intentionally caused fires;
(2)
To see to the enforcement of the fire prevention code adopted by the mayor and council;
(3)
To keep or cause to be kept adequate records of all fires, inspections, equipment, fire prevention efforts and other activities of the department;
(4)
To submit a report to the mayor and council on the activities of the department when requested to do so;
(5)
To establish rules and regulations concerning the discipline, good order, proper conduct, care and management of the fire department, subject to approval by the city council;
(6)
To investigate the causes of all extended or destructive fires occurring within the city.
(Code 1976, § 3-603)
Sec. 6-63. - Firemen—Qualifications, physical fitness requirements.
(a)
Qualifications. Any person employed by the city as a fireman shall have the following qualifications:
(1)
Be at least eighteen (18) years of age;
(2)
Have a high school diploma or a G.E.D. equivalent;
(3)
Have not been convicted of a felony within ten (10) years prior to employment;
(4)
Have good moral character as determined by investigation under the procedure approved by the state firefighters standards and training council;
(5)
Be in good physical condition as determined by a medical examination approved by the council;
(6)
Complete at least a basic training course of one hundred twenty (120) hours of instruction within twelve (12) months after being appointed a full-time, paid member of the department (if the department employs three (3) or more firemen); and
(7)
Train, drill or study in council-approved schools, classes or courses at least one hundred twenty (120) hours in each calendar year following the first year of employment.
(b)
Minimum physical fitness requirements. Each candidate for the position of fireman shall meet the following physical fitness requirements:
(1)
The candidate, after successfully completing the medical examination and with written authorization of the examining physician, shall run one and one-half (1½) miles within twelve (12) minutes;
(2)
The candidate will perform twenty-five (25) bent-knee sit-ups within ninety (90) seconds;
(3)
The candidate shall, from a completely extended arm position, pull the body upward so as to chin the horizontal bar being grasped by the hands, palms away, a total of five (5) consecutive times;
(4)
The candidate shall, from a horizontal position, successfully accomplish ten (10) push-ups as prescribed by the authority having jurisdiction;
(5)
The candidate, given a beam secured to a level floor and measuring twenty (20) feet long by three (3) to four (4) inches wide, and given a length of fire hose weighing at least twenty (20) pounds, shall walk the length of the beam carrying the length of the hose without falling off or stepping off the beam;
(6)
The candidate, given a weight of one hundred twenty-five (125) pounds, shall lift the weight from the floor and carry the weight one hundred (100) feet without stopping; and
(7)
The candidate, starting from an erect position with feet apart, the distance closely approximating shoulder width, shall move a fifteen-pound weight in the following manner: bend over, grasp the weight with both hands while it is at a point on the floor between the feet, and lift the weight to waist level, then place the weight on the floor approximately twelve (12) inches outside the left foot, and without letting go, raise the weight to waist level and touch it to the floor about twelve (12) inches outside the right foot. The weight shall then be moved alternately in this fashion from left foot to waist level to right foot; right to waist level to left until it has been moved seven (7) times in each direction with the total horizontal distance of travel being at least twenty-four (24) inches more than the space between the feet for each of the fourteen (14) moves. This shall be done in less than thirty-five (35) seconds.
(Code 1976, § 3-603; Ord. No. 81-2, 2-17-81)
State Law reference— Firefighter standards and training, OCGA § 25-4-2 et seq.
Sec. 6-64. - Same—Duties.
It shall be the duty of all firemen to become familiar with and proficient in the handling of all of the apparatus of the fire department, to be prepared at a moment's notice to respond to all alarms of fire, to observe all rules and regulations of the fire department established by the fire chief and to obey all orders of the commanding officer at all fires.
(Code 1976, § 3-603; Ord. No. 81-2, 2-17-81)
Sec. 6-65. - Daily inspections.
Each fire station and its equipment shall be examined daily. Such inspections shall be rigidly made and shall ensure that all apparatus is maintained in excellent working condition at all times, ready for immediate service and that all fire stations and surrounds are in a clean and sanitary condition.
(Code 1976, § 3-603)
Sec. 6-66. - Firefighting apparatus.
(a)
Right-of-way of apparatus. The fire department, including its apparatus, when going to any fire in the city or returning therefrom, shall have the right-of-way over the streets, lanes and ways of the city to the exclusion of all persons, vehicles and railroad trains of every kind.
(b)
Persons permitted on apparatus. No persons other than members of the fire department shall be permitted to ride upon the firefighting apparatus in going to or returning from fires.
(c)
Obstructing firefighting apparatus. It shall be unlawful for any person to obstruct in any manner any firefighting apparatus or any of the officers or members of the fire department in the performance of their duties.
(Code 1976, § 3-603)
Sec. 6-67. - Command at fires.
In case of fire, the officer of the highest rank at the fire shall take command of the fire department and direct the management thereof for the suppression of the fire in the best manner possible. Such commanding officer may cause any building to be removed, torn down or destroyed when such acts are necessary for the protection of other property and for the prevention of the spread of the conflagration.
(Code 1976, § 3-603)
Sec. 6-68. - Persons permitted within vicinity of fire.
No persons other than firemen, city officials, police officers and the property owner or his agent shall be allowed within the immediate vicinity of a fire, unless such persons are given permission to do so by the fire chief.
(Code 1976, § 3-603)
Sec. 6-69. - Obstructing fire plugs.
It shall be unlawful for any person to obstruct any fire plug so as to obstruct approaches to the same by the fire department.
(Code 1976, § 3-603)
Sec. 6-70. - False alarm.
It shall be unlawful for any person to make, give, send or turn in, in any manner or way whatsoever, a false fire alarm or riot alarm, knowing the same to be false.
(Code 1976, § 3-603)
Cross reference— False alarms generally, § 10-10.
Sec. 6-71. - Use of equipment outside city limits.
Upon the orders of the fire chief or the mayor, members of the fire department are authorized to go outside the corporate limits of the city for the purpose of extinguishing fires, rendering aid to other fire departments or for rendering aid in the case of accidents.
(Code 1976, § 3-603)

ARTICLE VI. - FIRE SERVICE FEES

Sec. 6-90. - Assessment and imposition of fees.
The city is hereby authorized to impose fees according to a schedule adopted by the mayor and council for services provided by the fire department to insurance companies whose insured are non-residents of the city, are involved in a motor vehicle collision in the city, and determined to be at fault where:
(1)
The coverage for such services is expressly provided by an insurance company to the non-resident insured and the services are lawfully billed to that insured; or
(2)
Emergency medical services are provided to the non-resident insured by the city's fire department, whenever the insured's medical insurance covers the services provided and the insured assigns the right to collect to the service provider; or
(3)
Other services are provided to the non-resident insured by the city's fire department which are expressly authorized by state or federal law to be billed to an insurance company.
(Ord. No. 2014-06, § 1, 7-15-14)
Sec. 6-91. - Administration of cost recovery.
(a)
The city manager shall institute processes necessary for fire department reporting, municipal court tracking of traffic citations, and finance department direct billing of fees authorized by this article.
(b)
The city manager may cause the functions and duties required by subsection (a) hereinabove to be provided using any procurement method authorized by this Code or any procurement policy of the city, including a contract with an outside vendor to perform said functions and duties.
(Ord. No. 2014-06, § 1, 7-15-14)
Sec. 6-92. - No fees charged to residents of city.
No fees under this article shall be imposed against any resident of the City of Union City, either with his or her insurance carrier or directly as a responsible party.
(Ord. No. 2014-06, § 1, 7-15-14)
