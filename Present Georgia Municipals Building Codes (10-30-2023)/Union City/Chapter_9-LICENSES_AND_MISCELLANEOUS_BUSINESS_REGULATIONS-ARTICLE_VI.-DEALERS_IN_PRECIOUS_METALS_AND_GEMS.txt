ARTICLE VI. - DEALERS IN PRECIOUS METALS AND GEMS[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— Municipal authority to regulate and license dealers in precious metals or gems, OCGA § 43-37-5.

Sec. 9-136. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Dealer in precious metals and gems means a person, as either owner or manager, whose business involves receiving through purchase or trade precious metals (such as gold, silver or platinum), jewelry, gems, precious or semiprecious stones, in any form but not one who purchases from licensed wholesalers in the normal course of business.
Employee means any person employed or allowed to perform duties, whether or not such person receives a salary, wages or other compensation or consideration, but not including the person covered on the license or renewal application.
Goods has its normal meaning, however it should be further construed in the broadest sense allowable and should include all forms of personal property.
Owner means any person, partner or stockholder owning twenty-five (25) percent or more of a business.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 9-137. - License required.
Every person doing business in the incorporated area of this city as a dealer in precious metals or gems shall, prior to commencing business, make proper application for and receive the appropriate business license.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-138. - Exclusions and exceptions.
Businesses which limit the purchase of used valuables to the acceptance of trade-ins for credit for new goods or the dispensing of refunds in regard to valuables previously purchased from the business shall not be regulated or covered under this article.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-139. - Application for permit.
Applicants for a permit under this article shall file with the city a written, sworn application on a form provided by the city, signed by the applicant if an individual, by all partners if a partnership or by the president and secretary if a corporation, and showing:
(1)
The name or names of the principals, agents, and employees of the applicant's business during the time that it will be operating in the city; the local address or addresses of such person or persons while engaged in such business; the permanent address or addresses of such person or persons; the capacity in which such person or persons will act (as proprietor, agent, employee or otherwise); the name and address of the person, firm or corporation for whose account the business will be carried on, if any; and if a corporation, the state of incorporation;
(2)
The fingerprints of the principals, agents and employees of the applicant's business, and other evidence which establishes, to the satisfaction of the city, the good character of such persons to carry on the business;
(3)
The applicant's place or places of business in the city, the zoning district at the place or places, and the length of time during which it is proposed that the business shall be conducted;
(4)
The place or places, other than the permanent place of business of the applicant, where the applicant within the six (6) months next preceding the date of the application conducted a similar business, stating the nature thereof and giving the post office and street address of any building or office in which such business was conducted;
(5)
A brief statement of the nature and the character of the advertising done or proposed to be done in order to attract customers;
(6)
Whether or not the person having the management or supervision of the applicant's business has been convicted of a crime, misdemeanor or violation of any municipal ordinance, the nature of such offense and punishment assessed therefor;
(7)
Credentials from the person, firm or corporation for which the applicant proposes to do business, authorizing the applicant to act as such representative; and
(8)
Such other information as to the identity or character of the person or persons having the management or supervision of the applicant's business or the method or plan of doing such business as the city may deem proper to fulfill the purpose of this article in the protection of the public good.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-140. - Character qualification.
No license or employee permit shall be issued to or renewed for any person who conducts or is employed in a business in which such owner or employee has, within five (5) years of issuance or renewal, been convicted of, or pled guilty or nolo contendere to, any felony or misdemeanor involving theft-related offenses. The licensee shall submit information to the city regarding the identity of all owners and employees on a continuing basis as changes occur. This information shall be forwarded to the police department for the purpose of enforcing these character qualifications.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-141. - False statements.
The application shall state thereon that "It is unlawful for any person to make a false statement on this application. Any false statement made on the application form will void the application ab initio and constitute a violation of this section."
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-142. - Surety bond.
(a)
Every applicant for a permit shall submit to the city cash, cashier's check or money order to be deposited in an escrow account in the amount of ten thousand dollars ($10,000.00), conditioned that the applicant shall comply fully with all applicable provisions of federal, state and local laws, rules and regulations concerning the activity for which the applicant is to be licensed, and that the property purchased is guaranteed to be in accordance with the specifications, standards and conditions represented to the purchaser by the applicant.
(b)
Any person aggrieved by any act of the principal named in such cash deposit in violation of the provisions of this article may proceed on such cash deposit against the principal.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81; Ord. No. 2010-20B, § 3, 7-20-10)
Sec. 9-143. - Employee permit.
Each person authorized or allowed to conduct any phase of business regulated in this article shall be considered an employee and must have an approved employee permit. This permit must be displayed along with the business license in accordance with license display requirements.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-144. - Business license and permit fee.
(a)
All businesses licensed under this article shall pay the following designated license and permit fees:
(1)
Dealers in precious metals or gems—Two hundred dollars ($200.00) per calendar year.
(2)
Employee permits—Fifty dollars ($50.00) per calendar year.
(b)
There shall be no proration or refund of any license fee. The license and permit issued under this article shall be a personal privilege and shall not be transferable.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-145. - Records to be kept.
All dealers of precious metals and gems shall, in addition to other records, keep books wherein shall be entered an accurate description of all property received by or pledged or sold to them. Such description shall include the brand name of the article, any identifying mark, number, initial, social security number or name of any person or company, and a statement of the kind of material of which it is made. In such books there shall be entered also the name of the person by whom same was delivered, deposited or sold, the name of the person receiving the item and the time when such transaction occurred. These entries shall be made no later than one (1) hour after the transaction. Such books and the articles so pledged or sold shall at all times be subject to inspection and examination by any law enforcement officer of the city or the state. Any person engaged in any business licensed under this article who shall fail or refuse to keep the records as provided in this section, or who shall make false entries concerning the transaction or who shall fail or refuse to permit an inspection by any law enforcement officer of the city or the state of his records and of the property pledged with or sold to him, shall be guilty of a violation of this article.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-146. - Daily report to city police.
Every business licensed hereunder shall make a daily report in writing to the city police department of all property pledged or purchased during the twenty-four-hour period ending at 10:00 p.m. on the date of the report. Such reports shall be typewritten and shall show: the name and address of the business; time of transaction; sequel number of the purchase tickets; amount paid or advanced; full description of articles, including kind, style, material, color, design, kind and number of stones in jewelry and all identifying names, marks, numbers, initials, or social security numbers; and a description of the person selling or trading the item, including name, address, race, weight and height.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-147. - Records maintained for four years.
The record of each purchase transaction regulated by this article shall be maintained for a period of not less than four (4) years from the date of transaction.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-148. - Special report.
It shall be the duty of each business licensed hereunder to keep an additional record book in which it shall list the name, address, race, height and age of every person from which the licensee receives smelted or nonformed precious metals. If the licensee receives or should be aware he is receiving smelted or nonformed precious metal from one (1) person or household more than twice in any one-year period, he shall immediately report each and every such occurrence to the chief of police of this city.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-149. - Identification required.
All businesses licensed under this article shall, when receiving or purchasing goods, require a proper identification (e.g., driver's license, military I.D. card, state approved I.D. card or company employee I.D. card) containing a picture showing positive identification of the person who is selling or trading, and such identification shall be verified.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-150. - Fingerprinting required.
All businesses licensed under this article shall require every person from whom it accepts goods to place the fingerprint of his right index finger (if right index finger is missing, the next finger in line will be used) in ink on the front of the copy of the purchase ticket which is to be sent to the city police department.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-151. - Erasers, obliterations, write-overs or type-overs are not allowed.
No person licensed under this article shall erase, obliterate, type over, write over or in any way cause the original entry on the records required under this article to become illegible. All purchase tickets filled out in error shall be sent to the city police department in their numbered order along with daily reports and shall have the word "VOID" typed or written across the front, such entry shall not be used to cause the original entry to be illegible. If an error is made and the ticket is still to be used, then a single line shall be drawn through the incorrect entry. This line shall not cause the original entry to be illegible and the correct information entry shall be made on the next available area.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-152. - Serial numbers.
No business licensed under this article shall accept any articles which have had the serial number altered, obliterated or removed from such article or which the person who is making the business transaction knows should have a serial number upon it, unless such person immediately notifies the police department.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-153. - Alteration.
Any item required to be kept or displayed shall be so kept in its original form as it existed when first received and its physical characteristics shall not be altered in any manner.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-154. - Purchased articles not to be disposed of for fifteen days after acquisition.
Every business licensed under this article shall hold, on the premises designated in the license for doing business, every item required to be listed under section 9-131 for at least fifteen (15) days before disposing the same by sale, transfer, shipment or otherwise. The provisions of this section shall not apply to purchases from licensed wholesalers in the normal course of business.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-155. - Display of goods pledged or purchased.
For a period of fifteen (15) days from the date of purchase or trade, all goods required to be listed under section 9-146 shall be kept in a clearly visible place on the premises, designated in the license for doing business, and readily available to the general public. Every item required to be displayed shall have attached to it either a copy of the purchase ticket or the number of the ticket.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-156. - Location of businesses.
All businesses licensed under this article shall be located in strict compliance with the city zoning ordinance and at a location with an identifiable street address, which shall be stated on the license or renewal application along with other required information. No business licensed under this article shall change its address without giving advance notice to the city and receiving an approval of the change, such approval to be granted if the new location complies with this article and the city zoning ordinance. No business licensed under this article shall be operated in or from a motel or hotel.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-157. - Hours of operation.
All businesses licensed under this article shall close their establishment and cease transacting any business no later than 10:00 p.m. each day and begin their operation and commence transacting business at no time before 7:00 a.m. each day.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-158. - Business transactions conducted outside.
No business transactions by any business licensed under this article shall be conducted outside the building designated as the normal place to conduct transactions unless the business does not have a building. Business transaction means any part of business including inspection or appraisal of any goods when it is possible for such goods to be taken inside the building where transactions normally are made ("possible" being here used in the broadest sense); and no persons other than those possessing a license or employee permit shall be allowed to conduct any form of business transactions either inside the establishment or on any part of outside land area specified on the license.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-159. - Separate business establishment.
No business licensed under this article shall be designed or operated so as to allow access to the business immediately from any other business without first exiting the other business into a common or open area to which the public at large has access.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-160. - Dealing with minors.
It shall be unlawful for any person licensed under this article to receive in purchase or trade any goods of any character or of any description from any person who has not attained the age of eighteen (18) years.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-161. - Stolen items.
Every person licensed under this article shall, immediately upon request, turn over to the city police department every item believed by the police department to be a stolen item. Such item shall be returned to the licensee only after it has been determined that such item is not stolen. If it is determined that such item is stolen, it shall be returned to its lawful owner.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-162. - Inspection.
As a condition of any license covered under this article, every licensee agrees to allow any law enforcement officer, pursuant to his official duties, to be and remain on or about his premises without the formality of a warrant, provided the officer does not unduly interfere in the licensee's operation.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-163. - Penalties.
Any person licensed under this article who shall engage in any of the following shall be guilty of a violation of municipal laws and punished as a misdemeanor, as defined by sate law:
(1)
Fail to make any entry of any required item in a permanent record book;
(2)
Make any false entry therein;
(3)
Falsify, obliterate, record so that information is illegible, destroy or remove from his place of business such permanent record book;
(4)
Refuse to allow any law enforcement officer of the city or the state to inspect such permanent record book, or any goods in his possession, during the ordinary hours of business or any reasonable time, or refuse to allow any member of the general public to inspect goods in his possession being held under purchase or trade (within the fifteen-day purchase period) during ordinary business hours;
(5)
Fail to maintain a record of each purchase or trade transaction for at least four (4) years; or
(6)
Violate any other provisions of this article.
Operation of any business under this article in violation of any provision of this section shall constitute a separate offense for each day of operation.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Sec. 9-164. - Revocation of license.
Any license issued under this article shall be subject to suspension or revocation in the same manner as specified in this chapter for other business licenses.
(Ord. No. 81-5, 4-21-81)
Secs. 9-165—9-180. - Reserved.
