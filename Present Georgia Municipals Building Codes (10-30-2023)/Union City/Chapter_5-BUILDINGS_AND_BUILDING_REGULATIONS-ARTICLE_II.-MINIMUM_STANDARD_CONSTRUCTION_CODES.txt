DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 5-16. - Adoption of state minimum standard codes.
It is the intent of the City of Union City to enforce the latest edition of the following state minimum standard codes, and all appendices thereto, as adopted and amended by the Georgia Department of Community Affairs:
(1)
International Building Code;
(2)
National Electrical Code;
(3)
International Fuel Gas Code;
(4)
International Mechanical Code;
(5)
International Plumbing Code;
(6)
International Residential Code for One- and Two-Family Dwellings;
(7)
International Energy Conservation Code; and
(8)
International Fire Code.
(Ord. No. 98-11, § 1, 7-21-98; Ord. No. 2007-11, § 1, 4-17-07)
Sec. 5-17. - Additional codes.
The following codes, the latest edition as adopted and amended by the Georgia Department of Community Affairs, are hereby adopted by reference as though they were copied herein fully for the purpose of enforcement within the City of Union City:
(1)
International Existing Building Code; and
(2)
International Property Maintenance Code, as amended in section 5-18 of this Code of Ordinances.
(Ord. No. 98-11, § 2, 7-21-98; Ord. No. 2007-11, § 2, 4-17-07; Ord. No. 2011-15, § 1, 9-20-11)
Sec. 5-18. - Amendment to the International Property Maintenance Code.
The International Property Maintenance Code, as adopted by the city, is hereby amended to remove Section 111, pertaining to Means of Appeal, from Chapter 1 of said Code.
(Ord. No. 2011-15, § 2, 9-20-11)

DIVISION 3. - BUILDING CODE

Sec. 5-56. - Adopted.
There is hereby adopted, for the purpose of establishing standards and procedures affecting the design, erection, and construction of buildings and structures, the International Building Code, 2006 edition with the Georgia State Amendments, as promulgated by the International Code Council, of which not less than one (1) copy has been and is on file in the office of the city clerk, and save and except such portions as hereinafter stricken or modified, is adopted and incorporated as fully as if set out at length herein.
(Code 1976, § 17-102; Ord. No. 80-10, § 1, 10-21-80; Ord. No. 86-7, § 3, 1-21-86; Ord. No. 89-5, § 2, 3-21-89; Ord. No. 91-9, § 3, 10-15-91; Ord. No. 2007-11, § 4, 4-17-07)
Sec. 5-57. - Definitions.
As used in the building code adopted in this division:
(1)
The words "building official" shall mean the officer or employee of the city whose duty it is to check and inspect buildings and the erection thereof to see that all building requirements of the city are met and complied with.
(2)
The term "corporation counsel" shall include city attorney.
(3)
The word "municipality" shall mean the city.
(Code 1976, § 17-102)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 5-58. - Appeals from grievances.
Any person aggrieved may take an appeal to the city council from any action taken or order given under the building code adopted by this division.
(Code 1976, § 17-102)

DIVISION 4. - PLUMBING CODE

Sec. 5-71. - Adopted.
There is hereby adopted for the purpose of establishing rules and regulations relating to the installation, repair, erection, alteration, use and maintenance of plumbing and sewer systems that are used, erected, installed or maintained in the city, that certain plumbing code known as the International Plumbing Code, 2006 edition with the Georgia State Amendments, and as thereafter amended or revised, as promulgated by the International Code Council, of which not less than one (1) copy has been and is on file in the office of the city clerk, and save and except such portions as are hereinafter stricken or modified, is adopted and incorporated as fully as if set out at length herein.
(Code 1976, § 17-103; Ord. No. 89-5, § 3, 3-21-89; Ord. No. 91-9, § 17, 10-15-91; Ord. No. 2007-11, § 6, 4-17-07)
Sec. 5-72. - Fee schedules.
Section 107.4. Schedule of permit fees. On all buildings, structures, or alterations, requiring a building permit, as set forth in section 105, a fee shall be paid at the time of filing application in accordance with the following schedule:
a.
Permit fees.
1.
No permit required by the building code of the City of Union City shall be transmitted to the applicant until the fees set forth herein shall have been paid. Nor shall an amendment to a permit be approved until the additional fee, if any shall be required, shall have been paid.
2.
For a permit for construction or alteration of a building or structure, the fee shall be as follows:
Three dollars ($3.00) per thousand dollars of the cost of the work to be performed and same being fifteen thousand dollars ($15,000.00) cost or less with no fee less than fifty dollars ($50.00).
Two dollars ($2.00) per thousand dollars of the cost of the work to be performed when said work exceeds fifteen thousand dollars ($15,000.00) in cost.
3.
No fee required by this subsection shall be less than fifty dollars ($50.00).
4.
For a permit for the removal of a building or structure from one lot to another, the fee shall be the same as would be required under subparagraph (2) above for new construction. Where the use of a street or streets of the city are required in the removal of a building or structure, the additional fee as required by a separate ordinance of the city shall be paid prior to the issuance of the permit for removal.
5.
For a permit for the demolition of a building or structure the fee shall be fifty dollars ($50.00).
(Ord. No. 91-9, § 18, 10-15-91)
Secs. 5-73—5-85. - Reserved.
DIVISION 5. - ELECTRICAL CODE

Sec. 5-86. - Adopted.
There is hereby adopted as the electrical code for the City of Union City, the National Electric Code, 2005 edition with the Georgia State Amendments, and as thereafter amended or revised, as promulgated by the National Fire Protection Association of which code not less than one (1) copy is on file in the office of the city clerk and the same is adopted and incorporated as fully as if set out at length herein.
(Code 1976, § 17-104; Ord. No. 89-5, § 4, 3-21-89; Ord. No. 91-9, § 19, 10-15-91; Ord. No. 2007-11, § 7, 4-17-07)
Sec. 5-87. - Permit required for work.
It shall be unlawful for any person, firm or corporation to make, or cause to be made, any electrical installations or work to be done without first obtaining a permit to do such work or installation from the city clerk. An application for a permit shall be made to the city clerk's office.
(Code 1976, § 17-104)
Sec. 5-88. - Inspection, approval of all work required.
The electrical inspector shall inspect and approve all electrical installation in the city and such inspection must be made before any electrical work is covered by construction. A certificate of inspection and approval must be issued by the electrical inspector and it shall be unlawful to use, or permit the use of, or to supply electrical power for wiring, light or heat, in a building or structure unless the required certificate of inspection and approval has been obtained.
(Code 1976, § 17-104)
Sec. 5-89. - Contractor's license required.
All persons, firms or corporations performing any electrical work or electrical contracting in the city shall have a valid current state electrical contractor's license.
(Code 1976, § 17-104)
Cross reference— Licenses and miscellaneous business regulations, Ch. 9.
Sec. 5-90. - Fee schedules.
Fee schedules will be set out from time to time and will be available at the city administrator's office.
(Ord. No. 91-9, § 20, 10-15-91)
Secs. 5-91—5-100. - Reserved.
