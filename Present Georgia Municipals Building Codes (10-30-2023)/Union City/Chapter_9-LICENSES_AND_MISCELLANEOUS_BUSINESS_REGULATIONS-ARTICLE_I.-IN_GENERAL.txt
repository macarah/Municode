ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 9-1. - Definitions.
The definitions used in section 9-42 shall likewise apply to this article.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 4-103A(1), 10-7-80)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 9-2. - License required.
Every business in this city, including any business located outside the city but engaged in business within the city, is hereby required to have a business license from the city for the privilege of engaging in a business, profession or occupation within the corporate limits, unless city licensing is prohibited under state law or the activity is exempted by this article.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 4-103A(2), 10-7-80)
Sec. 9-3. - License application.
(a)
Every person required to procure a license under the provisions of this article shall submit an application for the license to the administrator, which application shall conform to the requirements of this article in addition to any other provisions of this chapter.
(b)
Unless otherwise provided in this chapter, each application shall be a written statement upon forms provided by the city and submitted before February first of each year or ten (10) days prior to opening a new business.
(c)
Each application shall contain the following information:
(1)
Name and home address of the applicant if an individual, or home office address if a corporation or partnership;
(2)
Place where the proposed business is to be located;
(3)
Kind an class of business to be carried on as defined in section 9-42;
(4)
Names and home addresses of the partners, if a partnership;
(5)
Names and home addresses of the officers and directors, if a corporation; and
(6)
The amount of gross revenue for the preceding year and number of employees of the business for the third quarter of the preceding year and any other information as may be required by the administrator for the purpose of determining the amount of any business taxes to be collected under this chapter.
(d)
Each application shall be signed and sworn to by the applicant if an individual, or by a partner if a partnership, or by an officer if a corporation.
(e)
All information furnished or secured under the authority of this article shall be kept in strict confidence by the city, shall not be subject to public inspection, and shall be utilized solely by the officers of the city responsible for administering the provisions of this article and the city's business taxes.
(f)
False statements on any application for a license shall be grounds for immediate revocation of the license or denial of the application.
(g)
An application, license and registration fee of five dollars ($5.00) shall accompany the application along with any other fees as may be required elsewhere. This fee shall be nonrefundable.
(h)
Transient and temporary businesses are not required to pay the fee required by this section but shall pay the business tax under section 9-53. No license granted these businesses shall be valid after one hundred eighty (180) days.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 4-103A(2), 10-7-80)
Sec. 9-4. - Administration.
The administrator shall administer and enforce the provisions of this article for the application for and issuance of business licenses and the regulation of businesses under this article.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 4-103A(4), 10-7-80)
Sec. 9-5. - Rules and regulations.
The administrator shall have the power and authority to make and publish reasonable rules and regulations not inconsistent with this article or other laws of the city and the state or the Constitution of this state or the United States, for the administration and enforcement of the provisions of this article. These rules and regulations shall become effective upon adoption by the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 4-103A(5), 10-7-80)
Sec. 9-6. - Duties of administrator.
The administrator, or an authorized representative, shall have, among others, the following duties:
(1)
To prepare and provide the necessary forms for the registration and application for a business license and for the submission of any required information as may be necessary to properly administer and enforce the provisions of this article;
(2)
To issue to each person a business license within a reasonable time after the payment of the license fee assessed and any business taxes levied in this chapter; provided, however, where under other ordinances of the city, permits, certifications, and compliance with enumerated conditions are required for the operation of the business, the administrator shall not issue a business license until the applicant exhibits to the administrator the obtained permits, certifications, and compliance.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 4-103A(6), 10-7-80)
Sec. 9-7. - Duration of license.
Any license referred to in this article shall automatically expire on December thirty-first of the year of its issuance.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 4-103A(7), 10-7-80)
Sec. 9-8. - Procedure for issuance of license.
(a)
When any provision of this article provides for the review of an application for a license by a city officer designated therein, the administrator shall forward a copy of the application to that officer. The officer charged with the duty of reviewing the application shall make a recommendation thereon, favorable or otherwise, and shall return the recommendation to the administrator after receiving a copy of the application.
(b)
Upon the receipt of the recommendation of the reviewing officer as provided in this section, or upon the receipt of the application if no reviewing officer is designated, the administrator shall forward the recommendation and application to the mayor and council for consideration and action at its next regularly scheduled public meeting if review by the mayor and council is required.
(c)
No license shall be issued to any applicant whose place of business is not in full compliance with all minimum standard building codes adopted by the city.
(d)
Upon the express approval of the mayor and council when so required or otherwise upon a determination by the administrator that the application is in order and all requirements have been met, the administrator shall issue a business license to the applicant therefor, which license shall state the nature of the business authorized and bear the date of issuance and the signature of the administrator.
(e)
The granting of a business license under any provision of this chapter shall be deemed a privilege only and nothing contained in this chapter shall be construed as granting any person whose business is subject to municipal regulation any legal right to engage in that business.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 4-103A(8), 10-7-80)
Sec. 9-9. - Display of license or registration.
All persons shall exhibit and display the license or registration issued to them under this chapter in some conspicuous place near the primary entrance of their business establishment at which address the license or registration was issued. Any transient or nonresident person doing business within the city shall carry the license or registration either upon his person or in any vehicle or other conveyance which is used in the business and the person shall exhibit the same to any authorized enforcement officer of the city when so requested.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 4-103A(9), 10-7-80)
Sec. 9-10. - Renewal of licenses.
Unless otherwise provided, each licensee shall make a written application for renewal on forms supplied by the administrator no later than February first of each calendar year, which application shall contain substantially the same information as the initial application.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 4-103A(10), 10-7-80)
Sec. 9-11. - Revocation; suspension of licenses.
(a)
Whenever the state shall revoke any permit or license, the city license to deal in that product shall thereupon be automatically revoked without any action by the mayor and council or any municipal officer.
(b)
Whenever a holder of a license granted in this article by the mayor and council shall fail to open for business the establishment referred to in the license and begin the sale of products or product authorized by the license within six (6) months from the date of issuance of the license, or whenever a holder of a license shall begin the operation of a business and sale of the product or products as authorized in the license but shall fail to operate the business for a period of six (6) consecutive months, the holder shall automatically forfeit his license. The license holder shall receive notice, a hearing and all other due process as required in subsection (c) of this section.
(c)
No license which has been issued or which may hereafter be issued by the city to any person under this article shall be revoked, except for due cause as defined in this section and after a hearing and upon the prior seven-day written notice to the holder of the license of the time, place and purpose of the hearing and a statement of the charge upon which the hearing shall be held.
(d)
When circumstances warrant the immediate cessation of any business activity, the administrator shall have the authority to summarily and immediately suspend the activity upon written notice to the holder of the license for the activity. A suspension shall be valid, until a revocation proceeding may be held, up to a maximum of thirty (30) days.
(e)
Due cause for the revocation of a license shall consist of the violation of any laws or ordinances regulating the businesses, or violation of regulations made pursuant to authority granted for the purpose of regulating the businesses, or for the violation of any state or federal law, or for the violation of or conflict with any city ordinance other than traffic ordinances.
(f)
The hearing shall be conducted before the mayor and council by the administrator or the city attorney.
(g)
The mayor and council may, if it finds this chapter to have been violated by the licensee, his agent, or employee, or a license to have been issued in conflict with state law or this Code, revoke the license in its entirety, suspend the same for a specified period of time, place the licensee on probation, or place other conditions thereon as the mayor and council may deem necessary.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 4-103A(11), 10-7-80)
Sec. 9-12. - Change of address.
Any person moving from one (1) location to another shall notify the administrator of the move and the new address in writing on a form provided by the administrator no later than the day of moving. The same business license will be valid at the new location if the new location conforms to the zoning regulations of the city.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 4-103A(12), 10-7-80)
Sec. 9-13. - Licenses not transferable.
Business licenses shall not be transferable and a transfer of ownership shall be considered in the same light as the termination of the business and the establishment of a new business; therefore, a new license fee shall be required for the new owner of the business.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 4-103A(13), 10-7-80)
Sec. 9-14. - Branch, multiple, or joint businesses.
Any business with multiple locations or branches and any business which engages in several different businesses shall be deemed to be separate businesses for purposes of this article with the requirement of separate licenses or registrations for each.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 4-103A(14), 10-7-80)
Sec. 9-15. - Subsequent amendments.
This article shall be subject to amendment or repeal, in whole or in part, at any time, and no amendment or repeal shall be construed to deny the right of the city to assess, levy and collect any of the license fees prescribed. The payment of any license fee provided for in this chapter shall not be construed to deny the right of the city to assess, levy and collect any of the license fees prescribed. The payment of any license fee provided for in this chapter shall not be construed as prohibiting the assessment, levy or collection of additional license fees upon the same person.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 4-103A(15), 10-7-80)
Sec. 9-16. - Construction permits.
Any person obtaining a permit for grading, landscaping, trenching; or construction, repair, or demolition of any structure; or any other work which may require an inspection by a city inspector shall, at the time of making application for such permit, list the name and address of the person or business securing such permit, and the names and addresses of all contractors and subcontractors who shall perform any service in connection with the work for which such permit is sought. If, at the time of applying for such permit, any of such contractors and subcontractors have not been employed or contracted with, then upon the employment or contracting with such contractors or subcontractors, and before the performance of any service by same, such person shall immediately list his name and address on the form to be secured from the administrator and shall file same with the administrator. Any person obtaining such permit for any of the services in this section which require an inspection by a city inspector shall, prior to beginning the service, pay the appropriate inspection or permit fee as determined by the administrator in accordance with applicable ordinances.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 4-103B(1), 10-7-80)
Cross reference— Buildings and building regulations, Ch. 5.

Sec. 9-18. - Private detectives, private detective agencies; applications; licensing; regulations and prohibited activities.
(a)
Definition. Private detectives or private detective agencies are defined as those following the business or vocation pursued by all persons or agencies making investigations for others when the investigations involve the conduct, life or affairs of the persons investigated. These terms shall not include lawyers and accountants in the course of their practice or incident thereto.
(b)
Applications for licensing.
(1)
Any person who shall desire to engage in the business of a private detective or detective agency shall first make application to the chief of police and the application shall contain the following information:
a.
The name of the applicant, his age and present address;
b.
The places the applicant has resided in the past ten (10) years;
c.
The places the applicant has been employed for the past ten (10) years;
d.
The marital status of the applicant;
e.
Information concerning the experience the applicant has had in detective work;
f.
Information concerning the applicant's criminal record, if any;
g.
The names and addresses of two (2) residents of the state for character purposes;
h.
The names and addresses of two (2) credit references; and
i.
Any other information the chief of police deems pertinent.
(2)
The application shall be accompanied by a photograph not more than twelve (12) months old of the applicant.
(3)
The application shall be made under oath and the giving of false information shall result in the revocation of any license issued in reliance thereon.
(c)
Processing the application. It shall be the duty of the chief of police to accept each application presented to him as set out above. The chief shall then investigate the character, integrity and competence of the applicant. The chief shall then submit a written report to the city administrator together with recommendations for the issuance or denial of the license. The city administrator shall report to the mayor and council at the next regular meeting. The mayor and council shall determine the merits of the matter and either direct the issuance of the license or deny the same.
(d)
Age of applicants. No person shall be granted a license as a private detective unless that person has attained twenty-one (21) years of age.
(e)
Separate license for each private detective. A separate license must be obtained for each person engaged in activities described herein even though that person is an employee of another person duly licensed under this section.
(f)
Business tax. The annual business tax for each person shall be as set forth in article II of this chapter.
(g)
Annual licensing. Any person wishing to engage in the business of private detective or private detective agency shall make the initial application, pay the license fee and business tax and be granted an initial license prior to beginning any detective work in the city. Initial licenses shall expire on December thirty-first of the year in which they are granted. Thereafter the person shall make application for continuance of the business in the city annually on or before December fifteenth of each year for a license for the coming year. The procedure on the application shall be followed as was followed on the original application.
(h)
Police supervision. All licensed private detectives shall be subject to supervision and direction by police officers of the city.
(i)
Regulations and prohibited activities. Nothing contained in this section shall be construed to allow any person licensed hereunder to violate the laws of the United States, the state, or the city, nor shall any person licensed hereunder violate the civil or constitutional rights of any person nor shall any person licensed under this article use the license, credentials, or position to collect money or obtain property from any person. Should the city administrator receive a complaint from any person that a licensee has violated any provision of this section, he shall within thirty (30) days of receiving the complaint set up a hearing before the mayor and council and give the person against whom the complaint was made fifteen (15) days' notice of the hearing. At the hearing the mayor or, in his absence, the mayor pro tem shall preside. Both the complaining party and the person against whom the complaint is made shall be entitled to counsel of their choice; to subpoena and call witnesses; and be heard on the merits. Witnesses shall testify under oath and be subject to penalty for perjury if false testimony is given. The purpose of the hearing shall be to determine the fitness of the person against whom the complaint is made to continue to operate as a private detective in the city. After hearing evidence from both sides, the mayor and council shall vote "yes" or "no" to continue the license. If a majority of the council present and voting, vote "no," the license shall be thereupon revoked. No person whose license has been revoked shall be allowed to reapply for a license within twelve (12) months of the revocation.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 4-103B(3), 10-7-80)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
State Law reference— Operators of private detective businesses and private security businesses, OCGA § 43-38-1; restrictions on local granting of business license, OCGA § 43-38-14.1.
Sec. 9-19. - Garage sales and rummage sales; regulation of and license fee required.
Sales commonly known as garage sales or rummage sales conducted on residential property shall be subject to the following rules and regulations:
(1)
These sales shall be conducted only by the owner or tenant of the residential property use.
(2)
No such sales shall be conducted for a longer period than seventy-two (72) hours consecutively in any six-month period on one (1) residential lot.
(3)
No wares, goods, or merchandise shall be advertised or offered for sale or swap, or given away except goods, wares, and merchandise owned by the property owner or the tenant conducting the sale.
(4)
No goods, wares, or merchandise shall be purchased by the owner or tenant for the purpose of selling, swapping, or giving away same at such a sale.
(5)
Any person desiring to conduct such a sale shall notify the administrator of the intent at least forty-eight (48) hours prior to the time the sale is to begin. The administrator or his authorized representatives shall issue a license to the person to conduct a sale upon the payment of a license fee of ten dollars ($10.00) if the applicant otherwise meets the requirements of this section. This fee shall be in lieu of any other fees or business taxes in this chapter.
(6)
Sales for religious or charitable purposes only shall be exempt from the provisions of this section.
(7)
It shall be unlawful for any person to conduct a garage sale or rummage sale without first obtaining a license as described herein or in such a manner as to violate any of the provisions of this section and the administrator is authorized and directed to enforce the provisions of this section.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 4-103B(4), 10-7-80)
Sec. 9-20. - Bail bonds.
(a)
Prior to the issuance of a business license to write or post bail bond the applicant shall be required to submit cash, cashier's check or money order to be deposited in an escrow account with a value of twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000.00). The money shall be contingent upon the payment of the sums due the city on forfeited bonds written by the applicant.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to write or post bail bonds in the city and engage in the bail bonds business, unless and until that person has obtained a license as provided in this chapter and has posted an approved bond as provided in this section.
(c)
It shall be the duty of all persons lawfully engaged in business as bail bondsmen to file with the revenue collection officer a list of the names of all their employees, with their home address and home telephone number. Changes in the list of employees, adding new employees, must be filed with the revenue collection officer within three (3) days from the date of any such change. All new employees shall undergo a background investigation to be performed by the department of public safety. Every current employee shall undergo an annual background investigation to be performed by the department of public safety. A twenty-five dollar ($25.00) investigation fee shall be charged for each new and renewal investigation.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 4-103B(5), 10-7-80; Ord. No. 95-8, § 3, 12-19-95; Ord. No. 2010-20B, § 1, 7-20-10)

Upon requesting a permit the applicant must show evidence of personal liability insurance in the amount of one hundred thousand dollars ($100,000.00) for each person and three hundred thousand dollars ($300,000.00) for each accident plus ten thousand dollars ($10,000.00) for property damages and provide the name of an agent for service domiciled in the county.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 4-103B(6), 10-7-80)

Sec. 9-23. - Pawnbrokers.
(a)
License required. Each pawnbroker doing business within this municipality shall be required to obtain a license from the city clerk in the manner specified in this chapter.
(b)
Fee established. The annual business license fee for each pawnbroker doing business in this city shall be set from time to time and a schedule of such fee is on file in the city clerk's office.
(c)
Application. In addition to all other information required of an applicant in this chapter all those applying for pawnbroker's licenses shall include in their application a complete record of all arrests and convictions against the applicant and every partner, officer, or director of the applicant for violations of any and all laws and ordinances of the city, state, or federal government.
(d)
Procedure for issuance. The city clerk shall forward all applications for pawnbroker's licenses to the chief of police within forty-eight (48) hours of the time of receipt of the application. The police chief shall review the application and make a recommendation thereon, favorable or otherwise, and shall return such recommendation to the city clerk within seven (7) days after receiving a copy of the application. The city clerk shall then forward the application and recommendation to the mayor and council for consideration and action at its next regular scheduled public meeting.
(e)
Limitation on issuance. No pawnbroker's license shall be issued to any person who has been convicted of the offense of receiving stolen goods or of burglary or robbery.
(f)
Records. Each pawnbroker licensed under the terms of this chapter shall furnish to the police chief each week a list of every article pledged with him or sold to him during the previous week, giving a full and accurate description of the article, from whom it was received, and the hour of the day received.
(g)
Minors. No pawnbroker shall have any business dealings as a pawnbroker with a minor, nor shall a pawnbroker's license be issued to a minor, nor shall a pawnbroker employ a minor to assist him in his business.
(h)
Stolen goods. It shall be the duty of every pawnbroker to report to the police any article pledged with him, or which is sought to be pledged with him, if he shall have reason to believe that the article was stolen or lost and found by the person attempting to pledge it in the case of a lost article.
(Code 1976, § 15-204)
State Law reference— Pawnbrokers, OCGA § 44-12-130 et seq.
Sec. 9-24. - Used car dealers.
(a)
License required. Any used car or used motor vehicle dealer, as such terms are defined in OCGA section 43-47-2, who does business within this municipality shall be required to obtain a license from the city clerk in the manner specified in this chapter.
(b)
Fee established. The annual business license fee for each used car or used motor vehicle dealer doing business in the city shall be set from time to time and a schedule of such fee is on file in the city clerk's office.
(c)
Procedure for issuance. The city clerk shall forward all applications for used car dealer's licenses to the chief of police within forty-eight (48) hours of the time of receipt of the application. The police chief shall review the application and make a recommendation thereon, favorable or otherwise, and shall return such recommendation to the city clerk within seven (7) days after receiving a copy of the application. The city clerk shall then forward the application and recommendation to the mayor and council for consideration and action at its next regular scheduled public meeting.
(d)
Restriction on issuance. No license under this section shall be issued to any applicant who has not been licensed by the state board of registration of used car dealers.
(e)
Records. Each used car or used motor vehicle dealer licensed hereunder shall keep a record of all motor vehicles offered for sale, exchange, or disposal to the public, which record shall show the make of the motor vehicle, the year of its manufacture, its serial number, and its engine number, and which record shall always be kept available for the chief of police or any patrolman or police officer within the city and open to his inspection at any time. It shall be the further duty of every such person to immediately report to the chief of police the presence in his place of business of any motor vehicle on which the serial or engine number has been defaced or altered.
(f)
Inspections. It shall be the duty of the chief of police to make inspections from time to time for the purpose of seeing that the records required in this section are being kept.
(g)
Exceptions. Nothing in this section shall be deemed to apply to any individual making an isolated sale of his own vehicle.
(Code 1976, § 15-205)
State Law reference— Municipal authority to regulate and license used car dealers, OCGA § 43-47-14.
Sec. 9-25. - Junk dealers and junkyards.
(a)
License required. Each junk dealer, as such term is defined in this section, who does business within the city, shall be required to obtain a license from the city clerk in the manner specified in this chapter.
(b)
Definitions. For the purpose of this section, the following words and phrases shall have the meanings respectively ascribed to them herein:
Business premises or premises means the area of a junkyard as described in a junk dealer's license or application for license, as provided for in this section.
Junk means old iron, steel, brass, copper, tin, lead, or other base metals; old cordage, ropes, rags, fibers or fabrics; old rubber; old bottles or other glass; bones; wastepaper and other waste or discarded material which might be prepared to be used again in some form; and motor vehicles, no longer used as such, to be used for scrap metal or stripping of parts; but "junk" shall not include materials or objects accumulated by a person as by-products, waste, or scraps from the operation of his own business, or materials or objects held and used by a manufacturer as an integral part of his own manufacturing processes.
Junk dealer means a person who operates a junkyard, as defined below, within the city.
Junkyard means a yard, lot, or place, covered or uncovered, outdoors or in an enclosed building, containing junk as defined above, upon which occurs one or more acts of buying, keeping, dismantling, processing, selling, or offering for sale any such junk, in whole units or by parts, for a business or commercial purpose, whether or not the proceeds from such act or acts are to be used for charity.
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
(c)
Application. Application for license under this section shall be made as provided in section 9-1, except that such application shall contain the following additional information:
(1)
Trade names used during the previous five (5) years by the applicant and each person signing the application, along with the locations of prior establishments;
(2)
Names and addresses of employees of each person signing the application during the previous five (5) years;
(3)
Name, residence address, and telephone number of each person employed or intended to be employed in the business as of the time the application is filed;
(4)
A sketch of the actual premises to be used in connection with the business, giving distances in feet and showing adjoining roads, property lines, buildings, and uses; and
(5)
A description of the materials with which any buildings to be used in connection with the licensed business are or are to be made; a sketch giving distances, showing the location of such buildings on the business premises; and a diagram or plan giving distances and heights, showing floors, exits, entrances, windows, ventilators, and walls.
(d)
Review of application. No action on any application for a license to operate a junkyard shall be taken by the city council until the planning commission has reviewed such application and forwarded its recommendation thereof to the mayor and council.
(e)
Fee established. The annual business license fee for each junk dealer doing business in this municipality shall be set from time to time and a schedule of such fee is on file in the city clerk's office.
(f)
General operating requirements. The following general operating requirements shall apply to all junk dealers licensed in accordance with the provisions of this section:
(1)
The junkyard, together with things kept therein, shall at all times be maintained in a sanitary condition;
(2)
No space not covered by the license shall be used in the licensed business;
(3)
No water shall be allowed to stand in any place on the premises in such manner as to afford a breeding place for mosquitoes;
(4)
No weeds shall be permitted to attain a height of more than six (6) inches;
(5)
No garbage or other waste liable to give off a foul odor or attract vermin shall be kept on the premises; nor shall any refuse of any kind be kept on the premises, unless such refuse is junk as defined in this section and is in use in the licensed business;
(6)
No junk shall be allowed to rest upon or protrude over any public street, walkway, or curb, or become scattered or blown off the business premises;
(7)
Junk shall be stored in piles not exceeding ten (10) feet in height and shall be arranged so as to permit easy access to all such junk for firefighting purposes;
(8)
No combustible material of any kind not necessary or beneficial to the licensed business shall be kept on the premises; nor shall the premises be allowed to become a fire hazard;
(9)
Gasoline and oil shall be removed from any scrapped engines or vehicles on the premises;
(10)
No noisy processing of junk or other noisy activity shall be carried on in connection with the licensed business on any Sunday, Christmas, Thanksgiving, or at any time between the hours of 6:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m.;
(11)
The area on the premises where junk is kept, other than indoors, shall be enclosed, except for entrances and exits, with a solid vertical wall, planted screen or similar opaque screen or fence of a minimum height of six (6) feet measured from ground level. The fence or wall shall not contain any poster or advertising of any kind excepting one (1) sign of the licensee not exceeding the size set forth in the sign ordinance of the city. Entrances and exits shall not be wider or more numerous than reasonably necessary for the conduct of the licensed business;
(12)
The licensee shall permit inspection of the business premises by any police officer at any reasonable time;
(13)
No junkyard shall be allowed to become a nuisance; nor shall any junkyard be operated in such manner as to become injurious to the health, safety, or welfare of the community or of any residents close by.
(g)
Records. Each acquisition of junk shall be recorded in a permanent type register kept on the business premises, giving the name and residence address of the person from whom the acquisition was made, a description of the junk acquired, and the date of the transaction. Such data shall be held available for inspection by any police officer.
(h)
Stolen goods. Every junk dealer who shall receive or be in possession of any goods, articles, or things of value which may have been lost or stolen shall upon demand produce such article or thing to any member of the police department for examination.
(i)
Vehicles. Every vehicle used by a junk dealer in the conduct of his business shall bear thereon in legible characters the names and addresses of the owner and proprietor thereof.
(Code 1976, § 15-206)
Charter reference— Authority to regulate or prohibit junk dealers, § 1-103(17).
State Law reference— Junk dealers, OCGA § 43-22-1 et seq.
Sec. 9-26. - Auctions.
(a)
License required. Each person who desires to conduct an auction within this municipality shall be required to obtain a license therefor from the city clerk in the manner specified in this chapter.
(b)
Fee established. The business license fee for each auction licensed under this section shall be set from time to time and a schedule of such fee is on file in the city clerk's office.
(c)
Application. Application for a license under this section shall be made as provided in section 9-3, except that such application shall contain the following additional information:
(1)
A statement setting forth when, where, and for how long the auction sale will be conducted;
(2)
A certified copy of the state auctioneer's license of the person who is to conduct the auction.
(d)
Bond. Every applicant for a license under this section shall submit with the city clerk cash, cashier's check or money order to be deposited in an escrow account in the amount of two hundred fifty dollars ($250.00), conditioned that the applicant, if issued a license hereunder will comply fully with all of the provisions of the ordinances of this city and the statutes of this state regulating and concerning auctions and auctioneers, will render true and strict amounts of all his sales to any person or persons employing him to make the same, will not practice any fraud or deceit upon bidders or purchasers of property from him at any auction sale or suffer or permit any person in his employ to practice any such fraud or deceit, and will pay all damages which may be sustained by any person by reference of any fraud, deceit, negligence, or other wrongful act on the part of the licensee, his agent, or employees in the conduct of any auction or in the exercise of the calling of auctioneer. A liability insurance policy issued by an insurance company authorized to do business in this state which conforms to the above requirements may be permitted by the city clerk in his discretion in lieu of a bond. Bonds will be held by the city for a minimum period of twelve (12) months after business is terminated.
(e)
Cappers, boosters, or by-bidders. It shall be unlawful for any person to act or to employ another in any auction sale as a by-bidder or what is sometimes known as a "capper" or "booster," or to make or to accept any false or misleading bid, or to pretend to buy or sell any article sold or offered for sale at such auction.
(f)
Exemption. Nothing in this section shall be held to apply to auction sales conducted by trustees or referees in bankruptcy; executors, administrators, receivers, or other public officers acting under judicial process, nor to the sale of real property, at auction.

Sec. 9-27. - Special event tents.
Any person, firm or corporation is allowed the use of an on-premise special event tent for a special use or event, such as, but not limited, grand openings, seasonal sales, promotional sales, liquidation going out of business sales, fire sales or free promotions, only under the following conditions and requirements:
(a)
No tent shall be erected or used except in connection with a permanent structure and providing a paved parking area. The site must comply with the zoning ordinance of the City of Union City and all rules and regulations thereof.
(b)
The maximum size allowance for such tents shall not exceed sixty (60) feet by one hundred twenty (120) feet.
(c)
The maximum number of special event tent permits to be issued to a single business location or parcel shall be two (2) per year for a period of time not to exceed sixteen (16) days for each issuance.
(d)
All special event tents must be approved by the Union City Fire Marshal.
(e)
Special exceptions may be granted by the mayor and council provided the request is presented to them ten (10) days prior to the date of the event or use.
(f)
Permit fees: No permit shall be issued until the exact dimension and area and location of tent has been filed with the zoning administrator and fees have been paid as provided.

Sec. 9-28. - Solid waste handlers; licenses.
(a)
Definition. For the purposes of this section, the definition of solid waste and solid waste handling shall be the same as defined within section 7-2.
(b)
License required. No person shall conduct business as a solid waste handler within the city without first obtaining a business license from the city administrator in the manner specified in this section.
(c)
Fee established. The annual business license fee for each solid waste handler doing business within the city shall be set from time to time as deemed appropriate by the governing body. Current schedules of such license fees shall be kept on file in the city administrator's office.
(d)
Application. All persons desiring to obtain a license under this section shall submit an initial written application, and a renewal annually thereafter, to the city for such privilege upon forms to be approved by the governing body. Such applications shall state the name and address of the applicant, the location of the business, the service area of the business, proof of compliance with state license laws and with city ordinances (particularly with Chapter 7 of the city code), proof of ownership of county license, if required, and such other information as may be required by the governing body.
(e)
Letters of assurance required.
(1)
In addition to the information required of an applicant for a solid waste handler's license, each initial and renewal application must also include a letter of solid waste disposal assurance from a state-permitted solid waste disposal facility. The letter must be on the letterhead of the entity owning the disposal facility and must contain the following information:
a.
Name and address of the solid waste disposal facility to be utilized by the applicant.
b.
Name, address and telephone number of the entity to whom the state permit is issued.
c.
Name, title and telephone number of the authorized individual or company official signing the assurance letter on behalf of the disposal facility.
d.
Estimated life of permitted capacity remaining at current and projected rates of waste disposal in tons or cubic yards.
e.
A statement that sufficient disposal capacity exists to accommodate the solid waste handling applicant over the expected remaining life of the disposal facility based on the remaining capacity and the amount of waste received at the disposal facility.
f.
A statement that appropriate arrangements are in place such that the disposal facility will reserve a portion of its capacity on behalf of the solid waste handler license applicant.
(2)
In the event the proposed disposal facility has a remaining life of less than ten (10) years, the license application must include additional letters of solid waste disposal assurance containing the above-described information such that a total of ten (10) years of disposal assurance is provided.
(f)
Compliance with section. It shall be unlawful for any person to operate, control, manage or own any business which handles solid waste within the city without having complied with the provisions of this section. Noncompliance shall subject such a violator to the revocation of any business license which may have been issued pursuant to this section, and to a fine and/or imprisonment.
