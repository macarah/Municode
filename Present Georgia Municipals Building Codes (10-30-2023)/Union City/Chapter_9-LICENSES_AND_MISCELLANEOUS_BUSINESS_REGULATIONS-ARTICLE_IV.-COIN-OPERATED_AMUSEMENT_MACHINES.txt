ARTICLE IV. - COIN-OPERATED AMUSEMENT MACHINES

Sec. 9-91. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Amusement center means any establishment where any coin-operated amusement machine is maintained.
Amusement center proprietor means any proprietor of an amusement center as both terms are defined in this section.
Coin-operated amusement machine means any machine or device operated by means of the insertion of a coin, token or similar object for the purpose of receiving the temporary services of that machine for recreation or game of skill and for the playing of which a fee is charged. The term does not include vending machines in which recreational features are not incorporated, nor does the term include any coin-operated mechanical musical devices or children's rides.
Operator means any person, firm, corporation, partnership or association who sets up for operation by another and leases or rents for the purpose of operation by another any machine as regulated by this article, whether such leasing or renting be for a fixed charge or rental, or on the basis of a division of the income derived from such device, or otherwise.
Proprietor means any person, firm, corporation, partnership, association or club who, as the owner or lessee, has under his or its control any establishment, place or premises in or at which a coin-operated amusement machine is placed or kept for use or play, or an exhibition for the purpose of use or play by the general public.
(Ord. No. 82-1, § 5-18-82)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 9-92. - License required.
(a)
No person shall engage in the business of an operator or proprietor of any coin-operated amusement machines without first having obtained the proper license therefor.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to own, maintain or operate within the city a coin-operated amusement machine without having obtained a license from the city for the operation of such device. The provisions of this section shall not apply to devices owned and operated exclusively for the sale of merchandise, where neither the element of chance nor skill is involved.
(Ord. No. 82-1, 5-18-82; Ord. No. 82-2, 5-18-82)
Sec. 9-93. - Operator license fee.
The license fee for each operator shall be set from time to time and a schedule of such fees is on file in the office of the city clerk. All operator license fees shall be payable annually in advance. In no case shall any portion of the license fee be refunded to the licensee.
(Ord. No. 82-1, 5-18-82)
Sec. 9-94. - Amusement center proprietor license fee.
A proprietor's license for the operation of an amusement center is required. Said license is available at no charge to the applicant however, an amusement center proprietor is subject to a regulatory fee as established from time to time by the Mayor and Council. A schedule is on file in the office of the City Clerk.
(Ord. No. 82-1, 5-18-82; Ord. No. 94-1, § 1, 1-18-94; Ord. No. 98-6, § 2, 5-19-98)
Sec. 9-95. - Application for proprietor license.
(a)
Application for license shall be filed in writing with the city clerk, on a form to be provided by the city, and shall specify the following:
(1)
The name and address of the applicant, and, if a firm, corporation, partnership or association, the principal officers thereof and their addresses;
(2)
Proprietors shall list the address of the premises where the licensed machine or machines are to be operated, together with the character of the business as carried on at such place;
(3)
Proprietors shall list the number of machines to be maintained on the premises;
(4)
The name and address of the owner of the machine or machines, if other than the proprietor, which information shall not be released to the general public without the consent of the licensee;
(5)
Whether or not the person having management of or supervision over the applicant's business have been convicted of a crime, misdemeanor or the violation of any municipal ordinance, the nature of such offense, and the punishment assessed therefor.
(b)
The proper license fee shall accompany such application. The license shall be posted in a conspicuous place in the establishment of the licensee. Such license shall be nonassignable and nontransferable, and, in the case of a proprietor, shall apply only to the premises for which such license is issued. In addition, each machine shall have placed upon it by the licensee a sticker showing the license number of its operator and proprietor.
(Ord. No. 82-1, 5-18-82)
Sec. 9-96. - Character qualification.
No license shall be issued to or refunded for any person who has or employs a person who has, within five (5) years of the issuance or renewal, been convicted of, or pied guilty or nolo contendere to, any felony involving any federal or state offense or to any federal or state misdemeanor involving drug-related offenses or offenses involving moral turpitude. The licensee will submit the identity of all owners and employees to the city on a continuing basis as changes occur, this information to be forwarded to the city police department, prior to the issuance or renewal of any license or permit issued under this article, for the purpose of allowing a complete check in order that these character qualifications be fulfilled.
(Ord. No. 82-1, 5-18-82)
Sec. 9-97. - Increasing number of devices.
In case a proprietor licensed under the provisions of this article desires, after the expiration of any portion of any license year, to increase the number of machines to be used, played or exhibited for use or play in his establishment, he shall render to the city clerk payment of the proper license fee therefor, with any applicable credit for the previously paid license fee.
(Ord. No. 82-1, 5-18-82)
Sec. 9-98. - Revocation of license.
If it comes to the attention of the mayor and council that an amusement machine has created a nuisance to the surrounding community or that the operator or proprietor has violated any provision of this article, the following procedure will be followed:
(1)
The city clerk shall issue a notice of objection which shall include the address of the location and the specific reasons why the machine or machines in question are alleged to be a nuisance or alleged to be in violation of any provision of this article. The city clerk will then notify the proprietor that a notice of objection has been filed and will set a date for a hearing before the mayor and council. Extensions and necessary investigations wilt be granted and conducted at the discretion of the city clerk.
(2)
At the time of the hearing, any resident, the operator, the proprietor and the city clerk or other witnesses may be heard. Thereafter, the mayor and council shall issue a notice of decision which will call for the dismissal of the objection, removal of the machines or such other remedy as they deem appropriate under the circumstances which are consistent with the purpose of this article.
(3)
The decision of the mayor and council shall be in writing and shall state the reasons for their decision and shall be based upon the merits of the case.
(4)
The decision of the mayor and council shall be binding upon the proprietor and operator of the machine or machines under consideration, subject to appeal to the proper court.
(Ord. No. 82-1, 5-18-82)
Sec. 9-99. - Operating regulations.
All businesses licensed under this article shall be subject to the following regulations:
(1)
All licensed machines shall at all times be kept and placed in plain view of and open and accessible to any person or persons who may frequent or be in any place of business where such machines are kept or used. However, no machine shall be placed within five (5) feet of any public entrance or exit and no proprietor may keep or maintain a machine between any public entrance or exit and any cash register.
(2)
Nothing in this article shall be construed to authorize, permit or license any gambling device of any nature whatsoever.
(3)
The chief of police shall inspect or cause to be inspected any place or building in which any such machine or machines are operated or set up for operation, and he shall inspect, investigate and test such machines as needed.
(4)
It shall be unlawful for any proprietor to open his business to the public unless any attendant is present. Each attendant shall be of sufficient mental and physical capacity so as to be able to provide aid to patrons if needed or desired. The attendant shall not be less than eighteen (18) years of age.
(5)
As used in this article, "loitering" shall mean remaining idle in essentially one (1) location and shall include the concepts of spending time idly, loafing or walking about aimlessly, and it shall also include the colloquial expression "hanging around." It shall be unlawful for any person licensed to operate an arcade room to permit loitering on or in the immediate vicinity of any machine or business premises regulated under this article in such a manner as to:
a.
Create or cause to be created a danger of a breach of the peace;
b.
Create or cause to be created any disturbance or annoyance to the comfort and repose of any person;
c.
Obstruct the free passage of pedestrians or vehicles;
d.
Obstruct, molest or interfere with any person who is lawfully in a public place.
(6)
All proprietors shall require shirts and shoes to be worn at all times by any person frequenting their premises.
