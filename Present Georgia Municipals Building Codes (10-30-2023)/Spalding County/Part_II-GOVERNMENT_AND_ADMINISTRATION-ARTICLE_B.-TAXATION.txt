Sec. 2-5011. - Ad valorem taxes—Due date.
Beginning with the tax year 1984, and continuing each tax year thereafter, ad valorem taxes levied on tangible property in Spalding County, other than motor vehicles, due the State of Georgia, the County of Spalding and the Griffin-Spalding County School System shall become due and payable on November 15. All taxes due as provided herein remaining unpaid on November 15 in each year shall bear interest at the rate specified in Section 48-240, Official Code of Georgia Annotated, from November 15. The tax commissioners shall collect the interest on the unpaid taxes and account for such interest in his or her final settlement.
(Res. of 2-21-84)
Sec. 2-5012. - Same—Construction.
Nothing contained in this article shall be construed to impose any liability for the payment of any ad valorem taxes upon any person for property which was not owned on the first day of January of the applicable tax year.
(Res. of 2-21-84)
Sec. 2-5013. - Same—Applicability; penalties and interest on unpaid taxes.
(a)
The provisions of this article shall apply to all persons required by law to make annual tax returns of all their property in this state to the revenue commissioner of the State of Georgia.
(b)
Taxes not paid in full by November 15 of each tax year shall be subject to such penalties and interest as are provided by law.
(Res. of 2-21-84)
Sec. 2-5014. - Same—Effective date.
This article shall become effective immediately and shall be applicable to the 1984 tax year and all subsequent tax years thereafter, unless and until repealed or amended by the governing authority of Spalding County or as otherwise provided by law.
(Res. of 2-21-84)
Secs. 2-5015, 2-5016. - Reserved.
Sec. 2-5017. - One percent sales tax.
(a)
A one (1) percent sales and use tax, as authorized by the constitutional amendment set out in 1982 Georgia Laws, p. 2677, is hereby levied, commencing January 1, 1983, and continuing until terminated as set out in said constitutional amendment, upon the retail purchase, retail sale, rental, storage, use and consumption of tangible personal property, and upon the services described and set forth in the Georgia Retailers' and Consumers' Sales and Use Tax Act, on sales, uses and services rendered within the geographical area of Spalding County, Georgia.
(b)
The revenue commissioner of the State of Georgia is hereby respectfully requested to implement and make the collection of such taxes, commencing on January 1, 1983, and continuing as authorized by said amendment and as provided by the pertinent laws of Georgia.
(c)
The clerk of this board is hereby authorized and directed to furnish a certified copy of this section to the revenue commissioner of the State of Georgia so as to officially notify him of the levy of said tax, beginning January 1, 1983, and continuing until terminated pursuant to the pertinent provisions of said constitutional amendment, and to notify him of the request of this board that the collection of such taxes be implemented and made, beginning January 1, 1983, under and by virtue of said amendment and the pertinent laws of Georgia.
(Res. of 6-30-82)
Editor's note— A resolution adopted June 30, 1982, did not specifically amend this Code; hence its inclusion as § 2-5017 was at the discretion of the editor. The constitutional amendment referred to in subsection (a) was continued in effect by § 1 of 1987 Georgia Laws, p. 3696.
Sec. 2-5018. - Gross premiums tax on insurance other than life insurance.
In accordance with the Official Code of Georgia Annotated Section 33-8-8.2, there is hereby levied for the year 1984, and for each subsequent year thereafter, an annual tax upon each insurer doing insurance business within the unincorporated area of Spalding County, in an amount equal to two and one-half (2.5) percent of the gross direct premiums received during the calendar year from all insurance, other than life insurance, as defined in O.C.G.A. Section 33-3-5(2), (3), (4) and (5). "Gross direct premiums" shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. Section 33-8-8.2(a).
(Res. of 10-4-83, § 1)
Editor's note— Inclusion of the provisions of § 1 of a resolution adopted Oct. 4, 1983, as § 2-5018 was at the discretion of the editor, the resolution being nonamendatory of the Code.
