CHAPTER 11. - SMOKING POLICY

Sec. 2-11001. - Definitions.
When used in this chapter, the following words and phrases shall have the following meanings:
(a)
County-controlled building means any building or structure located on real estate that the county owns, leases or has under its control.
(b)
County vehicle means any motorized automobile or truck to which the county holds title, ownership, leases, or borrows for county governmental purposes.
(Ord. No. 96-03, § 1, 3-5-96)
Sec. 2-11002. - General prohibition on smoking.
(a)
Smoking shall be prohibited in all county controlled buildings and all county vehicles.
(b)
Smoking shall be prohibited in all parks operated by the Spalding County Parks and Recreation Department and in the Park at Sixth.
(Ord. No. 2012-08, § 1, 7-16-12)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 2012-08, § 1, adopted 7-16-12, repealed former § 2-11002, and enacted a new section as set out herein. The former section pertained to similar subject matter and derived from Ord. No. 96-03, § 2, 3-5-96; Ord. No. 2008-04, § 1, 4-7-08.
Sec. 2-11003. - Public smoking prohibited.
Smoking by the visiting public shall be prohibited inside of any county-controlled building.
(Ord. No. 96-03, § 3, 3-5-96)
Sec. 2-11004. - No smoking signs.
"No Smoking" signs shall be clearly and prominently placed at all entrance doors to county-controlled buildings in order to clearly indicate that no public smoking is permitted.
(Ord. No. 96-03, § 3, 3-5-96)
Sec. 2-11005. - Employee smoking only in designated areas.
Employees shall be prohibited to smoke except in designated smoking areas as identified herein.
(Ord. No. 96-03, § 5, 3-5-96)
Sec. 2-11006. - Designated smoking areas.
The following areas have been approved as designated smoking areas on property belonging to Spalding County:
Outdoor areas which are at least twenty-five (25) feet from the entrance or exit of any county controlled building. However, no smoking is allowed in any parks operated by the Spalding County Parks and Recreation Department or in the Park at Sixth.
(Ord. No. 2012-08, § 1, 7-16-12)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 2012-08, § 1, adopted 7-16-12, repealed former § 2-11006, and enacted a new section as set out herein. The former section pertained to similar subject matter and derived from Ord. No. 2003-01, § 1, 2-3-03; Ord. No. 2008-04, § 1, 4-7-08.
Sec. 2-11007. - Appearance of designated smoking areas.
It shall be the responsibility of those using the designated smoking areas to maintain a clean and healthy area by disposing of ashes and butts into a receptacle.
(Ord. No. 96-03, § 7, 3-5-96)
Sec. 2-11008. - Smokeless tobacco products.
While smokeless tobacco is not prohibited by this policy, those individuals using smokeless tobacco products shall not be permitted to dispose of spittle into trash cans and should instead dispose of spittle into a toilet or other appropriate area.
(Ord. No. 96-03, § 8, 3-5-96)
Sec. 2-11009. - Penalties.
Any person who violates any provision of this chapter, or any amendment to this chapter, or who fails to perform any act required hereunder, or commits any prohibited act, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and punishable by a fine of not more than one thousand dollars ($1,000.00).
(Ord. No. 96-03, § 9, 3-5-96)
Sec. 2-11010. - Enforcement.
The Spalding County Sheriff is duly appointed as the enforcement officer for the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 96-03, § 10, 3-5-96)
