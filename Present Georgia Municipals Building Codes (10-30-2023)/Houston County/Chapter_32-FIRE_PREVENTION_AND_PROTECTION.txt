ARTICLE II. - OPEN BURNING

Sec. 32-21. - Issuance of county permit authorization.
(a)
[Issuance.]
(1)
Permits will be issued beginning at 8:00 a.m. Monday through Sunday.
(2)
Permits may be issued between 3:00 p.m. and 5:30 p.m. for the following day's burning based on the afternoon forecast. The commission reserves the right to recall the permit the next morning if the morning forecast predicts a bad day.
(3)
Unless otherwise specified, all permits will be good for a 24-hour period beginning at 8:00 a.m. on the permitted day. Weather conditions and/or local ordinances may restrict hours of burning.
(4)
County unit offices will be open to issue permits on weekdays and district dispatcher will issue permits on all holidays, Saturdays and Sundays.
(5)
Permits must be obtained from the county where the burn is to be conducted. Exception to this guideline will be given to landowners who live within a county but have to call long distance to get that county office. In this case, landowners may call the adjoining county who will radio the county where the burn is to take place for a permit number. This type of permitting is only allowed between adjoining counties where positioning of telephone exchanges causes this situation.
(b)
Counties that have a burning ordinance in place.
(1)
The state forestry commission county ranger or the other authorized employee of the forestry unit will call the county permitting authority each morning Monday through Friday at 8:30 a.m. to discuss the burning conditions and the fire danger for that day.
(2)
Those persons calling the county permitting authority for permission to burn forest land will be referred to the local Georgia Forestry Commission County Headquarters to obtain a permit number and the fire weather information.
(Ord. of 1-31-95)
Sec. 32-22. - Outdoor burns requiring permits.
(a)
The state forestry commission will require a permit number to be obtained for all types of legal burns in the county outside incorporated city limits with the exception of cultivated cropland.
(b)
Landowners who burn improved pasture or cultivated crop residue must notify the county forest ranger or authorized employee prior to initiating the burn.
(c)
U.S. government military installations will not be required to obtain a permit. They will be urged, however, to make their intent to the burn known to the appropriate state forestry commission county office.
(d)
The burning of forest land inside city limits will require a permit number. No other burns inside the city limits will require a permit number.
(Ord. of 1-31-95)
Sec. 32-23. - Burn ban.
State forestry commission permitting must be in accordance with the environmental protection division regulations for air quality control.
(1)
Under the rules of air quality control, the following burns are not prohibited by EPD in counties with population less than 65,000.
a.
Burning of leaves by the residents of properties on which they fall.
b.
Burning of vegetative matter for agricultural purposes for production of harvesting crops.
c.
Forest land for timber production and pasture land for pasture improvement may be burned.
d.
Small fires are allowed to keep warm for outside working. Burning manmade materials or treated wood that could cause dense smoke is prohibited.
e.
Operating devices using open flame such as tar kettles, blow torches, welding torches, and other flame-making equipment is allowed.
f.
Burning for the cooking of food for immediate consumption and recreational activities is allowed.
g.
Demolition and construction materials (not roofing or synthetic materials that cause malodorous or heavy smoke) unless prohibited locally.
h.
Weed abatement and tree limbs from storm damage.
i.
Disposal of packing previously containing explosives can be burned.
j.
Burning for firefighter training.
k.
Land clearing debris from right-of-way clearing and/or construction activities is allowed when:
1.
Prevailing winds are away from populated areas.
2.
Burning material is at least 1,000 feet from any dwelling located in a predominately residential area.
3.
Dirt is minimized in the burning material.
4.
No more than one pile 60 feet by 60 feet in a nine acre area, or equivalent volume area ratio is burning at one time.
5.
No tires, asphaltic materials, heavy oils or items containing natural or synthetic rubber or plastics or any other material that is not plant growth.
l.
Burning of wood slabs at portable sawmill sites.
(2)
In counties exceeding 65,000 population, all except (1)g. and k. of the above are allowed. The state forestry commission will permit burns for forestry practices in counties exceeding 65,000 population. As of the latest U.S. census, these counties are Cobb, Fulton, Clayton, DeKalb, Gwinnett, Hall, Clarke, Lowndes, Dougherty, Floyd, Houston, Whitfield, Richmond, Bibb, Muskogee, and Chatham.
(3)
No person shall cause, suffer, allow or permit open burning from May 1st to September 30th of each year except as follows: Carrying out recognized agricultural procedures necessary for production or harvesting of crops, the "prescribed burning" of any forest land by the owner's or the owner's designee, for recreational purposes or cooking food for immediate human consumption, fires set for purposes of training fire-fighting personnel when authorized by the appropriate governmental entity, operation of devices using open flames such as tar kettles, blow torches, welding torches, portable heaters and other flame-making equipment, disposal of all packaging materials previously containing explosives, in accordance with U.S. Department of Labor Safety Regulations.
(4)
State forestry regulations and permitting procedures in accordance with the environmental protection division regulations for air quality control and those regulations found in the Article II, section 32-23 subsections (1) and (2) will be in effect before and after the dates of the burn ban.
(Ord. of 1-31-95; Amd. of 5-1-05)
Sec. 32-24. - EPD statewide prohibited burning actions.
(a)
Burning of tires, synthetic material or other materials that give off malodorous, heavy smoke emissions.
(b)
Burning of garbage.
(c)
Open burning of any material which exceeds 40 opacity of smoke density.
(Ord. of 1-31-95)
Sec. 32-25. - [County requirements.]
County ordinances with more restrictive requirements for outdoor burning will take precedence over the state forestry commission's state permitting law.
(Ord. of 1-31-95)
Sec. 32-26. - Additional county regulations.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to burn any processed wood products in the county.
(b)
All fires that are permitted are to be burned between the hours of 10:00 a.m and 6:00 p.m.
(c)
After a permit has been obtained from the state forestry unit the applicant must call the county fire department and report the following:
(1)
The size and nature of the fire.
(2)
The address of proposed burn.
(3)
The permit number issued by the state forestry unit.
(d)
The county fire department has the authority to deny any and all permits issued by the state forestry unit or the state department of natural resources.
(e)
All fires shall be closely supervised at all times with a garden hose in place and operable.
(f)
If the county fire department responds to an illegal burn the following course of action shall be taken:
(1)
First offense of subject or owner of property—Written warning and extinguishment.
(2)
Second offense—A written ticket and extinguishment of fire. Fine of $100.00 to go to the county fire department.
(3)
Third offense—Same as subsection (f)(2) with a fine of $200.00.
(4)
All other offenses—Fines to range from $200.00 to $15,000.00 at the discretion of the judge.
(Ord. of 1-31-95)

ARTICLE III. - FIREWORKS

Sec. 32-30. - Fireworks prohibited.
(a)
"Fireworks" as used in this section, means any combustible or explosive composition or any substance or combination of substances or article prepared for the purpose of producing a visible or audible effect by combustion, explosion, deflagration, or detonation, including blank cartridges, balloons requiring fire underneath to propel them, firecrackers, torpedoes, skyrockets, Roman candles, bombs, sparklers, and other combustibles and explosives of like construction, as well as articles containing any explosive or flammable compound and tablets and other devices containing an explosive substance.
The term "fireworks" shall not include:
(1)
Model rockets and model rocket engines, designed, sold and used for the purpose of propelling recoverable aero models; toy pistol paper caps in which the explosive content does not average more than 0.25 grains of explosive mixture per paper cap; nor toy pistols, toy cannons, toy canes, toy guns or other devices using such paper caps; nor shall the term "fireworks" include ammunition consumed by weapons used for sporting and hunting purposes.
(2)
Wire or wood sparklers of 100 grams or less of mixture per item; other sparkling items which are non-explosive and non-aerial and contain 75 grams or less of chemical compound per tube or a total of 200 grams or less for multiple tubes; snake and glow worms; trick noise makers which include paper streamers, party poppers, string poppers, snappers, and drop pops each containing 0.25 grains or less of explosive mixture.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person, firm corporation or other entity to sell, offer to sell or use, explode, cause to be exploded, manufacture, or transport any fireworks within the limits of Houston County, except those individuals and companies recognized by the State of Georgia who are in the pyrotechnic business who perform fireworks displays during publicly recognized and sanctioned 4th of July and/or other publicly sanctioned events.
(Res. of 6-21-05; Res. of 6-20-06)
