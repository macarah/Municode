DIVISION 2. - HOSPITAL AUTHORITY[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— Creation of hospital authority in each county and municipality, O.C.G.A. § 31-7-72.

Sec. 2-81. - Establishment.
There is need for a hospital authority, as defined in an act approved March 27, 1941 (1941 Ga. Laws, page 241), to function, exist and be operated in and for the county, and there is hereby activated, created and organized such an authority, to be known as the hospital authority of the county, as provided in and by the act approved March 27, 1941.
(Code 1976, § 2-8001)
Sec. 2-82. - Membership; appointment of members; term of office.
(a)
The hospital authority shall consist of a board of seven members representing all areas of the county at the discretion of the board of commissioners.
(b)
Unless otherwise provided, the successors of the original members shall be appointed for terms of four years from the dates of expiration of their respective terms of office, except that any person appointed to fill a vacancy created during a term shall be appointed only for the unexpired term of the member that he succeeds. Any member shall be eligible for reappointment. All appointments shall be made by the board of commissioners.
(c)
The hospital authority board shall consist of the following members: three county commissioners; the mayors of each of the three cities in the county (City of Warner Robins, City of Perry, and City of Centerville); and one member from the board of trustees of either Houston Hospitals, Inc. or Houston Healthcare Systems, Inc. The terms of all elected officials appointed to the hospital authority shall be the same as their respective elected terms of political office.
(Code 1976, § 2-8002; Mo. of 6-2-92; Res. of 10-5-99(1); Amd. of 3-16-21)

DIVISION 5. - HOUSTON DRUG ACTION COUNCIL[7]

Footnotes:
--- (7) ---
State Law reference— Drug Abuse Treatment and Education Act, O.C.G.A. § 26-5-1 et seq.

Sec. 2-186. - Organization and operation generally.
The corporation known as the Houston Drug Action Council, Inc. is organized and operated as a nonprofit corporation, and no part of the property of the corporation or any of its earnings or donations shall inure to the benefit of any member of the board of directors, employee or other private individual. No person shall own any stock in this corporation, and the affairs of this corporation shall be handled by its board of directors or any executive committee appointed by the board of directors.
(Res. of 7-12-90, § 1.1)
Sec. 2-187. - Membership and organization of board of directors.
(a)
The board of directors of the corporation described in this division shall consist of 15 members. The members of the board of directors shall be appointed by the board of commissioners and, so far as practicable or possible, shall be representative of a geographical cross section of the county.
(b)
The board of directors itself shall elect from its members a chairman, secretary and treasurer. These officers of the board of directors shall serve for a one-year term of service or until they submit a letter of resignation.
(c)
Seven directors shall serve four-year terms, and six directors shall serve two-year terms. When the original two-year terms expire, the terms will be expanded to four years.
(d)
A quorum for doing business shall consist of four members of the board of directors, and only the members of the board shall have the authority to conduct the business of the corporation.
(e)
The board of directors may hire an executive director to run the day-to-day activities of the corporation, but the executive director shall serve at the pleasure of the board of directors, and all of his duties and responsibilities shall come directly from the board of directors.
(f)
All nominations to the board of directors shall come from the board of commissioners, the presently sitting board of directors or the public at large.
(g)
All resolutions adopted or business transacted by the board of directors shall require a majority vote of the quorum present to transact such business.
(Res. of 7-12-90, § 2.1; Min. of 9-7-93(1); Min. of 8-15-95)
Sec. 2-188. - Removal of members of board of directors.
No member of the board of directors of the corporation described in this division shall be removed from office except by a majority of the members of the total board of directors with the acquiescence of and approval of the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 7-12-90, § 2.4)
Sec. 2-189. - Meetings of board of directors.
(a)
The board of directors of the corporation described in this division shall meet on a regular basis, at least once a month. In addition, special meetings of the board of directors may be called at any time by the chairman. The executive director has no authority to call meetings, but must go through the chairman in dealing with the board of directors. At least five days' written notice of called meetings must be given to the members of the board of directors unless such notice is waived in writing. The executive director shall attend all meetings of the board of directors.
(b)
Any and all actions taken by the board of directors shall be at a regularly called or special called meeting.
(Res. of 7-12-90, §§ 2.2, 2.3)
Sec. 2-190. - Powers and duties of board of directors.
The responsibilities and authority of the board of directors of the corporation described in this division are as follows:
(1)
To set, determine and define the general policies, goals and objectives of Houston Drug Action Council, Inc. and the Community Crisis Center.
(2)
To hire or appoint an executive director and to establish the responsibilities and qualifications for such position.
(3)
To oversee, assess and critique the performance of the organization and the duties, responsibilities and performance of the executive director and any and all employees of the corporation, and to establish and implement the policies, goals and objectives of the corporation.
(Res. of 7-12-90, § 5.3)
Sec. 2-191. - Prerequisite for service on board of directors.
All members appointed to the board of directors of the corporation described in this division must, prior to taking the position, read the article that appeared in the May 13, 1990, Macon Telegraph and News relating to prior activities of the corporation so as to more fully understand their objectives and responsibilities.
(Res. of 7-12-90, § 5.9)
Sec. 2-192. - Election and duties of officers.
(a)
Election. Nominees for the positions of officers of the corporation described in this division shall be selected by a nominating committee consisting of at least three members of the board of directors, which are to be appointed by the chairman. The officers shall be a chairman, secretary and treasurer. The officers of the corporation shall be elected by a quorum of the board of directors. The board of directors will elect an officer to replace any officer who resigns or is dismissed.
(b)
Duties of chairman. The chairman shall preside at meetings, appoint standing and special committees, act as ex officio member of all committees, sign warrants, drafts and contracts, appear on behalf of the agency in actions brought by it and against it, exercise other temporary assignments and powers given by the board of directors, work with the executive director to develop meeting agendas, and act for the board of directors in emergencies if action must be taken before the full board can meet. Such emergency actions are subject to ratification at the next meeting of the board of directors.
(c)
Duties of secretary. The secretary shall keep minutes of all meetings of the board of directors and shall have charge of the minute book, the seal of the corporation, and bylaws and other corporate documents. The secretary shall perform other such duties and have other such powers as may from time to time be delegated to him by the chairman or by the board of directors.
(d)
Duties of treasurer. The treasurer shall be charged with the management of financial affairs of the corporation and shall have the power to recommend action concerning the corporation's affairs to the chairman and to the board of directors. The treasurer shall be further charged with keeping the board of directors informed about the financial condition of the corporation and auditing the financial affairs of the corporation on a regular basis.
(Res. of 7-12-90, §§ 3.1—3.4)
Sec. 2-193. - Powers and duties of executive director.
The executive director who is appointed or hired by the board of directors of the corporation described in this division shall have the responsibility for the general day-to-day management of the business of the corporation. The executive director shall be responsible for the administration of the corporation's business and the carrying out of the policies and responsibilities set by the board of directors. The executive director shall not have the authority to borrow money, pledge assets of the corporation, dispose of assets of the corporation, encumber assets of the corporation, enter into contractual obligations for or on behalf of the corporation, or set policies or objectives for the corporation without the prior approval of and written consent of the board of directors.
(Res. of 7-12-90, § 5.4)
Sec. 2-194. - Seal.
The seal of the corporation described in this division shall be in such form as the board of directors may from time to time determine. If it is inconvenient to use such seal at any time, the signature of the chairman, attested to by the secretary, followed by the word "Seal" enclosed in parentheses or scroll, shall be deemed the seal of the corporation. The seal shall be in the custody of the secretary of the corporation and affixed by him or by the chairman on all official documents of the corporation.
(Res. of 7-12-90, § 4.1)
Sec. 2-195. - Personnel.
(a)
Generally. The Houston Drug Action Council, Inc. (Community Crisis Center) is a nonprofit agency and shall develop its own personnel policy and procedures. All employees, including any fulltime director, shall serve at the pleasure of the board of directors, and the board of directors shall have the sole authority to hire and dismiss such employees.
(b)
Administration of payroll and fringe benefits; funding. The corporation shall be responsible for and handle the payroll and fringe benefits for the Community Crisis Center staff. The funding of any executive director's salary and other personnel shall be provided by funds and grants received by the corporation and administered by the board of directors.
(c)
Hiring of relatives of employees or members of board of directors. It is hereby stated that it is the policy of the corporation not to hire as employees or as the executive director anyone who is closely related by blood or marriage to any member of the board of directors or other employees of the corporation. For purposes of this section, the term "closely related" is defined as meaning the spouse, child, parent, daughter-in-law or son-in-law, mother-in-law or father-in-law, aunt, uncle, grandchild or grandparent of members of the board of directors or other employees.
(Res. of 7-12-90, §§ 5.1, 5.2, 5.8)
Sec. 2-196. - Ownership of work-related products.
Any and all work-related products, including grants, programs developed or other related work products, whether or not developed during the employment of any person, shall remain the sole and exclusive property of the corporation described in this division, and any employee, consultant, member of the board of directors or officer of the corporation shall have no ownership or pecuniary interest in such work product.
(Res. of 7-12-90, § 5.5)
Sec. 2-197. - Conflict of interest.
It is incumbent upon the board of directors of the corporation described in this division, the executive director as appointed by the board of directors, and the employees and staff of the corporation not to engage in any activities that would constitute a conflict of interest, whether such conflict of interest be a monetary one or a philosophical one with the goals, objectives and policies of the corporation.
(Res. of 7-12-90, § 5.6)
Sec. 2-198. - Use of property.
All property, real, intangible and personal, of the corporation described in this division, or the facilities of the corporation, shall be used solely for the purpose of carrying out the corporation's goals and objectives and shall not be used for any private gain or by a private individual.
(Res. of 7-12-90, § 5.7)
Secs. 2-199—2-215. - Reserved.

DIVISION 8. - HOUSING AUTHORITY

Sec. 2-251. - Creation of authority.
There is hereby declared to be a need for a housing authority to function in the county; and there exists unsanitary and unsafe inhabited dwelling accommodations in the county; and there exists a shortage of safe and sanitary dwellings accommodations in the county available to persons of low income at rentals they can afford. Therefore, pursuant to the housing authorities laws of the state there is hereby created the Housing Authority of Houston County.
(Min. of 7-20-93(1), § 5)
Sec. 2-252. - Appointment of members; terms of office.
The housing authority of Houston County shall have four members who shall be appointed by the board of commissioners. The terms of the members shall be for five years. Effective April 27, 1993, the current and successive members of the Warner Robins Housing Authority shall serve as members of the Houston County Housing Authority.
(Min. of 7-20-93(1), § 5)
DIVISION 9. - DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY

Sec. 2-253. - Need for authority.
There is hereby determined and declared to be a pressing, existing and future need for a development authority (as more fully described and defined in the development authorities law, Ga. Laws 1969, p. 137) to function in the county for the purpose of developing and promoting for the public good and general welfare, trade, commerce, industry and employment opportunities in the county thereby promoting the general welfare of the citizenry and, in the furtherance of that purpose, to finance projects for air and water pollution control facilities by industries.
(Min. of 7-20-93(1), § 6)
State Law reference— Development authorities, O.C.G.A. ch. 69-15.

There is hereby activated in the county the public body corporate and politic known as the Development Authority of Houston County which was created upon the adoption and approval of the development authorities law and particularly section 2 thereof.
(Min. of 7-20-93(1), § 6)
Sec. 2-255. - Membership.
There are hereby elected as members of the first board of directors of the development authority the following: Two persons for terms of two years; two persons for the terms of four years, and three persons for terms of six years. Thereafter, each member shall serve a six-year term of office.
