Sec. 2-155. - Personnel policies.
(a)
The county has adopted certain personnel policies and regulations, specifically including the county's drug-free work place policy, that are compiled in the county personnel manual, a copy of which is available in the office of the county clerk.
(b)
Such personnel policies apply to all covered employees in all departments under the administration of the board of commissioners and other participating independent appointing authorities. These policies also apply to the employees in those departments whose department head is a countywide elected official, with the proviso that the policies do not take away any decision-making authority of the constitutional or elected officers.
(c)
All employees of the elected officials, including department heads and members of county agencies and county commissions, serve at the will of the elected official.
(d)
The board of commissioners can modify and update the policies in the future, as needed.
(e)
For all covered employees and for all participating offices, agencies and commissions, the personnel policies will have the force and effect of law in the county.
(Code 1996, § 2-87; Ord. No. 006-95, 10-17-1995)
Sec. 2-156. - Defense of county officials.
The members of the board of commissioners do hereby adopt such policy as is permissible under O.C.G.A. § 45-9-21 and agree to defend all or specific civil, criminal or quasi-criminal actions brought or maintained against the board of commissioners of the county, its supervisors, administrators, employees, county doctors or other elected or appointed officials. However, the board of commissioners of the county will not furnish a defense to any individual charged with a criminal charge involving theft, embezzlement or other like crimes with respect to property or money in which the county has an interest. The county may dispense state, federal and local funds for such purposes, including, but not limited to, attorney fees, court costs, deposition costs, witness fees and compensation, and all like costs, expenses and fees.
(Code 1996, § 2-86; Res. of 11-14-1989)
State Law reference— Defense of actions against member of municipal and county governing bodies and other public bodies, O.C.G.A. § 45-9-21.
Sec. 2-157. - Workers' compensation for certain officials.
While performing their elected duties, the board of commissioners, sheriff, probate judge, clerk of court and tax commissioner shall be covered under the workers' compensation laws of the state as stated in O.C.G.A. § 34-9-1 et seq.
(Code 1996, § 2-88; Res. of 9-12-1995, § 1)
