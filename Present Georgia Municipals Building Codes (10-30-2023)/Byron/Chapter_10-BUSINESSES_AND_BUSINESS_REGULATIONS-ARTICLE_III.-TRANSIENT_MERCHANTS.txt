DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 10-91. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Transient merchant means any person, as principal or agent or both, who is not a regular retail or wholesale merchant with a permanent place of business in the city, but rather one who displays samples, model goods, wares, or merchandise in or upon any lot, building, room, or structure of any kind, whether fixed or mobile, for the purpose of securing orders for the retail sale of such items of like kind or quality for immediate or future delivery. The term "transient merchant" does not include any person who:
(1)
Sells harvested, but unprocessed, agricultural or forestry products;
(2)
Conducts business in any industry or association trade show;
(3)
Sells items at a rummage, garage, antique, or similar sale when the net proceeds of such sale are to be used for charitable or benevolent purposes;
(4)
Sells items from the home of a resident of the city, provided such business activity otherwise complies with this Code and other city ordinances; or
(5)
Sells items at an organized show or exhibition held within the city by a person who has first obtained from the city a permit for the purpose of conducting such show or exhibition.
(6)
Sells at the home of a resident of the city by prior invitation of the owner or resident.
(7)
Sells exclusively on behalf of a recognized school, charitable, or religious organization, when such sales are exclusively for the benefit of such school, charity, or religious organization.
(Ord. of 4-3-1984, § 2; Code 1987, § 12-30; Ord. No. 2021-9 , 7-12-2021)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 10-92. - Violations.
Any person who shall undertake to do business as a transient merchant without having complied with the terms of this article or who shall make any false statements in any application for a transient merchant's permit or license shall be guilty of a criminal offense and, upon conviction, shall be punished as provided in section 1-8.
(Ord. of 4-3-1984, § 6; Code 1987, § 12-35; Ord. No. 2021-9 , 7-12-2021)
Sec. 10-93. - Compliance by corporation; resident agent required.
It shall be unlawful for any transient merchant who is a corporation to engage in business in the city unless such transient merchant shall comply with all applicable provisions of state law relating to corporations, shall have and continuously maintain a registered agent as provided in section 10-94 and shall obtain a permit under this article.
(Ord. of 4-3-1984, § 3; Code 1987, § 12-31(a); Ord. No. 2021-9 , 7-12-2021)
Sec. 10-94. - Designation of resident agent; duties.
(a)
Each transient merchant, whether an individual, a partnership, a corporation or any other recognized legal entity, shall file the name and permanent address of such merchant's registered agent with the city clerk.
(b)
Such registered agent shall be a resident of the city and shall be the landowner upon whose property the transient merchant is intending to transact the business. If such landowner is not a resident of the city, such registered agent shall be any other bona fide resident of the city who is over the age of 18 and sui juris. Such agent may be served with any process, notice or demand required or permitted by law to be served upon the transient merchant, in the same manner provided by law for the service of a summons and complaint.
(c)
Such registered agent shall agree in writing to act as such agent, and a copy of the agreement shall be filed with the permit application.
(d)
The city clerk shall maintain an alphabetical record of all transient merchants and the names and addresses of their registered agents.
(e)
Nothing contained in this article shall limit or affect the right to serve any process, notice or demand in any other manner permitted by law.
(Ord. of 4-3-1984, § 5; Code 1987, § 12-34; Ord. No. 2021-9 , 7-12-2021)

DIVISION 2. - PERMIT

Sec. 10-116. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any transient merchant who is not a corporation to engage in business in the city unless the transient merchant shall have and continuously maintain a registered agent as provided in section 10-94 and obtain a permit under this division.
(Ord. of 4-3-1984, § 3; Code 1987, § 12-31(b); Ord. No. 2021-9 , 7-12-2021)
Sec. 10-117. - Application.
Any transient merchant desiring to transact business in the city shall file with the city clerk an application for a permit prior to the transaction of any such business. Such application shall include the following information:
(1)
The name and permanent address of the transient merchant;
(2)
The name and permanent address of the transient merchant's registered agent or office;
(3)
The type of business to be conducted;
(4)
The date and state of incorporation, when the transient merchant is a corporation; if a foreign corporation, the date the corporation became qualified to transact business in this state;
(5)
The number of days during which the sale is to be conducted;
(6)
Evidence of either ownership by the transient merchant or a written lease or written permission from the owner of the property to the transient merchant regarding the property upon which the sale is to be conducted; and
(7)
The location where the sale is to be conducted.
(Ord. of 4-3-1984, § 4(a); Code 1987, § 12-32; Ord. No. 2021-9 , 7-12-2021)
Sec. 10-118. - Fee; issuance; transferability; area of operation; expiration.
(a)
At the time the application for a transient merchant permit is filed, the applicant shall pay a permit fee of $100.00 per day for each day the sale is to be conducted, which shall be paid into the general city treasury. After the applicant has complied with all the provisions of this article, a transient business permit shall be issued.
(b)
Such permit is not transferable, is valid only within the territorial city limits, is valid only for the business stated on the application, and shall expire on the last day named therein on which the sale is to be conducted.
(Ord. of 4-3-1984, § 4(b); Code 1987, § 12-33; Ord. No. 2021-9 , 7-12-2021)
