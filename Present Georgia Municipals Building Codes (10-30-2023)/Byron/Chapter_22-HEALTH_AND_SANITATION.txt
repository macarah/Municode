ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 22-1. - Nuisances.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to create or maintain a nuisance in the city. The following are specifically declared to be nuisances:
(1)
Stagnant water;
(2)
Filthy sewage disposal facilities;
(3)
Privies;
(4)
Filthy stock pens;
(5)
Decayed vegetables;
(6)
Decayed fruits; or
(7)
Anything causing offensive odors or that works hurt or damage to others.
(b)
It shall be the duty of any person creating or maintaining a nuisance to remove and abate the nuisance within 24 hours after being notified to do so by the city. Upon failure to do so, the person shall be issued a citation to appear in municipal court. Each 24 hours such nuisance is allowed to remain after the expiration of 24 hours following such notice shall constitute a separate violation.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to place or deposit melon rinds, tainted meats, dead animals, or any offensive or excrementitious matter of any kind on any public street or sidewalk.
(Code 1935, § 94; Code 1987, § 10-1; Ord. No. 2021-16 , 1-10-2022)

ARTICLE II. - PRIVATE SEWAGE DISPOSAL

Sec. 22-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Human excreta means the bowel and kidney discharges of human beings.
Sanitary water closet means any flush toilet properly connected with a public sanitary sewer system or a septic tank.
Septic tank means an underground cavity with watertight walls into which flows the effluent of sanitary water closets. The term "septic tank" shall be construed to mean a structure built in strict accordance with the plans and specifications of the county health department. These plans and specifications are adopted by the city.
Septic tank absorption field or drainfield means an underground system of pipe, leading from the septic tank, consisting of open jointed concrete or clay pipe, so distributed that the effluent from a septic tank is oxidized and absorbed by the soil. This system shall be constructed in accordance with the plans and specifications of the county health department.
(Ord. of 6-13-1959, § 1; Code 1987, § 10-20; Ord. No. 2021-16 , 1-10-2022)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 22-32. - Facilities for human excreta disposal required.
It shall be unlawful to construct, to maintain or use or permit the use of any residence, place of business or other building or place where persons reside, congregate, or are employed, which is not provided with means for the disposal of human excreta, either by a sanitary water closet or a septic tank, provided that the means for disposal of human excreta shall be provided for each residence, place of business, or other building for the exclusive use of the occupancy therein.
(Code 1935, § 96; Ord. of 6-13-1959, § 2(a); Code 1987, § 10-21; Ord. No. 2021-16 , 1-10-2022)
Sec. 22-33. - Septic tank permit; approval.
Before septic tank and drainfield construction, the person in charge of such construction shall obtain a permit from the county health department. After construction is completed and before it is covered with earth, an inspection shall be obtained and approval granted by a representative of the county health department.
(Ord. of 6-13-1959, § 2(G); Code 1987, § 10-22; Ord. No. 2021-16 , 1-10-2022)
Sec. 22-34. - Connection to private system required pending availability of public sewer.
Where a public sanitary or combined sewer is not available under the provisions of section 40-53, the building sewer shall be connected to a private sewage disposal system complying with this article. When such a sewer is so available, it shall be unlawful for any person to construct or maintain any septic tank or other facility intended or used for the disposal of sewage.
(Code 1987, § 10-23; Ord. No. 2021-16 , 1-10-2022)
Sec. 22-35. - Privies prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to maintain a privy or dry closet in the city.
(Code 1935, § 95; Code 1987, § 10-24; Ord. No. 2021-16 , 1-10-2022)
