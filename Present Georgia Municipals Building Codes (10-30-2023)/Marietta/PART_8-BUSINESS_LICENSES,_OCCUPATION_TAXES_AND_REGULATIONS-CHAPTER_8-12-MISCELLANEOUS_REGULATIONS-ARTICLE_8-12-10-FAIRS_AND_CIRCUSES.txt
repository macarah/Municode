ARTICLE 8-12-10 - FAIRS AND CIRCUSES

8-12-10-010 - Compliance with article required.
It is unlawful for any person to hold, maintain, conduct or sponsor any amusement activity as defined herein without having first complied with the provisions of this article.
(Code 1978, § 8-3061; Ord. No. 5390, § 3, 12/20/94; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
8-12-10-020 - Amusement activity defined.
An amusement activity within the meaning of this article shall include any fair, concert, carnival, circus or side show and those activities, rides and games normally associated with those amusements wherein the general public is allowed to view or participate for a price. This specifically excludes nonprofit organizations as defined by federal law for income tax purposes.
(Code 1978, § 8-3062; Ord. No. 5390, § 3, 12/20/94; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
8-12-10-030 - Violation of this article designated grounds for revocation of occupation tax certificate.
Any violation of the provisions of this article shall be grounds for immediate revocation of any occupation tax certificate hereunder.
(Code 1978, § 8-3063; Ord. No. 5390, § 3, 12/20/94; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
8-12-10-040 - False statements in application for occupation tax certificate.
It is unlawful for any person to make a false statement in an application for an occupation tax certificate hereunder.
(Code 1978, § 8-3064; Ord. No. 5390, § 3, 12/20/94; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
8-12-10-050 - Amusement activity occupation tax certificate, issuance limited to place zoned for business uses.
No occupation tax certificate shall be issued for the purpose of conducting an amusement activity except where the amusement activity is to be located in an area of the city which has been, or may be, zoned for business purposes.
(Code 1978, § 8-3065; Ord. No. 5390, § 3, 12/20/94; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
8-12-10-060 - Application form—Contents.
All persons desiring an occupation tax certificate to conduct an amusement activity shall make written application to the city manager upon forms to be supplied by the city. The application shall state the name and address of the applicant; the place where the proposed activity is to be located; the nature and character of the activity to be carried on; if a partnership, the names of the partners; if a corporation, the names of the officers. The application shall further include the general plan and layout of how the area concerned is to be used to include parking facilities, proof that all rides operated are in a safe and proper working condition, proof that first-aid facilities are available to adequately meet the needs of those attending the amusement activity, proof of liability insurance, inspections and approval by the fire, building, plumbing and electrical inspectors and such other related information as may be required by the city manager.
(Code 1978, § 8-3066; Ord. No. 5390, § 3, 12/20/94; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
8-12-10-070 - Issuance.
Upon approval of the application by the business license manager, an occupation tax certificate shall be issued to the designated location upon the payment of the prescribed occupation tax.
(Code 1978, § 8-3067; Ord. No. 5390, § 3, 12/20/94; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96; Ord. No. 5762, 10/8/97, § 15)
8-12-10-080 - Applicant for occupation tax certificate to be of good moral character, effect of criminal record.
No person shall be granted an occupation tax certificate to conduct an amusement activity unless that person, or the officers and directors of any corporation shall be of good moral character. This shall mean that in no event shall any such occupation tax certificate be granted to any person or to any corporation where the person or the officers and directors of any corporation have been convicted or have pled guilty to any crime involving moral turpitude within a period of five years immediately prior to the date of application of the occupation tax certificate.
(Code 1978, § 8-3068; Ord. No. 5390, § 3, 12/20/94; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
8-12-10-090 - Additional standards for issuance, amusement activity occupation tax certificate.
A.
In the issuance of occupation tax certificates for the purposes of conducting an amusement activity, an application may be denied on the basis of any one of the following standards, in addition to standards previously stated:
1.
Where there is evidence that the activity to be conducted involves illegal conduct.
2.
Where there is evidence that the location, type of structure or activity would create unreasonable difficulty in police supervision.
3.
Where there is evidence that an occupation tax certificate for the location would be unreasonably detrimental to the property values in the area.
4.
Where there is evidence that an occupation tax certificate in that location would be detrimental to traffic conditions or that there is a lack of sufficient parking spaces for automobiles. The applicant shall have sufficient parking on the premises so as to provide parking for his or her customers and so as to prevent parking on the streets or adjoining property. Parking facilities shall further be of such a nature as to adequately provide for the drainage of surface waters during inclement weather.
5.
Where there is evidence that the conducting of the business creates an unreasonable disturbance, congregation of intoxicated persons, nuisance, or causes the police to answer an unreasonable number of complaints or conduct extra surveillance of the premises.
B.
In addition to the above and in determining whether or not any occupation tax certificate applied for shall be granted, the following shall be considered in the public interest and welfare:
1.
The applicant's reputation, character, mental and physical capacity to conduct this business.
2.
If the applicant is a previous holder of an occupation tax certificate to operate an amusement activity whether or not he or she has violated any law, regulation or ordinance relating to the business.
3.
If the applicant is a previous holder of an occupation tax certificate to operate an amusement activity, the manner in which he conducted the business thereunder as to the necessity for unusual police observation and inspection in order to prevent the violation of any law, regulation or ordinance relating to the business.
4.
The number of occupation tax certificates already granted for similar businesses in the trading area of the place for which the occupation tax certificate is sought.
5.
If rides, such as merry-go-rounds, Ferris wheels, and the like are to be permitted upon the premises for which the occupation tax certificate is sought, and applicant has previously permitted rides upon any premises controlled or supervised by him or her, the manner in which he or she controlled or supervised those rides to prevent any injury or violation of any law, regulation or ordinance.
(Code 1978, § 8-3069; Ord. No. 5390, § 3, 12/20/94; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
8-12-10-100 - Condition of premises.
All premises shall be kept clean and in proper sanitary condition, and when food is served in connection with the amusement activity, full compliance shall be maintained with the provisions and regulations governing the condition of premises used for the storage and sale of food for human consumption. It is unlawful to permit any disturbance of the peace or obscenity or any lewd and illegal entertainment, conduct or practices on the premises.
(Code 1978, § 8-3070; Ord. No. 5390, § 3, 12/20/94; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
8-12-10-110 - Duration of occupation tax certificate.
No occupation tax certificate issued under this article shall be valid beyond the expiration date as stated on the occupation tax certificate.
(Code 1978, § 8-3071; Ord. No. 5390, § 3, 12/20/94; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
