ARTICLE 8-12-24 - PRIVATE TRESPASS TOWING

8-12-24-010 - Occupation tax certificate requirements—Impoundment area—Insurance.
A.
The governing authority of the City of Marietta having towing and storage firms operating within its territorial boundaries may issue an occupation tax certificate to engage in private trespass towing, pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 44-1-13 to any firm meeting the qualifications imposed by the City of Marietta. The fee for the occupation tax certificate shall be set by resolution. The maximum reasonable costs of removal, relocation, and storage pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 44-1-13 shall be compensatory, as such term is used in the public utility ratemaking procedures, and shall be established annually by the City of Marietta having towing and storage firms operating within its territorial boundaries; provided, however, that no storage fees shall be charged for the first 24-hour period running from the time the vehicle is removed from the property and no such fees shall be allowed for the removal and storage of vehicles in violation of this Code section.
B.
Only towing and storage firms issued permits by the public service commission pursuant to O.C.G.A. Title 46, regulated by the city in which they operate, and having a secure impoundment lot shall be permitted to remove vehicles at the request of a person in possession of such private real property made pursuant to O.C.G.A.
1.
The towing company shall maintain a 24-hour tow truck service capable of responding to all calls for service.
2.
A company shall have the capability of moving all types of motor vehicles including minibikes, motorcycles, automobiles, trucks, loaded and unloaded tractor trailers and buses.
3.
A company shall maintain a separate, fenced-off impoundment area for storage of vehicles. The impounding area shall be enclosed by a fence not less than six feet high with a gate which can be securely locked. An attendant will be on duty 24 hours a day, seven days a week. The impoundment area shall be lighted during hours of darkness in such a manner as to prevent and detect attempts at theft or vandalism.
4.
A company shall maintain a liability insurance policy for bodily injury of $100,000.00 per person and $300,000.00 per accident and for property damage of $100,000.00 per accident. Each wrecker service shall provide the city with a copy of the insurance certificate evidencing coverages upon purchase or renewal of the annual city occupation tax certificate.
(Code 1978, § 8-3231; Ord. No. 5390, § 3, 12/20/94; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
