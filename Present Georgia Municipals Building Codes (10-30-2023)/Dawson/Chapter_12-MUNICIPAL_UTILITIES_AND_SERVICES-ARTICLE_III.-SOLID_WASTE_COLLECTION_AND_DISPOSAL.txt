DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 12-60. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the following terms shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them:
City shall mean the City of Dawson, Georgia.
Class I: Single-family dwelling, i.e., a building, apartment, or mobile home or any part thereof used exclusively as living quarters for any number of individuals living together as single housekeeping unit which produce not more than one hundred (100) gallons of garbage weekly.
Class II: Small businesses which do not produce more than one hundred (100) gallons of garbage per week.
Class III: All businesses or residential complexes which produce more than one hundred (100) gallons of garbage per week.
Garbage: The ordinary accumulation of refuse in evidence from the operation of a household or place of business. It shall not be construed to include brick, stone, lumber, wood, boxes or construction debris.
Superintendent of sanitation department: The superintendent of sanitation department of this city, his designee or such other officer appointed by the city to perform those duties.
Trash: Leaves, pine straw, pine cones, acorns, garden clippings, lawn trimmings, rubble and other similar loose material.
(Ord. of 10-14-93)
Sec. 12-61. - Accumulation of garbage unlawful and declared nuisance.
Any person allowing garbage to accumulate by failing to comply with the payment of the fees and charges fixed and assessed under this article shall be liable to the full provisions of the law applicable to nuisances. Upon the property owned by such person being declared a nuisance by reason of any unauthorized accumulation of garbage on any premises, such person shall be subject to being tried for creation of a nuisance and as a violation of the terms of this article and upon conviction thereof in the municipal court shall be punished as provided in section 1-8.
(Ord. of 10-14-93)
Cross reference— Storage of waste matter generally, § 10-25.
Sec. 12-62. - Charges and pickup frequency—Schedule.
(a)
There are hereby adopted the following schedules of reasonable rates, tolls, fees and charges to be made against owners, occupants, tenants and lessees of buildings and premises of service, removal and disposal of garbage within the city. The following fair and reasonable rates and charges to be made and the amount collected for each monthly (or other as provided) period for operation herein, shall be determined as follows:
(1)
Class I: Residential buildings and premises. The collection and removal of garbage and trash from houses, buildings and premises used for residential purposes shall be made not less frequently than once a week. The fair and reasonable rate for this residential service per month, or any portion thereof shall be thirteen dollars ($13.00) for each single-family dwelling.
(2)
Class II: The fair and reasonable rate per month, or any portion thereof, for individual owners, occupants, tenants and lessees of offices in business and commercial buildings and premises for small businesses shall be fifteen dollars and fifty cents ($15.50).
(3)
Class III: The fair and reasonable rate per month, or any portion thereof, for owners, occupants, tenants and lessees of business and commercial buildings and premises which produce more than one hundred (100) gallons of garbage per week shall be two dollars and eighty-five cents ($2.85) per cubic yard.
The city may, by resolution, increase or decrease the rates above set forth.
(b)
The city shall assign each citizen or business to one (1) of the three (3) classes defined above, as provided by section 12-66.
(c)
The collection and removal of garbage and trash from business and commercial buildings and premises shall be as often as needed, commensurate with the needs presented by the volume of garbage, produced by such business and commercial buildings and premises.
(Ord. of 10-14-93)
Sec. 12-63. - Same—Liability therefor.
Volumes of garbage and trash produced and size and type of containers placed on premises shall be fixed by the city and the charges hereinabove described shall be imposed whether or not such services offered by the city are accepted or refused and such person or business shall be obligated to pay the fees and charges hereinabove set forth. The failure or refusal of any person liable for the payment of the fees and charges hereby fixed and imposed to accept the solid waste collection service offered by the city shall not relieve such person of the obligation to pay such fees and charges.
(Ord. of 10-14-93)
Sec. 12-64. - Same—Billing where no water service.
Any owners, occupants, tenants and lessees of residential buildings and premises or business or commercial buildings and premises who do not receive a monthly water bill shall be billed for the charges fixed and assessed by this article. All delinquent charges shall be collected as hereinafter set forth.
(Ord. of 10-14-93)
Sec. 12-65. - Collection steps in case of delinquency.
Should any person liable for payment of the fees and charges herein fixed and imposed, fail to pay the same within ten (10) days after having been billed therefor, the city shall be authorized to take any or all of the following steps to promote the collection of such bill:
(1)
Discontinue garbage collection service;
(2)
Discontinue all utility services provided to the delinquent person;
(3)
Authorize the city clerk to issue an execution therefor against such person in the same manner provided in cases of default in the payment of city utility bills.
(4)
Issue a citation, returnable to the city court, for violation of this article and punishable as provided in the section 5-7 of the city charter.
(5)
Pursuant to the O.C.G.A. § 12-8-39.3, enforce the collection of such fees and charges in the same manner as authorized by law for the enforcement of the collection and payment of state taxes, fees, or assessments
(Ord. of 10-14-93; Ord. No. 2011-03, § 1, 7-14-11)
Sec. 12-66. - Determination of classifications; appeals.
The superintendent of the sanitation department, or such other officer designated by council, shall determine and assign the proper classification, in writing, for each building or premises receiving sanitary collection, removal and disposal of garbage within the city in accordance with the schedule of charges in section 12-62. Any owner, occupant, tenant or lessee disagreeing with the designated charge assessed against them shall file such person's objection, in writing, with the council. The matter shall be heard at the next regular council meeting after the date of the appeal. The determination as to classification thus made by the council shall be final.
(Ord. of 10-14-93)
Sec. 12-67. - Exclusive right of collection of garbage; ownership of materials collected or dumped.
The council shall have the exclusive right of the collection, removal and disposal of garbage, provided however, the city may contract for such services with private individuals or companies, and no other private individual, business, agent or other operation shall be permitted to haul garbage produced by owners, occupants, tenants and lessees of residential buildings and premises or business and commercial buildings and premises without first having obtained permission from the council therefor. Any person desiring such permission shall file such application, giving the details of operation and any charges to be made all as may be required by the council. Once a permit is issued by the council for such operations the same shall be issued in the name of the applicant and such permit shall not be transferable. The council shall have full authority to make any reasonable regulations relating to the hauling of garbage over city streets and thoroughfares by outside collectors.
(Ord. of 10-14-93)
Sec. 12-68. - Refuse acceptable for collection.
The following refuse shall be acceptable for collection by the city:
(1)
Garbage: Putrescible animal and vegetable wastes resulting from the handling, preparation, cooking and/or consumption of foods;
(2)
Ashes: The residue from the burning of food, coal, coke or other combustible materials;
(3)
Rubbish: Nonputrescible solid wastes, excluding ashes, consisting of paper, cardboard, tin cans, yard clippings, food, glass, bedding, crockery, metal and similar materials;
(4)
Cardboard boxes: All cardboard boxes must be flattened and bundled before these items will be acceptable for collection. Under no circumstances shall cardboard boxes be placed in the city containers until the cardboard boxes have been flattened as outlined above.
(5)
Trash: As defined herein (section 12-60).
(Ord. of 10-14-93)
Sec. 12-69. - Refuse not acceptable for collection.
The following refuse shall not be acceptable for collection by the city:
(1)
Dangerous materials: Dangerous materials or substances, such as poisons, acids, caustics, infected materials and explosives;
(2)
Construction residues: Unusual quantities of materials resulting from the repair, excavation or construction of buildings or structures, such as earth, plaster, mortar and roofing material;
(3)
Unprepared materials: Materials which have not been prepared for collection in accordance with these regulations;
(4)
Industrial waste: The solid wastes resulting from industrial processes;
(5)
Water-filled containers: Containers that have not been covered and have water in them as a result.
(Ord. of 10-14-93)
Sec. 12-70. - Tree service permit; disposal of cuttings.
(a)
All holders of a tree service license or any person engaging in the cutting of trees for hire or compensation shall first be required to obtain a permit from the building official giving the location and date of the cutting proposed to be done.
(b)
This section shall apply to all debris which exceeds the definition of trash acceptable for collection by the city.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to trim or remove trees within the city unless the by product from those serviens is immediately removed from the premises and placed or deposited by the person trimming or removing said trees in a disposal site.
(Ord. of 10-14-93)
Sec. 12-71. - Hauling uncovered garbage.
It shall be unlawful for any person to haul garbage in uncovered vehicles upon the public streets of the city, and all garbage being hauled upon the city streets shall be adequately covered so to prevent the same from falling or being strewn upon the public streets of the city.
(Ord. of 10-14-93)
Cross reference— Littering, § 10-21.
Secs. 12-72—12-76. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - STORAGE AND PLACEMENT OF REFUSE; CONTAINERS

Sec. 12-77. - Nonconformance.
Sanitation crews will not be authorized to pick up garbage or trash unless properly prepared as required by this article. Trash allowed to accumulate near a public thoroughfare by reason of not conforming to the requirements of this article will subject the owner or occupant of the premises to a citation by the police for violation of this article.
(Ord. of 10-14-93)
Sec. 12-78. - Responsibility of residential households.
The owner or occupant of residential households are responsible for the proper storage of garbage and for the preparation and placement of garbage and trash containers near a public thoroughfare for pickup by garbage and trash trucks.
(Ord. of 10-14-93)
Sec. 12-79. - Placement in city receptacles.
It shall be unlawful for any person to throw, deposit, or place or cause to be thrown, deposited, or placed, upon any street or other public place or upon any private premises, any garbage as herein defined, unless such garbage is placed in a safe and sanitary receptacle, to be furnished at the expense of the city, which receptacle is more particularly hereinafter defined.
(Ord. of 10-14-93)
Cross reference— Containers to be collapsed before placing in public receptacle, § 10-24.
Sec. 12-80. - Wheeled garbage containers.
(a)
The owner, occupant, tenant or lessee occupying any building, house or structure within the city occupied as a residence or business using a wheeled garbage container shall have such container at the curb not later than 7:00 a.m. on the day designated by the city's sanitation superintendent with the exception of persons disabled because of age or physical condition living on premises where there is no resident or occupant physically capable of placing the container at the location established by the sanitation superintendent.
(b)
The owner, occupant, tenant or lessee occupying any building, house or structure within the city occupied as a residence or business using a wheeled garbage container shall return such container to the side or rear of the building no later than 7:00 p.m. on the regular garbage pickup day. Failure to comply with this provision shall result in the issuance of a citation by authorized city personnel. The first citation shall result in a written warning. Persons receiving second and subsequent citations shall be tried by municipal court.
(c)
Wheeled garbage containers furnished by the city shall not be removed from the premises to which they are delivered, and it shall be the duty of the occupant of the premises, in the event the dwelling is to be vacated by the occupant, to notify the sanitation department at least three (3) days prior to the vacancy.
(d)
In the event the wheeled garbage container furnished by the city is damaged, stolen or destroyed, through the negligence of the person to whom the container was assigned, that person shall reimburse the city for the cost of a replacement container.
(e)
The wheeled garbage container furnished by the city shall be subject to inspection by the city's sanitary superintendent.
(f)
The wheeled garbage containers furnished by the city are city property and it shall be unlawful for any person to destroy or deface such containers. Any person found guilty of violating this provision shall be fined within the discretion of the municipal court judge and within limits of section 1-8.
(Ord. of 10-14-93)
Sec. 12-81. - Furnishing of receptacles.
The city will furnish, at its expense, a container of approximately ninety (90) gallons, which container shall be furnished by the city to each household for the collection and storage of garbage or trash. Each household is responsible for the care, preservation, and security of such container.
(Ord. of 10-14-93)
Sec. 12-82. - Replacement of household containers.
(a)
Should garbage or trash containers become unserviceable through no neglect of the owners or occupants of the premises, then the same shall be replaced or repaired by the city.
(b)
Should such trash container be damaged through negligence, or not be present on the premises because of loss or theft, then in such event the household shall be responsible for replacing such container at the expense of the occupants of such property, which container shall be of a kind and type specified by the city.
(Ord. of 10-14-93)
Sec. 12-83. - City container use restrictions.
It shall be unlawful for any person to:
(1)
Place in any city owned garbage and/or trash containers and/or receptacles any boxes that are not first folded and/or flat;
(2)
To start and/or maintain any fire in any city owned garbage and/or trash containers and/or receptacles.
(Ord. of 10-14-93)
Sec. 12-84. - Building debris.
Any building debris such as plaster, roofing, concrete, brick bats and other such rubbish resulting from repairs, remodeling or construction of any building or private property will not be removed by the city. The owner of the premises or the contractor, professional landscaper, builder or the person doing the repairs, remodeling or construction shall haul away and dispose of all such debris, including stumps, trees and limbs, at such person's expense.
(Ord. of 10-14-93)
Sec. 12-85. - Bulky or heavy cast-offs.
Discarded furniture, fixtures, small appliances and similar bulky items may be placed individually near the public thoroughfare where other trash is normally placed for pickup. No special preparation is required. Extremely large and heavy cast-offs such as refrigerators, washing machines, or stoves that are too heavy for hand-loading will be picked up free of charge provided prior arrangements are made with the city's collector or city sanitation superintendent.
(Ord. of 10-14-93)
Sec. 12-86. - Collection of leaves, grass clippings, etc., generally.
Residential refuse such as leaves, grass clippings, straw, small yard and garden clippings and other similar materials may be placed near the public thoroughfare where other trash is normally placed for pick-up.
(Ord. of 10-14-93)
Sec. 12-87. - Yard trimmings disposal regulations.
(a)
Definitions.
Commercial solid waste means all types of solid waste generated by stores, offices, restaurants, warehouses and other non-manufacturing activities, excluding residential and industrial wastes.
Composting means the controlled biological decomposition of organic matter into a stable odor-free humus.
Leachate collection system means a system at a landfill for collection of the leachate which may percolate through the waste and into the soils surrounding the landfill.
Municipal solid waste means any solid waste derived from households, including garbage, trash and sanitary waste in septic tanks and includes solid waste from single-family and multifamily residences, hotels and motels, bunkhouses, campgrounds, picnic grounds, and day use recreation areas. The term includes yard trimmings and commercial solid waste but does not include solid waste from mining, agricultural or silvicultural operations or industrial processes or operations.
Municipal solid waste disposal facility means any facility or location where the final deposition of any amount of municipal solid waste occurs, whether or not mixed with or including commercial or industrial solid waste, including but not limited to, municipal solid waste landfills.
Municipal solid waste landfill means a disposal facility where any amount of municipal solid waste, whether or not mixed with or including commercial waste, industrial waste, nonhazardous sludges or small quantity generator hazardous waste, is disposed of by means of placing an approved cover thereon.
Yard trimmings means leaves, brush, grass clippings, shrub and tree prunings, discarded Christmas trees, nursery and greenhouse vegetative residuals, and vegetative matter resulting from landscaping development and maintenance, other than mining, agricultural and silvicultural operations.
(b)
Placement and disposal of yard trimmings; prohibitions.
(1)
It shall be unlawful to place or mix yard trimmings with municipal solid waste within the city.
(2)
Yard trimmings shall not be disposed at any municipal solid waste disposal facility having a liner and leachate collection system or requiring vertical expansion located within the city.
(c)
Sorting, storing, composting and collecting yard trimmings. Yard trimmings shall be sorted and stored in the following manner:
(1)
Small tree and shrubbery prunings must be bound together by twine, wire, rope and similar means in separate bundles of such size and weight as can be picked up intact by one adult person and loaded on the trash truck.
(2)
Large prunings such as complete small trees, or large limbs must be cut by the owner or occupant of the premises in not more than three-foot lengths so as to permit hand loading by not more than two (2) adult persons. These large prunings need not be bound together.
(3)
Extremely large and heavy prunings that cannot be hand-loaded and require the use of heavy loading machinery will be removed by the city only by prior arrangement on a work order basis at the cost plus ten (10) percent, to be paid by the owner or occupant of the premises.
(d)
Penalties. Any person violating the provisions of this section shall be punished by a fine not to exceed one hundred dollars ($100.00), by imprisonment for a term not to exceed thirty (30) days or a combination of such fine and imprisonment. Each day this section is violated shall constitute a new and separate offense.
(Ord. of 10-10-96)
