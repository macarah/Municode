ARTICLE VII. - USED CAR DEALERS

Sec. 13-140. - License required.
Any used car dealer, as such terms are defined in O.C.G.A. § 43-47-2, who does business within this municipality shall obtain a license from the city clerk in the manner specified in this chapter.
(Code 1974, § 32-205(1))
Sec. 13-141. - Occupation tax.
At the time of obtaining the license required by section 13-140 above, the applicant for license shall pay the occupation tax required by section 13-4 and the administrative fee required by section 13-2.
(Ord. No. 04-15, § 7, 12-9-04)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 04-15, § 7, adopted Dec. 9, 2004, repealed the former § 13-141 and enacted a new § 13-141 as set out herein. The former § 13-141 pertained to license fee and derived from Code 1974, § 32-205(2).
Sec. 13-142. - Review of application.
No action on any application for a license under this article shall be taken by the city council until the chief of police has reviewed such application and forwarded a recommendation thereon to the city clerk in the manner specified in this chapter.
(Code 1974, § 32-205(3))
Sec. 13-143. - Restriction on issuance of license.
No license under this article shall be issued to any applicant who has not been licensed by the state board of registration of used car dealers.
(Code 1974, § 32-205(4))
State Law reference— Registration and licensing, O.C.G.A. § 43-47-7; local regulation and licensing, O.C.G.A. § 43-47-14.
Sec. 13-144. - Records.
Each used car or used motor vehicle dealer licensed hereunder shall keep a record of all motor vehicles offered for sale, exchange or disposal to the public, which record shall show the make of each such motor vehicle, the year of its manufacture, its serial number and the engine number, and which record shall always be kept available for the chief of police or any police officer of the city and open to inspection at any time. It shall be the further duty of every such person to immediately report to the chief of police the presence in such place of business of any motor vehicle on which the serial or engine number has been defaced or altered.
(Code 1974, § 32-205(5))
Sec. 13-145. - Inspections.
It shall be the duty of the chief of police to make inspections from time to time for the purpose of seeing that the records required therein are being kept.
(Code 1974, § 32-205(6))
Sec. 13-146. - Exceptions.
Nothing in this article shall be deemed to apply to any individual making an isolated sale of such person's own vehicle.
(Code 1974, § 32-205(7))
Secs. 13-147—13-149. - Reserved.
