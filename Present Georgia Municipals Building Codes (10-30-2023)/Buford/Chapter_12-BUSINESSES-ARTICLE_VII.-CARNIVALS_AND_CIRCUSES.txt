ARTICLE VII. - CARNIVALS AND CIRCUSES

Sec. 12-243. - Tax license required.
In addition to the permit required by this article, any person coming under the provisions of this article shall obtain a business occupation tax license for any activity coming under the provisions of article VII, chapter 36.
(Code 2000, § 10-211)
Sec. 12-244. - Permit required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to conduct or operate in the city any carnival, circus, or other traveling show without first applying to the city commission for a permit therefor, which permit may be issued or refused as hereinafter provided.
(Code 2000, § 10-212; Ord. No. 740, § 1)
Sec. 12-245. - Application for permit.
Any person applying for a permit required by this article shall, either in writing or in person, furnish to the city commission the following information:
(1)
Name of carnival, circus, or traveling show, together with the names and home addresses of the owners and managers thereof;
(2)
Name of any local organization sponsoring the appearance in the city;
(3)
The number of and a detailed description of each show, ride, game, concession, and other entertainment or attraction to be offered, and the price of admission thereto;
(4)
Location of the site to be occupied or used in the city, and the length of time such carnival, circus, or traveling show is to be operated in the city;
(5)
Proposed general admission charges;
(6)
A detailed financial statement showing the financial worth and liabilities of each owner or partner. If the carnival, circus, or traveling show is owned by individuals or a partnership, a financial statement of the owner or partners will suffice; however, if the carnival, circus, or traveling show is owned by a corporation, then a financial statement of the corporation will be required;
(7)
A description of the type of buildings, tents, or other shelter to be provided for use by the public, its resistance to fire, and what fire protection is furnished for the protection of the public; and
(8)
A sworn statement from the owner or manager of such carnival, circus, or traveling show that no owner, member, employee, or operator of any show, ride, game, concession, or other entertainment or attraction has ever been convicted of a crime involving moral turpitude.
(Code 2000, § 10-213; Ord. No. 740, § 2)
Sec. 12-246. - Permit fee.
The fee for any permit to be issued under this article shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges and on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 2000, § 10-214; Ord. No. 740, § 5)
Sec. 12-247. - Issuance.
Based upon the application for the permit, the city commission may grant or refuse to grant a permit for such carnival, circus, or traveling show to be operated or conducted in the city, and may require such conditions and terms as it deems necessary to protect the health, morals, and welfare of the public, before issuing any permit.
(Code 2000, § 10-215; Ord. No. 740, § 3)
Sec. 12-248. - Financial assurance.
As a condition precedent to the issuance of a permit under this article, the city commission may require the applicant to file with the city a public liability insurance policy in an amount to be fixed by the city commission, conditioned to pay any damages which any member of the public may suffer from the negligence, willful misconduct or dishonesty of the owner, or any employee or operator of any show, ride, game, concession, or other entertainment or attraction thereof.
(Code 2000, § 10-216; Ord. No. 740, § 3)
Sec. 12-249. - Resident agent for service of process.
The city commission may also require the owner or manager of any carnival, circus, or traveling show to file a written power of attorney, appointing a resident of the county, an attorney in fact for the owner, manager, employees and operators thereof, to accept service of any suit filed against them by any member of the public for any injury or damage suffered.
(Code 2000, § 10-217; Ord. No. 740, § 4)
Secs. 12-250—12-275. - Reserved.
