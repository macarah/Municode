ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 28-1. - Letters of authority for issuance of citations.
(a)
The county sheriff and each post certified officer of the sheriff's department has been and continues to be authorized to enforce the county ordinances as an inherent function of their positions. In addition thereto, and not by way of limitation thereof, the board of commissioners may from time to time issue letters of appointment authorizing county employees to issue citations for violations of county ordinances whose subject matter falls within the employees' functional job area; provided, however, that the job position of the authorized employees must be included in one of the following job classifications:
(1)
Building inspector;
(2)
Code enforcement officer; or
(3)
Environment health technician.
(b)
Each letter of appointment must be spread on the minutes of the board of commissioners meetings before becoming effective.
(c)
Authority exercised pursuant to a letter of authority issued hereunder shall be exercised under the direct supervision of the county department head to whom the authorized employee reports and the county manager.
(d)
At the call of a case brought in an appropriate court or before an appropriate administrative body upon a citation issued pursuant to a letter of authority granted hereunder, the issuing employee shall present the evidence of the violation. The county attorney may also appear at any such proceeding if directed by the county manager.
(Res. No. 2003-12, 5-15-2003)
