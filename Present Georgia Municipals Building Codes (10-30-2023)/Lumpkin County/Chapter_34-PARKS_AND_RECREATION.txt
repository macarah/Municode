ARTICLE II. - COMMUNITY HOUSE

Sec. 34-48. - Transfer of management responsibility.
Since 1941, the county's community house property has been managed by the community house board of control; however, the board of commissioners has determined that it is necessary and desirable to transfer control of that property to the county park and recreation department and to transfer all operating funds of the community house board of control to the county finance department, effective January 22, 2009.
(Res. No. 2009-05, 1-22-2009)
Sec. 34-49. - Continuation of the board of control.
The member of the community house board of control, in recognition of the faithful stewardship of the community house, shall continue to serve as members of an honorary board of control. Upon the resignation or death of the last member serving on January 22, 2009, the honorary board of control shall be automatically dissolved.
(Res. No. 2009-05, 1-22-2009)
Sec. 34-50. - Management of property.
The county parks and recreation department shall exercise any necessary management, maintenance, and operational control of the community house facility, including, but not limited to, determining the appropriate use of the facility.
(Res. No. 2009-05, 1-22-2009)

ARTICLE III. - CANOE LAUNCH PARK

Sec. 34-70. - Lease of park property.
The Canoe Launch Park ("park") is owned by the United States and leased to the county for public park and recreational purposes under the terms of a certain lease between the county and the U.S. Army Corp of Engineers ("the lease"), a copy of which lease is available in the office of the county clerk.
(Res. No. 2009-64, § 1, 10-14-2009)
Sec. 34-71. - Commercial use of park restricted.
(a)
Generally. Any commercial use of the park other than by sublease or concession agreement is specifically prohibited. All subleases and agreements are subject to the provisions of the lease and must be approved by the district engineer.
(b)
Concessions. Concession proposals shall require review and approval by the county board after consideration and written response from such parties as the board deems appropriate.
(c)
Watercraft activities. All use of the park facilities by commercial entities for watercraft activities are by sublease, which shall be on an annual basis. A copy of the model lease is available from the county clerk.
(Res. No. 2009-64, § 2, 10-14-2009)
Sec. 34-72. - Concrete watercraft launch facility.
(a)
One primary use of the park is for putting canoes, boats, and other watercraft into the water and for taking such watercraft out of the water. Such watercraft for purposes of this article shall be referred to collectively as "canoes." To facilitate this activity, a concrete facility has been erected.
(b)
To enhance safety, no fishing and no placement of lawn chairs, ice chests, bait buckets or other objects shall be permitted at, or within 50 feet of, the facility except for those items associated with the placement of a canoe into the water or the removal of a canoe from the water.
(Res. No. 2009-64, § 3, 10-14-2009)
Sec. 34-73. - Fishing and general park activities permitted; restrictions.
Fishing and general park activities shall be permitted in other areas of the park, except as may otherwise be provided in this article or by signs posted at the park.
(Res. No. 2009-64, § 4, 10-14-2009)
Sec. 34-74. - Vehicle use.
Off-road vehicles are not allowed in the park. Motorized vehicles permitted by state law to travel on state highways are permitted in the parking areas only, and are not permitted in the dirt or grass areas outside the parking area.
(Res. No. 2009-64, §§ 5, 6, 10-14-2009)
Sec. 34-75. - Hours.
No use of the park is permitted when the gates providing access to State Highway 60 are locked. If signs are posted in the park limiting hours of operation for various activities, participating in such activities in violation of the posted signs is not permitted.
(Res. No. 2009-64, § 7, 10-14-2009)
Sec. 34-76. - Penalties.
(a)
Civil. Any person or entity who operates any commercial activity without a written and approved concession agreement or sublease, or who operates with a written and approved concession agreement or sublease, but acting in violation of any term or condition of said concession agreement or sublease, may be subject to a civil penalty of $500.00 for each occurrence. Each day of operation not in violation of this article shall constitute a separate violation.
(b)
Criminal. Any person or entity that violates any provisions of this article may be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 and incarceration for a term not to exceed six months for each occurrence. The criminal penalties set forth in this subsection shall be in addition to any civil penalties imposed pursuant to this section.
(Res. No. 2009-64, § 8, 10-14-2009)
