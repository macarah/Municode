ARTICLE VII. - FENCES AND WALLS

Sec. 20-400. - Definitions.
[The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:]
Drainage way means a conduit, ditch, or the like for draining water from an area.
Drainage swale means a natural recessed area in the earth's surface that provides storm water treatment by allowing portions of the runoff water to infiltrate into the ground versus completely running off to an outfall.
Drainage ditch means a long narrow excavation in the earth for carrying off excessive water.
Depression area means a recessed or lowered space that temporarily stores water runoff along impervious surfaces such as parking lots, streets, driveways, etc.
(Ord. No. 12-05, § 1, 8-20-2012)
Sec. 20-401. - Fences and walls.
No fence or wall may be installed nor permitted to be installed in such a location as to obstruct or impede the flow of water in any drainage way, drainage swale, drainage ditch or depression area.
(Ord. No. 12-05, § 1, 8-20-2012)
