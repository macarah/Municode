ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 17-1. - Reference point elevations.
The base or datum plane to which all levels taken by the city shall be referred shall be that plane at or near sea level from which the following local bench marks have elevations as follows: Elevation 1,074.95 feet at east end of car stop at starting point of Clarendon Avenue rail driven between car tracks. Elevation 1,088.33 feet nail in oak tree 50 feet east of Dudley Street (now Windsor Terrace) south corner of Covington Road. Elevation 1,011.00 feet pine tree south side of bathing pool. Elevation 1,099.49 feet first floor level of Avondale Grammar School. Elevation 1,002.95 feet nail in pine tree southeast of dam.
(Code 1973, § 2-7144)
Editor's note— This section is included because of its historic interest.
Sec. 17-2. - Street grades.
The word "grade" as applied to any street shall mean the elevation above sea level, as indicated, above the sidewalk, at the building line or curb line as indicated, and grades shall be fixed by resolution of the governing body on presentation of a profile which shall be known as the official profile. Official profiles shall bear endorsements of the mayor and city clerk showing the facts and dates of their approval by the governing body. No grade once established shall be changed except upon resolution by the governing body.
(Code 1973, § 2-7145)
Sec. 17-3. - Curb grades.
On streets or along hillsides where it is necessary for drainage economy to have the curb grades higher or lower than the center line of the street, the same shall be shown on the profile and the curb grade as well as the street grade shall be established.
(Code 1973, § 2-7146)
Sec. 17-4. - Street numbers.
No person shall apply a street number to or maintain on such premises without ascertaining the correct number from the city clerk and using the number so designated.
(Code 1973, § 2-7199)
Sec. 17-5. - Construction and repair of sidewalks, curbs, gutters and streets—Sidewalk construction on petition of property owner.
(a)
All applications for sidewalks shall be made by petition to the governing body, signed by the property owners abutting, and the contractor shall agree therein to lay such sidewalks in accordance with the specifications prescribed by the governing body.
(b)
On the granting of the petition, or on the passage of any resolution for the construction of any sidewalk, curb or gutter, the city clerk shall notify the city manager in writing, and the city manager shall have the necessary grading done as soon as possible thereafter, provided there is available cash on hand in the street fund to pay for the same. When the cash is not on hand, the governing body may require the property owners to pay for the cost of grading of such sidewalks.
(c)
A permit shall then be issued in writing by the city manager, on a form provided therefor, and on which shall be printed the specifications prescribed by the governing body. No permits shall be issued to any contractor who has not previously given bond to the city in the amount required by the governing body, conditioned to indemnify the city against loss or damage, for injury to person or damage to property due to the execution of the work, and to bind said contractor to comply with the terms of this chapter. Nothing in this section shall be construed to limit the right of the city to require property owners to put down sidewalks, where in the opinion of the governing body, the public welfare requires it, but in all such cases the contractor shall be required to obtain a permit from the city manager before beginning work.
(Code 1973, § 2-7153)
Sec. 17-6. - Reserved.
Sec. 17-7. - Construction and repair of sidewalks, curbs, gutters and streets—Apportionment of costs.
The governing body may require that the entire cost of the construction of sidewalks and curbs and three-fourths of the entire cost of gutters, when the construction of gutters is ordered by the governing body, be borne by the abutting property owners, except the alley crossings and quadrants at intersections.
(Code 1973, § 2-7154)
Sec. 17-8. - Same—Recovery of costs.
If any of the owners of the lots or properties shall refuse to build or repair such sidewalks within the time required by the notice, and agreeable thereto, the governing body, through the city manager, may contract for the construction or repair of same, and shall pay the cost thereof out of the general fund, and amounts so paid shall be a lien upon such lots or property and may be enforced by attachments at law or in equity or in the manner provided in the Charter; or the amount may be recovered against the owners by suit before any court of competent jurisdiction; and the city attorney is authorized to proceed to enforce the lien declared and fixed by the Charter and this section.
(Code 1973, §§ 2-7150, 2-7155)
Sec. 17-9. - Same—On initiative of governing body.
(a)
For the purpose of securing good and substantial sidewalks in the city and securing uniformity in the construction thereof, the governing body, wherever it is deemed necessary by it for the public welfare, may require the owner of any lot or part of a lot in the city fronting upon any public street to construct and keep in repair a good and substantial sidewalk of foot pavement along the whole street frontage of such person's lots or property, and of width and material prescribed by resolution of the governing body.
(b)
When so instructed by resolution by the governing body, the city clerk shall give the owners of lots written notice stating the action of the governing body, and specifically setting forth work to be done and the length of time within which the same must be done, provided that the time fixed shall not be less than twenty (20) days.
(Code 1973, §§ 2-7148—2-7150, 2-7155)
Sec. 17-10. - Paving on railroad right-of-way.
When any street occupied by the track of a railroad is about to be paved or otherwise improved, it shall be the duty of the railroad company to pave at its own expense that portion of such street lying within its right-of-way, using such materials and construction methods as shall be approved by the city manager.
(Code 1973, § 2-790)
Sec. 17-11. - Excavations; obstructions—Permit; restoration of surface.
(a)
No person, except in or as incident to the exercise of rights given by franchise, shall break or dig up the grounds in any public street, way or public place or lands of the city, or erect or place thereon any staging, or place or deposit thereon any brick, stone, timber or other building material without first having obtained a permit from the city manager in writing. In all cases in which a permit may be given for excavating in or obstructing any street, the city manager shall impose such restrictions and limitations as the city manager shall see fit to make the street safe and convenient for public travel by the erection of barriers, the maintaining of lights, the removal of rubbish or otherwise. It shall also be a condition of the permit that the person so excavating any street shall cause same to be refilled and tamped, and put in as good condition as it was before being opened. In case of a permit applicable to a paved street, the earth so excavated shall be hauled away and the excavation refilled with slag or sand well tamped on which the paving shall be laid as it was before, all done to the satisfaction of the city manager.
(b)
Any person making an excavation shall meet the requirements as set forth in the federal occupational safety and health regulations for construction, as adopted April 17, 1971, and each amendment thereafter, with the following exceptions:
(1)
Excavations involved in the construction of a basement or foundation for a single-family residential structure are exempt from all of these requirements, with the exception that any excavation or foundation grading on which construction does not proceed within thirty (30) days shall be fenced, so as to prevent general public entrance to the building site, or filled in. However, an extension may be allowed if justified and if approved by application to the city manager.
(2)
Table P-2, the line reading "ten (10) to fifteen (15) feet, likely to crack" and the column "Maximum Spacing, Vertical Feet," shall contain the number "4."
(Code 1973, § 2-7142)
Cross reference— Traffic and vehicles, ch. 18.
Sec. 17-12. - Same—Bond; cash deposit.
No person shall be given a permit to excavate a street without first having executed a good and sufficient bond to the city in such amount as required by the governing body, conditioned to save the city harmless on account of any loss or damages resulting from excavations, and without having first deposited a sufficient amount of cash with the city treasurer as will, in the opinion of the city manager, cover the cost of restoring the street or way to its condition prior to the excavating.
(Code 1973, § 2-7143)
Sec. 17-13. - Same—Repairs.
If any street in the portion which has been so opened or dug up shall require repairing or resurfacing within a period of six (6) months after it has been so disturbed, the city manager shall give notice in writing to the person who had or was given the right to make the excavation to make such repairs therein as are necessary. If such repairs are not made within seven (7) days from the day of notification, the city manager shall proceed at once to have such repairs made and the expense of the same shall be paid by the party making the excavations.
(Code 1973, § 2-7143)
Sec. 17-14. - Reserved.
Sec. 17-15. - Dangerous areas adjacent to streets.
All dangerous places upon private lots which are below the grade of the adjoining street shall be properly enclosed with fences or walls, and should the owner fail to so fence or wall upon being notified by the city manager, such person shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor.
(Code 1973, § 2-7186)
Sec. 17-16. - Cutting curbs and gutters; driveway construction; crossing gutter, etc.; permit to cut.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to cut a curb or gutter or to damage any sidewalk, in constructing a sidewalk or for any other purpose, without making petition to and obtaining from the city manager a permit to do so. If a permit is granted therefor, the party securing such permit shall perform such work at such person's own expense and as specified by the city manager.
(b)
No person shall be allowed to place a bridge, or other obstruction in or over any paved gutter; provided, however, that the city manager may grant a permit to any person to put in a temporary crossing over a sidewalk for the purpose of excavating on or filling in private premises or hauling in building materials upon such conditions as may be necessary to protect such sidewalk from damage while such work is going on, to be constructed and maintained at the expense of the person to whom such permit is granted.
(Code 1973, § 2-7182)
Sec. 17-17. - Roof gutters.
All structures that are built so as to project over the sidewalk shall be so provided with gutters that there shall be no drip upon the sidewalk.
(Code 1973, § 2-7183)
Sec. 17-18. - Drainage—Discharge of water.
No water shall be discharged from the roof of any building so as to flow across any sidewalk upon any street in which curbs or gutters or a public drain have been laid; and the conductor for such water shall be made by the owner of such building to connect with such drain, under the direction of the city manager.
(Code 1973, § 2-7176)
Sec. 17-19. - Same—Obstruction of flow of water.
No person occupying the streets or alleys of the city with such person's work or any appurtenances shall obstruct or impede the flow of water in any street, gutter, alley or drain; and when sand, mortar or other like building material shall be deposited on the streets to be used for building purposes, the contractor or builder shall provide suitable boxes or other receptacles for the same, or otherwise keep the gutter open.
(Code 1973, § 2-7181)
Cross reference— Buildings; construction, maintenance, and related activities, ch. 5.
Sec. 17-20. - New streets—Privately installed.
If any person shall lay out a new addition to the city or a new street, the same shall be surveyed and opened in such manner as to conform to the streets of the city already opened and accepted, and no new streets surveyed and opened by a private person shall be considered as streets of the city, or be taken charge of by the governing body unless a plat of same with all dimensions clearly marked thereon in figures is adopted by governing body, upon the recommendation of the city manager and filed with the city manager.
(Code 1973, § 2-7156)
Cross reference— Subdivisions, app. B.
Sec. 17-21. - Same—Opening by city; altering streets.
(a)
Whenever the governing body shall by resolution determine that the public interest demands that any new street shall be opened within the corporate limits, or any old street should be extended, widened or straightened, and the owners of the land through which same shall pass require damages, the governing body shall, by resolution, proceed to condemn land for the same and provide damages therefor in the manner set forth in the charter and in O.C.G.A. § 22-1-6 et seq. The notices required by law shall be served by any police officer of the city, and such publications and other notices shall be made by the city clerk. The names of any number of nonresident owners may be inserted in one advertisement.
(b)
Whenever the funds to settle the damages assessed as provided by law shall be in the hands of the treasurer for the benefit of the owner of the land, the governing body may condemn the land, by ordinance, and after allowing the property owners five (5) days' time in which to open such street, may order such street to be opened, and the same shall be and become a public thoroughfare. Any person failing or refusing to open such street shall be subject to punishment as provided in section 1-8 for each such day of such failure or refusal.
(Code 1973, §§ 2-7157, 2-7158)
Sec. 17-22. - Utility apparatus—Permit to install.
(a)
No person opening a public utility or furnishing public service shall locate any pole, wire, conduit, pipe or other apparatus without first having obtained a permit from the city manager in writing; one (1) copy shall be filed with the city clerk. The permit and installation shall be subject to the publication "Utility Accommodation and Standards," 1988, and each amendment thereafter, on file in the city clerk's office.
(b)
Any permit granted hereunder shall be subject to the right of the city, free of charge, to place its fire alarm, telegraph or other electric lines upon the poles or through the conduits so permitted to be maintained; and to the right of the city to license the location of wires or lines by any other person upon such poles or through such conduits upon the payment or tender to the owner thereof a reasonable compensation to be determined by the parties.
(c)
The city manager shall not grant permits for more than one (1) line of poles on any street, unless it is impossible to use two (2) or more lines of wires on the same pole, and shall use every endeavor to locate poles where they will be least objectionable.
(d)
The number of poles permitted in each block shall be determined by the city manager.
(e)
All utility wiring involving new construction shall be located underground.
(Code 1973, §§ 2-7151, 2-7159, 2-7161)
Sec. 17-23. - Same—Removal.
Any such person, at such person's own expense, shall remove such person's poles or conduits to other suitable locations whenever ordered to do so by the governing body.
(Code 1973, § 2-7160)
Sec. 17-24. - Placement of sharp objects.
No person shall throw, scatter, drop or place or cause or procure to be thrown, scattered, dropped or placed within the city any glass, tacks, nails, pieces of metal or any other substance likely to damage tires on any vehicle.
(Code 1973, § 2-7175)
Cross reference— Traffic and vehicles, ch. 18; vehicles damaging streets, § 18-8.
Sec. 17-25. - Removal of city property.
No person shall remove or carry away any city property in any of the public streets or other public places of the city except as authorized by the city.
(Code 1973, § 2-7170)
Sec. 17-26. - Fires in streets.
No person shall make any fire in any street for any purpose.
(Code 1973, § 2-7166)
Sec. 17-27. - Apertures in streets or sidewalks—Permit to make.
No person shall make or cause to be made any aperture in or under any street or sidewalk for any purpose without first having obtained a permit from the city manager; and no person shall leave any aperture opened or unfastened except while actually in use with workers present.
(Code 1973, § 2-7178)
Sec. 17-28. - Same—Cellar doors.
Every cellar door opening upon the pavement shall be of iron grating, and so constructed that when shut it shall be on a level with the pavement and have its gratings running at right angles to the line of the street. No cellar door shall be permitted to extend more than five (5) feet from the wall, nor be so constructed as to open downward.
(Code 1973, § 2-7179)
Sec. 17-29. - Same—Security and repair.
Whenever the owner or occupant of any premises upon which there is a cellar, hole, area, or vault opening on the street is notified by the city that the covering of the same is insecure and dangerous, it shall be such person's duty to repair the same at once. If such person fails to do so after five (5) days' notice, it shall be the duty of the city manager to have the same repaired or placed in safe condition and collect the cost thereof from the owner or occupant. No cellarway or other hole of like nature opening in or upon the public streets or sidewalks shall be allowed to be open when not in use. All such means of access to buildings shall be provided with a substantial lock.
(Code 1973, § 2-7185)
Sec. 17-30. - Projections upon sidewalk—Bay windows; steps.
No person shall build or erect any bay window or steps to project upon any sidewalk from the first story of any building.
(Code 1973, § 2-7189)
Sec. 17-31. - Same—Awnings.
No awning frames shall have supports placed on sidewalks or in parkways, but swinging awnings may be constructed and securely attached to the buildings extending not beyond the curbline, the rods of which shall be at least seven and one-half (7½) feet above the sidewalk, and the flaps and aprons of such awnings shall clear the sidewalk at least seven (7) feet.
(Code 1973, § 2-7188)
Sec. 17-32. - Mailboxes.
It shall be unlawful for any person to move a mailbox currently attached to any building to any street.
(Code 1973, § 2-7196)
Sec. 17-33. - Protection of sidewalk, curb and gutter.
No substance of a greater weight than four hundred (400) pounds shall be handled upon the sidewalk, curbing or guttering of the city, unless such sidewalk, curbing or guttering be sufficiently covered with wood so as to protect it from damage.
(Code 1973, § 2-7192)
Cross reference— Traffic and vehicles, ch. 18.
Sec. 17-34. - Use of the public right-of-way.
It shall be unlawful for any person to place or maintain a dumpster, pod-type storage container, construction materials and equipment, landscape materials and equipment, or portable toilet on the public streets, sidewalks or other public right-of-way within the city limits, unless the city manager or his designee makes a finding that the placement of any of the items set forth in this section on private property would be impractical due to the topography or other physical condition of the land and to set conditions as to the use and placement of such items on public streets, sidewalks or other rights-of-way, in which case any such item may be located on the public street, sidewalk or other public right-of-way with the city limits, as designated in such finding; provided, however, in the event of such finding by the city manager of his designee, no such item set forth in this section, with the exception of portable toilets, shall remain on the public street, sidewalk or other public right-of-way for more than three (3) days in any continuous thirty (30) day period.
(Ord. No. 0505, § 1, 7-25-05)
Sec. 17-35. - Maintenance of refuse receptacles, pod type storage containers and portable toilets.
Any person using or in possession of a refuse receptacle (garbage can or dumpster), pod-type storage container or portable toilet shall maintain such refuse receptacle, pod-type storage container or portable toilet free of graffiti.
(Ord. No. 0505, § 1, 7-25-05)
Sec. 17-36. - Requirement to have portable toilets.
(a)
Any person issued a building permit* in the city limits of Avondale Estates for construction or renovation of property, where said construction or renovation is valued at a cost estimate exceeding ten thousand dollars ($10,000.00), is required to have a portable toilet to serve their property from the date the construction or renovation begins until the work being performed receives final approval by the issuing authority, unless such person shall sign a statement that all persons performing work pursuant to said permit shall be allowed to use a toilet located on the property.
(b)
Portable toilets must have a working key or combination locking mechanism and shall be locked when not in use.
(Ord. No. 0505, § 1, 7-25-05)
*Note—Section 4 of Ord. No. 0505 states that this ordinance shall apply to all permits issued after July 25, 2005. For permits issued prior to July 25, 2005, the effective date shall be Oct. 1, 2005.
Secs. 17-37—17-39. - Reserved.

Sec. 17-40. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases when used in this article shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Park trees means trees, shrubs, bushes and other woody vegetation in public parks having individual names, and all areas owned by the city, or to which the public has free access as a park.
Street trees means trees, shrubs, bushes, and all other woody vegetation on land lying between property lines on both sides of all streets and other public ways within the city.
(Ord. No. 08-05, § 2, 10-27-08)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 17-41. - Tree board—Created and established; membership.
(a)
Created and established. There is hereby created and established a city tree board.
(b)
Appointment of members. The members of the board shall be appointed by the mayor.
(c)
Term of office. The terms of the members of the city tree board shall be two (2) years.
(d)
Compensation. Members of the city tree board shall serve without compensation.
(Ord. No. 08-05, § 2, 10-27-08)
Sec. 17-42. - Same—Duties and responsibilities.
It shall be the responsibility of the city tree board to study, investigate, counsel and advise the city manager who will administer a written plan for the care, preservation, pruning, planting, replanting, removal or disposition of trees and shrubs in parks, along streets and in other public areas. Such plan will be presented annually to the governing body and upon its acceptance and approval shall constitute the official comprehensive city tree plan. The city tree board, when requested by the governing body shall consider, investigate, make findings, report and recommend upon any special matter or question coming within the scope of its work.
(Ord. No. 08-05, § 2, 10-27-08)
Sec. 17-43. - Same—Organization.
The city tree board shall choose its own officers and make its own rules and regulations. A majority of the members shall be a quorum for the transaction of business.
(Ord. No. 08-05, § 2, 10-27-08)
Sec. 17-44. - Review of city manager's acts.
The governing body shall have the right to review the conduct, acts and decisions of the city manager relative to his/her actions that relate to this article.
(Ord. No. 08-05, § 2, 10-27-08)
Sec. 17-45. - City manager—Interference therewith.
It shall be unlawful for any person to prevent or interfere with the city manager or his designee(s), while engaging in the planting, cultivating, mulching, pruning, spraying or removing of any street tree, park tree, or tree on private grounds, as authorized in this article. Any person(s) found to be in violation of this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor.
(Ord. No. 08-05, § 2, 10-27-08)
Sec. 17-46. - Official tree species list.
No tree may be planted on city property without prior written permission from the city manager. The following list constitutes the official list of tree species that may be planted in city rights-of-way and city parks/greenspaces, respectively. Wherever and whenever practicable, overstory (large) trees should be planted to further the city's goal of protecting the city's existing healthy tree canopy and amplifying it whenever possible.

Sec. 17-47. - Planting, maintaining, removing trees, plants.
The city shall have the right to plant, prune, maintain and remove trees, plants and shrubs within the lines of all streets, alleys, avenues, lanes, squares and public grounds, as may be necessary to ensure public safety or to preserve or enhance the symmetry and beauty of such public grounds. The city tree board may recommend the removal of any tree or part thereof which is in an unsafe condition or which by reason of its nature is injurious to sewers, electric power lines, water lines, or other public improvements, or is affected with any injurious fungus, insect or other pest.
(Ord. No. 08-05, § 2, 10-27-08)
Sec. 17-48. - Tree topping.
It shall be unlawful for any person or city department to top any street tree, park tree, or other tree on public property except as expressly permitted by this section. Topping is defined as the severe cutting back of limbs to stubs larger than three (3) inches in diameter within the tree's crown to such a degree as to remove the normal canopy and disfigure the tree. Trees severely damaged by storms or other causes, or certain trees under utility wires or other obstruction, where other pruning practices are impractical, may be excepted from this section at the determination of the city manager. Any person(s) found to be in violation of this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor.
(Ord. No. 08-05, § 2, 10-27-08)
Sec. 17-49. - Pruning and removal of vegetation.
(a)
Every owner of any tree overhanging any street or right-of-way within the city shall prune the branches so that such branches shall not obstruct the light from any street lamp or interfere with visibility of any traffic control device or sign, or obstruct the view of any street intersection.
(b)
There shall be a clear space of eight (8) feet above the surface of the street or sidewalk.
(c)
Such owners shall remove all dead, diseased or dangerous trees, or broken or decayed limbs which constitute a hazard to the safety of the public or harbor insects or diseases which constitute a potential threat to other trees within the city.
(Ord. No. 08-05, § 2, 10-27-08)
Sec. 17-50. - Removal on private property.
(a)
The city shall have the right to cause the removal or pruning of trees, limbs or shrubs as necessary as indicated is section 17-49.
(b)
The city manager or his designee shall notify, in writing, the owners of such trees.
(c)
Removal shall be done by such owners at their own expense within ten (10) days of written notification.
(d)
In the event of failure of owners to comply with such provisions, the city shall have the authority to remove or prune such trees and shrubs and add the cost of removal to the property tax of the owner.
(Ord. No. 08-05, § 2, 10-27-08)
Sec. 17-51. - Removal of stumps.
All stumps of street and park trees shall be removed below the surface of the ground so that the top of the stump shall not project above the surface of the ground.
(Ord. No. 08-05, § 2, 10-27-08)
Secs. 17-52—17-59. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - LAKE AVONDALE ADVISORY BOARD

Sec. 17-60. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases when used in this article shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning.
Lake area shall mean all public land lying between the easterly curb line of Berkeley Road, the northerly curb line of Wiltshire Drive, the westerly curb line of Lakeshore Drive and the southerly curb line of Lakeview Place, plus the area commonly known as the "bird sanctuary" south of Wiltshire Drive as it parallels the dam of Lake Avondale.
Trees means trees, shrubs, bushes, and all other woody vegetation within the lake area.
(Ord. No. 10-14, § 1, 8-23-04)
Sec. 17-61. - Lake Avondale Advisory Board—Created and established; membership.
(a)
Created and established. There is hereby created and established a Lake Avondale Advisory Board.
(b)
Appointment of members. The five (5) members of the board shall be appointed by the board of mayor and commissioners. All members shall be citizens of the City of Avondale Estates.
(c)
Qualification of members. At least two of the members of Lake Avondale Advisory Board shall have either education and/or experience in watershed/lake management, natural resource management, environmental management, environmental engineering or a related field as determined by the governing body.
(d)
Restricted members. No more than two (2) of the members of Lake Avondale Advisory Board may reside within view of Lake Avondale.
(e)
Term of office. The terms of the members of Lake Avondale Advisory Board shall be two (2) years and shall be appointed in staggered terms so that not more than two (2) members of Lake Avondale Advisory Board shall have their initial terms expire simultaneously.
(f)
Compensation. Members of the Lake Avondale Advisory Board shall serve without compensation.
(Ord. No. 10-14, § 1, 8-23-04)
Sec. 17-62. - Same—Duties and responsibilities.
It shall be the duty and responsibility of the Lake Avondale Advisory Board to:
(a)
Study, investigate, counsel and advise the city manager in writing on any matter which affects the aesthetics, visibility or user friendliness of the lake area, it being understood Lake Avondale is a primary recreational resource of the city.
(b)
Exercise the sole authority to recommend preservation, pruning, planting, replanting, removal or disposition of trees in the lake area, including memorial plantings.
(c)
In conformity with section 17-49, ensure an eight (8) feet clear space be maintained between the ground and any tree limb in the lake area, where practicable, and eliminate any tree which unduly restricts fishing access or visibility so that the full vista of Lake Avondale may be enjoyed by residents.
(d)
Investigate, make findings, report and recommend regarding any special matter or question coming within the scope of its work when requested by the governing body.
(e)
Advise the city manager regarding any matter which either affects water quality or the fish or waterfowl populations of Lake Avondale.
(Ord. No. 10-14, § 1, 8-23-04)
Sec. 17-63. - Reserved.
Editor's note— Ord. No. 20-07, § 1, adopted Nov. 23, 2020, repealed § 17-63, which pertained to duty of city manager to comply; exceptions and derived from Ord. No. 10-14, § 1, adopted Aug. 23, 2004.
Sec. 17-64. - Organization.
The Lake Avondale Advisory Board shall choose its own officers and make its own rules and regulations. A majority of the members shall be a quorum for the transaction of business.
(Ord. No. 10-14, § 1, 8-23-04)
Sec. 17-65. - Unauthorized planting prohibited at Lake Avondale; procedure for requesting permission to plant trees in the lake area.
(a)
It shall be a violation of this Code for any person, organization or entity to plant any tree in the lake area without the written permission of the city manager.
(b)
Anyone desiring to plant any tree in the lake area, including, but not limited to memorial plantings, shall first file an application with the Lake Avondale Advisory Board describing the type of tree(s), location and manner of planting proposed. The Lake Avondale Advisory Board shall then make a written recommendation to the city manager regarding such request. The final decision to grant or deny a request to plant a tree in the lake area shall be made by the city manager and communicated to the requesting party in writing.
(Ord. No. 10-14, § 1, 8-23-04; Ord. No. 20-07, § 2, 11-23-2020)
