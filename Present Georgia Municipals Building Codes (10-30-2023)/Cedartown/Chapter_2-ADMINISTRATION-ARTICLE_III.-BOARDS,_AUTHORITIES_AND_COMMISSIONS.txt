DIVISION 2. - AUDITORIUM COMMISSION

Sec. 2-71. - Created; members.
(a)
There is hereby created and established an auditorium commission of the city, which shall be known as the Cedartown Civic Arts Commission (CCAC). The CCAC shall be composed of ten members. Appointments for membership on the CCAC shall be made by nominations from the CCAC subject to confirmation and approval by the city commission.
(b)
The term of office of each member shall be two years, or until their successors are appointed and qualify. Five members of the CCAC shall be appointed for a term of one year and five members shall be appointed for a term of two years. As the terms expire, they shall be refilled by appointment for two-year terms, so that one-half of the members shall be appointed each year, and the terms are staggered. Vacancies on the CCAC shall be filled by another appointment for the unexpired term and in the same manner. All of the members shall serve without compensation.
(c)
One member of the CCAC shall be a member of the City Commission of the City of Cedartown, appointed annually by the city commission.
(Code 1969, § 6½-1; Ord. No. 3-2011, § 1, 2-14-11; Ord. No. 1-2020 , § 1, 2-10-20)
Sec. 2-72. - Organization; bylaws.
Immediately after their appointment, the members of the CCAC shall meet and organize and shall elect such officers as may be necessary. The auditorium commission shall have the power and authority to adopt bylaws and rules; such organization may be accomplished by the formation of a 501(c)(3) non-profit corporation or other similar entity.
(Code 1969, § 6½-3; Ord. No. 17-1994, § 2, 12-12-94; Ord. No. 1-2020 , § 2, 2-10-20)
Sec. 2-73. - Responsibilities; auditorium director; authority generally.
(a)
The city commission shall have final control and authority over all functions and activities which may occur within the auditorium.
(b)
The Cedartown Civic Arts Commission shall be an advisory board and shall make recommendations to the city commission and/or the tourism and economic development department with respect to lease arrangements, programs, services, community benefits, and all other general functions and activities which may occur within the auditorium.
(c)
The city commission shall appoint an auditorium director, who shall be an employee of the city, and the director shall be under the supervision, control, and direction of the city manager or the head of the tourism and economic development department, as appropriate. The director shall report to the manager concerning general matters of governance affecting the civic arts commission, the auditorium, and all activities contemplated by this division. Further, the city manager, with approval of the city commission, shall have full and complete authority with regard to all matters of approval, change, and/or modification of the budget of the CCAC. All maintenance, upkeep, and general repairs of the auditorium shall be under the general charge and control of the city commission.
(Code 1969, § 6½-2; Ord. No. 17-1994, § 1, 12-12-94; Ord. No. 3-2011, § 2, 2-14-11; Ord. No. 1-2020 , § 3, 2-10-20)

Sec. 2-96. - Declaration of need; geographical boundaries.
There is hereby determined and declared a pressing, existing and future need for a downtown development authority (as more fully described and defined in the Downtown Development Authorities Law, 1981 Ga. Laws, page 1744), to function in the city for the purpose of developing and promoting for the public good and general welfare, trade, commerce and industry and employment opportunities in the following described geographic area:
All that tract or parcel of land lying and being in the City of Cedartown, Polk County, Georgia, and being more particularly described as follows:
Beginning at the northwest corner of the intersection of East Avenue and Marshall Street; running thence in a northerly direction along the west right-of-way line of Marshall Street to the north right-of-way line of Queen Street; running thence in an eastward direction along the north right-of-way line of Queen Street to the east right-of-way line of Greenwood Drive; running thence in a southerly direction along the east right-of-way line of Greenwood Drive to the south right-of-way line of East Avenue; running thence in a westerly direction along the south right-of-way line of East Avenue to the east right-of-way line of Highland Avenue; running thence in a southerly direction along the east right-of-way line of Highland Avenue to the south right-of-way line of Herbert Street; running thence in a westerly direction along the south right-of-way line of Herbert Street to the east right-of-way line of Philpot Street; running thence in a southerly direction along the east right-of-way line of Philpot Street to the south right-of-way line of Ware Street; continuing thence in a southerly direction on a line which would be a straight extension of Philpot Street to the south right-of-way line of Marietta Street; running thence in a westerly direction along the south right-of-way line of Marietta Street to the east right-of-way line of Main Street; running thence in a southerly direction along the east right-of-way line of Main Street to a point where the south right-of-way line of Canal Street would intersect the east right-of-way line of Main Street; running thence in a westerly direction along the south right-of-way line of Canal Street to a point where the west right-of-way line of South College Street would intersect the south line of Canal Street if extended in a straight line; running thence in a northerly direction along the west right-of-way line of College Street to the north right-of-way line of the Seaboard Airline Railroad; running thence in a westerly direction along the north right-of-way line of the Main line of the Seaboard Airline Railroad to the center of Cedar Creek; running thence in a northerly and westerly direction along the centerline of Cedar Creek to a point where the centerline of the creek is intersected by the centerline of Spring Branch; running thence in an easterly direction along the centerline of Spring Branch to the west right-of-way line of Furnace Street; running thence in a northerly direction along the west line of Furnace Street to the north line of Wissahickon Avenue; running thence in an easterly direction along the north line of Wissahickon Avenue to the east line of College Street; running thence in a southerly direction along the east line of College Street to the North line of East Avenue and running thence in an easterly direction along the north line of East Avenue to the point of beginning.
The area described above is more particularly shown according to a map showing the area in detail, a copy of which map is on file in the office of the city clerk, which area is hereby designated the downtown development area as that term is defined in the Downtown Development Authorities Law.
(Code 1969, § 17-31(a))
Sec. 2-97. - Created; purpose.
There is hereby activated in the city the public body corporate and politic known as the downtown development authority of the city which was created upon the adoption and approval of the Downtown Development Authorities Law and particularly section 2 thereof for the purpose of developing and promoting for the public good and general welfare, trade, commerce, industry and employment opportunities in the downtown development area.
(Code 1969, § 17-31(b))
Sec. 2-98. - Terms of office.
Appointments and reappointments to the downtown development authority shall be for four-year terms of office.
(Code 1969, § 17-31(c); Res. No. 28-1996, § 1, 8-12-96)
Sec. 2-99. - Composition; quorum; member qualifications.
(a)
The downtown development authority shall be composed of seven members. A majority of the members shall constitute a quorum for the purpose of any action to be taken by the authority. The authority shall have the right to promulgate any rules, regulations or bylaws governing its function, operation, meetings, deliberations or any other matters as might be deemed appropriate and the best interest of the authority by its membership.
(b)
Any person who is nominated for appointment as a member of the authority must meet the following qualifications:
(1)
The person must be a property owner residing within the corporate limits of the city; or
(2)
The person must be an owner and/or operator of a business located within the downtown development district and a taxpayer resident citizen of the county; or
(3)
Any person who meets the combined qualifications of subsections (1) and (2) of this section.
(c)
One of the members (directors) of the authority may be a member of the city commission.
(Res. No. 28-1996, § 2, 8-12-96)
Sec. 2-100. - Economic interest of members; officers; compensation; successor directors.
(a)
At least four of the directors who are qualified to serve as set forth in section 2-99 shall be persons who, in the judgment of the city commission, either have been or in some representative capacity are involved with an economic interest in the redevelopment and revitalization areas of the downtown development authority.
(b)
The member directors of the authority shall elect one of their number as chairman, a second as vice-chairman, and shall also select a secretary, and treasurer if necessary. Any office holder need not be a member of the authority to hold such office.
(c)
All members shall receive no compensation for their services to the city as members of the downtown development authority, but shall be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred by them in the performance of their duties.
(d)
Successor members shall be appointed for the unexpired terms or additional terms, as may be required to ensure the proper composition of seven members of the board of directors of the authority.
(Res. No. 28-1996, § 2, 8-12-96)
Sec. 2-101. - Continuing education requirement.
Unless a member of the downtown development authority is also a city commissioner, all members shall attend and complete at least eight hours of training concerning any downtown development and/or redevelopment program within the first 12 months of their appointment. Members in office on or before January 1, 1992, shall be exempt from this requirement, unless such members are reappointed for an additional term thereafter.
(Res. No. 28-1996, § 2, 8-12-96)
Sec. 2-102. - Adjustment for term length.
Any appointments made to the downtown development authority on and after July 1, 1994, in which there was any indication that the person served for a term longer than four years shall be adjusted so that all members of the authority appointed after such date shall serve for four-year terms, to comply with the Downtown Development Authorities Law.
(Res. No. 28-1996, § 4, 8-12-96)
Sec. 2-103. - Requirement to comply with statute.
The board of directors shall organize itself, carry out its duties and responsibilities and exercise its powers and prerogatives in accordance with the terms and provisions of the Downtown Development Authorities Law as it now exists and as it might hereafter be amended or modified.
(Code 1969, § 17-31(d))
Sec. 2-104. - Certified copy to secretary of state.
The secretary of the city commission shall furnish immediately to the secretary of the state a certified copy of Resolution Number 28-1996, as might be required by law.
(Code 1969, § 17-31(e); Res. No. 28-1996, § 1, 8-12-96)

DIVISION 4. - TREE COMMISSION

Sec. 2-136. - Created; members.
(a)
There is hereby created and established a city tree board for the city which shall consist of five members, composed of citizens and residents of this city, who shall be appointed by the city commission and shall be referred to as the Cedartown Tree Commission.
(b)
The term of the members shall be for three years. For the initial appointment to the first board, one member shall be appointed for a one-year term, two members shall be appointed for a three-year term. If a vacancy shall occur during the term of any member, his successor shall be appointed for the unexpired portion of the term.
(c)
The tree commission members shall serve without compensation.
(Ord. No. 3-1994, § 1, 2-14-94)
Sec. 2-137. - Organization, records, rules of procedure.
The tree commission shall choose its own officers, make its own rules and regulations, and keep a journal of its proceedings. A majority of the members shall be a quorum for the transaction of business.
(Ord. No. 3-1994, § 1, 2-14-94)
Sec. 2-138. - Responsibilities, duties.
It shall be the responsibility of the tree commission to study, investigate, counsel and develop and/or update annually and administer a written plan for the care, preservation, pruning, planting, replanting, removal or disposition of trees in the parks, along streets and in other public areas. Such plan will be presented annually to the city commission and upon its acceptance and approval shall constitute the official comprehensive city tree plan for the city. The tree commission, as requested by the city commission, shall consider, investigate, make findings, report and recommend upon any special matter of question coming within the scope of these duties.
(Ord. No. 3-1994, § 1, 2-14-94)
Sec. 2-139. - Review by city commission.
The city commission shall have the right to review the conduct, acts and decisions of the city tree board. Any person may appeal from any ruling or order of the city tree board to the city commission who may hear the matter and make the final decision.
(Ord. No. 3-1994, § 1, 2-14-94)
