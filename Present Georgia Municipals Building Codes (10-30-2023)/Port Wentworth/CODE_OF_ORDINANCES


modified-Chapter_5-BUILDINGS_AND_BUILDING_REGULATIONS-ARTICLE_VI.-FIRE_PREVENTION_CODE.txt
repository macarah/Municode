ARTICLE VI. - FIRE PREVENTION CODE[7]

State Law reference— Authority for municipality to exempt private residences and buildings of less than three stories from fire escape requirements, O.C.G.A. § 8-2-53; duty to require smoke detectors in certain structures, O.C.G.A. § 25-2-40.

Sec. 5-116. - Adopted; violation prohibited.
(a)
There is hereby adopted by reference that publication known as the Standard Fire Prevention Code, latest edition, recommended by the Southern Building Code Congress International, Inc.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to violate any of the provisions of the code adopted in subsection (a).
(Ord. No. 90-1, 1-11-90; Ord. No. 91-8, 9-26-91; Ord. No. 96-05, 3-28-96; Ord. No. 97-3, 2-13-97)
Sec. 5-117. - "Municipality" defined.
As used in the code adopted by the provisions of this article, the term "municipality" means the City of Port Wentworth.
(Ord. No. 90-1, 1-11-90)
Sec. 5-118. - Enforcement.
The code herein adopted shall be enforced by the fire prevention officer as appointed by the mayor and council of the City of Port Wentworth.
(Ord. No. 90-1, 1-11-90)
