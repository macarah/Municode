ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 42-1. - Closing streets.
The city shall have authority to close any public street, road, alley, sidewalk or other public way in the manner provided in the city Charter.
Secs. 42-2—42-20. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - CONSTRUCTION

Sec. 42-21. - Permit required.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to construct, excavate, dig, or lay any pavement for or perform any act that would require the removal or breaking of the surface or subsurface of any public street, sidewalk, curb, alley, or other public way, or to repair the same, without having first obtained a permit from the city clerk in the manner specified in this chapter.
(b)
No person shall begin to construct, reconstruct, repair, alter, or grade any driveway, curb, or curb-cut on the public streets without first obtaining a permit from the city clerk in the manner specified in this chapter.
Sec. 42-22. - Specifications.
(a)
All street and sidewalk pavements and all curbing shall be laid in conformity with the specifications established or approved from time to time by the mayor and council.
(b)
The standard for construction of curbs on each side of any city street, or any connecting street or road for which curbs have been prescribed, shall be not less than one vamp per lineal block giving on the crosswalks at intersections. Ramps shall be at least 40 inches wide and so constructed as to conform to all requirements of the Americans with Disabilities Act, as amended, and all other similar state or federal laws, statutes, or regulations promulgated in connection therewith.
Sec. 42-23. - Barricades.
Any person, firm, or corporation laying or repairing any pavement on a street, sidewalk, or other public place shall maintain suitable barricades to prevent injury to any person or vehicle by reason of the work. All such barricades shall be illuminated by a light so that they can be seen after dark.

ARTICLE III. - PERMIT PROCEDURE

Sec. 42-50. - Fees; bonds.
Each applicant for a permit under this chapter shall be accompanied by a permit fee in the amount established by the city council and, in the discretion of the mayor and city council, a surety bond in an amount sufficient to indemnify the city for any loss or damage caused by or resulting from the work undertaken pursuant to the permit. Upon the disapproval of any application for which a fee has been submitted under the provisions of this chapter, the city clerk shall refund such fee, provided that the applicant is not otherwise indebted to the city.
Sec. 42-51. - Standards for issuance of permit.
The city clerk shall consider the following standards in determining whether to issue a permit hereunder:
(1)
Whether the plans for the proposed operation have been approved by the street superintendent, to whom such plans shall be forwarded by the city clerk within a reasonable time after receipt thereof;
(2)
Whether the work shall be done according to the standard specifications of the city for public work of like character;
(3)
Whether the operation will unreasonably interfere with vehicular and pedestrian traffic and the means of ingress to and egress from the affected and adjacent properties; and
(4)
Whether the health, welfare, and safety of the public will be unreasonably impaired.
Sec. 42-52. - Supervision.
All work for which a permit is granted hereunder shall be under the direction and supervision of the street superintendent.
Sec. 42-53. - Procedure for the issuance of permits.
Every person required to procure a permit under the provisions of this chapter or any ordinance or law of this municipality relating to the subject matter of this chapter shall submit a written application for such permit to the city clerk with the following information:
(1)
The name and address of the applicant.
(2)
The purpose of which the application is submitted.
(3)
The nature and location of any work proposed to be done and the name of the person or firm who is to do such work.
(4)
The estimated cost of the proposed work.
(5)
The date of commencement and date of completion of the proposed work, and other date or plans as may reasonably be required by the city clerk or the street superintendent.
Sec. 42-54. - Review of application.
Upon receipt of the application for a permit under the provisions of this article, the city clerk shall forward such application to the street superintendent, who shall approve or disapprove such application within seven days after receipt thereof. After indicating approval or disapproval on the application, the street superintendent shall return the same to the city clerk.
Sec. 42-55. - Issuance of permit.
Upon receipt of an approved application from the street superintendent, the city clerk shall issue a permit to the applicant therefor, which permit shall state the nature of the work to be done or activity to be carried out and bear the date of issuance and the signature of the city clerk.
Sec. 42-56. - Display of permit.
It shall be the duty of anyone receiving a permit hereunder to keep the permit in a conspicuous place. It shall be unlawful for any person to exhibit such permit at or about any location other than that for which the permit was issued, or to misrepresent the number or the date of expiration.
Sec. 42-57. - Limitation of issuance.
Notwithstanding any other provision of this section, no permit shall be issued to any applicant therefor who has failed to satisfy any bonding requirements imposed upon persons engaged in the kind of work or activity proposed to be done or carried out by such applicant.

ARTICLE IV. - STREET AND SIDEWALK MAINTENANCE

Sec. 42-88. - Sidewalk maintenance.
It shall be the duty of the city to keep walkways in good repair at all times and to remove from them all snow or ice whenever feasible.
Sec. 42-89. - Maintenance of property between street and sidewalk.
(a)
All persons who own real estate in the city which abuts upon any sidewalk which is paved shall be required to keep that portion of such sidewalk which lies between the property line and the curb line of the adjoining street, and in good and smooth condition and free from growing weeds and other obnoxious and unsightly vegetation or other things which would detract from the beauty and cleanliness of the street or obstruct any motorist's view of any street or road.
(b)
Any property owner who shall place or allow grass to grow upon the portion of sidewalk lying between the property line and the curb line of the street as aforesaid, shall keep such grass properly mowed and free from rubbish of all kinds. If such owners are not in the possession of their property, then this section shall apply to their tenants or those who have possession or control of the same.
Sec. 42-90. - Reports of defects and obstructions.
It shall be the duty of every city officer and employee who has actual knowledge of any defect or obstruction, or of any unlawful deposit or discharge, in or on any public street, alley, curb, sidewalk, or other public way of the city to report the same to the street superintendent as soon as possible.
Sec. 42-91. - Encroachments.
It shall be unlawful for any person to erect or maintain any building or structure which encroaches upon or obstructs the view of any motorist using any public street or property, or to erect any poles or wires or to maintain any poles or wires over any public place, street, alley, or other public way, without having first secured a permit from the city clerk in the manner specified in this chapter.
Sec. 42-92. - Openings.
It shall be unlawful for any person to construct or maintain any opening or stairway in any public street, sidewalk, or alley without first obtaining a permit from the city clerk in the manner specified in this chapter. All such lawfully maintained openings shall be guarded by a suitable strong cover or railing approved by the street superintendent.
Sec. 42-93. - Trees and shrubs.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to plant, remove, injure, or cut any tree, bush, or shrub in or from any public street, parkway, or other public place without first obtaining a permit from the city clerk in the manner specified in this chapter.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to attach any sign, advertisement, or notice to any tree or shrub in any public place without first obtaining any applicable sign permits pursuant to the city's sign ordinance.
(c)
Any tree or shrub which hangs over any sidewalk, street, or other public place in the city in such a way as to impede or interfere with traffic or travel shall be trimmed by the owner of the premises on which such trees or shrub is located. Likewise, any tree or limb of a tree which is likely to fall on or across any adjoining property or public way shall be removed by the owner of the premises on which such tree grows or stands.
(d)
The director of public utilities may cause to be trimmed or removed any and all such trees or shrubs so as to eliminate any obstruction or danger to adjoining properties or public ways.
Sec. 42-94. - Poles and wires.
Any person or company which maintains poles and wires in the streets, alleys, or other public places of the city shall keep such wires and poles free and away from any nearby trees or shrubs in such places, and keep all such trees and shrubs properly trimmed, subject to the supervision of the director of public utilities, so as to prevent damage to either the poles and wires or the shrubs and trees.
Sec. 42-95. - Railroad crossing maintenance.
It shall be the duty of any railroad with trackage which traverses any parts of the city, and specifically at any point where it crosses a city alley, street or other right-of-way, to maintain such crossings in good order and repair. Further, all bridges, abutments or other similar crossings shall likewise be maintained by any railroad company in a good condition and state of repair.
Sec. 42-96. - Responsibility for damages.
(a)
Any contractor, sub-contractor, or any person or firm engaged in the construction or renovation of any building, structure, or any other improvement on real property located with the corporate limits of the city shall be responsible for any damages caused to city streets, sidewalks, or other municipal improvements by said construction or renovation. The city shall have the right to demand reimbursement for any costs it must incur to repair any damage done to city streets, sidewalks, or other improvements by contractors or builders as described in this section, including, but not limited to, materials, labor, consulting fees, engineering fees, or any other costs associated with making said repairs.
(b)
Upon completion of the repairs necessitated by damage described in subsection (a) of this section, the city shall demand reimbursement from the contractor, builder, or other person or firm who caused the damage, and payment shall be made within 30 days of the date of demand. If payment is not made within this time period, then the city shall have the right to withdraw any permits, licenses, or any other documents evidencing permission to the contractor by the city to continue with said construction or improvements, and shall withhold the issuance or renewal of any further licenses until such time as the contractor or builder makes payment in accordance with the city's demand.

ARTICLE V. - PROHIBITED ACTS

Sec. 42-117. - Defacing streets, sidewalks, and curbs.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to deface any public sidewalk, street, or curb in the city by painting any signs thereon, whether for commercial advertising purposes or not.
(b)
It shall likewise by unlawful for any person to walk or drive any vehicle upon or injure in any way any newly laid street, sidewalk, or curbing pavement while the same is guarded by a warning sign or barricade or is soft or newly laid.
Sec. 42-118. - Obstructions.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to cause, create, or maintain any obstruction on any street, alley, sidewalk, or other public way, except as may be provided in this chapter or in the ordinances and laws of the city.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to obstruct a sidewalk within the city by placement of any object, including, but not limited to, vending machines, items for sale, decorative items, or similar items on or above a sidewalk, unless a permit allowing the placement has been approved by the city. Applications for a permit shall be made to the city clerk's office. In the event the application is denied by the city clerk, the applicant may appeal this decision to the city council.
(c)
Upon the report of a violation, the owner of the property shall be personally served with a written notice or citation, to either remove the item or obtain a permit within five days of the date of their receipt of the notice. Failure to comply shall be considered a violation of this article. Notwithstanding the provisions herein, the city reserves the right to remove any items that present an immediate danger to public safety without prior notification to the owner.
(d)
After notice has been served as described herein, and the five-day compliance period has elapsed, the city may remove the nonpermitted items, and the owner shall be responsible for the cost of removal and storage. Any such costs shall constitute a lien against the property from which the nuisance originates. Items unclaimed after 30 days shall be considered abandoned property.
Sec. 42-119. - Riding on sidewalks.
It shall be unlawful for any person to ride any horse or any vehicle on any city sidewalks.
Sec. 42-120. - Deposits and discharges.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to deposit on any street or sidewalk any rubbish or any material that may be harmful to the pavement thereof, or any waste material, or any glass or other article that may cause injury to any person, animal, or property.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to discharge or to allow to be discharged onto any public street or sidewalk any water or other fluid material containing objectionable material such as sewage, waste milk, or other organic material.
Sec. 42-121. - Private use.
It shall be unlawful for any person, firm, or corporation to use any street, sidewalk, or other public place as space for the display of goods or merchandise for sale; or to write or make any sign or advertisement on any such pavement without first obtaining any applicable permits or the permission of the city clerk.
Sec. 42-122. - Burning leaves and rubbish.
It shall be unlawful for any person, firm, or corporation to burn any leaves, paper, rubbish, or other materials upon any of the public streets, sidewalks, or alleys of the city.
Secs. 42-123—42-142. - Reserved.
ARTICLE VI. - PARADES AND PUBLIC DEMONSTRATIONS

Sec. 42-143. - Permits and registration.
(a)
Any person who wishes to organize, form or conduct any march or demonstration regulated by this article shall be required to register with the chief of police at least 48 hours in advance of the event, on forms to be provided by the chief of police, to obtain a permit therefor.
(b)
An application for a permit to conduct the acts set forth in this article shall be made to the chief of police in writing, shall be signed by the person responsible for the conduct of the event, and shall contain the following information:
(1)
The date and time proposed for the event.
(2)
The route, place or other area of the proposed event.
(3)
The number of vehicles (if any), and the approximate number of persons who would participate in the proposed event.
(4)
The name and address of the person sponsoring or promoting the proposed event.
(5)
The name and address of the person making the application for the permit, and proper identification of that person.
(6)
The name and address of at least five principal parties who will also be responsible for, or who may participate in, the event, including proper identification of these parties.
(7)
The name, address, telephone number and authorization of the sponsoring organization and its leaders, if the activity is designed to be held by, for or on behalf of any person, group or organization other than the applicant.
(8)
A statement as to whether the parade, assembly or other activity will occupy all or only a portion of the width of the streets, the location of such streets, and any assembly areas for the group.
Sec. 42-144. - Review of permit application; procedure for approval or denial.
(a)
If the chief of police determines that the application has failed to meet the criteria set forth in section 42-143, it shall be returned to the applicant and no permit shall be granted until the application is completed in proper form.
(b)
The mayor shall have authority, in consultation with the chief of police or designated officer in charge and pursuant to authority granted him by the city council, to approve the permit after consideration of the criminal record, if any, of the applicant and the following criteria:
(1)
The extent of vehicular and pedestrian traffic, and street use by the group anticipated at the time and place of, and on the route of, the proposed occurrence.
(2)
The availability of appropriate police forces and other law enforcement agency protection to escort the proposed group or assembly, to direct traffic in conjunction with the proposed event and to otherwise properly handle the assembly to protect the general welfare of the participants, the public who may be watching the event, and other persons who may be affected by the conducting of this event within the city.
(3)
Whether the proposed event will unreasonably burden or interfere with the normal use of the streets, sidewalks or other public areas of the city.
(4)
Whether the concentration of persons, animals and/or vehicles at assembly points or anticipated to be a part of the activity will not unduly interfere with the movement of ambulances, police protection equipment, firefighting equipment, and similar emergency vehicles.
(5)
Whether the conduct of the event would be reasonably likely to cause injury to persons or property, to provoke disorderly conduct or create a disturbance.
(6)
Whether the activity or purpose as stated in the application would violate any provision of this Code or any other ordinance, statute or federal law.
Sec. 42-145. - Disposition; appeal.
If the chief of police approves the forwarded application to the mayor of the city council, pursuant to the criteria established above and the mayor determines, in view of the criteria, that the proposed event meets the criteria established pursuant to the provisions of section 42-144, he may grant the request. If there remains some determination concerning the criteria of the preceding section being met by the application, the mayor may set the matter over for a determination by the city council at its next regular or specially scheduled meeting. The council shall, upon a review of the application in conjunction with the criteria of section 42-144, make a final decision concerning the application. Any party aggrieved of a denial of a permit may, upon formal notification of the council in writing of the denial and the reasons therefor, appeal to the superior court of the county for authorization of the proposed event.
Sec. 42-146. - Permit content and conditions.
The permit issued under this article shall contain the following with respect to the parade, picket line or demonstration:
(1)
Starting time.
(2)
Duration.
(3)
Space between persons or vehicles involved.
(4)
Portions or areas of the streets, alleys, sidewalks or other public places to be used.
(5)
Other reasonable requirements as may be necessary to promote free movement of pedestrian or vehicular traffic or to protect the health, safety and property rights of participants and the general public.
