Chapter 7 - ELECTIONS[1]

Cross reference— Administration, ch. 2.
State Law reference— Voting and elections, Ga. Const. art. 2; elections generally, O.C.G.A. title 21; municipal elections, O.C.G.A. § 21-3-1 et seq.

Sec. 7-1. - Application of state law.
All elections in the city, including primary elections, shall be governed by the Georgia Municipal Election Code.
(Code 1985, § 2-2-1)
Sec. 7-2. - Election officials.
The clerk of the commission shall serve as the registrar and the municipal election superintendent.
(Code 1985, § 2-2-2)
Sec. 7-3. - Registration list.
The city shall maintain its own registration list.
(Code 1985, § 2-2-3)
Sec. 7-4. - Polling places.
The city shall consist of one voting district with the polling place being located at the city hall or at such other place as is duly authorized.
(Code 1985, § 2-2-4)
Sec. 7-5. - Expenses.
The municipal election superintendent is authorized to incur expenses for the preparation and conduct of an election, including the cost of purchasing identification badges for poll officers, the printing of ballots and voters' certificates, and the purchasing of all other supplies necessary for the conduct of an election. The superintendent is further authorized to incur all necessary expenses to provide voting booths for the proper conduct of all elections.
(Code 1985, § 2-2-5)
Sec. 7-6. - Nonpartisan elections.
All elections held for the purpose of filling city offices shall be nonpartisan, and no political party shall conduct primaries for the purpose of nominating candidates for such municipal elections.
(Code 1985, § 2-2-6)
Sec. 7-7. - Notice of candidacy.
Each candidate for municipal office in the city shall qualify by filing a notice of candidacy with the municipal election superintendent. Qualifying dates are established by the Municipal Election Code of the State of Georgia.
(Ord. No. 90-19A, § 1, 6-4-90; Ord. No. 97-008, § I, 6-23-97)
