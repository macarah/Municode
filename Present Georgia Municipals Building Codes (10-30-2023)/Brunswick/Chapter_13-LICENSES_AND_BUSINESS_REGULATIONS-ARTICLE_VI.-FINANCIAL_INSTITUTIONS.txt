ARTICLE VI. - FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS

Sec. 13-181. - Tax levied.
There is hereby levied upon all depository financial institutions located within the city a business license tax pursuant to the authority of O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.
(Ord. No. 811, § 1, 12-19-1984)
Sec. 13-182. - Amount of tax.
The rate of the tax levied by this article shall be 0.25 percent of the gross receipts of such depository financial institutions, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(b).
(Ord. No. 811, § 2, 12-19-1984)
Sec. 13-183. - When return due.
The return required by O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(c) shall be filed by March 1 of each year with the office of the finance director of the city.
(Ord. No. 811, § 3, 12-19-1984)
Sec. 13-184. - Collection.
The tax levied by this article shall be collected from all depository financial institutions located within the city.
(Ord. No. 811, § 4, 12-19-1984)
Sec. 13-185. - Application of tax.
The tax levied by this article shall apply to all depository financial institutions which have elected to file a return in 1984 based on 1983 gross receipts and which have taken the amount of such tax as a credit against state corporate income tax liability.
(Ord. No. 811, § 5, 12-19-1984)
Sec. 13-186. - Scope of tax.
It is the intent of the city commission that depository financial institutions of all kinds located within the city shall be taxed in the same manner and to the same extent pursuant to this article, so far as permitted by state and federal law, and that the distinctions made by the General Assembly of the State of Georgia in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-98 to recognize differences between banks and savings and loan associations shall be applicable to the tax levied by this article, to the extent required by law.
(Ord. No. 811, § 6, 12-19-1984)
Secs. 13-187—13-210. - Reserved.
