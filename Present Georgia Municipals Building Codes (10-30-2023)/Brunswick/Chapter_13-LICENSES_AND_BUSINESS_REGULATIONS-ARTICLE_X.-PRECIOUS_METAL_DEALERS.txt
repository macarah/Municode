ARTICLE X. - PRECIOUS METAL DEALERS[6]
Footnotes:
--- (6) ---
State Law reference— Municipal authority to regulate and license dealers in precious metals, O.C.G.A. § 43-37-5.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 13-326. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Buyer of precious metals or gem stones means any person, whether as owner, agent or employee and whether a resident of the city or not, who engages in the business of purchasing precious metals or gem stones or articles containing any precious metal or gem stone from members of the general public other than for resale to the general public in a retail jewelry or antique business conducted at the same location or as a pawnbroker.
Gem stones means diamonds, rubies, emeralds or sapphires.
Precious metals means gold, silver or platinum or any metal containing gold, silver or platinum.
(Ord. No. 764, § 2, 10-1-1980)
Sec. 13-327. - Records—Required.
Every buyer of precious metals and gem stones shall maintain a daily record in which shall be entered in legible English at the time of each transaction the following information:
(1)
The date and time of day of the transaction.
(2)
The name of the person conducting the transaction.
(3)
The name, age, address and race of the seller, a description of the general appearance and estimated height and weight of the seller; and the distinctive number from the seller's driver's license or other similar identification card.
(4)
A full identification and description of the article or articles including kind, style, material, color, design and kind and number of stones in jewelry, if any, and all identifying marks and numbers inscribed thereon.
(5)
The price paid for each item.
(6)
The number of the check issued for the purchase price if payment is made by check.
(7)
The signature of the customer.
(Ord. No. 764, § 8, 10-1-1980)
Sec. 13-328. - Same—Entries.
Entries in the records required by section 13-327 shall appear in chronological order in ink. No blank lines may be left between entries. No obliterations, alterations, or erasures may be made. Corrections shall be made by drawing a line of ink through the entry without destroying its legibility.
(Ord. No. 764, § 9, 10-1-1980)
Sec. 13-329. - Same—Filing with city; inspection.
The complete daily record of each day's transactions shall be filed on or before the following day in the office of the city marshal and shall, prior to filing, be open to the inspection of any duly authorized law enforcement officer during the ordinary hours of business or at any reasonable time.
(Ord. No. 764, § 10, 10-1-1980)
Sec. 13-330. - Minimum retention period.
Every buyer of precious metals or gem stones shall retain possession of each article or item purchased in the city for at least ten days following each purchase. It shall be unlawful for any buyer of precious metals or gem stones to alter in any manner, or to transfer possession of, any article or item purchased in the city within a period of ten days or less after purchase. In the event that any article or item so purchased shall be removed from the city limits within a period of ten days after purchase, the buyer of precious metals or gem stones shall notify the city marshal, in writing, prior to such removal, of the precise location and address to which the article or item shall be removed and shall consent to inspection of the same by the city marshal or any duly authorized law enforcement officer, at such location.
(Ord. No. 764, § 11, 10-1-1980)
Sec. 13-331. - Unlawful acts.
It shall be unlawful for any buyer of precious metals or gem stones to commit any of the following acts:
(1)
Fail to make an entry of any material matter in his daily record.
(2)
Make any false entry therein.
(3)
Falsify, obliterate or destroy such daily record or remove the same from his place of business except to file the same in the office of the city marshal.
(4)
Refuse to allow any duly authorized law enforcement officer to inspect such daily record or any goods in his possession, during the ordinary hours of business or at any reasonable time.
(5)
Fail to file his daily record in the office of the city marshal.
(6)
Fail to comply with this article in any other particular.
(Ord. No. 764, § 13, 10-1-1980)

DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 13-346. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for a buyer of precious metals and gem stones to engage in such business within the city without first obtaining a license therefor in compliance with the provisions of this division.
(Ord. No. 764, § 1, 10-1-1980)
Sec. 13-347. - Application.
The applicants for a license pursuant to this division shall file with the city marshal a written application under oath, signed by the applicant if an individual, by all partners if a partnership, and by the president if a corporation and showing as follows:
(1)
The name or names of the person having the management or supervision of the applicant's business during the time that it is proposed that it will be carried on within the city; the local address or addresses of such person while engaged in such business; the permanent address or addresses of such person or persons; the capacity in which such person will act (that is, whether as proprietor, agent or otherwise); the name and address of the person for whose account the business will be carried on, if any; and if a corporation, the name of the state in which the same is incorporated.
(2)
The place or places within the city where it is proposed to carry on the applicant's business and the length of time during which it is proposed that such business will be conducted.
(3)
Credentials from the person for which the applicant proposes to do business, authorizing the applicant to act as representative.
(4)
Such other reasonable information as to the identity and character of the person or persons having the management or supervision of the business and the manner of doing business as the city marshal may deem proper in order to fulfill the purpose of this article in the protection of the public interest.
(Ord. No. 764, § 3, 10-1-1980)
Sec. 13-348. - Bond.
Before any license shall be issued pursuant to this division, the applicant shall file with the city marshal a bond binding the applicant to the city in the full sum of $10,000.00, executed by the applicant as principal and by a surety approved by the city marshal, conditioned that the applicant shall comply fully with the ordinances of the city and the statutes of the state governing the conduct of such business and will pay all judgments rendered against the applicant for any violation of such ordinances or statutes and all judgments that may be recovered against the applicant by any person or persons for damages arising out of the conduct of such business. Action on the bond may be brought in the name of the city in its own behalf or for the use of any aggrieved person.
(Ord. No. 764, § 4, 10-1-1980)
Sec. 13-349. - Agent for service of process.
Before any license shall be issued pursuant to this division, the applicant shall file with the city marshal an instrument appointing the secretary of the city commission as his true and lawful agent for service of process upon the applicant in any suit or legal proceeding arising out of the business transacted pursuant to the license applied for or for the performance of the conditions of the required bond or for any breach thereof. Immediately upon service of process upon the secretary of the commission as herein provided, the secretary shall send to the licensee at his last-known address, by registered mail, a copy of such process.
(Ord. No. 764, § 5, 10-1-1980)
Sec. 13-350. - Display; separate licenses for separate places of business.
The license issued pursuant to this division shall be posted conspicuously in the place of business named therein. In the event that the applicant shall desire to do business in more than one place within the city, separate licenses shall be purchased and issued for each place of business, and shall be posted conspicuously in each place of business.
(Ord. No. 764, § 6, 10-1-1980)
Sec. 13-351. - License fees.
Licensees under this division shall pay a license fee of $300.00 for each calendar year or portion thereof in which the licensee shall engage in business within the city. For each business location in addition to one, the licensee shall pay an additional license fee of $75.00 for each calendar year or portion of year in which the licensee conducts business at such additional location or locations.
(Ord. No. 764, § 7, 10-1-1980)
Sec. 13-352. - Revocation.
(a)
The license issued pursuant to this division may be revoked by the city commission, after notice and hearing, for any of the following causes:
(1)
Any fraud, misrepresentation or false statement contained in the application for license.
(2)
Any fraud, misrepresentation or false statement made in connection with any purchase transaction.
(3)
Failure to maintain the daily record required by this article.
(4)
Any other violation of this article.
(5)
Conducting the business licensed under this article in an unlawful manner or in such a manner as to constitute a breach of the peace or to constitute a menace to the health, safety or general welfare of the public.
(b)
Notice of hearing for revocation of a license shall be given in writing, setting forth specifically the grounds of the complaint and the time and place of the hearing. The notice shall be served on the licensee by handing the same personally to the person in charge of the licensee's business, or by mailing the same, postage prepaid, to the licensee at his last-known address at least five days prior to the date set for the hearing.
(c)
The giving of such notice shall suspend the license pending the outcome of the hearing, and business operations under the license shall cease.
(Ord. No. 764, § 12, 10-1-1980)
