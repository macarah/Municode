ARTICLE III. - BOARD OF CONSTRUCTION ADJUSTMENTS AND APPEALS

Sec. 10-52. - Creation.
There is hereby created a city board of construction adjustments and appeals. Said board shall be constituted of the members of the county board of construction adjustments and appeals.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 14-46)
Sec. 10-53. - Purpose.
The purpose of the board of construction adjustments and appeals shall be to:
(1)
Review and decide specific appeals made by individuals concerning the interpretation or administration of the county construction code which includes by reference the state minimum standard codes.
(2)
Review and decide specific appeals made by individuals concerning the interpretation or administration of the county fire prevention and protection code, the International Fire Code, and the National Fire Prevention Codes for every structure and occupancy constructed, with the exception of those buildings, structures and uses specified in O.C.G.A. § 25-2-12(a)(2). The uses specified in the subsection of the state fire safety law are hospitals, nursing homes, jails, ambulatory healthcare centers, penal institutions and any structure which is owned, operated or occupied by the state. Requests for variances or waivers concerning buildings, structures and uses specified in such subsection must be submitted in writing to the county fire marshal who shall make a written comment regarding the request and shall forward all documentation to the state fire marshal's office for a decision. The county fire marshal shall ensure that any applicant granted a variance by the state fire marshal is provided with a written response of the decision.
(3)
Advise the board of commissioners, the department of planning and development, and the department of fire and emergency services on issues and matters of concern regarding the codes described in this section.
(4)
Review and decide upon specific appeals of interpretation by the director and waiver requests from the stormwater requirements of articles VI, VII or VIII of the development regulations.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 14-47)
Sec. 10-54. - Appointment; qualifications.
The board of construction adjustments and appeals shall consist of eight members representing or having the following qualifications:
(1)
One barrier free county representative.
(2)
One registered architect.
(3)
One registered civil engineer.
(4)
One registered electrical engineer.
(5)
One registered fire protection engineer.
(6)
One registered mechanical engineer.
(7)
One registered structural engineer.
(8)
One general contracting industry representative.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 14-48)
Sec. 10-55. - Terms of office.
Members shall be appointed to serve two calendar year terms. Appointments shall be effective January 1 of the first year of term. Members shall serve until replaced, except that any member who is absent without cause from two consecutive meetings of the board shall be automatically terminated from his appointment.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 14-49)
Sec. 10-56. - Initial appointment terms.
In order to establish permanent staggered terms, initial appointment terms shall be as follows:
(1)
Two-year initial appointment (Group One membership) shall include:
a.
One barrier free county representative.
b.
One registered architect.
c.
One registered civil engineer.
d.
One registered electrical engineer.
(2)
One-year initial appointment (Group Two membership) shall include:
a.
One registered fire protection engineer.
b.
One registered mechanical engineer.
c.
One registered structural engineer.
d.
One general contracting industry representative.
(3)
Following the board of construction adjustments and appeals first year, all terms shall be a two-year appointment.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 14-50)
Sec. 10-57. - Replacements.
A member appointed to fill a vacancy which occurs during the year shall be appointed to fill the unexpired term of the vacant position for the remainder of the calendar year.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 14-51)
Sec. 10-58. - Compensation.
Members shall be compensated as established by the board of commissioners. Normal costs of operations, such as meeting space, minutes, etc., shall be borne by the department of planning and development.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 14-52)
Sec. 10-59. - Limitation.
All members shall be residents of the county. Employees of the county government shall not be members.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 14-53)
Sec. 10-60. - Officers.
Members shall elect from among them a chair and a vice-chair. The chair and vice-chair shall be elected to serve one-year terms. The term of office shall begin immediately upon election.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 14-54)
Sec. 10-61. - Quorum.
Five members of the board of adjustments and appeals shall constitute a quorum.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 14-55)
Sec. 10-62. - Voting.
In varying the application of any provision of the codes or in modifying an order of the director of the department of planning and development or the director of the department of fire and emergency services, affirmative votes of the majority present, but not less than three affirmative votes, shall be required. A tie vote shall be deemed as a denial of a motion. A motion which fails by a majority vote shall not be deemed as approval of the opposite position, and a new motion must be made to approve the opposite position.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 14-56)
Sec. 10-63. - Conflict of interest.
If a board member has any interest in any matter which may result in a gain or loss to that board member, his immediate family by blood or by marriage, or to any individual, partnership or corporation with whom that member has had any regular business or contractual relationships within the previous 12 months, such member shall not participate in the consideration, discussion, questioning and voting on that particular matter before the board, nor shall the member take any action which may influence the vote of any other member. If the chair has a conflict of interest, the vice-chair shall preside over the meeting during consideration of that particular matter.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 14-57)
Sec. 10-64. - Records.
The director of the department of planning and development and the director of the department of fire and emergency services, or their designee, shall act as secretary of the board of construction adjustments and appeals on a rotating annual basis. The designated secretary shall direct the preparation of a detailed record of all proceedings, which shall set forth the reasons for the board's decision, the vote of each member participating therein, the absence of a member, and any failure of a member to vote.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 14-58)
Sec. 10-65. - Procedure.
The board of construction adjustments and appeals shall establish rules and regulations for its own procedure consistent with the provisions of the codes. Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 25-2-12(a)(7), the board shall not have the authority to grant any variance or waiver which relieves any building, structure or use or the proposed plans from compliance with state minimum fire safety standards.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 14-59)
Sec. 10-66. - Meetings.
(a)
Regular meetings. Regular meetings of the board of construction adjustments and appeals shall be once a month unless the chair determines that there is a lack of business to act upon during a particular month.
(b)
Special called meetings. The chair or a majority of the board of construction adjustments and appeals may call a special meeting at any time. The secretary shall prepare and cause to be delivered written notice stating the specific purpose of the special called meeting to each board member. No business other than the specific stated purpose shall occur at the special called meeting.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 14-60)
Sec. 10-67. - Appeals.
(a)
Administrative decisions.
(1)
The owner of a building, structure or service system, or his duly authorized agent, shall have the right to appeal an administrative decision made by the director of the department of planning and development or the director of the department of fire and emergency services, as appropriate, to the board of construction adjustments and appeals regarding any of the following:
a.
The rejection or refusal to approve the mode or manner of construction proposed to be followed or materials to be used in the installation or alteration of a building, structure or service system.
b.
Whether or not the provisions of the codes apply to a specific case.
c.
Whether or not an equally good or more desirable form of installation may be employed in a specific case.
d.
Whether or not the true intent or meaning of the codes, or any of the regulations pursuant to such codes, have been misconstrued or incorrectly interpreted.
(2)
In order to appeal an administrative decision, the applicant shall file a notice of appeal which shall be in writing. The notice of appeal shall be filed within 30 days after the decision being appealed is rendered by the applicable director.
(3)
A fee as may be established from time to time shall accompany such notice of appeal.
(4)
Appeals filed pursuant to this section shall be made on forms provided by the department of planning and development or the department of fire and emergency services, as applicable.
(5)
In the case of a building or structure which, in the opinion of the director of the department of planning and development or the director of the department of fire and emergency services, as applicable, is unsafe or dangerous, the director of the department of planning and development or the director of the department of fire and emergency services may, in his order, limit the time for such appeal to a shorter period.
(b)
Variances.
(1)
The board of construction adjustments and appeals, upon specific appeal and after a hearing, may vary the application of any provision of the codes to a specific case when, in their opinion, the enforcement thereof would do manifest injustice and would be contrary to the spirit and purpose of the codes or the public interest and upon a finding by the board of any of the following:
a.
That special conditions and circumstances exist which are peculiar to the building, structure or service system involved and which are not applicable to others.
b.
That the special conditions and circumstances are not the result of an action or inaction of the applicant.
c.
That granting the variance will not confer on the applicant any special privilege that is denied by the codes to other buildings, structures or service system.
d.
That the variance granted is the minimum variance that will make possible the reasonable use of the building, structure or service system.
e.
That the grant of the variance will be consistent with the general intent and purpose of the codes and will not be detrimental to the public health, safety and general welfare.
(2)
A fee as may be established from time to time shall accompany each variance application.
(3)
Variance applications filed pursuant to this section shall be made on forms provided by the department of planning and development or the department of fire and emergency services, as applicable.
(4)
In granting a variance, the board may prescribe a reasonable time limit within which the action for which the variance is required shall be commenced, completed or both. In addition, the board may prescribe appropriate conditions and safeguards in conformance with the codes. Violation of the conditions of the variance shall be deemed a violation of this article and the codes.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 14-61)
Sec. 10-68. - Decisions to be final and without delay.
(a)
Every decision of the board of construction adjustments and appeals shall be final, subject, however, to such remedy an aggrieved party might have at law or in equity. Every decision shall be in writing and shall indicate the vote upon the decision. Every decision shall be promptly filed in the offices of both the department of planning and development and the department of fire and emergency services and shall be open to public inspection. A copy of the decision shall be sent by mail or otherwise to the appellant, and a copy shall be publicly posted in the offices of the department of planning and development or the department of fire and emergency services, as appropriate, for two weeks after filing.
(b)
The board of construction adjustments and appeals shall, in every case, reach a decision without unreasonable or unnecessary delay.
(Ord. of 7-1-2012, § 14-62)
