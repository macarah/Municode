Chapter 86 - TRAFFIC AND VEHICLES[1]
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 86-1. - State laws adopted; application; penalties; amendment or repeal.
(a)
Adoption. Pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-372 through 40-6-376, O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-1 through 40-6-397 of that chapter, known as the Uniform Rules of the Road, and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 are hereby adopted as and for traffic regulations of the city with like effect as if recited in this subsection.
(b)
Application. The provisions of this chapter relating to the operation of vehicles refer to the operation of vehicles upon highways or streets of the city, and as follows:
(1)
Except where a different place is specifically referred to in a given section.
(2)
The provisions of this section shall apply to a vehicle operated at shopping centers or parking lots or similar areas which, although privately owned, are customarily used by the public as a through street or connector street.
(3)
The provisions relating to reckless driving and driving while under the influence of intoxicating liquors or drugs shall apply to vehicles operated upon highways or streets and elsewhere throughout the city.
(c)
Violations of section; penalties. Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-12 of this Code.
(d)
Amendment or repeal. A future amendment or repeal of any provision of O.C.G.A. § 40-6-1 et seq., Uniform Rules of the Road, shall amend or repeal the pertinent provision of this section.
(Code 1982, § 21-1)
Sec. 86-2. - Boarding and alighting from vehicles—Generally.
Persons entering or leaving a vehicle shall do so on the side nearest the curb.
(Code 1982, § 21-2)
Sec. 86-3. - Boarding and alighting from vehicles—Vehicles discharging passengers.
Any passenger vehicle, consisting of any sort of vehicle conveying passengers for compensation or otherwise, shall, before discharging passengers, drive as near to the right-hand curb of the street as possible to allow passengers to alight from such passenger vehicle at or as near the curb of the street as possible and in such area that it will be impossible for any meeting or following vehicle to pass between such passenger vehicle discharging passengers and the right-hand curb on the street.
(Code 1982, § 21-3)
Sec. 86-4. - Boarding and alighting from vehicles—Vehicles in motion.
No person shall board or alight from any vehicle while such vehicle is in motion, except that railway workers in the course of their employment in switching or otherwise operating or controlling cars on rails may do so.
(Code 1982, § 21-4)
Sec. 86-5. - Riding on portions of vehicles not intended for passengers.
No person shall ride on any portion of any vehicle not designed or intended for the use of passengers. Such prohibition shall not apply to an employee engaged in the necessary discharge of a duty, or to persons riding in truck bodies in space intended for merchandise.
(Code 1982, § 21-5)
Sec. 86-6. - Improperly loaded vehicles.
No driver shall operate upon any street in the city a vehicle which is overloaded, or which is loaded in such a manner that articles or freight on the vehicle is likely to fall therefrom, or which is so loaded that the brakes of such vehicle will not stop the vehicle within the distances specified by law when the brakes are properly applied.
(Code 1982, § 21-6)
Sec. 86-7. - Passengers on motor scooters and motorbikes.
It shall be unlawful for more than one person to ride upon a motor scooter or motorbike. Upon a violation of this section, both the operator and passenger shall be guilty of such violation.
(Code 1982, § 21-7)
Sec. 86-8. - Obstruction of traffic by use of streets.
No person shall occupy or use any street, lane, alley or way so as to interfere with or interrupt the usage thereof by other vehicles.
(Code 1982, § 21-8)
Cross reference— Streets, sidewalks and other public places, ch. 78.
State Law reference— Obstruction of highways, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-43.
Sec. 86-9. - Standing in street to hitchhike or solicit.
(a)
No person shall stand in the street for the purpose of soliciting a ride.
(b)
No person shall stand on a street for the purpose of soliciting employment, business or contributions from the occupant of any vehicle.
(Code 1982, § 21-9)
Cross reference— Peddlers and soliciting, ch. 66; streets, sidewalks and other public places, ch. 78.
Sec. 86-10. - Shrubbery and other hazards at street corners; duty of property owner.
It shall be the duty of every property owner to keep weeds as well as shrubbery on their respective lots in such a condition as not to create traffic hazards at street corners. Shrubbery, vines, trees or other things which would obstruct the view of a person passing a street corner or street intersection shall be kept in a condition so as not to obstruct the view of motorists using the streets.
(Code 1982, § 21-10)
Cross reference— Streets, sidewalks and other public places, ch. 78.
Sec. 86-11. - Truck routes.
The driver of a motor truck of a tractor-trailer type or of a weight of 2½ tons or more shall not drive such vehicle on any of the streets in the city other than the streets designated by an official sign indicating and designating such streets as truck routes; provided, however, that this section shall not apply to trucks engaged in delivery of personal property to customers at specific locations within the city.
(Code 1982, § 21-10)
Sec. 86-12. - Jake brake prohibited.
The use of exhaust brakes, exhaust retarders and Jake brake usage is prohibited. It shall be unlawful for the operator of any vehicle equipped with an engine speed governor, also known as a Jake brake, to use or otherwise apply such device within the city. Any violation of this article shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00.
(Res. No. 10-0218-12, 2-18-2010)
Secs. 86-13—86-40. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - ADMINISTRATION AND ENFORCEMENT[2]
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 86-41. - Responsibility for operation of vehicles.
It shall be unlawful for the owner or any other person employing or otherwise directing the driver of any vehicle to require or knowingly permit the operation of such vehicle upon a street or highway in any manner contrary to this chapter.
(Code 1982, § 21-20)
Sec. 86-42. - Duties and authority of police officers.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the police officers on traffic duty at intersections or heavily congested traffic zones to direct traffic so as to expedite such traffic and, to this end, it shall be the duty of police officers, where it can be safely done, to direct traffic through intersections controlled by traffic signal lights without regard to the signal light, so that when a red traffic signal light is holding traffic at a street intersection the traffic shall be released and directed to move forward when there is little or no traffic at or approaching such intersection on a green light; and in extremely heavily congested areas such police officer shall have the power and authority to direct traffic without regard to such automatic traffic signal light, even though there is substantial traffic on both or all of such intersecting streets at or near and approaching such intersection.
(b)
The object and purpose of this section is to eliminate traffic bottlenecks caused by red lights facing heavy traffic while a green light on the intersecting street is of no benefit or of small benefit because of the light traffic on such intersecting street.
(Code 1982, § 21-21)
Cross reference— Law enforcement, ch. 54.
State Law reference— Authority to arrest for traffic violation, O.C.G.A. § 40-13-30.
Sec. 86-43. - Temporary regulations.
The chief of police may establish temporary traffic regulations which the public must obey after being warned. Verbal warning by an authorized police officer of the city, or by printed signs erected in the area affected, shall constitute sufficient notice to the public of all temporary traffic regulations.
(Code 1982, § 21-22)
State Law reference— Temporary or experimental regulations authorized, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-371(a)(19).
Secs. 86-44—86-60. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - ACCIDENTS

Sec. 86-61. - Death and personal injuries.
The driver of any vehicle involved in an accident resulting in injury to, or death of, any person shall immediately stop such vehicle at the scene of such accident or as close thereto as possible, in which latter case such driver shall then forthwith return to, and in every event shall remain at, the scene of the accident until such driver has fulfilled the requirements set forth in section 86-63. Every such stop shall be made without obstructing traffic more than is necessary.
(Code 1982, § 21-30)
Sec. 86-62. - Damage to vehicle.
The driver of any vehicle involved in an accident resulting only in damage to a vehicle which is driven or attended by any person shall immediately stop such vehicle at the scene of such accident or as close thereto as possible, in which latter case such driver shall forthwith return to, and in every event shall remain at, the scene of such accident until such driver has fulfilled the requirements of section 86-63. Every such stop shall be made without obstructing traffic more than is necessary.
(Code 1982, § 21-31)
Sec. 86-63. - Duty to give information and render aid.
The driver of any vehicle involved in an accident resulting in injury to, or death of, any person or damage to any vehicle which is driven or attended by any person shall give such driver's name and address and the registration number of the vehicle such person is driving and shall, upon request, and if available, exhibit such driver's or operator's license to the person struck or the driver or occupant of, or person attending, any vehicle collided with, and shall render to any person injured in such accident reasonable assistance, including the transporting, or making arrangements for the transporting, of such person to a physician, surgeon or hospital for medical or surgical treatment, if it is apparent that such treatment is necessary or if such transporting is requested by the injured person.
(Code 1982, § 21-32)
Sec. 86-64. - Striking unattended vehicle; duty of driver.
The driver of any vehicle which collides with any vehicle which is unattended shall immediately stop and shall, then and there, either locate and notify the operator or owner of such vehicle of the name and address of the driver and owner of the vehicle striking the unattended vehicle, or shall leave in a conspicuous place on the vehicle struck a written notice giving the name and address of the driver and of the owner of the vehicle striking the unattended vehicle.
(Code 1982, § 21-33)
Sec. 86-65. - Striking fixtures; duty of driver.
The driver of any vehicle involved in an accident resulting only in damage to a fixture legally upon or adjacent to a street or highway shall take reasonable steps to locate and notify the owner or person in charge of such property of such fact and of such driver's name and address and of the registration number of the vehicle such driver is driving, and shall, upon request, and if available, exhibit such driver's or operator's license.
(Code 1982, § 21-34)
Sec. 86-66. - Accident report to be given to police.
The driver of a vehicle involved in an accident occurring within the city resulting in injury to, or death of, any person or property damage to an apparent extent of $500.00 or more shall immediately give notice to the police department of such accident by the quickest means of communication.
(Code 1982, § 21-35)
Cross reference— Law enforcement, ch. 54.
State Law reference— Accident reports, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-273 et seq.
Sec. 86-67. - Interfering with property involved in accidents.
It shall be unlawful to change, alter, carry away or interfere in any manner with any property or thing involved in any vehicle accident within the city referred to in section 86-66 until a police officer of the city shall have investigated such accident.
(Code 1982, § 21-36)

Sec. 86-103. - Towing another vehicle.
No vehicle shall pull more than one other vehicle, and the connection between such vehicles shall not exceed 16 feet.
(Code 1982, § 21-42)
Sec. 86-104. - U-turns.
The driver of any vehicle shall not turn such vehicle in order to proceed in the opposite direction upon any street in a business district or any street between intersections.
(Code 1982, § 21-43)
Sec. 86-105. - Damaging streets.
No vehicle shall be operated in such a manner as to damage the streets.
(Code 1982, § 21-44)
Cross reference— Streets, sidewalks and other public places, ch. 78.
Sec. 86-106. - Vehicles discharging passengers.
When any passenger vehicle is discharging passengers, other vehicles following or meeting such passenger vehicle may pass such vehicle discharging passengers only on the left side of such passenger vehicle discharging passengers, and shall pass the passenger vehicle discharging passengers at such moderate and reasonable speed that the driver of such passenger vehicle may stop the vehicle immediately if any passenger discharged from such passenger vehicle discharging passengers attempts to go around such passenger vehicle to the opposite side of the street by going around the front of such passenger vehicle discharging such person or by going around behind such passenger vehicle in which he has been riding so that no person discharged from any passenger vehicle will be negligently run down, run over or injured by any other vehicle passing, approaching or following the vehicle discharging such passengers.
(Code 1982, § 21-45)
Sec. 86-107. - Designation of stop intersections.
The mayor and city council or the police committee may from time to time designate intersections or other places at which vehicles on a certain street shall be required to stop and yield the right-of-way to other vehicles or pedestrians. Whenever any such intersection is designated as a stop intersection, the chief of police shall cause suitable signs, signals or markings giving notice thereof to be erected and maintained, and it shall be unlawful for any person to fail to comply with any such sign, signal or marking.
(Code 1982, § 21-47)

ARTICLE IV. - STOPPING, STANDING AND PARKING[3]

Sec. 86-141. - Illegal parking; enforcement of regulations.
(a)
Attachment of notice to vehicle. When it is impossible or impracticable to personally serve the owner, operator or person in charge of any illegally parked vehicle within the city with a copy of charges, a notice shall be attached to the vehicle in such a manner as is consistent with the rules and regulations of the state board of public safety.
(b)
Removal of notice. It shall be unlawful to remove any notice described in subsection (a) of this section attached to any illegally parked vehicle unless and until the offender shall have reported to the police department in accordance with such notice.
(c)
Removal of vehicle. It shall be unlawful for any person to remove any illegally parked vehicle from the place where the vehicle was illegally parked, except to report to the police station in compliance with the notice attached to such vehicle.
(d)
Impounding authorized. Whenever any vehicle shall be found parked in violation of this chapter or any city ordinance, it is subject to be impounded by the police department. Any vehicle impounded pursuant to this subsection shall remain impounded until all towing costs incurred by the city are paid by the owner or driver of such vehicle.
(e)
Liability of owner. The owner of a vehicle shall be equally liable with the operator for parking violations.
(Code 1982, § 21-50)
State Law reference— Removal of illegally stopped vehicles regulated, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-206.
Sec. 86-142. - Methods.
(a)
Except when necessary in obedience to traffic regulations or traffic signs or signals, the operator of a vehicle shall not stop, stand or park such vehicle in a roadway other than parallel with the edge of the roadway, headed in the direction of traffic, and with the curbside wheels of the vehicle within six inches of the edge of the roadway, except as follows:
(1)
Upon the streets which have been marked or signed for angle parking, vehicles shall be parked at the angle to the curb indicated by such marks or signs.
(2)
In places where, and at hours when, stopping for the loading or unloading of merchandise or materials is permitted, vehicles may back into the curb to take on or discharge loads.
(b)
Angle parking must conform to, and be within, the division lines.
(Code 1982, § 21-51)
Sec. 86-143. - Bicycles.
It shall be unlawful for any person to park a bicycle upon a street other than upon the roadway against the curb, or upon the sidewalk in a rack to support the bicycle, or against a building.
(Code 1982, § 21-52)
Sec. 86-144. - Parking for washing, greasing, repairing and advertising.
It shall be unlawful for any person to park any vehicle upon any street for the purpose of washing, greasing or repairing the vehicle or for the purpose of displaying advertising.
(Code 1982, § 21-53)
Cross reference— Advertising, ch. 6.
Sec. 86-145. - Parking in excess of 24 hours on public streets.
Any vehicle parked on a public street for over 24 hours shall be presumed to be abandoned and may be impounded by the police department. Any vehicle impounded by the police department by reason of a presumption of abandonment on account of parking such vehicle for a period exceeding 24 hours may be redeemed by the owner or his agent by paying towing and storage charges for such vehicle impoundment.
(Code 1982, § 21-54)
Sec. 86-146. - Parking of trucks.
(a)
Regulated. No person shall stop, stand or park a truck on a public street, except while actually engaged in the loading or unloading of merchandise.
(b)
Nighttime in business districts. The parking of trucks having a capacity of more than one-half ton on any street within the business district shall be prohibited for more than one hour between the hours of 6:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m., unless the truck is in charge of an awake attendant during all hours, and such parked truck shall be properly lighted.
(c)
Daytime. It shall be unlawful to park trucks in excess of one-half ton for more than one hour from 6:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m.
(d)
Nighttime in residential areas. It shall be unlawful to park or stand any truck of a weight in excess of 1½-ton capacity or any other freight conveyance of a weight in excess of 1½-ton capacity, except authorized emergency vehicles, on any city street within the residential area of the city between the hours of 7:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m. for a period of time longer than one-half hour.
(Code 1982, § 21-55)
Sec. 86-147. - Trucks containing flammable gas or hazardous material.
Except for the purpose of legitimate deliveries, all trucks containing flammable gas or any other hazardous material of any form must be parked in private parking lots and not on streets, public property or alleyways within the city.
(Code 1982, § 21-56)
Cross reference— Environment, ch. 38.
Sec. 86-148. - Driver to be present at parked trucks and buses of certain sizes.
It shall be unlawful to park a truck or bus with a body of more than eight feet wide or ten feet high on a public street without the driver actually being present.
(Code 1982, § 21-57)
Sec. 86-149. - Trailers.
It shall be unlawful to keep or park any trailer on any public street or other public place, or on any private property within the city, except that one trailer may be parked or stored in a private garage constructed and maintained in accordance with the provisions of the building code of the city in relation to accessory buildings constructed on the rear of residential building lots and also pursuant to building permits granted by the mayor and city council upon application for such permits; provided, however, that the mayor and city council may grant a permit for the parking of a trailer on private property, outside of such a garage, for a period not exceeding 90 days, upon application for such permit, where all residents within an area of 500 feet in all directions from the location of the property of the proposed parking of such trailer shall first consent thereto in writing to the mayor and city council.
(Code 1982, § 21-58)
Sec. 86-150. - Loading zones.
It shall be unlawful for any person to stand or park a vehicle in a passenger or freight loading zone, except while actually engaged in loading or unloading passengers or freight.
(Code 1982, § 21-59)
Sec. 86-151. - Parking in front of city hall.
It shall be unlawful for any person to stand or park a vehicle in front of city hall, except while actually engaged in loading or unloading passengers or freight.
(Ord. of 9-18-1997)
Secs. 86-152—86-180. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - RAILROADS

Sec. 86-181. - Compliance required.
It shall be unlawful for any conductor having charge of a train, or engineer having charge of a locomotive, to violate any provision of this article.
(Code 1982, § 21-70)
Sec. 86-182. - Unnecessary whistle blowing.
It shall be unlawful for any locomotive engineer or other person to unnecessarily blow the whistle of any railroad locomotive within the city.
(Code 1982, § 21-72)
Sec. 86-183. - Grade crossings.
It shall be unlawful for any railroad company whose road crosses any of the streets in the city to fail, neglect or refuse, upon being notified, to have suitable crossings made and kept in good repair at the different points where such roads cross the streets, alleys, ways and public roads in the city, or for any railroad company to refuse, neglect or fail to put in good traveling order all roads in the city turned or changed for the benefit of such railroad.
(Code 1982, § 21-73)
