ARTICLE II. - POLICE DEPARTMENT
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 54-31. - Personnel matters; responsibility and authority.
The chief of police and police committee, acting together, shall have sole responsibility to make recommendations of employment and dismissal of police department personnel and salary schedules to the mayor and city council, which shall, in session, set salaries, make employments and declare dismissals.
(Code 1982, § 17-30)
Sec. 54-32. - Employment of police authorized.
The mayor and city council are hereby authorized to employ a sufficient number of police officers as may be deemed necessary from time to time.
(Code 1982, § 17-31)
Sec. 54-33. - Knowledge of rules, regulations, laws and ordinances.
All police officers shall acquire a full and complete knowledge of the rules and regulations provided for the government of the police department and with the sections of this Code and the laws and ordinances of the city in order that they may enforce such rules, regulations, laws and ordinances.
(Code 1982, § 17-32)
Sec. 54-34. - Oath; duties.
The police officers employed by the city shall take the oath of office prescribed for the chief of police. Such police officers shall perform the same duties as the chief of police and such other duties as may be required by the mayor and city council, but shall remain subordinate to the chief of police.
(Code 1982, § 17-33)
Sec. 54-35. - Disposition of lost or stolen property.
All property coming into the possession of the police, and which is suspected to be lost or stolen, shall be deposited with the chief of police for safekeeping.
(Code 1982, § 17-35)

DIVISION 2. - CHIEF OF POLICE[2]

Sec. 54-61. - Appointment.
There shall be appointed annually an officer known as the chief of police, who shall hold office at the pleasure of the mayor and city council.
(Code 1982, § 2-60)
Charter reference— Appointment of marshal, § 18.
Sec. 54-62. - Oath; bond.
(a)
Before entering upon the duties of the office, the chief of police shall take the following oath:
"I do solemnly swear that I will faithfully perform all the duties imposed on me by the laws and ordinances of the city as chief of police of said city."
(b)
The chief of police shall give the bond prescribed by the mayor and city council, conditioned on the faithful performance of such duties.
(Code 1982, § 2-61)
Sec. 54-63. - Duties.
The chief of police shall have the responsibility for preserving peace, good order, safety and tranquility in the city; superintend, control and direct the members of the police department; and shall carry out the orders and directions of the mayor, the courts and the police committee about matters in which they severally have jurisdiction under the ordinances of the city.
(Code 1982, § 2-62)
Charter reference— Duties of marshal, §§ 36, 37; chief of police or designee to act as clerk of court, § 15(d).
Sec. 54-64. - Reports; arrests; prosecution of offenders.
The chief of police shall report, arrest and prosecute all offenders against the laws and ordinances of the city as shall come to his knowledge, and cause his subordinates to do the same.
(Code 1982, § 2-63)
Sec. 54-65. - Attendance at meetings of mayor and city council and sessions of municipal court.
(a)
The chief of police shall attend all meetings of the mayor and city council, when required.
(b)
The chief of police shall also attend the sessions of the municipal court as clerk of such court, keep a police docket in which shall be entered the names of the parties, the general nature of the offenses, the disposition of the cases and the amounts of the fines, and perform such other duties in connection with the municipal court as may be required of such officer.
(Code 1982, § 2-64)
Charter reference— Chief of police or designee to act as clerk of court, § 15(d).
Cross reference— Municipal court, § 34-31 et seq.
Sec. 54-66. - Care of prisoners.
The chief of police shall be responsible for the care and well-being of all prisoners confined in the city jail.
(Code 1982, § 2-65)
Sec. 54-67. - Reports.
The chief of police shall make the following reports:
(1)
The property received from his predecessor, which report shall be made at the first meeting after the chief of police has been installed as such officer.
(2)
A full report at the close of the year of all property received during the year, and of all property on hand at the close of the year turned over to his successor.
(Code 1982, § 2-66)
Sec. 54-68. - Record of property.
The chief of police shall keep a book in which he shall enter a schedule of all city property turned over to him by his predecessor and all such property as he may receive from other sources.
(Code 1982, § 2-67)
Sec. 54-69. - Delivery of property to successor.
It shall be the duty of the chief of police, at the expiration of his term, to turn over to his successor, by schedule, all writs, executions or unexecuted processes remaining in the chief of police's hands; all books, records and other papers and property belonging to the office or the city under the chief of police's control; and such persons as shall be confined under the chief of police's charge, with the cause of detention for each; and, upon failure to do as required in this section within five days after the expiration of the term of office, the chief of police shall be punished as for a misdemeanor.
(Code 1982, § 2-68)
