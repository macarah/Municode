ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 50-1. - Solid waste management plan.
(a)
Findings.
(1)
Coffee County and the cities of Ambrose, Broxton, Douglas and Nicholls jointly participated in the development and submission of their solid waste management plan, as required by the Georgia Solid Waste Management Act, O.C.G.A. § 12-8-20 et seq.
(2)
The county solid waste management plan, in compliance with the minimum standards and procedures of the Georgia Comprehensive Planning Act of 1989, has been reviewed by the Southeast Georgia Regional Development Center, forwarded to the state department of community affairs, and has been approved as being in compliance with the comprehensive planning act.
(b)
Adopted. The board of commissioners authorizes the adoption of the county solid waste management plan as amended, submitted to, and approved by the state department of community affairs.
(Code 2002, § 50-1; Res. of 5-3-1993)
Sec. 50-2. - Regulation of landfills and solid waste collection and recycling centers.
(a)
No landfill shall be operated in the county, other than a landfill designated by the county board of commissioners as the county landfill. No private municipal solid waste landfill shall be operated in the county, without a solid waste handling permit issued by the state environmental protection division.
(b)
No person shall deposit solid waste of any kind outside the gate of a solid waste collection and recycling center.
(c)
No scavenging operation of any kind shall be allowed at a solid waste collection and recycling center.
(d)
No person shall move, remove, or cross any fence, gate, barrier, or sign at a solid waste collection and recycling center.
(e)
The price on solid waste brought to a center will be set to defray total cost of solid waste and recycling operations.
(f)
The hours of operation of the centers will be set to maximize convenience to residents of that area.
(g)
All recovered materials (recyclables) brought to collection centers will be accepted free of charge; provided it is separated under current market specifications and likewise uncontaminated.
(h)
All collection centers will be manned during hours of operation to ensure compliance with this chapter and all applicable state and/or federal laws.
(i)
The attendant on duty will have full authority of the center, consistent with this chapter.
(j)
All rules listed in this section shall apply to all public and/or private property in the county, all waters and/or waterways of the county, and all county solid waste collection and recycling centers.
(k)
Recovered materials and recovered materials processing facilities are subject to rules as defined in chapter 391-3-4 et seq., of the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management.
(Code 2002, § 50-2; Ord. No. 03-01, § 50-2, 2-3-2003; Res. No. 2007-14, 10-15-2007)

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 50-23. - Proper collection and disposal of refuse; inspection of refuse storage, containers, collection vehicles, and disposal facilities.
(a)
All collection vehicles, hauling wet or moist waste, must be sufficiently watertight.
(b)
All open-type vehicles must be covered when hauling refuse.
(c)
The owner or occupant of any premises must be responsible for the sanitary handling of garbage and refuse on the premises occupied by him.
(d)
It shall be unlawful to dispose of garbage and other refuse in a manner which would create a nuisance.
(e)
It shall be unlawful to throw refuse on or litter any public or private property.
(f)
Should the county provide a bulk containerized waste collection system, it is further resolved that:
(1)
Containers will be serviced and emptied at least one time each week.
(2)
Persons using the container shall deposit all garbage and other acceptable refuse into the container.
(3)
No person shall set fire to the contents of any container.
(4)
No person shall deposit any burning or smoldering material in any container.
(5)
No dead animals shall be deposited in any container.
(6)
No person shall deposit large noncompactable articles in containers such as stoves, refrigerators, bed springs, tires, large tree limbs or similar items.
(7)
No person shall deposit any flammable or explosive materials in any container.
(8)
No person shall willfully damage or alter the location of any container without the consent of the county commissioners.
(9)
It shall be unlawful to disturb or scatter refuse in and around any refuse containers.
(g)
Authority for refuse collection will be at the discretion of the governments of the county and the incorporated municipalities.
(Res. of 9-16-1974; Res. of 11-17-1986)
Sec. 50-24. - Purpose.
It is the principal objective of this article to attain a clean and litter-free community through education and voluntary compliance. Enforcement procedures shall be utilized only where other means fail.
(Code 2002, § 50-31; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 5.01)
Sec. 50-25. - Jurisdiction.
The provisions of this article shall be applicable in the unincorporated areas of the county. This article shall govern the accumulation, handling and disposal of all solid waste in the unincorporated areas of the county.
(Code 2002, § 50-32; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 5.02)
Sec. 50-26. - Administration and interpretations.
(a)
It is the responsibility of the chairman of the board of commissioners or his designee to administer the provisions of this article.
(b)
The chairman or his designee, upon written request, shall provide interpretations concerning provisions of this article which are unclear or in dispute.
(Code 2002, § 50-33; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 5.03)
Sec. 50-27. - Enforcement and penalties.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the board of commissioners to enforce this article. The nonpayment of fees provided for in this article shall constitute a lien against the real and personal property of the person responsible for the payment of the fees. Should any person liable for the payment of the fees fail to pay the fees within 90 days of having been billed, the chairman or his designee is authorized and may issue an execution against such person and deliver the execution to the sheriff for levy and sale as provided by law. The execution shall also be recorded in the general execution dockets in the clerk of superior court's office and a list of nonpays periodically advertised in the legal organ of the county. Further, the execution and/or fi. fa. may also be delivered to the magistrate court of the county for any lawful avenue of debt collection to be followed for collection of the outstanding bill, including the process of continuing garnishment.
(b)
Violations of the provisions of this article shall be deemed a misdemeanor, with the exception of failure to pay the monthly garbage bill, which shall be handled through a civil action, and shall constitute an offense triable in the magistrate court of the county, and punishable as provided in section 1-12. In addition, appropriate actions and proceedings may be taken by law or in equity to prevent any violations of this article; to recover damages; or to restrain, correct or abate a violation; and these remedies shall be in addition to the penalties previously described in this subsection.
(c)
Whenever litter is thrown, deposited, dropped or dumped from any motor vehicle, boat, airplane, wagon, wheelbarrow or other conveyance in violation of this article or the rules and regulations promulgated by the chairman of the board of commissioners, it shall be prima facie evidence that the operator of the conveyance has violated this article.
(d)
Whenever any litter which is dumped, deposited, thrown or left on public or private property, in violation of this article, is discovered to contain any articles, including but not limited to, letters, bills, publications, or other writings, which display the name of the person in such manner as to indicate that the article belongs to such person, it shall be a rebuttable presumption that such person has violated this article.
(e)
Where any section of this article contains specific remedies or punishments, such specific remedies or punishments shall not be exclusive but shall be in addition to all other remedies and punishments provided in this chapter or otherwise provided by law for the violation of or noncompliance with this article.
(f)
In addition to all punishments and penalties provided anywhere in this article, otherwise than by law, the magistrate's court may impose the following additional or alternative sentences, either as a direct sentence, or as a condition to probation:
(1)
The court may direct the convicted person to pick up and remove from any public street or highway or public right-of-way any and all litter deposited by anyone else prior to the date of the execution of the sentence; or
(2)
The court may direct the convicted person to pick up and remove from any public beach, public park, private right-of-way, or, with the prior permission of the legal owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property, any private property upon which it can be established by any competent evidence that the person has deposited litter, any and all litter deposited by anyone prior to the date of the execution of the sentence.
(Code 2002, § 50-34; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 5.04; Ord. No. 03-01, § 50-34(a), 2-3-2003; Ord. of 4-5-2004)
Sec. 50-28. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Ashes means the residue from the burning of wood, coal, coke or other combustible materials.
Bags means plastic sacks designed for refuse with sufficient wall strength to maintain physical integrity when lifted by the top with no support beneath, securely tied at the top for collection, with a capacity not to exceed 30 gallons and a loaded weight not to exceed 35 pounds.
Brown goods means bulky waste such as sofas, chairs, mattresses and the like.
Building material means any material defined as but not limited to lumber, brick, block, stone, carpet, plumbing materials, plaster, concrete, roofing, floor tile, gutters or other substances accumulated as a result of the construction of or repairs or additions to structures or accessory structures or the demolition of such.
Bulky waste means a large appliance, piece of furniture or waste material from a residential source other than construction debris or hazardous waste, with a weight or volume greater than that allowed for containers.
Commercial establishment means any establishment which operates a commercial business in the county. For the purposes of this article, such establishments are divided into the following classifications:
(1)
Individual commercial establishment, which means a commercial establishment not part of a group commercial establishment.
(2)
Group commercial establishment, which means a commercial building with more than one commercial establishment or spaces for more than one commercial establishment.
Container means a receptacle (not including front-loading containers) made of plastic, metal or fiberglass with a capacity not to exceed 100 gallons, a loaded weight of not more than 75 pounds, a tightfitting lid, and handles of adequate strength for lifting.
Disposal facility means any facility or location where the final deposition of solid waste occurs and includes, but is not limited to, landfilling and solid waste thermal treatment technology facilities.
Disposal site means a refuse depository for the processing or disposal of refuse, including but not limited to sanitary landfills, transfer stations, incinerators, and waste processing separation centers, licensed, permitted or approved by all governmental bodies and agencies having jurisdiction.
Dump means to throw, discard, place, deposit, discharge, bury, burn, or dispose of a substance.
Dwelling unit means any structure suitable or commonly used for residential purposes by a single-family or family group, and includes all single-family residences, including modular homes and mobile homes, and includes each apartment, townhouse, condominium or cooperative unit of a multifamily structure. All residences are included whether or not occupied, except mobile homes held as inventory on a sale lot by a state-licensed mobile home dealer.
Front-loading container means a metal receptacle that can be mechanically lifted into the county's or its designee's collection vehicles for disposal of the refuse contained in the container.
Garbage means putrescible animal or vegetable wastes resulting from the handling, preparation, cooking, serving or consumption of food, including food containers.
Group residential development means a building or development with one or more individual rental dwellings, units or lots, attached or detached, on one parcel of land. Examples of group residential developments include apartments and manufactured home parks.
Hazardous refuse means waste designated as hazardous by the United States Environmental Protection Agency, or appropriate state agency.
Industrial establishment means any establishment involved in the production of any goods.
Industrial waste means solid waste generated by manufacturing or industrial processes or operations. The term "industrial waste" includes, but is not limited to, waste resulting from the following manufacturing processes: electric power generation; fertilizer and agricultural chemicals; food and related products and byproducts; inorganic chemicals; iron and steel products; leather and leather products; nonferrous metal and foundry products; organic chemicals; plastics and resins; pulp and paper; rubber and miscellaneous plastic products; stone, glass, clay, and concrete products; textiles; transportation equipment; and water treatment. The term "industrial waste" does not include mining waste or oil and gas waste.
Inert waste means wastes that will not or are not likely to cause production of leachate of environmental concern. Such wastes are limited to earth and earthlike products, concrete, cured asphalt, rock, bricks, yard trimmings, stumps, limbs, and leaves. The term "inert waste" excludes industrial and demolition waste not specifically listed.
Litter means all discarded materials, including but not limited to garbage, household trash, commercial trash, industrial waste, building materials, appliances or portions, refuse, waste materials, sand, gravel, slag, brickbats, rubbish, debris, dead animals, or any other discarded or used substance which is not handled in accordance with the provisions of this article.
Manufactured home park shall be a lot or parcel of land under single ownership or control or design, and developed for the purpose of providing spaces for one or more manufactured homes intended to be used for temporary or permanent living facilities.
Open dump means a disposal facility at which solid waste from one or more sources is left to decompose, burn or to otherwise create a threat to human health or the environment.
Owner means the person listed on each annual assessment roll, even though the roll may not be current as to record title.
Public or private property means the right-of-way of any road or highway; any body of water or watercourse or the shores or beaches thereof; any park, playground, building, refuge, or conservation or recreation area; and residential or farm properties, timberlands, or forests.
Recovered material means those materials which have known use, reuse, or recycling potential; can be feasibly used, reused or recycled; and have been diverted or removed from the solid waste stream for sale, use, reuse or recycling, whether or not requiring subsequent separation and processing.
Recovered materials processing facility means a facility engaged solely in the storage, processing, and resale or reuse of recovered materials. The term "recovered materials processing facility" shall not include a solid waste handling facility; provided any solid waste generated by such facility shall be subject to all applicable laws and regulations relating to such solid waste.
Refuse means any solid waste derived from households, including garbage, trash, and sanitary waste in septic tanks and means solid waste from single-family and multifamily residences, hotels and motels, bunkhouses, campgrounds, picnic grounds, and day use recreation areas. The term "refuse" includes yard trimmings and commercial solid waste, but does not include solid waste from mining, agricultural, or silvicultural operations or industrial processes or operations.
Rental property means that property rented or leased by a property owner to another, with or without a written agreement, thereby creating a landlord/tenant relationship, irrespective of the actual payment of money or services to the owner for use of the property by the tenant.
Residential unit means a dwelling or occupied living space.
Retail tire dealer means a person actively engaged in the business of selling new replacement tires.
Rubbish means nonputrescible solid wastes consisting of combustible and noncombustible materials.
Scavenge, scavenging means the unauthorized retrieval of discarded refuse items.
Scrap tire means a tire that is no longer suitable for its original intended purpose because of wear, damage, or defect.
Scrap tire carrier means any person engaged in picking up or transporting scrap tires not otherwise exempted in the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management for the purpose of removal to a scrap tire processor, end user, or disposal facility.
Scrap tire generator means any person who generates scrap tires. The term "scrap tire generators" may include, but is not limited to, retail tire dealers, retreaders, scrap tire processors, automobile dealers, private company vehicle maintenance shops, garages, service stations, and city, county, and state governments.
Scrap tire processor means any person who is approved by the environmental protection division to receive scrap tires from scrap tire generators or scrap tire carriers for the purpose of scrap tire processing.
Scrap tire sorter means any person, other than the original scrap tire generator, who handles mixed tires by separating used tires and retreadable casings from scrap tires.
Solid waste means any garbage or refuse; sludge from a wastewater treatment plant, water supply treatment plant, or air pollution control facility; and other discarded material including solid, liquid, semisolid, or contained gaseous material resulting from industrial, commercial, mining, and agricultural operations and from community activities, but does not include recovered materials; solid or dissolved materials in domestic sewage; solid or dissolved materials in irrigation return flows or industrial discharges that are point sources; or source, special nuclear, or by-product material.
Solid waste enforcement officer means the official designated by the board of commissioners as responsible for enforcing violations of this article.
Solid waste handling means the storage, collection, transportation, treatment, utilization, processing, or disposal of solid waste, or any combination of such activities.
Travel trailer and recreational vehicle space means a lot or parcel designed or developed for the purpose of providing spaces for two or more recreational vehicle units to be used as temporary or permanent living facilities.
White goods means bulky wastes such as refrigerators, freezers, washers, dryers and other large appliances.
Yard waste means waste accumulation of tree branches, tree limbs, parts of trees, bushes, shrubbery cuttings or clippings, grass clippings, and all other similar wastes.
(Code 2002, § 50-35; Ord. of 7-21-1997, §§ 1.01, 1.02; Ord. No. 03-01, § 50-35, 2-3-2003; Ord. of 4-18-2005, §§ (a)—(d))

DIVISION 2. - ADMINISTRATION

Sec. 50-60. - Authority of board of commissioners.
The chairman of the board of commissioners shall have charge of the administration of this article. The chairman is authorized, with the approval of the board of commissioners and under the supervision of the county attorney, to adopt and promulgate rules and regulations concerning solid waste collection, conveyance and disposal, and respecting the size, type and specifications, number and location of all containers for collection. Such rules and regulations shall have the force of law but shall not be contrary to anything in this article but supplementary to this article.
(Code 2002, § 50-61; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 2.01)
Sec. 50-61. - Ownership of refuse.
(a)
All refuse shall be and shall remain the property of the owner or occupant until it has been placed in vehicles of the county or its designee or taken to a licensed landfill or otherwise deposed of in compliance with this article. The county has no responsibility or liability for any injuries or damage resulting from the refuse.
(b)
The owner or occupant of any premises shall be responsible for the sanitary handling and disposal of all litter, waste, scrap tires, and municipal, commercial or industrial solid waste on the premises used or occupied by such person.
(Code 2002, § 50-62; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 2.02; Ord. No. 03-01, § 50-62, 2-3-2003)
Sec. 50-62. - Collection, hauling and disposal services.
(a)
All residential, commercial, industrial and institutional establishments in the unincorporated portions of the county shall utilize the solid waste collection service of the county for the disposition of refuse unless the establishment contracts with a private company for its solid waste collection. Refuse shall be neatly stored in containers approved by the county and shall be collected on a schedule set by the board of commissioners or its designee.
(b)
Legal disposal of all other refuse not addressed in subsection (a) of this section shall be the responsibility of the establishment producing the refuse.
(c)
In group residential developments, the owner of the development and their tenants shall be jointly and severally responsible for the solid waste collection bill. The schedule for such charges is as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges and maintained in the office of the county clerk.
(d)
All other residential units shall pay a sum, as set in the schedule of fees and charges, for collection of garbage and household trash by the county or its designee.
(e)
In group commercial establishments, the owner of the building shall be responsible for the solid waste collection bill. The schedule for such charges is as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges and maintained in the office of the county clerk.
(f)
In individual commercial establishments, the owner of the individual business shall be responsible for the solid waste collection bill. The schedule for such charges is as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges and maintained in the office of the county clerk.
(g)
It shall be the responsibility of the person identified in this section as responsible for the solid waste collection bill to notify the board of commissioners or its designee when moving to discontinue service. At the time service is discontinued, payment in full of all outstanding balances is required.
(h)
The county or its designee may decline to collect any:
(1)
Container not properly placed;
(2)
Container not approved by the board of commissioners;
(3)
Containers that contain sharp objects or liquids; or
(4)
Refuse not properly contained.
Where the county or its designee has reason to leave solid waste uncollected, the county or its agents shall inform the customer within two days by written notice, mailing, or telephone as to why the solid waste was not collected.
(i)
If a regularly scheduled collection is missed and a complaint is received by the board of commissioners, and where no fault can be found on the customer's part, a special collection of refuse will be required of the county or its designee within 48 hours.
(j)
Notwithstanding any other provision set forth in this section, the owner of one or more rental units, properties or lots of any kind, and their tenants, shall be jointly and severally responsible for paying the solid waste collection fees for said rental units, irrespective to the location of the property, or the geographical relationship of one property with the other.
(Code 2002, § 50-63; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 2.03; Ord. of 4-18-2005, § (e))
Sec. 50-63. - Late fees.
The monthly fee levied by this division and collected by the county shall be due and payable on the first day of the month. If the fee is not paid by the tenth of each month, there is levied a late charge as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges and maintained in the office of the county clerk.
(Code 2002, § 50-64; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 2.04; Ord. No. 1, § 2.04, 9-8-1998)
Sec. 50-64. - Receptacles and containers required.
(a)
Every occupied building or premises upon which refuse is accumulated or produced shall be required by this article to provide and keep in a suitable place, readily accessible to the solid waste collection crews of the county, or its designees, receptacles and containers provided or approved by the county as suitable for the storage of all such refuse which will normally accumulate between the established collection dates.
(b)
The board of commissioners shall determine the type, size, specifications, number and location of such receptacles and shall determine whether such receptacles and containers are in a serviceable condition. Every person in possession, charge of or control of any building or premises upon which refuse is accumulated or produced shall be initially provided a container by the county.
(c)
Should any additional containers be needed, either upon the determination of the customer or the county, they may be obtained for an additional fee, as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges, from the county or its designee.
(d)
Unless approved by the chairman of the board of commissioners or its designee, containers furnished by the county or its designees shall not be removed from the premises to which delivered.
(e)
If the container furnished by the county is damaged, lost or destroyed through negligence of the person to whom the container was assigned, the person shall reimburse the county for the cost of a replacement container.
(f)
Any spillage or overflow from the refuse container shall be cleaned up immediately by the customer.
(g)
Those customers with curbside pickup shall have their refuse container placed curbside or as close as practicable to the collection vehicle routes by 7:00 a.m. on the designated collection day. Curbside refers to that portion of the right-of-way adjacent to and within ten feet of paved or traveled roadways, including alleys.
(h)
Those customers with curbside pickup shall return their refuse container to the side or rear of their building no later than 7:00 p.m. on the designated garbage pickup day. Where the collection point is located more than 100 yards from a building, the container may be stored at another location approved by the county.
(Code 2002, § 50-65; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 2.05)
Sec. 50-65. - Receptacles and containers to be safe.
(a)
All receptacles and containers shall be maintained in good service condition at all times. Any receptacle or container which does not conform to the requirements of this article or to the rules and regulations of the county, or which is likely to hamper or injure the person collecting the contents or the public generally, shall be replaced promptly by the user of the receptacle or container.
(b)
If any such container, after request by the county or its designee, has not been replaced, this shall be considered a violation of this article and shall be treated accordingly.
(Code 2002, § 50-66; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 2.06)
Sec. 50-66. - Service for disabled.
The county shall provide collection service for disabled persons who are in fact unable by reason, age or infirmity to place their containers at the roadside. Persons with disabilities certified to the chairman of the board of commissioners or its designee by a licensed physician will be exempt from placing their refuse roadside. The county or its designee agrees to collect waste of such disabled persons in any mutually convenient place on the premises of such disabled persons, except the county or its designee shall not be required to go into houses or other dwellings.
(Code 2002, § 50-67; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 2.07)
Secs. 50-67—50-90. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - PRECOLLECTION PRACTICES

Sec. 50-91. - Applicability.
The practices and procedures outlined in this division shall be followed by persons in the unincorporated areas of the county in order to facilitate the collection of solid waste.
(Code 2002, § 50-91; Ord. of 7-21-1997, art. III)
Sec. 50-92. - Garbage.
Garbage placed in containers for collection shall be enclosed in bags as defined in section 50-28. Household trash shall be combined with garbage. All solid waste or litter containers or receptacles and their surrounding area shall be maintained in as sanitary a manner as is reasonably possible consistent with its use for solid waste and litter disposal.
(Code 2002, § 50-92; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 3.01; Ord. No. 03-01, § 50-92, 2-3-2003)
Sec. 50-93. - Injurious trash items.
Waste material with potential for inflicting injury, such as needles, broken glass, light bulbs, sharp pieces of metal, fluorescent tubes and television tubes shall be securely packaged or wrapped in such a manner as will prevent injury to the collection crews or other persons. The provisions of this section shall not apply to material placed in mechanically handled containers.
(Code 2002, § 50-93; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 3.02)
Sec. 50-94. - Hazardous refuse.
Hazardous refuse will not be collected by the county or its designee. No person shall place any items of hazardous refuse in any refuse receptacle or other container for collection by the county. Federal and state hazardous waste laws shall be observed by all persons, and any violations will be reported to the appropriate authorities.
(Code 2002, § 50-94; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 3.03)
Sec. 50-95. - Building materials.
(a)
Building materials will not be collected by the county or its designee. The owner of the premises or the contractor, builder or person doing the repairs, remodeling, construction or demolition shall be required to legally dispose of all such debris. Such debris may be disposed of at the county landfill or transfer station.
(b)
Building materials or any other refuse, garbage or trash items which may accumulate during the construction of a development or structure shall be contained in a trash bin, drop-off trailer, or similar container approved by the county. It shall be a violation of this article for any developer, builder, contractor or landowner to allow building materials or other debris to accumulate other than in a container from which such items cannot escape, except by human force, and from which such items may be easily gathered for disposal. The violations will be handled as described in division 1 of this article. Any person cited for violating this section shall have his building permit revoked until the violation is corrected to the satisfaction of the chairman of the board of commissioners.
(Code 2002, § 50-95; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 3.04)
Sec. 50-96. - Tree, shrubbery, and lawn trimmings.
(a)
In all residential, commercial and industrial zoning districts, trees and other yard debris cut by the owner or occupant shall be promptly taken by the owner or occupant to an approved landfill. When a tree or other yard debris is cut from private property by anyone other than the owner, such person shall remove and legally dispose of all such material. The owner, tenant or other persons in possession of the property shall be responsible to see that such contractor complies with this provision. Such material may be disposed of at the county landfill or transfer station.
(b)
In an agricultural zoning district (AF), yard debris may be burned on site in accordance with the state forestry department's rules and permitting process.
(c)
Wood may be collected and stored in all zoning districts for use in fireplaces and the like. Wood so collected and stored shall be located in the rear yard, not the front or side yards.
(d)
Yard trimmings shall not be placed in or mixed with solid waste. Yard trimmings shall not be disposed at any solid waste disposal facility having liners and leachate collection systems or requiring vertical expansion within the county. Yard trimmings shall be sorted and stockpiled or chipped, composted, used as mulch or otherwise beneficially reused or recycled to the maximum extent feasible. Any yard trimmings to be collected by any entity other than the property owner shall be sorted and stored in such a manner as to facilitate collection, composting, or other handling.
(Code 2002, § 50-96; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 3.05; Ord. No. 03-01, § 50-96, 2-3-2003)
Sec. 50-97. - Ashes.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to deposit hot ashes in garbage containers. Ashes shall not be placed or deposited upon streets, sidewalks or other publicly owned land.
(b)
No person shall deposit any flammable or explosive materials in any such container or receptacle.
(Code 2002, § 50-97; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 3.06; Ord. No. 03-01, § 50-97, 2-3-2003)
Sec. 50-98. - Pet litter.
Pet litter shall be placed in securely tied plastic bags and placed in the refuse container for collection by the county or its designee.
(Code 2002, § 50-98; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 3.07)
Sec. 50-99. - Household furniture, furnishings and brown goods.
Household furniture, furnishings and brown goods shall be placed on the shoulder of the road adjacent to the trash container or taken to the transfer station.
(Code 2002, § 50-99; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 3.08)
Sec. 50-100. - Appliances, white goods.
Appliances and other white goods shall be placed on the shoulder of the road adjacent to the trash container or taken to the transfer station.
(Code 2002, § 50-100; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 3.09)
Sec. 50-101. - Disposable diapers.
Disposable diapers shall be placed and tied securely in plastic bags and placed in the refuse container for collection by the county or its designee. No other type of human waste shall be placed in any refuse receptacle for collection by the county or its designee.
(Code 2002, § 50-101; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 3.10)
Sec. 50-102. - Tires.
(a)
Tires must be taken by the owner to the county landfill or transfer station for disposal.
(b)
All persons defined as scrap tire generators, scrap tire carriers, scrap tire processors, including scrap tire sorters, and retail tire dealers shall be subject to rules as defined in chapter 391-3-4 et seq., of the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management and handle scrap tires in accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 12-8-20 et seq., and the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management, chapter 391-3-4 et seq., applicable to solid waste.
(c)
No person shall deposit a scrap tire in any container or receptacle unless authorized by the owner of the receptacle or the county board of commissioners or their designee.
(Code 2002, § 50-102; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 3.11; Ord. No. 03-01, § 50-102, 2-3-2003)
Sec. 50-103. - Recycling encouraged.
The county board of commissioners hereby finds that it is in the best interest of citizens of the county, in order to promote the health, safety, and welfare of the citizens of the county, to recycle as many waste materials as possible in order to reduce the accumulation of litter and garbage and solid waste materials which must be properly disposed of; therefore, it is the policy of the county board of commissioners to encourage recycling whenever practicable.
(Ord. No. 03-01, § 50-103, 2-3-2003)

DIVISION 4. - PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES

Sec. 50-135. - Activities declared unlawful.
The activities outlined in this division are declared to be unlawful and in violation of this article.
(Code 2002, § 50-131; Ord. of 7-21-1997, art. IV)
Sec. 50-136. - Public and private property.
No person shall place any litter, refuse, garbage or trash in any street, median strip, alley or other public place of travel, nor upon any other public property, nor upon any private property, except with the written consent of the owner or occupant, and then only in accordance with the provisions of this article.
(Code 2002, § 50-132; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 4.01)
Sec. 50-137. - Drainageways.
No person shall impede or cause to be impeded any drainageway, public or private, by placing any litter, refuse, trash, trees, yard waste, or any other item in, on or over such public or private drainageways.
(Code 2002, § 50-133; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 4.02)
Sec. 50-138. - Appliances.
No person shall leave outside any building in a place accessible to children any appliance, refrigerator or other container which has an airtight snap lock or similar device without first removing the door from the appliance. This section shall not apply to any appliance, refrigerator or container which has been placed on or adjacent to the rear of the building and which has been crated, strapped or locked so it will be impossible for a child to obtain access to any compartment of the appliance.
(Code 2002, § 50-134; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 4.03)
Sec. 50-139. - Vacant units, undeveloped lots and rental units.
The county or its designee will not remove garbage, trash, building material, household trash or tree, shrubbery or lawn trimmings or any other debris that is accumulated on or in vacant units, undeveloped lots and rental units.
(Code 2002, § 50-135; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 4.04)
Sec. 50-140. - Scavenging.
No person other than the owner shall disturb or interfere with any container used for the purpose of storing refuse pending its collection, or remove any contents, or remove such container from its location.
(Code 2002, § 50-136; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 4.05)
Sec. 50-141. - Transportation of debris.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any vehicle transporting loose materials on any of the roads of the county to transport such loose materials without suitable covers securely fastened to the vehicle in such a manner as to prevent the loose materials from being deposited on the streets or adjoining public and/or private lands.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for a business or private person engaged in waste hauling or transportation for hire from businesses or private residences to dispose of materials as solid waste that have been intentionally sorted as recyclables by the business or private residence to a lawfully permitted disposal facility rather than placing the materials in the location designated by the county or appropriate authority for said recyclables.
(Code 2002, § 50-137; Ord. of 7-21-1997, § 4.06; Ord. No. 03-01, § 50-137, 2-3-2003)
Sec. 50-142. - Prohibited activities.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to dump or cause to be dumped any garbage, refuse, litter, junk, appliances, equipment, cans, bottles, lumber, trees, tree limbs, brush or parts of brush, dead animals or any parts of dead animals, debris, rubbish, waste material, or other discarded materials (collectively referred to in this section as litter) anywhere in the unincorporated areas of the county unless:
(1)
The property is specifically designated by the county for the disposal of litter and the person is authorized by the proper public authority to use such property;
(2)
The litter is placed into a specific litter receptacle or container installed on such property; or
(3)
The person is the owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property or has first obtained consent of the owner or tenant in lawful possession or unless the act is done under the personal direction of the owner or tenant, all in a manner consistent with the public welfare.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to dump any refuse or other material other than household garbage into a dumpster or other litter receptacle placed in and about the county by the board of commissioners. As used in this section, the term "household garbage" means kitchen, animal, vegetable, and fruit refuse matter and other refuse matter ordinarily generated as byproducts of a household or restaurant, such as tin cans, bottles, paper, cardboard, plastics, and wrapping or packaging materials.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to set fire to the contents of, indiscriminately scatter or disperse the contents of, or otherwise vandalize any containers provided by the county for the dumping of trash or garbage.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person to dump the carcass of a dead animal or any part of a dead animal, including the skeleton, the hide, the hooves, the scales, the intestinal organs, bones, etc., into a dumpster or at a dumpster site.
(Code 2002, § 50-241; Res. of 8-5-1996, §§ (a)—(d))
Secs. 50-143—50-167. - Reserved.

Sec. 50-168. - Garbage can per housing unit required.
Refuse (solid waste, garbage) within a manufactured home park shall be handled in the manner prescribed in this article in that each housing unit within the park shall be provided with a garbage can.
(Code 2002, § 50-161; Ord. of 2-16-1998, § A)
