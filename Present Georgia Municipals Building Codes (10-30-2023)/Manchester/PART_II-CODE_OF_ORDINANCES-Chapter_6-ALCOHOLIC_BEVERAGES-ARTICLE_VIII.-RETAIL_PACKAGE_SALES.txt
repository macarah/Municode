ARTICLE VIII. - RETAIL PACKAGE SALES

Sec. 6-169. - Special requirements for package distilled spirits sales.
(a)
No license for the retail sale of distilled spirits shall be located within 1,000 feet of another licensee.
(b)
No retail dealer license for the sale of distilled spirits shall be issued to any applicant whose building where the business will be conducted is not free standing (i.e., is part of a larger building or structure), unless the business shall have its own designated entrance.
(c)
Each store selling packaged distilled spirits shall have a minimum of 1,800 square feet.
(d)
Each store selling packaged distilled spirits shall maintain an inventory of at least $300,000.00 in distilled spirits for resale.
(e)
Once a licensee is issued under this section, the retail package store shall be operational within six months of the issuance of the license. If any licensee ceases operations for more than 30 consecutive calendar days the license shall be automatically revoked without any action from the city council.
(f)
All licensees shall have a sign on the entrance door which clearly indicates that all patrons must be 21 years of age or older to enter.
(g)
All customers of any licensee to purchase packaged alcohol must present a valid identification each time products are purchased to ensure all customers are 21 years of age or older.
(h)
No licenses shall be issued for any location on Main Street from 2nd Avenue to York Street.
(i)
Licensees shall be also permitted to conduct the sale of wine, malt beverages, tobacco products, beverages containing no alcohol which are commonly used to dilute distilled spirits, and bar supplies, limited to Styrofoam products, ice chests, koozies, packaged ice, paper products and alcoholic beverage recipe booklets, provided the proper permits and licenses are obtained from the City of Manchester to sell such items.
( Ord. of 12-13-2021 , § 2)
Sec. 6-170. - Maximum number of distilled spirits retail licenses permitted.
(a)
Subject to subsections (b) and (c) of this section, the city will not accept any additional applications for the retail sale of distilled spirits by the package, and no additional licenses for the retail sale of distilled spirits by the package shall be issued.
(b)
If, at any time and for whatever reason, the number of active licenses for the retail sale of distilled spirits by the package falls below two, then the city shall accept applications for such and, if the city receives enough qualified applicants, issue such additional licenses for the retail sale of distilled spirits by the package so as to bring the total number of active licenses for the retail sale of distilled spirits by the package to two.
(c)
Should there be more applicants who meet all of the license qualifications of section 6-171 than there are licenses to be given out, the applicant will be chosen at random.
( Ord. of 12-13-2021 , § 2)
Sec. 6-171. - Qualifications.
(a)
A licensee must meet all of the qualifications for a retail malt beverage and wine alcohol license as set forth in this chapter 6, in addition to the requirements in this section.
(b)
A licensee must be at least 21 years of age and a citizen or permanent resident of the United States.
(c)
A licensee shall not have been convicted within the past ten years of any felony, any misdemeanor involving moral turpitude, any other misdemeanor or violation of a city ordinance within the past four years, or at any time of any criminal offense relating to alcoholic beverages, taxes or gambling. This subsection shall apply with respect to the laws of the state, other states, the United States, and other countries. A plea of nolo contender or the forfeiture of a bond shall be considered a conviction for purposes of this subsection. The city council may at their discretion waive the conviction of a misdemeanor for purposes of this subsection if the city manager determines that the misdemeanor does not have a bearing on the applicant's fitness for a license.
(d)
A licensee shall not have been denied or had revoked, within the five years next preceding his application, any license to sell alcoholic beverages issued by any governmental entity.
(e)
A licensee shall be the owner of the premises to be licensed or the holder of a lease thereon for substantially the same period to be covered by the license.
(f)
Any person having two convictions for selling alcohol to a minor within a three-year period shall not be eligible for a license.
( Ord. of 12-13-2021 , § 2)
Sec. 6-172. - Breaking package or drinking on premises prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to open or consume any alcoholic beverages on premises licensed for the sale of alcoholic beverages by the package.
( Ord. of 12-13-2021 , § 2)
Sec. 6-173. - Lottery sales allowed.
It shall be lawful for licensees for the retail sale of distilled spirits by the package to also sell lottery tickets on their premises provided that the lottery tickets are issued by the Georgia Lottery Corporation and provided further that the licensee is an authorized retailer of the Georgia Lottery Corporation.
( Ord. of 12-13-2021 , § 2)
Sec. 6-174. - Loitering prohibited.
Loitering shall be prohibited at the premises of any licensee. For purposes of this section, "premises" shall include the licensed location and any street, sidewalk or parking lot immediately adjacent to the licensed premises.
( Ord. of 12-13-2021 , § 2)
Sec. 6-175. - Knowledge of article provisions.
Every licensee under this article shall, prior to applying for a license, read and familiarize himself with the provisions of this article, and an application shall constitute a certification by the applicant that he has done so. Every licensee shall maintain a copy of this article on the licensed premises and shall instruct each employee engaged in the sale or handling of alcoholic beverages concerning the relevant provisions of the article.
( Ord. of 12-13-2021 , § 2)
