ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 78-1. - Adoption of state law by reference.
(a)
The provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-372—40-6-376, known as the Uniform Rules of the Road, and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 are adopted as and for the traffic regulations of this city with like effect as if recited in this section.
(b)
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-8.
(Code 1989, § 17-1)
Sec. 78-2. - Move on green light.
On the display of a green light, drivers of vehicles shall immediately move forward in such a way and manner as not to block or impede traffic.
(Code 1989, § 17-2)
Sec. 78-3. - Speed limits on certain streets as designated.
Whenever any ordinance or regulation of the city determines that certain speed regulations shall be applicable upon specified streets or parts of streets or in certain areas, and appropriate signs indicating the speed are erected, it shall be unlawful for any person to drive a vehicle at a speed in excess of any speed so declared.
(Code 1989, § 17-4)
State Law reference— Municipal authority to alter maximum speed limit, O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-183, 40-6-371(a)(10); speed not to impede traffic, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-184.

Sec. 78-5. - Truck routes.
(a)
For the purpose of establishing truck routes, a truck is defined as a vehicle which has more than six wheels.
(b)
Except as specifically set out in this section, trucks shall be operated over the following streets only:
(1)
US 78 and SR 17 from East Robert Toombs Avenue to the intersection of SR 17 and US 78.
(2)
US 78 from the intersection of US 78 and SR 17 to the city limits.
(3)
SR 44 in its entirety.
(4)
SR 47 from the city limits to Andrew Drive.
(5)
Andrew Drive in its entirety.
(6)
Industrial Boulevard in its entirety.
(7)
Hospital Drive from Industrial Boulevard to SR 44.
(c)
Exceptions to the schedule in subsection (b) of this section are as follows:
(1)
Any truck may be deviated from a truck route and operated over a prohibited street where necessary to reach a destination point within the city for the conduct of business, provided streets upon which truck traffic is permitted are used until reaching the intersection of the prohibited street nearest such destination.
(2)
Any truck may be operated over a prohibited street where necessary to leave a point of origin within the city for the conduct of business, provided it leaves such prohibited street at the nearest intersection of a truck route to such point of origin.
(3)
Any truck may be operated over a prohibited street if owned or operated by the city, public utilities, any contractor or materialman, while engaged in the repair, maintenance or construction of streets, street improvements, or street utilities within the city.
(4)
Any truck tractor which has been separated from its trailer.
(Ord. of 8-10-1998(1), § 17-6)

Sec. 78-36. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Double parking means the parking of two or more vehicles abreast on the same side of any street or open public place or on opposite sides of such street unless a distance of 150 feet shall be unoccupied by parked vehicles on the opposite side of such street or space, both in front of and behind the parked vehicle, and unless an open driving space of not less than 30 feet shall remain between the parked vehicles on opposite sides of such space or street.
(Code 1989, § 17-20)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 78-37. - Parking within marked spaces.
All vehicles shall park so as to be between and not on or over any parking lines indicated by white painted stripes abutting the curbline; all such vehicles shall park wholly within all lines.
(Code 1989, § 17-22)
Sec. 78-38. - Double parking.
It shall be unlawful to double park any motor vehicle on any street or public place in the city at any time unless such vehicle is occupied by a licensed driver.
(Code 1989, § 17-23)
Sec. 78-39. - No parking in areas with yellow markings.
It shall be unlawful to park any vehicle on any city street or parking area where the street or parking area is designated as a no parking area with yellow markings.
(Code 1989, § 17-24)
Sec. 78-40. - Passenger and freight loading zones.
Passenger and freight loading zones shall be as designated in this section.
(1)
In no event shall more than one-half of the total curb length in any block be reserved for loading zone purposes.
(2)
All loading zones shall be indicated by red painted lines on curbs and by appropriate signs reading "loading zone only" or "passenger loading only."
(3)
In loading zones, standing time shall be limited.
(4)
No person shall stop, stand or park a vehicle for any purpose or length of time other than for the expeditious unloading and delivery of pickup and loading of passengers or materials in any place marked as a loading zone during hours when the provisions applicable to loading zones are in effect. In no case shall the stop for loading and unloading of materials exceed 30 minutes, or for loading and unloading of passengers exceed five minutes.
(Ord. of 8-10-1998(2))
Sec. 78-41. - No parking on sidewalks.
It shall be unlawful for any vehicle or motorcycle or any other motorized vehicle, to be parked on the sidewalk or other public right-of-way in any space that is not a designated parking area.
(Ord. of 4-10-00)
Cross reference— Stopping, standing, or parking prohibited in specified places, O.C.G.A. 40-6-203(1)(B).
