ARTICLE V. - CONSUMPTION ON PREMISES; LICENSING REQUIREMENTS, REGULATION AND TYPES OF LICENSES

Sec. 6-70. - License permitting the sale of alcoholic beverages on the premises for consumption on the premises.
(a)
Only the following types of establishments or activities shall be licensed under this article for the sale of alcoholic beverages on the premises for consumption on the premises:
(1)
Restaurants;
(2)
Brewpubs;
(3)
Drinking places;
(4)
Hotel/lounge;
(5)
Nightclub;
(6)
Indoor commercial recreational establishments;
(7)
Golf course; and
(8)
Performing arts facility.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-71. - Issuance of licenses limited to designated business; occupational tax certificate.
(a)
No license permitting the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises shall be issued to any person unless the business or the proposed business to be licensed hereunder is a restaurant, brewpub, drinking place, hotel/lounge, nightclub, indoor commercial recreational establishment, golf course, or performing arts facility as each of these terms is defined in this chapter.
(b)
Licenses shall only be issued for a business that is a permitted use in that particular zoning district and no license for the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises may be issued to any business licensed for operating as any type of adult entertainment establishment.
(c)
No license permitting the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises shall be issued to any person unless the business or the proposed business to be licensed hereunder has made application for and received an occupation tax certificate as a restaurant, performing arts facility, hotel, lounge, drinking place, nightclub, indoor commercial recreational establishment, golf course or brewpub.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-72. - Off-premises sale of alcoholic beverages for catered functions.
(a)
In addition to the occupation tax requirements of this Code and article I of this chapter, the following requirements shall apply to the licensing and operations for sales of alcoholic beverages off-premises for catered functions. Should this section conflict with any other provisions of this chapter or the occupation tax requirements of this Code, then this section shall control.
(b)
Any applicant who holds a license by the city under the terms and conditions set forth in chapter 6 to sell malt beverages or wine by the drink for consumption on the premises may be issued an off-premises license that authorizes such applicant to sell malt beverages or wine by the drink off-premises and in connection with an authorized catered function. Any applicant not licensed by the city who possesses a valid license from a jurisdiction within the State of Georgia to sell malt beverages or wine by the drink for consumption on the premises may be issued a license that authorizes such applicant to sell malt beverages or wine by the drink at an authorized catered function upon presentation of a copy of such license from the applicant's original issuing jurisdiction. For the purposes of this section, the term "authorized catered function" may include a function held inside a publicly or privately owned building or structure or a function held outside such a building or structure that is located within the city limits of the city.
(c)
Any applicant who holds a license issued by the city under the terms and conditions set forth in chapter 6 which authorizes the applicant to sell distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises may be issued an off-premises license which authorizes such applicant to sell distilled spirits by the drink off-premises and in connection with an authorized catered function. For purposes of this section the term "authorized catered function" may include a function held inside a publicly or privately owned building or structure or a function held outside such a building or structure that is located within the city limits of the city.
(d)
In order to distribute or sell distilled spirits, malt beverages or wine at an authorized catered function, an applicant shall be required to:
(1)
Apply to the city for an alcoholic beverage catered event permit. The application shall include the name of the applicant; the date, address and time of the event; and the applicant's alcoholic beverage license number.
(2)
If applicable, provide satisfactory reports as required to the state on forms provided by the state, stating the quantity of any and all alcoholic beverages transported from the applicant's primary premises to the location of the authorized catered function and such other information as may be required by the state.
(3)
If applicable, maintain original event permits and documents in the vehicle transporting the alcoholic beverage to the catered function at all times.
(4)
Pay an alcoholic beverage catered event permit fee according to the schedule approved from time to time by the city council.
(e)
The city clerk or his/her designee shall review the applicant's application and supporting documentation. Based upon the requirements set forth in this section, the city clerk or his/her designee shall either approve or deny, in writing, said alcoholic beverage catered event application.
(f)
All alcoholic beverage catered event applications must be submitted at least 20 calendar days prior to the planned event.
(g)
No limitations will be applied as to the number of alcoholic beverage catered event license applications made by a retail consumption dealer in any one calendar year.
(h)
The restrictions set forth in the section 6-13, hours of operation, shall apply to all sales of alcoholic beverages at authorized catered functions.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017; Res. of 3-12-2018)
Sec. 6-73. - Restaurants.
(a)
An establishment may be licensed to sell alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises as a restaurant where the establishment meets all other applicable requirements under this chapter.
(b)
A restaurant licensee shall be allowed to serve alcoholic beverages to persons seated at that restaurant's sidewalk café, if a sidewalk café permit has been issued to the restaurant.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-74. - Bottle house.
(a)
A bottle house license is not a license for selling alcoholic beverages of any type.
(b)
A bottle house license allows specific places of business to allow patrons to bring in and consume their own alcoholic beverages on the premises.
(c)
Businesses that are eligible for licensure as a bottle house must have a primary business that fits the Code of Ordinances definition for: bed and breakfast, art shop, nail salon, or spa.
(d)
The individual that supplies the alcoholic beverage must remove any unconsumed beverage from the facility.
(e)
The brown bagging of alcoholic beverages will be subject to this chapter and to state law; including but not limited to prohibition of consumption by one who is under 21 years of age, or intoxicated.
(f)
It shall be unlawful for any licensed bottle house to store any alcoholic beverage on the premises, except for temporary storage in a refrigerator or cooler while the person who furnished the alcoholic beverage is still in the place of business.
(g)
In the case of a bed and breakfast the resident owner/manager can store alcoholic beverages for his/her own personal consumption.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-75. - Brewpub licenses.
(a)
No person shall be permitted to own or operate a brewpub without first obtaining consumption on premises license from the city clerk or his/her designee, and each brewpub license holder shall comply with all other applicable state and local license requirements.
(b)
Possession of a brewpub license shall not prevent the holder of such license from obtaining another license authorized under section 6-70 (license permitting the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises) for the same premises.
(c)
A brewpub license does not authorize the holder of such license to sell alcoholic beverages at retail by package for consumption off the premises.
(d)
A brewpub licensee shall pay all state and local license fees and excise taxes applicable to individuals licensed as manufacturers, retailers, and, where applicable, wholesalers.
(e)
A brewpub licensee shall measure all beer manufactured on the premises and otherwise comply with applicable regulations respecting excise and enforcement tax determination of such beer as required. There is hereby levied an excise tax on all beer and malt beverage produced by a brewpub in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 3-5-80, as amended. Such tax shall be paid to city hall no later than the tenth day of each month for the preceding month's production. A late payment penalty not to exceed ten percent of the tax otherwise due shall be added to the amount due for any payment not received by the due date.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-76. - Performing arts facility.
(a)
Alcoholic beverages may be sold and/or dispensed at a performing arts facility only where the facility meets the definition for a performing arts facility set forth herein.
(b)
A business meeting the definition of a performing arts facility under this chapter shall be eligible to apply for and be considered for an annual license to allow alcoholic beverages to be sold for consumption on the premises from the city clerk or his/her designee.
(c)
A business granted a performing arts facility license for consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises shall be required to obtain all required licenses from the state and to follow all state and local laws, ordinances and rules governing the consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises.
(d)
Alcoholic beverages may be sold at a performing arts facility only at a bar located in the lobby area of the facility or by a waiter or waitress serving patrons within the facility.
(e)
In addition to the hours of sale requirements set out in this chapter, alcoholic beverages may not be dispensed at a performing arts facility earlier than one hour before the start of a performance or later than one hour after the performance has ended, provided that no alcoholic beverages shall be sold past the hours listed in this chapter for retail consumption on premise licensees.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-77. - Indoor commercial recreation establishments.
Indoor commercial recreation establishments that are licensed to serve alcoholic beverages shall only sell alcoholic beverages at a bar or by a waiter or waitress in the dining or bar areas at tables located therein. Alcoholic beverages shall not be sold in the areas of the establishment not intended predominately for food or beverage consumption.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-78. - Reserved.
Sec. 6-79. - Pitchers.
(a)
No consumption on the premises licensee shall sell, offer to sell or deliver alcoholic beverages, including malt beverages, in any container that holds more than 32 fluid ounces at any one time to one person.
(b)
A consumption on the premises licensee may sell, offer to sell or deliver alcoholic beverages, including malt beverages, in a pitcher containing not more than 64 fluid ounces to a group of persons consisting of at least two or more persons.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-80. - Courtyard, patio, and deck sales.
(a)
For the purposes of this chapter, an establishment may include a courtyard, patio, and/or deck area as a part of their premises within which they are allowed to serve and sell alcoholic beverages if:
(1)
The courtyard, patio, or deck is separated from the general public by a permanent wall, fence, hedge, or similar device or structure that is approved by the building official or his/her designee;
(2)
The patrons of the establishment may only access the courtyard, patio, or deck area through the primary structure of the establishment;
(3)
The location of the courtyard, patio, or deck does not encroach upon any public sidewalk, any public right-of-way, or any other area customarily used for ingress to the primary structure of the establishment, egress from the primary structure of the establishment, pedestrian traffic, or vehicular traffic;
(4)
The location and placement of the courtyard, patio, or deck shall first have been approved by the city clerk or his/her designee; and
(5)
The plans for the courtyard, patio, or deck shall first have been approved by the building official or his/her designee as being in conformity with applicable city building codes and all other applicable codes.
(b)
The use of the courtyard, patio, or deck shall be subject to the following conditions:
(1)
The use of the courtyard, patio, or deck shall be subject to the city's noise and nuisance ordinances and shall be subject to applicable state noise and nuisance statutes;
(2)
No additional license shall be required for the sale or service of alcoholic beverages in a courtyard, patio, or deck area; however, the licensee must apply for an amendment to his/her consumption on the premises license to reflect the additional service area added by the inclusion of the courtyard, patio, or deck area, and such application shall be accompanied by two copies of the plans for the proposed courtyard, patio, or deck area which shall be subject to the approval of the city manager or his/her designee;
(3)
The courtyard, patio, or deck area shall be adequately illuminated for the safety of the patrons as determined by the building official or his/her designee;
(4)
The licensee shall comply with all setback lines and buffer requirements of the city zoning ordinance which is in effect at the time said alcoholic beverage license application is submitted; and
(5)
The use of a courtyard, patio, or deck as a part of the premises may be suspended or revoked by the city clerk or his/her designee if its use interferes with other permissible uses in the area surrounding the premises or if its use becomes a public nuisance.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-81. - "Happy hour" promotions and sales.
(a)
No licensee or employee or agent of a licensee, in connection with the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises shall:
(1)
Offer any free alcoholic beverage to any person or group of persons;
(2)
Deliver more than one alcoholic beverage to one person at one time; with the exception of any one and one-half ounce serving of a distilled spirit at such time the customer is in possession of a malt beverage.
(3)
Encourage or permit on the licensed premises any game or contest which involves the drinking of alcoholic beverages or the awarding of alcoholic beverages as a prize.
(4)
Require or allow the purchase of a second or subsequent alcoholic beverage at the same time another alcoholic beverage is purchased or before the previous alcoholic beverage has been consumed or otherwise discarded; with the exception of any one and one-half ounce serving of a distilled spirit at such time the customer is in possession of a malt beverage.
(b)
No licensee shall advertise or promote in any way, whether within or without the licensed premises, any of the practices prohibited under subsection (a).
(c)
Nothing in this section shall prohibit the delivery of a bottle of wine to the patrons of a restaurant for consumption during the course of a meal.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-82. - Sidewalk cafe.
(a)
Definitions.
The following words terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this chapter, except where context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Downtown square business district means the portion of the downtown area along the following roads; (1) Robert Toombs Avenue between Allison Street and Jefferson Street; (2) Jefferson Street from Robert Toombs Avenue to Court Street; (3) Court Street from Jefferson Street to Allison Street; (4) Allison Street from Court Street to Robert Toombs Avenue; (5) Spring Street from Court Street to Robert Toombs Avenue; (6) Cheney Parkway from Court Street to Robert Toombs Avenue; (7) Public property designated as Map & Parcel number W23 061 by the county tax assessor's office; (8) Spring Street from Robert Toombs Avenue to Liberty Street.
Sidewalk cafe means the location and use of tables, chairs, and other associated equipment and furnishings on the public sidewalk, and operated as an extension of a licensed eating establishment or licensed alcoholic beverage premises in the downtown square business district.
(b)
Sidewalk cafes allowed in downtown square business district; permit required.
(1)
Sidewalk cafes shall be allowed in the area known as the downtown square business district subject to the provisions of this chapter.
(2)
No person shall operate a sidewalk cafe without first obtaining a permit from the city building official.
(3)
All applications for a permit shall be submitted to the city building official, and shall include the following information:
a.
A drawing depicting the location, size and number of tables and chairs at the proposed sidewalk café location, subject to the following:
1.
A minimum of 48 inches, clear and unobstructed, sidewalk space shall be maintained for pedestrian traffic at all times;
2.
If a sidewalk has less than 60 inches clear width, then passing spaces at least 60 inches by 60 inches shall be located at reasonable intervals not to exceed 200 feet along the sidewalk in order to assure compliance with the Americans With Disabilities Act (ADA Compliance Guide section 4.3).
3.
The area in which a sidewalk café is located shall not extend parallel in either direction beyond the outside wall of the building in which the restraint is located.
b.
The name, location, description, and mailing address of the business applying for the permit.
(4)
The applicant shall procure and shall maintain comprehensive general liability, and broad form property damage insurance as shall protect applicant from claims for damages for bodily injury, including accidental death, as well as claims for property damages, which may arise from operation of a sidewalk café by the applicant or any subcontractor or by anyone directly or indirectly employed by either of them. The amount of insurance shall not be less than $1,000,000.00.
(5)
The applicant shall provide a certificate of insurance acceptable to the city, which shall be attached to each signed application when transmitted for final approval. The city shall be named as an additional insured on all coverages. The certificates of insurance, and any subsequent renewals, shall reference the license.
(6)
The certificates of insurance shall contain a statement that says, "Coverages afforded under the policies will not be canceled unless written notice is given to the city at least 30 days before cancellation."
(c)
General regulations.
(1)
A covered trash container, design to be approved by the city, with a minimum capacity of 32 gallons, containing a disposable plastic liner, shall be provided within each sidewalk café area, and shall be emptied as often as necessary to prevent overflow and other unsanitary conditions.
(2)
Permit holders shall maintain sidewalk café areas and all equipment and furnishings in such condition as to be clean, sanitary, and safe at all times.
(3)
Receptacles for disposal of cigarettes shall be provided for use of the customers of the sidewalk café.
(4)
The number of patrons occupying the sidewalk café shall not be greater than the total number of chairs at each table within the sidewalk café.
(5)
All tables, chairs, trash containers, and other associated fixtures and equipment shall be removed from the public sidewalk area at the close of business every day, unless otherwise provided for and approved in the application process.
(d)
Regulations pertaining specifically to businesses holding alcoholic beverage licenses.
(1)
Those businesses holding alcoholic beverage licenses shall store all alcoholic beverages inside their principal building.
(2)
If customers choose to purchase alcoholic beverages inside the business and take them outside to the sidewalk café portion of the restraint, it shall be the responsibility of the permit holder to ensure all alcoholic beverage laws and ordinances are being complied with in the sidewalk café.
(3)
It shall be unlawful for anyone to leave the confines of the sidewalk café, except to enter the principal building containing the licensed establishment, with alcoholic beverages in their possession.
(4)
It shall be unlawful for the license holder or employees thereof to permit any person to leave the sidewalk café, except to enter the principal building containing the licensed establishment, with alcoholic beverages in their possession.
(5)
The general regulations as set out in section 6-3 shall also apply to those restaurants holding alcoholic beverage licenses.
(e)
Revocation, suspension.
(1)
Any violation of this chapter that may warrant revocation of a sidewalk café permit shall be reported by any person to the city building official who shall have the power, in his discretion, to suspend the permit for a period not to exceed ten days pending a hearing before the administrative hearing officer, appointed by the city administrator on the question of whether the permit shall be revoked for cause.
(2)
Upon a charge that there has been a violation of this chapter, the building official may order the use of the sidewalk café suspended, pending a hearing on that charge.
(3)
Before the denial of any application for a permit, or the revocation of any existing sidewalk café permit subject to the provisions of this chapter, the applicant or permit holder, as the case may be, shall be given notice in writing from the city building official to show cause before the administrative hearing officer, appointed by the city administrator, at a time and place specified therein not less than three days nor more than ten days from the date of service of the notice, why such application should not be denied or why such permit should not be revoked, as the case may be, stating grounds therefore, and at the appointed time and place the applicant or permit holder shall have an opportunity to show cause, if any exist, why such application should not be denied or such permit revoked, after which the administrative hearing officer shall take such actions as he or she in his or her judgement and discretion shall deem warranted under the facts. All decisions of the administrative hearing officer shall be in writing with reasons therefore stated and mailed or delivered to the applicant, permit holder or person in charge of the place of business if the permit holder cannot be located. In addition, at any hearing as provided herein, the party afforded the hearing shall have the opportunity to present evidence and cross-examine opposing witnesses.
(4)
Any person aggrieved by the action or decision of the administrative hearing officer to deny an application for a permit applied for under the provisions of this chapter or the revocation or suspension of a permit, shall have the right of certiorari to the superior court from the decision of the administrative hearing officer, as provided by law.
(f)
Penalty for violation.
Any person violating any provisions of this chapter shall be punished as provided in section 1-8 of this Code.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
