ARTICLE II. - APPLICATION REQUIREMENTS

Sec. 6-20. - Application procedures.
All persons must follow the applicable procedures hereinafter set forth.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-21. - Procedure for considering application.
The city clerk or his/her designee shall not consider any application for a license until the written application provided for herein is made, the investigative fee paid, the plat required herein filed and the fingerprinting and investigation of all persons as required under this chapter has been completed by the sheriff's department. The sheriff's department shall, upon completion of the investigation, file a written report of the results of such investigation, which report shall be attached to said application and form a part of the permanent city record. Upon the completion of all of the foregoing, the application, together with all data, shall be submitted to the city clerk or his/her designee for consideration pursuant to this chapter.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-22. - Application; data; information; requirement for.
Applicants are required to complete all aspects of all prescribed forms required under this chapter. The failure to provide all information required by such forms may result in denial of any application under this chapter. All applicants shall furnish all omitted data, information and records requested of them by the city clerk or his/her designee, and the failure to furnish such omitted data, information and records within 30 calendar days from the date of such request shall automatically serve to cause such application to be denied. The applicants, by filing an application for a license permitted under this chapter, agree to produce, for oral interview, any person or persons requested by the city clerk or his/her designee , considered by either as being important in ascertaining the facts relative to such application and/or licenses issued under this chapter. The failure to produce such person or persons within 30 calendar days after being requested to do so shall result in the automatic denial of any application pending under this chapter. The application form and all data requested by the city clerk or his/her designee shall be subscribed by the applicant(s) under oath before a notary public or other officer authorized to administer oaths. Any untrue or misleading information contained in, or material statement omitted from, an original, renewal or transfer application for a license shall be cause for the denial or revocation thereof.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-23. - Citizenship; residence requirements; corporation requirements.
No license for the sale of alcoholic beverages shall be issued in the name of any individual who is not a citizen of the United States or an alien lawfully permitted for permanent residence. Where the applicant is a corporation or other business entity other than an individual, any person, stockholder or principal owning more than a ten percent financial interest must meet the requirements contained herein as if the license was to be issued in their name, and in the event any license provided for hereunder shall be issued, such license shall be issued in the name of the corporation or business entity and the principal stockholder or principal behind such business entity. If the applicant is a partnership, the requirements contained herein shall be met by all partners, whether general or limited partners. If the applicant in whose name a license under this chapter is issued is not a county resident, such person must appoint and continuously maintain in the county a designated agent upon whom any process, notice or demand required under this chapter may be served. The registered agent must be a resident of the county. If the licensee is not a natural person, such as a corporation, it will be required to appoint and maintain a designated agent in the county if it does not have in the county any agent upon whom process can legally be served for such entity under the laws of the state. The license[e] shall file the name of such registered agent, along with the written consent to serve of such agent, with the city clerk or his/her designee.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-24. - Annexation of licensed businesses.
Any person, persons, establishment or other entity that holds a license from a county for the sale of alcoholic beverages and that is located in an area annexed by the city, shall have 30 calendar days from the effective date of the annexation to apply for the appropriate licenses and permits under this chapter. However, all applicable fees and costs for the equivalent license(s) in the city shall be waived for the year of annexation.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-25. - Other licenses.
The license fees provided for in this chapter are not levied in lieu of any other license fees or taxes that may be levied upon licensees, but are in addition thereto.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-26. - Applications for license.
(a)
The provisions of this Code regarding occupation taxes shall apply in addition to the provisions in this section.
(b)
All applications for licenses under this chapter shall be accompanied by a payment in cash, credit/debit card, personal/business check, or by cashiers/certified check, for all the fees and occupation taxes provided for in the city's Code for the classification into which the applicant falls.
(c)
All city applications shall be in original form and shall be accompanied by a full photocopy of all the appropriate state applications.
(d)
Any false statement or material misrepresentation in any application hereunder shall be grounds for the denial of any application or for the revocation of any license granted hereunder.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-27. - Notice of intention to secure license for sale of distilled spirits.
(a)
No application for a license for the sale of distilled spirits, either for retail sale or for consumption on premises, shall be acted upon until after the applicant has published in the newspaper that publishes the legal advertisements of the city a notice of his intention to secure a license for sale of distilled spirits. Such notice shall be published three times during the 30 calendar days immediately preceding the filing of the application for a license. Such notice shall be in large boldface type and shall state:
(1)
The type of license for which application has been filed;
(2)
The exact location of the place of business for which a license is sought;
(3)
The names and addresses of each owner of the business; and
(4)
If the applicant is a corporation, the names and titles of all corporate officers.
(b)
Proof of publication of the notice required by this section shall be attached to an application for a license for sale of distilled spirits, both for retail sale and for consumption on premises.
(c)
An applicant for a renewal license for the sale of distilled spirits shall not be subject to the notice requirements of this section. However, the notice section shall apply to all other applications for a license for the sale of distilled spirits, including, but not limited to, an application involving a new business, a change of location and a change of license holder.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-28. - Application forms.
(a)
All persons desiring to receive a license shall make application on a form prescribed by the city clerk or his/her designee. No application will be processed until the applicant has completed the required interview with the city clerk or his/her designee.
(b)
The application shall include but shall not be limited to: the name and address of the applicant; the proposed business to be carried on; if a partnership, the names and residence address of the partners, the name of the manager, and the name of all shareholders holding more than ten percent of any class of corporate stock, or any other entity having a financial interest in each entity which is to own or operate the establishment for which a license is sought. If the manager changes, the applicant must furnish the city clerk or his/her designee the name and address of the new manager and other information as requested within ten calendar days of such change.
(c)
There shall be separate processing fees to cover the expense of an investigation and the expense of processing the application, which fees shall not be refundable. The amount of the investigation fee and the processing fee for the application shall be as established by the city council, from time to time.
(d)
All applications shall contain proof that the location of the proposed premises complies with all zoning and planning requirements.
(e)
All applicants shall furnish data, information and records as required by the city clerk or his/her designee and to ensure compliance with the provisions of this chapter. Failure to furnish data as requested shall cause the application to be denied.
(f)
A complete set of fingerprints of each applicant for a new license shall be required.
(1)
Authority. This subsection is enacted pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 35-3-35(a) (1.2), the Georgia Umbrella Statute entitled "Dissemination of information to public agencies, political subdivisions, authorities, and instrumentalities."
(2)
Fingerprinting required. An individual seeking to receive an alcoholic beverage license shall be fingerprinted as a condition of submitting an application for a new license. By filing such application, the applicant consents to the city obtaining their criminal history record information (CHRI) from the Georgia Crime Information Center (GCIC) and the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI). While fingerprinting shall typically only be required at the time of the initial application, the submission by the applicant of a renewal application shall authorize the city to request a new set of fingerprints if needed and shall authorize the city to once again obtain the applicant's criminal history record information (CHRI) from the Georgia Crime Information Center (GCIC) and the Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI).
(3)
Access authorized; usage regulations.
a.
The city is hereby authorized to access national criminal history record information pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 35-3-35(a) (1.2).
b.
Information obtained and handled for any purposes pursuant to this subsection shall comply with all Georgia laws and the Federal Privacy Act.
c.
No information given as part of the request for a criminal history and no record obtained pursuant to this section may be entered on any database.
d.
No information given or obtained pursuant to this section shall be subject to the provisions of the Georgia Open Records Act.
(4)
Administration; fees; procedures.
a.
Applicant shall provide his/her fingerprints, which may be taken by the county sheriff's department or may be taken at any Georgia applicant processing services location so long as the applicant registers in such a manner that allows the county sheriff's department to receive the criminal history results.
b.
The applicant shall bear the cost of the electronic fingerprinting submission services set by the Georgia Applicant Processing Services and an administrative fee allowed by statute to be charged by the city, which shall be the maximum allowed by statute, which is currently $20.00, which fee shall include fingerprinting when carried out by the county sheriff's department.
c.
Upon receipt of the fingerprints and the appropriate fees, the city will transmit said fingerprints and appropriate fees (unless a satisfactory billing arrangement has been entered into between the city police department and the GCIC and/or FBI) to the GCIC. As provided by law, the GCIC will compare the subject's fingerprints against its criminal file and submit the fingerprints to the FBI for a comparison with nation-wide records, unless submission to the FBI is automatic pursuant to the use of live-scan. The results of the FBI check will be returned based on its current procedure, presently being directly to the city Police Department if submissions are made manually to the GCIC, or electronically from both the GBI and FBI where submissions are made electronically to the GCIC.
d.
In compliance with Federal law 95-544, which provides for the rendering of a "fitness determination," the city will decide whether the applicant has been convicted of, or is under pending indictment for enumerated disqualifiers, as set forth within this chapter.
e.
A person who has consented to the city for a criminal history based on fingerprinting record may request and receive a copy of the criminal history record report from the city at no additional charge. Should the person seek to amend or correct the record, he or she shall be responsible for contacting the GCIC as to Georgia records and/or the FBI concerning records from other jurisdictions maintained in its file.
(g)
All applications shall be sworn to by the applicant before a notary public or other officer authorized to administer oaths.
(h)
The city shall have 60 calendar days to process any completed application for a license under the terms of this chapter, provided that the 60 calendar days may be extended by agreement of the applicant and the city. In the event that the city does not issue a decision on the completed application within this time period, then the application fee shall be refunded to the applicant and shall be waived with respect to the present application only.
(i)
The city clerk or his/her designee shall provide written notice to any applicant whose application is denied under the provisions of this chapter, which shall set forth in reasonable detail the reasons for such denial and shall advise the applicant of the right to appeal under the provisions of this chapter.
(j)
In the event the applicant is denied a license by the state, any alcoholic beverage license fee paid to the city shall be refunded, but all other application and investigative fees paid to the city shall be retained by the city.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-29. - Separate application and separate license for each location of sale.
A separate application must be made for each location of sale, and, if such application is granted, a separate license must be issued for each location of sale.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-30. - Restriction upon transfers.
Licenses under this chapter may not be transferred to another person except as otherwise provided in this chapter. Instead, all persons desiring licenses shall make application to the city for a new license and pay any appropriate fees and occupation taxes, and that person must otherwise meet the requirements hereunder for a new license.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-31. - Change of location.
(a)
All persons desiring to change the physical location of their premises where alcoholic beverages are sold shall make application on a form prescribed by the city clerk or his/her designee.
(b)
An application for a change of location shall include the following information:
(1)
Proof of planning and zoning compliance;
(2)
Proof of building code compliance;
(3)
A legal description of the property upon which the new premises is located; and
(4)
An affidavit from a registered surveyor stating that the establishment complies with any distance or location requirements contained in this chapter or otherwise imposed by law or regulation.
(c)
There shall be separate processing fees to cover the expense of an investigation and the expense of processing the change of location application, which fees shall not be refundable. The amount of the investigation fee and the processing fee for the application shall be as established by the city council, from time to time.
(d)
Failure to furnish data as requested shall cause the change of location application to be denied.
(e)
The city shall have 60 calendar days to process any completed change of location application, provided that the 60 calendar days may be extended by agreement of the applicant and the city. In the event that the city does not issue a decision on the completed change of location application within this time period, then the application fee shall be refunded to the applicant and shall be waived with respect to the present change of location application only.
(f)
The city clerk or his/her designee shall provide written notice to any applicant whose application is denied under the provisions of this chapter, which shall set forth in reasonable detail the reasons for such denial and shall advise the applicant of the right to appeal under the provisions of this chapter.
(g)
If the proposed change of location relates to a license for the sale of distilled spirits on the premises or off-premises, the change of location application shall not be acted upon until the applicant has published in the newspaper which publishes the legal advertisements of the city a notice of his intention to change locations for the sale of distilled spirits. Such notice shall be published at least once during the 30 calendar days immediately preceding request for a change of location. Such notice shall be in large boldface type and shall state:
(1)
The exact location of the place of business for which the change is being sought;
(2)
The names and addresses of each owner of the business; and
(3)
If the applicant is a corporation, the names and titles of all corporate officers.
(h)
Proof of publication of the notice required by this section shall be attached to an application for change of location.
(i)
Any change of location allowed under this section may be denied or revoked on the same basis as for an application for license under this chapter.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-32. - Effect of expiration, surrender, revocation or suspension of license.
In the event that a license expires, is surrendered, revoked or suspended, the establishment shall immediately cease selling alcoholic beverages as of 11:59 p.m. on the date of said expiration, surrender, revocation or suspension. Any appeal procedures related to the suspension or revocation of a license by the city that is utilized by a licensee, as set forth herein, shall have the effect of tolling any revocation or suspension of the license until the appeal process has become final.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-33. - Substitution of licensee.
(a)
In the event that a licensee severs his association with a licensed establishment, whether by resignation or by termination with his employer, then the establishment may continue to sell alcoholic beverages under the existing license for a period not to exceed 45 calendar days from the date that is determined to be the date of severance which shall be subject to being shortened in the event that the license expires or is suspended or revoked.
(b)
The application for a substitute licensee shall require substantially the same information as the initial application, including a complete investigation of the criminal history of the licensee.
(c)
There shall be separate processing fees to cover the expense of an investigation and the expense of processing the substitute licensee application, which fees shall not be refundable. The amount of the investigation fee and the processing fee for the application shall be as established by the city council, from time to time.
(d)
Failure to furnish data as requested shall cause the substitute licensee application to be denied.
(e)
The city shall have 60 calendar days to process any completed substitute licensee application, provided that the 60 calendar days may be extended by agreement of the applicant and the city. In the event that the city does not issue a decision on the completed substitute licensee application within this time period, then the application fee shall be refunded to the applicant and shall be waived with respect to the present substitute licensee application only.
(f)
The city clerk or his/her designee shall provide written notice to any applicant whose application is denied, which shall set forth in reasonable detail the reasons for such denial and shall advise the applicant of the right to appeal under the provisions of this chapter.
(g)
Nothing in this section, however, shall prohibit one or more of the partnership holding a license to withdraw from the partnership in favor of one or more of the partners who were partners at the time of the issuance of the license. This section shall not prohibit transfer of stock between persons who held stock in the corporate ownership at the time of issuance of the license.
(h)
A licensee may take in partners or additional stockholders; provided, however, that any additional partners or any new stockholders holding more than ten percent of any class of corporate stock must be approved by the city council as provided hereunder.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-34. - License required.
(a)
No malt beverage, wines, distilled spirits or any other alcoholic beverage shall be sold or manufactured in the city except under a license issued under this chapter by the city clerk or his/her designee.
(b)
The requirements of this chapter shall be in addition to any other requirements for occupation taxes under this Code and if other provisions of the Code conflict with this chapter, then this chapter shall control.
(c)
Licenses which may be issued under this chapter include:
(1)
Manufacturing alcoholic beverages;
(2)
Wholesale malt beverages and wines;
(3)
Wholesale distilled spirits;
(4)
Retail malt beverages and wines;
(5)
Retail distilled spirits;
(6)
Consumption on the premises malt beverages and wines;
(7)
Consumption on the premises distilled spirits;
(8)
Sales off-premises for catered functions;
(9)
Any combination of subsection (c)(2) and (3) of this section;
(10)
Any combination under subsection (c)(4) and (5) of this section;
(11)
Any combination under subsection (c)(6) and (7) of this section;
(12)
Bottle house; and
(13)
Brew pub.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-35. - Annual renewal.
(a)
Licensees shall make a written application for renewal of licenses issued under this chapter on forms supplied by the city clerk or his/her designee by November 15 of each year, which application may require substantially the same information as the initial application, including a complete investigation of the criminal history of the licensee.
(b)
If the city clerk or his/her designee determines that it would be appropriate under the standards set forth within this chapter to renew the license under consideration, then he/she shall be authorized to approve the application for renewal. Any license which has not been renewed and paid for in full by close of business on the last business day prior to the end of the calendar year shall expire at 11:59 p.m. on December 31 and shall not serve to authorize the license holder to perform under such license after that date and time until such time as the new license has been issued to the license holder.
(c)
Any license which is not renewed during the calendar year during which it was issued but is renewed by January 31 of the subsequent year shall be charged an administrative surcharge of ten percent of the license fee in addition to the license fee itself.
(d)
Any license which is not renewed during the calendar year during which it was issued and is not renewed by January 31 of the subsequent year shall not be renewed. Any person desiring to have a license issued for such an establishment shall be required to initiate a new application process.
(e)
The city shall have a minimum of 30 calendar days to review a completed license renewal application. In the event that the city does not issue a decision on the completed renewal application within this time period, then the application fee shall be refunded to the applicant and shall be waived with respect to the present renewal application only.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-36. - Amount of fees to be set by city council.
All fees associated with alcohol licensing shall be determined from time to time and set by the city council.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-37. - Annual fees.
All annual license fees herein shall be paid prior to the issuance of the license. Any new license granted hereunder during a calendar year shall pay the full license fee without proration; provided, however, that if a license is granted after July 1, the license fee shall be one-half of the annual licenses for the remainder of such calendar year.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-38. - Miscellaneous.
In case of death of any person holding a license, or any interest therein, the license may, with the approval by motion of the city council, and subject to the terms of this chapter, be transferred to the administrator, executor or personal representative of the deceased person, or to the heirs at law of the deceased person, if such heirs meet all of the other qualifications contained herein. The license of the deceased person shall be held by the administrator, executor or personal representative of the deceased person only for the time necessary to complete administration of his estate and to dispose of the license or the interest therein, but in no event shall exceed 12 months.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
