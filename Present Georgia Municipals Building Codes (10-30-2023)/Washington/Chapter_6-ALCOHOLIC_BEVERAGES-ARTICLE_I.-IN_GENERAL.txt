ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 6-1. - Purpose.
This chapter has been enacted for the purposes, among others, of promoting the health and general welfare of the community, to establish reasonable and ascertainable standards for the regulation and control of the licensing and sales of alcoholic beverages; to protect and preserve schools and churches; to give effect to existing land use; and to preserve certain residential areas, with reasonable considerations, among others, to the character of the areas and their peculiar suitability for particular uses, the congestion in the roads and streets, and with a general view of promoting desirable living conditions and sustaining the stability of neighborhoods and property values.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-2. - Sales to be licensed; compliance with ordinance and state law required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or offer for sale for consumption off of the premises or to offer for sale for consumption on the premises any alcoholic beverages within the city without having first complied with the provisions of this chapter. No person shall sell or possess for the purpose of sale any alcoholic beverage unless such person has a license from the city to sell or possess for sale alcoholic beverages. No person shall sell alcoholic beverages or make deliveries of alcoholic beverages beyond the boundaries of the premises covered by the license except as specifically permitted herein.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-3. - Sale within the incorporated areas of the city; license a privilege.
(a)
Alcoholic beverages may be sold within the incorporated areas of the city under an annual license granted by the city clerk or his/her designee as governed by the terms and conditions hereafter provided.
(b)
Any and all licenses granted herein are not a right, but are a mere grant of a privilege to carry on the business during the term of the license subject to all terms and conditions imposed by the ordinances of the city and state law.
(c)
All licenses hereunder shall have printed on the front these words: "THIS LICENSE IS A MERE PRIVILEGE THAT IS SUBJECT TO BEING REVOKED OR SUSPENDED AND IS SUBJECT TO ANY FUTURE ORDINANCES WHICH MAY BE ENACTED. THE CITY IS UNDER NO OBLIGATION TO RENEW THIS LICENSE AFTER ITS EXPIRATION."
(d)
Whenever in this chapter any act is prohibited or is made or declared to be unlawful or an offense, or whenever in this chapter the doing of any act is required or the failure to do any act is declared to be unlawful, where no specific penalty is provided therefor, the violator of any such provision shall be subject to prosecution in municipal court and or the licensee shall be subject to the administrative penalties as set forth herein.
(e)
Any violation of this chapter, of Title 3 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated including any future amendments thereto or of Subtitle 560-2 of the Georgia Regulations pertaining to the alcohol and tobacco tax division of the Georgia Department of Revenue including any future amendments thereto shall subject a licensee to the administrative penalties as set forth herein.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-4. - Definitions.
The definitions set forth in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2 ("Alcoholic Beverages-General Provisions-Definitions"), shall be effective as definitions of the words, terms and phrases used in this chapter. All words, terms and phrases used herein, other than those specifically defined elsewhere in this chapter, shall have the respective meanings ascribed to them in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2, and shall have the same scope and effect that the same words, terms and phrases have where used in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2. For the purpose of this chapter the following definitions shall apply:
Administrative penalty (ies) means that penalty (ies) set forth in sections 6-155 and 6-156 of this chapter, as may be amended from time to time.
Alcohol means ethyl alcohol, hydrated oxide of ethyl, or spirits of wine, from whatever source or by whatever process produced.
Alcoholic beverages means and includes all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, malt beverages, wine or fortified wine.
Bed and breakfast means a business establishment open to the public, which provides sleeping accommodations for rent to the general public on a transient residence basis, maintaining at least one room available for rent, and having at least one dining room with kitchen facilities as required for an eating establishment, and deriving gross sales from the prepared food items and room rentals in an amount exceeding its gross sales from the sale of alcoholic beverages.
Beer or malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any other product, or any combination of such products in water containing not more than 14 percent alcohol by volume, and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer, strong beer, and hard cider as may be defined by state law.
Bona fide nonprofit civic organization is an entity that is exempt from federal income tax pursuant to the provisions of subsection (c), (d) or (e) of 26 U.S.C. Section 501.
Bottle house shall mean a specific place of business, open to the public, which allows guests, patrons, or members to bring in and consume the guest's, patron's, member's alcoholic beverages on the premises.
Brewpub is a restaurant in which beer or malt beverages are manufactured or brewed subject to state law barrel production limitations for retail consumption on the premises and sold solely in draft form.
Building code means and includes all building, plumbing, mechanical, and electrical codes and any other similar technical code adopted by the city.
Church shall mean a permanent building where persons regularly assemble for religious worship.
City shall mean the city of Washington, Georgia.
City administrator or his/her designee shall mean the city administrator and anyone designated by the city administrator to perform the official city duties as set forth in this chapter.
Courtyard means an area open or partially open to the sky and enclosed or surrounded on all sides by buildings, walls, fences or other such structures, or a combination thereof.
Distance for the purposes of this chapter only shall be measured in lineal feet and shall be the most direct route of travel on the ground from the front entrance of the proposed location to the nearest property line of the parcel of property to which the distance is being measured.
Distilled spirits shall mean any alcoholic beverages containing more than 21 percent alcohol obtained by distillation, mixed with water or other substance in solution, and includes brandy, rum, whiskey, gin, cordials, or other spirituous liquors by whatever name called to include fortified wines, as defined by the Federal Alcohol Administration.
Drinking place shall mean a place of business that is authorized to sell alcoholic beverages for immediate consumption on the premises, and where the sale of food (including nonalcoholic beverages) is incidental to the sale of such alcoholic beverages. A drinking place shall not impose a minimum charge or cover charge. Drinking places shall not permit the entry of any person that is under 21 years of age on the premises. Entertainment customarily incidental and subordinate to the traditional operations of a drinking place such as karaoke, individual or small group vocalists and/or instrumentalists that provide background music, recorded music, televised programming, etc., may be supplied for its guests; but in no circumstance shall chairs and/or tables be rearranged for the viewing of such entertainment. Drinking places may include, but shall not be limited to: bars or cocktail lounges.
Golf course shall mean an outdoor golf course covering three acres or more and containing at least nine tees, fairways and greens. "Outdoor golf course sales" refers to sales made outside the clubhouse or other primary structure and on or immediately adjacent to the golf course itself from a mobile or satellite location.
Growler shall mean a container of not more than 64 ounces that is capable of being sealed with a tamper-proof cap and that is designed to be used and is in fact used for the purpose of being filled with a malt beverage from a tap, for consumption off premises.
Hours of operation shall mean the hours during which a licensed establishment may remain open for various purposes, including the sale of alcoholic beverages on or off premises.
Hotel shall mean premises that consist of one or multiple buildings that are under single management that rents rooms and/or suites intended primarily as sleeping accommodations for public rental on a daily or weekly basis. All hotels shall maintain a central, internal lobby, and provide daily room cleaning and linen changes. Supportive activities that are customarily incidental and subordinate to the traditional operations of a hotel such as, but shall not be limited to: drinking places, meeting rooms, recreational and fitness facilities, restaurants, retail, swimming pools or other similar facilities/services, may be supplied. Entertainment shall be restricted to entertainment customarily incidental and subordinate to the traditional operations of a drinking place or restaurant such as karaoke, individual or small group vocalists and/or instrumentalists that provide background music, recorded music, televised programming, etc. Said forms of entertainment may be supplied for its guests, but in no circumstance shall chairs and/or tables be rearranged for the viewing of such entertainment. Motels shall be considered hotels.
Indoor commercial recreational establishment shall mean and is limited to an indoor establishment:
(1)
In which the sale of food (including nonalcoholic beverages) and alcoholic beverages is incidental to its primary activity on the premises and which shall derive at least 50 percent of its total annual gross sales from its primary activity;
(2)
In which meals are served, such place being provided with adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining equipment and a sufficient number and kind of employees to prepare, cook and serve suitable food for its customers. Said establishment shall be prepared to serve food every hour they are open; and
(3)
In which the primary activity on the premises of the indoor commercial recreational establishment shall be family oriented in nature, generally meaning a use that attracts a range of individuals from all age groups. Uses may specifically include, but are not limited to, bowling centers, billiard parlors, video arcades, skating arenas and other similar uses. Bingo parlors, dance halls, nightclubs, adult entertainment and/or sexually related entertainment activities as defined in the city's adult entertainment establishment ordinances, chapter 18 article 3 and similar uses are specifically excluded from this definition of indoor commercial recreational establishments.
License shall mean the written authorization by the city clerk or his/her designee to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages for the specified period of time.
Licensee means the individual to whom a license is issued or, in the case of a partnership, corporation or company, all partners, officers, members, managers, and directors of the partnership, corporation or company.
Live entertainment shall mean any combination of the following activities performed live by one or more persons for the amusement needs of the public generally, whether for a minimum charge or cover charge, or no cover charge at all, which may include, but shall not be limited to, the following: comedy, dancing, magic acts, musical acts (excluding karaoke), revues, theatrical acts (including plays), etc. Adult entertainment shall not be considered a form of live entertainment.
Lounge means a separate room connected with a part of, and adjacent to a restaurant or located in hotels as defined herein with all booths, stools and tables being unobstructed and open to view. All lounges shall be air conditioned and have a seating capacity of at least 20 persons.
Manager means the person who is on the premises of a licensed establishment and who is responsible for the supervision of employees who are involved in the service or sale of alcoholic beverages and who are authorized to interface with and to accept legal documents from the city regarding the sale of alcoholic beverages at that moment in time.
Manufacturer means any maker, producer, or bottler of an alcoholic beverage. The term also means:
(1)
In the case of distilled spirits, any person engaged in distilling, rectifying, or blending any distilled spirits;
(2)
In the case of malt beverages, any brewer; and
(3)
In the case of wine, any vintner.
Moral turpitude shall include any misdemeanor criminal offenses involving fraud, theft, identity theft or dishonesty, misdemeanor drug offenses, and any felony offense. However, all applicants shall be entitled to the full benefits of O.C.G.A. § 42-8-60 et seq. relating to first offender status if they were sentenced under the First Offender Act by the sentencing court.
Nightclub shall mean a place of business that is authorized to supply live entertainment for the amusement needs of the public generally, and may exclude persons under 18 years of age, and where the preparation and sale of food (including nonalcoholic beverages) and/or alcoholic beverages may be incidental to the provision of such live entertainment. Nightclubs may require a minimum charge or a cover charge. Nightclubs may include, but shall not be limited to: comedy clubs, dance clubs, dinner theaters, discotheques (including teen), etc.
Package alcoholic beverages means a bottle, can, keg barrel, or other original consumer container sold by a package store to a consumer for consumption off premises.
Package store shall mean a geographic location within the city wherein a license may be issued for the sale of package alcoholic beverages. Such package store must be operated as a distinct business and cannot be operated in conjunction with or as a part of any other business, except only as provided in this chapter and/or by the laws of this state.
Patio shall be defined as that portion of an establishment that is outside the main structure but only accessible from the main structure and is used for sales of food and/or beverages.
Performing arts facility shall mean and is limited to an establishment:
(1)
That has as its principal objective or business the presentation of live music, mainline dramatic arts, plays, theatre productions and stand-up comedy; and
(2)
That does not feature, show, allow, promote or advertise adult entertainment as defined and regulated in chapter 18 article 3 of the city's Code of Ordinances, including but not limited to adult dancing establishments, adult mini-motion picture theatres, adult motion picture theatres, adult motion picture arcades and erotic dance establishments.
Person means any individual, firm, partnership, cooperative, nonprofit membership corporation, joint venture, association, company, corporation, agency, syndicate, estate, trust, business trust, receiver, fiduciary, or other group or combination acting as a unit, body politic, or political subdivision, whether public, private, or quasi-public.
Premises shall mean:
(1)
The definite closed or partitioned in-facility including, but not limited to, a room, shop or building wherein alcoholic beverages are sold.
(2)
The temporarily or permanently defined areas of publicly owned property approved by the city for the consumption of alcoholic beverages on premises when the alcoholic beverages being consumed are sold by one holding a license for on premises consumption for an establishment that is immediately adjacent to such defined areas of publicly owned property.
(3)
The temporarily defined areas of publicly owned property approved by the city for the consumption of alcoholic beverages on premises when the alcoholic beverages being consumed are sold or distributed by one holding a license for on premises consumption within the temporarily defined areas of publicly owned property and the sales or distribution are actually occurring.
Restaurant shall mean a place of business that is authorized to prepare, cook and sell food (which shall include nonalcoholic beverages) for immediate consumption either on, or off the premises, and where the preparation and sale of alcoholic beverages for immediate consumption on the premises, may be incidental to the sale of such food, for adequate pay, without a minimum charge or cover charge. Restaurants shall not exclude any person, and shall derive at least 50 percent of their total annual gross sales from the preparation and sale of food. Entertainment customarily incidental and subordinate to the traditional operations of a restaurant such as karaoke, individual or small group vocalists and/or instrumentalists that provide background music, recorded music, televised programming, etc., may be supplied for its guests; but in no circumstance shall chairs and/or tables be rearranged for the viewing of such entertainment. Restaurants may include, but shall not be limited to: fast-food restaurants, full-service restaurants or limited-service restaurants.
Retail sales shall mean selling or offering for sale alcoholic beverages to any member of the public wherein consumption on the premises is prohibited.
Retailer or retail dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages at retail only to consumers wherein consumption on the premises is prohibited.
School means such state, county, city, church or other primary or secondary schools that teach the subjects commonly taught in the common schools of this state and shall include private schools where only specialized subjects such as law, stenography, business, music, art, medicine, dentistry, vocational occupations and other special subjects are taught.
Sidewalk café means an area on the sidewalk that is associated with an abutting restaurant that contains chairs and tables, and sometimes benches, that is used for the sale and the consumption of food and/or beverages, outdoors. In no circumstance shall any preparation and/or cooking of food and beverages, nor the cooling, refrigeration, storage or any other similar activity related to the preparation and serving of food and/or beverages, occur within the confines of a sidewalk café.
Temporary special event means an event hosted by a bona fide nonprofit civic organization in which alcoholic beverages are sold for consumption on the premises.
Temporary special event license means a license issued to a bona fide nonprofit civic organization, authorizing the bona fide nonprofit civic organization to sell alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises for a period not to exceed three calendar days.
Wholesaler or wholesale dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages to other wholesale dealers, to retail dealers, or to retail consumption dealers.
Wine shall mean any alcoholic beverages containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products. The term does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this section. Fortified wine means any alcoholic beverage containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes, either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. "Fortified wine" includes, but is not limited to, brandy.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-5. - Inspection of licensed establishments.
At all times that an establishment holding a license issued under this chapter is open for business, the establishment shall be open to inspection by any licensed inspector of the city, or to any person designated by the city administrator. In addition, if the premises are being used before or after hours by employees or the owners or their agents, the premises may be inspected at such time by the designated persons in this section.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-6. - License and signs to be exhibited.
(a)
Each license issued under this chapter shall at all times be kept in public in a public area plainly exposed to view upon the licensed premises.
(b)
All holders of a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages regulated by this chapter shall be required to post such signs in a conspicuous location in their place of business as are required by O.C.G.A. § 3-3-24.2 and Georgia Department of Revenue regulations.
(c)
Failure to post the notices as required herein shall constitute a violation of this chapter and shall be punishable in the municipal court. The penalty contained in this section shall be in addition to any administrative penalties that may be taken with regard to the holders' license as provided in this chapter.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-7. - Responsibility of licensee for violations.
The licensee shall be subject to administrative penalties for any acts of their agents or their employees that are in violation of this chapter or of Title 3 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, or of Subtitle 560-2 of the Georgia Regulations pertaining to the alcohol and tobacco tax division of the Georgia Department of Revenue.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-8. - Production of records; audits.
(a)
In conjunction with any application for a license, or in conjunction with any license which has been issued under the terms hereof, or in conjunction with the revocation, suspension or cancellation of any license, or in conjunction with the payment or nonpayment of any excise tax levied or to be collected under this chapter, the city clerk or his/her designee shall have the right to demand the timely production of all or any part of the records, books, documents, electronic or magnetic media and associated software, reports or invoices of any licensee for the purpose of auditing the records of such licensee, securing compliance by such licensee with the provisions of this chapter, proving or disproving violation of any part of this chapter by any licensee, or showing payment or nonpayment of any taxes, fees, charges or the like due hereunder.
(b)
All licensed establishments must maintain the following records for a three-year period and make the original records or certified copies available to the city upon the city's request within 72 hours:
(1)
Monthly income or operating statements; and
(2)
Monthly Georgia sales and use tax reports.
(c)
Any failure to comply with the terms of this section shall subject the licensee to administrative penalties.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-9. - Invoices.
(a)
Upon each and every delivery by a licensed wholesaler to a licensed retailer, an invoice in triplicate shall be prepared showing the quantities and brands of distilled spirits, malt beverages and wine delivered, together with the price thereof and the excise tax due and collected thereon. The original of such invoice shall be delivered by the wholesaler to the retailer simultaneously with such delivery. The wholesaler shall retain the second copy of such invoice and shall keep it for a period of 36 months after the date of delivery and during such thirty-six-month period, such invoices shall be made available for inspection by the city clerk or his/her designee or duly authorized representatives. The remaining copy of the invoice shall be distributed in accordance with Georgia law. Upon request of the city, a copy of such invoice shall be attached to any reports requested or required by the city.
(b)
Each retailer doing business in the city shall keep and maintain accurate and complete records regarding the purchases and sales made by such business including, but not limited to, the original invoice of purchase by him from a wholesaler of any alcoholic beverages and shall maintain such records for at least 36 months after the date of such purchase. Such records shall reveal the gross sales from the operation of their business and the exact quantities of distilled spirits, malt beverages and wines by size and type of container purchased by such retail dealer together with the dates of delivery of such shipments by wholesale dealers as well as the identity of each wholesale dealer from whom such licensee purchases alcoholic beverages. The city or its duly authorized representative shall have the right to inspect the records of each retailer and make a complete audit of the records of each retailer at any time. Failure of a retailer to maintain proper records showing receipt of alcoholic beverages, payment therefore, and payment of the taxes due thereon to the appropriate wholesaler, and records which clearly show the sales, the amount of sales, and the types of sales of each retailer may, at the discretion of the city clerk or his/her designee, after a hearing as herein provided, result in the revocation or suspension of such license.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-10. - Penalties established for failure to keep records.
(a)
If any wholesaler or retailer fails to keep and maintain the records of sales as required by this chapter or if a wholesaler or retailer refuses to allow an audit as provided by this chapter, then the license of such wholesaler or retailer shall be subject to administrative penalties.
(b)
In the event that an audit of the records required to be kept by wholesalers and retailers under the terms of this chapter are found to vary from the monthly reports required to be furnished by the wholesalers to the city and such discrepancies are not explained to the satisfaction of the city clerk or his/her designee, the license of such wholesaler or retailer shall be subject to administrative penalties. If it appears to the city clerk or his/her designee that any retailer participated in or conspired with any wholesaler to falsify or keep and maintain incorrect records of such sales in order to avoid payment of the taxes imposed by this chapter, then all licenses of the licensees involved in such scheme shall be subject to administrative penalties as provided for under this chapter. In the event that any person intentionally falsified or intentionally failed to keep and maintain correct records of such sales, then that person shall be subject to the penalty provisions contained in the city's Charter.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-11. - Duration of licenses.
(a)
All licenses, except as otherwise indicated, issued under this chapter shall be issued for the remaining portion of a calendar year, and all licenses shall expire at 11:59 p.m. on December 31 of each year regardless of issue date.
(b)
No licensee shall have any vested right to renewal of any license issued hereunder.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-12. - Distance requirements.
(a)
No person knowingly and intentionally may sell or offer to sell any distilled spirits for off-premises consumption:
(1)
Within 200 yards of any school property; or
(2)
Within 100 yards of any church building or alcoholic treatment center owned and operated by the state or any county or municipal government therein.
(b)
No person knowingly and intentionally may sell or offer to sell for off-premises consumption any malt beverages or wines:
(1)
Within 100 yards of any school property; or
(2)
Within 100 yards of any alcoholic treatment center owned and operated by the state or any county or municipal government therein.
(c)
No person knowingly and intentionally may sell or offer to sell alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises:
(1)
Within 100 yards of an alcoholic treatment center owned and operated by the state or any county or municipal government therein.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-13. - Hours of operation.
(a)
Retail distilled spirits. The exterior entrances of retail stores for the sale of distilled spirits hereunder must be locked during the hours for which the sale of any distilled spirits is prohibited. Licensees shall not engage in the sale of any distilled spirits except between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 11:45 p.m., Monday through Saturday, and between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m. on Sunday.
(b)
Retail malt beverages and wines. Licensees in the retail sale of malt beverages and wines shall be allowed to engage in the sale of such beverages for 24 hours a day Monday through Saturday and between the hours of 12:30 p.m. until 11:30 p.m. on Sunday.
(c)
Consumption on the premises. No distilled spirits, malt beverages or wine shall be sold for consumption on the premises except between the hours of 7:00 a.m. and 2:00 a.m. of the following morning on Monday through Friday, between the hours of 7:00 a.m. on Saturday and 1:00 a.m. of the following Sunday morning, and between the hours of 11:00 a.m. and 12:00 midnight on Sunday. Each and every person shall be required to vacate the premises no later than one hour past any required closing time.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017; Res. of 6-10-2019, § 1; Ord. of 12-9-2019)
Sec. 6-14. - Exterior advertisement prohibited.
(a)
No placard or sign which is visible from the exterior of the licensed premises shall make reference to the price of any distilled spirits sold therein, except that tags showing the prices of individual bottles or containers may be affixed to the bottles or containers or to the edge of the shelf whereon the bottles or containers are located.
(b)
Advertisement of malt beverages or wines cannot be affixed or displayed in the window of any establishment licensed for retail sale. Any placards or advertisements of malt beverages or wines must be displayed from inside the location. There shall be no advertisements to the price of any malt beverages or wines visible from the outside of such establishment.
(c)
No show-window advertising or display shall be visible from the exterior of the primary use structure, and no sign shall be erected anywhere within the city advertising the sale of distilled spirits by the drink.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-15. - Prohibition on service of alcoholic beverages.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to serve, dispense, or sell an alcoholic beverage while under the influence of alcohol.
(b)
If an employee violates this section, then the licensee shall be subject to administrative penalties.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Sec. 6-16. - Payment of taxes and fees.
Prior to issuance of any license pursuant to this chapter, an applicant must be in compliance with all applicable requirements under federal, state, and/or city ordinances. Compliance with state and city statute and ordinances shall specifically include the requirement that the applicant or business of the applicant is not delinquent in the payment of any tax or fee owed the city, including, but not limited to, personal or real property taxes, any occupational taxes, sales and use taxes, or payment for any required permit necessary for operation of applicant's business. For purposes of this section, any tax that has been paid, formally appealed to the proper authorities, or is being paid pursuant to a plan authorized and approved by the appropriate tax or revenue commissioner shall not be deemed delinquent.
(Ord. of 8-14-2017)
Secs. 6-17—6-19. - Reserved.
