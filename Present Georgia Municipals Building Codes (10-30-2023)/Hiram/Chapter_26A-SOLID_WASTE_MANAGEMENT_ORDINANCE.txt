Chapter 26A - SOLID WASTE MANAGEMENT ORDINANCE

Sec. 26A-1. - Purpose.
The purpose of this chapter is to provide for the protection of the public health, welfare, and safety by prescribing the conditions and requirements for solid waste collection, transportation, disposal, and management and penalties for noncompliance; to protect and preserve the environment through these conditions and requirements and establishment of a recycling program for various residential solid waste; and to accomplish these goals as efficiently and economically as possible.
(Ord. No. 2013-7, § 1(Exh. A), 6-4-2013)
Sec. 26A-2. - Short title.
This chapter shall be known and may be cited as the City of Hiram, Georgia "Solid Waste Management Ordinance."
(Ord. No. 2013-7, § 1(Exh. A), 6-4-2013)
Sec. 26A-3. - Scope of ordinance.
This chapter applies only to the collection and disposal of residential refuse.
(Ord. No. 2013-7, § 1(Exh. A), 6-4-2013)
Sec. 26A-4. - Definitions.
Unless the context indicates otherwise, the meaning of certain terms used in this chapter is as follows:
City means the City of Hiram, Georgia.
Commercial solid waste means all types of solid waste generated by any commercial establishment including, but not limited to, stores, offices, warehouses, restaurants, and other manufacturing and nonmanufacturing activities.
Construction/remodeling/demolition waste means waste building materials and rubble resulting from construction, remodeling, repair, and demolition operations to structures or property, including, but not limited to, wood, brick, metal, concrete, wall board, and other similar types of non-putrescible wastes generated by such activities.
Franchise means the authorization bestowed by the governing authority pursuant to this chapter to be the person provider the solid waste collection, transportation, and disposal services described in this chapter.
Franchisee means that person selected by the governing authority and authorized by resolution to provide the solid waste collection and disposal services provided for by this chapter.
Hazardous waste means solid or liquid waste material resulting from the manufacture or use of pesticides and drugs (other than normal household use); pathological wastes; highly flammable or explosive wastes; toxic wastes; sewage sludge; and other waste material determined to be a likely hazard to the public health, safety, and welfare including, but not limited to, asbestos and asbestos containing waste, lead and lead containing waste, radioactive materials, and all other materials classified as hazardous by state and federal law.
Person means any person, firm, partnership, association, corporation, company or organization of any kind.
Putrescible waste means waste that is capable of being decomposed by microorganisms, including kitchen wastes, manure, and household garbage.
Refuse means and includes the terms garbage, solid waste, recyclable materials, refuse, rubble, rubbish, trash, and other similar forms of putrescible and non-putrescible waste.
Residential refuse means solid waste derived from single-family households including refuse, garbage, trash, ashes, paper and paper products, agricultural product, and other similar types of putrescible waste. The term does not include yard waste and trimmings, white goods such as appliances and furniture, any construction/remodeling/demolition waste, or any commercial solid waste.
When not inconsistent with the context, words used in the present tense include the future tense and words in the singular also include the plural. The word "shall" is always mandatory not merely directory.
(Ord. No. 2013-7, § 1(Exh. A), 6-4-2013)
Sec. 26A-5. - Establishment of exclusive franchise for collection and disposal of residential solid waste.
(a)
Except as provided otherwise in this chapter, no person other than a franchisee duly authorized by the governing authority shall collect, transport, or dispose of residential refuse in the city.
(b)
Commencing after the first award by resolution of a franchise contract to a franchisee by the governing authority, requests for proposal shall be issued every three years soliciting proposals for the collection, transportation, and disposal of residential refuse. The city shall not be required to accept any proposal so submitted but may reject all proposals and rebid the award of the franchise contract as determined by the governing authority to be in the best interests of the city and the public health, welfare, and safety.
(c)
The request for proposals shall prescribe the terms, conditions, and requirements for due consideration of a proposal as well as the terms, conditions, and requirements for any franchise contract authorized to the extent not contrary to the language of this chapter or any other provision of law. Those responding to requests for proposal must also comply with all other requirements of law governing the provision of services to the city as they may be established or exist from time to time.
(d)
By resolution the governing authority shall select a person to provide all residential refuse collection, transportation, and disposal. Such a selection shall be exclusive, meaning that the person provided shall be the sole provider of residential refuse collection, transportation, and disposal throughout the city.
(e)
No award of a franchise or any services to be provided thereof as provided for in this chapter may be delegated, assigned, or otherwise transferred to any other person without the express permission of the governing authority, to be evidenced only by a duly authorized resolution to that effect.
(f)
Any person wanting to be considered for award of the exclusive franchise must meet the following minimum requirements and provide proper proof thereof:
(1)
Application shall be made as prescribed in a request for proposal. Proposals failing to provide the information prescribed in the request for proposal shall not be accepted or considered;
(2)
A notarized statement certifying that all solid waste collection, transportation, and disposal equipment meets the minimum requirements of the Federal Occupational Safety and Health Administration (OSHA) and any State of Georgia counterpart and, as applicable, the minimum requirements of the Georgia Department of Transportation (GDOT);
(3)
A notarized statement certifying that all heavy equipment drivers and operators are properly licensed as required by law;
(4)
Proof that the proposer, if awarded the franchise, will have the right to use a lawful site to dispose of the residential refuse to be collected and transported along with a letter of solid waste disposal assurance governing the next three years from the owner or operator where the solid waste is to be delivered attesting to the ability to accept and dispose of, within all bounds of applicable law, the solid waster to be delivered for disposal and describing what arrangements are in place to do so; and
(5)
Proof of general liability insurance and workers' compensation as prescribed in this chapter.
(g)
The franchise authorized by this chapter shall continue in force for a period of three years from the date of authorization, unless terminated earlier as provided for in this chapter or franchise agreement; provided, however, that within ten days after the passage of this chapter the franchisee initially authorized by the governing authority shall file with the city manager in writing its unconditional acceptance of this franchise and promise to comply with and abide by all its provisions, terms, and conditions of this chapter.
(h)
The franchisee shall indemnify and hold harmless the city, its elected officials, officers, agents and employees as a result of the grant of this franchise. Such indemnification shall be for, but not limited to, negligence on the part of the franchisee in the maintenance and operation of its refuse collection services.
(i)
The franchisee shall pay all expenses incurred by the city, its elected officials, officers, agents and employees in defending itself or themselves with regard to all damages and penalties mentioned in subsection (h) above. These expenses shall include all out-of-pocket expenses, such as attorney's fees, and shall also include the reasonable value of any services rendered by the city attorney or his assistants or any employees of the city.
(j)
A certificate showing that insurance coverage has been obtained and is currently in force and effect shall be filed with the city manager prior to the commencement of service by the franchisee and within 30 days of the anniversary date of the policy in each and every year.
(k)
The franchisee shall maintain insurance with a reputable company or companies licensed to do business in the State of Georgia to protect the city and the franchisee from any and all claims, injury or damage to the persons or property, both real and personal, caused directly or indirectly by the operation of the refuse collection service. The amount of such insurance shall be equivalent to that maintained by the city but, in no event, shall the insurance be less than $300,000.00 for property damage, per occurrence, $500,000.00 for personal injury, per person, and $1,000,000.00 for personal injury per occurrence. Workmen's compensation insurance shall also be maintained as required by the laws of Georgia.
(Ord. No. 2013-7, § 1(Exh. A), 6-4-2013)
Sec. 26A-6. - Termination of franchise.
(a)
In addition to all other rights and powers pertaining to the city by virtue of this franchise or otherwise, the city reserves the right to terminate or cancel this franchise and all rights and privileges of the franchisee hereunder in the event that the franchisee:
(1)
Violates any provision of this franchise or any rule, order, or determination of the city or the governing authority pursuant to this franchise, except where such violation is without fault or through excusable neglect;
(2)
Revocation of any permit, license, or other permission from any governmental authority necessary to engage in the collection, transportation, and disposal of residential refuse or the failure to timely obtain a renewal of any such permit, license, or other permission;
(3)
Violation of any law applicable to the collection, transportation, and disposal of residential refuse, including all rules of the Georgia Department of Natural Resources and any rules of the Federal Environmental Protection Agency;
(4)
Becomes insolvent, unable or unwilling to pay its debts, or is adjudged bankrupt;
(5)
Attempts to evade any of the provisions of this franchise or practices any fraud or deceit upon the city or the customers served;
(6)
Franchisee has a change in the ownership of 50 percent or more of its stock, without the city's consent; or
(7)
As otherwise provided for in a franchise agreement provided that no provisions in the franchise agreement may override or conflict with any of the provisions of this chapter.
(b)
Such termination and cancellation shall be by resolution duly adopted after 30 days notice to the franchisee and shall in no way affect any of the city's rights under this franchise or any provision of law. In the event that such termination and cancellation depends upon a finding of fact, such findings of fact as made by the governing authority or its designee shall be conclusive. Provided however, that before this franchise may be terminated and canceled under this section, the franchisee must be provided with an opportunity to be heard before the governing authority.
(c)
Upon such a termination, the governing authority, within its discretion, may adopt such temporary measures as deemed necessary to continue to provide these waste collection, transportation, and disposal services and as otherwise is deemed necessary to protect the public health, welfare, and safety until such time as a new franchise can be established.
(Ord. No. 2013-7, § 1(Exh. A), 6-4-2013)
Sec. 26A-7. - Participation required.
Except where provided otherwise in this chapter, all single-family dwellings within the city are required to comply with this chapter and may utilize only the services of the franchisee for the collection, transportation, and disposal of any solid waste. Exempt from this requirement of participation are unoccupied single-family dwellings, defined as a single-family dwelling that has been unoccupied continuously for a period of not less 60 days. Satisfactory proof must be provided to the city by the person wishing to claim this exemption. Should the city determine that false, fraudulent, or misleading information has been provided in an effort to utilize the exemption provided in this provision, then the owner of the property shall be assessed and required to pay all solid waste charges imposed by this chapter as if the property had been occupied for the full extent of the claimed period of unoccupancy. This assessment shall also be a charge and lien against the real property for which said fee is charged and the owner or owners thereof, superior to all other liens except liens for taxes or other assessments. In addition to any other applicable law, the owner of the property may also be fined as provided for in section 26-12.
(Ord. No. 2013-7, § 1(Exh. A), 6-4-2013)
Sec. 26A-8. - Pre-collection practices.
It shall be the duty of the occupant or owner of any residential premises to keep all refuse pending collection and disposal as follows:
(1)
Each residence shall be provided by the franchisee with one 90-gallon roll-a-way poly-cart (hereinafter "cart"), or a smaller roll-a-way cart if requested by the customer as well as such container(s) as necessary for purposes of storing items subject to recycling. All containers shall be durable, watertight, nonabsorbent, rust-resistant, rodent-proof, and easily cleanable. Each cart shall be equipped with handles and tightly fitted covers. Carts and containers that do not conform to these specifications or which have defects causing waste to be scattered upon the ground during collection or which hamper or injure the persons collecting the refuse shall be promptly replace. All refuse to be collected shall be free from liquid and placed in water tight plastic bags and the top secured and placed within the cart or recycling container(s) provided for each residence. All carts and recycling containers shall be kept clean and free of accumulated refuse or wastes and shall be treated with an effective insecticide if necessary to prevent the creation of a nuisance.
(2)
The franchisee shall distribute to all customers written instructions on collection practices and requirements.
(3)
Each owner shall prevent the continued, excessive and unsightly accumulation of refuse upon the property occupied by him or public thoroughfares adjoining his property.
(4)
It shall be a violation of this chapter to place or cause to be placed in any refuse container for collection any acid, explosive material, inflammable product, toxic waste, medical or quarantined waste products or any other dangerous or corrosive material of any kind.
(5)
The disposal of wearing, bedding or other refuse from homes or other places where highly infectious diseases have prevailed will be at additional expense to the resident. Such refuse should not be placed inside the cart for routine collection. The resident should contact the franchisee and arrange for special collection of these materials.
(6)
Ashes shall be placed and maintained in separate containers. It shall be the responsibility of the customer to ascertain that all ashes are cool and that no hot coals remain.
(7)
Yard waste or trimmings will be at additional expense to the resident and subject to the requirements of the franchisee.
(Ord. No. 2013-7, § 1(Exh. A), 6-4-2013)
Sec. 26A-9. - Storing refuse.
(a)
No person shall place any refuse in any street, alley, or other public place, or upon any private property whether owned by such person or not, within the city except it be in proper containers for collection or under express approval granted by the franchisee. Nor shall any person throw or deposit any refuse in any stream or other body of water.
(b)
Any unauthorized accumulation of refuse on any premises is hereby declared to be a nuisance and is prohibited. Failure to remove any existing accumulation of refuse within 30 days of the effective date of this chapter shall be deemed a violation of this chapter.
(c)
No person shall cast, place, sweep, or deposit anywhere within the city any refuse in such a manner that it may be carried or deposited by the elements upon any street, sidewalk, alley, sewer, parkway, or other public place or into any occupied premises within the city.
(Ord. No. 2013-7, § 1(Exh. A), 6-4-2013)
Sec. 26A-10. - Collection practices.
(a)
Refuse accumulated by residents shall be collected at curbside once each week. The day of collection shall be established by mutual agreement of the city and the franchisee. The franchisee shall observe three annual holidays. They are as follows: New Year's Day, Thanksgiving Day, and Christmas Day. Should one of these holidays fall on a collection day, the collection day shall be the following day.
(b)
There shall be no collections before 7:00 a.m. or after sunset.
(c)
The franchisee will provide routine maintenance or replacement of carts and recycling containers due to reasonable wear and tear. The customer shall be responsible for payment for any cart or recycling container which must be replaced as a result of abuse, neglect, loss, or theft.
(d)
The Franchisee should attempt to handle missed pick ups on the same day, if reported by 2:00 p.m. All missed pick ups shall be corrected within 24 hours.
(e)
Carts and recycle containers shall be placed at curbside by 7:00 a.m. on scheduled pick up days. Carts and recycle containers shall not be placed curbside more than 12 hours prior to the regularly scheduled pick up day and shall be removed within 12 hours following the regularly scheduled pick up day. Carts and recycle containers shall be placed within 15 feet of the curb or edge of the payment/asphalt of the street or alley and shall be accessible to the side of the street or alley from which the collection is made. However, carts and recycle containers shall not be placed in or on the surface of the roadway, street or alley.
(f)
Persons who are unable to place their cart or recycle containers at curbside due to physical handicaps may apply to the city for an exemption and, if approved, may receive back door service at their residence at the same monthly rate for curbside service. All persons residing within said residence must be physically handicapped and approved by the city before this service is provided.
(Ord. No. 2013-7, § 1(Exh. A), 6-4-2013)
Sec. 26A-11. - Fees charged for services provided.
(a)
The city shall have the power, right and authority to assess, levy, collect and enforce monthly a solid waste collection, transportation, and disposal assessment (hereinafter "sanitation assessment") in such amount or amounts as provided from time to time by resolution of the governing authority as well as a deposit and administrative fee to be paid by those required to participate in the solid waste program established by this chapter, such fees to be paid in connection with and prior to the commencement of solid waste collection, transportation and disposal services to defray the reasonable costs incurred by the city in establishing and operating this program. The monthly assessment and the deposit and administrative fee shall be charged to the occupant of the residential property being serviced. This monthly assessment as well as the deposit and administrative fee shall also be a charge and lien against the real property for which said fee was charged and the owner or owners thereof, superior to all other liens except liens for taxes or other assessments.
(b)
The governing authority may in its discretion establish a separate, reduced monthly charge for customers age 65 or older.
(c)
Subject to the approval of the governing authority, the city manager and/or his designee is directed to establish guidelines for a reduced monthly charge or waiver of any fee prescribed by this chapter for those who can demonstrate a financial hardship.
(d)
A 15 percent penalty shall be assessed on all accounts considered delinquent.
(e)
All accounts shall be considered delinquent if not paid by the 15th day of the month in which the monthly statement is issued. All delinquent accounts shall be assessed a 15 percent penalty and are subject to stoppage of service without notice. If a delinquent account is not paid within 30 days, all refuse collection may cease for that account. Service shall be resumed thereafter only on payment of the accumulated fees for the period of collection and of the period of non-collection plus penalties. Additionally, the city shall have the right to collect a deposit equal to two months of service fees before reestablishing sanitation collection at any property on which sanitation collection was discontinued due to nonpayment or late payment of the fee required by this chapter.
(f)
The stoppage of services hereinbefore authorized for non-payment of collection charges shall be in addition to the right of the city to proceed for the collection of such unpaid charges in any manner provided by law for the collection of a municipal claim and may be enforced in any manner provided by law for recovery of same.
(Ord. No. 2013-7, § 1(Exh. A), 6-4-2013; Ord. No. 2019-17, § 1(Exh. A), 12-3-2019)
Sec. 26A-12. - Penalties for customers who fail to comply; liens.
Excepting the failure to pay the monthly charges assessed for the services to be provided, occupants of single-family dwellings violating any of the provisions of this chapter shall be punished by a fine not exceeding $1,000.00. Such fines shall also be a charge and lien against the real property for which said fee was charged and the owner or owners thereof, superior to all other liens except liens for taxes or other assessments.
(Ord. No. 2013-7, § 1(Exh. A), 6-4-2013)
Sec. 26A-13. - Enforcement.
The city manager or his designee is entrusted with implementation, administration, and enforcement of this chapter. The City of Hiram, Georgia Police Department is also authorized to issue, as needed, any citations for violations of the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 2013-7, § 1(Exh. A), 6-4-2013)
Sec. 26A-14. - Collection of waste other than residential refuse.
The franchise and services provided pursuant to this chapter apply only to the collection, transportation, and disposal of residential refuse as defined in this chapter. The services provided by this chapter do not encompass the collection, transportation, and disposal of yard waste and trimmings, white goods (e.g., appliances and furniture), or any waste constituting or containing any hazardous waste. Each occupant or owner of property encompassed by this chapter must make separate arrangements with the franchisee for the collection, transportation, and disposal of such other waste items or product and will be subject to and responsible for the franchisee's fee schedule it charges for the collection, transportation, and disposal of same.
(Ord. No. 2013-7, § 1(Exh. A), 6-4-2013)
Sec. 26A-15. - Limited phase-in period for customers obligated by contract to another solid waste service provider; penalties.
In recognition that at the time of adoption of this chapter, some city residents in single-family dwellings may be already obligated by contract to another person or entity for the collection, transportation, and disposal of solid waste, the city prescribes the following:
(1)
Such residents will not be charged the fees prescribed by this chapter and shall not be required to participate in the exclusive franchise established hereby until expiration of such a contact, provided that the contract is lawful and enforceable and was properly executed prior to adoption of this chapter.
(2)
Such residents may not avoid, evade or be relieved of participation in the exclusive franchise established by this chapter, including payment of the fees associated with the exclusive franchise, by renewing or extending contracts after the adoption of this chapter that predate the adoption of the chapter.
(3)
Within 30 days of adoption of the ordinance from which this chapter derived, each resident who wishes to be exempted from participation because of the existence of a contract with another person or entity for the collection, transportation, and disposal of solid waste shall provide the city manager a copy of the contract and identify the date of expiration of the contract. Any resident who fails to do so will be required to pay the fees imposed by the city for the franchise regardless of the existence of any contract with another person or entity for the collection, transportation, and disposal of solid waste.
(4)
If such a contract is terminated earlier than its prescribed term, the resident is required to immediately notify the city manager. Upon such a termination, the resident shall be obligated to utilize the services of the franchisee and to pay the monthly charge required by the city, subject to all other provisions of this chapter.
(5)
If such a contract is terminable at will by the resident, then the resident shall terminate the contract immediately or as prescribed in the contract and immediately notify the city manager. Upon such a termination, the resident shall be obligated to utilize the services of the franchisee and to pay the monthly charge required by the city, subject to all other provisions of this chapter.
(6)
All fees imposed or accruing to a resident by operation of these provisions shall also be a charge and lien against the real property for which said fee was charged and the owner or owners thereof, superior to all other liens except liens for taxes or other assessments.
(Ord. No. 2013-7, § 1(Exh. A), 6-4-2013)
Sec. 26A-16. - Severability.
If any section, subsection, sentence, clause, phrase or portion of this chapter is for any reasons held invalid or unconstitutional by any court of competent jurisdiction, such portion shall be deemed a separate, distinct, and independent provision and such holding shall not affect the validity of the remaining portions hereof.
(Ord. No. 2013-7, § 1(Exh. A), 6-4-2013)
Sec. 26A-17. - Repealer.
All ordinances and parts of ordinances in conflict with this chapter are hereby repealed.
(Ord. No. 2013-7, § 1(Exh. A), 6-4-2013)
