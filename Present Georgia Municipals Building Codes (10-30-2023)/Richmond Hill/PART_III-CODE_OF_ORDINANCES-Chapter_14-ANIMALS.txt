ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 14-1. - Definitions.
modified
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Animal means any living creature, domestic or wild.
Animal control officer means an employee of the city whose duties specifically include enforcement of the provisions of this chapter.
At large means off the premises of the owner and not under the control of the owner, a member of his immediate family, or some other person, either by leash, cord, chain or other holding device.
Dangerous animal means any wild mammal, reptile or fowl that is not naturally tame or gentle but is of a wild nature or disposition and that would constitute a danger to human life or property if not kept or maintained in a safe manner or in secure quarters; and any domestic mammal, reptile or fowl that, because of its size, vicious propensity, or other characteristics, would constitute a danger to human life or property if not kept or maintained in a safe manner or in secure quarters.
Dangerous dog means any dog that, according to records of an appropriate authority:
(1)
Inflicts a severe injury on a human being without provocation, on public or private property;
(2)
Aggressively bites, attacks or endangers the safety of humans or other domesticated animals without provocation after the dog has been classified as a potentially dangerous dog and after the owner has been notified of such classification.
Governing authority means the mayor and city council.
Owner means any natural person or legal entity, including but not limited to a corporation, partnership, firm or trust, owning, possessing, harboring, keeping, or having custody or control of a dog or cat within the territorial boundaries of the city.
Potentially dangerous dog means any dog who has, without provocation, attempted to bite or harm any human or animal on public or private property.
Proper enclosure means an enclosure for keeping a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog, while on the owner's property, securely confined indoors or in a securely enclosed and locked pen, fence or structure suitable to prevent the entry of young children and designed to prevent the dog from escaping. Any such pen or structure shall have secure sides and a secure top; and if the dog is enclosed within a fence, all sides of the fence shall be of sufficient height and the bottom of the fence shall be constructed or secured in such a manner as to prevent the dog's escape either from over or under the fence. Any such enclosure shall also provide protection from the elements for the dog.
Records of appropriate authority means records of any state, county or municipal law enforcement agency; records of any county board of health; records of any federal, state or local court; or records maintained by the animal control officer pursuant to this article.
Severe injury means any physical injury that results in broken bones or disfiguring lacerations requiring multiple sutures or cosmetic surgery or a physical injury that results in death.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-2)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 14-2. - Bird sanctuary; wildlife.
(a)
The entire area embraced within the corporate limits of the city is designated as a bird sanctuary.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to trap, hunt, molest or kill any wild bird or to rob any wild bird's nest; however, if nuisance birds such as starlings or pigeons are found to be congregating in such number in a particular locality so as to constitute a nuisance or a menace to health or property in the opinion of the mayor and city council, those birds may be destroyed as humanely as possible, under the supervision of the animal control officer, in such numbers and in such manner as is deemed advisable by the mayor and city council.
(c)
It shall be unlawful to trap, hunt, molest or kill any wild game in the city except under supervision of the animal control officer, in such numbers and in such manner as is deemed advisable by the mayor and city council.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-1)
Sec. 14-3. - Dangerous animals.
It shall be unlawful for any person to permit any dangerous or vicious animal of any kind to run at large within the city. The animal control officer of the city shall be authorized to use such force as is necessary, including police officers, to prevent any such dangerous or vicious animal from causing harm to any person or property.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-3)
State Law reference— Wild animals, O.C.G.A. § 27-5-4 et seq.
Sec. 14-4. - Animals running at large prohibited.
modified
(a)
Whether licensed or unlicensed, any person owning or having custody of an animal within the city shall confine such animal on the premises of the owner or on the premises of some responsible person authorized by the owner. Animals shall not be permitted to run at large on any street, alley or any other place in the city other than the premises of the guardian or owner of the animal except on a leash not more than six feet long and in the care of a competent person.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to permit any animal, including, but not limited to cattle, horses, swine, sheep, goats or poultry to run at large in the city. If any such animal is found to be running at large in any public place in the city, it shall be impounded in the manner provided in this chapter.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-4; Ord. of 8-15-94, § 9-3-4; Ord. of 8-15-95, § 9-3-4; Ord. of 5-6-15, § I)
State Law reference— Livestock running at large, O.C.G.A. § 4-3-1 et seq.; permitting dogs in heat to run at large, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-6.
Sec. 14-5. - Impoundment of animals running at large.
modified
(a)
Authorized. It shall be the duty of the animal control officer to seize all animals running at large in violation of the provisions of this article and to impound those animals in the city pound or other suitable place. The animal control officer shall report any such impoundment to the appropriate administrative person at the city hall, giving a description of the animal impounded, and whether or not the animal has been licensed and tagged.
(b)
Notice to owner. Not later than three days after the impounding of any animal, the owner, if known, will be notified. If the owner of the animal is unknown at the expiration of five days, the animal will become eligible for adoption.
(c)
Redemption of animals. The owner of any animal impounded by the city may redeem the same by paying all costs, charges and penalties assessed that have accrued up to the time of making the redemption; and when the same are paid to the appropriate administrative person at city hall, it shall be the duty of the animal control officer to release the animal from the pound to the owner. The above-mentioned fees will be set and amended as needed by the mayor and city council. Under no circumstances will an animal be released without being treated for rabies if needed and being properly registered with city and issued appropriate tags.
(d)
Cost of impoundment. There will be a pickup fee for the first offense, a higher fee for the second offense, and a substantially higher fee for the third and subsequent offenses. In addition, there will be a daily boarding fee per animal. The above-mentioned fees will be set and amended by the mayor and city council as needed.
(e)
Disposition of unclaimed, unadopted or infected animals. If at the expiration of 20 days after impoundment any animal has not been claimed or picked up by the owner or owners, or has not been adopted by some other person, the animal will be disposed of in a humane manner. The responsible administrative person at the city hall, after consultation with the mayor, can order a stay of execution for any animal in special cases. Any animal that appears to be suffering from rabies or infected with hydrophobia, mange, or other infectious or dangerous disease shall not be released but may be destroyed as soon as the infection or disease is discovered.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-5)
Sec. 14-6. - Enclosures for animals or fowl.
Any housing or enclosure used for the keeping of animals or fowl will be well drained, free from accumulations of animal excrement and objectionable odors, and otherwise clean and sanitary.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-6)
Sec. 14-7. - Inhumane treatment of animals.
It shall be the responsibility of the animal control director to cite any person found to be keeping any animal in an inhumane manner in this city. Any such information shall be passed on to the mayor's office or animal control director. Any such person found to be guilty of inhumane treatment of any animal will be charged with a misdemeanor and will be punished appropriately.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-7; Ord. of 6-7-94(2), § 9-3-7)
State Law reference— Cruelty to animals, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-4; Georgia Animal Protection Act, O.C.G.A. § 4-11-1 et seq.
Sec. 14-8. - License and registration required.
(a)
All dogs and cats over three months of age kept, harbored or maintained by their owners in this city shall be required to be licensed and registered in a manner provided by this section.
(b)
Application for a dog or cat license shall be made at city hall or the county animal control office upon forms provided for that purpose; and the application shall state the name and address of the owner and the name, breed, color and sex of each dog or cat sought to be registered.
(c)
Each application for a dog or cat license shall be accompanied by a license fee as fixed from time to time by the mayor and city council and the county commission.
(d)
Upon payment of the license fee, the city clerk shall issue to the owner a license certificate and a metallic tag for each dog or cat licensed. The shape of the tag shall be changed every year and shall have stamped thereon the number on the certificate. Every owner shall be required to provide each dog or cat with a collar to which the license tag must be affixed, and shall see that the collar and tag are worn at all times. If a tag is lost or destroyed, a duplicate will be issued by the city clerk upon presentation of a receipt showing the payment of the license fee for the current year, and the payment of a fee for issuance of the duplicate.
(e)
Animal tags shall not be transferred from one animal to another, and no refunds shall be made on any fee because of death of the animal or the owner's leaving the city before expiration of the license period.
(f)
The provisions of this section shall not be construed to apply to dogs or cats whose owners are nonresidents temporarily within the city, nor to dogs or cats brought into the city for the purpose of participating in any show, nor to Seeing-Eye dogs properly trained to assist blind persons when those dogs are actually being used by blind persons for the purpose of aiding them in going from place to place.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-8; Ord. of 6-7-94(2), § 9-3-8)
Sec. 14-9. - Unlicensed dogs and cats running at large.
modified
It shall be unlawful for any owner or possessor of any licensed or unlicensed dog or cat to allow the animal to run at large within the city. All such dogs or cats found running at large shall be immediately impounded by the animal control officer as provided for in section 14-5.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-9; Ord. of 6-7-94(2), § 9-3-9; Ord. of 5-6-15, § II)
Sec. 14-10. - Vaccinations.
It shall be unlawful for the owner of any dog or cat to keep, maintain or allow such animal to run at large unless it shall have been vaccinated by a licensed veterinary surgeon with antirabies vaccine, within one year preceding the date on which such dog or cat is kept, maintained or allowed to run at large.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-10; Ord. of 6-7-94(2), § 9-3-10)
Sec. 14-11. - Rabies.
If a dog or cat is believed to have rabies or has been bitten by an animal suspected of having rabies, such animal shall be confined by a leash or a chain on the owner's premises and shall be placed under observation by the animal control department at the expense of the owner for a period of ten days. The owner shall notify the animal control officer of the fact that his dog or cat has been exposed to rabies; and at his direction, the animal control officer is empowered to have such dog or cat removed from the owner's premises to a veterinary hospital or an animal control shelter for observation for a period of ten days at the expense of the owner.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-11; Ord. of 6-7-94(2), § 9-3-11)
State Law reference— Control of rabies, O.C.G.A. § 31-19-1 et seq.
Sec. 14-12. - Nuisance.
modified
Any animal, licensed or unlicensed, that creates a nuisance to the inhabitants of the city will be impounded. The following conditions may be declared a nuisance:
(1)
Dangerous or diseased animals.
(2)
Dogs that chase motor vehicles.
(3)
Stray animals.
(4)
Female dogs in heat when not confined to the owners' property.
(5)
Animals tearing in to household garbage.
(6)
The keeping of any animal that shall disturb the comfort or repose of any persons in the city by making loud long, continuous noise, or frequent noise or encroaching upon the property of another causing damage to said property.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-12; Ord. of 6-7-94(2), § 9-3-12; Ord. of 5-2-00; Ord. of 5-6-15, § III)
Sec. 14-13. - Penalties.
(a)
The keeping of any animal in violation of this article shall be an offense against the city, punishable, upon conviction, as provided in section 1-12.
(b)
Violations of article II of this chapter shall result in the issuance of a state warrant pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 4-8-28.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-15; Ord. of 5-2-00)
Sec. 14-14. - Removal of canine fecal matter.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person owning, possessing, harboring or having care, charge, control or custody of any dog, not to remove any feces left by that dog on any sidewalk, gutter, street, tree, lawn, play area, park, or upon private property not owned by the person, or upon any other public premises and such is hereby deemed to be a public nuisance and prohibited. Dog waste shall be immediately removed by placing said matter in a closed or sealed container and thereafter disposing of it in a trash receptacle, sanitary disposal unit or other closed or sealed refuse container.
(b)
Each and every violation of this code section shall be punishable to the extent provided by code section 1-12.
(c)
This section shall not apply to visually impaired persons who have the charge, control or use of a guide dog.
(Ord. of 6-1-99)

Sec. 14-46. - Investigation.
Upon receiving a report of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog within the animal control officer's jurisdiction from a law enforcement agency, animal control agency, rabies control officer, or county board of health, the animal control officer shall make such investigations and inquiries with regard to such report as necessary to carry out the provisions of this article.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-13(a))
Sec. 14-47. - Notice to owner.
(a)
Whenever the animal control officer classifies a dog as a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog, notice shall be given the dog's owner in writing by certified mail to the owner's last known address of such classification or reclassification.
(b)
The notice to the owner shall meet the following requirements:
(1)
The notice shall be in writing and mailed by certified mail to the owner's last known address;
(2)
The notice shall include a summary of the animal control officer's finding that formed the basis for the dog's classification as a dangerous or a potentially dangerous dog;
(3)
The notice shall be dated and shall state that the owner, within 15 days after the date shown on the notice, has a right to request a hearing on the animal control officer's determination that the dog is a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog before the governing authority;
(4)
The notice shall state that if a hearing is not requested, the animal control officer's determination that a dog is a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog will become effective for the purpose under this section on the day after the last day on which the owner has a right to request a hearing; and
(5)
The notice shall include a form to request a hearing before the governing authority and shall provide specific instructions on mailing or delivering the form to the city.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-13(a), (b))
Sec. 14-48. - Hearing.
When the governing authority receives a request for a hearing, it shall schedule such hearing within 30 days after receiving the request. The governing authority shall notify the dog owner in writing by certified mail of the date, time and place of the hearing. At the hearing, the owner of the dog shall be given the opportunity to testify and present evidence; and in addition thereto the governing authority shall receive such other evidence and hear such other testimony as the governing authority may find reasonably necessary to make a determination either to sustain, modify or overrule the animal control officer's classification of the dog. Within ten days after the date of the hearing, the governing authority shall notify the owner in writing by certified mail of its determination on the matter. If such determination is that the dog is a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog, the notice shall specify it is effective on the third day following the date of receipt by the owner.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-13(c))
Sec. 14-49. - Certificate of registration.
(a)
Subject to the requirements of this article, the animal control officer shall issue a certificate of registration to the owner of a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog if the owner presents to the animal control officer or if the animal control officer finds sufficient evidence of:
(1)
A proper enclosure to confine the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog;
(2)
The posting of the premises where the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog is located with a clearly visible sign warning that there is a dangerous dog on the premises, which sign must substantially conform to the design provided by the department of natural resources; and
(3)
Payment to the city of a fee in the amount specified in the schedule of fees and charges to defray the cost of administering and enforcing this article.
(b)
In addition, the owner of a dangerous dog shall present evidence to the animal control officer of:
(1)
A policy of insurance in the amount of at least $15,000.00 issued by an insurer authorized to transact business within the state insuring the owner of the dog against liability for any personal injuries inflicted by the dangerous dog; or
(2)
A surety bond in the amount of $15,000.00 or more issued by a surety company authorized to transact business in this state payable to any person injured by the dangerous dog.
(c)
Certificates of registration shall be renewed on an annual basis. At the time of an annual renewal of the certificate of registration, the animal control officer shall require evidence from the owner or make such investigation as may be necessary to verify that the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog is continuing to be confined in a proper enclosure and that the owner is continuing to comply with other provisions of this article.
(d)
Issuance of a certificate of registration or the renewal of a certification of registration by the local government does not warrant or guarantee that the requirements specified in subsection (c) of this section are maintained by the owner of a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog on a continuous basis following the date of the issuance of the initial certificate of registration or following the date of any annual renewal of such certificate.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-13(d)—(f))
Sec. 14-50. - Change of status.
(a)
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall notify the animal control officer within 24 hours if the dog is on the loose, is unconfined, has attacked a human, has died, or has been sold or donated. If the dog has been sold or donated, the owner shall also provide the name, address, and telephone number of the new owner of the dog.
(b)
The owner of a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog shall notify the animal control officer if the owner is moving from the animal control officer's jurisdiction.
(c)
The owner of a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog who is a new resident of the state shall register the dog in the manner provided by this article within 30 days after becoming a resident.
(d)
The owner of a dangerous or a potentially dangerous dog who moves from one jurisdiction to another within the state shall register the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog in the manner provided by this article within ten days after becoming a resident in the new jurisdiction.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-13(g)—(j))
Sec. 14-51. - Possession of dangerous or potentially dangerous dog without certification.
It shall be unlawful for an owner to have or possess within the city a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog without a certificate of registration issued in accordance with section 14-49.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-14(a))
Sec. 14-52. - Restraint required.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for an owner of a dangerous dog to permit the dog to be outside a proper enclosure unless the dog is muzzled and restrained by a substantial chain or leash and is under the physical restraint of a responsible person.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for an owner of a potentially dangerous dog to permit the dog to be outside a proper enclosure unless the dog is restrained by a substantial chain or leash and is under the restraint of a responsible person.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-14(b), (c))
Sec. 14-53. - Warning sign.
It shall be unlawful for an owner of a dangerous dog to fail to post a warning sign on the premises where a dangerous dog is located in accordance with section 14-49.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-14(d))
Sec. 14-54. - Impoundment.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the animal control officer and any person under his employ to impound a dangerous dog if:
(1)
The owner does not secure a certificate of registration in accordance with section 14-49;
(2)
The owner does not secure liability insurance or meet the bond requirement of section 14-49;
(3)
The owner does not keep the dog within a proper enclosure or permits the dog to roam or run free.
(b)
Any dog impounded under the provisions of this section shall be returned to the owner upon the owner's compliance with the provisions of this article and upon payment of reasonable impoundment cost, as fixed by a schedule approved by the governing authority. Notice of impoundment under this section shall be given the owner, in writing, by certified mail, at his last known address. The notice shall state that if the owner has not complied with the provisions of this article within 20 days from receipt of such notice, the dog shall be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner; however, no dog shall be destroyed under this section unless it has been held a full 20 days, exclusive of the date of giving notice.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-14(e), (f))
Sec. 14-55. - Liability of owner.
The owner of a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog shall be solely liable for any injury to or death of a person caused by such dog. Under no circumstances shall the city or any employee or official of the city who enforces or fails to enforce the provisions of this article be held liable for any damages to any person who suffers an injury inflicted by a dog that has been identified as being a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog; by a dog the city has failed to identify as a dangerous or a potentially dangerous dog; by a dog that has been identified as being a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog but has not been kept or restrained in the manner described in this article; or by a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog whose owner has not maintained insurance coverage or a surety bond as required in this article.
(Ord. of 9-1-92, § 9-3-14(g))
