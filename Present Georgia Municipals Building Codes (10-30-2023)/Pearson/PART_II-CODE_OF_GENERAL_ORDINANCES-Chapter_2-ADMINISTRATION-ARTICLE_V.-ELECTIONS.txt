ARTICLE V. - ELECTIONS[4]

Sec. 2-180. - No absentee ballots used.
No absentee ballots shall be authorized for use in city elections.
(Code 1975, § 3-101)
Sec. 2-181. - City hall designated as polling place.
The city hall is hereby designated as the polling place for all elections.
(Code 1975, § 3-102)
Sec. 2-182. - Registrars.
The mayor and council shall appoint three persons who shall serve as registrars.
(Code 1975, § 3-103)
State Law reference— Person convicted of certain crimes not to be eligible for nomination or election to public office or serve as registrar, deputy registrar, or poll officer, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-8.
Sec. 2-183. - Compensation of managers.
The chief manager and the assistant managers for each election shall be compensated for the performance of their duties, in an amount as set by resolution of the mayor and council.
(Code 1975, § 3-104)
Sec. 2-184. - Election clerks.
There shall be two election clerks, who shall be compensated for the performance of their duties, in an amount as set by resolution of the mayor and council.
(Code 1975, § 3-105)
Sec. 2-185. - Public notice of elections.
Public notices of city elections shall be published in the Atkinson County Citizen at least 30 days prior to the elections.
(Code 1975, § 3-106)
Sec. 2-186. - Superintendent.
The mayor and council shall by resolution annually appoint a municipal election superintendent who shall exercise those powers and duties as set forth in O.C.G.A. §§ 21-2-70 and 21-2-70.1 and other applicable provisions of the state election code.
(Code 1975, § 3-107)
Sec. 2-187. - Voters registration deadline.
(a)
Any person desiring to vote in a general primary, general election, or presidential preference primary shall submit an application to register to vote by the close of business on the fifth Monday or, if such Monday is a legal holiday, by the close of business on the following business day prior to the date of such general primary, general election, or presidential preference primary.
(b)
Any person desiring to vote in any special primary or special election shall submit an application to register to vote no later than the close of business on the fifth day after the date of the call for the special primary or special election, excluding Saturdays, Sundays, and legal holidays of this state; except that:
(1)
If such special primary or special election is held in conjunction with a general primary, general election, or presidential preference primary, the registration deadline for such special primary or special election shall be the same as the registration deadline for the general primary, general election, or presidential preference primary in conjunction with which the special primary or special election is being conducted; or
(2)
If such special primary or special election is not held in conjunction with a general primary, general election, or presidential preference primary, but is held on one of the dates specified in O.C.G.A. § 21-2-540 for the conduct of special elections to present a question to the voters or special primaries or special elections to fill vacancies in elected county or municipal offices, the registration deadline for such a special primary or special election shall be at the close of business on the fifth Monday prior to the date of the special primary or election or, if such Monday is a legal holiday, by the close of business on the following business day.
(Code 1975, § 3-108)
Sec. 2-188. - Same; designation of specific office.
In any primary or election in which two or more offices with the same title are to be filled by vote of the same elections, each candidate shall designate the specific office he is seeking, name the person such candidate is seeking to succeed, and give such other appropriate designation as may be required by the secretary of state or municipal election superintendent each time such candidate qualifies with his party in the case of a primary, files a notice of candidacy in the case of an election, or files a notice of candidacy as a write-in candidate.
(Code 1975, § 3-111)
Sec. 2-189. - Qualifying fees for councilmembers.
The qualifying fees for councilmembers and the mayor shall be set by resolution of the mayor and council from time to time, and available for public examination in the fee and rate schedule in the office of the city clerk.
(Res. of 8-9-2005)
Sec. 2-190. - Compensation for managers and clerks.
The election superintendent shall designate two managers and three clerks each to be compensated in an amount which shall be fixed by resolution of the mayor and council from time to time.
(Res. of 8-9-2005)
Sec. 2-191. - Additional duties of superintendent.
The superintendent of election shall appoint a custodian of the voting machines.
(Res. of 8-9-2005)
Secs. 2-192—2-211. - Reserved.
