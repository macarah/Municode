ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 20-1. - Adoption of rules of road by reference.
Pursuant to Chapter 6 of Title 40 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-372 through 40-6-376, code sections of that chapter known as the Uniform Rules of the Road and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of this city with like effect as if recited herein.
Sec. 20-2. - Penalty.
Every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this chapter shall be punished in accordance with the provisions of section 1-14.
Sec. 20-3. - Official traffic signs and signals; designation, placement and maintenance.
(a)
The mayor and chairman of the public safety committee shall determine and designate the placing and character of all official traffic signs and signals. All such signs and signals shall be uniform throughout the city, so far as is practicable; provided that all official traffic signs and signals now erected and in operation are hereby designated official traffic signs and signals.
(b)
Subject to the selection required by subsection (a) above, the chief of police is hereby authorized, and as to those signs and signals required hereunder it shall be his duty, to place and maintain, or cause to be placed and maintained, all official traffic signs and signals.
(Code 1967, § 23-1)
Sec. 20-4. - Signs, signals, etc., to be obeyed.
All vehicular traffic in the city shall observe traffic signs, signals and traffic directions, whether same appear in the form of printed signs or lines and arrows painted on the surface of the streets or sidewalks.
(Code 1967, § 23-2)
Sec. 20-5. - Skaters or bicycle riders holding to vehicles.
It shall be unlawful for any person, while riding a bicycle or while skating, to catch on, or hold to, any moving automobile, or other vehicle, within the corporate limits of the city.
(Code 1967, § 23-5)
Bicycles and play vehicles, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-290 et seq.
Sec. 20-6. - Use of bicycles, roller skates and skateboards.
(a)
Every person riding a bicycle upon any public street or highway within the city shall be granted all the rights and shall be subject to all the duties applicable to the motor vehicle law, except those which by their nature have no application.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to ride a bicycle on any of the sidewalks of the city.
(c)
Every bicycle, when in use after dark, shall be equipped with a white-light lamp visible at least five hundred (500) feet in front of the bicycle and with a red reflector on the rear of the bicycle.
(d)
No person upon roller skates, a skateboard, coaster, toy vehicle, or any similar device shall be permitted to go upon any public street or highway in the city except while crossing a street at a crosswalk, and, when so crossing, such person shall be granted all the rights and shall be subject to all the duties applicable to pedestrians.
(e)
No person shall ride roller skates, a skateboard, or other device as described in this section or as may be inferred upon the sidewalks within the fire limits of the city.
Sec. 20-7. - Parking of vehicles carrying material extending over rear end.
All vehicles carrying ladders, lumber, pipe or any material extending six (6) feet or more over rear end of the body of the vehicle shall not park within the fire limits of the city.
(Code 1967, § 23-59)
Sec. 20-8. - Parking in residential areas.
It shall be unlawful for any vehicle having six (6) or greater wheels to park on public property, including public streets and street rights-of-way, in any residential zone of the city. This section shall not be applicable to any vehicle parked in a residential neighborhood for the purpose of pickup, delivery, loading or unloading.
(Ord. of 6-10-91)
Sec. 20-9. - Restrictions.
Parking shall be restricted at certain times, where;
(a)
No person shall park any vehicle between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 5:00 p.m. of Monday through Saturday of any week on or upon those portions of the streets and alleys of the city designated in subsection (b) hereof except a patron as such terms are defined in subsection (c) hereof.
(b)
Designated areas:
Third Avenue on both sides thereof from West 7th Street to West 8th Street;
West 8th Street on both sides thereof from 3rd Avenue to 4th Avenue;
4th Avenue on both sides thereof from West 8th Street to the city limits.
(c)
For purposes of this section "patron" shall mean an individual who is a customer, patron or client of a business, service, enterprise or profession which is located within a building which is adjacent to any of the streets or alleys designated in subsection (b) hereof and is then engaged in the act of being a customer, patron or client of such business, service, enterprise or profession or in the act of transiting between such vehicle and the location of any such business, service, enterprise or procession.
(d)
Upon conviction of violation of this section or the entry of either a plea of guilty or a plea of nolo contendere to a violation of this section, a fine of not less than twenty-five dollars ($25.00) for each such violation shall be imposed in addition to any and all other penalties, fines and punishments as shall be imposed in connection with any and all other violations.
(e)
The restrictions, limitations and prohibitions contained herein are in addition to all other restrictions, limitations and prohibitions that shall exist from time to time relative to parking of vehicle upon the street and alleys designated in subsection (b) hereof.
(Ord. of 4-1-01; Ord. No. 13-13, § 1, 12-19-13)
Sec. 20-10. - Illegal parking.
It shall be unlawful for any vehicle to violate the following conditions in reference to the use, occupation of any vehicle on the roadways or right of ways of any city street or state highway within the City of West Point:
(1)
To remain motionless for any period of time, that causes or may cause a delay in the normal flow of traffic;
(2)
To park or remain on a sidewalk that is intended for the use of pedestrian traffic;
(3)
To park in the direction of oncoming traffic;
(4)
To park in any parking area that has been clearly marked by sign or paint as being designated as a no parking or restricted parking zone;
(5)
To park in any parking area designated for handicap parking without a valid state issued permit.
(Ord. No. 2012-3, Exh. A, 4-9-12)
Sec. 20-11. - How vehicles to be parked; powers of department of transportation and local authorities.
(a)
Except as otherwise provided in this Code section, every vehicle stopped or parked upon a two-way roadway shall be stopped or parked with the right-hand wheels parallel to and within twelve (12) inches of the right-hand curb or as close as practicable to the right edge of the right-hand shoulder.
(b)
Except when otherwise provided by local ordinance, every vehicle stopped or parked upon a one-way roadway shall be stopped or parked parallel to the curb or edge of the roadway, in the direction of authorized traffic movement, with its right-hand wheels within twelve (12) inches of the right-hand curb or as close as practicable to the right edge of the right-hand shoulder or with its left-hand wheels within twelve (12) inches of the left-hand curb or as close as practicable to the left edge of the left-hand shoulder.
(c)
Local authorities may by ordinance permit angle parking on any roadway, except that angle parking shall not be permitted on any federal-aid or state highway unless the department of transportation has determined that the roadway is of sufficient width to permit angle parking without interfering with the free movement of traffic.
(d)
The department, with respect to highways under its jurisdiction, may place signs prohibiting, restricting, or limiting the stopping, standing, or parking of vehicles on any highway where, in its opinion, as evidenced by resolution or order entered in its minutes, such stopping, standing, or parking is dangerous to those using the highway or where the stopping, standing, or parking of vehicles would unduly interfere with the free movement of traffic thereon. Such signs shall be official signs, and no person shall stop, stand, or park any vehicle in violation of the restrictions on such signs.
(Ord. No. 2012-3, Exh. A, 4-9-12)
Sec. 20-12. - Stopping, standing, or parking outside of business or residential districts.
Outside of a business or residential district, no person shall stop, park, or leave standing any vehicle, whether attended or unattended, upon the roadway when it is practicable to stop, park, or so leave such vehicle off the roadway; but in every event, an unobstructed width of the highway opposite a standing vehicle shall be left for the free passage of other vehicles, and a clear view of the stopped vehicle shall be available from a distance of two hundred (200) feet in each direction upon the highway.
(Ord. No. 2012-3, Exh. A, 4-9-12)
Secs. 20-13—20-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - OPERATION

Sec. 20-31. - Truck routes designated.
The public safety committee shall be authorized to determine which streets shall be designated as truck routes through the city. Upon the determination by the public safety committee of which streets shall be truck routes, the committee shall cause suitable signs to be erected indicating the truck routes, and shall cause to be erected signs warning all trucks to remain on the marked truck routes through the city.
(Code 1967, § 23-32)
Sec. 20-32. - Load limit for trucks using streets; exceptions.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any truck having load capacity greater than twelve thousand (12,000) pounds to travel any street within the city, unless such street shall have been previously designated as a truck route.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any truck, or other motor vehicle, to travel on any street within the city when such truck or other motor vehicle shall be loaded with a useful load exceeding twelve thousand (12,000) pounds in weight, provided, however, that this section shall not apply or be of any effect as to those streets within the city which have previously been designated as federal or state highways.
(Code 1967, § 23-33)
Sec. 20-33. - Temporary traffic rules.
In cases where the traffic upon the streets of the city may become congested, and upon occasions of parades, at theaters and other public assemblages, where large numbers of vehicles are assembled, the police may make temporary rules regulating the traffic in such congested districts, and any person who, after being warned of the temporary traffic regulations by a police officer of the city, shall refuse to comply with such regulations shall be punished as provided in section 1-14 of this Code.
(Code 1967, § 23-35)
Sec. 20-34. - Police officers to be obeyed.
Operators of vehicles shall obey all visual, verbal or written instructions of police officers given in the line and scope of their duty as police officers.
(Code 1967, § 23-36)
State Law reference— Obedience to authorized persons directing traffic, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-2.
Sec. 20-35. - Yield intersections.
(a)
The driver of a vehicle approaching a posted yield right-of-way sign shall slow to a speed of not more than ten (10) miles per hour and shall yield right-of-way to all vehicles approaching from the right or left on the intersection roads or streets which are so close as to constitute an immediate hazard. If a driver is involved in a collision at an intersection or interferes with the movement of other vehicles after driving past a yield right-of-way sign, such collision or interference shall be deemed prima facie evidence of the driver's failure to yield right-of-way.
(b)
Yield right-of-way signs shall be erected at such intersections and in such places as may be authorized and directed by the public safety committee.
(Code 1967, § 23-37)
Sec. 20-36. - Right-of-way for emergency vehicles.
Ambulances, fire trucks and police cars shall have the right-of-way over all vehicles of private individuals, and it shall be a violation of this section if any vehicle shall fail to give the above listed vehicles the right-of-way and pull over to the right side of the street when any such vehicle approaches with siren sounding or the red light on the front burning.
(Code 1967, § 23-38)
State Law reference— Authorized emergency vehicles, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-6.
Sec. 20-37. - Driving through processions.
Processions shall not be broken through by other vehicles, except by permission of police or traffic officers.
(Code 1967, § 23-40)
State Law reference— Preventing or disrupting lawful procession, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-34; funeral processions, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-76.
Sec. 20-38. - Driving upon streets being repaired.
It shall be unlawful for any person to drive any car, motorcycle, bicycle, scooter or conveyance of any kind upon any street in the city that is being repaired. Such streets should be well marked and blocked off. If for any reason any such street is not marked and blocked off, it shall be unlawful to operate any conveyance upon such street if it is obvious that such street is under repair.
(Code 1967, § 23-41)
Sec. 20-39. - Right-of-way at intersections.
When the driver of any vehicle is approaching any street intersection in the city where no traffic-control is present, or where the existing traffic control device is inoperative, he shall give vehicles approaching that intersection on his right the right-of-way, and he shall, if necessary, slow up or stop in order to allow vehicles approaching on his right to pass before he proceeds.
(Code 1967, § 23-44)
State Law reference— Vehicles approaching or entering intersection, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-70.
Sec. 20-40. - Left turn lanes.
Left turns shall not be permitted from lanes having arrows indicating right turns or straight ahead traffic. No straight ahead traffic shall be allowed from lanes marked with arrows indicating left turns only.
(Code 1967, § 23-45)
State Law reference— Required position and methods of turning at intersections, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-120.

Sec. 20-61. - Speed regulated.
It shall be unlawful for railroad company whose road crosses any street to operate any train of cars or locomotive at the point where such railroad crosses such street at a rate of speed greater than twenty-five (25) miles per hour.
(Code 1967, § 23-91)
Sec. 20-62. - Blocking grade crossings.
(a)
The several railroads whose roads are in this city shall not allow any street, alley, or sidewalk to be obstructed by cars or other things left on any of the crossings. In loading or unloading freight, it shall be unlawful to stop longer than five (5) minutes unless the cars be uncoupled at the point of crossing so as to allow vehicles to pass.
(b)
Locomotives must not be left standing within thirty (30) feet of such streets or alleys or the continuation of the sidewalks thereof and to leave them as prohibited in this section shall be unlawful.
(Code 1967, § 23-92)

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 20-81. - Short title.
This article shall be known and may be cited as the "Parade Ordinance of the City of West Point."
(Code 1967, § 23-101)
Sec. 20-82. - Definitions.
For the purposes of this article, the following words and phrases shall have the meanings respectively ascribed to them:
Chief of police means the chief of police of the city.
Parade means any parade, march, ceremony, show, exhibition, pageant or procession of any kind, or any similar display, on or upon any street, park or other public place in the city.
Parade permit means a permit as required by this article.
(Code 1967, § 23-102)
Sec. 20-83. - Violations; penalty.
A violation of this article shall be punishable in accordance with section 1-14.
(Code 1967, § 23-114)
Sec. 20-84. - Interference.
No person shall unreasonably hamper, obstruct or impede, or interfere with any parade or parade assembly or with any person, vehicle or animal participating or used in a parade.
(Code 1967, § 23-112(a))
Sec. 20-85. - Driving through parades.
No driver of a vehicle shall drive between the vehicles or persons comprising a parade when such vehicles or persons are in motion and are conspicuously designated as a parade.
(Code 1967, § 23-112(b))
Sec. 20-86. - Parking on parade route.
The chief of police shall have the authority, when reasonably necessary, to prohibit or restrict the parking of vehicles along a highway or part thereof constituting a part of the route of a parade. The chief of police shall post signs to such effect. It shall be unlawful for any person to park or leave unattended any vehicle in violation of any such sign. No person shall be liable for parking on a street unposted in violation of this article.
(Code 1967, § 23-112(c))
Secs. 20-87—20-100. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PERMIT

Sec. 20-101. - Required.
No person shall engage in, participate in, aid, form or start any parade, unless a parade permit shall have been obtained from the chief of police.
(Code 1967, § 23-103)
Sec. 20-102. - Exceptions.
The provisions of this division shall not apply to:
(1)
Funeral processions.
(2)
Students going to and from school classes or participating in education activities, provided such conduct is under the immediate direction and supervision of the proper school authorities.
(3)
A governmental agency acting within the scope of its functions.
(Code 1967, § 23-103(1))
Sec. 20-103. - Application.
A person seeking issuance of a parade permit shall file an application with the chief of police on forms provided by such officer.
(1)
Filing period. An application for a parade permit shall be filed with the chief of police not less than fourteen (14) days nor more than sixty (60) days before the date on which it is proposed to conduct the parade.
(2)
Contents. The application for a parade permit shall set forth the following information:
a.
The name, address and telephone number of the person seeking to conduct such parade.
b.
If the parade is proposed to be conducted for, on behalf of, or by an organization, the name, address and telephone number of the headquarters of the organization, and of the authorized and responsible heads of such organization.
c.
The name, address and telephone number of the person who will be the parade chairman and who will be responsible for its conduct.
d.
The date when the parade is to be conducted.
e.
The route to be traveled, the starting point and the termination point.
f.
The approximate number of persons who, and animals and vehicles which, will constitute such parade; the type of animals, and description of the vehicles.
g.
The hours when such parade will start and terminate.
h.
A statement as to whether the parade will occupy all or only a portion of the width of the streets proposed to be traversed.
i.
The location by streets of any assembly areas for such parade.
j.
The time at which units of the parade will begin to assemble at any such assembly area or areas.
k.
The interval of space to be maintained between units of such parade.
l.
If the parade is designed to be held by, and on behalf of or for, any person other than the applicant, the applicant for such permit shall file with the chief of police a communication in writing from the person proposing to hold the parade, authorizing the applicant to apply for the permit on his behalf.
m.
Any additional information which the chief of police shall find reasonably necessary to a fair determination as to whether a permit should issue.
(3)
Late applications. The chief of police, where good cause is shown therefor, shall have the authority to consider any application hereunder which is filed less than fourteen (14) days before the date such parade is proposed to be conducted.
(Code 1967, § 23-104)
Sec. 20-104. - Fee.
The fee for a parade permit required by this division shall be in an amount determined by the city council from time to time and listed in the schedule of fees and charges maintained in the clerk-treasurer's office.
(Code 1967, § 23-104(4))
Sec. 20-105. - Standards of issuance.
The chief of police shall issue a permit under this division when, from a consideration of the application and from such other information as may otherwise be obtained, he finds that:
(1)
The conduct of the parade will not substantially interrupt the safe and orderly movement of other traffic contiguous to its route.
(2)
The conduct of the parade will not require the diversion of so great a number of police officers of the city to properly police the line of movement and the areas contiguous thereto as to prevent normal police protection to the city.
(3)
The conduct of such parade will not require the diversion of so great a number of ambulances as to prevent normal ambulance service to portions of the city other than that to be occupied by the proposed line of march and areas contiguous thereto.
(4)
The concentration of persons, animals and vehicles at assembly points of the parade will not unduly interfere with proper fire and police protection of, or ambulance service to, areas contiguous to such assembly areas.
(5)
The conduct of such parade will not interfere with the movement of firefighting equipment en route to a fire.
(6)
The conduct of the parade is not reasonably likely to cause injury to persons or property, to provoke disorderly conduct or create a disturbance.
(7)
The parade is scheduled to move from its point of origin to its point of termination expeditiously and without unreasonable delays en route.
(8)
The parade is not to be held for the sole purpose of advertising any product, goods or event, and is not designed to be held purely for private profit.
(Code 1967, § 23-105)
Sec. 20-106. - Notice of rejection.
The chief of police shall act upon the application for a parade permit within ten (10) days after the filing thereof. If the chief of police disapproves the application, he shall mail to the applicant, within fifteen (15) days after the date upon which the application was filed, a notice of his action, stating the reasons for his denial of the permit.
(Code 1967, § 23-106)
Sec. 20-107. - Appeal procedure.
Any person aggrieved shall have the right to appeal the denial of a parade permit to the city council. The appeal shall be taken within fourteen (14) days after notice. The city council shall act upon the appeal within ten (10) days after its receipt.
(Code 1967, § 23-107)
Sec. 20-108. - Alternative permit.
The chief of police, in denying an application for a parade permit, shall be empowered to authorize the conduct of the parade on a date, at a time, or over a route different from that named by the applicant. An applicant desiring to accept an alternate permit shall, within ten (10) days after notice of the action of the chief of police, file a written notice of acceptance with the chief of police. An alternate parade permit shall conform to the requirements of and shall have the effect of a parade permit under this division.
(Code 1967, § 23-108)
Sec. 20-109. - Notice to city and other officials.
Immediately upon the issuance of a parade permit, the chief of police shall send a copy thereof to the following:
(1)
The mayor.
(2)
The clerk-treasurer.
(3)
The fire chief.
(4)
The director of the department of public works.
(Code 1967, § 23-109)
Sec. 20-110. - Contents of permit.
Each parade permit shall state the following information:
(1)
Starting time.
(2)
Minimum speed.
(3)
Maximum speed.
(4)
Maximum interval of space to be maintained between the units of the parade.
(5)
The portions of the streets to be traversed that may be occupied by the parade.
(6)

ARTICLE VI. - MOTORIZED CARTS

Sec. 20-155. - Definition.
Motorized carts are defined as every motor vehicle having not less than four (4) wheels and an unladen weight of one thousand three hundred (1,300) pounds or less, and which cannot operate at more than twenty (20) miles per hour and designed to carry no more than the recommended manufacturing seating capacity and shall be consistent with the definition as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1(32). This includes golf carts and similar types of self-propelled vehicles.
(Ord. No. 21-1, § 1, 2-8-21)
Sec. 20-156. - License and registration required.
Motorized carts may be operated within the city only after the owner has completed the registration process pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 40-6-331(b), and only if a person who possesses a valid Georgia driver's license is operating the motorized cart. Registration fees are charged by the city to cover the costs of implementing and maintaining this article. It shall be the duty of every owner of a motorized cart that is operated on public roads, rights-of-way, or other public property in the jurisdiction of the city to register the motorized cart with the city within ten (10) days of the date of purchase/acquisition. There shall be a registration fee and a transfer fee as set out below.
(Ord. No. 21-1, § 1, 2-8-21)
Sec. 20-157. - Registration and transfer requirements.
(a)
All fees shall be listed in the city fee schedule.
(b)
The initial fees upon the adoption of this article shall be as follows; Registration every year, fifteen dollars ($15.00) annual registration fee, transfer fee of ten dollars ($10.00), late transfer fee (after ten (10) days of sale) of five dollars ($5.00) (fees to be the maximum provided under state law).
(c)
Registration with the city shall include a record of the model, make and vehicle identification number or serial number on such motorized cart, the name and address of the owner, a contact phone number, and any other such information as the city shall require, all of which shall be maintained at the police department. A person desiring to register a motorized cart shall bring the motorized cart to the West Point Police Department and complete an application of registration of such cart.
(d)
Upon registration, the police department shall issue a numerical decal to be applied to a conspicuous place on such cart to be plainly visible. This decal issued shall be nontransferable from the cart for which it is issued.
(e)
Before any motorized cart may be operated within the city, the cart must have affixed thereto an emblem as required by O.C.G.A. § 40-8-4.
(f)
There shall be a registration and licensing fee due annually on November 1 of each year.
(g)
Upon occurrence of a sale of the cart to another person, the registration must be transferred to the new owner within ten (10) business days of the change in ownership and a fee paid. If the registration is not transferred within ten (10) business days, a penalty will be applied in addition to the transfer charge; and the cart shall be considered an unregistered cart after the ten (10) business-day period. Dealers acquiring a registered cart exclusively for resale (non-rental) shall not be required to pay the transfer fee but shall notify the city of the transfer within ten (10) business days of receiving the cart, and of the ultimate disposition of the cart within ten (10) business days of sale.
(h)
The motorized cart will be inspected by the police department to assure compliance with this article at the time of registration. Motorized carts will not be issued a decal until all requirements of the article are met.
(i)
Cart dealers and distributors, as well as other commercial establishments, may rent carts to the public. Each such establishment renting carts shall be required to register and pay such fees on each rental cart in accordance with this article and shall maintain a written record of each person who rents each cart. Renters shall be required to furnish positive identification, shall be provided a copy of this article to read, and must be at least twenty-one (21) years of age.
(j)
Only those persons twenty-one (21) years of age or older may register a motorized cart. Cart registration may be in one (1) person's name only, and the registration form must be signed by that person.
(Ord. No. 21-1, § 1, 2-8-21)
Sec. 20-158. - Emblem and decal required.
(a)
Before any motorized cart may be operated within the city, the cart must have affixed thereto an emblem as required by O.C.G.A. § 40-8-4.
(b)
A current registration decal must be affixed to the motorized cart. The decal issued by the city shall be nontransferable from the motorized cart for which it was issued.
(Ord. No. 21-1, § 1, 2-8-21)
Sec. 20-159. - General regulations.
(a)
Motorized carts may only be operated on public roads, rights-of-way or other public property which are part of the city's street system which have a speed limit of thirty-five (35) miles per hour or less. The motorized cart must be equipped with functional headlights, taillights, and brake lights. Additionally, motorized carts must also be equipped with a flashing or rotating yellow or amber light mounted on the top. Motorized carts shall not be operated on public roads, rights-of-way, or other property of the city from sunset to sunrise.
(b)
If available on the motorized cart, all occupants of a motorized cart must wear seatbelts.
(c)
Motorized carts shall not be operated on the sidewalks or on any city property not designated for motorized use.
(d)
Restrictions placed upon licensed motorists shall apply in the same manner as if operating a motor vehicle under state and/or local law.
(e)
The maximum occupancy of a motorized cart traveling on public roads, rights-of-way, or other public property shall be one (1) person per designated seat.
(f)
All operators of motorized carts shall abide by all traffic regulations applicable to vehicular traffic when using the designated public roads, rights-of-way, or other public property of the city.
(g)
A city employee on city business, in a city vehicle, and in the exercise of his/her duties, may operate gasoline, diesel fuel or battery-operated powered vehicles or motorized carts upon the city's streets, parks, parking lots, and other public properties within the city limits.
(h)
Motorized carts cannot be operated on any street with a posted speed limit above thirty-five (35) miles per hour or on state routes. Motorized carts may cross state routes on city streets at designated intersections.
(i)
No motorized vehicle may be operated on any city street without the approved city decal.
(j)
All motorized carts noncompliant with the requirements herein shall not be allowed to be operated on any city street.
(k)
Any owner registering a motorized cart with the city and any operator of same agrees to abide by all the requirements of state law and this Code.
(Ord. No. 21-1, § 1, 2-8-21)
Sec. 20-160. - Liability.
The owner or operator of a motorized cart is liable for his or her own actions. Any person who operates or owns a motorized cart is responsible for procuring liability insurance in the amount of twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000.00) per person for bodily injury, fifty thousand dollars ($50,000.00) per accident for bodily injury to one (1) or more people, and twenty-five thousand dollars ($25,000.00) per accident for property damage. Proof of insurance coverage must be provided at the time of registration and always maintained on the motorized cart.
(Ord. No. 21-1, § 1, 2-8-21)
Sec. 20-161. - Violation-penalty.
It shall be unlawful for a motorized cart to be operated on the streets of the city except as expressly authorized in this article, and every person convicted of a violation shall be punished in accordance with section 1-14 of the Code of the city and/or as otherwise provided under the Code of the city and/or applicable state law.
(Ord. No. 21-1, § 1, 2-8-21)
Secs. 20-162—20-170. - Reserved.
