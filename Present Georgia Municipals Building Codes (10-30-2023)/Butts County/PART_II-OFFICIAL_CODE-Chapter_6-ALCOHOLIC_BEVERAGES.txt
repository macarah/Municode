ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 6-1. - Purpose of this chapter.
Pursuant to the power and authority vested in the official governing body to promote the general health, safety and welfare, this ordinance has been devised to regulate and control the sale of malt beverages, wine and distilled spirits. The plan includes reasonable constraints and reasonable taxation of the privilege to engage in the business of selling or otherwise dealing in such substances and products.
Sec. 6-2. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meaning ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Adult entertainment establishment means any adult bookstore, adult business, adult dancing establishment, adult hotel, adult motel, adult mini-motion picture theater, adult motion picture arcade, adult motion picture theater, adult video store, encounter center, or erotic dance establishment as defined in Chapter 52, Article II of the Butts County Code of Ordinances.
Alcoholic beverage means any beverage or drink or other substance intended for human consumption which is comprised of or contains malt beverage, wine or distilled spirits.
Bar, tavern, or saloon means any place devoted primarily to the retailing and on-premise drinking of malt, wine, or other alcoholic beverages, or any place where any sign visible from the public right-of-way is exhibited or displayed indicating that alcoholic beverages are obtainable for consumption on the premises. The incidental service of food for consumption on the premise shall be allowed. Live entertainment shall be allowed. No dancing shall be allowed.
Convenience store means a retail business engaged primarily in the retail sale of gasoline, prepackaged food items, candy and confections and non-alcoholic beverages.
Grocery store means a retail business engaged primarily in the retail sale of food and groceries.
Hotels and motels shall mean business establishments located in a C-2 zoning district that offer overnight accommodations to the public for hire, and which derive 51 percent or more of total annual gross income from the rental of rooms for overnight lodging.
Malt beverages means any alcoholic beverage obtained by fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than six percent alcohol by volume, and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer and strong beer. The term "malt beverage" does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine. This definition is not designed to be in conflict with O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2(12) and if there is any conflict therewith, the definition contained in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2(12) shall be the accepted definition.
Nightclub means an establishment having a seating capacity of at least 50 persons with all booths and tables being unobstructed and open to view and providing a band and/or other professional entertainment, The principal business of the nightclub shall be entertainment, and the serving of any alcoholic beverages shall be incidental thereto. Dancing shall be allowed within the establishment.
Private club means any nonprofit organized under the laws of this state which:
(a)
Has been in existence for at least one year prior to the filing of its application for a license to be issued pursuant to this chapter;
(b)
Has at least 150 regular dues-paying members;
(c)
Owns, hires, or leases building or space within a building for the use of its members. Said building or space must possess the following:
1.
An adequate and sanitary kitchen that meets health department requirements, dining room equipment and a minimum seating capacity of 75, of which 75 percent of said seating must be located within the interior of an enclosed, roofed building; and
2.
Is staffed with a sufficient number of employees for cooking, preparing, and serving meals for its membership; and
(d)
Has no member, officer, agent, or employee directly or indirectly receiving, in the form of salary or other compensation, any profits from the sale of malt beverage, wine and/or distilled spirits beyond a fixed salary. "Fixed salary," means the amount of compensation paid any member, officer, agent, or employee of a private club as may be fixed for him/her by its members at a prior annual meeting or by the governing body out of the general revenues of the club, and shall not include any commission on any profits from the sale of malt beverages, wine and/or distilled spirits. For the purpose of this definition, tips or gratuities, which are added to the bill, under club regulations, shall not be considered profits from the sale of malt beverages, wine and/or distilled spirits.
Proper identification means a document issued by a governmental agency containing a description of the person and the person's photograph and the person's date of birth. By way of example only and without limited the foregoing, proper identification may be a passport, driver's license, or an identification card authorized under the Official Code of Georgia. "Proper identification" shall never include a birth certificate.
Restaurant means:
(a)
Any public place kept, used, maintained, advertised, and held out to the public as a place where at least one meal per day is served three or more days per week and is staffed with a sufficient number of employees for cooking, preparing, and serving meals for its patrons; and
(b)
Has an adequate and sanitary kitchen that meets Health Department requirements, and dining room equipment and a minimum seating capacity of 75, of which 75 percent of said seating must be located within the interior of an enclosed, roofed building; and
(c)
Where the principal business is the serving of such meals which, excluding the sale of malt beverage, wine and distilled spirits, accounts for at least 51 percent of the gross revenue of the business. The serving of alcoholic beverages to be consumed on the premises are incidental to the serving of meals. Live entertainment shall be allowed.
Retail dealer means any person who engages in selling malt beverages, wine or distilled spirits at retail within the county, which includes any person serving malt beverages, wine or distilled spirits for a pecuniary consideration within the limits of the county.
Wholesale dealer means any person who sells malt beverages, wine or distilled spirits to a retail dealer or other wholesale dealers.
Wine means any beverage containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruit, berries, or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. "Wine" includes, but is not limited to, sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products. The term "wine" does not include cooking wine with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to this definition of wine.
Sec. 6-3. - General duty of retail licensees.
(a)
Retail licenses for the sale of malt beverages and wine and retail licenses for the sale of distilled spirits shall be issued only to individuals and not to corporations or business entities.
(b)
A person issued a retail license or licenses pursuant to this chapter shall be active in, and primarily responsible for, the management and operation of the business for which the license is granted.
(c)
No holder of license authorizing the sale of malt beverage and wine or holder of license authorizing the sale of distilled spirits at retail in the county, nor any agent or employee of the licensee, shall receive malt beverage, wine or distilled spirits delivered to the premise of the retail licensee by any means other than a conveyance owned and/or operated by a wholesale dealer with a license from the state and the county.
Sec. 6-4. - Reserved.
Sec. 6-5. - Residency requirements.
There shall be no residency requirements for licensee, but every licensee shall be a United States citizen.
Sec. 6-6. - Hours and days of operation.
(a)
No alcoholic beverage may be sold, dispensed, traded or delivered in Butts County between the hours of 12:00 a.m. (midnight) and 7:00 a.m.
(b)
No alcoholic beverages may be sold, served, dispensed, traded, distributed, or given away on Christmas Day, or on any other days prohibited by the laws of the state. Alcoholic Beverages may be sold and served on Sundays between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and Midnight only, for consumption on the premises in restaurants, hotels or motels possessing a valid license issued under section 6-9 of this chapter; malt beverages and wine may be sold by package sales on Sundays between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m. by persons possessing a valid license issued under section 6-9 of this chapter.
Sec. 6-7. - Penalty for violation of section 6-6.
Whenever a licensee is found to have violated any provision of section 6-6- relating to hours and days of operation, the following penalties will be imposed by Butts County through its code enforcement department or other designee:
1.
First Offense: Thirty-day suspension of LIcCENSE.
2.
Second Offense: Six-month suspension of license.
3.
Third Offense: One-year suspension of license, and licensee must reapply before any new license is granted.
For purpose of this section, a licensee shall be found to have violated the provisions of this chapter, and shall be subject to the penalties set forth herein, upon conviction on a charge or charges of acts or conduct that constitute a violation of the hours and days of operation provisions of this chapter. The term "conviction" shall include an adjudication of administrative finding guilt, a plea of guilty or nolo contendere, a bond forfeiture, or any other judicial or administrative disposition sustaining the charge of charges by a court of competent jurisdiction of by an administrative agency having jurisdiction under state law. There shall be no hearing before the Butts County Board of Commissioners. The penalties set forth herein shall not be stayed or abated during the pendency of any appeal.
Sec. 6-8. - Location of business.
(a)
No license shall be granted for the sale of malt beverage and wine or license for the sale of distilled spirits at any location that is:
1.
Within 1,500 feet of any church;
2.
Within 1,500 feet of any public school, church school, or private school teaching elementary, middle school, high school or college curriculum; (or)
3.
Within 50 yards of any residence on property with a residential zoning classification, located outside incorporated municipalities, unless the location where malt beverages, wine or distilled spirits are to be sold is already zoned commercial use or qualifies as a legal nonconforming commercial use.
(b)
Any person applying for a license under this chapter to sell malt beverage and wine or license to sell distilled spirits shall file, with their application, a plat or survey, by a licensed surveyor, showing the building from which sales will be made and the distances from the main entrance of that building to the closest prohibited inconsistent use, as set forth and as measured by the rules contained herein.
(c)
The provisions of this section shall apply and control notwithstanding any provision or land use ordinance of the county.
(d)
Distances shall be measured by the most direct route of travel in the public right-of-way, and shall begin at the main door of the premises of the licensee thence to the closest public right-of-way, thence to the main door of the building otherwise affected.
(e)
In the event that any new use is established nearer than the distances listed in subsection (a) above to an existing licensee, which would otherwise prohibit the issuance of a license, the licensee, as well as its successors and assigns, may continue to apply for and receive a license at the existing location so long as each of the following conditions is met:
(1)
The license was valid when the new use was established;
(2)
The license has not lapsed for a period of greater than one year since the new use was established;
(3)
The licensee is in all other respects in compliance with the ordinances of Butts County, Georgia; and
(4)
The licensee's alcohol business at the site has not materially increased since the time the new use was established. For purposes of this subsection, if the licensee's in-stock inventory of alcohol products is increased by 50 percent or more after the time the new use is established, it will be deemed a material increase in the alcohol business. It shall be the burden of the license holder to retain records showing its in-stock inventory at the time the new use is established.
(Ord. of 8-11-2014, § 1)
Sec. 6-9. - License required.
(a)
No person shall sell, dispense, trade, deliver, or convey malt beverages and wine, either at retail or wholesale, and for either on or off premises consumption, until that person has received a malt beverage/wine privilege license from the Butts County Board of Commissioners to permit such a business to be conducted. When granted, the license shall have imprinted thereon: "This license is a privilege and may therefore be suspended or revoked at any time." Any person holding a valid license for the sale of malt beverages and/or wine as of [date of passage of this ordinance], shall be deemed to hold a malt beverage/wine license until the expiration of the existing license or until such license is suspended, revoked or otherwise cancelled pursuant to the Butts County Code of Ordinances or other applicable law, whichever shall first occur.
(b)
No person shall sell, dispense, trade, deliver, or convey distilled spirits, either at retail or wholesale, for on premises consumption, until that person has received a distilled spirits privilege license from the Butts County Board of Commissioners to permit such a business to be conducted. When granted, the license shall have imprinted thereon: "This license is a privilege and may therefore be suspended or revoked at any time."
(c)
No license required in subsections (a) or (b) of this section shall be issued until the Tax Commissioner of Butts County certifies that the county has received payment of ALL ad valorem taxes then due for the property upon which the licensed activity shall be conducted.
(d)
No license required in subsections (a) or (b) of this section shall be renewed until the Tax Commissioner of Butts County certifies that the county has received payment of all ad valorem taxes then due for the property upon which the licensed activity is being conducted.
Sec. 6-10. - Application for license.
(a)
The county manager or his/her designated person shall prepare and the governing body shall approve a form to be completed by any person desiring (i) a license permitting retail package and on premises consumption of malt beverage and/or wine; and (ii) a license permitting on premises consumption of distilled spirits.
(b)
The application for a malt beverage/wine privilege license and application for distilled spirits privilege license shall be made annually under oath by the individual authorized to do so and shall contain, in addition to any other information required by the governing body:
(1)
The name, age, home address, business address, and telephone number of each individual and legal entity which has any interest, however slight, in the profits generated by the applicant or who expects to share in such profits.
(2)
The address at which the applicant wishes to be granted a license to operate a retail malt beverage and wine business and/or distilled spirits business.
(3)
A statement authorizing an investigation by the sheriff into the character, reputation, credit standing, financial stability and veracity of each and every individual described in subsection (1) above.
(4)
A statement acknowledging that the license will be a privilege only and subject to revocation as provided in section 6-30 of this chapter.
(5)
A statement agreeing to immediately notify the county manager in writing of any change in the individuals comprising the class of people described in subsection (1) above as having an interest and sharing in the profits of the undertaking.
(6)
A statement describing any conviction and sentence, or plea of nolo contendere and sentence, entered in any court in the United States or abroad for abroad for any offenses other than traffic violations. A conviction or a plea of nolo contendere to the offense of driving a motor vehicle or boat while under the influence of alcohol or drugs shall not be considered as a traffic violation for purposes of this subsection, and therefore must be disclosed.
(7)
A statement of any interest any of the persons described in subsection (1) above have in any other retail or wholesale business dealing in alcoholic beverages of any kind, including spirituous liquor, malt beverage or wine.
(8)
As an attachment thereto, a check in the sum of $250.00 payable to Butts County shall be attached to any application for a malt beverage/wine privilege license. This charge is for the administrative costs of processing the application and investigating the applicants. This fee shall not be refundable in whole or in part, whether the application is granted or denied, and it shall not constitute a credit against any other tax, fee, or license due the county under this Code of Ordinances nor does it guarantee approval of a license.
(9)
As an attachment thereto, a check in the sum of $250.00 payable to Butts County shall be attached to any application for a distilled privilege license. This charge is for the administrative costs of processing the application and investigating the applicants. This fee shall not be refundable in whole or in part, whether the application is granted or denied, and it shall not constitute a credit against any other tax, fee, or license due the county under this Code of Ordinances nor does it guarantee approval of a license.
(10)
A current inventory of the merchandise on hand and located or to be located in or on the premises in the event the license is granted, so as to comply with section 6-48 of this chapter.
(Res. No. 2020-12, 7-13-2020)
Sec. 6-11. - Consideration of the application.
The county manager or his designee shall cause an appropriate investigation to be made into the persons named and the information given on the application(s). He shall cause the fingerprinting process as required by O.C.G.A. § 3-3-3. He shall then cause the matter to be placed on the agenda for a regular meeting of the board of commissioners, at which time the board of commissioners may grant or deny the application(s) or continue the same, pending further investigation.
(Res. No. 2020-12, 7-13-2020)
Sec. 6-12. - Application to be made annually; expiration thereof.
(a)
Each license granted under this chapter shall automatically expire on the last day of December each year. The holder of a license must reapply on or before December 31 in order to be considered for renewal effective January 1 of the following year. Since the license is a privilege, there shall be no right to renewal, and a license may be denied for any reason set forth in this chapter.
(b)
The county manager may administratively renew any license granted under this chapter provided the holder of such license is in good standing and in compliance with all provisions of this chapter and any applicable local, State or Federal law or regulation.
(c)
When a license issued by the state revenue commissioner to engage in the business described in this chapter is revoked, such action shall automatically revoke the license issued by the county. When a license has been suspended for one year, a licensee must reapply for a license.
(Res. No. 2020-12, 7-13-2020)
Sec. 6-13. - Records and inspections of retailer.
Each licensee under this ordinance shall retain in his/her place of business the invoice of each purchase of malt beverage, wine and distilled spirits for at least six months. All records as well as the premises of the business shall be subject to inspection by a duly authorized agent of the board of commissioners at any time to ensure compliance of the requirements of this chapter including but not limited to food sales, display of licenses, on premises consumption of alcoholic beverages and excise tax requirements.
Sec. 6-14. - Nonconforming uses.
Those businesses licensed prior to August 1, 2001, which do not meet the established criteria regarding floor space, seating capacity, food stock or distance from churches, schools, or residences as of such date shall be considered nonconforming uses and, as such, shall not be allowed to operate as a malt beverage and/or wine establishment except in the name of the holder who was last issued a license prior to August 1, 2001, or in the name of a member of the license holder's immediate family. For purposes of this section, immediate family member shall include: husband, spouse, mother, father, sister, brother, son or daughter, provided, however, that these family members must meet all other requirements contained for license holders or required by this ordinance and the laws of the state. If, however, improvements are made to the nonconforming establishment which bring it into compliance with the requirements of this chapter, such establishment may be licensed and operate to sell malt beverage and wine by a person other than the previous holder or immediate family members referred to in this section.
Secs. 6-15—6-24. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - LICENSES

Sec. 6-25. - License required.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to sell, serve, distribute or give malt beverages or wine at wholesale or retail within the limits of the county without a proper and current malt beverage/wine privilege license.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to sell, serve, distribute or give distilled spirits at wholesale or retail within the limits of the county without a proper and current distilled spirits privilege license.
Sec. 6-26. - Wholesale license.
(a)
Wholesale licenses to sell malt beverages and wine shall be issued only to those persons who are licensed by the state to sell and distribute malt beverage and/or wine at wholesale. Application should be made with the board of commissioners, and shall show proof of licensing by the state and shall demonstrate understanding of all laws regulations pertaining to wholesale distribution in the county.
(b)
Wholesale licenses to sell distilled spirits shall be issued only to those persons who are licensed by the state to sell and distribute distilled spirits at wholesale. Application should be made with the board of commissioners, and shall show proof of licensing by the state and shall demonstrate understanding of all laws regulations pertaining to wholesale distribution in the county.
Sec. 6-27. - Special provisions for private clubs.
Notwithstanding any other provisions of this chapter, a malt beverage/wine privilege license and/or distilled spirits privilege license may be issued to a private club meeting all requirements set forth in this ordinance.
Sec. 6-28. - Fees.
(a)
Wholesale. The applicant for a wholesaler license shall pay to the county, at the time the license is issued, the sum of $100.00 for each wholesale license issued. This fee is in addition to the application fee, as set forth in [section] 6-10(b)(8) and/or 6-10(b)(9) of this chapter. An annual renewal fee as set forth in section 6-28(d) of this chapter is due on or before October 31 of each year.
(b)
Retail. The applicant for a retail license shall pay to the county at the time the application is submitted the fee set forth in section 6-10(b)(8) and/or 6-10(b)(9) of this chapter. A renewal fee required by section 6-28(d) of this chapter shall be paid at the time an application for renewal is submitted. The application and renewal fees are non-refundable and shall not be credited to any other fees due from the licensee. At the time a license is issued the applicant shall pay the sum set forth by section 6-28(d) of this chapter as a license fee.
(c)
Inspection. An inventory inspection will be completed by the county upon initial application and quarterly, thereafter each calendar year. Fees may be applicable as set forth by section 6-28(d) of this chapter.
(d)
Schedule of fees. A schedule of fees will be set by the Butts County Board of Commissioners at the first regular scheduled meeting in January of each year for the current year.
Sec. 6-29. - Transfer.
(a)
Licenses issued pursuant to this chapter shall be applicable to and valid with respect only to the business location referred to therein, and shall not be transferable to any person other than the named licensee, except as specifically provided herein.
(b)
Any licensee transferring his/her interest in and to the business location for which a license was issued may conditionally transfer such license, provided that, prior to such transfer, the transferee files an application for license pursuant to [section] 6-10 of this chapter; pays the required administrative fee; and such conditional transfer is approved, in writing, by the county manager. Since the license is a privilege, there shall be no right to transfer, and a proposed transfer may be denied for any reason.
(Res. No. 2020-12, 7-13-2020)
Sec. 6-30. - Suspension or revocation.
(a)
A license issued pursuant to this chapter shall be a mere grant of privilege to carry on business during the term of the license, subject to all terms and conditions imposed by this chapter and state law, or any future ordinances pertaining thereto, and subject to revocation or suspension at any time. In case of revocation, the license shall not be entitled to a return of the unused license fee.
(b)
The board of commissioners shall have the right to suspend or revoke any license issued under this chapter whenever a person doing business under such a license is charged and convicted of violating any provision of this chapter or any other law that the board of commissioners determines in injurious to the health, safety or welfare of the public. The term "conviction" shall have the meaning and application set forth in section 6-7 of this chapter.
(c)
A suspension or revocation does not require notice or a public hearing before the board of commissioners, since such action will be taken only upon the applicant receiving due process before the court or administrative body imposing the conviction. Notice of a revocation or suspension hearing shall be given, either personally or by leaving the notice at the licensed place of business.
Sec. 6-31. - Reserved.
Sec. 6-32. - Parking for restaurants and private clubs.
Restaurants and private clubs must provide adequate parking. Parking shall not create any hazard to traffic on any public street, right of way road, and must meet the off-street parking and off-street loading/unloading requirements for the zoning classification of the premises as set forth in the zoning ordinance. All parking areas must be paved and lighted.
Sec. 6-33. - Storage for retailers.
All retailers licensed pursuant to this chapter shall store all malt beverage, wine and/or distilled spirits in their possession on the premises for which the license is issued, and at no other place or places except upon written permission from the board of commissioners upon written application therefor.
Sec. 6-34. - Restrictions on dispensing alcoholic beverages.
No operator, manager, or employee of licensee shall be allowed to manage, dispense, or serve malt beverage, wine or distilled spirits if he/she has been convicted of a felony or of any crime involving moral turpitude, or any state or federal statute with reference to liquor, wine, or malt beverage.
Sec. 6-35. - Opening containers on licensed premises.
(a)
No person shall open any malt beverage, wine, or distilled spirit for consumption, or otherwise, within 100 feet of a place of sale, except within the confines of a private club or restaurant as defined under the terms of this chapter.
(b)
No holder of a license authorizing the sale of malt beverage, wine or distilled spirit, nor any agent or employee of the licensee, shall suffer or permit a violation of subsection (a) of this section.
(c)
The license holder shall display in a conspicuous place within the licensed premises a sign, which shall state that it is unlawful to consume any malt beverage and/or wine or to open any container of malt beverage and/or wine within 100 feet of the licensed business. This subsection shall not apply to private clubs or restaurant as defined under the terms of this ordinance.
Sec. 6-36. - Breach of peace or disorderly conduct on licensed premises.
No holder of a license authorizing the sale of malt beverage, wine or distilled spirits, nor any agent or employee of the licensee, shall permit on the licensed premises any disorderly conduct or breach of the peace.
Sec. 6-37. - Advertising.
(a)
No sign shall be erected anywhere within the county for promoting the sale of malt beverages, wine or distilled spirits, except as permitted in this chapter, any other sign regulations of the county notwithstanding.
(b)
Notwithstanding subsection (a) of this section, any holder of a valid license under this chapter may display signs promoting the sale of malt beverages, wine or distilled spirits, provided such sign(s) are displayed from the interior of the licensed premises and comply with all other ordinances and regulations of the county.
(c)
Any violation of this section shall be grounds of immediate revocation of the license.
Sec. 6-38. - Posting of license.
(a)
Every holder of a malt beverage/wine privilege license shall have printed and displayed for public view the name of the licensee, together with the inscription "Butts County malt Beverage/Wine License No. _______" in uniform letters not less than four nor more than eight inches in height.
(b)
Every holder of a distilled spirits privilege license shall have printed and displayed for public view the name of the licensee, together with the inscription "Butts County Distilled Spirits License No. _______" in uniform letters not less than four nor more than eight inches in height.
(c)
Any violation of this section shall be grounds of immediate revocation of the license.
Sec. 6-39. - Employee/server regulations.
The following regulations shall apply to all establishments holding a retail license for alcoholic beverages:
(a)
Permits required for servers of alcoholic beverages.
(1)
Every person who serves or dispenses alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises shall meet the character requirements set forth in section 6-34 of this chapter.
(2)
No person shall be permitted to serve or dispense alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises until such person has applied for and received a permit from the Butts County Board of Commissioners' Office. Prior to issuing or renewing said permit, the Butts County Sheriff's Department shall conduct a criminal background check on the applicant and shall collect a nonrefundable investigative fee of $25.00 from the applicant to offset the cost of this background check. Upon presentation of the criminal history information to the board of commissioners' office the applicant shall pay an additional fee of $10.00 for the issuance of the permit, which shall be valid for 12 months from the date of issue. The permit shall be in the form of a picture identification (I.D.) badge. Permits shall be renewed annually; a criminal background check is required for each renewal, and the fees specified herein shall be paid each time the permit is renewed.
(3)
No permit shall be issued until such time as a signed application has been filed with the Butts County Board of Commissioners' Office. The application shall include name, address, date of birth, general description of applicant, height, weight, hair color, eye color, social security number and a copy of a driver's license or picture I.D.
(4)
No alcoholic beverage permit shall be issued to any person (i) who has been convicted of more than one DUI offense within two years of the date of application for said permit; (ii) who is currently serving probation pursuant to any felony plea under the First Offender Act; (iii) who has been convicted of any felony; (iv) who has been convicted, within five years from the date of application for a permit, of any misdemeanor crime of moral turpitude; or (v) who has been convicted, within five years of the date of application for a permit, as a habitual violator. For purposes of this subsection, a conviction shall include an adjudication of guilt, a plea of guilty or a plea of nolo contendere in any criminal proceeding.
(5)
It shall be the duty of all persons holding any license to sell alcoholic beverages where such alcoholic beverages are sold for consumption on the premises to file with the Butts County Board of Commissioners' Office the name of the establishment, the license number and a list of all employees, with their home addresses and home telephone numbers, twice annually on/or before June 1 and again on/or before December 1.
(6)
All permits issued through administrative error can be terminated by the Butts County Board of Commissioners' designee. No pre-termination hearing shall be required, but any person may request review of the matter by the Butts County Board of Commissioners.
(7)
This section shall not be construed to include employees who do not serve or dispense alcoholic beverages at any time during their employment.
(8)
No licensee shall allow any employee required to hold a permit to work on the licensed premises without displaying, on their person in public view, the permit issued by the Butts County Board of Commissioners' Office.
(9)
In the event that any permit holder leaves the employ of a licensed establishment, the licensee shall immediately surrender the permit to the Butts County Board of Commissioners' Office.
(10)
All permits issued hereunder remain the property of Butts County and shall be worn by the employee at all times while at work or on duty with the licensed establishment.
(11)
The Butts County Sheriff's Department and the Butts County Code Enforcement Department shall be authorized to enforce this section. Failure to abide by this section shall render both the server and the licensee subject to prosecution hereunder. Any alleged violation of this section shall constitute an offense to be tried in the Magistrate Court of Butts County, and upon conviction thereof the Court shall be authorized to impose any fine and/or term of imprisonment authorized by O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60.
(12)
Upon a conviction as provided in subsection (11) above, the Butts County Board of Commissioners shall be authorized to suspend or revoke the alcoholic beverages license issued to the licensee upon a determination that the violation warrants such action.
(b)
Employment of underage persons. No licensee, nor any agent or employee of the licensee, shall allow or require a person in his/her employment under age of 18 to dispense, serve, or take orders for any malt beverage, wine or distilled spirit. The provisions of this section shall not prohibit persons under 18 years of age who are employed in grocery stores, supermarkets, convenience stores, or drugstores from handling malt beverage and/or wine which are sold off-premises consumption.
Sec. 6-40. - Sale to intoxicated persons.
No licensee, nor any agent or employee of the licensee, shall, sell, serve, distribute, or give away malt beverages, wine or distilled spirits to any person while such person in a state of noticeable intoxication.
Sec. 6-41. - Sales to underage person(s).
(a)
Except as otherwise authorized by law, no person knowingly, by himself or through another, shall sell, serve, distribute, or give away or permit any person in his employ to sell, serve,, distribute, or give away any malt beverage, wine or distilled spirit to any person under the lawful drinking age as established by the laws of the State of Georgia.
(b)
The prohibition contained in subsection (a) of this section shall not apply with respect to the sale or purchase of malt beverage, wine or distilled spirits for consumption:
1.
For a medical purposes pursuant to a prescription of a physician duly authorized to practice medicine in this state;
2.
At a religious ceremony; or
3.
In the home with parental consent.
(c)
The prohibition contained in subsection (a) of this section shall not apply with respect to the sale of malt beverage, wine or distilled spirits by a person when such person has been furnished with proper identification showing that the person to whom the malt beverage, wine or distilled spirit is sold is of legal age or older. For purpose of this subsection, the term "proper identification" means any document issued by a government agency containing a description of the person, such person's photograph, and giving such person's date of birth and includes, without being limited to, a passport, military identification card, drivers license, or an identification card authorized under O.C.G.A. § 40-5-100 requiring the department of public safety to issue identification cards to handicapped persons who do not have a motor vehicle drivers license. "Proper identification" shall not include a birth certificate.
Sec. 6-42. - Penalties for violation of sections 6-40 and 6-41.
Upon conviction of the offense(s) defined in sections 6-40 and 6-41. The following actions will be taken:
1.
First Offense: Thirty-day suspension of license.
2.
Second Offense: Six-month suspension of license.
3.
Third Offense: One-year suspension of license, and licensee must reapply for a license.
The term "conviction" shall have the same meaning and application set forth in section 6-7 of this chapter.
Sec. 6-43. - Reserved.
Sec. 6-44. - Limitation on number of licenses granted to one applicant.
No person shall hold a retail license under this chapter who also has any financial interest in a wholesale malt beverage and/or wine business.
Sec. 6-45. - Majority vote of board of commissioners.
Approval of an application for a license under this chapter shall be by a majority vote of the board of commissioners.
Sec. 6-46. - Brown bagging prohibited.
(a)
For the purpose of this section, "brown-bagging" means the bringing, taking or carrying of any alcoholic beverage into a business licensed to operate within the county but not licensed for consumption of any alcoholic beverage, malt beverage, wine or distilled spirit on the premises, provided that bringing an alcoholic beverage, malt beverage, wine or distilled spirit into a house, apartment room or other unit designed for private residential occupancy shall not fall within this definition.
(b)
"Brown-bagging" shall include the following acts: (i) any person who brown-bags; (ii) Any person participating in consumption of any malt beverage, wine or distilled spirit being brown bagged; (iii) any person who consumes a malt beverage, alcoholic beverage, wine or distilled spirit on any premises holding a business license, except for those premises licensed for on-premises consumption of malt beverage, wine or distilled spirits or otherwise exempted from the definition of brown bagging by virtue of the private residential character of the occupancy; and (iv) Any employee of the business establishment in whose presence brown bagging knowingly or with reckless indifference occurs.
(c)
Brown-bagging is prohibited within the county.
(d)
The license of any person upon whose premises brown-bagging knowingly or with reckless indifference occurs shall be subject to suspension or revocation by the board of commissioners. Such suspension or revocation action shall occur upon conviction of the licensee for violating this chapter. The penalties for brown-bagging are as follows:
1.
First Offense: Thirty-day suspension of license.
2.
Second Offense: Six-month suspension of license.
3.
Third Offense: One-year suspension of license, and licensee must reapply for a new license.
The term "conviction" shall have the same meaning and application set forth in section 6-7 of this chapter.
Sec. 6-47. - Business license.
Upon the granting of a license by the board of commissioners, the applicant shall have 14 days in which to pick up the same and pay the business license fee as described in this chapter. The business license tax to be paid by a wholesaler located elsewhere but doing business in Butts County shall be the maximum permitted under Georgia law. In all other respects, the licensing fees and process are set forth in this chapter and no license fee or any portion thereof shall be refundable in the event of revocation. Notwithstanding the above referenced code section, it is the intention of the governing body to prorate the business license fee on a quarterly basis during any one licensed year. For purpose of determining a quarter, any portion of any calendar quarter shall be considered a full quarter in determining the license fee in 25 percent increments. The fee to be paid shall be calculated by identifying the quarters as ending on the last day of March, June, September, and December. A license applied for during any part of the first quarter ending March 31 would pay the fee for the entire year. Any application made after March 31, but prior to July 1, would pay 75 percent of the annual license fee. Any application made after June 30, but prior to October 1, would pay 50 percent of the annual fee. Any application made after September 30, but prior to January 1 of next year, would pay 25 percent of such licensing fee.
Sec. 6-48. - Other inventory required.
(a)
No retail malt beverage/wine privilege license or retail distilled spirits privilege license shall be granted unless the applicant can show he/she has or will have on hand an inventory of foodstuffs having a wholesale value of at least $10,000.00 exclusive of malt beverages, wine or distilled spirits. Each licensee shall be required to maintain such inventory. "Foodstuffs" shall be defined, for the purpose of this section, as "any matter or substance that can be taken into and assimilated by the body for nourishment." Any licensee may include as part of the inventory count a maximum of 30 cases of cigarettes. No other tobacco products may be included in any inventory count. Further, in order to meet the inventory requirement of this section, any merchandise that a licensee desires for inclusion therein must remain on the licensed premises and not be stored in any facility other than such premises.
(b)
The code enforcement department shall be charged with the duty of enforcing this section, and he/she may require an inventory count to be taken from time to time when he/she is of the opinion that the licensee is not maintaining the inventory required herein.
ARTICLE III. - [EXCISE TAX]

Sec. 6-49. - Authority of county to levy tax.
The authority of the county to levy an excise tax on the sale of malt beverages as provided for in O.C.G.A. § 3-5-80, the authority to levy an excise tax on the sale of wine is contained in O.C.G.A. § 3-6-60, and the authority to levy an excise tax on distilled spirits is contained in O.C.G.A. § 3-4-131. The provisions contained in this article shall not be in conflict with the state law provisions cited in this section. No tax shall be imposed, levied or collected in any portion of the county in which a municipality within the county is imposing the tax authorized by O.C.G.A. § 3-5-80, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-60 or O.C.G.A. § 3-4-130.
Sec. 6-50. - Excise tax on malt beverages and wine.
(a)
There is hereby levied an excise tax on sale of malt beverages on each wholesaler of malt beverages as follows:
48/7 oz. Cans or bottle, $1.40 per case; 36/8 oz. Cans or bottles $1.20 per case;
24/14 oz. Cans or bottles, $1.40 per case; 24/16 oz. Cans or bottles, $1.60 per case;
12/32 oz. Cans or bottles, $1.60 per case; 24/7 oz. Cans or bottles, $.70 per case;
24/8 oz. Cans or bottles, $.80 per case; 12/12 oz. Cans or bottles, $.60 per case;
37/12 oz. Cans or bottles, $1.05 per case; 20/16 oz. Cans or bottles, $1.34 per case;
3.8 gallons, $5.11 per container; ¼ bbl., $3.00 per container; and ½ bbl., $6.00 per container.
(b)
There is hereby imposed an excise tax on the first sale or use of wine by the package in the amount of $0.22 per liter and proportionate tax at rates on all fractional parts of a liter.
(c)
On the package sale of distilled spirits and on the sale of distilled spirits to private clubs, a tax of $.022 per liter and a proportionate tax on fractional parts of a liter.
(d)
The excise tax provided for in this section shall be imposed upon and shall be paid by the licensed wholesale dealer.
(e)
The dealer on or before the tenth day of the month following the calendar month in which the beverages are sold or disposed of within the county by said wholesale dealer shall pay the taxes.
(f)
Each licensee responsible for the payment of the excise tax shall file a report itemizing for the preceding calendar month the exact quantities of malt beverages by size and type sold during the month within the county. The licensee shall file the report with the county.
(g)
The wholesaler shall remit to the county on the tenth day of the month next succeeding the calendar month in which the sales were made the taxes imposed by this section.
Sec. 6-51. - Excise tax on distilled spirits for consumption on premises.
(a)
There is imposed a sales tax on the sale of distilled spirits by the drink, which tax shall be three percent of the charge for the beverages. This tax does not apply to the sale of malt beverages nor to wines for which an excise tax has already been paid under section 6-50. Each retail licensee shall collect, report and remit the tax in the manner described in this section.
(b)
Every licensee or its agent is hereby authorized and directed to collect the tax herein imposed from purchasers of distilled spirits by the drink sold within its licensed premises. Such licensee or agent shall furnish such information as may be requested by the board of commissioners or its authorized agent to facilitate the collection of this tax.
(c)
All taxes collected by any licensee or agent hereunder shall be due and payable to the county on or before the tenth day of every month next succeeding each respective calendar month, as set forth herein.
(d)
On or before the tenth day of the month, a return for the preceding calendar month shall be filed with the board of commissioners or its authorized agent in such a form as the board of commissioners or its authorized agent may prescribe by every licensee or agent liable for the payment of tax hereunder.
(e)
All returns shall show the gross receipts from the sale of distilled spirits by the drink, amount of tax collected or authorized due for the related period, and such other information as may be required by board of commissioners or its authorized agent including but not limited to gross receipts from the sale of food, copies of sales and use tax reports, or other reports required by any State or Federal agency.
(f)
The person required to file the return shall deliver the return, together with the remittance of the net amount of tax due to the board of commissioners or its authorized agent.
(g)
Licensees collecting the tax shall be allowed a percentage of the tax due and accounted for and shall be reimbursed in the form of a deduction in submitting, reporting and paying the amount due, if the amount is not delinquent at the time of payment. The rate of the deduction shall be the same rate authorized for deductions from the state sales tax under the Georgia Retailers' and Consumers' Sale and Use tax Act, approved February 20, 1951 (Georgia Laws 1951, p. 360), as now or hereafter amended.
Sec. 6-52. - Failure to pay tax; penalties and interest.
(a)
Any person who fails to pay the tax herein imposed to the county, or fails to pay any amount of such tax required to be collected and paid to the county, within the time required, shall pay a civil penalty of 15 percent of the tax, or amount of the tax, in addition to the tax or the amount of the tax, plus interest on the unpaid tax or any portion thereof as set forth in subsection (b) of this section.
(b)
The amount of the tax, exclusive of penalties, shall bear interest at the rate of one percent per month, or fraction thereof, from the tenth day after the close of the monthly period for which the amount of any portion thereof should have been returned, until the date of payment.
(c)
If any part of the deficiency for which a deficiency determination has been made is due to gross negligence or disregard of rules and regulations, a penalty of 15 percent of the amount of such deficiency shall be added thereto in addition to the 15 percent prescribed by subsection (b).
(d)
Penalty for fraud or intent to evade. If any part of the deficiency for which a deficiency determination has been made is due to fraud or an intent to evade any provisions of this chapter or other authorized rules and regulations, a penalty of 25 percent of the deficiency shall be added thereto in addition to the 15 percent prescribed by subsection (b).
Sec. 6-53. - Types of retail sales prohibited.
No adult entertainment establishment, as defined in any chapter of Butts County, shall be permitted to serve, sell or disburse malt beverage, wine, distilled spirits or alcoholic beverages. This prohibition is for the purpose of reducing criminal activity and minimizing community blight, which studies of related communities including, but not limited to Austin, Texas, Amarillo, Texas, Beaumont, Texas, Indianapolis, Indiana, Los Angeles County, California, and St. Paul, Minnesota, have shown to be increased by the presence of adult entertainment establishments.
ARTICLE IV. - [ENFORCEMENT]

Sec. 6-54. - Jurisdiction of the Butts County Magistrate Court.
The Magistrate Court of Butts County is hereby granted jurisdiction to enforce any and all provisions of this chapter. In addition, to the administrative penalties set forth herein which shall be imposed by Butts County upon conviction of any licensee, the Magistrate Court is hereby authorized to impose a fine of up to $1,000.00, or incarceration in jail for a period of up to six months, upon any person convicted of violating any provision of this chapter.
