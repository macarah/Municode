ARTICLE I. - GENERAL PROVISIONS

Section 1.01. - Short title.
This article shall be known and may be cited as the subdivision regulations of the county.
Section 1.02. - Authority.
This article is adopted pursuant to the authority delegated to the county under the state constitution, Article IX, Section 2, Paragraph 1.
Section 1.03. - Purpose.
The expressed purpose of this article is to protect the public health, safety, morals and general welfare of the county residents:
1.
To encourage the development of sound and stable neighborhoods;
2.
To assure the provision of adequate streets, utilities, and other facilities and services to new land development projects;
3.
To conserve and protect the natural, historic and scenic resources of the county;
4.
To help maintain the economic viability of forestry and agricultural resources of the county;
5.
To prevent the spread of blight and slums within the county;
6.
To assure that residential and commercial lots within the county will be of such design, area, and width as will prevent health and sanitation problems;
7.
To prevent and reduce traffic congestion and traffic hazards within the county;
8.
To provide buffers to protect property values and maintain the scenic beauty of the county;
9.
To assure that drainage systems for subdivisions are constructed so as to not adversely affect the natural drainage systems of surrounding properties;
10.
To reduce maintenance problems with roads, drainage and other subdivision improvements within the county;
11.
To assure the timely completion of subdivision improvements within subdivisions in the county;
12.
To assure that the taxpayers of the county are not burdened with costs of completing subdivision improvements or correcting improperly constructed subdivision improvements, which costs should be the responsibility of private businesses;
13.
To prevent development in areas of the county unsuitable for residential or commercial uses because of soils, wetlands or drainage characteristics;
14.
To assure that all lots within the county will be accessible to fire fighting equipment and other emergency and service vehicles;
15.
To protect the investments of the buyers of lots within subdivisions;
16.
To preserve the environmental, historical and social heritage and character of the county;
17.
To encourage the design of roads within subdivisions so that speeding within subdivisions will be reduced.
Section 1.04. - Jurisdiction.
This article shall govern all subdivisions of land within the unincorporated areas of the county.
Section 1.05. - Scope.
1.
No person shall divide or subdivide or cause a subdivision to be made, by deed or map, of any parcel of land which is located within the boundaries of the county, except in conformity with the provisions of these regulations.
2.
Any owner or developer of any tract of land situated within the county who subdivides the same shall cause a plat of such subdivision, with reference to known or permanent monuments, to be made and recorded in the office of the clerk of superior court of the county. No such plat of subdivision shall be recorded unless and until it shall have been submitted to and approved and certified by the county planning commission, the county enforcement officer, and the county clerk.
3.
These regulations bear no relation to any private easement, covenant, agreement, or restriction, and the responsibility of enforcing such private easement, covenant, agreement, or restriction is not implied herein to any public official. When these regulations call for more restrictive standards than those required by private contract, the provisions of these regulations shall control.
Section 1.06. - Fees.
The county board of commissioners will establish a reasonable fee schedule to help cover the costs of administering the subdivision article. Said fees shall be submitted with preliminary or final plat and, upon acceptance of said plat for review and consideration, shall be deposited into the treasury of the county. The fee schedule for review of any and all subdivision applications shall be $200.00 per review.
(Ord. No. 2005-3, § 1, 7-18-05)
Section 1.07. - Administration.
The county compliance officer in collaboration with the Moultrie-Colquitt County planning commission shall administer these regulations, approval or disapproval of plans pursuant to.
(Ord. No. 2004-1, § 3, 2-16-04)
Section 1.08. - Enforcement.
The county compliance officer will have the responsibility of the enforcement of these regulations.
(Ord. No. 2004-1, § 1, 2-16-04)
ARTICLE II. - DEFINITIONS

Section 2.01. - Definitions.
Unless otherwise expressly stated, the following terms shall, for the purpose of this article, have the meaning herein indicated.
1.
Alley. A public or private street primarily designed to serve as secondary access to the side or rear of those properties whose principle frontage is on some other street.
2.
Comprehensive plan. The county adopted long-range plan (20 years).
3.
Concept plan. A preliminary presentation and attendant documentation of a proposed subdivision or site plan of sufficient accuracy to be used for the purpose of discussion.
4.
Cul-de-sac. A street with only one outlet and having the other end for reversal of traffic movement.
5.
Elevation certificate. A document produced by the Federal Emergency Management Agency to be used by registered land surveyors or other authorized individual to verify and record the actual as-built lowest floor elevation of a structure. This document will be required to be filed with the county board of commissioners when a structure is being constructed or substantially improved in a special flood hazard area (Zones A, AE, A-1 through A-30, AO and AH).
6.
Easement. A right-of-way granted, but not dedicated, for limited use of private land for a public purpose.
7.
Final plat approval. The official action of the planning commission taken on a subdivision after all conditions, engineering plans, and other requirements have been completed, and the required improvements have been installed, or guarantees posted for their completion.
8.
Final plat. The final map of all or a portion of a subdivision which is presented for final approval which is accurate and complete and suitable for recording pursuant to these regulations.
9.
Flag lot. A lot having a very narrow frontage on a public road, and a minimum of a 60-foot road frontage on a public road. No one development/subdivision shall consist of more than 25 percent of lots as flag lots.
10.
Flood hazard area. The channel and relatively flat area adjoining the channel of a natural stream, river, or body of water subject to flooding during major storm events. Specifically, such designation shall refer to:
(1)
Those areas within the county identified by the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) as being subject to flooding and delineated on flood insurance maps, or
(2)
Particular areas of the county which, based on actual observation of flooding or engineering studies, have been designated as local flood hazard areas by the county.
11.
Groundwater recharge districts. Those areas mapped by the department of natural resources which soils have high permeability characteristics.
12.
Groundwater recharge ordinance. The adopted county ordinance which regulates development in designated groundwater recharge areas.
13.
Lot. A designated parcel, tract, or area of land established by a plat or otherwise as permitted by law and to be used, developed, or built upon as a unit.
14.
Lot of record. A lot or parcel of land which has been lawfully recorded by subdivision plat or deed on the public records of the county on or before the date of adoption of this article.
15.
Maintenance guarantee. Any security which may be required and accepted to ensure that necessary improvements will function as required for a specific period of time.
15.5.
Major subdivision. A tract or parcel with nine or more lots with or without new roads in the subdivision.
16.
Marginal access street. A service street that runs parallel to a higher-order access to abutting properties and separation from through traffic.
17.
Minor subdivision. A subdivision of land of five to eight lots with no new roads being constructed, provided that such subdivision does not involve any new street, nor change in grade, drainage, or dirt moving.
18.
Planned unit development (PUD). A planned unit development in a single parcel of land within which a number of buildings are located in accordance with an overall plan of design.
19.
Planning commission. The county planning commission.
20.
Pre-application conference. An informal meeting between developers and county representatives which affords developers the opportunity to present their plans informally.
21.
Preliminary plat approval. The conferral of certain rights prior to final approval after specific elements of a development plan have been agreed upon by the planning commission and the applicant.
22.
Preliminary subdivision plat. A map indicating the proposed layout of a planned development that is submitted for preliminary approval.
23.
Plat. A map indicating the subdivision or re-subdivision of land, intended to be filed for record.
24.
Private subdivision. A subdivision providing for privately owned roads and other commonly owned property within the subdivision for which access is restricted to owners of, or invitees to, property within the subdivision.
25.
Resubdivision. A change in an approved or recorded subdivision plat if such changes affects any street or area reserved for public use or any lot line.
26.
River corridor district. All lands, inclusive of islands, being within 100 feet horizontally along the western side of the Little River as measured from the river bank.
27.
River corridor ordinance. The adopted county ordinance which regulates development within the Little River Corridor.
28.
Set-back. The distance between the street right-of-way line and the front line of a building, or any projection thereof, excluding uncovered steps.
29.
Soil erosion and sedimentation control plan. A plan for the control of soil erosion and sediment resulting from a land-disturbing activity. Appropriate copies of this plan are required to be delivered to the county appropriate for dispersal to the appropriate reviewers along with a land disturbing permit application. Approval of the plan by the appropriate natural resource conservation services office is required prior to preliminary plat consideration.
30.
Subdivision. Subdivision means any division of a tract or parcel of land into five or more lots, building sites, mobile home sites, or other divisions, resulting in any single lot of less than five acres with a minimum of a 60-foot right-of-way on a public road, for the purpose, whether immediate or future, of sale or legacy, and includes re-subdivision and, where appropriate to the context, relates to the process of subdividing or to the land or area subdivided.
31.
Variance. A variance is a relaxation of the terms of the this appendix where such variance will not be contrary to the public interest and where, owing to conditions peculiar to the particular property and not the result of any action of the applicant, a literal enforcement of this article's requirements would result in unnecessary and undue hardship.
32.
Wetlands. Those areas that are inundated or saturated by surface or ground water at a frequency and duration sufficient to support, and that under normal circumstances, do support a prevalence of vegetation typically adapted for life in saturated soil conditions. Wetlands generally include swamps, marshes, bogs, and similar areas.
33.
Wetlands ordinance. The adopted county ordinance which regulates development within the county's identified wetlands.
(Ord. No. 2001-2, §§ 1, 2, 11-6-01; Ord. No. 2004-1, § 4, 2-16-04; Res. No. 2017-R-4, 4-18-17)
ARTICLE III. - SUBDIVIDING PROCEDURE

Section 3.01. - Purpose.
The purpose of this article is to establish the procedure for the planning commission review and action on applications for proposed subdivisions. The process is intended to provide orderly and expeditious processing of such applications.
The documents to be submitted are intended to provide the planning commission with sufficient information and data to assure compliance with all county codes and specifications and ensure that the proposed development meets the design and improvement standards contained in this article.
Section 3.02. - Pre-application.
For the purpose of expediting subdivision applications and reducing subdivision design and development costs, the developer may request a pre-application conference in accordance with the following requirements:
Pre-application conference. The pre-application conference shall allow the applicant to meet with appropriate county representatives. These individuals may include: county compliance officer, county engineer and county planner, among others.
The applicant shall not be bound by the determination of the pre-application conference, nor shall the planning commission be bound by any such review.
The purpose of the meeting may be defeated if the technical staff does not have the preliminary plat far enough in advance to study them.
(Ord. No. 2004-1, § 1, 2-16-04)
Section 3.03. - Department of transportation approval.
For subdivisions abutting a road maintained by the state, DOT approval must be obtained for such subdivision's plan for access to state highway prior to submission for preliminary plat review.
Section 3.04. - Application.
The developer shall submit to the compliance officer a written application (obtained from code enforcement office) for subdivision review and approval by the county planning commission which meets once a month. To be placed on the planning commission's monthly agenda, an applicant must make an application request at least ten working days prior to the next planning commission meeting or the application will be reviewed at the following meeting. Within 45 days (see 3.06 3(c)) of submission of a complete application, the planning commission will act on the application. Failure of the planning commission to act within the period prescribed shall constitute a recommendation for approval to the county board of commissioners. The county planning commission will recommend to either "approve" or "not approve" the application to the county board of commissioners. The board of commissioners will either "approve" or "not approve" the application.
The following fee schedule for subdivision applications shall be effective: Minor subdivision application fees are established at $25.00, and major subdivision application fees are established at $50.00.
(Ord. No. 2000-3, § 1, 6-19-00; Ord. No. 2004-1, § 1, 2-16-04; Ord. No. 2006-1, § 1, 1-17-06)
Section 3.05. - Minor subdivision procedure.
Any applicant requesting approval of a proposed minor subdivision as defined, shall submit to the compliance officer appropriate number of copies of the subdivision plat meeting all final plat specifications, together with an executed application form and prescribed fees paid.
If the code enforcement office receives a written application for a minor subdivision, he must place the application on the planning commission's agenda at least ten working days prior to the next planning commission meeting or the application will be reviewed at the following meeting. The county planning commission will recommend to either "approve" or "not approve" the minor subdivision application to the county board of commissioners. If the application is approved by the board of commissioners, the county clerk shall affix onto the plat an approval stamp as required for recording.
(Ord. No. 2001-2, § 3, 11-6-01; Ord. No. 2004-1, § 1, 2-16-04; Ord. No. 2006-1, § 1, 1-17-06)
Section 3.06. - Major subdivision procedure.
1.
Preliminary plat approval. Any applicant seeking preliminary plat approval for this class of subdivision shall submit to the compliance officer appropriate number of copies of the preliminary plat along with an executed formal application.
2.
Preliminary plat requirements. The preliminary plat will include the following information:
a.
Proposed subdivision name and total acres. Subdivision names shall not duplicate the name of any other subdivision in the county.
b.
Name of owner of subdivision.
c.
The names of owners of all land contiguous to the proposed subdivision.
d.
A vicinity map showing the relative location of the proposed subdivision.
e.
Proposed street names, right-of-way, and roadway width.
f.
Location of utility and drainage easements.
g.
Lot lines, lot and block numbers (consecutively numbered or lettered), lot sizes, area in parks, etc.
h.
Topographic maps of all land subdivided, with scale and contour interval, as deemed appropriate by the planning commission.
i.
Minimum building set-back lines.
j.
Numerical scale, graphic scale, north arrow, date.
k.
All elevations shall be based on sea level datum.
l.
Soil characteristics, including soil types and capabilities, frequency and evaluations of seasonal high groundwater tables, occurrence of rock, and other impervious strata where the subdivision is not to be served by a public or community sewerage system. This should be superimposed on the plat.
m.
Plans for control of erosion and sedimentation.
n.
Location of all water supplies on or off the subdivision which will bear upon the location of the on-site sewage management systems.
o.
Delineation on plat of all wetlands and 100 year regulatory flood plains.
p.
If the state department of transportation approval and permits are required, submit a copy of the D.O.T. approval letter and permit.
q.
Site reservation for garbage dumpsters, with ten or more lots as needed.
Where, in the judgment of the planning commission, any requirement(s) under this section are determined as not necessary, the planning commission may waiver said requirement(s).
3.
Planning commission review procedures.
a.
Filing: The preliminary plat shall be deemed filed with the planning commission when it is filed with the compliance officer.
b.
Distribution and review: Copies of the preliminary plat shall be distributed by the code enforcement department to the appropriate county department heads and the Southwest Georgia RDC, when appropriate, for review and recommendations to the planning commission. Prior to the planning commission meeting, the RDC shall submit, in writing, copies of their recommendations to the planning commission. Recommendations from other agencies and departments may also be submitted.
c.
Approval: Approval or disapproval of the preliminary plat by the planning commission shall be accomplished within 45 days after the date of the scheduled regular planning commission meeting following submission of the application, except for providential cause. If, after the 45 days, the planning commission has failed to act, the preliminary plat shall be deemed approved, provided, that the subdivider may waive in writing this requirement and consent to an extension of such period. If the planning commission disapproves the preliminary plat, the reasons shall be stated in the resolution and the subdivider shall be so advised. The county planning commission will recommend to either "approve" or "not approve" the preliminary plat to the county board of commissioners. If the board of commissioners "approve" the preliminary plat, the subdivider shall prepare the final plat for review and approval by the planning commission and for final approval by the county board of commissioners. No land disturbing activities will be permitted after preliminary plat approval or before approval and recording of the final plat.
4.
Effect of preliminary plat approval. Preliminary plat approval shall confer upon the applicant the following rights for a three year period from the date of the preliminary plat approval:
a.
That the general terms and conditions on which preliminary plat approval was granted shall not be changed;
b.
The applicant may submit for final plat approval on or before the expiration date of preliminary approval, the whole or sections of the preliminary subdivision plat; and,
c.
The applicant may apply for and the planning commission may grant an extension on such preliminary approval for a specified time period.
5.
House numbers/street addresses. House numbers shall be assigned and street addresses shall be assigned by the county addressing department after preliminary plat approval.
6.
Final plat approval
a.
An applicant seeking final plat approval of a subdivision shall submit to the compliance officer appropriate number of copies of the final plat. The final plat shall be accompanied by a statement from the county road and bridge superintendent that the county is in receipt of "as built" plans showing all streets, drainage and retention facilities and utilities in exact location and elevation and identifying those portions already installed and those to be installed, and/or certified in the amount of performance guarantees required to assure completion of those improvements not yet installed.
b.
The planning commission shall recommend to "approve" or "not approve" the final plat to the county board of commissioners within 45 days after the date of the regular scheduled planning commission meeting or within such time as may be consented to by the applicant. Failure of the planning commission to act within 45 days shall constitute a recommendation to "approve" the final plat by the planning commission to the board of commissioners. Final plat shall be "approved" or "not approved" by the county board of commissioners. No land disturbing activities will be permitted before approval and recording of the final plat.
c.
Final approval of a subdivision shall expire 90 days from the date of the signing of the plat by the planning commission chairman, chairman of the county board of commissioners and county clerk unless within such period, the plat shall have been duly filed by the developer with the clerk of superior court. A notation will be placed on each plat pursuant to this requirement.
d.
No subdivision plat shall be accepted for filing by the clerk of superior court until it has been approved by the planning commission as indicated on the plat by the signature of the planning commission chairman, chairman of the county board of commissioners and the county clerk.
7.
Final plat requirements.
a.
The final plat shall conform to the preliminary plat as approved.
b.
The final plat shall be a professional drawing in a size which is suitable for filing in the land records of the county.
c.
If the proposed water/sewerage system for a subdivision requires the approval of the state department of human resources and/or the department of natural resources, the approval shall be obtained prior to making application for approval of the final plat. The approval shall be in writing to the planning commission.
d.
The health department's statement of approval must be done before the final plat is presented to the planning commission.
8.
A final plat shall show:
a.
Primary control points and bench marks with necessary descriptions and locations of such control points, including all dimensions, angles, bearings, and similar data necessary for proper location.
b.
Tract boundary lines, right-of-way lines of streets, easements and other right-of-way and property lines of residential lots and other sites, with accurate dimensions, bearings of deflection angles, radii, and area and central angle of all curves.
c.
Names and right of way of each street or other right-of-way.
d.
Location, dimensions and purpose of easements.
e.
Number or letters to identify each lot.
f.
Purpose for which sites, other than residential lots, are dedicated or reserved.
g.
Minimum buildings set-back lines on all lots and other sites.
h.
Location and description of monuments.
i.
The name on record of adjoining property owners.
j.
Reference to recorded subdivision plats of adjoining platted land by record names, data, and number.
k.
Certification by registered surveyor to accuracy of survey and plat.
l.
Title, numerical scale, graphic scale, north arrow, date.
m.
A statement, either directly on the plat or in an identified attached document of private covenants, if any.
n.
A copy of the deeds for dedications of public areas (streets, drainage facilities, etc.) to the county board of commissioners.

Upon final plat approval, the planning commission chairman, chairman of the county board of commissioners and the county clerk of the county shall inscribe on the plat the following: Pursuant to the land subdivision ordinance of the county, all the requirements of approval having been fulfilled, this final plat was given final approval by: The Colquitt County Board of Commissioners on the _______ day of _______, 20___.

Street, easements, alleys, etc. The approval of final plat by the planning commission and its subsequent recording does not constitute acceptance by the county of any street, easement, alley or other grounds shown on the plat. This will be done by formal dedication and by acceptance resolution of the county board of commissioners.

Where a minor subdivision, defined as not more than eight lots, is proposed which will require no new roadways or drainage improvements, the compliance officer may, at his discretion, allow for combined one step review and approval of the proposed development. Such review will entail the filing of a single application and final plat to satisfy the requirements of these regulations.
(Ord. No. 2001-2, § 5, 11-6-01; Ord. No. 2004-1, §§ 1, 6, 2-16-04)
Section 3.08.1. - Application for private subdivision.
An applicant requesting preliminary approval of a private subdivision shall comply with the requirements of this Code, including but not limited to chapter 10, appendix A, Zoning Ordinance, appendix B, Subdivision Regulations, and the county comprehensive plan, except to the extent the provisions of this article specifically differ.
(Res. No. 2017-R-4, 4-18-17)
Section 3.08.2. - Conditions of private subdivisions.
(a)
The following conditions shall be placed on any application for private subdivision approval. All of the following requirements shall be met before the application shall be considered for review by the planning commission.
(1)
All streets shall be constructed to public street standards.
(2)
Easements:
a.
Easements for private streets shall be designated on final plats as general-purpose public access and utility easements, along with the name of said private street.
b.
Easements shall at a minimum be of the same width as that required for a public street in accordance with requirements of the state department of transportation.
c.
Easements for private streets shall not be included in any calculation of minimum lot size or density limitations established by the county health department.
d.
Easements shall either;
1.
Be shown on the final plat such that each lot fronting the private street extends to the centerline of the private street. No lot shall be permitted to be divided by the general purpose public access and utility easement required and established for a private street; or
2.
Shall be drawn as its own discrete parcel to be dedicated to a private property owners association (not shown to be part of any lot).
(3)
Private streets shall be named, subject to approval of the E911 coordinator.
a.
The subdivider of land involving a private street shall install street signs with content containing the street name and the designation "private," as approved by the public works director.
b.
The sign signifying the private street shall be blue, to distinguish maintenance responsibilities in the field.
(4)
For building setback purposes, the setbacks shall be measured as if the streets were public streets.
(5)
The developer shall establish a private maintenance covenant recorded with the clerk of the superior court, creating a homeowners association with by-laws which shall specifically include the following:
a.
Clearly state that county shall not maintain, repair, resurface, rebuild, or otherwise improve streets, signs, drainage improvements or any other appurtenances within general purpose public access and utility easements established for private streets and all such improvements are the responsibility of the developer or the property page 2 owners association.
b.
Automatic mandatory membership of all purchasers of lots therein and their successors.
c.
Responsibility for maintenance, insurance and taxes.
d.
The covenant shall establish minimum annual assessments in an amount adequate to defray costs of ordinary maintenance and procedures for approval of additional needs assessments. The covenant shall also specify that a property owners association will hold the funds from such assessments.
e.
The covenant shall establish a formula for assessing maintenance and repair costs equitably to property owners served by the private street. The public works director shall determine such formula.
f.
The covenant shall include a periodic maintenance schedule. The public works director shall determine the maintenance schedule to be followed.
g.
The covenant shall run with the land.
h.
Authority to place liens on the real property of members who fail to pay their dues or assessments.
(6)
The developer shall comply with article V for the installation of water and sanitary sewer mains within the private development.
(7)
Gates and/or security guards may be permitted. However, roads and other areas shall be open to the normal operation of emergency, police and public service vehicles and personnel, and must be approved by the county emergency management director for compliance with that office's general order for security gate access.
(8)
It is assumed that the roads within a private subdivision will perpetually remain privately maintained, however, in the event the owners of the subdivision desire to dedicate roads to the county, said roads must meet all county standards and specifications at the proposed time of dedication. The roads must pass inspections by the department of roads and bridges prior to dedication of the streets to the county. There shall be no implied dedication of roads or streets to the county and no dedication shall be effective unless accepted in writing by the county board of commissioners.
(Res. No. 2017-R-4, 4-18-17)

ARTICLE IV. - DESIGN STANDARDS

Section 4.01. - Purpose.
The purpose of good subdivision and site design is to create a functional and attractive development, to minimize adverse impacts, and to ensure a project will be an asset to the community. To promote this purpose, the subdivision and/or site plan shall conform to the following standards which are designed to result in a well-planned community without adding unnecessarily to development costs.
Section 4.02. - Streets.
1.
General.
a.
The arrangement of streets shall conform to the circulation plan of the comprehensive plan or official map of the county.
b.
For streets not shown on the comprehensive plan or official map, the arrangement shall provide for the appropriate extension of existing streets.
c.
Residential streets shall be arranged so as to discourage through traffic and provide for maximum privacy.
2.
Access.
a.
Alignment and continuation. Streets within the subdivision shall be so arranged as to provide for the alignment and continuation of, or projection of, existing public streets, where feasible.
b.
Dead-end streets. Permanent dead end streets and alleys shall be prohibited. Subdivisions that are developed in phases and exhibit streets to be completed at a later date must provide for a temporary unpaved turn-around at the end of a paved street having a roadway diameter of at least 80 feet.
c.
Private streets. Private streets shall be prohibited.
d.
Intersections. The center line of no more than two streets shall intersect at any one point. No street nor its center line tangent shall intersect another street at less than 80 degrees and shall as nearly as possible intersect at 90 degrees.
e.
Curb radius. The curb radius at street intersections shall be no less than 20 feet except on major arterial streets where state department of transportation standards shall prevail.
f.
Curved streets. The inner curb radius shall not be less than 350 feet for major arterial streets, 250 feet for secondary streets, and 100 feet for collector and residential streets. Center line tangents between reverse curves shall not be less than 200 feet for secondary streets and 100 feet for collector and residential streets.
g.
Traffic control signs shall be installed by the county and the cost thereof paid by developer prior to final acceptance of streets.
h.
Streets shall be laid out so as to intersect as nearly as possible at right angles and no street shall intersect any other street at an angle less than 80 degrees.
i.
No street name shall be used which will duplicate or be confused with the name of existing streets. Street names shall be subject to the approval of the planning commission after coordination with the county's addressing and emergency dispatching department.
j.
Cul-de-sac or permanent dead-end street shall be terminated by a turnaround with an outside roadway diameter of at least 80 feet and a street property line diameter of at least 100 feet. Cul-de-sac streets shall not exceed 1500 feet in length. A cul-de-sac may have only four lots fronting on the turnaround.
k.
Minimum street right-of-way widths shall conform to the following:

Section 4.03. - Easements.
Except where alleys are permitted, the developer shall provide easements across lots or centered on rear or side lot lines for utilities and such easements shall be at least 20 feet wide. Where a subdivision is traversed by a water course, drainage way or stream, there shall be provided a storm water easement at such width adequate for the purpose.
Easements for the location of garbage receptacles and for ingress and egress thereto together with fences or other screens shall be provided when in the opinion of the planning commission, such easements and fences or screens are necessary to provide adequate garbage collection service to the proposed subdivision.
Section 4.04. - Lots.
a.
Lot dimension. Lot dimensions shall conform to the requirements of the county's zoning ordinance or county health department or as required by the county's environmental ordinances (groundwater, river corridor).
b.
Corner lots. Corner lots for residential use shall have extra width to permit appropriate building set-back from both abutting streets.
c.
Access. Each lot shall have required frontage 150 feet on a public street. Lot frontage requirement shall be measured at the "minimum" building setback line.
d.
Double frontage. Double frontage lots shall be avoided.
e.
Lot lines. Side lot lines shall be substantially at right angles to straight street lines or radial to curved street lines.
f.
Lot remnants. Lots below the minimum area or width left over after subdivision shall be prohibited. Such remnant areas shall be added to adjacent lots rather than remain as unusable parcels.
g.
Only one principal dwelling per lot. Except as herein provided, there shall be no more than one principal dwelling per lot other than within PUD developments. In agricultural communities (for farm hands and/or immediate family members only) within the unincorporated areas of the county, there shall be permitted, as a matter or right, to any owners of property as many as four residential uses on any parcel of land under single ownership where the following conditions can be met:
1.
Each such non-principal residential use shall occupy a land area not less than that, all as required by the county health department, for a sufficient water supply and sanitary sewage disposal system as determined by the state.
2.
Each such land area shall be so defined by permanent physical markers as to be given a numerical address and location designation.
3.
Each such land area shall receive approval from the county health authorities as to its suitability as a site for an effective sanitary sewage disposal system designed to accommodate wastes generated by the user of that land site.
4.
An acceptable domestic water supply shall be available to each satellite user of this special provision and such water supply shall meet local public requirements as administered by the county health authorities.
5.
No commercial use of this special provision will be allowed, except renting for residential uses.
6.
Each such use shall be accessible either by private drive or public roadways to the public thoroughfare system.
7.
Individual power supply sources shall be provided to each under this special provision and each utility installation shall meet such standards as have been adopted by local authorities.
8.
Facilities established under this special provision for residential use shall meet the requirements of local construction and use codes established by the county board of commissioners.
9.
Permits for construction will not be issued prior to the approval of each of the aforementioned conditions by the county compliance officer.
h.
Flag lots. Flag lots must have a minimum of a 60-foot road frontage on a public road.

Section 4.05. - Blocks.
Blocks longer than 1,500 feet or shorter than 400 feet between street intersections may be cause for disapproval.

ARTICLE V. - REQUIRED IMPROVEMENTS

Section 5.01. - Developer's responsibility.
Every subdivider, at his own expense, shall be required to install, or at his own expense, to have installed by the appropriate public utility, the requirements under this article.
Section 5.02. - Monuments and iron pipes.
Permanent reference monuments shall be placed at block corners and other points such as street intersections and point of curve, lot corners and tangent points and angle points and shall be marked with a solid iron rod or an iron pipe not less than one-half (½) inch in diameter, or at least two feet long, driven flush with the ground. Monuments shall be indicated on all plats. Removal of monuments and resetting by anyone other than a certified land surveyor is prohibited.
Section 5.03. - Curb and gutter.
All new streets may be required to have curb and gutter built in accordance with county and DOT standards and specifications for:
(1)
Storm water management (limited right-of-way precludes ditches)
(2)
Stabilization of pavement edge
(3)
Ten feet on each side of drainage inlets
(4)
Intersections
(5)
Corners
(6)
Tight radii
and that the planning commission has the authority to require curb and gutter with justification.
Section 5.04. - Street paving.
The developer shall pave any new road so that the combination and specifications for materials including base course prime and paving materials shall meet state department of transportation specification and revisions which are applicable at that time.
Paving may be one foot less in width than the finished base course, centered in the right-of-way.
The slope of the finished paved section from the centerline toward the ditch shall be at the rate of one-fourth inch per foot of width while the unpaved portion to the facing slope of the ditch shall be sloped one inch per foot of width.
All shoulders and drainage slopes, front and back, within roadways shall have a hearty ground cover of any permanent vegetation or plant material approved by the road and bridge superintendent. The width of grassed portion of each shoulder shall not be less than four feet from margin of ditch top front slope.
The road and bridge superintendent shall be notified when the application of the base course is completed. He shall inspect the work for compliance with these regulations. Upon approval, the paving may be applied. Prior to acceptance by the county, the road superintendent shall certify that the work has been completed in compliance with these regulations. In making the necessary inspections, the county administrator or his designee may seek the advice of qualified personnel of the state department of transportation, if and when available.
(Ord. No. 2004-1, § 12, 2-16-04)
Section 5.04.5. - Private subdivision paving policy.
It is hereby the policy of the county board of commissioners that with time permitting and provided manpower and equipment availability, the county board of commissioners, acting in a regularly scheduled board meeting, may authorize the county road and bridge department to apply the base course prime and paving materials to any private subdivision street provided the subdivision developer construct the roads to county/state department of transportation (D.O.T.) specifications and transfer title of said streets to the county board of commissioners. It is further declared that the subdivider shall pay for the total cost of paving materials, equipment fuel associated with the paving of the subdivision street(s). Prior to the acceptance of the street(s) and the beginning of the paving of the street(s), the road and bridge department shall prepare a cost estimate of the paving materials for the subdivider (developer). Payment of same shall be made in advance to the county by the developer. Any cost underruns shall be returned to the developer and any cost overruns shall be paid to the county by the developer.
Editor's note— Res. No. 2017-R-4, adopted Apr. 18, 2017, ratified § 5.04.5, which was previously attached to the subdivision regulations by reference but never adopted.
Section 5.05. - Grassing.
All rights-of-way, cleared easements, and any other disturbed areas subject to erosion shall be grassed so as to establish a permanent turf using any appropriate grass variety except Pensacola Bahia grass. Such grassing should be done in conjunction with the requirements of the erosion and sedimentation ordinance (chapter 26).
a.
Fertilizer mixed grade: The developer may select fertilizer mixed grade such as 10-10-10, 6-12, 5-10-15 or any other analysis within the following limits: Nitrogen five to ten percent and Potassium ten to 15 percent. The fertilizer shall be spread uniformly over the ground surface at the approximate rate of 600 pounds per acre.
b.
Mixing: The lime and fertilizer shall be uniformly mixed into the top four inches of soil by suitable harrows, rotary tillers, or other equipment satisfactory to the county engineer before any more work is done on the area. On cut slopes steeper than 3:1 the mixing specified above may be reduced to the maximum practical depth as determined by the engineer, except on serrated slopes where mixing may be omitted.
Section 5.06. - Storm sewers, catch basin, manholes, impoundment ponds.
All new streets shall be provided with the necessary storm sewers, catch basins and manholes, built in accordance with county standards.
Storm water detention facilities shall be provided when needed and such facilities shall be designed to applicable standards. The responsibility for operation and maintenance of detention facilities shall remain with the owner with permanent arrangements that such responsibility shall pass to any successive owner.
Prior to final plat approval, the applicant shall enter into an agreement with the county to ensure the continued operation and maintenance of the detention facility.
Section 5.07. - Holding ponds.
Holding ponds shall be fenced with a minimum height of four feet of hog wire fence with two strands of barb wire for a total height of no less than five feet. Fencing shall be approved by the county enforcement official.
Section 5.08. - Sidewalks.
Sidewalks may be required for safe pedestrian movement (where required, minimum width of four feet).
Section 5.09. - Street lighting.
Street lights may be required to help create a night time environment which provides quick, accurate, and comfortable vision for both driver and pedestrians.
Section 5.10. - Traffic control signs.
Traffic control signs are required in accordance with state standards and criteria. Prior to final plat approval the appropriate road sign fees shall be paid to the county by the developer and the county will install the required signs.
Section 5.11. - Fire hydrants.
If installed, fire hydrants shall be located and set in accordance with industry standard and specifications.
Section 5.12. - Street name signs.
At least two street name signs shall be placed at each four way street intersection and one at each "T" intersection. The design of street name signs shall be consistent with others in the county. Prior to final plat approval the appropriate road sign fees shall be paid to the county by the developer and the county will install the required signs.
Section 5.13. - Installation of utilities.
Water mains:
a.
Where a public water supply is within a reasonable distance, the subdivider shall install or have installed a system of water mains and connect to such supply. The installation of said mains and connection to each lot shall be installed prior to the paving of the street, if possible.
b.
Where a public water supply is not available, each lot in a subdivision shall be furnished with a water supply system approved by the health department.
Sanitary sewer disposal:
a.
When, in the written opinion of the health department, public sanitary sewers are within reasonable access of the subdivision, and is feasible, the subdivider shall provide sanitary sewer services to each lot within the bounds of the subdivision. All street sewers serving lots in the subdivision shall be installed by the subdivider.
b.
When, in the written opinion of the health department, if public sanitary sewer is not accessible, an alternate method of sewage disposal for each lot or a community sewage disposal system may be used when in compliance with the standards of the health department.
c.
Whenever the installation of a sanitary sewer is required, as provided by these rules and regulations, no new street shall be paved without such sewer being first installed in accordance with the requirements of the county's sewer specifications.
Underground utilities:
1.
Any underground utilities shall be installed with the surface having the same compaction as that of the paved way and marked on the plat to indicate the location.
2.
The facilities for underground utilities such as sewer, water and gas, including sewer and water laterals to each lot line when laid in streets, shall be in place prior to final surfacing of streets. All facilities for utilities shall, where possible, be placed in easements provided for that purpose in the subdivision, if public utilities are available.
ARTICLE VI. - DEDICATION AND RESERVATION

Section 6.01. - Land and street.
1.
All land designated for street purpose or street widening shall be laid out and shown on the map of the subdivision and shall have provided therefore rights-of-way not less than the widths specified in section 4.02(k).
2.
All streets which are designated as part of the general highway system of the county, or part, division, or section thereof, as amended, shall be coordinated with adjoining links in said system and dedicated at the same or greater widths.
Section 6.02. - Drainage easement.
All drainage easements offered for dedication to public use shall have a minimum width of 15 feet, except under unusual circumstances, where a greater or lesser width may be required and/or approved. The county may not accept an easement for drainage maintenance purposes along a creek or natural drainage channel.
Section 6.03. - Utility easement.
Utility easements for electric and telephone service lines, sewage lines, water lines, or other such utilities located along rear lot lines or side lot lines or passing through a lot shall be at least 20 feet wide and ten feet on each lot. No structure shall be built on such easement.
Section 6.04. - County maintenance easement.
Maintenance easements shall be provided when deemed necessary by the county road and bridge superintendent. All easements to the county shall, in addition to being shown on a final plat, be evidenced by a written easement agreement recorded in the clerk of superior court's office.
ARTICLE VII. - CONSTRUCTION SCHEDULE

Section 7.01. - Prior approval.
No construction activity of any kind including grading, installation of improvements, and building shall begin on any land subject to these regulations without prior final plat approval by the county board of commissioners and recording of the plat by the developer and/or land owner.
(Ord. No. 2006-1, § 1, 1-17-06)
Section 7.02. - Grading.
Grading operations may begin following final approval and recording of the final plat by the county board of commissioners.
(Ord. No. 2006-1, § 1, 1-17-06)
Section 7.03. - Inspections.
Periodic inspection during the installation of the physical improvements in a subdivision shall be made by a duly authorized official of the county to insure conformity with the approved plans and specifications. The subdivider shall notify the compliance officer when each phase of the installation is completed and ready for inspection.
(Ord. No. 2004-1, § 1, 2-16-04)
Section 7.04. - Sale and transfer.
No lot or parcel of land shall be sold or transferred until the final plat, of which said lot or parcel is a part, shall have been approved and recorded as provided for in these regulations.
ARTICLE VIII. - VIOLATIONS, PENALTIES AND AMENDMENTS

Section 8.01. - Violations.
In case of any violation or attempted violation of the provisions of these regulations, the county may institute any appropriate action or proceeding to prevent such violation or attempted violation.
Section 8.02. - Penalty.
1.
Any owner or proprietor of any tract of land who subdivided that tract of land and who violates the provisions of these regulations shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction thereof, shall be punished as provided by the law.
2.
Any subdivision hereafter established shall be designed, developed, and recorded in accordance with the provisions of these regulations. Failure to comply with the regulations herein shall result in the following:
A.
The county shall not accept the subdivision, nor shall the county improve, maintain, grade or pave any street within such subdivision unless such street shall have met the specifications described herein.
B.
No public agency shall authorize the extension of water service, sewer service or other publicly operated services into such subdivisions.
Section 8.03. - Amendments.
These regulations may be amended by the board of county commissioners.
ARTICLE IX. - APPEALS AND VARIANCES
This article is established to identify the procedure whereby any aggrieved party may appeal the decision of any county employee and/or the Moultrie-Colquitt County planning commission pursuant to these regulations.
(Ord. No. 2004-1, § 10, 2-16-04)

Section 9.01. - Appeal to the county commission.
Any party aggrieved because of the alleged error in any order, requirement, decision, or determination made by a county employee and/or Moultrie-Colquitt County planning commission in enforcement and/or administration of this article, may appeal in writing to the county administrator for and receive a hearing by the county commission for an interpretation of the pertinent ordinance provision. In exercising this power of interpretation, the county commission may, in conformity with the provisions of this article, reverse or affirm any order, requirement, decision or determination made by a county employee and/or Moultrie-Colquitt County planning commission.
(Ord. No. 2004-1, § 11, 2-16-04)
Section 9.02. - Appeal from the county commission.
Any party aggrieved by any decision of the county commission may seek review of such decision by a court of record, as provided by law.
Section 9.03. - Variances (hardship).
The planning commission may grant variances from the literal requirements of these regulations in cases where the size, shape, or topography of a parcel prevents it from being designed to conform to these regulations.
Such variances may be granted in such cases of unnecessary hardship on a finding that:
a.
There are extraordinary and exceptional conditions pertaining to the particular piece of property in question because of its size, shape or topography, and;
b.
The application of the article to this particular piece of property would create an unnecessary hardship, and;
c.
Such conditions are peculiar to the particular piece of property involved, and;
d.
Relief if granted, would not cause substantial detriment to the public interest or impair the purposes and intent of this article.
If the planning commission grants a variance, the reason for such variances must be put forth clearly in the minutes of the meeting at which the variance was granted.
Section 9.04. - Zoning.
All preliminary plats must conform to the existing zoning regulations applicable at the time the preliminary plat is submitted for approval.
ARTICLE X. - LEGAL STATUS PROVISIONS

Section 10.01. - Repeal.
All resolutions or ordinances and parts or sections of resolutions or ordinances in conflict with this article are hereby repealed.
Section 10.02. - Separability.
Should any sentence, section, subsection or provision of these regulations, or application of a provision of these regulations, be determined unconstitutional or invalid, such declaration shall not affect or impair the validity of the remainder of said regulations or the regulations as a whole or any part thereof, other than the part of application so declared to be unconstitutional or invalid, and the county declares that it would have adopted the remainder of said regulations despite such invalid provision.
Section 10.03. - Conflict with other regulations.
Whenever the provisions of these regulations and those of other articles or statutes apply to the same subject matter, that article requiring the highest or strictest standard shall govern.
(Ord. No. 2004-1, § 12, 2-16-04)
