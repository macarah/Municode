ARTICLE II. - LEGISLATIVE BODY

Sec. 2-37. - Regular meetings.
The regular meetings of the mayor and council of the city shall be held on the first Monday of each month at 7:00 p.m. at the city council room unless said meeting must be rescheduled due to conflicts such as holidays.
(Comp. Ords. 2005, § 2-101)
Sec. 2-38. - Special meetings.
Special meetings of the mayor and council may be held in accordance with the provisions of section 2.19(b) of the city Charter and O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1.
(Comp. Ords. 2005, § 2-102)
State Law reference— Open and public meetings, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1.
Sec. 2-39. - Order of business.
At each meeting of the mayor and council the following regular order of business shall be observed unless dispensed with by a majority vote of the members present:
(1)
Call to order by the mayor.
(2)
Presentation of the minutes of previous meetings by the city clerk for correction and approval.
(3)
Recognition of visitors.
(4)
Old business.
(5)
New business.
(6)
Adjournment.
(Comp. Ords. 2005, § 2-103)
Sec. 2-40. - General rules of order; quorum.
Except as otherwise provided in the city Charter or this Code, the rules of order and parliamentary procedure contained in Robert's Rules of Order, Newly Revised, shall govern the transaction of business by and before the mayor and council at its meetings in all cases to which they are applicable. Three councilmembers and the mayor or mayor pro tem shall constitute a quorum.
(Comp. Ords. 2005, § 2-104)
Sec. 2-41. - Disturbing meetings.
(a)
While the mayor and council are in session, the members must preserve order and decorum, and a member shall neither, by conversation or otherwise, delay or interrupt the proceedings or the peace of the mayor and council nor disturb any member while speaking or refuse to obey the orders of the mayor and council or its presiding officer, except as otherwise herein provided.
(b)
Any person making personal, impertinent, or slanderous remarks or who shall become boisterous while addressing the mayor and council shall be forthwith, by the presiding officer, barred from further audience before the mayor and council, unless permission to continue is granted by a majority vote thereof.
(Comp. Ords. 2005, § 2-105)
