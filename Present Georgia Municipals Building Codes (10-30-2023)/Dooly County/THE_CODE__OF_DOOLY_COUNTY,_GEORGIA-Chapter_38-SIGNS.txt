Chapter 38 - SIGNS

Sec. 38-1. - Signs near interstate highways.
(a)
This section shall apply to all outdoor advertising signs visible and intended to be read from the main traveled way of Interstate 75 in the County.
(b)
No sign shall be erected or maintained unless it is in compliance with the regulations of this section.
(c)
Signs must be constructed of durable materials, maintained in good condition, and not permitted to become dilapidated.
(d)
All signs located on sites abutting or visible from the Interstate 75 right-of-way shall conform with O.C.G.A. title 32, ch. 6, art. 3, pt. 2 (O.C.G.A. § 32-6-70 et seq.) and shall meet all Federal and State requirements necessary to obtain a permit under said statute. In instances where the sign controls of this section are more strict, these regulations shall apply:
(1)
No new, off-premises, outdoor advertising signs shall be erected in the County on Interstate 75 unless they are located within 600 feet of an on-going commercial or industrial activity as specified in State law and are restricted to an area 1,200 feet long, beginning 500 feet from the point where the pavement widens on the main traveled way to accommodate the longest entrance or exit ramp.
(2)
On premises signs located within 100 feet of a regularly used part of the activity being advertised and located on the same property and for sale or for lease signs located on the property being advertised would not be prohibited.
(3)
Any legally erected sign which would become non-conforming as a result of this section would be allowed to remain until purchased by the Department of Transportation or the County, provided that the sign owner meets the requirements of State laws and rules and regulations governing such signs.
(Ord. of 5-22-1986, § 1)
State law reference— Signs along interstate highways, O.C.G.A. § 32-6-70 et seq.
