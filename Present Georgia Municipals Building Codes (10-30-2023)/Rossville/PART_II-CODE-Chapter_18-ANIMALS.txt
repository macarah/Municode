ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 18-1. - Horses, cattle, etc., running at large; staking, driving and leading requirements.
It shall be unlawful for any person to allow or permit any horses, cattle, sheep, goats or other livestock to run at large on the streets of the city, or to be staked so that such animals can go upon the streets of the city, or to drive or lead any such animals or livestock along or on the sidewalks in the city, or to drive or lead any such animals or livestock along and over the streets of the city, unless fastened to a rope, chain or other substantial leading device. Any such rope, chain or other substantial leading device must be securely held by a responsible person during the entire time that such animals or livestock are being led or driven along and over the streets of the city.
(Code 1976, § 5-1)
State Law reference— Livestock running at large, O.C.G.A. § 4-3-1 et seq.
Sec. 18-2. - Poisoning animals.
It shall be unlawful for any person to put out or cause to be put out any poison within the city which is for the primary purpose of killing or causing harm to any dog, cat or other domesticated animal.
(Code 1976, § 5-6)
State Law reference— Georgia Animal Protection Act, O.C.G.A. § 4-11-1 et seq.; cruelty to animals, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-4.
Sec. 18-3. - Manner of keeping animals.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep or maintain any horse, cattle, sheep, goats or other livestock within the city.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep or maintain any chickens or turkeys within the city.
(c)
Should a complaint arise that anyone is maintaining any animal in violation of this section, the owner or keeper of the animal will be promptly summoned to the municipal court. If after hearing the judge or other hearing officer determines that such animal or animals are being held within the city, the owner or other person involved will be given a period of time not to exceed 15 days, to either dispose of the animal or chicken or turkey or to remove such animal or animals from the city. If after such period of time is expired the owner or keeper of the animal has failed to either dispose of the animal or to remove the same from the city, a new citation shall be issued and served upon the owner or keeper setting a time and place for hearing on the complaint before the municipal court judge. If the municipal court judge determines at the hearing that the owner or keeper has failed either to dispose of the animal or fowl or to remove such from the city, the judge shall order such animal or fowl impounded and disposed of.
(d)
The violation of any order of the municipal court judge, or judge pro tem, under this section shall be considered a misdemeanor.
(Code 1976, § 5-7)
Sec. 18-4. - Vicious dogs, other animals.
It shall be unlawful for any owner or other person to maintain or harbor within the city a vicious dog or other vicious animal. It shall be prima facie evidence of viciousness if a dog or other animal, without provocation, fiercely attacks or bites persons or animals not on the owner's premises. For the purposes of this chapter the term "vicious animal" means any animal, including dogs, which shall bite or in any other manner attack or attempt to attack any person within the city; except that any animal that bites, attacks or attempts to attack any person who is unlawfully upon its owner's or keeper's premises or which is provoked to attack shall not be deemed a vicious animal. After a judicial determination by the municipal court that an animal is vicious, the owner or keeper of such vicious animal shall immediately destroy such animal in a humane manner or shall immediately turn such animal over to the county animal control officer, or any official designated by the mayor and council for humane destruction.
(Code 1976, § 5-8)
State Law reference— Dangerous Dog Control Law, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq.; liability of owner or keeper of vicious or dangerous animal for injuries caused by animal, O.C.G.A. § 51-2-7.
Sec. 18-5. - Dead animals, disposal of carcass.
(a)
All carcasses shall be disposed of in a manner which complies with the rules and regulations of the county health department and the rules and regulations of all departments and agencies of the state whose primary purpose is to protect and promote the health of the citizens of this state.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person who owns or has an interest in any animal which has died to leave the body or carcass thereof anywhere inside the city without proper disposal thereof as provided in subsection (a) of this section.
(Code 1976, § 5-9)
Cross reference— Solid waste, ch. 66.
State Law reference— Dead Animal Disposal Act, O.C.G.A. § 4-5-1 et seq.
Sec. 18-6. - Poultry running at large prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for anyone to allow chickens or turkeys to run at large within the city. Any chicken or turkey found running at large in the city may be impounded and disposed of under the provisions of article III of this chapter.
(Code 1976, § 5-10)
Sec. 18-7. - Keeping dangerous or wild animals prohibited.
(a)
The keeping, maintaining or housing of dangerous or wild animals within the city is hereby prohibited, and each day of the violation of this section shall be a separate offense.
(b)
For purposes of this section, dangerous or wild animals shall be defined as: Pit Bull or Pit Bull Terrier or any mixed breed of dog which contains an element of a Pit Bull dog, venomous snakes or other reptiles, alligators, crocodiles, poisonous spiders, birds of prey, wild cats, apes larger than chimpanzees, wild boars, bears, wolves, foxes, and poisonous amphibians.
(c)
Residents shall have seven calendar days to remove said animal from the City of Rossville after being notified of city code.
(d)
An animal control officer or any law enforcement officer may immediately impound any vicious animal found to be kept, maintained, or housed in the City of Rossville and issue a citation to the property owner where the animal is found to be kept, maintained, or housed if said animal had attacked or bit another person or animal not on the owners property, or if they have not complied with this Code.
(e)
The city court shall fine the offender not less than $50.00 for the first offense, not less than $200.00 for the second offense, and not less than $750.00 for the third offense or any offense thereafter.
(Code 1976, § 5-11; Ord. No. 428, 8-11-08)
State Law reference— Wild animals, O.C.G.A. § 27-5-4 et seq.; liability of owner or keeper of vicious or dangerous animal for injuries caused by animal, O.C.G.A. § 51-2-7.
Secs. 18-8—18-40. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - DOMESTICATED ANIMALS

Sec. 18-41. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Animal means any domesticated dog, cat, raccoon, skunk, opossum or squirrel.
County animal control officer and/or control officer means such person as designated by the county board of commissioners and/or the city as an official or officer whose primary responsibility is to enforce the animal control ordinances of the county, and/or of the city; to assist in enforcing the animal control ordinances of the various municipalities within the county and/or city; to maintain the animal pound of the county and/or city; and/or to catch, collect or otherwise pick up animals in the county and/or city which are in violation of the ordinances of the county and/or city and in the respective municipalities within the county which are in violation of the ordinances of the respective municipalities.
Licensed veterinarian is a person with a Doctor of Veterinary Medicine Degree licensed to practice veterinary medicine in the State of Georgia or in the State of Tennessee.
Nuisance means, but is not limited to, defecation, urination, disturbing the peace, destroying private property, emitting noxious or offensive odors, emitting noxious or offensive sounds and noises, or otherwise endangering or offending the well-being of the inhabitants of the city.
Owner of an animal is a person who owns, harbors or keeps or knowingly permits an animal to remain on or about his premises for more than five days.
Premises means a parcel of land and the structures thereon.
Registered animal shall mean an animal licensed by the city as provided in this article.
Vaccination is protection against rabies by inoculation with antirabies vaccine, given in an amount sufficient to provide immunity from rabies for a minimum of one year.
Vicious animal means any animal which shall bite or in any other manner attack or attempt to attack any person within the city; except that any animal that bites, attacks or attempts to attack any person unlawfully upon its owner's or keeper's premises, or which is provoked to attack, shall not be deemed a vicious animal.
(Code 1976, § 5-21)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 18-42. - Identification tag required.
(a)
All animals over 90 days old within the city shall wear a collar or harness to which is attached a metal identification tag of the kind hereinafter prescribed. Identification tags will be provided by the city upon presentation of a certificate of inoculation or vaccination for rabies to an employee of the city designated by the city clerk at the city hall and upon a payment of a fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk. The application for the identification tag shall state the name and address of the owner and the name, breed, age, color and sex of such animal. The license so granted by the city shall expire on June 30 of each calendar year and shall be renewed on or before July 1 of each year.
(b)
Animals belonging to nonresidents of the city who do not keep such animals within the city for 15 consecutive days shall be exempt from the provisions of this section; provided, however, all the other provisions of this chapter must be complied with.
(c)
All moneys collected for the issuance of identification tags and licenses shall go into the city treasury to help defray the expense of animal and fowl control.
(Code 1976, § 5-22)
State Law reference— Rabies inoculation tags, O.C.G.A. § 31-19-6.
Sec. 18-43. - Inoculation.
All animals over 90 days old within the city shall be inoculated annually so as to provide immunity from rabies for a period of at least one year. All inoculations shall be administered by a licensed veterinarian or an authorized representative of the public health department.
(Code 1976, § 5-23)
State Law reference— Inoculation of dogs and cats against rabies, O.C.G.A. § 31-19-6.
Sec. 18-44. - Tags.
(a)
Each city identification tag shall be so contrived as to permit it to be securely attached to the animal's collar or harness and shall have its respective number, the year in which issued and the words "City of Rossville" indented and impressed thereon. The design or color of such tags shall be varied from year to year so as to permit easy ascertainment as to whether or not the tag is current.
(b)
A valid city identification tag shall be securely attached to the animal's collar or harness, which collar or harness must be secured to and on the animal at all times. It shall be the duty and responsibility of the owner to see that the same is so attached and secured at all times.
(c)
A valid rabies vaccination tag shall be securely attached to the animal's collar or harness, which collar or harness must be secured to and on the animal at all times. It shall be the duty and responsibility of the owner to see that the same is so attached and secured at all times.
(Code 1976, § 5-24)
State Law reference— Rabies inoculation tags, O.C.G.A. § 31-19-6.
Sec. 18-45. - Record of tags.
A complete record of animal tags issued by the city shall be kept by the city. Such record shall show when the tag was issued; the period of time for which the tag is valid; to whom the tag was issued and the address of such person; for what particular animal the tag was issued; and if the owner and keeper of the animal are different persons, then the name and address of each person shall be entered.
(Code 1976, § 5-25)
Sec. 18-46. - Misuse of tag.
It shall be unlawful for any person to remove a valid tag from any animal which has been tagged under the provisions of this article, or to place a tag upon any animal for which the same was not issued, or to procure a tag from the city or from any licensed veterinarian by misrepresentation, or to disfigure any tag, or to alter the figures or words upon any tag. This provision shall apply to both the tag issued by the city and the tag for rabies inoculation issued by a licensed veterinarian or an authorized representative of the public health department.
(Code 1976, § 5-26)
Sec. 18-47. - Restraint of animals.
The owner or keeper of each animal within the city shall be responsible for keeping it from becoming a nuisance or from endangering or injuring any other person or property, and to that end each animal must at all times:
(1)
Be tied on the owner's or keeper's property so that it cannot reach the property of any adjoining owner or other public right-of-way; or
(2)
When allowed off the property of the owner or keeper, be accompanied by the owner or other responsible keeper and be restrained on a leash, rope, chain or other substantial leading device held by the owner or keeper or be in a vehicle driven or parked from which it cannot escape.
(Code 1976, § 5-27)
Secs. 18-48—18-80. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - IMPOUNDMENT

Sec. 18-81. - Authorized.
Any animal found in this city in violation of any provision of this chapter may be impounded by duly authorized officers of the city.
(Code 1976, §§ 5-1, 5-28)
Sec. 18-82. - Right of entry.
Employees and agents of the city charged with the enforcement of this chapter shall have the right to go upon private property within the city for the purpose of enforcing the provisions of this chapter when necessary.
(Code 1976, § 5-34)
Sec. 18-83. - Notice to owner.
Upon impoundment of any animals for the violation of any provision of this chapter, the chief of police shall determine the ownership of such animal by all reasonable means. The owner, if known, of any animal impounded under the provisions of this article shall be given written notice of such impoundment.
(Code 1976, §§ 5-2, 5-28)
Sec. 18-84. - Redemption authorized.
The owner of any animal impounded under the provisions of this article shall redeem the same after receiving notice of such impoundment by claiming the same, providing acceptable proof of ownership, and payment of all fees and charges incurred pursuant to such impoundment.
(Code 1976, §§ 5-2, 5-3)
Sec. 18-85. - Redemption period.
Animals not registered under this chapter impounded under the provisions of this article shall be held for a period of three days. Registered animals so impounded shall be held for a period of five days. After such retention period, the animal may be disposed of as provided in this article.
(Code 1976, §§ 5-2, 5-3, 5-29)
Sec. 18-86. - Charges.
The charges for the impoundment, keeping, feeding, and selling of any animal impounded under the provisions of this article shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1976, § 5-4)
Sec. 18-87. - Sale of nonredeemed animals.
(a)
Any animal which is not registered under this chapter impounded under the provisions of this article which is not redeemed by its owner may be sold by the city at public auction. Notice of such sale shall be posted at the city hall and at the place of impoundment of such animal. Such animal shall be sold to the highest bidder for cash. The proceeds of such sale shall be paid over to the city clerk, who shall pay out all expenses and shall hold any balance subject to the use and order of the mayor and council.
(b)
Any registered animal impounded under the provisions of this article which is not redeemed by its owner may be disposed of by:
(1)
Adoption,
(2)
Sale, or
(3)
Destruction in such a humane manner as deemed appropriate.
(Code 1976, §§ 5-2, 5-29)
ARTICLE IV. - HUMANE CARE OF ANIMALS

Sec. 18-88. - Violations relating to caring for animals.
(a)
Failure to provide humane care. It shall be unlawful for the owner or custodian of any animal to refuse or fail to provide such animal humane care.
(b)
Animal suffering. It shall be unlawful for any owner or custodian of any animal to permit the animal to live in conditions that cause suffering to the animal, or to directly cause any suffering of an animal.
(c)
Abandonment. It shall be unlawful for the owner or custodian of any animal to abandon such animal on any street, road, highway or public place, or on private property when not in the care of another person.
(d)
Unattended animals. It shall be unlawful for the owner or custodian of any animal to leave such animal in any location unattended for longer than 24 hours. It shall be unlawful to keep or board animals on property that is not residentially occupied or is otherwise commercial property that is the location of an operating business, such that the property is visited daily during the week and at least once on the weekend.
(e)
Accumulation of waste matter. It shall be unlawful for any owner or custodian of any animal to permit the accumulation of waste matter from animals to collect and remain on the property of the owner or custodian, or on the property of others so as to cause or create an unhealthy, unsanitary, dangerous or offensive living condition on the owner's or custodian's property, or to abutting property of others. It shall be unlawful to allow animal waste matter to run off into any river, stream, lake, pond or other watercourse in the City of Rossville, or to run off onto any other person's property.
(f)
Failure to provide adequate shelter. It shall be unlawful for any owner or custodian of any animal to fail to provide adequate shelter from the elements for that animal, as appropriate to the species. Providing adequate shelter requires providing sufficient food and water, disposing of waste, providing sufficient temperature control (whether heat, ventilation, or cooling) to prevent suffering by the animal, and not maintaining conditions conducive to disease and infection.
(g)
Unsanitary, dangerous or offensive conditions. It shall be unlawful for any owner or custodian of any animal to permit or cause unsanitary, dangerous or offensive conditions by virtue of the size or number of animals maintained at a single location or due to the inadequacy of the facilities.
(h)
Rabies tag. Dogs and cats shall wear current, valid rabies tags attached to their collars at all times, except when removal is necessary for cleaning, grooming or other veterinary care. It shall be unlawful for any owner or custodian of a dog or cat to allow the animal to be outdoors without wearing a collar with a current rabies tag showing that the animal has been inoculated for rabies within one year of any such time as the rabies tag may be checked.
(i)
Proper disposal. It shall be unlawful for any owner or custodian of any animal to fail to properly dispose of that animal upon its death. Proper disposal means in a method provided by the Georgia Code at O.C.G.A. § 4-5-5, and includes burning, incineration, burial or rendering of the carcass within 24 hours after death or discovery. Dead animals that are buried must be buried at least three feet below the ground level, have not less than three feet of earth over the carcass, and must not contaminate ground water or surface water.
(j)
Separate violation per animal. Each animal kept in violation of this section shall count as a separate violation.
(Ord. No. 517, § 1, 2-8-21)
Sec. 18-89. - Sick, injured or contagious animals.
(a)
Impoundment of sick or injured animals. In any situation deemed appropriate by the animal control officer involving a sick or injured animal whose owner cannot be located and the condition of such animal is such that death or great suffering to the animal is likely, the animal control officer may take custody of such animal and may secure veterinary services for such animal. The owner of such animal shall be responsible for reimbursement of all expenses related to treatment and boarding of the animal.
(b)
Destruction of sick or injured animals. Any sick or injured animal, deemed by a licensed veterinarian to be suffering to such an extent that the humane destruction of the animal is appropriate and the owner of the animal cannot be located, shall be humanely destroyed by the veterinarian in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 4-11-5.1.
(c)
Infected or contagious animals. Any animal impounded by the animal control officer that is, in the opinion of a licensed veterinarian, infected with a disease likely to cause death or great suffering to the animal, or which is a contagious disease, or which will likely spread to other animals, shall be isolated and the owner of such animal shall be notified. If the owner cannot be located or the owner fails to take immediate steps to remove the animal from impoundment, the infected animal shall be humanely destroyed in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 4-11-5.1.
(d)
Reimbursement. The owner of the animal shall be responsible for reimbursement to the city for all expenses involving the boarding, treatment and destruction of any such animal.
(e)
Notification of bites. It shall be the duty of any person bitten by a wild or domestic animal to immediately notify an animal control officer. It shall be the duty of the owner, custodian, or any person having possession and knowledge of any animal which has bitten any person or animal, or of any animal which exhibits any signs of rabies, to notify the animal control officer and to confine such animal in accordance with this article.
(f)
Impoundment and quarantine. The animal control officer shall be authorized to impound and quarantine dogs that have been bitten by wild animals or bitten by dogs or cats that do not have current records of vaccination against rabies. Management of animals exposed to rabies shall be according to the procedures set forth in the Georgia Rabies Control Manual, latest edition, which is incorporated herein by reference as if set forth fully herein. The protocols for livestock, dogs, cats and ferrets possibly exposed to rabies, as well as the protocols for animal to animal exposure and human exposure shall be enforced as set forth in the Georgia Rabies Control Manual, latest edition. The animal control officer shall be authorized to take any and all steps set forth in said manual up to and including euthanizing and testing of animals.
(g)
Animals impounded for rabies quarantine. Animals impounded for rabies quarantine shall be isolated pursuant to the procedures of the Georgia Rabies Control Manual, latest edition. If the animal shows signs of rabies or dies during confinement, it will be sent off for testing. If the animal survives and does not show signs of rabies, it will be returned to the owner upon payment of fees.
(h)
Quarantine/isolation procedures. In certain circumstances, the animal control officer may allow the animal owner to quarantine the animal that has been bitten in lieu of the animal being euthanized and tested. The quarantine period varies depending on the circumstance, as set forth in the Georgia Rabies Control Manual, latest edition. In that case, the animal must be placed in a secure enclosure where no other animal or person can be exposed to the virus which can be transmitted into bite wounds, open cuts in skin, and onto mucous membranes. The animal must be kept in a secure enclosure approved by the animal control department and when deemed necessary may require two layers of fencing, separated by at least one foot. Food must be pushed in from a distance, and the animal cannot be approached by any person. Other protocols for quarantine shall be provided by the animal control officer. If the animal escapes from the enclosure, it will be impounded and euthanized for testing.
(Ord. No. 517, § 1, 2-8-21)
Sec. 18-90. - Cruelty to animals.
(a)
Cruelty to animals. It shall be unlawful for any person to willfully or maliciously strike, beat, abuse, intentionally run down with a vehicle, or cause or inflict unnecessary pain, injury, suffering or death to an animal through any act, omission or willful neglect, except that reasonable force may be used to defend his or her own person or property, or the person or property of another, from injury or damage being caused by a dog; or kill any dog causing injury or damage to any livestock, poultry, or pet animal as authorized by Georgia law, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-5. "Willful neglect" means the intentional withholding of food and water required by an animal to prevent starvation or dehydration.
(b)
Poisoning. No person, except a licensed veterinarian for humanitarian purposes, shall intentionally administer poison to any animal or knowingly leave any poisonous substance of any kind or ground glass in any place with the intent to injure any animal. This provision is not applicable to licensed exterminators using poisons as part of a pest control program or the use of commercial insecticides and rodent baits used to control insects and wild rodents.
(c)
Investigation. The animal control officer shall investigate complaints pertaining to cruelty to animals. The animal control officer may request the assistance of the sheriff's department or the appropriate law enforcement officers of a participating municipality.
(d)
Necessary pain. In the case of activities where physical pain is necessarily caused, such as medical and scientific research, food processing, customary and normal veterinary and agricultural husbandry practices, pest elimination, and animal training and hunting, failure to employ the most humane method reasonably available shall constitute cruelty to animals and shall be unlawful.
(Ord. No. 517, § 1, 2-8-21)
Sec. 18-91. - Enforcement.
(a)
Authorization. Animal control officers and law enforcement officers of the city shall be enforcement officials for this article. These officials shall have the authority to investigating complaints, impound and destroying animals, issuing citations, and take other lawful action as required to enforce the provisions of this article. It shall be a violation of this article to interfere with any law enforcement officer in the performance of his duties.
(b)
Citations upon affidavit. Upon the sworn affidavit of any citizen alleging a violation of this article, an animal control officer or law enforcement officer may issue a citation to the owner of the animal requiring the owner to appear before the municipal court of the city for an adjudication of the complaint on a date and time certain.
(c)
Inspection warrants. Animal control officers may apply for search and seizure warrants, or inspection warrants when they reasonably believe a violation of this article is occurring. The officer shall apply to the municipal court only upon cause and with an affidavit particularly describing the place, dwelling, structure, premises, or vehicle to be inspected and the purpose for which the inspection is to be made. In addition, the affidavit shall contain either a statement that consent to inspect has been sought and refused or facts or circumstances reasonably justifying the failure to seek such consent. Cause shall be deemed to exist if there is reason to believe that a condition of nonconformity with the provisions of this article exists with respect to the particular place, dwelling, structure, premises or vehicle.
(d)
Violations. It shall be a violation of this article to:
(1)
Fail to comply with any provision of this article;
(2)
Fail to comply with any lawful order of an animal control officer or law enforcement officer unless such order is lawfully stayed or reversed; or
(3)
Fail to pay costs imposed by the animal control officer unless payment thereof is lawfully stayed.
(e)
Multiple animals. When multiple animals are in violation of this article or are maintained in circumstances that violate this article, each animal shall constitute a separate violation, or in the alternative, a higher fine shall be imposed on the single violation. In addition, multiple violations on the same animal shall result in a higher fine.
(f)
Penalties. Any person violating the terms of this article, or rules and regulations promulgated pursuant thereto, may be punished by civil penalty or by criminal citation and fine, not to exceed $1,000.00. Separate offenses shall be deemed to be committed on each day during or on which a violation occurs or continues. Each animal shall constitute a separate offense, and each violation of a specific provision of this article shall constitute a separate offense.
(Ord. No. 517, § 1, 2-8-21)
