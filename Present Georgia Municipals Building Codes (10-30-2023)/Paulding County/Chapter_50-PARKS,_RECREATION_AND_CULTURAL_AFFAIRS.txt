ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 50-1. - Department designation and creation of the position of director.
(a)
The department shall be designated the parks, recreation and cultural affairs department and shall consist of the parks system, recreation department and the library services system and such other divisions as the board of commissioners may designate in the future.
(b)
The Paulding County Parks, Recreation and Cultural Affairs Department shall be operated under the direction and control of the parks, recreation and cultural affairs director. The director shall oversee and generally supervise all property, playgrounds, play fields, parks and other recreational areas of the county on a daily basis, together with such staff and other personnel which may be appropriate to provide for the recreational needs for the citizens of the county. The director shall be the department head in charge of the Paulding County Parks, Recreation and Cultural Affairs Department. The director shall further be the department head in charge of library services in Paulding County. The director shall be under the general supervision of the governing authority of Paulding County as provided by local law.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-2. - Duties and powers of the director.
The Director of the Paulding County Parks and Recreation Department shall be nominated and appointed by the board of commissioners and shall serve at the pleasure of the board of commissioners as established by local law. The director shall administer and force the provisions of this chapter and shall further have the following powers and duties:
(1)
Develop a season of events;
(2)
Coordinate scheduled times for facility use;
(3)
Provide supervision for public use of swimming pools, tennis courts and recreational facilities;
(4)
Evaluate and submit man power requirements to the parks and recreation commission and the governing authority of Paulding County;
(5)
Supervise all personnel assigned to the recreation department;
(6)
Schedule the operation and stocking of all concession stands;
(7)
Provide and maintain records and receipts of expenditures;
(8)
Direct the maintenance of grounds and buildings;
(9)
Supervise, direct and control all employees;
(10)
Plan, supervise and coordinate all recreational programs for the county;
(11)
Prepare, with the commission, all reports required to be submitted under this chapter;
(12)
Adopt policies to provide for safety regulations at all recreational activities;
(13)
Supervise the library services department;
(14)
To work with a county library coordinator to ensure the efficient delivery of services in the libraries of Paulding County; and
(15)
Have such other powers and duties as may be necessary to effectuate all matters set forth herein, together with those that may be prescribed from time-to-time by the appropriate elected officials of the governing authority.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-3. - Use of roadways and parks.
All roadways and parks shall be open for the use of pedestrians, bicycle riders and the occupants of vehicles in use for pleasure drives. No trucks or other motorized vehicles engaged in hauling for traffic or commercial uses shall operate on the park roadways, except in the delivery of building materials or other supplies necessary for the maintenance of the parks.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-4. - Driving vehicles in parks.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate any motor vehicle in, over, through or across any part of any public park of the county except upon roadways specifically designated and maintained for motor vehicle travel. This provision shall not apply to recreation department employees whose duties require these vehicles to drive maintenance equipment through and over parks.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-5. - Hours of operation of the public parks.
(a)
The director of the parks and recreation department is authorized to establish the hours of operation for each park within the county park system and is authorized to revise the hours as is deemed necessary. The hours for each park shall be posted at each park site.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person, except a properly authorized employee of the recreation department, to be in or on any property of any property beyond the established park hours without permission from either the parks and recreation department, the parks and recreation commission, or the board of commissioners.
(c)
Subsection (b) shall not apply to thoroughfares that are a part of the system of streets and highways of the county.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person, except an authorized employee of the parks and recreation department, to enter any public park of the county, any portion of any public park of the county, or any area of a park of the county which is gated and locked, or entry is otherwise restricted to the general public (it is hereby recognized that some parks (or portions of parks) may be locked for security reasons at hours or time when the park is otherwise in operation).
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-6. - Destruction of public property.
It shall be unlawful for any individual to:
(1)
Intentionally damage or destroy public property including, but not limited to, cutting, removing, damaging or destroying turf, trees, saplings, seedlings, brush or shrubs, whether alive or dead, or chip, blaze, box, girdle, trim or otherwise deface or injure any tree or shrub or break or remove any branch, foliage, flower, of any tree or shrub, or pick, gather, uproot, remove or destroy any flower, plant, or grass.
(2)
Intentionally remove or cause to be removed any turf, sand, earth, humus, peat, rocks, boulders, or gravel.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-7. - Fires in county parks.
(a)
No person shall build, light, or maintain any open or outdoor fire at any place within any Paulding County Park, except in areas or facilities provided and designated for this purpose, unless prior written permission has been obtained from the board of commissioners, the recreation director or his designee.
(b)
Approved fires in designated areas shall not be left unattended. All fires shall be completely extinguished upon leaving the immediate vicinity.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-8. - Firearms and dangerous weapons.
Except as may be authorized by state or federal laws, no person shall have in his possession while on park property a revolver, pistol, shotgun, rifle, air rifle, air gun, paint ball gun, or any gun or bow or other weapons that discharge projectiles, either by air, explosive substance, or any other force. No person shall discharge or set off anywhere on park property any explosive, revolver, pistol, shotgun, rifle, airgun or any bow or other weapons that discharge projectiles, either by air, explosive substance, or any other force.
Provided however, the following shall be deemed exceptions to this section:
(1)
Any discharge of firearms in areas so designed and designated for the purpose of rifle and pistol range shooting;
(2)
The use of bow and arrow in areas designed and designated for the purpose of archery target practice.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-9. - Alcoholic beverages.
No alcoholic beverages shall be possessed or consumed upon the premises of any Paulding County park facility.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-10. - Operation of vehicles.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate any motor vehicle upon or across any part of any public park of the county except upon areas designated for vehicular travel. This section shall not apply to park employees, volunteers and contractors whose duties require them to drive park maintenance equipment over such parks as authorized by the director or his designee.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a motor vehicle upon any road within any park of Paulding County at a speed in excess of 15 miles per hour. It shall be the duty of the director of parks and recreation to cause signs specifying such limit to be placed at the entrance of each park and at appropriate places within such parks so as to afford notice to the public of said speed limit.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-11. - Protection of wildlife, birds.
(a)
No person shall hunt, kill, trap, harm, chase, shoot, or throw objects at any animal, reptile, or bird; nor shall any person have in his possession the young of any wild animal, or the eggs or nest, or young of any reptile or bird.
(b)
No person shall give or offer or attempt to give any animal, reptile or bird any tobacco, alcohol, poisons, or other known toxic substances.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-12. - Animal control.
(a)
No person shall bring any animal into any park or recreational facility except when such animal is under the direction and control of the owner and is secured by a leash, chain or lead of sufficient strength to prevent it from escaping its owner. However, the director of the parks and recreation department, with proper notice duly posted at the park and/or recreation facility, shall have the authority to designate any park and/or recreation facility in its entirety, or any portion of any park or recreational facility, as an area in which animals may be allowed to walk without a leash, chain or lead of sufficient strength. As used in this section, "animal" shall include any type of animal including, but not limited to, dogs, cats and horses.
(b)
Representatives of the animal control department, county marshal's office and officers of the county sheriff's department are hereby authorized to issue a summons and citation to any person for violation of this section.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Secs. 50-13—50-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - PARKS AND RECREATION COMMISSION

Sec. 50-31. - Established, appointment, terms.
Pursuant to the authority of O.C.G.A. § 36-64-5, there is hereby created and established in the unincorporated area of Paulding County, a commission to be designated as the Paulding County Parks and Recreation Commission.
The members of this commission shall be appointed by the board of commissioners in the same manner as all boards and commissions are appointed by the county governing authority. The members of the commission shall serve without pay.
The terms of office for members on the commission shall be for five years or until their successors are appointed and qualified. Vacancies on the commission occurring other than by expiration of the term shall be filled by another appointment for the unexpired term in the same manner as other appointments are made by the county governing authority.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-32. - Recreation commission membership.
The commission shall consist of a total of nine members who shall be appointed by the governing authority of the county in the same manner as other appointments are made to boards and commissions.
Commission members shall be residents of the county and should be appointed based upon geographic diversity from the county. Each post commissioner is encouraged to recommend members to the chairman for nomination. The chairman shall make nominations to be voted on and confirmed by the board of commissioners.
All appointments to the Paulding County Parks and Recreation Commission shall be finalized no later than the first meeting of February of each year.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-33. - Recreation commission organization.
The parks and recreation commission members shall be authorized to elect one of their members as chairperson, one as vice-chair and one as secretary and such other officers as may be necessary to carry about its mission. The commission shall further be authorized and empowered to adopt by-laws, rules and regulations for the proper conduct for its meetings and the delivery of recreational services to the residents of the county which authority shall not be inconsistent with this chapter or state law.
The parks and recreation commission does not have recommending authority as to the library services system. The library services system utilizes its board of trustees pursuant to chapter 38 of this Code.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-34. - Parks and recreation commission authority.
The parks and recreation commission shall:
(1)
Provide, conduct and supervise all public playgrounds, play fields, indoor recreation centers and other recreational facilities owned, leased or under the direction and control of Paulding County;
(2)
Have the power to conduct any form of recreational activity or cultural activity which will employ the leisure time of the people of Paulding County in a wholesome or constructive manner;
(3)
Conduct such activities on properties which are under its own control, on public properties with the consent of the authorities thereof or on private properties with the owners;
(4)
Have the policy-making authority concerning all recreational activity within Paulding County, to the extent authorized under the chapter;
(5)
Have the duty to govern all recreational activities as outlined in this chapter.
The authority of the commission herein to make decisions as provided for above are subject only to the power and authority of the Paulding County Board of Commissioners, the governing authority of Paulding County, to veto or nullify any action which it deems is not in the best interests of the citizens of the county.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-35. - Parks and recreation commission personnel.
The Paulding County Parks and Recreation Commission shall have the power and authority to advise and recommend to the board of commissioners a person to act as a director of the Paulding County Parks and Recreation Department. Such director should be trained and properly qualified for the work. The board of commissioners shall not be obligated to follow such recommendation, but may consider the submission with all other pertinent matters which may come before it.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-36. - Parks and recreation commission finances.
The parks and recreation commission, in conjunction with the director, shall submit a proposed budget to the county governing authority for its review for operation of the park and recreational needs of Paulding County for the upcoming fiscal year. Any fees and revenues which the commission may receive as a result of the operation of recreational facilities and programs shall be submitted to the county treasury for deposit into the general budget. Any devise or bequest of money or other real or personal property to the commission shall be applied to the county treasury to be credited to the budget of the parks and recreation department for the temporary or permanent uses of providing for the recreational needs of residents of the county.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-37. - Parks and recreation commission reports.
The parks and recreation commission shall prepare and make an annual report to the county governing authority, together with such other reports as, from time-to-time, may be requested by the governing authority.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-38. - Parks and recreation commission spending limits.
In providing for the recreational needs for the citizens of Paulding County, the parks and recreation commission shall be authorized to request the expenditure of monies appropriated by the governing authority within the commissions (budget subject to the published county purchasing procedures and subject to the spending limits of the county. The commission is authorized to request the governing authority of Paulding County to appropriate such other monies as the commission may deem necessary for the recreational needs of the county.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Secs. 50-39—50-60. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - MULTI-USE TRAILS

Sec. 50-61. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Multi-use trail means any trail or path designated by the board of commissioners for pleasure or for use as an alternative mode of transportation and includes uses for walking, jogging, running, bicycling or horseback riding. Provided, however, the term "multi-use trail" shall not include the use of the multi-use trail by any motorized vehicle.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-4.
Sec. 50-62. - Part of parks system.
All multi-use trails located within the unincorporated areas of the county shall be designated as a part of the county parks system.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-63. - Operation of motorized vehicles.
No person shall operate a motorized vehicle, in, over, through or upon any multi-use trail except when specifically authorized by law.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Cross reference— Traffic and motor vehicles, ch. 70.
Sec. 50-64. - Riding horses.
No person shall ride a horse on a multi-use trail, except in the area designated for such use, nor in a reckless manner, or in any other manner which endangers other users of the trail.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-65. - Operation of bicycles.
No person shall operate a bicycle on a multi-use trail in a reckless manner or in any other manner which endangers other users of the trail.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-66. - Animals.
No person shall bring any animal on a multi-use trail except when such animal is under the direction and control of its owner and is secured by a leash, chain, or lead of sufficient strength to prevent it from escaping from its owner. As used in this section, the term "animal" shall include any type of animal, including, but not limited to, dogs, cats and horses. Representatives of the animal control department, county marshal's office and officers of the county sheriff's department are hereby authorized to issue a summons and citation to any person for violation of this section.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Cross reference— Animals, ch. 14.
Sec. 50-67. - Climbing bridges, trestles, etc.
No person shall climb on the exterior of, repel from or jump from any bridge, trestle, overpass, wall, cliff, embankment, building or monument which is located on or adjacent to a multi-use trail.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 50-68. - Penalty for violations.
Any person who violates any provision of this chapter shall be subject to summons and citation and punished as provided for in the general penalties section of the Code of Ordinances for Paulding County, Georgia, chapter 1.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 2 (Exh. B), 11-13-2018)
