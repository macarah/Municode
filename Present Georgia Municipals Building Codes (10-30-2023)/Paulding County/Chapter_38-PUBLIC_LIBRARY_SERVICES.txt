ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 38-1. - Purpose.
The object shall be the furnishing of library services to the people of the area under the terms and regulations set forth by the state governing public libraries affiliated with the state department of education.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 1 (Exh. A), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 38-2. - Division of parks, recreation and cultural affairs.
The Paulding County Library Services System shall be a division of the parks, recreation and cultural affairs department of the county pursuant to Chapter 50 of the Code of Ordinances. The director of the parks, recreation and cultural affairs department shall supervise the library system as the director with the assistance of a library services coordinator.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 1 (Exh. A), 11-13-2018)
Secs. 38-3—38-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - BOARD OF TRUSTEES[2]

--- (2) ---

Sec. 38-31. - Composition and purpose.
The board of trustees shall be comprised of not more than nine members. The purpose of the board of trustees is to act as a recommending body to the Paulding County Board of Commissioners concerning library related issues.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 1 (Exh. A), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 38-32. - Appointment.
Members of the board of trustees shall be appointed by the supporting tax agencies.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 1 (Exh. A), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 38-33. - Terms.
The appointments of members of the board of trustees shall be for terms of three years' duration; provided the initial members shall be appointed in such manner as to provide equally staggered terms. The terms of board members shall be concurrent with the fiscal year of the library and county. All appointments, except appointments to fill vacancies, shall be for terms beginning on July 1 of the year of appointment.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 1 (Exh. A), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 38-34. - Vacancies.
Vacancies occurring among members of the board of trustees shall be filled by appointment as set forth in the bylaws.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 1 (Exh. A), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 38-35. - Officers.
(a)
Officers of the board of trustees shall be a chair, a vice-chair, a secretary, and a treasurer.
(b)
The officers shall be elected at the last meeting of the fiscal year to serve for a period of one year. They may be reelected by vote of the board of trustees.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 1 (Exh. A), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 38-36. - Regular meetings.
The board of trustees shall meet four times a year.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 1 (Exh. A), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 38-37. - Quorum.
A quorum shall constitute a majority of those members of the board of trustees present and voting provided at least four are present.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 1 (Exh. A), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 38-38. - Special meetings.
Special meetings for special purposes may be called as set forth in the bylaws.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 1 (Exh. A), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 38-39. - Amendments.
Any proposed amendment to this article may be submitted in writing at any regular meeting of the board of trustees. Such proposed amendment must be signed by two active members of the board of trustees before being submitted to the meeting and shall be read to the meeting by the secretary. After reading the proposed amendment (or amendments) to this article at such meeting, the secretary shall mail to each member of the board of trustees a copy of the proposed change or changes as submitted and read. After such notice by the secretary, the proposed amendment to the constitution or bylaws shall be voted on at the next meeting and shall become a part of this article if approved by a majority of the members present and voting.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 1 (Exh. A), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 38-40. - Meetings.
(a)
The board of trustees shall meet at the Paulding County Library (unless otherwise designated) in January, April, July, and October on such dates and times as the board may direct.
(b)
Special meetings of the board of trustees for special purposes may be called, provided that the members have been notified three days in advance.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 1 (Exh. A), 11-13-2018)
Sec. 38-41. - History of library.
The Library Services Coordinator of the Paulding County Public Library Services System is directed to establish and maintain a history of the library services system with special emphasis on those citizens of this county who have contributed to the growth and development of the library services system through their efforts as members of the board of trustees. The history of the library may be maintained in electronic form or through bound albums.
(Res. No. 18-55, § 1 (Exh. A), 11-13-2018)
