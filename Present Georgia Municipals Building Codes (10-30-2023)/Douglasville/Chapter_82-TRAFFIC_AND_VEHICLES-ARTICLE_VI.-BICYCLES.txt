ARTICLE VI. - BICYCLES

Sec. 82-181. - Rules of road apply to bicycles.
All rules of the road that apply to motor vehicles in the city shall also apply to the operation and riding of bicycles unless otherwise provided in this chapter.
(Code 1977, § 24-71)
Sec. 82-182. - Manner of riding.
Riders of bicycles shall not ride with both hands off the handlebars or coast, when about to meet or pass any other person or vehicle, and they shall at all times have full control of their mounts.
(Code 1977, § 24-72)
Sec. 82-183. - Riding on sidewalks.
No person shall ride a bicycle upon a sidewalk within any business district in the city, and no person more than 14 years of age shall ride a bicycle upon any sidewalk in any district. Whenever any person is riding a bicycle upon a sidewalk, such person shall yield the right-of-way to any pedestrian and shall give an audible signal before overtaking and passing such pedestrian.
(Code 1977, § 24-76)
Sec. 82-184. - Acrobatic, fancy riding, racing prohibited.
No rider of any bicycle shall remove both hands from the handlebars, or both feet from the pedal, or practice any acrobatic or fancy riding on any sidewalk or any street; nor shall any person operating a bicycle upon a street or sidewalk participate in any race of speed or endurance in a contest with any other bicycle or vehicle.
(Code 1977, § 24-77)
Sec. 82-185. - Riding more than two abreast prohibited.
No more than two bicycles or other similar vehicle shall be ridden abreast on any street or sidewalk in the city.
(Code 1977, § 24-78)
Sec. 82-186. - Lights.
Every bicycle when in use at nighttime shall be equipped with a light on the front which shall emit a white light visible from a distance of 300 feet to the front and with a red reflector on the rear of a type approved by the state department of public safety which shall be visible from a distance of 300 feet to the rear when directly in front of lawful upper beams of headlights on a motor vehicle. A light emitting a red light visible from a distance of 300 feet to the rear may be used in addition to the red reflector.
(Code 1977, § 24-79)
