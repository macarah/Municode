Sec. 30-1. - Purpose.
The City of Douglasville Charter authorizes establishment of procedures for determining and proclaiming that an emergency situation or unusual occurrence exists within or outside the city and for making and carrying out all reasonable provisions deemed necessary to deal with or meet such emergencies for the protection, safety, health, and well-being of the citizens of the city. The city's operational response to emergencies and unusual occurrences shall be tailored to the unique requirements of any emergency situation, and shall be consistent and compatible with the emergency operation plan (EOP) developed by the Douglasville-Douglas County Emergency Management Agency. The purpose of this chapter 30 shall be to set forth standards and procedures supplemental to the EOP, and to establish administrative and operational measures the City of Douglasville should take in developing plans and preparing to respond effectively to an unusual occurrence. These provisions shall place responsibility with specific personnel, units, or positions within the City of Douglasville.
( Ord. No. O-2015-15, § 1(Exh. A) , 4-20-15)
Sec. 30-2. - Other sources of law.
These provisions are to be utilized by employees of the City of Douglasville as a guide during local emergencies. These provisions are not meant to be "standards" as contemplated by O.C.G.A. Title 38, chapter 3. These provisions are supplemental to the EOP developed by the Douglasville-Douglas County Emergency Management Agency, and in the event of any conflict, the provisions of O.C.G.A. Title 38 Chapter 3 and the EOP shall supersede the provisions of this chapter 30.
( Ord. No. O-2015-15, § 1(Exh. A) , 4-20-15)
Sec. 30-3. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Emergency means a federal emergency, a state emergency, or a local emergency.
Emergency command center means the central location from which any local emergency will be managed by city personnel.
Emergency management center means the location from which the location where Douglas County Emergency Management personnel will manage any state emergency or federal emergency.
Emergency operation center means a stationary or mobile emergency operations center (EOC) established at or near the location of any site-specific local emergency, where city personnel will maintain a perimeter and see to the immediate needs of persons in the area of the emergency site.
Federal emergency means a state of emergency declared by the President of the United States or other authorized federal official.
Local emergency or unusual occurrence means a state of emergency declared by the city's mayor or other authorized city official, and lasting until officially terminated; a local emergency may be declared and in effect either during or independent from a state emergency or a federal emergency.
Mobilize means assigned to emergency work as instructed by a superior employee or the mayor during a declared local emergency, which may include work on days and at times of day other than the employee's normal schedule; mobilizing an employee does not cause his travel time to the work site from home, or to home from a work site, to be compensable.
State emergency means "state of emergency" as defined by O.C.G.A. subsection 38-3-3(7).
( Ord. No. O-2015-15, § 1(Exh. A) , 4-20-15)
Sec. 30-4. - Conditions warranting a local emergency declaration.
The mayor may declare a local emergency whenever: (a) a federal emergency is in effect, (b) a state emergency is in effect, (c) whenever in his or her judgment, the imminent threat or actual occurrence of a disaster is of sufficient severity or magnitude to warrant extraordinary local government measures to prevent or alleviate the damage, loss, hardship or suffering threatened or caused thereby. By way of example, the these conditions include energy emergencies, disasters, or the imminent threat thereof, of manmade or natural origin caused by enemy attack, sabotage, acts of domestic or international terrorism, civil disturbance, fire, flood, earthquake, wind, storm, wave action, oil spill or other water contamination requiring emergency action to avert danger or damage, epidemic, air contamination, blight, drought, infestation, explosion, riot or other hostile action, radiological action, or other causes.
( Ord. No. O-2015-15, § 1(Exh. A) , 4-20-15)
Sec. 30-5. - Initiation of a local emergency declaration.
(a)
Declarations initiated by other than the police department. The mayor may initiate declaration of a local emergency, without assistance from other personnel, whenever he has information regarding an existing or threatened local disaster from the Douglas County Director of Emergency Management or from any other credible and reliable source. Whenever the mayor declares a local emergency not requested by the police department, the mayor shall have a member of the administration department contact the city manager and the police chief or the ranking officer of the police department command staff within 20 minutes of the declaration, after which the city manager shall designate the appropriate level of the local emergency.
(b)
Declarations initiated by the police department. The declaration of a local emergency by the mayor may be requested by the highest ranking police officer who is of a rank not lower than sergeant, and who is personally at the scene of any potential local emergency. Such officer shall immediately inform the police shift commander of the relevant facts constituting the emergency; the shift commander shall contact the police chief or the ranking officer of the police department command staff, who shall confirm or terminate the initiation within 20 minutes of its receipt. After confirmation of any request for a declaration of local emergency, a member of the police department administrative staff shall contact the mayor and city manager and deliver all pertinent information. The city manager shall designate the level of emergency and then contact all persons required by subsection 30-9(a) to report to the emergency command center (ECC), and inform the department heads for any city departments to be mobilized at the declared level of the emergency. Call-out of employees to be mobilized shall be the responsibility of the respective department heads. When an emergency has been declared, it is the responsibility of the city manager to assure that proper operational procedures are followed and coordinated with any assistance and direction from agencies outside city government.
( Ord. No. O-2015-15, § 1(Exh. A) , 4-20-15)
Sec. 30-6. - Designation of emergency level.
The city manager shall designate one of three levels for any declared local emergency, and the designated level may be revised prior to termination of the emergency:
(1)
Level 1 emergencies. At this level the police department, maintenance and sanitation department, and the communications department shall be mobilized. The city manager shall additionally contact Douglas County Fire Department and request a representative to participate. A level 1 emergency is the least pervasive emergency level.
(2)
Level 2 emergencies. At this level the police department, maintenance and sanitation department, development services division of the community and development services department, the information technology division of the community and development services department, the communications division of the community and development services department, the finance department, and the parks and recreation department shall be mobilized. The city manager shall contact the Douglas County Fire Department and request a representative to participate. A level 2 emergency declaration reflects a mid-level pervasive emergency level.
(3)
Level 3 emergencies. At this level the police department, maintenance and sanitation department, development services division of the community and development services department, the information technology division of the community and development services department, the communications division of the community and development services department, the finance department, the parks and recreation department, and the legal division of the administration department shall be mobilized. The city manager shall contact the Douglasville - Douglas County Water and Sewer Authority, the appropriate electric power utilities, and the Douglas County Fire Department and request a representative to participate. A level 3 emergency is the most pervasive emergency level.
( Ord. No. O-2015-15, § 1(Exh. A) , 4-20-15)
Sec. 30-7. - Intergovernmental contacts and cooperation.
Anytime a local emergency is declared, the city manager or his designee will contact the director of emergency management for Douglas County. Other agencies, such as hospitals, other law enforcement agencies, or volunteers shall be contacted and mobilized as appropriate and as determined by the city manager or the mayor. Use of the city's emergency warning system during any local emergency shall proceed in compliance with the written Douglas County E-911 Severe Weather Warning System Alert Policy, a copy of which is maintained by the city clerk.
( Ord. No. O-2015-15, § 1(Exh. A) , 4-20-15)
Sec. 30-8. - Intradepartmental contacts.
Each city department head and division head designated to report to the ECC in subsection 30-9(a) shall keep a list of subordinate employees in his department or division in his or her possession at all times, which list shall include the listed employees' home telephone numbers and other off-duty contact information. After declaration of a local emergency and determination of the emergency level, the department heads for each mobilized department shall promptly contact every department employee determined to be necessary by the department head, whether the emergency occurs during or after normal business hours. All contacted employees for a mobilized department shall report to their normal base locations to be issued equipment to be utilized during the local emergency and to receive initial instructions. After receiving equipment and instructions, mobilized employees will report to their assigned areas. City personnel who are not mobilized shall continue their normal workday activities unless otherwise directed by their department heads or the city manager. Each department head will set the number of employees to be summoned, based on the situation. Only full-time city employees shall be mobilized during local emergencies, unless otherwise directed by the city manager or the mayor.
( Ord. No. O-2015-15, § 1(Exh. A) , 4-20-15)
Sec. 30-9. - Emergency command center.
(a)
Location; persons to report. The emergency command center is the Douglasville Police and Municipal Court building located at 2083 Fairburn Rd. All of the following city personnel shall report initially to the emergency command center (ECC) during every local emergency:
Mayor
All city council members
City manager or assistant city manager
City attorney chief assistant or city attorney
Police chief or deputy police chief
Maintenance and sanitation director
Building official
IT director
Communications director
Finance director
Parks and recreation director
Executive assistant to the mayor
Executive assistant to the city manager
After initial reporting, personnel at the ECC may be released or assigned to other locations by the mayor or the city manager. The mayor and the city manager shall conduct information releases to the community and the media on a timely basis, either from the ECC or through authorized individual releases of information to the press and others at an established designated remote location. The information may include an update on the emergency situation, casualty information and other information as deemed appropriate by the mayor and city manager. No city employee shall give out information on behalf of the city concerning the local emergency without the express permission of the mayor or the city manager. City employees should direct inquires from members of the media to the ECC or to any designated remote location for release of information.
(b)
Communications.
(1)
Executive assistants. The executive assistants for both the mayor and the city manager shall assist the police department receptionist with answering and transferring phone calls to and from the ECC, shall direct callers to the appropriate locations for assistance, and shall update the mayor and the city manager on information as it is received. The executive assistants will keep a log of all phone calls taken, to insure all the needs of the community are met.
(2)
Duties of the communications director. The communications director shall be the city's liaison with the news media, and shall pass information to the mayor, councilmembers, and city manager for information received from the media. The communications director will continually update the community via City of Douglasville CitiTv, social media, and the city website as instructed by the mayor, council and city manager.
(3)
Emergency calls from citizens. During a declared local emergency, city employees shall direct incoming calls for assistance that are associated with the emergency to the ECC, except for life-threatening calls which shall be directed to E911 personnel dispatching appropriate first responder assistance, and then send such calls to the ECC.
( Ord. No. O-2015-15, § 1(Exh. A) , 4-20-15)
Sec. 30-10. - Emergency management center.
In the event of a state emergency with local impact, the assistance of the Georgia Emergency Management Administration (GEMA) may be needed. For such state emergencies, Douglas County will establish an emergency management center, and the police chief will send a representative from the police command staff to coordinate with staff from GEMA and Douglas County Emergency Management. This city employee will act as the liaisons regarding deployment of city assets, as well as keeping the communications open with the mayor and the city manager.
( Ord. No. O-2015-15, § 1(Exh. A) , 4-20-15)
Sec. 30-11. - Emergency operations center.
In addition to the ECC, a stationary or mobile emergency operations center (EOC) will be established at or near the location of any site-specific local emergency. The EOC shall be equipped so as to receive and dispatch calls, and so as to be able to communicate with the police department, the maintenance and sanitation department, and the Douglas County Fire Department. During local emergency operations, the EOC will be staffed only by essential personnel as assigned by the mayor or the city manager, and may include department heads, a representative from the state patrol, a representative from each utility company, and others. Personnel who are not essential to on-site management of the emergency will be assigned or re-assigned to a location other than the EOC by the mayor or the city manager.
( Ord. No. O-2015-15, § 1(Exh. A) , 4-20-15)
Sec. 30-12. - Radio communications among emergency personnel.
(a)
City radio system. In the normal course of business, city employees who use radios include employees of the police department, the maintenance and sanitation department, the development services division of the community and development services department, the information technology division of the community and development services department, the communications division of the community and development services department, the finance department, and the parks and recreation department, plus the mayor and the city manager. During a local emergency, the police department will use dispatch, traffic, patrol and investigations talk groups for non-emergency radio traffic. Tactical talk groups will be assigned by the police chief as the situation warrants, with one talk group assigned as an administrative talk group. Mutual talk groups such as events, 911 or park security, will be utilized for communications for system-wide announcements. The 911 talk group will be utilized only for communications regarding life-threatening matters. All police employees at the rank of captain or above will monitor the 911 talk group for any emergency communications. To promote clarity of communications with non-police individuals, for radio traffic on tactical talk groups during a local emergency, city employees shall use "plain talk" language, and shall avoid use of the "10-code" language.
(b)
Other radio systems. Communications of limited duration between users of the 800 MHz radio system may be patched through to other agencies with the assistance of E-911 using GIN/Motorbridge system, if authorized by the police chief during any local emergency. Use of other radio systems, such as the metro Atlanta UASI network, may occur during a local emergency and may be requested by any police employee at the ranks of captain or above, and may be authorized by the police chief.
( Ord. No. O-2015-15, § 1(Exh. A) , 4-20-15)
Sec. 30-13. - Transportation of citizens.
Transporting people from the emergency site to safe areas or relocation centers where family members will reunite will be done by mobilized city employees per the Douglas County Transportation plan Emergency Support Function #1 a copy of which is maintained by the city clerk.
( Ord. No. O-2015-15, § 1(Exh. A) , 4-20-15)
Sec. 30-14. - Traffic control and law enforcement.
The Douglasville Police Department has the overall responsibility for traffic control and law enforcement during local emergencies. The police chief shall instruct and direct the traffic control and law enforcement operations within the city limits during any local emergency.
( Ord. No. O-2015-15, § 1(Exh. A) , 4-20-15)
Sec. 30-15. - Personnel distribution during local emergencies.
Mobilized employees other than police will report initially to their daily work staging area to be issued instructions. On-duty police employees will report initially to the locations directed by their supervisors. Mobilized off-duty police employees shall be notified by police staff of any local emergency, and upon reporting initially shall contact the officer in charge of the local emergency for directions and instructions as needed. Mobilized employees shall remain at their assigned posts until excused or ordered to relocate by the unit supervisor, department head, or the mayor or city manager. Areas designated for various purposes may be modified during any local emergency, and personnel may be assigned to other locations.
( Ord. No. O-2015-15, § 1(Exh. A) , 4-20-15)
Sec. 30-16. - Control of the emergency area.
After any site-specific local emergency has been declared, the maintenance and sanitation director shall arrange the delivery of traffic barriers and any other useful maintenance equipment to secure the site, and the mayor or the city manager shall direct establishment of a perimeter. After establishment of the perimeter, only residents within the perimeter, their immediate family members, and those who have a business interest in the area will be allowed to enter the perimeter, and only if deemed reasonably safe. Authorization cards, obtainable by residents of the area at the EOC will be issued to non-emergency vehicles allowed to enter the perimeter and shall be distributed only at the time of the local emergency and immediately afterward. Authorized drivers within the perimeter must display a card on the dash of the vehicle while the vehicle is within the perimeter.
( Ord. No. O-2015-15, § 1(Exh. A) , 4-20-15)
Sec. 30-17. - Special emergency business license.
(a)
Generally. Authorization cards, obtainable at city hall will be issued to licensed repair personnel who have been called to the area to make emergency repairs to buildings. Persons other than residents of the emergency site, government staff and health care professionals may enter only with a valid authorization card. Personnel from the utility companies and private repair personnel who have been called to the area for a specific purpose by a specific resident or business in the area shall be excluded unless they possess an authorization card. Unlicensed repair personnel who have not been requested to come to the area shall be directed to the EOC for instructions and information about obtaining a business license and an authorization card. These restrictions will remain in effect until the emergency is terminated by expiration or by order of the mayor, or city manager.
(b)
Application. When registering, any person, partnership, corporation or other entity making application must, under oath, complete an application, providing the following information:
(1)
Name of applicant;
(2)
Permanent address and phone number of applicant;
(3)
Applicant's Social Security number or federal employer identification number;
(4)
If applicant is a corporation, the state and date of incorporation;
(5)
Tag registration information for each vehicle to be used in the business;
(6)
List of cities and/or counties where the applicant has conducted business within the past 12 months;
(7)
Georgia sales tax number or authorization;
(8)
Georgia business license number, if required.
(c)
Gouging prohibited.
(1)
Generally. To preserve, protect or sustain the life, health or safety of persons or property within a designated area upon the declaration of emergency or disaster, it shall be unlawful, during the duration of the emergency or subsequent recovery period, for any person, firm or corporation located or doing business within the city limits of Douglasville too overcharge for any goods, materials, foods, equipment, supplies, services, labor, motel rooms, temporary lodging or houses sold or rented within Douglasville.
(2)
Defined. Overcharging means charging prices for goods, materials, foods, equipment, supplies, services, labor, motel rooms, temporary lodging or houses, which exceed the customary charges by at least 25 percent or, in applicable cases, which exceed by at least 25 percent the suppliers' or providers' costs for such goods, materials, foods, equipment, supplies, services, labor, motel rooms, temporary lodging or houses. The existence of overcharging shall be presumed from a 25 percent or greater increase in the price at which the merchandise or rate, fee or cost/rental of housing was offered in the usual course of business immediately prior to the onset of the emergency or disaster, but shall not include increases in cost to the supplier directly attributable to the higher cost of material, supplies and labor costs resulting from the emergency.
( Ord. No. O-2015-15, § 1(Exh. A) , 4-20-15)
Sec. 30-18. - Agreements with other jurisdictions.
The City of Douglasville may enter into agreements with other jurisdictions to send or receive help, if needed, during emergency operations. It is the responsibility of the mayor, city manager or their designee, to request assistance from the various organizations. A list of contact people at such organizations shall be maintained at the ECC and the police department, and this list shall be updated periodically by the police department.
( Ord. No. O-2015-15, § 1(Exh. A) , 4-20-15)
Sec. 30-19. - Conforming to NIMS and ICS requirements.
The City of Douglasville shall implement its civil emergencies ordinance in conformity with the requirements of the federal government's National Incident Management System (NIMS) and Incident Command System (ICS). The city shall ensure that individuals in the employee positions identified in NIMS and ICS for basic level instruction and certification shall complete said training in accordance with NIMS and ICS guidelines and deadlines.
( Ord. No. O-2015-15, § 1(Exh. A) , 4-20-15)
Sec. 30-20. - Post-occurrence.
(a)
Unsafe building abatement. As soon as feasible after any local emergency, the building official of the City of Douglasville shall inspect all affected structures, buildings, residences, and living quarters within the city, to determine if the structures are safe to be occupied. When any structure is found to be unsafe, the city implement abatement procedures, and the police department shall maintain a list of unsafe structures. The police department shall regularly patrol newly condemned structures to prevent looting and unauthorized entry into the condemned structures.
(b)
Discussion meeting. Within one week after termination of any unusual occurrence, the city manager shall call a meeting of the city departments that were mobilized for the unusual occurrence, and discuss personnel performance, improvement of service delivery and related issues.
(c)
Annual review. The city manager shall periodically review annually the city's civil ordinance to assure that the plan remains current and within federal and state guidelines for emergency operations, and suggest any needed changes to assure clarity, efficacy and efficiency. Reviews should be conducted with input from city department heads, and reports, written or verbal, shall be submitted to the mayor for review.
( Ord. No. O-2015-15, § 1(Exh. A) , 4-20-15)
