ARTICLE VIII. - ADULT GAME ROOMS AND BILLIARDS ROOMS

Sec. 54-276. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Billiards means any of the several games played on a table surrounded by an elastic ledge or cushions with balls which are impelled by a cue and includes all forms of the game known as pool, except that form of pool known as skill pool heretofore defined as one of the several types of pinball games.
Billiards room means any public place wherein the game of billiards is permitted to be played and where there are two or more tables for such use.
Game room means any place of business that operates two or more mechanical games or pay devices or tables for which charge is made either directly or indirectly.
(Code 1981, § 14-151; Ord. No. 2008-8, § 28.01, 3-1-2004)
Sec. 54-277. - Penalty.
Any person operating premises in violation of the provisions of section 54-281 or 54-282 shall be guilty of a misdemeanor. Two or more violations within a calendar year period shall constitute valid ground for revocation of license. In addition to fines set by the municipal judge of the municipal court of city.
(Code 1981, § 14-158; Ord. No. 2008-8, § 28.08, 3-1-2004)
Sec. 54-278. - License.
(a)
Every operator of a game room shall apply for and obtain a license from the city council to operate a game room. Application for such a license shall be made upon forms provided by the city clerk.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to operate a game room or billiard rooms within the city without a license as required by this section.
(Code 1981, § 14-152; Ord. No. 2008-8, § 28.02, 3-1-2004)
Sec. 54-279. - Fee.
An application fee shall be paid to and collected by the city clerk when the application is submitted to cover the cost of administration of this article.
(Code 1981, § 14-153; Ord. No. 2008-8, § 28.03, 3-1-2004)
Sec. 54-280. - Restrictions.
The city council shall not issue a license to any applicant who:
(1)
Has been convicted of unlawfully selling intoxicating liquors or narcotic drugs;
(2)
Is a habitual user of alcoholic beverages or narcotic drugs;
(3)
Has been convicted of any felony crimes.
(Code 1981, § 14-154; Ord. No. 2008-8, § 28.04, 3-1-2004)
Sec. 54-281. - Prohibited conduct.
Licensees under this article shall not, and neither shall their employees:
(1)
Suffer or permit any gambling on the licensed premises at any time, nor the sale or use of any racing, football, or other parlay cards or gambling boards or devices;
(2)
Suffer or permit the licensed premises to become disorderly or permit any profane, obscene or indecent language thereon;
(3)
Continue to employ any person who has been convicted of unlawfully selling alcoholic beverages or narcotic drugs during the time in which such person been employed with the business.
(Code 1981, § 14-155; Ord. No. 2008-8, § 28.05, 3-1-2004)
Sec. 54-282. - Rules for operation of game rooms.
The following rules shall be observed by all operators of game rooms within the city:
(1)
All game rooms shall observe the hours of operation as set by the city council and shall be subject to changes in hours of operation as set by city council. The current hours of operation are Eastern Standard Time and as follows:
a.
Opening time: 7:30 a.m. (7:00 p.m. on Sunday);
b.
Closing time: 12:00 a.m. Monday through Saturday.
(2)
No play on any game shall be allowed during the times when game rooms are required by this section to remain closed.
(3)
All buildings shall conform to the state and city building code, and the operator shall acquire a certificate of compliance before operation shall begin.
(4)
No loud offensive noises shall be allowed to emanate beyond the licensed premises.
(5)
There must be an adult 18 years of age or older managing the business during hours of operation.
(6)
No alcoholic beverages including beer and wine shall be consumed on the premises of a game room or billiards room.
(7)
No person under the age of 18 years is permitted on premises of a game room or billiards room before 4:00 p.m. on any day the public or private school's are in session, unless such person is accompanied by his or her parent or legal guardian.
(8)
No license or owner of any game room or billiards room or any servant, agent, or employee of such a licensee or owner, shall permit upon the premises housing a mechanical amusement device arcade any of the following:
a.
Disorderly persons or minors;
b.
Intoxicated people or minors to loiter on the premises;
c.
Loitering signs must be posted outside premises and security lights must be on at dusk to closing time.
(9)
No licensee shall allow the licensed premises to become overcrowded so as to constitute a hazard to the health or safety of persons therein or to be in violation of any of the city's or state's fire, health or sanitation codes.
(10)
No game room or billiard room shall be operated so as to constitute a public nuisance.
(Code 1981, § 14-156; Ord. No. 2008-8, § 28.06, 3-1-2004)
Sec. 54-283. - Revocation of license.
(a)
After giving the operator or owner of a game room adequate notice and an opportunity to be heard, as provided in this section, the city council may revoke the license of any game room or billiard room operator who:
(1)
Violates the provisions of sections 54-281 or 54-282;
(2)
Is convicted of unlawfully selling alcoholic beverages or narcotic drugs; or
(3)
Is convicted of a felony crime.
(b)
Adequate notice shall mean the mailing of a certified letter, return receipt requested, no less than 15 days before the hearing date, or personal service upon such operator at the place of business or residence no less than 12 days before the hearing. The hearing shall be at any regular or special meeting of the city council.
(Code 1981, § 14-157; Ord. No. 2008-8, § 28.07, 3-1-2004)
