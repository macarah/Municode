ARTICLE V. - PAWNBROKERS AND DEALERS IN PRECIOUS METALS[3]

State Law reference— Pawnbrokers generally, O.C.G.A. § 44-12-130 et seq.; municipal authorities may license pawnbrokers, define their powers and privileges by ordinance, impose taxes upon them, revoke their licenses, and exercise such general supervision, O.C.G.A. § 44-12-136.

Sec. 54-148. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Dealer in precious metals and gems.
(1)
The term "dealer in precious metals and gems" means a person, whether known as a redemption center, gold and silver dealer, pawnshop, or any other name, either owner or manager whose business involves receiving by buying or trading jewelry, gems, or precious or semiprecious stones.
(2)
The term "dealer in precious metals and gems" does not include purchases from licensed wholesalers in the normal course of business, or pawnbrokers who only take precious metals or gems on pawn or pledge.
Employee.
(1)
The term "employee" means any person employed or allowed to perform duties, whether or not such person receives a salary, wages or compensation or consideration.
(2)
The term "employee" does not include the person covered on the license or renewal application.
Goods, whenever used in this article, shall carry its normal meaning, and further, it should be construed in the broadest sense allowable and includes all forms of personal property.
Owner means any person or partner or stockholder owning 25 percent or more of a business.
Pawnbroker means any person, who shall in any manner lend or advance money or other things for profit on the pledge or possession of personal property, or other valuable things other than securities or written evidences of indebtedness or who deals in the purchasing of personal property or other valuable things on condition of selling the same back to the seller at a stipulated price.
(Code 1981, § 14-61; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-1, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-149. - Violations; penalties.
Any person licensed hereunder who shall:
(1)
Fail to make any entry of any required item in this permanent record book;
(2)
Make any false entry therein;
(3)
Falsify, obliterate, record so that information is illegible, destroy or remove from his/her place of business, such permanent record book;
(4)
Refuse to allow any duly authorized law enforcement officer to inspect such permanent record book, or any goods in his/her possession, during the ordinary hours of business or any reasonable time, or refuse to allow any member of the general public to inspect goods in his/her possession being held under pawn or purchase within the seven-day purchase and 30-day pawn dates, pursuant to section 54-163 during ordinary hours of business;
(5)
Fail to maintain a record of each pawn or purchase transaction for at least four years;
(6)
Violate any other provisions of this article;
shall be guilty of a misdemeanor as defined by state law, and operation of any business hereunder in violation of any provision of this article shall constitute a separate offense for each day of operation.
(Code 1981, § 14-85; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-25, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-150. - Licenses required.
Every person doing business in the city, as a pawnbroker or dealer in precious metals or gems as defined in section 54-148, prior to commencing business shall make proper application for and receive the appropriate business license as set forth in section 54-151.
(Code 1981, § 14-62; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-2, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-151. - Application for license and permit.
(a)
Applicants for license or permit shall file a written, sworn application on a form provided by the city clerk, signed by the applicant if an individual, by the partner making application if a partnership, and by the president if a corporation, with the city clerk showing:
(1)
The name or address of the principals, agents and employees of the applicant's business during the time that it is proposed that it will be carried on in the city, the local address or addresses of such person or persons; capacity in which such person or persons will act (as proprietor, agent, employee or otherwise); the name and the address of the person, firm or corporation for whose account the business will be carried on, if any; and if a corporation, under the laws of what state it is incorporated;
(2)
The fingerprints of the principals, agents or employees of the applicant's business and other evidence which establishes to the satisfaction of the city clerk of the city subject to the approval of the mayor and council of the city the good character of such persons to carry on the business;
(3)
The place or places in the city where it is proposed to carry on the applicant's business, the zoning district at said place or places, and the length of time during which it is proposed that said business shall be conducted;
(4)
The place, other than the permanent place of business of the applicant, where the applicant within the six months next preceding the date of said application conducted any other type of business, stating the nature thereof and giving the post office and street address or office in which such business was conducted;
(5)
A brief statement of the nature and the character of the advertising done or proposed to be done in order to attract customers;
(6)
Whether or not the person having the management or supervision of the applicant's business have been convicted of a crime or violations of any municipal ordinance, the nature of such offense and the punishment assessed therefor;
(7)
Credentials from the person for which the applicant proposes to do business, authorizing the applicant to act as such representative; and
(8)
Such other information as to the identity or character of the person having the management or supervision of the applicant's business or the method or plan of doing such business as the city clerk of the city may deem proper to fulfill the purpose of this article in the protection of the public good.
(b)
The application shall state thereon that, "It is unlawful for any person to make a false statement on this application." Any false statement made on the application form will be cause to revoke the license issued thereunder and also shall constitute a violation of state law.
(Code 1981, § 14-63; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-3, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-152. - Exclusions; exceptions.
Businesses which limit the purchase of used valuables to the acceptance of trade-ins for credit for new goods or the dispensing of refunds in regard to valuables previously purchased from said business, shall not be required to be licensed as prescribed by this article nor to be covered under the provisions of this article.
(Code 1981, § 14-64; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-4, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-153. - Employee permit.
Each person authorized or allowed to conduct any place of business covered under this article shall be considered an employee and must have an approved license or an employee permit, and this permit must be displayed along with the business license in accordance with license display requirements.
(Code 1981, § 14-65; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-5, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-154. - Business license fee.
(a)
All businesses licensed hereunder, shall pay an annual license fee. The amount of the pawnshop license fee and initial registration shall be as established by the city council from time to time.
(b)
Any co-owner, agent, employee or any other persons authorized to transact business, other than the licensee, shall be required to have employee permits for each license under which he/she operates or transacts business. There shall be no proration or refund of any license fee. Licenses and permits issued hereunder shall be a personal privilege and shall not be transferable, except that employee permits may be transferred from one licensed business to another.
(Code 1981, § 14-66; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-6, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-155. - Character qualifications.
No license or employee permit shall be issued to or renewed for any person who conducts or is employed in a business in which such owner or employee has, within five years of issuance or renewal, been convicted of, or pled guilty or nolo contendere to, any felony involving any federal or state offense or any federal or state misdemeanor involving theft-related offenses; and the licensee will submit the identity of all owners and employees to the city clerk of the city, on a continuing basis as changes occur. This information is to be forwarded to the city police department for the purpose of allowing a complete check in order that these character qualifications be fulfilled prior to the issuance or renewal of any license or permit issued hereunder.
(Code 1981, § 14-67; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-7, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-156. - Location of businesses.
All businesses licensed hereunder shall be located in strict compliance with the city zoning ordinance, as set forth in chapter 94, and at a location with an identifiable street address, which shall be stated on the license or renewal application along with other required information; and no business licensed hereunder shall change its address without giving advance notice to the city clerk, the city building inspector and the city police department, and receiving approval of the change, said approval being granted if the new location complies with this article and the city zoning ordinance.
(Code 1981, § 14-68; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-8, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-157. - Records to be kept.
(a)
All pawnbrokers and dealers in precious metals and gems, in addition to other records, shall keep books wherein shall be entered an accurate description of all property received by or pledged or sold to them.
(1)
Such description shall include:
a.
The brand name of the article;
b.
Any identifying mark;
c.
Number;
d.
Initial;
e.
Social security number; or
f.
Name of any person or company and a statement of the kind of material of which it is made.
(2)
In such books there shall be entered also:
a.
The name, age, address, signature and identification number from a driver's license or similar identification of the person by who same was delivered, deposited or sold;
b.
The name of the broker, agent or employee receiving the item;
c.
The time when such transaction occurred;
d.
The price paid for the goods purchased; and
e.
The check number issued, if applicable.
(b)
A permanent bound set of the pawn tickets shall constitute the book required by this article. All books and records required by this article and items pledged or sold hereunder shall at all times be subject to inspection and examination by any law enforcement officer of the city or any federal law enforcement officer. Any person engaged in any business licensed hereunder, who shall fail or refuse to keep the records as provided in this article, or who shall make false entries concerning the transaction or who shall fail or refuse to permit an inspection by any of the aforementioned law enforcement officers of said records and of the property deposited with or sold to such person shall be guilty of a violation of this article.
(Code 1981, § 14-69; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-9, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-158. - Standard forms required.
All businesses licensed hereunder shall use a standard form for pawn and a standard form for purchase, and no ticket may be used for a dual purpose. Standard forms may differ only by the name of the business and business address. The design of this form shall be provided by the city police department and the forms shall be numbered and chronologically used and sent to the city police department. Each number missing from the chronological sequence shall be considered a separate violation of this article.
(Code 1981, § 14-70; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-10, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-159. - Weekly report to city police.
Every business licensed hereunder shall make a weekly report in writing to the city police department of all items pledged or purchased during the preceding weekly period ending on Saturday night at 10:00 p.m. Such report shall be due in the office of the chief of police of the city by 11:00 a.m. Monday following the week covered by the report. Such reports shall be legibly written or typed in English and shall show:
(1)
The name and address of the business;
(2)
The times of transactions;
(3)
The serial number of the pawn or purchase tickets;
(4)
The amount paid or advanced;
(5)
A full description of articles, including kind, style, material, color, design kind and number of stones in jewelry and all identifying names, marks, numbers, initials or social security numbers; and
(6)
A description of the person selling or pawning said item including name, address, color, weight and height.
(Code 1981, § 14-71; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-11, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-160. - Identification required.
All businesses licensed hereunder shall, when receiving in pawn or purchasing goods, require a proper identification, driver's license or military identification (ID) card or state-approved ID card or company employee ID card containing a picture showing positive identification of the person who is pawning, selling or bartering and verify the identification.
(Code 1981, § 14-72; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-12, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-161. - Purchased articles not to be disposed of for seven days after acquisition.
Every business licensed hereunder shall hold on the premises designated in the license for doing business every item required to be listed under section 54-157 for at least seven working days before disposing of same by sale, transfer or shipment. The provisions of this section shall not apply to items purchased from licensed wholesalers in the normal course of business.
(Code 1981, § 14-73; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-13, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-162. - Pawned articles not to be disposed of for 30 days after acquisition.
Every business licensed hereunder which takes goods on pawn shall hold such goods for at least 30 days, unless redeemed by the owner or on authority of the owner, before disposing of same by sale, transfer, shipment or otherwise.
(Code 1981, § 14-74; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-14, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-163. - Display of goods pledged or purchased.
For a period of seven working days from the date of purchase and 30 days from the date of pledge, unless redeemed by or by authority of the person who pledged the goods, all goods purchased or pawned and required to be listed under section 54-157 shall be kept on the premises designated in the license for doing business, shall have attached to it either a copy of the form required in section 54-157 or the number of such form. Any person desiring to examine any merchandise shall first file with the city police department an incident report or stolen property report and upon giving notice, in writing, to the owner or employee, which notice shall include:
(1)
A full description of the stolen items, including all identifying characteristics;
(2)
The person's name, address and identification; and
(3)
The person's signature;
such individual shall be allowed to examine any merchandise of the type described in said request on the premises. Such written information shall be kept in sequential, dated order in a permanent file or book. This record shall be kept in addition to all other records required herein.
(Code 1981, § 14-75; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-15, 6-4-1984; Ord. No. 1986-2, § 1, 2-3-1986)
Sec. 54-164. - Alteration.
Any items required to be kept or displayed under the provisions of sections 54-161 through 54-163 shall be kept in their original form as they existed when first purchased, and their physical characteristics shall not be altered in any numerical or material manner.
(Code 1981, § 14-76; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-16, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-165. - Erasures or obliterations or write-overs or type-overs are not allowed.
No persons licensed hereunder shall erase, obliterate, type over, write over or in any way cause the original entry on the records required by this article to become illegible. All pawn or purchase tickets filled out in error shall be sent to the city police department in their numbered order along with the weekly reports and shall have the word "Void" typed or written across the front. Such entry shall not be used to cause the original entry to be illegible. If an error is made, and the ticket is still to be used, then a single line shall be drawn through the incorrect entry. This line shall not cause the original entry to be illegible, and the correct information entry shall be made on the next available area.
(Code 1981, § 14-77; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-17, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-166. - Records maintained for four years.
The record for each pawn or purchase transaction regulated by this article shall be maintained for a period of not less than four years from date of this transaction.
(Code 1981, § 14-78; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-18, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-167. - Dealing with minors.
It shall be unlawful for any person licensed hereunder to receive in pawn or in purchase any goods of any character or of any description from any person who has not attained the age of 17 years.
(Code 1981, § 14-79; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-19, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-168. - Hours of operation.
All businesses licensed hereunder shall close their establishments and cease transacting any business no later than 11:00 p.m. each day and begin their operation and commence transacting business at no time before 7:00 a.m. each day.
(Code 1981, § 14-80; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-20, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-169. - Business transactions conducted outside the building normally used for business transaction, parking lots or rear lot area, etc.
(a)
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Business transaction means any part of business including inspection or appraisal of any goods when it is possible for such goods to be taken outside the building where transactions are normally made (the term "possible" is used herein in the broadest sense); and no person other than those possessing a license or employee permit shall be allowed to conduct any form of business transaction either inside the establishment or on any part of outside land area covered under address of the license.
(b)
No business transactions by any business licensed hereunder shall be conducted outside the building designated as the normal place to conduct transactions unless said business does not have a building.
(Code 1981, § 14-81; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-21, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-170. - Articles that have had serial numbers removed or altered or obliterated shall not be accepted.
No business licensed hereunder shall accept any article which has had the serial number altered, obliterated or removed from such article or which the person who is making the business transaction knows should have a serial number on it. Licensees shall immediately report any attempted business transaction of this nature to the city police department.
(Code 1981, § 14-82; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-22, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-171. - No article will be accepted unless the provisions of sections 54-157 and 54-158 are followed.
No business licensed hereunder shall take any item in pawn or purchase or take full possession or title thereto, without entering such transaction of such goods on his/her books and on the standard forms as provided for in sections 54-157 and 54-158.
(Code 1981, § 14-83; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-23, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-172. - Ownership of other pawn business in other town or same town.
Any owner of a business licensed hereunder shall not transfer goods from one business, where he/she has a minority or majority ownership, to another business without said goods meeting all regulations of the ordinances of the city regarding pawnbrokers and dealers in precious metals.
(Code 1981, § 14-84; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-24, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-173. - Inspection.
As a condition of the granting of the license hereunder, every licensee agrees to allow any duly authorized law enforcement officer, pursuant to his/her official duties, to be and remain about the licensed premises without the formality of a warrant, provided said officer does not interfere in the licensee's operation.
(Code 1981, § 14-86; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-26, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-174. - Nonformed precious metals.
It shall be unlawful for any person licensed hereunder to purchase precious metals in a melted, smelted or nonformed state unless the purchase is from a registered dealer in precious metals.
(Code 1981, § 14-87; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-27, 6-4-1984)
Sec. 54-175. - Stolen items.
Every person licensed hereunder shall, immediately, upon request, turn over to the city police department every item believed by the city police department to be a stolen item. The police department shall furnish a property receipt including the case number to the dealer surrendering such item, and such item shall be returned to the dealer only after it has been determined that such item is not stolen. If it is determined such item is stolen it shall be returned to its lawful owner.
(Code 1981, § 14-88; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-28, 6-4-1984; Ord. No. 1986-2, § 2, 2-3-1986)
Sec. 54-176. - State law.
This article is to be construed as consistent with state law. However, these provisions of this article are to be applied in lieu of state law and state fee and registration requirements, whenever possible. It is the intent of this article that all duties delegated to the chief law enforcement officer of the city shall be exercised as provided herein.
(Code 1981, § 14-89; Ord. No. 1984-4, § 1-29, 6-4-1984)
