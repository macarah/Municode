ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 22-1. - Municipal court established.
There is established a municipal court for the city. The council shall designate or appoint a recorder.
(Code 1969, § 2-5; Code 1981, § 7-1)
Sec. 22-2. - Right of entry of city employees; warrant.
Authorized employees of the city, so far as may be necessary for the performance of their duties, shall, upon presentation of proper credentials, have the right to enter, at reasonable times, any building, structure or premises in the city to perform any duty imposed upon them by virtue of their office; provided, however, where the owner or manager in charge of such building, structure or premises fails to authorize said employees to come upon the premises, building or structure as the duties of said employees may require, a warrant may be obtained, authorizing such employees to go upon such premises, building or structure for inspection for health, building and fire hazards, with or without the occupant's consent. Said warrant may be signed and issued by any justice of the peace or notary public ex officio justice of the peace of said county or by the mayor or the judge of the municipal court of the city. In addition, such person so refusing or hindering entry shall be guilty of a violation of this Code and punished as provided in section 1-8.
(Code 1969, § 11-7; Code 1981, § 7-2)
Secs. 22-3—22-22. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - BAIL; APPEARANCE AND SUPERSEDEAS BONDS AND RECOGNIZANCES

Sec. 22-23. - Scope of article.
Bail, appearance and supersedeas bonds and similar obligations, shall be accepted and shall be forfeited in the municipal court in the manner provided in this article.
(Code 1969, § 11-40; Code 1981, § 7-20)
Sec. 22-24. - Assessment of bonds.
It shall be the duty, province and responsibility of the arresting officer to determine the amount of bond on which the prisoner may be released. Such officer shall write in the amount of the bond (cash) in the space provided on the ticket ($_____) and that amount, no more and no less, shall thenceforth be acceptable regardless of who might suggest otherwise. Instead of a cash bond, a property bond in double the cash bond amount may be accepted on the responsibility of the person approving and accepting the personal property bond. The officer who receives the money in hand for the bond shall sign on the ticket and copies thereof in the proper space provided for bond receipt, and shall be held responsible to see that the money is properly accounted for to the city clerk and a proper receipt obtained therefor.
(Code 1969, § 11½-20; Code 1981, § 7-21)
Sec. 22-25. - Proceeding to forfeit upon nonappearance.
Upon the failure to appear of any principal in any bond or recognizance given by a person charged with or convicted of the violation of a municipal ordinance, or by a witness to appear and testify, the presiding officer of the municipal court shall proceed to forfeit such bond or recognizance in the manner practiced in the courts of this state.
(Code 1969, § 11-41; Code 1981, § 7-22)
State Law reference— Bond forfeiture proceedings, O.C.G.A. § 17-6-70.
Sec. 22-26. - Scire facias to issue; appropriating cash obligations.
The city clerk shall issue scire facias on all forfeited bonds, recognizances or other obligations, returnable to the next term of such court at the usual place of holding said court, against the principal and the principal's sureties, which shall be served by the police at least five days before the return thereof, or if the party resides out of the state or county, scire facias may be served by publication, as in cases of scire facias to revive judgments, and if said party resides in said county, outside of the corporate limits of the city, the same shall be served by the sheriff or the sheriffs' deputy. If, at such return term, no sufficient cause be shown to the contrary, judgment shall be entered up against such principal and sureties or such of them as have been served; provided, however, if such bond or recognizance given is cash or its equivalent, upon failure of the defendant to appear at said court at the appointed time, judgment shall be entered up against such defendant, and the bond so given shall be forfeited.
(Code 1969, § 11-42; Code 1981, § 7-23)
Sec. 22-27. - Rearresting defendant and carrying out judgment.
The principal or defendant then under conviction shall be rearrested by the police under proper order of the court, and the sentence or judgment of conviction carried out.
(Code 1969, § 11-44; Code 1981, § 7-24)
Sec. 22-28. - Execution of judgments.
Final judgments in all cases of forfeitures shall be enforced by execution issued by the clerk, bearing teste in the name of the presiding officer of the court, and shall be in favor of the city, and against the principal and the principals' sureties on said bond, which execution shall be turned over to the police for levy and sale.
(Code 1969, § 11-43; Code 1981, § 7-25)

ARTICLE III. - PRISONERS

Sec. 22-60. - Office of jailer created; appointment, bond powers.
(a)
Office created. There is hereby created the office of jailer.
(b)
Appointment. The jailer shall be appointed by the city manager as provided by law.
(c)
Bond. The jailer shall file a bond in the amount established by council conditioned upon the faithful performance of the duties of such office.
(d)
Power of arrest. The jailer shall be a conservator of the peace with full power to make arrests.
(e)
Control of prisoners. The jailer shall have charge of the jail and of all persons employed therein, and control of and custody of all persons lawfully confined therein.
(Code 1969, §§ 11½-17, 11½-25; Code 1981, § 7-30; Ord. of 11-11-1974, §§ 1—5)
Sec. 22-61. - Receiving prisoners; requiring prisoner's to work.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the jailer to receive into the jail such persons as may be committed thereto by any court, police magistrate or justice of the peace in and for the city, or brought thereto by any peace officer, or committed by any court or magistrate in the county, by virtue of any agreement thereto with the city.
(b)
The jailer shall require such persons to work at such labor as such person's strength will permit within and without the jail, not to exceed eight hours each working day, according to the respective sentences or commitments of such persons, and as is provided by statute.
(Code 1969, § 11½-19; Code 1981, § 7-31; Ord. of 11-11-1974, § 6)
Sec. 22-62. - Jailer may accept the bond funds.
The jailer shall maintain a proper record of the names of persons in custody and the amount of the bonds required to obtain their release. When a prisoner obtains sufficient funds to post the cash bond, the jailer may accept the same and sign the yellow copy of the ticket as receiving the same and release the prisoner. In all events a proper receipt for funds must pass in exchange for the funds obtained.
(Code 1969, § 11½-21; Code 1981, § 7-32)
Sec. 22-63. - Record of prisoners.
It shall be the duty of the jailer to keep a record of the names of all persons committed to the jail in a book kept for that purpose, showing the date of commitment, days of imprisonment, amount of fine and any other necessary data.
(Code 1981, § 7-33; Ord. of 11-11-1974, § 9)
Sec. 22-64. - Reports by jailer.
It shall be the duty of the jailer to make out and deliver to the city clerk, on the first day of each quarter, a statement duly sworn to showing the names of all persons who have been confined in the jail during the quarter past, the date of their committal, by whom sentenced, the number of days sentenced, and the names of all persons discharged or released during the past quarter and by what authority they were discharged or released.
(Code 1981, § 7-34; Ord. of 11-11-1974, § 11)
Sec. 22-65. - Personal possessions of prisoners.
All prisoners shall be relieved of any personal possessions on their person which might in any manner be hazardous to themselves or to other prisoners, or to the jail property during confinement, including moneys or other valuables. Such items shall be sealed in an envelope or other suitable container by the arresting officer and safely kept under lock and key for return to the prisoner upon such prisoner's release.
(Code 1969, § 11½-29; Code 1981, § 7-35)
Sec. 22-66. - Retaking and reporting escaped prisoners.
Whenever a prisoner escapes from the jail, it shall be the duty of the jailer immediately to pursue and retake the offender, and to notify the chief of police, giving such officer a description of the fugitive.
(Code 1981, § 7-36; Ord. of 11-11-1974, § 13)
Sec. 22-67. - Arrest of persons aiding escape, etc.
The jailer of the house of correction may arrest, without warrant, any person trespassing upon the jail, or the grounds belonging thereto, or attempting to rescue any prisoner, or assisting or attempting to assist any prisoner to escape or hinder or attempt to hinder the jailer in the lawful discharge of such jailer's duties.
(Code 1981, § 7-37; Ord. of 11-11-1974, § 8)
State Law reference— Aiding escape of prisoner prohibited, O.C.G.A. § 16-10-53.
Sec. 22-68. - Treatment of prisoners—Officer conduct; penalty.
The jailer shall treat all persons committed to such officer's charge humanely; and if such officer shall assault, abuse, beat or otherwise maltreat any person in custody, or knowingly suffer or permit the same to be done; or if such officer shall supply any such person with any intoxicating liquor as a beverage, or knowingly permit or suffer the same to be done, or shall be guilty of any lascivious behavior, or other improper conduct toward any person in custody, or knowingly suffer the same to be done, or shall fail to supply each person in custody with a sufficient quantity of wholesome food or drink, at reasonable or proper intervals, or with necessary bedding, such officer shall, in each case be subject to a penalty of not less than $10.00 nor more than $100.00, and a conviction of any provision of this section shall be deemed cause for removal from office.
(Code 1981, § 7-38; Ord. of 11-11-1974, § 14)
State Law reference— Cruelty by jailers prohibited, O.C.G.A. § 42-4-5.
Sec. 22-69. - Same—Medical care.
Whenever any person confined in the jail is or becomes sick, the jailer shall thereupon obtain the services of a physician for such person and shall then notify the city manager and police chief and shall see that the sick person is properly nursed and attended. Whenever such physician deems it necessary such physician may direct the removal of such prisoner to a hospital, but when such prisoner shall have sufficiently recovered, such prisoner shall be committed and required to serve out the time.
(Code 1969, § 11½-30; Code 1981, § 7-39; Ord. of 11-11-1974, § 12)
Sec. 22-70. - Visiting hours.
Visitors shall be permitted as much as 30 minutes at any one time of visitation in the hallway in the jail between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 10:00 a.m. in the morning and between the hours of 2:00 p.m. and 4:00 p.m. in the afternoons. All other hours of the day the jail shall be maintained closed to visitors. Not more than ten visitors altogether shall be permitted in the hallway at any one time, and any of these may be removed at any time for good cause in the judgment of the jailer.
(Code 1969, § 11½-18; Code 1981, § 7-40)
Sec. 22-71. - Discharge of prisoners.
All persons sentenced to the jail or confined therein for violation of city ordinances, may, at any time before the expiration of their term of imprisonment, discharge themselves therefrom by paying to the jailer thereof a sum equal to the pro rata share of their fine for the unexpired sentence. All persons shall be promptly discharged upon payment made, as herein provided, or upon written order of the mayor in case of emergency only, or some court of competent jurisdiction, or upon the expiration of their term of sentence. Before any prisoner shall be discharged from the jail, on payment or otherwise, the jailer shall notify the chief of police of such intended release. All moneys coming into the hands of the jailer belonging to the city shall be paid into the city treasury.
(Code 1981, § 7-41; Ord. of 11-11-1974, § 10)
