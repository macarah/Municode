ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 6-1. - Definitions.
As used in this chapter, the following definitions shall apply to the terms defined in this section:
Alcohol means ethyl alcohol, distilled spirits, hydrated oxide of ethyl, or spirits of wine, from whatever source or by whatever process produced.
Alcoholic beverages means all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, malt beverage, wine, or fortified wine.
Applicant means a person applying for any license authorized by this chapter.
Application means a form supplied by the city for the purpose of applying for any license authorized by this chapter.
Brewpub means any eating establishment in which beer or malt beverages are manufactured or brewed, subject to the barrel production limitation prescribed in O.C.G.A. § 3-5-36, for retail consumption on the premises and solely in draft form. As used in this definition, the term "eating establishment" means an establishment which is licensed to sell distilled spirits, malt beverages, or wines and which derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross food and beverage sales from the sale of prepared meals or food.
Church means a permanent building where persons regularly assemble for religious worship.
Distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume, including, but not limited to whiskey, rum, gin, brandy, vodka, tequila, and fortified wines.
Drugstore means any retail business that:
(a)
Is engaged primarily in the sale of prescription and over-the-counter medications;
(b)
Has a minimum inventory in pharmacy merchandise other than alcoholic beverages valued at no less than $50,000.00, the value of such items being the same as those indicated in the applicant's annual return to the county tax commissioner for ad valorem tax purposes. In any event, the values so indicated shall be the cost of such items to the applicant and not the price that the applicant charges in retail stores. However, automotive supplies specifically shall not be considered in determining said inventory.
Election day means that period of time beginning with the opening of the polls and ending with the closing of the polls for any city, county, state or federal election or referendum in which residents of the city are voters.
Family means any person related to a licensee within the first degree on consanguinity or affinity as determined according to civil law.
Fortified wine means any alcoholic beverage containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added, including but not limited to, brandy.
Grocery store means any retail business that:
(a)
Is engaged primarily in the sale of food and groceries;
(b)
Has a minimum inventory of food and groceries other than alcoholic beverages valued at no less than $5,000.00, the values of such items being the same as those indicated in the applicant's annual returns to the county tax commissioner for ad valorem tax purposes. In any event, the values so indicated shall be the cost of such items to the applicant and not the price that the applicant charges in retail stores. However, automotive supplies specifically shall not be considered in determining said inventory.
Hotel means any building or structure kept, used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public as a place where a minimum of 25 sleeping accommodations are offered for adequate pay to travelers and guests, and food is actually served and consumed in one or more dining rooms, having an adequate and sanitary kitchen and minimum seating capacity of 50; said sleeping accommodations and dining rooms being conducted on the same premises.
Interest in license means an interest held by an owner or co-owner of a license; a partner of a partnership which owns or co-owns a license; a stockholder in any corporation who owns ten percent or more of the stock in a corporation which owns or co-owns a license; an owner, co-lessor, sub lessor, partner in a partnership or stockholder in a corporation owning or leasing any real estate on which is located a retail consumption dealer, a retail package dealer or wholesale dealer; or anyone sharing in the income or corpus of any trust or estate having such an interest.
License means authorization granted by the city to operate as a retail consumption dealer, retail dealer or wholesale dealer.
License department means the city clerk, or in his/her absence, the mayor.
Licensee means a person holding a license.
Lounge means a separate room physically connected with, a part of an adjacent to a restaurant under the same ownership and open for business at the same hours, the principal activity of which is the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises. This term shall not include any room in which there is permitted any disc jockey, live entertainment, dancing, or other such activity.
Malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than six percent alcohol by volume, and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer and strong beer, but not including sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Meal, regular meal means a meal which is prepared on the premises according to the order of the patron or customer given to a waiter or waitress at the table, booth or counter area where the customer is seated and served by the waiter or waitress at said table, booth or counter area. The term "meal" or "regular meal" does not include food served in a specialty shop; nor food served over the counter; nor "short order meals" such as sandwiches, hot dogs or hamburgers; nor prepackaged, individual meals.
Motel means any building or structure kept, used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public as a place where a minimum of 25 sleeping accommodations are offered for adequate pay to travelers and guests, and food is actually served and consumed in one or more dining rooms, having an adequate and sanitary kitchen and a minimum seating capacity of 50; said sleeping accommodations and dining rooms being conducted on the same premises.
Natural person means a human being.
Package means a bottle, can, keg, barrel or other original consumer container.
Person means any individual, firm, partnership, cooperative, nonprofit membership corporation, joint venture, association, company, corporation, agency, syndicate, estate, trust, business trust, receiver, fiduciary or other group or combination acting as a unit, body politic or political subdivision, whether public, private or quasi-public.
Pool hall means a commercial establishment containing pool or billiard tables for which a charge is made for use of the equipment or payment of an entry into the establishment is required.
Premises means the definite closed or partitioned-in locality, whether a room, shop or building.
Private club means any nonprofit association organized under the laws of this state which:
(a)
Has been in existence at least one year prior to the filing of its application for a license to be issued pursuant to this chapter;
(b)
Has at least 200 regular dues-paying members;
(c)
Owns, hires or leases land, a building or space within a building for the reasonable use of its members, which land, building or space:
1.
Has suitable kitchen and dining room space and equipment and a minimum seating capacity of 50; and
2.
Is staffed with a sufficient number of employees for cooking, preparing, and serving meals for its members and guests a minimum of three days per week; and
(d)
Has no member, officer, agent or employee directly or indirectly receiving, in the form of salary or other compensation, any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages beyond a fixed salary.
Private recreational club means any for-profit corporation organized under the laws of this state which:
(a)
Has at least 200 regular dues-paying members;
(b)
Owns, hires or leases land, a building or space within a building for the reasonable use of its members, which land, building or space:
1.
Has suitable kitchen and dining room space and equipment and a minimum seating capacity of 50; and
2.
Is staffed with a sufficient number of employees for cooking, preparing, and serving meals for its members and guests a minimum of three days per week; and
(c)
Has no member, officer, agent or employee directly or indirectly receiving, in the form of salary or other compensation, any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages beyond a fixed salary.
Proper identification means a document issued by a governmental agency containing a description of the person and the person's photograph and the person's date of birth. For purposes of this chapter, "proper identification" shall include, but not be limited to, the following: a driver's license; a passport; and/or an identification card authorized under O.C.G.A. § 40-5-100 requiring the state department of driver services to issue identification cards to residents who do not have a motor vehicle driver's license. However, for the purposes of this chapter, "proper identification" shall not include a birth certificate.
Restaurant means:
(a)
Any public place kept, used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public as a place where meals are actually and regularly served, excluding periods for holidays, vacations and redecorating. Short order, quick or fast food shops are not considered restaurants. The serving of such meals and food shall be the principal business conducted, with the serving of alcoholic beverage to be consumed on the premises as only incidental to such business;
(b)
Having adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining room equipment and a minimum seating capacity of 50; and
(c)
Wherein the principal business is the serving of such meals which, excluding the sale of alcoholic beverages, account for at least 51 percent of the gross revenue of the business.
Retail consumption dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises at retail only to consumers and not for resale.
Retail package dealer means any person who sells unbroken packages, at retail, only to consumers and not for resale.
Seating capacity means the amount of individual seating which is located at tables for the purpose of serving the dining public, members and guests, but not including any seating located in a lounge, bar, or other area designated primarily for the serving of alcoholic beverages.
Tavern means any place or establishment devoted primarily to the retailing and on premises drinking of malt beverages, wine, distilled spirits or other alcoholic beverages with incidental service of food and live entertainment to be allowed.
Wholesaler or wholesale dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages to retail consumption dealers, retail package dealers or other wholesale dealers for resale.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added, including, but not limited to, sparkling wine, champagne, combinations of such beverages, vermouth, special natural wine, rectified wine and like products, but not including cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this section.
(Ord. No. 2000-6, § 1, 1-1-2001; Ord. No. 12(01)-2009, §§ 1, 2, 12-15-2009; Ord. of 10-4-2016 ; Ord. 201703 , § 1, 1-3-2017)
Sec. 6-2. - Construction of definitions.
Words and terms not explicitly defined in this chapter shall have the meaning given by common and ordinary use as defined in the latest edition of Webster's New Collegiate Dictionary.
(Ord. No. 2000-6, § 1, 1-1-2001)
Sec. 6-3. - License required.
Any person desiring to operate in the city as a retail consumption dealer or retail package dealer or to maintain a fixed location in the city as a wholesale dealer shall apply for and obtain a license from the city prior to commencing operations.
(Ord. No. 2000-6, § 1, 1-1-2001)
Sec. 6-4. - License for consumption; eligibility.
(a)
No retail consumption license shall be issued to any applicant whose establishment does not meet the requirements of a restaurant, hotel, motel, private club, or private recreational club as defined by this chapter.
(b)
No retail consumption dealer license shall be issued to any applicant whose establishment meets the definition of a pool hall pursuant to section 6-1, above.
(c)
The sale of alcoholic beverages in a lounge is permitted under the same retail consumption dealer license as the restaurant provided the lounge qualifies under the definitions of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 2000-6, § 1, 1-1-2001)
Sec. 6-5. - License for the package sale of distilled spirits; issuance restriction.
The following restriction shall apply to persons seeking a license under this chapter:
(1)
No person shall hold a license to operate as a retail consumption dealer or retail package dealer who also has an interest in a license to operate as a wholesale dealer.
(2)
No person or entity shall have an interest in more than one license for the package sale of distilled spirits issued by the city.
(3)
Every applicant shall, prior to applying for a license, read and familiarize himself or herself with the provisions of this chapter, and the application shall constitute a certification that applicant has done so. Every licensee shall maintain a copy of this chapter on the licensed premises and shall instruct each employee engaged in the sale or handling of alcoholic beverages concerning the relevant provisions of this chapter.
(4)
An applicant shall be active in the operation of the licensed business and shall be personally present on the licensed premises sufficiently to assure compliance with the provisions of this chapter. For the purposes of this chapter, a licensee shall not be considered active unless he or she is an owner, stockholder, or fulltime employee of the licensed business and is present on the licensed premises a average of ten hours per week.
(5)
A licensee must be of good moral character and a citizen of the United States or an alien lawfully admitted for permanent residence for at least one year prior to application.
(6)
No person, including members of a retail dealer licensee's immediate family, shall be issued, nor shall have a beneficial interest in more than two package distilled spirits licenses issued in this state.
(7)
A licensee shall not have been convicted of any felony, any misdemeanor involving moral turpitude, any sexually related crime, illegal sale or possession of controlled substances, illegal possession or sale of alcoholic beverages, or any criminal offense related to taxes or gambling. A guilty plea, plea of nolo contendere or the forfeiture of a bond shall be considered a conviction for the purposes of this subsection. Sentencing as first offender status shall not be considered as a conviction if the sentence was successfully completed without any violation of probation and with no adjudication of guilt ever being entered.
(8)
A licensee shall not have had revoked any license to sell alcoholic beverages issued by any governmental agency.
(9)
A licensee shall have and continuously maintain as registered agent a resident of Butts County, upon whom may be served any process, notice or demand required or permitted by law or under this chapter to be served upon the licensee or owner. The registered agent must be of at least 21 years of age.
(10)
All applications shall be complete and sworn to by the applicant.
(11)
Each applicant shall consent to and authorize a fingerprint analysis and investigation. Each applicant shall consent to a criminal background check and civil history check with the results therefrom being used in any public hearing concerning the license.
(12)
All applicants must show financial responsibility. Selling alcoholic beverages is a privilege, not a right, and financial requirements are reasonable. All applicants shall, at the time of application and periodically thereafter, upon request of the city, submit to the city clerk, or designee, evidence of financial responsibility. The evidence or certification shall include without limitation:
a.
Audited financial statements and other proof of the finances of the business, which may be provided at the business for confidentiality reasons, the review to be at the licensee's expense, and a sworn certification from applicant's CPA the licensee is a viable going concern, being operated in a fiscally responsible manner, which maintains reasonable reserves and maintains and follows a responsible business plan, and that the licensee is therefore "financially responsible" and meets the requirements of Jackson's alcohol regulations;
b.
Proof of liability insurance coverage in the amount of no less than $1,000,000.00 per occurrence, $2,000,000.00 aggregate with a company listed on the U.S. Treasury Circular 570;
c.
A $5,000.00 performance bond with an insurance company as surety, approved by the city and properly executed, requiring faithful observance and performance by the licensee of the rules and regulations of the City of Jackson as stated herein.
d.
Proof that the applicant is the owner or lessee of the business (a fully executed and recorded deed or a copy of the lease);
e.
An affidavit from applicant that applicant has adequate financial participation in the business to direct and manage the affairs of the business, and is not a mere surrogate for a person or entity that would not qualify for a license for any reason whatsoever, and a sworn certification from applicant's CPA, based upon personal knowledge, that said the said financial responsibility requirement has been met.
f.
Written acknowledgement that the city shall have the right to subpoena all and any part of the records, books, documents, reports or invoices of the applicant or licensee, securing compliance by such licensee with the provisions of this article, proving or disproving violation of any part of this article by licensee.
(Ord. No. 2000-6, § 1, 1-1-2001; Ord. of 10-4-2016 )
Sec. 6-6. - License; distance restrictions.
(a)
Licenses shall not be issued to authorize the sale of alcoholic beverages at any location which does not meet or exceed the following minimum distance separation requirements from the following establishments:
(1)
For distilled spirits dealers, the licensee's premises cannot be within 100 yards of any church building, or within 100 yards of any school building, educational building, school grounds, or college campus.
(2)
For wine or malt beverage dealers, the licensee's premises cannot be within 100 yards of any church building, school building, school grounds, educational building, or college campus.
(3)
No person shall sell distilled spirits, wine or malt beverages within 100 yards of any alcohol treatment center owned and operated by the state or any county or municipal government therein.
(4)
No person knowingly and intentionally may sell any alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises within 100 yards of any housing authority property. This subsection shall not apply at any location for which a license has been issued prior to July 1, 2000, nor to the renewal of such license. Nor shall this subsection apply at any location for which a new license is applied for if the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises was lawful at such location at any time during the 12 months immediately preceding such application.
(b)
As used in this section:
(1)
The terms "school building," "educational building" or "college campus" shall apply only to state, county, city or church school buildings and to such buildings at other schools in which are taught such subjects commonly taught in the common schools and colleges of the state and are public schools or private schools as defined in O.C.G.A. § 20-2-690(b).
(2)
The term "school grounds" shall mean and include all real estate owned, leased or controlled by any organization meeting the definition of "school" in this section. Such term shall include, but not be limited to, classroom buildings, administrative buildings, athletic fields, gymnasiums, libraries and cafeterias.
(3)
The term "housing authority property" means any property containing 300 housing units or fewer owned or operated by a housing authority created by O.C.G.A. § 8-3-1 et seq., the Housing Authorities Law.
(c)
For the purposes of this section, distances from the proposed licensed establishment to any church building, school building, school grounds, college campus, housing authority property and alcohol treatment centers shall be measured in the following manner:
(1)
From the front door of the structure or partial building unit from which the alcoholic beverages are sold or offered for sale; thence
(2)
In a straight line, regardless of obstructions, to the nearest public sidewalk, walkway, street, road or highway; thence
(3)
Along such public sidewalk, walkway, street, road or highway by the nearest route; thence
(4)
In a straight line to the front door of the building, or to the nearest portion of the grounds, whichever is applicable under the appropriate statue or regulation.
(d)
None of the provisions of this section shall serve to prevent licensed establishments in operation prior to the existence of a church building, school building, or educational building within these distances, from continuing to operate under its license
(Ord. No. 2000-6, § 1, 1-1-2001; Ord. of 10-4-2016 )
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.
Sec. 6-7. - Premises; commercial or residential-professional required.
(a)
Buildings for which alcohol licenses are sought must comply with the building and zoning ordinances of the city; regulations of the state revenue commissioner and the laws of the state.
(b)
For retail consumption licenses, no license to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages shall be issued unless the location has been zoned commercial or residential-professional (special events facilities only) under the zoning laws of the city.
(c)
For retail package sales licenses for beer, wine and distilled spirits, no license to engage in retail package sales shall be issued unless the location has been zoned commercial.
(Ord. No. 2000-6, § 1, 1-1-2001; Ord. of 10-4-2016 ; Ord. of 12-5-2017 , § 1)
Sec. 6-8. - Premises; entrance visibility conditions; architectural requirements, etc.
The front entrance of all licensed retail consumption and retail package sale premises shall be clearly visible from a public street; provided, however, that this restriction shall not apply where the premises are located in a hotel, motel, restaurant, shopping center or multiple-story business building.
Additional requirements for distilled spirits package sales and licenses.
In addition to all other requirements of this chapter, the City Code, the zoning ordinance, and applicable state laws and regulations, applicants for distilled spirits package sales must satisfy the following requirements:
(a)
The front entrance of all premises licensed to sell distilled spirits by the package shall be clearly visible from public street.
(b)
No license for the sale of distilled spirits by the package shall be issued to any applicant whose building where the business will be conducted contains less than 6,000 square feet of combined showroom and storage space.
(c)
In addition to the minimum square footage, retail dealers for the sale of distilled spirits by the package shall maintain a minimum inventory of at least $450,000.00 in distilled spirits, beer, wine or a combination thereof available for sale.
(d)
All buildings where the business will be conducted shall be architecturally designed to complement the historic buildings located around Jackson's courthouse square. All designs shall be submitted to the mayor for approval and no construction shall be commenced without such approval. All construction shall be pursuant to approved plans.
(e)
All buildings and premises shall provide adequate lighting to be safe for customers to transact business therein and all premises shall be landscaped in accordance with the requirements of the Jackson tree ordinance and other zoning ordinances as may relate to landscaping and parking spaces.
(f)
Where applicant for a license to sell distilled spirits by the package is not the owner of the premises, a copy of applicant's current lease with the owner of premises must be submitted. All leases will be filed with the city as well as all renewals and all lease terms shall include calendar year of each license renewal.
(Ord. No. 2000-6, § 1, 1-1-2001; Ord. of 10-4-2016 )
Sec. 6-9. - Premises outside city limits; effect of annexation.
All retail consumption dealers, retail package dealers and wholesale dealers operating lawfully at a location outside the corporate limits of the city and thereafter annexed in the city may continue in operation subject to this chapter, provided they meet all standards of this chapter other than location, and further provided they were in compliance with all state laws at the time of annexation.
(Ord. No. 2000-6, § 1, 1-1-2001)
Sec. 6-10. - License issuance; retail package dealer inventory requirements.
(a)
Retail package dealer licenses for malt beverages and wine shall be issued only to premises which meet the definition of "drugstore," "grocery store," "wine shop" or which are licensed for the sale of distilled spirits by the package.
(b)
As used in this section, "drugstore" means:
(1)
A retail business primarily engaged in the selling of prescription and over-the-counter medications.
(2)
Having a minimum inventory of $50,000.00 in prescription and over-the-counter medications, the value of such items being the same as those indicated in the applicant's annual returns to the county tax commissioner for ad valorem tax purposes. In any event, the values so indicated shall be the cost of such items to the applicant and not the price that the applicant charges in retail stores. However, automotive supplies specifically shall not be considered in determining said inventory.
(c)
As used in this section, "grocery store" means:
(1)
A retail business engaged primarily in the sale of food and groceries.
(2)
Having a minimum inventory of $5,000.00 of food, tobacco products, household supplies and periodicals, the values of such items being the same as those indicated in the applicant's annual returns to the county tax commissioner for ad valorem tax purposes. In any event, the values so indicated shall be the cost of such items to the applicant and not the price the applicant charges in retail stores. However, automotive supplies specifically shall not be considered in determining said inventory.
(d)
As used in this section, "wine shop" means:
(1)
A retail business primarily engaged in the retail sale of package wine produced in the State of Georgia and no other alcoholic beverages. "Primarily engaged" shall mean that the sale of package wine produced in the State of Georgia constitutes 51 percent or more of gross sales. Wine shops can also provide specialty items, including food, local crafts, home goods and décor and other related merchandise.
a.
A wine shop established in accordance with this section shall only be allowed to sell wine by the package and no other alcoholic beverages.
b.
A wine shop established in accordance with this section shall be limited to the sale of package wines produced within the State of Georgia.
c.
Wine shops may provide wine sampling and tastings, provided the wine shop meets all other requirements of this section and applicant acquires an ancillary wine sampling and tasting license.
(2)
Having a minimum inventory of $1,000.00 in wholesale inventory of wine, the value of such being the same as that indicated in the applicant's annual returns to the county tax commissioner for ad valorem tax purposes. In any event, the values so indicated shall be the cost of such items to the applicant and not the price the applicant charges in retail stores.
(e)
Retail package store means:
(1)
A package store with distilled spirits shall be issued only to premises which comply with section 6-5.
(Ord. No. 2000-6, § 1, 1-1-2001; Ord. of 10-4-2016 ; Ord. of 11-17-2020 )
Sec. 6-11. - Purchasing requirements.
No retail consumption dealer license or retail package dealer license shall be issued or renewed to any applicant whose business does not obtain alcoholic beverages exclusively from persons licensed by the state to sell alcoholic beverages to said licensees.
(Ord. No. 2000-6, § 1, 1-1-2001)
Sec. 6-12. - Maximum number of distilled spirits retail licenses permitted.
The city of shall issue a maximum of two licenses for the sale of distilled spirits by the package. No additional licenses shall be authorized unless and until the population of Jackson reaches 10,000, at which time one additional license may be permitted. Afterwards, one additional license may be permitted for each 5,000 population over the 10,000 initial threshold.
In determining population, the city shall utilize the most recent population figures published by the Three Rivers Regional Commission. In the absence of such figures, the city shall utilize the U.S. Census of 2010 or any future decennial census. This provision alone shall not bar the issuance of a license for applications submitted in accordance with this chapter as of the enactment date of this provision or renewals of licenses lawfully held as of the enactment date of this provision. Should an application submitted prior to the enactment of the ordinance codified in this chapter be denied, then this provision shall apply to any subsequent application for the same location.
(Ord. of 10-4-2016 )
Sec. 6-13. - Ancillary wine sampling and tasting license.
(a)
The holder of a retail package wine license shall be eligible for an ancillary license to provide samples and conduct tastings in conjunction with education and appreciation classes of wines offered for sale to customers under the conditions set forth in this section.
(b)
Wine samples shall be available only at the customer's request and shall be limited to no more than two ounces per sample, not to exceed a maximum of eight ounces within a consecutive two-hour period and not to exceed once per day.
(c)
Tastings may be held in conjunction with wine education and appreciation classes conducted outside of normal business hours or during normal business hours if the event is held in a separate tasting room. Tastings shall be limited to no more than two ounces of each wine, and no patron shall exceed a maximum of six wine products within a consecutive three-hour period and not to exceed once per day. No licensee shall conduct more than one tasting event per day, with a maximum two tasting events per calendar month.
(d)
Wine shall only be dispensed or poured by the licensee or its employees and no open containers shall be removed from the licensed premises.
(Ord. of 11-17-2020 )
Sec. 6-14. - Sale of open containers in Jackson Square.
new
The sale of open containers for the consumption of alcohol within the area designated as Jackson Square is permitted during the hours of operation for any outdoor event located within the Jackson Square that is permitted by the City of Jackson and sponsored by the City of Jackson, the Jackson Downtown Development Authority, Butts County, Butts County Chamber of Commerce, or the Jackson-Butts County Council for the Arts. For the purpose of this section, Jackson Square is defined as (Oak St. from the corner of Martin Luther King Junior Drive down to Byars St.; Third St. from Mimosa Lane down to Dempsey Avenue; Mulberry St. from Martin Luther King Junior Drive down Byars Street; and Second Street from Mimosa Lane down Holly Street). Permits for any event where open containers of alcohol are requested will have a fee of $100.00. All sales must be made by those licensed or permitted to sell alcohol within the district. All sales must be made within the hours of the scheduled event plus one hour after the scheduled event time ends. All containers must be a specific plastic cup regulated by the City of Jackson only available from those selling alcoholic beverages within Jackson Square. Alcohol consumption inside motor vehicles inside the Jackson Square is strictly prohibited. All other consumption of alcohol in the Jackson Square that does not adhere to the conditions set out in this section is strictly prohibited.
( Ord. No. 2023-007 , 4-18-2023)
