ARTICLE XIV. - YARD SALES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 42-291. - Definitions.
Yard sale means the casual sale of domestic and/or household items, collectables, tools, pets, clothing and other personal property from any location not zoned for retail sales and in compliance with all requirements of this Code relating to retail sales. The term "yard sale" encompasses the sale of personal items by any individual not engaged in the business of retail sales and includes activities commonly referred to as "garage sales," "rummage sales," "attic sales," "moving sales," or "estate sales." The term "yard sale" does not encompass the sale of agricultural products.
(Ord. of 5-3-2016 )
Sec. 42-292. - Number and length of yard sales.
No property shall hold or conduct more than four yard sales per calendar year, nor shall any owner or tenant of real property allow or permit more than four yard sales per address/lot per year. No property shall hold or conduct any yard sale for more than three consecutive days.
(Ord. of 5-3-2016 )
Sec. 42-293. - Administration and enforcement; penalties.
(a)
The provisions of this article shall be administered by any of the police department officers, code enforcement or other person officially designated by the city council to enforce the provisions of the official Code of the city or this article. The police department and code enforcement officers or other person officially designated by the city council [is] authorized to enter upon private or public property to examine any yard sale and to issue any citation for a violation of this article.
(b)
Proceedings to enforce this article shall be tried in the municipal court. Any person, firm or corporation who pleads guilty, nolo contendere or is convicted of violating the provisions of this article may be sentenced as provided in O.C.G.A. § 36-1-20 or such other general laws as may be in the future enacted to provide for punishment and penalties that may be imposed by the municipal court of this state. Each violation of this article may be punished according to the provisions of section 1-11 of the official Code of the city.
(c)
In addition to its right to seek all legal and equitable remedies, the city shall have the express right to seek injunctive relief against any person holding or conducting a yard sale in violation of this article. Nothing contained in this article shall be construed as granting or vesting in any person or entity other than the city, its council or agents, any right of enforcement by legal, equitable or injunctive process.
(Ord. of 5-3-2016 )
Sec. 42-294. - Parking of vehicles.
The person in charge of the premises upon which a yard sale is conducted shall control the parking of vehicles of patrons so as to avoid congestion and hazardous conditions. Failure to do so shall be sufficient cause for the sale to be immediately terminated.
(Ord. of 5-3-2016 )
Secs. 42-295—42-300 - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PERMIT

Sec. 42-301. - Required.
(a)
Any person who owns the property upon which the yard sale will take place, any person conducting the yard sale, and any person participating as a seller in the yard sale, must obtain a permit from the city clerk prior to the date of the yard sale.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to allow, conduct or participate as a seller in a yard sale in the city without first obtaining a permit from the city clerk.
(c)
Each permit issued under this division must be promptly displayed on the premises upon which the yard sale is conducted throughout the entire period of the permitted sale.
(d)
No property can obtain more than two permits per calendar year without the required fee of $25.00.
(Ord. of 5-3-2016 )
Sec. 42-302. - Applications.
All applications must be received by the clerk two weeks prior to yard sale.
At the time any person makes a request for the issuance of a permit under this division, the clerk shall obtain the following information:
(1)
Name of the person that will be conducting the sale.
(2)
Name of the owner of the property upon which the sale will be conducted.
(3)
Location at which the sale will be conducted.
(4)
Names of any persons who will be participating as a seller in the sale.
(5)
Date of the sale.
(Ord. of 5-3-2016 )
Sec. 42-303. - Interruption of sale.
If a permit holder commences a yard sale and the yard sale is interrupted or terminated for any reason, the holder of the permit shall not be allowed to restart the yard sale after the date and time permitted without obtaining a new permit.
(Ord. of 5-3-2016 )
Sec. 42-304. - Maximum number per calendar year.
The number of yard sales allowed per calendar year period under this article is as follows:
(1)
Residential property. Four yard sales per year will be allowed on residentially zoned property.
(2)
Church/civic/nonprofit organization property. Four yard sales per year will be allowed on church, civic or nonprofit organization property.
(3)
Commercial/industrial property. One yard sale per year will be allowed on commercially/industrially zoned property.
(Ord. of 5-3-2016 )
Sec. 42-305. - Fees.
(a)
If the yard sale is conducted on residential property, or on church/civic/nonprofit organization property, two free permits may be obtained during a calendar year period without payment of a fee. The remaining two permits allowed per calendar year period will be issued upon payment to the clerk of a fee of $25.00.
(b)
If the yard sale is conducted on commercial property a permit will be issued upon payment of a fee of $25.00. A letter of approval must be presented with application from property owner/ or manager of business located on property.
(c)
Fees paid for a permit under this section are nonrefundable.
(Ord. of 5-3-2016 )
Sec. 42-306. - Signs.
(a)
Signs are not permitted on the city's right-of-way or utility poles. If signs are placed on the city's right-of-way or utility poles, the person the permit is issued to will receive a city ordinance citation with a fine in the amount of $25.00 per sign.
(b)
Signs are not allowed to be placed on any property before 48 hours of approved yard sale or remain on property after 48 hours of yard sale. The person the permit is issued to will receive a city ordinance citation with a fine in the amount of $25.00 per sign.
(c)
Fees paid for a permit under this section are nonrefundable.
(Ord. of 5-3-2016 )
Secs. 42-307—42-310. - Reserved.
