ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 30-1. - Disclaimer of responsibility.
For the purposes of this chapter, the city disclaims all responsibility for loss or damage from causes beyond its reasonable control and especially from damage caused by the elements, an act of God, common enemy, thieves, vandals, strikers, malicious mischief makers, explosions, unavoidable accidents, invasions, insurrections, riots or order of any military or civil authority, whether the damage be direct or collateral.
(Code 1983, § 7-15)
Sec. 30-2. - Entry upon streets and lands of cemetery during certain hours.
(a)
Except as otherwise provided in this section, no person shall enter on foot, by vehicle or other means of transportation or movement upon the lands of the city or the streets, roads, alleys or other passageways in and serving the lands of the city commonly known as the West Hill Cemetery between the hours of 10:00 p.m. and 5:00 a.m. next of each day. It is the intent of the mayor and council that any unauthorized entry shall constitute a trespass upon public lands of the city, and public notice is given to that effect.
(b)
For purposes of subsection (a) of this section, the West Hill Cemetery shall be defined to include all lands, streets, roads, alleys and passageways of the city lying within the following boundaries:
South of Crawford Street, west of Ridge Street, north of West Emory Street and east of Loveman Lane/Castle Drive.
Travel upon Lewell Street, Ridge Street, West Emory Street, Crawford Street, Loveman Lane and Castle Drive are not prohibited by this section.
(c)
Subsection (a) of this section shall not be applicable to the following:
(1)
Any employee of the city, the county, the state or the United States engaged in any bona fide law enforcement or public safety duty at the time of entry nor to any other city employee engaged in bona fide duties involving cemetery maintenance, emergency or disaster relief or utilities maintenance or otherwise performing a valid public function or duty.
(2)
Any person entering West Hill Cemetery for purposes of parking or removing a parked vehicle while attending any officially permitted activity or event at the Harmon Field.
(d)
Any person violating this section will be subject to citation and summons to answer the charge in the city municipal court. Upon conviction for any violation, the violator may be subject to the general penalty as provided in subsection 1-7(a).
(e)
Nothing contained in this section shall prohibit the city from charging any violator with a violation of any other law or ordinance applicable to the violator's conduct.
(Code 1983, § 7-11)
Sec. 30-3. - Trespass upon grassed areas.
No person shall enter upon the grassed areas of any cemetery property for any purpose other than those related to interment, visitation of the deceased and maintenance.
(Code 1983, § 22-24(a))
Sec. 30-4. - Use of abutting and traversing streets.
All streets abutting or traversing cemetery properties may be used for recreation purposes, unless otherwise prohibited, except that no person shall operate any motorized model vehicle from such streets or allow such vehicle to pass over or through any cemetery property.
(Code 1983, § 22-24(b))
Sec. 30-5. - Activities prohibited during funeral processions, interments and memorial services.
No person shall engage in any recreational activities upon streets abutting or traversing through cemetery properties during a funeral procession, interment or memorial service. Any person encountering a funeral procession, interment or memorial service shall cease recreational activities or withdraw from the area in a fitting, appropriate and unobtrusive manner.
(Code 1983, § 22-24(c))
Sec. 30-6. - Flowers, trees and shrubs.
(a)
Artificial flowers. Artificial arrangements will be removed from the cemetery by the city after two months or when they become disarranged.
(b)
Potted plants. Potted plants, as long as they remain in good condition, watered and taken care of by the owner of the lot, will not be removed by the city.
(c)
New burials. When a new burial is made on any cemetery lot, all flowers will be removed after ten days, except one or two artificial arrangements and one or two potted plants in good condition may be left subject to subsections (a) and (b) of this section.
(d)
Seasonal arrangements. Seasonal arrangements such as Christmas, Easter, etc., wreaths and flowers will be removed after 30 days. If any lot has an abundance of flowers or wreaths which cause a maintenance problem to the lot, the cemetery sexton may remove those which interfere with maintenance.
(e)
Permanent planting. No permanent planting of any kind is allowed. The city will do all planting of trees, shrubs, etc., as space allows.
(f)
Containers. Only clay, plastic or recessable-type containers will be allowed for flowers.
(g)
Removal of detrimental trees and shrubs. If trees or shrubs situated in any lot in the old section of the cemetery shall by their roots or branches become detrimental to adjacent lots or avenues or unsightly or inconvenient to visitors, it shall be the duty of the cemetery sexton and he shall have the right to enter the lot and remove the trees and shrubs or such parts thereof as he shall determine to be detrimental or unsightly or inconvenient.
(Code 1983, § 7-12)
Cross reference— Vegetation, ch. 122.
Sec. 30-7. - Reserved.

Sec. 30-8. - Disinterment.
The city will not participate in a disinterment at West Hill Cemetery. No disinterment shall be allowed without prior receipt by the city of a certified copy of an order from a court of competent jurisdiction that grants to the petitioner the right to disinter the body in question.
(Ord. No. 02-08, § 1, 4-01-02)
Sec. 30-9. - Rules.
The city shall publish rules for the use and care of the city cemetery and shall provide copies to all funeral homes within Whitfield County and to members of the public upon request. All funeral homes and citizens making use of the city cemetery shall abide by such rules and all amendments thereto.
(Ord. No. 05-11, § 1, 6-20-2005)

ARTICLE II. - CITY SEXTON

Sec. 30-36. - Hours of availability.
(a)
The city sexton shall be available to mark grave sites for burial Monday through Friday between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 4:00 p.m.
(b)
The city sexton shall not mark grave sites for burial on Saturdays, Sundays, New Years Day, Memorial Day, July 4th, Thanksgiving Day and Christmas Day and any other day designated by the mayor and council as a city holiday for the then-current year.
(Code 1983, § 7-9; Ord. No. 05-11, § 2, 6-20-2005; Ord. No. 07-01, § 1, 2-5-2007; Ord. No. 14-06, § 1, 8-4-2014)
Sec. 30-37. - Digging of graves.
(a)
No grave shall be dug in the city cemetery until such gravesite has been marked by the city sexton.
(b)
Only gravediggers approved by the city sexton shall be allowed to open and close graves in the city cemetery.
(c)
The city sexton shall have the authority to oversee the opening and closing of graves and to require that such opening and closing of graves be in accordance with city standards.
(Code 1983, § 7-6; Ord. No. 05-11, § 3, 6-20-2005)
Secs. 30-38—30-65. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - LOTS

Sec. 30-66. - Price.
The purchase price for a lot in the city cemetery shall be set by the mayor and council from time to time.
(Code 1983, § 7-1)
Sec. 30-67. - Removal from reserve list.
The city clerk shall notify every person who has orally purchased or spoken for a cemetery lot but has not paid the full price existing at the time the lot was spoken for that, after 30 days from such notice, any cemetery lot being held in reserve shall be taken from the reservation and placed on the open list.
(Code 1983, § 7-2)

ARTICLE IV. - BURIALS

Sec. 30-96. - Register.
The city sexton shall keep a register in which he shall enter the date of all interments; the name, age and residence of the deceased; and, as far as he can ascertain, the cause of the death.
(Code 1983, § 7-8)
Sec. 30-97. - Conditions to interment.
No interment shall be made in the city cemetery until each of the following conditions is met:
(a)
Satisfactory proof of right to interment in the gravesite shall have been submitted to the city clerk. If the city clerk is not available then such proof may be presented to the city sexton.
(b)
All fees related to the gravesite shall have been paid.
(c)
The city sexton shall have marked the gravesite.
(d)
The city sexton shall have approved the proposed gravedigger.
(e)
The container for the remains shall have been approved by the city sexton.
(f)
For all underground burials, the city sexton shall have approved the vault to hold the container for the remains.
(Code 1983, § 7-14; Ord. No. 05-11, § 4, 6-20-2005; Ord. No. 09-07, § 1, 4-6-2009)
Sec. 30-98. - Depth of graves; supervision of interment.
At the cemetery, no grave for an adult shall be dug less than 4½ feet deep, and no grave for an infant shall be dug less than 2½ feet deep, nor shall any corpse be interred except under the direction and superintendence of the sexton.
(Code 1983, § 7-7)
Sec. 30-99. - Noise during burial.
The creating of any unnecessary noise whatsoever during a burial ceremony in or near a cemetery is prohibited and shall be a violation of this chapter.
(Code 1983, § 7-10)
Sec. 30-100. - Disclaimer.
The city's sole responsibility with respect to the opening and closing of a grave shall be to mark its location in reliance upon affidavits, cemetery lot easements or other furnished documents. The city shall have no liability for incorrect grave placement if it is subsequently determined that the interment right for a grave is vested in one other than as shown on previously submitted affidavits, cemetery lot easements or other documents.
(Ord. No. 05-11, § 5, 6-20-2005)
Sec. 30-101. - Stack burials.
Stack burials shall be allowed only in the following lots of the BB section of West Hill Cemetery: lots 163 through 183 and lots 164-A, 165-A, 166-A and 167-A. A stack burial will not be allowed if the depth of the bottom of the uppermost casket in the grave will be less than four and one-half feet below the natural surface of the grave.
(Ord. No. 09-07, § 2, 4-6-2009)
