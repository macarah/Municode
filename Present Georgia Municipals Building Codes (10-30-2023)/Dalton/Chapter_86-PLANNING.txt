ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 86-1. - Comprehensive land use plan adopted.
The provisions of the Georgia Coordinated Planning Act of 1989 (O.C.G.A. § 36-70-1 et seq.) requiring it and in conformity therewith and, further, in the interest of the public health, safety, and welfare of the people of the city and after a full and comprehensive study known as Project 2000: Dalton's Directions for the Future and after public hearing as required by law for the city-county planning commission which has transmitted its recommendation concerning adoption of the plan and upon recommendation of the urban area planning commission favorable to the adoption thereof after full study and debate, that document, attached to the ordinance of November 4, 1991 and officially kept by the city clerk within the minutes and a journal of the proceedings known as Project 2000: Dalton's Directions for the Future is adopted in its entirety consisting of the following:
Part I: Inventory and Assessment
Chapter I: Introduction
Chapter II: Population Element
Chapter III: Economic Development
Chapter IV: Natural Resource and Historic Resource Element
Chapter V: Community Facility Element
Chapter VI: Housing Element
Chapter VII: Urban Design Element
Chapter VIII: Land Use Element
Part II: Implementation Strategy
Chapter IX: Key Issues and Findings
Chapter X: Goals, Objectives and Policies
Chapter XI: Future Land Use Narrative
Chapter XII: Action Agenda
Appendix A:
Tables 1—49
Maps 1—28
Figures 1—14

Sec. 86-31. - Created.
There is created the Dalton-Whitfield County Planning Commission.
(Code 1983, § 16-2)
Sec. 86-32. - Hearings.
The city-county planning commission is authorized to hold all the public hearings required by law with regard to proposed amendments to the zoning ordinance or the zoning maps of the city or the county.
(Code 1983, § 16-3)
