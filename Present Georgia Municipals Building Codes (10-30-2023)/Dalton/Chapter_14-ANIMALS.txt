ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 14-1. - Animal control board.
(a)
There is created for the city an animal control board to consist of five adult resident citizens of the city and to be appointed for staggered terms of four years each by the mayor and council. The mayor and council shall in its appointments designate one appointee as chairman who shall hold the position of chairman throughout his term. The animal control board shall elect a chairman pro tem who shall act in the absence of the chairman or in any vacancy in the position of chairman, as well as a secretary who shall keep and certify minutes and proceedings of the animal control board.
(b)
Upon the death, resignation or other event creating a vacancy in the seat of any member of the animal control board, the mayor and council shall fill the vacancy by appointing a qualified individual to fill the unexpired term of the vacating member by passage of an appropriate resolution. For purposes of determining the time of the regular expiration of any term of office of any member of the animal control board, the first year for an initial appointee shall be deemed to run from the date of initial appointment to the board until the first regular meeting of the mayor and council in January, 1992. Thereafter, a year shall be deemed to run from the first regular meeting of the mayor and council in January of any calendar year until the first regular meeting of the mayor and council in the immediately succeeding calendar year.
(c)
No member of the animal control board shall be reappointed by the mayor and council to succeed himself for a successive term of service on the animal control board, except that members initially appointed for a term of three years or less or filling a vacancy in a term of three years or less may be reappointed for one full term.
(d)
Three members of the animal control board shall constitute a quorum for the conduct of any business or proceedings before the animal control board, and a vote of a majority of members present at any meeting of the board is required to take any action.
(e)
The chairman of the animal control board shall conduct all meetings of the animal control board, except in his or her absence when the meeting shall be conducted by the chairman pro tempore.
(f)
Each member of the animal control board shall have one vote on all matters to be decided.
(g)
No member of the animal control board shall be paid any salary or compensation for his or her service.
(h)
The sole responsibility of the animal control board shall be to conduct a hearing upon the request of a dog's owner as to the dog control officer's determination that the dog is a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 4-8-24.
(i)
Upon receipt by the city clerk of a request for a hearing pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 4-8-24(c), the city clerk shall schedule a hearing that is within 30 days of the receipt of the hearing request and shall notify the dog owner by certified mail or statutory overnight delivery of the date, time and place of the hearing. Said notice shall be mailed at least ten days prior to the hearing date.
(j)
At the hearing, the owner of the dog shall be given the opportunity to testify and to present evidence and in addition thereto the animal control board shall receive such other evidence and hear such other testimony as the animal control board may find reasonably necessary to make a determination either to sustain, modify, or overrule the dog control officer's classification of the dog.
(k)
Within ten days after the date of the hearing, the animal control board shall notify the dog owner in writing by certified mail or statutory overnight delivery of its determination on the matter. If such determination is that the dog is a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog, the notice shall specify the date upon which that determination is effective.
(Ord. of 2-1-1999(2), § 1(F)(5-74); Ord. No. 10-20, § 1, 12-6-10)

Sec. 14-26. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Domesticated animal means any animal raised so as to live in close proximity to humans. Such animals include dogs and cats, as well as varieties of poultry, livestock or reptiles.
Domesticated animal at large means any domesticated animal not under the restraint of a person capable of controlling it on or off the premises of the owner.
Owner means any person who owns, possesses, harbors or keeps or knowingly permits an animal to be harbored or kept or has an animal in his care or who permits an animal to remain on or about his premises for more than three days.
Public nuisance means any animal that unreasonably annoys humans, endangers the life or health of other animals or persons or substantially interferes with the rights of citizens, other than its owner, to enjoyment of life or property. The term "public nuisance" means and includes but is not limited to any animal that:
(1)
Is found at large;
(2)
Damages, the property of anyone other than its owner;
(3)
Chases vehicles;
(4)
Attacks other domestic animals;
(5)
Unprovoked, attacks pedestrians or passersby; or
(6)
Has been found by the animal control officer to be a public nuisance animal by virtue of being a menace to the public health, welfare or safety.
Vaccination means a protection against rabies by inoculation with antirabies vaccine, given in an amount sufficient to provide immunity from rabies for a minimum of one year.
(Ord. of 2-1-1999(2), § 1(F)(5-20))

Sec. 14-27. - Animal control officer.
(a)
There is created the office of animal control officer, which officer shall enforce this article. The city may at any time and from time to time contract or enter into an agreement with the county or any other municipality, county or combination thereof for staffing the position of animal control officer.
(b)
The animal control officer is authorized to issue citations to persons violating this article for trial in the municipal court of the city upon such citations.
(c)
The animal control officer, as well as employees and agents of the animal control officer and persons under his control and supervision, are empowered to seize animals as required by this article so long as the animals are within the city. The employees and agents of the animal control officer and persons under his control and supervision shall have the right to go on private property within the city for the purposes of enforcing this article, provided it is necessary for such purpose. Further, the animal control officer may set and issue fines as provided in this article when the person chooses to waive his right to a hearing in municipal court. The animal control officer shall have the authority to issue citations and subpoenas to witnesses and to require their appearance in municipal court on any matter relating to the enforcement of any of the sections contained in this article.
(d)
The animal control officer may be contacted after normal business hours by digital beeper and shall respond in the following circumstances:
(1)
In response to a report of a person bitten within the last hour by an animal;
(2)
In response to a report of an animal being injured and in distress;
(3)
In response to a request of the city police department for assistance in restraining an animal which threatens the safety of law enforcement personnel or others;
(4)
To investigate charges of animal cruelty when such investigation cannot reasonably be postponed until normal office hours; and
(5)
In response to such other situations which, in the judgment and discretion of the animal control officer, require an immediate response in order to protect the public.
(Ord. of 2-1-1999(2), § 1(F)(5-21))
Cross reference— Officers and employees, § 2-121 et seq.
Sec. 14-28. - Penalties.
Any owner whose animal is found to be in violation of any of the sections of this article may be cited by the animal control officer or through a sworn complaint alleging any violation by a person who has personal knowledge of such violation and who can identify the owner of the animal involved or the premises where the animal is located. Any owner found guilty of violating this article shall be fined as follows:
(1)
If the animal is reclaimed within the five-day waiting period as set forth in section 14-33, the penalty shall be a certificate for $55.00, redeemable by any participating veterinarian, from the city clerk for the sum of $50.00 once the animal has been sterilized by the veterinarian or a fine of $55.00 to be determined at the discretion of the animal control officer or the municipal court judge.
(2)
If the animal is not reclaimed, the owner shall be fined not less than $100.00 for the first offense.
(3)
Except for a first-time offense under subsection 14-32(a), other violations of this article shall be subject to a fine of not less than $100.00 and/or community service of up to 40 hours.
(Ord. of 2-1-1999(2), § 1(F)(5-25); Ord. No. 05-14, § 1, 9-19-2005)
Sec. 14-29. - Municipal court jurisdiction.
The municipal court of the city shall have jurisdiction over all alleged violations of any section of this article and shall have all powers granted to it which are necessary and incident to the hearing and disposition of matters brought before the court, such powers to include the authority to issue citations and summons for witnesses. The following persons have standing to file complaints for violations of this article:
(1)
The animal control officer or his agent.
(2)
Other law enforcement officers.
(3)
Public health officials.
(4)
Individuals with personal knowledge of the violation upon filing a sworn complaint with the municipal court.
(Ord. of 2-1-1999(2), § 1(F)(5-27))
Sec. 14-30. - Unlawful interference by third parties.
(a)
No person within the city shall, without the knowledge or consent of the owner, hold or retain possession of any animal that he does not own for more than 72 hours without first reporting the possession of such animal to the animal control officer. This subsection shall not apply to those nonprofit organizations whose purpose is the protection of animals.
(b)
No person within the city shall willfully cause an animal to be in violation of any section of this article.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person within the city to attack, assault or in any way physically or verbally threaten or interfere with the animal control officer or his agents in the performance of those duties required to enforce this article.
(d)
Any motor vehicle operator who strikes or runs down any animal within the city shall immediately give notice to the animal control office or appropriate law enforcement agency.
(e)
It shall be unlawful to tamper with, destroy or take any trap which has been set by the animal control officer or his agents.
(f)
No person within the city shall perform a cruel act on any animal nor shall any person harm, maim or kill any animal, except for the animal control officer or his agent as provided under subsection 14-33(d), or attempt to do so, except that a person may:
(1)
Defend his person or property or the person or property of another from injury or damage being caused by an animal; or
(2)
Kill any animal causing injury or damage to any livestock or poultry.
The method used for killing the animals shall be designed to be as humane as is possible under the circumstances. A person who humanely kills a dog under the circumstances described in this subsection shall incur no liability for such death. The penalty for a violation under this subsection shall be a fine of not less than $100.00 for the first offense.
(Ord. of 2-1-1999(2), § 1(F)(5-26))
Sec. 14-31. - Vaccinations.
All dogs and cats within the city must be vaccinated against rabies with the vaccine and in the manner prescribed by the state veterinarian and the state board of health. Vaccinated dogs and cats shall be provided with a proper tag. The owner of every dog or cat vaccinated shall obtain from a veterinarian a certificate of such information.
(Ord. of 2-1-1999(2), § 1(F)(5-22))
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 31-19-5.
Sec. 14-32. - Prohibited activities.
(a)
The following activities shall be prohibited, and the owner shall be subject to the issuance of a warning from the animal control officer or municipal court upon the first offense; subsequent offenses shall be fined pursuant to section 14-28.
(1)
Animals running at large. It is unlawful for any owner to cause or allow an animal to run at large in or on any public property, vacant lot, or private property within the city without consent of the property owner.
(2)
Animals disturbing the peace. It is unlawful for any person to allow any animal in his possession or control within the city to persistently or continuously bark, howl or make noise common to their species or otherwise to disturb the peace and quiet of the surrounding neighborhood. It shall likewise be unlawful to keep or maintain such animals in such a manner as to produce noxious or offensive odors, or to otherwise endanger the health and welfare of surrounding inhabitants.
(3)
Dog control. No person owning or otherwise having a dog in his care, charge, control, custody or possession within the city shall cause, permit or allow such dog to be in or upon any public area unless such dog is under restraint by leash or chain. Dogs are prohibited in those areas so designated and posted by the city.
(4)
Keeping of sick or diseased animals. It is unlawful for any person within the city to own any animal which is seriously sick or injured and not provided proper veterinary care for such animal. This section shall not be construed to include animals under active veterinary care or in veterinary hospitals.
(b)
The following activities within the city shall be subject to penalty provisions pursuant to section 14-28:
(1)
Cruelty. It shall be awful to abandon any animal or fail to provide it with sufficient, wholesome and nutritious food and water in sufficient quantities, proper air, shelter space and protection from the weather. No person shall beat, cruelly treat, torment, or otherwise abuse an animal.
(2)
Animal fights. It is unlawful for any person to promote, stage, hold, manage, conduct, carry on, train for, attend or own any animal for the purpose of participating in any game, exhibition, contest or fight in which one or more animals are engaged for the purpose of injuring, killing, maiming, or destroying themselves, or any other animal.
(3)
Female dogs or cats in heat. It shall be unlawful for the owner of any female dog or cat to allow the animal to run at large while in heat.
(4)
Vaccination requirement. All dogs and cats shall have proper tags evidencing vaccination as provided in section 14-31.
(5)
Destruction of property. No animal shall destroy or damage private or public property.
(6)
Transporting animals.
a.
Domesticated animals shall not be left unattended in vehicles without adequate ventilation, or during times of extreme heat or cold. Law enforcement officers who shall come upon a domesticated animal left unattended in any vehicle without adequate ventilation may use reasonable force to remove such animal and transport to the county animal shelter. Any such officer shall leave a written notice bearing the name of the officer and the location where the animal may be claimed;
b.
Transportation of domesticated animals in the uncovered bed of a pickup truck upon roads with a speed limit of 35 miles per hour or more shall require the animal to be properly secured in an enclosure that is secured safely to the pickup truck.
(Ord. of 2-1-1999(2), § 1(F)(5-23); Ord. No. 05-13, § 1, 9-19-2005; Ord. No. 10-12, § 1, 11-15-2010)

Sec. 14-33. - Impoundment.
(a)
Any animal seized and impounded under any of the sections of this article shall be detained for a period of five working days. If the ownership of the animal is not determined within that period, the animal shall be disposed of in such a manner as the animal control officer may designate. Any animal seized that is wearing identification tags shall be kept for ten days, and the animal control unit shall attempt to notify the owner by writing or phone if possible.
(b)
If, at any time during the required waiting period set forth in subsection (a) of this section, the owner indicates to the animal control officer his intention to not reclaim the animal, the balance of the waiting period shall be waived and the animal shall be disposed of pursuant to this section.
(c)
If, at any time during the required waiting period, examination of the animal by the animal control officer indicates the animal to be severely sick and/or to be a threat to the health of other animals detained in the impoundment, the balance of the waiting period shall be waived and the animal shall be disposed of pursuant to this section.
(d)
Nothing in this section shall prevent the animal control officer from killing an animal when he determines that it is a menace to public safety or to other animals and cannot be impounded with reasonable effort.
(e)
Any animal not sterilized which is released for purposes of adoption rather than to its owner for redemption shall be required to have a nonrefundable certificate purchased from the animal control officer in the amount of $55.00. This certificate may be redeemable by any participating veterinarian from the city clerk for the sum of $50.00 once the animal has been sterilized by the veterinarian.
(Ord. of 2-1-1999(2), § 1(F)(5-24))
State Law reference— Livestock running at large or straying, O.C.G.A. § 4-3-1 et seq.
Sec. 14-34. - Enclosures for keeping small animals.
Other than those animals prohibited in section 14-32, small animals, under ten pounds in weight, and miniature pot bellied pigs may be kept within the city limits, subject to the following:
(a)
Condition, size. All such animals must be provided with adequate housing and kept in such housing while not within the immediate presence of the owner. The pens or other enclosures wherein animals are kept shall have a solid floor made of cement or other suitable washable material, except when pens are 75 feet or more from the nearest neighbor's residence or place of business. Floor space in all these pens or enclosures, wherever located, must have the following minima:
1.
Miniature pot bellied pigs, 100 square feet per animal.
2.
Rabbits, guinea pigs, and all other small animals under ten pounds and not specifically provided for herein, four square feet per animal.
(b)
Location. Pens or yards where such animals are kept shall be placed at the following minimum distances from any residence or business establishment:
1.
Distance from any residence, except that of owner, or any business establishment, 50 feet.
2.
Distance from owner's residence, five feet.
(c)
Maximum number. The maximum number of such animals which may be kept on a single premises shall not exceed the following:
1.
Miniature pot-bellied pigs, one.
2.
All other small animals in any combination, ten.
(Ord. No. 05-08, § 1, 5-16-2005; Ord. No. 05-14, § 2, 9-19-2005)
Sec. 14-35. - Shelter/tethering requirements.
(a)
All domesticated animals shall be provided with proper shelter. Said shelter shall be constructed and maintained to protect the animal from rain, snow, ice, excessive cold or heat, and excessive direct sunlight, and shall be of a size to allow the animal sufficient space to stand, turn around, and lie down in a comfortable position appropriate to the age, size, and health of the animal.
(b)
Crates specifically designed for domesticated animals shall comply with this section provided such crate is appropriately sized for the animal.
(c)
Adequate shelter generally shall consist of four walls, a roof, a floor, and an opening of adequate size, based upon the size of the animal. Alternatively, metal or plastic drums may constitute adequate shelter. Such shelter shall be elevated not less than four inches above the ground to prevent the intrusion of water. The provisions set forth herein are in addition to the provisions for small animals as set forth in section 14-34 of this chapter.
(d)
Inadequate shelter shall include, but not be limited to, boxes, lean-tos, or the areas beneath or upon porches, decks, or crawl spaces.
(e)
Each domesticated animal shall be provided individual shelter.
(f)
An owner may confine his or her domesticated animal by tethering such animal with a running cable line or trolley system, attached to stationary objects at either end, and not less than 20 feet in length and mounted not less than six feet nor more than seven feet above ground level. The tethering of a domesticated animal to a single stationary object is prohibited.
(g)
The length of the tether shall allow access to the maximum available exercise area and shall allow free access to food, water, and shelter.
(h)
As an alternative to a running cable line or trolley system, an owner may tether a domesticated animal to a single stationary object upon meeting all of the following conditions:
(1)
The exercise area must consist of a minimum of 500 square feet of area upon the owner's lot or parcel;
(2)
The tether shall be made of braided or metal cable and may not consist of either a chain or rope;
(3)
The tether shall be attached to the stationary object by snap hook or other device utilizing a swivel connection;
(4)
The tether shall allow free access to food, water, and shelter; and
(5)
The tether shall not allow the animal to leave the owner's lot or parcel.
(i)
For the purposes of this section the following definitions shall apply:
Exercise area shall refer to the unobstructed area within which a domesticated animal may walk or run while tethered without encountering obstacles impeding movement.
Trolley system (or running cable line) shall refer to a tethering system consisting of a horizontal cable attached at both ends to a stationary object, typically a vertical post or tree. The horizontal cable shall have stops at each end so as to limit the travel distance and to prevent the domesticated animal from becoming wrapped around the post or tree.
(Ord. No. 10-12, § 2, 11-15-2010)
Sec. 14-36. - Cleanup requirement.
The owner of every domesticated animal shall be responsible for the removal of any excreta deposited by his or her domesticated animal on public or private property within the city.
(Ord. No. 10-12, § 3, 11-15-2010)
Sec. 14-37. - Sale of dogs and/or cats.

(Ord. No. 10-12, § 4, 11-15-2010)

Sec. 14-91. - Definitions.
(a)
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
City means the City of Dalton acting by and through its governing authority.
Dangerous dog means any dog that, according to the records of an appropriate authority:
(1)
Inflicts a severe injury on a human being without provocation on public or private property at any time after March 31, 1989; or
(2)
Aggressively bites, attacks or endangers the safety of humans without provocation after the dog has been classified as a potentially dangerous dog and after the owner has been notified of such classification.
Dog control officer means the individual selected by the city pursuant to this article to aid in the administration and enforcement of this article.
Governing authority means the mayor and council acting in its legislative capacity.
Owner means any natural person or legal entity, including but not limited to a corporation, partnership, firm or trust owning, possessing, harboring, keeping or having custody or control of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog.
Potentially dangerous dog means any dog that without provocation bites a human being on public or private property at any time after March 31, 1989.
Proper enclosure means an enclosure for keeping a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog while on the owner's property that is securely confined indoors or securely enclosed in a locked pen, fence or structure suitable to prevent the entry of young children and designed to prevent the dog from escaping. Any such pen or structure shall have secure sides and a secure top. If the dog is enclosed within a fence, all sides of the fence shall be of sufficient height, and the bottom of the fence shall be constructed and secured in such a manner as to prevent the dog's escape either from over or from under the fence. Any such enclosure shall also provide protection from the elements for the dog.
Records of an appropriate authority means records of any state, county or municipal law enforcement agency; records of any county or municipal animal control agency; records of any county board of health; records of any federal, state or local court; records of a dog control officer provided for in the Georgia Dangerous Dog Control law, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq.
Severe injury means any physical injury that results in broken bones or disfiguring lacerations requiring multiple sutures or cosmetic surgery or physical injury that results in death.
(b)
A dog that inflicts an injury upon a person when the dog is being used by a law enforcement officer to carry out the law enforcement officer's official duties shall not be a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog within the meaning of this article. A dog shall not be a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog within the meaning of this article if the injury inflicted by the dog was sustained by a person who, at the time, was committing a wilful trespass or other tort or was tormenting, abusing or assaulting the dog or had in the past been observed or reported to have tormented, abused or assaulted the dog or was committing or attempting to commit a crime.
(Ord. of 2-1-1999(2), § 1(F)(5-71))
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.

The dog control officer is authorized to make whatever inquiries or investigations are deemed necessary to ensure compliance with this article.

Sec. 14-96. - Certificate of registration required.
(a)
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this article for an owner to have or possess within the city a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog without a certificate of registration issued in accordance with this article.
(b)
The dog control officer shall issue a certificate of registration to the owner of a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog if the owner presents to the dog control officer or the dog control officer otherwise finds sufficient evidence of the following:
(1)
A proper enclosure to confine the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog.
(2)
The posting of the premises where a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog is located with a clearly visible sign warning that there is a dangerous dog on the property. (The sign posted pursuant to this subsection shall conform substantially to the design provided by the state department of natural resources pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 4-8-25(b)).
(3)
A policy of insurance in the amount of at least $15,000.00 issued by an insurer authorized to transact business in the state insuring the owner of the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog against liability for any personal injuries inflicted by the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog or a surety bond in the amount of $15,000.00 or more issued by a surety company authorized to transact business in the state and payable to any person injured by the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog.
(Ord. of 2-1-1999(2), § 1(F)(5-75))
Sec. 14-97. - Annual fee for certificate of registration.
(a)
Prior to receiving a certificate of registration for a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog pursuant to this article, the owner of such dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall pay to the city an annual fee of $25.00.
(b)
At the time of the annual renewal of the certificate of registration, the dog control officer shall require evidence from the owner or shall make such investigation as may be necessary to verify that the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog is continuing to be confined in a proper enclosure and that the owner is continuing to comply with other sections of this article.
(Ord. of 2-1-1999(2), § 1(F)(5-80))
Sec. 14-98. - Effect of issuance of certificate of registration.
The city's issuance through its dog control officer of a certificate of registration or renewal of certificate of registration pursuant to this article does not warrant or guarantee nor is it intended to warrant or guarantee to the public at large or to any specific person that the requirements of this article or of the dangerous dog control law, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq., are maintained by the owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog on a continuous basis following the date of issuance of the initial certificate of registration or following the date of any renewal of such certificate.
(Ord. of 2-1-1999(2), § 1(F)(5-78))
Sec. 14-99. - Notification required generally.
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall notify the dog control officer within 24 hours if the dog is on the loose, is unconfined, has attacked a human, has died or has been sold or donated. If the dog has been sold or donated, the owner shall also provide the dog control officer with the name, address and telephone number of the new owner of the dog.
(Ord. of 2-1-1999(2), § 1(F)(5-76))
Sec. 14-100. - Notice by owner of moving; registration by new resident.
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall notify the dog control officer if the owner is moving from the dog control officer's jurisdiction. For purposes of this section, the term "jurisdiction" shall mean the corporate limits of the city as established from time to time. The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog who is a new resident of the state shall register the dog, if the dog is kept within the city, with the dog control officer within 30 days after becoming a resident. The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog who moves from another county or municipality in the state to the city shall register the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog with the dog control officer within ten days after becoming a resident of the city.
(Ord. of 2-1-1999(2), § 1(F)(5-77))
Sec. 14-101. - Certain acts prohibited.
(a)
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this article for an owner of a dangerous dog to permit the dog to be outside a proper enclosure unless the dog is muzzled and restrained by a substantial chain or leash and is under the physical restraint of a responsible person. The muzzle shall be made in a manner that will not cause injury to the dog or interfere with its vision or respiration but will prevent it from biting any person.
(b)
It is unlawful and a violation of this article for an owner of a potentially dangerous dog to permit the dog to be outside a proper enclosure unless the dog is restrained by a substantial chain or leash and is under the restraint of a responsible person.
(Ord. of 2-1-1999(2), § 1(F)(5-81))
Sec. 14-102. - Confiscation generally.
(a)
A dangerous dog shall be immediately confiscated by the dog control officer or by a law enforcement officer or by another person authorized by the dog control officer if:
(1)
The owner does not secure the liability insurance or bond required by subsection 14-96(b)(3).
(2)
The dog is not validly registered as required by subsection 14-96(a).
(3)
The dog is not maintained in a proper enclosure.
(4)
The dog is outside a proper enclosure in violation of subsection 14-101(a).
(b)
A potentially dangerous dog shall be confiscated in the same manner as a dangerous dog if the dog is:
(1)
Not validly registered as required by subsection 14-96.
(2)
Not maintained in a proper enclosure.
(3)
Outside a proper enclosure in violation of subsection 14-101(b).
(c)
Any dog that has been confiscated under subsection (a) or (b) of this section shall be returned to its owner upon the owner's compliance with this article and upon the payment of the reasonable confiscation costs. For purposes of this subsection, the term "reasonable confiscation costs" shall mean the actual cost of capturing, confining and maintaining the dog prior to its actual return to the owner. If the owner does not comply with this article within 20 days of the date the dog was confiscated, the dog shall be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner.
(Ord. of 2-1-1999(2), § 1(F)(5-82))
Sec. 14-103. - Quarantine after confiscation.
The dog control officer, a law enforcement officer or any other person authorized by the dog control officer shall confiscate a dangerous dog immediately and place it in quarantine for the proper length of time as determined by the county board of health and, thereafter, shall see that the dangerous dog shall be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner if the dangerous dog either:
(1)
Attacks or bites a human being under circumstances constituting a violation of this article where the owner of such dangerous dog has a previous conviction for violation of this article and has knowingly and wilfully failed to comply with this article after such first occurrence; or
(2)
Aggressively attacks and causes severe injury or death to a human being under circumstances constituting a violation of this article after its owner has knowingly and wilfully failed to comply with this article.
(Ord. of 2-1-1999(2), § 1(F)(5-85(a))
Sec. 14-104. - Investigation and inquiries of reports; notice of classification generally.
(a)
Upon receiving a report of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog within the city from any law enforcement agency, animal control agency, rabies control officer or county board of health, the dog control officer shall make such investigations and inquiries with regard to such report as may be necessary to carry out this article. In exercising his duties the dog control officer is authorized to enter private lands or enclosures within the city as follows:
(1)
Upon announcing himself to the property owner, tenant or occupant of the land or enclosure in which the dog control officer requires entry and requesting consent.
(2)
If the owner, occupant or tenant shall refuse consent to the dog control officer upon his announcement and request for entry, upon search warrant as may be provided by law.
(b)
When the dog control officer shall classify a dog as a dangerous dog or reclassifies a potentially dangerous dog as a dangerous dog, the dog control officer shall promptly notify the dog's owner in writing by certified mail to the owner's last known address of such classification or reclassification. Such notice shall be complete upon its mailing.
(Ord. of 2-1-1999(2), § 1(F)(5-83))
Sec. 14-105. - Contents of notice of classification.
(a)
When the dog control officer shall classify any dog as a potentially dangerous dog, the dog control officer shall notify the dog's owner of such classification, and the notice to the owner shall:
(1)
Be in writing and mailed by certified mail to the owner's last known address.
(2)
Include a summary of the dog control officer's findings that form the basis for the dog's classification as a potentially dangerous dog.
(3)
Be dated and shall state that the owner, within 15 days after the date shown on the notice, has a right to request a hearing on the dog control officer's determination that the dog is a dangerous dog.
(4)
State that the hearing, if requested, shall be before the animal control board.
(5)
State that if a hearing is not requested, the dog control officer's determination that the dog is a potentially dangerous dog will become effective for all purposes under this article on a date specified in the notice, which shall be after the last day in which the owner has a right to request a hearing.
(6)
Include a form to request a hearing before the animal control board and provide specific instructions for mailing and delivering such request to the board.
(b)
When a dog is classified as a dangerous dog, the dog control officer shall notify the dog's owner of such classification and the notice to the owner shall:
(1)
Be in writing and mailed by certified mail to the owner's last known address.
(2)
Include a summary of the dog control officer's findings that form the basis for the dog's classification as a dangerous dog.
(3)
Be dated and shall state that the owner, within 15 days after the date shown on the notice, has a right to request a hearing on the dog control officer's determination that the dog is a dangerous dog.
(4)
State that the hearing, if requested, shall be before the animal control board.
(5)
State that if a hearing is not requested, the dog control officer's determination that the dog is a dangerous dog will become effective for all purposes under this article on a date specified in the notice, which shall be after the last day in which the owner has a right to request a hearing.
(6)
Include a form to request a hearing before the animal control board and provide specific instructions for mailing and delivering such request to the board.
(c)
When the animal control board receives a request for a hearing as provided in subsections (a) and (b) of this section, it shall schedule such hearing within 30 days after receiving the request. The animal control board shall notify the dog owner in writing by certified mail of the date, time and place of the hearing, and such notice shall be mailed to the dog owner at least ten days prior to the date of the hearing. At the hearing the owner of the dog shall be given the opportunity to testify and present evidence, and in addition thereto the animal control board shall receive such other evidence and hear such other testimony as the animal control board may find necessary to make a determination either to sustain, modify or overrule the dog control officer's classification of the dog.
(d)
Within ten days after the date of the hearing, the animal control board shall notify the dog owner in writing by certified mail of its determination on the matter. If such determination is that the dog is a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog, the notice shall specify the date upon which a determination is effective.
(Ord. of 2-1-1999(2), § 1(F)(5-84))
