ARTICLE II. - HEAVY EQUIPMENT OPERATIONS

Sec. 70-26. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Permanent entrance, exit or driveway means an entrance, an exit or a driveway that enters from or onto a public road that preexisted prior to any uses for which this article is intended and which requires no additions or deletions to the entrance, exit or driveway for its intended use.
Temporary entrance, exit or driveway means an entrance, an exit or a driveway that did not exist prior to the use for which this article is intended or is a preexisting entrance, exit or driveway that requires additional construction or deletions to the entrance, exit or driveway for its intended use.
(Ord. No. 66, 6-12-1992)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 70-27. - Purpose.
The purpose and intent of this article shall be that of providing a means of allocating the expense of maintaining county roads fairly amongst those parties who cause unusually heavy wear and tear on such roads for commercial purposes.
(Ord. No. 66, 6-12-1992)
Sec. 70-28. - Permit required; exemptions.
(a)
Any person operating equipment or vehicles of a gross weight of over 26,000 pounds for mining or construction purposes upon county-maintained or county-sanctioned roads shall be required to obtain a permit from the superintendent of roads within 48 hours of commencing any operation on any location within this county where a temporary entrance to the roadway is constructed and/or more than ten entrances or exits off and onto the roadway are to be made by vehicles classified under this article. Any person operating equipment or vehicles of a gross weight of 26,000 pounds for mining or construction purposes upon county-maintained or county-sanctioned roads shall not be required to obtain a permit from the superintendent of roads for any location within this county where a permanent entrance to the roadway is constructed.
(b)
Equipment used in harvesting, loading, or transporting logs or pulpwood is specifically exempted from this article.
(Ord. No. 66, 6-12-1992; Ord. No. 74, § 15, 3-18-1994)
Sec. 70-29. - Permit issuance.
(a)
Permits for transport of goods or equipment under this article shall be issued upon application by telephone notification to the office of the superintendent of roads of the following information:
(1)
The location of the operation, including land lot and land district where no address is available.
(2)
The approximate dates of commencement and completion of the operation.
(3)
The name and address of the person acting in a supervisory capacity of the operation.
(4)
The proposed route of the hauling operation.
(5)
The name of the licensed surety company issuing the bond for compliance or the name of the lending institution providing a letter of credit.
(b)
Permits shall be issued upon submission of proper information required for the application for a nominal fee as provided in the schedule of fees and charges maintained in the office of the clerk of the board of commissioners and shall not be unreasonably withheld.
(Ord. No. 66, 6-12-1992)
Sec. 70-30. - Failure to obtain permit.
Failure of any operator subject to this article to procure a permit to carry out the operations governed by this article shall constitute a violation.
(Ord. No. 66, 6-12-1992)
Sec. 70-31. - Required bond or letter of credit.
(a)
Every person applying for a permit under this article shall file a security bond or bank letter of credit with the superintendent of roads within five working days of being furnished surety bond forms by the superintendent of roads or the board of commissioners.
(b)
Any security bond or bank letter of credit filed with the superintendent of roads shall be written by a surety or lending institution authorized to transact business in the state. The security bond or bank letter of credit shall be in an amount of $1,000.00 and shall be made payable to the board of commissioners and conditioned upon the successful completion of operations with a satisfactory examination of all county-maintained or -sanctioned roads used during the performance of the operations.
(c)
Any security bond or bank letter of credit shall be transferable from one site to another within this county, provided that operator supplies complete information regarding any other site and that the security bond or bank letter of credit remains current for the duration of operations. Each additional site within the county shall require an additional security bond or bank letter of credit in the amount of $1,000.00.
(Ord. No. 66, 6-12-1992)
Sec. 70-32. - Release or execution of bond.
(a)
The security bond submitted as provided in section 70-31 shall remain in effect following the cessation of operations until the superintendent of roads has made an inspection of the permitted site and certified that the operation has complied with all of the requirements of this article. The board of commissioners may execute the security bond at any time following cessation of operations upon certification by the superintendent of roads that:
(1)
Damage or wear and tear to county-maintained or county-sanctioned roadbeds, rights-of-way, bridges, signage, or property of any type has been detected that is clearly attributable to operations covered under this article and under the operator's specific permit within 150 feet in either direction along the right-of-way under the permit;
(2)
The operator has been notified, in writing, of any such damage or wear and tear and has been requested to replace or repair the damaged property;
(3)
The operator has failed to repair or replace the damaged property within 60 days of receipt of the notice from the superintendent of roads; and
(4)
The operator has received written notice that the security bond or bank letter of credit shall be executed in favor of the board of commissioners within five days of the superintendent's certification of noncompliance.
(b)
The operator shall have five working days to notify the superintendent of roads of the termination of hauling activities under the permit. Upon such notification, the superintendent will carry out an inspection of all roads covered by the permit immediately and shall serve notice upon the operator of assayed damages. The operator has 60 days to repair or replace all damages to rights-of-way covered by the permit. The superintendent of roads must make a certified report to the board of commissioners within three working days of receiving notice from the operator of the cessation of operations.
(Ord. No. 66, 6-12-1992)
Sec. 70-33. - Appeals.
The operator may appeal the extent of damages assayed by the superintendent of roads, as provided in section 70-32, to the office of the board of commissioners or to the superior court of the county.
(Ord. No. 66, 6-12-1992)
Sec. 70-34. - Warning signs.
(a)
Warning signs, a minimum of 36 inches by 36 inches in size and orange in color, shall be placed prominently on the edge of the roadbed within 500 feet, but not less than 300 feet, from the place where equipment and heavy haulers are to enter the road so as to adequately warn oncoming traffic of the entrance of such equipment onto the roadway. Warning signs shall state in lettering visible from a distance of 150 feet in daylight: "Trucks entering highway."
(b)
The cost of construction and placement of the warning signs are the responsibility of the company and shall be installed in accordance with instructions of the superintendent of roads.
(Ord. No. 66, 6-12-1992)
Secs. 70-35—70-60. - Reserved.

Sec. 70-62. - Authority and jurisdiction.
modified
Whereas, timber harvesting operations occur in the unincorporated portions of Upson County on a recurring basis.
Whereas, timber harvesting is an important component of the local economy in Upson County, providing revenue to landowners, business opportunities, and jobs for local residents; it is the intent of the Governing Authority of Upson County to facilitate this industry while at the same time protecting county infrastructure and the safety of the community.
Whereas, timber harvesting in many instances requires the use of county-maintained roads in order to transport the product to market.
Whereas, it is in the public interest to protect the county road system from damage caused by timber harvesting operations to the extent allowed by state law.
Whereas, O.C.G.A. § 12-6-24 authorizes and establishes the terms and conditions under which this article may be adopted and enforced.
Whereas, it is the intent of the Governing Authority of Upson County to preserve and exercise where appropriate all powers and authority granted to it under state law, including but not limited to O.C.G.A. § 32-4-42 and O.C.G.A. § 40-6-371, that are not in conflict with O.C.G.A. § 12-6-24, in order to protect the health, safety and welfare of the community.

Sec. 70-83. - Damages claim.
modified
When damage results from a person or firm's harvesting activities, the Governing Authority of Upson County shall make and provide a written claim to the person or firm causing the damage within 30 business days after the governing authority becomes aware of the damage. Such claim may be given in person, by telefacsimile, email or mail. The claim shall describe the damage in detail and, in compliance with section 70-84(1) of this article, give the person or firm the opportunity to repair such damage within 30 days of the notification; provided, however, the county shall be authorized to repair the damage immediately if the governing authority or its designee determines the conditions present a threat to public safety, health or welfare and, upon making such repairs, shall present to the person or firm and the issuer of the applicable bond or letter of credit an itemized list of expenses incurred as a claim against the responsible party and the issuer of its bond or letter of credit. Upon the issuance of a claim as provided in this section the Governing Authority of Upson County or its designee shall notify the issuer of the bond or letter of credit that a claim has been made and will be resolved or adjudicated according to the terms of this article.
( Ord. No. 274 , § 3, 4-26-2022)
Sec. 70-84. - Response to claim.
modified
Within 30 days of receipt of the written claim described in section 70-73, the person or firm against whom the claim is submitted may:
(1)
Repair such damage at his or its own expense with the approval and supervision of the Governing Authority of Upson County or its designee. When repairs are completed to the satisfaction of the governing authority or its designee, the governing authority or its designee shall provide a written notification of satisfactory completion within five business days to the responsible person or firm and to the surety issuing the bond or the bank issuing the letter of credit, thereby terminating the claim.
(2)
In the event of inclement weather or other factors preventing repair of the damage, request a 30-day extension to repair the damage from the Governing Authority of Upson County, provided that no extensions shall exceed 90 days from the date the claim was tendered. Approval of any extension shall be at the discretion of the Governing Authority of Upson County or its designee.
(3)
Appeal the claim to the Magistrate Court of Upson County. Any such appeal must name the issuer of the bond or letter of credit as a party, who shall be served with all pleadings in the action and shall have the right to appear. The magistrate court will hear evidence and arguments within 30 days of the written appeal and issue a ruling within ten days of such hearing. Any such appeal shall toll the 30-day period, or any extension thereof, required by section 70-73 of this article. If the magistrate court rules in favor of the person or firm against whom the claim was made, the county shall have no right to recover any proceeds of the bond or letter of credit, and judgment shall be entered against the county. If the magistrate court rules in favor of the county the court shall determine the amount of damages to which the county is entitled to recover and enter judgment; accordingly, the Governing Authority of Upson County shall be authorized to call the bond or letter of credit and recover from the proceeds thereof an amount equal to the judgment entered by the court, up to the total amount of the bond or letter of credit. The portion of any judgment entered in favor of the county that exceeds the amount of the bond or letter of credit shall be subject to collection by any additional remedies at law or equity.
(4)
In the event the person or firm against whom the claim has been submitted fails to take any of the actions allowed under subsections (1), (2) or (3) of this section within the time required therein, such person or firm shall be deemed to have waived any and all rights to contest the call of the bond or letter of credit.
( Ord. No. 274 , § 3, 4-26-2022)

ARTICLE IV. - ACCESS OF PRIVATE DRIVEWAYS TO PUBLIC ROADS

Sec. 70-125. - Restrictions regarding safety hazards and standing or flowing water.
No driveway shall cross or enter a county road right-of-way if such driveway poses a safety hazard or if such driveway causes water to stand or flow on or across a public road. Any such driveway may be removed by the county and may be replaced only upon obtaining a permit therefor.
(Ord. No. 147, § 1, 7-26-2001)
Sec. 70-126. - Driveway installation.
Any person installing a driveway for the purpose of providing access from private property to county roads is required to install a culvert under such driveway entrance and to obtain a culvert installation permit from the superintendent of roads for such installation.
(Ord. No. 147, § 2, 7-26-2001)
Sec. 70-127. - Permit.
(a)
Applications for a driveway permit shall be made at the county building and zoning office and shall contain such information as required by that office. Such application shall be forwarded to the superintendent of roads or his designee, who shall personally inspect the site of the proposed driveway. The superintendent of roads shall approve the location of the driveway if there is no safety hazard and shall determine the size and type of culvert required to provide for the anticipated flow of water at the location and such ditching requirements as may be necessary. In approving the culvert installation, the county road superintendent may impose reasonable regulations, including but not limited to the following:
(1)
The manner of making the excavation or installation of culvert.
(2)
The location of excavation or installation.
(3)
Protection of underground utility installations.
(4)
Required safety precautions, such as barricades, lights, warning devices, etc.
(5)
The extent and size of the excavation and culvert.
(6)
Steps to be taken to protect nearby property owners and to ensure proper drainage.
(b)
The requirement of a culvert may be waived by the superintendent of roads on roads having a gradient of less than one percent at the point of entrance if, in his opinion, the lack of such a culvert would not interfere with adequate drainage of the county road and upstream lands.
(Ord. No. 147, § 3, 7-26-2001)
Sec. 70-128. - Approval; bond deposit or other security.
(a)
Upon approval of the driveway location and determination of the size and type of culvert required to be installed, the applicant shall have 30 days to install the driveway and culvert as approved by the superintendent of roads.
(b)
If required, the person installing a culvert shall furnish a bond, deposit or other security sufficient to ensure compliance with this article.
(c)
It shall be the duty of the person making any installation of culverts as authorized by this article to fill the excavation and restore the surface disturbed to a condition at least equal to the surface prior to the installation of the culvert. The restoration of the ditch area adjacent to the construction site including the removal of all debris and excess soil shall be considered part of the culvert installation.
(d)
The maintenance of the culvert shall be the responsibility of the property owners served. This maintenance shall include keeping the culvert pipe open and free of blockages.
(Ord. No. 147, § 4, 7-26-2001)
Sec. 70-129. - Completion of driveway.
The applicant shall notify the superintendent of roads upon completion of the driveway installation. At such time, the superintendent of roads shall make a final inspection.
(1)
If the driveway is not in accordance with the requirements of the superintendent of roads, the applicant shall correct or remove the installation within 15 days.
(2)
If the driveway meets all county requirements, the superintendent of roads shall notify the county building and zoning office to issue a driveway permit to the applicant.
(Ord. No. 147, § 5, 7-26-2001)
Sec. 70-130. - Permitted driveway entrance culverts.
Permitted driveway entrance culverts shall be in place prior to the issuance of a building permit for any structure built on or moved onto the parcel to which access is granted.
(Ord. No. 147, § 6, 7-26-2001)

ARTICLE V. - DISPLAY OF STREET ADDRESS NUMBERS

Sec. 70-141. - Display of street address numbers required.
(a)
Existing structures and locations. Every house, mobile home, apartment, commercial structure, location or other building or structure having a street address number assigned to it under the system of numbering in unincorporated Upson County shall display such number in a manner readily legible and visible from the adjacent road or street, fronting the property and in compliance with the requirements of this article.
Address numbers shall be reflective and contrast with their background. Address numbers shall be Arabic numerals or alphabet letters. Numbers shall be a minimum of three inches in height with a minimum stroke width of 0.5 inches. It is encouraged that these address numbers be a reflective white color, placed on a reflective blue background sign, as prescribed by Upson County. The numbers shall be visible to traffic approaching from either direction on the roadway.
(b)
New structures. Every new house, mobile home, apartment, commercial structure, location or other building or structure, permitted after the adoption of this article and having a street address number assigned to it under the system of numbering in unincorporated Upson County, shall display such number in a readily legible and visible manner from the adjacent road or street, fronting the property and in compliance with the requirements of this article.
Address numbers shall be a reflective white color, placed on a reflective blue background sign, as prescribed by Upson County. Address numbers shall be Arabic numerals or alphabet letters. Numbers shall be a minimum of three inches in height with a minimum stroke width of 0.5 inches. The numbers shall be visible to traffic approaching from either direction on the roadway.
(Ord. No. 225, § 1, 11-26-2013)
Sec. 70-142. - Method of display.
(a)
Residential. Upson County assigned street address numbers shall be affixed to street-side mailboxes or street-side posts or columns, provided that such signs are the ones required by Upson County as described in this article, and shall be located along the road right-of-way and not more than 20 feet from the intersection of the driveway to the premises with the curb line, pavement edge, or edge of travel way of the street in front of the property. Such street address numbers shall be placed such that they clearly indicate which driveway and structure they are associated with. Street-side address signs which are placed upon separate posts or columns must be placed outside the road right-of-way (ROW). Utility poles and phone pedestals can be used as a guide, indicating the location of the ROW boundary line. Where driveways or private roads split internally to different residences, additional address signage shall be provided, with guidance by the administrative officials. Residential complexes shall prioritize display of their street-side address(es) on any entrance signage, prior to placement on a separate mailbox, post or column. Where residential complexes have multiple numerical addresses, the entrance signage shall show the lowest and highest addresses in the range, separated by a dash.
Mobile home parks and apartment complexes, including duplex, triplex, quadruplex and similar dwelling structures, shall also be required to have each individual pad, lot number or apartment number displayed in a readily visible manner and in compliance with the requirements of this article. The lot, pad or apartment numbers shall be in sequence unless directional signs are provided for numbers not in sequence.
Additional assigned address numbers are encouraged to be placed on the primary structure or elsewhere on the property, where beneficial, in a position to be plainly legible and visible from the street or road fronting the property or from the driveway approaching the structure.
(b)
Commercial and other non-residential structures. Upson County assigned street address numbers shall be affixed to the primary street-side advertising sign and street-side facade of the primary commercial structure or other nonresidential structure, provided that such address signs and numbers are the ones required by Upson County as described in this article and shall be located at least seven feet above ground level, where possible. Directory signs, listing multiple businesses, shall show the lowest and highest addresses in the range, separated by a dash. Commercial or other non-residential structures which do not have a street-side facade shall affix address numbers at least seven feet above ground level on a wall, window or similar surface, facing the primary driveway or parking area approach, where they will be visible and unlikely to be obstructed. Where such commercial and other non-residential structures also have a stand-alone advertising sign or signs, the address numbers shall also be affixed to the primary advertising sign.
As with advertising signs, any separate street address signs must be placed outside the road right-of-way (ROW). Utility poles and phone pedestals can be used as a guide, indicating the location of the ROW boundary line.
(Ord. No. 225, § 2, 11-26-2013)
Sec. 70-143. - Size, condition of numbers.
Street address numbers shall be reflective and a minimum of three inches in height with a minimum stroke width of 0.5 inches. Address numbers shall be Arabic numerals or alphabet letters. For new construction, permitted after adoption of this article, all such address letters shall be reflective white and affixed to reflective blue signs which shall be at least six inches by 12 inches, six inches by 18 inches or another size authorized by Upson County. Street address numbers and signs to which they are affixed shall be maintained at all times in good condition and be readily legible and visible from the street by persons traveling along the street in each direction. The signs required by this article may be purchased from Upson County at the designated locations.
(Ord. No. 225, § 3, 11-26-2013)
Sec. 70-144. - Duty of owner; notification; maintenance.
The obligation of complying with the provisions of this article shall be upon the owner of the property. Property owners shall have until January 1, 2015, to comply with the provisions herein. Failure to comply within that time period shall constitute a violation of this article. Upon such violation, notice shall be given the owner of the property, or his renter, specifying the manner of such violation, and requiring that such violation be remedied and brought into compliance within ten days after receipt of such notice. Failure to comply within the ten days shall be punishable as provided herein.
All addresses and address signs shall be maintained and kept in good repair. It shall be the duty of the owner to ensure that damaged, missing and worn out or faded addresses and address signs are repaired or replaced, that the addresses remain visible and legible as required by this article.
(Ord. No. 225, § 4, 11-26-2013)
Sec. 70-145. - Enforcement.
The responsibility for making the inspections provided in this article, and for enforcement of the provisions herein, shall rest with the Upson County Building and Zoning Department and its successors and with the officer authorized, directed and empowered to enforce all provisions of the Code of Upson County.
Only in cases where the letter of the law cannot be met in a reasonable way or does not achieve the desired result, the designated official or his designee(s) may allow for the minimum variances from this article in order to still meet the article's intent. Records of these administrative variances shall be maintained by the department. An administrative variance application process may be used to fulfill this requirement.
(Ord. No. 225, § 5, 11-26-2013)
Sec. 70-146. - Violations.
(a)
Any person who shall do anything prohibited by this article as it now exists or as it may hereafter be amended, or who shall fail to do anything required by this article as it now exists or as it may hereafter be amended, is hereby declared to be in violation of this article and the pertinent regulations herein set forth.
(b)
Each and every day that any such violation exists shall be deemed a separate offense.
(c)
Any such violation, upon conviction thereof, as prescribed by the laws of Georgia, shall be punishable by a fine of $25.00 for the first offense, $50.00 for the second offense, and $100.00 for the third and each additional offense.
(d)
The imposition of any such fine or imprisonment, or both, or any violation shall not excuse the violation nor permit it to continue; and all such violators shall be required to correct or remedy such violations or defects within the time as prescribed by the court having jurisdiction of such matter, and, in the absence of any such completion time being fixed by the court, within a reasonable time after such violations occurs.
(e)
The application of any fine, imprisonment or other penalty shall not be construed to prevent the enforced removal of prohibited conditions or to postpone any action required.
(f)
The remedies herein set out for the purpose of enforcing the provisions of this article shall not be deemed to be exclusive, but shall be cumulative of all other remedies, civil or criminal, provided by the laws of Georgia, or by the ordinances of Upson County.
(Ord. No. 225, § 6, 11-26-2013)
