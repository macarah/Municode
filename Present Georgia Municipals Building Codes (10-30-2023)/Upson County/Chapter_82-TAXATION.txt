ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 82-1. - Sales and use tax.
There is authorized to be levied and collected within the county a sales and use tax in the amount of one percent on all sales and uses in the county as provided in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-110 et seq.

An official copy of the original resolution is on file in the office of the county clerk.

ARTICLE II. - TAX SALES[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Ad valorem taxation of property, O.C.G.A. § 48-5-1 et seq.

Sec. 82-26. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Interested party means:
(1)
Those parties having an interest in the property as revealed by a certification of title to the property conducted in accordance with the title standards of the State Bar of Georgia.
(2)
Those parties having filed a notice in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-3-9.
(3)
Any other party having an interest in the property whose identity and address are reasonably ascertainable from the records maintained by the tax commissioner, clerk of court, or other records maintained in the courthouse.
The term "interested party" shall not include the holder of the benefit or burden of any easement or right-of-way whose interest is properly recorded, which interest shall remain unaffected.
Redemption amount means the full amount of the delinquent ad valorem taxes, accrued interest at the rate specified in O.C.G.A. § 48-2-40, penalties as determined in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-2-44, and costs incurred by the county in collecting such taxes, including without limitation the cost of title examination and publication of notice.
(Ord. No. 85, § I, 1-12-1996)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 82-27. - Petition.
(a)
After an ad valorem tax lien based upon an approved digest has been delinquent for 12 months, the tax commissioner may commence tax foreclosure in accordance with this article.
(b)
The tax commissioner (referred to as "petitioner") may commence tax foreclosure by filing a petition in the superior court. The petition must include the following:
(1)
The identity of the petitioner and the name and address of the individual responsible for collection of the delinquent taxes;
(2)
The property address;
(3)
A description of the property;
(4)
The tax identification number of the property;
(5)
The calendar year for which the taxes are delinquent;
(6)
The principal amount of the delinquent taxes together with interest and penalties; and
(7)
The names and addresses of parties to whom copies of the petition are to be sent in accordance with subsection (c) of this section.
(c)
The petitioner shall mail copies of the petition by certified mail, return receipt requested, to all interested parties whose identity and address are reasonably ascertainable. Copies of the petition shall also be mailed by first class mail to the property address to the attention of the occupants, if any, and shall be posted on the property.
(d)
Simultaneous with the filing of the petition, the petitioner shall file a lis pendens notice at the office of the clerk of the superior court.
(e)
Within 30 days of filing the petition, a notice shall be published twice in the official organ of the county. The notice shall specify the following:
(1)
The identity of the petitioner and the name and address of the individual responsible for collecting the delinquent taxes.
(2)
The property address.
(3)
A description of the property.
(4)
The tax identification number of the property.
(5)
The applicable period of tax delinquency.
(6)
The principal amount of the delinquent taxes together with interest and penalties.
(7)
The date and place of filing the petition.
(Ord. No. 85, § II, 1-12-1996)
Sec. 82-28. - Hearing.
(a)
The petitioner shall request a judicial hearing not earlier than 30 days after the filing of the petition as provided in section 82-27. At such hearing any interested party has the right to be heard and to contest the delinquency of the taxes or the adequacy of the proceedings. If the superior court determines that the information set forth in the petition is accurate, the court may render its judgment and issue an order finding and ordering as follows:
(1)
The taxes are delinquent.
(2)
Proper notice has been given to all interested parties.
(3)
The property as described in the petition shall be sold in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-4-75 et seq.
(4)
The property shall be sold free and clear of all liens, claims and encumbrances other than the following:
a.
Rights of redemption provided under federal law;
b.
Tax liens held by state governmental entities other than the petitioner which are superior to the ad valorem taxes identified in the petition by virtue of O.C.G.A. § 48-2-56;
c.
The holders of easements and rights-of-way described in O.C.G.A. § 48-4-77(1)(c); and
d.
The holders of the benefits or burdens of any real covenants filed of record as of the date of filing of the petition.
(5)
The sale shall become final and binding 60 days after the date of the sale in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-4-81.
(b)
If, upon production of evidence, the court determines that any interested party died within the six-month period of time immediately preceding the filing of the petition, the court may postpone the hearing for a period up to six months, to allow the administrator or executor adequate time to close the estate.
(Ord. No. 85, § III, 1-12-1996)
Sec. 82-29. - Sale.
(a)
At any time prior to sale as provided in this article, any interested party may redeem the property by payment of the redemption amount to the petitioner, whereupon the petitioner shall dismiss the proceedings.
(b)
Following the hearing and order of the court in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-4-79, a sale of the property shall be advertised and conducted on the date, time, place and manner which are required by law of sheriff's sales. Such sale shall not occur earlier than 45 days following the date of the order of the superior court.
(c)
Except as otherwise authorized by law, the minimum bid price shall be the redemption amount. In the absence of a higher bid, the petitioner may, but is not obligated to, tender its own bid in an amount equal to the minimum bid price and thereby become the purchaser at the sale. From and after the moment of the sale and tender of the successful bid price, the sale shall be final and binding, subject only to the right of the owner of the property to redeem the property upon payment into the superior court of the full amount of the minimum bid price. Such right of redemption of the owner shall exist for a period of 60 days from and after the date of the sale and shall be in accordance with the following:
(1)
Redemption by an owner shall result in a dismissal of the proceedings, and the petitioner shall refund to the purchaser the full amount paid by the purchaser at the sale;
(2)
For purposes of redemption, the term "owner" shall mean the owner of record of fee simple interest in the property as of the date of filing the petition, together with such owner's successors in interest by death or operation of law. This right of redemption shall not otherwise be transferable; and
(3)
This right of redemption shall automatically terminate and expire upon failure to redeem within the 60-day period after the date of the sale.
(d)
If the property is not redeemed in accordance with subsection (c) of this section, within 90 days after the date of the sale the petitioner shall cause to be executed and delivered to the purchaser a tax deed, as described in O.C.G.A. § 48-4-81, together with such real estate transfer tax forms as may be required by law.
(e)
Within 90 days after the sale, the petitioner shall file a report of the sale with the court, stating whether a sale took place, the foreclosure sale price, and the identity of the purchaser.
(f)
If the sale price exceeds the minimum bid amount, the petitioner shall deposit the surplus into the registry of the court. Such surplus shall be distributed by the court to the interested parties, including the owner, as their interests appear and in the priority in which their interests exist.
(Ord. No. 85, § IV, 1-12-1996)

ARTICLE III. - HOTEL-MOTEL EXCISE TAX[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
Cross reference— Businesses, ch. 26.
State Law reference— Local excise taxes on rooms, lodgings and accommodations, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-50 et seq.

Sec. 82-56. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Lodgings means any rooms, lodgings, or accommodations furnished by any person licensed by or required to pay business or occupation taxes to the county for operating a hotel, motel, inn, lodge, tourist camp, tourist cabin, or any other place in which rooms, lodgings, or accommodations are regularly furnished for value for use by the public. The term "lodgings" does not include an accommodation which a person occupies or has the right to occupy for a period of more than ten consecutive days or for use as meeting rooms.
Operator means each person furnishing lodgings.
(Ord. No. 106, § 1, 7-10-1997)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 82-57. - Imposed; exemptions; allowance for reporting and payment.
(a)
There is hereby imposed an excise tax upon each operator, which tax shall be at the rate of five percent of the charge to the public for the furnishing of lodgings within the county. The tax shall be due from and remitted by each operator regardless of whether the tax is collected from the lodging public.
(b)
The tax hereby levied shall not be imposed upon any lodgings exempted from taxation by the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-50 et seq.
(c)
Each operator shall be allowed to retain three percent of the amount due as an allowance for reporting and paying the tax by its due date; no allowance shall be retained on any taxes that are delinquent.
(Ord. No. 106, § 2, 7-10-1997)
Sec. 82-58. - Collection.
The tax levied in this article may, at the option of the operator, be charged to the person using the lodgings and added to the other charges made therefor. If the tax is charged to the user of the lodgings, each such person shall be given a bill, invoice, receipt or other statement or memorandum of the price, charge or rent payable upon which the lodgings tax shall be stated, charged and shown separately. When the tax is collected from the person using the lodgings, the operator shall hold it as trustee for and on behalf of the county until it is paid to the county as required by this article.
(Ord. No. 106, § 3, 7-10-1997)
Sec. 82-59. - Filing of return; payment; penalty for nonpayment.
(a)
Every operator shall file with the county a sworn lodgings tax return not later than the 15th day of each month, on a form to be prescribed by the county, which return shall set forth the gross receipts from lodgings during the immediately preceding month, the amount thereof claimed to be exempt from the tax levied by this article, the amount of tax due the county, the amount of allowance claimed by the operator for reporting and paying the tax, and the net tax due. Each operator shall pay to the county, on the date such return is due, the amount of tax then due under this article.
(b)
There is hereby levied a penalty, at the rate of two percent per month or fraction thereof, on any portion of the tax that is not paid when due, and, in addition, the allowance to the operator for collecting the tax shall be forfeited as provided in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-52.
(Ord. No. 106, § 4, 7-10-1997)
Sec. 82-60. - Records required.
Every operator shall keep books and records showing the gross receipts from lodgings taxable under this article, including full information demonstrating any exemptions claimed on the returns made to the county. The county manager and the employees of that office shall have the right to inspect the books or records of any operator. Upon demand of the county manager, such books or records, specifically including a copy of the sales tax return filed by the operator with the state, shall be submitted for inspection by a representative of the county within 30 days. Failure of submission of such books or records within 30 days shall constitute a violation of this article, shall be grounds for revocation of the tax registration and license of such business to do business in the county, and shall authorize the county to institute legal actions against the operator for the discovery of the amount and the recovery of the tax due under this article. Records shall be kept by each operator for examination by the county manager, at that officer's discretion, to determine the amount of tax due under this article.
(Ord. No. 106, § 5, 7-10-1997)
Sec. 82-61. - Failure to pay.
If any operator fails to pay the tax or the penalties levied by this article, the county manager shall give written notice, by certified mail with return receipt, to the operator of such failure. The notice shall set forth the time periods for which the taxes levied by this article have not been paid and shall inform the operator of a time at which the operator shall appear before the county manager to respond to such notice. If the issue is not thereby resolved by the payment of any taxes and penalties determined to be due, the county manager shall suspend the license issued to such operation to conduct a lodgings business in the county until such time as all taxes due under this article have been paid.
(Ord. No. 106, § 6, 7-10-1997)
Sec. 82-62. - Proceeds.
The proceeds resulting from the imposition of the tax under this article, including penalties, shall be expended in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(a)(3).
(Ord. No. 106, § 7, 7-10-1997; Ord. No. 174, §§ 1, 2, 6-9-2005)
Sec. 82-63. - Penalties.
Any operator or agent of an operator who fails to file the return of taxes due under this article or who knowingly files a false return or who collects any such tax due from his patrons and fails to pay the tax to the county shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-11.
(Ord. No. 106, § 8, 7-10-1997)

State Law reference— Freeport exemption, O.C.G.A. § 48-5-48.2.

Sec. 82-91. - Enumeration of exempt tangible personal property.
There shall be exempt from county ad valorem taxation 100 percent of the following described tangible personal property:
(1)
Inventory of goods in the process of manufacture or production which shall include all partly finished goods and raw materials held for direct use or consumption in the ordinary course of the taxpayer's manufacturing or production business in this state. This exemption shall apply only to tangible personal property which is substantially modified, altered, or changed in the ordinary course of the taxpayer's manufacturing, processing, or production operations in this state.
(2)
Inventory of finished goods manufactured or produced within this state in the ordinary course of the taxpayer's manufacturing or production business when held by the original manufacturer or producer of such finished goods. This exemption shall be for a period not exceeding 12 months from the date such property is produced or manufactured.
(3)
Inventory of finished goods which, on January 1, are stored in a warehouse, dock, or wharf, whether public or private, and which are destined for shipment to a final destination outside this state and inventory of finished goods which are shipped into this state from outside this state and stored for transshipment to a final destination outside this state. The exemption provided for in this subsection shall be for a period not exceeding 12 months from the date such property is stored in this state.

ARTICLE VI. - BUSINESS LICENSE TAX (DEPOSITORY FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS)

Sec. 82-141. - Business license tax required on depository financial institutions.
For the year 2017 and succeeding years thereafter, any depository financial institutions having an office located in Upson County shall, in accordance with the Official Code of Georgia Annotated (O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93), pay an annual business license tax at a rate of .25 percent of the gross receipts of such depository financial institution.
( Ord. No. 259 , § 1, 10-10-2017)
Sec. 82-142. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the term:
Gross receipts shall mean gross receipts as defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.
Depository financial institutions shall mean state and national banks, state building and loan associations, and federal savings and loan associations.
( Ord. No. 259 , § 2, 10-10-2017)
Sec. 82-143. - Minimum Tax.
The minimum annual amount of business license tax due from any depository financial institution pursuant to Section 1 herein shall be $1,000.00.
( Ord. No. 259 , § 3, 10-10-2017)
State Law reference— Minimum license tax authorized IAW O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(a).
Sec. 82-144. - Filing of return.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(c), each depository financial institution subject to the tax imposed hereunder shall file a return of its gross receipts with the county manager's office on March of the year following the year in which such gross receipts are measured. The return shall be in the manner and in the form prescribed by the commissioners of the State of Georgia Department of Revenue based on the allocation method set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(d). The County Manager's office shall assess and collect the tax pursuant to this section based upon the information provided in the return. Taxes pursuant to this section shall be due no later than 30 days after filing of the return as prescribed herein, unless such period is extended by the board of commissioners.
( Ord. No. 259 , § 4, 10-10-2017)
Sec. 82-145. - Conflicts between specific and general provisions.
Where there is an apparent conflict in this article between specific and general provisions, it is the intention hereof that the specific shall control.
( Ord. No. 259 , § 5, 10-10-2017)
