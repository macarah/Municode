ARTICLE IV. - BILLIARDS OR POOL ROOMS

Sec. 50-121. - License requirement.
(a)
Each person keeping, operating or maintaining a billiard table, pool table or any other table of like character within the corporate limits of the city for use by the public shall be required to obtain a license from the city clerk in the manner specified in this article. Said license shall be classified as a "premises where alcoholic beverages will be sold, consumed used or where patrons have consumed alcoholic beverages" or a "premises where sale of alcoholic beverages, the use thereof, consumption or possession is prohibited."
(b)
As a prerequisite to the issuance of the license, the applicant shall visit the Tattnall County Sheriff Department for fingerprinting. The sheriff department will process the fingerprints through Georgia Crime Information Center (GCIC) for determination of any past criminal activity. The sheriff department will provide the city results of investigation.
(c)
The chief of police will review the Georgia Crime Information Center's background report of the applicant and provide recommendations to the mayor and council.
(d)
After its final investigation, the mayor and city council shall grant or deny the license applied for.
(Ord. of 3-3-1992, § 1; Res. No. 10-41, 7-13-2010; Res. No. 11-01, 8-3-2010)
Sec. 50-122. - Fees established.
The annual business license fee for each operator of a billiard or pool room in the city shall be an amount on file in the clerk's office for the first table, and an amount on file in the clerk's office for each additional table.
(Ord. of 3-3-1992, § 2)
Sec. 50-123. - Hours of operation.
(a)
Premises where alcoholic beverages are sold, consumed, used or where patrons have consumed alcoholic beverages. It shall be unlawful for the owner, operator or manager of any billiard or pool room in the city to permit such establishment to be open for business between the hours of 12:00 midnight through 6:00 a.m., Monday through Saturday, and from 12:00 midnight Saturday through 6:00 a.m. Monday.
(b)
Premises where sale of alcoholic beverages, the use thereof, consumption or possession is prohibited. It shall be unlawful for the owner, operator or manager of any billiard or pool room in the city to permit such establishment to be open for business between the hours of 11:00 p.m. and 8:00 a.m., Monday through Thursday, between the hours of 12:00 midnight and 8:00 a.m., Friday through Saturday, and between the hours of 12:00 midnight Saturday through 8:00 a.m. Monday. Should the owner of any establishment allow patrons to consume, use or possess alcoholic beverages on said premises he shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and subject to a fine up to $500.00 or six months in jail. This subsection shall likewise apply to controlled substances and shall also include patrons who have consumed alcohol or controlled substances away from said premises but while upon said premises have alcohol or controlled substances within their system. It being the intent of this licensing classification to provide recreation facilities for the youth of the city and it is declared by this article to be detrimental to their health and welfare to be in business establishments where patrons are either consuming, using or in possession of alcoholic beverages or controlled substances or have the same within their system.
(Ord. of 3-3-1992, § 3)
Sec. 50-124. - Minors.
It shall be unlawful for any person having charge or control of any pool room or billiard room open to the public for which alcoholic beverages are sold, consumed, used or where patrons have consumed alcoholic beverages to permit into such room any individual under the age of 21 years, whether for the purpose of playing billiards or pool or not.
(Ord. of 3-3-1992, § 4)
Sec. 50-125. - Clear view of premises required.
The owner, operator or manager of any billiard or pool room in the city shall not permit to be used on such premises any screens, shades, partitions or other devices of like character which shall have the effect of obstructing the view through the windows or doors of the place where the billiard or pool tables are kept.
(Ord. of 3-3-1992, § 5)
Sec. 50-126. - Doors.
The doors of a billiard or pool room licensed under this article shall be kept unlocked whenever the tables are in use, or when any person other than the proprietor or his agent is present in such place.
(Ord. of 3-3-1992, § 6)
Sec. 50-127. - Gambling.
It shall be unlawful for the owner, operator or manager of any billiard or pool room open for public use to allow gambling of any kind to occur upon such premises.
(Ord. of 3-3-1992, § 7)
