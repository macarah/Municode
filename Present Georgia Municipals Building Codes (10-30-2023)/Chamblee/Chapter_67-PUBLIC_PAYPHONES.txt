Chapter 67 - PUBLIC PAYPHONES

Sec. 67-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Ground wire specification means the listing of the conductor(s) used to connect the grounding electrode to the equipment-grounding conductor, to the grounding conductor, or to both, of the circuit at the service equipment or the source of a separately derived system.
Illuminated, direct means a public payphone structure illuminated by an internal light source.
Mast specifications means the measurements of the vertical structure rising from the public payphone housing that supports any wiring and rigging.
Permits and inspections director means the permits and inspections director of the city or his designated representative.
Public payphone means any telephone, coin operated or coinless, and its accessory structure, that is intended for the purpose of public usage, situated upon or attached to real property.
Public service commission means the Georgia Public Service Commission, regulating agency for all coin/coinless operated telephones within the State of Georgia.
Voltage use means the total voltage range used, measured in both watts and amps, to power the public payphone and any accessory structure.
(Ord. No. 444, § 1, 8-19-97)
Sec. 67-2. - Penalty for violation of chapter.
A violation of this chapter shall be punishable, upon conviction, as provided by section 1-6 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 444, § 1, 8-19-97)
Sec. 67-3. - Remedies.
In case any public payphone or related structure covered by this chapter is or is proposed to be erected, constructed, altered, converted or used in violation of any provision of this chapter, the permits and inspections director may, in addition to other remedies, and after due notice to the appropriate person, issue a citation for violation of this Code requiring the presence of the violator in municipal court, or institute injunction or other appropriate action or proceeding to prevent such unlawful erection, construction, alteration, conversion or use or to correct or abate such violation.
(Ord. No. 444, § 1, 8-19-97)
Sec. 67-4. - Exemptions.
The following types of public payphones are exempt from the provisions of this chapter:
(1)
Any public payphone located inside a business, office, or mall.
(2)
Any public payphone that contracts with a government agency.
(Ord. No. 444, § 1, 8-19-97)
Sec. 67-5. - Authority of the permits and inspections director.
This chapter shall be administered and enforced by the permits and inspections director or his designee.
(Ord. No. 444, § 1, 8-19-97)
Sec. 67-6. - Permit required.
Except as specifically excluded from the provisions of this chapter, it shall be unlawful for any person to erect or attach any public payphone in the city without first having obtained a public payphone permit.
(Ord. No. 444, § 1, 8-19-97)
Sec. 67-7. - Application for permit.
(a)
Applications for public payphone permits required by section 67-6 shall be filed by the payphone owner or his agent in the office of the permits and inspections director upon forms furnished by this office. The application shall describe and set forth the following:
(1)
The street address of the property upon which the subject public payphone is to be located, and the proposed location of the subject public payphone on the subject property. In the absence of a street address, a method of location acceptable to the permits and inspections director shall be used;
(2)
The name and address of the owner of the real property upon which the subject public payphone is to be located;
(3)
The written consent of the owner or his agent granting permission for the placement or maintenance of the subject public payphone;
(4)
A listing of the voltage use, mast specifications and ground wire specifications;
(5)
The name, address, telephone number, occupation tax certificate number, and public service commission certificate number of the public payphone company installing the subject public payphone;
(6)
The number of public payphones, if any, already located at the requested street address;
(b)
All applicants requesting direct illuminated public payphones must obtain an electrical permit.
(Ord. No. 444, § 1, 8-19-97)
Sec. 67-8. - Permit fees; registration of existing public payphones.
(a)
No public payphone permit shall be issued until the appropriate application has been filed with the permits and inspections director and fees have been paid as provided in this section. All public payphones defined or otherwise covered under this chapter shall require a permit. The fee for such permits shall be a registration/inspection fee of $25.00. The permit fee as provided in this section shall be payable only with the initial application for a public payphone permit.
(b)
Each public payphone, coin-operated or otherwise, which exists on the effective date of the ordinance from which this section is derived shall be registered by the owner with the permits and inspections director within 90 days of such effective date. Any public payphone, coin-operated or otherwise, which exists on the effective date of the ordinance from which this section is derived shall not be subject to payment of public payphone permit fees as set forth in this section.
(Ord. No. 444, § 1, 8-19-97)
Sec. 67-9. - Public payphone identification labels.
(a)
With each public payphone permit, the permits and inspections director shall issue a sticker bearing the same number as the permit with which it is issued. It shall be the duty of the permittee or his agent to affix such sticker to the side of the payphone structure so it will be easily seen. The absence of a proper sticker shall be prima facie evidence that the public payphone has been or is being erected or operated in violation of the provisions of this chapter.
(b)
The permits and inspections director shall inspect all existing public payphones in the city to determine if such structures conform to the provisions of this chapter. Identification stickers shall be provided for all public payphones in order to identify existing conforming and nonconforming signs.
(Ord. No. 444, § 1, 8-19-97)
Sec. 67-10. - Placement in right-of-way; creation of traffic hazard.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to erect, place or maintain within the right-of-way of any public road in the city any public payphone.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to erect, place or maintain in a place or position visible from any public road any unauthorized public payphone which:
(1)
Obstructs a clear view from any public road to any other portion of such public road, to an intersecting or adjoining public road, or to property abutting such public road in such a manner as to constitute a hazard to traffic on such roads;
(2)
Because of its nature, construction or operation, constitutes a dangerous distraction to or interferes with the vision of drivers of motor vehicles.
(c)
Any public payphone structure erected, placed or maintained on the right-of-way of any public road in violation of subsection (a) or (b), above, is declared to be a public nuisance, and the permits and inspections department may remove or direct the removal of the public payphone and its structure. Where any public payphone structure is erected, placed or maintained in violation of subsection (b), above, but not on the right-of-way of any public road, the permits and inspections department may order the removal of such structure by written notice to the owner of the structure or the owner of the land on which the structure is located. If such public payphone structure is not removed within 30 days after the giving of such order of removal, officials of the permits and inspections department are authorized to remove or cause to be removed such structure and to submit a statement of expenses incurred in the removal to the owner of the structure or to the owner of the land on which the structure is located. If payment or arrangement to make payment is not made within 60 days after the receipt of such statement, the department shall certify the amount thereof for collection to the city manager.
(Ord. No. 444, § 1, 8-19-97)
Sec. 67-11. - Maintenance.
(a)
All public payphones and their corresponding structures shall be maintained in good condition so as to present a neat and orderly appearance. The permits and inspections director may cause any public payphone to be removed, after due notice, if the public payphone or its corresponding structure shows gross neglect or becomes dilapidated.
(b)
The permits and inspections director will give the owner of the structure ten days' written notice to correct the deficiencies or to remove the public payphone structure. If the owner of the structure refuses to correct the deficiencies or remove the public payphone structure, the permits and inspections director shall have the structure removed at the expense of the owner.
(Ord. No. 444, § 1, 8-19-97)
