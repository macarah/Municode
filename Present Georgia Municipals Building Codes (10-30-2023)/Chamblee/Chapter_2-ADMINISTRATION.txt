ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 2-1. - Official organ.
Pursuant to article 1, section 44, of the city Charter, the Champion Newspaper is hereby designated as the official organ for the city.
(Code 1982, § 2-4-7; Ord. No. 451, § 1, 4-21-98)
Sec. 2-2. - Administering of oath of office.
Before any public officer takes office in the city government, he or she shall take the oath of office before the mayor or an officer of the state authorized to administer oaths, as required by Georgia law. Said oath, shall be written or printed, and when executed, filed with the city clerk.
(Ord. No. 816 , 12-20-22)

DIVISION 2. - MEETINGS[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Meetings to be open to public, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1; due notice requirements for other than regular meetings, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1(d); excluded proceedings, O.C.G.A. § 50-14-3.

Sec. 2-46. - Time and place of regular meetings.
The time and place of regular meetings of the mayor and council shall be scheduled by resolution. If any meeting date scheduled by such resolution falls on a federal or state holiday, the council may reschedule the meeting and cause to be published the date change.
(Code 1982, § 2-1-11; Ord. No. 406, 10-13-95; Ord. No. 792 , 2-16-21; Ord. No. 810 , 5-17-22)
Sec. 2-47. - Adjourned meetings.
If a quorum shall fail to attend any regular or special meeting of the mayor and council or if for any reason any meeting shall fail to complete transaction of the business before the meeting, the meeting may be adjourned to any date prior to the next regular meeting agreed upon by a majority of the members present.
(Code 1982, § 2-1-12)
Sec. 2-48. - Agenda; citizen appearances, agenda for special called meetings.
(a)
The agenda for regular meetings of the mayor and council shall be prepared on or before 12:00 p.m. on the Thursday immediately preceding the date of the monthly work session of the mayor and council. Persons desiring to bring matters before the council shall notify the city clerk by 12:00 p.m. on said Thursday in order to include such matters on the agenda for consideration by the mayor and council.
(b)
The agenda for special called meetings of the mayor and council shall be limited to those matters for which the special meeting was originally called and no further matter may be added to the agenda for consideration by the mayor and council unless there is an affirmative vote of two-thirds of those members of the council present at the special called meeting to add a matter not originally contemplated for consideration.
(Code 1982, § 2-1-13; Ord. No. 406, 10-13-95; Ord. No. 535, pt. I, 5-18-04; Ord. No. 547, 5-16-06)
Sec. 2-49. - Rules of procedure.
Except as otherwise provided by ordinance, the procedures of the mayor and council shall be governed by Robert's Rules of Order, Newly Revised.
(Code 1982, § 2-1-14)
Secs. 2-50—2-65. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - CITY OFFICIALS[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— City's power to define, regulate and alter the powers, duties, qualifications, compensation and tenure of all municipal officers, agents and employees, O.C.G.A. § 36-34-2(2).

Sec. 2-66. - Contracts or business transactions between city officials and city.
It shall be unlawful for any city official to enter into any contract or conduct any business transaction with the city or any department or subdivision thereof, except with the express approval of the mayor and council.
(Code 1982, § 2-4-5)
Sec. 2-67. - Duties of city clerk.
In addition to the duties of the city clerk under the city Charter, the city clerk shall perform the following duties:
(1)
Be the custodian of the city seal and affix its impression on documents whenever required;
(2)
Preserve the codes, records and documents belonging to the city and maintain a proper index to all records and documents;
(3)
Perform any other duties as may be required by the mayor and council.
(Code 1982, § 2-4-1; Ord. No. 747 , pt. I, 3-20-18)
Sec. 2-68. - Appointment and duties of city attorney.
(a)
The city attorney shall be appointed by the mayor and council at their first regular meeting of each year.
(b)
The city attorney shall be the legal advisor and representative of the city and in that capacity shall:
(1)
Prepare ordinances when so requested by the mayor and council;
(2)
Prepare for execution all contracts and instruments to which the city is a party when so requested, and approve, as to form, all ordinances, bonds and city contracts; and
(3)
Render any other legal services as may be required by the mayor and council.
(Code 1982, § 2-4-2)
Secs. 2-69—2-85. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - RECORDS MANAGEMENT[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— Records management programs for local governments, O.C.G.A. § 50-18-99.

Sec. 2-86. - Administrative responsibilities.
The mayor or his designee is responsible for the administration of the records management program, and the city clerk or his designee is designated as records management officer. It is the duty of the records committee to review and recommend approval of records retention schedules to the state records committee. Records retention schedules will be based on a review of the administrative, legal, fiscal and historical values of the records. The records management officer shall serve as chairman of the records committee. Retention schedules will be implemented following final approval by the mayor and council.
(Code 1982, § 2-4-21)
Sec. 2-87. - Archival depository.
The city records storage center will serve as an archival depository for city records determined to have permanent value.
(Code 1982, § 2-4-22)
Sec. 2-88. - Records center.
A centralized records center shall be established, and all city records not required for day-to-day office operation shall be transferred to the records center until destroyed in accordance with the approved retention schedule.
(Code 1982, § 2-4-23)
Sec. 2-89. - Retention schedule.
The city hereby adopts the records retention schedule for municipal governments as approved by the state records committee. A copy of such schedule is on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1982, § 2-4-24)
Sec. 2-90. - Compliance with article.
No city record shall be altered or destroyed except in accordance with the provisions of this article.
(Code 1982, § 2-4-25)
Secs. 2-91—2-105. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - FINANCE[5]

Footnotes:
--- (5) ---
State Law reference— Local government budgets and audits, O.C.G.A. § 36-81-1 et seq.; accounting for public funds, O.C.G.A. § 45-8-1 et seq.

Sec. 2-106. - Fiscal year.
The fiscal year of the city shall commence on January 1 and end on December 31 of each year.
(Code 1982, § 3-1-1)
State Law reference— Requirement for governing authority to establish a fiscal year, O.C.G.A. § 36-81-3.
Sec. 2-107. - Appointment of auditors.
The auditors for the city shall be appointed by the mayor and council at their first regular meeting of each year.
(Code 1982, § 2-4-3)
Sec. 2-108. - Annual budget; appropriations.
(a)
An annual budget and an appropriations resolution shall be adopted by the mayor and council prior to the first day of the fiscal year. However, if for good and sufficient reasons the budget cannot be adopted by the first day of the fiscal year, the budget shall be adopted not later than 45 days subsequent to the beginning of the fiscal year. If the budget and the appropriations resolution are not adopted prior to the beginning of the fiscal year, this section automatically authorizes the continuation of necessary and essential expenditures to operate the city. The budget as adopted shall be a balanced budget with anticipated revenues, including appropriated unencumbered surplus, equal to appropriated expenditures.
(b)
The city manager shall administer the budget, being responsible to the council for same, and may authorize the transfer of appropriated funds within and between departments with the approval of council as necessary to achieve the goals of the budget.
(Code 1982, § 3-1-2; Ord. No. 369, § 3-1-2, 12-11-92; Ord. No. 372, § 3-1-2, 4-9-93)
Sec. 2-109. - Fee schedule.
A record of all license fees, business and occupation taxes, fees and service charges established and required by the city for use or purchase of city services and for permits and licenses and any other regulatory or administrative fees or charges imposed by the city shall be kept and maintained by the city clerk and shall be available for public inspection during regular office hours.
(Code 1982, § 2-4-6)
Sec. 2-110. - Sanitary service fee.
(a)
Levy. There is hereby levied and assessed on each and every dwelling and business place and on each separate unit occupied as a dwelling or a place of business in the city a sanitary service fee in an amount as established from time to time by the mayor and council.
(b)
Date of payment. The sanitary service fee shall be due and payable on or before November 20 of each year.
(Code 1982, § 3-1-4; Ord. No. 596, pt. I, 2-17-09)
Sec. 2-111. - Purchasing.
Purchases shall conform to all requirements and procedures as established from time to time by the mayor and council.
(Code 1982, §§ 3-1-3(b), (c); Ord. No. 369, §§ 3-1-3(b), (c), 12-11-92; Ord. No. 632, pt. I, 4-19-11; Ord. No. 747 , pt. II, 3-20-18)
Sec. 2-112. - Provisions saved from repeal.
The provisions of the 1982 Code identified as subsection 3-1-3(a) on the subject of purchasing are not repealed and shall continue in full force and effect pending a revision of the subject matter by the city.
Sec. 2-113. - Sale or disposition of surplus, obsolete, unused or unserviceable property.
Whenever the city council shall determine that property of the city has become surplus, obsolete, unused or unserviceable, such property may be sold or otherwise disposed of by order of the city council, and an entry of the same shall be made in the inventory books of the city; and the money received therefrom shall be paid into the general fund of the city. The sale of any such property shall be conducted under the same provisions as set forth in sections 50-24, 50-25 and 50-26. For purposes of this section, property becomes unserviceable where such property cannot be beneficially or advantageously used under all circumstances.
(Ord. No. 450, § 2, 3-17-98)
