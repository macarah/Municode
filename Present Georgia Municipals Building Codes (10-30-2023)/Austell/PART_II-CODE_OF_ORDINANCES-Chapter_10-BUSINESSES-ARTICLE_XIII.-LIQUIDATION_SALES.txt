ARTICLE XIII. - LIQUIDATION SALES

Sec. 10-536. - Special license required.
(a)
No person in the city shall advertise, notice or hold out by advertising in newspapers, magazines, periodicals, billboards, radio, television, handbills, or in any other manner whatsoever that any sale of goods, wares or merchandise is a liquidation sale, including, but not limited to, an insurance, bankrupt, sell-out, bankruptcy, bankrupt stock, mortgage, mortgagee's, insolvent, assignee's, executor's, administrator's, receiver's, trustee's, pledgee's, lease-expiring, removal, retiring, quitting, liquidating, closing-out sale, going-out-of-business sale or other designation of similar name or import within the city, unless and until he shall first have obtained a special license to conduct the sale from the community affairs department as herein provided.
(b)
The provisions of this section shall apply to all such sales whether conducted by the owner, his agent, a consignee or otherwise.
(Code 1985, § 6-160; Ord. of 6-1-1987)
Sec. 10-537. - Application and inventory.
At least ten days prior to the commencement of any sale set forth in section 10-536, unless waived by the community affairs department, the person intending to hold the sale shall submit to the community affairs department an application in writing stating the reasons for the sale, an inventory of all goods, wares or merchandise on the premises where the sale is to take place, purchase orders showing the order date and the inventory and any other reasonable details required by the community affairs department as necessary to fully identify the goods, wares or merchandise to be sold at the sale.
(Code 1985, § 6-161; Ord. of 6-1-1987)
Sec. 10-538. - Special license fee.
(a)
The license fee for sales covered by this article shall be as provided in the fee schedule of the issuing department. The license shall be in addition to any other license fee or business tax being paid by the person to the city.
(b)
The application shall specify the period of time during which the sale shall continue. This time shall in no case exceed 90 days. A new application and special license shall be procured for each 90-day sale period or portion thereof. No extension shall be made for going-out-of-business sales unless approved by the council.
(Code 1985, § 6-162; Ord. of 6-1-1987)
Sec. 10-539. - Inspection of applications restricted.
The community affairs department shall file these applications and shall endorse on each application the date the special license is granted, and when it expires, and shall notify the city manager or his designee of the date of the expiration of the license.
(Code 1985, § 6-163; Ord. of 6-1-1987)
Sec. 10-540. - False statements prohibited.
No person shall make any false statement in any application required by this article or in any advertisement or notice of any sales of goods, wares or merchandise covered by this article.
(Code 1985, § 6-164; Ord. of 6-1-1987)
Sec. 10-541. - Scope of license.
The licenses issued under the provisions of this article shall be valid only for the inventoried goods, wares or merchandise as provided in section 10-537, and items ordered before obtaining the license required hereunder and items previously ordered, but on back order with the supplier. Additions to such inventory shall cause the goods, wares or merchandise so inventoried to lose their identity, and any such license issued shall be void. The licensee shall retain purchase orders on-site for inspection to determine compliance with this article; failure to do so shall be grounds for the city to revoke the license.
(Code 1985, § 6-165; Ord. of 6-1-1987)
Sec. 10-542. - Change in ownership.
If there shall be any change in ownership of the goods, wares or merchandise so inventoried, advertised or noticed for sale, notice thereof shall immediately be given in writing to the community affairs department, and a new license obtained as provided in sections 10-536 and 10-537.
(Code 1985, § 6-166; Ord. of 6-1-1987)
Sec. 10-543. - Advertising.
Only those goods, wares or merchandise set out in the inventory provided in section 10-537 shall appear in any advertising of the sale. No such advertising, either directly or indirectly, shall imply that the sale is being held with the approval of any officer or employee of the city.
(Code 1985, § 6-167; Ord. of 6-1-1987)
Sec. 10-544. - Sale of regular stock.
Nothing contained herein shall prevent a merchant from selling his regular stock in trade at any sale mentioned in section 10-536, the inventory of which would not cover his regular stock in trade, provided that the regular stock in trade is separated from the inventoried sale stock, and clearly designated as not being part of the goods, wares or merchandise as advertised in the sale notice.
(Code 1985, § 6-168; Ord. of 6-1-1987)
Sec. 10-545. - Exempt sales.
This article shall not apply to public or court officers, or persons acting under the directions of a city, state or federal court, and pawnbrokers selling unredeemed pledges in accordance with the law.
(Code 1985, § 6-169; Ord. of 6-1-1987)
Sec. 10-546. - Expiration of license.
At the end of the 90-day period specified in the special license issued hereunder, the special business license and the regular business license of any licensee will stand revoked.
(Code 1985, § 6-170; Ord. of 6-1-1987)
Sec. 10-547. - Intent.
The intent of the foregoing sections is to promote and preserve public morals and welfare, economic integrity and fair dealing, and to prevent fraud, deceit and dishonesty, by regulating the conduct of the type sales set out in section 10-537.
(Code 1985, § 6-171; Ord. of 6-1-1987)
Secs. 10-548—10-572. - Reserved.
