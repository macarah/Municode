ARTICLE XIV. - PAWNBROKERS[9]

Footnotes:
--- (9) ---
State Law reference— Pawnbrokers, O.C.G.A. § 44-2-130 et seq.; authority for local licensing, O.C.G.A. § 44-12-135.

Sec. 10-573. - License required.
Each pawnbroker doing business within the city shall be required to obtain a license from the community affairs department in the manner specified in this article.
(Code 1976, § 7-3061; Code 1985, § 6-80)
Sec. 10-574. - Bond, regulatory fee.
No pawnbroker's license shall be issued to any applicant unless such applicant shall post a bond in an amount up to $10,000.00, as set by the city, using as criteria for the amount: the permanent residence of the applicant; the length of time applicant has engaged in such a business; applicant's business record in connection therewith; any criminal record applicant may have; and the applicant's record of cooperation with the police. Upon application each applicant shall pay an application fee of $2,000.00 to defray the city's expense in processing the application and conducting an investigation of applicant. Each licensed pawnbroker shall thereafter pay an annual regulatory fee of $2,000.00 to defray city expense of regulating the operation of the pawnbroker.
(Code 1976, § 7-3068; Code 1985, § 6-81; Ord. of 11-22-1982; Ord. of 11-10-1997, § 1)
Sec. 10-575. - Review of application.
No action on any application for a pawnbroker's license shall be taken by the city until the police chief has reviewed such application and forwarded a recommendation thereon to the community affairs department in the manner specified in this article.
(Code 1976, § 7-3062; Code 1985, § 6-82)
Sec. 10-576. - Convictions limiting issuance.
No pawnbroker's license shall be issued to any person who has been convicted of the offense of receiving stolen goods or of burglary or robbery.
(Code 1976, § 7-3063; Code 1985, § 6-83)
Sec. 10-577. - Employees.
No person shall be employed in a pawn shop who has not been fingerprinted by the county sheriff's department and had an annual personal identification card issued by the county authorizing employment by a pawnshop.
(Code 1976, § 7-3069; Code 1985, § 6-84)
Sec. 10-578. - Hours of operation.
The hours within which a pawnshop may be open for business are from 7:00 a.m. to 8:00 p.m. each day except Sundays.
(Code 1976, § 7-3070; Code 1985, § 6-85)
Sec. 10-579. - Holding period before sale of articles.
All articles pawned or sold to a pawnbroker by the public shall be held for 30 days from the date they are pawned or sold to the pawnbroker. New articles, obtained from licensed wholesalers, may be sold without a waiting period, provided the pawnbroker holds a proper city business license to sell the same at retail.
(Code 1976, § 7-3071; Code 1985, § 6-86; Ord. of 11-22-1982)
Sec. 10-580. - Weapons.
It is unlawful for any licensee to sell, offer for sale, or expose for sale any kind of metal knucks, dirks, swords-in-cane, spears, bowie knives, switchblade knives, black jacks or any similar weapons.
(Code 1976, § 7-3065; Code 1985, § 6-87; Ord. of 11-22-1982)
Sec. 10-581. - Minors.
No pawnbroker shall have any business dealings as a pawnbroker with a minor, nor shall a pawnbroker's license be issued to a minor, nor shall a pawnbroker employ a minor to assist in such business.
(Code 1976, § 7-3066; Code 1985, § 6-88)
Sec. 10-582. - Records.
Each pawnbroker licensed hereunder shall furnish to the police chief each day a list of every article pledged with or sold to the licensee during the previous day, giving a full and accurate description of the article, from whom it was received, the hour of the day received and the right hand index finger print, or other finger if missing that finger, of each customer pawning or selling.
(Code 1976, § 7-3064; Code 1985, § 6-89)
Sec. 10-583. - Report of stolen goods.
It shall be the duty of every pawnbroker to report to the police any article pledged with such licensee, or which is sought to be pledged with such licensee, if such licensee shall have reason to believe that the article was stolen or lost and found by the person attempting to pledge it in the case of a lost article.
(Code 1976, § 7-3067; Code 1985, § 6-90)
Sec. 10-584. - Vehicle park and pawn business and license.
For a business to be able to obtain such license, it must be located in a zoning area permitting the sale of automobiles and meet the city codes for business establishments with car sales lots.
(Code 1985, § 6-91; Ord. of 8-7-1989)
