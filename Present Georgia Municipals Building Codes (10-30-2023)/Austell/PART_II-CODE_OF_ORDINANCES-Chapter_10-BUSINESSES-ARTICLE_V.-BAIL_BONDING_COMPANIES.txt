Sec. 10-219. - Professional bonding companies, definition.
All persons who hold themselves out as signers or sureties of bail bonds for compensation, and who are licensed as provided herein, are declared to be professional bonding companies, whether operating as a corporation, partnership or sole proprietorship.
(Code 1985, § 6-40; Ord. of 7-1-1996, § 1)
Sec. 10-220. - Applicability of article.
This article shall apply to any city business license issued to any professional bail bonding company as herein defined and to regulate the activities of others herein provided for in relation to this bonding business.
(Code 1985, § 6-41; Ord. of 7-1-1996, § 2)
Sec. 10-221. - Qualification for license.
(a)
Application. To qualify for a license as a professional bonding company, the applicant shall submit the following applications to the community affairs department office:
(1)
A completed application for bonding company. This application shall provide information in regard to the overall operation of a bonding business including the name of all partners, officers, stockholders and any other person or corporation having an interest in the bonding company.
(2)
A completed application for bonding company employees. This application shall provide personal information in order that a background investigation on the bonding company employees can be conducted. Any individual who will have power of attorney extended for the purpose of writing bonds must submit the above described applications.
(3)
The applicant furnished with the application, a complete set of the fingerprints and a recent credential-size full face photograph and a consent form permitting full background and criminal record examinations of each person who will act as an agent or employee of the bonding company. The fingerprints shall be processed by the police chief who shall certify the person to be free of any record of any crime of moral turpitude or any violation of law applicable to the bail bonding business.
(4)
The applicant must set forth affirmatively that the applicant and its employees meet the qualifications set forth herein.
(b)
Qualifications. No person including the applicant, its principals or employees, shall be permitted to sign professional bonds or act as an agent or representative of a bonding company if such person shall not meet the following qualifications:
(1)
No such person shall have been convicted of a felony offense, convicted of a crime involving moral turpitude, failed to meet bonding obligations in the city, Cobb County or any other county in the state or elsewhere, or failed to qualify under applicable state statutes governing who may act in such a capacity.
(2)
No persons employed by an agency having access to criminal history information, or persons related to those having such access, will be permitted to operate, own an interest in, or act in any capacity as an agent for a professional bonding company. For the purposes of this subsection, the term "persons related" includes spouses, children, stepchildren, parents, stepparents, grandparents, brothers, sisters, stepbrothers, stepsisters and first cousins.
(3)
Such person shall have reached the age of 21 years, be a citizen of the United States and shall have been a bona fide resident of the state for the immediately preceding one-year period.
(4)
The bail bonding company employing such person shall be located in the state and will be actively engaged in the bail bonding business and maintain a place of business accessible to the public.
(5)
Such person shall be of good character and the applicant (or its officers, if a corporation) shall be vouched for by two other reputable citizens who are residents of the county.
(Code 1985, § 6-42; Ord. of 7-1-1996, § 3)
Sec. 10-222. - Prohibited practices.
Professional bonding companies, their agents and employees shall be prohibited from taking the following actions:
(1)
Suggesting employment of attorney. Professional bonding companies shall not suggest or advise the employment, or name for employment, any attorney to represent the defendant, during the negotiations for the bondsman to sign the bond or subsequent thereto.
(2)
Soliciting business, etc., by bondmen. No professional bonding companies shall solicit business as a bondsman, nor shall such bondsman lounge about or around the county jail , for the purpose of engaging in or soliciting business as a bondsman; nor shall any officer or personnel of the county jail suggest to or advise in any manner whatever any prison of the city, the employment of the services of any particular professional bonding company to sign bonds for appearance in court at any time.
(3)
Participation in defense. Professional bonding companies shall not advise defendants, principals in bonds signed by them, or give any directions in the defense or disposition of the cases in which bonds are signed.
(4)
Payments to and by. No professional bonding companies, becoming surety on a bond and receiving the compensation in the case for signing a bond as surety, shall thereafter receive any other sum in the case to the final disposition of the case.
(5)
Indirect bonds. No professional bonding company may sign a bond in which another bonding company or third party receives compensation to arrange the release of an inmate from the county jail or other detention facility.
(6)
Persons prohibited from signing bonds. No attorney at law and no official authorized to admit to bail, nor any city or county official, shall become surety on any bond.
(7)
Fees of sureties. Professional bonding companies shall not, when receiving compensation, charge or receive more than ten percent of the principal amount of the bond from the defendant or from anyone acting for the defendants.
(8)
Prohibited payments and delegation of authority. No professional bonding company shall:
a.
Pay a fee or rebate or give or promise anything of value to a jailer, police officer, peace officer, city recorder, or any other person who has power to arrest or to hold in custody; or to any city official or city employee in order to secure a settlement, compromise, remission or reduction of the amount of any bail bond thereof;
b.
Pay a fee or rebate or give anything of value to an attorney in bail bond matters, except in defense of any action on a bond;
c.
Pay a fee or rebate or give or promise anything of value to the principal or anyone in the principal's behalf;
d.
Sign or countersign in blank any bond, or give a power of attorney to, or otherwise authorize, anyone to countersign such person's name to bonds unless the person so authorized is directly employed by the bondsman giving such power of attorney.
(Code 1985, § 6-43; Ord. of 7-1-1996, § 4)

Sec. 10-223. - Proof of financial responsibility.
It is unlawful for any professional bonding company to engage in the bonding business within the city without first offering proof of financial responsibility. Such proof shall be provided by the city by execution of an agreement with the city providing for an escrow account in any one or more financial institutions approved by the city. This escrow shall be ten percent of that company's bonding capacity and shall not be less than $10,000.00. If this escrow is encroached upon for any reason, or if the city council otherwise determines that additional escrow funds are required to insure the solvency or reliability of the professional bonding business, the city may require additional deposits to the escrow account.
(Code 1985, § 6-44; Ord. of 7-1-1996, § 5)
Sec. 10-224. - Bonds in excess of financial capacity.
In the event a bonding company issues and has outstanding bonds of a face amount in excess of its proven financial responsibility or bonding capacity, it is unlawful for that company to issue further bonds without first offering proof of additional financial responsibility to the extent of such new bonds or cancelling sufficient outstanding bonds to reduce its bonding limits to a dollar amount within its proof of financial responsibility.
(Code 1985, § 6-45; Ord. of 7-1-1996, § 6)
Sec. 10-225. - Monthly reports.
All bonding companies shall file with the police chief a monthly report. All reports shall be submitted on the form designed by the city council. These reports will include the following information and shall be filed in the form of an affidavit:
(1)
List by name, in alphabetical order, amount and date of bonds signed during each month.
(2)
List by name, in alphabetical order, case or indictment number and date of all bonds officially settled during each month.
(3)
List by name, amount and date of bond all judgments rendered by the city court, the state or superior court of Cobb County or other jurisdictions subject to collection in the city.
(4)
List by name, amount and date of bond all forfeitures pending in the city, Cobb County or other counties subject to collection in the city.
(5)
List personnel in the employment of submitting bonding company.
(6)
Report shall be filed with the city police chief on or before the tenth calendar day of each month.
(Code 1985, § 6-46; Ord. of 7-1-1996, § 7)
Sec. 10-226. - Equal access to jail.
All professional bonding companies which hold a currently effective license, issued by the community affairs department, shall be entitled to equal access to the jail of this city for the purpose of making bond.
(Code 1985, § 6-47; Ord. of 7-1-1996, § 8)
Sec. 10-227. - Termination of business.
Any professional bonding company which discontinues writing bail bonds during the period for which it is licensed shall notify the police chief and the community affairs department and immediately return such license.
(Code 1985, § 6-48; Ord. of 7-1-1996, § 9)
Sec. 10-228. - Employee termination.
Any professional bonding company which terminates the appointment of any employee authorized to sign bonds shall immediately file written notice thereof with the police chief and the community affairs department, with a copy given or mailed to the terminated employee. Such notice filed shall state the reasons, if any, for such termination. Information so furnished shall be privileged and shall not be used as evidence in any action against the bonding company.
(Code 1985, § 6-49; Ord. of 7-1-1996, § 10)
Sec. 10-229. - Denial, suspension, refusal to renew or revocation of license, penalties.
(a)
The city may deny, suspend, revoke or refuse to renew any license issued in accordance with these requirements for any of the following causes:
(1)
Any cause for which issuance of the license could have been refused had it then existed and been known to the city.
(2)
Material misstatement, misrepresentation or fraud in obtaining the license.
(3)
Misappropriation, conversion or unlawful withholding of money belonging to others and received in the conduct of business under the license.
(4)
Felony conviction of professional bonding company principal.
(5)
Fraudulent or dishonest practices in the conduct of business under the license.
(6)
Willful failure to comply with the provisions of this article.
(7)
Willful failure to return collateral security to the principal when the principal is entitled thereto.
(8)
When in the judgment of city, the licensee has, in the conduct of affairs under the license, demonstrated incompetency, or untrustworthiness, or conduct or practices rendering licensee unfit to carry on the bail bonding business, or making the licensee's continuance in such business detrimental to the public interest, or that the licensee is no longer in good faith carrying on the bail bond business, or that the licensee is guilty of rebating, or offering to rebate, or offering to divide the licensee's compensation, and for any of such reasons, is found by the city to be a source of detriment, injury or loss to the public.
(b)
Any violation of this article shall subject the bonding company, and its principals, agents or employees in violation, to suspension of the license and punishment as provided in section 1-9.
(Code 1985, § 6-50; Ord. of 7-1-1996, § 11)
Sec. 10-230. - Bonding procedures.
All bonding companies must comply with the bonding procedures as established for use in the city court.
(Code 1985, § 6-51; Ord. of 7-1-1996, § 12)
Sec. 10-231. - Display of signs.
(a)
Each professional bonding company, duly licensed by the city, shall be allowed to display a sign, in a place designated by the police chief, at or near the book-in book-out section of the county jail.
(b)
One sign per licensee shall be permitted and shall be limited to lettering and numbering of no more than three inches high and 1.5 inches in width for each letter or numeral.
(c)
Signs shall be limited to two lines to allow name of business at top and phone number at bottom.
(d)
Signs shall be restricted to black and white in coloring and shall be hung in place so designated on a first come first serve priority.
(Code 1985, § 6-52; Ord. of 7-1-1996, § 14)
Sec. 10-232. - Prenumbered receipt as evidence of payment.
(a)
All professional bonding companies, which accept money or any other consideration for any bail bond which they execute, must for each payment received, give to the persons paying the money or giving the consideration, a prenumbered receipt as evidence of payment, which receipt shall state the date, name of the principal amount of money or consideration received and purpose of which received, number of the power of attorney form attached to the bond, penal sum of the bond, and name of person making payment or giving consideration.
(b)
All professional bonding companies must retain a duplicate copy of each receipt issued as part of such person's records.
(Code 1985, § 6-53; Ord. of 7-1-1996, § 15)
Sec. 10-233. - Power of attorney.
All professional bonding companies must attach to each bail bond a duly executed power of attorney in an amount of at least the penal sum of the bond. This section shall not apply to any card or certificate of membership of any automobile club.
(Code 1985, § 6-54; Ord. of 7-1-1996, § 16)
