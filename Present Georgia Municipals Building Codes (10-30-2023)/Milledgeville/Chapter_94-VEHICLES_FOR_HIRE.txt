ARTICLE II. - TAXICABS[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
Editor's note— Ord. No. O-1009-012, adopted September 28, 2010, repealed and reenacted article II in its entirety to read as herein set out. Formerly, article II, sections 94-31—94-34, pertained to similar subject matter, and derived from the Code of 1986, §§ 12-196—12-199.

Sec. 94-31. - Definitions.
City shall mean the City of Milledgeville, Georgia a municipal corporation, said definition to include all areas within the corporate limits of the city.
Driver means any person who drives or operates a taxicab on the streets of Milledgeville.
Stand means a public place alongside the curb of a street or elsewhere which has been designated by the city as reserved exclusively for the use of taxicabs.
Street shall mean and include any street, alley, lane, avenue, court, or public place in the city.
Taxicab means a motor vehicle engaged in the business of carrying passengers for hire, or offering to carry passengers for hire, except motor vehicles used as ambulances, sight-seeing cars or buses, and except for limousines and buses operating on a fixed route.
Taxicab company means the holder of one or more taxicab regulatory permits to operate a taxicab under the provisions of this article, whether a person, firm, partnership, or corporation.
Taxicab driver's permit means the written authority granted by the city for an individual to drive or operate a taxicab within the city.
Taxicab regulatory permit means the right and privilege granted by the city for the operation of a taxicab within the corporate limits of the city issued upon the receipt of a regulatory fee established in the revenue ordinance.
Telephone stand means a place alongside a street or elsewhere where a taxicab company has been authorized by the city to install a telephone or call box for the taking of calls and the dispatching of taxicabs.
(Ord. No. O-1009-012, 9-28-2010)
Sec. 94-32. - Taxicab regulatory permit—Required; display; requirements of vehicle.
(a)
No person, firm or corporation shall operate a taxicab on the streets of the city unless a taxicab regulatory permit has been issued by the city for such taxicab. All taxicabs shall display a current taxicab regulatory permit issued by the city to signify compliance with this permit requirement in each vehicle. A taxicab regulatory permit shall be issued by the police department only after the taxicab has been inspected and found to comply with all requirements of this article, including the payment of the taxicab regulatory permit fee. The taxicab regulatory permit fee shall be established by mayor and council.
(b)
Before any application is approved for any taxicab regulatory permit, the following requirements shall be met:
(1)
The vehicle will be in good mechanical condition, having safe brakes, and all necessary lights and signals shall be operating.
(2)
Each vehicle shall be brought to the police department annually in January of the year for inspection by the employees of such department.
(3)
The vehicle shall have a current valid Georgia license plate displayed.
(4)
The vehicle shall be covered by a current and valid insurance policy as required in section 94-33.
(5)
The vehicle shall have at least four doors and a front and a rear seat.
(6)
The vehicle will be marked permanently with non-removable fixed signs or decals on both sides and on the rear of the exterior of the vehicle with at least four-inch letters and numerals, displaying the name of the company and the company number assigned to the vehicle.
(7)
The company number will be displayed at the rear of both front fenders on the side, and near the top of the fender, just ahead of the front doors, and on the rear of the vehicle in a position at the left rear side of the vehicle.
(8)
No signs shall be displayed in the windows of the vehicle.
(9)
A lighted marker shall be permanently affixed to the top of the vehicle, which marker identifies the vehicle as a taxicab.
(c)
The taxicab regulatory permit shall be displayed in the following manner.
(1)
The permit shall be permanently affixed to the vehicle by the police department.
(2)
It will be affixed so as to be clearly visible inside and outside of the vehicle.
(3)
A permit will be issued for a particular vehicle, and the permit shall not be transferable.
(4)
The license shall be valid for one year, unless suspended or revoked, and shall expire on December 31 of every year.
(5)
Each application for renewal shall be made within 30 days after the expiration date.
(Ord. No. O-1009-012, 9-28-2010)
State Law reference— Motor vehicle equipment, O.C.G.A. § 40-8-1 et seq.
Sec. 94-33. - Insurance.
(a)
Any taxicab company desiring to do business in the city shall give and maintain a policy of indemnity insurance with an indemnity insurance company authorized to do business in the state for each vehicle in use as a taxicab, or a self insurance certificate issued under state guidelines and regulated by the insurance commissioner of Georgia.
(b)
Proof of insurance shall be kept in the vehicle and on file at the police department.
(c)
The minimum insurance coverage shall be in accordance with state law.
(1)
Twenty-five thousand dollars per death or bodily injury per person.
(2)
Fifty thousand dollars per death or bodily injury per occurrence.
(3)
Twenty-five thousand dollars personal property damage.
(d)
Should the state law which requires motor vehicle liability minimum insurance coverage as evidence of security for bodily injury and property damage liability (O.C.G.A. § 40-9-1 et seq., the Motor Vehicle Safety Responsibility Act) be changed to require greater minimums in any category of liability listed in subsections (c)(1) through (3), the minimum amounts listed in this subsection shall be automatically amended to require such amounts upon the effective date of such legislation with no additional notice to the public and no formal action required by the city. Additionally, should there be enacted at any time laws affecting insurance requirements of vehicles for hire particularly, this article will automatically incorporate such requirements with no additional notice to the public and no formal action required by the council. Applicant must further provide certifications from the insurer or the insurer's agent that notification of the cancellation of the insurance for a vehicle or vehicles of the applicant shall be provided to the business license office of the city.
(Ord. No. O-1009-012, 9-28-2010)
State Law reference— Motor vehicle liability insurance, etc., O.C.G.A. §§ 33-34-4, 40-9-2(5).
Sec. 94-34. - Permit to drive taxicab.
(a)
Required; display. Before any person shall drive or operate any taxicab within the corporate limits of the city, he shall pay the required license fee and procure from the Record clerk a form of permit as required for each driver or operator. The permit issued along with a photograph of the person shall be displayed on the shirt or coat of the driver or operator, so that it may be observed at all times by passengers or officials of the marshal's office or police department. Such permit shall be valid for one year, unless suspended or revoked, and shall expire on December 31 of each year. Each application for renewal shall be made within 60 days of the expiration date.
(b)
Application. An application for a taxi driver's permit shall be made to the chief of police by each applicant. The application shall be approved by the chief of police before a license is issued. All applicants shall provide the following information on making application for a taxi driver's permit: Full name, date of birth, present address, former address, birth place, height, weight, eye color, color of hair, citizenship, build and complexion; shall provide a current and valid Georgia operator's license of the proper class; and shall state any traffic or criminal record. A photograph and fingerprints shall be taken by the police department.
(c)
Qualifications of applicant. An applicant for a driver's permit under this article must:
(1)
Be not less than 18 years of age.
(2)
Have no physical infirmities which make said applicant an unsafe or unsatisfactory taxicab driver.
(3)
No application shall be approved if the applicant has been convicted of a felony, a crime involving moral turpitude, any sex crime or an offense violating federal, state, or local laws, or ordinances, or regulations governing the manufacture, distribution, or sale of alcoholic beverages or illegal drugs during the five years preceding the date of application or if the applicant has been convicted of three or more traffic offenses during any one calendar year during the five years preceding the date of application; provided, however, that no permit shall be issued to any person who has in the five years prior to the application been convicted of driving under the influence of alcohol or drugs; further provided that no permit shall be issued to any person who at the time of making applications is under treatment for the abuse of alcohol or drugs.
(4)
Be a citizen of the United States or an alien admitted for permanent residence or who has otherwise been granted employment authorization by the United States Immigration and Naturalization Services.
(d)
Investigation; issuance. The chief of police shall make such investigation of the applicant as he deems necessary, including his physical condition, aptitude, ability to drive and character and shall endorse his recommendation upon the back of each application and transmit the application, together with the collected permit fee, to the clerk and treasurer. No driver's or operator's permit shall be issued unless recommended by the chief of police.
(e)
Revocation; suspension. After such permit shall have been issued, the permit shall be subject to temporary suspension by the chief of police for violation of any offense mentioned in subsection (b) or other serious violation of either criminal or traffic laws, pending further action on offenses or suspension or revocation by the municipal court judge for any cause or reason coming to his knowledge and notice in his official capacity.
(Ord. No. O-1009-012, 9-28-2010)
Sec. 94-35. - Taxicab stands.
Taxicabs shall not be permitted to park in the corporate limits of the city except at such stands as may be established by the police department, which parking stands shall be designated by appropriate signs. Whenever any such stand is established, such stand may be used by taxicabs upon a rotation basis of first come, first served. Any person who shall violate or fail to comply with any of the provisions of this section or who shall counsel, aid or abet in any such violation or failure to comply shall be deemed guilty of an offense and shall be punished as provided by section 1-8. The police department shall enforce this section.
(Ord. No. O-1009-012, 9-28-2010)
Sec. 94-36. - Fixed business address required.
Each taxicab company shall, as a condition of receiving one or more taxicab regulatory permits to operate a taxicab in the city, provide a physical address for the taxicab company. If the company headquarters is to be moved, the owner of the taxicab company shall notify the chief of police or his designee of the new location prior to relocation. If the taxicab company is located within the corporate limits of the city, the taxicab company shall conform with all zoning, business tax certificate, and other applicable laws of the city.
(Ord. No. O-1009-012, 9-28-2010)

ARTICLE III. - BUSES

Sec. 94-71. - Bus defined.
For all purposes of this article, the term "bus" shall mean all passenger-carrying motor vehicles having an installed seating capacity of more than six persons, exclusive of the operator or driver thereof, used for the purpose of carrying and conveying natural persons as passengers, whether the passengers are engaged in interstate or intrastate business, or both.
(Code 1986, § 12-211)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 94-72. - Bus stops—Designated.
The following identified and designated places and areas lying within the presently existing and defined "parking meter area" within the city are hereby identified and designated as bus stops:
(1)
In South Wayne Street, on the west side thereof and along the existing sidewalk curb, beginning at the north property line of West Green Street and extending north therefrom a distance of not more than 30 feet;
(2)
In South Wayne Street, on the east side thereof and along and adjoining the existing sidewalk curb, beginning at the south property line of East Hancock Street and extending south therefrom a distance of not more than 30 feet;
(3)
In North Wayne Street, on the west side thereof and along and adjoining the existing sidewalk curb, beginning at the north property line of West Hancock Street, and extending north therefrom a distance of not more than 30 feet; and
(4)
In West Hancock Street, on the north side thereof and along and adjoining the existing sidewalk curb, beginning at the east property line of North Wilkinson Street and extending east therefrom a distance of not more than 30 feet.
(Code 1986, § 12-212)
Sec. 94-73. - Same—Area to be used.
(a)
Extent. All bus stops shall extend from the sidewalk curbs towards the centerline of the public street of which they are a part and run parallel to the curbline, a sufficient width from the curbline, not exceeding nine feet, to permit the entry therein of a bus. Each such designated area shall be plainly marked and publicly identified. No device, rack, stand or sign shall be placed in any of such areas to forbid or prohibit or interfere with the free flow of vehicular traffic over, on or through the area; and nothing provided in this article shall prohibit the use of such areas by the general public for the purpose only of loading and unloading passengers to or from any motor vehicle, provided that the use thereof in such manner shall be restricted to stops of short duration for the exclusive purpose of loading and unloading passengers only.
(b)
Fire hydrant areas. The areas identified in this article are designated as bus stops notwithstanding the fact that fire hydrants are near to and opposite some of the bus stops, it being deemed appropriate to make use of such areas for such common public causes and uses, which do not interfere with one another.
(Code 1986, § 12-213)
Sec. 94-74. - Use of other than designated stops for loading, unloading.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any operator, conductor or driver of any bus to stop or park any bus operated, directed or driven by him for the purpose of taking aboard or discharging any passenger within the parking meter areas of the city, at any place or site therein other than a bus stop as designated in this article.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to enter or alight from any bus at any place within the parking meter areas of the city, other than a bus stop as designated in this article.
(Code 1986, § 12-214)
Sec. 94-75. - Standing overtime at stop.
No bus shall be kept, held or permitted to stand within any bus stop at any single entry therein for a length of time greater than two minutes, and for a violation of this section the driver, conductor or operator of such bus shall be deemed guilty of the offense against the city of overtime parking.
(Code 1986, § 12-215)
