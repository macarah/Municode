Section 10.16.1 - License to Sell Fireworks.
Consumer fireworks and/or fireworks and other terms or phrases not otherwise defined herein shall have the same meaning as provided for in O.C.G.A. § 25-10-1. A State of Georgia issued consumer fireworks distributor license is required to sell fireworks within the City of Roswell.
( 2016-08-13 , § 1, Amended 08/08/2016)
Section 10.16.2 - Where Fireworks Can Be Sold.
(a)
Consumer fireworks can be sold from a "permanent consumer fireworks retail sales facilities" or from "temporary structures for the retail sale of consumer fireworks" only in the CX- Commercial Mixed Use, CC- Commercial Corridor and CH- Commercial Heavy identified Unified Development Code (UDC) zoning districts.
(b)
As provided by state law, consumer fireworks may be sold from a "store" or "temporary structures for the retail sale of consumer fireworks" if such facility or structure complies with the requirements of the National Fire Protection Association.
(c)
"Temporary structures for the retail sale of fireworks" must be set-up within one thousand (1,000) feet of a fire hydrant or a fire department connection of a building unless otherwise determined by the City of Roswell Fire Chief. Beginning January 1, 2016 and thereafter, a distributor selling from a temporary consumer firework retail sales structure or stand shall pay a license fee of $500.00 per location to the City of Roswell and shall meet all state requirements. Such license fees shall be used by the city for public safety purposes.
( 2016-08-13 , § 1, Amended 08/08/2016)
Section 10.16.3 - Use, Prohibited Conduct, Hours of Use and Special Use Permits.
(a)
Consumer fireworks may only be ignited, used or exploded on any day beginning at the time of 10:00 a.m. and up and including the ending time of 9:00 p.m. On January 1, July 3, July 4, and December 31 of each year consumer fireworks may be ignited, used or exploded up to and including an end time of 11:59 p.m. On January 1 of each year consumer fireworks may also be ignited, used or exploded beginning at 12:00 midnight and up to and including the ending time of 1:00 a.m. If at any time these hours conflict with state law, state law shall control. The city may, in its sole discretion, grant special use permits for igniting, using or exploding consumer fireworks outside of these hours for a fee not to exceed the maximum allowed by state law.
(b)
Consumer fireworks may not be ignited, used or exploded indoors; in the public right of way; on property of others without their consent; within any park, historic site, recreational area or other area owned by the city. Consumer fireworks shall not be ignited, used or exploded within one hundred (100) yards of a water treatment plant; waste water treatment plant; a gas station or a facility that refines, processes, or blends gasoline or other flammable or combustible liquids or gases where the volume stored is in excess of five hundred (500) gallons; any public or private electric substation; a platform designed for landing use by helicopters, or a jail or prison. Consumer fireworks shall not be ignited, used or exploded within one hundred (100) yards of a hospital, nursing home or other health care facility regulated under O.C.G.A. Title 31 Chapter 7 without written consent of the owner or operator of such facility.
(c)
All persons selling and/or offering for sale consumer fireworks, igniting, using and/or exploding consumer fireworks, or possessing, manufacturing transporting or storing consumer fireworks, must meet age requirements as provided by state law.
( 2016-08-13 , § 1, Amended 08/08/2016)
Section 10.16.4 - Special Use Permit.
Any person wishing to use or explode any fireworks outside of the permitted hours contained in state law or herein, may apply to the Roswell City Administrator or her designee for a special use permit. Such application shall designate the time or times and location that such consumer fireworks are to be used or exploded and shall provide such other information as may be required by the City of Roswell Fire Chief and the City of Roswell Police Chief. Such application shall be remitted to the city administrator at least fifteen (15) days prior to the event. The cost of a special use permit for consumer fireworks shall be $100.00 and no city official or employee shall bear any liability for the issuance of such permit.
( 2016-08-13 , § 1, Amended 08/08/2016)
