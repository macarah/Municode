Sec. 18-461. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Fixed place of business means an office or other fixed facility, that is, a place, site, structure, or other similar facility, used exclusively for business, including, but not limited to, administrative or management activities, on a regular and continuing basis, through which an individual or business entity engages in a trade or business for which an occupational license is currently issued.
Gem means any precious or semi-precious stone which is cut and polished.
Gold or silver buyer means any person engaged in the business of purchasing or otherwise dealing in gold or silver or other precious metals of any kind or description, from members of the general public within the corporate limits of the city. Included in this definition are buyers of secondhand jewelry and gems and numismatic coins. Isolated transactions between persons not engaged in the business are not covered by the terms of this article.
Numismatic coins means coins whose value as collectors' items exceeds the value of the content of the precious metals in the coins.
Precious metal means gold, silver, or platinum or alloy containing gold, silver, or platinum.
( Ord. No. 01302012-1 , § I(18-301), 1-30-2012)
Sec. 18-462. - Legislative findings.
Engaging in the business of buying gold, silver, other precious metals, precious gems, jewelry, or numismatic coins within the corporate limits of the city is hereby declared to be affected with the public interest because of the opportunity it affords for the disposal of stolen property.
( Ord. No. 01302012-1 , § I(18-302), 1-30-2012)
Sec. 18-463. - Compliance with article required.
(a)
It is hereby declared to be unlawful, and an offense punishable as provided in section 1-7, to engage in the business of buying precious metals, precious gems, jewelry, or numismatic coins from members of the general public within the corporate limits of the city without first complying with the registration and other provisions of this article and obtaining a license as herein provided. Each day such offense occurs or continues shall constitute a separate offense.
(b)
Any holder in good standing of a current city general pawnbrokers licenses who is operating in compliance with the provisions of article VIII of this chapter will be considered to have met the requirements of this article, and will be not required to make a separate registration under the requirements of this article.
( Ord. No. 01302012-1 , § I(18-303), 1-30-2012)
Sec. 18-464. - Advertising.
No person, partnership, corporation or other entity covered by this article shall advertise, either in the news media, or portable signs, or in any other manner, the buying, selling or trading of, or otherwise dealing in, precious metals, precious gems, jewelry, or numismatic coins until they have complied with the provisions of this article. The violation of this section shall be unlawful and an offense punishable as provided in section 1-7, and each day such violation occurs or continues shall constitute a separate offense.
( Ord. No. 01302012-1 , § I(18-304), 1-30-2012)
Sec. 18-465. - Registration, payment of regulatory fee.
(a)
Required. All persons, partnerships, corporations or other entities engaging in the business of buying precious metals, precious gems, jewelry, or numismatic coins shall register with the tax and licensing official on a form to be provided by the tax and licensing official and shall pay the regulatory fee for gold and silver buyers as outlined in section 18-54. The applicant that registers for a corporation must also be at least a ten percent stockholder in that corporation.
(b)
Information. All applicants for registration under the provisions of this article shall furnish, in addition to such other information as the tax and licensing official shall require, the following information:
(1)
Name of the applicant.
(2)
Permanent address and local address of the applicant.
(3)
If the applicant is a partnership, the name and address of each member of the partnership.
(4)
If the applicant is a corporation, the name and address of each officer and each director of the corporation.
(5)
The name and address of each employee of the applicant who is actively engaged in the performance of such services.
(6)
If a resident, the length of time the applicant has been a resident of the state and the city.
( Ord. No. 01302012-1 , § I(18-305), 1-30-2012)
Sec. 18-466. - Effect of criminal conviction.
No person shall engage in the business or activity for which the license is required under this article, or shall be a member of a partnership, or an officer, stockholder or director in a corporation engaged in such business who shall have been convicted of a felony or a misdemeanor involving moral turpitude.
( Ord. No. 01302012-1 , § I(18-306), 1-30-2012)
Sec. 18-467. - Bond required.
Every person, partnership, corporation or other entity engaging in the business of buying precious metals, precious gems, jewelry, or numismatic coins shall file with the chief of police for the city a surety bond executed by such applicant secured by a corporate surety company, authorized to do business in the state, in an amount not less than $5,000.00 conditioned upon compliance with the provisions of this article, and upon the prompt report of all suspicious persons dealing with the obligor, and of stolen property or property suspected of being stolen, and for the returning to the chief of police or to the true and lawful owner of any such property or making payment therefor. The bond shall be taken in the name of the city, and the city or any person injured by the breach of said bond may bring an action on said bond in its or the person's own name to recover damages therefor.
( Ord. No. 01302012-1 , § I(18-307), 1-30-2012)
Sec. 18-468. - Investigation of applicant.
Within five days after receipt of an application as provided for herein, the chief of police shall cause an investigation to be made of the applicant and the applicant's proposed operation. If, upon investigation, the chief of police shall find that the applicant is of good moral character and has never been convicted of a felony or a misdemeanor involving moral turpitude, and has met all of the requirements of this article, the chief of police shall approve the issuance of a license to the applicant. The license shall be issued by the city police department, and the regulatory fee for gold and silver buyers as outlined in section 18-54 shall be paid.
( Ord. No. 01302012-1 , § I(18-308), 1-30-2012)
Sec. 18-469. - Notice to law enforcement officials.
If the license required by the provisions of this article is approved, the applicant shall file a notice with all law enforcement agencies of the county and city within 15 days after approval.
( Ord. No. 01302012-1 , § I(18-309), 1-30-2012)
Sec. 18-470. - Register.
Every licensee under this article shall keep a register upon which the licensee shall record the name, address, social security number or driver's license number, a brief description of the person, and other identification, of every person from whom the licensee purchases any precious metals, precious gems, jewelry, or numismatic coins, together with the date and time of day of said purchase; a description of each article purchased, including the name, make and serial or other identifying number or mark; and the amount paid therefor. Such entry shall be made at the time the transaction occurs. Before buying any precious metals, precious gems, jewelry, or numismatic coins, the licensee shall require positive identification from the seller, either from a driver's license or other government-issued photo identification.
( Ord. No. 01302012-1 , § I(18-310), 1-30-2012)
Sec. 18-471. - Reports to police.
Each licensee under this article shall file daily written reports at 10:00 a.m. with the chief of police, showing the name and description of the persons from whom precious metals, precious gems, jewelry, or numismatic coins were purchased by the licensee during the preceding 24 hours, and a description in detail of the nature, type and quantity of the precious metals, precious gems, jewelry, or numismatic coins. It is hereby made the duty of the chief of police to obtain such reports promptly each day. It shall be unlawful and an offense punishable as provided in section 1-7 for any licensee to fail or refuse to make such daily report in the manner described by this section, and such failure shall be grounds for the revocation of the license.
( Ord. No. 01302012-1 , § I(18-311), 1-30-2012)
Sec. 18-472. - Elapsed time before disposal.
No articles of personal property made of gold, silver or other precious metals or precious gems or jewelry or numismatic coins shall be sold, melted, altered or otherwise disposed of by any secondhand dealer until 14 days have elapsed from the time the chief of police has been given a copy or transcript of the register in which such articles of personal property made of gold, silver, platinum or other precious metals or gems or jewelry or numismatic coins are entered, and said articles shall not be transferred from within the city limits during said 14-day period of time.
( Ord. No. 01302012-1 , § I(18-312), 1-30-2012)
Sec. 18-473. - Licenses nontransferable.
Licenses issued under this article shall not be transferred.
( Ord. No. 01302012-1 , § I(18-313), 1-30-2012)
Sec. 18-474. - Disposition of stolen items.
In the event it is determined that any item covered by this article is the subject of any theft, embezzlement or other crime, the licensee shall surrender same upon demand to the chief of police or the chief of police's designee.
( Ord. No. 01302012-1 , § I(18-314), 1-30-2012)
Sec. 18-475. - Purchasing from minors prohibited.
It shall be unlawful and punishable as provided in section 1-7 for any person, partnership, corporation or other entity engaged in the business of buying precious metals, precious gems or jewelry or numismatic coins to purchase any of said items from a person less than 18 years of age.
( Ord. No. 01302012-1 , § I(18-315), 1-30-2012)
Sec. 18-476. - Revocation of license.
Licenses issued pursuant to this article shall be subject to revocation or suspension by the city manager upon recommendation of the chief of police for the violation of or the failure to comply with any provision of this article or for the violation of any provision of law, after reasonable notice and an opportunity to be heard has been given the licensee. The city manager shall immediately notify any licensee of such suspension or revocation.
( Ord. No. 01302012-1 , § I(18-316), 1-30-2012)
Sec. 18-477. - Location restrictions.
Every buyer of gold or silver shall have at least one fixed place of business within the corporate limits of the city and all items stored pursuant to this article shall be stored at said fixed place of business. A buyer of gold or silver may temporarily operate at a location other than their fixed place of business so long as the temporary location is properly zoned and the owner, occupier, or other person or entity entitled to the use of the location and the buyer of gold or silver are licensed pursuant to this article.
( Ord. No. 01302012-1 , § I(18-317), 1-30-2012)
