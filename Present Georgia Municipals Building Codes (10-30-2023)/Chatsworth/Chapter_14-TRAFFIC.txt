ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 14-1. - Adoption of state law by reference.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 40-6-372, §§ 40-6-1 through 40-6-395, known as the "Uniform Rules of the Road," are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of this municipality with like effect as if recited herein.
(b)
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-8.
(Ord. of 3-1-82)
State Law reference— Jurisdiction of municipal court of violations of driver's license statute, O.C.G.A. § 40-5-124; of unregistered motor vehicle statute, O.C.G.A. § 40-13-22.
Sec. 14-2. - Dangerous driving.
(a)
Any person who drives a motor vehicle upon any street of the city at a speed or in a manner so as to endanger, or likely to endanger, life, limb, or property of any person shall be guilty of dangerous driving, in violation of this Code.
(b)
Dangerous driving within the meaning of this section shall be deemed to include the following offenses, which are expressly prohibited:
(1)
Driving a motor vehicle when not under complete control, or with inadequate or improperly adjusted brakes.
(2)
Driving to the left of the center of the street.
(3)
Passing or attempting to pass two other vehicles abreast, moving in the same direction.
(4)
Backing into the line of traffic without ascertaining if the way is clear.
(5)
A failure to give adequate and timely signals of intention to turn and/or slow down or stop.
(6)
Exceeding a reasonable speed under the circumstances of traffic conditions obtaining at the time.
(7)
Passing other vehicles going in the same direction while crossing at an intersection, or while pedestrians are passing, or about to pass, in front of either of such vehicles.
(8)
Driving a vehicle out of an alley, or from behind a building, across a sidewalk into a street without bringing such vehicle to a full stop immediately before crossing such sidewalk, and sounding the horn.
(Ord. of 9-3-51, § 2)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-390.
Sec. 14-3. - Riding or driving on sidewalks.
It shall not be lawful for any person to ride, propel or run any bicycle or motor vehicle on any sidewalk, except to cross the sidewalk on a driveway.
(Code 1926, § 197)

ARTICLE II. - RAILROADS

Sec. 14-20. - Speed limit.
The speed limits of trains of the Louisville and Nashville Railroad Company, Atlanta Division, a branch of the Seaboard Systems Railroad, running through the city are hereby set at 45 miles per hour from 6:00 a.m. to 10:00 p.m. from milepost C 376.4 to milepost C 377.6 and at 60 miles per hour from 10:00 p.m. to 6:00 a.m. from milepost C 376.4 to milepost C 377.6.
(Code 1926, § 222; Ord. of 9-12-83)
Sec. 14-21. - Blowing whistle.
It shall be an offense to run a train within the city after approaching within 100 yards of the first public crossing, without plainly and distinctly blowing the whistle or sounding the horn while the train is in motion.
(Code 1926, § 220)
Sec. 14-22. - Obstructing crossing.
It shall be unlawful to obstruct any public street or crossing by engines or cars of any description being allowed to stand still on any portion of the ground laid out for such street or crossing for the space of 15 minutes, or by switching engines or cars within the limits of such street or crossing, or in any other way to obstruct or to interfere with the free passage of persons or vehicles on any part of such street; it being the true intent and meaning of this section that railroads have the necessary right of passing their engines and trains over such streets or crossings in their actual and necessary business, but that they have no right to obstruct the streets beyond their actual necessity of passing; and any building of switches or using of any street for switching cars or allowing cars of engines to stand thereon shall be a violation of this section.
(Code 1926, § 221)
Sec. 14-23. - Persons subject to punishment.
The engineer or person in charge of any engine, as well as the conductor or other person under whose order the act is done, and all other persons concerned in violating any section of this article, shall be punished as prescribed in section 1-8.
(Code 1926, § 223)
Secs. 14-24—14-39. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - DOWNTOWN PARKING DISTRICT[2]

Sec. 14-40. - Short title.
This article shall be known and may be cited as ``The Downtown Parking District Ordinance.''
(Ord. of 4-4-94, § A)
Sec. 14-41. - Purpose and intent.
The purpose of this article is to assure that all residents of and visitors to the city have equal access to parking spaces within the herein created downtown parking district. The intent of this ordinance is to create a two hour time limit parking zone not limited in scope to individual parking spaces. Said two hour parking zone shall be known as the downtown parking district.
(Ord. of 4-4-94, § B)
Sec. 14-42. - Definitions.
For purposes of this article the following terms, phrases, wording and their derivations shall have the meaning set forth herein:
Automobile is a self-propelled vehicle used for transportation.
City is the City of Chatsworth.
Downtown parking district is all the parking spaces along the side of the streets within the city limits of the city except those parking spaces immediately surrounding the Murray County Courthouse and except those parking spaces on municipal property designated for public parking.
No parking zone is any area within the downtown parking district in which parking shall not be permitted unless otherwise indicated. Any such area shall be identified by appropriately erected signs giving notice thereof.
Parking spaces is all side street parking within the city.
Person is any person, firm, partnership, association, corporation, company, other governmental entity or authority or any organization of any kind.
Street is any street, highway, sidewalk, or other public way or public grounds in the city.
(Ord. of 4-4-94, § C)
Sec. 14-43. - Two-hour parking.
It shall be unlawful for any person to park an automobile for more than two hours within the downtown parking district between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m. Monday through Saturday. The moving of an automobile from space to space within the downtown parking district is contemplated and is hereby specifically prohibited after the two-hour time limit has expired.
(Ord. of 4-4-94, § D)
Sec. 14-44. - No parking zone.
It shall be unlawful for any person to park an automobile within an appropriately designated no parking zone.
(Ord. of 4-4-94, § E)
Sec. 14-45. - Enforcement.
Enforcement of this article shall be provided by the Chatsworth Police Department.
(Ord. of 4-4-94, § F)
Sec. 14-46. - Fines, penalties and procedure.
Any person who parks an automobile within the downtown parking district in violation of this article shall be cited and fined $25.00. Said fine shall be due and payable to the city at the Chatsworth Police Department within 30 days from the date of said citation. Persons failing to voluntarily appear and pay said $25.00 fine(s) may be cited to the municipal court of Chatsworth, Georgia, for failure to pay such fine.
Any person convicted in the municipal court of Chatsworth, Georgia, of failure to pay any fine(s) assessed under this article shall promptly pay into the court any past due fines along with $25.00 per unpaid fine, and any court costs generated by failure to pay said fine(s).
( Ord. of 4-4-11(1), § 1 )
ARTICLE IV. - IMPOUNDING VEHICLES

Sec. 14-47. - Impounding vehicles authorized.
Under the following circumstances, the chief of police, or his designee, shall be authorized to order the impoundment of any vehicle to an appropriate location:
(1)
Any vehicle left abandoned and unattended due to the arrest of the driver thereof, when no other responsible, lawful driver designated by the owner shall be readily available;
(2)
Any vehicle that is evidence in any ongoing law enforcement investigation;
(3)
Any vehicle not properly tagged, titled, registered, or insured;
(4)
Any vehicle inoperable or abandoned pursuant to section 14-50.
(Ord. of 5-4-2009, § 1)
Sec. 14-48. - Delegation of authority.
The chief of police may delegate his authority set forth herein with respect to impounding vehicles to any certified peace officer employed by the police department.
(Ord. of 5-4-2009, § 1)
Sec. 14-49. - Responsibility for towing or other charge(s).
Any wrecker bill, towing, storage, or other related charge(s) for a vehicle impounded pursuant to this article shall be paid by the owner of such vehicle. Such fees shall not exceed the maximum towing fees permitted by the Georgia Public Service Commission and shall be approved by the chief of police.
(Ord. of 5-4-2009, § 1)
Sec. 14-50. - Inoperable and abandoned vehicles.
No vehicle shall be abandoned or left unattended upon a public street or highway or other public property, whether operable or not, for a period of more than 24 hours. Any vehicle so abandoned or left inoperable may be impounded by the police department at the owner's expense, and the owner may be subject to fine.
(Ord. of 5-4-2009, § 1)
Sec. 14-51. - Unauthorized removal of impounded vehicles.
It shall be unlawful for any person to remove or attempt to remove a towed and stored vehicle from the place of storage designated by the chief of police.
(Ord. of 5-4-2009, § 1)
