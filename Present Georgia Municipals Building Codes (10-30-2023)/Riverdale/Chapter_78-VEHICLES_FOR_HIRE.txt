Sec. 78-1. - Definitions.
Cab or taxicab means motor vehicles operated for hire, the destination of which is under the direction of the passenger transported herein. The term "cab" or "taxicab" shall not include limousines regulated by the state public service commission.
Company means any person or business owning, operating or purporting to operate a cab.
Driver or operator means any and every person in charge of, driving or operating a cab whether as owner, agent, employee or otherwise.
Highways means any of the public streets, alleys, lanes, boulevards, avenues, drives, circles or roads within the city.
Owner means any person, firm or corporation having control of the operation or maintenance and collection of the revenue derived from cabs.
Permit means the license or authority granted by the chief of police to engage in the taxicab business within the city.
Waiting time means the time when the cab is not in motion, beginning with the arrival of such cab at the point to which it has been called or the time consumed while standing at the direction of the passenger.
(Ord. No. 05-2006, 4-10-06)
Sec. 78-2. - Permits required.
No permit under this chapter is required if the operation of the taxicab in the city is limited solely to passing through the city limits without picking up a passenger or dropping off a passenger and if the trip of the passenger(s) inside the taxicab originated outside the city. Other than this exception, it shall be unlawful for any taxicab to be operated on the highways of the city unless and until the operator and driver comply with the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 05-2006, 4-10-06)
Sec. 78-3. - Classes of permits.
(a)
Three permit classifications. There shall be three classes of permits required under this chapter:
(1)
Company permit. This permit applies to persons, firms, partnerships, corporations or other entities engaged in the business of operating a taxicab in the city which are subject to an occupation tax under city regulations. Companies securing a company permit may only operate and carry on their business from the established place of business referred to and described in the application for said occupation tax permit.
(2)
Resident operator's permit. This permit applies to any person operating a taxicab on the streets of the city, who is operating under the auspices of a company permit.
(3)
Nonresident operator's permit. This permit applies to any person operating a taxicab on the streets of the city, who is not operating under the auspices of a company subject to an occupation tax in the city.
(b)
Requirements for a company permit. In order to secure a company permit, an applicant must provide information showing its qualifications on a form provided by the city, and must submit to a background investigation and fingerprinting. At a minimum, an applicant for a company permit must:
(1)
Be at least 21 years of age;
(2)
Be a citizen of the United States, an alien admitted for permanent residence or a person who has otherwise been granted employment authorized by the United States Immigration and Naturalization Service;
(3)
Hold a current occupation tax certificate issued by the city;
(4)
Maintain an office within the city limits staffed by company employees at all times during hours of operation from which all dispatching of vehicles will be performed;
(5)
Provide current certificate(s) of insurance evidencing that the applicant has obtained automobile liability insurance procured from a licensed insurance company within the state in the same amounts per taxicab as required of all vehicles by the state with respect to personal injury, liability, property damage liability and personal injury benefits available to drivers/operators, passengers, pedestrians and others. The insurance must provide specific coverage for a vehicle used in the business of transporting passengers (self insurance programs approved for commercial vehicles by the state insurance commissioners shall satisfy this requirement); and
(6)
Not be disqualified based upon conviction for any of the following offenses:
a.
Manslaughter or negligent homicide resulting from the operation of a motor vehicle;
b.
Driving a motor vehicle while under the influence of intoxicating liquor or drugs in the last ten years;
c.
Any felony in the commission of which a motor vehicle was used;
d.
Failure to stop and render aid or leaving the scene of an accident;
e.
Perjury or false swearing in making statements under oath in connection with this application for a permit;
f.
Perjury or false affidavit or statements under oath relating to the ownership or operation of motor vehicles;
g.
Conviction or forfeiture of bail on three charges of a violation of any state's motor vehicle laws within any 12-month period;
h.
The violation of any law involving moral turpitude; or
i.
Repeated violation of the city ordinances, county laws or any state's laws, which affect the safety of human life or limb on the streets of the city.
(c)
Requirements for all drivers/operators. All drivers operating a taxicab within the city limits shall:
(1)
Possess a class C driver's license;
(2)
Be competent to operate a motor vehicle;
(3)
Be familiar with the traffic laws and ordinances of the state and city;
(4)
Provide current certificate(s) of insurance evidencing that the applicant has obtained automobile liability insurance procured from a licensed insurance company within the state in the same amounts per taxicab as required of all vehicles by the state with respect to personal injury, liability, property damage liability and personal injury benefits available to drivers/operators, passengers, pedestrians and others. The insurance must provide specific coverage for a vehicle used in the business of transporting passengers (self insurance programs approved for commercial vehicles by the state insurance commissioners shall satisfy this requirement);
(5)
Maintain residency in the state;
(6)
Not be disqualified based upon conviction for any of the following offenses:
a.
Manslaughter or negligent homicide resulting from the operation of a motor vehicle;
b.
Driving a motor vehicle while under the influence of intoxicating liquor or drugs in the last ten years;
c.
Any felony in the commission of which a motor vehicle was used;
d.
Failure to stop and render aid or leaving the scene of an accident;
e.
Perjury or false swearing in making statements under oath in connection with this application for a permit;
f.
Perjury or false affidavit or statements under oath relating to the ownership or operation of motor vehicles;
g.
Conviction or forfeiture of bail on three charges of a violation of any state's motor vehicle laws within any 12-month period;
h.
The violation of any law involving moral turpitude; or
i.
Repeated violation of the city ordinances, county laws or any state's laws, which affect the safety of human life or limb on the streets of the city.
(7)
Be a citizen of the United States, an alien admitted for permanent residence or a person who has otherwise been granted employment authorized by the United States Immigration and Naturalization Service;
(8)
Not have been classified as a habitual offender by the department of public safety in the previous ten years;
(9)
Not have had a driver's license revoked by any state in the past ten years;
(10)
Provide authorization and any information necessary to enable the chief of police or his/her designee to investigate the driver's background, criminal history and traffic records;
(11)
Be of good character as determined by the aforesaid investigation; and
(12)
Possess an operator's permit issued by the city.
(d)
Requirements for all taxicabs/cabs. All taxicabs operating in the city shall:
(1)
Be equipped with a taximeter maintained in good working order at all times. Such taximeter shall be permanently affixed in front of the passengers' seat and be visible to them at all times. After sundown, the face of the taximeter shall be illuminated. It shall be operated mechanically by a mechanism of standard design and construction, driven either from the transmission or from one of the front wheels by a flexible and permanently attached driving mechanism. It shall be sealed at all points and connections that, if manipulated, would affect its correct reading and recording.
(2)
Be affixed with a vehicle permit sticker issued by the chief of police or his designee. Such stickers must at all times be displayed in the upper right corner of the rear window. Each vehicle will be assigned a numbered sticker and that sticker shall not be used on any other vehicle.
(3)
Such marking shall be affixed only by means of permanent vinyl lettering or paint and shall be a minimum of four inches in height. The four-inch lettering requirement can be met if the logo is a minimum of four inches in height and the lettering within the logo is a minimum of two inches in height.
(4)
Be marked with rates on the side of the vehicle so as to be plainly visible to persons seeking to use the taxicab.
(5)
Maintain signage in the passenger compartment stating, in clearly legible lettering of a minimum of one inch in height, "the public is invited to contact the city police department to report any instance of a taxi being operated while not in a clean and safe operating condition.
(6)
Be equipped with a roof light permanently mounted on the roof of the vehicle and maintained in good working order at all times.
(7)
Be maintained in good, safe and serviceable mechanical condition as verified in a statement signed by a technician at a location properly licensed to do business within the state, and be in compliance with all county and state licensing and emission standards.
(8)
Be maintained in a clean condition, free from foreign matter and offensive odors.
(9)
Transport as passengers only the number of persons specified by the vehicle manufacturer.
(10)
The minimum weight of a taxicab shall be 2,000 pounds, as determined by the manufacturer's specifications; and
(11)
Be no more than eight years old.
All taxicabs to be used by permit holders in the city shall be inspected annually and approved by the police department to insure they meet the above-listed requirements. Once a vehicle meets the requirements of inspection and all required fees have been paid, a sticker will be affixed to the right (passenger's side) of the windshield. All taxicabs may be subject to random inspections at any time. The police department may remove the sticker if the vehicle and/or driver comes out of compliance with this chapter. City-issued stickers are non-transferable.
(e)
Requirements for all drivers/operators.
(1)
Requirement to keep log books. Daily trip sheets shall be maintained for all taxis operated within the city. The driver shall retain the trip sheets in the vehicle for a period of 72 hours from the time of the recorded trip, after which they will be maintained in the office of the company permit holder for a period of no less than 120 days. All trip sheets shall be subject to the inspection of the police chief or his designee. As used in this subsection, the term "trip sheet" means a daily log of all passengers, the time, place of entry and the destination of each passenger, the amount charged and an itemization of any personal property left in the vehicle for hire.
(2)
Requirement to carry driver's license and map. No person shall be allowed to drive a taxicab who shall not at all times carry on his/her person a valid state driver's license and a suitable map or street guide of the Metropolitan Atlanta area and Clayton County.
(3)
Stopping to discharge/pickup passengers. It shall be unlawful for any taxicab to stop for the purpose of discharging or receiving passengers except at a point as near to the right-hand curb as possible.
(Ord. No. 05-2006, 4-10-06)
Sec. 78-4. - Permit application and fee.
(a)
Permit application. All permit applications must be completed and submitted to the chief of police or his/her designee on the forms provided by the city. All information requested on the form must be provided. In addition to the information requested on the form, all applicants must produce a copy of their drivers' license, proof of insurance, a complete statements of the business to be engaged in, the number and a description of all vehicles to be operated under the requested permit (including the make, model, year, license plate number and VIN number of each such vehicle) and, if the applicant is a business, copies of incorporation paperwork showing the legal address, officers and registered agent of the business. A non-refundable investigation fee of $100.00 is due at the time of submission of the permit application (regardless of the type of permit), which is designed to cover the costs of the investigation required before the chief of police of his/her designee can make a determination whether to grant the requested permit.
(b)
Permit fee. A regulatory fee of $300.00 shall be required annually for a company permit. A regularly fee of $50.00 shall be required annually for each resident and nonresident operator's permit. The permit fee is in addition to and is separate from the investigation fee described above.
(Ord. No. 05-2006, 4-10-06)
Sec. 78-5. - Transferability.
Any permit issued in accordance with this chapter shall not be transferable.
(Ord. No. 05-2006, 4-10-06)
Sec. 78-6. - Notification of new drivers.
It shall be the duty of a company permit holder to notify the chief of police or his/her designee whenever a new driver is hired or terminated from employment. Failure to so notify can result in revocation of the company permit.
(Ord. No. 05-2006, 4-10-06)
Sec. 78-7. - Denial, suspension and revocation of a permit.
(a)
An application for a permit or a permit issued under this chapter may be denied, suspended or revoked for due cause as defined in subsection (3) hereof.
(b)
The municipal court shall furnish a permit holder a notice setting forth the time, place, and purpose of a hearing at which it intends to consider the revocation or suspension of a permit. After a hearing, if the municipal court determines due cause exists, it may suspend, revoke or place on probation for a maximum of 12 months, with or without conditions, the permit.
(c)
A permit may be denied, suspended or revoked for any of the following reasons:
(1)
Where the applicant furnishes fraudulent or untruthful information or omits information requested in the application for a permit;
(2)
For failure to pay all fees, taxes, penalties or other charges imposed by the provisions of this chapter and of the City Code of Ordinances;
(3)
For failure to maintain all of the general qualifications applicable to the initial issuance of a permit;
(4)
For violation of any part of this chapter by the permit holder, its agents, partners, officers, employees or contractors;
(5)
Allowing the required insurance coverage to lapse shall result in the automatic suspension of the permit; or
(6)
For unauthorized transfer or other misuse of the permit.
(d)
An operator's permit may be suspended or revoked for having three or more moving violations in any 12-month period; and
(e)
Company holders under this chapter are responsible for violations of this chapter by their vehicle operators whether such operators are direct employees or independent contractors.
(Ord. No. 05-2006, 4-10-06)
Sec. 78-8. - Violations.
Repeated violations of traffic laws or ordinances, or of any ordinance provision regulating the conduct of taxicab drivers shall be cause for revocation of said permit to operate in the city.
It is a violation and ground for suspension or revocation of an operator permit for any operator to:
(1)
Commit more than three traffic infractions within any 12-month period;
(2)
Be found guilty of a felony;
(3)
Be guilty of reckless driving or driving or being in physical control of a vehicle while under the influence of alcohol or drugs;
(4)
Fail to deliver to the city police department within 24 hours any property of value left in a vehicle;
(5)
Allow a person to alight from the vehicle while it is in motion or discharge a passenger at any place other than the curb or pavement edge;
(6)
Allow a passenger to consume alcohol, intoxicating substances, or other controlled substances in the vehicle;
(7)
Transport a passenger to a destination by a route that is not the safest and most direct, unless the passenger specifically authorizes such alternate route or indirect route;
(8)
Pick up any additional passengers without the express consent of the original passenger;
(9)
Refuse to pick up as a passenger any person of proper deportment when the vehicle is available, or discriminate against any person because of race, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, age, or mental or sensory handicap;
(10)
Activate the meter when the vehicle is not engaged or fail to activate the meter at the beginning of each trip, unless the trip is made under contract.
(11)
Activate equipment indicating the vehicle is engaged when it is not, or fail to activate such equipment when the vehicle is engaged;
(12)
Engage in any unfair or deceptive acts or practices.
(Ord. No. 05-2006, 4-10-06)
Sec. 78-9. - Enforcement and penalty.
(a)
The police department shall enforce the regulations contained in this chapter.
(b)
Violations of the provisions of this chapter shall be punished by the payment of fines up to $1,000.00. Each separate occurrence shall be deemed a separate offense.
(c)
The violation of the provisions of this chapter by any person, corporation, partnership or other entity may be enjoined by instituting appropriate proceedings for injunction in the courts of competent jurisdiction in this state. Such actions may be maintained notwithstanding that other adequate remedies at law exist. Such actions may be instituted in the name of the city.
(d)
Violations of any provision of this chapter by any permit holder shall be due cause for revocation of any city permit or certificate, after notice and hearing before the city municipal court.
(Ord. No. 05-2006, 4-10-06)
Sec. 78-10. - Miscellaneous.
Nothing in this chapter shall be deemed to prohibit the charging of a flat rate for trips to and from Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport provided that such flat rates are conspicuously posted on the taxicab or set forth on a rate card provided to the customer prior to the trip.
(Ord. No. 05-2006, 4-10-06)
