Editor's note— Ord. No. 2009-11-38, adopted Nov. 30, 2009, deleted the former Ch. 22, Art. II, §§ 22-19—22-29 and enacted a new Art. II as set out herein. The former Art. II pertained to escort services and derived from Ord. No. 2007-02-07, § 1(ch. 11, art. 9, §§ 1—10, § 12), 2-26-2007.

Sec. 22-19. - Compliance.
No person shall conduct the business of an escort and/or dating service in the city without first meeting the requirements of this article and obtaining an escort service license, in addition to obtaining an occupational tax certificate pursuant to chapter 50, article V, pertaining to taxation. Any person violating any of the provisions of this article shall be punished as provided in section 1-7.
(Ord. No. 2009-11-38, 11-30-2009)
Sec. 22-20. - Copy of article provided upon request.
Upon request, the city manager or designee shall provide each applicant hereunder with a copy of this article.
(Ord. No. 2009-11-38, 11-30-2009)
Sec. 22-21. - Information concerning employees to be filed with the city manager; background investigation required.
(a)
All licensees under this article must file with the city manager or designee their home address, home telephone number and place of employment. Changes thereto shall be filed with the city manager or designee within ten days from the date the change becomes effective.
(b)
All employees of the licensee must submit to a background investigation not less than 30 days prior to commencing work to allow for the investigation of the employee. The city manager or designee shall provide the release form used to conduct the background investigation. After the release has been signed, the city shall perform the background investigation of the employee.
(Ord. No. 2009-11-38, 11-30-2009)
Sec. 22-22. - Records of clients to be kept.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the licensee to maintain correct and accurate records of the name and address of the persons receiving escort and/or dating services and to provide the name of the employee providing such service. Records shall be kept for a minimum of three years. These records shall be subject to inspection at any time by the city manager or designee.
(b)
Failure to maintain records as required in this section is a violation of this article.
(Ord. No. 2009-11-38, 11-30-2009)
Sec. 22-23. - Application; information to be given.
(a)
Any person desiring to engage in the business, trade or profession of providing and/or arranging dates, escorts or partners for persons shall, before engaging in such business, trade or profession, file an application for and obtain an escort service license with the city manager or designee. This application shall be typewritten or legibly handwritten and shall contain the following as appendices:
(1)
The applicant shall provide the name, home and business address of applicant, and date of birth, Social Security number and sex.
(2)
The applicant and employees must submit to a background investigation. The city manager or designee shall provide the release form used to conduct the background investigation. After the release form has been signed and witnessed, the background investigation shall be performed by the city. The release form must be signed at least 30 days prior to the issuance of a license to allow for the investigation of the applicant, and, for the employee, at least 30 days prior to the commencement of work to allow for the investigation of the employee.
(3)
The applicant must furnish a list of at least three character witnesses by name, address and telephone number.
(4)
Applicants shall furnish the name and address of any person having previously employed the applicant (in whatever position) for the last two years, if applicable.
(5)
Applicants shall make themselves available for photographing and such other investigation as may be required by the city. The photograph must be taken without head covering unless a signed statement is submitted indicating that the head covering is worn daily for religious or medical reasons. Dark glasses may not be worn in the photographs unless a doctor's statement is submitted supporting the wearing of dark glasses for medical reasons.
(b)
The city manager or designee shall be notified within ten days of any change of ownership and/or partners/employees.
(c)
A corporation, partnership, limited liability company or other business entity being established for the purpose of engaging in the business, trade or profession of providing and/or arranging dates, escorts or partners for persons must also obtain a license.
(Ord. No. 2009-11-38, 11-30-2009)
Sec. 22-24. - Qualifications of applicant.
No escort service license shall be granted to any person under the age of 18 years or who has been convicted, pled guilty or entered a plea of nolo contendere under any federal, state or local law of any crime involving:
(1)
Moral turpitude;
(2)
Illegal gambling;
(3)
Any felony criminal trespass, public indecency or misdemeanor involving any type of sexual-related crime;
(4)
Any theft or violence against person or property;
(5)
Any crime of possession, sale or distribution of illegal drugs;
(6)
Distribution of material depicting nudity or sexual conduct as defined under state law;
(7)
Criminal solicitation to commit any of these listed offenses; or
(8)
Attempts to commit any of these listed offenses for a period of ten years prior to the date of application for such certificate, and has been released from parole or probation.
(Ord. No. 2009-11-38, 11-30-2009)
Sec. 22-25. - Fees.
License fees are established from time to time by resolution of the city council. No license can be issued by the city manager until applicant satisfies the qualifications listed in this article and pays all license fees.
(Ord. No. 2009-11-38, 11-30-2009)
Sec. 22-26. - Unlawful or prohibited activities.
No person under 18 years of age shall be employed by an escort and/or dating service in any capacity.
(Ord. No. 2009-11-38, 11-30-2009)
Sec. 22-27. - Notice of denial of license.
The city manager or designee shall provide the applicant with written notice of the denial of the escort and/or dating service license and said decision shall be made within 60 days of receiving a completed application. The notice of denial shall include the grounds for denial.
(Ord. No. 2009-11-38, 11-30-2009)
Sec. 22-28. - Denial, suspension or revocation of license.
(a)
No license issued hereunder may be transferred.
(b)
Each license granted hereunder shall be subject to denial, suspension or revocation for violation of any rule or regulation of the city now in force or hereafter adopted relating to the operation of the business in question.
(c)
Whenever the city manager or designee determines there is cause to deny, suspend or revoke a license applied for or issued hereunder, the city manager or designee shall provide notice in accordance with section 22-29.
(d)
A license may be denied, suspended or revoked upon one or more of the following grounds:
(1)
The applicant or licensee is engaged in the escort and/or dating service under a false or assumed name or is impersonating another practitioner of a like or different name;
(2)
The applicant or licensee is addicted to the habitual use of intoxicating liquors, narcotics or stimulants to such an extent as to incapacitate such person to the extent that he is unable to perform his duties;
(3)
The applicant or licensee is guilty of fraudulent, false, misleading or deceptive advertising or practices;
(4)
The applicant or licensee has been convicted of or has pled guilty or nolo contendere to any sexual offense as set out in O.C.G.A. title 16, ch. 6 (O.C.G.A. § 16-6-1 et seq.) or to any offense involving the lottery, illegal gambling, illegal possession or sale of narcotics or alcoholic beverages or possession, any theft or violence against person or property, receiving of stolen property, the illegal distribution of material depicting nudity or sexual conduct as defined under state law, moral turpitude, or criminal solicitation of any such offenses, for a period of five years immediately prior to the filing of the application. If after having been granted a license, the licensee pleads guilty or enters a plea of nolo contendere to any of the above offenses, said license shall be subject to suspension and/or revocation;
(5)
The application contains materially false information or the applicant has deliberately sought to falsify information contained therein;
(6)
The applicant or licensee fails to pay all fees, taxes or other charges imposed by the provisions of this article;
(7)
The applicant or licensee fails to maintain all of the general qualifications applicable to initial issuance of a license under this article;
(8)
The applicant or licensee refuses to accept a client solely on the basis of race, color, national origin, religious belief or sex. License holders shall not refuse to accept a client unless the client is obviously intoxicated or dangerous;
(9)
The establishment is a threat or nuisance to public health, safety or welfare; or
(10)
Any other violation of this article.
(Ord. No. 2009-11-38, 11-30-2009)
Sec. 22-29. - Hearings.
(a)
Whenever the city manager or designee determines there is cause to suspend or revoke the license issued hereunder, the city manager or designee shall give the licensee written notice at least 15 days prior to an administrative hearing of the time, place, purpose of the hearing, and a statement or the charge(s) upon which the administrative hearing before the city manager or designee shall be held in accordance with this section.
(b)
The procedure for administrative hearings heard under this chapter by the city manager or designee shall be set forth by the city manager. The city manager or designee shall reach a decision on the matter before them within ten days following the close of the hearing and give written notice of said decision. The decision of the city manager or designee shall be final unless the applicant or licensee files a notice of appeal to the city manager or designee within 30 days of receiving notice of said decision.
(c)
The city council or designated hearing officer shall hear appeals to decisions by the city manager or designee to deny applications for permits, as well as appeals to decisions by the city manager or designee to suspend or revoke such permits.
(d)
Any such appeal to the city council or designated hearing officer shall be subject to de novo review and shall be in accordance with subsections (d), (e) and (f). A hearing before the city council shall be scheduled within 60 days following the receipt by the city manager or designee of the applicant or licensee's notice of appeal.
(e)
Applicants or licensees shall be given written notice of the date, time, and place when the matter at issue will be heard by the city council or designated hearing officer. The applicant or licensee shall be afforded the opportunity to be heard and present evidence. Twenty days' notice shall be deemed reasonable.
(f)
The procedure for hearings of the city council or hearing officer under this chapter shall conform to the administrative hearings procedure as passed by resolution of the city council. Upon close of the public hearing, the city council or hearing officer shall reach a decision on the matter before it, and the decision of the city council or hearing officer shall be final unless the applicant or permit holder applies to the county superior court by filing a petition for writ of certiorari within 30 days of the decision rendered by the city council or hearing officer.
(Ord. No. 2009-11-38, 11-30-2009)
Sec. 22-30. - No proration of license fee.
No license fees shall be prorated.
(Ord. No. 2009-11-38, 11-30-2009)
