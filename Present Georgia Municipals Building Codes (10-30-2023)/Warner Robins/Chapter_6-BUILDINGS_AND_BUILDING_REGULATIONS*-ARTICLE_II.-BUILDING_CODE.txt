ARTICLE II. - BUILDING CODE
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 6-26. - Adoption.
A certain document, one (1) copy of which is on file in the office of the city clerk, being marked and designated as the 2012 International Building Code, is hereby adopted as the building code of the city, which building code regulates the construction, alteration, removal, demolition, equipment, use and occupancy, location and maintenance of buildings and structures, including permits and penalties within the city.
(Code 1977, § 9-1001; Ord. No. 43-89, § 1, 8-21-89; Ord. No. 45-91, § 1, 8-19-91; Ord. No. 52-93, § 1, 10-4-93; Ord. No. 62-04, 12-20-04; Memo. of 7-12-11; Ord. No. 07-14, § 1, 3-17-14)
Charter reference— Adoption of technical codes by reference, § 2.26.
State Law reference— Buildings and housing, § 8-2-20 et seq.
Sec. 6-27. - Additions, insertions, deletions and changes.
Each townhouse shall be considered a separate building and shall be separated from adjoining townhouses by the use of separate exterior walls meeting the requirements of Table 600 for zero clearance from property lines as required for the type of construction, and shall have roof sheathing of noncombustible material, or fire retardant treated wood, or one (1) layer of five-eighths inch (16 mm) type X gypsum board attached to the underside of roof decking, for not less than four (4) feet (1219 mm) width on each side of the exterior dividing wall; or by a party wall, or one layer of five-eighths inch (16 mm) type X gypsum board attached to the underside of the ceiling joist throughout the entire townhouse.
(Ord. No. 46-91, § 2, 8-19-91)
Editor's note— Ordinance No. 45-91, § 8, adopted 8-19-91, repealed in its entirety § 6-27, derived from Code 1977, § 9-1002, regarding additions, insertions, deletions and changes to the building code. Ordinance No. 46-91, § 2, adopted 8-19-91, established a local amendment to the Standard Building Code. Such section, not being specifically amendatory, has been classified herein as § 6-27, regarding additions, insertions, deletions and changes to the building code at the discretion of the editor.
Sec. 6-28. - Requirements not covered by code.
Any requirements necessary for the strength or stability of an existing or proposed building or structure, or for the safety of the occupants thereof, not specifically covered by the code under this article, shall be determined by the building official subject to appeal to the mayor and council.
(Code 1977, § 9-1003)
Sec. 6-29. - Alternate materials and alternate methods of construction.
The provisions of this article are not intended to prevent the use of any material or method of construction not specifically prescribed by this article, provided the alternate has been approved and its use authorized by the building official. The building official shall approve any alternate, provided he finds that the proposed design is satisfactory and complies with the provisions of the Southern Standard Building Code, and that the material, method or work offered is, for the purpose intended, at least the equivalent of that prescribed in the building code in quality, strength, effectiveness, fire-resistance, durability and safety. The building official shall require that sufficient evidence or proof be submitted to substantiate any claims that may be made regarding its use. If, in the opinion of the building official, the evidence and proof are not sufficient to justify approval, the applicant may refer the entire matter to the mayor and council as specified in section 6-32.
(Code 1977, § 9-1004)
Sec. 6-30. - Liability.
Any suit brought against any officer or employee because of an act performed by him in the enforcement of any provision of this article shall be defended by the city attorney until the final termination of the proceedings.
(Code 1977, § 9-1005)
Sec. 6-31. - Reports.
The building official shall annually submit a report to the mayor and council covering the work of the department during the preceding year. He shall incorporate in the report a summary of the decisions of the mayor and council on appeal from his decision during the year.
(Code 1977, § 9-1006)
Sec. 6-32. - Appeals.
(a)
Whenever the building official shall reject or refuse to approve the mode or manner of construction proposed to be followed, or materials to be used in the erection or alteration of a building or structure, or when it is claimed that the provisions of this code do not apply, or that an equally good or more desirable form of construction can be employed in any specific case, or when it is claimed that the true intent and meaning of this article or any of the regulations hereunder have been misconstrued or wrongly interpreted, the owner of the building or structure, or his duly authorized agent, may appeal from the decision of the building official to the mayor and council. Notice of appeal shall be in writing and filed within ninety (90) days after the decision is rendered by the building official.
(b)
In case of a building or structure which, in the opinion of the building official, is unsafe, or dangerous, the building official may, in his order, limit the time for this appeal to a shorter period. Appeals hereunder shall be on forms provided by the building official.
(Code 1977, § 9-1007)
Sec. 6-33. - Fire limits designated.
All of the territory lying within the corporate limits of the city is hereby designated as the fire limits of the city.
(Code 1977, § 9-1008)

All construction or building development shall comply with the provisions and regulations of the National Flood Insurance Program floodplain management criteria promulgated by the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA). Such rules, regulations and subsequent revisions shall be incorporated herein by reference as part of the ordinances of the city. Such rules and regulations shall be found within the zoning regulations of the city.
(Code 1977, § 9-1010)

DIVISION 2. - SITE PLANS FOR COMMERCIAL BUILDINGS

Sec. 6-51. - Site plan to accompany commercial building permit application.
Applications for all commercial building permits shall be accompanied by a site plan.
(Code 1977, § 9-1015)
Sec. 6-52. - Site plan—Information required.
The site plan shall contain at least the following information:
(1)
Generally.
a.
Name, address, telephone number of property owner;
b.
Name, address, telephone number of person or firm preparing site plan;
c.
North arrow and scale;
d.
Distance to nearest land lot line or right-of-way intersection;
e.
Date;
f.
Title or project description.
(2)
Existing conditions.
a.
Existing contours or spot elevations;
b.
Location of existing structures, curbing, drives, etc.;
c.
Location of existing utilities (sanitary sewer, water, gas) with elevation data;
d.
Location of existing drainage facilities (ditches, culverts, inlets) with elevation data.
(3)
Proposed conditions.
a.
Proposed contours or spot elevations;
b.
Location of proposed structures, curbing, drives, etc.;
c.
Details of typical driveway entrance;
d.
Detail of pavement patch, if applicable;
e.
Parking layout;
f.
Drainage layout, with elevations;
g.
Utility layout, with elevations;
h.
Floodway limits (if applicable).
(Code 1977, § 9-1016)
Sec. 6-53. - Same—Review by city engineering department.
Such site plans shall be referred to the city engineering department for review immediately upon receipt. The city engineer shall act as promptly as possible, but in not more than five (5) days, barring extenuating circumstances.
(Code 1977, § 9-1017; Ord. No. 03-09, 2-17-2009)
Sec. 6-54. - Same—Additional information required by city engineering department.
If the city engineering department is unable to determine compliance with applicable laws or regulations from the original site plan, he may require additional plans and/or information and shall have five (5) days from the receipt of such plans and/or information in which to act.
(Code 1977, § 9-1018; Ord. No. 03-09, 2-17-2009)
Sec. 6-55. - Same—Approval prerequisite to issuance of building permit.
No permit for building construction shall be issued for a commercial lot until a site plan is approved by the city engineering department and the building official.
(Code 1977, § 9-1019; Ord. No. 03-09, 2-17-2009)
Sec. 6-56. - Same—Procedure on denial of permit.
If the decision is to deny the permit, the building official shall return the drawings to the applicant with his refusal to issue such permit. Such refusal shall, when requested, be in writing and shall contain the reasons therefor.
(Code 1977, § 9-1020)
Sec. 6-57. - Same—Right of appeal.
The applicant under this division shall have the right to appeal as set forth in section 6-32.
(Code 1977, § 9-1020.1)
Secs. 6-58—6-75. - Reserved.
