ARTICLE IV. - BUSINESS LICENSE

Sec. 10-65. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them on this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Business means business, trade, occupation, profession, avocation or calling of any kind, subject, by the provisions of this article, to a business license fee.
Engaged in business. Any person shall be deemed to be engaged in business and thus subject to the requirements of this article when he performs any act of selling goods or services or solicits business either as an owner, operator, or agent in any business, trade, profession or occupation within the city.
Person means any person, firm, partnership, corporation, association or group of individuals, or their representatives, acting as a unit.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-66. - Due date for business license.
License [fees] are due when a person starts engaging in an occupation or business as to which there is a license required under this chapter or any other ordinance of the city, and if not paid within thirty (30) days, then execution therefor shall issue as previously prescribed herein.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-67. - Manner, time of issuing execution.
Executions for nonpayment of business license and other indebtedness due the city shall be issued by the city clerk or tax clerk of the city when delinquent.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-68. - City marshal, code enforcement officer designated; enforce city code and business license.
It shall be the duty of the city marshal and/or code enforcement office to enforce all codes prescribed herein.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-69. - Levy fee; procedure when license not listed.
(a)
A business license fee is hereby assessed against every person engaging in each of the businesses listed in the municipal code book, hereby incorporated herein, in the amounts specified in the schedule.
(b)
When any person desires a license not listed, the business must first obtain from mayor and council their approval and have a fee set therefor.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-70. - Separate businesses.
Where a business conducts a business at more than one (1) store, location or place, each store, location or place shall be considered a separate business under the terms of this article and a separate business under the terms of this article and a separate business license fee shall be levied and paid, and should more than one (1) business license fee is levied by this article be conducted in or conjunction with one place or kind of business, each business shall be subject to a separate business license fee.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-71. - Registration required.
(a)
Every person engaging in any business in the city upon which a fee is levied by this article shall register the business with the clerk on or before January 30th of each year, upon forms to be supplied by the city clerk, giving the name, address and type of business and such other information as may be required by the clerk for the purpose of determining the amount of fee to be assessed by this article. Any person who begins business for a trade or profession in the city after January 30th in any year shall register his name and business as above provided.
(b)
All persons holding valid business licenses on or before December 31st shall be presumed to be continually engaged in business for the subsequent year for purposes of license renewal.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-72. - Payment of tax or fees.
All licenses assessed and fixed in this article shall be due and payable on or before January 30th of each year, except that in the case of any business commenced thereafter, the fees and taxes shall be due and payable when the business is commenced. Payment of the fee may be made by mail or personally to the city clerk following city approval of the business locations. The fee shall be payable in a single installment. The license shall expire on December 31st of each year.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-73. - Issuance of business license or tax certificate.
Under the payment of the license fees assessed in this article, it shall be the duty of the city clerk to issue to each person a business license provided that all information required by the clerk has been furnished; and provided further that the applicant is not disentitled to a license under other provisions of this Code, or federal or state law. Any corporate applicant may be required to produce a certificate from the Secretary of State of Georgia that such corporation is in good standing according to the records of the office.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-74. - Certificate to be displayed.
All person shall exhibit and display the certificate in some conspicuous place in their business establishment and shall exhibit it to any authorized enforcement officer of the city when requested to do so.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-75. - Accountability.
It shall be the responsibility of the city clerk to faithfully account for and pay over all monies to the city depository as of the end of each business day upon such forms or under such rules and regulations as the mayor and council may prescribe.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-76. - Change of address.
Any person moving from one location to another shall notify the city clerk of this move and the new address in writing no later than thirty (30) days after moving. The same business license certificate will be valid at the new location after change of address.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-77. - Commencing business after January first.
When any person commences business on or after January 1 in any year, the regulatory fee and the business or occupation tax for the remaining portion of the year shall be fifty (50) percent of the tax imposed for the entire year. [Such regulatory fee] will be pro-rated quarterly.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-78. - Classification.
(a)
In all cases the person applying for a license shall make oath, when required to do so, that the business conducted by the applicant falls within the class to which the ordinance fixing the license specifically applies.
(b)
Whenever in order to insure the proper collection of the licenses herein imposed and levied, it is necessary to examine the books of a licensee to determine his compliance with this article, it shall be lawful for any authorized representative of the city to examine the books and records of any licensee whose books and records are necessary to this determination. Each licensee affected hereby shall allow reasonable access to these books and records upon demand, and upon failure to do so, shall be subject to the penal provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-79. - How payable.
(a)
Licenses levied and collected under any of the foregoing sections shall be payable in United States currency or coupons of bonds, as provided in the act authorizing the issue of the bonds.
(b)
In the event any license, or any part thereof herein imposed shall be judged invalid for any reason, person engaging in the business or occupation falling under this invalid license, or part thereof, who has not paid his license here imposed, shall be liable and shall pay to the city the same license paid for the preceding year.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-80. - Past due licenses.
A condition to the issuance of any license shall be payment by the applicant of all past due licenses.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-81. - Penalties.
(a)
Any person entitled to have a license issued to him under the provisions of this article who does not pay for it within sixty (60) days from the time he first engages in business within the calendar year shall be subject to prosecution for doing business without a license.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to whom a license shall be granted under the provisions of this article to do any of the acts forbidden by this article, or to fail to do any of the things required to be done by the terms hereof, and it shall be unlawful for any persons to whom a license shall be granted to violate the terms hereof.
(c)
In addition to all other penalties herein provided, any person failing to obtain a license within thirty (30) days from the time he first engages in a business during the calendar year shall pay a penalty of twenty-five (25) percent of the scheduled amount of the license fee, which shall be added to and collected with the scheduled amount.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-82. - Revocation of business licenses.
(a)
No license which has been issued or which may hereafter be issued shall be revoked except for due cause as hereinafter defined.
(b)
Failure to pay city taxes, fees or other revenue due to the city within thirty (30) days of the due date shall constitute grounds for revocation.
(c)
Conviction of the licensee or an employee of same for any violation of federal or state law or city ordinance involving the operation of the licensed business shall constitute grounds for revocation.
(d)
An initial license may be denied of the applicant has been convicted of such violations at other locations in the past.
(e)
At the option of mayor and council a violation as set forth in subsection (c) hereof, as evidenced by a certified copy of the conviction, shall constitute grounds for refusal to renew said license rather than to revoke the current license. In such event the licensee may apply, by written request, for a hearing before mayor and council within thirty (30) days of such refusal to renew.
(f)
In the event mayor and council desire to revoke a current license based on a violation set out herein, they shall cause notice to be served upon the licensee, by registered or certified mail addresses to licensee's establishment, requiring the licensee to appear before mayor and council and show cause why the license should not be revoked. This hearing shall not be held sooner than five (5) days from the mailing of the notice and the notice shall specify the date, time and place of the hearing as well as the ground which could, if sustained, result in the revocation of the license.
(g)
Mayor and council shall notify the licensee of their decision within a reasonable time after the hearing in the same manner as provided above for service of notice of hearing. If such decision is to revoke the license no rebate shall be made on any portion of the license fee paid.
(h)
In lieu of revocation, mayor and council may, in their discretion, suspend the license for a specified period of time, place the licensee on probation or impose other conditions on the continued holding of the license.
(i)
The licensee may remain open for business after the hearing until a decision is reached and three (3) days have elapsed since the mailing of the decision to him.
(j)
This section shall not apply to alcoholic beverage licenses.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
Sec. 10-83. - Licensed premises to comply with zoning, fire and building regulations.
No license shall be issued under this article if the proposed premises do not comply with the zoning ordinances, the fire code or the building code of the city, nor shall any license issued hereunder authorize activity not permitted by the zoning ordinance, fire code or the building code of the city.
(Ord. No. 48-94, 11-21-94)
