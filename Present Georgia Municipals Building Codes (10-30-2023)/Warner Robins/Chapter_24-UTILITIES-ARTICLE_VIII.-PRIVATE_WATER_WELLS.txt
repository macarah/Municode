ARTICLE VIII. - PRIVATE WATER WELLS[7]

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 24-321. - Drilling for water within one-half mile of city wells.
No person shall drill, continue to drill or operate any water well within the city which shall be located or proposed to be located within one-half mile from any well which, in operation or under construction for the supply of water, constitutes or may constitute a part of the public water system of the city.
(Ord. No. 25-91, § 1, 4-15-91)
Sec. 24-322. - Private wells to supply only for specific purposes.
It shall be unlawful to drill or continue to drill any private water well for the purpose of supplying water therefrom or to supply water from any such private water well to any persons for domestic use other than for irrigation purposes; provided, however, that private water wells shall be allowed for industrial users under the terms and conditions contained in section 24-336 herein. Specifically prohibited is the use of any private water well for the purpose of supplying water for use within any dwelling house, commercial or business establishment within the city except as otherwise provided in this article.
(Ord. No. 25-91, § 2, 4-15-91; Ord. No. 85-99, § 1, 2-18-99)
Sec. 24-323. - Enforcement.
The city marshal shall have the power to prohibit the drinking or cooking use of water from any well or spring.
(Ord. No. 25-91, § 3, 4-15-91)
Sec. 24-324. - Open wells, cisterns.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate or maintain or permit to be operated or maintained any well or cistern within the city unless the same shall be covered or protected by an enclosure sufficient to prevent any person from falling into or entering such well or cistern.
(b)
Any covering or enclosure of any such well or cistern shall and must be approved by the building official.
(c)
The requirements of this section shall not prevent the owner or operator of such well or cistern from entering such well or cistern for the purpose of maintenance and operation purposes.
(d)
Any person violating any provisions of this division shall upon conviction therefor be punished as prescribed in section 1-7(c).
(Ord. No. 25-91, § 4, 4-15-91)
Sec. 24-325. - Log of new wells.
Every person, firm, or corporation to whom the utility director has issued a permit under the provisions of this article to construct a well shall, within thirty (30) days of the completion of the drilling, digging, boring, or excavating authorized by such permit, furnish the utility director with a log of such well. The log shall include information on the type of casing, the number and location of the perforations therein, the depth of the well and soil types at all levels of the well, as well as any other data requested by the utility director.
(Ord. No. 25-91, § 10, 4-15-91)
Sec. 24-326. - Wells constructed prior to 1991.
Nothing contained in this article shall prohibit the continued operation of any water well which had been constructed and was in continuous use on April 15, 1991, which may be within one-half mile of a city well; however, any modifications to any such wells must meet all the requirements of this article.
(Ord. No. 25-91, § 11, 4-15-91)
Secs. 24-327—24-330. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PERMITS

Sec. 24-331. - Required for construction, demolition of well.
No person, firm, or corporation shall dig, bore, deepen, or excavate any well or destroy an existing well unless the person, firm, or corporation has applied for and obtained a permit therefor from the utility department pursuant to the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 25-91, § 5, 4-15-91)
Sec. 24-332. - Investigation fee.
Each application for a permit required under this article shall be accompanied by an inspection and enforcement fee of fifty dollars ($50.00), no part of which shall be refundable.
(Ord. No. 25-91, § 6, 4-15-91)
Sec. 24-333. - Issuance.
If after investigation of the proposed well site and verification of the data supplied in the application the utility director determines that the proposed construction, destruction, or conversion of the well both complies with this article and is not likely to cause pollution or contamination of either the underground water or the well water, he shall issue a permit for such construction, destruction, or conversion.
(Ord. No. 25-91, § 7, 4-15-91)
Sec. 24-334. - Scope.
A permit issued pursuant to this article shall be valid to authorize only the proposed construction, destruction, or conversion described therein on the site specified.
(Ord. No. 25-91, § 8, 4-15-91)
Sec. 24-335. - Suspension or revocation.
The utility director may, upon ten (10) days' written notice, suspend or revoke any permit issued pursuant to this article where he determines the permittee either to have exceeded the scope of the permit or to have violated any other provision of this article.
(Ord. No. 25-91, § 9, 4-15-91)
Sec. 24-336. - Private water wells for industries.
When, in the sole discretion of the utility director and approved by the mayor (which discretion shall not be unreasonably withheld), it is determined that the city is unable to provide, at a cost reasonable to the city, water at a specified quality required for an industrial customer, the user shall be authorized to build a private water well under the following terms and conditions:
(1)
The water from such private water well shall only be used as an ingredient of the end-product of the industry, and all other potable water necessary at the industrial site shall be obtained from the city's water distribution system;
(2)
As required by section 24-321, the well is not within one-half mile of a well which is a part of the city's water system; and
(3)
The user shall obtain any necessary state or federal permits.
(Ord. No. 85-99, § 2, 2-18-99)
Secs. 24-337—24-350. - Reserved.
