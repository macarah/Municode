ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 46-1. - Disorderly conduct.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to:
(1)
Act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another, whereby any person is placed in reasonable fear of receiving a violent injury.
(2)
Act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another, whereby public property or property of any other person is placed in danger of being destroyed or damaged.
(3)
Endanger lawful pursuits of another by acts of violence, angry threats and abusive conduct.
(4)
Cause, provoke or engage in any fight, brawl or riotous conduct so as to endanger the life, limb, health or property of another or public property.
(5)
Assemble or congregate with another or others for the purpose of causing, provoking or engaging in any fight or brawl.
(6)
Jostle or roughly crowd or push any person in any public place.
(7)
Collect with others in bodies or in crowds for any unlawful purpose.
(8)
Assemble or congregate with another for the purpose of or with intent to engage in gaming.
(9)
Frequent any public place with the intent to obtain money from another by an illegal and fraudulent scheme, trick, artifice or device.
(10)
Assemble with another for the purpose of engaging in any fraudulent scheme, device or trick to obtain any valuable thing in any place or from any person in the county, or aids or abets therein.
(11)
Frequent any place where gaming or the illegal sale or possession of alcoholic beverages or narcotics or dangerous drugs is practiced, allowed or tolerated.
(12)
Utter, while in a state of anger, in the presence of another, any lewd or obscene words or epithets, which by their utterance tend to incite an immediate breach of the peace.
(13)
Use "fighting words" to or of another, in another's presence, which by their very utterance tend to incite an immediate breach of the peace.
(14)
Assemble or congregate with another for the purpose of doing bodily harm to another.
(15)
Act in a dangerous manner toward another.
(16)
Interfere, by acts of violence, with another's pursuit of a lawful occupation.
(17)
Congregate with another in or on any public way so as to halt the flow of vehicular or pedestrian traffic and refuse to clear such public way when ordered to do so by a peace officer or other person having authority.
(18)
Make any unreasonably loud and unnecessary noise.
(19)
Damage, befoul or disturb public property or the property of another, so as to create a hazardous, unhealthy or physically offensive condition.
(20)
Consume alcoholic beverages on any public street, sidewalk or any other public property within the county. Possession of open containers of alcoholic beverages or containers with the seal broken shall be prima facie evidence of consumption.
(21)
Consume alcoholic beverages whether in a vehicle or not, on any public or private parking lot within the county. Possession of open containers of alcoholic beverages or containers with the seal broken shall be prima facie evidence of consumption.
(22)
Possess, have under your control or use marijuana in an amount less than one ounce on any public or private street or property within the county.
(b)
Serious disorderly conduct. Serious disorderly conduct will exist when any of the above acts include the use of a firearm, knife or bodily injury is sustained during an act described in subsection (a) of this section.
(c)
Prosecution and penalties.
(1)
Any violation of this section may be prosecuted upon citation without a prosecuting attorney.
(2)
The county sheriff and his deputies are authorized to issue citations for violations of this section. The original of the citation shall be personally served upon the accused and a copy promptly filed with the court.
(3)
Each citation shall state the time and place at which the accused is to appear for trial, shall identify the offense with which the accused is charged, shall have an identifying number by which it may be filed with the court, shall indicate the identity of the accused and the date of service, and shall be signed by the officer who completes and serves it.
(4)
Any person arrested prior to the time of trial shall be permitted to post a cash bond for his future appearance in an amount established by the chief magistrate of the county; any person arrested who fails to post such bond shall be brought before a judicial officer within 24 hours of arrest or the next business day.
(5)
Any person arrested for violation of this section who has a blood or breath alcohol content of 0.05 percent or higher will be required to remain incarcerated for a minimum of four hours.
(Res. No. 94-05-01, 5-3-1994)

ARTICLE II. - DRUGFREE ZONES

Sec. 46-31. - Designation; enumeration.
Those areas of the county more particularly contained in the following list, being within 1,000 feet of parks, playgrounds, recreation centers, and other areas used for recreational purposes are hereby designated as drugfree zones:
(1)
Turner County Courthouse - 219 East College Avenue
(2)
Old Jail - 221 East College Avenue
(3)
Civic Center - 354 Lamar Street
(4)
Turner County Road Department
(5)
Emergency Medical Services (Office and Warehouse) - 754 East Hudson Avenue
(6)
Turner County Airport
(7)
Dakota Fire Station - US Hwy 41 North
(8)
Coverdale Fire Station - Sycamore to Coverdale Road
(9)
Amboy Fire Station - State Route 159
(10)
Bethel Fire Station - Purcell Road
(11)
Extension Service - 484 County Farm Road
(12)
Victoria Evans Memorial Library - North Street
(13)
Elections Office - East Hudson Avenue
(14)
Turner County Health Department
(15)
Courthouse Annex Building - 208 East College Avenue
(16)
Sheriff's Office and Jail - 1301 Industrial Drive
(17)
Inaha Fire Station - East Inaha Road
(18)
Harmony Fire Station - Whiddon Road
(Res. No. 2002-03-03, § 1, 3-6-2001)
Sec. 46-32. - Signage.
Signs designating areas within 1,000 feet of any lands and buildings set apart for the use as parks, playgrounds, recreation centers and other areas used for recreational purposes shall be posted by the county's personnel.
(Res. No. 2002-03-03, § 2, 3-6-2001)
