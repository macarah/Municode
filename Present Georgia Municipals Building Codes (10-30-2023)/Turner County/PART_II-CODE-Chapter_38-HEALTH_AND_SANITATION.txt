Sec. 38-31. - Existing rules and regulations adopted by reference.
There are currently in existence certain rules and regulations adopted by the county board of health and the state department of human resources with the purpose of preserving the public health, safety, and welfare of citizens of the county. This article specifically incorporates by reference the provision of the following rules and regulations which are in force at the time of the adoption of Resolution No. 93-003 (July 6, 1993), and as amended:
(1)
Rabies control rules and regulations of the county;
(2)
Rules and regulations for on-site sewage management systems of the county;
(3)
Rules and regulations for water supply of the county;
(4)
Rules and regulations pertaining to food service (adopted by the department of human resources and the county board of health and being chapter 290-5-14);
(5)
Rules and regulations for public swimming pools, spas and natural beaches of the county; and
(6)
Rules and regulations for tourist courts (adopted by the department of human resources and the county board of health and being chapter 290-5-18).
(Res. No. 93-003, § I, 7-6-1993)
Sec. 38-32. - Implementation and enforcement.
The duty of implementation and enforcement of the regulations of this article rest with the county board of commissioners, and by virtue of this article is hereby expressly delegated to the county board of health who is hereby charged with promulgation of sufficient procedures for ensuring compliance with the rules and regulations of this article by the citizens of the county. The county board of health is directed to implement procedures which will ensure that the citizens of the county will receive all procedural rights to which they are entitled under the laws of the state.
(Res. No. 93-003, § II, 7-6-1993)
Sec. 38-33. - Jurisdiction of magistrate's court.
The county magistrate's court shall have jurisdiction over violators of this article, and all procedures for enforcement of such article shall be as provided in O.C.G.A. tit. 15, ch. 10, art. 4 (O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq.). Complaints of violation of any provision of this article shall be brought before the magistrate's court by a designated representative of the county board of health and shall be prosecuted through that court.
(Res. No. 93-003, § IV, 7-6-1993)
