ARTICLE II. - HOUSE AND BUILDING NUMBERING

FOOTNOTE(S):
--- (2) ---
Cross reference— Zoning, app. A.
Sec. 18-31. - Purposes of article provisions.
The purposes of this article are to ensure that all buildings and houses in the county have properly displayed the house or building number designated for that particular house or building. The county board of commissioners recognizes that the proper display of such designated numbers is of benefit to the citizens of the county to, among other things, provide for accurate information in regard to providing county services and for emergency situations.
(Ord. No. 94-11-01, § 1, 11-1-1994)
Sec. 18-32. - Placement of assigned numbers; removal of old numbers.
After a house or building has been assigned its respective number or numbers, or after a house or building has been designated a new number, the owner, occupant or agent of any such house or building shall place or cause to be placed upon each house or building under the system established by the county or its designee, and all incorrect numbers and numbers which do not conform with the provisions of this article shall simultaneously be removed.
(Ord. No. 94-11-01, § 2, 11-1-1994)
Sec. 18-33. - Effective date of numbers.
All numbers assigned to houses and buildings shall be placed on such houses and buildings within 20 days after such numbers are assigned.
(Ord. No. 94-11-01, § 3, 11-1-1994)
Sec. 18-34. - Cost of numbers; responsibility of property owners.
The cost of numbers shall be borne by the property owner. The number shall not be less than three inches in height and shall be made of a durable and clearly visible material.
(Ord. No. 94-11-01, § 4, 11-1-1994)
Sec. 18-35. - Location of numbers.
The numbers shall be conspicuously placed immediately above, on or at the side of the proper door of each building or house so that the number can be seen plainly from the roadway. Whenever any building or house is situated more than 50 feet from the roadway, such number shall be placed near the walk, driveway or common entrance to such building or house, or upon a gatepost, fencepost or other appropriate place so as to be easily discernible from the roadway.
(Ord. No. 94-11-01, § 5, 11-1-1994)
Sec. 18-36. - Administration.
Upon application by any person to which this article applies requesting the correct property number of such person's property, the county, or its designee, shall provide such person with the correct number.
(Ord. No. 94-11-01, § 6, 11-1-1994)
Cross reference— Administration, ch. 2.
Sec. 18-37. - New building; numbers required.
Whenever any house or building shall be erected or located in the county, the owner shall procure the correct number or numbers as designated by the county or its designee for the property and shall immediately fasten the number or numbers so assigned upon such house or buildings as provided by this article.
(Ord. No. 94-11-01, § 7, 11-1-1994)
Sec. 18-38. - Enforcement.
Ordinance violations may be tried upon citations with or without a prosecuting attorney as well as upon accusations, as provided in O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq. Citations shall be issued by the board of commissioners or its designee. The county may, but is not required to, notify any persons subject to this article of noncompliance, and if such noncompliance is not rectified, such person shall be subject to prosecution as provided for in this section.
(Ord. No. 94-11-01, § 8, 11-1-1994)

ARTICLE III. - BUILDING CODES

Sec. 18-71. - Adoption.
The following codes are hereby adopted:
Standard Building Code
Standard Fire Prevention Code
Standard Mechanical Code
Standard Plumbing Code
National Electrical Code
CABO One- and Two-Family Dwelling Code
International Energy Conservation Code
Standard Gas Code
The latest editions of such codes shall be those adopted by the state department of community affairs.
(Res. No. 2000-01-02, 1-20-2000)
Sec. 18-72. - Conformity with article regulations; permits required.
No buildings, structures, or land shall be used or occupied, and no building, structure or part thereof shall be erected, constructed, reconstructed, moved, enlarged, or structurally altered unless in conformity with the regulations of this article. All contractors, builders or landowners shall be required to purchase permits before any construction within the county shall begin. Georgia Power, county EMC, or any other electrical supplier shall not connect or supply any building, structure or manufactured home with power until an electrical permit has been issued.
(Res. No. 2000-01-02, 1-20-2000)
Sec. 18-73. - Exemptions from permit requirements.
Although a permit is required, any farm shelter or structure which does not have plumbing and electricity shall not be required to pay a fee for such permit. Nothing contained in this article shall be construed to be restrictive in any way to the operation of a bona fide farm or the production of agricultural products including produce, livestock and forest products. This exclusion shall not apply to buildings constructed for human occupancy.
(Res. No. 2000-01-02, 1-20-2000)
Sec. 18-74. - Enforcement personnel.
The county board of commissioners shall employ inspectors and any other personnel necessary for the enforcement of such codes. There shall be no right to self-inspections by plumbing and utility contractors. All inspections shall be made by the county inspection officer.
(Res. No. 2000-01-02, 1-20-2000)

ARTICLE IV. - WATER CONSERVATION IN CONSTRUCTION

Sec. 18-111. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Commercial means any type of building other than residential.
Construction means the erection of a new building or the alteration of an existing building in connection with its repair or renovation or in connection with making an addition to an existing building and shall include the replacement of a malfunctioning, unserviceable or obsolete faucet, showerhead, toilet, or urinal in an existing building.
Residential means any building or unit of building intended for occupancy as a dwelling but shall not include a hotel or motel.
(Ord. of 3-5-1991, § 1)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 18-112. - Initiation of residential construction after July 1, 1991.
On or after July 1, 1991, no construction may be initiated within the county for any residential building of any type which:
(1)
Employs a gravity tank-type, flushometer-valve, or flushometer-tank toilet that uses more than an average of 1.6 gallons of water per flush; provided, however, that this subsection shall not be applicable to one-piece toilets until July 1, 1992.
(2)
Employs a showerhead that allows a flow of more than average of 2.5 gallons of water per minute at 60 pounds per square inch of pressure.
(3)
Employs a urinal that uses more than an average of one gallon of water per flush.
(4)
Employs a lavatory faucet or lavatory replacement aerator that allows a flow of more than two gallons of water per minute.
(5)
Employs a kitchen faucet or kitchen replacement aerator that allows a flow of more than 2.5 gallons of water per minute.
(Ord. of 3-5-1991, § 2)
Sec. 18-113. - Initiation of commercial building construction on or after July 1, 1992.
On or after July 1, 1992, there shall be no construction of any commercial building initiated within the county for any commercial building of any type which does not meet the requirements of section 18-112.
(Ord. of 3-5-1991, § 3)
Sec. 18-114. - Application of section 18-112 requirements.
The requirements of section 18-112 shall apply to any residential construction initiated after July 1, 1991, and to any commercial construction initiated after July 1, 1992, which involves the repair or renovation of or addition to any existing building when such repair or renovation of or addition to such existing building includes replacement of toilets or showers, or both.
(Ord. of 3-5-1991, § 4)
Sec. 18-115. - Exemptions.
New construction and the repair or renovation of an existing building shall be exempt from the requirements of sections 18-112—18-114 when:
(1)
The repair or renovation of the existing building does not include the replacement of the plumbing or sewage system servicing toilets, faucets or showerheads within such existing buildings;
(2)
When such plumbing or sewage system within such existing building, because of its capacity, design, or installation would not function properly if the toilets, faucets or showerheads required by this article were installed;
(3)
Such system is a well or gravity flow from a spring and is owned privately by an individual for use in such individual's personal residence; or
(4)
Units to be installed are:
a.
Specifically designed for use by persons with disabilities;
b.
Specifically designed to withstand unusual abuse or installation in a penal institution; or
c.
Toilets for juveniles.
(Ord. of 3-5-1991, § 5)
