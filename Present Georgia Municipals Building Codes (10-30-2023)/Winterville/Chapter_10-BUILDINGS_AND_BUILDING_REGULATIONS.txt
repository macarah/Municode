Sec. 10-1. - Adoption of state codes.
The following state minimum standards codes and permissive codes, as set forth in O.C.G.A. §§ 8-2-20 and 8-2-21, are adopted by the city as though fully set forth herein, as supplemented and amended by the state department of community affairs, and as may be amended by provisions of this chapter or of this Code generally:
(1)
International Building Code, 2012 edition, with Georgia Amendments, 2014, 2015, 2017 and 2018.
(2)
International Residential Code, 2012 edition, with Georgia Amendments, 2014, 2015 and 2018.
(3)
International Fire Code, 2012 edition, with Georgia Amendments, 2014.
(4)
International Plumbing Code, 2012 edition, with Georgia Amendments, 2014 and 2015.
(5)
International Mechanical Code, 2012 edition, with Georgia Amendments, 2014 and 2015.
(6)
International Fuel Gas Code, 2012 edition, with Georgia Amendments, 2014 and 2015.
(7)
National Electrical Code, 2011 edition.
(8)
International Energy Conservation Code, 2009 edition, with Georgia Supplements and Amendments, 2011 and 2012.
(9)
International Swimming Pool and Spa Code, 2012 edition, with Georgia Amendments, 2014.
(Code 1991, § 34-101)
Sec. 10-2. - Definitions of terms used in state codes.
(a)
Wherever the term "inspector," as in the term "building inspector," "plumbing inspector," etc., is used in the state codes, it means the person designated by the mayor and council to administer and enforce the provisions of said code.
(b)
Wherever the term "corporate counsel" is used in the state codes, it means the city attorney of this municipality.
(Code 1991, § 34-102)
Sec. 10-3. - Enforcement.
(a)
The county building inspector and the code enforcement officer are hereby authorized to enforce within the city the provisions of this chapter and the state minimum codes adopted herein.
(b)
The building inspector and the code enforcement officer are further authorized to render interpretations of this Code and the adopted state codes and to adopt policies and procedures in order to clarify the application of their provisions. Such interpretations, policies and procedures shall be in compliance with the intent and purpose of this chapter. Such policies and procedures shall not have the force and effect of law and shall not have the effect of waiving requirements specifically provided for in this Code or in the adopted state codes.
Sec. 10-4. - Copies on file.
Copies of the expressly incorporated state codes shall be on file for public inspection in the city clerk's office.
(Code 1991, § 34-103)
