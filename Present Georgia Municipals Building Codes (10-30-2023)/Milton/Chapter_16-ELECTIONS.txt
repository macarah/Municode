ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 16-1. - Compliance with state law required.
All elections and challenges thereto shall be in compliance with the Georgia Election Code codified at O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.
(Ord. No. 06-11-09, § 1(ch. 3, art. 1, § 1), 11-21-2006)
Sec. 16-2. - General elections held on first Tuesday in November.
In accordance with article II, government structure, election, and removal, section 2.11, elections, of the Charter, general municipal elections shall be held on the Tuesday next following the first Monday in November.
(Ord. No. 06-11-09, § 1(ch. 3, art. 1, § 2), 11-21-2006)
State Law reference— Elections and primaries generally, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.; November election, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-9.
Sec. 16-3. - Special elections governed by Charter.
Special elections shall be held as needed according to the city Charter, article II, government structure, election, and removal, section 2.11, elections, and O.C.G.A. title 21.
(Ord. No. 06-11-09, § 1(ch. 3, art. 1, § 3), 11-21-2006)
Sec. 16-4. - Appointment of superintendents.
The city manager shall appoint the municipal superintendent in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 21-2-70.1. The council shall provide for the superintendent in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 21-2-71.
(Ord. No. 06-11-09, § 1(ch. 3, art. 1, § 4), 11-21-2006)
Sec. 16-5. - Conducting elections.
(a)
The city shall coordinate all elections with the Fulton County Board of Elections and Registration. Under the provisions of Georgia Municipal Election Code, particularly O.C.G.A. § 21-2-45, the city may by ordinance contract or otherwise decide by agreement with the Fulton County Board of Elections and Registration for that board to coordinate and conduct all elections in the city.
(b)
In situations where the city has contracted and authorized the county to conduct such election, the Fulton County Board of Registration and Elections shall operate as the "superintendent" of the election and shall perform any and all functions of the city or any of its officials in connection with the conduct of such election or runoff thereof, except as hereinafter provided.
(c)
All other terms of the contract for conducting the election shall be governed by the contract entered into between the city and the Fulton County Board of Registration and Elections. Such terms to include, but not be limited to:
(1)
Payment of the election costs; and
(2)
The county's financial liability for any litigation arising by reason of the election, if any.
(d)
All decisions concerning the qualifications of the candidates shall be determined by the city clerk in accordance with the provisions of the Municipal Election Code and the City Code.
(Ord. No. 06-11-09, § 1(ch. 3, art. 1, § 5), 11-21-2006; Ord. No. 09-06-42, §§ 1—5, 6-1-2009)
Secs. 16-6—16-24. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - CANDIDATE QUALIFICATIONS[2]

State Law reference— Selection and qualification of candidates, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-130; subversive persons not to be nominated or elected, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-7; persons convicted of certain crimes not to be eligible for nomination or election to public office or to serve as primary or election official, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-8.

Sec. 16-25. - Requirements.
Candidates for mayor and city council shall meet the requirements as outlined in the city Charter, article II, government structure, election, and removal, section 2.11, elections.
(Ord. No. 06-11-09, § 1(ch. 3, art. 2, § 1), 11-21-2006)
Sec. 16-26. - Filing notice.
(a)
Governed by city and state law. Filing of the notice of candidacy for municipal office shall be conducted in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 21-2-132, and the city's Charter, article II, government structure, election, and removal, section 2.11, elections.
(b)
Qualifying period. The qualifying period shall commence no earlier than 8:30 a.m. on the last Monday in August immediately preceding the general election and shall end no later than 4:30 p.m. on the following Friday. In special elections, the qualifying period shall commence no earlier than the date of the call and shall end no later than 25 days prior to the election.
(Ord. No. 06-11-09, § 1(ch. 3, art. 2, § 2), 11-21-2006)
Sec. 16-27. - Fees.
(a)
Except as provided in subsection (b) of this section, each candidate shall pay a fee of three percent of the total salary of the office sought. Such fee shall be deposited into the city treasury.
(b)
A pauper's affidavit may be filed in lieu of the qualifying fee in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 21-2-132.
(Ord. No. 06-11-09, § 1(ch. 3, art. 2, § 3), 11-21-2006)
Sec. 16-28. - Withdrawal.
Any candidate may withdraw his or her name as a candidate prior to October 15 of the election year. No refund will be made to any candidate for the fee remitted to qualify for office.
(Ord. No. 06-11-09, § 1(ch. 3, art. 2, § 4), 11-21-2006)
Secs. 16-29—16-47. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - VOTING
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 16-48. - Voter eligibility.
The city council shall provide by contract for the use of the county voter registration list. To be eligible to vote in any municipal primary or election, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 21-2-227, a person must be:
(1)
A resident of the city; and
(2)
Registered as an elector with the county.
(Ord. No. 06-11-09, § 1(ch. 3, art. 3, § 1), 11-21-2006)
State Law reference— Registration of voters, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-210 et seq.
Sec. 16-49. - Absentee ballots.
Voting by absentee ballots in a method consistent with state law shall be permitted in municipal elections, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 21-2-380.
(Ord. No. 06-11-09, § 1(ch. 3, art. 3, § 3), 11-21-2006)
Secs. 16-50—16-78. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - RECORDS

Sec. 16-79. - Availability.
Voting records and documents shall be made available to the extent required by O.C.G.A. § 21-2-72.
(Ord. No. 06-11-09, § 1(ch. 3, art. 4), 11-21-2006)
Sec. 16-80. - Maintenance.
The maintenance of voting records and documents shall be kept in compliance with O.C.G.A. § 21-2-73.
(Ord. No. 06-11-09, § 1(ch. 3, art. 4), 11-21-2006)
