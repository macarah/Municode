Sec. 46-1. - Bonfires.
It shall be unlawful for any person, at any time, to build a bonfire of any description within the city limits except at places approved and designated by the board of aldermen and approved by the chief of the county fire department or his designated representative.
(Code 1983, § 8-21)
Sec. 46-2. - Fireworks.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell, possess or shoot any fireworks within the city, except as otherwise provided in this section. The sale, possession and use of cap pistols, caps for cap pistols and possession of fireworks for uses outside the city limits as authorized by state law is permitted.
(Code 1983, § 8-22)
Sec. 46-3. - Smoking.
(a)
No person shall smoke, use or attempt to smoke or use lighted cigars, cigarettes, pipes or tobacco, nor shall any person in the process of smoking or attempting to smoke use a lighter, matches or flame in such a dangerous and reckless manner as to set fire to any bedding, furniture, curtains, drapes or other flammable household fittings, equipment or contents or any part of any buildings, so as to endanger life or property in any way to any extent.
(b)
Every operator of boardinghouses, roominghouses, hotels, motels or apartment houses shall conspicuously post a copy of this section in every room occupied by a paying guest.
(Code 1983, § 8-23)
