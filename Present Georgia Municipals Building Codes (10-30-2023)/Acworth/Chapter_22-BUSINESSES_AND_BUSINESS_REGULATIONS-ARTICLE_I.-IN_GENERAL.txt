ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 22-1. - Auction houses.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person within the city to be allowed to operate a business as an auction house unless the business is located upon property zoned C-1, C-2 or C-3, and unless a business license has first been issued in accordance with Annex No. 92-013, and the appropriate fee for the business license has been paid.
(b)
The operator of an auction house shall provide sufficient parking for its customers, and if the operator of such business fails to provide a sufficient number of parking spaces to the extent that the flow of traffic on the city's public thoroughfares is impeded, the city shall have the right to revoke the business licenses previously issued to the operator of the business. Thirty days prior to exercising its right to revoke the auction house operator's business license, the city manager shall first give the operator written notice of the sections of either the O.C.G.A. or this Code which are alleged to have been violated by the auction house operator.
