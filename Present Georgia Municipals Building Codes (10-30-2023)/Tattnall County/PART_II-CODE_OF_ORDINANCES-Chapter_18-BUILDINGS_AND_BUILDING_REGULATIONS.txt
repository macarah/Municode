ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 18-1. - Building permit.
(a)
All new construction or substantial improvement of existing structures, whether private or commercial, having a wholesale value of $1,000.00 must be made and accompanied by a building permit issued by the county tax assessor's office.
(b)
Building permit costs shall be collected on new construction and substantial improvements of existing structures. The cost of the permit fee on properties requiring a new 911 address shall be in an amount as established by the board of commissioners from time to time, which will include a 911 sign and post. The cost of the permit fee on established properties with a 911 address shall be in an amount as established by the board of commissioners from time to time.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person, firm, corporation, association, partnership, joint venture, or other organization to commence new construction, or substantial improvement to an existing structure, of any kind, private or commercial, or any structure that is taxable on tax digest, whether built on site or portable, without first obtaining a building permit from the county tax assessor's office.
(d)
No permit shall be issued for the construction or installation of any building or other facility that shall during or after construction, be hazardous to the citizens of this county.
(e)
Any failure to obtain such a building permit for new construction or substantial improvement to an existing structure shall result in the collection of a fine based on value, to-wit:

Sec. 18-2. - Permitting procedures.
(a)
To obtain a building permit, mobile home location/relocation permit or a certificate of occupancy, an applicant must apply at the county tax assessor's office.
(b)
Requirements.
(1)
A 911 address must be obtained. The 911 address can be obtained from the emergency services center.
(2)
An on-site septic tank inspection must be obtained from the health department and/or old existing septic tanks must be re-inspected by the health department.
(3)
The applicant must present proof of current year paid taxes on mobile home.
(4)
The applicant must obtain location/relocation permit from the tax assessor's office. The fee for the permit shall be in an amount as established by the board of commissioners from time to time and includes a 911 sign and post. The applicant's address must also be displayed before calling code enforcement.
a.
Once the permit is acquired, only a hauler and installer registered and licensed with the state can locate and set up a manufactured home, modular home or mobile home within the county.
b.
Before a mobile home can be relocated, the current year tax decal and relocation permit must be affixed to the mobile home.
(5)
Once installation is complete, the installer must complete the installer certificate and attach the certificate to the inside of the panel box door in the mobile home. When all items on the attached compliance list are complete, the applicant may contact the code enforcement.
(6)
Contact code enforcement for inspection to obtain an occupancy permit.
(7)
When moving a mobile home out of the county or to a valid mobile home dealership, an exit permit must be purchased from the tax assessor office. applicant must present a receipt from the tax commissioner indicating all the county taxes have been paid. The exit permit shall be in an amount as established by the board of commissioners from time to time.
(c)
Compliance inspection checklist.
(1)
Street access. Each manufactured home stand or lot shall be provided with access frontage to a width of at least 30 feet to a public or private street or road.
(2)
Miscellaneous general standards.
a.
Exterior doors will be metal or wood and in working order.
b.
Floors will be intact and in good repair.
c.
Every window will have glass panes. No other material is acceptable.
d.
Siding of all units shall be completely intact and shall not expose any interior material including insulation, wiring, framing or similar material usually found within the exterior of the home.
e.
Rust or damage must not be visible on the exterior of the unit.
f.
All units have an operable, and in good working order, hot and cold water system, a toilet, kitchen sink, bathroom sink, tub, or shower, electrically certified heating appliance and cooking appliance.
g.
There shall be no exposed electrical wiring or exposed plumbing on the interior or exterior of the home. Electrical wiring must be encased in conduit from panel box on the service pole to a depth of 18 inches and all the way to the floor of the home. Water line and sewer line must be connected.
h.
Underpinning, skirting and other siding must be in place 15 days after inspection. It must cover the entire home with spaces. Skirting material must be masonry, concrete, stucco, masonite, metal or vinyl. No roofing tin-like material is to be used.
i.
Certification from the installer that the mobile home was set up and installed in accordance with the requirements of chapter 120-3-7 of Rules and Regulations for Manufactured Homes or the manufacturer's installation instructions, whichever is appropriate.
j.
All manufactured, modular, or mobile homes and all habitable spaces added to the home shall meet the minimum standards or the standards that from time to time are adopted by the county board of commissioners.
(Ord. of 6-1-2009)

ARTICLE II. - ELECTRICAL PERMIT

Sec. 18-23. - Issuance; exemptions.
(a)
No electrical connection permit shall be issued unless and until the applicant (proposed "purchaser") has:
(1)
Been issued a valid septic tank permit by the county board of health if a sewage disposal system is required;
(2)
Or if the purpose is to reconnect or re-establish electrical service to a site that had previously been provided with electricity, then a certification from the county board of health that any on-site sewage disposal system existing or required by the site complies with health laws and regulations;
(3)
Nor shall such a permit be issued until a mobile home site permit has been issued if one is required under the provisions of the mobile home site permit article.
(b)
Exemptions.
(1)
The provisions of this article shall not apply to those situations where the provider of electrical service is restoring service to a "purchaser" who had previously been disconnected due to nonpayment of utility bills or actions of the elements.
(2)
Any property which has been inspected by the county health department sanitation inspector and found to be in compliance with the county health code regulations, shall be exempt from further inspection and from the requirement of obtaining an electrical permit required by this article for a period of five years from the date of said inspection. The county health department shall maintain a list of all inspected properties, with the date of said inspection, and furnish a copy of same to all electrical providers. It shall not be a violation of this article for any electrical provider to provide electrical service to any property which has been so inspected within five years, as no electrical permit shall be required under this article for said property.
(Ord. of 3-6-2000, art. 4)
Sec. 18-24. - Self-contained travel trailer or motor home.
Should an applicant for an electrical connection permit represent his application for the permit that the electrical service requested on the subject site is to service a self-contained travel trailer or motor home and there will be no on-site disposal of sewage, then the applicant shall not be required to obtain any sewage disposal permit from the county health department prior to the issuance of an electrical connection permit; provided, however, the applicant must enter into a written agreement with the county in a form from time to time prescribed by the county, wherein the applicant acknowledges that the electrical connection permit shall authorize an electrical service for use by the self-contained travel trailer or motor home only, and should the electrical service be used to service any other facility, or should any on-site disposal of sewage occur on the subject site in violation of existing regulations of the county health department, then the electrical connection permit shall be deemed null and void and the "provider" upon the written request of the board shall have authority to terminate, disconnect and remove all electrical service from the subject site.
(Ord. of 3-6-2000, art. 5)
Sec. 18-25. - Authority and jurisdiction.
This article shall apply to all unincorporated land located within the boundaries of the county. Authority for electrical permits for property located within the city limits of incorporated towns of Cobbtown, Collins, Glennville, Manassas, and Reidsville shall vest with the city building inspector or other designated city representative.
(Ord. of 1-9-2004, § I(art. 1.2); Ord. of 4-4-2005(1), § I(art. 1.2))
Sec. 18-26. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the following terms shall have the following meanings, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Building means any home or other structure having a roof and intended for shelter, housing or enclosure of persons, animals, or property of any kind, character or description. This includes but is not limited to manufactured, mobile and modular homes.
Electrical connection permit means a permit, issued by the designated county official, who from time to time is designated by the board as the official authorized to issue such permits for the unincorporated area of the county, which permit authorizes a provider of electrical services to furnish electricity to a particular site, structure or purchaser.
Pre-approved locations means all existing buildings or locations with electrical service as of the effective date of the enactment of this article.
Provider of electrical service means any utility company or person, firm, corporation, partnership, or other legal entity which sells, furnishes or delivers for sale electrical service within the county.
Purchaser means any person or firm, corporation, partnership, society, club, or association of persons acting together as a unit who purchase or cause to be purchased electrical service within the county.
(Ord. of 1-9-2004, § I(art. 3); Ord. of 4-4-2005(1), § I(art. 3))
Sec. 18-27. - Occupancy permits; requirement.
(a)
New electrical services shall not be provided to any building by a utility company or individual whether by individual electrical meter, or power feeds or drop cords as shared service from another structure, without having applied for and received an approved electrical connection permit from the county.
(b)
All existing locations are pre-approved.
(c)
Existing locations can be put on an "out of compliance list" if found to be out of compliance with any county ordinance, state law, federal law, or past due on any state or county tax or user fees. After the owner or other person occupying the building has been given notice and 30 days to come into compliance, a list of the locations on the "out of compliance" list shall be provided to all utility providers serving the county.
(d)
An "out of compliance" list shall be periodically compiled by the designated county official not less than every quarter. An "out of compliance list" shall be posted at the county commissioners' office and shall be available to any person upon request at the county commissioners' office and a copy shall be provided to all power companies operating within the county.
(e)
Once a location has been placed on the "out of compliance list" and service interrupted for any reason an electrical permit must be acquired before service can be reinstated.
(f)
Existing locations not on the "out of compliance list" may be transferred without any new permits or interruptions of services.
(g)
In the case of a manufactured, mobile or modular home, a new electrical permit is required any time the home is relocated from a permanent site to another site.
(Ord. of 1-9-2004, § I(art. 4(4.1)); Ord. of 4-4-2005(1), § I(art. 4(4.1)))
Sec. 18-28. - Application.
(a)
Prior to the issuance of an electrical permit, the owner or an agent of the owner, shall apply to the designated county official for an electrical permit. The application shall, without limitation, include the following:
(1)
Name of the landowner.
(2)
Name of the owner of the unit, if different from landowner.
(3)
911 physical address as determined by the county 911 office.
(4)
Tax map and parcel number assigned to the property by tax assessor.
(5)
Mailing address of unit owner.
(6)
Type of use: residential or commercial.
(7)
Name of electric utility provider.
(8)
Signature of applicant.
(9)
Date of the application.
(b)
Other documents that are required and will become part of this application include:
(1)
Copy of paid tax receipt for the current year.
(2)
Copy of septic tank inspection approved issued by county health inspector.
(3)
Copy of location or building permit.
(4)
If the electrical permit is for a manufactured/mobile home or a modular home, certification from the installer that the home was set up and installed in accordance with the requirements of chapter 120-3-7 of Rules and Regulations for Manufactured Homes or the manufacturer's instructions, whichever is appropriate.
(c)
Temporary electrical service. A temporary electrical permit can be acquired in the name of the dealer or installer for the purpose of installing the home and assuring everything to be in working order.
(Ord. of 1-9-2004, § I(art. 4(4.2)); Ord. of 4-4-2005(1), § I(art. 4(4.2)))
Sec. 18-29. - Fee.
An applicant for an electrical permit shall pay a fee for the permit to be established, from time to time, by the county governing authority.
(Ord. of 1-9-2004, § I(art. 4(4.3)); Ord. of 4-4-2005(1), § I(art. 4(4.3)))
Sec. 18-30. - Issuance.
Upon the completion of the application for an electrical permit and payment of the permit fee, an inspection by a county official to ascertain that the building is in compliance with all state laws or county ordinances, a county electrical permit shall be issued to acquire electrical service from the appropriate utility provider.
(Ord. of 1-9-2004, § I(art. 4(4.4)); Ord. of 4-4-2005(1), § I(art. 4(4.4)))
Sec. 18-31. - Enforcement.
The tax assessors of the county, the sheriff of the county, the deputies, thereof, constables, and all other law enforcement officers shall be authorized to issue citations to violators of any provisions of this article. Such citation shall state the time and place at which the accused is to appear for trial; shall identify the offense with which the accused is charged; shall have an identifying number by which it shall be filed with the county, shall indicate the identify of the accused and the date of service; and shall be signed by the officer who completes and serves it.
(Ord. of 1-9-2004, § II(5.1); Ord. of 4-4-2005(1), § II(5.1))
Sec. 18-32. - Penalties.
(a)
Any person violating this article, or any provision hereof, upon conviction, shall be punished by imposition of a fine of or not to exceed $1,000.00.
(b)
No person accused of violating this article shall be arrested prior to trial, but any defendant who fails to appear for trial shall be arrested thereafter on a warrant of the court, at the court's direction, and shall be required to post a bond for his future appearance as directed by the court.
(c)
Violations of this article shall either by citation as described in section 18-31 or by accusation. Such proceedings may be tried with or without a prosecuting attorney. The county attorney may serve as prosecuting attorney when requested by the prosecuting officer.
(d)
Violations of this article may be tried in the state court or magistrate court of the county at the option of the prosecuting officer.
(e)
Each day there is a continuous violation of this article at an individual building shall constitute a separate offense. Each violation of this article at a separate building shall also constitute a separate offense.
(Ord. of 1-9-2004, § II(5.2); Ord. of 4-4-2005(1), § II(5.2))
Sec. 18-33. - Interpretation.
The regulations expressed in this article shall be considered as the minimum provisions for the protection of the health, safety, economy, good order, appearance, convenience, and welfare of the general public.
(Ord. of 1-9-2004, § II(art. 3(6.1)); Ord. of 4-4-2005(1), § II(art. 3(6.1)))
Sec. 18-34. - Conflict with other laws, ordinances, and regulations.
Whenever the requirements made under authority of this article impose higher standards than are required in any other statute or local ordinance or regulation, provisions of this article shall govern.
(Ord. of 1-9-2004, § II(art. 3(6.2)); Ord. of 4-4-2005(1), § II(art. 3(6.2)))

ARTICLE III. - HOUSE NUMBERING

Sec. 18-57. - Numbering.
(a)
The county commission has established a new address numbering system, which shall be administered by the county 911 division.
(b)
Each house, building or other occupiable structure shall be assigned a separate number. A number or alphabetical letter shall be assigned for each separate and distinct occupant or division (i.e., apartment, company, act) within a building or other occupied structure.
(c)
Existing numbers shall be changed only as necessary to insure the effectiveness or efficiency of the enhanced 911 system on the county, as determined in the judgment of the county commission.
(d)
All new buildings and structures must be assigned a number address by the county 911 division prior to or simultaneous with granting building permit, septic tank permit or electrical permit.
(e)
Written notification of the address assigned under the terms of this article to each house, building or structure shall be given or delivered to its respective owner.
(f)
Written notification of the address assigned under the terms of this article to each house, building or structure shall also be given to the U.S. Postal 911 address coordinator.
(Ord. of 5-7-2001(1))
Sec. 18-58. - Placement of assigned numbers.
(a)
Within 30 days of receiving notification of the house number assignment, the owner, occupant or agent of each house, building or business shall display the assigned number on the structure.
(b)
The property owner, occupant or agent of each house, building or other structure, shall pay for cost and installation of the numbers.
(c)
Residential numbers shall not be less than three inches (four inches recommended) in height. Business numbers shall not be less than six inches in height. All numbers shall be made of durable, clearly visible material and shall contrast with the color of the house, building or other structure.
(d)
All numbers shall be conspicuously placed immediately above, on or at the side of the appropriate door so that the number is clearly visible from the street. In cases where the building is situated more than 50 feet from the street, the building number shall be placed near the walk, driveway or common entrance to the building, or upon the mailbox, gatepost, fence or other appropriate place so as to be clearly visible from the street.
(Ord. of 5-7-2001(1))
