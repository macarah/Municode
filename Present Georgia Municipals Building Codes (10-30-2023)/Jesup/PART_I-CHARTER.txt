AMENDED AND RESTATED CHARTER
ARTICLE 1. - INCORPORATION AND POWERS

1.1 - Name.
The City of Jesup, Georgia and the inhabitants thereof, are reincorporated by the enactment of this Amended and Restated Charter and are hereby again constituted and declared a body politic and corporate under the name and style of the City of Jesup, Georgia, and by that name shall have perpetual succession.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
1.2 - Charter.
This Amendment shall constitute the Amended and Restated Charter of the City of Jesup, Georgia adopted by the Mayor and Board of Commissioners of the City of Jesup, Georgia (also the "City"), pursuant to The Municipal Home Rule Act of 1965 at GA. CODE ANN. § 36-35-1 (2016) et seq. and other applicable provisions of Georgia law (hereinafter the "Charter"). The City of Jesup, Georgia, in the County of Wayne, and the inhabitants thereof, are constituted and declared a body politic and corporate under the same name and style of the "City of Jesup" and by that name shall have perpetual duration and succession. In addition to those other powers, rights, and privileges set forth in this Charter and as otherwise may exist under the general laws of statewide application of the State of Georgia or the City of Jesup may contract and be contracted with, sue and be sued, plead and be impleaded in all courts of law and equity, make and enact, by and through its Mayor and Board of Commissioners, ordinances, resolutions, by-laws, rules, regulations, policies and procedures, use a common seal, and, in all actions whatsoever, exercise, enjoy, employ and implement any and all other powers, rights, and privileges incident to municipal corporations of this State, or cites thereof, as authorized by and under the general laws of statewide application of the State of Georgia or the acts of the Georgia General Assembly or the Constitution of the State of Georgia.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
1.3 - Corporate Limits.
The boundaries of this City shall be those existing on the effective date of the adoption of this Charter with such alterations as may be made from time to time in a manner provided by law. Specifically, the corporate limits of the City of Jesup shall extend to and embrace all of the following described territory, to-wit:
Beginning at a point located 46° 30' west a distance of 9,280.0 feet from the point of intersection of the center of Cherry Street and the center of the main line track of the Atlantic Coast Line Railroad in said City; thence north 43° 30' east a distance of 9,280.0 feet; thence south 46° 30' east a distance of 18,560 feet; thence south 43° 30' west a distance of 23,560.0 feet; thence north 46° 30' west a distance of 18,560.0 feet; thence north 43° 30' east a distance of 14,280 feet to the point of beginning.
The boundaries of this City at all times shall be shown on a map, a written description or any combination thereof, to be retained permanently in the office of the City Clerk and to be designated, as the case may be: "Official Map (or Description) of the Corporate Limits of the City of Jesup, Georgia." Photographic, typed, or other copies of such map or description certified by the City Clerk shall be admitted as evidence in all courts and shall have the same force and effect as the original map or description. The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners of the City of Jesup may provide for the redrawing of any such map, by ordinance, to reflect lawful changes in the corporate boundaries. A redrawn map shall supersede, for all purposes, the entire map or maps which it is designated to replace.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
1.4 - Powers Generally.
All powers, functions, rights, privileges, and immunities of the City of Jesup, its officers, agencies, or employees as exist from time-to-time under general laws of statewide application of the State of Georgia or the acts of the Georgia General Assembly or the Constitution of the State of Georgia, shall be carried into execution as provided by this Charter. The powers of the City of Jesup shall be construed liberally in favor of the City of Jesup and shall include all powers under the general laws of statewide application of the State of Georgia, the acts of the Georgia General Assembly, the Constitution of the State of Georgia, or under this Charter. The specific mention or failure to mention particular powers shall not be construed as limiting in any way the powers of the City of Jesup. If this Charter makes no provision, such shall be carried into execution as provided by ordinance or as provided by pertinent laws of the General Assembly of the State of Georgia. At all times during its perpetual existence, the City of Jesup shall exercise, enjoy and be entitled to the powers, rights, and privileges set forth in this Charter and as otherwise exist under the general laws of statewide application of the State of Georgia, the acts of the Georgia General Assembly or the Constitution of the State of Georgia.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
1.5 - Powers Illustrated.
Without limiting the foregoing, the City of Jesup shall also have the following additional powers, functions, rights, privileges, and immunities:
1.5.1 Alcohol regulations. To regulate the sale, possession, distribution, consumption, manufacture, transportation and taxation of alcoholic beverages, wine, spirits, liquors, cider and related beverages and products; to provide for the issuance of licenses for the sale, possession, distribution, consumption, manufacture, transportation and taxation of alcoholic beverages or products and the establishment of standards related to such licenses; to provide for zoning, land use and related restrictions on real property and land related to property and premises involved in the sale, possession, distribution, consumption, manufacture, transportation and taxation of alcoholic beverages or products.
1.5.2 Animal regulations. To regulate and license or to prohibit the keeping, harboring, maintenance or running at-large of animals, fishes and fowl, and to provide for the impoundment of the same, if in violation of any ordinance or lawful order; to provide for the disposition by sale, gift, or humane destruction of animals, fishes and fowl when not redeemed as provided by ordinance; and to provide punishment for violation of ordinances enacted hereunder.
1.5.3 Appropriations and expenditures. To make appropriations for the support of the government of the City of Jesup; to authorize the expenditure of money for any purpose authorized by this Charter and for any purpose for which a municipality is authorized by the laws of the State of Georgia; and to provide for the payment of expenses of the City of Jesup.
1.5.4 Building regulation. To regulate and to license the erection and construction of buildings and all other structures within the City of Jesup; to adopt building, housing, plumbing, electrical, gas, fire, life-safety, property-maintenance and heating and air conditioning codes; and to regulate all housing and building trades to the extent permitted under the general laws of statewide application of the State of Georgia, the acts of the Georgia General Assembly or the Constitution of the State of Georgia.
1.5.5 Business regulation and taxation. To levy and to provide for the collection of regulatory fees and taxes on privileges, occupations, trades, businesses, and professions as authorized by Title 48 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, or such other applicable laws as are or may hereafter be enacted by the Georgia General Assembly; to permit and regulate the same; to provide for the manner and method of payment of such regulatory fees and taxes; to revoke such permits; and to generally regulate trade, business, occupations and professions within the geographical and jurisdictional limits of the City of Jesup.
1.5.6 Cemeteries. To provide for the creation, construction, and maintenance of a cemetery or cemeteries for the interment of human remains.
1.5.7 Codification of Ordinances. To codify all ordinances of the City of Jesup, together with this Charter, into one book to be known as "The Code of the City of Jesup."
1.5.8 Commercial Development. To provide for standards, rules, regulations, and incentives, as permitted by general laws of statewide application, for the development of trade, business, commerce, employment opportunities, and real property improvement.
1.5.9 Condemnation.
1.5.9.1 Power. To condemn property for present or future use and for any public purpose deemed necessary by the Mayor and Board of Commissioners of the City of Jesup, utilizing procedures enumerated in Title 22 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, or such other applicable laws of the State of Georgia as are or may hereafter be enacted; and
1.5.9.2 Removal and Remediation. To remove, replace and cause to be remediated or removed any building, structure, or existing condition upon real property and land within the City of Jesup's corporate limits that is, or are, dangerous to life, limb, persons, or property, by reasons of decay, dilapidation, or unsanitary or dangerous conditions. Nothing in this subparagraph shall be construed to relieve the City of Jesup of any duty to provide owners or interested persons reasonable notice and opportunity to remedy an underlying condition necessitating removal and remediation. This subparagraph shall not be construed as authorizing the doing of any act or thing contrary to the Constitution of the State of Georgia or the laws of the State of Georgia. The City of Jesup shall have authority to adopt reasonable ordinances and resolutions for the purpose of carrying out this subparagraph.
1.5.10 Construction, Building and Property Maintenance Standards. To provide for minimum standards, regulations, rules and regulations related to construction, improvement, remodeling, repair, building, erection and maintenance of buildings, houses, structures, and improvements on real property and land.
1.5.11 Contracts. To enter into contracts and agreements with other governmental entities and with private persons, associations of persons, firms, partnerships, corporations, limited liability companies, organizations, joint ventures or other parties.
1.5.12 Emergencies. To establish procedures for determining and proclaiming that an emergency exists within or without the City of Jesup and to make and carry out all reasonable provisions deemed necessary to address or meet such emergencies for the protection, safety, health, or the well-being of the public at large.
1.5.13 Environmental protection. To protect and preserve the natural resources, physical and aesthetic environment, and vital geographic, geologic, hydrologic and historically-significant areas of the City of Jesup, the region, and the State of Georgia through the enactment of ordinances that preserve and improve air quality, restore and maintain water resources, control erosion and sedimentation, manage storm-water and establish a storm-water utility, manage solid and hazardous waste, provide for disposal of solid waste and inert materials and provide other necessary or beneficial actions for the protection of the environment.
1.5.14 Ethics. To adopt ethics ordinances and regulations governing such matters including, but not limited to, the conduct of elected municipal officials, appointed officials, contractors, vendors, and employees; establishing procedures for ethics complaints; and setting forth penalties for violations of such rules and procedures.
1.5.15 Fire and public safety regulations. To fix and establish fire-safety, life-safety, building and construction, and public-safety codes, rules, prohibitions, regulations, limitations and restrictions and, from time to time, to extend, enlarge, or restrict the same; to prescribe fire-safety, life-safety, building and construction, and public-safety codes, rules, prohibitions, regulations, limitations and restrictions not inconsistent with general laws of statewide application, relating to fire prevention and detection, public safety, crime prevention, firefighting and other matters generally affecting public health, safety, and welfare; and to prescribe penalties and punishment for violations thereof; and similar regulations for the overall promotion and preservation of public health, safety, and welfare.
1.5.16 Food Preparation and Delivery. To provide for codes, rules, prohibitions, regulations, limitations and restrictions related to the preparation, manufacture, distribution, dissemination or handling of food or other products intended for human consumption, and the premises or structures or vehicles and equipment utilized in such activities.
1.5.17 Garbage services and fees. To provide for the collection, accumulation, storage and disposal of garbage, waste, trash, refuse and similar items; and to levy, fix, assess, and collect garbage, refuse, trash collection and disposal and other sanitary service charge, taxes or fees; to enforce the payment of such charges, taxes, or fees; and to provide for the manner and method of collecting such service charges, taxes, or fees.
1.5.18 General health, safety, and welfare. To define, regulate, and prohibit any act, practice, conduct, or use of property which is detrimental to the health, sanitation, cleanliness, welfare, and safety of the general public and the City of Jesup and its inhabitants, citizens, businesses, and visitors; to provide for the enforcement of such standards; and to provide for the proper maintenance and disposal of wastes and debris.
1.5.19 Gifts. To accept or refuse gifts, donations, bequests, or grants from any source for any purpose legally permitted under the general laws of statewide application of the State of Georgia and the Georgia Constitution, and including on such terms and conditions as the donor or grantor may impose.
1.5.20 Health and sanitation. To prescribe standards of health, cleanliness, and sanitation and to provide for the enforcement of such standards.
1.5.21 Homestead exemption. To establish and maintain procedures for offering homestead exemptions to residents of the City of Jesup and maintaining current homestead exemptions of residents of the City of Jesup as authorized under the laws of the State of Georgia and the Georgia Constitution.
1.5.22 Inquiries and investigations. To make inquiries and investigations into the affairs of the City of Jesup and conduct of any department, office, or agency thereof and for this purpose may subpoena witnesses, administer oaths, take testimony, and require the production of evidence. Any person who fails or refuses to obey a lawful order issued in the exercise of these powers by the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners of the City of Jesup shall be punished as may be provided by applicable ordinances or applicable laws of the State of Georgia.
1.5.23 Jail sentences. To provide that persons given jail sentences in the City of Jesup's Municipal Court may work out such sentences in any public works or on the streets, roads, drains, and other public property in the City of Jesup; to provide for commitment of such persons to any jail; to provide for the use of pretrial diversion and any alternative sentencing allowed by under the laws of the State of Georgia and the Georgia Constitution; or to provide for commitment of such persons to any county work camp or county jail.
1.5.24 Motor vehicles. To regulate the operation of motor vehicles and pedestrian traffic, and to exercise control over all traffic of any form, including parking in, upon, across or over the highways, thoroughfares, streets, roads, alleys, lanes, sidewalks and walkways of the City of Jesup; to provide for the orderly operation of vehicles and equipment within the City of Jesup; and to provide for the safe, efficient and orderly control of traffic in, upon, across or over the highways, thoroughfares, streets, roads, alleys, lanes, sidewalks and walkways of the City of Jesup.
1.5.25 Municipal agencies and delegation of power. To create, alter, or abolish departments, boards, agencies, authorities, departments, boards, agencies, authorities, and offices of the City of Jesup and to confer upon such departments, boards, agencies, authorities, and offices the necessary and appropriate authorities, powers, rights and entitlements to effectuate their respective purposes.
1.5.26 Municipal courts. To create a municipal court with a judge or judges and associate judges as may be necessary, and to authorize the creation of a municipal court clerk's office or similar office as appointed by the Mayor and Board of Commissioners. The Clerk of the City of Jesup may be authorized to perform such duties, if appointed, and may occupy such role. The Board of Commissioners and the City of Jesup, though having the power and authority to do so, is under no obligation to create a municipal court clerk or similar office, and is likewise not under obligation to have the City Clerk perform such duties.
1.5.27 Municipal debts. To incur debts, obligations, encumbrances, liabilities and commitments as authorized by this Charter, the laws of the State of Georgia, and the Georgia Constitution; to both appropriate and borrow money for the payment of such debts, obligations, encumbrances, liabilities and commitments of the City of Jesup; to pledge the credit of the City of Jesup as authorized by this Charter, the laws of the State of Georgia, and the Georgia Constitution; and to issue bonds, mortgages, indentures, and other security instruments as authorized by this Charter, the laws of the State of Georgia, and the Georgia Constitution.
1.5.28 Municipal property ownership. To acquire, dispose of, lease, and hold in trust or otherwise any real, personal, or mixed property, in fee simple or lesser interest, inside or outside of the geographical limitations of the City of Jesup.
1.5.29 Municipal property protection. To provide for the preservation and protection of all property and equipment of the City of Jesup and the administration and use of same by the public and to prescribe penalties and punishment for violations thereof.
1.5.30 Municipal utilities. To acquire, lease, construct, operate, maintain, sell, and dispose of public utilities, including, but not limited to: a system of waterworks, water pumping plants and accessories, wells, cisterns, towers, water mains, piping, sewers and drains, sewage disposal, storm-water management, gas works, electricity generating plants, cable television and other telecommunications, transportation facilities, public airports, and any other public utility; to fix the taxes, charges, rates, fares, fees, assessments, regulations, and penalties for all such public utilities; and to provide for the withdrawal of service for refusal or failure to pay the same.
1.5.31 Nuisance. To define a nuisance and provide for its abatement whether on public or private property.
1.5.32 Penalties. To provide penalties for violation of any ordinances adopted pursuant to the authority of this Charter and the laws of the State of Georgia.
1.5.33 Planning and zoning. To provide for comprehensive City planning, to pass and adopt ordinances, rules and regulations for land use, zoning, restrictions on the use of real property and regulations for the development and maintenance of real property, signage, buildings, structures, and outside advertising; and to provide subdivision regulation, all to ensure a safe, healthy, and aesthetically pleasing community.
1.5.34 Police and fire protection. To exercise the power of arrest through law enforcement and authorized officers and to establish, operate or contract for police and fire-fighting agencies.
1.5.35 Public hazards: removal. To provide for the destruction and removal of any building or other structure or physical condition, which is or may become, dangerous, unsanitary, or detrimental to the public health, welfare or interest.
1.5.36 Public improvements. To provide for the acquisition, construction, building, operation, maintenance, or abolition of water and sewer systems, highways and streets, alleys, lanes, thoroughfares and roads, public ways, parks and playgrounds, recreational facilities, cemeteries, public buildings, libraries, public housing, parking facilities, and charitable, cultural, educational, recreational, conservation, sport, detention, penal, and medical institutions, agencies, and any facilities or improvements necessary to promote and protect the public health, wellbeing, and safety of the citizens of and visitors to the City of Jesup; to provide any other public improvements and to regulate the use of public improvements. For such purposes, property may be acquired by condemnation under Title 22 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated or such other applicable laws as are or may hereafter be enacted.
1.5.37 Public peace. To provide for the prevention and punishment of loitering, disorderly conduct, drunkenness, riots, and public disturbances.
1.5.38 Public transportation. To organize and operate or to participate in the operation of such public transportation systems as are deemed beneficial by the Mayor and Board of Commissioners of the City of Jesup. To the extent permitted by federal and state law, the Mayor and Board of Commissioners may provide, by ordinance, for the regulation of buses, trains, trolleys, streetcars, or other forms of public transportation operating in or traveling through the City of Jesup.
1.5.39 Public utilities and services. To grant franchises or make contracts for, or impose taxes on, public utilities and public service companies; and to prescribe the rates, fares, regulations, and standards and conditions of service applicable to the service to be provided by the franchise grantee or contractor, insofar as not in conflict with valid regulations of the Georgia Public Service Commission or its successor.
1.5.40 Purchasing. To make purchases of, or to otherwise lawfully acquire, goods, services, equipment, vehicles, and supplies.
1.5.41 Regulation of roadside areas. To prohibit or regulate and control the erection, removal, and maintenance of signs, billboards, trees, shrubs, fences, buildings, and any and all other structures or obstructions upon or adjacent to the rights of way of streets and roads or within view thereof, within or abutting the geographic limitations and boundaries of the City of Jesup; and to prescribe penalties and punishment for violation of such ordinances.
1.5.42 Remedy unsafe and unsanitary conditions. To remediate, remove, or cause or order to be remediated or removed, any building, structure, or existing condition within the City of Jesup's corporate limits that is dangerous to life, limb, persons, or property, by reasons of decay, dilapidation, or unsanitary or dangerous conditions pursuant to the City of Jesup's power to regulate the health, safety and welfare of its citizens.
1.5.43 Retirement and employee benefits. To provide and maintain a retirement plan, insurance, and such other employee benefits for appointed officers and employees of the City of Jesup as may be determined from time-to-time by the Mayor and Board of Commissioners.
1.5.44 Roadways. To build, construct, design, lay out, open, extend, widen, narrow, establish or change the grade of, abandon or close, construct, pave, curb, gutter, adorn with shade trees, or otherwise improve, maintain, repair, clean, prevent erosion of, and light the highways, thoroughfares, roads, streets, alleys, lanes, sidewalks and walkways within the geographic limitations and boundaries of the City of Jesup; to grant franchises and rights of way throughout those streets and roads and over the bridges and viaducts for the use of public utilities; and to require real estate owners to repair and maintain in a safe condition the sidewalks adjoining their lots or lands and to impose penalties for failure to do so.
1.5.45 Sewer Fees. To levy a fee, charge, or tax as necessary to assure the acquiring, constructing, equipping, operating, maintaining, and extending of a sewage disposal plant and sewerage system, and to levy on those to whom sewers and sewerage systems are made available a sewer service fee, charge or sewer tax for the availability or use of the sewers; to provide for the manner and method of collecting such service charges and for enforcing payment of the same; and to charge, impose and collect a connection fee or fees to those connected with the system.
1.5.46 Solid waste disposal. To provide for the collection and disposal of garbage, rubbish, and refuse and to regulate the collection and disposal of garbage, rubbish, and refuse by others; and to provide for the separate collection of glass, tin, aluminum, cardboard, paper, and other recyclable materials and to provide for the sale of such items, refuse, or their byproducts, when feasible to do so.
1.5.47 Special assessments. To levy and provide for the collection of special assessments for any public improvements as may be permitted by this Charter, the general laws of the State of Georgia, and Georgia Constitution.
1.5.48 Special Areas of Public Regulation. To regulate or prohibit junk dealers, and the manufacture and sale of intoxicating liquors; to regulate the transportation, storage and use of combustible, explosive and inflammable materials, the use of lighting and heating equipment, and any other business or situation which may be dangerous to persons or property; to regulate and control the conduct of peddlers and itinerant traders, theatrical performances, exhibitions, and shows of any kind, by taxation or otherwise; to license and tax professional fortunetelling, palmistry, and massage parlors; and to restrict adult novelty and bookstores to certain areas.
1.5.49 Taxes; ad valorem. To levy and provide for the assessment, valuation, revaluation, and collection of taxes on all property subject to taxation. All such valuations will be performed in accordance with general laws of statewide application.
1.5.50 Taxes; other. To levy and collect such other taxes and fees as may be allowed now or in the future by law.
1.5.51 Taxes. To impose, pass, enforce and collect and taxes or fees that are permitted by law.
1.5.52 Taxes on Insurance Premiums.The Mayor and Board of Commissioners are hereby empowered and authorized to assess, levy and collect a tax on fire, life, casualty and accident insurance premiums which are collected from policy holders residing in said city or when any policy holder resides out of the City of Jesup, but the property insured is located in said city, and said tax may be in addition to any other license, tax or fee the said city may be authorized to collect from insurance companies or their agents doing business in said city.
1.5.53 Taxicabs.To regulate and license vehicles operated for hire in the City of Jesup; to limit the number of such vehicles; to require the operators thereof to be licensed; to require public liability insurance on such vehicles in the amounts to be prescribed by ordinance; to inspect such vehicles and mandate standards of safety and cleanliness; and to regulate the parking of such vehicles.
1.5.54 Tourism, conventions, and trade shows. To authorize the City of Jesup to contract with private sector, nonprofit organizations, or other governmental agencies to promote tourism, conventions, and trade shows.
1.5.55 Urban redevelopment. To organize and operate an urban redevelopment program.
1.5.56 Water Fees. To levy a fee, charge, or tax as necessary to assure the acquiring, constructing, equipping, operating, maintaining, and extending of a water treatment plant and water system, and to levy on those to whom water and water systems are made available a water service fee, charge or water tax for the availability or use of the water; to provide for the manner and method of collecting such service charges and for enforcing payment of the same; and to charge, impose and collect a connection fee or fees to those connected with the system.
1.5.57 Other powers. To exercise and enjoy all other powers, functions, rights, privileges, and immunities necessary or desirable to promote or protect the safety, health, peace, security, good order, comfort, convenience, or general welfare of the City of Jesup and its inhabitants; to exercise all implied powers necessary or desirable to carry into execution all powers granted in this Charter as fully and completely as if such powers were fully stated herein; and to exercise all powers now or in the future authorized to be exercised by other municipal governments under other laws of the State of Georgia; and any listing of particular powers in this Charter shall not be held to be exclusive of others or restrictive of general words and phrases granting powers but shall be held to be in addition to such powers unless expressly prohibited to municipalities under the Constitution or applicable laws of the State of Georgia.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
ARTICLE 2. - GOVERNMENT STRUCTURE

2.1 - Administration.
The legislative authority of the government of the City of Jesup, Georgia, except as otherwise specifically provided in this Charter, shall be vested in the duly-elected Commissioners of the City of Jesup (or appointed as is otherwise permitted in this Charter) and its duly-elected Mayor (or, again, appointed as is otherwise permitted in this Charter). The Board of Commissioners and Mayor shall be elected in the manner provided by this Charter and as otherwise mandated by the Georgia Constitution and general laws of statewide application or appointed in the event of the occurrence of a vacancy in the office of Commissioner or Mayor as otherwise set forth herein.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)

2.3 - Elections by City; District.
2.3.1 Majority Vote in Mayoral Election; Runoffs. The Mayor shall be elected by a majority vote of the eligible voters of the City of Jesup at large voting at the elections of the City of Jesup. In the event that no candidate for Mayor obtains a majority vote of the eligible voters of the City of Jesup at large voting at the elections of the City of Jesup, then a run-off election shall be held. The candidates receiving the two highest numbers of votes in the election shall be included in the run-off election, and the candidate receiving the highest number of votes in the runoff of the qualified electors of the City of Jesup at large voting in such runoff shall be elected. Each candidate for Mayor shall reside in the City of Jesup as required under this Charter.
2.3.2 Majority Vote in Commissioner Elections; Runoffs. The Commissioners shall be elected by a majority vote of the eligible voters of the respective council districts voting at the elections of the City of Jesup. In the event that no candidate for a council post obtains a majority vote of the eligible voters of the council district voting in the election, then a run-off election shall be held. The candidates receiving the two highest numbers of votes in the election for such council post shall be included in the run-off election. The person receiving the highest number of votes of the eligible voters of the council district voting at such run-off election shall be elected. Each candidate for election to Commissioner shall, at all times, reside in the district which he or she seeks to represent as required by this Charter. Likewise, each Commissioner shall remain a resident of his or her district for as long as he or she holds the office of Commissioners for each district.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
2.4 - Qualifications for Office.
2.4.1 Notice of Intent to Seek Office. Each candidate for the offices of the Mayor or Commissioner for the City of Jesup shall file a notice of candidacy in accordance with applicable laws of statewide application or ordinance with the City Clerk of the City of Jesup, or his or her designee.
2.4.2 Commissioners. In addition to any other requirements or qualifications imposed by, or existing under, the Georgia Constitution and general laws of statewide application, no person shall be eligible to serve as Commissioner of the City of Jesup, Georgia unless such person (a) shall have been a resident of the City of Jesup for the twelve (12) months immediately prior to the date of election for that District's Commissioner, and (b) a resident of the District for which such person intends to qualify for election for the twelve (12) months immediately prior to the date of election for the District's Commissioner and (c) is registered and qualified to vote in municipal elections of the City of Jesup. Each Commissioner shall continue to reside both in the City of Jesup and the District for which such person was elected as Commissioner during that person's period of service as a Commissioner, and such person shall continue to be registered and qualified to vote in municipal elections of the City of Jesup. Each person desiring to offer as a candidate for Commissioner shall designate the District for which he or she is offering.
2.4.3 Mayor. In addition to any other requirements or qualifications imposed by, or existing under, the Georgia Constitution and general laws of statewide application, no person shall be eligible to serve as Mayor of the City of Jesup, Georgia unless such person (a) shall have been a resident of the City of Jesup for the twelve (12) months immediately prior to the date of election for Mayor and (b) is registered and qualified to vote in municipal elections of the City of Jesup. The Mayor shall continue to reside in the City of Jesup during that person's period of service as the Mayor, and to be registered and qualified to vote in municipal elections of the City of Jesup. Each person desiring to offer as a candidate for Mayor shall designate that he or she is qualifying for the position of Mayor.
2.4.4 Mayor Pro Tempore. In addition to any other requirements or qualifications imposed by, or existing under, the Georgia Constitution and general laws of statewide application and this charter, no person shall be eligible to serve as Mayor Pro Tempore of the City of Jesup unless that person is a Commissioner. Each Commissioner shall successively serve as mayor pro tempore for a period of twelve (12) months. The Commissioners shall rotate by district beginning with District 1 and serving for successive twelve (12) month terms.
2.4.5 Other Elective Offices Prohibited. Elected officials of the City of Jesup shall not hold other elected public offices.
2.4.6 Vacancies. The offices of Mayor and Commissioner shall become vacant upon the incumbent's death, resignation, removal, forfeiture of, or disqualification for, office.
2.4.7 Oath of Offices. The oaths of the offices of the Mayor and Board of Commissioners shall, to the extent that it is permitted under federal and state law, be as follows:
I do solemnly swear or affirm that I will faithfully execute the office of [Commissioner or Mayor, as the case may be] of the City of Jesup, and will, to the best of my ability, support and defend the Constitution of the United States, the Constitution of the State of Georgia, and the Charter, ordinances, and regulations of the City of Jesup. I am not the holder of any unaccounted for public money due this State or any political subdivision or authority thereof. I am not the holder of any office of trust under the government of the United States, any other state, or any foreign state which I by the laws of the State of Georgia am prohibited from holding. I am otherwise qualified to hold said office according to the Constitution and laws of Georgia. I have been a resident of the City of Jesup for the time required by the Constitution and laws of this State and by the Charter of the City of Jesup [and in the event of the office of Commissioner: of my district for the time required by the Constitution and laws of this State and by the Charter of the City of Jesup]. I will perform the duties of my office in the best interests of the City of Jesup to the best of my ability without fear, favor, affection, reward, or expectation thereof.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
2.5 - Terms and Compensation.
Except in the case of their special election to fulfill the unexpired term of a vacancy in the offices of the Mayor or a Commissioner, the Mayor and each member of the Board of Commissioners shall serve for terms of four (4) years and until their respective successors are elected and qualified. Except in the case of their special election to fulfill the unexpired term of a vacancy in the offices of the Mayor or a Commissioner, the terms of offices of the Mayor and the Commissioners generally shall begin on the first day in January immediately following the election of such Mayor or Commissioners unless the Georgia Constitution or general laws of statewide application requires the term to begin at the first organizational meeting in January or upon some other date. The Mayor and Commissioners shall receive compensation and expenses for their services as provided by ordinance.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
2.6 - Conduct of Elected and Appointed Officers; Conflicts of Interest; Holding Other Offices.
2.6.1 Conduct. Elected and appointed officers of the City of Jesup are trustees and servants of the residents of the City of Jesup and shall act in a fiduciary capacity for the benefit of such residents.
2.6.2 Conflicts of Interests Generally. No elected official, appointed officer, or employee of the City of Jesup or any agency or political entity to which this Charter applies shall knowingly perpetrate any of the following actions:
2.6.2.1  Business Transactions. Engage in any business or transaction, or have a financial or other personal interest, direct or indirect, which is incompatible with the proper discharge of that person's official duties or which would tend to impair the independence of the official's judgment or action in the performance of those official duties;
2.6.2.2  Voting. Vote upon any official matter coming before the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners of the City of Jesup in which such Mayor or Commissioner maintains a financial or other personal interest or where such official's judgment or action would be impaired;
2.6.2.3  Private Employment. Engage in or accept private employment, or render services for private interests when such employment or service is incompatible with the proper discharge of that person's official duties or would tend to impair the independence of the official's judgment or action in the performance of those official duties;
2.6.2.4  Confidential Information. Disclose confidential information, including information obtained at meetings which are closed pursuant to Title 50, Chapter 14 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, concerning the property, government, or affairs of the governmental body by which the official is engaged without proper legal authorization; or use such information to advance the financial or other private interest of the official or others;
2.6.2.5  Gifts. Accept any valuable gift, whether in the form of service, loan, thing, or promise, from any person, firm or corporation which to the official's knowledge is interested, directly or indirectly, in any manner whatsoever, in business dealings with the governmental body by which the official is engaged; provided, however, that an elected official who is a candidate for public office may accept campaign contributions and services in connection with any such campaign;
2.6.2.6  Adverse interest. Represent other private interests in any action or proceeding against this City of Jesup or any portion of its government; or
2.6.2.7  Financial Interest. Vote or otherwise participate in the negotiation or in the making of any contract with any business or entity in which the official has a financial interest.
2.6.3 Disclosure. Any elected official, appointed officer, or employee who shall have any financial interest, directly or indirectly, in any contract or matter pending before or within any department of the City of Jesup shall disclose such interest to the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners of the City of Jesup. The Mayor or any Member of the Board of Commissioners who has a financial interest in any matter pending before the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners shall disclose such interest, and such disclosure shall be entered on the official records of the City of Jesup, and that official shall disqualify himself or herself from participating in any decision or vote relating thereto. Any elected official, appointed officer, or employee of any agency or political entity to which this Charter applies who shall have any financial interest, directly or indirectly, in any contract or matter pending before or within such entity likewise shall disclose such interest to the governing body of such agency or entity.
2.6.4 Use of Public Property. No elected official, appointed officer, or employee of the City of Jesup or any agency or entity to which this Charter applies shall use property owned by such governmental entity for personal benefit or profit but shall use such property only in their capacity as an officer or employee of the City of Jesup.
2.6.5 Contracts Voidable and Rescindable. Any violation of this section which occurs with the knowledge, express or implied, of a party to a contract, agreement, purchase, or sale shall render said contract, agreement, purchase or sale voidable at the option of the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners.
2.6.6 Ineligibility of Elected Official. Except where authorized by law, neither the Mayor nor any Member of the Board of Commissioners shall hold any other elective or appointive office in the City of Jesup or otherwise be employed by said government or any agency thereof during the term for which that official was elected. No former Mayor or any former Member of the Board of Commissioners shall hold any appointive office in the City until one (1) year after the expiration of the term for which that official was elected.
2.6.7 Political Activities of Certain Officers and Employees. No appointive officer of the City of Jesup shall continue in such employment upon qualifying as a candidate for nomination or election to any public office. No employee of the City of Jesup shall continue in such employment upon qualifying for or election to any public office of the City of Jesup or any other public office which is inconsistent, incompatible or in conflict with the duties of the City employee. None of the Mayor, any member of the Board of Commissioners or any City employee may openly campaign for or against any candidate for public office or any public referendum in his or her official capacity as an elected official or public employee of the City of Jesup or to lend his or her office or position as an employee to such campaign.
2.6.8 Penalties for Violation.
2.6.8.1  Adjudication. Any violation under Code Section 2.6 shall be charged, determined and adjudicated in accordance with Article VI of the City of Jesup Official Code of Ordinances, as amended from time to time.
2.6.8.2  Forfeiture. Any Mayor or Commissioner of the City of Jesup who is determined to have concealed a financial conflict of interest or knowingly violated any of the requirements of this Section 2.6 shall be guilty of malfeasance in office or position and shall be deemed to have forfeited that person's office or position.
2.6.8.3  Ineligibility. Any Mayor or Commissioner of the City of Jesup, who shall forfeit an office or position as described in Paragraph 2.6.8 above, shall be ineligible for appointment or election to, or employment in, a position by the City of Jesup for a period of three (3) years thereafter.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
ARTICLE 3. - ADMINISTRATIVE OFFICERS

3.1 - City Manager.
The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners of the City of Jesup shall appoint a City Manager who shall neither be the Mayor nor a Commissioner. The City Manager shall be the chief administrative officer of the government of the City of Jesup. The City Manager shall devote all of his or her full working time and attention to the affairs of the City and shall be responsible to the Mayor and Board of Commissioners of the City of Jesup for the proper and efficient administration of the affairs of the City over which such officer has jurisdiction.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
3.2 - City Clerk.
The City Clerk shall be custodian of the official City seal and City records; maintain the City's records as required by this Charter or general laws of statewide application; attend all meetings of Council; keep a careful and accurate record of the official proceedings of the Mayor and Board of Commissioners, including a record of the votes on all questions before the said Mayor and Board of Commissioners; furnish to the City Manager and the Mayor and Board of Commissioners or any of them, such information as may be requested by any of them; keep and preserve all of the official records of the City of Jesup; and perform such other duties as may be assigned. The City Clerk shall be appointed by the City Manager of the City of Jesup.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018; Ord. of 3-1-2022(1), § 1)
3.3 - City Attorney.
The Mayor and Board of Commissioners of the City of Jesup, may, in their discretion, appoint a City Attorney, who shall represent the Mayor and Board of Commissioners, and whose duties shall include the drafting of such ordinances as may be required by the Boardof Commissioners, attend such meetings of the Mayor and Board of Commissioners as may be required, prosecute cases wherein violations of City ordinances are charged in the Municipal Court when requested by the Mayor and Board of Commissioners to do so, advise the Mayor and Board of Commissioners on all questions of law wherein advice is requested, and perform such other duties as may be required by the Mayor and Board of Commissioners. The Mayor and Board of Commissioners shall not delegate the authority to appoint or fix the compensation of the City Attorney to the City Manager.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
3.4 - Chief of Police.
The City Manager shall appoint a Chief of Police.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
3.5 - Chief of the Fire Department.
The City Manager shall appoint a Chief of the Fire Department.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
3.6 - Treasurer.
The Treasurer of the City of Jesup shall receive, account for and regularly disburse all moneys or property received by the City of Jesup and perform all other duties devolving upon him or her as Treasurer of the City of Jesup. The office of the Treasurer may be held simultaneously by the same person holding the office of Clerk of the City of Jesup. The Treasurer shall maintain and keep an accurate set of books of accounts, showing in detail all items of income and expense of the City; present to the City Manager and the Mayor and Board of Commissioners, a detailed statement of the financial conditions of the City; and collect and account for all revenues due to the City from whatever source. When the office of Clerk of the City of Jesup and Treasurer of the City of Jesup shall be held by the same person, all duties of the Clerk shall be performed by such person as "Clerk, City of Jesup" and all duties as Treasurer shall be performed as "Treasurer, City of Jesup." The Treasurer shall be appointed by the City Manager of the City of Jesup.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018; Ord. of 3-1-2022(2), § 1)
3.7 - Removal.
All officers and directors that are specifically appointed by the Board of Commissioners shall be employees at will and subject to removal or suspension at any time by the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners of the City of Jesup unless otherwise provided by law or ordinance.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
ARTICLE 4. - ELECTIONS; VACANCIES

4.1 - Conduct of Non-Partisan Elections.
Political parties shall not conduct primaries for offices of the Mayor and each of the Commissioners, and all names of candidates for offices of the Mayor and each of the Commissioners shall be listed without party designations.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
4.2 - Vacancies in Office.
4.2.1 Vacancies in the Office of the Mayor.
4.2.1.1  Permanent Vacancy. The office of Mayor shall become permanently vacant upon the incumbent's death, resignation, forfeiture of office, or removal from office in any manner authorized by this Charter or general laws of statewide application of the State of Georgia. A vacancy in the office of Mayor shall be filled for the remainder of the unexpired term by a special election if such vacancy occurs twelve (12) months or more prior to the expiration of the term of that office. If such vacancy occurs within twelve (12) months of the expiration of the term of that office, the Mayor and the Commissioners of the City of Jesup, or those members remaining, may appoint a successor for the remainder of the term. For purposes of this section, twelve (12) months shall be synonymous with three-hundred and sixty-five (365) days.
4.2.1.2  Temporary Vacancy. The office of the Mayor shall become temporarily vacant upon the incumbent's suspension from office of the Mayor or when the Mayor, from a bona fide sickness or other such unavailability, is unable to act. In the event of a temporary vacancy the Mayor Pro Tempore shall be appointed in accordance with the provisions outlined in § 2.4.4 of this Charter. The Mayor Pro Tempore shall serve for a term of twelve (12) months or until the next general election, whichever length of time is shortest. The Mayor Pro Tempore shall be clothed with all the rights and privileges of the Mayor and shall perform the duties of the office of the Mayor so long as such suspension, sickness, or other unavailability shall continue. Upon the discontinuation of the suspension, sickness, or other unavailability of the Mayor, as determined by a majority vote of the Board of Commissioners not serving as Mayor Pro Tempore, the Mayor Pro Tempore shall no longer be clothed with the rights and privileges of the Mayor, shall cease to perform the duties of the office of the Mayor, and shall no longer be Mayor Pro Tempore.
4.2.2 Vacancies in the Office of Commissioner. The office of a Commissioner shall become vacant upon the incumbent's death, resignation, forfeiture of office, or removal from office in any manner authorized by this Charter or the general laws of statewide application. A vacancy in the office of a Commissioner shall be filled for the remainder of the unexpired term by a special election, in accordance with Titles 21 and 41 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, if such vacancy occurs twelve (12) months or more prior to the expiration of the term of that office. If such vacancy occurs within twelve (12) months of the expiration of the term of that office, the Mayor and remaining Commissioners shall appoint a successor for the remainder of the term of the vacated office of Commissioner. This provision shall also apply to a temporary vacancy created by the suspension from office of a Commissioner or created by a Commissioner's inability to fulfill his or her official duties. For purposes of this section, twelve (12) months shall be synonymous with one-hundred and forty-four (144) days.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
4.3 - Eligible Voters.
The registration of eligible voters of the City of Jesup shall be integrated with the system of permanent registration of voters established by the Official Code of Georgia Annotated. No voter, even if otherwise qualified, shall be permitted to vote at any election within the municipality unless he or she is duly registered under the provisions of general law of statewide application.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
4.4 - Administration of Elections.
All elections shall be held at such polling locations as set by the Board of Commissioners and the Mayor by ordinance, and conducted in accordance with Chapter 2 of Title 21 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, the "Georgia Election Code," as now or hereafter amended or otherwise provided by law. Except as otherwise provided by this Charter, the Mayor and Board of Commissioners may, by ordinance or resolution, prescribe such rules and regulations as it deems appropriate to fulfill duties imposed under Chapter 2 of Title 21 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, the "Georgia Election Code," as now or hereafter amended or otherwise provided by law. The Mayor and Board of Commissioners may appoint such office or superintendent of elections as permitted by law.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
4.5 - Restrictions on Influencing Elections.
Neither the City Manager, nor any employee of the City of Jesup, in their official capacity, shall campaign for, solicit, take any part in securing, or contribute any work efforts or money towards the nomination or election of any candidate for the office of commissioner or mayor for the City of Jesup, except to answer such direct questions as may be asked of him or her and which he or she desires to answer.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
ARTICLE 5. - MEETINGS

5.1 - Regular and Special Meetings.
5.1.1 Regular. The Mayor and Board of Commissioners shall hold regular meetings at such times and places as shall be prescribed by ordinance of the Mayor and Board of Commissioners, but in no event not less than one (1) time per Gregorian calendar month. Unless prohibited from doing so by emergency.
5.1.2 Special. Special meetings of Mayor and Board of Commissioners may be held on call of the Board of Commissioners of the City of Jesup as shall be prescribed by ordinance of the City of Jesup.
5.1.3 Notice. All meetings of the Mayor and Board of Commissioners of the City of Jesup shall be public to the extent required by law, and notice to the public of special meetings shall be made fully as is reasonably possible as provided by Section 50-14-1 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, or other such applicable laws as are or may hereafter be enacted.
5.1.4 Rules. The Mayor and Board of Commissioners of the City of Jesup shall adopt its rules of procedure and order of business consistent with the provisions of this Charter and shall provide for keeping of a journal of the official proceedings and actions of the City of Jesup and the said Mayor and Board of Commissioners, which shall be a public record.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
5.2 - Voting.
5.2.1 Quorum Required. Four (4) Commissioners shall constitute a quorum and shall be authorized to transact business of the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners of the City of Jesup, Georgia. In the absence of a quorum, the presiding officer shall, at the instance of any member, compel the attendance of absent members; provided, however, the enactment of any ordinance or resolution, or the passage of any motion, or the transaction of any business requiring a vote of the board shall require at least four votes of the board. In instances where at least four (4) votes are not cast, the Abstention rules, as outlined in 5.2.4, shall not apply. Voting on the adoption of ordinances or the resolution upon official action shall be by voice vote, and the vote shall be recorded in the journal or other official records of the City of Jesup, Georgia.
5.2.2 Roll Call Vote. Any Commissioner shall have the right to request a roll call vote, and such vote shall be recorded in the journal.
5.2.3 Majority Vote Required. Except as otherwise provided in this Charter, the affirmative vote of the majority of the members of the Board of Commissioners (excluding the Mayor) voting upon any matter before them shall be required for the adoption of any ordinance, resolution, motion, or other official action of the Mayor and Board of Commissioners of the City of Jesup, or in those instances where it shall be necessary for the Mayor to vote in the event of a tie in the vote of the Commissioners, the affirmative vote of the majority of the Mayor and Board of Commissioners shall be required for the adoption of any ordinance, resolution, motion or other official action of the Mayor and Board of Commissioners of the City of Jesup.
5.2.4 No Abstentions. No Commissioner, who is otherwise eligible to vote, shall abstain from voting on any matter properly brought before the council for official action except when such commissioner has a conflict of interest, as set forth in Section 2.6.2, which is disclosed in writing prior to or at the meeting and made a part of the minutes. Any Commissioner, or the Mayor in the event of a tie in the votes of the Commissioners, when otherwise present and eligible to vote on a matter but refusing to do so for any reason other than a properly disclosed and recorded conflict of interest shall be deemed to have voted in the affirmative.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
ARTICLE 6. - BUDGET

6.1 - Budget, Fiscal, and Accounting Year.
The fiscal, budget, and accounting year of the City shall begin on the first day of July and end on the last day of June of each year.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
6.2 - Preparation and budgets.
The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners shall provide an ordinance on the procedures and requirements for the preparation and execution of an annual operating budget, a capital improvement plan and a capital budget, including requirements as to the scope, content and form of such budgets and plans.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
6.3 - Submission of Operating and Capital Budget to Mayor and the Board of Commissioners.
On or before a date fixed by the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners but not later than ninety (90) days prior to the beginning of each fiscal year, the City Manager shall submit to the Mayor and the Commissioners a proposed operating and capital budget for the ensuing fiscal year. The budget shall be accompanied by a message from the City Manager containing the important features of the budget, explanations of major changes recommended for the next fiscal year, a general summary of the budget, and such other pertinent comments and information as may be requested by the Mayor and the Commissioners. The operating budget and the capital budget hereinafter provided for, the budget message, and all supporting documents shall be filed in the office of the City Clerk and shall be open to public inspection.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
6.4 - Action by Mayor and the Board of Commissioners on Budget.
6.4.1 Amendments. The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners may amend the operating budget proposed by the City Manager; except, that the budget as finally amended and adopted must provide for all expenditures required by state law or by other provisions of this Charter and for all debt service requirements for the ensuing fiscal year.
6.4.2 Final Adoption of Operating and Capital Budgets. The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners by ordinance shall adopt the final operating budget and capital budget for the ensuing fiscal year not later than June 30 of each calendar year. If the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners fail to adopt the operating budget by this date, the amounts appropriated for operation for the immediately-prior fiscal year shall be deemed adopted for the following month expiring on July 31. Adoption of the operating budget and capital budget shall take the form of an appropriations ordinance setting out the estimated revenues and making appropriations according to fund and by organizational unit, purpose, or activity.
6.4.3 No Exceeding Appropriation. Unless the budget is otherwise amended as permitted in this Charter, the amount set out in the adopted operating budget for each organizational unit shall constitute the annual appropriation for such, and no expenditure shall be made or encumbrance created in excess of the otherwise unencumbered balance of the appropriations or allotment thereof, to which it is chargeable.
6.4.4 Changes in Appropriations.The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners, in accordance with general law of statewide application and this Charter, by ordinance, may make changes in the appropriations contained in the current operating budget, at any regular meeting, special or emergency meeting called for such purpose.
6.4.5 Independent Audit. There shall be an annual independent audit of all city accounts, funds and financial transactions by a certified public accountant selected by the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners. The audit shall be conducted according to generally accepted auditing principles. Any audit of any funds by the state or federal governments may be accepted as satisfying the requirements of this charter. Copies of annual audit reports shall be available at printing costs to the public.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
ARTICLE 7. - FINANCE

7.1 - Property taxes.
The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners may assess, levy and collect an ad valorem tax on all real and personal property within the corporate limits of the City that is otherwise subject to such taxation by the State of Georgia and County of Wayne. This tax is for the purpose of raising revenues to defray the costs of operating the City government, of providing governmental services, for the repayment of principal and interest on general obligations, and for any other public purpose as determined by the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners in their discretion.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
7.2 - Tax Levies.
The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners shall levy, by ordinance, such taxes as are necessary or conducive to the efficient and orderly operation of the City. The taxes and tax rates set by such ordinances shall be such that reasonable estimates of revenues from such levy shall at least be sufficient, together with other anticipated revenues, fund balances and applicable reserves, to equal the total amount appropriated for each of the several funds set forth in the annual operating budget for defraying the expenses of the general government of the City of Jesup.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
7.3 - Millage Rate; Due dates; payment methods.
The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners, by ordinance, shall establish a millage rate for the City property tax, a due date, and the time period within which these taxes must be paid. The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners, by ordinance, may provide for the payment of these taxes by two installments or in one lump sum, and further authorize the voluntary payment of taxes prior to the time when due. Any and all appeals related to this Article 7 shall be brought in accordance with general laws of statewide application.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
7.4 - Report of Tax Rate.
At the first regular meeting of the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners after July 15 of each year, or as soon as is reasonably practicable following adoption of the budget ordinance set forth in Article 6, the City Manager shall report in writing to the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners the estimated rate of tax levy required to produce an amount of income which, together with the estimated income from all other sources, will equal the appropriation made by the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners, and the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners shall, by ordinance, at that time, levy taxes at the rate specified in such report unless, by an amendment of the appropriation ordinance, reducing the appropriations, the levy of a lower rate may be made possible, but no such amendment shall reduce the appropriation made to pay the then existing City debt.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
7.5 - Occupation taxes and Regulatory Fees.
7.5.1 Occupation and Business Taxes. The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners, by ordinance, shall have the power to levy such occupation or business taxes as are not denied by law. The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners may classify businesses, occupations or professions for the purpose of such taxation in any way which may be lawful and may compel the payment of such taxes as provided in this Charter.
7.5.2 Regulatory Fees; Permits. The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners, by ordinance, shall have the power to require businesses or practitioners doing business or carrying on commerce within this City to obtain a permit for such activity from the City and pay a reasonable regulatory fee for such permit as provided by general law. Such fees may include the total cost to the City of regulating the activity, and if unpaid, shall be collected as provided in this Charter.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
7.6 - Franchises.
7.6.1 Generally. The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners shall have the power to grant franchises for the use of this City's streets and alleys for the purposes of, street railways, telephone companies, electric companies, electric membership corporations, cable television and other telecommunications companies, cellular communications companies, gas companies, transportation companies and other similar organizations. The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners shall determine the duration, terms, whether the same shall be exclusive or nonexclusive, and the consideration for such franchises; provided, however, no franchise shall be granted for a period in excess of 35 years and no franchise shall be granted unless the City receives just and adequate compensation therefor. The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners shall provide for the registration of all franchises with the City clerk in a registration book kept by the clerk. The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners may provide by ordinance for the registration within a reasonable time of all franchises previously granted.
7.6.2 Taxation. If no franchise agreement is in effect, the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners shall have the authority to impose a tax on gross receipts for the use of the City's streets and alleys for the purposes of railroads, street railways, telephone companies, electric companies, electric membership corporations, cable television and other telecommunications companies, gas companies, transportation companies and other similar organizations.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
7.7 - Service Charges.
The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners, by ordinance, shall have the power to assess and collect fees, charges, assessments, and tolls for water, sewers, sanitary and health services, or any other services or facilities provided or made available within and without the corporate limits of the City. If unpaid, such charges shall be collected as provided in this Charter.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
7.8 - Special Assessments.
The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners shall have the power to assess and collect the cost of constructing, repairing, reconstructing, paving, repaving, widening, or improving any public way, street, alley, lane, sidewalk, curbing, gutters, sewers, drains, ditches, piping, conduit, or other utility mains and appurtenances from the abutting property owners. If unpaid, such charges shall be collected as provided in this Charter. The amount of such assessments on each parcel of real estate, after having been made as herein provided, shall be a lien on said real estate from the date of the passage of the ordinance providing for the work and making the assessment. Such liens shall have rank and priority of payment next in point of dignity to liens for City taxes and other liens which, by law, would have priority; and said City of Jesup, by and through its Mayor and Board of Commissioners, may transfer such fi. fa. in writing, by authorizing, by proper resolution, the mayor to sign such transfer and the clerk to attest same. The transferee or transferees shall have all of the rights under such fi. fa. as were had by said City.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
7.9 - Construction; other taxes and fees.
This City shall be empowered to levy any other tax or fee allowed now or hereafter by law, and the specific mention of any right, power or authority in this article shall not be construed as limiting in any way the general powers of this City to govern its local affairs.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
7.10 - Collection of delinquent taxes, fees, and assessments.
The City may collect delinquent taxes, fees, assessments, or other revenue due the City under this Charter by whatever reasonable means as are not precluded by law. This shall include providing for the dates when the taxes, fees, or assessments are due; late penalties or interest; issuance, execution, and levies of fieiri facias; the sale or other disposition of public property at public outcry in the method in which public sales are conducted for property subject to such fieri facias.; creation and priority of liens; making delinquent taxes, fees, or assessments personal debts of the persons required to pay the taxes, fees, or assessments imposed; revoking City permits for failure to pay any City taxes, fees, or assessments; and providing for the assignment or transfer of tax executions.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
7.11 - General obligation bonds.
The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners shall have the power to issue bonds for the purpose of raising revenue to carry out any project, program or venture authorized under this Charter or general laws of statewide application and further to contract debt on behalf of, and issue bonds of, said City as the valid obligations of said City under and in accordance with the laws and limitations provided in the Constitution and general laws of statewide application of the State of Georgia providing for bonded indebtedness of municipalities, for the purpose of refunding valid and existing debts and bonded obligations of said City, and any other purpose permitted by law. Such bonding authority shall be exercised in accordance with the laws governing bond issuance by municipalities in effect at the time said issue is undertaken.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
7.12 - Revenue bonds.
Revenue bonds may be issued by the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners as state law now or hereafter provides. Such bonds are to be paid out of any revenue produced by the project, program or venture for which they were issued.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
7.13 - Short-Term Loans.
The City may obtain short-term loans as otherwise authorized by this Charter and general laws of statewide application.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
7.14 - Long term loans and other similar obligations.
The City, through official action of the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners, may enter into long-term loans and other obligations as otherwise authorized by laws of general statewide application.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
7.15 - Lease-Purchase Contracts.
The City, through official action of the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners, may enter into multiyear lease, purchase or lease purchase contracts for the acquisition of goods, materials, real and personal property, services, and supplies in accordance with this Charter and general laws of statewide application.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
7.16 - Anticipation Bonds and Notes.
Bonds and notes issued in anticipation of the collection of special assessments and bonds, notes, or registered warrants on the treasury issued in the anticipation of the collection of taxes, may be authorized by the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners, by ordinance, and shall not be deemed the creation of debt in the terms of bonded public indebtedness.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
7.17 - How much; when.
The Board of Commissioners shall, not later than the first regular meeting in December in each year, pass and adopt a license tax ordinance providing for the collection of all special taxes, licenses, and income taxes, as may be determined by the Board of Commissioners, and all such taxes shall be and become due upon the first day of January in each year. The Board of Commissioners may by ordinance designate the time within which all taxes shall be paid, including authority to accept payment of taxes in installments, upon such conditions as the Board of Commissioners may deem proper.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
7.18 - Revenue by any other legal means.
In addition to any of the foregoing revenue raising measures, the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners may raise revenue through any other legal means.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
ARTICLE 8. - EXPENDITURES

8.1 - Authority for Expenditures.
All expenditures of the City's funds or dispositions of the City's property or equipment shall be made as provided by this Charter, through draft, or electronic transfer of funds either executed or approved by both the City Manager and the Treasurer and which is documented in writing or electronically. In the event of the unavailability of either the City Manager or the Treasurer, then the Mayor may execute or approve such drafts or electronic transfers for the unavailable City Manager or Treasurer until the City Manager or Treasurer becomes available.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
8.2 - Purchasing and Contracting procedures.
The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners shall from time to time provide additional purchasing and contracting procedures that are consistent with the provisions of this Charter.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
8.3 - Sale and Lease of City Property.
8.3.1 Transfers. All real property and personal property of the City of Jesup not needed for public use, or that may have become unsuited for public use, may be sold or disposed of as provided for by general laws of statewide application or in such manner as the Mayor and Board of Commissioners may from time to time provide by ordinance if a method or procedure for disposal thereof is not otherwise mandated by a general law of statewide application.
8.3.2 Quitclaims. The Mayor and the Board of Commissioners may quitclaim any rights the City (or they in their official capacities) may have in property not needed for public purposes upon report by the City Manager and adoption of a resolution, both finding that the property is not needed for public or other purposes and that the interest of the City has no readily ascertainable monetary value.
8.3.3 Scrap lands. Whenever in opening, extending or widening any street, avenue, alley or public place of the City, a small parcel or tract of land is cut-off or separated by such work from a larger tract or boundary of land owned by the City, the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners may authorize the City Manager to sell and convey said cut-off or separated parcel or tract of land to an abutting or adjoining property owner or owners where such sale and conveyance facilitates the enjoyment of the highest and best use of the abutting owner's property. Included in the sales contract shall be a provision for the rights-of-way of said street, avenue, alley or public place. In the event that the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners authorize to sell such parcel or tract, then each abutting property owner shall be notified of the availability of the property and given the opportunity to purchase said property under such terms and conditions as set out by ordinance. All deeds and conveyances heretofore and hereafter so executed and delivered shall convey all title and interest the City has in such property, notwithstanding the fact that no public sale after advertisement was or is hereafter made.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
ARTICLE 9. - MUNICIPAL COURT

9.1 - Municipal Court—Creation; Name.
There shall be a court to be known as the Municipal Court of the City of Jesup.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
9.2 - Chief Judge; Associate Judge.
The municipal court shall be presided over by a chief judge or such part-time, full-time, or stand-by judges as shall be provided by ordinance.
9.2.1 Qualifications. No person shall be qualified or eligible to serve as a judge on the municipal court unless that person shall have attained the age of 21 years, shall be a member of the State Bar of Georgia and shall possess all qualifications required by law. All judges shall be appointed by the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners and shall serve until a successor is appointed and qualified.
9.2.2 Compensation. The Compensation of the judges shall be fixed by resolution of the Mayor and Board of Commissioners.
9.2.3 Removal. Judges serve at-will and may be removed from office at any time by the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners unless otherwise provided by ordinance.
9.2.4 Oath. Before assuming office, each judge shall take an oath, given by the Mayor, that the judge will honestly and faithfully discharge the duties of the office to the best of that person's ability and without fear, favor or partiality.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
9.3 - Convening.
The municipal court shall be convened at regular intervals as provided by ordinance.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
9.4 - Jurisdiction; Powers.
The municipal court shall have jurisdiction and authority to try and punish violations of this Charter, all City ordinances, and such other violations as provided by law.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
9.5 - Contempt Powers.
The municipal court shall have authority to punish those in its presence for contempt, provided that such punishment shall not exceed two hundred dollars ($200.00) or ten (10) days in jail.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
9.6 - Fines and Imprisonment.
The municipal court may fix punishment for offenses within its jurisdiction not exceeding a fine of 1,000 dollars ($1,000.00) or imprisonment for one-hundred and eighty (180) days or both such fine and imprisonment or may fix punishment by fine, imprisonment or alternative sentencing as now, or hereafter provided by law.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
[9.7 - Reserved.]
9.8 - Fee Schedule.
The municipal court shall have authority to establish a schedule of fees to defray the cost of operation, and shall be entitled to reimbursement of the cost of meals, transportation, and caretaking of prisoners bound over to superior courts for violations of state law.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
9.9 - Bail.
The municipal court shall have authority to establish bail and recognizances to ensure the presence of those charged with violations before said court, and shall have discretionary authority to accept cash or personal or real property as surety for the appearance of persons charged with violations. Whenever any person shall give bail for that person's appearance and shall fail to appear at the time fixed for trial, the bond shall be forfeited by the judge presiding at such time, and an execution issued thereon by serving the defendant and the defendant's sureties with a rule nisi, at least two (2) days before a hearing on the rule nisi. In the event that cash or property is accepted in lieu of bond for security for the appearance of a defendant at trial, and if such defendant fails to appear at the time and place fixed for trial, the cash so deposited shall be on order of the judge declared forfeited to the city, or the property so deposited shall have a lien against it for the value forfeited which lien shall be enforceable in the same manner and to the same extent as a lien for City property taxes.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
9.10 - Authority.
The municipal court shall have the same authority as superior courts to compel the production of evidence in the possession of any party; to enforce obedience to its orders, judgments and sentences; and to administer such oaths as are necessary.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
9.11 - Power to Compel.
The municipal court may compel the presence of all parties necessary to a proper disposal of each case by the issuance of summonses, subpoenas, and warrants which may be served as executed by any officer as authorized by this charter or by law.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
9.12 - Warrants.
Each judge of the municipal court shall be authorized to issue warrants for the arrest of persons charged with offenses against any ordinance of the city, and each judge of the municipal court shall have the same authority as a magistrate of the state to issue warrants for offenses against state laws committed within the City of Jesup.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
9.13 - Certiorari.
The right of certiorari from the decision and judgment of the municipal court shall exist in all criminal cases and ordinance violation cases, and such certiorari shall be obtained under the sanction of a judge of the Superior Court of County under the laws of the State of Georgia regulating the granting and issuance of writs of certiorari.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
9.14 - Rules for Court.
With the approval of the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners, the judge shall have full power and authority to make reasonable rules and regulations necessary and proper to secure the efficient and successful administration of the municipal court; provided, however, that the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners may adopt in part or into the rules and regulations applicable to municipal courts. The rules and regulations made or adopted shall be filed with the City Clerk and shall be available for public inspection.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
ARTICLE 10. - MISCELLANEOUS

10.1 - Prior Ordinances.
All ordinances, resolutions, rules and regulations now in force in the City of Jesup and not inconsistent with this charter are hereby declared valid and of full effect and force until amended or repealed by the Mayor and the Board of Commissioners of the City of Jesup.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
10.2 - Bonds for Officials.
The officers and employees of the City of Jesup, both elective and appointive, shall execute such surety or fidelity bonds in such amounts and upon such terms and conditions as the Board of Commissioners and the Mayor shall from time to time require by ordinance or as may be provided by law.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
10.3 - Pending Matters.
Except as specifically provided otherwise by this charter, all rights, claims, actions, orders, contracts and legal or administrative proceedings shall continue and any such ongoing work or cases shall be completed by such city agencies, personnel or offices as may be provided by the Mayor and Board of Commissioners of the City of Jesup.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
10.4 - Construction.
Section captions in this Charter are informative only and are not to be considered as a part thereof. The singular shall include the plural, the masculine shall include the feminine, and vice versa.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
10.5 - Severability.
If any article, section, subsection, paragraph, sentence, or part thereof of this Charter shall be held to be invalid or unconstitutional, such invalidity or unconstitutionality shall not affect or impair other parts of this Charter unless it clearly appears that such other parts are wholly and necessarily dependent upon the part held to be invalid or unconstitutional, it being the legislative intent in enacting this Charter that each article, section, subsection, paragraph, sentence or part thereof be enacted separately and independent of each other.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
10.6 - Repealer.
All previous Charters of the City of Jesup are repealed in their entirety and all amendatory acts thereto are likewise repealed in their entirety, including any and all provisions from any and all previous Charters that may not have been specifically addressed by this Charter. All other laws and parts of laws in conflict with this Charter are hereby repealed.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
10.7 - Existing Personnel and Officers.
Except as specifically provided otherwise by this Charter, all personnel and officers of the City of Jesup and their rights, privileges and powers shall continue beyond the time this Charter takes effect until such time as the present Mayor and Board of Commissioners shall pass a transition ordinance detailing any changes in personnel and appointive officers required or desired and arranging such titles, rights, privileges and powers as may be required or desired to allow a reasonable transition.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
10.8 - Effective Date.
This Charter shall become effective on January 2, 2018.
(Ord. of 1-2-2018)
