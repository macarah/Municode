Sec. 2-161. - Chief executive officer and head of administrative branch; powers and duties.
The city manager shall be the chief executive officer of the city and head of the administrative branch of the city government. He shall be responsible to the board of commissioners for the proper administration of all affairs of the city and to that end, and subject to the provisions of the Charter of the city, he shall have power and shall be required to:
(1)
Appoint and remove all officers and employees of the city, except as may otherwise be provided by the Charter of the city and, except that he may authorize the head of a department or office to appoint and remove subordinates in such department or office;
(2)
Ensure that all laws and ordinances of the city are enforced;
(3)
Exercise control over all of the departments or divisions of the city;
(4)
Attend all meetings of the board;
(5)
Prepare and submit to the board an annual budget, setting out the probable needed expenditures in all departments of the city for the ensuing year, which report shall show, as nearly as can be determined, the probable revenue of the city from all sources;
(6)
Keep the board advised as to the financial condition and needs of the city;
(7)
Supervise and direct the conduct of all officers and employees of the city, and supervise the performance of all contracts made by any person for all work done in the city, and make all purchases of materials and supplies used by the city under competitive bids, and under such rules and regulations as the board may prescribe;
(8)
Appoint, discharge and fix the salaries of employees in all departments. All such appointments shall be made upon merit and fitness for the performance of the duties required;
(9)
Perform such other duties as may be prescribed by the Charter of the city, and such other duties as may be required of him by the board.
(Code 1976, § 2-82)
Sec. 2-162. - Oaths of office.
Each of the following officers and employees of the city shall be required to take an oath of office before entering upon the discharge of his duties, which oath shall be subscribed by the person taking it before the city attorney or any other person authorized to administer oaths and preserved in the office of the city clerk:
(1)
Officers. The city manager and each officer and department head.
(2)
Police officers. Every member of the police department, including any private, special, temporary or substitute police officer which the city may appoint.
(3)
Firefighters. Every member of the fire department who serves on a full-time basis.
(Code 1976, § 2-84)

Sec. 2-165. - Bonds may be required.
When required by the board of commissioners, officers and employees of the city shall, before entering upon the duties of their offices, give a good and sufficient surety bond to the city, duly approved by the city attorney, and conditioned upon the faithful performance of their duties and for proper application and payment of all money or property coming into their hands by virtue of their offices, and in such amount as required by the board of commissioners.
(Code 1976, § 2-87)
Sec. 2-166. - Administrative policy and procedures.
(a)
Duties of officers. Each officer shall perform all duties required of his office by state law, the Charter, this Code, the ordinances of the city and such other duties not in conflict therewith as may be required by the city manager, or, in the case of the city manager, by the board of commissioners.
(b)
Duties of department heads. The heads of departments shall:
(1)
Responsibility to city manager. Be immediately responsible to the city manager for the effective administration of their respective departments and all activities assigned thereto.
(2)
Submission of reports to city manager. Submit such reports to the city manager as may be required by him.
(3)
Establish and maintain system of recordkeeping. Establish and maintain such system of keeping records as may be required by the city manager.
(4)
Authority over employees. Have power, when authorized by the city manager, to appoint and remove, subject to civil service regulations as may from time to time be in force, all subordinates under him.
(5)
Maintenance of city property. Be responsible for the proper maintenance of all city property used in his department.
(c)
Departments. Each department shall furnish, upon the direction of the city manager, any other department such service, labor and materials as may be requisitioned by the head of such department and as its own facilities permit through the same procedure and subject to the same audit and control as other expenditures are incurred.
(Code 1976, § 2-88)
Sec. 2-167. - City marshal.
The city marshal shall perform all duties required of him by the Charter of the city, as well as such other duties as may be required of him by the city manager.
(Code 1976, § 2-99)
Sec. 2-168. - Other officers and employees.
The city manager shall designate all other officers employed by the city, as well as outline and delegate their duties.
(Code 1976, § 2-100)
Sec. 2-169. - Personnel policies, rules and general procedures, position classification and pay plan adopted; effective dates.
The personnel policies, rules and general procedures, together with the position classification and pay plan for the city, is hereby adopted. The personnel policies, rules and general procedures shall become effective on April 1, 1998, and the position classification and pay plan shall become effective on July 1, 1998.
(Code 1976, § 2-107)
