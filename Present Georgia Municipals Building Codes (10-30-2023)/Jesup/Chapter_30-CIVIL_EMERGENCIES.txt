Sec. 30-1. - Short title.
This article shall be known as the "Restated Emergency Management Ordinance of the City of Jesup, Georgia."
(Ord. of 6-7-2022(3))
Sec. 30-2. - Purpose.
It is the purpose of this chapter to assist the City of Jesup, Georgia in its mission protect the health, safety, and welfare of citizens and property within the city. This chapter is further enacted pursuant to and for the additional purposes permitted by O.C.G.A. §§ 38-3-27 through 38-3-28 and 38-3-54 through 38-3-56. This chapter shall repeal and replace in its entirety the current provisions of chapter 30 Civil Emergencies, inclusive of all of articles I, II and III thereof, as currently existing in the Official Code of Ordinances of the City of Jesup, Georgia.
(Ord. of 6-7-2022(3))
Sec. 30-3. - Emergency management and related emergency services.
(a)
Emergency management means the preparation for the carrying out of all emergency functions, other than functions for which military forces are primarily responsible, to prevent, minimize and repair injury and damage resulting from emergencies, energy emergencies, disasters or their imminent threat, of a manmade or natural origin, caused by enemy attack, sabotage, civil disturbance, fire, flood, earthquake, wind, storm, wave action, chemical spill or release, disease, contagion, epidemic, pandemic, air or water contamination, blight, drought, infestation, explosion, riot or other hostile action, or other causes. Emergency management and related emergency services, which may be employed in the event of a declaration of a public emergency, may include, but are not limited to, any or all of the following: Firefighting services; police services; medical and health services; rescue; engineering; warning services; communications; defense from radiological, chemical and other special weapons; evacuation of persons from stricken areas; emergency welfare services; emergency transportation; property, plant and equipment protection; temporary restoration of public utility services; and other functions related to civilian protection, together with all other activities necessary or incidental to the preparation for, and carrying out of, such functions.
(b)
Locally declared emergencies. As used in this chapter, a "locally declared emergency" or a "declaration of local emergency" shall mean a declaration by the board of commissioners of an actual or impending emergency or disaster of natural or human origins or pandemic, influenza emergency or impending or actual enemy attack or a public health emergency within or affecting the city.
(c)
State declared emergencies. As used in this chapter, a "state declared emergency" or a "state of emergency" shall mean a declaration by the governor of an actual or impending emergency or disaster of natural or human origin, or pandemic influenza emergency, or impending or actual enemy attack, or a public health emergency, within or affecting or against the city, Wayne County, Georgia, the State of Georgia the United States.
(d)
Federal declared emergencies. As used in this chapter, a "state declared emergency" or a "state of emergency" shall mean a declaration by the United States government of an actual or impending emergency or disaster of natural or human origin, or pandemic influenza emergency, or impending or actual enemy attack, or a public health emergency, within or affecting or against the city, Wayne County, Georgia, the State of Georgia the United States.
(Ord. of 6-7-2022(3))
Sec. 30-4. - Unlawful acts during emergencies.
It shall be unlawful for any person to commit any of the following acts during any of a locally-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or federal-declared emergency:
(1)
Willfully obstruct, hinder or delay any member of the emergency management agency in the enforcement of any lawful rule or regulation issued pursuant to this article or in the performance of any duty imposed by virtue of this chapter 30 of the Official Code of Ordinances of the City of Jesup.
(2)
Refuse entry or access to any authorized representative or agent of the city who requests entry for purposes of evaluating sites involved with emergency management functions to protect the health, safety, or welfare of the public, and who presents appropriate credentials.
(3)
Obstruct, hamper or interfere with any such representative while that individual is in the process of carrying out his or her official duties.
(4)
Perform or commit any act forbidden by any lawful rule or regulation issued pursuant to this article if such act is of such a nature as to give assistance to the enemy; imperil the life or property of any inhabitant of the city; or prevent, hinder or delay the defense or protection of the city.
(5)
Wear, carry or display, without authority or official governmental authorization, any mark or identification specified by the emergency management agency wherein such person represents that he or she is affiliated with, or acting under, the authority of such emergency management agency.
(Ord. of 6-7-2022(3))
Sec. 30-5. - Conditions authorizing declaration of emergency; procedures; continuation of emergencies and meetings of the board.
(a)
A declaration of emergency giving rise to the application of this chapter shall be deemed to exist when federal government has declared the existence of a federal-declared emergency or the state government has declared the existence of a state-declared to exist within the City of Jesup or within Wayne County, Georgia or otherwise affecting.
(b)
In addition to the foregoing section 30-5(a), four or more members of the board of commissioners shall have the right to, in concert, declare a local-declared emergency to exist, giving rise to application of this chapter, when, in their collective opinion, one or more of the following conditions exist:
(1)
There is an extreme likelihood of danger of destruction of life or property due to unusual conditions;
(2)
Unusual or extreme weather conditions, making use of city streets or areas difficult or impossible;
(3)
Civil unrest, commotion or uprising is imminent or exists;
(4)
There is a stoppage or loss of electrical power affecting a major portion of the city;
(5)
Federal or state government has declared the existence of a federal-declared emergency or state-declared emergency within the State of Georgia, and as a consequence of such declaration or the emergent conditions giving rise to such conditions, the city may also be directly affected by such conditions; or
(6)
An actual or threatened occurrence of a disaster or emergency, which may result in the large-scale loss of life, injury, property damage or destruction or in the major disruption of routine community affairs, business or governmental operations in the city and which is of sufficient severity and magnitude to warrant extraordinary assistance by federal, state and local departments and agencies to supplement the efforts of available public and private resources.
Any emergency declared to exist by action of the board of commissioners shall extend for a period of no longer than 72 consecutive hours unless otherwise extended as provided for in section 30-5(c).
(c)
Procedures for declaration of an emergency by the board of commissioners.
(1)
Prior to such action to declare a local emergency by the board of commissioners, the members of board of commissioners participating in such declaration of a local emergency shall first attempt to contact all other members of the board of commissioners, the mayor, the city manager and the city attorney, and to arrange for an emergency meeting of the board of commissioners to discuss such proposed action, which such meeting may be in person, by audio-visual means as determined by the circumstances of the underlying emergency conditions, or by telephone if both an in person meeting or a meeting by audio-visual means are not possible. In the event of an emergency meeting under this section, reasonable efforts to notify the public and local news media shall also be made by those commissioners calling such meeting.
(2)
Upon the declaration of local emergency by the board of commissioners, an official "declaration of local emergency," in substantially the same form set forth below, shall be signed by those commissioners declaring such emergency to exist and filed in the office of the city clerk and shall be communicated to the citizens of the city using the most effective and efficient means available. The declaration shall state the nature of the emergency or disaster, the conditions that require the declaration and any sections of this chapter which shall be in effect.

(d)
Continuance. No state of local emergency that is declared solely by action of the board of commissioners under section 30-5(a) shall continue for longer 72 hours, unless extended by action of the board of commissioners at or during an official meeting of the board prior to which such meeting notice that is required by law is provided to the public. Four or more members of the board of commissioners shall have the right to, in concert, renew the declaration of a local emergency. The declaration of a local emergency by action of the board of commissioners may be renewed for periods of up to 14 consecutive days until an end of such emergency is declared by federal or state authorities or the board of commissioners otherwise finds that emergency conditions no longer exist in the city. The board of commissioners may end a state of local emergency previously declared to exist by the board at any time by resolution.
(e)
Board meetings. During an emergency, meetings of the board of commissioners may be held at such locations as determined to be appropriate by the board under the circumstances giving rise to the emergency. When physical meetings in person are not possible given such emergency, meetings of the board of commissioners may be by video conferencing. In the event that both physical meetings and video conferencing are not possible for the conduct of board meetings during an emergency, then meetings of the board of commissioners may be held telephonically. In all such events, notice of any proposed meeting, including the location or methodology of such meeting, shall be provided to the public and to local news media as soon as is practicable under the circumstances. When such meetings occur outside of the city, all actions taken by the board of commissioners shall be as valid and binding as if performed within the city. Such meetings may be called by the city manager or any two members of the board without regard to or compliance with time-consuming procedures and formalities otherwise required by law.
(Ord. of 6-7-2022(3))
Sec. 30-6. - Powers following a declaration of emergency.
(a)
Activation of emergency operations plan. A declaration of federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency shall automatically activate the local emergency operations plan and shall be authority for the deployment of personnel and use of any forces to which the plan applies and for use or distribution of any supplies, equipment, materials, and facilities assembled, stockpiled or arranged to be made available pursuant to the Georgia Emergency Management Act or any other laws applicable to emergencies or disasters.
(b)
Contracts with local governments. In addition to the normal agreements embodied in the applicable local emergency operations plan for mutual emergency assistance, the city may contract with any state or federal agency, county, or other municipality for the administration of a local emergency response program.
(c)
Request for assistance. Consistent with a declaration of federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency, the city manager may request the state or federal government, or any agency thereof, the governor of the state or county, or other municipality to provide assistance, provided that the disaster or emergency is beyond the capacity of the city to meet adequately and such outside assistance is necessary to supplement local efforts to protect property, public health and safety, or to avert or lessen the threat of a disaster or conditions giving rise to an emergency.
(d)
Location of city business. The affairs and business of the city may be conducted at places other than the regular or usual location, within or outside of the city, when it is not prudent, expedient or possible to conduct business at the regular location. The board of commissioners or the city manager may establish one or more temporary locations for the continuation of city business or may otherwise move, transfer or relocate city offices, personnel or departments as may be necessary under the circumstances of the emergency.
(e)
Public works contracts. Upon declaration of a state of federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency, the city may contract for public works without letting such contract out to the lowest, responsible bidder and without advertising and posting notification of such contract for four weeks; provided, however, that the emergency must be of such nature that immediate action is required and that the action is necessary for the protection of the public health, safety and welfare. Any public works contract entered into pursuant to this subsection shall be entered on the minutes of the city as soon as practical and the nature of the emergency described therein in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 36-91-22(e). Any E-Verify affidavit or other state required affidavit shall be obtained from any contractor if otherwise required by law.
(f)
Purchasing. Upon declaration of a state of federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency, the purchasing ordinances, regulations or policies may be suspended. City officials shall continue to seek to obtain the best prices during the state of federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency.
(g)
Code enforcement. Upon declaration of a state of federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency, the board of commissioners may temporarily suspend the enforcement of the ordinances of the city, or any portion thereof, where the federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency is of such nature that immediate action outside the Official Code of Ordinances of the City of Jesup is required, such suspension is consistent with the protection of the public health, safety and welfare, and such suspension is not inconsistent with any federal or state statutes or regulations.
(h)
Fees. Upon declaration of a state of federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency, the city may temporarily reduce or suspend any permit fees, application fees or other rate structures as necessary to encourage the rebuilding of the areas impacted by the disaster or emergency. The term "fees" include fees or rates charged by the city for building permits, land disturbance permits, zoning applications, special land use permits, temporary land use permits and other fees relating to the reconstruction, repair and clean-up of areas impacted by the disaster or emergency. The term "fees" does not include fees collected by the city on behalf of the state or federal government or fees charged by the city pursuant to a state or federal statute or regulation.
(i)
Temporary dwellings. Upon the declaration of a state of federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency, the city or its designees may issue temporary mobile home, trailer, recreational vehicle or other temporary dwelling structures or parks in any zoning district, even though not otherwise permitted by development code, temporary permits while the primary dwelling is being repaired. The temporary permit shall not exceed six months in duration. Upon expiration of the temporary permit and/or extension, the temporary dwelling must be removed.
(j)
Further actions by the city manager in the event of a federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency.
Following a declaration of a state of federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency and during the continuance of such state of emergency, the city manager is authorized to implement local emergency measures to protect life and property or to bring the conditions giving rise to such emergency under control as set out in this section 30-6(b) and the following subparts.
(1)
The city manager and his or her official designees shall have the legal authority to exercise the powers and discharge the duties conferred by law, including the implementation of the applicable local emergency operations plan, coordination of the emergency responses of public and private agencies and organizations, coordination of recovery efforts with county, state and federal officials, and inspection of emergency or disaster sites.
(2)
In responding to a federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency and in conducting necessary and appropriate survey of the damages caused by the emergency, the city manager and his or her official designee is authorized to enter at a reasonable time upon any property, public or private, for the purpose of evaluating sites involved with emergency management functions to protect the health, safety, and welfare of the public. The city manager is authorized to execute a right-of-entry agreement or similar agreement to use property for these purposes on behalf of the city; however, any such document shall be later presented for ratification by the board of commissioners at its next official meeting.
(3)
Additional emergency powers. The city manager shall have and may exercise for such period as the federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency exists or continues, the following additional emergency powers:
a.
Use, employ, dispatch, allocate and assign employees of the city to assist in the safety and preservation of life, limb and property of its citizenry;
b.
Close streets and sidewalks, and delineate areas within the city wherein an emergency exists;
c.
Impose emergency curfew regulations;
d.
Close business establishments within the affected area that present the imminent risk of harm to persons or property or as otherwise necessary to protect human health;
e.
Close any or all city-owned buildings and other facilities to the use of the general public;
f.
Do all reasonable acts necessary and incidental to the preservation of life, limb and property of the citizenry of the city;
g.
Take such actions as may be authorized or compelled under any federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency, or by any emergency order or directive issued by the federal or state government in connection with such emergency within the city;
h.
Direct and compel the evacuation of all or part of the population from any stricken or threatened area, for the preservation of life or other disaster mitigation, response or recovery;
i.
Prescribe routes, modes of transportation and destinations in connection with evacuation;
j.
Make provision for the availability and use of temporary emergency housing, emergency shelters and/or emergency medical shelters;
k.
Transfer the direction, personnel or functions of any city departments and agencies or units thereof for the purpose of performing or facilitating emergency services;
l.
Utilize all available resources of the city and subordinate agencies over which the city has budgetary control as reasonably necessary to cope with the federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency;
m.
Utilize public property when necessary to address the federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency;
n.
Suspend any law, code provision or regulation prescribing the procedures for conduct of city business, or the orders, rules or regulations of any city agency, if strict compliance with any ordinance, resolution, order, rule or regulation would in any way prevent, hinder or delay necessary action in coping with the federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency, provided that such suspension shall provide for the minimum deviation from the requirements under the circumstances and further provided that, when practicable, specialists shall be assigned to avoid adverse effects resulting from such suspension;
o.
Provide benefits to citizens upon execution of an intergovernmental agreement for grants to meet disaster-related necessary expenses or serious needs of individuals or families adversely affected by a federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency or disaster in cases where the individuals or families are unable to meet the expenses or needs from other means, provided that such grants are authorized only when matching state or federal funds are available for such purposes;
p.
Perform and exercise such other functions, powers and duties as may be deemed necessary to promote and secure the safety and protection of the civilian population, including individuals with household pets and service animals prior to, during, and following a major disaster or federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency; and
q.
Take such actions as may be authorized or compelled under any declaration of a federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency, or under any emergency order or directive issued by the federal or state government in connection with such emergency within the city.
(Ord. of 6-7-2022(3))
Sec. 30-7. - Closed or restricted areas and curfews during a federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency.
(a)
To preserve, protect or sustain the life, health, welfare or safety of persons, or their property, within a designated area under a declaration of federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency, it shall be unlawful for any person to travel, loiter, wander or stroll in or upon the public streets, highways, roads, lanes, parks or other public grounds, public places, public buildings, places of amusement, eating places, vacant lots or any other place during a declared emergency between hours specified by the city manager until the curfew is lifted.
(b)
To promote order, protect lives, minimize the potential for looting and other crimes, and facilitate recovery operations during a federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency, the city manager shall have discretion to impose reentry restrictions on certain areas. The city manager shall exercise such discretion in accordance with the applicable local emergency operations plan, which shall be followed during emergencies. The provisions of this section shall not apply to persons acting in the following capacities:
(1)
Officially authorized and essential law enforcement personnel;
(2)
Officially authorized and essential health care providers;
(3)
Officially authorized and essential personnel of the city;
(4)
Officially authorized National Guard or federal military personnel;
(5)
Officially authorized and essential firefighters;
(6)
Officially authorized and essential emergency response personnel;
(7)
Officially authorized and essential personnel or volunteers working with or through an emergency management agency (EMA);
(8)
Officially authorized and essential utility repair crews;
(9)
Citizens seeking to restore order to their homes or businesses while on their own property or place of business; and
(10)
Other authorized and essential persons as designated on a list compiled by the city manager.
(c)
Enforceability. This section shall be enforced by officers of the law enforcement personnel approved to provide aid and assistance during the federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency. Nothing contained in this section shall prohibit a law enforcement officer from bringing other charges under state law.
(d)
Effective date. This section shall become effective only upon the signing of a declaration of emergency under section 30-5(c)(2), stating this section is in effect.
(Ord. of 6-7-2022(3))
Sec. 30-8. - Registration of building and repair services.
(a)
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 38-3-56, before building, constructing, repairing, renovating or making improvements to any real property, including dwellings, homes, buildings, structures or fixtures within an area in the city designated in a federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency or disaster, any person, firm, partnership, corporation or other entity must register with the city clerk and secure a building permit that is posted at the work site. Each day any such entity does business in the city without complying with this chapter constitutes a separate offense.
(b)
The cost of registration fees in a federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency or disaster is fixed at $50.00 per annum. Registration is nontransferable. The cost of the emergency building permit shall be equal to the cost for a building permit under existing regulations. The permit shall only be authorized for repairs.
(c)
When registering, any person, partnership, corporation or other entity making application must, under oath, complete an application, providing the following information:
(1)
Name of applicant;
(2)
Permanent address and phone number of applicant;
(3)
Applicant's Social Security number or federal Employer Identification number;
(4)
If applicant is a corporation, the state and date of incorporation;
(5)
Tag registration information for each vehicle to be used in the business;
(6)
List of cities and/or counties where the applicant has conducted business within the past 12 months;
(7)
Georgia sales tax number or authorization;
(8)
Georgia business license number, if required;
(9)
Copy of license from Secretary of State, if required;
(10)
A signed and sworn affidavit verifying the applicant's legal presence in the United States as required by O.C.G.A. § 50-36-1; and
(11)
At least one secure and verifiable document as defined in O.C.G.A. § 50-36-2.
(d)
Effective date. This section shall become effective only upon the signing of a declaration of a federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency, stating this section is in effect. Unless otherwise specified in the declaration of emergency or otherwise extended by the board of commissioners, the provisions of this section 30-8 shall remain in effect during the state of emergency and for a subsequent period of three months following the end of such emergency.
(Ord. of 6-7-2022(3))
Sec. 30-9. - Enforcement and remedies.
(a)
Law enforcement. In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 38-3-4, the city police department shall be authorized to enforce the orders, rules and regulations contained in this chapter and/or implemented by the city manager or local governing authority during any federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency.
(b)
Failure to comply with any of the requirements or provisions of the regulations contained in this chapter, or with any code section, order, rule or regulation made effective by the city manager or local governing authority upon or after the declaration of an emergency shall constitute a violation of the provisions of this chapter. Any person who violates any provision in this chapter shall, upon conviction thereof, be punishable by a fine not exceeding $1,000.00, imprisonment for a term not exceeding 180 days, or both such fine and imprisonment, for each violation. Each person assisting in the commission of a violation, shall be guilty of separate offenses. Each day during which a violation or failure to comply continues shall constitute a separate violation.
(c)
Injunctive relief. In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 38-3-5, in addition to the remedies prescribed in this section, the city manager is authorized to obtain an injunction to restrain violation of laws, code sections, orders, rules and regulations which are contained in the Georgia Emergency Management Act and/or this Code, and/or which are implemented by the local governing authority during a declared emergency.
(d)
Except as otherwise provided in this chapter, the ordinance from which this chapter is derived may be enforced by the city police department.
(Ord. of 6-7-2022(3))
Sec. 30-10. - Participation in county emergency management agency.
It is the desire and intent of the city mayor and board of commissioners that the city be a part of the county emergency management agency.
(1)
Appointment of emergency management director. The chairman of the county commission, with concurrence of the mayors of cities within the county and the city manager of the city, may nominate for appointment, by the Governor of the State of Georgia, a director of emergency management for the entire county.
(2)
Duties of emergency management director. When appointed, the emergency management director is charged with the following duties on behalf of the city:
a.
Represent the mayor and board of commissioners on matters pertaining to emergency management;
b.
Assist city officials in organizing city departments;
c.
In conjunction with city and county departments, develop the county plan for emergency functions. Such plan shall be in consonance with the state natural disaster operations plan and nuclear emergency operations plan, and shall be submitted for approval to the governing officials of the county and the cities in the county, and then to the state emergency management agency for approval;
d.
Maintain the emergency management agency and carry out the day-to-day administration of the county emergency management program, including the submission of required reports to the state emergency management agency;
e.
Submit reports as required by governing officials in keeping with good management practices (e.g., financial, daily activity, etc.);
f.
With the authority of governing officials, obtain a facility to be used as the county emergency operating center; and
g.
Coordinate the activities of the county emergency operating center staff during periods of an emergency, under the supervision of the county governing officials.

(4)
Powers during an emergency or disaster. Upon a manmade or natural disaster, an actual enemy attack upon the United States or any other federal-declared emergency, state-declared emergency or local-declared emergency which may affect the lives and property of the citizens of the city, the Board of Commissioners of the City of Jesup as set out in section 30-5(b), or jointly with the chairman of the county commissioners, or in their absences their legally appointed successors, may determine that an emergency or disaster exists and thereafter shall have and may exercise the following powers for such period as such emergency or disaster exists or continues:
a.
Enforce all rules, laws and regulations relating to emergency management, and assume direct operational control over all emergency management resources;
b.
Seize or take for temporary use any private property for the protection of the public;
c.
Sell, lend, give or distribute all or any such property or supplies among the inhabitants of the county, and maintain a strict accounting of property or supplies distributed and for funds received for such property or supplies; and
d.
Perform and exercise such other functions and duties, and take such emergency actions as may be necessary to promote and secure the safety, protection and well being of the inhabitants of the county.
(5)
Volunteers. All persons, other than officers and employees of the city, volunteering in service pursuant to the defense under this article shall serve without compensation. While engaged in such service, such volunteers shall have the same immunities as officers and employees of the city performing similar duties.
