Editor's note— Section 1 of an ordinance adopted July 7, 2015, amended div. 2 in its entirety to read as herein set out. Former div. 2, §§ 2-221—2-224 pertained to similar subject matter and derived from the 1976 Code, §§ 2-210—2-213.

Sec. 2-221. - Statement of purpose and declaration of need.
(a)
There exists in the city an essential need for a downtown development authority to revitalize and redevelop the city, to promote trade, commerce, industry and employment opportunities in the city and to likewise promote the general welfare of the public by creating a climate favorable to the location of new industry, trade and commerce and to foster the further development of existing industry, trade and commerce within the city.
(b)
The revitalization and redevelopment of the central business district of the City of Jesup is essential to develop and promote for the public good and general welfare trade, commerce, industry, and employment opportunities and promote the general welfare of this city by maintaining a climate favorable to the location of new industry, trade, and commerce and the development of existing industry, trade, and commerce within the city.
(c)
It is, therefore, in the public interest and is vital to the public welfare of the people of this city, and it is declared to be the public purpose of this division, so to revitalize and redevelop the central business districts of the City of Jesup.
(d)
Likewise, the continued revitalization and redevelopment of the city is in the public interest and vital to the public welfare. Therefore, be it known to all persons that the public purpose of this division is to protect and serve the public interest and the public welfare through the redevelopment and revitalization of the city.
(e)
The city has previously created and activated the Downtown Development Authority of Jesup, Georgia under that resolution activating the Downtown Development Authority of the Commissioners of the City of Jesup, Georgia dated November 20, 1984. The purpose of this division is to restate the provisions governing the said Downtown Development Authority of Jesup, Georgia.
(Ord. of 7-7-2015(1), § 1)
Sec. 2-222. - Intent and objective.
It is the intent and objective of this division to continue and perpetuate the Downtown Development Authority of Jesup, Georgia, to authorize it to carry out the full range of its powers as provided by the Downtown Development Authorities Law of 1981, as amended, and to promote the Downtown Development Authority of Jesup, Georgia in fulfilling its purposes as stated in such law.
(Ord. of 7-7-2015(1), § 1)
Sec. 2-223. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, or in the alternative if so defined therein shall have the same meaning as set forth in the O.C.G.A. § 36-42-3, as amended from time to time, should those definitions differ.
Cost of the project or cost of any project means and includes:
(1)
All costs of acquisition (by purchase or otherwise), construction, assembly, installation, modification, renovation, or rehabilitation incurred in connection with any project or any part of any project;
(2)
All costs of real property, fixtures, or personal property used in or in connection with or necessary for any project or for any facilities related thereto, including, but not limited to, the cost of all land, estates for years, easements, rights, improvements, water rights, connections for utility services, fees, franchises, permits, approvals, licenses, and certificates; the cost of securing any such franchises, permits, approvals, licenses, or certificates; and the cost of preparation of any application therefor and the cost of all fixtures, machinery, equipment, furniture, and other property used in or in connection with or necessary for any project;
(3)
All financing charges and loan fees and all interest on revenue bonds, notes, or other obligations of the downtown development authority which accrues or is paid prior to and during the period of construction of a project and during such additional period as the authority may reasonably determine to be necessary to place such project in operation;
(4)
All costs of engineering, surveying, and architectural and legal services and all expenses incurred by engineers, surveyors, architects, and attorneys in connection with any project;
(5)
All expenses for inspection of any project;
(6)
All fees of fiscal agents, paying agents, and trustees for bondholders under any trust agreement, indenture of trust, or similar instrument or agreement; all expenses incurred by any such fiscal agents, paying agents, and trustees; and all other costs and expenses incurred relative to the issuance of any revenue bonds, notes, or other obligations for any project;
(7)
All fees of any type charged by the downtown development authority in connection with any project;
(8)
All expenses of or incidental to determining the feasibility or practicability of any project;
(9)
All costs of plans and specifications for any project;
(10)
All costs of title insurance and examinations of title with respect to any project;
(11)
Repayment of any loans made for the advance payment of any part of any of the foregoing costs, including interest thereon and any other expenses of such loans;
(12)
Administrative expenses of the downtown development authority and such other expenses as may be necessary or incidental to any project or the financing thereof or the placing of any project in operation; and
(13)
The establishment of a fund or funds for the creation of a debt service reserve, a renewal and replacement reserve, or such other funds or reserves as the downtown development authority may approve with respect to the financing and operation of any project and as may be authorized by any bond resolution, trust agreement, indenture of trust, or similar instrument or agreement pursuant to the provisions of which the issuance of any revenue bonds, notes, or other obligations of the downtown development authority may be authorized.
(14)
Any cost, obligation, or expense incurred for any of the foregoing purposes shall be a part of the cost of the project and may be paid or reimbursed as such out of proceeds of revenue bonds, notes, or other obligations issued by the downtown development authority.
Downtown development area means the geographical area within the City of Jesup designated as such by the resolution of the Commissioners of the City of Jesup as modified by any subsequent resolution of the Commissioners of the City of Jesup.
Project means:
(1)
The acquisition, construction, installation, modification, renovation, or rehabilitation of land, interests in land, buildings, structures, facilities, or other improvements located or to be located within the downtown development area, and the acquisition, installation, modification, renovation, rehabilitation, or furnishing of fixtures, machinery, equipment, furniture, or other property of any nature whatsoever used on, in, or in connection with any such land, interest in land, building, structure, facility, or other improvement, all for the essential public purpose of the development of trade, commerce, industry, and employment opportunities in its authorized area of operation; and
(2)
The provision of financing to property owners for the purpose of installing or modifying improvements to their property in order to reduce the energy or water consumption on such property or to install an improvement to such property that produces energy from renewable resources.
(3)
A project may be for any industrial, commercial, business, office, parking, public, or other use, provided that a majority of the directors of the downtown development authority determine, by a duly adopted resolution, that the project and such use thereof would further the public purpose of this division. Such term shall include any one or more buildings or structures used or to be used as a not-for-profit hospital, not-for-profit skilled nursing home, or not-for-profit intermediate care home subject to regulation and licensure by the department of community health and all necessary, convenient, or related interests in land, machinery, apparatus, appliances, equipment, furnishings, appurtenances, site preparation, landscaping, and physical amenities.
Revenue bonds or bonds means any bonds of the downtown development authority, which are authorized to be issued under the Constitution and laws of Georgia, including refunding bonds but not including notes or other obligations of the downtown development authority.
(Ord. of 7-7-2015(1), § 1)
Sec. 2-224. - Powers.
The Downtown Development Authority of Jesup, Georgia shall have those powers, and fulfill those duties, as created, imposed and existing under the Downtown Development Authorities Law of 1981, as amended. In addition thereto, the Downtown Development Authority of Jesup, Georgia shall have the following additional powers:
(1)
To sue and be sued;
(2)
To adopt and amend a corporate seal;
(3)
To make and execute contracts and other instruments necessary to exercise the powers of the authority, any of which contracts may be made with the county or may be made with any one or more municipal corporations in the county, and the county and all municipal corporations therein are hereby authorized to enter into contracts with the authority;
(4)
To receive and administer gifts, grants and devises of any property and to administer trusts;
(5)
To acquire by purchase, gift or construction any real or personal property desired to be acquired as part of any project or for the purpose of improving, extending, adding to, reconstructing, renovating or remodeling any project or part thereof already acquired, or for the purpose of demolition to make room for such project or any part thereof;
(6)
To sell, lease, exchange, transfer, assign, pledge, mortgage or dispose of, or grant options for any such purposes, any real or personal property or interest therein;
(7)
To mortgage, convey, pledge or assign any properties, revenues, income, tolls, charges or fees owned or received by the authority;
(8)
To appoint officers and retain agents, engineers, attorneys, fiscal agents, accountants and employees and to provide for their compensation and duties;
(9)
To extend credit or make loans to any person including any industrial entity, for the planning, design, construction, acquisition or carrying out of any project, which credit or loans shall be secured by loan agreements, mortgages, security agreements, contracts and all other instruments or fees or charges, upon such terms and conditions as the authority shall determine reasonable in connection with such loans, including provision for the establishment and maintenance of reserves and insurance funds, and in the exercise of powers granted by this section in connection with a project for the person to require the inclusion in any contract, loan agreement, security agreement or other instrument, such provisions for guaranty, insurance, construction, use, operation, maintenance and financing of a project as the authority may deem necessary or desirable;
(10)
To acquire, accept or retain equitable interests, security interests or other interests in any property, real or personal, by mortgage, assignment, security agreement, pledge, conveyance, contract, lien, loan agreement or other consensual transfer in order to secure the repayment of any monies loaned or credit extended by the authority;
(11)
To construct, acquire, own, repair, remodel, maintain, extend, improve and equip projects located on land owned or leased by the authority or land owned or leased by others and to pay all or part of the cost of any such project from the proceeds of revenue bonds of the authority or from any contribution or loans by persons or any other contribution, all of which the authority is hereby authorized to receive and accept and use;
(12)
To borrow money and issue its revenue bonds and bond anticipation notes from time to time and use the proceeds thereof for the purpose of paying all or part of the cost of any project, including the cost of extending, adding to or improving such project, or for the purpose of refunding any such bonds of the authority theretofore issued, and to otherwise carry out the purposes of this chapter and to pay all other costs of the authority incident to, or necessary and appropriate to, such purposes, including the providing of funds to be paid into any funds to secure such bonds and notes; provided that all bonds and notes shall be issued in accordance with the procedures and subject to the limitations set forth in O.C.G.A. § 36-62-8;
(13)
As security for repayment of authority obligations, to pledge, mortgage, convey, assign, hypothecate or otherwise encumber any property, real or personal, of the authority and to execute any trust agreement, indenture or security agreement containing any provisions not in conflict with law, which trust agreement, indenture or security agreement may provide for foreclosure or forced sale of any property of the authority upon default on the obligations either in payment of principal or interest or in the performance of any term or condition contained in the agreement or indenture; the state on behalf of itself and each county, municipal corporation, political subdivision or taxing district therein hereby waives any right it or county, municipal corporation, political subdivision or taxing district may have to prevent the forced sale or foreclosure of any property of the authority so mortgaged or encumbered, and any such mortgage or encumbrance may be foreclosed in accordance with law and the terms thereof;
(14)
Any authority authorizing an air transportation facility is empowered to contract with any county or municipality in the state, and any county or municipality in the state is empowered to contract with any authority to furnish air transportation services where such service is not otherwise in existence; and
(15)
To do all things necessary or convenient to carry out the powers expressly conferred above.
(Ord. of 7-7-2015(1), § 1)
Sec. 2-225. - Directors.
The board of directors of the downtown development authority shall consist of seven directors.
(1)
Directors shall be:
a.
Taxpayers residing in the municipal corporation for which the authority is created;
b.
Owners or operators of businesses located within the downtown development area and who shall be taxpayers residing in the county in which is located the municipal corporation for which the authority is created; or
c.
Persons having a combination of the qualifications specified in paragraphs a. and b. of this subsection;
d.
Provided, however, that one of such directors may be a member of the governing body of the municipal corporation.
(2)
Not less than four of the directors having the qualifications specified in subsection (1) of this section shall be persons who, in the judgment of the Commissioners of the City of Jesup, either have or represent a party who has an economic interest in the redevelopment and revitalization of the downtown development area.
(3)
Notwithstanding subsection (1) of this section, one director appointed to the board may reside outside the county; provided, however, that such appointed director owns a business within the downtown development area and is a resident of the State of Georgia. If subsequently to his or her appointment to the board pursuant to this subsection, the director ceases to own a business within the downtown development area or reside in the State of Georgia, such director shall relinquish his or her seat on the board.
(4)
Successors to the directors shall be appointed by the governing body of the municipal corporation.
(5)
Terms. The directors of the downtown development authority shall serve for a term of office for four years. The term of a director who is also a member of the Commissioners of the City of Jesup shall also end when such director is no longer a member of the Commissioners of the City of Jesup. If at the end of any term of office of any director a successor to such director has not been appointed, the director whose term of office has expired shall continue to hold office until a successor is appointed. Said four-year terms shall coincide, and run parallel with, the regular terms of the Commissioners of the City of Jesup.
(6)
Training requirements. Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 36-42-7, any director of the downtown development authority, except for a director who is also a member of the Commissioners of the City of Jesup, shall attend and complete at least eight hours of training on development and redevelopment programs within the first 12 months of the director's appointment to the downtown development authority.
(7)
Removal from office. The death of a director, physical or mental disability of a director preventing his or her service as a director, or the disqualification from service, by operation of law or otherwise, of any director shall automatically result in said director's immediate removal.
(8)
Appointment of officers. The directors shall elect one of their members as chairman and another as vice chairman and shall also elect a secretary and a treasurer or a secretary-treasurer, either of whom may but need not be a director. Officers shall remain in such designated post until such post is filled or reappointed by the membership.
(9)
Quorum. A simple majority of the board of directors of the downtown development authority shall constitute a quorum. However, no action shall be taken by the Downtown Development Authority of Jesup, Georgia without the affirmative vote of a majority of the full directorship of the board.
(10)
Faithful execution of duties required. The directors shall faithfully carry out their duties in an exemplary manner and abide by all laws and regulations binding on the downtown development authority and its members.
(11)
Compensation and expense reimbursement. The directors shall receive no compensation for their services but shall be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred by them in the performance of their duties.
(12)
Bylaws, operating procedures and long range plans. The directors appointed according to this division shall, at the earliest possible time, ensure that a set of bylaws, operating procedures and long range plans of development have been developed and adopted.
(13)
Publication of reports. The directors appointed according to this division shall cause to be published both interim and annual reports of the downtown development authority's activities, plans and accomplishments.
(14)
Provision of annual audit. The directors appointed according to this division shall annually employ an independent certified public accountant to perform and publish an audit of financial transactions and the financial position of the downtown development authority.
(Ord. of 7-7-2015(1), § 1)
Sec. 2-226. - Boundaries of the central business district.
(a)
The boundaries of the authority's district shall be redefined as follows: For a point of beginning, commencing at the intersection of United States Highway 84 (Georgia State Route 38 or also known as South First Street) and West Pine Street; and thence, from said point of beginning proceeding east by southeast along West Pine Street to its intersection with Northeast Brunswick Street; and thence from said intersection proceeding generally south by southwest along Brunswick Street to its intersection with East Orange Street; and thence from said intersection, proceeding generally west by northwest along Orange Street to its intersection with United States Highway 84 (Georgia State Route 38 or also known as South First Street); and thence from said intersection returning then north by northeast along United States Highway 84 (Georgia State Route 38 or also known as South First Street) to its intersection with West Pine Street, which is the point of beginning.
(b)
The Commissioners of the City of Jesup shall have, and solely retain, the authority to redefine the boundaries of the authority's district from time to time.
(Ord. of 7-7-2015(1), § 1)
Sec. 2-227. - City of Jesup not bound.
The downtown development authority shall have no power or authority to bind the City of Jesup by any contract, agreement, financial obligation, indebtedness, or otherwise; and no contract, agreement, financial obligation, or indebtedness incurred by the downtown development authority shall ever be a claim or charge against the City of Jesup, provided that both the downtown development authority and the City of Jesup shall be bound to each other by contracts, agreements, financial obligations or indebtedness between themselves.
(Ord. of 7-7-2015(1), § 1)
Sec. 2-228. - Dissolution.
Should the downtown development authority for any reason be dissolved, title to all property of any kind and nature, real and personal, held by the joint board at the time of such dissolution shall be conveyed to the City of Jesup; or title to any such property may be conveyed prior to such dissolution in accordance with provisions which may be made therefor in any resolution or trust instrument relating to such property, subject to any liens, leases, or other encumbrances outstanding against or in respect to such property at the time of such conveyance.
(Ord. of 7-7-2015(1), § 1)
Secs. 2-229, 2-230. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - JOINT BOARD OF TOURISM[7]

Footnotes:

Sec. 2-231. - Short title.
This division will be known and may be cited as the "Joint Board of Tourism for the Cities of Jesup, Odum and Screven and Wayne County Act."
(Ord. of 1-15-2013, § 1)
Sec. 2-232. - Definitions and references.
As used in this division, the term:
Cities means the Cities of Jesup, Odum and Screven, which are bodies public and politic in Wayne County, Georgia.
Conventions means that term as defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-50 et seq.
County means Wayne County, Georgia.
Joint board means the new joint board of tourism for the Cities of Jesup, Screven, and Odum and Wayne County that is created hereunder.
Special events means events which, in the judgment of the joint board, will promote tourism in the Cities of Jesup, Odum and Screven and Wayne County, Georgia.
Tourism means that term as defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-50 et seq.
Trade show means that term as defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-50 et seq.
(Ord. of 1-15-2013, § 2)
Sec 2-233. - Creation of joint board; status, tax exemption, and sovereign immunity.
(a)
There is hereby created a new body public and politic to be known as the Joint Board of Tourism for the Cities of Jesup, Odum and Screven and Wayne County (hereinafter in this division also referred to as the "joint board"). The joint board is intended to be an agency and instrumentality of the City of Jesup and Wayne County and a governmental unit for purposes of Sections 103, 141 and 150 of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended, and, is intended to be a subordinated entity to the City of Jesup and Wayne County to the extent necessary under Section 265(b)(3)(E)(ii) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended.
(b)
The joint board shall have its principal office in the geographical limits of the City of Jesup, as amended from time to time, and its legal situs or domiciliary for the purposes of this division shall be the said geographical limits of the City of Jesup.
(c)
The exercise of the powers conferred upon the joint board in this division shall constitute essential governmental functions for public purposes. The properties of the joint board, both real and personal, and the income of the joint board are declared to be public properties and income used for the benefit and welfare of the people of the Cities of Jesup, Odum and Screven and Wayne County, and not for the purpose of private or corporate benefit, and such properties, to the extent of the joint board's ownership thereof or other interest therein, and all income and obligations of the joint board shall be exempt from all taxes and special assessments of the State of Georgia or any city, county, or other political subdivision thereof. The joint board shall have all of the exemptions and exclusions from taxes as are now granted to cities and counties in the State of Georgia for the operation of properties or facilities similar to the properties and facilities to be owned or operated, or both, by the joint board.
(d)
Any action to protect or enforce any rights under the provisions of this division or any suit or action against the joint board shall be brought in the Superior Court of Wayne County, Georgia.
(e)
The property of the joint board shall not be subject to levy and sale under legal process.
(f)
The records of the joint board shall be public records which are subject to O.C.G.A. § 50-18-70 et seq.
(g)
Nothing in this division shall be construed to abridge or change the powers and duties of other authorities, departments, boards, and like agencies of Wayne County, Georgia or the Cities of Jesup, Odum or Screven.
(Ord. of 1-15-2013, § 3)
Sec. 2-234. - Members and meetings.
(a)
The joint board shall consist of eight appointed members, who shall serve terms of four years.
(1)
Three members shall be appointed by the board of commissioners of Wayne County, Georgia.
(2)
Three members shall be appointed by the board of commissioners of the City of Jesup, Georgia.
(3)
One member shall be appointed by the board of commissioners of the City of Odum, which such member shall be a resident of the City of Odum.
(4)
One member shall be appointed by the board of commissioners of the City of Screven, which such member shall be a resident of the City of Screven.
(5)
The city manager of the City of Jesup and the county administrator of Wayne County, Georgia shall be ex officio, nonvoting members of the joint board.
(b)
The initial term of the members of the joint board shall end on December 31, 2016.
(c)
Appointments for succeeding terms shall be made in January of the calendar year following expiration of terms with said appointments before effective thereupon.
(d)
Any member may resign at any time by filing a written notice of resignation with the joint board.
(e)
The joint board shall meet at such times as may be necessary to transact the business coming before it. Meetings shall be conducted in accordance with Robert's Rules of Order.
(f)
At the first meeting of the joint board, the members shall elect a chairperson, a vice-chairperson, and a secretary-treasurer from its membership. Thereafter, commencing in the year 2014, at the first meeting of the joint board and each calendar year thereafter, the members shall elect a chairperson, a vice-chairperson, and a secretary-treasurer from its membership for that calendar year. The chairperson shall preside at meetings of the joint board. The vice-chairperson shall preside at meetings in the absence of the chairperson. In the absence of both the chairperson and vice-chairperson, the members present at a meeting shall elect a temporary chairperson to preside at that meeting so long as the chairperson and vice-chairperson both remain absent from the meeting.
(g)
The joint board shall have the right to elect or hire the chief executive officer or program coordinator of the joint board, to set the salary or wages of such person, to pay a salary or wages to such person, and to otherwise set the terms and conditions of the employment of such person.
(h)
Four members shall constitute a quorum. Official action may be taken by majority vote of those members voting on a matter if a quorum is present and voting on such matter, except that the bylaws of the joint board may only be initially adopted or subsequently amended by majority vote of all members. All members present at a meeting, including the chairperson, vice-chairperson or any other member presiding at such meeting, shall be entitled to vote on all matters that come before the meeting. No vacancy on the joint board shall impair the right of the quorum to exercise all the rights and perform all the duties of the joint board.
(i)
Members shall receive no compensation for their services as members of the joint board, but may be reimbursed for their proper and reasonable expenses incurred in the performance of their duties, subject to any limitations imposed by general law of the State of Georgia on the reimbursement of public officials and subject to any limitations which may be contained from time to time in the bylaws of the joint board.
(Ord. of 1-15-2013, § 4)
Sec. 2-235. - Purpose.
The purpose of the joint board is to plan, coordinate, and administer an effective tourism program for Wayne County and the Cities of Jesup, Odum and Screven in such manner as the joint board shall determine to be appropriate. All proceeds of the hotel-motel tax excise tax collected by the Cities of Jesup, Odum and Screven and Wayne County, which are provided to the joint board, shall be expended in compliance with the applicable provisions of state law, inclusive of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51, as amended.
(Ord. of 1-15-2013, § 5)
Sec. 2-236. - Powers.
(a)
The joint board shall have all powers allowed by law and consistent with the provisions of this division as are necessary or convenient to carry out its corporate purpose, including, without limitation, the power to:
(1)
Adopt and alter a corporate seal.
(2)
Purchase advertising promoting tourism, conventions, trade shows, and special events.
(3)
Encourage, solicit, promote, procure, sponsor, co-sponsor, and service conventions, trade shows and special events.
(4)
Conduct activities to foster better public understanding on the part of individuals and businesses of the importance of tourism and the convention and visitors industry to the economy of Wayne County and the Cities of Jesup, Odum and Screven.
(5)
Conduct activities to encourage and assist the cooperation between the businesses and industries servicing tourists, conventions, and special events.
(6)
Engage in fundraising activities in furtherance of its corporate purpose.
(7)
Acquire by purchase, lease, or otherwise and to hold, lease, and dispose of real and personal property of every kind and character for its corporate purposes.
(8)
Acquire in its own name by purchase, on such terms and conditions and in such manner as it may deem proper, real property or rights of easements therein or franchises necessary or convenient for its corporate purpose, to use the same so long as its corporate existence shall continue, to lease or make contracts with respect to the use of the same, or to dispose of the same in any manner it deems to the best advantage of the joint board.
(9)
Appoint, select, and employ a chief executive officer, program director, officers, agents, and employees, and independent consultants including but not limited to engineering, architectural, and construction experts, fiscal agents, economists, and attorneys and fix their respective compensations; and to delegate to the chief executive officer the authority and responsibility necessary to administer properly the day-to-day business of the joint board within policies set by the joint board and subject to its review. The powers delegated to the chief executive officer may, at the election of the joint board, include the making of recommendations as to the hiring and termination of other employees and their compensation, the management of the joint board's offices and properties, the making of budget recommendations, and the hiring of independent consultants.
(10)
Appoint an advisory committee and other committees of persons from the public and private sectors without regard to their place of residence.
(11)
To make contracts of every kind and character, and, without limitation, and, without limiting the generality of the above, the joint board shall be permitted to enter into the following:
a.
Contracts under which hotel-motel taxes collected by the Cities of Jesup, Odum and Screven and Wayne County are paid to and expended by the joint board as contemplated by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(b).
b.
Contracts under which the joint board purchases administrative and financial management services from the Cities of Jesup, Odum and Screven and Wayne County to be performed by personnel at the cost of such governmental entity, which shall include the costs of payroll, employee benefits, supplies, and overhead reasonably allocable to the performance of such services.
c.
Lease contracts relating to leases of real property, personal property, or both real and personal property.
(12)
Accept loans and grants of money or materials or property of any kind from the United States of America or any agency or instrumentality thereof, upon such terms and conditions as the United States of America or such agency or instrumentality may impose.
(13)
Accept loans and grants of money or materials or property of any kind from the State of Georgia or any agency or instrumentality or political division thereof, upon such terms and conditions as the State of Georgia or such agency or instrumentality or political subdivision may impose.
(14)
Sell, lease, grant, exchange, or otherwise dispose of any property, both real and personal, or interest therein.
(15)
Sue and be sued in contract and in tort and to complain and defend in all courts.
(16)
Advise and recommend plans to other public and private sector entities for the promotion of tourism, conventions, and trade shows.
(17)
Conduct studies and develop plans for improving tourism in Wayne County and the Cities of Jesup, Odum and Screven.
(18)
Receive and disburse public funds appropriated by Wayne County and the Cities of Jesup, Odum and Screven, including but not limited to revenues derived from the hotel-motel excise tax collected by them; and to receive and disburse funds from private sources and other revenues which may be received from time to time which would assist in the accomplishment of its corporate purpose.
(19)
Do all things necessary or convenient to accomplish its corporate purpose and to exercise any power permitted by the laws of the state to be exercised by private corporations which will further the joint board's ability to accomplish such purpose, so long as the exercise of such power is not in conflict with the Constitution or laws of this state.
(b)
The powers enumerated in each paragraph of subsection (a) of this section are cumulative of and in addition to those powers enumerated in the other paragraphs of subsection (a) of this section and any other powers elsewhere in this division or which may reasonably be inferred from the provisions of this division. This division shall be liberally construed to affect the described purposes, and in interpreting this division, the courts are not to apply "Dillon's Rule."
(Ord. of 1-15-2013, § 6)
Sec. 2-237. - Finances.
The joint board shall submit quarterly financial statements to the board of commissioners of Wayne County and the board of commissioners of the City of Jesup.
(Ord. of 1-15-2013, § 7)
Sec. 2-238. - Bylaws.
The joint board may, by affirmative vote of a majority of all members, adopt bylaws to govern the joint board, its employees, and operation and may by affirmative vote of all members repeal, replace, or amend such bylaws.
(Ord. of 1-15-2013, § 8)
Sec. 2-239. - Liability limited.
Neither the members of the joint board nor any person executing bonds, notes, leases, or other agreements or obligations on behalf of the joint board shall be personally liable thereon by reason of such execution.
(Ord. of 1-15-2013, § 9)
Sec. 2-240. - Cities and Wayne County not bound.
The joint board shall have no power or authority to bind Wayne County or the Cities of Jesup, Odum and Screven by any contract, agreement, financial obligation, indebtedness, or otherwise; and no contract, agreement, financial obligation, or indebtedness incurred by the joint board shall ever be a claim or charge against the Wayne County or the Cities of Jesup, Odum and Screven, provided that both the joint board and Wayne County or the Cities of Jesup, Odum and Screven shall be bound to each other by contracts, agreements, financial obligations or indebtedness between themselves.
(Ord. of 1-15-2013, § 10)
Sec. 2-241. - Dissolution.
Should the joint board for any reason be dissolved, title to all property of any kind and nature, real and personal, held by the joint board at the time of such dissolution shall be conveyed to the Wayne County, Georgia and the City of Jesup jointly; or title to any such property may be conveyed prior to such dissolution in accordance with provisions which may be made therefor in any resolution or trust instrument relating to such property, subject to any liens, leases, or other encumbrances outstanding against or in respect to such property at the time of such conveyance.
(Ord. of 1-15-2013, § 11)
