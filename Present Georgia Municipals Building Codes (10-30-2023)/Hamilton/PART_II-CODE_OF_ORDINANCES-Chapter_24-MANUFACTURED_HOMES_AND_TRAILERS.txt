ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 24-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Building official means the duly appointed building inspector of the city, or his authorized representative.
Health officer means the legally designated health authority of the city, or his legal agent.
Mobile home space means a plot of ground within a mobile home park designed for the accommodation of not more than one mobile home for single-family occupancy.
Service buildings means a building, housing facilities such as recreational, maintenance, laundry, and office structures necessary to the successful development and management and use of a mobile home park.
Travel trailer means a mobile unit designed for camping, recreational travel, or vacation use which is equipped with a chassis and provides partial housekeeping facilities.
(Ord. No. 1974-01-14, § 1, 1-14-1974)
Secs. 24-2—24-20. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - REQUIREMENTS

Sec. 24-21. - Application for a mobile home park.
The mayor and city council may recommend approval of a planned mobile home park and issue a permit or license for the operation of a mobile home park when all of the provisions of this article have been met, and an application and all accompanying plans and supporting data shall be filed in triplicate with the mayor and council at least two weeks prior to a regular meeting of the council. The application shall include the following:
(1)
The name and address of the applicant;
(2)
The location and description of the boundaries of the property proposed to be used for a planned mobile home park development;
(3)
A complete mobile home park plan showing all existing conditions and proposed site development as required in this section, including preliminary plans of all buildings, improvements, and facilities constructed or to be constructed within the mobile home park (on a topographic map);
(4)
Any other information requested by the mayor and council to determine the proposed park's compliance with legal requirements, the effect which the proposed park may have on its environment and the compatibility of the adjacent area for park development within the framework for long-range land development objectives.
(Ord. No. 1974-01-14, § 2, 1-14-1974)
Sec. 24-22. - Dimensional and site development requirements (mobile home park plan).
The mobile home park plan shall show or propose all requirements listed in this article, and, in addition, all said requirements shall be complied with before a certificate of occupancy or permit, or license, can be issued.
(Ord. No. 1974-01-14, § 3, 1-14-1974)
Sec. 24-23. - Minimum number of mobile home spaces.
No park shall be permitted with less than 20 spaces.
(Ord. No. 1974-01-14, § 4, 1-14-1974)
Sec. 24-24. - Required lot (mobile home space) area.
Well-defined mobile home spaces shall be provided, consisting of a minimum of 4,500 square feet for each space.
(Ord. No. 1974-01-14, § 5, 1-14-1974)
Sec. 24-25. - Yard requirements.
Each space shall be at least 50 feet wide and clearly defined. There shall be at least a 20-foot side yard and a 15-foot clearance between mobile homes, including mobile homes parked end to end. No mobile home shall be located closer than 15 feet to any building or street within the park, nor within 20 feet of any exterior street or boundary line of the park.
(Ord. No. 1974-01-14, § 6, 1-14-1974)
Sec. 24-26. - Drainage.
The park shall be located on a well-drained and properly graded site. Necessary site draining improvements as approved by the city shall be provided.
(Ord. No. 1974-01-14, § 7, 1-14-1974)
Sec. 24-27. - Interior drives and walkways.
All mobile home spaces shall abut an interior paved drive of not less than 20 feet in width, which shall have unobstructed access to a public street or highway in accordance with ordinances of this city. Hard surface walkways not less than three feet wide shall be provided from mobile home spaces to service buildings. All interior drives and walkways within the park shall be adequately maintained by the owner.
(Ord. No. 1974-01-14, § 8, 1-14-1974)
Sec. 24-28. - Off-drive parking.
Each mobile home space shall be provided with at least one off-drive parking space, dust-free and adequately marked. In addition, the developer shall provide one additional off-drive parking space, dust-free and adequately marked, for each three mobile home spaces in the park.
(Ord. No. 1974-01-14, § 9, 1-14-1974)
Sec. 24-29. - Signs.
The following non-flashing and non-animated signs are permitted in accordance with the conditions specified in this section:
(1)
Park identification sign. One park identification sign shall be permitted, said sign not to exceed 24 square feet in area for the purpose of denoting the name of the mobile home park.
(2)
Mobile home identification sign. One mobile home identification sign not exceeding one square foot in area shall be permitted for each mobile home. Said sign shall indicate only the name and address of the occupant of the mobile home.
(3)
No sign shall be located as to impede or obstruct the visibility of the operator of any vehicle at any intersection of street lines with one another, or the edges of driveways with the street lines.
(Ord. No. 1974-01-14, § 10, 1-14-1974)
Sec. 24-30. - Improvements to mobile homes.
(a)
No permanent addition of any kind shall be built into or become a part of any mobile home, with the exception of awnings and porches, and provided all mobile homes must be on permanent foundations which are concealed from sight by the use of landscaping, skirting, fencing, painting, or other appropriate means.
(b)
The owner of a mobile home park shall provide a concrete patio of at least four inches thick on the access side of the mobile home so as to lie beneath both doors; provided, however, said patio shall be a minimum of eight feet in width and a minimum of 40 feet in length.
(Ord. No. 1974-01-14, § 11, 1-14-1974)
Sec. 24-31. - Special conditions and safeguards.
(a)
In recommending any planned mobile home park, the mayor and council may attach special conditions and safeguards to protect both the occupants of the park and the occupants of surrounding property, including such matters as protection against noise, lights, dust, and fire. Where required to serve these ends, walls, plantings, surfacing, or other natural or artificial means for protection may be required as a part of such special conditions on which the recommendation for a mobile home park is based. Failure to meet such conditions shall be grounds for refusal to issue a certificate of occupancy, license, or permit.
(b)
Whenever there is not a public fire hydrant within 1,000 feet of each mobile home space, as measured along the roadways, the mobile home park shall be provided with hydrants for use within 20 feet of each mobile home space, equipped for use with a standard garden hose.
(Ord. No. 1974-01-14, § 12, 1-14-1974)
Sec. 24-32. - Mobile homes on individual lots.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to place a trailer or mobile home, whether permanent, dependent, or independent, designed for use for human habitation, and then use the same for living, sleeping, or business purposes, on any premises in the city except in authorized mobile home parks duly permitted and maintained pursuant to the provisions of this article.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to park, for use or occupancy, any mobile home or trailer as defined herein overnight on any street, alley, highway, or other public place within the city.
Sec. 24-33. - Placement of trailers on construction sites.
Contractors shall have the right to place trailers upon construction sites during the period of construction, but the same shall not be used for living quarters but shall be used for purposes limited only to the construction in progress on such site, and shall only be placed upon the premises upon which the construction is in progress.
Sec. 24-34. - General requirements.
(a)
Minimum size of mobile homes. No mobile home shall be placed in a planned mobile home park which has less than 500 square feet of floor space and does not contain a built-in bathroom with water closet, lavatory, and shower or tub which are in working condition.
(b)
Water, sewage, and electricity. Each mobile home space shall be provided with, and each mobile home shall be connected to, a central sanitary sewerage and water supply system approved by the county health officer. Electrical outlets shall be provided as specified by the city electrical code.
(c)
Lighting. All interior drives and walkways within the park shall be lighted at night with electrical lamps spaced at intervals of not more than 100 feet, or equivalent lighting as approved by the city.
(d)
Refuse collecting facilities. Individual, watertight, covered refuse containers of not less than ten gallons nor more than 20-gallon capacity shall be provided at each occupied mobile home space, and stands must be provided to hold the refuse containers upright or buried.
(e)
Recreation area. At least five percent of the gross land area within the mobile home park shall be set aside for recreation use. If a swimming pool is provided, the area of said swimming pool may be counted as part of the area allowed for recreational use, and such pool must be constructed in accordance with standards recommended by the state department of public health.
(Ord. No. 1974-01-14, § 13, 1-14-1974)
Sec. 24-35. - Development compliance.
At least 20 spaces of a planned mobile home park must be completely constructed according to the mobile home park plan approved by the mayor and council within two years after such approval by the council. The failure of a developer or applicant or owner of the land on which the mobile home park plan is approved by the mayor and council, to construct this minimum of 20 spaces within the specified period of time, shall be cause for the mayor and council to reconsider the approval heretofore granted for said planned mobile home park.
(Ord. No. 1974-01-14, § 15, 1-14-1974)
Sec. 24-36. - Legal provisions.
Violations and penalties. Any person violating any provision of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, and upon conviction, shall be punished for each offense by a fine according to the penalties described in the general provisions chapter of this Code. Each day such violation continues shall be deemed a separate offense. At the discretion of the mayor's court, a violator of this article may be given a reasonable length of time to rectify or correct the violation.
(Ord. No. 1974-01-14, § 16, 1-14-1974)
