Sec. 12-1. - Purpose of chapter.
The purpose of this chapter is to provide for coordination of information between affected county, city and state agencies, and to promote the health, safety and welfare of the people of the city.
(Ord. No. 1985-24, § I, 9-9-85)
Sec. 12-2. - Definition.
For purposes of this chapter, the term "mobile home" means a mobile or relocatable home as such terms are defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-5-440.
(Ord. No. 1985-24, § II, 9-9-85)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 12-3. - Penalty for violation of chapter.
Any person who shall do any act prohibited by this chapter or shall fail to do any act required by this chapter shall be fined not less than $25.00 nor more than $200.00 for each offense. Each day the violation continues shall constitute a separate offense and will be treated as such.
(Ord. No. 1985-24, § VIII, 9-9-85)
Sec. 12-4. - Applicability of zoning regulations.
The issuance of any permit under the provisions of this chapter shall be in no way construed as superseding the requirements for the location or relocation of mobile homes as required by state law and local ordinance. The issuance of a permit under the provisions of this chapter shall not prohibit the city planning and building department, or other administrative agencies, from requiring a relocation permit for the mobile home place where such mobile home is permitted by the regulations of a planning commission. In such case, if the move is required by a planning commission, the owner of such mobile home shall apply for and obtain a permit under this chapter for such relocation as if such relocation was the result of his own initiative.
(Ord. No. 1985-24, § III, 9-9-85)
Sec. 12-5. - Permit required.
(a)
No person shall own or have located within the city any mobile home unless application for a permit shall have been made and a permit received as provided by this chapter.
(b)
No person shall initially locate, relocate or remove any mobile home in the city without having made such application and having obtained such permit.
(Ord. No. 1985-24, § IV, 9-9-85)
Sec. 12-6. - Administrative fee.
There is hereby imposed an administrative fee to cover the administrative costs of implementing the provisions of this chapter, in the amount set forth in the schedule of fees and charges. Such fee shall be paid to the tax commissioner by the owner at the time that the mobile home decal is secured.
(Ord. No. 1985-24, § VI, 9-9-85)
Sec. 12-7. - Decal.
All persons owning a mobile home based in the city on January 1 of each year shall obtain from the tax commissioner a decal before April 1, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-5-493. This requirement includes all mobile homes, even if exempt from taxation because of homestead exemption or in accordance with state law. Owners of mobile homes locating in the city after January 1 of any year shall be required to report their mobile homes to the tax commissioner's office within ten working days. Such decals shall be affixed to the mobile homes in such a manner as to cause them to be easily visible for inspection from the street or drive.
(Ord. No. 1985-24, § VII, 9-9-85)
Sec. 12-8. - Reports by park owners and operators.
(a)
Every owner and operator of a mobile home park is required to give the tax commissioner an inventory of all mobile homes based in the park as of January 1 of each year. An updated lot map of each park showing any changes shall be furnished to the tax commissioner for this purpose. This shall be done no later than January 20 of each year. The tax office will make available an inventory of record as of January 1.
(b)
Every owner and operator of a mobile home park is required to report any mobile home removals from the park prior to the home being removed, if known to the park manager, operator or owner. Any mobile home added to the mobile home park shall be reported to the tax commissioner within ten working days of the mobile home arrival.
(Ord. No. 1985-24, § V, 9-9-85)
