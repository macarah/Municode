DIVISION 2. - LICENSES GENERALLY

Sec. 22-161. - Separate license required for each place of business.
For the purposes of this article, each branch establishment or location wherein a representative of the owner is employed and is authorized to transact business for such owner shall be deemed a separate place of business for which a separate license shall be required, provided that warehouses and distributing plants used in connection with and incidental to a business licensed under the provisions of this article shall not be deemed to be separate places of business or branch offices.
(Code 1988, § 12-42)
Sec. 22-162. - Multiple businesses at same location.
A person engaged in two or more businesses at the same location shall be required to obtain separate licenses for conducting each of such businesses for which a license is required.
(Code 1988, § 12-43)
Sec. 22-163. - Application.
(a)
Generally. Every person required to procure a license under the provisions of this article shall submit an application for such license to the city clerk, which application shall conform to the requirements of this section.
(b)
Form. Each application shall be a written statement upon forms provided by the city clerk.
(c)
Contents. Each application shall contain the following information:
(1)
The name and home address of the applicant if an individual, or the home office address if a corporation or partnership;
(2)
The place where the proposed business is to be located;
(3)
The kind of business to be carried on;
(4)
The name and home address of each partner, if a partnership;
(5)
The names and home addresses of the officers and directors, if a corporation;
(6)
A complete record of all arrests and convictions against the applicant and every partner, officer, or director of the applicant for violations of any laws and ordinances of the city, state, or federal government; and
(7)
Such additional information which the city clerk or council may find reasonably necessary to the fair administration of this article.
(d)
Verification. Each application shall be sworn to by the applicant if an individual, or by a partner if a partnership, or by an officer if a corporation.
(e)
Confidentiality. All information furnished or secured under the authority of this section shall be kept in strict confidence of the city clerk, shall not be subject to public inspection, and shall be utilized solely by the officers of the city responsible for administering the provisions of this article.
(f)
False statements. False statements on any application for a license shall be grounds for denial or immediate revocation of such license.
(Code 1988, § 12-30)
State Law reference— Confidentiality of information, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-15.
Sec. 22-164. - Minimum age requirement for certain businesses.
No license shall be issued to any individual for the operation of coin-operated amusement devices, billiard and pool rooms, or pawnbroker businesses unless such individual is at least 21 years of age.
(Code 1988, § 12-31)
Sec. 22-165. - Payment of fee.
(a)
Time of payment; proration of fee. Each application for a license under this article shall be accompanied by the amount of the fee chargeable for such license, such amount to be prorated by quarters to the end of the fiscal year.
(b)
Receipt. The city clerk shall issue a receipt to the applicant for the amount of the fee tendered with the application for a license, provided that such receipt shall not be construed as approval of the application, nor shall it entitle or authorize the applicant to open or maintain any business contrary to the provisions of this article.
(c)
Refund upon disapproval of application. Upon the disapproval of any application for which a fee has been submitted under the provisions of this article, the city clerk shall refund such fee, provided that the applicant is not otherwise indebted to the city.
(Code 1988, § 12-32)
Sec. 22-166. - Issuance.
(a)
Procedure.
(1)
Review by police chief. If any provision of this article provides for the review of an application for a license by the police chief, the city clerk shall forward a copy of the application to the police chief within 48 hours of the time of the receipt of the application. The police chief shall make a recommendation thereon, favorable or otherwise, and shall return such recommendation to the city clerk within seven days after receiving a copy of the application.
(2)
Consideration and action by council. Upon the receipt of the recommendation of the police chief as provided in subsection (a)(1) of this section, or upon the receipt of the application if no reviewing officer is designated, the city clerk shall forward such recommendation and/or application to the governing body for consideration and action at its next regular scheduled public meeting.
(3)
Compliance with building and construction codes. No license shall be issued to any applicant whose place of business is not in full compliance with all building and construction codes adopted by the city.
(4)
Issuance. Upon the express approval of the governing body, the city clerk shall issue a business license to the applicant therefor, which license shall state the nature of the business authorized and bear the date of issuance and the signatures of the mayor and city clerk.
(5)
License deemed grant of privilege. The granting of a business license under the provisions of this article shall be deemed a privilege only, and nothing contained in this chapter shall be construed as granting to any person whose business is subject to municipal regulation any legal right to engage in such business.
(b)
Standards for issuance. The governing body, in passing upon an application for issuance of a license for the conduct of any of the businesses regulated by this article, may take into account the following factors in deciding whether to grant or deny such application:
(1)
The proximity of other businesses of like kind to the proposed location.
(2)
The proximity of an establishment licensed to sell malt beverages, wine or other alcoholic beverages to the proposed location.
(3)
The character of the neighborhood immediately adjacent to the proposed location.
(4)
The proximity of churches, schools, and playgrounds to the proposed location.
(5)
Whether the proposed location has adequate off-street parking facilities or other parking available for its patrons.
(6)
Whether the location would tend to increase and promote traffic congestion and resulting hazards therefrom.
(7)
The feelings and attitudes of the citizens residing in the area adjacent to the proposed location.
(8)
The information set forth in the application.
(9)
Whether any similar license previously issued for such location was ever revoked for cause by the governing body.
(10)
Whether the applicant has ever been convicted of or pled guilty to a crime involving moral turpitude.
(11)
Whether the business at the proposed location contributes to the public good or convenience.
(12)
The moral character of the applicant.
(Code 1988, §§ 12-33, 12-34)
Sec. 22-167. - Inspections and investigations.
(a)
Right of entry. Whenever inspections of the premises used for or in connection with the operation of a licensed business or occupation are provided for or required by ordinance, or are reasonably necessary to secure compliance with any ordinance provision or to detect violations thereof, it shall be the duty of the licensee, or the person in charge of the premises to be inspected, to admit thereto for the purpose of making the inspection any officer or employee of the city who is authorized or directed to make such inspection, at any reasonable time that admission is requested.
(b)
Refusal to allow inspection. In addition to any other penalty which may be provided, the mayor may revoke the license of any licensed proprietor of a licensed business in the city who refuses to permit any officer or employee who is authorized to make such inspection or take such sample to make the inspection or take an adequate sample of the commodity, or who interferes with such officer or employee while in the performance of such duty in making such inspection; provided that no license shall be revoked for such cause unless written demand is made upon the licensee or person in charge of the premises, in the name of the city, stating that such inspection or sample is desired, at the time it is sought to make the inspection or obtain the sample.
(c)
Procedure for investigation or inspection prior to issuance of license. Upon the receipt of an application for a license or permit where ordinances of the city require an inspection or investigation before the issuance of such permit or license, the city clerk shall refer such application to the proper officer for making such investigation within 48 hours of the time of such receipt. The officer charged with the duty of making the investigation or inspection shall make a report thereon, favorable or otherwise, within ten days after receiving the application or a copy thereof. The city clerk will ask the county health department to make an inspection in regard to such licenses in connection with the care and handling of food and the preventing of nuisances and spread of disease, for the protection of health. The building inspector shall make or cause to be made any such inspections relative to the construction of buildings or other structures. All other investigations, except where otherwise provided, shall be made by the chief of police or by some other officer designated by the mayor.
(Code 1988, § 12-36)
Sec. 22-168. - Display.
It shall be the duty of any person conducting a business licensed under this article to keep such person's license posted in a conspicuous place on the premises used for such business at all times.
(Code 1988, § 12-35)
Sec. 22-169. - Change of location of business.
In the absence of any provision to the contrary, the location of any licensed business or occupation may be changed, provided ten days' notice thereof is given to the city clerk, and provided that all building and zoning requirements are complied with.
(Code 1988, § 12-39)
Sec. 22-170. - Transfer.
All licenses issued under this article shall be personal to the licensee to whom issued, but in cases where the ownership is changed and both the name and location of the licensed business or occupation are maintained, the governing body may allow the license to be transferred.
(Code 1988, § 12-40)
Sec. 22-171. - Expiration and renewal.
(a)
All annual licenses issued under this article shall terminate on the last day of the calendar year when no provision to the contrary is made.
(b)
Each licensee shall make a written application for renewal on forms supplied by the city clerk on or before November 15 of each calendar year, which application shall contain substantially the same information as the initial application and be accompanied by all required fees.
(c)
Any applicant for renewal of a license shall be entitled to a refund of fees tendered if he withdraws his application for renewal prior to final action on the license by the governing body.
(Code 1988, § 12-37)
Sec. 22-172. - Duplicate licenses.
A duplicate license shall be issued by the city clerk to replace a license previously issued under this article which has been lost, stolen, defaced, or destroyed without any willful conduct on the part of the licensee, upon the filing of a sworn affidavit attesting to such fact. No fee shall be charged for issuance of a duplicate license.
(Code 1988, § 12-41)
Sec. 22-173. - Suspension, revocation, probation, or placement of conditions on license.
The governing body, after affording the licensee notice of the charges and an opportunity to be heard with respect to any revocation proceedings, may, if it finds that this article or any city ordinance or state or federal law with respect to the operation of the business or the licensee's competence or qualifications to operate the business has been violated by the licensee or the licensee's agent or employee, revoke such license in its entirety, suspend the license for a specified period of time, place the licensee on probation or place other conditions thereon as it may deem necessary.
(Code 1988, § 12-38)
