ARTICLE II. - CITY CEMETERIES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 26-31. - Applicability; enforcement; right of council to alter regulations in case of hardship.
(a)
The provisions of this article apply to all cemeteries owned, operated or maintained by the city.
(b)
The rules and regulations in this article shall be interpreted, applied and enforced by the public works director or city administrator. In cases of hardship, necessity or unreasonableness in the application or enforcement of any of these rules and regulations, the majority of the city council shall have the right to alter the application and enforcement of these rules and regulations so as to relieve the hardship or unreasonableness, and such action shall not constitute forfeiture nor shall it have the effect of changing or abridging the rule, covenant or regulation.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-59), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-32. - Cemetery trust perpetual care fund.
The mayor and council shall have the authority to amend the portion of the cemetery grave space price to be transferred to the cemetery trust perpetual care fund. The perpetual care fund shall be governed by an escrow agent appointed by the mayor and council, and the interest will be returned to the city at least annually for the maintenance of municipal cemeteries owned by the city.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-58), 3-10-1997)
State Law reference— Municipality as trustee of cemetery funds, O.C.G.A. § 36-37-5.
Sec. 26-33. - Authority and duties of cemetery director.
(a)
The cemetery director shall be authorized to locate and oversee opening/closing of all grave spaces by prequalified contractors approved by the city administrator. A list of these contractors is on file at the director's office. The cemetery director shall oversee the maintenance and care of the cemeteries, and shall approve and locate the space for erecting all headstones and footstones. The cemetery director shall maintain maps and records identical to city hall and report such sales to city hall as directed by the city administrator. All monies for grave space sales are paid by the customer at city hall. Customers are given a paid receipt during normal business hours.
(b)
Funeral homes are responsible for the opening and closing of graves. The city reserves the right to restrict funeral homes that are fraudulent from entering city cemeteries. The cemetery director reserves the right to remove or adjust monuments, footmarkers, etc., that are improperly installed.
(Code 1988, § 6-2; Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-2), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-34. - Limitations on municipal liability, liability for third parties and businesses performing funeral services, interment or installation of monuments or other structures.
(a)
The city shall not be liable for any loss or damage from causes beyond its reasonable control, and especially from damage caused by force majeure, common enemy, thieves, vandals, strikers, malicious mischief makers, explosions, unavoidable accidents, invasions, insurrections, riots or order of any military or civil authority, whether the damage be direct or collateral, and monuments or foot stones that are improperly installed by contractors.
(b)
All third party persons or businesses in charge of any funeral service or interment, such as duly licensed funeral directors or funeral home owners, contractors, heirs, legal assignees, private individuals, conducted within any municipally owned cemetery will be held liable for all cemetery damages, including but not limited to damage to stone walls, street/road surfaces, gates or iron work, general lawns and monuments, caused by the conduction of any funeral service or interment, regardless if said damage was caused by themselves, their contractors, or any other participants brought into the cemetery for the purpose of any funeral service or interment under their supervision, either directly or indirectly, as a funeral home director or funeral home owner.
An acknowledgment of responsibility will be signed by the funeral home director or funeral home owner and kept on file in the city clerk's office and shall be legally binding for all funeral services or internments conducted by the funeral home director or funeral home owner or their employees. The acknowledgment shall remain in effect and enforceable until rights of interment are relinquished in writing in any Calhoun city cemeteries.
(c)
Any third party installer of a monument shall be liable to the cemetery, to its customers, and to third persons for damages to their respective property and for other damages arising due to the negligence or intentional act of such installer, which liability may not be waived by contract.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-40), 3-10-1997; Ord. No. 1008 , § 1, 3-9-2020)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 1008 , § 1, adopted Mar. 9, 2020, changed the title of § 26-34 from city not responsible for damage from certain causes to limitations on municipal liability, liability for third parties and businesses performing funeral services, interment or installation of monuments or other structures.
Sec. 26-35. - Visitation hours.
City cemeteries shall be open for visitation during the hours from one-half hour after sunup to one-half hour before sundown.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-54), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-36. - Firearms.
No firearms shall be permitted within any city cemetery except with special permit from the city or other duly constituted authorities.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-53), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-37. - Alcoholic beverages.
No person shall be permitted to have alcoholic beverages within any cemetery except as authorized by the cemetery director.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-55), 3-10-1997)
Cross reference— Alcoholic beverages, ch. 6.
Sec. 26-38. - Walking or driving on lots or grave spaces.
Persons within the cemetery grounds shall use only the avenues, walks, alleys and roads and shall not walk, drive or ride upon the lots, and grave spaces.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-56), 3-10-1997)
Cross reference— Traffic, ch. 90.
Sec. 26-39. - Vehicles.
Automobiles shall not be driven through the grounds at a greater speed than ten miles per hour, and must always be kept on the righthand side of the cemetery roadway. Automobiles are not allowed to park or to come to a full stop in front of an open grave unless such automobiles are in attendance at the funeral. No off-road recreational vehicles shall be allowed in city cemeteries.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-57), 3-10-1997)
Cross reference— Traffic, ch. 90.
Sec. 26-40. - Gathering flowers; damaging trees or other plants; feeding or disturbing birds or animals.
All persons are prohibited from gathering flowers, either wild or cultivated, or breaking trees, shrubbery or plants, or feeding or disturbing the birds or other animal life, in the cemeteries of the city.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-37), 3-10-1997)
Cross reference— Animals, ch. 14.
Sec. 26-41. - Monument contractors and grave contractors; work hours; standards for monuments.
(a)
All monument contractors and grave contractors must hold a valid license from the city and an installation approval from the cemetery director.
(b)
The permitted working hours for grave contractors are as follows except as otherwise permitted by the city administrator: Monday through Sunday, daylight until dark.
(c)
The permitted work hours for monument contractors are Monday through Friday, 8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.
(d)
All work must be completed by 5:00 p.m. on the last working day before the following holidays: Thanksgiving and Christmas.
(e)
No excavation work can be instituted that cannot be completed by Friday at 5:00 p.m. before holidays. Once excavation work begins, the contractor must pursue installation diligently until completed. When any questionable work results, the cemetery director is empowered to temporarily stop all work until the matter is resolved to the satisfaction of the city.
(f)
All monuments must be of solid material base and die, marble or granite. Flush markers can be bronze. No other material is permitted. All monuments must have a concrete or cement foundation, not less than four inches thick, of adequate strength and construction. All monuments and markers shall be in accordance with the regulations on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-29), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-42. - Monuments to be set at ground level in certain lots.
Monuments of any kind shall be set level with the ground (flush) within cemetery lots in the southern addition to Chandler Cemetery and further designated lot nos. 848, 849, 850, 851, 852, 853, 919, 918, 917, 916, 915, 914, 920, 921, 922, 923, 924, 925, 991, 990, 989, 988, 987, 986, 992, 993, 994, 995, 996, 997, 1063, 1062, 1061, 1060, 1059, 1058, 1064, 1065, 1066, 1067, 1068, 1069, 1135, 1134, 1133, 1132, 1131, 1130, 1136, 1137, 1138, 1139, 1140, 1141, 1207, 1206, 1205, 1204, 1203, 1202, 1208, 1209, 1210, 1211, 1212, 1213, 1279, 1278, 1277, 1276, 1275, 1274, 1280, 1281, 1282, 1283, 1284, 1285, 1334, 1333, 1332, 1331, 1330, and 1329.

Sec. 26-43. - Raising or depressing surface of lots or graves.
The owners of cemetery lots are prohibited from raising or depressing the surface of the lot or grave above or below the surrounding ground.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-31), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-44. - General authority of city regarding improvements, care of lots, interments and other work.
All grading, landscape work and improvements of any kind and all care on lots shall be done, and all trees and shrubs and herbage of any kind shall be planted, trimmed, cut or removed, and all openings and closings of lots and all interments, disinterments and removals shall be made, only when permitted by the city.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-32), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-45. - Right of city to remove or change improvements or alterations on lots.
All improvements or alterations of individual property in the cemetery shall be under the direction of and subject to the consent, satisfaction and approval of the city. Should they be made without its written consent, the city shall have the right to remove, alter or change such improvements or alterations at the expense of the lot or grave space owner, or in any event at any time when, in its judgment, they become unsightly to the eye.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-33), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-46. - Right of city to remove offensive, improper or injurious monuments, structures or objects.
If any monument or other structure, or any inscription, be placed in or upon any lot or grave space which shall be determined by the cemetery director to be offensive, improper or injurious to the appearance of the surrounding lots or grounds or the cemetery generally, he shall have the right and it shall be his duty to enter upon such lot and cause the removal of such offensive or improper object.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-30), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-47. - Pruning and removal of trees and shrubs.
If any tree or shrub, situated in any lot, shall, by means of its roots or branches, become detrimental in any way to the adjoining lot, avenue or walk, it shall be the duty and privilege of the city, and it hereby reserves the right, to enter upon the lot and remove the trees or shrubs, or any part thereof. No tree growing in any lot or border shall be pruned or removed without the consent of the city.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-34), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-48. - Removal of flowers, floral designs, trees, shrubs or other plants.
(a)
Right of city to prevent removal. The city reserves the right to prevent the removal of any flowers, floral designs, trees, shrubs or plants or herbage of any kind from the cemetery. No flowers will be removed from any grave space without permission of the cemetery director.
(b)
Removal by city. The city shall have authority to remove all floral designs, flowers, weeds, trees, shrubs, plants or herbage of any kind from the cemetery, when, in the judgment of the cemetery director, they become unsightly, dangerous, detrimental or diseased. The city shall not be liable for damage to floral pieces, baskets or frames in which or to which such floral pieces are attached.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-35, 6-36), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-49. - Restrictions on flowers and floral arrangements; disposal of floral frames.
(a)
Only one floral arrangement shall be placed on the monument with a saddle clip. If no monument is in place, a shepherd's hook is required, and the flowers must be in a plastic container hung on the shepherd's hook that does not touch the ground. Any flowers which are determined by the cemetery director to be outside of the appropriate area shall be removed by the city.
(b)
All floral arrangements are to be placed in clay or plastic containers. Glass, tin, wire and cement containers are prohibited. Any other type container must be approved by the cemetery director.
(c)
The cemetery director is authorized to remove funeral flowers after 72 hours unless permission to remain longer is given by the city due to unusual circumstances.
(d)
Floral frames, when removed from the lot, unless called for within ten days by those lawfully entitled to them, shall be disposed of by the city.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-38), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-50. - Signs, notices or advertisements.
No signs, notices or advertisements of any kind shall be allowed in any cemetery, except by the city.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-39), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-51. - Burial of animals.
Burial in municipal cemeteries of the city shall be restricted to human remains and the burial of any animal or fowl remains shall not be permitted.
(Code 1988, § 6-4; Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-4), 3-10-1997)

Subdivision III. - Vested Rights of Interment

Sec. 26-111. - Family plots inalienable; right of interment generally; reversion of lot to city.
(a)
Reversion of lot to city. Whenever an interment of the remains of a member or of a relative of a member of the family of the record owner, or of the remains of the record owner, is made in a lot transferred by deed or certificate of ownership to an individual owner and the owner dies without making disposition of the lot either in his will by a specific device or by a written declaration filed and recorded in the office of the city clerk or clerk of superior court, the lot thereby becomes inalienable and shall be held as the family lot of the owner, with the lot reverting to the city for protection of those who may be interred therein.
(b)
Right of interment generally. In a family lot, one grave, niche or crypt may be used for the owner's interment, and one for the owner's surviving spouse, if any, who by law has a right of interment in it. In those remaining, if any, the parents and children of the deceased owner, in order of death, may be interred without the consent of any person claiming interest in the plot.
(c)
Order of right of interment. If no parent or child survives, the right of interment goes in the order of death, first, to the spouse of any child of the record owner, and second, in the order of death, to the next heir at law of the owner or the spouse of any heir at law.
(d)
Waiver of interment right in favor of other relative. Any surviving spouse, parent, child or heir who has a right of interment in a family plot may waive such right in favor of any other relative, or spouse of a relative, of either the deceased owner or of his spouse, and upon such waiver the remains of the person in whose favor the waiver is made may be interred in the lot.
(e)
Interment right of owner's spouse. The spouse of any owner of any plot which contains more than one interment space has a vested right of interment of his remains in the lot or grave space, and any person thereafter becoming the spouse of the owner has a vested right of interment of his remains in the lot if more than one interment space is unoccupied at the time the person becomes the spouse of the owner.
(f)
Termination of vested right of spouse. No conveyance or other action of the owner without the written consent or joinder of the spouse of the owner divests the spouse of a vested right of interment, except that a final decree of divorce between them terminates the vested right of interment unless otherwise provided in the decree.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-17—6-22), 3-10-1997; Ord. No. 1027 , § 6, 9-27-2021)
Sec. 26-112. - Designation of representative when lot has several owners.
When there are several owners of the rights of interment in a lot or space, they may designate one or more persons to represent the lot or grave space and file written notice of designation with the city. In the absence of such notice of written objection to its so doing, the city is not liable to any owner for interring or permitting any interment or refusing an interment in the lot or grave space upon the request or direction of any co-owners of the lot or grave space.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-23), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-113. - Waiver of vested right.
A vested right of interment may be waived, and is so waived and terminated upon the interment elsewhere of the remains of the person in whose vested.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-24), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-114. - Limitations on scope of vested right.
No vested right of interment gives to any person the right to have his remains interred in any interment space in which the remains of any deceased having prior vested right of interment have been interred, nor does it give any person the right to have the remains of more than one deceased person interred in a single interment space.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-25), 3-10-1997)

Subdivision IV. - Grave Opening and Closing; Interments and Disinterments

Sec. 26-131. - Responsibility for digging graves and moving dirt.
Funeral homes shall be responsible for digging graves and moving dirt in city-owned cemeteries.
(Code 1988, § 6-3)
Sec. 26-132. - Weekend interments.
(a)
Weekend interments in cemeteries owned and operated by the city will not be accepted after 4:00 p.m. Eastern Standard Time or daylight saving time as is effective on Saturdays and Sundays.
(b)
The 4:00 p.m. time limit applies to any portion of the interment held on cemetery grounds.
(c)
In emergency cases, interments later than 4:00 p.m. may be held for an additional $50.00 fee. Emergency cases should be scheduled with the cemetery director.
(Code 1988, § 6-5; Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-5), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-133. - Interments on holidays.
There will be no interments on the following holidays in cemeteries owned and operated by the city: Christmas and Thanksgiving.
(Code 1988, § 6-6; Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-6), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-134. - Authorization to open lot.
The city may supervise the opening of a lot for any purpose on proper written authorization by any lot owner of record made out on forms approved by the city and duly filed in the office of the city clerk, unless there are written instructions to the contrary on file in such office.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-26), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-135. - Time, manner and supervision of interments and disinterments.
(a)
All interments, disinterments and removals must be made at the time and as permitted by the city.
(b)
Only city employees will supervise the opening or closing of graves. The cemetery director reserves the right to refuse to permit opening or closing of a grave of questionable ownership, with the approval of the city administrator or director of public works.
(c)
All state and local health laws must be complied with prior to any interment in any city cemetery.
(d)
All interments must be inside containers of minimum standard as approved by the cemetery director (minimum standard is pine construction or equal).
(e)
No interment will be made in any city cemetery before payment of the fees specified in the regulations in the office of the city clerk, unless credit has been extended by the city clerk.
(f)
No interment will be made in any city cemetery until the owner of the burial lot shall execute an affidavit directing interment of the remains of the deceased in the burial lot.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-41), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-136. - Right of city to postpone interment until more expedient time.
The city may refuse to make an interment until a more expedient time if the remains arrive at the cemetery entrance after 5:00 p.m., or if too many funerals arrive at the same hour.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-42), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-137. - Delay of interment due to protest or noncompliance with rules.
The city shall be in no way liable for any delay in the interment of a body where a protest to the interment has been made, or where the rules and regulations in this article have not been complied with, and, further, the city reserves the right, under such circumstances, to place the body in a funeral home or a receiving vault until the full rights have been determined. The city may require any protest to be in writing and to be filed in the office of the city clerk.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-43), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-138. - Notification of city prior to interment.
The right is reserved by the city to insist upon at least 24 hours' notice before any interment. Interment requests with less than 24 hours' notice may be refused. All requests with less than 12 hours' notice shall be refused.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-44), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-139. - City not liable for damages due to error in interment permit or mistake in identity.
The city shall not be liable in damages for an error in the interment permit or for mistake or error in the identity of the person sought to be interred or cremated.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-45), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-140. - City not responsible for orders given by telephone or mistakes caused by improper instructions.
The city shall not be held responsible for any order given by telephone, or for any mistake occurring from the lack of precise and proper instructions as to the particular space, size and location in a lot where interment is desired.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-46), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-141. - Procedure when instructions from lot owner not available.
When instructions from the lot owner regarding the location of an interment space in a lot cannot be obtained, or are indefinite, or when for any reason the interment space cannot be opened when specified, the city may, in its discretion, authorize opening of it in such location in the lot as it deems best and proper so as not to delay the funeral, and the city shall not be liable in damages for any judgment so made.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-47), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-142. - Right of city to remove and reinter remains in case of error.
If an error shall be committed in the interment of the remains of any person, the city reserves the right to authorize the removal of the remains and reinter the remains in such property of equal value and similar location as may be substituted and conveyed in lieu of the mistaken property.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-48), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-143. - Permission required for disinterment.
No disinterment from any lot shall be made except on express permission of the city administrator, city clerk or cemetery director of the city first obtained. The city clerk may insist that an order from a proper court of law first be secured by those seeking the disinterment.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-49), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-144. - City not liable for damages incurred in making disinterment.
The city shall exercise due care in permitting a disinterment and removal, but it shall assume no liability for damage to any casket, burial case, vault or urn incurred in making the disinterment or removal.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-50), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-145. - Restrictions on disinterment.
Removal of a body or cremated remains by the heirs of the deceased so that the lot or grave space may be sold for profit to themselves, or removal contrary to the expressed or implied wish of the original lot owner, or if repugnant to the ordinary sense of decency, is absolutely forbidden.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-51), 3-10-1997)
Sec. 26-146. - Limitation on number of bodies interred in same grave, vault, crypt or niche.
Not more than one body, or the remains of more than one body, shall be interred in any one grave, vault, crypt or niche, without authorization of the city.
(Ord. No. 607A, § 1(6-52), 3-10-1997)
