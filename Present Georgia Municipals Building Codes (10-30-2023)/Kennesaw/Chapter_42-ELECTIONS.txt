Sec. 42-1. - Authority for county to conduct municipal elections.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 21-2-45(c), the city does hereby authorize the county to conduct elections held pursuant to the Georgia Municipal Election Code (O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.) and requests the county to perform the following functions:
(1)
The county shall perform all duties as superintendent of elections as specified under chapter three, title 21 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated (O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.) with the exception of the qualification of candidates.
(2)
The city does hereby establish and authorize the payment for such services by the county under subsection (1) of this section from the city treasury.
(Ord. of 6-21-93, § 3; Ord. No. 2007-14, 4-2-07)
Sec. 42-2. - Election officials.
The mayor and council shall by resolution either appoint the county to perform all duties as superintendent of elections pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 21-2-45(c)(1) or appoint a municipal election superintendent, election managers, registrars, absentee ballot clerk and any other officials as are necessary, all of whom shall exercise those powers and duties set forth in the Georgia Municipal Election Code (O.C.G.A. § 21-2-1 et seq.).
(Code 1986, § 2-2-1; Ord. No. 2007-14, 4-2-07)
State Law reference— Manner of selecting municipal superintendent, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-70.1.
Sec. 42-3. - Voter registration deadline.
The city hereby elects to use the county registration list for the registration of electors. Persons desiring to vote in city elections shall register with the county registrar no later than 5:00 p.m. on the day 30 days prior to the date of the election in the case of a general election or five days prior to the date of a special election.
(Code 1986, § 2-2-2; Ord. No. 2007-14, 4-2-07)
Sec. 42-4. - Notice of candidacy; qualifications.
(a)
Except as otherwise provided by state law, all candidates for municipal office shall file their notice of candidacy and pay the prescribed qualifying fee by the date prescribed in section 42-5 in order to be eligible to have their names placed on the election ballot by the election superintendent in the following manner:
Each candidate for municipal office or a designee shall file a notice of candidacy in the office of the election superintendent during the city's qualifying period. The city council shall designate the days of the qualifying period, which shall be no less than three days and no more than five days. The days of the qualifying period shall be consecutive days. Qualifying periods shall commence no earlier than 8:30 a.m. on the last Monday in August immediately preceding the general election and shall end no later than 4:30 p.m. on the following Friday; and, in the case of a special election, the municipal qualifying period shall commence no earlier than the date of the call and shall end no later than 25 days prior to the election.
(b)
The hours of qualifying each day shall be from 8:30 a.m. until 4:30 p.m. with one hour allowed for the lunch break; provided, however, that should the city have normal business hours which cover a lesser period of time it shall conduct qualifying during its normal business hours. Except in the case of a special election, notice of the opening and closing dates and the hours for candidates to qualify shall be published at least two weeks prior to the opening of the qualifying period.
(c)
The residency requirements for a candidate for any municipal office, except offices of a judicial nature, shall be 12 months residency within the municipality; provided, however, that municipalities may by charter provide for lesser residency requirements for candidates for municipal office.
(Code 1986, § 2-2-3; Ord. No. 95-15, § 1, 8-7-95; Ord. of 9-2-97; Ord. No. 2007-14, 4-2-07)
State Law reference— Filing notice of candidacy for office, O.C.G.A. § 21-2-132(d)(3).
Sec. 42-5. - Candidate qualification fee.
Any citizen who desires to be a candidate for mayor or councilmember shall qualify as required in section 42-4 and pay at that time such qualification fee as shall be established by the mayor and council, which fee shall not exceed three percent of the annual compensation of the office.
(Code 1986, § 2-2-4; Ord. No. 2007-14, 4-2-07)
