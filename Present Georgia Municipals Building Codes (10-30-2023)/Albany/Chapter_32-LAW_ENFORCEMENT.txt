ARTICLE II. - POLICE DEPARTMENT
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 32-19. - Chain of command.
(a)
The chief of police is the ranking officer of the police department and is vested with authority over all personnel within the department, both sworn officers and civilian personnel. In such capacity the chief of police may delegate any of the chief of police's responsibilities to any of his or her subordinates. Should the chief of police be off duty and if in the opinion of the ranking officer at the police station, a decision requiring the personal attention of the chief of police is necessary, then the chief should always be consulted.
(b)
The deputy chief of police is second in command of the police department and is directly charged with the operation of the department in the absence of the chief of police. In such absence the deputy chief of police assumes all of the duties and authority of the chief and at all times shall assist the chief police in the performance of his or her duties. The deputy chief of police shall take the same oath and give the same bond as prescribed for the chief of police. In case of sickness of the chief of police or in the chief of police's absence from the city the deputy chief of police shall act in the chief of police's place and all duties imposed by the Code upon the chief of police are hereby imposed upon the deputy chief of police when acting for the chief.
(c)
In the absence or unavailability of the chief of police, then in order of seniority, the deputy chiefs of police are authorized to assume the responsibilities and duties imposed by law on the chief of police.
(Code 1975, § 22-39; Code 1985, § 21-16; Ord. No. 10-105, § 2, 1-26-2010)
Sec. 32-20. - Bonds.
(a)
The chief of police of the city shall execute a bond and all of the remaining police officers of the police department shall execute bonds conditioned as provided in section 2-131. The amounts of the bonds shall be established by ordinance or resolution of the board of city commissioners, from time to time, and made available for public examination in the office of the city clerk.
(b)
Unsworn civilian employees of the department shall come under the provisions of section 2-131.
(Code 1975, § 22-2; Code 1985, § 21-17)
Sec. 32-21. - Duties.
(a)
All members of the police force, shall, perform all duties imposed upon them by law.
(b)
It shall be the duty of every police officer to guard the city by day and by night, to enforce obedience to all laws and ordinances of the city, to preserve peace and order at all hours of the day or night. The time and place of their duty shall be designated by the chief of police subject to the control of the city manager.
(c)
All members of the police force are hereby empowered, authorized, directed and instructed to police Chehaw Park, and to enforce thereon all of the laws of the state, and all of the laws and ordinances generally of the city, and all of the ordinances, rules and regulations which specifically pertain to Chehaw Park, as fully as if Chehaw Park were within the corporate limits of the city. Police officers shall have the power in the park to make arrests and to issue and serve summons and citations for the violation of the laws, ordinances, rules and regulations, returnable to the courts generally of the state or to the municipal court judge of the city, as the case may be.
(Code 1975, § 22-4; Code 1985, § 21-18)
Sec. 32-22. - Arms and uniforms—Kind; style.
The kind and style of uniform and arms shall be determined by the chief of police. Each officer and member of the force shall give a receipt for his or her equipment, which shall be charged to him or her and, if unaccounted for when retiring from office, the value thereof shall be recovered on his or her bond.
(Code 1975, § 22-5; Code 1985, § 21-19; Ord. No. 89-183, § 1, 12-27-1989)
Sec. 32-23. - Same—Property of city.
The uniforms and equipment furnished to the police force by the city shall remain the property of the city and shall be, when a change is ordered or on resignation, termination or retirement from office, returned to the city. The members of the force shall be liable on their bonds for any loss or careless destruction of or damage to their uniforms or equipment.
(Code 1975, § 22-6; Code 1985, § 21-20)
Sec. 32-24. - Arrest by police; appearance bonds.
All police officers shall arrest offenders against the ordinances of the city and bring or have them before the court, the recorder or other person acting in his or her stead, as early as practicable. Such police officer may, in his or her discretion and according to the exigencies of the case, hold such offender until proper bond be given for such offender's appearance for trial or serve written summons, as provided by law, upon such offender, requiring such offender to be and appear at the proper session of the police court in conformity with the schedule as laid down by the municipal court judge.
(Code 1975, § 22-7; Code 1985, § 21-21)

DIVISION 2. - CHIEF OF POLICE

Sec. 32-53. - Appointment; oath.
The chief of police shall be appointed in accordance with the provisions of Charter section 3(i). Before qualifying and entering upon the discharge of his or her duties, the chief of police shall make and subscribe before some officer authorized to administer the same, the following oath, and a record shall be made of same:
I, _______, do solemnly swear that I will faithfully and impartially discharge the duties imposed upon me as chief of police of the city.
(Code 1975, § 22-21; Code 1985, § 21-32)
Sec. 32-54. - Duties generally.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the chief of police to attend all meetings of the board of city commissioners and all trials before the police court. It shall be the chief of police's duty, as well as members of the police force, to execute all warrants, serve all summonses, subpoenas, citations, notifications or rules placed in his or her hands by the mayor, the board of city commissioners or city clerk, in accordance with the requirements of the city ordinances. The chief of police shall collect all fines and costs imposed by the mayor or the board of city commissioners and police court.
(b)
It shall be the duty of the chief of police at all times of the day or night to preserve the public peace, prevent the commission of crime and arrest offenders, to protect the rights of persons and property, to regard the public health, to provide proper police at fires, to protect the firefighters and property thereat, to protect strangers and travelers at railway stations, to enforce obedience to all ordinances of the board of city commissioners which are applicable to police or health, to attend all meetings of the board of city commissioners and to see that the ordinances, rules and regulations of the same are carried into effect and to discharge such other duties as may be required of him or her by ordinances.
(Code 1975, § 22-31; Code 1985, § 21-34)
Sec. 32-55. - Chief executive officer; responsible for good order of city and conduct of police force.
(a)
The chief of police shall be the chief executive officer of the police department, but shall always be subject to the orders and regulations of the city manager and it shall be the chief of police's duty to promulgate the same. It shall be the duty of the police force to respect and obey all orders of the chief of police, not in conflict with the law or ordinances of the city.
(b)
The chief of police shall be held responsible for the good order of the city and the general conduct of the officers and employees of the police force. It is enjoined on the chief of police to pay frequent visits to various portions of the city at uncertain times and thus be able to supervise the conduct of officers and employees.
(c)
The chief of police shall hold his or her deputy chief, officers and civilian employees of his or her departments strictly responsible for the proper discharge of their duties. The chief of police shall be responsible for the proper care of the police station, apparatus and property of the city belonging to the department. The chief of police shall have the authority to make recommendations for promotions of members in the department subject to the approval of the city manager. The chief of police shall be held accountable for the organization and operation of the police department and is responsible for its training, acquisition of equipment, and overall performance of duty. The chief of police shall have the responsibility of preparing a budget for the police department and submitting same to the city manager on an annual basis.
(d)
The chief of police shall be authorized to establish rules and regulations governing the conduct and activities in operation of the police department including the delineation of beats. The chief of police shall furnish a copy of such rules and regulations to the city manager. The chief of police shall be empowered by the city manager to authorize overtime for members of the police department when pressure of police business so dictates.
(Code 1975, § 22-32; Code 1985, § 21-34)
Sec. 32-56. - Arrests.
It shall be the duty of the chief of police, for any violation of the ordinances of the city, to arrest or see to the arrest of all persons so offending and hold them in custody until a trial is had in the manner prescribed for the trial of offenders. Any such person may be discharged from arrest upon such person's giving bond for his or her appearance before the municipal court.
(Code 1975, § 22-34; Code 1985, § 21-35)
State Law reference— Arrest of persons generally, O.C.G.A. § 17-4-1 et seq.
Sec. 32-57. - Fire alarms.
Upon the alarm of fire in the city, the chief of police or shift commander upon request from the fire department shall respond forthwith to the fire, to give assistance as the fire chief may require.
(Code 1975, § 22-35; Code 1985, § 21-36; Ord. No. 02-116, § 1, 5-28-2002)
Sec. 32-58. - Riots; enlisting aid, etc.
It shall be the duty of the chief of police to suppress all riots or public outbreaks within the city or its police jurisdiction. For this purpose the chief of police shall be and is hereby empowered with authority to call upon any or all citizens capable of bearing arms, to aid in suppressing such riots or outbreaks, and, upon their neglecting or refusing to render such aid, the chief of police shall report their names to the mayor or recorder.
(Code 1975, § 22-37; Code 1985, § 21-37)
State Law reference— Immunity of persons assisting law enforcement officers, O.C.G.A. § 16-3-22.
Sec. 32-59. - Prisoners, property, etc.
The chief of police shall take proper care of and be accountable for all city property that shall be put in his or her charge and report to the city manager and board of city commissioners on the condition of the same at such times as may be required. The chief of police shall be responsible for seeing that city prisoners who are confined to the county jail shall have clean and healthy conditions, that they be provided with the necessary articles as health and comfort require, and that they receive a proper diet in suitable quantity.
(Code 1975, § 22-36; Code 1985, § 21-38)
Sec. 32-60. - Reserved.

Sec. 32-61. - Reports.
The chief of police shall make a report in writing to the city manager each month showing the condition and activities of the police department and including any other data deemed necessary by the city manager.
(Code 1975, § 22-33; Code 1985, § 21-40)
