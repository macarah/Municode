DIVISION 3. - 911 ADVISORY BOARD

Sec. 2-2-151. - Created.
The General Assembly of the State of Georgia has enacted legislation authorizing each county of the State of Georgia to create an advisory board consisting of individuals knowledgeable of emergency "911" systems and the emergency needs of the citizens of the local government.
(Res. of 6-4-91)
Sec. 2-2-152. - Compensation.
The members of the advisory board shall not be compensated from moneys deposited in the emergency telephone system fund.
(Res. of 6-4-91)
Sec. 2-2-153. - Membership.
The advisory board appointed by the board of commissioners shall consist of fourteen (14) members, one (1) member each as follows: commissioner, city council, sheriff's department, city fire department, county fire department, citizen—city at-large, citizen — county at-large, ambulance service, electric department, physician, emergency management, Lamar EMC, city police department and the Milner City Council.
(Res. of 6-4-91; Res. of 8-6-91)
Sec. 2-2-154. - Duties and responsibilities.
The board of commissioners of Lamar County does hereby ordain that the advisory board shall assist Lamar County as follows:
(1)
Reviewing and analyzing the progress by public safety agencies in developing "911" system requirements;
(2)
Recommending steps of action to effect the necessary coordination, regulation, and development of the "911" system;
(3)
Identifying mutual aid agreements necessary to effect the "911' system;
(4)
Assisting in the promulgation of necessary rules, regulations, operating procedures, schedules, and other such policy and administrative devices as shall be deemed necessary and appropriate; and
(5)
Providing other services as may be deemed appropriate by the Lamar County Board of Commissioners.
(Res. of 6-4-91)
Secs. 2-2-155—2-2-170. - Reserved.
DIVISION 4. - LOCAL EMERGENCY PLANNING COMMITTEE

Sec. 2-2-171. - Name.
This organization shall be known as the local emergency planning committee of Lamar County, Georgia (hereinafter known as the L.E.P.C.)
(Mo. of 6-20-95)
Sec. 2-2-172. - Purpose and functions.
The L.E.P.C. shall exist to promote and facilitate the safety of all persons in Lamar County with respect to their potential exposure to hazardous materials which could be released into the environment.
The functions and duties of the L.E.P.C. are those authorized by the Lamar County Board of Commissioners in accordance with, but not necessarily limited to the provisions of the Federal Title III of the Superfund Amendments and Reauthorization Act (SARA) of 1993. More specifically, the L.E.P.C. shall engage in at least the following activities:
(1)
Conduct regular meetings to address all pertinent issues.
(2)
Develop and maintain an inventory of known hazardous materials in the county.
(3)
Develop and update a hazard/risk analysis.
(4)
Develop and periodically update emergency response procedures for off-site emergency response personnel.
(5)
Identify private/public sector resources available to deal with hazardous materials emergencies.
(6)
Review, process and respond to requests from the public for pertinent information.
(7)
Review, maintain and process all appropriate reports and records as required by law.
(8)
Develop and periodically update emergency warning procedures and evacuation plans.
(9)
Coordinate training programs on hazardous materials safety and emergency response procedures.
(10)
Coordinate emergency response exercises.
(11)
Provide expertise and assistance to industries and businesses upon request.
(12)
Provide community "out-reach" services on hazardous materials safety.
(13)
Receive, maintain, and disseminate emerging legislation relating to hazardous materials.
It is not and never has been the intent that the L.E.P.C. be a local regulatory enforcement, permitting, or policy-making body or agency. Further, the L.E.P.C. shall not be placed in a position to interfere with the "one-step permitting" process in the State of Georgia.
(Mo. of 6-20-95)
Sec. 2-2-173. - Membership.
(a)
Number. The L.E.P.C. shall consist of as many members as shall be deemed necessary by the Lamar County Board of Commissioners, the Georgia Emergency Response Commission and the requirements as set forth in SARA III.
(b)
Categories. There shall be two (2) categories of membership: full voting members and associate members.
Full voting members: Only one (1) member from any single organization, individual government department, or special interest group may serve as a full voting member.
Associate members: Up to two (2) additional members from any single organization, individual government department, or special interest group may serve as associate members.
(c)
Term. The term of membership in the L.E.P.C. shall be for four (4) years, at which time re-appointment may be made. Each term shall commence on the date of appointment.
(d)
Appointments. Appointments shall be made as follows:
Full voting members shall be appointed by the Director of the Lamar County Emergency Management Agency acting for the Lamar County Board of Commissioners.
Full Voting Members of the L.E.P.C.
(e)
Removal. Removal authority is delegated to the Director of the Lamar County Emergency Management Agency. In addition, Associate Members may be removed by majority vote of the full voting members of the L.E.P.C.
(f)
Vacancies. Vacancies in membership shall be filled as follows:
Full voting members: A vacancy for a full voting member shall be filled as soon as such a vacancy occurs.
Associate members: A vacancy for an associate member may be filled when such a vacancy occurs and the organization, individual government department, or special interest group nominates a person for the position.
Additions. Additions in membership shall be made as follows:
Full voting members: An additional full voting member shall be appointed as such time that it is determined that an organization, individual government department, or special interest group should have representation on the L.E.P.C.
Associate members: An additional associate member may be appointed at any time that are less than two (2) associate members for any participating organization, individual government department, or special interest group on the L.E.P.C.
(Mo. of 6-20-95)
Sec. 2-2-174. - Officers.
(a)
Positions. The officers of the L.E.P.C. shall consist of a chairperson, vice-chairperson, and secretary/treasurer. The chairperson and vice-chairperson shall be selected from the full voting members. The secretary/treasurer may be selected from the staff of the Lamar County Emergency Management Agency.
(b)
Election. The election of officers shall be made as the first order of business at the first meeting of each calendar year by majority vote of the full voting members.
(c)
Term. The term of office shall be for four (4) years. If the membership of an officer is terminated before the next election, a special election shall be conducted to fill the vacancy.
(d)
Removal. An officer may be removed from office at any time by a majority vote of all full voting members.
(e)
Vacancies. Any vacancy in office may be filled by a special election by the full voting members.
(Mo. of 6-20-95)
Sec. 2-2-175. - Duties of the officers.
(a)
Chairperson. The chairperson shall preside at all meetings of the L.E.P.C., prepare order during its meetings, appoint all subcommittees, and sign minutes and such records, vouchers, and other documents connected with the work of the L.E.P.C. requiring such a signature.
(b)
Vice-chairperson. In the absence of the chairperson or in the event of his/her inability or refusal to act, the vice-chairperson, unless otherwise determined by the L.E.P.C., shall perform the duties the chairperson, and when so acting shall have all of the powers of the chairperson. He/she shall also perform other such duties and may be assigned by the chairperson.
(c)
Secretary/treasurer. The secretary/treasurer shall have charge of all books, papers, records, and other documents of the L.E.P.C., shall keep the minutes of all L.E.P.C. meetings, shall conduct all correspondence pertaining to the office of the secretary, shall compile statistics and other data as may be required for use by the members of the L.E.P.C., and shall maintain and oversee all financial records. He/she also shall perform such other duties as may be assigned by the chairperson.
(Mo. of 6-20-95)
Sec. 2-2-176. - Subcommittees.
Standing subcommittees shall be created by majority vote of the full voting members whenever deemed appropriate. Members of those subcommittees shall be appointed and given mandate by the chairperson within thirty (30) days after creation of such subcommittees or after the occurrence of a vacancy on the subcommittee. The membership of the subcommittee will be reappointed or revised in January of each year.
Ad hoc subcommittees may be formed at any time for special purposes or assignments. They shall cease to function when their specific task is completed.
All subcommittees shall have a chairperson appointed by the L.E.P.C. chairperson. Associate members may serve on all subcommittees.
(Mo. of 6-20-95)
Sec. 2-2-177. - Meetings.
(a)
Regular meetings. The L.E.P.C. shall normally meet bimonthly for regular meetings unless otherwise specified by the chairperson, but not less than quarterly.
(b)
Special meetings. The chairperson may call such special meetings as may be deemed necessary to carry out the duties of the L.E.P.C. Also, upon the request of at least four (4) members, the chairperson shall call a meeting within ten (10) business days.
(c)
Subcommittee meetings. The subcommittees shall meet at least bimonthly on alternate months from the regular meetings of the L.E.P.C. Additional meetings may be called by a subcommittee chairperson as necessary to complete projects.
(d)
Quorum. A quorum for a regular or special meeting shall consist of one-third (⅓) of all full voting members. A quorum shall be required to transact business. No quorum shall be required for subcommittee or ad hoc committee meetings.
(e)
Agenda. Any member may request the chairperson to place an item on the agenda. If the chairperson should decline to do so, a member may have such item placed on the agenda by submitting it in writing to the chairperson with supporting signatures of three (3) members.
(f)
Rules of order. The deliberations of all meeting of the L.E.P.C. shall be governed by Robert's Rules of Order, Newly Revised.
(g)
Compliance with open meetings ordinances. The L.E.P.C. is acting for the public and in the public's interest. As such the L.E.P.C. shall operate in compliance with extant open meeting laws and ordinances.
(h)
Notice of meetings. Notice of time, place, and agenda items to be considered at each regular meeting shall be given in writing to all members at least five (5) business days prior to each meeting. Notice of time and place of regular meetings will be provided to the local newspaper for publication the week prior to meeting. Any matters not appearing on the agenda may be considered upon a favorable vote of a majority of the members present. Notice of special meetings and intended agenda items shall be given to all members by telephone at least five (5) business days in advance of any special meetings. Notice of subcommittee meetings and the intended agenda items shall be given in any manner determined appropriate by the subcommittee chairpersons.
(Mo. of 6-20-95)
Sec. 2-2-178. - Voting.
(a)
One vote. Each full voting member, including the chairperson, shall be entitled to one vote. Associate members shall not vote except as provided for in sections (b) and (c) below.
(b)
Proxy votes. Associate members shall be allowed to vote by proxy for an absent full voting member. No other proxy votes shall be allowed.
(c)
In the event of an unfilled vacancy of a full voting member, an associate member from the same organization, governmental department, or special interest group shall be permitted to vote until the vacancy is filled.
(d)
Abstentions. Members may register their abstentions on any vote which shall be reflected in the minutes. Members are encouraged to abstain on matters that pose for them a conflict of interest.
(e)
Determination of actions. All final actions, committee positions, or policy recommendations shall require the favorable vote of a majority of those full voting members present at a duly called meeting.
(Mo. of 6-20-95)
Sec. 2-2-179. - Reports and recommendation.
(a)
Annual report. By February 1st of each year, the L.E.P.C. shall make a report describing its activities for the preceding calendar year to the chairperson of the Lamar County Board of Commissioners.
(b)
Review of draft reports. A draft of any proposed annual report shall be circulated to all members of the L.E.P.C. at least fourteen (14) business days prior to consideration of the full L.E.P.C. at a regularly schedule meeting.
(c)
Circulation of reports. No report of any kind shall be released in the name of the L.E.P.C. unless and until it has been duly adopted by a favorable vote of a majority of the full voting members of the L.E.P.C.
(d)
Recommendations. The L.E.P.C. may address matters regarding legislation, ordinances, and resolutions to be endorsed or opposed by the L.E.P.C. in between regularly scheduled meetings. This may be accomplished by means of telephone conferences provided that the provisions of the Georgia Open Meetings Law are met. In the event that the L.E.P.C. is divided into smaller groups to accomplish the logistical requirements of telephone conferencing, the chairperson shall be a party to each conference.
Sec. 2-2-180. - Amendments.
These bylaws may be amended or replaced upon the affirmative vote of a majority of all full voting members at any duly called meeting of the L.E.P.C. provided that any proposed changes have been circulated to all members seven (7) business days prior to any action thereon.
(Mo. of 6-20-95)

DIVISION 5. - BOARD OF TAX ASSESSORS

Sec. 2-2-200. - Board of tax assessors.
(1)
Members of the BTA appointed or reappointed to such office on and after January 11, 2005, shall be appointed by the board in accordance with this section and as follows:
(a)
The terms of members first appointed or reappointed by the board shall be for the following terms:
Chairman of the board nominee—January 1, 2005 through December 31, 2007;
District 1 commissioner nominee—January 1, 2005 through December 31, 2007;
District 2 commissioner nominee—January 1, 2005 through December 31, 2007;
District 3 commissioner nominee—January 1, 2005 through December 31, 2009;
District 4 commissioner nominee—January 1, 2005 through December 31, 2009.
(b)
Except as provided in subsection (3), all subsequent terms of members of the BTA appointed or reappointed shall be for terms of four (4) years, which initial appointments or reappointments provided for in subsection (a). The term of each BTA member appointed or reappointed upon the nomination of a district commissioner shall run concurrently with the nominating commissioner's term of office and until a successor is appointed.
(2)
Any member of the existing BTA shall be eligible for reappointment after review by the board.
(3)
Any vacancies to the BTA, whether occurring as a result of death, resignation, removal, or otherwise shall be filled by appointment by the board. The term of the individual appointed to fill such vacancy shall be only for the remainder of the unexpired term of the position which created the vacancy. The person appointed to fill vacancy shall possess the same qualifications as a regular appointed full term BTA member.
(4)
The terms of individuals serving on the BTA at the time this section is adopted shall not be shortened or lengthened by this section. Appointments made after the adoption of this section shall be made in accordance with the provisions of this section.
(Res. of 11-11-05)
