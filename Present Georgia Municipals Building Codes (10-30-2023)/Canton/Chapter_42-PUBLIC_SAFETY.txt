Sec. 42-22. - Tampering with alarm system.
It shall be unlawful for any person to willfully, maliciously or mischievously interfere or tamper with any fire alarm box or any of the appliances or apparatus connected therewith located within the corporate limits.
(Code 1985, § 4-2-4; Code 2002, § 42-29)
Sec. 42-23. - Key lockboxes.
(a)
When required. Where access to or within a structure or an area is restricted because of secured openings or where immediate access is necessary for life-saving or firefighting purposes, the city fire marshal is authorized to require a key box to be installed in an accessible location. The key lockbox shall be of an approved type and shall contain keys to gain access as required by the city fire marshal.
(b)
Structures affected. The following types of buildings or structures within the city shall be equipped with a key lockbox to be located at or near the recognized public entrance, such location to be approved by the fire marshal's office.
(1)
All commercial buildings;
(2)
All industrial buildings;
(3)
All multifamily buildings;
(4)
All gated communities with restricted vehicle access, including apartments, condominiums, and residential developments;
(5)
All hotels and motels.
(c)
Key lockbox contents. The key lockbox shall contain keys for the following and shall be labeled so as to be easily identifiable in the field:
(1)
Keys to the locked points of egress, whether on the interior or exterior of such structure;
(2)
Keys to the locked mechanical rooms;
(3)
Keys to the locked elevator rooms;
(4)
Keys to the elevator controls;
(5)
Keys to any fence or secured area;
(6)
Keys to any other area as directed by the fire marshal.
(d)
Key lockbox for affected residential structures.
(1)
All apartment and condominium complexes, and all gated communities with restricted vehicle traffic shall be required to install a key lockbox at a location to be determined by a field inspector from the fire department.
(2)
The key lockbox shall be located at a height of not less than four feet and not more than 12 feet above final grade.
(3)
The key lockbox shall contain the following keys that shall be labeled so as to be easily identified in the field:
a.
Alarm room area where alarm panel is installed;
b.
Alarm panel;
c.
Any special keys or tools to reset pull stations;
d.
All other areas as specified by the fire marshal's office.
(4)
Lockboxes need not be supervised.
(5)
Only apartment or condominium owners, residential management companies, or managers may purchase key lockboxes.
(e)
Further requirements.
(1)
No steps, displays, signs or other fixtures, or structure protrusions, shall be located under the lockbox which would allow intruders to access the lockbox without assistance.
(2)
The fire marshal shall designate the type of key lockbox system to be implemented within the city and shall have the authority to require all structures to use the designated system.
(3)
The owner or operator of a structure required to have a key lockbox shall, at all times, keep a key in the lockbox that will allow for access to the structure.
(4)
Any change in occupancy or ownership of any affected structure shall require a new lockbox.
(5)
The city shall be authorized to implement rules and regulations for the use of the key lockbox system.
(Code 2002, § 42-30; Ord. of 5-2-2002(3))

Sec. 42-25. - Outdoor burning.
(a)
Purpose. This section is intended to promote and safeguard the public health, safety, comfort, air quality, and living conditions of the citizens of the city relative to outdoor burning.
(b)
Applicability.
(1)
This section applies to all outdoor burning within the city.
(2)
The requirements of this section are supplemental to state laws and regulations governing outdoor burning restrictions and restrictions governing the disposal of construction waste. To the extent any state law, environmental protection division rules, or other adopted codes is more stringent than the requirements contained in this section, such other law, rule, and/or code shall be controlling.
(c)
Definitions.
(1)
Bonfire means an outdoor fire larger than a recreational fire in which only logs are being burned for ceremonial purposes.
(2)
Fire marshal means the Fire Marshal of the City of Canton Fire Department or his/her designee.
(3)
Garbage means any waste material other than charcoal or logs that includes, but is not limited to, plastic products, Styrofoam, fiberglass, recycling wire (burned to access metal), tires, painted/varnished wood, clothing, furniture, mattresses, boxes, papers, and kudzu vines.
(4)
Logs mean pieces of wood cut from trees that are dried and clean of soil so as not to produce large amounts of smoke when burning.
(5)
Outdoor burning means kindling or maintaining a fire where the products of combustion are emitted directly into the ambient air without passing through a stack or a chimney.
(6)
Recreational fire means an outdoor fire in which only logs are being burned where the fuel is not contained in an incinerator, outdoor fireplace, barbeque grill or barbeque pit and has a total fuel area of three feet or less in diameter and two feet or less in height for pleasure, religious, ceremonial, cooking, warmth or similar purposes, and which is not used to dispose of garbage or yard waste.
(7)
Yard waste means limited yard debris: Leaves, pine straw, and dry brush/limbs no more than six inches in diameter that have fallen or been cut from growth on one's own property.
(d)
Exceptions to outdoor burning restrictions. Restrictions on outdoor burning do not apply to the following:
(1)
Grilling or cooking using charcoal, wood, propane or natural gas.
(2)
Burning in a stove, furnace, fireplace or other heating device within a building used for human or animal habitation, unless garbage is being burned.
(3)
Burning in a chimney, fire bowl or other similar device or outdoor fireplace, unless garbage is being burned.
(4)
The use of propane, acetylene, natural gas, gasoline or kerosene in a device intended for heating, construction, or maintenance activities (asphalt kettles, road flares, smudge pots, etc.).
(5)
Incinerators installed in accordance with all state and local codes.
(6)
Fire department practice burns that comply with all state and local codes.
(e)
Burning restrictions.
(1)
Burning of petroleum-based products, such as tires, plastics, and roof shingles, which produce black smoke, is prohibited.
(2)
Burning of garbage of any kind is prohibited.
(3)
Burning of any material in a barrel is prohibited.
(4)
No burning is allowed when the fire danger or fire danger forecast is class 3 or higher as per the Georgia Forestry Commission.
(5)
No burning is allowed on windy days (ten mph sustained or higher) or on days when the atmospheric conditions (cloudy, overcast, or raining) would cause the smoke to remain low to the ground.
(6)
No commercial burning is allowed in the city. The term "commercial burning" means any burning by any party other than a person who resides in a residential dwelling located on the property on which the burning is taking place.
(7)
The fire marshal shall be authorized to ban any or all forms of outdoor burning when atmospheric or local conditions make outdoor fires hazardous.
(8)
The fire marshal shall be authorized to require the extinguishment of any fire if it adversely interferes with the enjoyment of life or use of property.
(9)
The fire marshal shall be authorized to ban outdoor burning in a limited geographical area upon receipt of medical documentation from a resident that the resident has a medical condition(s) adversely affected by smoke.
(10)
No burning on Sunday.
(11)
No burning on any of the public streets, sidewalks, alleys or parks of the city.
(f)
Exceptions to burning prohibitions. The fire chief may issue an outdoor burning permit when special circumstances warrant exceptions to prohibitions against burning. Special circumstances would include:
(1)
Carrying out recognized agricultural procedures necessary for production or harvesting of crops;
(2)
Disposal of vegetative debris from storm damage; or
(3)
Any other special circumstances as determined by the fire chief.
(g)
Safeguards and limitations for outdoor burning.
(1)
Recreational fires shall not be located within 25 feet of a structure or other combustible material. Conditions which could cause a fire to spread within 25 feet of a structure shall be eliminated prior to ignition. Burning is allowed between 10:00 a.m. and 10:30 p.m.
(2)
Any type of outdoor burning referenced in this section must be attended by an adult who must be watching the fire at all times. A water hose that can reach the fire or fire extinguisher must be on hand and ready for use if needed to limit or extinguish the fire.
(3)
When extinguishing an outdoor fire, no smoldering or hot coals shall remain.
(h)
Outdoor burning requiring a permit. No person shall start or maintain any outdoor burning listed in this section without first obtaining a permit issued by the City of Canton Fire Department. Permits are only good for the date issued:
(1)
A bonfire shall not be located within a minimum of 100 feet of a structure or other combustible material, and shall be a minimum of 300 feet from property lines. The bonfire shall not be located on any asphalt or concrete. There shall be a 100-foot clear area around the fire area, clear of combustible materials, debris or material that could cause fire to spread. Distances may be increased by the fire chief depending on the size of the bonfire. Burning is allowed between 10:00 a.m. and 10:30 p.m. The permit will be valid for specified dates and times.
(2)
For burning yard waste, permits will only be issued to parcels of two or more acres, subject to the following regulations: The fire pile shall not be larger than four feet wide by four feet deep by two feet high (only one fire pile at a time shall be burning per address/permit) and shall not be not located within 50 feet of a structure or other combustible material and/or within 25 feet of a public roadway. Conditions which could cause a fire to spread within 50 feet of a structure shall be eliminated prior to ignition. Burning is allowed between 10:00 a.m. and one hour before sunset, October 1 through April 30. No permits will be issued after 12:00 p.m.
(3)
Any type of outdoor burning referenced in this article must be attended by an adult who must be watching the fire at all times. An appropriate means for containing and/or extinguishing the fire shall be provided.
(4)
When extinguishing an outdoor fire, no smoldering or hot coals shall remain unless otherwise permitted in this Code.
(Ord. No. 2016-19, § 1, 8-18-2016; Ord. No. 2022-0421-08 , §§ 2, 3, 4-21-2022)
