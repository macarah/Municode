Chapter 19 - CEMETERIES

Sec. 19-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Burial space means and consists of enough land in which persons may be buried in single grave spaces, as defined in the plat of the cemetery and as described in the deed of the burial rights to the owner thereof. The term shall include those references to cemetery burial spaces.
Superintendent means the city employee designated as such by the city manager.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-2. - Burials; location restricted.
It shall be unlawful for any person to bury any dead body except in a public or private cemetery.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-3. - Burial space subject to restrictions, covenants, rules and regulations; authority of superintendent.
(a)
All burial spaces owned and purchased within the public cemeteries of the city, and all such spaces sold, shall be subject to the restrictions, covenants, rules and regulations in this chapter and shall be subject to such other rules, regulations, amendments or alterations and ordinances as shall be adopted and regularly passed by the city from time to time. A reference to such rules and regulations in the deed or certificate of ownership shall have the same force and effect as if fully set forth therein.
(b)
The superintendent, or his designee, is authorized to supervise the upkeep, care and operations of city cemeteries.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-4. - Purchase price of burial spaces.
The purchase price of burial spaces in the public cemeteries shall be as set out in the fee schedule as set by the city council.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-5. - Burial rights in public cemeteries.
The City of Canton is no longer selling burial rights. The provisions of this chapter 19, however, shall apply to all prior conveyances of burial rights by the City of Canton.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011; Ord. No. 2022-0421-04 , § 1, 4-21-2022)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 2022-0421-04 , § 1, adopted April 21, 2022 amended § 19-5 and in doing so changed the title of said section from "burial rights in public cemeteries; supervision of sale and conveyances, execution, form and conditions of deed" to "Burial rights in public cemeteries," as set out herein.
Sec. 19-6. - Ownership construed; use and division; reversion to city.
Ownership of a burial space consists only in the right of interment. The space can be used for no other purpose and cannot be divided into smaller portions than originally laid down in the burial space. Should a space holder die without devise of the space or any known descendants, title to such space shall revert to the city, for the protection of those who may be interred therein.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-7. - Deed to be issued.
A deed or certificate of ownership shall be issued to each burial space holder on full payment of the purchase money of such burial space.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-8. - Right of purchaser; taking for debt or selling for secular use.
When a right has once been purchased within the enclosure of a public cemetery for burial purposes, it becomes for all time the property of the purchaser and his transferees and cannot be taken for debt or sold for secular uses.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-9. - Owner's change of address, duty to notify city; sufficient and proper legal notification described.
It shall be the duty of a burial space owner to notify the city of any change in his post office address. Notice sent to a burial space owner at the last address on file in the office of the city clerk shall be considered a sufficient and proper legal notification.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-10. - Certain rights and privileges reserved by city.
The following rights and privileges are hereby expressly reserved by the city in its conveyance of any cemetery burial space: At any time and from time to time, the city may:
(1)
Resurvey, enlarge, diminish, replat, alter in shape or size or otherwise change all or any part, portion or subdivision of the property mapped and platted.
(2)
File amended maps or plats thereof and use the same for the erection of buildings or for any purposes or uses connected with, incident to, or convenient for the care, preservation or preparation, for the disposal or interment of dead human bodies, or other cemetery purposes, together with easements and rights-of-way over and through such premises for the privilege of, installing, maintaining and operating pipelines, conduits or drains for sprinklers, drainage, electric or communication lines, or for any other like purposes.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-11. - Easements or rights of interment in roads, drives, alleys or walks.
No easement or right of interment is granted by the city to any burial space in any road, drive, alley or walk within a cemetery, but such road, drive, alley or walk may be used as long as the city devotes it to that purpose.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-12. - Transfer or assignment; prerequisite to validity.
No transfer or assignment of any cemetery burial space or interest therein shall be valid until the consent of the city has been endorsed thereon and the same has been recorded on the books of the city.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-13. - Charge for transfer of ownership.
The city may fix a charge for all transfers of ownership of burial spaces. No transfer of ownership shall be complete or effective until all charges are paid.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-14. - Subdivision of burial spaces; interment of persons with no interest in burial space.
The subdivision of cemetery burial spaces is not allowed by any person except the city, and no person not having an interest therein shall be interred in any space except by written consent of all persons interested in such space and the city; provided, however, a relative of any record owner may be buried in such space, as provided in this chapter or by the laws of the state.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-15. - Family burial space inalienable.
(a)
Reversion to city. Whenever an interment of the remains of a member or of a relative of a member of the family of the record owner, or of the remains of the record owner, is made in a burial space transferred by deed or certificate of ownership to an individual owner and the owner dies without making disposition of the space either in his will by a specific device, or by a written declaration filed and recorded in the office of the city clerk, the space thereby becomes inalienable and shall be held as the family burial space of the owner, with title to the same reverting to the city for protection of those who may be interred therein.
(b)
Right to burial without consent. In a family burial space one grave, niche or crypt may be used for the owner's interment; one for the owner's surviving spouse, if any, who by law has a right of interment in it; and in those remaining, if any, the parents and children of the deceased owner, in order of death, may be interred without the consent of any person claiming any interest in the burial space.
(c)
Order of right of interment. If no parent or child survives, the right of interment goes in the order of death, first, to the spouse of any child of the record owner, and second, in the order of death, to the next heir at law of the owner or the spouse of any heir at law.
(d)
Interment right waived in favor of other relative. Any surviving spouse, parent, child or heir who has a right of interment in a family burial space may waive such right in favor of any other relative, or spouse of a relative, of either deceased owner or of his spouse, and upon such waiver the remains of the person in whose favor the waiver is made may be interred in the burial space.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-16. - Right of spouse of owner—Described.
The spouse of any owner of any burial space which contains more than one interment space has a vested right of interment of his remains in the burial space, and any person thereafter becoming the spouse of the owner has a vested right of interment of his remains in the space if more than one interment space is unoccupied at the time the person becomes the spouse of the owner.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-17. - Same—Vested right of spouse; joinder prerequisite to divesting.
No conveyance or other action of the owner without the written consent or joinder of the spouse of the owner divests the spouse of a vested right of interment, except that a final decree of divorce between them terminates the vested right of interment unless otherwise provided in the decree.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-18. - Burial spaces having several owners; representation by designated owner.
When there are several owners of the rights of interment in a burial space, they may designate one or more persons to represent the burial space and file written notice of designation with the city clerk. In the absence of such notice or of written objection to its so doing, the city is not liable to any owner for interring or permitting an interment or refusing an interment in the burial space upon the request or direction of any co-owners of the burial space.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-19. - Waiver of vested right.
A vested right of interment may be waived and is so waived and terminated upon the interment elsewhere of the remains of the person in whom those rights are vested.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-20. - Scope of vested right.
No vested right of interment gives to any person the right to have his remains interred in any interment space in which the remains of any deceased having a prior vested right of interment have been interred, nor does it give any person the right to have the remains of more than one deceased person interred in a single interment space.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-21. - Authorization to open burial space.
The city may inter or open a burial space for any purpose on proper written authorization by any burial space owner of record made out on forms approved by the city and duly filed in the office of the city clerk unless there are written instructions to the contrary on file in such office.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-22. - Owners shall not permit interments for remuneration.
Cemetery owners shall not allow any interments to be made in their burial spaces for a remuneration.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-23. - Raising or depressing surface of burial spaces or graves prohibited.
Except for temporary conditions caused by a burial, the owners of cemetery burial spaces are prohibited from raising or depressing the surface of any burial space or grave above or below the surrounding ground.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-24. - Authority for grading, landscaping, improvements, plantings, interments, disinterments and removals.
In the cemetery, all grading, landscape work and improvements of any kind and all care on burial spaces shall be done, and all trees and shrubs and herbage of any kind shall be planted, trimmed, cut or removed, and all openings and closing of burial spaces, and all interments, disinterments and removals shall be made only by the authority of the city.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-25. - Improvements or alterations of individual property; direction and approval of city; removal when unsightly.
All improvements or alterations of individual property in the cemetery shall be under the direction of and subject to the consent, satisfaction and approval of the city; and should they be made without its written consent, the city shall have the right to remove, alter or change such improvements or alterations at the expense of the burial space owner, or in any event at any time when, in its judgment, they become unsightly to the eye.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-26. - Right to remove detrimental trees and shrubs.
If any tree or shrub, situated in any burial space, shall, by means of its roots or branches, become detrimental in any way to the adjoining space, avenue or walk, it shall be the duty and privilege of the city, and it hereby reserves the right, to enter upon the burial space and remove the trees or shrubs, or any part thereof. But no tree growing in any burial space or border shall be pruned or removed without the consent of the city.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-27. - Flowers, trees, shrubs and herbage—Right to prevent removal.
The city reserves the right to prevent the removal of any flowers, floral designs, trees, shrubs or plants or herbage of any kind from any cemetery. No flowers will be removed from any grave space without the permission of the superintendent.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-28. - Same—Authority to remove; liability for frames or baskets.
The city shall have the authority to remove all floral designs, flowers, weeds, trees, shrubs, plants or herbage of any kind from the cemetery when, in the judgment of the superintendent, they become unsightly, dangerous, detrimental or diseased. The city shall not be liable for damage to floral pieces, baskets or frames in which or to which such floral pieces are attached. The superintendent is authorized to remove funeral flowers after seven days.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-29. - Same—Gathering or breaking; feeding or disturbing birds or animals.
All persons are prohibited from gathering flowers, either wild or cultivated, or breaking trees, shrubbery or plants or feeding or disturbing the birds or other animals life in the cemeteries of the city.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-30. - Control of floral arrangements; disposal of floral frames.
(a)
Only one floral arrangement shall be placed on each grave and only at the base of the monument. Any flowers which are determined by the superintendent to be outside of the appropriate area may be removed by the city.
(b)
The city requires that all permanent floral arrangements be placed in marble or granite containers. All other containers are prohibited, except as may be permitted by the superintendent.
(c)
The superintendent is authorized to remove funeral flowers after seven days unless notified by the family or space owners.
(d)
Floral frames, when removed from the burial space, unless called for within ten days by those lawfully entitled to them, shall be disposed of by the city.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-31. - Signs, notices or advertisements prohibited.
No signs, notices or advertisements of any kind shall be allowed in any cemetery, except by the city.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-32. - Responsibility for damage.
The city disclaims all responsibility for loss or damage from causes beyond its reasonable control, and especially from damage caused by the elements, an act of God, a common enemy, thieves, vandals, strikers, malicious mischief makers, explosions, unavoidable accidents, invasions, insurrections, riots or orders of any military or civil authority, whether the damage is direct or collateral.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-33. - Interments, disinterments and removals; time, manner and charges for same; opening and closing graves; grave service; embalming.
(a)
All interments, disinterments and removals shall be done by funeral homes licensed by the state. The funeral homes must notify the city before any work is done, as provided in this chapter. All work must be done in the manner provided in this chapter and any other regulation of the city, and subject to the payment of such charges as are fixed by the city.
(b)
No grave service will be made ready for burial before one hour before sunrise or later than one hour before sunset.
(c)
All state and local health laws must be complied with prior to any interment in any city cemetery.
(d)
All interments must be inside containers of minimum standard as approved by the superintendent (minimum standard is pine construction or equal). These containers must be in vaults made of 12-gauge steel (minimum), or concrete, unless approved by the superintendent. All vaults shall be placed underground.
(e)
No interment will be made in any city cemetery before payment of the fees specified in the regulations in the office of the city clerk, unless credit has been extended by the city clerk.
(f)
No interment will be made in any city cemetery until the owner of the burial space shall execute an affidavit directing interment of the remains of the deceased in such burial space.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-34. - Charges for opening graves, interment and disinterment.
The charges for opening graves, interment and disinterment in the public cemeteries shall be as set out in the fee schedule of the city.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-35. - Delay of interment; liability and right of city.
The city shall be in no way liable for any delay in the interment of a body where a protest to the interment has been made, or where this chapter has not been complied with. The city may require any protest to be in writing and to be filed in the office of the city clerk.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-36. - Reservation of right to require specified notice before interment.
The right is reserved by the city to insist upon at least 24 hours' notice before any interment. Interment requests with less than 24 hours' notice may be refused. All requests with less than 12 hours' notice shall be refused.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-37. - Liability for interment permit and identify of person sought to be interred or cremated.
The city shall not be liable in damages for an error in the interment permit nor for a mistake or error in the identity of a person sought to be interred or cremated.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-38. - Responsibility for telephone orders or mistakes caused by vague instructions.
The city shall not be held responsible for any order given by telephone, or for any mistake occurring from the want of precise and proper instructions as to the particular space, size and location in a space where interment is desired.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-39. - Procedure when instructions from burial space owner not available.
When instructions from the burial space owner regarding the location of an interment space in a burial space cannot be obtained, or are indefinite, or when for any reason the interment space cannot be opened when specified, the city may, in its discretion, open it in such location in the burial space as it deems best and proper so as not to delay the funeral; and the city shall not be liable in damages for any error so made.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-40. - Disinterment—Permission prerequisite; liability of city.
No disinterment from any space shall be made except with the express permission of the city clerk first obtained. The city clerk may insist that an order from a proper court of law first be secured by those seeking the disinterment. The city shall have no liability for damage to any casket, burial case, vault or urn, incurred in the disinterment or removal.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-41. - Removal of body for specified reasons repugnant to sense of decency prohibited.
Removal of a body or cremated remains by the heirs of the deceased so that the burial space may be sold for profit to themselves, or removal contrary to the expressed or implied wish of the original burial space owner, or if repugnant to the ordinary sense of decency, is absolutely forbidden.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-42. - Limitation on number of bodies interred in same grave, vault, crypt or niche.
Not more than one body, nor the remains of more than one body, shall be interred in any one grave, vault, crypt or niche.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-43. - Visitation hours; loitering in cemeteries.
(a)
City cemeteries shall be open for visitation during the following hours, unless otherwise posted: Between one-half hour after sunup and one-half hour before sundown.
(b)
Loitering in city cemeteries is prohibited. The presence of any person in any of such cemeteries during prohibited hours shall be prima facie evidence of loitering.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-44. - Refreshments prohibited within cemeteries; exception.
No person shall have refreshments within any cemetery except as authorized by the superintendent.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-45. - Markers; temporary markers.
(a)
All graves shall have a permanent slab or marker made of either bronze, marble, or granite. No grave may contain both a slab and a marker unless the marker is a family marker. No slab or marker may be more than six inches in height above the level ground of the burial space.
(b)
The marker shall contain identification information for the person buried in the grave.
(c)
A temporary marker may be placed on the grave for 120 days, at which time a permanent marker shall be required.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-46. - Persons within grounds to use only avenues, walks, alleys and roads.
Persons within the cemetery grounds shall use only the avenues, walks, alleys and roads and shall not walk, drive or ride upon the burial spaces.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-47. - Motor vehicles regulated.
Motor vehicles shall not be driven through the cemetery grounds at a greater speed than ten miles per hour, and must always be kept on the right-hand side of the cemetery roadway. Motor vehicles are not allowed to park or to come to a full stop in front of an open grave unless such motor vehicles are in attendance at the funeral. No off-road recreational vehicles shall be allowed in city cemeteries.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-48. - Interpretation, application and enforcement of provisions.
This chapter shall be interpreted, applied and enforced by the public works committee. In cases of hardship, necessity or unreasonableness in the application or enforcement of any of the rules and regulations in this chapter, the majority of the public works committee shall have the right to alter the application and enforcement of these rules and regulations so as to relieve the hardship or unreasonableness, and the same shall not constitute forfeiture nor shall it have the effect of changing or abridging any rule, covenant or regulation under this chapter.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
Sec. 19-49. - Violation of chapter.
Any person performing any act forbidden by this chapter or failing to perform any act required by this chapter shall have breached this chapter and, upon conviction thereof, shall be punished as provided by section 1-11.
(Ord. No. 20111215-02, § 1, 12-15-2011)
