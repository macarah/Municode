ARTICLE II. - OFFICERS AND EMPLOYEES

Sec. 2-19. - County attorney.
The sole commissioner shall from time to time appoint a qualified individual to serve as county attorney. The county attorney shall have the following duties:
(1)
Advising the sole commissioner and other county officers regarding powers and duties under the law;
(2)
Preparing ordinances and resolutions and legal documents on behalf of the county;
(3)
Reviewing and drafting proposed contracts for the county;
(4)
Representing the county in court;
(5)
Performing road right-of-way work for the county, such as examining titles, preparing right-of-way deeds, and preparing releases;
(6)
Performing such other legal work as may be assigned or designated by the sole commissioner.
(Res. of 1-6-1998(1))
Sec. 2-20. - County clerk.
The sole commissioner shall from time to time appoint a qualified individual to serve as county clerk. The county clerk shall have the following duties:
(1)
Recording the sole commissioner's official actions;
(2)
Typing and distributing the agenda;
(3)
Bookkeeping and maintaining other records;
(4)
Preparing and processing correspondence and reports;
(5)
Managing the sole commissioner's office;
(6)
Serving as executive secretary to the sole commissioner;
(7)
Signing warrants and checks;
(8)
Performing such other clerical work and other duties as may be assigned by the sole commissioner from time to time.
(Res. of 1-6-1998(2))
Sec. 2-21. - Ethics.
The sole commissioner adopts the code of ethics promulgated by the Association County Commissioners of Georgia, in pertinent part as set forth herein. Each county officer and employee shall at all times:
(1)
Continue to make honesty the keystone of their efforts, by being always mindful of their oaths of office, and by practicing honesty and impartiality in all actions;
(2)
Keep the public informed regarding county operations and activities since it is the county's belief that "county business is ever public business";
(3)
Expend all county income economically for the greatest good of all county residents;
(4)
Provide a certified annual audit of all county income, expenditures, and investments;
(5)
Establish and follow a budget for county income and expenditures;
(6)
Employ only persons found to be properly qualified by training or experience for key county jobs;
(7)
Appraise all real and personal property impartially for tax purposes;
(8)
Encourage the commercial, cultural, and industrial development of the county through sound planning and practical cooperation with other local governments, institutions, state agencies, and federal agencies; and
(9)
Remember always that "In God We Trust" in pursuit of the American way of free enterprise and independence through "local government—the bulwark of democracy."
(Res. of 5-8-1962)

ARTICLE III. - BOARDS, COMMISSIONS AND AUTHORITIES

Sec. 2-46. - Heart of Georgia Planning and Development Commission.
(a)
Membership. The Heart of Georgia Planning and Development Commission shall consist of two members from each of the counties participating in the area planning commission. Of the said two members from each county, one shall be appointed by the governing authority of the municipality wherein the county seat is located (or by the governing authority of participating municipalities within said county) and the other to be appointed by the sole commissioner.
(b)
Term of office. The term of office for all members shall be five years, except that the initial appointment by the county shall be for a term of five years. Any vacancy in membership shall be filled for the unexpired term by the governing authority making the appointment that has become vacant. The appointing authority shall also have the power to remove any member it may appoint for cause, or written charges, after public hearing. All members shall serve without compensation.
(c)
Organization, rules, staff, and finances. The commission shall elect a chairman and vice-chairman from among its appointive members. The term of office of the chairman and vice-chairman shall be for one year or until reelected or their successors are elected. The commission shall appoint a secretary who may be an employee of the commission. The commission shall meet at least once each month at the call of the chairman and at such other times as the chairman or board may determine, shall adopt rules for the transaction of business, and shall keep a record of its resolutions, transactions, findings, and determinations, which record shall be made public. The commission may appoint such employees and staff as it may deem necessary for its work. In the performance of its duties, the planning commission may cooperate with, contract with, or accept funds from federal, state, or local public or semipublic agencies or private individuals or corporations, may spend such funds, and may carry out such cooperative undertakings and contracts. It may make expenditures for the purchase of required equipment and supplies. The expenditures of the commission, exclusive of gifts to the commission and contracts receipts, shall be within the amount appropriated for the purpose by the participating governing authority.
(d)
Powers and duties. It shall be the function and duty of the commission to make such careful and comprehensive surveys and studies of existing conditions and probable future developments and to prepare such plans for physical, social, and economic growth as will best promote the public health, safety, morals, convenience, prosperity, or the general welfare, as well as efficiency and economy in the development of the entire south central area of the state and each political subdivision therein. In general, the commission shall have such powers and duties and responsibilities as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 36-70-3, as amended.
(Res. of 12-1-1965, § 4)
Sec. 2-47. - Cochran-Bleckley Recreation Commission.
(a)
Established. There is hereby established a joint commission for supervised recreation for the City of Cochran and the county, to be known as the Cochran-Bleckley Recreation Commission.
(b)
Membership; appointment of first six recreation commissioners. The number serving on the joint recreation commission shall be seven, and those persons shall serve without pay. The mayor of the City of Cochran, with the approval of the council of said city, shall appoint three persons to serve on the joint recreation commission; the sole commissioner shall appoint three persons to serve on the joint recreation commission; and the six appointed persons shall recommend a seventh member of the joint recreation commission to the mayor and sole commissioner for approval and appointment.
(c)
Terms of office; vacancies. The terms of office of the six members of the joint recreation commission first appointed by the mayor and the county commissioners shall be for five years, or until their successors are appointed and qualified; except that the appointing authorities in making the initial appointments or in filling vacancies hereby vary the initial terms of members or the terms of persons appointed to fill vacancies in such manner so that the members of such joint recreation commission first appointed shall be appointed for such terms that the term of one member from the City of Cochran shall expire annually thereafter and one member from outside the city limits of Cochran will expire annually thereafter. The seventh member shall serve a one-year term.
(d)
Seventh member; appointment. As soon as the six joint recreation commissioners have been sworn in by any person authorized to administer oaths, the joint recreation commission shall convene and recommend a seventh member, who shall be appointed by the mayor and sole commissioner to serve for an initial period of one year.
(e)
Officers; election. The seven appointed joint recreation commissioners shall immediately proceed to elect one of their members as president or chairman and shall elect such other officers as they deem necessary or expedient.
(f)
Vacancies. Upon expiration of the original term, each succeeding term shall be for a period of five years with the exception of the seventh member, as set forth in subsection (d) of this section. In the event of resignation, death, or inability or refusal to serve, or any other circumstances creating a vacancy, of any member of the joint recreation commission, the governing body that appointed that member shall appoint a successor for the unexpired term, with the exception of the seventh member who shall be recommended by the remaining six members of the joint recreation commission to the mayor and sole commissioner, who shall make the appointment as set out in subsection (d) of this section.
(g)
Powers and duties. The joint recreation commission shall have power to adopt bylaws, rules, and regulations for the proper conduct of recreation for the City of Cochran and the county and to provide, conduct, and supervise public playgrounds, playfields, indoor recreation centers, and other recreation facilities owned or controlled by the City of Cochran and/or the county. The joint recreation commission shall have the power to conduct any form of recreation or cultural activities that will employ the leisure time of the people in a wholesome or constructive manner, and it may conduct such activities on property under its own control, on public properties with the consent of the authorities thereof, and on private property with the consent of the owners.
(h)
Superintendent; other personnel. The joint recreation commission shall have the power to appoint or designate someone trained or properly qualified for the work to act as superintendent, to appoint or designate such other personnel as the joint recreation commission deems proper, and to provide for the compensation of the superintendent and other personnel.
(i)
Annual budget. The joint recreation commission shall submit a budget annually for the ensuing year to the governing authority of the City of Cochran and to the sole commissioner for the approval of each entity. The joint recreation commission may receive fees and revenues for the operation of recreation facilities and programs and may also solicit or receive any gifts or bequests of money or other personal property or any donations to be applied, principal or income, for either temporary or permanent use for playgrounds or recreation purposes, but if the acceptance thereof for such purposes will subject the municipality or county to additional expense for improvements, maintenance, or renewal, the acceptance of any grant or devise of real estate shall be subject to the approval of the governing body of such municipality or county. Money received for such purposes, unless otherwise provided by the terms of the gift or bequest, shall be deposited with the treasurer of the joint recreation commission to the account of the joint recreation commission or to such other body having charge of such work, at the direction of the recreation commission, and the same may be withdrawn and paid out by the joint recreation commission in the same manner as money appropriated for recreation purposes. The city council of the City of Cochran and the sole commissioner shall, as they deem necessary, appropriate funds for recreation purposes and place the funds in the account of the joint recreation commission on any equal basis.
(j)
Ex officio members. The mayor of the City of Cochran and the sole commissioner shall be ex officio members of the joint recreation commission.
(k)
Annual report. The joint recreation commission shall make an annual report to the City of Cochran governing authority and to the sole commissioner and such other reports as from time to time may be requested by the governing authorities.
(Res. of 8-16-1977)
Sec. 2-48. - Hospital authority board.
(a)
Creation. The hospital authority of the county is created and organized as set forth in this section.
(b)
Powers and duties. The hospital authority of the county shall be deemed to exercise public and essential governmental functions and shall have all of the powers necessary or convenient to carry out and effectuate the purposes and provisions of this section, including, but without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the following powers:
(1)
To sue and be sued;
(2)
To have a seal and alter the same;
(3)
To make and execute contracts and other instruments necessary to exercise the powers of the hospital authority;
(4)
To acquire, lease and operate hospital projects and to provide construction, reconstruction, improvements, alteration and repair of any such project and to lease and rent for any number of years any lands, buildings, structures or facilities in any existing or hereafter established hospital project and to establish rates and charges for the use of the services of the hospital authority;
(5)
To accept gifts, grants or devises of any property; to acquire by the exercise of the right of eminent domain any property essential to the purposes of the hospital authority;
(6)
To sell, lease, exchange, transfer, assign, pledge, mortgage or dispose of any real or personal property or interest therein;
(7)
To mortgage, pledge, assign any revenues, incomes, tolls, charges or fees received by the hospital authority; to issue certificates of indebtedness for the purpose of providing funds for carrying out the duties of the hospital authority, provided such certificates shall not extend beyond a period of 40 years from the date of issuance thereof, and provided further that such certificates shall be repayable from the revenues realized from the project or any part thereof;
(8)
To borrow money for any corporate purposes; to appoint officers, agents and employees;
(9)
To make use of any facilities afforded by the federal government or any agency or instrumentality thereof; and
(10)
To exercise any and all powers now or hereafter possessed by private corporations performing similar functions.
(c)
Board of trustees; membership. There is hereby established a board of trustees to manage the affairs of the hospital authority. The board shall consist of seven posts on the hospital authority the county, numbered one through seven, with terms staggered so that two posts expire in each successive year for a period of three years, and one office expires in the fourth successive year, as set forth below.
(1)
Posts 1 and 2. The persons filling posts 1 and 2 and all successors shall be appointed for four-year terms expiring in even-numbered years.
(2)
Posts 3 and 4. The persons filling posts 3 and 4 and all successors shall be appointed for four-year terms expiring in odd-numbered years.
(3)
Posts 5 and 6. The persons filling posts 5 and 6 and all successors shall be appointed for four-year terms expiring in even-numbered years two years after the expiration of the terms of posts 1 and 2.
(4)
Post 7. The person filling post 7 and all successors shall be appointed for a term of four years, expiring in odd-numbered years two years after the expiration of the terms of posts 3 and 4.
(d)
Vacancies. All unexpired terms for posts shall be filled only for the part of the term remaining. Any member of said authority shall be eligible for reappointment. The procedure for filling all vacancies on the board of the hospital authority, either for an unexpired or full term, shall be as follows: The sole commissioner will submit a list of three proposed persons for each vacancy on the board of the hospital authority; and the board, at its next regular meeting, shall select persons from said list to fill the vacancies that exist.
(e)
Officers. The trustees shall elect one of their members as chairman and another as vice-chairman. The trustees shall also elect a secretary and treasurer. The treasurer need not be a trustee. No trustee or other person executing certificates or other obligations on behalf of the hospital authority shall be personally liable thereon.
(Res. of 8-12-1964; Res. of 11-15-1979)
Sec. 2-49. - Joint development authority.
(a)
By joint and concurrent resolutions of the county and the City of Cochran, Georgia, there has been created and activated the public body corporate and politic known as the Bleckley-Cochran Development Authority.
(b)
The board of directors of the authority shall consist of eight members, four of whom shall be appointed by the Cochran city council and four of whom shall be appointed by the sole commissioner of Bleckley County. Each of the members of the board of directors of the authority must be a taxpayer residing in the city, in the case of members appointed by the city council, or the county, in the case of members appointed by the sole commissioner. Not more than one member appointed by the county may be a county commissioner and not more than one member appointed by the city may be a member of the city council. Directors of the authority shall serve for terms of two years each, unless they cease to be eligible prior to the expiration of such term. At the end of any term of office of any director, or upon becoming ineligible, a successor thereto shall be elected by the sole commissioner of the county or the Cochran city council, as the case may be, determined according to the entity that appointed the director whose term is expiring or otherwise terminating. A director whose term of office shall have expired shall continue to hold office until his successor shall be so elected.
(c)
The board of directors shall organize itself, carry out its duties and responsibilities, and exercise its powers and prerogatives in accordance with the terms and provisions of the Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq., as it may now exist or may be amended or modified.
(d)
The creation and activation of the authority is not intended to, and shall in no way or to any extent, impair or otherwise affect the existence, purpose, organization, powers, or function of any other industrial development or other authority previously created by constitutional amendment or act of the Georgia General Assembly, including, without limitation, the Bleckley-Cochran Industrial Development Authority, created pursuant to an amendment to the Georgia Constitution by 1966 Ga. Laws (Act No. 126), page 1042 and continued in effect by 1987 Ga. Laws (Act No. 32), page 3558.
(Ord. of 10-14-2005)

ARTICLE IV. - EMPLOYEE BENEFITS

Sec. 2-70. - Pension plan.
(a)
Adoption; elections. The county elects to become a participating employer in the Association County Commissioners of Georgia Restated Pension Plan sponsored by the Association County Commissioners of Georgia by adopting the accompanying plan and trust in full as if the employer were a signatory to that agreement; provided, however, that the county makes the following elections granted under the provisions of the plan.
(b)
Definitions; rules of construction. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Average annual compensation means the highest average of the participant's compensation over five consecutive plan years during the ten plan years immediately prior to the employee's normal retirement date, or during the ten plan years immediately prior to the employee's termination of service if the employee terminates service prior to his normal retirement date. For an employee who does not receive compensation during the period selected, the term "average annual compensation" means the average over the period he has compensation.
Compensation specifically includes the employee's total compensation according to federal tax form W-2.
Effective date. The original effective date of the plan is the first day of the employer's plan year commencing January 1, 1990.
Employee means any employee who is a participant in any retirement plan to which the county contributes and any employee of the county who is covered by a collective bargaining agreement between employee representatives and the county unless the collective bargaining agreement requires the employee to be included within the plan.
Participant means any employee of the county who meets the definition of "employee" as set forth in this subsection.
Participating group means all employees of the county excluding those other employees who are considered seasonal and/or parttime. No employee shall become a member of the participating group if the employee is currently an active participant in another pension plan to which the county contributes either directly or indirectly from county tax receipts, fines, forfeitures or any other source of funds.
Plan means the Association County Commissioners of Georgia Restated Pension Plan, which is adopted for county employees. The county is adopting this plan in substitution for and as an amendment of a previously existing retirement plan.
Plan entry date means the effective date and January 1 of every plan year.
(c)
Eligibility. Each employee shall become a participant in the plan on the plan entry date (if employed on that date) coincident with or immediately following the date he satisfies the eligibility conditions set forth herein. The employee satisfies the eligibility condition on the date on which the employee completes three years of service.
(d)
Participant nondeductible contributions. The plan does not permit participant nondeductible voluntary contributions and does not require participant contributions.
(e)
Normal retirement age. Normal retirement age under the plan is the later of the date the participant attains 65 years of age or the third anniversary of the first day of the plan year in which the participant commenced participation in the plan, but not to exceed age 70.
(f)
Normal retirement pension amount; calculation. A participant's normal retirement pension shall equal 50 percent of the participant's average annual compensation up to $6,600.00 plus one percent of the participant's excess compensation (above $6,600.00) plus $36.00, multiplied by the participant's years of service. The plan administrator shall compute a participants' normal retirement pension in the form of a straight life annuity continuing for the life of the participant.
(g)
Early retirement pension; eligibility; calculation. A participant may retire at any time prior to normal retirement age if the participant has received credit for at least three years of participation, has completed at least ten years of service, and is within five years of his normal retirement date. A participant's early retirement pension shall equal the participant's vested accrued benefit at his early retirement date, actuarially reduced for payment prior to the participant's normal retirement date.
(Ord. of 6-21-1990)
Sec. 2-71. - Disability pension plan.
(a)
Generally. The disability plan provides a disability pension, if the participant has attained age 50 years and has completed ten years of service.
(b)
Payment of disability pension. The plan permits payment of the participant's disability pension at any time after the participant terminates employment because of disability.
(Ord. of 6-21-1990)

Sec. 2-73. - Workers' compensation.
(a)
Certain elected officials of the county shall be covered under the Association County Commissioners of Georgia Group Self-Insured Workers' Compensation Fund. While performing their elected duties, the sole commissioner, sheriff, probate judge, clerk of court, magistrate judge, tax commissioner and coroner shall be covered under the worker's compensation laws of the state.
(b)
Active firefighters and rescue personnel of the county shall be covered under the Association County Commissioners of Georgia Group Self-Insured Workers' Compensation Fund, while performing their duties. For purposes of this section, the term "active" shall mean those firefighters and rescue personnel who are listed on the volunteer roles of the county fire department or the county emergency management agency and who have met the county's training criteria for emergency personnel.

ARTICLE V. - RECORDS MANAGEMENT

Sec. 2-105. - Intent; public records.
The county recognizes that its records are public records that contain information needed for varying lengths of time for the conduct of public business. The county records contain information that retains value as legal, audit, and historical evidence for varying lengths of time to protect the rights of the government and its citizens. The county records are for varying lengths of time vital for the continuity of government and for the protection of the rights of citizens. Efficient and cost-effective management of records is in the best interest of the government and of the public.
(Res. of 11-21-1996)
State Law reference— Inspection of public records, O.C.G.A. § 50-18-70; retention of public records, O.C.G.A. § 50-18-99.
Sec. 2-106. - Records coordinator.
The county clerk is hereby designated as the coordinator of the records management plan provided for herein and authorized to act for and on behalf of the county in directing and coordinating all records management matters under the Georgia Records Act and records retention schedules approved by the county.
(Res. of 11-21-1996)
Sec. 2-107. - Retention schedule; destruction of records.
Records shall be maintained in accordance with approved retention schedules. No record shall be destroyed except as authorized by a state records committee approved retention schedule.
(Res. of 11-21-1996)
