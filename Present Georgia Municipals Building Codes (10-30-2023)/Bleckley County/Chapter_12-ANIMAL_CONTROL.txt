Chapter 12 - ANIMAL CONTROL
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 12-1. - Purpose and intent.
The sole commissioner finds that there are many domesticated animals roaming and running loose in the county, including but not limited to dogs and cats, which cause hurt, inconvenience, damage, and injury to persons, other animals, and to public and private property. The county further finds that it is in the best interest of the health and welfare of the people living or visiting in the county to reduce the probability of hurt, inconvenience, damage, and injury to persons, other animals, and to public and private property.
(Ord. of 5-16-2002, § 2)
Sec. 12-2. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in articles I through V of this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Animal control officer means a person designated by the sole commissioner as a law enforcement officer or agency for the purpose of administration and enforcement of the provisions of this chapter.
Animal shelter means any facility operated by the county or under contract with the county or any other entity for the purpose of impounding or caring for animals held under the authority of this chapter.
Domesticated animal means any animal raised so as to live in close proximity to man. Such animals shall include dogs and cats as well as varieties of poultry, livestock or reptiles.
Domesticated animal at large means any domesticated animal running loose not under the restraint or control of the owner or a representative of the owner on or off the premises of the owner.
Inhumane treatment of an animal means failure, omission, or neglect to provide adequate food, water, shelter and protection from the elements to an animal.
Jurisdiction means the unincorporated area of Bleckley County, Georgia.
Owner means any person who owns, possesses, harbors, or keeps, or knowingly permits an animal to be harbored or kept or has an animal in his care, or who permits an animal to remain on or about his premises for more than seven days.
Public nuisance means any animal that unreasonably annoys humans, causes hurt, inconvenience, or damage to another, endangers the life or health of other animals or persons, or substantially interferes with the rights of citizens, other than their owners, to enjoyment of life or property. The term "public nuisance" shall mean and include, but is not limited to, any animal that:
(1)
Is found at large;
(2)
Damages the property of anyone other than its owner;
(3)
Chases vehicles;
(4)
Attacks other domestic animals;
(5)
Unprovoked, growls, threatens to do harm to, or attacks pedestrians or passersby; or
(6)
Has been found by the animal control officer to be a public nuisance animal by virtue of being a menace to the public health, welfare, or safety.
Vaccination means protection against rabies by inoculation with antirabies vaccine, given in an amount sufficient to provide immunity from rabies for a minimum of one year.
(Ord. of 5-16-2002, § 3)

ARTICLE II. - ANIMAL CONTROL OFFICER

Sec. 12-23. - Office established; appointment.
(a)
There is created the office of animal control officer, which office shall enforce the provisions of articles I through V of this chapter.
(b)
The animal control officer shall be appointed by the sole commissioner.
(c)
The City of Cochran, a municipality located within the county, has an animal control officer and animal shelter. The sole commissioner may contract with the City of Cochran for joint or separate animal control services of an animal control officer and in providing an animal shelter for impounded animals.
(Ord. of 5-16-2002, § 4(A), (D), (E))
Sec. 12-24. - Authority; duties; alternate assignment of duties.
(a)
The animal control officer is authorized to issue citations to persons violating this article for trial in the magistrate court upon such citations.
(b)
The animal control officer, as well as employees and agents of the animal control officer and persons under his control and supervision, are empowered to seize animals as required by this article so long as the animals are within the county.
(c)
These employees and agents of the animal control officer, and persons under his control and supervision, shall have the right to go on private property within the county for the purposes of enforcing the provisions of this article, provided it is necessary for such purpose.
(d)
The animal control officer shall have the authority to issue citations and subpoenas to witnesses and require their appearance in magistrate court on any matter relating to the enforcement of any of the provisions contained herein.
(e)
The sole commissioner may assign additional animal control duties to any officer or employee of the county government who is subject to the jurisdiction of the governing authority of the county.
(f)
With the consent of the sheriff, assignment of the additional duties of animal control officer to the county sheriff or to a sheriff's deputy.
(g)
With the consent of the county board of health and the rabies control officer, the sole commissioner may assign the additional duties of animal control officer to a rabies control officer appointed pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 31-19-7.
(h)
An animal control officer is authorized to make whatever inquiry is deemed necessary to ensure compliance with the provisions of this chapter. Law enforcement officers of the county shall cooperate with animal control officers in enforcing the provisions of this chapter.
(i)
A person carrying out the duties of an animal control officer shall not be authorized to make arrests unless the person is a law enforcement officer having the powers of arrest.
(Ord. of 5-16-2002, § 4(B), (C), (F)—(J))
Secs. 12-25—12-51. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - RABIES CONTROL

Sec. 12-52. - Vaccination of dogs and cats.
(a)
The owners and keepers of all cats and dogs are hereby required to have such cats and dogs vaccinated against rabies by a licensed veterinarian. No person shall own, keep or harbor any cat or dog over six months of age unless such cat or dog is vaccinated.
(b)
All cats and dogs shall be vaccinated against rabies by a licensed veterinarian in accordance with the latest edition of Compendium of Animal Rabies Vaccine and Recommendations for Immunizations, published by the National Association of State Public Health Veterinarians.
(c)
A certificate of vaccination shall be issued to the owner of each animal vaccinated.
(d)
Each owner shall also receive a durable vaccination tag indicating the year for which the certificate was issued. The tag shall be securely attached to the collar and worn continuously by the cat or dog to which it has been issued. Tags are not transferable.
(Ord. of 5-16-2002, § 5)
State Law reference— Duties of local government regarding rabies control, O.C.G.A. § 31-19-1.

ARTICLE IV. - PROHIBITED ACTS

Sec. 12-78. - Running at large; nuisance.
The following acts and omissions are prohibited:
(1)
Animals running at large. It is unlawful for any owner to cause or allow an animal to run at large in or on any public property, or private property without consent of the property owner.
(2)
Animals disturbing the peace. It is unlawful for any person to allow any animal in his possession or control to persistently or continuously bark, howl or make noise common to their species or otherwise to disturb the peace and quiet of the surrounding neighborhood. It shall likewise be unlawful to keep or maintain such animals in such a manner as to produce noxious or offensive odors, or to otherwise endanger the health and welfare of surrounding inhabitants.
(3)
Dog control. No person owning or otherwise having a dog in his care, charge, control, custody or possession shall cause, permit or allow such dog to be in or upon any public area unless such dog is under complete control and restraint of owner. Dogs are prohibited in those areas so designated and posted by the county.
(4)
Cat control. No person owning or otherwise having a cat in his care, charge, control, custody or possession shall cause, permit or allow such cat to be in or upon any public area unless such animal is under complete control and restraint of owner. Cats are prohibited in those areas so designated and posted by the county.
(5)
Failure to clean up any bodily waste or discharge from animals. It is unlawful for any owner or person in control of an animal to fail, omit, or neglect removing and cleaning up any bodily waste or discharge by such animal on public or private property of another.
(6)
Destruction of property. It shall be unlawful for any animal to destroy or damage private or public property.
(Ord. of 5-16-2002, § 6(A)(1)—(5), (B)(5))
Sec. 12-79. - Cruelty and neglect.
The following acts and omissions are prohibited:
(1)
Keeping of sick or diseased animals. It is unlawful for any person to own or any animal that is seriously sick, diseased, or injured without providing proper treatment and care for such animal. This section shall not be construed to include animals under active veterinary care or veterinary hospitals.
(2)
Putting out poison. It shall be unlawful for any person to scatter or put out or otherwise distribute on his premises within the county any poison in any form or manner whatsoever which is or may likely be sufficiently lethal or of such strength as to cause death to any animal without first obtaining a permit to do so from the office of commissioner of the county. The commissioner, in his discretion, may issue or refuse to issue such permit. If issued, no charge shall be made for the same.
(3)
Failure to provide nourishment and shelter. It shall be unlawful for any owner of a cat or dog to fail, omit, or neglect to provide for such cat or dog adequate food, water, shelter, and protection from the elements.
(4)
Inhumane treatment; abandonment. It shall be unlawful for any person to treat any cat or dog in a cruel and inhumane manner, to abandon it, or to release it upon public or private property with the intention of abandoning it.
(5)
Maiming, killing. No person, other than the animal control or other county officer in the exercise of his power and performance of his duties, shall perform a cruel act on any animal or harm, maim, or kill any animal or attempt to do so; except that a person may:
a.
Defend his person or property, or the person or property of another, from injury or damage being cause by an animal; or
b.
Kill any animal causing injury or damage to any livestock or poultry.
(6)
Animal fights. It is unlawful for any person to promote stage, hold, manage, conduct, carry one, train for, attend or own any animal for the purpose of participating in any game, exhibition, contest or fight in which one or more animals are engaged for the purpose of injuring, killing, maiming, or destroying themselves, or any other animal.
(7)
Female dogs or cats in heat; running at large. It shall be unlawful for the owner of any female dog or cat to allow the animal to run at large while in heat.
(8)
Failure to comply with vaccination requirement. It shall be unlawful for the owner of any dog or cat to fail, omit, or neglect vaccinating such animal and attaching the proper tags to such animal evidencing the vaccination as provided in section 12-52.
(Ord. of 5-16-2002, §§ 6(A)(6), (7), (B)(1)—(4), 9(E))
State Law reference— Cruelty to dogs, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-5; female dogs in heat running at large, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-6; cruelty to animals, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-4; rabies control, O.C.G.A. § 31-19-1.
Sec. 12-80. - Penalties.
(a)
For violations of any provision of section 12-78, the owner shall be subject, upon the first offense, to the issuance of a warning from the animal control officer or magistrate court, and, upon the second offense, to penalties as provided in section 1-7.
(b)
For violations of any provisions of section 12-79, the owner shall be subject to penalties as provided in section 1-7.
(Ord. of 5-16-2002, § 6)

ARTICLE V. - IMPOUNDMENT

Sec. 12-104. - Waiting period; destruction.
(a)
Any animal seized and impounded under any of the provisions of this article shall be detained for a period of five working days; and if the ownership of the animal is not determined within that period, the animal shall be disposed of in such a manner as the animal control officer may designate. Any animal seized that is wearing identification tags shall be kept for ten days and the animal control unit shall attempt to notify the owner by writing or phone if possible.
(b)
If, at any time during the required waiting period set forth in subsection (a) of this section, the owner indicates to the animal control officer his intention to not reclaim the animal, the balance of the waiting period shall be waived and the animal shall be disposed of pursuant to this section.
(c)
If, at any time during the required waiting period, examination of the animal by the animal control officer indicates the animal to be severely sick and/or to be a threat to the health of other animals detained in the impoundment, the balance of the waiting period shall be waived and the animal shall be disposed of pursuant to this section.
(d)
Nothing herein shall prevent the animal control officer from killing an animal when he determines that it is a menace to public safety or to other animals and cannot be impounded with reasonable effort.
(Ord. of 5-16-2002, § 7)
Sec. 12-105. - Fees.
The fee for impoundment of any animal under the provisions of this article shall be in the amount established from time to time by the sole commissioner.
(Ord. of 5-16-2002, § 8)
Sec. 12-106. - Unlawful interference by third parties.
(a)
No person shall, without the knowledge or consent of the owner, hold or retain possession of any animal which he does not own for more than 72 hours without first reporting the possession of such animal to the animal control officer. This section shall not apply to those nonprofit organizations whose purpose is the protection of the animals.
(b)
No person shall willfully cause an animal to be in violation of any portion of this chapter.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to attack, assault, or in any way physically or verbally threaten or interfere with the animal control officer or his agents in the performance of those duties required to enforce this article.
(d)
It shall be unlawful to tamper, destroy or take any trap that has been set by the animal control officer or his agents.
(Ord. of 5-16-2002, § 9(A)—(D))
Sec. 12-107. - Magistrate jurisdiction.
The magistrate court of the county shall have jurisdiction over all alleged violations of any portion of this chapter and shall have all powers granted to it which are necessary and incident to the hearing and disposition of matters brought before the court, such powers to include the authority to issue citations and summons for witnesses. The following persons have authority to file complaints on behalf of the county for violations of this chapter:
(1)
Animal control officer or his agent;
(2)
Other law enforcement officer;
(3)
Public health officials.
(Ord. of 5-16-2002, § 10)

ARTICLE VI. - DANGEROUS DOGS

Sec. 12-128. - Applicability.
This article applies to that portion of the county that is unincorporated and enforcement of this article shall be within the unincorporated area of the county.
(Ord. of 2-15-1990)
State Law reference— Jurisdiction of local governments regarding dangerous dog control, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-22.
Sec. 12-129. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article and article VII of this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Dangerous dog means any dog that:
(1)
Inflicts a severe injury on a human being without provocation on public or private property; or
(2)
Aggressively bites, attacks, or endangers the safety of humans without provocation after the dog has been classified as a potentially dangerous dog and after the owner has been notified of such classification.
Dog control officer means the county emergency management director whose duties specifically include enforcement of the provisions of this article.
Owner means any natural person or any legal entity, including but not limited to, a corporation, partnership, firm, or trust, owning, possessing, harboring, keeping, or having custody of a dog within the incorporated area of the county.
Potentially dangerous dog means any dog that has, without provocation, bitten a human being on public or private property.
Proper enclosure means an enclosure for keeping a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog, while on the owner's property, securely confined indoors or in a securely enclosed and locked pen, fence, or structure suitable to prevent the entry of young children and designed to prevent the dog from escaping. Any such pen or structure shall have secure sides and a secure top and, if the dog is enclosed within a fence, all sides of the fence shall be of sufficient height and the bottom of the fence shall be constructed or secured in such a manner as to prevent the dog's escape from over or under the fence. Any such enclosure shall also provide protection from the elements for the dog.
Severe injury means any physical injury that results in broken bones or disfiguring lacerations requiring multiple sutures or cosmetic surgery or a physical injury that results in death.
(Ord. of 2-15-1990)
Sec. 12-130. - Dog control officer; no arrest authority.
The county emergency management director is the dog control officer for the county with the duties as set out in O.C.G.A. §§ 4-8-20 through 4-8-30, the Georgia Dangerous Dog Control Law. The county dog control officer shall not be authorized to make arrests.
(Ord. of 2-15-1990)
State Law reference— Designation of officer; no arrest authority, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-22.
Sec. 12-131. - Notice to owner; hearing.
(a)
The dog control officer shall make such investigations and inquiries as may be necessary to identify dangerous dogs and the owners of dangerous dog within the unincorporated areas of the county.
(b)
When the dog control officer classifies a dog as a dangerous dog or reclassifies a potentially dangerous dog as a dangerous dog, the dog control officer shall notify the dog's owner in writing by certified mail sent to the owner's last known address of such classification or reclassification. Such notice shall be complete upon its mailing.
(c)
The notification to an owner that a dog has been classified as dangerous or as potentially dangerous shall contain a summary of the dog control officer's findings upon which the classification is based. The notice shall be dated and shall state that the owner has 15 days from the date shown on the notice to request a hearing before the county animal control board on the dog control officer's determination that the dog is a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog.
(d)
The notice to the owner shall also state that if a hearing is not requested, the dog control officer's determination and classification that the dog is a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog shall be final and conclusive after the date specified in the notice, which shall be the last day on which the owner has a right to request a hearing.
(e)
The notice shall include a form to request a hearing and shall provide instructions for mailing and delivering such request to the animal control board.
(Ord. of 2-15-1990)
State Law reference— Investigation, notice to owner, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-23; notice requirements, hearing, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-24.
Sec. 12-132. - Certificates of registration; requirements.
The county dog control officer shall be authorized to issue a certificate of registration for a dangerous dog or for a potentially dangerous dog to the owner of such dog upon presentation by the owner to the dog control officer of the following:
(1)
Sufficient evidence of a proper enclosure in which to confine the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog;
(2)
Sufficient evidence of the posting of the premises where the dog is located with a clearly visible sign warning that there is a dangerous dog on the property and containing a symbol designed to inform children of the presence of a dangerous dog; and
(3)
Sufficient evidence of a policy of insurance in the amount of $15,000.00 issued by an insurer authorized to transact business in the state insuring the owner of a dangerous dog against liability for any personal injuries inflicted by the dangerous dog; or a surety bond in the amount of $15,000.00 or more issued by a surety company authorized to transact business in the state payable to any person injured by the dangerous dog owned by the named owner.
(Ord. of 2-15-1990)
State Law reference— Registration, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-25.
Sec. 12-133. - Duty of owner to report.
It shall be the duty of the owner of any dangerous dog in the county to inform the dog control officer within 24 hours if the dog is loose, is unconfined, has attacked a human, has died, or has been sold or donated. If the dog has been sold or donated, the owner shall also provide the dog control officer with the name, address, and telephone number of the new owner of the dog. If the owner is moving outside the county, notice of such move shall be given to the dog control officer.
(Ord. of 2-15-1990)
Sec. 12-134. - Fees.
The fee to register a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog in the county is $50.00 per year, due and payable to the county on or before April 1 of each year.
(Ord. of 2-15-1990)

ARTICLE VII. - ANIMAL CONTROL BOARD

Sec. 12-152. - Jurisdiction; open meetings.
The animal control board of the county is established to hear and determine, pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 4-8-24, matters regarding dangerous dogs or potentially dangerous dogs that arise in the unincorporated areas of the county. Meetings of the animal control board shall be open to the public.
(Ord. of 2-15-1990)
Sec. 12-153. - Members.
The animal control board of the county shall consist of the following officers: the commissioner or commissioners of the county, the sheriff of the county, and the health environmental officer of the county.
(Ord. of 2-15-1990)
Sec. 12-154. - Clerk; attorney.
The county clerk shall serve as the clerk to the animal control board. The county attorney shall serve as the presiding officer and legal adviser to the board but shall have no vote.
(Ord. of 2-15-1990)
Sec. 12-155. - Hearings; notice; procedure.
(a)
The animal control board shall schedule a hearing within 30 days of receipt of a request for a hearing from the owner of a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog. The animal control board clerk shall notify the dog owner in writing by certified mail of the time, date, and place of the hearing. The hearing shall be open to the public. The notice to the dog owner shall be mailed at least ten days prior to the date of the hearing.
(b)
At the hearing, the dog control officer shall make an opening statement, and the dog owner or his counsel may make an opening statement. Following the opening statements, the dog control officer shall present evidence, oral, written, or otherwise, on which the classification of the dog was determined.
(c)
Following the evidentiary presentation by the dog control officer, the dog owner or his counsel shall present such evidence, oral, written, or otherwise, upon which the objection is made to the classification by the dog control officer. The animal control board shall also receive such other evidence and hear such other testimony as the board may find reasonably necessary to make its determination.
(d)
At the conclusion of the evidentiary portion of the hearing, the dog control officer shall make a final statement followed by the final statement of the dog owner or his counsel. The hearing shall then be closed for the animal control board to determine whether to sustain, modify, or overrule the dog control officer's classification of the dog.
(e)
Within ten days after the date of the hearing, the animal control board shall notify the dog owner in writing by certified mail of its determination of the matter. If such determination is that the dog is a dangerous dog or a potentially dangerous dog, the notice shall specify the date upon which the determination is effective.
(Ord. of 2-15-1990)
Sec. 12-156. - Prohibited acts.
(a)
It is unlawful in the unincorporated areas of the county for an owner of a dangerous dog to permit the dog to be outside a proper enclosure unless the dog is muzzled and restrained by a substantial chain or leash and is under the physical restraint of a responsible person. The muzzle shall be made in a manner that will not cause injury to the dog or interfere with its vision or respiration but will prevent it from biting any person.
(b)
It is unlawful in the unincorporated areas of the county for the owner of a potentially dangerous dog to permit the dog to be outside a proper enclosure unless the dog is restrained by a substantial chain or leash and is under the restraint of a responsible person.
(Ord. of 2-15-1990)
State Law reference— Muzzles and restraints, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-26.
Sec. 12-157. - Confiscation of dogs; disposition.
(a)
A dangerous dog in the unincorporated areas of the county shall be immediately confiscated by the dog control officer or by another person authorized by the dog control officer if:
(1)
The owner of the dog does not secure the liability insurance or bond required by this article and by state law;
(2)
The dog is not validly registered as required by this article and by state law;
(3)
The dog is not maintained in a property enclosure; or
(4)
The dog is outside a proper enclosure in violation of this article and state law.
(b)
A potentially dangerous dog shall be confiscated in the same manner as a dangerous dog if the potentially dangerous dog is:
(1)
Not validly registered as required by law;
(2)
Not maintained in a proper enclosure; or
(3)
Outside a proper enclosure in violation of this article and of state law.
(c)
Any dog that has been confiscated under the provisions of this article shall be returned to its owner upon the owner's compliance with the provisions of this article and upon the payment of reasonable confiscation costs as determined by the animal control officer. In the event that the owner of a dangerous dog or of a potentially dangerous dog has not complied with the provisions of this article within 20 days of the date that the dog was confiscated, the dog shall be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner by a licensed veterinarian. The cost of destruction shall be paid by the dog owner.
(Ord. of 2-15-1990)
State Law reference— Confiscation and destruction, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-27.
Sec. 12-158. - Owner unknown; notice by publication.
When the owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog is unknown, and cannot be determined after reasonable effort, the dog shall be confined by the dog control officer. Notice of the confinement shall be given by publication once a week for two consecutive weeks in the newspaper in which sheriff's advertisements are made. The notice shall include a general description of the dog, the color and gender thereof, a description of the general vicinity where the dog was found, and the telephone number to call to claim the dog. Any confined dog that has not been claimed by the 20th day after the first publication of the notice in the newspaper shall be disposed of as provided in this article.
(Ord. of 2-15-1990)
Sec. 12-159. - Liability for damages.
The owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall be solely liable for any injury to or death of a person caused by such dog.
(Ord. of 2-15-1990)
State Law reference— Liability for damages caused by vicious or dangerous animals, O.C.G.A. § 51-2-7.
Sec. 12-160. - Penalties.
Any person who violates any provision of this article and article VI of this chapter shall be subject to penalties as set forth in section 1-7.
(Ord. of 2-15-1990)
