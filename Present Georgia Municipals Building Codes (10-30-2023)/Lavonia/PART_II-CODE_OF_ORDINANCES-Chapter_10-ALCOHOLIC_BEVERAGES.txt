ARTICLE II. - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGE CONTROL
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 10-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcoholic beverage means as defined pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2(2) and includes malt beverages, wine and distilled spirits.
Dancehall or entertainment center where alcoholic beverages are consumed on the premises means any business licensed and open to the public on a regular basis as a dancehall, entertainment center or social club which offers for its regular patrons alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises, and for which a license must be obtained for the consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises.
Distilled spirits means as defined pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2(8) and includes any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume, including, but not limited to, all fortified wines.
Election day means one hour before the polls open to one hour after the polls close.
Malt beverage means as defined pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2(13) and includes fermented beverages made in whole or in part from malt, or any similar fermented beverage which contains no more than six percent of alcohol by volume.
Minor means an underage person under the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23.
Open bar means any business or establishment which derives more than 70 percent of its annual gross income from the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises.
Package means as defined pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2(16) to include a bottle, can, keg, barrel or other original consumer container.
Posts, chapters and clubs of nationally chartered veterans and fraternal organizations means those which in the case of veteran organizations are recognized as representatives of veterans by the Veterans Administration, and as to fraternal organizations, have charters from Elks, Moose or similar organizations and which have complied with section 501(A) of the United States Internal Revenue Code (26 USC 501(a)) for a tax exempt status.
Premises means the space or area owned, leased and/or controlled by the licensee and used by him for the purpose of operating under the license; except that in the case of hotels and motels, the term "premises" shall include only the portion of the property where alcoholic beverages are sold. The term "premises" is further defined as one physically identifiable place of business consisting of one room, or two or more contiguous rooms, operating under the same trade name where alcoholic beverages are sold. Any premises outlet which cannot be determined as one identifiable place of business shall require additional licenses regardless of such establishment having the same trade name, ownership or management.
Private club means an organization complying with section 501(A) of the United States Internal Revenue Code (26 USC 501(a)) for a tax exempt status. The club must have been in existence at least one year prior to the filing of its application for a license to be issued pursuant to this article; it must have at least 75 regular dues-paying members; it must own, hire or lease a building or space within a building for the reasonable use of its members, which building or space has suitable kitchen and dining room space and equipment as is staffed with a sufficient number of employees for cooking, preparing and serving meals for its members and guests; and it must have no member, officer, agent or employee directly or indirectly receiving, in the form of salary of other compensation, any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages beyond a fixed salary.
Residence means the act or fact of living or regularly staying at or in some place for the discharge of a duty or the enjoyment of a benefit or the place where one actually lives as distinguished from his domicile or place of temporary sojourn.
Resident means a person whose primary residence is within the territorial limits of the county.
Restaurant means any public place kept, used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public as a place where meals are served, and where meals are actually and regularly served, such place being provided with adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining room equipment, air conditioning, having employed therein a sufficient number and kind of employees to prepare, cook and serve suitable food for its guests. At least one meal per day shall be served at least six days a week, with the exception of holidays, vacations and periods of redecoration, and the serving of such meals shall be the principal business conducted, with the service of alcoholic beverages to be consumed on the premises as only incidental thereto. A restaurant shall provide at least 75 seats for customers.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-1))
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 10-32. - Penalty for violations.
(a)
Except as otherwise provided in this chapter, the violation of any of the provisions of this chapter shall be punishable as provided in section 1-12 and shall also subject the holder of a license issued pursuant to this chapter to suspension or revocation of such license in compliance with this chapter.
(b)
The violation of any of the provisions of this chapter by a holder of any alcoholic beverages license shall be subject to the following punishment:
(1)
First violation. A minimum fine of $500.00 and license suspension to be probated.
(2)
Second violation. A minimum fine of $1,000.00 and minimum license suspension of 30 days.
(3)
Third violation. A minimum fine of $1,500.00 and license revocation of 60 days.
(4)
Fourth violation. A minimum fine of $2,000.00 and permanent license suspension.
For the purposes of determining violation history, the rules and regulations of the state department of revenue will be utilized.
(c)
The provisions of this section are in addition to and not in lieu of other provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-2); Ord. of 4-7-2003, § I)
Sec. 10-33. - Prohibited activities.
No holder of a license issued under this article, nor any agent or employee of the licensee shall do any of the following upon the licensed premises:
(1)
Knowingly furnish or cause to be furnished or permit any other person in his employ to furnish to any minor alcoholic beverages of any kind.
(2)
Employ minors on premises where alcoholic beverages are sold. It shall be unlawful for any person to allow a minor in his employment to dispense, serve, sell or take orders for any alcoholic beverages; however, the provisions of this subsection shall not prohibit minors who are employed in supermarkets, convenience stores or drugstores from selling or handling alcoholic beverages which are sold for consumption on the premises.
(3)
Knowingly sell alcoholic beverages to any person while such person is in an intoxicated condition.
(4)
Sell alcoholic beverages upon the licensed premises on any day, or at any time, when such sale or consumption is prohibited by law.
(5)
Permit any disturbance of the peace or obscenity or any lewd, immoral or improper entertainment, conduct or practice on the licensed premises. Such misconduct shall be immediately reported to the city police department.
(6)
Sell or offer for sale alcoholic beverages by use of vending machines.
(7)
Receive or cause to be delivered at the licensed premises any alcoholic beverages by any means other than by a conveyance owned and operated by a wholesale dealer with a permit from the city to make such deliveries. Transportation of alcoholic beverages by any other means shall be grounds for revocation of all licenses concerned.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-23))
Sec. 10-34. - Hours, days of operation.
(a)
The hours of sale of alcoholic beverages are as follows:
(1)
Retail sales not for consumption on premises, 7:00 a.m. to 12:00 midnight, Monday through Saturday, except where contrary to state regulations for the sale of alcoholic beverages.
(2)
Consumption on the premises, 8:00 a.m. through 12:00 midnight, Monday through Saturday, except where contrary to state regulations for the sale of alcoholic beverages.
(b)
No alcoholic beverages shall be sold on Sundays or Christmas Day.
(c)
The sale of alcoholic beverages at any other time is prohibited.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-24))
Sec. 10-35. - Food sales by consumption on the premises licensees.
(a)
Except as otherwise provided all consumption on the premises license holders in the city shall maintain at least 60 percent of the immediate preceding 12-month gross income in the restaurant from the sale of food. Accounting records verified by the license holder as accurate shall be available for inspection by the city on a quarterly basis. Additionally, and except as otherwise provided, all consumption on the premises license holders as defined in this chapter shall maintain at least 75 seats for customers and shall be a dance hall or entertainment center where alcoholic beverages are consumed on the premises; a post-chapter entity of a nationally charted veterans and fraternal organizations; a private club, or a restaurant as defined in this chapter.
The city may examine the records of the premises license under this subsection at any reasonable time to ascertain and verify that the requirements of this section and other provisions of the alcoholic beverages ordinance of the city are being met.
(b)
Within 120 days after having been issued a consumption on the premises license, the licensee shall submit to the city a statement from a certified public accountant or a registered public accountant that the income requirements set forth in subsection (a) of this section have been met for the first 90 days after the applicant received the license for on the premises consumption. Thereafter at the time of license renewal, each consumption on the premises license holder shall submit to the city an annual statement from a certified public accountant or a registered public accountant that the income requirements set forth in subsection (a) of this section have been met.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-25); Ord. of 12-16-2002, § I; Ord. of 6-6-2011, § I)
Sec. 10-36. - Failure to furnish required report.
Upon failure of any person selling alcoholic beverages to furnish promptly to the city when due any report required by the ordinances of the city or to pay any tax due the city for the sale of such commodities, there shall be assessed a penalty of ten percent of the amount of tax due or that shown due by any such report. The penalty shall be in addition to any such tax.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-26))
Sec. 10-37. - Storage.
All alcoholic beverages shall be stored only on the premises for which a license required by this article is issued.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-27))
Sec. 10-38. - Sunday business.
All licensees under this article who open their place of business on Sunday for the sale of merchandise other than alcoholic beverages shall cover alcoholic beverages within the view of the public so that the alcoholic beverages cannot be seen on Sunday.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-28))
Sec. 10-39. - False representation of age.
It shall be unlawful for any minor to falsely misrepresent his age in any manner whatsoever, in violation of this article or other applicable law.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-29))
Sec. 10-40. - Minors.
It shall be unlawful for any minor to purchase, drink or possess any alcoholic beverage in the city.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-30))
Sec. 10-41. - Inspection.
Premises operated under the provisions of this article shall be open to inspection by authorized personnel of the city at all times.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-31))
Sec. 10-42. - Happy hour.
There will be no designated hours advertised as happy hour allowed in establishments licensed to sell alcoholic beverages by the drink.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-32))
Sec. 10-43. - Open alcoholic beverages; open bars.
(a)
Open containers of alcohol.
(1)
Opened alcoholic beverages shall not be allowed in public access areas in the city.
(2)
Public access areas as used herein is defined to include all city parking places, all public property owned by the city, any private property owned and patrolled by the city pursuant to an agreement with the city and the private owner, all public parks, all public recreational facilities, and all public sidewalks.
(3)
An opened alcoholic beverage as used herein is defined as any alcoholic beverage in which the seal is broken or cap removed or which is open to be accessible for consumption, to include alcoholic beverages poured in cups, containers, glasses, mugs, steins, or any other open alcoholic beverage.
(4)
A violation of this section is punishable as provided by the Code of Ordinances of the City of Lavonia.
(b)
Open bars. There shall be no license issued under this article for an open bar in the city.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-33); Ord. of 7-5-2005(1), § I)
Sec. 10-44. - Reporting and acting on violations.
(a)
Any violation of this article that may warrant revocation of a license issued under this article shall be reported by any person to the city which shall have the power in its discretion to suspend the license or permit for a period of not to exceed ten days pending a hearing before the city council on the question of whether or not the license or permit shall be revoked for cause, in compliance with this article.
(b)
Upon a charge that there has been a violation of this article, the clerk is authorized to order the place of business charged closed, pending a hearing on the charge.
(c)
The chief of police, or any police department shift commander on duty at the time, shall have the authority to close any location holding a license issued under this article for a period not in excess of 24 hours in the event there shall occur on the premises any violation of law or municipal ordinance, breach of the peace, disturbance or altercation resulting in violence, or other occurrence which is contrary to the public health, welfare, safety or morals, when in the judgment of the chief of police, or such shift commander, such action is necessary to protect the public health, welfare, safety or morals.
(d)
The city council shall have the right to suspend any alcoholic beverage license pending the hearing provided for in this section where, in the judgment of the city council, such action is necessary to protect the public health, safety, welfare and morals.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-3))
Sec. 10-45. - Exception.
(a)
The requirements of this chapter shall not apply to a hotel or motel not required to be licensed pursuant to this chapter holding an event for business development purposes at which malt beverages or wine are served, without charge, for guests of the hotel or motel.
(b)
Provided the exception described in subsection (a) is subject to the following limitations:
(1)
There is a four-hour per day limitation on business development events;
(2)
There will be no alcoholic beverages served for business development on Sunday;
(3)
All alcoholic beverages served for business development must be purchased from a duly licensed alcoholic beverage licensee doing business in the City of Lavonia; and
(4)
There shall be imposed on the hotel or motel an annual license fee of $100.00 per year or any part of a year and the license shall be for only a calendar year (January 1—December 31).
(Ord. of 2-2-2009, § I)

Sec. 10-71. - Classification of licenses.
Alcoholic beverage licenses shall be classified as follows:
(1)
Malt beverages, package.
(2)
Wine, package.
(3)
Distilled spirits, package.
(4)
Malt beverages, consumption on the premises.
(5)
Wine, consumption on the premises.
(6)
Distilled spirits, consumption on the premises.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-4))
Sec. 10-72. - License declared privilege.
Notwithstanding anything in this division to the contrary, the sale of alcoholic beverages in the city is a privilege, and not a right, and the issuance of a license under this division shall not create any property rights in the license holder.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-5))
Sec. 10-73. - Application for license; disclosure of interest.
(a)
All applications for a license under this division shall be in the name of the owner of the business seeking the application. If the owner is a corporation, partnership or association, the application shall also contain the names of all officers, partners or associates who own any interest in the business, and the name of the principle resident managing officer and registered agent. The application shall show that the license is for the use of the owner and the owner shall be named. If the owner of the premises is someone other than the applicant, the name of the owner of the premises shall be listed and the applicant shall show proof that the owner of the premises has been given notice of the applicant's intent to apply for an alcoholic beverage license.
(b)
No person shall have, own or enjoy any ownership, interest in, share in the profits from, or otherwise participate in the business of any alcoholic beverages licensee in the city unless a full description of such interest shall have been furnished to the city council at the time such interest arose. It shall be the duty of the licensee to report to the council, within five days, any change in any interest in such licensee's business including, but not limited to:
(1)
Any division of the profits.
(2)
Any division of net or gross sales for any purpose whatsoever.
(3)
Any change in the payment of rents or leases.
(4)
Any change in the ownership of any lease or building or land used in such business.
(5)
Any change in the ownership of any corporation that has any interest in such business or the change of management of such corporation.
(c)
After receipt of the notice referred to in subsection (a) of this section, the council shall notify such person within a reasonable time of any objection to the ownership or interest set forth therein, and it shall then be incumbent upon such person to dispose of such interest within 30 days after the mailing of the notice to do so by the council to such person at his address as shown on such notice of interest. If such interest is not disposed of within 30 days after the mailing of the notice by the council, or if no objections are filed within such 30-day period, or if such person fails to notify the council of the transfer or any such interest within 20 days after the acquisition thereof, then the license as provided for in this division may be revoked by the council until disposition of the interest has been made or notice given to the council of the transfer.
(d)
The report required by this section shall be in the form required by the council. It shall be an amendment to the licensee's permanent license application, which is on file with the council and, as such, shall be under oath and verified as otherwise required of license applications.
(e)
Whenever there is any application for an alcoholic beverage license, which application is the initial application following a suspension or revocation of a previously held alcoholic beverage license, the city shall call for a notice to be published describing the name of the applicant, the location of the business premises, and giving the time and date when the application will be considered. This notice must be published at least seven days prior to the application being considered. This notice does not pertain to the annual renewal of licenses previously granted by the city council. The license applicant shall be required to pay to the city the current sum as the cost of publication.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-6))
Sec. 10-74. - Application contents and terms.
(a)
All applications for licenses to sell alcoholic beverages shall be made in person by the applicant, and in writing on forms furnished by the city, signed by the applicant in compliance with this division and shall contain, but not be restricted to, the following statements and information:
(1)
The name, age, address and length of residency of the applicant.
(2)
The name, location and description of the premises or place of business which is to be operated under such license.
(3)
A statement of whether the applicant, or any person with an interest in the application, has made application at any previous time for any distilled spirits, malt beverage or wine license and the disposition of such application.
(4)
Whether the applicant, or any person with an interest in the application, has ever been convicted of a crime other than for misdemeanor traffic violations.
(5)
Whether a previous license issued to the applicant, or to any person with an interest in the application, has been revoked by any state or subdivision thereof or by the federal government and the reason therefor.
(6)
Whether any other person is to be interested directly or indirectly in the profits or losses, or both, of the proposed business.
(b)
All new applications for alcoholic beverage licenses shall be accompanied by a check or cashier's check, payable to the city, for the proper amount of the license fee. If the license is refused, such fee shall be returned to the applicant.
(c)
All applications for the renewal of alcoholic beverage licenses shall be filed with the city prior to November 15. Payment of license fees for the renewal of licenses may be made at the time of application or by December 31 and, upon approval of renewal and payment, a license will be issued.
(d)
The making of any untrue or misleading statement in the application for a license shall be sufficient cause for the refusal, suspension, revocation or cancellation of such license, as the city council shall deem proper.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-7))
Sec. 10-75. - Minimum age of applicant.
Every applicant for a city alcoholic beverage license shall be at least 21 years of age.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-8))
Sec. 10-76. - Investigation of applicant.
Upon receipt by the city of any application for an alcoholic beverage license or for the transfer of any license, the chief of police shall investigate, and shall attach thereto, the entire police record of every person named in such application.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-9))
Sec. 10-77. - Fingerprinting.
Every applicant for a license under this division, or for a transfer of such license, shall be fingerprinted by the police department of the city before such license is issued pursuant to the authority of O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-10))
Sec. 10-78. - Statement of employees.
The city council may, in its discretion, require a personal statement of any employee of any licensed establishment for the licensing purposes of this division.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-11))
Sec. 10-79. - License fees.
(a)
The applicant for a malt beverages package license shall pay to the city clerk, at the time his application is submitted, the sum referred to in section 2-191 as an annual license fee for operation for one year at one location or business where the products will be sold.
(b)
At the time his application is submitted, the applicant for an on the premises malt beverages consumption license shall, in addition to all other fees, pay to the clerk the sum referred to in section 2-191 as an annual license fee for the sale of malt beverages, when such sales are by the drink. Malt beverages may be sold in posts, chapters and clubs of nationally chartered veterans and fraternal organizations for such annual fee.
(c)
At the time his application is submitted, the applicant for a wine package license shall pay to the clerk of the city, as an annual license fee, the sum referred to in section 2-191 for operation for one year at one location or business where the products will be sold.
(d)
At the time his application is submitted, the applicant for an on the premises wine consumption license shall, in addition to all other fees, pay to the clerk the sum referred to in section 2-191 as an annual license fee for the sale of wine, when such sales are by the drink. Wine may be sold in posts, chapters and clubs of nationally chartered veterans and fraternal organizations for such fee.
(e)
The applicant for a distilled spirits package license shall pay to the clerk of the city, at the time his application is submitted, the sum referred to in section 2-191 as an annual license fee for operation for one year at one location or business where the products are sold.
(f)
At the time his application is submitted, the applicant for a distilled spirits consumption license shall, in addition to all other fees, pay to the clerk the sum referred to in section 2-191 for an annual license fee for the sale of distilled spirits when such sales are by the drink. Distilled spirits may be sold in posts, chapters and clubs of nationally chartered veterans and fraternal organizations for a fee referred to in section 2-191.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-12))
Sec. 10-80. - Proration of fee.
An applicant for any license under this division who makes application at any time after January 1 and before July 1 shall pay the full annual license fee. An applicant for any license under this division who makes application on or after July 1 shall pay half the annual license fee.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-13))
Sec. 10-81. - Performance bond.
In addition to the requirements set forth in this division, all persons obtaining a distilled spirits package license or a license for the consumption of distilled spirits on the premises shall post a performance bond in the amount of their total annual license fee for distilled spirits to ensure compliance with the provisions of this division.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-14))
Sec. 10-82. - Duration and renewal.
The city license issued pursuant to this division shall be valid only for the calendar year indicated thereon and shall expire at 12:00 midnight on December 31 of the year in which issued. A licensee that desires to continue in business during the next calendar year must make a renewal application, on such forms as prescribed by the city, for such year on or before November 15 of the preceding year. The payment of license fees for renewal of licenses may be made at the time of application or by December 31 and the license will be issued upon approval or renewal by the council and payment of the required fee.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-15))
Sec. 10-83. - Limitation on issuance; fees nonrefundable.
No license required by this division shall be issued for less than one calendar year, or remainder thereof, subject to the provisions of section 10-82, and, in case of the revocation or surrender of such license before the expiration of such calendar year, the holder thereof shall not be entitled to receive any refund.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-16))
Sec. 10-84. - Termination of business.
All licenses issued pursuant to this division shall be valid only so long as the premises is actively engaged in such business, with the exception of holidays, vacations and periods of redecoration. If the licensee shall cease to be actively engaged in such business, the license shall be invalid and the licensee of such business shall immediately notify the city council and return his license thereto.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-17))
Sec. 10-85. - Transfer.
(a)
No city license issued under this division may be transferred from one person to another or from one location to another without permission and approval of the city council upon written application made.
(b)
Any licensee desiring to discontinue business at one location and commence business at some other location must make a complete new application for such new business.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-18))
Sec. 10-86. - Denial of license.
(a)
Where contrary to the public interest and welfare, no license to sell alcoholic beverages shall be issued by the city council for:
(1)
Any person, as determined by the city council by reason of his business experience, financial standing, trade associations, personal associations, record of arrest or reputation in any community in which he has resided, who is not likely to maintain the operation for which he is seeking a license in conformity with federal, state or local laws, rules and regulations.
(2)
Any person who has been convicted under any federal, state or local law of any felony involving moral turpitude.
(3)
Any person convicted under any federal, state or local law of a misdemeanor, particularly, but not limited to, those involving alcoholic beverages, gambling or tax law violations, if such conviction tends to indicate that the applicant will not maintain the operation for which he is seeking a license in conformity with federal, state or local laws, rules and regulations.
(4)
A location that, in the judgment and discretion of the city council, is not suitable because of traffic congestion, general character of the neighborhood, or by reason of the effect which such an establishment would have on the adjacent and surrounding properties, or on the neighborhood.
(5)
A location within an area where, in the judgment of the city council, the number of alcoholic beverages licenses already granted makes it contrary to the public interest or welfare.
(6)
A location at which the operation of the proposed business would be in violation of the city Code or any state or federal law or regulation.
(7)
A location at which a previous alcoholic beverages license has been revoked or suspended, and where, in the judgment of the city council, the problems which have arisen from operation of such a license at such location indicate that it is not in the interest of public health, safety, welfare or morals that the sale of alcoholic beverages be permitted at such location.
(8)
Which the granting of such license would constitute a violation of state law or regulations.
(b)
No person, association, partnership, corporation or other entity which operates a sexually oriented business as defined under chapter 26, article IV, or which is subject to or licensed under chapter 26, article IV, shall, after the effective date of the ordinance from which this chapter is derived, sell, distribute, serve or suffer the consumption or possession of any intoxicating liquor, beer or wine or controlled substance upon the premises of any sexually oriented business.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-19))
Sec. 10-87. - Grounds for suspension and/or revocation.
The following shall be grounds for the suspension and/or revocation of an alcoholic beverage license:
(1)
The violation by the license holder or his employee of any state or federal law or regulation, except misdemeanors or any ordinance of the city, other than misdemeanor traffic violations. The determination of whether any such violation has occurred shall be made by the city council and an actual conviction in a court for such offense shall not be necessary in order to suspend or revoke the license.
(2)
The failure of the license holder or his employee to promptly report to the police department any violation of law or municipal ordinance, breach of the peace, disturbance or altercation resulting in violence occurring on the premises.
(3)
Any conduct on the part of the license holder or his employee contrary to the public welfare, safety, health or morals.
(4)
Operating or conducting the business in a manner contrary to the public welfare, safety, health or morals, or in such a manner as to constitute a nuisance.
(5)
The violation of any state law or regulation or municipal ordinance pertaining to alcoholic beverages.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-20))
Sec. 10-88. - Notice of denial, suspension or revocation; hearing.
Before the city council shall deny any application for a license required by this division, or for the transfer of any such license, or shall suspend or revoke any existing license, the applicant or licensee, as the case may be, shall be given written notice from the city to show cause before the city council at a place and time specified therein which shall not be less than three days nor more than ten days of service of the notice, why such application for license or for transfer license should be denied, or why such license should not be suspended or revoked, and stating the grounds therefor. At the appointed time and place, the applicant or licensee shall have an opportunity to show cause, if any exist, why such application should not be denied or such license suspended or revoked. At the show cause hearing, the applicant shall be afforded an opportunity to present evidence, to cross examine witnesses, to retain counsel, and such other due process rights as may be afforded under applicable state or federal law. After the conclusion of the show cause hearing, the city council shall inform the applicant in writing of its decision on the application within ten days.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-21))
Sec. 10-89. - Products offered for sale restricted.
No retail dealer who has a license for the package sale of distilled spirits shall sell or offer for sale or display or keep in stock at his place of business where distilled spirits are offered for sale any other product of commodity except the following:
(1)
Wines, only if the retail dealer holds a valid and current license to sell wine at such place of business.
(2)
Malt beverages or beer, only if the retail dealer holds a valid and current license to sell malt beverages or beer at such place of business.
(3)
Tobacco products.
(4)
Beverages containing no alcohol and commonly used to dilute distilled spirits.
(5)
Ice sold in sealed containers only.

Paper, styrofoam or plastic cups.

DIVISION 3. - EXCISE TAXES

FOOTNOTE(S):
--- (3) ---
Cross reference— Finance, § 2-161 et seq.; taxation, ch. 86.
Sec. 10-111. - Rate of taxation—Malt beverages.
In addition to the annual license fees imposed and required in this article, there is levied and imposed upon each wholesale dealer selling malt beverages within the city an excise tax on malt beverages so sold, as follows:
(1)
Except as provided in subsection (2) of this section, such excise tax shall be computed at the rate of $0.004166 per ounce.
(2)
All malt beverages, sold in or from a barrel or bulk container and being commonly known as tap or draft beer, shall not be subject to the excise tax provided for in subsection (1) of this section, but there is imposed upon each wholesale dealer selling such malt beverages within the city an excise tax of $6.00, for each barrel or bulk container having a capacity of 15½ gallons sold by such wholesale dealer within the city and at a like rate for fractional parts thereof.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-34))
Sec. 10-112. - Same—Wine.
In addition to the annual license fees imposed and required in this article, there is levied an excise tax computed at the rate of $0.22 per wine liter, which shall be paid to the city on all wine sold, displayed or stored in the city. Such tax shall be prorated on miniatures, half pints, pints, fifths, half gallons and other quantities and on wine gallons so that each bottle shall be taxed on the basis of $0.22 per wine liter.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-35))
Sec. 10-113. - Same—Distilled spirits by the drink.
There is levied and imposed an excise tax upon the sale of distilled spirits by the drink in the amount of three percent of the charge for the beverage to the public.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-36))
Sec. 10-114. - Same—Package sale of distilled spirits.
There is levied and imposed an excise tax on all package sales of distilled spirits in the amount of $0.22 per liter.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-37))
Sec. 10-115. - Method of payment.
The tax levied by this division shall be paid to the city by each wholesale dealer on all beverages coming under this article sold within the city by each wholesale dealer selling, shipping or in any way delivering malt beverages or wine to a retailer in the city, who shall collect the excise tax at the time of delivery, and shall remit the same together with a summary of all such deliveries on or before the tenth day of the month next succeeding the calendar month in which such sales were made.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-38))
Sec. 10-116. - Payment of tax; report.
The summary report made by each wholesale dealer to the city shall show the exact quantities of taxed beverages, by size and type of container and the amount of excise tax collected.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-39))
Sec. 10-117. - Right of audit.
The city shall have the right to audit and to require the production of records from each wholesaler supplying retailers in the city and each retailer so supplied.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-40))
Sec. 10-118. - Failure to make timely report; penalty.
(a)
The failure to make a timely report and remittance shall render a wholesale dealer liable for a penalty equal to ten percent of the total amount during the first 30-day period following the date such report and remittance were due and a further penalty of five percent of the amount of such remittance for each successive 30-day period, or any portion thereof, during which such report and remittance are not filed. The filing of a false or fraudulent report shall render the wholesale dealer making such report liable for a penalty equal to 25 percent of the amount of the remittance which would be required under an accurate and truthful report.
(b)
Such failure to make a timely report or remittance, or the filing of a false or fraudulent report, shall also constitute grounds for the revocation of the business license issued by the city to the wholesale dealer.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-41))
Sec. 10-119. - Sale prohibited when tax not paid.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell at retail or otherwise within the city any beverage taxed by this division on which the required tax has not been paid to the wholesaler or distributor or the city.
(Ord. of 3-5-2002, § 1(1-42))
