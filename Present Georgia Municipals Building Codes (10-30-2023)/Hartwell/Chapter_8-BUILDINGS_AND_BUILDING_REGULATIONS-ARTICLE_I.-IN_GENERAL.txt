ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 8-1. - Definitions.
For the purposes of this chapter, terms used in this chapter shall be defined as set forth in the city zoning regulations (chapter 42), unless otherwise defined by the codes adopted in this chapter.
(Comp. 1976, § 20-106; Code 1989, § 5-1; Ord. of 8-13-1984, § 5)
Sec. 8-2. - Fire district established.
An official fire district is hereby established, to comprise the following area:
All of that area included inside a point of beginning at the center of the intersections of the rights-of-way of Webb Street and West Franklin Street continuing eastward along West Franklin Street and Carolina Street; then north to West Johnson Street and Carolina Street; then east to Carter Street and East Johnson Street; then south to Benson Street and Carter Street; then west on Railroad Street and west on Alvins Alley to the corner of Alvins Alley and Webb Street; thence north to the point of beginning at West Franklin Street and Webb Street. This district boundary may be modified from time to time and shall be further described by maps available to the public and found in the building inspector's office.
(Comp. 1976, § 20-106; Code 1989, § 5-5; Ord. of 8-13-1984, § 4)
Sec. 8-3. - Conflicts.
In the event of a conflict between the city's technical codes, any provision of this chapter, or any provision of any other chapter of this Code the most stringent standard shall govern. In the event of a conflict between a general and specific provision of the city's technical codes and any provision of this Code, the specific shall prevail over the general.
(Comp. 1976, §§ 20-106—20-111; Code 1989, § 5-6; Ord. of 8-13-1984, § 9)

The International Property Maintenance Code adopted in this subsection 8-4(a)(10) is amended by the replacement of section 302.4 with the following:
Sec. 302.4. Weeds. All premises and exterior property shall be maintained free from w eeds or plant grow th in excess of eight (8) inches. All noxious weeds shall be prohibited. Weeds shall be defined as all grasses, annual plants and vegetation, other than trees or shrubs: provided, however. this term shall not include cultivated flowers and gardens.
Upon failure of the owner or agent having charge of a property to cut and destroy weeds after service of a notice of violation, they shall be subject to prosecution in accordance with Section 106.3 and as prescribed by the authority having jurisdiction. Upon failure to comply with the notice of violation, any duly authorized employee of the jurisdiction or contractor hired by the jurisdiction shall be authorized to enter upon the property in violation and cut and destroy the weeds growing thereon, and the costs of such removal shall be paid by the owner or agent responsible for the property. The City shall also have the right, as provided in Section 106.3, to file a lien against the property, in the name of the owner, for the costs of any such cutting, destruction, and removed.
(b)
It is the city's intent at all times to enforce the latest edition of each and every code adopted by the city in this section, as such code may be adopted, amended, revised or supplemented from time to time by the state board of community affairs, and all such amendments and subsequent editions and revisions shall be deemed applicable to and enforceable in the city as notice of the adoption of such amended and revised codes by the state department of community affairs is received by appropriate city officials, and as each such notice is received by city officials, each new code edition, amendment, revision or supplement shall be held to be in full force and effect as if it were the original adopted code described in this section.
(Ord. No. 2012-04, § 1, 11-5-2012; Ord. No. 2020-01, § 1, 3-2-2020)
Sec. 8-5. - Building official to determine requirements not covered by codes.
Any requirements necessary for the strength, stability or proper operation of an existing or proposed building, structure, electrical, gas, mechanical or plumbing system, or for the public safety, health and general welfare, not specifically covered by this Code or other technical codes shall be determined by the building official.
