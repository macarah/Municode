ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 36-1. - Access to premises; grant of easements.
(a)
Duly authorized agents of the city shall have access at all hours to the premises of the consumer for the purpose of installing or removing city property, inspecting piping, reading and testing meters, or for any other purpose in connection with the water, sewer, and gas service and its facilities.
(b)
Extensions to the system shall be made only when the consumer shall grant or convey, or shall cause to be granted or conveyed, to the city a permanent easement or right-of-way across any property traversed by the water, sewer, and gas lines.
(Comp. 1976, § 10-120; Code 1989, § 10-7; Ord. of 3-7-1983, § 7)
Sec. 36-2. - Search warrants.
If the city manager or any other city official designated by the city manager has been refused access to a building, structure or property, or any part thereof, and is able to demonstrate probable cause to believe that there may be a violation of this article, or that there is a need to inspect or sample as part of a routine inspection and sampling program of the city designed to verify compliance with this article or any permit or order issued hereunder, or to protect the overall public health, safety and welfare of the community, the city manager may seek issuance of a search warrant from the Hartwell Municipal Court.
(Code 1989, § 10-85; Ord. of 10-2-1995, § 7.2)
Sec. 36-3. - Disconnection of services.
(a)
In addition to disconnection of services for nonpayment as provided in this chapter, the city reserves the right to discontinue water, sewer or natural gas service, or any combination thereof, without notice for the following reasons:
(1)
To prevent fraud or abuse.
(2)
In response to the consumer's willful disregard of the city's rules.
(3)
Emergency repairs.
(4)
Insufficiency of supply due to circumstances beyond the city's control.
(5)
Legal processes.
(6)
Direction of public authorities.
(7)
Strike, riot, fire, flood or unavoidable accident.
(b)
Re-establishing service following disconnection to prevent fraud or abuse or due to willful disregard of the city's rules shall require payment of the same reconnection fee applicable to disconnections for nonpayment. Re-establishing service following disconnection for any other reason may, at the city council's discretion, be subject to a reconnection fee.
(Comp. 1976, § 10-120; Code 1989, § 10-10; Ord. of 3-7-1983, § 10)
Sec. 36-4. - Notice of service interruptions.
Under normal conditions the consumer will be notified of any anticipated interruptions of service by the city.
(Comp. 1976, § 10-120; Code 1989, § 10-5; Ord. of 3-7-1983, § 5)
