ARTICLE IV. - FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS

Sec. 10-134. - Annual business license tax levied.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93, there is hereby levied for each year an annual business license tax upon state and national banking associations, federal savings and loan associations and state building and loan associations, at the rate of 0.25 percent of the gross receipts of the institutions.
(b)
As used in this section, the term "gross receipts" means gross receipts as defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.
(c)
Notwithstanding any other provisions of this chapter, the minimum amount of the business license tax due from any depository financial institution pursuant to this section shall be $1,000.00 per year.
(Comp. 1976, § 3-105-A; Code 1989, § 11-19; Ord. of 11-7-1983(1), § 1)
Sec. 10-135. - Return required; payment due date.
(a)
Each depository financial institution within the city shall file a return of its gross receipts with the city on March 1 of the year following the year in which the gross receipts are measured.
(b)
The returns shall be in the manner and in the form prescribed by the commissioner of the department of banking and shall be based upon the allocation method set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(d).
(c)
The tax assessed in this section shall be calculated and collected based upon the information provided in the return.
(d)
The due date of the taxes shall be April 1 of each year.
(Comp. 1976, § 3-105-A; Code 1989, § 11-20; Ord. of 11-7-1983(1), § 2)
