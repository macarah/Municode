ARTICLE X. - BILLIARD ROOMS

Sec. 10-401. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Billiard room means and refers to any public place where a person is permitted to play the game of billiards and for which a charge is made for use of equipment.
Billiards means and refers to any of the several games played on a table surrounded by an elastic ledge of cushions with balls which are impelled by a cue and shall include all forms of the game known as "carom billiards," "pocket billiards," and "English billiards."
Owner means and refers to any person owning or having the care, control, management or possession of any billiard room in the corporate limits of the city.
(Ord. No. 2001-010, § 6(11-47A), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 10-402. - Permit required.
No person shall maintain or operate a billiard room without first obtaining a permit issued under the terms and conditions of this article.
(Ord. No. 2001-010, § 6(11-47B), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 10-403. - Application for permit.
An applicant for a permit under the provisions of this article shall file with the city manager or designee a written application on a form provided for that purpose which shall be signed by the applicant, who shall be the owner of the business sought to be licensed. A separate application shall be filed for each location sought to be permitted. The following information shall be required in the application:
(1)
Name, address and telephone number of the applicant, including the trade name by which applicant does business and the street address of the premises, and if incorporated, the name registered with the secretary of state;
(2)
Name, address and telephone number of the operator of the premises to be permitted;
(3)
Number of billiard tables in the premises to be permitted;
(4)
Whether a previous license of applicant, or if applicable, corporate officer of applicant, has been revoked within two years of filing of the application; and
(5)
A statement that all the facts contained in the application are true.
(Ord. No. 2001-010, § 6(11-47C), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 10-404. - Nature of permit.
(a)
A permit issued under this article expires December 31 of each year unless it is rescinded or canceled earlier.
(b)
A permit issued under this article is effective for a single place of business only.
(c)
A permit issued under this article vests no property right in the permittee except to maintain and operate a billiard room in accordance with the terms and conditions of this article.
(Ord. No. 2001-010, § 6(11-47D), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 10-405. - Permit fees.
(a)
Every person who owns, controls, manages, or operates a billiard room in the corporate limits of the city shall pay for, and there is hereby levied on, each such billiard room, a permit fee per billiard table in an amount as established from time to time by city council resolution and shown in the city's fee schedule (but tables which constitute coin operated amusement machines shall not be counted for purposes of this section). The fee for issuing a replacement permit for a lost, destroyed or mutilated permit is as established from time to time by city council resolution and shown in the city's fee schedule.
(b)
Fees for permits granted in the second half of the licensing year shall be reduced by one-half from the fee required by subsection (a) of this section. No other proration or reduction shall be allowed, regardless of the date on which such permit is applied for.
(Ord. No. 2001-010, § 6(11-47E), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 10-406. - Display of permit.
A permit issued under this article shall be displayed at or near the entrance of the business premises, and such display shall be permanent and conspicuous.
(Ord. No. 2001-010, § 6(11-47F), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 10-407. - Refusal to issue or renew permit.
The city clerk shall refuse to approve issuance or renewal of a permit for one or more of the following reasons:
(1)
A false statement as to a material matter made in an application for a permit;
(2)
Revocation of a permit, pursuant to this article, of the applicant or corporate officer of the applicant within two years preceding the filing of the application;
(3)
The applicant for such permit has, within the past ten years, been convicted of a crime involving moral turpitude;
(4)
The game room will be in a location which does not comply with section 10-408.
(Ord. No. 2001-010, § 6(11-47G), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 10-408. - Restrictions on the location of billiard rooms.
(a)
A billiard room which either sells alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises or engages in package sales of such beverages or allows alcoholic beverages to be consumed on the premises shall not be located:
(1)
Within 1,000 feet of the property line of a private residential dwelling;
(2)
Within 750 feet of the property line of any public library or branch thereof;
(3)
Within 1,000 feet from the property line of any church, shrine, chapel, mortuary or other place used exclusively for religious purposes;
(4)
Within 1,000 feet of the property line of any school or college campus;
(5)
Within 750 feet of a regular stop where a school bus for the transportation of school children in the public schools of the county shall board or discharge passengers;
(6)
Where the proposed location is 1½ miles or less from an existing location for which the city has issued a game room license; or
(7)
Within 750 feet of a location for which a license to sell beer or wine has been issued by the city, the county, or any other governmental jurisdiction.
(b)
The distance requirements set forth in subsection (a) of this section shall apply in any and all directions from the property lines of the proposed location of the game room, as measured in a straight line. Notwithstanding any provision of this section, nothing in this article shall be applied in such as manner as to prohibit the operation of a billiard room in a particular location if such billiard room is fully in operation in such location on the date the ordinance from which this article is derived became effective.
(Ord. No. 2001-010, § 6(11-47H), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 10-409. - Effect of indebtedness to city on permit.
The city manager or designee shall not issue or renew a permit under this article and shall suspend or cancel a permit if it is determined that the applicant or permittee is indebted to the city for any fee, cost, penalty, or delinquent tax.
(Ord. No. 2001-010, § 6(11-47H), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 10-410. - Replacement of permit.
A replacement permit may be issued for a lost, destroyed, or mutilated permit upon application on a form provided by the city clerk. A replacement permit shall have the word "REPLACEMENT" stamped across its face and shall bear the same number as the one it replaces.
(Ord. No. 2001-010, § 6(11-47I), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 10-411. - Transferability of permit.
A permit issued under the provisions of this article shall not be assignable or transferable.
(Ord. No. 2001-010, § 6(11-47J), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 10-412. - Cancellation of permit.
If any person who owns, operates, manages, or controls any billiard room in the corporate limits of the city shall violate any provision of this article, the city manager shall have the power and authority to cancel all permits issued under this article to any of the foregoing by giving written notice, stating the reason justifying such cancellation, and the same shall be canceled ten days from such date of notice. No permit shall be issued within a period of one year to anyone whose permit has been canceled, except at the discretion of the governing body. If the permit of a person owning, operating, managing, or controlling any billiard room in the corporate limits of the city is canceled, such person shall not be permitted to own, operate, manage or control a billiard room until a new permit is granted.
(Ord. No. 2001-010, § 6(11-47K), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 10-413. - Appeal from refusal to issue or renew permit or canceling permit.
If the city manager refuses to approve the issuance of a permit or the renewal of a permit to an applicant or permittee or cancels a permit, the action is final unless the applicant or permittee, within ten days of the receipt of written notice of the action, files a written appeal with the recorder of the municipal court, who shall, within ten days after the appeal is filed, consider all the evidence in support of or against the action appealed, and render a decision, either sustaining or reversing the action. If the recorder of the municipal court sustains the action, the applicant or permittee may, within ten days of that decision, file a written appeal with the city manager to the governing body setting forth specific grounds for the appeal. The governing body shall, within 30 days, grant a hearing to consider the action. The governing body has authority to sustain, reverse, or modify the action appealed. The decision of the governing body is final.
(Ord. No. 2001-010, § 6(11-47L), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 10-414. - Responsibility of permittee.
The permittee under this article shall not permit any of the following on the premises of any billiard room for which such person holds a permit:
(1)
The sale, purchase, possession or consumption of any alcoholic beverages as the same are defined by the laws of the state; provided, however, that if such billiard room is an establishment which is authorized to sell alcoholic beverages and derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross revenues from the sale of products or services other than alcoholic beverages, this prohibition shall not apply; and provided further, however, that if alcoholic beverages are sold by the drink for consumption on the premises of a billiard room, then the sale, serving or use of alcoholic beverages therein shall be prohibited unless the establishment derives at least 75 percent of its revenue from the sale of products or services other than alcoholic beverages;
(2)
The sale, purchase, possession or use of any drugs, including heroin, cocaine, marijuana, opium, morphine, codeine and any drugs that are cited under O.C.G.A. § 16-13-20 et seq., known as the Controlled Substances Act;
(3)
Any boisterous conduct;
(4)
Any loitering;
(5)
A person 16 years or younger currently enrolled in primary or secondary school to frequent a billiard room until after such person is released from school each day during a school term, except on Saturdays and school holidays;
(6)
Any gambling;
(7)
Any animals, except Seeing Eye dogs;
(8)
Any person under the influence of intoxicating liquors or drugs;
(9)
Any soliciting;
(10)
Any credit;
(11)
The operation of a billiard room by someone other than an adult 21 years or older in attendance at all times.
(Ord. No. 2001-010, § 6(11-47M), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 10-415. - Hours.
A billiard room can be open from 6:00 a.m. to 12:00 midnight Monday through Saturday, except that school-age children shall be required to vacate the premises at 11:00 p.m. Monday through Thursday. This section shall not be construed so as to prohibit the operation of a business, which includes a billiard room, outside the hours set forth herein, as long as the billiard room is not operated during the prohibited hours, and as long as the operation of such business is not otherwise prohibited under some other provision of this Code.
(Ord. No. 2001-010, § 6(11-47N), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 10-416. - Inspections.
(a)
The premises on which a billiard room is located shall conform to all building and fire prevention codes of the city and the fire inspectors of the city and such fire inspectors' assistants and the building inspector may enter into the premises where such billiard room is located at any time during normal business hours for the purpose of inspecting such premises for fire hazards.
(b)
All law enforcement personnel of the city shall have the right to enter into the premises of permittee at any time during normal business hours for the purpose of enforcement of the terms of this article.
(c)
With respect to any permittee which also sells alcoholic beverages on the premises of a billiard room or any place operated in connection therewith, the city manager or his or her designee, and other appropriate city officials shall be entitled to inspect any and all financial records of such permittee to establish that such permittee derives the required percentage of its revenue from the sale of products or services other than alcoholic beverages.
(Ord. No. 2001-010, § 6(11-47O), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 10-417. - Existing laws; violations not authorized.
Nothing herein shall be construed or have the effect to license, permit, authorize or legalize any machine, device, billiard table, the keeping, exhibition, operation, display or maintenance of which is now illegal or in violation of any ordinance of the city, of the penal code of the state, or the constitution of the state.
(Ord. No. 2001-010, § 6(11-47P), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 10-418. - Return for taxes.
Each operator shall return on the city tax return, every machine located in such operator's establishment on January 1 of each year for city taxes. Such return must be made between January 1 and March 31 of each year.
(Ord. No. 2001-010, § 6(11-47Q), 8-6-2001)
Sec. 10-419. - Exclusions.
This article shall not apply to billiard tables or billiard rooms operated by private industrial concerns, Young Men's Christian Associations, religious orders, charitable institutions, state, county or city institutions, fraternal orders, or bona fide clubs using such tables for members or employees only.
(Ord. No. 2001-010, § 6(11-47R), 8-6-2001)
