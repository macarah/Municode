ARTICLE II. - PROPERTY TAXES[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Ad valorem taxation of property, O.C.G.A. § 48-5-1 et seq.; municipal taxation, O.C.G.A. § 48-5-350 et seq.

Sec. 110-27. - Returns; required, date, assessment.
All taxable property shall be returned for taxes to the city manager on or before April 1 in each year, and the return shall include all property held, owned or controlled in the city on January 1 in the year in which the return is made; provided, however, that no return is required of a taxpayer whose property is the same as that taxed in the preceding year by the city. All property, whether returned or not, shall be assessed by the Burke County Board of Assessors from the best information obtainable.
(Code 1974, § 8-21; Code 1995, § 98-27)
State Law reference— Time for making tax returns, O.C.G.A. § 48-5-18; municipal board of tax assessors, O.C.G.A. § 48-5-353.
Sec. 110-28. - Fixing tax rate.
(a)
Date. At such time the tax digest is made available by the county, or as soon thereafter as practicable, in each year, the city council shall prescribe and fix the rate of taxation for the current year.
(b)
Meeting; report; submitting digest. It shall be the duty of the city manager to make a report to a meeting of the city council called for the purpose of fixing the rate of taxation for the current year. The tax digest shall show the gross amount and value of property entered therein for taxation for the current year. At such meeting, the city council shall proceed to fix and declare the rate of taxation as provided in this article.
(Code 1974, §§ 8-22, 8-23; Code 1995, § 98-28)
Sec. 110-29. - Due and delinquent dates.
All taxes levied as provided in this article shall be considered due on receipt of the bill and delinquent after 60 days from the date of receipt of the tax bill.
(Code 1974, § 8-24; Code 1995, § 98-29)
Sec. 110-30. - Tax executions; issuing, enforcement.
Should any person owing taxes to the city fail or refuse to pay such taxes when required to do so, the city manager shall issue execution for such taxes, after the expiration of ten days from such refusal, which execution shall be directed to the chief of police of the city, and all and singular the sheriffs, deputy sheriffs and constables of the state, which execution shall be enforced by levy and sale under the same rules governing sheriffs' sales.
(Code 1974, § 8-30; Code 1995, § 98-30)
State Law reference— Tax executions, O.C.G.A. § 48-3-1 et seq.

ARTICLE III. - HOTEL/MOTEL TAX[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— Local taxation of rooms, lodgings or accommodations, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-50 et seq.

Sec. 110-45. - Excise tax levied.
Pursuant to the provisions of an act of the general assembly authorizing each municipality in the State of Georgia to impose, levy and collect an excise tax upon the furnishing for value to the public of any room or rooms, lodgings or accommodations furnished by any person or legal entity licensed by or required to pay business or occupation taxes to the municipality for operating a hotel, motel, inn, lodge, tourist camp, cabin or any other place in which rooms, lodgings or accommodations are regularly furnished for value, provided no such tax shall be levied upon the sale or charges for any rooms, lodgings or accommodations furnished for periods of more than 30 consecutive days, or for the use of meeting rooms, there is hereby levied an excise tax in the amount of five percent of the charges to the public for such furnishing of any room or rooms, lodgings or accommodation within the city.
(Ord. No. 2006-03-01, § 1, 4-24-2006; Ord. No. 2017-08-01, § 2, 8-21-2017)
State Law reference— Room tax authorized, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51 et seq.
Sec. 110-46. - Forms to city manager.
Each person, firm, or corporation collecting said tax shall on or before the 20th day of each month transmit to the city manager or a designated representative upon forms as prescribed by the city returns showing the total charges for the furnishing of such rooms, lodging or accommodations as provided for herein during the preceding calendar month and shall account for and remit the tax levied herein.
(Ord. No. 2006-03-01, § 2, 4-24-2006)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-53.2.
Sec. 110-47. - Allowable taxes.
Each person, firm or corporation collecting said tax as provided for herein shall be allowed three percent of the tax due and accounted for and remitted to the city in the form of a deduction in submitting the report and paying the amount due provided said amount due is not delinquent at the time of payment.
(Ord. No. 2006-03-01, § 3, 4-24-2006)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-52.
Sec. 110-48. - Penalties.
(a)
When any person, firm or corporation liable hereunder shall fail to make any return or pay the full amount of the tax required herein, there shall be imposed a specific penalty to be added to the tax in the amount of five percent or $5.00, whichever is greater, for each additional 30 days, or fraction thereof, during which the failure continues, not to exceed 25 percent or $25.00 in the aggregate, whichever is greater; provided, however, if such failure is due to providential cause shown to the satisfaction of the city manager in affidavit form attached to the return and remittance within ten days of due date, such returns may be accepted exclusive of penalties and interest. In the case of a false or fraudulent return or the failure to file a return, where willful intent exists to defraud the city of any tax due herein, a specific penalty of 50 percent of the tax due shall be assessed.
(b)
When any person, firm or corporation fails to remit the tax, or any portion thereof, on or before the day when such tax shall be required by law to be paid, there shall be added to the amount due interest at the rate provided for executions from the date until paid.
(c)
All penalties and interest imposed herein shall be payable and collectible by the city in the manner as if they were a part of the tax imposed.
(Ord. No. 2006-03-01, § 4, 4-24-2006)
Sec. 110-49. - Revenues budgeted and applied for promotion of city.
All revenues raised pursuant to said tax shall be budgeted and applied for the promotion of the City of Waynesboro in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(a)(3)(A).
(Ord. No. 2006-03-01, § 5, 4-24-2006)
