Sec. 70-1. - Wetlands protection for certain area.
The mayor and city council hereby resolve to protect all wetlands located within the Waynesboro Industrial Park from development or disturbance, to include all phases of its development. Tap-ons for any new development within jurisdictional wetlands located within the above-stated growth corridor are hereby prohibited.
(Code 1995, § 66-1; Ord. No. 92-2, 3-17-1992)
Sec. 70-2. - Prerequisites to establishment of wells and private water systems.
The city's public water system provides potable water. Private wells may not be established within the city limits for potable water purposes, but may be established for other specific purposes. The following guidelines shall govern the application for and approval of a permit to drill a private well.
(1)
A building permit to drill a private well shall be applied for and upon approval issued.
(2)
In addition to a building permit, electrical and plumbing permits shall be required. Costs of these permits shall be based upon the then current fee schedule for the applicable codes.
(3)
A private well shall meet all location requirements of the city zoning ordinance.
(4)
Well construction shall comply with Georgia Department of Natural Resources and Burke County Health Department requirements.
(5)
A backflow preventor, approved by the city, shall be installed at the meter on the customer's side of the water service.
(6)
The water system fed by the well shall not be connected to the city's water system at any time. The owner or occupant of the property shall continue to use the city's water system as its only source of potable water. The property owner is directly responsible for the prevention of any crossover connection.
(7)
Water from the well system shall not be discharged into the city's sanitary sewerage system.
(8)
Each well system shall be made available for immediate inspection by the city at any reasonable time and without prior notice. An annual inspection shall be made by the city building inspector/code enforcement officer, who shall charge an inspection fee, as established by council, of $25.00 per year.
(Code 1995, § 66-2; Ord. No. 1056, § I, 6-7-1982)
