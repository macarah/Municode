DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-136. - Board of Commissioners meetings.
The regular monthly workshop and regular monthly meeting of the Dade County Commission shall be held in the Dade County Administration Building, Trenton, Georgia, on the first Thursday of every month, beginning at 6:00 p.m. The regular monthly meeting shall immediately follow the conclusion of the monthly workshop.
(Res. No. R-21-10, 9-2-2010)

DIVISION 2. - HOUSING AUTHORITY

Sec. 2-156. - Created.
Pursuant to the Housing Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 8-3-1 et seq., there is created in and for the county a housing authority.
(Res. of 11-7-1985)
Sec. 2-157. - Composition; appointment.
The housing authority shall be composed of five (5) commissioners who shall be residents of the county appointed by resolution of the board of commissioners. If a commissioner at any time ceases to be a resident of the county, his term of office shall end.
(Res. of 11-7-1985)
Sec. 2-158. - Terms.
The commissioners appointed to the housing authority shall serve for terms of five (5) years, provided the initial commissioners shall be appointed in such a fashion that such terms shall be staggered annually. If at the end of any term of office of any commissioner a successor thereto shall not have been elected, the commissioner whose term of office shall have expired shall continue to hold office until his successor shall be so elected.
(Res. of 11-7-1985)
Sec. 2-159. - Powers.
The housing authority shall have the powers granted to it under state law to issue revenue bonds for the purpose of providing funds to finance housing in the county.
(Res. of 11-7-1985)
Sec. 2-160. - Compensation; expenses.
No member of the housing authority shall receive any compensation for serving as a commissioner, but may be reimbursed for his actual expenses incurred in the performance of his duties.
(Res. of 11-7-1985)
Secs. 2-161—2-180. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY

Sec. 2-181. - Created.
Pursuant to the Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-62-1 et seq., there is created in and for the county a development authority.
(Res. of 6-11-1980)
Sec. 2-182. - Composition; appointment.
The development authority shall be composed of seven (7) directors who shall be taxpayers of the county and one (1) of whom is an officer or employee of the county appointed by resolution of the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 6-11-1980)
Sec. 2-183. - Terms.
The directors appointed to the development authority shall serve for terms of six (6) years, provided the initial directors shall be appointed in such a fashion that such terms shall be staggered. If at the end of any term of office of any director a successor thereto shall not have been elected, the director whose term of office shall have expired shall continue to hold office until his successor shall be so elected.
(Res. of 6-11-1980)
Sec. 2-184. - Powers.
The development authority shall have the powers granted to it under state law to issue revenue bonds for the purpose of developing and promoting for the public good and general welfare trade, commerce, industry and employment opportunities in the county, thereby promoting the general welfare of the citizenry.
(Res. of 6-11-1980)

DIVISION 4. - AREA PLANNING COMMISSION

Sec. 2-206. - Membership.
The Coosa Valley Area Planning and Development Commission shall consist of two (2) members from each of the counties participating in the area planning commission.
(Res. of 11-15-1960, § 1)
Sec. 2-207. - Appointment.
Of the two (2) members of the area planning commission from each county, one (1) shall be appointed by the governing authority of the municipality wherein the county seat is located and the other shall be appointed by the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 11-15-1960, § 1)
Sec. 2-208. - Term of office.
The term of office for all members of the area planning commission shall be five (5) years, except that the initial appointment by this county shall be for a term of three (3) years.
(Res. of 11-15-1960, § 2)
Sec. 2-209. - Filling of vacancies.
Any vacancy occurring in the membership of the area planning commission shall be filled for the unexpired term by the governing authority making the appointment which has become vacant.
(Res. of 11-15-1960, § 2)
Sec. 2-210. - Removal.
The appointing authority shall have the power to remove any member of the area planning commission it may appoint for cause, on written charges, after a public hearing.
(Res. of 11-15-1960, § 2)
Sec. 2-211. - Compensation.
All members of the area planning commission shall serve without compensation.
(Res. of 11-15-1960, § 2)
Sec. 2-212. - Organization.
The area planning commission shall adopt rules for the transaction of business and shall keep a record of its resolutions, transactions, findings and determinations, which record shall be a public record.
(Res. of 11-15-1960, § 3)
Sec. 2-213. - Officers.
The area planning commission shall elect a chairman and vice-chairman from among its appointive members. The term of the office of the chairman and vice-chairman shall be for one (1) year or until reelected or their successors are elected. The area planning commission shall appoint a secretary who may be an employee of the commission.
(Res. of 11-15-1960, § 3)
Sec. 2-214. - Meetings.
The area planning commission shall meet at least once each month at the call of the chairman and at such other times as the chairman or board of commissioners may determine.
(Res. of 11-15-1960, § 3)
Sec. 2-215. - Employees and staff.
The area planning commission may appoint such employees and staff as it may deem necessary for its work.
(Res. of 11-15-1960, § 3)
Sec. 2-216. - Interagency cooperation; expenditures.
In the performance of its duties, the area planning commission may cooperate with, contract with or accept funds from federal, state or local public or semipublic agencies or private individuals or corporations, may spend such funds and may carry out such cooperative undertakings and contracts. The commission may make expenditures for the purchase of required equipment and supplies. The expenditures of the commission, exclusive of gifts to the commission and contract receipts, shall be within the amounts appropriated for the purpose by the participating governing authorities.
(Res. of 11-15-1960, § 3)
Sec. 2-217. - Powers; duties.
It shall be the function and duty of the area planning commission to make such careful and comprehensive surveys and studies of existing conditions and probable future developments and to prepare such plans for physical, social and economic growth as will best promote the public health, safety, morals, convenience, prosperity or the general welfare as well as efficiency and economy in the development of the entire northwest Georgia area and each political subdivision therein. In general, the area planning commission shall have such powers, duties and responsibilities as set forth in this division.
(Res. of 11-15-1960, § 4)
