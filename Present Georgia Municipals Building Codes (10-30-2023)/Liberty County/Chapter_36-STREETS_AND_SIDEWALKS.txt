ARTICLE II. - ROAD AND DITCH PROTECTION

Sec. 36-25. - General limits on heavy vehicles.
Heavy laden vehicles shall not proceed routinely over the county's unimproved road system during periods of rain or inclement weather when clearly said travel causes or has caused excessive damage to the roadway such that road conditions for the passage of smaller and lighter passenger vehicles has been substantially hampered.
(Code 1987, § 15.5-51; Ord. of 9-4-1990, § 1)
Sec. 36-26. - Penalty for violations.
Failure of any person to comply with any of the provisions of this article shall constitute a misdemeanor, and be punished according to section 1-8.
(Code 1987, § 15.5-53; Ord. of 9-4-1990, § 3)
Sec. 36-27. - Administration and enforcement.
The magistrate court of the county shall have jurisdiction over violations of this article and all procedures for enforcement of this article shall be as provided in O.C.G.A. Title 15, Chapter 10, Article 4 (O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq.). Complaints of violations of any provision of this article shall be brought before the magistrate court on a citation issued by a designated representative of the county board of commissioners or the chief magistrate of the county and shall be prosecuted through the court. Violations of this article will be tried upon citation either with or without a prosecuting attorney as determined by the county board of commissioners. Service of citation shall be performed by the constable of the magistrate court of the county. Citations shall meet all of the requirements as specified in the O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63 as amended.
(Code 1987, § 15.5-54; Ord. of 9-4-1990, § 4)

Sec. 36-58. - Purpose and intent.
This article is intended to require notice of timber harvesting operations within the unincorporated areas of Liberty County pursuant to O.C.G.A. §12-6-24, and, in accordance with Title 32 of the Official Code of Georgia, provide regulations further governing use of public roads by persons engaged in said timber harvesting operations in order to assure the public safety and to protect the county roadways and environment.
(Ord. No. 2021-003, § I, 6-17-2021)
Sec. 36-59. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
County administrator means the county administrator for Liberty County, or his or her designee.
Notice means the notice required to be given to the public works department by the timber harvester as provided herein.
Person means any natural person, firm, corporation, limited liability company, partnership, or other legal entity of whatever form.
Public works department means the Liberty County Public Works Department.
Public works director means the Director of the Liberty County Public Works Department.
Surety bond means a valid surety bond, executed by a surety corporation authorized to transact business in the State of Georgia, protecting the County against any damage caused by such person or firm in an amount specified by the governing authority of Liberty County not exceeding $5,000.00 or, at the option of the timber harvester, a valid irrevocable letter of credit issued by a bank or savings and loan association, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 7-1-4, in the amount of an in lieu of such bond. Any such surety bond or letter of credit shall be in form prescribed by or acceptable to the county administrator and shall be for the calendar year in which delivered; provided that no more than one bond or letter of credit shall be required from each timber harvester, regardless of the number of tracts harvested in the county for so long as the bond or letter of credit remains in effect. The bond or letter of credit required herein shall be valid only for the calendar year in which it was delivered.
Timber means softwood or hardwood trees harvested for commercial purposes.
Timber harvester means any person harvesting standing timber in any unincorporated area of Liberty County, Georgia for delivery as pulpwood, logs, poles, posts, or wood chips to any woodyard or processing plant located inside or outside the State of Georgia.
(Ord. No. 2021-003, § I, 6-17-2021)
Sec. 36-60. - Notice required.
(a)
Written notice shall be required of any timber harvester harvesting timber in the unincorporated areas of the County for each separate tract to be harvested. Said written notice shall be (a) delivered to the public works department prior to entering onto the subject tract, if possible, but in no event later than 24 hours after entering onto said tract; and (b) in such form and containing such information as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 12-6-24(b)(1). Notice shall be effective only as to the tract described therein upon receipt by the public works department (as confirmed by written acknowledgment from said public works department to the timber harvester) and compliance with all other applicable requirements of this article. Further, within 24 hours following completion of timber harvesting operations, the responsible timber harvester shall give notice of cessation of cutting to the public works director or other designee of the county administrator. Such notice may be given in person, by transmission of an electronic record via telefacsimile, e-mail, or such other means as approved by the governing authority of the county, or by mail.
(b)
Upon notification published by the Director of the Georgia Forestry Commission that a state-wide notification website or platform is available for public use, persons wishing to utilize said website or platform to provide the notice required by this article may do so at their option, and the county will accept notifications submitted in this manner. On and after a date specified and published by the Director of the Georgia Forestry Commission, use of the aforesaid state-wide notification website or platform shall be mandatory and shall be the sole means of providing the notice required by this article. On and after said date, submission of the notice by any of the means listed in this section shall cease and will no longer be deemed acceptable or in compliance with this article.
(c)
At all times during any timber harvesting operations subject to this article, the timber harvester shall additionally ensure that the requisite notice is continuously posted and displayed in good condition and in a conspicuous location on each tract of land identified in said notice. For purposes of this section, the notice shall be deemed posted in a "conspicuous location" if clearly visible and located in the immediate vicinity where the entrance road or trail providing vehicular access to the subject tract meets the nearest public right-of-way.
(Ord. No. 2021-003, § I, 6-17-2021)
Sec. 36-61. - Requirement of surety bond.
(a)
A surety bond shall not be required prior to notice becoming effective. However, following the giving of any notice, in the event that the public works director determines that any activities relating to the timber harvesting are being conducted in a manner that poses likelihood of damage to any public rights-of-way or danger to the public safety, the public works department shall be authorized to demand a surety bond from the timber harvester. The surety bond shall be delivered to the public works department within three business days following receipt of written demand for the same, unless within said time a written notice of appeal is received by the public works department contesting the requirement of the surety bond. In the event of such contest, the public works department shall, no later than five business days, schedule an administrative hearing in such matter before the county administrator, who shall deliver a decision within three business days of said hearing. The decision of the county administrator shall be conclusive and binding for all purposes. Following any contest as provided above, all timber harvesting authorized pursuant to the applicable notice shall be suspended until the surety bond, if any, is delivered to the public works department as required by the county administrator.
(b)
The bond or letter of credit required by this section shall protect the county against any damage requiring re-ditching or repair of existing ditch structures or the removal of any harvesting residue, including tree tops, debris, logs, pulpwood and other materials, placed in or around the county's rights-of-way caused by such person or firm tendering the bond or letter of credit. The proceeds of such bond or letter of credit shall be available to reimburse the county for any cost incurred to repair such damages or remove such debris in or around the county's rights-of-way. The proceeds of such bond or letter of credit shall also be available to reimburse the county for any costs incurred to maintain or repair county roads damaged by the ingress or egress of motor vehicles engaged in the harvest operations located within 500 feet of any point of ingress or egress of the timber harvesting operation.
(c)
In the event a bond or letter of credit tendered pursuant to the requirements of this article is revoked by the surety or bank, then a valid replacement bond or letter of credit must be delivered by the timber harvester to the public works department within five business days after the date of revocation in order for timber harvesting operations to continue. In addition, if the timber harvester caused its revocation, the amount of the bond or letter of credit required shall be increased to $7,500.00 after the first revocation, and $10,000.00 after a second revocation caused by the person or firm tendering the bond or letter of credit. The maximum amount of the bond or letter of credit shall not exceed $10,000.00.
(d)
In no event shall the delivery of a surety bond (or the failure to require a surety bond) limit the liability of any person engaged in timber harvesting and related activities for damages to public property or rights-of-way or for other personal injury or property damage, and all such persons shall be and remain at all times fully liable to the county for any and all such injury and damage. Furthermore, the right of the county to call such bond or letter of credit in accordance with the provisions of this article shall be in addition to any other remedies available to the county at law or in equity for damage to county roads or rights-of-way.
(Ord. No. 2021-003, § I, 6-17-2021)
Sec. 36-62. - Claim on surety bond.
(a)
When damage results from a timber harvester's activities, the public works director or such other designee of the county administrator shall make and provide a written claim to the timber harvester causing the damage within 30 business days after the public works director becomes aware of the damage. Such claim may be given in person, by telefacsimile, email or mail. The claim shall describe the damage in detail and, in compliance with subsection (b) immediately below, give the timber harvester the opportunity to repair such damage within 30 days of the notification; provided, however, the county shall be authorized to repair the damage immediately if the public works director or other designee of the county administrator determines that the conditions present a threat to public safety, health or welfare and, upon making such repairs, shall present to the timber harvester and the issuer of the applicable bond or letter of credit an itemized list of expenses incurred as a claim against the timber harvester and the issuer of its bond or letter of credit. Upon the issuance of a claim as provided in this section the public works director or other designee of the county administrator shall notify the issuer of the bond or letter of credit that a claim has been made and will be resolved or adjudicated according to the terms of this article.
(b)
Within 30 days of receipt of the written claim described in subsection (a) immediately above, the timber harvester against whom the claim is submitted may:
(1)
Repair such damage at his or its own expense with the approval and supervision of the public works director or other designee of the county administrator. When repairs are completed to the satisfaction of the public works director or other designee of the county administrator, the public works director or other designee of the county administrator shall provide a written notification of satisfactory completion within five business days to the timber harvester and to the surety issuing the bond or the bank issuing the letter of credit, thereby terminating the claim.
(2)
In the event of inclement weather or other factors preventing repair of the damage, request a 30 day extension to repair the damage from the public works director or other designee of the county administrator who provided written notice of the claim, provided that no extensions shall exceed 90 days from the date the claim was tendered. Approval of any extension shall be at the discretion of the public works director or other designee of the county administrator.
(3)
Appeal the claim to the Magistrate Court of Liberty County. Any such appeal must name the issuer of the bond or letter of credit as a party, who shall be served with all pleadings in the action and shall have the right to appear. The magistrate court will hear evidence and arguments within 30 days of the written appeal and issue a ruling within ten days of such hearing. Any such appeal shall toll the 30-day period, or any extension thereof, required by subsection (b)(2) above. If the magistrate court rules in favor of the timber harvester against whom the claim was made, the county shall have no right to recover any proceeds of the bond or letter of credit, and judgment shall be entered against the county. If the magistrate court rules in favor of the county, the court shall determine the amount of damages to which the county is entitled to recover and enter judgment accordingly; the county shall be authorized to call the bond or letter of credit and recover from the proceeds thereof an amount equal to the judgment entered by the court, up to the total amount of the bond or letter of credit. The portion of any judgment entered in favor of the county that exceeds the amount of the bond or letter of credit shall be subject to collection by any additional remedies at law or equity.
(c)
In the event the timber harvester against whom the claim has been submitted fails to take any of the actions allowed under subsection (b) above within the time required therein, said timber harvester shall be deemed to have waived any and all rights to contest the call of the bond or letter of credit.
(Ord. No. 2021-003, § I, 6-17-2021)
Sec. 36-63. - Culverts.
Prior to commencing timber harvesting operations, the public works director shall inspect the point of access to the public road from the tract described in the notice in order to determine its suitability. If a culvert is required at the point of access, it shall be promptly installed by the timber harvester as directed by the public works director. In the event that a timber harvester elects to commence operations prior to inspection by the public works director (and prior to the installation of any needed culvert), the timber harvester shall deliver the surety bond required by this article prior to commencing timber harvesting operations, in which case notice shall not be or remain effective for such timber harvesting operations until delivery of said surety bond as required by this article.
(Ord. No. 2021-003, § I, 6-17-2021)
Sec. 36-64. - Signs
Prior to commencing any timber harvesting operations, the timber harvester shall post two signs in each direction from the point of access onto the involved public road. One sign at a distance of 1,000 feet from the point of access stating "Warning: Logging Operations Ahead," and one sign at a distance of 500 feet stating "Slow Truck Entering Highway." All signs shall be 36 inches × 36 inches, erected in a diamond shape, painted orange with black legend and posted at least three feet from the travel surface of the roadway. Prior to and at all times during any timber harvesting operations subject to this article, the timber harvester shall additionally ensure that the requisite notice is continuously posted and displayed in good condition and in a conspicuous location on each tract of land identified in said notice. For purposes of this Section, the notice shall be deemed posted in a "conspicuous location" if clearly visible and located in the immediate vicinity where the entrance road or trail providing vehicular access to the subject tract meets the nearest public right-of-way.
(Ord. No. 2021-003, § I, 6-17-2021)
Sec. 36-65. - Roadway safety.
During the period of timber harvesting operations, the timber harvester shall comply with the following:
(1)
All harvesting operations shall be conducted on the tract identified in the notice and off public roads and rights-of-way. Logging and skidding of logs on public roads and rights-of-way are strictly prohibited.
(2)
Ditches constituting a part of the public drainage system or otherwise benefitting a public right-of-way shall be kept clear of all debris and residue at all times to permit proper drainage.
(3)
There shall be no blockage of public roads or rights-of-way at the access site, and all public roads and rights-of-way must remain open and kept free of debris and residue at all times.
(4)
No equipment, trucks, trailers, or other personal property shall be parked or located on any public road or right-of-way.
(5)
Mud shall not be carried onto paved public roads by logging vehicles. Where necessary, a temporary construction exit of gravel shall be maintained by the timber harvester to eliminate any hazard to traffic or unsightly conditions on the public right-of-way.
(6)
During periods of rain or inclement weather, logging vehicles shall not be operated over the unimproved public roads when such travel causes sufficient damage to the road system that the passage of smaller and lighter vehicles is hampered.
(Ord. No. 2021-003, § I, 6-17-2021)
Sec. 36-66. - Enforcement.
(a)
Upon notice from the public works director or other designee of the county administrator, work on any project that is being done contrary to the provisions of this article shall be immediately stopped. Such notice shall be in writing and shall be given to the timber harvester and the owner of the subject property, and shall state the conditions under which work may resume. When an emergency exists, written notice shall not be required.
(b)
If the conditions are not brought into compliance within five business days of the notice, the public works department shall be authorized to accomplish the work (with costs thereof to be reimbursed by the timber harvester), and any violation of the provisions of this article shall be punished as for a misdemeanor.
(Ord. No. 2021-003, § I, 6-17-2021)
Sec. 36-67. - No fee or permit; consultation with county.
Consistent with O.C.G.A. § 12-6-24:
(a)
No fee shall be charged to provide and submit the notice required by this article,
(b)
No permit, including a driveway permit, shall be required of the person or firm engaged in a timber harvesting operation as defined by said Code section; provided that timber harvesters may be asked to consult with the public works department for the purpose of minimizing damage to the county's roads, rights-of-way and infrastructure, and are urged to follow recommendations from county officials. In the event that a timber harvester elects not to follow any recommendation made by the public works department, the timber harvester shall deliver the surety bond required by this article prior to commencing timber harvesting operations, in which case notice shall not be or remain effective for such timber harvesting operations until delivery of said surety bond as required by this article. Notwithstanding the forgoing, timber harvesters bear ultimate responsibility for their actions, and nothing in this article shall preclude the county from taking any and all legal action necessary to protect its property and the health, safety and welfare of its citizens.
(c)
This article applies to activities which qualify as forestry land management practices or agricultural operations under O.C.G.A. § 12-7-17 (5) and (6) on land that is zoned for or used for forestry, silvicultural or agricultural purposes. It shall not authorize land disturbing activities incidental to development in conflict with the limitations set forth in O.C.G.A. § 12-7-17(6).
(Ord. No. 2021-003, § I, 6-17-2021)
Sec. 36-68. - Prosecution of violations.
(a)
Any person violating any of the provisions of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor prosecutable in the Magistrate Court of Liberty County and punishable by a fine not to exceed $500.00 for each violation and imprisonment in the county jail for a period of not longer than 12 months; provided that violation of the notice requirements of this article shall be punishable by a fine not exceeding $1,500.00.
(b)
The public works director or other designee of the county administrator shall be authorized to issue citations in order to initiate such prosecution.
(c)
The imposition of any fine or other sentence pursuant to this section shall in no way limit the liability of any person for damage done to public property or public rights-of-way, and the county shall be authorized to additionally pursue recovery for any such damage by any and all means permitted by law or in equity, to include, without limitation, the filing of a suit for damages, or the seeking of injunctive relief to stop any such damage.
(Ord. No. 2021-003, § I, 6-17-2021)
