ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 30-1. - Grievance procedure for handicapped persons.
(a)
The board of commissioners hereby adopts an internal grievance procedure providing for the resolution of complaints alleging any action prohibited by the office of revenue sharing's regulations implementing section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973, as amended. Section 504 states, in part, that "no otherwise qualified handicapped individual shall, solely by reason of such person's handicap, be excluded from the participation in, be denied the benefits of, or be subjected to discrimination under any program or activity receiving financial assistance …. "
(b)
Complaints should be addressed to the county.
(1)
A complaint shall be filed in writing or verbally, contain the name and address of the person filing it, and briefly describe the alleged violation of the regulations.
(2)
A complaint should be filed within ten days after the complainant becomes aware of the alleged violation.
(3)
An investigation, if appropriate, shall follow a filing of a complaint. The investigation will be conducted by the handicap coordinator or such coordinator's designated appointee. These rules contemplate informal, but thorough, investigations, affording all interested persons and their representatives, if any, an opportunity to submit evidence relevant to a complaint. Under federal law, the board of commissioners need not process complaints from applicants for employment or from applicants for admission to postsecondary educational institutions.
(4)
A written determination as to the validity of the complaint and description of resolution, if any, shall be issued by the handicap coordinator and a copy forwarded to the complainant not later than 30 days after its filing.
(5)
The handicap coordinator shall maintain the files and records of the board of commissioners relating to the complaints filed.
(6)
The right of a person to a prompt and equitable resolution of the complaint filed hereunder shall not be impaired by the person's pursuit of other remedies such as filing of a section 504 complaint with the office of Revenue Sharing, U.S. Department of Treasury. Utilization of this grievance procedure is not a prerequisite to the pursuit of other remedies.
(7)
The complainant can request a reconsideration of the case in instances where such complainant is dissatisfied with the resolution. The request for reconsideration should be made within ten days to the county administrator.
(8)
These rules shall be construed to protect the substantive rights of interested persons, to meet appropriate due process standards and to assure that the board of commissioners complies with section 504 and the office of revenue sharing regulations.
(Code 1987, § 12-1; Res. of 10-2-1984)
Sec. 30-2. - After-hours use of recreational facilities.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to be in any manner upon any land used for recreational purposes by the county recreation department after the hour of 11:00 p.m. of any day unless:
(1)
Such person has obtained a permit issued by the county recreation department;
(2)
Such person is a participant in or a spectator at an event sponsored or sanctioned by the county recreation department; or
(3)
Otherwise established by the county recreation department.
(b)
Any person found in violation of this section shall be subject to prosecution for criminal trespass under O.C.G.A. § 16-7-21.
(Code 1987, § 12-2; Res. of 8-3-1982)
Sec. 30-3. - After-hours use of Sunbury Public Landing.
(a)
Violation. It shall be unlawful for any person to use or otherwise be in any manner upon the Sunbury Public Landing during periods of darkness, except for the limited purpose of launching and retrieving water craft. No other activity, whether for recreational purposes or otherwise, shall be permitted on or about said Sunbury Public Landing during such periods of darkness. For the purposes of this section, the Sunbury Public Landing shall include the dock, pier, and related facilities located at said landing.
(b)
Penalty. Any person who violates the provisions contained herein shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction thereof, shall be punished according to section 1-8.
(c)
Enforcement. The magistrate's court of the county shall have jurisdiction over violations of this section and all procedures for enforcement hereof shall be as provided in O.C.G.A. Title 15, Chapter 10, Article 4 (O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq.). Complaints of violations of any provision contained herein shall be brought before the magistrate's court on a citation issued by the recreation director of the county or the chief magistrate of the county and shall be prosecuted through that court. Violations of this section will be tried upon citations either with or without a prosecuting attorney as determined by the recreation director of the county. Service of citation shall be performed by the constable of the magistrate's court of the county. Citations shall meet all the official requirements as specified in the O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63, as amended.
(Code 1987, § 12-3; Ord. of 9-2-1997, § I)

Sec. 30-6. - Smoking and use of tobacco-related products prohibited in county-owned or operated indoor facilities, parks and vehicles.
(a)
Public purpose. Based upon the reports of the U.S. Surgeon General and other reliable and credible scientific bodies indicating that the smoking of cigarettes, cigars, pipes and similar products, together with the secondary smoke generated thereby, is a contributing cause of illness among both smokers and passive smokers (those inhaling the smoke of other), to include, without limitation, cancer, heart disease and lung disorders; it is the finding of the board of commissioners of the county, that the smoke produced by the smoking of cigarettes, cigars, pipes and similar products is both hazardous and detrimental to the health, safety, well-being and comfort of the nonsmoking public. The Board of Commissioners of Liberty County, Georgia, therefore, finds that it is in the interest of public health, safety, and the general welfare of the employees, residents and visitors of the county to prohibit in county-owned or county-operated indoor facilities, parks and vehicles the inhaling, exhaling, burning, use, or carrying of any lighted or heated cigar, cigarette, or pipe, or any other lighted or heated tobacco product intended for inhalation, in any manner or in any form. The term "smoking" also includes the use of an e-cigarette that creates a vapor, in any manner or in any form, or the use of any oral smoking device for the purpose of circumventing the prohibition of smoking in this section.
(b)
Definitions. As used in this section, the following words shall have the meanings as set forth below unless otherwise required by context:
Buildings and parks owned, operated or leased by the county includes those buildings, parks and other facilities, or parts thereof, under the control of the county.
E-cigarette means any electronic oral device, such as one composed of a heating element, battery and/or electronic circuit that provides a vapor of nicotine and/or other substances, and the use or inhalation of which simulates smoking. The term "e-cigarette" includes any such device, whether manufactured, distributed, marketeted, or sold as an e-cigarette, e-cigar or e-pipe, or under any other product name or descriptor and is considered smoking a tobacco-related product.
Smoking means the inhaling, exhaling, burning or carrying any lighted or heated cigar, cigarette, or pipe, or any other lighted or heated tobacco product intended for inhalation, in any manner or in any form. The term "smoking" also includes the use of an e-cigarette that creates a vapor, in any manner or in any form, or the use of any oral smoking device for the purpose of circumventing the prohibition of smoking in this section.
Tobacco-related products means any product from the plant called tobacco or the family of plants from the genus Nicotiana. These products shall include, but are not limited to, pipe tobacco, smoking tobacco, cigars, cigarettes, cigarillos, chewing tobacco, snuff or smokeless tobacco.
(c)
Tobacco-related product use prohibited.
(1)
Tobacco-related products are prohibited in and throughout all buildings and parks owned, operated or leased by the county, as well as prohibited within 25 feet of the exterior of such buildings on property owned, operated, or leased by the county.
(2)
Tobacco-related product use is likewise prohibited in all enclosed-cab vehicles owned and/or operated by the county, unless the individual wishing to use a tobacco-related product first receives the express consent of all other occupants of the vehicle.
(d)
Notice. The county administrator, or his designee, shall cause signs to be conspicuously posted throughout buildings and parks owned, operated or leased by the county informing the public that using a tobacco-related product is prohibited by county ordinance. The size, design, location and content of such signs shall be determined by the county administrator, or his designee.
(e)
Penalty. Any person found violating the provisions of this section, upon conviction, shall be fined not less than $50.00, but no more than $100.00, per offense.
(f)
Enforcement. The magistrate court of the county shall have jurisdiction over violations of this section and all procedures for enforcement hereof shall be as provided in O.C.G.A. Title 15, Chapter 10, Article 4 (O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq.), as amended. Complaints of violations of any provision contained herein shall be brought before the magistrate court on a citation issued by the county administrator or his designee or the chief magistrate of the county and shall be prosecuted through that court. Violations of this section will be tried upon citations either with or without a prosecuting attorney as determined by the said county administrator (or his designee). Service of citation shall be performed by the constable of the magistrate court of the county. Citations shall meet all the official requirements as specified in the O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63, as amended.
(Code 1987, § 12-6; Ord. No. 1999-001, § l, 3-2-1999; Ord. No. 2004-003, § 1, 7-6-2004; Ord. No. 2010-005, 6-17-2010)
Sec. 30-7. - Restricted use of vehicles in county parks.
(a)
Vehicles prohibited. In addition to, and not in limitation of, any and all local, state, and federal laws and regulations governing motor vehicles and their operation, no vehicle shall be permitted in or on any park or other area owned or operated by the county for recreational purposes (each, a "park"), except as follows:
(1)
Cars, trucks, vans, motorcycles, buses, and similar passenger vehicles which are registered, insured, and otherwise authorized by law to operate on public roadways in the county shall be permitted only on driveways and roads designated for ingress and egress to and from a park, as well as areas intended for parking, and only for those purposes; through traffic or other use of such access and parking areas is strictly prohibited.
(2)
This prohibition shall not apply to (i) any ambulance, fire truck, law enforcement vehicle, or other authorized emergency vehicles being used for emergency or official purposes; (ii) any vehicle owned by the county or operated by its employees, agents, or contractors performing official duties or other work on behalf of the county; (iii) any vehicle owned by a utility provider or operated by its employees, agents, or contractors performing work on behalf of the utility provider in the normal course of business and authorized to be in the park for such purpose; or (iv) any vehicle specifically permitted, in writing or by posting, by the board of commissioners or the county recreation department.
(b)
Vehicle defined. For purposes of this section, "vehicle" means any device propelled other than by muscular power in, upon, or by which persons or property may be transported on land, including without limitation, automobiles, trucks, buses, vans, motorcycles, dirt bikes, minibikes, motorized bicycles, motorized scooters, motorized skateboards, golf carts, low-speed vehicles, all-terrain vehicles (ATVs), utility vehicles, off-highway vehicles (OHVs), tractors, and self-balancing personal transporters. "Vehicle" shall not include electric personal assistive mobility devices, power wheelchairs, mobility scooters, or similar mobility assisting devices used by persons whose ambulatory mobility has been impaired by age, disability, or illness.
(c)
Penalty. Any person operating a vehicle in violation of this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction thereof, shall be punished according to section 1-8 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 2021-002, § I, 6-1-2021)

ARTICLE II. - REDUCTION OF FALSE ALARMS

Sec. 30-31. - Purpose.
The purpose of this article is to encourage alarm owners to properly use and maintain the operational effectiveness of alarm systems in order to improve their reliability and reduce or eliminate false alarms. The governing authority of the county finds that excessive false alarms unduly burden the county sheriff's department and other fire rescue responders and impairs limited public safety resources.
(Code 1987, § 12-55; Ord. No. 2014-001, § I, 7-1-2014)
Sec. 30-32. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alarm system means an assembly of equipment and devices arranged to signal the presence of a hazard requiring urgent attention by public safety personnel, but does not include alarms installed in motor vehicles, domestic violence alarms, or alarms designed to primarily elicit a medical response.
False alarm means the activation of an alarm system to summon public safety personnel to respond to a hold-up, break-in, burglary, unauthorized entry or attempted entry, fire, destruction of property, or other event requiring law enforcement or fire rescue response when in fact the service called for is not needed, as determined by the responding public safety personnel.
False alarm administrator means the person designated by the county administrator to administer this article.
Public safety personnel means the county sheriff's department or other fire rescue responders funded, in whole or in part, by the board of commissioners.
(Code 1987, § 12-56; Ord. No. 2014-001, § I, 7-1-2014)
Sec. 30-33. - Fee established; notification of false alarm.
(a)
Fee established. To discourage excessive false alarms at any residential, commercial, industrial, institutional, educational or other location and better protect the lives and property of the citizens and residents of the county, as well as to reduce unnecessary use of public safety resources and help to defray the costs of responding to false alarms, a false alarm service fee is hereby established.
(b)
Rate. The owner or lessee of any residential, commercial, industrial, institutional, educational or other property with an alarm system shall pay to the board of commissioners a false alarm service fee for each false alarm responded to by public safety personnel, based upon the total number of false alarms occurring at such location during the preceding 12 months. The false alarm service fee shall be charged as shown in the following schedule:

  All false alarm service fees assessed by this section shall be due and payable within 30 days following notice thereof.
(c)
Notification of false alarm. Following a false alarm at any residential, commercial, industrial, institutional, educational or other property, the false alarm administrator shall notify the responsible owner or lessee of the alarm system and the false alarm service fee due under this section. Such notice may be given by mailing a copy of the notice of false alarm prescribed by this section to the alarmed premises, or by leaving a copy of such notice of false alarm at the alarmed premises.
(d)
Exceptions. Each of the following circumstances shall not constitute a false alarm and no fee shall be assessed hereunder:
(1)
Alarm conditions activated by a person working on the alarm system with prior notification to the county sheriff's department; or
(2)
Alarms that can be substantiated as being activated by documented electrical interruptions, floods, or other natural disasters.
(Code 1987, § 12-57; Ord. No. 2014-001, § I, 7-1-2014)
Sec. 30-34. - False alarm review board; appeals.
(a)
Review board appointed. There shall be appointed by the board of commissioners a false alarm review board that shall hear all appeals authorized under this section. The false alarm review board shall be comprised of three persons, who shall be appointed by the board of commissioners for a two-year term. From among the three appointed members, a chairperson shall be elected by the false alarm review board to preside over its meetings. Any two members of the false alarm review board shall constitute a quorum and a majority vote shall be dispositive of any issue presented. All members of the false alarm review board shall serve at the pleasure of the board of commissioners, who may remove any member for any or no reason in its sole discretion. Despite the expiration of a board member's term, such member shall continue to serve until a qualified successor shall be appointed. Any member may be appointed to successive terms. The false alarm administrator shall act as the secretary of the false alarm review board, but shall not serve as a member.
(b)
Appeals. Any owner or lessee of an alarmed premises may appeal the determination of a false alarm and/or the assessment of a false alarm service fee under this article to the false alarm review board. Such appeal shall be in writing and filed with the county administrator within ten days of notification of such determination and/or assessment. Upon filing of such appeal, the false alarm review board shall give reasonable notice to the appellant and shall hold a hearing on the appeal.
(Code 1987, § 12-58; Ord. No. 2014-001, § I, 7-1-2014)
Sec. 30-35. - Duties of alarm system owners.
(a)
Shutoff requirement. Alarm systems employing audible signals shall be equipped with shutoff devices that automatically silence the audible signal 15 minutes after the alarm is initially activated.
(b)
Maintenance and operation. The owner or lessee of any property with an alarm system shall:
(1)
Maintain the premises and alarm system in a manner that will minimize or eliminate false alarms; and
(2)
Respond or cause their representative to appear at the alarm location within a reasonable period of time when notified by public safety personnel or the involved monitoring company to provide access to the premises, deactivate the alarm system, or provide security at the alarm location.
(Code 1987, § 12-59; Ord. No. 2014-001, § I, 7-1-2014)
ARTICLE III. - ILLEGAL DISCHARGE OF FIREARM

Sec. 30-36. - Prohibited conduct.
Unless otherwise provided in section 30-37, it shall be unlawful for any person within the unincorporated areas of the county to discharge a firearm:
(1)
On any property within the unincorporated area of the county, except that the discharge of firearms shall be permitted consistent with this article on property zoned dunes and marshland (DM-1) and on any single lot, tract, or parcel of land, consisting of no less than two acres and wholly located within one or more of the following zoning districts: agricultural (A-1), agricultural residential (AR-1), single-family residential (R-1), two-family residential (R-2), and one- and two-family residential (R-2A);
(2)
Within 1,000 feet of any schoolhouse or any dwelling or structure intended and suitable for residential occupancy, the conduct of in-person business, or as a place of regular worship or public meeting, without the express permission of the owner of said dwelling or structure;
(3)
On any park or other property owned or controlled by the Board of Commissioners of Liberty County, without the prior written consent of said board or its designated department, agency, or official;
(4)
On the property of another, without the express permission of the owner of said property;
(5)
So as to fire across, or cause any bullet, ball, shot, or other projectile from said firearm to land on, the property of another, without the express permission of the owner of said property;
(6)
At night, the same being the period beginning 30 minutes after sunset until 30 minutes before sunrise; or
(7)
When target shooting, without taking the following safety precautions: (a) use of an appropriate natural or man-made backstop (e.g. upland slope, berm, timbers, etc.) of such material, size, and orientation that will safely stop and render harmless the bullet or other projectile being fired; and (b) use of targets of such material and placed at such height and relation to a backstop so as to significantly minimize any overshoot or ricochet.
(Ord. No. 2021-004, § I, 8-19-2021, eff. 9-1-2021)
Sec. 30-37. - Regulatory exceptions.
Notwithstanding section 30-36, this article and the prohibitions contained herein shall not apply:
(1)
To any person acting with legal justification in the defense of person or property;
(2)
To any property owned or controlled by the United States or the State of Georgia, or any department or authority thereof, including, without limitation, the United States Army;
(3)
To law enforcement officers, animal control officers, or other government employees or agents authorized to carry a firearm when in the performance of their official duties; or
(4)
With respect to section 30-36(1) and (2), the (a) business location of a duly licensed firearms dealer or indoor or outdoor sport shooting range, or (b) lands regularly and actively used as of September 1, 2021 as a hunting club, hunting preserve, shooting preserve, or similar operations, whether public or private, and the activities thereon, so long as said lands are continuously or seasonally used for such purposes in compliance with applicable law, including, without limitation, the Georgia Game and Fish Code, O.C.G.A. § 27-1-1 and related regulations of the Georgia Board of Natural Resources; or
(5)
With respect to section 30-36(2), lands regularly and actively used as a hunting club, hunting preserve, shooting preserve, sport shooting range, or similar operations, whether public or private, and the activities thereon, which violate said section 30-36(2) solely as a result of changed conditions in or around the locality of such lands and so long as said lands have been used for such purposes for at least a year prior to said changed conditions and are continuously or seasonally used thereafter for the same purposes in compliance with applicable law, including, without limitation, the Georgia Game and Fish Code, O.C.G.A. § 27-1-1 and related regulations of the Georgia Board of Natural Resources; or
(6)
With respect to section 30-36(6), any person holding a duly issued hunting license and engaged in the night hunting of permitted species in accordance with law.
(Ord. No. 2021-004, § I, 8-19-2021, eff. 9-1-2021)
Sec. 30-38. - Cumulative of state law.
This article shall be subject to all existing and future laws of the State of Georgia regulating firearms and their discharge, and shall remain in full force and effect to the extent not preempted or otherwise prohibited by said laws.
(Ord. No. 2021-004, § I, 8-19-2021, eff. 9-1-2021)
