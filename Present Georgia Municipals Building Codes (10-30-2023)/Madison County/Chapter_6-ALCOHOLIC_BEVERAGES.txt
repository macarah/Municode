ARTICLE II. - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 6-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcohol: Ethyl alcohol, hydrated oxide of ethyl, or spirits of wine, from whatever source or by whatever process produced.
Alcoholic beverage: Includes all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, malt beverage, wine or fortified wine.
Alcoholic beverage ordinance: The name by which this chapter shall be referred.
Applicant: The individual or entity which applies for a license hereunder and holds the license in the event the application is granted.
Application includes applications both for new licenses (i.e., where the applicant is not the current holder of a valid license) and renewal licenses, unless specifically stated otherwise.
Beer or malt beverage: Any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than six percent alcohol by volume, and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer and strong beer. The term does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine. Calendar year. The period of time from the date of issuance of a license to the date of December 31 of the year of issuance of said license.
Clerk: The Clerk of the Board of Commissioners of Madison County, Georgia.
Board: The Board of Commissioners of Madison County, Georgia.
Catered function: An event at which alcoholic beverages will be sold or served by a licensed alcoholic beverage retailer off the premises of said retailer. Such event shall not exceed one 24 hour period and shall comply with the times of service specified for the service of alcoholic beverages set out in this chapter.
Church: Any permanent building which houses the main sanctuary in which persons regularly assemble for religious worship, but shall not include a residence used for religious purposes.
Distilled spirits: Any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume, including but not limited to all fortified wines.
Election day: The day of any general election, primary election, run-off election, special election, or referendum within the unincorporated areas of the county, beginning with the opening of the polls and ending with the closing of the polls.
Event hall: A privately owned indoor establishment which:
(1)
Provides a gathering space for rental purposes;
(2)
Charges a rental fee for use of the event hall for special events;
(3)
May allow live musical entertainment in compliance with all noise ordinances; and
(4)
Does not allow sexually related adult entertainment to be performed in the event hall
The primary activity on the premises of the indoor event hall shall be family oriented in nature, generally meaning a use which attracts a range of individuals from all age groups. Uses may specifically include, but are not limited to, corporate events, wedding receptions, birthday parties, holiday parties, and other similar uses. Bingo parlors, dance halls, nightclubs, taverns, billiard parlors, video arcades, skating arenas, adult entertainment and/or sexually related entertainment activities, and similar uses are specifically excluded from this definition of event hall.
Farm winery: A winery which makes at least 40 percent of its annual production from agricultural produce grown in the state where the winery is located and:
(A)
Is located on premises, a substantial portion of which is used for agricultural purposes, including the cultivation of grapes, berries, or fruits to be utilized in the manufacture or production of wine by the winery; or
(B)
Is owned and operated by persons who are engaged in the production of a substantial portion of the agricultural produce used in its annual production.
For purposes of this paragraph, the board of commissioners shall determine what is a substantial portion of such winery premises or agricultural produce.
Financial interest: Includes, but is not limited to, holding any indebtedness or security interest in a business.
First public notice: The date of posting of the sign required in subsection 6-64(a) hereof on the premises.
Fixed salary: The amount of compensation paid any member, officer, agent, or employee of a private club as may be fixed for him by its members at a prior annual meeting or by the governing body out of the general revenue of the club and shall not include any commission on any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages.
Fortified wine: Any alcoholic beverage containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes, either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term includes but is not limited to brandy.
License: The authorization by the board of commissioners to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages.
Licensee: The individual to whom a license is issued, or in the case of a partnership or corporation, or limited liability company or all partners, members, managers, officers, and directors of the partnership, limited liability company or corporation and in the case of any other business entity, any person filling an analogous role in such entity.
Manufacturer: Any maker, producer, or bottler of alcoholic beverages.
Person: Any individual, firm, partnership, cooperative, nonprofit membership corporation, joint venture, association, company, corporation, agency, syndicate, estate, trust, business trust, receiver, fiduciary, or other group or combination acting as a unit, body politic, or political subdivision, whether public, private, or quasi-public.
Private club: Any nonprofit association organized under the laws of this state which:
(1)
Has been in existence at least one year prior to the filing of its application for a license to be issued pursuant to this chapter;
(2)
Has at least 75 regular dues-paying members;
(3)
Owns, hires, or leases a building or space within a building for the reasonable use of its members, which building or space:
a.
Has suitable kitchen and dining room space and equipment;
b.
Is staffed with a sufficient number of employees for cooking, preparing, and serving meals for its members and guests; and
(4)
Has no member, officer, agent, or employee directly or indirectly receiving, in the form of salary or other compensation, any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages beyond a fixed salary.
Restaurant: An established place of business which is licensed to sell alcoholic beverages and actively in operation where meals with substantial entrees selected by the patron from a full menu are served during all hours of operations with
(1)
Suitable kitchen and dining room space and equipment to serve meals or appetizers for at least 75 patrons; and
(2)
A minimum of 70 percent of its gross income deriving from the sale of such meals or appetizers
Retailer: Any person, except as to distilled spirits who sells alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises, at retail, only to consumers and not for resale or any person who sells alcoholic beverages in unbroken packages, at retail, only to consumers and not for resale.
School building or educational building: A governmental or church school buildings and such buildings at such other schools to teach the subjects commonly taught in the common schools of this state or a building, publicly or privately owned, which is use for teaching or giving institution in any subjects to pupils of the school, college, university, or other institution of education.
Tasting room: An outlet for the promotion of a farm winery's wine by providing samples of such wine to the public and for the sale of such wine at retail for consumption on the premises and for sale in closed packages for consumption off the premises. Samples of wine can be given free of charge or for a fee.
Wholesaler or wholesale dealer: Any person engaged in distribution or selling to other wholesale dealers, retailers dealers, or to retail consumption dealers for the purpose of resale any alcoholic beverage.
Wine: Any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes, either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term includes but is not limited to all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products. The term does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this section.

Sec. 6-32. - General provisions.
In the event any provision of this chapter is in conflict with any state or federal law or with any rule, regulation or order of any state or federal agency having jurisdiction over the subject matter of this chapter, it is hereby deemed to be the intention of the board of commissioners that the state or federal law, rule, regulation or order, as the case may be, shall prevail over any such provision, and the remaining portion of this chapter shall be deemed to be, and continue, in full force and effect.
All provisions of Official Georgia Code Annotated Title 3 applicable to alcoholic beverage sales are incorporated herein if not in conflict with this chapter 6 of the Madison County Code and made a part hereof, including any amendments thereto.
Each licensee and its agents and employees shall be subject to and comply with all ordinances and laws of Madison County, the state or units thereof and the federal government.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
Sec. 6-33. - Excise taxes.
(a)
There is hereby imposed and levied an excise tax upon all wholesale dealers in malt beverages and/or wines selling, transferring or dispensing malt beverages and/or wine within Madison County in the maximum amount allowed by law as now exists or hereafter amended.
(b)
For sellers of distilled spirits by the drink, there is hereby imposed and levied a specific excise tax of three percent of the charge to the public for each such beverage sold.
(c)
The excise tax provided for in subparagraph (a) hereinabove shall be imposed upon and shall be paid by the wholesale dealer in malt beverage and/or wine selling, transferring or dispensing such malt beverages and/or wine within Madison County. The taxes shall be paid by such wholesale dealer on or before the tenth day of the month following the calendar month in which the beverages are sold, transferred or dispensed within Madison County. Each wholesaler responsible for the payment of the excise tax shall file a report on or before the tenth day of each month with the clerk showing for the preceding calendar month the exact quantities of malt beverage and/or wine, by size and type of container, constituting a beginning and ending inventory for the month, sold, transferred or dispensed within Madison County. The wholesaler shall remit the tax imposed hereunder to Madison County on or before the tenth day of the month next succeeding the calendar month in which such sales or transfers were make. This tax shall be in addition to all other taxes and/or license fees heretofore or hereafter imposed and to be paid and collected as provided by this chapter.
(d)
The excise tax provided for in subparagraph (b) hereinabove shall be imposed upon and shall be paid by the Class F licensee selling distilled spirits by the drink within the county. The taxes shall be paid by such licensee on or before the tenth day of the month following the calendar month in which the beverages are sold within the county. Each Class F licensee shall file a report on or before the tenth day of each month with the clerk showing for the preceding calendar month the exact number of drinks sold and the charge for same within the county. Such licensee shall remit the tax imposed hereunder to the county on or before the tenth day of the month next succeeding the calendar month in which such sales or were made.
(e)
No person shall offer for sale or transfer for gift any alcoholic beverage unless the excise tax levied herein has been paid on the items sold, dispensed or otherwise transferred. No retailer of alcoholic beverages shall purchase any such items except from a wholesale dealer duly licensed by proper authority of the laws of Georgia.
(f)
The code enforcement officer or designee shall be authorized to enter upon the premises of any licensee at responsible hours to make any inspection of books, records or inventory as deemed necessary to ensure compliance with this section.
(g)
Failure to make a timely report and remittance of the aforesaid taxes shall render a responsible party liable for a penalty equal to 50 percent of the total amount due in addition to the amount due. Additionally, if said report is not filed or if said taxes are not remitted and paid within 30 days from the date that taxes are due, the board shall have the right to suspend and/or revoke any alcoholic beverage license issued to or on behalf of the responsible party, and further to prohibit said responsible party from making any deliveries of any type whatsoever within the unincorporated areas of Madison County, Georgia.
(h)
Failure to file a timely report and remittance of the aforesaid taxes shall render a responsible party liable for interest on the unpaid tax at the rate of one percent per month from the time the tax became due until the tax is paid or at the rate specified in O.C.G.A. § 48-2-40, whichever is greater. Interest shall be computed on a monthly basis for any portion of a month during which payment is delinquent.
(i)
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell at retail or otherwise within the unincorporated areas of Madison County, Georgia, any alcoholic beverage on which the tax required in this chapter has not been paid.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)

Sec. 6-35. - Hours of operation.
(a)
No retail dealer shall engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages except between the hours of 6:00 a.m. and 12:00 midnight, Monday through Saturday, and between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m. on Sundays, and shall not permit the sale of alcoholic beverages on Christmas Day or any other day on which state law prohibits the sale of alcoholic beverages. On any county, state, and/or national election day, unless prohibited by state law, retail dealers may only engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages beginning one hour after the voting polls close and ending at 12:00 midnight. "Election day" as used herein does not include early voting days but only the election date itself. All persons shall be off the premises of the package stores at 12:00 midnight on weekdays and Saturdays, and 11:30 p.m. on Sundays, at which time said package store shall close for business. Grocery or convenience stores where the principal business is the sale of groceries and other like items and the sale of malt beverages and wine is incidental thereto may remain open during other hours but shall not sell malt beverages or wine except during the time and hours specified herein.
(b)
The business hours of wholesale dealers shall be the same hours and days as retail dealers are allowed to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages by the terms of (a) above.
(c)
The chairman of the board of commissioners may, upon determining that there is an immediate danger of civil disorder or natural disaster or any other immediate threat to the public peace and order, by executive order, suspend the sales of all beverages licensed under this chapter until such danger or threat has passed and for a reasonable period of time thereafter.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
Sec. 6-36. - Sales of alcoholic beverages near churches, school buildings or other sites.
(a)
Prohibited.
(1)
No person knowingly and intentionally may sell or offer to sell:
a.
Any distilled spirits in or within 100 yards of any church building or within 200 yards of any school building, educational building, school grounds, or college campus;
b.
Any alcoholic beverages within 100 yards of any school building, school grounds, or college campus. This subparagraph shall not apply to any location for which a license has been issued prior to July 1, 1981, nor to the renewal of such license. Nor shall this subparagraph apply at any location for which a new license is applied for if the sale of alcoholic beverages was lawful at such location at any time during the 12 months immediately preceding such application;
c.
Any alcoholic beverages within 100 yards of an alcoholic treatment center owned and operated by this state or any county or municipal government therein. This paragraph shall not apply to any business having a license in effect on July 1, 1981.
(2)
As used in this subsection, the term "school building" or "educational building" shall apply only to state, county, city, or church school buildings and to such buildings at such other schools in which are taught subjects commonly taught in the common schools and colleges of this state and which are public schools or private schools as defined in O.C.G.A. § 20-2-690(b).
(b)
Exceptions. Nothing contained in this Code section shall prohibit the licensing of the sale or distribution of alcoholic beverages by:
(1)
Hotels of 50 rooms or more which have been in continuous operation for a period of at least five years preceding July 1, 1981.
(2)
Bona fide private clubs, owning their own homes, subject to licensing under O.C.G.A. § 3-7-1 et seq.; and
(3)
Licensees for the retail sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises only who shall be subject to regulation as to distances from churches, schools and colleges by counties and municipalities.
(c)
Distances. For purposes of this section, distances shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground.
(d)
Housing authority property.
(1)
As used in this subsection, the term "housing authority property" means any property containing 300 housing units or fewer owned or operated by a housing authority created by Housing Authorities Law (O.C.G.A. § 8-3-1 et seq.)
(2)
No person knowingly and intentionally may sell any alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises within 100 yards of any housing authority property. This subsection shall not apply at any location for which a license has been issued prior to July 1, 2000, nor to the renewal of such license; nor shall this subsection apply at any location for which a new license is applied for if the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises was lawful at such location at any time during the 12 months immediately preceding such application.
(e)
State law requirements. Whenever state law contains requirements in excess of or in addition to those contained herein, state law shall control.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
Sec. 6-37. - Minors and inebriates.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to provide alcoholic beverages to any persons under the age permitted by O.C.G.A § 3-3-23, under any conditions whatsoever. Furthermore; it shall be unlawful for any person to misrepresent their age in any manner whatsoever for the purposes of purchasing or otherwise acquiring any beverage licensed hereunder.
(b)
No alcoholic beverages shall be sold to any intoxicated person, nor shall any intoxicated person be allowed to remain upon the premises of any place of business selling alcoholic beverages. It shall be unlawful to provide alcoholic beverages to any person in a state of noticeable intoxication as provided by O.C.G.A § 3-3-22.
(c)
No employees of any business selling alcoholic beverages shall work in said place of business while intoxicated and no employee shall drink alcoholic beverages while working and employed in such business.
(d)
No alcoholic beverages shall be sold to any person who is mentally incompetent.
(e)
Employment and supervision of underage persons: for all licensees, it shall be unlawful for any person to allow or require a person in his employment less than eighteen years of age or the minimum age designated by law, whichever is greater, to dispense, serve, sell or take orders for any alcoholic beverages. Alcoholic beverages may not be sold by any licensee hereunder unless there is on duty at the licensed location at the time of such sale a person of at least 21 years of age who is in charge of that location at that time. Such on duty personnel shall be present at the premises except for job related absences not exceeding one hour.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
Sec. 6-38. - Display of price.
Retailers shall indicate plainly by tags or labels on the bottles or containers or on the shelf immediately below where the containers are placed, or on an exposed sign prominently placed, the price of all alcoholic beverages exposed or offered for sale.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
Sec. 6-39. - Compliance with health rules.
All retail licensees under this chapter are required to comply with all rules and regulations and requirements of the Madison County Board of Health, state board of health, and any and all other state, federal, and local public health authorities.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
Sec. 6-40. - Penalties for violation.
(a)
Violations: It shall be a violation of this chapter for any licensee to permit any person, including the licensee, to engage in any activity on the premises for which the license is issued or within the place of business, which is in violation of the laws or regulations of the state or federal government or any ordinance of Madison County.
(b)
Penalties: Any person holding any license issued pursuant to this chapter or any employee or agent of such person who violates any provision of this chapter, or directs, consents to, permits, or acquiesces in such violation either directly or indirectly shall, by such conduct, subject the license to suspension, revocation or probation upon conditions. Violation of any provision of this chapter for failure to comply with any of its requirements shall constitute a misdemeanor. In addition, a maximum civil fine of $1,000.00 or imprisonment for not more than 60 days, or both, may be imposed on the licensee or permittee for each violation, and in addition may be liable to pay all costs and expenses involved in such case. Each day any violation continues shall be considered a separate offense.
(c)
In addition thereto and totally and separately distinct therefrom, any violation of any of the provisions of this chapter or failure to comply with any of its requirements shall be grounds for immediate suspension or revocation of any and all licenses, regardless of the number of locations involved, and regardless of whether or not the holder of the license knew of or contributed to such violation. It is the intent of this subsection, without limiting the generality of the foregoing, to grant to the board the right to suspend or revoke any or all licenses held by a licensee when any violation of this chapter occurs at any place of business where alcoholic beverages are sold pursuant to a license issued hereunder.
(d)
Any violation of the general statute law of Georgia pertaining to alcoholic beverages shall constitute a violation of this chapter.
(e)
The Madison County Sheriff is authorized to conduct any enforcement actions necessary to determine compliance with this chapter.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
Sec. 6-41. - Farm wineries.
Farm wineries holding a Class E license are authorized to manufacture, sell, and serve wine (samples and by the drink) to the extent authorized by state law (O.C.G.A. §§ 3-6-21.1 through 3-6-21.3, as same may be amended in the future).
The surety bond required as a condition upon issuance of a license pursuant to this code section shall be the same as that required pursuant to O.C.G.A 3-6-21 with respect to wineries.
Wines sold at retail by a manufacturer shall have levied thereon an excise tax and such tax shall be reported and remitted to the board of commissioner.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
Sec. 6-42. - Catered function.
(a)
Special event catering permit. The holder of a Class A, B, E, or F license hereunder who is also a "licensed alcoholic beverage caterer" as defined by O.C.G.A. § 3-11-1 (as same may be amended in the future) may apply for an off-premises permit which authorizes such licensed alcoholic beverage caterer to sell alcoholic beverages by the drink off premises and in connection with an authorized catered function.
The application shall include the following:
(1)
Name of the caterer;
(2)
The date, address, and time of the event;
(3)
The licensed alcoholic beverage caterer's license number;
(4)
A copy of the contract between the caterer and the entity sponsoring the event; and
(5)
Proof of permission for same to be held at its location
The application shall be by written application under oath on the form as provided by the clerk. Such application is a public record and open to public inspection as provided by the Georgia Open Records Act
The application for the permit must be accompanied by a fee of $25.00 and made at least 30 days prior to the date of the event and the permit shall be issued by the clerk upon the clerk's verification that all requirements have been met.
(b)
Annual catering permit. The holder of a Class A, B, E, or F license hereunder who is also a "licensed alcoholic beverage caterer" as defined by O.C.G.A. § 3-11-1 (as same may be amended in the future) may apply for an off-premises permit which authorizes such licensed alcoholic beverage caterer to sell alcoholic beverages by the drink off premises and in connection with authorized catered functions. An annual permit authorizes the holder to cater up to 20 events per year.
The application shall include the following:
(1)
Name of the caterer;
(2)
Restaurant/business name and address; and
(3)
The licensed alcoholic beverage caterer's license number.
The application shall be by written application under oath on the form as provided by the clerk. Such application is a public record and open to public inspection as provided by the Georgia Open Records Act
The application for the permit must be accompanied by a fee of $150.00 and made at least 30 days prior to the date of the first catered event and the permit shall be issued by the clerk upon the clerk's verification that all requirements have been met.

DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 6-61. - Duty of county clerk.
The county clerk is directed to mail out, no later than the last week in September of each year, applications for the upcoming year to all current holders of alcoholic beverage licenses.
The clerk shall further accept and process all proper and complete applications for licenses including but not limited to, redaction of personal information on copies, forwarding documentation to the probate judge department as well as the sheriffs department for criminal investigation purposes, preparing information for the board for voting purposes concerning the issuance of license and the printing of the approved license.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
Sec. 6-62. - License requirements.
It shall be unlawful for any person, to sell or offer for sale, at retail or wholesale, within Madison County any alcoholic beverage without having a license issued as provided for under the provisions of this chapter or in violation of the terms of such license or this chapter. The sale of alcoholic beverage within Madison County is declared to be a privilege and not a right and such privilege shall not be exercised except as licensed hereunder. It shall be unlawful for any person to manufacture, sell or offer for sale, at retail or wholesale, within Madison County any alcoholic beverage not specifically licensed hereby.
No person, firm, corporation, club, partnership, or any other entity shall offer for sale or sell any alcoholic beverage within the unincorporated areas of the county without first obtaining the appropriate license herein required.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
Sec. 6-63. - License application procedures.
(a)
The applicant for a license under this chapter shall make a written application under oath on the forms as provided by the county clerk or the clerk's designated representative, herein the "clerk". Such application is a public record and open to public inspection as provided by the Georgia Open Records Act, unless and except to the extent any information thereon is prohibited from disclosure by state of federal law. The application shall include such matters as may be reasonably required by the county to aid in its decision on whether to issue a license or not.
(1)
Applications shall be under oath, and shall authorize criminal background checks of all applicants.
•
The applicant shall upon turning in a complete application to the clerk, at the same time provide certified funds for $50.00 made payable to Madison County for the investigation/application fee.
•
The sheriff's department shall obtain fingerprints and make an arrest and conviction investigation, including national, state and local information, concerning any applicants hereunder.
•
Any person making a false statement in any application for a license, or statement in connection with renewal thereof, shall be guilty of an offense and punished as provided by state law relating to false swearing, and further, a license, if previously granted or renewed, may be revoked for the violation. It shall be an offense for any person to give other than the true and correct legal name of the intended licensee.
(b)
All renewal applications must be filed before November 1st to be considered by the board. All new applications may be processed at any time during the year under the same stipulations as a renewal.
(c)
Applications will be considered and license granted only at a regular business meeting of the board.
(d)
No license defined herein shall be issued to any person who is not a legal resident of the United States and less than 21 years of age.
(e)
If the owner of the business is an individual, the application shall be made and signed by such individual.
(f)
Where the applicant is a business entity or private club, the application shall be signed by an agent of the applicant as follows:
(1)
If the owner of the business is a corporation, limited liability company, or partnership, the application shall be signed by a designated manager-agent of the entity. The manager-agent must be the individual who does in fact have and maintain regular, managerial and supervisory authority over the business conducted on the licensed premises. No license shall be granted to any entity that is not duly registered and in good standing with the Georgia Secretary of State. Any person who has been designated as an agent of an entity whose license has been revoked is ineligible to act as an agent thereafter.
(2)
If the application is being made on behalf of a private club, the application shall be signed by an officer of such private club who does in fact have and maintain regular, managerial and supervisory control over the activities authorized under the license.
(g)
A license application may be denied to any applicant where it appears that the individual signing the application would not have adequate supervisory participation in the proposed business to direct and manage its affairs, or where it appears that the application is intended to be a mere surrogate for a person or persons who would not otherwise qualify for a license for any reason whatsoever. The burden is on the applicant to provide reasonably convincing evidence that this requirement has been met.
(h)
No license shall be granted to any person who is serving as a commissioner of Madison County, Georgia, nor shall any license by granted to any business in which a commissioner holds any interest.
(i)
No license shall be granted where the individual signing the application:
(1)
Has been convicted or plead guilty or entered a plea of nolo contendere to any felony or misdemeanor involving alcoholic beverages or drugs within a period of five years immediately prior to the filing of the application:
(2)
Has been convicted of any felony within the five years previous to the date of the application; or
(3)
Is on probation or parole for any offense, local, state or federal, at the time of application.
(4)
Has been found in violation of the ordinances of Madison County or any other county or municipality, governing alcoholic beverages licenses within the last five years immediately prior to the filing of the licensee's application.
(5)
Has been convicted of the violation of any state laws or federal laws pertaining to the manufacture, possession, use, transportation or sale alcoholic beverages, or the taxability thereof, within the last five years immediately prior to the filling of the licensee's application
(j)
Further, no license shall be granted to a business entity or club:
(1)
That has an officer who has been convicted or entered a plea of nolo contendere or is on probation or parole as set out in this section, or
(2)
In which any person who owns greater than a ten percent interest has been convicted or entered a plea of nolo contendere or is on probation or parole as set out in this section.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
Sec. 6-64. - Advertisement.
(a)
When alcoholic beverages were not previously sold on the premises, applicants for a new license (i.e., applicants who do not currently hold a valid license) shall post advertisement at the premises to be licensed as set forth below.
(1)
A sign shall be posted at least 30 days prior to consideration of the application by the board in a prominent position on the building, if there is a building. In the event there is no building on the premises, the notice shall be posted on the lot so as to be easily read from the nearest road and no more than 25 feet from the shoulder thereof. The applicant must submit a building plan and front elevation of the proposed building.
(2)
The following is the wording and specifications for the sign that must be posted when applying for a new alcoholic beverage license:
"NOTICE IS HEREBY GIVEN THAT THE OWNER OF THIS ESTABLISHMENT HAS MADE APPLICATION TO THE BOARD OF COMMISSIONERS OF MADISON COUNTY GEORGIA FOR A CLASS _______ ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGE LICENSE. THE HEARING ON SUCH APPLICATION WILL BE HELD AT THE REGULAR MEETING OF THE MADISON COUNTY BOARD OF COMMISSIONERS ON _______. MORE THAN THIRTY (30) DAYS AFTER THE POSTING OF THIS NOTICE.
THIS NOTICE POSTED ON THE ___ DAY OF _______, 20___.
__________________
APPLICANT'S SIGNATURE"
The sign must be a minimum size of four and one-half square feet and letters must be a minimum of four inches.
(b)
Applicants for both new and renewal licenses shall publish a newspaper advertisement two times in the legal organ of Madison County, Georgia, during the 30-day period immediately prior to the hearing of the application by the board, in the following form:
"NOTICE IS HEREBY GIVEN THAT THE OWNER OF _______ LOCATED AT _______ HAS MADE APPLICATION TO THE BOARD OF COMMISSIONERS OF MADISON COUNTY FOR A CLASS ___ ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGE LICENSE. THE HEARING ON SUCH APPLICATION WILL BE HELD AT THE REGULAR BUSINESS MEETING OF THE MADISON COUNTY BOARD OF COMMISSIONERS ON _______. MORE THAN THIRTY (30) DAYS AFTER THE FIRST PUBLIC NOTICE
DATED _______
__________________
APPLICANT'S SIGNATURE"
Applicant is required to show proof of two newspaper advertisements by affidavit of publisher or his designee and is responsible for expenses incurred for such advertising.
(c)
No application will be considered until proof of full compliance with the requirements of this section has been shown to the board.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
Sec. 6-65. - Application fee for new and renewal licenses.
Application for new and renewal licenses shall be accompanied by a non-refundable application fee in the amount of $50.00. Said fee shall be paid by cash, or certified check, cashier's check or money order made payable to Madison County Board of Commissioners. Should the application be denied the application fee will not be refunded.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
Sec. 6-66. - Investigations and recommendations.
(a)
Applications for new licenses shall be sworn and directed to the clerk who shall inspect the application. The clerk shall forward a copy of the application to the sheriff and the sheriff (or his deputy) shall cause the suitability of the location of the establishment for which the license is sought to be fully investigated. After all of the foregoing requirements have been fulfilled, applications for the original licenses shall be presented to the board.
(b)
All applications, both for new licenses and renewals, shall require a criminal background check on the applicant, and no license shall be granted in the absence of a criminal history fingerprint report that complies with the requests of this chapter. A signed letter from the sheriff shall be submitted to the board advising of the applicant's compliance with the ordinance.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
Sec. 6-67. - Hearing, consideration and denial of license.
(a)
All applications presented shall be noted as to the date and exact time of filing, and after filing such applications shall be presented and considered by the board, in the order of their filing at the next regular monthly meeting of the board, provided 30 days have elapsed after the date of filing and all necessary investigation and advertisements have been completed.
(b)
In determining whether or not any license applied for hereunder shall be granted, in addition to all the other provisions of this chapter, in the public interest and welfare the following will be considered:
(1)
If applicant is a previous holder of a license to sell any alcoholic beverage, whether or not he has violated any law, regulation or ordinance relating to such business; and
(2)
Whether the applicant has previously had an alcoholic beverage license suspended or revoked.
(c)
No license shall be granted for the sale of alcoholic beverages at retail where the proposed location does not have a front entrance to the premises which is clearly visible from a public street road or highway, except as to a private club.
(d)
No screen, blind, curtain, partition, articles or things which prevent a clear view into the interior shall be permitted in the window or from the doors of any retail store for the sale of alcoholic beverages by the package and no booth, screen, partition or other obstruction shall be permitted within the interior of any such store. Each building shall be so lighted that the interior of the premises is visible day or night and all parking areas adjacent to or used in connection with said business shall be fully lighted and unobstructed.
(e)
No license shall be granted for the sale of alcoholic beverages from a building occupied as a dwelling.
(f)
Each retail business establishment which is licensed to sell alcoholic beverages shall post in a conspicuous place a notice which shall contain the provisions of the laws of this state which deal with the unlawful sale of alcoholic beverages to underage persons and the penalties for violating such laws.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
Sec. 6-68. - Annual license fees.
(a)
The following annual license fees shall apply for the sales of alcoholic beverages:
(1)
CLASS A — Class A licenses allow the retail sales of malt beverages and wine for on-or off-premises consumption at private clubs, restaurants and event halls only (and for on-premises consumption at catered events if a permit is issued pursuant to section 6-42 hereof). The annual fee shall be $1,000.00.
(2)
CLASS B — Class B licenses allow the retail sales of malt beverages and wine for off-premises consumption (and for on-premises consumption at catered events if a permit is issued pursuant to section 6-42 hereof). The annual fee shall be $1,000.00.
(3)
CLASS C — Class C licenses allow the sale and distribution of malt beverages and wine at wholesale. The annual fee shall be $200.00.
(4)
CLASS D — Class D licenses allow the manufacturing of the malt beverages and wine by wholesale dealers whose principal place of business is Madison County, Georgia. The annual fee shall be $1,000.00.
(5)
CLASS E — Class E licenses allow the manufacturing and distribution of wine by a farm winery (and for on-premises consumption at catered events if a permit is issued pursuant to section 6-42 hereof). The annual fee shall be $0.00 if you fall under one of the other license types. If you do not fall under one of the other license types the fee will be $1,000.00.
(6)
CLASS F — Class F Licenses allow the sale of Distilled Spirits by the drink for restaurants only and shall permit the sale and consumption of distilled spirits on the licensed premises as an incident to the sale and consumption of ready to eat foods or drinks. No alcoholic beverages may be consumed on premise grounds outside of the licensed premises, excepting that a restaurant holding a Class F license may sell alcoholic beverages by the drink off the licensed premises pursuant to a catering permit issued pursuant to section 6-42 hereof. The annual fee shall be $1,200.00.
(b)
When a license has been approved and granted, the fee for same shall be paid to the board by certified check, cashier's check or money order in the full amount of the license fee.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
Sec. 6-69. - License period; lapse for nonuse.
(a)
All licenses shall be issued for a calendar year. In case of revocation or surrender of a license before the expiration of such calendar year, the holder thereof shall not be entitled to receive any refund whatsoever.
(b)
All licenses must be obtained no later than 60 days from the date of the approval of the application, and if not so obtained, said license approval shall be void.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
Sec. 6-70. - Renewal: denial.
(a)
All licenses granted hereunder shall expire on December 31 of each year and shall be renewable at the option of the board. Licensees who desire to renew their licenses shall file an application with the clerk for such renewal (on forms approved by the board) on or before November 1 of each year.
(b)
Renewal applications shall be considered by the board at its first regularly scheduled meeting in December of each year. By affidavit of publisher or his designee, applicant is required to show proof of two newspaper advertisements published during the 30-day period immediately prior to the hearing by the board.
(c)
Any holder of a license issued pursuant to this division who fails to file a proper application for a license for the following year on or before November 1 of each year and who files an application after November 1 shall be required to pay, in addition to the license fee imposed by this chapter, an additional amount equal to one-half the amount required for the license for which application is made.
(d)
The board may grant or deny for cause the renewal of any license.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
Sec. 6-71. - Transfer of license to another person or location.
(a)
No alcoholic beverage license shall be transferred from one person to another during the year in which the license was obtained, except in the case of death of a person holding a license, in which event his executor or administrator may continue to operate under the license for six months from the date of his qualification.
(b)
Upon transfer of ownership of any licensed business hereunder, the new owner may not operate the business under the old license under this article, but must make application for a new license hereunder.
(c)
In the event of the signer of an approved application ceasing to meet the requirements of subsection 6-63(g) hereof, the license is immediately null and void, and application must be made for a new license hereunder.
(d)
No license shall be transferred from one location to another. Each license, other than the license for a licensed alcoholic beverage caterer, is issued for a specific location only and may not be transferred to another location without making a new application.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
Sec. 6-72. - Reserved.

Sec. 6-73. - Suspension or revocation.
(a)
The actions of any person who provides untrue or misleading information or who makes any material omissions in connection with an application for a license under this division shall be attributed to the named licensee. If a license has been granted under such circumstances, the same shall be subject to revocation whether or not the actions or omissions were those of the named licensee.
(b)
Should any licensee be convicted or plead guilty or plead nolo contendere to any felony, such license shall be subject to revocation and cancellation by the board.
(c)
The board shall have the full power and authority to revoke, refuse to renew, suspend or place on probation any license to sell alcoholic beverages for violation of this chapter 6 of the Code of Madison County, article III of Chapter 26 of the Code of Madison County, or violation of any state or federal laws.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
Sec. 6-74. - Display of license.
The county license shall at all times be posted in a conspicuous place within the building, and if said license is not so posted, said business shall not be authorized to sell alcoholic beverages until same shall be posted.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
Sec. 6-75. - Due process.
(a)
Under this chapter, the board has the discretionary powers, within the guidelines of due process as hereinafter defined, to grant, refuse and to suspend or revoke any licenses in accordance with the standards set forth in this division.
(b)
All decisions of the board denying, suspending, or revoking such licenses shall be in writing with the reasons therefore stated and mailed or delivered to the applicant personally within ten days from the date of the decision by the board.
(c)
Upon application within ten days of the receipt of notice, any applicant or license holder aggrieved by the decision of the board regarding the issuance, suspension or revocation shall be afforded a hearing with an opportunity to present evidence and cross-examine opposing witnesses.
(d)
Upon revocation of a license for cause, the license holder shall not be reissued a license at any time.
(Res. of 12-19-2022 , § 1)
