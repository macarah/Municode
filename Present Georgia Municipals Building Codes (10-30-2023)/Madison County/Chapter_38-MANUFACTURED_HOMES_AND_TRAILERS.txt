ARTICLE II. - MOVERS; INSTALLERS

Sec. 38-31. - Registration and transportation permit required.
(a)
Anyone who moves and sets up manufactured homes in the county must be registered with the building inspection department. To register an individual must go to the county building inspection department. Individuals will be required to present state license as an installer which is issued through the state. License must remain current with state and if suspended or revoked will void registration with building inspection office.
(b)
No mover, hauler, or person shall move a mobile or manufactured home into the county, or relocate a mobile or manufactured home existing within the county to another location within or outside the county, without first obtaining a transportation permit from the county building inspector's office.
(Res. of 2-23-2015(1) , §§ A(1)—(3))
Editor's note— Res. adopted Feb. 23, 2015(1) , §§ A(1)—(3), amended § 38-31 in its entirety to read as set out herein. Former § 38-31 pertained to registration required and derived from Ord. adopted April 26, 1994, § 1.
Sec. 38-32. - Maintenance of list; fees; reregistration.
The building inspection department will maintain a list of all registered contractors and keep them informed of any changes in the building codes or interpretations that may affect them. The fee for this registration will be set by the board. Contractors are required to reregister at the beginning of each new year.
(Ord. of 4-26-1994, § 2)
Sec. 38-33. - Permit on file with building inspector; required prior to moving; verification.
A building permit shall be required to be filed in the office of the building inspector for each manufactured home unit which is henceforth located to or moved within the county. Permits are required prior to moving manufactured homes into the county or onto any lots located in the county. Contractors are required to verify issuance of permits or to obtain permits themselves.
(Ord. of 4-26-1994, § 3
Sec. 38-34. - Penalties; enforcement.
(a)
For violations of this article, a citation will be issued for each individual offense.
(b)
This article shall be enforced by the office of the building inspector of the county. Citations for violations may be issued by the chief building inspector of the county.
(c)
Any person violating this article shall be tried before the magistrate court of the county. Upon conviction, a violation of this article may be punished as provided in section 1-9.
(Ord. of 4-26-1994, § 4)
