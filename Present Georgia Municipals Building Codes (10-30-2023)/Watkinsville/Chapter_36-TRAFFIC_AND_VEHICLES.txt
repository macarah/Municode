Sec. 36-2. - Avoiding traffic control device.
It shall be unlawful for the operator of any motor vehicle to drive such motor vehicle through or across any sidewalk, filling station, convenience store, or public way for the purpose of avoiding the requirements of any official control device.
(Code 1982, § 11-102; Code 1994, § 38-2)
Sec. 36-3. - Raised automobiles.
It shall be unlawful for any person to modify the normal height of a motor vehicle so as to cause the rear of the vehicle to be raised higher than the normal height of the vehicle. The first such offense shall result in a warning being issued to the offender and a ten-day period to bring the vehicle into compliance. Failure to comply following proper notice will result in a fine.
(Code 1982, § 32-109; Code 1994, § 38-3)
Sec. 36-4. - Surcharge added to fine schedule.
A 20 percent surcharge will be added to the existing fine schedule to cover the costs of the monthly reports for the peace officer and prosecutor training fund and the peace officers' annuity and benefit fund.
(Code 1994, § 38-4; Ord. of 6-10-1992, § 1)

Sec. 36-27. - Designation of no parking or limited time parking areas.
(a)
Authority to designate. The council shall have, and is hereby granted, authority, from time to time, to designate by appropriate resolution, certain areas upon the streets and roads and public ways within the limits of the city as no parking or limited time parking areas for the parking or stopping of vehicles; and to regulate vehicular and pedestrian traffic upon the streets, roads and public ways; and to designate the same by the erection of appropriate signs or markings thereof.
(b)
Unlawful stopping or parking in designated area. It shall be unlawful for the operator of any motor vehicle to stop or park any vehicle in any area thus designated as a no parking area and/or to exceed the posted parking time limit in a limited time parking area. It shall be unlawful for any pedestrian to use or remain upon any portion of the streets, roads or public ways other than those designated for pedestrian traffic.
(c)
Penalty for violation. Any person convicted of a violation of this section shall be punished by a fine of not less than $10.00 and not more than $25.00 upon a first conviction; and a fine of not more than $50.00 upon a second or subsequent conviction, the violations of which occurred within 30 days of the first violation.
(Code 1982, § 11-103; Code 1994, § 38-6)
