Chapter 22 - MUNICIPAL COURT[1]

Sec. 22-1. - Appointment and qualifications of judge.
No person shall be qualified or eligible to serve as judge unless such person resides in the judicial circuit in which the city is located.
(Code 1982, § 5-102; Code 1994, § 14-2)
Sec. 22-2. - Bailiff.
(a)
The bailiff of the municipal court shall be appointed by the mayor by and with the advice and consent of the city council.
(b)
The duties of the bailiff shall consist generally of seeing that the courtroom is in proper condition for sessions of court, assisting in keeping order while the court is in session, and doing such other acts of assistance as may be required of such bailiff by the judge of the court and the city clerk.
(Code 1982, § 5-103; Code 1994, § 14-3)
Sec. 22-3. - Record of cases.
A record of all cases heard in the municipal court for violation of this Code or other municipal ordinances shall be kept in a suitable bound volume by the city clerk. Such record shall contain the name of the defendant, the nature of the offense charged, the final disposition of the case, and the date of final disposition.
(Code 1982, § 5-104; Code 1994, § 14-4)
Sec. 22-4. - Service of summons.
Any person charged with violating any city ordinance shall receive notice by service of summons as provided in this section. Such summons may be issued by the city clerk, the building inspector or any police officer of the city. The summons shall be directed to the accused and shall distinctly state the offense charged, and the day, hour, and place of trial, requiring the accused to appear before the judge or the municipal court to answer the accusations made. Service of the summons shall be made by a police officer of the city either by serving the accused personally or by leaving a copy at the accused person's most notorious place of abode, except that in the case of a summons issued for violations of laws or ordinances relating to the parking of motor vehicles, such summons may be directed to an unknown person as owner of an automobile designated by the summons and may be served upon such person by leaving a copy in or attached to such automobile.
(Code 1982, § 5-105; Code 1994, § 14-5)
Sec. 22-5. - Subpoenas.
The city clerk shall issue subpoenas for the appearance of all witnesses necessary for the prosecution or for the defense in any case pending before the municipal court. All subpoenas shall be served in the same manner as a summons.
(Code 1982, § 5-106; Code 1994, § 14-6)
Sec. 22-6. - Failure to obey summons or subpoena.
Any person who fails to appear at the time and place set out in any summons or subpoena served upon such person shall be guilty of contempt of court and, upon conviction, shall be punished for such contempt of court.
(Code 1982, § 5-107; Code 1994, § 14-7)
Sec. 22-7. - Arrest and bond.
When a police officer or other authorized law enforcement officer or individual shall have effectuated a proper arrest upon any person for violation of any provision of this Code or any other applicable municipal ordinance or state or federal law, and a trial cannot be had immediately, the arresting officer or individual may allow the arrested person to post bond with good security therefor to ensure such arrested person's appearance before the municipal court or other appropriate court for disposition of said matter. The amount of said bond, the security therefor and the procedures in connection therewith shall in all respects be in conformity with, and the same as, the published procedures, amounts and qualifications as established from time to time by the county sheriff. Upon failure or refusal to post such bond, the arresting officer or individual may confine the arrested person in the county jail or such other similar facility as may be reasonably available until a trial can be held. Further, and in accordance with the bonding procedures as promulgated from time to time by the county sheriff, an arrested person may be released upon such person's own recognizance and without security, pending trial.
(Code 1982, § 5-108; Code 1994, § 14-8)
Sec. 22-8. - Forfeiture of bond.
Upon the failure of a person to appear in the municipal court at the time and place fixed by the summons, the judge of the court shall pass a rule requiring the principal and surety on such bond to show cause on the date named therein, which date shall not be less than ten days from the passage of such ruling, why such principal and surety should not be required to pay the amount of the bond. If no sufficient cause is shown, the judge shall enter judgment against the principal and surety for the amount of the forfeited bond and shall direct the city clerk to issue execution thereon.
(Code 1982, § 5-109; Code 1994, § 14-9)
Sec. 22-9. - Court costs.
The costs shall be charged against a defendant in the municipal court in the event of such defendant's conviction and shall be paid into the city treasury.
(Code 1982, § 5-110; Code 1994, § 14-10)
Sec. 22-10. - Collection of fines.
When directed by the judge of the municipal court, the city clerk shall issue executions for fines imposed by the court, including the costs, which executions may be levied upon any goods or chattels, lands or tenements of the person so fined.
(Code 1982, § 5-112; Code 1994, § 14-12)
