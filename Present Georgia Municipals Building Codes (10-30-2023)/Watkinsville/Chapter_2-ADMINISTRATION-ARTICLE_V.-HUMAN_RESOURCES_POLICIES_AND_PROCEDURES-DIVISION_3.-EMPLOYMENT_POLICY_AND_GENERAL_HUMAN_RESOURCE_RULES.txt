DIVISION 3. - EMPLOYMENT POLICY AND GENERAL HUMAN RESOURCE RULES

Sec. 2-343. - Equal employment opportunity policy.
Per the Civil Rights Act of 1964, as amended by the Equal Employment Opportunity Act of 1972, Watkinsville declares and reaffirms a policy of equal employment opportunity and non-discrimination in providing all services to the public. It is the policy of the city that all employees and applicants for employment shall receive equal opportunity in all employment matters without regard to race, age, color, religion, national origin, sex, political belief or affiliation, veteran status or disability.
(Ord. of 3-6-2020, ch. 3(A))
Sec. 2-344. - Objective.
These policies provide for recruitment of qualified applicants, development of employees and orderly procedures for administering the human resources system per the following principles:
(1)
Recruiting, selecting, and advancing employees on the basis of their relative ability, knowledge, and skill, including open competition of qualified applicants for initial appointment;
(2)
Providing equitable and adequate compensation to employees, within budgetary means;
(3)
Training employees, as needed, to assure high quality of performance;
(4)
Retaining employees on the basis of the adequacy of their performance, correcting inadequate performance, and separating employees whose inadequate performance cannot be corrected;
(5)
Assuring fair treatment of applicants and employees in matters of human resources administration without regard to race, age, color, religion, national origin, sex, political belief or affiliation, veteran status or disability;
(6)
Assuring employees are protected against coercion for partisan political purposes and are prohibited from using official authority for the purpose of interfering with or affecting the results of an election.
(Ord. of 3-6-2020, ch. 3(B))
