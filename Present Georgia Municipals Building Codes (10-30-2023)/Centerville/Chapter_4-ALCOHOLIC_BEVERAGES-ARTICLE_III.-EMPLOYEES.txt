ARTICLE III. - EMPLOYEES

Sec. 4-237. - Employment of minors; entering licensed premises.
(a)
Employment prohibited; exceptions. No licensee shall allow or require a person in the licensee's employment under the age required by state law to dispense, serve, sell or take order for any alcoholic beverage; provided, however, the provisions of this section shall not prohibit persons under the age required by state law who are employed in supermarkets, convenience stores, breweries or drug stores from selling or handling alcoholic beverages which are sold for consumption off the premises.
(b)
Prohibited entry; exceptions. It shall be unlawful for any licensee or licensee's agent or employee to allow any person who is not authorized by law to purchase, possess or consume alcoholic beverages to enter a licensed premises; provided, however, the provisions of this subsection shall not apply to restaurants, convenience stores, drug stores, supermarkets or bona fide private clubs.
State Law reference— Persons under 18 not allowed to serve, sell, or take orders for alcoholic beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-24.
Sec. 4-238. - Duty of license holder to file employee list.
It shall be the duty of all holders of licenses issued pursuant to this article to file with the police chief a list of the home addresses, telephone numbers, and places of employment of any employees, resident managers, or other agents who will be working in or on the licensed premises. Changes in the list of employees, with names of new employees, shall be submitted to the police chief within three business days of said changes. The list required by this section shall be kept on the licensed premises and shall be subject to review at all reasonable times for review by authorized city personnel.
