ARTICLE V. - REGULATIONS AND RESTRICTIONS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-335. - Compliance with chapter required.
No person shall operate an establishment involving the sale of alcoholic beverages without having first complied with the provisions of this chapter.
(Code 1992, § 3-43; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-336. - Off-premises sales prohibited.
No retailer shall sell or deliver any alcoholic beverage to any person except in the retailer's place of business.
(Code 1992, § 3-5; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-337. - Maintenance of premises.
Each licensee under this chapter shall keep and maintain an orderly, respectable and sanitary place of business.
(Code 1992, § 3-6; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-338. - Brown bagging.
It shall be unlawful for a licensee or any other commercial establishment to allow customers to bring with them their own alcoholic beverages, which is known as "BYOB" or "brown bagging."
(Code 1992, § 3-47; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-339. - Storage of inventory or stock.
No licensed retailer shall keep any alcoholic beverage stored in any warehouse in the city, nor shall he enter into any type of arrangement whereby alcoholic beverages ordered by him are stored for him by any licensed wholesaler. A retailer shall keep no inventory or stock or alcoholic beverage at any place except at his licensed place of business and within his licensed place of business. The storage space for alcoholic beverage shall be immediately adjacent to the room in which it is licensed to do business. Further, there shall be but one entrance to the storage area and this shall be through the retail store area.
(Code 1992, § 3-48; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-340. - Open container possession.
It shall be unlawful for any person to drink or have in his possession an open container of any alcoholic beverage while:
(1)
On any public street, sidewalk, park, or other public place within the city, or upon or within any motor vehicle while on the streets, sidewalks, parks and public places of the city.
(2)
While on private property, open to public view, without the express permission of the owner, agent or person in lawful possession thereof.
(3)
On the premises upon which a place of business is conducted and licensed under this article, whether the bottle or other container so opened or consumed is bought or obtained at that place of business or elsewhere unless the establishment is licensed for the consumption on the premises of that particular classification of alcoholic beverage.
(Code 1992, § 3-49; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-341. - Signs of licensed retailers.
All signs of licensed alcoholic beverage retailers shall meet the requirements of the state department of revenue alcohol regulations and shall also comply with the provisions of chapter 46 pertaining to signs.
(Code 1992, § 3-9; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-342. - Posting of sign required.
(a)
The holder of a license to sell alcoholic beverages shall post in the most conspicuous place in his establishment a sign printed in letters at least four inches high reading: "Sale of alcoholic beverages to persons under 21 years of age strictly prohibited."
(b)
All retail consumption dealers and retail dealers in the state who sell at retail any alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises shall post, in a conspicuous place, a sign which clearly reads: "Warning: Drinking alcoholic beverages during pregnancy can cause birth defects." Such signs shall contain letters at least as big as the letters on the warning signs concerning drinking of alcoholic beverages furnished by the state department of revenue.
(Code 1992, § 3-50)
Sec. 4-343. - Security cameras required for certain establishments.
(a)
Video equipment requirements. Any licensee under this chapter who sells any alcoholic beverage packaged to go is required to install a continuous video recording system dedicated to each register area with cameras and lenses of a type, number and location approved by the chief of police or his designee. Such cameras must be capable of producing a retrievable and identifiable image on film or tape that can be made a permanent record and that can be enlarged through the projection or other means. Any establishment remaining open and having three or more employees on duty at all times during business hours are hereby exempted from the provisions of this section.
(b)
Camera maintenance. Cameras meeting the requirements of this section shall be maintained in proper working order at all times and shall be in operation during all hours in which such establishment is opened for business. The camera shall be subject to monthly inspection by the chief of police or his designee and establishments must have immediate availability of a back-up camera system in the event the primary system becomes inoperable. If a crime occurs or an employee believes a crime has occurred, the police department shall be contacted immediately and the film shall be retrieved by a designated police officer.
(Code 1992, § 3-7; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-344. - Adding to or refilling original containers and misrepresenting contents prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to add to the contents of a bottle or to refill empty bottles or in any other manner to misrepresent the quantity, quality or brand name of any alcoholic beverages, spirituous liquors or distilled spirits.
State Law reference— Adulteration or misbranding of alcoholic beverages, O.C.G.A. § 51-1-24.
Sec. 4-345. - Sale to intoxicated persons prohibited.
Sale of alcoholic beverages to any person who is intoxicated or already under the influence of any alcoholic beverages is prohibited.
State Law reference— Similar provision, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-22
Secs. 4-346—4-363. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - LOCATION RESTRICTIONS

Sec. 4-364. - Building to be complete; attachment of evidence of ownership or copy of lease to application.
No alcoholic beverage license shall be issued to any person unless the building in which the establishment will be located is complete, and every applicant for a retail license shall attach to his application evidence of ownership or lease of the premises as required herein.
(Code 1992, § 3-42; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-365. - Location must comply with state law and city zoning regulations.
No license shall be granted to any person for the operation of an establishment offering the sale of alcoholic beverages in any area prohibited by law or by the zoning regulations for the city.
(Code 1992, § 3-40; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-366. - Proximity of premises to churches, schools, colleges, public library, nursing homes.
(a)
No new license shall be issued for the sale of any beer, wine, malt beverages, or distilled spirits for a location nor shall any existing license be transferred to a new location which will be in or within 300 feet of a church building or within 600 feet of any school building, school grounds, college campus, public library or nursing home.
(b)
For the purpose of this section, distance shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground.
(c)
Each application for license shall show the location of the proposed building and the distance to the nearest church, nursing home, public library, school ground and private residence.
(d)
The school grounds and college campuses, referred to in this section, shall apply only to state, county, city or church school grounds and college campuses and to such other schools and colleges as teach the subjects commonly taught in the common schools and colleges of the state.
(e)
No distance requirements shall apply to any establishment within these provisions if such licensed premises is located within the city designated downtown area as registered with the state secretary of state's office.
(f)
The provisions of this section shall not apply at any location for which a license has been issued prior to the adoption of the ordinance from which this chapter is derived, nor to the renewal of such license.
(Code 1992, § 3-44; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21.

DIVISION 3. - HOURS AND DAYS OF OPERATION

Sec. 4-391. - Legal hours for sales and purchases.
(a)
Package stores. The sale of package distilled spirits shall be permitted at any time between 8:00 a.m. and 11:45 p.m. on Monday through Saturday and between 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m. on Sunday. No retail dealer or licensee for package sales or of distilled spirits shall allow any persons inside the establishment after said hours other than regular employees who are engaged in activities to close, clean or stock the establishment, or if the doors to the establishment are unlocked. The only exception to the foregoing may be caused by emergency repairs to equipment and/or facilities.
(b)
Beer and wine packaged to go. Licensees whose only sale of alcoholic beverages consist of beer (malt beverages) and/or wine by the package, may sell such beverages at any time except between 12:00 a.m. and 12:30 p.m. on Sunday and between 11:30 p.m. on Sunday and 12:00 a.m. on Monday.
(c)
All alcoholic beverages consumed on-premises. Licensees for the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on-premises shall not keep such establishment open between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 8:00 a.m. on any day of the week, nor at any time on Sundays. Licensees shall not sell alcoholic beverages between 12:00 midnight on Saturday and 10:59 a.m. on Sunday provided, however that licensees may be open but not sell alcoholic beverages from 12:00 midnight on Saturday until 11:00 a.m. on Sunday. For purposes of this section, establishments will be considered "open" if, at any time past 2:00 a.m., there are any persons inside the establishment other than regular employees who are engaged in activities to close, clean or stock the establishment, or if the doors to the establishment are unlocked. The only exception to the above may be caused by emergency repairs to equipment and/or facilities. Nothing contained in this section shall allow areas commonly regarded as lounges or bars to be open for any reason on Sunday or on Christmas Day.
(d)
Dual licenses. If any establishment holds both a on-premises consumption license and packaged-to-go license for alcoholic beverages, then the hours governing establishments holding a on-premises consumption license shall prevail.
(e)
Election day. The sale of alcoholic beverages by the package and for consumption on the premises shall be lawful during the polling hours of any election; provided, however, that nothing herein shall authorize the sale of alcoholic beverages within 250 feet of a polling place during such time as the polls are open.
(f)
Sales on Christmas Day. No licensee shall sell or offer for sale any alcoholic beverages at any time on Christmas Day. Licensees of food and restaurant establishments and stores open on Christmas Day will be responsible to ensure that alcoholic beverages on display are plainly marked with a sign stating "Not For Sale."
(g)
Alcohol percent labels. It shall be unlawful for any person to have in his possession, custody or control any spirituous liquor for retail sale unless the container shall have firmly affixed thereto a label on which there is stated in plain words or figures, the true alcoholic content by volume of the alcoholic beverage.
(h)
Selling beer to unauthorized persons. Licensee shall not give, sell or offer for sale any alcoholic beverage to any person who is noticeably intoxicated or who is of unsound mind, or who is a drunkard whose intemperate habits are known to the license holder or any of his agents or employees.
(Code 1992, § 3-45; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007; Ord. No. 2008-12, 12-16-2008; Ord. No. 2009-04, 7-7-2009; Ord. No. 2011-6, 11-22-2011; Ord. No. 2019-6, 11-19-2019 )
State Law reference— Sale of alcoholic beverages on Sunday, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-7; sale of alcoholic beverages on Sundays and election days generally, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20; Sunday sales by farm wineries, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-21.2; Sunday sales of distilled spirits by private clubs, O.C.G.A. § 3-7-2.
Sec. 4-392. - Sunday sales permits.
(a)
Any establishment holding a license for consumption on the premises, as defined in this chapter, deriving at least 50 percent of its total annual gross sales from the sale of meals or food prepared, served, and consumed on the premises located within the city limits of Centerville and any such licensed establishment which derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross income from the rental of rooms for overnight lodging is authorized to sell and serve distilled spirits, wine or malt beverages by the drink on Sunday from 11:00 a.m. until 11:30 p.m., provided that the establishment holds a valid city license to sell and serve distilled spirits, wine or malt beverages by the drink. A separate license for such Sunday sales shall not be required in addition to the regular, annual license which shall cover sales for all seven days of the week subject to the times set forth for hours of sale under this chapter.
(b)
License holders who plan to offer for sale and to serve distilled spirits, wine and malt beverages for consumption on the premises shall submit to the city clerk's office a certification by a public accountant that the applicant meets one of the requirements set out in subsection (a) of this section. This certification shall be based upon a review of the applicant's total gross annual income for the licensed location during the 12 months immediately preceding the initial or renewal license application. If the applicant has been in business less than 12 months at the proposed location, then such certification shall be for the time that the applicant has been in business, annualized. If the applicant is filing an application for a new business, a certification from a public accountant will not be required for the initial application, but instead, the applicant must certify under oath that the person applying for the new license intends to meet the requirements set out in subsection (a) of this section. A certification from a public accountant will be required upon each renewal thereafter.
(c)
The application shall be accompanied by the permit fee specified in the city fee schedule in chapter 22 of the Centerville Code of Ordinances.
(d)
Upon determination by the city clerk's office that the applicant meets the requirements for offering for sale and serving distilled spirits, wine and malt beverages for consumption on the premises in accordance with subsections (a) and (b), above, the city clerk shall issue the license, which shall be kept permanently and prominently displayed by the licensee within the licensed location. It shall be a violation of this Code for any licensee to fail to display such permit as provided in this subsection.
(e)
The determination of an establishment's eligibility to offer for sale and to serve distilled spirits, wine and malt beverages on Sunday shall be made annually upon the renewal of the on-premises consumption license. Prior to renewal of such license, the licensee shall submit current information regarding eligibility as required in subsection (b) of this section. During the 12-month term of the license, the establishment shall remain in full compliance at all times with the requirements set out in subsection (a) of this section.
(f)
Any person who shall give materially false statements in an application for a license to offer for sale and to serve distilled spirits, wine and malt beverages or who shall offer for sale and serve distilled spirits, wine and malt beverages on Sunday after the licensee knows it is no longer qualified for such Sunday sales under the provisions of this section, or who otherwise fails to comply with any of the provisions of this section shall be deemed in violation of this Code and upon conviction, shall be punished in the Municipal Court of the City of Centerville as provided in section 1-15. If the establishment's license for consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises is suspended, probated, or revoked as provided in this chapter, then the privilege of Sunday sales is also suspended, probated or revoked in the same manner.
(g)
Individuals attending any function between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m. on Sunday at a licensed establishment which does not meet the requirements set out in subsection (a) of this section for Sunday sales, where alcoholic beverages are to be furnished shall not be charged any type of admission or cover charge.
(Code 1992, § 3-46; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007; Ord. No. 2011-6, 11-22-2011; Ord. No. 2019-6, 11-19-2019 )
State Law reference— Sale of alcoholic beverages on Sunday, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-7; sale of alcoholic beverages on Sundays and election days generally, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20; Sunday sales by farm wineries, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-21.2; Sunday sales of distilled spirits by private clubs, O.C.G.A. § 3-7-2.

DIVISION 4. - MINORS

Sec. 4-413. - Prohibited acts by persons under 21 years of age.
Except as otherwise authorized by law and this Code, it shall be unlawful for any person under the age of 21 years to:
(1)
Purchase or consume alcoholic beverages.
(2)
Have in his possession and control any alcoholic beverages.
(3)
Allow anyone to purchase from him any alcoholic beverages.
(4)
Attempt to or enter the premises of an establishment for the purchase of alcoholic beverages.
(Code 1992, § 3-51; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-414. - Providing or selling alcoholic beverages prohibited.
It shall be unlawful to sell or furnish in any manner whatsoever alcoholic beverages to any person under the age of 21 years, except that beer, malt beverages, and wine in reasonable quantities may be sold and served at tables in restaurants and food type establishments to persons age 21 years or older for consumption by such persons only, when accompanied by persons under the age of 21 years.
Sec. 4-415. - Persons under 21 years prohibited in premises licensed to sell distilled spirits.
No person under the age of 21 years shall enter into a package store which sells distilled spirits.
(Code 1992, § 3-51; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 4-416. - Persons under 18 years prohibited from taking orders for alcoholic beverages.
Persons who are 18 years or older may take orders for alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises of restaurants.
Sec. 4-417. - Exception for private functions.
This division shall not prohibit invited persons under 21 years of age from being present in separate and distinct rooms restricted to private functions such as wedding parties and not open to the general public. Such persons shall not be permitted to consume alcoholic beverages.
(Code 1992, § 3-51; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
State Law reference— Sale to underage persons prohibited, O.C.G.A. §§ 3-3-23, 3-3-23.1; restrictions on employment of underage persons, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-24.
Sec. 4-418. - Identification check.
It shall be the duty of any person selling or otherwise furnishing alcoholic beverages to any person to request to see and to be furnished with proper identification in order to verify the age of such person. For purposes of this section, the term "proper identification" means any document issued by a governmental agency containing a description of the person, such person's photograph, or both, and giving such person's date of birth, and includes, without being limited to, a passport, military identification card, driver's license, or an identification card authorized under the O.C.G.A §§ 40-5-100 through 40-5-104. The term "proper identification" does not include a birth certificate and does not include any traffic citation or complaint form.
(Code 1992, § 3-10; Ord. No. 2007-11, 12-4-2007)
