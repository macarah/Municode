ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 12-1. - Minors.
It shall be unlawful for any parent, guardian, or other person having the custody or control of any minor to permit, allow, or encourage such minor to violate any section of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-2. - Commercial soliciting and soliciting for funds in residential areas.
The following provisions shall serve the interests of safety, fraud, and the protection of residents' privacy. This section shall not be construed to include non-commercial door-to-door canvassing of any sort, which is protected by the United States Constitution, requires no permit, and may be done anonymously.
(a)
Any person engaged in or desiring to engage in the sale of any product or service, or the solicitation of funds by going door-to-door in residential areas of the city, whether on a temporary or permanent basis, shall first register with the department of public safety of the county by completing a personal information questionnaire and receiving a personal identification card which shall be the registrant's permit to engage in the sale of products or services.
(b)
The department of public safety shall provide each person completing such questionnaire with an identification card of suitable size which shall have thereon the following information:
(1)
The name of the card bearer; and
(2)
The card bearer's residence and telephone number; and
(3)
The card bearer's nationality; and
(4)
The card bearer's date of birth; and
(5)
The card bearer's physical characteristics, including height, weight, hair color, eye color, color of complexion; and
(6)
The card bearer's gender; and
(7)
The card bearer's photograph; and
(8)
The card bearer's thumb print; and
(9)
Where applicable, any other identifying physical characteristics of the card bearer, such as scars or birthmarks; and
(10)
The name and address of the business represented; and
(11)
The expiration date, which shall be between sixty (60) days and one (1) year from the date of issue; and
(12)
The card bearer's personal signature; and
(13)
The signature or facsimile of the director of public safety.
(c)
Violations. It shall be unlawful to commit any of the following acts when engaging in the sale of products or services or the solicitation of funds:
(1)
To engage in the sale of products or services or the solicitation of funds without having first registered with the police department and receiving the identification card; or
(2)
To engage in the sale of products or services or the solicitation of funds without having the identification card on the person; or
(3)
To lend one's identification card to any other person; or
(4)
To use the identification card of another for any purpose whatsoever; or
(5)
To refuse to show such identification card upon the request of any police officer, the occupant of any residence, apartment, apartment complex, or apartment complex manager or owner where registrant is making a call; or
(6)
To engage in the sale of products or services or the solicitation of funds where the resident has clearly displayed a sign which reads "no soliciting" or any sign to that effect.
(d)
Other:
(1)
The questionnaire to be completed by the person seeking an identification card shall be furnished by the police chief.
(2)
Any identification card bearer shall have the right to receive a new identification card upon application any time after the expiration of the current card.
(3)
Any resident of the city may display on his or her property a sign reading "no soliciting" or any sign to that effect, provided that the sign is constructed and displayed in conformity with chapter 15.5 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-3. - Picketing.
(a)
Location.
(1)
Picketing and demonstrations shall be conducted only on the public sidewalks maintained by the city, except that picketing and demonstrations may take place on the grassy area of Market Street, no more than six (6) feet from the curb.
(2)
Picketing and demonstrations shall be conducted in a manner that does not hamper, obstruct, impede, or otherwise interfere with pedestrian or motor vehicle traffic.
(3)
Picketing and demonstrations shall not be conducted in front of or be focused on any particular residence.
(4)
Picketing and demonstrations shall be conducted in a way that does not block entrances to any building, area, or other structure.
(5)
Picketing and demonstrations shall not hamper, obstruct, impede, or otherwise interfere with the ability to enter or exit a building, area, or any other structure.
(b)
Notice.
(1)
The chief of police shall receive at least five (5) hours of notice in writing by the person planning to picket or demonstrate.
(2)
This written notice shall include the name of the person planning to picket or demonstrate, and where applicable, the name of the organization planning to picket, and shall also include the name of the places or business, public facility or other location or entity which will be picketed and the hours that the picketing or demonstration will be conducted.
(3)
At the request of the chief of police, the person planning to picket or demonstrate may be required to provide the city with an agreement to indemnify and save the city harmless for any damages, demands, or expenses of every character which may be in any way caused by the picketing or demonstration.
(c)
Other.
(1)
No person participating in picketing or demonstrations shall create any loud, disturbing, and unnecessary noise, including any noise that is in violation of section 10-20 of this Code.
(2)
No person participating in picketing or a demonstration shall use a bullhorn, microphone, loudspeaker, or any other means of magnifying one's voice.
(3)
No person shall unreasonably hamper, obstruct, impede, or otherwise interfere with any picketing or demonstration.
(4)
If at any time picketing or a demonstration in any way obstructs, impedes, or otherwise interferes with any type of motor vehicle or pedestrian traffic, including traffic moving in and out of a building, participants shall disperse or move along as directed by a police officer.
It shall be unlawful to refuse to disperse or move along if so directed by a police officer.
(5)
Picketers and demonstrators shall not act in a way that violates any provision of this chapter or any other part of this Code, or the laws of this state.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)

Sec. 12-5. - Fortunetelling.
It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in fortunetelling, palm reading, or any other act purporting to predict the future, where a charge, donation, or profit is made in exchange for such services.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-6. - Pawnbrokers, secondhand dealers, auction houses and junk dealers.
(a)
Definitions.
(1)
Junk. Any scrap, old scrap automobiles, iron, brass, copper, tin, lead, rags, mutilated bags and sacks, rope, empty bottles, paper or bagging, rubber tires, batteries, tubes, boots and shoes, bones and hides, hose and electrical wiring or other articles of any kind that are useless for their original purpose.
(3)
Junk dealer. Any person who shall purchase or make a business of purchasing, whether as principal or agent, junk, as defined in this section.
(b)
Requirements.
(1)
All pawnbrokers, dealers in secondhand articles, auction houses and junk dealers shall furnish to the chief of police a full and complete list each day of every article taken in pawn, or bought by such dealers, giving a full description of such article, with the mark, number, brand, monogram, or letters, numbers, or other distinguishing features of any kind, on such articles so pawned or bought by such dealers in their places of business or outside their places of business. This list shall include fingerprinting of the right index finger of each person who pawns any article on the date for which the daily report is made.
(2)
All pawnbrokers, dealers in secondhand articles, auction houses and junk dealers shall keep accounts, showing from whom all such articles were received and the hour and day of receipt, and they shall have their goods arranged in stock so as to enable the goods to be inspected by the police department. When it becomes necessary to visit any such dealer, the chief of police will give a written order to the proper officer to perform such duty and such dealer shall permit such officer to examine any article in such dealer's stock.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-7. - Shoplifting law adopted.
Pursuant to the Official Georgia Code, § 16-8-14 of the Georgia Code, as amended, or as may hereafter from time to time be amended, known as "Theft by Shoplifting" is hereby adopted as an ordinance of this city, with like effect as if fully set out herein. Unless another penalty is expressly provided herein or by state law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-7 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-8. - Restriction on outdoor water of landscape.
Outdoor watering for purposes of planting, growing, managing, or maintaining ground cover, trees, shrubs, or other plants may occur only between the hours of 4:00 p.m. and 10:00 a.m.; provided, however, that this limitation shall not create any limitation upon the following outdoor water uses:
(1)
Commercial raising, harvesting, or storing of crops; feeding, breeding, or managing livestock or poultry; the commercial production or storing of feed for use in the production of livestock, including, but not limited to, cattle, calves, swine, hogs, goats, sheep, and rabbits, or for use in the production of poultry, including, but not limited to, chickens, hens, ratites, and turkeys; producing plants, trees, fowl, or animals; or the commercial production of aquacultural, horticultural, dairy, livestock, poultry, eggs, and apiarian products or as otherwise defined in O.C.G.A. § 1-3-3;
(2)
Capture and reuse of cooling system condensate or storm water in compliance with applicable city ordinances and state guidelines;
(3)
Reuse of gray water in compliance with O.C.G.A. § 31-3-5.2 and applicable local board of health regulations;
(4)
Use of reclaimed waste water by a designated user from a system permitted by the Environmental Protection Division of the Georgia Department of Natural Resources to provide reclaimed waste water;
(5)
Watering personal food gardens;
(6)
Watering new and replanted plant, seed, or turf in landscapes, golf courses, or sports turf fields during installation and for a period of thirty (30) days immediately following the date of installation;
(7)
Drip irrigation or irrigation using soaker hoses;
(8)
Hand watering with a hose with automatic cutoff or handheld container;
(9)
Use of water withdrawn from private water wells or surface water by an owner or operator of property if such well or surface water is on said property;
(10)
Watering horticultural crops held for sale, resale, or installation;
(11)
Watering athletic fields, golf courses, or public turf grass recreational areas;
(12)
Installation, maintenance, or calibration of irrigation systems; or
(13)
Hydroseeding.
(Ord. No. 353, § 1, 1-1-11)
Sec. 12-9. - Enforcement of watering restrictions.
(a)
No person shall use or allow the use of water in violation of the restrictions on outdoor water use contained in section 12-8.
(b)
The police department shall be the enforcement authority for sections 12-8 and 12-9. The city manager may also authorize other departments as may be deemed necessary to support enforcement.
(c)
Any violation of sections 12-8 and 12-9 may be enforced by a citation returnable to the municipal court or by any other legal means as set forth in this Code.
(Ord. No. 353, § 1, 1-1-11)

ARTICLE II. - OFFENSES AGAINST PUBLIC PEACE AND ORDER

Sec. 12-20. - Disorderly conduct.
It shall be unlawful and shall constitute disorderly conduct for any person within the city limits to violate any of the following provisions of this section:
(1)
To act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another whereby any person is placed in fear of safety of his life, limb or health; or
(2)
To act in a violent or tumultuous whereby the property of any person is placed in danger of being destroyed or damaged; or
(3)
To endanger or interfere with the lawful pursuits of another by acts of violence, angry threats, or abusive conduct; or
(4)
To be found jostling or roughly crowding or pushing any person in any public place; or
(5)
To assemble in or to frequent any public place for deceitful, fraudulent, or unlawful purposes, or to aid and abet therein, as defined by the current ordinances of the city or this state; or
State Law reference— Theft by deception, O.G.C.A. § 16-8-3.
(6)
To accost or attempt to force one's company upon any person; or
(7)
To utter while in a state of anger in the presence of another any obscene words or epithets or "fighting words;" or
(8)
To aid, abet, or encourage a minor to do any act which constitutes disorderly conduct or a breach of the peace.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-21. - Public indecency.
(a)
Prohibited conduct. A person commits the act of public indecency when he or she performs any of the following acts in a public place:
(1)
An exposure of the sexual organs;
(2)
An appearance in a state of partial or complete nudity; or
(3)
An act of sexual intercourse; or
(4)
Urinates or defecates in a public place other than a bathroom facility; or
(5)
A caress or indecent fondling of the body of another person.
(b)
Exceptions. The prohibited conduct set forth in subsections 12-21(a)(1) and (2) of this chapter shall not apply to the following persons:
(1)
Any child under the age of ten (10); or
(2)
Any female person exposing a breast while breastfeeding an infant under two (2) years old.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-22. - Consumption of alcoholic beverages.
A person unlawfully consumes alcoholic beverages when he or she commits any of the following acts:
(1)
To consume alcoholic beverages or transport open containers of alcoholic beverages on or upon public areas or places of business patronized by the public in an intoxicated condition; or
(2)
To be and appear in any public area or place of business patronized by the public in any intoxicated condition; or
(3)
To drink any intoxicating beverage in any automobile on the streets of the city, whether such automobile is moving or parked; or
(4)
To drink or possess any intoxicating beverage at any public place within the city; or
(5)
To bring or carry into any enclosure, field, or stadium where events sponsored by or participated in are being held any intoxicating beverage, or to have any intoxicating beverage in his or her possession while in or on such enclosure, field, or stadium.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-23. - Interference with public transportation.
A person unlawfully interferes with public transportation when he or she acts with the intent to obstruct, hinder, interfere with, or otherwise disrupt or disturb the operation or operator of a public school bus or any other vehicle used for public transportation.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-24. - Sale of ice cream, etc., near schools.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or offer for sale, within two hundred and fifty (250) yards of any public school building located within the city, between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 4:00 p.m., any ice cream, milk, fruit, or other edible products from a pushcart, truck, or other conveyance, or by means other than from a regulatory established building or store duly licensed to operate such a building.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-25. - Loitering.
(a)
Generally. A person commits the act of loitering when the person is knowingly in a place at a time or in a manner not usual for a law-abiding individual under the circumstances that warrant a justifiable and reasonable alarm or immediate concern for the safety of persons or property in the vicinity.
(1)
Among the circumstances which may be considered in determining whether alarm is warranted is the fact that the person takes flight upon the appearance of a law enforcement officer, or refuses to identify himself or herself, or manifestly endeavors to conceal himself or herself or any object.
(2)
Unless flight by the person or other circumstances make it impracticable, a law enforcement officer shall, prior to any arrest for an offense under this section, afford the person an opportunity to dispel any alarm or immediate concern which would otherwise be warranted by his or her presence or conduct.
(3)
No person shall be convicted of any offense under this section if the law enforcement officer failed to comply with the foregoing procedure or if it appears that the explanation given by the person was true and would have dispelled the alarm or immediate concern.
(b)
For the purpose of procuring others to engage in sexual acts for hire.
(1)
It shall be unlawful for any person to loiter, while a pedestrian or in a motor vehicle, in or near any thoroughfare or place open to the public, parking lots of hotels and motels, in a manner and under circumstances manifesting the purpose of inducing, enticing, soliciting or procuring another to commit an act of prostitution, sodomy, masturbation for hire or pandering. Among the circumstances which may be considered in determining whether this purpose is manifested are the following: that that person is a known prostitute, pimp, sodomist, masturbator for hire or panderer and repeatedly beckons to, stops or attempts to stop, or engages passersby in conversation, or repeatedly stops, or attempts to stop motor vehicle operators by hailing, or waving of arms or any bodily gesture for the purpose of inducing, enticing, soliciting or procuring another to commit an act of prostitution, sodomy, masturbation for hire or pandering. No arrest shall be made for a violation of this subsection unless the arresting officer first affords that person the opportunity to explain his conduct, and no one shall be convicted of violating subsection (b) if it appears at trial that the explanation given was true and disclosed a lawful purpose.
(2)
For the purpose of subsection (b)(1), "known prostitute, pimp, sodomist, panderer or masturbator for hire" is a person who, previous to the date of arrest for violation of this section, had been convicted of a violation of a sexual related law of any city or law of any state defining and punishing acts of soliciting, committing or offering or agreeing to commit prostitution, sodomy, masturbation for hire or pandering.
(3)
For the purpose of subsection (b)(1), "any person" shall also include panderers or solicitors of sexual acts, commonly referred to as "johns" or "tricks," who loiter in a manner and under circumstances manifesting the purpose of participating in, procuring, purchasing or soliciting any sexual act for hire made illegal by state law. Among the circumstances which may be considered in determining whether this purpose is manifested are the following: that that person, while a pedestrian or in a motor vehicle, repeatedly beckons to, attempts to stop, engages or attempts to engage in conversation with any person by hailing, waving arms or any bodily gesture for the purpose of inducing, enticing, soliciting or procuring another to commit an act of prostitution, sodomy, masturbation for hire or pandering.
State Law reference— Authority of city to adopt ordinances proscribing loitering and related acts for purposes of procuring others to engage in sexual acts for hire, O.C.G.A. § 16-6-24.
(c)
For the purpose of engaging in drug-related activity.
(1)
It shall be unlawful for any person to loiter in or near any thoroughfare, place open to the public, or near any public or private place in a manner and under circumstances manifesting an intent to engage in drug-related activity contrary to any provision of Title 16, Chapter 13, of the Official Code of Georgia (O.C.G.A. § 16-13-1 et seq.)
(2)
Among the circumstances which may be considered in determining whether such intent is manifested are the following:
a.
Such person is a known unlawful drug user, possessor, or seller. For the purposes of this chapter, a "known unlawful drug user, possessor, or seller" is a person who has, within the knowledge of the arresting officer, been convicted in any court within this state of any violation involving the use, possession, or sale of any controlled substance as defined in Title 16, Chapter 13 of the Official Code of Georgia (O.C.G.A. § 16-13-1 et seq.), or such person who has been convicted of any violation of any of the provisions of such title and chapter of the Official Code of Georgia, or substantially similar laws of any political subdivision of this state or of any other state; or
b.
Such person is currently subject to a court order prohibiting his or her presence in a high drug activity geographic area; or
c.
Such person is physically identified by the officer as a member of a "gang," or any other association which has been known to be engaged in illegal drug activity; or
d.
Such person takes flight or manifestly endeavors to conceal himself upon the appearance of a police officer; or
e.
Such person manifestly endeavors to conceal any object which reasonably could be involved in an unlawful drug-related activity; or
f.
Such person manifestly possesses any instrument, article, or thing whose customary or primary purpose is for the sale, administration or use of controlled substances, including, but not limited to the following: crack pipes, push wires, chore boys, hand scales, hypodermic needles, razor blades, or other cutting tools; or
g.
Such person is seen driving or sitting in a vehicle that is registered to a known unlawful drug user, possessor, or seller, or a person for whom there is an outstanding warrant for a crime involving drug-related activity.
(d)
Minors. It shall be unlawful for any parent, guardian, or other person having the custody or control of any minor to permit, allow, or encourage such minor to violate this section.
(e)
Responsibility of proprietor, managers, etc. It shall be unlawful for the proprietor, manager, or other person having charge or control of any public or other place to permit, allow, or encourage any person to violate this section.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)

(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-28. - Noise.

(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)

It shall be unlawful for any person to knowingly and willfully obstruct or hinder any law enforcement officer, firefighter, EMS personnel, paramedic or other emergency personnel when such person is in the lawful discharge of his or her duties.
(b)

Fails or refuses to submit to arrest;
(2)
Endangers or interferes with any law enforcement officer by taking flight upon the appearance of a law enforcement officer;
(3)
Endangers or interferes with any law enforcement officer by aiding another person in taking flight or by concealing another who is taking flight in any manner;
(4)

(5)
Fails or refuses to stop for a safety checkpoint or takes flight to avoid a safety checkpoint.
(Ord. No. 290, § 1, 4-5-05)
ARTICLE III. - OFFENSES AGAINST PUBLIC SAFETY

Sec. 12-30. - Discharging weapons.
It shall be unlawful for any person to fire or discharge any gun, pistol, cannon, air rife, or to shoot a slingshot within the city, except when necessary for law enforcement officers or for the purpose of self defense, or by military officials when on drill or parade or at a funeral in honor of the dead.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-31. - Knives.
It shall be unlawful to have, possess, or carry upon the streets or other public places of the city any of the following implements, unless such implements are securely wrapped so as to be not readily available for harmful use:
(1)
Any knife which has a blade which opens automatically by hand pressure applied to a button, spring, or other device in the handle, the blade of which when open projects from the handle more than two (2) inches.
(2)
Any knife which has an obstruction or other device of any kind which holds the blade partly open, the blade of which when open projects from the handle more than two (2) inches.
(3)
Any knife of any kind the blade of which, when open, projects from the handle more than three (3) inches including all knives such as butcher knives or other knives that do not close.
(4)
Any ice pick or similar device having a sharp point; provided, however, that this subsection shall not apply to any person delivering ice.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-32. - Minors.
In the event of the conviction under section 12-30 or 12-31 of any person who is a minor, the chief of police shall seize and retain the weapon or other instrument until any fine and costs imposed are paid.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-33. - Criminal trespass by motor vehicle.
(a)
A person commits the offense of criminal trespass by motor vehicle when the person, after having been requested not to do so by a law enforcement officer or by the owner or an authorized agent of the owner, parks or stands an occupied or unoccupied motor vehicle in, or repeatedly drives a motor vehicle through or within, a parking area which is located on privately owned property and is provided by a merchant, a group of merchants, or a shopping center or other similar facility for customers if:
(1)
The parking area is identified by at least one sign as specified in this paragraph, and if the parking area contains more than one hundred fifty (150) parking spaces then by at least one (1) such sign for every one hundred fifty (150) parking spaces, each such sign containing the following information in easy to read print:
a.
Notice of the elements of the crime of criminal trespass by motor vehicle;
b.
Identification of the property which is reserved for customer use only;
c.
Identification of the merchant, group of merchants, or shopping center or other similar facility providing the parking area; and
d.
Warning that violators will be prosecuted.
(2)
The motor vehicle is parked, is standing, or is being operated other than for the purpose of:
a.
Transporting some person to or from the interior of the place of business of a merchant identified by the sign or signs in the parking area or to or from the interior of the shopping center or other facility so identified;
b.
Making use of a telephone, vending machine, automated teller machine, or other similar facility located in the parking area; or
c.
Carrying out an activity for which express permission has been given by the owner of the parking area or any authorized representative of the owner.
(b)
Any person violating this section shall be subject to a monetary fine:
(1)
Not to exceed fifty dollars ($50.00) for the first such violation;
(2)
Not to exceed one hundred dollars ($100.00) for the second such violation; and
(3)
Not to exceed one hundred fifty dollars ($150.00) for the third and each subsequent violation.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-34. - Criminal trespass.
(a)
A person commits the offense of criminal trespass when he or she intentionally damages any property of another without consent of that other person and the damage thereto is five hundred dollars ($500.00) or less or knowingly and maliciously interferes with the possession or use of the property of another person without consent of that person.
(b)
A person commits the offense of criminal trespass when he or she knowingly and without authority:
(1)
Enters upon the land or premises of another person or into any part of any vehicle, railroad car, aircraft, or watercraft of another person for an unlawful purpose;
(2)
Enters upon the land or premises of another person or into any part of any vehicle, railroad car, aircraft, or watercraft of another person after receiving, prior to such entry, notice from the owner, rightful occupant, or, upon proper identification, an authorized representative of the owner or rightful occupant that such entry is forbidden; or
(3)
Remains upon the land or premises of another person or within the vehicle, railroad car, aircraft, or watercraft of another person after receiving notice from the owner, rightful occupant, or, upon proper identification, an authorized representative of the owner or rightful occupant to depart.
(c)

Sec. 12-35. - Carrying a concealed weapon.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to carry a concealed weapon when such person knowingly has or carries about his or her person, unless in an open manner and fully exposed to view, any bludgeon, metal knuckles, firearm, knife designed for the purpose of offense and defense, or any knife in violation of section 12-31 of this Code of Ordinances, or any of its subsections, in whole or in part, or any other dangerous or deadly weapon or instrument of like character outside of his home or place of business.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to carry any concealed weapon in violation of this section 12-35 when such person knowingly has or carries in his or her vehicle in a manner in which such weapon is concealed in any manner unless the weapon is carried in an open manner and fully exposed to view or unless the weapon is in the glove compartment, console, trunk, or similar compartment so that the weapon is not available for immediate possession.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to be in the presence of or have knowledge of a concealed weapon in violation of this section 12-35 and fail to notify a law enforcement officer of the presence of such concealed weapon.
(d)
The provisions of this code section 12-35 shall not apply to law enforcement officers, peace officers retired from state or federal law enforcement agencies, judges, magistrates, solicitors-general or district attorneys, or to any person in possession of a valid permit issued according to O.C.G.A § 16-11-129.
(Ord. No. 290, § 1, 4-5-05)

ARTICLE IV. - OFFENSES AGAINST GOVERNMENT OPERATIONS

Sec. 12-40. - Defacing of official signs, notices, etc.
No person shall deface, obliterate, remove, take down, disturb, or in any other manner interfere with any sign bearing the name of any street or public place, or any bulletin board, or sign or notice erected, posted, or placed by the authority of the city council or any officer of the city.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-41. - Alarm systems.
(a)
Definitions.
(1)
Alarm system. Any assembly of equipment and devices designated and arranged to detect and signal an unauthorized intrusion into a premise or to signal an attempted robbery at a protected premise, and with respect to such signal a public safety officer is expected to respond.
(2)
False alarm. The activation of a burglary or robbery alarm by other than a forced entry or attempted forced entry to the premises and at a time when no burglary or robbery is taking place on the premises.
(3)
Premises. Any dwelling, business or public establishment, or, where more than one such dwelling, business or public establishment is situated in a common development, "premises" means the entire such development.
(4)
Telephonic alarm system. Any mechanism, equipment or device which is designed to operate automatically through the use of telephone facilities to transmit a signal, message or warning to another location.
(b)
Prohibitions.
(1)
It is unlawful for anyone to activate any robbery or burglary alarm for the purpose of summoning a police officer except in the event of an actual or attempted robbery or burglary.
(2)
It is unlawful for a person to notify a police officer of an activated alarm when he or she knows that such activation was apparently caused by an electrical or other malfunction of the alarm system, or was apparently caused by maintenance or service of the alarm system, without notifying the police officer of such apparent malfunction or repair.
(c)
Fees. For a police officer's response to any false alarm, the director of public safety shall charge and collect from the person or business having or maintaining such burglary and/or robbery alarm system on the premises owned and occupied by him or her, fees as follows:
(1)
For a first response to premises at which no other false alarm has occurred within a calendar year period, no fee shall be charged, but the person having or maintaining such burglary and/or robbery alarm shall, within three (3) working days after notice to do so, make a written report to the director of public safety on forms prescribed by him or her setting forth cause of such false alarm, the corrective action taken, whether such alarm has been inspected by an authorized serviceman, and such other information as the director of public safety may require to determine the cause of such false alarm and corrective action necessary.
(2)
For a second response to premises after a first response within a calendar year period, a fifteen-dollar ($15.00) fee shall be charged and paid within ten (10) days' of notice and same, and a written report shall be required as for a first response and the director of public safety shall be authorized to inspect or cause to be inspected the alarm system at such premises and to prescribe necessary corrective action.
(3)
For a third response to premises after a second response within a calendar year, a twenty-dollar ($20.00) fee shall be charged and paid within ten (10) days' notice of same, and a written report shall be required as for a first response and the director of public safety shall be authorized to inspect or cause to be inspected the alarm system at such premises, prescribe necessary corrective action, and shall give notice to the person or business having or maintaining such alarm system of the conditions and requirements of this section, including the fee requirements for four (4) or more false alarm responses by the police within each calendar year.
(4)
After a fourth response to premises within a calendar year, and for all succeeding responses within that calendar year, a twenty-five-dollar ($25.00) fee shall be charged and paid within ten (10) days' notice of same, and if such false alarms are as a result of failure to take necessary corrective action prescribed by the director of public safety, the director of public safety may order the disconnection of such alarm system and it shall be unlawful to reconnect such alarm system until such corrective action is taken, provided that no disconnection shall be ordered as to any premises required by law to have an alarm system in operation.
(d)
User fees. Any person or business which maintains an alarm system which has a direct connection to an alarm display panel located in the communications center of DeKalb County Department of Public Safety shall be charged a monthly fee for said connection, in an amount as established by DeKalb County ordinance, as amended from time to time.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-42. - False reports.
(1)
No person shall knowingly and willfully harass or attempt to harass or mislead any police officer, other law enforcer, or authorized emergency response entity or person by submitting false or misleading information or unauthorized use of any telecommunication device of whatever nature to summon police aid without reasonable cause.
(2)
On the first offense, the person or persons shall receive a warning. On the second and all subsequent offenses, the person or persons shall be subject to fine or imprisonment as provided in section 1-7 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-43. - Refusal to obey official request.
For the prupose of promoting the safety and welfare of the public it shall be unlawful for a person in a gathering to refuse to obey the reasonable official request or order of a peace officer to move by dispersing those gathered.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-44. - Giving false name, address, or birthdate to law enforcement officer.
It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to give a false name, address, or date of birth to a law enforcement officer in the lawful discharge of his duties with the intent of misleading the officer as to his identity or birthdate.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-45. - Sale of vehicles.
It shall be unlawful for a person to sell more than four (4) vehicles per year within the City of Clarkston without first obtaining the necessary city, state, and federal licenses and permits. Any person without such license or permit who sells four (4) or less vehicles within the City shall sell only vehicles titled in his name and located on his own property. Upon conviction of any such violation, such person shall be punished as provided in section 1-7 of this Code of Ordinances.
(Ord. No. 288, § 1, 12-7-04; Ord. No. 296, § 1, 9-6-05)

ARTICLE V. - OFFENSES AGAINST PUBLIC HEALTH

Sec. 12-50. - Sale of unwholesome food products.
The selling or offering for sale, for food, by any peddler or other person in the city, of unsound or unwholesome vegetables, fruits, meats, eggs, fish, rancid butter, or any other unsound or unwholesome food product, shall cause the person so offending to be punished upon conviction thereof, as provided in section 1-7. The offending person shall forfeit his license upon conviction.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-51. - Pharmaceuticals.
It shall be unlawful for any person to give free samples of proprietary or patent medicines to any person, except that a pharmaceutical sales representative may give free samples of proprietary or patent medicines to a medical professional while all parties involved are acting in the course and scope of their professional duties.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-52. - Waste and disposal thereof.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to throw or deposit any slops, garbage, or filth of any kind into any street, or other public or private place.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to throw or deposit upon the streets or other public places, or on private property without the consent of the owner, except at such places as may be properly designated by the city, any glass, broken ware, dirt, paper, rubbish, garbage, or filth or any kind.
(c)
It shall be unlawful to set on fire any leaves, rubbish, or trash of any kind anywhere within the city.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person to deposit offal or dead animals at any place within the city.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for any person to place any gutter or spout so as to convey filth from kitchens or elsewhere into any street of the city.
(f)
It shall be unlawful for any person to use the streets, sidewalks, or gutters as a means of drainage to carry off any used and dirty water or other filthy fluids.
(g)
It shall be unlawful for any person to throw or discharge from any lot or building any water, fluid, or liquid substance so as to injuriously affect the surface of any city street or sidewalk so as to make it unsafe for travel.
(h)
It shall be unlawful for any person within the city to allow waste water from washing cars or from other sources to run across the sideawalk, and such person shall provide an adequate means of disposal thereof. Operations of filling stations may not wash their premises between the hours of 7:30 a.m. and 6:00 p.m.
(i)
It shall be unlawful to sweep or push litter from sidewalks into the streets. Such litter shall be deposited in a proper receptacle which shall be covered to prevent scattering by wind and animal.
(j)
It shall be unlawful for any person to haul or transport for any distance any dirt, soil, sand, rubbish or other material, over or upon or in the streets of the city, in any wagon, cart, wheelbarrow, truck, or other vehicle from which such dirt, soil, sand, rubbish, or any like substance escapes or leaks.
For the purposes of this section, every tenant or occupant of a house or other premises, opposite or nearest to which such garbage, slops or filth may be found, shall be presumed to be the offender.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)

Sec. 12-54. - Restraint of animals while off of owner's property.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the owner, or any person having custody or control of any animal, to keep the animal under restraint at all times while the animal is off of the real property limits of such owner or person having custody or control of the animal.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person who possesses, harbors, or is in charge of any animal not to immediately remove excrement deposited by the animal on any street, right-of-way, or upon public or private property unless such property belongs to said person. It shall be the duty of any person who having custody or control of any animal, while not on their own property, to have in their possession a device, equipment, or other means for picking up and removal of animal excrement. The provisions of this section shall not apply to any animal aiding the handicapped (e.g., guide dog) or to an animal when in police or rescue activities.
(Ord. No. 280, § 1, 3-2-04)
Sec. 12-55. - Animal regulation in Armistead and Milam Parks.
For the health and safety of children and others who use city parks for recreational purposes, it shall be unlawful for any person to harbor, possess or be in charge of a restrained or unrestrained animal, with the exception of seeing eye dogs for disabled persons, within areas of Armistead Park or Milam park which are primarily for the use of children or for athletic practices and events and where such areas are designated by signs which indicate that animals are not allowed. Violations of this section are punishable pursuant to the provisions of section 3.06 of the Charter and section 1-7 of this Code of Ordinances.
(Ord. No. 321, § 1, 2-6-07)
