Sec. 14-1. - City manager as director of personnel.
The city manager shall have the authority to direct and administer the operation of all departments of City Government so long as said administration is consistent with applicable state and federal laws as well as the City Charter and duly adopted ordinances.
(Ord. No. 363, § 2, 10-4-11)
Sec. 14-2. - City clerk as assistant administrator of human resources.
Separate and distinct from the city clerk's duties under the Charter, the city clerk shall serve as assistant administrator of human resources and shall administer all human resources functions of the city government under the direction of the city manager. The city clerk shall maintain personnel records for city employees in a manner consistent with state and federal law and regulations.
(Ord. No. 363, § 2, 10-4-11)
Sec. 14-3. - Department heads.
The chief of police, finance officer, and director of public works shall be considered "department heads" for the purpose of administering city government. All department heads shall be directly supervised by the city manager and shall supervise their department pursuant to the direction of the city manager. In the absence of any department head, the city manager shall serve as the head of such department. Department heads serve at the pleasure of the city manager.
(Ord. No. 363, § 2, 10-4-11)
Sec. 14-4. - Anti-discrimination policy.
It is the policy of the city that all employees are entitled to a work environment free from all forms of discrimination including sexual harassment. No employee of the city shall be discriminated against in promotion, discipline, pay, or any other conditions of employment based on sex, race, age, religion, national origin, citizenship, political affiliation, disability, sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other characteristic that is protected by law.
(Ord. No. 363, § 2, 10-4-11)
Sec. 14-5. - Merit system.
Hiring, firing, and other terms of employment with the City of Clarkston shall be governed exclusively by merit. Employees of the city that have successfully completed a probationary period may only be subjected to a substantial adverse employment action, such as demotion or termination, for cause. However, the city manager shall also have the authority to make necessary lay-offs or reductions in work hours when a lack of work or budget constraints necessitate such action. The city manager shall promulgate rules for employee discipline and an appeal procedure that includes due process for non-probationary employees. An employee that is dissatisfied with an appeal pursuant to the procedure established by the city manager shall have certiorari to the Superior Court of DeKalb County as his exclusive remedy.
(Ord. No. 363, § 2, 10-4-11)
Sec. 14-6. - Employee handbook.
The city manager shall promulgate an employee handbook and shall distribute the same to every employee of the City of Clarkston. The employee handbook shall set forth the key policies, goals, benefits and expectations of employment with the city. The content of the employee handbook shall be determined by the city manager, so long as it is consistent with the terms of the City Charter and Code of Ordinances, as well as applicable state and federal laws and regulations.
(Ord. No. 363, § 2, 10-4-11)
