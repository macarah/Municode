Chapter 40 - OUTDOOR WATERING
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 40-1. - Authority and purpose.
The county, under its powers pursuant to state law, has adopted this chapter to protect the public health and welfare. This chapter implements the county's authority to impose water use restrictions, conditioned upon a restricted water use period declared by the county. This is pursuant to the terms of this chapter or a declared drought response level implementation declared by the state pursuant to chapter 391-3-30 of the Rules of the Georgia Department of Natural Resources, Environmental Protection Division, entitled Outdoor Water Use (Ga. Comp. Rules and Regs. 391-3-30-.01 et seq.)
(Ord. of 12-3-10, Exh. A)
Sec. 40-2. - Jurisdiction.
The Putnam County Code Enforcement Department shall be the enforcement authority for this ordinance. The county manager may also authorize other departments as may be deemed necessary to support enforcement.
(Ord. of 12-3-10, Exh. A)
Sec. 40-3. - Repealer.
All ordinances or parts of ordinances in conflict with this chapter are repealed.
(Ord. of 12-3-10, Exh. A)
Sec. 40-4. - Severability.
The sections, paragraphs, sentences, clauses and phrases of this chapter are severable. Should any section, paragraph, sentence, clause or phrase of this chapter be rendered invalid by any court of law, the remaining sections, paragraphs, sentences, clauses or phrases shall not be affected but shall continue in effect until amended or repealed by action of the governing authority of Putnam County.
(Ord. of 12-3-10, Exh. A)
Sec. 40-5. - Effective date.
This chapter shall go into effect on December 3, 2010.
(Ord. of 12-3-10, Exh. A)
Sec. 40-6. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Address mean the house number (a numeric or alphanumeric designation) that, together with the street name, describes a physical location of a specific property.
Declared drought response level (DDRL) means one of four levels of drought that may be declared by the state department of natural resources based on the severity of drought conditions, with one being the least severe and four being the most severe, as provided in Ga. Comp. Rules and Rags. 391-3-30-.01 et seq., pertaining to outdoor water use.
Even-numbered address means a house number ending with the number 0, 2, 4, 6, 8, or no house number.
Odd-numbered address means a house number ending with the number 1, 3, 5, 7, or 9.
Outdoor watering means any watering of decorative lawns, trees or shrubbery by water users.
Water users means all persons or municipalities using water from any public water source irrespective of that person's responsibility for billing purposes for use of the water.
(Ord. of 12-3-10, Exh. A)
Secs. 40-7—40-10. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - EMERGENCY WATER RESTRICTIONS

Sec. 40-11. - Restriction on outdoor water[ing] of landscape.
Outdoor watering for purposes of planting, growing, managing, or maintaining ground cover, trees, shrubs, or other plants in the unincorporated area may occur only between the hours of 4:00 p.m. and 10:00 a.m.; provided, however, that this limitation shall not create any limitation upon the following outdoor water uses:
(1)
Commercial raising, harvesting, or storing of crops; feeding, breeding, or managing livestock or poultry; the commercial production or storing of feed for use in the production of livestock, including, but not limited to, cattle, calves, swine, hogs, goats, sheep, and rabbits, or for use in the production of poultry, including, but not limited to, chickens, hens, ratites, and turkeys; producing plants, trees, fowl, or animals; or the commercial production of aquacultural, horticultural, dairy, livestock, poultry, eggs, and apiarian products or as otherwise defined in O.C.G.A. § 1-3-3;
(2)
Capture and reuse of cooling system condensate or stormwater in compliance with applicable ordinances and state guidelines;
(3)
Reuse of gray water in compliance with O.C.G.A. § 31-3-5.2 and applicable local board of health regulations;
(4)
Use of reclaimed waste water by a designated user from a system permitted by the Environmental Protection Division of the Georgia Department of Natural Resources to provide reclaimed wastewater;
(5)
Watering personal food gardens;
(6)
Watering new and replanted plant, seed, or turf in landscapes, golf courses, or sports turf fields during installation and for a period of 30 days immediately following the date of installation;
(7)
Drip irrigation or irrigation using soaker hoses;
(8)
Hand watering with a hose with automatic cutoff or handheld container;
(9)
Use of water withdrawn from private water wells or surface water by an owner or operator of property if such well or surface water is on said property;
(10)
Watering horticultural crops held for sale, resale, or installation;
(11)
Watering athletic fields, golf courses, or public turf grass recreational areas;
(12)
Installation, maintenance, or calibration of irrigation systems; or
(13)
Hydroseeding.
(Ord. of 12-3-10, Exh. A)
Sec. 40-12. - Declaration of period of restricted water use.
The board of commissioners or county manager shall declare a period of restricted water use in either of two circumstances:
(1)
State DDRL announcement. In the event of imposition by the state department of natural resources of a DDRL encompassing the geographical area of the county or any part thereof, the board of commissioners or county manager shall declare a water emergency and the level thereof. Upon the declaration, the restrictions of this chapter shall apply to all water use within the county until the board of commissioners or county manager lifts the restrictions; provided, however, that the restrictions shall remain in full force and effect until any state outdoor watering ban has been lifted.
(2)
Local declaration. In the event of prolonged drought, failure of equipment, or any other factor that substantially affects or may affect the supply of water to the county and the county's water users, the board of commissioners or county manager may declare an emergency and the level thereof. Upon the declaration, the restrictions of this chapter shall apply to all water use within the county until the restrictions are lifted. The county personnel, state agency, federal agency, or the state governor should where appropriate, condition the declaration on the identification of an emergency or water shortage.
(Ord. of 12-3-10, Exh. A)
Sec. 40-13. - Notice of declaration.
Upon declaration of a period of restricted water use, the board of commissioners or county manager shall notify all users through news releases to the local media, through notices mailed to all users, or through such other means as the board of commissioners or county manager shall deem expedient and appropriate. Notice shall be given right away after the declaration of a period of restricted water use is made.
(Ord. of 12-3-10, Exh. A)
Sec. 40-14. - Watering methods.
During a period of restricted water use of any level, outdoor watering is restricted to bucket, can or handheld hose watering with automatic shutoff nozzle. The use of automatic sprinkler systems is prohibited, except watering of newly installed landscaping as permitted herein and except testing of a newly installed or newly repaired sprinkler system by a commercial enterprise engaged in the installation or repair of lawn irrigation systems.
(Ord. of 12-3-10, Exh. A)
Sec. 40-15. - Level one restrictions.
In the event of a declaration of a level one period of restricted water use, the following restrictions shall apply within the county:
(1)
Outdoor water use may occur on scheduled days within the hours of 12:00 midnight to 10:00 a.m. and 4:00 p.m. to 12:00 midnight.
(2)
Scheduled days for odd-numbered addresses are Tuesdays, Thursdays and Sundays.
(3)
Scheduled days for even-numbered addresses are Mondays, Wednesdays and Saturdays.
(4)
Use of hydrants for any purpose other than firefighting, public health, safety or flushing is prohibited.
(Ord. of 12-3-10, Exh. A)
Sec. 40-16. - Level two restrictions.
In the event of a declaration of a level two period of restricted water use, the following restrictions shall apply within the county:
(1)
Outdoor water use may occur on scheduled days within the hours of 12:00 midnight to 10:00 a.m.
(2)
Scheduled days for odd-numbered addresses are Tuesdays, Thursdays and Sundays.
(3)
Scheduled days for even-numbered addresses and golf course fairways are Mondays, Wednesdays and Saturdays.
(4)
The following uses are prohibited:
a.
Using hydrants for any purpose other than firefighting, public health, safety or flushing.
b.
Washing hard surfaces, such as streets, gutters, sidewalks and driveways except when necessary for public health and safety.
(Ord. of 12-3-10, Exh. A)
Sec. 40-17. - Level three restrictions.
In the event of a declaration of a level three period of restricted water use, the following restrictions shall apply within the county:
(1)
Outdoor water use may occur on the scheduled day within the hours of 12:00 midnight to 10:00 a.m.
(2)
The scheduled day for odd-numbered addresses is Sunday.
(3)
The scheduled day for even-numbered addresses and golf course fairways is Saturday.
(4)
The following uses are prohibited:
a.
Using hydrants for any purpose other than firefighting, public health, safety or flushing.
b.
Washing hard surfaces, such as streets, gutters, sidewalks, driveways, except when necessary for public health and safety.
c.
Filling installed swimming pools except when necessary for health care or structural integrity.
d.
Washing vehicles, such as cars, boats, trailers, motorbikes, airplanes, golf carts.
e.
Washing buildings or structures except for immediate fire protection.
f.
Noncommercial fundraisers, such as carwashes.
g.
Using water for ornamental purposes, such as fountains, reflecting pools, and waterfalls except when necessary to support aquatic life.
(Ord. of 12-3-10, Exh. A)
Sec. 40-18. - Level four restrictions.
In the event of a declaration of a level four period of restricted water use, no outdoor water use is permitted, unless the use is exempted under the provisions of this chapter or specifically by the board of commissioners or county manager.
(Ord. of 12-3-10, Exh. A)
Sec. 40-19. - Enforcement.
Persons violating water use restrictions as provided herein shall be given one warning in writing prior to the issuance of a citation, unless:
(1)
The period of restricted water use is declared in response to a DDRL imposed by the state and applicable in the geographical limits of the county; or
(2)
The declaration of a period of restricted water use specifically provides that no warnings will be given.
(Ord. of 12-3-10, Exh. A)
Sec. 40-20. - Penalties.
Any person violating the provisions of this chapter shall be liable to the county in the amount of up to $100.00 for the first violation and up to $1,000.00 for the second violation. Third and subsequent violations shall be subject to a fine of up to $1,000.00 and imprisonment for a period of up to 90 days. In addition, third and subsequent violations may, at the discretion of the county, result in immediate shut off of water service for such period as is deemed expedient and necessary under the circumstances, with approval of the board of commissioners.
(Ord. of 12-3-10, Exh. A)
