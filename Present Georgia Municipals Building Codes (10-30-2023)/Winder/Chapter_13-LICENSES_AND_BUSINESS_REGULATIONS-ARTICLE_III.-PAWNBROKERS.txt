Sec. 13-83. - Definition.
For the purpose of this article, a "pawnbroker" is any person with whom property is deposited as security for the payment of a debt.
(Ord. of 2-24-78, § 15-61)
State Law reference— "Pawnbroker" defined, Ga. Code Ann., § 44-12-130.

Sec. 13-85. - Shop operated with other business.
When a pawn shop is operated in conjunction with another business, the hours as set forth in section 13-91 shall not apply. When operating a pawnbroker business in conjunction with another business, the opening and closing hours shall be the same as with the other business, provided however that such closing hour shall in any event be no later than 10:00 p.m. No pawnbroker may transact any pawnbroker business on Sunday, but shall be allowed to transact any other business not prohibited by other ordinances or state law.
(Ord. of 2-24-78, §§ 15-63, 15-74; Ord. (6-W-84) of 12-12-84)
State Law reference— Sunday operation of businesses, Ga. Code Ann., § 10-1-550 et seq.
Sec. 13-86. - Weekly report; false statements.
All pawnbrokers shall make a correct and truthful weekly report in writing and submitted electronically in a format approved by the Winder Police Department on Monday of each week to the chief of police, wherein he or they shall supply the information required by section 13-93, pertaining to all property sold or pledged to them during the previous week. Such report shall be signed by the pawnbroker making such report. Any pawnbroker making any willfully false or misleading statements in such report shall be punished as provided in section 13-88.
(Ord. of 2-24-78, § 15-73; Ord. (W-25-07) of 3-6-07)
Sec. 13-87. - Transactions with minors.
No pawnbroker shall receive any article in pawn or purchase any article for resale from any person under eighteen (18) years of age.
(Ord. of 2-24-78, § 15-71)
State Law reference— Contracts of minors, Ga. Code Ann., § 13-3-20.
Sec. 13-88. - Penalties.
Any pawnbroker or person running a pawnshop or employee thereof violating the provisions of this article shall be punished as provided by section 1-8, and in addition the recorder or the mayor and council may also, on proper proof, revoke the license of any pawnbroker violating the provisions of any law or ordinance pertaining to pawnbrokers.
(Ord. of 2-24-78, § 15-76)
State Law reference— Penalties relating to records of pawnbrokers, Ga. Code Ann., § 44-12-137.
Sec. 13-89. - Premises open to police.
It shall be the duty of every pawnbroker to admit to his premises the chief of police or any other police officer who may desire at any time to search for article missing or stolen, or to make any inspection authorized by this article, without the formality of a search warrant.
(Ord. of 2-24-78, § 15-65)
Sec. 13-90. - Inspection of pledges and purchases.
All articles pledged or sold to pawnbrokers shall, at all times, be subject to inspection and examination by the police.
(Ord. of 2-24-78, § 15-66)
Sec. 13-91. - Business hours.
No pawnbroker shall open his place of business or conduct any business therein between the hours of 6:00 p.m. and 8:00 a.m.; provided, however, that such a place of business may remain open until 6:30 p.m. on Fridays and until 8:00 p.m. on Saturdays.
(Ord. of 2-24-78, § 15-62)
Sec. 13-92. - Display of pledges.
Every pawnbroker shall have his pledges openly displayed so that the inspection authorized by this article may be easily made.
(Ord. of 2-24-78, § 15-64)
Sec. 13-93. - Records—Generally.
Every pawnbroker shall maintain a book, in permanent form, in which shall be entered in legible English at the time of each loan, purchase or sale:
(1)
The date of transaction;
(2)
The name of the person conducting the transaction;
(3)
The name, age and address of the customer; a description of the general appearance of the customer; and the distinctive number from the customer's driver's license or other similar identification card; the identification used must contain a photo of customer;
(4)
An identification and description of the pledged or purchased goods, including, if reasonable available, the serial, model or other number, and all identifying marks inscribed thereon;
(5)
The number of the receipt or pawn ticket;
(6)
The price paid or the amount loaned;
(7)
The number of the check issued for the purchase price or loan if payment is made by check;
(8)
The maturity date of the transaction; and
(9)
The signature of the customer.
(10)
It will be the duty of the owner or employees to have the customer hold the number of their receipt in front of their chest and a color photo made. The photo supplies/equipment will be purchased by the licensee and preserved and furnished to the police for development/processing at the city's expense of the city sees the necessity.
(Ord. of 2-24-78, § 15-67; Ord. (W-4-93) of 3-2-93; Ord. (W-26-07) of 3-6-07)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, Ga. Code Ann., § 44-12-132.
Sec. 13-94. - Same—Receipt.
A receipt providing all of the information specified in the preceding section shall be signed, a thumb print of customer affixed, and a copy given to the person pledging or selling property.
(Ord. of 2-24-78, § 15-67; Ord. (W-4-93) of 3-2-93)
Sec. 13-95. - Same—Inspection.
The records required to be kept by section 13-93 shall be subject to inspection and examination by the police during business hours and when the proprietor or any of his agents or employees are present.
(Ord. of 2-24-78, § 15-69)
Cross reference— Police, ch. 20.
State Law reference— Similar provisions, Ga. Code Ann., § 44-12-133.
Sec. 13-96. - Same—When entries made.
The entries required by the preceding section shall be made as soon after the transaction as is possible and, in no event, more than one (1) hour thereafter.
(Ord. of 2-24-78, § 15-68)
Cross reference— Entries made at time of loan, purchase or sale, Ga. Code Ann., § 44-12-132.
Sec. 13-97. - Same—False entries.
It shall be unlawful for any person engaged in the business of a pawnbroker or his clerk, agent or employee to make any false entry in any books kept as required by section 13-93.
(Ord. of 2-24-78, § 15-70)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, Ga. Code Ann., § 44-12-137.
Sec. 13-98. - Goods to be held thirty days.
Any pawnbroker or person operating under a pawnbroker's license who takes goods on pawn or buys goods, taking full title thereto, the "goods" being used in the broadest sense and including all kinds of personal property, shall hold such goods so taken in pawn or purchased for at least thirty (30) days before disposing of the same by sale, transfer, shipment or otherwise. This section cannot be waived by contract, but it does not apply and is not intended to cover purchases from reputable wholesale supply houses and manufacturers. A thirty-day hold may be placed by police on any suspected items pawned or purchased.
(Ord. of 2-24-78, § 15-72; Ord. (W-4-93) of 3-2-93)
State Law reference— Secured transactions, Ga. Code Ann., title 11, ch. 9; authority of city to define the powers and privileges of pawnbrokers, Ga. Code Ann., § 44-12-136.
Sec. 13-99. - Outside storage of goods and motor vehicle resale.
All pawn broker or person operating under a pawn broker's license shall not store goods offered for sale outside the building where the business is located. Any pawn broker or person operating under a pawn broker's license who takes motor vehicles on pawn or buys motor vehicles for resale shall also obtain a license for the operation of a motor vehicle dealership from the state and the city and shall comply with all other laws and regulations regulating the sale of motor vehicles.
(Ord. (W-03-03) of 10-8-03; Ord. (W-27-07) of 3-6-07)
