ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 3-1. - Definitions.
The following definitions will apply to the words and terms used in this chapter:
Alcohol: Ethyl alcohol, hydrated oxide of ethyl or spirits of wine, from whatever source or by whatever process produced.
Alcoholic beverage: All alcohol, distilled spirits, malt beverage, wine or fortified wine as defined in this chapter.
Alcoholic beverage caterer: Any alcoholic beverage licensee for consumption on premises that has also been licensed from its local county or municipality to sell alcoholic beverages by the drink off-premises and in connection with an authorized catered function. The alcoholic beverage caterers license shall be for only the type of alcoholic beverage for which the licensee is licensed to sell or serve on premises.
Alcoholic beverage license: Any license authorized by this chapter including, but not limited to, beer or malt beverages for consumption on premises, beer or malt beverages by the package, brew pubs, wine for consumption on premises, wine by the package, distilled spirits for consumption on premises in restaurants, bars, pubs or taverns, and distilled spirits by the package.
Bar, pub or tavern: Any establishment selling alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises not otherwise qualified as a restaurant or brew pub.
Brew pub: Any restaurant in which beer or malt beverages are manufactured or brewed, subject to the barrel production limitation prescribed in O.C.G.A. section 3-5-36 for retail consumption on the premises solely in draft form.
Dance hall: A "dance hall" for the purposes of this chapter shall include any business where one (1) of its purposes is conducting, facilitating or promoting public dancing on the premises.
Distilled spirits: Any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than twenty-one (21) percent alcohol by volume including, but not limited to, all fortified wines.
Downtown Overlay District: The area of the City of Winder subject to the Downtown Overlay District provisions of the Comprehensive Zoning Ordinance of the City of Winder, Georgia.
Fortified wine: Any alcoholic beverage containing more than twenty-one (21) percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. "Fortified wine" includes, but is not limited to, brandy.
Gallon or wine gallon: A United States gallon or liquid measure equivalent to the volume of two hundred thirty-one (231) cubic inches or the nearest equivalent metric measurement.
Growler: A glass or ceramic container specifically designed to transport draft malt beverages, not exceeding one (1) gallon capacity and not less than twelve (12) ounces, filled and made available for retail sale only if properly cleaned and securely sealed with a tamperproof cap or seal.
Live entertainment: For the purposes of this chapter live entertainment shall include, but is not limited to, any musical performance, theatrical performance, literary reading, and disc jockey performance, where the audience is present at the actual time of occurrence.
Malt beverage: Any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than six (6) percent alcohol by volume, and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer and strong beer. The term "malt beverage" does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Manufacturer: Any maker, producer or bottler of an alcoholic beverage.
Minor: Any person under twenty-one (21) years of age.
Package: A bottle, can, keg, barrel or other original consumer container.
Person: Any individual, firm, partnership, cooperative, nonprofit membership corporation, joint venture, association, company, corporation, agency, syndicate, estate, trust, business trust, receiver, fiduciary or other group or combination acting as a unit, body politic or political subdivision, whether public, private or quasi-public.
Premises: The space or area owned, leased and/or controlled by the licensee and used by him for the purpose of operating under the license; except that in the case of hotels and motels, "premises" shall include only that portion of the property where alcoholic beverages are sold or served.
Private club: An organization existing under the laws of the State of Georgia having at least forty (40) members regularly paying monthly, quarterly, semi-annual or annual dues, organized and operated exclusively for pleasure, recreation and other nonprofitable purposes, no part of net earnings of which inure to the benefit of any shareholder or member, and owning, hiring or leasing a building or space therein for the reasonable use of its members; provided, that no member or officer, agent or employee of the club is paid, or directly or indirectly receives, in the form of salary or other compensation, any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages to the club or its members or guests beyond the amount of such salary as may be fixed by its members at any annual meeting, or by its governing board out of the general revenue of the club.
Public entertainment: For the purposes of this chapter, public entertainment shall include live entertainment and public dancing.
Public entertainment facility: A business which is open to the public and provides public entertainment or live entertainment, including, but not limited to, dance halls, is a public entertainment facility for the purposes of this chapter.
Restaurant: The word "restaurant" shall mean any public place kept, used, maintained, advertised, and held out to the public as a place where meals are served and where meals are actually and regularly prepared and served, such place being provided with adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining room equipment and having employed therein a sufficient number and kind of employees to prepare, cook and serve suitable food for its guests. The serving of such meals and food shall be the principal business conducted, shall be available to the customers during all hours of operation, and the serving of alcoholic beverages to be consumed on the premises as only incidental thereto. A restaurant shall derive at least fifty (50) percent of its monthly gross food and beverage sales from the sale of prepared meals and foods.
Retail dealer: Any person who sells alcoholic beverages, either in unbroken packages or for consumption on the premises, at retail only to consumers and not for resale.
Special events facility: A building or structure which is used for special events such as weddings, anniversaries, reunions, fundraisers, meetings, conferences, concerts or other events which are held during specific hours on a specific day and which may be limited by the sponsor of the event as to invitation or participation.
Special events facility owner: The person or entity who owns a special event facility located in the City of Winder, Georgia. The owner need not be a resident of the City of Winder but must be either a person over the age of twenty-one (21) or an entity wholly owned by person or persons over the age of twenty-one (21). The special events facility owner is referred herein as the "owner".
Special events facility license: A license, granted annually, granting a special events facility owner the right to sell or offer for sale ale, beer, malt beverages and wine for consumption only on the premises of a special events facility.
Wholesaler or wholesale dealer: Any person who sells alcoholic beverages to other wholesale dealers or to retail dealers.
Wine: Any alcoholic beverage containing not more than twenty-one (21) percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. "Wine" includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines and like products. The term "wine" does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this section.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)

Sec. 3-3. - Application—Contents generally.
Applications for licenses to sell alcoholic beverages of any kind shall be made in person by the applicant to the city license and permit office in writing on forms furnished by said clerk, signed by the applicant if an individual, or by all partners if a partnership, or by a duly authorized agent if a corporation, verified by oath, and shall contain the following statements and information:
(1)
The name, age, social security number and address of the applicant, in case of an individual; in case of a partnership, all partners or persons entitled to share in the profits thereof, their ages, social security number and addresses; in case of a corporation, the names, ages, social security number and addresses of all the officers.
(2)
The location and description of the premises or place of business which is to be operated under such license.
(3)
A statement of whether the applicant, or any member of the partnership, or any officer of the corporation, has made application at any previous time for any alcoholic beverage license in the city, and the disposition of such application.
(4)
Whether any of the persons signing the application, or, if a corporation, any of the officers, have ever been convicted of a crime, other than for violation of traffic laws.
(5)
Whether a previous license issued to applicants or any of them by any state or subdivision thereof or by the federal government has been revoked, and the reasons therefor.
(6)
The length of residence of the applicant, and if such residence is less than one (1) year prior to the date of application, the last prior place of residence of the applicant.
(7)
A drawing to scale, showing the nearest church, school or college or by the affidavit of a registered surveyor that the proposed location of the business complies with the minimum distance requirements.
(8)
Each applicant for an alcoholic beverage license shall attach to his application evidence of ownership of the building or proposed building or a copy of the lease if the applicant is leasing the building.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-4. - Eligibility of applicant generally.
No alcoholic beverage license shall be issued to:
(1)
Any person unless he is the manager or the person actually in charge of the daily operation of the business. Such person must have been a resident of the State of Georgia for at least one (1) year prior to the submission of the application for license and must be a citizen of the United States or a duly-registered foreign national. The applicant must continue to live in the State of Georgia as long as he/she holds an alcoholic beverage license. The applicant must be at least twenty-one (21) years of age.
(2)
A person who, by reason of reputation, character or mental or physical capacity, is not, in the judgment of the city council, a fit or qualified person to conduct such a business.
(3)
A person who has been convicted of a felony or a crime involving mortal turpitude or convicted or pled nolo contendere of a violation of any state, local or federal laws pertaining to the manufacture, possession, transportation, or sale of alcoholic beverages, or the taxability thereof, all within the last five (5) years.
(4)
A partnership, if any member of the partnership is disqualified under this section.
(5)
A corporation, if any officer, manager or director thereof is disqualified under this section.
(6)
A person whose place of business is conducted by a manager or agent, unless said manager or agent possesses the same qualifications required of the alcoholic beverage license holder.
(7)
A person who is the previous holder of an alcoholic beverage license and is, in the judgment and discretion of the city council, an unfit person to have a license because of the manner in which he conducted business thereunder, as to the necessity for unusual police observation and inspection in order to prevent the violation of any law or regulation relating to such business.
(8)
Any person as close kin as brothers or sisters, by blood or marriage, to one already holding the maximum two (2) licenses permitted under this division.
(9)
A person who is a full-time paid employee of the city or any elected or appointed official of the city or his or her spouse.
(10)
A location at which a previous alcoholic beverage license has been revoked or suspended, and where, in the judgment of the city council, the problems which have arisen from the operation of an alcoholic beverage license at such location indicate that it is not in the interest of public health, safety, welfare or morals that the sale of alcoholic beverages be permitted at such location.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-5. - Issuance of licenses.
Issuance of licenses under this chapter are limited as follows:
(1)
No person shall hold a license in any retail category hereunder and a license under any wholesale category hereunder at the same time.
(2)
Licenses for the sale of distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises shall be issued only to restaurants, provided, however, that such licenses may be issued to bars, pubs, taverns, public entertainment facilities or brew pubs located in the downtown overlay district.
(3)
Notwithstanding anything in this article to the contrary, the sale of alcoholic beverages in the city is a privilege, and not a right, and the issuance of a license hereunder shall not create any property rights in the license holder.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-6. - Same—Payment of application fees.
All applications for alcoholic beverage licenses shall be accompanied either by lawful money of the United States, certified check or cashier's check payable to the city for the proper amount of the investigation fee of one hundred dollars ($100.00) payable in advance which shall not be refundable whether the license applied for is granted or denied. No investigation fee is charged for event permits, alcoholic beverage caterer, and special events facility licenses.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-7. - Application—Investigation/fingerprints.
Upon receipt by the city license and permit office of any application for an alcoholic beverage license, or for the transfer of any such license, the office shall forward the same to the chief of police for investigation, who, following such investigation, shall recommend either approval or disapproval of the application. The city license and permit office shall then submit the application, with the approval or disapproval of the chief of police indicated thereon, to the city administrator for review and either approval or denial. Every partner of a partnership applicant, every corporate officer, owner and manager of a place of business selling alcoholic beverages shall be fingerprinted by the GAPS system or any future replacement system before a license is issued under this chapter. The city administrator shall either approve or deny the application within thirty (30) days from receiving the application from the license and permit office. If the application is denied, the administrator shall give notice of such denial to the applicant with the reasons for denial stated in the notice. The applicant shall have the right to appeal the administrator's denial of the application to the city council by filing a written appeal with the administrator within thirty (30) days of the administrator's denial notice. The appeal shall be presented to the city council in accordance with the procedures set forth in section 3-10.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12; Ord. (W-2-21) of 11-18-21, § I)
Sec. 3-8. - Prorated payment of license fees.
The license fees provided for in this chapter for the manufacture or sale or offer for sale at wholesale or retail of alcoholic beverages are due and payable upon delivery of the license to the licensee. Any person who is doing business as of the first day of January or who begins to do business at any time during the month of January shall pay the full annual license fee. Any person who begins to do business in any month after January shall pay a prorated fee based on the number of months during which the licensee does business and any portion of a month shall count as a whole month. The prorated fee shall be rounded up to the nearest dollar amount.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-9. - False statements.
The making of any untrue or misleading statement in an application for an alcoholic beverage license shall be sufficient cause for the refusal, suspension, revocation or cancellation of such license, as the city council deems proper.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-10. - Denial or revocation.
Before the city council shall uphold or reverse the city administrator's denial of an application for a license under this article or shall revoke any existing license, the applicant or license holder, as the case may be, shall be given notice in writing from the city license and permit office to show cause before the city council at a time and place specified therein not less than two (2) days nor more than fourteen (14) days from the date of service of the notice, why said application for license should not be denied, or why said license should not be revoked, as the case may be, stating the grounds therefor, and at the appointed time and place the applicant or license holder shall have an opportunity to show cause, if any exists, why said application should not be denied or said license revoked, after which the city council shall take such action as it, in its judgment and discretion, shall deem warranted under the facts. No application for an alcoholic beverage license shall be acted upon by the city council except at a regular meeting of said council. A revocation hearing herein provided for need not be at a regular meeting of the city council, but may be at such time and place as shall be fixed in said notice.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12; Ord. (W-2-21) of 11-18-21, § II)
Sec. 3-11. - Suspension, revocation, grounds.
The following shall be grounds for the suspension and/or revocation of an alcoholic beverage license. Whether or not said grounds exist shall be determined by the city council in its sole discretion:
(1)
The violation by the license holder or his agents or employees of any provision of this chapter or of any state or federal law or regulation, except misdemeanors, or any ordinance of the city, other than traffic violations. The determination of whether any such violation has occurred shall be made by the city council, and an actual conviction in a court for such offense shall not be necessary in order to suspend or revoke the license.
(2)
The failure of the license holder or his employee to report promptly to the police department any violation of law or municipal ordinance, breach of the peace, disturbance or altercation resulting in violence occurring inside the premises.
(3)
Any conduct on the part of the license holder or his employee contrary to the public welfare, safety, health or morals.
(4)
Operating or conducting the business in a manner contrary to the public welfare, safety, health or morals, or in such manner as to constitute a nuisance.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-12. - Suspension pending hearing.
The city council shall have the right, upon the recommendation of the chief of police, to suspend any alcoholic beverage license pending the hearing provided for in section 3-10 of this division, where, in the judgment of the city council, such action is necessary to protect the public health, safety, welfare and morals.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-13. - Change of location.
The location named in the licensee's application shall not be changed without permission and approval of the city council upon written application made. Approvals as to change of location shall be at the discretion of the city council.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-14. - Sale of interest by licensee.
Where an alcoholic beverage license holder is a partnership or a corporation, or one (1) or more partners as the case may be, may withdraw from the partnership or the corporation in favor of one (1) or more of the partners who were partners at the time of the issuance of the license. The sale by a partner of his interest in the partnership to a person who is not at the time of the sale already a partner may constitute a ground for revocation of the license unless the new partner is a person qualified to hold a license, and unless the transaction is approved by the city council.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-15. - Transfer.
All alcoholic beverage licenses shall be personal to the licensee to whom issued. Upon proper application and payment of the nonrefundable investigation fee of one hundred dollars ($100.00), the city council may permit the license to be transferred upon payment of a transfer fee equal to an amount prorated by the remaining term of the original license, but not to exceed one-half (½) of the original license fee, provided that the transferee meets all of the qualifications which would have been required had the transferee been an applicant for an alcoholic beverage license. Upon the death of any license holder, his personal representatives or his heirs may continue to operate under said license for the balance of the year without the payment of any fee, if otherwise qualified as license holders, or the license may be transferred to a qualified transferee within thirty (30) days after the condition of the estate of the deceased license holder will permit, upon payment of the above transfer fee.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-16. - Term; refund of fee.
No license under this chapter shall be issued for less than a calendar year, or the remainder thereof, and in case of the revocation or surrender of such license before the expiration of such calendar year, the holder thereof shall not be entitled to receive any refund.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-17. - Expiration.
All alcoholic beverage licenses shall expire at midnight on December 31 of the year in which issued except event permits which shall expire at the conclusion of the event.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-18. - Renewal.
(a)
All licenses granted under this chapter shall expire on December 31 of each year. Licensees who desire to renew their licenses for the ensuing year shall file an application and submit the requisite fees to the city administrator or his/her designee on the forms provided for the renewal of alcoholic beverage licenses. Applications for renewal must be filed on or before November 15 of each year, otherwise, no renewal license shall be granted, but an application for renewal filed after November 15 shall be treated as an initial application and the applicant shall be required to comply with all rules and regulations for the granting of licenses as if no previous license had been held. Application for renewal licenses must contain complete information and the failure to furnish complete information within thirty (30) days after being requested to do so shall result in automatic denial of the renewal. The city manager shall review and either approve or disapprove of all renewal license applications that have been properly filed on or before December 31.
(b)
Any licensee under this chapter that fails to renew its license annually in accordance with this section shall have its ability to sell any alcoholic beverages terminated immediately upon having been given written notice of same by the city. Upon such termination the former licensee shall be required to make a new license application in accordance with the provision of this chapter as if no previous license had been held.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12; Ord. (W-2-21) of 11-18-21, § III)
Sec. 3-19. - General regulations pertaining to all licenses.
(a)
Employment of underaged persons. It shall be unlawful for any person to allow or require a person under his employment and under the age of eighteen (18) to dispense, sell, serve or take orders for any alcoholic beverages. This section shall not prohibit persons under eighteen (18) years of age who are employed in supermarkets, convenience stores, or drug stores from selling or handling alcoholic beverages which are sold for consumption off the premises.
(b)
License not transferrable to another location. Each license is issued for a specific location only and may not be transferred to another location without making a new application.
(c)
Compliance with regulations. Each license shall be subject to and comply with all ordinances and laws of the city, State of Georgia, and the United States of America.
(d)
Conditional use permit.
(1)
No licenses issued under this chapter shall be issued except for locations in the following zones as defined by the comprehensive zoning ordinance of the city: B-1, B-2 and I. Any license granted under this chapter shall also be considered as a conditional use permit subject to the provisions of this subsection.
(2)
In deciding upon any application for a conditional use permit for a license under this chapter the city council may, on their own motion or upon the suggestion of the applicant, or upon consideration of the recommendations of the planning board or the zoning administrator, grant the application subject to any of the following conditions: (i) such conditions deemed necessary to promote and protect the health, safety and general welfare. (ii) such conditions deemed necessary to protect neighboring properties and to lessen any potentially adverse effects of the use. (iii) such conditions set forth on an approved site plan such as, but not limited to, the following: setback requirements from any lot line; specified or prohibited locations for buildings, parking, loading or storage areas; maximum building dimensions and height; landscaping or planted area which may include the location, type and maintenance and plant material within a designated buffer area; fences, walls, earth berms, or other landscape buffer provisions or protective measures; preservation of existing trees or vegetation; conditions to eliminate or reduce undesirable views, light, glare, dust or odor; hours of operation; architectural details to be compatible with existing buildings in the area; adherence to specific site plans as adopted; and other requirements that the city council may deem appropriate and necessary for the protection of public health and welfare.
(3)
No conditional use permit for a license under this chapter shall be granted: (i) for a location not suitable in the judgment and discretion of the city council because of traffic congestion, general character of the neighborhood or by reason of the effect which such use would have on the adjacent and surrounding properties or neighborhood. (ii) for a location within an area where, in the judgment of the city council, the number of alcoholic beverage licenses already granted makes it contrary to the public interest and welfare. (iii) for a location at which the operation of the proposed use would be in violation of the zoning ordinance of the city.
(e)
Interest of public employees. No license shall be granted under this chapter to any city, state or federal employee whose duties include the regulation or policing of alcoholic beverages or licenses, or any tax-collecting activity.
(f)
Inspection of books and accounts; reports. Upon demand by the director of finance, any person holding a license under this chapter from the city shall open to such official his place or places of business for the purpose of enabling such official to ascertain and gain such information as may be necessary for the determination of the proper classification of such license holder for license fee purposes and determination of the correct amount of license fee to which such person is subject. Upon demand by the director of finance, any person holding a license from the city shall furnish the city, during normal business hours, at such person's place of business, all books of account, invoices, papers, reports and memoranda containing entries showing amount of purchases, sale receipts, inventory or other information, from which the correct amount of the fee to which he is subject may be determined, including exhibition of bank deposit books, bank statements and copies of sales reports, and any other such document as the city may deem necessary. The city license and permit office shall send to each holder of a license to sell distilled spirits by the drink for consumption on the premises a monthly report form containing such information as the director of finance may deem necessary to determine the licensee's compliance with this chapter, and said report form shall be completed and returned to the city by the twentieth day of the following month. Any person holding a license from the city shall secure, preserve, maintain and keep for a period of three (3) years the records and documents enumerated and referred to in this section.
(g)
False representation of age. It shall be unlawful for any person to falsely misrepresent his age in any manner whatsoever for the purposes of purchasing or otherwise acquiring any alcoholic beverage.
(h)
Prohibited acts. It shall be unlawful for any licensee to:
(1)
Knowingly allow gambling of any kind or character to be carried on in his place of business.
(2)
Knowingly allow any person to frequent his place of business for the purpose of soliciting for prostitution.
(3)
Knowingly allow any criminal act to be perpetuated on the premises, such as the sale of drugs, sale of non-tax paid alcoholic beverages, sale of stolen property, or other offenses.
(4)
Fail to report to the police department at the earliest possible time known incidents of a criminal nature that happen in his premises.
(5)
Willfully withhold from any law enforcement agency any information pertaining to any crime that may have happened on his premises and to which he was a witness.
(6)
No person holding an alcoholic beverage license shall sell or give away, or permit the sale or gift to, or the procuring for, any alcoholic beverage to any person who is physically or mentally incapacitated due to the consumption of any alcoholic beverage.
(i)
Adding to, refilling bottles, misrepresentation as to quantity, etc. It shall be unlawful for a licensee hereunder or his agents to add to the contents of a bottle or to refill an empty bottle or in any manner to misrepresent the quantity, quality or brand name of any beverage licensed hereunder. Provided however that "growlers" shall be allowed to be filled and refilled by any licensee authorized to sell malt beverages by the package. The filling of growlers by means of a tapped keg shall not constitute the breaking of a package as contemplated by O.C.G.A. section 3-3-26. Growlers may only be filled from kegs procured by the licensee from a duly licensed wholesaler. Growlers may be filled and made available for retail sale only if properly cleaned and securely sealed with a tamperproof cap or seal.
(j)
Alcohol promotions. No licensee or employee or agent of a licensee shall engage in any of the following practices in connection with the sale or other disposition of alcoholic beverages:
(1)
The giving away of any ticket, token, or any other item that can be exchanged for any alcoholic beverages with the sale of any other alcoholic beverage.
(2)
The sale of two (2) or more alcoholic beverages for a single price, or the sale of one (1) alcoholic beverage with a ticket, token, or any other item redeemable for a subsequent alcoholic beverage. Also prohibited hereunder is the sale of all such beverages a customer can or desires to drink at a single price.
(k)
Suspension of sales during civil emergency. The mayor, or the chief of police in the absence of the mayor, may, upon determining that there is an immediate danger of civil disorder or natural disaster or any other immediate threat to the public peace and order, by executive order suspend the sales of all beverages licensed under this chapter until such danger or threat has passed and for a reasonable period of time thereafter.
(l)
Reserved.
(m)
On-premises entertainment.
(1)
No holder of a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages issued hereunder shall knowingly allow any performance or entertainment or act on the licensed premises which, when applied to contemporary standards, the dominant theme taken as a whole appeals to the prurient interests or is licentious or obscene. This provision shall be construed so as to be consistent with the Winder Adult Entertainment Ordinance [chapter 3.5, article II, of this Code].
(2)
It shall be unlawful to permit on the premises so licensed any contest or form of entertainment which consists of the wetting or soaking of the upper torso of a female or the pelvic areas of a male or female. This provision shall be construed so as to be consistent with the Winder Adult Entertainment Ordinance [chapter 3.5, article II, of this Code].
(3)
It shall be unlawful to permit on the premises so licensed any contest or form of entertainment which consists of fighting, boxing, wrestling or other violent physical contact sport or event.
(4)
Upon the violation hereof by any such licensee, its license may, after a hearing before the judge of municipal court, be revoked, suspended, or put on probation under conditions.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12; Ord. ( W-20-17 ) of 11-14-17; Ord. (W-2-21) of 11-18-21, § IV)
Sec. 3-20. - Building.
No alcoholic beverage license shall be issued to any person unless the building in which the business will be located is or unless proposed plans and specifications and a building permit of a proposed building to be built are attached to the application. The completed building or the proposed building shall comply with this Code of Ordinances, regulations of the state revenue commissioner and the laws of the state. The proposed building shall also be subject to final inspection and approval, when completed, by the building inspector. Each building in which the business will be located shall contain sufficient lighting so that the building itself and the premises on all sides of the building are readily visible at all times. Premises licensed for sale of alcoholic beverages by the package shall be lighted so as to reveal the inside retail area of the building and so as to reveal all of the outside premises of said building.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-21. - Proximity to school, churches or other sites.
No alcoholic beverages shall be sold or offered for sale in violation of O.C.G.A. section 3-3-21 provided, however, that the provisions of that section shall not apply as to consumption of alcohol by the drink on premises within the city limits as established by the city council from time to time so that within the city limits there shall be no distance requirements as to the consumption of alcohol by the drink on premises as set forth in that section.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12; Ord. ( W-10-17 ) of 5-2-17)
Sec. 3-22. - Signs.
The city shall provide for all retail licensees under this article a decalcomania sign to post on the premises of their places of business. Licensees shall not post more than one (1) authorized sign at their places of business and shall not display outside signs or signs in their windows of any other type which advertise either brand, product or price.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-23. - Drive-in window.
No license holder under this article shall sell any alcoholic beverages through or by means of a drive-in window.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-24. - Deliveries.
It shall be unlawful for any licensee under this article to make deliveries of any alcoholic beverage by the package or drink beyond the boundaries of the premises covered by the license.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-25. - Time of operation.
(a)
No alcoholic beverages shall be sold by the bottle, package or by the drink in the city on Christmas Day or Sunday except as provided in this section. On days when business is permitted, the sale of alcoholic beverages shall cease at 1:55 a.m. and shall not resume before 6:00 a.m. except on Saturdays, when the sale of alcoholic beverages shall cease at 1:55 a.m. Sunday morning and shall not resume before 6:00 a.m. on the following Monday morning. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the package sale of malt beverages, wine and/or distilled spirits shall be permitted on Sundays between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m.
(b)
Notwithstanding subsection (a), the sale of alcoholic beverages shall be lawful on Sundays from 11:00 a.m. until 1:55 a.m. Monday morning in any licensed restaurant.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12; Ord. ( W-2-20 ), § I, of 4-7-20)
Sec. 3-26. - Unlawful possession of open containers.
It shall be unlawful for any person to carry about on any street or public place in the city, either upon his person or in any vehicle operated or owned by him, or occupied by him, any open containers of alcoholic beverages or to possess such beverages in an open container on the premises but outside any establishment holding a permit for the sale thereof.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-27. - Allowing on-premises alcoholic beverages purchased elsewhere.
No alcoholic beverage licensee for the sale of alcoholic beverages or any other business shall authorize or permit any patron or customer to bring on said property alcoholic beverages purchased elsewhere.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-28. - Misrepresenting age.
It shall be unlawful for any minor to misrepresent his or her age in any manner whatsoever, in order to acquire alcoholic beverages.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-29. - Sales to minors.
Any alcoholic beverage license holder under this chapter who shall sell alcoholic beverages to a minor shall be fined a maximum of one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) and a minimum of thirty-day suspension of the license for the first offense. For the second offense, the holder shall be fined one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) and a revocation of the license. For the purposes of this section, sales by either the alcoholic beverage license holder or any employee or agent of such holder shall be deemed a violation by the holder of the license.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-30. - Acquiring for minors.
No person shall knowingly and intentionally act as agent to purchase or acquire any alcoholic beverages for or on behalf of a minor, or otherwise procure for or furnish to a minor any alcoholic beverages, except for medical purposes upon the prescription of a duly licensed physician under the laws of the state.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-31. - Conduct on premises.
It shall be unlawful for any person to permit on any premises licensed under this chapter any acts which are contrary to law or subversive of the public morals. If the premises are commonly resorted to for the commission of such acts of the proprietor's knowledge and should in reason know what is occurring and does nothing to prevent it, he shall be guilty of a violation of this section. The person in charge of such premises at the time that such acts are being committed shall immediately report such conduct to the police department. Minors are not allowed on the premises of bars, pubs or taverns.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-32. - Closing times.
(a)
All premises upon which alcoholic beverages are sold by the bottle or package not for consumption on the premises and not in conjunction with another authorized business shall be closed and empty of customers between the hours of 1:55 a.m. and 6:00 a.m. except on Sundays when said premises shall be closed between the hours of 1:55 a.m. Sunday morning and 6:00 a.m. the following Monday morning. Notwithstanding the foregoing, premises where malt beverages, wine and/or distilled spirits are sold by the package may be open on Sundays between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m.
(b)
All premises upon which alcoholic beverages are sold for consumption on the premises shall be closed and empty of customers between the hours of 1:55 a.m. and 6:00 a.m., except on Sundays, when said premises shall be closed between the hours of 1:55 a.m. Sunday morning and 6:00 a.m. the following Monday morning.
(c)
Notwithstanding subsection (b), restaurants, as defined in section 3-1 of this chapter, whereupon alcoholic beverages are sold for consumption on the premises shall be allowed to be open on Sundays from 11:00 a.m. until 1:55 a.m. the following Monday morning.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12; Ord. ( W-2-20 ), § II, of 4-7-20)
Sec. 3-33. - Possession of alcoholic beverages upon which taxes have not been paid.
(a)
An individual may possess and transport in this city the following quantities of alcoholic beverages upon which Georgia taxes have not been paid:
(1)
In the case of distilled spirits, not in excess of one-half (½) gallon.
(2)
In the case of malt beverages, not in excess of one thousand four hundred forty (1,440) ounces or five (5) standard cases of twelve-ounce cans or its equivalent.
(3)
In the case of wine, not in excess of one-half (½) gallon.
(b)
The provisions of this section shall apply only with respect to alcoholic beverages purchased by the possessor outside of this state in accordance with the laws of the place where purchased and brought into this state by the purchaser. The burden of proof that the beverages were purchased outside this state and in accordance with the laws of the place where purchased shall in all cases be upon the possessor of the beverages.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-34. - Catered functions.
(a)
An authorized catered function is any event at which an alcoholic beverage caterer sells alcoholic beverages and a meal is served.
(b)
No authorized catered function shall take place on the premises of any alcoholic beverage licensee who is not also the caterer for the function.
(c)
All alcoholic beverage licensees for consumption on premises must also have an alcoholic beverage caterers license in order to sell or serve the type of alcoholic beverages for which they are licensed on premises at any authorized catered function or event off-premises. Alcoholic beverage caterers licenses can only be issued to licensees that already hold a license for consumption on premises.
(d)
All alcoholic beverage caterer licensees must obtain an event permit from the City of Winder prior to selling or serving any alcoholic beverages at any authorized catered function or event. The application shall include the name of the caterer, the date, address, and time of the event; and the licensed alcoholic beverage caterer's license number. When the caterer is licensed by a local government other than the City of Winder, an event permit fee of fifty dollars ($50.00) shall be charged and the city shall levy local excise taxes on the total quantity of alcoholic beverages brought into the city by the caterer.
(e)
At any event at which alcoholic beverages are served, sold or offered for sale, the license holder shall provide a City of Winder uniformed police officer for safety and security purposes beginning at the time the first drink is poured or served and until fifteen (15) minutes following the close of the event. At least one (1) officer shall be provided for the first two hundred (200) persons in attendance and at least one (1) additional officer for every additional one hundred (100) persons in attendance.
(f)
All persons employed at the alcoholic catered event must be at least twenty-one (21) years of age.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-35. - Exception for city sponsored events.
(a)
Whenever the city council shall determine that the public interest would be served by such action, the council may designate by resolution that the provisions of ordinance section 3-26 shall not apply to city sponsored events at such place, at such time and in such manner as specifically designated by resolution.
(b)
In the event that the city council does enact a resolution excepting a specific event from the provisions of ordinance section 3-26, the following provisions shall control in that instance:
(1)
Two-drink on-street limit. Any establishment licensed to dispense alcoholic beverages by the drink for consumption on the premises is authorized to dispense beer or wine only (and not distilled spirits) in a paper or plastic cup, or other container other than a can, bottle, or glass, away from or for removal from the premises; provided, however, that no establishment shall dispense to any person more than two (2) such alcoholic beverages at a time away from or for removal from the premises, and no person shall possess or remove at one (1) time more than two (2) such alcoholic beverages from the licensed premises. A single one-half (½) carafe container shall be considered to be two (2) such alcoholic beverages. Employees of licensed establishments, if on duty and not consuming alcoholic beverages, may exceed this limit during the performance of their duties, if allowed by the resolution.
(2)
Size limited to sixteen ounces for single beverage or one-half carafe for wine. No container in which beer or wine is dispensed pursuant to this section shall exceed sixteen (16) fluid ounces in size for a single drink; provided, however, that wine may be dispensed in a one-half (½) carafe container not exceeding twenty (20) fluid ounces in size. No person shall hold in his or her possession on the streets and sidewalks, in parks and squares, or in other public places within the defined area any open alcoholic beverage container which exceeds the restrictions contained in this section.
(3)
Drinking from can, bottle, or glass prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any person to drink or attempt to drink any alcoholic beverage from a can, bottle, or glass or to possess in an open can, bottle, or glass any alcoholic beverage on the streets, sidewalks, rights-of-way, and parking lots, whether public or private.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-36. - Violations.
Any alcoholic beverage license holder or his agents or employees violating this chapter shall forfeit his privilege of doing business in the city, and the alcoholic beverage license theretofore granted by the city may be revoked or suspended; and in addition thereto such person shall be subject to the penalties provided in section 1-8 for violations of this Code, or such other penalties that may be provided for in this chapter.
(Ord. (W-10-12) of 9-11-12)
Sec. 3-37. - Reporting of disciplinary actions.
(a)
Definitions.
Bar, for the purposes of this section, means any licensee that derives seventy-five (75) percent or more of total annual gross revenue from the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises.
Disciplinary action means any citation or arrest arising out of the violation of any law, rule, regulation, resolution or ordinance of the city relating to the manufacture, distribution, sale or possession of alcoholic beverages against a licensee, an employee of a licensee, or any person holding a financial interest in the license of the licensee on the premises or place of business or any licensee.
Licensee, for the purposes of this section, means any entity licensed by the city that operates a bar.
(b)
Reporting. Within forty-five (45) days following any disciplinary action against a licensee taken by any officer, department, agency or instrumentality of the city, the city clerk, or the clerk's designee, shall report the disciplinary action and the identity of the licensee to the Georgia Department of Revenue.
(Ord. ( W-7-16 ) of 9-22-16)
