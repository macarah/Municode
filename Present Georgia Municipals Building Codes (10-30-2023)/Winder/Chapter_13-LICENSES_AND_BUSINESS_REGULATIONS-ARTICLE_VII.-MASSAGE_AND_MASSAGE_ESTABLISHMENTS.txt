ARTICLE VII. - MASSAGE AND MASSAGE ESTABLISHMENTS

Sec. 13-145. - Definitions.
For the purpose of this article, the following words and phrases shall have the meanings respectively ascribed to them:
Massage: Any method of pressure on or friction against or stroking, kneading, rubbing, tapping, pounding, vibrating or stimulating of the external parts of the human body with the hands or with the aid of any mechanical electrical apparatus or appliances with or without any such supplementary aids as rubbing alcohol, liniments, antiseptics, oils, powder, creams, lotions, ointment or other such similar preparations commonly used in the practice of massage, under such circumstances that it is reasonably expected that the person to whom the treatment is provided or some third person on his or her behalf will pay money or give any other consideration or any gratuity therefor.
Massage establishment: Any commercial establishment having a source of income or compensation derived from the practice of massage as defined in this section, and which has a fixed place of business where any person, firm, association or corporation engages in or carries on any of the activities defined as massage.
(Ord. (W-11-91) of 10-8-91)
Sec. 13-146. - Massage prohibited, exemptions.
(a)
Prohibited. The practice of massage as defined in section 13-145 and massage establishments as defined in section 13-145 are prohibited within the city.
(b)
Exemptions. This article shall not apply to the following individuals while engaged in the performance of the duties of their respective professions:
(1)
Physicians, surgeons, chiropractors, osteopaths or physical therapists who are duly licensed to practice their respective professions in the state;
(2)
Nurses who are registered under the laws of the state;
(3)
Athletic directors, coaches or trainers who are affiliated with an accredited educational institution or professional sports team and whose work is limited to athletic team members;
(4)
Licensed cosmetologists or barbers who provide services customarily given in such establishments; provided that such services comply with the rules and regulations set forth by the state board governing cosmetologists and barbers;
(5)
Massage therapist. For the purposes of this article, a massage therapist is defined as a person who:
a.
Has reached the age of 18 years.
b.
Has not been convicted or pled guilty or entered a plea of nolo contendere under any federal, state or local law of any crime involving moral turpitude, illegal gambling, any felony, public indecency, misdemeanor involving any type of sexually related crime, any crime of possession, sale or distribution of illegal drugs, distribution of materials depicting nudity or sexual activity as defined under state law, criminal solicitation to commit any of these listed offenses, or attempts to commit any of these listed offenses for a period of ten years prior to the date of business license application and has been released from parole or probation.
c.
Is certified as a massage therapist by the state, or by any other state of the Union, provided that any state's requirements are consistent with subsections b. and d.; or
d.
Can demonstrate graduation from a school in good standing of massage therapy, consisting of at least 550 hours of a course of study consisting of a curriculum of physical culture, massage, anatomy, hydrotherapy, hygiene and other related subjects; or
e.
Is certified by the National Certification Board of Therapeutic Massage and Bodywork.
(Ord. (W-11-91) of 10-8-91; Ord. (W-4-98) of 6-1-98)
