ARTICLE IV. - GAS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 5-66. - Definitions.
The following definitions are provided for the purpose of interpretation and administration of this article:
Certain appliances: Conversion burners, floor furnaces, central heating plants, vented wall furnaces, water heaters and boilers.
Certificate of approval: A document or tag issued and/or attached by the inspector to the inspected material, piping or appliance installation, filled out, together with the date and address of the premises, and signed by the inspector.
Gas system: The municipal gas distribution system or any person distributing gas within the corporate limits of the city or authorized and proposing to so engage.
Inspector: The building inspector acting as gas inspector, or any person appointed as gas inspector, and shall include each assistant inspector, if any, from time to time acting as such under this article by appointment of the mayor and council of the city.
(Code 1968, § 12-3)
Sec. 5-67. - Violations.
Any person who shall fail to comply with or who shall violate any of the provisions of this article or the gas code adopted herein shall be guilty of an offense, and upon conviction thereof shall be punished as provided in section 1-8 of this Code of Ordinances, and in addition thereto the license of such person may be suspended or revoked.
(Code 1968, § 12-18)
Sec. 5-68. - Liability of city, etc.
This article shall not be construed as imposing upon the city any liability or responsibility for damages to any person injured by any defect in any gas piping or appliance mentioned herein, or by installation thereof, nor shall the city, or any official or employee thereof, be held as assuming any such liability or responsibility by reason of the inspection authorized hereunder or the certificate of approval issued by the inspector.
(Code 1968, § 12-17)
State Law reference— Municipal liability, Ga. Code Ann., § 36-33-1 et seq.
Sec. 5-69. - License required.
No person shall engage in or work at the installation, extension or alteration of consumer's gas piping or certain gas appliances, until such person has a contractor's license issued by the Georgia Secretary of State.
(Code 1968, § 12-10; Ord. ( W-1-20 ), § XII, of 4-7-20)
Cross reference— Licenses and business regulations, ch. 13.

Sec. 5-89. - Resolution of controversies.
The building inspector acting as gas inspector, shall resolve all controversies which may arise under this article, and in so doing shall be subject to the control and direction of the mayor and city council.
(Code 1968, § 12-8)
Sec. 5-90. - Information and advice.
It shall be the duty of the inspector to confer from time to time with representatives of the local health department, the local fire department and the gas system, and otherwise obtain from proper sources all helpful information and advice, presenting same to the appropriate officials from time to time for their consideration.
(Code 1968, § 12-6)
Sec. 5-91. - Supplementary to other laws.
In interpreting and applying the provisions of the gas code adopted in this article, they shall be held to be the minimum requirements for the promotion of the public health, safety and general welfare; and as supplementary to all other laws, codes or regulations of the state, county or city, formulated in the interest of public health, safety or general welfare; and where the gas code imposes a greater restriction than is imposed by other laws, codes or regulations, the gas code shall govern, but where any other laws, ordinances, rules or regulations impose greater restrictions than are required by the gas code, such other provisions shall govern.
(Code 1968, § 12-4)
Charter reference— Power of city to protect the public health, safety and welfare, § 1-3(33), (37).
Sec. 5-92. - Enforcement by inspector generally.
The inspector is authorized and directed to enforce all of the provisions of this article, and the inspector, upon presentation of proper credentials, may enter any building or premises at reasonable times for the purpose of making inspections or preventing violations of this article.
(Code 1968, § 12-6)
Sec. 5-93. - Interpretation.
The building inspector acting as gas inspector shall interpret this article and the gas code adopted herein. The construction made by the gas inspector shall have the legal effect of, and shall be enforceable as, an administrative determination of the legislative intent.
(Code 1968, § 12-7)
Sec. 5-94. - Disconnection of service.
The inspector shall be authorized to disconnect any gas piping or fixture or appliance for which a certificate of approval is required but has not been issued with respect to same, or which, upon inspection, shall be found defective or in such condition as to endanger life or property. In all cases where such disconnection is made, a notice shall be attached to the piping, fixture or appliance disconnected by the inspector, which notice shall state that the same has been disconnected by the inspector, together with the reason or reasons therefor; and it shall be unlawful for any person to remove said notice or reconnect said gas piping or fixture or appliance without authorization by the inspector, and such gas piping or fixture or appliance shall not be put in service or used until the inspector has attached his certificate of approval in lieu of his prior disconnection notice.
(Code 1968, § 12-6)
Sec. 5-95. - Attestation, filing of copies.
One (1) copy of the gas code adopted in this article shall be on file in the building inspection office.
(Code 1968, § 12-2; Ord. ( W-1-20 ), § XIX, of 4-7-20)
Sec. 5-96. - Permit—Required.
No person shall install a gas conversion burner, floor furnace, central heating plant, vented wall furnace, water heater, boiler, consumer's gas piping or convert existing piping to utilize natural gas without first obtaining a permit to do such work from the permit clerk; however, permits shall not be required for setting or connecting other gas appliances, or for the repair of leaks in house piping.
(Code 1968, § 12-13; Ord. ( W-1-20 ), § XX, of 4-7-20)

Sec. 5-99. - Permit—Gas service department.
The gas service department shall not be required to obtain permits to set meters, or to extend, relocate, remove or repair its service lines, mains or other facilities, or for work having to do with its own gas system.
(Code 1968, § 12-13)
Sec. 5-100. - Use of existing piping.
Notwithstanding any provision in this article to the contrary, consumer's piping presently installed, or piping installed to supply gas other than natural gas, may be converted to natural gas, if the inspector finds, upon inspection and proper tests, that such piping will render reasonably satisfactory gas service to the consumer and will not in any way endanger life or property; otherwise, such piping shall be altered or replaced, in whole or in part, to conform with the requirements of this article.
(Code 1968, § 12-9)
Sec. 5-101. - Fees for inspections—Generally.
The fees for inspections and reinspections under this division shall be fixed from time to time by the mayor and council.
(Code 1968, § 12-16)
Sec. 5-102. - Same—Payment.
Any and all fees under this division shall be paid by the person to whom the permit is issued.
(Code 1968, § 12-16)
Sec. 5-103. - Rough piping inspection.
A rough piping inspection shall be made after all new piping authorized by the permit therefor has been installed, and before any such piping has been covered or concealed or any fixtures or gas appliances have been attached thereto.
(Code 1968, § 12-14)
Sec. 5-104. - Final inspection, test—When made.
The final piping inspection shall be made after all piping authorized by the permit has been installed and after all portions thereof which are to be concealed by plastering or otherwise have been so concealed, and before any fixtures or gas appliances have been attached thereto.
(Code 1968, § 12-14)
Sec. 5-105. - Same—Pressure test.
The final piping inspection shall include a pressure test, at which time the piping shall stand an air pressure equal to not less than the pressure of a column of mercury six (6) inches in height, and the piping shall hold this air pressure for a period of at least ten (10) minutes without any perceptible drop. A mercury column gauge shall be used for the test. All tools, apparatus, labor and assistance necessary for the tests shall be furnished by the installer of such piping.
(Code 1968, § 12-14)
Sec. 5-106. - Approval of work.
The inspector shall issue a certificate of approval at the completion of the work for which a permit for consumer piping has been issued, if after inspection it is found that such work complies with the provisions of this article. A duplicate of each certificate issued covering consumer's gas piping shall be delivered to the gas service department and used as its authority to render gas service.
(Code 1968, § 12-15)
Sec. 5-107. - Gas vents.
All gas vents shall be vented above the roof line, except that side vents will be allowed provided that no vent shall be located within six (6) feet of the side or top of a window, door or other opening. No vent shall be allowed in any area directly below a door, window or other opening. No vent shall be allowed in any area directly below a door, window or other opening. For the purposes of this section any door, window or similar appurtenance that does not open shall be considered part of the wall. See Diagram "A" below.
(Ord. (15-W-89) of 10-3-89; Ord. (25-W-89) of 12-11-89)
