ARTICLE II. - RECORDS MANAGEMENT

Sec. 2-31. - Establishment of program.
The board of commissioners does adopt and utilize the rules and regulations outlined in the Georgia Records Act (O.C.G.A. § 50-18-90 et seq.) as a basis for establishing a records management program in and for the county.
(Res. of 7-12-88)
Sec. 2-32. - Adoption of statewide common standards.
The statewide General Common Standards which are applicable to county records and approved by the county records committee are hereby implemented and made the common standards of the county.
(Res. of 7-12-88)
Sec. 2-33. - Position of records management officer created.
The position of records management officer is hereby created. The records management officer shall administer a records management program for the county under the direction of the county records committee.
(Res. of 7-12-88)
Sec. 2-34. - Administration.
The board of commissioners is responsible for the administration of the records management program. The records management officer for the county will act for and in behalf of the county in directing and coordinating all records management matters.
(Res. of 7-12-88)
Sec. 2-35. - Records committee; created, composition, duty.
There is hereby created a records committee consisting of the probate court judge, the clerk of the superior court, the county clerk, and the records management officer. It is the duty of the records committee to review and recommend approval, disapproval or modification of records retention schedules to the board of commissioners. Review of records retention schedules will be based upon legal, fiscal, administrative and historical retention requirements. The records management officer shall serve as chairman of this committee, and the final approval authority shall be the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 7-12-88)
Sec. 2-36. - Annual budget.
The county records committee shall prepare and submit a budget request to the board of commissioners as required by county local legislation.
(Res. of 7-12-88)
Sec. 2-37. - Archival depository.
An archival depository will be selected to store those records determined to have permanent historical value.
(Res. of 7-12-88)
Sec. 2-38. - Storage in records center; alteration, destruction of records.
(a)
A centralized records center shall be established and all records not required for day-to-day office operation shall be transferred to the records center until destroyed in accordance with the approved records retention schedule.
(b)
No record shall be altered or destroyed except in accordance with provisions of this article.
(Res. of 7-12-88)
Sec. 2-39. - Activities, duties of records management officer.
(a)
The records management officer shall act for the county records committee in matters of:
(1)
Establishing and maintaining an active and continuing program for the economical, efficient and effective management of records;
(2)
Developing and issuing rules, procedures and regulations subject to review by the county records committee, the department of archives and history, and the state records committee;
(3)
Establishing standards for economical, efficient management relating to the creation, utilization, maintenance, retention, preservation and disposition of records, filing and copying equipment, supplies, microfilming of records, and vital records programs;
(4)
Assisting the head of each county administrative office and the office of each county elected public official in developing and implementing a program of records management;
(5)
Implementing records management procedures and regulations approved by the county records committee;
(6)
Developing a records management center program.
(b)
It shall be the duty of the records management officer to:
(1)
Direct the records management program to achieve efficient and economical management practices relating to the creation, maintenance, utilization, retention, preservation and disposition of records, filing equipment, microfilm equipment, supplies, microfilming of records and vital records;
(2)
Assist in the scheduling of records, develop a records control schedule for the county and maintain the schedule on a current basis;
(3)
Supervise implementation of records retention schedule;
(4)
Supervise physical transfer of records from high-cost office space to low-cost records center or an archival depository;
(5)
Conduct a microfilm program and supervise a county microfilm laboratory;
(6)
Develop and supervise a program for the training of records management assistants and filing personnel;
(7)
Prepare and submit reports of status and progress of the records management program to the county records committee;
(8)
Review, evaluate and recommend more efficient paperwork that will simplify operations throughout all offices of the county;
(9)
Conduct surveys to determine the most efficient and economical filing systems and filing equipment for use by the county;
(10)
Recommend the selection and utilization of filing equipment and supplies.
(Res. of 7-12-88)
Sec. 2-40. - Duties of department heads.
It shall be the duty of the head of each administrative office of the county to:
(1)
Cooperate fully with the county records committee and the records management officer in complying with the provisions of this article and the provisions of the state records act;
(2)
Cause to be made and preserve records containing adequate and proper documentation of the organization policies, decisions, procedures and essential transactions of the office and designed to furnish the information necessary to protect the legal and financial rights of the county and of persons directly affected by the office's activities;
(3)
Implement records management procedures and regulations issued by the county records committee and the records management officer in conjunction with the department of archives and history and the state records committee;
(4)
Submit to the records management officer, in accordance with the rules and regulations of the state records committee, a recommended disposition standard for each records series in his custody;
(5)
Establish necessary safeguards against the removal or loss of records and such further safeguards as may be recommended by the records management officer. Such safeguards shall include notification of all officials and personnel that county records are public property and as such shall constitute a record of public acts. Further, that such records shall not be placed in the custody of private or semipublic institutions or turned over to private individuals, and that no records in the custody of a county office are to be destroyed or otherwise alienated except in accordance with the provisions of the state records act.
(Res. of 7-12-88)
