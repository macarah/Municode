ARTICLE III. - FINAL PLAT

Sec. 17.5-41. - Preparation of final plat.
The developer shall have a registered civil engineer or surveyor prepare the final plat of the subdivision.
(Ord. No. 133, § 12(b), 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-42. - Fee for filing final plat.
After approval of the preliminary plat, the final plat original tracing shall be filed with the City Planner for transmittal to the county subdivision administrator. Any fees required by the county shall be collected by the subdivision administrator.
(Ord. No. 133, § 13, 10-2-67; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 14, 6-17-13)
Sec. 17.5-43. - Review of final plat and certification.
The final plat will be forwarded by the county subdivision administrator to the following persons in the various county departments for required certification in the following sequence:
(1)
Property numbering engineer—Planning department.
(2)
Chief water engineer—Water division—Public works department.
(3)
Chief sewer engineer—Sewer division—Public works department.
(4)
Chief public health engineer—Sanitary engineering division—Health department.
(5)
Subdivision drainage engineer—Roads and bridges division—Public works department.
(6)
Road engineer—Roads and bridges division—Public works department.
(7)
Director or head—Public works and utilities departments.
Any one (1) of the above individuals may withhold certification of the final plat if the plat or additional required data does not comply with the requirements of his department.
(Ord. No. 133, § 14, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-44. - Submission to Planning Commission.
Upon receipt of certification by all of the above department authorities, the final plat shall be returned to the City Planner by the subdivision administrator. The Planning Commission shall approve or disapprove the final plat and such action shall be entered upon the plat and certified by the secretary of the Planning Commission.
(Ord. No. 133, § 15, 10-2-67; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 14, 6-17-13)
Sec. 17.5-45. - Concurrence by City Commission.
After final action by the Planning Commission and before approval by the chairman of the county board of commissioners of roads and revenue, the final plat shall be transmitted to the City Council for review and concurrence.
(Ord. No. 133, § 16, 10-2-67; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 13, 6-17-13)
Sec. 17.5-46. - Approval by chairman of county board of commissioners.
After final action by the Planning Commission and the City Council, the final plat and other supplementary material required will be transmitted to the county subdivision administrator who shall in turn submit said plat to the chairman of the county board of commissioners of roads and revenues for his approval.
(Ord. No. 133, § 17, 10-2-67; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 13, 6-17-13)
Sec. 17.5-47. - Recordation.
The approved final plat will be recorded by the county subdivision administrator and transmitted to the City Planner who shall return it to the developer.
(Ord. No. 133, § 18, 10-2-67; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 14, 6-17-13)
Sec. 17.5-48. - Final plat to be on linen.
The final plat shall be composed of linen or of comparable tracing material. All data shall be drawn in drafting ink.
(Ord. No. 133, § 19, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-49. - Scale of final plat.
Final plats shall be prepared at a scale of not less than one hundred (100) feet to one (1) inch and shall have a maximum sheet size of not more than twenty-four (24) inches in width and thirty-six (36) inches in length, and a minimum sheet size of not less than twelve (12) inches in width and sixteen (16) inches in length.
(Ord. No. 133, § 20, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-50. - Final plat not to vary substantially from preliminary plat.
The final plat shall compare favorably with the approved preliminary plat on file in the Doraville City Hall, and shall comply with the regulations of the zoning chapter.
(Ord. No. 133, § 21, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-51. - Information final plat to contain.
The final plat shall show the following:
(1)
Sufficient data to determine readily and reproduce on the ground the location, bearing and length of every street line, lot line, boundary line, and building line, whether curved or straight. This shall include but not be limited to the radius, length of arc, internal angles and tangent distance for the centerline of curved streets and curved property lines that are not the boundary of curved streets.
(2)
Tract boundary lines, land lot and district lines, City and county limit lines, right-of-way lines of streets, easements and other rights of way, and property lines of residential lots and other sites.
(3)
All dimensions shall be accurate to the nearest one-tenth ( 1/10 ) of a foot and all angles accurate to the nearest minute.
(4)
Name and right-of-way width of each street including necessary right-of-way required for present or future widening of major, minor, collector, residential or other streets as shown on the thoroughfare plan.
(5)
House numbers. Numbers will be placed on the final plat by the property numbering engineer.
(6)
Title, north arrow, date, scale, land lot numbers, and district numbers.
(7)
Location, dimensions and purpose of any easements and any areas to be dedicated to public use or sites for other than residential use with notes stating their purpose and limitations.
(8)
Lots or sites numbered in numerical order and blocks lettered alphabetically. All lot and block numerals shall be kept in a uniform sequence on all plats and units of the subdivision.
(9)
Accurate location, material and description of monuments and markers. Two (2) or more permanent monuments meeting the requirements of the county public works department shall be installed in each subdivision.
(10)
Lots which shall not be built upon until detailed plans for grading and drainage have been approved by the county public works departments.
(11)
Covenants to be recorded on the property (these may be referenced on final plat and be attached thereto).
(12)
Total acreage in site and total acreage in drainage area for each culvert or other drainage structure.
(Ord. No. 133, § 22, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-52. - Certifications to be on final plat.
A blank space of at least thirty (30) square inches shall be provided on the final plat to allow room for the following certifications by county officials and the other certificates required by sections 17.5-53 and 17.5-54.
(1)
Certification of the road engineer of acceptance of street design and improvements.
(2)
Certification of the subdivision engineer of acceptance of site location and drainage improvements.
(3)
Certification by the water engineer, sewer engineer, health engineer and director of the county public works department that the subdivider has complied with one (1) of the following alternates:
a.
All improvements have been installed in accordance with requirements.
b.
A performance bond has been posted in sufficient amount to assure completion of all required improvements.
c.
Any other guarantees of satisfactory completion of required improvements acceptable to the various departments.
(Ord. No. 133, § 23, 10-2-67)

The following wording for the engineers' and owners' acknowledgements shall be shown and certified on the final plat:
(a)

(Ord. No. 133, § 24, 10-2-67)

Sec. 17.5-55. - Protective covenants to meet minimum zoning requirements.
The final plat shall not contain protective covenants stipulating lower standards than the minimum restrictions required by the zoning ordinance pertaining to yard, building site, height, floor area and off-street parking.
(Ord. No. 133, § 26, 10-2-67)
Secs. 17.5-56—17.5-70. - Reserved.
