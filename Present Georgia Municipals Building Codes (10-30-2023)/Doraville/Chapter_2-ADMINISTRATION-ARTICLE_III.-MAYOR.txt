Sec. 2-57. - Authority of Mayor to declare emergency.
modified
In addition to any and all powers enumerated in the City Code, the Mayor, as the chief executive of the City, shall have the right to declare an emergency to exist when, in the Mayor's opinion, one (1) or more of the following conditions exist:
(1)
There is extreme likelihood of danger of destruction of life or property due to unusual conditions;
(2)
Unusual or extreme weather conditions, making use of City streets or areas difficult or impossible;
(3)
Civil unrest, commotion or uprising is imminent or exists;
(4)
There is a stoppage or loss of electrical power affecting a major portion of the City; and/or
(5)
Such other emergency condition that provides good cause for exercising the emergency powers set forth in this article.
(Code 1969, § 2-81; Ord. No. 2022-18 , § 1, 3-14-22)
Sec. 2-58. - Powers of City Manager after declaration of emergency.
After Mayor's declaration of emergency, the City Manager or designee shall have the right to exercise any or all of the following powers:
(1)
To use employees of the City to assist in the safety and preservation of life, limb and property of the citizenry of the City.
(2)
To close streets and sidewalks and to delineate areas within the City wherein an emergency exists.
(3)
To impose emergency curfew regulations.
(4)
To close any and all municipally owned buildings and other facilities to the use of the general public.
(5)
To do any and all acts necessary and incidental to the preservation of life, limb and property of the citizenry of the City.
(Code 1969, § 2-82; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 8, 6-17-13)
Editor's note— Section 8 of Ord. No. 2013-11, adopted June 17, 2013, changed the title of § 2-58 from "Powers of Mayor after declaration of emergency" to "Powers of City Manager after declaration of emergency."
Sec. 2-59. - Duration, extension of emergency powers.
No emergency power of the City Manager or designee may be effective for more than seventy-two (72) hours after the declaration of an emergency by the Mayor. However, upon declaration of a second or further emergencies to exist the emergency powers as set forth herein may be exercised during such further emergency period but never for more than seventy-two (72) hours in one (1) declared emergency period.
(Code 1969, § 2-83; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 1, 6-17-13)
Sec. 2-60. - Termination of emergency period.
No emergency period shall extend beyond the next regular, special or called meeting of the Mayor and City Council.
(Code 1969, § 2-84)
Sec. 2-61. - Emergency in portion of City, designation of area.
The declaration of any emergency as to a portion of the City shall specify with exactness the area in which the emergency is declared to exist.
(Code 1969, § 2-85)
Sec. 2-62. - City Manager's immediate staff.
The City Manager shall have designated immediate staff in his role as administrator. The Aide to the City Manager position shall be designated as an immediate staff position. Other positions may be added, modified, or deleted by Council as immediate staff. The immediate staff of the City Manager will be hired, disciplined or removed in the same manner as any employee working for any City Department Head. The City Manager shall exclusively supervise his immediate staff.
(Ord. No. 2009-08, § 1, 5-18-09; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 1, 6-17-13)
Editor's note— Section 1 of Ord. No. 2013-11, adopted June 17, 2013, changed the title of § 2-62 from "Mayor's immediate staff" to "City Manager's immediate staff."
Sec. 2-63. - City Manager and Council communications on personnel issues.
All personnel concern(s) of Councilmembers shall be sent, in writing, on a form solely utilized for such purpose, to the City Manager's office at City Hall within fifteen (15) business days of the event triggering the concern(s). The City Manager or designee shall respond to the form regarding Council concern(s) on personnel issues in writing within fifteen (15) business days of receipt of the written concern(s). Such time frames may be varied to accommodate previously scheduled holidays or paid time-off periods.
(Ord. No. 2009-08, § 2, 5-18-09; Ord. No. 2010-08, § 1, 3-1-10; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 1, 6-17-13)
Editor's note— Section 1 of Ord. No. 2013-11, adopted June 17, 2013, changed the title of § 2-63 from "Mayor and Council communications on personnel issues" to "City Manager and Council communications on personnel issues."
Sec. 2-64. - Mayor's salary; expenses; benefits.
(a)
Effective January 1, 2018, the salary of the Mayor shall be eighteen thousand dollars ($18,000.00) per year, paid in accordance with the established pay periods of all other employees of the City.
(b)
The Mayor shall be afforded three thousand dollars ($3,000.00) in expenses per year, reimbursed pursuant to the established policy and ordinances of the City. Qualified expenditures shall be categorized as follows:
(1)
General office supplies necessary for the performance of Mayoral duties;
(2)
Travel expenses to educational seminars and conferences as provided by the yearly budget appropriation, including per diem meal expenses in accordance with the City's policies and mileage reimbursement;
(3)
Professional memberships necessary for the performances of his/her duties as Mayor.
(4)
Educational materials, as well as copying and printing costs, necessary for the performance of his/her duties;
(5)
Business meals;
(6)
Customary mileage reimbursement while performing his/her duties as Mayor, excluding travel to and from the City Council regular meeting location;
(7)
Use of cell phone for performing Mayoral duties; and
(8)
Other expenses as specifically provided in the yearly budget.
(c)
The Mayor shall be entitled to participate in the group benefits afforded each employee of the City, with the exception that the Mayor shall be responsible for payment of one hundred (100) percent of the premiums for said benefits.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 2, 5-6-13; Ord. No. 2017-19 , 8-21-17)
