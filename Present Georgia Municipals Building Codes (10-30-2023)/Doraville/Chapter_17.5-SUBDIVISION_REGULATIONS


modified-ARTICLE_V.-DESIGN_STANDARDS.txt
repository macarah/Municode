ARTICLE V. - DESIGN STANDARDS
DIVISION 1. - STREETS

Sec. 17.5-91. - Generally.
The arrangement, character, extent, width, grade and location of all streets shall conform to the thoroughfare plan and shall be considered in their relation to existing and planned streets, to topographical conditions, to public convenience and safety, and in their appropriate relation to the proposed uses of the land to be served by such streets.
(Ord. No. 133, § 36, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-92. - Arrangement where not shown on thoroughfare plan.
Where such is not shown in the thoroughfare plan, the arrangement of streets in a subdivision shall either:
(a)
Provide for the continuation or appropriate projection of existing principal streets in surrounding areas; or
(b)
Conform to a plan for a neighborhood approved or adopted by the Planning Commission to meet a particular situation where topographical or other conditions make continuance or conformance to existing streets impracticable.
(Ord. No. 133, § 37, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-93. - Local streets.
Local subdivision streets shall be so designed that their use by through traffic will be discouraged.
(Ord. No. 133, § 38, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-94. - Special treatment along major transportation facility.
Where a subdivision abuts or contains an existing or proposed expressway or arterial street or other major transportation facility, the Planning Commission may require marginal access streets, reverse frontage with screen planting contained in a nonaccess reservation along the rear property line, deep lots with rear service alleys, or such other treatment as may be necessary for adequate protection of residential properties and to afford separation of different modes or character of traffic.
(Ord. No. 133, § 39, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-95. - Platting requirements when subdivision borders major transportation right-of-way.
Where a subdivision borders on or contains a railroad right-of-way or expressway right-of-way or other major transportation facility right-of-way, the Planning Commission may require a street approximately parallel to and on each side of such right-of-way, at a distance suitable for the appropriate use of the intervening land, as for park purposes in residential districts, or for commercial or industrial purposes in appropriate districts. Such distances shall also be determined with due regard for the requirements of approach grades and future grade separations.
(Ord. No. 133, § 40, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-96. - Reserve strips prohibited.
Reserve strips controlling access to streets shall be prohibited except where their control is definitely placed in the county or City.
(Ord. No. 133, § 41, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-97. - Street jogs.
Street jogs with centerline offsets of less than one hundred twenty-five (125) feet shall be avoided.
(Ord. No. 133, § 42, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-98. - Intersections—Generally.
Street intersections shall be as nearly at right angles as practicable.
(Ord. No. 133, § 43, 10-2-67)

Sec. 17.5-101. - Half streets.
Half streets shall be prohibited, except where essential to the reasonable development of the subdivision. Where it is determined to be practicable to require the dedication of the other half when the adjoining property is subdivided, the other half of the street shall be platted within such tract.
(Ord. No. 133, § 46, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-102. - Temporary dead-end streets.
Temporary dead-end streets may be platted where the Planning Commission deems desirable and where the land adjoins property that is not subdivided, in which case such streets shall be carried to the boundaries thereof.
(Ord. No. 133, § 47, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-103. - Length of permanent dead-end streets; turn-around required.
Dead-end streets, designed to be so permanently, shall not be longer than eight hundred (800) feet unless absolutely impractical due to topographical or other conditions that are unavoidable in the opinion of the Planning Commission and shall be provided at the closed end with a turn-around having an outside roadway diameter of at least one hundred (100) feet, and a street right-of-way diameter of at least one hundred twenty (120) feet unless otherwise designed by the county roads engineer.
(Ord. No. 133, § 48, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-104. - Street names.
No street names shall be used which will duplicate or be confused with the names of existing streets, except for courts, culs-de-sac or loop streets leading off a main street. Street names shall be subject to the approval of the Planning Commission and concurrence by the City Council.
(Ord. No. 133, § 49, 10-2-67; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 13, 6-17-13)

Sec. 17.5-106. - Grades—Minimum.
No street grade shall be less than one (1) percent and no one (1) percent grade shall be longer than three hundred (300) feet.
(Ord. No. 133, § 51, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-107. - Minimum horizontal curve radius.
No street shall have a horizontal curve radius of less than one hundred fifty (150) feet.
(Ord. No. 133, § 52, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-108. - Minimum sight distance.
All streets shall have a minimum sight distance of at least two hundred (200) feet.
(Ord. No. 133, § 53, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-109. - Design of intersections.
Street intersections shall not be designed in such a manner as to create a traffic hazard. A minimum of one hundred fifty (150) feet clear sight distance in each direction from the intersection shall be provided. Where a subdivision street enters an existing major or minor thoroughfare, a minimum of two hundred fifty (250) feet sight distance in each direction shall be maintained. If, due to other restrictions, this minimum sight distance cannot be maintained, the developer shall, at his expense, provide adequate traffic signal lights subject to the approval and installation by the county public safety department.
(Ord. No. 133, § 54, 10-2-67)
Secs. 17.5-110—17.5-120. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - EASEMENTS

Sec. 17.5-121. - Generally.
Permission must be obtained by the subdivider from the department of public works, water system or sewer department, as determined by the jurisdiction, for the dedication of utility easements prior to the submission of such dedication.
(Ord. No. 133, § 55, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-122. - Drainage.
Where a subdivision is traversed by a watercourse, drainage way, natural channel, or stream, there shall be provided an easement conforming substantially to the limits of such watercourse plus additional width as necessary to accommodate future construction, but not to exceed the floodplain plus five (5) feet, as recommended by the county public works department. The adequacy of such easements shall be based upon assuming one hundred (100) percent runoff for the drainage area.
(Ord. No. 133, § 56, 10-2-67)
Secs. 17.5-123—17.5-130. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - BLOCKS

Sec. 17.5-131. - Generally.
The lengths, widths and shapes of blocks shall be determined with due regard to:
(a)
Provision of adequate building sites suitable to the special needs of the type of use contemplated.
(b)
Zoning requirements as to lot sizes and dimensions.
(c)
Needs for convenient access, circulation, control, and safety of street traffic.
(d)
Limitations and opportunities of topography.
(Ord. No. 133, § 57, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-132. - Desirable maximum and minimum lengths.
The desirable maximum block length is twelve hundred (1,200) feet and the desirable minimum length is six hundred (600) feet.
(Ord. No. 133, § 58, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-133. - Pedestrian easements may be required.
Pedestrian easements, not less than ten (10) feet, may be required where deemed essential to provide circulation, or access to schools, playgrounds, shopping centers, transportation and other community facilities.
(Ord. No. 133, § 59, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-134. - Preservation of trees.
The developer shall preserve all trees of major growth in the subdivision not required to be removed for building construction or site improvements.
(Ord. No. 133, § 60, 10-2-67)
Secs. 17.5-135—17.5-150. - Reserved.
DIVISION 4. - LOTS

Sec. 17.5-151. - Generally.
The lot size, width, depth, shape and orientation, and the minimum building setback, side yard and rear yard lines shall be in accordance with requirements of the zoning ordinance.
(Ord. No. 133, § 61, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-152. - Corner lots.
Corner lots for residential use shall have a greater width than that required for inside lots, as specified by the zoning ordinance for the zoning district within which such lots are located, in order to permit appropriate building setback from and orientation to both streets.
(Ord. No. 133, § 62, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-153. - Frontage.
Each lot shall front upon a publicly maintained street.
(Ord. No. 133, § 63, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-154. - Double frontage and reverse frontage.
Double frontage, and reverse frontage lots, shall be avoided except where essential to provide separation of residential development from traffic arteries or to overcome specific disadvantages of topography or orientation. A planted screen reservation of at least ten (10) feet in width, and across which there shall be no right of access, shall be provided along the line of lots abutting such traffic artery or other disadvantageous use.
(Ord. No. 133, § 64, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-155. - Side lot lines.
Side lot lines shall be substantially at right angles or radial to street lines.
(Ord. No. 133, § 65, 10-2-67)
Secs. 17.5-156—17.5-170. - Reserved.
DIVISION 5. - PUBLIC SITES AND OPEN SPACES

Sec. 17.5-171. - Where subdivision located in path of proposed expressway, etc.
Where a proposed subdivision or any part thereof is located in the path of a proposed expressway, major or minor thoroughfare, other principal street or major transportation facility, such subdivision shall not be approved unless the subdivision is redesigned to allow for the right-of-way of the proposed highway or transportation facility. If the subdivision is not approved because of inability to meet the preceeding requirements, the applicant may seek relief by filing a petition at such time with the county commissioners, who may grant relief only in accordance with the following:
(a)
The county commissioners may authorize changes in the plat so that the total number of lots will be equal to those that would be obtained if the proposed major right-of-way were a minor street; or
(b)
The county commissioners may authorize purchase of the property or portion thereof in the proposed right-of-way within one (1) year of the date of submission of the preliminary plat; or
(c)
The county commissioners may approve the preliminary plat subject to its meeting all requirements pertaining to land development in the zoning ordinance and in these regulations; and
(d)
Any such above action by the county commissioners shall be subject to review and concurrence by the City Council.
(Ord. No. 133, § 66, 10-2-67; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 13, 6-17-13)
Sec. 17.5-172. - Park sites.
Where a site for a public park is deemed suitable by the Planning Commission or City or county park authorities, in a subdivision, such site shall be reserved for public usage. The subdivider shall either dedicate said site to the City or the county without cost or allow a period of one (1) year from the date of submittal of the preliminary plat during which time the City Council or the county commissioners may authorize purchase of said property for public park purposes.
If not otherwise dedicated by the subdivision plat, and if the reserved site has not been authorized for purchase by the above authorities within the required time, the preliminary plat shall be deemed to have been approved subject to all other requirements of these regulations and the zoning ordinance. The final plat shall be processed in the required manner when submitted by the developer.
(Ord. No. 133, § 67, 10-2-67; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 13, 6-17-13)
Sec. 17.5-173. - School sites.
Where a site for a public school is deemed suitable by the Planning Commission or school authorities, in a subdivision, such site shall be reserved for public usage. The subdivider shall either dedicate the site to the school authorities without cost or shall allow a period of one (1) year from the date of submittal of the preliminary plat during which time the proper school authorities may authorize purchase of the property for public school purposes.
If not otherwise dedicated by the subdivision plat, and if the reserved site has not been authorized for purchase by the school authorities within the required time, the preliminary plat shall be deemed to have been approved subject to all requirements of these regulations and the zoning ordinance. The final plat shall be processed in the required manner when submitted by the developer.
(Ord. No. 133, § 68, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-174. - Dedication of parks and playgrounds.
Parks and playgrounds may be dedicated to the county with the approval of the county commissioners or to the City with the approval of the City Council. If so dedicated and accepted, the land shall be used exclusively and solely for recreational purposes for a period of at least twenty-five (25) years.
(Ord. No. 133, § 69, 10-2-67; Ord. No. 2013-11, § 13, 6-17-13)
Sec. 17.5-175. - Size and location of parks and playgrounds.
The size and location of parks and playgrounds are subject to the approval of the director of community services when to be dedicated to the county.
(Ord. No. 133, § 70, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-176. - Procedure when open space requirements not met.
Where the proposed subdivision is too small to provide space of suitable size for public open space use intended, the open space provided may be combined with that provided or to be provided in adjoining areas.
(Ord. No. 133, § 71, 10-2-67)
Secs. 17.5-176—17.5-190. - Reserved.
DIVISION 6. - GENERAL SUITABILITY

Sec. 17.5-191. - When subdivision may be disapproved.
The Planning Commission shall not approve the subdivision or parts thereof if from adequate investigation conducted by any public agency concerned, it has been determined that in the best interest of the public the site, or parts thereof, is not suitable for development purposes of the kind proposed.
(Ord. No. 133, § 72, 10-2-67)
Sec. 17.5-192. - Residential uses prohibited for certain land.
Land subject to flooding and land deemed to be topographically unsuitable shall not be platted for residential occupancy, nor for such other uses as may increase danger to health, life, or property or aggravate erosion or flood hazard. Such land within the plot shall be set aside for such uses as shall not be endangered by periodic or occasional inundation or shall not produce unsatisfactory living conditions.
(Ord. No. 133, § 73, 10-2-67)
Secs. 17.5-193—17.5-200. - Reserved.
