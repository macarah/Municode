Sec. 6-47. - Casual and isolated activity.
Nothing contained in this article shall be interpreted so as to require any person who may engage in casual or isolated activity and commercial transactions, where such involve personal assets and are not the principal occupation of the individual, to obtain a occupation tax certificate and pay a fee therefor.
(Ord. No. 191, § 20, 11-1-76)
Sec. 6-48. - List of contractors required for permit.
(a)
Any person obtaining a permit for the construction, repair or demolition of any structure shall, at the time of making application for such permit, list the name and address of all contractors and subcontractors who shall perform any service in connection with the construction, repair or demolition of the structure for which such permit is sought. If, at the time of applying for such permit, any of such contractors or subcontractors have not been employed or contracted with, upon such employment or entering into the contract with such contractors or subcontractors, and before the performance of any service by same, such person shall immediately list their names and addresses on the form to be secured from the building inspector and file the same with the building inspector.
(b)
All subtrades, including subcontractors and all other contractors engaged in the construction, repair or demolition of any structure or building shall be required to obtain a occupation tax certificate pursuant to the provisions of this section.
(Ord. No. 191, § 27, 11-1-76; Ord. No. 93-4, § 1, 7-6-93)

All persons shall, prior to operating an amusement device where human beings are conveyed, obtain from the City a permit. Upon requesting a permit, the applicant must show evidence of personal liability insurance in the amount of one hundred thousand dollars ($100,000.00) for each person and three hundred thousand dollars ($300,000.00) for each accident plus ten thousand dollars ($10,000.00) property damages and name the agent for service domiciled in the county.
(Ord. No. 191, § 33, 11-1-76)

Sec. 6-53. - Coin-operated amusement devices.
(a)
Definitions. For purposes of this section, a coin-operated amusement device shall mean any device or machine activated or operated by inserting a coin into the machine, or paying for the use of a machine, which after activation entitles the operator to play a game or engage in a contest of skill using the machine. For purposes of explanation only, machines or devices such as pinball machines, billiard tables and video and computer games, are deemed "coin-operated amusement devices."
(b)
Exclusions. The term coin-operated amusement device shall not include musical jukeboxes, amusement rides or coin-operated vending machines.
(c)
Levy of occupation tax certificate. There is hereby levied on each coin-operated amusement device located in the City and which is made available for use by the general public an annual occupation tax certificate fee as determined by the City Council and listed in the schedule of fees and charges maintained in the City Clerk's office. Such fee is payable in accordance with the provisions of this article and is due on or before the date when an annual occupation tax certificate must be obtained in the City.
(d)
Required display of license. Each coin-operated amusement device licensed by the City shall have displayed on its face the occupation tax certificate herein required in such a fashion that it is clearly visible from the front of the machine.
(e)
Identification. Effective January 1, 1982, and for each year thereafter, each applicant for a occupation tax certificate for a coin-operated amusement device required by this section shall list, on each application for such a occupation tax certificate for the operation of a coin-operated amusement device, a list of all coin-operated amusement devices for which the applicant has obtained a license from the City, together with a statement as to the location of each machine.
(Ord. No. 191-3, § I, 8-4-81; Ord. No. 191-4, § I, 11-2-81; Ord. No. 191-5, § I, 2-1-82; Ord. No. 191-6-A, § I, 3-7-83; Ord. No. 191-9, § I, 3-7-88)
Sec. 6-54. - Vending machines.
(a)
Definitions. For purposes of this section, a "vending machine" shall mean any device or machine activated or operated by inserting a coin into the machine, in return for which the person inserting such coin will receive a product such as, by way of example only, candy, cigarettes, gum, sandwiches and other similar items.
(b)
Levy of annual registration fee. There is hereby levied on every vending machine located in the City, and which is made available for use by the general public, a registration fee in an amount determined by the City Council and listed in the schedule of fees and charges maintained in the City Clerk's office, to be paid in addition to any occupation tax certificate required by the provisions of this article. Such fee is payable in accordance with the provisions of this article and is due on or before the date when an annual occupation tax certificate must be obtained in the City.
(c)
Registration certificate. The registration fee shall be accompanied by a registration certificate filed with the City Clerk, which shall contain the following information:
(1)
The name, address and telephone number of the owner of the machine.
(2)
The location where the machine will be maintained during the year.
(3)
The description of the machine, including the serial number, brand name or manufacturer's name and the type of machine.
(4)
Whether or not the machine is made available generally to the public.
(5)
The names of all persons with access to the interior portions of the machine.
(d)
Exclusions. This registration fee shall not apply to machines owned by charitable or tax-exempt organizations as defined by the United States Internal Revenue Code. This section shall not apply to machines which are owned by the owner of the property upon which the machines are maintained and which are operated as a convenience to patrons patronizing the premises.
(Ord. No. 191-5, § II, 2-1-82)
Sec. 6-55. - Vending machines or amusement devices, identification.
Each coin-operated vending machine or amusement device which is operated, maintained and used within the City shall have conspicuously stamped upon it or affixed thereon for identification purposes the name, address and telephone number, if any, of the owner or operator of the machine at his own expense. In addition, the owner or operator shall comply with all other City, county and state regulations regarding vending machines. Any prohibited use or type of vending machine is not impliedly permitted or authorized in this section.
(Ord. No. 191, § 16, 11-1-76)
Sec. 6-56. - Financial institutions, occupation tax certificate tax.
(a)
Levied. Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93, there is hereby levied for the year 1984, and for each year thereafter, an annual occupation tax certificate tax upon state and national banking associations, federal savings and loan associations and state building and loan associations, at the rate of one-fourth (0.25) percent of the gross receipts of such institutions. "Gross receipts" shall mean "gross receipts" as defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93. Notwithstanding any other provisions of this section, the minimum amount of occupation tax certificate tax due from any depository financial institution pursuant to this section shall be one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) per year.
(b)
Due date; filing of return. Each depository financial institution within the City shall file a return of its gross receipts with the City on March first of the year following the year in which such gross receipts were measured. Such returns shall be in the manner and in the form prescribed by the commissioner of the department of banking and shall be based upon the allocation method set forth in subsection (d) of O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93. The tax levied pursuant to this section shall be assessed and collected based upon the information provided in such return. The due date of taxes levied by this section shall be April 1, 1984, and April first of each subsequent year.
(Ord. No. 191-8, § I, 12-5-83)
State Law reference— Authorization to levy and collect a business license tax from depository financial institutions, O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.
