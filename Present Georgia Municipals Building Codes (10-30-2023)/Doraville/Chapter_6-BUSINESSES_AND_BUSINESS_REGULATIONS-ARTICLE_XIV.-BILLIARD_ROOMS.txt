ARTICLE XIV. - BILLIARD ROOMS

Sec. 6-801. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the following terms shall have the meaning ascribed thereto:
Billiard means any of the several games played on a table surrounded by an elastic ledge of cushions with balls which are impelled by a cue and shall include all forms of the game known as "carom billiards," "pocket billiards," and "English billiards."
Billiard room means any public place where a person is permitted to play the game of billiards.
Governing authority means the Mayor and Council of the City.
(Ord. No. 2009-39, § 1, 12-7-09)
Sec. 6-802. - Hours of operation.
No licensee, owner, or person in charge of a billiard room shall allow the same to remain open between the hours of 2:00 a.m. Saturday and 12:30 p.m. Sunday, and between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 9:00 a.m. on all other days.
(Ord. No. 2009-39, § 1, 12-7-09)
Sec. 6-803. - Minors.
It shall be unlawful for any person licensed for, owning or in charge of a billiard room in the City to allow any person under the age of twenty-one (21) years to participate in billiards, or for any other purpose to enter or remain in a billiard room in which alcoholic beverages are offered for sale, unless accompanied by parent or legal guardian who is at least twenty-one (21) years old.
(Ord. No. 2009-39, § 1, 12-7-09)
Sec. 6-804. - Gambling prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person in charge of a billiard room to permit games of chance, betting or gambling of any kind within such billiard room.
(Ord. No. 2009-39, § 1, 12-7-09)
Sec. 6-805. - View of operations.
All billiard tables shall be and remain in open and unobstructed view of any enforcement official as said official enters the billiard area.
(Ord. No. 2009-39, § 1, 12-7-09)
Sec. 6-806. - Alcoholic beverage license required for service.
Billiard rooms that desire to sell or serve alcoholic beverages must comply with chapter 3 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 2009-39, § 1, 12-7-09)
Secs. 6-807—6-899. - Reserved.
