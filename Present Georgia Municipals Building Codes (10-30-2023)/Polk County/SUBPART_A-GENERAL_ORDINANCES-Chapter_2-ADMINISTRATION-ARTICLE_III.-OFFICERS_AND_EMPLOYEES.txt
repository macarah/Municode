DIVISION 2. - CODE OF ETHICS[1]
Footnotes:

Subdivision I. - In General

Sec. 2-72. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
County official means any person who is an elected county representative.
Employee means any person who is a full-time or part-time employee of the county.
Government or county means Polk County, Georgia government.
Immediate family means the employee or county official, spouse and children of said parties.
(Res. of 1-7-2003, art. A, § 1)
Sec. 2-73. - Acceptance of gifts.
(a)
No county official shall solicit or accept directly or indirectly anything of value from any person, corporation, or group which:
(1)
Has, or is seeking to obtain contractual or other business or financial relationships with the county;
(2)
In exchange for the thing of value seeks to have a county official exercise a matter of discretion in his favor;
(3)
In exchange for the thing of value seeks to have interests which may be affected by the performance or nonperformance of the official duty of the county official.
(b)
This section shall not apply to campaign contributions which shall be reported in accordance with state law.
(Res. of 1-7-2003, art. A, § 2)
Sec. 2-74. - Intent.
It is the intent of this section that county officials avoid any action whether or not specifically prohibited by section 2-73 which might result in, or create the appearance of the following:
(1)
Using public office for private gain;
(2)
Impeding government efficiency or economy;
(3)
Affecting adversely the confidence of the public in the integrity of the government; or
(4)
Purposefully or intentionally violating laws involving moral turpitude as defined by state law.
(Res. of 1-7-2003, art. A, § 3)
Sec. 2-75. - Financial interests of members.
County officials may not:
(1)
Have direct or indirect financial interests that conflict substantially with their responsibilities and duties as government servants; or
(2)
Engage in, directly or indirectly, financial transactions as a result of, or primarily relying upon, information obtained from their office. Aside from these restrictions, county officials are free to engage in lawful financial transactions to the same extent as private citizens.
(Res. of 1-7-2003, art. A, § 4)
Sec. 2-76. - Use of public property.
A county official shall not use county government property of any kind for other than officially approved activities, nor direct employees to use such property for the personal purposes of such official.
(Res. of 1-7-2003, art. A, § 5)
Sec. 2-77. - Use of confidential information.
A county official shall not directly or indirectly, make use of, or permit others to make use of, for the purpose of furthering a private interest, official information not made available to the general public.
(Res. of 1-7-2003, art. A, § 6)
Sec. 2-78. - Coercion by county official.
A county official shall not use his position in any way to coerce, or give the appearance of coercing, another person to provide any financial benefit to him or persons within his immediate family, or those with whom an official has business or financial ties.
(Res. of 1-7-2003, art. A, § 7)
Sec. 2-79. - Voting in matters of personal interest.
A county official shall not vote on an ordinance or amendment in a meeting that would directly affect his private business, business interests or property; nor such interests of his immediate family.
(Res. of 1-7-2003, art. A, § 8)
Sec. 2-80. - Unauthorized purchases.
A county official shall not order any goods and services for the county, nor obligate county funds for any payment, without prior official authorization for such an expenditure.
(Res. of 1-7-2003, art. A, § 9)
Sec. 2-81. - County attorneys used for private business.
A county official shall not use the attorney or attorneys who are under retainer by the county for personal or private business without paying just compensation.
(Res. of 1-7-2003, art. A, § 10)
Sec. 2-82. - Unauthorized use of public employees.
A county official shall not use his superior position to request or require an employee to:
(1)
Do clerical work on behalf of his family, business, social, church or fraternal interests;
(2)
Purchase goods and services to be used for personal, business or political purposes;
(3)
Work for him personally without offering just compensation;
(4)
Perform work allegedly for the benefit of the county without prior official authorization.
(Res. of 1-7-2003, art. A, § 11)
Sec. 2-83. - Reserved.

Sec. 2-84. - Penalties.
The provisions of this division shall be observed by county officials as set forth above and any violation thereof shall subject the offender to disciplinary action which may include censure and either public or private reprimand. Power to administer a greater punishment shall include power to administer the lesser.
(Res. of 1-7-2003, art. A, § 13)

Subdivision II. - Administration

Sec. 2-112. - Ethics committee.
The ethics committee shall consist of three persons, one from each commission district, all appointed by the board of commissioners. All members shall be residents of the county and shall serve a two-year term.
(Res. of 1-7-2003, art. B, § 1; Ord. No. 2018-06, § 1, 4-3-2018)
Sec. 2-113. - Receipt of complaints.
(a)
All ethics complaints shall be filed in writing with the county manager. The county manager shall present written complaints to the ethics committee in a called meeting within ten days after receiving written complaints. A copy of any filed complaint shall be forwarded to the party against whom the complaint is filed.
(b)
Upon receipt of a complaint in proper form, the ethics committee shall review it to determine whether the complaint is unjustified, frivolous, patently unfounded or fails to state facts sufficient to invoke the disciplinary jurisdiction for the county board of commissioners. The ethics committee shall be empowered to subpoena parties, collect evidence and information concerning any complaint and to add the findings and results of its investigations to the file containing such complaint. Upon completion of an investigation of a complaint, files shall be maintained by the county clerk.
(c)
Upon completion of its investigation of a complaint, the ethics committee shall be empowered to dismiss those complaints which are unjustified, frivolous, patently unfounded or which fail to state facts sufficient to invoke the disciplinary jurisdiction of the county board of commissioners; provided, however, that a rejection of such complaint by the ethics committee shall not deprive the complaining party of any action he might otherwise have at law or in equity against the respondent government servant.
(d)
The ethics committee shall be empowered to conduct probable cause investigations, to take evidence and hold hearings.
(e)
The ethics committee shall be empowered to adopt forms for formal complaints, subpoenas, notices, applications for reinstatement and any other written instruments necessary or desirable under these rules.
(f)
Should the committee determine that disciplinary action is warranted, it shall make written findings and recommendations to the county board of commissioners within ten days after concluding their review and investigation of a complaint.
(g)
The official against whom a complaint is filed may present evidence to the ethics committee and/or county commission concerning any alleged complaints.
(Res. of 1-7-2003, art. B, § 2; Ord. No. 2018-06, § 1, 4-3-2018)
Sec. 2-114. - Additional regulations.
This division shall be cumulative to any other ordinance, resolution or act now existing.
(Res. of 1-7-2003, art. B, § 3)
Sec. 2-115. - Right to appeal.
Any final decision by the county commission pursuant to this division shall be reviewable by the superior court of the county. The review by the superior court shall be limited to an inquiry of whether there was any evidence before the board which supported the decision of the board. Provided, however, no action of the board refusing or failing to take action pursuant to this code of ethics shall be reviewable by the superior court.
(Res. of 1-7-2003, art. B, § 4)
Sec. 2-116. - Conflict of interest.
No elected official, appointed officer, or employee of the county or any agency or political entity to which this division applies shall knowingly engage in any business or transaction or have any financial or other personal interest, direct or indirect, which is incompatible with the proper discharge of his official duties or which would tend to impair his independence of judgment or action in the performance of his official duties.
(Res. of 1-7-2003, art. B, § 5)

Subdivision III. - Conflict of Interest and Code of Ethics

Sec. 2-146. - Acceptance of gifts, gratuities, special privileges.
(a)
Employees and county officials shall not accept any gifts, loans, rewards, favors, or services that may reasonably tend to improperly influence them in the discharge of their official duties. This limitation is not intended to prohibit the acceptance of articles of negligible value which are distributed generally not to prohibit loans from regular lending institutions. It is particularly important that employees and officials guard against relationships which might be constructed as, or give the appearance of: favoritism, coercion, unfair advantage or collusion.
(b)
Employees shall not use or attempt to use their position to secure an economic benefit or advantages, special privileges or exemptions for themselves or others including the use of knowledge obtained through their employment or by virtue of their position.
(c)
Employees shall not accept employment or engage in any business or professional activity which they may reasonably expect would require or induce them to disclose confidential information acquired by them by reason of their official position.
(Res. of 1-7-2003, art. C, § 1)
Sec. 2-147. - Proprietorship of creations.
All plans, designs, reports, specifications, drawings, devices, inventions, and other items produced or created by employees during working hours or through the use of county facilities or equipment or at the request of the county shall be and become the sole property of the county.
(Res. of 1-7-2003, art. C, § 2)
Sec. 2-148. - Confidential information.
Employees shall not disclose confidential information gained by reason of their official position, nor shall they otherwise use such information for their personal gain or benefit.
(Res. of 1-7-2003, art. C, § 3)
Sec. 2-149. - Conflict of interest.
(a)
If an employee of the county is an officer, director, agent or member of, or owns interest in any entity which is subject to the regulation of, or which has financial commitments with the county, they shall file a sworn statement to this effect with the county clerk.
(b)
Employees shall not transact any business in their official capacity with any business entity of which he is an officer, director, agent, member, or in which he owns a controlling interest, excluding civic, charitable or religious organizations.
(c)
Employees shall not have personal investment in any enterprise which will create a conflict or a perceived conflict between their private interest and the public interest.
(d)
This prohibition shall extend to employees, county officials and their immediate families.
(Res. of 1-7-2003, art. C, § 4)
