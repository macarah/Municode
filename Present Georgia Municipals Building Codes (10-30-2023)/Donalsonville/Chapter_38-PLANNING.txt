ARTICLE II. - DOWNTOWN DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY[1]

Footnotes:
--- (1) ---
State Law reference— Downtown Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-42-1 et seq.

Sec. 38-19. - Declaration of need.
There is hereby determined and declared a pressing, existing and future need for a downtown development authority to function in the city for the purpose of developing and promoting for the public good and general welfare, trade, commerce, industry and employment opportunities in that geographic area shown on a map thereof on file in the office of the city clerk, called the downtown development area, as that term is defined in the Downtown Development Authorities Law.
(Code 1984, § 8-1-1; Code 1996, § 66-31)
Sec. 38-20. - Activation.
There is hereby activated in the city the public body corporate and politic known as the downtown development authority, which authority shall be comprised, authorized and shall act pursuant to the Downtown Development Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 36-42-1 et seq.
(Code 1984, § 8-1-2; Code 1996, § 66-32)
Sec. 38-21. - Directors.
Each of the persons named as initial directors of the downtown development authority shall serve in such capacity for the number of years designated by the mayor and council, and if at the end of any term of office of any director, a successor therein shall not have been elected, then the director whose term of office shall have expired shall continue to hold office until his successor shall be so elected.
(Code 1984, § 8-1-3; Code 1996, § 66-33)
Sec. 38-22. - Powers and duties; organization.
The board of directors shall organize itself, carry out its duties and responsibilities and exercise its powers and prerogatives in accordance with the terms and provisions of the Downtown Development Authorities Law as it now exists and as it might hereafter be amended or modified.
(Code 1984, § 8-1-4; Code 1996, § 66-34)

ARTICLE III. - PLANNING COMMISSION

Sec. 38-48. - Created.
In order to guide and accomplish a coordinated and harmonious development of the city which will, in accordance with existing and future needs, best promote the public health, safety, morals, order, convenience, prosperity, and the general welfare, as well as efficiency and economy in the process of development, the city planning commission, referred to in this article as the planning commission, is hereby created and established, and the commission shall be organized and empowered as set forth in this article.
(Code 1984, § 8-1-11; Code 1996, § 66-61)
Sec. 38-49. - Composition; appointment and term of members.
The planning commission shall consist of five members, who shall be residents of the city and who shall be appointed by the mayor and council. The terms of the members shall be for four years.
(Code 1984, § 8-1-12; Code 1996, § 66-62)
Sec. 38-50. - Filling of vacancies; removal of members.
Any vacancy in membership on the planning commission shall be filled for the unexpired term by the mayor and council, who shall also have the authority to remove any member for cause, on written charges, after a public hearing.
(Code 1984, § 8-1-13; Code 1996, § 66-63)
Sec. 38-51. - Compensation of members; reimbursement for expenses.
All members of the planning commission shall serve without compensation but may be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in connection with their official duties.
(Code 1984, § 8-1-14; Code 1996, § 66-64)
Sec. 38-52. - Election and term of chairperson.
The planning commission shall elect its chairperson from among its members. The term of the chairperson shall be one year with eligibility for re-election.
(Code 1984, § 8-1-15; Code 1996, § 66-65)
Sec. 38-53. - Appointment of secretary.
The planning commission shall appoint a secretary, who may be an officer or employee of the city.
(Code 1984, § 8-1-16; Code 1996, § 66-66)
Sec. 38-54. - Rules of procedure; times of meetings.
The planning commission shall make its own rules of procedure and determine its times of meeting.
(Code 1984, § 8-1-17; Code 1996, § 66-67)
Sec. 38-55. - Powers and duties.
(a)
It shall be the function and duty of the planning commission to make such careful and comprehensive surveys and studies of existing conditions and probable future developments and to prepare such plans for physical, social and economic growth as will best promote the public health, safety, morals, convenience, prosperity or the general welfare as well as efficiency and economy in the development of its political jurisdiction. In particular, the planning commission shall have the power and duty to:
(1)
Prepare a master plan or parts of such plan for the development of the city.
(2)
Prepare and recommend for adoption to the mayor and council a zoning ordinance or resolution and map for the city.
(3)
Prepare and recommend for adoption to the mayor and council regulations for the subdivision of land within the city, and to administer the subdivision regulations that may be adopted.
(4)
Prepare and recommend for adoption to the mayor and council, plats or an official map showing the exact location of the boundary lines of existing, proposed, extended, widened or narrowed streets, and public open spaces or public building sites, together with regulations to control the erection of buildings or other structures within such lines, within its political jurisdiction or a specified portion thereof.
(b)
The planning commission may make, publish and distribute maps, plans and reports and recommendations relating to the plan and development of the city to public officials and agencies, public utility companies, civic, educational, professional and other organizations and citizens. It may recommend to the executive or legislative officials of the city programs for public improvements and the financing thereof. All public officials shall, upon request, furnish to the planning commission, within a reasonable time, such available information as it may require for its work. The planning commission, its members and employees, in the performance of its functions, may enter upon any land, make examinations and surveys, and place and maintain necessary monuments and marks; provided, however, that the planning commission shall be liable for any injury or damage to property resulting therefrom. In general, the planning commission shall have such powers as may be necessary to enable it to perform its functions and promote the planning of its political jurisdiction.
(Code 1984, § 8-1-18; Code 1996, § 66-68)
Sec. 38-56. - Expenditures.
The expenditures of the planning commission, exclusive of gifts, shall be within amounts appropriated for the purpose by the mayor and council.
(Code 1984, § 8-1-19; Code 1996, § 66-69)
Sec. 38-57. - Employees and staff; contracts for services.
The planning commission may appoint such employees and staff as it may deem necessary for its work and may contract with the state planning agency and city planners and other consultants for such services as it may require.
(Code 1984, § 8-1-20; Code 1996, § 66-70)
Sec. 38-58. - Meetings at which official action taken open to public.
All meetings of the planning commission at which official action is taken shall be open to the public.
(Code 1984, § 8-1-21; Code 1996, § 66-71)
Sec. 38-59. - Records to be public.
The records of the planning commission shall be a public record.
(Code 1984, § 8-1-22; Code 1996, § 66-72)
