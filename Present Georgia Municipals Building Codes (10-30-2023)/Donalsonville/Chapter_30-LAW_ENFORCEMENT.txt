Chapter 30 - LAW ENFORCEMENT
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 30-1. - Towing and storage of vehicles.
The city adopts Policy 31 of the county sheriff's office, "Wrecker Qualification/Rotation Lists" and the "Georgia Department of Public Safety Maximum Rate Tariff for Nonconsensual Town Services," both as same may be amended from time to time. The police department shall utilize the rotation list and impose fees pursuant to these policies and documents. Nothing in this section shall prevent the owner or operator of the vehicle from securing towing and storage services from the towing service of their choosing with applicable rates to be determined between the towing service and the owner or operator of the vehicle.
(Ord. No. 04-02-19, 5-7-2019)
Secs. 30-2—30-18. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - POLICE DEPARTMENT

Sec. 30-19. - Chief of police; appointment.
The police department shall be headed by a chief of police appointed by the mayor and council from time to time.
(Code 1996, § 54-31)
Sec. 30-20. - Composition.
The police department shall consist of those officers and employees of those ranks and positions as approved by the mayor and council.
(Code 1984, § 4-1-1; Code 1996, § 54-32)
Sec. 30-21. - Responsibilities.
The police department shall be responsible for patrol, traffic control, investigation of accidents, investigation of crimes, apprehension of offenders, court appearances, security of business establishments and for any other matters of public safety and law enforcement as directed by the mayor and council.
(Code 1984, § 4-1-2; Code 1996, § 54-33)
Sec. 30-22. - Interfering with equipment.
No person other than a duly authorized member of the police department shall ride in any vehicle belonging to the police department, nor use, borrow, or otherwise interfere with any equipment of the police department unless authorized to do so by the chief of police.
(Code 1996, § 54-34)
