ARTICLE IX. - YARD SALES

Sec. 10-386. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Goods are meant to include any goods, warehouse merchandise or other property capable of being the object of a sale regulated in this article.
Location means any permanent structure used as a domicile with a permanent street address.
Yard sale means and includes all sales entitled "yard sale," "garage sale," "attic sale," "rummage sale," "porch sale," "flea market sale," or any similar casual sale of tangible personal property which is advertised by any means whereby the public at-large is or can be made aware of said sale.
(Code 2002, ยง 10-251; Ord. of 7-8-2002)
Sec. 10-387. - Permit.
(a)
Required. It shall be unlawful for any person to conduct a yard sale in the city without first filing with the city clerk the information hereinafter specified and obtaining from such clerk a license to do so, to be known as a "yard sale permit."
(b)
Fees. The fee for such permit shall $5.00 per day of sales.
(c)
Term. Such permit shall not be issued to any one person or location in excess of six times within any 12-month period, nor for more than two consecutive calendar days. No permit shall be issued to the same person or location less than two weeks following a previously issued permit to the same person or location.
(d)
Hours. It shall be unlawful for any person to conduct a yard sale before 8:00 a.m. or after 6:00 p.m. of any given day.
(e)
Displayed. Each permit issued under this article must be prominently displayed on the premises upon which the yard sale is conducted throughout the entire period of the permitted sale.
(Code 2002, ยง 10-252; Ord. of 7-8-2002)
Sec. 10-388. - Information to be filed.
The information to be filed with the clerk, pursuant to this article, shall be:
(1)
Name, address and telephone number of person, firm, group, corporation, association, or organization conducting said sale.
(2)
Location at which the sale is to be conducted.
(3)
Name of owner of the property upon which said sale is to be conducted, and written consent of owner if applicant is other than the owner.
(4)
Number and dates of days of sale.
(5)
Date of any past sale.
(6)
Whether or not applicant has been issued any other vendor's license by any local, state or federal agency.
(Code 2002, ยง 10-253; Ord. of 7-8-2002)
Sec. 10-389. - Civil penalty.
Any person conducting a yard sale without having been issued a permit for same, or who shall violate any of the other terms and regulations of this article, shall, upon conviction, be fined not less than $25.00 nor more than $100.00 or be imprisoned for a period not to exceed ten days for each violation.
