ARTICLE II. - FLEA MARKETS[2]

Sec. 10-19. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Flea market means the sale by multiple parties on an irregular basis of items retail within a stall.
Licensee means the person receiving the general license for a flea market. The term "licensee" means such person shall be directly responsible for subletting booths to individual vendors.
Stall means a space of not more than eight by ten feet clearly defined by rails or other boundaries.
(Code 2002, § 10-121; Ord. of 7-8-1985, § 32-215(1))
Sec. 10-20. - License.
(a)
Required. No person shall operate a flea market without a city license.
(b)
Fees. Annual license fees for operation of a flea market shall consist of:
(1)
A base fee; and
(2)
Additional fees per stall for all stalls available for use.
Such fees shall be as established from time to time by the mayor and council.
(c)
Payment of fee. This license fee shall be paid annually or at such other time as the mayor and council may direct.
(d)
Need for additional license. Any vendor who operates a stall in a flea market for 20 days out of a 28-day period of time shall be considered a regular business and not a vendor, and shall be required to obtain a regular separate business license from the city.
(Code 2002, § 10-122; Ord. of 7-8-1985, § 32-215(2), (5))
Sec. 10-21. - Enclosed; fence and stall.
All flea markets shall be conducted behind a privacy fence of a height not less than five feet which prohibits a view of the sale area. Each vendor operating as a part of the flea market shall operate within a stall.
(Code 2002, § 10-123; Ord. of 7-8-1985, § 32-215(3))
Sec. 10-22. - Signs.
No signs or other products shall be displayed on any street, right-of-way, sidewalk or other city property in connection with the operation of a flea market.
(Code 2002, § 10-124; Ord. of 7-8-1985, § 32-215(4))
