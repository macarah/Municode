Sec. 12-160. - Massage therapy definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Board means the state board of massage therapy established under O.C.G.A. § 43-24A-1 et seq.
License means a valid and current certificate of registration issued by the state board of massage therapy as provided by O.C.G.A. § 43-24A-1 et seq.
Massage and/or massage therapy means the application of a system of structured touch, pressure, movement, and holding to the soft tissue of the body in which the primary intent is to enhance or restore health and well-being. The term "massage" or "massage therapy" includes complementary methods, including without limitation the external application of water, superficial heat, superficial cold, lubricants, salt scrubs, or other topical preparations and the use of commercially available electromechanical devices which do not require the use of transcutaneous electrodes and which mimic or enhance the actions possible by the hands; the term "massage" or "massage therapy" also includes determining whether massage therapy is appropriate or contraindicated, or whether referral to another health care provider is appropriate. The term "massage therapy" shall not include the use of ultrasound, fluidotherapy, laser, and other methods of deep thermal modalities.
Massage therapist means a person who administers massage or massage therapy for compensation.
Massage therapy assistant means any person who does not qualify as a massage therapist as that term is defined in this section, but who assists a massage therapist and who works under the direct supervision of a licensed massage therapist.
(Ord. No. 06-02, § (a)(22-122), 6-12-2006)
Sec. 12-161. - Practice by person not licensed; public nuisance.
The practice of massage therapy is declared to be an activity affecting the public interest and involving the health, safety, and welfare of the public. Such practice by a person who is not licensed to practice in the state is declared to be a public nuisance, harmful to the public health, safety, and welfare.
(Ord. No. 06-02, § (a)(22-123), 6-12-2006)
Sec. 12-162. - State license.
The city recognizes that the state has not formed a state board at this time for massage therapists to apply for a license in the state. Consequently, all massage therapy shall be prohibited within the city limits until such state board is formed as provided by O.C.G.A. § 43-24A-1 et seq. Any and all massage and/or massage therapy clinics and/or parlors existing within the city limits at this time shall apply for a state license as provided by O.C.G.A. § 43-24A-1 et seq., as soon as the state board is formed.
(Ord. No. 06-02, § (a)(22-124), 6-12-2006)
Sec. 12-163. - Fines and punishment.
Any person who practices massage therapy without a valid license in violation of O.C.G.A. § 43-24A-1 et seq., upon being convicted, shall be punished by a fine of not more than $500.00 for each offense or with imprisonment for not more than 180 days, or both. Upon being convicted a second or subsequent time under this section, such person shall be punished by a fine of not more than $1,000.00 for each offense, imprisonment for not more than six months, or both.
(Ord. No. 06-02, § (a)(22-124), 6-12-2006)
