ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 38-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Commercial vehicle means every vehicle designed, maintained or used primarily for the transportation of property.
Curb loading zone means a space adjacent to a curb reserved for the exclusive use of vehicles during the loading or unloading of passengers or materials.
Established place of business means the place actually occupied either continuously or at regular periods by a dealer or manufacturer where his books and records are kept and a large share of his business is transacted.
Farm tractor means every motor vehicle designed and used primarily as a farm implement, for drawing plows, mowing machines and other implements of husbandry.
Freight curb loading zone means a space adjacent to a curb for the exclusive use of vehicles during the loading or unloading of freight or passengers.
Impervious surface means those areas which prevent or impede the infiltration of stormwater into the soil as it entered in natural conditions prior to development. Common impervious areas for the purpose of this chapter shall mean driveways, parking lots, compacted surfaces and any surface made from asphalt or concrete.
Official time standard, whenever certain hours are named in this chapter, means standard time or daylight saving time, as may be in current use in the city.
Passenger curb loading zone means a place adjacent to a curb reserved for the exclusive use of vehicles during the loading and unloading of passengers.
Registration means the registration certificate and registration plates issued under the laws of the state pertaining to the registration of vehicles.
Road tractor means every motor vehicle designed and used for drawing other vehicles and not so constructed as to carry any load thereon independently or any part of the weight of a vehicle or load so drawn.
Traffic control device means any device, whether manually, electrically or mechanically operated, by which traffic is alternately directed to stop and permitted to proceed.
Traffic division means the traffic division of the police department or, if a traffic division is not established, the police department.
(Code 1972, § 17-1(6), (8), (11), (13), (15), (25), (30), (40), (43), (56), (57); Code 1994, § 98-1; Ord. No. 2018-06, § 1, 12-10-2018)
Sec. 38-2. - Adoption of state law; penalty for violation.
(a)
O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-1—40-6-395, known as the Uniform Rules of the Road, and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 are adopted as and for the traffic rules of the city with like effect as if recited in this chapter.
(b)
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any section of this chapter shall be punished by a fine of not more than $500.00 or by imprisonment of not more than 60 days, or both such fine and imprisonment.
(Code 1994, § 98-2; Ord. of 9-13-1983(1))
Sec. 38-3. - Police administration.
Traffic control in the city shall be the direct responsibility of the chief of police and those officers employed in the police department assigned to duty in traffic control. In addition to any other duty imposed upon the police department in this Code, the department and the officers therein shall have the duty to:
(1)
Enforce the street traffic rules of the city and of the state;
(2)
Make arrests for traffic violations;
(3)
Investigate accidents;
(4)
Issue summons when justified;
(5)
Cooperate with other officers and officials of the city and the mayor and council in the administration of traffic laws and in developing ways and means to improve traffic conditions; and
(6)
Carry out those duties imposed upon the department and the officers therein by this chapter.
(Code 1972, § 17-2; Code 1994, § 98-3)
Sec. 38-4. - Traffic engineering.
The city may, from time to time, require traffic engineering assistance in planning and studying traffic problems. The mayor and council may, when the need arises, contract for such services with a person offering such or may enter into a contract with the county or another municipality to provide such services.
(Code 1972, § 17-3; Code 1994, § 98-4)
Sec. 38-5. - Records of traffic violations.
The police department shall keep a record of all violations of this chapter and of the state vehicle laws of which any person has been charged, together with a record of the final disposition of all such alleged offenses. These records shall be filed alphabetically under the name of the person charged and shall be permanent records and public records.
(Code 1972, § 17-4; Code 1994, § 98-5)
Sec. 38-6. - Investigation of accidents.
It shall be the duty of the police department to investigate traffic accidents, to arrest and to assist in the prosecution of those persons charged with violations of law causing or contributing to such accidents.
(Code 1972, § 17-5; Code 1994, § 98-6)
Sec. 38-7. - Records of traffic accidents.
The police department shall maintain a suitable system of filing traffic accident reports. These records shall be permanent and public records.
(Code 1972, § 17-6; Code 1994, § 98-7)
Sec. 38-8. - Traffic accident studies.
Whenever the traffic accidents at any particular location become numerous, the chief of police shall make a study of such location and such accidents and make a recommendation to the mayor and council for remedial measures.
(Code 1972, § 17-7; Code 1994, § 98-8)
Sec. 38-9. - Annual traffic safety report.
Between January 15 and January 30 of each year, the chief of police shall prepare a traffic report which shall be filed with the mayor. Such report shall contain the following information:
(1)
The number of charges made for each category of traffic offense.
(2)
The number of traffic accidents investigated, the number of persons killed and the number of persons injured.
(3)
The recommendations of the police department to improve traffic safety.
(Code 1972, § 17-8; Code 1994, § 98-9)
Sec. 38-10. - Authority of police and fire personnel.
Officers of the police department are authorized to direct all traffic by voice, hand or signal in conformance with traffic laws, provided that, during a fire, an accident or other emergency or to expedite traffic or to safeguard pedestrians, such officers may direct traffic as conditions may require. Personnel of the fire department, when at the scene of a fire, may direct or assist the police in directing traffic thereat or in the immediate vicinity.
(Code 1972, § 17-9; Code 1994, § 98-10)
Sec. 38-11. - Reports to state department of public safety and other state agencies.
It shall be the duty of the chief of police to see that timely reports are filed with the state department of public safety and other agencies of the state when required by law.
(Code 1972, § 17-10; Code 1994, § 98-11)
Sec. 38-12. - Coasters, roller skates and similar devices.
No person upon roller skates or riding in or by means of any coaster, toy vehicle or similar device shall go upon any roadway, except while crossing a street on a crosswalk. When so crossing such person shall be granted all of the rights and shall be subject to all of the duties applicable to pedestrians.
(Code 1972, § 17-13; Code 1994, § 98-12)
Sec. 38-13. - Crosswalks and safety zones.
The chief of police, with the approval of the mayor and council, is authorized to:
(1)
Designate and maintain, by appropriate devices, marks or lines upon the surface of the roadway, crosswalks at intersections and elsewhere where in his opinion there is a danger to pedestrians crossing the roadway.
(2)
Establish safety zones of such kind and character and at such places as he may deem necessary for the protection of pedestrians.
(Code 1972, § 17-15; Code 1994, § 98-13)
Sec. 38-14. - Traffic lanes.
The chief of police, with approval of the mayor and council, is authorized to mark traffic lanes upon the roadway of any street or highway where a regular alignment of traffic is necessary.
(Code 1972, § 17-16; Code 1994, § 98-14)
Sec. 38-15. - Traffic control devices.
(a)
Authority to install. The chief of police, with the approval of the mayor and council, shall place and maintain official traffic control devices when and as required under this chapter to make this chapter effective and may place and maintain such additional official traffic control devices as he may deem necessary to regulate, warn or guide traffic under this chapter or state law.
(b)
Specifications. All traffic control signs, signals and devices shall conform to the specifications contained in the Georgia Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices for Streets and Highways, as revised. All signs and signals required for a particular purpose shall so far as practicable be uniform as to type and location throughout the city. All traffic control devices so erected and not inconsistent with state law or this Code shall be official traffic control devices.
(Code 1972, § 17-17(A), (B); Code 1994, § 98-15)
Sec. 38-16. - Stopping, standing or parking.
(a)
The provisions of O.C.G.A. tit. 40, art. 10, concerning stopping, standing or parking restrictions, are hereby incorporated herein.
(b)
In addition to the parking, standing and stopping prohibitions incorporated in subsection (a) of this section, it shall be unlawful for any person to stop, stand or park a vehicle:
(1)
On a crosswalk or within ten feet thereof.
(2)
Within any area marked as a coach or bus stop.
(3)
On the roadway side of any other vehicle stopped or parked at the edge or curb of a street.
(4)
In any area along a street where the curb has been painted yellow, provided that such painting is authorized by the mayor and council.
(5)
Along any street whereby the nature of the street as to width, grade or curvature would impede traffic and create conditions dangerous to life, limb and property.
(6)
For the purpose of washing, greasing or repairing a vehicle except for the purpose of making emergency repairs. In such an emergency, such vehicle shall be moved or towed away with dispatch unless the vehicle has been involved in an accident in which event the vehicle shall not be moved except at the direction of an officer.
(c)
When any police officer finds a vehicle standing, stopped or parked in violation of this section, such officer is authorized to move such vehicle or require the owner, driver or person in charge of the vehicle to move the vehicle to a safe and lawful point.
(d)
When any police officer finds a vehicle unattended and standing, stopped or parked in violation of this section, such officer is authorized to see to the removal of such vehicle to a lawful and safe place.
(e)
The removal or moving of a vehicle under subsections (c) and (d) of this section shall not prevent or preclude prosecution for violating this section.
(f)
Whenever any motor vehicle without a driver is found standing, stopped or parked in violation of any of the restrictions imposed by the city or by state law, the officer finding such vehicle shall take its registration number and may take any other information displayed on the vehicle which may identify its user and shall conspicuously affix to such vehicle a traffic citation on a form provided by the city for the driver to answer to the charge against him at a specified time and a specified place. In any prosecution charging a violation of any law or restriction governing the standing or parking of a vehicle, proof that the vehicle described in the complaint was parked in violation of any such law or restriction, together with proof that the defendant named in the complaint was at the time of such parking the registered owner of the vehicle, shall constitute in evidence a prima facie presumption that the registered owner of the vehicle was the person who parked or placed the vehicle at the point where and for the time during which the violation occurred.
(g)
The mayor and council may by resolution provide for the establishment, installation and maintenance of parking meters in the city.
(h)
Specific street parking restrictions.
(1)
Except as otherwise provided in this section and in section 38-18, it shall be unlawful for any person to park any vehicle on any street; only off-street parking shall be permitted.
(2)
It shall be unlawful for any person, including but not limited to the owner or user of any vehicle described herein or anyone owning, renting, leasing, or in possession of property described herein, to allow any vehicle on any property having frontage along a residential street to be parked on any surface other than an impervious surface under the entire base of the vehicle.
(3)
Temporary parking.
a.
Notwithstanding subsection (h)(1) and (h)(2) of this section, this section shall not prohibit any nonresident from parking his vehicle on a residential street during temporary visits to a residence which has all available impervious surface or gravel surface parking spaces utilized by other vehicles. For the purpose of this section, a "temporary visit" shall mean no more than six continuous hours at a time.
b.
Notwithstanding subsection (h)(3)a. of this section, it shall be unlawful for any person to park any vehicle on any street in such a manner as to reduce the width of the navigable portion of the street to less than 14 feet.
(4)
For the purposes of this section, the term "residential street" shall include the following areas: Forest Hill Court, Laurel Circle, High Point Court, Laurel Street School Lane, Woodland Drive, Ahyoka Drive, Wahsega Way, Trahlyta Terr., Tsali Trail, Sanders Drive, Ellington Street, Sanders Way, Helen Street, Eubanks Drive, Jones Court, Grove Court, Orchard Place, Lakeland Circle, Dellwood Circle, Huron Drive, Erie Place, Ontario Avenue.
(i)
Violations of this section shall be punishable pursuant to section 1-8.
(Code 1972, § 17-19(A), (B)5, 6, 13, 15—18, (C)—(G); Code 1994, § 98-16; Ord. No. 01-01, § 1(98-16), 3-12-2001; Ord. No. 03-09, § 1(98-16), 6-9-2003; Ord. No. 2018-06, § 2, 12-10-2018)
Sec. 38-17. - Stop signs and yield right-of-way signs.
(a)
The mayor and council may by resolution specifically designate certain streets or parts of streets as through streets. When such is done it shall be the duty of the chief of police to see to the placement and maintenance of a stop sign or a yield right-of-way sign at appropriate places at each intersection of such through street or part thereof.
(b)
The mayor and council may by resolution direct the chief of police to see to the placement and maintenance of a stop sign or a yield right-of-way sign at any intersection in the city where it is determined that a hazardous condition exists and such sign is necessary to prevent loss of life, limb or property.
(Code 1972, § 17-20(A), (B); Code 1994, § 98-17)
Sec. 38-18. - Large vehicles.
(a)
Parking in residential areas.
(1)
It shall be unlawful for any person to park or store on property having a residential zoning classification, any large vehicle, in excess of 10,000 pounds, gross vehicle weight rating (GVWR), provided that a person may temporarily park such a vehicle in a residential area while actually engaged in delivery of products or services, including loading and unloading operations, while the driver is on duty and remains with the vehicle. For the purposes of this section, the term "large vehicle" means tractors, trailers, cabs, trucks, busses, vehicle carriers, tow trucks, towing vehicles, and truck cabs, but shall not include pickup trucks with no special equipment and which are used for personal transportation only.
(2)
It shall be unlawful for any person to store in a residential area any parts of the vehicles described in subsection (a)(1) of this section or their accessories, including materials hauled by the vehicles, unless such storage has been approved by the mayor and council.
(3)
Recreational vehicles, when parked in the rear yard on an impervious surface (e.g., concrete, asphalt) or a gravel surface permitted under section 38-16(h), are specifically excluded from the prohibition contained in this subsection.
(4)
For the purposes of this subsection, the term "recreational vehicles" means vehicles that are used primarily for recreational purposes, are not used primarily for transportation, and are not used for commercial purposes.
(b)
Moving across curb. No large vehicle, cab, trailer, truck or tractor trailer, as designated in subsection (a) of this section, shall be pulled, pushed or in any way moved across any curb in the city without prior permission from the police department.
(c)
Servicing. It shall be unlawful to service a vehicle, as designated in subsections (a) and (b) of this section, except for emergency service as may be rendered upon permission of the police department.
(Code 1994, § 98-18; Ord. of 8-12-1980(1), § 17-26; Ord. No. 01-01, § 2(98-18), 3-12-2001)
Sec. 38-19. - Off-road vehicles.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 40-7-5, the following shall apply within the city:
(1)
No off-road vehicle shall be operated in an area zoned for residential use.
(2)
Any area used for off-road vehicles must first be established for this use and approved by the mayor and council.
(3)
No off-road vehicle shall be operated in an area adjacent to a zoned residential area, if the area of operation is within 1,000 feet of the zoned residential area.
(4)
In areas where off-road vehicles are allowed to operate, the hours of operation shall be restricted to the hours between 10:00 a.m. and 7:00 p.m.
(Code 1994, § 98-19; Ord. of 5-22-1979)
State Law reference— Off-Road Vehicle Act of 1975, O.C.G.A. § 40-7-1 et seq.; enforcement and penalties, O.C.G.A. § 40-7-6.
Sec. 38-20. - Use of traffic control signal monitoring devices; penalty.
(a)
The city elects to use traffic control signal monitoring devices within the jurisdictional limits of the city as provided by O.C.G.A. § 40-6-20(f).
(b)
The driver of a motor vehicle shall be liable for a civil monetary penalty of not more than $70.00 per violation if such motor vehicle is found, as evidenced by recorded images produced by a traffic control signal monitoring device, to have been operated within the city limits in disregard or disobedience of a circular red or red arrow signal in violation of O.C.G.A. § 40-6-20(f).
(c)
The police department shall send by first-class mail addressed to the owner of the motor vehicle postmarked not later than ten days after the date of the alleged violation:
(1)
A citation for the violation, which shall include the date and time of the violation, the location of the intersection, the amount of the civil monetary penalty imposed, and the date by which the civil monetary penalty shall be paid;
(2)
A copy of the recorded image;
(3)
A copy of a certificate sworn to or affirmed by a trained law enforcement officer or a technician employed by a law enforcement agency for which such device is authorized and stating that, based upon inspection of recorded images, the owner's motor vehicle was operated in disregard or disobedience of a circular red or red arrow signal in violation of subsection (a) of this section and that such disregard or disobedience was not otherwise authorized by law;
(4)
A statement of the inference provided by O.C.G.A. § 40-6-20(d) which is herewith incorporated by reference and of the means specified therein by which such inference may be rebutted;
(5)
Information advising the owner of the motor vehicle of the manner and time in which liability as alleged in the citation may be contested in court; and
(6)
Warning that failure to pay the civil monetary penalty or to contest liability in a timely manner shall waive any right to contest liability and result in a civil monetary penalty;
however, only warning notices and not citations for violations shall be sent during the 30-day period commencing with the installation of a traffic control signal monitoring device at such location.
(Ord. No. 06-04, § (a)(98-20), 5-8-2006)
Sec. 38-21. - Signage required for use of traffic control signal monitoring devices.
(a)
Prior to placing any traffic control signal monitoring devices in use, the public works department shall erect a sign on every highway that comprises a part of the state highway system at that point on the highway which intersects the jurisdictional limits of the city.
(b)
The department of public works shall also erect a sign on each public road on the approach to the next traffic control signal for such road when a traffic control device is monitoring such next signal for such road, subject to inspection.
(c)
Such signs shall be at least 30 inches by 30 inches in measurement and shall warn motorists that traffic control signal monitoring devices are being employed.
(d)
The city project manager shall inspect and certify the placement of all required signage before placing any traffic control signal monitoring devices in use and shall periodically inspect to ensure that the required signage has not been removed, modified or otherwise rendered ineffective. It shall be the duty of the city project manager to replace and re-erect such signage as required by law or this chapter during all times the traffic control signal monitoring device program is in effect within the city.
(Ord. No. 06-04, § (a)(98-21), 5-8-2006)
Sec. 38-22. - Traffic control signal monitoring program.
(a)
The city project manager shall operate a traffic control signal monitoring program using approved traffic control signal monitoring devices in strict accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-20(f) and 40-6-20 through 40-14-24. All definitions of terms used therein are expressly incorporated by reference for the purposes of this chapter.
(b)
The timing of any traffic control signal which is being monitored by a traffic control signal monitoring device shall conform to regulations promulgated by the state department of transportation pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 32-6-50. The city project manager shall cause each traffic control signal monitoring device placed in operation within the city to be independently tested for accuracy by a qualified technician, other than its manufacturer, at regular intervals and record and maintain the results of each test. The test results log shall be a public record subject to inspection.
(Ord. No. 06-04, § (a)(98-22), 5-8-2006)
Sec. 38-23. - Reporting of traffic control signal monitoring device use.
The city project manager or his designee shall submit not later than February 1 of each year a report on traffic control signal monitoring device use during the preceding calendar year to the governor, the lieutenant governor, the speaker of the house of representatives, the board of commissioners, the city manager, and the judge of the municipal court. Such report shall include:
(1)
A description of the locations where the traffic control signal monitoring devices were used;
(2)
The number of violations recorded at each location and in the aggregate on a monthly basis;
(3)
The total number of citations issued;
(4)
The number of civil monetary penalties and total amount of such penalties paid after citation without contest;
(5)
The number of violations adjudicated and results of such adjudications, including a breakdown of dispositions made;
(6)
The total amount of civil monetary penalties paid; and
(7)
The quality of the civil adjudication process and its results.
(Ord. No. 06-04, § (a)(98-23), 5-8-2006)
Sec. 38-24. - Designation of city project manager.
For purposes of administering this chapter and any traffic control signal monitoring program operated in accordance herewith, the chief of police is hereby designated as the city project manager.
(Ord. No. 06-04, § (a)(98-24), 5-8-2006)
Sec. 38-25. - Parking of commercial vehicle prohibited.
(a)
Except as otherwise expressly permitted in this Code, in all commercial or office-institutional zoning districts, it is unlawful for any person to permit or cause any commercial vehicle as defined in this chapter to be parked or stored in a commercial or office-institutional districts unless:
(1)
The vehicle is engaging in leading or unloading activity where the driver is present and in charge thereof;
(2)
The vehicle is owned or is being used by a business located on the property; or
(3)
A business on the property is conducting operations and the vehicle is being used in connection with such activity.
(b)
Where a commercial vehicle is parked in a parking lot located in a commercial or office-institutional zoning district pursuant to subsection (a) herein, such vehicle shall park only in areas designated and posted as loading zones and/or loading docks. The use of loading zones or loading docks by operators of commercial vehicles for sleeping or parking overnight is strictly prohibited.
(c)
The provisions herein shall not apply to truck rental, leasing, or sale facilities operating and conducting business pursuant to a valid business license. Commercial vehicles may be parked overnight or for storage purposes only in warehouse and industrial zoning districts within the city. Parking on vacant commercial property is prohibited unless authorized pursuant to a permit issued by the city.
(d)
Parking in hotel or motel lots.
(1)
Commercial vehicles may be parked in the parking lots of hotels and motels provided that the operators of said vehicles are registered guests of the hotel or motel, staying overnight and provided that said vehicles are parked within the lines of a designated parking space clearly marked for trucks.
(2)
Where said commercial vehicle is a tractor trailer, only the cab of the tractor trailer may be parked in a hotel or motel parking lot in accordance with this subsection.
(3)
Any hotel or motel providing designated parking areas for the parking of commercial vehicles pursuant to this subsection is prohibited from acting as a routing agents for said vehicles.
(e)
The owner or person in possession of any real property or the vehicle operator, or both, may be punished as provided in section 1-8 of this Code for a violation of this section.
(f)
After giving proper notice of violation and five business days thereafter to correct the deficiencies, the city shall have the authority to enter the property and bring it into compliance with these regulations by impounding any vehicle found parked on the property in violation of this section. The expense of such city action shall be charged against the property owner, become a lien on the property, and be collected by the same means, process and manner by which taxes are collected by the city. The impounded vehicle may be reclaimed by the vehicle's owner who shall be responsible for payment of the towing and storage fees, otherwise the vehicle shall be disposed of in accordance with state law.
(Ord. No. 2019-03, § 1, 12-9-2019)

ARTICLE II. - DISPLAYING MOTOR VEHICLES FOR SALE

Sec. 38-52. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Display for sale means to display or affix any sign, marking or other device on or to a motor vehicle in such manner as to be visible to passing motorists or pedestrians and which indicates the vehicle is available for alienation.
Motor vehicle means every vehicle which is self-propelled. Boats and trailers will be included in this category.
Vehicle sales lot means an area maintained by a business, licensed for the sale of new or used motor vehicles, and upon which such vehicles are parked for display in conjunction with such business.
(Code 1994, § 98-41; Ord. of 11-8-1988, § 1(A))
Sec. 38-53. - Exemptions.
This article shall not apply to the parking of motor vehicles on vehicle sales lots.
(Code 1994, § 98-42; Ord. of 11-8-1988, § 1(E))
Sec. 38-54. - Parking on private property.
No person shall park any motor vehicle on private property in such a manner as to display it for sale, except in accordance with the following:
(1)
The vehicle owner or his agent shall obtain a permit from the city clerk for display of each vehicle. The clerk shall charge such fee as fixed from time to time by the mayor and council for issuance of a permit.
(2)
No person may display for sale more than one motor vehicle owned by him at one time.
(3)
No person may display for sale more than three motor vehicles owned by him within any period of 12 consecutive months.
(4)
No vehicle owner may display a motor vehicle for sale on property owned by another without the written consent of the property owner, and no property shall be used to display more than three motor vehicles for sale in any period of 12 consecutive months.
(Code 1994, § 98-43; Ord. of 11-8-1988, § 1(B))
Sec. 38-55. - Parking on public property.
(a)
Except as provided in subsection (b) of this section, no person shall park any motor vehicle on public property in such a manner as to display it for sale.
(b)
Nothing in this section shall prohibit the use or parking of a vehicle in conjunction with legitimate entertainment or shopping so long as the vehicle is lawfully parked in a parking space or lot available for such use and the duration of such parking does not exceed the time devoted to such entertainment or shopping.
(Code 1994, § 98-44; Ord. of 11-8-1988, § 1(C), (D))
Secs. 38-56—38-83. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - SPEED LIMITS

Sec. 38-84. - Maximum speed.
Unless otherwise posted, the maximum speed that an automobile, truck, tractor, motorcycle or other motor-driven vehicle may be driven or operated on any street, highway or thoroughfare in the corporate limits shall be 30 miles per hour. However, the mayor and council may from time to time establish greater or lesser limits in school zones, in areas of congestion and in areas where the mayor and council deem the operation at 30 miles per hour to be dangerous to life, limb and property or in areas where a greater speed is authorized as a result of traffic and road conditions. When such greater or lesser limits are established, the mayor and council shall adopt a resolution to that effect and shall cause signs to be erected warning the public of the differing speed restriction.
(Code 1972, § 17-18; Code 1994, § 98-66)
