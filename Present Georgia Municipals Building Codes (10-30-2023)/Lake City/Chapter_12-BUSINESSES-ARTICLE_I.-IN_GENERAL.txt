ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 12-1. - Coin-operated devices and vending machines.
Each coin-operated device and vending machine shall have affixed thereon a stamp or decal provided and affixed by the city to indicate that the business license tax for the current year has been paid. It shall be unlawful for any person to offer such device or machine for operation if such current stamp is not affixed thereto.
(Code 1972, § 16-24; Code 1994, § 22-1)
Sec. 12-2. - Telephone company occupational license tax.
(a)
As used in this section, the term "actual recurring local service revenues" means those revenues customarily included in the Uniform System of Accounts as prescribed by the Federal Communications Commission for Class "A" and "B" companies; provided, however, that only the local service portion of the following accounts shall be included:
(1)
Basic local service revenue, as defined in 47 CFR 32.5000;
(2)
Basic area revenue, as defined in 47 CFR 32.5001;
(3)
Optional extended area revenue, as defined in 47 CFR 32.5002;
(4)
Public telephone revenue, as defined in 47 CFR 32.5010;
(5)
Local private line revenue, as defined in 47 CFR 35.5040; provided, however, that the portion of such accounts attributable to audio and video program transmission service where both terminals of the private line are within the corporate limits of the municipal authority shall not be included;
(6)
Other local exchange revenue, as defined in 47 CFR 32.5060;
(7)
Local exchange service, as defined in 47 CFR 32.5069;
(8)
Network access revenue, as defined in 47 CFR 32.5080;
(9)
Directory revenue, as defined in 47 CFR 32.5320; provided, however, that the portion of such accounts attributable to revenue derived from listings in portion of directories not considered white pages shall not be included;
(10)
Nonregulated operating revenue, as defined in 47 CFR 32.5280; provided, however, that the portion of such accounts attributable to revenues derived from private lines shall not be included; and
(11)
Uncollectible revenue, as defined in 47 CFR 32.5300.
(b)
Telephone companies shall pay, on or before August 31 of each year, an occupational license tax for that year in the amount of three percent of the actual recurring local service revenues received by the company from subscribers located within the city. The annual amount due may be computed by annualizing the taxable revenues billed during the month of June preceding the date of payment.
(Code 1994, § 22-2; Ord. of 2-8-1973)
State Law reference— Power of municipal authorities concerning telegraph or telephone company service, O.C.G.A. § 46-5-1.
Sec. 12-3. - Fortunetelling.
(a)
Any person within the corporate limits of the city wishing to carry on the business of telling fortunes or carrying on the trade, business or profession of phrenology, clairvoyance, palmistry or other business of like kind or nature in which the future of any person is foretold or attempted to be foretold shall first obtain a business license from the city.
(b)
An application must be submitted with proper documentation that the business:
(1)
Must not be located within 500 feet of a:
a.
Church.
b.
School.
c.
Residence.
(2)
Shall be located within a manufacturing zoning district and meet any other requirements contained in the zoning regulations in chapter 42.
(c)
Hours of operation will not be later than 10:00 p.m.
(d)
The regulatory fee per year, per location, will be in an amount as established by the mayor and council from time to time.
(Code 1994, § 22-3; Ord. of 3-8-1988)
Sec. 12-4. - Automobile-related businesses.
(a)
Any person or entity wishing to operate an automobile-related business within the corporate limits of the city shall first obtain an occupation tax certificate from the city. For purposes of this section, an automobile-related business means any of the following types of businesses (as those businesses are defined in section 42-124 of this Code): automobile broker; automobile dealer; automobile repair garage; automobile retail establishment; automobile sales establishment; automobile salvage shop; automobile service station; convenience store; gasoline service station or used automobile sales establishment.
(b)
The submission of an application seeking an occupation tax certificate to operate an automobile broker, an automobile dealer, an automobile repair garage, an automobile sales establishment, an automobile salvage shop, an automobile service station, or a used automobile sales establishment (as each of these businesses is defined in section 42-124 of this Code) must be accompanied by documentation establishing that the proposed business would be located on property within the M manufacturing zoning district and that the location of the proposed business would comply with any other applicable requirements contained in chapter 42, zoning, of this Code.
(Ord. No. 2015-4, §1, 10-12-2015; Ord. No. 2018-01, § 1, 1-8-2018)
