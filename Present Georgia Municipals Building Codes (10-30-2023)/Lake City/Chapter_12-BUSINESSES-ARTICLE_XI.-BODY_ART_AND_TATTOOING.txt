Editor's note— Ord. No. 2018-07, § 1, adopted August 13, 2018, set out provisions intended for use as §§ 12-412—12-422. To preserve the style of this Code, and at the editor's discretion, these provisions have been included as §§ 12-432—12-442.

Sec. 12-432. - Body art and tattoo businesses—Regulations adopted.
(a)
The city hereby adopts and incorporates by reference as if set out at length herein the latest editions of the Clayton County Board of Health Rules and Regulations for Body Art (hereinafter combined and referred to as the "regulations").
(b)
All body art and/or tattoo businesses of every kind or character operating in the city shall be operated in accordance with the regulations, and no permit shall be issued for the operation of such business(es) unless the same is to be operated in the city in accordance with the regulations. It shall be unlawful for any person to fail to comply with this subsection.
(c)
Where reference is made in the regulations to the duties of certain officials named therein, the designated official of the city who has duties corresponding to those of the named official in the regulations shall be deemed to be the responsible official insofar as enforcing the provisions of the regulations is concerned.
(d)
At least one copy of the regulations shall be kept on file in the office of the city clerk. The regulations shall be enforced in conjunction with and addition to any requirements under this article. Body art and tattoo business operators and employees shall be required to abide by the regulations and requirements of this article at all times. Ignorance of either shall not be considered a valid defense or excuse against enforcement as allowed by law.
(Ord. No. 2018-07, § 1, 8-13-2018)
Sec. 12-433. - Permit required.
No person shall operate a tattoo establishment or engage in the practice or business of body art or tattooing as a body art or tattoo operator or tattoo artist unless and until the person has first secured a license to do so from the city. Each application for a license to engage in the practice or business of body art or tattooing as a tattoo operator or tattoo artist shall include payment of an investigative fee and an annual license fee in amounts to be determined by the governing body from time to time in a separate ordinance.
(Ord. No. 2018-07, § 1, 8-13-2018)
Sec. 12-434. - Application to be made to police department; minimum age; fingerprinting, photographing, medical certificate required.
Any person desiring to operate a body art or tattoo establishment or desiring to operate as a body art or tattoo operator or tattoo artist shall make application for a permit to the police department, on forms supplied by that department. The minimum age of each applicant shall be 18 years of age. Fingerprints of all applicants shall be taken by the bureau. Applicants for permits shall also furnish to the detective bureau two photographs showing a front and side picture of the full face of the applicant, size two and one-half inches by two and three-fourths inches. Applicants for permits shall present to the bureau a medical certificate from a medical doctor certifying that the person is sound physically and mentally, has good eyesight and is not infected with a disease which can be transmitted through openings in the human skin.
(Ord. No. 2018-07, § 1, 8-13-2018)
Sec. 12-435. - Permit issued in name of applicant; nontransferable.
All licenses granted shall be issued in the name of the individual person applying, shall contain the location of the tattoo establishment where the applicant shall operate and shall not be transferable.
(Ord. No. 2018-07, § 1, 8-13-2018)
Sec. 12-436. - Body art and tattooing prohibited.
No tattoo operator or tattoo artist shall tattoo any person who is under the age of 18, any person who is under the influence of alcohol, drugs or other stimulants, or depressants or any person of unsound mind.
(Ord. No. 2018-07, § 1, 8-13-2018)
Sec. 12-437. - Grounds for revocation of permit.
(a)
Any person who shall conduct a body art or tattoo establishment or engage in the business as a tattoo operator or tattoo artist without first securing a permit, or who shall violate any of the regulations, or who violates any of the sections of this article shall be subject to revocation of their body art permit upon appropriate notice of hearing before the municipal court.
(b)
An artist's or operator's permit shall also be subject to revocation upon proof that the holder of the permit has become infected with a disease which can be transmitted through openings in the human skin.
(Ord. No. 2018-07, § 1, 8-13-2018)
Sec. 12-438. - Location restricted.
No permit shall be issued for the operation of a body art or tattoo establishment or to any person engaged as a body art or tattoo operator or tattoo artist in any area which is zoned for residential use by the zoning ordinance of the city as it may now or hereafter exist. A tattoo parlor, studio or corporation may only be located on properties zoned manufacturing (M) under city zoning ordinance.
(Ord. No. 2018-07, § 1, 8-13-2018)
Sec. 12-439. - Permit not in lieu of business license.
The permits issued hereunder shall be cumulative and not in lieu of any business license issued by the city.
(Ord. No. 2018-07, § 1, 8-13-2018)
Sec. 12-440. - Operating requirements.
(a)
Every portion of a tattoo establishment, including appliances and apparatuses shall be kept clean and operated in a sanitary condition in accordance with the regulations.
(b)
Price rates for all services rendered shall be prominently posted in the reception area in a location available to all prospective clientele.
(c)
All employees shall practice hygiene as required by the regulations. A separate dressing room for each sex must be available on the premises.
(d)
The room in which tattooing is done shall be in accordance with the regulations.
(e)
A toilet shall be located in the establishment and shall be accessible at all times that the tattooing establishment is open for business. The lavatory shall be supplied with hot and cold running water, soap and sanitary towels, as well as any other requirements imposed by the regulations.
(f)
All tables and other equipment shall be constructed of easily cleanable material, shall be painted or finished in a light color, with a smooth washable finish, and shall be separated from waiting customers or observers in accordance with the requirements of the regulations.
(g)
The operator shall wash his hands thoroughly with soap and water before starting to tattoo; the hands shall be dried with individual, single-use towels, and the operator must wear surgical gloves during the tattooing. These requirements shall be in addition to, not in place of, any requirements under the regulations.
(h)
No tattooing shall be done on any skin surface that has rash, pimples, boils, infections, any communicable disease, or manifests any evidence of unhealthy conditions. These requirements shall be in addition to, not in place of, any requirements under the regulations.
(i)
No skin area shall be penetrated, abraded or treated with chemicals for the purpose of removing, camouflaging or altering any blemish, birthmark, scar or tattoo. These requirements shall be in addition to, not in place of, any requirements under the regulations.
(j)
Safety razors with a new, single-service blade for each customer or patron or a straight edge razor may be used and shall be thoroughly cleaned and sterilized before use on each customer or patron. These requirements shall be in addition to, not in place of, any requirements under the regulations.
(k)
The area to be tattooed shall first be thoroughly washed for a period of two minutes with warm water to which has been added an antiseptic liquid soap. A sterile single-use sponge shall be used to scrub the area. After shaving and before tattooing is begun, a solution of 70 percent alcohol shall be applied to the area with a single-use sponge used and applied with a sterile instrument. These requirements shall be in addition to, not in place of, any requirements under the regulations.
(l)
Only petroleum jelly in collapsible metal or plastic tubes, or its equivalent as approved by a licensed physician, shall be used on the area to be tattooed and it shall be applied with sterile gauze. The name, address and telephone number of the licensed physician shall be provided to the occupation tax administrator. These requirements shall be in addition to, not in place of, any requirements under the regulations.
(m)
The use of styptic pencils, alum blocks, or other solid styptic to check the flow of blood is prohibited. These requirements shall be in addition to, not in place of, any requirements under the regulations.
(n)
Inquiry shall be made, and anyone giving a history of recent jaundice or hepatitis may not be tattooed. These requirements shall be in addition to, not in place of, any requirements under the regulations.
(o)
Single-service or individual containers of dye or ink shall be used for each patron and the container therefore shall be discarded immediately after completing work on a patron and any dye in which the needles were dipped shall not be used on another person. Excess dye or ink shall be removed from the skin with an individual sterile sponge or a disposable paper tissue which shall be used only on one person and then immediately discarded. After completing work on any person, the tattooed area shall be washed with sterile gauze saturated with an antiseptic soap solution approved by a licensed physician whose name, address and telephone number have been provided to the occupation tax administrator, or a 70 percent alcohol solution. The tattooed area shall be allowed to dry and petroleum jelly from a collapsible or plastic tube shall be applied, using sterile gauze. A sterile gauze dressing shall then be fastened to the tattooed area with adhesive. These requirements shall be in addition to, not in place of, any requirements under the regulations.
(p)
Nothing contained in this chapter shall be construed to eliminate other requirements of the regulations, the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, or city ordinance governing the maintenance of premises, nor to preclude authorized inspection thereof, whenever such inspection is deemed necessary by appropriate department officials.
(q)
All requirements under this section shall be in addition to, not in place of, any requirements under the regulations.
(Ord. No. 2018-07, § 1, 8-13-2018)
Sec. 12-441. - Care of instruments.
(a)
All clean and ready-to-use needles and instruments shall be kept in a closed glass or metal case or storage cabinet while not in use. Such cabinet shall be maintained in a sanitary manner at all times. These requirements shall be in addition to, not in place of, any requirements under the regulations.
(b)
A steam sterilizer (autoclave) shall be provided for sterilizing all needles and similar instruments before use on any customer, person or patron. (Alternate sterilizing procedures may only be used when specifically approved by a licensed physician whose name, address and telephone number have been provided to the occupation tax administrator.) Sterilization of equipment will be accomplished by exposure to live steam for at least 30 minutes at a minimum pressure of 15 pounds per square inch, temperature of 240 degrees Fahrenheit or 116 degrees Celsius.
(c)
The needles and instruments required to be sterilized shall be so used, handled and temporarily placed during tattooing so that they will not be contaminated. These requirements shall be in addition to, not in place of, any requirements under the regulations.
(d)
All requirements under this section shall be in addition to, not in place of, any requirements under the regulations.
(Ord. No. 2018-07, § 1, 8-13-2018)
Sec. 12-442. - Fines and punishment.
(a)
Any person who practices body artistry, tattooing or operates a body art or tattoo establishment without a valid permit, upon being convicted, shall be punished in accordance with the regulations.
(b)
Any person found to be in violation of the requirements of this article other than those in subsection (a) above, shall, upon being convicted, be punished in accordance with the regulations.
(Ord. No. 2018-07, § 1, 8-13-2018)
