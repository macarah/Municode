ARTICLE VII. - NON-CONSENSUAL TOWING PERMITS

Sec. 12-195. - Definitions.
The following terms when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
License means a private trespass towing license issued by the city pursuant to this article and to O.C.G.A. § 44-1-13(d).
Towing and storage firm means a business entity that removes vehicles from private real property at the request of a person in possession of such private real property pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 44-1-13(a).
(Ord. No. 03-10, § 1(art. VI, § 22-131), 12-8-2013)
Sec. 12-196. - License required.
It shall be unlawful for any towing and storage firm to operate within the city without a valid non-consensual towing permit. The city shall charge an annual fee for the permit. The annual permit fee shall be in an amount as established by the mayor and council from time to time. Permits shall be valid for 12 months after the date of issuance.
(Ord. No. 03-10, § 1(art. VI, § 22-132), 12-8-2013)
Sec. 12-197. - Qualifications.
(a)
In order to qualify for and keep a permit, a towing and storage firm must adhere to the following conditions:
(1)
Maximum reasonable costs for removal, relocation, and storage of vehicles shall not exceed the maximum rate tariff as set by the state department of motor vehicle safety from time to time.
(2)
No storage fees shall be charged for the first 24 hours after a vehicle is removed from a property.
(3)
No fees for removal or storage shall be charged for any removal or storage that occurs in violation of O.C.G.A. § 44-1-13.
(4)
For each vehicle removed, the towing and storage firm must notify the local law enforcement agency having jurisdiction over the property from which the vehicle is removed as soon as reasonably possible after such removal that the vehicle has been towed and of the license tag identification information for the vehicle.
(5)
A secure impoundment facility must be maintained.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for a towing and storage firm to fail to adhere to these conditions.
(Ord. No. 03-10, § 1(art. VI, § 22-133), 12-8-2013)
Sec. 12-198. - Adoption of other regulations.
All other applicable regulations contained in O.C.G.A. § 44-1-13 and adopted by the state department of motor vehicle safety are hereby adopted by reference.
(Ord. No. 03-10, § 1(art. VI, § 22-134), 12-8-2013)
Sec. 12-199. - Penalties.
(a)
For each failure to provide the notice required by section 12-197(a)(4), violators shall be subject to a fine of $200.00. The city's receipt from another jurisdiction of a report that the vehicle has been stolen shall raise a rebuttable presumption of a failure to provide the notice required by section 12-197(a)(4).
(b)
Failure to obtain a permit required by this article may result in a civil penalty of $2,500.00.
(c)
In addition to, or in lieu of, the penalty provided by subsection (a) of this section, the city may terminate the permit of a towing and storage firm for any failure to comply with the terms and conditions of this article.
(Ord. No. 03-10, § 1(art. VI, § 22-135), 12-8-2013)
