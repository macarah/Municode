ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 10-1. - Definitions.
modified
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Boarding house shall mean "rooming house," as defined within the Code.
Building official or building inspector means the qualified building official or qualified building inspector, or his designee, as designated by the city.
Code enforcement officer means the qualified code enforcement official, or his designee, as designated by the city.
Rooming house shall mean any dwelling or that part of any dwelling containing one or more rooming units in which space is let by the owner or occupant to three or more persons who are not related by genetics, adoption or marriage.
Rooming unit shall mean any room or group of rooms within a dwelling used or intended to be used for living and sleeping, but not for cooking or eating purposes.
(Ord. No. 2023-01, § 1, 6-12-2023)
Sec. 10-2. - Enforcement.
(a)
The building official and the code enforcement official are hereby authorized to enforce within the city the provisions of this chapter, the zoning regulations in chapter 42, and the state minimum codes adopted herein.
(b)
The building official and the code enforcement official are further authorized to render interpretations of this chapter and the adopted state codes and to adopt policies and procedures in order to clarify the application of their provisions. Such interpretations, policies and procedures shall be in compliance with the intent and purpose of this chapter. Such policies and procedures shall not have the force and effect of law and shall not have the effect of waiving requirements specifically provided for in this Code or in the adopted state codes.
Sec. 10-3. - Final decision.
The city manager or his designee shall constitute the building and construction board of appeals of the city (hereinafter referred to as the "appeals board"). The duties of the appeals board will be to act as a reviewing body in matters pertaining to building and construction standards, devices, appliances and equipment. The building and construction board of appeals shall review and render a decision, when requested to do so by any person, firm or corporation, on any interpretation or ruling made by the building official or the code enforcement official relative to this chapter. Any decision or ruling rendered by the appeals board shall be final, subject to appeal to and decision of the mayor and council.
Sec. 10-4. - Duties of code enforcement officer.
It shall be the duty of the code enforcement officer to see to the proper construction, alteration, repair, redesigning, remodeling, demolition, moving, removal, equipment, use and occupancy, location and maintenance of all buildings of every nature in the city.
(Code 1972, § 4-1; Code 1994, § 18-1)
Sec. 10-5. - Codes adopted; amendments.
(a)
The codes listed in this section are adopted for the purpose of establishing rules and regulations for the construction, alteration, repair, redesigning, remodeling, demolition, moving, removal, equipment, use and occupancy, location and maintenance of buildings and structures.
(b)
Unless otherwise provided for by subsequent ordinance or another provision of this Code, the technical building and construction codes listed in this section are adopted by reference and incorporated into this Code as if set out fully herein, including the administration and appendix chapters also contained in those codes. The state minimum codes enumerated under O.C.G.A. § 8-2-20(9), as now or hereafter revised or modified pursuant to state law:
(1)
International Building Code, 2012 Edition, with Georgia Amendments 2014.
(2)
International Residential Code, 2012 Edition, with Georgia Amendments 2014.
(3)
International Fire Code, 2012 Edition, with Georgia Amendments 2014.
(4)
International Plumbing Code, 2012 Edition, with Georgia Amendments 2014.
(5)
International Mechanical Code, 2012 Edition, with Georgia Amendments 2014.
(6)
International Fuel Gas Code, 2012 Edition, with Georgia Amendments 2014.
(7)
National Electrical Code, 2011 Edition.
(8)
International Energy Conservation Code, 2009 Edition, with Georgia Supplements and Amendments 2011 and 2012.
(9)
International Property Maintenance Code, 2006 Edition, with Georgia Amendments 2009.
(10)
International Existing Building Code, 2006 Edition, with Georgia Amendments 2009.
(11)
International Swimming Pool and Spa Code, 2012 Edition, with Georgia Amendments 2014.
(c)
Wherever the term "municipality" or "city" is used in the codes adopted under this section, it shall be held to mean the City of Lake City.
(d)

If any of the publications listed in subsection (b) of this section are less stringent than the state minimum codes promulgated pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 8-2-20 et seq., then the state minimum code shall control.

A copy of each of the publications listed in subsection (b) of this section, as adopted by the state, is filed in the clerk's office and available for review by the public. The same is adopted and incorporated as fully as if set out at length herein, and from the date on which this section shall take effect, the provisions thereof shall be controlling within the city.
(Code 1972, § 4-2; Code 1994, § 18-2)
Sec. 10-6. - References to officials in adopted technical codes.
Where reference is made to the duties of certain officials named within the technical codes which are adopted by reference in this chapter, then that official of the city as designated by the city manager who has duties corresponding to those of the named official in said technical code, shall be deemed to be the responsible official insofar as enforcing the provisions of that technical code are concerned.
Sec. 10-7. - Tests.
The building official or code enforcement official may require tests or test reports as proof of compliance. Required tests are to be made at the expense of the owner, or his agent, by an approved testing laboratory or other approved agency.
Sec. 10-8. - Violation of codes.
It shall be unlawful for any person to do any act prohibited by any code adopted in section 10-5 or to fail to do any act required by such codes.
(Code 1972, § 4-6; Code 1994, § 18-3)
Sec. 10-9. - Issuance of building permits.
Building permits shall be issued in accordance with the applicable sections of this Code as set out in the zoning regulations in chapter 42.
(Code 1972, § 4-3; Code 1994, § 18-4)
Sec. 10-10. - Building permit fees.
Fees for building permits shall be as set forth in the building code adopted in section 10-5.
(Code 1972, § 4-4; Code 1994, § 18-5)
Sec. 10-11. - Revocation of building permit.
The code enforcement officer shall have the authority to revoke any building permit and to seek injunctive relief against any person in violation of any applicable code adopted in section 10-5 or in the zoning regulations in chapter 42.
(Code 1972, § 4-7; Code 1994, § 18-6)
Sec. 10-12. - Performing inspections; contracting for inspection services; self-inspection by master plumbers or utility contractors not authorized.
(a)
Inspections required under the provisions of the building code shall be made by the building official or such official's duly authorized representatives. Upon presentation of proper credentials, the building official or representative may enter, at reasonable times, any building, structure or premises in the city to perform any duty imposed upon such official by the code. The building official and representatives are hereby authorized and directed to enforce all provisions of the code and are hereby invested with all police power necessary for the purpose of making arrests, issuing copies of charges and prosecution of any person as may be charged with any violation of this chapter or other ordinances of the city.
(b)
All installations of any equipment, appliance or system for which a permit has been issued as prescribed in the code shall be subject to inspection by the building official or representatives.
(c)
The mayor and council shall, from time to time, have the authority to enter into a contract with other municipalities or the county for building, electrical, gas, heating and air conditioning, or plumbing inspection. An inspector for another political subdivision may be appointed as such for the city by the mayor and council.
(d)
The provisions of O.C.G.A. § 8-2-26(d), relating to self-inspection of water and sewer projects by master plumbers or utility contractors, shall not be applicable within the city.
(Code 1972, § 4-5; Code 1994, § 18-7; Ord. No. 96-2, § 1, 6-10-1996; Ord. No. 96-03, § 1, 6-10-1996)
Sec. 10-13. - Temporary structures.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to erect or cause to be erected any temporary structure or tent to be used for public assemblages or for public religious, educational or entertainment purposes without first obtaining a permit from the mayor and council. Any person desiring to erect such a tent or structure shall apply in writing for a permit. The application shall be delivered to the city clerk and shall reveal the following:
(1)
The type of structure or tent desired;
(2)
The material from which the tent or structure is made;
(3)
The equipment expected to be used inside and outside the structure when erected;
(4)
The exact use to be made of the structure;
(5)
The number of people expected to use the facility;
(6)
The arrangements for traffic control;
(7)
Parking;
(8)
The desired location of the structure;
(9)
The precautions to be taken against fire; and
(10)
The sanitary facilities available.
(b)
The mayor and council shall consider the application within ten days of its submission and shall render a decision within 15 days. The mayor and council shall consider all pertinent factors, including but not limited to noise, dust, safety, traffic and nuisance, if any, to other citizens and property owners.
(c)
A permit shall not be issued unless the proposed structure or tent conforms to the applicable sections of this chapter, the zoning regulations in chapter 42, all sanitary and health regulations and all other sections of this Code and other ordinances or unless the structure or tent, as finally constructed, complies with plans upon which the permit was requested.
(d)
No temporary structure or tent shall ever be used as a residence.
(e)
Permits for temporary structures shall expire 90 days after issuance, unless issued for a shorter period of time by vote of the mayor and council. One renewal may be granted by the mayor and council following expiration of the original permit term.
(f)
Nothing in this section shall be construed as interfering with the use of tents for funeral purposes, Boy Scout activities and children's at-home recreation.
(Code 1972, § 4-8; Code 1994, § 18-8)
Sec. 10-14. - Fencing.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to allow any fence to be located on any property in the city unless such fence is constructed of metal, including chainlinks, wood, or other similarly solid, rigid, durable material and so as not to have openings, holes or gaps larger than four inches in any dimension, except for doors and gates. Additionally, all fencing shall be constructed such that all structural supports, framing, and other such components are facing towards the interior of the property.
(b)
In addition to the requirements of subsection (a) of this section, any fence required by this Code, except for article IV, division 2, of this chapter, to be erected shall be of such opaque material and so constructed as to completely obscure any view through any portion of the fence.
(Ord. No. 03-02, § 1(18.9), 4-14-2003)
Sec. 10-15. - Rooming houses.
new
Every rooming house, boarding house, single room occupancy residence and building with a similar special use shall conform to the requirements of this section.
(1)
Permits and licenses. No person shall operate a rooming house, boarding house, residence hotel, apartment hotel or single room occupancy residence unless such person holds a valid and current permit issued by the Lake City Architecture and Design Review Committee and a valid and current business license issued by the city. The permit and license shall be in the name of the owner for the specific location where the business is to be located. If the owner resides out the state of Georgia, the owner shall designate an operator, who shall be a Georgia resident, and who shall act as the responsible person on behalf of the owner for purposes of this chapter 10. The permit and business license shall at all times be displayed in a conspicuous and public place within the business on the wall adjacent to the front door of the structure. The permit and license are for regulatory purposes and shall not be transferable.
(2)
Recommendations for revocation of permits. Whenever, upon inspection of any building regulated under this section, the building official or code enforcement officer finds that conditions or practices exist which are in violation of any provisions of this Code, or of any rule or regulations adopted pursuant hereto, notice shall be given in writing to the operator of such house that the existence of such conditions or practices is unlawful and, unless they are corrected within a reasonable period to be determined by the building official, revocation of the operator's permit will be recommended to the city. At the end of the designated correction period, the building official shall reinspect such building and, if it is found that such conditions or practices have not been corrected, may file with the code enforcement official a written recommendation that such permit be revoked, stating the reasons therefor. This shall not prevent the city or its code enforcement officers from taking any other appropriate action against the owner or operator for violations of this Code.
(3)
Operation in violation of this chapter. No person shall operate a rooming house or shall let to another for occupancy any room, unless such rooming house or room complies with the following requirements:
a.
Each rooming house and room shall be in compliance with the minimum standards set forth in this article and all applicable rules, regulations, and standards set forth in the City Code and state and federal law, including, and not limited to, life safety codes.
b.
Each rooming house shall be equipped with at least one flush water closet, one lavatory, an installed tub or shower for each four persons or fraction thereof within the rooming house, including members of the family, if they are to share the use of the facilities. All such facilities shall be properly connected to the water supply and sewer system as required by applicable code. Each flush water closet, lavatory, tub, or shower required above, shall be located within the rooming house, in a room, or rooms, which affords privacy, is accessible by a common interior hall without going outside the rooming house, is accessible by a common interior hall without going through sleeping quarters of others and is not more than one story removed from the room of an occupant intended to share the facilities.
c.
Each rooming unit within a rooming house shall provide not less than 75 square feet of finished floor space per occupant in a designated bedroom.
(Ord. No. 2023-01, § 2, 6-12-2023)

DIVISION 2. - LOW-FLOW FIXTURES[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Water efficiency requirements, O.C.G.A. § 8-2-1 et seq.; flow-rate restrictions on plumbing fixtures, O.C.G.A. § 8-2-3.

Sec. 10-120. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Commercial building means any type of building other than residential.
Construction means the erection of a new building or the alteration of an existing building in connection with its repair or renovation or in connection with making an addition to an existing building and includes the replacement of a malfunctioning, unserviceable or obsolete faucet, showerhead, toilet or urinal in an existing building.
Plumbing fixture means any toilet, urinal, showerhead, bathroom, lavatory or kitchen faucet and replacement aerators.
Residential building means any building or unit of a building intended for occupancy as a dwelling unit, but does not include a hotel or motel.
Toilet means any fixture consisting of a water-flushed bowl with a seat, used for the disposal of human waste.
Urinal means any fixture consisting of a water-flushed bowl, but not provided with a seat, used for the disposal of human waste.
(Code 1994, § 18-81; Ord. No. 91-1, § 2, 3-11-1991)
Sec. 10-121. - Purpose.
It is the purpose of this division to require the use of ultra-low-flow plumbing fixtures in all new construction or when replacing plumbing fixtures during renovation or remodeling of existing buildings for the purpose of conserving water, to maintain the integrity of drinking water supplies and to reduce wastewater flows.
(Code 1994, § 18-82; Ord. No. 91-1, § 1, 3-11-1991)
Sec. 10-122. - Enforcement.
(a)
This division shall be enforced by the code enforcement officer who shall have full authority to issue citations for violations of this division.
(b)
Any person violating this division shall be tried before the judge of the municipal court and, upon conviction or entering of a plea of guilty or nolo contendere, each violation of this division shall subject such person to the punishments as provided in section 1-8. Each violation shall constitute a separate offense, and each day that such violation continues shall constitute a separate offense.
(Code 1994, § 18-83; Ord. No. 91-1, § 7, 3-11-1991)
Sec. 10-123. - Remedies for violations.
(a)
In addition to criminal prosecution pursuant to section 10-122, any building or plumbing contractor, developer, owner or other person who violates this division shall be subject to suspension of his business license for a period of up to 90 days.
(b)
In addition to the penalties referred to in section 10-122, the city may take such other actions as authorized by law to compel compliance with the requirements of this division and may specifically maintain an action or proceeding in any court of competent jurisdiction to comply with or restrain any violation of this division.
(Code 1994, § 18-84; Ord. No. 91-1, § 8, 3-11-1991)
Sec. 10-124. - Standards for plumbing fixtures.
(a)
No plumbing fixture shall be installed which does not meet the standards listed in this section or the state minimum plumbing code, whichever is stricter. This includes all plumbing fixtures installed in newly constructed buildings or when replacing plumbing fixtures during remodeling or renovation of existing buildings, except as noted in section 10-126.
(b)
The standards are as follows:
(1)
A water closet or toilet that:
a.
Is a dual-flush water closet that meets the following standards:
1.
The average flush volume of two reduced flushes and one full flush may not exceed 1.28 gallons;
2.
The toilet meets the performance, testing, and labeling requirements prescribed by the following standards, as applicable:
(i)
American Society of Mechanical Engineers Standard A112.19.2-2008; and
(ii)
American Society of Mechanical Engineers Standard A112.19.14-2006 Six-Liter Water Closets Equipped with a Dual Flushing Device"; and
3.
Is listed to the WaterSense TM Tank-Type High Efficiency Toilet Specification; or
b.
Is a single-flush water closet, including gravity, pressure-assisted, and electro-hydraulic tank types, that meets the following standards:
1.
The average flush volume may not exceed 1.28 gallons;
2.
The toilet must meet the performance, testing, and labeling requirements prescribed by the American Society of Mechanical Engineers Standard A112.192/CSA B45.1 or A112.19.14; and
3.
The toilet must be listed to the WaterSense TM Tank-Type High Efficiency Toilet Specification;
(2)
A showerhead that allows a flow of no more than an average of 2.5 gallons of water per minute at 60 pounds per square inch of pressure;
(3)
A urinal and associated flush valve that:
a.
Uses no more than 0.5 gallons of water per flush;
b.
Meets the performance, testing, and labeling requirements prescribed by the American Society of Mechanical Engineers Standard A112.19.2/CSA B45.1;
c.
For flushing urinals, meets all WaterSense TM specifications for flushing urinals; and
d.
Where non-water urinals are employed, complies with American Society of Mechanical Engineers Standard A112.19.3/CSA B45.4 or American Society of Mechanical Engineers Standard A112.19.19/CSA B45.4. Non-water urinals shall be cleaned and maintained in accordance with the manufacturer's instructions after installation. Where non-water urinals are installed they shall have a water distribution line roughed-in to the urinal location at a minimum height of 56 inches (1,422 mm) to allow for the installation of an approved backflow prevention device in the event of a retrofit. Such water distribution lines shall be installed with shut-off valves located as close as possible to the distributing main to prevent the creation of dead ends. Where non-water urinals are installed, a minimum of one water-supplied fixture rated at a minimum of one water-supplied fixture unit shall be installed upstream on the same drain line to facilitate drain line flow and rinsing;
(4)
A lavatory faucet or lavatory replacement aerator that allows a flow of no more than 1.5 gallons of water per minute at a pressure of 60 pounds per square inch in accordance with American Society of Mechanical Engineers Standard A112.18.1/CSA B.125.1 and listed to the WaterSense TM High-Efficiency Lavatory Faucet Specification; and
(5)
A kitchen faucet or kitchen replacement aerator that allows a flow of no more than 2.0 gallons of water per minute.
(Code 1994, § 18-85; Ord. No. 91-1, § 3, 3-11-1991)
Sec. 10-125. - Applicability.
Section 10-124 shall apply to any residential construction, and to any commercial construction, which involves the repair or renovation of or addition to any existing building when such repair or renovation of or addition to such existing building includes replacement toilets or showers, or both.
(Code 1994, § 18-86; Ord. No. 91-1, § 4, 3-11-1991)
Sec. 10-126. - Exemptions.
New construction and the repair or renovation of existing buildings shall be exempt from the requirements of section 10-124 when:
(1)
The repair or renovation of the existing building does not include the replacement of the plumbing or sewer system servicing toilets, faucets or showerheads within such existing building.
(2)
Such plumbing or sewer system within such existing building, because of its capacity, design or installation, would not function properly if the toilets, faucets or showerheads required by this division were installed. This exemption includes but is not limited to showers and faucets installed for safety purposes, such as an emergency eyewash station.
(3)
Such system is a well or gravity-flow system from a spring and is owned privately by an individual for use in such individual's personal residence.
(4)
Units to be installed are:
a.
Specifically designed for use by the handicapped;
b.
Specifically designed to withstand unusual abuse or installation in a penal institution; or
c.
Toilets for juveniles.
(Code 1994, § 18-87; Ord. No. 91-1, § 5, 3-11-1991)
Sec. 10-127. - Procedure for obtaining exemption.
(a)
No owner shall be entitled to an exemption from the requirements of section 10-124 unless he shall first have obtained an exemption in writing from the code enforcement officer. Application for exemption shall be made on a form provided by the code enforcement officer and shall include a listing of the specific exemption sought; the basis for such exemption; and, where such exemption is based on technological unfeasibility, the affidavit of a registered professional engineer or architect verifying the unfeasibility of the water conservation device for the particular existing structure.
(b)
A fee as fixed from time to time by the mayor and council shall be charged for issuance of such an exemption.
(c)
The code enforcement officer shall review the plans for the facility and may make inspections of existing premises where exemptions from the requirements of this division are sought.
(d)
If, upon the basis of such investigation and any inspections, the code enforcement officer determines that the requested exemption falls within those parameters provided by section 10-126, he shall issue such exemption in written form, which form shall bear his signature and date.
(Code 1994, § 18-88; Ord. No. 91-1, § 6, 3-11-1991)

Each general contractor shall be responsible for procuring a grading permit by any subcontractor employed by him to engage in grading or other activity involving the clearing of land before such work is begun. The penalty for the failure of a subcontractor to procure a grading permit and the fee therefor shall be assessed by the city against the employing general contractor.
(Code 1994, § 38-67; Ord. of 3-27-1975(1), § 21-2)
