Sec. 30-1-1. - Definitions.
As used in this chapter:
Administrative fee means a component of an occupation tax which approximates the reasonable costs of handling and processing the occupation tax.
Employee means an individual whose work is performed under the direction and supervision of the employer and his employer withholds FICA, Federal Income Tax, or State Income Tax from such individual's compensation and whose employer issues to such individual for purposes of documenting compensation a form I.R.S. W-2 but not a form I.R.S. 1099.
Director of community and economic development shall be deemed to include the person serving as director of community and economic development of the city and the designated representative of said director.
Location or office shall include any structure or vehicle where a business, profession, or occupation is conducted, but shall not include a temporary or construction work site which serves a single customer or project or a vehicle used for sales or delivery by a business or practitioner of a profession or occupation which has a location or office. The renter's or lessee's location which is the site of personal property which is rented or leased from another does not constitute a location or office for the personal property's owner, lessor, or the agent of the owner or lessor. The site of real property which is rented or leased to another does not constitute a location or office for the real property's owner, lessor, or the agent of the owner or lessor unless the real property's owner, lessor, or the agent of the owner or lessor, in addition to showing the property to prospective lessees or tenants and performing maintenance or repair of the property, otherwise conducts the business of renting or leasing the real property at such site or otherwise conducts any other business, profession, or occupation at such site.
Occupation tax means a tax levied on persons, partnerships, corporations, or other entities for engaging in an occupation, profession, or business and enacted by a local government as a revenue-raising ordinance or resolution.
Regulatory fees means payments, whether designated as license fees, permit fees or by another name, which are required by local government as an exercise of its police power and as a part of or as an aid to regulation of an occupation, profession or business. The amount of a regulatory fee shall approximate the reasonable cost of the actual regulatory activity performed by the local government. A regulatory fee may not include an administrative fee. Regulatory fees do not include development impact fees as defined by paragraph (8) of O.C.G.A. § 36-71-2 or other costs or conditions of zoning or land development. Regulatory fees shall be determined in accordance with one of the following methods:
(1)
A flat fee for each business or practitioner of a profession or occupation doing business in the jurisdiction as authorized by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-8;
(2)
A flat fee for each type of permit or inspection requested;
(3)
An hourly rate determined by the hourly wage or salary, including employee benefits, of the person or persons assigned to investigate or inspect multiplied by the number of hours estimated for the investigation or inspection to be performed;
(4)
A hourly rate as determined by paragraph (3) of this definition with the addition of other expenses reasonably related to such regulatory activity, such as administrative and travel expenses, multiplied by the number of hours estimated for the investigation or inspection to be performed;
(5)
For construction projects that are classified as new construction, the number of square feet of construction or the number of square feet of construction to be served by the system to be installed, in conjunction with and limited by the building valuation data, as established from time to time by the International Code Council or by similar data, and in conjunction with and limited by the hourly rate described in paragraph (3) or (4) of this definition; or
(6)
For construction projects that are classified as renovation and all other construction projects other than those classified as new construction, the cost of the project in conjunction with and limited by the building valuation data that conforms with the principles and methods established from time to time by the International Code Council or by similar data, and in conjunction with and limited by the hourly rate described in paragraph (3) or (4) of this definition.
(Ord. No. 95-46, § 1, 12-21-95; Ord. No. 06-14, § 2, 7-11-06; Ord. No. 13-10, § 1, 8-13-13)
Sec. 30-1-2. - Violations enumerated.
(a)
The following acts or omissions are declared to be violations of this title:
(1)
Engaging in any business, profession, trade or calling without procuring the proper business tax registration to do so as provided in this title, if required, or without registering with the city as required in section 30-1-7;
(2)
Failing to make the return or statement to the director of community and economic development as required by section 30-1-9(b);
(3)
Giving or filing, or directing the giving or filing, of any false information regarding any tax due under the provisions of this title or upon which tax due under this title is based or determined;
(4)
Failing or refusing to permit the director of community and economic development to examine books or records as provided in section 30-1-10;
(5)
Failure to comply with any federal, state or city law or regulation applicable to the particular business being conducted; or
(6)
Failure to comply with any of the requirements of this title.
(b)
Any person who shall fail to pay the occupation tax or administrative fee assessed pursuant to this chapter (by the 1 st day of April) shall, upon conviction therefor in the municipal court, be subjected to a civil fine of up to five hundred dollars ($500.00).
(Ord. No. 95-46, § 1, 12-21-95; Ord. No. 13-10, § 2, 8-13-13)
Sec. 30-1-3. - Punishment; revocation of business tax registration.
(a)
Upon the failure of any business to pay the applicable administrative fee, occupation tax or business license fee, or any part thereof, before it becomes delinquent, or upon failure to make any of said returns within the time required herein, or upon failure to make a true return, or upon failure to amend a return to set forth the truth, or upon failure to permit inspection of its books as provided herein, any business tax registration granted by the city under this chapter permitting the owner of said business to do business for the current year shall be, ipso facto, revoked. No new business tax registrations shall be granted by the city for the operation of a business for which any part of the occupation tax, administrative fee or business license fee herein provided for is at that time unpaid, or to an individual, firm, or corporation who has failed to submit adequate records as requested by the director of economic development, or his designee, in accordance with the provisions found in section 30-1-10.
(b)
Additionally, in any instance in which a business shall fail to comply with any federal, state or city law or regulation applicable to such business, the mayor and council in their legislative discretion, may revoke or suspend the registration permitting such business to conduct its business or to operate in the city provided the mayor and council shall conclude from evidence produced in a public hearing held, after appropriate public notice, that revocation or suspension, as the case may be, of registration is appropriate and justified.
(c)
Notwithstanding any other provision in this chapter, in the case of those practitioners whose right to practice or conduct business cannot be suspended or infringed by a local government, only the imposition of civil penalties shall be permitted and pursued by the city in the case of delinquent occupation tax, administrative fee or business license fees. Any such practitioner who fails to remit a tax or fee required under this chapter shall, upon conviction of same in the municipal court, be fined in an amount not to exceed one thousand dollars ($1,000.00).
(Ord. No. 95-46, § 1, 12-21-95; Ord. No. 96-18, § 1, 3-14-96; Ord. No. 03-15, §§ 1, 2, 4-22-03)
Sec. 30-1-4. - Exemptions.
(a)
The occupation tax or other fee imposed in this chapter shall not apply to any business, profession, trade or calling involved solely in interstate or foreign commerce, unless and until the same shall cease to be a part of such interstate or foreign commerce.
(b)
The legal right of the producer who sells the product of his own farm is recognized, but this right does not authorize any person purchasing goods or other things of value within or without the city to offer the same for sale within the city without proper business tax registration.
(c)
Before any disabled veteran or blind person shall be granted a permit to enter business without the payment of an occupation fee or tax in the city, such disabled veteran or blind person shall present to the director of economic and community development a certificate of exemption from the payment of such fee issued by the State Commissioner of Veterans' Service.
(d)
Any practitioner whose office is maintained by and who is employed in practice exclusively by the United States, the state, a municipality or county of the state, instrumentalities of the state, or a municipality or county of the state shall not be required to pay an occupation tax or administrative fee for that practice.
(e)
The occupation tax or other fee in this chapter shall not apply to nonprofit organizations.
(f)
The following businesses are not covered by the provisions of this chapter but may be assessed an occupation tax, administrative fee or other type of tax pursuant to the provisions of other general laws of the State of Georgia or by local law:
(1)
Those businesses regulated by the Georgia Public Service Commission;
(2)
Those electrical service businesses organized under Chapter 3 of Title 46 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated;
(3)
Any farm operation for the production from or on the land of agricultural products, but not including agribusiness;
(4)
Cooperative marketing associations governed by O.C.G.A. Section 2-10-105;
(5)
Insurance companies governed by O.C.G.A. Section 33-8-8, et seq.;
(6)
Motor common carriers governed by O.C.G.A. Section 46-7-15;
(7)
Those businesses governed by O.C.G.A. Section 48-5-355; (Businesses that purchase carload lots of guano, meats, meal, flour, bran, cottonseed, or cottonseed meal and hulls.)
(8)
Agricultural products and livestock raised in the State of Georgia, governed by O.C.G.A. Section 48-5-356;
(9)
Depository financial institutions governed by O.C.G.A. Section 48-6-93; and
(10)
Facilities operated by a charitable trust governed by O.C.G.A. Section 48-13-55.
(g)
The administrative fee and occupation tax upon real estate brokers shall be in accordance with O.C.G.A. Section 48-13-17 and the fee schedule set forth in this chapter.
(Ord. No. 95-46, § 1, 12-21-95)
Sec. 30-1-5. - Enforcing officer.
It shall be the duty of the director of community and economic development to ensure that the provisions of this title relating to business tax registration, regulatory fees, administrative fees and occupation taxes are observed and enforced. He shall inspect all business tax registrations issued by the city as often as may be necessary to ensure that each such license is proper and sufficient for the business being transacted thereunder.
(Ord. No. 95-46, § 1, 12-21-95)
Sec. 30-1-6. - Registration as a prerequisite to doing business.
(a)
For calendar year 1996 and any succeeding year thereafter, each person, firm or corporation engaged in business, trade, profession or occupation in the City of LaGrange with a location in the city, shall pay an administrative fee and occupation tax for said business trade, profession, or occupation, obtain the registration required by this chapter and maintain such registration during the current year as a prerequisite to doing and continuing to do business in the city during such year, except in those instances which, under the law of Georgia, local government cannot suspend the right of a practitioner to conduct business.
(b)
If the taxpayer has no permanent business location in the City of LaGrange, and has obtained registration and paid an occupation tax in another jurisdiction, the applicable business tax registration shall be shown to the director of community and economic development or to such officer's designee to any police officer of the city upon request.
(c)
Unless otherwise allowed pursuant to article V, article VI or article VII of chapter 30-5 or other provision of the city code, any business location in the City of LaGrange shall be operated out of a building as such term is defined within chapter 25D-1 of the Code.
(Ord. No. 95-46, § 1, 12-21-95; Ord. No. 96-18, § 1, 3-14-96; Ord. No. 04-34, § 1, 10-12-04; Ord. No. 21-09, § 2, 6-22-21)
Sec. 30-1-7. - Obtaining on coming into city; registration required; failure to obtain.
(a)
All persons subject to the occupation tax or other fee pursuant to this chapter shall be required to obtain the necessary registration for the business as described in this chapter, and in default thereof, the officer or agent soliciting for or representing such persons shall be subject to the same penalty as other persons who fail to obtain a registration.
(b)
Every person commencing business in the city after January 1 of each year shall likewise obtain the registration herein provided for before commencing the same; and any person transacting, or offering to transact in the city any of the kinds of business, trade, profession or occupation without first having so obtained said registration, shall be subject to penalties provided therefor.
(Ord. No. 95-46, § 1, 12-21-95)
Sec. 30-1-8. - Half-year registration.
Any new business, trade, profession or calling which shall begin business in the city after July 1 of any year shall pay an occupation tax or business license fee equal to fifty (50) percent of the amount required for such business, trade, profession or calling for a full year, in addition to the full, non-prorated administrative fee.
(Ord. No. 95-46, § 1, 12-21-95)
Sec. 30-1-9. - Payment of occupation fee or tax; late fee; interest; filing return.
(a)
Each administrative fee, occupation fee or other fee due under this chapter shall be paid for each calendar year. Such occupation fee or tax shall be due and payable on the first day of January each year. A penalty of ten (10) percent of the occupation fee, tax or other fee shall be assessed against those persons or other entities described in section 30-1-24 of this article that shall fail to pay the occupation tax or fee imposed hereby on or before the first day of April of each year. If the occupation tax and fees are not paid by the first day of April of each year, interest at one and five-tenths (1.5) percent per month shall thereafter accrue and be due and payable.
(b)
All businesses classified in section 30-1-24 for which a tax is imposed based upon the number of employees shall, on or before the first day of April of each year, file with the director of community and economic development a return which shall reflect the total number of employees for the previous calendar year. Any person who shall fail to file annually the returns required under this chapter shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-1-6 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 95-46, § 1, 12-21-95)
Sec. 30-1-10. - Inspection of books and records.
The director of community and economic development or his duly authorized agent may inspect the books and records of any business in order to audit or verify any return filed, or to determine the average number of employees of those entities required to pay an occupation tax hereunder.
(Ord. No. 95-46, § 1, 12-21-95)
Sec. 30-1-11. - Prohibited fee or tax.
No license fee, administrative fee or occupation tax is intended to be imposed by this title which is prohibited under or by virtue of the laws of the State of Georgia or the United States of America.
(Ord. No. 95-46, § 1, 12-21-95)
Sec. 30-1-12. - Location and transferability of registration.
Proper business tax registration shall be required at each business location and a registration granted under this title shall not authorize the holder thereof to exercise or carry on the business described in such registration at any place other than that named in the registration; nor shall a registration be transferred from one location to another location, or from one person to another person, unless authorized by the mayor and council and then only as provided by specific ordinances.
(Ord. No. 95-46, § 1, 12-21-95)
Sec. 30-1-13. - Appeal.
Any person shall have the right to appeal any decision under the provisions of this title to the mayor and council. Such appeal shall be taken within a reasonable time after the decision complained of or the application of the provisions complained of, but in no event more than thirty (30) days after the decision from which the appeal is taken. Such appeal shall be filed in writing with the director of community and economic development and shall be heard by the mayor and council at the next regular meeting, or at such other time as may be fixed by the mayor and council after giving public notice thereof and due notice to the parties in interest.
(Ord. No. 95-46, § 1, 12-21-95)
Sec. 30-1-14. - Paying occupation tax of business with no location in Georgia.
Registration and assessment of the administrative fee and occupation tax is hereby imposed upon those businesses and practitioners of professions with no location or office in the State of Georgia if that business's largest dollar volume of business in Georgia is in LaGrange, Georgia, and the business or practitioner:
(1)
Has one (1) or more employees or agents who exert substantial efforts within the jurisdiction of LaGrange for the purpose of soliciting business or serving customers or clients; or
(2)
Owns personal or real property which generates income and which is located within the jurisdiction of the city.
(Ord. No. 95-46, § 1, 12-21-95)
Sec. 30-1-15. - Number of businesses considered to be operating in the city.
Where a person conducts business at more than one (1) fixed location, each location or place shall be considered a separate business for the purpose of the administrative fee and flat fee portion of the occupation tax. In computing the per employee component of the occupation tax, businesses which operate in more than one (1) location or place within the city shall be taxed in accordance with section 30-1-24.
(Ord. No. 95-46, § 1, 12-21-95)
Sec. 30-1-16. - Purpose and scope of tax.
The occupation tax levied herein is for revenue purposes only and is not for regulatory purposes, nor is the payment of the occupation tax made a condition precedent to the practice of any such profession, trade or calling. The occupation tax only applies to those businesses and occupations which are covered by the provisions of O.C.G.A. Sections 48-13-5 to 48-13-26. All other applicable businesses and occupations are taxed by the local government pursuant to the pertinent general and/or local law and ordinance.
(Ord. No. 95-46, § 1, 12-21-95)
Sec. 30-1-17. - Evidence of qualification required if applicable.
Any business required to obtain health permits, bonds, certificates of qualification, certificates of competency, or to satisfy any other regulatory requirement shall first, before the issuance of a city business registration, shall evidence that such requirements have been met. Any business required to pay personal property ad valorem taxes of Troup County, Georgia, shall likewise show evidence that the most recent personal property ad valorem taxes assessed have been paid.
(Ord. No. 95-46, § 1, 12-21-95; Ord. No. 01-19, § 1, 5-8-01)
Sec. 30-1-18. - Requirements of public hearing.
The mayor and council of the city shall conduct at least one (1) public hearing before adopting any ordinance or resolution regarding the occupation tax and shall further hold one (1) or more public hearings in any year which revenue from occupation taxes is greater than revenue from occupation taxes from the preceding year, said hearing to be a part of the process of determining how to use the additional revenue.
(Ord. No. 95-46, § 1, 12-21-95)
Sec. 30-1-19. - Calculation of number of employees.
The number of employees of the business or practitioner is to be computed on a full-time position basis or full-time position equivalent basis, provided that for the purposes of this computation an employee who works forty (40) hours or more weekly shall be considered a full-time employee and that the average weekly hours of employees who work less than forty (40) hours weekly shall be added and such sums shall be divided by forty (40) to produce full-time position equivalents.
(Ord. No. 95-46, § 1, 12-21-95)
Sec. 30-1-20. - Occupation tax of practitioners of certain professions and occupations.
Practitioners of professions and occupations listed in paragraphs 1 through 18 of subsection (c) of O.C.G.A. section 48-13-9 shall elect as their entire occupation tax one of the following:
(1)
The occupation tax resulting from application of the other provisions of this chapter; or
(2)
A fee which shall total four hundred dollars ($400.00) per practitioner who is licensed to provide the service, such tax to be paid at that practitioner's office or location.
(Ord. No. 95-46, § 1, 12-21-95)
Secs. 30-1-21—30-1-23. - Reserved.

All businesses and practitioners of professions and occupations shall pay to city, according to the schedule set forth in section 30-1-9, an administrative fee of thirty-five dollars ($35.00) per business location within the city, in addition to an occupation tax composed of the following:
(1)
Seventy-five dollars ($75.00) for each business location within the city; and
(2)
A tax on the number of employees employed by said business within city. The per employee tax rate shall be as follows:
Number of Employees     Rate
0 to 4 .....$ 0.00
plus
5—19, each .....35.00
plus
20—49, each .....27.00
plus
50—99, each .....19.00
plus
100—249, each .....10.00
Above 249, no additional per employee tax.
(3)
The per employee tax set forth in subsection (2) above shall be computed, at the discretion of the business, in aggregate for all locations within the jurisdiction, or separately for each location within the jurisdiction.
(Ord. No. 95-46, § 1, 12-21-95)
