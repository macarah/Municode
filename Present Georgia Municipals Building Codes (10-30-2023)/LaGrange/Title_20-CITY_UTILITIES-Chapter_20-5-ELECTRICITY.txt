Sec. 20-5-1. - Meter location.
It shall be unlawful for any person, firm or corporation to install or cause to be installed any electric meter in the city except on the exterior of any building; all electrical meters installed and maintained on the exterior of any such building shall be installed and maintained at a place on the exterior of such building which is accessible to the meter reader and other employees of the city and shall be positioned so that the centerline of the meter shall be not more than six (6) feet, nor less than five (5) feet from the permanent grade line or level directly under such meter location.
(Code 1972, § 24-15; Ord. No. 92-10, § 1, 4-14-92)
Sec. 20-5-2. - Separate meter for each living unit and building.
With the exceptions specified in this chapter, every living unit, and each separate building not constituting a living unit, furnished electricity by the city shall be served through a separate electric meter, and it shall be unlawful for any person to connect, maintain, serve or receive electricity furnished by the city in any other manner. The following buildings are excepted from the foregoing requirements: Industrial buildings, private garages and outbuildings not used or usable for residence, business, commercial or professional purposes, provided the same are wired and used and maintained in all respects in accordance with other applicable ordinances and codes.
(Code 1972, § 24-16)
Sec. 20-5-3. - Wire entrance at convenient point.
Property owners must consult the city and determine the most convenient point for the service wire to enter the building, as the city will only furnish wires from the poles to the nearest convenient point.
(Code 1972, § 24-17)

Sec. 20-5-5. - Outdoor lighting service—Schedule 10.
(a)
Availability. Outdoor lighting service is available throughout the city's service area from lines of adequate capacity.
(b)
Applicability. Applicable to outdoor lighting ballast operated vapor lamp fixtures, either high pressure sodium (HPS), metal halide (MH), or induction fluorescent (IF) on poles conforming to city specifications. Service will be rendered only at locations which in the opinion of the city are readily accessible for installation and maintenance.
(c)
Special provisions:
(1)
The city reserves the right to determine which of the lighting options offered under this rate schedule will be available at a particular location.
(2)
For installations that are deemed by the city to be more costly or difficult than a normal installation, the customer will be required to pay a contribution in aid of construction. The contribution may be billed as a lump sum amount or as an increase in the monthly rate as determined by the city.
(3)
In the event the city relocates an existing lighting system at a customer's request, the customer may be required to pay the costs associated with the relocation.
(4)
The customer may be required to reimburse the city for maintenance work which is required because of vandalism if that vandalism becomes excessive or, in the city's opinion, is caused by the customer or circumstances that should have been under control of the customer.
(5)
All rates in this schedule are for night service only. Continuous burn lights must be installed behind the customer's meter.
(6)
Customer owned security lighting systems shall be metered. The city will replace bulbs and photocells in customer owned lighting for the monthly fee of two dollars ($2.00) per fixture.
(7)
In the event a customer discontinues an outdoor lighting service prior to the city recovering capital expenses incurred in the installation of same, city may assess a capital recovery charge equal to the greater of twenty-five dollars ($25.00) or the unrecovered capital costs less salvage value of the removed fixtures and poles.
(8)
Customers may purchase the poles and lighting fixtures installed on their property by paying a capital charge determined by the city plus the cost of any work performed by the city to relocate the power supply lines to a point behind the customer's meter.
(9)
Customer owned lighting that is separately metered may be served under the city's general electric service tariff.
(10)
Lighted signs may be flat billed using one of the monthly rates below based on the city's estimate of the sign wattage and hours of operation.

Sec. 20-5-11. - Meter check readings.
A fee of ten dollars ($10.00) shall be charged by the city to reread or check a utility meter reading upon customer request. This fee may be waived by a customer service representative in those cases where the original meter reading is deemed unreasonable when compared to previous meter readings or the meter was estimated due to fault of the city. The fee will not be waived following estimation of a meter reading due to fault of the customer. If the check reading reveals that the original reading was in error, no fee shall be charged.
(Ord. No. 96-15, § 5, 3-14-96)
Sec. 20-5-12. - Meter testing.
A fee of twenty dollars ($20.00) shall be charged by the city to test a utility meter upon customer request. If a meter test reveals that a meter is registering more than one hundred two (102) percent of the actual consumption, the fee shall be waived and a credit up to six (6) months for the overbilling applied to the customer account.
(Ord. No. 96-15, § 5, 3-14-96)
