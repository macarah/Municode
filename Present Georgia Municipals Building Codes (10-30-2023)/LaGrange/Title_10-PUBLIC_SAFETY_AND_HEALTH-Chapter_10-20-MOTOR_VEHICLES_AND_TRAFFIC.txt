Article I - IN GENERAL

Sec. 10-20-1. - Jurisdiction.
Any violation of this chapter which is also a violation of the laws of Georgia may, in the discretion of the recorder, be tried as an offense under this chapter or may be treated as an offense of such state law. In the latter event, the recorder shall bind the defendant over to appropriate court having jurisdiction over said offense.
(Code 1972, § 23-211)
State Law reference— Commitment hearings, O.C.G.A. § 17-7-20 et seq.
Sec. 10-20-2. - Statutes adopted by reference.
Pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. sections 40-6-372—40-6-376, that chapter known as the Uniform Rules of the Road and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. section 40-1-1 are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of the city with like effect as if recited in this section. Any future amendment or repeal of a provision of such laws shall so amend or repeal the pertinent provision, if any, of the original ordinance adopted by the city pursuant to the authority of O.C.G.A. section 40-6-372 without any action by the city being required.
(Code 1972, § 23-1)
Sec. 10-20-3. - Offenses by persons owning or controlling vehicles.
It is unlawful for the owner or any other person, employing or otherwise directing the driver of any vehicle, to require or knowingly permit the operation of such vehicle upon a highway in any manner contrary to law.
(Code 1972, § 23-170)

Sec. 10-20-6. - Railroad crossings; train speed.
(a)
At every place in the city at which any railroad track or tracks intersect or cross a public street or right-of-way, the railroad company which owns and operates such track or tracks shall cause traffic-warning devices to be erected and maintained as follows:
(1)
The Greenville Street, Main Street, Park Avenue, Vernon Road, Depot Street between Morgan and Whitesville Streets and Hines Street at McGee Street crossings shall each have installed and maintained an automatically activated train approach signaling device as described and specified in sections 5-128 through 5-137 of The Georgia Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices, adopted July 3, 1962, as amended on November 17, 1966, and as the same may be thereafter amended; provided, however, that a railroad company having a track or tracks at any of the above named crossings upon which a train does not activate an installed and maintained automatically controlled signaling device, as above-referred to, shall provide, for the protection of users of such crossing, a flagman or watchman at each such crossing prior to each train crossing such intersection and to remain until such crossing is completed.
(2)
All other crossings within the corporate limits of the city shall have erected and installed a railroad crossing "crossbuck sign" described in and specified by The Georgia Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices, section 1-111, and the stop sign described and specified in section 1-33, subparagraph 6.
(b)
Railroad trains shall not be operated at speeds in excess of 45 miles per hour within the corporate limits of the city.
(Code 1972, § 23-11; Ord. No. 89-30, § 1, 8-22-89)

Sec. 10-20-12. - Motorized carts.
(a)
Definitions. As used in this section, the following terms shall have the meanings designated below:
(1)
Licensed driver shall mean an individual with a currently valid driver's licence or any other license or permit to operate a motor vehicle issued under, or granted by, the laws of this state, except a temporary license or instruction permit.
(2)
Motorized cart means every vehicle having no less than three (3) wheels and an unladen weight of thirteen hundred (1300) pounds which cannot operate at more than twenty (20) miles per hour and which is designed to carry not more than two (2) persons, including the driver.
(3)
Street means the entire width between the boundary lines of every way publicly maintained when any part thereof is open to the use of the public for purposes of vehicular traffic.
(b)
Except as provided herein, it shall be unlawful for any person to operate a motorized cart on any street within the corporate limits of the city.
(c)
Motorized carts may be operated only by a licensed driver and only on that portion of any street within the city with a speed limit of thirty (30) miles per hour or less, and during daylight hours only.
(d)
Motorized carts shall not be operated upon any street or highway within the city under the jurisdiction of the department of transportation except at crossings or intersections designated for that purpose by the department of transportation, regardless of the speed limit upon said street or highway.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a motorized cart on any herein allowed portion of the streets of the city with a number of passengers or occupants in excess of the recommended specifications of the manufacturer of said motorized cart.
(f)
No regulation or prohibition contained within this section shall apply to motorized carts owned by golf courses, country clubs or such organized entities which own such carts and make them available to members or the public on a rental basis.
(g)
Any person who violates the terms of this section shall if convicted be punished as provided in section 1-1-5.
(h)
Any parent, legal guardian or person legally in loco parentis or other person having legal custody or control of or responsibility for a minor child who violates the terms of this section, and who has knowingly or recklessly allowed or caused said minor child to violate this section shall upon conviction be punished as provided in section 1-1-5.
(Ord. No. 00-23, § 1, 9-12-00)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 00-23, § 1, adopted Sept. 12, 2000, enacted provisions which pertained to motorized carts, designaged as § 10-20-120. Such provisions were redesignated by the editor as § 10-20-12 for purposes of classification.

(a)

(b)
Skating or riding on the sidewalks, alleyways, promenades, or any parking lot or parking deck within the downtown retail parking district (as defined in Code section 10-20-100) is prohibited. In addition to such facilities within the downtown retail parking district, this prohibition also extends to the public parking lot which abuts Broome Street and the Troup County Government Center property which abuts Ridley Avenue.
(c)
Skating or riding is allowed upon the sidewalks outside of the downtown retail parking district, however, persons so skating or riding shall not take hold of or hang onto any vehicle or in any way interfere with the natural progress of vehicles along and in the streets of the city, nor shall two (2) or more persons join hands while skating or riding on said sidewalk. In all instances, persons skating or riding on the sidewalk shall yield the right-of-way to and may not interfere with pedestrians on the sidewalks.
(d)
Any person violating subsections (a), (b) or (c) of this Code section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and upon conviction thereof shall be punished in accordance with section 1-1-6 of the Code.
(e)
No parent, legal guardian or person legally in loco parentis or other person having legal custody or control of or responsibility for a minor between six (6) and through and including sixteen (16) years of age shall at any time, through insufficient control or supervision, allow any such child to engage in an offense defined above in subsections (a), (b) or (c) hereinabove. Responsibility for the prevention of such offenses by any minor child shall be attached to the parent or other such person having legal custody or control of the child upon proof of prior notification of said child's potential to so offend. Such prior notification shall be established through confirmation by local law enforcement agency records or other evidence that the parent or other such person having legal custody or control of the child was notified, within the immediately preceding twelve (12) months, of at least two (2) previous warnings against such child for an offense referenced hereinabove in subsection (a), (b) or (c).
(f)
Any person who shall violate the provisions of subsection (d) of this section shall, after trial and upon conviction, be penalized as provided in section 1-1-6 of this Code. The municipal court may, in its discretion, consider alternate sentencing, including parenting classes, for first offenders or in other appropriate circumstances.
(Ord. No. 06-12, § 1, 6-22-06; Ord. No. 16-07, § 1, 4-26-16)
Sec. 10-20-14. - Operation of off-road vehicles.
(a)
Off-road vehicle defined. As used in this section, the term "off-road vehicle" means any motorized vehicle designed for, or capable of, cross-country travel on or immediately over land, water, snow, ice, marsh, swampland, or other natural terrain, which vehicle is not intended for use predominantly on public roads. It includes, but is not limited to, motorcycles characterized as dirt bikes, four-wheel drive or low-pressure tire vehicles, three- and four-wheel "all terrain" type vehicles, and any other means of transportation deriving power from any source other than muscle or wind. However this term shall not include any motorboat; any military, fire, or law enforcement vehicles; any vehicle used exclusively on airports; farm machinery and farm tractors; self-propelled equipment for harvesting and transportation of forest products, for clearing land, for planting crops, for utility services and maintenance, for earth moving, construction or mining; and, self-propelled lawnmowers, garden or lawn tractors, or golf carts; while such vehicles are being used exclusively for their designed purposes.
(b)
Restrictions. Any person operating an off-road vehicle under any of the following conditions shall be deemed to be in violation of this section and subject to the penalties provided elsewhere in this Code for ordinance violations. It shall be unlawful to operate an off-road vehicle:
(1)
On any private property within the express written permission of the owner or occupant of the property, or of his or her agent. Failure to post signs does not imply consent for off-road motor vehicle use on private property.
(2)
On city streets, road or utility right-of-way, or sidewalks within the city, public playgrounds, public recreational areas, and like public property.
(3)
Responsibility of vehicle owner. The owner of any off-road vehicle shall not authorize nor shall the owner permit any other person to operate such vehicle in violation of any of the provisions of this section.
(c)
Enforcement; punishment. It shall be the duty of the chief of police of the city to ensure the enforcement of this section at all times, to assure the cessation of nuisances emanating from or created by the types of vehicles herein described, which are found to be operating illegally or in a manner not consistent with this Code. Any person who shall violate this code section shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-1-6 of this Code, but the fine therefor shall not be less than five hundred dollars ($500.00) per violation.
(Ord. No. 20-16, § 1, 7-28-20)

Sec. 10-20-91. - Streets in residential zones.
(a)
In order to protect and promote the public health, safety and welfare of the citizens of the city, to insure the safe and orderly transition of traffic in residential areas and to protect property values in certain residential areas in the city, the parking of certain vehicles within residential zones ES-R, SU-R, and TN-R is prohibited upon the public streets, alleys and rights-of-way within such residential districts.
(b)
The vehicles, the parking of which is prohibited by subsection (a) are defined as:
(1)
Any truck or other vehicle which has a rated capacity of greater than one (1) ton or which has a wheel base of one hundred twenty-five (125) inches or more;
(2)
Truck tractors having dual rear wheels; and
(3)
Trailers or semitrailers having dual rear wheels, or either such trailers having an overall length of greater than twelve (12) feet; provided, however, that an exception to the prohibition outlined in this subparagraph may be granted, upon application to the building official, and in the discretion of the building official, provided said trailer or semitrailer at issue may be parked in the street safely without unduly impeding sight distance, traffic flow, federal, postal or other deliveries and emergency vehicle ingress and egress.
(c)
Nothing in this section shall prohibit the parking of vehicles of public or private utility companies, or commercial vehicles making deliveries or vehicles in use in the construction, repair or maintenance of any road, street or alley in such districts, but only for the period of time required for the delivery of service reasonably required by such vehicle.
(Code 1972, § 23-13; Ord. No. 99-30, § 1, 8-10-99; Ord. No. 99-30A, § 1, 8-10-99; Ord. No. 21-09, § 4, 6-22-21)
Sec. 10-20-92. - Parking vehicle for purpose of repair or sale.
It shall be unlawful for any person to park a vehicle on the public streets of the city for the purpose of making repairs on same. It shall be unlawful for a vehicle not in use to be parked on the public streets of the city for sale.
(Code 1972, § 23-14)
Sec. 10-20-93. - Signs, etc., to designate parking areas and manner of parking.
(a)
Parking areas and the manner of parking shall be accomplished by appropriate markings on the street, by appropriate markings on the curb, or by installation of signs. Yellow lines on the curb or a no parking sign adjacent to the street right-of-way shall signify a no parking area. In areas where parking is permitted, parking shall be in accordance with the parking lines or as shown on the street surface and where there are no markings, parking shall be parallel to the curb on the right side of such street and in a manner so as to allow the proper passage of traffic on such street.
(b)
Vehicles shall at all times be parked wholly within the parking spaces as marked upon the public streets of the city. It shall be unlawful for any person to park any vehicle so that the same is not wholly within an area designated by the lines on the streets as a parking space.
(Code 1972, §§ 23-190, 23-199)
State Law reference— How vehicles to be parked, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-200.
Sec. 10-20-94. - Parking beyond curb.
No vehicle shall be parked in the space between the property line and the curb.
(Code 1972, § 23-191)
State Law reference— How vehicles to be parked, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-200.
Sec. 10-20-95. - Alleys, driveways and intersections.
(a)
Parking of a motor vehicle in front of or which blocks the entrance to a public alley or public or private driveway is prohibited.
(b)
In order to provide for safe sight distance, it shall be unlawful to park a motor vehicle within up to thirty (30) feet on either side of the entrance to a public or private driveway. The area upon which parking is prohibited shall be clearly marked by a yellow stripe along the curb. The city manager is hereby granted the discretion to determine the distance from the entrance to the driveway upon which parking shall be prohibited in order to protect appropriate sight distance up to and including a distance of thirty (30) feet.
(Code 1972, § 23-192; Ord. No. 88-117, § 1, 10-25-88; Ord. No. 04-11, § 1, 3-23-04)

Sec. 10-20-103. - Loading/unloading spaces—Regulation of use.
(a)
The creation of loading and unloading spaces upon the public streets and thoroughfares of the city is hereby authorized in order to control and regulate the loading and unloading of vehicles upon such public streets and thoroughfares and thereby limit and reduce, as much as is possible, traffic congestion, hazards, or obstructions caused by or resulting from the uncontrolled and unregulated loading and unloading of vehicles.
(b)
Any such loading or unloading space, when designated, shall be marked by a sign identifying the same as a loading/unloading space and shall be cross-hatched with yellow stripes for additional identification.
(c)
No commercial vehicle shall use a loading/unloading space for any period in excess of thirty (30) continuous minutes and no other vehicle shall use such space for a period in excess of fifteen (15) continuous minutes; provided, however, no vehicle shall be parked in a loading/unloading space unless such vehicle is actively loading or unloading material or merchandise and then only in accordance with and for the time permitted by the provisions hereof and in no event shall such loading/unloading space be used for parking purposes only.
(d)
No vehicle shall be permitted to park or stop in a traffic lane or to obstruct traffic upon the public streets or thoroughfares of the city for the purpose of loading or unloading unless a space for that purpose or unless off-street parking which can be used for such purpose is not available within a distance of three hundred (300) feet but then only for a continuous period not to exceed fifteen (15) minutes.

Sec. 10-20-107. - Permit parking areas.
(a)
Program established. There is established in the city a permit parking program whereby motor vehicles bearing a special parking permit issued pursuant to this code section may be parked on streets or within certain areas known as permit parking areas. For the purposes of this code section, the following streets areas are designated as permitted parking only:
(1)
Ridley Avenue between Smith Street and Mitchell Avenue;
(2)
W. Bacon Street between New Franklin Road and Marshall Street; and
(3)
Highland Avenue between Ridley Avenue and N. Greenwood Street.
(b)
Application for permit. The application for a parking permit shall provide the following information:
(1)
The name of the operator of the motor vehicle to be permitted;
(2)
The operator's residential address;
(3)
The operator's state driver's license number;
(4)
The motor vehicle make, model and license number; and
(5)
Any other information reasonably requested on the application form. The applicant shall also provide proof of residency within the designated permitted parking area.
(c)
Permit term; fee. Annual parking permits shall be valid for the calendar year of issuance, will expire on December 31st of each year, and shall be issued free of charge.
(d)
Issuance of permits. The department of community development shall issue parking permits as follows: A permit shall be issued only to a motor vehicle operator who resides or operates a business on property that is located within the designated permit parking area, which permit shall be issued in accordance with the following limitations:
(1)
For single-family dwelling units (one residential structure containing only one housing unit) or commercial uses:
One parking permit may be issued for each valid street address where said dwelling unit or commercial use contains off-street parking.
(2)
For multiple family dwelling units (including but not limited to, apartments, condominiums, attached dwellings, row houses and townhouses):
One parking permit may be issued for each legally constituted and existing dwelling unit in any two-family or multiple-family dwelling facility at a valid street address within the permit parking area where said dwelling unit contains off-street parking availability.
(3)
Additional parking permits may be issued, for good and sufficient cause, to any resident or business operator within the permit parking area if, in the opinion of the LaGrange Police Department, exceptional circumstances exist warranting an additional permit issuance.
(e)
Permit form and display.
(1)
The parking permit shall be a hang tag type and shall be displayed only on the motor vehicle for which it was issued in accordance with the limitations of use. The permit shall be prominently displayed by hanging from the rear view mirror so that is clearly visible from outside the vehicle. Information shown on the permit shall include the expiration date.
(2)
Visitors to a residence within the permitted parking area may utilize the registered owner's parking permit while visiting said residence.
(f)
The department of community development office may issue a temporary parking permit, valid for not more than one year, for a vehicle operated by a person who provides health care services on a regular basis at an address within a permit parking area. Such temporary permit shall be a permit bearing special validation and may be issued upon satisfactory proof of need presented by the resident at such address.
(g)
Limitations of use. A motor vehicle which displays a valid parking permit shall be allowed to stand or park in the parking permit area for which the permit has been issued. Such permits shall not authorize a motor vehicle to stand or park in any place where or during any time when the stopping, standing or parking of a motor vehicle is otherwise prohibited or the area is designated for other uses.
(h)
Signage. Signs will be erected in each permitted parking area indicating that the street is designated as permitted parking only and that parking is prohibited without a permit.
(i)
Penalties for violations.
(1)
It shall be unlawful for any person to stand or park any motor vehicle on any street within the permitted parking area which does not display a valid parking permit. However, a public utility vehicle or commercial vehicle which is actively engaged in utility work or delivery or picking up of items is exempt from obtaining a permit and will not be in violation of this section.
(2)
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this code section for a person to furnish false information in an application for any permit authorized by this section or to represent falsely that such person is eligible for such permit.
(3)
A person holding a valid parking permit issued pursuant to this code section shall not be allowed the use or display of such permit on a vehicle other than that for which the permit was issued or a visitor to his/her residence or business.
(4)
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this code section for a person to produce or display a facsimile or counterfeit parking permit in order to evade the limitations on parking in a permit parking area.
(5)
Violation of this section shall constitute an offense and shall be punishable as provided under Code section 1-1-6. In addition to said penalty, a vehicle found to be in violation of this code section is subject to immediate removal, at the owner's expense, by a law enforcement officer.
(Ord. No. 16-01, § 1, 3-22-16)

Sec. 10-20-111. - Illegally parked vehicles, owner/driver citation.
For parking violations under article III of this chapter, citation may issue to either the registered owner of the vehicle or the driver of the vehicle at the time of the alleged violation. Proof that the defendant was at the time of the violation the registered owner of the vehicle shall permit the trier of fact in its discretion to infer that such owner of the vehicle was the driver of the vehicle at the time of the alleged violation. Such an inference may be rebutted if the owner of the vehicle:
(1)
Testifies under oath in open court that he or she was not the operator of the vehicle at the time of the alleged violation;
(2)
Presents to the court prior to the return dates established on the citation a certified copy of a police report showing that the vehicle had been reported to law enforcement as stolen prior to the alleged violation; or
(3)
Submits to the court prior to the return date established on the citation a sworn notarized statement identifying the name of the operator of the vehicle at the time of the alleged violation.
(Ord. No. 04-12, § 1, 3-23-04)
