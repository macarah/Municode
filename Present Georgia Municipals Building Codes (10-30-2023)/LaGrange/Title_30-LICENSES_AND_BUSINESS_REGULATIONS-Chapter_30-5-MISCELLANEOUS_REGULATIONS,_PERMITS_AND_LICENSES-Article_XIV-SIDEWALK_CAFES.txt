Editor's note— Ord. No. 14-11, § 3, adopted October 14, 2014, repealed the former Art. XIV, §§ 30-5-315—30-5-321, and enacted a new Art. XIV as set out herein. The former Art. XIV pertained to similar subject matter and derived from Ord. No. 96-66, adopted December 19, 1996.

Sec. 30-5-315. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the following words shall have the meanings as ascribed to them in this section unless the context clearly indicates otherwise:
DT-MX downtown mixed use district means and refers to that area of the city which is now or hereafter zoned DT-MX downtown mixed use district.
Sidewalk cafe. means the location and use of tables and chairs and other associated equipment and furnishings on the public sidewalk or public pedestrian way and operated as an extension of a licensed food service establishment in the DT-MX downtown mixed use district of the city, which sidewalk cafe shall be an incidental activity of the establishment.
(Ord. No. 14-11, § 3, 10-14-14; Ord. No. 21-09, § 3, 6-22-21; Ord. No. 21-18, § 1, 11-9-21)
Sec. 30-5-316. - Permit.
No person shall operate a sidewalk cafe in the DT-MX downtown mixed use district without a permit issued by the city manager. Such permit shall be renewed on an annual basis in accord with the schedule for administrative, license and occupation fees set forth in section 30-1-9 of the Code.
(Ord. No. 14-11, § 3, 10-14-14; Ord. No. 21-09, § 3, 6-22-21)
Sec. 30-5-317. - Application.
Any person desiring to operate a sidewalk cafe in the DT-MX downtown mixed use district shall submit an application, on a form prescribed by the department of community development, setting forth the name of the food service establishment, the name of the owner of the establishment, the name of the individual applying for the permit, the number and type of tables, chairs, and associated equipment and furnishings requested to be allowed pursuant to the permit, and such other and further information as the director deems appropriate to fully describe the planned operation.
(Ord. No. 14-11, § 3, 10-14-14; Ord. No. 21-09, § 3, 6-22-21; Ord. No. 21-18, § 2, 11-9-21)
Sec. 30-5-318. - Permit fee.
The annual fee for a permit to operate a sidewalk cafe in the DT-MX downtown mixed use district shall be twenty-five dollars ($25.00).
(Ord. No. 14-11, § 3, 10-14-14; Ord. No. 21-09, § 3, 6-22-21)
Sec. 30-5-319. - Regulations.
The operation of a sidewalk cafe pursuant to a permit issued hereunder shall comply with the following regulations:
(1)
Limitations on area. The area in which a sidewalk cafe is operated shall not extend so far from the outside wall of the establishment to prevent a minimum of six (6) feet of unobstructed pedestrian traffic on the sidewalk between the sidewalk cafe area and the curb or nearest obstacle. For establishments which adjoin a public pedestrian space other than a conventional sidewalk, the area in which a sidewalk cafe is operated shall be located and be of such dimensions as in the discretion of the city manager will not unduly encroach upon said public space.
(2)
Sanitation. The area in which the sidewalk cafe is operated shall be constantly maintained so that it is free of trash, garbage, discarded food products or other debris. Cleaning the sidewalk, sidewalk pavers or paved surface in the area in which a sidewalk cafe is operated shall be the responsibility of the permit holder. Any and all damage, stains or discoloration of sidewalk or sidewalk pavers must be repaired and/or replaced at the expense of the sidewalk cafe operator. The operator of a sidewalk cafe shall provide sufficient trash receptacles for use by the operation and shall not use public trash receptacles in conjunction with the operation.
(3)
Reserved.
(4)
Liability and insurance. Any applicant requesting a permit to operate a sidewalk cafe pursuant to this article shall agree in writing, upon a form required by the city manager, to hold the city harmless for and against all claims resulting from operation of the sidewalk cafe, and shall also file with the city manager proof of general liability insurance in the amount of one million dollars ($1,000,000.00) covering such operation on which the city shall be listed as an additional insured.
(5)
Furniture removal. The city may require removal of all tables and chairs used in conjunction with operation of the sidewalk cafe for inclement weather or as may otherwise be deemed necessary by the city.
(6)
Lighting and miscellaneous. Adequate exterior lighting must be provided for use of a sidewalk cafe area in operation between dusk and dawn. Only chairs, tables, umbrellas and other approved associated equipment and furnishings are authorized for placement on sidewalks. Neither food preparation nor food service appurtenances shall be allowed within the area.
(7)
Alcoholic beverages/open containers. Sidewalk cafe permit holders shall be exempt from the restrictions of the open container prohibition of code section 30-20-15 if such establishment is otherwise licensed to sell alcoholic beverages under all applicable laws and ordinances. All relevant provisions of chapter 30-20 (Alcoholic Beverages) of the code shall be enforced regarding the sale, service, possession, and consumption of alcoholic beverages.
(8)
Smoking. Those businesses which are prohibited by state or local law from allowing smoking within the interior of their establishment may not allow smoking within the area permitted for a sidewalk cafe.
(Ord. No. 14-11, § 3, 10-14-14; Ord. No. 20-15, § 1, 7-28-20; Ord. No. 21-18, § 3, 11-9-21)
Sec. 30-5-320. - Revocation and suspension of permit.
The approval of a sidewalk cafe permit by the city manager is conditional. A sidewalk cafe permit may be revoked or suspended by the city manager, in his discretion, if it is found that the permit is not in compliance with any of the city's regulations with respect to sidewalk cafes or alcoholic beverages, or upon violation of any such related ordinance or regulation. Such decision may be appealed to the mayor and council under the provisions of Code section 30-20-14.
(Ord. No. 14-11, § 3, 10-14-14)
Sec. 30-5-321. - Standards.
The city manager shall have the authority to prescribe standards for the type, color and quality of tables, chairs, umbrellas or other approved associated equipment and furnishings used in conjunction with a sidewalk cafe as herein defined. A copy of such standards shall remain on file in the office of the city manager.
(Ord. No. 14-11, § 3, 10-14-14; Ord. No. 21-18, § 4, 11-9-21)
Sec. 30-5-322. - Alcohol awareness training.
(a)
No sidewalk cafe permit shall issue for a restaurant where alcohol is served until the manager of the licensed establishment shall have first attended an alcohol awareness training session approved by the city manager that consists of at least two (2) hours of instruction on the sale and service of alcoholic beverages; recognition and identification of persons who are intoxicated; recognition and identification of persons who are not safe to drive a vehicle; recognition of persons who are under the influence of alcohol to the extent they are a danger to themselves or others; and recognition of minors.
(b)
All employees of a sidewalk cafe where alcohol is served who are directly involved in the sale or serving of alcoholic beverages, prior to said sale or service, shall have first attended an alcohol awareness training session of the same duration and including the same areas of instruction as is required for the manager in subsection (a) above.
(c)
A certificate of completion of the training as required by this code section shall be filed with the department of community development.
Secs. 30-5-323—30-5-329. - Reserved.
