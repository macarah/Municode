Chapter 10-21 - ENVIRONMENT
Article I - IN GENERAL

Sec. 10-21-1. - Intent.
It is declared to be the intent of the governing authority of the city, in furtherance of its responsibility to protect the public health, safety and welfare of its citizens, to enact the following regulations governing the maintenance of property and the removal of hazards therefrom, all in order to assure the prevention and abatement of nuisances.
(Ord. No. 97-4, § 2, 1-14-97)
Sec. 10-21-2. - Definitions.
Except as specifically defined in this section, all words used in this chapter have their customary dictionary definitions. Unless otherwise expressly stated, the following words shall have the meaning herein indicated:
Ashes shall mean the residue from the burning of wood, coal or other combustible material.
Breeding places shall mean a mound or mass of construction debris, garbage, trash, cut vegetation or woody material.
Buffer shall mean a ten-foot wide strip of land maintained in such a manner that the growth of vegetation does not interfere with the property of another or exceed twelve (12) inches in height.
Developed property shall mean property upon which a structure has been erected which requires permanent location on the ground or which is attached to something having permanent location on the ground.
Filth shall mean foul, rotten or putrid matter which causes offensive odors and creates unhealthy conditions.
Garbage shall mean putrescible animal and vegetable waste resulting from the handling, preparation, cooking and consumption of food.
Inoperable vehicle shall mean any vehicle that is resting on blocks, without tires, with major parts missing, in a state of repair which precludes use, or lacking required current registration certificate.
Litter shall mean any kind of discarded and uncontrolled or uncontained solid waste, garbage, trash, dirt, sand, gravel, refuse, debris, dead animal or thing or substance, whether liquid or solid, that may be carried or deposited, in whole or in part, by wind, rain, snow, water, animal or any other such force into any public or private property, or into any river, public waterway, drain, sewer or receiving basin.
Livestock means all animals of the equine, bovine or swine class, including goats, sheep, mules, horses, hogs, cattle, and other grazing animals, and all rarities, including, but not limited to, ostriches, emus and rheas.
Person shall mean firms, partnerships, associations, organizations, trusts, companies and bodies, politic and corporate, or any other group of individuals acting as a unit, or any combination thereof, as well as individuals.
Poultry shall mean domestic fowl, including, but not limited to, water fowl such as geese and duck, birds which are bred for meat and egg production, exhibition, or competition; game birds such as pheasants, partridge, quail and grouse, as well as guinea fowl, pigeons, dove, peafowl; and all other avian species.
Private property shall mean property privately owned by one (1) or more persons.
Public property shall mean any street, alley, sidewalk or other public thoroughfare in the city or property owned and controlled by any governmental entity.
Refuse shall mean all putrescible or nonputrescible waste (except body waste) including, but not limited to, garbage, rubbish, ashes, street cleanings, dead animals, abandoned automobiles, equipment, appliances, machinery, and solid market and industrial waste.
Rubbish shall mean nonputrescible waste (excluding ashes) consisting of both combustible and noncombustible waste, including, but not limited to, paper, cardboard, cans, yard clippings, wood, woody tree cuttings, glass, bedding, crockery and similar materials.
Running at large shall mean any livestock which is not under manual control of a person and which is on any public road of the city or any property not belonging to the owner of the livestock, unless by permission of the owner of such property.
Street shall mean the entire width between boundary lines of every right-of-way publicly maintained when any part thereof is open to the use of the public for purposes of vehicular or pedestrian travel.
Trash shall mean organic and inorganic waste that is too large or heavy to be routinely broken down, compressed, or otherwise disassembled to fit into the normal solid waste containers authorized for use by a household or business for the collection of its waste. Trash includes, but is not limited to, items such as lumber, bedding, furniture and garden tools, but does not include yard trimmings.
Undeveloped property shall mean property that does not have upon it anything constructed or erected which requires permanent location on the ground or which is attached to something having a permanent location on the ground.
Vegetation shall mean grass, weeds, or plants having no permanent woody stem less than one (1) inch in diameter at breast height and showing no visible signs of having been maintained, as distinguished from a tree or shrub.
(Ord. No. 97-4, § 2, 1-14-97)
Sec. 10-21-3. - Penalty for violation.
Any person found to be in violation of this chapter shall be subject to the penalties as described in section 1-1-6 of the Code.
(Ord. No. 97-4, § 2, 1-14-97)
Secs. 10-21-4—10-21-13. - Reserved.
Article II - ENVIRONMENTAL STANDARDS

Sec. 10-21-14. - Inoperable vehicles.
(a)
Except as provided herein, the open storage of inoperable or unclaimed operable vehicles on private property is prohibited.
(b)
Any person lawfully engaged in the repair of damaged or inoperable vehicles may store such vehicles on private property for a period not to exceed one hundred eighty (180) days, providing that such vehicles shall be screened from view on all sides of the property upon which the vehicles are stored by a building, fence, landscaping or other visual buffer.
(c)
Only a person lawfully engaged in the operation of a junkyard or salvage yard in accordance with the zoning and other requirements of the City Code shall be authorized to store inoperable, damaged or unclaimed vehicles for a period exceeding one hundred eighty (180) days. Such vehicles shall be screened from view by a fence or shrubbery on all sides of the property upon which the vehicles are stored.
(d)
A vehicle lacking a current, valid Georgia tag shall be considerable inoperable for the purposes of this section.
(e)
For the purposes of this section, either the owner of the real property upon which such vehicle is situated, or the lawful possessor in control of said real property, or both, may be held liable for violation of this section.
(Ord. No. 97-4, § 2, 1-14-97; Ord. No. 01-22, § 1, 9-11-01; Ord. No. 01-33, § 1, 12-11-01)
Sec. 10-21-15. - Health standards.
All developed and undeveloped property shall be maintained in such a manner to prevent the establishing of breeding places for small animals or insects that are destructive, annoying or injurious to the public health.
(Ord. No. 97-4, § 2, 1-14-97)
Sec. 10-21-16. - Animals.
No person shall livestock or poultry to run at large in the city.
(Ord. No. 97-4, § 2, 1-14-97)
Sec. 10-21-17. - Property maintenance.
The growth of grass shall not exceed one (1) foot in height on any developed property. The growth of vegetation on any undeveloped property shall not encroach upon any developed property adjacent thereto. Undeveloped property lying adjacent to and bordering developed property shall provide a ten-foot buffer with vegetation no greater than one (1) foot in height.
(Ord. No. 97-4, § 2, 1-14-97)
Sec. 10-21-18. - Collections of filth.
No person shall allow the collection of filth, such as but not limited to, stagnant water, decayed vegetables and fruits, dead animals, tainted meat, sewage or excrement of any kind to remain on land owned or controlled by him.
(Ord. No. 97-4, § 2, 1-14-97)
Sec. 10-21-19. - Littering.
No person shall dump, deposit or throw litter, including but not limited to refuse, garbage, rubbish, construction or demolition debris, woody or vegetative debris, appliances, equipment of any type or inoperable vehicles, or trash, on any public or private property.
(Ord. No. 97-4, § 2, 1-14-97)
Sec. 10-21-20. - Streets.
No person shall cast, place, sweep or deposit anywhere within the city any refuse in such a manner that it may be carried or deposited by the elements upon any street, public right-of-way, sidewalk, alley, sewer or onto any occupied premises.
(Ord. No. 97-4, § 2, 1-14-97)
Sec. 10-21-21. - Washing or repairing vehicles.
No person shall wash for profit or gain or repair any vehicle on the streets, sidewalks or rights-of-way in the city. Sidewalks in front of all properties shall be kept clean from oil and grease. Businesses or persons engaged in washing, repair or maintenance of vehicles shall collect all wash water in a system connected to the city sanitary sewer system. Such sewer connections must have a properly installed interceptor or separator as may be required by the building official.
(Ord. No. 97-4, § 2, 1-14-97)
Sec. 10-21-22. - Bringing fertilizer materials into city.
It shall be unlawful for any person to bring into the city any conveyance containing blood and bones, tankage, fish scrap or any odorous material for making fertilizers, and to allow the same to remain within the city for more than two (2) hours. It shall be unlawful for any railroad company to keep any car containing these materials on its tracks or premises at any one (1) place within the city for more than thirty (30) minutes.
(Ord. No. 97-4, § 2, 1-14-97)
Sec. 10-21-23. - Uncovered wells.
All wells not properly covered, on enclosed or unclosed lots in the city, are hereby declared nuisances, and it shall be unlawful for any person to have an uncovered well on his premises or to refuse or neglect to cover the same and properly protect all persons and animals from danger of any open well on his premises within six (6) hours of notice so to do by any authorized officer of the city.
(Ord. No. 97-4, § 2, 1-14-97)
Sec. 10-21-24. - Burning leaves, rubbish.
(a)
Leaves, rubbish or refuse of any kind shall not be set on fire or burned on any paved street or sidewalk.
(b)
All authorized open burn permits issued by the LaGrange Fire Department may be revoked and such fire immediately extinguished if such is deemed a danger to public safety, reveals signs of environmental harm, or upon substantiated nuisance complaints from neighbors or other citizens.
(c)
Control burns shall only be authorized between 10:00 a.m. and sunset.
(Ord. No. 97-4, § 2, 1-14-97)
Sec. 10-21-25. - Notice to correct conditions.
If it is determined that any of the conditions specified in sections 10-21-17 (property maintenance) and 10-21-18 (collections of filth) exist on any property within the city, the responsible party (owner or occupant) of said property shall be notified in writing that the conditions exist, and that if the conditions are not abated within fifteen (15) days after receipt of such notice, the city may direct that such conditions be abated at city expense. If, after diligent search, the responsible party cannot be located then said notice may be posted on the property and at such other public places in the city as the building official or his designee may determine.
(Ord. No. 11-11, § 1, 6-30-11)
Sec. 10-21-26. - [Liens].
If the conditions specified in sections 10-21-17 (property maintenance) and 10-21-18 (collections of filth) are abated at city expense as provided in section 10-21-25, the city shall assess a lien against the property for the costs described in this section. Said lien shall be filed in the office of the clerk of the superior court and recorded as other liens are recorded. A copy of the lien shall also be kept in the office of the assistant city manager. The actual direct cost of abating the conditions remedied, together with the necessary expenses for preparing, filing and recording the lien, including any legal expenses, shall be assessed against the property and shall be collected in the same manner as provided for the collection of unpaid ad valorem taxes.
(Ord. No. 11-11, § 1, 6-30-11; Ord. No. 16-12, § 4, 12-13-16)
Secs. 10-21-27—10-21-34. - Reserved.
Article III - NUISANCES

Sec. 10-21-35. - Generally.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to create a nuisance on his premises or premises occupied by him, or to permit a nuisance to remain six (6) hours after notice. The following, among others, are declared to be nuisances: Stagnant water, decayed vegetables, decayed fruits and fruit refuse, dead animals or tainted meat, filthy privies, filthy water or excrement, broken or ruptured sewer lines, raw sewage or anything that causes offensive odors or that works hurt, damage or inconvenience to another; the conducting by pipes, sewers or otherwise into any street, public alley or on the lot of another, of any filthy water or excrement of any kind, such as comes from kitchens, bathtubs, laundry places, privies, stables and other places.
(b)
All nuisances and dangerous objects in and about the streets and other public places may be removed at once by the city, without any notice, and the expense incurred in the removal shall be paid by the owner of the dangerous objects or of the person maintaining the nuisance.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for the person creating or maintaining any nuisance within the city to fail or refuse to abate and remove same after having been ordered to do so in accordance with the provisions of the charter and ordinances of the city.
(d)
Any nuisance on private property may be removed on the written complaint of any officer or inhabitant of the city, which complaint must set forth particularly the nature and location of same and the person complained against. On such complaint being filed with the municipal court judge, he shall issue a summons to the person charged with maintaining such nuisance, requiring him to show cause before the municipal court judge at some time therein specified, within not more than five (5) days, why said nuisance complained of should not be abated. Such notice shall be served personally by any policeman at least one (1) day before the hearing. At the trial, the court shall hear evidence as to the facts and pass an order as may be just. If the thing complained of is a nuisance, the order shall specify within what time it is to be abated by the defendant. If it is not abated within the time specified in the order, the court shall issue a writ directed to the chief of police directing and requiring the abatement of the nuisance.
(e)
Whenever the person creating or maintaining any nuisance within the city shall fail to abate after notice to do so, and it shall become necessary to issue a writ, directed to the chief of police, requiring him to remove same, it shall be the duty of the chief of police to keep an account of the necessary expenses incurred and to render to the court a statement of same. The court shall issue an execution against such order for such amount, the same to be levied upon and collected in the same manner as is provided for the levy in collection of executions for taxes.
(Ord. No. 97-4, § 2, 1-14-97)
