Editor's note— Ord. No. 09-10, § 1, adopted June 25, 2009, repealed the former Art. VII, Amusement Rides, § 30-5-181, and enacted a new Art. VII, Carnivals, as set out herein.

Sec. 30-5-181. - Amusement activity defined.
Amusement activity means an enterprise principally devoted to offering amusement or entertainment to the public for a price in, upon, or by means of amusement rides in any number or combination, whether or not associated with other structures or forms of public attraction, and which is located at a temporary location.
(Ord. No. 09-10, § 1, 6-25-09)
Sec. 30-5-182. - License required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to hold, maintain, conduct or sponsor any amusement activity within the meaning of this article without having first obtained a license and having complied with the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 09-10, § 1, 6-25-09)
Sec. 30-5-183. - Application.
All persons desiring a license to conduct an amusement activity shall make written application upon forms available in the office of community development. Such application shall state the name and address of the applicant; the place where the proposed amusement activity is to be located; the nature and character of the activity to be carried on; if a partnership, the names of the partners; and, if a corporation, the names of the corporate officers. The application shall further include the general plan and layout of how the area concerned is to be used, to include parking facilities, and, if applicable, proof that the amusement activity is possessed of a valid permit from the State of Georgia pursuant to the Amusement Ride Safety Act.
(Ord. No. 09-10, § 1, 6-25-09)
Sec. 30-5-184. - Insurance requirement.
If for any reason the operator of an amusement activity is not required to have in force liability insurance or other surety pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 34-12-14 (Amusement Ride Safety Act), said applicant shall also present to the city evidence of public liability insurance in an amount not less than three hundred thousand dollars ($300,000.00) for injury to one (1) person; two million dollars ($2,000,000.00) for all injuries arising from one (1) accident; and fifty thousand dollars ($50,000.00) for property damages, such insurance to be written by a company authorized to transact business in the State of Georgia.
(Ord. No. 09-10, § 1, 6-25-09)
Sec. 30-5-185. - Condition of premises; orderly operation.
All premises of an amusement activity shall be kept clean and in proper sanitary condition and, when food is served in connection with the amusement activity, full compliance shall be maintained with the provisions and regulations governing the conditions of premises used for the storage and sale of food for human consumption.
(Ord. No. 09-10, § 1, 6-25-09)
Sec. 30-5-186. - Issuance; fee; duration.
If the license application meets the conditions set forth in this article, a license shall be issued for the designated location after payment of a regulatory fee in the amount of one hundred dollars ($100.00). Any license issued under this article shall not be valid beyond a period of fourteen (14) days.
(Ord. No. 09-10, § 1, 6-25-09)
Secs. 30-5-187—30-5-200. - Reserved.
