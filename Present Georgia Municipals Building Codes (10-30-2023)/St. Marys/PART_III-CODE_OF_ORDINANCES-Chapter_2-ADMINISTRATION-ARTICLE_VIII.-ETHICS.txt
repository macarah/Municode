DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 2-351. - Definitions.
As used in this division, the term:
Business means any corporation, partnership, proprietorship, organization, self-employed individual and any other entity operated for economic gain, whether professional, industrial or commercial, and entities which for purposes of federal income taxation are treated as nonprofit organizations.
Complaint means a written statement filed with the mayor or mayor pro tem containing specific allegations of misconduct by a member; provided, however, such allegations must be filed within six months of discovery of the alleged misconduct.
Confidential information means information which has been obtained in the course of holding public office, employment, an independent contract or otherwise acting as an official or employee, and which information is not available to members of the public under state law or other law or regulation and which the official or employee is not authorized to disclose.
Governing authority or member of the governing authority means the mayor or any council member of the city and any member appointed to any board, authority, or commission of the city.
Interest means any direct pecuniary benefit, which is not a remote or slight interest, held by or accruing to a member of the governing authority as a result of a contract or transaction that is or may be the subject of an official act or action by or with the city. A member of the governing authority shall be deemed to have an interest in transactions involving:
(1)
Any person in the member's immediate family (spouse, domestic partner, mother, father, sister, brother, and natural or adopted children);
(2)
Any person with whom a contractual relationship exists whereby the member may receive any payment or other benefits unless the member is receiving a benefit for goods or services in the normal course of business for which the member has paid a commercially reasonable rate;
(3)
Any business in which the member is a director, officer, employee, agent or shareholder, except as otherwise provided herein;
(4)
Any person of whom the member is a creditor, whether secured or unsecured.
(Ord. of 11-10-08(2), § 1; Ord. of 8-24-09, § 1)
Sec. 2-352. - Prohibitions.
(a)
No member of the governing authority shall:
(1)
By conduct give reasonable basis for the impression that any person can improperly influence him/her or unduly enjoy his/her favor in the performance of official acts.
(2)
Directly or indirectly request, receive or agree to receive a gift, loan, favor, promise or thing of value for himself/herself or another person if it could reasonably be considered to influence the member in the discharge of official duties.
(3)
Disclose or otherwise use confidential information acquired by virtue of his/her official position for his/her or another person's private gain.
(4)
Use his/her official position to attempt to secure privileges that are not available to the general public.
(5)
Engage in, accept employment with, or render services for any private business or professional activity when such employment or rendering of services is adverse to and incompatible with the proper discharge of official duties.
(6)
Engage in any activity or transaction that is prohibited by law now existing or hereafter enacted, which is applicable to him/her by virtue of being a member of the governing authority.
(7)
Use his/her position to request or require a city employee to:
a.
Do clerical work on behalf of the member's family, business, social, church or fraternal interest when such work is not furthering a city interest;
b.
Perform any work outside the employee's normal course of municipal employment;
c.
Purchase goods or services to be used for personal, business or political purposes; and
d.
Work for the member personally without paying the city employee just compensation.
(8)
Use government property of any kind for other than officially approved activities, nor shall he/she direct city employees to use such property for any purposes other than those officially approved.
(9)
Use his/her position in any way to coerce, or give the appearance of coercing, another person to provide any financial benefit to himself/herself or persons having an interest.
(10)
Order any goods and services for the city without prior applicable required authorization for such expenditure.
(11)
Draw per diem or expense monies from the city to attend a seminar, convention or conference and then fail to attend the seminar, convention or conference without refunding the pro-rata unused per diem or expense monies to the city.
(12)
Use the city attorney or attorneys who are under retainer by the city for personal or private business without paying just compensation.
(b)
Subsection (a) of this section shall not apply in the case of:
(1)
An occasional nonpecuniary gift of insignificant value.
(2)
An award publicly presented in recognition of public service.
(3)
A commercially reasonable loan or other financial transaction made in the ordinary course of business by an institution or individual authorized by the laws of Georgia to engage in the making of such a loan or financial transaction.
(4)
Campaign contributions made and reported in accordance with Georgia law.
(5)
The borrowing of funds from any bank or lending institution, which offers the lowest available rate of interest in the community for such loan. This section shall not apply in the case of the designation of a bank or trust company as a depository for city funds.
(6)
Contracts entered into under circumstances that constitute an emergency situation, provided that the mayor prepares a written record explaining the emergency.
(7)
Contracts entered into with a member of the governing authority, or with a business in which a member of the governing authority has an interest, provided that such contract is the result of a competitive bid, disclosure of the nature of such member's interest is made prior to the time any bid is submitted, and a waiver of the prohibition contemplated by this section is issued by the city manager following disclosure.
(c)
Subsection (a) of this section shall not apply to a member of the governing authority who is a licensed professional and appears on behalf of any applicant in such professional capacity so long as disclosures required by section 2-353, are made to the council or mayor 30 days prior to any action being taken, and the member is associated with the project at the time the initial application is filed.
(Ord. of 11-10-08(2), § 1; Ord. of 8-24-09, § 1)
Sec. 2-353. - Disclosure of conflicts of interest.
(a)
An appointed member of the governing authority who has an interest that he/she has reason to believe may be affected by his/her official acts or actions or by the official acts or actions of the governing authority shall disclose the precise nature of such interest by written or verbal statement 30 days prior to the governing authority's taking official action on a matter affecting such interest and abstain from discussion and voting.
(b)
An elected member of the governing authority shall disclose the nature of any interest he/she has at the time such matter is presented to mayor and council for discussion. Such written or verbal statements shall be recorded into the minutes of the meeting and thus become part of the public record. Following any disclosure, the member shall refrain from all ex parte communications with other members regarding the matter in which he/she has an interest.
(Ord. of 11-10-08(2), § 1; Ord. of 8-24-09, § 1)
Sec. 2-354. - Disqualification.
A member of the governing authority shall disqualify himself/herself from participating in any official act or action of the city in which he/she has an interest, when such pecuniary benefit is not available to the public at large.
(Ord. of 11-10-08(2), § 1; Ord. of 8-24-09, § 1)
Sec. 2-355. - Prohibited contracts.
The city shall not enter into any contract involving property or services with a member of the governing authority or with a business in which a member of the governing authority has an interest, except as may be approved in advance by the consent of a majority of the other members of the mayor and council.
(Ord. of 11-10-08(2), § 1; Ord. of 8-24-09, § 1)
Sec. 2-356. - Restrictions on contracts with former members of the governing authority.
The city shall not enter into any contract with any person or business represented by a person, who has been within the preceding 12-month period a member of the governing authority, unless a contract is awarded as a result of a competitive bid, or a committee selection process, or as may be approved in advance by the consent of a majority of the members of the mayor and council.
(Ord. of 11-10-08(2), § 1; Ord. of 8-24-09, § 1)

DIVISION 2. - ADMINISTRATION

Sec. 2-371. - Complaints; investigating committee.
(a)
Any person having a complaint against any member of the governing authority for an alleged violation under this article shall file in writing a complaint using the approved official ethics complaint form, setting forth the particular facts and circumstances which constitute the alleged violation against the governing authority. The complaint shall be filed with the mayor, or in the event the complaint regards the mayor, shall be filed with the mayor pro tem. No action will be taken on any compliant received unless said complaint uses the official City of St. Marys Board of Ethics Complaint Form, properly completed including name, address and signature of complainant.
(b)
Upon receipt of the complaint, the mayor or the mayor pro tem, if the complaint regards the mayor, shall appoint three members of the board of ethics who shall constitute an investigating committee to determine whether the complaint sets forth significant facts and circumstances so as to warrant a hearing before the board of ethics. Appointment to the investigating committee shall be made on a rotational basis commencing at the top of the official list of members on file with the city clerk.
(c)
The investigating committee shall conduct an investigation, which in all circumstances, must at least include interviewing the member of the governing authority who is the subject of the complaint. The investigating committee shall provide a written report to the board of ethics within 30 days of appointment. The mayor, or mayor pro tem if the complaint regards the mayor, shall approve an extension of time for reporting if exigent circumstances exist.
(d)
In the event the complaint does not set forth sufficient facts to constitute an alleged violation and is found unjustified, frivolous or patently unfounded, the investigating committee shall make a recommendation to dismiss to the board of ethics. In the event the complaint is found to state sufficient facts to warrant a hearing before the board of ethics, the board shall take action as provided herein.
(e)
The complaint shall not be announced to the public until it is found to be justified by the investigating committee, but shall be subject to open records under Georgia law.
(f)
The investigating committee shall review any additional complaint filed against any member of the governing authority even if the additional complaint is based upon the same act or occurrence of another complaint. No member of the governing authority shall be subject to a hearing based on additional complaints regarding the same act or occurrence already decided upon in which no violation is found, unless the investigating committee, relying upon all files and records related to the original complaint, determines the additional complaint sets forth sufficient facts to constitute an alleged violation that is separate from the original act or occurrence or that it sets forth new information that may justify a new hearing before the board of ethics.
(Ord. of 11-10-08(2), § 1; Ord. of 3-9-09, § 1; Ord. of 8-24-09, § 1)
Sec. 2-372. - Creation, composition and authority of board of ethics.
(a)
The board of ethics shall be comprised of eight members. The mayor and each city council member shall appoint one person who shall be a resident of the city and who shall possess strong professional leadership. The mayor or councilperson shall appoint these members of the board at the beginning of each term of office for the mayor or councilperson. Said board member shall serve as a board member during the mayor or council members' term of office. One member shall be appointed by the Camden County Bar Association and be an attorney and good standing member of the State Bar of Georgia.
(b)
The board of ethics shall meet as required under its own procedures; however, the board shall meet at least one time per year in January in order to elect a chair to serve for that calendar year. Such meetings shall be held at city hall and shall be conducted as required by the Georgia Open Meetings Act. The board shall conduct its annual meeting and any general administrative business meetings with at least five or more members of the board of ethics present. However, an official hearing shall only be conducted with a quorum which shall consist of at least five members, one of which shall be the member appointed by the Camden County Bar Association, and a majority vote of the board members sitting at any meeting or hearing shall govern the decisions of the board.
(c)
The governing authority of the city shall provide meeting space for the board of ethics. Subject to budgetary procedures and requirements of the city, the city shall provide the board of ethics with such supplies and equipment as may be reasonably necessary for it to perform its duties and responsibilities.
(d)
Any member may be removed from office for any misfeasance, malfeasance or nonfeasance and for any cause which, in the opinion of the St. Marys City Council, casts doubt upon the members ability to perform his or her duties effectively and without favor. Prior to removal of any member of the board of ethics, the member shall be furnished with written notice of the charges against him or her and shall be afforded an opportunity for hearing after reasonable notice served personally or by mail. The notice shall include time, place and nature of the hearing. The member so charged shall be given the right to present evidence in his or her own behalf and only after a finding by a majority of the City Council of St. Marys, that good cause for removal exists, i.e., misfeasance, malfeasance or nonfeasance, may a member be removed. If a pending issue is before the board of ethics at the time notice is given to any member, said pending issue will be tabled pending resolution of the charge or charges against the member of the board of ethics. Once removed from the board, a former member may not be re-appointed for five years.
(Ord. of 11-10-08(2), § 1; Ord. of 3-9-09, § 2; Ord. of 8-24-09, § 1; Ord. of 2-22-11; Ord. of 6-20-11)
Sec. 2-373. - Board of ethics powers and duties.
(a)
The constituted board of ethics shall have the following duties and powers:
(1)
To establish procedures, rules and regulations governing its internal organization and conduct of its affairs.
(2)
To hold a hearing, if necessary, within 60 days after the board's receipt of written report from the investigating committee. The mayor, or mayor pro tem if the complaint regards the mayor, shall approve an extension of time for reporting if exigent circumstances exist.
(3)
To adopt forms, issue subpoenas, and take testimony. All testimony shall be under oath which may be administered by a member of the board.
(4)
To prescribe forms, approved by the city attorney, for the disclosure required in this division and to make available to the public information disclosed as provided in this section.
(5)
To receive and hear complaints of violations of the standards required by this division.
(6)
To make such investigation and response to a complaint as it deems necessary to determine whether any person has violated any provisions of this division.
(7)
To hold such hearings and make such inquiries as deemed necessary to investigate and rule upon complaints.
(8)
To report its findings to the governing authority for such action as the governing authority deems appropriate.
(b)
The board of ethics may consult with the city attorney, however in the event of a conflict, the board of ethics may consult with outside counsel upon majority vote.
(c)
At the direction of mayor and council, the board of ethics may be asked to provide an advisory opinion based upon a real or hypothetical set of circumstances.
(Ord. of 11-10-08(2), § 1; Ord. of 8-24-09, § 1)
Sec. 2-374. - Service of complaint; hearings and disposition of complaints.
(a)
The board of ethics as appointed herein set forth, shall cause the warranted complaint to be served upon the member of the governing authority charged as soon as practicable. Service may be in personal service or by certified mail, return receipt requested.
(b)
A hearing deemed necessary by the board of ethics, shall be held within 60 days after the board's receipt of the written report from the investigating committee.
(c)
The procedure may include the following:
(1)
Proceedings generally:
a.
The proceedings shall be informal but orderly. The facts not in dispute may be stipulated; however, the presiding officer may in his or her discretion reject any stipulated fact.
b.
The complainant and then the member of the governing authority who is the subject of the inquiry may make an opening statement to the board of ethics. The complainant shall present his or her evidence first. After that, the member of the governing authority who is the subject of the inquiry may present his or her evidence. After all evidence is presented, the complainant may make a closing argument following which the member of the governing authority who is the subject of the inquiry may make a closing argument. The complainant may then make a final statement.
c.
All hearings shall be open to the public as a meeting under the Georgia Open Meetings Law. The presiding officer of the board of ethics may order the removal of any person disrupting the proceedings.
(2)
Witnesses:
a.
The member of the governing authority who is the subject of inquiry, the complainant or the board of ethics may request the attendance of city employees as witnesses when their testimony will aid in establishing the facts in the case. Requests shall be made to the city manager who will make reasonable efforts to assure the attendance of city employees. The member of the governing authority who is the subject of inquiry, the complainant or the board of ethics may subpoena any witness to appear and testify at any hearing conducted by the board. The board of ethics acting through the presiding officer unless overruled by a majority of the board of ethics members in attendance may in its discretion, limit the number of witnesses from whom testimony will be taken in order to eliminate duplicate testimony.
b.
Nothing in this section shall be construed to establish any right in the member of the governing authority who is the subject of inquiry or the complainant or a duty on the part of the city to compel the attendance of any witness.
c.
No person shall directly or indirectly use, or threaten to use, any official authority or other influence which tends to discourage any other person from testifying.
d.
All witnesses shall testify under oath or affirmation.
e.
At least one week before the hearing, the member of the governing authority who is the subject of inquiry and the complainant shall provide the city clerk a list of witnesses or speakers who are expected to be present and testify. Persons not on the list may not testify unless permitted by the board of ethics acting through the presiding officer unless overruled by a majority of the board of ethics members in attendance.
f.
The member of the governing authority who is the subject of inquiry, the complainant or the board of ethics may insist on the sequestration of witnesses.
(3)
Cross-examination: The member of the governing authority who is the subject of inquiry or the complainant may conduct such cross-examination as shall be required for a full and true disclosure of the facts. The members of the board of ethics may also examine the witnesses.
(4)
Evidence:
a.
Irrelevant, immaterial, or unduly repetitious evidence shall be excluded. Objections to evidentiary offers may be made and may be noted in the record.
b.
Evidence may be admitted if it is the type commonly relied upon by reasonably prudent persons in the conduct of their affairs.
c.
Objections to evidentiary offers may be made and may be noted in the record.
d.
Documentary evidence may be received in the form of copies of excerpts if the original is not readily available. Upon request, and at the discretion of the presiding officer, the parties will be given the opportunity to compare the copy with the original.
e.
When a hearing will be expedited and the interest of the parties will not be substantially prejudiced, any part of the evidence may be received in written form including, but not limited to, the use of depositions, interrogatories, affidavits, or other written forms of evidence as allowed by the presiding officer in his or her discretion.
f.
Rules of evidence as generally understood in the civil and criminal courts are not binding.
(5)
Recordings or transcripts: If an audio or video recording or a written transcript or other type of recording is made by either the member of the governing authority who is the subject of inquiry, the complainant or the board of ethics during a hearing, then the recording or transcript shall be made immediately available to either party or the board of ethics who may be charged the cost of reproduction. A recording on behalf of the city of all hearings under this section will be made using a device approved by the presiding officer.
(Ord. of 11-10-08(2), § 1; Ord. of 8-24-09, § 1; Ord. of 6-20-11)
Sec. 2-375. - Penalty and member rights.
(a)
Any member of the governing authority who knowingly violates any provision of the code of ethics provided in this division shall be subject to public reprimand or censure by the governing authority of the city.
(b)
At any hearing held by the board of ethics, the member of the governing authority who is the subject of inquiry shall have the right to written notice of the allegations at least ten business days before a hearing, to be represented by counsel, to hear and examine the evidence and witnesses and to present evidence and witnesses in opposition or in extenuation.
(Ord. of 11-10-08(2), § 1; Ord. of 8-24-09, § 1)
Sec. 2-376. - Appeals.
(a)
Any member of the governing authority or the complainant adversely affected by these findings of the board of ethics may obtain judicial review of such decision as provided in the section.
(b)
An action for judicial review may be commenced by filing an application for a writ of certiorari in the Superior Court of Camden County within 30 days after the decision of the board of ethics. The filing of such application shall act as supersedes.
(Ord. of 11-10-08(2), § 1; Ord. of 8-24-09, § 1)
