ARTICLE IV. - CANVASSERS, SOLICITORS AND PEDDLERS[4]

Editor's note— Ord. No. 2015-16 , § 1(Exh. A), adopted Sept. 8, 2015, repealed art. IV in its entirety, and enacted new provisions to read as herein set out. Former art. IV, §§ 22-400—22-414 pertained to similar subject matter, and derived from Ord. No. 2015-15, § 1(Exh. A), adopted Aug. 11, 2015.

Sec. 22-400. - Purpose and intent.
The purpose of this article is to regulate the sale of goods and services by solicitation of residences and businesses in Banks County, and to prohibit criminal activity and abusive techniques utilized by canvassers or solicitors which would adversely affect the public health, safety and welfare of the people.
(Ord. No. 2015-16 , § 1(Exh. A), 9-8-2015)
Sec. 22-401. - Definitions.
[The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:]
Applicant shall mean the person seeking a license or a permit.
Canvasser shall mean one who offers one or more services or goods for sale to residents or one who seeks political support or inquiries as to issues of public opinion or sentiments. This term shall be interchangeable with "solicitor" and "peddler''
Canvassing shall mean travelling from place to place or door to door on foot or in a vehicle and offering to sell goods or services, or the seeking of political support or the act of inquiring as to issues of public opinion or sentiment. This term shall be interchangeable with soliciting and peddling.
Goods shall mean any sort of personal property, including but not limited to brushes, vacuums, magazines, newspapers, luggage, house wares, electronics, computers, furniture, food, agricultural products, flowers, tools, clothing, decorative accessories, rugs, paintings, sculpture, dishes, and other types of wares and merchandise.
License shall mean a business license.
Peddler shall mean one who offers goods or merchandise for sale along the street or from door to door or house to house on behalf of a business, individual, vocation or occupation; one who engages in peddling. This term shall be interchangeable with "canvasser" and "solicitor."
Peddling or to peddle shall mean travelling from place to place or door to door on foot or in a vehicle and exhibiting, offering to sell or selling goods or services to households, businesses or passers-by. It shall also mean travelling from place to place, door to door taking orders for the sale of goods or services for delivery at another time or place. The solicitation of orders combined with a separate transaction to make delivery to the purchaser as part of a scheme or design to evade the provisions of this article shall be deemed peddling. Visits by sales representatives pursuant to a previously arranged appointment with a specific business or person or as a part of an on-going business relationship shall not be deemed peddling. This term shall be interchangeable with "canvassing" and "soliciting."
Permit shall mean the authorization by the Banks County Code Enforcement Officer granting a canvasser, peddler or solicitor permission to canvass, peddle or solicit in Banks County, and shall be referred to as a "solicitor's permit."
Services mean the provisions of any sort of professional or trade service to another to include general maintenance, construction, painting, lawn service, house cleaning, mending, sharpening, motor repair, appliance repair, etc.
Soliciting shall mean travelling from place to place or door to door on foot or in a vehicle and exhibiting, offering to sell or selling goods or services to households, businesses or passers-by or requesting contributions. This term shall be interchangeable with "canvassing" and "peddling."
Solicitor shall mean one who offers one or more services or goods for sale to residents or requests contributions by either traveling from place to place or door to door on foot or in a vehicle. This term shall be interchangeable with "canvasser" and "peddler."
(Ord. No. 2015-16 , § 1(Exh. A), 9-8-2015)
Sec. 22-402. - General information.
(a)
Any person, firm or corporation engaging or offering as a canvasser, solicitor, or peddler in person at residences and places of business in the unincorporated areas of the county, with or without the consent or invitation of the occupant, for the purpose of canvassing, soliciting, or peddling orders, sales, subscriptions, contributions, or conducting business of any kind, including giving away any goods or services shall be referred to as a solicitor.
(b)
Any person, firm or corporation engaging or offering as a canvasser solicitor, or peddler in person at residences and places of business in the unincorporated areas of the county, with or without the consent or invitation of the occupant, for the purpose of canvassing, soliciting, or peddling orders, sales, subscriptions, contributions, or conducting business of any kind, including giving away any goods or services shall be required to obtain a permit, an identification badge, a business license, and a state peddler's license.
(Ord. No. 2015-16 , § 1(Exh. A), 9-8-2015)
Sec. 22-403. - Registration, permit and license required.
(a)
A solicitor prior to soliciting orders, sales, subscriptions, contributions, or conducting business of any kind, including giving away any goods or services in person at residences and places of business in the unincorporated areas of the county without the consent or invitation of the occupant shall:
(1)
Be 18 years of age at the time of application;
(2)
Be a citizen of the United States or a legal alien;
(3)
File an application for a permit with the code enforcement officer;
(4)
Obtain a business license from the code enforcement officer or be the agent or employee of a firm, or individual with a valid business license.
(b)
Persons requesting permits for soliciting door to door in private residential areas, shall be required to produce information on each vehicle(s) being used, including at least two photos of each vehicle (front/side view and rear/side view which photographs the vehicle license plate. This information will be provided to the Banks County Sheriff's Office.
(c)
Each individual person must obtain a permit, to include those persons driving for the person performing the actual soliciting or peddling.
(d)
Any individual working as an employee of a business may apply for a permit using the owner's business license, but must show proof of employment from the owner of the business and have written permission from the owner to apply for a permit. Proof of employment shall be two current pay check stubs, or evidence of an employment agreement.
(e)
An independent contractor (persons independently employed or receiving 1099 forms instead of a W-2 form) must apply for a separate permit and business license.
(f)
All application for permits must include at a minimum the following information and shall be signed by the applicant:
(1)
Name and address of the applicant;
(2)
Date of birth;
(3)
Social security number;
(4)
Physical description;
(5)
Name, address and telephone number of the applicant's employer;
(6)
Name, address and telephone number of the applicant's supervisor;
(7)
Brief description of products or services to be sold;
(8)
Description of motor vehicles to be used by the applicant in conducting the canvassing or solicitation;
(9)
Criminal history;
(10)
Photocopy of driver's license or other official photo identification of the applicant.
(Ord. No. 2015-16 , § 1(Exh. A), 9-8-2015)
Sec. 22-404. - Peddler's license requirements.
In addition to the requirements of this article, peddlers as defined by state law must first obtain a license from the Probate Court of Banks County under the requirements of O.C.G.A. § 43-32-1 et seq. The requirements of that statute are separate from this article and obtaining that license shall not satisfy the requirements of this article. The applicant is responsible for payment of any fees for a state peddler's license.
(Ord. No. 2015-16 , § 1(Exh. A), 9-8-2015)
Sec. 22-405. - Fees.
(a)
The fee for a solicitor's permit shall be $100.00 for each permit. This fee includes the application, background investigation, copy of the solicitation code and an ID badge.
(b)
Permits for residential solicitation will be issued for a period of two weeks only and may be renewed only once during the calendar year, providing no complaints have been received.
(c)
A renewal fee shall be $50.00.
(d)
Fees are nonrefundable.
(Ord. No. 2015-16 , § 1(Exh. A), 9-8-2015)
Sec. 22-406. - Authorization of permit.
A permit shall be granted or refused after an investigation of the applicant and any canvassers, solicitors peddlers named in the application.
(1)
Upon approval of the application and issuance of a permit to canvass, solicit, or peddle, an identification badge will be issued to the applicant.
(2)
Each solicitor must wear the identification badge issued by the code enforcement officer at all times while, canvassing, soliciting or peddling is underway; and show other photo identification if requested by any citizen, homeowner, resident or law enforcement personnel.
(3)
Permits shall be displayed at the request of any citizen or law enforcement personnel.
(4)
It shall be a violation of this chapter to fail or refuse to display a permit or official identification badge when requested.
(Ord. No. 2015-16 , § 1(Exh. A), 9-8-2015)
Sec. 22-407. - Investigation and background check.
(a)
A background investigation to review the criminal history records of the applicant is required prior to the issuance of a permit.
(b)
Two forms of positive identification are required of the applicant.
(c)
Fingerprints of the applicant shall be taken by the sheriff's office for identification and background check purposes. Applicant is responsible for fingerprint charges.
(d)
The criminal history record shall be used for investigative purposes only and shall give rise to no presumption or inference of guilt. Due to the inclusion of criminal history records, the application shall be regarded as confidential and shall not be produced for public inspection without a court order.
(Ord. No. 2015-16 , § 1(Exh. A), 9-8-2015)
Sec. 22-408. - Disqualification and denial of permit.
(a)
No permit shall be issued to any applicant who provides any false or misleading information in connection with the application required by this article.
(b)
No permit or badge shall be issued to any applicant for canvassing or solicitation under the age of 18 or who shall have been convicted, pled guilty or entered a plea of nolo contender for any federal, state or local law of any crime involving moral turpitude, or has pending a charge involving the elements of assault and battery, or any civil judgment involving unethical or improper business actions, including but not limited to actions which would constitute fraud and deceit under the laws of the state, illegal gambling, any felony, criminal trespass, public indecency, misdemeanor involving any type of sexual related crime, any theft or violence against a person or property, any crime of possession or sale, or distribution of illegal drugs, distribution of material depicting nudity or sexual conduct as defined under state law, criminal solicitation to commit any of these listed offenses or attempts to commit any of these listed offenses.
(c)
If, as a result of the investigation authorized by this article, the applicant's character or business responsibility is found to be unsatisfactory, the code enforcement officer shall endorse on such application his disapproval and the reasons for such denial, and shall notify the applicant that the application is denied.
(Ord. No. 2015-16 , § 1(Exh. A), 9-8-2015)
Sec. 22-409. - Approval and issuance of permit.
(a)
If, as a result of the investigation authorized by this article, the applicant's character and business responsibility are found to be satisfactory, the code enforcement officer shall endorse on such application his approval and reasons for such approval, who shall, upon payment of regulatory fees as established issue a solicitor's permit.
(b)
A solicitor's permit shall contain the name, address, physical description and organizational affiliation of the permit; a description of the solicitation activity to be engaged in; an expiration date; and the signature of the code enforcement officer. The code enforcement officer shall keep a record of all solicitors' permits issued.
(c)
A solicitor's permit will be issued a minimum of ten days after initial application is made for a permit, in order to give time for processing, investigation and ID badge preparation.
(Ord. No. 2015-16 , § 1(Exh. A), 9-8-2015)
Sec. 22-410. - Exemption from obtaining permit.
(a)
Persons, business and organizations exempted from local regulation by operation of state or federal law or by the Constitution of the United States, or of the state, are exempt from the requirements of this article;
(b)
Bona fide charitable organizations tax exempt under section 501(C)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code are exempt from the requirements of this chapter unless so noted elsewhere;
(c)
Bona fide charitable or nonprofit organizations whose field sales representatives are under the age of 18 years are not required to obtain a solicitor's permit;
(d)
Solicitors for nonprofit organizations who are regularly enrolled in a public or private school in Banks County shall not be required to obtain identification badges or a solicitor's permit;
(e)
Persons engaging in political canvassing.
(Ord. No. 2015-16 , § 1(Exh. A), 9-8-2015)
Sec. 22-411. - Regulations for canvassers, solicitors and peddlers.
(a)
Solicitors may only make one solicitation call at the same location/address for each individual business within any consecutive six month period, unless specifically invited back by the homeowner/occupant.
(b)
Solicitors must inform homeowner/resident immediately in English in understandable terms the purpose for the solicitation.
(c)
Solicitors may not represent the solicitor is participating in any contest, game or other competitive endeavor, or offering the occupant an opportunity to participate in any such contest, game or endeavor.
(d)
Solicitors may not use vulgar, insulting or threatening language in the course of any solicitation.
(e)
Solicitors may not remain on any part of the property of the residence after the occupant has verbally indicated for the solicitor to leave or a refusal to make a purchase.
(f)
Solicitors may not lend, rent, or sell their permit to another person or business, or use the permit of another solicitor, except as provided in [section] 22-403(d).
(g)
Door to door solicitation in residential communities or neighborhoods may occur only during the hours of 9:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. No solicitation is allowed on Sundays or holidays.
(h)
Only one solicitor may go to any one residence at a time, unless more are invited back by the homeowner/resident.
(i)
Solicitors may not solicit from anyone under the age of 18 or request entrance into a private residence from anyone under the age of 18.
(j)
Solicitors may not enter private residences unless specifically invited by the homeowner/resident who is at least 18 years of age.
(k)
Solicitors may only use the main or front entrance of the residence.
(l)
Solicitors must respect all "no solicitation" signs, if there is a sign prohibiting solicitation at the entrance to a community, subdivision, business or residence.
(m)
Solicitors may not approach a homeowner, resident or occupant when the homeowner, resident or occupant is in the yard where a "no solicitation" sign prohibiting solicitation in a community or subdivision, business or residence is posted.
(n)
Solicitors may not touch or in any way attach any information to the homeowner, resident or occupant's mailbox, paperbox, or mailbox post, or to the entrance door or residence porch, porch columns, railings, or wall of the residence.
(o)
Solicitors may not throw, lay, or in any way toss items into a private business, homeowner, or resident's yard or in any other way "litter."
(p)
Solicitors shall not engage in any aggressive or intimidating sales or solicitation practices, and such will not be tolerated.
(q)
Soliciting at commercial businesses shall be allowed only if the owner or duly authorized agent agrees in writing for solicitation on the business premises.
(r)
If a complaint is registered against a solicitor soliciting in a residential area of Banks County, all permits for the group or business may be revoked immediately by the Banks County Code Enforcement Officer after investigation of such complaint.
(Ord. No. 2015-16 , § 1(Exh. A), 9-8-2015)
Sec. 22-412. - Regulations for solicitation on streets/highways/rights-of-way.
(a)
Soliciting on streets/highways/rights of way is permitted only for non-profit and/or charitable organizations with a current valid business license registered with Banks County (unless exempt under Banks County Business License regulations).
(b)
Soliciting on streets/highways/rights-of-way will only be allowed Monday through Fridays and only between the hours of 9:00 a.m. to 11:00 a.m. and from 2:00 p.m. to 4:00 p.m.
(c)
Deleted.
(d)
Based on public safety concerns, one person for each intersecting point shall be allowed to solicit on streets/highways/rights-of-way.
(e)
Any one group or business will be limited to five days each year to solicit on streets/highways/rights of way in Banks County.
(f)
Solicitors on streets/highways/rights of way shall not engage in any aggressive or intimidating sales or solicitation practices, and such will not be tolerated.
(g)
If a complaint is received against any person or persons soliciting on a street/highway/rights of way, all permits for the group or business may be revoked by the code enforcement officer after investigation of the complaint.
(Ord. No. 2015-16 , § 1(Exh. A), 9-8-2015)
Sec. 22-413. - Due process.
Any person, firm, corporation, association or partnership who believes he/she has been denied due process of law under this article shall be entitled to a hearing upon written appeal to the board of commissioners which appeal shall allege denial of due process. Unless such hearing is requested within five working days after the alleged denial of due process, such person shall be presumed to have accepted summary process.
(Ord. No. 2015-16 , § 1(Exh. A), 9-8-2015)
Sec. 22-414. - Violations and enforcement.
(a)
Any person, firm, corporation, association or partnership violating any of the above regulations may have its solicitor's permit revoked by the Banks County Code Enforcement Officer upon investigation of the alleged violation.
(b)
Persons canvassing, soliciting or peddling without a permit or in violation of the permit shall be issued a citation punishable as set forth in subsection (c) of this section.
(c)
Any person, firm, corporation, association or partnership violating any provision of this article as the same exists or as it may hereinafter be amended, or shall fail to do anything required by this article as the same exists or as it may hereafter be amended, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, amenable to the process of the Magistrate or State Court of Banks County, and upon conviction, shall be punished as provided in the Code of Banks County, Georgia, section 1-11 for each violation in the discretion of the court.
(d)
The enforcement of the provisions of this article shall be conducted by the Banks County Code Enforcement Officer or the Sheriff/Deputy Sherriff of Banks County.
(Ord. No. 2015-16 , § 1(Exh. A), 9-8-2015)
