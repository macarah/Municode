Sec. 2-31. - Meetings.
(a)
The Board of Commissioners of Banks County shall hold regular meetings on the second Tuesday of each month, and may adjourn from day to day, and may hold called sessions whenever in their opinion public necessity requires it. The structure at 150 Hudson Ridge, Homer, Georgia, 30547 is hereby designated a Banks County Court House Annex and the board of commissioners shall hold regular meetings at the Court House in said county or at the Court House Annex in said county on the second Tuesday in each month and may adjourn from day to day and may hold called sessions whenever in their opinion public necessity requires it.

Sec. 2-32. - Agenda.
There shall be a written agenda prepared prior to each meeting of the board of commissioners. Persons who desire to address the board of commissioners shall notify the clerk of the board of commissioners by no later than noon on the Friday preceding the next meeting. The board of commissioners may by majority vote allow a person not otherwise on the agenda to address the board of commissioners.

Sec. 2-62. - Exclusion of poll workers from social security.
(a)
Findings. Officials of the county did enter into a plan and agreement with the employees retirement system of the state for extending to its employees the insurance system established by Title II of the Social Security Act. The original agreement did include the services of election officials and election workers. It is, at this time, the desire of the board of commissioners to exclude the services performed by election officials or election workers for each calendar year prior to the year 2000 in which the remuneration paid for such services is less than $1,000.00, and for each year after 1999 in which the remuneration paid is less than the adjusted amount, as described in this section, beginning with services performed in the year that this modification was mailed or delivered by other means to the board of commissioners.
(b)
Adjustment of excludable amount. The $1,000.00 limit on the excludable amount of remuneration paid in a calendar year for the services specified in this modification will be subject to adjustment for calendar years after 1999 to reflect changes in wages in the economy without any further modification of the agreement, with respect to such services performed during such calendar years, in accordance with Section 218(c)(8)(B) of the Social Security Act.
(c)
Contract amendment. The contract approved and accepted by the employees retirement system of the state is hereby amended to exclude the services referenced in subsection (a) of this section effective January 1, 1996.
(Res. No. 96-9, 9-10-1996)

Sec. 2-106. - Continuation of joint authority, addition of additional participating counties and change in name.
It is hereby found and determined that there is a need for the authority to operate as a joint development in Rabun County, in addition to operating in Banks and Habersham Counties. Rabun County is hereby added as a participating county of the authority and the authority is continued as a joint development authority under the name the "Joint Development Authority of Banks, Habersham and Rabun Counties." This joint and concurrent resolution, when adopted by the governing bodies of each of the participating counties, shall be deemed to continue the authority, with Rabun County being added as a participating county, rather than creating a new joint development authority. The authority shall transact business pursuant to, and exercise the powers provided by the provisions of, the Development Authorities Law, codified in the Official Code of Georgia Annotated Title 36, Chapter 62, as the same now exists and as it may be hereafter amended.
(Res. No. 2007-01, § 1, 1-9-2007)
Sec. 2-107. - Members and officers.
(a)
The number of members of the authority shall be nine. Three members shall be appointed by the governing body of each participating county and each such member shall be a resident and taxpayer of such county. Except as provided below, the term of office of each member shall be three calendar years.
(b)
The persons appointed by the governing bodies of Banks and Habersham Counties, respectively, who currently hold office as members of the authority, shall continue to serve as members of the authority for the balance of their current terms of office.
(c)
One of the members initially appointed by the governing body of Rabun County shall serve a term ending December 31, 2007, one shall serve a term ending December 31, 2008, and one shall serve a term ending December 31, 2009. Such initial members shall be appointed by separate resolution of the governing body of Rabun County.
(d)
After expiration of the term of each member, the terms of office of their respective successors shall be terms of three calendar years. If, at the end of any term of any member, a successor to such member has not been appointed, the member whose term of office has expired shall continue to hold office until his successor is appointed, and such successor's term shall commence on the date of his or her appointment and shall end on the December 31, that is, three years after the scheduled end of the immediately preceding term.
(e)
The members of the authority shall constitute its board of directors. A majority of the members of the authority shall constitute a quorum, but no action may be taken by the authority without the affirmative vote of a majority of the full membership of the authority.
(f)
The members shall receive no compensation for their services as members or directors of the authority, but shall be reimbursed for their actual expenses incurred in the performance of their duties.
(g)
The current officers of the authority shall continue to hold office for their current terms or until their successors are elected and qualified.
(Res. No. 2007-01, § 2, 1-9-2007)

DIVISION 5. - BANKS COUNTY E-911 ADVISORY BOARD

Sec. 2-166. - E-911 advisory board.
(a)
Banks County E-911 Advisory Board Overseeing Authority will be the county board of commissioner's.
(b)
Banks County E-911 Advisory Board voting members will consist of the following: Banks County E-911 Director or appointee, Banks County Sheriff or appointee, Banks County Fire/EMS Chief or appointee, Banks County EMA Director or appointee, Banks County Marshall or appointee, Homer Fire Chief or appointee, Baldwin Fire Chief or appointee, Baldwin Chief of Police or appointee, Maysville Chief of Police or appointee, Maysville Fire Chief or appointee, Department of Natural Resources Law Enforcement Official and Georgia Forestry Area Ranger or appointee.
(c)
A quorum of the advisory board shall be a majority of the membership. No business shall be conducted by the advisory board unless the quorum is present.
(d)
Guest speakers will be permitted to attend if proper notice is given and topic directly relates to an emergency system or need. No guest will be allowed to vote on any topic.
(e)
The advisory board shall hold regular quarterly meetings at 2:00 pm on the second Monday of every third month following October 8, 2001. The meetings will be held at the county fire administrative offices training room. The meetings will not be held unless the quorum is present and quarterly meeting dates may be changed by a vote from the quorum. Special called meetings may be necessary from time to time and can be called by the chairman or a majority of the board. All recommendations shall be sent directly to the county board of commissioner's and the county E-911 director.
(f)
On the first meeting of the county E-911 advisory board the members will elect a chairman for the meetings and a secretary.
(g)
The duties of the advisory board include, but are not limited to the following:
(1)
Review and analyze the progress by public safety agencies in the continued development of the 9-1-1 and central communications systems;
(2)
Recommend steps of action to affect the necessary coordination, regulation and development of the 9-1-1 system;
(3)
Identify and or review mutual aid agreements necessary to the operation of the 9-1-1 system;
(4)
Assist in the promulgation of necessary rules, regulations, operating procedures, necessary to notify various agencies that an emergency exists and insure proper response, and other such policy as shall be deemed necessary and appropriate;
(5)
Act as a quality assurance board in the management and operations of the central dispatch center;
(6)
Make recommendations concerning equipment and coordinate the acquisition of appropriate radio equipment for all users to insure compatibility with the system as a whole.
(Ord. No. 2001-31, 10-26-2001)
DIVISION 6. - BANKS COUNTY PARKS AND RECREATION BOARD

Sec. 2-217. - Created.
There is hereby created an Advisory Board for the Banks County Parks and Recreation Department to be known as the Advisory Board of the Banks County Parks and Recreation Department (the "advisory board").
(Res. No. 2002-04, § 1, 1-8-2002; Res. No. 2021-08 , § 1, 7-13-2021)
Sec. 2-218. - Members.
The advisory board shall consist of seven members who shall be residents of Banks County, Georgia; and appointed by the commissioners. Initially the terms of the advisory board shall be as follows: (i) three years for three members from July 1, 2021; (ii) two years for two members from July 1, 2021; and (iii) one year for two members from July 1, 2021.
After the initial terms of the members, the commissioners shall select the successor members to the advisory board and each member so selected shall serve a term of three years ("regular term"). Each member of the advisory board shall be eligible for reappointment by the commissioners but they can only serve two regular terms. Each member of the advisory board shall continue to serve until a successor is appointed by the commissioners. If a vacancy occurs on the advisory board, the commissioners, as soon as practicable, shall select a successor to serve the remaining term (the "interim term") of the vacated member. Each year of a regular term shall begin on July 1 and end on June 30, notwithstanding the fact that a member may be appointed to a regular term on the advisory board on a date other than July 1 and an existing member shall serve until the member's successor is appointed.
(Res. No. 2002-04, § 2, 1-8-2002; Res. No. 2016-02 , § 1, 2-9-2016; Res. No. 2021-08 , § 2, 7-13-2021)
Sec. 2-219. - Advisory board.
(a)
The advisory board shall elect one of its members as chairperson and another member as vice chairperson and it shall also elect a secretary. A majority of the members of the advisory board shall constitute a quorum necessary for the transaction of business, and a majority vote of those present at any meeting (no absentee voting or voting by proxy) at which there is a quorum shall be sufficient to do and perform any action permitted by this resolution. No vacancy in membership on the advisory board shall impair the right of the quorum to transact any and all business as aforesaid. The advisory board shall adopt the rules and regulations for its governance, provided, however, such rules and regulations shall not conflict with the provisions of this resolution. The advisory board shall continue in existence until terminated or abolished by resolution of the commissioners.
(b)
Officers shall be elected annually, and shall serve for the term of one year, or until their successors are elected and qualified. Any or all officers thus elected may serve consecutive years, if re-elected. All officers shall be re-elected by majority vote after proper nomination and second. In case of a tie, the vote will be postponed until the next meeting with a majority vote.
(c)
Chairperson shall be responsible for:
(1)
Developing an agenda for each meeting with input from county staff, advisory board members and the public. At minimum each meeting shall contain the following items: Call to order, approval of minutes, departmental updates, other business, public comments and adjournment.
(2)
Providing the meeting agenda to the board of commissioners ("BOC") by the second Tuesday prior to the meeting.
(3)
Facilitating each advisory board meeting in a fair and open manner, showing no partiality to any advisory board member, county staff or public participants.
(4)
Conducting annual elections of advisory board officers and representatives during the June meeting.
(5)
Presenting all advisory board recommendations to the BOC.
(d)
Vice-chairperson shall be responsible for:
(1)
In the absence of the chairperson, shall perform all the duties of the chairperson.
(e)
Secretary shall be responsible for:
(1)
Shall keep a true and permanent record of the minutes of all meetings of the advisory board.
(2)
Record the attendance of advisory board members.
(3)
Email or tum in hard copy recorded minutes to the park and recreation director at the conclusion of each advisory board meeting.
(4)
Email recorded minutes no later than the Monday after the advisory board meeting to the county clerk for distribution to BOC.
(f)
Park and recreation director shall be responsible for:
(1)
Shall keep a record of minutes of the meetings of the advisory board recording all official action of the advisory board, and have custody of its records which shall be housed at the office of the park and recreation director.
(g)
All advisory board members shall be responsible for:
(1)
Attendance at designated meetings.
(2)
Informally communicating important advisory board proposals to the citizens of the county for purposes of discussion and feedback.
(3)
Harmoniously working with other advisory board members.
(4)
Devoting the time and thought necessary to carry out the duties of the advisory board.
(5)
Recognizing that each member serves in an advisory capacity only and has no authority to make policy.
(6)
Members are obligated to keep problems and business of a controversial nature in strict confidence.
(7)
Supporting final decisions of the advisory board. When the advisory board votes on any decisions of policy it is considered to be thinking of the group and should be accepted by everyone regardless of their vote.
(8)
Always be abreast of new developments in the field of recreation.
(9)
Should, when designated to do so, be willing to represent and speak at official occasions.
(Res. No. 2002-04, § 3, 1-8-2002; Res. No. 2021-08 , § 3, 7-13-2021)
Editor's note— Res. No. 2021-08 , § 3, adopted July 13, 2021, changed the title of § 2-219 from "same—advisory board elect" to read as herein set out.
Sec. 2-220. - Compensation.
The members of the advisory board shall serve without compensation. The members of the advisory board may be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in the performance of their duties, if the expenses are approved by the commissioners prior to being incurred.
(Res. No. 2002-04, § 4, 1-8-2002; Res. No. 2021-08 , § 4, 7-13-2021)
Sec. 2-221. - Duties.
The advisory board shall study and review the existing policies and programs of the county parks and recreation department and make recommendations to the commissioners on needed changes to the existing policies and programs, if any. After review and study the advisory board shall make recommendations on new programs for the county parks and recreation department and the need for additional equipment or for the replacement of existing equipment being used in the recreation program. In addition, the advisory board shall study and make recommendations to the commissioners on any question referred to them by the commissioners concerning the parks and recreation program in the county. Each recommendation shall have been voted on by the advisory board and the numerical vote of the advisory board on the recommendation shall be included in the report to the commissioners. The advisory board does not have the power or authority to enter into contracts or other agreements or to bind or obligate the county. The sole purpose of the advisory board is to study and make recommendations to the commissioners concerning the parks and recreation program in the county.
(Res. No. 2002-04, § 5, 1-8-2002; Res. No. 2016-02 , § 2, 2-9-2016; Res. No. 2021-08 , § 5, 7-13-2021)
Sec. 2-222. - Absences.
The absence of an advisory board member from three consecutive meetings of the advisory board during any calendar year shall result in notification by the secretary to the advisory board chairperson and the absent advisory board member as a reminder of the attendance requirements. The secretary shall also notify the chairperson of the board of commissioners of the absences and the chairperson shall have the authority to determine if the absence result in a vacated position. Once the seat on the advisory board is deemed vacated, the chairperson of the board of commissioners of the county shall submit the vacancy to the board of commissioners for appropriate action (appointment of a new member to the advisory board).
(Res. No. 2013-02, § 1, 1-8-2013; Res. No. 2016-02 , § 3, 2-9-2016; Res. No. 2021-08 , § 6, 7-13-2021)
Editor's note— Res. No. 2016-02, § 3, adopted Feb. 9, 2016, changed the title of § 2-222 from [unexcused absences] to read as herein set out.
