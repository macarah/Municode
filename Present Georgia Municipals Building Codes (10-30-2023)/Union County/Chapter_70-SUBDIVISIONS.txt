ARTICLE II. - SUBDIVISION ROAD REGULATIONS

FOOTNOTE(S):
--- (2) ---
Editor's note—The subdivision regulations and county road specifications referred to in this article are on file in the office of the commissioner.
Sec. 70-31. - Jurisdiction.
This article shall govern all subdivision roads constructed subsequent to December 27, 1988, in the unincorporated portions of the county.
(Res. of 12-27-1988, § 103)
Sec. 70-32. - Responsibility for building roads.
It is the responsibility of the subdivider to construct access roads from existing streets/roads to connect with proposed subdivisions. Access roads shall meet the county subdivision regulations prior to approval of subdivision plat. Distance shall not be a factor.
(Res. of 12-27-1988, § 201)
Sec. 70-33. - Continuation of existing roads.
Existing roads adjacent to the subdivision shall be continued at the same or greater width, but in no case shall be less than the required width.
(Res. of 12-27-1988, § 202)
Sec. 70-34. - Road right-of-way widths.
The right of way width shall be a minimum of 60 feet for all roads within the subdivision.
(Res. of 12-27-1988, § 203)
Sec. 70-35. - Road width and base depth.
The width of roads shall be a minimum of 19 feet with a six-inch depth of base gravel on all roads within the subdivision.
(Res. of 12-27-1988, § 204)
Sec. 70-36. - Road grades and shoulder slopes.
The maximum grade on roads shall be 16 percent provided, however, that grades in excess of 12 percent shall be paved. Road shoulders and slopes shall be improved at a minimum slope of 3 to 1. Lot access points and drainage areas shall have a minimum size of 15-inch culvert installed set at a grade to prevent undue flooding.
(Res. of 12-27-1988, § 205)
Sec. 70-37. - Road pavement specifications.
Road paving shall be according to the county road specifications.
(Res. of 12-27-1988, § 206)
Sec. 70-38. - Road names.
Road names shall require the approval of the commissioner. Roads that are obviously in alignment with roads already existing and named shall be given the name of the existing road. Names of new roads shall not duplicate nor closely approximate those of existing roads.
(Res. of 12-27-1988, § 207)
Sec. 70-39. - Road jogs.
Road jogs with centerline offsets of less than 125 feet shall not be permitted.
(Res. of 12-27-1988, § 208)
Sec. 70-40. - Culs-de-sac.
Except where topographic or other conditions make a greater length unavoidable, culs-de-sac, or dead-end roads, shall not be greater in length than 1,200 feet. They shall be provided at the closed end with a turnaround having at least a radius of 80 feet. Temporary turnarounds shall be provided for dead-end which are scheduled to be extended at a later date.
(Res. of 12-27-1988, § 209)
Sec. 70-41. - Alleys.
Alleys may be required at the rear of all lots used for multifamily, commercial, or industrial developments. Alleys in residential subdivisions shall not be permitted, unless the subdivider provides evidence satisfactory to the commissioner of the need for alleys.
(Res. of 12-27-1988, § 210)
Sec. 70-42. - Tangents.
Between reverse curves, there shall be tangents having a slight distance of not less than 200 feet at six feet above ground level.
(Res. of 12-27-1988, § 211)
Sec. 70-43. - Road intersections.
Road intersections shall be as nearly at right angles as possible. No road intersection shall be at an angle of less than 75 degrees. Four-way intersections should be eliminated in favor of three-way or T intersections.
(Res. of 12-27-1988, § 212)
Sec. 70-44. - Road markers.
There shall be placed at least one standard reflectorized road name sign at every intersection, and two diagonally opposed when deemed necessary by the commissioner.
(Res. of 12-27-1988, § 213)
Sec. 70-45. - Paving requirement; exception.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 32-4-40 et seq., the county will not accept roads in subdivisions as part of the county road system unless such subdivision roads are paved. A graveled road may be accepted as a public road at the discretion of the commissioner provided, however, that the road has been inspected and found to have been built to county road specifications.
(Res. of 12-27-1988, § 214; Res. of 1-4-1993)
