ARTICLE II. - SOLID WASTE AND SCRAP TIRE MANAGEMENT

Sec. 66-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Commercial solid waste means all types of solid waste generated by stores, offices, restaurants, warehouses and other nonmanufacturing activities as defined in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(4.1).
Disposal facility means any facility or location where the final deposition of solid waste occurs, and includes, but is not limited to, landfilling and solid waste thermal treatment technology facilities as defined in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(8).
Dump means to throw, discard, place, deposit, discharge, bury, burn or dispose of a substance.
Industrial solid waste means waste generated by manufacturing or industrial processes or operations. Such waste includes, but is not limited to, fertilizer, agricultural chemicals, food and food related products and other byproducts as defined in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(12.1).
Inert waste means wastes that will not, or are not likely to, cause production of leachate of environmental concern. Such wastes are limited to earth and earth-like products, concrete, cured asphalt, rocks, bricks, yard trimmings, stumps, limbs and leaves. This definition excludes industrial and demolition waste not specifically listed in this definition (see Section 391-3-4-.06 of the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management).
Litter means discarded materials of every kind as defined in O.C.G.A. § 16-7-42(1).
Municipal solid waste means any solid waste derived from households, including garbage, trash and sanitary waste in septic tanks, and includes solid waste from single-family and multifamily residences, hotels and motels, bunkhouses, campgrounds, picnic grounds and day use recreation areas. The term includes commercial solid waste, but does not include solid waste from mining, agricultural or industrial processes or operations as defined in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(18).
Open dump or dump site means a disposal site, location or facility at which solid waste from one or more sources is left to decompose, burn or to otherwise create a threat to human health or the environment as defined in Section 391-3-4-.01(44) of the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management.
Public or private property means the right-of-way of any road or highway; any body of water or watercourse or the shores or beaches thereof; any park, playground, building, refuge or conservation or recreation area; and residential or farm properties, timberlands or forests as defined in O.C.G.A. § 16-7-42(2).
Recovered materials mean materials which have a known use, reuse or recycling potential, and have been diverted or removed from the solid waste stream for sale, use, reuse or recycling as defined in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(25).
Recovered materials processing facility means a facility engaged solely in the storage, processing and resale or reuse of recovered materials as defined in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(26).
Scrap tire means a tire that is no longer suitable for its original intended purpose because of wear, damage or defect, or one that cannot be retreaded or otherwise recycled as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(31).
Scrap tire carrier means any person engaged in picking up or transporting scrap tires not otherwise exempted under Section 391-3-4-.19(5.g) of the Georgia Rules of Solid Waste Management for the purpose of removal to a scrap tire processor, end user or disposal facility as set forth in § O.C.G.A. 12-8-22(32).
Scrap tire generator means any person who generates scrap tires as defined in Section 391-3-4-.19(2.i) of the Georgia Rules of Solid Waste Management.
Solid waste means any garbage or refuse; sludge from a wastewater treatment plant, water supply treatment plant or air pollution contract facility; and other discarded material, including solid, liquid, semisolid or contained gaseous material resulting from industrial, commercial, mining and agricultural operations and from community activities, but does not include recovered materials; solid or dissolved materials in domestic sewage; solid or dissolved materials in irrigation return flows or industrial discharges that are point sources subject to a permit under 33 USC 1342; or source, special nuclear or byproduct materials as defined by the Federal Atomic Energy Act of 1954, as amended (68 Stat. 923) as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(33).
Solid waste handling means the storage, collection, transportation, treatment, utilization, processing or disposal of solid waste, or any combination of such activities, as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(34).
Tire retailer means any person engaged in the business of selling new, retreaded or used replacement tires as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(39).
Waste means all discarded substances and materials exceeding ten pounds in weight or 15 cubic feet in volume, or any such substance in any weight or volume if biomedical waste, hazardous waste (as such term is defined in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-62), or any such substance or material dumped for commercial purposes, with the exception of nonhazardous, low-impact animal byproducts classified by the department of natural resources. This term includes, but is not limited to, bottles, boxes, containers, papers, tobacco products, tires, dead animals, including their bedding and other wastes from such animals, appliances, mechanical equipment or parts, building or construction materials, tools, machinery, wood, motor vehicles and motor vehicle parts, vessels, aircraft equipment, waste oil, batteries, antifreeze, sludge from a wastewater treatment facility, water supply treatment plant or air pollution control facility, air contaminants from any source or facility and any other discarded material or substance of every kind and description resulting from domestic, industrial, commercial, mining or governmental operations, including household, commercial, construction and demolition waste, or industrial solid waste which is nonhazardous, nonmedical and in a form classified as solid waste by the state department of natural resources as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 16-7-51.
(Ord. No. O-2000-004, § 1, 4-25-2000)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2
Sec. 66-32. - Disposal.
(a)
The owner or occupant of any premises within the county shall be responsible for the sanitary handling and disposing of all litter, scrap tires and municipal, commercial or industrial solid waste on the premises used or occupied by such person.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to dump, open dump or permit the dumping of litter, scrap tires, municipal, commercial or industrial solid waste or recyclables at any place in the county, including, but not limited to, any public or private property or waters in the county, unless such litter or waste originates in the county or other areas authorized by the commissioner, and the:
(1)
Property is designated by the commissioner or his duly designated agent for the disposal of litter, municipal, commercial or industrial solid waste, scrap tires or recovered materials, and the person is authorized to use such property;
(2)
Litter, municipal, commercial or industrial solid waste, recyclables or scrap tires is placed into a receptacle or container installed specifically for such property; and
(3)
Property has a valid solid waste handling permit issued by division of environmental protection.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to dump or place waste within the county, unless such person is authorized to do so by law or by a duly issued permit, in or on any:
(1)
Public highway, road, street, alley or thoroughfare, including any portion of the right-of-way thereof, or on any other public lands, except in containers or areas lawfully provided for such dumping;
(2)
Fresh water lake, river, canal, stream or creek; or
(3)
Public or private property, unless such dumping will not adversely affect the public health, and is not in violation of any other local, state or federal law, rule or regulation.
(d)
All persons defined as scrap tire generators, scrap tire carriers or tire retailers shall be subject to rules as defined in Section 391-3-4-.19 and handle scrap tires in accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 12-8-20 et seq. and the Rules for Solid Waste Management, Chapters 391-33-4 and 392-3-4.19 applicable to solid waste and tires.
(Ord. No. O-2000-004, § II, 4-25-2000)
Sec. 66-33. - Transportation.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to drive or operate a vehicle in the county hauling municipal, commercial or industrial solid waste which leaks, flows freely or spills from such vehicle.
(b)
No person shall drive or move any truck or other vehicle within the county unless such truck or other vehicle is constructed, loaded or covered so as to prevent any load, contents or litter from being blown, scattered or in any manner deposited in or upon any street, sidewalk or other public place or private property within the county. Any litter or municipal, commercial or industrial solid waste hauled on a moving vehicle shall be covered in such a manner that litter will not blow or escape from such vehicle while such vehicle is moving or parked on public or private property in the county.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any vehicle to transport litter or waste on any public streets or roadways in the county without a suitable covering or other restraint device securely fastening such litter or waste to the vehicle so as to prevent any material from being deposited onto other motor vehicles, pedestrians or the roadway and its adjoining areas. However, this subsection shall not prohibit the necessary and permitted spreading of any substance in public road maintenance or construction operations.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for a business or private person engaged in waste hauling or transportation for hire from businesses or private residences to a county landfill or lawfully permitted dump site to dispose of materials as solid waste that have been intentionally sorted as recyclables by the business or private residence customers rather than placing the materials in the location designated by the county or appropriate authority for such recyclables.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for any person to drive or move any truck or other vehicle within the county, the wheels or tires of which carry or deposit in or upon any street, sidewalk or other public place or private property within the county, any mud, dirt, sticky substance, litter or foreign matter of any kind.
(Ord. No. O-2000-004, § III, 4-25-2000)
Cross reference— Traffic and vehicles, ch. 82
Sec. 66-34. - Container and receptacle regulations.
(a)
All solid waste or litter containers or receptacles within the county shall be maintained in as sanitary a manner as is reasonably possible consistent with the use of such containers or receptacles for solid waste and litter disposal.
(b)
Persons using solid waste and litter containers or receptacles shall deposit only authorized solid waste and refuse in the containers or receptacles.
(c)
No person shall deposit a scrap tire in any container or receptacle unless authorized by the owner of the receptacle, the commissioner or his designee.
(d)
No person shall deposit any burning or smoldering material in a container or receptacle.
(e)
No person shall set fire to the contents of any container or receptacle.
(f)
No person shall deposit large noncompatible articles in containers or receptacles, including, but not limited to, stoves, refrigerators, bed springs, automobile parts, boat parts, large tree limbs or air conditioning units, except containers or receptacles designated for such purpose only.
(g)
No any person shall deposit any flammable or explosive materials in any container or receptacle.
(h)
No dead animals, livestock or poultry shall be deposited in any container or receptacle, except receptacles designed for such purpose and so designated by the commissioner.
(i)
No person shall willfully damage or alter the location of any container or receptacle without the written consent of the commissioner.
(j)
No salvage or scavenging operations shall be conducted in or around containers or receptacles, except by written consent of the commissioner.
(k)
No person shall deposit any solid waste at a county solid waste collection and recycling center unless such solid waste is contained in such a manner that it can be handled by the attendant. Solid waste shall be deposited only during the official hours of operation of the center, unless express written permission is given by the commissioner or his designee.
(l)
No person shall deposit solid waste, water or litter of any kind at a county solid waste collection and recycling center into county owned receptacles or containers designated for the collection of recovered materials. Only authorized materials such as glass, aluminum, newspaper, cardboard, plastic and tin or other accepted material may be deposited in the appropriate container designated for such material.
(Ord. No. O-2000-004, § IV, 4-25-2000)
Sec. 66-35. - Landfill and collection and recycling center regulations.
(a)
No landfill shall be operated in the county, other than a landfill designated by the commissioner as the county landfill, and no private municipal solid waste landfill shall be operated in the county without a solid waste handling permit issued by the state environmental protection division, with the exception of inert construction and demolition landfills which have been properly permitted by the state department of natural resources/environmental protection division.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to deposit solid waste of any kind outside the gate of a solid waste collection and recycling center.
(c)
No scavenging operations of any kind shall be allowed at a solid waste collection and recycling center without the express written permission from the commissioner.
(d)
No person shall move, remove or cross any fence, gate, barrier or sign at a solid waste collection and recycling center.
(e)
Price on solid waste brought to a solid waste collection and recycling center will be set to defray the total cost of solid waste and recycling operations.
(f)
Hours of operation of solid waste collection and recycling centers will be set to maximize convenience to residents of the area.
(g)
All recovered materials (recyclables) brought to solid waste collection and recycling centers will be accepted free of charge, provided, such materials are separated under current market specifications and are uncontaminated.
(h)
The attendant on duty at the solid waste collection and recycling center will have full authority of the center consistent with the provisions of this article.
(i)
All regulations set forth in this section shall apply to all public and/or private property, waters and/or waterways and solid waste collection and recycling centers within the county.
(Ord. No. O-2000-004, § V, 4-25-2000)
Sec. 66-36. - Accumulation.
(a)
No owner or occupant of any property shall bury or burn litter or waste without the prior authorization and written permission from the commissioner or applicable regulatory agency, including, but not limited to, the environmental protection agency, environmental protection division and/or the state forestry service. Nothing in this subsection shall authorize or be construed to permit the burial or burning of any material which is otherwise prohibited by state or federal law.
(b)
No owner or occupant of any property shall allow the accumulation upon such property of garbage, litter or waste where such material creates or causes a health hazard to neighbors or other citizens, or which is unsightly or emits foul or obnoxious odors.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to set fire to or indiscriminately scatter or disperse the contents of, or otherwise vandalize, any containers used for the storage or dumping of litter or waste.
(d)
The conduct set forth in subsections (a)—(c) of this section shall constitute a separate violation of this article for each day the garbage or waste material remains or continues to unlawfully pollute, contaminate or burn on such premises.
(Ord. No. O-2000-004, § VI, 4-25-2000)
Sec. 66-37. - Violations; penalties.
(a)
Any person who violates any provision of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction in the county magistrate court, shall be punished as follows:
(1)
For the first offense, a fine of not less than $100.00 nor more than $1,000.00 and/or up to 30 days' imprisonment. Each day the violation continues shall constitute a separate offense as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 16-7-43. However, this subsection shall not preclude the county from choosing to seek civil redress in a court of competent jurisdiction in addition to the criminal prosecution, it being the intent of the county to have both the civil and criminal rights of prosecution in this area; and/or
(2)
For the second and subsequent offenses, the violator shall be guilty of a misdemeanor of a high and aggravated nature, which shall be punishable by a fine of not less than $750.00 nor more than $1,000.00, and/or up to 60 days' imprisonment. Each day the violation continues shall constitute a separate offense as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 16-7-53-B.
(b)
In the sound discretion of the judge of the court with jurisdiction, the violator may also be directed to pick up and remove litter from any public streets or highways and/or other public rights-of-way or private areas for a distance not to exceed one mile, any litter the violator has deposited and all litter deposited by any other person prior to the date of execution of the sentence as provided in O.C.G.A. § 16-7-43(b).
(c)
In the sound discretion of the judge of the court with jurisdiction, the violator may be directed to pick up and remove litter from any public park, private right-of-way or, with the prior permission of the legal owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property, any private property upon which it can be established by competent evidence that the violator has deposited litter thereon, and all litter deposited by any other person prior to the date of execution of the sentence.
(d)
The court may order the publication of the names and photographs of persons convicted of violating this article.
(e)
The court may order the violator to repair or restore damaged property, pay damages resulting from such violation or perform public service related to the repair or restoration of property damaged by the violator.
(f)
In the case of an improper garbage or waste disposal site, the property owner, contractor, developer, builder or other person responsible for the property shall cause the property to be cleaned and to come into full compliance with the provisions of this article. The county shall not be responsible for any costs of cleanup or remediation.
(g)
The expenses incurred by the county for cleanup, enforcement of violations and penalties shall be chargeable to the violator, including, but not limited to, court costs, filing fees, special investigations, mutual aid assistance from other agencies and other costs necessary for the reasonable enforcement of this article.
(h)
In addition to actions filed by the county for violations of this article, any state or federal agency may independently file separate or concurrent charges within their respective applicable authority and seek conviction within a court of competent jurisdiction.
(Ord. No. O-2000-004, § VII, 4-25-2000)
Sec. 66-38. - Yard trimmings.
Yard trimmings shall not be placed in or mixed with solid waste. Yard trimmings shall not be disposed of at any solid waste disposal facility having liners and leachate collection systems or requiring vertical expansion within the county. Yard trimmings shall be sorted and stockpiled or chipped, composted, used as mulch or otherwise beneficially reused or recycled to the maximum extent feasible. Any yard trimmings to be collected by any person other than the property owner shall be sorted and stored in such a manner as to facilitate collection, composting or other handling.
(Ord. No. O-2000-004, § XI, 4-25-2000)
Sec. 66-39. - Recycling.
The commissioner finds that it is in the best interest of the citizens of the county, in order to promote the health, safety and welfare of the citizens of the county, to recycle as many waste materials as possible in order to reduce the accumulation of litter and garbage and solid waste materials which must be properly disposed of; therefore, it is the policy of the commissioner to encourage recycling, whenever practicable.
(Ord. No. O-2000-004, § XII, 4-25-2000)
Sec. 66-40. - Enforcement; inspections; appeals.
(a)
Enforcement of this article shall be the responsibility of the commissioner, the environmental education and enforcement officer or his designee and/or the county marshal's office and/or the county sheriff's office.
(b)
Any person authorized to enforce this article shall be empowered to enter any property, upon reasonable cause, at reasonable or necessary times in order to properly inspect for violations of this article, subject to the condition that to allow entry onto private property for inspection, the alleged violation of this article must be visible from a public road or right-of-way, or upon such officer having received a valid complaint alleging a violation of this article, or by a judge's order upon such officer having received information/allegations that constitute reasonable suspicion that a serious unlawful act or threat to the health and safety of the community and/or the environment has occurred or is about to occur.
(c)
Appeals for violations of this article may be made to the county magistrate court, or a higher court if the offender chooses. The offender shall always have the right to consult his attorney at any time before the hearing is scheduled for court.
(Ord. No. O-2000-004, § VIII, 4-25-2000)
Sec. 66-41. - Civil remedies and abatement of nuisance.
(a)
If any person violates any provision of this article, the county or other appropriate authority may, in addition to other remedies, institute an action for injunction, cleanup or stop work orders, mandamus, irreversible damage fines, lien on property or other appropriate action or proceeding to prevent such unlawful acts or to correct or abate any such violation as set forth in O.C.G.A. §§ 16-7-52, 16-7-53. In addition, the county may immediately revoke or suspend any and all business, building, development or any and all other county issued permits related to the property involved with the violation until such time that compliance is met, or until the ruling of a court of competent jurisdiction is obtained, at which time, respective permits may be reissued.
(b)
Upon finding evidence of a violation of any provision of this article, a written notice of violation may be issued at the discretion of the enforcing officer in lieu of a citation. In the absence of corrective action, or if a second violation occurs, the evidence constituting the notice of violation may be submitted as evidence for consideration as a first offense before a court with competent jurisdiction and the pending case shall be treated as a second offense by the court as set forth in subsection 66-37(a)(2).
(c)
If a person is found guilty of a violation of the provisions of this article, the court and/or commissioner may cause written notice to be given, or incorporate into the court order to the violator instructing that person to properly address any provision still remaining in violation of this article for which such violator is convicted. Such notice shall be by personal service, but if the violator cannot be served with such notice, then such notice shall be sent by registered mail to the violator's last known address.
(d)
Upon failure, neglect or refusal of any person so notified to properly address the provisions of such notice within 20 days after receipt of the notice, the commissioner is authorized and empowered to cause the cleanup, removal or disposal of, including, but not limited to, any litter or any type of waste which has been dumped, deposited, thrown or left on public or private property in violation of this article on behalf of the county. The expense incurred by the county for such cleanup, removal or disposal shall be chargeable to the violator, and a statement of the amount due for such expenses shall be sent by the commissioner and/or the court by registered mail.
(e)
When the full amount of such charges are not paid by the violator within 30 days after receipt of the statement as provided in subsection (d) of this section, the commissioner shall cause to be recorded in the execution docket a sworn statement showing the cost and expense incurred by the county, the dates of county action, the location of the property for which action was taken and the name of the person to be charged for the expenses incurred. The recordation of such statement shall constitute a lien on the personal and real property of the person to be charged, and shall remain in full force and effect until final payment is received in full, including accrued interest from the date of recording and any and all costs. Such amount as shall constitute final payment shall be subject to collection in the manner fixed by law for the collection of taxes.
(f)
If wastes or hazardous materials in a dump site cannot be removed from public or private property without causing further endangerment to the environment and/or public health, whether legal or illegal, caused with intent or by accident, acts of God or force of nature, or where a permit for such longterm or permanent disposal has been issued by the proper authority as set forth in section 66-35, the county may attach or otherwise affix a permanent addendum to the deed of real property wherein the dump site exists, stating the approximate size and location of the dump on the property and an approximate description of the materials contained therein, as well as the approximate dates such dumping occurred, which shall be recorded on the property plat as a fill area as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-30. The purpose of such attachment is to preserve the health and safety of current and future landowners and users of such properties, to promote the integrity of the property description and values, and to record the location of such dump sites. The county's exercise of this subsection shall in no way be mandatory, and the county or its agents shall not be held liable for exercise of action or inaction for implementation of this subsection, nor for the costs of such action or inaction. (See Section 391-3-4-.06 of the Georgia Rules of Solid Waste Management.)
(g)
The provisions of this section shall apply with full force and effect regardless of the provisions of any order of the court in which the violator was convicted. This section should not be construed as an excuse for failure on the part of the violator to perform any cleanup ordered by the court, nor shall it be considered as a mitigating factor in any contempt action against a violator who has failed to obey the order of the court.
(Ord. No. O-2000-004, § IX, 4-25-2000)
Sec. 66-42. - Evidence of violations.
(a)
Whenever litter, or any type of waste as defined in this article, is thrown, deposited, dropped or dumped by any person or from any motor vehicle, boat, airplane or other conveyance in violation of this article, it shall be prima facie evidence that such person, or the operator of the conveyance, has violated this article.
(b)
Whenever litter, or any type of waste as defined in this article, which is dumped, deposited, thrown or left on public or private property in violation of this article is discovered to contain any article, including, but not limited to, letters, bills, publications or other writings which display the name of a person on such article, in such a manner as to indicate that the article belongs or belonged to such person, it shall be a rebuttable presumption that such person has violated this article.
(Ord. No. O-2000-004, § X, 4-25-2000)

ARTICLE III. - INERT WASTE LANDFILL

Sec. 66-71. - Intent.
It is the intent of this article to prevent the dumping of solid waste:
(1)
Along and on the public streets, right-of-ways, alleys and highways of the county and the shoulders of such streets, rights-of-ways alleys and highways;
(2)
On public and private property in the county.
(Ord. No. O-2000-006, 4-25-2000)
Sec. 66-72. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Construction/demolition waste means waste building materials and rubble resulting from construction, remodeling, repair and demolition operations on pavements, houses, commercial buildings or other structures. Such wastes include, but are not limited to, asbestos-containing waste, wood, bricks, metal, concrete, wall board, paper cardboard, inert waste landfill material and other nonputrescible wastes which have a low potential for groundwater contamination.
Hazardous waste means any solid waste which has been defined as a hazardous waste in regulations promulgated by the administrator of the United States Environmental Protection Agency pursuant to the federal act which are in force and effect on February 1, 1991, codified as 40 CFR § 261.3, and any designated hazardous waste.
Household garbage means all solid waste accumulation resulting from normal daily activities of private residents attendant to food preparation, personal hygiene, housekeeping and property maintenance. This definition does not include furniture or other wood products, yard trimmings, debris, concrete, paint containers, clothes, appliances, riding toys, liquid wastes or oversized material that cannot be containerized in plastic disposable bags, including household trash compactor bags, no larger than 39-gallon capacity size.
Industrial liquid waste means liquid or waterborne waste from an industrial process.
Inert waste landfill means a disposal facility accepting only waste that will not, or is not likely to, cause production of leachate of environmental concern. Such wastes are limited to earth and earth-like products, concrete, cured asphalt, rock, bricks, yard trimmings and land clearing debris such as stumps, limbs and leaves. This definition excludes industrial and demolition waste not specifically listed in this definition. This definition also excludes household garbage, liquid waste, hazardous waste and other similar types of refuse.
Liquid waste means any waste material that is determined to contain "free liquids" as defined by method 9095 (paint filter liquids test), as described in Test Methods for the Evaluation of Solid Wastes, Physical/Chemical Methods (Environmental Protection Agency publication number SW-846).
(Ord. No. O-2000-006, 4-25-2000)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2
Sec. 66-73. - Private landfills.
(a)
Privately owned inert waste landfills shall be allowed in the unincorporated area of the county, provided, all interested parties fulfill the requirements of this section, and comply with the following regulations:
(1)
Access to a privately owned inert waste landfill located on property fronting on a road which is part of the county road maintenance system shall be approved by the county department of roads and the commissioner.
(2)
The road accessing the inert waste landfill from a road which is part of the county road maintenance system shall meet standard requirements, as determined from time to time by the county. The county will not maintain such road, and the owner of an inert waste landfill shall sign an affidavit and agreement to maintain all portions of such road to meet the standards set forth in this section.
(3)
Access to the inert waste landfill site shall be limited to authorized entrances, which shall be closed when the site is not in operation.
(4)
The applicant for a permit to use property as a privately owned inert waste landfill shall be responsible for providing evidence that the property is properly zoned for the desired purpose and that a site acceptability permit has been issued by the state approving the use of such property as a privately owned inert waste landfill.
(5)
The applicant for a permit shall pay a $100.00 fee with each application for a permit to use property as a privately owned inert waste landfill, and the fee shall be used to defray the cost of processing the application. An additional $100.00 fee per year for renewal of the permit will be required. Permit and renewal of permit approval shall be at the discretion of the commissioner.
(6)
The owner or permit holder shall be responsible for leveling the dumped material with appropriate grading equipment and obtaining maximum practical compaction.
(7)
A uniform compacted layer of clean earth cover which is not less than one foot in depth shall be placed over all exposed inert waste material at least monthly.
(8)
The inert waste landfill site shall be graded and drained to minimize runoff onto the landfill surface, prevent erosion and drain water from the surface of the landfill.
(9)
Appropriate means shall be provided to prevent and control fires. Stockpiled soil is considered to be the most satisfactory firefighting material.
(10)
A uniform compacted layer of final cover which is not less than two feet in depth and a vegetative cover shall be placed over the final lift no later than one month following final placement of inert waste within the lift.
(11)
The applicant for a permit to use property as a privately owned inert waste landfill site must furnish site and operational plans showing that the facility is consistent with the waste reduction goals of the county. Such plans shall be reviewed by the county for compliance. Annual reporting of weights of materials accepted, origin of wastes and weights, and weights processed (diverted from disposal for reuse) shall be required. Reporting shall be on a fiscal year basis (July 1—June 30) to the county.
(12)
The commissioner or his designee shall have the right to inspect privately owned inert waste landfill at its discretion to ensure that the site complies with the laws, ordinances, rules and regulations of the county and state.
(13)
Within 30 days of commencing solid waste handling activities, the landfill operator will provide to the commissioner a copy of the notice having been placed on the recorded property deed that the property will be used for landfilling operations. Upon final closure of the landfill, all deeds for real property which have been used for inert waste landfilling shall include notice of the landfill operations, the date the landfill operation commenced and terminated, an accurate legal description of the actual location of the landfill and a description of the type of solid wastes which have been deposited in the landfill. The owner or operator of the landfill must submit with the notice of final closure to the commissioner confirmation that the information required in this section has been recorded on the property deed.
(14)
Notice of final closure of the landfill must be provided to the commissioner within 30 days of receiving the final load of waste. Any site not receiving waste for in excess of 180 days shall be deemed abandoned and in violation of this article unless such site has been properly closed. Notice of closure must include the date of final waste receipt and an accurate legal description of the boundaries of the landfill.
(15)
There shall be no less than two public hearings in regular meetings of the commissioner to consider whether or not to issue a permit for the operation of a privately owned inert waste landfill. The applicant shall be responsible for providing evidence of notice of a public hearing of such matter. Such notice shall state the dates, times and locations of the hearings, and shall be published in a local newspaper of general circulation at least 15 days in advance of the first hearing, and shall be posted on the property for the same period of time.
(b)
No person shall willfully empty, dump or otherwise place any solid waste, garbage, dead animals or other discarded materials or refuse upon the right-of-way of any public road, state highway, state aid road or public property, or upon the lands of another person.
(Ord. No. O-2000-006, 4-25-2000)
Sec. 66-74. - Enforcement; violations; penalties.
(a)
All law enforcement agencies, officers and officials of the state, or any political subdivision thereof, including enforcement officers of the public safety department, enforcement division or any enforcement agency, officer or any official of any commission of the state, or any political subdivision thereof, are authorized, empowered and directed to enforce compliance with this article.
(b)
If any person violates any provision of this article, the county attorney, or other appropriate authority of the county, may, in addition to other remedies, institute injunction, mandamus or other appropriate action or proceeding to prevent such unlawful act or to correct or abate such violation.
(c)
Any person violating any provision of this article shall be guilty of violating a duly adopted ordinance of the county and, upon conviction, such a violation shall be punished as provided in section 1-12. O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq. shall govern the trial of any violation of this article, and any violation of this article may be tried upon citation as contemplated by O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63.
(Ord. No. O-2000-006, 4-25-2000)

ARTICLE IV. - LITTER CONTROL

Sec. 66-111. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Litter means garbage, refuse, rubbish, any and all other waste material including but not limited to all discarded sand, gravel, slag, brickbats, tin cans, trash, debris, dead animals, bottles, boxes, containers, papers, tobacco products, tires, appliances, mechanical equipment or parts, building materials, wood, cuttings, trees, limbs, and any other discarded substance of any kind, as well as any object which if thrown or deposited as provided in this article, tends to create a danger to the public health, safety and welfare.
(Ord. No. O-99-003, § 4, 6-22-1999)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2
Sec. 66-112. - Manner of deposit.
(a)
No person shall throw or deposit litter in or upon any street, sidewalk or other public place or upon private property within the county, except in public or private receptacles intended for the collection of litter.
(b)
Any person placing litter in receptacles for collection shall do so in such a manner as to prevent the litter from being carried or deposited by the elements or animals upon any street, sidewalk or other public place or upon private property within the county.
(c)
Any person owning or occupying property or a place of business within the county shall keep their premises and the sidewalk in front of their premises, free of litter.
(d)
No person, while a driver or passenger in a vehicle or conveyance of any kind, shall throw or deposit litter in or upon any street, sidewalk, waters or other public place or upon private property within the county.
(e)
No person shall drive or move any truck or other vehicle within the county, unless such truck or vehicle is so constructed, loaded or covered as to prevent any load, contents or litter from being blown, scattered or in any manner deposited in or upon any street, sidewalk or other public place or upon private property within the county. No person shall drive or move any truck or other vehicle within the county, the wheels or tires of which carry unto or deposit in or upon any street, sidewalk or other public place or upon private property within the county, mud, dirt, sticky substance, litter or foreign matter of any kind.
(Ord. No. O-99-003, § 1, 6-22-1999)
Sec. 66-113. - Violation; penalty.
(a)
Any person who violates any provision of this article shall be guilty of violating a duly adopted ordinance of the county and, upon conviction, shall be punished as provided in section 1-12. O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq. shall govern the trial of any violation of this article, and any violation of this article may be tried upon citation as contemplated by O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63.
(b)
Whenever any litter is discovered to contain any article, including, but not limited to, letters, bills, publications or other writings which display the name of a person thereon, addressed to such person or in any other manner indicating that the article belongs or belonged to such person, it shall be a rebuttal presumption that such person has violated this article.
(c)
Whenever litter is thrown, deposited, dropped or dumped from any motor vehicle, boat, airplane or other conveyance, it shall be prima facie evidence that the operator of the conveyance has violated this article.
(d)
In the sound discretion of a court in which conviction is obtained, the person may be directed to pick up and remove from any public street, highway or right-of-way for a distance not to exceed one mile any litter the person has deposited and any litter deposited by anyone else prior to the date of execution of sentence.
(e)
In the sound discretion of the judge of a court in which conviction is obtained, the person may be directed to pick up and remove from any public beach, public park, private right-of-way or, with the prior permission of the legal owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property, any private property upon which it can be established by competent evidence that the person has deposited litter, any and all litter deposited by any person prior to the date of execution of sentence.
(f)
In the sound discretion of the judge of a court in which conviction is obtained, the violator may be ordered to repair or restore property damaged, or pay damages resulting from such littering, or perform public service related to the removal of litter or to the restoration of an area polluted by litter.
(Ord. No. O-99-003, § 2, 6-22-1999)
Sec. 66-114. - Notice of violation; failure to comply.
(a)
If any person is found guilty of a violation of the provisions of this article as set forth in section 66-113, the commissioner shall cause written notice to be given to the violator, instructing the violator to properly dispose of any litter still remaining in violation of this article for which the violator was convicted. Such notice shall be given by personal service, but if the violator cannot be served with such notice, such notice shall be sent by registered mail to the violator's last known address.
(b)
Upon failure, neglect or refusal of any person notified as set forth in subsection (a) of this section to properly dispose of litter within 20 days after receipt of such notice, the commissioner is authorized and empowered to cause the removal and disposal of such litter on the behalf of the county. The expense incurred by the county for such disposal shall be chargeable to the violator, and a statement of the amount due shall be sent to the violator from the commissioner's office by registered mail.
(c)
When the full amount of the charges for cleanup and disposal are not paid by the violator within 30 days after receipt of the statement for such charges, the commissioner shall cause to be recorded in the execution docket a sworn statement showing the cost and expense incurred for the work, the date the work was done, the location of the property on which such work was performed and the name of the person to be charged. The recordation of such statement shall constitute a lien on the personal and real property of the person to be charged, and shall remain in full force and effect until final payment is made, including accrued interest from the date of recording and any and all costs. Such amount as shall constitute final payment shall be subject to collection in the manner fixed by law for the collection of taxes.
(d)
This section shall apply with full force and effect regardless of the provisions of any order of the court in which the violator was convicted. This section should not be construed as an excuse for failure on the part of the violator to perform any cleanup ordered by the court, nor shall it be considered as a mitigating factor in any contempt action against a violator who has failed to obey the order of the court.
(Ord. No. O-99-003, § 3, 6-22-1999)
Secs. 66-115—66-150. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - OIL AND HAZARDOUS WASTE CLEANUP

Sec. 66-151. - Applicability.
This article shall apply to all unincorporated areas of the county and all incorporated areas of the county in which there exists a valid fire and EMS service contract with the county.
(Ord. No. O-99-004, § I, 6-22-1999)
Sec. 66-152. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Corrective action contractor means any person contracting with the county to perform any activities associated with the cleanup of environmental hazards. Such term shall not include any employee, division or department of the county.
Disposal means the discharge, deposit, injection, dumping, spilling, leaking or placing of any oil, solid waste or hazardous waste into or on any land or water or so that such oil, solid waste, hazardous waste or any constituent thereof may enter the environment or be emitted into the air or discharged into any waters, including groundwaters, and including, both intentional and unintentional acts.
Environment means any surface water, groundwater, drinking water supply, road, land surface or surface strata, or ambient air within the county or under the jurisdiction of the county.
Environmental hazard means oil, hazardous constituents, hazardous substances and hazardous wastes.
Facility means any:
(1)
Building, structure, installation, equipment, pipe or pipeline, pipe into a sewer or publicly owned treatment works, well, pit, pond, lagoon, impoundment, ditch, landfill, storage container, transfer container, motor vehicle, rolling stock, vessel or aircraft; or
(2)
Site or area where a hazardous waste, hazardous constituent or hazardous substance has been deposited, stored, disposed of, placed or has otherwise come to be located.
Hazardous constituent means any substance listed as a hazardous constituent in regulations promulgated by the administrator of the United States Environmental Protection Agency and appearing on the list of hazardous constituents, codified as 40 CFR 261 Appendix VIII, as amended.
Hazardous substance means any substance listed on the list of hazardous substances and reportable quantities, codified as 40 CFR 302, Table 302.4, as amended, or any substance listed on the list of extremely hazardous substances and their threshold planning quantities, codified as 40 CFR 355, Appendix A, as amended.
Hazardous waste means any solid waste capable of posing a substantial present or potential hazard to human health or the environment when improperly treated, stored, transported or disposed of or otherwise managed, based on the factors set forth in regulations promulgated by the administrator of the United States Environmental Protection Agency, which are codified as 40 CFR 261.11(a)(3), as amended, if such solid waste contains any substance which is listed on any of the following lists:
(1)
List of hazardous constituents, codified as 40 CFR 261, Appendix VIII, as amended;
(2)
Groundwater monitoring list, codified as 40 CFR 264, Appendix IX, as amended;
(3)
List of hazardous substances and reportable quantities, codified as 40 CFR Table 302.4, and all appendices thereto, as amended;
(4)
List of regulated pesticides, codified as 40 CFR 180, as amended;
(5)
List of extremely hazardous substances and their threshold planning quantities, codified as 40 CFR 355, Appendix A, as amended; or
(6)
List of chemicals and chemical categories, codified as 40 CFR 372.65, as amended.
Oil means and includes, but is not limited to, gasoline, crude oil, fuel oil, diesel oil, lubricating oil, sludge, oil refuse, oil mixed with waste and any other petroleum related product.
Person means an individual, trust, firm, joint stock company, corporation, partnership, association, authority, county, municipality, commission, political subdivision of the state or any agency, board, department or bureau of any other state or the federal government.
Person who has contributed or who is contributing to a release means:
(1)
The owner or operator of a facility;
(2)
Any person who, at the time of disposal of any oil, hazardous waste, hazardous constituent or hazardous substance, owned or operated any facility at which such oil, hazardous waste, hazardous constituent or hazardous substance was disposed of;
(3)
Any person who, by contract, agreement or otherwise, arranged for disposal, treatment or transport of oil, hazardous wastes, hazardous constituents or hazardous substances. A person who arranged for the recycling of recovered materials consisting solely of scrap paper, scrap plastic, scrap glass, scrap textiles, scrap rubber other than whole tires, scrap metal or spent load-acid, nickel-acid, nickel-cadmium and other batteries, and not consisting of any residue from a pollution control device, shall not be deemed to have arranged for treatment or disposal under this subsection; and
(4)
Any person who accepts or accepted any oil, hazardous wastes, hazardous constituents or hazardous substances for transport to disposal or treatment facilities or sites selected by such person, from or at which facility or site there is a release of oil, hazardous waste, hazardous constituent or hazardous substance.
Release means any intentional or unintentional act or omission resulting in the spilling, leaking, pumping, pouring, emitting, emptying, discharging, injecting, landscaping, leaching, dumping or disposing into the environment, including, but not limited to, the abandonment or discarding of barrels, containers and other closed receptacles of any oil, hazardous waste, hazardous constituent or hazardous substance. Accidental discharges of oil made by a person during maintenance of that person's personal vehicle or farm machinery shall be exempt.
Reportable quantity means the amount of a hazardous substance which, if released into the environment in such quantity within any 24-hour period, must be reported to the environmental protection division of the state department of natural resources in the event of a spill or release. The reportable quantity for mixtures is the amount of the hazardous substance components of a mixture. Reportable quantities are as set forth in 40 CFR 302, Designation, Reportable Quantities and Notifications, as amended.
Site means the portion of the owner's contiguous property and any other owner's property affected by a release exceeding a reportable quantity.
(Ord. No. O-99-004, § II, 6-22-1999)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2
Sec. 66-153. - Reporting.
(a)
Any person owning, transporting or otherwise having control over any oil, hazardous waste, hazardous substance or hazardous constituent who has knowledge of any spill or release of any such oil, hazardous waste, hazardous substance or hazardous constituent in either a quantity equal to or exceeding the reportable quantity, or in an unknown quantity, shall immediately notify both the county and the environmental protection division of the state department of natural resources.
(b)
Failure to immediately notify either or both the county and the environmental protection division of the state department of natural resources shall be a violation of this article.
(Ord. No. O-99-004, § III, 6-22-1999)
Sec. 66-154. - Cleanup.
(a)
Each person who has contributed, or who is contributing, to the disposal or release of oil, hazardous constituent, hazardous substance or hazardous waste in a reportable quantity shall immediately commence proper, effective and environmentally sound cleanup of the disposal or release. Failure to immediately commence proper cleanup procedures or to immediately request such from the county shall be a violation of this article. Any cleanup conducted in an improper fashion, or which results in additional contamination or a spread of contamination, shall be a separate violation of this article.
(b)
In regard to actual cleanup, mitigation, monitoring and prevention measures, medical services or any other services necessitated by the release or disposal of an environmental hazard (the cleanup operation), the county, in the sole discretion of the commissioner or his authorized agent or representative, may contribute assistance to the cleanup operation; assume complete control and direction of the cleanup operation; initiate, commence and complete cleanup operations; conduct post cleanup inspections; conduct post-cleanup remedial or corrective operations; or contract with a corrective action contractor to perform any of such operations and inspections. Interference by any person with the operations and inspections shall be a separate violation of this article.
(Ord. No. O-99-004, § IV, 6-22-1999)
Sec. 66-155. - Liability for cleanup costs and punitive damages.
(a)
Each person who has contributed, or who is contributing, to the disposal or release of oil, hazardous constituent, hazardous substance or hazardous waste in a reportable quantity shall be jointly, severally and strictly liable to the county for the reasonable costs and expenses of activities associated with the cleanup of environmental hazards, including, but not limited to, actual cleanup, mitigation, monitoring and prevention measures and medical and legal expenses incurred by the county or any of its employees, departments or political subdivisions. The person may, in addition, be liable for punitive damages in an amount at least equal to the costs and expenses incurred by the county and not more than three times the costs and expenses incurred by the county for activities associated with the cleanup of environmental hazards. Costs, damages and legal expenses incurred by the county may be recovered in a civil action instituted in the name of the commissioner. All costs, expenses and damages recovered by the county pursuant to this section shall be deposited into the general fund.
(b)
Any action for the recovery of costs and punitive damages shall be commenced within six years of the date on which all costs have been incurred.
(c)
During or following the undertaking of any corrective action, any person may seek contribution from any other person, other than the county or any subdivision of the county, who has contributed, or is contributing, to any release of oil, hazardous waste, hazardous constituent or hazardous substance. Such claims for contribution shall be governed by the law of the state. In resolving contribution claims, the court may allocate costs among liable parties using such equitable factors as the court determines to be appropriate. In any action filed by the county for the recovery of costs, expenses and damages pursuant to this section, any third party claim for contribution may, upon the motion of the county, be severed and maintained as a separate action.
(Ord. No. O-99-004, § V, 6-22-1999)
Sec. 66-156. - Corrective action contractors.
A corrective action contractor shall not engage in activities within the county associated with the cleanup of environmental hazards created by others unless and until he provides proof of property and bodily injury liability insurance with coverage amounts of no less than $1,000,000.00 per person and $5,000,000.00 per incident.
(Ord. No. O-99-004, § VII, 6-22-1999)
Sec. 66-157. - Violation; penalty.
Any person violating any provision of this article shall be guilty of violating a duly adopted ordinance of the county and, upon conviction, such violation shall be punished as provided in section 1-12. O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq. shall govern the trial of any violation of this article, and any violation of this article may be tried upon citation as contemplated by O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63.
(Ord. No. O-99-004, § VI, 6-22-1999)
