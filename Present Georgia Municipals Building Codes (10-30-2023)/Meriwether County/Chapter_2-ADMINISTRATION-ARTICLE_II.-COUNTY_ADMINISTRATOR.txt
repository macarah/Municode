ARTICLE II. - COUNTY ADMINISTRATOR

Sec. 2-35. - General duties.
The county administrator shall be the chief executive officer of the county and head of the administrative branch of the county government. He shall be responsible to the board of commissioners for the proper administration of all affairs of the county, except as otherwise provided by law. It shall be his duty to:
(1)
Appoint and remove all officers and employees of the county. Any personnel duties given to the county administrator by this section may be delegated to a personnel manager designated as such by the board of commissioners.
(2)
See that all rules and regulations adopted by the board of commissioners are enforced.
(3)
Exercise control over all departments or divisions of the county which the board of commissioners has exercised, or that may be created.
(4)
Attend all meetings of the board of commissioners, with the right to take part in the discussion, provided, he shall have no vote before the board.
(5)
Prepare and submit to the board of commissioners an annual budget.
(6)
Keep the board of commissioners fully advised as to the financial condition and needs of the county.
(7)
Perform such other duties as may be required of him by the board of commissioners.
(Code 1991, § 2-61; Mo. of 8-10-1983; Ord. No. 93-04, 11-30-1993; Ord. No. 95-01, § 2, 2-13-1995)
Sec. 2-36. - Purchasing agent.
The county administrator shall be the chief purchasing agent for the county, by whom all purchases of supplies and all contracts therefor shall be made. He shall countersign with the finance director or chairperson, or in the absence of the chairperson, the vice-chairperson, all vouchers for the payment of same under such rules and regulations as the board of commissioners may adopt, not inconsistent herewith. The board shall adopt rules for regulating purchases made by the county and the county administrator shall be governed thereby, to include a determination from time to time of any maximum limitation on the amount the county administrator is authorized to spend for the purchase of any item or service by the county without the consent of the duly qualified members of the board.
(Code 1991, § 2-62; Mo. of 8-10-1983(a))
Sec. 2-37. - Co-sign payroll checks.
The county administrator is vested with the authority to countersign with the finance director or the chairperson or, in his absence from the county, the vice-chairperson, all payroll checks for the employees of the county.
(Code 1991, § 2-63; Mo. of 8-10-1983(b))
Sec. 2-38. - Supervision of roads, bridges.
The county administrator shall be directly responsible for the construction and maintenance of all roads and bridges under the jurisdiction of the county. He shall appoint a public works director whose primary duties shall be to supervise the construction and maintenance of all county roads and bridges and other construction projects as assigned.
(Code 1991, § 2-64; Mo. of 8-10-1983(b))
Sec. 2-39. - Reports.
The board of commissioners shall have the right to require the county administrator to go before them at any time and make such reports as they may deem necessary.
(Code 1991, § 2-65; Mo. of 8-10-1983(a))
Sec. 2-40. - Full-time position.
The county administrator shall devote his full time to his office, and shall hold no other office or employment while so engaged, unless approved in advance by the board of commissioners.
(Code 1991, § 2-67; Mo. of 8-10-1983(c))
