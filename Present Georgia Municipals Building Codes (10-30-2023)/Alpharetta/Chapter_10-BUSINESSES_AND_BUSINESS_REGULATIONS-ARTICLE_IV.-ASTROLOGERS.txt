ARTICLE IV. - ASTROLOGERS

Sec. 10-127. - Astrologers, etc.
(a)
Definition. The term "astrologer" shall include the terms "clairvoyant," "mesmerist," "card reader," "fortuneteller," "mind reader," "planet reader," "palmist," "seer," "trance medium," "test medium," "business medium," "phrenologist," "hypnotic healer," and "electric magnetic healer."
(b)
Prohibition. It shall be unlawful for any person to practice in the city the calling or profession of astrologer.
(c)
Advertising. It shall be unlawful for any person to advertise by sign, circular, handbill, newspaper or other publication in the city the practice of astrology.
(d)
Exception. The prohibitions set forth in this section shall not apply to any religious or spiritual functions of a bona fide church or religion, unless it is an alleged church or religion organized primarily to confer a certificate or commission upon a person for a price.
(Code 1989, § 12-20; Ord. No. 205, §§ 1—4, 2-11-1985)
State Law reference— Fortunetelling, phrenology, astrology, clairvoyance, etc., regulated, O.C.G.A. § 36-1-15.
