Chapter 12 - CEMETERIES[1]

State Law reference— Georgia Cemetery and Funeral Services Act of 2000, O.C.G.A. § 10-14-1 et seq.; city as trustee of cemetery funds, O.C.G.A. § 36-37-4 et seq.

Sec. 12-1. - Cemetery authority—Created.
There is created a cemetery authority to be known as the Alpharetta Cemetery Authority.
(Code 1989, § 6-1; Ord. No. 142, § 1, 8-25-1975)
Sec. 12-2. - Same—Composition; accounting for and auditing account.
The cemetery authority shall consist of the building and grounds committee. All funds coming under the purview of the authority shall be held in trust by the city, and shall be accounted for and audited the same as any other fiduciary account held by the city.
(Code 1989, § 6-2; Ord. No. 226, § 1, 5-27-1987)
Sec. 12-3. - Same—Property; finances.
The cemetery authority is authorized to hold cemetery property in trust for the city and is authorized to receive gifts, trusts, income from trusts, real estate and donations of every kind, and to hold the same as trustee for the city; to convey real estate so received to any person, church or corporation as trustees for the city; to invest in bank certificates or government bonds; to accept deeds to secure debt from any source; and to expend any and all moneys received for the upkeep and maintenance of the city cemeteries. Any gifts and trusts of real estate shall be received by the cemetery authority as trustees for the city. The authority shall have the right to convey lots or other real property connected with the city cemeteries as trustee for the city.
(Code 1989, § 6-3; Ord. No. 142, § 1, 8-25-1975)
Sec. 12-4. - Rest Haven Cemetery; assumption of ownership.
The city does assume full ownership and possession of Rest Haven Cemetery and claims all of the land located therein, with the exception of all lots privately owned.
(Code 1989, § 6-4; Ord. No. 142, § 1, 8-25-1975)
