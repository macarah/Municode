ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 20-1. - Fire prevention code—Adoption.
As to fire prevention code, adopted by reference, see section 8-29(a)(3).
(Code 1989, § 9-1)
Sec. 20-2. - Same—Modifications.
The chief of the fire department shall have the power to modify any of the provisions of the fire prevention code upon application in writing by the owner or lessee, or the duly authorized agent of either, when there are practical difficulties in the way of carrying out the strict letter of the code, provided that the intent of the code shall be observed, public safety secured, and substantial justice done. The particulars of such modification when granted or allowed, and the decision of the chief thereon, shall be entered upon the records of the department, and a signed copy shall be furnished the applicant.
(Code 1989, § 9-3; Ord. No. 165, § 11, 5-14-1979)
Sec. 20-3. - Same—New materials, processes or occupancies which require permits.
The mayor and city council, or their designee, and the chief of the fire department shall act as a committee to determine and specify, after giving affected persons an opportunity to be heard, any new materials, processes or occupancies which shall require permits, in addition to those now enumerated in the fire prevention code. The chief shall post such list in a conspicuous place in such chief's office, and distribute copies thereof to interested persons.
(Code 1989, § 9-4; Ord. No. 165, § 13, 5-14-1979)
Sec. 20-4. - Same—Appeals.
Whenever the chief of the fire department shall disapprove an application or refuse to grant a permit applied for, or when it is claimed that the provisions of the fire prevention code have been misconstrued or wrongly interpreted, the applicant is permitted to appeal from the decision of the chief of the fire department to the city council within 30 days from the date of the decision appealed.
(Code 1989, § 9-5; Ord. No. 165, § 12, 5-14-1979)
Sec. 20-5. - Limits of districts in which storage of explosives and blasting agents is prohibited.
The limits referred to in chapter 56 of the fire prevention code, in which storage of explosives and blasting agents is prohibited, are all areas within the incorporated limits of the city, except those areas zoned "I," light industrial district.
(Code 1989, § 9-6; Ord. No. 165, § 4, 5-14-1979)
Sec. 20-6. - Limits in which bulk storage of liquefied petroleum gases is to be restricted.
The limits referred to in chapter 61 of the fire prevention code, in which bulk storage of liquefied petroleum gas is restricted, are all areas within incorporated limits of the city, except those areas zoned "I," light industrial district.
Sec. 20-7. - Domestic sprinkler installation code.
The domestic sprinkler installation code attached as Exhibit A to the ordinance from which this section is derived shall govern the installation of domestic sprinklers in the city. Such code is hereby incorporated herein by reference as fully as if set out at length herein.
(Code 1989, § 9-12; Ord. No. 220, § 1, 11-25-1986)
Sec. 20-8. - Outdoor burning prohibited.
(a)
Except as otherwise provided by law, or as otherwise set forth in this Code, no persons shall cause, suffer, allow or permit open burning of refuse, rubbish, brush, wood, trash, leaves, tree limbs or other combustible materials anywhere in the city.
(b)
The following are exceptions to the prohibition on outside burning:
(1)
Outdoor burning in connection with the preparation of food for immediate human consumption;
(2)
Camp fires and fires used solely for recreational or ceremonial occasions, provided that the burning is approved in advance by the fire department and conducted pursuant to such conditions as the fire department may require;
(3)
Burning for the purpose of training private firefighting personnel when conducted in accordance with the guidelines set forth by the department of natural resources, subject to approval of the fire chief;
(4)
Burning associated with the operation of devices that require open flames, such as tar kettles, blow torches, welding torches, portable heaters and other flame-making equipment, provided that the use of such equipment does not violate other local or state laws; and
(5)
Burning associated with the disposal of packaging materials previously containing explosives, in accordance with the U.S. Department of Labor Safety Regulations.
(6)
Fires in recreational fire pits, as defined in the Unified Development Code (the "UDC") and subject to the size and location requirements contained in the UDC. Such fires are not required to be approved in advance by the public safety department and are not limited to cooking purposes. Fires in fire pits must be attended at all times, and smoke from a fire pit that creates a nuisance is prohibited. For the purposes of this section only, nuisance is defined as smoke that enters a neighboring home with windows and doors closed. If the public safety department receives complaints from more than one neighboring household within a twenty-four hour period, an officer shall respond and inspect, and make a reasonable determination based upon the nuisance standard in this section as to whether a fire should be extinguished and whether a warning or citation should be issued.
(c)
The fire chief, or his designee, and the director of environmental services, or his designee, shall be responsible for the enforcement of the provisions of this section, and may issue citations for violations thereof. Any person violating any provision of this section shall be fined not less than $50.00 nor more than $1,000.00 for each offense, and a separate offense shall be deemed committed on each day during or on which a violation occurs or continues. In addition, any person violating any provision of this section which results in a fire requiring the response of the fire department shall also be liable for the costs incurred by the fire department in responding to the fire, including actual labor costs, costs of equipment operation, and costs of materials.
(Code 1989, § 9-13; Ord. No. 187, § 16, 9-20-1982; Ord. No. 199, 5-14-1984; Ord. No. 439, § 1, 3-6-2000; Ord. No. 770, § 1, 11-12-2018)
Sec. 20-9. - Use of fire plugs.
(a)
No person, other than a city employee in the discharge of official duty, shall use water from a city fire plug for any purpose whatsoever unless that person has been granted a permit to do so by the city.
(b)
Any person found using water from a city fire plug by deliberately turning on such fire plug for personal use without the express permission of the qualified officials as required in subsection (a) of this section will be subject to punishment as provided in section 1-8.
(Code 1989, § 9-14; Ord. No. 95, §§ 1, 2, 10-26-1965)
Sec. 20-10. - Fire safety standards for plan review of new commercial construction.
(a)
The city adopts the minimum fire safety standards as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 25-2-12(a).
(b)
The fire chief shall be responsible for the reviews, inspections and enforcement required by such minimum fire safety standards.
(c)
At such time that the minimum standards are revised by the state, such revisions shall be incorporated into the city's standards, provided that nothing herein shall prohibit the city from adopting fire safety provisions more stringent than the state standards.
(Code 1989, § 9-15; Res. No. 396, 4-8-1991)
Sec. 20-11. - Enforcement powers.
The director of public safety, or his designees or officers, are authorized to enforce the provisions of the fire prevention code and this chapter. This authority shall include the power to arrest by the issuance of a citation and impose penalties in accordance with section 1-8 of the this Code. With respect to this chapter 20, the director of public safety may, but in no way is required to, promulgate, and from time to time amend, a list of applicable fees and penalties.
(Code 1989, § 9-16; Ord. No. 319, § 1, 9-27-1993; Ord. No. 806 , § 1, 9-8-2020)
Secs. 20-12—20-33. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - STANDARDS FOR MULTIFAMILY HOUSING

Sec. 20-34. - Scope.
(a)
The fire prevention standards set forth in this article shall apply to multifamily housing with three or more units and containing three or less stories.
(b)
Sections 20-42 and 20-43 shall apply to all attached and detached residential units.
(Code 1989, § 9-20; Ord. No. 204, 10-22-1984)
Sec. 20-35. - Accessibility for firefighting equipment.
(a)
Reasonable accessibility shall be provided to all buildings for firefighting equipment including ladder trucks on multiple-story projects. A minimum of ten feet of level ground on all sides of the building shall be considered reasonable for ground ladders. Accessibility for ladder trucks shall be determined and approved by the city fire department.
(b)
Access to the job site shall consist of roads or streets being finished to the extent that curbing and base coat must be applied before combustible construction starts, and shall be maintained throughout construction operations.
(Code 1989, § 9-22; Ord. No. 204, § 2, 10-22-1984)
Sec. 20-36. - Partitions for multifamily dwellings.
(a)
Every dwelling unit shall be separated from every other dwelling unit, hallway, or breezeway by partitions and floor-ceiling assemblies having a fire resistance rating of not less than one hour and be completely firestopped at each floor-ceiling level. The one-hour wall assemblies of the halls, corridors or breezeways may stop at the ceiling level if the ceiling is five-eighths-inch type X sheetrock and a smoke stop of one layer of one-half-inch regular sheetrock on the hall side of each wall and is continued to the roof deck. Exception: if the ceiling of the uppermost unit is one layer five-eighths-inch type X sheetrock, the one-hour partition shall be allowed to stop at the ceiling.
(b)
Buildings having only a single row of units shall have fire-stops between each unit. These fire-stops shall be not less than five-eighths-inch type X sheetrock, taped with one coat of joint compound. This firestopping must extend into soffits.
(c)
In buildings having units back to back, where roof trusses are perpendicular to the tenant separations, the fire stops shall separate every two units, but shall not, however, exceed 3,000 square feet between stops.
(d)
Doors to hallways and breezeways shall have a minimum fire rating of 20 minutes in a 20-minute wood frame with a closer. Door openings within 15 feet horizontally of a single exit stair shall have a three-quarter-hour door, with a closer in steel frame.
(e)
Buildings within 20 feet of other buildings shall have exterior exposed walls of not less than a one-hour fire rating with no openings.
(f)
Buildings using floor truss systems shall protect the end of these trusses where they meet the one-hour rated wall between units. The trusses shall be banded at the wall by two-inch nominal wood of sufficient width to completely fire stop the trusses horizontally. Note: Fee simple townhouses require two-hour separation, per city building code.
(Code 1989, § 9-23; Ord. No. 204, § 3, 10-22-1984)
Sec. 20-37. - Fire wall requirements for multifamily dwellings.
(a)
No building shall have more than 6,000 square feet per floor, nor more than six units between a four-hour fire wall.
(b)
Fire wall definition. Fire walls shall be self-supporting from the foundation, and shall extend through and 36 inches from combustible roofs, and flush with the outside of roof overhang. Fire walls need not extend through fire-resistance roof decks. Fire walls shall be four-hour rated. Certification of block will be required. There shall be no unprotected opening in fire walls.
(Code 1989, § 9-24; Ord. No. 204, § 4, 10-22-1984)
Sec. 20-38. - Requirements for early fire detection system and exits in multifamily dwellings.
(a)
An approved single station smoke detector, powered by the house electrical system, shall be installed in every living unit. There shall be additional detectors located in each exit corridor every 30 feet. For additional protection, a battery-powered backup detector is required.
(b)
Exits in multifamily units shall be as follows: every living unit shall have access to at least two separate exits remote from each other as required by 5-5.1 of the Life Safety Code adopted by the city.
(c)
Exception 1. Any living unit which has an exit directly to the street or yard at ground level or by way of an outside stairway, or an enclosed stairway with a fire resistance rating of one hour or more serving that apartment only and not communicating with any floor below the level of exit discharge or other area not a part of the apartment served, may have a single exit.
(d)
Exception 2. A building of any height with not more than four living units per floor with a smoke-proof tower or outside stair in accordance with the requirements of 5-2.3 of the Life Safety Code, adopted by the city, as the exit immediately accessible to all living units served thereby, may have a single exit. (The term "immediately accessible" means there is not more than 20 feet of travel distance to reach an exit from the entrance door of any living unit.)
(e)
Exception 3. Any building three stories or less in height with one-hour horizontal and vertical separation between living units, may have a single exit, under the following conditions:
(1)
The stairway is completely enclosed with a partition having a fire resistance rating of at least one hour, with self-closing one-hour fire protection rated doors protecting all openings between the stairway enclosure and the building.
(2)
The stairway does not serve more than one-half story below the level of exit discharge.
(3)
All corridors serving as access to exits have at least a one-hour fire resistance rating.
(4)
There is not more than 35 feet of travel distance to reach an exit from the entrance door of any living unit.
(Code 1989, § 9-25; Ord. No. 204, § 5, 10-22-1984)
Sec. 20-39. - Protection of hazardous areas.
All hazardous areas shall be separated from other parts of the building by construction having a fire resistance of at least one hour, with three-quarter-hour labeled doors, with steel frame and a closer. These areas shall include, but not be limited to, boiler or furnace rooms, general storage and maintenance shops, or parking or storage of motorized vehicles. This does not include furnaces or hot water heaters located within individual units which serve only that unit, nor does this include storage rooms in individual units.
(Code 1989, § 9-26; Ord. No. 204, § 6, 10-22-1984)
Sec. 20-40. - Control of flame spread.
The flame spread on carpet, wall coverings, paneling, or wood in multifamily units shall be as follows:
(1)
Vertical exits, corridors and breezeways shall have a Class "B" (26-75) flame spread rating.
(2)
The interior of the units shall have a Class "C" (76-200) flame spread rating.
(Code 1989, § 9-27; Ord. No. 204, § 7, 10-22-1984)
Sec. 20-41. - Opening through floors.
(a)
Any opening through floors from one unit to another for heating or plumbing shall be sealed with lightweight concrete or other permanently attached non-combustible material.
(b)
Any opening through any floor for any purpose shall be sealed in a like manner.
(c)
The double wall furnace vent must be sealed tight at each floor level and at the ceiling level of the top unit. The double wall furnace vent is not required to be enclosed in the attic space, but proper clearances must be maintained.
(d)
If a combustion air vent pipe is required for combustion air to the furnace, it shall extend up through the floor-ceiling assembly and into the attic 18 inches above the ceiling, with proper firestopping at each floor level.
(e)
Each attic space shall have an attic access measuring 22 inches by 36 inches. This access cannot be located in a closet, unless the closet is a walk-in, and must be shown on the plans.
(Code 1989, § 9-28; Ord. No. 204, § 8, 10-22-1984)
Sec. 20-42. - Chimney chases.
(a)
Exterior chase. A chimney chase located on an exterior wall shall have a five-eighths-inch type X fire rated sheetrock on the inside of the chimney chase, on the house side only.
(b)
Interior chase. The chase shall be constructed of metal studs with five-eighths-inch type X fire sheetrock on the outside, completely sealed from the rest of the building with no combustibles inside the chase.
(c)
General. If there is no fireplace above, firestopping at floor-ceiling levels may be omitted if there are no combustibles inside the chase. If there is a fireplace on the floor above, the bottom of the floor joist must be covered with five-eighths-inch type X fire rated sheetrock with metal fire stops installed around the flue.
(Code 1989, § 9-29; Ord. No. 204, § 9, 10-22-1984)
Sec. 20-43. - Firebox and fireplace openings.
(a)
The firebox must be installed on a wooden platform with a one-half-inch plywood floor. No wood shall be used to construct the raised hearth above the sub-floor. The hearth must extend at least 20 inches from the firebox.
(b)
The woodwork shall not be placed within six inches of the fireplace opening. The woodwork above and projecting more than 1½ inches from the fireplace opening shall not be placed less than 12 inches from the top of a fireplace opening.
(Code 1989, § 9-30; Ord. No. 204, § 10, 10-22-1984)
Secs. 20-44—20-61. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - CLEAN-UP, ABATEMENT, REMOVAL OF DEBRIS, HAZARDOUS MATERIAL OR REFUSE[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Georgia Hazardous Waste Management Act, O.C.G.A. § 12-8-60 et seq.

Sec. 20-62. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Debris means any loose material not attached to the land or any structure, or the remains of anything broken or destroyed.
Fire chief means the fire chief of the city or his appointed designee.
Hazardous material means any material that, because of its quantity, concentration or physical or chemical characteristics, poses significant present or potential hazard to human health and safety if released into the environment. Hazardous materials include, but are not limited to, explosives, pyrotechnics, flammable gas, flammable liquid, poisonous gas, poisonous liquid, irritating material, material that causes diseases in humans, radioactive material, corrosive material, or liquefied petroleum gas.
Refuse means all waste substances, including garbage, as well as combustible and non-combustible wastes.
(Code 1989, § 9-51; Ord. No. 257, § 1, 5-8-1989)
Sec. 20-63. - Deposit or release of debris, hazardous material and refuse; clean-up or abatement; liability for costs.
In the event the city fire department is summoned to clean up or abate the effects of any deposit or release of debris, hazardous material or refuse in the city, the following described persons shall be jointly and severally liable to the city for the payment of all costs incurred by the city as a result of such clean-up or abatement activity:
(1)
Any person whose negligent or willful act or omission proximately caused such deposit or release;
(2)
The person who owned or had custody or control of the debris, hazardous material or refuse at the time of or immediately prior to such deposit or release, without regard to fault or proximate cause; and
(3)
The person who owned or had custody or control of the container which held such debris, hazardous material or refuse at the time of or immediately prior to such deposit or release, without regard to fault or proximate cause.
(Code 1989, § 9-52; Ord. No. 257, § 2, 5-8-1989)
Sec. 20-64. - Verification and supervision.
In the event that any person other than a member of the city's fire department undertakes, either voluntarily or upon order of the fire chief or other city official, to clean up or abate the effects of the deposit or release of any debris, hazardous material or refuse, the fire chief may take such action as is necessary to supervise or verify the adequacy of the clean-up or abatement. The persons described in section 20-63 shall be liable to the city for all costs incurred as a result of such supervision or verification.
(Code 1989, § 9-53; Ord. No. 257, § 3, 5-8-1989)
Sec. 20-65. - Determination of costs; charges for service; disposition of funds; fee schedule.
(a)
The charges to persons under this article shall be based on actual costs incurred by the city and shall include, but shall not necessarily be limited to, actual labor costs or city personnel, including benefits and administrative overhead, cost of equipment operation, cost of materials obtained directly by the city and the cost of any contract labor and materials. Labor costs shall be based on the current fiscal year salary and benefit budget established for each employee. Equipment costs shall be based on the equipment cost schedule established in the city's financial system. The cost for materials used shall be based on the actual costs incurred to purchase, lease, or contract for goods and/or services during clean-up and abatement activities.
(b)
The charges for the provision of services under this article shall be binding upon all persons liable under this article as well as their successors in interest, assigns, estates and heirs.
(c)
All moneys received under this article shall be placed in a general fund revenue account.
(Code 1989, § 9-54; Ord. No. 257, § 4, 5-8-1989; Ord. No. 595, 6-19-2006)
Sec. 20-66. - Fire suppression services.
The authority to recover costs under this article shall not include actual fire suppression services which are normally or usually provided by the city's fire department.
(Code 1989, § 9-55; Ord. No. 257, § 5, 5-8-1989)
Sec. 20-67. - Collection of charges.
The city may enforce the provisions of this article by civil actions in an appropriate court for the collection of amounts due or other appropriate relief.
(Code 1989, § 9-56; Ord. No. 257, § 6, 5-8-1989)
Sec. 20-68. - Service not to be refused for nonpayment.
Nothing in this article shall authorize any city department or city personnel to refuse or delay any service to any person who has not reimbursed the city as provided in this article.
(Code 1989, § 9-57; Ord. No. 257, § 7, 5-8-1989)
Secs. 20-69—20-90. - Reserved.

Commercial hood suppression system(s);
(e)

Private hydrant system(s);
(h)
Fire pump(s);
(i)
Spray paint booth(es);
(j)
Emergency generator(s);
(k)
Cleaning agent suppression system(s);
(l)
Underground storage tank(s); and/or
(m)
Elevator or other lift system(s).
(Ord. No. 806 , § 2, 9-8-2020)
Sec. 20-93. - Compliance filings.
Every record required, under section 20-70, to be maintained on the premises of a facility shall, at the sole cost of the owner of the facility, be provided to the record custodian, by the method and in the format specified by the record custodian.
