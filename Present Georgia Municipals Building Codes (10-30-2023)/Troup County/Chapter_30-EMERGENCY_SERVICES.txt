ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 30-1. - 9-1-1 charge on prepaid wireless service.
(a)
9-1-1 charge on prepaid wireless transactions. In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2, there is hereby imposed a prepaid wireless 9-1-1 charge as defined by O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(a)(4) upon every prepaid wireless retail transaction occurring within the jurisdiction of the public service answering point in the amount of 75 cents.
(b)
Collection of 9-1-1 charge on prepaid wireless transactions. Prepaid wireless 9-1-1 charges collected by sellers shall be remitted to the commissioner of the department of revenue at the times and in the manner provided by O.C.G.A. tit. 48, ch. 8, with respect to the sales and use tax imposed on prepaid wireless calling service.
(c)
Administrative provisions. The Troup County Clerk is hereby directed to file with the state revenue commissioner a certified copy of this ordinance and amendments thereto, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(j)(1), within ten days of enactment of this section.
(d)
Depositing of funds; use of funds. In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(j)(5), funds received by Troup County from charges imposed by this section shall be deposited in the Emergency Telephone System Fund maintained by Troup County pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134 and kept separate from general revenue of the jurisdiction; all such funds shall be used exclusively for the purposes authorized by O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134(f).
( Ord. No. 2011-05 , §§ 1—4, 6-21-2011; Ord. No. 2012-01 , §§ 1—4, 7-26-2011)
Editor's note— Sections 1—4 of Ord. No. 2011-05 , adopted June 21, 2011, did not specify manner of inclusion; hence codification as § 30-1 was at the discretion of the editor.
The 9-1-1 charge on prepaid wireless service shall become effective January 1, 2012.

ARTICLE II. - ALARM SYSTEMS[2]

--- (2) ---

Sec. 30-31. - Purpose of article.
The purpose of this article is to regulate the installation and use of alarm systems, to establish standards for control of and maintenance of the various alarm systems that are intended to bring about sheriff's department, fire department and/or other emergency department response, and to provide penalties for violations.
(Code 1987, § 8.6-11)
Sec. 30-32. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alarm system means an assembly of equipment and devices designated and arranged to detect and signal an unauthorized intrusion into the premises, to signal an attempted burglary, robbery or other criminal activity in or upon the premises, a medical emergency needing medical attention, and/or an ambulance, or like occurrences.
Enforcement authority means the county, through the fire department, sheriff's department, or any other designated department.
Owner means the owner, lessee or occupant of the premises having an alarm system regulated under the provisions of this article.
Unauthorized alarm means the activation of an alarm system signal resulting in a response by the sheriff's department, fire department, or other county emergency department where none of the events or emergencies for which the alarm system has been installed have, in fact, occurred.
(Code 1987, § 8.6-12)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-4.
Sec. 30-33. - Scope of article provisions.
The provisions of this article shall apply to the installation, alteration, equipment, use and maintenance of alarm systems installed within the unincorporated area of the county.
(Code 1987, § 8.6-13)
Sec. 30-34. - Validity of other laws.
Nothing contained in this article shall be construed to prevent the enforcement of other laws or ordinances which prescribe more restrictive limitations.
(Code 1987, § 8.6-14)
Sec. 30-35. - Display of address required.
The owners of such alarm systems must display their address on the property such that is visible from the right-of-way.
(Code 1987, § 8.6-15)
Sec. 30-36. - Prohibited equipment.
An owner shall not use or operate, attempt to use or operate or cause to be used or operated, or arrange, adjust, program or otherwise provide or install any device or combination of devices that will, upon being activated, either mechanically, electronically, or by any other automatic means, call and deliver a recorded message to any 911 telephone number at the county E 911 center.
(Code 1987, § 8.6-16)
Sec. 30-37. - Prohibited connection.
An owner shall not place, install, join, connect or otherwise locate any central station alarm system equipment or device for direct access with the 911 telephone number.
(Code 1987, § 8.6-17)
Sec. 30-38. - Responsibility for installation and operation.
The owner of an alarm system shall be responsible for the proper installation and operation of the alarm system and obtain a written description of the function of the system along with the procedure and means for activating and deactivating the system.
(Code 1987, § 8.6-18)
Sec. 30-39. - Unauthorized alarm; user fees.
(a)
A user fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk is hereby imposed upon any owner for all false fire, burglar, holdup, panic, and/or medical alarms at all locations in the unincorporated county, in excess of two during any 30-day period. After four unauthorized alarms within any 30-day period, the matter may be referred to the proper enforcement authority for appropriate action which may include the issuance of a citation and prosecution for violation of this article. Notwithstanding anything contained in this subsection to the contrary, an owner installing or having installed on the premises a new alarm system shall be granted a grace period of 30 days beginning with the date of the initial installation, during which grace period the user fees provided in this section shall not apply.
(b)
Any unauthorized alarm which is caused by the alarm company will not be charged against the homeowner but will be charged against the alarm company. All alarm companies are required to contact the county E 911 center and advise what lines they are working on before they begin work and after the work is complete. An unauthorized alarm caused without notification to the E 911 center will result in a $50.00 fine per occurrence to the alarm company.
(Code 1987, § 8.6-19)
Sec. 30-40. - Deactivation of alarm systems.
The owner shall be responsible for deactivating or causing to deactivate the alarm system within a reasonable time following notification by the enforcement authority or any other county department providing notice.
(Code 1987, § 8.6-20)
Sec. 30-41. - Exception and force majeure.
In determining the number of unauthorized alarms chargeable to an owner, certain alarms shall be excluded as an unauthorized alarm if such alarm was determined to be caused under any one or more of the following circumstances:
(1)
Criminal activity or unauthorized entry onto the protected premises;
(2)
Earthquake or other natural occurrence causing structural damage to the protected premises;
(3)
High winds sufficient to activate motion detection systems or causing physical damage to the protected premises;
(4)
Flooding of the protected premises due to overflow of natural drainage;
(5)
Lighting bolt causing physical damage to the protected premises;
(6)
Telephone line malfunction verified in writing by a telephone company supervisor;
(7)
Electrical service interruption verified in writing by the electrical service supplier;
(8)
The generation of an unauthorized alarm which is beyond the reasonable control of the system user.
(Code 1987, § 8.6-21)
Sec. 30-42. - Violations and penalties.
(a)
Any person who violates other provisions of this article or any regulations promulgated pursuant hereto shall be guilty of the violation of this article and, upon conviction, shall be punishable by a fine of not more than $500.00 for the first violation and a fine of not more than $1,000.00 for each violation thereafter, or such other amounts as determined by the magistrate court.
(b)
Any person who intentionally activates any alarm system for any reason other than for those authorized by this article or during controlled testing of equipment shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor and, upon conviction thereof, shall be punishable by a fine of not more than $1,000.00 or up to 30 days in jail.
(c)
No person shall operate any audible alarm system that does not have an automatic 15-minute shutoff after activation. The enforcement authority shall take all appropriate actions to shut off an audible alarm that does not automatically shut off within 15 minutes of activation.
(Code 1987, § 8.6-22)
Sec. 30-43. - Exceptions.
The provisions of this article shall not apply to the following:
(1)
An alarm system installed in a motor vehicle; or
(2)
An alarm system installed upon the premises of a federal, state, county or municipal government or to the county school system.
(Code 1987, § 8.6-23)
Sec. 30-44. - Confidentiality of information on alarm system.
The information furnished and secured pursuant to this article is and shall be personal in nature and confidential and not subject to public inspection except where specifically required under provisions of the Georgia Open Records Act, O.C.G.A. §§ 50-18-70—50-18-76. Any employee of the enforcement authority, or other person providing the confidential information secured under this article to any unauthorized person, or using the information to the detriment of the owner, shall be guilty of a violation of this article and subject to the penalties under this article.
(Code 1987, § 8.6-24)
Sec. 30-45. - Enforcement responsibility.
Enforcement of this article shall be the responsibility of the appropriate enforcement authority of the county.
(Code 1987, § 8.6-25)
Sec. 30-46. - Liability of the county.
(a)
The county shall take every reasonable precaution to ensure that alarm messages or signals received by the county E 911 center are given appropriate attention and are acted upon with dispatch. Nevertheless, the county shall not be liable for any omission or neglect regarding the inspection of the installation or alteration of equipment, transmission of alarm signals, alarm messages, or the relaying of any such signals or messages to the E 911 center. Further, the county shall not be liable for any failure or neglect to respond appropriately upon receipt of a signal from an alarm system, nor for the failure or neglect of the county E 911 center or any other person or agency providing dispatch or monitoring services to properly notify the county's sheriff, fire, or other emergency response departments.
(b)
If any part of this article shall be declared unconstitutional by the valid judgment or decree of any court of competent jurisdiction such unconstitutionality shall not affect the remainder of the enactment, and such remainder shall remain in full force and effect.
(c)
All laws, ordinances and resolutions, or parts thereof, which conflict with the provisions of this article are hereby repealed.
(Code 1987, § 8.6-26)
