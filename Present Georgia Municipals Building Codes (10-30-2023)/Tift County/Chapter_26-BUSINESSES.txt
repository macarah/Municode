Sec. 26-26. - Business registration required for businesses operating in Tift County, Georgia.
(a)
For 2003 and succeeding years, each person or entity engaged in any business, or who is a practitioner of a profession or occupation in the unincorporated area of Tift County, Georgia (hereinafter "county"), whether maintaining a location or office in the county or, in the case of a business with no location in the county, exerting substantial efforts and conducting significant business within the county shall register annually with and obtain a county business license from the Tift County Code Administration Director (hereafter "director") or his/her designee at the county office of code enforcement on or before July 1 of each year and such license shall be conspicuously displayed in the business location in Tift County. If the taxpayer has no permanent business location in Tift County, such business registration shall be displayed to the director or his/her designees or to any law enforcement officer of Tift County upon request and shall be conspicuously displayed on any mobile business unit.
(b)
Upon registration, the director shall as soon as practicable inspect such registrant's business location or job sites, and perform a reasonable investigation of such registrant's business activities to insure that such activities comply with all applicable state or local building, health, or safety codes; to determine whether such registrant has in effect any and all required insurance coverage, and any required state or federal licenses or permits; that the registrant has complied with section 26-30 herein; and to determine whether such registrant's business, trade, profession or occupation poses any threat to the safety, welfare, or public health of the citizens of Tift County. Inspection need not be completed prior to issuance of a county business license, but such license is subject to suspension if upon inspection the director finds the registrant has not complied with any and all applicable local, state and or federal ordinances, regulations, and laws or has failed to pay the regulatory fees required under subsection (c) below.
(c)
Upon registering, each registrant shall pay an annual regulatory fee in the amount of $45.00 to defray the costs of inspections contemplated at subsection (b) above.
(Ord. No. 2003-03, 5-12-03; Ord. No. 2003-04, 6-16-03; Ord. No. 2004-01, 3-8-04)
Sec. 26-27. - Construction of terms; definitions.
(a)
Wherever the term "Tift County" or "county" is used herein, such term shall be construed to mean Tift County, Georgia.
(b)
As used in this article, the term:
(1)
Location or office shall include any structure or vehicle where a business, profession or occupation is conducted, but shall not include a temporary or construction work site which serves a single customer or project or a vehicle used for sales or delivery by a business or practitioner of a profession or occupation which has a location or office. A renter's or lessee's location which is the site of personal property which is rented or leased from another does not constitute a location or office for the personal property's owner, lessor, or the agent of the owner or lessor. The site of real property which is rented or leased to another does not constitute a location or office for the real property's owner, lessor, or the agent of the owner or lessor unless the real property's owner, lessor, or the agent of the owner or lessor, in addition to showing the property to prospective lessees or tenants and performing maintenance or repair of the property, otherwise conducts the business of renting or leasing real property at such site or otherwise conducts any other business, profession or occupation at such site.
(2)
Person shall be held to include individuals, sole proprietors, corporations, LLCs, LLPs, partnerships, nonprofits, or any other form of business organization or entity, but specifically excludes charitable nonprofit organizations.
(3)
Practitioners of professions and occupations shall not include a practitioner who is an employee of a business, if the business registers, pays the regulatory fee, and obtains the county business license.
(4)
Tift County Code Administration Department (the "department")is the agency charged with enforcement of this article.
(5)
Tift County Code Administration Director (the "director") is the director of the code administration department and the person charged with enforcement of this article.
(Ord. No. 2003-03, 5-12-03; Ord. No. 2003-04, 6-16-03; Ord. No. 2004-01, 3-8-04)

Cafe, restaurant, cafeteria: Must have approval from all appropriate regulating agencies before license is issued.

(k)
Massage therapist: Must show certification.

Said report shall specify the article or thing giving a full description thereof such as a number, mark, and all other details as will make identification of such articles clear and positive. Person or persons operating a pawnshop or engaged in the business of a pawnbroker shall hold their shops open to visitation and inspection by the sheriff or his deputies. Precious metals dealer: Must be approved by the sheriff.
(n)
Sales, closing out, quitting business, going out of business: It shall be unlawful and a violation of this article for any person, firm, partnership, or corporation to sell, offer or expose for sale, or to include in such inventory for sale any goods, wares, or merchandise which are not an actual part of the original stock of goods of the applicant. It shall further be unlawful for any person to hold or conduct a "Closing Out Sale," "Going Out of Business Sale" or a sale that is designated in any manner by which it is represented that the business of such person is being terminated unless such business is in fact terminated at the end of such sale; provided, that any such sale shall not last for more than 60 consecutive calendar days. The board of commissioners may, upon request of the business owner, grant an additional 30 days for such sale upon evidence that such extension is necessary; provided, however, that only one such extension may be granted. These provisions shall not apply to trustees in bankruptcy, executors or administrators of estates, receivers or public officials acting under judicial process.
(o)
Taxicabs, transportation, nonemergency: Before issuance of any license, applicant must have proof of insurance coverage, property damage and personal liability for a minimum of $50,000.00 and deposit a copy of such policies with the code administration director or his designee covering each and every vehicle used in such taxicab/nonemergency transportation business. Each taxicab shall frame and display license in view of passengers at all times.
(p)
Tree surgery: In addition to business license, must also have proof of liability insurance in the minimum amounts of $50,000.00 for bodily injury and $100,000.00 property damage indemnifying Tift County and any person injured or damaged resulting from the pursuit of such activities.

Trenching and pipe laying: In addition to business license, must also obtain permit and post bond as set by the board of commissioners prior to engaging in any work within the county's right-of-way.
(r)
Commercial and residential building contractors: Must verify current Tift County contractor's license and must obtain liability insurance coverage of at least $100,000.00 and workers compensation as required by Georgia law and provide proof thereof before the issuance of license.
(s)
Any business not specifically identified above but required to be licensed by the state shall present such license.

Sec. 26-51. - Applicability of article.
(a)
This article shall be applicable to all stores, swap shops, pawnshops, and businesses dealing, buying, selling, swapping, and any form of pawning of used merchandise, furniture, machinery, appliances, utensils, firearms, gold, silver, coins, precious metals, jewelry and precious stones, whether such sales, trades, purchases and swaps are retail, wholesale, or by any pawning in the unincorporated areas of the county. Specifically excluded are businesses dealing in buying or selling new merchandise. Isolated transactions between persons not engaged in business are not covered by the terms of this article.
(b)
This article is applicable to any business, person, association, partnership or corporation who shall engage in the business of selling, purchasing, exchanging, swapping or pawning of used merchandise, furniture, machinery, appliances, utensils, firearms, gold, silver, coins, precious metals, jewelry and precious stones within the unincorporated areas of the county.
(Ord. of 4-4-88(1), §§ 1, 3; Mo. of 2-9-89)
Sec. 26-52. - Enforcement of article.
The county magistrate's court shall have jurisdiction over violators of this article, and all procedures for enforcement of such article shall be as provided in O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq. Complaints of violations of any provision of this article shall be brought before the magistrate's court by the county sheriff, any county deputy sheriff, the chairman of the board of commissioners or a designated representative of the chairman of the board of commissioners and shall be prosecuted through that court.
(Ord. of 4-4-88(1), § 7)
Sec. 26-53. - Penalty for violation of article.
Any person convicted of violating this article shall be punished as provided in section 1-11.
(Ord. of 4-4-88(1), § 6)
Sec. 26-54. - Engaging in business affected with public interest.
Engaging in the business of buying, selling, swapping or pawning used merchandise, furniture, machinery, appliances, utensils, firearms, gold, silver, coins, precious metals, jewelry and precious stones within the unincorporated areas of the county is hereby declared to be affected with the public interest due to the opportunity it affords for the disposal of stolen property.
(Ord. of 4-4-88(1), § 2; Mo. of 2-9-89)
Sec. 26-55. - Dealing with minors.
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this article for any business to receive in pawn, trade or to buy from minors used merchandise, furniture, machinery, appliances, utensils, firearms, gold, silver, coins, precious metals, jewelry and precious stones. A minor, for the purpose of this article, is any individual who has not reached the age of 18 years.
(Ord. of 4-4-88(1), § 4)
Sec. 26-56. - Regulations pertaining to buying and pawning of merchandise; receiving merchandise in swaps; reports of purchases.
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this article for any business covered by this article to buy, receive in pawn or receive in a swap any used merchandise, furniture, machinery, appliances, utensils, firearms, gold, silver, coins, precious metals, jewelry and precious stones, without complying with the following regulations:
(1)
At the time of the sale, pawn or swap there shall be obtained from the seller, on a written form which the seller must sign, the following information: seller's name, residence address, date of birth, place of employment and identification of the item sold which will include the kind, serial number and manufacturer of the item.
(2)
The information set out in subsection (1) of this section shall be placed upon a written form which the seller shall sign, and this information shall be made in duplicate on this form. Below the signature of the seller the name of the business and the location shall be shown, along with the date and hour of the transaction. Following this, the manager or person in charge of the business shall sign his name.
(3)
The forms set forth in subsection (2) of this section shall be provided by the county sheriff's department to the business. The original signed form showing the information called for in subsection (2) of this section shall be delivered by the business to the county sheriff's department on the following day after the transaction is completed. Such delivery shall be made by hand or by placing the original in the U.S. mail, addressed to the county sheriff's department with adequate postage thereon.
(4)
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this article for any person, business, association, partnership or corporation, pawnshop or firm required by subsection (1) of this section to fail to obtain the information required in subsection (1) of this section or fail to provide such information to the county sheriff's department as required by subsection (3) of this section.
(5)
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this article for any person selling, pawning or swapping as set forth in subsection (1) of this section to give false information to the seller or to answer falsely any of the information required in subsection (1) of this section.
(Ord. of 4-4-88(1), § 5; Mo. of 2-9-89)

ARTICLE IV. - ITINERANT MERCHANTS[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— Peddlers and itinerant traders, O.C.G.A. § 43-32-1 et seq.; exception from local peddler or itinerant vendor licensing requirements for persons selling agricultural products, O.C.G.A. § 43-32-6.

Sec. 26-76. - Applicability of article.
This article shall be applicable to all traveling and itinerant dealers; salesmen; and buyers of gold, silver, jewelry, coins and other precious metals of any kind or description, whether such sales, trades and purchases are retail, wholesale or by any form of pawn. Specifically excluded are wholesalers, buyers and sellers who sell, trade and deal only with mercantile establishments located in the unincorporated areas of the county. Isolated transactions between persons not engaged in business are not covered by the terms of this article.
(Ord. of 5-2-88, § 1)
Sec. 26-77. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Itinerant or traveling dealer, salesman or buyer means a person, association, partnership or corporation which does not have a business license in the county and who shall engage in the business of purchasing, trading, or otherwise dealing in gold, silver, jewelry, coins and other precious metals of any kind or description, from members of the general public within the unincorporated areas of the county.
(Ord. of 5-2-88, § 2)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 26-78. - Penalty for violation of article.
Any person violating any provision of this article shall, upon conviction, in the county magistrate court, be punished as provided in section 1-11.
(Ord. of 5-2-88, § 12)
Sec. 26-79. - Engaging in business affected with public interest.
Engaging in the business of buying or dealing gold, silver, jewelry, coins and other precious metals by a traveling or itinerant dealer, salesman or buyer within the unincorporated limits of the county is hereby declared to be affected with the public interest because of the opportunity it affords for the disposal of stolen property.
(Ord. of 5-2-88, § 3)
Sec. 26-80. - License—Registration required before issuance.
(a)
All applicants for a business license under the provisions of this article shall, at least 72 hours prior to the time the applicant desires the business license, register with the county sheriff on a form to be provided by the sheriff. All applicants for registration and license shall furnish, in addition to such other information as the sheriff shall require, the following information to be written and signed on the registration form:
(1)
Complete name of the person, corporation, partnership or association and its address.
(2)
The name of the person who will conduct the business in the county and his local address.
(3)
The location of the business in the unincorporated area of the county and the hours that such business will operate.
(4)
The length of time the business is to be conducted, including the dates of opening and closing.
(b)
It is hereby declared to be unlawful and a violation of this article for any itinerant or traveling dealer, salesman or buyer to engage in the buying, selling, trading or otherwise dealing in gold, silver, jewelry, coins or other precious metals within the unincorporated areas of the county without first complying with the registration set forth in subsection (a) of this section, other provisions of this article, and obtaining a license as provided in this section.
(c)
No person shall engage in the business or activity regulated by this article or shall be a member of a partnership, association or an officer, stockholder or director in a corporation engaged in such business or activity, who shall have been convicted of a felony or misdemeanor involving moral turpitude.
(d)
Within 72 hours after the registration as provided for in this section, the sheriff shall cause an investigation to be made of the applicant and his proposed business operation. If, upon investigation, the sheriff shall find that the applicant is of good moral character and has never been convicted of a felony or a misdemeanor involving moral turpitude, and has met all the requirements of this article, he shall approve the issuance of a business license to the applicant. Upon approval by the sheriff and upon payment of a license tax to the county as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges, a license shall be issued to the applicant. It shall be the duty of the county sheriff's department to enforce this article.
(Ord. of 5-2-88, § 4)
Sec. 26-81. - Same—Licensee required to keep records.
Every licensee under the provisions of this article shall keep a register upon which the licensee shall record the name, address, weight and height, social security number, driver's license number, a brief description of the person, or other identification of every person who buys, sells, trades for, or otherwise disposes of gold, silver, jewelry, coins or other precious metals, together with the date and time of the transaction, the amount paid or advanced, a full description of each article or item including the kind, style, material, color, make, all identifying names, numbers or marks. Such entry shall be made at the time the transaction occurs. Prior to dealing with a person the licensee shall require positive identification, either from a driver's license, social security card or other identifying document.
(Ord. of 5-2-88, § 5)
Sec. 26-82. - Same—Revocation or suspension.
Licenses issued pursuant to this article shall be subject to revocation or suspension by the chairman of the board of commissioners upon recommendation by the sheriff for the violation of or the failure to comply with any provision of this article or for the violation of any law of the state, after reasonable notice and an opportunity to be heard has been given the licensee. The chairman of the board of commissioners shall immediately notify any licensee of any suspension or revocation of his license.
(Ord. of 5-2-88, § 11)
Sec. 26-83. - Report to sheriff; contents; submission.
Every licensee shall file daily written reports with the county sheriff by 10:00 a.m. on each business day, setting forth the transactions of the preceding business day and give on the report or reports the information set forth in section 26-81. It is hereby made the duty of the sheriff to obtain such reports promptly each business day.
(Ord. of 5-2-88, § 6)
Sec. 26-84. - Selling, melting or disposing of articles.
No gold, silver, jewelry, coins or other precious metals shall be sold, melted, altered or disposed of by any licensee until five days have elapsed from the time the sheriff has been given the report in which such articles or items are entered, and such articles or items shall not be transferred from within the county during the five-day period of time.
(Ord. of 5-2-88, § 7)
Sec. 26-85. - Dealing with minors.
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this article for any licensee to receive in pawn, trade or to buy from minors or to sell to minors goods, metals or articles of any character or description. A minor, for the purpose of this article, is an individual who has not reached the age of 18.
(Ord. of 5-2-88, § 8)
Sec. 26-86. - Prohibited items; exemptions.
No licensee shall take in pawn, receive, sell, trade or exchange any of the following articles: Shotgun or rifle with a barrel length of less than 18 inches, knucks, dirk, sword cane, blackjack, Bowie knife, spring-back knife, switchblade knife, or any knife with a blade exceeding four inches in length. It is not the intention of this section to prohibit case knives, butcher knives or other knives customarily used for domestic or industrial purposes only, nor shall any licensee own or keep on hand any of the weapons named or referred to in this section.
(Ord. of 5-2-88, § 9)
Secs. 26-87—26-110. - Reserved.

All applicants for permits shall furnish, in addition to such other information as the clerk shall require, the following information to be written, signed and attested to on the registration form:
(1)
Complete name of the person or company and its permanent address.
(2)
Phone number of the permanent office of the person or company.
(3)
