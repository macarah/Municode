ARTICLE IV. - NATURAL GAS

Sec. 45-93. - Geographic area served.
The city operates a natural gas enterprise that serves a geographic area, prescribed by the state public service commission, that includes the city and portions of Bartow, Floyd and Gordon counties.
(Res. No. 14-0018, 9-11-2014)
Sec. 45-94. - Residential and commercial general service.
(a)
General residential and commercial service is applicable to any customer whose annual natural gas consumption requirements are less than 2,000 Mcf. Such services are available anywhere on the existing city system, both inside and outside the corporate limits.
(b)
A base tap fee will be charged for all new general services which will include up to 200 feet of new service line not greater than three-quarter inch in diameter. After the initial 200 feet, additional per-foot fees shall apply. The tap fee for installation of new service lines larger than three-quarter inch in diameter shall be based on the city's actual installation costs.
(c)
Tap fees and other installation costs, monthly base charges and city distribution fees shall be in the amount provided in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(d)
The charge for the cost of gas per Mcf may vary monthly and shall be based on all applicable commodity, pipeline and other gas charges associated with the purchase of natural gas for delivery to the customer.
Sec. 45-95. - Large volume users.
(a)
Initial service charge. A tap fee will be based city's actual cost to install service line and meter set.
(b)
Availability. Anywhere on the existing city system both inside and outside the corporate limits where sufficient capacity is available. Additional capacity may be made available by main line extension on a case by case assessment.
(c)
Applicability. Applicable to any large volume customer whose annual natural gas requirements are greater than 2,000 Mcf.
(1)
Monthly base charge will be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(2)
City distribution fee will be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(3)
The charge for the cost of gas per Mcf may vary monthly and shall be based on all applicable commodities, pipeline, and other gas charges associated with the purchase of natural gas for delivery to the customer.
(Res. No. 14-0018, 9-11-2014)

Sec. 45-97. - Special provisions.
Whenever curtailment or interruption of gas delivered hereunder is required, the city shall verbally or in writing issue a curtailment order to the customer specifying quantity of gas to be curtailed and the time at which such curtailment is to be made. When restoration of service is permissible, the city shall similarly issue a restoration order specifying the quantity of gas to be restored and the time at which such restoration is to be made. The customer shall carry out all such orders at the time specified therein. A curtailment order shall be issued at least 12 hours in advance of its effective time. In the event the customer fails to discontinue the use of gas after 12-hours' notice that the interruptible gas under his schedule is not available, or if the customer shall exceed the demand set forth in the available amount issued under curtailment, all gas so used shall be paid for by the customer at a rate as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk, in addition to the regular commodity charge for such gas.
(Res. No. 14-0018, 9-11-2014)
Sec. 45-98. - Poultry gas service.
(a)
Initial service charge. A tap fee will be based city's actual cost to install service line and meter set.
(b)
Availability. Anywhere on the existing city system both inside and outside the corporate limits where sufficient capacity is available. Additional capacity may be made available by main line extension on a case by case assessment.
(c)
Applicability. Applicable to any poultry grower agreeing to use natural gas purchased from city as their primary fuel source for space heating on the premises served; no other fuel may be used for these purposes. No gas may be resold or transported to other facilities.
(1)
Monthly base charge will be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk and deposit will be waived.
(2)
City distribution fee will be based on the municipal gas authority's negotiated poultry program rate.
(Res. No. 14-0018, 9-11-2014)
Sec. 45-99. - Compressed natural gas service.
(a)
Initial service charge. A tap fee will be based city's actual cost to install service line and meter set.
(b)
Availability. Anywhere on the existing city system both inside and outside the corporate limits where sufficient capacity is available.
(c)
Applicability. Applicable to any company whose business involves the compressing and selling of natural gas to the public or private fleets.
(1)
Monthly base charge as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(2)
City's distribution fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(3)
The charge for the cost of gas per Mcf may vary monthly and shall be based on all applicable commodities, pipeline, and other gas charges associated with the purchase of natural gas for delivery to the customer.
(d)
Fees. Tap fees and deposits are required consistent with the city's utility collections resolution.
(Res. No. 14-0018, 9-11-2014)
