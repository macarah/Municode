Sec. 7-1. - Fire inspectors.
The chief of the fire department is permitted to detail such members of the fire department as inspectors as shall from time to time be necessary. The chief of the fire department shall recommend to the city manager and governing body the employment of technical inspectors, who, when such authorization is made, shall be selected through an examination to determine their fitness for the position. The examination shall be open to members and nonmembers of the fire department, and appointments made after examination shall be for an indefinite term, with removal only for cause.
(Code 1963, § 7-2(c))
Sec. 7-2. - Reports of fire department activities; recommendations.
A report of the activities of the fire department shall be made annually and transmitted to the city manager; it shall contain all proceedings under the fire prevention code, with such statistics as the chief of the fire department decides to include therein or as requested by the city manager; the chief of the fire department shall also recommend any amendments to the fire prevention code which, in the chief's judgment, shall be desirable.
(Code 1963, § 7-2(d))
Sec. 7-3. - Cooperation by other city officials.
The chief of the fire department may request and shall receive so far as may be necessary, in the discharge of such chief's duties, the assistance and cooperation of other officials of the municipality.
(Code 1963, § 7-2(e))
Sec. 7-4. - Plan review and certificate of occupancy fees.
In addition to other fees and licenses as may be required, a plan review fee for each review by the fire department of proposed construction plans shall be assessed in the amount of ten cents ($0.10) for each one thousand dollars ($1,000.00), or fraction thereof, of construction cost with a minimum fee of twenty dollars ($20.00). A fire safety certificate of occupancy, issued by the fire department upon satisfactory completion of construction, shall require a fee of fifty dollars ($50.00).
(Code 1963, § 7-1(c))
Sec. 7-5. - Permits and licenses.
(a)
Permits covered by Chapter 4 of the fire prevention code, and required in various sections of this chapter or in other city fire prevention ordinances are for the purpose of giving the fire department more effective control over hazardous conditions regulated by the code and related ordinances. Where fees are charged for such permits, the money for fees shall be handled by the city clerk or the clerk's designated agents.
(b)
Where licenses are required by the city, in addition to the permits required by the fire prevention code, and the licenses are renewable each year, permits shall remain in effect during the time that the business license is valid. In cases where business licenses may not be required, the permit shall remain in effect for one year after issuance. The chief of the fire department shall have authority to revoke any permit where the applicant fails to meet code requirements.
(Code 1963, § 7-1(d), (e))
Sec. 7-6. - Appeals.
Whenever the fire chief shall disapprove an application or refuse to grant a permit applied for, or when it is claimed that the provisions of the fire prevention code or other provisions of this chapter do not apply or that the true intent and meaning of the code has been misconstrued or wrongly interpreted, the applicant is permitted to appeal from the decision of the chief to the governing body within thirty (30) days from the date of the decision appealed.
(Code 1963, § 7-14)
Sec. 7-7. - Penalties.
(a)
Any person who shall violate any of the provisions of the fire prevention code hereby adopted, or other provisions of this chapter, or fail to comply therewith, or who shall violate or fail to comply with any order made thereunder, or who shall build in violation of any detailed statement of specifications or plans submitted and approved thereunder, or any certificate or permit issued thereunder, and from which no appeal has been taken, or who shall fail to comply with such an order as affirmed or modified by the governing body, or by a court of competent jurisdiction, within the time fixed herein, shall severally for each such violation and noncompliance respectively, be guilty of a misdemeanor, punishable as provided in section 1-8. The imposition of one penalty for any violation shall not excuse the violation or permit it to continue; and all such persons shall be required to correct or remedy such violations or defects within a reasonable time; and, when not otherwise specified, each day that prohibited conditions are maintained shall constitute a separate offense.
(b)
The application of the above penalty shall not be held to prevent the enforced removal of prohibited conditions.
(Code 1963, § 7-16)

Sec. 7-20. - Establishment of fire lanes on private property, devoted to public use.
(a)
Fire lane access to buildings by fire apparatus referred to in the fire prevention code are hereby established as follows: All premises which the fire department may be called upon to protect in case of fire, and which are not readily accessible from public roads, shall be provided with suitable gates, access roads, and fire lanes so that all buildings on the premises are readily accessible to fire apparatus. All exposures within the city (except single-and two-family dwellings) shall have fire apparatus access to a public thoroughfare a minimum width of twenty (20) feet, as approved by the fire marshal on at least one side of the exposure. Access to alternate sides of the exposure shall be provided for fire apparatus in cases where more than two hundred (200) feet of hose lay, as measured from the access side of the exposure, would be required to reach the far side of the exposure. Hose lay may be increased to four hundred (400) feet, provided the exposure has a total sprinkler system. All access requirements shall be approved by the fire marshal.
(b)
These lanes shall be established on private property, devoted to public use, where the parking of motor vehicles or other obstructions can interfere with the ingress and egress of fire department vehicles for the protection of persons and property, such as at shopping centers, bowling lanes, theaters, hospitals, churches, and similar locations. The width and route of such lanes shall be specifically determined by the city.
(c)
The marking of fire lanes on private property devoted to public use, shall be approved by the chief of the fire department and the chief of the police department. When it is determined that fire lanes are needed, the designation of the area as a fire lane shall be prepared with a description of the area. Copies of the description and the designation shall be signed by the fire chief and the police chief and shall be filed in the city clerk's office. Marking of the fire lanes shall be done by installing a "no parking, fire lane" sign, and the owner of the location shall provide yellow markings on the street and curb to identify the fire lane area. Markings shall be as determined by the fire chief. The fire chief may waive the requirement for yellow markings on the curb, but not on the street, upon a showing to his or her professional satisfaction that yellow markings on the street alone will be so designed as to provide an equal level of protection of the safety of the public as yellow markings on both the curb and the street.
(d)
Parking of motor vehicles obstructing fire lanes shall be prohibited.
(e)
Any person who shall park in a designated fire lane, in violation of the markings at said location, shall, upon the entering of a plea of guilty, nolo contendere, or a finding of guilty, be fined one hundred dollars ($100.00).
(Code 1963, § 7-10; Ord. No. 92-6, § 2, 3-16-92; Ord. No. 2002-18, § 1, 5-20-02)
Sec. 7-21. - Fire hydrant and water main installation, spacing and maintenance.
(a)
Water mains installed in shopping centers, malls and industrial areas, and in other areas not specified in this section, shall be a minimum of eight (8) inches in diameter, provided an adequate gridiron is completed. The fire marshal shall determine the adequacy of the gridiron and may require larger pipe sizes depending upon the size, construction, layout and occupancy of the buildings serviced.
(b)
Water mains installed in multiple housing developments shall be a minimum of six (6) inches in diameter, but only where an adequate gridiron is completed, and no longer than six hundred (600) feet in length shall be permitted between interconnecting mains. Should six hundred (600) feet between interconnecting mains be exceeded, then all mains shall be eight (8) inches in diameter at a minimum. The fire marshal shall determine the adequacy of the gridiron and may require larger pipe sizes, depending upon the size, construction, layout and occupancy of the buildings serviced.
(c)
Fire hydrants installed in shopping centers, malls and industrial areas, and in areas not otherwise specified in this section, shall be spaced a maximum of three hundred (300) feet apart, and so located that all buildings in the complex can be reached by comparatively short hose lays as determined by the fire marshal.
(d)
Fire hydrants installed in multiple housing developments shall be spaced a maximum of five hundred (500) feet apart and located in such a way as to permit hose lays of no more than four hundred (400) feet. Should it become necessary for the city to place hydrants on private property to meet the above requirements, the owners of said properties so serviced shall be required to maintain said hydrants according to the specifications of the city.
(e)
Fire hydrants installed by private companies and individuals must comply with installation specifications of the city.
(f)
All owners of private property, including owners of multi-family structures, within the city, with existing exposures which do not meet the current code requirements for fire hydrants and water main installations, shall be required to meet the following minimum standards:
(1)
Where the existing fire hydrant spacing exceeds the requirements of this section by more than fifty (50) per cent, additional fire hydrants shall be installed to reduce the spacing to no more than fifty (50) per cent of the requirements of this section.
(2)
Where existing fire hydrant spacing requires hose lays of more than six hundred (600) feet, additional fire hydrants shall be installed to reduce such hose lays to no more than six hundred (600) feet.
(3)
Sizing and installation of piping and water mains servicing fire hydrants shall conform with the requirements of this section.
(4)
No fire hydrant connected to a sprinkler system, standpipe system, or other private fire protection system shall be considered in determining the minimum code requirements for fire hydrants.
(Code 1963, § 7-11)
Sec. 7-22. - Fireworks.
(a)
Defined. The term "fireworks" shall include and mean any combustible or explosive composition, or any substance or combination of substances, or article prepared for the purpose and producing a visible and/or an audible effect by combustion, explosion, deflagration or detonation, and shall include blank cartridges, toy pistols, toy cannon, toy canes or toy guns in which explosives are used, the type of balloons which require fire underneath to propel the same, firecrackers, torpedoes, skyrockets, Roman candles, cherry bombs, sparklers or other fireworks of like construction, and any fireworks containing any explosive or flammable compound, or any tablets or other device containing any explosive substance; however, the term "fireworks" shall not include toy pistol paper caps in which the explosive content does not average more than one-fourth (0.25) grain per cap, and toy pistols, toy cannon, toy cones, toy guns or other devices using paper caps.
(b)
Prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any person to offer for sale, expose for sale, sell, use, ignite, explode or posses any fireworks within the city.
(Code 1963, § 16-15; Ord. No. 2001-03, § 1, 1-16-01)

Sec. 7-23. - Smoke detectors.
(a)
Smoke detectors are required for all the following:
(1)
For every multi-family dwelling containing three (3) or more dwelling units, a smoke detector shall be installed in each dwelling unit. If, in the opinion of the chief of the fire prevention bureau, a single smoke detector in a dwelling unit will not provide adequate warning signals, the chief shall require the installation of additional smoke detectors. Additionally, for every such multi-family dwelling containing three (3) or more dwelling units, smoke detectors shall be installed in each hallway or corridor, exitway, and lobby as provided in subsection (2) below.
(2)
For every hotel, motel, dormitory, rooming house, and such other similar structures, smoke detectors shall be installed in each bedroom and residential room, and in each hallway or corridor, exitway and/or lobby. The installation of the required detectors in hallways, corridors, exitways and lobbies shall be on thirty-foot centers on smooth ceilings. For areas of beamed or joisted ceilings, the chief of the fire prevention bureau shall approve placement based on practical knowledge and in conjunction with NFPA 72, 72E, and 74.
(3)
For every business occupancy, assembly occupancy, church, school, auditorium and such other similar structures, a smoke detector shall be placed in each assembly room and in each hallway or corridor, exitway and/or lobby. The installation of the required detectors in hallways or corridors, exitways and lobbies shall be on thirty-foot centers on smooth ceilings. For areas of beamed or joisted ceilings, the chief of the fire prevention bureau shall approve smoke detector placement based on practical knowledge and in conjunction with NFPA 72, 72E, and 74.
(4)
Battery powered smoke detectors shall not be installed in any new construction or existing construction involving renovation where such cost of renovation exceeds forty (40) per cent of the value of the structure for which renovation is being performed. Additionally, where fire, water or other damage has resulted in damage to an existing structure where replacement of wiring and/or ceilings is required as a portion of repairs, battery power smoke detectors shall be prohibited.
(b)
For every multi-family dwelling containing four (4) dwelling units no charcoal or gas burners shall be kindled or used on combustible balconies or within ten (10) feet of combustible patios on ground floors.
(Code 1963, § 5-6; Ord. No. 96-07, § 1, 3-4-96)
Sec. 7-24. - Open burning.
(a)
No person, without having obtained a permit therefor, shall cause, suffer, allow or permit open burning in any area of the city except as follows:
(1)
Open burning in a reasonable fashion for the purpose of cooking food for immediate human consumption.
(2)
Fires set for the purpose of training fire fighting personnel of the College Park Fire Department, or for the purpose of teaching fire safety techniques to industrial fire brigades or civilians who reside or work within the city, provided that such training is being conducted by city personnel.
(3)
Operation of devices using open flames such as tar kettles, blow torches, welding torches, portable heaters, and other flame-making equipment where approved safety measures are used.
(4)
Warming fires in a barrel of fifty-five gallon capacity or less, provided that the outside temperature is fifty (50) degrees Fahrenheit or less, that the fire does not procedure dense smoke or obnoxious odors, and that the fire is attended by an individual over the age of seventeen (17) years of age. Untreated wood or lumber shall be the only material or substance allowed in the warming fire. No permit or fee is required for warming fires.
(5)
Reduction of leaves on the premises on which they fall by the person in contact with the premises; however, only during the times when the leaves are dry and there is no wind. The chief of the fire department shall have the authority to order that any fire be put out if he determines there is a danger to public safety, a nuisance or significant environmental harm.
(b)
If there are adequate disposal facilities reasonably available for the particular combustible materials involved, the following open burning may be permitted if conducted in strict compliance with the provisions of this section:
(1)
Disposal of tree limbs from storm damage;
(2)
For weed abatement, disease, and pest prevention; and
(3)
Open burning in other than predominantly residential areas for the purpose of land clearing or construction or right-of-way maintenance.
(c)
Standards; inspection.
(1)
Materials such as heavy oils, gasoline, asphaltic materials, plastic, items containing natural or synthetic rubber, or any other material producing dense smoke and/or obnoxious odors shall not be used for starting or maintaining an open fire.
(2)
All burning shall be located on private property so as not to interfere with any traffic on public streets or sidewalks.
(3)
No burning shall occur prior to the city's inspection of the site and air curtain destructor pit and the issuance of a burning permit.
(4)
All pit fires shall be operated in strict compliance with the Air Curtain Destructor Operating Guide and Procedure, as published by the North Georgia Region and Environmental Protection Division.
(5)
The location of the air curtain destructor shall be at least one thousand (1,000) feet from any occupied or habitable structure. This distance may be increased by the College Park Fire Department on a site-specific basis at the discretion of the fire chief.
(6)
During pit fire operations, fire shall be attended at all times by a person qualified to operate the heavy equipment used in the pit fire operation. Heavy equipment capable of securing and covering the pit is required to be at the pit site at all times during burning operations.
(7)
A fence or barrier surrounding the combustion pit as depicted in the Air Curtain Destructor Operating Guide and Procedures shall be required at all times.
(8)
Pit burning is not permitted at night. After dark, the pit may continue to be uncovered to allow the burning coals and ember to die down during the night, provided the pit is attended.
(9)
Pit fires may be fueled only between one (1) hour after sunrise and two hours prior to sundown (but in no event later than four o'clock p.m.). Fueling of pit fires is prohibited at all other times.
(10)
Except for a reasonable period to get a fire started, no smoke of a shade darker than a #2 of the Ringlemann, chart, or equivalent opacity, shall be emitted by a source of open burning.
(11)
Only wood wastes consisting of trees, logs, large brush, stumps relatively free of soil, and untreated lumber may be burned. Sawdust, other densely packed wood wastes, paper (any type), chemically treated, coated or impregnated wood, or nonwood construction materials shall not be burned. Any material or substance not specifically allowed herein shall not be burned.
(d)
No burning shall be allowed in violation of restrictions imposed by the Environmental Protection Agency or the State Environmental Protection Division.
(e)
Permit application; contents; terms:
(1)
For all burning permitted under this section, the property owner shall obtain all necessary permits from the Environmental Protection Division of the Department of Natural Resources of the State of Georgia.
(2)
In addition to state approval, the property owner shall submit a burning permit application to the College Park Fire Department. Such application shall include:
a.
A plat or survey indicating that the air pit destructor in which the burning is to occur is located more than one thousand (1,000) feet away from any occupied or habitable structure.
b.
The names and qualifications of the individuals who will be attending to the fire during pit fire operations.
c.
A permit application fee in the amount of one hundred dollars ($100.00).
d.
An applicant shall submit a performance bond in the amount of five hundred dollars ($500.00). Said bond may be in the form of cash, an irrevocable letter of credit, or in a form suitable to the city and executed by a surety or sureties, reasonably acceptable to the city. If the applicant does not comply with the provisions of this section or with the conditions of the burning permit after issuance, the entire performance bond fee shall be forfeited to the city and the city may use said sum to stabilize the site and bring it into compliance. If the applicant does comply with the provisions of this section and with conditions of the burning permit, the performance bond shall be released by the city following extinguishment of the fire under the permit.
(3)
Duration of permit. A permit issued by the College Park Fire Department shall be valid for thirty (30) days after issuance and shall be issued on a site-specific basis.
(4)
Revocation. A burning permit may be suspended, revoked, or modified by the fire chief upon finding that the holder or his successor in title is not in compliance with the approved plan or that the holder or his successor is in violation of this section.
(5)
Stop-work orders. The fire chief may issue a stop-work order when atmospheric conditions or local circumstances make open burning hazardous. In addition, the fire chief may issue a stop-work order on any burning permit which has already been issued in the event that the holder of such permit is found to be in violation of the requirements of this section.
(f)
Denial of a burning permit, modification or grant with conditions of such a permit, and/or issuance of a stop-work order by the fire chief shall entitle the person applying for a burning permit or the holder of such a permit to a hearing before the city manager within thirty (30) days after receipt by the manager of written notice of appeal. Such written notice of appeal shall only be effective if submitted in writing within ten (10) days of the date of the action or notice from which appeal is requested.
(g)
Neither the issuance of a burning permit, nor compliance with the provisions of this section, shall relieve any person of the responsibility for damages to any person or property otherwise imposed by law nor impose any liability upon the city for damage to any person or property.
(h)
Any person who violates the provisions of this section, or who violates any order or determination of the fire chief promulgated pursuant to this section shall be cited for such violation and, upon conviction, shall be punished by fine or imprisonment, or both, as established by section 1-8 of the Code of Ordinances.
(Ord. No. 97-21, § 1, 8-18-97)

Sec. 7-26. - Consumer fireworks—Special use permit.
(a)
Consumer fireworks is defined in section 8-20, definitions in chapter 8 (health and sanitation), article II (noise).
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person, firm, corporation, association or partnership to discharge, use, ignite, or cause to be ignited any consumer fireworks anywhere in the city without first being issued a special use permit as provided in subsection (c) below.
(c)
Consumer fireworks—Special use permit.
(1)
An application for a consumer fireworks special use permit for the discharge, use, or ignition of consumer fireworks shall be made to the city code enforcement office located in the city police department and shall include the following information:
a.
The name of the person, firm, corporation, association, or partnership seeking the special use of consumer fireworks.
b.
The name of any official event or events associated with the special use of consumer fireworks.
c.
The time or times of the requested use of consumer fireworks.
d.
The street address location or locations of the requested use of consumer fireworks.
(2)
The application for a consumer fireworks special use permit shall require an administrative fee of ten dollars ($10.00).
(3)
Applications for a consumer fireworks special use permit must be submitted no later than three (3) business days before the planned discharge, use, or ignition of consumer fireworks within the city.
(4)
The duration of the consumer fireworks special use permit shall not be longer than seven (7) calendar days from the date of issuance.
(5)
City code enforcement will receive the application and make a determination as to whether or not the special use of fireworks is appropriate on a case-by-case basis.
(d)
Consumer fireworks shall not be discharged, used, or ignited, and no special use permit shall allow for the discharge, use, or ignition of consumer fireworks, within the city during times and hours in conflict with the city's general noise ordinance contained in chapter 8, article II of the city Code of Ordinances.
(e)
No consumer fireworks special use permit is required for the ignition or use of consumer fireworks on the following days: on January 1, July 3, July 4, and December 31 of each year after the time of 9:00 p.m. and up to and including the time of 11:59 p.m.; and on January 1 of each year beginning at the time of Midnight and up to and including the ending time of 1:00 a.m.
(Ord. No. 2020-11, § 1, 7-10-20)
