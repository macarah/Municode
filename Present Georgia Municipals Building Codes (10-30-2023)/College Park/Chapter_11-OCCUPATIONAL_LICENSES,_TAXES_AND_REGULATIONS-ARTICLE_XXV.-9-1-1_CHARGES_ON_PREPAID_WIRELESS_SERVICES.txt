ARTICLE XXV. - 9-1-1 CHARGES ON PREPAID WIRELESS SERVICES[11]

Footnotes:
--- (11) ---
Editor's note— Ord. No. 2011-17, § 1, adopted December 22, 2011, enacted provisions intended for use as article XXIV, §§ 11-400—11-403. Inasmuch as there are already provisions so designated as article XXIV, and at the discretion of the editor, said provisions have been redesignated as article XXV, §§ 11-426—11-429.

Sec. 11-426. - 9-1-1 charge on prepaid wireless transactions.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2, there is hereby imposed a prepaid wireless Emergency 9-1-1 charge as defined by O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(a)(4) upon every prepaid wireless retail transaction occurring within the jurisdiction of the public service answering point in the amount of $1.50.
(Ord. No. 2011-17, § 1, 12-22-11; Ord. No. 2018-12, § 1, 12-3-18)
Sec. 11-427. - Collection of 9-1-1 charge on prepaid wireless transactions.
Prepaid wireless Emergency 9-1-1 charges collected by retailers shall be remitted to the Georgia Department of Revenue, as the contracted collection partner of the Georgia Emergency Communications Authority, at the times and in the manner provided by O.C.G.A. 46-5-134.2 and any other Georgia Department of Revenue or Georgia Emergency Communications Authority regulation or policy.
(Ord. No. 2011-17, § 1, 12-22-11; Ord. No. 2018-12, § 1, 12-3-18)
Sec. 11-428. - Administrative provisions.
The clerk of the city is hereby directed to file with the state revenue commissioner a certified copy of this ordinance and amendments thereto, in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(j)(1), within ten (10) days of enactment of this ordinance or future amendment.
(Ord. No. 2011-17, § 1, 12-22-11; Ord. No. 2018-12, § 1, 12-3-18)
Sec. 11-429. - Depositing of funds; use of funds.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134.2(j)(4), funds received by this city from charges imposed by this ordinance shall be deposited in the Emergency Telephone System Fund maintained by the city pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134 and kept separate from general revenue of the jurisdiction; all such funds shall be used exclusively for the purposes authorized by O.C.G.A. § 46-5-134(f).
(Ord. No. 2011-17, § 1, 12-22-11; Ord. No. 2018-12, § 1, 12-3-18)
