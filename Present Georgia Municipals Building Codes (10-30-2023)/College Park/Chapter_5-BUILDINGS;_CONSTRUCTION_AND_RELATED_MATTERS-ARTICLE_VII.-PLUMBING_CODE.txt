DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 5-120. - Plumbing inspector—Building official to act as plumbing inspector.
The Building Official shall be in direct charge of all plumbing work done in the city.
(Code 1963, § 18-6)
Sec. 5-121. - Same—Authority, duties; noncompliance with order.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the plumbing inspector to make inspections of plumbing already in, upon complaint of its unsanitary conditions; and upon notice to do so, the owner, agent or lessee of such property shall make such changes as are ordered by the plumbing board, and failure to make any such changes within five (5) days after notice shall subject the owner or agent or lessee to punishment as provided in section 1-8 upon conviction of failure to comply with the notice.
(b)
It shall also be the duty of the plumbing inspector to inspect all plumbing done in the city or at any location using city water during the progress of such work, as well as when it is completed, such inspection to be made from time to time, and to be thorough and complete.
(Code 1963, § 18-7)
Sec. 5-122. - Permit—Required.
It shall be unlawful to construct, erect, install, alter or repair, or to commence the construction, erection, installation or alteration, or any equipment, appliances or system provided for in the code without first filing with the Building Official an application in writing and obtaining a formal permit in conformity with this article.
(Code 1963, § 18-33)
Sec. 5-123. - Same—Application form; addenda.
(a)
An application for permit shall be submitted in such form as the Building Official may prescribe.
(b)
Such application shall be made by the owner or the owner's authorized representative, and it shall be the responsibility of the owner to see that a permit is obtained before any installation or work is started.
(c)
Such application shall contain the full names and addresses of the applicant and of the owner.
(d)
Such application shall describe briefly the proposed work and shall give such additional information as may be required by the Building Official for an intelligent understanding of the work.
(e)
When required by the Building Official, two (2) or more copies of specifications and of drawings drawn to scale with sufficient clarity and detail to indicate the nature and character of the work shall accompany every application. Such drawings and specifications shall contain the name and address of the designer and information in the form of notes or otherwise as to the quality of materials, where quality is essential to conformity with the code. Such information shall be specific, and the code shall not be cited as a whole or in part, nor shall the term "legal" or its equivalent be used as a substitute for specific information.
(f)
The Building Official may require details, computation, stress diagrams and other data necessary to describe the construction and basis of calculations, and they shall bear the signature of the person responsible for the design.
(g)
All drawings, specifications and accompanying data shall bear the name and address of the designer. In the case of buildings or structures of group C, D and E occupancy, and all buildings or structures exceeding two (2) stories in height or five thousand (5,000) square feet in area and costing fifty thousand dollars ($50,000.00) or more, except one- and two-family dwellings and farm buildings, such designer shall be an architect or engineer legally registered under the laws of this state regulating the practice of architecture or engineering and shall affix such architect's or engineer's official seal to said drawings, specifications and accompanying data.
(Code 1963, §§ 18-34, 18-35)
Sec. 5-124. - Same—Action on application.
(a)
Applications for permits shall be examined within a reasonable time after filing. If after examination no objection is found to the same, and it appears that the proposed work will be in compliance with the laws and ordinances applicable thereto, the application shall be approved and a permit issued for the proposed work. If examination reveals otherwise, such application shall be rejected, and the applicant notified by a written report or otherwise.
(b)
The issuance or granting of a permit or approval of plans and specifications shall not be construed to be a permit for, or an approval of, any violation of any of the provisions of the code. No permit presuming to give authority to violate or cancel the provisions of the code shall be valid, except insofar as the work or use which it authorizes is lawful.
(c)
The issuance of a permit based on applications, plans and/or specifications shall not prevent the Building Official from thereafter requiring the correction of errors in said applications, plans and/or specifications or from preventing the work from being carried on thereunder when in violation of the code or of other ordinances of the city.
(Code 1963, § 18-36)
Sec. 5-125. - Same—Registration.
Individuals, contractors, firms or corporations licensed to perform work governed by the code shall register their license number with the Building Official before obtaining permits.
(Code 1963, § 18-37(b))
Sec. 5-126. - Same—Expiration; revocation.
(a)
A permit under which no work is commenced within six (6) months after issuance, or where the work commenced is suspended or abandoned for six (6) months, shall expire by limitation, and fees paid shall be forfeited. The application, plans and specifications shall be reviewed, and a new permit required before work is started or resumed.
(b)
The Building Official may revoke a permit or approval issued under the provisions of the code in case there has been any false statement or misrepresentation as to a material fact in the application or plans on which the permit or approval was based.
(Code 1963, §§ 18-38, 18-39)
Cross reference— Conditions of permit, § 5-153.
Sec. 5-127. - Inspections—Performing; self-inspection by master plumbers or utility contractors not authorized.
(a)
Inspections required under the provisions of the code shall be made by the Building Official or such official's duly authorized representatives. Upon presentation of proper credentials, the Building Official or representative may enter, at reasonable times, any building, structure or premises in the city to perform any duty imposed upon such official by the code. The Building Official and representatives are hereby authorized and directed to enforce all provisions of the code and are hereby invested with all police power necessary for the purpose of making arrests, issuing copies of charges and prosecution of any person or persons as may be charged with any violation of this or other ordinances of the city.
(b)
All installations of any equipment, appliance or system for which a permit has been issued as prescribed in the code shall be subject to inspection by the Building Official or representatives.
(c)
The provisions of O.C.G.A. Section 8-2-26(d), relating to self-inspection of water and sewer projects by master plumbers or utility contractors, shall not be applicable within the city.
(Code 1963, § 18-32; Ord. No. 96-24, § 1, 8-19-96)
Sec. 5-128. - Same—Notices; fees.
(a)
Inspection notices in writing upon blanks furnished by the city clerk shall be filed before 4:00 p.m. giving notice to the department of the location and of work to be inspected. The notice shall be given to the inspector, and work involved shall be inspected as soon as possible thereafter. No work, however, may be covered until proper inspection has been made.
(b)
There shall be a fee as established by resolution of mayor and council. The above fee will entitle the contracting plumber two (2) inspections. If additional trips are required to inspect work that is noted to be inferior, a reinspection fee as established by resolution of mayor and council will be charged for each subsequent inspection, such payment to be made to the city clerk before additional inspections are made.
(Code 1963, § 18-15; Ord. No. 93-1, § 4, 1-4-93; Ord. No. 2010-25, § 7, 8-16-10)
Sec. 5-129. - Unsafe systems, appliances or equipment; remedial action.
(a)
Systems, appliances or equipment covered by the code that may be or shall be found upon inspection to have become dangerous or unsafe shall, unless made safe and so certified by the Building Official, be removed or the use of same discontinued.
(b)
The term "dangerous or unsafe systems, appliances, or equipment" shall include systems, appliances or equipment which:
(1)
Are structurally unsafe, unstable or unsanitary;
(2)
Constitute a fire hazard;
(3)
Are unsuitable or improper for the use to which they are put;
(4)
Constitute a hazard to health or safety because of inadequate maintenance, dilapidation or obsolescence; or
(5)
Are otherwise dangerous to life or property.
(Code 1963, § 18-41; Ord. No. 88-11, § 13, 5-2-88)
Sec. 5-130. - Same—Notice.
(a)
Upon receipt of information that systems, appliances or pieces of equipment covered by the code are unsafe, the Building Official shall make or cause to be made an inspection; and if it is found that an unsafe condition exists, such official shall serve or cause to be served on the owner or owner's executors, administrators, agents, lessees or other persons who may have a vested or contingent interest in the same, a written notice containing a description of the particulars in which the systems, appliances or equipment are unsafe, and an order requiring the same to be made safe, as may be deemed necessary by the Building Official.
(b)
If the person to whom such notice and order is addressed cannot be found, after diligent search, then such notice and order shall be sent by certified mail to the last known address of such person, and a copy of such notice shall be posted in a conspicuous place on the premises to which it relates. Such mailing and posting shall be deemed adequate notice.
(Code 1963, § 18-42)
Sec. 5-131. - Same—Disregard of notice.
If the person served with a notice or order to remove existing unsafe conditions from systems, appliances or equipment or to discontinue the use of same within a reasonable time as set by the Building Official fails to comply with the requirements thereof, the Building Official shall advise the city manager of all the facts in the case.
(Code 1963, § 18-43)
Sec. 5-132. - Violations; notice.
(a)
Whenever the Building Official is satisfied that systems, appliances or equipment, or any work in connection therewith, covered by the code, are being erected, installed, altered or repaired in violation of the provisions or requirements of the code, or in violation of plans or specifications submitted and approved thereunder, or of a permit issued thereunder, such official shall serve a written notice or order upon the person responsible therefor directing discontinuance of such illegal action and the remedying of the condition that is in violation of the provisions or requirements of the code.
(b)
In case such notice or order is not promptly complied with, the city manager may request the city attorney or other proper prosecuting authority to institute an appropriate action or proceeding at law or in equity to restrain, correct or remove such violation, or the execution of work thereon, or to prevent the use of any systems, appliances or equipment in violation of or not in compliance with the provisions of the code, or with respect to which the requirements thereof or of any order or direction made pursuant to provisions contained therein shall not have been complied with.
(Code 1963, § 18-44)
Sec. 5-133. - Stopping of work by Building Official.
Whenever the Building Official determines work is being performed in a defective manner or in any manner in violation of this code of ordinance, he shall order, in writing, all further work to be stopped and may require suspension of further work until such defective performance or violation has been corrected.
(Code 1963, § 18-45; Ord. No. 88-11, § 14, 5-2-88)
Sec. 5-134. - Penalties; persons held responsible for violation.
(a)
A person who shall violate a provision of the plumbing code or fail to comply therewith or with any of the requirements thereof, upon conviction thereof in the city court, shall be punished as provided in section 1-8 for each day's continuance thereof.
(b)
The owner of a building, structure or premises where anything in violation of the code shall be placed or shall exist, and any architect, engineer, builder, contractor, agent, or other person employed in connection therewith and who may have assisted in the commission of such violation, shall each be guilty of a separate offense and upon conviction thereof shall be punished has herein provided.
(Code 1963, § 18-46)
Sec. 5-135. - Abatement of violation.
The imposition of the penalties herein prescribed shall not preclude the city from instituting an appropriate action or proceeding to prevent an unlawful erection, construction, reconstruction, alteration, repair, conversion, maintenance or use, or to restrain, correct or abate a violation, or to prevent the use of any equipment, appliance or system covered by the code.
(Code 1963, § 18-47)
Sec. 5-136. - Appeals—Authorized; time limit.
(a)
Whenever the Building Official shall reject or refuse to approve the mode or manner of work proposed to be followed or materials to be used in the work, or when it is claimed that the provisions of the code do not apply or that an equally good or more desirable form of procedure can be employed in any specific case, or when it is claimed that the true intent and meaning of the code or any of the regulations thereunder have been misconstrued or wrongly interpreted, the applicant or applicant's duly authorized agent may appeal from the decision of the Building Official.
(b)
In case of a building or structure which, in the opinion of the Building Official is unsafe or dangerous, the Building Official may in the order limit the time for such appeal to a shorter period. Appeals hereunder shall be on forms provided by the Building Official.
(Code 1963, § 18-48)
Sec. 5-137. - Same—Procedure; variations and modifications.
(a)
Within seven (7) days of the decision of the Building Official, an aggrieved party may appeal the decision to the construction board of adjustment and appeals. Such board, when so appealed to and after a hearing, may vary the application of any provision of the code to any particular case when, in its opinion, the enforcement thereof would do manifest injustice and would be contrary to the spirit and purpose of the code or public interest, or when in its opinion the interpretation of the Building Official should be modified or reversed.
(b)
A decision of the board to vary the application of any provision of the code or to modify an order of the Building Official shall specify in what manner such variation or modification is made, the conditions upon which it is made and the reasons therefor.
(Code 1963, § 18-49)
Sec. 5-138. - Same—Decision.
(a)
Every decision of the board shall be final, subject, however, to such remedy as provided in section 5-139. It shall be in writing and shall indicate the vote upon the decision. Every decision shall be promptly filed in the office of the Building Official and shall be open to public inspection; a certified copy shall be sent by mail or otherwise to the appellant and a copy shall be kept publicly posted in the office of the Building Official for two (2) weeks after filing.
(b)
The board shall, in every case, reach a decision without unreasonable or unnecessary delay.
(c)
If a decision of the board reverses or modifies a refusal, order or disallowance of the Building Official or varies the application of any provision of the code, the Building Official shall immediately take action in accordance with such decision.
(Code 1963, § 18-50)
Sec. 5-139. - Same—To construction board of adjustment and appeals.
(a)
Within two (2) weeks after the filing of the decision of the plumbing board of the city in the office of the Building Official, an aggrieved party may appeal to the construction board of adjustment and appeals.
(b)
Said appeals shall be heard, conducted and decided as provided in sections 5-137 and 5-138.
(c)
A copy of every decision so rendered by the board shall be forwarded to the state building administrative board, an agency of the state, for review.
(d)
Every decision of the board shall be final, subject, however, to such remedy as any aggrieved party have at law or in equity.
(Code 1963, § 18-51)
Sec. 5-140. - Weekly reports of building and water departments.
In order to secure the greatest cooperation between departments, the city clerk shall furnish the plumbing inspector a list of all permits for buildings which have been issued. In like manner, the water department shall furnish a list of taps, meters, connections, etc., so that the plumbing inspectors may be informed in all matters affecting the department. These reports shall be made each week and shall be complete with location, name and address of owner, and name and address of the person engaged to handle the construction.
(Code 1963, § 18-16)
Secs. 5-141—5-149. - Reserved.

Sec. 5-150. - Standard Plumbing Code—Adopted.
For the purpose of establishing rules and regulations for the installation, maintenance, and repair of all plumbing, plumbing fixtures and appliances, in order to secure the public health, sanitation, safety and welfare, that certain code, known as the Standard Plumbing Code, being particularly the 1991 edition, as amended, and as published by the Southern Building Code Congress International, Inc., and the whole thereof, save and except such portions as are hereinafter deleted, modified or amended, is hereby adopted and incorporated as fully as if set out at length herein. A copy of the Plumbing Code, as amended, shall be maintained on file in the office of the city clerk, and in the office of the Building Official, where it shall be available for public inspection. The provisions of the Plumbing Code shall be controlling in the installation, maintenance and repair of all plumbing, plumbing fixtures and appliances in the city, except where the provisions of such plumbing code are in conflict with this Code of Ordinances, in which case the provisions of this Code of Ordinances shall prevail.
(Code 1963, § 18-27; Ord. No. 88-11, § 10, 5-2-88; Ord. No. 91-22, § 1, 9-16-91)
Sec. 5-151. - Same—Amendments.
(a)
Building sewers and water pipes shall connect to the public sewer line and water main at a point designated by the proper municipal authorities.
(b)
Bituminous fiber pipe for new installations or repairs of existing installations is prohibited.
(c)
The use of stainless steel sleeve no-hub for new installations or repairs of existing installations is prohibited below grade. The use of stainless steel sleeve no-hub for new installations or repair of existing installations is permitted above grade, so long as such installation shall conform to the standards found in the plumbing code.
(d)
The use of ABS and PVC plastic pipe for installation under concrete slab on grade for new installations or repairs of existing installations is hereby allowed, subject to the following minimum standards and specifications:
(1)
Plastic pipe shall be a minimum of schedule 40.
(2)
Pipe shall be laid on a continuous compacted bed with proper fall.
(3)
The first one (1) foot of backfill material shall be clean and free of debris, the remaining backfill shall be free of debris and compacted.
(4)
Plastic pipe shall have a dirt or sand covering and not be in contact with concrete slab. Piping must be sleeved at concrete penetrations.
(e)
Section 1205.1.1 of said Standard Plumbing Code is amended to read as follows:
1205.1.1. Materials for underground water service pipe systems, shall be type M, copper tube, brass, cast-iron pressure pipe or galvanized steel. Water pipe in fittings with more than 8.0 per cent lead shall not be used.
"Materials for underground and lawn sprinkler systems, shall be type M, copper tube, brass, cast-iron pressure pipe, galvanized steel, or pressure rated plastic as listed in Table 500. Water pipe in fittings with more than 8.0 per cent lead shall not be used. The minimum working pressure of plastic piping installed outside of the foundation walls shall be one hundred sixty (160) psi at seventy-three (73) degrees Fahrenheit with permanent identification markings."
(f) Section 1210.1.1 of said Standard Plumbing Code is hereby amended as follows:
1210.1.1. Above ground. Materials for water distribution pipes and tubing shall be brass, copper water tube minimum type M, stainless steel water tube minimum grade H, cast-iron pressure pipe or galvanized steel, all to be installed with approved fittings; except that changes in direction in copper tube (ASTM B88) may be made with bends having a radius of not less than four (4) diameters of the tube, provided that such bends are made by the use of forming equipment which does not deform or create a loss in cross-sectional area of the tube.
(g) Section 1210.1.2 of said Standard Plumbing Code is hereby amended to read as follows:
1210.1.2. Under ground. Inaccessible water distribution piping under slabs shall be copper wire tube minimum type M, brass, cast-iron pressure pipe or galvanized steel pipe, all to be installed with approved fittings or bends. Any material subject to corrosion shall be protected when used in corrosive soils.
(Code 1963, § 18-28; Ord. No. 91-22, § 2, 9-16-91)

(a)
No equipment, appliance or system, as hereinafter specified in the code, shall be installed, constructed, erected, altered or repaired, except in conformity with the provisions of the code.
(b)
No equipment, appliance or system, as hereinafter specified in the code, shall be altered in any manner that would be in violation of the provisions of the code or of any authorized rules or approval of the Building Official made and issued thereunder.
(Code 1963, § 18-29)
Sec. 5-153. - Conditions of permit.
All work performed under a permit issued shall conform to the approved application and plans and all codes or ordinances governing the work or equipment for which permits were issued. All work shall be performed by an individual contractor, firm or corporation licensed to perform such work, except when performed by an individual installing, altering or repairing plumbing, plumbing fixtures, air conditioning and heating fixtures, or electrical services in a single-family dwelling owned and occupied by such person.
(Code 1963, § 18-37(a))
Sec. 5-154. - Use and maintenance of equipment.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to maintain, use, repair or alter any equipment, appliance or system that has been or will be installed, constructed or erected after the adoption of the code, that is in violation or will violate the provisions as hereinafter specified.
(b)
No equipment, appliance or system, as hereinafter specified in the code, shall be used unless it is in a safe condition.
(Code 1963, § 18-30)
Sec. 5-155. - Equipment, appliances or systems affected.
The provisions of the code shall apply to any equipment, appliance or system installed, constructed, erected, altered, repaired, maintained or used in the city.
(Code 1963, § 18-31)
Sec. 5-156. - Purpose.
It is the purpose of this ordinance [sections 5-156 through 5-159.4] to require the use of ultra low-flow plumbing fixtures in all new construction, or when replacing plumbing fixtures during renovation or remodeling of existing buildings for the purpose of conserving water, to maintain the integrity of drinking water supplies and to reduce wastewater flows.
(Ord. No. 91-2, § 1, 2-18-91)
Sec. 5-157. - Definitions.
For the purpose of this ordinance [sections 5-156 through 5-159.4], the following terms shall have the meanings respectively ascribed to them:
Commercial building means any type of building other than residential.
Construction means the erection of a new building or the alteration of an existing building in connection with its repair or renovation or in connection with making an addition to an existing building and shall include the replacement of a malfunctioning, unserviceable, or obsolete faucet, shower head, toilet, or urinal in an existing building.
Plumbing fixture means any toilet, urinal, shower head, bathroom, lavatory or kitchen faucet, and replacement aerators.
Residential building means any building or unit of a building intended for occupancy as a dwelling unit, but does not include a hotel or motel.
Toilet shall mean any fixture consisting of a water flushed bowl with a seat, used for the disposal of human waste.
Urinal means any fixture consisting of a water flushed bowl but not provided with a seat, used for the disposal of human waste.
(Ord. No. 91-2, § 1, 2-18-91)
Sec. 5-158. - Standards for plumbing fixtures.
(a)
No plumbing fixture shall be installed within the city which:
(1)
Employs a gravity tank type, flush-a-meter valve, or flush-a-meter tank toilet that uses more than an average of 1.6 gallons of water per flush; provided, however, this paragraph shall not be applicable to one-piece toilets until July 1, 1992;
(2)
Employs a shower head that allows a flow of more than 2.5 gallons of water per minute at sixty (60) pounds per square inch of pressure;
(3)
Employs a urinal that uses more than an average of 1.0 gallons of water per flush;
(4)
Employs a lavatory faucet or lavatory replacement aerator that allows a flow of more than 2.0 gallons of water per minute; or
(5)
Employs a kitchen faucet or kitchen replacement aerator that allows a flow of more than 2.5 gallons of water per minute.
(b)
The flow restriction device in a shower head must be a permanent and integral part of the shower head and must not be removable to allow flow rates in excess of that stated in subsection (a)(2) above.
(c)
Lavatory faucets located in restrooms intended for use by the general public shall be of the metering or self-closing type, in addition to the flow requirements listed in subsection (a)(4) above.
(Ord. No. 91-2, § 1, 2-18-91)
Sec. 5-159. - Applicability of restrictions.
(a)
The requirements of section 5-158 shall apply to any residential construction initiated after July 1, 1991 and to any commercial construction initiated after July 1, 1992, which involves the repair or renovation of or addition to any existing building when such repair or renovation of or addition to such existing building includes replacement toilets or showers or both.
(b)
This ordinance [sections 5-156 through 5-159.4] shall not apply to any construction of a residential building, the contract for which was entered into prior to July 1, 1991, nor shall it apply to any construction of a commercial building, the contract for which was entered into prior to July 1, 1992.
(Ord. No. 91-2, § 1, 2-18-91)
Sec. 5-159.1. - Exemptions.
New construction and the repair or renovation of existing buildings shall be exempt from the requirements of section 5-158 when:
(1)
The repair or renovation of the existing building does not include the replacement of the plumbing or sewerage system servicing toilets, faucets or showerheads within such existing building;
(2)
When such plumbing or sewerage system within such existing building, because of its capacity, design, or installation would not function properly if the toilets, faucets or showerheads required by this ordinance [sections 5-156 through 5-159.4] were installed. This exemption includes, but is not limited to, showers and faucets installed for safety purposes, such as an emergency eyewash station;
(3)
Such system is a well or gravity flow system from a spring and is owned privately by an individual for use in such individual's personal residence;
(4)
Units to be installed are:
a.
Specifically designed for use by the handicapped;
b.
Specifically designed to withstand unusual abuse or installation in a penal institution; or
c.
Toilets for juveniles.
(Ord. No. 91-2, § 1, 2-18-91)
Sec. 5-159.2. - Procedure for obtaining exemption.
(a)
No owner shall be entitled to an exemption from the requirements of section 5-158 unless he shall first have obtained an exemption in writing from the office of chief building inspector. Application for exemption shall be made on a form provided by the chief building inspector and shall include a listing of the specific exemption sought; the basis for such exemption; and, where such exemption is based on technological unfeasibility, shall provide the affidavit of a registered professional engineer or architect verifying the unfeasibility of the water conservation device for the particular existing structure.
(b)
A fee as fixed from time to time by the mayor and council of the city shall be charged for issuance of such an exemption.
(c)
The chief building inspector shall review the plans for the facility and may make inspections of existing premises where exemptions from the requirements of this ordinance [sections 5-156 through 5-159.4] are sought.
(d)
If, upon the basis of such investigation and any inspections, the chief building inspector determines that the requested exemption falls within those parameters provided by section 5-159.1 of this ordinance [sections 5-156 through 5-159.4], he shall issue such exemption in written form, which form shall bear his signature and date.
(Ord. No. 91-2, § 1, 2-18-91)
Sec. 5-159.3. - Enforcement responsibility.
(a)
This ordinance [sections 5-156 through 5-159.4] shall be enforced by the chief building inspector who shall have full authority to issue citations for violations of this ordinance [sections 5-156 through 5-159.4].
(b)
Any person violating this ordinance [sections 5-156 through 5-159.4] shall be tried before the judge of the city court, and upon conviction or entering of a plea of guilty or nolo contendere, each violation of this ordinance [sections 5-156 through 5-159.4] shall be subject to a penalty not to exceed five hundred dollars ($500.00) or imprisonment not to exceed ninety (90) days, or by both such fine and imprisonment. Each violation shall constitute a separate offense, and each day that such violation continues shall constitute a separate offense.
(Ord. No. 91-2, § 1, 2-18-91)
Sec. 5-159.4. - Remedies for violations.
(a)
In addition to criminal prosecution, any building or plumbing contractor, developer, owner or other person who violates the provisions of this ordinance [sections 5-156 through 5-159.4] shall be subject to suspension of his business license for a period of up to ninety (90) days.
(b)
In addition to the penalties provided in the preceding section, the city may take such other actions as authorized by law to compel compliance with the requirements of this ordinance [sections 5-156 through 5-159.4] and may specifically maintain an action or proceeding in any court of competent jurisdiction to comply with or restrain any violation of this ordinance [sections 5-156 through 5-159.4].
(Ord. No. 91-2, § 1, 2-18-91)
