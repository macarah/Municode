DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-238. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Hotel means every building or other structure kept, used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public to be a place where food is actually served and consumed and sleeping accommodations are offered for adequate pay to travelers and guests, whether transient, permanent or residential, and whether conducted in the same building or in separate buildings or structures used in connection therewith that are on the same premises and are a part of the hotel operation:
(1)
Which maintains 50 or more rooms used for the sleeping accommodations of such guests;
(2)
Which maintains an adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining room equipment to serve food as required therein;
(3)
Which operates one or more public dining rooms (excluding banquet rooms) with a combined seating capacity of at least 30, where meals are regularly served to guests; provided that consistent with the definition of the term "lounge" in this section, in no event shall the seating capacity of the lounge exceed that of such public dining rooms;
(4)
Which employs sufficient personnel to serve food as required in this article; and
(5)
Which derives at least as much gross income from the sale of such meals prepared, served, and consumed in the hotel as it does from its sale of alcoholic beverages.
Lounge means a separate room connected with, a part of and adjacent to a restaurant (as defined in this section) or located in a hotel (as defined in this section), provided that in no event shall the seating capacity of the lounge exceed that of its connected restaurant. The bar area incorporated within a restaurant and where food is served and consumed shall not be considered a lounge.
Mobile food vehicle means a unit mounted on or pulled by a motorized vehicle where food, including prepackaged foods and nonalcoholic beverages, for individual portion service is prepared, stored, served, or dispensed.
Motels means buildings or structures meeting the qualifications set out in the definition for "hotels" and shall be classified in the same category as hotels. Hotels shall have the privilege of granting franchises for the operation of a lounge, restaurant or nightclub in their premises, and the holder of such franchise shall be included in the definition of the term "hotel."
Pour means sell for beverage purposes, sell for consumption on the premises, and sell by the drink.
Pouring outlet means any place where liquor, wine, and/or malt beverages (unless specifically modified) are poured or proposed to be poured and in the case of liquor and wine, the term "pouring outlet" shall mean only a restaurant, hotel, private club, lounge, public stadium, coliseum or auditorium as defined in this section, or any retail shop or establishment that is specifically approved by the city council.
Premises means the definite, closed-in or partitioned-in locality (whether room or building) wherein pouring takes place.
Private club means any nonprofit association organized under the laws of the state which:
(1)
Has been in existence at least one year prior to the filing of its application for a license to be issued pursuant to this chapter.
(2)
Has at least 50 regular dues-paying members.
(3)
Owns, hires, or leases a building or space within a building for the reasonable use of its members, which building or space:
a.
Has suitable kitchen and dining room space and equipment.
b.
Is staffed with a sufficient number of employees for cooking, preparing, and serving meals for its members and guests.
(4)
Has no member, officer, agent, or employee directly or indirectly receiving, in the form of salary or other compensation, any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages beyond a fixed salary.
Public stadium, coliseum or auditorium means any facility owned or controlled by the city, the county, or a public authority having a seating capacity in excess of 2,500 people.
Restaurant means any public place kept, used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public as a place where meals are actually and regularly served, without sleeping accommodations, which:
(1)
Maintains an adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining room equipment to serve food as required in this article and/or maintains an affiliation with a mobile food vehicle that is parked on the premises to serve food as required in this article.
(2)
Provides a regular seating capacity for at least 30 persons; provided that consistent with the definition of the term "lounge" in this section, in no event shall the seating capacity of the lounge exceed that of its connected restaurant.
(3)
Employs sufficient personnel to serve food as required in this section.
(4)
Derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross food and liquor sales from the sale of such meals prepared, served and consumed on the premises, including the sale of meals prepared by an affiliated mobile food vehicle that are served and consumed on the premises. The city clerk shall review the gross income figures from each establishment and determine if the annual sales meet the required ratio between food and liquor and make appropriate recommendations to the city council.
Wine specialty shop means a retail seller of package wine that shall be allowed to sell wine and craft beer for consumption on the premises, in which:
(1)
No less than 75 percent of the gross revenue of the business shall be derived from the package sale of table wine, fortified wines, port, sherry and/or wine accessories. Consumption on the premises sales must be 25 percent or less of the business's gross revenue; free samples distributed under this section shall not be included in the calculation of consumption on the premises sales.
(2)
It shall be unlawful for a wine specialty shop to sell or have on the premises spirituous liquors or packaged malt beverages.
(3)
A wine specialty shop shall be allowed to sell growlers containing craft draft beer, as long as they follow all provisions set forth under state law.
(4)
Packaged sales of craft draft beers other than craft draft beer growlers is prohibited. Malt beverages fees shall apply for consumption on the premises of craft draft beers.
(5)
Only the licensee or an employee shall dispense wine/craft/draft beers from a tap or bottle. Free samples of wine or craft draft beers shall not exceed two ounces nor shall any one individual be offered more than three samples within any 24-hour period.
(6)
Sampling or tasting of wine or craft draft beers is only permitted within the designated portion of the premises.
(7)
A retail establishment operating as a wine specialty shop shall provide the business license clerk's office, at the time of alcohol license renewal, a statement prepared by the licensee's bookkeeper or accountant of the gross sales of the business for the preceding calendar year. The statement shall include the total gross sales for the establishment and document the percentage of sales in dollars attributed to consumption on the premises as compared to package sales and other general merchandise sales. Copies of the state sales tax returns for the same period shall be attached to the statement. The statement must be properly notarized and certified to be true and correct by the licensee or his agent under penalty of law, and shall accompany the renewal license fee payment on or before December 31 of each year. The city reserves the right to require, for any reasons deemed necessary by the city, a certified audit for sales of wine.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-276))

Sec. 4-239. - Pouring outlets generally.
The following regulations shall apply to pouring outlets:
(1)
The building or proposed building where pouring will take place shall meet all requirements of the building inspector, and plans, specifications, and/or photographs for the building or proposed building must be approved by city council.
(2)
No pouring of liquor and/or wine shall be permitted between 1:30 a.m. Sunday and 11:00 a.m. Sunday and between the hours of 1:30 a.m. and 10:00 a.m. on other days.
(3)
No pouring of malt beverages shall be permitted between 1:30 a.m. Sunday and 11:00 a.m. Sunday and between the hours of 1:30 a.m. and 6:00 a.m. on other days.
(4)
No liquor or wine may be consumed on the premises of the pouring outlet between the hours of 2:00 a.m. Sunday and 11:00 a.m. Sunday and between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 10:00 a.m. on other days.
(5)
No malt beverage may be consumed on the premises of the pouring outlet between the hours of 2:00 a.m. Sunday and 11:00 a.m. Sunday and between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 6:00 a.m. on other days.
(6)
Lounges and pouring outlets, except for restaurants and private clubs, shall not keep their doors open to the public, or allow members of the public to remain on the premises, beyond the prohibited hours of consumption.
(7)
In no event shall drinks be mixed or sold, nor malt beverages sold, during the prohibited hours, based upon the timely sale of tickets, chits, decanters or other devices.
(8)
No outlet manager, nor other employee of an outlet, shall permit violations of subsections (2) through (7) of this section.
(9)
Pouring licensees may sell malt beverages by the pitcher, or wine by the bottle or decanter, for consumption on the premises.
(10)
Hotels shall have the privilege of granting franchises for the operation of a lounge or restaurant in their premises, provided the hotel and the franchisee meet all the requirements of this article.
(11)
It shall be unlawful for any person to employ knowingly in any pouring outlet in any capacity whatsoever, including performers, entertainers and musicians, any person who has pleaded guilty or has been convicted of soliciting for prostitution, pandering, letting premises for prostitution, keeping a disorderly place, illegally dealing in drugs, sex offenses or for any charge relating to the manufacture or sale of intoxicating liquors.
(12)
No signs advertising sale by the drink affixed to the premises shall be permitted, unless specifically approved by city council; provided, however, there shall be no prohibition against utilizing signs showing the name of the pouring outlet.
(13)
It shall be the duty of the management of a pouring outlet to maintain a copy of this article at the outlet and to instruct each employee on its terms.
(14)
The state laws and regulations relating to the sale and distribution of distilled spirits, as revised, promulgated by the state department of revenue, and especially as related to retail sale for consumption, are hereby incorporated into and made a part of this article as if fully set out in this section.
(15)
A pouring licensee shall make immediate report to, and receive approval by, the city council of any change in the interests in or ownership of the pouring outlet and/or any change in the information as stated in the original application for license.
(16)
No person already holding a Class W license shall be granted a pouring license.
(17)
A beer pouring license and a beer and wine package license may not be issued at the same outlet location unless said outlet also sells food prepared on the premises of the outlet for consumption on the premises of the outlet.
(18)
Quarterly income reports. On or before the 20th day of the months of January, April, July, and October, the licensee of each pouring outlet which pours liquor (except private clubs) shall file with the city clerk a gross income report of the outlet for the preceding month, certified by the licensee, which contains a percentage breakdown of the amount of liquor sales of the outlet relative to the outlet's total gross income from the sale of food or non-food goods, if specifically approved by the city council, for the applicable quarter. Failure to file a timely report as provided herein, or the filing of a fraudulent report, shall constitute grounds for the suspension or revocation of the alcohol pouring license issued by the city to such licensee.
(19)
Sunday alcohol sales will be permitted in a licensed pouring establishment which:
a.
Obtains a Sunday sales license, as provided in section 4-267; and
b.
Either:
1.
Derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross sales from the sale of prepared meals or food in all of the combined retail outlets of the individual establishment where food is served;
2.
In any licensed establishment which derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross income from the rental of rooms for overnight lodging; or
3.
Is located on the premises of a distillery, brewery, farm winery, wine specialty shop, or wine tasting room, as those terms are defined in this chapter.
(20)
Upon application to and specific approval by city council, for any food-sales percentage requirement set forth in this chapter, the city council may in its discretion allow non-food sales to count towards any required ratio of alcohol sales to total sales.
(21)
Outdoor and sidewalk seating at pouring outlets. Any holder of or applicant for a pouring license may include a separate application for outdoor or sidewalk seating. Applications for outdoor or sidewalk seating shall be considered and approved on a case-by-case basis by city council. Applications for outdoor or sidewalk seating shall include sufficient plans, designs, photographs, or other materials to allow city council to evaluate the proposed outdoor or sidewalk seating. Unless the pouring licensee has a separate license for outdoor or sidewalk seating, such seating is prohibited.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-277))
Sec. 4-240. - Entertainment at pouring outlets.
(a)
Bands or orchestras and patron dancing shall be permitted at pouring outlets:
(1)
Where adequate space exists;
(2)
Where all fire and safety regulations are met;
(3)
Where the appropriate license fee has been fully paid; and
(4)
Where prior approval of the city council is obtained.
(b)
Prior approval may be given only after notice of dates, time, approximate number of guests expected, nature of musical source and such other information as may be required is given in writing to the city council.
(c)
Where music or dancing is provided on a regular basis, the city council may singly approve such regular basis, provided notice of any substantial departure from such regular basis is given to the city council. In all cases wherein music or dancing is provided on other than a regular basis or entertainment other than bands or patron dancing is provided, prior approval for each such occasion shall be obtained from the city council.
(d)
The city council may require a pouring outlet providing such music and dancing to provide such police attendance and protection as, in the opinion of the chief of police, is adequate to meet the need therefor.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-278))
Sec. 4-241. - Temporary permit; standards for issuance; accounting.
(a)
Notwithstanding any other provision of this Code, upon proper application, the city council may grant a permit for an isolated or casual pouring of liquor, malt beverages and wine, provided that there is no objection from the state and provided that the proceeds from the sales under such permit shall be donated to a corporation or organization qualified as an exempt organization under applicable provisions of the Internal Revenue Code. The city council is authorized to delegate its authority to grant or deny such permits to the chief of police or to the city clerk.
(b)
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Proper application means an application containing the following information:
(1)
As to the corporation or organization receiving donations:
a.
Name.
b.
Address.
c.
Nature and purpose.
(2)
As to the applicant (who must be an authorized member of the applying organization):
a.
Name.
b.
Address.
(3)
Proposed location.
(4)
Dates of sales.
(c)
No permit shall be granted under this section unless the following requirements are satisfied:
(1)
The applicant shall be qualified to hold a permanent alcoholic beverage pouring license of the same classification under this Code.
(2)
The proposed location shall not be a prohibited location in violation of this Code.
(3)
The dates of sale under the permit shall not violate laws and regulations of the city or the state.
(4)
A permit fee shall be paid by the applicant to the city in an amount as set forth in the fee schedule maintained by the city clerk or by state law.
(5)
The application shall comply with regulations set forth in section 4-239.
(6)
When applicable, the application shall comply with section 4-240.
(d)
All applicants shall, within five working days from the expiration of the permit, file with the city council an accounting of the sales under any temporary granted permit.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-279))
Sec. 4-242. - Off-premises licenses.
(a)
Unless otherwise prohibited by law, notwithstanding any other provision of this Code, the city council may grant a license to permit the off-premises pouring of malt beverages, wine, and liquor, under the following terms and conditions:
(1)
An off-premises license may only be issued to licensees who hold in good standing an alcohol pouring license issued by the city, and no such licensee shall be permitted to pour any alcoholic beverage off premises which cannot be lawfully poured on the licensee's premises.
(2)
An application for an off-premises pouring license shall contain the following information:
a.
Name of licensee.
b.
Address of licensee.
c.
Type of on-premises pouring license held by licensee.
d.
Type of off-premises license required by licensee.
(3)
No off-premises license shall be valid until the licensee pays an annual license fee in the amount established in the fee schedule maintained by the city clerk or by state law.
(4)
The amount of all sales of alcoholic beverages and food sold off premises need not be included in the licensee's gross income figures for the purpose of establishing the licensee's food drink ratio reports that the licensee files with the city pursuant to this Code. However, the amount of such sales shall be included in the licensee's gross income figures for the purpose of establishing the amount of the mixed drink taxes due from the licensee to the city.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for an off-premises licensee to pour any malt beverages, wine, or liquor at any off-premises location until the licensee shall first obtain an off-premises pouring license. Such license may be issued by the city clerk for any period up to three days. Any license for a period longer than three days must be issued by the city council. An off-premises permit may be issued in accordance with the following:
(1)
An application for an off-premises pouring license shall contain the following information:
a.
Name of licensee.
b.
Date and description of licensee's off-premises license.
c.
Street address of the proposed off-premises location.
(2)
No permit for a particular off-premises location shall be issued if the requested building is a prohibited location as that term is defined in section 4-160, or fails to meet all requirements of the building inspector for an on-premises pouring outlet.
(3)
It shall be unlawful for any off-premises licensee to sell liquor at an off-premises location unless food is served at the event.
(4)
No off-premises permit shall be valid until the licensee pays a license fee in the amount established in the fee schedule maintained by the city clerk or by state law. The license fee shall be based on a per-day usage charge.
(5)
Unless otherwise provided for in this section, all regulations of this Code controlling the pouring of alcoholic beverages on premises shall govern the pouring of alcoholic beverages off premises.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-280))
Sec. 4-243. - Open container provisions.
(a)
The purpose of this section is intended to set forth certain exceptions and provisions applicable only on Thursday, Friday, and Saturday between the hours of 11:00 a.m. to 10:00 and Sunday from 12:30 p.m. to 10:00 p.m., or during special events as designated by the Cave Spring City Council, and only to licensees whose establishments:
(1)
Are located within an approved entertainment district, as designated by majority vote of the city council.
(2)
Possess a license to sell alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises.
Except as specifically set forth in this section, all such licensees will remain subject to all other provisions of this chapter.
(b)
Outside consumption of alcoholic beverages permitted.
(1)
Container. No container in which an alcoholic beverage is dispensed and removed from the licensed premises shall exceed 16 fluid ounces in size. No person shall hold in his possession on the public streets and sidewalks, plazas, in parks and/or any other public place within an entertainment district any open alcoholic beverage container, which exceeds 16 fluid ounces in size. Any establishment licensed to sell alcoholic beverages by the drink for consumption on the premises which is located within an approved entertainment district is authorized to dispense alcoholic beverages in a clear plastic cup with the name of the establishment that is licensed to sell alcohol identified on the side of the cup for removal from the premises.
(2)
Drinking from can, bottle, or glass prohibited. Except as specifically set forth in in this article with respect to a clear plastic cup, it shall be unlawful for any person to drink or attempt to drink any alcoholic beverage from a can, bottle, glass, cup, or container, on the public streets, sidewalks, plazas, rights-of-way, and public or private parking lots within the city.
(3)
Purchase from licensed premises within an approved entertainment district allowed off premises. Alcoholic beverages purchased in accordance with this chapter may be consumed in the approved entertainment district except as prohibited in this section. Alcoholic beverages consumed pursuant to this provision must be purchased from a licensed establishment authorized to sell alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises within the approved entertainment district. Licensed establishments shall only be permitted to serve from their approved premises as approved on their alcohol license and shall not serve from temporary locations upon the premises unless specifically allowed by issuance of a special event permit in accordance with this chapter.
(4)
Consumption limited to certain areas in entertainment district. No alcoholic beverage purchased pursuant to this provision may be consumed outside the boundary of the approved entertainment district or within the approved entertainment district on any parcel used as a public park, church, day care center or school unless specifically allowed by issuance of a special event permit in accordance with this chapter.
(5)
Regulated hours. After midnight, consumption on premises licensees shall discontinue the sale of alcoholic beverages within clear plastic cups and shall not allow alcoholic beverages to be removed from their licensed premises.
(6)
Any licensed establishment that allows patrons to leave an establishment with an alcoholic beverage as regulated herein shall have an 11-inch by 8½ inch sign posted at the door for public view whereas a patron exiting the establishment can read the following: "All patrons leaving this establishment with an alcoholic beverage do hereby take full responsibility to only consume an alcoholic beverage served in a clear plastic cup not to exceed 16 ounces in size and obtained by an establishment licensed to sell alcoholic beverages in the entertainment district boundary as designated by council and kept on file at the clerk's office. Any individual that leaves the permitted area with an alcoholic beverage in an open container is in violation of city code and may be subject to a citation and/or fine."
(7)
No abrogation of other laws. The provisions of this section shall not be deemed to abrogate or otherwise impact any state law or local ordinance pertaining to public drunkenness, disorderly conduct, driving with an open container or under the influence of alcohol, or similar laws.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-281); Ord. of 10-13-2021 , §§ I, II)

DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 4-265. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to pour or offer to pour any distilled spirits, wine or malt beverages within the corporate limits of the city without having a pouring license, or to carry on such activity in violation of the terms of such license or this chapter.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-301))
State Law reference— Local license for sale of distilled spirits by the drink, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-110; local licensing of private clubs, O.C.G.A. § 3-7-40 et seq.
Sec. 4-266. - Separate license for each outlet; transfer; surrender.
(a)
A separate license shall be required for each pouring outlet, and a separate application shall be made for each such place.
(b)
Licenses for approved outlets under this article shall be transferable upon:
(1)
Approval of the transferee in accordance with the procedures established for new applicants; provided, that except for the advertisement requirements, the approval as to location shall not be required.
(2)
Payment of a transfer fee in an amount provided in the schedule of fees, as kept and maintained by the city clerk.
(c)
Immediately upon the sale or closing of a pouring outlet, it shall be the duty of the licensee to surrender his license to the city council.
(d)
Notwithstanding subsections (a) through (c) of this section, upon the resignation or termination of employment of a person who holds the license for an outlet who is not himself the owner of the outlet, the outlet's owner or board of directors, if applicable, shall immediately file an application for the transfer of the license to another licensee. During the pendency of such application, and until its approval, the outlet may continue to operate under the authority of its license, upon the designation by the outlet of an interim licensee who meets the same qualifications of a licensee provided in this article, and upon the approval of such proposed interim licensee by the chief of police and the chairperson of the city council.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-302))
Sec. 4-267. - License for pouring; fee.
(a)
Type of licenses. Licenses under this article shall be classified as follows:
(1)
Liquor pouring. Outlet for pouring liquor, which shall permit only the sale of liquor by the drink for consumption on the premises.
(2)
Wine pouring. Outlet for pouring wine, which shall permit only the sale of wine by the drink for consumption on the premises.
(3)
Malt beverage pouring. Outlet for pouring malt beverages, which shall permit only the sale of malt beverages by the drink for consumption on the premises.
(4)
Sunday pouring. Outlet possessing one or more of the preceding licenses which shall also permit Sunday sales of alcohol by the drink for consumption on the premises.
(b)
Fee established. The amount of the license fee for each class shall be as set forth in the license fee schedule of the city as kept and maintained by the city clerk.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-303))
Sec. 4-268. - Application, qualification; regulation.
(a)
Applications for all pouring licenses (malt beverage, liquor, and/or wine) shall be the same as, and in accordance with, the requirements applicable to the sale of liquor by the package at retail. All provisions of this chapter pertaining to the qualifications and licensing for a liquor retail package store license, except as provided herein, shall apply and be enforced in the sale of malt beverages, liquor, and/or wine for consumption on the premises; provided, however, that the 500-yard limitation restricting the location of liquor package outlets, as provided in section 4-160, shall not apply to pouring establishments.
(b)
Unless otherwise provided in this article, all regulations controlling the sale of liquor at retail by the package (section 4-124 shall be applicable as if fully set out as a separate section in this article), together with the additional provisions contained in sections 4-239 and 4-240 and the penal provisions of this chapter shall govern the sale of liquor, wine and/or malt beverages for consumption on the premises.
(c)
No application shall be considered from, and no license shall be granted, to an applicant whose business location for a liquor pouring outlet is anything other than a restaurant, hotel, private club, lounge, or any retail shop or establishment that is specifically approved by the city council.
(d)
It is the intention of this article that liquor by the drink be sold only at bona fide restaurants, distillery tasting rooms or cocktail rooms, farm winery wine tasting rooms, wine specialty shops, malt beverage tap rooms, hotels, private clubs, and any retail shop or establishment that is specifically approved by the city council under the restrictions set out in this article, and not at walk-in bars or sham establishments.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-304))
Sec. 4-269. - Action upon pouring license.
Action upon a pouring license shall be the same as is set forth in article II of this chapter.

Sec. 4-270. - Renewal, suspension, revocation, and monetary sanction.
(a)
Renewal. The provisions set forth in this section shall apply to the renewal, suspension, and revocation of licenses under this article.
(b)
Summary action. There shall be no summary seizure of licenses under this division, unless there exists in the city a state of emergency duly declared; after any such seizure, a hearing shall be held as soon as practicable.
(c)
Action by city council. The city council shall have full power and authority to suspend, refuse to renew, place on probation, or revoke a license under this article, and to impose a monetary sanction not to exceed $2,000.00 under the conditions set forth in this section. Before taking any such action except suspending a license for failure to pay an imposed monetary sanction when due, the city council shall conduct a hearing, at least five days' notice of which hearing shall have been given to the affected licensee. Sufficient grounds for the suspension, refusal to renew, probation, revocation of a license, or imposition of a monetary sanction include, but are not limited to, the following:
(1)
A licensee has failed to open a business within six months after approval of the license.
(2)
A licensee has ceased to operate the business for a period of three consecutive months.
(3)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has sold alcoholic beverages during a period of suspension.
(4)
A licensee gave false or misleading information in the original application or renewal process.
(5)
A licensee has failed to pay any fee, license fee, or other amount of money due to the city under this chapter or any other city licensing ordinance.
(6)
A licensee has failed to maintain any and all of the general qualifications applicable to the initial issuance of a license as set forth in this article.
(7)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has served or sold any alcoholic beverage to any person that the licensee or the licensee's employee or agent, knew or should have known to be in a state of intoxication.
(8)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has served or sold any alcoholic beverage to any person under the age of legal consumption in the state.
(9)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has violated any other law, ordinance, or regulation governing the operation of an establishment licensed to sell alcoholic beverages or which is reasonably related to the operation of such establishment.
(10)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has violated any other section of this chapter.
(11)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has been convicted or pled guilty or no contest to any violation of its license to sell or distribute alcoholic beverages issued by the state department of revenue, the city, the county and/or the county sheriff's office.
(12)
A licensee has failed to satisfy the criteria for obtaining a license to sell alcoholic beverages under this article.
(13)
A licensee, or such licensee's employee or agent, has violated any federal, state, or local laws or ordinances relating to the use and sale of drugs and alcoholic beverages.
(14)
When the continued operation of the licensed business is detrimental to the health, safety, or welfare of the public.
(d)
Suspension. The failure of a licensee to pay when due any monetary sanction assessed pursuant to the terms of this chapter shall result in the immediate suspension of all alcohol licenses issued by the city to such licensee. A suspension under this subsection shall continue until all monetary sanctions and other sums due the city by the licensee are paid in full.
(Ord. of 6-25-2019, § 1(4-306))
