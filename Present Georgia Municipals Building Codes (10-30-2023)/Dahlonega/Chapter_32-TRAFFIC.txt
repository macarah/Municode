ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 32-1. - Adoption of uniform rules of the road.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-372 and 40-6-374, the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 and O.C.G.A. title 40, ch. 6 (O.C.G.A. § 40-6-1 et seq.) are adopted by reference.
(b)
Penalties for violation of such provisions shall not exceed 60 days in jail and a $500.00 fine per offense.
( Ord. No. 83-1, 9-6-1983 )
Sec. 32-2. - Traffic signs, signals, devices and markings ratified.
The location and existence of all traffic control signs, signals, devices and markings in place on the adoption date of this Code are ratified and confirmed.
Sec. 32-3. - Speed zones; use of radar.
The following speed zones are established based upon a traffic engineering investigation as required by state law. It is unlawful to use radar detection devices on any state route, county road or city street not listed in this section. This section includes any legally posted speed limits before, during or after any construction/work zones on any city street or state route. Any person convicted of a violation of this section can be punished to the maximum as provided by state law. The Lumpkin County Sheriff's Office and City of Dahlonega law enforcement officials may enforce the zones established in this section as does the Georgia State Patrol. City of Dahlonega sworn law officers with police arrest powers will be the enforcing law enforcement agents. The city marshal shall also serve as chief of police and shall perform all duties as are authorized by law for municipal sworn enforcement officers.

Sec. 32-4. - One-way streets.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any vehicle, motorized or unmotorized, to be driven or otherwise operated on Warwick Street other than eastbound between Waters Street and Meaders Street.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any vehicle, motorized or unmotorized, to be driven or otherwise operated on Hawkins Street other than westbound between Meaders Street and Walters Street.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any vehicle, motorized or unmotorized, to be driven or otherwise operated along the specified section of Choice Avenue between South Chestatee Street and South Park Street in a westerly direction as noticed on posted signs.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any vehicle, motorized or unmotorized, to be driven or otherwise operated along the specified section of Enota Drive between Boyd Circle and Short Street in an easterly direction as noticed on posted signs.
(e)
Any person failing to abide by these regulations shall be guilty of a civil offense which shall be punishable by a fine of not less than $150.00 nor more than $250.00 in the municipal court.
( Ord. No. 2005-6, §§ 1—3, 9-12-2005 ; Ord. No. 2015-11, § 1, 9-1-2015 ; Ord. No. 2015-10, 9-15-2015 )
State Law reference— Authority to designate one-way streets, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-47.
Sec. 32-5. - Vehicles permitted in historic district (B3).
All vehicles operating within the city historic district (B3), which is defined as the street system appearing within the area labeled B3 on the official zoning map, shall be standard automobile, limousine, tour bus, light delivery truck, van or horse drawn (as permitted by ordinance) vehicles which are compatible with and not damaging to the infrastructure and ambiance of the historic district. Such vehicles shall be no more than 13 feet in height and 45 feet long. Concrete mixer trucks and trucks with dump bodies or trailers are prohibited. However, any vehicle which has valid pickups or deliveries within the B3 district shall be permitted to make said pickups or deliveries. Any violation of this section shall be punishable by a fine not less than $100.00 and not more than $500.00.
( Ord. No. 2009-4, 6-1-2009 )

Sec. 32-30. - Multiple violations in a single day.
Each violation of the provisions of this article and other ordinances of the city relating to the parking of vehicles committed by the same person during the same day shall constitute a separate punishable offense against this article or other ordinances of the city and may be treated as such, though there may be multiple violations by the same person in the same or different parking spaces during the same day.
( Ord. No. 77-1, Amend. 4, § 7, 7-5-1988 )
Sec. 32-31. - Removal, storage and redemption of vehicles.
Whenever any vehicle shall be parked on any street or highway within the city in violation of any provision of this article, and when the city manager or his designee shall determine that such a vehicle is an impediment to traffic, the city shall have the right to impound the vehicle and remove it to some safe place. Any vehicle so removed and impounded shall be released to the owner thereof, or his authorized agent, upon giving a satisfactory cash or surety bond in an amount not less than $25.00 as eventual condemnation money to cover the cost and expense of impoundment and storage. The bond shall be approved by the city manager or an individual authorized by him. The bond shall be subject to final judgment rendered by the judge in such matter and shall be considered as a condemnation money bond. If no bond is tendered as aforesaid, the vehicle shall be impounded and await disposition by final judgment of the court by the judge unless the judge for good reason shown to him may enter an order authorizing the owner, or his duly authorized agent, to retain possession of the vehicle under appropriate conditions fixed by the court. The city shall be responsible for notifying the owner of any vehicle so impounded as appears from the registration plates or other ascertainable evidence.
( Ord. No. 77-1, Amend. 4, § 8, 7-5-1988 )
Sec. 32-32. - Immobilization of vehicles by use of wheel locks.
The city manager, or his designee, is hereby authorized to place or direct the placing of wheel locks on any motor vehicle which is parked in violation of any provision of this article relating to parking which has not been disposed of by judgment of the municipal court or by the posting of bond for appearance in the court. Any designated official may release such motor vehicle from wheel locks upon the posting by any person of proper bond for appearance in the court with respect to each violation of this article by the motor vehicle which has not been disposed of as aforesaid. A wheel lock fee, in an amount established by the city, shall be assessed on any motor vehicle for the release of the wheel locks.
( Ord. No. 77-1, Amend. 4, § 9, 7-5-1988 )
Sec. 32-33. - Removal of certain illegally parked vehicles.
(a)
Any vehicle parked or left standing in violation of any provisions of this article or any ordinance of the city prohibiting or regulating parking of vehicles upon the streets or other places of the city, or for a period exceeding 24 hours, is hereby declared a public nuisance and a trespasser on the public right-of-way, and an obstruction upon the streets, and a hazard to traffic thereon, and any law enforcement officer upon discovering any such vehicle shall remove such vehicle or cause the same to be removed to any city-owned-or-operated property, where the same shall be kept and detained until the owner of such vehicle, his agent or representative obtains an order from the law enforcement officer authorizing such vehicle to be surrendered, or upon the order from the city manager after a hearing shall be had thereon as herein provided.
(b)
If such vehicle remains in the custody of the city exceeding a period of 30 days, under any one of the circumstances enumerated in subsection (a) of this section, and after a reasonable effort has been made on the part of the law enforcement officers to locate the owner of such vehicle, then and in that event, the same may be advertised for sale one time in the official county organ and ten days after such advertisement same may be sold at public sale on the property where the vehicle is located. Proceeds of said sale, after expenses, are to be deposited in the general funds of the city.
( Ord. No. 77-1, art. I, div. 1, § 1, 3-11-1977 )
Sec. 32-34. - Parking not to obstruct traffic.
No person shall park any vehicle upon a street, other than an alley, in such a manner or under such conditions as to leave available less than ten feet of the width of the roadway for free movement of vehicular traffic.
( Ord. No. 77-1, art. I, div. 1, § 2, 3-11-1977 )
Sec. 32-35. - Parking in alleys.
No person shall park a vehicle within an alley in such a manner or under such conditions as to leave available less than ten feet of the width of the roadway for the free movement of vehicular traffic, and no person shall stop, stand or park a vehicle within an alley in such position as to block the driveway entrance to any abutting property.
( Ord. No. 77-1, art. I, div. 1, § 3, 3-11-1977 )
Sec. 32-36. - Parking for certain purposes prohibited.
No person shall park a vehicle upon any roadway for the principal purpose of:
(1)
Displaying such vehicle for sale;
(2)
Washing, greasing or repairing such vehicle except repairs necessitated by an emergency.
( Ord. No. 77-1, art. I, div. 1, § 4, 3-11-1977 )
Sec. 32-37. - Parking adjacent to schools.
(a)
The city manager is hereby authorized to erect signs indicating no parking upon either or both sides of any street adjacent to any school property when such parking would, in his opinion, interfere with traffic or create a hazardous situation.
(b)
When official signs are erected indicating no parking upon either side of a street adjacent to any school property as authorized in subsection (a) of this section, no person shall park a vehicle in any such designated place.
( Ord. No. 77-1, art. I, div. 1, § 5, 3-11-1977 )
Sec. 32-38. - Parking prohibited on narrow streets.
(a)
The city traffic engineer is hereby authorized to erect signs indicating no parking upon any street when the width of the roadway does not exceed 20 feet or upon one side of a street as indicated by such signs when the width of the roadway does not exceed 30 feet.
(b)
When official signs prohibiting parking are erected upon narrow streets as authorized in subsection (a) of this section, no person shall park a vehicle upon any such street in violation of any such sign.
( Ord. No. 77-1, art. I, div. 1, § 6, 3-11-1977 )
Sec. 32-39. - Standing or parking on one-way streets.
The city manager is authorized to erect signs upon the left-hand side of any one-way street to prohibit the standing or parking of vehicles, and when such signs are in place, no person shall stand or park a vehicle upon such left-hand side in violation of any such sign.
( Ord. No. 77-1, art. I, div. 1, § 7, 3-11-1977 )
Sec. 32-40. - Standing or parking on one-way roadways.
In the event a highway includes two or more separate roadways and traffic is restricted to one direction upon any such roadway, no person shall stand or park a vehicle upon the left-hand side of such one-way roadway unless signs are erected to permit such standing or parking. The city manager is authorized to determine when standing or parking may be permitted upon the left-hand side of any such one-way roadway and to erect signs giving notice thereof.
( Ord. No. 77-1, art. I, div. 1, § 8, 3-11-1977 )
Sec. 32-41. - No stopping, standing or parking near hazardous or congested places.
(a)
The city manager is hereby authorized to determine and designate by proper signs placed not exceeding 100 feet in length in which the stopping, standing, or parking of vehicles would create an especially hazardous condition or would cause unusual delay to traffic.
(b)
When official signs are erected at hazardous or congested places as authorized in subsection (a) of this section, no person shall stop, stand, or park a vehicle in any such designated place.
( Ord. No. 77-1, art. I, div. 1, § 9, 3-11-1977 )
Sec. 32-42. - Definitions with regards to parking meters and meter spaces.
For the purposes of this chapter, the following words and phrases shall have the meanings respectively ascribed to them by this section:
Operator means every individual who shall operate a vehicle as the owner thereof, or as the agent, employee or permittee of the owner.
Parking meter means any mechanical device or meter not inconsistent with this chapter placed or erected for the regulation of parking by authority of this chapter, including pay-and-display parking meters.
Parking meter space means the space alongside the curb in which a vehicle shall be properly parked, which shall be indicated clearly by painted lines or otherwise, and adjacent to which a parking meter is installed within four feet of the front line of such space.
Public holidays means the holidays of Christmas, Thanksgiving, New Year's Day, the Fourth of July, Labor Day, Martin Luther King, Jr. Day and Memorial Day.
Vehicle means any device in or upon or by which any person or property is or may be transported upon a highway, road or street, except a device which is operated upon rails or tracks.
( Ord. No. 2020-06, 1-17-2020 )
Sec. 32-43. - Designation and use of spaces.
Lines or markings shall be painted or placed upon the curb or upon the street adjacent to each parking meter for the purpose of designating the parking space for which such meter is to be used, and each vehicle parking adjacent or next to any parking meter shall park within the lines or markings so established. It shall be unlawful for any person to park any vehicle across any such line or marking or to park any vehicle in such position that the vehicle shall not be entirely within the area so designated by the lines or markings. When a parking space in any parking meter zone is parallel with the adjacent curb or sidewalk, any vehicle parked in such parking space shall be parked with the foremost part of the vehicle nearest to the meter. Each of the meters shall be so arranged as to show or display a signal which shall indicate whether the time limits during which parking in that space is permitted has expired.
( Ord. No. 2020-06 , 1-17-2020)
Sec. 32-44. - Operation of meters; exceptions.
Except in a period of emergency determined by the Mayor or City Manager of Dahlonega, or except in compliance with directions of a law enforcement officer, when any vehicle shall be parked in a space regulated by a parking meter, between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 5:00 p.m. on any day except Sunday and public holidays, the owner, operator or driver of such vehicle shall, upon entering such parking space, immediately pay $1.00 per hour as directed by meter signage.
( Ord. No. 2020-06, 1-17-2020)
Sec. 32-45. - Parking time limits—Posted notices.
Parking or standing in any designated parking space shall be lawful only for the period of time designated by official signage.
( Ord. No. 2020-06, 1-17-2020)
Sec. 32-46. - Parking overtime; separate offenses; inoperative timing device.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to cause, allow, permit or suffer any vehicle registered in the name of or operated by such person to be parked overtime.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to cause, allow or permit any vehicle to stay parked in any designated metered parking space beyond the purchased designated unit of time specified by such meter.
(c)
The exceeding of each segment of time or fraction thereof beyond the initial lawful unit of time shall constitute a separate violation of this section.
(d)
The fact that the timing device on any parking meter is not showing available paid time shall be presumptive evidence, as to a parked vehicle then found in the parking space regulated by such parking meter, that the owner or driver failed to provide payment via coin or credit card, and the digital indication by such meter of a "violation" shall be presumptive evidence of unlawful parking.
( Ord. No. 2020-06, 1-17-2020)
Sec. 32-47. - City of Dahlonega employees.
Any clearly marked vehicle belonging to the City of Dahlonega, the driver of which is engaged in the performance of his duties as an employee of the City of Dahlonega, is hereby exempted from the provisions of this chapter and the driver of any such vehicle is authorized to park the vehicle on any parking space governed by a parking meter without the necessity of payment.
( Ord. No. 2020-06, 1-17-2020)
Sec. 32-48. - Defacing, etc., meters.
It shall be unlawful for any person to deface, injure, tamper with or open or willfully break, destroy or impair the usefulness of any parking meter installed under the provisions of this chapter.
( Ord. No. 2020-06, 1-17-2020)
Sec. 32-49. - Fees for parking meters.
The fee for parking in metered spaces shall be $1.00 per hour for each space and shall be indicated on the parking meter.
( Ord. No. 2020-06, 1-17-2020)
Sec. 32-50. - Use of funds.
All the revenue derived from the installation and use of parking meters shall be used exclusively for the purchase and maintenance of parking meters, parking enforcement, additional parking facilities and related administrative costs in the downtown area to be accounted for separately.
( Ord. No. 2020-06, 1-17-2020)
Sec. 32-51. - Collections, installation and maintenance.
Installation and maintenance of parking meters shall be the responsibility of the department of public works.
( Ord. No. 2020-06, 1-17-2020)
Sec. 32-52. - Reserved.
Sec. 32-53. - Items attached to parking meter posts.
It shall be unlawful and a violation of the provisions of this chapter for any person to attach animals or other objects, including bicycles, mopeds and motorcycles, to any parking meter post.
( Ord. No. 2020-06, 1-17-2020)
Sec. 32-54. - Reserved.
Sec. 32-55. - Parking meter violations.
The civil fines for parking meter violations are hereby adopted to be the same as those set forth in subsection 32-29(b)(1) of this Code for extended parking beyond the posted time limit.
( Ord. No. 2020-06, 1-17-2020)

The provisions of this division prohibiting the standing or parking of a vehicle shall apply at all times or at those times herein specified or as indicated on official signs except when it is necessary to stop a vehicle to avoid conflict with other traffic or in compliance with the directions of a law enforcement officer or official traffic control device.

The provisions of this division imposing a time limit on parking shall not relieve any person from the duty to observe other and more restrictive provisions prohibiting or limiting the stopping, standing, or parking of vehicles in specified places or at specified times.

This section would not apply to a designated area of the city where the county has designated this area as a produce sales area for local farmers to sell their produce.
(c)

Sec. 32-77. - Parking zones.

Sec. 32-78. - Parking prohibited in certain locations, on certain days and hours.

( Ord. No. 77-1, Amend. 4, § 5, 7-5-1988 )

No vehicle shall be parked for a longer period than three hours within the zones designated for maximum three-hour parking within the city, during the hours of 8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. on any given day. A vehicle, regardless of changing spaces, will violate this section if the cumulative total parking time for that vehicle during a given business day exceeds three hours within the city's parking areas limited to no more than three-hour parking.
( Ord. No. 77-1, Amend. 4, § 6, 7-5-1988 )

( Ord. No. 77-1, Amend. 1, § 2, 5-16-1980 )
Sec. 32-83. - Loading zone.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any vehicle, motorized or unmotorized, to be driven or otherwise operated along the specified section of Choice Avenue between South Chestatee Street and South Park Street in a westerly direction as noticed on posted signs.
(b)
The former westbound lane of Choice Avenue between South Chestatee Street and South Park Avenue shall be a 30-minute loading/unloading zone.
(c)
Signage shall be placed in appropriate places designated by the city engineer or city manager indicating loading zone only and any other signs which the city engineer or city manager may deem appropriate for the proper and safe flow of traffic.
(d)
Any person failing to abide by these regulations shall be guilty of a civil offense which shall be punishable by a fine of not less than $150.00 nor more than $250.00 in the municipal court.

Sec. 32-84. - Parking for advertising purposes.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate or to park on any street or other property owned by the city any vehicle for the primary purpose of displaying advertising, including, but not limited to, auction signs, general real estate "for sale" signs, advertisements for specific events, retail advertising signs, etc., except upon special permission of the city council.
( Ord. No. 77-1, Amend. 4, § 11, 7-5-1988 )
Sec. 32-85. - Parking within prohibited distance of stop signs.
It shall be unlawful for anyone to park a vehicle within 20 feet of a stop sign except within a parking space officially authorized and marked by the city.
( Ord. No. 77-1, Amend. 4, § 12, 7-5-1988 )
Sec. 32-86. - Designation of compact vehicle parking zone.
(a)
On North Chestatee Street in the existing diagonal parking spaces between Warwick Street and the Downtown Square, a white skipped or dashed line will be painted on the pavement near the opening of each vehicular parking space. The line shall be painted near the rear of the space and parallel to the direction of travel of the street. The white skipped or dashed line (the "compact vehicle line") shall define the qualification of any vehicle entering the space as compact or not. Any vehicle, its load, attachments or accoutrements, including, but not limited to, trailer hitches, accessory racks, ladders, lumber or anything else being transported by the vehicle after it has been parked, protruding into or touching the compact vehicle line shall be deemed in violation of this compact vehicle regulation. The front wheels and tires of the parked vehicle shall at all times be within the parking space and shall not be atop any items other than the parking surface, including, but not limited to, sidewalks, curbs, signs and/or grassed, dirt or unpaved areas adjacent to the parking space. Any parked vehicle found atop sidewalks, curbs, signs and/or grassed, dirt or unpaved areas adjacent to the parking space shall be in violation of this section.
(b)
Any vehicle found in violation will be subject to citation. Enforcement officers will cite and confirm the violation prior to issuing any citation. Signage identifying this as a violation shall be placed in prominent locations along North Chestatee Street.
( Ord. No. 77-1, Amend. 8, 2-6-2017 )
Sec. 32-87. - Electric vehicle charging.
Wherever electric vehicle charging stations are located throughout the city, the city shall erect a sign designating that those particular spaces shall be reserved only for those using the car charging station to charge an electric vehicle. A citation shall be issued by city code enforcement for any vehicle parked in such reserved spaces which is not utilizing the electric vehicle charging station to charge an electric vehicle. The specified fine will be $25.00.
( Ord. No. 2015-2, §§ 1, 2, 4-6-2015 )
Sec. 32-88. - Bus parking.
(a)
Five short-term bus parking spaces shall be located in the former westbound lane of Enota Drive between Boyd Circle and Short Street. These spaces are not to be used for loading and unloading. These spaces should not be used for regular travel lanes at any time.

ARTICLE III. - BICYCLES[3]

Sec. 32-120. - Riding bicycles on the sidewalks in the historical and central business districts.
(a)
It is unlawful for any person to ride a bicycle upon a public sidewalk in the historical and central business districts.
(b)
The terms "historical business district" and "central business district," as used in this section, mean the area designated by the zoning ordinance of the city, as now or hereafter amended.
(c)
Violation of any of the provisions of subsection (a) of this section is a traffic offense and shall be punished as follows:
(1)
First offense. It is a traffic infraction for any person to violate subsection (a) of this section, and such person shall be punished by a fine of $25.00. This fine shall not be suspended or deferred, but the court may authorize community service in lieu of all or part of this fine.
(2)
Second offense.
a.
Every person who violates subsection (a) of this section a second time within a five-year period shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, punishable by a fine not less than $50.00 nor more than $100.00. Fifty dollars of the fine shall not be suspended or deferred, but the court may authorize community service in lieu of all or part of this fine.
b.
Upon conviction of a second violation of subsection (a) of this section within a five-year period, the court may order forfeiture of the bicycle which was ridden in violation of subsection (a) of this section, unless it is proven to the court by a preponderance of the evidence that the defendant is not the owner of the bicycle and the owner did not or could not have reasonably known that the bicycle would be ridden in violation of subsection (a) of this section.
(3)
Third or subsequent offense.
a.
Every person who violates subsection (a) of this section a third or more times within a five-year period shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, punishable by a fine of not less than $100.00 nor more than $150.00. One hundred dollars of the fine shall not be suspended or deferred, but the court may authorize community service in lieu of all or part of the fine.
b.
Upon conviction of a third or subsequent violation of subsection (a) of this section within a five-year period, the court may order forfeiture of the bicycle which was ridden in violation of subsection (a) of this section, unless it is proven to the court by a preponderance of the evidence that the defendant is not the owner of the bicycle and the owner did not or could not have reasonably known that the bicycle would be ridden in violation of subsection (a) of this section.
( Ord. No. 96-8, §§ 2, 3, 1-6-1996 )
Sec. 32-121. - Negligent bicycling.
(a)
It is unlawful for any person to ride a bicycle in a negligent manner upon any publicly owned property or way open to the public in the city. For purposes of this section, the phrase "to ride in a negligent manner" means the riding or propelling of a bicycle in such a manner as to endanger or be likely to endanger any person or property; provided, however, that any person riding a bicycle on private property with the consent of the owner in a manner consistent with the owner's consent shall not be guilty of negligent bicycling.
(b)
Negligent bicycling is a traffic misdemeanor, punishable by a fine of not less than $50.00 nor more than $250.00. Twenty-five dollars of the fine shall not be suspended or deferred, but the court may authorize community service in lieu of all or part of the fine.
(c)
Upon conviction of a violation of this section, the court may order forfeiture of the bicycle which was ridden in violation of this section, unless it is proven to the court by a preponderance of the evidence that the defendant is not the owner of the bicycle and the owner did not or could not have reasonably known that the bicycle would be ridden in violation of this section.
( Ord. No. 96-8, § 4, 1-6-1996 )
Sec. 32-122. - Seizure of bicycles.
Whenever a law enforcement officer has probable cause to believe a bicycle was used or is being used in violation of this article, such bicycle may be seized as evidence and held until the disposition of the charge. An observation by a law enforcement officer that a bicycle is in motion upon the ground or pavement in the historical or central business districts, whether or not such bicycle is accompanied by a person, shall be sufficient to establish probable cause of a violation of Section 32-120(a).
( Ord. No. 96-8, § 5, 1-6-1996 )
Sec. 32-123. - Bicycles; riding on sidewalks outside historical and central business districts.
(a)
Whenever any person is riding a bicycle upon a sidewalk outside the historical and central business districts, such person shall yield the right-of-way to any pedestrian. The terms historical and central business districts, as used in this section, mean any area designated by the city zoning ordinance, as now or hereafter amended.
(b)
Violation of any provision of this section is a traffic infraction.
( Ord. No. 96-8, § 6, 1-6-1996 )
