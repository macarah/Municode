DIVISION 1. - SALES FROM PUBLIC OR PRIVATE PROPERTY LOCATIONS

Sec. 46-261. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
City. The City of Americus, a Georgia municipal corporation.
Goods. For the purposes of this division, "goods" is defined as merchandise, wares, fabric, material, furniture, clothing, shoes, and other articles of commerce; provided, however, that "goods" shall not include the sale of motorized vehicles or boats.
Governing body. The mayor and city council of the City of Americus.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-262. - Enforcement and penalty for violation.
(a)
The finance director shall be the enforcement officer and shall insure that the provisions are complied with prior to the issuing of any license.
(b)
Any person, firm, partnership, or corporation that violates the terms of this division shall upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-8 of this Code.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-263. - Location requirements.
Except as otherwise provided in this Code:
(1)
No merchant, trader, manufacturer, dealer, salesperson, or other person, either in person or by or through agents, servants, or employees, shall offer for sale any kind or character of goods except from established places of business.
(2)
No article of any kind or nature whatsoever shall be sold or offered for sale from vehicles, stands, or containers while standing or located in the streets, parks, or other property of the city.
(3)
No display shall be erected or installed, nor shall any sales of goods or merchandise outside of a permanent place of business, take place within 50 feet of the curb or paved edge of a public street or roadway.
(4)
Sales shall be permitted only on property where controlled vehicular ingress and egress or adequate off-street parking is provided.
(5)
No portable structure shall be erected in connection with the sale of goods within the city.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-264. - Sales from private property.
(a)
Notwithstanding any other provisions of this division to the contrary, goods may be sold from vehicles, stands, or containers, while located on private premises, but not until and unless the persons offering the same for sale have secured the written consent of the owner of such premises to sell the same thereon, and have paid for and obtained from the finance director a regular license to conduct such business within the city.
(b)
In order to obtain such license, the seller shall have filed an application with the finance director for such license, which application shall contain, at a minimum the following information:
(1)
Name of applicant;
(2)
A copy of picture identification;
(3)
Permanent address and telephone number of applicant;
(4)
Local address and telephone number of the applicant;
(5)
Applicant's date of birth and age, if an individual;
(6)
Applicant's Social Security number or federal employer identification number;
(7)
If applicant is a partnership or corporation, the name and home address of each partner, principal, or corporation officer;
(8)
If applicant is a corporation, the day, date, and state of incorporation;
(9)
If applicant is a partnership or corporation, the name, home address, date of birth, age, and Social Security number of the principal representative in the city;
(10)
Name and home address of all employees to work in the city;
(11)
Name of business represented by applicant, if different from applicant;
(12)
Permanent address of the business represented by the applicant;
(13)
Name of agent conducting the sale, if any;
(14)
Local address and telephone number of sales agent, if any;
(15)
Type of merchandise or service offered for sale;
(16)
A brief statement of the nature and character of advertising done or proposed to be done in order to attract customers;
(17)
Place where business is to be conducted and written approval from the property owner of such location;
(18)
Fingerprints of the person or persons having the management of supervision of the applicant's business;
(19)
Dates to be in business and total number of days to be in business;
(20)
Hours in which the business will be conducted;
(21)
Manner in which business is to be conducted;
(22)
State sales tax number or authorization;
(23)
State business license, if required;
(24)
State license tag number of each vehicle to be used; and
(25)
List of cities where business has been conducted by the applicant within the past 12 months.
(c)
The license application shall be signed under oath by the applicant.
(d)
In the event that the applicant provides all required information to the finance director and the applicant has not been previously convicted of a felony or a crime involving moral turpitude as evidenced by the fingerprint records submitted in accordance with section 46-69 hereof, and the applicant otherwise qualifies for the license, then such license shall be issued by the clerk upon payment of the appropriate fees therefor.
(e)
A separate application, license, and fee shall be required for each separate location from which such applicant proposes to do business.
(f)
In the event that a licensee violates any of the provisions of this division, then the license may be revoked by the finance director, and the justification of each revocation may be considered by the clerk in the event that such licensee applies for a license under this article at a future date.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-265. - Applicability to residents and nonresidents.
The prohibitions provided in this division shall apply to all persons, whether residents of the city, nonresidents of the city, residents of the state or nonresidents of the state.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-266. - Agricultural products exemption.
The provisions of this division shall not apply to the sale by the producers of agricultural products within the state; provided, however, such producers shall fully comply with all regulations heretofore and hereafter prescribed by the governing body and by any federal or state agency or department relative to the inspection and the sale of such products.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-267. - Exceptions.
(a)
This division shall not apply to sales made to or by dealers, traders, manufacturers, merchants, or businesses at their established places of business within the incorporated limits of the city.
(b)
This division shall not apply to any sidewalk sale, flea market, art show, or arts and crafts show or other merchandise on the public sidewalks, walkways, or other open areas at the invitation of or as a part of the downtown promotional activities of the downtown development authority or an organization licensed to do business as a charity under Georgia law.
(c)
This division shall not apply to yard sales conducted in accord with the provisions of section 46-3 of this Code.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-268. - Fees.
Licensees under this division shall pay a daily license fee of $100.00 per day for each day and for each location from which the licensee proposes to do business within the city.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-269. - Fingerprints to accompany application.
Any applicant for license to be issued pursuant to this division shall accompany the application with one complete set of fingerprints of the applicant and the applicant's manager or agent, if the operation of the business will be conducted by one other than the applicant; if the applicant is a partnership, of all partners of said partnership; if the applicant is a corporation, of all officers and directors of said corporation. The fingerprints shall be taken by the city's police department on forms to be provided by said department, and the applicant shall pay a fee of $15.00 for each individual fingerprinted.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
DIVISION 2. - SALES BY SOLICITORS
Subdivision I. - Generally

Sec. 46-270. - Definitions.
The following words, terms, or phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meaning ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
City means the City of Americus.
Goods means, for the purposes of this division, merchandise, wares, fabric, material, furniture, clothing, shoes, and other articles of commerce.
Governing body means the Mayor and City Council of Americus.
Solicitor means any person who solicits orders for the sale of goods or services on behalf of a business, individual, vocation, or occupation by means of door-to-door or house-to-house solicitation.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-271. - Exemptions.
The following shall be exempt from compliance with the requirements of division 2 of this article:
(1)
Representatives or agents of charitable or nonprofit organizations or corporations registered with the Georgia Secretary of State, or tax-exempt organizations which have been recognized as such by the Internal Revenue Service of the United States Treasury Department, shall be treated as exempt from the provisions of this division.
(2)
Any sales representative who calls upon prospective customers at their prior invitation shall be treated as exempt from the provisions of this division of this article.
(3)
Any sales representative who does not demand, accept, or receive payments in advance of final delivery and who has had, for the previous six months, a regularly established place of business or permanent residence in the city shall be treated as exempt from the provisions of this division. Any person who is exempt under this subsection must be soliciting orders only for goods capable of being delivered at one time and must have on such person proper identification which substantiates the claim to an exemption.
This division shall not apply to any individual or individuals engaged in door-to-door canvassing activities solely for the purpose of dispensing public information, engaging in religious activities, or engaging in political campaign activities.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-272. - Violation of other ordinances and laws.
It is unlawful for any person while engaging in any activity for which a permit is required by this division to violate any city ordinance, criminal law of this state, or any state or federal consumer protection law.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-273. - Hours of operation.
It is unlawful for any person to engage in any of the conduct for which a permit is required by this division between the hours of 7:00 p.m. and 9:00 a.m., according to the standard time in effect.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-274. - Restriction on number of persons soliciting.
It is unlawful for more than two individuals to engage in solicitation upon any premises at the same time for the same goods or services. Each individual member of a group engaged in solicitation in violation of this provision shall be deemed to have violated this section.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-275. - Persons with criminal records.
It is unlawful for any person with a criminal record as described in section 46-525, whether or not otherwise eligible for an exemption under section 46-502, to engage in any of the activities for which a permit is required by this division.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-276. - Frequency of solicitation of same premises.
It is unlawful for any person to make more than one solicitation call at the same premises for identical goods or services within any consecutive two-week period, without receiving a prior invitation therefor from the occupant of any such premises. This section includes solicitation upon the same premises by employees, agents, or representatives of any person, organization, or corporation more than once during such period without a prior invitation.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-277. - Solicitation to be at main entrances.
It is unlawful for any person to solicit or attempt to solicit at a place of residence at any entrance or part of the building other than the main entrance to the residence. A solicitor shall honor any sign or notice located at the premises, or any verbal direction from the owner or occupant of the premises, that states or advises persons that no solicitation is authorized upon the premises.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-278. - Identification and announced purpose of call.
At each dwelling, whether it is an apartment unit or private residence, the solicitor shall, at the time of initial contact with a prospective customer, identify himself or herself, identify his or her organization or corporation, and inform the occupant in unambiguous terms of the purpose of the solicitation.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-279. - Fraud, etc.
It is unlawful for any person engaged in solicitation to misrepresent the purpose of the solicitation or use any false or deceptive statements or any misrepresentation to induce a sale or contribution, or use any plan, scheme, or ruse which misrepresents the status or purpose of the person making the call.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Subdivision II. - Permit

Sec. 46-280. - Required.
Except as otherwise provided in this Code, any person engaged in or desiring to engage in any type of selling, soliciting, or any other business, occupation, or vocation, which by its nature requires going from door-to-door or house-to-house in the city, whether on a temporary or a permanent basis shall obtain a solicitor's permit from the city finance director.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-281. - Application.
(a)
Questionnaire. The finance director shall prepare a questionnaire requiring pertinent information regarding the physical description, identity, and background of each applicant for a permit, to include the following:
(1)
Name, local address, and telephone number.
(2)
Date and place of birth.
(3)
Driver's license number and issuing state.
(4)
Social Security number.
(5)
Race and sex.
(6)
Height and weight.
(7)
Eye color and hair color.
(8)
Name, address and telephone number of the organization represented.
(9)
Name and telephone number of immediate supervisor.
(10)
Product or service.
(11)
A list of all arrests, convictions, and the disposition of each charge, other than minor traffic violations.
(b)
The questionnaire form shall also bear the following statement:
"Official Code of Georgia Annotated § 16-10-71, provides that a person to whom a lawful oath or affirmation has been administered or who executes a document knowing that it purports to be an acknowledgment of a lawful oath or affirmation commits the offense of false swearing when, in any matter or thing other than a judicial proceeding, he knowingly and willfully makes a false statement."
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-282. - Investigation and issuance.
(a)
Upon filing of an application for a solicitor's permit, the police department shall review the application for the purpose of ascertaining whether the applicant has plead to, or has been convicted of, a felony, or a misdemeanor involving violence or moral turpitude. After ascertaining that the application has been properly completed, and that the applicant has not been disqualified by virtue of prior pleas or conviction, the finance director shall approve the application.
(b)
The finance director shall charge a fee of $100.00 for each permit issued.
(c)
In any case in which it appears to the finance director that a solicitor's permit should not be issued to an applicant, the finance director shall so inform the applicant of such decision in writing, and shall include in such writing a written statement of the reasons why the permit cannot be issued.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-283. - Expiration and renewal.
Each solicitor's permit shall indicate thereon an expiration date which is one year from the date of issue.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-284. - Denial; suspension or revocation.
(a)
No solicitor's permit shall be issued to any person who has been found guilty of any misdemeanor involving violence or moral turpitude any time within five years prior to the date of application, nor shall a permit be issued to any person convicted of a felony.
(b)
The permit of any solicitor charged with a felony or a misdemeanor involving violence or moral turpitude shall be deemed suspended from the time of lawful arrest, formal accusation, or indictment, whichever shall first occur. Such suspension shall remain in effect until the solicitor is convicted or acquitted.
(c)
The permit of any solicitor who is convicted of a felony, or of a misdemeanor involving moral turpitude or violence, shall be deemed revoked from the time of such conviction. The permit of any solicitor convicted of having violated any provision of this article after issuance of the permit shall be deemed revoked from the time of such conviction.
(d)
Any suspension or revocation occurring pursuant to the provisions of this division shall be effective by operation of law, whether or not any formal notification to the solicitor is given or received.
(e)
It is unlawful for any person to act as a solicitor while such person's permit has been suspended or after it has been revoked.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Sec. 46-285. - Appeals.
A person to whom the finance director refuses to issue a solicitor's permit or whose solicitor's permit is suspended or revoked may appeal to the mayor and city council. Such appeal shall be submitted to the city clerk in writing within 15 calendar days of the date of the notice of denial, suspension, or revocation of a solicitor's permit. The legal and factual basis for such appeal shall be stated in the written notice of appeal. Such appeal shall be heard and determined by the city council within 45 days of the date of filing of the notice of appeal.
(Ord. No. O-2012-19, 7-19-2012)
Secs. 46-286—46-329. - Reserved.
