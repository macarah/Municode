ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 86-1. - State law—Adoption by reference.
Pursuant to chapter 6 of title 40 of the Official Code of Georgia Annotated (Code §§ 40-6-372—40-6- 376, Code §§ 40-6-1—40-6-397) of that chapter known as the Uniform Rules of the Road, and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1, are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of the City of Americus with like effect as if recited herein.
(Code 1962, § 23-152(a); Code 1986, § 20-1; Ord. No. O-2013-4, § 1, 2-21-2013)
Sec. 86-2. - Same—Penalty.
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this article shall be punished as provided in section 1-8 of this Code.
(Code 1962, § 23-153; Code 1986, § 20-2; Ord. No. O-2013-4, § 2, 2-21-2013)
Sec. 86-3. - Riding on outside of vehicle.
It shall be unlawful for any person to ride on the fenders, bumpers or any other portion of any motor vehicle, except on the inside thereof.
(Code 1962, § 23-27; Code 1986, § 20-30)

ARTICLE II. - ADMINISTRATION AND ENFORCEMENT
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 86-25. - Authority of police and fire department officials.
(a)
It shall be the duty of the officers of the police department or such officers as are assigned by the chief of police to enforce this chapter and all other traffic laws of this city and all of the state vehicle laws applicable to street traffic in this city.
(b)
Officers of the police department, or such officers as are assigned by the chief of police, are hereby authorized to direct all traffic by voice, hand, or signal in conformity with traffic laws, provided, that in the event of a fire or other emergency or to expedite traffic or to safeguard pedestrians, officers of the police department may direct traffic as conditions may require notwithstanding the provisions of the traffic laws.
(c)
Officers of the fire department, when at the scene of a fire, may direct or assist the police in directing traffic threat or in the immediate vicinity.
(Code 1962, § 23-9; Code 1986, § 20-4)
Sec. 86-26. - Authority of chief of police to delegate power and duties.
The chief of police shall have the power to delegate to any other officer in the police department the duties and responsibilities conferred on such chief by this chapter.
(Code 1962, § 23-10; Code 1986, § 20-5)
Sec. 86-27. - Authority of utilities director.
(a)
Signs on one-way streets. Whenever this chapter or any ordinance of this city designates any one-way street or alley, the utilities director shall place and maintain signs giving notice thereof, and no such regulation shall be effective unless such signs are in place. Signs indicating the direction of lawful traffic movement shall be placed at every intersection where movement of traffic in the opposite direction is prohibited.
(b)
Speed signs. The police chief shall cause to be erected and the utilities director shall erect throughout the city signs indicating the maximum speed limit and/or such other information as will be informative to drivers of motor vehicles as it pertains to traffic control regulations at said location or in the city.
(c)
Turn signs. The police chief is hereby authorized to determine those intersections at which drivers of vehicles shall not make a right or left turn. The utilities director shall place proper signs at such intersections. The making of such turns may be prohibited between certain hours of any day and permitted at other hours, in which event the same shall be plainly indicated on the signs, or they may be removed when such turns are permitted.
(d)
Turn markers. The utilities director is authorized to place markers, buttons, and signs within or adjacent to intersections indicating the course to be traveled by vehicles turning at such intersection, and such course to be traveled as so indicated may conform to or be other than as prescribed by this chapter or other law or ordinance.
(e)
Marking crosswalks, safety zones, traffic lanes. The utilities director is hereby authorized:
(1)
To designate and maintain, by appropriate devices, marks or lines upon the surface of the roadway, crosswalks at intersections where there is particular danger to pedestrians crossing the roadway and at such other places as such engineer may deem necessary.
(2)
To establish safety zones of such kind and character and at such places as may be necessary for the protection of pedestrians.
(3)
To make lanes for traffic on street pavements at such places as may be advisable, consistent with this chapter and other traffic ordinances of the city.
(f)
Stop signs. Whenever this chapter or any ordinance of the city designates and describes a through street it shall be the duty of the utilities director to place and maintain a stop sign or yield right-of-way sign on each intersection of such through street or intersection of that portion described and designated as such by this chapter or any ordinance of this city.
(g)
Hazardous locations. The police chief is hereby authorized to determined and designate intersections where particular hazards exist upon streets other than those designated by this chapter or any other ordinance of this city as through streets, and to place at such determined and designated hazardous intersections at one or more entrances to any such intersection either a stop sign or yield right-of-way sign.
(Code 1962, §§ 23-12, 23-32, 23-54, 23-55, 23-64, 23-118, 23-119; Code 1986, § 20-6; Ord. No. O-92-03-15, 3-23-1992)
Sec. 86-28. - Temporary traffic rules.
Where the traffic upon the streets becomes congested, and upon occasions of parades, at theaters and other public places where large numbers of vehicles are assembled, the police may make temporary rules regulating the traffic in such congested districts.
(Code 1962, § 23-62; Code 1986, § 20-17)
Sec. 86-29. - Play streets; authority to establish.
The police chief shall have authority to declare any street or part thereof a play street and to place appropriate signs or devices in the roadway indicating and helping to protect the same.
(Code 1962, § 23-71; Code 1986, § 20-40; Ord. No. O-92-03-15, 3-23-1992)
Secs. 86-30—86-46. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - VIOLATIONS AND PENALTIES

Sec. 86-47. - Owner liable for parking violation.
It shall be unlawful for any person to allow, permit, or suffer any vehicle registered in such person's name to stand or park in any street, in violation of this chapter or any other ordinance of the city regulating the parking of vehicles. Such person shall be equally liable with the operator for such violation and in addition thereto may have the vehicle impounded; provided, however, this shall not apply in those instances described in O.C.G.A. § 40-6-207.
(Code 1962, § 23-144; Code 1986, § 20-80)
Sec. 86-48. - Presumption in reference to illegal parking.
(a)
In any prosecution charging a violation of any law or regulation governing the standing or parking of a vehicle proof that the particular vehicle described in the complaint was parked in violation of any such law or regulation, together with proof that the defendant named in the complaint was at the time of such parking the registered owner of such vehicle, shall constitute in evidence a prima facie presumption that the registered owner of such vehicle was the person who parked or placed such vehicle at the point where, and for the time which, such violation occurred; provided, however, this shall not apply in those instances described in O.C.G.A. § 40-6-207.
(b)
The foregoing stated presumption shall apply only when the procedure in section 86-49 has been followed.
(Code 1962, § 23-142; Code 1986, § 20-81)
Sec. 86-49. - Notice on illegally parked vehicle.
Whenever any motor vehicle without a driver is found parked or stopped in violation of any of the restrictions imposed by this chapter or other ordinance of this city or by state law, the officer finding such vehicle shall take its registration number and may take other information displayed on the vehicle which may identify its user, and shall conspicuously affix to such vehicle a notice in writing, on a form provided by the city clerk for the driver to answer to the charge at the time and place specified in the notice.
(Code 1962, § 23-143; Code 1986, § 20-82)
Sec. 86-50. - When summons for arrest to be issued.
In the event any person fails to comply with a notice given to such person or attached to a vehicle or fails to make appearance pursuant to a summons, or if any person fails or refuses to deposit bail as required and within the time permitted by ordinance, the city clerk shall forthwith secure and issue a summons for such person's arrest.
(Code 1962, § 23-146; Code 1986, § 20-83)
Sec. 86-51. - When warrants for arrest to be issued.
When it shall be made to appear to the municipal court that any person has been lawfully served with a copy of charges to appear in such court for trial and shall fail to appear at the time and place set forth in such copy of charges, it shall be the duty of the municipal judge to forthwith issue a warrant for the arrest of such person and deliver same to the chief of police.
(Code 1962, § 23-150; Code 1986, § 20-86)
Secs. 86-52—86-75. - Reserved.

Sec. 86-79. - Driving in a procession generally.
Each driver in a funeral or other procession shall drive as near to the right-hand edge of the street or roadway as practicable and shall follow the vehicle ahead as close as is practicable and safe.
(Code 1962, § 23-42; Code 1986, § 20-35)
Sec. 86-80. - Vehicles comprising funeral procession.
(a)
Vehicles of a funeral procession shall have the right-of-way over all other vehicles, except authorized emergency vehicles, provided each shall identify itself by burning regular headlights, and by keeping in close formation.
(b)
When the lead vehicle in a funeral procession has entered an intersection on a green light, all other cars in the procession may proceed through the intersection, even though such signal may change to red.
(Code 1962, § 23-34; Code 1986, § 20-36)
Sec. 86-81. - Driving through funeral or other procession.
No driver of a vehicle shall drive between the vehicles comprising a funeral or other authorized procession while they are in motion and when such vehicles are conspicuously designated as required in section 86-80.
(Code 1962, § 23-41; Code 1986, § 20-37)
Sec. 86-82. - Signal to leave parking space.
Whenever vehicles are parked parallel to the curbing, before the operator of a parked vehicle or a vehicle stopped or standing at the edge of the street or roadway as prescribed in this chapter, shall execute a movement into any portion of the street or roadway normally used for moving traffic, such person shall signal the intention to do so by first giving the signal as prescribed in O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-123—40-6-125, and shall start such movement only when the operator can do so with reasonable safety and without creating a hazard to other traffic moving lawfully within the street or roadway.
(Code 1962, § 23-38; Code 1986, § 20-39)

(Code 1962, § 23-120; Code 1986, § 20-49)
Sec. 86-90. - Criminal trespass by a motor vehicle.
(a)
A person commits the offense of criminal trespass by a motor vehicle when the person, after having been requested not to do so by a law enforcement officer or by the owner or an authorized agent of the owner, parks or stands an occupied or unoccupied motor vehicle in, or repeatedly drives a motor vehicle through or within, a parking area which is located on privately owned property and is provided by a merchant, a group of merchants, or a shopping center or other similar facility for customers if:
(1)
The parking area identified by at least one sign as specified in this section, and if the parking area contains more than 150 parking spaces then by at least one such sign for every 150 parking spaces, each sign containing the following information in easy-to-read printing:
a.
Notice of the elements of the crime of criminal trespass by motor vehicle;
b.
Identification of the property that is reserved for customers' use only;
c.
Identification of the merchant, group of merchants, or shopping center or other similar facility providing the parking area; and
d.
Warning that violators will be prosecuted.
(2)
The motor vehicle is parked, is standing, or is being operated other than for the purpose of:
a.
Transporting some person to or from the interior of the place of business of a merchant identified by the sign or signs in the parking area, or to or from the interior of the shopping center or other facility so identified;
b.
Making use of a telephone, vending machine, automatic teller machine, or other similar facility located in the parking area;
c.
Meeting the requirements of a situation in which it has unexpectedly become impossible or impractical for the motor vehicle to continue to travel on the public roads; or
d.
Carrying out an activity for which express permission has been given by the owner of the parking area of an authorized representative of the owner.
(b)
Any person violating this section shall be subject to a monetary fine as follows:
(1)
Not to exceed $50.00 for the first such violation;
(2)
Not to exceed $100.00 for the second such violation;
(3)
Not to exceed $150.00 for the third or subsequent such violation.
(Code 1986, § 20-50; Ord. No. O-87-06-11, 6-22-1987)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-252.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 86-113. - Park within markings.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to park any vehicle on any portion of the streets within the city that have been or are, at the time of such parking, marked in sections indicating by such marking where and how vehicles shall be parked, otherwise than in conformity to such markings, leaving all the wheels of such parked vehicle clearly within the space indicated by such marks for parking of vehicles.
(b)
The provisions of this section shall not apply to commercial vehicles over 25 feet in length delivering or receiving cargo from buildings not equipped with off-street loading and unloading capabilities, providing said vehicles park in such manner for no more than 15 minutes, block only one lane of traffic, and that such vehicle is moved upon demand of any motorist whose exit from parking may be blocked by the delivery vehicle.

ARTICLE V. - ABANDONED VEHICLES

Sec. 86-172. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Abandoned motor vehicle means a motor vehicle, which has been parked or left standing on the public streets of the city for more than five days in a nonoperating state of repair.
Abandonment means the act of the owner leaving the vehicle on the streets or sidewalks of the city for a period in excess of five days, when the owner of the vehicle does not own the property abutting the street or sidewalk upon which the vehicle is parked and does not reside, room, have a place of business, lease, rent, or otherwise exercise control and dominion over the abutting real estate.
Hazard to normal flow of traffic means a vehicle parked on the public streets of the city in a nonoperational condition and is hereby defined to be a hazard to the normal flow of traffic on the streets of the city.
Nonoperational vehicle means a vehicle that will not start, run and move under its own power in its existing condition.
Vehicle means any automobile, truck, trailer, motorcycle, wagon, or other artificial contrivance used, or capable of being used, as a means of transportation on land.
(Code 1962, § 23-106; Code 1986, § 20-70)
State Law reference— Similar definitions, O.C.G.A. § 40-11-1.

It shall be unlawful to abandon any vehicle upon the streets or sidewalks of the city.
