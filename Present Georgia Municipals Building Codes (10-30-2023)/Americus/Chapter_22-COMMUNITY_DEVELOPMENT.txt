State Law reference— City Business Improvement District Act, O.C.G.A. § 36-43-1 et seq.

Sec. 22-19. - Established.
(a)
There is hereby established a central city business improvement district in the downtown development authority area of the city, effective January 1, 2003, said city business improvement district being established pursuant to the provisions of the City Business Improvement District Act (O.C.G.A. § 36-43-1 et seq.).
(b)
The legal boundaries of the city business improvement district hereby established are more particularly described as follows:
Start at the intersection of the north right-of-way line of West Church Street with the east right-of-way line of Hampton Street in the City of Americus as the point of beginning of the boundary description described herein; thence run in an easterly direction along the north right-of-way line of Church Street to the west right-of-way line of Prince Street; thence run in a northerly and westerly direction along the west right-of-way line of Prince Street to the west right-of-way line of North Jackson Street; thence run along the west right-of-way line of North Jackson Street in a southerly direction to the north right-of-way-line of West Jefferson Street; thence run in a westerly direction along the north right-of-way line of West Jefferson Street to the east right-of-way line of North Hampton Street; thence run in a southerly direction along the east right-of-way line of North Hampton Street to the north right-of-way line of West Church Street and the point of beginning of the boundary description herein.
There is excluded from the boundary of said district any property on which is located a telephone central office and switching facilities which serve an area exceeding the boundaries of the district.
(Code 1986, § 14-80; Ord. No. O-02-12-33, 12-19-2002)
Sec. 22-20. - Adoption of district plan.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 36-43-4, the district plan for the provisions of supplemental services to the district presented to the city council on December 9, 2002, is hereby approved until amended by the city council. A copy of such district plan as adopted, and as hereafter amended, may be obtained from the office of the clerk of the city during reasonable business hours.
(b)
The city council shall have the right to amend the district plan adopted at such time or times, as the city council may deem appropriate.
(Code 1986, § 14-81; Ord. No. O-02-12-33, 12-19-2002)
Sec. 22-21. - Establishment of ad valorem tax rates.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 36-43-4, there is hereby established an annual millage rate of one mill upon all real and personal property located within the district, which millage rate is in addition to all millage rates upon real and personal property previously established for property located in the city.
(b)
The millage rate authorized pursuant to this section shall be subject to change annually by the city council at the time that the city approves its annual operating and capital improvements budgets, and at such other times as may be deemed appropriate by the city council; provided, however, that in no event shall the millage rate authorized in this section exceed five mills.
(c)
The taxes established or authorized pursuant to this section shall be assessed against the real and personal property, shall be liens upon that property until paid, and the city shall be authorized to enforce such liens in the same manner as other city tax liens are enforced.
(Code 1986, § 14-82; Ord. No. O-02-12-33, 12-19-2002)
Sec. 22-22. - Business license and occupation tax surcharge.
(a)
The city shall also collect a surcharge on business license and occupation taxes upon businesses and occupations located within said district and such business license and occupation taxes shall be considered tax liens and may be collected in the same manner as other city taxes.
(b)
All surtaxes are to be paid during the occupational license tax period annually from January 1 to March 15 of each year. There will be no prorated city business improvement district surtaxes.
(c)
The business license surtax and occupation surtax shall equal 50 percent of the existing business license and occupation tax.
(d)
No business license or occupation license shall be issued by the city until all business license and occupational taxes for such business or occupation has been paid, including the surtax authorized by this section.
(Code 1986, § 14-83; Ord. No. O-02-12-33, 12-19-2002)
Sec. 22-23. - Design and rehabilitation standards.
The design and rehabilitation standards set out in article VII, of chapter 14 shall remain in effect and be fully enforceable in the city business improvement district.
(Code 1986, § 14-84; Ord. No. O-02-12-33, 12-19-2002)
Sec. 22-24. - Contracts with downtown development authority.
(a)
The city shall be authorized to approve a contract, on an annual basis, with the city downtown development authority established pursuant to the Downtown Development Authorities Law (O.C.G.A. § 36-42-1 et seq.) in connection with the special services to be provided to the city business improvement district. Such contract may include provisions authorizing the downtown development authority to expend funds collected by the city pursuant to this article for the provision of supplemental services to said district and may further authorize the downtown development authority to enter into a contract with third parties, including for profit and nonprofit corporations, for the provision of such supplemental services.
(b)
There shall be established in the city budget a line item for the receipt and expenditure of the funds collected pursuant to this article, and no such funds may be expended without such budget authorization.
(c)
In the event that the city council enter into a contract with the downtown development authority in connection with the administration of the city business improvement district and the expenditure of funds collected pursuant to this article in connection therewith, the city council shall be required to review and approve, on an annual basis, the budget of the downtown development authority for the expenditure of such funds.
(Code 1986, § 14-85; Ord. No. O-02-12-33, 12-19-2002)
Sec. 22-25. - Collection and expenditure of funds.
All funds collected pursuant to this article may only be expended for supplemental services provided in the city business improvement district plan adopted by the city council.
(Code 1986, § 14-86; Ord. No. O-02-12-33, 12-19-2002)
