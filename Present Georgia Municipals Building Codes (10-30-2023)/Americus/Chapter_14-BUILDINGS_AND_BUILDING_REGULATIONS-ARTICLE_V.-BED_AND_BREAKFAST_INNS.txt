ARTICLE V. - BED AND BREAKFAST INNS

Sec. 14-199. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Bed and breakfast inn means a residential dwelling containing not more than eight guestrooms or suites, designed and operated to provide for overnight accommodations of guests who limit their stay to not more than seven consecutive nights, with adequate off-street parking of one off-street parking place for each guestroom, and adequate off-street parking for the owner, operator and staff of the inn.
Building permits means all permits required to be obtained from the city in accord with the provisions of this Code or other relevant ordinance or statute.
Exterior architectural features means the architectural style, general design and general arrangement of the exterior of the building or other structure including, but not limited to, the kind or texture of the building material and the type and style of all windows, doors, signs and other appurtenant architectural fixtures, features, details or elements relative to the building structure.
License means all licenses, whether business license, building license or other documents, which must be obtained by the owner or operator of a business as required by the various provisions of this Code.
(Code 1986, § 5-151; Ord. No. O-87-10-19, § 1, 10-19-1987)
Sec. 14-200. - Establishment, maintenance and operation of bed and breakfast inn.
Any bed and breakfast inn established, maintained or operated within the city shall at all times comply with the following requirements:
(1)
The building in which the bed and breakfast inn is located shall comply with all requirements for residential buildings contained in the city building code as it now exists or as it is hereafter amended.
(2)
There shall be no building or buildings located upon the lot where the bed and breakfast inn is located, except the principal building and any accessory building or buildings as may be required; provided that such accessory building or buildings shall comply with the other requirements contained in this Code regarding such accessory buildings.
(3)
The owner or operator of the bed and breakfast inn shall maintain a current guest register, which shall include the names, addresses and dates of occupancy of all guests at the inn.
(4)
The inn may serve its guests only breakfast (morning meal) and dinner (evening meal) at the inn, and no other meal shall be served, except that the owner/occupant of the inn shall not be subject to this requirement for their own meals.
(5)
The owner or operator of the bed and breakfast inn shall comply with all business license, building permit requirements and revenue collection ordinances of the city and the state.
(Code 1986, § 5-152; Ord. No. O-87-10-19, § 2, 10-19-1987)
Sec. 14-201. - Architectural design.
The exterior architectural features and architectural design of the building used as a bed and breakfast inn must be maintained and must be in keeping with the general architectural design and general appearance of the neighborhood. No exterior alterations, other than those necessary to ensure the safety of the structure, shall be made to the dwelling for the purpose of providing a bed and breakfast inn.
(Code 1986, § 5-153; Ord. No. O-87-10-19, § 3, 10-19-1987)
Sec. 14-202. - Planning commission approval of plans.
As a condition precedent to any building permit being issued under this article, or before any building under this article is used for a bed and breakfast inn, the city planning commission shall approve any plans, specifications or other building itself to see that the architectural design, the exterior architectural features, and the general appearance of the building is in keeping with the architectural design and general appearance of the neighborhood.
(Code 1986, § 5-154; Ord. No. O-87-10-19, § 4, 10-19-1987)
Sec. 14-203. - Violation of article.
Any violation of the provisions of this article regarding bed and breakfast inns, or any other violation of any ordinance contained in this Code relating to bed and breakfast inns shall be a misdemeanor and shall subject the offender to punishment as provided in section 1-8. In addition thereto, any such violation shall be cause for revocation or nonrenewal of the city business license of the owner or operator of the bed and breakfast inn by the finance director.
(Code 1986, § 5-156; Ord. No. O-87-10-19, 10-19-1987)
Secs. 14-204—14-229. - Reserved.
