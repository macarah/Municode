Sec. 42-19. - Findings and purpose.
(a)
The city council does hereby find:
(1)
Numerous studies have found that tobacco smoke is a major source of the contributor to indoor air pollution.
(2)
Reliable studies, including those published by the Surgeon General of the United States, have shown that the inhalation of the smoke injected into the atmosphere by the use of tobacco products by others is a cause of and contributing factor of disease, including, without limitation, lung cancer in healthy nonsmokers.
(3)
It is the obligation of all employers to use their best efforts in furnishing a safe workplace for their employees.
(b)
The purpose of this article is to protect the health of nonsmokers in public city-owned buildings.
(Code 1986, § 12-70; Ord. No. O-92-10-105, 10-26-1992)
Sec. 42-20. - Smoking prohibited in certain buildings.
No person shall smoke or carry a lighted cigar, cigarette, pipe, or match, or use any spark, flame, or fire-producing device for the purpose of smoking in any building owned or operated by the city.
(Code 1986, § 12-71; Ord. No. O-92-10-105, 10-26-1992)
Sec. 42-21. - Posting of signs required.
There shall be conspicuously posted, in every building owned or operated by the city, signs notifying all persons that no smoking is allowed in the building. Such signs shall contain letters of not less than two inches in height indicating "No Smoking" in the premises or, in the alternative, shall contain the international symbol for "No Smoking," which consists of a pictorial representation of a burning cigarette enclosed in a red circle, with a red bar bisecting the circle.
(Code 1986, § 12-72; Ord. No. O-92-10-105, 10-26-1992)
Sec. 42-22. - Violation of article.
No person shall smoke in any properly placarded place, nor shall any person remove any placard or sign required to be erected by or under the authority of this article.
(Code 1986, § 12-73; Ord. No. O-92-10-105, 10-26-1992)
