Chapter 18 - CEMETERIES

Sec. 18-1. - Sale of lots.
The selling of all lots in the cemeteries shall be done by the cemetery clerk, who shall make out and deliver to the purchaser, upon receipt of the purchase money, a certificate of ownership of burial rights to the same, which shall be signed by the mayor and the city clerk on behalf of the city. The price of all lots shall be as prescribed by the city council. All payments for lots must be made prior to burial unless payment arrangements are made by written agreement with the cemetery clerk prior to burial. In the event a burial is performed in a lot in which burial rights have not been purchased in advance of such burial, the purchase of such lot shall incur a penalty of $5,000.00 in addition to the price of the lot. The payment for the purchaser of the burial lot and the penalty shall be paid to the city within five calendar days of the receipt of an invoice from the city for such obligations. There shall be no appeal with respect to any penalty imposed under this section.
(Code 1962, § 6-1; Code 1986, § 6-1; Ord. No. O-04-12-33, 12-16-2004; Ord. No. O-2021-11, 7-22-2021)
Sec. 18-2. - Perpetual care areas—Establishment, designation.
The cemetery clerk in charge of cemeteries, under the direction and control of the city council, shall be authorized to designate, by appropriate plat areas in such cemeteries, areas to be designated as perpetual care areas.
(Code 1962, § 6-10; Code 1986, § 6-2)
Sec. 18-3. - Same—Platting; sale of spaces.
The spaces specified as those designated for burial rights shall be platted by adequate and proper plat and shall be sold to the general public at prices to be established by ordinance of the city council.
(Code 1962, § 6-12; Code 1986, § 6-4)
Sec. 18-4. - Same—Responsibility for maintenance; erection of markers.
Maintenance of the designated areas shall remain the responsibility of the city council. Markers that are erected on such burial spaces by the owners of interment rights shall be erected in accordance with rules and regulations as promulgated and adopted by the city council.
(Code 1962, § 6-13; Code 1986, § 6-5)
Sec. 18-5. - Same—Maintenance.
The cemetery clerk in charge of city owned and operated cemeteries is further charged with the responsibility of maintaining perpetual care in the designated areas. The cemetery clerk shall have the authority to remove or cause to be removed any flowers or other decorations which appear to be worn or deteriorated. The cemetery clerk shall remove or cause to be removed all Easter, Christmas and other religious holiday flowers and similar holiday commemoration items placed upon cemetery lots 30 calendar days after the date of the respective holiday. The cemetery clerk shall have the authority to authorize the pruning, removal, or transplantation of any tree, shrub, plant, bush, or object including, but not limited to, ornaments, bird feeders and other items placed on lots by the owner of a lot of the designated agent thereof in areas so designated by the cemetery clerk. No person shall plant, prune, or remove any trees, shrubs, or plants or without the written consent of the cemetery clerk. All grass cutting in the common areas of a cemetery, which does not include lots already sold by the city, shall be mowed and maintained by the city. If the cemetery clerk deems it necessary or appropriate to do so in order to maintain the proper appearance of a cemetery, the cemetery clerk shall be authorized to cause any objects, things, or trash which are not permanently affixed to or within a lot, or which are found in the common areas of the cemetery, to be removed from such lot. No person shall be authorized to water any portion of the cemetery without the prior receipt of express permission from the cemetery clerk to do so.
(Code 1962, § 6-14; Code 1986, § 6-6; Ord. No. O-2021-11, 7-22-2021)
Sec. 18-6. - Burials outside public cemeteries, permission required.
No person shall bury or cause to be buried, any dead person in any other place in the city than the public cemeteries, without permission of the city council.
(Code 1962, § 6-5; Code 1986, § 6-7)
Sec. 18-7. - Disinterment—Application.
Any person desiring to disinter a body which is buried in any cemetery located in the city shall make an application in writing to the city and county health authorities giving the name of the person buried in said grave, if known, and the reason that such applicant desires to disinter said body. This application shall be signed by the person, who desires to disinter, said body and the health authorities shall act on such application at their next meeting. The application shall contain the relationship of the applicant to the deceased person.
(Code 1962, §§ 6-6, 6-7; Code 1986, § 6-8)
Sec. 18-8. - Same—Permit discretionary.
The health authorities may issue a permit to remove said body from the cemetery if in their discretion it is desirable.
(Code 1962, § 6-8; Code 1986, § 6-9)
Sec. 18-9. - Defacing of tombstones, etc.
No person shall willfully mutilate, or in any manner damage or deface any monument, tombstone, gate, fence, railing, or other enclosure in or around the cemeteries, or structures, or break down, damage, destroy or pluck, without consent of the owner, any wild or cultivated flower, shrub, plant or tree therein. Any person convicted of violating this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-8.
(Code 1962, § 6-4; Code 1986, § 6-10)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-22 et seq.
Sec. 18-10. - Building materials, etc.; removal required.
All piles of earth, rubbish, building materials, or internment materials of any kind, shall be removed by the person placing the same in the cemeteries, within 24 hours after the completion of the work for which they were used. In the event that all piles of earth, rubbish, building materials, or internment materials of any kind, are not removed within 24 hours after the completion of the work, the person placing the same shall incur a penalty of $250.00 for the cleaning of such earth, rubbish or building materials. The payment of such penalty shall be made no later than five calendar days from the receipt from the city of an invoice for such obligations. There shall be no appeal with respect to any penalty imposed upon this section.
(Code 1962, § 6-3; Code 1986, § 6-11; Ord. No. O-2021-11, 7-22-2021)
Sec. 18-11. - Motor vehicles, entering cemetery.
Motor vehicles may be allowed to enter any cemetery under the following restrictions:
(1)
All motor vehicles so entering a cemetery shall be for attending funerals, for visiting gravesites, for preparing gravesites prior to a burial, to undertake work on a cemetery lot authorized under this chapter, or for business purposes related to the cemetery. No person shall be authorized to drive a motor vehicle, ride a bicycle, motorcycle, all-terrain vehicle, or similar mode of personal transportation, or walk, jog, or run through a cemetery or within a cemetery except for one of the foregoing purposes.
(2)
All motor vehicles so entering a cemetery shall not be driven at a rate of speed in excess of that permitted in section 86-76.
(3)
Only city maintenance vehicles shall be permitted off paved road surfaces unless prior written permission is granted by the cemetery clerk.
(Code 1962, § 6-2; Code 1986, § 6-12; Ord. No. O-2021-11, 7-22-2021)
Sec. 18-12. - Hours; scheduling.
(a)
The hours of public use and access of all cemeteries shall be the same as that in section 66-2 governing the hours of operation of public parks.
(b)
The hours of the cemetery clerk's office shall be from 8:00 a.m. to 12:00 p.m. Monday through Friday. The public works department should be contacted for any emergencies outside of the cemetery clerk's regular hours.
(c)
Any work undertaken in the cemeteries including, but not limited to coping, monument work, and the digging of new graves, shall be scheduled in advance with the cemetery clerk.
(d)
All funeral services, including services for pre-purchased lots, must be scheduled in advance with the cemetery clerk. Funeral services shall be scheduled in the order in which the cemetery clerk is contacted about such service.
(e)
Funeral services scheduled on the same day will be scheduled a minimum of two hours apart, unless scheduled with written permission from the cemetery clerk.
(f)
No graves shall be dug more than 24 hours in advance of the scheduled funeral service. Any grave dug prior to the funeral service during such designated time frame shall be properly covered and made safe to the public by the party responsible for digging such grave. All graves must be closed by the party responsible for digging such grave immediately after the funeral service is concluded and the family and guests have departed.
(Ord. No. O-2021-11, 7-22-2021)
Sec. 18-13. - Violation.
Upon a person's conviction or upon such person' s entry of a plea of nolo contendere in the municipal court of the city to any violation of this chapter, in addition to any civil penalties which may be provided in this chapter for the violation of this chapter, such person shall also be subject to punishment as provided in section 1-8 of the Code of Ordinances of the city.
(Ord. No. O-2021-11, 7-22-2021)
