Sec. 9-1-1. - Definitions.
[For the purposes of this chapter, the following words and terms shall have the meaning ascribed thereto:]
Employee. The term "employee" shall mean any person at least 18 years of age who renders any service in connection with the operation of a massage establishment and receives compensation from the operator of such establishment.
Licensee. The term "licensee" shall mean any person, partnership, firm, corporation, or other entity to which a license has been issued to practice massage, or operate a massage establishment as defined herein.
Massage or massage services. The term "massage" or "massage services" means the manipulation of soft tissue for therapeutic purposes and includes but is not limited to effleurage (stroking skin without going deep), petrissage (kneading movement by lifting and grasping), tapotement (brisk blows in rapid alternating fashion), compression, vibration, friction, nerve strokes and other movements, whether by hand or with mechanical or electrical apparatus for the purpose of muscular massage. This may include the use of oil, salt glows, hot and cold pack and other recognized forms of massage therapy.
Massage establishment. The term "massage establishment" means any establishment where one or more massage therapists provide massage services for money or similar consideration, and which constitutes a fixed place of business for any person, partnership, firm, corporation or other entity to engage in any of the activities defined as "massage." A "massage establishment" includes but is not limited to a massage parlor, health garden, oriental clinic, spas, or any other establishment wherein massage or related services are rendered.
Massage therapist. The term "massage therapist" means any person engaged in the practice of "massage" as defined in this section, provided that the massage or massage services are offered in businesses that provide other personal care services, such as a fitness center, beauty salon, or other personal care services.
Physical therapist. The term "physical therapist" means a person licensed by the State of Georgia to practice physical therapy as defined by the O.C.G.A., section 43-33-3.
(Ord. No. 93-02, § 1, 5-25-93; Ord. No. 94-15, § 1.C, 12-13-94; Ord. No. 2003-08, § 1A., 7-22-03)
Sec. 9-1-2. - License required.
Any person desiring to engage in the practice as a massage therapist, or any person, partnership, firm, corporation or other entity desiring to operate the business of a massage establishment shall apply for and obtain a license from the city department of permits and licenses (the department) prior to commencing operations.
(Ord. No. 93-02, § 1, 5-25-93; Ord. No. 94-15, § 1.C, 12-13-94)
Sec. 9-1-3. - Exemptions.
This chapter shall not apply to the following individuals while engaged in the personal performance of the duties required of their respective professions:
(1)
Physicians, surgeons, chiropractors, osteopaths, and other licensed practitioners of the healing arts;
(2)
Nurses who are registered under the laws of this state;
(3)
Barbers and beauticians who are duly licensed under the laws of this state, except that this exemption shall apply solely to the massaging of the neck, face, scalp, and hair of the customer or client for cosmetic or beautification purposes; and
(4)
Physical therapists licensed under the laws of this state.
(Ord. No. 93-02, § 1, 5-25-93; Ord. No. 94-15, § 1.C, 12-13-94)
Sec. 9-1-4. - Requirement for license; qualifications.
(a)
A license to provide massage services shall be issued to any person who is at least 18 years of age and:
(1)
Is a graduate of an educational program or course of study of not less than 500 hours in duration and which is accredited by a professional massage accrediting organization. The curriculum of such program or course of study shall include but not be limited to the following subjects: physical culture, anatomy, physiology, massage, hydrotherapy, electrotherapy, hygiene, and health service management. Other similarly related subjects may be substituted for those herein provided. In the case of an applicant who has graduated from a program conducted in a foreign country, such applicant must submit credentials approved by the department and have satisfactorily completed a three-month traineeship under the supervision of an individual meeting the eligibility requirements for licensing under this chapter; or
(2)
Is not a graduate of an educational program or course of study meeting the criteria set forth in subsection (1) above, but has had an apprenticeship and practical experience for a period of not less than two years under the direct supervision of a massage or physical therapist who would otherwise qualify for a license under this chapter. Additionally, such applicant must have received training and experience in areas which include but are not limited to the following: physical culture, anatomy, physiology, massage, hydrotherapy, electrotherapy, hygiene, and health service management. Other similarly related areas may be substituted for those herein provided. The applicant in this instance must furnish with the application an affidavit of previous employment, together with an affidavit of the person(s) under whom his or her apprenticeship or practical experience was obtained, providing the necessary requirements for qualification under this section. Further, the applicant must have successfully passed, within the last year, the National Certification Examination for Professional Massage and Bodywork developed by The Psychological Corporation working in conjunction with the National Certification Board for Bodywork and Massage Therapies; and
(b)
A license to operate a massage establishment shall be issued to any person, partnership, firm, corporation or other entity if the person(s) having a beneficial interest in the establishment is (are) at least 18 years of age, and:
(1)
Employs one or more massage therapists meeting the requirements set forth in subsection (a) above;
(2)
If a corporation, is an officer of the corporation which is organized pursuant to the laws of this state or authorized to do business in this state; and
(3)
Meets all provisions of this chapter except those related to paragraphs (1) and (2) of this section.
(Ord. No. 93-02, § 1, 5-25-93; Ord. No. 94-15, § 1.C, 12-13-94)
Sec. 9-1-5. - License application.
Every applicant for a license to engage in the practice as a massage therapist or operator of a massage establishment shall file an application under oath with the department upon a form provided by the department. An annual license fee in such amount as provided in this chapter shall be tendered with the application. Copies of the application shall be forwarded to the building inspection department, the fire department and police department. These departments shall within 30 days inspect any premises proposed to be operated as a massage establishment and shall make written verification to the department concerning compliance with applicable codes.
(a)
Each application shall contain the following information:
(1)
The names and addresses for the past five years of the applicant and each person who would have a beneficial interest in the license. However, if the applicant is a corporation, only the names and addresses of the registered agents and those stockholders owning at least five percent of the stock must be disclosed;
(2)
The name of the business operation, including a description of all services to be provided;
(3)
The location, mailing address and telephone number where the business is to be conducted;
(4)
The name, address, social security number and date of birth of each employee working at the massage establishment. Changes in the list of employees with the names of new employees, together with all information relating thereto, and all exhibits must be filed with the department within three days from the date of change;
(5)
Business, occupation or employment of the applicant for the three years immediately preceding the date of application;
(6)
The massage or similarly related business license history of the applicant; whether such person or entity has had a business license revoked or suspended in this or another county or state, and if so, the reason therefor, and the business activity or occupation of the applicant subsequent to such action of suspension or revocation;
(7)
All criminal convictions other than misdemeanor traffic violations, including the date(s) and place(s) of conviction(s), and nature of the offense(s);
(8)
The name, address and description of any massage business or other establishment owned or operated by a person whose name is required to be given by this chapter; and
(9)
A description of any other business to be operated on the same premises or on adjoining premises owned or controlled by the applicant; and
(b)
Applicant shall submit with the application certain documentation as required by the department, including but not limited to, the following:
(1)
A blueprint or a scale drawing of the establishment;
(2)
Proof that the applicant and all employees providing massage services are at least 18 years of age;
(3)
A copy of the applicant's diploma or certificate of graduation from an accredited school or other institution of learning as provided in section 9-1-4(a)(1), or if applicable, a copy of the examination results as provided in section 9-1-4(a)(2). However, if the applicant will not engage in the practice of massage as heretofore defined, such applicant need not provide a diploma or certificate of graduation, or results of the examination;
(4)
A signed and notarized consent form authorizing the department and the police department, their respective agents and employees to seek information and conduct a background check on the applicant and all employees (in the case of a massage establishment), and to conduct an investigation into the truth of the statements set forth in the application and the qualifications of the applicant for a license;
(5)
A signed and notarized consent form authorizing the fire department, their respective agents and employees to inspect the premises to ensure compliance with the Fire Safety Code, all other codes and laws under their jurisdiction, and applicable provisions of this chapter;
(6)
A current health certificate from a physician on the applicant and all employee trainees providing massage services, if applicable, indicating that they do not have a communicable disease or otherwise would not present a threat to the health, safety and welfare of their respective clientele; and
(7)
A copy of this chapter signed by the applicant and all employee trainees providing massage services, if applicable, acknowledging that he or she has read and understands the provisions contained therein.
(Ord. No. 93-02, § 1, 5-25-93; Ord. No. 94-15, § 1.C, 12-13-94)
Sec. 9-1-6. - Operating requirements.
(a)
Every portion of a massage establishment, including appliances and apparatus, shall be kept clean and operated in a sanitary condition.
(b)
Price rates for all services rendered shall be prominently posted in the reception area in a location available to all prospective clientele.
(c)
All employees shall practice pursuant hygiene and wear clean, nontransparent outer garments. A separate dressing room for each sex must be available on the premises.
(d)
All massage establishments shall be provided with clean, laundered sheets and towels in sufficient quantity as required in the business' normal operation and shall be laundered after each use thereof and stored in a sanitary manner.
(e)
Nothing contained in this chapter shall be construed to eliminate other requirements of any statute or ordinance governing the maintenance of premises, nor to preclude authorized inspection thereof, whenever such inspection is deemed necessary by appropriate department officials.
(Ord. No. 93-02, § 1, 5-25-93; Ord. No. 94-15, § 1.C, 12-13-94)
Sec. 9-1-7. - License fees.
The license fee for an applicant to be engaged in the practice as a massage therapist shall be $200.00 per year. If the applicant is the operator of a massage establishment and is not a massage therapist, the license fee shall be as provided in Title 3, Chapter 2, Article D of this Code.
(Ord. No. 93-2, § 1, 5-25-93; Ord. No. 94-15, § 1.C, 12-13-94)
Sec. 9-1-8. - Training permits; permit fees.
(a)
The department may issue a training permit to an unlicensed trainee who is at least 18 years of age and is currently, or will be in the immediate future, enrolled in an educational program or course of study as required under section 9-1-4(a)(1), or preparing to sit for the examination as required under section 9-1-4(a)(2) of this chapter.
(b)
A training permit shall allow the holder thereof to work only under the direct supervision of a person licensed under this chapter who has worked as a massage therapist for a period of not less than one continuous year prior to assuming the supervisory role.
(c)
The training permit issued pursuant to this section shall be valid up to six months or expire immediately upon the securing of a license granted under the provisions of this chapter.
(d)
A training permit may be subject to renewal, at the discretion of the department, when good and sufficient cause is shown. However, in any event, the training permit may not be renewed more than one time and a failure to timely satisfy the educational or experience requirements is not deemed to be good and sufficient cause.
(e)
The permit fee for an apprentice or trainee shall be $50.00 per year.
(Ord. No. 93-02, § 1, 5-25-93; Ord. No. 94-15, § 1.C, 12-13-94)
Sec. 9-1-9. - Persons under 18 years of age.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for a licensee to provide massage services to any person under the age of 18 years unless such person has with him or her at the time of such patronage a written order from an appropriate practitioner of the healing arts directing that the treatment is to be given.
(b)
It shall be the duty of the licensee to determine the age of the person patronizing such establishment, and a violation of this section shall be grounds for revocation of the license of such licensee.
(Ord. No. 93-02, § 1, 5-25-93; Ord. No. 94-15, § 1.C, 12-13-94)
Sec. 9-1-10. - Alcoholic beverages prohibited.
No person shall sell, give, dispense, provide or keep, or cause to be sold, given, dispensed, provided or kept, any alcoholic beverage on the premises of any massage establishment.
(Ord. No. 93-02, § 1, 5-25-93; Ord. No. 94-15, § 1.C, 12-13-94)
Sec. 9-1-11. - Inspection required.
The department and the police department, their respective authorized agents or employees shall be authorized from time to time to make inspections during regular business hours of each massage establishment for the purpose of determining that Georgia law and the provisions of this chapter are fully complied with. It shall be unlawful for any licensee hereunder to fail to allow the city's department officers and officials access to the premises or to hinder the officer or official in any manner.
(Ord. No. 93-02, § 1, 5-25-93; Ord. No. 94-15, § 1.C, 12-13-94)
Sec. 9-1-12. - Refusal to grant or restore license; discipline of licensees; suspension, revocation, or restriction of license.
(a)
The department shall have authority to refuse to grant a license to an applicant or to discipline a licensee under this chapter upon a finding by the department that the licensee or applicant has:
(1)
Displayed an inability or has become unable to work as a massage therapist or as an operator of a massage establishment, as the case may be, with reasonable skill and safety to clientele by reason of illness, use of alcohol, drugs, narcotics, chemicals, or any other type of substance, or as a result of any mental or physical condition. In enforcing this paragraph, the department may, upon reasonable grounds, require the licensee or applicant to submit to a mental or physical examination by an appropriate practitioner of the healing arts. The expense of such examination shall be borne by such licensee or applicant. The results of such examination shall be admissible in any hearing before the department, the mayor and council or court of competent jurisdiction, in the event of an appeal. If a licensee or applicant fails to submit to such an examination when properly directed to do so by the department, unless such failure was due to circumstances beyond the licensee's or applicant's control, the department may enter a final order upon proper notice, hearing, and proof of such refusal; or
(2)
Been convicted in the last five years of the following offenses: sodomy, aggravated sodomy, or solicitation of sodomy; public indecency; prostitution; pimping; pandering or pandering by compulsion; distribution of materials that are obscene or depicting nudity or sexual conduct as defined under state law; any offense involving the use of or threat of force or violence upon the person(s) of another; any offense involving narcotics, dangerous drugs or dangerous weapons; or any felony involving moral turpitude. For the purpose of this section, a conviction shall include a finding or verdict of guilty, plea of guilty, or a plea of nolo contendere in a criminal proceeding regardless of whether the adjudication of guilt or sentence is withheld or not entered thereon pursuant to provisions of the O.C.G.A. sections 42-8-60 through 42-8-64, relating to first offenders, or any comparable rule or statute; or
(3)
Knowingly made misleading, deceptive, untrue, or fraudulent representations to a client, customer, or other person or entity in connection with the provisions of massage services or any document connected therewith; or practiced fraud or deceit or intentionally made any false statement in obtaining or attempting to obtain a license provided for in this chapter; or
(4)
Performed massage services contrary to this chapter; or knowingly aided, assisted, procured, or advised any person to perform massage services contrary to this chapter; or knowingly performed any act which in any way aids, assists, procures, advises, or encourages any unlicensed person to perform massage services; or
(5)
Engaged in any unprofessional, unethical, deceptive, deleterious practice harmful to the public, which conduct or practice need not have resulted in actual injury to any person; unprofessional conduct shall also include any departure from or failure to conform to the acceptable and prevailing minimal standards applicable to massage therapists or operators of massage establishments; or
(6)
Failed to report to the department any act or omission of a licensee or applicant or any other person who violates the provisions of this chapter.
(b)
When the department finds that any person is unqualified to be granted a license or finds that any person should be disciplined pursuant to subsection (a) of this section, the department may take such action as more fully set forth in Title 1, Sec. 1-1-8.
(c)
For purposes of this section, a licensee or applicant, whether the same is a person, partnership, firm, corporation or other entity, who may or may not perform massage services, and all persons having a beneficial interest in the operation of a massage establishment (except stockholders owning less than five percent of the stock in a corporation) shall be subject to the provisions of this section and be treated as if they were the licensee or applicant.
(d)
Where the department refused to grant a license to an applicant or has revoked a license under this chapter, the affected licensee or applicant shall be disqualified from reapplying for such license for 12 months immediately following such action taken by the department.
(Ord. No. 93-02, § 1, 5-25-93; Ord. No. 94-15, § 1.C, 12-13-94)
Sec. 9-1-13. - Unlicensed practice constitutes public nuisance; injunctions.
The provision of massage services and the operation of a massage establishment is declared to be an activity affecting the public interest and involving the health, safety, and welfare of the public. Such provision of services and operation by a person, partnership, firm, corporation or other entity who is not licensed, or otherwise authorized, is declared to be a public nuisance, harmful to the public health, safety, and welfare. The department, or any person or organization having an interest therein may bring a petition to restrain or enjoin such unlicensed practice in a court of competent jurisdiction. It shall not be necessary in order to obtain an injunction under this chapter to allege or prove that there is no adequate remedy at all, or to allege or prove any special injury.
(Ord. No. 93-02, § 1, 5-25-93; Ord. No. 94-15, § 1.C, 12-13-94)
Sec. 9-1-14. - Penalty.
A person who violates any provision of this chapter shall be punishable as provided generally for violations of this Code.
(Ord. No. 93-02, § 1, 5-25-93; Ord. No. 94-15, § 1.C, 12-13-94)
