ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 58-1. - Provisions saved from repeal.
Any ordinances adopting a comprehensive plan and shortterm work updates are not repealed and shall continue in full force and effect.
Secs. 58-2—58-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - ENVIRONMENTAL PLANNING CRITERIA[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
Cross reference— Environment, ch. 34.

Sec. 58-31. - Findings.
(a)
Pursuant to the Rules of the Georgia Department of Community Affairs, chapter 110-3-2, a public hearing was conducted on September 27, 1999, in order to gain input from the citizens of the county concerning the adoption of part V environmental planning criteria. Such public hearing's time, date, place and purpose was duly advertised in the county legal organ prior to such public hearing.
(b)
After adoption of an appropriate resolution of September 27, 1999, the board of commissioners transmitted such part V environmental planning criteria to the Georgia Mountains Regional Development Center for its review and comments, after which, to wit: October 21, 1999, the Georgia Mountains Regional Development Center forwarded such criteria to the state department of community affairs, recommending its approval.
(c)
The state department of community affairs, after due consideration, has determined that part V environmental planning criteria, which is to be adopted by the county, complies with the minimum standards and procedures for local coordinated comprehensive planning.
(Res. of 2-14-2000)
Sec. 58-32. - Adoption.
The board of commissioners adopts part V of the state department of community affairs environmental planning criteria established and ratified by the General Assembly pursuant to O.C.G.A. 50-8-1 et seq.
(Res. of 2-14-2000)
