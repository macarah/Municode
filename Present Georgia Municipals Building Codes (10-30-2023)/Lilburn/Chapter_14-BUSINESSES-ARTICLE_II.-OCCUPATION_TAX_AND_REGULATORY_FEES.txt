ARTICLE II. - OCCUPATION TAX AND REGULATORY FEES

Sec. 14-25. - Occupation tax required; occupation tax required for business dealings in the city.
(a)
For the year 1995 and succeeding years thereafter, each person engaged in any business, trade, profession, or occupation in Lilburn, Georgia, whether with a location in Lilburn or in the case of an out-of-state business with no location in Georgia exerting substantial efforts within the state pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7, shall pay an occupation tax for said business, trade, profession, or occupation; which tax and any applicable registration shall be displayed in a conspicuously in the place of business, if the taxpayer has a permanent business location in Lilburn, Georgia. If the taxpayer has no permanent business location in Lilburn, Georgia, such business tax registration shall be shown to the city clerk or this officer's deputies or to any police officer of said Lilburn, Georgia, upon request.
(b)
An occupation tax registration shall be issued by the licensing and revenue manager upon receipt of the application, payment of all occupation taxes, and the determination that the provisions of this chapter have been satisfied. The licensing and revenue manager shall not issue a registration to any business engaged in an unlawful activity or operated in such a manner as to violate lawful ordinances adopted by the mayor and council. Unlawful activity shall include, but not be limited to, activities in violation of the zoning regulations and heating, electrical, health and building codes. If an applicant is denied a registration, the applicant may file a written appeal from the denial to the mayor and council. The occupation tax registration shall be displayed in a conspicuous place in the taxpayer's place of business, if the taxpayer has a permanent business location in Lilburn, Georgia. If the taxpayer has no permanent business location in Lilburn, Georgia, such occupation tax registration shall be shown to the licensing and revenue manager or this officer's deputies or to any police officer of said Lilburn, Georgia, upon request.
(Code 2001, § 3-4-1; Ord. of 7-10-1995; Ord. of 2-12-1996; Ord. No. 458-13, 6-10-2013)
State Law reference— Authority to levy occupation tax, O.C.G.A. §§ 48-13-6, 48-13-7.
Sec. 14-26. - Administrative and regulatory fee structure; occupation tax structure.
(a)
A non-prorated, nonrefundable administrative fee as approved by the mayor and council shall be required on all occupation tax accounts for the initial start-up, renewal, or reopening of those accounts. (The administrative fee can only approximate the reasonable cost of handling and processing the occupation tax, return, or application).
(b)
A regulatory fee will be imposed as provided under O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9 on those applicable businesses. (For examples of the types of businesses that a municipality can and cannot regulate, see O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9(b) and (c)). A regulatory fee may not include an administrative fee.
(c)
The regulatory fee schedule for persons in the taxicab occupation and profession is $100.00.
(Code 2001, § 3-4-3; Ord. of 2-10-2003; Ord. No. 458-13, 6-10-2013)
State Law reference— Administrative fee authorized, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-10; regulatory fees, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-8 et seq.
