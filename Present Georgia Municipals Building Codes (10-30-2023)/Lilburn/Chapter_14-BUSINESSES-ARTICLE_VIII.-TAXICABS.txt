ARTICLE VIII. - TAXICABS

Sec. 14-231. - Compliance with article; scope of provisions.
It shall be unlawful for any person, not operating under the rules and regulations of the public service commission, to transport any person from any place within the city to any other place either within or without the city in a motor vehicle and to charge such person being so transported anything of value for such transportation without first having complied with the provisions of this article.
(Code 1972, § 16-1; Code 2001, § 9-3-1)
Sec. 14-232. - Operator's license—Application.
Before engaging in any business described in this article the person responsible for such business shall apply to the mayor or his designee for a license to carry on the business, submitting with the application a schedule of rates to be charged, a schedule of the hours the motor vehicle will be operated, the type of motor vehicle and its passenger capacity.
(Code 1972, § 16-2; Code 2001, § 9-3-2)
Sec. 14-233. - Same—Authority of council to pass on qualifications.
The application required by this article shall be submitted to the council for its approval or disapproval. The council shall pass upon the moral and financial qualifications of the applicant.
(Code 2001, § 9-3-3)
Sec. 14-234. - Same—Operator's office.
No license to carry on any business regulated by the provisions of this chapter shall be granted to any person who does not maintain an office and place of business in the city. The hours that such office and place of business are to be kept open shall be approved by the mayor or his designee.
(Code 1972, § 16-3; Code 2001, § 9-3-4)
Sec. 14-235. - Same—Fee.
The license fee for the operation of any business regulated by this chapter shall be fixed for such business from time to time by resolution.
(Code 1972, § 16-5; Code 2001, § 9-3-5)
Sec. 14-236. - Same—Revocation by council.
In the event any business governed by this chapter or any vehicle used and operated in conducting such business is being conducted or operated in violation of any of the provisions of this Code or other ordinances of the city or the laws of the state, or is being operated for an illegal or immoral purpose, the mayor and council may revoke the license granted to the person operating such business.
(Code 1972, § 16-6; Code 2001, § 9-3-6)
Sec. 14-237. - Driver's permit—Required.
No person shall operate a cab, taxicab, livery or sightseeing car within the city unless such driver has been issued a permit by the chief of police.
(Code 1972, § 16-12; Code 2001, § 9-3-7)
Sec. 14-238. - Same—Application.
Before any person shall drive or operate any vehicle governed by this chapter he shall make written application to the chief of police for a driver's permit upon blanks furnished by the police department. Such application shall show the age and home address of the applicant, shall state whether he is of sound physical condition, whether he has good eyesight, whether he uses intoxicating liquors, drugs or narcotics, whether he has ever been convicted of the violation of any city, state or federal criminal law and, if so, the number of times and the kind of offense, and shall furnish such other information as may be required.
(Code 1972, § 16-12; Code 2001, § 9-3-8)
Sec. 14-239. - Same—Issuance; qualifications of drivers.
The chief of police is hereby charged with the duty of investigating the facts stated in the application required by the preceding section before granting a driver's permit, and he shall deny a permit to any person who is not 21 years of age and, in the opinion of the chief of police, not physically, mentally and morally fit to operate a taxicab upon the streets and highways of the city. Any person denied a permit on the basis of unfitness may appeal that denial to the mayor and council within ten days of the chief's decision by filing written notice of appeal with the city clerk.
(Code 2001, § 9-3-9)
Sec. 14-240. - Same—Permit card; fee.
If the chief of police grants a permit to an applicant pursuant to the provisions of this article, the city clerk shall issue the applicant a permit card to which a photograph of the driver shall be affixed and which shall be kept in view of the passengers at all times when such person is operating a taxicab or other vehicle. No person shall operate any such vehicle upon the streets of the city unless the driver has posted such permit card in plain view of the passengers of the vehicle he is driving. Upon the issuance of the permit, the city clerk shall collect from the applicant such fee as fixed from time to time by the mayor and council.
(Code 1972, § 16-9; Code 2001, § 9-3-10)
Sec. 14-241. - Same—Revocation by judge of municipal court.
Upon proper showing by the police or by the records of other courts, the judge of the municipal court may revoke any driver's permit issued pursuant to the provisions of this article for such period as he may deem proper.
(Code 1972, § 16-10; Code 2001, § 9-3-11)
Sec. 14-242. - Liability insurance policy.
(a)
No person shall operate a taxicab or automobile for hire in the city under the terms of this chapter unless that person has in force a policy of liability insurance issued by some company licensed to do business in the state, for the protection of the passengers carried, and of the public, against personal injury and death and property damage caused by the negligence of such person, his servants, agents or employees. Such policy of insurance shall be for the benefit of and subject to suit or action thereon by any person who shall sustain actionable injury or loss protected thereby, and shall provide in respect to each vehicle so operated, indemnity in such sums as required by the state financial responsibility laws.
(b)
On or before January 1 of each year, persons operating taxicabs or automobiles for hire in the city shall file with the city clerk evidence that insurance as required by O.C.G.A. § 33-34-4 is in full force and effect.
(c)
Before any new taxicab or automobile for hire may be operated in the city subsequent to that date, or before any vehicle may be substituted for any taxicab or automobile for hire formerly in operation, satisfactory evidence must be presented to the mayor to show that such new or substitute vehicle is covered by such insurance.
(Code 1972, § 16-11; Code 2001, § 9-3-12)
Sec. 14-243. - Motor vehicles—Mechanical condition and inspection.
Each vehicle shall be subject to periodical inspection by the chief of police or his representative. If, upon inspection, any such motor vehicle is found unfit for use by the chief of police or his representative, then that vehicle shall not be used until it is put in proper mechanical condition and approved by such officer or his representative for further use.
(Code 1972, § 16-14; Code 2001, § 9-3-13)
Sec. 14-244. - Same—Maximum number of passengers permitted.
The number of persons to be transported in any motor vehicle regulated by the provisions of this article shall be plainly printed on each side thereof, along with the name of the company operating the motor vehicle.
(Code 1972, § 16-15; Code 2001, § 9-3-15)
Sec. 14-245. - Same—Identification.
(a)
Every motor vehicle used in conducting any business governed by the provisions of this chapter shall have plainly printed on each side thereof the name of the company operating the vehicle for hire.
(b)
Every motor vehicle used in conducting any such business shall have printed on the rear thereof an identification number not less than five inches in height and so placed so as to be plainly visible at all times.
(Code 1972, § 16-16; Code 2001, § 9-3-16)
Sec. 14-246. - Same—Schedule of rates to be posted.
Every motor vehicle used in conducting any business regulated by this article shall have posted therein in a place plainly visible to passengers, a schedule of rates as approved by the mayor and council, the size, form and substance of such posted schedule to be approved by the city manager or designee. Schedules shall be approved that clearly communicate the schedule of rates.
(Code 1972, § 16-17; Code 2001, § 9-3-17)
Sec. 14-247. - Penalty for violation of chapter.
Any person convicted of a violation of this chapter shall be punished as provided in section 1-8.
(Code 1972, § 16-18; Code 2001, § 9-3-18)
Secs. 14-248—14-274. - Reserved.
