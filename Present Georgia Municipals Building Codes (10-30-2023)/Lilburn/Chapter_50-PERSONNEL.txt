Sec. 50-21. - Title.
This article may be cited as the "City of Lilburn Merit System Act."
( Ord. No. 2021-561 , 1-11-2021)
Sec. 50-22. - Definitions.
The following terms when used in this article shall have the following meanings unless the context clearly requires otherwise:
Appointing authority means the officer, commission, board or body having the power of appointment, employment or removal from positions in any office, department, commission, board or institution; or any person or group of persons having the power by virtue of the Constitution, ordinance, statute, or lawfully delegated authority to make appointments or employments to positions of employment in Lilburn, Georgia.
Board means the merit system board of Lilburn, Georgia.
Classified employee means any employee holding a position in the classified service as defined in this article.
Classified service means all appointed offices and positions of trust or employment in the service of Lilburn, Georgia, except those placed in the unclassified service by this article.
Executive secretary means the executive secretary of the merit system board of Lilburn, Georgia.
Position means any office of employment in the service of Lilburn, Georgia.
Public hearing means the opportunity, given after public notice of at least five days, for any person to appear and be heard on the matter involved, at a hearing open to the public.
( Ord. No. 2021-561 , 1-11-2021)
Sec. 50-23. - Merit system board; creation; qualification of members; term of appointment; removal.
(a)
Creation of board. There is hereby created the City of Lilburn merit system board which shall consist of five qualified members appointed by the mayor and council.
(b)
Selection of board members. The Mayor and each council member shall select one candidate for appointment to the board, for a total of five members.
(c)
Qualifications of board members. Neither the governing authority nor the employee shall appoint to the board as a member thereof any person who:
(1)
Has not been a resident of the city for one or more year's next preceding appointment or during the term of appointment.
(2)
Holds an elective or appointive office in federal, state, county or municipal government, provided that prior appointment as a member of the board shall not disqualify a person from being reappointed hereto; or
(3)
Shall have held political office, is or shall have been a salaried employee of the city during the 12 months next preceding his or her appointment to the board.
(d)
Terms of appointment, vacancies. The council shall appoint the members of the board. The length of the terms of the board shall be one year. A vacancy in the membership of the board caused by a member's death, resignation, disqualification, or other conditions shall be filled by appointment of the governing authority for the unexpired term of such member. The council member who shall have chosen the member who is retiring shall have the right to select the replacement for the vacancy.
(e)
Chairperson and vice chairperson. At its initial meeting and annually thereafter, the board shall elect one member chairperson and another vice chairperson.
(f)
Removal of board members. No member of the board may be removed from office prior to the expiration of his term except for cause after having been granted a notice and afforded a public and open hearing before the mayor and council. Prior to the hearing, the member shall be served personally or by registered or certified mail addressed to his residence as shown in the files of the mayor and council, at least ten days before the date set for hearing, with written specifications of the charges against him.
( Ord. No. 2021-561 , 1-11-2021)
Sec. 50-24. - Meetings of the board.
The board shall hold its meetings at the city hall as frequently as necessary in order to fulfill the provisions of this article. Three members shall constitute a quorum for the conduct of business and official action of the board shall require three affirmative votes. All of said meetings shall be conducted in compliance with the Georgia Open Meetings Law, O.C.G.A. Ch. 50-14. Ten days written notice thereof shall be given to each member by the executive secretary of the board, who shall, under the direction of the board, also keep minutes of each meeting.
(Code 2001, 2-5-4; Ord. of 2-13-1995)
( Ord. No. 2021-561 , 1-11-2021)
Sec. 50-25. - Duties of the board.
It shall be the duty, function and responsibility of the board to represent the interest of the public in the improvement of personnel administration. It shall be the duty of the board:
(1)
After public hearing, to submit to the city manager for presentation to the mayor and council the board's recommendations regarding additions, deletions or amendments to the personnel policies and procedures thereof, for the administration of this article;
(2)
To hear and determine appeals of adverse actions for classified employees;
(3)
To recommend rules and regulations to the governing authority for honorably discharged veterans of any war in conformity with the constitution of the State of Georgia providing that equal preference be accorded such veterans as exist under federal civil service laws.
( Ord. No. 2021-561 , 1-11-2021)
Sec. 50-26. - Powers of the board.
The board, each member of the board, and the executive secretary each shall have the power to administer oaths, subpoena witnesses, and compel the production of documents, including, but not limited to, documents and correspondence, audio visual tapes, and computer generated information relevant to any hearing authorized by this article. Any person who shall fail to appear in response to a subpoena or to answer any questions or produce any such documents pertinent to any such hearing shall be subject to sanctions in a court of law.
( Ord. No. 2021-561 , 1-11-2021)
Sec. 50-27. - Executive secretary—Appointment; removal.
(a)
The mayor and council shall appoint the executive secretary. Such executive secretary shall be a person competent in the field of public personnel administration and thoroughly in sympathy with the application of the merit system.
(b)
The person appointed as executive secretary may hold this position while performing other unrelated duties as a city employee and may be removed by the mayor and council from the position as executive secretary at any time without cause when a change in this position is deemed appropriate by the mayor and council. The mayor and council's decision to remove a person from this position shall be final.
( Ord. No. 2021-561 , 1-11-2021)
Sec. 50-28. - Same—Duties.
It shall be the duty of the executive secretary to:
(1)
Attend meetings of the board, act as its secretary, and record its official actions. Keep records of the minutes of merit board meetings and any other records necessary for the proper administration of this article.
(2)
Secure the attendance of witnesses and production of books, papers, public records, correspondence, audio/visual tapes, computer generated information, and other documentary evidence pertinent to any hearings authorized by this article.
(3)
Prepare an annual report of the activities of the merit system board to include; number of meetings or hearings conducted, the date of such meetings or hearings, the purpose of the meetings or hearings as well as any actions or recommendations issued by the merit board and presented to the mayor and council for consideration and approval. The annual report shall be forwarded to the city manager for presentation to the mayor and council.
(4)
Recommend any additions, deletions or revisions to the personnel policies and procedures and forward same to the city manager for presentation to the mayor and council.
(5)
After the mayor and council's approval thereof, to publish the content of such rules and their modifications for public distribution and to give immediate notice thereof to all appointing authorities affected thereby. Thereafter all appointing authorities and classified and other employees affected by these rules shall assist in all proper ways in carrying them into effect.
(6)
Be responsible to the mayor and council and to the board for those duties prescribed in this article.
(7)
Perform any other lawful acts required to effectuate the purpose of this article.
( Ord. No. 2021-561 , 1-11-2021)
Sec. 50-29. - Unclassified service.
The classified service does not include the following members of the unclassified service:
(1)
Officers elected by the people and persons appointed to fill vacancies to such elective offices;
(2)
Officers and employees specifically exempted by law, and including the city manager, assistant city manager, chief of police, planning director, the position of captain within the police department, and all current employees who are designated as department heads. Effective January 11, 2021, all vacant positions within the City of Lilburn will be filled as unclassified positions.
(3)
Members of boards or special commissions appointed by the mayor and council for special purposes to service with or without compensation;
(4)
City attorney; judge of the municipal court; and solicitor (prosecuting attorney);
(5)
Persons temporarily appointed or designated to make or conduct a special inquiry, investigation, or examination where such appointment or designation is certified by the board to be for employment which should not be performed in the classified service;
(6)
Members of the board of registrars;
(7)
Volunteer personnel;
(8)
Emergency employees;
(9)
Part-time employees working less than 20 hours per week or as designated in the personnel policies and procedures.
( Ord. No. 2021-561 , 1-11-2021)
Sec. 50-30. - Penalties.
Any person in the city who willfully violates, conspires with or solicits another to violate any of the provisions of this article shall be guilty of an offense and shall, on conviction, be punished as prescribed in chapter 1 of this Code, and if such convicted person is in the classified service, he shall be dismissed there from and shall not be eligible for re-employment, reappointment, or reinstatement for a period of five years from the date of such conviction.
( Ord. No. 2021-561 , 1-11-2021)
Sec. 50-31. - Oaths.
The board, the executive secretary and employees shall take the oath of office as prescribed by the mayor and council. The executive secretary shall take his or her oath before the chairperson of the board.
( Ord. No. 2021-561 , 1-11-2021)
Sec. 50-32. - Severability.
Should any section or provision of this article be held to be unconstitutional or invalid such section or provision shall not affect the validity of this article as a whole or any part thereof other than the part so held to be unconstitutional.
( Ord. No. 2021-561 , 1-11-2021)
Sec. 50-33. - General repeal.
All laws and parts of laws in conflict with this article are hereby repealed.
( Ord. No. 2021-561 , 1-11-2021)
Sec. 50-34. - Effective date.
This article shall take effect when a resolution is adopted by the governing authority.
( Ord. No. 2021-561 , 1-11-2021)

Sec. 50-68. - Policy of city.
It is hereby declared to be the policy and purpose of the city to extend at the earliest date possible under the agreement entered into by the State of Georgia and the federal social security administration, to the employees and officials thereof, and its instrumentalities, not excluded by law or by this article, the benefits of the system of old age and survivors' insurance as authorized by the federal social security act, the state enabling act, and amendments thereto. In pursuance of that policy, and for that purpose, the city shall take such action as may be required by applicable state or federal laws or regulations.
(Code 2001, § 2-5-31; Ord. of 6-18-1973)
Sec. 50-69. - Agreements authorized.
The mayor, or other chief executive officer of the city, is hereby authorized and directed to execute all necessary agreements and amendments thereto with the Employees' Retirement System of Georgia for coverage of the employees and officials as provided for in this chapter, to include any employees that are in positions covered by a public retirement system, in the manner by law provided, namely, police officers.
(Code 2001, § 2-5-32; Ord. of 6-18-1973)
Sec. 50-70. - Withholdings from salaries.
Withholdings from salaries or wages of employees and officials for the purpose provided for in this chapter hereof are hereby authorized to be made in the amounts and at such times as may be required by applicable state or federal laws or regulations, and shall be paid over to the state agency.
(Code 2001, § 2-5-33; Ord. of 6-18-1973)
Sec. 50-71. - Appropriations by city.
There shall be appropriated from general funds such amounts at such times as may be required by applicable state or federal laws or regulations for employer's contributions, administrative expenses, and such funds shall be paid over to the employees' retirement system of Georgia in accordance with regulations established by that agency.
(Code 2001, § 2-5-34; Ord. of 6-18-1973)
Sec. 50-72. - Records and reports.
The city shall keep such records and make such reports as may be required by applicable state or federal laws or regulations.
(Code 2001, § 2-5-35; Ord. of 6-18-1973)
Sec. 50-73. - Excluded persons.
There is hereby excluded from this article any authority to make any agreement with respect to any position, or any employee or official not authorized to be covered by applicable state or federal laws or regulations.
(Code 2001, § 2-5-36; Ord. of 6-18-1973)
