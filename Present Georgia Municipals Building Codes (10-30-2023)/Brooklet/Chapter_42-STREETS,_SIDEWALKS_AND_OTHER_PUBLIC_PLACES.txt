Chapter 42 - STREETS, SIDEWALKS AND OTHER PUBLIC PLACES
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 42-1. - Obstructing streets, sidewalks.
(a)
Unlawful. No person shall place any lumber or timber, or leave any vehicle, barrels or boxes or any other obstruction in or on any of the streets or sidewalks of the city, so that passage thereon is impeded.
(b)
Necessary obstructions; regulations.
(1)
Permit required; scope. Whenever it shall become absolutely necessary for any one to obstruct said streets or sidewalks, or any part thereof, permission shall first be obtained from the mayor who shall under no circumstances grant a permit to obstruct more than one-half the width and not over 100 feet of length of said street. If the obstruction desired is a sidewalk, the mayor shall, if he thinks the circumstances require it, grant a permit to obstruct all of said sidewalk in width, but not more than 100 feet in length.
(2)
Barricade and lights required. Each person to whom said permit is granted shall keep at each end of said obstruction from sundown to sunup, a proper barricade and a lighted lantern or lanterns, the light therefrom to be sufficient to make the obstruction plainly visible.
(Code 1986, §§ 13-1, 13-2)
Sec. 42-2. - Fences, ditches on line of street, sidewalk.
No fence or ditch shall be erected or dug on the line of any street or sidewalk in the city without obtaining a permit from the mayor.
(Code 1986, § 13-3)
Sec. 42-3. - Erection of telephone or telegraph poles; permit required.
No one shall erect any telephone or telegraph poles on the streets or sidewalks of the city without first obtaining a permit from the mayor.
(Code 1986, § 13-4)
Sec. 42-4. - Removal of utility poles required upon notice.
(a)
Authority. It shall be the duty of any electric light company, telephone or telegraph company, or any other public utility now existing under franchise from the city or otherwise, or hereafter authorized to set poles for the stringing of wires thereon, upon notice from the mayor and council to that effect, to remove or reset any poles belonging to such company, its successors or assigns, when such removal or resetting of poles is necessary in the judgment of the mayor and council, to facilitate the improvement of any street or portion of street by changing the grade thereof or otherwise, or to allow the improvement or promote the convenience of any lot on such street, the expense of the removal to be borne by the company using the poles.
(b)
Failure to comply. Upon failure or refusal to remove or reset such poles upon ten days' notice from the mayor and council the city will enter upon the property aforesaid and remove or reset such poles, charging the expense of same to the company owning or controlling the same, the said expenses added to the business license of said company for the next ensuing year, and no license to do business in the city shall be issued to said company until the expense of removing or resetting said pole has been paid.
(Code 1986, § 13-5)
Sec. 42-5. - Excavations for water and sewer connections, other purposes.
(a)
Approval required. No person shall excavate any street of the city for the purpose of laying sewer or water pipes or for any other purpose unless such person shall have first obtained the approval therefor from the mayor and council, and under such regulations for such excavation and laying of said pipes and the making of such connections as are now in existence or may hereafter be provided by the mayor and council of the city.
(b)
Pavement to be restored. Wherever a connection is made and the street is excavated as provided herein, if the street is to be paved at the time of such excavation, the person at whose instance the connection was made shall replace the street paving and sidewalk along and through the connection to as good a condition as it was before the work was commenced and shall pay to the city the cost of such work if the work is done by employees of the city.
(c)
Bond required. As a prerequisite to such excavation and connections to sewers and water pipes and before the person performing such work shall obtain such permission he shall execute a bond with good security to be approved by the clerk of council, conditioned to pay the said mayor and council any damages it may sustain by reason of the excavation and the making of said connections.
(Code 1986, § 13-6)
Sec. 42-6. - Areas adjacent to railway tracks to be maintained in good condition.
(a)
Duty. All railway companies, corporations, individuals, or company of individuals, who now have or may hereafter have, own, maintain, use or operate by lease or otherwise, any railway track in the city, shall be and are hereby required to keep all streets, lanes, alleys, sidewalks, etc., where said track adjoins or crosses said street, lanes, alleys or sidewalks in a good and safe condition for travel.
(b)
Grade of grade crossing. All grade crossings where a railway track crosses a street shall not be more than ten percent grade.
(c)
Notice to correct condition. The city shall give railway companies, or other parties above named, 15 days' notice to put any place in the condition above required and if said parties shall fail or refuse to fix same, then the city shall fix same and issue execution instanter against said parties for the amount expended.
(Code 1986, § 13-7)
Sec. 42-7. - Display of merchandise on streets and sidewalks; special permission required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to place and display his goods, wares, or merchandise on the streets and sidewalks of the city, or offer them for sale thereon, without special permission to do so granted by the mayor or mayor pro tem of the city.
(Code 1986, § 13-8)
Sec. 42-8. - Installation of sidewalks, curbs, and gutters; publication; protest against improvements; pro rata assessment of cost of improvements against abutting property.
(a)
Whenever the mayor and council shall deem it necessary to pave any street or part thereof or to install or improve sidewalks, curbs, and gutters within the limits of the city, the mayor and council shall, by resolution, declare such improvements necessary to be done, and shall publish such resolution once a week for two consecutive weeks in a newspaper having general circulation in the city and published in the county. If the owner of a majority of the linear front footage of the lands abutting on said proposed improvements shall not within 15 days after the last day of publication of such resolution, file with the clerk of the city council their protest in writing against such improvements, then the mayor and council shall have power to cause the improvements to be made, and to contract therefor, and to make assessment and to fix liens as provided for herein. Any number of streets or any parts thereof proposed to be improved may be included in one resolution. Any protest or objection shall be made as to each street separately.
(b)
Each lot or parcel of land abutting such improvements shall be charged on the basis of linear front footage at an equal rate per foot of such frontage with its just pro rata of the entire cost of the said improvements, less any amounts paid by railroads or others, and less any amounts received from the federal or state governments as federal aid or state aid road funds for such improvements, and less any amounts appropriated from the general receipts of the city for such improvements.
(Code 1986, § 13-9)
Sec. 42-9. - Street numbers.
(a)
Posting required. The owner or owners of fee simple title to or a life estate or estate for years in any real estate upon which there is situated a residence, business or other building or structure to which there is assigned a street number by the city, shall post the street number of such business, residence or other building or structure in one of the following manners:
(1)
By affixing the street number to the front of the residence, business, building or other structure in a prominent manner such that the same can be easily viewed from the street or road upon which such residence, business, building or other structure fronts;
(2)
By incorporating the street number into or onto a sign located on the property, subject to any sign regulations that the city may have in place, in such a manner that the same can be easily viewed from the road upon which such residence, business, building or other structure fronts; or
(3)
By affixing the street number to a mailbox, fence or other appurtenance associated with the residence, business, building or other structure in a prominent manner such that the same can be easily viewed from the street or road upon which such residence, business, building or other structure fronts.
(b)
Assignment of street numbers by city. The city has established an address numbering system, and shall utilize such system, or may establish a new numbering system, for assigning numbers to businesses, residences or other buildings or structures within the city. Any structure with a continuous roofline, which has power connected and/or can reasonably be occupied, shall be assigned a separate number. A number or alphabetical letter shall be assigned for each separate and distinct occupant or division (i.e., apartment, company, etc.) within a building or other occupied structure. No temporary or aesthetic addresses will be assigned for any structures or bare properties within the city. Existing numbers shall be changed only as necessary to ensure the effectiveness and efficiency of the 911 system for Bulloch County, as determined in the judgment of the appropriate city official or officials. Appropriate notification of the address assigned under the terms of this section to each residence, business, building or other structure shall be provided to the appropriate post office of the United States Postal Service.
(c)
Size.
(1)
Residential properties. The size of street numbers posted on property zoned residential shall be a minimum of three inches in height, and shall not exceed four inches in height.
(2)
Non-residential properties. The size of street numbers posted on property zoned anything other than residential shall be a minimum of four inches in height, and shall not exceed one foot in height.
(d)
Variances. Any person desiring a variance from the size requirements for street numbers may make application for such variance to the Brooklet City Council by making such request in writing, and further requesting that such matter be placed on the agenda of a subsequent city council meeting.
(e)
Existing structures. The Mayor and Council of the City of Brooklet shall encourage the owner or owners of the fee simple title to or a life estate or estate for years in any real estate upon which there is situated a residence, business, or other building or structure, to the extent already not properly posted in accordance with this section, to post the assigned numbers in accordance herewith within 60 days from the effective date of the ordinance from which this section was derived. It shall be unlawful for any number to be posted other than the number assigned by the city. The cost and installation of the number shall be paid for by the owner or owners of the fee simple title to or a life estate or estate for years in any such applicable residence, business or other building or structure to which there is assigned a street number by the city.
(f)
Building permits. No building permit shall be issued for any residence, business or other building or structure to be erected, repaired, altered or modified in the city after the effective date of the ordinance from which this section was derived until the owner or owners of the fee simple title to or a life estate or estate for years in the real estate upon which there is situated a residence, business or other building or structure to which is assigned a street number by the city until the applicable person has obtained the assigned number from the city, and properly posted such number in accordance with this section.
(Ord. No. 2018-3, 9-20-2018)

ARTICLE II. - SPECIAL EVENTS

Sec. 42-21. - Definitions.
The following definitions shall apply to the interpretation and enforcement of this article:
Chief of police means the chief of police of the City of Brooklet, Georgia, or his or her designee for the administration of this article.
Impacts government services on public rights-of-way means where a reasonable person would reasonably expect a need for the provision of municipal services to a degree significantly over and above that which the city routinely provides under ordinary every day circumstances.
Producer or host means any person, group of persons, organization, association, club, or other entity responsible for planning, producing and conducting an event.
Special event or event means any organized activity having as its purpose entertainment, recreation and/or education, such as a festival or celebration, foot or vehicle race, parade or march, rally or assembly which takes place on a public street, sidewalk or right-of-way, or occurs on private property and impacts government services on public rights-of-way.
Vendor means any person or persons who engage in the sale to the public of any food or food products, goods, services, or merchandise of whatever nature from any location, either mobile or stationary, on a temporary itinerant basis on any public street, sidewalk, or right-of-way. The definition of vendor, as used in this article, shall not be construed to include or prohibit a permanent business, operating under the provisions of the occupation tax ordinance and other applicable laws, from displaying goods or merchandise on a public sidewalk immediately in front of and adjacent to the permanent business structure so long as such displays do not impede the normal flow of pedestrian traffic and so long as other laws and ordinances pertaining to such business or displays are complied with.
(Ord. of 2-25-2015, § 1)
Sec. 42-22. - Permits.
(a)
Required. It shall be unlawful for any person, group, organization, association, club or other entity to conduct or cause to be conducted any special event in the City of Brooklet without having obtained a valid permit for such event.
(b)
Exemptions. The following special events are exempt from the provisions of this article:
(1)
Special events where the attendance that is reasonably expected is less than 75 people;
(2)
Special events occurring on private property used and occupied as a private residence, which special event is hosted by at least one of the occupants of such private residence, regardless of the number of attendees;
(3)
Special events occurring upon a city or county-owned sports facility, including without limitation, a ball field, tennis court or pool, provided that the special event constitutes a use for which the sports facility was intended, and regardless of the number of attendees;
(4)
Special events hosted by a church on property owned by the church, but only if the property is used on a regular basis, at least bi-monthly, to conduct worship services, and regardless of the number of attendees;
(5)
Events hosted by a school on property owned by the school or a governmental entity, provided that the property is used on a regular basis, at least weekly, to conduct classes;
(6)
Funeral processions conducted under the supervision and direction of a funeral director in conjunction with any funeral;
(7)
A governmental agency acting within the scope of its agency.
(c)
Geographic limitation; responsibility for security and sanitation service. A special event shall be limited to the specific geographic area(s) within which the event is to take place and the producer of a special event shall be responsible for the provision of private security and sanitation services within the boundaries of the event during the event and for a period of one hour past the conclusion of the event or until the area is cleared of all activities and structures related to such event. The chief of police shall determine the amount of private security reasonably required for the event that is to be provided by the producer, if any.
(d)
Time limitation. Except for clearing of the area, no special event shall be allowed to continue beyond the time approved on the permit for the event.
(e)
Delineation of boundaries; preservation of areas for circulation. The outermost boundaries of the special event shall be fully and clearly delineated on a map, which shall be no smaller than 8½ x 11 inches in size, and attached to the application for a permit. All public streets and/or sidewalks within and adjacent to such area(s) shall be clearly identified. The chief of police, or designee, shall require, as part of the approval for the event, that areas no less than five feet in width shall be designated and preserved for public circulation and access to adjoining business establishments outside the area(s) delineated for the event.
(f)
Maps for walks or runs. The producer of a special event such as a walk or run, as to which the provisions of subsection (d) above would not be practicable, shall submit with the application for a permit a map, which shall be no smaller than 8½ x 11 inches in size, showing the exact route of the course for the walk or run.
(Ord. of 2-25-2015, § 2)
Sec. 42-23. - Application; fee.
(a)
A producer of a special event shall make application for a permit for such event at city hall on a form prescribed by the chief of police. Application forms may be obtained from the police department.
(b)
An application for a special event should be filed at least 30 days prior to the date the event is scheduled to occur. However, no application shall be accepted earlier than one year prior to the date of the event.
(c)
A nonrefundable application fee of $10.00 shall be paid at the time the application for a permit is filed. Such fee shall be payable to the City of Brooklet by cash, money order, or cashier's check.
(Ord. of 2-25-2015, § 3)
Sec. 42-24. - Identification of producer.
The producer of an event, if a group or organization, shall be fully identified on the application. However, a special event permit shall be issued only to an individual person, who may be the actual producer or a designated agent of the producer, and such individual person shall be solely and fully responsible for compliance with all provisions, including all financial requirements, of this chapter and other applicable laws.
(Ord. of 2-25-2015, § 4)
Sec. 42-25. - Administrative review.
The chief of police shall cause the application to be circulated to each government department and/or other agency whose services the chief of police determines would be affected by the nature and activities of the proposed event. Each such department or agency shall review the application and note the services which it will be required to perform, the number of personnel to perform such services, the length of time to perform such services, the cost to perform such services, and any other information which would assist the chief of police in facilitating the event and services required.
(Ord. of 2-25-2015, § 5)
Sec. 42-26. - Special conditions on permit.
Each department of the government reviewing an application may recommend in writing certain conditions or restrictions as deemed necessary to facilitate the event, to comply with other laws or regulations, and/or to the safety, health and welfare of the community. In reviewing the recommendations of the departments, the chief of police may impose such conditions or restrictions as a part of the permit and such special conditions so imposed shall be construed to have the full force and effect of law as a provisions of this article. A violation of such conditions or restrictions shall be deemed a violation of this article.
(Ord. of 2-25-2015, § 6)
Sec. 42-27. - Final approval of permit; minimum review.
(a)
The chief of police is authorized to approve and issue a permit for a special event after considering all comments and conditions of the reviewing departments and agencies as well as other information pertaining to the proposed event as described on the application, and after the payment of all applicable fees and costs.
(b)
A minimum of ten working days shall be allowed for the administrative review of an application and the issuance of a permit.
(c)
The chief of police shall either issue or deny the permit no later than 30 working days following the date the completed application is filed. If the permit is denied, the producer shall be notified in writing of the denial and the reason(s) for the denial.
(Ord. of 2-25-2015, § 7)
Sec. 42-28. - Emergency issuance of permit.
In extraordinary circumstances, the chief of police, shall be authorized to waive or otherwise expedite any or all of the review process and to issue a special event permit upon payment by the producer of all applicable fees and costs for such event.
(Ord. of 2-25-2015, § 8)
Sec. 42-29. - Permit fees.
The fee for a permit to conduct a special event shall be based on all services to be provided by the government for such event and shall be equal to the estimated actual cost to the government to provide such services. The initial permit fee shall be the aggregate of the estimated costs of such services calculated by each government department. If, at the conclusion of the event, the cost of government services is greater than the initial payment, the producer of the event shall be billed for the difference. Failure to pay the outstanding amount within 30 days of the billing date shall be a violation of this article.
(Ord. of 2-25-2015, § 9)
Sec. 42-30. - Liability.
The producer of any special event shall hold harmless the City of Brooklet, its officers, employees and agents from liability of damages arising from any acts or omissions emanating from a special event. Proof of such waiver shall be filed with, and made a part of, the application form. The producer shall be responsible for providing any and all other insurance that may be necessary for any special event. The amount and kind of insurance required shall be determined by the chief of police, or his designee, after considering the nature of the special event, and the potential for harm and the degree of harm to persons and property. The producer shall provide proof to the chief of police, or his designee, that such insurance is in effect prior to the issuance of a permit.
(Ord. of 2-25-2015, § 10)
Sec. 42-31. - Vendors.
(a)
Sales permitted. The sale of food, or any merchandise or services of any type by a vendor shall be allowed as a component of a special event provided such vendor is approved and authorized in writing by the producer of the event and shall be conducted in accordance with such conditions and limitations as shall be imposed in writing by the producer and submitted as part of the application for a permit.
(b)
Authorization of vendors. The producer of a special event shall have sole responsibility and authority to allow or disallow sidewalk or street vending as a component of an event. Such identification shall be not less than three inches, shall state that the bearer is an official participant in the event, and shall bear the signature of the producer of the event.
(c)
Identification required. Any vendor authorized by the event producer shall be required to prominently display on his or her person a badge identifying the vendor as an authorized participant in the event. Such identification shall be not less than three inches by three inches, shall state that the bearer is an official participant in the event, and shall bear the signature of the producer of the event.
(d)
Permit not required. Vendors authorized by the producer of an event as a component of the event shall not be required to obtain any separate vendors permit to operate during the period of the event. However, all other vendors, of whatever nature, not authorized as a participant in the special event shall obtain the appropriate licenses and permits as required under other applicable ordinances.
(e)
Unauthorized vending prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any vendor not authorized by the producer of a special event as provided under this section to engage in such business within a distance of 25 yards of such event from one hour before until one hour after the event.
(f)
Food sales. The vendor of any food, whether hot or cold, as a part of a special event shall be subject to all rules and regulations of the county health department. It shall be the responsibility of the producer of an event to assure compliance with this section by any such vendors.
(Ord. of 2-25-2015, § 11)
Sec. 42-32. - Glass containers prohibited.
The use of glass containers within the area of any special event shall be prohibited.
(Ord. of 2-25-2015, § 12)
Sec. 42-33. - Police services.
Traffic and crowd control shall be provided by the City of Brooklet Police Department and/or the Bulloch County Sheriff's Department, and the number of officers and/or special arrangements shall be determined by the chief of police of the City of Brooklet.
(Ord. of 2-25-2015, § 13)
Sec. 42-34. - Toilet facilities.
The producer of a special event shall be required to provide temporary outdoor toilet facilities at any event at which 100 or more persons are expected to be in attendance. The number of toilets and their locations shall be determined by the city building inspector.
(Ord. of 2-25-2015, § 14)
Sec. 42-35. - Tents, stages, etc.
Tents, arbors, stages, grandstands or other temporary facilities constructed or erected as a part of a special event shall be approved by the city building inspector.
(Ord. of 2-25-2015, § 15)
Sec. 42-36. - Street closing.
The complete or partial closure of any street, sidewalk or other public way as a part of a special event shall be approved by the chief of police.
(Ord. of 2-25-2015, § 16)
Sec. 42-37. - Banners, signs, etc.
Temporary, noncommercial banners or signs may be permitted overhead across public streets for the purpose of designating "starting" and "finish" lines for a race. Small banners, pennants, signs or other decorations which do not interfere with vehicular or pedestrian traffic, and which do not damage or deface any tree or public structure, may be allowed as part of a special event. Approval of the chief of police shall be obtained by the event producer for any banners or signs permitted in this section. The producer of the event shall be responsible for the removal of such banners, pennants or decorations within 48 hours after the close of the event.
(Ord. of 2-25-2015, § 17)
Sec. 42-38. - Duration of permit.
No permit issued under this chapter shall be valid prior to or past the date of the special event for which it is issued.
(Ord. of 2-25-2015, § 18)
Sec. 42-39. - Cessation of activities.
The safety, health, welfare and good order of all citizens and the community shall be first and foremost and every special event shall be conducted in such manner. The chief of police may terminate any activity, whether a part of or the entire event, which does not meet this community standard. Upon notification of the event producer in person by the chief of police to cease any activity, it shall be unlawful for such producer and/or any participants to permit such activity to continue.
(Ord. of 2-25-2015, § 19)
Sec. 42-40. - Picketing and demonstrations, etc.
Any person, group or organization engaged in any picketing, demonstrations, assembly, gathering, procession or other activity protected by the U.S. Constitution shall be prohibited from blocking the ingress and egress of any public or private place. Any person, group or organization engaged in such activities is required to make application as set forth in section 42-23(a) and (b) of this article. However, any such person, group or organization shall be exempt from the permit requirement in section 42-22(a) of this article and the fees set forth in section 42-23(c) of this article.
(Ord. of 2-25-2015, § 20)

ARTICLE III. - VEHICLE WEIGHT LIMITATIONS AND LIMITATIONS ON LARGE TRUCKS

Sec. 46-50. - Vehicles having more than six wheels.
It shall be unlawful for any vehicle having more than six wheels (excluding buses) to utilize any road or street within the City of Brooklet, except for the following roads, streets or highways, which shall be designated by signs as truck routes:
(1)
Highway 80;
(2)
Brooklet-Denmark Road;
(3)
South Parker Avenue; and
(4)
North Parker Avenue.
Provided however, such prohibition shall not apply to any such vehicle in instances where a delivery or pickup is to be made at a location which is not on a designated truck route, and such vehicle does not exceed the weight limitations set forth in section 46-51 of this article. Any person driving or in charge or control of any such vehicle when upon roads or streets other than those designated as truck routes, shall be prepared to present for the inspection of police officers, the person's log book, weight slips, delivery slips or other written evidence of the person's destination and point of origin to justify the presence of the restricted vehicle on a road or street other than a designated truck route.
(Ord. of 10-21-2021(1))
Sec. 46-51. - Weight limitations.
No motor vehicle shall utilize any road or street within the City of Brooklet other than Highway 80 that exceeds the weight limitations prescribed by state law for county roads (presently, O.C.G.A. § 32-6-26(±) and (g)). At the time of the adoption of this article, a maximum total gross weight limit of a vehicle and load shall not exceed 56,000 pounds unless said vehicle is making a pickup or delivery on such road. Provided however, such weight limitations may be exceeded for any vehicle without first obtaining a state permit when the load on any single axle does not exceed 23,000 pounds, the load on any tandem axle does not exceed 46,000 pounds, and the maximum total gross weight limit of the vehicle and load does not exceed 80,000 pounds when:
(1)
Hauling forest products from the forest where cut to the first point of marketing or processing;
(2)
Hauling live poultry or cotton from a farm to a processing plant;
(3)
Hauling feed from a feed mill to a farm;
(4)
Hauling granite, either block or sawed, or any other naturally occurring raw ore or mineral for further processing, from the quarry or stockpile area to a processing plant located in the same or an adjoining county and construction aggregates hauled to any point, unless otherwise prohibited;
(5)
Hauling solid waste or recovered materials from points of generation to a solid waste handling facility or other processing facility;
(6)
Hauling concrete that is in a freshly mixed and unhardened state for delivery to a customer; or
(7)
Hauling poultry waste from the point of origin to a farm.
No lift axle may be used in computing the maximum total gross weight authorized for any vehicle or load under this section.
(Ord. of 10-21-2021(1))
