ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 22-1. - Office of health officer established.
The office of health officer is established for the city through the county health department.
(Code 1986, § 8-1)
Sec. 22-2. - Requirements and prohibitions.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Human excreta means the bowel and kidney discharges of human beings.
Sanitary privy means any privy which is so built, rebuilt, constructed or reconstructed as to conform with the plans and specifications promulgated and recommended by the department of public health of the state, which plans and specifications are hereinafter adopted in this section.
Sanitary water closets means any flush toilet properly connected with the city sanitary sewerage system, or a septic tank of approved construction.
Septic tank means an underground cavity with watertight walls into which flows the effluent of sanitary water closets and from which the effluent does not come to the surface of the ground.
(b)
Sewerage systems.
(1)
Every residence and building in which human beings reside, are employed or congregate, shall be required to have a sanitary method of human excreta disposal: namely, either a sanitary water closet connected with the city sewerage system, where sewer connection is available or in case such sewer connection is not available, either a septic tank of type approved by the department of public health of the state, or a sanitary privy.
(2)
It shall be unlawful to dispose of human excreta within the corporate limits of the city except in a sanitary water closet connected with the city sewerage system, where sewer connection is available; or in case such sewer connection is not available, in a septic tank of type approved by the department of public health of the state.
(3)
It shall be unlawful for any person owning or leasing any premises in the city to permit the disposal of human excreta on any property leased or rented by such person or by his agent, except in a sanitary water closet connected with the city sewerage system where sewer connection is available, in a septic tank of the type approved by the department of public health of the state.
(4)
No septic tank other than of type approved by the department of public health of the state shall be constructed or maintained within the corporate limits of the city.
(Code 1986, §§ 16-26, 16-27; Ord. of 11-12-1934)

ARTICLE II. - PUBLIC WATER SYSTEM

Sec. 22-20. - Use of public water system required for potable water.
The City of Brooklet's public water system shall be used as the sole source of potable water within the city except as provided for in sections 22-21, 22-22 and 22-23.
(Ord. of 7-19-2012)

(b)

(Ord. of 7-19-2012)
Sec. 22-22. - Private wells allowed where public system extension not economically feasible.
The City of Brooklet shall not be required to extend the public water system where the city reasonably determines that the anticipated revenues resulting from such extension shall not be sufficient to recover the costs associated with such extension over the initial five-year period of service. Under such circumstances, the owner(s) of the property may apply for a private well permit for potable water purposes as provided for in section 22-23.
(Ord. of 7-19-2012)
Sec. 22-23. - Permit for private well.
No private well shall be constructed within the City of Brooklet unless a permit is obtained from the city. The following shall govern the application for approval of a permit to drill a private well:
(1)
No private well shall be constructed to be used as a source of potable water unless the circumstances set forth in section 22-22 are applicable. Where such circumstances are applicable, any permit issued shall clearly provide that the well may be utilized as a source of potable water. Otherwise, the use of a private well as a source of potable water is prohibited except for wells grandfathered under section 22-21.
(2)
A building permit to drill a private well shall be applied for, and such permit shall be issued, upon the applicant's meeting all applicable building, electrical and plumbing codes, and any other requirements under state or federal law or regulations, and upon approval by the building inspector, and payment of the applicable fee, which fee shall be set forth on a schedule of fees located at Brooklet City Hall.
(3)
A private well shall meet any and all location requirements of the zoning ordinances of the City of Brooklet.
(4)
Well construction shall comply with all state and federal laws or regulations, and with any recommendations from the State Department of Human Resources, or any similar agencies regulating wells within the State of Georgia.
(5)
There shall be no interconnection between any private water supply and the city's public water system. The property owner(s) are responsible for the prevention of any such interconnection.
(Ord. of 7-19-2012)
Sec. 22-24. - Inspection.
Each private well system shall be made available for immediate inspection by the city at any time and without prior notice.
(Ord. of 7-19-2012)
Sec. 22-25. - Penalty.
Any person violating the provisions of this article shall be punished as provided for in section 1-8.
(Ord. of 7-19-2012)
