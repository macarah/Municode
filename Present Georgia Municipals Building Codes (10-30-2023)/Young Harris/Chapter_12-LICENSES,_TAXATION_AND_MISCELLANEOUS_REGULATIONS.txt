Chapter 12 - LICENSES, TAXATION AND MISCELLANEOUS REGULATIONS
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 12-1. - Practice prohibited.
The practice of fortunetelling, phrenology, astrology, clairvoyance, palmistry or other kindred practices, businesses or professions for which a charge is made, or a donation is accepted, for the services is prohibited within the corporate limits of the city.
(Code 2005, § 58-91; Ord. No. 86-03-001, § 3.1, 3-4-1986)

ARTICLE III. - GAME, ARCADE, BILLIARD AND POOL ROOMS

Sec. 12-46. - License required.
Each person keeping, operating or maintaining a coin-operated game or device designed and manufactured for amusement purposes and operated by tokens or prepayment of playing opportunities, a billiard table, pool table or any other game, device, machine or table of a similar character within the corporate limits of the city for profit and for use by the public shall be required to obtain a license from the city clerk. The place of operation of such devices shall be referred to as a game, billiard or pool room.
(Code 2005, § 58-61; Ord. No. 86-03-001, § 2.1, 3-4-1986)
Sec. 12-47. - Licensing prerequisites.
All applications for licenses to operate a billiard table or pool table shall certify that the application has been made and a license granted to such applicant by the tax commissioner of the county. Such application shall contain a provision that the applicant has not been convicted of a felony and that he will not permit vagrants or persons under the influence of intoxicants to enter or play in his place of business.
(Code 2005, § 58-62; Ord. No. 86-03-001, § 2.2, 3-4-1986)
Sec. 12-48. - Hours of operation.
It shall be unlawful for the owner, operator or manager of any game, billiard or pool room in the city to permit such establishment to be open for business between the hours of 12:00 midnight and 6:00 a.m., or permit the use of billiard or pool tables between the hours of 12:00 midnight on Saturday and 6:00 a.m. on Monday.
(Code 2005, § 58-63; Ord. No. 86-03-001, § 2.3, 3-4-1986)
Sec. 12-49. - Minors.
It shall be unlawful for any person having charge or control of any game, billiard or pool room open to the public to admit into such room any minor during school day hours, whether or not such admittance is for the purpose of playing the games, billiards or pool.
(Code 2005, § 58-64; Ord. No. 86-03-001, § 2.4, 3-4-1986)
Sec. 12-50. - Clear view of premises required.
The owner, operator or manager of any game, billiard or pool room in the city shall not permit to be used on such premises any screens, shades, partitions or other devices of a similar character which shall have the effect of obstructing the view through the windows or doors of the place where the games, or billiard or pool tables are kept.
(Code 2005, § 58-65; Ord. No. 86-03-001, § 2.5, 3-4-1986)
Sec. 12-51. - Doors.
The doors of all game, billiard or pool rooms licensed under this article shall be kept unlocked whenever the tables are in use, or when any person, other than the proprietor or his agent, is present in such place.
(Code 2005, § 58-66; Ord. No. 86-03-001, § 2.6, 3-4-1986)
Sec. 12-52. - Gambling and intoxicants.
It shall be unlawful for the owner, operator or manager of any game, billiard or pool room open for public use to allow:
(1)
Gambling of any kind to occur upon such premises;
(2)
Any vagrant or person under the influence of intoxicants to enter or play upon such premises; or
(3)
The consumption or use of intoxicants upon such premises.
(Code 2005, § 58-67; Ord. No. 86-03-001, § 2.7, 3-4-1986)
Sec. 12-53. - Termination of license.
The business license of any game, billiard or pool room may be terminated for violation of any of the provisions of, and in accordance with the procedures established in, this article.
(Code 2005, § 58-68; Ord. No. 86-03-001, § 2.8, 3-4-1986)
Sec. 12-54. - Violations; penalties.
(a)
Any person who shall keep, operate or maintain a game, billiard or pool room in violation of the provisions of section 12-46 shall be punished as provided in section 1-10.
(b)
Any person who shall keep, operate or maintain a game, billiard or pool room in violation of sections 12-47 through 12-53 shall, upon conviction, be punished by a fine not to exceed $100.00 and costs, or by imprisonment not to exceed 30 days, or both such fine and imprisonment. Any and all of such penalties are to be imposed in the discretion of the judge of the police court. Any fine imposed pursuant to the terms of this section may be collected by execution and levy and/or garnishment.
(Code 2005, § 58-69; Ord. No. 86-03-001, § 2.9, 3-4-1986)
Secs. 12-55—12-81. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - FINANCE INSTITUTIONS TAXES[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Local depository financial institutions business license tax, O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.

Sec. 12-82. - Annual business license tax levied.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93, there is hereby levied for the year 1984, and for each year thereafter, an annual business license tax upon all depository financial institutions located within the corporate limits of the city, at a rate of one-fourth of one percent of the gross receipts of such depository financial institutions. For the purposes of this section, the term "gross receipts" means as defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93. The term "depository financial institutions" means state and national banks, state building and loan associations, and federal savings and loan associations.
(Code 2005, § 58-151; Ord. No. 83-12-003, § 1, 12-6-1983)
Sec. 12-83. - Minimum annual business license tax.
The minimum annual amount of business license tax due from any depository financial institution pursuant to section 12-82 shall be $1,000.00.
(Code 2005, § 58-152; Ord. No. 83-12-003, § 2, 12-6-1983)
Sec. 12-84. - Filing return; tax assessment and collection.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(c), each depository financial institution subject to the tax levied pursuant to this article shall file a return of its gross receipts with the city council on March 1 of the year following the year in which such gross receipts are measured. Such return shall be in the manner and form prescribed by the commissioner of the department of revenue, based on the allocation method set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(d). The city council shall assess and collect the tax levied pursuant to this article, based upon the information provided in such return.
(Code 2005, § 58-153; Ord. No. 83-12-003, § 3, 12-6-1983)
Sec. 12-85. - Tax due date.
Taxes levied pursuant to this article shall be due no later than 30 days after filing of the return prescribed by section 12-84 unless such time is extended by the city council.
(Code 2005, § 58-154; Ord. No. 83-12-003, § 4, 12-6-1983)
Secs. 12-86—12-113. - Reserved.

Sec. 12-114. - License fee levied.
There is hereby levied for the year 2022, and for each year thereafter, an annual license fee in the amount of $25.00 upon each insurer doing business within the city. For each separate business location in excess of one not covered by section 12-115, which is operating on behalf of such insurers within the city, there is hereby levied a license fee in the amount of $25.00. For the purposes of this article, the term "insurer" means a company which is authorized to transact business in any of the classes of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5.
(Ord. No. 2021-ORD-10, § 1, 11-2-2021)
Sec. 12-115. - License fees for insurers insuring certain risks at additional business locations.
For each separate business location, not otherwise subject to a license fee under this article, operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing and, in connection with such loans or sales, offers, solicits or takes applications for insurance through a licensed agent of an insurer for insurance, said insurer shall pay an additional license fee of $10.00 per location for the year 2022, and for each year thereafter.
(Ord. No. 2021-ORD-10, § 1, 11-2-2021)
Sec. 12-116. - License fees due date.
License fees imposed in sections 12-114 and 12-115 of this article shall be due and payable on the first day of 2022 and on the first date of each subsequent year.
(Ord. No. 2021-ORD-10, § 1, 11-2-2021)
Sec. 12-117. - Gross direct premiums tax imposed on life insurers.
There is hereby levied for the year 2022, and for each year thereafter, an annual tax, based solely upon gross direct premiums, upon each insurer writing life, accident and sickness insurance within the State of Georgia in an amount equal to one percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.1. "Gross direct premiums" as used in this section shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 12-114.
(Ord. No. 2021-ORD-10, § 1, 11-2-2021)
Sec. 12-118. - Gross direct premiums tax levied on all other insurers.
There is hereby levied for the year 2022, and for each year thereafter, an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer, other than an insurer transacting business in the class of insurance designated in subsection 1 of O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5, doing business within the State of Georgia in an amount equal to two and one-half percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2. "Gross direct premiums" as used in this section shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 12-114.
(Ord. No. 2021-ORD-10, § 1, 11-2-2021)
