Chapter 66 - PARKS AND RECREATION[1]

Cross reference— Cemeteries, ch. 26; streets, sidewalks and other public places, ch. 74.

Sec. 66-1. - Control of city parks and public property.
The city shall have control of Jack Day Park, Church Street Park, Patriot Park, Burch Park, May Harp Park, Dickson Springs Park, City Center Park and any other park or public property now or hereafter owned or operated by the city.
(Ord. No. 0-38-95, § 7-1(b), 10-16-95; Ord. No. 0-16-06, §§ 1, 2, 7-6-06; Ord. No. 0-16-07, § 1, 7-19-07; Ord. No. 0-3-12, art. 1, 4-5-12; Ord. No. 0-27-21 , Art. 1, 11-18-21)
Sec. 66-2. - Entry after dark.
It shall be unlawful for any person to enter or be upon the grounds or premises of any city park or public property without proof of permission issued by the proper respective authority between 9:00 p.m. and 6:00 a.m., except for Jack Day Park and City Center Park, where lighting is provided for nighttime activities, where it shall be unlawful for any person to enter or to be upon the grounds or premises between 12:00 a.m. and 6:00 a.m.
(Ord. No. 0-38-95, § 7-2(b), 10-16-95; Ord. No. 0-16-06, §§ 1, 2, 7-6-06; Ord. No. 0-16-07, § 1, 7-19-07; Ord. No. 0-3-12, art. 1, 4-5-12; Ord. No. 0-27-21 , Art. 1, 11-18-21)
Sec. 66-3. - Alcoholic beverages prohibited.
The consumption of any alcoholic beverage is prohibited in any city park unless such alcoholic beverage is purchased from a vendor operating under a valid City of Fayetteville permit or if the alcoholic beverage is consumed at a special event or catered function permitted for the sale or consumption of alcohol.
(Ord. No. 0-16-06, §§ 1, 2, 1, 2, 7-6-06; Ord. No. 0-16-07, § 1, 7-19-07; Ord. No. 0-27-21 , Art. I, 11-18-21)
Sec. 66-4. - Refuse and dumping.
All refuse shall be disposed of in a proper waste facility within the city parks. In the event that any city park does not maintain a waste facility, the refuse shall be disposed of at another suitable waste facility. At no time is littering and dumping permitted within a city park or on public property.
(Ord. No. 0-16-06, §§ 1, 2, 1, 2, 7-6-06; Ord. No. 0-16-07, § 1, 7-19-07; Ord. No. 0-3-12, art. 1, 4-5-12)
Sec. 66-5. - Dogs.
All dogs in city parks or public property shall be kept on leash unless located within the dog park at the City Center Park. Dog waste shall be immediately removed by placing said matter in a closed or sealed container and thereafter disposing of it in a trash receptacle or other closed sealed refuse container.
(Ord. No. 0-16-06, §§ 1, 2, 1, 2, 7-6-06; Ord. No. 0-16-07, § 1, 7-19-07; Ord. No. 0-3-12, art. 1, 4-5-12; Ord. No. 0-27-21 , Art. 1, 11-18-21)
Sec. 66-6. - Park trails.
The walking paths or trails located within any city park are only for use by pedestrians, persons who use a wheelchair and persons operating non-motorized vehicles. Motorized recreation vehicles such as dirt bikes or go-carts are prohibited within the city parks. Parking is only allowed in designated areas.
(Ord. No. 0-16-06, §§ 1, 2, 1, 2, 7-6-06; Ord. No. 0-16-07, § 1, 7-19-07)
Sec. 66-7. - Fishing.
City parks which maintain ponds or lakes may be fished from a location along the shoreline or from a dock. All watercraft are prohibited within city parks.
(Ord. No. 0-16-06, §§ 1, 2, 1, 2, 7-6-06; Ord. No. 0-16-07, § 1, 7-19-07)
Sec. 66-8. - Soliciting.
Solicitation of the sale of any service or good is not allowed in any city park without obtaining a permit from the city.
(Ord. No. 0-16-07, § 1, 7-19-07)
Sec. 66-9. - Organized events.
For the purposes of this section 66-9 an "organized event" shall be defined as any event which is advertised by any media outlet, or any other method of advertising; any rally; the posting or handing out of flyers, papers, advertisements; any event where any participant implements the use of any sound amplification system device or plays amplified music; any gathering where alcohol is consumed; or any gathering sponsored by an organization.
Organized events are only permitted in the Gazebo area of Jack Day Park, Church Street Park, Patriot Park, Amphitheater, Holiday Dorsey-Fife House, City Hall and City Center Park and Dickson Springs Park. Organized events are not allowed within any other city park or on public property. This section shall not be construed to prohibit organized events at a licensed event center located on private or leased property within the city.
(Ord. No. 0-16-07, § 1, 7-19-07; Ord. No. 0-3-12, art. 1, 4-5-12; Ord. No. 0-27-21 , Art. 1, 11-18-21)
Sec. 66-10. - Camping prohibited.
Notwithstanding the provisions of any other ordinances, no person shall be allowed to camp or reside overnight with or without a recreational vehicle, tent, sleeping bag, vehicle or other structure at a city park or on public property.
(Ord. No. 0-3-12, art. 1, 4-5-12)
Sec. 66-11. - Fires within the parks and on public property.
No outdoor cooking of food or outdoor fires shall be permitted within the city parks or on public property, except in areas specifically designated by the city.
(Ord. No. 0-3-12, art. 1, 4-5-12)
Sec. 66-12. - Posting signs.
No park visitor may affix any bill, sign or notice on any tree, building or fixture, or handouts to another person in any of the parks.
( Ord. No. 0-27-21 , Art. 1, 11-18-21)
Sec. 66-13. - Model airplane or drone flying.
No person shall engage in model airplane or drone flying in any public park unless the use is a contracted vendor flying on behalf of the city.
( Ord. No. 0-27-21 , Art. 1, 11-18-21)
Sec. 66-14. - Enforcement.
Citations for violation of this chapter may be issued by the city manager or his/her designee, or a police officer of the city. The citation shall be returnable to and tried before the municipal court of the city. Any person, firm, or corporation violating any provisions of this chapter shall, upon conviction, be fined in an amount as authorized by the City's Charter.
(Ord. No. 0-16-06, §§ 1, 2, 1, 2, 7-6-06; Ord. No. 0-16-07, § 1, 7-19-07; Ord. No. 0-3-12, art. 1, 4-5-12; Ord. No. 0-27-21 , Art. 1, 11-18-21)
