CHAPTER 3-3. - FIRE DEPARTMENT[6]
modified

Sec. 3-3-1. - Injuring fire apparatus.
It shall be unlawful for any person to injure or damage in any way and by any means, any of the apparatus of the fire department.
(Code 1958, § 10-3)
State Law reference— Criminal damage to property, OCGA § 16-7-22 et seq.; interference with government property, OCGA § 16-7-24 et seq.
Sec. 3-3-2. - Obstruction of fire plugs.
It shall be unlawful for any person to obstruct with building material or otherwise, or to place any post nearer than three (3) feet of any fire plug on their side, such as would obstruct the approaches of the fire department to the same.
(Code 1958, § 10-4)
Cross reference— Water, Ch. 5-2.
State Law reference— Obstruction or hindering of firefighters, OCGA § 16-10-24.1.
Sec. 3-3-3. - Members of fire department vested with police power during fire.
The chief and each member of the fire department are hereby vested with all the powers of a police officer of the city, insofar as to make arrests within the city, during a fire.
(Code 1958, § 10-6)
Cross reference— Police department, Ch. 3-2.
State Law reference— Power of state fire marshal and staff to make arrests and carry weapons, OCGA § 25-2-9.
Sec. 3-3-4. - City firefighting equipment not to be used outside city, exception.
(a)
No firefighting equipment or personnel of the city shall be used outside the corporate limits of the city, except for:
(1)
The purpose of extinguishing fires at schools, churches, industrial plants, veterans' clubs, radio stations or in response to calls for aid from other municipalities within Hall County unless approved by the city manager.
(2)
The purpose of complying with the terms and conditions of any automatic aid agreement entered into with any other governmental entity.
(Code 1958, § 10-8; Ord. No. 97-63, § I, 10-21-97)
State Law reference— Mutual aid resource pacts, OCGA § 25-6-1 et seq.
