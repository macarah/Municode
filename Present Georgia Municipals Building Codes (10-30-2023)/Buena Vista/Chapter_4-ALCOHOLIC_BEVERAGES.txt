ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 4-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcohol means ethyl alcohol, hydrated oxide of ethyl, or spirits of wine, from whatever source, or by whatever process produced.
Alcoholic beverage means any beverage containing alcohol, however sold, and shall include, but not be limited to, all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, malt beverage, wine, and fortified wine.
Brewer means the manufacturer of malt beverages.
Distilled spirits or liquor means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 24 percent alcohol by volume.
Election day means that period of time beginning with the opening of the polls and ending with the closing of the polls.
Financial interest means an ownership interest, as well as any indebtedness or security interest in a business.
Fixed salary means the amount of compensation paid to any member, officer, agent, or employee of a private club as may be fixed for such person by its members at a prior annual meeting or by the council of the organization out of the general revenue of the club, and shall not include any commission on any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages. For the purposes of this definition, tips or gratuities that are added to the bills under club regulations shall not be considered to be revenue from the sale of alcoholic beverages.
Fortified wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 24 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "fortified wine" includes, but is not limited to, brandy.
Hotel means a building or other structure providing sleeping accommodations for hire to the general public, either transient, permanent, or residential. Such hotel shall maintain a minimum of 20 rooms available for hire. Motels meeting the qualifications set out herein for hotels shall be classified in the same category as hotels. Hotels shall have the privilege of granting franchises for the operation of any licensed establishment hereunder, and the holder of such franchise shall be included in the definition of the term "hotel" under this chapter.
Licensed building means the physical location, as designated by the street or road address appearing on the alcoholic beverage license issued by the City, where alcoholic beverages can be sold by a licensee under this chapter; provided, however, that such licensed building shall not include parking lots or sidewalks, whether public or private, adjacent or appurtenant to the licensed building.
Licensee means the individual to whom a license is issued, or in the case of a corporation, the shareholders, officers, and directors of the corporation, or in the case of a partnership, all partners of the partnership, or in the case of a limited liability company, all members, officers, and owners of the limited liability company, or in the case of any other legal entity, the owners and officers of such legal entity.
Malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any other similar product, or any combination of such products, in water, containing not more than 14 percent alcohol by volume and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer and strong beer. The term "malt beverage" does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Manager means any person who supervises the regular operations of a business licensed under this chapter.
Manufacturer means any maker, producer, or bottler of an alcoholic beverage. The term "manufacturer" also means:
(1)
In the case of distilled spirits, any person engaged in distilling, rectifying, or blending any distilled spirits;
(2)
In the case of any malt beverages, any brewer; and
(3)
In the case of wine, any wine vintner.
Mixed drink means any distilled spirit or spirituous liquors served for consumption on the premises by the drink that may or may not be diluted by any other liquid.
Nuisance means anything that works hurt, inconvenience, or damage to another, and the fact that the act done may otherwise be lawful shall not keep it from becoming or being a nuisance. The inconvenience complained of shall not be fanciful, or such as would affect only one of fastidious taste, but it shall be such as would affect an ordinary reasonable person.
Owner means any person, partner, shareholder, member or other legal entity owning any percentage of a business.
Package means a bottle, can, keg, barrel, or other original consumer container.
Police Chief means the Police Chief of the City or the assistant chief acting on his behalf.
Premises means the space or area owned, leased, or controlled by the licensee and used by the licensee for the purpose of operating under a license issued in accord with this chapter. The term "premises" shall also include the parking area outside of the licensed business, whether public or private, where patrons of the licensed business park vehicles when patronizing the licensed business. The term "premises" includes, but is not limited to, the licensed building.
Private club means a bona fide private club, patriotic organization, charitable organization, or fraternal organization organized and existing under the laws of the state, having bylaws and being a part of a national organization in existence for at least one year immediately prior to the application for a license hereunder, having at least 75 members regularly paying monthly, quarterly, or semi-annual dues, organized and operating exclusively for fraternal brotherhood, pleasure, recreation, and other nonprofit purposes, no part of the net earnings of which inures to the benefit of any shareholder or member, and owning, hiring, or leasing a building or space therein for the reasonable use of its members, with suitable kitchen and dining space and equipment, and maintaining and using a sufficient number of employees for cooking, preparing, and serving meals for its members and guests. Such private club shall have no member, officer, agent, or employee directly or indirectly receiving, in the form of salary or other compensation, any profit from the sale of alcoholic beverages beyond a fixed salary.
Restaurant means any establishment which is licensed under this chapter which derives at least 40 percent of its total annual gross sales from the sale of prepared meals and which is publicly held out as a place where hot meals are prepared and served at least five days a week, including holidays. Such prepared meals must be prepared on the premises of the licensee.
Retail consumption dealer means any person who sells distilled spirits for consumption on the premises only to consumers and not for resale.
Retailer orretail dealer means, except as to distilled spirits, any person who sells alcoholic beverages, either in unbroken packages or for consumption on the premises, at retail only to consumers and not for resale. With respect to distilled spirits, the term "retailer" or "retail dealer" means any person who sells distilled spirits in unbroken packages at retail only to consumers and not for resale.
Wholesaler orwholesale dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages to other wholesale dealers, to retail dealers, or to retail consumption dealers.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 24 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes, either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "wine" includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products. The term "wine" does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to this definition.
(Ord. of unknown date, § 6-1)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2.
Sec. 4-2. - Activity declared a privilege.
Notwithstanding anything in this chapter to the contrary, the sale of alcoholic beverages in the City is a privilege, and not a right, and the issuance of a license hereunder shall not create any property rights in the license holder.
(Ord. of unknown date, § 6-2)
Sec. 4-3. - Responsibility for enforcement.
(a)
It shall be the responsibility of the City Clerk and assisted by the Police Chief or his designee to enforce the licensing provisions of this chapter.
(b)
It shall be the responsibility of the Police Chief or the Chief's agents to enforce the operating regulations of this chapter.
(Ord. of unknown date, § 6-3)
Sec. 4-4. - Drinking or possessing alcoholic beverages in park, playground or school.
It shall be unlawful for any person to drink or possess any alcoholic beverage upon real property of the City that is used as a City park, playground or school.
(Ord. of unknown date, § 6-4)
Sec. 4-5. - Open containers; removal of beverages prohibited.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to drink or have in his possession an opened container of any alcoholic beverage on any public street, sidewalk, park, parking lot, or other public place within the City, or upon or within any motor vehicle on the streets, sidewalks, parks, parking lots, and public places of the City.
(b)
All alcoholic beverages sold for consumption on the premises where sold, including, but not limited to, the sale of malt beverages, wine or mixed drinks, shall be consumed only at the licensed building. It shall be unlawful for any person to remove from the licensed building any alcoholic beverages sold for consumption on the premises. Each licensee authorized to sell alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises where sold shall be responsible for ensuring that no person removes any alcoholic beverages from the licensed building in any type of container.
(Ord. of unknown date, § 6-5)
State Law reference— Open containers of alcohol in vehicles, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-253.
Sec. 4-6. - Unlawful to furnish to minors; responsibility of licensee.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person holding an alcoholic beverage license or any other person, or any licensee's agent or employee, to sell, give away, or permit the sale or gift of any alcoholic beverage to any person under the age specified by state law, except for medical purposes pursuant to a prescription given by a physician duly authorized to practice medicine in the state, or in connection with a religious ceremony, or in the home with parental consent. No person shall be convicted under this section if such person has been furnished with the proper identification showing the person who is being furnished alcoholic beverages is over the age required by state law. For the purposes of this section, the term "proper identification" means any document issued by a governmental agency containing a description of the person, such as the person's photograph or other written description, and giving the person's date of birth, and includes, without being limited to, a passport, military identification card or driver's license.
(b)
It shall be the duty and responsibility of the licensee, and the licensee's agent or employee, to inspect the identification of all patrons to ensure that no person is allowed to enter the premises where entry is prohibited.
(Ord. of unknown date, § 6-6)
State Law reference— Sale to minors, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23.
Sec. 4-7. - Consumption, possession by minors.
Except as hereinafter provided, it shall be unlawful for any person under the age specified by state law to purchase or possess any alcoholic beverages except for medical purposes pursuant to the prescription of a physician duly licensed to practice medicine in the state, or in connection with a religious ceremony; provided, however, the foregoing provision shall not apply to any person who is of the age required by state law and who is an active member of the regular armed forces of the United States who produces a valid military identification card.
(Ord. of unknown date, § 6-7)
State Law reference— Consumption, possession by minor, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23; authority of City to prohibit possession by particular class, O.C.G.A. § 37-8-11.
Sec. 4-8. - Acquiring for minors; responsibility of licensee.
(a)
No person shall knowingly and intentionally act as an agent or otherwise purchase or acquire any alcoholic beverage for or on behalf of any person under the age required by state law, or otherwise procure for or furnish to any person, under the age required by state law, any alcoholic beverage, except for medical purposes under the prescription of a physician duly licensed under the laws of the state.
(b)
It shall be the duty and responsibility of the licensee, and the licensee's agent or employee, to report known or suspected violations of this section to the City Police Department.
(Ord. of unknown date, § 6-8)
Sec. 4-9. - Employment of minors; entering licensed premises.
(a)
General prohibitions.
(1)
Except as provided in subsection (a)(4) of this section, no wholesale dealer or package licensee shall allow or require any employee under the age of 18 years to dispense, sell, serve, take orders for, or handle alcoholic beverages.
(2)
No person or business licensed to allow consumption on the premises shall allow or require any employee under the age of 18 years to dispense, sell, serve, take orders for, or handle alcoholic beverages.
(3)
This section shall not prohibit the employment of persons under the age of 18 years at licensed premises where such persons do not dispense, sell, serve, take orders for, or handle alcoholic beverages.
(4)
This section shall not prohibit persons under the age of 18 years being employed in supermarkets, convenience stores, grocery stores, or drugstores from selling or handling alcoholic beverages that are sold for consumption off the premises.
(b)
Entering licensed premises. It shall be unlawful for any licensee, any licensee's agent, or any employee of a licensee to permit or allow any person who is not authorized by law to purchase, possess, or consume alcoholic beverages to enter a licensed premises; provided, however, and subject to the provisions of subsection (c) of this section, that the provisions of this subsection shall not apply to restaurants, hotels, theaters, convenience stores, drugstores, supermarkets, or private clubs.
(c)
Persons under the age of 21 years.
(1)
It shall be unlawful for any person under the age of 21 years to enter into or remain in a restaurant, hotel, or private club which has a license to sell alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises where sold at any time during which the restaurant, hotel, or private club is selling or serving alcoholic beverages, but does not:
a.
Have an employee available to serve meals prepared on the premises; and
b.
Have at least one public dining room with adequate and sanitary kitchen facilities and staff available to accept orders and prepare, cook, and serve food for its guests during the time that such person under the age of 21 years enters or is present within the licensee's business establishment.
(2)
Nothing in subsection (c)(1) of this section shall be construed to prevent a person under the age of 21 years from entering into areas of a hotel, such as the registration area, the lobby, hallways, or guestrooms, during such times.
(Ord. of unknown date, § 6-9)
State Law reference— Employment of underage persons, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-24.
Sec. 4-10. - Misrepresentation of age; responsibility of licensee to report.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person in any manner whatsoever to falsely misrepresent that such person is of the age required by state law to purchase alcoholic beverages, or that such person is of the age required by state law and in military service for the purpose of buying or possessing alcoholic beverages in violation of this chapter, and the penalty for such misrepresentation shall be provided in section 1-9.
(b)
It shall be the duty and responsibility of the licensee, his agent or employee to promptly report any person misrepresenting their age for the purposes of purchasing alcoholic beverages.
(Ord. of unknown date, § 6-10)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23.
Sec. 4-11. - Days and hours for sales.
(a)
No person may sell or offer to sell any distilled spirits, wine or malt beverages on Sundays.
(b)
No retail sales of spirituous liquors or distilled spirits for off-premises consumption shall be made in the City, except between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 11:00 p.m.
(c)
No retail sales of spirituous liquors or distilled spirits by the drink, or malt beverages or wine shall be made in the City in any establishment licensed for on-premises consumption except between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 2:00 a.m. the following day, except that on Saturday, the sales of such alcoholic beverages shall cease and stop at 12:00 midnight Sunday morning. Additionally, it shall be the responsibility of the licensee's agent or employee to see that all patrons vacate the portion of the premises where alcoholic beverages are sold within two hours after the time prescribed for ceasing the sale of alcohol. Also, it shall be their responsibility to make sure the parking area is closed by 2:15 a.m.
(Ord. of unknown date, § 6-11)
State Law reference— Sales of alcoholic beverages on Sundays and election days, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20.
Sec. 4-12. - Sale of alcoholic beverages near church, school or housing authority property.
(a)
No person knowingly and intentionally may sell or offer to sell:
(1)
Any distilled spirits in or within 100 yards of any church building or within 200 yards of any school building, educational building, school grounds or college campus which are also located in the City.
(2)
Any wine or malt beverages within 100 yards of any school building, school grounds, church or college campus. This subsection shall not apply to any renewal of such license, nor shall this subsection apply to any location for which a new license is applied for if the sale of wine and beer was lawful at such location any time during the 12 months immediately preceding such application. Nothing in this subsection shall prohibit a grocery store licensed for the retail sale of only wine and malt beverages for consumption off the premises from selling wine or malt beverages within 100 yards of any school building, school grounds, or college campus, where so permitted by resolution or ordinance of the county or City. As used in this subsection, the term "grocery store" means a retail establishment which has a total retail floor space of at least 10,000 square feet of which at least 85 percent is reserved for the sale of food and other nonalcoholic items, conducts all of its sales inside the building containing its retail floor space, and meets such other criteria as may be required by the local governing authority of the county or City.
(3)
Any distilled spirits, wine or malt beverages within 100 yards of an alcoholic treatment center owned and operated by the state or any county or municipal government therein.
(b)
Nothing contained in this section shall prohibit the licensing of the sale or distribution of alcoholic beverages by bona fide private clubs, owning their own homes, subject to licensing under state law.
(c)
As used in this section, the term "school building" or "educational building" shall apply only to state, county, City or church school buildings and to such buildings at such other schools in which are taught subjects commonly taught in the common schools and colleges of the state, and which are public schools or private schools as defined in O.C.G.A. § 20-2-690(b).
(d)
As authorized pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21(b)(3), the provisions of subsection (a) of this section shall not be applicable to the retail sale of alcoholic beverages by licensees for consumption on the premises where sold, where such licensee is a restaurant as defined under section 4-1.
(e)
No person may knowingly or intentionally sell any alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises within 100 yards of any housing authority property. This subsection shall not apply to any location for which a new license is applied for if the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises was lawful at such location at any time during the 12 months immediately preceding such application.
(f)
For purposes of this section, distances shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground. For purposes of calculating such measurement, the following method shall be used:
(1)
In a straight line from the front door of the structure from which alcoholic beverages are sold or offered for sale;
(2)
To the front door of the building of a church, government-owned treatment center, or retail package store; or
(3)
To the nearest property line of the real property being used for school, educational or housing authority purposes.
All renewal applications shall use the measurements required in the initial application and license.
(Ord. of unknown date, § 6-12)
Sec. 4-13. - Security cameras required in establishments for sales by the package for off-premises consumption; exemption; penalty for violation.
(a)
Licensed establishments engaged in alcohol sales. Any establishment which is licensed by the City to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages by the package for off-premises consumption, and which is open to the public for business any day of the week, is required to install security cameras and recording mechanisms of a type and number approved by the Chief of Police.
(b)
Cameras and recording mechanisms. Cameras and recording mechanisms meeting the requirements of this section must be capable of producing a retrievable image on videotape that can be made a permanent record and can be enlarged through projection or other means. Cameras and recording mechanisms meeting the requirements of this section shall be maintained in proper working order at all times and shall be subject to inspection by the Chief of Police or his designee.
(c)
Violations constituting offenses. Violation of any provisions of this section shall constitute an offense hereunder and be punishable as follows:
(1)
First offense. A fine of $500.00 and the judge of the municipal court may impose a suspension of all alcoholic beverage licenses from the City for a period not to exceed 30 days.
(2)
Second offense. An offense will be considered a second offense if it occurs within 365 calendar days from the date of the occurrence of the first offense. A second offense will incur a fine of $1,000.00 and all alcoholic beverage licenses to the establishment issued by the City will be suspended for a minimum period of 60 days.
(3)
Third offense. An offense will be considered a third offense if it occurs within a 365-calendar-day period when two other convictions of the offenders have taken place prior to the present offense and within said 365-day period. A third offense shall incur a fine of $1,500.00 and all alcoholic beverage licenses to the establishment issued by the City will be suspended for a minimum of six months.
(Ord. of unknown date, § 6-81)

ARTICLE II. - EXCISE TAXES

Sec. 4-45. - Malt beverages.
(a)
There is hereby levied and imposed upon all wholesale dealers selling malt beverages within the City a specific excise tax in the amount of $0.05 per 12-ounce container, or any portion thereof, so as to graduate the tax on bottles, cans and containers of various sizes of malt beverages sold by each wholesale dealer within the City, and an excise tax on tap or draft beer in the amount of $6.00 for each container sold, as hereinafter specified, containing not more than 15½ gallons, and at a like rate for fractional parts where the beverage is sold in or from a barrel or bulk container, such beverage being commonly known as tap or draft beer, to be paid as follows:
(1)
Each wholesale dealer selling malt beverages within the City shall file a report by the tenth day of each month following the preceding calendar month the exact quantities of malt beverage, by size and type container, constituting a beginning and ending inventory for the month sold within the City.
(2)
Each such wholesale dealer shall remit to the City on the tenth day of the month next succeeding the calendar month in which such sales were made the amount of excise tax due in accordance with this section.
(b)
No decal, stamp or other identifying mark shall be required on malt beverages sold in the City.
(c)
The excise tax provided for herein shall be in addition to any license fee, tax or charge which may now or in the future be imposed upon the business of selling malt beverages at retail or wholesale within the City.
(d)
The failure to make a timely report and remittance shall render a wholesale malt beverage dealer liable for a penalty equal to 20 percent of the total amount due during the first 30-day period following the date such report and remittance were due and a further penalty of ten percent of the amount of such remittance for each successive 30-day period or any portion thereof during which such report and remittance are not filed. The filing of a false or fraudulent report shall render the wholesale dealer making such report liable for a penalty equal to 50 percent of the amount of the remittance which would be required under an accurate and truthful report.
(Ord. of unknown date, § 6-41)
State Law reference— Local excise taxes on malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-80 et seq.

(a)
There is hereby imposed and levied the following specific excise tax upon all retail dealers in wine within the City:
(1)
$0.06 on each one-half pint bottle or container.
(2)
$0.12 on each one pint bottle or container.
(3)
$0.18 on each one quart bottle or container or on bottle or container known as a "fifth."
(4)
$0.22 on each liter bottle or container.
(5)
$0.44 on each one-half gallon jug or bottle or container.
(6)
$0.88 on each one gallon jug or container.
(7)
Wines in other containers shall be taxed at the rate of $0.88 per gallon of wine contained therein.
(b)
At the time of delivery of the wine for sale to the retail dealer, the wholesale dealer shall collect from the retail dealer the amount of the taxes the retail dealer is obligated for under this section and the wholesale dealer shall pay the tax collected to the City Treasurer, on or before the 20th day of the month following delivery of the wine to the retail dealer and send an accurate record of all wine sold the previous month with said payment.
(Ord. of unknown date, § 6-51)
State Law reference— Local excise taxes on sale of wine, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-60 et seq.
Sec. 4-47. - Distilled spirits for sale by package.
(a)
There is hereby levied and imposed upon all retail dealers of distilled spirits for sale by the package within the City an excise tax computed at the rate of $0.22 per liter or pro rata portion thereof upon all distilled spirits for sale by the package within the City. No tax is hereby levied on fortified wine.
(b)
At the time of delivery of the distilled spirits for sale by the package, the wholesale dealer shall collect from the retail dealer the amount of the taxes the retail dealer is obligated for under this section, and the wholesale dealer shall pay the tax collected to the City Clerk, on or before the 20th day of the month following delivery of the distilled spirits for sale by the package and send an accurate record of all distilled spirits sold the previous month with said payment.
(c)
This excise tax shall apply only to retail dealers of distilled spirits for sale by the package within the City and shall not apply to holders of any type of "mixed drink" license issued by the City.
(Ord. of unknown date, § 6-61)
State Law reference— Local excise tax on sale of distilled spirits by the package, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-80 et seq.
Sec. 4-48. - Distilled spirits sold for mixed drinks.
(a)
Imposition and rate of tax. There is hereby imposed and levied upon every sale of a mixed drink purchased in the City a tax in the amount of three percent of the purchase price of said drink.
(b)
Persons liable for tax. Every licensee is hereby authorized and directed to collect the tax herein imposed from the purchaser of mixed drinks sold within the licensee's licensed premises. Such licensee shall furnish such information as may be requested by the Clerk and/or Police Chief to facilitate the collection of this tax.
(c)
Determination; returns and payments.
(1)
Due date of taxes. All amounts of such cases shall be due and payable to the Clerk monthly on or before the 20th day of the month following the sale of the mixed drink.
(2)
Return; time of filing; persons required to file; contents. On or before the 20th day of the month following the month of sale of the mixed drink, the return for the preceding monthly period shall be filed with the Clerk showing the gross revenues from sale of mixed drinks, the amount of tax collected or otherwise due for the related period, and such other information as may be required by the Clerk.
(3)
Collection fee allowed; licensees. Licensees collecting the tax shall be allowed a percentage of the tax due and accounted for and shall be reimbursed in the form of a deduction in submitting, reporting and payment of the amount due, if said amount is not delinquent at the time of payment. The rate of the deduction shall be the same rate authorized for deductions from state tax under the Georgia Retailers and Consumers Sales and Use Tax Act, approved February 20, 1951 (O.C.G.A. title 48, ch. 8), as now or hereafter amended.
(d)
Deficiency determinations.
(1)
Recomputation of tax; authority to make; basis of recomputation. If the Clerk and/or Police Chief is not satisfied with the return of the tax or the amount of the tax required to be paid to the City by any person, such Clerk may compute and determine the amount required to be paid upon the basis of any information within such Clerk and/or Police Chief's possession or that may come into such Clerk and/or Police Chief's possession. One or more deficiency determinations may be made of the amount due for one or more monthly periods.
(2)
Interest on deficiency. The amount of the determination shall bear interest at the rate of one percent per month or fraction thereof, from the due date of the taxes.
(3)
Notice of the Clerk and/or Police Chiefs determination; service of. The Clerk or designated representatives shall give to the licensee written notice of such Clerk and/or Police Chief's determination. The notice may be served personally or by mail; if by mail, such service shall be addressed to the licensee at the licensee's address, as it appears in the records of the Clerk. Service by mail is complete when delivered by certified mail with return receipt signed by the licensee, any partner of the licensee, any officer of the licensee or manager of the licensee.
(4)
Time within which notice of deficiency determination to be mailed. Except in the case of failure to make a return, every notice of a deficiency determination shall be mailed within three years after the tenth day of the calendar month following the monthly period for which the amount is proposed to be determined, or within three years after the return is filed, whichever period shall last expire.
(e)
Determination if no return made.
(1)
Estimate of gross receipts. If any licensee fails to make a return, the Clerk shall make an estimate of the amount of the gross receipts of the licensee, or as the case may be, of the amount of the total sales in the City which are subject to the tax. The estimate shall be made for the periods in respect to which the licensee fails to make the return and shall be based upon any information which is or may come into the possession of the Clerk. Written notice shall be given in the manner prescribed in subsection (d)(3) of this section.
(2)
Interest on amount found due. The amount of the determination shall bear interest at the rate of one percent per month, or fraction thereof, from the tenth day of the month following the monthly period for which the amount or any portion thereof should have been returned until the date of payment.
(f)
Penalties and interest for failure to pay tax. Any licensee who fails to pay the tax herein imposed to the City, or fails to pay any amount of such tax required to be collected and paid to the City, within the time required, shall pay a penalty of ten percent of the tax, or amount of the tax, in addition to the tax or amount of the tax, plus interest on the unpaid tax or any portion thereof.
(g)
Collection of tax.
(1)
Action for tax; time limit. At any time within three years after any tax or any amount of tax required to be collected becomes due and payable and at any time within three years after the delinquency of any tax or any amount of tax required to be collected, the Clerk may bring an action in a court of competent jurisdiction in the name of the City to collect the amount delinquent, together with interest, court fees, filing fees, attorney's fees and other legal fees incident thereto.
(2)
Duty of successors or assignees of licensee to withhold tax from purchase money. If any licensee liable for any amount under this section sells the business or quits the business, the licensee's successors or assigns shall withhold a sufficient amount of the purchase price to cover such tax until the former owner produces a receipt from the Clerk showing that such former owner has paid or a certificate stating that no amount is due.
(3)
Liability for failure to withhold; certificate of notice of amount due; time to enforce successor's liability. If the purchaser of a business fails to withhold a sufficient amount of the purchase price as herein required, such purchaser shall personally be liable for the payment of the amount required to be withheld by such purchaser to the extent of the purchase price.
(4)
Tax credit or interest paid more than once erroneously or illegally collected. Whenever the amount of any tax or interest has been paid more than once, or has been erroneously or illegally collected or received by the City under this section, it may be offset by the Clerk. If the licensee determines that such licensee has overpaid or paid more than once, which fact has not been determined by the Clerk, the licensee will have three years from the date of payment to file claim in writing stating the specific grounds upon which the claim is founded. The claim shall be audited. If the claim is approved by the Clerk, the excess amount paid the City may be credited on any amounts then due and payable from the person by whom it was paid, or such person's administrators or executors.
(h)
Administration of section.
(1)
Authority of Clerk. The Clerk and/or Police Chief shall administer and enforce the provisions of this section for the levy and collection of the tax imposed by this section.
(2)
Rules and regulations. The Clerk shall have the power and authority to make and publish reasonable rules and regulations not inconsistent with this chapter or other laws of the City and state, or the Constitution of the state or the United States for the administration and enforcement of the provisions of this section and the collection of the taxes hereunder.
(3)

ARTICLE III. - ENTERTAINMENT DISTRICT

Sec. 4-70. - Creation of entertainment district boundaries.
The entertainment district shall comprise the area set forth in Exhibit A, codified as section 4-77. Generally speaking, the entertainment district shall be bounded by that portion of 4th Avenue from corner of Broad Street to McDuffie Street and that portion of McDuffie from the corner of 4th to where 5th Avenue begins (excluding that portion of McDuffie in front of Cornerstone Church). All references herein to the "entertainment district" shall refer to that area that is specifically set forth. These boundaries may be changed by a duly enacted ordinance of the city.
(Ord. of 10-4-2022(1), § 3-181)
Sec. 4-71. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcoholic beverage establishment means an establishment with a license duly issued by the city to sell alcohol for consumption on the premises pursuant to the alcoholic beverage ordinances of the city. For the purposes of this article, an "alcoholic beverage establishment" also includes the licensed establishments further defined herein to-wit; brewpub, brewery or microbrewery, taproom and tasting room.
Brewery or microbrewery means a place where malt beverages are manufactured or brewed as authorized and licensed by regulation of the Georgia Department of Revenue prescribed by state law.
Brewpub means any restaurant or eating establishment in which beer or malt beverages are manufactured or brewed as prescribed by the Code of Georgia.
Specialty cup means a clear, plastic 16-ounce cup to which a specialty sticker provided by the city is attached and which notes the date of use written by the providing business.
Taproom means an establishment whose primary purpose is in the retail sale of malt beverages and wine.
Tasting room means an outlet for the promotion of a farm winery's wine/alcohol which provides samples of wine/alcohol to the public and for sale and as defined by the law of the state.
(Ord. of 10-4-2022(1), § 3-182)
Sec. 4-72. - Specialty cup required for consumption of alcoholic beverages off premises.
(a)
Those persons desiring to possess or consume off premises any alcoholic beverage purchased from an alcoholic beverage establishment as defined above which is located within the entertainment district in accordance with the restrictions set forth herein are required to acquire and use as the container a specialty cup from the alcoholic beverage establishment within the entertainment district from whom said person purchased the alcoholic beverage.
(b)
Only alcoholic beverage establishments who are authorized to sell alcoholic beverages within the entertainment district are authorized to issue said specialty cups.
(c)
A person's display of a specialty cup does not relieve alcoholic beverage establishments of their responsibility to determine if a person has attained the age of 21 years before dispensing alcohol to that person in said specialty cup or otherwise.
(d)
Said specialty cups are disposable and are intended for single use, to be determined by the date marked on the cup, which date is to be marked by the alcoholic beverage establishment.
(e)
Said specialty cups may only be issued by a participating alcoholic beverage business establishment within the hours of 12:00 noon and 10:00 p.m. Monday through Saturday. On Sunday specialty cups may only be issued between the hours of 2:00 p.m. and 10:00 p.m.
(f)
It shall be unlawful within the entertainment district for any person to drink, attempt to drink, or possess any alcoholic beverage in an unsealed can, glass, or metal container, on the streets, sidewalks, rights-of-way, and/or parking lots, whether public or private. This section shall not prohibit the possession of containers of alcoholic beverages with unbroken seals.
(Ord. of 10-4-2022(1), § 3-183)
Sec. 4-73. - Consumption of alcoholic beverages off premises.
(a)
Subject to the restrictions set forth herein and within the boundaries of said entertainment district, those persons who have attained the age of 21 years may be allowed to remove specialty cups containing alcoholic beverages from the alcoholic beverage establishment premises and may be allowed to possess and consume said alcoholic beverages anywhere within said entertainment district, including the premises of other alcoholic beverage establishment premises.
(b)
Each alcoholic beverage establishment within the entertainment district shall have the discretion to choose whether to issue specialty cups in order to allow persons to leave its premises with a specialty cup containing an alcoholic beverage.
(c)
Each alcoholic beverage establishment within the entertainment district shall have the discretion to choose whether to allow persons to enter its premises with a specialty cup containing alcoholic beverages.
(d)
Each alcoholic beverage establishment within the entertainment district choosing to allow persons to either leave its premises or to enter its premises with a specialty cup containing alcoholic beverages shall notify the city so that specialty cup stickers can be provided and so that the city will have knowledge as to which businesses are participating.
(e)
Those businesses without an alcoholic beverage license may choose to not allow individuals onto their premises with a specialty cup containing alcoholic beverages.
(f)
Persons are not allowed to possess a specialty cup containing an alcoholic beverage while in a motor vehicle.
(g)
Any licensed establishment that allows patrons to leave the establishment with an alcoholic beverage in a specialty cup as provided in this ordinance shall maintain posted inside all exit doors for clear public view a map of the current boundaries of the Entertainment District and a sign of at least 11 inches by 8½ inches that states the following:
"All Patrons leaving this establishment with an alcoholic beverage in an open container do hereby assume full responsibility to consume such alcoholic beverage only if it has been served in a clear, plastic cup not to exceed 16 ounces in size and obtained from an establishment licensed to sell alcoholic beverages within the Entertainment District outlined on the map below. Any individual who leaves the Entertainment District with an alcoholic beverage in an open container is in violation of the City of Buena Vista Code of Ordinances and may be subject to a citation, arrest, incarceration, and or fine pursuant to the Code of Ordinances."
(Ord. of 10-4-2022(1), § 3-184)
Sec. 4-74. - Suspension of food/alcohol ratio.
The existing ordinance requiring that any restaurant or eating establishment with an alcohol consumption license derive at least 30 percent of its annual gross food and beverage sales from the sale of prepared meals or foods shall be suspended for those alcoholic beverage establishments (to include taprooms, breweries, microbreweries and brewpubs) located within the downtown entertainment district electing to use specialty cups for off premises consumption within the boundaries of the Downtown Buena Vista Entertainment District.
(Ord. of 10-4-2022(1), § 3-185)
Sec. 4-75. - Licensing; special events; operations.
Notwithstanding any provision to the contrary, the city may from time to time waive the licensing requirements required herein, may modify or suspend the hours of operation in the entertainment district and may provide a temporary special event license for all businesses within the downtown area as defined by the city.
(Ord. of 10-4-2022(1), § 3-186)

Christmas Parade; and
(3)
Such other times as may be designated pursuant to section 3-186 above.
