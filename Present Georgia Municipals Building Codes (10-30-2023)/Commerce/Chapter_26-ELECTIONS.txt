Sec. 26-1. - Registration lists and records.
The city shall maintain its own permanent registration system. The form of the registration cards shall be in accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 21-2-217. Such cards and other documents of the board of registrars shall be open for public inspection at the office of the city clerk during normal business hours.
(Code 1987, § 2-203)
State Law reference— Designation of form of municipal registration, O.C.G.A. § 21-3-124; preparation of list of electors in municipalities electing to maintain their own registration list, O.C.G.A. § 21-3-134.
Sec. 26-2. - Polling places.
The polling place within the city shall be determined and announced by the city council three weeks prior to any election.
(Code 1987, § 2-402)
State Law reference— Duty of governing authority to fix location of polling places, O.C.G.A. § 21-3-163.
Sec. 26-3. - Plurality of votes required for election.
Candidates for any public municipal office shall be elected by a plurality of the votes cast to fill such office.
(Code 1987, § 2-406)
State Law reference— Vote required for nomination or election, O.C.G.A. § 21-3-407.
Sec. 26-4. - Qualifying fees.
The qualifying fee for mayor and city council positions shall be three percent of the annual salary for the office.
The qualifying fee shall be paid to the city clerk at the time the candidate qualifies for election to the office.
(Ord. No. 99-07, 6-14-99)
Sec. 26-5. - Conduct of elections by county.
The mayor and council may authorize the county to conduct any or all municipal elections as authorized by O.C.G.A. § 21-2-45 and may authorize intergovernmental contracts with the county for such purposes upon such terms and conditions as the mayor and council may agree.
(Ord. No. 2022-006, 9-19-22)
