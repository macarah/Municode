DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 10-351. - Number authorized for county magistrate court.
(a)
The number of full-time magistrates authorized for the Magistrate Court of Fulton County shall be no fewer than nine.
(b)
The number of part-time magistrates authorized for the Magistrate Court of Fulton County shall be no fewer than 22.
(c)
The number of magistrates, full-time and part-time, may be increased from time to time by the majority vote of the judges of the Superior Court of Fulton County and a majority vote of the judges of the State Court of Fulton County; provided, however, that the judges of the Superior Court of Fulton County and the judges of the State Court of Fulton County shall first obtain the approval of the governing authority who shall first approve available appropriated funds for such additional magistrates.
(1983 Ga. Laws (Act No. 431), page 4373, § 1; 1992 Ga. Laws (Act No. 867), page 5733, § 1; 16-0338, 4-20-16)
Sec. 10-352. - Selection of full-time magistrates; vacancies.
(a)
On the effective date of this article, the nine persons holding office as full-time magistrates of the Magistrate Court of Fulton County shall continue to hold office for the balance of the four-year term to which they have been appointed. The chief magistrate shall appoint all other magistrates, full-time and part-time, and shall fill any vacancies in such office through appointment with the approval of a majority vote of the judges of the Superior Court of Fulton County and a majority vote of the judges of the State Court of Fulton County.
(b)
The chief magistrate shall be elected at the nonpartisan general election held immediately preceding the expiration of the term of office and conducted as provided for in O.C.G.A. § 21-2-139.
(c)
A vacancy in the office of chief magistrate shall be filled for the remainder of the unexpired term by a majority vote of the judges of the Superior Court of Fulton County and a majority vote of the judges of the State Court of Fulton County.
(1983 Ga. Laws (Act No. 431), page 4373, § 2; 1992 Ga. Laws (Act No. 867), page 5733, § 2; ; 2013 Ga. Laws (Act No. 283), page 4395, § 1; 16-0338, 4-20-16)
Sec. 10-353. - Part-time magistrates.
(a)
Part-time magistrates shall be compensated on an hourly basis at a base amount that is not less than $50.00 per hour for each hour worked as certified by the chief magistrate to the governing authority; provided however that each part-time magistrate shall receive the minimum monthly salary of not less than $592.58, which salary shall be paid without regard to the number of hours worked and shall be credited against any amount due to such part-time magistrate for hours actually worked. The minimum hourly rate may be applied retroactively to hours certified by the chief magistrate to the governing authority since January 1, 2016, subject to the availability of appropriated funds.
(b)
For purposes of compensation, part-time magistrates may be treated as vendors or part-time, temporary employees. If treated as part-time, temporary employees, such part-time magistrates shall be exempt from over-time pay, shall not be entitled to or participate in any benefits, including any retirement plan, nor shall they be entitled to receive paid holidays, sick leave, or other leave.
(1983 Ga. Laws (Act No. 431), page 4373, § 2A; 1984 Ga. Laws (Act No. 1094), page 4952, § 1; 1996 Ga. Laws (Act No. 892), page 4368, § 1; 2014 Ga. Laws (Act No. 647), page 4286, § 1; 16-0338, 4-20-16)
Sec. 10-354. - Budget oversight.
The court administrator of the Magistrate Court of Fulton County shall be the chief magistrate or his or her designee. The court administrator shall have oversight of the budget of the Magistrate Court of Fulton County. After county funds have been appropriated for the operation of the court, the court administrator shall have the authority to make changes to line item appropriations.
(2015 Ga. Laws (Act No. 284), page 4178, § 1)
Sec. 10-355. - Payroll adjustments.
The Fulton County Finance Department shall make all payroll adjustments related to appointment, transfer, termination, or other personnel action requiring payroll adjustment upon written notification by the court administrator.
(2015 Ga. Laws (Act No. 284), page 4178, § 2)
Sec. 10-356. - Unexpended appropriations.
Any unexpended county appropriated funds remaining with the court at the end of a fiscal year shall lapse to the general fund of Fulton County.
(2015 Ga. Laws (Act No. 284), page 4178, § 3)
Secs. 10-357—10-385. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - JUSTICES OF THE PEACE EMERITUS[10]

--- (10) ---
Editor's note— Notwithstanding the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 15-10-120 that converted justices of the peace into magistrates, the 1992 amendment to 1967 Ga. Laws (Act No. 124), page 2313, § 1 did not reference magistrates.

Sec. 10-386. - Office created; appointment.
In all counties in the State of Georgia having a population of 600,000 or more according to the United States decennial census of 1990 and any future such census, there is hereby created the office of justice of the peace emeritus. Any justice of the peace or notary public ex-officio justice of the peace holding office on January 1, 1967, who has been commissioned for such office for at least four consecutive terms, and who is also a practicing attorney and a member of the State Bar of Georgia, shall be appointed justice of the peace emeritus.
(1967 Ga. Laws (Act No. 124), page 2313, § 1; 1992 Ga. Laws (Act No. 1268), page 2351, § 1)
Sec. 10-387. - Authority.
The authority of such justices of the peace emeritus shall be limited to the following:
(1)
To officiate at and perform marriage upon duly authorized marriage license,
(2)
To administer oaths,
(3)
To attest the execution of written documents,
(4)
To issue warrants returnable to the proper courts,
(5)
To render advice and counsel to any justice of the peace or notary public ex-officio justice of the peace in the county wherein such justice of the peace emeritus was commissioned as a justice of the peace or notary public ex-officio justice of the peace,
(6)
To serve as justices of the peace or notary public ex-officio justice of the peace in any militia district in the county wherein such justice of the peace emeritus has been commissioned as a justice of the peace or notary public ex-officio justice of the peace when directed by the governor to do so,
(7)
To have and use a seal,
(8)
To certify acts performed by him,
(9)
To certify all papers, certificates and documents issued by him as authorized in this section, and
(10)
To charge fees for the performance of any duty or act authorized by this section in the same amount as are provided by law for justices of the peace and notaries public of this state.
(1967 Ga. Laws (Act No. 124), page 2313, § 2)
Sec. 10-388. - Issuance of commissions.
The governor shall be authorized to issue appropriate commissions to any justice of the peace or any notary public ex-officio justice of the peace appointed justice of the peace emeritus.
(1967 Ga. Laws (Act No. 124), page 2313, § 3)
Sec. 10-389. - Dockets; furnishing and maintaining.
Each justice of the peace emeritus appointed pursuant to the provisions of this division shall keep and maintain a docket of all causes brought before him and acts performed by him in the same manner as a justice of the peace. Said dockets shall be furnished by the authority required to furnish dockets for justices of the peace of any such county.
(1967 Ga. Laws (Act No. 124), page 2313, § 4)
Sec. 10-390. - Terms.
Said justices of the peace emeritus shall hold office during good behavior and upon his continuing to be a member in good standing of the State Bar of Georgia.
(1967 Ga. Laws (Act No. 124), page 2313, § 5)
Sec. 10-391. - Compensation limited to fees for services performed.
There shall be no compensation, except the fees authorized herein, attached to said office, or any benefits other than such fees for actual services performed, and no part of such fees shall be chargeable to the state or county.
(1967 Ga. Laws (Act No. 124), page 2313, § 6)
Secs. 10-392—10-420. - Reserved.
