DIVISION 2. - ELECTRICAL

Sec. 8-40. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Dwelling and place of business include buildings of any type of construction without limitation, and shall specifically include mobile homes and manufactured housing units.
Permit issuer means the county official designated by the county board of commissioners to issue the electrical permit as provided in this division.
Provider of electrical service means any utility company or person, firm, corporation, or partnership which sells, furnishes or delivers for sale electrical service within the county.
Purchaser means any person, firm, corporation, partnership, society, club, or association of persons acting together as a unit who purchase or cause to be purchased electrical service within the county.
(Ord. of 9-21-1995, § 1; Ord. of 3-9-2000, § 1)
Sec. 8-41. - Violations and penalties.
A violation of this division is a misdemeanor offense punishable by a fine of no more than $200.00. All alleged violations of this division shall be tried in the magistrate court of the county.
(Ord. of 9-21-1995, § 5)
Sec. 8-42. - County board of tax assessors personnel designated as permit issuers.
Staff personnel of the office of the county board of tax assessors are hereby designated as permit issuers in lieu of the clerk to the county board of commissioners. Such designation shall remain effective unless and until the board of commissioners should designate other personnel to perform such function.
(Ord. of 9-21-1995, § 1; Ord. of 3-9-2000, § 1)
Sec. 8-43. - Electrical permit required; exceptions.
(a)
No person or provider of electrical service shall furnish electricity to any new purchaser, reconnect or re-establish electrical service to any existing purchaser, or furnish additional electrical services to any existing purchaser where new electrical services are being added, until the purchaser obtains a valid electrical permit from the permit issuer of the county. Before the occupation of any premises as a dwelling or place of business, and before the issuance of an electrical permit, the permit issuer shall determine that the purchaser has obtained any permit required by the county board of health and any other permit that may be required by any county building regulation.
(b)
In addition, before the permit issuer can issue an electrical permit to a purchaser who wants electrical service furnished to a mobile home or manufactured housing unit, the purchaser must provide proof to the permit issuer that purchaser has properly registered the mobile home or manufactured housing unit with, and obtained a current year registration decal from, the office of the county tax commissioner.
(c)
The provisions of this section shall not apply to restoration of service by the provider of electrical service to a purchaser who had previously been disconnected due to nonpayment of utility bills or actions of the elements. In addition, the provisions of this section shall not apply to any service site that existed prior to January 1, 1991.
(Ord. of 9-21-1995, § 2; Ord. of 3-9-2000, §§ 2—4)
Sec. 8-44. - Providers to furnish list of electrical service connections.
(a)
All electrical service providers in the county shall provide to the office of the county tax assessor a copy of the list of service connections that such providers are required to provide to the county health department. The list shall be furnished to the tax assessor's office at the same time and in the same manner as required for the copy provided to the county health department except as otherwise provided in this section.
(b)
In addition to the information furnished to the county health department, the electrical service provider shall include in the required list a statement as to whether the electrical service was provided to a mobile home or manufactured housing unit, if such information is available to the electrical service provider.
(Ord. of 9-21-1995, § 7; Ord. of 3-9-2000, § 3)
ARTICLE III. - MANUFACTURED HOMES, TRAILERS, MOBILE HOMES, AND MOBILE HOME PARKS

Sec. 8-50. - Definitions.
Mobile homes, manufactured home, or trailer. These terms are to be considered synonymous and to refer to:
Any structure designed or used for residential occupancy or other use built upon or having a frame(s) or chassis to which wheels may be attached by which it may move or be moved upon a highway, whether or not such structure actually has, at any given time, such wheels attached, including any such vehicle which has been placed on a masonry or other stationary foundation. This definition shall not include travel trailers of recreational vehicles when such vehicles are not used for permanent or semi-permanent living or sleeping quarters.
Mobile home park. Any lot, tract, or parcel of land used, maintained or intended to be used, leased or rented for occupancy by two or more mobile homes together with accessory structures provided in connection therewith. This definition shall not include mobile home sales lots on which unoccupied mobile homes are parked for the purpose of inspection and sale nor shall it include property containing two or more mobile homes occupied by family members of the land owner.
Mobile home, manufactured home, trailer subdivision. All divisions of a tract or parcel whether immediate or future, for sale, legacy or other development, whether partially or in whole, for the accommodation of mobile homes, manufactured homes, and which result in separate ownership by more than one person of the subdivided tract or parcels.
Location Permits. A location permit must be obtained by any individual or business seeking to locate a home within the county as provided by this article, unless otherwise exempted. All manufactured homes over ten years old shall be subject to an inspection prior to the issuance of a location permit unless otherwise exempt.
Inspection official. A person designated by the county board of commissioners to inspect manufactured homes, mobile homes, and trailers.
(Ord. of 12-19-2016)
Sec. 8-51. - Moving or relocating a manufactured home, mobile home or trailer from one location in the county to another.
(a)
Anyone desiring to move or relocate a manufactured home, mobile home or trailer must first submit an application requesting the same to the county board of commissioners' office.
(b)
At the time an application for relocation is submitted, the inspection official shall inspect the manufactured home to determine if the home is suitable for relocation and that the home complies with the minimum health and safety standards set forth;
(c)
Once the inspecting official has inspected the manufactured home he or she shall submit his or her recommendation for the approval or denial for a permit for relocation to the county board of commissioners who shall then vote on the application for the permit.
(d)
No location permit to move or relocate a manufactured home shall be issued unless the following criteria have been met:
(1)
The applicant provides proof that all outstanding property taxes are paid in full at the time of said application;
(2)
The applicant provides a copy of the sewage and septic permit issued by the health inspector;
(3)
The applicant provides the name, contact information, proof of liability insurance and bond of the company that is responsible for relocation the manufactured home;
(4)
The designated inspecting official has determined that the home is intact and structurally sound and capable of being moved to a different location without being severally damaged or destroyed during the move and that the home complies with the minimum health and safety standards set forth herein;
(5)
The county board of commissioners has voted to approve the issuance of the permit.
Failure to meet any of the requirements of this section shall result in denial of an application for location of a manufactured home.
(Ord. of 12-19-2016)
Sec. 8-52. - Permits—Moving/relocating within the county.
The cost of a permit required for moving or relocating a manufactured home, mobile home or trailer in the county shall be $25.00.
All permits issued under the terms of this division shall indicate the date issued. Any permits issued hereunder shall be valid only for the specified manufactured home and for the location of such manufactured home as indicated on the permit.
Nothing contained in this section shall be so construed as to relieve any person from the responsibility of obtaining a relocation permit each time such person moves or relocates a manufactured home.
Permits shall be valid for 60 days.
Moving or relocating a manufactured home in violation of this section shall subject an owner, a retailer, and/or a manufactured home transporter to a fine not to exceed $1,000.00.
(Ord. of 12-19-2016)
Sec. 8-53. - Moving or relocating a manufactured home, mobile home or trailer from outside of Jeff Davis County to a location within the county.
(a)
Anyone desiring to move or relocate a manufactured home, mobile home or trailer from outside Jeff Davis County to within the county must first submit an application requesting the same to the county board of commissioners' office.
(b)
At the time that an application for relocation is sought, no manufactured home that is ten years old or older, as determined by its manufactured date, shall be allowed to relocate within the county and no permit for the same shall be issued if said manufactured home unless the home passes inspection by the inspection official and that the home is deemed habitable by the local health department standards.
(c)
After an application for relocation is completed, the manufactured home at issue must be inspected by the designated county inspecting official to determine if the manufactured home is suitable for relocation and that the home complies with the minimum health and safety standards set forth herein;
(d)
Once the inspecting official has inspected the manufactured home he or she shall submit his or her recommendation for the approval or denial for a permit to the county board of commissioners who shall then vote on the application for the permit.
(e)
No location permit to move or relocate a manufactured home shall be issued unless the following criteria have been met:
(1)
The applicant provides proof that all outstanding property taxes are paid in full at the time of said application;
(2)
The applicant provides a copy of the sewage and septic permit issued by the health inspector;
(3)
The applicant provides the name, contact information, proof of liability insurance and bond of the company that is responsible for relocation of the manufactured home;
(4)
The designated inspecting official has determined that the home is intact and structurally sound and capable of being moved to a different location without being severally damaged or destroyed during the move and that the home complies with the minimum health and safety standards set forth herein;
(5)
The county board of commissioners has voted to approve the issuance of the permit.
Failure to meet any of the requirements of this section shall result in denial of an application for location of a manufactured home.
(Ord. of 12-19-2016)
Sec. 8-54. - Permits—Moving/relocating from outside the county to within the county.
The cost of a permit required for moving or relocating a manufactured home, mobile home or trailer into the county shall be $40.00.
All permits issued under the terms of this division shall indicate the date issued. Any permits issued hereunder shall be valid only for the specified manufactured home and for the location of such manufactured home as indicated on the permit.
Nothing contained in this section shall be so construed as to relieve any person from the responsibility of obtaining a relocation permit each time such person moves or relocates a manufactured home.
Permits shall be valid for 60 days.
Moving or relocating a manufactured home in violation of this section shall subject an owner, a retailer, and/or a manufactured home transporter to a fine not to exceed $1,000.00.
(Ord. of 12-19-2016)
Sec. 8-55. - Abandoned manufactured homes.
(a)
Any manufactured home abandoned by its owner or any manufactured home transporter on private property or public right-of-way in the county shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and subject to a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 and up to 60 days in jail.
(b)
Both the owner and the transporter may be charged with the offense described herein.
(c)
A manufactured home shall be considered abandoned if it is left on a public right-of-way for a period of five days and is not otherwise removed from said right-of-way during that time or if it is left on private property for a period of 180 consecutive days unless the county board of assessors is notified by the owner that the property will be vacant for a period of time.
(d)
If a manufactured home is so abandoned, the county may take all reasonable steps to remove the manufactured home from the private property or right-of-way. The cost associating with said removal shall be chargeable to both the owner and the transporter. In such event, if the sale of the manufactured home is necessary to recover said costs, the county may take all legal avenues available to dispose of said manufactured home, including issuing a fi fa for said cost and selling the manufactured home in the same manner that a manufactured home would be sold to recover for any outstanding ad valorem tax.
(Ord. of 12-19-2016)
Sec. 8-56. - Mobile home park.
Any one desiring to set up a mobile home park in the county must first submit an application for a permit to the county board of commissioners along with a $50.00 application fee.
Once the application has been submitted the inspection official shall then inspect all of the mobile homes that are to be located in the mobile home park.
Once the inspecting official has inspected the manufactured home he or she shall submit his or her recommendation for the approval or denial for a permit to the county board of commissioners who shall then vote on the application for the permit.
If the application is approved the mobile home park owner shall then pay to the county the sum of $100.00 for a mobile home park permit. The permit shall expire at the end of three years. At the expiration of the permit the mobile home park owner must apply for a new permit. If at any time during the term of a permit, the mobile home park owner desires to add an additional mobile home or homes into the park then he or she must comply with the application process as set forth herein for relocating mobile homes into the county.
All mobile home parks shall be classified as commercial property and the owner of said mobile home park shall be taxed per hook up.
All mobile homes located in a mobile home park shall be subject to all of the provisions set forth in this article.
Failure of a mobile home park owner to comply with any of the provisions set forth in this article shall result in a fine of $1,000.00 and up to 60 days in jail and shall be required to remove the homes that are in non-compliance from the county at his or her expense.
(Ord. of 12-19-2016)
Sec. 8-57. - Minimum health and safety standards.
Every pre-owned manufactured home shall be in compliance with the Federal Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards Act 42 U.S.C. 5401-5445 (the HUD Code) and shall not have been altered in such a way that the home no longer meets the HUD requirements.
All floors, interior walls and ceiling shall be in sound condition. Doors and windows shall be operable, watertight and in good working condition.
The exterior of all manufactured homes shall be free of loose or rotting boards and any other condition that may allow rain or moisture to invade the interior of the home. The roof shall be intact and contain no obvious defects that would allow water to seep into the home.
Every plumbing fixture, water and waste pipe of all manufactured homes shall be in a sanitary working condition when properly connected and shall be free from leaks and obstructions. Each home shall contain a kitchen sink, a bathroom, a tub or shower, a toilet and running water.
Electrical systems shall be properly installed and shall be in working condition. Distribution panels shall be in compliance with the approved listing, complete with required breakers with all unused openings covered with solid covers approved and listed for that purpose. Each home shall contain a working hot water heater.
Each bedroom of a manufacture home shall have at least one operating window of sufficient size to allow egress if necessary.
The kitchen shall have at least one operating window or other ventilation device.
Each manufactured home shall contain one operable battery powered smoke detector in each bedroom and in the kitchen, which must be installed in accordance with the manufacturer's recommendations.
(Ord. of 12-19-2016)
Sec. 8-58. - Miscellaneous.
All mobile homes brought into the county shall be set up and anchored.
All mobile homes brought into the county shall have skirting installed no later than 45 days after the mobile home has been set up.
(Ord. of 12-19-2016)
