ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 28-1. - Authority of mayor and council to regulate traffic.
The authority to make and enforce all rules and regulations governing traffic and the use of the streets of the city by vehicles and pedestrians, shall be vested in the mayor and council, and the mayor and council shall have full power to designate directions of traffic, time limits, and locations for parking, reservation of parking places, maximum and minimum speeds insofar as same shall not conflict with the laws of the state, to establish through streets, truck routes, and stop crossings, and to install such street signs and indicators and markings as shall be necessary or advisable for the operation and enforcement of such rules and regulations. No such rule or regulation shall be effective unless and until appropriate signs, indicators or markings are installed.
(Code 1973, § 15-1)
Sec. 28-2. - Uniform traffic act; adoption by reference.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-372 through 40-6-376, the provisions of O.C.G.A. title 40, chapter 6 (O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-1 to 40-6-395) (except for O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-393 through 40-6-394), known as the Uniform Rules of the Road, and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 are adopted as and for the traffic regulations of the city with like effect as if recited herein.
(Code 1973, § 15-2)
Sec. 28-3. - Prohibited parking in defined areas.
(a)
Pursuant to the powers provided under the law to the mayor and council to the city, it is now hereby prohibited and made illegal to park any motor vehicles, any other self-propelled vehicles, any towed or other transported items along State Route 96 from a defined area running from the intersection of Collins Street and State Route 96 to the intersection of Liberty Street and State Route 96.
(b)
Penalty for the violation of this article shall be determined by and in accordance with the Motor Vehicle Act of the state and the same is incorporated herein by reference.
(Code 1973, § 15-3)
Sec. 28-4. - Permits for parades and processions.
No funeral, procession, or parade containing 50 or more persons or five or more vehicles, excepting the Armed Forces of the United States, the military forces of this state, and the forces of the police and fire department, shall occupy, march, or proceed along any street except in accordance with a permit issued by the chief of police and such other regulations as are set forth in this chapter which may apply.
(Code 1973, § 15-4)
Sec. 28-5. - Funeral processions to be identified.
A funeral composed of a procession of vehicles shall be identified as such by the display upon the outside of each vehicle of a pennant of a type designated by the chief of police, or by burning lights.
(Code 1973, § 15-5)
Secs. 28-6—28-28. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - TRAFFIC CONTROL DEVICES

Sec. 28-29. - Authority to place, maintain, etc.—Electric traffic control devices.
As an aid in facilitating the movement of traffic on the streets of the city and in reducing accidents, the chief of police and the city electrician are hereby authorized and directed to place and maintain electric traffic control devices and signals at such locations as may be designated from time to time by resolutions of the commission of the city.
(Code 1973, § 15-6)
Sec. 28-30. - Authority to place, maintain, etc.—Other traffic control devices.
The chief of police of the city shall place and maintain, other than electrically operated, traffic control devices when and as required under this chapter and other traffic ordinances of the city to make effective the provisions of this chapter and such ordinances, and, with the approval of the city manager, any place and maintain such additional traffic control devices as he may deem necessary to regulate traffic under this chapter and other traffic ordinances of the city or under state law, or to guide or warn traffic.
(Code 1973, § 15-7)
Secs. 28-31—28-48. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - OPERATION OF VEHICLES

Sec. 28-49. - Speed restrictions.
(a)
No person shall drive a vehicle on a highway at a speed greater than is reasonable and prudent under the conditions then existing.
(b)
Where no special hazard exists, the following speeds shall be lawful, but any speed in excess of such a limit shall be prima facie evidence that the speed is not reasonable or prudent and is unlawful.
(1)
Fifteen miles per hour in passing hospitals and in passing schools while schools are in session and in passing churches during the hours of service therein.
(2)
Twenty-five miles per hour within the corporate limits, except as herein otherwise provided.
(3)
Thirty-five miles per hour within the zones designated, and when signs are posted at either end of a speed zone.
(c)
The fact that the speed of a vehicle is lower than the foregoing prima facie limits shall not relieve the driver from the duty to decrease speed when approaching and crossing an intersection, when approaching a hill crest, when traveling upon any narrow or winding roadway, or when a special hazard exists with respect to pedestrians or other traffic or by reason of weather or highway conditions, and speed shall be decreased as may be necessary to avoid colliding with any person, vehicle, or other conveyance on or entering the highway in compliance with legal requirements and the duty of all persons to use due care.
(Code 1973, § 15-8)
Secs. 28-50—28-71. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - STOPPING, STANDING AND PARKING

Sec. 28-72. - Parking lines; angle and parallel parking.
On streets where white lines are shown for guidance, parking must conform with such lines. The driver must park his vehicle entirely within the boundaries designated by such lines. No person shall park and leave unattended a vehicle in a roadway other than parallel with the edge of the roadway, headed in the direction of traffic, and with the curb-side wheels of the vehicle within 12 inches of the edge of the roadway, except upon those streets which have been marked or signed for angle parking and on such streets vehicles shall be parked at the angle to the edge of the roadway indicated by such marks or signs.
(Code 1973, § 15-9)
Secs. 28-73—28-102. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - BICYCLES

Sec. 28-103. - Persons riding bicycles to obey traffic regulations.
Every person riding a bicycle shall be subject to the provision of this chapter applicable to the driver of any vehicle except those provisions of such chapter which by their very nature can have no application.
(Code 1973, § 15-10)
Sec. 28-104. - Clinging to moving vehicle.
Any person riding upon a bicycle shall not attach the same or himself to any moving vehicle upon any roadway.
(Code 1973, § 15-11)
Sec. 28-105. - Carrying extra passengers on bicycles.
The operator of a bicycle shall not carry any other person upon the handlebars, frame or tank, nor shall any other person ride upon such vehicle unless riding in an extra seat provided for each extra person.
(Code 1973, § 15-12)
Sec. 28-106. - Speed.
It shall be unlawful for any person to ride any bicycle at an unusual speed, or in a disorderly manner, or as to endanger the life or limb of another.
(Code 1973, § 15-13)
Sec. 28-107. - Penalties.
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person convicted of a violation of any provisions of this article shall be punished by a fine of not more than $500.00 or by imprisonment for not more than 30 days or by both such fine and imprisonment.
(Code 1973, § 15-15)
Sec. 28-108. - Repeal.
The existing ordinances covering the same matters as embraced in this article are hereby repealed and all ordinances or parts of ordinances inconsistent with the provisions of this article are hereby repealed.
(Code 1973, § 15-16)
Secs. 28-109—28-133. - Reserved.
ARTICLE VI. - COURT COSTS

Sec. 28-134. - Costs assessed on fines imposed by municipal court.
Any person against whom a fine is imposed by the municipal court of the city for the violation of any of the laws or ordinances of the city or of the laws of the state shall be subject to additional costs in the amount of $10.00 per violation to be used for the improvement or enhancement of information technology of the department of police.
(Ord. of 11-21-2005, § 15.20)
