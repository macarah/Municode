ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 11B-1. - Title.
This chapter shall be known and cited as the "Flea Market Ordinance for Columbus, Georgia."
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 101, 11-17-87)
Sec. 11B-2. - Purpose; function.
(a)
This chapter is adopted in order to promote the health, safety, morals and general welfare of the inhabitants of Columbus, Georgia, by regulating the location, design and maintenance of flea markets.
(b)
This chapter establishes minimum standards for flea markets.
(Ord. No. 87-159, §§ 102, 103, 11-17-87)
Sec. 11B-3. - Definitions.
(a)
General. Unless the context of this chapter requires, the following definitions shall be used in the interpretation and administration of the chapter. Words used in the present tense include the future; the singular number shall include the plural, and the plural the singular; the word "structure" shall include "arranged", "designed", "constructed", "altered", "converted", "rented", "leased", or "intended to be used"; and the word "shall" is mandatory and not optional.
(b)
Definitions.
Accessory Structure. Any structural addition which includes awnings, cabanas, carports, Florida rooms, porches, ramadas, storage cabinets, and similar appurtenant structures.
Building. A roofed structure erected for permanent use.
Building inspector. The director of the inspections and code enforcement division of Columbus, Georgia.
Booth. A structure constructed for the purpose of displaying and selling merchandise.
Common area. Any area or space designed for joint use of tenants occupying flea markets.
Density. The number of flea market booths per gross acre.
Easement. A vested or acquired right to use land, other than as a tenant, for a specific purpose; such right being held by someone other than that of the owner who holds title to the land.
Enforcing agency. The director of the inspections and code enforcement division of Columbus, Georgia, charged by law with the duty to enforce the provisions of this chapter.
Flea market. An activity where stalls or spaces within the bounds of a specific location are made available to the vendor of handicraft items or the vendors of new merchandise where each new item does not have a price in excess of $50.00 or to vendors of secondhand items; all such vendors may not be manufacturers or the holders of any other retail license for the sale of similar merchandise.
Flea market stand. That part of an individual lot which has been reserved for the placement of flea market booths, appurtenant structures or additions.
Governing body. The council of Columbus, Georgia.
Lot line. The line bounding the lot as shown on the accepted plot plan.
Market management. The person who owns or has charge, care or control of the flea market.
Permit. A written document issued by the inspections and code enforcement division allowing a person to locate a flea market or to construct, alter or extend a flea market under the provisions of this chapter.
Person. Any individual, firm, trust, partnership, public or private association or corporation, or other entity.
Private street. A private way which affords principal means or access to individual flea market stands and auxiliary buildings.
Public street. A private way which affords principal means of access to abutting properties.
Rights-of-way. The area, either public or private, over which the right of passage exists.
Service building. A structure housing sanitary, operational, office, recreational, maintenance and other facilities built to conform to required standards.
Sewer connection. Pipes, fittings and appurtenances from the drain outlet of the flea market stand and the inlet of the corresponding sewer riser pipe of the sewer system serving the flea market.
Sewer riser pipe. That portion of the sewer lateral which extends vertically to the ground elevation and terminates at a designated point at each flea market stand.
Water connection. All pipes, fittings and appurtenances from the water riser pipe to the water inlet pipe of the distribution system within the flea market.
Water riser pipe. The water riser pipe is that portion of the water service pipe which extends vertically to the ground elevation and terminates at a designated point at each flea market stand.
Water service pipe. The water service pipe consists of all pipes, fittings, values and appurtenances from the water main of the flea market distribution system to the water outlet of the distribution system within the flea market stand.
(Ord. No. 87-159, §§ 201, 202, 11-17-87)
Sec. 11B-4. - Variances.
Where, because of topographical or other conditions peculiar to the site, strict adherence to the provisions of this chapter would cause an unnecessary hardship, the planning division may authorize a variance, if such variance can be made without destroying the intent of these regulations. In granting variances, the planning division may impose such conditions as will, in its judgment, secure substantially the objective of the standards of requirements so varied.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 604, 11-17-87)
Sec. 11B-5. - Conflict of ordinances; effect of partial invalidity.
In any case where a provision of this chapter is found to be in conflict with a provision of any other ordinance or code of the municipality, the provision which establishes the higher standard shall prevail.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 602, 11-17-87)
Sec. 11B-6. - Penalties for violation.
Any person who violates any provisions of this chapter shall, upon conviction, be punished as set forth in section 1-8 of this Code; and each day's failure of compliance with any such provision shall constitute a separate violation.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 601, 11-17-87)
Secs. 11B-7—11B-20. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - PERMIT

Sec. 11B-21. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to locate a flea market or to construct, alter or extend any flea market within the limits of the city, unless he holds a valid permit issued by the inspections and code enforcement division. Construction must begin within 180 days after issuance of the permit.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 301, 11-17-87)
Sec. 11B-22. - Applications.
All applications for permits for flea markets shall be submitted to the inspections and code enforcement division, after submission and approval of the final site plan by the planning division.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 302, 11-17-87)
Sec. 11B-23. - Flea market site plan layout; approval.
(a)
Generally; required information. A flea market site plan layout shall be drawn at a scale no smaller than 100 feet to one inch and shall show the following information.
(1)
Five black or blue line prints of the final site plan or before the first working day of the month shall be submitted to the planning division and shall contain the following information:
a.
Name and address of applicant; if the applicant is a partnership, the names and addresses of the partners; if the applicant is a corporation, the names and addresses of the officers and directors.
b.
Name and address of owner of land.
c.
Location (vicinity map) and legal description of the flea market.
d.
Scale of one inch equals 100 feet.
e.
North arrow.
f.
The area and dimensions of the tract of land.
g.
The number, location and size of all flea market stands.
h.
The location, width and area of roadways, parkways, streets, driveways and walks.
i.
The location of service buildings and any other proposed structures.
j.
Location and design of parking spaces.
k.
Gross density.
(2)
Review and comment of the flea market site plan layout shall be noted in a report of the planning division and on all five prints as submitted.
(3)
Approval of the final site plan shall be noted on all five prints and attested to by the signature of the director of the planning division. Three prints shall be returned to the applicant, and two prints retained in the files of the planning division.
(b)
Requested reports; contents. Before acting on the flea market site plan layout, the planning division may request a report from any person or agency directly concerned with the proposed development, such as the district highway engineer, and public utilities. Such reports shall certify compliance with or note deviations from the requirement of this chapter, and include comments on other factors which bear upon the public interest. The responsibility of securing said reports is that of the planning division staff.
(c)
Application for permit to construct flea market; plans. Any time after approval of the final site plan by the planning division, application may be made to the inspections and code enforcement division for a permit for the construction of the flea market. Five complete sets of construction plans shall be submitted to the inspections and code enforcement division with 1 set to be returned to the planning division upon issuance of a permit containing the following:
(1)
The approved final site plan.
(2)
Location of water and sewer lines and user pipes and manholes.
(3)
Plans and specifications of the water supply, sewer lines, storm drainage system, refuse and sewerage disposal facilities.
(4)
Plans and specifications of all buildings to be constructed, altered or extended within the flea market.
(5)
The location of details of lighting and electrical systems.
(d)
Review of application; issuance of construction permit. Upon review of the application, and subject to evidence that the flea market meets the minimum requirements of the Georgia Department of Health by the Columbus department of public health, the inspections and code enforcement division shall issue a permit when a review of the application and inspection of the site demonstrates that the proposed or existing flea market meets the requirements of this chapter.
(e)
Procedure for site plan layout approval. The procedure for obtaining site plan layout approval of a flea market is as follows:
(1)
All plats submitted to the planning division in acceptable order on or before the first working day of the month will be reviewed and action taken no later than the 15th day of the same month.
(2)
All flea market site plan layouts submitted to the planning division in acceptable order on or before the 15th day of each month will be reviewed and action taken no later than the first working day of the following month.
Please note: When a first or 15th day of the month falls on a weekend or holiday, preliminary site plans may be submitted on the following work day.
a.
At least five black or blue line prints of the site plan layout prepared in accordance with the requirements of these regulations shall be submitted.
b.
Review, comment and/or approval of the site plan shall be made by the director of the planning division, the review, comment and/or approval shall be noted on all copies of the site plan as submitted. Four prints shall be returned to the applicant and one print retained by the planning division.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 302.1, 11-17-87)
Cross reference— Buildings, Ch. 8; streets and sidewalks, Ch. 18; subdivisions, Ch. 19; site plan, tree protection, § 20A-8; Zoning, Ch. 22; mapped streets requirements, § 22-29; planning advisory commission, § 22-38.
Sec. 11B-24. - Inspections.
(a)
The inspections and code enforcement division is hereby authorized and directed to make such inspections as are necessary to determine satisfactory compliance with this chapter.
(b)
The inspections and code enforcement division shall have the power to enter at reasonable times upon any private or public property for the purpose of inspecting and investigating conditions relating to enforcement of this chapter.
(c)
The inspections and code enforcement division may request inspections by and verifications of compliance from the department of public health relating to any of the rules and regulations of state and/or local health department. Representatives of the department of public health shall have the same powers of entry and inspection as provided for the inspections and code enforcement division.
(d)
The health officer's representative shall notify the inspections and code enforcement division of such violations and requirements for corrections.
Cross reference— Health and sanitation, Ch. 13.
Sec. 11B-25. - Revocation.
Any permit for a flea market may be revoked when it is found to be in violation of this chapter.
(1)
Should the inspections and code enforcement division find that conditions exist which are in violation of any provision of this chapter, shall give notice to the permittee or owner that unless such conditions or practices are corrected within 30 days, the permit will be revoked.
(2)
If at the end of 30 days, a further inspection reveals that the conditions or practices have not been corrected, the inspections and code enforcement division shall then revoke the permit and give notice of such suspension in writing to the permittee or owner. Upon notice of revocation, the permittee or owner shall cease operation and/or construction of the flea market.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 304, 11-17-87)
Sec. 11B-26. - Inspections to determine compliance of existing flea markets; exemption.
Upon enactment of this chapter all existing flea markets shall be inspected by the inspections and code enforcement division, to determine compliance or noncompliance with the terms as set forth within. All flea markets which have less than one acre of land will be exempt from this requirement, but shall be required to come into compliance with all other provisions of this chapter within 12 months from the date of the inspection.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 305, 11-17-87)
Sec. 11B-27. - Appeals.
(a)
Petition. Any person affected by any notice which has been issued in connection with the enforcement of any provision of this chapter relating to flea markets, or of any regulation adopted pursuant thereto, may request and shall be granted a hearing on the matter before the board of zoning appeals, provided that such person shall file in the office of the inspections and code enforcement division a written statement of the grounds of such request. The filing of the request for a hearing shall operate as a stay of the notice and suspension except in the case of an order issued under section 11B-28. Upon receipt of such request, the board of zoning appeals shall set a time and place for such hearing and shall give the petitioner written notice thereof.
(b)
Hearing. At such hearing, the petitioner shall be given an opportunity to be heard and to show why such notice should be modified or withdrawn.
(c)
Order of board of zoning appeals. After such hearing the board of zoning appeals shall make findings as to compliance with the provisions of this chapter and regulations issued thereunder and shall issue an order sustaining, modifying or withdrawing the notice. Upon failure to comply with any order sustaining or modifying a notice, the permit of the flea market affected by the order shall be revoked.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 306, 11-17-87)
Cross reference— Board of zoning appeals, § 22-37.
Sec. 11B-28. - Emergencies.
(a)
Whenever the director of the inspections and code enforcement division finds that an emergency exists which requires immediate action to protect the public health, including violations of any of the applicable rules and regulations of the department of public health or Georgia Department of Public Health, he may, without notice or hearing, issue an order reciting the existing of such an emergency and requiring that such action be taken as he may deem necessary to meet the emergency including the suspension of the permit or license. Notwithstanding any other provisions of this chapter, such order shall be effective immediately. Any person to whom such an order is directed shall comply therewith immediately, but upon petition to the board of zoning appeals, he shall be afforded a hearing as soon as possible.
(b)
The provisions of section 11B-27 shall be applicable to such hearing and the order issued thereafter.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 307, 11-17-87)
Secs. 11B-29—11B-40. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - TECHNICAL STANDARDS[2]

Sec. 11B-41. - Minimum size of flea markets.
The minimum size of a flea market shall be one acre.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 401, 11-17-87)
Sec. 11B-42. - Density.
The maximum number of flea market booths per gross acre exclusive of off street parking requirements.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 402, 11-17-87)
Sec. 11B-43. - Flea market booths generally.
(a)
Flea market booths shall be located on individual stands.
(b)
Each booth shall contain not more than 100 square feet of area.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 403, 11-17-87)
Sec. 11B-44. - Site improvement requirements.
(a)
Generally. Condition of soil, ground water level, drainage and topography shall not create hazards to the property or the health or safety of the occupants. The site shall not be exposed to objectionable smoke, noise, odors or other adverse influences, and no portion subject to unpredictable and/or sudden flooding, subsidence or erosion shall be used for any purpose which would expose persons or property to hazards.
(b)
Soil and ground cover. Exposed ground surfaces in all parts of every flea market shall be paved, or covered with stone screenings, or other solid material, or protected with a vegetative growth that is capable of preventing soil erosion and of eliminating objectionable dust and/or mud.
(c)
Site drainage requirements. The ground surface in all parts of every flea market shall be graded and equipped to drain all surface water in a safe, efficient manner in order to eliminate water pockets, low areas or bogs.
(d)
Separation between flea market booths. Flea market booths shall be separated from each other and from other buildings by at least ten feet of open space.
(e)
Setbacks, buffer strips, screening.
(1)
All flea markets shall be located at least 25 feet from any public street or highway right-of-way and at least ten feet from the exterior property boundary lines.
(2)
All flea markets adjacent to residential uses shall be provided with screening of a well-maintained opaque fence(s) along the boundary line separating the market and adjacent zoning and uses. Such screening shall be a minimum of six feet in height.
(f)
Street system.
(1)
General requirements. All flea markets shall be provided with safe and convenient vehicular access from abutting public streets or roads or highways.
(2)
Access. Access to flea markets shall be designed to minimize congestion and hazards at the entrance or exit and allow free movement of traffic on adjacent streets. The entrance road connecting the market streets with a public street or road shall have a minimum road pavement width of 36 feet where parking is permitted on both sides, or a minimum road pavement width of 27 feet where parking is limited to one side.
(g)
Off-street parking. Off-street parking areas shall be provided in all flea markets. Such areas shall be furnished at the rate of five and one-half spaces per 1,000 square feet of gross sales area.
(h)
Walks, general requirements. All flea markets shall be provided with safe, convenient, all-season, dust free, pedestrian access of adequate width for intended use and durable and convenient to maintain. Sudden changes in alignment and gradient shall be avoided.
(i)
Flea market stands.
(1)
The flea market stand shall be constructed of materials which will provide an adequate foundation for the placement of flea market booths, thereby securing the superstructure against uplift, sliding, rotation and overturning.
(2)
The flea market stands shall not heave, shift or settle unevenly under the weight of the flea market booth due to inadequate drainage, vibration or other forces acting on the superstructure.
(j)
Identification. Each booth shall be numbered. Each number shall be easily identified from the nearest street or driveway.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 404, 11-17-87)
Cross reference— Streets and sidewalks, Ch. 18; traffic, Ch. 20; off-street parking requirements, § 22-32.
Sec. 11B-45. - Water supply requirements.
(a)
Generally. An accessible, adequate, safe and potable supply of water shall be provided in each flea market. Where a public supply of water of satisfactory quantity, quality and pressure is available, connection shall be made thereto and its supply used exclusively. When a satisfactory public water supply is not available, a private water supply system may be developed and used as approved by the Georgia Department of Public Health in accordance with rules and regulations for water supply quality control.
(b)
Compliance with plumbing code. The water supply system of the flea market shall be constructed and connected in accordance with the plumbing code of the city.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 405, 11-17-87)
Cross reference— Plumbing code, § 8-11.1 et seq.; water, § 13-351 et seq.
Sec. 11B-46. - Restroom and sewage disposal.
(a)
There shall be a minimum of one central restroom facility located on the premises. Within that central restroom there shall be separate facilities for men and women.
(b)
Hot and cold water shall be furnished to every lavatory and sink; cold water shall be furnished to every water closet and urinal.
(c)
Restrooms shall contain the minimum of fixtures as required by the Standard Plumbing Code.
(d)
An adequate and safe sewage system shall be provided in all flea markets for conveying and disposing of all sewage. Such system and connection shall be designed, constructed and maintained in accordance with the provisions of the plumbing code of the city, and the approval of the Georgia Department of Public Health and Georgia Water Quality Control Board.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 406, 11-17-87)
Cross reference— Plumbing code, § 18-11.1 et seq.; sewers, § 13-173 et seq.
Sec. 11B-47. - Electrical distribution system.
Every flea market shall contain an electrical wiring system consisting of wiring, fixtures, equipment and appurtenances for each flea market stand, which shall be installed and maintained in accordance with applicable electric codes of the city.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 407, 11-17-87)
Cross reference— Electrical code, 8-17 et seq.
Sec. 11B-48. - Structural requirements.
All structures to include booths shall comply with the building code of the city. All materials used shall be grade marked lumber, as specified by Standard Building Code.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 408, 11-17-87)
Sec. 11B-49. - Fireplaces, etc.
Cooking shelters, barbecue pits, fireplaces and woodburning stoves shall be so located, constructed, maintained and used as to avoid fire hazards and smoke nuisance, both on the property on which used and on neighboring property. No open fire shall be permitted except in facilities provided. No open fire shall be left unattended. No fuel shall be used and no material burned which emits dense smoke or objectionable odors. No refuse shall be burned at any time.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 408.1, 11-17-87)
Cross reference— Fire protection generally, § 11-1 et seq.; fire prevention code, § 11-21 et seq.; nuisances, § 13-98 et seq.
Sec. 11B-50. - Refuse handling.
(a)
Generally. The storage, collection and disposal of refuse in the flea markets shall be so conducted as to create no health hazards, rodent harborage, insect breeding areas, accident or fire hazards or air pollution.
(b)
Containers. All refuse shall be stored in flytight, watertight, rodent-proof containers provided by the flea market park operator for each flea market stand in sufficient number and capacity to properly store all refuse. The containers may be placed at curbside for collection only on days of scheduled collection. At other times containers shall be located in a protected manner and restricted from general view.
(c)
Refuse collection stands. Where refuse collection stands are provided for common or joint use, such stands shall be located not more than 150 feet from using flea market stand, and shall be so designed as to prevent containers from being tipped, so minimize spillage and container deterioration and to facilitate cleaning around them.
(d)
Bulk waste containers. Where bulk waste containers of three cubic yards are utilized, a suitable concrete pad shall be provided for parking the container, supplied with a drain connected to sewage disposal facilities and available hot and cold water connections convenient for washing the container.
(e)
Collection; transportation. All refuse containing garbage shall be collected at least twice weekly. Where suitable collection service is not available from municipal or private agencies, the flea market operator shall provide this service. All refuse shall be collected and transported in covered vehicles or covered containers.
(f)
City disposal site. Where municipal or private collection service is not available, the flea market operator shall dispose of the refuse by transporting to the city disposal site.
(g)
Refuse incinerators. Refuse incinerators and their operation shall comply with provisions of the air quality standards of Georgia Department of Public Health, and shall be operated only when attended by some person specifically authorized by the owner or operator of the flea market.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 409, 11-17-87)
Cross reference— Solid waste collection and disposal, § 13-167 et seq.
Sec. 11B-51. - Insect and rodent control.
(a)
Extermination. Grounds, buildings and structures shall be maintained free of insect and rodent harborage and infestation. Extermination methods and other measures to control insects and rodents shall conform with the requirements of the health authority.
(b)
Accumulations of debris. Flea markets shall be maintained free of accumulations of debris which may provide rodent harborage or breeding places for flies, mosquitoes and other pests.
(c)
Storage areas. Storage areas shall be so maintained as to prevent rodent harborage; lumber, pipe and other building materials shall be stored at least one foot above the ground.
(d)
Exterior openings. Where the potential for insect and rodent infestation exists, all exterior openings in or beneath any structure shall be appropriately screened with wire mesh or other suitable materials.
(e)
Growth of weeds, etc. to be controlled. The growth of brush, weeds and grass shall be controlled to prevent harborage of ticks, chiggers and other noxious insects. Flea markets shall be so maintained as to prevent the growth of ragweed, poison ivy, poison oak, poison sumac and other noxious weeds considered detrimental to health. Open areas shall be maintained free of heavy undergrowth of any description.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 410, 11-17-87)
Cross reference— Prevention and control of mosquitoes and rodents, § 13-112; growth of weeds, § 13-113.
Sec. 11B-52. - Fuel supply and storage.
(a)
Natural gas system. Natural gas piping systems shall be installed and maintained in accordance with applicable gas codes of the city.
(b)
Liquified petroleum gas systems. Liquefied petroleum gas systems shall be installed and maintained in accordance with applicable gas codes of the city.
(c)
Fuel oil supply system. All fuel oil supply systems shall be installed and maintained in accordance with applicable gas codes of the city.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 411, 11-17-87)
Cross reference— Gas code, § 8-15 et seq.
Sec. 11B-53. - Fire protection.
(a)
The flea markets shall be subject to fire prevention ordinances adopted by the Council of Columbus.
(b)
Flea markets shall be kept free of litter, rubbish and other flammable materials.
(c)
Portable fire extinguishers of a type approved by the fire prevention bureau shall be kept in service buildings and at all other locations designated by the fire protection bureau and shall be maintained in good operating condition.
(d)
Fires shall be made only in stoves, incinerators and other equipment intended for such purposes.
(e)
Fire hydrants shall be installed as required by fire department and approved by the department of utilities.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 412, 11-17-87)
Cross reference— Fire protection generally, § 11-1 et seq.; fire prevention code, § 11-21 et seq.
Secs. 11B-54—11B-60. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - REQUIREMENTS FOR OPERATION

Sec. 11B-61. - Responsibilities of flea market management.
(a)
Compliance required. The person to whom a permit for a flea market is issued shall operate a flea market in compliance with this chapter and regulations issued hereunder and shall provide adequate supervision to maintain the park [market], its facilities and equipment in good repair and in a clean and sanitary condition.
(b)
Notification of tenants. The flea market management shall notify market tenants of all applicable provisions of this chapter and inform them of their duties and responsibilities under this chapter and regulations issued hereunder.
(c)
Placement of booths. The flea market management shall supervise the placement of each flea market booth on its stand which includes securing its stability and installing all utility connections.
(d)
Notification of communicable or contagious disease. The flea market management shall notify the department of public health immediately of any suspected communicable or contagious disease within the flea market.
(Ord. No. 87-159, § 501, 11-17-87)
