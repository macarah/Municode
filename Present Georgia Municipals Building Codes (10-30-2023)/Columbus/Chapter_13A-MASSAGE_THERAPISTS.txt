Sec. 13A-1. - Definition.
A person who applies manual or mechanical massage or acupressure or any similar treatment to the human trunk or limbs shall be deemed within the terms of this chapter as massage therapist engaged in massage therapy.
(Ord. No. 98-87, § 1, 9-8-98)
Sec. 13A-2. - License required, application; information required.
(a)
Any person, sole proprietor, partnership, corporation, or any other form of business organization desiring to engage in the business, trade, or profession of massage therapy shall before engaging in such business, trade, or profession, file an application for a license addressed to the finance director. Such application shall be on a form approved by the finance director and shall set forth the following:
(1)
Name and address of applicant;
(2)
Name and address of any person, firm, corporation, or partnership having previously employed the applicant for the previous two years;
(3)
If the applicant is a corporation or partnership, the address or addresses of such corporation or partnership as well as the names and addresses of the officers of the corporation and partners of the partnership;
(4)
A copy of certification of the applicant's completion of a course of study which consisted of a curriculum of physical culture, massage, hydrotherapy, electrotherapy, or hygiene of not less than 500 hours at a school accredited by one of the following organizations or its successors:
(i)
Accrediting Commission of Career Schools and Colleges of Technology (ACCSCT).
(ii)
Accrediting Council for Continuing Education & Training (ACCET).
(iii)
Commission on Massage Therapy Accreditation (COMTA).
(iv)
Integrative Massage & Somatic Therapies Accreditation (IMSTAC).
Or, alternatively a copy of certification issued by the National Certification Board for Therapeutic Massage and Bodywork. If the applicant is a corporation or partnership, a copy of either of these certifications awarded to the employee or agent engaged in the practice of massage therapy must be provided with the application.
(5)
Any other information which is determined to be required in order to assess the eligibility of the applicant for licensure as a massage therapist practitioner.
(b)
The finance department shall forward the application to the Columbus Police Department for a criminal record check to determine if applicant qualifies pursuant to the provisions of 13A-6(3) and 13A-6(4).
(c)
Within five days of receipt the chief of police shall provide the finance department with pertinent criminal records of the applicant or a statement certifying that the applicant has no criminal record which would constitute a reason for denial of the application.
(d)
The finance department will inform the applicant of either approval or denial of application within 15 working days of the original filing date of the application.
(Ord. No. 98-87, § 1, 9-8-98; Ord. No. 03-18, § 1, 3-25-03)
Sec. 13A-3. - Issuance of license.
If the application is submitted in proper form and the applicant is found to be eligible for licensure pursuant to section 13A-6, the occupation tax section of the department of finance shall issue a business license to such applicant as provided in section 19-36.
(Ord. No. 98-87, § 1, 9-8-98)
Sec. 13A-4. - Denial of application; notice and right of appeal.
If an applicant is found to be ineligible for licensure pursuant to section 13A-6, the occupation tax section of the finance department shall recommend to the finance director that the application be denied. Upon receipt of such a recommendation the finance director shall review the application and any other information which may be provided by the occupation tax section. If the finance director determines that the applicant is ineligible for licensure pursuant to section 13A-6 the application shall be denied and the applicant shall be notified in writing of the reasons for such action.
(1)
Any applicant whose application for a massage therapy license is denied may appeal the denial to the Columbus council at any time within 30 days after the date of the denial. An applicant wishing to appeal a denial pursuant to this section shall request an appeal hearing in writing to the clerk of council, stating the reasons the denial should not have been issued.
(2)
Upon receipt of such a request the clerk of council shall schedule an appeal hearing before council and shall deliver written notification to the applicant and the finance director concerning the time the appeal will be heard by the council.
(3)
At the time scheduled by the clerk of council the council shall consider the appeal of the applicant, but no license shall be approved by council unless the applicant can show to the satisfaction of a majority of the members of council that there has been a mistake of fact concerning the applicant's ability to satisfy the criteria for licensure specified herein.
(Ord. No. 98-87, § 1, 9-8-98)
Sec. 13A-5. - Revocation of license.
The license granted hereunder shall be subject to revocation for any of the reasons specified in section 13A-6. Whenever in the opinion of the chief of police or the finance director there is cause to revoke such license, the following procedures shall apply:
(1)
The chief of police or finance director shall notify the clerk of council in writing of the recommendation to seek revocation of the license and the reasons therefor.
(2)
The clerk of council shall give written notice to the holder of the license, the chief of police and the finance director at least two weeks in advance that the council will hear a recommendation that the license be revoked. This notice shall include date and time of the hearing and the specific reasons for which this recommendation to council will be made.
(3)
The Council of Columbus, Georgia shall hear the recommendation for revocation at one of its regular meetings at which time the holder of the license and any other interested parties will be heard.
(4)
No license shall be revoked until a majority of the council so decides at the time of or subsequent to the hearing on the recommendation for revocation.
(Ord. No. 98-87, § 1, 9-8-98)
Sec. 13A-6. - Grounds for revocation or denial of license.
Occurrence of any of the following shall be considered grounds for denial of an application or revocation of such licenses:
(1)
Failure of the license holder to maintain initial qualifications for obtaining the license under this chapter.
(2)
Operation of the business of a massage therapist under a false or assumed name, or is impersonating another practitioner licensed under this chapter.
(3)
Conviction of or a plea of guilty or nolo contendere or forfeiture of a bond for any state or federal felony by the applicant at any time within five years preceding the date of application, or by a license holder at any time.
(4)
Conviction of or a plea of guilty or nolo contendere or forfeiture of a bond for a state or federal misdemeanor violation of any laws relating to prostitution, public indecency, masturbation for hire, pimping, pandering, solicitation of sodomy, distributing obscene materials or the giving of massages in places used for lewdness or prostitution by the applicant at any time within two years preceding the date of application, or by a license holder at any time.
(5)
The inclusion of materially false information or the attempt by an applicant or license holder to deliberately falsify information contained in an application or license renewal request.
(6)
Violations by the license holder of any state law prohibiting false or fraudulent statements in advertising or the unauthorized practice by the license holder of any other profession licensed by the state.
(7)
Repeated violations of city ordinances by the license holder as set forth in section 19-20 of the Columbus Code.
(Ord. No. 98-87, § 1, 9-8-98)
Sec. 13A-7. - Records required to be kept.
It shall be the duty of any person so granted a license to maintain correct and accurate records of the names and addresses of the persons receiving treatment at said establishment, the type of treatment administered, and the name of the employee of the establishment administering said treatment. Said records shall be subject to inspection at any time by any member of the police department or finance department of Columbus.
(Ord. No. 98-87, § 1, 9-8-98)
Sec. 13A-8. - Practitioner certificate required, application; information required.
(a)
Any man or woman desiring to engage in the practice of massage therapist, whether for a business licensed as a massage therapy business as provided herein or for any other business shall, before engaging in such practice, file an application for a massage therapist practitioner's certificate addressed to the director of finance of the city. Such application shall be on a form approved by the finance director and shall set forth the following:
(1)
Name and address of practitioner;
(2)
Name and address of any person, firm, corporation, or partnership having previously employed the practitioner for the previous two years;
(3)
A copy of certification of the applicant's (practitioner) completion of a course of study which consisted of a curriculum of physical culture, massage, hydrotherapy, electrotherapy, or hygiene of not less than 500 hours at a school accredited by one of the following organizations or its successors:
(i)
Accrediting Commission of Career Schools and Colleges of Technology (ACCSCT).
(ii)
Accrediting Council for Continuing Education & Training (ACCET).
(iii)
Commission on Massage Therapy Accreditation (COMTA).
(iv)
Integrative Massage & Somatic Therapies Accreditation (IMSTAC).
Or, alternatively a copy of certification issued by the National Certification Board for Therapeutic Massage and Bodywork. If the applicant is a corporation or partnership, a copy of either of these certifications awarded to the employee or agent engaged in the practice of massage therapy must be provided with the application.
(4)
Any other information which is determined to be required in order to assess the eligibility of the applicant for certification as a massage therapist practitioner.
(b)
The finance department shall forward the application to the Columbus Police Department for a criminal record check to determine if the practitioner qualifies under the provisions of section 13A-11(3) and section 13A-11(4).
(c)
Within five days of receipt the chief of police shall provide the finance department with pertinent criminal records of the applicant or a statement certifying that the applicant has no criminal record which would constitute a reason for denial of the application.
(d)
The finance department will inform the applicant of either approval or denial of application within 15 working days of the original filing date of the application.
(e)
It shall be the duty of each person who receives a massage therapy practitioner's certificate to file with the finance department changes of the practitioner's home address, home telephone number, and location of practice.
(Ord. No. 98-87, § 1, 9-8-98; Ord. No. 03-18, § 2, 3-25-03)
Sec. 13A-9. - Issuance of certificate; certificate displayed.
If the application is submitted in proper form and can be approved, then the occupation tax section of the department of finance is authorized to issue a certificate to such practitioner. The certificate is to be prominently displayed where the massage therapist practices and will be subject to inspection by any member of the finance department, special enforcement division, or Columbus Police Department.
(Ord. No. 98-87, § 1, 9-8-98)
Sec. 13A-10. - Revocation of certificate; procedures.
The certificate granted hereunder shall be subject to revocation for cause. Whenever, in the opinion of the chief of police or finance director, there is cause to revoke the certificate, the following procedures shall apply:
(1)
Written notice shall be given to the practitioner at least two weeks in advance that the Council of Columbus, Georgia will hear a recommendation that the certificate be revoked and this notice shall include the specific ground or grounds in which this recommendation to council will be made.
(2)
The clerk of council shall so notify in writing the practitioner, the director of finance, and the chief of police as to the time a recommendation for revocation will be heard by the council.
(3)
The Council of Columbus, Georgia shall hear a recommendation for revocation at one of its regular meetings at which time the practitioner and any other interested parties will be heard.
(4)
No certificate shall be revoked until a majority of the council so decides at the time of or subsequent to the hearing on the recommendation for revocation.
(Ord. No. 98-87, § 1, 9-8-98)
Sec. 13A-11. - Grounds for revocation or denial of certificate.
Occurrence of any of the following shall be considered grounds for revocation or denial of a massage therapy practitioner certificate:
(1)
Failure of the practitioner to maintain initial qualifications for obtaining the certificate under this chapter.
(2)
The practitioner is engaged in the business of a massage therapist under a false or assumed name, or is impersonating another practitioner who possesses a valid certificate under this chapter.
(3)
Conviction of or a plea of guilty or nolo contendere or forfeited bond by the applicant for any state or federal felony within five years prior to the date of application or at any time after the date of application; conviction of or a plea of guilty or nolo contendere or forfeited bond by the applicant for a state or federal misdemeanor violation of any laws relating to prostitution of public indecency, masturbation for hire, pimping, pandering, solicitation of sodomy, distributing obscene materials or the giving of massages in places used for lewdness or prostitution as prescribed by state law within two years prior to the date of application or at any time after the date of application.
(4)
The original application for massage therapy practitioner certificate, contains materially false information or the practitioner has deliberately sought to falsify information contained therein.
(5)
Violations by the practitioner of any state law prohibiting false or fraudulent statements in advertising or the unauthorized practice of any other profession licensed by the state.
(Ord. No. 98-87, § 1, 9-8-98)
Sec. 13A-12. - Records required to be kept.
It shall be the duty of any practitioner granted a massage therapy practitioner's certificate to maintain correct and accurate records of the names and addresses of the persons receiving treatment, the date of service, and the type of treatment administered. Said records shall be subject to inspection at any time by any member of the police department or finance department of the City Of Columbus.
(Ord. No. 98-87, § 1, 9-8-98)
Sec. 13A-13. - Penalty.
Any person convicted of violating any provisions of this chapter shall be punished as provided by section 1-8 as codified in the Code of Ordinances of the City of Columbus.
(Ord. No. 98-87, § 1, 9-8-98)
