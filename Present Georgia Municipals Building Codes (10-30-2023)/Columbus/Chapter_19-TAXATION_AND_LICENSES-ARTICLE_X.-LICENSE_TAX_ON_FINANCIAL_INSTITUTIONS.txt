Sec. 19-152. - Financial institutions' business license tax.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. section 48-6-93 (empowering counties to levy and collect a business license tax from depository financial institutions), there is hereby levied an annual business license tax upon state and national banking associations, federal savings and loan associations and state building and loan associations at the rate of 0.25 percent of the Georgia gross receipts, as defined and allocated in O.C.G.A. sections 48-6-93 and 48-6-95, of said institutions. Pursuant to O.C.G.A. section 48-6-93, the minimal annual amount of said business license tax shall be $1,000.00.
(Ord. No. 04-75, § 2, 8-17-04)
Sec. 19-153. - Due date; filing of return.
(a)
Each depository financial institution within Columbus, Georgia, shall file a return of its gross receipts with Columbus, Georgia, on March 1 of the year following the year in which such gross receipts were measured. Said returns shall be in the manner and in the form prescribed by the commissioner of the department of banking and shall be based upon the allocation method set forth in subsection (d) of Code section 48-6-93, O.C.G.A. The taxes levied pursuant to this article shall be assessed and collected based upon the information provided in said return.
(b)
The due date of taxes levied by this article shall be April 1, 1984, and April 1 of each subsequent year.
(Ord. No. 83-83, § 3, 10-18-83)
