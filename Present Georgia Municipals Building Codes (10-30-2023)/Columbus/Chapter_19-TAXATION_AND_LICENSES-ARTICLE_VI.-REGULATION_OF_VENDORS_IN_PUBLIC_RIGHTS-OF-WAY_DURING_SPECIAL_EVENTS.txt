ARTICLE VI. - REGULATION OF VENDORS IN PUBLIC RIGHTS-OF-WAY DURING SPECIAL EVENTS

Sec. 19-90. - Construction of terms; definitions.
(a)
Terms which are defined in section 19-37 for purposes of Article IV shall have the same meanings in this article.
(b)
As used in this article, the term:
(1)
Right-of-way means any street, median, curb, sidewalk, easement, park, open space or any other property owned by the city.
(2)
Special event means a fair, festival, show, or similar event located in Uptown Columbus, sponsored by a single organization, lasting at least four successive days, and open to all citizens for free or for an entry fee of not more than $15.00 per person.
(3)
Uptown Columbus means the part of Columbus within the area bounded on the west by the Chattahoochee River, on the north by 14th Street; on the east by 10th Avenue, Victory Drive, and a line extending from the intersection of Lumpkin Boulevard and Victory Drive south to the Chattahoochee River; and on the south by the Chattahoochee River.
(4)
Vendor means any person who sells food, beverages, arts, crafts, or any other goods from a location on the right-of-way.
(Ord. No. 96-39, § I, 5-14-96)
Sec. 19-91. - Award of vending rights.
(a)
Sponsors of special events may apply to the city manager's office for the exclusive rights to authorize vendors to operate in Uptown Columbus during the special events. The city manager's office shall establish procedures to govern the application process, which shall include, but not be limited to, the following:
(1)
Identity of the sponsor.
(2)
Identification of the beginning and ending dates of the special event.
(3)
Submittal of a map identifying the approximate placement of each planned vending location.
(4)
Identification of the approximate numbers of proposed vendors who will be selling in each of the following categories:
(i)
Food and beverages;
(ii)
Beverages only;
(iii)
Arts and crafts;
(iv)
Souvenirs; and
(v)
Goods of any other type.
(b)
The city manager's office shall review all submitted applications and shall submit those which are approved to city council for action. The city council may at its sole discretion elect to award exclusive vending rights to an applicant for the period of the special event.
(Ord. No. 96-39, § I, 5-14-96)
Sec. 19-92. - Vendors prohibited from selling in the right-of-way except as authorized.
(a)
During any special event for which a sponsor has been awarded exclusive vending rights pursuant to this article, no vendor shall operate in the right-of-way except with the express written permission of the sponsor, which shall be posted in plain view at each vending location.
(b)
During any special event the sponsor of which has been awarded exclusive vending rights pursuant to this article, no vendor shall operate in any right-of-way outside Uptown Columbus.
(c)
This section shall not apply to any vendor authorized to operate permanently in a public facility or park.
(Ord. No. 96-39, § I, 5-14-96)
Sec. 19-93. - Responsibilities of special event sponsor.
Any special event sponsor who applies for and is awarded exclusive vending rights pursuant to this article shall comply with the following provisions:
(1)
Adequate provision shall be made for security and traffic control at no cost to the city.
(2)
Adequate provision shall be made for garbage and waste collection at no cost to the city.
(3)
Adequate provision shall be made for litter control at no cost to the city.
(4)
Adequate provision shall be made for compliance with all applicable health, safety, building and fire codes at no cost to the city.
(5)
Adequate provision shall be made for the placement and removal of portable toilets at no cost to the city.
(6)
The sponsor shall take such actions as are necessary to assure that all rights-of-way used by the sponsor, by vendors authorized by the sponsor, or by participants in the special event are at the conclusion of the special event restored to a condition at least equal to that existing prior to the special event.
(7)
The sponsor shall, not later than seven days before the first day of the special event, deliver to the finance department a list of all vendors the sponsor has authorized to operate during the special event, identifying the name of the individual responsible for each vending location, the individual's permanent residential address, and the category of sales for which the vendor is authorized.
(8)
The sponsor shall issue all authorized vendors a certificate of authorization in a form acceptable to the finance department.
(Ord. No. 96-39, § I, 5-14-96)
Sec. 19-94. - Obligations of vendor.
Each vendor operating during a period in which exclusive vending rights have been awarded pursuant to this article shall comply with the following provisions:
(1)
The vendor shall obtain any and all permits and pay any and all taxes, license fees, permit fees or other levies which may apply to the vending location and which are not expressly waived herein.
(2)
The vendor shall post in plain view at each vending location proof of authorization to operate, which shall be issued by the special event sponsor as provided herein.
(3)
The vendor shall comply with all applicable health, safety, fire, building and alcohol code provisions.
(4)
The vendor shall keep the vending location free of litter and debris.
(5)
The vendor shall at the conclusion of the special event restore the vending location to a condition equaling that prior to the special event.
(Ord. No. 96-39, § I, 5-14-96)
Sec. 19-95. - Regulatory fees levied.
(a)
A regulatory fee shall be levied on all sponsors of special events who apply for and receive exclusive vending rights pursuant to the provisions of this article.
(1)
The regulatory fee for sponsors of special events of more than three but fewer than eight days duration shall be $7,500.00.
(2)
The regulatory fee for sponsors of special events of eight or more days duration shall be $15,000.00.
(b)
Vendors operating in conjunction with special events the sponsors of which pay the regulatory fee provided herein shall be relieved of any obligation to pay the occupation tax levied in section 19-39 on gross receipts earned at a properly authorized vending location during the special event.
(Ord. No. 96-39, § I, 5-14-96)
Sec. 19-96. - Right of licensed businesses to operate.
Nothing herein shall in any way impinge on the right of businesses properly licensed pursuant to section 19-36 to operate in the manner permitted by the license.
(Ord. No. 96-39, § I, 5-14-96)
Sec. 19-97. - Conflicts between specific and general provisions.
Where there is an apparent conflict in this article between specific and general provisions, it is the intention hereof that the specific shall control.
(Ord. No. 96-39, § I, 5-14-96)
Sec. 19-98. - Severability.
If any section, provision or clause of any part of this article shall be declared invalid or unconstitutional, or if the provisions of any part of this article as applied to any particular situation or set of circumstances shall be declared invalid or unconstitutional, such invalidity shall not be construed to effect the portions of this article not so held to be invalid, or the application of this article to other circumstances not so held to be invalid. It is hereby declared as the intent that this article would have been adopted had such invalid portion not been included herein.
(Ord. No. 96-39, § I, 5-14-96)
Sec. 19-99. - Repeal of conflicting provisions.
All ordinances or parts of ordinances in conflict with this article, and not preserved hereby, be, and the same are, hereby repealed.
(Ord. No. 96-39, § I, 5-14-96)
