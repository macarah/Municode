The penalties chargeable to persons delinquent in payment of ad valorem taxes shall be:

ARTICLE II. - OCCUPATION AND PROFESSIONS TAX[1]

Footnotes:
--- (1) ---
State Law reference— Local occupations and professions taxes, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-5 et seq.

Sec. 17-23. - Levied; amount.
(a)
Except as to those businesses and practitioners of professions and occupations excluded by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-16(a) and except as to those persons excluded by O.C.G.A. § 43-12-1, there is hereby levied an occupation tax on those businesses and practitioners of professions and occupations which have one or more locations or offices within the city.
(b)
There is hereby levied an occupation tax on those businesses and practitioners of professions and occupations with no location or office in the state if the business or practitioner:
(1)
Has one or more employees or agents who exert substantial efforts within the city for the purpose of soliciting business or serving customers or clients; or
(2)
Owns personal or real property which generates income and which is located in the city.
(c)
The tax levied by this section shall be based on number of employees of the business or practitioner. All local businesses shall pay an administrative fee. Businesses with home offices outside of the city shall pay a regulatory fee.
(d)
The amount of the tax and the fees referenced in subsection (c) of this section shall be as established by the city.
( Ord. No. 05-94, 12-30-1994 ; Ord. of 1-14-1995)
Sec. 17-24. - Denial, suspension or cancellation of license.
(a)
In addition to any criminal penalty that may be imposed the city council shall have the right to deny, suspend or cancel any license if the:
(1)
License application was not filed in good faith or was filed by some person as a subterfuge for any other person.
(2)
Applicant or licensee has failed to comply with any provisions of this article.
(3)
Place of business for which the license was issued and/or the type of business for which the license was issued has been found conducive to criminal activity or is detrimental to the general health and welfare of the community.
(4)
Licensee or applicant has been convicted of, or plead guilty or nolo contendere to, a felony or crime of moral turpitude.
(5)
Licensee or applicant has made any misstatement, nondisclosure or concealment of fact in the application.
(b)
Before any denial, suspension or cancellation of a license granted under this article, the applicant or licensee shall be afforded notice and a hearing as follows:
(1)
The notice shall be served personally or by certified mail not less than five days before the hearing. The notice shall include a:
a.
Statement of the time, place and nature of the hearing;
b.
Statement of the legal authority under which the hearing is to be held;
c.
Reference to the sections of the ordinances and statutes involved;
d.
Short and plain statement of the matters asserted;
e.
Statement as to the right of any party to representation by legal counsel at the hearing, the right of any party to present evidence on all issues, and the right of any party to subpoena witnesses and documentary evidence.
(2)
At the hearing, all parties may be represented by legal counsel, and may respond to and present evidence on all issues involved.
(c)
All decisions denying, suspending, or revoking licenses shall be in writing, with the reasons therefor stated, and shall be mailed or delivered to the applicant or licensee at the business address shown on the application for the license.
( Ord. No. 01-96, §§ 1—3, 4-9-1996 )
Secs. 17-25—17-51. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - INSURANCE LICENSE TAX[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Municipal authority to impose license fees on insurers, O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8 et seq.

Sec. 17-52. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Gross direct premium means gross direct premium as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4.
Insurer means a company that is authorized to transact business in any classes of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5.
(Ord. of 12-8-1975; Ord. of 11-7-1988)
Sec. 17-53. - Insurer license fees.
There is hereby levied for the year 2012 and for each year thereafter an annual license fee upon each insurer doing business within the city in the amount of $40.00. For each separate business location in excess of one not covered by section 17-56, which is operating on behalf of such insurers within the city, there is hereby levied a license fee in the amount of $40.00. For the purposes of this section, the term "insurer" means a company which is authorized to transact business in any of the classes of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5.
(Ord. No. 02-94, § 1, 3-8-1994 ; Ord. No. 2011-04, § 1, 9-13-2011)
Sec. 17-54. - Gross premiums tax imposed on life insurers.
(a)
There is hereby levied for the year 2012 and for each year thereafter an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer writing life, accident and sickness insurance within the state in an amount equal to one percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.1. Gross direct premiums as used in this section shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4.
(b)
The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 17-53.
(Ord. of 12-8-1975; Ord. of 11-7-1988; Ord. No. 2011-04 , § 3, 9-13-2011)
Sec. 17-55. - Gross premiums tax, all other insurers.
(a)
There is hereby levied for the year 2012 and for each year thereafter an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer, other than an insurer transacting business in the class of insurance designated in subsection 1 of O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5, doing business within the state in an amount equal to 2.5 percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2. Gross direct premiums as used in this section shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4.
(b)
The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 17-53.
(Ord. of 12-8-1975; Ord. of 11-7-1988; Ord. No. 2011-04 , § 4, 9-13-2011)
Sec. 17-56. - License fees for insurers insuring certain risks at additional business locations.
For each separate business location, not otherwise subject to a license fee hereunder, operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing and in connection with such loans or sales offers, solicits or takes application for insurance through a licensed agent of an insurer for insurance said insurer shall pay an additional license fee of $14.00 per location for the year 2012 and for each year thereafter.
(Ord. No. 2011-04 , § 2, 9-13-2011)
Sec. 17-57. - Due date for license fees.
License fees imposed in sections 17-53 and 17-56 shall be due and payable on the first day of 2012 and on the first date of each subsequent year.
(Ord. No. 2011-04 , § 5, 9-13-2011)
Secs. 17-58—17-83. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - MECHANICAL AMUSEMENT DEVICES[3]
Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— Coin operated amusement devices, O.C.G.A. § 48-17-1 et seq.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 17-84. - Definitions of terms.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Mechanical amusement device means any machine, which upon insertion of a coin, slug, token, plate or disc may be operated by the public generally for use as a game, entertainment or amusement, whether or not registering a score. It shall include such devices as marble machines, pinball machines, skill ball, mechanical grab machines and all games, operations or transactions similar thereto under whatever name that may be indicated.
Person, firm, corporation, or association, as used herein, includes the following: any person, firm, corporation or association which owns any such machine; the person, firm, corporation, or association in whose place of business any such machine is placed for use by the public; and the person, firm, corporation, or association having control over such machine; provided, however, that the payment of such fee by any person, firm, corporation or association enumerated herein shall be deemed a compliance with this article.
( Ord. No. 02-93, § 1, 5-4-1993 )
Sec. 17-85. - Penalty.
Unless stated otherwise, violations of this article are punishable as provided in section 1-7.
( Ord. No. 02-93, § 11, 5-4-1993 )
Sec. 17-86. - Seizure and destruction of machine.
If the chief of police shall have reason to believe any mechanical amusement device is used as a gambling device, such machine may be seized by the police and impounded and, if upon trial the exhibitor is found guilty, such machine shall be destroyed by the police.
( Ord. No. 02-93, § 10, 5-4-1993 )
Sec. 17-87. - Gambling devices not permitted.
Nothing in this article shall in any way be construed to authorize, license or permit any gambling devices whatsoever, or any mechanism that has been judicially determined to be a gambling device, or in any way contrary to law, or that may be contrary to any future laws of the state.
( Ord. No. 02-93, § 2, 5-4-1993 )
Sec. 17-88. - Prohibitions and restrictions.
No person holding a license under this article shall permit persons under 18 years of age to play or operate any mechanical amusement device as defined in section 17-84, unless there is present on the premises an adult over 21 years of age, and no such business shall permit the use of said mechanical amusement device to be operated between the hours of 12:00 a.m. and 6:00 a.m.
( Ord. No. 02-93, § 8, 5-4-1993 )
Secs. 17-89—17-119. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 17-120. - Required.
Any person displaying for public patronage or keeping for operation any mechanical amusement device as herein defined by section 17-84 shall be required to obtain a license from the city, upon payment of a license fee. Application for such license shall be made to the city upon a form to be supplied by the city clerk for that purpose.
( Ord. No. 02-93, § 3, 5-4-1993 )
Sec. 17-121. - Application.
The application for such license shall contain the following information:
(1)
Name and address of the applicant, age, date and place of birth.
(2)
Prior convictions of applicant, if any.
(3)
Place where machine or device is to be displayed or operated and the business conducted at that place.
(4)
Description of machine to be covered by the license, mechanical features, name of manufacturer, and serial number.
No license shall be issued to any applicant unless he shall be over 21 years of age and a citizen of the United States.
( Ord. No. 02-93, § 4, 5-4-1993 )
Sec. 17-122. - Inspection.
The application for a license shall be made out in duplicate, with one copy being referred to the city building inspector.
(1)
The city building inspector shall inspect all wiring and connections to the machine, and all sanitary facilities in the building and determine if the same complies with the electrical and building code of the city, and shall either recommend or not recommend the application.
(2)
No license shall be issued to any applicant unless recommended by the city building inspector and approved by city council.
( Ord. No. 02-93, § 5, 5-4-1993 )
Sec. 17-123. - Display of license.
(a)
The license herein provided for shall be posted permanently and conspicuously at the location of the machine in the premises wherein the device is to be operated or maintained to be operated.
(b)
A license shall not be transferable from person to person nor place to place, (unless approved by city council) and shall be usable only at the place and by the person designated in the license.
( Ord. No. 02-93, § 7, 5-4-1993 )
Sec. 17-124. - Revocation of license.
Every license issued under this division is subject to the right, which is hereby expressly reserved, to revoke the same should the licensee, directly or indirectly, permit the operation of any mechanical amusement device contrary to the provisions of this article, the ordinances of the city, or the law of the state. Said license may be revoked by the city council after written notice to the licensee, which notice shall specify the ordinance or law violations with which the licensee is charged, if after a hearing the licensee is found to be guilty of such violations. Ten days' notice of the hearing shall be given the licensee. At such hearing the licensee and his attorney may present and submit evidence and witnesses in his defense.
( Ord. No. 02-93, § 9, 5-4-1993 )
Secs. 17-125—17-146. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - FRANCHISE FEES
DIVISION 1. - CABLE OR VIDEO FRANCHISE FEE[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— Franchising of cable and video services, O.C.G.A. § 36-76-1 et seq.

Sec. 17-147. - Franchise fee for state-issued cable or video franchise.
The city hereby requires a franchise fee of five percent of gross revenues generated within the city for any cable or video state franchise issued within its corporate boundaries by the state.
( Ord. No. 2008-01, § 1, 1-22-2008 )
Sec. 17-148. - Authorized designee.
The city hereby authorizes the mayor or manager, upon receipt of notice to the city of its right to designate a franchise fee for an applicant for or holder of an existing state franchise, to provide written notice to the Secretary of State and each applicant for or holder of a state franchise within a service area that is wholly or partially located within the city limits of the five percent franchise fee rate applicable to such applicant or holder of a state franchise.
( Ord. No. 2008-01, § 2, 1-22-2008 )
DIVISION 2. - TELEGRAPH AND/OR TELEPHONE FRANCHISES RATE

Sec. 17-149. - Due compensation for telephone companies with end user retail customers within the city.
(a)
The city hereby requires due compensation of three percent of actual recurring local service revenues, as defined in paragraphs (8) and (9) of subsection (b) of O.C.G.A. § 46-5-1; provided, however, that any company which pays in excess of three percent of actual recurring local service revenues pursuant to an existing franchise agreement shall continue to pay in accordance with the agreement until the expiration of the franchise agreement or December 31, 2012, whichever occurs first, and any company which pays in excess of three percent of actual recurring local service revenues in accordance with an occupational license tax arrangement shall continue to pay in accordance with such payment schedule until December 31, 2012.
(b)
Regarding any telephone company that does not have retail, end user customers located within the city's municipal boundaries, the payment by such company to a municipal authority in accordance with the rates set by regulations promulgated by the department of transportation for the use of its rights of way shall be considered the payment of due compensation.
( Ord. No. 2009-08, § 2, 11-10-2009 )
Sec. 17-150. - Authorized designee.
The city manager, or his designee, shall, on behalf of the city, exercise day-to-day administrative duties necessary to fulfill the regulatory authority of the city under O.C.G.A. § 46-5-1, et seq. and shall perform the following duties:
(1)
Review application information submitted by a telephone company to the city and, if an application is incomplete, notify the telegraph or telephone company within 15 business days of the receipt of such application, identifying in such notice all application deficiencies.
(2)
Report the receipt of a completed application to the council within 60 calendar days of the receipt of such completed application.
(3)
Review payments of due compensation submitted by the company to ensure compliance with the provisions of the amended law and this section.
(4)
Provide a coordination function between a telephone company and all city departments on any matter relating to the amended law and this section.
(5)
Arrange and evaluate, no more than once a year, a comprehensive review of the records of a company which is reasonably related to the calculation and payment of due compensation.
(6)
Provide to all telegraph and telephone companies located in its rights of way written notice of annexations and changes in municipal corporate boundaries.
( Ord. No. 2009-08, § 3, 11-10-2009 )
Secs. 17-151—17-175. - Reserved.
ARTICLE VI. - GARAGE SALES[5]

Footnotes:
--- (5) ---
Editor's note— Ord. No. 2010-06, § 1(a)—(e), adopted Nov. 22, 2010 , added provisions that were not specifically amendatory. At the editor's discretion, said provisions were included as §§ 17-176—17-180.

Sec. 17-176. - Definition.
[The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:]
Garage sale means the sale or trading of clothing, furniture, household items, dishes, antiques or similar goods or merchandise, other than in the normal course of business, or the sale or trading of such goods, as outlined in this definition, by a person not regularly engaged in such business. Such goods or merchandise do not need to be attended for a sale to be deemed to be in existence. "Garage sale" shall include "yard sales," "carport sales," "rummage sales" and similar types of sales or events.
( Ord. No. 2010-06, § 1(a), 11-22-2010 )
Sec. 17-177. - Number and duration.
Garage sales shall be allowed not more than four times in any calendar year for any premises. No sale may run more than two consecutive days.
( Ord. No. 2010-06, § 1(b), 11-22-2010 )
Sec. 17-178. - Display area.
All displays of garage sale merchandise shall be limited to that portion of the property which is at least five feet back from the property line on all sides, and at least ten feet back from any curb or road edge.
( Ord. No. 2010-06, § 1(c), 11-22-2010 )
Sec. 17-179. - Signage requirements.
Signs advertising a garage sale may be placed seven days prior to the sale and must be removed not later than 48 hours upon completion of the sale, and any temporary signage must be otherwise consistent with App. A, § 4.44.7 of the Springfield Municipal Code. Each garage sale sign shall include the date(s) of the sale and the street address of the sale. No signs pertaining to the garage sale shall be placed on public property, nor in any median, nor in any public right-of-way. Signs may be placed on private property only with the consent of the property owner.
( Ord. No. 2010-06, § 1(d), 11-22-2010 )
Sec. 17-180. - Prohibited acts.
No individual, firm, corporation or other partnership shall be permitted to engage in the business of promoting or conducting garage sales for others for a fee or other consideration.
( Ord. No. 2010-06, § 1(e), 11-22-2010 )
Sec. 17-181. - Permission of property owner.
Where a garage sale is held upon a premises by any individual, organization, or group which does not own said premises, the party conducting the garage sale shall obtain written permission by the owner of the premises, naming the party conducting the sale; and the owner's express permission to the conduct the sale; and the date and time when the sale shall commence; and the date and time when the sale shall conclude; and the physical address of the premises upon which the sale shall be conducted; and the signature of the owner. The party conducting the sale shall keep this written permission on site and in his or her possession throughout the duration of the garage sale and shall produce the same to any city official, city officer, or law enforcement officer who requests it for review.
( Ord. No. 2011-01, § 1(f), 1-11-2011 )
Sec. 17-182. - Limitations.
Garage sales or yard sales are limited to residential property.
(Ord. No. 2012-12 , 6-26-2012)
Sec. 17-183. - Exemptions.
Bona fide charitable and non-profit civic organizations shall be exempt from the rules set forth in this article, however, upon request shall provide the proper documentation.
(Ord. No. 2012-12 , 6-26-2012)
