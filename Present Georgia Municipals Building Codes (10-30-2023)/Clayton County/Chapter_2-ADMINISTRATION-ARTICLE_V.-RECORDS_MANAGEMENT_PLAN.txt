Sec. 2-160. - Purpose.
The purpose of this article is to demonstrate compliance with O.C.G.A. § 50-18-99 by establishing a records management plan for Clayton County government. It is also the purpose of this article to provide direction for the management of public records in Clayton County in order to ensure the retention of records essential to meet the needs of the board of commissioners, the county, its political subdivisions, and its citizens, insofar as the records affect the administration of government, legal rights and responsibilities, and the accumulation of information of value for research purposes of all kinds. It is further the purpose of this article to consolidate all records management operations under the direction and control of the board of commissioners through the county records officer of the county. Records requiring retention for several years will be transferred to and managed within secure, low-cost records storage, whether physical or electronic medium, in accordance with standards provided by this statute and in compliance of O.C.G.A. § 50-18-99 as amended. In furtherance of these purposes, this article is based upon the premises that:
(1)
The records of the county are county assets as defined by section 70-74(2).
(2)
All records contain information needed for varying lengths of time for conducting public business.
(3)
Certain records are essential for varying lengths of time for continuity of government or for the protection of the rights and privileges of its citizens.
(4)
Certain records are essential as documentary evidence of the county's history and heritage.
(Ord. No. 2021-138, § 1, 7-6-21)
Sec. 2-161. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the term:
Director means any director of a county department under the jurisdiction of the board of commissioners. For purposes of abbreviation, "department director" shall also include members of all boards and authorities under the jurisdiction of the board of commissioners.
Official means any elected official of the county.
County court(s) mean(s) the juvenile, probate, magistrate, state, and superior courts, or any specialized courts created by the state Constitution or statutes of this state.
County department means an executive, law enforcement, or judicial agency, department, board, commission, authority, institution or instrumentality of the county created by the state Constitution, statutes of this state, or ordinances of the county.
Archives and records center means any facility operated by the department of information technology for the storage, processing, servicing, and security of tangible records that must be retained for varying periods of time but need not be retained in county office equipment or office space. Archives and records center also means any facility maintained by the county for the preservation of those tangible records or other materials that have been determined by the county to have sufficient historical and other value to warrant their continued preservation by the county and that have been accepted for retention.
Record refers to any county record or court record as defined within this document.
County record means any information that:
(1)
Is inscribed on a tangible medium or that is stored in an electronic or other medium and is retrievable in perceivable form.
(2)
Is prepared, owned, used or retained by a county department or political subdivision.
(3)
Relates to an activity, transaction, or function of a county department or political subdivision; and
(4)
Is necessary to satisfy the fiscal, legal, administrative, or historical policies, requirements, or needs of the county department or political subdivision.
The term record does not include:
(1)
Library or museum materials made or acquired and preserved solely for reference or exhibition purposes.
(2)
Extra copies of a document, preserved only for convenience of reference.
(3)
A stock of publications.
Court record means any information that:
(1)
Is inscribed on a tangible medium or that is stored in an electronic or other medium and is retrievable in perceivable form.
(2)
Is prepared, owned, used or retained by the county courts.
(3)
Relates to an activity, transaction, or function of a county court; and,
(4)
Is necessary to satisfy the fiscal, legal, administrative, or historical policies, requirements, or needs of the county courts.
Electronic record means any information recorded in a form that requires a machine to process and access the information.
Electronic records system is a term to indicate a secure computer system provisioned and maintained by the department of information technology that provides information storage and records management software used to store electronic records.
Electronic records management system (ERMS) means a commercial or open-source purpose-built software used by an organization to manage records from creation to final disposition. The system's primary functions are categorizing and locating records and identifying records that are due for disposition. The electronic records management system manages records regardless of medium or storage location.
(Ord. No. 2021-138, § 1, 7-6-21)
Sec. 2-162. - Designation of county records officer position.
The division manager archives and records position within the department of information technology is hereby designated as the county records officer. The county records officer shall administer a records management program under the direction of the department of information technology.
(Ord. No. 2021-138, § 1, 7-6-21)
Sec. 2-163. - Policies, guideline, and procedures.
This records management plan will adhere to state law for records management. Further, the board of commissioners shall develop and issue additional policies, as needed, for the effective governance and management of its records and to ensure their security, accessibility, disposition. Further, the department of information technology shall develop and issue supplemental standards, guidelines, and procedures, as needed for same.
(Ord. No. 2021-138, § 1, 7-6-21)
Sec. 2-164. - Retention schedules.
The statewide common records retention schedules, as issued by the Georgia Archives, applicable for county governments and law enforcement agencies are hereby adopted by reference and made the common schedule of the county. As required by the special needs of the county departments, the county records officer shall develop specific retention schedules for county records in addition to the common schedule.
The court schedule, as issued by the Judicial Council of Georgia, shall hereby be adopted by reference and made the common schedule of the county courts.
Each official, department, court and political subdivision of the county shall maintain records in accordance with the schedules named above, without regard to the technology or medium used to create or communicate the record.
(Ord. No. 2021-138, § 1, 7-6-21)
Sec. 2-165. - Records storage.
Records requiring retention for several years will be transferred to and managed within secure, low-cost records storage in accordance with standards provided by this ordinance and in compliance of O.C.G.A. § 50-18-99 as amended. All records not required for normal operations shall be either destroyed or transferred to appropriate records storage; provided, however, no records shall be destroyed, altered, or transferred except in accordance with approved retention schedule and policies, standards, and rules for the governance of information.
For tangible records, there is hereby created an archives and records center under the direction of the department of information technology.
For electronic records requiring long term retention, the department of information technology shall procure and maintain a scalable, secure, and adequate electronic records management system.
(Ord. No. 2021-138, § 1, 7-6-21)
Sec. 2-166. - County records officer—Actions.
The county records officer shall act in matters of:
(1)
Establishing and maintaining an active and continuing program for the economical, efficient, and effective management of records.
(2)
Establishing and implementing policies, standards, guidelines, and procedures relating to the creation, utilization, maintenance, retention, preservation, and destruction of records.
(3)
Assisting the officials and directors in developing and implementing a program of records management.
(Ord. No. 2021-138, § 1, 7-6-21)
Sec. 2-167. - Same—Duties.
It shall be the duty of the county records officer to:
(1)
Ensure that current retention requirements as issued by the Georgia Archives and in state law, are reflected in county records management practice.
(2)
Approve the disposition of records pursuant to retention schedule.
(3)
Collaborate with the chief information security officer to ensure the proper security and classification of county records.
(4)
Develop and supervise a program of training in records management for selected personnel.
(5)
Prepare and submit reports of status and progress of the records management program to the board of commissioners.
(6)
Review, evaluate, and recommend efficient electronic records management systems that will simplify operations throughout all offices of the county; and,
(7)
Conduct an annual audit, presented to the board of commissioners, of departments to ensure that they are operating according to the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 2021-138, § 1, 7-6-21)
Sec. 2-168. - Responsibilities of officials and directors.
It shall be the responsibility of the officials and directors to:
(1)
Cooperate fully with the board of commissioners and the county records officer in complying with the provisions of the Georgia Records Act and this article.
(2)
Cause to be made and to preserve records containing adequate and proper documentation of the organization, policies, decisions, procedures, and essential transactions of the office, and designed to furnish the information necessary to protect the legal and financial rights of the county and of persons directly affected by the officials' or directors' activities.
(3)
Implement records management policies, standards, and rules issued by the board of commissioners.
(4)
Ensure the timely destruction of records as described in section 2-170 of this ordinance.
(5)
Establish necessary safeguards against the removal or loss of records and such further safeguards as may be recommended by the county records officer. Such safeguards shall include notification to all officials and directors that records are public property and, as such, constitute a record of public acts. Such records shall not be placed in the custody of private or semi-private individuals, and no records in the custody of an official or director are to be destroyed or otherwise alienated except in accordance with the provisions of the Georgia Records Act and this article.
(6)
Ensure that confidential information is protected from disclosure in compliance with the Georgia Open Records Act (O.C.G.A. § 50-18-70 et seq) and Ordinance No. 2014-288 (Section 70-69).
(Ord. No. 2021-138, § 1, 7-6-21)
Sec. 2-169. - Electronic record as official record.
If a state law requires that a record be retained, such requirement shall be satisfied by retainment as an electronic record so long as:
(1)
The information in the record accurately reflects the information set forth after the record was first generated in its final form; and,
(2)
The record remains accessible for the retention period required by law.
(Ord. No. 2021-138, § 1, 7-6-21)
Sec. 2-170. - Authorization for destruction of records.
(a)
The department of information technology shall establish and manage the destruction process with oversight and review by the county records officer. Records may be destroyed only in accordance with provisions of the county's approved retention schedules. Once eligible for destruction, authorization for destruction shall be obtained as follows:
(1)
A notice of destruction shall be sent to the official, department director, or designated records management personnel within the department for review.
(2)
The department shall have 90 days from receipt of the destruction notice to review and approve the destruction.
(3)
If records should not be destroyed, the department must inform the county records officer, in writing prior to the expiration of the 90-day period of the justification for an exception. Exceptions to the destruction of eligible records are listed in subsection (b) below.
(4)
In the absence of department approval of the destruction, the county records officer may approve the destruction of the records.
(5)
Once approved, the Department of Information Technology will securely destroy the records.
(b)
An exceptions process shall be established as part of the destruction process. A records destruction may be denied if the respective department is currently party to an active criminal investigation, the respective department is undergoing an active financial audit, or the record in question is subject to any open litigation whether criminal or civil.
(c)
All records shall be destroyed in such manner that they cannot be read, interpreted, or reconstructed. Items not included in the definition of a record in the definition section of this ordinance do not require specific authorization prior to disposition.
(Ord. No. 2021-138, § 1, 7-6-21)
Sec. 2-171. - Inspection of records.
In accordance with the Georgia Open Records Act (O.C.G.A. § 50-18-70 et seq), all public records shall be open for personal inspection and copying, except those which by order of a court of this state or by law are specifically exempted from disclosure. Records shall be maintained by county officials, departments, courts, and any political subdivision of the county to the extent and in the manner required by county ordinance, policy, standards, and regulations.
(Ord. No. 2021-138, § 1, 7-6-21)
