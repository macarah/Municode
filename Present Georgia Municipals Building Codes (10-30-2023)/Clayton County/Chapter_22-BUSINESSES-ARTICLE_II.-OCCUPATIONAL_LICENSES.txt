DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 22-26. - Purpose.
The purpose of this article is to provide the authority by which the county may regulate and require a business or practitioner of a profession or occupation to apply for an occupational license and pay an occupational tax for the privilege of conducting a business activity in the unincorporated area of the county, and to establish a level of taxation as would promote the economic development of the county, and attract and encourage businesses and practitioners of professions and occupations to locate or remain located in the county. Further, the purpose of this article is to provide the authority by which the county may regulate and require a business or practitioner of a profession or occupation to pay a regulatory fee where the county performs investigations and inspections of such businesses or practitioners of professions or occupations as a means of protecting the health, safety, and welfare of the citizens of the county, or in the course of enforcing state and county building, health, and safety codes.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-1), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96)
Sec. 22-27. - Construction of terms; definitions.
As used in this article, the term:
Administrative fee means a component of an occupation tax which approximates the reasonable cost of handling and processing the occupation tax.
Business means any form, type or manner of enterprise, establishment, trade, profession or occupation, or the like, that any person, as hereinafter defined, shall conduct.
County means the unincorporated area of Clayton County, Georgia.
Department means the Clayton County Department of Community Development.
Dominant line means the type of business, within a multiple-line business, that the greatest amount of income is derived from.
Employee means an individual whose work is performed under the direction and supervision of the employer and whose employer withholds FICA, federal income tax, or state income tax from such individual's compensation or whose employer issues to such individual, for purposes of documenting compensation, a form I.R.S. W-2 but not a form I.R.S. 1099.
Gross receipts means the gross sales of merchandise and the gross receipts of the business, trade, profession or occupation from all earnings, fees, commissions, brokerage charges and rentals and from all other income whatsoever arising from or growing out of the conduct of the business, trade, profession or occupation without any deduction whatsoever, except as hereinafter provided. "Gross receipts" shall not mean to include the following:
(1)
Sales, use, or excise taxes;
(2)
Sale returns, allowances, and discounts;
(3)
Interorganizational sales or transfers between or among the units of a parent-subsidiary controlled group of corporations, as defined by 26 U.S.C. Section 1563(a)(1), or between or among the units of a brother-sister controlled group of corporations, as defined by 26 U.S.C. Section 1563(a)(2), or between or among wholly owned partnerships or other wholly owned entities;
(4)
Payments made to a subcontractor or an independent agent/contractor;
(5)
Governmental and foundation grants, charitable contributions, or the interest income derived from such funds received by a nonprofit organization which employs salaried practitioners otherwise covered by this article, if such funds constitute 80 percent or more of the organization's receipts; and
(6)
Proceeds from sales to customers outside the state.
Home occupation means any occupation or activity carried on by a member of the family residing in a residentially zoned property.
License means a license issued to a business or practitioner of a profession or occupation, as required by this article, to conduct such business, profession or occupation in the unincorporated area of Clayton County, Georgia.
Location or office shall include any structure or vehicle where a business, profession, or occupation is conducted, but shall not include a temporary or construction work site which serves a single customer or project or a vehicle used for sales or delivery by a business or practitioner of a profession or occupation which has a location or office.
Occupation tax means a tax levied on persons, partnerships, corporations, or other entities for engaging in an occupation, profession, or business for revenue-raising purposes.
Person means to include any individual, sole proprietor, partnership, corporation, association, company, firm or other form of business organization or entity.
Practitioner of profession or occupation means one who, by state law, requires state licensure regulating such profession or occupation. "Practitioners of professions or occupations" shall not mean to include a practitioner who is an employee of a business, if the business pays an occupation tax.
Regulatory fees means payments, whether designated as license fees, permit fees, or by another name, which are required by a local government as an exercise of its police power and as a part of, or an aid to, the regulation of an occupation, profession, or business. The amount of a regulatory fee shall approximate the reasonable cost of the actual regulatory activity performed by the county. A regulatory fee does not include an administrative fee. Development impact fees as defined by paragraph 8 of O.C.G.A. § 36-71-2, or other costs or conditions of zoning or land development, are not considered regulatory fees.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-2), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96)
Sec. 22-28. - License, regulatory fee, occupation tax required; when required.
For the year 1996, and succeeding years thereafter, it shall be unlawful for any person to begin or carry on any business, for which an occupational tax or regulatory fee is required by this article, without first filing an application with the department, and obtaining a license, annually, evidencing the payment of an occupational tax or regulatory fee for carrying on the business, to be effective as of the first day of January of each year. Each person engaged in any business upon real property located in the county, or, in the case of an out-of-state business, with no real property location in the state, but exerting substantial efforts within the state pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7, shall pay an occupation tax or regulatory fee for the privilege of carrying on such business. The license, issued upon receipt of payment of the occupational tax or fee, shall be displayed in a conspicuous place within the place of business, if the licensee has a business location in the county. If the licensee has no business location in the county, such licensee shall show the license to any agent of the department or to any police officer of the county, upon request.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-3), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96)
Sec. 22-29. - Application required; furnishing accurate information; time for filing; late filing; applicable tax/fee; issuance of licenses.
(a)
Any person desiring a license shall submit an application with the department in accordance with the forms and procedures established by the department. The correctness of all information required in the application shall be sworn to or affirmed by the applicant on his own behalf, or, in the case of a partnership, corporation or other legal entity, by such principal or corporate officer empowered to make binding agreements on behalf of the partnership, corporation or entity. Failure to provide the required information or knowingly falsifying information on the application may result in disapproval, revocation, if the license was issued, or such other action by the department as authorized under provisions of this article.
(b)
Applications for a license shall be submitted to the department on or before March 31 of each year for which the license is required, or no less than five days preceding the day in which a person anticipates commencing business in the county. Such tax for new businesses shall be due and payable on the date of the commencement of the business.
(c)
Applications not received by the department by on or before March 31 of each year for which the license is required shall be considered late, as shall applications which are received within five days preceding the commencement of business by persons not in business in the county during the preceding year. Persons submitting late applications for a license shall be liable for the penalties established in the article.
(d)
Reserved.
(e)
It shall be the duty of the department to issue a license contemplated by this article after an application has been filed and verification has been received that all applicable requirements of federal, state and/or county statutes and/or ordinances have been met, and payment of the prescribed occupational tax or fee is received. Compliance with state and county statutes and ordinances shall specifically include a requirement that the applicant or business of the applicant is not delinquent in the payment of any personal or real property taxes. For purposes of this section, any tax that has been paid, formally appealed to the proper authorities, or is being paid pursuant to a plan authorized and approved by the appropriate tax or revenue commissioner shall not be deemed delinquent.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-4), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96; Ord. No. 99-15, §§ 1, 2, 2-2-99; Ord. No. 2004-162, § 1, 10-19-04)
Sec. 22-30. - License period; proration of fee.
All licenses required herein shall issue and be effective for the calendar year beginning the first day of January, and the occupational taxes and/or fees required by this article shall be paid in full before the issuance of the license. Businesses which commence operation after January 1 of any year and whose occupational tax is based upon gross receipts combined with profitability ratios and number of employees of the business shall pay an occupation tax based upon anticipated revenue for the balance of the calendar year. Businesses described under section 22-56 of this article whose occupation tax and/or fee is based upon a fixed amount shall pay the full amount of the occupation tax and/or fee unless the business commences operation on or after July 1 in any year. In this event, the applicant shall pay an amount equal to 50 percent of the fixed amount of the standard tax and/or fee. Regulatory fees shall not be reduced.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-5), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96)
Sec. 22-31. - Eligibility requirements for exemption for disabled veterans.
Eligibility requirements for an exemption of the occupational tax and/or fee for a disabled veteran must meet the requirements of the state department of veterans' affairs.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-6), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96)
Sec. 22-32. - Display of license; posting of name and telephone number for emergency purposes.
(a)
Every licensee shall display his license in a prominent, conspicuous place upon the business premises.
(b)
All businesses, including separate business establishments within one building, shall post, in a conspicuous place, the current name and telephone number or numbers of a person for contact in the event of an emergency. It shall be the duty of the licensee to comply with this requirement.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-7), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96)
Sec. 22-33. - License required for each place of business; nontransferable.
(a)
A license shall be required for each place of business within the county without regard to other business locations previously licensed to the same licensee.
(b)
The license is not transferable to any person, or location.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-8), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96)
Sec. 22-34. - Suspension and revocation.
(a)
Any license or permit which has been issued or which may hereafter be issued pursuant to this chapter may be suspended or revoked by the license review board for cause as set out is section 2-90.4 of this code.
(b)
It is unlawful and a violation of this section to operate a business after the license has been suspended or revoked. It is a rebuttable presumption of a violation of this section for a business to be occupied by anyone other than the owner or manager after the license is suspended or revoked. Each day the business is open or otherwise operated in violation of this section shall be a separate violation and upon conviction shall be punished as provided for in section 1-12 of this code. The owner or manager may apply for a permit from the director to occupy the business with sufficient persons to bring it into compliance with the code or for similar purposes other than engaging in business. At all times the business is occupied, all entrances must be posted as "CLOSED FOR BUSINESS" and the general public must be denied entrance.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-9), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96; Res. No. 02-34, § 1, 3-19-02; Ord. No. 2010-26, § 1, 2-2-10; Ord. No. 2011-193, § 1, 11-15-11; Ord. No. 2017-12, § 1, 2-21-17; Ord. No. 2019-51, § 3, 5-7-19)
Sec. 22-35. - Execution issued for delinquent occupation tax or fee.
(a)
In addition to the other remedies provided for the levy and collection of the occupation tax and/or fee herein described, the department may cause an execution to issue for any tax and/or fee becoming delinquent and remaining unpaid. The execution shall be filed against any assets of the licensee and recorded on the general execution docket in the county superior court deed records. The execution shall include the following:
(1)
The name, address and business location of the licensee;
(2)
The correct amount of the tax and/or fee assessed against the licensee, including a ten-percent penalty applied after 90 days from the due date, and interest at the rate of 12 percent per annum from the date such tax and/or fee was due.
(b)
The lien upon which the execution may issue shall become fixed on and the date such tax or fee was due. The execution shall be levied by the sheriff of the county upon the property of the licensee in the same manner as are ad valorem property tax executions. All proceedings relative thereto shall be in accordance with state laws, and the licensee in such execution shall have rights of defense by affidavit of illegality or as otherwise provided by the applicable state laws. When a nulla bona entry has been entered by the sheriff upon an execution issued by the department, the person against whom the entry was made shall be required to show cause before the director why his business license shall not be suspended or revoked until such delinquent tax and/or fee is paid in accordance with section 22-34.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-10), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96)
Sec. 22-36. - Penalties.
(a)
A violation of the provisions below shall result in the assessment of a penalty of ten percent of the tax or fee due and interest on delinquent occupation taxes, regulatory fees, and administrative fees at a rate of 1.5 percent per month.
(1)
Any person who shall attempt to conduct business in the county without having first applied for and obtained a license provided for by this article;
(2)
Any person who shall submit an application after the date prescribed by this article; or
(3)
Any person who shall fail to pay any occupational tax or fee assessed by the department on or before March 31.
(b)
Any person who has submitted false information in such person's application for a license shall be liable for a penalty in the amount of 20 percent of the occupational tax or fee owed for each calendar year, or portion thereof, plus an amount equal to the cost of such audits and field inspections deemed necessary by the department for its investigation. The penalty provided for in this paragraph shall be assessed in addition to any other penalty prescribed by this article.
(c)
Any person who shall violate the provisions of this article shall place in jeopardy such person's privilege of doing business in the county. The department may, for just cause, suspend or revoke the license for any such person.
(d)
Any person who shall violate the provisions of this article and conduct business without a license may have a case made against him in the magistrate court for doing business without a license, and as a misdemeanor shall, upon conviction, be fined as provided by law. Each separate day of doing business without a license shall be considered a separate offense.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-11), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96; Ord. No. 2004-162, § 2, 10-19-04)
Sec. 22-37. - Appeals.
Any person who is of the opinion that such person's occupation tax and/or fee has been incorrectly assessed, or that such person's dominant line classification is incorrect, shall have the right to appeal to the board of commissioners; provided, that such person shall first have stated such position in writing to the department without receiving a satisfactory resolution.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-12), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96)
Sec. 22-38. - Effect of amendments to this article.
This article may be amended from time to time, and all businesses subject to the requirement of obtaining a license pursuant to this article shall be subject to the amendments.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-13), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96)
Sec. 22-39. - Temporary operating permit.
(a)
New businesses. Temporary operating permits may be issued after a formal application for a new business license. A temporary operating permit is for the limited purpose of allowing a business to begin operations during the investigation and application period and shall in no event issue for a period greater than 15 days. The director or his designee may impose such limitations and requirements in his sole discretion as he deems fit and proper to protect and ensure the health, safety, and welfare of the general public and to protect the interest of the county. The director, or his designee, shall be under no duty to issue a temporary operating permit to any applicant. A temporary operating permit may be renewed in the discretion of the director.
(b)
Fees. No additional fee shall be required for the issuance of a temporary operating permit other than the payment of all other fees and taxes pursuant to this chapter.
(Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96; Ord. No. 99-15, § 3, 2-2-99; Ord. No. 2004-162, § 3, 10-19-04)
Sec. 22-40. - Home occupations.
(a)
Additional requirements. A home occupation shall be subject to all provisions and requirements of this chapter and article 6.10, home occupation standards, of the county zoning ordinance. Any applicant may file an administrative appeal as identified within article 13.22, administrative appeal process, of the county zoning ordinance. The following conditions apply to all type I home occupations:
(1)
The use of a dwelling unit for the home occupation shall be clearly incidental and subordinate to the residential use of the property.
(2)
The home occupation shall not involve the employment of any person other than those residing at the location of the home occupation.
(3)
At least one person residing on the premises shall be the primary operator of the home occupation.
(4)
The home occupation shall not involve any exterior storage or display of products, equipment, or materials that can be visible from the street.
(5)
The home occupation shall not make any use of accessory structures, including detached garages and sheds.
(6)
The home occupation shall not utilize more than 25 percent of the total floor area of the primary structure.
(7)
The home occupation shall not require any exterior, structural or aesthetic alterations to the dwelling unit that change the residential character of the dwelling unit.
(8)
The home occupation shall not require any additional entrances to the dwelling unit.
(9)
The home occupation may have a sign, attached to the primary structure, not exceeding two square feet, as authorized by article 8, sign [regulations] (SS) of the county zoning ordinance. No offsite signs or signs in the yard of the property shall be permitted.
(10)
The home occupation shall not require increasing or enhancing the size, capacity, or flow of the water, gas, septic, sewer, or electrical system beyond that which is standard for a residence.
(11)
The home occupation shall not provide parking for customers or visits for business purposes that require the addition of any off street parking spaces.
(12)
The home occupation shall not require the use of commercial vehicles for pickup and deliveries other than from the U.S. Postal Service or other express couriers.
(13)
No more than one vehicle associated with the home occupation may be parked at the site. Such vehicle is limited to one and one-half ton carrying capacity and must be used exclusively by the resident.
(14)
The following are permitted home occupations provided they do not violate any of the provisions of the previous paragraph:
a.
Dressmaking, sewing and tailoring;
b.
Painting, sculpturing, writing and other fine arts;
c.
Telephone answering and marketing;
d.
Home crafts, such as model making, rug weaving, and lapidary work;
e.
Instruction or teaching, such as academic tutoring, performing arts, or fine arts limited to one student at any given time;
f.
Computer application and internet sales, not including sale of computers;
g.
Office space for professionals, such as attorneys, drafters, realtors, insurance agents, engineers, architects, and other consultants;
h.
Administrative or clerical support services, such as transcription, court reporters, stenographers, notary public, or addressing services;
i.
Repair of clocks, instruments or other small appliances which do not create a nuisance due to noise, vibration, glare, fumes, odors or result in electrical interference;
j.
Barbershops and beauty parlors; limited to one operator and station;
k.
General construction and maintenance contractors;
l.
Lawn care and maintenance providers;
m.
Janitorial and cleaning services;
n.
Consultants and representatives for the sales industry, having no product displays onsite.
(15)
All personal care homes, boarding homes and group homes are required to obtain a business license.
(b)
The following provisions shall apply to all type II home occupations and require the approval of a conditional use permit, as identified within article 6.10, home occupation standards, of the county zoning ordinance prior to the issuance of the business license:
(1)
The home occupation shall not involve the employment of any more than one person who does not reside at the location of the home occupation.
(2)
At least one member residing on the premises shall be the primary operator of the business.
(3)
The home occupation shall not require any exterior storage or display of equipment, materials, or appliances being serviced by the home occupation.
(4)
No more than 40 percent of the total floor area of any level of the primary structure shall be used for the home occupation.
(5)
Business practices in accessory structures, such as detached garages and sheds, shall not be permitted.
(6)
The home occupation shall not require any exterior structural or aesthetic alterations to the dwelling unit that change the residential character of the dwelling unit.
(7)
The home occupation may have a sign, attached to the primary structure, not exceeding two square feet, as authorized by article 8, sign [regulations] (SS), of the county zoning ordinance. No offsite signs or signs in the yard of the property shall be permitted.
(8)
The home occupation shall not require increasing or enhancing the size, capacity, or flow of the water, gas, septic, sewer, or electrical system beyond what is standard for a residence.
(9)
No more than two additional parking spaces shall be added to the lot(s) on which the residence is located.
(10)
The home occupation shall not require the use of commercial vehicles for pickup and deliveries other than from the U.S. Postal Service or other express couriers.
(11)
Due to incompatibility with the residential character and qualities of residential zoning districts, no license shall be issued for a home occupation in any of the following trades or businesses:

Taxicab/limousine services;

Wrecker/towing service.

Sec. 22-41. - Requirement for guest register and necessary information.
(a)
Definitions. In addition to the definitions provided in other sections of this Code, the following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this section shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
(1)
Guest shall mean any person hiring or occupying for a consideration, on a temporary, transient, or nonpermanent basis, a room, rooms, mobile home or trailer or portion thereof, for sleeping purposes; a "guest" may hire or occupy for a consideration, whether rent is paid in money, goods, labor, or otherwise; "guest" includes employees of an establishment who reside or live upon the premises of the establishment;
(2)
Establishment shall mean hotel, motel, apartment, rooming house, extended stay facility, mobile home park, or any other place used or intended to be used by a "guest" and/or "guests" for sleeping purposes located in the unincorporated area of Clayton County.
(b)
Requirement for guest register and necessary information.
(1)
Every proprietor or manager of an establishment shall maintain a guest register, and shall require each guest, at the time of hiring or occupying the premises, to present a legal form of identification, to sign his or her full name on the register, together with his or her full residence address, as well as provide the full name and residence address of each member of the guest's party; in the event that said guest or his or her party travels by means of motor vehicle of any kind, the guest shall also enter at the same time on said register the make, type, and license number, including the name of the state or country of the license, of the motor vehicle.
(2)
The proprietor or manager of the establishment shall, at the time of the registering, assign the guest to a room, rooms, or mobile home or trailer, enter opposite the guest's name the number or other identifying information as to the room, rooms, or mobile home or trailer, assigned, and enter the date of the registration.
(3)
When the guest or guests check out, the proprietor or manager of the establishment shall cause that fact to be entered on the guest register, together with the date of leaving.
(4)
All entries in the guest register, whether by the guest or proprietor or manager, shall be made in ink and shall not in any manner be erased, obliterated or defaced.
(c)
The guest register maintained by the proprietor or manager of an establishment shall be kept in a conspicuous place on the premises of the establishment and shall be maintained for no less than two years.
(d)
The property owner, general manager or employee in charge of the property may be cited for any violation of this section and punished as provided for in section 1-12 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 2004-74, § 1, 5-18-04; Ord. No. 2017-6, § 1, 1-3-17)

All information regarding gross receipts or number of employees of any person gathered in accordance with this article shall be restricted to the employees of the county who are charged with the administration of the license process or are otherwise authorized by law to receive such information, and shall be used only for the purposes provided for in this article, its administration and revision.

Any person, not a county employee, who violates the confidentiality of this information shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be liable to punishment, as provided in section 1-12. Sec. 22-56. - Method of computing tax for persons classified under certain state law.
(a)
Persons described in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9(c) (1) through (20) shall elect, as the method of computing their occupation tax, one of the following:
(1)
The occupation tax based on gross receipts combined with profitability ratios, as set forth in section 22-49 of this article; or
(2)
A fee of $400.00 for each person licensed to provide the service, such fee to be paid at the person's office. A person paying according to this subsection shall not be required to provide information to the county relating to the gross receipts generated by the person. The fee applies to each person in the business who qualifies as a practitioner under the state's regulatory guidelines and framework.
(b)
The election required under this section is to be made on an annual basis and must be done by March 31 of each year. When a person commences business on or after July 1 in any year and elects the fixed fee method, the fee for the remaining portion of the year shall be as provided in section 22-30.

Sec. 22-57. - Exemption for persons practicing exclusively for government.
Any person whose office is maintained and who is employed in practice exclusively by the United States, the State of Georgia, a municipality or county, or an instrumentality of the United States, the State of Georgia, a municipality or county, shall not be required to obtain a license or pay an occupation tax for that practice.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-27), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96)
Sec. 22-58. - Purpose and scope of occupation tax.
The occupation tax provided for herein is levied for revenue purposes only and is not for regulatory purposes, nor is the payment of the tax made a condition precedent to the practice of any such profession, trade, or calling. The occupation tax only applies to those businesses which are covered by the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-5 through § 48-13-26. All other applicable businesses and occupations are taxed by the county pursuant to the pertinent general and/or local law and ordinance.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-28), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96)
Sec. 22-59. - When tax due and payable; penalty for delinquency.
The occupation tax levied pursuant to the provisions of this article shall be for the calendar year 1996 and succeeding calendar years thereafter. The occupation tax shall be due and payable on March 31 of each year, unless otherwise specifically provided. In the event any person commences business on any date after March 31, in any year, the tax shall be due and payable on the date of the commencement of the business. If any tax levied is not paid by March 31 of each year, the person owing such tax shall be subject to a penalty of ten percent of the tax or fee due and interest on delinquent occupation taxes, regulatory fees and administrative fees at a rate of 1.5 percent per month.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-29), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96; Ord. No. 99-15, § 5, 2-2-99; Ord. No. 2004-162, § 4, 10-19-04)
Sec. 22-60. - Allocation of gross receipts of business with multiple intrastate or interstate locations.
(a)
For those businesses that have multiple locations inside and outside of the county where the gross receipts can be allocated to each location, the gross receipts used to determine the occupation tax assessed will be those gross receipts attributed to each county location. Where the dollar amount of gross receipts attributed locally cannot be determined in those businesses with multiple locations, the total gross receipts will be divided by the total number of locations in the county and elsewhere and allotted to those locations. Upon request, the business or practitioner with a location or office situated in more than one jurisdiction shall provide the following:
(1)
Financial information necessary to allocate the gross receipts of the business or practitioner; and
(2)
Information relating to the allocation of the business's or practitioner's gross receipts by other local governments.
(b)
Where the business has locations outside of the county and taxation is levied for criteria other than gross receipts in other local governments, the county shall not assess more than the allotted share of gross receipts for the local operation.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-30), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96)

(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-35), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96)
Sec. 22-66. - Taxes levied on amount of business to be transacted during current calendar year.
(a)
All occupation taxes levied under this article are levied on the amount of business to be transacted during the current calendar year.
(b)
The person subject to such occupation tax for the current calendar year shall, at the end of the preceding year, and on or before March 31 of the current calendar year, file with the department, on a form furnished by the department, a signed return setting forth the number of employees and amount of gross receipts within the state of such business for the entire preceding calendar year.
(c)
Where a business, subject to the occupation tax for the calendar year, has been in operation for only a part of the preceding year, the amount of gross receipts for such partial year shall be reported in the return. The return shall also estimate the receipts on an annual basis based on those generated during the partial year. The resulting estimate shall be used as the basis for the gross receipts of the business for the current calendar year in establishing the business tax liability.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-36), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96; Ord. No. 99-15, §§ 6—8, 2-2-99)
Sec. 22-67. - Filing of return showing actual gross receipts during preceding calendar year; procedure when taxes overpaid or underpaid.
(a)
On or before February 15 of each year, the person liable for the occupation tax levied for the year shall file with the department, on a form furnished by such office, a signed return setting forth the number of employees and actual amount of the gross receipts of such business during the preceding calendar year.
(b)
If the occupation tax for the preceding year based on the return and on the rate of such tax provided for in this article is greater than the amount of occupation tax paid by the person based on the estimate returned, the difference in the amount shall be due and payable by the licensee to the county on or before March 31 of the current year, and such amount shall be deemed delinquent if not paid on or before March 31.
(c)
If the amount of the occupation tax for the preceding year, based on the return provided for in this section and on the rate of the tax provided for in this article, is less than the amount of occupation tax paid by the licensee based on the estimate filed pursuant to this section, the difference in the amount shall be refundable by the county to the taxpayer on or before March 31; provided, however, if such business continues to be conducted in the county during the current year, such difference in amount shall be credited by the county toward the amount of occupation tax to be paid to the county by the licensee for the current year.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-37), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96; Ord. No. 99-15, §§ 9—11, 2-2-99; Ord. No. 2010-252, § 1, 12-14-10)

Sec. 22-70. - Returns confidential.
Except in the case of judicial proceedings or other proceedings necessary to collect the occupation tax hereby levied, it shall be unlawful for any officer, employee, agent, or clerk of the county or any other person to divulge or make known, in any manner, the amount of gross receipts or any particulars set forth or disclosed in any occupation tax return required under this article. All contents of such return shall be confidential and open only to the officials, employees, agents, or clerks of the county using such returns for the purpose of this occupation tax levy and the collection of the tax. Independent auditors or bookkeepers employed by the county shall be classed as "employees." Nothing herein shall be construed to prohibit the publication by county officials of statistics, so classified as to prevent the identification of particular reports or returns and items thereof; or the inspection of the records by duly qualified employees of the tax departments of the State of Georgia, the United States, and other local governments.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-40), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96)
Sec. 22-71. - Inspection of books and records, penalty for deficiency.
In any case, the department, through its officers, agents, employees, or representatives, may inspect the books of the business or person for which the returns are made. The books or records shall be submitted for inspection within 30 days. Failure of submission of such books or records within 30 days shall be grounds for revocation of the license currently existing to do business in the county. Adequate records shall be kept by the licensee for examination by the department. If, after examination of the books or records, it is determined that a deficiency occurs as a result of under-reporting, a penalty of 20 percent shall be assessed, plus interest, as heretofore provided from the time the tax should have been paid (if correctly returned) until paid.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-41), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96)
Sec. 22-72. - License revoked for failure to pay tax, file returns, or permit inspection of books.
Upon the failure of any business to pay the occupation tax or any part thereof before it becomes delinquent, or upon failure to make any returns within the time required herein, or upon failure to make a true return, or upon failure to permit inspection of its books as above provided, any business tax registration granted by the county under this article permitting the business to operate in the county, shall be, ipso facto, revoked. No new business tax registration shall be granted by the county for the operation of a business for which any part of the occupation tax herein provided for is at that time unpaid, or to a person who has failed to submit adequate records as requested by the department, in accordance with provisions of this article. In the case of those businesses where the county cannot suspend the right of the licensee to conduct business, the imposition of civil penalties shall be permitted and pursued by the county in the case of delinquent occupation tax.
(Ord. No. 95-4, § I(2-16-42), 1-3-95; Ord. No. 96-09, § I, 1-16-96)
