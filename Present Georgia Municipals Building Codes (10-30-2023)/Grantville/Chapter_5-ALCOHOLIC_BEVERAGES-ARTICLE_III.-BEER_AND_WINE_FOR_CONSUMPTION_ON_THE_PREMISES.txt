ARTICLE III. - BEER AND WINE FOR CONSUMPTION ON THE PREMISES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 5-149. - Purpose.
The purpose of this article is to establish the regulation and taxation of the sale of beer and wine for consumption on the premises. Retail sale of beer and wine under this article shall only occur in a restaurant or food establishment wherein 50 percent of the gross revenues result from the sale of food.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.010; Ord. No. 635, § 1, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-150. - Definitions.
For the purpose of this article, the definitions set forth in the Official Code of Georgia, the existing ordinances of the city, and Webster's dictionary primary definitions shall apply in the order set forth herein.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.020; Ord. No. 635, § 1.1, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-151. - Title 3 of the Official Code of Georgia incorporated by reference.
For the purposes of this article, there are incorporated by reference the following:
(1)
The statutes set forth in title 3 of the Official Code of Georgia (O.C.G.A. § 3-1-1 et seq.) regarding alcoholic beverage regulation and those pertaining to beer and wine as ordinances of the city. All regulatory and prosecutorial powers granted the commissioner in title 3 are hereby granted to the mayor, police chief and other approved officers of the city; and
(2)
All laws and regulations of the federal and state government regarding the regulation of beer and wine.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.030; Ord. No. 635, § 1.2, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-152. - Compliance with article required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or offer for sale at wholesale or retail any alcoholic beverages within the city without having first complied with the provisions of this article.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.040; Ord. No. 635, § 1.3, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-153. - Inspection of stock.
The mayor, city clerk, chief of police, or other officer directed by the mayor and council may make inspections to determine whether or not the terms and provisions of this article are complied with. Reasonable stacking and placing regulations may be created by the mayor with the advice and consent of the rest of the council.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.050; Ord. No. 635, § 1.4, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-154. - Penalties and punishment; suspension or revocation of license and permit by mayor and council.
Any person who shall violate any of the provisions of this article, or who shall assist another to violate the provisions of this article shall be deemed guilty of a crime against the city, and upon conviction punished as provided in the city Charter establishing the municipal court; and in addition thereto, the license of such offender may be suspended or revoked in the discretion of the city council.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.060; Ord. No. 635, § 1.5, 5-24-1993)
Secs. 5-155—5-170. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - LICENSES, APPLICATIONS AND ISSUANCES

Sec. 5-171. - Fee.
The annual license fee shall be $525.00.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.070; Ord. No. 635, § 2, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-172. - License required for sale; restrictions on applicant.
No alcoholic beverage shall be sold except under a license granted by the mayor and council, as provided herein. Restrictions for applying for a license under this chapter shall include the requirement that the applicant has resided continuously for the one-year period next preceding the date of application, and that the applicant is a citizen of the state and the United States with full rights of citizenship. Good moral character shall also be a requirement for application.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.080; Ord. No. 635, § 2.1, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-173. - Application, deposit, time to pay for initial license; council approval.
The mayor shall create a standard application for licensure hereunder which shall include the filing of all relevant and pertinent data needed to make an informed decision by the council including but not limited to the filing of a state department of revenue form ATT-17 and a survey by a registered surveyor of the location. Failure to furnish information requested in the application within 30 days of request shall automatically dismiss the application. The applicant shall deposit a nonrefundable processing fee of $100.00 to cover the expense of processing and investigating the application. No application shall be acted upon until after proper notice and public hearing not less than 15 days nor more than 45 days after the completion of a timely filed application. Upon approval the licensing fees shall be paid within ten days.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.090; Ord. No. 635, § 2.2, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-174. - Payment of license fees; amount; license for each location; penalty.
(a)
All annual license fees hereunder shall be paid in advance on or before January 1 of each year and any new licensee granted hereunder during a calendar year shall pay the full license fee without proration; provided however that if a license is granted after July 1, the license fee shall be one-half of the annual license for such calendar year.
(b)
Any person licensed hereunder shall be required to renew his license on or before January 1 of each year. A penalty of ten percent of the license fee shall be assessed for failure to renew the license on or before March 1; after March 1 the offender shall be subject to citation and a daily fine of up to $50.00 and five days in jail for each day beer and wine continues to be sold thereafter.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.100; Ord. No. 635, § 2.3, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-175. - Restriction on other mercantile establishment.
No consumption license will be issued to any applicant unless at least 50 percent of the establishment's gross sales volume is in food service. Licensees shall file reports every six months verifying that the requirement herein has been met. Further, no sales shall occur in such establishment until it has been completed in accordance with presubmitted plans and specification and is in conformity with applicable ordinances of the city.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.110; Ord. No. 635, § 2.4, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-176. - Grounds for suspension or revocation; notice; hearing; refund.
(a)
Any license which has been issued or which may hereafter be issued by the city to a licensee hereunder may be suspended or revoked for due cause after a hearing is held by the mayor and council for the purpose of considering any such suspension or revocation. At least five days prior to such hearing written notice of the time, place and purpose of such hearing, a statement of the charges upon which such hearing is to be held shall be given to the holder of such license for which suspension or revocation is to be considered.
(b)
Due cause for the suspension or revocation of a license shall consist of a violation of any laws or ordinances applicable to or regulating such business.
(c)
The mayor is hereby authorized to immediately suspend any license issued hereunder in any emergency situation such as civil disorders or natural disaster, or in any situation wherein the mayor deems such immediate suspension necessary for the protection of health and welfare of the citizens of the city.
(d)
When a license is suspended or revoked, the city shall not refund any portion of the license tax to the holder of such suspended or revoked license.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.120; Ord. No. 635, § 2.5, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-177. - Automatic revocation of city license upon revocation of state license.
Whenever the state shall revoke any permit or license to any licensee hereunder, the licensee's city license shall thereupon be automatically revoked without any further action by the mayor and council or any officer of the city.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.130; Ord. No. 635, § 2.6, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-178. - Restriction of transfer of license.
No license granted in accordance with the provisions of this article shall be transferrable.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.140; Ord. No. 635, § 2.7, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-179. - Employees of licensees to be fingerprinted; procedure.
No person may be employed to serve beer and wine by an establishment holding a license hereunder until such person has been fingerprinted by the police department and has been issued a letter of clearance by the police department indicating the person to be employed to serve beer and wine is eligible for such employment.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.150; Ord. No. 635, § 2.8, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-180. - Hours of operation.
A licensee for the sale of beer and wine shall not engage in the sale of such beer and wine except between the hours of 8:30 a.m. and 11:45 p.m., Mondays through Saturdays.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.160; Ord. No. 635, § 2.9, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-181. - Keeping or storing without appropriate license.
It shall be unlawful for any person operating under a license for the sale of beer or wine or both to store or have therein any whiskey or intoxicating liquors without an appropriate license therefor.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.170; Ord. No. 635, § 2.10, 5-24-1993)
Secs. 5-182—5-205. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - EXCISE TAX

Sec. 5-206. - Excise tax, levied; amount, payment, duties of dealers and wholesalers.
In addition to all other taxes or license fees heretofore or hereafter imposed upon retail dealers engaged in the city in the business of selling beer and wine, as defined under the laws of the state, there is imposed and levied upon all hereafter described dealers within the city an excise tax.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.180; Ord. No. 635, § 3.1, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-207. - Payment; report.
The excise tax levied under this division shall be paid and collected in the following manners: The taxes shall be paid by the wholesale dealer on or before the tenth day of the month following the calendar month in which the beverages are sold or disposed of within the county by said wholesale dealer. Said wholesale dealer shall also file a report itemizing for the preceding calendar month the exact quantities of alcoholic beverages by size and type of container, sold during the month within the city. The wholesale dealer shall file the report and pay the tax at the office of the city clerk.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.190; Ord. No. 635, § 3.2, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-208. - Records and invoices.
Each wholesale dealer or distributor selling, shipping, or delivering beer or wine to any retail dealer and any retail dealer in the city shall, as a condition to the privilege of conducting business in the city:
(1)
Keep and maintain true and correct records, including invoices, of all sales, shipments or deliveries of beer and wine to each retail dealer in the city, such records to be preserved for a period of not less than one year and to be made available on request for the inspection of any duly authorized representative of the city.
(2)
Collect from each retail dealer in the city at time of delivery of beer and wine, the amount of tax due under the terms of this section and hold the same in trust for the city until such tax is remitted to the city on or before the tenth day of each calendar month.
(3)
On or before the tenth day of each calendar month, make a verified and comprehensive report to the city which shall correctly show and reflect all sales and deliveries of beer and wine to or for retail dealers in the city for the calendar month immediately preceding the date of said report.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.200; Ord. No. 635, § 3.3, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-209. - Noncompliance by wholesale dealer; distributor.
Any wholesale dealer or distributor failing or refusing to make the reports or pay the taxes required herein, shall be subject to a fine for each day beyond which the reports or taxes were due of up to $50.00 per day and ten days in jail and may be subject to the suspension or revocation of license to do business in the city.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.210; Ord. No. 635, § 3.4, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-210. - Unlawful retail sales and deliveries.
It shall be a violation of this article for any person, firm or corporation to sell at retail or delivery within the city any beer or wine on which the taxes herein provided for have not been paid.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.220; Ord. No. 635, § 3.5, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-211. - Requirements of wholesalers.
All wholesale dealers or distributors shall be licensed by the state revenue department and shall comply with all laws and regulations of the state revenue department before they sell or deliver any beer or wine in the city. Deliveries and sales shall be made only to retailers licensed under the provisions of this article, and all deliveries shall be made in conveyances owned and operated by such wholesale dealers or distributors.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.230; Ord. No. 635, § 3.6, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-212. - Retailers to purchase from licensed wholesalers only.
Retail dealers in beer and wine licensed under the provisions of this article shall not buy or accept deliveries of beer or wine from wholesalers, dealers or distributors, except who are licensed by the state revenue department.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.240; Ord. No. 635, § 3.7, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-213. - Regulations governing retail storage.
All licensed retailers shall store all beer and wine on the premises for which the license was issued and at no other place. All beer and wine shall be available at all times for inspection by the mayor and council, or their duly authorized representatives. Any beer and wine found in any retailer's stock that is sold or distributed by a wholesaler who is not licensed in accordance with the laws of the state to make sales and deliveries in the city shall be subject to immediate confiscation.
(Prior Code, § 5.24.250; Ord. No. 635, § 3.8, 5-24-1993)
Sec. 5-214. - Grantville Recreation Fund account; establishment and use.
Fifty percent of all revenues derived by the city from the sale of beer and wine in food establishments shall be deposited into an account to be named "The Grantville Recreation Fund" account and be used exclusively for the development, building, enhancement, and maintenance of the recreation facilities in the city and that no funds from this account shall be spent without discussion and agreement by majority vote of the council. These funds are to be spent where the council feels the need is greatest and where benefit will be derived by the greatest number of citizens. There is no predetermined formula or division intended between any of the locations, but will be used as needed to benefit as many citizens as possible.
(Ord. No. 665.5, 3-25-1996)
Secs. 5-215—5-234. - Reserved.
