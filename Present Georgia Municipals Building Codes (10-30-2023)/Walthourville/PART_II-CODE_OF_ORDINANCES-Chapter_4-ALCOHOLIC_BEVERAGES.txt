Chapter 4 - ALCOHOLIC BEVERAGES
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 4-1. - Excise taxation.
The following excise tax on alcoholic beverages shall be assessed:
(1)
Malt beverages.
a.
Where malt beverages, commonly known as tap or draft beer, are sold in or from a barrel or bulk container, a tax of $6.00 on each container sold containing not more than 15½ gallons and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 15½ gallons.
b.
Where malt beverages are sold in bottles, cans, or other containers, except barrel or bulk containers, a tax of $0.05 per 12 ounces and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 12 ounces.
(2)
Wine. Wine, $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter.
(3)
Distilled spirits. Distilled spirits, excluding fortified wine, $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter.
The tax will be collected by the various wholesalers and distributors who will account to the city once a month for the tax collected together with copies of all invoices for said month.
(Ord. No. 74-05-01, 5-14-1974)
State Law reference— Excise tax on distilled spirits, O.C.G.A. § 3-4-80; excise tax on malt beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-5-80; excise tax on wine, O.C.G.A. § 3-6-60.
Sec. 4-2. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcoholic beverages includes, but is not limited to, beer, wine, distilled spirits and all beverages of a similar nature and a similar alcoholic content.
Licensee means an establishment holding a license to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages.
Premises means the definite closed or partitioned-in locality, whether room, shop or building wherein alcoholic beverages are sold.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 101, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-3. - Premises maintained in sanitary condition; unlawful to permit disturbance of the peace, obscenity, etc.
All premises shall be kept clean and in proper sanitary condition and in full compliance with the provisions and regulations governing the conditions of premises used for storage and sale of food for human consumption. It shall be unlawful to permit any disturbance of the peace or obscenity or any lewd, immoral or improper entertainment, conduct, or practices.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 102, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-4. - Premises.
No consumption on the premises license shall be issued, unless:
(1)
The building in which the premises will be located is complete; and
(2)
The detailed plans of said buildings are attached to the application; or
(3)
Unless proposed plans and specification and a building permit of a proposed building to be built are attached to the application.
The completed building or the proposed building shall comply with ordinances of the city, regulations of the state revenue commissioner, and the laws of the state. The proposed building shall also be subject to final inspection and approval, when completed, by the code enforcement inspector. Each building in which the business will be located shall provide sufficient lighting so that the building itself and all sides of the building are readily visible at all times so as to reveal all of the outside of said building. Each applicant for a consumption on the premises license shall attach to his application evidence of ownership of the building or proposed building or a copy of the lease if the applicant is leasing the building. All premises within a building for which a consumption on the premises license shall be issued, shall afford therein adequate sanitary toilet facilities and shall be adequately illuminated so that all hallways, passageways and open areas may be clearly seen by customers therein.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 103, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-5. - Inspection of premises.
The business premises of the holder of consumption on the premises license shall be open to inspection at any time, and all times, by officers or officials charged to conduct such inspections.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 104, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-6. - Scope of license.
(a)
A licensee for the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises shall have the right to serve beer or wine, provided he shall secure a business license therefor and pay the license fee as specified by the current business license ordinance.
(b)
No licensee shall set or give away, or permit the sale or gift to, or the procuring for, any such alcoholic beverages to any minor, to any person who is mentally incompetent, or to any person who is physically or mentally incapacitated due to the consumption of alcoholic beverages.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 105, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-7. - Possession, consumption by minors; suspension or revocation of license for chapter violations.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any minor to drink or possess any alcoholic beverages, except:
(1)
For medical purposes pursuant to the prescription of a physician duly licensed to practice medicine in this state;
(2)
In connection with a religious ceremony; or
(3)
In the home with the consent of a parent or guardian.
(b)
For violation of any section of this chapter, the licenses shall be subject to suspension or revocation.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 106, 9-11-2001)
State Law reference— Possession of alcoholic beverages by persons under 21 years of age prohibited, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23.
Sec. 4-8. - Factors considered in issuing licenses or in renewing licenses.
In determining whether or not any license applied for hereunder or any renewal or any license shall be granted in addition to all provisions of this chapter and any other provisions of this Code not inconsistent herewith, the following shall be considered in the public interest and welfare:
(1)
The applicant's status as a convicted person, as defined in section 4-9.
(2)
If the applicant is a previous holder of a license to sell alcoholic beverages, whether or not he has violated any law, regulation, or ordinances relating to such business.
(3)
If the applicant is a previous holder of a license to sell alcoholic beverages, the manner in which he conducted the business hereunder as to the necessity for unusual police observation and inspection in order to prevent the violation of any law, regulation or ordinances relating to such business.
(4)
The location for which the license is sought as to traffic congestion, general character or neighborhood, and the effect such an establishment would have on the adjacent and surrounding property values.
(5)
The number of licenses already granted for similar businesses in the trading area of the place for which the license is sought.
(6)
A person whose license issued under the police powers of any governmental entity has been previously suspended or revoked.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 107, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-9. - No licenses issued to convicted persons.
No original license shall be issued to any person organized for pecuniary gain where any individual having any interest either as owner, partner, principal stockholder, directly or indirectly beneficial or absolute, or his spouse, shall have been convicted or shall have taken a plea of nolo contendere within ten years immediately prior to the filing of said application for any felony or misdemeanor of any state or of the United States or any municipal ordinance or county resolution (except traffic violations). The term "conviction" shall include an adjudication of guilt or a plea of guilty or nolo contendere or the forfeiture of a bond when charged with a crime; where the violation is for a misdemeanor, forfeiture of bond, or municipal ordinance or county resolution, the city council after investigation, may waive same as a disqualification.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 108, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-10. - No license granted to applicants not a U.S. citizen and state resident.
No license shall be granted to any applicant who is not a citizen of the United States and a resident of the state. Where the applicant is a corporation, the principal stockholder must meet the requirements of this section and a license shall be issued to the corporation and the principal stockholder. If the applicant is a partnership, the requirements of this section shall apply to all partners.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 109, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-11. - Sale from residence or home prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to dispense or sell alcoholic beverages to any person from any private home or residence.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 110, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-12. - Hours of sale.
It shall be unlawful for any establishment other than grocery and convenience-type stores engaged in the sale of alcoholic beverages to remain open or sell, barter, trade, or give away any such beverages from and after the hour of 2:55 a.m. on Monday through Friday, or to sell, barter, trade or give away such beverages after the hour of 2:55 a.m. on Saturday or to remain open after the hour of 4:00 a.m. on Monday through Saturday. Said premises shall be cleared by 4:00 a.m. to include the parking lot. Such establishments are to remain closed and are prohibited from engaging in the sale of such beverage before the hour of 8:00 a.m. the following day.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 111, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-13. - Sunday, holidays and election day closing.
It shall be unlawful for any establishment engaged in the sale of alcoholic beverages to remain open on Sunday after 2:55 a.m. or on Christmas day. Pursuant to state law, the sale of alcoholic beverages is allowed on statewide and local election days; provided, however, that the person or establishment engaged in the sale of alcoholic beverages has a current city and state alcoholic beverage license.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 112, 9-11-2001)
State Law reference— Sale of alcoholic beverages on election days and Christmas day, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20.
Sec. 4-14. - Operation on Sunday.
(a)
It shall be lawful for any establishment engaged in the sale of alcoholic beverages by drink, other than grocery and convenience-type stores, to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages by drink and to remain open to sell, barter, trade, or give away any such beverages up and until the hour of 2:55 a.m. on Sunday. It shall be lawful for such establishments to remain open until the hour of 4:00 a.m.; although between the hours of 2:55 a.m. and 4:00 a.m. on Sunday, they are prohibited from engaging in the sale of such alcoholic beverages, and the premises are to be cleared by 4:00 a.m. Furthermore, it shall be lawful for any establishment engaged in the sale of alcoholic beverages by drink, other than grocery and convenience-type stores, to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages by drink and to remain open to sell, barter, trade, or give away any such beverages on Sundays from 12:30 p.m. until 12:00 midnight, per state law. The licensing fee for operation on Sundays shall be $100.00 and must have 50 percent food sales to obtain a license.
(b)
Establishments licensed to purchase and sell beer and/or wine are permitted to remain open until 4:00 a.m. each Sunday morning at which time the premises of said establishments shall be cleared including the parking lot. The licensee is reminded of the city ordinance forbidding the sale of any form of alcoholic beverages after 2:55 a.m. Said establishments shall be permitted to purchase and sell beer and/or wine from Sunday afternoon at 12:30 p.m. until Sunday at 12:00 midnight, per state law. The licensee is warned that the first violation of said laws and ordinances witnessed by state, county, city or other law enforcement officers or determined to have occurred by a court of competent jurisdiction shall be sufficient reason for an immediate 30-day suspension of said alcohol licenses by the city. Within the 30-day suspension period, a public hearing shall be held by the mayor and city council at which time the licensee shall have the opportunity to show why such license should not be permanently revoked by the mayor and city council.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 113, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-15. - Sale to minor prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to dispense, sell, or give to any person under the legal drinking age, as defined by state law, any alcoholic beverage, except for medical purposes pursuant to the prescription of a physician duly licensed to practice medicine in this state, or in connection with a religious ceremony, or in the home with the consent of a parent or guardian.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 114, 9-11-2001)
State Law reference— Possession of alcoholic beverages by persons under 21 years of age prohibited, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23.
Sec. 4-16. - Sale or dispensing by minors.
It shall be unlawful for any person under the age required by state law, to dispense or sell to any other person, any alcoholic beverage of any kind or character; provided, however, the provisions of this section shall not prohibit persons under the age required by state law who are employed in supermarkets, convenience stores, breweries or drugstores from selling or handling alcoholic beverages which are sold for consumption off the premises.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 115, 9-11-2001)
State Law reference— Persons under 18 years of age not allowed to serve or sell alcoholic beverages, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-24.
Sec. 4-17. - Misrepresentation of age.
It shall be unlawful for any person under the legal drinking age, as defined by state law, to willfully misrepresent his age to any other person for the purpose of inducing such other person to dispense, sell, or give to the person making such false representation, any alcoholic beverage of any kind or description.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 116, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-18. - Compliance with health standards.
All persons operating establishments for the sale of alcoholic beverages within the city shall comply with the health standards of the city as prescribed by the mayor and city council.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 117, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-19. - Distance from libraries, churches, schools, etc.
(a)
License for on-premises consumption. No original license for consumption on premises shall be issued for any location in the city unless the building in which the business of the proposed location is situated more than 300 feet from any property line of any library, church, shrine, chapel, or a mortuary, or other place used exclusively for religious services, and more than 300 feet from any property line of any school or college campus, and more than 150 feet from any area zoned residential. The distance set out shall be measured in a straight line and shall be computed from the property line of the aforesaid library, church, school, or residential area to the building of the location in which is located the business for which the license is being sought.
(b)
License for off-premises consumption. Licenses for consumption off the premises only may be issued provided the building in which the business of the proposed location is situated more than 150 feet from any area zoned residential or 300 feet from any church when measured from the portal or public entrance of such building where the license is sought to the nearest portal of any church. Such measurement shall be made by following the public streets or roads of the city from such portal or public entrance of the building in which the proposed location is situated to the portal of the said nearest dwelling in an area zoned residential or to the portal of any church, whichever the case may be.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 118, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-20. - Term of license.
No license shall be issued for less than a calendar-year period, or the remainder thereof, and in case of the revocation or surrender of such license before the expiration of such calendar-year period, the holder thereof shall not be entitled to receive any refund whatsoever.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 119, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-21. - Requirements after approval of application.
All licenses must be obtained and fees paid not later than 30 days from the date of approval of the application and if not so obtained, the permit shall be void.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 120, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-22. - Suspension or revocation for due cause.
No license which has been issued or which may hereafter be issued by the city hereunder shall be suspended or revoked, except for due cause as hereinafter defined, and after a hearing and upon the prior three-day written notice to the holder of such license of the time, place and purpose of such hearing and a statement of the charge upon which such hearing shall be held. Due cause for the suspension or revocation of such license shall consist of the violation of any laws, ordinances, or resolution regulating such businesses, or violation of regulations made pursuant to authority granted for the purpose of regulating such business, or for the violation of any state or federal law, or for the violation of any municipal ordinance or county resolution other than traffic ordinances.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 121, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-23. - Renewal.
All licensees hereunder shall be required to apply for renewal of licenses annually on forms prescribed by the mayor and city council and must comply with all the provisions.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 122, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-24. - License a grant or privilege; authority of city to deny application.
Licenses hereunder shall be mere grants or privileges and the mayor and city council shall have the discretion to approve or deny any application for a license to sell alcoholic beverages by the drink.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 123, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-25. - Licenses not transferable: exceptions for partnerships.
(a)
Licenses hereunder shall not be transferable, except as otherwise provided herein. In case of the death of any person holding such a license, or any interest therein, the same may be transferred to the administrator, executor, or the lawful heirs of the deceased person, if otherwise qualified.
(b)
Nothing in this section, however, shall prohibit one or more of the partners in a partnership holding a license to withdraw from the partnership in favor of one or more of the partners who were partners at the time of the issuance of the license. Such a withdrawal shall not, however, serve to bring any new ownership into the partnership.
(c)
Should a transfer of a location be approved, there shall be no pro rata return of any license fee and the new location shall be considered as a new license hereunder.
(d)
However, a licensee may take in partners or additional stockholders where it is determined that the additional capital furnished is to be used exclusively for additional inventory or expanding the facilities of the business or for building new facilities and where it appears that the licensee himself receives directly none of the additional capital invested. Under this section, an additional partner or new principal stockholder must be approved by the mayor and city council.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 124, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-26. - Opening of establishment.
(a)
All applicants for licenses hereunder must, within six months after the approval of said license, open for business the establishment referred to in the license and begin the sale of the product authorized by the said license.
(b)
Failure to open the establishment and begin the sale as referred to above within the six-month period shall serve as automatic forfeiture and cancellation of the unused license and no refund of license fees shall be made to the license holder.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 125, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-27. - Forfeiture of license for nonoperative businesses.
Any holder of a license hereunder who shall begin the operation of the business and sale of the product as authorized in the said license, but who shall, for a period of three consecutive months thereafter, cease to operate the business and sale of the product authorized in said license shall, after said three-month period, automatically forfeit his license; which license shall, by virtue of said failure to operate, be cancelled without the necessity of any further action.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 126, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-28. - Mayor and city council to prescribe application forms and request information; failure of applicant to provide persons for interrogation.
The mayor and city council shall prescribe forms for new or renewal applicants hereunder. All applicants shall furnish all data, information, and records requested of them by the mayor and city council and failure to furnish such data, information, and records within 30 days from the date of such request shall automatically serve to dismiss, with prejudice, the application. Applicants, by filing an application, agree to produce for oral interrogation any person requested by the mayor and city council. Failure to produce such person within 30 days after being requested to do so shall result in the automatic dismissal of such application.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 127, 9-11-2001)
Secs. 4-29—4-59. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - SALE FOR ON-PREMISES CONSUMPTION

Sec. 4-60. - License for sale for consumption on premises authorized.
With the approval of the city council, a license authorizing the sale of alcoholic beverage for consumption on the premises, may be issued. Licenses shall be displayed prominently at all times on the premises for which the same was issued.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 201, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-61. - Registration cards pertaining to on-premises consumption establishments.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for the holder of any retail license hereunder for consumption on the premises of alcoholic beverages to operate a business without having a dealer registration card signed by an official designated by the city.
(b)
Every person working or serving alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises in a place of business shall have an employee registration card setting forth thereon the name of the employer and the location of the place of employment. It shall be unlawful for the holder of any retail license hereunder for consumption on the premises of alcoholic beverages to permit any person to work or serve in or about such place of business, unless such person has such a registration card.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for the holder of any retail license hereunder for consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises to fail to notify the representative, within five days after the termination of employment of any employee working in any place where alcoholic beverages are served.
(d)
Applications for registration cards will be made at the city police department, and a fee is required to cover the cost of laminating the permanent card. Applicants are required to have three 1½-inch by 1½-inch black and white photographs when applying for such registration card. All applicants will be fingerprinted and a copy of these prints will be forwarded to the FBI. A temporary letter will be issued upon application, same being valid for 30 days only. The permanent registration card will be issued within 30 days from the date of application.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 202, 9-11-2001)
State Law reference— Fingerprinting required for issuance of a permit or license, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2(c).
Sec. 4-62. - Misrepresentation of quality, quantity, etc., of alcoholic beverages.
It shall be illegal for licensees hereunder to add to the contents of a bottle or to refill empty bottles or in any other manner to misrepresent the quantity, quality, or brand name of any alcoholic beverage.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 203, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-63. - Display of prices.
All licensees hereunder shall display in prominent places on the premises the current prices of wines, champagnes, or malt beverages by the drink.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 204, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-64. - No sales outside of licensed premises.
It shall be unlawful for any sales to be made outside of the building, premises, or place of business licensed for such sale.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 205, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-65. - Advertising.
No licensee hereunder shall advertise in any manner, except as provided by state law.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 206, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-66. - Violations by licensee.
Violation of any of the provisions of this chapter by the holder of a license hereunder shall subject the holder of such license to revocation of said license.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 207, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-67. - Duty of management to keep copy of chapter on premises.
It shall be the duty of the management of premises licensed hereunder to maintain a copy of this chapter on such premises and to instruct each and every employee of the terms thereof.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 208, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-68. - Sale without license unlawful.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell alcoholic beverages for use or consumption on the premises without the license required herein.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 209, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-69. - License fee; refunds.
Each original application for license under this article shall be accompanied by a certified check or cash or for the full amount of the license fee, together with a separate certified check or cash, in an amount to be determined from time to time by the mayor and city council, to defray the cost of investigation and report. If the application is denied and the license refused, the deposit representing payment shall be retained as the cost of filing and processing the application.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 210, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-70. - Compliance with applicable rules.
All applicable state laws, rules and regulations governing the sale of alcoholic beverages must be complied with by any applicant for a license hereunder.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 211, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-71. - Certification by applicant of knowledge of regulations.
No person shall be approved for a license hereunder, or approved for employment by a licensee, unless such person shall have read these regulations in full text and certify in writing the fact of such reading and knowledge thereof.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 212, 9-11-2001)
Secs. 4-72—4-100. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - SALE FOR OFF-PREMISES CONSUMPTION

Sec. 4-101. - License of sale for consumption off-premises authorization.
With the approval of the mayor and city council, a license authorizing the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption off the premises may be issued.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 301, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-102. - Sale of beer and wine in grocery stores.
Grocery stores shall be allowed to sell beer and wine by the carton (not for consumption on premises), provided they have the required beer and wine license.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 302, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-103. - License fee—Refunds.
Each original application for license under this article shall be accompanied by a certified check or cash or for full amount of the license fee, together with a separate certified check or cash, in an amount to be determined from time to time by the mayor and city council, to defray the cost of investigation and report. If the application is denied and the license refused, the deposit representing payment shall be retained as the cost of filing and processing the application.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 303, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-104. - Same—Initial license, renewal; payment.
An annual license fee shall be charged for the initial license issued to any licensee hereunder, and a like amount for each renewal license. All license fees hereunder shall be paid in advance on or before December 31 of each year and any new license granted hereunder during the calendar year shall pay the full license fee without proration.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 304, 9-11-2001)
Sec. 4-105. - Sale of alcoholic beverages by the drink not permitted.
Licenses hereunder shall not permit the sale of alcoholic beverages by the drink.
(Ord. No. 01-09-01A, § 305, 9-11-2001)
