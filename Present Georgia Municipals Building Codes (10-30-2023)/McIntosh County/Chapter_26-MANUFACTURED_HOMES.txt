State Law reference— Standards and requirements for construction, installation, and licensing of manufactured homes and mobile homes, O.C.G.A. §§ 8-2-110 et seq., 8-2-130 et seq., and 8-2-160 et seq.

Sec. 26-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Certificate of permanent location means a certificate properly obtained and filed with the clerk of superior court, as required by O.C.G.A. § 8-2-182.
Certificate of removal from permanent location means a certificate properly obtained and filed, as required by O.C.G.A. § 8-2-184.
Chief appraiser means the chief appraiser and any member of the appraisal staff in the office of tax assessors of the county.
Clerk of superior court means the clerk of superior court of McIntosh County.
Decal means the mobile home location permit obtainable from the tax commissioner and referred to in O.C.G.A. § 48-5-492.
Home means a manufactured home or mobile home, but shall not include a home for which a certificate of permanent location has issued and been properly filed and shall not include any other home which is exempt from the requirements of O.C.G.A. § 48-5-492.
Magistrate court means the magistrate court of the county.
Manufactured home means a new or used structure, transportable in one or more sections, which, in the traveling mode, is eight body feet or more in width or 40 body feet or more in length or, when erected on site, is 320 or more square feet and which is built on a permanent chassis and designed to be used as a dwelling with or without a permanent foundation when connected to the required utilities and includes the plumbing, heating, air conditioning, and/or electrical systems contained therein; such term also has the meaning specified in O.C.G.A. § 8-2-160(4).
Manufactured home statute refers to the ad valorem taxation of motor vehicles and mobile homes, article 10, title 48, chapter 5 (O.C.G.A. § 48-5-440 et seq.).
Mobile home means a new or used structure, transportable in one or more sections, which, in the traveling mode, is eight body feet or more in width or 40 body feet or more in length or, when erected on site, is 320 or more square feet and which is built on a permanent chassis and designed to be used as a dwelling with or without a permanent foundation when connected to the required utilities and includes the plumbing, heating, air conditioning, and/or electrical systems contained therein; such term also has the meaning specified in O.C.G.A. § 8-2-160(6).
Owner means a person or an entity, other than a lien holder or security interest holder, having an interest in a home or being named on a title as an owner of a home. Such term shall also include the person or entity which leases a home. The term "owner" also has the meaning specified in O.C.G.A. § 40-2-21.
(Ord. of 7-10-2007(1), intro. 4)
Sec. 26-2. - Penalties.
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by law, every person or entity convicted of a violation of any provision of this chapter shall be punished pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-5-440 et seq. Each day a home owner is in violation of O.C.G.A. § 48-5-492 shall constitute a separate offense. The remedies and penalties stated in this chapter shall be in addition to the remedies and penalties stated in O.C.G.A. § 48-5-451.
(Ord. of 7-10-2007(1), § 7)
Sec. 26-3. - Adoption by reference.
The ad valorem taxation of motor vehicles and mobile homes, article 10, title 48, chapter 5 (O.C.G.A. § 48-5-440 et seq.) is hereby adopted as an ordinance of the county with the effect as if restated herein verbatim.
(Ord. of 7-10-2007(1), § 1)
Sec. 26-4. - Display of decal.
The owner of any home shall display the decal on the exterior portion of the home. Such display of decal shall be on the side of the home facing the nearest public roadway. If the home is not visible from a public roadway, then such decal shall be displayed on the side of the home facing the driveway which leads to or near the home.
(Ord. of 7-10-2007(1), § 2)
Sec. 26-5. - Enforcement.
The chief appraiser of the county may enforce this chapter by issuance of a citation furnished by the board or by any other written means which complies with O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63, together with a notice informing the owner of the requirements of O.C.G.A. § 48-5-492 and state department of revenue Rule No. 560-11-9-.05. The citation shall require the owner to appear in person before the magistrate court at a certain time and date, which date shall be not earlier than 15 days nor more than 30 days from the date the citation is issued to the owner. If the name of the owner is not known to the chief appraiser, then such notice and citation shall issue to the occupant of the home.
(Ord. of 7-10-2007(1), § 3)
Sec. 26-6. - Owner to notify chief appraiser.
The owner of any home which has obtained a certificate of removal from permanent location shall promptly notify the chief appraiser and the office of tax assessors of such certificate.
(Ord. of 7-10-2007(1), § 4)
Sec. 26-7. - Prosecution.
This chapter may be prosecuted in the magistrate court, pursuant to article 4, title 15, chapter 10 (O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq.).
(Ord. of 7-10-2007(1), § 5)
Sec. 26-8. - Violation.
Violations of this chapter may be prosecuted by the county attorney or by the chief appraiser.
(Ord. of 7-10-2007(1), § 6)
