Sec. 12-166. - License.
(a)
No person shall conduct business as a pawnbroker, or maintain an establishment for the purposes of conducting business as a pawnbroker, until a business license has been obtained from the city administrator. Such license application shall be made to the city administrator and license issued only upon approval by the governing body.
(b)
No action on any application for pawnbroker's license shall be taken until the chief of police has reviewed and investigated such application and forwarded his recommendation thereon to the governing body for its consideration, approval, or denial.
(c)
No pawnbroker's license shall be issued to any person or to any pawnbroker who employs anyone who has been convicted of any crime involving as an element thereof the theft of property.
(d)
The annual regulatory fee for each pawnbroker doing business within the city or for each individual establishment maintained for the purpose of doing business as a pawnbroker within the city shall approximate the reasonable cost of the actual regulatory activity performed by the city. A schedule of regulatory fees imposed by the city shall be on file with the city clerk.
(e)
Any person violating any subsection of this section shall be subject to revocation of his license to do business.
(Ord. No. 84-1, §§ 6-64—6-67, 6-72; Ord. No. 2015-03, § 2, 3-2-2015 )
Sec. 12-167. - Hours of operation.
A pawnbroker shall not conduct business in the city except between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 7:00 p.m. or sundown, whichever occurs first, Monday through Saturday.
(Ord. No. 84-1, § 6-67, 2-6-84)
Sec. 12-168. - Records.
(a)
Each pawnbroker licensed under this article shall furnish to the chief of police each week a list of every item pledged with him or sold to him during the previous week. Such list may be a copy of the information required to be kept by O.C.G.A. § 44-12-132 or any other list maintained containing the same information as follows:
(1)
The date of transaction.
(2)
The name of the person conducting the transaction.
(3)
The name, age and address of the customer, a description of the general appearance of the customer and a distinctive number from the customer's driver's license. In the event the customer has no valid driver's license, the pawnbroker shall make and retain a photograph of such customer.
(4)
An identification and description of the pledged or purchased goods, including, if reasonably available, the serial, model or other number, and all identifying marks inscribed thereon.
(5)
The number of the receipt or pawn ticket.
(6)
The price paid or the amount loaned.
(7)
The number of the check issued for the purchase price or loan if payment is made by check.
(8)
The maturity date of the transaction.
(9)
The signature of the customer.
(b)
Entries shall appear in chronological order in ink. No blank lines may be left between entries. No obliterations, alterations, or erasures may be made. Corrections shall be made by drawing a line of ink through the entry without destroying its legibility. The book shall be open to the inspection of any duly authorized law enforcement officer during the ordinary hours of business, or at any reasonable time.
(Ord. No. 84-1, § 6-68, 2-6-84)
State Law reference— Records, O.C.G.A. § 44-12-132 et seq.
Sec. 12-169. - Thumbprints.
Any pawnbroker who shall receive as a pledge or purchase any item, good or ware valued in excess of ten dollars ($10.00) or any revolver, pistol, shotgun, rifle or any other firearm shall be required to take a thumbprint of such person pledging or selling any of the above items. Such thumbprints must be kept by the pawnbroker as part of the records required by the state and for the period of time required therein.
(Ord. No. 84-1, § 6-69, 2-6-84)
Sec. 12-170. - Minors.
No pawnbroker shall have any business dealings as a pawnbroker with a minor, nor shall a pawnbroker's license be issued to a minor, nor shall a pawnbroker employ a minor to assist him in his business.
(Ord. No. 84-1, § 6-70, 2-6-84)
State Law reference— Records, O.C.G.A. § 44-12-132 et seq.
Sec. 12-171. - Stolen goods.
It shall be the duty of every pawnbroker to report to the police any article pledged with him or which is sought to be pledged with him, if he shall have reason to believe the article was stolen, or lost and found by the person attempting to pledge it in the case of a lost article. No pawnbroker shall accept any item, good or ware upon which a manufacturer's identity or serial number has been removed, obliterated, or in any way caused to become unreadable.
(Ord. No. 84-1, § 6-71, 2-6-84)
Sec. 12-172. - Certain purchased articles to be held thirty days.
Any pawnbroker or person operating under a pawnbroker's license who purchases goods, other than those purchased through wholesalers or on order in the normal course of business, taking full title thereto (the word "goods" being used in the broadest sense and including all kinds of personal property) shall hold such goods purchased for at least thirty (30) days before disposing of same by sale, transfer, shipment or otherwise.
(Ord. No. 84-1, § 6-72, 2-6-84)
