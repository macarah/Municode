PART II. - THE PRELIMINARY PLAT
ARTICLE VII. - PRELIMINARY PLAT REQUIREMENTS
The preliminary plat for a subdivision shall be clearly and legibly drawn at a scale of not less than one hundred (100) feet to one (1) inch. The sheet size shall not exceed forty-eight (48) inches by thirty-six (36) inches, provided, however, a scale of two hundred (200) feet to one (1) inch may be used to avoid sheets in excess of forty-eight (48) inches by thirty-six (36) inches. The minimum sheet size shall be eight and one-half (8½) inches by eleven (11) inches. The city engineer may approve other scales and sheet sizes as deemed appropriate.

Sec. A. - The preliminary plat.
The preliminary plat shall consist of a scale map or maps depicting the following:
(1)
Streets, street names, and right-of-way widths.
(2)
Other rights-of-way and easements.
(3)
Lot lines drawn to scale.
(4)
Lot and block identification.
(5)
Minimum building setback lines.
(6)
Subdivision boundary lines and total subdivision land area in acres.
(7)
Key plan (if more than one (1) sheet), legend, and notes.
(8)
Dimensions, data and lines as necessary to clearly indicate that all applicable requirements of the zoning ordinance are met. Where appropriate, such information may be shown on the preliminary site plan.
(9)
Title, scale, north arrow, and date.
(10)
Name, address and telephone number of owner of record and of subdivider.
(11)
Proposed name of subdivision and its acreage.
(12)
The location of the subdivision and developments in its vicinity including land lot and district numbers and lines, and city and county names and limit lines.
(13)
Name of owners of record of adjoining land.
(14)
Location of existing structures on the tract.
(15)
Natural features such as lakes, ponds, streams, creeks, state waters, wetlands, 100-year flood plains, and other significant features. The source of flood plain information shall be shown.
(16)
Location of all known existing or previous landfills.
(17)
Topography with mean sea level contours at intervals no greater than five (5) feet. The source of topographic information shall be shown.
(18)
Pavement widths.
(19)
Proposed public or semi-public sites for parks, churches and other community facilities, if any.
(20)
Generalized storm drainage plan including catchbasin locations, tentative sewer and culvert sizes, spot elevations of key system elements, final disposition of surface water.
(21)
Sequence and geographic limits of construction phases proposed for large scale development.
(22)
A written summary of the proposed subdivision giving information as to the overall development plan including, as appropriate, the types and size of structures, number of housing units, types of land uses, anticipated traffic generation, and other pertinent information so that the affects of the subdivision can be considered by the planning and zoning board.
(23)
Description of the anticipated utility systems required to serve the proposed subdivision including projected average and peak demands or flows for potable water, and fire protection.
(24)
Description of proposed stormwater management practices for the subdivision including the ownership and proper maintenance provisions of all stormwater detention facilities within the subdivision.
(25)
Such additional information as may be reasonably required to obtain an adequate understanding of the subdivision.
Sec. B. - Electrical distribution.
The city owns and operates its own electrical distribution system. The preliminary plat shall be supplemented by having the electrical load requirements to each lot, building, or facility to be served shown thereon. Any loads requiring other than 120/240 volt singlephase service shall be specified in detail.
Underground electrical distribution is the normal and preferred method of construction.
The subdivider shall be responsible for payment of an amount equal to one hundred (100) percent of the cost of underground construction to the city.
(Ord. No. 07-05, § 1, 7-9-07)
Sec. C. - Wastewater disposal.
Unless the subdivision will be served by any public sewer system, the preliminary plat shall be supplemented by an engineering report setting forth in detail how disposal of wastewater will be accomplished. The engineering report shall be acceptable to all county and state agencies having jurisdiction with respect to wastewater disposal matters.
ARTICLE VIII. - PRELIMINARY PLAT PROCEDURES

Sec. A. - Application for preliminary plat approval.
Submission of a preliminary plat to the planning and zoning board by a subdivider shall occur when the following have been received at the office of the city clerk or city administrator.
(1)
Preliminary plat filing fee of two hundred fifty dollars ($250.00).
(2)
Eight (8) copies of the preliminary plat.
Sec. B. - Review procedures for preliminary plat.
The following procedures shall be followed in reviewing the preliminary plat:
(1)
The planning and zoning board by way of the city clerk or city administrator shall distribute at least one (1) copy of the proposed preliminary plat to the city engineer who shall thereupon analyze and acquaint himself with the preliminary plat and forward comments to the planning and zoning board. In order to have review action completed for the next planning and zoning board meeting, the preliminary plat must be submitted at least thirty (30) days prior to the meeting.
(2)
After review of the comments by the city engineer, the city clerk or city administrator shall notify the subdivider of the time and place at which the planning and zoning board will act upon the preliminary plat. The city clerk or city administrator shall advise the subdivider in such notification if an unfavorable recommendation is to be made to the planning and zoning board and offer the subdivider the opportunity of making written withdrawal of his application and arranging for an early conference to discuss the reason for an unfavorable recommendation. If the subdivider elects to withdraw his application, he shall not be refunded the preliminary plat filing fee nor shall he be required to pay an additional filing fee in the event he resubmits his application within one (1) year after the initial submittal.
(3)
The planning and zoning board shall be furnished recommendations of the city engineer, after which the planning and zoning board shall tentatively approve or disapprove the preliminary subdivision. The ground for disapproval of any plat shall be stated upon the records of the planning and zoning board.
Sec. C. - No construction work nor site clearance authorized.
No construction work nor site clearance shall be deemed to have been authorized on a proposed land subdivision as a result of any action taken by the planning and zoning board in connection with approving or disapproving a preliminary plat.
Sec. D. - Expiration of preliminary plat approval.
Approval of a preliminary plat shall expire and be null and void after a period of one (1) year unless an extension of time is approved by the planning and zoning board.
