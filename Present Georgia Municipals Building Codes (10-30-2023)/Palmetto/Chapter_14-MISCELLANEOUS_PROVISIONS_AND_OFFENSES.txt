Chapter 14 - MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS AND OFFENSES
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 14-1. - State law misdemeanors.
Any act the commission of which constitutes a misdemeanor under the laws of the state is prohibited within the city, and if committed within the city, is hereby declared to be an offense against the city; provided however, that the penalty therefor shall be as prescribed by statute.
(Code 1977, § 12-1)
Sec. 14-2. - Air pollution control; adoption of county regulation.
The air pollution control health regulation, number 24 of Fulton County, a copy of which is on file in the city administrator's office, is hereby adopted by reference and made a part of this Code, in its entirety, except that section 22.1 is struck in its entirety and the following inserted in lieu thereof:
"Section 22.1: Any person who shall violate any of the provisions of, or who fails to perform any duty imposed by this regulation or who violates any order or determination of any lawful authority promulgated pursuant to this regulation shall be punished as provided in section 1-9 of the Code of Ordinances, and in addition thereto may be enjoined from continuing such violation. Each day violations occur shall constitute a separate offense."
(Code 1977, § 11-3)
Secs. 14-3—14-25. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - OFFENSES INVOLVING PROPERTY RIGHTS

Sec. 14-26. - Trespassing.
It shall be unlawful for any person within the city to knowingly and without authority:
(1)
Enter upon the land or premises of another person or into any part of any vehicle, railroad car, aircraft or watercraft of another person, for an unlawful purpose.
(2)
Enter upon the land or premises of another person or onto any part of any vehicle, railroad car, aircraft or watercraft of another person, after receiving, prior to such entry notice from the owner or rightful occupant that such entry is forbidden.
(3)
Remain upon the land or premises of another person or within the vehicle, railroad car, aircraft or watercraft of another person, after receiving notice from the owner or rightful occupant to depart.
(4)
To enter or remain upon any land or premises of the city, or any other public place within the city, after the expiration of any posted closing time, or after being notified by an official responsible for the control or management of such public place that such is at that time not open to the public.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 12-27, 9-8-80)
State Law reference— Trespass, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-21.
Sec. 14-27. - Vandalism.
It shall be unlawful for any person within the city to:
(1)
Intentionally damage any property of another without his consent and the damage thereto is one hundred dollars ($100.00) or less.
(2)
Knowingly and maliciously interfere with the possession or use of the property of another person without his consent.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 12-26, 9-8-80)
State Law reference— Criminal damage to property, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-23.
Sec. 14-28. - Advertising matter.
(a)
Placing in or on motor vehicles. It shall be unlawful for any person within the city, without the consent of the owner or person in charge of a motor vehicle which is parked on a public street or on private grounds, to place on or inside such parked motor vehicle any placard, handbill, card or advertising matter.
(b)
Distribution generally. It shall be unlawful for any person distributing or causing to be distributed within the city circulars, handbills, papers or other printed advertising through the city, to throw or scatter the same upon any of the streets, sidewalks or alleys or other public places in the city; and further, it shall be unlawful for any person receiving circulars, handbills, papers or other printed advertising matter from distributors, to throw or scatter the same upon the streets, sidewalks, alleys or other public places in the city.
(c)
Pasting, posting, fastening. It shall be unlawful for any person within the city to paste, print, nail, tack or otherwise fasten any card, banner, handbill, sign, poster or advertisement or notice of any kind, or cause the same to be done, on any curbstone, lamppost, pole, fence, wire, bridge or tree upon any public property within the city, or upon any private property without the written consent of the owner of such property.
(d)
Tearing down, mutilating. It shall be unlawful for any person within the city, without lawful authority, to tear down or mutilate any bill, placard or other advertisement lawfully posted.
(Code 1977, § 12-2)
Sec. 14-29. - Animals in the parks.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person who harbors, possesses or is in charge of a domestic restrained or unrestrained animal (such as dogs, cats, etc.) to bring said animal onto public-owned tennis courts and outdoor basketball courts.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to ride, graze or walk a horse or other type hoofed animal within municipal parks.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person who possesses, harbors, or is in charge of any animal not to immediately remove excrement deposited by the animal while in the municipal park, on municipal athletic fields, or on municipal sidewalks. Animal excrement shall be removed from the park, athletic fields, and municipal sidewalks, and disposed of in a sanitary means.
(d)
It shall be the duty of any person having custody or control of any animal in the municipal park on an athletic field, or on the sidewalk to have in such person's possession a device or equipment for picking up and removal of the animal excrement. The provisions of this section shall not apply to an animal aiding the handicapped (e.g., guide dog) or to an animal when in police or rescue activities.
(Ord. No. 02-03, § 1, 4-1-02)
Secs. 14-30—14-45. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - OFFENSES INVOLVING PUBLIC PEACE AND ORDER

Sec. 14-46. - Affray.
It shall be unlawful for two (2) or more persons to engage in an affray or altercation within the city to the disturbance of the public tranquility.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 12-18, 9-8-80)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-32.
Sec. 14-47. - Public disturbance.
It shall be unlawful for any person to recklessly or knowingly commit any act which may reasonably be expected to prevent or disrupt a lawful meeting, gathering, procession or the public tranquility within the city.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 12-19, 9-8-80)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-34.
Sec. 14-48. - Drinking in public.
It shall be unlawful for any person to consume any spirituous malt or alcoholic beverages in or upon any street, alley, sidewalk, park or other public way or public place within the city or within any public building thereon. Such shall be an offense against the city, punishable by the terms and conditions applicable to the municipal court.
(Code 1977, § 12-3.5)
Sec. 14-49. - Public drunkenness.
It shall be unlawful for any person to be and appear to be in an intoxicated condition on the streets of the town, or in any public place, or place of business patronized by the public, or in an automobile, or within the curtilage of any private residence not his own other than by invitation of the owner or lawful occupant, which condition is made manifest by boisterous or by indecent condition or by act of voicing profane or loud or unbecoming language, within the city.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 12-20, 9-8-80; Ord. No. 99-01, 1-4-99)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-41.
Sec. 14-50. - Loitering.
(a)
Generally. It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to gather, loiter, congregate or assemble with others, on any of the public streets, sidewalks or other public places within the city, in such manner, and/or to such an extent as to prevent or interfere with the free and unobstructed passage or travel of pedestrians thereon or thereover, or with the free and unobstructed access of pedestrians to, and/or from private property or buildings or other structures abutting on such streets, sidewalks or other public places.
(b)
Near election places. It shall be unlawful for any person or persons, within the legal hours of election on any election day, to loiter, gather, congregate or assemble at any point or place on the streets, sidewalks or other public places in the city nearer than twenty-five (25) feet from the entrance or entrances of any voting place or precinct within the city.
(Code 1977, § 12-6)
State Law reference— Loitering or prowling, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-36; obstructing highways, streets, etc., O.C.G.A. § 16-11-43.
Sec. 14-51. - Abusive or obscene language.
It shall be unlawful within the city for any person, without provocation, to use to or of another, in his presence, opprobrious or abusive words which by their very utterance tend to incite an immediate breach of the peace; or words that as a matter of common knowledge and under ordinary circumstances will, when used to or of another person in his presence naturally tend to provoke violent resentment, or words commonly called fighting words.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 12-22, 9-8-80)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-39.
Sec. 14-52. - Litter control.
(a)
General provisions.
(1)
Purpose and intent. The purpose of this section is to protect the public health, safety, environment, and general welfare through the regulation and prevention of litter. The objectives of this section are:
a.
Provide for uniform prohibition throughout the city of any and all littering on public or private property; and
b.
Prevent the desecration of the beauty and quality of life of the city and prevent harm to the public health, safety, environment, and general welfare, including the degradation of water and aquatic resources caused by litter.
(2)
Applicability. This section shall apply to all public and private property within the city.
(3)
Compatibility with other regulations. This section is not intended to interfere with, abrogate, or annul any other ordinances, rule or regulation, statute, or other provision of law. The requirements of this section should be considered minimum requirements, and where any provision of this section imposes restrictions different from those imposed by any other ordinance, rule or regulation, or other provision of law, whichever provisions are more restrictive or impose higher protective standards for human health or the environment shall be considered to take precedence.
(4)
Severability. If the provisions of any subsection, paragraph, subdivision or clause of this section shall be judged invalid by a court of competent jurisdiction, such order of judgment shall not affect or invalidate the remainder of any subsection, paragraph, subdivision or clause of this section.
(b)
Definitions.
Litter means any organic or inorganic waste material, rubbish, refuse, garbage, trash, hulls, peelings, debris, grass, weeds, ashes, sand, gravel, slag, brickbats, metal, plastic, and glass containers, broken glass, dead animals or intentionally or unintentionally discarded materials of every kind and description which are not "waste" as such term in defined in O.C.G.A., § 16-7-51, paragraph 6.
Public or private property means the right-of-way of any street, road or highway; and body of water or watercourse or the shores or beaches thereof; any park, playground, building, refuge, or conservation or recreation area; timberlands or forests; and residential, commercial, industrial, or farm properties.
(c)
Prohibition against littering public or private property or waters. It shall be unlawful for any person or persons to dump, deposit, throw or leave or to cause or permit the dumping, depositing, placing, throwing or leaving of litter on any public or private property in the city or any waters in the city unless:
(1)
The property is designated by the state or by any of its agencies or political subdivisions for the disposal of such litter, and such person is authorized by the proper public authority to use such property;
(2)
The litter is placed into a receptacle or container installed for such purpose on such property; or
(3)
The person is the owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property, or has first obtained consent of the owner or tenant in lawful possession, or unless the act is done under the personal direction of the owner or tenant, all in a manner consistent with the public welfare.
(d)
Vehicle loads causing litter. No person shall operate any motor vehicle with a load on or in such vehicle unless the load on or in such vehicle is adequately secured to prevent the dropping or shifting of material from such load onto the roadway.
(e)
Violations, enforcement and penalties.
(1)
Violations. It shall be unlawful for any person to violate any provision or fail to comply with any of the requirements of this section. Any person who has violated or continues to violate the provisions of this section, may be subject to the enforcement actions outlined in this section or may be restrained by injunction or otherwise sentenced in a manner provided by law.
(2)
Evidence.
a.
Whenever litter is thrown, deposited, dropped or dumped from any motor vehicle, boat, airplane, or other conveyance in violation of this section, it shall be prima facie evidence that the operator of the conveyance has violated this section.
b.
Except as provided in subsection (a), whenever any litter which is dumped, deposited, thrown or left on public or private property in violation of this section is discovered to contain any article or articles, including but not limited to letters, bills, publications or other writing which display the name of the person thereon in such a manner as to indicate that the article belongs or belonged to such person, it shall create a rebuttable presumption that such person has violated this section.
(3)
Penalties. Any person who violates this section shall be guilty of a violation and, upon conviction thereof, shall be punished as follows:
a.
By a fine of not less than two hundred dollars ($200.00) and not more than one thousand two hundred dollars ($1,200.00); and
b.
In addition to the fine set out in subsection a. above, the violator shall reimburse the city for the reasonable cost of removing the litter when the litter is removed or is ordered removed by the city; and
c.
1.
In the sound discretion of the court, the person may be directed to pick up and remove from any public street or highway or public right-of-way for a distance not to exceed one (1) mile any litter he has deposited and any and all litter deposited thereon by anyone else prior to the execution of sentence; or
2.
In the sound discretion of the court, the person may be directed to pick up and remove any and all litter from any public property, private right-of-way, or with prior permission of the legal owner or tenant in lawful possession of such property, any private property upon which it can be established by competent evidence that he has deposited litter. Pick up and removal shall include any and all litter deposited thereon by anyone prior to the execution of sentence; and
d.
The court may publish the names of persons convicted of violating this section.
(d)
Enforcement. All law enforcement agencies, officers and officials of this state or any political subdivision thereof, or any enforcement agency, officer or any official of any commission of this state or any political subdivision thereof, are hereby authorized, empowered and directed to enforce compliance with this article.
(Ord. No. 04-03, § 1, 5-3-04)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 04-03, § 1, adopted May 3, 2004, repealed section 14-52 in its entirety and replaced it with a new section 14-52. Former section 14-52 pertained to litter on highways, land or water of another, and derived from Ord. No. 80-8, § 12-23, adopted Sept. 8, 1980.
State Law reference— Littering, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-40 et seq.
Cross reference— Solid waste, § 24-151 et seq.
Sec. 14-53. - Disorderly conduct.
It shall be unlawful for any person within the corporate limits of the city to violate any of the following provisions of this section:
(1)
Any person who shall act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another whereby any person is placed in fear of safety of his life, limb or health;
(2)
Any person who shall act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another whereby the property of any person is placed in danger of being destroyed or damaged;
(3)
Any person who shall cause, provoke or engage in any fight, brawl or riotous conduct as so to endanger the life, limb, health or property of another;
(4)
Any person who shall assemble or congregate with one (1) or more persons for the purpose or with the intent in gaming;
(5)
Any person who shall frequent any public place with the intent to obtain money from other persons by illegal and fraudulent schemes, artifices or devices;
(6)
Any person who assembles for the purpose of engaging in any fraudulent scheme, device or trick to obtain any valuable thing in any place or from any person in the city or who shall aid or abet therein;
(7)
Any person who shall accost or force his company upon any person;
(8)
Any person who frequents any place where gaming or the illegal sale or possession of alcoholic beverages, narcotics or dangerous drugs is practiced, allowed or tolerated;
(9)
Any person who shall use fighting words directed towards any person who becomes outraged and thus creates a turmoil;
(10)
Any person who shall by acts of violence interfere with another's pursuit of a lawful occupation;
(11)
Any person who shall congregate with one (1) or more persons in or on any public way so as to halt the flow of vehicular or pedestrian traffic and who refuses to clear such public way when ordered by the city police or other lawful authority;
(12)
Any person who shall disrupt, by excessive noise or disorderly actions, the undisturbed activities of any institution of learning, house of worship or any hospital;
(13)
Any person who shall throw any bottle, paper, can, glass, stick, stone, missile or any other debris on public property.
(Ord. No. 90-03, 4-2-90)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 90-03, adopted Apr. 2, 1990, added § 12-29, which section has been redesignated by the editor as § 14-53.
Sec. 14-54. - Curfew.
(a)
Purpose. It is the responsibility of the mayor and council for the city to protect the health, safety and welfare of the citizens of Palmetto. The mayor and council are concerned about the increase in criminal activity engaged in by minors especially those crimes that occur after hours and/or times in which juveniles should be at home. In an effort to keep our community safe by reducing the numbers of crimes that are committed by and against minors after hours, the mayor and council believe that a curfew ordinance is in the best interest of the citizens of Palmetto. Accordingly, the mayor and council for the City of Palmetto find and declare that the purpose of this section is to reduce the number of crimes committed by and against juveniles after hours, and to safeguard the welfare of our children.
(b)
Definitions. The following words and phrases, whenever used in this section, shall be construed as defined in this subsection:
Adult means:
(1)
A person who is at least eighteen (18) years of age and authorized by a parent or guardian to have the care and custody of a minor; or
(2)
An emancipated minor.
City means the City of Palmetto, Georgia.
Curfew hours means between the hours of 11:00 p.m. on any Sunday through Thursday and 6:00 a.m. on the following day, or on Fridays and Saturdays, between the hours of 12:00 midnight and 6:00 a.m. the following day except in circumstances set out in subsection (d) of this section.
Emancipated minor means:
(1)
Any person under the age of eighteen (18) who is married or who is not under the care, custody, and control of a parent, guardian, person standing in loco parentis, or the juvenile court of competent jurisdiction; or
(2)
Any person under the age of eighteen (18) who has had the disabilities of minority removed by a court of competent jurisdiction.
Emergency means an unforeseen combination of circumstances or the resulting state that calls for immediate action. The term includes, but is not limited to, a fire, a natural disaster, an automobile accident, or any situation requiring immediate action to prevent serious bodily injury or loss of life.
Establishment means any privately-owned place of business operated for a profit to which the public is invited, including, but not limited to, any place of amusement or entertainment.
Guardian means:
(1)
A person who, under court order, is the guardian of the person of a minor; or
(2)
A public or private agency with whom a minor has been placed by the court.
Minor means any person less than eighteen (18) years of age.
Operator means any individual, firm, association, partnership or corporation operating, managing, or conducting any establishment. The term includes the members or partners of an association or partnership and officers of a corporation.
Parent means a person who is a natural parent, an adopted parent, or stepparent of another person. ;p0; Public place means any place to which the public has access including, but not limited to, streets, highways, and the common areas of schools, hospitals, apartment houses, office buildings, transport facilities and shops.
Remain means:
(1)
Linger or stay; or
(2)
Fail to leave the premises when requested to do so by an officer or the owner, operator, or the person in control of the premises.
Serious bodily injury means bodily injury that cases death or creates a substantial risk of serious permanent disfigurement, or protracted loss, or impairment of the function of any bodily member or organ.
(c)
(1)
It is unlawful for any person less than eighteen (18) years of age to loiter, wander, stroll or play in or upon the public streets, highways, roads, alleys, parks, playgrounds or other public grounds, public places, public buildings, places of amusement, eating places, vacant lots or any place unsupervised by an adult having the lawful authority to be at such places, between the hours of 11:00 p.m. on Sunday through Thursday and 6:00 a.m. of the following day, or on Fridays and Saturdays, between the hours of 12:00 midnight and 6:00 a.m. the following day, except in circumstances set out in subsection (d) of this section.
(2)
It shall be unlawful for the parent, guardian, or other person having custody or control of any child less eighteen (18) years of age to permit, or by insufficient control, to allow, such child to be in or upon the public streets or any other places listed in this section within the city between the hours of 11:00 p.m. on any Sunday through Thursday and 6:00 a.m. the following day, or on Fridays and Saturdays, between the hours of 12:00 midnight and 6:00 a.m. the following day, except in circumstances set out in subsection (d) of this section.
(3)
It shall be unlawful for any owner, operator, or employee of an establishment to knowingly allow a minor to be in or upon the premises of an establishment in the city between the hours of 11:00 p.m. on any Sunday through Thursday and 6:00 a.m. the following day, or on Fridays and Saturdays between the hours of 12:00 midnight and 6:00 a.m. the following day, except in circumstances set out in subsection (d) of this section. It is a defense to prosecution under this section that the owner, operator or employee of an establishment promptly notified the Palmetto Police Department that a minor was present on the premises of the establishment during curfew hours and refused to leave.
(d)
The provisions of this section shall not apply in the following instances:
(1)
When a minor is accompanied by his or her parent, guardian or other adult person having the lawful care and custody of the minor;
(2)
When the minor is upon an errand directed by his or her parent or guardian or other adult person having the lawful care and custody of such minor;
(3)
When the minor is attending an official school, religious, or other recreational activity supervised by adults and sponsored by the city, a civic organization, or other similar entity that takes responsibility for the minor; or going to or returning home from, without any detour or stop, an official school, religious, or other recreational activity supervised by adults and sponsored by the city, a civic organization, or other similar entity that takes responsibility for the minor;
(4)
When the minor is returning directly home from lawful employment that makes it necessary to be in the above-referenced places during the proscribed period of time;
(5)
When the minor is attending or traveling directly to or from an activity involving the exercise of first amendment rights of free speech, freedom of assembly or free exercise of religion;
(6)
When the minor is in a motor vehicle with parental consent for normal travel, with interstate travel through the city excepted in all cases from the curfew; or
(7)
When the minor is involved in an emergency situation.
(e)
(1)
A police officer of the city who has probable cause to believe that a minor is in violation of this section shall:
a.
Ascertain the name and address of the minor;
b.
Issue the minor a written warning that the minor is in violation of this section; and
c.
Order the minor to go promptly home by a direct route.
(2)
Notwithstanding paragraph (1) of this subsection, a police officer who has probable cause to believe that the minor is in violation of this section, shall transport the minor to the police department if:
a.
The minor has received one (1) previous warning for violation of this article; or
b.
Reasonable grounds exist to believe the minor has engaged in delinquent conduct.
(3)
When a minor is taken to the police department, the minor's parents shall be immediately contacted. If after this contact there is still probable cause to believe that the minor was violating this section, the minor shall be held until the parent comes to take the minor home. If no parent has arrived within two (2) hours, or the parent has refused to retrieve the minor for whatever reason, the minor shall be turned over to the custody of the local juvenile authorities until a parent can take custody of him or her. When the parent arrives, he or she shall be given a copy of this section.
(4)
Second violation.
a.
In case of a second violation by a minor, the chief of police shall, by certified mail, send to the minor's parent written notice of said violation with a warning that any subsequent violation will result in full enforcement of the curfew ordinance, including enforcement of parental responsibility and of applicable penalties. Said notice shall be sent to the minor's parent at his or her last known address and evidence of its mailing shall constitute sufficient notice of the minor's violation.
b.
If, after the mailing of the warning notice set forth in (e)(4)a. herein, a parent violates subsection (c)(2) of this section (in connection with a third violation by a minor), this shall be treated as a first offense by the parent. Upon conviction, a person shall be subject to a fine not to exceed one thousand dollars ($1,000.00) and costs, or imprisonment in the city jail for not more than sixty (60) days, or work on the public streets or work on the public works of the city for not more than sixty (60) days, or be subject to any one (1) or more of the punishments; subject to all limitations contained in the Charter of the city. Each violation of this article shall constitute a separate offense.
(5)
Any minor who shall violate any of the provisions of this section more than two (2) times shall be dealt with through the applicable juvenile court, or such other court as shall have jurisdiction of said minor.
(Ord. No. 2011-02, § 1, 6-6-11)

ARTICLE IV. - OFFENSES INVOLVING PUBLIC SAFETY

Sec. 14-71. - Weapons—Carrying a pistol without a license.
It shall be unlawful for any person to carry on or about his person within the city, outside of his home, motor vehicle or place of business any pistol or revolver without having on his person a valid license issued by the judge of the probate court of the county in which he resides. No permit shall be required for persons with a valid hunting license or fishing license on their person or to persons not required by law to have hunting licenses who are engaged in legal hunting, fishing or sports shooting when said persons have the permission of the owner of the land on which said activities are being conducted and fully provided that the pistol or revolver, whenever loaded, shall be carried only in an open and fully exposed manner.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 12-25, 9-8-80)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-128.
Sec. 14-72. - Same—Discharging firearms.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to fire, discharge, shoot any gun, pistol or other firearm within the city, unless it is in a regularly licensed shooting gallery, or in actual defense of self, habitation or property.
(b)
Subsection (a) of this section does not apply to:
(1)
Law enforcement officers engaged in their official duties as such.
(2)
Members of the armed forces of the United States or national guard, engaged in their official duties as such.
(Code 1977, § 12-14)
Sec. 14-72.1. - Same—Improper display or reckless conduct re firearm.
It shall be unlawful for any person to cause bodily harm to or endanger the bodily safety of another person by consciously disregarding a substantial and unjustifiable risk so that his or her act or omission will cause harm or endanger the safety of the other person, and that disregard constitutes a gross deviation from the standard of care which an ordinary person would exercise in the situation. In addition to the above, any person who recklessly displays a firearm which endangers the bodily safety of another person by consciously disregarding a substantial and justifiable risk that his or her act or omission will cause harm or endanger the safety of the other person, and such disregard constitutes a gross deviation from the standard of care which a reasonable person would exercise in this situation shall also engage in such unlawful conduct [sic].
(Ord. No. 90-01, 2-5-90)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 90-01, adopted Feb. 5, 1990, added a new § 12-25(a), which section has been redesignated by the editor as § 14-72.
Sec. 14-73. - Same—Carrying knives, razors and similar devices.
It shall be unlawful to have, possess or carry upon the streets, alleys, sidewalks or other public property of the city, any of the following implements unless the same are securely wrapped so as not to be readily available for harmful use:
(1)
Any knife which has a blade which opens automatically by hand pressure applied to a button, spring or other device in the handle, the blade of which projects from the handle more than two (2) inches.
(2)
Any knife which has any obstruction or other device of any kind which holds the blade open, the blade of which when open projects from the handle more than two (2) inches.
(3)
Any knife of any kind, the blade of which when open projects from the handle more than four (4) inches. This shall include all knives, such as butcher knives or other knives which do not close.
(4)
Any razor.
(5)
Any ice pick or similar device having a sharp point, except that this shall not apply to any person delivering ice.
(Code 1977, § 12-15)
State Law reference— Carrying concealed knives, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-126.

(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to discharge or allow to be discharged any chemical, fertilizer or other substance intended for dispersal on the soil or foliage from an aircraft within the city.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to allow any chemical, fertilizer or other substance intended for dispersal on the soil or foliage released from an aircraft outside the city in a manner in which it would be reasonably calculated that such substances would settle on the soil, buildings or foliage, within the city.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to fly or operate an aircraft at an altitude lower than two hundred (200) feet, except for take-off and landing, within the city.
(Ord. No. 80-5, § 12-17, 6-2-80)

ARTICLE V. - OFFENSES INVOLVING PUBLIC MORALS

Sec. 14-91. - Public indecency.
It shall be unlawful for any person to perform any of the following acts in a public place within the city:
(1)
An act of sexual intercourse.
(2)
A lewd exposure of the sexual organs.
(3)
A lewd appearance in a state of partial or complete nudity.
(4)

Sec. 14-92. - Dives.
(a)
Defined. The word "dive" as used in this section is defined as a place where alcoholic malt or vinous liquors are kept or sold, and where men and women are permitted to come and drink alcoholic, malt or vinous liquors, either in the place or room where such liquors are kept and sold, or in a place of business or other place connected therewith, or in a place not connected with such place where such liquors are kept and sold, and where men and women drink or commit acts of immorality in such place, or a place where men and women idle away their time without visible means of support and do not do any work.
(b)
Maintaining. It shall be unlawful for any person, either for himself, or as agent of another, to keep or maintain a dive within the city.
(c)
Patronizing. It shall be unlawful for any person to go into a dive for the purpose of drinking alcoholic, malt or vinous liquors, or for any person without visible means of support, or who does not do any work, to go to such place for the purpose of idling away his time, or for any reason at such place to commit acts of immorality.
(Code 1977, § 12-4)
Sec. 14-93. - Gaming.
It shall be unlawful for any person to engage in any of the following acts within the city:
(1)
Making or placing a bet upon the partial or final results of any game or contest or upon the performance of any participant in such game or contest.
(2)
Making or placing a bet upon the result of any political nomination, appointment, election or upon the degree of success of any nominee, appointee or candidate.
(3)
Playing and betting for money or any other thing of value at any game played with cards, dice, or balls.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 12-24, 9-8-80)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-2.
Sec. 14-94. - Begging, soliciting alms.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person habitually to ask, beg or solicit alms in the city from any person whomsoever except as hereinafter provided:
(1)
No person except personnel from the Community Chest, Salvation Army and American Red Cross shall solicit any funds from the general public or on the streets for charitable, eleemosynary, or fraternal purposes until and unless a permit shall have been granted by the police committee and approved by the governing body.
(2)
Before any permit shall be granted the names of all persons who shall be engaged in such solicitation shall accompany the application, with the statement that no person soliciting such fund will be paid a salary or commission.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person in the city to solicit or secure charitable contributions under false pretenses.
(Code 1977, § 12-6)
Sec. 14-95. - Possession of marijuana.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 36-32-6, it shall be unlawful for any person to possess one (1) ounce or less of marijuana within the city.
(Ord. No. 87-3, § 1, 5-4-87)
State Law reference— Authority to so provide, O.C.G.A. § 36-32-6.

ARTICLE VI. - OFFENSES INVOLVING GOVERNMENT FUNCTIONS

Sec. 14-111. - Obstruction of officers.
It shall be unlawful for any person to knowingly or willfully obstruct or hinder any officer of the police department in the lawful discharge of his official duties.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 12-17, 9-8-80)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 16-10-24.
Sec. 14-112. - False alarm.
It shall be unlawful for any person to transmit in any manner to the fire department, police department, or to any other group which is organized for the purpose of preventing or controlling emergencies within the city, a false report of an emergency, knowing or having reason to know at the time that there is no reasonable grounds for believing that such emergency exists.
(Ord. No. 80-8, § 12-21, 9-8-80)
Cross reference— Alarm systems, ch. 3.
State Law reference— False alarms, O.C.G.A. § 16-10-26 et seq.
