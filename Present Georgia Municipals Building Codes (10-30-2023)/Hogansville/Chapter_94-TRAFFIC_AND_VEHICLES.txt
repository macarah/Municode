ARTICLE II. - TRAFFIC
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 94-31. - Uniform rules of the road adopted.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-372—40-6-376, O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-1—40-6-395, known as the uniform rules of the road (except for O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-393 and 40-6-394), are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of the city with like effect as if recited in this article.
(b)
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by state law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this section shall be punished in accordance with section 1-7.
(Code 1988, § 10-1-1)
Editor's note— O.C.G.A. § 40-13-21 grants jurisdiction to the municipal court over all violations relating to traffic upon the public roads, streets and highways where the penalty for the offense does not exceed that of the grade of misdemeanor. O.C.G.A. § 40-13-22 grants jurisdiction to the municipal court over operators of unregistered or unlicensed vehicles. O.C.G.A. § 40-5-124 grants jurisdiction to the municipal court over offenses involving driver's licenses.
Sec. 94-32. - Temporary traffic regulations.
In cases where traffic upon the streets of the city may become congested upon occasions of parades, at theaters and other public assemblages where large numbers of vehicles are assembled, the police may make temporary rules directing and regulating the traffic in these congested districts, and any person who, after being warned of the temporary traffic regulations, shall violate them shall be liable therefor as for other violations of this Code.
(Code 1988, § 10-1-2)
Sec. 94-33. - Traffic, speed, truck, parking and other zones, signs and traffic control devices.
(a)
Except as may otherwise be provided in this article, upon approval by resolution of the city council, the city manager is authorized to designate and maintain by appropriate traffic control signs, markings and devices:
(1)
Crosswalks at intersections where there is particular danger to pedestrians crossing the roadway;
(2)
Other safety zones for pedestrians;
(3)
Traffic lanes;
(4)
Speed, parking, truck or other traffic control zones;
(5)
Stop, yield, one-way and other directionals and devices; and
(6)
Any other sign, marking, device or zone necessary for orderly and safe conditions on the streets of the city.
(b)
All traffic control signs, signals, devices and markings shall conform to specifications in the Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices adopted by the state transportation board. All signs and signals required under this article for a particular purpose shall so far as practicable be uniform as to type and location throughout the city. All traffic control devices so erected and not inconsistent with the provisions of state law or this Code shall be official traffic control devices of the city.
(c)
The city clerk shall keep and maintain an accurate official map setting out all traffic zones, markings, signs and other traffic control devices. The map and any amendments or changes thereto shall be adopted by resolution of the city council and the map shall be known as the official traffic control map of the city. An official copy thereof shall be kept in the office of the city clerk; shall be available to the public; and copies certified by the city clerk shall be admissible in court as proof of the location of any traffic zone, marking, sign or other traffic control device.
(d)
No person shall be charged with violating a traffic zone, marking, sign or other traffic control device unless appropriate signs, markings or devices are in fact operating or in existence on streets involved.
(e)
Any violation of any traffic zone, marking, sign or other traffic control device established under this article shall be a violation of this Code.
(Code 1988, § 10-1-3)
Sec. 94-34. - Leaving vehicles on streets; repairing, displaying for sale.
It shall be unlawful to park a vehicle on the public streets of the city for the purpose of making repairs on the vehicle. It shall be unlawful for a vehicle not in use to be parked on the public streets of the city for sale.
(Code 1988, § 10-1-4)
Cross reference— Streets, sidewalks and other public places, ch. 82.
Sec. 94-35. - Washing vehicles on streets; cleaning up oil and grease by garages.
It shall be unlawful for the owner, operator or proprietor of any garage to wash or allow to be washed any motor vehicle on the streets or sidewalks. The sidewalks in front of garages and oil and gasoline filling stations shall be kept clean from oil and grease. Containers of oil and grease shall not be kept adjacent to sidewalks or in any place where oil or grease leaking from or wasted around such containers shall drain to the street or sidewalk.
(Code 1988, § 10-1-5)
Cross reference— Streets, sidewalks and other public places, ch. 82.
Sec. 94-36. - Putting glass, etc., on highway prohibited.
(a)
No person shall throw or deposit upon any street or highway any glass bottle, glass, nails, tacks, wire, cans, rubbish, garbage, trash, or other dangerous or unsightly material which is likely to injure any person, animal, or vehicle upon such street or highway.
(b)
Any person who drops or throws, or permits to be dropped or thrown, upon any street or highway any destructive or injurious material shall immediately remove that material or cause it to be removed.
(c)
Any person removing a wrecked or damaged vehicle from a highway shall remove any glass or other injurious substance dropped upon the highway from such vehicle.
(Code 1988, § 10-1-6)
State Law reference— Local litter ordinances, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-48.
Sec. 94-37. - Driving over curb.
No person shall drive or back a vehicle over the curb on any street except at a point where a driveway is provided.
(Code 1988, § 10-2-1)

Sec. 94-71. - Engine brakes and compression braking systems prohibited.
new
(a)
Purpose. The purpose of this section is to prohibit the excessively loud and noisy use of engine brakes and compressed air-braking devices or systems along designated streets and roadways within the city.
(b)
Definitions. For the purposes of this section the following words and phrases are defined as follows:
(1)
Engine brake. Any device which, by the release of compressed air within an engine cylinder prior to the ignition of fuel therein, results in the slowing of said vehicle and/or braking without the use of wheel brakes, said devises commonly referred to as "Jacob's brakes," "Jake brakes," "dynamic brake," "C-brake," or "Paccar brake."
(2)
Compression brake. Any device which, by the release of compressed air within an engine cylinder prior to the ignition of fuel therein, results in the slowing of said vehicle and/or braking without the use of wheel brakes and said devices are commonly referred to as "Jacob's brakes," "Jake brakes," "dynamic brake," "C-brake," or "Paccar brake."
(c)
Prohibitions. It shall be unlawful for the driver of any vehicle to use or operate or cause to be used or operated within designated areas in the city, any engine brake, compression brake or mechanical exhaust device designed to aid in the braking or deceleration of any vehicle that results in excessive, loud, unusual or explosive noise from such vehicle; however, the use of an engine or compression brake shall not be prohibited if such use is necessary in the case of a traffic emergency or other imminent danger. Observation, upon the braking of a vehicle, of a sudden loud, engine or compression braking noise, uncharacteristic of wheels brakes, shall be prima facie evidence that an engine brake or compression brake was utilized by the driver of the vehicle.
(d)
Areas of prohibition. The prohibitions set forth herein shall apply to all public streets and roadways within the city.
(e)
Signage. The city shall be authorized to post signs on any appropriate streets within the city stating "VEHICLE NOISE LAWS ENFORCED," "ENGINE BRAKE ORDINANCE ENFORCED" "ENGINE BRAKE USE PROHIBITED" or similar signage consistent with this section, to be installed at locations deemed appropriate by the city manager, chief of police, and/or the public works director to advise motorists of the prohibitions contained in this section. The provisions of the ordinance codified in this section shall be in full force and effect even if no signs are installed.
(f)
Exceptions. Emergency vehicles shall be exempt from the application of this section.
(g)
Enforcement. Any person violating this section shall be guilty of misdemeanor and upon conviction thereof shall be punished in accordance with section 1-7 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 22-1205 , § 1, 12-5-2022)

Sec. 94-81. - Parking, markings designating parking areas and manner of parking.
Parking areas and the manner of parking shall be designated by appropriate markings on the street or on the curb, or by appropriate signs. Yellow lines on the curb or a no parking sign adjacent to the street right-of-way shall signify a no parking area. In areas where parking is permitted, parking shall be in accordance with the parking lines shown on the street surface and, where there are no lines, parking shall be parallel to the curb on the right side of the street in such a manner so as to allow the safe and proper flow of traffic.
(Code 1988, § 10-3-1)
Sec. 94-82. - Parking beyond curb.
No vehicle shall be parked in the space between the property line and the curb.
(Code 1988, § 10-3-2)
Sec. 94-83. - Parking of bus/motor carriers.
No person operating a bus and/or motor carrier upon the public streets or highways of the city shall stop or park such vehicle for the purpose of taking on or discharging passengers on or along those streets or highways unless it is at least eight feet removed from the centerline of the street or highway, and the vehicle shall be so parked that no portion thereof shall be within eight feet of the centerline of the street or highway; providing further that any operator of such vehicle may stop or park only on the right side of any street or highway in the direction in which the vehicle may be traveling.
(Code 1988, § 10-3-3)
Sec. 94-84. - Parking at city hall.
It shall be unlawful to park any vehicle on High Street in front of the city hall between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 5:00 p.m., Monday through Friday of each week, for periods of more than 15 minutes. There shall be no parking in front of city hall on the south side of High Street at any time. The police department shall patrol and keep appropriate notice of these parking restrictions on High Street in front of the city hall.
(Code 1988, § 10-3-4; Ord. No. 21-0719 , § 1, 7-19-2021)
Sec. 94-85. - No overnight parking in city parking lots; parking only in marked parking spaces.
There shall be no overnight parking in city parking lots between the hours of 1:00 a.m. and 6:00 a.m., nor shall there be parking along any city streets except in marked parking spaces.
(1)
Parking is prohibited on the east side of Lawrence Street for a distance of 225 feet from the intersection of Church Street to the entrance of Melson Homes.
(2)
The no parking zone on the north side of East Main Street located near 412 East Main Street, also known as the Old Post Office, is a two-hour parking zone.
(Code 1988, § 10-3-5; Ord. No. 21-0719 , § 1, 7-19-2021)
Sec. 94-86. - Parking restrictions of designated areas.
(a)
The area located on the north side of East Main Street, known as 406 E. Main Street, one parking space shall be designated as handicapped parking only.
(b)
Parking shall be prohibited on both sides of the 100 block of College Street.
(c)
On the west side of Bass Road, parking shall be as follows:
(1)
The parking of motor vehicles along the west side of Bass Road from its intersection with Highway 54 South to Williams Street shall be limited to vehicles with no more than two axles and a height of no more than eight feet.
(2)
Any vehicle parking along Bass Road with more than two axles or a height greater than eight feet shall be subject to a fine of $50.00 and the impoundment of the vehicle at the owner's expense.
(Code 1988, § 10-3-6; Ord. of 6-2-1997; Ord. of 12-1-1997)
Sec. 94-87. - Collection from parking meters.
The city council shall cause the containers placed in parking meters for the purpose of receiving deposited coins, pursuant to the provisions hereof, to be sealed at the time they are placed in the meters. The city clerk/treasurer is authorized to assign competent persons to make such collections. Collection shall be made by removing the sealed containers from the meters, and replacing the containers with empty sealed containers. The person making such collection shall then promptly turn over the collected containers with the seals unbroken, together with all contents thereof, to the city clerk/treasurer, who shall break the seals on the containers and remove the money therefrom.
(Code 1988, § 10-3-18)
Sec. 94-88. - Use of proceeds.
The coins required to be deposited in parking meters as provided in this article are levied and assessed as fees to provide for the proper regulation and control of traffic upon the public streets and also to cover the costs of supervision and regulating the parking of vehicles in the parking meter zones, and to cover the costs of the purchasing, regulation and control of the parking meters described in this article.
(Code 1988, § 10-3-19)

Sec. 94-111. - Railroads required to provide protection at grade crossings.
In the interest of public safety, and for the protection of those using the streets of the city, it shall be the duty of every person operating trains across grade crossings in the city to provide at each such grade crossing complete public safety from danger from approaching trains for all persons using the streets at such crossings. For this purpose, persons operating trains, or having control of the tracks, shall place and keep at all times a watchman or flagman, or shall install, operate and maintain standard automatic gates or signaling devices sufficient to protect the public fully from the danger of any approaching train. The sufficiency of such standard signaling devices shall be approved by the city council prior to installation.
(Code 1988, § 10-4-1)
State Law reference— Authority to require protective devices at grade crossings, O.C.G.A. § 32-6-200; no duty to erect blowposts in cities, railroad engineer must signal on approach of intersection, O.C.G.A. § 46-8-191.
Sec. 94-112. - Train not to cross unprotected grade crossing.
No train shall be run across any grade crossing where adequate provision, as required in this article, has not been made for the protection of human life.
(Code 1988, § 10-4-2)
Sec. 94-113. - Speed limits for trains.
No train shall proceed across any grade crossing in the city at a greater rate of speed than 35 miles per hour, nor shall any train be run anywhere within the city at a greater rate of speed than 35 miles per hour.
(Code 1988, § 10-4-3)
Sec. 94-114. - No orders to be given requiring excess speed.
No person shall issue any order requiring a greater rate of speed for trains than 35 miles per hour across grade crossings and 35 miles per hour at other places within the city.
(Code 1988, § 10-4-4)
Sec. 94-115. - Train without engine not to be run across grade crossings; exception.
No train shall be run by any means across a grade crossing without having a locomotive attached thereto, unless there is a watchman at the crossing at the time.
(Code 1988, § 10-4-5)
Sec. 94-116. - Unnecessary blowing of whistle prohibited.
No locomotive whistle shall be blown within the city limits, except where it is necessary to prevent accidents, or where it is necessary to signal the engineers of other trains of the approach of any passenger train or any section following the passenger train, or when it is necessary to signal a brakeman to protect the rear of such train when the train has made a nonscheduled stop and there is danger of a rear end collision.
(Code 1988, § 10-4-6)
State Law reference— Tolling of bell in lieu of blowing whistle inside cities, O.C.G.A. § 46-8-191.
Sec. 94-117. - Train, freight cars not to stand on grade crossing.
(a)
Trains. No train shall stand at rest upon any grade crossing for a longer period than three minutes at any one time, nor shall any train or part thereof stand at rest in such position so as to obstruct any grade crossing for a longer period.
(b)
Freight cars. Freight cars shall not be permitted to stand at rest at any grade crossings for the purpose of discharging or taking on freight of any character.
(Code 1988, §§ 10-4-7, 10-4-8)
Sec. 94-118. - Persons responsible for violations.
Responsibility for failure to comply with the requirements of this article shall be upon the person or persons operating trains, together with all the officers, employees and agents of persons operating trains who have it within their power or authority to comply or to enforce compliance with such requirements; and any of such persons may be convicted and punished for violation of any such requirement.
(Code 1988, § 10-4-9)
State Law reference— Limitation of liability of certain officers and employees, O.C.G.A. § 48-6-197.

ARTICLE V. - PARADES[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---

Sec. 94-141. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Parade means any march, ceremony, show, exhibition, pageant, or procession of any kind, or any similar display, in or upon any street, park or other public place in the city.
Parade permit means a permit as required by this article.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-2)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 94-142. - Permit required.
No person shall engage in, participate in, aid, form or start any parade, unless a parade permit shall have been obtained from the chief of police.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-3)
Sec. 94-143. - Exceptions.
This article shall not apply to:
(1)
Funeral processions;
(2)
Students going to and from school classes or participating in educational activities, providing such conduct is under the immediate direction and supervision of the proper school authorities;
(3)
A governmental agency acting within the scope of its functions.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-4)
Sec. 94-144. - Application for parade permit.
(a)
Requirement. A person seeking issuance of a parade permit shall file an application with the chief of police on forms provided by the city.
(b)
Filing period. An application for a parade permit shall be filed with the chief of police not less than 48 hours nor more than 30 days before the date on which it is proposed to conduct the parade.
(c)
Contents. The application for a parade permit shall set forth the following information:
(1)
The name, address and telephone number of the person seeking to conduct the parade;
(2)
If the parade is proposed to be conducted for, on behalf of, or by an organization, the name, address and telephone number of the headquarters of the organization, and of the authorized and responsible heads of that organization;
(3)
The name, address and telephone number of the person who will be the parade leader and who will be responsible for its conduct;
(4)
The date when the parade is to be conducted;
(5)
The route to be traveled, the starting point and the termination point;
(6)
The approximate number of persons who, and animals and vehicles which, will constitute the parade; the type of animals, and description of the vehicles;
(7)
The hours when the parade will start and terminate;
(8)
A statement as to whether the parade will occupy all or only a portion of the width of the streets proposed to be traversed;
(9)
The location by streets of any assembly areas for the parade;
(10)
The time at which units of the parade will begin to assemble at any assembly area or areas;
(11)
The interval of space to be maintained between units of the parade;
(12)
If the parade is designed to be held by, and on behalf of or for, any person other than the applicant, the applicant for the permit shall file with the chief of police a communication in writing from the person proposing to hold the parade, authorizing the applicant to apply for the permit on his behalf; and
(13)
Any additional information which the chief of police shall find reasonably necessary to a fair determination as to whether a permit should issue.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-5)
Sec. 94-145. - Late applications for parade permit.
The chief of police, where good cause is shown, shall have the authority to consider any application under this article which is filed less than 48 hours before the date such parade is proposed to be conducted.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-6)
Sec. 94-146. - Parade permit fee.
There shall be paid at the time of filing the application for a parade permit such fee as fixed from time to time by the city council.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-7)
Sec. 94-147. - Standards for issuance of parade permit.
The chief of police shall issue a permit as provided for under this article when, from a consideration of the application and from such other information as may otherwise be obtained, he finds that:
(1)
The conduct of the parade will not substantially interrupt the safe and orderly movement of other traffic contiguous to its route;
(2)
The conduct of the parade will not require the diversion of so great a number of police officers of the city to properly police the line of movement and the areas contiguous thereto as to prevent normal police protection to the city;
(3)
The conduct of such parade will not require the diversion of so great a number of ambulances as to prevent normal ambulance service to portions of the city other than that to be occupied by the proposed line of march and areas contiguous thereto;
(4)
The concentration of persons, animals and vehicles at assembly points of the parade will not unduly interfere with proper fire and police protection of, or ambulance service to, areas contiguous to such assembly areas;
(5)
The conduct of such parade will not interfere with the movement of firefighting equipment en route to a fire;
(6)
The parade is scheduled to move from its point of origin to its point of termination expeditiously and without unreasonable delays en route; and
(7)
The parade is not to be held for the sole purpose of advertising any product, goods or event, and is not designed to be held purely for private profit.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-8)
Sec. 94-148. - Notice of rejection of application for parade permit.
The chief of police shall act upon the application for a parade permit within three days after the filing thereof. If the chief of police disapproves the application, he shall mail to the applicant, within five days after the date upon which the application was filed, a notice of his action, stating the reasons for his denial of the permit.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-9)
Sec. 94-149. - Appeal procedure.
Any person aggrieved shall have the right to appeal the denial of a parade permit to the city council. The appeal shall be taken within three days after notice. The city council shall act upon the appeal within 14 days after its receipt.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-10)
Sec. 94-150. - Alternative permit.
The chief of police in denying an application for a parade permit shall be empowered to authorize the conduct of the parade on a date, at a time, or over a route different from that named by the applicant. An applicant desiring to accept an alternate permit shall, within five days after notice of the action of the chief of police, file a written notice of acceptance with the chief of police. An alternate parade permit shall conform to the requirements of, and shall have the effect of a parade permit under, this article.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-11)
Sec. 94-151. - Notice to city and other officials.
Immediately upon the issuance of a parade permit, the chief of police shall send a copy thereof to the following:
(1)
The city manager;
(2)
The mayor;
(3)
The city attorney;
(4)
The fire chief.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-12)
Sec. 94-152. - Contents of parade permit.
Each parade permit shall state the following information:
(1)
Starting time;
(2)
Minimum speed;
(3)
Maximum speed;
(4)
Maximum interval of space to be maintained between the units of the parade;
(5)
The portions of the streets to be traversed that may be occupied by the parade;
(6)
The maximum length of the parade in miles or fractions thereof;

Sec. 94-153. - Duties of permittee.
A permittee under this article shall comply with all permit directions and conditions and with all applicable laws and ordinances.

Sec. 94-154. - Possession of permit.
The parade chair or other person heading or leading such activity shall carry the parade permit upon his person during the conduct of the parade.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-15)
Sec. 94-155. - Public conduct during parades.
(a)
Interference. No person shall unreasonably hamper, obstruct or impede, or interfere with any parade or parade assembly or with any person, vehicle or animal participating or used in a parade.
(b)
Driving through parades. No driver of a vehicle, streetcar or trackless trolley shall drive between the vehicles or persons comprising a parade when such vehicles or persons are in motion and are conspicuously designated as a parade.
(c)
Parking on parade route. The chief of police shall have the authority, when reasonably necessary, to prohibit or restrict the parking of vehicles along a highway or part thereof constituting a part of the route of a parade. The chief of police shall post signs to such effect, and it shall be unlawful for any person to park or leave unattended any vehicle in violation thereof. No person shall be liable for parking on a street unposted in violation of this article.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-16)
Sec. 94-156. - Revocation of parade permit.
The chief of police shall have the authority to revoke a parade permit issued under this article upon application of the standards for issuance as set forth in this article.
(Code 1988, § 9-7-17)
Sec. 94-157. - Horses.
Any person or group riding or otherwise directly in control of any horse, mule, donkey or other animal in a parade shall provide, at such person's own expense, a follower, who shall follow along behind such animal or group of animals and collect any excrement dropped by such animal before the next person or vehicle crosses over the point where such waste was dropped.
