ARTICLE II. - BUILDING PERMITS

Sec. 8-19. - Permit required.
A building permit shall be required for any new construction, any addition to, alteration of, repair to, or relocation of old construction, setting up of factory-built housing and industrialized buildings by any individual, partnership, or corporation within the unincorporated area of the county in which such work or value of the structure shall be in excess of $4,500.00.
(Ord. No. 041389B, § 200, 4-13-1989; Ord. No. 07092015B, § 200, 7-9-2015)
Sec. 8-20. - Owner of property and/or contractor to make application before commencement of construction.
The permit application shall be made by the owner of the property and/or contractor performing the work prior to the commencement of any work activity, and no work shall be performed prior to the granting of a permit.
(Ord. No. 041389B, § 201, 4-13-1989; Ord. No. 07092015B, § 201, 7-9-2015)
Sec. 8-21. - Application made to tax appraiser.
Application for a building permit shall be made to the tax appraiser of the county on a form approved by the county and provided for such purpose. Further, a copy of the plat of the property shall be attached to the building permit and a copy of construction plans shall be provided to the tax appraiser when plans are used for the construction project. The plan owner may retrieve said plans within six months after final inspection. If not retrieved within the six-month period, plans will become property of the county and will be subject to disposal.
(Ord. No. 041389B, § 202, 4-13-1989; Ord. No. 07092015B, § 202, 7-9-2015)
Sec. 8-22. - Minimum lot size.
No building intended for use as a residential dwelling unit shall be constructed or moved to any lot with an area of less than 43,560 square feet, except in an approved subdivision.
(Ord. No. 041389B, § 203, 4-13-1989; Ord. No. 07092015B, § 203, 7-9-2015)
Sec. 8-23. - Governmental or public utility connections.
(a)
No connection for service of electricity by a public utility shall be made until after the granting of a written permit by the building inspector as provided by this article.
(b)
The governmental or public utility asked to provide service to a location for any class of work as set forth in section 8-19 shall be provided with and have in its possession a signed copy of the original permit issued by the tax assessor's office.
(Ord. No. 041389B, § 300, 4-13-1989; Ord. No. 07092015B, § 300, 7-9-2015)
Sec. 8-24. - Issuance of permit.
The tax assessor shall have up to seven days after the date the fully completed application and all attachments thereto are received, including a copy of a plat of the property, a copy of construction plans, and a copy of a certificate of approval of septic tank from the county health department to issue a building permit.
(Ord. No. 041389B, § 400, 4-13-1989; Ord. No. 07092015B, § 400, 7-9-2015)
Sec. 8-25. - Permit display on premises.
The permit issued by the tax assessor's office shall be prominently displayed on the premises where the work is being done or the setting up of factory-built housing or an industrialized building, is taking place.
(Ord. No. 041389B, § 401, 4-13-1989; Ord. No. 07092015B, § 401, 7-9-2015)
Sec. 8-26. - Payment of fees subject to issuance or approved amendment of permit.
A permit shall not be issued until the fees prescribed in this article have been paid. Nor shall an amendment to a permit be approved until the additional fee, if any, due to an increase in the estimated cost of the building or structure, shall have been paid.
(Ord. No. 041389B, § 500, 4-13-1989; Ord. No. 07092015B, § 500, 7-9-2015)
Sec. 8-27. - Accurate records.
The county shall keep a permanent and accurate accounting of all permit fees and other monies collected, the name of all persons upon whose account the same was paid, the date and amount thereof.
(Ord. No. 041389B, § 501, 4-13-1989; Ord. No. 07092015B, § 501, 7-9-2015)
Sec. 8-28. - Schedule of permit fees.
On all buildings, structures, or alterations requiring a permit, a fee for each permit shall be paid as required at the time of filing of the application, in accordance with the following schedules.
(1)
Buildings and improvements.
a.
Single-family (including factory-built housing and industrialized buildings): The permit fees shall be established by the board, from time to time. Note: Mobile homes and pre-fab buildings that already have built-in plumbing may be excluded from the plumbing fee. This exclusion will also apply to built-in mechanical.
b.
Non-commercial; outbuildings and swimming pools (e.g., storage buildings, shops, barns, etc.); structures not attached to homes; single-family dwellings, mobile homes, pre-fab homes; buildings with a value of less than $100,000.00: The permit fees shall be established by the board, from time to time.
c.
Commercial, industrial, multi-family, public, and accessory fee schedule: The permit fees shall be established by the board, from time to time.
d.
Moving of building or structure. For the moving of a building or other structure, the fee shall be established by the board from time to time.
e.
Demolition of building or structure. For the demolition of a building or structure, a permit is required. A complimentary permit shall be issued to the building or structure owner, however failure to obtain the permit prior to the demolition of such building or structure shall constitute a violation of this regulation and require payment to be established by the board, from time to time.
(2)
Electrical. The fees for service size shall be established by the board, from time to time. Each circuit connected in panel shall be in an amount established by the board from time to time, in addition to the charges in this subsection. Double pole circuits shall be considered as one circuit.
(Ord. No. 041389B, § 502, 4-13-1989; Ord. No. 07092015B, § 502, 7-9-2015)
Sec. 8-29. - Reinspection.
If it is necessary to make a reinspection for a required building or electrical inspection because of improper work, the owner or contractor shall pay a reinspection fee to be determined by the board from time to time.
(Ord. No. 041389B, § 503, 4-13-1989; Ord. No. 07092015B, § 503, 7-9-2015)
Secs. 8-30—8-46. - Reserved.

(a)
Unless otherwise provided for by subsequent ordinance or another provision of this Code, the county hereby adopts and incorporates by reference, as if set out at length herein, the latest editions of the state minimum standard codes enumerated in O.C.G.A. § 8-2-20(9)(b), as adopted and amended by the department of community affairs.
(1)
International Building Code, latest adopted version.
(2)
International Residential Code, latest adopted version.
(3)
Fire Code, latest adopted version.
(4)
International Plumbing Code, latest adopted version.
(5)
International Mechanical Code, latest adopted version.
(6)
International Fuel Gas Code, latest adopted version.
(7)
National Electrical Code, latest adopted version.
(8)
International Energy Conservation Code, latest adopted version.
(b)
If any of the publications listed in subsection (a) of this section are adopted as a state minimum code pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 8-2-20 et seq., then the edition adopted by the state shall control. Sec. 8-49. - Tests.
The county building official may require tests or test reports as proof of compliance. Required tests are to be made at the expense of the owner, or his agent, by an approved testing laboratory or other approved agency.
Sec. 8-50. - General inspection required prior to temporary or permanent electrical service connection.
Prior to any temporary or permanent electrical service being connected to any premises for which a permit is required, the building inspector or other designee of the board of commissioners will inspect the premises and determine if the work has been performed in accordance with this chapter.
(Ord. No. 041389B, § 700, 4-13-1989; Ord. No. 07092015B, § 700, 7-9-2015)
Sec. 8-51. - Periodic inspection.
The building inspector or other designee by the board of commissioners will periodically inspect the premises for which permits have been issued to determine if the activities are being conducted in accordance with the chapter.
(Ord. No. 041389B, § 701, 4-13-1989; Ord. No. 07092015B, § 701, 7-9-2015)
Sec. 8-52. - Investigation.
The county shall have the power to conduct such investigations as it may reasonably deem necessary to carry out its duties as prescribed in this chapter, and for this purpose to enter at a reasonable time upon any property, public or private, for the purpose of investigating and inspecting the work being performed.
(Ord. No. 041389B, § 702, 4-13-1989; Ord. No. 07092015B, § 702, 7-9-2015)
Sec. 8-53. - Access to premises.
No person, corporation, or organization shall refuse entry or access to any authorized representative or agent of the county who requests entry for purposes of inspection, and who presents appropriate credentials, nor shall any person, corporation, or organization obstruct, hamper, or interfere with any such representative or agent while in the process of carrying out their official duties.
(Ord. No. 041389B, § 703, 4-13-1989; Ord. No. 07092015B, § 703, 7-9-2015)
Sec. 8-54. - Violations.
In case of violation or attempted violation of the provisions of this chapter, the county may institute any appropriate action or proceeding to prevent such violation or attempted violation.
(Ord. No. 041389B, § 800, 4-13-1989; Ord. No. 07092015B, § 800, 7-9-2015)
Sec. 8-55. - Penalty.
A violation of this chapter shall constitute a misdemeanor and shall be tried in the county magistrate court and upon conviction shall be punishable according to section 1-9, general penalty.
(Ord. No. 041389B, § 801, 4-13-1989; Ord. No. 07092015B, § 801, 7-9-2015)
Sec. 8-56. - Appeal to the superior court of the county.
Any party aggrieved because of the alleged error in any order, requirement, decision, or determination made by the building inspector or any other party in enforcement of this chapter may perfect an appeal to the superior court in the county by filing same in the manner prescribed by law.
(Ord. No. 041389B, § 900, 4-13-1989; Ord. No. 07092015B, § 900, 7-9-2015)

Sec. 8-86. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Commercial means any type of building other than residential.
Construction means the erection of a new building or the alteration of an existing building in connection with making an addition to an existing building and shall include the replacement of a malfunctioning, unserviceable, or obsolete faucet, showerhead, toilet, or urinal in an existing building.
Residential means any building or unit of a building intended for occupancy as a dwelling but shall not include a hotel or motel.
(Ord. No. 031491-D, § 1, 3-14-1991)
Sec. 8-87. - Permitted construction.
(a)
No construction may be initiated within the unincorporated areas of the county for any residential building of any type which:
(1)
Employs a gravity tank-type, flushometer valve, or flushometer-tank toilet that uses more than an average of 1.28 gallons of water per flush;
(2)
Employs a showerhead that allows a flow of more than an average of 2.5 gallons of water per minute at 60 pounds per square inch of pressure;
(3)
Employs a urinal that uses more than an average of 0.5 gallons of water per flush;
(4)
Employs a lavatory faucet or lavatory replacement aerator that allows a flow of more than 1.5 gallons of water per minute; or
(5)
Employs a kitchen faucet or kitchen replacement aerator that allows a flow of more than 2.0 gallons of water per minute.
(b)
There shall be no construction of any commercial building initiated within the unincorporated areas of the county for any commercial building of any type which does not meet the requirements of subsections (a)(1) through (5) of this section.
(Ord. No. 031491-D, §§ 2, 3, 3-14-1991)
Sec. 8-88. - When applicable.
The requirements of section 8-87 shall apply to any residential construction which involves the replacement, renovation or addition to any existing building when such repair or renovation of or addition to such existing building includes replacement of toilets or showers, or both.
(Ord. No. 031491-D, § 4, 3-14-1991)
Sec. 8-89. - Exemptions.
(a)
New construction and the repair or renovation of an existing building shall be exempt from the requirements of sections 8-87 and 8-88 when:
(1)
The repair or renovation of the existing building does not include the replacement of the plumbing or sewage system servicing toilets, faucets, or showerheads within such existing buildings;
(2)
Such plumbing or sewage system within such existing building, because of its capacity, design, or installation would not function properly if the toilets, faucets, or showerheads required by this chapter were installed;
(3)
Such systems is well or gravity flow from a spring and is owned privately by an individual for use in such individual's personal residence; or
(4)
Units to be installed are:
a.
Specifically designed for use by the handicapped;
b.
Specifically designed to withstand unusual abuse or installation in a penal institution; or
c.
Toilets for juveniles.
(b)
The owner, or his agent, of a building undergoing new construction or repair or renovation who is entitled to an exemption as specified in subsection (a)(2), (3), or (4) of this section shall obtain the exemption by applying at the office of the tax assessor for the county. A fee to be determined by the board from time to time shall be charged for the inspection and issuance of such exemption.
(Ord. No. 031491-D, § 5, 3-14-1991)
Sec. 8-90. - Enforcement; penalty.
(a)
This article shall be enforced by the office of the building inspector of the county. Citations for violations may be issued by the building inspector of the county.
(b)
Any person, corporation, partnership or other entity violating this article shall be tried before the magistrate court of the county. Upon conviction, a violation of this article may be punished according to section 1-9, general penalty.
(Ord. No. 031491-D, § 6, 3-14-1991)

ARTICLE VI. - SOLAR INSTALLATIONS

Sec. 8-138. - All solar systems; general regulations.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Small scale solar collection system means a solar collection system that transforms solar energy into another form of energy or transfers heat from a collector to another medium using mechanical, electrical, or chemical means, principally for use on the same property.
Solar collection system means a solar energy system that transforms solar energy into another form of energy or transfers heat from a collector to another medium using mechanical, electrical, or chemical means.
Solar farm means a solar collection system placed in an area for the purpose of generating photo voltaic electric power principally for use off-site, or where the solar collection system is the primary land use on the parcel where it is located, or where the total area of a ground based or free standing solar collection system occupies more than one acre. This is also referred to as a "solar park," "solar power plant" or "solar photovoltaic farm."
(b)
Solar collectors shall not emit unreasonable glare. Exterior surfaces of the collectors and related equipment shall have a non-reflective finish. Solar panels shall be placed and arranged such that reflected solar radiation or glare shall not be directed onto the habitable portion of adjacent buildings or properties, or onto roadways, in such manner as to cause a nuisance, impair the vision of a driver of any vehicle, or be detrimental to public health, safety, and welfare.
(c)
Requirements for labeling and signage, installation, structural certification, and roof covering must comply with applicable building codes. All systems must be clearly marked, including, but not limited to:
(1)
Main service disconnects;
(2)
All conduit, raceways, enclosures, cable assemblies and junction boxes to alert the fire service to avoid cutting them, every ten feet, at turns and at penetrations, and at all combiner and junction boxes.
(d)
Emergency personnel access. To ensure emergency personnel access, in particular for fire departments, roof-mounted systems shall provide access and spacing requirements in order to:
(1)
Ensure roof access and egress.
(2)
Provide pathways throughout the roof area.
(3)
Provide smoke ventilation opportunity areas.
(e)
Proof of utility. No permit shall be issued without proof that any electrical utility with power to the property has been informed of applicant's intent to install a solar collection system.
(f)
Derived solar easements. Solar easements-property owners installing any solar energy collection system shall be responsible for negotiating with surrounding property owners any desired solar easement, and for properly recording and enforcing same.
(g)
Clearance. A ten-foot area clear of trees, bushes or brush is required surrounding all ground-mounted photovoltaic arrays. Vegetation in the area must be kept trimmed to less than 18 inches.
(h)
Conformance with National Electrical Code. All systems must be installed according to the adopted version of the National Electrical Code.
(Ord. No. SOL020917, 2-9-2017)
Sec. 8-139. - Small scale solar collection systems.
Small scale solar collection systems shall be considered an accessory use in all zoning districts in accordance with the following requirements:
(1)
Freestanding solar panels.
a.
When located on the ground or attached to a framework located on the ground, freestanding solar panels shall not exceed 20 feet in height above the ground, or the height of existing structure, whichever is lower.
b.
Freestanding solar panels shall conform to all setback requirements as for other uses in the districts in which they are located.
c.
The total coverage of a lot with freestanding solar panels cannot exceed 50 percent lot coverage or the maximum allowable coverage for the district in which they are located, whichever is less.
d.
Small solar panel systems not connected to the electrical system of the structures on the property or to the electrical grid in any way, such as solar security or decorative yard lights, and where the solar panel area measures less than two square feet, are exempt from permitting.
(2)
Roof-mounted solar panels.
a.
Roof-mounted solar panels shall not project vertically more than the height requirements for the district in which they are located.
b.
The owner shall provide proof of notification to the managing authority of any airport located within five miles of the proposed location where the size of the installation exceeds one acre.
(Ord. No. SOL020917, 2-9-2017)
Sec. 8-140. - Solar farms.
Solar farms shall be permitted, in accordance with the following regulations:
(1)
The manufacturers' or installers' identification and appropriate warning signage shall be posted at the site in a clearly visible manner. At least one sign shall be posted at the entrance to the facility showing the owner's name, address and emergency contact information. On-site power lines shall, to the maximum extent practicable, be placed underground.
(2)
Solar farms shall adhere to the requirements of the district in which they are located, including, but not limited to, setbacks, height, lot area coverage requirements and buffer requirements.
a.
Where a solar farm is located on a major street, solar plant structures must be located a minimum of 100 feet from the right-of-way.
b.
A solar farm with solar panels within 100 feet of any street must have sufficient barriers to prevent a passenger vehicle leaving that street at the maximum speed limit from entering the solar field. A "passenger vehicle" means every motor vehicle having a gross vehicle weight of 10,000 pounds or less.
(3)
The following requirements shall be met for permit applications:
a.
A descriptive plot plan including setbacks, panel sizes, locations of property lines, buildings, and road rights-of-way.
b.
The plan shall include a fire suppression system sufficient to prevent a fire from spreading off the site. This can be accomplished by removing all vegetation, and spreading gravel or other fire resistant material underneath any equipment likely to drop burning debris should a fire occur. A minimum six-foot wide firebreak composed of gravel or other fire resistant material placed around the perimeter of the installation can also be used in lieu of, or in addition to the above.
c.
No solar farm shall be installed until evidence has been given to the county that the electric utility company has been informed of the customer's intent to install an interconnected customer-owned generator. Off-grid systems shall be exempt from this requirement.
d.
An affidavit or evidence of agreement between lot owner and the facility's owner or operator confirming that the owner or operator has permission of the property owner to apply for necessary permits for construction and operation of the urban solar farm.
e.
Proof of notification to the managing authority of any airport located within five miles of the proposed location.
f.
Any other relevant studies, reports, certificates and approvals as may be reasonably requested by the county, including, but not limited to, design review.
(4)
The following requirements shall be met for decommissioning solar farms:
a.
Each applicant for a permit must submit decommissioning plans that describe the anticipated life of the solar project, and identify the party responsible for decommissioning, and the estimated decommissioning costs in current dollars. The decommissioning plan shall also state that the land owner has been informed by the lessee of the decommissioning responsibility and projected cost, should that responsibility be allocated to the land owner in the contract.
b.
Solar farms which have not been in active and continuous service for a period of one year shall be removed at the owner's or operator's expense.
c.
The site shall be restored to as natural condition as possible within six months of the removal.
d.
A site will be considered decommissioned when, after inspection and approval by the county, all structures and equipment are removed and the site is re-vegetated.
(Ord. No. SOL020917, 2-9-2017)
