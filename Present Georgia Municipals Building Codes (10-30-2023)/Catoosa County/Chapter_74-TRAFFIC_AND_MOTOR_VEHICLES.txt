Chapter 74 - TRAFFIC AND MOTOR VEHICLES[1]
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 74-1. - Adoption of uniform rules of the road.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-372—40-6-376, O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-2—40-6-395, known as the uniform rules of the road, and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1, are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of the county with like effect as if recited in this chapter.
(b)
Unless another penalty is expressly provided by state law, every person convicted of a violation of any provision of this chapter shall be punished in accordance with section 1-19.
State Law reference— Adoption of uniform rules of the road by local authorities, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-372.
Sec. 74-2. - Temporary traffic regulations.
In cases where traffic upon the roads of the county may become congested upon occasions of parades, or at theaters and other public assemblages where large numbers of vehicles are assembled, the county law enforcement officers may make temporary rules directing and regulating the traffic in these congested districts; and any person who, after being warned of the temporary traffic regulations, shall violate them shall be liable therefor as for other violations of this Code.
Sec. 74-3. - Traffic zones; signs and traffic control devices.
(a)
Upon approval by resolution of the board of commissioners, the public works director is authorized to designate and maintain by appropriate traffic control signs, markings and devices:
(1)
Crosswalks at intersections where there is particular danger to pedestrians crossing the roadway.
(2)
Other safety zones for pedestrians.
(3)
Traffic lanes.
(4)
Speed, parking, truck or other traffic control zones.
(5)
Stop, yield, one-way and other directional devices.
(6)
Any other sign, marking, device or zone necessary for orderly and safe conditions on the roads and streets of the county.
(b)
All traffic control signs, signals, devices and markings shall conform to specifications in the Manual on Uniform Traffic Control Devices adopted by the state transportation board. All signs and signals required under this chapter for a particular purpose shall so far as practicable be uniform as to type and location throughout the county. All traffic control devices so erected and not inconsistent with the provisions of state law or this Code shall be official traffic control devices of the county.
(c)
The county clerk shall keep and maintain an accurate official map setting out all traffic zones, markings, signs and other traffic control devices. The map and any amendments or changes thereto shall be adopted by resolution of the board of commissioners, and the map shall be known as the official traffic control map of the county. An official copy thereof shall be kept in the office of the county clerk; shall be available to the public; and copies certified by the county clerk shall be admissible in court as proof of the location of any traffic zone, marking, sign or other traffic control device.
(d)
No person shall be charged with violating a traffic zone, marking, sign or other traffic control device unless appropriate signs, markings or devices are in fact operating or in existence on the streets involved.
(e)
Any violation of any traffic zone, marking, sign or other traffic control device established hereunder shall be a violation of this Code.
Sec. 74-4. - Speed detection devices.
(a)
The county is authorized to use speed detection devices for the purpose of traffic control on the roads and streets in the county.
(b)
Such speed detection devices shall be used only for the purpose of public safety and traffic control, and shall not be used for the purpose of raising revenue.
(c)
In accordance with the Georgia Uniform Rules of the Road, codified in the O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-181 through 40-6-183, and O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-370 through 40-6-376, and O.C.G.A. §§ 40-14-1 through 40-14-16 as applicable, the following list of roads, speed limits for the portions thereof identified therein, and radar approval for use on said roadways are approved as attached hereto and incorporated herein by reference, consisting of the letter of November 12, 2003, from the Georgia Department of Transportation to the Catoosa County Sheriff's Office and the List of Roads, Catoosa County, Number 08-21-03. More specifically, the speed zones and speed limits listed in said attachments are hereby established based on an engineering study and traffic investigation that has been completed as provided by law. Said study is on file in the office of the Sheriff of Catoosa County and the office of the Board of Commissioners of Catoosa County. Any person convicted of a violation of this ordinance shall be punished as provided by law. This section is not intended to repeal any prior ordinance or speed limits unless speed limits for the sections of roads listed are in conflict with prior set limits; to the extent of any conflict, the terms of this ordinance dated December 16, 2003, shall supersede and repeal the conflicting portions of any such prior ordinance or ordinances. (Copy of Ordinance and List of Roads on file in County Commission Office.)
(Res. of 6-2-88; Ord. of 12-16-03)
Sec. 74-5. - Covering of loads.
No person shall operate or load any vehicle on the public streets and roads of this county unless the vehicle is constructed, loaded and securely covered so as to prevent any of its load from dropping, escaping or shifting in such a manner as to create a safety hazard or in such a manner so as to litter the streets and roads of the county.
Sec. 74-6. - Parking in deceleration or acceleration lanes prohibited.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to park any automobile, truck, bus, truck tractor, trailer, tractor, trailer combination, motorcycle, moped or other motor vehicle within or upon the deceleration or acceleration lanes lying in the unincorporated areas of the county.
(b)
Deceleration and acceleration lanes shall be defined for the purpose of this section as those strips of pavement lying adjacent to and to the east of the northbound lanes of traffic and adjacent to and to the west of the southbound lanes of traffic that are not part of the regular roadway but afford access into and out of the driveways and side roads along the highway.
(c)
Violation of the provisions of this section shall be a violation of a county ordinance and shall be prosecuted in the state court of the county under the authority of O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq. A violation of this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-19.
Sec. 74-7. - Motor vehicle registration.
(a)
Definitions.
(1)
As used in this section, the term "resident" means a person who has a permanent home or abode in Catoosa County, Georgia to which, whenever he or she is absent, he or she has the intention of returning. For purposes of this section, there is a rebuttable presumption that any person who, except for infrequent, brief absences, has been present in Catoosa County, Georgia for 30 days or more is a resident.
(2)
As used in this section the term "motor vehicle" means each motor vehicle, tractor, motorcycle or trailer which is required to be registered and licensed under O.C.G.A. § 40-2-8 and O.C.G.A. § 40-2-20.
(b)
Unlawful acts.
(1)
It shall be unlawful for any resident of Catoosa County, Georgia who owns, rents or leases a motor vehicle to fail to register his or her motor vehicle in this state as required under O.C.G.A. § 40-2-8 and O.C.G.A. § 40-2-20 within 30 days after becoming a resident of Catoosa County, Georgia.
(2)
It shall be unlawful for any resident of Catoosa County, Georgia who purchases, rents or leases a new or used motor vehicle to fail to register his or her motor vehicle in this state as required under O.C.G.A. § 40-2-8 and O.C.G.A. § 40-2-20 within 30 days after acquiring the motor vehicle.
(c)
Enforcement. Prosecutions for violations of this section shall be made upon citation as provided in O.C.G.A. §§ 15-10-62 and 15-10-63, as amended. The Sheriff of Catoosa County, Georgia or his lawful deputies are hereby authorized to issue citations for violations of this section.
(d)
Penalty. Any person violating this article shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be punished by a fine not exceeding $500.00 for the first violation and a fine not exceeding $1,000.00 or 30-days imprisonment, or both, for a second or subsequent violation.
(e)
Exemption. The provisions of this section shall not apply to a "non-resident" or "visitor" as those terms are defined in O.C.G.A. § 40-2-90.
(Ord. of 11-21-00, §§ I—V)

DIVISION 2. - OPERATION ON PRIVATE PROPERTY

Sec. 74-61. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Allowing, allow or other tense of these words shall include knowingly permitting, allowing or facilitating any persons to operate or conduct activities prohibited under this division. This definition shall further include and be applicable to the owner (of a legal or equitable interest) of or lessee of real property or properties in the unincorporated areas of the county that allows, facilitates or permits any person to operate a motorcycle or conduct any activities prohibited in this division on his land (whether owned individually, jointly or with others). These terms shall further include such owner or lessee allowing such operation expressly or implicitly.
Motorcycle includes any two wheeled, three wheeled, or four wheeled self-propelled vehicle weighing less than 1000 pounds; the term shall not be construed to include any car, truck or farm implement or tractor. The term shall not be construed to include any self-propelled lawn equipment such as riding lawn mowers, self propelled push lawn mowers, or other equipment customarily used for lawn care activities.
Operation, operate or other tense of these words shall include driving, riding on, steering, allowing a motorcycle engine to be operating or running (whether in motion or in a stationary position) or otherwise causing a motorcycle to emit noise by engaging its engine, horn or other device located on a motorcycle.
Property includes real property and all fixtures thereto, together with the air space above the property to a height of 100 feet.
(Ord. of 3-6-90, § 5; Ord. of 7-27-00, § I)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 74-62. - Racetracks excepted.
The terms defined in section 74-61 shall not include the operation of a motorcycle upon a racetrack licensed and operated pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 43-25-1 et seq., which are regulated exclusively by the state.
(Ord. of 3-6-90, § 5)
Sec. 74-63. - Unlawful activities related to noise.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a motorcycle upon private property in the unincorporated areas of the county in violation of this division.
(b)
No person shall operate or allow the operation of a motorcycle upon private property in the unincorporated areas of the county between the hours of 11:00 a.m. and 1:00 p.m. on any day whatsoever. No person shall operate or allow the operation of a motorcycle on private property in the unincorporated areas of the county between the hours of 7:00 p.m. and 8:00 a.m. on any day whatsoever.
(c)
No person shall operate or allow the operation of the motorcycle on private property in the unincorporated areas of the county in excess of a total or aggregate of one hour between 8:00 a.m. and 11:00 a.m. on any day whatsoever. No person shall operate or allow the operation of a motorcycle on private property for a total or aggregate of one hour between such hours on any day whatsoever. No person shall operate or allow the operation of a motorcycle on private property in the unincorporated areas of the county in excess of a total or aggregate of one hour between the hours of 1:00 p.m. and 7:00 p.m. on any day whatsoever. No person shall operate or allow the operation of a motorcycle on private property following the operation of a motorcycle for a total or aggregate of one hour between such hours on any day whatsoever. No person shall operate or allow the operation of one or more motorcycles on the same property or contiguous tracts of property for more than one hour, twice per day, during the time set forth in this subsection; that is, the total or aggregate time of operation or allowance of operation shall be for one hour between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 11:00 a.m., regardless of the number of motorcycles being operated; likewise, the total or aggregate time of operation or allowance of operation shall be for one hour between the hours of 11:00 p.m. and 7:00 p.m. regardless of the number of motorcycles being operated.
(d)
The provisions of subsection (c) of this section shall not apply to the operation or allowance of the operation of motorcycles if the sound from such operation is muffled so that it is not audible to the human ear at any property line of any other tract of property; that is, this section shall apply if any noise from such operation can be heard by the human ear upon any other tract of property or at any property line of any other tract of property (other than the tract of property upon which the motorcycle is being operated). It shall be the affirmative duty of the person operating and the person allowing the operation of a motorcycle to ascertain whether such noise is audible to the human ear at any and all other property lines adjoining access to such other tracts; such affirmative duty shall take place during the hours set forth in subsection (c) of this section.
(e)
It shall further be unlawful for any property owner or lessee of real property to allow the operation of any motorcycle in violation of this division beyond the time limits set forth in this section or at times prohibited by this section.
(f)
This provision shall not apply to motorcycle race tracks allowed under division 3.
(Ord. of 3-6-90, § 6; Ord. of 7-27-00, §§ II, III)
Sec. 74-64. - Punishment.
(a)
Any person engaging in any activities in violation of this division shall be punished as provided in section 1-19. For purposes of this division, a violation shall occur when any section or subsection (or portion thereof) of this division is violated.
(b)
Separate violations will occur for each 15-minute period in any portion of a day that any person violates any subsection (or portion thereof) of section 74-63 relating to operating or allowing the operation of a motorcycle.
(Ord. of 3-6-90, § 7)

DIVISION 3. - RACETRACKS

Sec. 74-86. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Motorcycle includes any two wheeled, three wheeled, or four wheeled self-propelled vehicle weighing less than 1000 pounds; the term shall not be construed to include any car, truck or farm implement or tractor. The term shall not be construed to include any self-propelled lawn equipment such as riding lawn mowers, self propelled push lawn mowers, or other equipment customarily used for lawn care activities.
Race or races includes any competition, trial run, practice run or other similar activity.
Racetrack shall not be construed to include racetracks licensed or operated pursuant to O.C.G.A. ch. 25 of title 43.
(Ord. of 2-21-89, § 1; Ord. of 7-27-00, § IV)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 74-87. - Purpose.
The purpose of this division is to limit the operation or conducting of any motorcycle race track or other place where motorcycle races are held in the unincorporated areas of the county for the purpose of protecting and preserving the public health, safety and welfare.
(Ord. of 2-21-89, § 2; Ord. of 7-27-00, § VI)
Sec. 74-88. - Authority under home rule.
Each county has the legislative power to adopt reasonable ordinances relating to its property, affairs and local government for which no provision has been made by general law and not inconsistent with the constitution or any applicable charter provision. (Opinion of the Attorney General 70-167; Ga. Const. art. IX, § II, ¶ 1 (Home Rule for Counties).) This division is being adopted pursuant to the authority set forth in these laws.
(Ord. of 2-21-89, § 2)
Sec. 74-89. - Further authority under police power.
The governing authority of each county is authorized to adopt ordinances for the governing and policing of the unincorporated areas of the county, violations of such ordinances may be punished by fine or imprisonment or both pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 36-1-20. This division is further being adopted pursuant to the authority granted in such law.
(Ord. of 2-21-89, § 2)
Sec. 74-90. - Prohibition.
(a)
Prohibition. It shall be unlawful for any person to operate or conduct any motorcycle race on any motorcycle track or other places where such races would be held in the unincorporated areas of the county except as provided herein.
(b)
Racing hours of operation. Motorcycle racing shall be permitted on the following days and during the following hours only: Saturday: 10:00 a.m. to 10:00 p.m.; Sunday: 1:00 p.m. to 6:00 p.m. Sanction races which shall be permitted on Friday, 10:00 a.m. to 10:00 p.m. and continue on Saturday and Sunday during the hours provided above.
(c)
Motorcycle racing practice. Motorcycle racing practice shall be allowed on any two nights per week excluding Wednesday. Race practice shall be permitted from 3:30 p.m. to 9:00 p.m. on the two days per week selected for practice. Race practice shall also be permitted on the days and during the hours provided in (b) above.
(d)
Outdoor commercial racing of motorcycles shall be subject to the following requirements:
(1)
A special use permit shall be required as provided under the zoning ordinance;
(2)
The operator shall obtain all necessary state and federal permits, if any, for motorcycle racing;
(3)
A traffic impact study must be submitted with the application for a special use permit;
(4)
No damaged motorcycles or other vehicles shall be stored on the site of the motorcycle raceway for more than seven days;
(5)
A noise abatement plan and air pollution and dust abatement plan shall be submitted with the special use permit. The plan shall be approved by the planning commission as a condition to the issuance of the special use permit;
(6)
An ambulance, provided at the expense of the race track operator shall be on station at each motorcycle race;
(7)
A uniformed law enforcement officer, provided at the expenses of the race track operator, shall be present at each motorcycle race;
(8)
The board of commissioners reserves the right to reconsider this article six months from the date of adoption or at any later time if the operation of motorcycle race tracks does not appear appropriate.
(Ord. of 2-21-89, § 2; Ord. of 7-27-00, § VII)
Sec. 74-91. - Punishment—Operators.
Any person engaging in any activities in violation of this division shall be punished as provided in section 1-19. For purposes of this section, a violation shall occur when a person operates or conducts any motorcycle racetrack or other place where such races would be held on one day or any part of any one day. Separate violations will occur for each successive day or portion thereof that such person operates or conducts any motorcycle on any motorcycle racetrack or other place where such races are to be held.
(Ord. of 2-21-89, § 2)
Sec. 74-92. - Same—Property owners.
A person shall be deemed to violate this division if he is the owner of real property in the county and he knowingly allows any person to operate or conduct such activities prohibited in section 74-91. A person shall be deemed to have violated this division if he is the lessee of real property and he knowingly allows any person to operate or conduct such prohibited activities. A person shall be deemed to have violated this division if he otherwise knowingly assists in the operation of any motorcycle racetrack or other place where such races are held.
(Ord. of 2-21-89, § 2)
