Chapter 5 - ANIMALS
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 5-1. - Horses, cattle, and so forth, running at large; staking, driving and leading requirements; impounding.
It shall be unlawful for any person to allow or permit any horses, cattle, sheep, goats, or other livestock to run at large on the streets of the city, or to be staked so that they can go upon the streets of the city, or to drive or lead any such animals or livestock along or on the sidewalks in the city, or to drive or lead any such animals or livestock along and over the streets of the city, unless fastened to a rope, chain, or other substantial leading device. Any such rope, chain, or other substantial leading device must be securely held by a responsible person. Animals in violation of this chapter shall be impounded by members of the police department, other authorized employees or agents of the city, or the county animal control officers or their employees or agents.
(Code 1975, § 2-10)
Sec. 5-2. - Impounded animals—Disposal when unredeemed within certain time; notice.
(a)
If any animal impounded as provided in section 5-1 is not claimed and redeemed by the owner, or keeper thereof, within five (5) days after such impoundment, or when ownership of the animal is relinquished by the owner, the animal may be disposed of.
(b)
As methods of disposal, the animal may be:
(1)
Placed in an adoptive home or rescue organization;
(2)
Sold; or
(3)
Destroyed in a humane manner if, in the opinion of a licensed accredited veterinarian, the animal has a temperament or condition such that euthanasia is the only reasonable course of action.
(c)
In the case of the sale of such animal, the animal control officer is hereby authorized to execute a bill of sale to any such purchaser which is sufficient to vest title to the animal in such purchaser. Any proceeds from the sale of such animal will be used first to pay the costs associated with the impoundment, including, but not limited to, removal of the animal from the premises, shelter and care of the animal, notice, hearing, and disposition of the animal.
(d)
Any funds remaining following the sale of such animal will:
(1)
Be paid into the treasury of the city if the owner is unknown or cannot be found; or
(2)
Be paid to the owner if the owner is known.
(e)
The agency responsible for conducting the sale must keep a record of all sales, disbursements, and distributions made.
(Code 1975, § 2-11; Ord. No. 21/03, § 6, 10-11-2021; Ord. No. 22/01, § 1, 1-10-2022)

(a)
It is unlawful for any person to keep, maintain, or permit any animal or fowl in the city in such manner as to create an unsanitary condition or to create an obnoxious condition, or to become excessively noisy or to become a nuisance to the neighbors or citizens of the city or to disturb the peace of the city.

It is unlawful for any person to keep or maintain any animal or fowl in a state of malnourishment or neglect.
(c)
The animal control officer or a police officer may immediately impound the animal of any person determined to be in violation of this section and promptly summon the owner to the municipal court. The owner will be afforded a hearing in the municipal court and, if the municipal court determines that a the person has violated the provisions of this Section, the municipal court may impose a fine of not more than one hundred dollars ($100.00) on the person or order the animal impounded and disposed of in the manner provided by section 5-29.

Sec. 5-7. - Abandonment of domesticated animals and unattended animals.
(a)
It is unlawful for the owner or custodian of any animal to abandon such animal on any street, road, highway, public place, or on private property when not in the care of another person. As used in this subsection, the term "abandon" means to leave an animal with no intent to continue to provide care or shelter for the animal, or no intent to return and reclaim the animal. Intent to abandon can be inferred if an animal is left unattended for more than forty-eight (48) hours.
(b)
It is unlawful for the owner or custodian of any animal to leave such animal in any location unattended for longer than reasonably necessary, but in no case longer than forty-eight (48) hours, to ensure the animal has adequate food, water, and shelter.
(Ord. No. 22/01, § 6, 1-10-2022)

Sec. 5-27. - Restraint and shelter of animals.
(a)
The owner or keeper of each animal within the city is responsible for keeping it from becoming a nuisance or from endangering or injuring any other person, animal, or property, and accordingly:
(1)
It is unlawful for the owner or keeper of any animal to allow the animal to go on the property of any other person or any public right-of-way, roadway or street.
(2)
When allowed off the property of the owner or keeper, all animals must be accompanied by the owner or other responsible keeper and be restrained on a leash, rope, chain, or other substantial leading device held by the owner or keeper or be in a vehicle driven or parked from which the animal cannot escape. The owner or responsible keeper restraining the animal must have the strength and ability to control the animal.
(3)
When an animal is on the property of the owner or the person having possession of, responsibility for, or the custody of an animal, the animal must be kept in control. Control of animal means the animal is either enclosed in a fence or other enclosure sufficient to contain the animal, contained by an electronic fence and shock collar or similar technology, tethered consistent with subparagraph (c); or under direct voice command.
To qualify as under direct voice command, the person must be 1) outside with the animal, 2) in close proximity to the animal, and 3) the animal must be demonstrably responsive to voice commands. A person inside a structure will not be considered under this section to have voice command over an animal that is outside. Close proximity means within a distance where the animal can respond to spoken (not shouted) commands.
(b)
The following are the minimum standards for indoor shelter for animals:
(1)
The ambient temperature must be compatible with the health of the animal.
(2)
Indoor housing facilities must be adequately ventilated by natural or mechanical means to provide for the health of the animal at all times.
(c)
All pets must have shelter available when tied or confined while unattended outdoors for any length of time.
(d)
If a dog is tied or confined unattended outdoors or in an unheated enclosure, a shelter of suitable size to accommodate the dog must be provided. The shelter must provide sufficient space for the dog and, at a minimum, must:
(1)
Allow the dog to turn around freely;
(2)
Have at least three (3) sides, a floor, and a roof;
(3)
Keep the dog dry under any and all weather conditions; and
(4)
Have suitable drainage, which rapidly eliminate excess water.
(e)
The following are the outdoor enclosure space standards:
(1)
Small dogs. Dogs weighing twenty (20) pounds and under must have a minimum of twenty-five (25) square feet enclosure per dog.
(2)
Medium dogs. Dogs weighing twenty-one (21) to fifty (50) pounds must have a minimum of fifty (50) square feet enclosure per dog.
(3)
Large dogs. Dogs weighing fifty-one (51) pounds and over must have a minimum of one hundred (100) square feet enclosure per dog.
(4)
Structural strength. The housing facilities must be structurally sound and maintained in good repair to protect the animals from injury and to contain the animals.
(f)
Animals may only be tethered under the following circumstances:
(1)
A person restraining an animal with a tether must attach the tether to a properly fitting collar or harness worn by the animal. A person may not use choke collars, pinch collars, prong collars or similar devices, nor may a person wrap a tether directly around an animal's neck;
(2)
A person may not retrain an animal with a tether in a manner that does not allow the animal to access adequate food, water, or shelter, including shade when the temperature is above eighty (80) degrees Fahrenheit;
(3)
The length of the tether should be no less than five (5) times the length of the animal, measured from the tip of the animal's nose to the base of its tail. In any event, a person may not restrain an animal with a tether less than ten (10) feet in length;
(4)
The animal must be tethered in a safe manner and not in a fashion that subjects the animal to tangling and choking risks; and
(5)
A person restraining an animal with a tether must ensure that the area in which the animal is confined is free of garbage, fecal matter, or other debris that can cause harm to the animal.
(g)
Tying, staking, or fastening any animal within any public right-of-way, roadway, or street or in such a manner that the animal has access to any public right-of-way, roadway, or street is prohibited.
(h)
Every female dog or cat in heat must be confined in a building or other enclosure in such a manner that such female dog or cat cannot come into contact with another animal except for planned breeding.
(i)
Leave or confining an animal in any unattended motor vehicle under conditions that endanger the health or well-being of an animal due to heat, cold, lack of adequate ventilation, or lack of food or water, or other circumstances that could reasonably be expected to cause suffering, disability, or death to the animal is prohibited.

Sec. 5-44. - Additional safeguard requirements for a vicious or potentially dangerous animal.
(a)
In addition to the requirements set forth in article II of chapter 5, the following safeguard actions must be taken by the owner of a vicious animal in recognition of the concern for the safety of the residents of the city:
(1)
An owner or keeper of a vicious animal must keep the animal inside his or her residence at all times unless the animal is directly placed and kept within a secure enclosure. A secure enclosure means a securely enclosed pen, fence, or other structure of adequate size to humanely confine the vicious animal. The enclosure must prevent the entry of young children and be designed to keep the vicious animal from escaping. Any such pen or structure must have secure sides and a secure top attached to all sides. The sides must either be buried two (2) feet into the ground or sunken into a concrete pad. The gate to the enclosure must be inward opening and kept securely locked. If the animal is enclosed within a fence, all sides of the fence must be of sufficient height and the bottom of the fence must be constructed or secured in such a manner as to prevent the animal's escape from over or from under the fence, and all entry gates to the fence must be kept securely locked. Any such enclosure must also provide protection from the elements for the animal. An electric fence or similar technology, direct voice command, or tethering are not secure methods of enclosure for the vicious animal.
(2)
The enclosure shall be completely surrounded by a separate perimeter fence. The enclosure may not share common fencing with the perimeter fence. The perimeter fence shall be of sufficient height to prevent the vicious animal from escaping by jumping over the fence. The perimeter fence shall be sufficiently secured to prevent the vicious animal from escaping by crawling or digging under or knocking over the fence. If there is a gate in the fence, the gate shall be kept securely locked.
(3)
Except when being transported to a licensed veterinary facility, a vicious animal must not be allowed off the property of the owner or keeper. If transport for veterinary care be needed, the animal must be securely and humanely confined within a vehicle. Movement of the animal from the residence of the owner to and from the transporting vehicle and to and from the veterinary facility must be conducted with the animal securely leashed by a leash no longer than six (6) feet in length and muzzled by a muzzle or device constructed so as to prevent the animal from biting, or the animal is closed and locked in a cage or crate.
(4)
Except as may be allowed in this article, the owner or keeper of a vicious animal must at all times keep the vicious animal within either the residence of the owner or keeper or the secure enclosure as set forth in this section. It is unlawful to permit the animal to be outside the residence or the secure enclosure of the owner or keeper unless the animal is properly muzzled by a muzzle or device constructed so as to prevent the animal from biting, and the animal is attended by the owner or keeper, who is capable of controlling the animal, and the animal is restrained by a secure collar and leash of sufficient strength to prevent escape. The leash must be no longer than six (6) feet in length. The animal must be kept at least fifteen (15) feet within the perimeter boundaries of the property unless the perimeter boundary is securely fenced.
(5)
The owner or keeper of a vicious animal must maintain at all times either a policy of insurance or a surety bond in a minimum amount of fifty thousand dollars ($50,000.00) to cover claims for any personal injuries inflicted by the vicious animal, which policy or surety bond must be issued by an insurer or surety, as the case may be, authorized to transact business in this state. A copy of the insurance policy or surety bond, and all renewals thereof, must be provided by the owner or keeper to the city's animal control department.
(6)
The premises where a vicious animal is kept must be posted with clearly visible signs warning that there is an animal on the premises that presents a danger to human beings. The signs must include a symbol sufficient to convey without words the message that there is an animal on the premises that presents a danger to human beings. The signs must be conspicuously displayed on each exterior side of the enclosure for each fifty (50) feet of the enclosure, with a minimum of two (2) signs, in addition to a sign on each ingress and egress point to the enclosure. The signs must be a minimum of ten (10) inches tall and fourteen (14) inches long. In addition, signs must be placed at each door to the house if the vicious animal is kept at any time in the house. The signs must also comply with the requirements set forth by the Georgia Department of Natural Resources. A copy of the Department of Natural Resources' sign requirements are available from the animal control department.
(7)
The owner or keeper of a vicious animal must, prior to placing the animal on the premises, have the animal micro-chip registered, at the owner's expense, and provide the registration number to the city's animal control department. The owner or keeper must keep the micro-chip registration information updated and provide the city's animal control department with the updated registration information.
(b)
In addition to the requirements set forth in article II of chapter 5, the following safeguard actions must be taken by the owner of a potentially dangerous animal in recognition of the concern for the safety of the residents of the city;
(1)
An owner or keeper of a potentially dangerous animal must keep the animal inside his or her residence at all times unless the animal is directly placed and kept within a proper enclosure. A proper enclosure means a securely enclosed pen, fence, or other structure of adequate size to humanely confine the potentially dangerous animal. The enclosure must prevent the entry of young children and be designed to keep the potentially dangerous animal from escaping. Any such pen or structure must have secure sides and a secure top attached to all sides. The gate to the enclosure must be inward opening and kept secured. If the animal is enclosed within a fence, all sides of the fence must be of sufficient height and the bottom of the fence must be constructed or secured in such a manner as to prevent the animal's escape from over or from under the fence, and all entry gates to the fence must be kept secured. Any such enclosure must also provide protection from the elements for the animal. An electric fence or similar technology, direct voice command or tethering is not a proper method of enclosure for the potentially dangerous animal.
(2)
Except as otherwise may be allowed in this article, the owner or keeper of a potentially dangerous animal must at all times keep the potentially dangerous animal within either the residence of the owner or keeper or the proper enclosure as set forth in this section. It is unlawful to permit a potentially dangerous animal to be outside the residence or the proper enclosure of the owner or keeper unless the potentially dangerous animal is attended by the owner or keeper, who is capable of controlling the animal, the animal, is restrained by a secure collar and leash no longer than six (6) feet in length, and the animal is muzzled by a muzzle or device constructed so as to prevent the animal from biting.
(3)
The premises where a potentially dangerous animal is kept must be posted with clearly visible signs warning that there is an animal on the premises that presents a danger to human beings. The signs must include a symbol sufficient to convey the message that there is an animal on the premises that presents a danger to human beings. The signs must be conspicuously displayed on each exterior side of the enclosure for each fifty (50) feet of the enclosure, with a minimum of two (2) signs, in addition to a sign on each ingress and egress point to the enclosure. The signs must be a minimum of ten (10) inches tall and fourteen (14) inches long. In addition, signs must be placed at each door to the house if the vicious animal is kept at any time in the house.
(4)
The owner or keeper of a potentially dangerous animal must, prior to placing the animal on the premises, have the animal micro-chip registered, at the owner's expense, and provide the registration number to the city's animal control department. The owner or keeper must keep the micro-chip registration information updated and provide the city's animal control department with the updated registration information.

Sec. 5-45. - Owner notification requirements.
The owner or keeper of a vicious or potentially dangerous animal must report to the city's animal control department within twenty-four (24) hours of any of the following events:
(1)
Escape of the animal;
(2)
An attack on a human or domestic animal by the vicious animal;
(3)
Transfer of location of the animal;
(4)
Transfer of ownership of the animal; or
(5)
Death of the animal.
(Ord. No. 07/8, §§ 4, 5, 9-17-2007; Ord. No. 22/01, § 20, 1-10-2022)
Sec. 5-46. - Impoundment of vicious or potentially dangerous animals.
(a)
A law enforcement officer or animal control officer must immediately impound any animal the officer believes poses a threat to the public safety or any vicious or potentially dangerous animal as defined in this article which is not held, kept, or otherwise maintained according to the requirements of this article. At the time said vicious or potentially dangerous animal is impounded, the animal control officer must provide the owner or keeper of said animal with a written citation to appear in the city's municipal court regarding the violations of this article.
(b)
The owner or keeper of the animal is responsible for payment of all expenses associated with the impounding of the animal if it is determined by the city's municipal court that the owner has violated any of the provisions of this article.
(c)
The city's municipal court may order the city's animal control officer to euthanize an animal if the court determines that the animal is a vicious or potentially dangerous animal, the owner or keeper is guilty of violating one (1) or more provisions of this article, and the owner or keeper has either not timely exercised his appeal rights or has exhausted his appeal rights regarding the criminal conviction.
(d)
An animal control officer or any law enforcement officer may pursue a vicious or potentially dangerous animal onto private property to effect capture of the animal believed to be in violation of this article. Any law enforcement officer or animal control officer has the authority to enter onto private or public property for the purpose of ensuring compliance with the provisions of this article.
(e)
If the animal is classified as a vicious or potentially dangerous animal, and the animal is not ordered to be euthanized, the animal will not be returned to the owner until payment of reasonable confiscation cost. In the event the owner has not complied with the provisions of this section and also satisfied the requirements set forth in this article within ten (10) days of the date the animal was classified, said animal will be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner; provided, however, upon showing for good cause, the director of public safety or his or her designee may extend said time, up to an additional fifteen (15) days, upon payment by the owner of the per diem boarding cost for said animal.
(Ord. No. 07/8, §§ 4, 5, 9-17-2007; Ord. No. 18-01, § 1, 1-8-2018; Ord. No. 18-05, § 3, 2-12-2018; Ord. No. 22/01, § 21, 1-10-2022)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 22/01, § 21, adopted Jan. 10, 2022 amended § 5-46 and in doing so changed the title of said section from "Impoundment of vicious animals" to "Impoundment of vicious or potentially dangerous animals," as set out herein.
Sec. 5-47. - Registration of vicious or potentially dangerous animal.
(a)
It is unlawful for an owner or keeper to have or possess within the city limits a vicious or potentially dangerous animal without a certificate of registration issued by the city's animal control department in accordance with the provisions of this article.
(b)
The owner or keeper of a vicious or potentially dangerous animal must obtain a certificate of registration annually.
(c)
The animal control department must issue a certificate of registration to the owner or keeper of a vicious or potentially dangerous animal if the animal control department finds sufficient evidence that the owner or keeper has complied with all of the provisions of this chapter, including those provisions regarding this article.
(d)
The owner or keeper of any vicious or potentially dangerous animal who brings a vicious animal into the city must satisfy all of the requirements of this article and register the vicious animal with the animal control department within ten (10) days of the arrival of the vicious animal in the city.
(e)
No certificate of registration will be issued to any person who has been convicted of two (2) or more violations of this article.
(f)
No certificate of registration for a vicious or potentially dangerous animal will be issued to any person who has been convicted of.
(1)
A felony as defined in O.C.G.A. 17-10-6.1;
(2)
The felony of dog fighting as provided for in O.C.G.A. 16-12-37, or the felony of aggravated cruelty to animals as provided for in O.C.G.A. 16-12-4; or
(3)
A felony involving trafficking in illegal drugs provided for in O.C.G.A. 16-13-31 from the time of conviction until two (2) years after the completion of his or her sentence, nor to any person residing with such person.
(g)
Liability; sovereign immunity. Issuance of a certificate of registration or the renewal of a certificate of registration by the city does not warrant or guarantee that the requirements specified in this article are maintained by the owner of a vicious dog on a continuous basis following the date of the issuance of the initial certificate of registration or following the date of any annual renewal of such certificate. Under O.C.G.A. § 4-8-31, under no circumstances will the city or any of its employees be held liable for any damages to any person who suffers an injury inflicted by a dog as a result of failure to enforce the provisions of this chapter. Nothing in this chapter is intended to be or will be construed as a waiver of the city's entitlement to sovereign immunity.
(Ord. No. 07/8, §§ 4, 5, 9-17-2007; Ord. No. 22/01, § 22, 1-10-2022)

Sec. 5-48. - Maximum number of animals that may be kept on one residential property.
(a)
No owner or lessee of a residential property in the city may permit more than one (1) vicious animal to be kept or maintained upon any residential property.
(b)
No owner or lessee of a residential property in the city may permit more than three (3) potentially dangerous animals to be kept or maintained upon any residential property.
(Ord. No. 07/8, §§ 4, 5, 9-17-2007; Ord. No. 22/01, § 23, 1-10-2022)
Sec. 5-49. - Additional requirements of chapter 5 of the City Code of Ordinances.
The regulations and requirements for vicious animals established by this article are in addition to the remaining requirements for all animals governed by chapter 5 of the LaFayette City Code. Nothing contained in this article relieves the owner or keeper of a vicious or potentially dangerous animal from compliance with the remaining provisions and regulations of chapter 5 of the LaFayette City Code with respect to said vicious or potentially dangerous animal. In the event of a conflict between any regulations established by this article and the remaining regulations of chapter 5 of the LaFayette City Code, the more stringent regulations will govern.
(Ord. No. 07/8, §§ 4, 5, 9-17-2007; Ord. No. 22/01, § 24, 1-10-2022)
Sec. 5-50. - Exemptions from vicious or potentially dangerous animal definition.
(a)
An animal that is being used by a law enforcement officer to carry out law enforcement duties is not a vicious or potentially dangerous animal within the meaning of this article.
(b)
An animal will not be considered a vicious animal or otherwise in violation of this article if the injury inflicted by the animal was sustained by a person who, at the time, was: committing a willful trespass or other tort; committing or attempting to commit a crime: tormenting, abusing, or assaulting the animal, or had in the past been observed or reported to have tormented, abused, or assaulted the animal.
(Ord. No. 07/8, §§ 4, 5, 9-17-2007; Ord. No. 22/01, § 25, 1-10-2022)

Sec. 5-51. - Violations.
(a)
The owner or keeper of a vicious animal that violates the provisions of this Article is guilty of a misdemeanor of high and aggravated nature. In addition to any confinement that might be imposed for a conviction under this article, for the first conviction a fine of not less than one hundred fifty dollars ($150.00), for the second conviction a fine of not less than five hundred dollars ($500.00) will be imposed, and for a third or subsequent conviction a fine of not less than seven hundred fifty dollars ($750.00) will be imposed.
(b)
The owner or keeper of a potentially dangerous animal that violates the provisions of this article is guilty of a misdemeanor. In addition to any confinement that might be imposed for a conviction under this article, for the first conviction a fine of not less than one hundred dollars ($100.00), for the second conviction a fine of not less than two hundred dollars ($200.00) will be imposed, and for a third or subsequent conviction a fine of not less than three hundred dollars ($400.00) will be imposed.
In addition, the maintenance of any vicious or potentially dangerous animal in noncompliance with the terms of this article is deemed and declared a public nuisance and will subject the owner or keeper to any and all penalties and remedies available under the laws of the State of Georgia and the ordinances of City of LaFayette relative to the abatement of a public nuisance.
(Ord. No. 07/8, §§ 4, 5, 9-17-2007; Ord. No. 22/01, § 26, 1-10-2022)

Sec. 5-61. - Definitions.
For the purposes of this article, the following terms, phrases, words and their derivations shall have the meanings given herein:
Livestock means any domesticated animals of the type raised on a farm, and includes, but is not limited to, cattle, hogs, sheep, goats, horses, ponies, burros, llamas, or mules.
The term person carries the same meaning as found in section 1-2.
(Ord. No. 02/05, § 1, 7-19-2002)
Sec. 5-62. - Restrictions.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to keep, maintain or otherwise have livestock, either temporarily or permanently, on lots containing less than two (2) acres of permanent pasture land within the city. Said two (2) acres of permanent pasture land shall be measured so as to exclude any structures from the calculation of the required two (2) acre minimum.
(b)
Said restriction shall in no way affect the keeping of livestock for the limited and temporary purpose of licensed sales, including, but not limited to, auction sales.
(c)
Nothing in this section shall prohibit a show, carnival, fair, rodeo, or circus licensed by the city from displaying or using such animals in entertainment programs.
(Ord. No. 02/05, § 2, 7-19-2002)
Sec. 5-63. - Administration.
It shall be the duty of the city manager, and such employees of the city as he may appoint, to administer and the animal control officer of the city to enforce the provisions of this article. The city manager and animal control officer of the city may enter upon private or public property to determine the presence of livestock thereon and to remove any such livestock existing thereon.
(Ord. No. 02/05, § 3, 7-19-2002)
Sec. 5-64. - Violations.
(a)
Whenever the city manager, or the animal control officer of the city, shall determine livestock to exist within the city in violation of this article, he shall issue a written notice to the owner or occupant of the premises on which the livestock is found specifying the violation of this article and requesting that the livestock be removed from the city within ten (10) days from the date the written notice is served as provided for in subsections (b) and (c) of this section.
(b)
Such written notice may be served personally, or by certified mail, on the owner of the land as shown on the most recent city ad valorem tax digest, or to the occupant if the owner is not in possession of the premises.
(c)
If it is not possible to serve notice to the owner by the above specified methods, the notice to him to remove the livestock may be given by posting such notice on the property where the livestock is found and, in addition, by posting such notice to a public bulletin board at the city hall.
(Ord. No. 02/05, § 4, 7-19-2002)
Sec. 5-65. - Removal.
Ten (10) days after the date of service of the written notice requesting removal of the livestock from the city, the animal control officer of the city will cause the livestock to be removed from the premises. Such livestock will be disposed of in accordance with section 5-2 of this chapter. The owner or occupant of the premises from which the livestock is removed will be liable to the city for any expenses incurred in removing and disposing of said livestock.
(Ord. No. 02/05, § 5, 7-19-2002; Ord. No. 22/01, § 27, 1-10-2022)
Sec. 5-66. - Penalty.
(a)
Upon the failure, neglect, or refusal of any owner or occupant so notified to remove such livestock within the designated time given for such removal, the animal control officer of the city shall issue a citation for violation of this article. The citation and summons shall require the owner or occupant to appear in the municipal court of the city for violation of this article. Violation of this article may be punished by a fine of five hundred dollars ($500.00) and/or imprisonment of thirty (30) days. Each day following the expiration of the ten-day period provided for in the written notice served on the owner and/or occupier of the premises where livestock is found the owner or occupant fails to remove such livestock as set forth above shall be considered a separate offense.
(b)
The removal of the livestock from the premises prior to the expiration of the ten-day period provided for in subsection 5-64(a) shall be considered compliance with the provision of this article, and no further action shall be taken against the owner of the livestock or the owner or occupants of the premises.
(Ord. No. 02/05, § 6, 7-19-2002)
Sec. 5-67. - Liability.
Neither the city, the city manager, or the city's agents or employees shall be liable for any loss or damage to the livestock while being removed or as a result of any subsequent sale or other disposition thereof.
(Ord. No. 02/05, § 7, 7-19-2002)
