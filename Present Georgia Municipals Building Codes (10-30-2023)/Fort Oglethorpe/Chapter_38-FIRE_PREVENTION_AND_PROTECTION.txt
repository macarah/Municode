ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 38-1. - Parking near fire; obstructing approach of fire apparatus.
It shall be unlawful for any person, knowing that fire has broken out in the city, to park an automobile within 500 feet of the fire, or to drive or park an automobile in any manner so as to obstruct the approach of the fire engine or other firefighting equipment.
(Code 1986, § 10-1-7)
Cross reference— Traffic and vehicles, ch. 90.
Sec. 38-2. - Provisions saved from repeal.
Ordinance number 97-81 adopted January 26, 1998 on the subject of open burning and hazardous materials is not repealed and shall continue in full force and effect pending a revision of the subject matter by the city.

ARTICLE II. - FIRE DEPARTMENT[2]

--- (2) ---
Cross reference— Administration, ch. 2.

Sec. 38-31. - Creation.
A department to be known as the Fort Oglethorpe Fire Department is created. The object of the department shall be the prevention of fire and the protection of life and property within the authorized fire protection limits of the city.
(Code 1986, § 4-2-1)
Sec. 38-32. - Officers.
(a)
The fire department shall consist of a chief, deputy chief, assistant chief and other officers as the chief and city council may deem necessary for effective operation of the department.
(b)
The chief shall be appointed by the city council for an indefinite period of time, and his tenure of office shall depend on his good conduct and efficiency. The chief shall be technically qualified by training and experience and shall have the ability to command personnel and to hold their respect and confidence. He shall be removed from office only for just cause and may request a public hearing before the city council.
(c)
The chief shall be held accountable for the efficient operation of the department, and shall make written and verbal reports to the city council and city manager as may be required. All other department and company officers shall be accountable to the chief only.
(d)
The deputy and assistant chiefs and all other department and company officers shall be appointed by the chief. Such officers shall be accountable only to the chief and subject to removal by him, to be approved by the city manager.
(Code 1986, § 4-2-2)
Sec. 38-33. - Duties of the chief.
(a)
The fire chief shall formulate a set of rules and regulations to govern the department and shall be responsible to the city manager for the personnel, morale and general efficiency of the department.
(b)
The chief shall determine the number and type of companies of which the department is to be composed and shall determine the response of such companies to alarms.
(c)
The chief shall, at least once a month, conduct or cause to be conducted suitable drills or instruction in the operation and handling of equipment, first aid and rescue work, salvage, a study of buildings in the authorized fire protection limits, fire prevention, water supplies, and all other matters generally considered essential to good firefighting and safety of life and property from fire.
(d)
The chief is required to assist the proper authorities in suppressing the crime of arson by investigating or causing to be investigated the cause, origin and circumstances of all fires.
(e)
The chief or his authorized representative is empowered to enter any and all buildings and premises at any reasonable hour for the purpose of making inspections and to serve notice upon the owner or occupant to abate, within a specified time, any and all fire hazards that may be found.
(f)
Any person so served with a notice to abate any fire hazard shall comply with such notice and promptly notify the chief.
(g)
The chief shall see that complete records are kept of all fires, inspections, apparatus and minor equipment, personnel and other information about the work of the department.
(h)
The chief shall report monthly to the city manager the condition of the apparatus and equipment; the number of fires during the month, their location and cause, and date and loss occasioned by the fire; the number and purpose of all other runs made; the number of members responding to each fire or other run; and any changes in membership.
(i)
The chief shall make a complete official annual report to the city manager within 60 days after the close of the fiscal year, such report to include the information specified in subsection (g) of this section, together with comparative data for previous years and recommendations for improving the operation of the department.
(Code 1986, § 4-2-3)
Sec. 38-34. - Chief of fire department; police powers.
The fire chief or assistant fire chief shall have authority to exercise police powers in the enforcement of any provision of this Code or any other ordinance pertaining to the fire department, right-of-way, inspection of buildings, and fire escapes, or other provisions or ordinances pertaining to fire protection.
(Code 1986, § 4-2-4)
Sec. 38-35. - Membership.
(a)
The membership of the fire department shall consist of such persons as may be appointed by the fire chief and shall be able-bodied citizens residing within the fire protection limits, preferably property owners whose business activities are normally within the confines of the fire protection limits, and who have telephones in their homes. Determination of whether candidates for appointment are able-bodied shall be made by the chief after a medical and physical examination has been made in a manner prescribed by the chief and approved by the city manager.
(b)
Any member of the department may be suspended or discharged from the department by the chief at any time he may deem such action necessary for the good of the department. On written request of such member to the city council, he shall be given a public hearing on the charges brought by the chief.
(c)
Each member shall be issued a badge designating his rank.
(d)
Each member of the department driving a car shall be issued a suitable fire department insignia to be attached to the car.
(Code 1986, § 4-2-5)
Sec. 38-36. - Special officers.
(a)
The fire department may elect a president, vice-president, secretary, treasurer or any other officers necessary in any volunteer firefighters club that they choose to organize. Such officers may be elected in any manner and for any term the membership may decide upon, and their duties shall be to arrange for and manage any or all social or other functions sponsored by the department.
(b)
The functions and duties of the special officers shall in no way interfere with those of the regular department officers who are charged with the responsibility for all fire service activities of the department.
(Code 1986, § 4-2-6)
Sec. 38-37. - Equipment.
(a)
The fire department shall be equipped with such modern and up-to-date apparatus and other equipment as may be required to maintain its efficiency and properly protect life and property from fire.
(b)
Recommendations for apparatus and equipment needed shall be made by the chief, and after approval by the city manager shall be purchased in such manner as may be designated by the city council.
(c)
All equipment of the department shall be safely and conveniently housed in such places as may be designated by the city manager.
(d)
Suitable arrangements or equipment shall be provided for citizens to turn in any alarm, and for notifying all members of the department so that they may promptly respond.
(e)
No person shall use any fire apparatus or equipment for any private purpose, nor shall any person willfully and without proper authority take away or conceal any article used in any way by the department.
(f)
No person shall enter any place where fire apparatus is housed or handle any apparatus or equipment belonging to the department unless accompanied by or having the special permission of an officer or authorized member of the department.
(g)
No person shall willfully or knowingly interfere or tamper with or injure or damage in any way or by any means whatever any equipment or material or apparatus of any kind connected with or used by any fire company as a part of their machinery or material for extinguishing fires, or knowingly or willfully put any obstruction in front of any engine, reel, or hook and ladder house.
(h)
No person shall injure, destroy or in any manner interfere with the electric fire alarm of the city, or with the batteries, boxes, stations, wires, or other apparatus or appurtenances of or used in connection with the system.
(i)
The fire chief is authorized to enter into agreements or contracts with nearby incorporated communities having recognized and rated fire departments or governing bodies of other organizations to provide the members of such communities or organizations with fire protection or to establish or participate in any recognized mutual aid system or network.
(j)
No apparatus shall be rented out or permitted to leave the authorized fire protection limits except to respond to alarms turned in from contract subscribers of the volunteer fire department, or in response to a call for aid at a fire in a neighboring community without the consent of the senior officer in charge of the department. The officer in charge of the department shall have power to assign equipment for response to calls for outside aid in accordance with subsection (i) of this section, and in other cases only when the absence of such equipment will not jeopardize protection within the normal fire protection limits.
(Code 1986, § 4-2-7)
Sec. 38-38. - Parking near fire stations.
No person shall park any vehicle or otherwise cause any obstruction to be placed within 50 feet of the entrance to any fire station or other place where fire apparatus is stored.
(Code 1986, § 4-2-8)
Sec. 38-39. - Firefighters; facilities and equipment; use in extinguishing fire authorized.
In case of fire, all firefighters on duty are authorized to use all available public or private facilities and equipment in extinguishing such fire.
(Code 1986, § 4-2-10)
Sec. 38-40. - Interference with firefighters prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to interfere with a firefighter on duty in the firefighter's use of any public or private equipment being used by the firefighter in fighting and extinguishing a fire.
(Code 1986, § 4-2-11)
Sec. 38-41. - Fire vehicles, riding restricted to firefighters.
No person not a member of a fire company of the city shall ride upon any wagon or truck going to or returning from a fire.
(Code 1986, § 4-2-12)
Sec. 38-42. - Personal cars of department members responding to fire.
All motor equipment and all personal cars of fire department members shall have the right-of-way over all other traffic when responding to an alarm.
(Code 1986, § 4-2-13)
Sec. 38-43. - Water being used in extinguishing fire, cutting off or order to cut off prohibited.
No person shall cut off or order the water cut off at any public or private water hydrant when the water is being used by the fire department in fighting and extinguishing a fire.
(Code 1986, § 4-2-14)
Sec. 38-44. - Persons permitted within vicinity of fire.
No person except firefighters, council members and other city officers, the police force, the property owners and their agents, and insurance agents, shall be allowed within the immediate vicinity of a fire, except by request of the fire chief or the officers in command of the department.
(Code 1986, § 4-2-15)
Sec. 38-45. - Electricians; duty at fire.
It shall be the duty of the membership corporation or other appropriate electric power provider to have a competent electrician turn off the electric current or take such other means as are necessary to protect the safety of firefighters and other citizens. Such electrician, immediately upon his arrival at a fire, shall report to the fire chief, and in his absence to the assistant fire chief, and, at the request of such officer, disconnect wires, cut off the current, or take such other action as to such wires as may be necessary for safety of firefighters and other citizens. Upon the failure of the company to have an electrician at each fire as required by this section, the company shall be subject to punishment as provided in Charter section 4.13.
(Code 1986, § 4-2-16)
Sec. 38-46. - Looters at fires.
The fire chief, or the officer in command at the fire, shall cause to be arrested by the police any person who shall be caught stealing goods or any article of value from any store, house or its vicinity during a fire.
(Code 1986, § 4-2-17)
Sec. 38-47. - Insurance reports of fire losses paid.
All fire insurance companies or their authorized representatives operating within the corporate limits of the city shall file with the fire chief a report of all fire losses paid within 15 days after each claim is paid.
(Code 1986, § 4-2-18)
Sec. 38-48. - Fire hydrants.
As referenced in section 38-81 state minimum fire safety standards adopted, incorporated by reference; amendments. The International Fire Code (IFC) is incorporated by reference. IFC outlines fire protection systems and appliances public and private. City ordinance requires the following in addition to those requirements of the IFC.
(a)
[Fire hydrant installation.] Fire hydrants installed in the city shall meet ANSI/AWWA C205 standards, ISO standards and have a minimum of one 4½-inch steamer, or pumper connection, and two 2½-inch connections. All fire hydrants installed in the city shall be approved by the fire official, and the City of Fort Oglethorpe utilities department head. Fire hydrants without a pumper connection shall not be permitted. All hydrants cut off valves shall be accessible and readily identified. Hydrant cut off valves shall be located within a 3-foot. radius of the fire hydrant it serves unless otherwise authorized by the fire official, or City of Fort Oglethorpe utilities department head.
(b)
Water supply. Adequate water supply to meet a minimum needed fire flow (NFF) as defined by ISO shall be provided for all buildings constructed within the city. More capacity may be required under certain circumstances by the fire official. Water mains, appliances, and fire hydrants shall be installed to provide the NFF or available water whichever is greater. All fire flow tests shall be witnessed by the fire official, or representative, prior to final approval. At no time shall a water main in a predominately commercial area, or serving a commercial structure, be less than 8 inches diameter and must meet NFPA 24 standards as adopted and amended by 120.3.3. At no time shall a water main serving any other area be less than 6 inches in diameter. No dead end water mains shall be permitted. All water mains shall be part of a grid, or loop, system to provide for the use of more than one hydrant at a time.
(c)
Locations of fire hydrants. Approved fire hydrants shall be located every 300 feet. Fire hydrants may be located every 500 feet in those areas determined by the fire official to be predominately residential, one and two family dwellings only. Maximum distance from the nearest hydrant to the most remote exterior point of any building shall be 300 foot. The maximum distance may be increased to 500 feet for those one and two family dwellings determined by the fire official to be located in a predominately residential area. The distance shall be measured on a roadway surface meeting the fire department access requirements outlined in the SFPC, with distance being added for turns and obstacles that may increase the way hose may be laid.
(d)
Maintenance. In addition to those requirements in the IFC, all hydrants found to be defective shall be repaired to proper working order within 36 hours of notification. If the hydrant is not repaired within the time specified the City of Fort Oglethorpe will repair the hydrant and all costs will be charged to the owner of said hydrant. The owner of said hydrant will be responsible for the maintenance and repair of hydrants, and will be charged for the repairs if done by the City of Fort Oglethorpe when a defective fire hydrant is found, when a fire hydrant is found to be out of service, and when a hydrant is fixed. Any fire hydrant not located within a public right-of-way or within an easement which has been deeded to the city shall be deemed private and will be the responsibility of the owner of the property where the fire hydrant is located.
(e)
Obstruction. It shall be unlawful for any person to obstruct or hinder the approach of the Fire Department to any fire hydrants. In order to assure access to fire hydrants, the following requirements must be met:
(1)
A minimum of 36 inches of clearance is required around a fire plug; and
(2)
No person or entity shall construct any fence or other structure which would encroach the 36-inch minimum clearance for fire hydrants as outlined in subsection (1) of this section and no fence shall be constructed between the fire hydrant and the roadway; and
(3)
Fire Hydrants shall not be buried, caps shall be at least 12 inches from grade; and
(4)
No person shall allow trees, bushes or other growth upon the person's property to interfere with the approach to any fire hydrant or to encroach the 36-inch minimum clearance; and
(5)
A fire plug may not be painted except by an authorized agent or employee of the fire department of the City of Fort Oglethorpe; and
(6)
Parking of motor vehicles within 15 feet of a fire hydrant is prohibited. Parking lot lines may be required as necessary.
(f)
[Exceptions.] Exceptions to the above shall be other installations acceptable to and approved by the authority having jurisdiction.
(Ord. No. 97-81, § 4-2-9, 1-26-1998; Ord. No. 2000-53, § 2, 12-11-2000; Ord. No. 2003-1, § 1, 3-24-2003; Ord. No. 2011-12, § 1, 3-14-2011)
Sec. 38-49. - False alarms.
It shall be unlawful for any person to give or cause to be given a false alarm with the intent to deceive, or to pull the slide of any station or signal box, except in the case of fire. All fire alarms shall be maintained so not to cause undue false alarms.
(Ord. No. 97-81, § 4-2-19, 1-26-1998; Ord. No. 2000-53, § 2, 12-11-2000; Ord. No. 2003-1, § 1, 3-24-2003)
Sec. 38-50. - Fire alarm apparatus, injuring, destroying or interfering with same.
It shall be unlawful for any person to injure, destroy, or in any manner interfere with the electric fire alarm of the city, or with the batteries, boxes, stations, wires, or other apparatus or appurtenances thereof, or used in connection therewith.
(Ord. No. 97-81, § 4-2-20, 1-26-1998)
Sec. 38-51. - Flammable weeds, grasses and debris, notice to remove, failure to comply, removal by city and assessment of gross cost thereof.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to have and maintain within the city any vacant lot or premises whereon flammable weeds, grasses and debris are permitted to remain after being notified by the fire marshall or the fire department of the city that the same constitutes a fire hazard.
(b)
After ten days written notice to the owner, the city may, by its own forces, eliminate such fire hazard by the removal of such grasses, weeds, or other debris thereon and thereupon access the cost thereof against the owner of the property.
(Ord. No. 97-81, § 4-2-21, 1-26-1998)
Sec. 38-52. - Penalties.
Any person who violates any of the provisions of this chapter or fails to comply therewith, shall for each and every violation and non-compliance respectively, be guilty of a misdemeanor, as defined by the state. The imposition of one penalty for any violation shall not excuse the violation to continue. Penalties cannot exceed $1,000.00 or imprisonment of more than one year, or both, with the amount of fine/imprisonment being within the discretion of the municipal court of the city based upon the severity or repetition of the infraction.
(Ord. No. 97-81, § 4-2-22, 1-26-1998)
Sec. 38-53. - Jurisdiction of violation of this chapter.
As provided by law, the municipal court of the city shall have the venue and jurisdiction of any citations issued under this chapter.
(Ord. No. 97-81, § 4-2-23, 1-26-1998)
Sec. 38-54. - Mutual aid resource pact.
(a)
The city fire department may render aid and assistance during disastrous incidents and other immediate response emergencies outside its jurisdiction.
(b)
The city fire department may form a mutual aid resource pact, known as the Georgia Mutual Aid Group, with other jurisdictions, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 25-6-1 et seq., the Georgia Mutual Aid Group being a public corporation.
(c)
The Georgia Mutual Aid Group shall be governed by O.C.G.A. § 25-6-1 et seq.
(d)
The city fire department fire chief or his/her designee shall be authorized to act on behalf of the city fire department on all matters relating to the activities and functions of the mutual aid resource pact once it has been established.
(e)
The city fire department may withdraw from the Georgia Mutual Aid Group by resolution and vote of the city council pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 25-6-1 et seq.
(f)
The city fire department's liabilities, privileges, and immunities, including those of its firefighters and other person's acting on its behalf, shall be governed by O.C.G.A. § 25-6-1 et seq.
(Ord. No. 99-54, §§ 1—6, 11-8-1999)

ARTICLE III. - FIRE PREVENTION CODE

Sec. 38-81. - State minimum fire safety standards adopted; incorporated by reference; amendments.
There are adopted for the purposes of the public safety and welfare those certain fire prevention codes recognized as the state minimum fire safety standards and known as the National Fire Protection Association (National Fire Code and National Electrical Code), the American Insurance Association (National Building Code), the successor to the National Board of Fire Underwriters, the American Standards Association, and the Standard Building Code Congress (Standard Building Code) and the rules, regulations and amendments promulgated by the state safety fire commissioner that prevent the destruction of life and property by fire, explosion and related hazards, except such portions as are hereafter deleted, modified or amended. These codes, including the modifications and amendments, are incorporated by reference, and made part of this section as if fully set forth in this section. Copies of the specifications, rules and regulations shall be kept on file and open for public inspection in the office of the building official.
(Ord. No. 95-2, § 4-2-31, 4-24-1995)
Sec. 38-82. - Purposes, intent and scope.
(a)
Purposes and intent. It is the primary purpose of this article to provide for the reasonable protection of life and property from the hazards created by fire, smoke, explosion or panic created from a fear of fire or smoke. It is the intent of this article to accomplish its purpose:
(1)
Through educational efforts and programs designed to change high-risk attitudes and behaviors that are the root causes of most fire-related problems;
(2)
Through consultations and informational programs, develop in people an awareness of the provisions of this article and other applicable laws, and aid them to determine the options available to correct hazards or problems noted; and
(3)
By providing through this article reasonable, realistic and practical safeguards against fire and its related hazards. This article is declared to be remedial and shall be construed to secure beneficial interests and purposes, which are public safety, health and general welfare, through safety to life and property from fire, explosion and other hazards incident to the use and occupance of buildings, structures or premises.
(b)
Scope.
(1)
Applicability. The provisions of this article shall apply to all new and existing buildings, structures, premises, facilities and applicable conditions within the city; however, existing situations or conditions not in strict compliance with this article may be permitted to continue unless they are deemed by the fire marshal to constitute a distinct hazard to life or to property from fire, smoke or explosion. The provisions of this article shall not apply to the interior of one- or two-family dwellings or to the premises upon which such dwellings are located or to the interior of any apartment dwelling unit, provided such dwellings or apartments are used strictly for residential purposes. This article shall apply to such dwelling units whenever an activity coming under this article is conducted in such units and which creates a hazard from fire, smoke or explosion to life or to property.
(2)
Federal and state authority. The provisions of this article shall not be held to deprive any federal or state agency or any applicable governing body having jurisdiction of any power or authority which it had on the effective date of the adoption of this article or of any remedy then existing for the enforcement of its orders, nor shall it deprive any individual or corporation of its legal rights as provided by law.
(3)
Appendices. The appendices included in the codes adopted in section 38-81 are not intended for enforcement unless specifically referenced in the text of such codes.
(4)
Referenced standards. Standards referenced in the codes adopted in section 38-81 shall be considered an integral part of such codes without separate adoption. If specific portions of a standard are denoted by code text, only those portions of the standard shall be enforced. Permissive and advisory provisions in a standard shall not be construed as mandatory.
(5)
Existing buildings and special historic buildings. The provisions of this article shall apply to existing buildings or structures in the city; however, the provisions of such codes shall be coordinated by the fire marshal. The provisions of this article shall apply to special buildings or structures identified by the state or by the city as historic buildings; however, the provisions of this article shall be coordinated by the fire marshal with the applicable provisions of applicable state laws or regulations.
(Ord. No. 95-2, § 4-2-32, 4-24-1995)
Sec. 38-83. - Organization, administration and enforcement.
There is created the bureau of fire prevention and community emergency education within the city for the purpose of implementing the provisions of this article. The bureau shall be under the direction and general supervision of the fire marshal. The fire marshal is responsible to the fire chief for the implementation of the provisions of this article and for the direction and supervision of the bureau. The bureau shall strive to improve the safety of the lives and property from fire and associated hazards within the city through programs of safety education through consultations, and through the inspection of buildings, structures, facilities and premises as often as deemed appropriate by the fire marshal. As the fire chief and fire marshal deem appropriate, the bureau may develop and implement, in coordination and cooperation with other departments, agencies or organizations, a comprehensive program of community emergency education.
(Ord. No. 95-2, § 4-2-33, 4-24-1995)
Sec. 38-84. - Appointment of the fire marshal.
The fire chief shall recommend the fire marshal to the city council, who shall have discretion in appointment of the fire marshal. The fire marshal shall possess the required qualifications to be deputized by the state fire marshal. The fire marshal shall serve at the discretion of the council.
(Ord. No. 95-2, § 4-2-34, 4-24-1995)
Sec. 38-85. - Assistance to the fire marshal.
The fire chief may detail or assign members of the fire department to the bureau of fire prevention and community emergency education to assist the fire marshal. Such members shall be under the direction and supervision of the fire marshal during their detail or assignment to the bureau. The fire chief, upon request of the fire marshal with approval by the city council, may fill positions deemed necessary for carrying out the purposes and programs of the bureau, in accordance with the personnel policies and regulations of the city. The selection shall be open to members and nonmembers of the fire department. Personnel assigned to fill positions with the bureau shall be under the direction and supervision of the fire marshal.
(Ord. No. 95-2, § 4-2-35, 4-24-1995)
Sec. 38-86. - Enforcement assistance.
The chief of police, the city marshal, the building official or law enforcement officers of the city shall render assistance to the fire marshal or the fire chief in the implementation and enforcement of this article upon the request of the fire marshal, or as appropriate, the fire chief.
(Ord. No. 95-2, § 4-2-36, 4-24-1995)
Sec. 38-87. - Inspections.
The fire marshal is authorized to inspect, or to cause to be inspected, as he deems appropriate or necessary based on fire experience in the community, on complaint, or on other applicable grounds, and as resources permit, buildings, structures, premises and facilities coming within the scope of this article. The purpose of such inspections is to ascertain if any condition exists which could be reasonably construed to cause a fire or explosion or that could contribute to the spread of a fire or its by-products, or to ascertain any significant nonconformance with the purposes, intent and other provisions of this article or of any other applicable law, rule or regulation affecting safety to life and/or property from fire, explosion or related hazards. The fire marshal is authorized to apply other applicable laws, and to cause to be corrected any condition which could be reasonably construed to cause a fire or explosion, or that could contribute to the spread of a fire, or any significant nonconformance with the purposes, intent or provisions of this article affecting safety to life and/or property from fire, explosion or related hazards.
(Ord. No. 95-2, § 4-2-37, 4-24-1995)
Sec. 38-88. - Right of entry to conduct inspections.
The fire marshal or his authorized representatives may, to the extent permitted by law, have free access to and the right to enter at all reasonable times any building, structure, facility or premises coming under the scope of this article, whether completed or under construction, for the purpose of conducting inspections and otherwise implementing the provisions of this article. Before entering any building, structure, facility or premises not normally open to the public, except when a fire or other emergency condition exists or has occurred, the fire marshal or his representative shall obtain the consent of the occupant, owner or person in charge at the time. Where consent to enter is refused, the fire marshal may seek an order or an administrative search warrant from the municipal court, or if deemed appropriate, from the superior court of Catoosa County authorizing his entry or that of his representatives for the purpose of conducting the inspection or investigation. The term "emergency condition," for purposes of this section, shall mean any condition or circumstance which the fire marshal or fire chief judges to constitute an immediate threat or danger to the occupants of the building, structure, facility or premises or to persons or property exposed to a fire, the hazards associated with fire, explosion, the threat of fire or explosion, or exposed to risks from some other hazardous condition or circumstance.
(Ord. No. 95-2, § 4-2-38, 4-24-1995)
Sec. 38-89. - Implied consent.
Any application for or the acceptance of any approval, permit, license or certificate requested, or required pursuant to this article or other applicable law or ordinance of this city or state, constitutes agreement and consent by the person making the application, or accepting the approval, permit, license or certificate to allow the fire marshal or his representatives to enter the building, structure, facility or premises at any reasonable time to conduct inspections as may be deemed appropriate by the fire marshal or as otherwise provided by this article or other applicable ordinances, rules, regulations or state law.
(Ord. No. 95-2, § 4-2-39, 4-24-1995)
Sec. 38-90. - Notice of or order to eliminate dangerous or unlawful conditions.
(a)
Whenever the fire marshal or his designated representative finds any violation of this article or any fire hazard, he is authorized to issue or serve a written notice of the findings of an inspection as set forth in this chapter and to notify the building official.
(b)
Whenever the fire marshal or his designated representative finds any violation of this article or any fire hazard which is deemed to create a serious or significant potential threat to life in event of fire or explosion, he is authorized to issue a written order to correct or abate the violation or hazard. Such order when issued shall set forth a reasonable and realistic time frame or date limit for correction, abatement or compliance. The time frame for correction, abatement or compliance established by or as directed by the fire marshal, or his designated representative, may be reasonably correlated by him to the seriousness or relative degree of hazard, and/or to the complexity of the means of correction or abatement.
(c)
Where deemed necessary, due to the potential of risk of life or property, the fire marshal may issue a verbal order for the immediate correction or abatement of a violation or fire hazard. This may be followed by a written order or notice of action.
(Ord. No. 95-2, § 4-2-40, 4-24-1995)

If such person cannot be served by delivery as noted, service may be accomplished by affixing a copy of the notice or order in a conspicuous place at or near the entrance of the building, structure, facility or premises, or by delivering or sending a copy of the notice or order to the known agent of the owner or occupant, or in the event of an absentee owner with no known agent, by sending a copy by certified or registered mail to the owner's last known residence, business or post office address.

New construction. Sec. 38-98. - Modifications and appeals.
(a)
Existing conditions. The fire marshal shall have the authority to grant a variance to the provisions of this article, or of the codes or standards adopted by reference by this article, upon application in writing by the owner and/or occupant of an existing building, structure, facility or premises, or by his authorized agent, when there are practical difficulties in the way of carrying out the strict letter of this article or the codes or standards adopted by reference by this article or the city. Such modification or variance may be granted by the fire marshal provided in his judgment the spirit, intent and purposes of this article are observed, the public safety shall be reasonably and practically secured, the modification is in the interest of the community, and substantial justice will be done. The particulars of any variance granted or allowed and the decision of the fire marshal shall be approved or disapproved by the city council at its next duly scheduled meeting.
(b)
Right to appeal. Whenever the fire marshal or fire chief shall issue an order pursuant to this article, disapprove an application, refuse to grant a permit, refuse to grant a waiver or modification, or when it is claimed that the provisions of this article or the codes and standards adopted by reference by this article or the city do not apply or that their true intent and meaning have been misconstrued or wrongly interpreted, the applicant or aggrieved party may appeal from the order or decision of the fire marshal or fire chief to the board of adjustments and appeals as established by the adopted building code of the city within 30 days after the decision is rendered by the fire marshal or fire chief. The provisions of the applicable building code relative to time limits, variations and modifications, and decisions as connected with the appeals to the board, shall be applicable to appeals from the fire marshal or the fire chief relating to this article. If not satisfied with the decision of the board, appeal may be made to the court of appropriate jurisdiction. Every decision of the board relative to this article shall be filed in the office of the fire marshal.
(Ord. No. 95-2, § 4-2-48, 4-24-1995)
Sec. 38-99. - General provisions.
(a)
Interference with fire officials. It shall be unlawful for any person to interfere with, abuse or obstruct in any way the fire chief, fire marshal or their representatives in carrying out any actions in accordance with their official duties as set forth by this article, or by any other applicable local ordinance, or by any law or regulation of the state.
(b)
Unlawful use of credentials; unlawful impersonation of fire official. It shall be unlawful for any unauthorized person to use an official or unofficial badge or credential so as to impersonate an authorized fire official or to otherwise impersonate a fire official.
(c)
Authority to investigate fires, explosions, false alarms or related incidents. The fire marshal or his authorized representatives are authorized to investigate the cause, origin and circumstances of any fire or explosion, or to investigate any incident resulting from the sending or sounding of a false alarm of fire or a bomb threat. When in the judgment of the fire marshal reasonable cause exists that a fire or explosion may have been of criminal origin, the fire marshal or his authorized representative is authorized to locate and take custody of any or all physical evidence relating to the cause of the fire or explosion and to continue the investigation to the extent deemed appropriate within the limits of fire prevention bureau resources and within the limits of applicable state laws. The fire marshal shall inform the police department of each fire of criminal or suspected criminal origin, false alarms or bomb threats. Also, he shall assist the police department in its investigations of reported incidents if so requested by that department. The police department shall be responsible for the investigation of fires and/or explosions of criminal or suspected criminal origin, false alarms or bomb threats so as to identify, apprehend and assist in the prosecution of the person responsible for such criminal act.
(d)
Evacuation of buildings, structures, facilities or premises. When, in the judgment of the fire marshal, the fire chief or their authorized representatives in their absence, there is an actual or serious potential danger to the occupants of or persons in close proximity of any building, structure, facility or premises because of unsafe structural conditions, the presence of explosives, explosive fumes or vapor, toxic fumes, gases or materials, the fire marshal, the fire chief, or their authorized representatives in their absence, may order the complete or partial immediate evacuation of any building, structure, facility or premises and, as necessary, areas in such proximity to these as to be endangered. All persons under order to evacuate shall promptly leave the evacuation area and shall not reenter until authorized to do so by the official who issued the evacuation order. This section shall not pertain to bomb threats where no bomb or explosive device is known to exist. In such bomb threat situations, the decision to evacuate or not to evacuate is the responsibility of the person in charge of the building, structure, facility or premises at that time.
(e)
Permits and approvals. No person shall maintain, store or handle materials, or conduct processes which produce conditions hazardous to life or property, or install equipment used in connection with such activities without first obtaining approval, or as appropriate based on the provisions of this article, a permit from the fire marshal. Where conditions or activities are also regulated by a state or federal agency and a permit, license or certificate is required by the regulating agency, no local permit is required; however, the approval of the fire marshal is required and he shall have the authority to apply the provisions of this article where a hazardous condition is discovered.
(f)
Tests. All equipment required by this article or other applicable codes adopted by the city or by the state shall be maintained in a safe, sound and operational condition. The fire marshal may require tests or test reports as proof of compliance. Required tests are to be made at the expense of the owner or his agent by an approved testing laboratory or other approval agency or firm acceptable to the fire marshal.
(g)
Authority to expose or stop work. Whenever any construction or installation coming under this article is subject to inspection prior to use and is covered or concealed without having first been inspected, the fire marshal may require by verbal and/or written order that such work be exposed for inspection. The fire marshal shall be notified when the installation is ready for inspection within a reasonable period of time. When any construction or installation work is being performed in violation of the plans, specifications or understandings as approved by the fire marshal, a written order may be issued to the responsible party to stop work on that portion of the work which is in violation. The order shall state the nature of the violation. No work shall be continued on that portion until the violation has been corrected.
(h)
Administrative liability. Any officer or employee of the city empowered or authorized to administer or act under the provisions of this article or other applicable codes in force in the city or adopted or required by the state, and acting for the city or state in the discharge of his duties, shall not be held liable personally; and he is relieved from all personal liability from any damages that may accrue to any person or property as a result of any act required, implied or permitted, in the discharge of his duties, or as required, implied or permitted by this article or other applicable codes in force in the city or adopted or required by the state.
(i)
Municipal liability. The city shall not be liable for any damage to persons or property resulting from any act or any failure to act in connection with any provision of this article or other applicable codes in force in the city or adopted or required by the state.
(j)
Permitting and inspection. The inspection or permitting of any building or plan by any employee of the division of fire prevention of the fire department under the requirements of this article shall not be construed in any court as a warranty of the physical condition of such building or the adequacy of such plan. Neither the city or any employee of the city shall be liable in tort for damages for any defect or hazardous or illegal condition or inadequacy in such building or plan, nor for any failure or any component of such building, which may occur subsequent to such inspection or permitting.
(Ord. No. 95-2, § 4-2-49, 4-24-1995)

Sec. 38-101. - Approvals and fire regulatory permits.
(a)
Permit required. No person shall maintain, store or handle materials or conduct processes which produce conditions hazardous to life or property, or install equipment used in connection with such activities without first obtaining approval, or as appropriate, a fire regulatory permit from the fire marshal as provided; however, the approval of the fire marshal is required, and he shall have the authority to apply the provisions of this article where a hazardous condition is discovered.
(b)
New activities. Any person desiring to conduct or engage in any new process or activity requiring a fire regulatory permit under this article shall apply for a permit as set forth in this section and shall be issued the permit prior to engaging in the conducting of the process or activity, or before occupying a building or structure requiring a permit.
(c)
Existing activities. Persons known to be conducting processes or activities required to be permitted under this article on or before the date of adoption of this article, shall be advised that a permit is required. Such persons may also be advised of any violations noted under this article that must be corrected before a permit can be issued. The fire marshal shall exercise reasonableness and shall apply the applicable provisions of sections 38-81 and 38-98 when applying the provisions of this article or of any of the technical standards adopted by reference under this article or otherwise adopted by the city council, when such are being applied to existing conditions or activities. Where there is noncompliance, the fire marshal may issue a temporary permit with an established expiration date. A temporary permit issued in connection with this subsection may be extended provided a plan of corrective action has been submitted prior to the expiration of the permit, evidence clearly indicates that compliance is being obtained, and there are practical difficulties in completing the corrective action by the expiration date.

Permitted action. A fire regulatory permit shall constitute permission to maintain, store or handle materials, or to conduct processes which may produce conditions hazardous to life or property, or to install equipment used in connection with such activities. A permit shall not be a substitute for any license or certificate required by law. A permit issued shall be construed to be a license to proceed with work and shall not be construed as authority to violate, cancel, alter or set aside any of the provisions of this article. The issuance of a permit shall not be construed as preventing the fire marshal from thereafter requiring correction of errors in plans or in construction, or of violations of this article.
(e)
Transfer of permit prohibited. A fire regulatory permit shall not be transferable, and any change in use or occupancy of a building or premises shall require a new permit. A fire regulatory permit shall continue until revoked or for such a period of time as designated at the time of issuance.
(Ord. No. 95-2, § 4-2-51, 4-24-1995)
Sec. 38-102. - Applying for a fire regulatory permit; related provisions.
(a)
Application for permit. An application for a permit shall be made to the fire marshal on a form available at the division of fire prevention. The form shall be fully completed. The application shall be accompanied by such data as may be required by the fire marshal and by the required fee, as set forth in subsection (c) of this section.

Such filing shall also constitute an understanding on the part of the permittee that the permittee, not the city, the fire marshal or the fire chief, shall bear the full responsibility for any damages to persons and/or property in connection with any fire, explosion or other accident or event involving any process, material, installation or activity covered by a permit or approval issued in connection with any of the provisions of this article.

Processing fee. There shall be a processing fee as stated in the schedule of fees and charges for the issuance of a fire regulatory permit. Rejected. If an application for a permit or approval is rejected by the fire marshal or his authorized representative, the applicant shall be advised of the reasons for such rejection.

Display of permits. A copy of the fire regulatory permit shall be posted or shall otherwise be readily accessible at each place a permitted activity is performed. As required by the fire marshal, a permit holder may be required to carry the permit.

Records. A record of fire regulatory permits issued and any exceptions granted shall be maintained in the fire prevention bureau. Such record shall be available for public inspection during normal business hours upon request to the fire marshal.
(h)
Duration of permits; limitations on issuance. ARTICLE IV. - OPEN BURNING

Sec. 38-121. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
(a)
A fire permit is issued by the fire department of the City of Fort Oglethorpe allowing for the outdoor burning of a limited nature during the burning period. Permits may be applied for Monday through Friday on the day of the proposed burning activity. A permit for burning activity on Saturday must be sought on the immediately preceding Friday.
(b)
A fire control device is an operable fire extinguisher or operating water hose attached to a functioning and open water hydrant.
(c)
A blue permit when issued by the fire office of the City of Fort Oglethorpe allows for the reduction of leaves on the premises on which they fall by the person in control of the premises, or for disposal of tree limbs from storm damage as outlined in the Rules for Air Quality Control, chapter 391-3-1 (5)(a), (1) and (7).
(d)
A white permit is required for burning on construction sites, in heavily populated areas, or other areas not outlined in the blue print. A white permit shall be issued without an inspection of the proposed burning area and material first being conducted by an authorized agent of the fire department of the City of Fort Oglethorpe, and payment of the inspection and issuance fee determined by the fire department of the City of Fort Oglethorpe.
(e)
Material or burning is any matter of a flammable nature or which is capable of being or becoming combustible.
(f)
Structure or dwelling is any man made building, whether for commercial, public or personal use, or other improvement upon real property.
(g)
Owner or occupant is the record owner or the person occupying the premises upon which burning is to take place under a lease or rental or other agreement for occupation. In the case of a corporation or other legal entity, "owner" or "occupant" shall include an authorized agent of the corporation or legal entity.
(h)
Burning period is the hours between 9:00 a.m. and 4:30 p.m. of the same day consisting of a total of 7½ hours per day and consisting of the same hourly framework whether eastern daylight time or eastern standard time is in effect; however, no burning period exists on Sunday.
(i)
Waste disposal service is the city garbage collection and disposal service or any public or private entity performing garbage collection and disposal within the incorporated limits of the City of Fort Oglethorpe.
(j)
Burning activity is any activity or action resulting in the combustion or burning of any material within the incorporated limits of the City of Fort Oglethorpe.
(k)
Exceptional circumstances are those state of facts wherein the need to burn material on a construction site, requires or creates a specific and articulable hardship on the applicant for a white permit as determined by the fire office of the City of Fort Oglethorpe.
(l)
Rules for Air Quality Control refers to chapter 391-3-01-02(5)(a), (b) revised September, 1995.
(Ord. No. 97-81, § 4-4-1, 1-26-1998; Ord. No. 2000-53, § 2, 12-11-2000; Ord. No. 2003-1, § 1, 3-24-2003)
Sec. 38-122. - Burning prohibited—Time, place and manner of prohibition; exceptions.
(a)
Except as otherwise provided in subsection (b) of this section, all burning within the incorporated limits of the City of Fort Oglethorpe is prohibited.
(b)
Burning will be allowed, within the incorporated limits of the City of Fort Oglethorpe, if:
(1)
The material to be burned cannot be removed by the waste disposal service employed within the incorporated limits of the City of Fort Oglethorpe;
(2)
There is no other reasonable method to dispose of the material;
(3)
The material is at least 50 feet from any structure; unless greater distance is imposed later in this code;
(4)
An owner or occupant of the premises upon which the material is to be burned is present with a functioning fire control device for the duration of the burning activity;
(5)
The material to be burned does not in any way consist of paper, cardboard, paperboard, treated wood, foam, lead paint, asbestos, rubber, plastics, tires, roofing materials (including shingles) sheetrock or gypsum board (or similar building materials), tar paper, petroleum products, or other material of a polluting nature; and
(6)
All burning must conform to "Rules for Air Quality Control"; reference O.C.G.A., § 391-3-1-02(5)(a), (b) revised September, 1995, as follows.
(a)
No person shall cause, suffer allow, or permit open burning in any area of the State except as follows:
1.
Reduction of leavers on the premises on which they fall by the person in control of the premises, unless prohibited by local ordinance and/or regulation;
2.
Carrying out recognized agricultural procedures necessary for production or harvesting of crops;
3.
Destruction of combustible demolition or construction materials either on site or transported to a burning facility upon approval by the director, unless prohibited by local ordinance and/or regulation;
4.
The burning over of any forest land by the owners of such land;
5.
For recreational purposes or cooking food for immediate human consumption;
6.
Fires set for purposes of training fire-fighting personnel when authorized by the appropriate governmental entity and the guidelines set forth by the director are strictly observed;
7.
Disposal of tree limbs from storm damage;
8.
For weed abatement, disease, and pest prevention;
9.
Operation of devices using open flames such as tar kettle, blow torches, welding torches, portable heaters and other flame-making equipment;
10.
Setting and maintenance by contractors and tradesmen of miscellaneous small fires necessary to such activities as streetpaving work installation or repair of utilities, provided that such fires are kept small in size, no smoke emissions exceeded 40 percent opacity, and that local ordinances and regulations do not prohibit such actions;
11.
Open burning in other than predominantly residential areas for the purpose of land clearing or construction or right-of-way maintenance proved the following condition are met:
(i)
Prevailing winds at the time of the burning are away from the major portion of the area's population;
(ii)
The location of the burning is at least 1,000 feet from any dwelling located in a predominately residential area;
(iii)
The amount of dirt on or in the material being burned is minimized;
(iv)
Heavy oils, asphaltic materials, items containing natural or synthetic rubber, or any material other than plant growth are not being burned;
(v)

ARTICLE V. - HAZARDOUS MATERIALS

Sec. 38-141. - Purpose.
To insure as far as possible, the safety of the citizens and personnel of the city from the possible harmful effects of hazardous materials, the following article is adopted as an amendment to the fire prevention and protection ordinance to insure that the quantities and types of hazardous chemicals and extremely hazardous substances will be known and regulated within the city to insure as far as possible the safety of the citizens of the city from the dangers of hazardous materials.
(Ord. No. 97-81, § 4-5-1, 1-26-1998)
Sec. 38-142. - Scope.
The following article is applicable to any facility that is required to prepare or have available material safety-data sheet(s) (MSDS) for hazardous chemicals under the Federal Occupational Safety and Health Act of 1970 and all regulations promulgated under that act, and any facility required to submit reports for extremely hazardous substances, as defined in 40 C.F.R. 355, Appendix A, and any facility owning, handling, transporting, storing or in any other manner or method dealing in class A, B and C explosives and blasting agents within the city.
(Ord. No. 97-81, § 4-5-2, 1-26-1998)
Sec. 38-143. - Reporting requirements.
(a)
The owner or operator of a facility subject to this article shall submit a material safety data sheet and any other required forms to the fire safety division, of the fire department.
(b)
Forms must be submitted on all hazardous chemicals present at the facility at any one time in amounts equal to or greater than 9,000 pounds (or 3,540 kilograms) and for all extremely hazardous substances present at the facility in an amount greater than or equal to 500 pounds (or 227 kilograms which is approximately 55 gallons) or the threshold planning quantity (TPQ), whichever is lower as defined in Title 40, Part 355, Appendix A of the Code of federal regulations, which provision is incorporated herein.
(1)
The owner or operator of a facility that is subject to this part shall submit the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) Tier 24 to the fire and safety division, of the fire department, on an annual basis beginning on September 1, 1998.
(2)
The owner and operator will also be required to submit a material safety data sheet on all hazardous chemicals as designated by Title 29 Code of Federal Regulations section 1910.120, which provisions are incorporated herein.
(3)
The term hazardous chemical does not apply to the following:
a.
Any food, food additive, color additive, or cosmetic regulated by the Food and Drug Administration.
b.
Any substance present as a solid in any manufactured item to the extent exposure to the substance does not occur under normal conditions of use.
c.
Any substance to the extent it is used for personal, family, or household purposes, or is present in the same form and concentration as a private package for distribution and used by the general public.
d.
Any substance to the extent it is used in a research laboratory or a hospital, or other medical facility under the direct supervision of a technically qualified individual.
(c)
The facility will be subject to unannounced inspections by the fire safety division of the fire department to insure compliance with the requirements of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 97-81, § 4-5-3, 1-26-1998)
Sec. 38-144. - Labeling requirements.
(a)
All locations within the city limits that contain hazardous materials must label all containers in conformity with current DOT guidelines.
(b)
The NFPA 704 system will also be required to be placed on a readily visible location of facilities to indicate hazardous materials. The above mentioned plaque must be securely mounted to the side of any truck, wall, construction trailer, etc. and be in a place at the location that is readily visible at all times; and be a minimum of at least 48 inches above ground level.
(Ord. No. 97-81, § 4-5-4, 1-26-1998)
Sec. 38-145. - Penalties.
(a)
MSDS reporting: Any person other than a government entity who violates any requirement of the article shall be in violation of this chapter.
(b)
Inventory reporting: Any person other than a governmental entity who violates any requirement shall be in violation of this chapter.
(c)
Continuing violations: Each day a violation described in paragraph (a) or (b) of this section continues shall constitute a separate violation.
(d)
Failure to permit: Any person that fails to obtain or maintain a required hazardous materials permit shall be in violation of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 97-81, § 4-5-5, 1-26-1998)
Sec. 38-146. - Explosives.
(a)
All class A, B and C explosives and blasting agents must be reported to the fire safety division. A permit will also be required for all explosives either stored or used within the city. A fee of $200.00 will be required of the entity involved with the above classes of explosives. The fee must be paid prior to issuance of the permit.
(b)
All units that store or contain explosives either mobile or fixed must have the proper DOT or NFPA plaques affixed to all four sides of the building or vehicle containing explosives.
(c)
Penalties.
(1)
Failure to report regulated explosives is a violation of this chapter.
(2)
Failure to obtain an explosive permit is a violation of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 97-81, § 4-5-6, 1-26-1998)
Sec. 38-147. - Penalties.
Any person who violates any of the provisions of this chapter, hereby adopted, or fails to comply therewith, shall for each and every violation and non-compliance respectively, be guilty of a misdemeanor as defined by the state. The imposition of one penalty for any violation shall not excuse the violation to continue. Penalties cannot exceed $1,000.00 or imprisonment of more than one year, or both, with the amount of fine/imprisonment being within the discretion of the municipal court of the city based upon the severity or repetition of the infraction.
(Ord. No. 97-81, § 4-5-8, 1-26-1998)
Sec. 38-148. - Jurisdiction of violation of this chapter.
As provided for by law, the municipal court of the city shall have the venue and jurisdiction of any citations issued under this chapter.
(Ord. No. 97-81, § 4-5-9, 1-26-1998)

ARTICLE VI. - FIRE LANE PROVISIONS

Sec. 38-150. - Purpose and scope.
It is the declared purpose of this chapter to provide for the designation and identification of fire lanes, for the manner, method and language for the posting of signs; to establish the penalty for violation of this chapter, to identify and designate the persons who have authority to enforce the chapter and the limits of such authority, to prohibit parking in the fire lanes; to provide for the method of submitting properties to this chapter, to repeal all ordinances in conflict herein; and for other purposes.
(Ord. No. 2001-17, § 1, 8-27-2001)
Sec. 38-151. - Definitions.
Enforcement officers shall refer to any duly authorized law enforcement officer employed by the city.
Fine lanes are areas designated by the fire official providing access for fire department vehicles to buildings, fire department connections and fire hydrants. This includes all alleys, driveways or lanes, devoted to public use, where the parking of motor vehicles or other obstructions can interfere with ingress or egress of fire department vehicles for the protection of person and properties including, but not limited to, shopping centers, theaters, hospitals, bowling lanes, churches, multi-family housing, and high-rise buildings.
Fire official means the fire officer or other designated authority, or their duly authorized representative, charged with the administration and enforcement of the fire prevention and protection ordinance.
Master plans means all original plans drawn in accordance with this chapter; all copies distributed shall be from this original drawing.
Need fire flow as defined by insurance services office fire suppression rating schedule.
New building for the purpose of this chapter shall mean any commercial or public facility whose plans were submitted after this amendment goes into effect.
Plats shall refer to maps created by the building owner and approved by the fire marshal or his designee which depict the location and boundaries of land and all existing fire lanes in accordance with this chapter. Such maps shall be drawn to scale.
Property owners shall refer to each person, firm, corporation, or entity possessing any estate, or leasehold right in the property being designated as fire lanes.
Ticket shall mean an order issued out of court by an enforcement officer of the city directing a violator of this chapter to comply with all civil fine set forth.
(Ord. No. 2001-17, § 1, 8-27-2001)
Sec. 38-152. - Identification.
(a)
Every existing building shall be accessible to fire department apparatus by way of access roadways with all-weather driving surfaces of not less than 20 feet (6.1 m) of unobstructed clear width, with adequate roadway turning radius capable to supporting the imposed loads of the fire apparatus and having a minimum vertical clearance of 13 feet 6 inches (4.1 m).
(1)
Where fire protection systems approved by the fire official are provided, the above required clearances may be modified by the fire official.
(2)
The fire official shall have the authority to require an increase in the minimum access widths where they are inadequate for fire or rescue operations.
(b)
Every new building shall be accessible to fire department apparatus by way of designated fire lanes with an all weather surface of not less than 20 feet (6.1 m) of unobstructed clear width. There shall be a minimum roadway turning radius of 35 feet (10.6 m). Three shall be a minimum vertical clearance of 13 feet 6 inches (4.1 m). The designated fire lanes shall be paved, purred concrete, or equivalent, capable of supporting the gross vehicle weight of fire apparatus.
(1)
Where fire protection systems approved by the fire official are provided, the above required clearances may be modified by the fire official.
(2)
The fire official shall have the authority to require an increase in the minimum access widths where they are inadequate for fire or rescue operations.
(c)
During construction, when combustibles are brought onto the site in such quantities as deemed hazardous by the fire official, access roads and a suitable water supply, acceptable to meet the required needed fire flow, shall be provided and maintained.
(d)
The areas designated as fire lanes hall have signs posted meeting the following criteria:
(1)
Signs shall read "No Parking Fire Lane", be constructed of a material to withstand the elements, such as metal, and lettering shall contrast against the background.
(2)
Letters shall not be less than two inches in height.
(3)
One sign shall be placed at the beginning of the fire lane and one at the end of the fire lane; each sign shall be erected not more than 50 feet apart.
(4)
Signs posted shall not be more than 4 feet from the edge of the curb and shall be visible from both driving directions, regardless of time of day, of the driving surfaces.
(e)
Painted lines, or equivalent, may be required by the fire official for that portion of the fire lane that is located in the parking areas, or is subject to blocking by parked vehicles. Such lines shall be yellow and clearly define an area 20 feet clear width from the building as no parking fire lane.
(f)
The fire official may require curbs of nonparking areas be painted red or other distinctive colors.
(Ord. No. 2001-17, § 1, 8-27-2001)
Sec. 38-153. - Approval; subsequent maintenance.
All fire lanes shall be approved by the fire official pursuant to these regulations, and thereafter these fire lanes shall be maintained by the property owner. Designated fire lanes or roads deemed necessary for fire department access by the fire official shall be maintained in a passable condition.
(a)
Private property owners or their representatives shall be responsible for keeping all fire lanes within the city's jurisdiction free of all obstructions.
(b)
Public property owners, the City of Fort Oglethorpe or their representatives shall be responsible for keeping all fire lanes within the city's jurisdiction free of obstructions.
(Ord. No. 2001-17, § 1, 8-27-2001)
Sec. 38-154. - Erection of signs.
No person or property owner shall place, erect or maintain any sign for control of parking or traffic which includes the words "Fire Lane" unless such location has been designated a fire lane by the fire official. Any failure to meet the applicable requirements specified in the City of Fort Oglethorpe Code shall be subject to a fine of $150.00 for each violation; provided, however, that the fine will be waived if the required specifications are made within 14 days he shall, on the 15 th day after receiving the citation, be subject to the $150.00 fine for each violation and an additional $10.00 fine for each violation for each day that the owner fails to comply with the provisions of this section until the area is properly designated and constructed. Each additional day shall be construed as an additional violation. All fines assessed under this section shall be paid into the treasury of the city.
(Ord. No. 2001-17, § 1, 8-27-2001)
Sec. 38-155. - Violations.
No person shall park any motor vehicle, including a motorcycle, in a fire lane; provided, however, this section shall not apply to the parking of an authorized emergency vehicle on official business.
(a)
Enforcement of this section shall be through a civil action municipal court by the issuance of a ticket which shall be either left with the vehicle or delivered to the person in possession thereof. For purposes of such civil action, it shall be presumed that the registered owner of said vehicle is in control or possession thereof.
(1)
The first violation of parking in a fire lane within any 30 day period shall be punished by a $50.00 civil fine; provided, however the second violation within any 30 day period shall be punishable by a civil fine of $80.00; and a civil fine of $100.00 may be levied for each violation thereafter occurring within any 30 day period.
(2)
The person receiving a ticket for violation of this section may pay the civil fine(s) by return mail to the municipal court of the city or his designated receive within 48 hours of the issuance of the ticket or may request a hearing within 48 hours to contest the issuance of the ticket. In the event the civil fine is not paid within 48 hours, the municipal court may issue a warrant for failure to appear requiring the offender to show cause why he failed to pay the fine within 48 hours, the municipal court may issue a citation for contempt requiring the offender to show cause why he failed to pay the fine within the time frame allowed by this section. Upon conviction thereof, the municipal court may impose a fine as provided by law.
(b)
Upon any person to whom a ticket has been issued under this section, a uniform traffic citation may be obtained with a hearing date thereon from the police officer or uniformed fire inspector whereupon it shall be returnable to the municipal court.
(Ord. No. 2001-17, § 1, 8-27-2001)
Sec. 38-156. - Enforcement authority.
A duly authorized law enforcement officer employed by the city and uniformed fire inspectors shall have the authority for enforcement of fire lanes.
(Ord. No. 2001-17, § 1, 8-27-2001)
Sec. 38-157. - Emergency authority.
In addition to all powers authorize by state law, as amended, in the event of any fire, explosion, bomb threat, or similar emergency, the fire department in the city shall be authorized to prevent the blocking of any public or private street, road or alley, way or driveway, or emergency lane, during any such emergency or remove any vehicles or obstructions necessary. The officers, members, agent or employees of the fire department of the city shall not be liable at law for any act or acts done while actually fighting a fire or performing duties as the scene of an emergency.
(Ord. No. 2001-17, § 1, 8-27-2001)
Sec. 38-158. - Immunity from liability from enforcement of chapter pertaining to maintenance and clearing.
The City of Fort Oglethorpe assumes no liability for any damages, injuries, or deaths resulting from enforcement or lack of enforcement, of the laws pertaining to maintenance and clearing of the fire lanes. The city has the authority to issue individual tickets or citations for fire lane violations, but the property owner has the ultimate responsibility to clear the fire lanes.
(Ord. No. 2001-17, § 1, 8-27-2001)
Sec. 38-159. - Enforceability of properly placed signs.
The disregard or disobedience of the instruction of any sign placed in accordance with the provisions of this chapter by the driver of a vehicle shall be deemed prima facie evidence of a violation of law, without requiring proof by whom and by what authority such sign has been erected.
(Ord. No. 2001-17, § 1, 8-27-2001)
Sec. 38-160. - Petition requesting application of chapter to properties.
Properties may become subject to this chapter by submitting a petition from the property owner of fully authorized agent of the property owner requesting this chapter to apply. The petition shall be submitted to the fire official and, upon approval, the property shall become subject to this chapter and its subsequent revisions.
(Ord. No. 2001-17, § 1, 8-27-2001)
Sec. 38-161. - Descriptions for fire officials.
Property which falls within the jurisdiction of the City of Fort Oglethorpe shall have all fire delineations visually depicted on a plat. These plats shall be designed by the owner and submitted to the City of Fort Oglethorpe Fire Department and copies shall be maintained. Each visual depiction shall identify all building exterior walls, traffic and parking lanes, and sidewalks. The area(s) to be designed as fire lane(s) shall be delineated in red ink. The plat shall state a scale of th measurement and shall be on paper or a series of pages of not less than eight and one-half inches by 11 inches. The plat identification shall specify the name of the property, a brief legal description of the property, and the length and width of the fire lane(s), as approved by the fire official, with whom all master plats will be filed.
(Ord. No. 2001-17, § 1, 8-27-2001)
