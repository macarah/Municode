ARTICLE II. - PLANNING COMMISSION[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
Cross reference— Administration, ch. 2.

Sec. 62-31. - Established.
(a)
Pursuant to Ga. Const. art. IX, § II, ¶ 4, which authorizes municipalities to adopt planning and zoning regulations within their jurisdiction and to establish a planning and zoning commission, the city council establishes by ordinance the city planning commission, referred to in this article as the planning commission, for the purpose of providing planned growth and development of the city because it appears to be advantageous to the welfare of the city that a comprehensive and continuous planning program be undertaken and the city council needs a city planning commission to assist in advising the city council to accomplish a coordinated and harmonious development of the city which will, in accordance with existing and future needs, best promote public health, safety, morals, order, convenience, prosperity and the general welfare, as well as efficiency and economy in the process of development.
(b)
The planning commission shall be governed by the provisions of this article.
(Ord. of 2-28-1994, § 1)
Sec. 62-32. - Membership.
The planning commission shall consist of five members. All members shall be residents of the city or the owner of a licensed business within the city or an employee of the Catoosa County Board of Education at the time of appointment. Members shall be appointed by the city council. The terms of the members shall be for four years. Except for purposes of reducing the number of planning commission members from seven to five, any vacancy in membership shall be filled for the unexpired term by the city council. The city council shall have the authority to remove any member for cause, on written charges, after a public hearing. All members shall serve without compensation but may be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in connection with their official duties.
(Ord. of 2-28-1994, § 2; Ord. No. 2018-02, 2-26-2018)
Sec. 62-33. - Organization, rules, staff and finances.
The planning commission shall elect its chairman from among its members. The term of the chairman shall be one year with eligibility for reelection. The commission shall appoint a secretary, who may be an officer or employee of the city. The planning commission shall make its own rules of procedure and determine its time of meeting. All meetings of the planning commission shall be open to the public, and all records of the commission shall be public records. The planning commission may make recommendations to the city council for appointment of such employees and staff as the planning commission may deem necessary for its work and may recommend the city council contract(s) with the state planning agency and other consultants for such services as the planning commission may require. The expenditures of the planning commission, exclusive of gifts, shall be within the amounts appropriated for the planning commission in the fiscal year budget. The majority of the planning commission shall constitute a quorum.
(Ord. of 2-28-1994, § 3; Ord. No. 2018-02, 2-26-2018)
Sec. 62-34. - Powers and duties.
From and after the time when the planning commission shall have organized and selected its officers, and shall have adopted its rules of procedure, its powers and duties are as set forth pursuant to Ga. Const. art. IX, § II, ¶ 4.
(Ord. of 2-28-1994, § 4)

ARTICLE III. - FIVE-YEAR WORK PLAN

Sec. 62-66. - Findings.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 50-7-8.2 and 12-2-8, the state department of community affairs has established minimum standards and procedures for coordinated and comprehensive planning.
(b)
The city has compiled, reviewed, modified and finalized a five-year short-term work program update to the City of Fort Oglethorpe Comprehensive Plan: 2010 (1991) in compliance with the state's minimum planning standards and procedures.
(Ord. No. 97-12, 4-14-1997)
Sec. 62-67. - Adopted.
The document entitled City of Fort Oglethorpe Five-Year Work Plan: 1996—2000 (December, 1995) is officially approved and adopted.
(Ord. No. 97-12, 4-14-1997)
