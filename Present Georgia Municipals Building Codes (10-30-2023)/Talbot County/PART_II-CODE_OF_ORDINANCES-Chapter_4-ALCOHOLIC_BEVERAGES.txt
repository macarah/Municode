Sec. 4-1. - Definitions.
Alcohol means the product of distillation of any liquid, whether rectified or diluted, whatever may be the origin thereof, and shall include synthetic ethyl alcohol.
Governing authority means the Board of County Commissioners of Talbot County, Georgia.
Person means and includes any individual, partnership, corporation or association.
Premises for the purpose of this chapter shall be limited to the physical extremities of the package store area.
Retailer or retail distributor mean any person, firm or corporation engaged in selling, at retail, any distilled spirits or alcohol, for beverage purposes, in unbroken packages.
Spirituous liquors or distilled spirits mean any alcoholic beverage containing alcohol obtained by distillation, mixed with water or other substance in solution, and includes brandy, rum, whisky, gin, cordials, or other spirituous liquors by whatever name called, to include fortified wines as defined by Federal Alcohol Administration, but nothing in this chapter shall prohibit the sale of wines from natural fermentation of fruits, berries, and other products. Hereinafter in this chapter the word "liquor" will be used to mean spirituous liquors, distilled spirits and alcoholic beverages as described in this section.
Wholesaler or wholesale distributor means any person, firm, or corporation engaged in distribution or selling to retailer for the purpose of resale, any of the distilled spirits or alcohol as defined in this chapter.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-2. - Legal age for liquor license.
No retail liquor license shall be granted to any person (as defined herein) unless such person is of legal age and has been a resident of the county, for a minimum of one year prior to filing an application for such license; same requirements for a corporation.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-3. - Liquor license limit per person.
Only three retail liquor licenses shall be issued to any one person in the county, and no person who holds a retail liquor license in any other county or municipality of the state, shall be eligible to receive a retail liquor license. Persons as used in this section shall include all members of such licensee's family and "family" shall be deemed to include any person related to the holder of such license within the first degree of consanguinity or affinity as [computed] according to the civil law.
(a)
No employee of the county, shall be eligible to receive a retail liquor license.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-4. - Location of premises.
No retail liquor license shall be granted to any person unless the entire business premises of the proposed location is situated beyond 200 yards from the property line of any church and beyond 200 yards from the property line of any school ground or college campus, the same to be measured by way of the nearest travelled road, street, or highway, as provided by law. Each application for retail liquor license shall include a scale drawing of the location of the proposed premises showing the distance to the nearest church or school or a certificate of a registered surveyor that such location complies with this chapter with reference to this section.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-5. - Separate license per business.
A separate retail liquor license shall be required for each place of business.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-6. - Building plans and specifications required.
No retail liquor license shall be issued to any person unless the building in which the business will be located is complete and detailed plans of said building and outside premises are attached to the application or unless proposed plans and specifications and a building permit of a proposed building to be built are attached to the application. The completed building or the proposed building shall comply with ordinances of the county, regulations of the state revenue commissioner and the laws of the state. The proposed building shall also be subject to final inspection and approval when completed, by the governing authority of the county. Each building in which the business will be located shall contain sufficient lighting so that the building itself and the premises on all sides of the building are readily visible at all times from the front of the street on which the building is located so as to reveal the inside retail area of the building and so as to reveal all of the outside premises of said building. Each applicant for a retail liquor license shall attach to his application evidence of ownership of the building or proposed building or a copy of the lease if the applicant is leasing the building. All establishments should carry the designation of "package store".
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-7. - Package requirements.
All licensed retailers shall sell or offer to sell in the original unbroken package only, and shall sell no packages containing less than required by state law.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-8. - Beverages for sale.
No retailer shall sell or offer for sale or display or keep in stock, at his place of business where liquor is offered for sale, any other product or commodity except the following:
(a)
Wines and beer when properly licensed; and
(b)
Beverages containing no alcohol commonly used to dilute distilled spirits.
No beverages of any kind may be opened or consumed in said place of business.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-9. - Deliveries.
No retailer shall sell or deliver any liquor to any person except in said retailer's place of business.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-10. - Days and hours of operation.
No retailer shall be in his place of business, or open his place of business, or furnish, sell or offer for sale any liquor at any of the following times:
(a)
At any time on Thanksgiving Day and Christmas Day; or
(b)
At any time on Sunday other than between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m. during which period sales shall be permitted; or
(c)
At any time in violation of a local Ordinance or regulation or a special order of the governing authority; or
(d)
At any time on any day during which any local, State primary, general or special election is being held in the county; or
(e)
On any day before 8:00 a.m. or after 11:45 p.m.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983; Ord. Of 7-6-2015)
Sec. 4-11. - Underage and/or intoxicated persons.
No retailer shall give, sell or offer to sell any liquor to any person who is under the age of 19 years, or who is noticeably intoxicated, or who is of unsound mind, or who is a habitual drunkard whose intemperate habits are known to said retailer. At any time a state driver' license shall be considered the only legal proof of age which would relieve a person making a sale from responsibility thereof.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-12. - Sale of liquor to minors prohibited.
The holder of a license to sell liquor shall post in the most conspicuous place in his establishment a sign printed in letters of at least four inches high reading as follows: "Sale of Liquor to Minors Strictly Prohibited."
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-13. - Storage of liquor.
No licensed retailer shall keep any liquor stored in any bonded or other type warehouse in the county, nor shall he enter into any type of arrangement whereby liquor ordered by him is stored for him by any licensed wholesaler. A retailer shall keep no inventory or stock of liquor at any place except his licensed place of business, and within his licensed place of business his storage space for liquor shall be immediately adjacent to the room in which he is licensed to do business.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-14. - Liquor license application.
Application for a retail liquor license shall be made on forms furnished by the governing authority as follows:
(a)
For the remainder of the year 1983, applications will be considered any time.
(b)
A retail liquor license shall be valid for 12 months form date of issue indicated thereon and no such license shall be renewed. A licensee who desires to continue in business subsequent to his license year must make a new application two months before the expiration of his current license. 1983 license shall expire December 31, 1983.
(c)
In addition to the completion and submission of application form as required by the county commissioners of the county, each applicant shall attach thereto a completed application form with all attachments and requirements for a state license.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-15. - Annual retail liquor license fee.
The annual fee for a retail liquor license shall be $5,000.00 per annum or any part thereof with no refund, and shall be paid prior to the issuance of any license, said fee to accompany the application and shall be either in cash or a bank certified check.
(a)
A retail liquor license for the remainder of the year 1983 shall be $3,315.40. Said fee shall accompany the application and shall be either cash or bank certified check.
(b)
In addition to the annual retail liquor license fee required there is hereby levied an excise tax computed at the rate of 22¢ per liter which shall be paid to the governing authority on all liquors sold, displayed or stored in said county. Said tax shall be paid to the governing authority by the wholesale distributor on all liquors sold to retailers in the county as follows: Each wholesale distributor selling, shipping, or in any way delivering liquor to any retailer, shall collect the excise tax at the time of delivery and shall remit the same together with a summary of all deliveries to each retailer on or before the 20th day of the month following. The 22¢ per liter shall be pro-rated down on ½ pints, pints, fifths, half gallons, and etc. and wine gallons so that each bottle or each individual size container shall be taxed on the basis of 22¢ per liter. It shall be unlawful and a violation of this chapter for any wholesale distributor to sell, ship or deliver in any manner any liquor to a retailer without having collected said tax. It shall be unlawful and a violation of this chapter for any retailer to possess, own, ho store, display or sell any liquor on which such tax has not been paid.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-16. - Eligibility requirements—Generally.
(a)
An applicant must be at least 21 years of age, of good moral character, a citizen of the United States and a resident of the county; provided, however, that residency by an applicant within the county shall not be a requirement if the applicant designates a resident of the county who shall be responsible for any matter relating to the license.
(b)
An applicant shall not have been convicted within the past:
(1)
Ten years of any felony.
(2)
Two years of any misdemeanor, to include misdemeanor violations of county ordinances, with the exception of traffic offenses; provided however, that any traffic offenses involving the use or consumption of drugs, alcohol or other controlled substances, or an offense resulting in automatic suspension or revocation of driving privileges or licenses shall be included as a misdemeanor requiring a two-year waiting period before being eligible for a license under this article. This section shall apply with respect to the laws of this state, other states, the United States and other countries. A plea of nolo contendere or the forfeiture of a bond shall be considered a conviction for purposes of this subsection. The governing authority may waive the conviction of a misdemeanor at its discretion for purposes of this subsection if it is determined that the misdemeanor does not have a bearing on the applicant's fitness for a license.
(c)
An applicant shall not have been denied or had revoked, within the three years next preceding his application, any license to sell alcoholic beverages issued by any governmental entity.
(d)
An applicant shall be the owner of the premises to be licensed or the holder of a lease thereon for the period to be covered by the license.
(e)
Any person having two convictions for selling alcohol to a minor within a three-year period shall not be eligible for a license.
(f)
The governing authority, in its discretion, may consider any other circumstances which reflect favorably or unfavorably upon an applicant or proposed location of the business, and shall have the authority to deny any applicant a license upon a finding that it would be contrary to the public interest and welfare to do so.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983; Ord. of 11-24-2009, § 1)
Sec. 4-17. - Applications presented in person.
All applications for a retail liquor license shall be presented in person.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-18. - Transferability of license prohibited.
No retail liquor license shall be transferable or assignable to any person or other location, and in the event that a licensed business is sold or closed, it shall be the duty of the licensee to immediately surrender his license to the governing authority.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-19. - Terms of license.
All holders of a license for the sale of liquor shall keep a copy of this chapter in his premises and shall instruct any person working there with respect to the terms hereof and each licensee or his agents selling liquor shall at all times be familiar with the terms hereof. The licensee shall be held responsible for any acts of his employees in any violation of this chapter or of the laws of the state or the rules and regulations of the state revenue commissioner.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-20. - Inspections.
The business premises of the holder of a retail liquor license for the sale of liquor shall be open to inspection at any and all times by officers or officials authorized to conduct such inspections.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-21. - False statements; revocation of license.
The making of any statement on an application for license to sell liquor which shall be later found to be false shall constitute ground for revocation of said license.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-22. - Age limits of employees.
No person under the age of 18 years shall be employed in any capacity in any place of business licensed under this article.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983; Ord. of 11-24-2009, § 2)
Sec. 4-23. - Gambling, betting, etc.
There shall be no gambling, betting, games of chance, punchboard vending machines, slot machines, pin-ball machines, lotteries or tickets or chances therein or the operation of any schemes for hazarding money or any other things of value in any licensed retailer's place of business.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-24. - Display of license.
Licenses under this chapter shall be displayed prominently at all times on the premises for which same was issued.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-25. - Violation of terms of license.
No person shall engage in the sale of liquor in the county without first complying with the rules and regulations set out in this chapter. Any person violating the rules and regulations set out in this chapter shall be subject to revocation of his license and punishment as prescribed by the governing authority. In addition to the rules and regulations hereinabove set out, each licensee doing business in the county under this chapter shall comply with all laws of the state, federal laws, and rules and regulations of the state revenue commissioner relating to the sale and distributing of distilled spirits in Georgia, and any violation of same shall subject said licensee to immediate suspension or revocation of his retail liquor license and shall also subject said licensee to criminal prosecution by the proper authority as provided by law and the ordinance of the county.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-26. - Annual wholesale liquor license fee.
The annual fee for a wholesale liquor license shall be $1,500.00 per annum.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
Sec. 4-27. - Repeal of conflicting provisions.
All ordinances and parts of ordinances in conflict herewith are hereby expressly repealed.
(Ord. of 5-3-1983)
