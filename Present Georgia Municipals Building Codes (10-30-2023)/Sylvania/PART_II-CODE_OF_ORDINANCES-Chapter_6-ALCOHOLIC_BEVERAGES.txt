ARTICLE I. - DEFINITIONS AND PURPOSE

Sec. 6-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcohol means ethyl alcohol, hydrated oxide of ethyl, or spirits of wine, from whatever source or by whatever process produced.
Alcoholic beverage means and includes all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, wine, or fortified wine.
Chain grocery store means a grocery store with gross annual sales exceeding $1,000,000.00 in this city, or projected annual sales at the location for which the license is sought exceeding $1,000,000.00 based upon the operation of another of the chain's stores of similar size in a municipality with a population of 6,000 persons or less.
Food caterer means any person who prepares food for consumption off the premises.
Grocery store means a business offering for retail sale to the public grocery and related items excluding gasoline, motor oil, cigarettes and prepared food cooked or heated on the licensed premises (including, but not limited to, hot dogs, hamburgers, pizza, fried chicken, sandwiches and bakery items).
Importer means any person who imports an alcoholic beverage into this state from a foreign country and sells the alcoholic beverage to another importer, broker, or wholesaler and who maintains a stock of the alcoholic beverage.
Licensed alcoholic beverage caterer means any retail dealer licensed pursuant to article II of this chapter.
Licensee means the applicant for or holder of a license to sell alcoholic beverages in the city.
Malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than six percent alcohol by volume and including ale, porter, brown, stout, larger beer, small beer and strong beer. The term does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Manufacturer means any maker, producer or bottler of an alcoholic beverage. The term also means, in the case of malt beverages, any brewer.
Package means a bottle, can, keg, barrel or other original consumer container.
Package licensee means the applicant for or holder of a license for the retail sale of malt beverages and/or wine by the package.
Person means any individual, company, corporation, association, partnership, or other legal entity.
Person having an ownership interest in the licensee means any natural person, partnership, limited partnership, limited liability company, corporation, or other legal entity, who possesses a 25-percent or greater ownership interest in the licensee.
Person having the primary responsibility for managing the business means a person actively involved in the day-to-day operations of the business located on the licensed premises, and who has authority to prescribe any/or enforce rules, regulations and guidelines prescribed by the licensee for employees engaging in the sale or dispensing of malt beverages and/or wine on the licensed premises, and who has the final authority to discipline any employee violating such rules, regulations and guidelines.
Person holding license on behalf of licensee means a natural person that holds the license on behalf of a sole proprietor, a corporation, a partnership, a limited liability company, or other legal entity.
Pouring licensee means the applicant for or holder of a license for the retail sale of malt beverages and/or wine by the drink.
Purchase of a going business means the sale by a licensee to a separate person or entity of substantially all of the stock in trade of the licensed business at the licensed premises, including the good will of the licensed business, and including the continued use of the trade name, together with an agreement by the seller not to use the same or confusingly similar trade name in the county. However, a purchase shall not be considered a "purchase of a going business" unless the business continues to be operated under the same trade name and at the same location for a period of 12 months subsequent to the date of purchase.
Restaurant means any public place kept, used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public as a place where meals are served and where meals are actually and regularly served, such place being provided with adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining room equipment, air conditioned, having employed therein a sufficient number and kind of employees to prepare, cook, and serve suitable food for its guests. At least one meal per day shall be served on at least three days per week, with the exception of holidays, vacations, and period of redecoration, and the serving of such meals shall be the principal business conducted, with the service of alcoholic beverages to be consumed on the premises as only incidental thereto. A restaurant shall provide at least 24 seats for its customers, and shall derive at least 50 percent of its gross revenue from the sale of food prepared on the premises. A restaurant must also have a valid food service establishment permit as required by O.C.G.A. § 26-2-371.
Wholesaler or wholesale dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages to other wholesale dealers, to retail dealers or to retail consumption dealers.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products. The term does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this section.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22; Ord. No. 511, § 1, 8-16-22)
Sec. 6-2. - Purpose.
This chapter has been enacted in accordance with a plan designed for the purposes, among others, of promoting the health and general welfare of the community; to establish reasonable standards for the regulation and control of the licensing, sale and dispensing of alcoholic beverages, to protect and preserve the schools; to give effect to existing land use; to protect those of minor age in the community; and to preserve reasonable and orderly land use, with reasonable considerations, among others, to the character of the areas and their peculiar suitability for particular uses, the congestion in the roads and streets, and with a general view of promoting desirable living conditions and sustaining the stability of neighborhoods and property value ; to protect against the evils of concentration of outlets for malt beverages and wine in one person or entity or to prevent undesirable persons or entities from controlling the malt beverage and wine industry in the city; and to ensure that only grocery stores are licensed to sell malt beverages and wine at retail in the city as an incidental part of their business and to protect against the evils of beer and wine stores per se; to insure that only restaurants (and not bars per se) are authorized to sell malt beverages and/or wine by the drink; and to provide for the licensing of wholesale dealers and importers with physical locations in the city.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
ARTICLE II. - RETAIL LICENSING

Sec. 6-3. - License required.
It shall be lawful to sell malt beverages and wine at retail by the package in the city only when granted a license by the city. Additionally, it shall be lawful to sell malt beverages and wine at retail by the drink only when granted a license by the city.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-4. - Retail licensee qualifications—General requirements for individual licensees and persons holding a license on behalf of a sole proprietor, corporation or partnership.
All individual retail licensees and/or persons holding a license on behalf of a sole proprietor, a corporation, a limited liability company or a partnership shall meet the following qualifications:
(1)
Such person shall be a resident of the United States.
(2)
Such person shall be of good moral character and shall not have been convicted of or pleaded nolo contendere to any felony, any violation of controlled substance laws, any violation of law regulating the sale of alcoholic beverages, or any violation of law prohibiting operation of a motor vehicle while under the influence of alcohol or drugs, in any state of the United States, within the five years immediately preceding the filing of the application.
(3)
Such person shall not have had his license to sell alcoholic beverages of any kind revoked for cause by any city, county or state of the United States within five years immediately preceding the filing of the application.
(4)
Such person shall be the owner of the premises, or the holder of a valid written lease thereon, or the person with a primary responsibility for managing the business to be operated on the premises.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-5. - Same—Sole proprietor.
The person having the primary responsibility for managing the business to be operated on the licensed premises, or the owner of the premises, or the holder of a valid lease thereon shall be the holder of the license on behalf of a sole proprietorship, and shall possess all of the qualification set forth in section 6-4.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-6. - Same—Corporation.
The person having the primary responsibility for managing the business to he operated on the licensed premises, or the holder of the majority of shares in the corporation shall be the holder of the license on behalf of the corporation, and shall possess all of the qualifications set forth in section 6-4.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-7. - Same—Partnerships.
The person having the primary responsibility for managing the business to be operated on the licensed premises, or a general partner, shall be the holder of the license on behalf of the partnership, and shall possess all of the qualifications set forth in section 6-4.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-8. - Limited liability companies.
The person having the primary responsibility for managing the business to be operated on the licensed premises, or, if the LLC entity has a manager, such manager, or if the LLC entity has no manager, a member of the LLC, shall be the holder of the license on behalf of the LLC, and shall possess all the qualifications set forth in section 6-4.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-8.1. - Change of person holding license.
In the event there is a change in the person holding the license for an entity licensed by the city to sell alcoholic beverages, and said entity timely files with the city manager the information regarding the change as required by subsection 6-13(b), the new person who shall be the "person holding the license on behalf of licensee" shall be given a period of 90 days to meet the qualifications under this chapter before such entity's license is cancelled, suspended, or revoked.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-9. - Same—Persons having an ownership interest in licensee.
Any person having an ownership interest in the licensee:
(1)
Shall not have been convicted of or pleaded nolo contendere to any felony, any violation of controlled substances laws, any violation of law regulating the sale of alcoholic beverages, or any violation of law prohibiting operation of a motor vehicle while under the influence of alcohol or drugs in any state of the United States within the five years immediately preceding the filing of the application.
(2)
Shall not have had an ownership interest of 25 percent or greater in any entity which has had its license to sell alcoholic beverages of any kind revoked for cause by any city, county or state of the United States within five years immediately preceding the filing of the application.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-10. - Grocery sales for package sales of beer and wine.
(a)
General qualifications. A package licensee must prove that it is a grocery store.
(b)
Making application. The licensee must prove that it has experienced grocery sales of at least $25,000.00 during the three months immediately preceding the filing of the application at the licensed premises or the premises to be licensed, excluding the sale of gasoline, motor oil, cigarettes and prepared food cooked or heated on the licensed premises (including, but not limited to, hot dogs, hamburgers, pizza, fried chicken, sandwiches and bakery items). Such proof shall be made by providing copies of sales tax returns and cancelled checks or other evidence of payment of sales tax together with an affidavit from the licensee or the person who shall hold the license on behalf of the licensee, swearing or affirming under oath that the premises has experienced grocery sales of at least $25,000.00 excluding the sale of gasoline, motor oil, cigarettes and prepared food cooked or heated on the licensed premises (including, but not limited to, hot dogs, hamburgers, pizza, fried chicken, sandwiches and bakery items). The mayor and council may, in their discretion, require an audited financial statement by a certified public accountant providing for a detailed analysis of sales, categorizing sales of gasoline, motor oil, cigarettes, and prepared food cooked or heated on the licensed premises (including, but not limited to, hot dogs, hamburgers, pizza, fried chicken, sandwiches and bakery items). The cost of such audited financial statement shall be borne by the licensee.
(c)
Checks and audits after issuance. The licensee is subject to subsequent checks and audits of its books and records after issuance of the license by the city clerk or his or her designated agent during regular business hours, the cost of which shall be borne by the city. The licensee shall maintain grocery sales of at least $50,000.00, excluding the sale of gasoline, motor oil, cigarettes and prepared food cooked or heated on the licensed premises (including, but not limited to, hot dogs, hamburgers, pizza, fried chicken, sandwiches and bakery items), during the 12-month period immediately preceding such licensee's application for renewal. If the license has been issued for a period of less than 12 months immediately preceding the filing of the application for renewal by such licensee, the $50,000.00 grocery sales requirement shall be prorated for the period in which such licensee has held a license immediately preceding the filing of the application for renewal.
(d)
Checks and audits prior to renewal. The licensee must prove that it has experienced grocery sales of at least $50,000.00 for the period immediately preceding the filing of the application as set forth in subsection (c) above. Such proof shall be made by providing copies of sales tax returns and cancelled checks or other evidence of payment of sales tax together with an affidavit from the licensee or the person who holds the license on behalf of the licensee, swearing or affirming under oath that the premises has experienced grocery sales of at least $50,000.00 during the 12-month period immediately preceding such licensee's application for renewal (or in an amount prorated for the period in which such licensee has held a license immediately preceding the filing of the application for renewal) excluding the sale of gasoline, motor oil, cigarettes and prepared food cooked or heated on the licensed premises (including, but not limited to, hot dogs, hamburgers, pizza, fried chicken, sandwiches and bakery items). The mayor and council may, in its discretion, and upon reasonable suspicion that the licensee has not experienced the requisite grocery sales, require an audited financial statement by a certified public accountant, providing for a detailed analysis of sales, categorizing gasoline, motor oil, cigarettes, and prepared food cooked or heated on the licensed premises (including, but not limited to, hot dogs, hamburgers, pizza, fried chicken, sandwiches and bakery items), the cost of which shall be borne by the licensee.
(e)
Exceptions, chain grocery stores; purchase of going business.
(1)
Chain grocery stores. The provision of this section requiring an applicant to prove grocery sales of at least $25,000.00 during the three months immediately preceding the filing of the application shall not, at the option of any chain grocery store applicant, apply to chain grocery stores. If such applicant's sales do not equal or exceed $250,000.00 for the first three months of operation subsequent to the issuance of a malt beverage and wine license, such license shall be revoked and the applicant must reapply for a malt beverage and wine license, which application shall not be considered for a period of three months subsequent to revocation under this section. The holder of the license on behalf of the applicant must furnish the city clerk sales tax returns and cancelled checks or other evidence of payment of sales tax for its first three months of operation within 30 days of the end of the three-month period in order to demonstrate requisite grocery sales, together with an affidavit from the person holding a license on behalf of the licensee that the subject chain grocery store has experienced grocery sales of at least $250,000.00 during the three-month period. Additionally, the mayor and council may, in its discretion, require an audited financial statement providing for a detailed analysis of sales, categorizing sales of gasoline, motor oil, cigarettes and prepared food cooked or heated on the licensed premises (including, but not limited to, hot dogs, hamburgers, pizza, fried chicken, sandwiches and bakery items). The cost of such audited financial statement shall be borne by the licensee.
(2)
Purchase of going business. The provision of this section requiring an applicant to prove grocery sales of at least $25,000.00 during the three months immediately preceding the filing of the application shall not, at the option of the purchaser of a going business holding a valid malt beverage and wine license in the city, apply to such applicant. If such applicant's sales do not equal or exceed $25,000.00 for the first three months of operation subsequent to the issuance of a malt beverage and wine license, such license shall be revoked and the applicant must reapply for a malt beverage and wine license, which application shall not be considered for a period of three months subsequent to revocation under this section. The holder of the license on behalf of the applicant must furnish the city clerk sales tax returns and cancelled checks or other evidence of payments of sales tax for its first three months of operation within 30 days of the end of the three-month period in order. to demonstrate requisite grocery sales, together with an affidavit from the person holding a license on behalf of the licensee that the subject grocery store has experienced grocery sales of at least $25,000.00 during the three-month period. Additionally, the mayor and council may, in its discretion, require an audited financial statement providing for a detailed analysis of sales, categorizing sales of gasoline, motor oil, cigarettes and prepared food cooked or heated on the licensed premises (including, but not limited to, hot dogs, hamburgers, pizza, fried chicken, sandwiches and bakery items). The cost of such audited financial statement shall be borne by the licensee.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-11. - Pouring license—Restaurant.
(a)
General qualifications. A pouring licensee must prove that it is a restaurant.
(b)
Making application. The licensee must prove that it meets or shall meet before commencing business the definition of a restaurant as provided in this chapter, except for the percentage of food sales, which must be subsequently proven as hereinafter set forth.
(c)
Proof of food sales after issuance.
(1)
Initial proof. The licensee must prove that at least 50 percent of its gross revenue for the first three months that it began selling beer and/or wine by the drink was derived from the sale of food prepared on the licensed premises, or such license shall be immediately revoked. Such proof as hereinafter set forth shall be provided to the city clerk within four months of the issuance of the license. Such proof shall be made by providing copies of sales tax returns, cancelled checks or other evidence of payment of sales tax; records of the licensee providing proof of the dollar amount from sales of alcoholic beverages; and an affidavit from the licensee or the person who holds the license on behalf of the licensee, swearing or affirming on oath that 50 percent of the gross revenue for the first three months that the licensee began selling alcoholic beverages by the drink was derived from the sale of food prepared on the licensed premises. The mayor and council may, in its discretion, require an audited financial statement by a certified public accountant providing for a detailed analysis of the sources of the gross revenue. The cost of such audited financial statement shall be borne by the licensee.
(2)
Checks and audits after issuance. The licensee is subject to subsequent checks and audits of its books and records after issuance of the license by the city clerk or his designated agent during regular business hours, the cost of which shall be borne by the city.
(3)
Checks and audits prior to renewal. The licensee must prove that at least 50 percent of its gross revenue is derived from food prepared on the licensed premises during the 12 months immediately preceding such licensee's application for renewal (or, if licensed for less than 12 months, for the period that the license has been held). The mayor and council may, in its discretion, and upon reasonable suspicion that the licensee has not met the requisite food sales for such period, require an audited financial statement by a certified public accountant, providing for a detailed analysis of the sources of gross revenue. The cost of such audited financial statement shall be borne by the licensee.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-12. - Sale of malt beverages or wine near churches, schools.
No license to sell malt beverages and wine shall be granted at a location within 100 yards of any school building, school grounds, or college campus. As used in this article the term "school building" shall apply only to state, county, city, or church school buildings and to such buildings at such other schools in which are taught subjects commonly taught in the common schools and colleges of this state and which are public schools or private schools as defined in O.C.G.A. § 20-2-690(b). The 100-yard distance shall be measured along the most direct legal route of travel on the ground and from building to building at any part thereof. However, this provision shall not apply at any location for which a license has been issued prior to the adoption of the ordinance from which this section derives, nor at any location for which a new license is applied for if the sale of malt beverages and wine was lawful at such location at any time during the 12 months immediately preceding application for a malt beverage and wine license. Provided, however, the within and foregoing 100-yard distance requirement shall be reduced to 50 feet with respect to locations within the Sylvania Downtown Central Business District as shown, for purposes of this chapter, on the map attached to Ord. No. 508, as exhibit "A", for which a pouring license for either malt beverages, wine, or both, has been issued and is in effect.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-13. - Application forms; contents; change in information.
(a)
All applications for licenses under this article shall be made on forms furnished the applicant by the city clerk and shall indicate the following:
(1)
The names and addresses of all persons having an ownership interest in the business applying for the license to sell malt beverages and wine, together with the interest each person or any member of his immediate family has in any other business license to sell malt beverages and wine in the city.
(2)
The ownership of the land and building where the business is to be operated, together with a copy or copies of deeds to and leases thereon.
(3)
Whether the licensee, the person who shall hold the license on behalf of the licensee, or any person having an ownership interest in the licensee, has been convicted of or pleaded nolo contendere to any felony, any violation of controlled substances laws, any violation of law regulating the sale of alcoholic beverages, or any violation of law prohibiting operation of a motor vehicle while under the influence of alcohol or drugs, in any state of the United States within the five years immediately preceding the filing of the application.
(4)
Whether the licensee, the person who shall hold the license on behalf of the licensee or any person having an ownership interest in the licensee has had his license to sell alcoholic beverages of any kind revoked for cause by any city, county or state of the United States within five years immediately preceding the filing of the application.
(5)
The name of the licensee (business entity), and the name, address and telephone number of the person who shall hold the license on behalf of the business entity, if different.
(6)
A set of fingerprints of persons having an ownership interest in the licensee as well as the person holding the license on behalf of the licensee, which fingerprints must be processed through the Georgia Crime Information Center (GCIC) and the National Crime Information Center (NCIC).
(7)
Photographs of the person managing the business, together persons with an ownership interest primarily responsible for with photograph is of all in the business.
(8)
Any other information called for by the mayor and council.
(b)
Any change in any relationship declared in the application with regard to persons interested in the ownership of the business, or in the relationship between the person holding the license on behalf of the business and the business itself, must be filed with the city manager when the change is made. The failure to so file within a period of 30 days after the change is made shall be grounds for cancellation, revocation or suspension of the license by the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-14. - Application fee.
A fee in the amount of $130.00 shall be paid to the city at the time of the filing of the application under this article to defray costs and expenses in connection with the filing of the application and the investigation of the applicant and of the facts contained in the application.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-15. - Public hearing on application.
All applications for licenses shall be acted upon by the mayor and council after a public hearing and advertisement thereof in at least two consecutive issues of the legal organ of the city, which notice shall contain a particular description of the location of the proposed business and shall give the name of the applicant, and the name of the person who shall hold the license on behalf of the applicant, if different, and if a partnership, the names of all general and limited partners, and if a corporation or limited liability company, the names of the person who shall have primary responsibility for managing the business together with the names of the owners having at least 25 percent of the shares of stock each in such corporation, together with the names of the officers and board of directors, and if a limited liability company, the names of the owners of at least 25 percent interests each in such limited liability company. The public hearing shall be held not sooner than ten days nor more than 20 days subsequent to the date of the first advertisement.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-16. - Issuance of license.
When the malt beverage and/or wine license has been approved by the mayor and council and the applicant has deposited with the city manager the required application fee, the required alcohol license tax, the required occupation tax, and any other fees and taxes due the city by the applicant, the applicable license or licenses will be issued. The license, when issued, shall authorize the licensee to conduct the retail sale of either malt beverages, wine, or both.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-17. - Term of license.
Licenses issued under this article shall have a term beginning January 1 of the year of issuance and shall terminate on December 31 of the year of issuance at midnight. Licenses issued after January 1 of any given year shall be valid only through December 31 of that year at midnight, and such licensee shall pay all fees and taxes owed for a full year. In the event of revocation, surrender or suspension of the license prior to the expiration of the term, the holder shall not be entitled to receive any refund of any fees or taxes whatsoever.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-18. - Transferability of license.
(a)
Generally. Licenses under this article shall not be transferable except as provided in this section.
(b)
Sole proprietor. If the holder of this license is the sole proprietor of the business, the license may, in the discretion of the mayor and council, be transferred to the personal representative of his estate or to a member of his immediate family upon the sole proprietor's death, provided that such person meets all of the qualifications set forth in section 6-5. If the holder of the license on behalf of the sole proprietor is not the owner of the business, but rather is the person having the primary responsibility for managing the business to be operated on the premises, and such person ceases to be the person with such primary responsibility, the license may, in the discretion of the mayor and council, be transferred to a person meeting all qualifications set forth in section 6-5.
(c)
Corporations. If the holder of the license on behalf of a corporation ceases to be either the person having the primary responsibility for managing the business to be operated on the premises, or the holder of the majority of shares in the corporation, subsequent to the issuance of the license, the license may, in the discretion of the mayor and council, be transferred to a person meeting all qualifications set forth in section 6-6.
(d)
Partnerships. If the holder of the license on behalf of a partnership ceases to be either the person having primary responsibility for managing the business to be operated on the premises, or a general partner in the business, subsequent to the issuance of the license, the license may, in the discretion of the mayor and council, be transferred to a person meeting all qualifications set forth in section 6-7.
(e)
Limited liability companies. If the holder of the license on behalf of a limited liability company ceases to be either the person having primary responsibility for managing the business to be operated on the premises, or the manager or a member of the LLC, subsequent to the issuance of the license, the license may, in the discretion of the mayor and council, be transferred to a person meeting all of the qualifications set forth in section 6-8.
(f)
Sale of licensed premises. No license shall be transferred from one business entity to another upon the sale or lease of the licensed premises. However, this subsection shall not apply to a sole proprietor or to a partnership which continues its business on the licensed premises in the form of a corporation or limited liability company after issuance of the license provided that the sole proprietor remains the owner of 100 percent of the shares/interests in the corporation or limited liability company, or in the case of a partnership, as long as the same partners remain the owner of 100 percent of the shares/interests in the corporation or limited liability company.
Provided further, if there is a transfer in ownership of the licensee, but the manager for a new applicant remains the same as the former licensee, and that manager was the holder of the license on behalf of the former licensee, and shall be the holder of the license on behalf of the new applicant, the license may, in the discretion of the mayor and council, be transferred to the new applicant for a period of 90 days commencing with the date of the sale or transfer in ownership, in order for the new applicant to have the time needed to complete the application process for a license for the new applicant.
(g)
Transfer of location. The holder of a valid license issued as provided in this chapter may request that the license be transferred from the licensed premises to a new location of the licensed business, which license may be transferred upon approval by the mayor and council.
Sec. 6-19. - Renewal of license.
(a)
Renewal process. All holders of retail malt beverage and wine licenses in the city shall apply for renewal thereof for the subsequent year between November 1 of the current year for which the licensee holds a valid license and November 30 of such year. In the application for renewal, the licensee shall state any changes in information that was provided on the licensee's original application to the city for a malt beverage and/or wine license. A package licensee shall also provide proof of grocery sales as provided for in subsection 6-10(d). A pouring licensee shall also provide proof of sales of prepared foods on the licensed premises as provided for in subsection 6-11(c)(3). All malt beverage and wine licenses shall be deemed automatically renewed for the subsequent year unless the mayor and council deny renewal thereof on or before December 31 of the current year. There shall be no application fee for renewal of an existing license. If an application for renewal is not filed within the requisite time limits set forth herein, such licensee shall begin anew the application process as though the licensee had never held a malt beverage and/or wine license in the city. All applications for renewal shall be accompanied by payment of the malt beverage and/or wine license tax, which amount shall be returned if renewal of such license is denied.
(b)
Change in information. In the event there has been a change in any person having an ownership interest in the licensee, or in the person holding the license on behalf of the licensee, a set of fingerprints of such new person or persons shall be made and processed through the Georgia Crime Information Center (GCIC) and the National Crime Information Center (NCIC). If any such person has been convicted of or pleaded nolo contendere to any felony, any violation of controlled substances laws, any violation of law regulating the sale of alcoholic beverages, or any violation of law prohibiting the operation of a motor vehicle while under the influence of alcohol or drugs, in any state of the United States within five years immediately preceding the filing of the application for, renewal, said license shall not be renewed, or if already renewed, shall be revoked.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-20. - Allowing or permitting of breaking of packages or drinking of contents thereon on premises.
No holder of a retail license which authorizes the sale of malt beverages and/or wine by the package in the city, nor any agents or employees of the licensee, shall knowingly and intentionally allow or permit the breaking of any package or packages containing alcoholic beverages on the premises where sold or allow or permit the drinking of the contents of such package or packages on the premises where sold.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-21. - Prohibited acts on licensed premises.
No holder of a license which authorizes the sale of malt beverages and/or wine at retail in the city by the package or by the drink, nor any agents or employees of the licensee, shall do any of the following upon licensed premises:
(1)
Sell malt beverages or wine to any person while this person is in an intoxicated condition;
(2)
Permit on the licensed premises any disorderly conduct or breach of the peace;
(3)
Sell, offer for sale, or possess on the licensed premises any kind of alcoholic liquor, the sale of or possession of which is not authorized under or this chapter;
(4)
Sell malt beverages or wine, either retail or wholesale, within 100 yards of any school building, school grounds, or college campus, except that such distance requirements shall be reduced to 50 feet with respect to locations within the Sylvania Downtown Central business district shown, for purposes of this chapter, on the map attached to Ord. No. 508, as exhibit "A", for which a pouring license for either malt beverages, wine, or both, has been issued.
(5)
Sell malt beverages or wine to persons under the legal drinking age as provided by the laws of the state.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-22. - Proper identification required for purchase.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or for any person to purchase malt beverages or wine in the city unless the purchaser exhibits proper identification to the seller. For purposes of this chapter, the term "proper identification" means any document issued by a governmental agency containing a description of the person, such person's photograph, or both, and giving such person's date of birth and includes without being limited to a passport, military identification card or driver's license. "Proper identification" shall not include a birth certificate. This section applies to all persona regardless of age, and regardless of whether such person appears to be of proper and lawful age to purchase malt beverages or wine.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-23. - Wholesalers; license required for delivery.
Properly licensed wholesale dealers are authorized to make sales or deliveries with respect to retailers, only to retailers properly licensed under this chapter for the operation of establishments in the city. Deliveries shall be made in a conveyance owned, leased and operated by a wholesaler licensed under this chapter, and hie license or permit shall at all times when deliveries are made in the city be in the conveyance making these deliveries and shall be subject all times to inspection by all duly authorized authorities of the city. Under no circumstances shall any wholesale dealer sell or deliver any distilled spirits to any establishment in the incorporated areas of the city.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-24. - Retailers: licensee required to accept delivery.
The retail dealers in malt beverages and wine, licensed under this chapter shall not buy or accept deliveries of malt beverages or wine from wholesalers or other persons offering them for sale except from wholesalers duly authorized under this chapter. These retailers shall not accept delivery of malt beverages or wine except directly to the premises for which their license and permit was issued, and by no other means than a conveyance owned or leased and operated by a wholesaler properly licensed to make deliveries of malt beverages and wine in the city.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-25. - Beverages must be sold on licensed premises.
All licensed premises shall be referred the malt beverages and wine in the possession of a retailer shall be stored and sold in and upon the for which the license was issued, which premises described in the application for licenses hereinabove to, and at no other place.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-26. - Hours of sale regulated.
Except as hereinafter set forth in this section, it shall be unlawful for any person to sell, give away or offer for sale, or in any other manner dispense or deliver any malt beverage or wine within the corporate limits of the city during the hours of 12:00 midnight on each Saturday and until 7:00 a.m. on each Monday. Provided, however, package licensees are authorized to sell and offer for sale malt beverages and wine by the package within the corporate limits of the city on Sundays between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and 11:30 p.m.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-27. - Public consumption.
It shall be unlawful for any person to drink or consume any malt beverage or wine on or upon the public streets, roads or alleys in the city, or any public park or playground, or on any other public property.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-28. - Prohibition against consumption on premises.
Persons obtaining retail licenses under this article for the sale of malt beverages and wine by the package shall not allow the consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises of their respective places of business.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-29. - Brown bagging prohibited.
It is prohibited for any person to bring in his own malt beverage or wine in any restaurant in the city.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)

(a)
No package licensee under this article shall advertise the sale of malt beverages or wine at his establishment by any sign or similar device on the exterior of the building in which his business is located, or at any other exterior location on the lot or parcel of land on which his building is located, or on any other tract or parcel of land with n the city limits. Within the building in which his business is located, such licensee may display any number of signs, but such signs shall not be so prominently visible from outside such building as to defeat the intent of the restriction on exterior advertisement.

Signs which are located in a window of a building in which a package licensee's business is located and all lighted signs, wherever located, shall be considered to be so prominently visible from outside such building as to defeat the intent of the restriction on exterior advertising.

There shall be no prohibition against the sale and purchase of malt beverages or wine on election days.

There is hereby imposed upon each licensed wholesale dealer making deliveries to retail licensees located within the city an excise tax, in addition to the excise taxes levied by the state, as follows:
(1)
Malt beverages.

Sec. 6-36. - License required.
(a)
It shall be lawful to sell alcoholic beverages at wholesale in the city when granted a license by the city. The license requirement shall apply only to wholesale dealers with a physical business location in the city. Wholesale dealers with one or more physical business locations outside of the city, who possess required licenses from the State of Georgia, are not required to obtain a license from the city, and are allowed to dis tribute alcoholic beverages within the city provided that such wholesale dealers otherwise comply with federal, state and local law and regulations.
(b)
It shall be lawful for importers having a physical business location within the city to import alcoholic beverages from foreign countries, and sell the same to another importer, broker, or wholesaler who maintains a stock of such alcoholic beverages.

Sec. 6-37. - Wholesale/import licensee qualifications—General requirements for individual licensees and persons holding a license on behalf of a sole proprietor, corporation, LLC or partnership.
All individual licensees and/or persons holding a license on behalf of a sole proprietor, a corporation, an LLC, or a partnership shall meet the following qualifications:
(1)
Such person shall be a resident of the United States.
(2)
Such person shall be of good moral character and shall not have been convicted of or pleaded nolo contendere to any felony, any violation of controlled substance laws, any violation of law regulating the sale of alcoholic beverages, or any violation of law prohibiting operation of a motor vehicle while under the influence of alcohol or drugs, in any state of the United States, within the five years immediately preceding the filing of the application.
(3)

(4)
Such person shall be the owner of the premises, or the holder of a valid written lease thereon, or the person with a primary responsibility for managing the business to be operated on the premises.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)

Sec. 6-39. - Same—Corporation.
The person having the primary responsibility for managing the business to be operated on the licensed premises, or the holder of the majority of shares in the corporation shall be the holder of the license on behalf of the corporation, and shall possess all of the qualifications set forth in section 6-37.

Sec. 6-40. - Same—LLCs.
The person having the primary responsibility for managing the business to be operated on the licensed premises, or, if the LLC entity has a manager, such manager, or if the LLC entity has no manager, a member of the LLC, shall be the holder of the license on behalf of the LLC, and shall possess all the qualifications set forth in section 6-37.

Sec. 6-41. - Same—Partnerships.
The person having the primary responsibility for managing the business to he operated on the licensed premises, or a general partner, shall be the holder of the license on behalf of the partnership, and shall possess all of the qualifications set forth in section 6-37.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)

Shall not have been convicted of or pleaded nolo contendere to any felony, any violation of controlled substances laws, any violation of law regulating the sale of alcoholic beverages, or any violation of law prohibiting operation of a motor vehicle while under the influence of alcohol or drugs in any state of the United States within the five years immediately preceding the filing of the application.

ARTICLE IV. - SUSPENSION AND REVOCATION

Sec. 6-49. - Suspension and revocation—For due cause.
(a)
Grounds. The mayor and council may suspend, revoke or deny renewal of any alcoholic beverage license issued under this chapter for due cause. For purposes of this section, due cause is defined as follows:
(1)
The conviction of or a plea of nolo contendere by the licensee or any employee or agent of the licensee to a violation of any federal, state, county or municipal law or ordinance relating to the sale of alcoholic beverages, which violation occurred on the licensed premises.
(2)
The conviction of or a plea of nolo contendere by any person having an ownership interest in the business located on the licensed premises to any felony, any violation of controlled substances laws, any violation of law regulating the sale of alcoholic beverages, or any violation of law prohibiting operation of a motor vehicle while under the influence of alcohol or drugs in any state of the United States subsequent to the date of issuance of the license.
(3)
The revocation for cause by any city, county or state of the United States subsequent to the date of the issuance of the license with respect to an entity that a person having an ownership interest in the business located on the licensed premises as defined herein held an ownership interest in any such entity equaling or exceeding 25 percent.
(4)
The failure to provide any change in information set forth in the licensee's original or renewal application for a malt beverage and wine license within 30 days after the date of such change.
(b)
Hearing.
(1)
Probable cause suspension by mayor. The mayor may, upon probable cause that there is due cause to suspend or revoke a malt beverage and wine license, immediately suspend any license issued under this chapter after an informal hearing with the licensee or the holder of the license on behalf of the licensee. Such hearing shall be held at City Hall between 8:30 a.m. and 9:00 p.m. on any day, Sunday through Saturday, upon two hours' notice, verbal or written, by the Sylvania City Council or its designated agent, to the licensee or the person holding the license on behalf of the licensee. If the licensee or the person holding the license on behalf of the licensee fails to appear at the hearing, or if neither can be notified after reasonable effort, the mayor may, in his discretion, suspend any such license without hearing.
(2)
Hearing before council. A hearing before the full mayor and council shall then be held after written notice to such licensee, or the person holding such license on behalf of the licensee, not sooner than two days nor later than ten days subsequent to the date of suspension. Such licensee shall be given at least two days' written notice of the hearing. All decisions of the mayor and council regarding the suspension or revocation of licenses issued under this chapter shall be in writing and the reasons therefor stated, and shall be mailed or delivered to the licensee.
(c)
Notice requirements where no probable cause suspension. In all other cases where there is no probable cause suspension by the Sylvania City Council, a licensee against whom suspension or revocation proceedings are being commenced shall be given at least five days' notice of a hearing before the mayor and council, which notice shall be in writing, and shall be mailed or delivered to such licensee or the person holding such license on behalf of the licensee. All decisions of the mayor and council regarding the suspension or revocation of licenses issued hereunder shall be in writing and the reasons therefore stated, and shall be mailed or delivered to the licensee.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-50. - Suspension and revocation—Violation of ordinance or state law.
(a)
Grounds. The mayor and council may suspend, revoke or deny renewal of any license issued under this chapter for a violation by the licensee or by any employee or agent of the licensee of any provision of this ordinance relating to the licensing and sale of alcoholic beverages, or any amendment thereto, or any provisions of Georgia law relating to the licensing and sale of alcoholic beverages, or any amendment thereto. It shall not be a prerequisite to proceeding under this provision that the licensee or any employee or agent of the licensee be convicted or plead nolo contendere in a court of law to such violation.
(b)
Hearing.
(1)
Probable cause suspension by mayor. The mayor, upon probable cause that a violation of this chapter has occurred, or that there has been a violation of Georgia law relating to the licensing and sale of alcoholic beverages, immediately suspend any license issued under this chapter after an informal hearing with the licensee or the holder of the license on behalf of the licensee. Such hearing shall be held at City Hall between 8:30 a.m. and 9:00 p.m. on any day, Sunday through Saturday, upon two hours' notice, verbal or written, by the mayor or his designated agent, to the licensee or the person holding the license on behalf of the licensee. If the licensee or the person holding the license on behalf of the licensee fails to appear at the hearing, or if neither can be notified after reasonable effort, the mayor may, in his discretion, suspend any such license without hearing.
(2)
Hearing before council. A hearing before the full mayor and council shall then ba held after written notice to such licensee, or the person holding such license on behalf of the licensee, not sooner than two days nor later than ten days subsequent to the date of suspension. Such licensee shall be given at least two days' written notice of the hearing. All decisions of the mayor and council regarding the suspension or revocation of licenses issued under this chapter shall be in writing and the reasons therefor stated, and shall be mailed or delivered to the licensee.
(c)
Notice requirements where no probable cause suspension. In all other cases where there is no probable cause suspension by the mayor, a licensee against whom suspension or revocation proceedings are being commenced shall be given at least five days' notice of a hearing before the mayor and council, which notice shall be in writing, and shall be mailed or delivered to such licensee or the person holding such license on behalf of the licensee. All decisions of the mayor and council regarding the suspension or revocation of licenses issued hereunder shall be in writing and the reasons therefore stated, and shall be mailed or delivered to the licensee.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-51. - Conduct of hearings.
At all hearings before the full council on the issue of suspension, revocation, or the denial of the renewal of any malt beverage and wine license, the licensee shall be afforded an opportunity to present evidence and to cross-examine opposing witnesses. Strict rules of evidence shall not apply. A majority vote of the council members present shall carry any motion. The mayor shall vote only in the case of a tie.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-52. - Revocation, suspension and other penalties, revocation or suspension.
If a majority of the council present at the hearing finds by a preponderance of the evidence that there are grounds for revoking or suspending a license, revocation of such license is authorized. The mayor and council, may, in its discretion, upon consideration of mitigating factors, impose a lesser penalty of suspension for a specific period of time.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-53. - Minimum suspensions for underage sales.
In cases of sales of malt beverages or wine to persons under the legal drinking age in violation of state law and/or this chapter, the following minimum suspension periods shall apply for any given calendar year:
(1)
First violation (during current calendar year): 15 days.
(2)
Second violation (during current calendar year): 30 days.
(3)
Third violation (during current calendar year): Revocation.
In determining the number of violations under this subsection, only violations which occur on or after January 1, 2001, shall be considered. Nothing contained herein shall preclude the mayor and council from completely revoking a license or from suspending any license for a period of time in addition to the minimum periods set forth in this subsection.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-54. - Additional penalty.
Any violation of the provisions of this chapter shall be punishable as provided in section 1-12 of the Code of Ordinances of the City of Sylvania.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-55. - Effective date.
This chapter shall become effective upon adoption.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-56. - Repealer.
It is the purpose and intent to completely replace the current Chapter 6 of the Code of Ordinances of the City of Sylvania dealing with alcoholic beverages, sections 6-1—6-39, inclusive, as amended. The entire chapter 6, sections 6-1—6-39, inclusive, shall stand repealed upon this new ordinance becoming effective, except that any violations that occurred prior to the effective date of repeal shall continue to be violations of and prosecuted under the ordinance being repealed.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)

In the event any part or parts of the ordinance codified in this chapter are determined by any court of competent jurisdiction to be unconstitutional or unlawful, each and every offending part shall be deemed stricken herefrom, and the remaining part or parts of this chapter shall remain fully effective and enforceable, and shall be construed to carry out the purposes and intents of the ordinance codified in this chapter.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)

ARTICLE V. - CATERERS

Sec. 6-70. - Alcoholic beverage license required.
(a)
License required. Any alcoholic beverage caterer who possesses a valid license from the city or another city or county in Georgia and possesses a valid license from the State of Georgia to sell malt beverages or wine by the drink and/or by the package at a fixed location may be issued by the city an event permit authorizing such licensed alcoholic beverage caterer to sell malt beverages or wine by the drink off premises and in connection with an authorized catered function.
(b)
A licensed authorized alcoholic beverage caterer may only sell that which is authorized by such caterer's alcohol beverage license. For example, if the alcoholic beverage caterer possesses a valid license to sell malt beverages, such caterer may only sell malt beverages at the authorized catered event or function.
(c)
An alcoholic beverage caterer shall submit an application for an off-premises event permit to the city clerk.
(d)
It is unlawful for any person to engage in, carry on or conduct the sale or distribution of alcoholic beverages off premises and in connection with a catered event or function without first having obtained a permit as provided herein.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-71. - Food caterer.
Any food caterer, as defined herein, operating within the city shall apply for an alcoholic beverage caterer's license in order to distribute or sell any malt beverages or wine off the premises of the food caterer's business. This does not affect any requirements that the food caterer have a license to sell alcoholic beverages on the premises of the food caterer's business.
(Ord. No. 508, 3-1-22)
Sec. 6-72. - Event permit and application—Fee.
(a)
Each alcoholic beverage caterer wishing to sell or distribute malt beverages or wine at an authorized catered event or function within the city must apply for an event permit. The application for the event shall include the name of the caterer, the date, address, time and name of the event, the caterer's alcohol license number, and the quantity of alcohol beverages to be transported from the licensee's primary location to the location of the authorized catered event or function. When the catered function is domiciled in the city, and the city is not the governing authority which issues the alcoholic beverage caterer's license, there shall be charged an event permit fee of $50.00 and there shall furthermore be a levy of local excise taxes on the total quantity of alcoholic beverages brought into the city by the caterer.
(b)
The original event permit shall be kept in the vehicle transporting the alcoholic beverages to the catered event or function, and shall be available for inspection at the catered event or function during the duration of such event or function.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for a licensed alcoholic beverage caterer to distribute or sell alcoholic beverages off premises except as authorized by the event permit.
(d)
The event permit will be valid for the length of the authorized event or function or as approved by the city. After expiration of the event, the alcoholic beverage caterer shall apply for a new event permit in the same manner as the original permit.
(e)
The event permit required by the caterer is an addition to any special events permit that may be required by a producer under the city's special events permit set forth in article III of chapter 62 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 511, § 2, 8-16-22)
Sec. 6-73. - License tax.
There shall be a license tax levied on all licensed alcoholic beverage caterers in the amount of $100.00 per year, which tax shall be due and payable upon the filing of the application for the license. If the license is not issued in connection with such application, the license tax shall be returned.
(Ord. No. 511, § 2, 8-16-22)
Sec. 6-74. - Duration and renewal of licenses.
Any license issued to any licensed alcoholic beverage caterer shall automatically expire December 31 of the year of its issuance.
Renewal: In the case of renewal, the license tax shall be due and payable at the time application is made for renewal, which amount shall be returned if renewal is denied.
(Ord. No. 511, § 2, 8-16-22)
Sec. 6-75. - Effective date.
This chapter shall become effective upon adoption of the ordinance codified herein.
(Ord. No. 511, § 2, 8-16-22)
Sec. 6-76. - Severability.
In the event any part or parts of the ordinance codified in this chapter are determined by any court of competent jurisdiction to be unconstitutional or unlawful, each and every offending part shall be deemed stricken herefrom, and the remaining part or parts of the ordinance shall remain fully effective and enforceable, and shall be construed to carry out the purposes and intents of the ordinance.
(Ord. No. 511, § 2, 8-16-22)
