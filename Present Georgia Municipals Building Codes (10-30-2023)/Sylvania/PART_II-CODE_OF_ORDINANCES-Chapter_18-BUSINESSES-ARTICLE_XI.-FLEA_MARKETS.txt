Sec. 18-261. - Purpose and intent.
It is the purpose and intent of this article to:
(1)
Serve and protect the health, safety and welfare of the general public through the regulation of flea markets;
(2)
To establish a uniform set of rules and regulations which are fair and equitable;
(3)
To provide economic development opportunities for small entrepreneurs in the city;
(4)
To provide a variety of goods and services for sale;
(5)
Promote stable vendors who will enrich the city's ambience and be assets to public security;
(6)
Ensure that persons vending on private property have the permission of the property owner prior to providing goods and services to the public;
(7)
Ensure that persons vending on private property do not impede on public vending spaces.
(Ord. No. 431, § 1, 9-20-05)
Sec. 18-262. - Definition of flea market.
Flea market means any event at which two or more vendors offer merchandise for sale, and at which a fee is charged to vendors for the privilege of offering or displaying merchandise for sale or exchange, or at which a fee is charged to prospective buyers for admission to the area where merchandise is offered or displayed for sale or exchange.
The term "flea market" shall not include and shall not apply to yard sales which are regulated at article VII.
(Ord. No. 431, § 2, 9-20-05)
Sec. 18-263. - C-2 zones only.
Flea markets shall be permitted in C-2 zones only.
(Ord. No. 431, § 3, 9-20-05)
Sec. 18-264. - Conducted under covered area.
All vending tables, booths and displays shall be operated within a covered or enclosed area, which area shall contain at a minimum a cement or concrete floor, and a shingle or metal roof having a minimum roof pitch of two × 12, which structure shall be subject to the design regulations set forth at appendix B, article 14.
(Ord. No. 431, § 4, 9-20-05)
Sec. 18-265. - Assistant vendors.
No vendor shall have more than two assistant vendors.
(Ord. No. 431, § 5, 9-20-05)
Sec. 18-266. - Forty-eight consecutive hours.
No vending tables, booths, displays or other vending apparatus shall be placed on the licensed premises for more than 48 consecutive hours.
(Ord. No. 431, § 6, 9-20-05)
Sec. 18-267. - Occupation tax certificate—Owner/operator.
The flea market owner/operator (as applicable) shall at all times that the flea market is in operation prominently display a valid occupation tax certificate.
(Ord. No. 431, § 7, 9-20-05)
Sec. 18-268. - Occupation tax certificate—Vendor.
Each vendor shall at all times that such vendor is vending on the licensed premises prominently display a valid occupation tax certificate.
(Ord. No. 431, § 8, 9-20-05)
Sec. 18-269. - Regulatory license fee.
The flea market owner/operator (as applicable) shall, in addition to the occupation tax, pay an annual regulatory license fee in the amount of $75.00.
(Ord. No. 431, § 9, 9-20-05)
Sec. 18-270. - Restroom facilities.
Each flea market shall have available to vendors and patrons permanent adequate restroom facilities at the licensed premises, which facilities shall comply with the applicable standard codes set forth at section 14-1.
(Ord. No. 431, § 10, 9-20-05)
Sec. 18-271. - Special use exception.
Flea markets shall be subject to the special use exceptions set forth in appendix B, section 8-3013-1. With respect to the offstreet parking regulations contained in said article, flea markets shall have three spaces for each vendor permitted to sell or display merchandise on the licensed premises at any given time.
(Ord. No. 431, § 11, 9-20-05)
Secs. 18-272—18-279. - Reserved.
