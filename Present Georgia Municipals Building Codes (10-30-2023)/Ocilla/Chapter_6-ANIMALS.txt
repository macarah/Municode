ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 6-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Animal means any dog or cat or other domesticated animal that is kept as a pet within the City limits.
Exposed to rabies means any animal that has been bitten or exposed to any other animal known or suspected to have been infested with rabies.
Free-running line means a line which is attached to or secured by a swivel or other device which will prevent the line from being wrapped around some object.
Humanely destroyed means the injection by a licensed veterinarian of a euthanasia drug.
Inoculated means an animal which has been administered an antirabic vaccine by a licensed veterinarian of the State.
Kennel means a person engaged in the commercial business of buying, selling or boarding animals.
Neutered means any animal which has been castrated or otherwise operated upon to prevent fertilization as evidenced by a certificate from a licensed veterinarian.
Owner means any person, agent of a person or family member of a person owning, keeping or harboring an animal.
Spayed means any female animal which has been operated upon to prevent conception as evidenced by a certificate from a licensed veterinarian.
(Code 2003, § 10-5001; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-2. - Vicious and destructive animals.
It shall be unlawful for any owner of a dog to allow said animal to habitually and repeatedly chase, snap at, attack, or bark at pedestrians, other domestic animals, bicyclists or vehicles, or to allow an animal to turn over garbage cans, to damage flowers, or to conduct itself so as to be a nuisance.
(Code 2003, § 10-5002; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
State Law reference— Responsible Dog Ownership Law, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-20 et seq.
Sec. 6-3. - Animals prohibited to run at large.
It shall be unlawful for any person to permit an animal under his care, custody or control to run at large. An animal shall not be considered at large when located on the property of the person having care, custody or control of the animal or when the animal is on a leash that is not more than six feet in length and under the control of a person capable of controlling the animal.
(Code 2003, § 10-5003; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002; Ord. No. 2009-1 , 1-6-2009)
Sec. 6-4. - Notification of injured animal.
It shall be unlawful for any person knowingly injuring an animal by running over or into same or contacting same with an automobile, motorcycle, bicycle or other vehicle to fail to immediately notify the owner of said animal or the Police Department or the Animal Control Officer.
(Code 2003, § 10-5004; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-5. - Animals which attack persons.
Any animal which has ever bitten any person other than the members of the owner's immediate family so as to break the skin, to the knowledge of the said owner, except in defense of property or person, must be kept in a secure enclosure, muzzled or accompanied by an adult person who by means of leash shall have such animal firmly under control at all times. It shall be unlawful to keep an animal in violation of this section.
(Code 2003, § 10-5005; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-6. - Molesting, teasing and baiting animals.
It shall be unlawful for any person to tease, molest, bait or in any way bother any animal not belonging to said person or legally under the person's control.
(Code 2003, § 10-5006; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-7. - Humane treatment.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any owner of an animal not to furnish for such animal adequate protection from the elements.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to treat any animal in a cruel and inhumane manner, to abandon it, or to release it upon public or private property with the intention of abandoning it.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to secure an animal by a leash less than ten feet in length which is not free running as herein defined.
(d)
No person except the owner or the owners authorized agent shall kill any animal unless necessary for the protection of persons property. Any person killing an animal shall do so in a humane manner.
(Code 2003, § 10-5007; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
State Law reference— Cruelty to animals, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-4.
Sec. 6-8. - Interference.
It shall be unlawful for any person to resist, interfere with, hinder or molest the Animal Control Officer or any of the employees under said person or the health officer in the performance of the duties imposed upon them by this article or by State law.
(Code 2003, § 10-5008; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-9. - Records.
The Animal Control Officer shall cause all notice forms to be serially numbered and shall cause the records with respect to said notice forms and the disposition of the same to be maintained in such a manner that all such forms shall be capable of being accounted for.
(Code 2003, § 10-5009; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-10. - Public disturbance by animals prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any owner of an animal to allow said animal to commit any act by which may reasonably be expected to prevent or disrupt a lawful meeting, gathering or procession on public property, including streets, schools, parks, picnic areas, playgrounds and athletic fields.
(Code 2003, § 10-5010; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-11. - Anti-rabies vaccination.
(a)
The owners and keepers of dogs and cats in the City are hereby required to have such dogs and cats vaccinated against rabies by a licensed veterinarian. No person shall own, keep or harbor any dog or cat over three months of age within the City unless such dog or cat is vaccinated.
(b)
All dogs and cats shall be vaccinated against rabies by a licensed veterinarian, in accordance with the latest Compendium of Animal Rabies Vaccines and Recommendations for Immunizations, published by the National Association of State Public Health Veterinarians.
(c)
A certificate of vaccination shall be issued to the owner of each animal vaccinated.
(d)
Each owner shall also receive a durable vaccination tag indicating the year for which the certificate was issued. The tag shall be securely attached to the collar and worn continuously by the dog or cat to which it has been issued. Tags are not transferable.
(Code 2003, § 10-5013; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Secs. 6-12—6-40. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - IMPOUNDMENT

Sec. 6-41. - Authority.
It shall be the duty of the Animal Control Officer to seize and impound, subject to the provisions of this article, all animals, whether domesticated or undomesticated, found in violation of the provisions of this chapter within the City, whether such animal shall be in the immediate presence of its owner or custodian or otherwise.
(Code 2003, § 10-5014; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-42. - Citation alternative.
In the alternative to impounding an animal under the provisions of this article, the owner of such animal shall be cited to appear in the City Court to answer charges for the violation of the provisions of this chapter; provided, however, such citation shall not preclude the impoundment of the animal.
(Code 2003, § 10-5015; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-43. - Right of entry.
The Animal Control Officers are hereby authorized to enter upon any unfenced lot, tract or parcel of land for the purpose of seizing and impounding any animal found thereon in violation of this article.
(Code 2003, § 10-5016; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-44. - Notice.
Not later than two days after any animal has been impounded, notice shall be given to the owner or if the owner is unknown, then notice thereof shall be posted, for three days or until the dog is disposed of, on a bulletin board at the animal control shelter or at City Hall provided for this purpose with a description of the animal and the time and place of taking, together with the time and date of posting the notice. The time for redemption of the animal shall not begin until such notice has been given or posted.
(Code 2003, § 10-5017; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-45. - Right to redeem.
The owner of any animal which has been impounded under the provisions of this article shall have the right to redeem the same upon the payment of any and all fees which may be due and payable for the impoundment of such animal; provided, however, the payment of such impoundment fees shall not bar the imposition of any fine which may be imposed for the violation of this chapter.
(Code 2003, § 10-5018; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-46. - Time for redemption.
All impounded animals shall be redeemed within three days after the posting of the notice of impoundment. Any such animal not redeemed within such time may be sold, destroyed or otherwise disposed of.
(Code 2003, § 10-5019; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-47. - Redemption of unvaccinated animals.
The owner of any impounded domesticated animal which has not been licensed and vaccinated as required by this chapter cannot redeem his animal until such animal has been licensed and vaccinated. After these requirements have been met, the owner may redeem his animal by making a deposit of $10.00 in addition to the required redemption fees.
(Code 2003, § 10-5020; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-48. - Redemption by person other than owner.
If the owner of any animal impounded under this article shall fail to redeem his animal within the time allowed for redemption, any other person may, upon complying with all provisions of this article, including payment of the redemption fee, redeem the animal from the pound and be the lawful owner of the animal thereafter.
(Code 2003, § 10-5021; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-49. - Disposition of funds.
All moneys received and collected under the provisions of this article shall be deposited in the general fund of the City.
(Code 2003, § 10-5022; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Secs. 6-50—6-71. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - RABIES CONTROL[2]

--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Control of rabies, O.C.G.A. § 31-19-1 et seq.

Sec. 6-72. - Quarantine of suspected animal.
Any animal which bites or scratches a person, or which otherwise gives symptoms of having rabies, shall be immediately reported to the Animal Control Officer and thereupon be securely quarantined at the direction of the Animal Control Officer, for a period of 12 days, and it shall not be released from such quarantine except by permission of the Animal Control Officer or under the Officer's direction. At the discretion of the Animal Control Officer, such quarantine may be on the premises of the owner, at a shelter designated as the City Pound or, at the owner's option and expenses, in a veterinary hospital of his choice. In the case of stray animals, or in the cases of animals whose ownership is not known, such quarantine shall be at the shelter designated as the City Pound.
(Code 2003, § 10-5023; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-73. - Duty of owner to surrender animal; redemption.
The owner upon demand by the Animal Control Officer shall forthwith surrender any animal which has bitten a human, or which is suspected as having been exposed to rabies, for supervised quarantine, the expense of which shall be borne by the owner. Said animal may be reclaimed by the owner if it is adjudged free of rabies, upon payment of fees determined by the Mayor and City Council from time to time.
(Code 2003, § 10-5024; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-74. - Disposition of dead rabid animal.
When rabies has been diagnosed in an animal under quarantine or rabies suspected by a licensed veterinarian and the animal dies while under such observation, the Animal Control Officer shall immediately send the head of such animal to the State Health Department of Pathological Examination and shall notify the proper public health officer of reports of human contacts and the diagnosis.
(Code 2003, § 10-5025; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-75. - Areawide quarantine.
(a)
When imposed. When the report indicates a positive diagnosis of rabies, the Animal Control Officer may order an areawide quarantine for a period of 60 days and upon invoking such quarantine, no pet animal shall be taken into the streets or permitted to be in the streets, during such period of quarantine. During such quarantine, no animal may be taken or shipped from the City without permission of the Animal Control Officer.
(b)
Vaccination. During the areawide quarantine period, and as long afterward as the Animal Control Officer decides it is necessary to prevent the spread of rabies, the Animal Control Officer may require that all dogs, three months old or older, shall be vaccinated against rabies. All vaccinated dogs shall be restricted to (leashing or confinement in an on-premises enclosure) for 30 days following vaccination. During the quarantine period, the Animal Control Officer is empowered to provide a program of mass immunization by the establishment of temporary canine rabies vaccination clinics strategically located through the area of the health jurisdiction.
(c)
Redemption of strays restricted. No animal which has been impounded by reason of its being a stray, unclaimed by its owner, shall be adopted from any animal shelter during the period of rabies emergency quarantine, except by special authorization of the Animal Control Officer.
(Code 2003, § 10-5026; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-76. - Destruction, isolation of bitten animals.
Animals bitten by a known rabid animal shall be immediately destroyed, or if the owner is unwilling to destroy the exposed animal, strict isolation of the animal in a kennel, designated by the Animal Control Officer, for six months shall be enforced. If the animal has been previously vaccinated, within the time limits established by the public health service based on the kind of vaccination used, re-vaccination and restraint (leashing and confinement) for 30 days shall be carried out.
(Code 2003, § 10-5027; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-77. - Extension of quarantine.
In the event there are additional cases of rabies occurring during the period of areawide quarantine, such period of quarantine may be extended for an additional period of time.
(Code 2003, § 10-5028; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-78. - Destruction, removal of rabid animals.
No person shall kill, or cause to be killed, any rabid animal, any animal suspected of having been exposed to rabies, or any animal biting a human, except as herein provided, nor remove such animal from the City limits without written permission from the Animal Control Officer.
(Code 2003, § 10-5029; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-79. - Surrender of carcass.
The carcass of any dead animal exposed to rabies shall, upon demand, be surrendered to the Animal Control Officer.
(Code 2003, § 10-5030; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-80. - Report of bite cases.
It shall be the duty of every physician, or other medical practitioner, to report to the Animal Control Officer the names and addresses of persons treated for bites inflicted by animals, together with other such information as will be helpful in rabies control.
(Code 2003, § 10-5031; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-81. - Failure to obey.
No person shall fail or refuse to surrender any animal for quarantine or destruction as required herein when demand is made therefor by the Animal Control Officer.
(Code 2003, § 10-5032; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-82. - Report of suspected cases.
It shall be the duty of every licensed veterinarian to report to the Animal Control Officer any animal considered by the veterinarian to be a rabies suspect.
(Code 2003, § 10-5033; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
Sec. 6-83. - Exemptions.
Hospitals, clinics and other premises operated by licensed veterinarians for the care and treatment of animals are exempt from the provisions of this article, except where specifically stated.
(Code 2003, § 10-5034; Ord. No. 2002-8, 10-8-2002)
