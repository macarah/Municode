Sec. 70-2. - Authority to designate crosswalks, safety zones, and mark traffic lanes.
The chief of police is hereby authorized to:
(1)
Designate and maintain, by appropriate devices, marks, or lines upon the surface of the roadway, crosswalks at intersections, where in such officer's opinion there is particular danger to pedestrians crossing the roadway, and at such other places as such officer may deem necessary.
(2)
Establish safety zones of such kind and character and at such places as such officer may deem necessary, for the protection of pedestrians.
(3)
Mark lanes for traffic on street pavements at such places as such officer may deem advisable, consistent with other traffic ordinances of this city and the laws of the state; to indicate by white lines on street pavements parking spaces for vehicles.
(Code 1987, § 17-2)
State Law reference— Power of local authorities generally, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-371.

Sec. 70-5. - Same—Railroad trains.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any railroad train or engine to operate within the city at a speed greater than 50 miles per hour, between 6:00 a.m. to 8:00 p.m. and 40 miles per hour from 8:00 p.m. to 6:00 a.m.
(b)
The engineer or the conductor or both or any other person in charge and authority on such railroad train or engine may be held liable and responsible for the operation of such railroad train or engine within the city.
(Code 1987, § 17-5)
Sec. 70-6. - Railroad car, train, engine blocking or standing upon city streets.
(a)
Prohibited; time limits. It shall be unlawful for any railroad company, whose trains operate in the city or for any employee of such railroad company, to permit any car, train or engine to stand upon or block any street in the city for a time longer than ten minutes in the daytime and 15 minutes in the nighttime, such nighttime period to commence at 7:00 p.m. and end at 6:00 a.m.
(b)
Violation; penalty.
(1)
The punishment for such unlawful behavior shall be a fine of $500.00.
(2)
For each additional ten minutes in the daytime, the fine will be an additional $500.00. For each additional 15 minutes in the nighttime, the fine will be an additional $500.00.
(3)
If the railroad brakes the train at the Main Street crossing within ten minutes in the daytime and 15 minutes at nighttime, so that the crossing can be used by traffic on State Highway No. 121, the fine will be reduced to zero.
(c)
Assistance by police department. Whenever possible, the city police department will assist any train crew to find the reason for any train breakdown and get it on its way.
(Code 1987, § 17-5.1)
State Law reference— Responsibility of railroad employees for obstructing crossings, O.C.G.A. § 46-8-197.
Sec. 70-7. - Weight restrictions on certain streets.
(a)
The chief of police and superintendent of public works shall determine and designate city streets upon which shall be prohibited the use of motor vehicles with a gross weight in excess of five tons (6,000 pounds) and shall erect the appropriate street signs giving notice of such weight restriction.
(b)
The chief of police shall maintain a written list of the city streets subject to such weight restrictions.
(c)
When street signs are so erected giving notice to persons of such vehicle weight restrictions, no person shall operate a vehicle upon any such street in violation of the weight restrictions, subject to the following exceptions:
(1)
The driver of a truck or other commercial vehicle shall have the right to use such portion of such streets as may be necessary, and no more, in receiving or making commercial deliveries of articles to or from any point along the line of the street;
(2)
The driver of such a vehicle shall have the right to use such portion of such streets as may be necessary, and no more, in commuting only from his place of business to his residence located on such street.
(d)
Any violation of this Code section shall be punished as a misdemeanor pursuant to the general penalty provisions found under section 1-2.
(e)
Notwithstanding any other provision of this section, in the case of an emergency as determined by the chief of police, fire chief, or superintendent of public works or their designees, said individuals may temporarily allow a motor vehicle, the aggregate weight which is five tons or more, to operate on and over any of the above designated streets.
(Code 1987, § 17-6; Ord. of 1-10-95; Ord. No. 2013-05, §§ 1—4, 5-14-13; Ord. No. 2016-02, § 1, 2-9-16)

Sec. 70-16. - Bicycles.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to ride a bicycle on any sidewalk in the downtown business area at any time. The downtown business area is defined as Main Street from U.S. Highway 84 to Park Street and Central Avenue (both sides of the railroad tracks) from Taylor Street to Main Street.
(b)
When riding bicycles on sidewalks in other than the downtown area, bicyclists shall yield the right-of-way to pedestrians utilizing such sidewalks. When a question of doubt arises as to who has right-of-way, bicyclists shall yield such right-of-way to pedestrians as well as to all motor vehicles. When in doubt, yield.
(Code 1987, § 17-16)

Sec. 70-18. - Skateboarding self propelled or motorized scooter, roller skates or roller blades.
(1)
Riding skateboards in the downtown business district.
a.
It is unlawful for any person to ride a skateboard, self propelled or motorized scooter, roller skates or roller blades upon a public sidewalk, parking lot or roadway in the downtown business district.
b.
"Downtown business district" as used in this chapter, means the area designated by the city, as now or hereafter amended.
(2)
Penalties.
a.
Violation of any of the provisions of subsection (1) of this section is a traffic offense, whether or not the act at issue occurred upon a roadway, and shall be punished as follows:
1.
First offense. It is a traffic infraction for any person to violate subsection (1) of this section, and shall be punished by a fine of $25.00. This fine shall not be suspended or deferred, but the court may authorize community service in lieu of all or part of this fine.
2.
Second offense.
i.
Every person who violates subsection (1) of this section a second time within a five-year period shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, punishable by a fine not less than $50.00 nor more than $100.00. Fifty dollars of the fine shall not be suspended or deferred, but the court may authorize community service in lieu of all or part of this fine.
ii.
Upon conviction of a second violation of subsection (1) of this section within a five-year period, the court may order forfeiture of the skateboards, self propelled or motorized scooter, roller skates or roller blades which were ridden in violation of subsection (1) of this section, unless it is proven to the court by a preponderance of the evidence that the defendant is not the owner of the skateboard, self propelled or motorized scooter, roller skates or roller blades and the owner did not or could not have reasonably known that the skateboard would be ridden in violation of subsection (1) of this section.
3.
Third or subsequent offense.
i.
Every person who violates subsection (1) of this section a third or more times within a five-year period shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, punishable by a fine of not less than $100.00 nor more than $150.00. One hundred dollars of the fine shall not be suspended or deferred, but the court may authorize community service in lieu of all or part of the fine.
ii.
Upon conviction of a third or subsequent violation of subsection (1) of this section within a five-year period, the court may order forfeiture of the skateboards which were ridden in violation of subsection (1) of this section, unless it is proven to the court by a preponderance of the evidence that the defendant is not the owner of the skateboard, self propelled or motorized scooter, roller skates or roller blades and the owner did not or could not have reasonably known that the skateboard would be ridden in violation of subsection (1) of this section.
(3)
Negligent skateboarding, self propelled or motorized scooter, roller skates or roller blades.
a.
It is unlawful for any person to ride a skateboard, self propelled or motorized scooter, roller skates or roller blades in a negligent manner upon any publicly owned property or way open to the public in the city. For purposes of this section, "to ride in a negligent manner" means the riding or propelling of a skateboard in such a manner as to endanger or be likely to endanger any person or property: Provided, however, that any person riding a skateboard, self propelled or motorized scooter, roller skates or roller blades on private property with the consent of the owner in a manner consistent with the owner's consent shall not be guilty of negligent skateboarding.
b.
Negligent skateboarding, self propelled or motorized scooter, roller skates or roller blades is a traffic misdemeanor, punishable by a fine of not less than $50.00 nor more than $250.00. Twenty-five dollars of the fine shall not be suspended or deferred, but the court may authorize community service in lieu of all or part of the fine.
c.
Upon conviction of a violation of this section, the court may order forfeiture of the skateboards, self propelled or motorized scooter, roller skates or roller blades which were ridden in violation of this section, unless it is proven to the court by a preponderance of the evidence that the defendant is not the owner of the skateboard and the owner did not or could not have reasonably known that the skateboard would be ridden in violation of this section.
(4)
Seizure of skateboards, self propelled or motorized scooter, roller skates or roller blades. Whenever a law enforcement officer has probable cause to believe skateboards, self propelled or motorized scooter, roller skates or roller blades were used or are being used in violation of this chapter, such skateboards, self propelled or motorized scooter, roller skates or roller blades may be seized as evidence and held until the disposition of the charge. An observation by a law enforcement officer that a skateboard is in motion upon the ground or pavement in the downtown business district, whether or not such skateboard is accompanied by a person, shall be sufficient to establish probable cause of a violation of subsection (1) of this section.
(5)
Third party liability. It is expressly the purpose of this section to provide for and promote the health, safety and welfare of the general public and not to create or otherwise establish or designate any particular class or group of persons who will or should be especially protected or benefited by the terms of this section.
It is the specific intent of this section that no provisions nor any term used in this section is intended to impose any duty whatsoever upon the city or any of its officers or employees, for whom the implementation and enforcement of this section shall be discretionary and not mandatory.
Nothing contained in this section is intended nor shall be construed to create or form the basis of any liability on the part of the city, or its officers, employees or agents, for any injury or damage resulting from any action or inaction on the part of the city related in any manner to the enforcement of this section by its officers, employees or agents.
(6)
Saving. The enactments for this section shall not affect any case, proceeding, appeal or other matter currently pending in any court or in any way modify any right or liability, civil or criminal, which may be in existence on the effective date of this section.
(Ord. No.2010-01, § 1, 5-11-10)
Sec. 70-19. - Motorized carts.
(a)
Purpose and intent. The purpose of this section is to protect, maintain, and enhance health, safety and general welfare of present and future citizens of the City of Blackshear.
It has come to the attention of mayor and council that the use of motorized carts on city streets creates a potential traffic hazard for other vehicular traffic as well as a danger for the safety to the occupants of motorized carts.
Therefore, the intent of this section is to regulate the use of motorized carts within the corporate limits of the City of Blackshear as authorized by O.C.G.A. § 40-6-331 and other provisions of applicable law.
(b)
Operations and limitations.
(1)
Motorized carts may be operated within the corporate limits of the city by those persons who are 16 years of age and older and have a valid motor vehicle drivers license; or
(2)
Motorized carts may be operated within the corporate limits of the city by those persons who are 15 years of age, but not yet 16 years of age, and possess a valid instructional drivers permit (Georgia Class "CP") and accompanied in the front seat by a person at least 21 years of age who holds a valid motor vehicle drivers license.
(3)
All operators of motorized carts shall abide by all applicable traffic regulations applicable to vehicular traffic as set forth in Georgia Law and the this Code.
(4)
Motorized carts shall not be operated on sidewalks or in city parks other than in designated parking areas.
(5)
Motorized carts operated on the streets of the city one half hour before sunset until one-half hour after sunrise must be equipped with fully functional headlights, taillights and turn signal lights.
(6)
Motorized carts may cross streets and highways that are part of the state highway system. However, motorized carts may cross such streets only at crossings or intersections designated for that purpose by the department of transportation.
(7)
Motorized carts may cross streets and highways that are part of the city street system and used by other types of motor vehicles only at crossings or intersections designated for that purpose by the city council.
(8)
Motorized carts must be equipped with a slow-moving vehicle emblem pursuant to O.C.G.A § 40-8-4. Said emblem must be affixed to the rear of said motorized cart in a conspicuous place on the motorized cart, clearly visible to other operators of motor vehicles.
(9)
Each motorized cart must not have a number of passengers on it which exceeds the manufacturer's recommended occupancy. It shall be the responsibility of the operator of the motorized cart to know the correct number of passengers allowed. Each motorized cart passengers must be securely seated in the motorized cart at all times when the motorized cart is in motion.
(10)
No motorized cart may be operated on a street within the corporate limits of the city where the posted speed limit on such street is in excess of 35 miles per hour.
(11)
Notwithstanding any other terms and conditions set forth in this section a person who is otherwise lawfully authorized to operate a motorized cart within the corporate limits of the city, may operate a motorized cart on the right-of-way from Lakeview Drive to Golf Course Road.
(12)
No motorized cart shall be operated on a street within the city unless it has securely affixed thereto a fully functional rear view mirror.
(c)
Registration. Before any motorized cart may be operated within the corporate limits of the city, the cart must be registered and receive a decal from the city police department signifying its registration. A person desiring to register a motorized cart shall bring documentation setting forth a description of said cart to the city police department and complete an application for registration and licensing of said motorized cart. Upon registration, and licensing the police department shall issue a decal bearing a three digit number to be applied to the rear of such cart so as to be plainly visible. There shall be a fee of $15.00 dollars for such registration. The decal issued shall be non-transferable from the cart for which it is issued. The registration shall be effective until the motorized cart is sold or the title thereto is transferred. If a cart is sold or title is transferred the registration becomes null and void upon the sale or title transfer.
Before any motorized cart may be operated within the corporate limits the city, the cart must have affixed thereto an emblem as required by O.C.G.A. § 40-8-4. The emblem shall conform with those standards and specifications adopted for slow-moving vehicles by the American Society of Agricultural Engineers in December 1966, and contained within such Society's standard ASAE S276.1, or shall be an emblem of the same shape and size painted on such vehicle in a bright and conspicuous retro-reflective red-orange paint. Such emblem shall be mounted on the rear of such vehicles, in the approximate horizontal geometric center of the vehicle, at a height of three to five feet above the roadway, and shall be maintained at all times in a clean and reflective condition.
(d)
Reserved.
(e)
Restrictions. No motorized carts shall be operated on any state or federal route within the city limits, except as set forth in other subparts of this section.
(f)
Liability insurance coverage. Each person owning or operating a motorized cart that is operated within the City of Blackshear is responsible and liable for his or her own actions or failure to act. Liability insurance coverage varies and each person operating a motorized cart shall provide and verify his or her insurance coverage.
(g)
Exception.
(1)
This section shall not apply to any self-propelled, two-wheeled vehicle.
(2)
This section shall not apply to motorized carts operated by employees of the city.
(h)
Enforcement. This section shall not apply to motorized carts owned by golf courses, country clubs, or other such organized entities which own such carts and make them available to members or the public on a rental basis, provided that such motorized carts are used only on the premises of such golf courses, country clubs, or other such organized entities.
The city police department shall be charged with enforcement of this section.
(i)
Penalty. Except as set forth in [subsection (c)] of this section. Any person violating any provision of this section shall be punished by a fine for the first offense of not less than $25.00 and not more than $49.00. For the second offense, the fine shall be $50.00. If a third offense is committed within one year of conviction of a second offense, the minimum fine shall be $100.00 and the owner's registration shall be revoked. The registered owner or family member cannot thereafter register a motorized cart for use in the city for a period of two years following a third conviction. If a registration is revoked, the owner shall pay a re-registration fee of $200.00.
(Ord. No. 2009-07, § A, 4-14-09)
