ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 38-1. - Bonfires.
It shall be unlawful for any person, during a holiday or at any time, to build a bonfire of any description within the city limits, except at places approved and designated by the chief of the fire department.
(Code 1987, § 10-6)
Sec. 38-2. - Smoking of cigarettes, cigars, pipes and other devices.
The chief of police shall have the authority to determine that the smoking of cigarettes, cigars, pipes, and other devices used for smoking tobacco, shall be a fire hazard at any gathering of more than 200 people inside any building within the city. If the chief of police determines that the smoking of cigarettes, cigars, pipes, and other devices used for smoking tobacco is a fire hazard at such gathering and assembly, such chief shall announce to the gathering that there shall be no smoking. Any person who shall smoke after such announcement shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-12.
(Code 1987, § 10-7)
Sec. 38-3. - Fireworks prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell, display, shoot or explode any fireworks in the city; provided, however, public fireworks exhibitions by a professional company may be allowed upon approval by the fire chief or his designee.
(Ord. No. 2014-03, § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 38-4. - Illegal use of water from fire hydrant.
It shall be unlawful for any person to use water from any fire hydrant, whether public or private, for any purpose other than for the extinguishment of fires, or for lawful city purposes.
(Ord. No. 2014-03, § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 38-5. - Tampering with fire hydrant.
It shall be unlawful for any person to tamper or interfere with any fire hydrant of the city, except in case of fire and under instructions from the chief of the fire department, without first obtaining a permit from the fire chief, and without notifying the chief of the nature of such interference.
(Ord. No. 2014-03, § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 38-6. - Obstructing fire station entrance.
It shall be unlawful for any person to obstruct, in any manner, the entrance to any fire station in the city.
(Ord. No. 2014-03, § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 38-7. - Conditions for lighting fires.
It shall be unlawful for any person to burn or set fire to trash, rubbish, weeds, grass, or any other combustible material, on any premises within the city without first having confined the area so to be burned so as not to endanger any wood, fences, houses or any property of any description whatsoever; and before any fire shall be so set it shall be the duty of the person responsible to obtain a burn permit from the fire chief or his designee.
(Ord. No. 2014-03, § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 38-8. - Fire lanes.
It shall be unlawful for any person to park in, block any, or stop for any reason in any fire lane in the city, either by traffic or other means.
(Ord. No. 2014-03, § 1, 12-9-14)
Sec. 38-9. - Penalty provision.
Violations of any provisions of this chapter shall be punished in the same manner as provided for punishment of violations of validly enacted ordinances of the City of Blackshear. Any person convicted under this Code section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be subject to punishment by imprisonment for not more than six months or a fine of not more than $1,000.00 or both, plus court costs and fees, or any other penalty authorized under the Charter or state law.
(Ord. No. 2014-03, § 1, 12-9-14)
Secs. 38-10—38-30. - Reserved.
