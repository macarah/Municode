ARTICLE III. - WATER CONSERVATION

Sec. 34-84. - Imposition of restrictions.
If the city's water supply becomes critical and the city manager advises the council that it is necessary to impose restrictions on the use of water by consumers thereof, the mayor, by and with the advice and consent of the city council, may impose restrictions on such use for the health and safety of city consumers.
(Code 2003, § 58-91; Ord. of 7-3-2001, art. III, § 1)
Sec. 34-85. - Issuance of proclamation; determining restrictions on water use.
If the mayor shall determine that a condition exists warranting the imposition of restrictions on the use of city water, he shall issue a proclamation thereof imposing one or more of the following restrictions:
(1)
No outside watering of any kind, including street flushing, public fountains, park irrigation, watering lawns, gardens, shrubbery, or other plants.
(2)
No commercial and/or public water use such as swimming pools and motor vehicle washing of any kind.
(3)
No commercial and home laundry use except during the hours of 7:00 p.m. to 7:00 a.m. only.
(Code 2003, § 58-92; Ord. of 7-3-2001, art. III, § 2)
Sec. 34-86. - Right of entry.
Inspection of all premises in the city by duly authorized city officers bearing proper identification shall be permitted. Such officers may enter upon the land of all properties for the purpose of inspection, observation, measurement and testing in accordance with the provisions of this article.
(Code 2003, § 58-94; Ord. of 7-3-2001, art. III, § 4)
Sec. 34-87. - Issuance of warning.
Any person found to be violating the provisions of this article shall be served a written notice stating the nature of the violation and shall be issued a warning summons for this violation.
(Code 2003, § 58-95; Ord. of 7-3-2001, art. III, § 5)
Sec. 34-88. - Penalty for subsequent violations.
Any person found guilty of a violation of this article, subsequent to being issued a warning summons, shall be punished as provided in section 1-13.
(Code 2003, § 58-93; Ord. of 7-3-2001, art. III, § 6)
Secs. 34-89—34-119. - Reserved.
