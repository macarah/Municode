Chapter 22 - OFFENSES AND MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS[1]
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 22-1. - Injuring property of city.
It shall be unlawful for any person to intentionally damage, in any way, any implement or other property of the city.
(Code 1980, § 11-102; Code 2003, § 34-1)
Sec. 22-2. - Disorderly conduct.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person within the corporate limits of the city to violate any of the following:
(1)
Any person who shall act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another whereby any person is placed in danger of safety of his life, limb or health;
(2)
Any person who shall act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another whereby the property of any person is placed in danger of being destroyed or damaged;
(3)
Any person who shall cause, provoke or engage in any fight, brawl or riotous conduct so as to endanger the life, limb, health or property of another;
(4)
Any person who shall assemble or congregate with another or others for the purpose or with the intent to engage in gaming;
(5)
Any person who shall frequent any public place with the intent to obtain money from other persons by illegal and fraudulent schemes, tricks, artifices or devices;
(6)
Any person who assembles for the purpose of engaging in any fraudulent scheme, device or trick, to obtain any valuable thing in any place or from any person in the city, or who shall aid or abet therein;
(7)
Any person who shall use fighting words directed towards any person who becomes outraged and thus creates a turmoil;
(8)
Any person who shall, by acts of violence, interfere with another's pursuit of a lawful occupation; or
(9)
Any person who shall congregate with another or others in or on any public way so as to halt the flow of vehicular or pedestrian traffic and refuses to clear such public way when ordered by the city police or other lawful authority.
(b)
Any person who shall violate any provision of subsection (a) of this section may be charged with the offense of disorderly conduct and, on conviction thereof, may be found guilty of disorderly conduct.
(Code 1980, § 11-121; Code 2003, § 34-2)
Sec. 22-3. - Drinking in public.
It shall be a violation of this chapter and punishable as such for any person to drink any alcoholic beverage, as that term is defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-1-2, in any public building or other public place of the city, except upon the premises of any licensed retail beer parlor.
(Code 1980, § 11-123; Code 2003, § 34-3)
Sec. 22-4. - Drunkenness.
No person shall be drunk or intoxicated on the streets or at any public places of the city, or on the premises of another, without consent of the owner thereof.
(Code 1980, § 11-124; Code 2003, § 34-4)
Sec. 22-5. - Malicious mischief.
No person in the city shall commit any act of mischief which in any way tends to injure the person or the property of any other or to destroy such property or to injure or destroy any public property.
(Code 1980, § 11-134; Code 2003, § 34-5)
Sec. 22-6. - Camping.
No person shall camp on the streets, square or any other public place of the city.
(Code 1980, § 11-135; Code 2003, § 34-6)
Sec. 22-7. - Obstruction of streets and littering.
No person shall place any obstruction in or on any street, alley or ditch of the city; nor shall any person throw any waste matter, garbage, trash, or refuse matter of any kind upon the streets, alleys or ditches of the city.
(Code 1980, § 11-136; Code 2003, § 34-7)
Sec. 22-8. - Discharging firearms.
It shall be unlawful for any person to fire any gun, pistol or firearm of any type or any air rifle or slingshot within the city limits, except for persons discharging shotguns for recreational purposes on farmland only, provided that such discharge of shotguns shall not occur within 400 yards of any dwelling.
(Code 1980, § 11-148; Code 2003, § 34-8; Ord. of 11-5-1996)
Sec. 22-9. - Urination and defecation.
It shall be unlawful for any person to urinate or defecate in any public place within the city.
(Code 1980, § 11-162; Code 2003, § 34-9)
Sec. 22-10. - Application of chemicals.
The aerial application of herbicides, pesticides, and other chemicals within the corporate limits of the city is prohibited.
(Code 2003, § 34-10)
Sec. 22-11. - Vehicles in cemeteries.
It shall be unlawful for any motor vehicle to enter or park on the premises of the city cemetery after sundown.
(Code 1980, § 5-101; Code 2003, § 34-11)
Secs. 22-12—22-40. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - CURFEW OF MINORS

Sec. 22-41. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Adult means any person 18 years of age or older.
Minor means any person under the age of 18 years.
Public place means any street, highway, alley or right-of-way, including, but not limited to, any park, sidewalk or playground; any place, lot, land or building open to the public including, but not limited to, any mall, shopping center, restaurant, hotel or motel, place of amusement or entertainment, parking lot and adjacent areas thereto, cemetery, schoolyard, body of water or watercourse, and any vacant lot or land contained within the city limits.
(Code 2003, § 34-41; Ord. of 9-3-1996, § 1)
Sec. 22-42. - Prohibited activity; exceptions.
It shall be unlawful for any minor to engage in any activity in any public place including, but not limited to, wandering, loitering, strolling, playing or driving, between the hours of 11:00 p.m. on any day and 6:00 a.m. the following day, without supervision by an adult having lawful authority to be at such place; provided, however, that on Fridays and Saturdays the effective hours shall be between 12:00 midnight and 6:00 a.m. the following day; and provided, further, that the provisions of this section shall not apply in the following instances:
(1)
When a minor is accompanied by his parent, guardian, or other adult person 21 years of age or older having the lawful care and custody of the minor.
(2)
When the minor is upon an emergency errand directed by his parent, guardian, or other adult person 21 years of age or older having the lawful care and custody of the minor.
(3)
When the minor is returning directly home from lawful employment that makes it necessary to be in the places referred to in this section during the prescribed period of time.
(4)
When the minor is attending or traveling directly to or from an activity involving the exercise of first amendment rights.
(5)
When the minor is in a motor vehicle with parental consent for normal travel, with interstate and intrastate travel through the city being excepted in all cases from this section.
(Code 2003, § 34-42; Ord. of 9-3-1996, § 2)
Sec. 22-43. - Responsibility of person having custody of minor.
It shall be unlawful for the parent, guardian, or other person having custody or control of any minor to encourage, permit, or by insufficient supervision allow such minor to be in or upon any public place listed or referred to in section 22-42 within the city between the hours of 11:00 p.m. on any day and 6:00 a.m. the following day, or on Fridays and Saturdays between the hours of 12:00 midnight and 6:00 a.m. the following day except in circumstances set out in section 22-42(1)—(5). A person charged with violation of this section for the first time shall be given a warning. Upon subsequent violation, the person shall be charged with such violation and, if found guilty, such person shall be punished as provided in section 1-13.
(Code 2003, § 34-43; Ord. of 9-3-1996, § 3)
