DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 22-191. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Applicant means not only the person applying for a license under this article, but also all employees of the applicant, whether paid or not, who will be engaging in the occupation of peddler within the corporate limits of the city.
Peddler means any person, as principal or agent, or both, which is not a regular retail or wholesale merchant with a permanent place of business in the city, traveling by foot, wagon, automobile, truck or any other means of conveyance, from place to place, house to house or street to street, carrying, conveying or transporting wares, merchandise of whatever type or character, all foodstuff or provisions, offering and exposing such wares, merchandise, foodstuff or provisions for sale or making sales and delivering articles to prospective purchasers within the city, or who, without traveling from place to place, shall sell or offer such articles for sale from a wagon, vehicle of any type, railroad car or any other type of vehicle or conveyance. Such term shall also mean any person traveling from house to house or business to business offering personal services such as, but not limited to, lawn maintenance or care, home improvement sales or services, or any other type of service or labor of whatever type or character.
Sec. 22-192. - Duty of police to enforce.
It shall be the duty of any police officer of the city to require any person seen peddling, and who is not known by such police officer to be duly licensed, to produce his peddler's license and to enforce the provisions of this article against any person found to be in violation of this article.
Sec. 22-193. - Records.
The chief of police shall report to the city licensing clerk all convictions for violations of this article, and the city licensing clerk shall maintain a record of each license issued and record the reports of violations of this article in such record.
Sec. 22-194. - Hours of operation.
All peddlers licensed under this article shall be permitted to peddle or solicit door to door within the city only between the hours of 9:00 a.m. and 5:00 p.m. Monday through Friday of each week, excluding holidays.
(Code 1976, § 7-1112)
Sec. 22-195. - Prohibited in residential areas; exception.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any peddler, hawker or itinerant merchant to sell or offer to sell to the general public, at retail or wholesale, from motorized or self-propelled vehicles, wagons or any other types of conveyances, on residential streets within the city.
(b)
This section shall not be applicable to newsboys or bona fide merchants who merely deliver goods in the regular course of business into particular residential areas.
(Code 1976, § 7-1113)
Sec. 22-196. - Use of streets, public ways.
No peddler shall have any exclusive right to any location in the public streets; nor be permitted a stationary location; nor be permitted to operate in any congested area where his operations might impede or inconvenience the public. For the purposes of this article, the judgment of a police officer, exercised in good faith, shall be deemed conclusive as to whether the area is congested or the public is impeded or inconvenienced.
(Code 1976, § 7-1113)
Sec. 22-197. - Solicitations without permit.
(a)
Legislative intent. It is the intent of the council in enacting the ordinance from which this section derives, to improve the quality of life and economic vitality of the City of Valdosta, and to protect the safety of the general public against certain abusive conduct of persons engaged in solicitation, by imposing reasonable time, manner and place restrictions on solicitation while respecting the constitutional rights of free speech for all citizens.
(b)
Definitions.
Aggressive solicitation includes, but is not limited to soliciting in any of the following manners:
(1)
By intentionally or recklessly blocking the safe or free passage of a person or vehicle by any means, including unreasonably causing a pedestrian or vehicle operator to take evasive action to avoid physical contact. Acts authorized by a lawfully issued permit shall not constitute obstruction of pedestrian traffic for purposes of this section;
(2)
By approaching, seeking or following a person before, during or after solicitation if that conduct is intended, or likely to cause a reasonable person to:
a.
Fear bodily harm to oneself or another;
b.
Fear damage to or loss of property; or
c.
Be intimidated.
(3)
By using profane or abusive language or gestures, either during the solicitation or following refusal;
(4)
By touching or causing physical contact with another person without that person's consent in the course of soliciting if the touching or physical conduct is likely to cause a reasonable person to feel restrained or intimidated; or
(5)
By intentionally or recklessly using obscene, abusive or threatening language or gestures, toward a person being solicited.
Automated-teller machine means a device, linked to a financial institution's account records, that is able to carry out transactions, including but not limited to, account transfers, deposits, cash withdrawals, balance inquiries and mortgage and loan payments.
Automated-teller machine facility means the area comprised of one or more automated-teller machines, and any adjacent space that is made available to banking customers.
Hospitality and commercial district means the area defined as follows on the map of the City of Valdosta attached hereto as Exhibit "A" and incorporated herein by specific reference thereto, described also as follows:
All properties located within the corporate limits of the City of Valdosta having frontage on any of the following roads: James Road; Baytree Road; North Oak Street between Inner Perimeter and Roosevelt Drive; North Oak Street between Georgia Avenue and West Brookwood Drive; North Valdosta Road; Inner Perimeter Road between North Valdosta Road and North Oak Street; North Ashley Street; North Lee Street between East Magnolia Street and East Martin Luther King, Jr. Drive; Martin Luther King, Jr. Drive between Lee Street and Toombs Street; East Hill Avenue; and N. St. Augustine Road.
Specifically, all properties fronting on North Valdosta Road beginning at the point where the corporate limits of the City of Valdosta intersect with North Valdosta Road and continuing southeast along North Valdosta Road to the intersection of Inner Perimeter Road to then include all such property bounded by Inner Perimeter Road, North Oak Street, and North Valdosta Road. Then, continuing south to include all such property fronting on and bounded by North Oak Street and North Ashley Street until such point that North Oak Street and North Ashley Street intersect with Roosevelt/Pendleton Drive. Then, continuing south to include all properties fronting on and bounded by North Patterson Street and North Ashley Street until such point that North Patterson Street and North Ashley Street intersect with Woodrow Wilson Drive.
Then, continuing south to include all properties fronting on North Patterson Street and all properties fronting on North Ashley Street until such point that North Patterson Street intersects with Georgia Avenue. The boundaries then expand west to include those properties fronting on and bounded by Georgia Avenue, North Oak Street, North Patterson Street, and West Brookwood Drive. Then, continuing south to include all properties fronting on North Oak Street, North Patterson Street, and North Ashley Street until such point that North Oak Street, North Patterson Street and North Ashley Street intersect with Magnolia Street.
The corridor then incorporates the official boundaries of the Central Valdosta Development Authority as follows: All properties fronting on and bounded by Magnolia Street, Lee Street, Martin Luther King, Jr. Drive and Toombs Street.
The Corridor then extends west to include all properties fronting on East Hill Avenue until such point that East Hill Avenue intersects with North St. Augustine Road. The corridor then encompasses all those properties fronting on and bounded by St. Augustine Road, Norman Drive, Baytree Road, the western-most city limits line and East Hill Avenue. The corridor then extends east to include all properties fronting on Baytree Road.
Public property means all property owned, operated or controlled by a governmental entity within the city boundaries of Valdosta, Georgia, including, but not limited to, streets, public sidewalks, tree lawns, parks, playgrounds, publicly-owned parking lots, schools, libraries, post offices, municipal transit facilities and other public lands and buildings.
Solicit or soliciting is any request made in person on a street, sidewalk, or public place asking for an immediate donation of money or other thing of value, including the purchase of an item or service for an amount far exceeding its value, under circumstances where a reasonable person would understand that the purchase is a donation. The solicitation may be, without limitation, by the spoken, written, or printed word, by gesture, or by other means of communication.
(c)
Prohibited conduct. It shall be unlawful to:
(1)
Aggressively solicit within the City of Valdosta, Georgia.
(2)
Solicit within the hospitality and commercial district.
(3)
Solicit on private property if the owner, tenant or lawful occupant has asked the person not to solicit on the property, or has posted a sign clearly indicating that solicitations are not welcome on the property.
(4)
Solicit within 30 feet of any automated-teller machine without the consent of the owner or other person legally in possession of the machine, provided; however, when an automated-teller machine is located within an automated-teller machine facility, the distance shall be measured from the entrance or exit of the automated-teller machine facility.
(5)
Solicit within 30 feet of a line of pedestrians waiting to obtain access to a building or event.
(6)
Solicit within 30 feet of the area of an outdoor approved area as defined in section 7-1034(d) of Ordinance No. 2004-35.
(7)
Solicit within 30 feet of any pay telephone; provided, however, that when a pay telephone is located within a telephone booth or other facility, the distance shall be measured from the entrance or exit of the telephone booth or facility.
(8)
Solicit on public property within 30 feet of an entrance to a building.
(9)
Solicit on public property within 30 feet of an entrance to a parking lot.
(10)
Solicit on a public right-of-way designed for vehicular traffic, including medians.
(11)
Solicit between the hours of 8:00 p.m. and 8:00 a.m.
(12)
Solicit in a false or misleading manner which includes, but is not limited to the following: (i) stating that the solicitor is from out of town and stranded when such is not true; (ii) stating or suggesting falsely that the solicitor is either a present or former member of the Armed Service indicated; (iii) wearing or displaying an indication of physical disability, when the solicitor does not suffer the disability indicated; (iv) use of any makeup or device to simulate a deformity; or (v) stating that the solicitor is homeless, when he or she is not.
(d)
Penalties.
(1)
Upon the first or subsequent offenses, individuals violating subsection (c)(1) of this section shall be issued a written citation, including a summons to appear in Valdosta Municipal Court, be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00, or confinement not to exceed 30 days or both.
(2)
Individuals violating subsections (c)(2)—(12) of this section, shall be issued a warning citation, which shall not include a summons to appear in Valdosta Municipal Court. On the second or subsequent offense, the individual shall be issued a written citation, including a summons to appear in Valdosta Municipal Court, be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00, or confinement not exceed 30 days or both.
(Code 1976, §§ 7-1112, 11-1001; Ord. No. 2008-34, 7-24-2008)
Sec. 22-198. - Application for license.
An applicant for a license under this article shall file in writing with the city licensing clerk a sworn application, in duplicate, on a form to be furnished by the city licensing clerk, which shall give the following information:
(1)
Name and description of the applicant.
(2)
Applicant's legal and local addresses.
(3)
A brief description of the nature of the business and the goods to be sold and, in the case of farm or orchard products, whether such products are produced or grown by the applicant.
(4)
If the applicant is employed, the name and address of the applicant's employer, together with credentials establishing the exact relationship.
(5)
The length of time for which the right to do business is desired.
(6)
If a vehicle is to be used, a brief description of the vehicle, together with the license number or other means of identification for each such vehicle.
(7)
A photograph of the applicant taken within 60 days immediately prior to the date of filing the application, which photograph shall be two inches by two inches, showing the head and shoulders of the applicant in a clear and distinguishing manner.
(8)
The fingerprints of the applicant and the names of at least two reliable property owners within the county who will certify as to the applicant's good character and business responsibility, or, in lieu of the names of references, any other available evidence as to the good character and business responsibility of the applicant as will enable an investigator designated by the city to properly evaluate the applicant's character and business responsibility.
(9)
A statement as to whether or not the applicant has been convicted of any crime, misdemeanor or violation of any municipal ordinance, the nature of the offense and the punishment or penalty assessed for such offense.
(10)
The applicant shall file with his application a statement by a reputable physician, duly licensed by the state, dated not more than ten days prior to submission of the application, certifying the applicant to be free of infectious, contagious or communicable disease.
(11)
If the applicant is a corporation, the names and addresses of the current corporate officers, the registered office of the corporation, and a duly attested corporate resolution acknowledging the authority of the corporation to transact the type of business as set forth in the application.
(12)
If the applicant is a corporation or form of business other than a sole proprietorship, the names and addresses of all employees who will engage in the occupation of peddler on behalf of the corporation. All information for the application for a license as enumerated in subsections (1)—(11) of this section shall be provided for each such employee.
(13)
At the time of filing the application, a fee as determined by the city council and as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk shall be paid to the city clerk to cover the cost of the investigation.
(14)
All applicants shall submit with any application a verification from the state setting forth the sales tax identification number of the applicant.
(Code 1976, § 7-1112)
Sec. 22-199. - Investigation and issuance.
(a)
Upon receipt of an application for a license as required by this article, the original application shall be referred to the chief of police, who shall cause such investigation of the applicant's business and moral character to be made as he deems necessary for the protection of the public good.
(b)
If, as a result of such investigation, the applicant's character or business responsibility is found to be unsatisfactory, the chief of police shall endorse on such application his disapproval and his reasons for such disapproval, and shall return the application to the city licensing clerk, who shall notify the applicant that his application is disapproved and that no license will be issued.
(c)
If, as a result of such investigation, the character and business responsibility of the applicant are found to be satisfactory, the chief of police shall endorse his approval on the application, execute a license addressed to the applicant for the carrying on of the business applied for, and return such license, along with the application, to the city licensing clerk, who shall, upon payment of the prescribed license fee, deliver the license to the applicant. Such license shall contain the signature and seal of the issuing officer, and shall show the name and address of the licensee, the class of license issued, the kind of goods to be sold under such license, the amount of the fee paid, the date of issuance and the length of time the license shall be operative, as well as the license number and other identifying description of any vehicle used in such peddling. The city licensing clerk shall keep a permanent record of all licenses issued. Each employee of the applicant shall be deemed a licensee for purposes of this section, and a separate license shall be issued to each such employee, containing all of the information as required in this subsection.
Sec. 22-200. - Fees.
The fee for a license required by this article shall be as determined by the city council and shall be set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Code 1976, § 7-1112)
Sec. 22-201. - Display.
A license issued to the applicant and all employees by the city licensing clerk under this article shall be conspicuously displayed upon all vehicles used by the applicant so as to be easily seen, and shall be displayed so as to be conspicuous during such time as such licensee is engaged in the occupation of peddling.
Sec. 22-202. - Exhibition upon request.
Peddlers shall exhibit their licenses at the request of any citizen.
Sec. 22-203. - Transferability.
No license issued under the provisions of this article shall be used at any time by any person other than the person to whom it was issued.
Sec. 22-204. - Revocation.
(a)
Licenses issued under the provisions of this article may be revoked by the city licensing clerk after notice and hearing, for any of the following causes:
(1)
Fraud, misrepresentation or false statement contained in the license application.
(2)
Fraud, misrepresentation or false statement made in the course of carrying on the applicant's business as a peddler.
(3)
Any violation of this article.
(4)
Conviction of any crime or misdemeanor involving moral turpitude.
(5)
Conduct of the business of peddling in an unlawful manner or in such a manner as to constitute a breach of the peace or to constitute a menace to the health, safety or general welfare of the public.
(b)
Notice of the hearing for revocation of a license shall be given in writing, specifically setting forth the grounds of complaint and the time and place of the hearing. Such notice shall be mailed to the licensee at his last known address at least five days prior to the date set for the hearing.
Sec. 22-205. - Appeal.
Any person aggrieved by the action of the city licensing clerk or the chief of police in the denial of a license application under this article, or in the decision with reference to the revocation of a license, shall have the right of appeal to the city council. Such appeal shall be taken by filing with the council, within 14 days after notice of the action complained of has been mailed to such person's last known address, a written statement fully setting forth the grounds for the appeal. The council shall set a time and place for a hearing on such appeal, and notice of such hearing shall be given to the appellant in the same manner as provided in section 22-204(b) for a notice of hearing on revocation. The decision and order of the council on such appeal shall be final and conclusive.
Sec. 22-206. - Expiration of license.
All annual licenses issued under the provisions of this article shall expire on December 31 in the year when issued. Other licenses, other than annual licenses, shall expire on the date specified in the license.
Secs. 22-207—22-250. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - CHARITABLE SOLICITATIONS

Sec. 22-251. - License required; exception.
It shall be unlawful for any person to solicit money, financial assistance or property, or to sell or offer to sell any article, tag, service emblem, publication, ticket, advertisement, subscription or anything of value on the streets, in any place of business, or by house-to-house canvass, or in any other public or private place, or by telephone, personal solicitation or otherwise in the city, upon representation that such sale, subscription or solicitation, or the proceeds, is for a charitable, educational, religious, patriotic or philanthropic purpose, unless such person shall first obtain a license so to do as provided in this division; however, this article shall not apply to any society, association or corporation organized and operated exclusively for religious, educational, philanthropic, benevolent, fraternal or other eleemosynary purposes where the solicitations of such funds and benefits shall be conducted solely among the members of such association, society or corporation.
(Code 1976, § 7-1122)
Sec. 22-252. - Application required; contents.
Any person desiring a permit to solicit funds as required in this division, shall procure a license and upon application for such license shall furnish the city manager with the following on the application:
(1)
Name of organization applying for permit to solicit and the address of its headquarters;
(2)
Names and addresses of its principal officers and manager;
(3)
The purpose for which any receipts derived from such solicitation are to be used;
(4)
The name or person by whom the receipt of such solicitation shall be disbursed;
(5)
The name and address of the person who shall be in direct charge of conducting the solicitation;
(6)
An outline of the methods to be used in conducting the solicitation;
(7)
The time when such solicitations shall be made, giving the proposed dates for the beginning and ending of such solicitations;
(8)
The amount of any wages, fees, commissions, expenses or emoluments to be expended or paid to anyone in connection with such solicitation, together with the manner in which such wages, fees, expenses, commissions or emoluments are to be expended, to whom paid, and the amount;
(9)
A financial statement for the last preceding fiscal year of any funds collected for the purpose set out in section 22-251 by the organization of persons seeking a permit for such solicitation, giving the amount of money so raised, together with the cost of raising it, and the financial distribution of the money;
(10)
A full statement of the character and extent of the charitable, religious, educational or philanthropic work being done by the application organization within the city; and
(11)
Such other information as may be required by the city manager to fully determine the kind and character and worthiness of the proposed solicitations and as to whether or not such solicitations are in the interest of protecting the health, life and property of the citizens of the city, and in the interest of preserving and enforcing good government for the security of the city and its inhabitants.
(Code 1976, § 7-1123)
Sec. 22-253. - Revocation of license.
The license provided in this division to be issued may be revoked by the council on proper showing that the purpose of such person is not for the best interest of the citizens of the city or for any other cause the council may deem proper.
(Code 1976, § 7-1124)
