ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 98-1. - Water emergency.
(a)
City manager authorized to declare emergency. In the event of prolonged drought, failure of equipment, or for any other reason that could substantially affect the supply of water to the city and the city water users, the city manager is authorized for the health and safety of the city to declare an emergency. If an emergency is declared, the city manager shall notify all users through news releases to the media and through notices mailed to all users as soon as practical after the emergency is declared.
(b)
Restricted use in the event of an emergency. When an emergency is declared by the city manager requiring the conservation of water, the following restrictions shall be effective:
(1)
No outside watering of any kind shall be allowed, including street flushing, public fountains, park irrigation, lawn, garden and shrubbery watering.
(2)
No commercial, industrial and/or public use, such as motor vehicle washing, noncritical manufacturing processes, filling of swimming pools and other related uses, shall be allowed.
(3)
No commercial and home laundry shall be allowed except during the hours of 7:00 p.m. to 7:00 a.m. only.
(c)
Violations; penalties. Any person found in violation of this section shall on the first violation be served written notice stating the nature of the violation and issued a warning ticket. Upon the second violation, water service shall be terminated to the residence or business address of the violator and a reconnection fee of $50.00 shall be assessed. For all such subsequent violations, a reconnection fee of $200.00 for each violation shall be assessed.
(Ord. No. 87-46, §§ 1—3, 7-9-1987)
