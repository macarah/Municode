DIVISION 2. - HOSPITAL AUTHORITY[9]

Footnotes:
--- (9) ---
State Law reference— Hospital authority created, O.C.G.A. § 31-7-72.
State Law reference— Hospital Authorities Law, O.C.G.A. § 31-7-70 et seq.

Sec. 2-291. - Need for authority; creation.
There is a need for a hospital authority to function in the city, and there is created such an authority.
(Code 1976, § 2-8001)
Sec. 2-292. - Name of authority.
The hospital authority created in this division is to function in the city and the county jointly and shall be known as "The Hospital Authority of Valdosta and Lowndes County, Georgia."
(Code 1976, § 2-8002)
Sec. 2-293. - Appointment of members; vacancies.
(a)
The hospital authority shall consist of eight members, four of whom shall be appointed by the mayor and council, and four of whom shall be appointed by the governing body of the county.
(b)
Members shall be allowed to serve an unrestricted number of consecutive five-year terms.
(c)
When a vacancy occurs as result of resignation, death or disability, at its next regular meeting following the vacancy occurring, the hospital authority shall notify the appropriate governing authority of the vacancy, and simultaneously therewith submit three names from which the governing authority, at its next regular meeting, will select one to fill the vacancy and duly notify the appointee and the hospital authority.
(d)
When a vacancy is about to occur as result of the expiration of a member's term, at its regular meeting at least 30 days prior to the occurrence of the vacancy, the hospital authority shall notify the appropriate governing body of the vacancy, and simultaneously therewith submit three names from which the governing authority at its next regular meeting shall select one of the three to fill the vacancy and duly notify the appointee and the hospital authority.
(e)
A licensed physician of the medical staff of any hospital operated by the authority may be appointed as a member thereof.
(Code 1976, § 2-8003; Res. No. 82-23, 12-9-1982)
Sec. 2-294. - Division of authority.
The mayor and council and the governing body of the county shall have equal voting power with respect to matters pertaining to the hospital authority.
(Code 1976, § 2-8004)
Secs. 2-295—2-310. - Reserved.
DIVISION 3. - VALDOSTA-LOWNDES COUNTY PARKS AND RECREATION BOARD[10]

Footnotes:
--- (10) ---
Editor's note— This division is derived from the "Memorandum of Agreement By and Between Lowndes County, Georgia, City of Valdosta, Georgia, City of Hahira, Georgia, City of Remerton, Georgia, City of Lake Park, Georgia, and City of Dasher, Georgia," as adopted by the mayor and council in August of 1999.

Sec. 2-311. - Established.
There is established by the city and county a board to be known and designated as the Valdosta-Lowndes County Parks and Recreation Board ("the board").
Sec. 2-312. - Compensation; appointment; terms of office; ex-officio members.
(a)
The parks and recreation board shall consist of nine members. One member shall be appointed by the county, who shall be a member of the board of commissioners; one member shall be appointed by the city, who shall be a member of the mayor and council; one member at-large shall be appointed each by the county and city; one member shall be appointed each by the City of Hahira, City of Lake Park, City of Remerton and City of Dasher; one member shall be appointed by the boards of education of the county and city, on a rotating basis, with the initial appointment being made by the county board of education.
(b)
The initial appointment for one of the county appointees shall be for a term of two years, and the initial appointment of the other shall be for a term of three years. The initial appointment for one of the city appointees shall be for a term of two years, and the initial appointment of the other shall be for a term of three years. The initial appointment for the appointee of the City of Hahira shall be for a term of three years. The initial appointment for the appointee of the City of Lake Park shall be for a term of three years. The initial appointment for the appointee of the City of Remerton shall be for a term of two years. The initial appointment for the appointee of the City of Dasher shall be for a term of two years. The initial appointment for the appointee from the county board of education shall be for a term of two years. Thereafter all appointments shall be for a term of three years.
(c)
The city manager and the county manager shall be ex-officio members of the parks and recreation board.
Sec. 2-313. - Organization; operation.
The parks and recreation board shall appoint one of its members as chairman, and one of its members as secretary. The parks and recreation board shall meet a minimum of once a month, and the secretary shall keep official minutes of the meetings and all votes of the board. All meetings of the board must be conducted in accordance with Robert's Rules of Order, and a majority of the board shall constitute a quorum.
Sec. 2-314. - Duties.
The parks and recreation board shall serve in an advisory capacity regarding the operation and management of the parks and recreation program with the county and city. As one of its primary functions, the board shall have the responsibility to make recommendations on operational issues to the county manager, city manager and director, and the city and council will consider and respond to such recommendations. Each fiscal year an annual budget shall be prepared, and shall be submitted to and approved by the county and city. In addition, the board shall send a minimum of monthly reports to the city and the county. The director shall report to the board on all activities. The staff retained for the operation of the parks and recreation program shall report directly to the director.
Sec. 2-315. - Funding of programs.
(a)
The county has agreed to fund sufficient funds from its county-wide digest to provide for a parks and recreation program composed of the following activities:
(1)
Parks and recreational administration.
(2)
Mathis Auditorium.
(3)
Jaycee Park.
(4)
Parks.
(5)
Therapeutics.
(6)
Aquatics.
(7)
Instructional programming.
(8)
Athletics manager.
(9)
Pinevale.
(10)
Youth sports.
(11)
Adult athletics.
(12)
Hosted tournaments.
(13)
Senior citizens.
(14)
Special events.
(15)
After school program.
(16)
Summer camp program.
(b)
In the day-to-day operation of the parks and recreation program, the expenses necessarily incurred thereby will be paid by the City of Valdosta and City of Hahira, and such cities will be reimbursed monthly for actual expenses incurred by such cities; however, such reimbursement shall not exceed the annual budget appropriation for the parks and recreation program without prior approval. Detailed expense reports will be required as set forth by the county finance director. Expense reports shall be furnished to the county finance director no later than the 15th day of each month and payment shall be made by the county no later than the first day of the following month.
Sec. 2-316. - Activities.
The following activities shall be funded and operated under the parks and recreation program:
(1)
Parks and recreation administration. The parks and recreation administration division shall oversee the daily operations of the parks, recreation and community affairs department and administer and evaluate departmental policies and procedures, and provide quality recreational programming, facilities and special events for the entire community.
(2)
Mathis Auditorium. The Mathis Auditorium division shall provide and maintain meeting facilities, including a multipurpose room of 5,000 square feet provided in the community for banquets and meetings, and a 1,200-seat auditorium facility with stage support for community groups, shows and concerts promoted by outside organizations.
(3)
Jaycee Park. The Jaycee Park division shall provide a facility for meetings, rentals for private functions, instructional classes and camps for the entire community.
(4)
Parks. The parks division shall maintain parks and facilities in a safe, attractive and accessible condition, and develop new parks for public use and provide a variety of recreational opportunities.
(5)
Therapeutics. The therapeutics division shall provide:
a.
Therapeutic programs designed to meet social, recreational and educational needs of the mentally and physically disabled community in the city and county;
b.
A quality social, recreational and educational program to the mentally and physically disabled adults and children; and
c.
Quality summer programs for the special needs population.
(6)
Aquatics. The aquatics division shall provide quality aquatic programming that will benefit the citizens of the city and county, and provide a quality aquatic facility that is operated in compliance with the state standards. A pool is operated at the Fry Street location, which meets state pool operating and safety standards with hired, professionally trained aquatic managers and certified lifeguards. Such location provides a variety of aquatics, including swimming lessons, free swimming, specialty classes and summer events.
(7)
Instructional programming. The instructional programming division will provide quality programming for the residents of the city and county, to include a variety of instructional, educational and informative programs.
(8)
Athletic manager/coordinator. The athletic manager/coordinator oversees the daily athletic operations that are used in adult programs, weekend tournaments and special athletic events, as well as coordinating the training and supervision of all athletic personnel.
(9)
Pinevale. The Pinevale division's responsibilities shall be to meet the athletic, recreational and social needs of the Pinevale Youth Center participants, by providing a variety of youth-oriented programs through the Pinevale Youth Center.
(10)
Youth sports division. The youth sports division provides a quality, economical and community-oriented recreational youth sports program for the city and county citizens, by providing seasonal leagues for boys and girls four to 18 years of age, including baseball, football, basketball and soccer for boys, and softball, soccer, basketball and cheerleading for girls.
(11)
Adult athletics. The adult athletics division offers a variety of programs and events to both adult men and women, which events consist of softball, basketball, soccer, football and tennis.
(12)
Hosted tournaments. The hosted tournaments division provides quality tournaments for the citizens of the city and county. Such division currently provides 12 quality softball invitational tournaments per year; four state parks and recreation association hosted tournaments per year; and four American Softball Association tournaments per year.
(13)
Senior citizens. The senior citizens division shall establish quality programs for all senior citizens 55 years of age and older. Such division also provides facilities and programs for senior activities and instructional programming. A senior center is currently under construction, and the construction is funded jointly by the city and county.
(14)
Special events. The special events division provides special events for the recreation of the county and city.
(15)
After school program. The after school program division provides afternoon activities for elementary school age children in the community.
(16)
Summer camp program. The summer camp program division provides an eight-week summer day camp for school age children in the city and county. Such division provides a quality eight-week camp program for an average of 30 children per site, per week.
Sec. 2-317. - Capital improvements.
Unless otherwise agreed by separate written agreement, the county and cities agree and understand that the funding contemplated by the agreement as set forth in section 2-315 shall be for the annual operating budget of the county-wide recreational program. Any capital improvement or acquisitions of parks or other facilities for recreational purposes shall be at the expense of the individual government or by separate contract between the parties, or any combination thereof.
Sec. 2-318. - Parks and recreation department.
The current and future employees of the city parks and recreation department and the current director shall continue to be considered employees of the city and subject to all of the benefits granted city employees and the personnel rules, regulations and procedures of the city, including its grievance procedure. Upon a vacancy in the office of director, the city and county will jointly conduct a solicitation phase for the hiring of a director. The city manager and county manager will jointly hire any new director.
Sec. 2-319. - Director's duties.
The director shall be responsible for the general organization and staffing for the parks and recreation program, including, but not limited to, the following:
(1)
Report to and be responsible to the city manager.
(2)
Develop a plan for the parks and recreation program.
(3)
Prepare an annual budget for the parks and recreation program. In the preparation of the budget, the director will work closely with the county manager, city manager and the parks and recreation board. The budget will be submitted on the format approved by the county finance department.
(4)
Maintain complete and accurate records, under the supervision of the city's private sector certified public accountant, of all activities of the parks and recreation program and all receipts and disbursements of funds. A copy of such audit is to be furnished to the county each year.
(5)
Oversee the execution of the parks and recreation program plan.
(6)
Attend the monthly meeting of the advisory board and respond to their suggestions and inquiries.
(7)
Make monthly reports to the advisory board, city manager and county manager, as they may require.
(8)
Such other duties as may be prescribed by the city manager.
Secs. 2-320—2-330. - Reserved.
DIVISION 4. - TREE COMMISSION

Sec. 2-331. - Creation and purpose.
Effective the 9 th day of August, 1984 there is hereby created a commission to be known and designated as the City of Valdosta Tree Commission and to be herein referred to as the "commission" for the purpose of regulating the planting, maintenance and removal of trees and shrubs in public places; providing for the pruning and removal of trees on private property which endangers public safety and to generally improve the general welfare and health of the people of the City of Valdosta and Lowndes County.
(Code 1976, § 2-8061; Ord. No. 84-25, § 1, 8-9-1984; Ord. No. 2016-1, § 1, 1-21-2016)
Sec. 2-332. - Members; terms; vacancies.
The commission shall consist of ten members as follows: Eight members shall be appointed by Mayor and Council of the City of Valdosta and two City of Valdosta employees shall be designated by the city manager to serve as ex-officio members. The members appointed by mayor and council shall be citizens of the City of Valdosta, and the original respective terms of the members were as follows:
(1)
One member for one year;
(2)
Two members for three years;
(3)
Four members for four years.
Thereafter upon the expiration of such terms, successors shall be appointed for a term of four years by the mayor and council. Vacancies caused by death, resignation or otherwise, shall be filled for the unexpired term by mayor and council.
(Code 1976, § 2-8062; Ord. No. 84-25, § 2, 8-9-1984; Ord. No. 86-22, 4-10-1986; Ord. No. 2016-1, § 2, 1-21-2016)
Sec. 2-333. - Powers and authority.
The commission herein created shall have the power and authority:
(1)
To study and determine the problems and needs of the City of Valdosta for the planting of trees and shrubberies in public places.
(2)
To recommend to the proper authorities and officials of the City of Valdosta, the types and kinds of trees or shrubs to be planted upon the right-of-way of municipal streets, public parks, and public facilities.
(3)
To assist the city in a program to protect and maintain existing trees, shrubs and green areas on all city owned property and public properties.
(4)
To recommend to mayor and council removal of any trees or shrubbery deemed to be hazardous to the public safety or public health.
(5)
To assist the proper officials and make available to the general public news and information regarding the selection, planting and maintenance of trees, shrubbery and other greenery within the corporate limits of the city; to recommend from time to time as necessary ordinances and other programs that will be beneficial to protect and maintain existing trees, shrubs and green areas and to encourage the development of private property owners of landscaped areas with trees and shrubs.
(Code 1976, § 2-8063; Ord. No. 84-25, § 3, 8-9-1984; Ord. No. 2016-1, § 3, 1-21-2016)
Sec. 2-334. - Officers and meetings.
Upon appointment of the initial commission by mayor and council, said commission shall meet and elect a chairman (non-voting unless there is a tie), vice chair, treasurer, and secretary. The commission shall then provide for the rules and procedures for the holding of regular and special meetings of the commission as deemed advisable and necessary.
(Code 1976, § 2-8064; Ord. No. 84-25, § 4, 8-9-1984; Ord. No. 2016-1, § 4, 1-21-2016)
Secs. 2-335—2-360. - Reserved.
DIVISION 5. - PLANNING COMMISSION

Sec. 2-361. - Creation of the Greater Lowndes Planning Commission.
There is established a Greater Lowndes Planning Commission to succeed to the general powers, duties and responsibilities of the former Greater Lowndes County Planning Commission, and as more fully set forth in this division. All acts, decisions, determinations and findings heretofore made by the abolished Greater Lowndes County Planning Commission are ratified and confirmed and shall remain in force and effect until changed. Any unfinished business of the abolished Greater Lowndes County Planning Commission, including any scheduled hearings or hearings in progress, shall be assumed, continued and carried out by the new commission. All 13 membership appointments of the abolished Greater Lowndes County Planning Commission shall continue uninterruptedly until the expiration date of their term as original members of the new commission. Future membership appointments and reappointments, including the addition of a 14th member representing the City of Dasher, shall continue as prescribed in section 2-362.
(Ord. No. 96-56, § 2, 12-5-1996)
Sec. 2-362. - Membership.
The planning commission shall consist of 14 appointed members: five members appointed from the county commission, five from the mayor and council, two appointed by the mayor and council of the City of Hahira, one appointed by the mayor and council of the City of Lake Park, and one appointed by the mayor and council of the City of Dasher. All members shall be appointed for five-year terms and until their successors are appointed.
(Ord. No. 96-56, § 3, 12-5-1996)
Sec. 2-363. - Compensation.
All members of the planning commission shall serve without compensation but shall be reimbursed for actual expenses incurred in connection with their official duties.
(Ord. No. 96-56, § 4, 12-5-1996)
Sec. 2-364. - Organization, rules, staff and officers.
The planning commission shall elect from its members a chairman and a vice-chairman, each to serve for a term of one calendar year and until a successor is elected. The commission shall meet at least once each month at the call of the chairman and at such other times as the chairman or majority of commission members shall determine. The commission shall adopt rules and regulations for the conduct of its meetings and the transaction of its business, and shall keep records of its resolutions, motions, transactions, findings, determinations and recommendations. These records shall be public records. The commission may appoint such employees and staff as it deems necessary for its work, within the budget provided for its operation by the respective member governments, or other funds lawfully available to it.
(Ord. No. 96-56, § 5, 12-5-1996)
Sec. 2-365. - Finances.
In the performance of its duties, the planning commission may cooperate with, contract with, and accept funds from federal, state, local, public or semi-public agencies, or private individuals or corporations, and may expend such funds and carry out cooperative undertakings and contracts with any of them within its lawful powers. The commission is authorized to make expenditures for the purchase of required equipment and supplies, all, with exception of gifts to the commission or contract receipts, to be within the amounts appropriated for the purpose by the member governments of the commission. Notwithstanding any other provisions of this division to the contrary, all checks or other orders of the commission for the withdrawal of money from banking institutions shall be signed in accordance with the commission's operating procedures as authorized by the member governments.
(Ord. No. 96-56, § 6, 12-5-1996)
Sec. 2-366. - Powers, duties and responsibilities.
(a)
It shall be the function and duty of the planning commission, subject to the direction and control of the respective member governments, to make surveys and studies of existing conditions and future developments and to prepare such plans for physical, social and economic growth as will best promote the public health, safety, morals, convenience, prosperity, general welfare, efficiency and economy in the development of the member governments. In particular, the commission shall have the power and duty, subject to the direction and control of the member governments, to:
(1)
Prepare and maintain a local comprehensive plan for each of the member governments in accordance with state guidelines and regulations, and make recommendations on any amendments to such plan.
(2)
Prepare and recommend to the member governments the adoption of amendments or revisions to local development regulations, including zoning ordinances and maps, subdivision regulations, special ordinances, and the like.
(3)
Prepare and recommend to the member governments for the adoption of subdivision plats, or official maps, and amendments, showing the exact location of the boundary lines of existing, proposed, extended, widened, narrowed or closed streets, public open spaces, or public building sites.
(b)
The commission shall have such additional duties and responsibilities and shall perform such additional functions and services as may be called for by the member governments.
(Ord. No. 96-56, § 7, 12-5-1996)
Secs. 2-367—2-390. - Reserved.
DIVISION 6. - HISTORIC PRESERVATION COMMISSION

Sec. 2-391. - Creation and purpose.
There is created a commission to be known as the Valdosta Historic District Preservation Commission, which shall be part of the planning function of the city.
(Code 1976, § 2-8070)
Sec. 2-392. - Members; terms; vacancies.
The historic preservation commission shall consist of seven members appointed by the mayor and council, who shall be residents of the city and who have demonstrated special interest, experience or education in history, architecture, or the preservation of historic resources. The term of office for each commission member shall be three years. On appointment of the first commission, two members shall be appointed for a term of three years, two members for a term of two years, and two members for a term of one year. All appointments thereafter shall be for a term of three years. In the event of a vacancy, the appointment shall be for the unexpired term in the same manner as the initial appointment. Members of the commission shall serve without compensation, although they may be reimbursed for expenses incurred.
(Code 1976, § 2-8071; Ord. No. 85-5, 2-13-1985)
Sec. 2-393. - Powers.
The commission created in this division shall be authorized to:
(1)
Prepare and maintain an inventory of all property within the city having the potential for designation as historic property.
(2)
Recommend to the mayor and council specific places, districts, buildings, structures, objects or works of art to be designated by ordinance as historic properties or historic districts.
(3)
Review applications for certificates of appropriateness, and grant or deny such applications in accordance with the provisions of this division.
(4)
Recommend to the mayor and council that the designation of any place, district, site, building, structure, objects or works of art as an historic property or as an historic district or as part of an historic district be revoked or removed.
(5)
Promote the acquisition by the city of facade easements and conservation easements in accordance with the provisions of the Facade and Conservation Easement Act of 1976 (O.C.G.A. § 44-10-1 et seq.).
(6)
Conduct educational programs on historical properties located within the city and on general historic preservation activities.
(7)
Make such investigations and studies on matters relating to historic preservation, including the consultation with historic preservation experts as the mayor and council or the commission itself may deem necessary or appropriate for the purposes of preserving historic resources.
(8)
Seek out local, state, federal and private funds for historic preservation and make recommendations to the mayor and council concerning the most appropriate uses of any funds acquired.
(9)
Submit to the historic preservation section of the department of natural resources a list of historic properties or historic districts designated and review and make comments to this agency concerning the nomination of properties within its jurisdiction to the National Register of Historic Places.
(10)
Perform historic preservation activities as the official agency of the city historic preservation programs.
(11)
Participate in private, state and federal historic preservation programs and with the consent of the mayor and council enter into agreements to do so.
(Code 1976, § 2-8072)
Sec. 2-394. - Adoption of rules and standards.
The historic preservation commission shall adopt rules and standards for the transaction of its business and for consideration of application for designations and certificates of appropriateness, such as bylaws, removal of membership, provisions, and designated guidelines and criteria. The commission shall have the authority to adopt rules and standards without amendment to this division.
(Code 1976, § 2-8073)
Sec. 2-395. - Officers and meetings; public records.
The historic preservation commission shall also provide for the time and place of its regular meetings and a method for calling of special meetings. The commission shall elect such officers as it deems appropriate from among its members. A quorum of the commission shall consist of a majority of the members. All meetings of the commission shall be public meetings, and a public record shall be kept of the commission's resolutions, proceedings and actions.
(Code 1976, § 2-8074)
Sec. 2-396. - Conflict of interest.
At any time the historic preservation commission reviews a project or application in which a member of the commission has ownership or other vested interest, or that member is employed by a financial institution that may be considering financing of the project or application, that particular member shall be prohibited from voting on the particular project or application.
(Code 1976, § 2-8075)
Sec. 2-397. - Designation of historic districts and properties; criteria for selection of historic districts; preliminary research by commission.
The historic preservation commission shall compile and collect information and conduct surveys of historic resources within the city. The commission shall present to the mayor and council recommendations for historic districts and properties. Prior to the commission's recommendations of an historic district or historic property to the mayor and council for designation, the commission shall prepare a report consisting of:
(1)
A physical description of the property or district.
(2)
A statement of historical, cultural, architectural and/or aesthetic significance of the property or district.
(3)
A map showing district boundaries and classifications of individual properties in the districts, or showing boundaries of individual historic properties.
(4)
A statement justifying district or individual property boundaries.
(5)
Representative photographs of the district or properties.
(Code 1976, § 2-8076)
Sec. 2-398. - Boundaries of an historic district.
Boundaries of an historic district shall be included in the separate ordinances designating such districts and shall be shown on the official zoning map of the city.
(Code 1976, § 2-8077)
Secs. 2-399—2-420. - Reserved.
DIVISION 7. - KEEP LOWNDES-VALDOSTA BEAUTIFUL

Sec. 2-421. - Created.
A Keep Lowndes-Valdosta Beautiful (KLVB) Commission is created for the purpose of assisting the city and the county in establishing community-wide policies for the purpose of decreasing the amount of loose refuse in the city and county and for the purpose of promoting and establishing beautification programs for the city and county.
(Ord. No. 97-12, § I, 4-10-1997)
Sec. 2-422. - Duties.
(a)
The duties of the KLVB commission shall be to:
(1)
Promote public interest in the general improvement of the environment of the city and the county;
(2)
Initiate, plan, direct and coordinate education programs for litter control for the community on a sustainable basis;
(3)
Implement and maintain the Keep America Beautiful System;
(4)
Study, investigate and develop plans for improving the health, sanitation, safety and cleanliness of the city and the county by beautifying the streets, highways, alleys, river and stream banks, lots, yards and other similar places in the city and county;
(5)
Aid in the prevention of fires, diseases and other casualties by the removal and elimination of trash and other debris from the streets, highways, alleys, lots, yards, plots and other similar places;
(6)
Encourage the placing, planting and/or preservation of trees, flowers, plants, shrubbery and other objects or ornamentation in the city and county; and
(7)
Advise and recommend plans and cooperate with other agencies for the beautification of the city and county.
(b)
Nothing in this section shall be construed to abridge or change the powers and duties of other commissions, departments, boards and like agencies of the city or county, or the effect of other ordinances or resolutions regarding these matters adopted prior to April 10, 1997.
(Ord. No. 97-12, § II, 4-10-1997)
Sec. 2-423. - Authority.
In order to perform its duties, the KLVB commission shall have the following authority:
(1)
To adopt bylaws to facilitate the attainment of its purposes and functions.
(2)
To initiate, plan, direct and coordinate community-wide efforts to achieve its goal.
(3)
To solicit and accept donations and appropriations of money, services, products, property and facilities for expenditure and use by the commission for the fulfillment of commission purposes.
(4)
To make recommendations to city and county governing bodies as well as to the private sector regarding measures which it deems necessary to accomplish its objectives.
(Ord. No. 97-12, § III, 4-10-1997)
Sec. 2-424. - Membership.
The KLVB commission shall consist of not more than 12 voting members who shall be residents of the city and the county and who shall serve without remuneration. Four of the members shall be appointed by the city council, and four members shall be appointed by the board of commissioners of the county. Four members shall be appointed by the sitting KLVB commission. In addition to the appointed members of the commission, the solid waste manager for the county and the director of public works for the city shall serve as nonvoting ex officio members of the commission.
(Ord. No. 97-12, § IV, 4-10-1997)
Sec. 2-425. - Terms of office of members.
The terms of office of the members of the KLVB commission shall generally be for three-year staggered terms, with approximately one-third of the membership being appointed every year. The terms of four of the 12 voting initial members selected will expire on June 30, 1998; terms of four of the initial members will expire on June 30, 1999; terms of the remaining four of the initial members will expire on June 30, 2000. Members may serve a maximum of two consecutive terms or six years. Members shall hold office until their successors have been appointed and qualified. The appropriate governing authority shall fill each vacancy from a list of names submitted by the commission.
(Ord. No. 97-12, § V, 4-10-1997)
Sec. 2-426. - Officers.
The KLVB commission shall elect from its membership a chairman and such other officers as may be desired. Officers shall be elected on a fiscal year (July 1—June 30) basis for a term of one year but may be reelected for succeeding terms.
(Ord. No. 97-12, § VI, 4-10-1997)
Sec. 2-427. - Disbursal of funds; records.
(a)
The KLVB commission shall have the power to receive and disburse funds within the limitations of appropriations, gifts and grants which have been previously approved jointly by the city and the county, and in connection with projects or undertakings. Within the same limitation, the commission shall be empowered to contract with any private or public agency with the approval of the city and/or the county to the extent required for its proper operation and to create and contract with not-for-profit corporations.
(b)
The commission shall keep such records as will enable it to make such reports and accountings as are requested by the city and/or the county, which shall be subject to verification and examination by auditors of the city and/or the county.
(c)
Any project or undertaking begun by the commission shall be terminated at any time upon the decision of either the board of commissioners of the county or the city council that its continuance is not in the public interest.
(Ord. No. 97-12, § VII, 4-10-1997)
Secs. 2-428—2-450. - Reserved.
DIVISION 8. - AIRPORT AUTHORITY

DIVISION 9. - METROPOLOTIAN PLANNING ORGANIZATION

Sec. 2-471. - Policy committee established.
The City of Valdosta and Lowndes County do hereby establish a Metropolitan Planning Organization (MPO) Policy Committee to ensure a continuing, cooperative, and comprehensive transportation planning process for the Valdosta/Lowndes MPO and that said policy committee be empowered to establish a technical coordinating committee, a citizens advisory committee and other ad hoc or advisory committees as required or deemed necessary. The MPO policy committee will:
(1)
Be the forum for cooperative decision-making and shall consist of principal elected officials of the participating local general governments within the Valdosta/Lowndes County MPO area and appointed officials of designated agencies, which oversee or operate major transportation modes within the area. The MPO policy committee:
a.
Reviews and approves all transportation study programs;
b.
Ensures that the transportation program is current and responsive to laws, rules, regulations;
c.
Designates and prioritizes transportation improvement projects recommended in the planning process;
d.
Serves as liaison between the planning process and the governmental units and designates membership and operations of the technical coordinating committee, the citizens advisory committee and other ad hoc study committees.
(2)
Consist of the following members:
a.
Chairman, Lowndes County Board of Commissioners (or representative);
b.
Mayor, City of Valdosta (or representative);
c.
Annually rotating small city mayor (mayor or representative of Hahira, Lake Park, Remerton or Dasher);
d.
Lowndes County Manager;
e.
Valdosta Manager;
f.
SGRDC executive director;
g.
Georgia DOT Commissioner (or representative);
h.
Nonvoting members:
1.
Division administrator—Federal Highway Administration;
2.
Regional administrator—Federal Transit Administration.
(Res. No. 2003-16, 11-20-2003)

DIVISION 10. - PUBLIC ART ADVISORY COMMITTEE

Sec. 2-476. - Purpose of program.
It is the intent and purpose to create in the City of Valdosta a program to further the purchase and foster the appreciation of works of public art. Also to promote the creation of works of art and the purchase of same for the citizens and the City of Valdosta in order to promote the aesthetic value to the entire community and encourage the preservation and protection of works of public art and to encourage economic development.
(Ord. No. 2010-9, § 1, 2-25-2010)
Sec. 2-477. - Public art advisory committee.
(a)
There is hereby created a public art advisory committee to be appointed by Mayor and Council of the City of Valdosta to administer the public art program.
(b)
The public art advisory committee shall consist of nine members. Five members appointed from the membership of the Lowndes-Valdosta Art Commission, including at least two professional artists and at least two members from the Board of Directors of the Lowndes-Valdosta Art Commission. One member from the general membership of the Lowndes-Valdosta Art Commission. Four members of the public art advisory committee shall be members of the community at large.
(1)
The initial board shall be appointed by mayor and council as follows: One professional artist who is a member of the Lowndes-Valdosta Art Commission, one board member of Lowndes-Valdosta Art Commission and one member from the community at large for a term of three years; one professional artist who is a member of the Lowndes-Valdosta Art Commission, one member of the Board of Lowndes-Valdosta Art Commission and one member from the community at large for a term of two years; one member at large from the Lowndes-Valdosta Art Commission for a term of one year; two members from the community of the City of Valdosta for a term of one year. Thereafter members shall be appointed to three-year terms.
(2)
Ex-officio members shall include a representative of the following departments to be determined by the city manager and the department head: Finance, planning and zoning, and public works. Ex-officio members shall also include the city manager and the mayor or the mayor's representative as appointed by the mayor.
(3)
Five members shall reside, own property or work within the corporate limits of the City of Valdosta. The remaining four members shall be from the community at large.
(Ord. No. 2010-9, § 1, 2-25-2010)
Sec. 2-478. - Powers and duties.
The public art advisory committee shall have the following powers and duties:
(1)
The committee shall act as an advisory body to the mayor and the city council and as an advocate for public arts and aesthetics in all aspects of city life. It shall encourage, develop and support a variety of programs which reflect the cultural art needs and experience of all citizens and shall encourage participation by all citizens in the cultural art activities carried on in the city;
(2)
Develop, maintain and update a public art master plan identifying locations for public artwork;
(3)
Develop public art program guidelines and amendments thereto as deemed appropriate;
(4)
Approve the location and installation of artwork based on public art program guidelines; and
(5)
Request annual funding from various public, private, foundation and other potential sources to support the capital and operational needs of the public art program.
(Ord. No. 2010-9, § 1, 2-25-2010)
Sec. 2-479. - Election of officers, quorum, bylaws, procedures of meetings.
Each year, the membership of the public art advisory committee shall meet as soon as practical after the effective date of this division and shall elect from among themselves a chairperson and vice-chairperson, each of whom shall continue as voting members. The chairperson shall preside over the meetings of the committee and the vice-chairperson shall preside in his or her absence. Such officer shall have such other powers, duties and responsibilities as are set out elsewhere herein. The committee shall also choose a secretary who may or may not be a member of the committee to keep minutes and records of the committee. Five members of the committee shall constitute a quorum. A majority of a quorum may exercise any and all powers of the committee. The committee may adopt its own bylaws, rules and procedures to govern internal workings in the conduct of its business. The committee shall meet at least quarterly and at such other times as it may deem necessary.
(Ord. No. 2010-9, § 1, 2-25-2010)
Sec. 2-480. - Compensation of members.
The members shall receive no compensation but shall be reimbursed from the funds of the committee for reasonable and necessary expenses incurred in pursuing the business of the committee.
(Ord. No. 2010-9, § 1, 2-25-2010)
Sec. 2-481. - Funding.
(a)
There shall be created a public art fund administered by the public art advisory committee which shall consist of all contributions received from cash grants and donations to the program for public art projects from governmental or private resources, and all other funds allocated by the City of Valdosta through the budgetary process for the purchase and fostering of public art.
(b)
The public art fund shall be used solely for expenses associated with the selection, commissioning, acquisition, transportation, maintenance, education promotion, administrative duties, removal and insurance of works of art.
(Ord. No. 2010-9, § 1, 2-25-2010)
Sec. 2-482. - Ownership of artwork.
Unless otherwise expressly agreed in writing ownership and title of all art acquired through spending of funds in the public art fund shall be vested in the City of Valdosta.
(Ord. No. 2010-9, § 1, 2-25-2010)
Sec. 2-483. - Program guidelines and master plan.
The public art advisory committee may adopt specific criteria for review of proposed permanent public art projects and guidelines therefore.
(1)
The initial guidelines and criteria for review of proposed permanent public art projects shall be as follows:
a.
Relevance of the work to the City of Valdosta, its values, culture and people;
b.
Technical suitability of the work for outdoor display, including its maintenance and conservation requirements; and
c.
Relationship of the work to the site, especially how it serves to activate or enhance the public space.
(2)
In general, a proposed work of public art will not be accepted if it is not original sculpture or painting. If accepted artwork is a multiple, it will only be accepted if it is a limited edition. Generally reproductions; unlimited editions; decorative, ornamental and functional elements of architecture; directional elements, such as super graphics, signage and color-coding usually are not considered artwork unless done by an artist.
a.
Acceptable media include:
1.
Sculpture: in the round, bas-relief, mobile, fountain, kinetic, electronic;
2.
Mosaic: ceramic, glass, metal, wood, mixed media;
3.
Fountains or water elements;
4.
Crafts: Clay fiber, textiles, wood, metal, plastics, stained glass;
5.
Mixed media;
6.
Earthworks and environmental artworks;
7.
Decorative, ornamental or functional elements designed by an artist; and
8.
Murals and paintings.
b.
Unacceptable criteria: The artwork does not meet professional standards for public art due to one or more of the following:
1.
Faults of design or workmanship which will either pose a public safety hazard or diminish the value of the work.
2.
The possibility that the artwork is fraudulent or not authentic.
3.
The artwork appears to be of inferior quality relative to the quality of other works in the city's collection.
c.
The public art advisory committee will consider the following questions and criteria when conducting its review:
1.
Aesthetic quality and artistic merit;
2.
Placement and sitting;
3.
Fabrication, handling and installation;
4.
Maintenance requirements;
5.
Conservation and preservation;
6.
Liability and safety; and
7.
Community.
(Ord. No. 2010-9, § 1, 2-25-2010)
Sec. 2-484. - Definitions.
As used in this division the following terms shall mean as follows:
Artists or professional artist. Practitioner in the visual arts, generally recognized by critics and peers as a professional of serious intent and ability. Indications of a person's status as a professional artist include, but are not limited to, income realized through the commission of artwork, frequent or consistent art exhibitions, placement of artwork in public institutions or museums, receipt of honors and awards, and training in the arts.
Art, artwork or works of art. Tangible creations by artists exhibiting the highest quality of skill and aesthetic principles and includes all forms of the visual arts conceived in any medium, material or combination thereof, including, but not limited to, paintings, sculptures, engravings, carvings, frescos, stained glass, mosaics, mobiles, tapestries, murals, photographs, video projections, digital images, bas-relief, high relief, fountains, kinetics, collages, drawings, monuments erected to commemorate a person or an event, functional furnishings, such as artist designed searing and pavers, architectural elements designed by an artist, and artist designed landforms or landscape elements.
(1)
The following shall not be considered artwork or works of art for purposes of the program:
a.
Reproductions or unlimited copies of original artwork.
(Ord. No. 2010-9, § 1, 2-25-2010)
