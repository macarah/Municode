Editor's note— Ord. No. 766, § 1, adopted June 8, 2015, enacted new provisions designated as art. VIII, §§ 14-120—14-122. Inasmuch as article and sections so numbered already exist, said provisions have been redesignated as art. IX, §§ 14-150—14-152 at the discretion of the editor.

Sec. 14-150. - Sale of fireworks.
The sale of fireworks is permitted as authorized by Georgia state law as follows:
(1)
All fireworks shall be sold in the district having zoning classification C-2.
(2)
Temporary structures for the sale of fireworks may only be set up and sold within one thousand (1,000) feet of a fire hydrant or fire department unless the city fire marshal shall permit the designated location in writing.
(3)
Non-profit groups benefitting from the sale of fireworks shall directly participate in operating a temporary fireworks retail stand.
(Ord. No. 766, § 1, 6-8-15)
Sec. 14-151. - License to sale fireworks.
(a)
The license fee to sell consumer fireworks at a permanent location such as a retail sale facility or store shall be one hundred dollars ($100.00) per location. The application for license shall be available at city hall and shall issue upon submission of a properly completed application.
(b)
The license fee to sell consumer fireworks at a temporary retail sales stand shall be five hundred dollars ($500.00) per location. The application for license shall be available at City Hall and shall issue upon submission of a properly completed application.
(Ord. No. 766, § 1, 6-8-15)
Sec. 14-152. - Use of fireworks.
The use of fireworks is permitted only on the days and during such hours as is permitted by Georgia state law. The use of fireworks shall be prohibited at such locations as are prohibited by Georgia state law.
(Ord. No. 766, § 1, 6-8-15)
