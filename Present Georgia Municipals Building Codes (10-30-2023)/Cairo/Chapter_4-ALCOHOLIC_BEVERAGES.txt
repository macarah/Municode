ARTICLE I. - PURPOSE AND LICENSING

Sec. 4-1. - General policies and purpose.
(a)
Alcoholic beverages may be sold, manufactured, distributed and imported in the City of Cairo only after the issuance of a license by the city to a licensee who complies with the rules and regulations of this chapter, and with the licensing, regulatory and revenue requirements of the State of Georgia.
(b)
Notwithstanding anything in this chapter to the contrary, the sale of alcoholic beverages in the city is a privilege and not a right, and the issuance of a license hereunder shall not create any property rights in the licensee; however, such privilege shall not be exercised except in accordance with the licensing, regulatory, and revenue requirements of this Code.
(c)
The city council designates the city manager for the purpose of administering this chapter. Any powers granted to or duties imposed upon the city manager may be delegated by the city manager to other city personnel. The city manager shall be responsible for the interpretation of the requirements, standards, definitions, or any other provision of this chapter, unless that authority is provided to another administrative official.
(d)
The holder of every alcoholic beverage license hereunder shall have available in his place of business at all times a copy of this chapter, and the holder, as well as all employees and agents of the business, shall be conclusively presumed, for purposes of this chapter, to have at all times complete and full knowledge of all provisions of this chapter. The license holder shall be responsible for compliance herewith by all persons on the premises.
(e)
The purposes of this chapter include, but are not limited to, the following:
(1)
Compliance with state law.
(2)
Prevention and control of the sale of alcoholic beverages by unfit persons.
(3)
Protection of the public health, safety, and general welfare.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-2. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Agent, designated means a resident of the City of Cairo, appointed by a licensee who does not live in the City of Cairo, who shall be responsible for any matter relating to the license. This person may also be the managing agent if that person is a resident of Cairo.
Agent, managing means an individual that is at least twenty-one (21) years of age, is a U.S. citizen or an alien lawfully admitted for permanent residency, has day-to-day managerial authority over the business conducted on the licensed premises, including the sale of alcoholic beverages, and is employed full-time by the licensed business. This person may also be the applicant or owner.
Alcohol means ethyl alcohol, hydrated oxide of ethyl, or spirits of wine; from whatever source or by whatever process it is produced.
Alcoholic beverage means any beverage containing alcohol and shall include but not be limited to beer or malt beverages, wines, fortified wines, liquor or distilled spirits as defined in this section.
Art shop means a retail business devoted exclusively to providing art education that is limited to instruction in painting, sculpture and similar crafts. An art shop may also sell and display portraits, paintings, sculptures, art supplies and similar artwork and crafts. An art shop shall not allow activities that would cause the business to be an "adult entertainment establishment" as defined in section 4-42 of this Code.
Authorized catered alcohol event means an event at a location not otherwise licensed for consumption of alcoholic beverages by the drink at which alcoholic beverages are furnished, for consideration, and sold, dispensed, or provided free of charge to persons present at the event, by the drink, pursuant to a permit obtained under this section.
Barrel means thirty-one (31) gallons in the case of malt beverages, and fifty-three (53) gallons in the case of distilled spirits.
Bed and breakfast (typically shortened to B&B or BnB) means an overnight accommodation and a morning meal in a dwelling unit provided to transients for compensation. Bed and breakfasts are often private family homes.
Brewer means a manufacturer of malt beverages.
Brewery means a facility where malt beverages are manufactured that produces more than ten thousand (10,000) barrels (or the equivalent) per year of malt beverages.
Brewpub means any eating establishment in which malt beverages are manufactured on premises, subject to the barrel production limitation prescribed in section 4-63, and are offered for sale to wholesalers; or to consumers for consumption on the premises along with the sale of food; or by the package for consumption off the premises. A brewpub is also authorized to sell distilled spirits, malt beverages, or wines, and which derives at least fifty (50) percent of its total annual gross food and beverage sales from the sale of prepared meals or food; provided, however, that when determining the total annual gross food and beverage sales, barrels of malt beverages sold to licensed wholesale dealers or to the public for consumption off the premises, shall not be used.
Broker means any person who purchases or obtains an alcoholic beverage from an importer, distillery, brewery, or winery and sells the alcoholic beverage to another broker, importer, or wholesaler without having custody of the alcoholic beverage or maintaining a stock of the alcoholic beverage.
Business means any person, corporation, partnership, or other legal entity which exerts substantial efforts within the city, engages in, causes to be engaged in, and/or represents or holds out to the public to be engaged in any occupation or activity with the object of gain or benefit, either directly or indirectly.
Brown-bagging or BYOB means the possession of, or bringing, taking, or carrying, of any alcoholic beverage into any business licensed to operate within the City of Cairo, or at a location different from where said alcoholic beverage was purchased; provided that bringing an alcoholic beverage into a house, apartment, room or other unit designated for private residential occupancy, or that shall be operated by a not for profit organization, shall not fall within this definition.
Case means a box or receptacle containing not more than two hundred eighty-eight (288) ounces of malt beverages on the average.
Caterer of food means any person who prepares food for consumption off its premises.
Caterer of licensed alcoholic beverages means any retail alcohol dealer licensed by the State of Georgia and holding a license from a Georgia county or municipality to sell or dispense alcoholic beverages. An off-premises special event permit may also be required to sell alcoholic beverages in connection with an authorized catered function.
Church means a body of communicants gathered into church order: united under one (1) form of government by the profession of the same faith and the observance of the same ritual and ceremonies, place where persons regularly assemble in a facility for worship, congregation, or organization for religious purposes.
City special event means an event, held on designated city property, which may include streets and rights-of-way located in the city's downtown business district or a special entertainment district that is organized and sponsored by the City of Cairo or the downtown development authority (DDA), consistent with the provisions of section 4-161 and other applicable provisions of this chapter.
College campus means a collection of two (2) or more buildings used for educational purposes, on either one (1) parcel or contiguous parcels that are under the same ownership by either a public or private institution of higher learning.
Distilled spirits or liquor means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than twenty-four (24) percent alcohol by volume.
Distiller means a manufacturer of distilled spirits.
Establishment means any physical location or section thereof for the operation of a business.
Farm winery means a winery which makes at least forty (40) percent of its annual production from agricultural produce grown in the state where the winery is located.
Food caterer means any person, or individual, company, corporation, association, partnership or other legal entity, who prepares food for consumption off the premises.
Fortified wine, classed as liquor, means any wines or other beverages made by fermentation to which there has been added distilled spirits, and where the alcohol content is more than twenty-one (21) percent alcohol by volume. "Fortified wine" includes, but is not limited to, brandy.
Fruit grower means any person who grows peaches, apples, pears, grapes, or other perishable fruits in this state and who manufactures distilled spirits from the perishable fruits grown in this state.
Gallon or wine gallon means a United States gallon of liquid measure equivalent to the volume of two hundred thirty-one (231) cubic inches or the nearest equivalent metric measurement.
Georgia farm winery means a farm winery which is licensed by the state to manufacture wine in Georgia.
Governing authority means the mayor and council of the City of Cairo.
Growler means a reusable, resealable, and professionally sanitized glass jug used to transport malt beverages or wine for off-premises consumption that is not to exceed sixty-four (64) ounces, and that is filled with malt beverages or wine from a keg by a licensee, or an employee of a licensee, with a malt beverage and/or wine license for consumption off premises issued by the City of Cairo, and purchased by a licensee from a duly licensed wholesaler.
Hard cider means an alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of the juice of apples, containing not more than six (6) percent alcohol by volume, including, but not limited to, flavored or carbonated cider. For purposes of this chapter, hard cider shall be deemed a malt beverage. The term does not include "sweet cider."
Home brewed malt beverage, for purposes of this chapter, shall be limited to malt beverages produced by an individual(s) pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 3-5-4.
Hotel or motel, fifty-room, shall mean that business establishment containing a minimum of fifty (50) guest rooms for transient occupancy and constructed to normal size and condition for the hotel or motel industry.
Housing authority means any property containing three hundred (300) housing units or fewer, which property is owned or operated by a housing authority created by article 1 of chapter 3 of title 8 of the Housing Authorities Law.
Importer means any person who imports an alcoholic beverage into this state from a foreign country and sells the alcoholic beverage to another importer, broker or wholesaler and who warehouses a stock of the alcoholic beverage.
Legal age, as used in this chapter, is identical with the legal age provided in the acts of the legislature of the State of Georgia relative to alcoholic beverages and as the same may from time to time be amended.
License shall mean the authorization by the governing authority of the City of Cairo to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages.
Licensee means any person who holds a license from the city to engage in the selling, manufacturing, distributing or importing at retail or wholesale, of any alcoholic beverages.
Lounge means a separate room connected with a part of, and adjacent to, a restaurant with all booths, stools, and tables being unobstructed and open to view. All lounges shall be air conditioned and have a seating capacity of at least forty (40).
Malt beverage or beer means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than fourteen (14) percent alcohol by volume, and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer and strong beer. The term "malt beverage" does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Manufacture means to produce or bottle malt beverages/beer, wine, or distilled spirits/liquor.
Manufacturer means:
(1)
In the case of distilled spirits, any person engaged in distilling, rectifying, or blending any distilled spirits;
(2)
In the case of malt beverages, any brewer; and
(3)
In the case of wine, any vintner; provided, however, that a vintner that blends wine with distilled spirits to produce a fortified wine shall not be considered a manufacturer of distilled spirits.
Minor means any person less than twenty-one (21) years of age.
Nonprofit, charitable and civic organization, means a bona fide nonprofit civic organization which is exempt from Federal Income Tax pursuant to the provisions of subsection (c), (d), or (e) of 26 U.S.C. Section 501 of the Internal Revenue Code, as amended.
Open-air cafe means a premises located within a pedestrian court area and adjacent sidewalk of a restaurant where food is available for purchase during all hours of operation and where alcoholic beverages may be purchased from a restaurant facility for consumption within the open-air cafe.
Package means a bottle, can, keg, barrel, or other original consumer container.
Person means any individual, firm, partnership, cooperative, nonprofit membership corporation, social club, joint venture, association, company, corporation, limited liability company, limited liability partnerships, agency, syndicate, estate, trust, business trust, receiver, fiduciary or other group or combination acting as a unit, body politic, or political subdivision, the plural as well as the singular member, whether public, private, or quasi-public.
Premises means one (1) physically identifiable place of business operated by the same ownership and overall management consisting of one (1) or more contiguous rooms with only one (1) address registered as a single place of business. "Premises" is further defined to include the following areas:
(1)
In the case of hotels or motels of fifty (50) rooms, or private clubs, the boundary lines, as well as the swimming pool which is owned by said hotel, motel or club.
(2)
Outdoor areas or patios approved pursuant to this chapter.
(3)
In the case of a malt beverage and/or wine licensee where a golf course is located adjacent to and contiguous with a restaurant, the playing area of the golf course.
(4)
In the case of an open-air cafe, the pedestrian court area and sidewalk in front of the building in which the restaurant is contained.
Private club, bona fide, means any nonprofit association organized under the laws of this state which:
(1)
Has been in existence at least one (1) year prior to the filing of its application for a license to be issued pursuant to this chapter;
(2)
Has at least seventy-five (75) regular dues-paying members;
(3)
Owns, hires or leases a building or space within a building for the reasonable use of its members, which building or space:
a.
Has suitable kitchen and dining room space and equipment; and
b.
Is staffed with a sufficient number of employees for cooking, preparing and serving meals for its members and guests; and
(4)
Has no member, officer, agent or employee directly or indirectly receiving, in the form of salary or other compensation, any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages beyond a fixed salary.
Private function means any affair where attendance is by invitation only, where no business transactions are conducted during the private function and there is no cost to attend or to consume the food or alcohol either by donations or by any other means. Exception: Should there be an entrance fee or ticket purchase required, an alcohol license and event permit is also required.
Private residence means a house, dwelling or building wherein not less than one (1) family customarily resides. This shall not apply to any of the above which has been unoccupied for a period of twelve (12) consecutive months immediately prior to the filing of an application hereunder.
Resident means a person whose primary residence is within the territorial limits of the city.
Restaurant or eating establishment means any public place kept, used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public as a place where meals are actually and regularly prepared and served, such place being provided with adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining room equipment, air conditioned, having employed therein a sufficient number and kind of employees to prepare, cook and serve suitable food for its guests for every hour they are open. At least one (1) meal per day shall be served at least five (5) days a week, except that restaurants that open for business more than five (5) days a week, shall be required to serve at least one (1) meal per day each day that the restaurant is open, with the exception of holidays, vacation and periods of redecoration, and the serving of such meals shall be the principal business conducted, with the serving of alcoholic beverages to be consumed on the premises as only incidental thereto.
Retail consumption dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages at retail for consumption on the premises only to consumers and not for resale.
Retail package dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages, in unbroken packages, at retail, intended for carry-out, only to consumers and not for resale.
Retail package distilled spirits/liquor store means a retail business establishment owned by an individual, partnership, corporation, association, or other business entity which is primarily engaged in the retail sale of distilled spirits, malt beverages, and wine in unbroken packages, not for consumption on the premises, except as authorized under this chapter.
School shall mean only such state, county, city or other schools as teach the subjects commonly taught in the common schools of this state and shall not include private schools where only specialized subjects such as law, stenography, business, music, art, medicine, dentistry, vocational occupations and other special subjects are taught.
Sidewalk cafe means an area in front of a restaurant, as defined by projecting its interior width outward perpendicular to the street to a point at which it intersects the roadway, or within the sidewalk frontage of the building where the licensed restaurant is located) and used by a restaurant for additional seating.
Special entertainment district means an area designated by the city council where alcohol can be sold and then consumed on the streets and sidewalks within the clearly-marked bounds of the district.
Standard case means six (6) containers of 1.75 liters, twelve (12) containers of seven hundred fifty (750) milliliters, twelve (12) containers of one (1) liter, twenty-four (24) containers of five hundred (500) milliliters, twenty-four (24) containers of three hundred seventy-five (375) milliliters, forty-eight (48) containers of two hundred (200) milliliters, or one hundred twenty (120) containers of fifty (50) milliliters.
Tasting room means an outlet for the promotion of a manufacturer's product by providing samples of such product to the public on the premises where manufactured, or on premises adjacent thereto under common ownership, and for the sale of such product at retail for consumption on the premises and for sale in closed packages for consumption off the premises. Samples can be given free of charge or for a fee. Offsite tasting rooms of a domestic farm winery may only be located within a special entertainment district designated by the council.
Vintner means one (1) who blends wine with distilled spirits to produce a fortified wine.
Wholesaler or wholesale dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages to other wholesale dealers, to retail package dealers, or to retail consumption dealers.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than twenty-one (21) percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. "Wine" includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products. The term "wine" does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at the point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this section.
Wine, fortified, classed as distilled spirits/liquor, means any alcoholic beverage containing more than twenty-one (21) percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term includes, but is not limited to, brandy.
Wine tasting means an event as defined in the rules and regulations of the Georgia Department of Revenue and approved by a permit issued by the city.
Winery means a place for the manufacture of wine.
Winery, farm winery means a winery which makes at least forty (40) percent of its annual production from agricultural produce grown in the state.
Winery, Georgia farm, means a farm winery which is licensed by the state to manufacture wine in Georgia.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-3. - Compliance with chapter required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or offer for sale at wholesale or retail any alcoholic beverages, as defined in section 4-2, which shall include malt beverages/beer, wine, and distilled spirits, within the city without having first complied with the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-4. - Licenses—Required; consideration and approval, conditional approval, subsequent issuance.
(a)
No person shall manufacture, distribute, sell, handle, or possess for sale, or otherwise deal in, alcoholic beverages without first obtaining all applicable licenses or permits required by this chapter.
(b)
Each original application provided for in this chapter shall be considered and passed upon by the mayor and council in regular or special session, legally assembled. Upon approval of the application and payment of the required license fee, the applicant shall be entitled to the issuance of the license; provided, however, the mayor and council reserve the right to approve such issuance of the license or licenses conditioned upon full and final compliance by the licensee with all applicable city ordinances.
(c)
The license fees as required in section 4-12 of the Code of Ordinances of the City of Cairo are due and payable upon approval of the application or by commencement of business.
(d)
The city license issued shall be valid for the license year indicated thereon and shall expire at midnight on the last day of March and is due and payable by April 1 of each year thereafter.
(e)
Each person holding a license or permit pursuant to this chapter shall display the license or permit prominently at all times on the premises for which it is issued.
(f)
A separate license shall be required for each place of business.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-5. - Qualifications for licensing.
(a)
To qualify for an alcoholic beverage license, an applicant shall meet the following:
(1)
Designate a "managing agent", who may also be the applicant or owner of the business, who meets the defined qualifications in section 4-2. In the case of a nonprofit private club, the managing agent may be an officer of the organization in lieu of a full-time employee if the individual is otherwise qualified.
(2)
A licensee for the sale of beer and wine at retail must be a citizen or legal resident alien of the United States possessing authority to own a business, or if the applicant is not a resident of the City of Cairo, the applicant must designate a resident of Cairo as "designated agent" who shall be responsible for any matter relating to the license. All licensed establishments must have and continuously maintain in Cairo a "designated agent" upon whom any process, notice or demand required or permitted by law or under this chapter to be served upon the licensee or owner, may be served. The licensee shall file the name of such agent, along with the written, notarized consent of such agent with the city in such form as may be prescribed. Residency by an applicant within the city issuing the permit or license for beer and wine shall not be a requirement by the city if the applicant designates a resident of the city who shall be responsible for any matter relating to the license.
(3)
Have a certificate of occupancy that has been issued by the city for the premises for which an alcoholic beverage license is sought.
(4)
A certificate of residency is required for a licensee of a retail distilled spirits package store certifying that the applicant has been a bona fide resident of the city.
(b)
Where the application is made on behalf of a corporation, partnership, a limited liability corporation, limited liability partnership, or sole proprietorship, the license shall be issued jointly to the organization and an individual that meets the qualifications of a managing or designated agent.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-6. - Application process.
(a)
Any person desiring to obtain an alcoholic beverage license in the city shall submit a written and signed application on forms provided by the city clerk and in connection therewith, shall, under oath, answer all questions, supply all information, and furnish all certificates, affidavits, and other supporting data as required thereby.
(b)
All applications for licenses to sell alcoholic beverages of any kind shall be filed in person by the applicant with the city clerk and shall contain but not be restricted to the following statements and information:
(1)
The name, age, address and length of residency of applicant.
(2)
The name of the corporation, partnership, sole proprietorship, or other organization applying for the license. Said name shall include the legal name as well as the trade name of the business.
(3)
A statement of whether the applicant or any person with an interest in the application has made application at any previous time for any alcoholic beverage license and the disposition of such application.
(4)
Whether the applicant or any person with an interest in the application has ever been convicted of a crime, other than for traffic violations.
(5)
Whether a previous license issued to the applicant or any person with interest in the application has been revoked by any state or subdivision thereof or by the federal government and the reason therefor.
(6)
The identity of any other person interested directly or indirectly in the profits or losses, or both, of the proposed business.
(7)
The physical address and detailed plans of such building and outside premises, and evidence of ownership of the building, or a document that evidences the right of the applicant to use the premises, including, but not limited to, a deed, lease, sublease, management agreement, or concession agreement, shall be attached to the application.
(8)
The individual owner applying, or one (1) partner, or one (1) corporate officer shall be fingerprinted for city purposes, except that in the case any of the above has current prints on file as a privilege licensee of the city, this requirement may be waived. Notwithstanding this provision, however, city officials may require fingerprints from other partners or corporate officers, and new set of prints from the above referred to privilege licensees. Applicants, by filing an application, agree to produce for oral interrogation any person or persons requested by the police department and considered as being important in the ascertainment of the facts relative to such license. The failure to produce such person or persons within thirty (30) days after being requested to do so shall result in the automatic dismissal of such application.
(9)
The city may require sixty (60) days for processing any application for license required under this section.
(10)
Every new application for an alcoholic beverage license hereunder shall be accompanied by a drawing showing the nearest church, school or college, alcohol treatment center, or housing authority relative to the proposed location of the business showing that it complies with the distance requirements as specified in this chapter. Applications for renewal of licenses, or for premises that have been licensed in the preceding twelve (12) months, are exempted from this subsection.
(c)
All applications for licenses shall indicate which of the following is to be considered for licensing for the sale of:
(1)
Malt beverage, retail package;
(2)
Malt beverage, retail pouring - consumption on the premises;
(3)
Malt beverage, retail pouring - consumption off premises at a catered function by the drink;
(4)
Malt beverage, retail brewpub (includes sale of beer for consumption and package),
(5)
Malt beverage brewery;
(6)
Wine, retail package;
(7)
Wine, retail pouring - consumption on the premises;
(8)
Wine, retail pouring - consumption off premises at a catered function;
(9)
Winery, Georgia farm winery;
(10)
Distilled spirits, (liquor), manufacture (distillery);
(11)
Distilled spirits, (liquor), retail, package;
(12)
Distilled spirits (liquor), retail, pouring - consumption on the premises;
(13)
Distilled spirits (liquor), retail, pouring - off premises at a catered function by the drink.
(14)
Tasting room;
(15)
Beer and/or wine and/or liquor - complimentary consumption on premises in nail and hair salons, or art shops, art studios, art galleries, and bed and breakfast locations.
(d)
Upon filing an original application or reapplication (except renewals) with the city clerk of the City of Cairo, the applicant shall deposit in cash or by check an application fee of sixty dollars ($60.00) for malt beverages/beer and/or wine only; or one hundred dollars ($100.00) for distilled spirits/liquor package or distilled spirits/liquor pouring; or a maximum of one hundred dollars ($100.00) total for all alcohol licenses applied for, to cover the expense of investigation and processing the application, which fee shall not be refundable if the application is denied or withdrawn and shall not apply against the fee of any license granted hereunder. There shall be no investigation fee charged wholesalers licensed by the State of Georgia.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-7. - Additional application requirements for distilled spirits/liquor.
(a)
Distilled spirits/liquor—Package sales. All applications for license made on forms provided by the city clerk, whether original or for renewal, must be accompanied by a full and complete statement under oath of information relative to any and all interests, as hereinbefore defined in this section, in retail liquor package stores. This shall include the names and addresses of all persons with interest in the ownership of the business of selling packaged liquor at retail, together with any interest each person or members of his family has in any other retail liquor store; the ownership of the land and building where such retail business is operated; the amount of rental paid for said land and building and the manner in which the same is determined and to whom and at what intervals it is paid; the name and address (by affidavit from owner, lessor or sub-lessor of such land and building) of all persons having any whole, partial, beneficial or any other interest in and to the land and building on and in which said retail liquor store is to be located; a copy of the proposed lease, and any other information requested by the police department or the mayor and council pertinent to the application under investigation.
(1)
Limitation on number of licenses within a family and/or corporation.
a.
No person and/or member of his family and/or corporation shall own, hold or control any interest whatsoever in more than one (1) package store license in the City of Cairo to engage in the business of selling liquor by the package at retail.
b.
As used in this paragraph, an interest in the license shall be deemed to exist if the person involved is the outright owner of the license, a co-owner of a license, a general or limited partner in a partnership which owns all or any part of a license, a stockholder in any corporation which owns all or any part of a license, an owner or leasing any real estate which is occupied by a retail liquor store, or shares in any income or corpus of any trust fund or estate having any interest in a retail liquor store.
(2)
Citizenship, residence requirement.
a.
No license for the retail sale of liquor by the package shall be granted to any applicant who has not resided continuously within the State of Georgia for a period of twelve (12) months next preceding the date on which the application is made, except that the wholesalers or distillers are exempt from this residency provision. A licensee that sells packaged liquor must remain a resident of the State of Georgia during the tenure of their license.
(3)
No license shall be issued for a new liquor package store, or for the relocation of an existing liquor store, to be located within five hundred (500) yards of any other business licensed to sell package liquor.
(4)
Conducting a public hearing on original application.
a.
No original application for a license hereunder shall be acted upon or issued by the mayor and council until after advertisement and public hearing thereon, as required in subsection (a)(5) of this Code section.
b.
A public hearing upon all original and completed applications for a license hereunder shall be called by the mayor and council for a regular meeting not more than sixty (60) days after the filing of the application.
(5)
Advertisement of notice to secure a retail package dealer's license for sale of distilled spirits by the package; signs posted.
a.
All applicants for original licenses hereunder shall give notice of the purpose of making such application by advertisement, at least once during the thirty (30) days immediately preceding the date of a public hearing to be held by the mayor and council, in the newspaper which publishes the legal advertisements of the city. Such notice shall be in large boldface type and shall contain the location of the proposed business and shall give the name of the applicant, and if a partnership, the names of the partners, and if a corporation, the names of the officers, and the date and time the mayor and council would hear the application. It shall be the responsibility of the applicant for an original license to advertise the notice of hearing required as follows:
"Notice of application for retail license for distilled spirits/liquor package.
The undersigned has made application to the Mayor and Council of the City of Cairo for a retail license for package distilled spirits/liquor at _______. This application will be heard by the Mayor and Council at its meeting to be held at ___ o'clock p.m. on the ___ day of _______, 20___.
/s/ ___________ Owner(s); ___________ Address of Owner(s)".
b.
The applicant shall cause to be placed upon the location of the proposed business at least a week prior to the date of hearing, a sign or signs stating the following:
1.
"PACKAGE DISTILLED SPIRITS/LIQUOR LICENSE APPLIED FOR. HEARING BEFORE THE MAYOR AND COUNCIL FOR THE CITY OF CAIRO, GEORGIA, AT ___ O'CLOCK P.M. ON THE ___ DAY OF _______, 20___."
2.
The sign or signs required by subsection (2) shall be not less that twenty-four (24) inches by thirty-six (36) inches and shall face toward all public streets, alleys, sidewalks or other public property adjoining the proposed location. Such signs shall be placed where they can be easily seen from all public properties adjoining the proposed location.
(6)
Each applicant for an original license shall submit a plot plan and floor plan prepared by an architect, engineer, contractor, surveyor or other qualified person showing all compliance with all requirements contained in this section.
(7)
Minimum requirements; floor space of retail outlet, parking required, adequate refuse storage required, visibility from exterior.
a.
Any structure housing a licensed retail package store, or proposed therefor, under the terms of this section, shall contain not less than one thousand (1,000) square feet of floor space, including customer service and display area, storage area and service areas.
b.
Adequate parking shall be provided as defined elsewhere in this Code. Adequate refuse storage space shall be provided with access for servicing by refuse collection vehicles.
c.
The store so occupied for retail sales shall be constructed so that three (3) entire outside walls can be seen from the exterior, and visibility, as defined elsewhere in this section, must be provided from the exterior into the customer service and display area. Visibility, as required, may not be blocked by any signs, vending machines, displays or other obstructions.
(8)
Completion of proposed licensed premises. Where a building in which a retailer proposes to operate under the provisions of this section is, at the time of application for such license, not in existence, or not yet completed, a license may be approved for such location provided the plans and specifications for the proposed building are filed with the city clerk of Cairo and show clearly a compliance with the other provisions of this section and applicable ordinances of the city. No license shall then be issued and no sales shall be allowed in such establishment until it has been completed in accordance with all other provisions of this section and other applicable ordinances of the city.
(9)
Acceptance and consideration of application after rejection. When any license or permit to operate a retail package store under this section is rejected by the mayor and council, the city clerk shall not accept, and the mayor and council shall not consider, any application from the applicant for a license within twelve (12) months from the time of such rejection by the mayor and council.
(10)
Time limit for commencement of business in licensed establishments; forfeiture for non-use. Any holder of a license hereunder who shall begin the operation of the business authorized in the license, but who shall for a period of six (6) consecutive months thereafter cease to operate the business as authorized in said license, shall, upon expiration of such six-month period, automatically forfeit such license, which license shall by virtue of such failure to operate, be cancelled without the necessity of any further action of the mayor and council and no refund of license fee shall be made to the license holder; provided, however, that a license holder may apply to the mayor and council to cease business operations for an illness, or other such reasons as the mayor and council in its discretion may approve.
(b)
Distilled spirits—Consumption on premises.
(1)
A public hearing upon all original and completed applications for a license hereunder shall be called by the mayor and council for a regular meeting not more than sixty (60) days after the filing of the application.
(2)
No original application for a license hereunder shall be acted upon or issued by the mayor and council until after advertisement and public hearing thereon, as required in this section.
(3)
Advertisement of notice to secure a retail package dealer's license for consumption of distilled spirits on the premises; signs posted.
a.
All applicants for original licenses hereunder shall give notice of the purpose of making such application by advertisement, at least once during the thirty (30) days immediately preceding the date of a public hearing to be held by the mayor and council, in the newspaper which publishes the legal advertisements of the city. Such notice shall be in large boldface type and shall contain the location of the proposed business and shall give the name of the applicant, and if a partnership, the names of the partners, and if a corporation, the names of the officers, and the date and time the mayor and council would hear the application. It shall be the responsibility of the applicant for an original license to advertise the notice of hearing required as follows:
"Notice of application for retail license for distilled spirits/liquor consumption on the premises (mixed drinks). The undersigned has made application to the Mayor and Council of the City of Cairo for a retail license for consumption of distilled spirits (liquor) at _______. This application will be heard by the Mayor and Council at 6:00 p.m. on _______, 20___.
/s/___________ Owner(s); Address of Owner(s):___________."
b.
The applicant shall cause to be placed upon the location of the proposed business at least a week prior to the date of hearing, a sign or signs stating the following:
"DISTILLED SPIRITS/LIQUOR CONSUMPTION ON THE PREMISES LICENSE APPLIED FOR. HEARING BEFORE THE MAYOR AND COUNCIL FOR THE CITY OF CAIRO, GEORGIA WILL BE HELD AT 6:00 P.M. ON THE ___ DAY OF _______, 20___."
c.
The sign or signs required by subsection (4)(b)[(a)(5)b.] shall be not less that twenty-four (24) inches by thirty-six (36) inches and shall face toward all public streets, alleys, sidewalks or other public property adjoining the proposed location. Such signs shall be placed where they can be easily seen from all public properties adjoining the proposed location.
(4)
General requirements of business licensed hereunder. No application for any license hereunder shall be granted unless the following conditions are met:
a.
Licenses shall be granted only to private clubs, non-profit organizations with facilities, restaurants which may include lounges operated in conjunction with such restaurants, and hotels of fifty (50) or more guest rooms.
b.
All restaurants applying for or having pouring licenses must, in addition to all other requirements relating to the sale of alcoholic beverages by the drink, meet and maintain the following requirements:
1.
Meet all health and sanitation requirements set forth for food service establishments by the city, state and federal government.
2.
Must have installed and operative cooking facilities and kitchen equipment for accommodating the demands of sixty (60) food customers.
3.
Must have a complete place setting of metal cutlery and non-disposable dishes in regular use for each of sixty (60) food customers.
4.
Must have seating, at booths or tables designed for food service, sufficient for at least sixty (60) persons, and such booths or tables shall be large enough to accommodate at least two (2) place settings of metal cutlery and non-disposable dishes.
5.
Such a restaurant as contemplated by this section shall be, at all times during the hours hereinafter set forth, prepared to serve full-course meals with a substantial variety of culinary choices, or substantial substitutes therefor, and shall print and maintain a complete menu of same and furnish such printed menu to each food customer, with prices which are competitive with other restaurants within the city, whether or not those restaurants engage in the business of pouring mixed drinks.
6.
Foods listed on the required printed menu, or substantial substitutes therefor, must be available from 11:00 a.m., until 9:00 p.m., or if all sales of mixed drinks are stopped before 9:00 p.m., then from 11:00 a.m., until such earlier time as all mixed drink sales have ceased, at least five (5) days a week, with the exception of holidays, vacations, and periods of redecorating. The hours for service of food may be prolonged, but in no event shall mixed drinks be sold at any hour contrary to this chapter.
7.
Restaurants shall maintain at least fifty (50) percent of their business volume from the sale of food, private clubs exempted. Food sales shall include all consumable items sold on the premises except alcoholic beverages.
8.
In the event food sales fall below fifty (50) percent of the business volume of the licensee for any two (2) consecutive quarters, then the city manager shall request a hearing for the suspension or revocation of such license in accordance with regulation of this chapter. Nothing herein provided shall prevent the chief of police from suspending or revoking such license for any other violations of this chapter.
9.
The gross square footage of the dining area, lounge area, serving area, kitchen, restrooms, and storage rooms of any restaurant business other than private clubs, licensed hereunder must be a minimum of two thousand (2,000) square feet. Parking requirements for restaurants licensed hereunder shall be the same as those required in the present ordinances of the city, except that existing restaurants and nonconforming structures will be exempt from such requirements.
10.
Completion of proposed licensed premises. Where a building in which a retailer intends to operate under the provisions of this section is at the time of the application for such license not in existence or not yet completed, a license may be issued for such location provided the plans for the proposed building show clearly a compliance with the other provisions of this section. No sales or consumption will be allowed in such establishment until it has been completed in accordance with said plans and is in conformity with all other provisions of this section and the city Code.
c.
Special qualifications for private clubs. Private clubs, as defined herein, shall furnish along with license applications, a copy of the articles of incorporation from the State of Georgia, if incorporated by the state, and documentation of the valid up-to-date status of its national or state charter from its parent organization, together with documentation of its compliance denoting a tax-exempt status under Section 501 of the U.S. IRS Code. Private clubs whose primary purpose is not categorized by the term or terms "lodge," "fraternal order," or "veterans post," such as private clubs for golf, tennis and other similar recreation are exempt from the above provision which relates to a national or state charter from a parent organization.
d.
Each applicant for an original license, except private clubs, shall submit a plot plan and floor plan prepared by an architect, engineer, contractor, surveyor or other qualified person, showing all compliance with all distance, square foot, kitchen arrangement and food serving area table and seating arrangement requirements contained herein.
e.
On renewals, the same as the preceding shall be accomplished unless the applicant states in the reapplication that there has been no change in any of the above compliance items.
f.
Sale or delivery to unlicensed premises. It shall be unlawful for any licensee hereunder to make deliveries of any alcoholic beverage by the drink beyond the boundaries of the premises covered by the license.
g.
Exterior advertisements and media advertising prohibited.
1.
No signs of any kind, painted or electric, advertising any brand or price of alcoholic beverages shall be permitted on the exterior, or in the window of any licensed premises. No placard or sign of any kind which is visible from the exterior of the licensed premises shall make reference to the price of liquors sold therein.
2.
No outside signs of any kind shall indicate that liquor by the drink is served within the licensed premises except that restaurants may add to the name of the business the words "and Lounge" onto any legal signs or advertising.
h.
Coin-operated devices; provisions. Coin-operated equipment is permitted on the premises for the following purposes under this section: vending machines, such as cigarettes; vending a service, such as music or television; and for amusement, excluding any equipment designed or used for gambling purposes or refunds given in such manner as tickets, money or prizes to entice play.
i.
Package, broken package, carry-out sales prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any retail consumption dealer to sell distilled spirits in packages for carryout at any time; provided, however, that a retail consumption dealer may allow a patron to remove a partially-consumed bottle of wine as set out in section 4-92.
j.
Service in back rooms prohibited. The sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption by persons in any back room or side room which is not open to general public use is prohibited except that private parties or conventions, which have been scheduled in advance, may be served in public or private dining rooms or meeting rooms, and provided further that this prohibition shall not apply to private clubs hereunder.
k.
Off-street parking. The requirements of off-street parking for this section shall be the same as would be required under the ordinances or codes for a new building, or under the zoning ordinances or codes for a new business.
l.
Visibility; shrubbery. On any premises occupied by a license holder hereunder, the immediate proximity of the building shall be maintained so as to keep vegetation low and vision unobstructed. Failure to comply with this provision shall be grounds for suspension or revocation of consumption-on-premises licenses, or denial or renewal of such licenses.
m.
Premises to be well-lighted. The exterior of each building in which alcoholic beverages are sold for consumption on the premises shall have sufficient lighting so that all entrances and parking areas are clearly visible at all times when the premises are open for business. Also, the lounge and restaurant areas, if any, and all tables, booths and other areas where customers are served, and all passageways for customers shall be sufficiently well-illuminated so that they may be viewed by those in such areas.
n.
Fire and safety; maximum occupancy provisions. All drinking establishments licensed or to be considered for licensing for consumption on the premises of any alcoholic beverages shall be measured as to the net usable floor area, and publicly visible posters stating the maximum number of persons allowable inside the building where such beverages are consumed shall be posted by the fire chief or assistant fire chief. The maximum allowable floor space per occupancy in the net usable floor area shall be fifteen (15) square feet. The alcoholic beverage licensee will be responsible for seeing that the established occupancy limits are not exceeded, and the violation of this provision shall be due cause for suspension or revocation by the mayor and council after proper hearing, of the alcoholic beverage license.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-8. - Right to investigate.
(a)
Upon receipt by the city clerk of any application for an alcoholic beverage license, or for the transfer of any license, either the owner, designated agent, or the managing agent listed on the application shall consent to a criminal background check on the forms supplied by the city clerk and police department.
(b)
The city clerk of the City of Cairo shall cause an inquiry to be made into the tax records to determine if any applicant or other parties interested in an application have any outstanding or special assessments that are delinquent or any other monies owing to the City of Cairo. No license shall be issued nor shall a license be renewed until all such debts are paid in full.
(c)
All applications required by this chapter shall be investigated by the police department, building department, and fire department; and the report of such approval or disapproval shall be presented to the mayor and council for consideration with the application.
(d)
The chief of police, or any policeman of the city, may enter upon the premises operated under the provisions of this chapter during operating hours for the purpose of inspecting the premises and enforcing this chapter and shall have access during the inspection of all books, records, and supplies relating to the sale, storage, or possession of alcoholic beverages.
(e)
Upon approval by the mayor and council of an original application for license of one (1) or more of the above type sales of alcoholic beverages, such sales of alcoholic beverages may be made by the applicant after a license is issued for all, or the remaining part, of the license year in which the license is issued, unless the license is revoked or suspended as provided in section 4-17.
(f)
Wholesale sales of alcoholic beverages in Cairo by wholesalers licensed by the State of Georgia are hereby declared legal, subject to the right of the city to charge a wholesaler for any violation of state law or regulation, and to request a hearing thereon as set out in section 4-17.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-9. - Fingerprinting of alcoholic beverage license applicants and certain employees; fees for fingerprinting.
(a)
As a prerequisite to the issuance of any such initial license only, the applicant listed in the application for a license to sell malt beverages/beer, wine, or liquor in the City of Cairo, who has not heretofore been licensed by the City of Cairo to sell alcoholic beverages at retail, shall be required to submit to a complete fingerprinting record to be sent to the Georgia Bureau of Investigation which shall search the files of the Georgia Crime Information Center for any instance of criminal activity during the ten (10) years immediately preceding the date of the application. Information received from them shall be used by the city to supplement the information contained on the application. No license to sell alcoholic beverages of any kind shall be issued to any person convicted of a felony within ten (10) years, or a misdemeanor within five (5) years, immediately preceding the date of the application. The city shall be reimbursed for the cost of fingerprinting by the applicant that is being fingerprinted.
(b)
The city chief of police, within his own discretion, from time to time, may require employees working in an establishment selling alcoholic beverage, to submit information for purposes of supplementing any information available to the city with respect to employees. Each such employee shall pay a fee for local records check as set by the chief of police. The chief of police may require them to apply for complete fingerprinting for criminal history check within all provisions hereinafter as apply to employees of package liquor stores or liquor pouring establishments, and in the discretion of the chief of police such additional fees as are reasonably related to recovery of the city's cost in time, equipment and material for such additional fingerprint search shall be charged.
(c)
If it should be found there is a violation of this chapter by the person fingerprinted, the chief of police shall notify the licensee that such person is not eligible for continued work. No person shall continue working after receiving such notice of ineligibility. It shall be the responsibility of the licensee after receiving notification of the ineligibility of such worker to ensure that the worker ceases to work.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-10. - Grounds for denial of application for license or permit.
(a)
The following shall be sufficient cause for the denial of an application or permit:
(1)
Failure of the applicant to meet the qualifications for licensing as set forth in this chapter.
(2)
The making of any untrue or misleading statement in the application for an alcoholic beverage license.
(3)
Failure to meet distance requirements as provided in this chapter.
(4)
Where it appears that the applicant would not have adequate participation in the proposed business to direct and manage its affairs, or where it appears that the application is intended to be a mere surrogate for a person or persons who would not otherwise qualify for a license for any reason whatsoever.
(b)
When contrary to the public interest and welfare, no original application shall be considered and no license to sell alcoholic beverages of any kind shall be issued by the mayor and council to or for:
(1)
Any person, as determined by the mayor and council by reason of such person's business experience, financial standing, moral character, mental capacity, trade associations, personal associations, record of arrest, or reputation in any community in which he has resided, who is not likely to maintain the operation for which he is seeking a license in conformity with federal, state and local laws, rules and regulations.
(2)
A location at which a previous alcoholic beverage license has been revoked or suspended, and where, in the judgment of the mayor or council, the problems which have arisen from the operation of an alcoholic beverage license at such location indicate that it is not in the interest of public health, safety, welfare or morals that the sale of alcoholic beverages be permitted at such location.
(3)
A location not suitable in the judgment and discretion of the city because of traffic congestion, general character of the neighborhood, or by reason of the effect which such an establishment would have on the adjacent and surrounding properties or on the neighborhood.
(4)
A location within an area where, in the judgment of the city, the number of alcoholic beverage licenses already granted makes it contrary to the public interest or welfare.
(5)
Any person who is an elected official or employee of the City of Cairo, Georgia.
(6)
The operation of a distilled spirits package business licensed hereunder by any person already holding one (1) such license.
(7)
No person, or his or her spouse, parent or child, who has a direct financial interest in a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages at wholesale shall hold any license or have an interest in any other license issued under the terms of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-11. - Use of persons with prior convictions; prohibited.
(a)
No licensee shall work any person in any premises for the sale of alcoholic beverages in any capacity found to have been convicted of, or who shall have entered a plea of nolo contendere to, any felony of any state, or of the United States, within a period of ten (10) years immediately prior to the filing of any such application. The term "conviction" shall include an adjudication of guilt or plea of guilty, or a plea of nolo contendere or the forfeiture of a bond by a person charged with a crime.
(b)
Notwithstanding the above provisions, the mayor and council, in its sole discretion, may direct the issuance of a letter of eligibility to persons who have in the past been convicted of, or plead guilty to, or entered a plea of nolo contendere, or forfeited a bond on any crime not involving force or violence, and the mayor and council after a thorough investigation of all the facts, including parole or probation officer's reports, judge's recommendations, chief of police's recommendation and any other evidence bearing on the character of the applicant have determined that such action is in keeping with the public good and ends of justice, or the public interest and welfare.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)

(a)
A licensee that desires to continue in business during the next or subsequent license year must make application for such year on or before February 28 of that year.
(b)
If application for renewal is not received and approved, and payment of license fees received before April 1 of such renewal year, all sales of such alcoholic beverages shall cease as of the last legal hour in March for such sales.
(c)
After renewal applications have been approved by the police chief, building official, fire chief, and city council, upon payment of the license fees, the city clerk may cause renewal licenses to be issued to licensees.
(d)
Upon renewal, any license fee submitted after the due date of April 1 of such renewal year, shall be considered delinquent and assessed a penalty in the amount of ten (10) percent of the fees due.
(e)
If there have been any changes in the ownership, managing agent, location, or operation of the business, a new application must be submitted to the city council for approval.
(f)
After five (5) years applicant must be re-fingerprinted and a background check must be performed at the applicant's expense.

Sec. 4-14. - Duty to remain current.
(a)
It is the duty and responsibility of the licensee to notify the city clerk of any changes to the information contained in the original application, including a change in the registered agent. Any amendments to the original application shall be made in writing on forms provided by the city clerk and shall be under oath and verified as otherwise required of license applications.
(b)
Failure to maintain a current application shall be grounds for revocation of the license.
(c)
No person shall have, own or enjoy any ownership, interest in, share in the profits from, or otherwise participate in the business of any alcoholic beverage licensee in the city unless a full description of such interest shall have been furnished to the city clerk at the time such interest arose. It shall be the duty of the licensee to report to the city clerk, within five (5) days, any change in any interest in such licensee's business including, but not limited to:
(1)
Any division of the profits;
(2)
Any division of net or gross sales for any purpose whatsoever;
(3)
Any change in the ownership of any corporation that has any interest in such business or the change of management of such corporation.
(d)
After receipt of such notice, the city clerk shall notify such person within a reasonable time of any objection to the ownership or interest set forth therein, based on the interest holder's ability to qualify as a licensee under this chapter. In the event such interest is not disposed of within thirty (30) days after the mailing of the notice by the city clerk, or in the event no objections are filed within such thirty-day period, or in the event such person fails to notify the city clerk of the transfer of any such interest within twenty (20) days after the acquisition thereof, then the license as provided for herein may be subject to suspension or revocation by the city council.
(e)
The licensee shall make all reports required by this section in the form required by the city clerk and such reports shall be an amendment to the licensee's application on file with the city and as such shall be under oath and verified as otherwise required of license applications.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-15. - Transfers, changes of ownership, locations.
(a)
No city license may be transferred from one (1) person to another or from one (1) location to another without permission and approval of the city council upon receipt of a written application. Approvals as to any change shall be at the discretion of the city council.
(b)
In case of the death of any person owning a license, or any interest therein, with the approval of the mayor and council and subject to the terms of this section, the license may be transferred to the administrator, executor or personal representative of the deceased person, or to the heir(s) at law of the deceased person, if such administrator, executor, personal representative or heir(s) meet all of the other qualifications contained herein. The license of such deceased person shall be held by the administrator, executor or personal representative of such deceased person only for the time necessary to complete execution of his estate and disposition of the license or his interest therein, but in no event to exceed six (6) months.
(c)
Nothing in this section, however, shall prohibit one (1) or more of the partners in a partnership holding a license from withdrawing from the partnership and assigning his interest in such partnership to one (1) or more of the partners who were partners at the time of the issuance of the license. Such withdrawal shall not, however, serve to bring any new ownership into the partnership, unless all provisions of this section are fully complied with, and then only upon the approval of the mayor and council.
(d)
Any change in the ownership interests contained on the application shall cause the immediate cessation of sales of any alcoholic beverages and no sales of alcoholic beverages shall be made until any such change or changes in the ownership interests are approved by the mayor and council, provided, however, that this provision shall not apply in a situation where one or more individuals who have ownership interests in the license cease to a have such ownership interest, but the remaining ownership interest remains unchanged except as to the division of the remaining interests.
(e)
Any licensee desiring to discontinue business at one (1) location and commence business at some other new location must make a complete new application for such new location along with payment of a transfer fee as required in section 4-12. The new location shall meet all requirements of a new license to be issued hereunder, except payment of the license fee. The unearned portion of the license fee may be transferred to the new location in the event the transfer of location is approved by the mayor and council.
(f)
Upon the sale of an existing business, the city license issued for that business location shall remain in effect pending approval or denial of a new application by the city council, provided that the buyer or transferee meets all the qualifications of an applicant as required by this chapter, and provided that the buyer or transferee has immediately upon the date of sale or prior to the date of sale made proper written application and has paid the application fee.
(g)
In the event of a change in the designated agent of a licensee, the city license shall remain in effect pending approval or denial of the appointment of a new agent, provided:
(1)
The licensee notifies the city clerk within five (5) days of the change of the designated agent;
(2)
The licensee submits a proper written application for approval thereof;
(3)
The licensee pays a transfer fee as required by section 4-12;
(4)
The transferee meets all qualifications of a designated agent as required by this chapter; and
(5)
There has been no change of ownership, location, or operation of the business.

Sec. 4-16. - Surrender, suspension, revocation, or denial of renewal or transfer.
(a)
The following may be grounds for the suspension and/or revocation of an alcoholic beverage license or permit:
(1)
The violation by the licensee of any local, state, or federal law or regulation, except misdemeanors, or any ordinance of the city, other than traffic violations. The determination of whether any such violation has occurred shall be made by the hearing officer and an actual conviction in a court for such offense shall not be necessary in order to suspend or revoke the license or a permit.
(2)
The failure of the licensee or his employee to report promptly to the police department any violation of law or municipal ordinance, breach of the peace, disturbance or altercation resulting in violence, occurring on the premises.
(3)
Any conduct on the part of the owner of the business, the licensee, or his employee contrary to the public welfare, safety, health or morals. While not to be considered a comprehensive or exhaustive listing of prohibited behavior, specific instances of prohibited behavior by the owner, the agent, the licensee and/or his employee include, but are not limited to, the following:
a.
Consumption or under the influence of alcoholic beverage while working or on duty;
b.
Breach of the peace, disturbance or altercation resulting in violence;
c.
The conviction of the owner, licensee, or employee of any felony reasonably related to the ability of the licensee to operate and maintain the premises in a proper manner;
d.
The violation of any state law or regulation governing the manufacture, sale, distribution or transportation of alcoholic beverages;
e.
The conviction of the owner or the licensee of driving under the influence of alcoholic beverages and/or drugs on two (2) or more occasions;
f.
Permitting the solicitation of patrons on the licensed premises for prostitution or any other unlawful act where the licensee or the licensee's employee or agent knew or should have known of such conduct;
g.
The selling or serving of any alcoholic beverage to any person that the licensee or the licensee's employee or agent knew or should have known to be in a state of intoxication;
h.
The failure to comply with any and all federal, state or municipal tax laws and regulations applicable to the operation of establishments licensed to sell alcoholic beverages;
i.
Failure by the licensee to adequately supervise and monitor the conduct of the employees, patrons and others on the licensed premises or on any property owned or leased by the owner or licensee, including but not limited to, parking lots and parking areas, or on any parking lots or areas which may be lawfully used by patrons of a licensed establishment, in order to protect the safety and well-being of the general public and of those utilizing the premises.
(4)
For the purposes of this section, the definition of an employee shall include any agent or independent contractor that is working for hire on the licensed premises.
(5)
Operating or conducting the business in a manner contrary to the public welfare, safety, health or morals, or in such manner as to constitute a nuisance. Any combination totaling three (3) or more violations of municipal ordinances, breach of the peace, disturbance or altercation resulting in violence, all occurring on the premises within any thirty-day period shall constitute prima facie evidence that the licensee is operating or conducting an alcoholic beverage business in a manner which is contrary to the public welfare, safety, health or morals. The business license holder shall, upon suspension for violations of this section, have the burden of proving such occurrences were beyond his control and not related to the operation of the business.
(6)
The chief of police shall have the right to suspend any alcoholic beverage license or permit pending a hearing as provided for in section 4-17 where in the judgment of the city council such action is necessary to protect the public health, safety, welfare or morals.
(7)
The chief of police or any police department shift commander on duty at the time may close any location holding an alcoholic beverage license for a period not in excess of twenty-four (24) hours in the event there shall occur on the premises any violation of law or municipal ordinance, breach of the peace, disturbance or altercation resulting in violence, or other occurrence which is contrary to the public health, welfare, safety or morals, when in the judgment of the chief of police or shift commander such action is necessary to protect the public health, welfare, safety or morals.
(b)
In the case of the revocation or surrender of an alcoholic beverage license or permit before expiration, the holder thereof shall not be entitled to receive any refund.
(c)
Any disciplinary action against a licensee shall be reported to the State Department of Revenue within forty-five (45) days of such disciplinary action.
(d)
All licenses issued pursuant to this chapter shall be valid only so long as the licensee is actively engaged in such business, with the exception of holidays, vacations, and periods of redecoration, and in the event the licensee shall cease to be actively engaged in such business, such license shall be invalid and the licensee of such business shall immediately notify the city clerk and return his license thereto.
(e)
The violation of any of the provisions of this chapter by a holder of an alcoholic beverage license or permit, or his agent, or employee, shall be unlawful, and shall be punishable as provided in section 9-3 of this Code, and shall also subject the holder of such license to suspension or revocation of such license or any combination thereof as the city council deems proper.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-17. - Hearing on suspension, revocation, or denial of renewal or transfer.
(a)
Notice, hearing. Any license or permit which has been issued or which may hereafter be issued by the city to any licensee may be denied, suspended or revoked, or transfer refused, by the mayor and council for due cause. Before the mayor and council shall deny, suspend or revoke a license or permit, or deny a transfer of any license, the applicant shall be given notice in writing from the city clerk to show cause before the mayor and council at a time and place specified therein not less than three (3) days nor more than thirty (30) days from the date of service of the notice by registered or certified mail or statutory overnight delivery to the last known address of the applicant/licensee appearing in the files of the city clerk, why the application or permit, or renewal of a license, or transfer of a license should not be denied, or why the license should not be revoked or suspended, as the case may be, stating the grounds therefor, and at the appointed time and place the applicant or licensee shall have an opportunity to show cause, if any exist, why the application should not be denied or the license revoked or suspended, after which the mayor and council shall take such action as it, in its judgment and discretion, shall deem warranted under the facts. The hearing herein provided for need not be at a regular meeting of the mayor and council but may be at such time and place as shall be fixed in the notice. All decisions denying, suspending or revoking any license or application shall be in writing, with the reasons therefor stated, and mailed or delivered to the applicant or licensee. In addition, at any hearing as provided herein, the party afforded the hearing shall have the opportunity to present evidence, have an attorney, and have testimony recorded at his expense.
(b)
The decision of the mayor and council shall be final unless appealed by certiorari to the superior court of Grady County. If the decision is appealed, the city attorney, or his designee, shall represent the city in superior court.
(c)
In all instances of a denial of any application for an alcoholic beverage license or the revocation of any existing alcoholic beverage license, the applicant, licensee or any person(s) with twenty-five (25) percent or more interest, shall not reapply for a license for at least two (2) years from the final date of the denial or revocation.
(d)
Effect of revocation by state. Whenever the state shall revoke any permit or license to sell any alcoholic beverage at retail, the city license to deal in such product shall thereupon be automatically revoked without any action by the mayor and council or any municipal officer. If the state has suspended the license or placed the same on probation, then the city license is automatically suspended or probated, as the case may be.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-18. - Employee permits.
(a)
No person shall be employed to dispense, sell, serve, take orders, or mix alcoholic beverages, or be in any managerial position, by an establishment holding a license for the sale of distilled spirits by the package or for consumption on premises or an establishment holding a license for malt beverage or wine for consumption on premises unless such person has been approved by the chief of police or his designee. The individual named on the alcoholic beverage license as the managing agent for such establishment shall be exempt from obtaining an employee permit.
(b)
The employee shall furnish, at the time of presenting the application, two (2) forms of valid, current identification. Such application shall contain the following information: applicant's name, date of birth, height, weight, race, sex, residence address, telephone number, and disclosure of arrest record. Applicant must give permission to the police department to obtain and inspect any criminal history on such applicant which is in the possession of any law enforcement agency.
(c)
Upon approval by the chief of police or his designee, such person shall be issued an employee permit which shall contain the name and expiration date. Such employee permit shall remain the property of the city and shall be in the possession of the employee at any time he or she is working at any licensed establishment, and shall be produced upon the request of any law enforcement officer of the city.
(d)
No person shall be granted an employee permit that has been convicted, plead guilty, or entered a plea of nolo contendere to any crime involving the sale or furnishing of alcoholic beverages to an underage person or any felony within ten (10) years of the date of the application.
(e)
No person shall be granted an employee permit if he or she has been the holder of an alcoholic beverage license or employee permit which has been revoked within five (5) years of the date of the application.
(f)
An employee permit shall be valid for three (3) years from the date of issue. At the expiration of three (3) years, the employee permit may be renewed upon the submission of a renewal application, the payment of the appropriate fee, and upon determination that such individual remains qualified under this chapter.
(g)
The employee permit is non-transferable and is valid only for the individual named on the permit. Such permit is valid for the individual named while employed in any establishment licensed in the city.
(h)
An employee permit may be suspended or revoked by the chief of police if it is determined that the individual has violated any provision of this chapter or committed any offense which would make him or her ineligible to hold such a permit.
(i)
Falsifying or failing to disclose any information required by this chapter shall be grounds for denial or revocation of the employee permit.
(j)
Notification of any denial, suspension, or revocation of an employee permit shall be in writing and served either in person or by certified mail and shall contain the reasons for such action and the notice of the right to appeal the decision.
(1)
Decisions of the chief of police that adversely affect or aggrieve any applicant, certificate holder, or permit holder under this chapter may be appealed to the mayor and council, as provided in section 4-17 of this Code. All appeals shall be submitted in writing to the chief of police within ten (10) calendar days after notification of the adverse decision.
(k)
No licensee shall allow any employee required to hold an employee permit to work on the premises unless the employee has in his/her possession a current valid employee permit. Provided, however, that an individual may be employed for a period of time not to exceed fourteen (14) days pending the submission of the application to and the approval of the permit by the police department.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)

ARTICLE II. - OPERATING RULES AND REGULATIONS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-36. - Responsibility of licensees and employees.
(a)
No holder of a license authorizing the sale of alcoholic beverages in the city nor any agent or employee of the licensee, shall do any of the following upon the licensed premises:
(1)
Knowingly furnish or cause to be furnished or permit any other person in the holder's employ to furnish alcoholic beverages to any minor.
(2)
Knowingly sell, barter, exchange, give, provide, or furnish alcoholic beverages to any person who is in a state of noticeable intoxication.
(3)
Sell alcoholic beverages upon the licensed premises on any day or at any time when such sale or consumption is prohibited by law.
(4)
Permit any disturbance of the peace or obscenity or any lewd, immoral or improper conduct on the licensed premises. Such misconduct shall be immediately reported to the city police department.
(5)
Sell, offer for sale, possess, or permit the consumption on the licensed premises of any kind of alcoholic beverages, the sale, possession, or consumption of which is not authorized under holder's license.
(6)
Allow or permit the breaking of any packages containing alcoholic beverage on the premises where a license has been issued for package sales. This Code section shall not apply with respect to sales pursuant to a license for consumption on the premises.
(7)
Add to the contents of a bottle or to refill empty bottles or in any other manner to misrepresent the quantity, quality or brand name of any alcoholic beverage.
(8)
Sell or offer for sale alcoholic beverages by use of vending machines.
(9)
Except when licensed as a distillery, brewery, or winery, retail package and retail consumption dealers shall not buy nor accept deliveries of alcoholic beverages from any person other than a wholesale dealer or distributor licensed by the state or by any means other than by a conveyance owned and operated by a wholesale dealer with a license from the city to make such deliveries. Transportation of alcoholic beverages by any other means shall be grounds for revocation of all licenses concerned.
(b)
The sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption by persons in any back room or side room which is not open to the general public use is prohibited, except that private parties and conventions, or a properly permitted wine tasting, which have been scheduled in advance, may be served in public or private dining rooms or meeting rooms, and provided further that this prohibition shall not apply at private clubs hereunder, nor to the sale for consumption hereunder to the registered guests of any licensed hotel or motel in their designated rooms.
(c)
All alcoholic beverages shall be stored only on the premises for which the license is issued.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-37. - Days and hours of sale.
(a)
Daily, Sunday, holiday, and on election days.
(1)
Malt beverages or wine package location, not for consumption on premises:
a.
It shall be unlawful for any licensee licensed to sell packaged beer or wine hereunder to permit the sale or engage in the sale of such beer or wine except from 5:00 a.m. to 12:00 midnight Monday through Saturday and 12:30 p.m. to 11:30 p.m. on Sunday.
(2)
Package liquor stores:
a.
Licensees shall not engage in the sale of any commodity prior to 8:00 a.m. or after 11:45 p.m., Monday through Saturday and on Sunday prior to 11:30 a.m. or after of 11:30 p.m. No retail dealer of distilled spirits shall be in its place of business Monday through Saturday any time prior to 6:00 a.m. or 30 minutes past closing time of 11:30 p.m. No retail dealer of distilled spirits shall be in its place of business on Sunday prior to 10:00 a.m. or 30 minutes past the closing time of 11:30 p.m.
(3)
Nothing contained in this section shall prohibit a package retailer from being in its place of business at any time:
a.
For purposes of responding to emergency situations such as fire or burglary;
b.
For purposes of taking inventory, making repairs, renovating, or any other alcoholic beverage business purpose which does not involve the presence of persons other than the retailer, its agents or employees, when the activities could not reasonably be carried out during regular business hours, provided that the licensee posts on all door entrances to the place of business a sign to read: "CLOSED, NO CUSTOMERS ALLOWED ON PREMISES".
(4)
Beer and wine on-the-premises consumption:
a.
It shall be unlawful for any licensee licensed to sell packaged beer or wine hereunder to permit the sale or engage in the sale of such beer or wine except from 5:00 a.m. to 12:00 midnight Monday through Saturday and 11:00 a.m. to 11:30 p.m. on Sunday. All customers will clear the premises no later than 1:00 a.m. on Sundays, and all owners and employees shall vacate the premises before 2:00 a.m. on weekdays and on Sundays. For security purposes, if any work is needed to be done during legally closed hours, the police department must be notified at least one (1) hour in advance.
(5)
Liquor pouring:
a.
On-the-premises consumption. Its shall be unlawful for any licensee to sell alcoholic beverages except during the hours of 8:00 a.m. to 11:45 p.m. Monday through Thursday and Saturday, during the hours of 8:00 a.m. on Friday to 1:00 a.m. on Saturday and 11:00 a.m. to 11:30 p.m. on Sunday. All customers will clear the premises no later than 1:00 a.m. on weekdays and Sunday, and no later than 2:00 a.m. on Saturday; and all owners and employees shall vacate premises before 2:00 a.m. on weekdays and Sunday and before 3:00 a.m. on Saturday except as set forth herein. For security purposes, if any work is needed to be done during legally closed hours, the police department must be notified at [least] one (1) hour in advance.
(6)
Wholesalers: The business hours of any wholesaler licensed hereunder shall be from sunrise to sunset.
(b)
Hotels, motels, grocery stores, convenience stores and restaurants shall not be required to close unrelated functions and activities not licensed under this chapter or customarily operated at hours other than the foregoing specified hours, but all alcoholic beverages of any nature must be and remain in the custody and control of the licensee or his employees, and may not be dispensed in any manner, and the licensee is hereby required to evict any other person possessing any of the above. For the purposes of this paragraph, "restaurant" shall be defined by the definition of that work contained in section 4-2 of this Code.
(c)
Holidays: It shall be unlawful for any licensee to sell any alcoholic beverage at a package beer, wine, or liquor location; or at any beer, wine, or liquor on-premises consumption location during Christmas Day.
(d)
Election days:
(1)
Except as provided in subsection (e)(2) of this section, the sale of alcoholic beverages in compliance with this Code shall be authorized and legal on any election day.
(2)
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell alcoholic beverages within two hundred fifty (250) feet of any polling place or of the outer edge of any building within which such polling place is established on election days. As used in this section, the term "day" means that period of time beginning with the opening of the polls and ending with the closing of the polls.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19; Ord. No. 821, § 1, 12-9-19)
Sec. 4-38. - Distance requirements; method for measurement of distances.
(a)
Malt beverage or wine - manufacturer, retail package, and retail consumption dealer distance requirements.
(1)
No malt beverage or wine - manufacturer, retail package, or retail consumption dealer shall be located:
a.
Within one hundred (100) yards of any school building, school grounds or college campus.
b.
Within one hundred (100) yards of any property containing three hundred (300) housing units or fewer, which property is owned or operated by a housing authority created by article 1 of chapter 3 of title 8 of the Housing Authorities Law.
c.
Within one hundred (100) yards of an alcoholic treatment center owned and operated by this state or any county or any municipal government.
(2)
This subsection shall not apply:
a.
To a grocery store licensed for the retail sale of package wine and malt beverages for consumption off the premises, which shall be allowed to sell wine and malt beverages within one hundred (100) yards of any school building, school grounds or college campus. As used in this subparagraph, the term "grocery store" means a retail establishment which has a total retail floor space of at least ten thousand (10,000) square feet of which at least eighty-five (85) percent is reserved for the sale of food and other nonalcoholic items and conducts all of its sales inside the building containing its retail floor space.
b.
To hotels of fifty (50) or more guest rooms.
c.
To any premises or location where an alcoholic beverage license was issued or approved prior to the effective date of this ordinance [from which this chapter derives], so long as alcoholic beverages are being sold or dispensed in compliance with the other provisions of this chapter.
d.
To any premises or location where alcoholic beverages are being sold or dispensed in compliance with the other provisions of this chapter and a school or college campus is built or erected within the minimum distances provided for in this section.
e.
To bona fide private clubs owning their own building.
(b)
Distilled spirits - manufacturer, retail package, or retail consumption dealer distance requirements.
(1)
No distilled spirits - manufacturer, retail package, or retail consumption dealer shall be located:
a.
Within one hundred (100) yards of any church building or within two hundred (200) yards of any school building, education building, school grounds or college campus.
b.
Within one hundred (100) yards of any property containing three hundred (300) housing units or fewer, which property is owned or operated by a housing authority created by article 1 of chapter 3 of title 8 of the Housing Authorities Law.
c.
Within one hundred (100) yards of an alcoholic treatment center owned and operated by this state or any county or municipal government.
d.
Within fifty (50) feet of any private residence. The distance for the purpose of this paragraph shall be measured in all directions from the nearest wall enclosing the building where alcoholic beverages are to be sold to the nearest point of any residential property line, except that the provisions of this paragraph shall not apply on residences located adjacent to or in areas of commercially-zoned property.
(2)
The distance provided for herein shall not apply:
a.
To any premises or location where alcoholic beverages are being sold or dispensed in compliance with the other provisions of this chapter and a school or church is built or erected within the minimum distances provided for in this section.
b.
To any premises or location licensed as an importer, so long as the same is allowed by the state; provided, however, no signs or other forms of advertisement shall be placed on or near the licensed premises, which directly or indirectly advertise that alcoholic beverages are stored or warehoused on the premises.
c.
To hotels of fifty (50) or more guest rooms.
d.
To bona fide private clubs owning their own buildings.
(c)
Unless otherwise provided in this chapter, all measurements to determine distances required by this chapter for the issuance of licenses shall be measured in the following manner:
(1)
In a straight line from the front door of the structure from which alcoholic beverages are sold or offered for sale;
(2)
To the front door of the building of a church, government-owned treatment center, or a retail package store; or
(3)
To the nearest property line of the real property being used for school or educational purposes or property that is owned or operated by a housing authority.
(d)
If the sale of alcoholic beverages was lawful at a location during the twelve (12) months immediately preceding an application for renewal or a new application for an alcoholic beverage license, the premises shall be grandfathered in regarding any distance requirements as outlined in this chapter.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-39. - Employment of minors.
(a)
No person shall allow or require a person in his employment under eighteen (18) years of age to dispense, serve, sell or take orders for any alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises.
(b)
The provisions of this section shall not prohibit persons under legal age who are employed in supermarkets, convenience stores, breweries or drugstores from selling or handling alcoholic beverages which are sold for consumption off the premises.
(c)
Nothing contained in this Code section shall be construed to prohibit any person under twenty-one (21) years of age from:
(1)
Dispensing, serving, selling, or handling alcoholic beverages as a part of employment in any licensed establishment;
(2)
Being employed in any establishment in which alcoholic beverages are distilled or manufactured; or
(d)
Testimony by any person under twenty-one (21) years of age, when given in an administrative or judicial proceeding against another person for violation of any provision of this Code section, shall not be used in any administrative or judicial proceeding brought against such testifying person under twenty-one (21) years of age.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-40. - Furnishing to, purchase of, or possession of alcoholic beverages by persons under twenty-one years of age; use of false identification.
(a)
The following regulations involving minors shall apply:
(1)
No licensee, or employee of such licensee, or any person acting on behalf of, or with the knowledge of such licensee, shall give, sell, offer to sell, knowingly, by himself or through another, furnish, cause to be furnished, or offer to furnish any person any alcoholic beverage who is under the lawful drinking age established by Georgia law.
(2)
No person under twenty-one (21) years of age shall purchase, drink, or sell or knowingly possess any alcoholic beverage.
(3)
No person knowingly or intentionally shall act as an agent to purchase or acquire any alcoholic beverage for or on behalf of a person under twenty-one (21) years of age.
(4)
No person under twenty-one (21) years of age shall misrepresent his age, identity or use any false identification for the purpose of purchasing or obtaining any alcoholic beverage.
(5)
No licensee, employee of any licensee, or any person acting on behalf of any licensee shall furnish, sell, or give alcoholic beverages to any person on any day or at any time when the sale of same is prohibited by state law or this chapter.
(b)
It shall be the affirmative duty of every licensee, or any person acting on behalf of the licensee, to check the proper identification of every person when selling or otherwise furnishing an alcoholic beverage to such person, or to permit, within the licensee's premises, any person in possession of an alcoholic beverage who is under the lawful drinking age established by Georgia law; and the failure to make such request and verification in any case where the person to whom the alcoholic beverage is sold or otherwise furnished is less than the legal age, may be considered by the trier of fact in determining whether the person selling or otherwise furnishing such alcoholic beverage did so knowingly.
(c)
For purposes of this chapter, "proper identification" means any document issued by a governmental agency containing a description of the person, such person's photograph, and giving such person's date of birth and includes, without being limited to, a passport, military identification card, driver's license or an identification card authorized under O.C.G.A. § 40-5-100 et seq. "Proper identification" shall not include a birth certificate.
(d)
If a driver's license is altered or indicates that such license is falsified, or is not the driver's license of the person presenting it, or that such person is under the age of twenty-one (21) years, the person to whom said license is tendered shall be authorized to either write down the name and address and license number, or to seize the license, and shall immediately thereafter summon a law enforcement officer who shall be authorized to seize the license either at the scene or at such time as the license can be located.
(e)
No person shall be convicted under this section if such person has been furnished with proper identification showing that the person who is being furnished alcoholic beverages is of legal age or over.
(f)
The prohibition contained in subsection (a) above shall not apply with respect to the sale, purchase or possession of alcoholic beverages for consumption:
(1)
For medical purposes pursuant to a prescription of a physician duly authorized to practice medicine in this state.
(2)
At a religious ceremony.
(3)
In the home of the minor with the consent of said minor's parent or legal guardian.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-41. - Persons under legal age; exception.
(a)
Underage persons on licensed premises. No person who holds an alcoholic beverage license allowing on the premises consumption, or his agent or employee, shall allow any person who has not attained legal age to be in, frequent, or loiter about the licensed premises unless such person is accompanied by a parent or legal guardian, or unless such person is an employee of the license holder; provided, however, that such person shall be permitted in restaurants or other places serving food without being accompanied by a parent or legal guardian. The provisions of this section shall not apply to a person in the military service who has reached the age of eighteen (18) years and who has on his or her person a valid military identification card.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)

Performance of actual or simulated sexual acts; use of artificial devices or inanimate objects; display of visual images of sexual acts or nudity shall be prohibited. No person shall perform on licensed premises acts of or acts which constitute or simulate:

The touching, caressing, or fondling of the breast, buttocks, anus, or genitals; or

(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to show, display, or exhibit, on licensed premises, any film, still picture, electronic reproduction, or any other visual reproduction or image of any act or conduct described in subsection (a) or (b) of this Code section.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-43. - Brown-bagging prohibition.
(a)
Brown-bagging as defined herein is prohibited within the corporate limits of the City of Cairo. Upon conviction, such persons shall be punished pursuant to the provisions of section 9-3 of this Code.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for a licensee or any other business that has been issued a business occupation tax certificate to allow customers to bring with them their own alcoholic beverages, a practice commonly referred to as "BYOB" or "brown bagging."
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any alcoholic beverages to be served, stored, kept or consumed by any person on the premises of any business establishment for which a city business/occupation tax certificate has been issued, excluding those businesses with a valid alcoholic beverage license or as otherwise exempted by this chapter.
(d)
Every person whether acting as an individual owner, employee of an owner or operator for an owner or whether acting as an agent or independent contractor for the owner, employer, who participates, allows, directly or indirectly, brown-bagging in violation of this section shall be guilty of an offense hereunder.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-44. - Vaporized and powdered forms of alcoholic beverage; strictly prohibited.
(a)
No person shall purchase, offer for sale or use, sell, or use any vaporized form of an alcoholic beverage produced by an alcohol vaporizing device. This paragraph shall not apply to a product that contains alcohol as otherwise lawfully prescribed by a licensed health care practitioner.
(1)
No person shall possess any alcohol vaporizing device, including but not limited to any machine known as an alcohol without liquid (AWOL) machine. This shall not apply to any nebulizer or atomizer used to supply a product that contains alcohol as otherwise lawfully prescribed by a licensed healthcare practitioner.
(2)
No operator shall keep or allow to be kept on the licensed premises thereof any vaporized form of an alcoholic beverage produced by an alcohol vaporizing device.
(b)
No person or licensee shall manufacture, use, offer for use, purchase, offer to purchase, sell, or possess powdered alcohol.
(1)
This Code section shall not apply to the use of powdered alcohol for bona fide research purposes by a health care provider that operates primarily for the purpose of conducting scientific research, a state institution, private college or university, pharmaceutical or bio-technology companies.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-45. - Hotels; in-room sales in hotels.
(a)
In order to be eligible for a consumption on the premises alcoholic beverage license, a hotel must:
(1)
Be used and held out to the public as a place where food is served and consumed and sleeping accommodations are offered to guests for adequate pay;
(2)
Contains fifty (50) or more guest rooms; and
(3)
Contain one (1) or more public dining rooms.
(b)
A hotel may contract for the operation of a restaurant on its premises; such operation may qualify for and be granted a consumption-on-premises license.
(c)
Licenses obtained by hotels for delivery of alcoholic beverages in unbroken packages of the type for which it has a valid in-room service license by an employee of the hotel to registered guests' rooms or to a registered guest at any other location in the same building as the hotel when such beverages have been ordered by such guests and/or to provide a cabinet or other facility in a hotel guest's room which contains alcoholic beverages for which the hotel is licensed, and which is provided upon written request of the guest, and which is accessible by lock and key only to the guest, and for which the sale of the alcoholic beverages contained herein is final at the time requested; except for a credit which may be given to the guest for any unused and unopened portion. All alcoholic beverages sold or distributed under this section shall be obtained by the hotel directly from a licensed wholesaler and shall be kept stored separate from any other alcoholic beverage purchased for use in other licensed premises of the hotel until sold or served.
(d)
This is a separate license from the license required for a bar located in the establishment.

Except as set forth in this section, a brewpub licensee shall be subject to all sections of this chapter, and each brewpub licensee shall comply with all other applicable state and local license requirements including the limited sale of malt beverages to the public, and may sell on all days and at all times that sales of malt beverages by retailers are lawful within the city.

(1)

(h)
A Georgia farm winery licensee shall be authorized, in accordance with regulations of the state, to acquire and receive deliveries and shipments of wine in bulk from out-of-state producers and shippers in an amount not to exceed forty (40) percent of its annual production.
(i)
Any wine received in bulk pursuant to paragraph (h) of this subsection shall have levied thereon the requisite taxes as prescribed by section 4-176, and such taxes shall be reported and remitted to the city.

The boundaries of a specific event area(s) shall be defined and shown on a map, which, along with all notations, references, and explanatory matter thereon, are hereby adopted by reference and declared to be a part of, on the date of adoption, of this ordinance [from which this article derives]. a.
An area beginning at the intersection of South Broad Street and the railroad tracks, then east along the railroad tracks to a point in line with the east right-of-way margin of 1st St., SE, then south along said eastern margin of 1st St., SE to its intersection with 2nd Ave., SE, then west long the north right-of-way of 2nd Ave., SE, continuing across South Broad Street onto the north right-of-way of M. King, Jr. Ave., SW, then west along that right-of-way to its intersection with the western right-of-way margin of 1st St., SW, then north along 1st St., SW, crossing over 1st Ave., SW, and continuing north in a line to the railroad tracks, then east to the point of beginning. (Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-136. - Open areas and patio sales.
(a)
Alcoholic beverage sales can be made by a licensed consumption on premises establishment in a patio/open area type environment if the establishment has been approved as provided in this section.
(b)
The requirements for approval are:
(1)
The patio/open area must be enclosed by some structure providing for public ingress/egress only through the main licensed premises. The only exception to this would be a fire exit as required by building and fire codes. Such fire exit would not be for general public use unless an emergency exists and would be of the type which sounds an alarm so that the establishment would be alerted of unauthorized use if no emergency exists. The height of such structure shall be a minimum of three and one-half (3.5) feet above the patio floor. The structure is not required to be solid nor does it have to restrict visibility into or out of the patio/open area. The patio/open area shall meet all setback requirements as set forth in this Code.
(2)
The patio/open area shall not be required to be covered by a roof.
(3)
The patio/open area, shall be used merely as an extension of the interior seating area. Service in the patio/open area shall be only by waiter or waitress or by customer self-service at the selling location; i.e., no outside bar or service window.
(c)
It is prohibited for an establishment to be in violation of the city's noise ordinance as defined in chapter 12 of the Code of Ordinances and to allow any noise to be created in a patio/open area which may be heard from a distance of one hundred (100) feet from the patio/open area. Any measurement made pursuant to this section shall describe the shortest straight line distance from the patio to the point in question.
(d)
Patio/open area plans must be reviewed and approved on an individual basis by the chief of police. Patio/open areas must be permitted and approved by the city's building inspection and fire departments as required by their governing regulations or codes.
(e)
Nothing contained herein shall prohibit a hotel or motel with a consumption on the premises license from making sales and allowing consumption of alcoholic beverages in the patio areas of such hotel or motel. "Patio areas," as the term is used herein, do not have to conform to the standards in this section.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-137. - Sidewalk cafes.
(a)
Sidewalk cafes shall be operated by qualified restaurants on public sidewalks and regulated by the City of Cairo to protect the public health, safety, welfare, and the interest of the city and the primary use of the sidewalks by pedestrian traffic.
(1)
The use of sidewalks for restaurant tables shall only be permitted incidental to the operation of a "restaurant", and the use of the sidewalk to operate a sidewalk cafe is a privilege, not a right, and any person who violates the conditions of this section may be forbidden to operate a sidewalk cafe after notice and a hearing as per section 4-17.
(2)
Sidewalk cafes may be ordered closed temporarily by the City of Cairo whenever deemed necessary in the sole discretion of the city, whenever the area may be needed for a public purpose, including but not limited to construction, maintenance, public health, safety or welfare, emergencies, and public events such as parades or street parties.
(3)
The city may order the immediate removal or relocation of all, or parts of, a sidewalk cafe in emergency situations or for safety consideration, without notice. The city and its officers and employees shall not be responsible for sidewalk cafe fixtures relocated during emergencies.
(4)
In no event shall the uses permitted in this section reduce that portion of any sidewalk customarily used by pedestrians to less than five (5) feet in width. Additionally, contiguous pedestrian flow must be maintained between two (2) or more adjacent sidewalk cafes. The city may designate portions of the sidewalk which must not be obstructed under any circumstances, and may restrict usage in any manner deemed necessary by the city.
(5)
Only the area in front of a restaurant, as defined by projecting its interior width outward perpendicular to the street to a point at which it intersects the roadway (or within the sidewalk frontage of the building where the licensed restaurant is located) can be used by the restaurant.
(6)
Nothing will be placed within five (5) feet of a fire hydrant, pedestrian crosswalk, or handicapped corner curb cut.
(7)
Sidewalk cafe operators shall maintain the sidewalk and the curb and gutter immediately adjacent to it in a clean and safe condition. Refuse shall not be disposed of in the city's sidewalk trash receptacles.
(8)
No tables, chairs, or other parts of sidewalk cafes shall be attached, chained or in any manner affixed to any tree, post, sign, or other fixture, curb, or sidewalk in or near the permitted area. All tables and chairs must be of a design, material, and specification suitable for outside dining.
(b)
Only a properly licensed restaurant with a sidewalk cafe, which holds a valid alcohol license for consumption on the premises, may serve alcohol to customers in the sidewalk cafe, subject to the following restrictions:
(1)
Alcohol may only be possessed in the interior portion or the sidewalk cafe portion of the restaurant during the times as allowed for consumption of alcoholic beverages on premises.
(2)
Alcohol may not be served in its original container, but must be served in clear plastic cups. No bottles, glass, or cans are allowed.
(3)
Only a single serving of alcohol may be in possession of any patron.
(4)
Patrons must be seated while consuming an alcoholic beverage.
(5)
Service in the sidewalk cafe area shall be only by waiter or waitress or by customer self-service at the selling location; i.e., no outside bar or service window.
(6)
Smoking is prohibited at tables included in a sidewalk table plan permit area and within five (5) feet of any such area boundary.
(7)
Within the designated area of a sidewalk cafe, the operator holding a permit under this section shall have the right to limit access and occupancy to only bona fide paying customers of the operator's restaurant who are behaving in a lawful manner.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-138. - Private clubs.
(a)
Private clubs, meeting the definition of this chapter, are eligible to apply for a license for consumption on premises of alcoholic beverages.
(b)
No license shall be issued or renewed for the sale of alcoholic beverages prior to the furnishing of evidence, satisfactory to the city, that such private club has been approved as a tax exempt entity by the IRS.
(c)
A license issued under this subsection shall authorize the sale of alcoholic beverages for consumption only on the premises where sold.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-139. - Happy hour promotions.
(a)
As used in this subsection, the term "drink" means a single portion beverage which contains any alcoholic beverage.
(b)
This section shall be construed to cover, include and apply to all alcoholic beverages including malt beverages, wine, and distilled spirits for consumption on premises by properly licensed establishments in the city. All on-premises licensees are prohibited from selling or giving away alcoholic beverages under the following circumstances:
(1)
Offering all you can drink for a set price during a set time.
(2)
Serving multiple drinks for a single price unless the drinks are part of a variety sampler of which no more than a total of sixteen (16) ounces can be served as part of the sampler and which sampler shall be comprised of at least four (4) different varieties of malt beverages or wine or three (3) different mixes containing distilled spirits.
(3)
Making a single price the basis for a required purchase of two (2) or more servings.
(4)
Serving alcoholic beverages by the pitcher, except to two (2) or more persons at any one (1) time.
(5)
Offering reduced-price drinks to any segment of the population for any period of time as an inducement to patronize the premises; except between the hours of 5:00 p.m. and 7:00 p.m., Monday through Saturday, drinks may be sold at a reduced price which is no less than half the price usually charged.
(6)
Selling alcoholic beverages for less than half the normal retail price, or selling alcoholic beverages in pitchers or in jumbo sizes for less than half the normal retail price. Nothing contained in this subsection shall be construed to prohibit the dispensing of drinks in pitchers or in jumbo sizes, provided that such pitchers or jumbo sizes shall be available at all times that the licensee is open for business.
(7)
Using coupons or other special promotional items as an inducement to purchase alcoholic beverages.
(8)
Sponsoring, conducting or allowing contests or other promotions which have as their primary purpose increasing the consumption of alcoholic beverages.
(9)
Offering or delivering any free alcoholic beverage to the general public.
(c)
The prohibitions and restrictions in the foregoing subsection which apply to licensees or agents or employees of licensees shall not:
(1)
Apply at private functions;
(2)
Prohibit offering of free food or entertainment at any time;
(3)
Prohibit including drinks as part of the price of a hotel, travel, entertainment, or meal package;
(4)
Prohibit the sale or offer to sell, wine or malt beverage by the bottle, pitcher, or carafe;
(5)
Prohibit any motel or hotel from offering room service to registered guests.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-140. - Non-profit organizations with facilities.
(a)
Non-profit organizations meeting the criteria of subsection (b) below may obtain a consumption on premises license subject to the following conditions that the sale of alcoholic beverages on the premises shall be clearly incidental to the primary function of the non-profit organization.
(b)
To qualify for a consumption on premises license, the nonprofit organization must be exempt from federal income taxes pursuant to section 501(c), (d) or (e) of the Internal Revenue Code and must have a building, facility or grounds that is primarily used for public education, research, conservation and enjoyment, or that serves as a repository for a collection of literary, natural, or scientific objects of interest, or works of art where such facility or building is arranged, intended, designed and open to be viewed by members of the public with or without an admission fee. This shall include, but not limited to, a non-profit historical museum.
(c)
Non-profit organizations shall be subject to all sections of this chapter, and shall comply with all other applicable state and local license requirements, and may sell on all days and at all times that sales by retailers are lawful within the city.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-141. - Grady Cultural Center, Zebulon Theater, Grady County Museum and History Center, and downtown development authority.
(a)
The sale and consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises of the Grady Cultural Center, the Zebulon Theatre, and the Grady County Museum and History Center shall be allowed at city approved events.
(b)
The downtown development authority, after obtaining the proper license or permit, is allowed to sell alcohol by the drink at the Grady Cultural Center at events approved by the city. There shall be no alcoholic beverage license or permit fees paid to the city by the downtown development authority, the Grady Cultural Center, the Zebulon Theater, or the Grady County Museum and History Center, or their designee(s), but a license or permit may be issued which must be displayed on the premises. If no license is obtained for these non-profit organizations, alcohol event permits may be considered for special temporary events held, if applicable, and may also be catered by a licensed alcoholic beverage caterer.
(c)
The consumption on the premises license provided for herein is exempted from the food sale requirements provided for in section 4-7(b)(4) of this chapter.
(d)
The hours for sale of alcoholic beverages on the premises of either of these organizations shall be the same as provided in section 4-37 of this chapter.

ARTICLE IV. - SPECIAL ALCOHOL EVENTS

Sec. 4-160. - Special event temporary alcohol permits—Non-profits only.
(a)
Alcohol event permits may be issued by the City of Cairo for special events that are considered to be of a community nature, and that are advertised and open to the entire community, for the length of the event not to exceed one (1) day.
(b)
All alcohol event permits applications are required to be filed at least thirty (30) days prior to the event.
(1)
Any alcohol permit that is issued to a non-profit event applicant must hold a valid license which authorize the licensee to sell alcohol for consumption on the premises, or by the package for consumption off the premises, may be issued an off-premises license which authorizes such licensed alcoholic beverage non-profit applicant to sell alcohol by the drink off-premises and connection with an authorized non-profit function.
a.
If a group or individual has a fund-raising event where an admission fee is charged and alcoholic beverages are provided, then a special event permit is required. All alcoholic beverages for the event must be acquired from a wholesale distributor or provided by a licensed alcoholic beverage caterer. However, if a group or individual gathering has an event such as a wedding or any gathering where alcohol is provided to guests with no expectation of payment, then a special event permit would not be required. In this case, the alcohol must be obtained from a Georgia licensed retailer of alcoholic beverages.
(2)
Holders of a special temporary alcohol event permit shall be considered the same as any other licensee and subject to all laws, rules and regulations and other applicable sections of this chapter relating thereto. Entities selling, and persons consuming, alcoholic beverages under this section shall obey all state laws and city ordinances.
(3)
No more than four (4) temporary alcohol permits may be issued to a non-profit organization in any one (1) calendar year pursuant to this section.
(4)
Except as provided in this paragraph, manufacturers, brokers, importers, shippers, wholesalers, and retailers shall not make any donations of alcoholic beverages to any non-profit charitable or civic organization except where:
a.
A non-profit charitable or civic organization has obtained a special event permit, wholesalers shall be authorized to make donations of alcoholic beverages, provided that the alcoholic beverages were obtained through proper distribution channels and all applicable taxes have been paid.
b.
A non-profit charitable or civic organization has been issued a temporary special use permit authorizing the organization to sell alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises or to sell or auction wine only in unbroken packages for consumption off premises. Unlicensed individuals, licensed retailers, and licensed wholesalers shall be authorized to make donations of alcoholic beverages to such organizations, provided that the alcoholic beverages were obtained through proper distribution channels and all applicable taxes have been paid or will be paid. The organization holding the temporary special use permit may ship or otherwise transport donated wine to the location specified in the permit.
c.
The amount of such donations shall not exceed the amount necessary for the event for which a special event permit has been obtained.
(5)
At the request of a non-profit charitable or civic organization that holds a special event permit, manufacturers, brokers, importers, shippers, or wholesalers may donate services to the organization by having permitted representatives provide pouring services and product information during the event.
(6)
Nothing shall prohibit cash donations to charitable and civic organizations provided that such a donation is unconditional and not related to the purchase of a particular brand or brand label of alcoholic beverage.
(7)
Permits issued pursuant to this section shall be valid only for the place specified in the permit and are issued under the terms and conditions as determined by the city council and may include notification requirements, security, sanitation, clean-up, beginning and ending dates and times, and more. The permit issued by the city shall not be effective until such time as a permit has been obtained from the state.
(8)
Permittees may hold such events on the premises of a licensed manufacturer or wholesaler provided that all alcoholic beverages to be served or sold at the event are purchased from a licensed wholesaler or donated pursuant to this Code section.
(9)
If the event is a beer or wine tasting, then the event shall also be subject to the provisions of section 4-165.
(10)
If a designee has been appointed by the Grady Cultural Center, Zebulon, or Grady County Museum and History Center for alcoholic beverage services for consumption on the premises, then only the designee shall submit an application for a special temporary event permit to be held at these locations.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19; Ord. No. 842, § 3, 7-11-22)

Sec. 4-161. - City sanctioned or sponsored, and non-profit charitable, special event temporary alcohol permits.
(a)
A city sanctioned or sponsored special alcohol event is one (1) that is held on designated city property, which may include streets and rights-of-way, located in the city's designated special entertainment districts, that is organized and sponsored by the City of Cairo or an affiliate board or authority, such as the downtown development authority (DDA), consistent with the provisions of section 4-160 where alcohol is sold and consumed.
(b)
During city-sponsored special events allowing alcoholic beverages, members of the public shall be authorized to purchase, possess, and consume alcoholic beverages within a designated special event area subject to distance requirements for alcohol sales as outlined in this chapter and any law regulating the time for selling such beverages.
(c)
Bona fide nonprofit charitable and civic organizations, colleges and universities desiring to sell alcoholic beverages may apply to the city for a special temporary event permit at no fee, and if approved, may be authorized to sell distilled spirits and malt beverages for consumption on the premises or to sell wine for off-premises consumption, or both, for a period not to exceed three (3) consecutive days, subject to distance requirements for alcohol sales as outlined in this chapter and any law regulating the time for selling such beverages.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-162. - Special event use permits.
(a)
A special event use permit may be issued for the sale of alcoholic beverage for certain events which would otherwise require a retail dealer's license. Events that shall qualify shall be specified by the state; provided, however, that estate sales, the sale of inventory authorized under a bankruptcy proceeding, inventory auctions, and activities that are similar in nature shall so qualify. Such permit shall not be valid for more than ten (10) days.
(b)
The licensee for a permitted special event use permit shall designate and clearly mark, by the use of ropes, barricades, stanchions or similar devices, a specific area that alcoholic beverages may be consumed.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to drink, or attempt to drink, any alcoholic beverage from a can, bottle, or glass, or to possess in an open can, bottle, or glass any alcoholic beverage on the streets, sidewalks, rights-of-way, and/or parking lots, whether public or private.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-163. - Outside consumption of alcohol—Open container; exception.
(a)
As used in this section, "open container" means any container, containing alcoholic beverages, which is immediately capable of being consumed from or the seal of which has been broken.
(b)
No person shall possess an open container of any alcoholic beverage while walking, standing, or otherwise occupying any public street, road, or highway, sidewalk adjacent thereto, public parking lot, or other property owned or leased by the city, except as provided for in this section. However, any restaurant which is licensed to sell wine for consumption on the premises may permit a patron to remove one (1) partially-consumed bottle of wine per section 4-92.
(c)
The prohibition of this section shall not apply where the city council has approved by ordinance, a city-owned or leased building, location or facility, as one (1) where alcoholic beverages may be sold and consumed, or at any city-sponsored event or approved special event in an area designated as a special entertainment district. The prohibition of this section shall not apply within a special entertainment district during a permitted event during the hours allowed for the sale and consumption of alcoholic beverages; provided all alcoholic beverages shall be in clear, plastic cups no greater than sixteen (16) ounces, with said cups to be disposed of before leaving the specific boundaries of the area. In addition, the person consuming or possessing such alcohol is required to wear an approved date-stamped wristband or armband provided by the licensed alcohol dealer.
(d)
No person shall possess an open container of any alcoholic beverage on the premises of any retail package dealer, including parking lots adjacent thereto, at any time.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-164. - Catered events.
(a)
Any licensed alcoholic beverage caterer who additionally holds a valid license which authorizes the licensee to sell alcohol for consumption on the premises, or by the package for consumption off the premises, may be issued an off-premises license which authorizes such licensed alcoholic beverage caterer to sell alcohol by the drink off premises and in connection with an authorized catered function. The alcoholic beverage caterer shall submit a written application on forms provided by the city and shall pay to the city a permit fee per section 4-12. Only a licensed alcoholic beverage caterer may distribute or sell alcoholic beverages by the drink at an authorized catered event, after the approval of a catered event permit application which has been signed by the caterer and the host or sponsor, as well as the owner of the venue or the owner's authorized agent if the host or sponsor is not the owner of the venue, giving their consent to the distribution of alcoholic beverages at that location. A licensed alcoholic beverage caterer may only sell that type of alcoholic beverage which is authorized by his alcoholic beverage license.
(b)
If the licensed alcohol caterer does not maintain a place of business within the city, there shall be an out of town permit fee as set out in section 4-12. At all times during an authorized catered function, the alcoholic beverage caterer's alcohol license and the event permit shall be conspicuously posted and made available for inspection upon request by any police officer.
(c)
Before a licensed alcoholic beverage caterer may sell or dispense alcoholic beverages at any authorized catered function, such caterer shall apply for a catered event permit with the city clerk or his/her designee at least thirty (30) working days prior to the event.
(1)
The application for a catered event permit shall include the name of the alcoholic beverage caterer, the caterer's license number, type of alcohol to be sold, and the date, address and time of the event.
(2)
If the catered event permit is granted, it shall be good only for the specific event at the specified address and times set forth in the application, not in excess of six (6) hours, unless otherwise approved by the city council.
(3)
The permit shall be kept in the vehicle used to transport alcoholic beverages in unbroken packages to the event at all times during transportation to the event. Such vehicle shall be marked only with the state license number with lettering that shall be two (2) inches high and one (1) inch wide on each side of the vehicle. No other wording or advertisement of the business shall be allowed.
(d)
Licensed caterers shall maintain a record of all alcoholic beverages transported for each event.
(e)
No alcoholic beverages shall be transported, distributed, or sold to other than licensed locations in the city except to approved, catered events; unless otherwise authorized by this chapter or by state law.
(f)
No licensed alcoholic beverage caterer shall employ any person under twenty-one (21) years of age to dispense, serve, sell or handle alcoholic beverages at authorized catered functions.
(g)
No catered event permit will be issued for an event to be held at any location with respect to which any alcohol license has been denied or revoked within the past twelve (12) months.
(h)
No broken packages of alcoholic beverage may be removed by the licensed alcoholic beverage caterer from the site of the authorized catered event to his/her place of business or any other location, but shall become the property of the event sponsor.
(i)
The hours and days of sale or distribution of alcoholic beverages at an authorized catered event shall be conducted in compliance with this chapter. Except as set forth above in this section, an off-premises permit holder must comply with all other provisions set forth in this chapter.

Sec. 4-165. - Tasting; malt beverage and wine tasting.
(a)
A license shall be required for a "tasting room" location set up as a retail business for the tasting and selling of malt beverages and wine, and all the restrictions provided in this Code shall also apply.
(b)
A request to conduct a special malt beverage or wine tasting event may be approved by the city council when such request is made in writing on an application furnished by the city clerk, and a non-refundable permit fee is submitted as set forth in section 4-12. If issued, the tasting shall be conducted under the following conditions:
(1)
The applicant shall be an establishment that is licensed to sell beer or wine, in original packages for off-premises consumption.
(2)
The applicant shall maintain on the premises and offer for sale at all times a variety of wines from not less than fifteen (15) manufacturers/brands of wine and/or malt beverages.
(3)
The applicant's establishment shall have minimum interior floor area of one hundred (100) square feet devoted to the storage, display, and sale of wine and/or malt beverages.
(4)
The applicant for a wine or malt beverage tasting must hold a valid retail package wine or malt beverage license.
(5)
No tasting may be conducted on the premises of any place of business licensed to sell distilled spirits in the unbroken package or container at retail.
(c)
All tasting events shall be subject to the following restrictions:
(1)
No customer shall receive more than eighteen (18) ounces of wine per event or tasting, and no individual portion shall exceed three (3) ounces.
(2)
No customer shall receive more than thirty-two (32) ounces of malt beverage per event or tasting, and no individual portion shall exceed eight (8) ounces.
(3)
Customers shall not remove opened packages or unsealed bottles from the premises.
(4)
If the tasting event is held for charitable purposes, the event holder shall conspicuously display a sign at the event identifying the name of the charity. Records shall be maintained showing the amount raised, the cost of putting on the event, and the actual amount remitted to the charity.

All wines secured for tasting purposes must be obtained through a retail or wholesale wine outlet.

Home brewed malt beverages for purposes of this section shall be limited to malt beverages produced by an individual(s) pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 3-5-4 and any applicable rules and regulations of the Georgia Department of Revenue.

ARTICLE V. - EXCISE TAX

Sec. 4-176. - Excise tax required of wholesale dealers and manufacturers.
(a)
In addition to the license fees required by this chapter, there is hereby levied and imposed upon each wholesale dealer distributing malt beverages, wine, or distilled spirits within the City of Cairo a specific excise tax as follows.
(1)
For malt beverages sold in bottles, cans, or other containers, except barrel or bulk containers, a tax of five cents ($0.05) per twelve (12) ounces, or metric equivalent, and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of twelve (12) ounces.
(2)
For malt beverages sold in or from a barrel or bulk container, commonly known as tap or draft beer, a tax of six dollars ($6.00) on each container sold containing not more than fifteen and one-half (15½) gallons, or metric equivalent, and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of fifteen and one-half (15½) gallons.
(3)
For wine or distilled spirits, or wine sold to persons outside this state for resale or consumption outside this state, a tax computed on the basis of twenty-two cents ($0.22) per liter, or metric equivalent, and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter.
(b)
Brewers, distillers, or wineries serving or selling alcoholic beverages directly to the public must remit all local excise taxes as set forth in subsection (a) to the City of Cairo.
(c)
Malt beverages and wines which contain less than one-half (½) of one (1) percent alcohol by volume shall not be subject to any tax levied under this Code section.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-177. - Payment of taxes; reports.
(a)
Excise taxes provided for in this section shall be imposed upon and paid by the licensed wholesale dealer; provided, however, that such taxes shall be imposed upon and shall be paid by the licensed local manufacturer of malt beverages, distilled spirits, or wine served or sold directly to the public pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-5-24.1.
(b)
Taxes shall be paid on or before the tenth day of the month following the calendar month in which alcohol is disposed of in the city.
(c)
Each wholesaler or manufacturer of alcoholic beverages responsible for the payment of the excise tax shall file a report with the city for the preceding calendar month showing the exact quantities of malt beverages, by size and type of container; and for wine and spirituous liquors, showing the quantities delivered to each dealer and the amount of excise tax collected.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-178. - Penalty and accrued interest on late reports and delinquent payments.
The failure to make a timely report and remittance shall render a wholesale dealer or licensed brewer or distiller liable for a penalty equal to ten (10) percent of the total amount due, plus interest accrued at the rate of twelve (12) percent per annum. The clerk of the City of Cairo shall issue an execution against the said dealer, firm, or corporation so delinquent on his or its property, for the amount of the excise tax due and delinquent, plus penalty and interest accrued, and shall proceed to collect same in the same manner as is done in the case of executions issued for collection of city ad valorem property taxes.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-179. - False reports or nonpayment declared grounds for revocation of licenses of wholesale dealers or manufacturers of alcoholic beverages.
At any time it is found that false and fraudulent reports have been made by the wholesale dealer or brewer, his agents, officers, or employees, and upon which payment of excise tax was reported or paid to the city, and at any time the monthly report and payment called for in section 4-177 is past due over thirty (30) days, the licensee shall be given not less than three (3) days nor more than ten (10) days' notice of hearing before the mayor and council to show cause why his license to sell malt beverages in the City of Cairo should not be suspended or revoked because of such false and fraudulent reports, or nonpayment of taxes past due more than thirty (30) days.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
Sec. 4-180. - Sale of alcoholic beverages upon which tax was not paid prohibited; penalties.
It shall be a violation of this Code for any person to sell at retail or otherwise within this city, any alcoholic beverages on which the tax as above set out has not been paid to the wholesaler or to the city as above provided. In addition to the penalties provided for in section 9-3 of this Code, the violation of this article shall be deemed a sufficient ground for the revocation of the license of any retail or wholesale dealer to sell alcoholic beverages within the city.
(Ord. No. 810, § 1, 2-11-19)
