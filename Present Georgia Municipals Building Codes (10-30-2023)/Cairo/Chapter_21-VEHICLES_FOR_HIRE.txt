Chapter 21 - VEHICLES FOR HIRE
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Secs. 21-1—21-19. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - TAXICABS

Sec. 21-20. - License—Required.
No person shall operate a taxicab within the city without first procuring a license.
(Code 1965, § 31-19)
Sec. 21-21. - Same—Application.
(a)
Any person proposing to operate a taxicab shall make written application to the city clerk on or before the first day of April of each year, stating the name, age, color, and sex of applicant; ownership, model, make, and serial number of vehicle; whether or not such vehicle is covered by public liability and property damage insurance.
(b)
The applicant shall submit with each application a written, sworn statement signed by the owner or shop foreman of a licensed automobile repair shop approved by city manager, that such motor vehicle has been inspected, tested, and found to be in good mechanical condition and safe for operation.
(Code 1965, § 31-20)
Sec. 21-22. - Same—Annual fees.
The annual fee shall be as prescribed in section 14-4.
(Code 1965, § 31-21; Ord. No. 690, § 1, 4-28-08)
Sec. 21-23. - Same—Granting or denying.
(a)
Action by license committee: The city clerk shall examine each application and endorse such recommendation thereon as it considers best for the welfare, interest and protection of the public, and transmit the application to the city clerk for submission to the governing body for action.
(b)
Action by governing body: The governing body shall act upon each application within a reasonable time and shall have full authority to grant or deny any application upon any ground which it deems reasonable and just, either with or without cause.
(Code 1965, §§ 31-24, 31-25)
Sec. 21-24. - Same—Insurance.
(a)
No application or vehicle license shall be grated until the vehicle has been covered by liability and property damage insurance in the following amounts: One hundred thousand dollars ($100,000.00) liability coverage per person, three hundred thousand dollars ($300,000.00) liability coverage per accident, and fifty thousand dollars ($50,000.00) property damage.
(b)
The insurance shall run from the first day of April of any year in which a taxicab license is issued through the thirty-first day of March of the year following. The insurance policy shall run concurrently with the license issued to operate taxicabs in the city, whether or not the license to operate is issued on the first day of April of any year or some date subsequent thereto.
(c)
No insurance policy presented with the application for a taxicab license will meet the requirements of this section unless the policy is issued by an insurance company licensed to do business within the state.
(d)
Should the insurance cease to be effective or operative, the license shall be immediately suspended and revoked.
(Code 1965, §§ 31-26, 31-27; Ord. No. 710, § 1, 5-11-09)
Sec. 21-25. - Same—Suspension and revocation.
Should the holder of any taxicab license violate any of the provisions of this article, such holder's license may be suspended by the city manager without notice at the next regular meeting of the governing body, and may be revoked by the governing body after notice and hearing.
(Code 1965, § 31-28)
Sec. 21-26. - Driver's permit—Application; carrying.
(a)
All taxicab drivers or operators shall, on or before the first day of April of each year, file with the city clerk an application for a driver's permit stating his age, color and sex.
(b)
The applicant shall also submit a statement signed by some physician practicing medicine in the city that such applicant has been examined and found to be physically fit to perform such duties.
(c)
A driver's permit, when granted, shall be carried upon the driver's person at all times while engaged in the operation of a taxicab.
(Code 1965, § 31-22)
Sec. 21-27. - Same—Fee.
The annual fee for a driver's permit shall be five dollars ($5.00).
(Code 1965, § 31-23)
Sec. 21-28. - Vehicle markings; lights.
(a)
All taxicabs shall be distinctively marked with painted signs and equipped with lights prescribed by the chief of police and approved by the city manager.
(b)
Such lights shall be turned on and displayed at all times between sunset and sunrise while the taxicab is in operation.
(Code 1965, § 31-11)
Sec. 21-29. - Safe condition of vehicle required for operation.
No person shall operate a taxicab within the city unless it is in good repair and safe mechanical condition.
(Code 1965, § 31-12)
Sec. 21-30. - Vehicle inspections.
(a)
Every taxicab shall be inspected and tested by a trained and experienced mechanic between the first and fifth day of each calendar month.
(b)
The results of such inspection shall be sworn to by the owner or shop foreman of a licensed automobile repair shop approved by the city manager and filed with the chief of police.
(c)
The chief of police or city manager may require such mechanical inspection weekly or bi-monthly if the age, operation or mechanical condition of any particular vehicle is deemed to be unsafe for public use and hire.
(Code 1965, § 31-13)
