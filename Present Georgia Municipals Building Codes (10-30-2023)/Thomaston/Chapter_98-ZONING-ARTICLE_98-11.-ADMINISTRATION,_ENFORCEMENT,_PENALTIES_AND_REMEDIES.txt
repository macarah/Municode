ARTICLE 98-11. - ADMINISTRATION, ENFORCEMENT, PENALTIES AND REMEDIES

Sec. 98-11.1. - Authority of the building official.
The building official shall administer and enforce the provisions of this chapter. The building official shall also review construction drawings and architectural plans; issue building permits, certificates of occupancy and certificates of zoning compliance; and conduct premises inspections. The building official shall also serve as code enforcement officer.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-11.2. - Certificates of zoning compliance.
A.
No representation by any official or employee of the city shall in any way legally bind the city or constitute any determination that the building(s) or use(s) located on or proposed for the property involved complies with the city zoning ordinance, or any appendix thereto, in the absence of a certificate of zoning compliance. No owner of property within the city or his or her agent, successor or assign shall be entitled to rely upon any such representation or claim any vested right to build or maintain any building or structure or conduct any use located on or proposed for the property as a result of any such representation.
B.
The owner of the property or his or her agent may apply for a certificate of zoning compliance from the building official to determine in advance whether a building, structure or use existing or proposed for any property located within the city is being used or may be used or developed in compliance with this chapter.
C.
Such applications shall be in writing on a form provided by the city requiring the following information regarding the property for which a certificate is being requested:
1.
Identity of the owner of the property;
2.
Identity of any agent of the owner of the property;
3.
Property address and Property Identification Number;
4.
A complete description and inventory of all existing buildings and structures on the property; (tier 2, only)
5.
A complete description and inventory of all proposed buildings and structures to be located on the property; (tier 2, only)
6.
A complete description and inventory of all uses established on the property; (tier 2, only)
7.
A complete description and inventory of all uses to be established on the property; (tier 2, only) and
8.
An ALTA survey of the property representing the current condition. (tier 2, only)
D.
The building official shall provide a zoning certificate to the owner within ten business days of receipt of an application for a certificate. Certification of the zoning of the property, the future land use map designation and the zoning designations of abutting properties shall comprise a tier 1 certificate. Certification of compliance of the building(s), use(s) and site shall comprise a tier 2 certificate. Applications for zoning certificates will be processed within ten business days of receipt of a complete application and payment of the appropriate fee.
E.
Issuance of a tier 2 zoning certificate shall merely constitute confirmation that the buildings, structures and uses located on or proposed for the property have been determined to be in compliance with the zoning ordinance of the city effective on the date of issuance of the certificate and may be legally conducted thereon as of the date of issuance of the certificate.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-11.3. - Certificates of occupancy (CO).
A.
Certificates of occupancy shall be issued by the building official. No certificate of occupancy shall be issued unless the lot, use and building comply with all provisions of this chapter. A CO must be issued by the building official prior to any occupancy of a building or use of a lot and shall be required under the following circumstances:
1.
Initial use of any lot or expansion of an existing use of a lot;
2.
A change in the use of an existing building or buildings on the lot or a change in use of the lot;
3.
A building has been erected, moved onto the lot, or altered in any manner that required a building permit; or
4.
Any nonconforming lot, building or use existing as of the effective date of this chapter or amendment thereto that is changed to a conforming lot, building or use.
B.
The building official shall maintain a record of all CO's. A copy of a certificate shall upon receipt of a written request be furnished to any person having a proprietary or tenancy interest in the building or lot involved.
C.
The building official shall upon receipt of an application for a certificate of occupancy assess the building and the proposed use and make a determination as to compliance with all applicable zoning; building; traffic and life safety codes, standards, and regulations in cooperation with the Thomaston fire chief and Thomaston police chief. The building official shall also assess compliance of the construction or alteration with the plans submitted with the building permit application and approved by the building official. The building official shall issue a certificate of occupancy to the applicant upon a determination that these requirements have been met. The CO shall clearly identify the use being permitted, any limitations imposed on the use, and a description of any portion of a building or land not to be so used. The building official shall decline to issue a CO for any building, lot or portion thereof upon a determination that these requirements have not been met and shall notify the applicant in writing via hand delivery or certified mail return receipt requested of the denial of the certificate stating the reasons for such denial. Such notice shall be deemed to have been given a minimum of three business days following such mailing.
D.
The building official may exclude single family and two family dwellings from the requirement to obtain a certificate of occupancy and may deem final inspection to be final approval.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-11.4. - Conformance prerequisite to issuance of occupation tax permit.
The city clerk shall issue an occupation tax permit only upon written confirmation by the building official that the proposed use conforms to the standards of the zoning district in which the business entity is located or is to be located, or written confirmation that the proposed use is a legal, nonconforming use. A duly issued certificate of occupancy shall be evidence of such conformity.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-11.5. - Special event permits subject to city manager approval.
A.
Authority to approve a special event shall rest with the city manager who may issue an administrative permit to conduct a special event subject to the following standards:
1.
The period of the special event shall not exceed 14 calendar days;
2.
The event shall be conducted a minimum of 200 feet from any residential district or use;
3.
No sleeping accommodations shall be permitted on-site;
4.
Employees associated with the event shall be uniformed and provided with identification badges;
5.
Security shall be provided on-site during operating hours;
6.
Sanitary facilities to accommodate expected participant volumes shall be provided;
7.
An indication of parking adequate to meet the projected demand.
B.
Any outdoor use that requires a tent(s) larger than 144 square feet shall be subject to approval by the Thomaston fire chief.
C.
Applications for special event permits, including payment of all applicable fees, shall be filed with the city clerk no later than ten business days prior to the event.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-11.6. - Interpretive authority of the city manager.
A.
The city manager shall have the authority to determine whether a proposed use of property that is not specifically permitted or prohibited in a particular zoning district may be permitted. The city manager may exercise discretion and deny the use based on a finding of inconsistency with other uses allowed in the zoning district or allow the use as substantially similar to uses permitted in that zoning district. Such authority shall not apply to uses requiring approval as a special use.
B.
The city manager shall consider the following factors in assessing similarities between the proposed use and uses permitted in the zoning district in which such use is proposed:
1.
Amount of vehicle traffic the proposed use will generate compared to uses permitted in the zoning district;
2.
Impact of the proposed use on public infrastructure and services relative to uses permitted in the zoning district;
3.
Relationship of the proposed use to the purposes of the zoning district;
4.
Manner of operation, including the hours of operation and days of the week, compared to uses permitted in the zoning district; and
5.
Consistency of the proposed use with the goals, objectives and policies of the comprehensive plan.
C.
The applicant shall be responsible for submitting evidence and information establishing the comparative similarities of the proposed use with uses permitted in the zoning district assigned to the property.
D.
A decision of the city manager to deny a proposed use may be appealed to the board of zoning appeals as provided in subsection 98-13.2.3, powers, duties and responsibilities, generally. The criteria used by the board of zoning appeals in determining whether the city manager erred in his or her decision shall be the consistency of the proposed use with the above factors compared to permitted uses. The city manager shall provide written notice to the city council concerning any decision involving the exercise of his or her interpretive authority immediately upon rendering such decision.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-11.7. - Administrative variances.
The building official is hereby authorized to grant an administrative variance from the standards of this chapter upon a finding that the intent of the ordinance can be achieved and equal performance obtained by granting such variance. The criteria applied by the board of zoning appeals in deciding variance applications found in subsection 98-13.2.3, powers, duties and responsibilities, generally, shall be used by the building official in rendering a decision. Authority to grant an administrative variance shall be limited to the following deviations from the standards of the ordinance:
A.
Reduction in front yard setback or a yard adjacent to a public street, not to exceed ten feet.
B.
Reduction in side yard setback, not to exceed five feet.
C.
Reduction in rear yard setback, not to exceed five feet.
D.
Increase in building height, not to exceed five feet.
E.
Reduction in distance between buildings on a lot, not to exceed five feet.
F.
Number of parking spaces, not to exceed ten percent of the applicable parking standard, excluding accessible parking.
G.
Reduction in buffer width, not to exceed 25 percent of the required width, provided the intent of the required buffer can be achieved and the buffer is not a condition of zoning.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-11.8. - Inspection; right of entry.
Upon presentation of city identification to the developer, contractor, owner, owner's agent, operator or occupant, city employees or agents authorized by the city manager may enter any property during all reasonable hours for purposes of performing inspections to determine compliance with the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-11.9. - Penalties for violation.
Any person violating any provision of this chapter shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in chapter 1, general provision, section 1-11, general penalty; continuing violations, of this Code.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-11.10. - Remedies.
A.
In the event any building is constructed, reconstructed, repaired, converted or maintained, or any building or lot is used in violation of this chapter, the building official or other appropriate authority may, in addition to other remedies and after due notice by hand delivery or certified mail return receipt requested to the owner of the premises in violation, issue a citation for violation of this chapter. Such notice shall be deemed to have been given a minimum of three business days following such mailing. The building official may also institute injunction, mandamus or other appropriate action or proceeding to prevent unlawful construction, reconstruction, alteration, conversion, maintenance or use of a building or lot, to abate the violation, or to prevent occupancy of a building or lot in violation of this chapter.
B.
Where a violation of this chapter exists, the building official may, in addition to other remedies, require that public utility services be withheld from the premises until such time as the building, the use of the lot or the premises themselves are no longer in violation of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
