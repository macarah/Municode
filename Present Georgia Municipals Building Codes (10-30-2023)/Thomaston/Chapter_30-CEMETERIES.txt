ARTICLE I. - CITY CEMETERIES

Sec. 30-1. - Applicability of this chapter.
The provisions of article I shall provide for the governance of all cemeteries established by the city.
(Ord. No. 1115, 8-20-19)
Sec. 30-2. - Description of cemeteries.
(a)
Glenwood Cemetery. That certain property located in the southeastern portion of the city, containing 8.25 acres, in Land Lot Nos. 217 and 218 of the Tenth Land District, shown and delineated as GLENWOOD CEMETERY, on that plat of survey entitled "PLAT FOR CITY OF THOMASTON," prepared by Charles Lamar Moore and Gary F. Self, Registered Professional Land Surveyors, and of record in Plat Record 13, page 176, in the office of the clerk of superior court of the county, which said plat, together with the metes, bounds, courses and distances shown and delineated thereon, is by this reference, incorporated herein, in aid of this description, and all future contiguous additions thereto that are dedicated by the city for cemetery purposes.
(b)
South View Cemetery.
(1)
Tract 1: That certain property located in the southern portion of the city, as is shown and designated as Sections A, A Extension, B, C, D, E, F, G and H on plats of record in Plat Book 1, page 159, and Plat Book 7, pages 224 and 225, Plat Book 29, page 244 and Plat Book 33, page 40, in the clerk's office, superior court of the county, is set aside as a cemetery to be known as South View Cemetery, and is to have such driveways, walkways, burial lots, and memorial lots as are more fully shown by the plat of the sections of the cemetery referred to in this section, which said plats, together with the metes, bounds, courses and distances shown and delineated thereon, are by this reference, incorporated herein, in aid of this description, and all future contiguous additions thereto that are dedicated by the city for cemetery purposes; and
(2)
Tract 2: That certain property located in the southern portion of the city, abutting the southern boundary line of the above described portion of South View Cemetery, containing 20 acres, more or less, situate, lying and being Land Lots Nos. 219 and 224 of the Tenth Land District and more particularly described in those quitclaim deeds recorded in Deed Book 254, pages 254 through 259, in the office of the clerk of the superior court of the county, and all future contiguous additions thereto that are dedicated by the city for cemetery purposes.
(Ord. No. 1115, 8-20-19; Ord. No. 1144, 3-2-21)
Sec. 30-3. - Conditions, restrictions, rules and regulations.
Burial lots in each of said cemeteries shall be held and used subject to the conditions, restrictions, limitations, rules and regulations set forth herein, and shall be used for interment of human remains, and for no other purpose.
(1)
Conditions, restrictions, rules and regulations specifically applying to Glenwood Cemetery:
a.
No through traffic. It shall be unlawful to use the roads in Glenwood Cemetery as a route for through traffic.
b.
Gate closings. The gates will be open to traffic from sunup to sundown.
c.
Curbing, fences, etc. The city reserves the right to remove any curbing, fences, etc., that are deemed to be inappropriate for Glenwood Cemetery or is a traffic hindrance.
(2)
Conditions, restrictions, rules and regulations applying to South View Cemetery.
a.
i.
Grave slabs and headstone monuments shall be permitted in Sections A, A Extension, B, C and D, of the cemetery as shown on the plats mentioned in the description of South View Cemetery referenced above in this subsection, subject to the provisions of this chapter.
ii.
Headstone monuments shall be allowed in Section E, lots 111—257, Section F, lots 91—156, Section G, lots 1—58 and Section H of the cemetery as shown on the plats mentioned in the description of South View Cemetery referenced above in this subsection, subject to the provisions of this chapter. No grave slabs are allowed in the sections listed herein.
iii.
As to Sections E lots 111-257; Section F, lots 1-156; Section G, Lots 59-230; and Section H, lots —172, an exception shall be made to permit markers in each of the designated lots, to include veteran and non-veteran markers. The markers shall either be flat bronze, granite or marble markers. The dimensions of the flat bronze markers shall not exceed 24 inches in length, 12 inches in width, with a three-fourths-inch rise, and it shall weigh no more than 18 pounds, for a single lot. The dimensions of the flat markers made of granite or marble shall not exceed 24 inches in length, 12 inches in width with a four-inch rise, and it shall weigh no more than 130 pounds, for a single lot. Should there be two contiguous lots at which one marker is desired: (1) the dimensions of the flat bronze marker shall not exceed 48 inches in length, 20 inches in width, with a three-fourths-inch rise, and it shall weigh no more than 36 pounds, and (2) the dimensions of the markers made of granite or marble shall not exceed 48 inches in length, 20 inches in width with a four-inch rise, and it shall not weigh more than 260 pounds. Further, all markers shall be placed in the ground and installed flush with the ground surface. The markers shall be installed so that lawnmowers may easily traverse the grave site without causing damage to the mower or the stone marker. Installation of the markers shall be performed by either a funeral home or monument company. The city reserves, and it shall have, the right to remove any markers that are non-conforming to the provisions set forth herein above. The city shall not be liable for any damage to the markers.
iv.
As to Section F, lots 1—90 ("cremation section"), an additional exception shall be made to permit horizontal markers for any individual buried in this section provided that the total number of markers per lot does not exceed two. The lots are six feet wide and nine feet long, and four urns are allowed in each lot.
Said markers shall either be flat bronze grave markers, similar to the flat bronze grave markers for veterans, or flat granite or marble markers, similar to those for veterans. The dimensions of the flat bronze markers shall not exceed 24 inches in length, 12 inches in width, with a three-quarter-inch rise, and they shall weigh approximately 18 pounds. The dimensions of the flat markers made of granite or marble shall not exceed 24 inches in length, 12 inches in width with a four-inch rise, and they shall weigh approximately 130 pounds. Further the markers shall be placed flat in the ground and installed flush with the ground surface. Installation shall be performed by either a funeral home or monument company, and the markers shall be installed so that lawnmowers may easily traverse the grave site without causing damage to the mower or the stone marker. The city shall be allowed to remove said markers if they are non-conforming to the provisions set forth herein above.
b.
Mausoleums may be placed only in Section E, lots 1—110.
c.
Cremains may be placed in the cremation section, which is Section F, lots 1—90. For Section F, lots 1—90, only, up to two urns are allowed per lot. Cremains may also be placed in another section, if the lot is purchased at the full price of the lot selected, and only one urn is allowed per lot.
d.
The following lots in Section C are to be sold as a one grave lot due to the ambulance drive encroaching upon the lots. The cost of the lot will be one-half the lot cost for Section C as set forth in the schedule of fees at the time of purchase. The schedule of fees for Section C is set for each lot being a two grave lot. The lots include 34, 35, 72, 110, 111, 146, 147, 182, 183, 381, 404, 427, 454, 477, 504, 527, 554, 627, 654, 677, 704, 727, 754, 777, 804, 827.
e.
No curbing, fencing, border or enclosure of any sort shall be erected on, in or around any burial lot in the cemetery, and the city reserves the right to remove any such enclosure. The city reserves the right to erect curbing along the walks and driveways, if it so desires.
f.
It shall be unlawful for any person to install a cement or gravel floor or covering on any burial lot.
(3)
Conditions, restrictions, rules and regulations applying to all cemeteries of the city:
a.
For each burial lot and mausoleum lot the city shall charge and receive a sum as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges adopted, from time to time, by the mayor and council, copies of which shall be available from the city clerk's office.
b.
Conveyances of burial lots shall be executed in the name of the city by the mayor or city manager, and the city clerk. Such conveyances shall not convey fee simple title, but shall convey to the purchaser of each burial lot an easement for the exclusive right of interment and sepulture in the lot, shall state the maximum number of graves allowed on each such burial lot as shown by the plat referred to in the deed, with the exception of the lots described in section 30-3(2)d. and by reference therein made shall convey each lot subject to all the provisions of this chapter and all amendments thereto as fully as if set out in each such conveyance. A copy of this chapter shall be delivered to the buyer upon purchase of each lot.
c.
At the time the city conveys any burial lot, in addition to collecting the purchase price of the burial lot, the city shall collect from the purchaser an amount sufficient to have such conveyance recorded in the clerk's office, county superior court, and shall have such conveyance so recorded before delivery thereof to such purchaser.
d.
The city manager or his designee is designated as superintendent of the cemeteries.
e.
It shall be unlawful to remove, cover, or in any other manner disturb the survey markers located in the cemetery.
f.
For sections allowing slabs, all slabs shall conform to the contour of the lot on which they are located, and the top of all slabs shall be flush with the ground so that no portion of the slab shall exceed ground level at any point.
g.
A mausoleum must be located a distance from the front line of the lot on which it is placed at least equivalent to the depth, from front to rear, of the structure itself, and, from all other sides of the lot, it shall be located a distance equal to the greatest height of the structure. No bodies shall be placed in such mausoleum except in sealed caskets and in hermetically sealed compartments.
h.
Each poured headstone foundation shall extend beyond the bottom base of the headstone at least one inch in all directions, and the vertical measurement of the poured headstone foundation shall be the same as the vertical measurement of the bottom base of the headstone monument.
i.
The maximum width of a headstone monument base shall not exceed 60 percent of the burial lot width, and there shall be no more than one upright marker for every two graves permitted on a burial lot.
j.
No mound over any grave nor any other change in the grade or contour of any burial lot, except temporarily in connection with interment, shall be made by any lot holder or other person.
k.
The number of graves located in or on a burial lot shall not exceed the maximum fixed for each such lot and the graves and grave slabs shall be symmetrically placed as illustrated by the lot detail on plats of the cemetery referred to in this chapter.
l.
No monument, statue, inscription or other thing which is unsightly or which in any way negatively reflects on any person, living or dead, or on any religious sect, shall be erected or maintained in the cemetery. The city reserves the right to remove any such item.
m.
No trees or shrubs of any kind shall be planted, or allowed to remain, within the borders of any burial lot or lots. Plantings in all areas outside of burial lots shall be under the control of the mayor and council. The city retains the right to enter upon any area of the cemeteries and remove any tree or shrub which is either deemed to be in violation of this subsection or which may cause damage to any grave site or the area around a grave site.
n.
Any dead flowers or unsightly items are to be removed from the grounds. Any such items left on graves may be removed at the discretion of the cemetery superintendent or any agent contracted with the city to care for cemeteries. Such items will be removed without warning and without compensation to the parties placing such items on the cemetery grounds.
o.
The mayor and city council reserve to themselves and their successors in office the right to alter, amend, modify or add to the rules, regulations, conditions and restrictions set forth in this chapter, at any time it is deemed advisable so to do in order to carry out the purposes of this chapter, or to assure the proper care and administration of the city's cemeteries.
p.
It shall be unlawful to bury a decedent in a lot in which the decedent owns no interest and has no permission to be buried in.
(Ord. No. 1115, 8-20-19; Ord. No. 1130, 7-21-20; Ord. No. 1135, 9-15-20; Ord. No. 1141, 12-15-20; Ord. No. 1142, 1-5-21; Ord. No. 1144, 3-2-21; Ord. No. 1146, 3-16-21; Ord. No. 1173 , 7-14-22)
Sec. 30-4. - Grave site opening—Permit required; inspection of work by city manager or his designee; noncompliance.
(a)
Permit required. It shall be the duty and responsibility of the funeral home to obtain a permit through the city in order to open a grave site located on any lot within any city cemetery. The permit shall be obtained a minimum of 24 hours in advance of the site opening. The notice may be waived by the city in extenuating circumstances.
(b)
Permit fee. The fee charged by the city for the issuance of a permit to open a grave site shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges.
(c)
Responsibilities to ensure burial in proper lot. It shall be the responsibility of the funeral home contracted to perform the interment or representative of the decedent, whichever party completes the permit application, to provide a copy of the lot deed as part of the application process. It shall be the responsibility of the city's representative, upon receipt of a valid permit application, permit fee, and the lot deed to locate the grave site and to mark same in such manner as to be clearly identifiable to the funeral home or at the discretion of the city's representative, he or she may require the funeral home to have an employee physically present at the grave site at the time of the marking thereof. If the employee from the funeral home is present at the time of marking of the grave site, and the lot has room for more than one grave, he or she shall confirm the specific grave wherein the funeral home has been directed to bury the decedent. In no event shall any party attempt to open a grave site prior to verification of the site.
(d)
Inspection. The city manager or his designee shall have the right to enter upon any burial lot in any city cemeteries to inspect work in progress and to determine whether or not the work is in compliance with this chapter. If it is determined that the work being performed does not comply with the provisions of this chapter, authority is given to the city manager or his designee to suspend the work, and shall allow work to resume only to correct the violations and to proceed thereafter only in conformity with this chapter.
(e)
Noncompliance. In the event a funeral home is not in compliance with any of the provisions set forth in this chapter, said funeral home will not be issued a permit until such time as it takes whatever action is necessary to remedy its non-compliance.
(f)
Offense. The opening of a grave in the city cemetery without a permit shall be an offense punishable as provided in section 1-11.
(Ord. No. 1141, 12-15-20; Ord. No. 1169 , 4-19-22)

Sec. 30-5. - Liability.
There shall be no liability whatsoever, either ex delicto or ex contractu, on the part of the city and/or its officials or officers, or their successors in office, and/or its agents or employees, to any purchasers of any lots in the cemetery, or to any person holding under them, or to the family or relatives of any person buried in the cemetery or to any person, or the family of such person, who has erected any monument, marker or mausoleum therein, or to any person whatsoever, by reason of any act or acts, thing or things, omission, negligence or otherwise relating to the maintenance, upkeep or ownership of the cemetery.
(Ord. No. 1115, 8-20-19)
Sec. 30-6. - Applicability of article I.
In accepting any conveyance of any burial lot in any cemetery established by the city, each purchaser agrees that all provisions of article I are valid and that such purchaser and his heirs and assigns shall hold the lot subject to all the provisions of article I and subject to all amendments hereto hereafter made by the mayor and city council of the city. All parties, by interring a member of their family or other person in or on any such burial lot, accept the provisions of article I and all amendments thereto. All purchasers of such lots, their heirs, representatives and assigns, and all persons causing members of their families or others to be buried in any city cemetery are estopped to contest the validity any provision set forth in article I.
(Ord. No. 1115, 8-20-19)
Sec. 30-7. - Speed limits, vehicle sizes, skateboards, animals, and definitions.
(a)
Speed. It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a motor vehicle or other vehicle at a speed in excess of five miles per hour within any city cemetery.
(b)
Vehicle size. It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a motor vehicle of one ton in size or larger on the cemetery grounds unless it is for interment or disinterment purposes, or for ground maintenance.
(c)
Skateboards. It shall be unlawful to ride or use a skateboard in any city cemetery.
(d)
Animals. It shall be unlawful for any person to possess an animal, with the exception of an "assistance dog", on cemetery grounds.
(e)
Definitions. The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning.
Motor vehicle means any self-propelled vehicle.
Vehicle means any device, in, upon, or by which any person or property may be transported, other than a motor vehicle.
Assistance dog means any dog trained in accordance with O.C.G.A. 16-11-107.1 serving in such role for its owner while on cemetery grounds.
(f)
It shall be unlawful to enter a city cemetery with a vehicle extension, such as a cart or trailer, unless it is for interment or disinterment purposes, or for grounds maintenance.
(Ord. No. 1115, 8-20-19)
Sec. 30-8. - Presence at night prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to be in any city cemetery between sundown and sunup, except for law enforcement patrol.
(Ord. No. 1115, 8-20-19)
Sec. 30-9. - Posting of traffic signs.
Signs informing the public of each traffic regulation set forth in this chapter shall be posted at each entrance of each cemetery to which they apply.
(Ord. No. 1115, 8-20-19)
Sec. 30-10. - Penalty for violation.
Violators of any of the terms of this chapter shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-11 of this Code. Additionally, any funeral home that violates the provisions of subsection 30-3(3)p. shall be prohibited and restricted from performing burials within any city cemetery until such time as the funeral home has moved the body from the incorrect lot to the correct lot.
(Ord. No. 1115, 8-20-19; Ord. No. 1141, 12-15-20)

ARTICLE II. - ABANDONED CEMETERY AND BURIAL GROUNDS

Sec. 30-21. - Definitions.
(a)
Abandoned cemetery means a cemetery which shows signs of neglect including, without limitation, the unchecked growth of vegetation, repeated and unchecked acts of vandalism, or the disintegration of grave markers or boundaries and for which no person can be found who is legally responsible and financially capable of the upkeep of such cemetery. Burial grounds, which are separately defined herein, shall fall within the definition of a cemetery for purposes of determining whether the burial ground has been abandoned.
(b)
Burial ground means an area dedicated to and used for interment of human remains. The term shall include privately owned burial plots, individually and collectively, once human remains have been buried therein. The fact that the area was used for burial purposes shall be evidence that it was set aside for burial purposes.
(c)
Cemetery or cemeteries means any land or structure in this state dedicated to and used for interment of human remains. It may be either a burial park for earth interments or a mausoleum for vault or crypt interments or a combination of one or more thereof.
(d)
Preserve and protect means to keep safe from destruction, peril, or other adversity and may include the placement of signs, markers, fencing, or other such appropriate features so as to identify the site as a cemetery or burial ground and may also include the cleaning, maintenance, and upkeep of the site so as to aid in its preservation and protection.
(Ord. No. 1115, 8-20-19)
Sec. 30-22. - Authorization to inspect to abandoned cemeteries and burial grounds.
The city manager or his designee shall have the right to enter upon any alleged private cemetery or burial ground located with the corporate limits of the city to determine whether or not the land has in fact been used as a cemetery or burial ground and to further determine whether the cemetery or burial ground has been abandoned or is not being maintained.
(Ord. No. 1115, 8-20-19)
Sec. 30-23. - Duty to maintain.
The person and/or entity legally responsible for the upkeep of any private cemetery or burial ground located within the corporate limits of the city, whether or not that person or entity is financially capable of doing so, shall maintain such cemetery or burial such that the same does not constitute a nuisance or otherwise a hazard to the public.
(Ord. No. 1115, 8-20-19)
Sec. 30-24. - Failure to maintain.
Upon an inspection and determination by the city manager or his designee that a private cemetery or burial ground has been abandoned, the city manager or his designee shall notify the person and/or legal entity legally responsible for the upkeep of the private cemetery or burial ground of the signs of neglect including, without limitation, the unchecked growth of vegetation, repeated and unchecked acts of vandalism, or the disintegration of grave markers or boundaries. The notice shall be in writing and delivered via U.S. Mail to the last known address of the person and/or legal entity legally responsible for the upkeep of the private cemetery or burial ground. The notice shall give the person and/or legal entity legally responsible for the upkeep of the private cemetery or burial ground 30 days to remediate the deficiencies set forth in the notice.
(Ord. No. 1115, 8-20-19)
Sec. 30-25. - Declaration of abandonment.
Where the city manager or his designee have provided notice to person and/or legal entity legally responsible for the upkeep of the private cemetery or burial ground of deficiencies and the same have not been remediated within the time frame set forth in this article; or in the event that upon reasonable efforts the city manager or his designee are unable to locate the person and/or legal entity legally responsible for the upkeep of the private cemetery or burial ground and the same is not in compliance with this chapter; then the city manager may request that the mayor and council declare that the private cemetery or burial ground has been abandoned. Upon a declaration by the mayor and council that a private cemetery or burial ground has been abandoned the city manager or his designee shall have the right to:
(1)
Enter the property and expend public money to preserve and protect the abandoned cemetery or burial ground;
(2)
Provide for reimbursement of such funds by billing any legally responsible person or entity or levying upon any of his/its property as authorized by chapter 46, article II of the Code of Ordinances; and/or,
(3)
Exercise the power of eminent domain to acquire any interest in land necessary for that purpose.
After a declaration of abandonment no additional interments shall be authorized in the cemetery or burial ground.
(Ord. No. 1115, 8-20-19)
Sec. 30-26. - Penalty for violation.
Violators of any of the terms of article II shall, upon conviction, be punished as provided in section 1-11 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 1115, 8-20-19)
