ARTICLE 98-7. - BUILDING AND PERMITTING

Sec. 98-7.1. - State minimum standard codes adopted.
A.
Section 18-27, adoption of construction and technical codes, of chapter 18, buildings and building regulations, of this Code adopts and incorporates by reference the state minimum standard codes for construction and provides for the administration and enforcement of these state minimum standard codes as adopted and amended by the state department of community affairs.
B.
All development and use of property within the city limits shall comply with Life Safety Code (NFPA 101, 2012 Edition), the Georgia Accessibility Code, ISPSC, the International Swimming Pool and Spas Code, and the IEBC, the International Existing Building Code.
C.
Building code adopted. The International Building Code (IBC), defined as the Georgia State Minimum Standard Code by the Georgia Legislature at O.C.G.A. § 8-2-20(9), as may hereafter be amended or revised, is made a part of this article as fully as though stated verbatim here.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-7.2. - Permitting authority.
The officials listed below shall be authorized to issue the referenced permits, subject to all valid ordinances of the city prescribing standards for issuing certain permits and approvals, or in the absence of such ordinances, subject to the valid ordinances of the county. No development or building permit approved by the city shall grant permission for the use, construction or alteration of any land or building that would violate any provision of this chapter or any other codes or laws of the city, county, state or federal government.
A.
Demolition permits shall be issued by the city building official.
B.
Building permits shall be issued by the city building official.
C.
Land disturbance permits (LDPs) shall be issued by the city.
D.
Flood damage prevention ordinance. Any development within a recognized flood plain shall comply with chapter 54, floods, of this Code. All necessary permits shall be obtained from the building official prior to initiation of land disturbance.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-7.3. - Land disturbance permit required.
A land disturbance permit shall be required for any proposed use of land to document and insure compliance with all provisions of this chapter prior to issuance of any building permit and before any improvement, clearing, grading or alteration of land commences. All applications for a land disturbance permit shall be made to the building official in compliance with section 46-99, application/permit process, of article III, soil erosion, sedimentation and pollution control, of chapter 46, environment.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-7.4. - Building permit required.
A.
The owner, authorized agent or contractor who seeks to construct, enlarge, repair, move, demolish or otherwise alter a building, or to install, alter, repair, remove, convert or replace any electrical, gas, mechanical, plumbing or fire sprinkler system, the installation of which is regulated by the construction codes of the city, or to cause any such work to be done, shall apply to the building official and obtain the required permit for the work prior to commencing any such work. No building or other structure shall be located, erected, moved, added to, or structurally altered prior to issuance of a building permit by the building official. No building permit shall be issued except in conformity with this chapter.
B.
Penalty for commencing work prior to issuance of a permit. Any person who initiates construction of a building or alteration of land prior to obtaining a building permit or any other required permit shall be subject to a fine equal to twice the amount of the specified fee.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-7.5. - Application for building permit.
A.
All applications for building permits shall be accompanied by scaled architectural plans and construction drawings, as appropriate, in triplicate, showing the dimensions of the lot to be built upon; the size and location of any existing buildings or structures on the lot; the shape, size, height, proposed use, and location on the lot of the building or structure proposed to be erected or altered; and such other information as may be necessary to determine compliance with this chapter.
B.
Permit issuance. No building permit issued by the building official shall grant permission for the use, construction upon or alteration of any land that as proposed to be used, built upon or altered would be in violation of any provisions of this chapter or any other codes and laws of the city, county or the state, except as provided here.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-7.6. - Plan submittal requirements.
A.
Approval of all construction drawings and architectural plans shall be required for any proposed use of land prior to issuance of any land disturbance or building permits for determining compliance with the standards of this chapter. No clearing, grubbing, site improvement, grading or alteration of land or any expansion, erection, placement or exterior renovation of any building shall be initiated prior to a determination of such compliance. Construction drawings, site plans, architectural plans and other development plans required by this chapter shall only be prepared by individuals currently registered for such work in accordance with applicable state law.
B.
Building permit applications shall be reviewed by the building official and shall be accompanied by complete plans signed and sealed by the preparer. All applications for building permits shall be accompanied by three sets of printed plans and shall also be submitted in electronic format, drawn to scale and containing the following information:
1.
Contact information for that individual who may be reached at all times concerning the project;
2.
Address of the property proposed for development and parcel identification number;
3.
A scaled drawing indicating the configuration and dimensions of the lot to be built upon;
4.
Dimensions and locations of all existing and proposed buildings;
5.
Proposed site improvements and proposed alterations to any existing buildings or site improvements;
6.
Existing and proposed use of each building, or portion thereof;
7.
Location of any existing buildings on adjoining lots.
8.
Number of dwelling units each building is designed to accommodate;
9.
Building setbacks;
10.
Vehicle access, parking and loading;
11.
Open space, as appropriate;
12.
Any canopy tree having a DBH of 12 inches or more and any understory or ornamental tree having a caliper of eight inches or more with a designation of such trees to be preserved or removed;
13.
Location of all solid waste containers; and
14.
Such other information concerning the lot and adjacent lots as may be essential for determining compliance with the standards of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-7.7. - Building plan compliance prerequisite to permit issuance.
When a lot has special conditions assigned to any approval by the city, those conditions shall be noted on all applicable plans and drawings. The building official shall not issue a building permit unless the plans and specifications submitted with the application for a permit comply with such special conditions.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-7.8. - Submittal, approval and filing of building plans and civil construction drawings.
Three sets of printed building plans and civil construction drawings shall be submitted to the building official. Upon approval, the building official shall sign each set indicating such approval. One copy of the plans shall be filed with the zoning commission secretary, as appropriate, one copy filed with the building official and one copy returned to the applicant. Building plans and civil construction drawings shall also be submitted in electronic format.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-7.9. - Conditions of building permit issuance; permits not transferable.
A.
One copy of the building plans shall be returned to the applicant following approval by the building official, together with the permit upon payment of all applicable fees. In the event that no substantial construction progress has been made within six months of the date of issuance of the building permit, or the work authorized by the permit is suspended or abandoned for a period of six months or more, the permit shall become null and void. Building permits shall not be transferable.
B.
No building permit shall be issued by any officer, department or employee of the city unless the application for such permit has been examined and approved by the building official certifying that the proposed building or alteration complies with all provisions of this chapter and the approved plans. Any building permit or license issued in conflict with the provisions of this chapter or the approved plans shall be null and void.
C.
No building permit shall be issued and no building shall be erected on any subdivided lot within the city limits of Thomaston unless the lot is a lot of record that lawfully existed prior to adoption of this chapter unless otherwise provided.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-7.10. - Construction and use to be as provided in approved plans and applications.
Building permits or certificates of occupancy issued based on plans and applications approved by the building official shall authorize only the use, arrangement and construction set forth in such approved plans and applications, and for no other use, arrangement or construction. Use, arrangement, or construction inconsistent with that so authorized shall be deemed a violation of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-7.11. - Construction progress.
In the event substantial construction progress has not been made within six months of the date of issuance of the building permit, the permit shall become invalid. For purposes of this section "substantial construction progress" for vertical construction shall comprise footer installation and building framing, including roofing.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-7.12. - Conformance prerequisite to issuance of occupation tax permit.
The city clerk shall issue an occupation tax permit only upon written confirmation by the building official that the proposed use conforms to the standards of the zoning district in which the business entity is located or is to be located, or written confirmation that the proposed use is a legal, nonconforming use. A duly issued certificate of occupancy shall be evidence of such conformity.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-7.13. - Stop work order; revocation of permits.
When the building official determines that any building is being constructed, altered, or modified; or any land is being used in violation of this chapter; and in his or her judgment immediate cessation of such construction, alteration, or modification of buildings; or use of land is required to prevent harm to any person, firm, corporation, or public agency; or to preserve the status quo; he or she may issue a stop work order in writing to the responsible party. The building official may also revoke any building permit or certificate of occupancy for any land, building or use in violation of this chapter to protect public health, safety and welfare.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-7.14. - Applicability to land, buildings and open space.
A.
No building, land or open space shall be used or occupied, and no building or portion of a building shall be erected, constructed, reconstructed, moved, or structurally altered unless in conformity with the standards of this chapter applicable to the zoning district in which the lot is located.
B.
No change in occupancy type shall be permitted prior to a review and inspection of the premises has been performed by the building official and the fire marshal, as applicable, to assess compliance of the proposed use with the standards of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-7.15. - Buildings under construction.
Nothing in this chapter shall require any change in the construction or intended use of a building that is lawfully under construction or for which a building permit has been lawfully issued as of the effective date of this chapter and the construction of which shall be diligently pursued until completion.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-7.16. - Development projects under construction.
Nothing in this chapter shall require any change in the development or the proposed use of property that is lawfully under construction or for which a development plan or preliminary plat has been approved as of the effective date of this chapter, provided that construction shall commence within six months of the effective date of this chapter and shall be completed within two years of the approval of a development plan or preliminary plat.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-7.17. - Approvals for residential, commercial and industrial developments on state highways.
No building permit shall be issued for residential, commercial or industrial development fronting on a state highway until approval of street access by the Georgia Department of Transportation (GDOT) has been obtained by the applicant for all entrances and exits, curb radii, drainage and other matters within the jurisdiction of GDOT.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
Sec. 98-7.18. - Public uses.
Public uses shall be authorized in all zoning districts except where otherwise prohibited. A government proposing to build or operate a public use within the city limits shall provide written notice to the city of any plans to establish such public use and shall submit a site plan and architectural drawings, as appropriate, to the building official for approval in accordance with all applicable provisions of this Code and shall obtain at no cost a building permit for information purposes, only.
(Ord. No. 1166 , § 1(Exh. A), 4-5-22)
