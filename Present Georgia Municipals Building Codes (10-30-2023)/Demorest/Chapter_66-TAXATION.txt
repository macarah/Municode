Sec. 66-31. - Business license tax imposed.
Except as to those businesses and practitioners of professions and occupations excluded by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-16(a), and except as to those persons excluded by O.C.G.A. § 43-12-1:
(1)
There is hereby levied an annual occupation tax on those businesses and practitioners of professions and occupations that have one or more locations or offices within the corporate limits of the city in the amount of $100.00.
(2)
There is hereby levied an annual occupation tax of $100.00 on those businesses and practitioners of professions and occupations with no location or office in the state if the business or practitioner:
a.
Has one or more employees or agents who exert substantial efforts within the corporate limits of the city for the purpose of soliciting business or serving customers or clients; or
b.
Owns personal or real property which generates income and which is located in the corporate limits of the city.
State Law reference— Basis of occupation tax, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-10; licenses for out of state practioners, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-7; licensing of real estate brokers and agents, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-17.
Sec. 66-32. - Due date.
The occupation tax levied by the provisions of this article shall be due and payable to the city annually within 30 days following January 1 of each year. In the event that any person commences business on or after January 1 in any year, the tax shall be due and payable to the city 30 days following the commencement of the business.
Sec. 66-33. - Penalties and interest for late payment.
(a)
Should any occupation tax imposed by this article remain due and unpaid for 90 days from the due date of the tax, the person liable for the tax or fee shall be subject to and shall pay a penalty of ten percent of the tax or fee due. Such tax shall be considered delinquent if not paid within 90 days of the due date of the tax.
(b)
The person liable for the tax imposed by the provisions of this article shall pay to the city interest on delinquent occupation tax at a rate of 1½ percent per month.
Sec. 66-34. - Civil fine for failure to pay.
The municipal court shall impose a civil fine for failure to pay the occupation tax levied by the provisions of this article. Such a civil fine shall not exceed $500.00 and may be enforced by the contempt power of the court.

ARTICLE III. - INSURANCE TAX

Sec. 66-61. - Insurers license fees.
There is hereby levied for the year 1998, and for each year thereafter, an annual license fee upon each insurer doing business within the city in the amount of $25.00, until changed by ordinance or resolution of the council. For each separate business location in excess of one which is not covered by section 66-62, and which is operating on behalf of such insurers within the city, there is hereby levied a license fee in the amount of $25.00, until changed by ordinance or resolution of the council. For the purposes of this article, the term "insurer" means a company authorized to transact business in any of the classes of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5.
(Ord. of 12-2-1997, § 1)
Sec. 66-62. - License fees for insurers insuring certain risks at additional business locations.
For each separate business location not otherwise subject to a license fee hereunder, operated and maintained by a business organization which is engaged in the business of lending money or transacting sales involving term financing and in connection with such loans or sales offers, that solicits or takes application for insurance through a licensed agent of an insurer for insurance, said insurer shall pay an additional license fee of $10.00, until changed by ordinance or resolution of the council, per location for the year 1998 and for each year thereafter.
(Ord. of 12-2-1997, § 2)
Sec. 66-63. - Gross premiums tax imposed on life insurers.
There is hereby levied for the year 1998, and for each year thereafter, an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer writing life, accident and sickness insurance within the state in an amount equal to one percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.1. The term "gross direct premiums," as used in this section, shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 66-61.
(Ord. of 12-2-1997, § 3)
Sec. 66-64. - Gross premiums tax, all other insurers.
There is hereby levied for the year 1998, and for each year thereafter, an annual tax based solely upon gross direct premiums upon each insurer, other than an insurer transacting business in the class of insurance designated in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5(1), doing business within the state in an amount equal to 2.5 percent of the gross direct premiums received during the preceding calendar year in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2. The term "gross direct premiums," as used in this section, shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4. The premium tax levied by this section is in addition to the license fees imposed by section 66-61.
(Ord. of 12-2-1997, § 4)
Sec. 66-65. - Due date for license fees.
License fees imposed in sections 66-61 and 66-62 shall be due and payable on the first day of each year.
