ARTICLE III. - PERSONS INELIGIBLE FOR CONTRACT OR LICENSE[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
Cross reference— Contractors' licensing and qualification, § 18-161.

Sec. 22-61. - Title.
This article shall be known as the "Lincoln County Ineligibility for Contract or License Ordinance."
(Ord. No. 35, § 1, 10-4-1994)
Sec. 22-62. - Conditions for ineligibility.
No person shall be awarded a contract by the board of commissioners if that person or business entity has:
(1)
Been convicted of or has pled guilty to two or more felonies within the preceding five years in any court of this state or of the United States;
(2)
Received two or more notices of violation or citations for violations of any environmental protection laws of this state or of the United States within the past five years and has been ordered to pay a civil penalty fine, been convicted of or entered a plea of guilty to any such violations, or has entered a plea of nolo contendere to any such citations or charges.
(Ord. No. 35, § 2, 10-4-1994)
Sec. 22-63. - Violation.
Any person or employees of a person convicted of a violation referred to in section 22-62 shall be ineligible for the awarding of a contract or the issuing of an occupational or regulatory license by the board of commissioners for a period of five years following such conviction or admission of guilt of such act or conduct.
(Ord. No. 35, § 3, 10-4-1994)
Sec. 22-64. - Documentation of appeal required.
Any person having been convicted of or had a judgment rendered against him which has been reversed by an appellate court of the proper jurisdiction shall present such documentation as is satisfactory to the board of commissioners to show that this article is not applicable.
(Ord. No. 35, § 4, 10-4-1994)
Sec. 22-65. - Affidavit required.
(a)
The board of commissioners shall not execute a contract or issue an occupational or regulatory license to any person until such person has executed and filed with the clerk of the board of commissioners, an affidavit executed under the penalty for false swearing that such person has not been convicted of any violation set out in section 22-62(1), nor has there been an admission of guilt or plea of nolo contendere as set out in section 22-62(2).
(b)
In the case of a firm or corporation, such affidavit shall be executed by the president and, in the case of a partnership, by the general partners.
(Ord. No. 35, § 5, 10-4-1994)
Sec. 22-66. - Violation of article.
Violations of any provisions of this article shall be tried in the appropriate court of the county and shall be punished as provided in section 1-9.
(Ord. No. 35, § 8, 10-4-1994)
