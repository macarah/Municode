ARTICLE II. - NOISE[2]
modified

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Limits on sound volume produced by radio, tape player or other mechanical sound-making device or instrument from within motor vehicle, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-14.

Sec. 18-19. - Definitions.
modified
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Amplifier means any device that is used to make sound intensity larger or greater.
Electronically reproduce means any device that uses electrical energy to reproduce sound from voice, phonograph records, audio tapes, videotapes, compact disks or any other sound storage systems. The term "electronically reproduce" also means any device that uses atmosphere as a medium to receive signals to reproduce sound.
Instrument means any device that produces sound, including but not limited to bells, whistles, gongs, drums, sirens and musical instruments.
Mechanical means any sound that is produced by a mechanical device.
Music means a series of orderly tones or sounds produced in succession or combination having unity and continuity.
Music instrument means any instrument that is capable of producing musical sounds, including but not limited to horns, woodwinds, string instruments, drums, pianos, organs, keyboards, bells or whistles.
Noise means any sound that is undesirable, unwanted, loud or unpleasant.
Sanctioned events means any event permitted or approved by the board of commissioners.
(Code 2000, § 42-31; Ord. of 8-3-1992)
Sec. 18-20. - Unlawful noises; exceptions.
modified
(a)
The following shall be deemed as unlawful acts:
(1)
For any person to make or cause to be made any noise, music, or mechanical sound that is considered excessive, unnecessary, or unusually loud, or any noise which annoys, disturbs, injures, or endangers the comfort, repose, health, peace or safety of others by any person that is at a distance of 100 feet or more, from the source, between the hours of 10:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m. (or at other hours if plainly audible from a distance of 200 feet or more) within the unincorporated limits of the county. This section shall not apply if the noise is being emitted from a location that is zoned as agricultural so long the noise being made is related to agriculture, such as agriculture machinery and animals.
(2)
For any person or entity to keep an animal or bird which by frequent or continuous noise, disturbs the residents of the area. This section shall only apply to a residential district and shall not apply to locations that are properly zoned and/or permitted for the keeping of animals or birds.
(3)
For any person or entity to create any noise adjacent to any hospital, school, court, or church while in use, which disturbs the patients, employees, students, occupants and/or members thereof, unless the person or entity possesses a permit issued by the county.
(4)
For the purpose of sound measurement, the measurement of the noise shall be made by the auditory senses and based on a straight line, from the source of the noise. Bass reverberations are included.
(5)
Any person violating this section shall, upon conviction, be punished by a fine, by imprisonment not exceeding six months, or by a combination of such punishments. Each violation of any provisions of this code or any such ordinance shall constitute a separate offense.
(b)
Exceptions. The following uses and activities shall be exempt from these regulations:
(1)
Noises of safety signals and warning devices.
(2)
Noises from any authorized emergency vehicle when responding to an emergency call or situation.
(3)
Noises from emergency work that is necessary to restore safe public conditions.
(4)
Domestic power tools, lawn mowers, or agricultural, construction and industrial equipment, when used and operated with a muffler.
(5)
Firearms being discharged from an established firing range or from any property so long as they are being discharged in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 16-11-103.
(Code 2000, § 42-32; Ord. of 8-3-1992; Ord. of 11-7-2016)
Sec. 18-21. - Permit required; exceptions.
(a)
Any person using a motor vehicle or trailer towed by a motor vehicle in a business or for political purposes equipped with an amplifier, electronic reproduction devices, mechanical devices or music instruments shall obtain a permit from the county to operate these devices.
(b)
Subsection (a) of this section does not apply to organized or sanctioned festivals, parades, sporting events when used within the confines of the sanctioned event.
(c)
Subsection (a) of this section does not apply to law enforcement officers when used in the course of their duties.
(Code 2000, § 42-33; Ord. of 8-3-1992)
Sec. 18-22. - Citation.
(a)
If the existence of a nuisance is complained of and found to exist, a citation will be issued by the sheriff or his deputy to the person causing the violation and the person who engaged the violators.
(b)
The magistrate judge of the county shall have jurisdiction and power, upon conviction, to order the abatement or set punishment.
(Code 2000, § 42-34; Ord. of 8-3-1992)
Sec. 18-23. - Violation.
Conviction for violation of this article shall be punishable according to the general penalties described in section 1-11.
(Code 2000, § 42-35; Ord. of 8-3-1992)

ARTICLE III. - THEFT OF UTILITIES

Sec. 18-50. - Definitions.
modified
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Electric company means any company, electric membership corporation, municipality, or political subdivision engaged in the manufacture or sale of electricity.
(Ord. No. 9, § 8, 4-19-2004)
Sec. 18-51. - Prohibited conduct.
The following practices or acts are hereby declared to be illegal and are prohibited:
(1)
The cutting, removing or in any manner making ineffective any seal, locking band or lock on an electric, gas or water meter.
(2)
Restoration of service by any means after service has been terminated for nonpayment or obtaining electricity, gas or water without making the proper deposit with a utility company or receiving proper authorization from a utility company.
(3)
Obtaining electricity, gas or water by use of jumper wires, or by any other means which bypass a metering device either partially or completely.
(4)
Damaging or tampering in any manner with any part of a metering device belonging to a utility company.
(5)
Changing or altering the normal installed position of a metering device in any fashion which causes the normal accurate recording of utility service received to be altered and inaccurate.
(6)
Obtaining electricity, gas or water by use of a metering device which is not authorized and installed by a utility company.
(7)
Interfering with the automatic registration, recording and transmission of electricity, gas or water consumption when readings are recorded and/or transmitted electronically.
(8)
Fraudulently obtaining, or attempting to obtain, service from a utility company by using a false name or identification.
(9)
Fraudulently obtaining, or attempting to obtain, service from a utility company by placing the account in the name of someone else after the service has been disconnected for nonpayment or theft of service while the person in whose name the service was in when disconnected is still living at the location of the service.
(10)
Any other reception of electrical power, gas service or water service without proper authorization from a utility company.
(11)
Using a meter other than the meter provided by the utility company servicing said person's location for the purpose of obtaining electricity, gas or water. Possession of such an unauthorized meter shall be prima facie evidence of a violation, subject to rebuttal by the defendant. A violation of this subsection shall be a separate and distinct offense, in addition to any other violation that may be established.
(Ord. No. 9, § 1, 4-19-2004)
Sec. 18-52. - Penalty.
Alleged violations of this article shall be tried in the magistrate court of the county. Any person found guilty of violating any section or part of this article shall be punished according to the general penalties described in section 1-11.
(Ord. No. 9, § 2, 4-19-2004)
Sec. 18-53. - Restitution.
Any person found violating any section or part of this article will be required to make full restitution to the utility company for the cost of the services obtained in violation of this article, for damage to equipment of a utility company and for all associated costs of the investigation. In calculating the amount of restitution to be paid, the court is authorized to consider the following evidence:
(1)
The cost of the service received as a result of violating this article shall be computed by the utility company.
(2)
The cost of any damage to utility equipment shall be based on the actual costs. Costs may be computed and updated annually.
(3)
Investigative and any associated costs will be computed and updated annually by the utility company.
(Ord. No. 9, § 3, 4-19-2004)
Sec. 18-54. - Presumption.
Any person in possession of any premises as owner, occupant, or tenant who is found to have an electric, gas or water meter which has been tampered with or altered in violation of this article or found to be receiving electricity, gas or water as a result of any of the methods described in this article shall be presumed to have knowingly violated the terms of this article. Such receipt of electricity, gas or water shall be deemed prima facie evidence of an intent to defraud or deprive the utility company of recovering proper charges for payment for such service.
(Ord. No. 9, § 4, 4-19-2004)
ARTICLE IV. - DISORDERLY CONDUCT

Sec. 18-55. - Defined; declared unlawful.
new
Those acts described in section 18-56 shall be deemed disorderly conduct, and it shall be unlawful for any person to do or engage in any of such acts.
(Ord. of 3-4-2013)
Sec. 18-56. - Specific acts enumerated.
new
The following acts shall be defined as disorderly conduct in violation of this article:
(1)
Act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another, whereby any person is placed in danger of or in fear of his/her safety, life, limb or health;
(2)
Act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another, whereby public property or property of another person is placed in danger of being destroyed or damaged;
(3)
Endanger or interfere with the lawful pursuits of another by acts of violence or threats of bodily harm;
(4)
Cause, provoke, or engage in any fight, brawl, or riotous conduct so as to endanger the life, limb, health or property of another or public property;
(5)
Assemble or congregate with another or others and cause, provoke, or engage in any fight or brawl;
(6)
Collect in bodies or in crowds and engage in unlawful activities;
(7)
Assemble or congregate with another or others and engage or attempt to engage in gambling;
(8)
Frequent any public place and obtain money from another by an illegal and fraudulent scheme, trick, artifice, or device or attempt to do so;
(9)
Assemble with another or others and engage in any fraudulent scheme, device or trick to obtain or attempt to obtain any valuable thing in any place or from any person;
(10)
Utter, in a public place or any place open to the public, any obscene words or epithets;
(11)
Frequent any place or vehicle where gambling or the illegal sale or possession of alcoholic beverages or narcotics or dangerous drugs is practiced, allowed, or tolerated;
(12)
Use fighting words directed towards any person, causing such person to become outraged and thus create turmoil;
(13)
Assemble or congregate with another or others and do bodily harm to another or property of another or public property;
(14)
Interfere with another's pursuit of a lawful occupation by acts of violence or threats thereof;
(15)
Congregate with another or others in or on any public way so as to halt the flow of vehicular or pedestrian traffic and to refuse to clear such public way when ordered to do so by a peace officer or other person having authority;
(16)
Damage, befoul or disturb public property or the property of another so as to create a hazardous, unhealthy, or physically offensive condition;
(17)
Appear in public in an intoxicated state or condition, whether such intoxication is by alcohol or drugs;
(18)
Expose any part of one's body in an indecent or rude manner;
(19)
Create loud noises, which disrupt, disturb, or otherwise interfere with the peace and tranquility of the public;
(20)
Attempt to hinder, delay, obstruct, or prevent any law enforcement officer from performing his duty;
(21)
Keep a disorderly house or house of ill fame and all adults residing in the house shall be considered the keeper;
(22)
Linger or loiter along public sidewalks, streets, or public ways of the county;
(23)
Fail or refuse to remove himself from the private property of another when requested to do so by the owner or occupants thereof;
(24)
Alone or in concert with others, with the intent of appropriating merchandise to his own use, without paying for the same, or to deprive the owner of possession thereof, or the value thereof, in whole or in part, by concealing or taking possession of the goods or merchandise from a retail establishment, or to alter the price tag or other price marking of goods or merchandise of any store or retail establishment, wrongfully causing the amount paid to be less than the merchants stated price for the merchandise or property or value thereof or $100.00 or less;
(25)
No person may jump, dive, or congregate or assemble for the purpose of jumping or diving from any publicly owned bridge within the jurisdictional limits of the county. This shall not apply to persons who are tethered or suspended from the bridge for the purpose of repair, evaluation or maintenance of the bridge structure and who have the legal authority from the state or county to do so. Nothing in this provision requires the state or any political subdivision thereof to post signs notifying the public of this provision.
(Ord. of 3-4-2013)
Sec. 18-57. - Continued conduct.
new
Continuation of the disorderly conduct constitutes additional offenses. Each day that the disorderly conduct continues shall constitute a new and separate offense and the judge of the magistrate court in said county may impose such sentence as the circumstances may require.
(Ord. of 3-4-2013)
Sec. 18-58. - Penalty for violation of article.
new
Any person, who pleads guilty, nolo contendere or is convicted of violating the provisions of this article, shall be punished as provided in O.C.G.A. § 36-1-20 or such other general law as may be in the future enacted to provide for the punishment and penalties that may be imposed by the magistrate courts of the state. As provided in O.C.G.A. § 36-1-20, each violation of this article may be punished as provided in section 1-19. In addition to the fine and/or confinement, the judge may impose community service.
(Ord. of 3-4-2013)

ARTICLE V. - NUISANCE PARTY/NUISANCE PARTY LOCATION[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
Editor's note— An ordinance of October 7, 2019, set out provisions intended for use as §§ 27-1—27-4. For purposes of classification, and at the county's direction, these provisions have been included as §§ 18-71—18-74.

Sec. 18-71. - Definition.
new
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Nuisance party means a social gathering or party which is conducted on any premises within the county and which, by reason of the conduct of the persons in attendance, results in any one or more of the following conditions or offenses occurring at the site of the said party or social gathering, or on neighboring public or private property: Disorderly conduct; illegal open container; unlawful sale, furnishing, dispensing or consumption of beer or intoxicating liquor; sale or furnishing of beer or intoxicating liquor to an underage person; possession or consumption of beer or intoxicating liquor by an underage person; illegal use of a controlled substance; public indecency; littering; the damage or destruction of property without the consent of the property owner; unlawful pedestrian or vehicular traffic; standing or parking of vehicles that obstructs the free flow of traffic on the public streets and sidewalks or that impedes the ability to render emergency services; unlawfully loud noise; or any other conduct or condition that threatens injury, inconvenience, or alarm to persons or damage to property is hereby declared to be an unlawful public nuisance.
(Ord. of 10-7-2019, § 27-1)
Sec. 18-72. - Duty to control premises.
new
Any person who is an owner, occupant, tenant, or otherwise has rightful possession or possessory control, individually or jointly with others, of any premises, who either sponsors, conducts, hosts, invites, or permits a social gathering or party on said premises which is or becomes a nuisance party, as defined in section 18-71, and which nuisance is either the intentional result of, or within the reasonable expectations of, the person or persons having such possessory control is deemed to be in violation of this section.
Any property owner, overseer, or other person having direct or indirect control of any property and who keeps and maintains, either by himself or others, a common, ill-governed, and disorderly house or property, to the encouragement of gaming, drinking, or other misbehavior, or to the common disturbance of the neighborhood or orderly citizens, after having been placed on notice and made aware of such activity, either by officials of county government or by law enforcement, is deemed to be in violation of this code section.
(Ord. of 10-7-2019, § 27-2)
Sec. 18-73. - Order to cease and disperse.
new
Any law enforcement officer who witnesses, at (or related to) a social gathering, any one of the enumerated offenses in section 18-72 or any other conduct or condition that threatens injury, inconvenience, or alarms to persons or damage to property, is authorized to declare the gathering a nuisance party. The officer will issue an order to disperse pursuant to this section.
A party or social gathering that is or becomes a nuisance party, as defined in section 18-71, shall cease upon the order of the sheriff, or the sheriff's designee; and all persons not residing therein at the site of such social gathering or party shall leave the premises immediately. Any person who fails or refuses to obey and abide by such an order shall be guilty of a violation of this section.
When the sheriff's office issues an order to disperse, any person who fails to leave the party will, at minimum, be cited for violation of 18-71 and may be subject to arrest for other violations, such as "disorderly conduct" or "obstruction of an officer."
(Ord. of 10-7-2019, § 27-3)
Sec. 18-74. - Penalty.
new
Whoever violates this section is guilty of misdemeanor.
Continuation of any of the above actions on each new day constitutes a new and separate offense and the magistrate judge of the county shall impose such sentence as the circumstances may require.
Any person who pleads guilty, nolo contendere, or is convicted for violation of this article, shall be punished as provided under O.C.G.A. § 36-1-20 or such other general laws as may in the future be enacted to provide for the punishment and penalties that may be imposed by the magistrate courts of the state with the maximum fine of $1,000.00 and/or 60 days in jail. In addition to the fine or confinement, the judge may impose community service.
(Ord. of 10-7-2019, § 27-4)
