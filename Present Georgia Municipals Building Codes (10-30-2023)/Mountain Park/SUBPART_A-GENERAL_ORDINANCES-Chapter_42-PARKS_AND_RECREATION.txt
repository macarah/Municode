ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 42-1. - Enforcement of chapter provisions.
The provisions of this chapter shall be enforced and administered by the code compliance officer in conjunction with and concurrently with any other enforcement agency, office or department designated in this chapter.
(Code 1982, § 4-3-12)
Sec. 42-2. - Violation; penalty.
Any person violating any provision of this chapter and/or concealing a violation of, or harboring, assisting or protecting a person charged with or convicted of a violation of this chapter shall be punished as provided in section 1-11 of this Code.
(Code 1982, § 4-3-11(h))
Sec. 42-3. - Reservations for use of picnic grounds.
Arrangements to use the picnic grounds may be made with the city clerk who shall maintain a reservation book for this purpose. Any person using the facilities without a reservation may be removed from the premises.
(Code 1982, § 4-3-2)
Sec. 42-4. - Motor vehicles.
It shall be unlawful for any person to drive, operate and/or park any motor vehicle, minibike or motorcycle within any city park, or on any city property, except in areas designated for such use. This section does not apply to city employees when municipal duties require them to drive over such park and/or to park their vehicles or equipment in order to perform city business.
(Code 1982, § 4-3-11(a))
Cross reference— Traffic and vehicles, ch. 58.
Sec. 42-5. - Noises.
It shall be unlawful for any person to make any unnecessary, loud noises, engage in noisy disputes or conversation, engage in any indecent or loud acts of behavior, or in any other manner disturb the public peace, quiet and order in any of the city parks or city property.
(Code 1982, § 4-3-11(c))
Cross reference— Noise, § 113-41 et seq.
Sec. 42-6. - Alcoholic beverages.
It shall be unlawful for any person to possess and/or consume any alcoholic beverage, or be under the influence of alcohol, malt and/or vinous beverage, within or upon the lakes, parks, picnic areas, playgrounds and swimming pool of the city; provided, however, that this section shall not apply to nor shall it prohibit the possession, use or consumption of alcoholic, malt and/or vinous beverages within the premises of the city's civic center/senior citizen's building by persons or groups who have applied for and been granted a special use permit for such premises through and by the city clerk's office.
(Code 1982, § 4-3-11(b))
Sec. 42-7. - Malicious mischief.
It shall be unlawful for any person to cut, break, mutilate, deface, or in any other manner destroy or injure any public property, real or personal, belonging to, owned by, or leased or used by the city.
(Code 1982, § 4-3-11(d))
Sec. 42-8. - Water pollution.
It shall be unlawful for any person to pollute or disturb any spring, branch, pond, fountain, or other water owned by or leased to the city.
(Code 1982, § 4-3-11(e))
Sec. 42-9. - Posting signs.
It shall be unlawful for any person to affix any bill, sign or notice on any tree, building or fixture in any of the parks or on any city property. It shall be unlawful for any person to place any paper, books, refuse or trash of any kind in any of the public parks or on any city property, except in containers provided for such.
(Code 1982, § 4-3-11(f))
Sec. 42-10. - Park hours.
(a)
All city parks, lakes, and property shall be closed at night. For purposes of this section, at night shall mean the time between 30 minutes after sundown and 30 minutes before sunrise.
(b)
No person shall be permitted to be on the premises or property of any city park or property at night, except authorized city employees or persons engaged in activities authorized by the mayor and council.
(c)
No person shall be permitted to boat on Lake Cherful or Lake Garrett at night, except authorized city employees or persons engaged in activities authorized by the mayor and council.
(d)
No person shall be permitted to fish in Lake Cherful or Lake Garrett at night, except from private property with the authorization of the owner or occupant of said private property.
(e)
Notwithstanding the foregoing, the mayor shall be authorized to declare special events and suspend or modify the rules of this article during said special events.
(Code 1982, § 4-3-11(g); Ord. No. 185-A-96, § 5, 6-17-1996; Ord. No. 330-19 , § 1(A), 1-28-2019; Ord. No. O341-22 , § 1, 8-22-2022)
Sec. 42-11. - Smoking in public places.
(a)
Smoking shall be prohibited on public property within the city, except as provided herein. Smoking shall be defined to include, but not be limited to, smoking cigarettes, pipes and cigars, and shall also include the use of vapor products. Public property shall include city parks, city buildings, city parking areas, the city pool and any other city property expressly designated as a non-smoking area.
(b)
The city administrator shall be authorized to designate and post smoking areas in public areas where smoking can be conducted without being a nuisance to other citizens. In such areas, smoking shall be permitted.
(c)
Any person found smoking in public areas outside of designated smoking areas may be warned, and upon further failure to comply with this section, may be issued citation. The minimum fine for a first offense upon a finding of guilt for violation of this section shall be $50.00, plus applicable surcharges.
( Ord. of 8-23-2021 , § 1)
Secs. 42-12—42-40. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - LAKES AND POOLS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 42-41. - Fees and charges.
Any fee or charge authorized or imposed by this article shall be as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the city clerk.
(Ord. No. 93A-96, § 2, 8-26-1996)
Sec. 42-42. - Swimming tag required.
No person shall swim in either lake, nor shall any swimmer be admitted to the swimming pool, unless wearing a swimming tag and being in the lawful possession of the same, and being in regulation swimming attire.
(Code 1982, § 4-3-3; Ord. No. 185-A-96, § 2, 6-17-1996)
Sec. 42-43. - Fees for swimming tags.
Fees and restrictions for each category of eligible persons shall be fixed from time to time by the mayor and council. Such fees shall be paid before tags are issued and are nonrefundable.
(Code 1982, § 4-3-4(b))
Sec. 42-44. - Attire.
No person shall swim in either lake, nor shall any swimmer be admitted to the swimming pool, unless being in regulation swimming attire.
(Code 1982, § 4-3-3; Ord. No. 185-A-96, § 2, 6-17-1996)
Sec. 42-45. - Hours of operation of swimming pool.
The hours of operation of the swimming pool shall be set by the mayor and council and shall be conspicuously posted.
(Code 1982, § 4-3-5)
Sec. 42-46. - Powers and duties of the lifeguard.
It shall be the duty of the lifeguard to enforce the rules and regulations prescribed by the mayor and council and to preserve good order within the confines of the swimming pool area. He shall have the power to evict any person from the area who fails or refuses to obey any lawful or reasonable order or who persists and continues in any conduct dangerous to any person. Any person who refuses or fails to obey the instructions of the lifeguard shall be guilty of a misdemeanor. The lifeguard shall be diligent in his efforts to protect the life and property of all persons using the swimming pool.
(Code 1982, § 4-3-6)
Sec. 42-47. - Fishing.
modified
(a)
No person aged sixteen or older may fish in either Lake Cherful or Lake Garrett without a city-issued permit and state fishing license. No person shall place in the lakes a trot line, seine, net, electrical device, dynamite or other substance or device declared to be illegal by the state game and fish division. Notwithstanding the foregoing, no city-issued permit shall be required to fish Lake Cherful or Lake Garrett on the Saturday, Sunday or Monday of Memorial Day or Labor Day weekends, or on the 4th of July.
(b)
Only artificial bait may be used when fishing in Lakes Cherful and Garrett. Anyone fishing with live or non-artificial bait in Lakes Cherful or Garrett shall be in violation of this article and upon citation and conviction shall be punished by fine in an amount no more than $1,000.00 and no less than $100.00.
(c)
All fishing in Lakes Cherful and Garrett shall be catch and release only. Anyone possessing fish caught in Lakes Cherful or Garrett and which has not been released immediately following catch shall be in violation of this article and upon citation and conviction shall be punished by fine in an amount of no more than $1,000.00 and no less than $100.00.
(d)
As a condition of any sentence for conviction of this section, the judge of the municipal court may revoke the convicted defendant's city-issued permit to fish in the lakes of the city.
(Code 1982, § 4-3-7; Ord. No. 185-A-96, § 2, 6-17-1996; Ord. No. 321-15 , § 1, 4-27-2015; Ord. of 10-26-2020(1) , § 1; Ord. No. O343-23 , § 1A, 4-24-2023)
Sec. 42-48. - Hunting.
No person shall hunt on or from the waters of any lake in this city and the lakes of this city are hereby designated as a refuge for waterfowl of every kind and description.
(Code 1982, § 4-3-8)
Secs. 42-49—42-70. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - BOATING

Sec. 42-71. - Permit required.
modified
The use of motorized boats, jetboats, and other motorized personal watercraft on the lakes of the city is strictly prohibited in the City of Mountain Park, except as provided herein. All boats shall require a city permit. Boating in the lakes in the city requires a state registration, if applicable, city permit and picture identification for boaters 16 to 65 years old. Up to two honorary permits (no charge) per household will be issued to boaters over 65 years old who are property owners or residents of the city.
(Ord. No. 93A-96, § 1, 8-26-1996; Ord. No. 329-18 , § 1(A), 10-22-2018; Ord. No. O348-23 , § 1A, 7-24-2023)
Sec. 42-72. - Permit procedures.
Boating permits may be purchased at the city hall during regular business hours. Applicants who are city residents or city property owners must show a picture identification, proof of residency or property ownership and a current state boat registration. Nonresident or non-property owner applicants must show valid identification and state boat registration. All applicants must sign that they agree to abide by all city and state regulations relative to boating and the use of city properties. At the time of purchase, each permit shall be completed and signed by the applicant and a city representative. With all permits, a key to the boat ramp may be rented for a deposit. Fees for resident and nonresident boating permits shall be set from time to time by resolution of the mayor and city council.
(Ord. No. 93A-96, § 2, 8-26-1996; Ord. No. 297-08, § I, 1-26-2009)
Sec. 42-73. - Receipt of regulations.
Each person receiving a permit shall receive a copy of the state's boating regulations and a copy of this Code relative to boating, fishing and the use of lakes, parks and properties.
(Ord. No. 93A-96, § 3, 8-26-1996)
Sec. 42-74. - Violations.
Any violation of any provision of this article or of any other state or federal regulations relative to boating shall, in addition to any other remedy available to the city, result in revocation of the city boating permit.
(Ord. No. 93A-96, § 4, 8-26-1996)
Sec. 42-75. - Boat operation on Lake Garrett and Lake Cherful.
No person shall operate on Lake Garrett or Lake Cherful any motor powered boat, jetboat, or other motorized personal watercraft, except that electric motors capable of making no more than 5 horsepower or 112 pounds of thrust shall be permissible on Lake Cherful. Nothing in this section shall be deemed to prohibit the use of rowboats, canoes, kayaks, or sailing craft with a properly issued permit.
(Code 1982, § 4-3-9; Ord. No. 297-08, § II, 1-26-2009; Ord. No. 329-18 , § 1(B), 10-22-2018)
Secs. 42-76—42-100. - Reserved.
