ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 22-1. - Name of court.
In keeping with state law as provided in O.C.G.A. § 36-32-1 et seq., the court shall be known as the Municipal Court of the City of Mountain Park. The Municipal Court of the City of Mountain Park is established and described in article VII of the city Charter.
(Ord. No. 141-A-96, § 1, 10-21-1996)
Sec. 22-2. - Sessions.
Regular sessions of the municipal court shall be held at the council chambers or at such other place as may be designated by the municipal judge at such time as the municipal judge directs.
(Code 1982, § 2-3-1; Ord. No. 141-A-96, § 2E, 10-21-1996)
Sec. 22-3. - Procedure.
The procedure and rules for the municipal court shall be the same as those for magistrate courts of the state.
(Code 1982, § 2-3-3; Ord. No. 141-A-96, § 3, 10-21-1996)
Sec. 22-4. - Indigent defense program.
The judge of the municipal court shall, upon inquiry and determination of indigency, appoint counsel to represent any and all indigent defendants charged with offenses before the municipal court. The judge shall maintain an appointment list of practicing attorneys from local bar associations or otherwise, and shall appoint counsel therefrom at the city's expense for legal representation of indigent defendants charged with offenses before the court. Counsel appointed for legal representation of indigent defendants shall be compensated from the city's indigent defense fund in accordance with the guidelines established by the council for this purpose.
(Code 1982, § 2-3-6)
Sec. 22-5. - Indigent defense fund.
(a)
There is hereby created an indigent defense fund for the purpose of compensating attorneys appointed to represent indigent defendants charged with offenses before the municipal court of the city. The council shall appoint such monies as it deems necessary to adequately fund the indigent defense program.
(b)
Attorneys appointed by the judge to represent indigent defendants shall be compensated from the city's indigent defense fund at a rate as set by the council from time to time.
(Code 1982, § 2-3-7)
Secs. 22-6—22-40. - Reserved.

DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 22-41. - Clerk.
(a)
Duties. The clerk of the municipal court shall maintain and keep in a safe place all records and documents pertaining to the operation of the court, sign all documents as may be required by the court and perform such other duties as may be required by the judge.
(b)
Salary. The salary of the court clerk shall be set by the council by resolution.
(c)
Appointment. The council appoints the city clerk as clerk of the municipal court.
(Code 1982, § 2-3-4; Ord. No. 141-A-96, §§ 5A, 5B, 10-21-1996)

Sec. 22-61. - Appointment.
The council shall appoint a judge of the municipal court from time to time by resolution.
(Ord. No. 141-A-96, § 2B, 10-21-1996)
Sec. 22-62. - Term.
The judge of the municipal court shall serve at the pleasure of the council.
(Code 1982, § 2-3-2; Ord. No. 141-A-96, § 2A, 10-21-1996)
Sec. 22-63. - Powers.
The judge of the municipal court shall exercise those powers set out in sections 7.10 and 7.12 of article VII of the city's Charter at his discretion and to promote justice.
(Ord. No. 141-A-96, § 2C, 10-21-1996)
Sec. 22-64. - Salary.
The salary of the municipal judge shall be set by the council by resolution.
(Ord. No. 141-A-96, § 2D, 10-21-1996)

DIVISION 3. - SOLICITOR

Sec. 22-91. - Term.
The solicitor of the municipal court shall serve at the pleasure of the council.
(Ord. No. 141-A-96, § 4A, 10-21-1996)
Sec. 22-92. - Salary.
The solicitor shall receive a salary as set by the council by resolution.
(Ord. No. 141-A-96, § 4B, 10-21-1996)
Sec. 22-93. - Duties.
The solicitor shall prosecute offenses against the provisions of this Code and ordinances of the city before the court.
(Ord. No. 141-A-96, § 4C, 10-21-1996)
Sec. 22-94. - Appointment.
The council shall appoint a solicitor of the municipal court from time to time by resolution.
(Ord. No. 141-A-96, § 4D, 10-21-1996)
