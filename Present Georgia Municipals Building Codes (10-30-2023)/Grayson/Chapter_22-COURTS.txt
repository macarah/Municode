Chapter 22 - COURTS
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 22-1. - Intergovernmental agreement with the county.
The mayor is authorized to sign any necessary intergovernmental agreement with the county to grant jurisdiction over citations issued by the city for violations of ordinances of the city to the county recorder's court and to authorize that any fines collected or such cases be retained by the county.
(Ord. of 10-21-2002(1))
Secs. 22-2—22-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - CODE ENFORCEMENT BOARD

Sec. 22-31. - Purpose.
The purpose of this article is to promote, protect, and improve the health, safety, and welfare of the citizens of the city by imposing administrative fines and noncriminal penalties for violations of the Code.
(Ord. of 4-19-2004, § I(B))
Sec. 22-32. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
City means the City of Grayson, Georgia.
City attorney means the city attorney and/or his designee.
Code means zoning ordinances and resolutions, ordinances and resolutions enacting subdivision regulations, environmental ordinances and resolutions, state minimum standard codes provided for by state law, ordinances and resolutions enacted pursuant to other ordinances and resolutions regulating the development of real property, and ordinances and regulations providing for control of litter and debris, control of junked or abandoned vehicles, and control of overgrown vegetation. Notwithstanding the above, the term "code" shall not include:
(1)
Those codes and ordinances requiring a permit, unless the alleged violator has failed to secure all necessary valid permits under said codes and ordinances; or
(2)
Any local amendments to the state minimum standard codes provided for by state law that have not been adopted in conformity with state law.
Code inspector means any authorized agent or employee of the city whose duty it is to ensure code compliance.
Council means the mayor and council of the city.
Enforcement board means the code enforcement board of the city.
Solicitor means the chief prosecutor for the code enforcement board.
Violation involving the health or safety of a third party means a violation that creates a legitimate concern for the health and safety of a third-party occupant of a dwelling place or that creates an immediate and substantial danger to the environment.
(Ord. of 4-19-2004, § II; Ord. of 6-20-2011)
Sec. 22-33. - Board.
(a)
The enforcement board shall have three members and one alternate who shall serve at the pleasure of the mayor and council. The alternate shall serve on the enforcement board in the absence of a member. Members may be removed for cause by the mayor and council.
(b)
The enforcement board members and alternate shall be permanent residents of the city.
(c)
The initial members of the board shall serve as follows:
(1)
One member shall serve a two-year term;
(2)
Two members shall serve four-year terms; and
(3)
One member shall serve as the alternate for a one-year term.
Upon expiration of the initial terms of the members, the remaining terms shall be for three years for each member.
(d)
The members of the enforcement board shall elect one member to serve as chairperson, one member to serve as vice-chairperson, and one member to serve as secretary.
(e)
The members shall not receive compensation for service on the enforcement board; however, members may receive reimbursement for costs and expenses necessarily incurred by the members.
(Ord. of 4-19-2004, § III)
Sec. 22-34. - Powers.
The enforcement board shall have the following powers and duties under the provisions of this article:
(1)
To hear and decide cases alleging violations of the Code, to the maximum extent allowed by O.C.G.A. § 36-74-1 et seq.;
(2)
To adopt rules for the conduct of its hearings, which rules shall, at a minimum, ensure that each side has an equal opportunity to present evidence and argument in support of its case;
(3)
To subpoena alleged violators and witnesses to its hearings, with the approval of the Gwinnett County Recorder's Court or other court with jurisdiction over violations of the ordinances and Code of the City of Grayson, Georgia. Subpoenas may be served by the sheriff, marshal, or police department of Gwinnett County or by any other individual authorized by O.C.G.A. § 24-13-24 to serve subpoenas;
(4)
To subpoena evidence to its hearings in the same way as provided in subsection (3) of this section;
(5)
To take testimony under oath;
(6)
To issue orders having the force of law to command whatever steps are necessary to bring a violation into compliance;
(7)
To impose fines upon violators and the real property of violators; and
(8)
To exercise any and all other powers set forth in O.C.G.A. § 36-74-1 et seq. and this article.
(Ord. of 3-16-2015)
Sec. 22-35. - Code inspector.
(a)
The council shall appoint a code inspector to serve at the pleasure of the council for the purpose of performing investigations and ensuring code compliance.
(b)
The code inspector may not perform investigations or inspections of rental property within the city unless there is a probable cause that a violation of applicable codes exists or did exist.
(1)
Conditions which may appear in plain view may form the necessary basis for probable cause.
(2)
If such probable cause exists and a violation is found, the code inspector is required to notify the violator and allow reasonable time to correct the violation.
(3)
If the violation is not corrected within a prescribed period of time, the enforcement officer shall proceed by calling a hearing before the enforcement board. Repeat violators shall not be afforded with additional time to correct a continuing violation.
(c)
If the code inspector has substantial reason to believe a violation presents a serious threat to the public health, safety, and welfare or if the violation is irreparable or irreversible in nature, the code inspector shall make a reasonable effort to notify the violator and immediately notify the board and request a hearing.
(Ord. of 4-19-2004, § IV; Ord. of 3-16-2015)
Editor's note— Ord. of 3-16-2015 added a new § 22-34, and in so doing renumbered §§ 22-34—22-37 as §§ 22-35—22-38, as set out herein.
Sec. 22-36. - Hearings.
(a)
The enforcement board shall meet on the fourth Monday of each month at a time and place determined by the board. Additional meetings may be initiated by the code inspector as provided in O.C.G.A. § 36-74-1 et seq. The enforcement board shall not be required to meet in any month where there is no business to conduct. A quorum of the enforcement board shall be two members. The chairperson shall preside over hearings before the enforcement board.
(b)
Notice of a schedule hearing shall be made via hand delivery by the sheriff, code inspector, or other person designated by the council, certified mail or statutory overnight delivery, or by leaving the notice at the violator's usual place of residence with someone over 15 years old.
(c)
Hearings shall be open to the public, and minutes shall be kept of all hearings. All testimony before the enforcement board shall be under oath and recorded. The enforcement board shall have the authority to issue subpoenas of witness and evidence. Transcripts of the hearings shall be made available upon request and the costs incurred by the city transcribing the hearing shall be assessed to the party making the request. All tapes of the hearings shall be maintained for six months following the date of the hearing.
(1)
The city attorney, solicitor, code inspector or other member of the administrative staff of the City of Grayson, as designated by the council, shall present each case before the enforcement board.
(2)
Violators may be represented by an attorney and provide witnesses.
(d)
The state rules of civil procedure and formal rules of evidence shall not apply; however, fundamental right of due process shall be observed by the enforcement board.
(e)
At the conclusion of a hearing, the enforcement board shall issue findings of fact and conclusions of law and an order with its ruling and any proper relief as provided by law. The findings and conclusions shall be made by motion and approved by of a majority of present members. The secretary of the enforcement board shall certify the order. The certified copy of an order of the board may include notice of a specific date to perfect compliance and any applicable fine imposed. The order shall be recorded with the county clerk of superior court so as to constitute notice to subsequent purchasers.
(Ord. of 4-19-2004, § V; Ord. of 6-20-2011; Ord. of 3-16-2015)
Editor's note— See editor's note to § 22-35.
Sec. 22-37. - Administrative fines.
(a)
The enforcement board may impose a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 per day if the violation involves the health or safety of a third party.
(b)
The enforcement board may impose a fine not to exceed a total of $1,000.00 if the violation does not involve the health or safety of a third party.
(c)
The enforcement board may order the violation corrected by a date certain and waive or suspend all or a portion of the fine if compliance is obtained by the specified date.
(d)
In determining the amount of administrative fine, the enforcement board shall consider:
(1)
The gravity of the violation;
(2)
Any actions taken by the violator to correct the violation; and
(3)
Any previous violations committed by the violator.
(e)
The enforcement board may record a certified copy of an order imposing an administrative fine with the Clerk of Superior Court of Gwinnett County. Such order shall constitute a lien against the land on which the violation exists and upon any real or personal property owned by the violator. Three months from the filing of an order constituting a lien, the enforcement board may request that council to foreclose on the lien. No liens shall continue for more than 20 years after the recording of the order with the clerk of court.
(Ord. of 4-19-2004, § VI; Ord. of 3-16-2015)
Editor's note— See editor's note to § 22-35.
Sec. 22-38. - Appeal.
An aggrieved party, including the city, may appeal an order of the enforcement board within 30 days following the issuance of the order to the county superior court. Such an appeal shall be de novo. (Ord. of 4-19-2004, § VII; Ord. of 3-16-2015)
