Chapter 34 - SOLID WASTE[1]

Editor's note— An ordinance adopted Sept. 25, 2007, amended Ch. 34 in its entirety to read as herein set out. Former Ch. 34, §§ 34-1—34-20, pertained to similar subject matter, and derived from Ord. of 8-19-2002, intro. ¶ and §§ 2—20; Ord. of 1-16-2006.

Sec. 34-1. - Determination.
The city wishes to exercise its power under the law to provide to all persons in the city garbage and solid waste collection and disposal. Considerations of economy and efficiency show that the best way for the city to provide such service is by using a franchisee as an instrumentality.
(Ord. of 9-25-2007, intro. ¶)
Sec. 34-2. - Short title.
This chapter shall be known and may be cited as the "Solid Waste Management Ordinance of the City of Grayson, Georgia."
(Ord. of 9-25-2007, § 1)
Sec. 34-3. - Definitions.
When not inconsistent with the context, words used in the present tense include the future, words in the singular number include the plural. The word "shall" is always mandatory not merely directory.
"City" shall mean the City of Grayson, Georgia.
"Franchisee" shall mean United Waste Service a/k/a Robertson Sanitation (hereinafter "United").
"Person" shall mean any person, firm, partnership, association, corporation, company or organization of any kind.
"Refuse" shall mean and include the terms garage, solid waste, recyclable materials, refuse, rubble, rubbish, trash, yard waste, yard trimmings, white goods and other similar forms of waste.
(Ord. of 9-25-2007, § 2)
Sec. 34-4. - Collection by franchisee.
There is hereby granted by the city to United the right and privilege to operate and maintain a residential refuse collection service in, upon, along, across, above and over the streets, alleys, public ways, and public places in the City of Grayson, Georgia.
All residential refuse accumulated in the city shall be collected, conveyed and disposed of by the franchisee. No other person, firm or corporation shall collect or convey over any of the streets or alleys of the city, or dispose of, any residential refuse accumulated in the city.
Persons may become exempt from the provisions of this chapter by providing the City of Grayson with documentation certifying that the person disposes of their own refuse in a safe and lawful manner in compliance with all other laws, regulations and rules of the City of Grayson and the State of Georgia. Any such exemptions shall be solely for individual persons and no person shall be allowed to dispose of any refuse accumulated in the city by any other person.
(Ord. of 9-25-2007, § 3)
Sec. 34-5. - Collection supervised by franchisee.
All residential refuse accumulated in the city shall be collected, conveyed and disposed of by the franchisee. The franchisee shall have the authority to recommend regulations concerning pre-collection practices, the days of collection, type and location of waste containers, and such other matters pertaining to the collection, conveyance and disposal as it shall find necessary, and to recommend changes and modifications to the same, provided that such regulations are not valid without the consent of the mayor and city council.
(Ord. of 9-25-2007, § 4)
Sec. 34-6. - Pre-collection practices.
It shall be the duty of the occupant or owner of any residential premises to keep all refuse pending collection and disposal as follows:
(1)
Each residence shall be provided with one 90-gallon roll-a-way poly-cart (hereinafter "cart"). All refuse shall be free from liquid and placed in water tight plastic bags and the top secured and placed within the cart provided for each residence. All carts shall be kept clean and free of accumulated refuse or wastes and shall be treated with an effective insecticide if necessary to prevent the creation of a nuisance.
(2)
Each owner shall prevent the continued, excessive and unsightly accumulation of refuse upon the property occupied by him or public thoroughfares adjoining his property.
(3)
It shall be a violation of this chapter to place or cause to be placed in any refuse container for collection any acid, explosive material, inflammable product, toxic waste, medical or quarantined waste products or any other dangerous or corrosive material of any kind.
(4)
The disposal of wearing, bedding or other refuse from homes or other places where highly infectious diseases have prevailed will be at additional expense to the resident. Such refuse should not be placed inside the cart for routine collection. The resident should contact the franchisee and arrange for special collection of these materials.
(5)
Ashes shall be placed and maintained in separate containers. It shall be the responsibility of the customer to ascertain that all ashes are cool and that no hot coals remain.
(6)
Yard waste or trimmings shall be placed inside proper lawn waste bags to ensure collection. Yard waste or trimmings shall consist of lawn and shrub trimmings, small branches and other such inert material that will fit inside paper lawn waste bags. However, if the yard waste consists of small branches or tree limbs, the branches or limbs may be tightly bundled in pieces not to exceed four feet in length and no bundle shall weight more than 50 lbs. It shall be a violation of this chapter to place or cause to be placed for collection any yard waste or trimmings that are not properly contained inside a paper lawn waste bag or branches and limbs that are not properly bundled.
(7)
Carts shall be placed at curbside by 7:00 a.m. on scheduled pick up days. Carts shall not be placed curbside more than 12 hours prior to the regularly scheduled pick up day and shall be removed within 12 hours following the regularly scheduled pick up day. Carts shall be placed within 15 feet of the curb or edge of the payment/asphalt of the street or alley and shall be accessible to the side of the street or alley from which the collection is made. However, carts shall not be placed in or on the surface of the roadway, street or alley.
(8)
Yard waste and trimmings shall be placed at the curbside by 7:00 a.m. on the scheduled pick up day. All other conditions in subsection (7) above apply.
(9)
Persons who are unable to place their cart at curbside due to physical handicaps may apply to the city for an exemption and, if approved, may receive pull house service from their residence. All persons residing within said residence must be physically handicapped and approved by the city before this service is provided.
(Ord. of 9-25-2007, § 5)
Sec. 34-7. - Storing refuse.
No person shall place any refuse in any street, alley, or other public place, or upon any private property whether owned by such person or not, within the city except it be in proper containers for collection or under express approval granted by the franchisee. Nor shall any person throw or deposit any refuse in any stream or other body of water.
Any unauthorized accumulation of refuse on any premises is hereby declared to be a nuisance and is prohibited. Failure to remove any existing accumulation of refuse within 30 days of the effective date of this chapter shall be deemed a violation of this chapter.
No person shall cast, place, sweep, or deposit anywhere within the city any refuse in such a manner that it may be carried or deposited by the elements upon any street, sidewalk, alley, sewer, parkway, or other public place or into any occupied premises within the city.
(Ord. of 9-25-2007, § 6)
Sec. 34-8. - Collection practices.
(a)
Residential.
(1)
Refuse accumulated by residents shall be collected at curbside once each week. The day of collection shall be established by mutual agreement of the city and the franchisee. The franchisee shall observe three annual holidays. They are as follows: New Year's Day, Thanksgiving Day, and Christmas Day. Should one of these holidays fall on a collection day, the collection day shall be the following day.
(2)
There shall be no collections before 7:00 a.m. nor after sunset.
(3)
The franchisee shall collect a reasonable accumulation of refuse from each household during a collection period for the standard charge but shall not be required to collect more than one 90-gallon cart and 15 bags or bundles of yard waste or trimmings per week.
(b)
Commercial.
(1)
Any business or commercial establishment whose refuse collection requirements may be satisfied by the use of the 90-gallon cart to be provided to residential customers may make use of the services of the franchisee under the same terms and conditions as a residential customer.
(2)
Any business or commercial establishment whose refuse collection needs cannot be satisfied by use of the 90-gallon cart to be provided for residential customers shall be responsible for contracting for the safe and lawful disposal of their refuse. Each year, prior to payment of any occupation tax due to the city, the business or commercial establishment must provide certification to the city that sanitation service has been arranged for by the business.
(3)
The franchise granted by this chapter is for residential refuse collection only and nothing in this chapter shall be construed to require commercial or business customers to use the services of the franchisee.
(Ord. of 9-25-2007, § 7)
Sec. 34-9. - Fees.
(a)
For all residences except multi-family buildings which come under subsection (b) below, the rate for refuse collection shall be $48.06 paid quarterly.
(b)
Duplexes, triplexes and quadruplexes will be billed $48.06 per occupied unit in the building per month. No refuse collection shall be provided by the franchisee for multiple-family dwellings containing more than four dwelling units. These buildings shall be considered commercial or business customers.
(c)
The rate for business customers eligible to use the residential collection services as provided in this chapter shall be $48.06 paid quarterly.
(d)
The rate for citizens over age 65 or who qualify for tax exemptions as a result of medical disabilities shall be $32.85 paid quarterly.
(e)
The franchisee will provide routine maintenance or replacement of carts due to reasonable wear and tear. The customer shall be responsible for payment for any cart which must be replaced as a result of abuse, neglect, loss, or theft. The cost of replacement of the cart shall be billed to the customer on the next regular bill.
(Ord. of 9-25-2007, § 8)
Sec. 34-10. - Delinquent accounts.
(a)
A ten percent penalty shall be assessed on all accounts considered delinquent.
(b)
All accounts shall be considered delinquent if not paid by the 15th day of the month in which the monthly statement is issued. All delinquent accounts are subject to stoppage of service without notice. If a delinquent account is not paid within 30 days, the city may cease all refuse collection for that account. Service shall be resumed thereafter only on payment of the accumulated fees for the period of collection and of the period of non-collection plus penalties.
(c)
The stoppage of services hereinbefore authorized for non-payment of collection charges shall be in addition to the right of the city to proceed for the collection of such unpaid charges in a manner provided by law for the collection of a municipal claim, including, but not limited to, issuance of an execution for the collection of said charges and the levy, advertisement and sale of real property in order to enforce payment of said execution.
(Ord. of 9-25-2007, § 9)
Sec. 34-11. - Duration and acceptance of franchise.
(a)
This franchise and the rights, privileges, and authority granted shall continue in force until October 1, 2012, provided that within ten days after the passage of this chapter the franchisee shall file with the city administrator its unconditional acceptance of this franchise and promise to comply with and abide by all its provisions, terms, and conditions. Such acceptance and promise shall be in writing duly executed and sworn to, by or on behalf of the franchisee before a notary public or other officer authorized by law to administer oaths.
(b)
Should the franchisee fail to comply with subsection (a) above, it shall acquire no rights, privileges, or authority under the franchise whatever.
(Ord. of 9-25-2007, § 10)
Sec. 34-12. - Hold harmless and indemnification.
(a)
The franchisee shall indemnify and hold harmless the city, its elected officials, officers, agents and employees as a result of the grant of this franchise. Such indemnification shall be for, but not limited to, negligence on the part of the franchisee in the maintenance and operation of its refuse collection services.
(b)
The franchisee shall pay all expenses incurred by the city, its elected officials, officers, agents and employees in defending itself or themselves with regard to all damages and penalties mentioned in subsection (a) above. These expenses shall include all out-of-pocket expenses, such as attorney's fees, and shall also include the reasonable value of any services rendered by the city attorney or his assistants or any employees of the city.
(c)
A certificate showing that insurance coverage has been obtained and is currently in force and effect shall be filed with the city administrator prior to the commencement of service by the franchisee and within 30 days of the anniversary date of the policy in each and every year.
(d)
The franchisee shall maintain insurance with a reputable company or companies licensed to do business in the State of Georgia to protect the city and the franchisee from any and all claims, injury or damage to the persons or property, both real and personal, caused directly or indirectly by the operation of the refuse collection service. The amount of such insurance shall not be less than $300,000.00 for property damage, per occurrence, $500,000.00 for personal injury, per person, and $1,000,000.00 for personal injury per occurrence. Workmen's Compensation Insurance shall also be maintained as required by the laws of Georgia.
(Ord. of 9-25-2007, § 11)
Sec. 34-13. - Restriction on transfer or assignment.
The franchisee may not transfer or assign this franchise without the prior express and written approval of the mayor and city council of the city.
(Ord. of 9-25-2007, § 12)
Sec. 34-14. - Franchisee fee.
(a)
The city shall pay the franchisee 85 percent of all revenues collected as a result of billings provided under this chapter. The city shall bill its residents on or about the first day of January, April, July and October. Payment by the city to the franchisee shall be submitted by the twenty-eighth day of February and the thirtieth day of May, August and November.
(b)
The franchisee shall be bound by variances and special exceptions when granted by the mayor and city council. The franchisee shall receive the same pro rata share as a normal billing when a customer's bill is adjusted up or down.
(c)
Rates provided in this chapter shall remain constant through September 30, 2010; thereafter, the franchisee may request a rate adjustment which shall become effective if approved by the mayor and council of the city. After the first rate adjustment is approved by the mayor and council of the city, applications for future rate adjustments may be considered no more frequently than every 24 months.
(d)
The city shall bill customers directly.
(e)
The city shall be entitled to retain 15 percent of all revenues collected as administrative costs for providing the billing service.
(Ord. of 9-25-2007, § 13)
Sec. 34-15. - Requests for variances and special exceptions.
The city shall establish a written procedure whereby any person aggrieved by the regulation or a fee charged by the city shall have the right to appeal such regulation or fee and seek relief.
Persons filing requests for variances or special exceptions on the form provided by the city shall have their requests heard and ruled on within 60 days by the mayor and council.
(Ord. of 9-25-2007, § 14)
Sec. 34-16. - Amendments to chapter.
This chapter may be amended, modified, revoked, or otherwise changed by the Mayor and City Council of the City of Grayson at any time and from time to time as said mayor and city council deem appropriate.
(Ord. of 9-25-2007, § 15)
Sec. 34-17. - Penalties for failure to comply.
Any person convicted of violating any of the provisions of this chapter shall be punished by a fine not exceeding $1,000.00.
(Ord. of 9-25-2007, § 16)
Sec. 34-18. - Termination of franchise.
(a)
In addition to all other rights and powers pertaining to the city by virtue of this franchise or otherwise, the city reserves the right to terminate or cancel this franchise and all rights and privileges of the franchisee hereunder in the event that the franchisee:
(1)
Violates any provision of this franchise or any rule, order, or determination of the city or the mayor and city council made pursuant to this franchise, except where such violation is without fault or through excusable neglect.
(2)
Becomes insolvent, unable or unwilling to pay its debts, or is adjudged bankrupt;
(3)
Attempts to evade any of the provisions of this franchise or practices any fraud or deceit upon the city;
(4)
Franchisee has a change in the ownership of 50 percent or more of its stock, without the city's consent.
(b)
Such termination and cancellation shall be by ordinance duly adopted after 30 days notice to the franchisee and shall in no way affect any of the city's rights under this franchise or any provision of law. In the event that such termination and cancellation depends upon a finding of fact, such findings of fact as made by the mayor and city council or its representative shall be conclusive. Provided however, that before this franchise may be terminated and canceled under this section, the franchisee must be provided with an opportunity to be heard before the mayor and city council.
(Ord. of 9-25-2007, § 17)
Sec. 34-19. - Customer complaints.
(a)
The city shall maintain a register of complaints it receives from customers and shall regularly notify franchisee of these complaints.
(b)
The franchisee should attempt to handle missed pick ups on the same day, if reported by 2:00 p.m. All missed pick ups shall be corrected within 24 hours.
(Ord. of 9-25-2007, § 18)
Sec. 34-20. - Recycling collection practices.
(a)
Each residence shall receive, upon request, an 18 gallon recycling bin.
(b)
For all residents desiring to voluntarily participate in recycling, franchisee shall collect recyclables curbside each week.
(c)
The franchisee reserves the right to change the type and number of recovered items.
(Ord. of 9-25-2007, § 19)
Sec. 34-21. - Lien and delinquency enforcement.
(a)
The city shall have the power, right and authority to assess, levy, collect and enforce monthly, quarterly or annually, a sanitation or garbage and solid waste collection and disposal assessment (hereinafter "sanitation assessment") in such amount or amounts provided for in a fee schedule duly adopted by the council of the city based on the type of property and not the amount of solid waste collected, which sanitation assessment shall be a charge and lien against the real property for which said sanitation assessment was charged and the owner or owners thereof, superior to all other liens except liens for taxes or other assessments. The city shall have the power and authority to enforce the collection of said sanitation assessment by execution to be issued by the city clerk against the real property and against the owner thereof for the amount so assessed, which execution shall be levied and the property advertised and sold in the same manner and with the same effect as tax executions are now levied and property sold thereunder by the city.
(b)
The city shall have the right to discontinue sanitation or garbage and solid waste collection and disposal (hereinafter "sanitation collection") for any property whose assessment is past due 30 days or more. The city shall have the right to withhold sanitation collection to said property until such time as all past due amounts and penalties due thereon are paid in full, whether or not the owner of the property changes hands. Additionally, the city shall have the right to collect a deposit before reestablishing sanitation collection at any property on which sanitation collection was discontinued due to nonpayment or late payment of a sanitation assessment.
(c)
The above-referenced enforcement and collection options of the city shall be in addition to the right of the city to proceed to collect the sanitation assessment in any lawful manner.
(Ord. of 9-25-2007, § 20; Ord. of 4-21-2008)
