Sec. 18-34. - Violation; penalty.
(a)
Any person violating any provision of the various construction codes adopted in section 18-31 shall be punished as provided in section 1-9.
(b)
A conviction of the violation of any provision of the various construction codes adopted in section 18-31 shall automatically suspend the business license of the offending contractor. Upon a showing of good cause, the license of such contractor may be reinstated by an application to the board of commissioners.
(Code 1979, § 2-5-3.7)

Sec. 18-36. - Installation or maintenance by homeowner.
Nothing in this chapter shall prevent a homeowner from installing electrical, mechanical or plumbing systems or maintaining his home within his own property boundaries, provided such work is done by such homeowner and is used exclusively by him or his family. Such privilege does not convey the right to violate any of the provisions of this chapter, nor is it construed as exempting any such property owner from obtaining a permit, paying required fees and requesting inspections.
(Code 1979, § 2-5-5)

Sec. 18-40. - Inspectors.
With the approval of the board of commissioners, the director may appoint such number of officers, inspectors, assistants and other employees as shall be authorized from time to time. A person shall not be appointed as inspector who has not had at least five years' experience as an inspector, builder, engineer, construction foreman, plumber, electrician or mechanical contractor.
(Code 1979, § 2-5-9)
Sec. 18-41. - Restrictions on employees.
An officer or employee connected with the department, except one whose only connection is as a member of a board established by this chapter, shall not be financially interested in the furnishing of labor, material or appliances for the construction, alteration or maintenance of a building, or in the making of such plans or specifications thereof, unless he is the owner of such building. Such officer or employee shall not engage in any work which is inconsistent with his duties or with the interests of the department.
(Code 1979, § 2-5-10)

The director may revoke a permit or approval issued under the provisions of this chapter or chapter 90, in case there has been any false statement or misrepresentation as to a material or fact in the application or plans on which the permit or approval was based.
(Code 1979, § 2-5-14)

Sec. 18-49. - Liability.
Any officer, employee or member of a board charged with the enforcement of this chapter, acting for such board in the discharge of his duties, shall not thereby render himself liable personally, and he is relieved from all personal liability for any damage that may accrue to persons or property as a result of any act required or permitted in the discharge of his duties. Any suit brought against any officer or employee because of such act performed by him in the enforcement of any provision of this chapter shall be defended by the county attorney until the final termination of the proceedings.
(Code 1979, § 2-5-18)
Sec. 18-50. - Enforcement.
(a)
Any person who violates any provision of this chapter or fails to comply with the requirements thereof shall, upon conviction, be subject to penalties and costs as set forth in section 1-9. The owner or tenant of any building, structure, premises or part thereof, and any architect, builder, contractor, agent or other person who commits, participates in, assists in or maintains such violation, may each be found guilty of a separate offense and suffer penalties provided in this subsection.
(b)
In addition to the provisions of subsection (a) of this section, it shall be the duty of the director to enforce the provisions of this chapter, including, but not being limited to, the right to refuse to issue, approve, sanction or certify any permit, license or certificate. In addition thereto, if any building, structure or other construction and improvements or work, subject to the provisions of this chapter, is or is proposed to be erected, constructed, reconstructed, altered, converted, maintained or done, or any building, structure or work is or is proposed to be done or used in violation of this chapter, the director, board of commissioners or county attorney, or any adjacent or neighboring property owner who would be specially damaged by such violation, in addition to other remedies, may institute injunction, mandamus or other appropriate action or proceeding to prevent such unlawful erection, construction, reconstruction, alteration, conversion, maintenance or use, or to correct or abate such violation, or to prevent the occupancy of such building, structure or other construction and improvements.
(Code 1979, § 2-5-26)
Sec. 18-51. - Conflict with other chapters.
Nothing contained in this chapter shall abrogate, modify, alter or change the requirements of any other chapter of this Code; provided, however, that if a conflict should exist between such other provisions and the provisions of this chapter, the more restrictive provision shall apply and be controlling.
(Code 1979, § 2-5-27)
