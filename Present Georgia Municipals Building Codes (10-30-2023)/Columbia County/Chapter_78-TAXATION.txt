ARTICLE II. - FINANCIAL INSTITUTIONS[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
State Law reference— Local depository financial institutions business license tax, O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.

Sec. 78-31. - Levied.
(a)
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93, there is levied for the year 1984 and each year thereafter an annual business tax upon all depository financial institutions located within the county which tax shall be at the rate of a one-quarter percent of the gross receipts of depository financial institutions.
(b)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Depository financial institution means state and national banks, state building and loan associations and federal savings and loan associations.
Gross receipts means gross receipts as defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.
(Code 1979, § 2-10-40; Ord. of 10-4-1983, § 1)
Sec. 78-32. - Minimum tax amount.
The minimum annual amount of business license tax due from any depository financial institution pursuant to section 78-31 shall be $1,000.00.
(Code 1979, § 2-10-41)
Sec. 78-33. - Filing of return.
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(c), each depository financial institution subject to the tax set forth in section 78-31 shall file a return of its gross receipts with the board of commissioners on March 1 of each year following the year in which such gross receipts are measured. Such return shall be in the manner and in the form prescribed by the commissioner of the department of revenue based on the allocation method set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(d). The board of commissioners shall assess and collect the tax levied in section 78-31 based upon the information provided in the return.
(Code 1979, § 2-10-42)
Sec. 78-34. - Due date of tax.
Taxes levied pursuant to section 78-31 shall be due not later than 30 days after filing of the return as set forth in section 78-33, unless such time is extended by the board of commissioners.
(Code 1979, § 2-10-43)
Sec. 78-35. - Copy of this article to be sent to financial institutions.
The clerk of the board of commissioners is directed to forward a copy of this article to each depository financial institution located in the county, and to the home office of each such depository financial institution if located outside of the county.
(Code 1979, § 2-10-44)
Secs. 78-36—78-65. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - HOTEL AND MOTEL TAX[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
Cross reference— Businesses and business regulations, ch. 22.
State Law reference— County hotel/motel occupancy tax, O.C.G.A. § 48-13-50 et seq.

Sec. 78-66. - Levied; exemptions; use of proceeds of the tax.
Pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(a)(3), there is levied and imposed an excise tax at the rate of five percent of the charge to the public for and upon the furnishing for value to the public of any room, lodging or accommodation furnished by any person within the unincorporated areas of the county, operating a hotel, motel, inn, lodge, tourist camp, tourist cabin or other place in which rooms, lodgings or accommodations are regularly furnished for value. Such tax shall not include charges for homes, apartments or accommodations furnished for extended occupancy or for the use of meeting rooms.
The proceeds of this excise tax shall be used in the manner provided for in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-51(a)(3).
(Code 1979, § 2-10-70; Ord. of 3-18-1986, § 1; Ord. of 4-3-1990, § 1; Ord. No. 07-14, § 1, 12-4-2007)
Sec. 78-67. - Due date of tax.
The hotel and motel excise tax levied in section 78-66 shall become delinquent for each month after the twentieth day of each succeeding month during which it remains unpaid.
Interest and penalties as specified in section 78-73 shall be applied to any portion of the hotel-motel excise tax remaining unpaid after the twentieth day of each month.
(Code 1979, § 2-10-71; Ord. No. 04-2, § 1, 4-20-2004; Ord. No. 18-06, § 1, 6-5-2018 )
Sec. 78-68. - Dealer's collection fee.
Dealers collecting the hotel and motel tax shall be allowed a percentage of the tax due and accounted for, in the amount of three percent, in the form of a deduction in submitting, reporting and paying the amount due, if such amount is not delinquent at the time of payment. Upon the failure to make a timely report and remittance by the 20th day of the month next succeeding the month in which such sales were made, such collection fee shall be forfeited in addition to all other applicable penalties.
(Code 1979, § 2-10-72)
Sec. 78-69. - Remittance; application of funds.
Dealers shall pay the hotel and motel tax with such remittance form as prescribed by the county comptroller. Each dealer shall remit the tax, payable to the board of commissioners, to the county comptroller's office. During the years 1990 and 1991, 60 percent of the hotel and motel tax collected shall be deposited to the general fund of the county, and 40 percent of such tax collected shall be applied to the development of tourism and industrial development within the county. During the years 1992 and 1993, 40 percent of the hotel and motel tax collected shall be deposited to the general fund of the county, and 60 percent of such tax collected shall be applied to the development of tourism and industrial development within the county. During the years 1994 and 1995, 20 percent of the hotel and motel tax collected shall be deposited to the general fund of the county, and 80 percent of such tax collected shall be applied to the development of tourism and industrial development within the county. Beginning in the year 1996, 100 percent of the hotel and motel tax collected shall be used exclusively for development of tourism and industrial development within the county.
(Code 1979, § 2-10-73)
Sec. 78-70. - Verification of books and records.
All books and records of each dealer shall be subject to inspection and audit by the county to verify compliance with this article.
(Code 1979, § 2-10-74)
Sec. 78-71. - In addition to other taxes.
The excise tax provided for in this article shall be in addition to any license fee or occupation tax or charge which may be imposed upon dealers within the incorporated areas of the county.
(Code 1979, § 2-10-75)
Sec. 78-72. - Violation; penalties.
Any person who violates any provision of this article or fails to comply with any requirements of this article shall, upon conviction, in addition to any interest or penalty provided for in this article, be subject to penalties and costs as set forth in section 1-9.
(Code 1979, § 2-10-76; Ord. No. 00-10, § 9, 10-3-2000; Ord. No. 03-1, § 3, 2-18-2003)
Sec. 78-73. - Interest, penalties for delinquent or deficient payment, fraud.
(a)
Interest upon failure to pay tax. When any innkeeper fails to make any return or to pay the full amount of the tax required by an ordinance authorized by this article, there shall be imposed, in addition to other penalties provided by law, a penalty to be added to the tax in the amount of five percent or $5.00, whichever is greater, if the failure is for not more than 30 days and an additional five percent or $5.00, whichever is greater, for each additional 30 days or fraction of 30 days during which the failure continues.
(b)
Penalty for late payment. The penalty for any single violation shall not exceed 25 percent or $25.00 in the aggregate, whichever is greater.
(c)
Penalty for fraud or intent to evade. In the case of a false or fraudulent return or of a failure to file a return where willful intent exists to defraud Columbia County of any tax due under an ordinance authorized by this article, a penalty of 50 percent of the tax due shall be assessed.
(d)
Mitigating factors. If the failure is due to providential cause, an innkeeper may demonstrate the applicability of such mitigation in the procedure set out in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-58 and if shown to the satisfaction of the Columbia County governing authority, the return may be accepted exclusive of penalties and interest.
(Ord. No. 03-1, § 1, 2-18-2003; Ord. No. 04-2, § 2, 4-20-2004; Ord. No. 18-07, § 1, 6-5-2018 )
Sec. 78-74. - Failure to file report.
If any person subject to this article fails to file a report, the board of commissioners, or its designee, shall make an estimate of the amount of the gross receipts of the person, or as the case may be, of the amount of the total rentals which are subject to the tax. The estimate shall be made for the period or periods for which the person failed to make the report and shall be based upon any information which is in the board of commissioner's possession, or which may come into its possession pursuant to inspection or audit under section 78-70. Upon the basis of the estimate, the board of commissioners, or its designee, shall compute and determine the amount required to be paid, adding to the sum computed a penalty equal to 25 percent thereof. One or more determinations may be made for one or more periods. The tax amount determined to be paid shall be subject to any applicable interest or penalty provided for in this article.
(Ord. No. 03-1, § 2, 2-18-2003)
Secs. 78-75—78-100. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - INSURANCE PREMIUM TAX[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— Local tax on life insurance companies, O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.1; local tax on other than life insurance companies, O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2; general assembly authorized to provide for taxation of insurance companies based on gross direct premiums received from insurance policies within the unincorporated areas of counties, Ga. Const. art. IX, § IV, ¶ I(c).

Sec. 78-101. - Levied on gross direct premiums.
Beginning January 1, 1984, and annually thereafter, there is levied a tax of 2½ percent upon the gross direct premiums of all foreign, alien and domestic insurance companies. The tax shall be in addition to the taxes levied by O.C.G.A. § 33-8-4, and such tax may be levied upon the gross direct premiums received by such companies on or after January 1, 1984.
(Code 1979, § 2-10-30; Ord. of 8-17-1983, § 2)
Sec. 78-102. - Calculation of tax; allowable deductions.
The insurance premium tax shall be levied upon premiums derived from policies insuring persons, property or risks in the unincorporated areas of the county from January 1 to December 31 of each year, without regard to business ceded to or assumed from other companies. The tax shall be imposed upon gross premiums received from direct writing without any deduction allowed from premium abatement of any kind or character, for reinsurance or for losses or expenses of any kind; provided, however, deductions shall be allowed for premiums returned, changes of rates or cancelled policies, provided that deductions shall be permitted for returned premiums or assessments, including all policy dividends, refunds or other similar returns paid or credited to policyholders.
(Code 1979, § 2-10-31)
Sec. 78-103. - Filing with state insurance commissioner.
Within 45 days after the adoption of the ordinance from which this article is derived, the clerk of the board of commissioners is directed to file a certified copy of this article with the state commissioner of insurance.
(Code 1979, § 2-10-32)
Sec. 78-104. - Authority to levy.
The taxes levied by this article are levied pursuant to authority granted by state law.
(Code 1979, § 2-10-33)

Sec. 78-136. - Purpose and scope of tax.
The occupation tax levied in this article is for revenue purposes. The occupation tax applies only to those businesses, practices and occupations which are covered by the provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 48-13-5—48-13-26. All other applicable businesses, practices and occupations are taxed by the local government pursuant to the pertinent general and/or local laws and ordinances.
(Ord. No. 15-29, § 1(Exh. A), 11-17-2015)
Sec. 78-137. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Applicant means the natural person applying for the occupational tax benefit. For a sole proprietor the sole proprietor is the natural person application. For a partnership, corporation, limited liability company or other entity, the natural person applicant is an officer having authority to bind the entity.
Business means any person, partnership, corporation, limited liability company or other entity who engages in, causes to be engaged in, and/or is represented as being engaged in any occupation, practice of a profession, sale of any goods or services, commercial activity, or other activity with the object of financial gain, benefit or advantage, either directly or indirectly.
Dominant service or product means the type of service or product from which a business or practice providing multiple services or products derives the greatest amount of income.
Employee means an individual whose work is performed under the direction and supervision of the employer, and whose employer withholds FICA, federal income tax or state income tax from such individual's compensation, or whose employer issues to such individual, for purposes of documenting compensation, a form I.R.S. W-2, but not a form I.R.S. 1099.
First responder means peace officers certified under the Georgia Peace Officer Standards and Training Council as provided for in O.C.G.A. § 35-8-10 et seq., certified emergency medical technicians, paramedics, and cardiac technicians as provided for in O.C.G.A. § 31-11-52 et seq., and certified firefighters as provided for in O.C.G.A. § 25-4-1 et seq.
Location and office mean, and shall include, any structure or vehicle where a business, profession or occupation is conducted, but shall not include a temporary or construction work site which serves a single customer or project, or a vehicle used for sales or delivery by a business or practitioner of a profession or occupation which has a location or office.
Occupational tax means a tax levied on persons, partnerships, corporations or other entities for engaging in an occupation, profession or business which has been enacted by the county as a revenue-raising ordinance or resolution.
Practice, practitioner and practitioners of professions and occupations mean one who state law requires licensure regulating such profession or occupation. Such terms shall not include a practitioner who is an employee of a business if the business pays an occupation tax.
(Ord. No. 15-29, § 1(Exh. A), 11-17-2015; Ord. No. 16-04 , § 1, 3-29-2016)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 78-138. - Payment required.
For the calendar year 1995 and succeeding years thereafter, except those businesses and practitioners of professions and occupations excluded by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-16(a) each business and practitioner of professions and occupations with one or more locations or offices located within the county shall pay an occupation tax per fixed location as set out in this article.
(Ord. No. 15-29, § 1(Exh. A), 11-17-2015)
Sec. 78-139. - Tax on businesses and practitioners of professions and occupations with no location or office in the state.
(a)
Those businesses, practitioners of professions and occupations with no location or office within the state shall pay an occupational tax, as set out in this article, if such business has one or more employees or agents who exert substantial efforts within the county for the purpose of soliciting business or serving customers or clients; or
(b)
The number of employees of a business or practice, subject to this section, shall include only those employees engaged in substantial efforts within the state.
(c)
Businesses and practitioners subject to this section shall be required to pay an occupation tax only to one local government in the state. Such payment will be made to the county only if the largest dollar volume of business is done or service is performed within the county.
(d)
If a business or practitioner subject to this article provides the county development services division with proof of payment of a local business or occupation tax in another state which purports to tax the business' or practitioner's sales or services in the state, the business or practitioner shall be exempt from this county's occupational tax.
(Ord. No. 15-29, § 1(Exh. A), 11-17-2015)

Sec. 78-141. - Issuance of tax certificate; proper zoning required; revocation of tax certificate.
(a)
Under the payment of the occupational taxes assessed in this article, it shall be the duty of the development services division to issue to each business an occupational tax certificate, provided that all information required has been furnished. The information required shall include the name and address of the business, a description of the type of business and the products and services being provided, the North American Industry Classification System (NAICS) code of the business, the number of employees, the applicant for the business and the applicant's relationship to the business, and any other information deemed necessary by the director. Provided further that the business or applicant is not disentitled to such under other provisions of this Code, or federal or state law. All businesses, other than a natural person as sole proprietor, are required to produce a certificate from the Georgia Secretary of State that such corporation is in good standing according to the records of his office.
(b)
No tax certificate shall be issued unless the business and premises for which such application is made shall comply to all respects with the county zoning ordinance and with all other laws and ordinances applicable thereto.
(c)
Those businesses, practitioners of professions and occupations with no location or office within the county shall be exempt from the occupational tax upon providing a completed occupational tax registration for businesses outside Columbia County and a paid occupational tax certificate from another jurisdiction within Georgia.
(Ord. No. 15-29, § 1(Exh. A), 11-17-2015)
Sec. 78-142. - Election of tax by certain practitioners.
(a)
Practitioners of professions and occupations listed in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-9(c)(1)—(19) shall elect one of the following as their entire occupation tax:
(1)
The occupation tax based on the number of employees, as set forth in section 78-140; or
(2)
A fee per practitioner who is licensed to provide the service, as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk. A practitioner paying according to this subsection shall not be required to provide information to the county relating to the gross receipts of the business or practice. The per practitioner fee applies to each person in the business who qualifies as a practitioner under the state's regulatory guidelines and framework.
(b)
The election between subsections (a)(1) and (a)(2) of this section shall be made on an annual basis, and must be submitted to the county development services division prior to January 1 of each calendar year.
(Ord. No. 15-29, § 1(Exh. A), 11-17-2015)
Sec. 78-143. - Classification of businesses or practitioners with more than one type of service or product.
(a)
A business or practitioner with more than one type of service or product shall be classified according to such business' or practitioner's dominant service or product.
(b)
The business or practice occupational tax certificate of each business or practice operated in the county shall identify the services or products provided by the business or practitioner. No business or practitioner shall provide or offer to provide any products or services without first having such product or service registered with the county's development services division, and shall have such noted on the business' or practitioner's occupational tax certificate which is required to be displayed.
(Ord. No. 15-29, § 1(Exh. A), 11-17-2015)
Sec. 78-144. - Local government using different tax criteria.
(a)
When more than one local government levies an occupation tax on a business or practitioner which has locations encompassed by the county and other local governments, and such governments use different criteria to establish the amount of occupation tax, the county shall base its tax on the number of employees who are employed within the county's geographical jurisdiction by such business or practitioner. In the case of an employee who works for the same business or practice in more than one municipal corporation or county, the county shall include such person as an employee in its occupation tax calculations if the employee works for the longest period of time within the calendar year within the county's geographic jurisdiction.
(Ord. No. 15-29, § 1(Exh.. A), 11-17-2015)
Sec. 78-145. - Transfer of certificate.
Occupational tax certificates shall not be transferrable. Transfer of ownership or change in location shall be treated as the termination of the business and the establishment of a new business and a new certificate shall be required.
(Ord. No. 15-29, § 1(Exh. A), 11-17-2015)
Sec. 78-146. - Reserved.
Sec. 78-147. - Real estate brokers subject to tax.
Real estate brokers maintaining a principal or branch office within the county are subject to the occupation tax imposed by this article. The license, occupation or professional tax shall permit the broker and the broker's affiliated associate brokers and salespersons to engage in all of the brokerage activities set forth in O.C.G.A. § 43-40-1 without further licensing or taxing other than the state licenses identified pursuant to O.C.G.A. ch. 40, tit. 43. No additional license, occupation or professional tax shall be required of the broker's affiliated associate brokers or salespersons.
(Ord. No. 15-29, § 1(Exh. A), 11-17-2015)
Sec. 78-148. - Option to establish exemption or reduction in tax.
(a)
The board of commissioners may, by subsequent ordinance or resolution, provide for an exemption or reduction in the occupation tax to one or more types of businesses or practitioners of occupations or professional as part of a plan for economic development or attracting, encouraging or maintaining selected types of businesses or practitioners of selected occupations or professions. Such exemptions or reductions in the occupation tax shall not be granted arbitrarily or capriciously, and the reasons for the granting of such exemption shall be set forth in the minutes of the meetings in which the board of commissioners makes such a determination.
(b)
State and local authorities, as well as nonprofit organizations, are exempt from any occupation tax levy.
(Ord. No. 15-29, § 1(Exh. A), 11-17-2015)
Sec. 78-149. - When tax due and payable.
All occupation taxes authorized and imposed by this article, shall be due and payable annually within 30 days following January 1 of each year. In the event that any person commences business on any date after January 1, the tax shall be due and payable prior to the commencement of the business.
(Ord. No. 15-29, § 1(Exh. A), 11-17-2015)
Sec. 78-150. - Amount of fee or tax due from businesses commenced on or after July 1.
When any person commences business on or after July 1 in any calendar year, the occupation tax for the remaining portion of the year shall be 50 percent of the tax imposed for the entire year.
(Ord. No. 15-29, § 1(Exh. A), 11-17-2015)
Sec. 78-151. - Display of certificate and licenses.
(a)
Each business or practitioner shall exhibit and display all occupational tax certificates issued to them under this Code in a conspicuous place in their business establishment at which address the certificate was issued. Any transient or nonresident business or practitioner shall carry the occupational tax certificate either upon his person or in any vehicle or other conveyance which is used in the business and the person shall exhibit the same to any authorized enforcement or police officer.
(b)
Each business or practitioner subject to any special or occupation tax under this article, who is also licensed by the state, shall post the state license in a conspicuous place in the licensee's place of business, and shall keep the license there at all times while the license remains valid.
(c)
Any business or practitioner who is required to obtain health permits, bonds, certificates of qualification, certificates of competency or any other regulatory matter shall provide the county development services division with proof that such requirements has been fulfilled prior to providing or offering to provide services or products in the county.
(Ord. No. 15-29, § 1(Exh. A), 11-17-2015)
Sec. 78-152. - Inspection of books and records.
The board of commissioners, through its designated representative, may inspect the books and records of any business or practitioner operating in the county for the purpose of verifying the information related to the calculation, assessment and collection of the occupation tax imposed by this article.
(Ord. No. 15-29, § 1(Exh. A), 11-17-2015)
Secs. 78-153, 78-154. - Reserved.
Sec. 78-155. - Conflicts between specific and general provisions.
Where there is an apparent conflict in this article between specific and general provisions, it is the intention of this section that the specific shall control.
(Ord. No. 15-29, § 1(Exh. A), 11-17-2015)
Sec. 78-156. - Delinquency; violation; penalties.
(a)
Interest for all delinquent occupation taxes under this article shall accrue at a rate of 1.5 percent per month.
(b)
Should any occupation tax imposed by this article remain due and unpaid for 90 days from the due date of such tax, the business or practitioner liable for the tax shall be subject to a penalty of ten percent of the tax due.
(c)
Should any business or practitioner fail to renew its occupational tax certificate by July 1 following its expiration, the occupational tax certificate permitting the business will be automatically revoked.
(d)
Any business or practitioner convicted of violating any provision of this article shall be subject to suspension by the county of such business' or practitioner's occupational tax certificate and shall be subject to the penalties set forth in section 1-9 of the Code of Ordinances of Columbia County, Georgia.
(Ord. No. 15-29, § 1(Exh. A), 11-17-2015)
Sec. 78-157. - Exemption for persons practicing independently as licensed or certified first responders.
Any first responder shall be exempt from the payment of any occupational tax levied herein; provided however that this exemption shall only apply to the practice of such persons when they are working outside of their primary employment, as independent contractors on special assignments for which a license or certification is used or required.
(Ord. No. 16-04 , § 2, 3-29-2016)

ARTICLE VI. - IMPROVEMENTS AND SERVICES FINANCING

Sec. 78-191. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Department head means the employee, agent, contractor or elected official of the county charged with the responsibility of carrying out the duties set forth in this article.
Tax assessor means the employee in charge of the county tax assessor's office.
Tax commissioner means the county tax commissioner.
(Code 1979, § 2-8-1)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 78-192. - Purpose; scope.
The purpose of this article is to provide for the services and improvements set forth in this section, and to provide for the manner and method of assessing the costs of such services and improvements, or the creation of special tax districts to pay for such services and improvements. The following services and improvements are covered by this article:
(1)
Police and fire protection;
(2)
Garbage and solid waste collection and disposal;
(3)
Public health facilities and services, including hospitals, ambulances, emergency rescue services and animal control;
(4)
Street and road construction and maintenance, including curbs, sidewalks, streetlights and devices to control the flow of traffic on streets and roads;
(5)
Parks, recreational areas, programs and facilities;
(6)
Stormwater and sewerage collection and disposal systems; and
(7)
Development, treatment, storage, purification and distribution of water.
(Code 1979, § 2-8-2)
Sec. 78-193. - Preliminary survey and recommendation.
Whenever a proposal to adopt or provide any of the improvements or services as set forth in section 78-192 has been filed with the board of commissioners, or the board of commissioners on their own motion have considered a proposal to furnish such improvement or to provide such utility, the board of commissioners shall cause the appropriate department head to make a preliminary survey and recommendation to the board of commissioners.
(Code 1979, § 2-8-3)
Sec. 78-194. - Hearing; notice.
Whenever the board of commissioners shall determine from the preliminary survey that such project has merit and will serve a public need, and that the costs thereof shall be borne in whole or in part by the owners of the property affected, the board of commissioners shall set a time and place for a public hearing before the board of commissioners on such preliminary survey. Notice of such hearing shall be given to the owners of the property affected, by regular mail, if practicable, at least ten days in advance of such hearing, and by publication of such notice, one time, not less than ten days in advance of such hearing, in the newspaper in which notices of sheriff's sales for the county are published. Such notice shall generally describe the improvement or service to be constructed or furnished, the area affected and the manner in which the costs therefor shall be assessed or taxed against the owners of the affected property.
(Code 1979, § 2-8-4)
Sec. 78-195. - Action on proposal.
At the time and place fixed for a hearing as set forth in section 78-194, and after publication of the notice of such hearing, the board of commissioners shall proceed to hear the proposal, including objections thereto, if any, of any party at interest, and shall take such action rejecting such proposal, postponing such proposal until some future date, or approving the proposal, whichever action, in the judgment of the board of commissioners, will best promote the public interest.
(Code 1979, § 2-8-5)
Sec. 78-196. - Resolution authorizing work.
If the proposal for service or improvement is accepted by the board of commissioners, and in the best judgment of the board of commissioners the costs of such work shall be assessed against the affected property owners, the board of commissioners shall pass a resolution authorizing the necessary work to be done in order to provide such improvement or service, and shall provide in such resolution the manner and method of assessing the costs therefor against the property owners affected, and designating the department head whose responsibility it is to carry out such proposals.
(Code 1979, § 2-8-6)
Sec. 78-197. - Report of work and cost.
The department head charged by the resolution passed pursuant to section 78-196 shall, upon completion of the work or upon instituting the service, make a full report thereof to the board of commissioners, which report shall include the final costs of the project, and whether or not the costs, in whole or in part, to be assessed against the affected property owners is greater than or less than the amount anticipated in the preliminary survey.
(Code 1979, § 2-8-7)
Sec. 78-198. - Assessment resolution.
Upon receipt of the report as set forth in section 78-197, the board of commissioners shall, if the cost is greater or less than the anticipated cost, pass a resolution providing for the amount to be assessed against the affected property owners.
(Code 1979, § 2-8-8)
Sec. 78-199. - Notice to owner of assessment; collection.
The department head shall cause the affected property owner to be notified of the amount of the assessment as set forth in section 78-198, and shall collect the assessment in full in accordance with the terms and conditions of payment as set forth in the resolution. Failure to receive such notice shall not invalidate the assessment.
(Code 1979, § 2-8-9)
Sec. 78-200. - Imposition of lien.
The costs of any improvement constructed pursuant to this article or resolution as set forth in this article shall be a lien upon the affected properties from the date of the completion of such improvement, and shall be enforceable in the same manner as executions for taxes are enforced, and shall have the same rank and character as liens for taxes.
(Code 1979, § 2-8-10)
Sec. 78-201. - Executions for assessments in arrears; issuance.
Whenever the owner of any property shall fail to pay any assessment when due, the department head shall notify the clerk of the board of commissioners of the amount of the assessment in arrears, the name of the property owner and the affected property. The clerk shall issue an execution therefor, which execution shall be issued in the name of the board of commissioners, and shall be signed by the chairman of the board of commissioners. Such execution shall issue in rem against the property and in personam against the owner of such property, and in addition to the amount in arrears, shall include the sum as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk for the issuance of such execution. Subsequent costs shall be the same as those now allowed by law to the sheriff for the performance of similar services.
(Code 1979, § 2-8-11)
Sec. 78-202. - Recording of execution; enforcement.
All executions issued against property and the owner of such property shall immediately be recorded on the general execution docket in the office of the clerk of the superior court, and shall be handed to the sheriff, and subsequent proceedings thereon shall be as provided by law for the enforcement of execution for taxes.
(Code 1979, § 2-8-12)
Sec. 78-203. - Creation of special tax districts.
In the judgment of the board of commissioners, whenever services or improvements to be furnished under this article cannot be paid for in the manner and method provided in this article, the board of commissioners shall, by appropriate resolution, create a special tax district within the unincorporated areas of the county in which the services or improvements shall be furnished. Such resolution shall provide for the services or improvements to be furnished, the costs thereof, the area affected thereby and the amount to be assessed against each property owner in such district, and shall designate the department head who is charged with the responsibility of carrying out the improvements or services.
(Code 1979, § 2-8-13)
Sec. 78-204. - Notice of completion to tax assessor and tax commissioner.
Upon completion of the improvements or institution of the services furnished pursuant to a resolution adopted under section 78-203, the department head shall notify the tax assessor and tax commissioner of the action taken, the district involved and the costs to be assessed against the property owners of such district.
(Code 1979, § 2-8-14)
Sec. 78-205. - List of taxpayers to be furnished to tax commissioner.
The tax assessor's office shall compile a list of the property owners within the special tax district, and shall immediately furnish such list to the tax commissioner.
(Code 1979, § 2-8-15)
Sec. 78-206. - Collection of assessments.
The tax commissioner shall, on the next succeeding tax bill, place on such tax bill the amount to be assessed against the property owners affected by such assessment, pursuant to the resolution for such work, which shall be collected in the same manner as other ad valorem taxes levied by the county.
(Code 1979, § 2-8-16)
Secs. 78-207—78-250. - Reserved.
ARTICLE VII. - ENERGY EXCISE TAX

Sec. 78-251. - Energy excise tax definitions.
As used in this article, the terms: "dealer;" "energy;" "local sales and use tax;" and "purchaser" shall have the same meanings as provided in O.C.G.A. § 48-13-110.
(Ord. No. 13-02, § 1, 4-2-2013)
Sec. 78-252. - Imposition; rate; remitting; recovery; liabilities.
In accordance with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-110 et seq., there is imposed within the territorial limits of the special district created pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-111, an excise tax on the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy when such sale, use, storage, or consumption would have constituted a taxable event for purposes of sales and use tax under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-1 et seq. but for the exemption in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2.
(Ord. No. 13-02, § 2, 4-2-2013)
Sec. 78-253. - Excise tax rate; phase-in.
(a)
Such excise tax shall be levied and collected by the governing authority of Columbia County pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 48-13-112 and 48-13-114 and as provided in this section.
(b)
Except as otherwise provided in this section, the rate of such excise tax shall be phased in over a four-year period as follows:
(1)
For the period commencing January 1, 2013, and concluding at the last moment of December 31, 2013, such excise tax shall be at a rate equivalent to 25 percent of the total amount of local sales and use tax in effect in such special district that would be collected on the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy but for the exemption in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2;
(2)
For the period commencing January 1, 2014, and concluding at the last moment of December 31, 2014, such excise tax shall be at a rate equivalent to 50 percent of the total amount of local sales and use tax in effect in such special district that would be collected on the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy but for the exemption in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2;
(3)
For the period commencing January 1, 2015, and concluding at the last moment of December 31, 2015, such excise tax shall be at a rate equivalent to 75 percent of the total amount of local sales and use tax in effect in such special district that would be collected on the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy but for the exemption in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2; and
(4)
On or after January 1, 2016, such excise tax shall be at a rate equivalent to 100 percent of the total amount of local sales and use tax in effect in such special district that would be collected on the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy but for the exemption in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2.
(c)
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2(c)(4), if a project of regional significance under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3(92) is started in the special district, it shall not be subject to the phase-in period contained in paragraphs (1), (2), and (3) of subsection (b) of this section, and the sale, use, storage, or consumption of energy in connection therewith shall be subject to the rate specified in paragraph (4) of subsection (b) of this section notwithstanding the January 1, 2016 limitation in that paragraph (4).
(d)
(1)
Following such initial imposition during the phase-in time period, on or after January 1, 2016, the rate of the excise tax levied and collected by the governing authority of Columbia County shall be directed by O.C.G.A. § 48-13-112(d). Such rate shall be controlled by the maximum amount of local sales and use tax in effect in the special district, but in no event more than two percent except in the case where the municipal option water and sewer tax is levied where that rate will be three percent within such municipality.
(2)
In the event the total rate of local sales and use taxes in effect in the special district decreases from two percent to one percent, the rate of the excise tax under this article shall likewise be reduced at the same time such local sales and use tax rate reduction becomes effective.
(3)
In the event the total rate of local sales and use taxes in effect in the special district increases from one percent to two percent, the rate of the excise tax under this article shall likewise be increased at the same time such local sales and use tax rate increase becomes effective.
(Ord. No. 13-02, § 3, 4-2-2013)
Sec. 78-254. - Imposition; remittance; recovery.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-13-112(c), such excise tax shall be:
(1)
Imposed only at the time sales and use tax on the sale or use of such energy would have been due and payable under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-30 but for the exemption under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2;
(2)
Due and payable in the same manner as would be required under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-1 et seq., except as other provided in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-110 et seq.;
(3)
A debt of the purchaser of energy until it is paid and shall be recoverable at law in the same manner as authorized for the recovery of other debts;
(4)
Remitted to the governing authority of Columbia County by the dealer collecting such excise tax; and
(5)
A liability of the dealer at the applicable rate on the charges actually collected or the amount of excise taxes collected from purchasers, whichever is greater.
(Ord. No. 13-02, § 4, 4-2-2013)
Sec. 78-255. - Exemption certificates.
(a)
The governing authority of Columbia County, or the collecting officer appointed under section 78-258 of this article, shall make diligent efforts to identify all energy providers and purchasers in the special district and to ascertain whether such a purchaser has completed a uniform exemption certificate provided from the website of the Georgia Department of Revenue seeking to qualify such purchaser for the exemption under O.C.G.A. § 48-8-3.2. Such certificate shall be utilized by such governing authority or collecting officer for the purpose of determining the applicability of the excise tax under this article.
(b)
An energy provider shall be authorized to rely upon such uniform exemption certificates. An energy provider shall not be liable for failing to collect and remit the excise tax imposed pursuant to this article if the purchaser has failed to submit such uniform exemption certificate to the energy provider.
(Ord. No. 13-02, § 5, 4-2-2013)
Sec. 78-256. - Commencement of collections.
(a)
Due date of tax. The excise tax imposed pursuant to this article shall become effective upon the date of its adoption and shall be due and payable to the governing authority of Columbia County monthly on or before the twentieth day of every month and each respective month in which such taxes are collected, and payment shall be accompanied by a return for the preceding monthly period showing the gross sales and purchases arising from all sales and purchases taxable under this section during the preceding calendar month.
(b)
Interest upon failure to pay tax. When any dealer fails to make any return or to pay the full amount of the tax required by an ordinance authorized by this article, there shall be imposed, in addition to other penalties provided by law, a penalty to be added to the tax in the amount of five percent or $5.00, whichever is greater, if the failure is for not more than 30 days and an additional five percent or $5.00, whichever is greater, for each additional 30 days or fraction of 30 days during which the failure continues.
(c)
Penalty for late payment. The penalty for any single violation shall not exceed 25 percent or $25.00 in the aggregate, whichever is greater.
(d)
Penalty for fraud or intent to invade. In the case of a false or fraudulent return or of a failure to file a return where willful intent exists to defraud the governing authority of any tax due under an ordinance authorized by this article, a penalty of 50 percent of the tax due shall be assessed.
(e)
Mitigating factors. If the failure is due to providential cause, a dealer may demonstrate the applicability of such mitigation in the procedure set out in O.C.G.A. § 48-8-123 and if shown to the satisfaction of the Columbia County governing authority, the return may be accepted exclusive of penalties and interest.
(Ord. No. 13-02, § 6, 4-2-2013; Ord. No. 14-13, § 6, 11-4-2014; Ord. No. 18-05, § 1, 6-5-2018 )
Sec. 78-257. - Allocation, distribution, and expenditure of proceeds.
(a)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-13-114(b), the excise tax proceeds shall be allocated and distributed by the governing authority of Columbia County at the end of each calendar month. An amount equal to one percent of such proceeds collected by Columbia County shall be paid into the general fund of Columbia County to defray the costs of collection and administration.
(b)
The remainder shall be distributed in accordance with the intergovernmental agreement as required under O.C.G.A. § 48-13-114(b) and (c). Within 30 days following the end of each calendar month, the remainder shall be distributed to the general fund of Columbia County and the general fund of each participating municipality in accordance with the applicable provisions of O.C.G.A. § 48-13-114(c)(1) or (2) as follows:
(1)
If two local sales and use taxes are in effect in the special district, an amount equal to one-half of the proceeds shall be distributed to the general fund of Columbia County and the general fund of each participating municipality located in the county according to the same proportionate share as specified under the distribution provisions of the first local sales and use tax and an amount equal to one-half of the proceeds of the excise tax shall be distributed to the general fund of Columbia County and the general fund of each participating municipality located in such county according to the same proportionate share as specified under the distribution provisions of the second local sales and use tax; or
(2)
If only one such local sales and use tax is in effect in the special district, then the proceeds of the excise tax shall be distributed to the general fund of Columbia County and the general fund of each participating municipality located in the county according to the same proportionate share as specified under the distribution provisions of the local sales and use tax.
(c)
Except as provided in subsection (a) of this section, proceeds of the excise tax deposited in the general fund of Columbia County may be expended for any lawful purpose as determined by the governing authority of Columbia County.
(Ord. No. 13-02, § 7, 4-2-2013)
Sec. 78-258. - Administrative provisions; audits; examinations; collecting officer.
(a)
The governing authority of Columbia County shall be authorized to designate a collecting officer for collection and administration of the excise tax under this article.
(b)
The collecting officer shall be authorized to provide for procedures necessary to the proper implementation of this article, including, but not limited to, periodic auditing of dealers collecting and remitting the excise tax. The collecting officer, or the designee of the collecting officer, is specifically authorized to examine the books, papers, records, financial reports, equipment, or other facilities of any purchaser which is required to remit the excise tax under this article.
(c)
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-13-118, as part of the audit report required under O.C.G.A. § 36-81-7, the auditor shall include, in a separate schedule, a report of the revenues pertaining to the excise tax under this article.
(Ord. No. 13-02, § 8, 4-2-2013)
