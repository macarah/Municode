ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 14-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Abandoned animal means any animal which has been left unattended or uncared for on public or private property.
Advisory board means the animal services advisory board created under this article.
Animal means any member of the kingdom Animalia.
Animal Services Department means the Animal Services Department within the Community and Leisure Services division of the Columbia County Government.
Animal control officer means any employee of the county or the county sheriff, duly commissioned under the legal authority of the county to enforce the rules and regulations of this chapter.
Animal shelter means any facility operated by or under contract for the county for the purpose of impounding or harboring seized, stray, homeless, abandoned or unwanted dogs, cats and other animals.
Animal running at-large means any animal not under immediate control, which is not on a leash, not in a vehicle or not confined within the property limits of his owner, but shall not include animals which are actively engaged in sporting activities, including, but not limited to, hunting, field trials, shows, competition or other exhibitions.
Authority means the animal services advisory board, as deemed the hearing board for dangerous and vicious dog appeals.
Cat means any animal classified as Felis domestica.
Cruel tethering means to tether an animal in a manner that:
(1)
Causes injury or illness;
(2)
Utilizes a tether that is too short for an unattended animal or for the animal to urinate or defecate in a separate area from there area where it must eat, drink, or lie down; or
(3)
Does not permit the animal access to adequate food, water, shade, or shelter.
County kennel means any premises designated by the animal care and control division for the purpose of impounding and caring for dogs, cats and other animals found running at-large in violation of this chapter.
Dangerous dog means any dog that, that is defined as such in title 4, chapter 8 of the Georgia State Code.
Dog means the animal classified as Canis domestica.
Guard or watch dog means any dog trained by a recognized training facility for the purpose of protecting individuals from assault and/or preventing property loss or damage.
Impoundment means taking physical possession and control of an animal and transporting such animal to the county kennel.
Inoculation means the administration of an antirabies vaccine, approved by the county board of health, administered by a licensed veterinarian or other person approved and authorized by the board of health of the county to administer inoculations.
Livestock means animals which include fowl, horses, cows, goats, sheep, pigs or any other four-legged animal used for pleasure or profit, excluding dogs and cats.
Owner means any natural person or legal entity, including, but not limited to, a corporation, partnership, firm or trust, who owns, harbors, keeps, feeds, maintains, has lawful possession of, or knowingly causes or permits an animal to be harbored or kept, or has an animal in his care, or who permits an animal to remain on or about his premises; provided, however, this shall not include a person hired or acting as custodian of the animal for its owner. In the case of a dog owned by a minor, the term "owner" includes the parents or person in loco parentis with custody of the minor.
Public nuisance animal means any animal as set forth in section 14-12.
Public or private animal refuge means harborers of unwanted animals who provide food, shelter and confinement for a group of dogs, cats or a combination thereof.
Recognized training facility means any person holding a state kennel license, and conducting a business of training dogs for purposes of protecting individuals from assault and/or preventing property loss or damage.
Records of an appropriate authority means records of any:
(1)
State, county or municipal law enforcement agency;
(2)
County or municipal animal care and control agency;
(3)
County board of health; federal, state or local court; or
(4)
An animal control officer provided for in this chapter.
Serious injury means any physical injury as defined in title 4, chapter 8 of the Georgia State Code.
Sexually mature animal means any animal that has reached the age of 180 days.
Shelter means any structurally sound enclosure that has a minimum of three solid sides, a roof, and solid bottom. The shelter shall offer clean, sanitary, and humane protection from the weather. The shelter must be sufficient to permit the animal to retain body heat and keep the animal dry and protected from the elements.
Sterilization means the surgical removal of the reproductive organs of any animal in order to render the animal unable to reproduce.
Vicious animal means any animal as defined in title 4, chapter 8 of the Georgia State Code.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 14-2. - Animal Services Department to carry out purposes of chapter.
It shall be the duty of the Animal Services department to carry out the purposes of this chapter, and to provide for a suitable impoundment of all animals in violation of this chapter which are seized by the Animal Services department.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-3. - Duties of animal control officers.
Animal control officers shall have the authority to issue citations for violations of this chapter, to impound animals as provided in this chapter, and to perform such other duties as are set forth in this chapter. An animal control officer shall wear a uniform and a county identification badge identifying him as an animal control officer.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Cross reference— Officers and employees, § 2-186 et seq.
Sec. 14-4. - Animal Services Advisory Board created.
There is created an Animal Services Advisory Board which shall consist of seven members who shall be appointed as provided for in the bylaws promulgated by the board of commissioners for the operation of the Animal Services Advisory Board. The terms of all existing members of the Animal Services Advisory Board shall terminate as of December 31, 2007. The members of the Animal Services Advisory Board appointed for terms beginning on or after January 1, 2008 shall serve for the terms as specified in the bylaws of the Animal Services Advisory Board. Each member of the Animal Services Advisory Board shall serve without pay and shall carry out the duties set forth in section 14-5 of the Code of Ordinances. The manager of the Animal Services Department shall serve as an advisor to the Animal Services Advisory Board and shall attend all meetings thereof. The board of commissioners may by resolution duly adopted promulgate bylaws to govern the operations of the Animal Services Advisory Board.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Cross reference— Boards and commissioners, § 2-31 et seq.
Sec. 14-5. - Duties of the Animal Services Advisory Board.
The Animal Services Advisory Board is authorized to propose, for adoption by the board of commissioners, rules and regulations covering the operation of the Animal Services Department including, but not limited to, the impounding and disposal of animals, the disposal of dead animals, the fees to be charged in connection therewith when not otherwise provided for in this chapter, and the manner and method to be employed for such actions. Such rules and regulations shall not become effective until approved by the board of commissioners. The Animal Services Advisory Board shall not be authorized to spend any monies of or create any debt in the name of the county. Any rules and regulations approved by the board of commissioners shall be typed and posted at the county kennel or shelter, shall be published once in the official gazette of the county, at the county's expense, and shall be available for distribution to individuals requesting a copy of such through the Animal Services Department. The Animal Services Advisory Board shall hear and determine matters and shall serve as the animal control board for the purpose of hearing and determining matters under title 4, chapter 8 of the Georgia State Code.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-6. - Restraint of animals.
(a)
Animals running at-large. It shall be unlawful for any owner or possessor of an animal to allow such animal to run at-large within the unincorporated areas of the county, whether or not such animal is wearing a collar and tag. Any animal found running at-large, whether wearing a collar and tag or not, may be impounded by an animal control officer or any police officer. The officer may pursue the animal onto private property in order to capture such animal.
(b)
Duty of animal owners. It shall be the duty of every owner or custodian of an animal to exercise reasonable care to protect other people, property and animals from injuries or damage which might result from such person's animal's behavior. If the owner or custodian of any animal is a minor, the parent or guardian of such minor shall be responsible to see that such reasonable care is exercised.
(c)
Duty to keep dogs under restraint while on owner's property. It shall be the duty of every owner, possessor or custodian of a dog to exercise reasonable care to prevent the dog from leaving the real property limits of its owner, possessor or custodian. At such times as a dog is unattended, such dog shall be securely and humanely enclosed within a house, building, fence, pen or other enclosure from which it cannot climb, dig, jump or otherwise escape on its own volition.
(d)
Duty to keep dog under restraint while off property. It shall be the duty of the owner or custodian of any dog to keep such dog under restraint and control at all times while the dog is off the real property limits of the owner, possessor or custodian.
(1)
For the purpose of this subsection (d), a dog is deemed under control when it is:
a.
Securely and humanely confined within a vehicle;
b.
Properly confined within a secure enclosure with the permission of the owner of the property where the enclosure is located;
c.
Securely restrained by a leash or other device held by a competent person;
(2)
This subsection (d) shall not apply to dogs which are actively engaged in sporting activities, including, but not limited to, hunting, field trials, shows, competitions or other exhibitions.
(e)
All livestock shall be properly housed with an adequate food and water supply, and confined within a fenced enclosure.
(f)
No person shall tie, stake or fasten any animal within any street, highway, road, alley, sidewalk, right-of-way or other public place. Without the express permission of the property owner, no person shall tie, stake or fasten any animal so that the animal could cross any property limits or boundaries.
(g)
Failure to restrain unlawful. Failure to keep any animal under restraint or control as provided for in this section shall be unlawful and punishable as provided in section 14-20.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-7. - Wildlife.
All complaints and matters concerning wild animals, not of a domestic nature, and complaints involving game animals or protected species and/or hunting, shall be forwarded to the game and fish division of the state department of natural resources or the United States Department of Agriculture, Law Enforcement Division.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-8. - Impoundment of animals; disposition.
(a)
All animals found running at-large in violation of this chapter may be seized or impounded by an animal control officer or any officer empowered to act by law, and detained at the county kennel. In the alternative, in his discretion, the animal control officer may return the animal to its owner. If the animal is impounded, the Animal Services Department shall notify the owner, if the owner is known or can be reasonably ascertained, that the animal has been impounded. The owner of any animal impounded may, within five days after such impoundment, reclaim the animal by the payment of an impoundment fee, plus boarding fee, as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the county clerk's office, for each day the animal was impounded. If a dog or cat which is impounded has not been inoculated for rabies as required by this article, the owner, upon reclaiming the dog or cat, must agree to promptly take the dog or cat to a veterinarian, have such animal properly inoculated, and pay the cost for such inoculation. Failure to comply with such agreement shall cause the owner of such animal to be in violation of this chapter and subject the owner to penalties as set forth in section 14-20.
(b)
If any animal impounded by the Animal Services Department evidences symptoms of contagious, infectious or fatal diseases, is seriously ill or severely injured, the Animal Services Department shall determine whether to treat the animal or euthanize the animal. Such action may be taken to control the spread of disease or to eliminate any further pain and suffering of the animal. If the owner is known or can be reasonably ascertained and the circumstances of the situation permit, reasonable attempts should be made to stabilize the animal and to afford the owner the opportunity to pick up the animal.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-9. - Reclamation of impounded animals.
Any animal impounded pursuant to the provisions of this article must be reclaimed, by its owner by payment of a fee, and boarding fee for each day such animal was impounded, as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk. If a reclaimed animal has been treated according to section 14-10(c), such person reclaiming such animal shall reimburse the county for the cost of treatment. The Animal Services Department shall cause the owner of such animal to have such animal vaccinated or inoculated as required by this article, at the expense of the person reclaiming such animal. Even if the owner of such animal fails to reclaim the animal, the owner shall be liable to pay the county the impoundment fee, boarding fee and all costs of treatment provided to the animal.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-10. - Humane disposition.
(a)
If an animal is not claimed by its owner within five days of impoundment and at the discretion of the manager of the Animal Services Department or his designee, the animal may be made available for adoption to the public or transferred to an animal rescue group, provided that the animal does not show any apparent signs of illness, injury, contagious or infectious diseases, aggressive or vicious behaviors, and space is available to house the animal. If an animal displays any of these traits, then it shall be the duty of the Animal Services department to euthanize the animal by an injection of sodium pentobarbital in a humane and painless manner as possible by a person approved by the state to euthanize animals. Animals shall not be provided to any public or private institution, animal dealer or other individual who may use or supply the animal for research purposes.
(b)
Any animal which is suffering excessively or which represents a danger to persons or other animals by dangerous, aggressive, feral, or vicious behaviors may be euthanized in the manner set forth in subsection (a) of this section, at the discretion of the manager of the Animal Services Department or his designee, without regard to the five-day holding period.
(c)
When, in the opinion of the manager or his or her designee of the Animal Services Department, a diseased or injured animal which has been taken into custody is in need of immediate treatment so as to lessen the animal's suffering or to prevent the spread of a communicable disease to other animals, may immediately obtain the services of, or place the animal with, a licensed veterinarian for the purpose of administering necessary treatment. The owner of the animal shall be liable to reimburse the county for all costs of such treatment.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-11. - Voluntary owner surrender of owned animals
If a citizen of Columbia County desires, he or she may voluntarily surrender ownership of an animal to the Columbia County Animal Services department contingent upon the Animal Services department having space available and within approval by the managers or the manager's designee's. Dogs will only be accepted after showing of proof that it has current vaccinations for rabies, distemper, hepatitis, parainfluenza, parvo virus, and boardatella. Cats will only be accepted after showing of proof that it has current vaccinations for distemper, rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, and panleukopenia. If the animal is medically unsuitable for adoption and/or shows signs or symptoms of a medical issue or disease, then it cannot be surrendered to the Columbia County Animal Services department. The person surrendering the animal must at the time of transaction sign a transfer of title to ownership form as well as pay a fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk. The Animal Services department may immediately offer the animal for adoption, hold the animal for an indeterminate amount of time, or euthanize the animal.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-12. - Public nuisance animal.
(a)
A "public nuisance animal" means and includes any animal that meets any of the following criteria:
(1)
Is repeatedly found running at-large.
(2)
Is found on the property or damages the property of any person other than its owner or keeper without the express consent of such other owner or the occupant of said other property.
(3)
Attacks a person or another animal without provocation
(4)
Acts in a vicious manner so as to menace any person or other animal.
(5)
Excessively makes disturbing noises, including, but not limited to, continued and repeated howling, barking, whining or other utterances causing unreasonable annoyance, disturbance or discomfort to neighbors or others in close proximity to the premises where the animal is kept.
(6)
Creates unsanitary conditions or offensive and objectionable odors in enclosures or surroundings, and thereby creates unreasonable annoyances or discomfort to neighbors or others in close proximity to the premises where the animal is kept.
(b)
No person shall be charged with violating subsection (c) of this section on the basis of the animal being a public nuisance animal pursuant to subsection (a)(5) of this section unless written warning has previously been issued by an officer authorized to enforce provisions of the Code within 90 days of the charged violation and there are at least two complaining witnesses who shall have signed a written statement stating the address of the animal owner, a description of the animal, the circumstances causing the violation and the date, time and location of the violation. Such complainant shall be required to appear in a court of relevant jurisdiction to address the concern. An animal shall not be deemed a public nuisance animal pursuant to subsection (a)(5) of this section, if at the time a person is trespassing upon the property upon which the animal is kept or any person is on the land adjacent to that on which the animal is kept and is intentionally trying to incite the animal and cause it to make noise or a disturbance.
(c)
It is a violation of this section for any person to maintain or keep an animal in such a manner or in such conditions that it is a public nuisance animal or otherwise allow an animal owned or maintained by it to be a public nuisance animal.
(d)
A public nuisance animal may be impounded and the owner or keeper of such animal may be charged with a violation of this section and shall be subject to penalties and costs as set forth in section 1-9 of the Code. Each day that a violation of this section continues to exist shall constitute a separate offense.
(e)
Complaints concerning animals being kept in violation of chapter 90 of this Code shall be investigated and handled through the county planning department.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Cross reference— Nuisances, § 34-31 et seq.
Sec. 14-13. - Dead animals.
An employee of the Animal Services Department or any other county employee may remove dead animals from public property only if the animal in question does not belong to the complainant. Carcasses of animals on private property must be disposed of by the Animal Services Department or property owner or possessor in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 4-5-1 et seq. or delivered by the owner to the county kennel or shelter. Any dog, cat or small animal carcass to be disposed of by the Animal Services Department from a private residence must first be contained in a plastic bag and delivered to the county kennel or shelter. A fee as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk will be charged. All dead livestock, including horses, cattle and any other large animal, must be disposed of by the animal owner, property owner or possessor thereof in accordance with state and local regulations.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Cross reference— Solid waste, ch. 70.
Sec. 14-14. - Dangerous Dog Control.
All matters pertaining to the control of dangerous or vicious dogs shall be investigated in accordance with title 4, chapter 8 of the Georgia State Code and in accordance with jurisdiction provided by the state law.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-15. - Impoundment of biting animals.
(a)
In accordance with rules and regulations promulgated by the county board of health, except as provided in subsection (b) of this section, an animal known to have bitten a person shall be confined in the county kennel or shelter for a period of ten days. All expenses incurred for boarding of such animal for such ten-day period, including reclamation fees as set forth in section 14-9, shall be paid by the owner or possessor of such biting animal. It is within the Animal Control Officer's discretion to allow a ten-day quarantine to be performed at the owner's home if said animal owner can show proof of a current anti-rabies vaccination for the biting animal and the animal is not a threat of biting another person while under home quarantine.
(b)
In accordance with rules and regulations promulgated by the state department of health, all pets or wild animals known to have bitten a person, which cannot reasonably be held for a rabies observation period, shall be euthanized and a lab specimen of brain tissue sent to the state department of epidemiology for testing and control of rabies.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-16. - Adoption.
(a)
The Animal Services Department or its designee may offer any animal for adoption that has not been reclaimed by its owner within five days after the impoundment of such animal, or shall immediately offer such animal for adoption if the animal is voluntarily surrendered to the Animal Services Department by its owner. All persons adopting animals shall be over 18 years of age, and shall provide proper and humane care, feeding, shelter and protection from the weather, and veterinary treatment as required. It is further required by this chapter that the animal be sterilized as provided for in this chapter 14, article III. Any person adopting an animal shall not use the animal for breeding, exhibition or profit, and shall not engage in any research involving the use of the animal or supply the animal to any individual or institution which may use the animal in research. The frequency with which animals may be adopted and placed in any household may be restricted in the discretion of the Animal Services Department manager.
(b)
Adoptions fees may be set by the board of commissioners from time to time and shall be set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk. In addition, the board of commissioners may require that all or part of the costs of examination, rabies inoculation, neutering or spaying be paid by the adopting party.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-17. - Vaccinations.
(a)
All dogs and cats owned, possessed, harbored or residing within the county shall be inoculated for the prevention of rabies by a veterinarian licensed to practice veterinary medicine in the state, using only those vaccines prescribed and/or approved by the state department of human resources.
(b)
All dogs and cats shall be vaccinated against rabies at three months of age. All dogs and cats shall be revaccinated within 12, 24 or 36 months as specified by the type of vaccine used. Any dog or cat found not to be vaccinated by three months of age must have consent of a licensed veterinarian stating the reason why such dog or cat should not be vaccinated.
(c)
Any person keeping, owning, harboring or having any other type of animal, excluding dogs and cats, is not required to have the animal vaccinated against rabies unless required by the laws of this state, but such failure to have such animal vaccinated is at the risk of such person.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-18. - Interpretation of rules and regulations.
All rules and regulations concerning the inoculation of animals shall be interpreted by the county board of health.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-19. - Interference with Animal Control Officer/Animal Traps.
(a)
It shall be unlawful to interfere with any animal control officer or other officer empowered to act by law, or to take or attempt to take any animal from the county vehicle used to transport such animal, or to take or attempt to take any animal from the county kennel or shelter, impounding area, or county owned traps.
(b)
A citizen of Columbia County, Georgia, may, upon request, utilize an animal trap from animal services provided such citizen abides with all relevant rules and regulations, including but not limited to those regulations related to times to set the trap, duration of the use of the trap, and the provision of adequate care for the animal until it is collected by an Columbia County Animal Services officer. Any citizen utilizing a county-owned animal trap is responsible for the cost to replace the same if it is stolen, damaged, or lost, regardless of fault by the citizen. The clerk of the county commission's office shall maintain a schedule of costs for the replacement of animal traps.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-20. - Violation; penalty.
Any person violating the provisions of this article shall be subject to penalties and costs as set forth in section 1-9. Each day that a violation of this article continues to exist shall constitute a separate offense.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)

ARTICLE II. - CREULTY TO ANIMALS

Sec. 14-61. - Abandonment.
(a)
No person may abandon an animal. As used in this section "abandonment" is defined as deserting, forsaking, or intending to give up absolutely an animal without securing another owner or without providing the necessities of life. "Necessities of life" includes:
(1)
Adequate water which means a constant access to a supply of clean, fresh, and potable water provided in a suitable manner for the species;
(2)
Adequate food which means provision at suitable intervals of quantity of nutritious and suitable food for the species and age, as well as sufficient to maintain a reasonable level of nutrition to allow for proper growth and weight; and
(3)
Adequate shelter which means shelter that reasonably may be expected to protect the animal from physical suffering or impairment of health due to exposure to the elements or adverse weather.
(b)
No person shall release any domesticated animal on any public or private property with the intention of abandoning the animal.
(c)
If an animal is impounded by the Animal Services department; and an owner is known, the owner is required to reclaim the animal in accordance with section 14-9 of this chapter or make arrangements to surrender said animal to the custody of the county. However; if said owner fails to reclaim their animal or surrender the animal, said animal is considered abandoned.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-62. - Prohibited; Exceptions.
No person shall, by his act, omission or neglect, cause unjustifiable physical pain, suffering or death to any living animal. This section shall not apply to the slaughter of animals for the purpose of providing food, nor shall it apply to any person who hunts wild animals in compliance with the game and fish laws of this state, nor shall it apply to killing or injuring an animal for humane purposes or in the furtherance of medical or scientific research.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-63. - Permitted acts.
No person shall perform a cruel act on, harm, maim or kill any animal, or attempt to do so, except a person may:
(1)
Defend his person or property or the person or property of another from injury or damage being caused by an animal; or
(2)
Kill any animal causing injury or damage to livestock or poultry, unless prohibited by local, state or federal law.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-64. - Physical abuse.
It is unlawful for any person to willfully or maliciously kill; maim; disfigure; torture; beat with a stick, chain, club or other object; mutilate; burn or scald with any substance; drive over or otherwise cruelly set upon any animal, except that reasonable force may be employed to drive off vicious or trespassing animals, and a whip or stick may be reasonably, properly and humanely used in the legitimate training and using of an animal.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-65. - Failure to care for and maintain.
(a)
It is unlawful for any person to fail, refuse or neglect to provide an animal in his charge or custody, as owner or otherwise, with proper food, drink, shade, sanitary living conditions, ventilation, care or shelter that is consistent with what a reasonable person of ordinary knowledge would believe is the normal requirement and feeding habit for such animal's size, species, breed, age, and physical condition, or to carry an animal in or upon any vehicle in a cruel or inhumane manner.
(b)
Any animal habitually kept outside shall be provided with a structurally sound, weatherproof enclosure, large enough to accommodate the animal. If more than one animal is habitually kept outside, each animal shall have access to shelter large enough to either accommodate all animals kept outside or shall have access to individual shelter for each animal kept outside.
(c)
Persons using tethering devices must comply with the following regulations:
(1)
A person owning or keeping a dog confined shall provide the dog with access to clean water, food, and the appropriate shelter.
(2)
The shelter shall be an appropriate size for the dog and enable the dog to stand, lie down, and turn.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-66. - Keeping diseased or crippled animal.
It is unlawful for any person to have, keep or harbor an animal which is afflicted with any infectious disease that threatens the life or health of other animals or humans, or any incurable painfully crippling condition. The Animal Services Department may impound such diseased or painfully crippled animals so as long as a warrant has been obtained by the county sheriff or officer of appropriate jurisdiction, and all such animals impounded may be disposed of as soon thereafter as conveniently possible in accordance with section 14-10. The Animal Services Department shall not be required to give any notice of the disposition of such an animal.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-67. - Authority of the Animal Services Department in case of animal neglect.
Whenever the Animal Services Department finds that any animal is or will be without proper care because of injury, illness, incarceration or other voluntary or involuntary absence of the owner or person responsible for the care of such animal, or because of the failure, refusal or neglect of the owner or person responsible for the care of such animal to provide proper care, the Animal Services Department may impound such animal and handle the animal as any other impounded animal; and in the event of sickness or injury, the Animal Services Department may take such action as called for to prevent undue pain and suffering of the animal, including immediate humane disposal of the animal.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-68. - Violation; penalties.
Any person who violates any provision of this article or fails to comply with any requirements of this article shall, upon conviction, be subject to penalties and costs as set forth in section 1-9.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Secs. 14-69—14-100. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - STERILIZATION

Sec. 14-101. - Policy.
The provisions for this article are adopted by reference from O.C.G.A. 4-14-1 et seq. Sterilization of dogs and cats in shelters, as well as county ordinance 14-16 Adoption.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-102. - Provisions required; responsibility for costs; proof.
(a)
The Animal Services department shall make provisions for the sterilization of all dogs or cats acquired from such shelter by:
(1)
Providing sterilization by a licensed veterinarian before relinquishing custody of the animal; or
(2)
Entering into a written agreement with the person acquiring such animal guaranteeing that sterilization will be performed by a licensed veterinarian within 30 days after acquisition of such animal in the case of an adult animal, or within 30 days of the sexual maturity of the animal in the case of an immature animal; provided, however, that the requirements of this section shall not apply to any privately owned animal which the shelter, may have in its possession for any reason if the owner of such animal claims or presents evidence that such animal is the property of such person.
(b)
All costs of sterilization pursuant to this section shall be the responsibility of the person acquiring such animal, and, if performed prior to acquisition, may be included in any fees charged by the shelter
(c)
Any person acquiring an animal from the Animal Services department, which animal is not sterile at the time of acquisition, shall submit to the such department a signed statement from the licensed veterinarian performing the sterilization required by subsection (a)(2) of this section within seven days after such sterilization attesting that such sterilization has been performed.
(d)
The Animal Services department operated by a political subdivision of this county shall maintain and furnish to any person acquiring an animal from the shelter, a current list of veterinarians licensed in the state with offices in the county who have notified the shelter, that they are willing to perform sterilizations.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-103. - Violation; penalty.
It shall be a misdemeanor to fail or refuse to comply with the requirements of this article, and any person convicted of such misdemeanor shall be subject to penalties and costs as set forth in section 1-9. Officials from the animal shelter shall have the authority to issue citations for failure to comply with this article.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
Sec. 14-104. - Conflict with other ordinances.
All ordinances and parts of ordinances in conflict with this article are repealed.
( Ord. No. 20-03 , § 1(Exh. A), 3-17-2020)
