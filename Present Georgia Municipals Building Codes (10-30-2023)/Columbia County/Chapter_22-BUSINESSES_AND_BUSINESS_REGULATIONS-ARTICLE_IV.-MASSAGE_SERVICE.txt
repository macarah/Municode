DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 22-71. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Licensee means the person to whom a license is issued under the terms of this article.
Massage means the application of a system of structured touch, pressure, movement, and holding to the soft tissue of the body in which the primary intent is to enhance or restore health and well-being. The term includes complementary methods, including without limitation the external application of water, superficial heat, superficial cold, lubricants, salt scrubs, or other topical preparations and the use of commercially available electromechanical devices which do not require the use of transcutaneous electrodes and which mimic or enhance the actions possible by the hands; the term also includes determining whether massage therapy is appropriate or contraindicated, or whether referral to another health care provider is appropriate. Massage therapy shall not include the use of ultrasound, fluidotherapy, laser, and other methods of deep thermal modalities. Massage services shall include the practice of Reiki.
Massage business means the business of owning, operating, conducting and carrying on the offering or providing of massage services to members of the public.
Operator's license means a license to own, operate, conduct and carry on the business of offering or providing massage to members of the public, which license is issued pursuant to the terms of this article.
Premises means one physically identifiable place of business where massages are performed. This term shall include reception rooms, guest rooms, office areas, patio areas and any other areas located within the property lines of the land on which the massage business is located.
(Ord. No. 17-06 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-6-2017)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 22-72. - Report of licensed employees.
Within ten days after a written request of the licensing and permits office, a holder of an operator's license under this article shall provide to the licensing and permits office a list of the names, telephone numbers, addresses and license numbers of the people working in the massage business who hold a then current massage license issued by the State of Georgia, and shall provide a list of the names, telephone numbers and addresses of all other employees working in the massage business.
(Ord. No. 17-06 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-6-2017)
Sec. 22-73. - Hours of operation.
The licensed owner or operator of the massage business shall provide in writing to the licensing and permits office the hours of operation of the business. No licensee under this article shall operate a massage business except between the hours of 6:00 a.m. and 11:00 p.m.
(Ord. No. 17-06 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-6-2017)
Sec. 22-74. - Inspection required.
The Columbia County Sheriff's Office, and authorized agents or employees designated by Columbia County shall be authorized from time to time to make inspections during regular business hours of each massage business for the purpose of determining that state law and regulations, including but not limited to Georgia Rules, Chapter 345, Georgia Board of Massage Therapy, 345-6, Professional and Unprofessional Conduct, and the provisions of this article, are fully complied with. It shall be unlawful for any licensee under this article to fail to allow such county officers and officials access to the premises or to hinder the officer or official in any manner.
(Ord. No. 17-06 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-6-2017)
Sec. 22-75. - Duty to actively supervise and monitor.
It shall be the duty of the licensee, or that officer signing on behalf of an entity licensee, to actively supervise and monitor the conduct of any and all employees, independent contractors, customers, and all other persons on the premises in order to assure compliance with the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 17-06 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-6-2017)
Sec. 22-76. - Exemptions.
This article shall not apply to the following individuals while engaged in the personal performance of the duties required of their respective professions:
(1)
A state licensed physician, osteopath, physical therapist, occupational therapist, chiropodist, podiatrist chiropractor, or licensed assistant of such professionals, who performs massage services only in the normal course of performing medical duties;
(2)
A registered nurse or licensed practical nurse licensed in the state, who performs massage services only in the normal course of performing nursing duties;
(3)
Any athletic trainer working as such within the athletic department of any accredited school, college, university or seminary, or in connection with any professional athletic team or competition;
(4)
Hospitals, medical clinics, nursing and convalescent homes and other similar facilities licensed under the laws of the state, and dedicated to medical or nursing practices where massage services may be given, and the employees and/or independent contractors of such facilities who perform the massage services for the patients at such facilities in the normal course of carrying out their duties at such facility; and
(5)
Any person otherwise exempt by state law.
(Ord. No. 17-06 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-6-2017)
DIVISION 2. - BUSINESS OPERATING LICENSE

Sec. 22-77. - License required.
Any person or entity desiring to engage in the business of offering or providing massage services, in the unincorporated areas of the county, before doing so, shall apply for and obtain an operator's license.
(Ord. No. 17-06 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-6-2017)
Sec. 22-78. - Qualifications for operator's license.
In order to obtain an operator's license for a massage business under this article, the applicant must satisfy the following requirements:
(1)
Either the applicant, if a natural person, or the officer with authority to bind the applicant who signs the application, if an entity applicant, must be a state licensed massage therapist and must reside within 50 miles of the premise location of the massage business.
(2)
Citizenship; residency. A license will be issued only to a person who is a citizen of the United States or an alien lawfully admitted for permanent residence in the United States. Where an applicant is other than a natural person, it must be an entity organized and existing under the laws of the United States or one of its states, an agent of the entity must meet the qualifications set forth herein.
a.
Where the applicant for a license is an entity other than a natural person, the application shall be made jointly in the name of the entity and an officer having authority to bind the entity. Said entity shall be registered to do business in the State of Georgia.
b.
Licenses issued to entities must be issued jointly to the entity and all of the other persons required by this section to join in the application. All respective partners, officers, directors, or members, as applicable, shall be responsible for the actions of the named licensee and the conduct of the licensed business.
(3)
The following individuals are not eligible for an operator's license, even if they possess all of the qualifications listed herein.
a.
Any person convicted in the last ten years of any offenses involving the use of or threat of force or violence upon the person of another; any offenses involving narcotics, dangerous drugs, or dangerous weapons; any felony involving moral turpitude; any offenses involving sexual behavior, whether felony or misdemeanor, as set out in O.C.G.A. Title 16, Crimes and Offenses, Chapter 6, Sexual Offenses. For the purpose of this subsection, a conviction shall include a finding or verdict of guilty, plea of guilty, or a plea of nolo contendere in a criminal proceeding regardless of whether the adjudication of guilt or sentence is withheld or not entered thereon pursuant to provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 42-8-60 through 42-8-64, relating to first offenders, or any comparable rule or statute.
b.
Any person who has been found to employ any person who is not a licensed massage practitioner under state law and allowing or permitting such person to administer massage in the establishment except as may be allowed by state law.
c.
Any person who allows the use of an individual's state issued massage license by an unlicensed person.
d.
Any person who has knowingly provided false or forged information to the board of commissioners in obtaining a license under this article.
e.
Any person who has used or attempted to use an operator's license that has expired or been revoked.
f.
Any person who violates Georgia Rules, Chapter 345, Georgia Board of Massage Therapy, 345-6, Professional and Unprofessional Conduct on the premises.
g.
Any person who violates a provision of this article.
(4)
Process, notice or demand required or permitted by law or under this article may be served. The registered agent must be a resident of the county. If the licensee is not a natural person, the licensee will be required to appoint and maintain a registered agent in the county if it does not have an agent in the county upon whom process can legally be served on such entity under the laws of the state. The licensee shall file the name of such registered agent, along with such registered agent's written consent to serve as such agent, with the office of development services.
(5)
An application for an operator's license may be denied where it reasonably appears to the planning commission that the applicant is intended or likely to be a surrogate for a person who would not otherwise qualify for an operator's license.
(6)
An application for an operator's license may be denied where it reasonably appears to the planning commission that the premises are being used for an illegal purpose. The circumstances which may be considered in determining an illegal purpose as a basis of denial under this subsection include but are not limited to:
a.
Frequency of citizen complaints related to the premises;
b.
Frequency of calls for police services at the premises; and
c.
Prior warnings, investigations or arrest related to the premises.
(Ord. No. 17-06 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-6-2017)
Sec. 22-79. - Permitted licenses per location/home occupations.
A massage business is not a permitted home occupation, except that mobile massage businesses may use a residentially zoned property solely for administrative purposes.
(Ord. No. 17-06 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-6-2017)
Sec. 22-80. - Application for operator's license.
(a)
Forms. A person or entity desiring to obtain an operator's license shall make application to the licensing and permits office on the forms prescribed by the development services division.
(b)
Operator's license. The application for an operator's license for a massage business shall require, but shall not be limited to, the following information:
(1)
The name and address of each applicant qualified under the requirements of section 22-78.
(2)
The name and location of the proposed business to be carried on, and the name and address of the owner of the premises in which the business is to be carried on. If the business is to be operated on an outcall basis, then the address will be the address and premises where the office of the massage business is located.
(3)
Each applicant qualified under the requirements of section 22-78 shall submit to a background check pursuant to Section 22-90 of this chapter.
(c)
Incomplete applications; swearing to before notary public. Failure to pay the license, administrative and investigative fees at the time of filing the license application, or failure to furnish all information as required by an application form, will cause the application to be deemed to be incomplete and no action will be taken thereon until such application is complete. All applications shall be sworn to by the applicant before a notary public or other officer empowered by law to administer oaths.
(Ord. No. 17-06 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-6-2017)
Sec. 22-81. - Approval procedures.
An application for a license under this article shall be presented to the planning commission within 45 days after the date such application is deemed complete. The licensing and permits office shall notify the applicant in writing of the date on which the application will be presented to the planning commission. At the time the application is presented to the planning commission, the applicant may appear and present any matters in support of the application, and any other person may appear and present matters in opposition to the application. If the application presentation is made to the planning commission, upon the completion of the presentation, the planning commission shall take action denying or granting the application or deferring action for a period not to exceed 45 days in order to obtain further information. If the application is denied, the planning commission shall set forth the reasons for such denial.
In all instances in which an application for an operator's license is denied under the provisions of this article, the applicant may not reapply for an operator's license for at least one year from the date of such denial.
(Ord. No. 17-06 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-6-2017)
Sec. 22-82. - Revocation, suspension of operator's license.
Any license granted under this article shall be subject to revocation or suspension for cause. Whenever, in the opinion of development services director, there is cause to revoke or suspend an operator's license, a written notice of intention to revoke or suspend such license shall be furnished to the holder thereof not less than ten days before the meeting of the planning commission, at which time the holder of the license may make such showing as he may deem proper. After a hearing, the planning commission may revoke or suspend the license if, in its discretion, it is in the best interest of peace and good order of the county, or there has been any violation of the ordinances of the county or of the laws of the United States or the state in the conduct of the massage business or the performing of massage services to members of the public. Cause for revocation or suspension of an operator's license under this article shall include, but not be limited to, the following actions by the license holder:
(1)
Conviction in the last ten years of any offenses involving the use of or threat of force or violence upon the person of another; any offenses involving narcotics, dangerous drugs, or dangerous weapons; any felony involving moral turpitude; any offenses involving sexual behavior, whether felony or misdemeanor, as set out in O.C.G.A. Title 16, Crimes and Offenses, Chapter 6, Sexual Offenses. For the purpose of this subsection, a conviction shall include a finding or verdict of guilty, plea of guilty, or a plea of nolo contendere in a criminal proceeding regardless of whether the adjudication of guilt or sentence is withheld or not entered thereon pursuant to provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 42-8-60 through 42-8-64, relating to first offenders, or any comparable rule or statute.
(2)
Employing any person who is not a licensed massage practitioner under state law and allowing or permitting such person to administer massage in the establishment except as may be allowed by state law.
(3)
Allowing the use of an individual's state issued massage license by an unlicensed person.
(4)
Providing false or forged information to the board of commissioners in obtaining a license under this article.
(5)
Using or attempting to use an operator's license that has expired or been revoked.
(6)
Use of the premises for illegal activity.
(7)
A violation of Georgia Rules, Chapter 345, Georgia Board of Massage Therapy, 345-6, Professional and Unprofessional Conduct on the premises.
(8)
Violation of any provision of this article.
(Ord. No. 17-06 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-6-2017)
Sec. 22-83. - Automatic license forfeiture for nonuse.
Any licensee holding an operator's license under this article, who, except for medical cause, shall, for a period of three consecutive months, cease to operate the massage business as authorized in the operator's license, shall automatically forfeit the license without the necessity of any further action. If the massage business operation is interrupted as a result of damage to the premises where the massage business is located which makes it impractical to operate the massage business, and if repairs of such damage are commenced within such three-month period and diligently pursued to completion, or as a result of the premises being remodeled, then this section shall not apply to such cessation of operation. If a license is issued for the operation of a massage business in a building which has not yet been completed, the license will not be forfeited unless the licensee fails to begin operations of the massage business within a period of 180 days from the date of issuance of the license.
(Ord. No. 17-06 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-6-2017)
Sec. 22-84. - Expiration; renewal of license.
Each license granted under this article shall expire on December 31 of the calendar year in which the license was issued. A licensee who desires to renew his license shall file an application with the licensing and permits office, on the form provided for license renewal. Applications for renewal must be filed on or before October 31 of the year in which the license being renewed expires. If a renewal application is received after October 31, the county cannot assure the applicant that a renewal license will be issued by January 1 of the following calendar year, and the licensee will have to cease operations from such January 1 until the renewal license is issued. If a license renewal application is received after the December 31 expiration date, such application shall be treated as an application for a new license and the applicant shall be required to comply with all rules, regulations and fees for the granting of licenses as if no previous license had been issued.
Any person applying for renewal of a license must pay the license, administrative and investigative fees at the time of filing the application. Failure to pay such fees at that time shall result in the application for renewal being treated as not filed or as being withdrawn.
Any person applying for a renewal license who meets the qualifications of section 22-78 shall be issued a renewed operator's license.
(Ord. No. 17-06 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-6-2017)
Sec. 22-85. - Separate application and operator's license for each business location.
Each operator's license under this article shall be limited to the operation of a massage business at a specific location or out of which specific location an mobile massage business is operated as described in the license. A separate application must be made and separate operator's licenses must be obtained for each location at which a massage business is conducted or out of which an outcall massage business is operated. Notwithstanding any provision to the contrary, the licensing and permits office shall review and approve or deny applications for separate operator's licenses at an existing facility holding a then current operator's license.
(Ord. No. 17-06 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-6-2017)
Sec. 22-86. - Display of license.
An operator's license shall, at all times, be kept plainly exposed to public view at the premises for which the license was granted. The license shall include a color photo of the natural person applicant.
The state massage license shall, at all times, be kept plainly exposed to public view at the place where the holder of the massage license is performing massage services.
(Ord. No. 17-06 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-6-2017)
Sec. 22-87. - Fees.
Upon making application for a license under this article, the applicant shall pay license, administrative and investigative fees for such license as set forth in the schedule of fees and charges on file in the office of the county clerk, and such other fees as may be established from time to time by the board of commissioners, by resolution, adopted in open meeting and kept on file in the licensing and permits office. The initial schedule of fees shall be adopted contemporaneously with the adoption of the ordinance from which this article is derived. Such schedule of fees shall remain in effect until amended by the board of commissioners. All applicants shall be furnished a copy of the schedule of fees at the time of application for a license under this article.
All operator's licenses granted under this article shall be for a calendar year or the balance thereof and the full license fee shall be paid for a license if the license is issued on or after January 1 but prior to April 1 of such year. Three-fourths of the license fee shall be paid if the license is issued on or after April 1 but prior to July 1, one-half of the license fee shall be paid if the license is issued on or after July 1 but prior to October 1, and one-fourth of the license fee shall be paid if the license is issued on or after October 1 of the calendar year for which the license is issued. The proration of license fees shall have no effect upon the administrative and investigative fees which shall be paid in full in accordance with the provisions of article IV.
Each application for an operator's license for a massage business under this article shall be accompanied by the full amount of the license fee, together with the investigative and administrative fees applicable to such license. If the applicant is denied a license, the license fee shall be refunded, but the investigative and administrative fees shall be retained and are nonrefundable.
(Ord. No. 17-06 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-6-2017)
Sec. 22-88. - Transferability.
No license under this article shall be transferable.
(Ord. No. 17-06 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-6-2017)
Sec. 22-89. - Violation; penalties.
(a)
Any violation of the provisions of this article shall constitute a misdemeanor and shall be punishable by penalties and costs as set forth in section 1-9.
(b)
In addition to the penalties set forth in subsection (a) of this section, the board of commissioners may revoke or suspend an operator's license for violation of this article as set forth in section 22-77, which shall be a distinct and separate penalty. Any violation of this article, or any failure to comply with the requirements of this article, shall be grounds for immediate suspension or revocation of any and all licenses held by the licensee regardless of the number of licenses or locations involved, and regardless of whether or not the licensee knew of or contributed to such violation. It is the intent of this subsection, without limiting the generality thereof, to grant to the board of commissioners the right to suspend or revoke any and all licenses held by a licensee when any violation of this article occurs at any place of business operated by the licensee pursuant to a license.
(Ord. No. 17-06 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-6-2017)
Sec. 22-90. - [Massage license application.]
(a)
This section is enacted pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 43-24A-22 to regulate the business of massage services.
(b)
An applicant seeking to apply for a massage license shall submit his/her fingerprints taken by the Columbia County Sheriff's Department, along with appropriate fees.
(c)
Upon receipt of the fingerprints and the appropriate fees, the Columbia County Sheriff's Office will transmit the fingerprints and appropriate fees to the Georgia Bureau of Investigation. The Georgia Bureau of Investigation will compare the subject's fingerprints against its criminal file and, (1) if no disqualifying conduct is found therein (or) (2) if necessary, submit the fingerprints to the Federal Bureau of Investigation for a comparison with nationwide records. The results of the Federal Bureau of Investigation check will be returned to the Georgia Bureau of Investigation, which will disseminate the state and national results to Columbia County Sheriff's Office who will provide the results to the development services division.
(d)
The development services division shall render a fitness determination based upon the results of the criminal background check and communicate its fitness determination to such individual. In rendering such fitness determination, the development services division will decide whether the individual has been convicted of, or is under pending indictment for:
(1)
Any felony or a misdemeanor involving force or threat of force;
(2)
Any felony or a misdemeanor involving controlled substances;
(3)
Any sex-related felony or a misdemeanor;
(4)
Any felony or a misdemeanor involving a moral turpitude; or
(5)
Any other crime bearing upon the individual's fitness and ability as determined by the development services division.
(e)
Should the individual seek to amend or correct his or her record, he or she must contact the Georgia Bureau of Investigation for a Georgia state record or the Federal Bureau of Investigation for records from other jurisdictions maintained in its file.
(Ord. No. 17-06 , § 1(Exh. A), 6-6-2017)
