ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 46-1. - Enforcement of chapter provisions; penalties.
(a)
Any person who violates any provision of this chapter, or fails to comply with the requirements of this chapter shall, upon conviction, be subject to penalties as set forth in section 1-9. Each day such violation continues shall be considered a separate offense. The owner or tenant of any building, structure, premises or part thereof, and any architect, builder, contractor, agent or other person who commits, participates in, assists in or maintains such violation may each be found guilty of a separate offense and suffer the penalties set forth in this subsection.
(b)
In case any building or structure is or is proposed to be erected, constructed, reconstructed, altered, converted or maintained, or any building, structure or other premise is or is proposed to be used, or in any other manner any person fails to follow the provisions of this chapter or is in violation of this chapter, the county department head who is charged with the enforcement of any provision of this chapter, the county attorney or other appropriate authority of the county or any adjacent or neighboring property owner who would be especially damaged by such violation, in addition to the other remedies provided in this section, may institute injunction, mandamus or other appropriate action or proceeding to prevent such unlawful erection, construction, reconstruction, alteration, conversion, maintenance or use, or to correct or abate such violation, or to prevent the occupancy of any such building, structure or premises.
(c)
Each county department head who is charged with the enforcement of any provision of this chapter, or his authorized representative, is designated as an agent of the county for the purpose of issuing citations to any person violating any provision of this chapter.
(Code 1979, § 2-7-1; Ord. No. 00-10, § 7(a), 10-3-2000)
Sec. 46-2 - Fees.
For the services, licenses and permits provided by the board of health as described in this chapter, the board of health shall charge fees as established from time to time by the board of health with the approval of and adoption by the Board of Commissioners of Columbia County, Georgia. Fees may be set by resolutions from time to time adopted by the board of health modifying existing fees and/or establishing new fees and modifying, deleting or creating services, provided by and licenses and permits issued by the board of health. Such resolutions shall not become effective until the provisions thereof have been approved and adopted by a resolution of the Board of Commissioners of Columbia County, Georgia which must have two readings in open meetings duly held, at which a quorum is present and acting throughout. The fees as established and/or modified hereunder from time to time shall be kept as part of the "Schedule of Fees and Charges" maintained by the Columbia County Clerk's Office.
(Ord. No. 10-07, § 1, 12-14-2010)
Secs. 46-3—46-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - FOOD SERVICE AND ESTABLISHMENTS[2]

Footnotes:
--- (2) ---
Cross reference— Businesses and business regulations, ch. 22.

Sec. 46-31. - Operation and maintenance of food service establishments.
The operation and maintenance of food service establishments shall be according to the rules and regulations of the department of human resources public health, "Food Service," Chapter 290-5-14, as adopted by the state department of human resources, filed March 5, 1996, effective March 25, 1996, as amended by the department of human resources, such rules and regulations being incorporated into and made a part of this Code, subject to the enforcement provisions in this article. A "food service establishment," as defined by such rules and regulations, means any establishment for the preparation and serving of meals, lunches, short orders, sandwiches, frozen desserts or other edible products. The term includes, restaurants; coffee shops; cafeterias; short order cafes; luncheonettes; taverns; lunchrooms; places which manufacture, wholesale or retail sandwiches or salads; soda fountains; institutions, both public and private; food carts; itinerant restaurants; industrial cafeterias; catering establishments; food vending machines and vehicles and operations connected with such machines; and similar facilities. The term "food service establishment" shall not mean a food sales establishment, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 26-2-21, which does not provide seating or facilities for consumption of food on the premises.
(Code 1979, § 2-7-130)
Sec. 46-32. - Enforcement.
(a)
The county board of health or its authorized agent shall be authorized to enforce this article, and to enter upon and inspect the premises of any food service establishment as provided in O.C.G.A. art. 2, ch. 5, tit. 31.
(b)
Each and every food service establishment subject to inspection under the provisions of this section shall be required to pay to the county board of health an annual inspection fee as determined by the board of health and approved by the board of commissioners, which fee shall be due within 30 days of receipt of notification from the board of health. Establishments failing to pay such fee shall be considered in violation of the provisions of this article.
(c)
The county board of health or its authorized agent shall be authorized to issue citations for violations of the provisions of this article or of O.C.G.A. art. 13, ch. 2, tit. 26.
(Code 1979, § 2-7-131)
Sec. 46-33. - Violation; penalties.
Any person who shall violate the provisions of this article shall, upon conviction, be subject to penalties and costs as set forth in section 1-9.
(Code 1979, § 2-7-132; Ord. No. 00-10, § 7(d), 10-3-2000)
Secs. 46-34—46-65. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - TOURIST COURTS AND ACCOMMODATIONS

Sec. 46-66. - Operation and maintenance.
The operation and maintenance of tourist courts and tourist accommodations shall be according to the rules and regulations of the department of human resources, "Tourist Accommodations," Chapter 290-5-18, as adopted by the state department of human resources, filed March 5, 1996, effective March 25, 1996, and as amended by the department of human resources, such rules and regulations being incorporated into and made a part of this Code, subject to the enforcement provisions in this article. The term "tourist accommodation" means a tourist court as defined in O.C.G.A. § 31-28-1 as any facility consisting of two or more rooms or dwelling units providing lodging and other accommodations for tourists and travelers, and includes tourist courts, tourist cottages, tourist homes, trailer parks, trailer courts, motels, motor hotels, hotels and any similar place, and any food, beverage, laundry, recreational or other facilities or establishments operated in conjunction with such facilities. The term includes any facility consisting of two or more rooms or dwelling units, either joined together or separate, on a common piece of property, furnished for pay, and further includes campgrounds, recreational vehicle parks, and bed and breakfast inns.
(Code 1979, § 2-7-140)
Sec. 46-67. - Enforcement.
(a)
The county board of health or its authorized agent shall be authorized to enforce this article, and to enter upon and inspect the premises of any tourist court or tourist accommodation establishment.
(b)
Each and every tourist court or tourist accommodation establishment subject to inspection under the provisions of this article shall be required to pay to the county board of health an annual inspection fee as determined by the board of health and approved by the board of commissioners, due within 30 days of receipt of notification from the board of health. Establishments failing to pay such fee shall be considered in violation of the provisions of this article.
(c)
The county board of health or its authorized agent shall be authorized to issue citations for violations of the provisions of this article.
(Code 1979, § 2-7-141)
Sec. 46-68. - Violation; penalties.
Any person who shall violate any provision of this article shall, upon conviction, be subject to penalties as set forth in section 1-9.
(Code 1979, § 2-7-142; Ord. No. 00-10, § 7(e), 10-3-2000)

ARTICLE IV. - REGULATION OF SMOKING[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
Editor's note— Ord. No. 04-08, § 1, adopted Sept. 21, 2004, supplied provisions for Art. IV, §§ 46-69—46-81. In order to preserve the style of the Code, at the discretion of the editor, these provisions have been included as Art. IV, §§ 46-81—46-94, to read as set out herein.

Sec. 46-81. - Definitions.
The following words and phrases, whenever used in this article, shall be construed as defined in this section:
Attached bar means a bar area of a restaurant.
Business means a sole proprietorship, partnership, joint venture, corporation, or other business entity formed for profit-making purposes, or as a nonprofit entity for the manufacture, distribution, sale, delivery or provision of any type of goods or services.
Employee means a person who is employed by an employer in consideration for direct or indirect monetary wages or profit, and a person who volunteers his or her services for a non-profit entity.
Employer means a person, business, partnership, association, corporation, including a municipal corporation, trust or non-profit entity that employs the services of one or more individual persons.
Enclosed area means all space between a floor and ceiling that is enclosed on all sides by solid walls or windows (exclusive of doorways), which extend from the floor to the ceiling.
Health care facility means an office or institution providing care or treatment of diseases, whether physical, mental, or emotional, or other medical, physiological, or psychological conditions, including but not limited to, hospitals, rehabilitation hospitals or other clinics, including weight control clinics, nursing homes, homes for the aging or chronically ill, laboratories, and offices of surgeons, chiropractors, physical therapists, physicians, dentists, and all specialists within these professions. Including all waiting rooms, hallways, private rooms, semiprivate rooms, and wards within health care facilities.
Place of employment means an enclosed area under the control of a public or private employer that employees normally frequent during the course of employment, including but not limited to, work areas, employee lounges, restrooms, conference rooms, meeting rooms, classrooms, employee cafeterias, hallways, and vehicles. A private residence is not a "place of employment" unless it is used as a child care, adult day care, or health care facility.
Public place means an enclosed area to which the public is invited or in which the public is permitted, including but not limited to, banks, educational facilities, health care facilities, hotels and motels, laundromats, poling places, public transportation facilities, reception areas, restaurants, retail food production and marketing establishments, retail service establishments, retail stores, shopping malls, theaters, and waiting rooms. A private residence is not a "public place" unless it is used as a childcare, adult day care, or health care facility.
Restaurant means an eating establishment, including but not limited to, coffee shops, cafeterias, sandwich stands, and private and public school cafeterias, which gives or offers for sale food to the public, guests or employees, as well as kitchen and catering facilities in which food is prepared on the premises for serving elsewhere. The term "restaurant" shall include an attached bar.
Retail tobacco store means a retail store which derives not less than 85 percent of its gross revenues from the sale of tobacco products and accessories and in which the sale of other products is merely incidental.
Service line means an indoor line in which one or more persons are waiting for or receiving service of any kind, whether or not the service involves the exchange of money.
Shopping mall means an enclosed public walkway or hall area that serves to connect retail or professional establishments.
Smoking means inhaling, exhaling, burning, or carrying any lighted cigar, cigarette, pipe, weed, plant, or other combustible substance in any manner or in any form.
Sports arena means sports pavilions, stadiums, gymnasiums, health spas, boxing arenas, swimming pools, roller and ice rinks, bowling alleys, and other buildings, structures or places where members of the general public assemble to engage in physical exercise, participate in athletic competition, or witness sports or other events.
(Ord. No. 04-08, § 1(46-69), 9-21-2004)
Sec. 46-82. - Public areas where smoking is prohibited.
Smoking is prohibited in all public places and sports arenas within the unincorporated areas of Columbia County, including but not limited to, the following places:
(1)
Aquariums, galleries, libraries, and museums.
(2)
Areas available to and customarily used by the general public in the facilities of any business entity patronized by the public, including but not limited to reception areas, waiting rooms, rest rooms, hallways, lounges and display areas.
(3)
Attached bars.
(4)
Banks.
(5)
Bingo facilities.
(6)
Buildings and vehicles owned, leased, or operated by Columbia County.
(7)
Educational facilities.
(8)
Convention facilities.
(9)
Elevators.
(10)
Facilities primarily used as theaters for exhibiting a motion picture, stage, drama, dance, lecture, musical recital, or other entertainment performances.
(11)
Health care facilities.
(12)
Hotels and motels except as otherwise provided in section 46-85(b).
(13)
Laundromats.
(14)
Licensed childcare and adult day care facilities.
(15)
Lobbies, hallways, clubhouses, recreation rooms, meeting rooms and other common areas in apartment buildings and complexes, condominiums, trailer parks, retirement facilities, nursing homes, and other multiple-unit residential facilities.
(16)
Polling places.
(17)
Public transportation facilities, including busses and taxicabs, operating under the authority of Columbia County, and ticket, boarding and waiting areas of public transit depots.
(18)
Restaurants, including attached bars.
(19)
Retail stores, retail service establishments and retail food production and marketing establishments.
(20)
Rooms, chambers, places of meeting or public assembly, including school buildings, under the control of an agency, board, commission, committee or council of Columbia County or a political subdivision of the State of Georgia when a public meeting is in progress, to the extent the place is subject to the jurisdiction of Columbia County.
(21)
Service lines.
(22)
Shopping malls.
(Ord. No. 04-08, § 1(46-70), 9-21-2004)
Sec. 46-83. - Prohibition of smoking in places of employment.
(a)
Smoking shall be prohibited in all enclosed facilities within places of employment without exception including common work areas, auditoriums, classrooms, conference and meeting rooms, private offices, elevators, hallways, medical facilities, cafeterias, employee lounges, stairs, restrooms, vehicles and all other enclosed facilities.
(b)
The prohibition on smoking shall be communicated to all present employees by the effective date of this article (January 1, 2005) and to all subsequent employees upon their application for employment.
(Ord. No. 04-08, § 1(46-71), 9-21-2004)
Sec. 46-84. - Additional areas where smoking is prohibited.
Smoking is prohibited within a distance of ten feet from any door which is used as an entrance to or exit from an enclosed area where smoking is prohibited. This ten feet distance shall be measured from the center of the door in question. This distance requirement shall not apply to any door which has a properly operating vertical air curtain.
(Ord. No. 04-08, § 1(46-72), 9-21-2004)
Sec. 46-85. - Smoking not prohibited.
Notwithstanding the provisions of sections 46-82 through 46-84, smoking is not prohibited in the following areas:
(1)
Private residences, except when used as a licensed child care, adult day care, barbershop, beauty shop, personal care home or other health care facility.
(2)
Hotel and motel rooms rented to guests and designated as smoking rooms; provided, however, that not more than 20 percent of guest rooms in a hotel or motel may be so designated.
(3)
Retail tobacco stores provided that smoke from such stores does not infiltrate areas where smoking is prohibited under the provisions of this article.
(4)
Outdoor areas of places of employment except those covered by the provisions of this section.
(5)
Outdoor dining areas which may be covered by a roof but must be open on at least three sides.
(Ord. No. 04-08, § 1(46-73), 9-21-2004)
Sec. 46-86. - Establishment of additional restricted areas.
Notwithstanding any other provision in this article, an owner, operator, manager, or other person in control of an establishment, facility or outdoor area may declare the entire establishment, facility or outdoor area as a nonsmoking location. Smoking shall then be prohibited in any place in which a sign conforming to the requirements of section 46-87 is posted.
(Ord. No. 04-08, § 1(46-74), 9-21-2004)
Sec. 46-87. - Posting of signs.
(a)
"No Smoking" signs or the international "No Smoking" symbol (consisting of a pictorial representation of a burning cigarette enclosed in a red circle with a red bar across it) shall be clearly and conspicuously posted in every public place and place of employment where smoking is prohibited by this article, by the owner, operator, manager, or other person in control of that place.
(b)
Every public place and place of employment where smoking is prohibited by this article shall have posted at every entrance a conspicuous sign clearly stating that smoking is prohibited.
(c)
All ashtrays and other smoking paraphernalia shall be removed from any area where smoking is prohibited by this article by owner, operator, manger or other person having control of the area.
(Ord. No. 04-08, § 1(46-75), 9-21-2004)
Sec. 46-88. - Nursing homes, long-term care facilities and assisted living facilities.
Nursing homes, long term care facilities and assisted living facilities shall each adopt smoke free policies and enforce them subject to any restrictions or limitations thereon contained in the rules and regulations promulgated by the Georgia Office of Nursing Home Regulatory Services where applicable.
(Ord. No. 04-08, § 1(46-76), 9-21-2004)
Sec. 46-89. - Nonretaliation.
No person or employer shall discharge, refuse to hire, or in any manner retaliate against an employee, applicant for employment or customer because that employee, applicant or customer exercises any rights afforded by this article or reports or attempts to prosecute a violation of this article.
(Ord. No. 04-08, § 1(46-77), 9-21-2004)
Sec. 46-90. - Exemptions.
(a)
Any nonprofit organization under the laws of the state of its organization which meets the criteria set forth in either (a) or (b) below, may apply to the board of commissioners for an exemption from this article:
(1)
A private club which:
a.
Has at least 75 regular dues-paying members who pay dues of at least $50.00 per year;
b.
The membership in the club is limited in number and by specified membership criteria;
c.
Is not open to the general public, but only to its members and their guests;
d.
Substantially all of its activities are for the pleasure or recreation or other nonprofitable purposes of its members and it does not receive more than 35 percent of its gross revenues from sources outside of its membership; and
e.
No part of its net earnings inures to the benefit of any for profit private entity or individual.
(2)
A post or organization of past or present members of the Armed Forces of the United States;
a.
At least 75 percent of the members of which are past or present members of the Armed Forces of the United States and substantially all of the other members are spouses, widows or widowers of past or present members of the Armed Forces of the United Sates; and
b.
No part of the net earnings of which inures to the benefit of any for profit private entity or individual.
(b)
Any organization seeking to avail itself of this exemption shall make exception for this exemption to the board of commissioners on such forms and following such procedures as may be provided for by the board of commissioners. The board of commissioners shall then make a determination as to whether the applicant meets the criteria for this exemption and on the basis of such determination grant or deny the exemption to the applicant. Any exemption granted by the board of commissioners may be revoked by the board of commissioners at any time that it determines that the requirements for the exemption are no longer being met.
(c)
Any exemption granted to an entity shall not apply during any time that the facilities operated by the entity are open to the public or are used by a non-member for meetings, gatherings, social events and the like. In such circumstances, the provisions of this article must be complied with.
(Ord. No. 04-08, § 1(46-78), 9-21-2004)
Sec. 46-91. - Enforcement.
(a)
This article shall be enforced by any law enforcement officer or code enforcement officer of Columbia County or an authorized designee.
(b)
Notice of the provisions of this article shall be given to each person or entity paying employment taxes in Columbia County.
(c)
Any citizen who desires to register a complaint under this article may initiate enforcement with any police officer, fire marshal, code enforcement officer, building inspector or building official of Columbia County.
(d)
The health department, fire marshal or other designees shall, while an establishment is undergoing otherwise mandated inspections, inspect for compliance with this article.
(e)
An owner, manager, operator, or employee of an establishment regulated by this article shall inform persons violating this article of the appropriate provisions hereof.
(f)
In addition to the remedies provided by the provisions of this section, Columbia County may apply for injunctive relief to enforce the provisions of this article in any court of competent jurisdiction.
(Ord. No. 04-08, § 1(46-79), 9-21-2004)
Sec. 46-92. - Violations and penalties.
(a)
Any person who violates any provision of this article, shall, upon conviction, be subject to the penalties as set forth in section 1-9 of this Code.
(b)
In addition to the penalties established by this section, a violation of this article by a person who owns, manages, operators or otherwise controls a public place or place of employment may result in the suspension or revocation of any permit or license issued to the person for the premises on which the violation occurred.
(Ord. No. 04-08, § 1(46-79), 9-21-2004)
Sec. 46-93. - Public education.
The Columbia County Health Department shall engage in a continuing program to explain and clarify the purposes and requirements of this article and to assist owners, operators, and managers in their compliance with it.
(Ord. No. 04-08, § 1(46-80), 9-21-2004)
Sec. 46-94. - Government agency cooperation.
The Columbia County Health Department shall annually request other governmental and educational agencies having facilities within Columbia County to establish local operating procedures in cooperation and compliance with this article.
(Ord. No. 04-08, § 1(46-81), 9-21-2004)
Secs. 46-95—46-99. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - BODY ART[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
Editor's note— Ord. No. 11-12, adopted March 15, 2011, set out provisions intended for use as §§ 46-90—46-123. At the editor's discretion, to prevent duplication of section numbers, these provisions have been included as §§ 46-100—46-133.

Sec. 46-100. - Authority.
The legal authority for this article is Section 16-5-71 and 16-5-71.1, Section 31-3-4, Section 31-3-5, Title 31 Chapter 40, and Section 48-13-9 all in the Official Code of Georgia Annotated.
(Ord. No. 11-12, 3-15-2011)
Sec. 46-101. - Purpose.
The purpose of this article is to establish reasonable standards for individuals providing body art services and for the body art establishments within which the body art services are provided.
(Ord. No. 11-12, 3-15-2011)
Sec. 46-102. - Fees.
Body art establishments and body artists subject to inspection under the provisions of this article shall be required to pay to the board of health an annual inspection fee as determined by the board of health, and approved by the county and the district health director. This fee is due within 30 days of receipt of notification from the board of health. Fees must also be paid to the board of health for the annual renewal of body artist licenses and body art establishment permits. Failure to pay such fees shall be considered in violation of the provisions of this article.
(Ord. No. 11-12, 3-15-2011)
Sec. 46-103. - Exemptions.
Anything herein to the contrary notwithstanding, this article does do not apply to a physician or osteopath licensed under O.C.G.A. tit. 43, ch. 34, or a healthcare provider, including but not limited to physician assistants, nurse practitioners, registered nurses, licensed practice nurses and medical technicians acting under the direct supervision of such licensed physician or osteopath.
(Ord. No. 11-12, 3-15-2011)
Sec. 46-104. - Definitions.
The following terms as used in this article shall have the meanings as set forth below:
Antimicrobial solution means any solution used to retard the growth of microorganisms for application to human skin and labeled accordingly and has been approved by the FDA.
Antiseptic means an agent or substance that will destroy or inhibit the growth and development of infectious microorganisms on human skin or mucous membranes which has been approved by the FDA.
Applicant means any person who applies to the board of health for a body art license, guest body art license, body art establishment permit, or temporary body art establishment permit.
Aseptic technique means to render or maintain free from infectious material so as to prevent transfer or transmission of infectious agents.
ASTM means the American Society for Testing Materials International.
Autoclave means an apparatus for sterilization utilizing steam pressure at a specific temperature over a period of time per manufacturer's specifications.
Biomedical waste means liquid or semi-liquid blood or other potentially infectious materials, sharps or other contaminated items, which if compressed, would release blood or other potentially infectious materials in a liquid or semi-liquid state and as further defined in Rule 391-3-4-.15 of the Georgia Department of Natural Resources—Environmental Protection Division.
Blood means human blood, human blood components, and products made from human blood.
Bloodborne pathogens means pathogenic microorganisms present in human blood that can cause disease in humans. These pathogens include but are not limited to Hepatitis B virus (HBV), Hepatitis C virus (HCV), and Human Immunodeficiency virus (HIV).
Board of health means the Board of Health of Columbia County.
Body art, body art services or body art procedures means the practice of physical body adornment by means of tattooing, body piercing, or permanent cosmetics. This definition does not include practices considered medical procedures by the Georgia Board of Registration in Medicine.
Body art establishment, body art studio, tattoo establishment, tattoo parlor or tattoo studio means any permanent building or structure on a permanent foundation in which all or any of these body art services are performed.
Body art establishment permit means a written permit issued by the board of health to a body art establishment stating that such establishment, after inspection, was found to be in compliance with this article.
Body artist or tattoo artist means a person who performs tattooing, body piercing or permanent cosmetics, and who is responsible for complying with applicable provisions of this article.
Body art license shall mean a license issued by the board of health to a specifically identified person authorizing such person to engage in the practice of body art in accordance with this article for a stated period of time.
Body art regulation shall mean the rules, this article and any regulations and guidelines promulgated by the board of health pursuant hereto or to O.C.G.A. § 31-40-5 as amended from time to time.
Body piercing means puncturing or penetrating the skin or mucosa of a client for the purpose of inserting jewelry or other adornment into the body for non-medical purposes. Body piercing does not include ear piercing, which is performed on the ear lobe with an ear piercing gun using sterile materials.
Cleaning room shall mean the area in a body art establishment used in the sterilization, sanitation or other cleaning of instruments or other equipment used for the practice of body art.
Client or customer shall mean an individual upon whom any body art services are performed.
Contaminated means the presence or the reasonably anticipated presence of blood, other potentially infectious materials, or potentially harmful chemicals on an item or surface.
Contaminated waste shall mean any biomedical waste; contaminated items that would release blood or other potentially infectious material in a liquid or semi-liquid state if compressed; items on which there is dried blood or other potentially infectious material and which are capable of releasing these materials during handling; sharps and any wastes containing blood or other potentially infectious materials, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 12-8-22(1.1) and also in 29 CFR Part 1910.1030(latest edition), known as Occupational Exposure to Bloodborne Pathogens.
County means the Board of Commissioners of Columbia County, Georgia.
Critical item(s) means those aspects of performing body art services which, if violated, constitute the greatest hazards to health and safety, including imminent health hazards. Critical items shall include the following:
(1)
Autoclave does not meet minimum time, pressure or temperature requirements;
(2)
Lack of a monthly negative spore or microbiological monitoring test for quality control;
(3)
Non-disposable tubes and needles are not sterilized as per guidelines of the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention;
(4)
Reuse of single use articles;
(5)
Sterile instruments are not properly handled;
(6)
Reusable instruments are not handled properly;
(7)
Employees with infectious lesions on hands not being restricted from body art procedures;
(8)
Employees not practicing proper cleanliness and good hygienic practices;
(9)
Water supply not approved or hot and cold running water under pressure not available;
(10)
Approved sewage and liquid waste disposal not available or improper disposal of liquid wastes;
(11)
Cross connection allowing back-siphonage present in plumbing system;
(12)
Toilet and hand washing facilities not available for employees;
(13)
Insect and rodent evidence, harborage, or outer opening present;
(14)
Toxic items not properly stored, labeled, or used.
Decontamination means the use of physical or chemical means to remove, inactivate, or destroy bloodborne pathogens on a surface or item to the point where they are no longer capable of transmitting infectious particles and the surface or item is rendered safe for handling, use, or disposal.
Department means the office of environmental health or any other designee/agent authorized to act on behalf of the board of health such as the Georgia Division of Public Health.
Disinfectant means a solution registered as a disinfectant by the EPA and intended to destroy or inactivate specific viruses, bacteria, or fungi on clean, inanimate surfaces.
Disinfection means the destruction of disease-causing microorganisms on inanimate objects or surfaces, thereby rendering these objects safe for use or handling.
Ear piercing means the puncturing of the outer ear for non-medical purposes, but excluding piercing performed on the ear lobe with an ear piercing gun using sterile materials.
Easily cleanable means that surfaces are readily accessible and made of such materials and finish and so fabricated that residue may be effectively removed by normal cleaning methods.
Environmental health inspector means an official appointed by the board of health who is responsible for licensing, permitting, and inspection of body art establishments.
EPA means the United States Environmental Protection Agency.
Equipment means all machinery, including fixtures, containers, vessels, tools, devices, implements, furniture, display and storage areas, sinks, and all other apparatus and appurtenances used in connection with the operation of a body art establishment.
FDA means the United States Food and Drug Administration.
Germicidal solution means any solution which destroys microorganisms and is so labeled.
Gloves means medical grade disposable single use gloves labeled for surgical or examination purposes.
Guest body artist shall mean a visiting body artist possessing a guest body art license issued by the board of health to perform body art.
Guest body art license means a temporary license for a period not to exceed 30 days which will permit the guest body artist to practice body art in accordance with this article under the direct supervision of a body artist holding a valid body art license issued by the board of health.
Hot water means water that attains and maintains a minimum temperature of 110°F.
Imminent health hazard means any condition, deficiency, or practice, as discovered by the environmental health inspector which, if not corrected, has the potential to result in disease transmission, injury, or loss of life to any person.
Instruments means hand pieces, needles, needle bars, and other instruments that may come in contact with a client's body or may be exposed to bodily fluids during any body art procedure.
ISO means the International Standards Organization.
Jewelry means any ornament used in any body art procedure which is inserted into a newly pierced area. Sec. 46-106. - Prohibited procedures.
The following body art procedures shall not be performed in Columbia County, Georgia.
(1)
Implants, 3-D procedures or other procedures involving insertion of foreign objects under the skin are prohibited.
(2)
Any body art procedures requiring medical equipment (ex. scalpels, derma punches) other than needles shall be prohibited.
(3)
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 16-5-71.1, it shall be unlawful for any person to pierce the body, with the exception of the ear lobes, of any person under the age of 18 for the purposes of allowing the insertion of earrings, jewelry, or similar objects into the body, unless the body piercing is performed in the presence of the person's parent or legal guardian. Nipple and genital piercing is prohibited on minors regardless of parental or legal guardian consent.
(4)
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 16-5-71(a), No person under the age of 18 shall be tattooed.
(5)
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 16-12-5(b), it shall be unlawful for any person to perform tattooing, piercing, or cosmetic micropigmentation procedures on any area of a person within one inch of the nearest part of the eye socket.
(Ord. No. 11-12, 3-15-2011)
Sec. 46-107. - Body art license.
No person shall provide body art services in Columbia County, Georgia who does not have a body art license which is current and in good standing. An application for a body art license can be obtained from the board of health. To obtain a body art license, the following procedures must be met:
(1)
Any person who desires to become a body artist must be at least 18 years of age and must work as an apprentice under a licensed body artist for a period of at least one year within the immediately preceding 36 months. The trainer licensed body artist must provide direct oversight and supervision of the work being performed by the apprentice. Within ten days prior to beginning to serve as the trainer for an apprentice, the licensed body artist must notify the board of health in writing of this arrangement. At the time that the training arrangement ceases to exist, the licensed body artist shall notify the board of health in writing within ten days of such termination that the training process has been discontinued without being completed, or that the training of the apprentice has been successfully completed.
(2)
The applicant must be in compliance with this article, all Columbia County requirements and all applicable law. The applicant must have all Columbia County taxes paid current.
(3)
Information to be provided in the application for a body artist license shall include but shall not be limited to:
a.
Name.
b.
Date of birth.
c.
Sex.
d.
Residence address.
e.
Mailing address.
f.
Phone number.
g.
Place(s) of employment as a body artist.
h.
Photo I.D.
i.
Proof of successful completion of an OSHA approved Bloodborne Pathogen/Universal Precautions training program and Basic First Aid/CPR class given or approved by the board of health.
j.
Listing of the various forms of body art for which the applicant may be licensed.
(4)
The applicant for his/her initial body art license must provide proof of his/her training and experience which must be at least one year working as an apprentice under the direct supervision of a licensed body artist within the immediately preceding 36 months and the completion of the following courses: Bloodborne Pathogen/Universal Precautions, Tattoo Infection Control and Basic First Aid/Adult CPR training, which shall be acceptable to the board of health. Training/courses provided by professional body art organizations/associations or by equipment manufacturers may also be submitted for consideration.
(5)
Individuals seeking body art license shall submit a completed application on a form provided by the board of health, pay a license fee determined by the board of health from time to time, and provide proof of satisfaction of all requirements for the license.
(6)
Body artists who are providing body art services on the date of adoption of this article shall make application for a body art license within 30 days after the date on which this article becomes effective. Such applicants may obtain a body art license if they meet the requirements therefore except for the completion of the courses listed in this section 46-107(4) above; provided, however, that such courses must be successfully completed and the body artist certified as required within six months after the effective date of this article. Failure of the body artist to complete such courses and obtain such certification will result in immediate suspension of the body artist license.
(7)
A body artist from a jurisdiction other than Columbia County, Georgia who wants to provide body art services or temporary body art services in Columbia County must provide proof of experience with a minimum of one year experience working under the direct supervision of an experienced body artist within the last 36-month period or a minimum of 24 months experience working independently within the last 36-month period, proof of licensing in the other jurisdiction which must be current and in good standing, Bloodborne Pathogen/Universal Precautions, Tattoo Infection Control and Basic First Aid/Adult CPR. Proof of such applicant's experience may be a copy of a business license, tax records, copy of another state's tattoo artist license, or other similar city, county, state, or federal documents which show the prospective applicant as having been in business or operating for 18 months elsewhere. The environmental health inspector shall have final approval on other forms of documentation submitted as evidence or proof.
(8)
A body artist shall only perform those body art services that are requested in the body art license application and which are approved by the board of health.
(9)
The body artist license shall be valid from the date of issuance and shall automatically expire annually on January 31 of each year. Body artist licenses which are in good standing may be renewed annually prior to January 31 of each year.
(10)
In order for a body art license to be renewed proof of completion of the required courses set out in this section 46-107(4) of this article must be provided and a renewal fee in the amount set from time to time by the board of health must be paid to the board of health.
(11)
All body artist licenses shall be posted in a prominent and conspicuous area inside the body art establishment where they may be readily observed by clients.
(12)
A copy of the licensing standards for body art services shall be maintained by the licensed body artist and placed where they will be accessible to all staff members working in the body art establishment where the body artist is providing such services.
(Ord. No. 11-12, 3-15-2011)
Sec. 46-108. - Guest body artist license.
To obtain a guest body art license, the following procedures must be followed:
(1)
The board of health may issue a 30 day license to an individual holding a license or similar certificate or registration to engage in the practice of body art issued under the jurisdiction of another city, county, or state of the United States. Such guest body art license will allow a person to practice body art under the direct supervision of a body artist holding a valid body art license issued by the board of health. The issuance of a guest body art license is conditioned upon the applicant demonstrating the following:
a.
The applicant has received training as a body artist equivalent to the minimum training requirements for a body artist set by this article;
b.
The applicant shall provide a letter of consent signed by the licensed body artist who will providing the direct supervision of the applicant, a copy of the body art license or certificate held by the applicant, a copy of the body art license held by the supervising body artist, a copy of the body art establishment permit for the establishment where the applicant will provide any body art services;
c.
An application fee paid to the board of health.
(2)
No visiting body artist shall provide body art services without a guest body art license issued by the board of health.
(3)
The supervising body artist must notify the board of health in writing that he/she will provide the direct supervision of the guest body artist at least ten days prior to the date of issuance of the requested guest body artist license.
(4)
No person shall be issued more than two guest body art licenses in any 12-month period.
(Ord. No. 11-12, 3-15-2011)
Sec. 46-109. - Operator/owner responsibilities.
The responsibilities of an operator/owner of a body artist establishment include, but are not limited to the following:
(1)
The operator/owner shall be responsible for compliance with the requirements of all applicable law.
(2)
Operator/owner shall not allow any body artist who is under the influence of alcohol, drugs or any other substance which does impair his/her ability to comply with this article while performing body art services.
(3)
Operator/owner shall refuse to allow body art services to be provided to, and a body artist shall refuse to perform body art services on, any person who is under the influence of alcohol or drugs or any other substance which may impair the mental capabilities of such person or any person who is mentally incompetent and unable to make reasonable cognitive decisions at the time of seeking body art services.
(4)
Live animals shall not be allowed inside the body art studio or in other areas within the building under the control of the body art establishment permit holder. However, this exclusion does not apply to fish in aquariums or caged reptiles which must be located in the lobby area only. Reptiles may not be handled during business hours. Service animals accompanying disabled persons shall be permitted in the body art establishment.
(5)
Operator/owner shall ensure the skin of a body artist shall be free of rash, any lesion or visible sign of infection. A body artist shall not be allowed to provide any body art services upon any area of a client that evidences the presence of any rash, lesion or other visible signs of infection.
(6)
Any future body art procedures not covered within this article which have the potential for transmitting infectious disease must receive written board of health approval prior to being offered to clients or customers.
(7)
Operator/owner shall not permit body art services to be performed by anyone other than a currently licensed body artist.
(8)
No person except a duly licensed physician, healthcare provider, including but not limited to physician assistants, nurse practitioners, registered nurses, licensed practice nurses and medical technicians acting under the direct supervision of the licensed physician shall be allowed to remove or attempt to remove any body art from a person.

Sec. 46-113. - Variance procedures.
The board of health may grant a variance from the body art establishment or temporary body art establishment provisions for a permit or temporary permit either upon its own motion or upon request of the applicant if it finds that a literal enforcement of such provision will result in demonstrated unnecessary hardship to the applicant and that such a variance will not be contrary to the public interest, public health and/or health and safety of clients. A request for a variance shall be filed by an applicant in writing, setting forth in detail the basis upon which the request is made. Within 30 days of filing for a request for a variance, the board of health shall notify the applicant by certified mail of its approval or denial. If the request is denied, the applicant may request a hearing on the denial. The board of health shall then set a hearing date, time and place. At the hearing, the applicant shall present his/her case. The board of health shall then make a decision which shall be final.
(Ord. No. 11-12, 3-15-2011)
Sec. 46-114. - Minimum standards.
The following minimum standards will apply to body art establishments:
(1)
Each body art establishment where body art procedures are administered shall provide a work area separate from the areas where observers or visitors are permitted.
(2)
At least one work area shall provide complete privacy for clients by means of doors, nonabsorbent curtains, or similar approved partitions.
(3)
A body art establishment shall have a cleaning room to be used exclusively for the cleaning, disinfection, and sterilization of instruments.
a.
The cleaning room shall have a separate NSF approved instrument sink or stainless steel instrument sink reserved only for instrument disinfection, which shall be equipped with hot and cold running water.
b.
The cleaning room shall be separated from any other area in the body art establishment by means of doors, nonabsorbent curtains, or similar approved partitions.
c.
The cleaning room shall be equipped with an ultrasonic cleaning unit and a medical grade autoclave. The autoclave shall be used to sterilize all non-disposable and reusable body art equipment.
d.
The instrument sink, ultrasonic cleaning unit and autoclave shall each be separated by a minimum distance of 24 inches, unless using a board of health approved splashguard.
e.
The cleaning room walls, doors, windows, skylight, and similar closures shall be constructed of smooth, nonabsorbent, durable material and be maintained in good repair.
f.
The cleaning room ceiling shall be maintained in good repair allowing for easy and effective cleaning.
(4)
A hand washing sink shall be provided for each private work station and every two workstations. Hand washing sinks shall be no more than 30 feet from any workstation.
(5)
No hand washing sink or instrument sink shall be used as a janitorial sink.
(6)
Each location shall have the facilities to properly dispose of all waste material. All materials must be disposed of in accordance with Georgia Department of Natural Resources—Environmental Protection Division—Solid Waste Management—Chapter 391-3-4.15.
(7)
The use of common towels and cloths is prohibited. Hand sinks shall be equipped with a soap dispenser and single use disposable towels.
(8)
Sanitary facilities and controls.
a.
Water supply.
1.
Enough potable water for the needs of the body art studio shall be provided from an approved source that is a public water system; or a nonpublic water system that is constructed, maintained and operated according to applicable state and local codes.
2.
Water from a public water system shall meet 40 CFR 141—National Primary Drinking Water Regulations and state drinking water quality standards
3.
Water from a nonpublic system shall meet state drinking water quality standards.
i.
Water from a nonpublic water system shall be sampled and tested at least annually and as required by state water quality regulations.
ii.
The most recent sample report for the nonpublic water system shall be retained on file in the body art establishment or the report shall be maintained as specified by state water quality regulations.
b.
Sewerage. All sewerage, including liquid water, shall be disposed of into a public sewerage system or by a private sewerage disposal system constructed and operated according to law.
c.
Plumbing. Plumbing shall be sized, installed, and maintained according to all applicable building codes. There shall be no cross-connection between the potable water supply and any other water supply or other source of contamination.
(9)
Toilet facilities.
a.
Toilet installation. Toilet facilities shall be designed, installed, and maintained according to law. There shall be separate toilet rooms and/or toilet fixtures to accommodate clients and operators; facilities that have separate equipment cleaning and hand washing areas for personnel may suffice with a common restroom.
b.
Toilet rooms. Toilet rooms opening directly into work or client waiting areas shall be completely enclosed and shall have tight-fitting, self closing, solid doors, which shall be closed except during cleaning or maintenance.
c.
[Ventilation.] All toilet rooms shall have sufficient mechanical ventilation to keep them free of excessive heat, steam, condensation, vapors, obnoxious odors, smoke, and fumes.
d.
Toilet fixtures. Toilet fixtures shall be kept clean and in good repair. A supply of toilet tissue shall be provided at each toilet at all times. Easily cleanable receptacles shall be provided for waste materials. Toilet rooms shall have at least one covered waste receptacle.
(10)
Lavatory facilities.
a.
Lavatory installation. Lavatory facilities shall be designed, installed, and maintained according to law. Facilities shall be of sufficient number and location to permit convenient use by clients and operators.
b.
Lavatory faucets. Each lavatory shall be provided with hot and cold water tempered by means of a mixing valve or combination faucet. Any self-closing, slow-closing, or metering faucet used shall be designed to provide a flow of water for at least 20 seconds without the need to reactivate the faucet.
c.
Lavatory supplies. A soap dispenser and a supply of antiseptic, hand-cleaning soap or detergent shall be available at each lavatory. A supply of single use sanitary towels shall be conveniently located near each lavatory. Easily cleanable covered waste receptacles shall be conveniently located near the hand washing facilities.
d.
Lavatory maintenance. Lavatories, soap dispensers, paper towel dispensers, and all related fixtures shall be kept clean and in good repair.
(11)
Solid waste.
a.
Containers.
1.
Garbage and refuse shall be kept in durable, easily cleaned containers that do not leak and do not absorb liquids. Containers shall be kept in a clean and sound condition.
2.
Containers used in work areas shall be kept covered when not in use and after they are filled.
3.
There shall be a sufficient number of containers to hold all the garbage and refuse that accumulate.
b.
Garbage and refuse shall be disposed of at such frequency to prevent the development of odor and the attraction of insects, rodents, or vermin.
(12)
The premises shall be kept in such condition as to prevent the entrance, harborage, or feeding of insects, rodents, or vermin.
(13)
Floors and floor coverings of all work areas, dressing rooms, locker rooms, toilet rooms and vestibules shall be constructed of smooth, nonabsorbent, durable material and maintained in good repair. Carpeting is allowed in the lobby area only and shall be of closely woven construction, properly installed, easily cleanable, and maintained in good repair.
(14)
Walls and ceilings.
a.
Walls and ceilings, including doors, windows, skylight, and similar closures shall be constructed of smooth, nonabsorbent, durable material and be maintained in good repair. Additionally, work area(s) floors, walls and ceilings shall be clean to sight and touch. Facilities existing in a fully completed condition on October 1, 2010, may be exempted from requirements for a ceiling with smooth, nonabsorbent materials; provided that on October 1, 2010, they do not have ceilings of smooth nonabsorbent materials. At such time as the ceilings of such Facilities need to be repaired or replaced, the smooth nonabsorbent materials must be used.
b.
Light fixtures, vent covers, wall-mounted fans, and similar equipment attached to walls and ceilings shall be easily cleanable and maintained in good repair.
(15)
Floors, walls, ceilings, and attached equipment and decorative materials shall be kept clean and maintained in good repair.
(16)
Lighting.
a.
Permanently fixed artificial light sources shall be installed to provide at least 50 foot candles of light on all work area surfaces and at equipment washing work levels.
b.
Permanently fixed artificial light sources shall be installed to provide at a distance of 30 inches from the floor at least ten foot candles of light in all other areas.
(17)
Ventilation. All rooms shall have sufficient ventilation to keep them free of excessive heat, steam, condensation, vapors, obnoxious odors, smoke, and fumes.
(18)
Poisonous or toxic materials.
a.
There shall be present in the body art studio only those poisonous or toxic materials necessary for maintaining the establishment and cleaning or sanitizing equipment, as well as controlling insects and rodents.
b.
Containers of poisonous or toxic materials shall be prominently and distinctly labeled according to law for easy identification of contents.
c.

Sec. 46-125. - Infection control and environment.
(a)
The body artist must remove all jewelry, watches, rings, necklaces and the like prior to performing any body art services.
(b)
Prior to the start of a body art procedure, the body artist must inspect his/her hands for hangnails, small cuts, sores, and abrasions. If a cut, sore, or abrasion is detected, a bandage should be applied for added protection before gloving. Fingernails must be trimmed to insure that gloves are not punctured.
(c)
Before performing body art services, the body artist must thoroughly wash his/her hands in hot running water with liquid soap, then rinse the hands and dry with disposable paper towels. Hand sanitizers are to be used after each hand washing. Hand washing shall be done as often as necessary to remove contaminants.
(d)
Medical grade single use disposable latex or approved non-latex examination gloves shall be worn during the providing of body art services. Gloves shall be changed and properly disposed of each time there is an interruption in the body art services, the gloves become torn or punctured, or whenever the ability of gloves to function is properly compromised. Under no circumstances shall a single pair of gloves be used on more than one individual.
(e)
Thorough hand washing is required after client contact, after handling blood and body fluids, after wearing gloves, and prior to exiting the work area.
(f)
A body artist shall maintain the highest degree of personal cleanliness, conform to standard hygienic practices, and wear clean clothes, apron, or smock when performing body art services. If contaminated, these items shall be changed promptly after the services performed on that client are completed.
(g)
The skin of the body artist shall be free of rash or infection. No artist affected with boils, infected wounds, pen sores, abrasions, weeping dermatological lesions or acute respiratory infection shall work in any area of a body art establishment in any capacity in which there is a likelihood that the individual could contaminate body art equipment, supplies, or working surfaces with body substances or pathogenic organisms.
(h)
Any body artist who is experiencing symptoms of diarrhea, vomiting, fever, rash, or skin infections shall refrain from activities involving any body art services. Any employee of a body art establishment who is diagnosed or shows symptoms of a blood borne pathogen disease, must be excluded from the body art establishment, until such time as he/she has a return to work notice from a physician.
(i)
Any item or instrument used in providing for body art services that is contaminated during the procedure shall be discarded and replaced immediately with a new disposable item or a new sterilized instrument or item before the procedure resumes.
(j)
Universal precautions shall be observed. All employees shall be trained in universal precautions.
(1)
Assume all human blood, plasma, serum, body fluids (semen, saliva, breast milk, vaginal secretions and any fluid contaminated with blood) and tissues to be contaminated with Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) and/or Hepatitis viruses (e.g., HBV, HCV).
(2)
The most susceptible route of occupational infection for HIV, HBV, and HCV is by accidental needle sticks, but may include contamination of the mucous membranes, or through broken, abraded, or irritated skin. Use appropriate caution and maximum protection to prevent such contact.
(3)
Proper decontamination procedures, emergency biohazard spill management, and proper use of biosafety equipment shall be utilized.
(4)
Use aseptic technique. Thorough hand washing is essential after client contact, after handling blood and body fluids, after wearing gloves, and prior to exiting the work area.
(5)
Needles and needle bar assemblies shall be sterilized prior to use. It is permissible to use sterilized disposable needles and disposable needle bar assemblies for body art.
(6)
Do not remove a used needle from a needle bar by hand. Needles shall be removed from needle bars through the use of gloves and pliers or similar means to prevent accidental puncture. Dispose of needles and other sharps in puncture proof, auto-clavable containers, or disposable biohazard containers.
(7)
After soldering a new needle to the needle bar, the completed assembly (needle and needle bar) shall be de-fluxed, neutralized, and sterilized.
(8)
All regulated wastes shall be disposed of in labeled, manufacturer's color-coded waste containers.
(9)
Infectious material spills shall be cleaned using an EPA registered disinfectant and universal precautions.
(10)
Clean all work areas and equipment used in handling human biohazardous materials with an EPA registered disinfectant when concluding work to protect personnel from accidental infection.
(11)
Eating, drinking, use of tobacco products, and applying cosmetics or lip balm are not permitted in the work area, with the exception of a drink in a closed container. Food shall be stored in cabinets or refrigerators designated and used for this purpose only. Food storage cabinets or refrigerators shall be located outside the work area.
(12)
All procedures shall be performed carefully to minimize the creation of aerosols.
(13)
Employees shall report all work related accidents, incidents, and unexplained illness to your supervisor and/or physician immediately.
(14)
Soiled gloves shall be removed in a manner to minimize the risk of self contamination or cross contamination after each operation and prior to contacting work surfaces, door knobs, wall switches, or telephones. Dispose of used gloves in a bagged, trash container.

Sec. 46-126. - Sterilization.
(a)
An ultrasonic cleaning unit and operational medical grade autoclave shall be provided in each body art studio.
(b)
Ultrasonic cleaning units shall be clearly labeled "biohazardous" and placed away from the autoclave and workstations.
(c)
The ultrasonic cleaning unit and medical grade autoclave shall be used and maintained according to manufacturer's specifications. Each ultrasonic cleaning unit and medical grade autoclave shall be emptied and thoroughly cleaned and disinfected at least once each day that the unit is used. The ultrasonic cleaning unit and autoclave shall be made available for inspection.
(d)
Used non-disposable instruments shall be kept in a separate puncture resistant container until cleaned. The cleaning method shall include the following:
(1)
Instruments shall be placed into an ultrasonic cleaning unit
(2)
After removal from the ultrasonic cleaning unit, instruments shall then be brush scrubbed in hot water and soap, then rinsed in clean water
(3)
Instruments shall then be packed individually in sterilized packs and sterilized in a medical grade autoclave. All sterilized packs shall contain either a sterilized indicator or internal temperature indicator.
(4)
Prior to being placed in the autoclave all equipment shall be bagged, labeled, initialed, dated and sealed.
(5)
Each autoclave bag may hold no more than one individual item (ex. one tube or needle bar).
(e)
After sterilization, the packaged instruments shall be stored in a clean dry cabinet or other tightly covered container reserved and labeled for storage of sterile instruments.
(f)
If a sterilized package has been breached or allowed to get wet, the instrument(s) must be re-packaged and sterilized again before use.
(g)
A cleaning schedule log of the sterilization equipment must be maintained at the body art establishment.
(h)
Spore indicators shall be used a minimum of at least once a month and the results must be kept at the body art establishment for a minimum of two years. An independent commercial testing laboratory contracted by the operator/owner shall perform monthly biological spore testing of the autoclave. A provision shall be included in the contract between the operator/owner with the commercial testing laboratory requiring the commercial testing facility to notify the board of health of any failure of the autoclave to eradicate all living organisms, including spores.
(i)
Upon notification of a positive microbiological monitoring report, the sterilizer shall be immediately checked for proper use and function and the operator/owner shall cease use of the sterilizer immediately upon receipt of the positive report. Three consecutive negative biological tests must be achieved before the body art establishment is re-opened. The body art establishment shall have the option to obtain a properly functioning sterilizer with a negative biological report and to remove from the premises the sterilizer with the positive report in order to remain open.
(Ord. No. 11-12, 3-15-2011)
Sec. 46-127. - Cleaning.
(a)
Each body art studio must be kept clean and sanitary. The operator/owner must develop and implement a written cleaning schedule that includes appropriate methods of decontamination and tasks or procedures to be performed.
(b)
This written schedule must be based on the location within the studio, the type of surfaces to be cleaned, type of possible contamination present, the tasks or procedures to be performed, and their location within the body art establishment.
(c)
The following procedures must be adhered to:
(1)
Clean and sanitize all equipment and work surfaces with an appropriate EPA registered or approved disinfectant after completion of the body art service on each client and at the end of the work shift if surfaces have become contaminated since the last cleaning.
(2)
Remove and replace protective coverings such as plastic wrap and aluminum foil which are placed on equipment, work surfaces and chairs after the completion of each body art services on each client.
(3)
Inspect and decontaminate, on a daily basis, reusable receptacles such as bins, pails, and cans that have the likelihood of becoming contaminated. When contamination is visible, clean and decontaminate receptacles immediately.
(Ord. No. 11-12, 3-15-2011)
Sec. 46-128. - Disposal of waste.
(a)
Needles, razors, or other sharp instruments used during the performance of body art services, shall be placed in puncture resistant, closed containers immediately after use.
(b)
Used needles shall not be purposely bent or broken, or otherwise manipulated by hand.
(c)
Containers of sharp waste shall be sent to a facility where they are either incinerated, rendered non-hazardous, or deposited in a landfill approved to accept biomedical waste in compliance with Georgia Department of Natural Resources—Environmental Protection Division—Solid Waste Management—Chapter 391-3-4.15. Written documentation of proper sharps and biohazard waste disposal must be maintained at the body art studio for a period of at least two years.
(d)
Contaminated waste, which may release liquid blood or body fluids when compressed or may release dried blood or body fluids when handled, must be placed in an OSHA approved "red bag", which is marked with the International Biohazard Symbol. It must then be disposed of in compliance with Georgia Department of Natural Resources—Environmental Protection Division—Solid Waste Management—Chapter 391-3-4.15.
(e)
Waste containers shall be kept closed when not in use.
(f)
Disposable waste shall be handled, stored, and disposed of to minimize direct exposure of personnel to waste materials.
(g)
At least one covered waste receptacle shall be provided in each operator area and each toilet room. Receptacles in the operator area shall be emptied daily and solid waste shall be removed from the premises at least weekly or more often if necessary. All refuse containers shall be covered and maintained.
(h)
Solid waste shall not be stored outdoors unless it is in a secured and lidded dumpster, or otherwise complies with this article.
(i)
Less than one gallon per day of contaminated waste such as blood, fluids, used inks, or other liquid waste may be deposited directly into a drain connected to a sanitary sewer system. Disposal of amounts of contaminated waste of one gallon per day or more must be made through the use of private collection and disposal provider and in accordance with applicable law. Disposable needles and other sharp items shall be placed intact into puncture-resistant containers with a biohazard label before disposal. Filled sharps containers shall be considered regulated waste and must be disposed of in accordance with Georgia Department of Natural Resources, Environmental Protection Division—Solid Waste Management: Chapter 391-3-4.15.
(j)
Waste potentially contaminated with small amounts of blood or other infectious body fluids (e.g., gauze, wipes, disposable lap cloths), which do not meet the definition of regulated waste, shall be double-bagged in sealed, impervious bags to prevent leakage of the contained items. These bags shall be of sufficient strength to prevent breakage or leakage and shall not contain any sharps. The waste bags shall be containerized and disposed of in an approved sanitary landfill.

Sec. 46-129. - Incident reporting.
(a)
A record of each accident and incident involving the injury or threat of injury to clients, or staff occurring in the body art establishment shall be retained. Incidents or accidents resulting in serious medical conditions, e.g., lacerations, hematomas, actual or suspected abuse of clients by staff, shall be reported via telephone to the next-of-kin or responsible person at the earliest practicable hour, but not to exceed 24 hours of the occurrence, and in writing to the board of health within three days of the occurrence.
(b)
Reports shall contain at a minimum: name of the body art establishment, staff member name, client age and sex, date of the incident or accident, location, witness names, identified cause of incident or accident, extent and type of injury and how treated, e.g., hospitalization, and the date of the report.

Sec. 46-130. - Inspections.
(a)
The body art establishment and its records shall be available for review and examination by properly identified representatives of the board of health.
(b)
A copy of the inspection report shall be displayed within plain view upon entering the premises and also shall be available for public inspection at the appropriate Columbia County offices.
(c)
Body art establishments shall be inspected at least twice each calendar year.
(d)
Department or board of health environmental health inspectors shall complete OSHA approved Bloodborne Pathogens/Universal Precautions, Tattoo Infection Control and Basic First Aid/Adult CPR training courses. In addition, these inspectors shall receive training from the department or board of health staff in performing inspections of body art establishments.
(e)
Inspection results—Reporting and scoring.
(1)
Inspection results for body art establishments shall be recorded on standard board of health forms.
(2)
The scoring system shall include a weighted point value for each requirement in which critical items are assigned values of five points, with minor violations having assigned values of either one or two points as determined by the board of health.
(3)
The rating score of the body art establishments shall be the total of the weighted point values for all violations subtracted from 100.
(f)
Violation correction.
(1)
Correction of critical and minor violations shall be within five and ten calendar days, respectively.
(2)
Upon declaration by a representative of the board of health of an imminent health hazard which can not be immediately corrected, said representative of the board of health shall issue an order requiring the body art establishment to immediately cease operations until authorized by the board of health to reopen.
(3)
In the case of a temporary body art establishment, all critical violations shall be corrected within 24 hours. If critical violations are not corrected within 24 hours, the temporary body art establishment shall immediately cease operations until authorized to resume by the board of health or its duly authorized representative. Upon declaration of an imminent health hazard which can not be immediately corrected, the representative of the board of health shall issue an order requiring the temporary body art establishment to immediately cease operations until authorized to reopen.

Sec. 46-131. - Enforcement and penalties.
(a)
A body artist or body artist establishment which fails to comply with this article, shall be subject to the sanctions available to the board of health pursuant to O.C.G.A. tit. 31, ch. 5, Administration and Enforcement.
(b)
Enforcement of these rules and regulations shall be in accordance with O.C.G.A. tit. 31, ch. 5. The board of health shall have the power and authority to suspend or revoke permits for failure to comply with the provisions of this chapter.
(c)
The board of health shall have primary responsibility for the enforcement of this article within its jurisdiction as part of the Code of Ordinances of Columbia County, Georgia for the purpose of enforcement. The board of health or its authorized agents may be authorized to issue citations for violations of the provisions of this article.
(d)
The board of health or its authorized agent may without prior notification enter upon and inspect the premises of any body art establishment.
(e)
The board of health is empowered to institute appropriate proceedings in a court of competent jurisdiction for the purpose of enjoining violation of any applicable provision of the O.C.G.A. tit. 31, or this article.
(f)
When an application for a permit or license is denied or a permit or license previously granted is proposed to be suspended or revoked, the applicant or holder thereof shall be afforded notice and hearing as provided in O.C.G.A. tit. 31, ch. 5.
(g)
If an application is denied or a permit or license is suspended or revoked, the applicant or holder of the permit or license must be notified in writing, specifically stating reasons why the action was taken.
(h)
The purpose of these procedures is to state the minimum actions to be taken to fulfill the obligation of the board of health in assuring compliance with this article when the continued operation of a body art establishment or the providing of body art services by a body artist presents a substantial and imminent health hazard to the public or when a body art establishment or a body artist in violation of this article or the laws of the State of Georgia.
(i)
Suspension is effective upon service of a written notice thereof, and body art services must cease immediately.
(j)
The notice must state the basis for the suspension and advise the permit or license holder of the right to a preliminary hearing on request within three days of service of the notice by the board of health.
(k)
If requested, the preliminary hearing will be held by an experienced supervisory level employee of the board of health not directly involved in the suspension.
(l)
The rules of evidence will not apply, but both the board of health and the permit or license holder may present witnesses, records and argument.
(m)
The hearing official will be authorized immediately to rescind or modify the suspension or to continue the suspension with or without conditions.
(n)
If the suspension is not rescinded, the permit or license holder will have the right to request an evidentiary hearing. If a hearing is not requested, upon correction of all violations, the operator/owner may request an inspection to reinstate the permit or license.
(o)
For the purpose of this article a notice of hearing is properly served when delivered in person or by registered or certified mail.
(p)
The board of health may summarily suspend a permit to operate a body art establishment or a license to be a body artist if it determines through inspection, or examination of employees, records, or other means as specified in this article, that an imminent health hazard exists.
(q)
If operations of a body art establishment or the activities of a body artist are discontinued due to the existence of an imminent health hazard or otherwise according to law, the permit or license holder shall obtain approval from the board of health before resuming operations.
(Ord. No. 11-12, 3-15-2011)
Sec. 46-132. - Severability.
If any provision or subpart thereof contained in this article is found to be invalid unconstitutional, or in conflict with the law by a court of competent jurisdiction, the validity of all remaining provisions or subparts thereof shall not be so affected but shall remain in full force and effect.
(Ord. No. 11-12, 3-15-2011)
Sec. 46-133. - Effective date.
