ARTICLE VIII. - HAMPTON YOUTH COUNCIL[9]

Editor's note— Ord. No. 455 , § 1, adopted July 24, 2018, amended Art. VIII in its entirety to read as herein set out. Former Art. VIII, §§ 2-341—2-346, pertained to similar subject matter, and derived from Ord. No. 443 , 12-12-17.

Sec. 2-341. - Hampton Youth Council created.
(a)
There is created a board to be known as the City of Hampton Youth Council wherein its members serve as advisory youth council to the mayor and council.
(b)
The youth council members must attend Hampton High School or reside within the city limits and be between the ages of 14 through 19 actively enrolled in a public, private, or home school, in grades ninth through twelfth.
(c)
Youth council members shall be chosen from applicants who express an interest in public service during the application process which shall be between August 15 and September 15 of each year.
(d)
Youth council members will be selected by a committee appointed by the mayor in accordance with Charter Section 2.32(6).
(e)
The selection of the youth council shall occur on or before September 30 of each year, and they will be recognized and sworn in at the following October meeting of the city council.
(f)
The youth council will attend city council meetings and, along with city staff, coordinate activities.
(Ord. No. 455 , § 1, 7-24-18; Ord. No. 518 , § 1, 7-14-20)
Sec. 2-342. - Election of officers.
The youth council shall elect a chairman to conduct meetings and a vice-chairman to conduct meetings in the absence of the chairman. Elections shall be held at the first regular meeting after selection by the mayor's appointed committee.
(Ord. No. 455 , § 1, 7-24-18)
Sec. 2-343. - Vacancies.
Irregular vacancies on the youth council shall be filled as they occur and regular vacancies shall be filled by appointment in September or October of each year.
(Ord. No. 455 , § 1, 7-24-18)
Sec. 2-344. - Meetings; records.
The youth council shall hold at least one regular meeting per month, with the option to meet more often as needed or desired. Public record of such meetings shall be kept by the office of the city clerk. All meetings shall be open to the public.
(Ord. No. 455 , § 1, 7-24-18)
Sec. 2-345. - Absences from meetings.
Absences from three consecutive regular meetings of the youth council shall cause a member to be removed from their seat, unless such absence is excused by a majority vote of the board, with such excuse duly entered upon its minutes.
(Ord. No. 455 , § 1, 7-24-18)
Sec. 2-346. - Duties and responsibilities.
The youth council shall have the duty and responsibility to abide by all rules and regulations of Youth Council Handbook.
(Ord. No. 455 , § 1, 7-24-18; Ord. No. 518 , § 1, 7-14-20)
