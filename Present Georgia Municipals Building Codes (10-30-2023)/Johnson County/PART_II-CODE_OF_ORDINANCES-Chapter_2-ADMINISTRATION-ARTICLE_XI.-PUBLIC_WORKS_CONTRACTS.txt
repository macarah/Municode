ARTICLE XI. - PUBLIC WORKS CONTRACTS[1]

Sec. 2-285. - Construction contracts.
(a)
Construction contacts, excluding roads, over $100,000.00 will be let in accordance with the Georgia Local Government Public Works Construction Law, O.C.G.A § 36-91-1 et seq.
(b)
Construction contracts between $2,500.01 and $99,999.99 will be secured by either competitive sealed bids or sealed proposals based upon the delivery method and in the best interest of the county.
(c)
Construction contracts under $2,500.00 shall be considered as normal repair, maintenance or operating expense. The contact may be bid or offered with written competitive quotations without regard for advertising
(Code 2002, § 1-2-8)
Sec. 2-286. - Advertisement of contract opportunities.
(a)
Contract opportunities shall be posted conspicuously in the county courthouse and advertised twice in the four weeks preceding the bid/proposal opening. The first advertisement must run at least four weeks before the bid opening date. The second advertisement must follow at least two weeks later.
(b)
Plans and specifications must be available on the first day of the advertisement and must be available to the public.
(Code 2002, § 1-2-8)
Sec. 2-287. - Bonds and oath.
(a)
Bid, payment and performance bonds shall be required on all projects over $50,000.00 and may be required on contracts over $20,000.00 at the discretion of the county commissioners. bid bonds or proposal guarantees must be five percent of the bid price.
(b)
All contractors must execute a written oath stating that they have not unlawfully restricted competitive bidding on the project.
(Code 2002, § 1-2-8)
Sec. 2-288. - Record keeping and retention.
(a)
The county administrator shall create and maintain the following files for use in the purchasing and contracting process:
(1)
Bidders list;
(2)
Purchase order file;
(3)
Requisition file by department; and
(4)
Bid, proposal and quotation file both completed and solicitations
(b)
All records are subject to the provisions of O.C.G.A. §§ 50-18-70 through 50-18-77 (the "Georgia Open Records Act"), and shall be retained in accordance with the retention schedule established by the Georgia Secretary of State, department of archives and history.
(Code 2002, § 1-2-10)
State Law reference— Disposal of public records, O.C.G.A. § 50-18-102.
Sec. 2-289. - Disposition of real property.
Disposition of real property shall be conducted at a public sale in compliance with O.C.G.A. § 36-9-3 or as otherwise provided for by state law, with the following exceptions:
(1)
A redemption of real property acquired by a tax deed;
(2)
A grant of easement or license;
(3)
A grant or conveyance of right-of-way or other transportation purpose;
(4)
A conveyance to any other unit of government;
(5)
A conveyance of recreational area to the recreation authority.
(Code 2002, § 1-2-11)
State Law reference— Authority to control and dispose of county real property, O.C.G.A. § 36-9-2.
Sec. 2-290. - Disposition of personal property.
The board of commissioners shall be the sole determinant of whether a particular item or group of items has no further value to the county. They may deem such personal property as surplus and order it sold by public auction, by sealed bids, by spot bids or by other means most advantageous to the county.
(Code 2002, § 1-2-11)
