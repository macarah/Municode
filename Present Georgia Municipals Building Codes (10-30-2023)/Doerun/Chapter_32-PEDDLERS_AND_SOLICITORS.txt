Sec. 32-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Canvass means to go from place to place and seek to obtain orders for goods, wares or merchandise, or actually obtain orders for them.
Peddle means to carry from place to place any goods, wares or merchandise and offer to sell or barter them, or actually sell or barter them.
Solicit means to request or accept, directly or indirectly, money, credit, property, financial assistance or any other thing of value to be used for any charitable purpose.
(Code 2000, § 58-1)
Sec. 32-2. - Registration required.
Any person peddling, soliciting or canvassing within the city shall be required to register and obtain an identification card as provided by this chapter.
(Code 1982, § 7-2-1; Code 2000, § 58-2)
Sec. 32-3. - Registration fee; application for identification card.
Any person desiring to peddle, solicit or canvass within the city shall pay to the city clerk or his designated representative a registration fee as fixed from time to time by the council and shall make application with the chief of police for an identification card which shall show payment of the registration fee and the days that the registrant has registered to peddle, solicit or canvass within the city.
(Code 1982, § 7-2-2; Code 2000, § 58-3)
Sec. 32-4. - Exhibition of registration card.
Each registrant shall at all times while in the city have upon his person the registration card and shall exhibit the card when requested to do so by any law enforcement officer or by any municipal authority and by any person being solicited. Possession of this registration card shall not in any way represent an endorsement or approval of any products or project by the city.
(Code 1982, § 7-2-3; Code 2000, § 58-4)
Sec. 32-5. - Unlawful acts.
It shall be unlawful for any person to:
(1)
Peddle, canvass or solicit before 8:00 a.m. or after 9:00 p.m.;
(2)
Peddle within the fire limits of the city at any time when such fire limits shall include a smaller area than the entire area within the city limits;
(3)
Peddle, canvass or solicit without having registered with the city clerk in accordance with this chapter or to peddle, solicit or canvass without having on his person and in his possession an identification card issued in accordance with this chapter;
(4)
Solicit, canvass or peddle without the express prior permission of any occupant or property owner at any residence, apartment complex, or shopping mall, other than areas open to public parking, where there is posted a sign forbidding such activity;
(5)
Remain on private premises for the purpose of peddling, canvassing or soliciting, after being asked to leave the premises; or
(6)
Continue peddling, canvassing or soliciting that is directed to any person, after being refused by such person, upon the public streets, public parks or other public areas or parks. Such action constitutes harassment.
(Code 1982, § 7-2-4; Code 2000, § 58-5)
