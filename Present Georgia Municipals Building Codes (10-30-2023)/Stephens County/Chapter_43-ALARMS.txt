Chapter 43 - ALARMS
ARTICLE I. - PREAMBLE AND ENACTMENT CLAUSE

Sec. 43-1. - [Purpose.]
Stephens County desires to develop and promote a program which will address abuse and waste of emergency resources, reduce unnecessary safety risks posed to the county emergency response personnel and citizens during false alarm responses and reduce the numbers of false alarm dispatches within the unincorporated portion of the county.
(Ord. of 5-22-2018)
ARTICLE II. - FALSE ALARMS

Sec. 43-2. - Alarm defined.
For the purposes of this chapter, the word "alarm" shall mean any equipment, device or system used for the purpose of providing burglar alarms, fire alarms, or other security alarms.
(Ord. of 5-22-2018)
Sec. 43-3. - Reserved.
Sec. 43-4. - False alarms limited.
It shall be unlawful for the owner of an alarm to have over three false alarms per 12 consecutive months transmitted from a central or relay office to the Stephens County Emergency Response Personnel. The 12-month period begins on the date of the first false alarm. It shall be the duty of the owner of the alarm to comply with this section.
(Ord. of 5-22-2018; Ord. of 3-26-2019)
Sec. 43-5. - Audible false alarms.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for the owner of an alarm to have over three false audible alarms per 12 months from the date of the first false alarm.
(b)
The alarm user will be fined for each false alarm call beginning with the fourth occurring per 12 months at the following rate of $300.00 for the fourth alarm, $400.00 for the fifth alarm, and $500.00 for the sixth and subsequent alarms.
(c)
It shall be the duty of the owner of the alarm to comply with the provisions of this section.
(Ord. of 5-22-2018; Ord. of 3-26-2019)
Sec. 43-6. - False alarm exemptions.
An alarm will not be considered false if it is determined that the alarm was caused by:
(a)
A natural or manmade catastrophe, or other "Act of God" such as tornadoes, floods, earthquakes, or other similarly violent conditions.
(b)
Vandalism causing physical damage to the property.
(c)
Attempted entry of a location causing visible, physical or other evidence of damage to the location which has caused the alarm to sound.
(d)
Severe weather or failure of electrical power not caused by the subscriber.
(e)
The test of a local alarm system by an authorized alarm agent or alarm company employee who is present at the premises inspecting, servicing, repairing, or installing the alarm only if the authorized alarm agent or alarm company notifies 911 prior to the test.
(Ord. of 5-22-2018)
Sec. 43-7. - Appeals.
The alarm use[r] can file a written appeal to the county administrator by setting forth the reasons for the appeal within ten days after receipt of notification of a false alarm. The filing of an appeal with the county administrator stays the assessment of the fine and suspension until the Stephens County Board of Commissioners makes a final decision.
(Ord. of 5-22-2018)
