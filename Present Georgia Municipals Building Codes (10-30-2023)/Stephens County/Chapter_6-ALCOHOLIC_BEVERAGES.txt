ARTICLE II. - MALT BEVERAGES AND WINE
PURPOSE
Be it ordained by the Stephens County Board of Commissioners, for the purposes of:
Promoting the health and general welfare of the community;
Establishing reasonable and ascertainable standards for the regulation and control of the licensing and sale of alcoholic beverages;
Protecting and preserving schools and churches' and
Preventing undesirable persons from engaging in or having any interest in alcoholic beverages; it is hereby ordained as follows:
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 6-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcohol means ethyl alcohol, hydrated oxide of ethyl, or spirits of wine, from whatever source or by whatever process produced.
Alcoholic beverage means and includes all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, malt beverage, wine, or fortified wine as defined in this section.
Board means the Stephens County Board of Commissioners.
Brown bagging means possession of an open glass bottle, can, or other container containing an alcoholic beverage or consumption of an alcoholic beverage on any premises for which a county business license has been issued, and occurs at a location different from where the alcoholic beverage was purchased.
Clerk means the clerk of the board of commissioners.
Church means any permanent building which houses the main sanctuary in which persons regularly assemble for religious worship and which is publicly designated as a church and which is either owned or held under a lease of at least five years by or on behalf of such persons, but shall not include a residence also used for religious purposes. The term "church" shall not include the temporary housing of a religious group in an otherwise commercial center.
Code enforcement officer means the Stephens County Sheriff Department, Stephens County Marshal, or any individual so designated by the board.
Distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume, including, but not limited to, all fortified wines.
Election day means that period of time beginning with the opening of the polls and ending with the closing of the polls. As used in this chapter, the term "election" is defined as any statewide primary election and the statewide general election and any runoff held for such primary or general election. Such term also includes local elections,
Financial interest includes, but is not limited to, holding any indebtedness or security interest in a business.
Licensee means the individual to whom a license is issued, or, in the case of a partnership, limited liability company or corporation, all partners, members, managers, officers, and directors of the partnership, limited liability company or corporation and in the case of any other business entity, any person filling an analogous role in such entity.
Licensed premises means any premises in which alcoholic beverages are sold or dispensed for consumption on the premises and shall include any premises which are required by law to be licensed to sell or dispense alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises.
Liter means metric measurement currently used by the United States.
Malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any other similar product or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than six [bottles], 9.5 percent alcohol by volume, and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer, and strong beer. The term does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine. Also included are beverages known as "nonalcoholic" beer."
Minor is defined pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23, as enacted or amended.
Nonprofit private club means any nonprofit association organization under the laws of the state which:
(1)
Has been in existence at least one year prior to the filing of its application for a license to be issued pursuant to this chapter;
(2)
Has at least 75 regular dues-paying members;
(3)
Owns, hires, or leases a building or space within a building for the reasonable use of its members, which building or space:
a.
Has suitable kitchen and dining room space and equipment; and
b.
Is staffed with a sufficient number of employees for cooking, preparing, and serving meals for its members and guests; Georgia code O.C.G.A. § 3-7-1; and
(4)
Has no member, officer, agent, or employee directly or indirectly receiving, in the form of salary or other compensation, any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages beyond a fixed salary.
Owner means any person, corporation, or partnership or any other entity having a financial interest in the income of the business. Owner shall also include any person, corporation, or partnership operating a business under a management contract.
Operator means and includes the owner, license holder, operator, manager, and person in charge of any licensed premises.
Peace officer means any peace officer, county marshal, sheriff, or any other county designee.
Person means any individual, firm, partnership, cooperative, nonprofit membership corporation, joint venture, association, company, corporation, agency, syndicate, estate, trust, business trust, receiver, fiduciary, or other group or combination acting as a unit, body politic, or political subdivision, whether public, private, or quasi-public. Person shall include all members of a retail dealer licensee's family.
Premises means the space or area owned, leased, or controlled by a licensee pursuant to this chapter and used by him for the purpose of operating under the license and there shall be a separate license for each premises outlet. In the case of a licensee where a golf course is located adjacent to and contiguous with a restaurant, the term "premises" shall include the playing area of the golf course.
Resident means a person whose primary residence is within the territorial limits of the county.
Restaurant means any public place kept, used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public as a place where meals are served and where meals are actually and regularly served, without sleeping accommodations, such place being provided with adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining room equipment and a seating capacity of at least 50 people, with at least two meals per day being served for not less than five days a week, for at least nine months each calendar year, and the serving of alcoholic beverages to be consumed on the premises as only incidental thereto. A minimum of 50 percent of its gross income deriving from the sale of such meals or appetizers, with each sale, which includes an alcoholic beverage including a reasonable order of a meal or appetizer.
School means governmental or church school buildings and such buildings at such other schools as teach the subjects commonly taught in the common schools and colleges of this state. The term "school building" includes only those structures in which instruction is offered.
Stephens County is a political subdivision of the State of Georgia and, for the purposes of this chapter, the unincorporated portions thereof.
Package means a bottle, can, keg, barrel, or other original consumer container.
Retail package means sales of malt beverages or wine packaged to go and not for consumption on-the-premises sales.
Retail consumption dealer means any person who sells alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises, at retail, only to consumers and not for resale.
Retail package dealer means any person who sells unbroken packages, at retail, only to consumers and not for resale.
Tax means the tax imposed by this chapter.
Veterans' organization means a veterans group duly certified by their national organization and with a membership of not less than 25 persons.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes, either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, and combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines and like products. The term does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this section.
(Ord. of 10-27-2009)

Sec. 6-32. - Violations.
Any firm, person or corporation who shall do anything prohibited by this chapter as the same exists or as it may hereafter be amended or who shall fail to do anything required by this chapter as the same exists or as it may hereafter be amended shall be guilty of a violation of a county ordinance, amenable to the process of the magistrate court of the county and, upon conviction, shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 or by confinement in the county jail not to exceed 60 days, or both, in the discretion of the court.
(1)
It shall be a violation of this chapter for any person, including the licensee, to engage in any activity on the premises for which the license is issued or within the place of business which is in violation of the laws or regulations of the state or federal government or any ordinance of Stephens County.
(2)
Any person holding any license issued pursuant to this chapter or any employee or agent of such person who violates any provision of this chapter, or directs, consents to, permits, or acquiesces in such violation either directly or indirectly shall, by such conduct, subject the license to suspension, revocation or probation upon conditions. In addition, a maximum civil fine of $1,000.00 and/or up to 60 days in county jail, for each violation. In considering the amount of the civil fine, if any, the administrative hearing officer shall consider the following factor:
a.
The gravity of the violation;
b.
Any previous violations for which a fine was not assessed or for which the administrative hearing officer reduced and assessed fine;
c.
The cost to Stephens County related to compliance or enforcement efforts undertaken by Stephens County in pursuing the case; and
d.
The cost incurred by Stephens County in order to mitigate a condition remaining as the result of the violation.
(3)
The imposition of a civil fine under the provisions of this section shall not prevent the suspension, revocation, or nonrenewal of the license or the taking of punitive or remedial action for any other violation of the ordinances of Stephens County or for subsequent violations of this section. No license shall be renewed if the licensee prior to renewal has not paid any civil fines imposed pursuant to this section. Any action taken by the administrative hearing officer to suspend or revoke a license issued hereunder, shall not preclude, and may be in addition to, any criminal prosecution by a proper authority as provided by the laws and ordinances of the county, the state, or the United States. Whenever any action is taken by the administrative hearing officer to suspend or revoke any license issued hereunder, the administrative hearing officer shall provide written notice to the licensee of the action taken and state the reasons therefore. The licensee shall have 15 days following notification of such action to request a hearing before the governing authority. The licensee shall be entitled at such hearing to present evidence and cross-examine opposing witnesses.
(4)
The Stephens County Sheriff, the Stephens County Marshal, or a code enforcement officer as designated by the board is authorized to conduct any enforcement actions necessary to determine compliance with this chapter.
(5)
It shall be a violation not to require and properly check the identification to ensure that an underage person is not sold, served, or does not have in his possession alcoholic beverages while in a licensed establishment. Identification in this section shall mean any document issued by a governmental agency containing a description of the person, such person's photograph and giving such person's date of birth and shall include, without being limited to, a passport, military ID card, driver's license or state department or [of] public safety ID card.
(6)
Except as otherwise specifically provided for by law, it is unlawful for any person to sell, possess, conceal, store, or convey any alcoholic beverage on which any tax or license fee imposed by this chapter has not been paid (O.C.G.A. § 3-2-33a).
(7)
The license shall not permit the breaking of the package or packages on the premises where sold and shall not permit the drinking of the contents of the package or packages on the premises where sold (O.C.G.A. § 3-4-25).
(8)
Malt beverages in quantities exceeding the amount specified in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-8, upon which the taxes imposed by or authorized pursuant to this chapter have not been paid in this state, are declared to be contraband; and any motor vehicle, watercraft, or aircraft used in transporting such beverages in excess of the amount specified in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-8 are also declared to be contraband and subject to seizure and disposition as provided by this chapter (O.C.G.A. § 3-5-3(b)).
(Ord. of 10-27-2009)
Sec. 6-33. - Remedies.
If any provision of this chapter has been violated or is being violated, in addition to other remedies, the county may institute injunction, mandamus, or other appropriate action or proceeding to protect or abate such violation.
(Ord. of 10-27-2009)
Sec. 6-34. - Hours of operation.
(a)
The hours of sale of packaged malt beverages and wines shall be between 7:00 a.m. to 11:45 p.m., Monday through Saturday and 12:30 p.m. to 11:30 p.m. on Sundays. The hours of sale for consumption on the premises of malt beverages and wines shall be 11:00 a.m. to 12:45, Monday through Friday and 12:30 p.m. to 12:00 midnight on Sundays. Hours on Saturday and Christmas Eve shall be 8:00 a.m. to 11:45 p.m.
(b)
If a licensed business is open for less than the hours stated in subsection (a), then sales of malt beverages and wines shall be restricted to those regular store hours. However, in no event shall sales be made during hours other than described in subsection (a).
(c)
All holders of licenses issued hereunder must, within three months after the issuance of such license, open for business the establishment referred to in the license. Failure to open the licensed establishment as referred to within such period shall serve as forfeiture and cancellation of the unused license and no refund of license fee shall be made to the license holder.
(Ord. of 10-27-2009; Ord. of 1-22-2019)
State Law reference— O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20 (2009)
Sec. 6-35. - Advertising.
Licensees under this chapter shall not display any sign or decal either outside the premises or at any location in the county, which advertises the sale of malt beverages and wines, either directly, indirectly, or by inference or suggestion. Any advertising signs within the principal building shall not be displayed within view of the general public from the outside of the building. The county shall provide for all retail and consumption licensees an orange decalcomania sign to post on the front of the licensed location.
(1)
Any license obtained under this chapter shall be displayed on the licensed premised in public view.
(2)
Each licensee shall also post in a conspicuous place a sign with letters at least two inches in size stating: "The sale of alcoholic beverages to persons under the age of 21 years is strictly prohibited".
(3)
Retail package licensees shall indicate plainly by tags or labels on the bottles or containers or on the edge of the shelf immediately below or above where the containers are placed the price of an alcoholic beverage exposed or offered for sale. No other sign may be exposed within or without the retail establishment showing prices or indicating that alcoholic beverages are for sale on the premises.
(4)
No person holding a retail dealer's license to deal in distilled spirits by the package shall display any advertisement of or information regarding the price or prices of any distilled spirits in any show window or other place visible from outside the licensee's place of business.
(5)
All retail consumption dealers and retail dealers in this state who sell at retail any alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises shall post, in a conspicuous place, a sign which clearly reads: Warning: "Drinking alcoholic beverage during pregnancy can cause birth defects".
(Ord. of 10-27-2009)
Sec. 6-36. - Underage persons.
(a)
If any license holder, agents or employees of license holders under this article sell alcoholic beverages to a person under the legal age to purchase alcoholic beverages as provided by state law, the license shall be suspended for a minimum of 30 days. The board of commissioners in its sole discretion may suspend the license for a longer period of time or revoke the license, depending upon the severity of the offense evidenced by the facts in each case, including, but not limited to, consideration of prior violations of law by the license holder. If the board of commissioners determines a license holder and/or his agents and/or employees have sold alcoholic beverages to an underage person or persons on at least three separate occasions, then the license shall be revoked and application for a new license shall not be authorized until the expiration of 12 months following the date of revocation.
(b)
License holders may not allow a person under the age of 18 years of age to sell malt beverages or wine.
(c)
No person shall knowingly and intentionally act as agent to purchase or acquire any alcoholic beverage for or on behalf of a person who is under the minimum age 21 to purchase alcoholic beverages, as provided by state law, or otherwise procure for or furnish to a person who is under the minimum age 21 to purchase alcoholic beverages, as defined by state law, any alcoholic beverage, except for medical purposes upon the written prescription of a duly licensed physician under the laws of the state, or at a religious ceremony.
(d)
It is unlawful for any person who is under the minimum age 21 to purchase alcoholic beverages, as provided by state law, to falsely misrepresent his or her age in any manner whatsoever in violation of this article or applicable laws.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for any person who is under the minimum age 21 to purchase alcoholic beverages, as provided by state law, to purchase, drink, or possess any alcoholic or malt beverage or wine in the county outside of any municipalities.
(f)
It shall not be considered a violation of this article for a person who is under the minimum age 21 to purchase alcoholic beverages, as provided by state law, to purchase, drink, or possess any alcoholic or malt beverage if such person is at the time bona fidely acting for and on behalf of law enforcement agencies conducting investigations of license holders and/or their employees.
(g)
No person shall allow or require a person in his/her employment under 18 years of age to dispense, serve, sell, or take orders for any alcoholic beverage.
(h)
No person under 21 years of age shall misrepresent his or her identity or use any false identification for the purpose of purchasing or obtaining any alcoholic beverage.
(i)
This Code section shall not prohibit persons under 18 years of age who are employed in supermarkets, convenience stores, breweries, or drugstores from selling or handling alcoholic beverages, which are sold for consumption off the premises (O.C.G.A. § 3-3-24).
(Ord. of 10-27-2009)
Sec. 6-37. - Sales on Christmas Day and election day.
(a)
Except as specifically authorized by law, no person knowingly and intentionally shall sell or offer to sell alcoholic beverages on Christmas Day. This subsection shall not apply to bona fide nonprofit private clubs.
(b)
The sale of alcoholic beverages on any election day shall be permitted in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20.
(Ord. of 10-27-2009; Ord. of 1-22-2019)

Sec. 6-38. - Dispensing and miscellaneous regulations.
The following dispensing and miscellaneous regulations shall apply to license holders under this article:
(1)
License holders may not sell on the licensed premises less than 24 ounces of malt beverages, whether in a container or a multiunit container, unless they have consumption licenses or sell less than one-half pint of wine.
(2)
License holders may not sell malt beverages or wines on the licensed premises to any person who is in an intoxicated condition (O.C.G.A. § 3-3-22).
(3)
License holders shall not permit any disorderly conduct or breach of the peace on the licensed premises.
(4)
License holders may not sell, offer for sale, possess or permit the consumption on the licensed premises of any kind of alcoholic or malt beverages and wines for which a license holder is not authorized to sell under his license. The license holder shall not allow brown bagging on the licensed premises.
(5)
Malt beverages or wines may not be sold or offered for sale through vending machines on the licensed premises.
(6)
Deliveries of malt beverages and wines to the licensed premises may be made only by a conveyance owned and operated by a wholesale dealer with a license from the board of commissioners to deliver such beverages in the county.
(7)
No retail license holder shall accept delivery of malt beverages and wines from any wholesale dealer unless the wholesale dealer has fully complied with the state laws regulating and taxing the sale of such beverages at wholesale.
(8)
No retail or consumption license holder shall deliver malt beverages and wines at any location other than the licensed premises.
(9)
All licensees shall store all malt beverages and wines in their possession only on the premises for which the license was issued.
(10)
Every license holder shall have available on the licensed premises a copy of this article and shall be responsible for compliance with the provisions of this article by all persons on the premises.
(11)
It shall be unlawful for a licensee hereunder or his agent to add to the contents of a bottle or refill an empty bottle or container in any manner to misrepresent the quantity, quality or brand name of any beverage licensed hereunder.
(12)
No retail dealer shall knowingly and intentionally allow or permit the breaking of any package or packages containing alcoholic beverages on the premises where sold or allow or permit the drinking of the contents of such package or packages on the premises where sold. This Code section shall not apply with respect to sales pursuant to a license for consumption on the premises (O.C.G.A. § 3-3-26).
(13)
Any malt beverage possessed, sold, or offered for sale by a retail dealer which was purchased or otherwise acquired from any person other than a wholesale dealer authorized to do business under this chapter is declared to be contraband and shall be seized by the Commissioners or the appropriate local authorities and disposed of by the Commissioners in the manner provided in this title [article] (O.C.G.A. § 3-5-27).
(Ord. of 10-27-2009)
Sec. 6-39. - Taxes.
The tax levied by this article shall be paid to Stephens County by each wholesale dealer on all beverages coming under this article within Stephens County by each wholesale dealer selling, shipping or in any way delivering malt beverages or wine to a retailer in Stephens County, who shall collect such deliveries on or before the tenth day of the month next succeeding the calendar month in which such sales were made.
(1)
There is levied and imposed upon each wholesale dealer selling malt beverages outside municipalities in the county an excise tax in the following amounts:
a.
Where malt beverages, commonly known as tap or draft beer, are sold in and from a barrel or bulk container, a tax of $6.00 on each container sold containing not more than 15½ gallons and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 15½ gallons.
b.
Where malt beverages are sold in bottles, cans, or other containers, except barrels or bulk containers, a tax of $0.05 per 12 ounces and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of 12 ounces.
(2)
There is levied and imposed upon each wholesale dealer selling wine outside municipalities of the county an excise tax on the first sale or use of wine by the package, which tax shall be $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter
(3)
The taxes imposed by this section shall not be levied with respect to any sales of wine or beer, which are exempt from taxation, by federal or state law.
(4)
Except as otherwise expressly provided for by law, no person knowingly and intentionally shall possess, sell, or purchase any distilled spirits upon which the taxes imposed by this title have not been paid (O.C.G.A. § 3-3-29).
(5)
A summary report made by each wholesale dealer shall show the exact quantities of taxed beverages, by size and type of container and the amount of excise tax collected.
(6)
No person shall manufacture, distribute, sell, handle, or possess for sale, or otherwise deal in, alcoholic beverages without first obtaining all applicable licenses required by this title [article].
(7)
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell at retail or otherwise within Stephens County any beverage taxed by this article on which the required tax has not been paid.
(Ord. of 10-27-2009)

Sec. 6-43. - Entertainment, attire and conduct prohibited.
The purpose of this article is to regulate activities on premises licensed for the sale of distilled spirits, including, but not limited to, adult entertainment establishments. This article is not a de facto prohibition of legal expression but request[s] a balancing of the rights of the community and the adult entertainment establishments. This article does not allow any business or activity that is otherwise unlawful.
Pursuant to the Constitution of the State of Georgia, article 3, section 6, paragraph VII:
(1)
No person shall perform on a premises licensed hereunder acts of or acts which constitute or simulate:
a.
Sexual intercourse, masturbation, sodomy, bestiality, oral copulation, flagellation, or any sexual acts which are prohibited by law;
b.
The touching, caressing, or fondling of the breast, buttocks, anus, or genitals; or
c.
The displaying or any portion of the female breast below the top of the areola or the displaying or any portion of any person's pubic hair, anus, cleft of the buttocks, vulva, or genitals.
(2)
No person shall use on licensed premises artificial devices or inanimate objects to perform, simulate, or depict any of the prohibited conduct or activities described in subsection (1).
(3)
It shall be unlawful for any person to show, display, or exhibit, on licensed premises, any film, still picture, electronic reproduction, or any other visual reproduction or image of any act or conduct described in subsection (1) or (2) of this section.
(4)
No erotic entertainment/dance establishment business shall serve, sell distribute or offer for consumption or possession any alcoholic beverages or controlled substances on the premises.
(Ord. of 10-27-2009)

DIVISION 2. - LICENSE

Sec. 6-61. - Required.
No person shall engage in the business of wholesaling, retailing or otherwise providing malt beverages and wines of any kind in the unincorporated areas of the county without first obtaining a license therefore under this division.
(Ord. of 10-27-2009)
Sec. 6-62. - Classification.
Only the following classes of licenses shall be authorized under this division:
(1)
[License required.] It shall be unlawful for any person to sell or offer for sale, at retail or wholesale, within Stephens County any malt beverage or wine, without having a license issued under the provisions of this division or in violation of the terms of such license or this division. The sale of malt beverages or wine within Stephens County is declared to be a privilege and not a right and such privilege shall not be exercised except as licensed hereunder. It shall be unlawful for any person to manufacture, sell or offer for sale, at retail or wholesale, within Stephens County any alcoholic beverage not specifically licensed hereby, except for those sales authorized by the ordinance regulating the sale of malt beverages and wine at private clubs. It shall be unlawful for any person to bring an alcoholic beverage of any kind onto the premises of any restaurant or similar eating facility or to permit same within Stephens County except as provided for hereunder.
(2)
Classes: The licenses shall be divided into the following classes:
a.
Class B-1. Wholesale malt beverages, which shall permit only the sale of malt beverages at wholesale plus application fee;
b.
Class B-2. Retail malt beverages by the package in food stores, grocery stores, supermarkets and convenience food stores, which shall permit the sale of malt beverages by the package as an item incidental to the sale of food and groceries plus application fee;
c.
Class B-3. Retail malt beverages by the drink, which may only be issued for a restaurant, golf courses and private clubs, as defined herein plus application fee;
d.
Class C-1. Wholesale wine, which shall permit only the sale of wine at wholesale plus application fee;
e.
Class C-2. Retail wine by the package in food stores, grocery stores, supermarkets and convenience food stores, which shall permit the sale of wine by the package as an item incidental to the sale of food and groceries plus application fee;
f.
Class C-3. Retail wine by the drink, which may only be issued for a restaurant, golf courses and private clubs, as defined herein plus application fee.
(3)
Fees. The basic fees for each type of license listed in subsection (2) hereinabove shall be as follow:

Class B-3 350.00
Class C-1 100.00
Class C-2 300.00

  (4)
[Fees set by resolution.] The board will periodically set a nonrefundable application and/or renewal fee by resolution. License fees shall be such as are specified from time to time by resolution adopted by the board of commissioners. License fees shall be payable in advance for the entire year, beginning January 1 and ending December 31, of each year. The payment of license fees shall be a prerequisite to the issuance of a license. License fees shall not be prorated for a part of a year, but the full license fee shall be payable for any part of a year. The suspension or revocation of any license granted pursuant to this article shall not entitle the licensee to a return of any portion of the license fee.
(5)
[Modification of license.] In the event a licensee desires to modify or amend the license held by such licensee, a new application shall be made with such information as the Clerk may require to reasonably facilitating the evaluation of such modification accompanied by basic class fees and application fees.

Sec. 6-63. - Applications.
(a)
All applications for licenses to sell malt beverages and wine shall be made in person by the applicant to the clerk of the board of commissioners in writing on forms furnished by the clerk. The application shall contain, but is not restricted to, the following information:
(1)
Name;
(2)
Age;
(3)
Address;
(4)
Length of residency in the county of the applicant;
(5)
Name of business;
(6)
Location of business;
(7)
Description of the premises or place of business where the license is to be effective;
(8)
Statement as to whether the applicant or any person with any interest in the applicant has made an application previously for any malt beverage or wine license and the disposition of such application;
(9)
Whether the applicant or any person with any interest in the applicant has ever been convicted of a crime other than traffic violations;
(10)
Whether a previous license issued to the applicant or any person with any interest in the applicant has been revoked by any state or subdivision thereof or by the federal government and the reason for such revocation;
(11)
Whether any other person is interested directly or indirectly in the profits or losses of the proposed business.
(12)

Every application for a license shall comply with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2(c) concerning fingerprints of the applicant.

Sec. 6-64. - Qualifications and conditions of license holders.
The following qualifications shall apply to applicants for a license under this division:
(1)
All applicants for license shall be at least 21 years of age.
(2)
Every applicant for a license under this chapter shall make written application to the Stephens County Clerk on forms furnished by the commissioners. The applicant shall answer all questions on the application, under oath, and shall supply all information and furnish all certificates, affidavits, bonds and other supporting data documents as required by this chapter.
(3)
No license shall be granted under this chapter to any applicant who is not a citizen or resident legal alien of the United States.
(4)
It shall be unlawful for any county employee, or his spouse or minor child directly involved in the issuance or alcoholic beverage licenses under this chapter to have any whole, particle [partial], or beneficial interest in any license to sell alcoholic beverages in the county.
(5)
Except as otherwise specifically provided for by law, it is unlawful for any person to sell, possess, conceal, store, or convey any alcoholic beverage on which any tax or license fee imposed by this title [chapter] has not been paid.
(6)
All owners who are residents of the county shall make application for a license in their own name. Where the owner is a corporation, partnership, association, or nonresident, the application shall be made in the name of the resident officer, partner or associate who owns a substantial interest in the business or in the name of the principal resident, managing officer, or managing agent. In such cases, the application shall show that the license is for the use of the owner who shall be named in the application.
(7)
If the alcoholic beverage license applicant is not a legal resident of Stephens County, Georgia, the applicant must designate one or more residents of Stephens County as a managing officer or managing agent. In addition the managing officer or managing agent must be a lawful citizen of the United States or a legal resident alien.
(8)
Where an application for a license is submitted for a nonprofit private club or a veteran's organization, the application shall be made in the name of the highest elected officer and/or the manager of the club or organization.
(9)
When contrary to the public interest and welfare, no license shall be issued by the board of commissioner to an applicant under the following circumstances:
a.
The applicant has been convicted or pled guilty to any federal, state or local law classified as a felony involving moral turpitude within ten years immediately preceding the filing of the application for license;
b.
The applicant has been convicted or pled guilty to any federal, state or local law classified as a felony not involving moral turpitude;
c.
The applicant was convicted or pled guilty to any federal, state or local law classified as a misdemeanor, including, but not limited to, laws involving alcoholic beverages, malt beverages, wine, drugs, pornography, gambling, or tax law violations, if such conviction or plea tends to indicate that the applicant will not operate the business for which he is seeking a license in conformance with federal, state, or local laws, rules, regulations, or resolutions;
d.
The applicant is an official or employee of the county; or
e.
The applicant is a person, firm, partnership, association, or corporation where any manager, partner, officer, or director thereof has been convicted or pled guilty to any felony or lesser offense involving moral turpitude.
(10)
Any licensee may not sell alcoholic beverages hereunder unless there is on duty at the licensed location at the time of such sale a person of at least 21 years of age who is in charge of that location at the time. Such on-duty personnel shall be present at the premises except for job-related absences not exceeding one hour.

The applicant for a retail package license for the sale of malt beverages or wine shall pay to the clerk of the board of commissioners at the time of application the sum of $300.00 as an annual license fee, plus application fee for operation for one year at one location or one business where the product will be sold.
(b)

(c)
Any wholesale dealer in malt beverages or wines licensed by the state or the agent of such wholesale dealer will be granted a license upon payment of $100.00 annually for malt beverages, and $100.00 annually for wine plus the application fee; the distribution to be made outside the municipalities in the county. Each application for a license under this division shall be accompanied by a certified check or cash for the full amount of the license fee. The license fees required by this section shall not be prorated for applicants whose businesses operate for less than one year. No refund shall be made to license holders who have their licenses revoked or suspended by the board of commissioners.

(a)
After filing the application with the clerk of the board of commissioners, the applicant for a license under this division shall publish a notice of the application in a local newspaper in the county at least four times within the four weeks prior to the regular board of commissioners meeting at which the application is to be presented and considered. The notice shall contain the name of the person applying for the license and the name of the person for whom the application is submitted, if different, and the location of the proposed premises. An affidavit of the publisher or his authorized agent certifying that the advertisement has been published shall accompany each application.

The application may be reviewed and considered by the board of commissioners at the next regularly scheduled board of commissioners meeting following the necessary publication of the application. Wholesale, retail and consumption on the premises licenses for the sale of malt beverages and wine outside municipalities in the county will be issued by the board of commissioners pursuant to the rules and regulations set forth in this division. The holding of any licenses under this division is declared to be a privilege and not a right.
(Ord. of 10-27-2009)
Sec. 6-68. - Renewal and transfer.
(a)
A licensee under this division who desires to renew his license for a subsequent calendar year must file a renewal application with the clerk of the board of commissioners on or before November 15 of the preceding year. Payment of license fees for renewal shall be made at the time of application and, upon approval of the renewal application by the board of commissioners, the license will be issued. Applications for renewal shall be treated as applications for new licenses but need not be advertised and/or resurveyed. An application for renewal of an existing license may be acted upon by the board of commissioners at the first regularly scheduled meeting following the filing of the renewal application.
(b)
No alcoholic beverage license shall be transferred from one person to another in which the license was obtained, except in the case of the death of a person holding a license, in which event his executor or administrator may continue to operate under the license for six months or the balance of the year from the date of his qualification.
(c)
Each license is issued for a specific location only and may not be transferred to another location without making a new application.
(d)
Upon the death of any license holder, his personal representative or heirs may continue to operate under the original license for the balance of the year without payment of any fee, provided those persons are otherwise qualified as license holders. Otherwise the license may be transferred to a qualified transferee within 30 days after the condition of the estate of the deceased licensee will permit. In such case, the transferee shall pay a transfer fee equal to one-half of the original license fee.
(e)
Any licensee desiring to discontinue business at one location and commence business at another location must make a completely new application to transfer the license to the new location.
(Ord. of 10-27-2009)
Sec. 6-69. - Building, location and distance requirements.
(a)
No license under this division shall be issued for any location that is in violation of O.C.G.A. § 3-3-21. No license shall be issued for a location not suitable in the judgment and discretion of the board of commissioners because of traffic congestion, general character of the neighborhood, or because of the effect, which such an establishment would have on the adjacent and surrounding properties and neighborhood.
(b)
No license shall be issued for a location within an area where in the judgment and discretion of the board of commissioners, the number of malt beverage or wine licenses already granted makes it contrary to the public interest or welfare.
(c)
No license shall be issued for a location at which the operation of such a licensed business would violate any zoning rules and regulations of the county as now or hereafter adopted or amended.
(d)
No license shall be issued for a location where a previous license has been revoked or suspended, and where, in the judgment of the board of commissioners, the problems which have arisen from the operation of a licensed business at such a location indicate that it is not in the interest of public health, safety, welfare, or morals that the sale of malt beverages or wines be permitted at such a location.
(e)
No license shall be issued for a location where it would violate state or local law, regulations or resolutions.
(f)
No license for retail sales for consumption on the premises shall be issued under this division for a location where the nearest point of the main structure of the business is located within 300 feet of the nearest point of the main structure of any school building, school grounds where school children congregate or instruction occurs or church as measured in a straight line between the two structures. The term "school" shall include kindergartens, primary and secondary schools, colleges and other educational institutions whether public or private. This provision shall not apply where it would prohibit the renewal of an alcoholic beverage license for the sale of malt beverages and wine at a location where a license holder had been granted a license under the terms of any prior county resolution relating to the sale of malt beverages and wines. If a school or church subsequently locates within 300 feet of a licensed premise, this provision shall not be applicable under such circumstances and such an event would not cause a license holder to be in violation of this provision or prohibit the renewal of a license. In such cases, the same license holder for the same place of business may renew the license as long as all of the other provisions of this division are complied with.
(g)
No license shall be issued for any location in which the licensed premise contains a drive-in window for the sale of malt beverages and wine.
(h)
The licensed premises shall be subject to inspection by authorized personnel of the county during regular business hours.
(i)
Consumption on the premises licenses shall be issued only to restaurants, nonprofit private clubs and veteran organizations. Retail package licenses shall not be issued to restaurants, nonprofit private clubs and veteran organizations.
(j)
No person knowingly and intentionally may sell or offer to sell any malt beverage or wine for consumption on the premises within 100 yards of any housing authority property.
(k)
As used in this section, the term "school building" or "educational building' shall apply only to state, county, city, or church school buildings and to such building at such other schools in which are taught subjects commonly taught in the common schools and colleges of this state and which are public schools and private schools as defined in O.C.G.A. § 20-2-690(b). The term "school building" includes only those structures in which instruction is offered.
(l)
The term "church building" as used in this section shall mean the main structure used by any religious organization for purposes or worship.
(m)
As to any location in the future, if the distance requirements in this section are met at the time of issuance of any license, the subsequent opening and operation of a church or school within the distance prohibited in this section shall not prevent the continuance of an existing license or the issuance of a new license to any subsequent owner of such property. The distance requirements herein shall not apply at any location for which a new license is applied for, if the sale of alcoholic beverages was lawful at such location at any time during the 12 months immediately preceding such application.
(n)
For the purposes of this section, distance shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground and shall be measured in the following manner:
(1)
From the nearest point of the main structure of the business regulated under this title; and
(2)
To the nearest point of the main structure of any school building, school grounds where children congregate or instruction occurs, college campus or church; [and]
(3)
Along a straight line which describes the shortest distance between the two property lines.

Sec. 6-72. - Revocation or suspension.
The following shall be grounds for the suspension or revocation of any alcoholic beverage license issued under this division. The existence of such grounds shall be determined by the board of commissioners in its sole discretion:
(1)
Violation by the licensee of any state or federal law or regulation, except misdemeanors, or of any resolution or ordinance of county, other than traffic violations.
(2)
The failure of the licensee or his employees to report promptly to the sheriffs department any violation of law, regulation, county resolution or breach of the peace, disturbance, or altercation occurring on the premises
(3)
Any conduct on the part of the licensee or his employees contrary to the public welfare, safety, health or morals.
(4)
Operating or conducting a business in a manner contrary to the public welfare, safety, health or morals or in such a manner as to constitute a nuisance.
(5)
The violation of any state or federal law or regulation, municipal ordinance, or county resolution or ordinance pertaining to alcoholic and malt beverages and wines. This ground specifically includes any violation of any provision of this article
(6)
The violation by the licensee of the Georgia Controlled Substances Act (O.C.G.A. § 16-13-20 et seq.).
(7)
The violation by the licensee of any federal, state or local law prohibiting the sale of pornographic materials.
(8)
Failure of the licensee to furnish to the clerk of the board of commissioners any report required by this division or any report requested in writing by the clerk.
(9)
Allowing a person to work in the licensed business who had an alcoholic beverage license revoked by any jurisdiction for violation of its laws or rules within a period of five years immediately preceding the filing of an application for a license under this division.
(10)
Allowing a person to work in the licensed business that has been convicted of or pled guilty to any federal, state or local law classified as a felony not involving moral turpitude.
(11)
Allowing a person to work in the licensed business who has violated the provisions of this chapter or any law of the United Sates or any state law pertaining to the sale of malt beverages, wines or alcoholic beverages within the past five years from the date of conviction.
(12)
Allowing a person to work in the licensed business who has violated the provisions of this chapter or any law of the United States or any state law pertaining to the sale of malt beverages, wines, or alcoholic beverages within the past five years from the date of conviction.
(13)
It shall be unlawful for any person employed or working in any capacity in any licensed premises to solicit or encourage patrons to purchase drinks to be consumed by or otherwise disposed of by any such person so employed or working (O.C.G.A. § 3-3-42).
(14)
The following factors shall be considered on any revocation or suspension as set out above:
a.
Consistency of penalties mandated by this chapter and those set by Stephens County;
b.
Impact of the offense on the community;
c.
The cost to Stephens County related to compliance or enforcement efforts undertaken by Stephens County in pursuing the case; and
d.
The cost incurred by Stephens County in order to mitigate a condition remaining as the result of the violation;
(15)
Any person who violates any provision of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor of a high and aggravated nature.

Sec. 6-74. - Hearings.
(a)
A license holder under this division shall be entitled to a hearing by the board of commissioners concerning any alleged violation, which could lead to revocation or suspension of the license. The decision to revoke or suspend any license shall be in the discretion of the Stephens County Board of Commissioners.
(b)
Upon the report to the board of commissioners of any violation of this chapter by the license holder, the clerk of the board of commissioners shall give notice to the license holder in writing to show cause before the board of commissioners why the license should not be revoked or suspended.
(c)
A written notice from the clerk shall specify the time and place for the hearing which shall be not less than three days or more than 14 days from the date of service of the notice.
(d)
At the hearing the licensee shall have the opportunity to show cause why the license should not be suspended or revoked.
(e)
The board of commissioners shall hear relevant evidence as to the alleged violation of this chapter.
(f)
The hearing need not be at a regular meeting of the board of commissioners but may be at such a time and place as shall be fixed in the notice.
(g)
The decision of the board of commissioners shall be by majority vote of the members present at the hearing; provided, however, two members must concur to pass any order.
(h)
Within three days from the date of the hearing, the board of commissioners shall render a decision in writing and furnish a copy of the decision to the licensee. A decision of the board shall be effective immediately, whether or not the licensee can be personally served with a written copy of the decision.
(i)
Except as otherwise provided in this division, a licensee who has had his license revoked may reapply for a license after 30 days have passed from the effective date of the revocation. Such a reapplication shall conform with all procedural requirements applicable to a new application. The board of commissioners shall consider all matters relevant to the application, including the previous revocation.
(j)
No applicant may apply for a license during any period in which an existing license is under suspension.

ARTICLE III. - FARM WINERIES
PURPOSE
A farm winery may sell its wine and the wine of any other state farm winery licenses at retail in a tasting room or other facility on the premises of the winery for consumption on the premises and in closed packages for consumption off the premises and to sell its wine and the wine of any other state farm winery licenses at retail for consumption on the premises and in closed packages for consumption off the premises in tasting rooms at a location within the county that is one of the five additional locations in the state authorized by O.C.G.A. § 3-6-21.1(b), but only if the annual production of wine by the farm winery is made in the state in accordance with the source of berries, fruits or grapes as provided by the laws of the state and the rules and regulations of the state department of agriculture.
(Ord. of 2-28-2012)
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY AND LICENSE

Sec. 6-75. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Board of commissioners means the Stephens County Board of Commissioners.
Commissioner means the commissioner of the department of revenue.
Distributor means a person or wholesaler who sells alcoholic beverage to retailers.
Farm winery means a winery which makes at least 40 percent of its annual production from agricultural produce grown in the state where the winery is located and is located on premises, a substantial portion of which is used for agricultural purposes, including the cultivation of grapes, berries, or fruits to be utilized in the manufacture or production of wine by the winery; or is owned and operated by persons who are engaged in the production of a substantial portion of the agricultural produce used in its annual production.
Fortified wine means any alcoholic beverage containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added.
Georgia farm winery means a farm winery which is licensed by the commissioner to manufacture wine in Georgia.
Manufacturer means any maker, producer, or bottler of an alcoholic beverage, in the case of wine, any vintner.
Package means a bottle, can, keg, barrel or other original consumer container.
Retail sales of wine means that each license for retail sales of wine and malt beverages consumption on the premises shall require that all consumption shall be at the farm winery site for which said license is issued. This shall include wedding, dining facilities, and festivals associated with the winery.
Tasting room means an outlet for the promotion of a farm winery's wine providing samples of such wine to the public and for the sale of such wine at retail for consumption on the premises and for sale in closed packages for consumption off the premises. Samples of wine can be given free of charge or for a fee.
Vintner means a winemaker or merchant who produces or makes wine from grapes.
Wholesaler means a farm winery shall be granted a wholesale license for sale and distribution as provided by O.C.G.A. § 3-6-21.1.
(Ord. of 2-28-2012)
Sec. 6-76. - License required.
(a) No entity shall engage in the business of a farm winery of any kind in the unincorporated area of the county without first obtaining a license therefore under this chapter and a license to operate a farm winery issued by the commissioner. It shall also be unlawful for any entity to sell or make deliveries beyond the boundaries of the premises covered by the license except at tasting rooms licensed as provided herein and by the commissioner.
(1)
Certain alcoholic beverages may be sold by a farm winery in the unincorporated area of the county under a license granted by the board of commissioners upon terms and conditions provided in this chapter and by state law.
(2)
All licenses in this chapter shall be a mere grant of privilege to carry on the business during the term of the license, subject to all terms and conditions imposed by this Code and the state law.
(3)
All licenses pursuant to this chapter shall have printed on the front these words: "This license is a mere privilege subject to be revoked and annulled and is subject to any further ordinances which may be enacted."
(4)
Any holder of a license for a farm winery issued pursuant to this chapter is required to apply for and obtain a farm winery license from the state before any sales commence. Additionally, county licensees are required to abide by all applicable state regulations and laws.
(b)
A farm winery which is qualified and licensed by the state shall be allowed to manufacture wine or to distribute such wine at wholesale and at retail at its tasting room, provided that the farm winery has filed an application with copies of its license and permits from the state, along with the license fees and other requirements as hereinafter set out. A farm winery may also sell at retail in packages or for consumption on the premises or at additional licensed tasting rooms wine manufactured by other wineries.
(c)
Retail sales of wine and malt beverages for consumption on the premises licenses shall require that all consumption be at the farm winery site or approved tasting rooms, which includes wedding or dining facilities associated with the farm winery.
(d)
In addition to the license for the farm winery operation, the board of commissioners may, upon application, issue additional licenses to the farm winery as to malt beverages as follows:
(1)
Retail package dealer who sells malt beverages, including but not limited to beer, lager and stout, in unbroken packages at retail only and not for resale.
(2)
Retail consumption dealer who sells malt beverages, including but not limited to beer, lager and stout, for consumption on the premises to consumers and not for resale.
(3)
The farm winery must make an application for the sale of malt beverages but shall not be subject to any location restrictions, any of the required sales ratios, or to any other restriction.
(e)
The board of commissioners may also issue a farm winery license for additional tasting rooms on or off the primary premises if the additional tasting room(s) are owned or leased by the primary farm winery owner and said rooms have been licensed by the commissioner.
(Ord. of 2-28-2012)
Sec. 6-77. - Applications.
(a)
All applications for a farm winery license shall be made in person by the applicant to the clerk of the board of commissioners in writing on forms furnished by the clerk. The application shall contain, but is not restricted to, the following information:
(1)
Name;
(2)
Age;
(3)
Address;
(4)
Length of residency in the county of the applicant;
(5)
Name of business;
(6)
Location of business;
(7)
Description of the premises or place of business where the license is to be effective;
(8)
Statement as to whether the applicant or any person with any interest in the applicant has made an application previously for any malt beverage or wine license and the disposition of such application;
(9)
Whether the applicant or any person with any interest in the applicant has ever been convicted of a crime other than traffic violations;
(10)
Whether a previous license issued to the applicant or any person with any interest in the applicant has been revoked by any state or subdivision thereof or by the federal government and the reason for such revocation;
(11)
Whether any other person is interested directly or indirectly in the profits or losses of the proposed business.
(12)
Proof that all state and local taxes for any real or personal property where the farm winery will be located are current.
(b)
Each applicant for a wine license shall attach to his application evidence of ownership of the business premises or a copy of the current lease for the premises upon which license is to be effective.
(c)
Every application for a license shall be accompanied by a scale drawing showing the nearest church, funeral chapel, school and college or shall have attached thereto an affidavit of a registered surveyor that the proposed location of the licensed premises complies with the distance requirements of this division and any state or federal laws.
(d)
Every application for a license shall comply with the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 3-3-2 (c) concerning fingerprints of the applicant.
(Ord. of 2-28-2012)
Sec. 6-78. - License fees.
(a)
The license of the primary farm winery site shall cost $950.00, which shall cover the operation of the farm winery under the state farm winery laws and county application fee in addition:
(1)
To sell wine or malt beverages, including but not limited to beer, lager and stout, in unbroken packages on the farm winery premises at retail and not for resale, which license shall be at no additional fee;
(2)
To sell wine or malt beverages, including but not limited to beer, lager and stout, for consumption on the farm winery premises to consumers, which license shall be an at no additional fee;
(3)
For each additional tasting room location on the primary farm winery premises, which license shall be at no additional fee;
(4)
For each additional tasting room location off the primary farm winery premises that is owned or leased by the licensed entity and which is located off of the primary farm winery premises, which license shall be at no additional fee;
(5)
For the wholesaler who sells beer, malt beverages or wine to a farm winery and whose principal place of business is located in the county, which license shall be at no additional fee.
(b)
The board shall not prorate the annual license fee for partial year issuance.
(Ord. of 2-28-2012)
Sec. 6-79. - Days and hours of sale.
Farm wineries operate during such days and hours as provided by the Farm Wineries Act, as amended, including but not limited to O.C.G.A. § 3-6-21.2.
(Ord. of 2-28-2012)
Sec. 6-80. - Routes and signage.
Farm wineries use routes and signage in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 3-6-21.4.

Sec. 6-81. - License categories.
(a)
The alcoholic beverage license issuable under article V of this chapter are:
(1)
Farm wineries;
(2)
Tasting rooms of farm wineries;
(3)
Retail package dealer who sells malt beverages, including but not limited to beer, lager and stout, in unbroken packages at retail only and not for resale;
(4)
Retail consumption dealer who sells malt beverages including, but not limited to, beer, lager and stout, for consumption on the premises to consumers and not for resale;
(b)
The board of commissioners shall not issue an alcoholic beverage license for the sale of distilled spirits including, but not limited to, fortified wine to a farm winery.
(Ord. of 2-28-2012)
Sec. 6-82. - Revocation of state license.
(a)
When the commissioner revokes any state farm winery license issued by the state, permission by the county shall automatically become invalid.
(b)
A copy of the current state license shall accompany all application and renewals of licenses. Failure to present a valid copy of a current and valid state license will result in a refusal to issue or renew the license. Timely application for renewal is determined by the date on which a complete and proper application has been submitted.
(c)
When the state revokes an alcoholic beverage permit or license issued under this chapter, the board of commissioners shall automatically revoke the county license without any action.
(Ord. of 2-28-2012)
Sec. 6-83. - Excise tax.
An excise tax is hereby levied to be computed at the rate of $0.22 per liter and a proportionate tax at the same rate on all fractional parts of a liter on the first sale or use of the wine by the package that shall be paid to the county. Such tax shall be paid to the designee of the county board of commissioners by the licensee on wine sold in accordance with the terms hereof in the county. It shall be unlawful and a violation of this chapter for any licensee in accordance with the terms hereof to sell or deliver wine in any manner without collecting said tax.
(Ord. of 2-28-2012)
ARTICLE IV. - TEMPORARY SPECIAL EVENT LICENSE
Purpose
License may be issued to any person who meets the qualifications for a period not to exceed ten total days in any one year for an approved special event. To qualify for a temporary special event license an application must be submitted and the applicant must pay the fee as required in the county fee schedule and shall be required to comply with all the general ordinances, local, state and federal laws. Each individual special event shall not exceed three days.
DIVISION 1. - TEMPORARY SPECIAL EVENTS

Sec. 6-84. - Time limit of temporary license; criteria.
(a)
The special event must meet the following criteria prior to the issuance of a license to sell alcoholic beverages:
(1)
All applicants for license shall be at least 21 years of age.
(2)
The location at which the special event is to take place must be approved in writing by the owner of the property.
(3)
Any special event license issued shall be non-transferrable.
(4)
Applicants must comply with all local, state and federal laws governing the sale and consumption of alcoholic beverages.
(b)
As a condition on the issuance of a temporary special event license, the licensee shall indemnify and hold the county harmless from claims, demand or cause of action which may arise from activities associated with the special event.
(c)
The applicant seeking a temporary special event license must meet all requirements of chapter 6 of the Code of Stephens County, Georgia, except those altered by this section or those not applicable to a temporary special event.
(Ord. of 12-12-2017)
Sec. 6-85. - License fees.
A fee of $50.00 per day will be paid to the county clerk of the Stephens County Board of Commissioners.
