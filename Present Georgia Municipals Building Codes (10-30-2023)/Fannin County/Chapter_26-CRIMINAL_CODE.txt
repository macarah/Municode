Chapter 26 - CRIMINAL CODE
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Secs. 26-1—26-18. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - INDIGENT DEFENSE SERVICES

Sec. 26-19. - Submission of application.
Each person lacking the financial ability to provide for themselves representation in his/her defense in criminal proceedings in the state court, magistrate court or probate court of Fannin County, and desiring that the county provide such services to him/her, shall submit an application to the county requesting indigent defense representation and setting the specifics supporting their needs for such indigent representation. The application shall be submitted to the magistrate on a form approved by the board of commissioners of Fannin County.
(Ord. of 2-22-2005, § 1)
Sec. 26-20. - Application fee.
Each applicant for indigent defense services shall pay an application fee of $50.00 to the clerk of the court in which indigent representation is being sought. Such fee must be paid either at the time the application is submitted or at the time the representation is provided.
(Ord. of 2-22-2005, § 2)
Sec. 26-21. - Waiver of fee.
The fee required by section 26-20 may be waived only upon order of the presiding judge scheduled to hear the case in which the applicant is seeking a waiver of the application fee. The judge may order a waiver of the fee only upon a finding by the court that the defendant is unable to pay the fee or that paying the fee would cause hardship to the defendant.
(Ord. of 2-22-2005, § 3)
Sec. 26-22. - Transfer of funds to county.
Within ten days after the end of each month, the clerk of the court in receipt of the application fees required by section 26-20 shall remit the total of all application fees collected during the previous month to the board of commissioners of the county.
(Ord. of 2-22-2005, § 4)
Sec. 26-23. - Funds to be kept in separate account.
The funds remitted to the board of commissioners pursuant to section 26-22, shall be maintained in a separate fund for indigent defense services and shall be expended only for the purpose of providing legal representation to indigent defendants in the state court, magistrate court and probate court of Fannin County.
(Ord. of 2-22-2005, § 5)
