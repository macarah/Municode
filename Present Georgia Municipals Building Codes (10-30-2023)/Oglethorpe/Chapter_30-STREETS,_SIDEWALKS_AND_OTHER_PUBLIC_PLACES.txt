ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 30-1. - Littering prohibited.
No person shall place any garbage, rubbish, trash, paper, or any litter of any kind whatsoever upon any street, sidewalk, alley, park, lane, or other public grounds of the city.
(Code 1986, § 17-1)
State Law reference— Littering, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-40 et seq.
Sec. 30-2. - Obstructing ditches, drains.
It shall be unlawful for any person to fill up any ditch or drain in any of the streets or alleys of the city or to purposely place any obstruction therein to prevent the free flow of water.
(Code 1986, § 17-2)
Sec. 30-3. - Obstructing streets, sidewalks.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person, including a merchants, to convert any public street, sidewalk, or alley in the city to his use, or place any obstruction in or upon any street, sidewalk, or alley, such as, wood, barrels and boxes, merchandise, timber, or lumber; except necessary for building purposes. Such obstruction shall be removed by the police or street department at the expense of the owner thereof or the person causing said obstruction. This section shall apply to goods, wares, merchandise, produce, boxes, barrels, soft drink boxes and machines, ice machines and refrigerators, and any other item placed on the streets or sidewalks by the owners or occupants of any building in the city. This section and the prohibitions contained herein shall not be limited to only those items listed in this section, but shall apply to any and all obstructions, regardless of their nature, except those expressly relieved of said restriction.
(b)
Any person found guilty in municipal court shall be given a reasonable amount of time to remove the obstructions. If said obstructions are not removed in said time period, the offender shall be guilty of an offense.
(Code 1986, § 17-3)
Sec. 30-4. - Obstruction to vision at intersections.
It shall be unlawful for the owner or person in charge of any lot, parcel of land within the city to allow any obstruction to vision (in the triangle formed by the lines of two intersecting streets or a street and any alley and a line joining points of such lines 25 feet distant from its point of intersection) by permitting any vegetation to grow or be maintained between the heights of three feet and ten feet above the grade of the centerline of the intersection, or by constructing or maintaining any fence or other structure which constitutes an obstruction to view within the triangle.
(Code 1986, § 17-4)
Sec. 30-5. - Changing street name signs without authority.
It shall be unlawful for any person to place or cause to be placed any sign designating a street, avenue or other public place by a different name than that by which it is generally and legally known, or to refuse to remove the same from their property when requested to do so by an officer of the city.
(Code 1986, § 17-5)
Sec. 30-6. - Temporary sign ordinance.
Temporary signs including but not limited to paper, vinyl or corrugated plastic (Coroplast™) placed on or near city rights of way and used for commercial advertising by businesses which may or may not already operate within the City of Oglethorpe, but whose home office is located elsewhere must purchase a permit for placement within the city limits. This permit shall require a nonrefundable payment of $250.00 which shall be good for a period of 14 days, after which such permitted temporary signs must be removed. Any signs placed without permission or left in place longer than 14 days will be removed and discarded. Payment must be made within five days following notification for signs placed without permission.
Political signs of the same type materials and construction described above shall be exempt from the permit process, however placement of such signs shall not take place in excess of 60 days prior to an election. Any signs left in place longer than seven days after such election will be removed and discarded.
Commercial signs of the same type materials and construction described above for businesses in the downtown Oglethorpe area shall be exempt from the permit process, however such signs may be placed for a period not to exceed seven calendar days per 30-day period. Any signs left in place for longer will be removed and discarded.
Folding sidewalk signs (sandwich board, A-frame, etc.) are exempt from the permit process, but shall be limited to one sign per business, either single or double sided, but can be placed only in front of the business for which the advertisement is directly associated and only during business hours. Corrugated plastic stake signs can be used for identifying licensed, home-based businesses for a maximum of one year, after which they must be removed or replaced with permanent signage.
Signs of any type posted on utility poles are strictly prohibited and will be promptly removed and discarded.
(Ord. of 11-16-2021)
Secs. 30-7—30-28. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - EXCAVATIONS

Sec. 30-29. - Definitions.
The term "public place," as employed in this article, shall be construed to mean a street, alley, sidewalk, or other public property owned by the city or under its supervision.
(Code 1986, § 17-12)
Sec. 30-30. - Permit to excavate.
(a)
Required. No person shall dig or excavate in any public place within the city or install, repair, or replace any of his property or equipment in such public place, or do any other work in or on any public place, which requires the removal or breaking of the surface or under part of any such public place, unless and until such person shall have first secured, in the manner provided in this article, permission to do so. If any person shall do any of the acts referred to in this section without first having secured permission to do so in the manner provided in this article, he shall, upon conviction thereof, be punished as provided in section 1-7.
(b)
Application. Each person desiring permission to dig or excavate in any public place shall file with the city clerk a written or printed application, setting forth:
(1)
The character and location of such work to be done in any such public place;
(2)
The probable number of square yards of surface to be removed; and
(3)
The probable date of beginning and the completion of the work.
(c)
Fees and deposits. In the event the applicant for the permit to excavate is qualified to perform the work himself, including replacing the street to its condition prior to the excavation, there shall be no charge for the issuance of the permit. If the city must fill and resurface the street or public way, the applicant shall deposit with the city, at the time of application, a sum of money which shall be fixed by the city engineer and shall be based on the cost per square yard of replacement, plus ten percent.
(d)
Grant or refusal. Each application, as referred to in this section, shall be delivered by the city clerk to the mayor and council, who shall without unreasonable delay either grant or reject such application. Upon rejection of the application, the applicant shall have the right to present the matter to the mayor and council, the order of which shall be final in such matter, and upon final rejection, the deposit of money referred to in this section shall be returned to the person who deposited the same.
(Code 1986, §§ 17-13—17-16)
Sec. 30-31. - Failure to complete or restore.
Upon the failure of any person, whose application to dig or excavate shall have been granted and who shall have begun the work:
(1)
To complete the same without delay;
(2)
Upon the completion of such work, to restore the public place to the same or as good a condition as the same was before such work was done; or
(3)
To comply with any reasonable rules or regulations of the mayor and council from time to time given to such person for the completion and restoration of such public place;
such person shall upon conviction, in addition to other penalties, forfeit to the city any deposit of money so made with the city clerk.
(Code 1986, § 17-17)
Sec. 30-32. - Restoration by city.
Upon the failure of any person to make such repairs as herein required, the mayor and council is empowered and authorized to cause such repairs to be made and such public place to be restored to its original condition, and the cost thereof, plus ten percent thereof, shall be charged to and paid by such person who so failed to make such repairs. Such sum of money shall constitute and be a debt due from such person to the city.
(Code 1986, § 17-18)
Sec. 30-33. - Use or refund of deposit.
The deposit of money made under section 30-30 shall stand as a guarantee and may be employed by the city for the replacement of such public place in as good condition as the same was immediately before the excavation or digging, and upon default of the person so making such deposit to restore immediately the public place to as good condition as the same was immediately before the excavation or digging was done by him, the mayor and council may employ the whole or any part of such deposit of money necessary for such restoration. Upon the restoration of the public place to such conditions the same was immediately before such digging or excavating, the city clerk shall, upon the certificate of the mayor and council, restore to the person so making such deposit such sum of money so deposited, or in case any part thereof is employed by the city for such restoration of such public place, upon such certificate, the city clerk shall refund such part of such sum of money remaining.
(Code 1986, § 17-19)
Sec. 30-34. - Prompt work.
All digging or excavating to be done, as provided in this article, shall be promptly started and completed without delay after permission shall have been granted. Such public place shall be, immediately upon the completion of such work, restored to the same or as good condition as the same was before such work was done thereon. Such work or restoration shall be at all times and in all respects in compliance with the orders and supervision of the mayor and council and from time to time given to such person for such purpose.
(Code 1986, § 17-20)
Sec. 30-35. - Warning devices; posting permit.
Upon the granting of an application for permission to dig or excavate in any public place and in all other cases provided for in this article, it shall be the duty of the applicant, before beginning any such work, to enclose the place where such work is being done or otherwise afford adequate protection to street traffic. He shall post and keep posted at such place a placard showing that the work is being done under a permit granted by the city, with sufficient other information thereon to give ample warning. In addition thereto, he shall place sufficient markings at night, by a red light, to notify those having the right to use the same that such public place, or part thereof, is closed.
(Code 1986, § 17-21)
Sec. 30-36. - Decision of mayor and council final.
Any controversy between the person making deposit in order to dig or excavate in any public place and the city clerk or city engineer, respecting any of the duties to be performed by any of them, may be submitted to the mayor and council by any of the parties. When so submitted, the action of the mayor and council thereon shall be binding on all parties.
(Code 1986, § 17-22)
Secs. 30-37—30-60. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - TREES

Sec. 30-61. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Park tree mean trees, shrubs, bushes, and all other woody vegetation in public parks having individual names, and all areas owned by the city, or to which the public has free access as a park.
Street tree means trees, shrubs, bushes, and all other woody vegetation on land lying between property lines on either side of all streets, avenues, or ways within the city.
(Ord. of 11-2-1998, § 1)
Sec. 30-62. - City tree board.
(a)
Creation and establishment. There is hereby created and established a city tree board which shall consist of five members, citizens and residents of this city, who shall be appointed by the mayor with the approval of the council.
(b)
Term of office. The term of the five persons to be appointed by the mayor shall be three years. In the event that a vacancy shall occur during the term of any member, his successor shall be appointed for the unexpired portion of the term.
(c)
Compensation. Members of the board shall serve without compensation.
(d)
Duties and responsibilities. It shall be the responsibility of the board to study, investigate, council and develop and/or update annually, and administer a written plan for the care, preservation, pruning, planting, replanting, removal, or disposition of trees and shrubs in parks, along streets and in other public areas. Such plan will be presented annually to the city council and upon its acceptance and approval shall constitute the official comprehensive city tree plan for the city. The board, when requested by the city council, shall consider, investigate, make finding, report, and recommend upon any special matter of question coming within the scope of its work.
(e)
Operation. The board shall choose its own officers, make its own rules and regulations and keep a journal of its proceedings. A majority of the members shall be a quorum for the transaction of business.
(f)
Interference with board prohibited. It shall be unlawful for any person to prevent, delay, or interfere with the city tree board, or any of its agents, while engaging in and about the planting, cultivating, mulching, pruning, spraying, or removing of any street trees, park trees, or trees on private grounds, as authorized in this article.
(g)
Review by city council. The city council shall have the right to review the conduct, acts, and decisions of the city tree board. Any person may appeal from any ruling or order of the city tree board to the city council who may hear the matter and make the final decision.
(Ord. of 11-2-1998, §§ 2—6, 17, 19)
Sec. 30-63. - Street tree species to be planted.
The following list constitutes the official street tree species for the city:
(1)
Height of 30 inches or greater: Leyland Cypress, Cedar, Ginkgo, Southern Magnolia, Oak, Pine, Beech, Elm, Red Maple, Bradford Pear.
(2)
Height of 30 inches or less: Crape Myrtle, Japanese Magnolia, Trident Maple, Red Bud, Silverbell, Yoshino Cherry, Holly, Dogwood, Japanese Maple, Fringetree (Grancy Greybeard) and Viburnums.
No species other than those included in this section may be planted as street trees without written permission of the city tree board.
(Ord. of 11-2-1998, § 7)
Sec. 30-64. - Spacing.
The spacing of street trees will be in accordance with the species size classes listed in section 30-63, and no trees may be planted closer together than the following:
(1)
Small trees: 30 feet;
(2)
Medium trees: 40 feet; and
(3)
Large trees: 50 feet;
except in special plantings designed or approved by a landscape architect.
(Ord. of 11-2-1998, § 8)
Sec. 30-65. - Distance—From curb and sidewalk.
The distance trees may be planted from curbs or curblines and sidewalks will be in accordance with the species size classes listed in section 30-63. No trees may be planted closer to any curb or sidewalk than the following: large trees, four feet.
(Ord. of 11-2-1998, § 9)
Sec. 30-66. - Same—From street corners and fireplugs.
No street tree shall be planted closer than 20 feet of any street corner, measured from the point of nearest intersecting curbs or curblines. No street tree shall be planted closer than ten feet of any fireplug.
(Ord. of 11-2-1998, § 10)
Sec. 30-67. - Utilities.
No street trees other than those species listed as small trees in section 30-63 may be planted under or within ten lateral feet of any overhead utility wire, or over or within five lateral feet of any water line, sewer line, transmission line or other utility.
(Ord. of 11-2-1998, § 11)
Sec. 30-68. - Public tree care.
The city shall have the right to plant, prune, maintain, and remove trees, plants, and shrubs within the lines of all streets, alleys, avenues, lanes, squares, and public grounds, as may be necessary to ensure public safety or to preserve or enhance the symmetry and beauty of such public grounds. The city tree board may remove or cause to order to be removed, any tree or part thereof which is in an unsafe condition or which by reason of its nature is injurious to sewers, electric power lines, gas lines, water lines, or other public improvements, or is affected with any injurious fungus, insect, or other pest. This section does not prohibit the planting of street trees by adjacent property owners providing that the selection and location of said trees is in accordance with sections 30-63 through 30-67.
(Ord. of 11-2-1998, § 12)
Sec. 30-69. - Tree topping.
It shall be unlawful as a normal practice for any person or city department to top any street tree, park tree, or other tree on public property. The term "topping" is defined as the severe cutting back of limbs to stubs larger than three inches in diameter within the tree's crown to such a degree so as to remove the normal canopy and disfigure the tree. Trees severely damaged by storms or other causes, or certain trees under utility wires or other obstructions where other pruning practices are impractical may be exempted from this article at the determination of the city tree board.
(Ord. of 11-2-1998, § 13)
Sec. 30-70. - Pruning, corner clearance.
Every owner of any tree overhanging any street or right-of-way within the city shall prune the branches so that such branches shall not obstruct the light from any street lamp or obstruct the view of any street intersection and so that there shall be a clear space of eight feet above the surface of the street or sidewalk. Said owners shall remove all dead, diseased or dangerous trees, or broken or decayed limbs which constitute a menace to the safety of the public. The city shall have the right to prune any tree or shrub on private property when it interferes with the proper spread of light along the street from a streetlight or interferes with visibility of any traffic control device or sign.
(Ord. of 11-2-1998, § 14)
Sec. 30-71. - Dead or diseased tree removal on private property.
The city shall have the right to cause the removal of any dead or diseased trees on private property within the city, when such trees constitute a hazard to life and property, or harbor insects or disease which constitute a potential threat to other trees within the city. The city tree board will notify in writing the owners of such trees. Removal shall be done by said owners at the owners' own expense within 60 days after the date of service of notice. In the event of failure of owners to comply with such provisions, the city shall have the authority to remove such trees and charge the cost of removal on the owner's property tax notice.
(Ord. of 11-2-1998, § 15)
Sec. 30-72. - Removal of stumps.
All stumps of street and park trees shall be removed below the surface of the ground so the top of the stumps shall not project above the surface of the ground.
(Ord. of 11-2-1998, § 16)

ARTICLE IV. - BUILDING NUMBERS AND STREET NAMES

Sec. 30-103. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Address program administrator means the official of the city charged with the administration of this article, including his authorized representative. The police chief, or such person as may be designated by the city council, shall serve as the address program administrator.
Address program administrator assistant means the official of the city charged with assisting the address program administrator. The address program administrator assistant shall be the city clerk or such other person as designated by the city.
Building means a structure having a roof supported by columns or walls for the shelter of persons for residential or business use. When separated by division walls from the ground up without openings, each such enclosure may be deemed a separate building. Buildings without divisions, as described in this article, that are used as multifamily or multiuse facilities may, at the discretion of the address program administrator, be assigned more than one address.
Concurrence. The mayor and council shall have the right to disapprove any action of the address program administrator by a majority vote. If the mayor and council shall fail to act within two regular meetings after the action of the address program administrator, the mayor and council shall be deemed to have concurred with the action.
Driveway means a private way, beginning at the property line of a lot abutting a public road, private road, easement, or private right-of-way, and leading to a building, lot, or physical location.
Private road means any one-way or two-way road for ingress and/or egress, serving two or more properties, but not for use by the general public.
Public road means any road, street, highway, thoroughfare, or other way of passage that has been irrevocably dedicated to the public or in which the public has acquired rights by prescription, without regard to whether it is open for travel.
Road address means the combination of numbers and road names assigned under the terms of this article which identifies a particular building, lot, or other physical location.
(Ord. No. 438, 9-2-2005)
Sec. 30-104. - Purpose and intent.
The purpose and intent of this article is to provide a uniform system of assigning addresses for all properties and buildings throughout the city in order to facilitate provision of adequate public safety and emergency response service, and to minimize difficulty in locating properties and buildings for public service agencies and the general public.
(Ord. No. 438, 9-2-2005)
Sec. 30-105. - Administration and application.
The address program administrator and program assistant, with concurrence of the mayor and council will be responsible for interpretation and administration of this article including:
(1)
Assigning all numbers for properties and buildings.
(2)
Maintaining a database of addresses of each property and building.
(3)
Designating individual unit addresses within multiple housing units in conformity with this article.
(4)
Recommending to the mayor and council such changes to this article or to the road name system as may be necessary to facilitate the perpetuation and efficiency of the address system.
(Ord. No. 438, 9-2-2005)
Sec. 30-106. - Enforcement.
Owners or occupants of buildings already constructed which do not comply with this article will be notified and requested to meet the requirements within 60 days from the date of notification. A warning notice will be issued by registered or certified mail after the 60 days, if the requirements have not been met. The owner or occupant who does not comply with this article within 30 days after delivery of a warning notice shall be deemed guilty of a misdemeanor and shall be punished. Violation of this article may also be enforced by seeking an order to compel compliance of civil remedies against the violators. Prosecution for violation of this article shall be upon citation, as provided in O.C.G.A. §§ 15-10-62 and 15-10-63, in the municipal court of the city. Any person, business, establishment, congregation, group, hunting camp, or other legal entity violating this article may be given a summons and tried in municipal court of city. The address program administrator is authorized to issue citations for the enforcement of the provisions contained within this article, as provided by O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63.
(Ord. No. 438, 9-2-2005)
Sec. 30-107. - Display of road address numbers.
(a)
Each location noted on the master address list for E911 shall be required to purchase an address sign as described herein. The city shall bill the residents or owner of the business the sum in an amount established by the city for each sign installed. The signs shall be installed on a metal post and metal sign with the display of official reflective address numbers so that they are clearly visible from the street or road on which the driveway to the property is on. No other signs will be allowed to be displayed. The following criteria shall be used to properly display the numbers:
(1)
Address numbers must meet the minimum size requirements indicated as follows:
a.
Posts upon which they are mounted must bear reflective letters three inches in height.
b.
Residences and businesses, in the incorporated areas of the city, must place posts bearing a blue sign with white reflective address numbers three inches in height, and in immediate proximity to the driveway or private road that leads to such residence or business.
(2)
Numbers for a multiple dwelling complex or nonresidential building shall be three inches in height and shall be placed on a post. Individual units within a multiple dwelling complex shall be required to display a unit reflective number, which is three inches in height on the front door or immediately adjacent to the door.
(3)
On a corner lot, the house number shall face the street named in the address.
(b)
The address program administrator and program assistant, with the concurrence of the mayor and council, will have the right to authorize and approve alternate methods of displaying numbers which meet the intent of this article when strict adherence to these standards cannot be met or is impractical.
(Ord. No. 438, 9-2-2005)
Sec. 30-108. - Types of numbers.
The following two types of numbers are to be used under this article:
(1)
A primary number is to be assigned to each street frontage of each improved property. The primary number must be posted on that property if the property is occupied by a dwelling unit; structure, or active use and the owner, occupant, or person in charge is notified.
(2)
Secondary numbers may be used when a number of units, structures, and uses coexist on the same parcel of land. Examples of secondary numbers include apartment projects, mobile home parks, office parks, planned unit developments, recreational vehicle parks, recreational areas, shopping centers, and other uses where the use of secondary numbers would clarify the location of a unit or use for public safety purposes. To provide secondary numbers, the address program administrator shall work with the owner, manager, or person in charge of the project to determine a logical numbering system.
(Ord. No. 438, 9-2-2005)
Sec. 30-109. - Roads; exempt and excluded structures.
(a)
The intent of this section is to exclude from this article those structures that do not present a significant danger to human life if destroyed by fire or other events.
(b)
The following structures shall be excluded from the provisions of this article:
(1)
Agricultural buildings not requiring a separate mailing address, such as, a barn, poultry house, outbuilding, or equipment storage building.
(2)
Storage and accessory building for the use of the occupant of another building on the same property. Buildings used as dwelling units, offices, or the nominal workstation of an employee, or such building that requires a separate mailing address, shall be exempt.
(Ord. No. 438, 9-2-2005)
Sec. 30-110. - New road names.
(a)
No new public roads in the incorporated area of the city shall be named without the approval of the mayor and council.
(b)
No private road names shall be recognized by this article or added to the city's list of road names without the approval of the mayor and council.
(c)
The name of any public and/or private road shall not duplicate or be confusingly similar to an existing road name. This restriction shall not apply to extensions of existing roads.
(Ord. No. 438, 9-2-2005)
Sec. 30-111. - Numbers for future structures and lots.
Following the adoption of the ordinance from which this article is derived, all utility services regulated by the state public service commissioner or any utility cooperative service organization providing service in the incorporated area of the city shall withhold service from any building, dwelling, mobile home, or other structure not exempt under this article until the owner or other requesting party has furnished the utility with a valid, assigned number.
(Ord. No. 438, 9-2-2005)
Sec. 30-112. - Implementation by city departments.
Every department and official under the jurisdiction of the mayor and council shall withhold nonemergency service until the occupant, owner or requesting individual has provided the department with a valid, assigned number. Nonemergency service includes any services except law enforcement, fire, rescue, or nonemergency medical service.
(Ord. No. 438, 9-2-2005)
Secs. 30-113—30-137. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - PARADES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 30-138. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Parade means a parade, march, ceremony, show, exhibition, pageant, or procession of any kind, or any similar display in or upon any street, park or other public place in the city. Movement from one point to another is not a required element of a parade.
Parade permit means a permit required by this article.
Sporting event means all events, whether organized or unorganized, in which persons gather primarily for participation in or observation of actions by the participants involving physical or mental exertion.
Sec. 30-139. - Interference.
No person shall unreasonably hamper, obstruct, impede, or interfere with any parade or parade assembly or with any person, vehicle, or animal participating or used in a parade.
Sec. 30-140. - Driving through parades.
No driver of a vehicle shall drive between the vehicles or persons comprising a parade when such vehicles or persons are in motion or are conspicuously designated as a parade.
Sec. 30-141. - Parking on parade route.
The chief of police shall have the authority, when reasonably necessary, to prohibit or restrict parking of vehicles in any areas where such parking would disrupt the conduct of a parade. The chief of police shall post signs to such effect. It shall be unlawful for any person to park or leave unattended any vehicle in violation thereof. No person shall be liable for parking on an unposted street in violation of this article.
Sec. 30-142. - Prohibited conduct.
(a)
It is unlawful for any person to stage, present, or conduct any parade without first obtaining a permit.
(b)
It is unlawful for any person knowingly to participate in a parade for which a permit has not been granted.
(c)
It is unlawful for any person in charge of or responsible for the conduct of a duly licensed parade to knowingly fail to comply with any condition of the permit.
Secs. 30-143—30-167. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PERMIT

Sec. 30-168. - Required.
No person shall engage in, participate in, aid, form, or start any parade unless a parade permit has been obtained from the police department.
Sec. 30-169. - Exceptions.
This article shall not apply to:
(1)
Funeral processions;
(2)
Students going to and from school classes or participating in educational activities; provided such conduct is under the immediate direction and supervision of proper school authorities;
(3)
A government agency acting within the scope of its functions;
(4)
Any sporting event.
Sec. 30-170. - Application—Filing with police department.
A person seeking issuance of a parade permit shall file an application with the police department on forms provided by the police department not less than ten days nor more than 30 days before the date on which it proposes to conduct the parade.
Sec. 30-171. - Same—Contents.
The application for a parade permit shall set forth the following information:
(1)
The name, address and telephone number of the persons seeking to conduct such parade.
(2)
If the parade is proposed to be conducted for, on behalf of, or by any organization, the name, address and telephone number of the headquarters of the organization and of the authorized and responsible heads of such organization.
(3)
The name, address and telephone number of the person who will be the parade chairperson and who will be responsible for its conduct.
(4)
The date when the parade is to be conducted.
(5)
The route to be travelled, if any, the starting point and termination point.
(6)
The approximate number of persons who, and animals and vehicles which, will constitute such parade; the type of animals and description of vehicles.
(7)
The hours when such parade will start and terminate.
(8)
A statement as to whether the parade will occupy all or only a portion of the widths of the streets, if any, proposed to be traversed.
(9)
If such parade is to be held in a city park, a statement as to whether the parade will occupy all or only a portion of the city park, or other public area on which the parade is proposed to take place.
(10)
The location, by streets, of any assembly areas for such parade.
(11)
The time at which units of the parade will begin to assemble at such assembly area.
(12)
The interval of space to be maintained between units of such parade.
(13)
If the parade is designed to be held by, on behalf of, or for any person other than the applicant, the applicant for such permit shall file with the police department a communication in writing from the person proposing to hold the parade, authorizing the applicant to apply for the parade on his behalf.
(14)
Information regarding any public address or loudspeaker system to be used during such parade, including the approximate number of decibels to be emitted from such system.
Sec. 30-172. - Late applications.
Late applications for a parade permit will not be considered.
Sec. 30-173. - Standards for issuance.
The police department shall issue a parade permit when, from a consideration of the application and from such other information as may otherwise be obtained, it is found:
(1)
The conduct of the parade will not substantially interrupt the safe and orderly movement of other traffic contiguous to its route or place of occurrence.
(2)
The conduct of the parade will not require the diversion of so great a number of police officers of the city to properly police the line of movement or the place of occurrence and the areas contiguous thereto as to prevent normal police protection to the city.
(3)
The conduct of such parade will not require the diversion of so great a number of ambulances and other emergency vehicles as to prevent normal ambulance and other emergency service to portions of the city other than that to be occupied by the proposed parade and areas contiguous thereto.
(4)
The concentration of persons, animals, and vehicles at assembly points of the parade will not unduly interfere with police, fire, and other emergency services to areas contiguous to such assembly areas.
(5)
The conduct of such parade will not interfere with the movement of firefighting equipment en route to a fire.
(6)
The conduct of the parade is not reasonably likely to cause injury to persons or property, to provoke disorderly conduct or to create a disturbance.
(7)
The parade is not to be held for the sole purpose of advertising any product, goods or event, and is designed to be held purely for private profit.
(8)
The parade is scheduled to move from its point of origin to its point of termination expeditiously and without unreasonable delays en route.
Sec. 30-174. - Notice of rejection.
The police department shall act upon the application for a parade permit within three business days after the filing thereof. If the police department disapproves the application, the department shall mail to the applicant, within three business days after the date which the application was filed, a notice of its action stating the reasons for its denial of the permit. Notice shall be deemed complete when mailed.
Sec. 30-175. - Appeal procedure.
Any person aggrieved shall have the right to appeal the denial of a parade permit to the city council. The appeal shall be taken within 60 days after notice. The city council shall act upon the appeal within 30 days after its receipts.
Sec. 30-176. - Alternative permits.
The police department, in denying an application for a parade permit, shall be empowered to authorize the conduct of the parade on a date, at a time, or over a route, or in a place, different from that named by the applicant. An applicant desiring to accept an alternate permit shall, within five days after notice of the action of the police department, file a written notice of acceptance with the police department. An alternate parade permit shall conform to the requirements of and shall have the effect of a parade permit under this article.
Sec. 30-177. - Contents of permit.
Each parade permit shall state the following information:
(1)
Starting time.
(2)
The portions of the streets, if any, to be traversed that may be occupied by the parade.
(3)
The portions of any parks or public property to be occupied by the parade.
(4)
The maximum length of the parade in miles or fractions thereof.
(5)
Such other information as the mayor, city council, city attorney, and chief of police shall find necessary to the enforcement of this article.
Sec. 30-178. - Duties of the permittee.
(a)
Compliance. A permittee under this division shall comply with all permit directions and conditions and with all applicable laws and ordinances.
(b)
Possession of permit. The parade chairperson or other person heading or leading such activity shall carry the parade permit on his person during the conduct of the parade.
(c)
Parade cleanup. The parade chairperson or other person heading or leading such activity shall be solely responsible for the cleanup of any papers, bottles, cans, animal excretions, or any other refuse left by participants in the parade.
(1)
The parade chairperson or other person heading or leading such activity shall have a reasonable amount of time, but not to exceed 48 hours, to carry out such cleanup after any such activity.
(2)
If such cleanup is not completed by the parade chairperson or other person heading or leading such activity within a reasonable amount of time, but not to exceed 48 hours, the city may commence cleanup and hold the parade chairperson or other person heading or leading such activity financially liable for any and all costs directly incurred by the city in such cleanup activity.
(3)
In no instance shall the parade chairperson or other person heading or leading such activity be responsible for cleanup of the refuse left by spectators or other nonparticipants in the parade.
Sec. 30-179. - Revocation of permit.
The chief of police shall have the authority to revoke a parade permit issued under this division upon application of the standards for issuance, as set forth in this article.
