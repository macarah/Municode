ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 34-1. - State traffic laws adopted by reference.
(a)
Adoption of state law by reference. Pursuant to O.C.G.A. §§ 40-6-372—40-6-376, O.C.G.A. title 49, ch. 6 (O.C.G.A. § 40-6-1 et seq.), known as the "Uniform Rules of the Road," and the definitions contained in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1 are hereby adopted as and for the traffic regulations of this city with like effect as if recited herein.
(b)
Penalties. Unless another penalty is expressly provided for by law, every person convicted of any provision of this chapter shall be punished as provided in section 1-7.
(Ord. No. 441, §§ 1, 2, 11-3-2008)
Sec. 34-2. - Traffic control signs, signals, devices and markings.
The location and existence of all traffic control signs, signals, devices and marking in place on the adoption date of this Code are hereby ratified and confirmed.
Sec. 34-3. - Weight restrictions.
(a)
There is hereby established for South Sumter Street from its intersection with State Route 49 southeast to the city limits, a maximum total gross weight limit for all vehicles, loaded or unloaded, using said street. The maximum total gross weight of any vehicle using South Sumter Street shall not exceed 26,000 pounds.
(b)
The total gross weight established herein shall not apply to vehicles making a pickup or delivery on said street.
(Code 1986, § 20-15)
State Law reference— Vehicle size and load limits, O.C.G.A. § 32-6-20 et seq.; enforcement of load limits, O.C.G.A. § 32-6-27.
Sec. 34-4. - Truck routes.
(a)
The following streets or portions of streets in the city are hereby designated as truck routes:
(1)
Riverview Drive (aka State Route No. 26).
(2)
Buck Creek Bypass (County Road No. 275).
(3)
Chatham Street (aka State Route No. 49).
(4)
West Bypass (aka State Route 128 Bypass).
(b)
All motor vehicles having a gross weight in excess of 26,000 pounds, including the load thereon, or having an overall length in excess of 30 feet, except vehicles designed to carry passengers, are prohibited from using any street within the city, including those that may have a state route designation, except those designated as truck routes in subsection (a) of this section, except:
(1)
When the terminal, parking lot, or headquarters of the restricted motor vehicle is not on a designated truck route, ingress to and egress from such places shall be made by the most direct route available between said terminal, parking lot, or headquarters and the nearest designated truck route.
(2)
When making a pickup or delivery on a street not designated as a truck route, ingress to and egress from such location shall be made by the most direct truck route available between such location and the nearest designated truck route.
(c)
Any person driving or in charge or control of any of the restricted vehicles defined in subsection (b) of this section, when upon streets other than those designated as truck routes, shall be prepared to present for inspection his log book, weight slips, delivery slips and/or other written evidence of his designation and point of origin to justify the presence of the restricted vehicle on a street other than a designated truck route.
(Ord. of 12-21-1987, §§ 1—3)
State Law reference— Vehicle size and load limits, O.C.G.A. § 32-6-20 et seq.; enforcement of load limits, O.C.G.A. § 32-6-27.
Sec. 34-5. - Funeral processions to be identified.
A funeral composed of a procession of vehicles shall be identified as such by the display, upon the outside of each vehicle, of a pennant of a type designated by the chief of police, or by burning lights.
(Code 1986, § 20-4)
State Law reference— Authority to regulate processions, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-371(a)(3).
Secs. 34-6—34-28. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - ADMINISTRATION AND ENFORCEMENT

Sec. 34-29. - Authority to place, maintain, etc.—Electric traffic control devices.
As an aide in facilitating the movement of traffic on the streets of the city and in reducing accidents, the chief of police and the city electrician are hereby authorized and directed to place and maintain electric traffic control devices and signals at such locations, as may be designated from time to time by resolutions of the city.
(Code 1986, § 20-8)
Sec. 34-30. - Same—Other traffic control devices.
The chief of police of the city shall place and maintain, other than electrically operated, traffic control devices when and as required under this chapter and other traffic ordinances of the city to make effective the provisions of this chapter and such ordinances, and, with the approval of the city manager, may place and maintain such additional traffic control devices as he may deem necessary to regulate traffic under this chapter and other traffic ordinances of the city or under state law, or to guide or warn traffic.
(Code 1986, § 20-9)
Secs. 34-31—34-48. - Reserved.

Sec. 34-49. - Parking lines angle, and parallel parking.
On streets where white lines are shown for guidance, parking must conform with such lines. The driver must park his vehicle entirely within the boundaries designated by such lines.
(Code 1986, § 20-16)
Sec. 34-50. - Sumter Street parking.
There shall be no parking permitted on the west side of Sumter Street in the city, between the hours of 7:30 a.m. to 9:00 p.m., and between the hours of 2:30 p.m. to 4:00 p.m., from the intersection of North Randolph Street and Sumter Street southward along Sumter Street for a distance of 200 yards. The department of transportation shall be requested to post the necessary signs as soon as possible.
(Code 1986, § 20-21)

ARTICLE IV. - BICYCLES[3]

Footnotes:
--- (3) ---
State Law reference— Bicycles, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-290 et seq.; local authority to regulate stopping, standing and parking, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-371(a)(7).

Sec. 34-74. - Persons riding bicycles to obey traffic regulations.
Every person riding a bicycle shall be subject to the provision of this Code applicable to the driver of any vehicle, except those provisions of such chapter which by its very nature can have no application.
(Code 1986, § 20-22)
Sec. 34-75. - Speed.
It shall be unlawful for any person to ride any bicycle at an unusual speed, or in a disorderly manner, or as to endanger the life or limb of another.
(Code 1986, § 20-25)
Sec. 34-76. - Use of bicycles restricted.
No person shall operate a bicycle upon any sidewalk in the business district of this city. This section shall not prohibit children from moving bicycles in such area while dismounted.
(Ord. No. 410, § 1(20-26), 5-7-1990)
Sec. 34-77. - Application of article to skateboards.
This article and the prohibitions contained herein shall also apply to skateboards and other toy vehicles. No person shall operate a skateboard or other toy vehicle upon any sidewalk in the business district of this city.
(Ord. No. 410, § 1(20-27), 5-7-1990)

ARTICLE V. - LOW-SPEED VEHICLES[4]

Footnotes:
--- (4) ---
State Law reference— Low-speed vehicles, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-360 et seq.

Sec. 34-98. - Exceptions.
No low-speed vehicle shall be operated within the intersection of Sumter and Chatham at the red light in the city, or at the Macon Street-Chatham intersection Street (Piggly Wiggly). Low-speed vehicles shall access South Macon Street via the alley along city hall and cross Sumter at Terry's Barbershop. The city will install caution signs at the designated crossings over state highways.
(Ord. No. 441, § 4, 11-3-2008)
Sec. 34-99. - Registration fee.
Each low-speed vehicle operating within the city limits shall be required to register said vehicle at the city police department with the chief of police. There shall be imposed a $15.00 one-time registration fee. Low-speed vehicle owners shall register said vehicles within 30 days of purchase. Operators shall be required to place a decal on a prominent place on the rear bumper of the low-speed vehicle. Said decals will be furnished by the city.
(Ord. No. 441, § 5, 11-3-2008)
Sec. 34-100. - Required equipment.
All low-speed vehicles shall be equipped with amber lights on the rear of the vehicle and a sign to the effect, "Low-Speed Vehicle." Operators of low-speed vehicles shall have a valid learner's license (with an adult in vehicle) or regular class driver's license.
(Ord. No. 441, § 6, 11-3-2008)
Sec. 34-101. - Prohibited vehicles.
Four-wheelers and go-carts are strictly prohibited from operation on city streets within the corporate limits of the city.
(Ord. No. 441, § 7, 11-3-2008)
Sec. 34-102. - Time for operation.
Low-speed vehicles may be operated in the designated areas and streets from 30 minutes prior to sunrise to 30 minutes after sundown.
(Ord. No. 441, § 8, 11-3-2008)
