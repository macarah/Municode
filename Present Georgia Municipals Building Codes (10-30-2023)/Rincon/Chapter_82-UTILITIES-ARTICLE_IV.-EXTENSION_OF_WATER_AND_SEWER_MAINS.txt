ARTICLE IV. - EXTENSION OF WATER AND SEWER MAINS[2]

Sec. 82-96. - Petition for extension.
(a)
A person desiring to have the city's water or sewer mains extended to his property may do so by petitioning the city council for such extension. Such petition must include the plans for the extension.
(b)
There shall be no extension of the water or sewer system until such time as the petition and plans for extension have been approved by the city council. The city council, at their discretion, may require each petitioner to submit such plans, specifications and other pertinent information in order to reach a decision that is in the best interest of the citizens of the city.
(Code 1984, § 8-201)
Sec. 82-97. - Determination of city council.
In making a determination, the city council shall consider, but not be limited to, the following factors:
(1)
The cost to the city for the extension of the water and/or sewage system.
(2)
The benefits which the city would derive by extending the water and/or sewage system.
(3)
The adequacy of the design plans for the extension of the water and/or sewage system.
(4)
The possible adverse impact upon the city's present water and sewage system.
The city council shall either approve or reject all plans submitted to them for the extension of the water and/or sewage system within six months after the submission of the plan by the petitioner.
(Code 1984, § 8-202)
Sec. 82-98. - Inspection; costs.
(a)
Final connection to the city water and sewage system shall not be made until the city council has completed a final inspection and determined that all requirements of the city building code have been met.
(b)
All costs for the submission of the plan and final inspection shall be borne by the petitioner and paid prior to the final connection by the city's water and sewage system.
(Code 1984, § 8-203)
