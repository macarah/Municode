ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 6-1. - Sales tax.
There is hereby levied a tax of one percent on all items subject to tax under the Georgia Retailers and Consumers Tax Act, as amended (O.C.G.A. § 48-8-1 et seq.).
(Res. of 12-23-76; Res. of 3-27-79; Res. of 12-18-79)
State Law reference— Joint county and municipal sales and use tax, O.C.G.A. § 48-8-80 et seq.; special districts where joint tax to be levied, O.C.G.A. § 48-8-83; imposition of tax by counties and municipalities, O.C.G.A. § 48-8-84.
Sec. 6-2. - Gross premiums tax on insurance other than life insurance.
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2, there is hereby levied for each calendar year a tax upon each foreign, alien and domestic insurance company doing business within the unincorporated area of the county, in an amount equal to 2½ percent of the gross direct premiums received during the calendar year on all insurance, other than life insurance, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 33-3-5(2), (3), (4) and (5). Gross direct premiums shall mean gross direct premiums as used in O.C.G.A. § 33-8-8.2(a).
(Res. of 8-16-83, § 1)
Sec. 6-3. - Financial institutions business license tax.
(a)
As used in this section:
Depository financial institutions shall mean state and national banks, state building and loan associations, and federal savings and loan associations.
Gross receipts shall mean gross receipts as defined in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93.
(b)
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93, there is hereby levied for each calendar year a business license tax upon all depository financial institutions located within the county at a rate of twenty-five hundredths percent of the gross receipts of such depository financial institutions.
(c)
The minimum annual amount of business license tax due from any depository financial institution pursuant to subsection (b) shall be $1,000.00.
(d)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(c), each depository financial institution subject to this tax shall file a return of its gross receipts with the board of commissioners by March 1 of the year following the year in which such gross receipts are measured. Such return shall be in the manner and in the form prescribed by the state revenue commissioner based on the allocation method set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-6-93(d). The board of commissioners shall assess and collect the tax levied pursuant to this section based upon the information provided in the returns.
(e)
Taxes levied pursuant to this section shall be due no later than 30 days after the filing of the return prescribed by subsection (d) unless extended by the board of commissioners.
(Res. of 9-20-83, §§ 1—4)
Sec. 6-4. - Fortunetelling.
(a)
No person shall be licensed in the county, outside of incorporated limits, to tell fortunes and read palms and do other things of a like nature, without first having paid a license tax, in the maximum amount allowed by state law, to the board of commissioners, and having received a license therefor. In no instance shall such a license be issued to any person unless he has been a resident of the county for a minimum period of two years, and who has owned real estate in the county for such period, on which not less than two years' taxes have been assessed and paid. Such business shall be conducted on those premises and at no other place.
(b)
The board of commissioners may revoke such license at any time within its sole discretion, and it shall be discretionary to retain the tax thus paid for such license.
(Res. of 12-21-56)
State Law reference— Fortunetelling, palmistry, etc., may be taxed not more than $1,000.00 per year, O.C.G.A. § 36-1-15.
Sec. 6-5. - Judicial in rem tax foreclosure for delinquent real property taxes.
(a)
The county and the county tax commissioner shall be authorized, from and after the effective date of the ordinance from which this section derives, to utilize the procedures provided for judicial in rem tax foreclosure as contained in O.C.G.A. §§ 48-4-75 through 48-4-82, said state statute being incorporated into this section by specific reference thereto.
(b)
Such procedures may be used for the collection of any and all ad valorem taxes assessed against real property within the county which remain unpaid for a period of 12 months following the date upon which the taxes initially become delinquent.
(c)
Any proceedings filed in accordance with this section shall be designed solely to enforce the lien for ad valorem taxes against the property subject to such taxation, and such proceedings shall not constitute an action for personal liability for such taxes against the owners or owners of the subject property.
(d)
For the purposes of proceedings filed in superior court pursuant to this section, the county tax collector shall be defined as the Harris County Tax Commissioner.
(e)
Prior to any proceeding being filed in the superior court pursuant to this section, the tax commissioner shall cause the county attorney to examine the title to the property which is subject to the ad valorem tax levy and report to the tax commissioner the name of the owner or owners of the subject property and any other interested parties as defined by state law along with all title exceptions or defects. The county attorney shall also provide to the tax commissioner a legal description of the subject property. The county attorney shall prepare the petition, file and prosecute the matter to conclusion in superior court.
(f)
Nothing contained in this section shall prohibit the county or the county tax commissioner from proceeding to collect delinquent ad valorem taxes in another manner or pursuant to another county ordinance or state statute as the methods for collections of such ad valorem taxes provided in this section are not exclusive, but are merely cumulative of the methods by which the county or the county tax commissioner may collect such taxes pursuant to state law. The county tax commissioner shall consult with the county attorney in determining the method used in collecting a delinquent ad valorem taxes.
(g)
Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 48-4-76(a), the judicial in rem tax foreclosure procedure authorized by this section may also be utilized by all municipalities located with the county regarding the collection of such municipalities' respective taxes. Upon approval of the ordinance from which this section derives, the county clerk notify the governing body of each municipality in the county of the approval of said ordinance and the effective date thereof.
(Ord. No. 04-10, § 1, 6-1-2010)
