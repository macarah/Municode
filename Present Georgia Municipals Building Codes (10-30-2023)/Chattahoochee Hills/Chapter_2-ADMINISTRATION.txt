Chapter 2 - ADMINISTRATION
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 2-1. - Meeting times.
(a)
As described in section 2.09 of the Charter, the council shall meet at least once a month. Regular meetings shall be held on the first Tuesday of each month at 6:00 p.m. The council may designate alternate meeting times and days so long as the time is published either electronically on the city website or through other means, including posting at the building designated as city hall at least 24 hours before the meeting.
(b)
If the regular meeting falls on a federal holiday, the council may reschedule the meeting and publish the change as described in subsection (a) of this section.
(c)
Special meetings may be called in accordance with the Charter section 2.09.
(d)
Any meeting of the council may be continued or adjourned from day to day, or for more than one day, but no adjournment shall be for a longer period than until the next regular meeting thereafter.
(Ord. No. 07-12-02, art. 1, § 1, 12-12-2007; Ord. No. 08-02-26, art. 1, § 1, 2-5-2008; Ord. No. 10-12-80, art. 1, § 1, 12-7-2010; Ord. No. 11-09-95, art. 1, § 1, 9-6-2011; Ord. No. 19-06-199 , § 1, 6-6-2019)
Sec. 2-2. - Compliance with state law.
(a)
All meetings shall be held in accordance with the Georgia Open and Public Meetings Act codified at O.C.G.A. § 50-14-1 et seq.
(b)
All city records subject to the Georgia Open Records Act, codified at O.C.G.A. § 50-18-70 et seq., shall be available to the public. The city clerk shall comply with any valid requests under the Georgia Open Records Act and prepare any materials requested.
(Ord. No. 07-12-02, art. 1, § 2, 12-12-2007; Ord. No. 08-02-26, art. 1, § 2, 2-5-2008; Ord. No. 10-12-80, art. 1, § 2, 12-7-2010)
Sec. 2-3. - Document retention.
Pursuant to the Georgia Records Act, O.C.G.A. § 50-18-90 et seq., the city adopts the records management plan and record retention schedules recommended by the Georgia secretary of state as amended from time to time by future resolutions of the mayor and council of the city. The city clerk shall coordinate all records management for the city including storage, archiving, and destruction of records. Records shall be maintained according to approved retention schedules. All requests made under the Georgia Open Records Act, O.C.G.A. § 50-18-70 et seq., excepting standardized routine requests of the city police department, fire department, and municipal court shall be made to the court clerk. No record shall be destroyed except as provided in the approved retention schedule; provided, however, a written record may be destroyed if it is otherwise preserved by means of microfiche, scanning, or other reliable photographic or digital means as permitted by law. Changes to the retention schedules shall be adopted by resolution of the mayor and council.
(Ord. No. 07-12-02, art. 1, § 3, 12-12-2007; Ord. No. 08-02-26, art. 1, § 3, 2-5-2008; Ord. No. 10-12-80, art. 1, § 3, 12-7-2010)
Sec. 2-4. - Fees.
The city clerk shall charge the full amount permitted by state law to copy the records subject to the Georgia Open Records Act. If compilation and replication of such documents takes more than 15 minutes, the city clerk may charge an additional reasonable administrative charge.
(Ord. No. 07-12-02, art. 1, § 4, 12-12-2007; Ord. No. 08-02-26, art. 1, § 4, 2-5-2008; Ord. No. 10-12-80, art. 1, § 4, 12-7-2010)
Sec. 2-5. - Subpoenas.
(a)
The council, when sitting as a judicial or quasi-judicial body pursuant to public law, its charter, or its ordinances, shall compel the attendance of witnesses by subpoena under the same rules as prevail in the superior courts, and any witness who shall fail to respond to the subpoena, or who shall refuse to testify under oath, shall be guilty of contempt.
(b)
The subpoenas as authorized in the preceding section shall bear witness in the name of the mayor; shall be issued by the city clerk; and shall be served by the chief of police, any police officer, or by such other person as the mayor may designate.
(c)
Should any person fail to respond to a subpoena of the council, after having been lawfully served, without good cause, or should the person refuse to testify under oath, then the council shall hold the person in contempt, and, in their discretion, punish the person by the imposition of a fine according to the maximum allowed by state law. Each of the failures or refusals herein described shall constitute a separate contempt.
(Ord. No. 07-12-02, art. 1, § 5, 12-12-2007; Ord. No. 08-02-26, art. 1, § 5, 2-5-2008; Ord. No. 10-12-80, art. 1, § 5, 12-7-2010)
Sec. 2-6. - Reimbursement and travel.
The city manager shall establish a system for reimbursement of business-related expenses and travel for all municipal employees.
(Ord. No. 07-12-02, art. 1, § 6, 12-12-2007; Ord. No. 08-02-26, art. 1, § 6, 2-5-2008; Ord. No. 10-12-80, art. 1, § 6, 12-7-2010)
Sec. 2-7. - Teleconference meetings.
(a)
Under circumstances necessitated by emergency conditions involving public safety or the preservation of property or public services, The city council and commissions may meet by means of teleconference so long as the notice required by the Georgia Open Meetings Law is provided and means are afforded for the public to have simultaneous access to the teleconference meeting.
(b)
On any other occasion of the meeting of the city council and/or the commissions, and so long as a quorum is present in person, a member may participate by teleconference if necessary due to reasons of health or absence from the jurisdiction so long as the other requirements of the Georgia Open Meetings Law are met.
(c)
Absent emergency conditions or the written opinion of a physician or other health professional that reasons of health prevent a member's physical presence, no member shall participate by teleconference pursuant to this ordinance more than twice in one calendar year.
(Ord. No. 20-07-212 , § 1, 7-7-2020)
Secs. 2-8—2-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - PERSONNEL

Sec. 2-31. - Personnel manual.
The city manager shall establish by ordinance a personnel manual for all municipal employees. In addition to sections deemed necessary by the city manager, the manual shall include policies regarding equal employment opportunity, nondiscrimination, and sexual harassment. The manual will be updated from time to time as required by federal and state laws.
(Ord. No. 07-12-02, art. 2, § 1, 12-12-2007; Ord. No. 08-02-26, art. 2, § 1, 2-5-2008; Ord. No. 10-12-80, art. 2, § 1, 12-7-2010)
Sec. 2-32. - Job descriptions.
The city shall maintain a job specification for each position held by a municipal employee. The city may provide a job specification for contracted positions. Such specification shall describe the duties of the position, the qualifications necessary, licenses required, to whom such employee reports, expected salary range, and such other information as is necessary to recruit for such position effectively. Such specifications may be amended from time to time by the city manager.
(Ord. No. 07-12-02, art. 2, § 3, 12-12-2007; Ord. No. 08-02-26, art. 2, § 3, 2-5-2008; Ord. No. 10-12-80, art. 2, § 2, 12-7-2010)
Sec. 2-33. - Benefits.
It shall be the policy of the city to attract and retain a qualified workforce through the provision of a comprehensive package of benefits. To that end, the city shall be authorized to contract with benefits providers for the purpose of providing insurance, retirement, pension plans and other benefits deemed necessary by the council upon approval of the benefit program by the city council. Eligibility for benefits shall be specified in the personnel manual and the annual cost to employees included in the annual operating budget.
(Ord. No. 07-12-02, art. 2, § 4, 12-12-2007; Ord. No. 08-02-26, art. 2, § 4, 2-5-2008; Ord. No. 10-12-80, art. 2, § 3, 12-7-2010)
Sec. 2-34. - Records.
The city shall keep such records and make such reports as may be required by applicable state or federal laws or regulations.
(Ord. No. 07-12-02, art. 2, § 5, 12-12-2007; Ord. No. 08-02-26, art. 2, § 5, 2-5-2008; Ord. No. 10-12-80, art. 2, § 4, 12-7-2010)

ARTICLE IV. - CITY BOARDS AND COMMISSIONS

Sec. 2-77. - Planning commission, parks and recreation commission; authority to create additional boards and commissions.
The city shall have a planning commission and a parks and recreation commission. The city may have additional boards and commissions as may be created by the city council. All boards and commissions shall have the powers and duties and be subject to and governed by the regulations set forth in this article and this Code. The duties of each commission are defined in the corresponding Code section. All members shall be appointed by the mayor and approved by a majority of the city council.
(Ord. No. 07-12-02, art. 4, §§ 1, 2, 12-12-2007; Ord. No. 08-02-26, art. 4, §§ 1, 2, 2-5-2008; Ord. No. 10-12-80, art. 4, §§ 1, 2, 12-7-2010; Ord. No. 12-05-103, art. 4, § 1, 5-1-2012; Ord. No. 12-06-106, art. 4, § 1, 6-5-2012; Ord. No. 12-08-108, art. 4, § 1, 8-7-2012; Ord. No. 12-11-109, § 1, 11-6-2012)
Sec. 2-78. - Planning commission.
(a)
The planning commission shall consist of either five or seven members who shall serve one-year terms, which shall begin on April 1 and end on March 31 of the following year. Members may serve successive terms. Except as provided in subsection (b) of this section or otherwise in this Code, the council shall establish qualifications for members of the planning commission. Each member shall be nominated by the mayor and approved by the council.
(b)
Each member must be a resident of the city.
(c)
Members must attend two-thirds of regularly scheduled meetings within their yearly term. Failure to do so warrants removal from the commission by the city council. This requirement does not include attendance at special called meetings or sub-committee meetings.
(d)
Members filling vacancies shall serve the remainder of the term to which they were appointed. A consecutive appointment is permissible. If no replacement has been appointed at the end of a member's term, they may continue to serve at the pleasure of the mayor and council, except in such cases when a replacement has not been named due to a decrease in the number of members.
(e)
Any member may be removed for cause by a vote of at least three members of the council.
(f)
The planning commission may receive reimbursement for customary expenses previously approved and incurred in conjunction with any authorized activity of the planning commission.
(g)
The planning commission shall meet in accordance with an established schedule of meetings approved by the city council. The chair of the planning commission shall have the authority to cancel any meeting for which no action items or public hearings are scheduled on the agenda provided that notice of such cancellation must be given to the city clerk in sufficient time for the city clerk to provide public notice of such cancellation as required under state law.
(h)
It shall be the function and duty of the planning commission to review and make recommendations for action by the mayor and city council on the following:
(1)
All applications for annexation, rezoning, master plans, variances, and concurrent ordinances;
(2)
Applications for use permits and modifications to same;
(3)
Amendments to the text of the city zoning ordinance, city subdivision regulations, the city comprehensive plan, the future land use map, and the zoning map;
(4)
Perform other functions of the planning commission as may be required by federal and state law;
(5)
Nothing contained herein shall prevent the mayor and city council from hereafter assigning other duties to the planning commission.
(i)
Any application that is to be considered by the planning commission must be submitted to the department of community development in accordance with the established filing schedule as prepared by said department.
(j)
At the close of each public hearing, the chair of the planning commission shall call the members to order for consideration and a vote on the issue currently before them. The vote of the planning commission shall be nonbinding and shall be made in the form of a recommendation to the mayor and city council from the following list:
(1)
Approval as recommended by staff with recommended conditions;
(2)
Approval as recommended by staff with revised conditions as presented by the planning commission;
(3)
Denial.
(k)
The planning commission may move to table an item for a specified period of time in order to receive additional information necessary to make a recommendation to the mayor and city council. Should a request to table an item be made by the applicant and/or the property owner, the planning commission shall consider the request and if granted shall not table said request longer than for a period of 90 days. The 90-day period shall begin on the same day the action is made by the planning commission. If the tabled item is not returned within the prescribed period, the item shall then be automatically withdrawn and shall be required to follow the prescribed and established procedure to re-file applications before the planning commission.
(Ord. No. 08-02-26, art. 4, §§ 3, 4, 8, 2-5-2008; Ord. No. 10-12-80, art. 4, §§ 3, 4, 8, 12-7-2010; Ord. No. 12-05-103, art. 4, § 2, 5-1-2012; Ord. No. 12-06-106, art. 4, § 2, 6-5-2012; Ord. No. 12-08-108, art. 4, § 2, 8-7-2012; Ord. No. 12-11-109, § 2, 11-6-2012)
Sec. 2-79. - Parks and recreation.
(a)
The parks and recreations commission shall consist of not less than five members nor more than seven members who shall serve one-year terms, which shall begin on April 1 and end on March 31 of the following year. Members may serve successive terms. Except as provided in subsection (b) of this section or otherwise in this Code, the council shall establish qualifications for members of the parks and recreations commission.
(b)
Each member shall be nominated by the mayor and approved by the council.
(c)
Members must attend two-thirds of regularly scheduled meetings within their yearly term. Failure to do so warrants removal from the commission.
(d)
Members filling vacancies shall serve the remainder of the term to which they were appointed.
(e)
A consecutive appointment is permissible. If no replacement has been appointed at the end of a member's term, they may continue to serve at the pleasure of the mayor and council, except in such cases when a replacement has not been named due to a decrease in the number of members.
(f)
Any member may be removed for cause by a vote of at least three members of the council.
(g)
The parks and recreation commission may receive reimbursement for customary expenses previously approved and incurred in conjunction with any authorized activity of the parks and recreations commission.
(h)
The parks and recreation commission shall meet in accordance with an established schedule of meetings as approved by the city council. The chair of the parks and recreation commission shall have the authority to cancel any meeting for which no action items or public hearings are scheduled on the agenda provided that notice of such cancellation must be given to the city clerk in sufficient time for the city clerk to provide public notice of such cancellation as required under state law.
(i)
It shall be the function and duty of the parks and recreation commission to perform the following duties:
(1)
Recommend to the mayor and city council policies and procedures for the use of city parks.
(2)
Coordinate between and with volunteer groups that wish to coordinate use of the parks, improvements to the parks, and recreational uses of the parks.
(3)
Make recommendations to the mayor and city council regarding acquisition, improvement, maintenance, and operation of city parks.
(Ord. No. 08-02-26, art. 4, §§ 3, 4, 8, 2-5-2008; Ord. No. 10-12-80, art. 4, §§ 3, 4, 8, 12-7-2010; Ord. No. 12-05-103, art. 4, § 2, 5-1-2012; Ord. No. 12-06-106, art. 4, § 3, 6-5-2012; Ord. No. 12-08-108, art. 4, § 3, 8-7-2012; Ord. No. 12-11-109, § 3, 11-6-2012; Ord. No. 19-06-199 , § 2, 6-6-2019)
Sec. 2-80. - Quorum.
A majority of actual commission members establishes a quorum. Except as set forth herein, any action taken requires a majority of affirmative votes of the quorum present. A quorum is required for all commission meetings. In the event a quorum is not present at a previously scheduled meeting, the commission members in attendance at such meeting shall have the authority to set the next meeting date/time and shall have the right to notify, or caused to be notified, the absent members of such next meeting date/time.
(Ord. No. 07-12-02, art. 4, § 5, 12-12-2007; Ord. No. 08-02-26, art. 4, § 5, 2-5-2008; Ord. No. 10-12-80, art. 4, § 5, 12-7-2010; Ord. No. 12-05-103, art. 4, § 4, 5-1-2012; Ord. No. 12-06-106, art. 4, § 4, 6-5-2012; Ord. No. 12-08-108, art. 4, § 4, 8-7-2012; Ord. No. 12-11-109, § 4, 11-6-2012)
Sec. 2-81. - Procedure.
(a)
Each commission shall adopt the city council's rules and procedures as its own, except for those rules that govern the dates of meetings. Unless a meeting is cancelled as provided herein, the planning commission shall meet on a business day during the week which is one or two weeks immediately following the first Tuesday of each month. The parks commission may determine the date and time of its meetings, but shall meet no less than once every three months. The date and time of each commission meeting as well as agenda items to be considered shall be publicized in the same manner as meetings of mayor and council. Regular commission meetings may be cancelled by the passage of a motion by a majority of a quorum of the commission acting during a meeting or individually by the chair of the commission without a meeting. All meeting cancellations shall be publicized in the same manner as meeting cancellations of mayor and council.
(b)
Each of the commissions shall elect, annually, no later than the second meeting after the start of its annual term, two of its members to serve as chair and vice-chair, respectively.
(c)
All meetings at which official action is taken shall be open to the public and all records maintained by the committee shall be public records unless expressly exempted by a provision of the Georgia Open Records Act. The commissions shall keep minutes of their formal proceedings, and records of their examinations and other official actions, all of which shall be filed in the office of the city clerk. Copies of the minutes shall be sent to the mayor and each member of the council. The minutes of the meetings shall be a public record. This section shall not be construed as prohibiting closed sessions when permitted by the Georgia Open Meetings and Open Records Acts.
(Ord. No. 07-12-02, art. 4, § 6, 12-12-2007; Ord. No. 08-02-26, art. 4, § 6, 2-5-2008; Ord. No. 10-12-80, art. 4, § 6, 12-7-2010; Ord. No. 12-05-103, art. 4, § 5, 5-1-2012; Ord. No. 12-06-106, art. 4, § 5, 6-5-2012; Ord. No. 12-08-108, art. 4, § 5, 8-7-2012; Ord. No. 12-11-109, § 5, 11-6-2012; Ord. No. 14-05-120, 5-6-2014)
Sec. 2-82. - Training.
The council may establish a mandatory training program for commission members.
(Ord. No. 07-12-02, art. 4, § 7, 12-12-2007; Ord. No. 08-02-26, art. 4, § 7, 2-5-2008; Ord. No. 10-12-80, art. 4, § 7, 12-7-2010; Ord. No. 12-05-103, art. 4, § 6, 5-1-2012; Ord. No. 12-06-106, art. 4, § 6, 6-5-2012; Ord. No. 12-08-108, art. 4, § 6, 8-7-2012; Ord. No. 12-11-109, § 6, 11-6-2012)
Sec. 2-83. - Historic commission.
(a)
The historic commission shall be composed of not less than five members nor more than seven members who shall serve one-year terms, which shall begin on April 1 and end on March 31 of the following year. Members may serve successive terms. Except as provided in subsection (b) of this section or otherwise in this Code, the council shall establish qualifications for members of the historic commission.
(b)
Each member shall be nominated by the mayor and approved by the city council.
(c)
Members must attend two-thirds of regularly scheduled meetings within their yearly term. Failure to do so warrants removal from the historic commission.
(d)
Members filling vacancies shall serve the remainder of the term to which they were appointed.
(e)
A consecutive appointment is permissible. If no replacement has been appointed at the end of a member's term, they may continue to serve at the pleasure of the mayor and council, except in such cases when a replacement has not been named due to a decrease in the number of members.
(f)
Any member may be removed for cause by a vote of at least three members of the city council.
(g)
The historic commission members may receive reimbursement for customary expenses previously approved and incurred in conjunction with any authorized activity of the historic commission.
(h)
The historic commission shall meet in accordance with an established schedule of meetings as approved by the city council. The chair of the historic commission shall have the authority to cancel any meeting for which no action items or public hearings are scheduled on the agenda provided that notice of such cancellation must be given to the city clerk in sufficient time for the city clerk to provide public notice of such cancellation as required under state law.
(i)
The powers and responsibilities of the historic commission shall be as follows:
(1)
Prepare an inventory of any property within the city that meets that criteria for consideration as a historic resource, which includes buildings, structures, objects, sites or districts that:
a.
Are associated with events that have made a significant contribution to the broad patterns of our history, or
b.
Are associated with the lives of significant persons in our past, or
c.
Embody the distinctive characteristics of a type, period, or method of construction, or that represent the work of a master, or that possess high artistic values, or that represent a significant and distinguishable entity whose components may lack individual distinction; or
d.
Have yielded or may be likely to yield, information important to history or pre-history.
(2)
Advise on the restoration or preservation of any historic properties acquired by the city.
(3)
Conduct an education program on historic properties located within the city.
(4)
Make investigations and studies of matters relating to historic preservation as the city council may from time to time deem necessary or appropriate.
(5)
Seek out state, federal or private funds for historic preservation and make recommendations to the city council for the use of funds acquired.
(6)
Promote the use of long-term conservation and preservation mechanisms including conservation easements and transfer or purchase of development rights.
(7)
Undertake identification and mapping of gravesites, cemeteries and burial grounds in the city.
(8)
Identify and make recommendations for the preservation of scenic viewsheds and other elements of the cultural landscape that contribute to the unique rural environment of the city as described in the comprehensive plan, the zoning ordinance, and other city documents.
( Ord. No. 19-02-191 , § 2, 2-5-2019; Ord. No. 19-06-199 , § 3, 6-6-2019)

ARTICLE V. - CITY FINANCE

Sec. 2-108. - City accountant.
(a)
The mayor and city manager shall nominate a city accountant, subject to ratification by the council. The city accountant may also serve as the city treasurer, city tax collector, and city finance director. Unless otherwise specified, the terms "city accountant," "city treasurer," and "city tax collector" are used interchangeably in this Code.
(b)
The city may contract with a third party to serve as city accountant.
(c)
The city accountant shall perform at least the following duties.
(1)
Managing, planning, directing, and maintaining the city's financial operations, including serving as chair of the city finance department, if such a department is established;
(2)
Directing and supervising the work of personnel involved in performing the accounting, utility billing, property tax, payroll, and purchasing functions for the city; and
(3)
Providing technical leadership in budgeting, overseeing and directing investment opportunities and debt administration, as well as ensuring proper record retention policies are strictly followed.
(Ord. No. 07-12-02, art. 5, § 1, 12-12-2007; Ord. No. 08-02-26, art. 5, § 1, 2-5-2008; Ord. No. 10-12-80, art. 5, § 1, 12-7-2010)
Sec. 2-109. - City tax collector.
(a)
The mayor and city manager shall nominate a city tax collector, subject to ratification by the council. The city tax collector also may serve as the city treasurer, city accountant, and city finance director.
(b)
The city may contract with a third party, including the tax commissioner of the county, to serve as city tax collector.
(c)
The city tax collector shall perform at least the following duties.
(1)
Managing, planning, and directing the collection of all city taxes.
(2)
Notifying delinquent taxpayers of their status in accordance with state law.
(Ord. No. 07-12-02, art. 5, § 2, 12-12-2007; Ord. No. 08-02-26, art. 5, § 2, 2-5-2008; Ord. No. 10-12-80, art. 5, § 2, 12-7-2010)
