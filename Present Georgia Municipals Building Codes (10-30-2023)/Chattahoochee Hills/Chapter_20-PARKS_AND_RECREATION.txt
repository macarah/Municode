ARTICLE II. - CITY PARK REGULATIONS

Sec. 20-19. - Hours of park operation posted.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to be within the bounds of any city park at any time other than daylight hours; provided, however, that this prohibition will not apply to any persons engaged in any organized activity that has been permitted by the city.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to enter or leave any park except through established entrances or exits and within established time periods.
(c)
It shall be unlawful to gain or attempt to gain admittance to any park or facility in any park where a charge is made, without paying that charge.
(d)
No persons shall congregate or gather within 150 yards of any entrance to a designated park amphitheater during the hours when paid performances are being given in the amphitheater, unless each such person shall:
(1)
Hold a valid ticket for the performances being given on such night, or are in the process of purchasing a ticket for the performance;
(2)
Be delivering goods or services to the amphitheater or to persons holding valid tickets for such performance;
(3)
Be dropping off or picking up passengers who hold valid tickets for such performance; or
(4)
Be situated on private property of which such person is the lawful occupant, family member of such lawful occupant or the expressly invited guest of such lawful occupant.
(Ord. No. 10-03-69, art. 1, § 1, 3-30-2010; Ord. No. 10-12-78, art. 1, § 1, 12-7-2010; Ord. No. 12-11-110, art. 1, § 1, 11-6-2012)
Sec. 20-20. - Public conduct in parks.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to possess and/or consume any alcoholic beverage, or be under the influence of any alcoholic beverage within any public park and/or within any building under the supervision of the parks director unless such usage is expressly permitted by signage or published rules of the particular park or recreation building and approved by the city council or by permit granted by the director of parks and recreation or his designee.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to have in their possession or under his direct control any controlled substance as defined in O.C.G.A. § 16-13-1 on any city property.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to create or maintain a nuisance or disturbance or engage in any disorderly conduct as defined in section 18-41, which, because of noise, use of vile language or danger to the health, safety or morals of the citizens of the city or users of a city park, interferes with the use and enjoyment of a park.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person to destroy or injure any public property, real or personal, belonging to, owned by, leased to or used by the city.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for any person to disregard the notices, prohibitions or directions on any park sign.
(f)
Except as otherwise permitted in this article, it shall be unlawful for any person to place or display any commercial sign or any advertisement on city property other than political signs or advertisements which may be displayed but which shall not be affixed in any manner to city property. This rule shall not apply to any signs or advertisements regarding any city-sponsored or city-authorized program, festival, or other event that occurs in the park. Signs or advertisements regarding city-sponsored or city-authorized programs, festivals or other events may be placed or displayed only with written permission by the director of parks and recreation or his designee, and only at the locations and during the times authorized in writing by the director of parks and recreation or his designee.
(g)
No sign, banner, or any other item shall be hung from a tree located in the park unless it is hung in a manner that does not puncture, constrict, or otherwise damage the tree. Any rope, line, or other apparatus used to hang items from trees shall be removed entirely before departure from the park or the end of the event.
(h)
It shall be unlawful for any person to knowingly, or through reckless behavior, destroy or damage the lawn, plants, walls, road or other infrastructure of a park.
(i)
It shall be unlawful for any person to use a playscape, park bench, picnic table, restroom or facilities for other than its intended use and/or purpose.
(j)
It shall be unlawful for any person to play, practice or otherwise participate in any game, sport or other recreational activity that is destructive to the lawn, plants, walls, road, or other infrastructure of a park, except at or upon places designated for that game, sport or recreational activity.
(k)
It shall be unlawful for any person to use any area for changing clothing, other than an area designated for that purpose.
(l)
It shall be unlawful for any person to drive stakes, posts, poles or any other device or dig holes for the purpose of securing stakes, posts, poles or any other device for any reason, including to erect a tent, stage or other structure, except by permit issued by the director of parks and recreation or his designee.
(m)
It shall be unlawful for any person to bring in, erect, or permit a minor child in one's custody to bring in and/or erect any inflatable amusements, amusements requiring water, or any amusements requiring an independent power source, except during permitted special events and festivals. This rule shall not apply to lakes or rivers. For purposes of this subsection, inflatable amusements shall not include balls or balloons, and amusements requiring water shall not include water pistols.
(n)
It shall be unlawful for any person to erect a tent or canopy with a finished dimension greater than 12 feet by 12 feet, or be in possession of an erected tent or canopy with the same dimensions, without receiving the written permission of the director of parks and recreation or his designee, as well as all other applicable permits required by the city.
(o)
It shall be unlawful for any person to bring in, carry, possess, or permit a minor child in one's custody to bring in, carry or possess, glass containers except for authorized vendors who shall retain and remove from the park all glass containers utilized in their activities. This rule shall not apply to designated park amphitheater areas when performances are being given therein.
(p)
It shall be unlawful for any person to hunt, trap, shoot, maim or kill any animal or wildlife, or attempt to do any of the above to any animal or wildlife within any of the city parks without the written permission of the city council, unless threatened with bodily injury or death, except as may be permitted by state law.
(q)
It shall be unlawful for any person to abandon any animal in a park. All pets brought into a park must be on a leash or tether. Persons with pets must clean up after the pet.
(r)
All horses that are brought to a city park must have proof of a current negative Coggins Test. Any out-of-state transport of horses into the city is required to have a veterinarian's health certificate.
(s)
It shall be unlawful for any person to pollute or disturb any river, stream, spring, branch, pond, fountain or other water owned by or leased to the city within a park facility. No person shall climb or lie upon any tree, shrub, fence, statue, monument or fountain.
(t)
Littering or dumping of household trash or other refuse is prohibited on park property. It is prohibited to dispose of charcoal anywhere inside of a park other than in city-provided receptacles that are clearly marked for charcoal disposal.
(u)
Campfires are prohibited on park property except in designated, permitted areas. All campfires in the parks must receive permission from the director of parks and recreation or his designee, who shall maintain a registry of parties receiving such permission and shall operate under the guidance of the city fire chief.
(Ord. No. 10-03-69, art. 1, § 2, 3-30-2010; Ord. No. 10-12-78, art. 1, § 2, 12-7-2010; Ord. No. 12-11-110, art. 1, § 2, 11-6-2012)
Sec. 20-21. - Parks activities.
Except by special permit granted by the director of parks and recreation or his designee, the following activities are prohibited in the parks:
(1)
Tents or canopies outside marked primitive camping areas. All tents or canopies must have a finished dimension less than 12 feet by 12 feet.
(2)
Moonwalks, dunk tanks, or any equipment that calls for independent power.
(3)
All games and activities that could damage any lawns.
(4)
Amplified music. When amplified music is permitted, it shall cease by 10:30 p.m. and shall comply with section 18-7, governing noise and public nuisances.
(5)
Sale of food or other items.
(6)
Filming or photographing for commercial purposes.
(Ord. No. 10-12-78, art. 1, § 3, 12-7-2010; Ord. No. 12-11-110, art. 1, § 3, 11-6-2012)
Sec. 20-22. - Additional regulations.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to drive any vehicle upon or across any part of any public park of the city, except upon roadways laid out and maintained for vehicular travel. This section shall not apply to city employees whose duties require them to drive park maintenance equipment over the park, contractors or volunteers with explicit permission from the director of parks and recreation or the director of public works, or park users who receive permission from the director of parks and recreation or his designee for the purposes of loading or unloading only.
(b)
The parking of vehicles by users of a park shall be allowed in approved parking areas in the public parks of the city, as long as such parking is in accordance with traffic laws, rules and regulations of the parks department, and provided the occupants of automobiles do not create a disturbance or violate any law or ordinance of the city or the state.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any vehicle to be parked on any of the drives, avenues or parking lots in any public park outside the posted hours of park operations, except for activities permitted by the city.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person operating a motor vehicle upon any road within any of the city parks to exceed the speed of 15 miles per hour, with the exception of Cochran Mill Road where the posted speed limit shall prevail.
(e)
Any person who shall interfere with the use of a park or violate any ordinance of the city, park rules or regulations, or state act may be required to immediately leave the park by any authorized park employee, code enforcement officer or law enforcement officer, and thereafter such offender shall not return to the park or use park facilities for a period of 24 hours.
(f)
The parks commission shall have the authority to recommend to council amendments and/or changes to the rules, regulations and procedures relating to parks and parks-related matters.
(g)
It shall be unlawful for any person or group to make a change to any city's park or park property without the written permission of the director of parks and recreation or his designee.
(Ord. No. 10-12-78, art. 1, § 4, 12-7-2010; Ord. No. 12-11-110, art. 1, § 4, 11-6-2012)
Sec. 20-23. - Enforcement.
The police department and/or city code enforcement shall have jurisdiction to enforce municipal and state laws in city parks.
(Ord. No. 10-03-69, art. 1, § 3, 3-30-2010; Ord. No. 10-12-78, art. 2, § 1, 12-7-2010; Ord. No. 12-11-110, art. 2, § 1, 11-6-2012)
Sec. 20-24. - Penalty.
Violation of this section shall be punishable as a misdemeanor in the municipal court and, in addition to the penalties therein imposed, a report of each conviction under this section shall be given to the director of the parks and recreation department of the city, who shall have the power, after taking all facts and circumstances into consideration, to suspend use of the facilities of the parks and recreation department for a period not to exceed one year for each offense.
(Ord. No. 10-03-69, art. 1, § 4, 3-30-2010; Ord. No. 10-12-78, art. 2, § 2, 12-7-2010; Ord. No. 12-11-110, art. 2, § 2, 11-6-2012)
Sec. 20-25. - Defacement, harm, or damage.
It shall be unlawful for any person to deface, harm or damage any park buildings, wildlife, property, equipment or signs located in any park owned by the City of Chattahoochee Hills. It shall be unlawful for any person to dig up, cut, damage or remove any trees, tree limbs, shrubbery, flowers and other vegetation, rocks, mulch, historical artifacts, shell casings or bullets, arrowheads, or other items of previous occupants of the land that now comprise the parks without first obtaining a permit issued by the City of Chattahoochee Hills.
(Ord. No. 19-08-204 , § 1, 8-6-2019)
Sec. 20-26. - Bicycles and horses.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to ride a bicycle or horse on the trails of the parks when any sign posted by the city at the entrance to the park states that the trails are closed. No person shall be subject to a fine or other penalty under this ordinance unless such person gained entrance to the park by traveling past such sign stating the trails are closed.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to ride a bicycle or horse on the trails of the parks when the trails are wet and muddy to the extent there is standing water in puddles on the trails. No person shall be subject to a fine or other penalty under this ordinance unless such person shall have received a written warning of a previous violation of this ordinance within the previous 12 months by a law enforcement officer of the city.
(Ord. No. 19-08-204 , § 2, 8-6-2019)
Secs. 20-27—20-51. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - PARKING AT COCHRAN MILL PARK AND HUTCHESON FERRY PARK

Sec. 20-52. - Short title.
This article shall be known as the "Cochran Mill and Hutcheson Ferry Parking Ordinance."
(Ord. No. 13-03-113, art. 3, § 1, 3-5-2013)
Sec. 20-53. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Motor vehicle has the same definition as proscribed in O.C.G.A. § 40-1-1(33);
School trip means off-campus travel by students of a particular school, sanctioned by that school, to Cochran Mill Park or Hutcheson Ferry Park as part of an educational class.
(Ord. No. 13-03-113, art. 3, § 2, 3-5-2013)
Sec. 20-54. - Applicability.
(a)
The terms of this article shall apply to any motor vehicle which is unattended in the parking lot of Cochran Mill Park or Hutcheson Ferry Park.
(b)
Any motor vehicle subject to this article must display a parking pass at all times.
(c)
Motor vehicles that are only involved in active loading or unloading are exempt from this article.
(Ord. No. 13-03-113, art. 3, § 3, 3-5-2013)
Sec. 20-55. - Types of parking passes.
(a)
Resident passes. These are annual passes provided to residents of the city free of charge. Passes may only be used between sunup and sundown except at city-sanctioned events, city-approved events or in conjunction with city-permitted activities during which these time restrictions shall not apply. A resident may request passes for any and all motor vehicles registered to that resident. These passes shall be active from the date received until the end of the calendar year in which they are issued.
(b)
Annual passes. These are passes that are active from the purchase date until the end of the calendar year in which they are purchased. Passes may only be used between sunup and sundown except at city-sanctioned events, city-approved events or in conjunction with city-permitted activities during which these time restrictions shall not apply. The license plate of the motor vehicle shall be recorded and the annual pass shall only be displayed in that motor vehicle. These passes shall not be pro-rated based on the date of purchase.
(1)
For motor vehicles with three or more axles or for motor vehicles with anything in tow, annual passes shall cost $35.00.
(2)
For all other motor vehicles, annual passes shall cost $25.00.
(c)
Daily passes. These are passes that are active only for the date indicated on the pass and are only valid between sunup and sundown on that date except at city-sanctioned events, city-approved events or in conjunction with city-permitted activities during which these time restrictions shall not apply.
(1)
For motor vehicles with three or more axles or for motor vehicles with anything in tow, daily passes shall cost $10.00.
(2)
For all other motor vehicles, daily passes shall cost $5.00.
(d)
School trip passes. These are passes that are active only for the date indicate on the pass and are only valid between sunup and sundown on that date except at city-sanctioned events, city-approved events or in conjunction with city-permitted activities during which these time restrictions shall not apply. These passes are provided free of charge to an educational facility and are to be used for motor vehicles transporting students to and from Cochran Mill Park or Hutcheson Ferry Park for a school trip.
(Ord. No. 13-03-113, art. 3, § 4, 3-5-2013)
Sec. 20-56. - Obtaining parking passes.
(a)
Resident passes and annual passes may be obtained at city hall during regular business hours.
(b)
Daily passes may be obtained at Cochran Mill Park or Hutcheson Ferry Park on the day the pass is to be used.
(c)
School trip passes may be obtained at city hall during regular business hours or at other locations selected by the city.
(d)
Groups renting the pavilion or field at Cochran Mill Park or Hutcheson Ferry Park will be provided ten daily passes free of charge.
(e)
If someone purchases a $25.00 annual pass, they may upgrade to the $35.00 annual pass at any time by bringing the $25.00 annual pass and $10.00 to city hall during regular business hours.
(Ord. No. 13-03-113, art. 3, § 5, 3-5-2013)
Sec. 20-57. - Enforcement.
(a)
The city police department shall enforce compliance with this article. Additionally, at least one video camera and a sign with the wording "For Your Safety, Park Monitored by Police" may be installed at each park.
(b)
Failure to display the appropriate parking pass shall result in a fine of $25.00. No motor vehicle may be cited for failure to display the appropriate parking pass more than one time each day. Failure to display the appropriate parking pass includes failure to display any parking pass.
(c)
Failure to leave the parking lot of Cochran Mill Park or Hutcheson Ferry Park within one hour of sundown or arrival more than one hour prior to sunup, except at city-sanctioned events, city-approved events or in conjunction with city-permitted activities during which these time restrictions shall not apply, shall result in a fine of $25.00. No motor vehicle may be cited for this offense more than one time per sundown-to-sunup period.
(Ord. No. 13-03-113, art. 3, § 6, 3-5-2013; Ord. No. 16-06-154, § 1, 6-7-2016; Ord. No. 20-12-216 , § 1, 12-3-2020)
