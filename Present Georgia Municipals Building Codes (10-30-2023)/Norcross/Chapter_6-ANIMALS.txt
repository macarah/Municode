Sec. 6-1. - County animal control ordinance—Adopted.
The Gwinnett County Animal Control Ordinance, as now or hereafter amended, as adopted by ordinance of the County Board of Commissioners on January 16, 2007, is hereby adopted by reference and incorporated herein as if fully set out as a general ordinance of the city to the extent that the animal control ordinance is not inconsistent with the city local amendments under this chapter. A copy of the current animal control ordinance and any additions, deletions or local amendments thereto shall be maintained in the Office of the City Clerk and be available for inspection by the public.
(Code 1979, § 4-4-1; Code 1998, § 10-1)
Sec. 6-2. - Same—Local amendments.
For the purposes of this chapter, the following sections of the animal control ordinance are amended as follows:
Sec. 10-26. Definitions. This section is amended by adding the following: "in the unincorporated area of Gwinnett County" to read "inside the City of Norcross."
Sec. 10-28. Adoption of ordinance by municipalities within county boundaries. All persons owning animals in the city may buy permits from the Gwinnett County animal control unit and will receive the same benefits as those who live in the unincorporated areas. All license fees shall be retained by Gwinnett County to defray the cost of the operation of the animal control unit. The Gwinnett County animal control unit is hereby given the power to enforce the city animal control ordinance in the city.
(Code 1979, § 4-4-2; Code 1998, § 10-2)
Sec. 6-3. - Restraint and muzzling of dogs.
The City Manager or officer designated by him may restrain or order to be restrained or muzzled any dog for any of the following:
(1)
Being found at-large or unmuzzled, as the case may be, while an order of the City Manager or officer designated by him for the restraint or muzzling of such dog in is effect;
(2)
Being found in a school, schoolyard, church, on the grounds of a church, or public recreational area;
(3)
Having bitten or otherwise injured any person;
(4)
Having killed, maimed, or otherwise caused damage to another domesticated animal;
(5)
Having caused damage to real personal property owned by an individual other than the person owning or harboring said dog;
(6)
Chasing any vehicle upon any public way or on any way to which the public has a right of access.
(Ord. No. 09-2005, § 10-3, 12-5-2005)
Sec. 6-4. - Removal of canine fecal matter.
It shall be unlawful for any person owning, possessing, harboring or having care, charge, control or custody of any dog not to remove any feces left by that dog on any sidewalk, gutter, street, lot or public area. Dog waste shall be immediately removed by placing said matter in a closed or sealed container and thereafter disposing of it in a trash receptacle, sanitary disposal unit or other closed or sealed refuse container.
(Ord. No. 09-2005, § 10-4, 12-5-2005)
Sec. 6-5. - Cruelty.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to overload, cruelly treat, maim, bruise, deprive of necessary sustenance, ill-use or, in any manner whatsoever, torture or abuse any animal.
(b)
Each and every violation of this section shall be punishable by fines of $25.00 and not more than $300.00.
(Ord. No. 09-2005, § 10-5, 12-5-2005)
