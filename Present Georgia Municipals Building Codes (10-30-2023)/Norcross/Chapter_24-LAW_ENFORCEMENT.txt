ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 24-1. - Chief of Police; powers and duties.
The Chief of Police, or police officers under his direction, shall:
(1)
Attend all meetings and municipal court sessions or as directed by the City Manager.
(2)
Execute all orders and directions of the City Manager.
(3)
Abate and remove any nuisance upon direction by the judge of the municipal court.
(4)
Maintain the peace, good order and tranquility of the city by the enforcement of all municipal ordinances and state or federal laws.
(5)
Place upon the municipal court docket the names of all persons violating any of the municipal ordinances and arrest and take into custody all persons who violate any city ordinances, state, or federal laws.
(6)
Under the general direction of the City Manager, be in charge of the Police Department and have supervision over the members thereof in the exercise of their powers, duties and authority and in hiring, discharging or suspending any members.
(7)
Should any arrested person be jailed at the order of a police officer, enter or cause to be entered the name of the person arrested; the date of arrest; and the name of the arresting officer or other person, on the station docket.
(8)
Post with the city a performance bond for the faithful performance of duties in an amount set by resolution of the City Council payable to the city.
(9)
Serve all subpoenas and warrants of the municipal court.
(10)
Approve appearance bonds.
(11)
Issue and serve summons for any violation of any law or ordinance and serve all processes, executions, attachments and the like of the Chief, City Clerk, Municipal Court, General Government Administration Department, City Manager, or the Mayor and City Council.
(12)
Deliver all monies received by any member of the Police Department while acting in the capacity of a law enforcement officer for the city or while executing administrative duties to the General Government Administration Department.
(13)
Maintain a docket with entries therein on all executions issued by authority of the city showing any payments thereon.
(Code 1979, § 4-1-1; Code 1998, § 50-1; Ord. No. 18-95, § V, 11-6-1995)
Sec. 24-2. - Patrol powers.
(a)
The police officers of the city shall have the authority to patrol over any street, alley, path, sidewalk, or park, in and around any stores, cafes, taverns, restaurants, dancehalls, service stations or any other establishment in the city.
(b)
An officer while on duty for the city shall have authority to enter any type of business while open to the public and check to see if it is being carried on in a lawful manner. After business hours, and while closed to the public, an officer for the city has the authority to check places of business, to see if the doors and windows are safely locked.
(c)
An officer for the city shall have the authority to check and investigate, enter and patrol any park, business, residence or public place within the city limits if the officer has probable cause or sufficient reason to believe some act is being committed which would involve the violation of a provision of this Code, state law or city ordinance, a personal injury or damage to property, or if any of the above appear to be imminent.
(d)
No police officer shall enter a private dwelling without a search or arrest warrant unless he has the voluntary consent of the owner or occupant or he is in pursuit of a fugitive whom the officer has personal knowledge or probable cause to believe has committed or attempted to commit a felony.
(Code 1979, § 4-1-2; Code 1998, § 50-2)
Sec. 24-3. - Abuse of position.
(a)
No police officer shall use official position or official identification cards or badges for:
(1)
Personal or financial gain;
(2)
Obtaining privileges not otherwise available to the officer except in the performance of duty; or
(3)
Avoiding consequences of illegal acts.
(b)
Police officers may not lend their identification cards or badges to another person, or permit them to be photographed or reproduced without the approval of the Chief.
(Code 1979, § 4-1-3; Code 1998, § 50-3)
Sec. 24-4. - Abuse of process; withholding evidence.
Police officers shall not convert to their own use, manufacture, conceal, falsify, destroy, remove, tamper with, or withhold evidence or information, or make false accusations of a criminal or traffic charge.
(Code 1979, § 4-1-4; Code 1998, § 50-4)
Sec. 24-5. - Departmental reports; truthfulness.
Police officers shall submit all necessary reports on time and in accordance with established departmental procedures. Reports submitted by officers shall be truthful and complete and no officer shall knowingly enter or cause to be entered any inaccurate, false, misleading or improper information.
(Code 1979, § 4-1-5; Code 1998, § 50-5)
Sec. 24-6. - Endorsements and referrals.
Police officers shall not recommend or suggest in any manner, except in the transaction of personal business, the employment or procurement of a particular product, professional service or commercial service such as an attorney, ambulance, towing service, bondsman, mortician or the like. In the case of ambulance or towing service, when such service is necessary and the person needing the service is unable or unwilling to procure it or requests assistance, officers shall proceed in accordance with established departmental procedures.
(Code 1979, § 4-1-6; Code 1998, § 50-6)
Sec. 24-7. - police cars; riders.
No person, other than a city employee, or person duly deputized, or city officials, shall at any time be permitted to be a passenger in a police car, unless such person is involved in an accident and needing medical attention or is participating in an official departmental activity.
(Code 1979, § 4-1-7; Code 1998, § 50-7; Ord. No. 18-95, § V, 11-6-1995)
Secs. 24-8—24-32. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - RESERVE POLICE FORCE[2]

Sec. 24-33. - Supplemental nature of article.
Nothing contained in this article shall be construed to abrogate or impair the powers of the courts or any department of the city to enforce any provisions of its Charter or its ordinances or regulations, nor to prevent or punish violations thereof, and the powers conferred by this chapter shall be in addition to and supplemental to the powers conferred by any other law, the Charter, or this Code.
(Code 1979, § 4-8-3(d); Code 1998, § 50-41)
Sec. 24-34. - Creation, supervision.
There is established in the city a reserve police force which shall be operated under the supervision and direction of the Chief of Police.
(Code 1979, § 4-8-1; Code 1998, § 50-42)
Sec. 24-35. - Approval, certificate of members.
No person shall be permitted to serve in the reserve police force until such time as that person is certified by all applicable state agencies and that person's service on the reserve police force has been approved by the Chief of Police.
(Code 1979, § 4-8-2; Code 1998, § 50-43)
Sec. 24-36. - Oath.
The members of the reserve police force shall take and subscribe the oath of office as regular police officers of the city.
(Code 1979, § 4-8-1; Code 1998, § 50-44)
Sec. 24-37. - Training.
The Chief of Police shall establish such training programs as deemed necessary in order to properly train the members of the reserve police force.
(Code 1979, § 4-8-3(a); Code 1998, § 50-45)
Sec. 24-38. - Compensation.
Members of the reserve police force shall receive no compensation.
(Code 1979, § 4-8-3(b); Code 1998, § 50-46)
Sec. 24-39. - Powers, regulations.
The members of the reserve police force shall have only the powers specifically delegated to them by the Chief of Police and shall at all times be bound by the specific regulations enacted by the city that pertain to the reserve police force together with all applicable regulations contained in the Police Department's standard operating procedures manual.
(Code 1979, § 4-8-1; Code 1998, § 50-47)
Sec. 24-40. - Status of membership.
No member of the reserve police force shall have any constitutionally protected right to a position with the city, may be disciplined or dismissed by the Chief of Police with or without notice and without the right to hearing, including any name-clearing hearing, and shall at all times be treated by the city as a volunteer at will whose service may be terminated with or without cause.
(Code 1979, § 4-8-3(c); Code 1998, § 50-48)
Sec. 24-41. - Employee benefits denied.
Members of the reserve police force shall not be eligible for any employee benefits provided by the city to its work force, including, but not limited to, hospitalization and disability insurance, hourly pay, overtime pay, sick leave, vacation, personal time, comp time, pension benefits and/or supplemental pay.
(Code 1979, § 4-8-3(d); Code 1998, § 50-49)
Sec. 24-42. - Call to service.
The members of the reserve police force shall be subject to call to service by the Chief of Police at such times as their assistance shall be required by the Chief of Police.
(Code 1979, § 4-8-1; Code 1998, § 50-50)
