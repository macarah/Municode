ARTICLE VIII. - TELEPHONE COMPANIES

Sec. 16-243. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Recurring local service revenues means:
(1)
Monthly charges for local exchange service, including:
a.
Charges for additional listings and joint users;
b.
The guarantee portion of the charge for semipublic pay station services;
c.
Charges for local message rate service, including mobile service messages; and
d.
Subscriber station revenues from teletypewriter exchange service.
(2)
Charges for Morse transmission, signaling, data transmission, metering and supervisory control, where both terminal points are within the city limits.
(3)
All charges for local private line services (except audio and video program transmission service) where both terminals of the private line are within the city limits.
Nothing in this definition shall preclude the charging of a separate franchise fee or tax for the transmission of audio or video programs to customers.
(Code 1979, § 3-2-17; Code 1998, § 38-296; Ord. No. 2-95, 4-3-1995)
Sec. 16-244. - Franchise fee imposed.
Notwithstanding the provisions in this article to the contrary, each telephone company operating in the city shall pay a franchise fee in the amount of three percent of the recurring local service revenues received by the company from subscribers located within the city.
(Code 1979, § 3-2-17(a); Code 1998, § 38-297; Ord. No. 2-95, 4-3-1995)
Sec. 16-245. - Payment.
The franchise fee shall be paid in quarterly installments, each of which shall be due on or before the last day the second month following the end of each calendar quarter and shall be based on recurring local service revenues billed during such calendar quarter.
(Code 1979, § 3-2-17(a); Code 1998, § 38-298; Ord. No. 2-95, 4-3-1995)
