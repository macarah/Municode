ARTICLE II. - FOOD SERVICE ESTABLISHMENTS

Sec. 20-19. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Health Officer means the Environmental County Manager or his authorized representative.
Nuisance means whatever is dangerous or detrimental to human life or health and whatever renders or tends to render soil, air, water or food impure or unwholesome.
Person means a person, firm, corporation, society or association.
(Code 2005, § 30-31; Ord. of 1-30-1990, § 1-2)
Sec. 20-20. - Rules and regulations adopted.
The rules and regulations of the State Department of Human Resources, titled and published as Ga. Comp. Rules and Regs. ch. 511-6-1, Food Service, is adopted as written.
(Code 2005, § 30-32; Ord. of 1-30-1990, § 1)
Sec. 20-21. - Inspection fee.
A yearly inspection fee, payable to the County, shall be paid in full on or before August 1 of each year.
(Code 2005, § 30-33; Ord. of 7-11-1995, § 2)
Sec. 20-22. - Citations.
(a)
Upon information being made known to the Health Officer or his designee, he shall cause a citation to be issued requiring said violator to appear before the Judge of the Magistrate Court on a day and time certain, then and there to stand trial for violation of this regulation.
(b)
Citations issued hereunder shall be pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63 and shall be personally served upon the person accused. Each citation shall state the time and place at which the accused is to appear for trial.
(c)
The County Environmental Health Specialists are authorized to issue citations for the enforcement of this article as authorized by the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63.
(Code 2005, § 30-34; Ord. of 1-30-1990, § 1-5; Ord. of 7-11-1995, § 1)
Sec. 20-23. - Violations; penalties.
(a)
Any person violating the terms of this article, or rules and regulations promulgated pursuant thereto, may be punished by section 1-13. Separate offenses shall be deemed to be committed on each day during or on which a violation occurs or continues.
(b)
In addition to all other provisions of this article, any violation of this article deemed to be a continuing nuisance may be abated by an application for injunction in the Superior Court of the County or other court of competent jurisdiction.
(Code 2005, § 30-35; Ord. of 1-30-1990, § 1-6)

ARTICLE IV. - PROPERTY MAINTENANCE CODE

Sec. 20-80. - Adopted.
A certain document, 3 copies of which are on file in the Office of the Chairperson of Walker County, Georgia, being marked and designated as the International Property Maintenance Code, 2021 edition, excluding Appendix "B", but otherwise including any future editions, supplements, and amendments, including local amendments adopted by the county, as published by the International Code Council, is hereby adopted as the Property Maintenance Code of Walker County, Georgia, for regulating and governing the conditions and maintenance of all property, buildings and structures; by providing the standards for supplied utilities and facilities and other physical things and conditions essential to ensure that structures are safe, sanitary and fit for occupation and use; and the condemnation of buildings and structures unfit for human occupancy and use, and the demolition of such existing structures as herein provided; providing for the issuance of permits and collection of fees therefor; and each and all of the regulations, provisions, penalties, conditions and terms of said Property Maintenance Code on file in the Office of the Chairperson of Walker County, Georgia, are hereby referred to, adopted, and made a part hereof, as if fully set out in this article, with additions, insertions, deletions and changes, if any, prescribed in this article.
(Ord. of 2-19-2015, § 1; Ord. No. O-02-22, § 1, 3-10-2022)
Sec. 20-81. - Code Official designated.
The Chairperson of Walker County, Georgia, shall appoint a property maintenance inspection official designated as the Code Official as prescribed in section 103 of the International Property Maintenance Code, 2021 edition, excluding Appendix "B", but otherwise including any future editions, supplements, and amendments, including local amendments adopted by the county, as published by the International Code Council.
(Ord. of 2-19-2015, § 2; Ord. No. O-02-22, § 2, 3-10-2022)
Sec. 20-82. - Validity.
If any section, subsection, sentence, clause or phrase of this article is, for any reason, held to be unconstitutional, such decision shall not affect the validity of the remaining portions of this article. The Board of Commissioners hereby declares that it would have passed this article, and each section, subsection, clause or phrase thereof, irrespective of the fact that any 1 or more sections, subsections, sentences, clauses and phrases be declared unconstitutional.
(Ord. of 2-19-2015, § 3)
Sec. 20-83. - Article does not affect court proceedings.
Nothing in this article or in the Property Maintenance Code hereby adopted shall be construed to affect any suit or proceeding impending in any court, or any rights acquired, or liability incurred, or any cause of action acquired or existing, under any act or ordinance hereby repealed as cited in section 20-82; nor shall any just or legal right or remedy of any character be lost, impaired or affected by this article.
(Ord. of 2-19-2015, § 4)
Sec. 20-84. - Amendments.
(a)
Section 302.8 of the International Property Maintenance Code adopted by Walker County is deleted in its entirety and replaced with the following:
Section 302.8; Motor vehicles. Except as provided for in other regulations, no more than 2 inoperable or unlicensed motor vehicles shall be parked, kept or stored outside on any premises; such vehicles must be covered with a tarp or vehicle covering and parked behind a building, home or other structure obstructing the view from the road. All vehicles of any type are permitted to undergo major overhaul, including mechanical and body work, provided that such work is performed inside a structure, such as a garage, or similarly enclosed area designed and approved for such purposes. Painting of vehicles is prohibited unless conducted inside an approved spray booth.
(b)
Section 308.2 of the International Property Maintenance Code adopted by Walker County is deleted in its entirety and replaced with the following:
Section 308.2; Disposal of rubbish. Every occupant of a structure shall dispose of all rubbish in a clean and sanitary manner by placing such rubbish in a plastic or galvanized trash container with a lid until removed from the structure and disposed of at a rubbish storage facility or garbage facility.
(c)
Section 308.3 of the International Property Maintenance Code adopted by Walker County is deleted in its entirety and replaced with the following:
Section 308.3; Disposal of garbage. Every occupant of a structure shall dispose of garbage in a clean and sanitary manner by placing such garbage in a plastic or galvanized trash container with a lid until removed from the premises for disposal at a garbage disposal facility.
(Ord. No. O-02-20, §§ 1—3, 10-22-2020)
