ARTICLE IV. - SOLID WASTE FLOW CONTROL
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 28-474. - Intent.
(a)
It is the intent of the Walker County Board of Commissioners in enacting this article to provide for the uniform collection and disposal of solid waste within unincorporated Walker County, and in the incorporated areas to the extent possible, to affirm that Walker County has all power and authority necessary to manage waste generated within unincorporated Walker County and disposed of therein, and to curb thereby the desecration of the beauty of the County, which is harmful to the health, welfare and safety of its citizens.
(b)
This article governs the management of solid waste in Walker County, including, but not limited to, collector permit requirements, permit procedures, vehicle requirements, transportation and disposal of solid waste, waste acceptance policies, and enforcement.
(Res. of 7-22-2009, § 50-3.1)
Sec. 28-475. - Purpose.
The purposes of this article include, but are not necessarily limited to, the following:
(1)
To provide environmentally sound, cost-efficient solid waste management in accordance with the Georgia Comprehensive Solid Waste Management Act, O.C.G.A. § 12-8-20 et seq., the rules and regulations promulgated by the Environmental Protection Division of the Georgia Department of Natural Resources, and the Walker County Solid Waste Management Plan, as amended from time to time.
(2)
To protect the health, welfare, and safety of the County's citizens and to protect and enhance the quality of the environment.
(3)
To provide for the safe and proper handling of waste generated, collected, and/or disposed of within unincorporated Walker County.
(4)
To provide a convenient and effective means of financing Walker County's waste management services.
(5)
To provide for safe operation at the facilities of Walker County Solid Waste Management.
(6)
To secure data for solid waste management planning both for Walker County and its cities as part of a solid waste management plan.
(7)
To provide for the uniform handling of scrap tires within unincorporated Walker County and to prevent the illegal dumping of scrap tires, in accordance with State rules and regulations.
(8)
To provide for the regulation of hazardous waste disposal within unincorporated Walker County.
(Res. of 7-22-2009, § 50-3.2)
Sec. 28-476. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Bio-medical waste means all waste defined as bio-medical waste by the Solid Waste Management Act, or by the rules and regulations promulgated by the Environmental Protection Division under the authority of that Act. It also includes wastes such as human tissues, organs, and body parts and fluids that are removed during surgery or autopsy, or other medical procedures and specimens of body fluids and their containers.
Bulky waste means such items as large household appliances (i.e., washing machines, clothes dryers, water heaters, stoves, refrigerators and dishwashers), furniture, tree stumps, large timber, car bodies, large bundles of carpet waste, large packing containers, tanks, and other items whose volumes are not significantly reduced through normal compaction methods.
Business day means any day the County facilities are designated by the Director to be open for business.
Collector means a person who, pursuant to agreements, verbal or written, with or without compensation, does the work of collecting and/or transporting solid wastes from industries, offices, retail outlets, businesses, and/or similar locations, or from residential dwellings; provided, however, this definition shall not include an individual who collects and/or transports waste from a single-family dwelling unit owned or occupied by such individual.
Collector permit means a permit required to engage in the business of solid waste collecting within unincorporated Walker County and issued by Walker County in accordance with sections hereinafter.
Commercial solid waste means all waste defined as commercial solid waste by the Solid Waste Management Act, or by the rules and regulations promulgated by the Environmental Protection Division under the authority of that Act.
Director means the Director of the Walker County Solid Waste Management Facilities.
Disposal or dispose means the discharge, deposit, discard, injection, dumping, spilling, leading, throwing, or placing of any solid waste or hazardous waste into or on any land or water.
Disposal facility means any facility or location where any treatment, utilization, processing or deposition of solid waste occurs.
Disposal operation means the performance of solid waste disposal, which includes administration, personnel, land, equipment, design and other elements necessary or used in the work of solid waste disposal.
Disposal site means the location where the final deposition of solid waste occurs.
Dump means to throw, discard, place, deposit, discharge, bury, burn, or dispose of a substance, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 16-7-51.
Environmental Protection Division means the Environmental Protection Division of the Georgia Department of Natural Resources, including, but not limited to, its Director, and any successor entity or entities.
Hazardous waste means all waste defined as hazardous waste by the Hazardous Waste Management Act, or by the rules and regulations promulgated by the Environmental Protection Division under the authority of that Act.
Hazardous Waste Management Act means the Georgia Hazardous Waste Management Act, codified at O.C.G.A. §§ 12-8-60 through 12-8-83, as the same has been heretofore or may be hereafter amended.
Household hazardous waste means hazardous waste used or generated in the household or similar source or any item, product or material containing or contaminated by any household hazardous waste.
Industrial solid waste means all waste defined as industrial solid waste by the Solid Waste Management Act, or by the rules and regulations promulgated by the Environmental Protection Division under the authority of that Act.
Municipal solid waste means any solid waste derived from households, including garbage, trash, and sanitary waste in septic tanks and means any solid waste from single-family and multifamily residences, hotels and motels, bunkhouses, campgrounds, picnic grounds, and day use recreation areas.
Person means an individual, trust, firm, joint stock company, corporation (including a government corporation), consortium, joint venture, commercial entity, partnership, association, state, municipality, authority, commission, political subdivision of a state, or any interstate body and shall include each department, agency, and instrumentality of the United States.
Processed tires are all those tires used on motor vehicles which have been chipped or sliced. Sliced tires are tires which have been cut in half around the circumference of the tire. Chipped tires are tires which have been cut or ground into pieces smaller than sliced tires.
Processing or processing operation means any method, system or other treatment designed to change the physical form or chemical content of solid waste and includes all aspects of its management (administration, personnel, land, equipment, buildings and other elements).
Public or private property means the right-of-way of any road or highway; any body of water or watercourse or the shores of beaches thereof; any park, playground, building, refuge or conservation or recreation area; and any residential or farm properties, timberlands, or forest.
Putrescible waste means wastes that are capable of being quickly decomposed by microorganisms. Examples of putrescible wastes include but are not necessarily limited to kitchen wastes, animal manure, offal, hatchery and poultry processing plant wastes, and garbage.
Recycling means the process by which reclaimed resources are transformed into new products in such a manner that the original products may lose their identity.
Restricted waste, excluding municipal solid waste, means any solid, liquid, or sludge;
(1)
Contained in a drum, barrel, box, pail, transportable tank, or any other container;
(2)
Transported in a bulk tank;
(3)
Residue or waste from a pollution control process;
(4)
Residue or waste from an industrial process; and
(5)
Residue, debris, and waste from the cleanup of a spill or a release of a chemical substance or commercial product or waste associated with subsections (1) through (4) of this definition.
The term "restricted waste" shall include hazardous waste but shall not include household hazardous waste, provided that such household hazardous waste is disposed of at a solid waste handling facility designated by the County to accept such household hazardous waste and in accordance with the policies and procedures of the County. Other restricted wastes may include, but are not limited to, animal wastes and carcasses, asbestos, ash, biomedical wastes, chemicals, containers, clothing, debris, equipment and instruments, grease and oil, latex plant (vegetal) materials, sludges, washwaters, or other wastes. Sources of such material include, but are not limited to, research, veterinary and medical laboratories, mortuaries, taxidermists, automobile washes and laundries (both commercial and industrial), publicly owned treatment works, and industrial treatment facilities, food processing and restaurant related facilities. The Director has the full authority to determine whether a waste is a restricted waste.
Retail tire dealer means a person actively engaged in the business of selling new replacement tires, as defined in Ga. Comp. Rules and Regs. R. 391-3-4-.19(2) of the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management.
Scrap tire means a tire that is no longer suitable for its original intended purpose because of wear, damage, or defect, as defined in Ga. Comp. Rules and Regs. R. 391-3-4-.19(2) of the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management.
Scrap tire carrier means any person engaged in picking up or transporting scrap tires not otherwise exempted in the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management for the purpose of removal to a scrap tire processor, end user, or disposal facility, as defined in Ga. Comp. Rules and Regs. R. 391-3-4-.19(2) of the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management.
Scrap tire generator means any person who generates scrap tires, including but not limited to, retail tire dealers, retreaders, scrap tire processors, automobile dealers, private company vehicle maintenance shops, garages, service stations, and city, county and state governments, as defined in Ga. Comp. Rules and Regs. R. 391-3-4-.19(2) of the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management.
Scrap tire processor means any person who is approved by the Environmental Protection Division to receive scrap tires from scrap tire generators or scrap tire carriers for the purpose of scrap tire processing, as defined in Ga. Comp. Rules and Regs. R. 391-3-4-.19(2) of the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste management.
Scrap tire sorter means any person, other than the original scrap tire generator, who handles mixed tires by separating used tires and retreadable casings from scrap tires, as defined in section 391-3-4-.19(2) of the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management.
Solid waste means putrescible and nonputrescible waste except water-carried body waste and materials destined for recycling, and shall include garbage, ashes, street refuse, dead animals, animal manures, commercial solid waste, industrial solid waste, municipal solid waste, residue from incineration, food processing wastes, demolition waste, dredging waste, construction waste, yard trimmings, and any other waste material in a solid or semi-solid state not otherwise defined in the section.
Solid waste handling facility means any location where any storage, collection, transportation, treatment, utilization, processing or disposal of solid waste, or any combination thereof, occurs.
Solid waste handling permit means written authorization to engage in solid waste handling or to construct or operate a solid waste handling facility that is granted to a person by the Environment Protection Division pursuant to the Solid Waste Management Act, or by the rules and regulations promulgated by the Environmental Protection Division under the authority of that Act.
Solid Waste Management Act means the Georgia Comprehensive Solid Waste Management Act codified at O.C.G.A. §§ 12-8-20 through 12-8-59.2, as the same has been heretofore or may be hereafter amended.
Yard trimmings means leaves, brush, grass clippings, shrub and tree prunings, discarded Christmas trees, nursery and greenhouse vegetative residuals, and vegetative matter resulting from landscaping development and maintenance other than mining, agricultural, and silvicultural operations.
(Res. of 7-22-2009, § 50-3.3)
Secs. 28-477—28-505. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - SOLID WASTE

Sec. 28-506. - Permit required.
(a)
All solid waste generated or otherwise found within unincorporated Walker County shall be collected by persons holding valid Collector permits for such activity, granted in accordance with this section. Each such Collector permit shall expire 1 year subsequent to the issuance date of the initial permit issued pursuant to the section. A Collector permit issued pursuant to the provisions of this section shall be a mere grant or privilege to carry on the business during the term of such permit and subject to all terms and conditions imposed by this section and related laws and other ordinances and resolutions of Walker County.
(b)
This section shall not be construed to require a permit for the transportation of waste through Walker County, provided that such waste was not generated within the borders of Walker County.
(Res. of 7-22-2009, § 50-3.4)
Sec. 28-507. - Collector permit application issuance, and appeal procedures; fees.
(a)
Each collector must submit an application for a collector permit to the County upon a form approved by the Director. This applicant shall, under penalty of perjury, certify that all information contained in the permit application and all information submitted in connection with the permit application is true and correct. At a minimum, the application for a collector permit shall require a collector to provide the following information:
(1)
The name, address and telephone number of the applicant.
(2)
A list of all vehicles proposed to be used within unincorporated Walker County for the purpose of collecting solid waste, including the following information for each vehicle.
a.
The State motor vehicle registration number;
b.
Description of chassis by year and manufacturer;
c.
Description of the body by year and manufacturer;
d.
The legal weight limit;
e.
The volume of the body of the vehicle in cubic yards; and
f.
A certification by the Collector that it maintains insurance coverage for each vehicle in an amount not less than the minimum coverage required by Georgia law.
(3)
A list of the area or areas served by the vehicles listed in the permit application. The applicant is obligated to notify the County of changes to the list within 10 days.
(4)
A list of all contracts and agreements covering the collection of solid waste generated within unincorporated Walker County. This list shall include the names of the parties to the contract or agreement, the term of the contract and the termination date of the contract, and the names of any disposal locations specified in the contract or agreement.
(5)
A copy of the Notification required by section 391-3-4-.06(2) of the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management (Permit by Rule for Collection, Transportation, Processing, and Disposal).
(6)
Any additional data and information deemed necessary by the County, the Director, or any of his designees in order to verify the accuracy of information contained in the permit application forms and attendant documents.
(b)
The collector permit fee shall be paid according to a fee schedule to be established and adjusted from time to time by the Director with approval from the Chairperson, payable to the County at the time the initial permit application is filed and at the time each renewal application is filed thereafter; provided, however, that the permit fee required hereunder shall be waived for any municipality. The Board of Commissioners shall be authorized to increase the collector permit fee when it shall determine that such an increase shall be necessary in order to offset increasing costs of operation; provided, however, that there shall be at least 30 days' notice of any increase in the collector permit fee.
(c)
An application for an initial or renewed collector permit submitted pursuant to this section may be granted or denied by the Director or his designee. Such application may be denied for 1 or more of the following reasons:
(1)
The applicant has failed to provide some or all of the information required by subsection (a) of this section.
(2)
The applicant has supplied false information to the County or any other employee, officer, or official of any agency, department, or authority of Walker County, the State of Georgia, or the United States.
(3)
The applicant has failed to pay any permit fee, rates, fees, penalty, or interest required or imposed pursuant to this section or has otherwise failed to comply with any of the provisions contained in this section.
(4)
The applicant has failed to comply with any applicable legal or procedural requirements imposed by State law.
(d)
The Director or his designee shall grant or deny a collector permit application within 60 days of the applicant's submission of a completed application. If the collector permit application is denied, the applicant may appeal such denial by filing a written appeal with the Board of Commissioners within 10 days of the denial. A hearing shall then be set before the Board of Commissioners and prior written notice of such hearing shall be sent to the appellant. Ten days' written notice shall be deemed reasonable, but a shorter or longer period of notice may be authorized as the Board of Commissioners may, in the exercise of his discretion, determine to be justified by the circumstances. Unless all parties consent otherwise, the hearing shall be held within 30 days of the filing of the appeal. The Board of Commissioners may affirm, reverse, or modify the permitting decision.
(Res. of 7-22-2009, § 50-3.5)
Sec. 28-508. - Collector vehicle requirements.
(a)
A collector may use only a qualifying vehicle to collect or transport solid waste within unincorporated Walker County.
(1)
To qualify, a vehicle must be owned or leased by the applicant and must be listed in the permit application, as described in section 28-507. If a collector obtains a vehicle that the collector intends to use for purposes of collecting solid waste after the permit application is filed, the collector shall provide the information required by section 28-507(a) to the County, upon forms approved by the Director within 10 days of the acquisition, purchase or lease of the qualifying vehicle.
(2)
A qualifying vehicle shall have the business name, business address, and/or telephone number, tare weight, and vehicle identification number painted or securely affixed to each side of the vehicle in letters and numbers at least 3 inches in height. When roll-offs are used, the tare weights of the collection vehicle must appear on the collection vehicle and the tare weight of each box must appear on each roll-off container. A regularly used business logo may also be displayed. No other names or numbers, not required by law, shall be displayed.
(b)
Vehicle construction and maintenance. Solid waste disposal vehicles utilized by collectors of solid waste must meet the following minimum requirements, both prior to and after the issuance of a permit:
(1)
The collection vehicle body shall be capable of being readily emptied, as defined by the Director.
(2)
The collection vehicle shall be kept in a sanitary condition.
(3)
The collection vehicle must be so equipped that all loading openings on the bodies have tightly fitting doors or covers which latch, clamp or fasten to keep them closed and rubber or other suitable gasket to render them leakproof, spillproof, dustproof, and odorproof to the maximum extent practicable.
(4)
Roll-off boxes must be of welded construction and doors must be tightly fitted so as to render them leak proof and spill proof. Provisions shall be made to facilitate application of a tightly fitting tarpaulin cover or diaper.
(5)
All unloading doors must be equipped with chains to keep them securely fastened in an open position when unloading.
(6)
The collection vehicle shall be equipped with heavy-duty front hooks, loops or hackles, good and serviceable tires and other accessories as necessary for operation and/or navigation in or about any solid waste handling facility operated by the County.
(7)
Each collection vehicle used or proposed for use by a collector together with the contents of any collection vehicle shall be subject at all times to inspection by law enforcement personnel and the County prior to discharge of the contents.
(8)
Each collector must ensure that each collection vehicle contains accurate documents available for inspection which identify the city, county, and state of origin and nature of the solid waste contents of the vehicle.
(Res. of 7-22-2009, § 50-3.6)
Sec. 28-509. - Transporting waste.
Each collector shall comply with the following requirements for transporting solid waste generated within unincorporated Walker County:
(1)
Solid waste shall be suitably enclosed or covered to prevent littering, spillage of solid waste or fluids, and/or infiltration of rainwater. Tarpaulins must be used to cover compactor box openings, roll-off tops, or other openings. Tarpaulins must be kept in good repair at all times.
(2)
Any spillage of solid waste upon the roads leading to or upon any solid waste handling facility is the sole responsibility of the collector and shall be immediately cleaned and removed by the collector. Any and all costs incurred by Walker County related to any such spillage shall be immediately remitted to the County by the collector. This provision shall be in addition to any penalties authorized elsewhere by any other provision of law.
(Res. of 7-22-2009, § 50-3.7)
Sec. 28-510. - Disposal of waste.
Except as otherwise specifically provided in this section, each person who collects solid waste generated within Walker County shall dispose of such waste at a solid waste handling facility operated by the County. This section shall not be construed to prohibit the source separation of materials from solid waste prior to collection of such solid waste for disposal.
(Res. of 7-22-2009, § 50-3.8)
Sec. 28-511. - Collector records.
(a)
Each collector permitted to collect solid waste within unincorporated Walker County shall keep such records, receipts, invoices, and other pertinent papers in such form as the Board of Commissioners, Director, or any of their designees may require and for a period no less than 3 years. Such records, receipts, invoices, and other pertinent papers shall include, but not be limited to, documents evidencing the tonnage of waste each collector has collected within the borders of Walker County, the solid waste handling facility or disposal site where such waste is deposited, and the date of disposal.
(b)
The Board of Commissioners, the Director, or any of their designees may examine the books, papers, records, financial reports, equipment, and other facilities of any collector permitted to collect solid waste within unincorporated Walker County in order to verify compliance with this section.
(Res. of 7-22-2009, § 50-3.9)
Sec. 28-512. - County solid waste facility procedures, rules, and waste acceptance policy.
(a)
In order for the County to manage safely and cost effectively solid waste generated within unincorporated Walker County, the Director shall establish policies and procedures for the acceptance of restricted waste at any solid waste handling facility operated by the County.
(b)
Municipal solid waste, commercial solid waste, and industrial solid waste.
(1)
Municipal solid waste and commercial solid waste generated within unincorporated Walker County shall be accepted at any solid waste handling facility operated by the County subject to any fees and rates established pursuant to this section and any other policies and procedures established by the County.
(2)
Only industrial solid waste that has been evaluated by the County pursuant to policies and procedures established by the Director may be accepted for disposal, provided that the acceptance of such waste is consistent with the Solid Waste Management Act, the Hazardous Waste Management Act, or the rules and regulations promulgated by the Environmental Protection Division under the authority of those statutes.
(c)
Hazardous waste. No hazardous waste shall be accepted for disposal at a solid waste handling facility operated by the County, other than household hazardous waste that has been approved for acceptance by the County. No person shall deposit or attempt to deposit hazardous waste at any solid waste handling facility operated by the County or on the County's property, other than household hazardous waste that has been approved for acceptance by the County.
(d)
Yard trimmings. Yard trimmings will be accepted only at a solid waste handling facility operated by the County and only in compliance with any policies and procedures of the County, O.C.G.A. § 12-8-40.2, any successor statute, and the rules and regulations promulgated by the Environmental Protection Division, under the authority of that statute.
(e)
All other solid waste, including, but not limited to, asbestos, bulky waste, construction or demolition waste, scrap tires, shall be accepted only at a solid waste handling facility operated by the County or at such other solid waste handling facility as designated by the County, in compliance with the policies and procedures established by the Director and any applicable rules and regulations promulgated by the Environmental Protection Division.
(f)
The Director shall have the authority to establish any other policies and procedures it deems necessary to address the solid waste management needs of Walker County, Georgia, including, but not limited to, limitations of liability, reservation of rights, and general rules for the operation of County facilities. In addition, the Director shall have the authority to establish penalties for violations of its rules and regulations, including, but not limited to, the temporary suspension of a collector's permit (not to exceed 30 days) and permit revocation subject to the provisions of this section.
(Res. of 7-22-2009, § 50-3.10)
Sec. 28-513. - Solid waste handling fees.
(a)
The Board of Commissioners shall establish all rates and fees collected or assessed in accordance with this section, including, but not limited to, tipping fees for any waste deposited at a solid waste handling facility operated by the County.
(b)
The Board of Commissioners shall periodically review all rates and fees collected or assessed in accordance with the section and may adjust or eliminate any such rates and fees. Nothing herein shall be construed to prohibit the Board of Commissioners from changing such rates and fees; provided, however, no adjustment to such rates and fees may occur without at least 30 days' notice to the public.
(c)
All fees imposed or collected by the County shall comply with O.C.G.A. § 12-8-39 and any successor statue.
(d)
The County is specifically authorized to require a letter of credit or a bond from any Collector to ensure payment of rates and fees, including tipping fees. The Director shall establish the minimum letter of credit or bond amount that may be required, but in no event shall the amount of any letter of credit or bond be less than the previous month's tipping fees from the Collector who is required to post the bond or provide the letter of credit.
(Res. of 7-22-2009, § 50-3.11)
Sec. 28-514. - Emergency powers; right to assume collection.
(a)
The Board of Commissioners, the Director, or any of their designees reserve the right to assume responsibility for all or part of the collection of solid waste within unincorporated Walker County should it be determined to be in the best interest of the health and welfare of the citizens of Walker County. Such action shall be on a temporary basis, not to exceed 30 days, until a hearing before the Board of Commissioners taking such action is held.
(b)
Prior to any hearing described in subsection (a) of this section, notice to all affected holders of Collector's permits of the time, place, and purpose of such hearing shall be given by prior written notice to the last known addresses of such collectors. Three business days' written notice shall be deemed reasonable, but a shorter or longer period of notice shall be authorized as the Board of Commissioners may deem justified by the circumstances.
(c)
The proceeding shall be conducted informally, but compatibly with due process. All interested parties, including the County or its designee, shall be allowed to present any evidence, documents, or statements in support of their position. The Board of Commissioners shall then determine whether the action described in subsection (a) of this section should be made permanent or whether any other action by the Board of Commissioners is justified under the circumstances, including, but not limited to, entering into an exclusive franchise agreement for the collection of solid waste within Walker County.
(d)
Any person adversely affected by any decision made by the Board of Commissioners pursuant to this section and desiring to appeal further must do so by Writ of Certiorari directly to the State Court of Walker County within 30 days of such adverse decision. There shall be no intermediate appeal to any other body.
(Res. of 7-22-2009, § 50-3.12)
Sec. 28-515. - Enforcement and penalties.
(a)
Unless otherwise specifically provided by resolution of the Board of Commissioners, the enforcement of this section shall be within the jurisdiction of the County's enforcement personnel, including law enforcement personnel, employees of the County empowered to enforce codes and ordinances, and the Director and his designees. For the limited purposes of enforcement of this section, the Director and his designees shall be considered a Walker County enforcement officer.
(b)
Each day a violation continues shall constitute a separate violation.
(c)
To facilitate the enforcement of this section, the Director or his designees shall have the authority to request documents and information from any person generating solid waste within Walker County that demonstrate or otherwise evidence the solid waste such person generates or has generated in Walker County.
(d)
Pursuant to the authority granted by O.C.G.A. § 36-1-20(b), a violation of any section of this division shall result in the following:
(1)
A fine of not more than $1,000.00;
(2)
Incarceration in the County Jail not to exceed 60 days; and/or
(3)
The Director may, in the exercise of its discretion, revoke a collector's permit for repeated violations. Any revocation shall be subject to the provisions of the sections hereinbelow.
(e)
Nothing contained in this section shall be construed to limit or constrain any Federal or State agency or authority from enforcing Federal or State laws and regulations, including, but not limited to, those laws and regulations regarding the generation, collection, transportation, and/or disposal of solid waste or hazardous waste in Walker County.
(Res. of 7-22-2009, § 50-3.13)
Sec. 28-516. - Permit revocation.
(a)
To revoke a collector's permit, the Director shall bring before the Board of Commissioners, at his next regularly scheduled meeting, a request indefinitely to revoke a collector's permit. Notice to the holder of such permit of the time, place, and purpose of such hearing shall be given by prior written notice, either by certified mail, statutory overnight delivery, or by hand delivery. Ten days' written notice shall be deemed reasonable, but a shorter or longer period of notice may be authorized as the Board of Commissioners may deem justified by the circumstances.
(b)
In all hearings before the Board of Commissioners conducted pursuant to this section, the following procedures shall apply; and the proceeding shall be as informal as is compatible with due process:
(1)
The Board of Commissioners shall read or cause to be read the charges against the permit holder. The Board of Commissioners shall then read or cause to be read any response filed by the permit holder.
(2)
The Board of Commissioners shall hear the evidence upon which the charges have been filed against the permit holder and shall not consider any additional evidence beyond the scope of these charges. The Board of Commissioners may exclude evidence which is purely cumulative in nature.
(3)
The order of proof shall be as follows:
a.
The Director or designee of the Director shall present evidence in support of the charges.
b.
The permit holder shall then present its evidence.
c.
The Board of Commissioners may allow each party to present rebuttal evidence of any new matter raised.
d.
The evidence of each party may be supported by the submission of pertinent documents and/or testimony.
(4)
The permit holder and the County may each be represented by counsel, and may present, examine, and cross examine witnesses. Additionally, the Board of Commissioners may question both parties and all witnesses to obtain any information deemed necessary to evaluate the charges.
(c)
Upon the conclusion of the presentation of the evidence, the Board of Commissioners may indefinitely revoke the collector's permit, suspend the Collector's permit for a period of time it shall determine in the exercise of its discretion, or allow the collector to retain the permit with or without additional conditions that the Board of Commissioners deems justified by the circumstances.
(d)
Any person adversely affected by any revocation made by the Board of Commissioners and desiring to appeal further shall do so by writ of certiorari directly to the Walker County State Court within 30 days of such adverse decision. There shall be no intermediate appeal to any other body.
(e)
This section shall not apply to citations issued pursuant to section 28-515.
(Res. of 7-22-2009, § 50-3.14(1))

DIVISION 3. - SCRAP TIRE MANAGEMENT

Sec. 28-541. - General handling and disposal measures.
(a)
The owner or occupant of any premises shall be responsible for the sanitary handling and disposal of all scrap tires located on the premises used or occupied by such person.
(b)
It shall be unlawful to cause, suffer or allow the dumping of scrap tires at any place within unincorporated Walker County including, and without limitation, in or on any public road, including any portion of the right-of-way thereof; any public or private property within Walker County; or any waters within Walker County, unless such scrap tires originate within Walker County or other areas authorized by the Board of Commissioners and:
(1)
The property is designated by the Board of Commissioners or its duly designated agent and the Environmental Protection Division for the collection or disposal of scrap tires and the person is authorized to use such property;
(2)
The scrap tires are placed into a receptacle or container installed specifically for such property; and
(3)
The property has a valid solid waste handling permit or other applicable identification numbers, approvals and/or permits issued by the Environmental Protection Division when required.
(c)
All scrap tire generators, scrap tire carriers, and scrap tire processors, including scrap tire sorters, shall be subject to O.C.G.A. § 12-8-40.1, as amended, and Ga. Comp. Rules and Regs. R. 391-3-4.-19 of the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management and shall handle scrap tires in accordance with the provisions of the Solid Waste Management Act, and the Georgia Rules for Solid Waste Management, Ga. Comp. Rules and Regs. ch. 391-3-4 et seq., that are applicable to scrap tires, except where the requirements of this division are more stringent.
(Res. of 7-22-2009, § 50-3.14(2))
Sec. 28-542. - Accumulation.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to store more than 5 scrap tires on or around property that they own or occupy; provided, however, that a retail tire dealer may store up to 100 scrap tires per garage bay in storage, up to a maximum of 100 scrap tires.
(b)
Tires shall be stored in covered or enclosed areas to prevent the accumulation of water.
(c)
Tires must be stored in a manner that avoids disease vectors and provides protection from fire hazards.

DIVISION 4. - HAZARDOUS WASTE DISPOSAL

Sec. 28-567. - Hazardous waste facilities and discharge.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Disposal means the discharge, deposit, injection, dumping, spilling, leaking or placing of any solid waste or hazardous waste into or on any land or water so that such solid waste or hazardous waste or any constituent thereof may enter the environment or be emitted into the air or discharged into any water, including groundwater.
Hazardous constituent means any substance listed as a hazardous constituent in regulations promulgated by the administrator of the United States Environmental Protection Agency pursuant to the Federal Act which are in force and effect, codified as appendix VIII to 40 CFR 261, Identification and Listing of Hazardous Waste.
Hazardous waste means any solid waste which has been defined as a hazardous waste in regulations promulgated by the administrator of the United States Environmental Protection Agency pursuant to the Federal Act which are in force and effect, codified as 40 CFR 261.3, and any designated hazardous waste.
Hazardous waste facility means any property or facility that is intended or used for storage, treatment or disposal of hazardous waste.
Solid waste means solid waste as defined by regulations promulgated by the administrator of the United States Environmental Protection Agency pursuant to the Federal Act which are in force and effect on February 1, 1988, codified as 40 CFR 261.1, 261.2(a)—(d), and 261.4(a).
Storage means the containment or holding of hazardous waste, either on a temporary basis or for a period of years, in such a manner as not to constitute disposal of such hazardous waste.
(b)
Unlawful activities.
(1)
It shall be unlawful for any person to own, establish, operate or maintain a hazardous waste facility in the unincorporated areas of the County.
(2)
The disposal of any solid waste or hazardous waste or any hazardous constituent thereof on any land or water shall be unlawful in unincorporated areas of the County.
(c)
Violations; penalties.
(1)
Any person who violates this section shall be subject to section 1-13.
(2)
Each day any violation continues shall be deemed a separate offense.
(3)
Whenever any provision of this section has been or is being violated, the appropriate County authority or any adjacent or neighboring property owner who would be damaged by such violation, in addition to other remedies, may institute injunction or other appropriate actions or proceedings and prosecute the same to final judgment to correct or abate such violation.
(Code 2005, § 50-31; Ord. of 7-19-1988, §§ II—IV)
Sec. 28-568. - Requirements for issuance of permit.
No permit shall be issued by the County unless and until each of the following shall occur:
(1)
A land use and zoning designation study shall be adopted by the Board of Commissioners which shall designate specific purposes for each land or land type in the County.
(2)
A study shall be performed by the County on issuing permits for disposal of hazardous waste materials in the County, including regulation of such disposal and use.
(3)
A proposal to the Board of Commissioners from the Director on regulations concerning the issuance of permits and the regulation of hazardous waste disposal in the County.
(4)
The passage by the Board of Commissioners of an amended ordinance adopting regulations concerning the issuance of County permits and the regulation of hazardous waste disposal in the County.
(5)
That all applicable State and Federal laws, rules and regulations have been complied with by the ordinance referred to in subsection (4) of this section.
(6)
That the permit applicant has obtained a permit issued by the Environmental Protection Division pursuant to the Hazardous Waste Management Act.
(Res. of 7-22-2009, § 50-3.18)
Sec. 28-569. - Exception for existing disposers; exception for generators.
(a)
Any disposer of hazardous waste materials who, as of the date of the original passage of this section had already received proper authorization, including all applicable permits by the Environmental Protection Division, and any other Federal or State agency necessary to legally perform hazardous waste disposal in the County, shall not be affected by the provisions of this section.
(b)
This section shall not apply to any generator of hazardous waste materials so long as the generator otherwise complies with any and all applicable requirements of State and Federal law and so long as the property on which the generator is operating is not a disposal facility under State and Federal laws and regulations.
(Res. of 7-22-2009, § 50-3.20)
Sec. 28-570. - Medical and hospital waste.
(a)
Processing, incinerating and disposal. It shall be unlawful for any person to process, incinerate or dispose of body substances, body fluids, body parts or anything contaminated by body substances, body fluids or body parts, including, but not limited to, needles, bandages and clothing, and medical and hospital waste, in the unincorporated areas of the County.
(b)
Delivery to licensed commercial establishments. Doctors, hospitals and clinics located within the unincorporated areas of the County are authorized and directed to deliver medical and hospital waste to duly licensed commercial establishments for proper disposal or incineration as required by applicable law, rule or regulation.
(c)
Exemptions. This section shall have no application to medical and hospital waste disposal facilities located within the unincorporated areas of the County that comply with the environmental requirements on the effective date of the ordinance from which this section is derived.
(d)
Violations; penalties.
(1)
Any person who violates this section shall be guilty of an offense and, upon conviction in the Magistrate Court of the County, shall be punished according to section 1-13.
(2)
Whenever any provision of this section has been or is being violated, the appropriate County authority or any adjacent or neighboring property owner who would be damaged by such violation, in addition to other remedies, may institute injunction or other appropriate actions or proceedings and prosecute the same to final judgment to correct or abate such violation.
(Code 2005, § 50-32; Ord. of 3-13-1990, §§ II—V)
