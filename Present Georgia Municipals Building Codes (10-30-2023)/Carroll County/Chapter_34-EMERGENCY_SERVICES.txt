ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 34-1. - Authority of county to license, regulate and franchise.
The governing authority of the county is hereby authorized to franchise by the granting of nonexclusive franchises to all persons, firms, and corporations doing business in ambulance service in the unincorporated area of the county. A franchise may be granted for all the unincorporated area of the county or for any portion of the unincorporated area of the county as may be established by the governing authority as a special franchise district. A special franchise district may be abolished, merged, modified, or redistricted by the governing authority by resolution after same has been established. The governing authority is hereby empowered to levy a franchise fee on franchised firms.
(Ord. of 5-25-99)
Sec. 34-2. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the following terms shall have the respective meanings described to them in this section:
Advanced life support (ALS) for ambulance services, includes the services of basic life support (BLS) and advanced emergency care. ALS personnel provide: intravenous therapy, endotracheal intubation, anti-shock trousers, a cardiac monitor, cardiac defibrillator, drugs, relief of pneumothorax or other invasive procedures and services.
Ambulance means any vehicle which is equipped to transport patients, in a reclining position, to or from health care facilities.
Ambulance service means the providing of emergency care and transportation on the public streets and highways of this county for a wounded, injured, sick, invalid or incapacitated human being to or from a place where medical or hospital care is furnished.
Base station physician means a physician authorized to practice under state guidelines, knowledgeable in the medical protocols, radio procedures and general operating policies of the EMS system, and a person from whom EMS personnel may take medical direction by radio or other remote communication device.
Cardiac technician means a person who, having been trained and certified as an emergency medical technician and having completed additional training in advanced cardiac life support techniques in a training course approved by the department of human resources, is so certified by the composite state board of medical examiners.
Communications center means the single 911 public safety answering point (PSAP) facility that notifies first responders and dispatches ambulances operating in the service area.
County means Carroll County, Georgia, which contains approximately 502 square miles and encompasses the cities of Bowdon, Carrollton, Mt. Zion, Roopville, Temple, Villa Rica, and Whitesburg. All areas of the county are considered part of this chapter.
E-911 means the county emergency 911 system that facilitates the placing of calls by persons in need of emergency services to the applicant.
Emergency means a request for ambulance services for non-convalescent requests for medical assistance that may include life-threatening conditions, limb-threatening conditions, or those conditions within the standard procedure as promulgated by the medical director.
Emergency medical technician means a person who has been certified by the department of human resources of the state after having successfully completed emergency medical care training approved by that department.
EMS means emergency medical service that provides emergency ambulance services.
EMS system means the comprehensive coordinated arrangement of resources and functions to respond to medical emergencies and provide emergency ambulance service.
First responder means the services providing initial response to medical emergencies by utilizing a rapid response unit, staffed and equipped to perform emergency medical procedures.
Medical director means the licensed physician (or his/her designee) who has the responsibilities of medical director in accordance with state requirements.
Medical priority dispatch system (MPDS) means that system to prioritize incoming medical calls and is approved by the medical director.
Medical protocol means a written statement of standard procedure, promulgated by the medical director as the medical appropriate standard of out-of hospital care for a given clinical condition.
Mutual aid agreement means a written agreement between providers of emergency medical services where the parties agree to provide EMS assistance to one another.
Paramedic means any person who has been certified as an advanced emergency medical technician by the composite state board of medical examiners before July 1, 1988, or any person who has been certified by that board on or after July 1, 1988, as having been trained in emergency care techniques in a paramedic training course approved by the department of human resources, but all such persons shall be designated on or after July 1, 1988, as paramedics.
Patient means an individual who is either ill, sick, injured, wounded, helpless, or otherwise incapacitated, and who is in need of, or is at risk of needing medical care or assessment during transportation to or from a health care facility, and who is reclining or should be transported in a reclining position.
Person means any individual, firm partnership, association, corporation, company, group of individuals acting together for a common purpose, or organization of any kind, including any governmental agency other than that of the United States.
Public safety officers means any on duty, career or volunteer employee, of the Bowdon Police Department, county animal control, county E-911, county fire rescue and emergency management agency, county prison, county sheriff's department, Carrollton Fire Department, Carrollton Police Department, Mt. Zion Police Department, Temple Police Department, Villa Rica Police Department, and Whitesburg Police Department.
Service Area means that area which is contained within the boundaries of the county.
(Ord. of 5-25-99)
Sec. 34-3. - Applicability of article generally.
The provisions of this article shall apply to all emergency ambulances operated within the county, but shall not apply to ambulance service or calls for service operated by county and shall not apply to vehicles operated solely for non-emergency services.
(Ord. of 5-25-99)
Sec. 34-4. - Requirement of franchise.
No person shall operate an ambulance or ambulance service within the unincorporated limits of county without first obtaining from the board of commissioners a franchise as herein provided. This article shall not prevent ambulance service located outside of the county from picking up a patient outside of the county and bringing the patient into the county. This article will not prevent any ambulance service located outside the county from occasionally responding to an emergency call in the county pursuant to a mutual aid agreement. This article will not prevent any ambulance service located outside the county from picking up a patient in the county for transfer to a location outside the county either as an emergency or as a nonemergency transfer.
(Ord. of 5-25-99)
Sec. 34-5. - Nonexclusive franchise.
Each franchise granted under this division will be a nonexclusive franchise. The county does not agree to restrict the number of franchises in all or any part of the county for any person, firm or corporation in the same business, a competing business or any related business.
(Ord. of 5-25-99)
Sec. 34-6. - Application; requirements for issuance of franchise.
An application for a franchise under this article shall be made with the clerk of the board of commissioners of the county by petition, which shall set forth the name, address and place of business of the applicant, if an individual, and if a partnership the name and address and place of business of each partner, and the place of business, and if a corporation, the name and address of each of the officers of the corporation, and the place of business of such corporation; the trade name under which the applicant does or proposes to do business; the name of the insurance company with which the applicant has made arrangements to secure insurance as required by the provisions set forth herein; together with the following information:
(1)
Written and proof that all personnel of applicant will provide ambulance services are certified emergency medical technicians, cardiac technicians or paramedics as those positions are certified by the state department of human resources, and comply with the provisions set forth in section 34-7;
(2)
Written assurance and proof that each ambulance to be used by applicant within the county is certified by the state department of human resources and complies with the provisions set forth in section 34-8;
(3)
Written assurance and proof that applicant will maintain within the county limits a minimum of three 24-hour, seven day per week ALS units ambulances, neither of which shall exceed 60 months in age (remounted vehicles will be deemed new as of the date the chassis is originally titled), equipped and staffed to provide advanced life support services, as defined by the state department of human resources, at all times except when such ambulances services, in addition to written assurance and proof that each ambulance maintained hereunder shall be staffed by at least one paramedic or cardiac technician;
(4)
Written assurance and proof that applicant is properly licensed by the state to provide ambulance services within state;
(5)
Written assurance and proof that the applicant will either install mobile or make available portable radio units for each ambulance operated within the county which are capable of communications with law enforcement and fire department personnel and complies with the provisions set forth in section 34-9;
(6)
Written assurance and proof that applicant will maintain a written log of requests for service calls and will in addition make such written records available for inspection by the county upon request all in order to determine compliance with the requirements hereunder;
(7)
Written assurance and proof that all requirements of this article and all other applicable ordinances of the county and statutes of the state have been met and fully complied with in order to engage in ambulance services;
(8)
Written assurance of state mandated workers' compensation insurance and proof of public liability insurance in an amount not less than $1,500,000.00 for one injury, not less than $1,500,000.00 for all injuries out of one accident, not less than $1,500,000.00 property damage, and not less than $1,500,000.00 for injuries caused by malpractice;
(Ord. of 5-25-99)
Sec. 34-7. - Employees of applicant.
(a)
Professional skills and training of personnel. Applicant shall require mandatory attendance of its personnel at in-service training and shall ensure that all field personnel working in the system possess appropriate understanding of the ambulance service system.
(b)
Rights and responsibilities of field personnel. Professional field ambulance personnel shall have a direct linkage to those physicians who are empowered to oversee clinical policy and procedure. This direct linkage and personnel responsibility applies to compliance of vehicles, on board equipment, collection and recording of primary data. Personnel are prohibited from operating equipment that is substantially out of compliance with system standards, as provided through regional EMS council, personnel are prohibited from falsifying or omitting data from reports.
(c)
Character and competence of employees. All persons employed by the applicant in the performance of work under this contract shall be competent and hold and maintain appropriate permits or certification for their professions.
(d)
Professional conduct and dress. The applicant's employees and agents will provide courteous and professional conduct and appearance at all times. The ambulance service personnel shall have an adopted uniform to be worn when on duty with identifiable insignia indicating their level of certification as assigned by the state. Uniform styles shall be determined by the applicant. All field personnel must undergo physical examination and applicant is responsible for making sure its employees undergo blood and urine tests to determine the presence of illegal drug use at a frequency as county and applicant mutually determine. The requirements under OSHA regulations are the responsibility of the applicant.
(Ord. of 5-25-99)
Sec. 34-8. - Vehicle and equipment standards.
(a)
Ambulances furnished under the agreement shall meet or exceed vehicle specifications as specified in the document entitled "Federal Specification Ambulance Emergency Medical Care Vehicle" as published by the General Service Administration, DOT Federal Specification KKK 1822 in effect at the time of manufacture.
(b)
Each ambulance shall be equipped with all required equipment and supplies for operations as required by state and local regulations and approved medical protocols. The specific intent of this provision is to ensure that the applicant have adequate equipment to service the county.
(c)
The applicant shall comply with or exceed the maintenance standards as outlined in Section 203 of the Standards Accreditation of Ambulance Services as published by the most recent edition of the Commission on the Accreditation of Ambulance Services. All costs of maintenance and repairs, including parts, supplies, labor, subcontracted services and costs of extended warranties shall be at the applicant's expense.
(d)
The applicant shall establish policies and procedures to insure that all ambulances are maintained in a reasonably clean manner both interior and exterior at all times.
(Ord. of 5-25-99)
Sec. 34-9. - Communications.
(a)
Each ambulance shall be equipped with a mobile radio with radio frequency approved by the E-911 director that can communicate with county E-911, and all other public safety agencies within the boundaries of the county. Each ambulance crew shall have a portable radio and/or pager which can be accessed by county E-911. The E-911 center shall not be responsible for the dispatching or coordination of any convalescent or non-emergency radio traffic or units. The E-911 center shall be responsible for only those units assigned to the center for emergency calls.
(b)
The county shall provide the Applicant with 24-hour per day emergency dispatch along with time and voice recordings (maintained for 30 days only). The county will furnish to the applicant that information and or documentation without charge.
(c)
The applicant shall maintain a radio frequency license with the appropriate authorities that is compatible with county E-911.
(d)
Any expense incurred to maintain the communications link between the applicant and E-911 shall be the responsibility of the applicant.
(e)
The applicant shall furnish and ensure the continuous reliable availability of a medical director and on-line medical control with the emergency room concerned with the call.
(Ord. of 5-25-99)
Sec. 34-10. - Data collection and reporting required.
Applicant's data collection and reporting systems shall meet the following minimum standards, excepting that information that is privileged and confidential by law, which reports shall be furnished to the county upon request. Applicant shall maintain complete records and provide reasonable access to county as requested, including:
(1)
Each request for service;
(2)
Equipment failure reports;
(3)
Vehicle maintenance records;
(4)
Patient account records;
(5)
Deployment planning reports;
(6)
Continuing education and certification records documenting training compliance;
(7)
Summary of clinical/service inquiries and resolutions; and
(8)
Verification of drug free workplace compliance.
(Ord. of 5-25-99)
Sec. 34-11. - Franchise fee.
Together with the written application described herein, applicant shall submit to the clerk of the board of commissioners by certified check a $1,500.00 processing fee to cover expenses of investigation and processing of the application and defer the costs to the county of administering this ordinance, which fee shall not be refundable.
(Ord. of 5-25-99)
Sec. 34-12. - Approval and denial of license; public hearing.
Upon determination by the clerk of the board of commissioners that the application is complete as to form, it shall be presented to and received by the board of commissioners at a regular meeting and shall either be granted, if the application is found to comply with the provisions of this article and if the board of commissioners in their legitimate discretion determine that such issuance is in the best interest of the citizens of the county, or denied at such meeting, or any subsequent meeting if the matter is tabled or otherwise postponed. The board of commissioners may, in their discretion, call for, advertise and hold a public hearing in order to receive comments on the proposed application for franchise prior to decision. If the application for franchise is approved by the board of commissioners, the application will be returned to the clerk of the board of commissioners with the board of commissioners approval thereon.
(Ord. of 5-25-99)
Sec. 34-13. - Hearings.
All hearings under this division will be held as public hearings before the board of commissioners of the county. All persons desiring to be heard will be heard and any person appearing at the hearing may be represented by an attorney. The hearing may be adjourned to a later date for completion of debate and all action of the board of commissioners relating to this hearing will be open to the public.
(Ord. of 5-25-99)
Sec. 34-14. - Revocation.
(a)
The franchise issued pursuant to this article may be revoked by the board of commissioners after reasonable notice to the licensee and opportunity for a hearing thereon for any one (1) or more of the following reasons:
(1)
Fraud, misrepresentation or false statements contained in conjunction with the provision of ambulance services hereunder;
(2)
Fraud, misrepresentation or false statements made in conjunction with the provision of ambulance service hereunder;
(3)
Violation of any of the provisions of this article;
(4)
Conducting the business franchise pursuant to the provisions of this article in an unlawful manner, in a manner to constitute a public nuisance, or in manner to constitute a breach of peace or constitute a menace to the health, safety or the general welfare of the public.
(b)
Notice of hearing for revocation of a franchise shall be given in writing to the franchisee, setting forth specifically the grounds of the compliant and the time, date and place of the hearing thereon. Such notice shall be served on the franchisee by handing the same to the person in charge of the franchised business or by mailing the same, postage prepaid to the franchise at his last known address or to the designated agent for the service of process or the registered agent of the franchisee, at least five (5) days prior to the date set forth the hearing.
(c)
Such service of the notice shall suspend the license pending the outcome of the hearing, and any business operation conducted pursuant to the provisions of the franchise shall promptly cease and terminate.
(Ord. of 5-25-99)
Sec. 34-15. - Renewal.
Any franchise granted under this article, after issuance, must be renewed annually not later than January 1 of each year; and such franchise shall be considered for renewal only in the event a current franchisee is in full and complete compliance with all of the provisions of this article and all other appropriate ordinances of the county and the laws of this state. Renewal shall comport with the requirements set forth in section 34-12 of this article.
(Ord. of 5-25-99)
Sec. 34-16. - Nontransferable.
No franchise under this article may be sold, transferred or assigned by franchise, or by operation of law, to any other person, firm or corporation. Any such sale, transfer or assignment or attempted sale, transfer or assignment shall be deemed to constitute a voluntary surrender of such franchise, which will thereafter be null and void; provided and excepting, however, that if the franchisee is a partnership and one or more of the surviving partners may acquire, by purchase or otherwise the interest of the deceased partner or partners without effecting a surrender or termination of the franchise; and in such case the franchise, upon notification to the county, shall be placed in the name to the surviving partner.
(Ord. of 5-25-99)
Sec. 34-17. - Automatic revocation.
Whenever the state shall revoke any license required by the franchisee to operate ambulance services, the franchisee granted herein shall be automatically revoked without further action by the board of commissioners or any other officer of the county.
(Ord. of 5-25-99)
Sec. 34-18. - Inspection.
The county may at any time during reasonable business hours inspect the records of the company to determine whether the company is complying with the terms of this article. At the request in writing of any patient who has ridden in an ambulance, the company will provide the account number or patient number applying to that patient. The county may copy at the county's expense, any company records that the county has a right to inspect. The county will not remove any records from the company's office without the consent of the company.
(Ord. of 5-25-99)
Sec. 34-19. - Term and renewal of franchise.
The franchise will be in full force and effect from the _______ day of _______ _______, to the _______ day of _______, _______. This franchise may be renewed in its present form or under revised conditions at any time before or after its expiration.
(Ord. of 5-25-99)
Sec. 34-20. - Existing ambulance services.
All existing ambulance services operating within the county on the date of the adoption of this division shall be required to comply with this division within 90 days of its adoption.
(Ord. of 5-25-99)
Sec. 34-21. - Legal status provisions.
(a)
Notice. Notice of violations will be sent by registered mail to the named person in the application.
(b)
Penalty. Any person, firm or corporation violating any provision of this division shall be fined not less than $500.00 nor more than $1000.00 for each offense, and a separate offense shall be deemed committed on each day during or on which a violation occurs or continues.
(Ord. of 5-25-99)

Sec. 34-51. - Intent.
It is the intent of this article to provide the necessary organization, powers, and authority to enable the timely and effective use of all county resources to prepare for, respond to and recover from disasters and/or emergencies, natural or manmade, likely to affect the health, security, safety, or property of the county and its residents, whether such events occur within or without the corporate limits of the county.
(Res./Ord. of 12-2-03, § 1; Ord. of 8-6-13, § I)
Sec. 34-52. - Definitions.
As used in this article, the following terms shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, unless context clearly indicates otherwise:
Building contractor shall mean any person, firm, partnership, corporation or other entity engaging in, undertaking or carrying on any business consisting of or relating to building construction, repair, renovation or making improvements to real property including dwellings, homes, buildings, structures, or fixtures attached thereto.
Civil emergency shall include but not be limited to any condition of unrest, riot, civil disobedience, affray, unlawful assembly, hostile or military or paramilitary action, war, terrorism or sabotage.
Disaster, whether natural or manmade, shall include but not be limited to flood, fire, cyclone, tornado, earthquake, severe high or low temperatures, water contamination or pollution, land contamination or pollution, air pollution, blizzard, landslide, mudslide, hurricane, building or structural collapse, high water table, pandemic disease, epidemic, riot, blight, drought, civil emergency, utility emergency, severe energy shortages, snow, ice, windstorm, hazardous substance spills or releases, chemical spills or releases, petroleum spills or releases, biological matter spills or releases, radiation releases or exposures, infestation, explosions, sabotage, mass transportation accidents or public health emergencies.
Doing business. Any building contractor shall be deemed to be "doing business" subject to the requirements of this article if:
(1)
He has or operates an office, agency, project site or place of business located in the unincorporated areas of the county, whether permanently, temporarily, periodically, or otherwise, that provides the following activities in the unincorporated areas of the county expressly including but not limited to the construction, renovation or repair of dwellings or buildings or the making of improvements to real property or any fixtures attached thereto; or
(2)
He performs the following activities or services in the unincorporated areas of the county expressly including but not limited to the construction, renovation or repair of dwellings or buildings or the making of improvements to real property or any fixtures attached thereto regardless of the location of the principal office.
Emergency means the imminent threat or occurrence of a disaster, civil emergency or utility emergency affecting the county and its residents and inhabitants where the chairman determines that the exercise or discharge of emergency or disaster powers is necessary to save lives, protect property, to protect the public health and safety, or to lessen or to avert the threat of a catastrophe or calamity within the county.
Governor's state of emergency. The term "governor's state of emergency" is defined, pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 38-3-3(7), as a condition declared by the governor when, in the governor's judgment, the threat or actual occurrence of a disaster is of sufficient severity and magnitude as to warrant extraordinary efforts in preventing or alleviating the damage, loss, hardship, or suffering threatened or caused thereby.
Utility emergency shall include but not be limited to conditions which endanger or threaten to endanger the safety, potability, availability, transmission, distribution, treatment, or storage of water, natural gas, gas, fuel, electricity, communication, garbage or sewage.
(Res./Ord. of 12-2-03, § 1; Ord. of 8-6-13, § II)
Sec. 34-53. - Emergency management and response powers.
The chairman of the county board of commissioners is authorized, if the chairman finds that the county or any part thereof is suffering or is in imminent danger of suffering a disaster, emergency, civil emergency, or utility emergency, to declare a local disaster or emergency and issue orders exercising the following authority:
(1)
Temporarily suspend, limit, cancel, convene, reschedule, postpone, continue, or relocate all meetings of the board of commissioners, and any committee, commission, board, authority, or other county body as deemed appropriate by the chairman.
(2)
Suspend or limit the sale, distribution, dispensing, or transportation of alcoholic beverages, explosives, and combustible products and require the closing of those businesses or parts of businesses insofar as the sale, distribution, dispensing, or transportation of these items is concerned.
(3)
Establish a curfew during such hours of the days or nights and affecting such categories of persons as may be designated.
(4)
Require closing of business establishments.
(5)
Prohibit the sale or distribution within the county of any products which could be employed in a manner which would constitute a danger to public safety.
(6)
Temporarily close any and all streets, alleys, sidewalks, bike paths, public parks or public ways.
(7)
Temporarily suspend or modify, for not more than 60 days, any regulation or ordinance of the county, including, but not limited to, those regarding health, safety, and zoning. This period may be extended upon approval of the board of commissioners.
(8)
Control, restrict, allocate, or regulate the use, sale, production, or distribution of food, water, fuel, clothing, and/or other commodities, materials, goods, services, and resources.
(9)
Suspend or limit burning of any items or property within the county unincorporated limits.
(10)
Require emergency services of any county officer or employee. If regular county forces are determined to be inadequate, then to acquire the services of such other personnel as the chairman can obtain that are available, including citizen volunteers. All duly authorized persons rendering emergency services shall be entitled to the privileges and immunities as are provided by state law to other registered and identified disaster emergency workers.
(11)
Utilize all available resources of the county as reasonably necessary to manage the disaster or emergency.
(12)
Direct and compel the evacuation of all or part of the population from any stricken or threatened areas within the county if the chairman deems this action is necessary for the preservation of life, property, or other disaster or emergency mitigation, response or recovery and to prescribe routes, modes of transportation and destination in connection with an evacuation.
(13)
Purchase or lease materials, supplies, or equipment during the local disaster or emergency, and to authorize any or all agencies or county officials to directly purchase or lease those materials, supplies, or equipment essential to continue the work of the county involved without the advertisement of bids.
(14)
Hire and contract for construction, engineering, architectural, building, electrical, plumbing, and/or other services essential to continue the work of the county without the advertisement of bids.
(15)
Make application for local, state, or federal assistance.
(16)
Establish and control routes of transportation, ingress or egress.
(17)
Control ingress and egress from any designated disaster or emergency area or home, building or structures located therein.
(18)
Utilize private property as necessary to cope with emergency or disaster conditions, subject to any requirements established by law for compensation.
(19)
Appropriate and expend funds, execute contracts, authorize the obtaining and acquisition of property, equipment, services, supplies, and materials without strict compliance with procurement regulations or procedures, public works laws, and public bidding laws.
(20)
Transfer the direction, personnel, or functions of county departments and agencies for the purpose of performing or facilitating emergency or disaster services.
(21)
Accept services, gifts, grants, loans, equipment, supplies, and/or materials whether from private, nonprofit, or governmental sources.
(22)
Hire or contract for construction, engineering, architectural, building, electrical, plumbing, and/or other professional or construction services essential to the continuation of activities of the county without the advertising of bids or compliance with procurement requirements, public works laws, and public bidding laws.
(23)
Terminate or suspend any process, operation, machine, device, or event that is or may negatively impact the health, safety, and welfare of persons or property within the county.
(24)
Delegate authority to such county officials as the chairman determines reasonably necessary, convenient, or expedient.
(25)
Require the continuation, termination, disconnection, or suspension of natural gas, electrical power, water, sewer, communication or other public utilities or infrastructure.
(26)
Close or cancel the use of any county owned or operated building or other public facility.
(27)
Disinfect, abate, demolish, remove or clean up any building, structure, site, wreckage or debris.
(28)
Declare, issue, enforce, modify and terminate orders for quarantine and isolation of persons or animals posing a threat to public health.
(29)
Exercise such powers and functions in light of the exigencies of emergency or disaster including the waiving of compliance with any time-consuming procedures and formalities, including notices, as may be prescribed by law.
(30)
Issue any and all such other orders or undertake such other functions and activities as the chairman reasonably believes is required to protect the health, safety, and welfare of persons or property within the county or otherwise preserve the public peace or abate, clean up, or mitigate the effects of any emergency or disaster.
(Res./Ord. of 12-2-03, § 1; Ord. of 2-5-13, § 1; Ord. of 8-6-13, § III)
Sec. 34-54. - Filing of declaration.
Any declaration of disaster emergency issued by the chairman shall be promptly filed with the clerk of the board of commissioners and the public should be notified through available media of said declaration.
(Res./Ord. of 12-2-03, § 1; Ord. of 8-6-13, § IV)
Sec. 34-55. - Effective term of declaration.
The declaration of a local disaster or emergency shall be in effect for a period of up to 30 days. this period may be extended by the chairman only upon approval of the board of commissioners.
(Res./Ord. of 12-2-03, § 1; Ord. of 8-6-13, § V)
Sec. 34-56. - Succession of authority.
If the chairman is unavailable, the vice-chairman of the board of commissioners shall have said authority, followed by the member of the board of commissioners having held that elected office for the longest period of time.
(Res./Ord. of 12-2-03, § 1; Ord. of 8-6-13, § VI)
Sec. 34-57. - Building contractor registration required.
(a)
No person, firm, partnership, corporation or other entity shall engage in, undertake or carry on any business, in a governor's state of emergency in whole or in part within the unincorporated areas of the county, consisting of or relating to building, constructing, repairing, renovating or making improvements to real property including dwellings, homes, buildings, structures, or fixtures attached thereto without having registered the name of the business with the building official and having paid fees as provided by this article.
(b)
All building contractors doing business or proposing to do business in the unincorporated areas of the county during a governor's state of emergency or the period during which the disaster continues to cause disruptions in the disaster area, which shall not exceed three months after the emergency declaration, shall register and file applications with the building official, or his designee, at the department of community development. The building contractor shall, under oath, provide the building official with a statement describing the general nature of the business to be conducted and give true and correct information as may be called for on the registration form, application or certificate provided by the county.
(c)
Building contractor registration fees, during a period of a governor's state of emergency are hereby fixed under the terms and conditions of this article at $50.00 per annum. Registration fees shall be paid in full at the time of issuance of the registration certification.
(Res./Ord. of 12-2-03, § 1; Ord. of 8-6-13, § VII)
Sec. 34-58. - Overcharging prohibited.
(a)
Overcharging prohibited. In order to preserve, protect, or sustain the life, health, or safety of persons or their property, it shall be unlawful during the duration of a governor's state of emergency or period during which the disaster in the county continues to cause disruptions in the disaster area, which shall not exceed six months, for any person, firm, or corporation located or doing business in the county to overcharge for any goods, materials, services or housing sold within the county.
(b)
Overcharging. The term "overcharging" is defined as charging prices for goods, materials, services, or housing which are substantially in excess of the customary charges or in applicable cases substantially in excess of the supplier's or provider's costs for such goods, materials, services or housing. The existence of overcharging shall be presumed from a substantial increase in the price at which the goods, materials, services, or housing was offered in the usual course of business immediately prior to the onset of the emergency, but shall not include increases in costs to the supplier directly attributable to higher costs of materials, supplies, and labor costs resulting from the emergency.
(Res./Ord. of 12-2-03, § 1; Ord. of 8-6-13, § VIII)
Sec. 34-59. - Prohibited conduct during disaster or emergency.
During the period an actual or impending local disaster or emergency has been declared, no person shall:
(1)
Enter or remain upon the premises of any establishment not open for business to the general public, unless such person is the owner or agent of the establishment.
(2)
Violate any of the orders duly issued by the chairman or authorized personnel.
(3)
Willfully obstruct, hinder, or delay any duly authorized peace officer, building official, code enforcement officer, or other authorized personnel in the enforcement of the provisions of this article, or any order or regulation issued under authority provided for in this article.
(Res./Ord. of 12-2-03, § 1; Ord. of 8-6-13, § IX)
Sec. 34-60. - Location of board of commissioners meetings and department.
(a)
Whenever an emergency or disaster makes it imprudent or impossible to conduct the affairs of the county at its regular locations, the board of commissioners may meet at any place, inside or outside the unincorporated limits of the county. Any temporary disaster meeting location for the board of commissioners shall continue until a new location is established or until the emergency or disaster is terminated and the board of commissioners is able to return to its normal location.
(b)
Whenever an emergency or disaster makes it imprudent or impossible to conduct the affairs of any department of the county at its regular location, such department may conduct its business at any place, inside or outside the unincorporated limits of the county, and may remain at the temporary location until the emergency or disaster is declared ended or until the department is able to return to its normal location.
(c)
Any official act or meeting required to be performed at any regular location of the board of commissioners or of its departments shall be valid when performed at any temporary location established under this article.
(Res./Ord. of 12-2-03, § 1; Ord. of 8-6-13, § X)
Sec. 34-61. - Mutual aid agreements.
(a)
The chairman may, on behalf of the county, enter into such reciprocal aid, mutual aid, joint powers agreements, intergovernmental assistance agreements, or other compacts, plans, or agreements with other governmental entities for the protection of life and property. Such agreements may include the furnishing or exchange of supplies, equipment, facilities, personnel, and/or services.
(b)
The board of commissioners or any of its committees, commissions, or authorities may exercise such powers and functions in light of the exigencies of the emergency or disaster and may waive compliance with time consuming procedures and formalities prescribed by law.
(Res./Ord. of 12-2-03, § 1; Ord. of 8-6-13, § XI)
Sec. 34-62. - Authority to enter property.
During the period of a declared emergency or disaster, a county employee or authorized agent may enter onto or upon private property if the employee or authorized agent has reasonable grounds to believe that there is a true emergency or disaster and an immediate need for assistance for the protection of life or property, and that entering onto the private land will allow the employee or authorized agent to take such steps to alleviate or minimize the emergency or disaster or to prevent or minimize risk or danger to lives or property from the declared emergency or disaster.
(Res./Ord. of 12-2-03, § 1; Ord. of 8-6-13, § XII)
Sec. 34-63. - Scope and application.
(a)
The provisions of this article shall apply to all boards, commissions, committees, authorities, and officers under the jurisdiction of the board of commissioners.
(b)
This article shall apply to the extent that any orders, rules, or regulations authorized by this article are not inconsistent with any orders, rules, or regulations promulgated by the governor or by any state agency exercising a power delegated to it by him. Furthermore, any orders, rules, or regulations authorized by this article that are inconsistent with any orders, rules, or regulations promulgated by the governor or by any state agency exercising a power delegated to it by him shall be suspended during the period of time and to the extent that the conflict exists.
(c)
In order to attain uniformity so far as practicable throughout the country in measures taken to aid emergency management, all orders, rules, and regulations made pursuant thereto shall be taken or made with due consideration to the orders, rules, regulations, actions, recommendations, and requests of federal authorities relevant thereto and, to the extent permitted by law, shall be consistent with such orders, rules, regulations, actions, recommendations, and requests.
(Res./Ord. of 12-2-03, § 1; Ord. of 8-6-13, § XIII)
Sec. 34-64. - Authority for enforcement.
The law enforcement agencies having jurisdiction, the county health department, the county environmental health department, the fire rescue department, or such other persons as authorized by the chairman of the board of commissioners, are hereby authorized and directed to enforce the orders, rules, and regulations made or issued pursuant to this article.
(Res./Ord. of 12-2-03, § 1; Ord. of 8-6-13, § XIV)
Sec. 34-65. - Penalties.
Violation of the provisions of this article upon conviction shall be punishable by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 per violation or imprisonment not to exceed 60 days, or both such fine and imprisonment. Each violation shall constitute a separate offense for each day the violations continue.
(Res./Ord. of 12-2-03, § 1; Ord. of 8-6-13, § XV)

ARTICLE III. - EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES REVIEW COMMITTEE

Sec. 34-81. - Creation.
The county emergency medical services (EMS) review committee is established by the county board of commissioners for the purpose of advising the chairman of the county commission on matters relating to the development and coordination of the emergency medical services system that serves the residents of the county.
(Res. of 6-2-15)
Sec. 34-82. - Mission.
The mission of the committee is to protect the public health and welfare by ensuring that prompt, quality, and coordinated emergency medical services are provided to the residents of the county. This mission is achieved through the systematic examination of service delivery procedures, equipment, and processes toward the goal of developing and maintaining high quality emergency medical care.
(Res. of 6-2-15)
Sec. 34-83. - Functions and duties.
Functions and duties of the EMS committee shall be to:
(1)
Recommend goals and objectives for a coordinated emergency medical services system.
(2)
Review and make recommendations for system improvements from the emergency medical services quality assurance data, medical best practice standards, and other such data as needed.
(3)
Improve the emergency medical services system by increased communications between the system members (i.e. EMS zone provider, E-911 Communications, fire/rescue, EMA, etc.)
(4)
Review and analysis of EMS zone provider's response times and other operational data on a regular basis.
(5)
Review and make recommendations of any EMS zone provider's operational issues brought to the attention of the committee.
(6)
Review and make recommendations for the improvement of the EMS zone provider's services to the county.
(7)
Increase community awareness of the EMS system in the county.
(8)
Review and make recommendations to ensure that EMS system personnel are prepared to respond to disasters, acts of terrorism, and other hazards.
(Res. of 6-2-15)
Sec. 34-84. - Membership.
(a)
Members. The voting members of the EMS committee shall consist of the following:
(1)
Chief of Carroll County Fire Rescue;
(2)
Chief of City of Carrollton Fire Department;
(3)
Director of Carroll County E-911;
(4)
Director of Carroll County Emergency Management Agency (EMA);
(5)
Current EMS zone provider.
Non-voting members shall be the county emergency medical director and Tanner Health System.
Any voting member of the EMS committee may be represented with full voting rights by a designated member of his/her respective agency if the voting member is unable to attend a meeting.
(b)
Attendance. Members missing more than four meetings in one fiscal year without a designee present shall be deemed to have excessive absences. In the event that a member has excessive absences, the chair shall correspond with the respective appointing authority for the agency reporting the absences.
(c)
Compensation. The members shall receive no compensation for serving on the EMS committee.
(d)
Terms. Each member of the EMS committee shall serve as long as he/she holds the position established for the members.
(Res. of 6-2-15)
Sec. 34-85. - Officers and duties.
(a)
The committee shall elect from its membership at the first meeting of the committee each calendar year a chairperson, vice-chairperson, and secretary for one-year terms who will serve immediately upon election. Officers may succeed himself or herself. If a vacancy occurs, the EMS review committee shall fill the office from its membership to complete the unexpired term.
(b)
Duties. The powers and duties of the officers shall be as follows:
(1)
Chair. It shall be the duty of the chair to set the meeting schedule for each fiscal year. The chair shall conduct the meetings. The chair shall appoint all ad hoc sub-committees necessary. The chair shall be the primary liaison between the committee and the chairman of the board of commissioners.
(2)
Vice-chair. The vice-chair shall assume the duties of the chair should the chair be not present at any meeting.
(3)
Secretary. The secretary shall keep the minutes of the committee and shall distribute same in a timely manner to the committee. The secretary shall be responsible for ensuring proper advertising of the meetings as required by the Georgia Open Meetings Act and shall additionally perform such other administrative tasks as requested by the chair in the furtherance of the work of the committee.
(Res. of 6-2-15)
Sec. 34-86. - Meetings.
(a)
Regular meetings. Regular meetings shall be held quarterly. Additional meetings shall be held at the call of the chairperson or as directed by the EMS review committee. Meetings shall be conducted in accordance with the simplified Robert's Rules of Order.
(b)
Quorum. A quorum of EMS review committee shall be three voting members or voting member designees. Official action may not be taken in the absence of a quorum. The EMS review committee may act by majority vote of the members in attendance if a quorum of members is present.
(c)
Requirements. All meetings of the EMS review committee must comply with the requirements of the Georgia Open Meetings (O.C.G.A. ch. 50-14) and Open Records (O.C.G.A. ch. 50-18) Laws.
(Res. of 6-2-15)
Sec. 34-87. - Operating year.
The operating year for the EMS Committee shall be January 1 until December 31.
(Res. of 6-2-15)
Sec. 34-88. - Amendments.
Proposed amendments to this article shall be presented in writing by any person to the chair with a copy to the secretary, who shall then notify all members of the proposed amendments in writing. Proposed amendments shall be brought before the committee not sooner than 30 days thereafter. Approval of any amendment to this article shall require the affirmative vote of no fewer than four voting members of the committee and approval by the board of commissioners. Amendments so adopted shall become effective immediately upon approval.
(Res. of 6-2-15)
