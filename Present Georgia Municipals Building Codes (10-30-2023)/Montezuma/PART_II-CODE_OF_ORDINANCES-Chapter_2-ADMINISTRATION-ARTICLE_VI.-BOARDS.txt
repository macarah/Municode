DIVISION 2. - BOARD OF APPEALS

Sec. 2-231. - Board of appeals created.
A board of appeals is hereby created.
(Code 1990, § 2-156; Ord. No. 300, § 1, 7-22-1997)
Sec. 2-232. - Membership and appointment.
The board of appeals shall consist of five members residing within the city appointed by the mayor of said city and ratified by the city council. One member may be a member of the planning and zoning commission. No other member of the board of appeals shall simultaneously hold any employment or elected office within the city government that is inconsistent with his duties herein. Members shall serve without compensation.
(Code 1990, § 2-157; Ord. No. 300, § 2, 7-22-1997)
Sec. 2-233. - Terms of office.
(a)
The term of office of each member shall be for one year, or thereafter until a successor is appointed. Members may be reappointed. Any vacancy in the membership shall be filled for the unexpired term in the same manner as the initial appointment.
(b)
Members shall be removable for cause by the mayor upon written charges and after a public hearing.
(c)
Any member of the board of appeals shall be disqualified to act on any matter in which the member has an interest, either direct or indirect.
(Code 1990, § 2-158; Ord. No. 300, § 3, 7-22-1997)
Sec. 2-234. - Officers.
The board of appeals shall elect one of its members, other than the member of the planning commission, as chairman and one of its members as vice-chairman. The chairman and vice-chairman shall serve for one year or until reelected or until a successor is elected. The board of appeals shall appoint a secretary who may be an employee of the city.
(Code 1990, § 2-159; Ord. No. 300, § 4, 7-22-1997)
Sec. 2-235. - Meetings: procedures; times; minutes.
(a)
The board of appeals shall adopt rules of procedures. Meetings of the board of appeals shall be at the call of the chairman and at such other times as the members of the board of appeals may determine. The chairman or, in his absence, the vice-chairman, may administer oaths and compel the attendance of witnesses by subpoena.
(b)
The board of appeals shall keep minutes of its proceedings, showing the vote of each member upon each question or, if absent or failing to vote, indicating such fact. The board of appeals shall keep records of the examinations and other official actions, all of which shall be immediately filed in the office of the clerk of said city and shall be a public record.
(Code 1990, § 2-160; Ord. No. 300, § 5, 7-22-1997)
Sec. 2-236. - Powers and jurisdiction.
The board of appeals shall have the power and jurisdiction to hear and decide appeals, when it alleged there is an error in any order, requirement, decision or determination made by the appropriate city official under:
(1)
The provisions of this Code pertaining to buildings and building regulations.
(2)
Any nuisance provision of this Code.
(3)
The planning and development provisions of this Code.
(4)
The zoning chapter of this Code.
(5)
The soil and sedimentation control provisions of this Code.
(6)
The subdivisions chapter of this Code.
(Code 1990, § 2-161; Ord. No. 300, § 6, 7-22-1997)
Sec. 2-237. - Appeal procedure.
(a)
Appeals to the board of appeals may be taken by any person aggrieved or by any official of the city. Such appeal shall be taken within 15 days after the decision is made, unless an exception is granted by the appeal board, by filing with the chief administrative officer a written notice of appeals specifying the grounds thereof. The clerk shall forthwith transmit to the board of appeals all the papers constituting the record upon which the appeal was taken.
(b)
An appeal shall stay all proceedings in furtherance of the action appealed from unless the clerk certifies to the board of appeals that, by reason of facts stated in the certificate, a stay would, in his opinion, cause imminent peril to life or property. In such a case, proceedings shall not be stayed otherwise than by the board of appeals or by a restraining order granted by a court of record on application, and notice to the city, for good cause.
(Code 1990, § 2-162; Ord. No. 300, § 7, 7-22-1997)
Sec. 2-238. - Public hearings required.
Before the board of appeals acts upon an application for relief or special exception, it shall hold a public hearing thereon. The notice of the time and place of such hearings shall be published at least 15 days prior to the hearing in the official organ of the county. At such hearing any party may appear in person or by agent or attorney.
(Code 1990, § 2-163; Ord. No. 300, § 8, 7-22-1997)
Sec. 2-239. - Reapplication.
If an application for relief or special exception is denied by the board of appeals, a reapplication for such shall not be made earlier than 12 months from the date of the original application.
(Code 1990, § 2-164; Ord. No. 300, § 1, 7-22-1997)
Sec. 2-240. - Appeal to superior court.
(a)
Any person or person severally or jointly aggrieved by any decision of the board of appeals may present an appeal to the superior court. Such appeal to the superior court shall be the same as an appeal to the superior court from any decision made by the probate court and as specified in the Official Code of Georgia Annotated, except, however, that the appeal shall be in writing, and filed within 30 days from the date of the decision of the board of appeals, and served upon the city. Upon failure to file the appeal within 30 days, the decision of the board of appeals shall be final.
(b)
On appeal, the case shall be heard by the judge of the superior court without a jury, unless one is demanded in writing by one of the parties within 30 days from the filing of the appeal.
(Code 1990, § 2-165; Ord. No. 300, § 10, 7-22-1997)

DIVISION 3. - TREE BOARD

Sec. 2-260. - Established.
(a)
There is hereby created the city tree board. The board shall consist of one member of the city council, a member of the city planning commission, the chief administrative officer, the public works chief, one member of the local garden club (if any) and any such additional members as the city council may designate. In making appointments, the council shall seek to appoint individuals with expertise in landscape design, horticulture and related areas of knowledge.
(b)
The terms of the members of the city tree board shall be for one year. Members may be reappointed at the discretion of the city council.
(c)
The members shall serve without compensation.
(d)
The board shall cooperate with and coordinate activities with all departments of the city, and all departments of the city shall cooperate with the board.
(e)
Each year the board shall elect a chairman who shall be a voting member of the board.
(f)
A meeting may be called by an elected chairman or two board members.
(g)
One-half of the board members in addition to the chairman shall constitute a quorum.
(Code 1990, § 20.5-3.2; Ord. No. 345, 6-10-2003)

DIVISION 4. - DOWNTOWN DEVELOPMENT AUTHORITY BOARD

Sec. 2-281. - Established; members; terms.
The city has created the downtown development authority board that consists of seven members appointed by the city council for terms of six years.
Sec. 2-282. - Qualifications; compensation.
All members of the board must be residents of the city and owners or operators of businesses located within the downtown development area. At least four of the members must have or represent a party who has an economic interest in the redevelopment and revitalization of the downtown development area. All members shall serve without compensation.
Sec. 2-283. - Vacancies.
Any vacancy in membership shall be filled for the unexpired term by the city council who shall also have the authority to remove any member for good cause including, but not limited to, failure to regularly attend meetings of the board or illness or incapacitation to the extent that the member cannot perform the duties of a member of the board.
