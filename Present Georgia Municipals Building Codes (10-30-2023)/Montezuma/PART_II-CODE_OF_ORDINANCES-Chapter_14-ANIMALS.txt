Chapter 14 - ANIMALS[1]
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 14-1. - Cruelty prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to willfully or maliciously inflict unnecessary or needless cruelty, torture or abuse, or cruelly beat, strike or abuse any animal or by an act of omission or neglect cause or inflict any unnecessary or unjustifiable pain, suffering, injury or death to any animal whether such animal belongs to such person or to another, except that reasonable force may be implemented to drive away vicious or trespassing animals, and any animal endangering the life or safety of any person may when necessary be killed.
(Code 1990, § 4-1; Ord. No. 241, 3-26-1991)
State Law reference— Cruelty to animals, O.C.G.A. § 16-12-4.
Sec. 14-2. - Care of animals.
It shall be unlawful for any person, whether owner, person in possession or any person in charge of any animal, to fail, refuse or neglect to provide such animal with the necessary food and water as well as necessary medical treatment for such animal.
(Code 1990, § 4-2; Ord. No. 241, 3-26-1991)
Secs. 14-3—14-22. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - LICENSING AND RABIES VACCINATION

Sec. 14-23. - License required.
Each owner or person residing within the limits of the city in possession of or custody of a dog being more than six months of age at any time within the year after January 1 of each year and within 30 days from the date such dog becomes six months of age, must pay a dog license tax to the city and obtain a license therefor.
(Code 1990, § 4-36; Ord. No. 241, 3-26-1991)
Sec. 14-24. - Term of license.
The license year shall commence on January 1 of each year and shall end on December 31 of that same year.
(Code 1990, § 4-37; Ord. No. 241, 3-26-1991)
Sec. 14-25. - Amount of license tax.
The dog license tax for each animal shall be in the amount established by resolution of the city council. Such license tax shall be paid at the office of the city administrator in the city hall.
(Code 1990, § 4-38; Ord. No. 241, 3-26-1991)
Sec. 14-26. - Rabies vaccination required.
It shall be the duty of each dog owner or person in possession or custody thereof to have such animal inoculated with antirabies vaccine or any other similar vaccine by a veterinarian as required by the laws of the state. It shall be the duty of each dog owner or person in possession or custody thereof, to have an inoculation tag firmly affixed to that animal at all times. No license can be issued for an animal without the owner or person in possession or custody thereof presenting evidence to the city that the animal has been inoculated for rabies.
(Code 1990, § 4-39; Ord. No. 241, 3-26-1991)
Sec. 14-27. - Certificate and license tag.
Upon payment of the dog license fee, the city administrator's office shall deliver to the owner or person in possession of the animal an appropriate certificate and tag for each animal licensed. The certificate shall include the name and address of the owner of the animal, date of vaccination, tag number, breed, color, age, sex and such other information as may be required. The owner or person in possession or custody of the animal must firmly affix the tag to that animal, which tag must be worn at all times. It shall be unlawful for any owner or person in possession or custody of a dog to allow the animal to go about without having the rabies inoculation tag and license tag affixed to the animal.
(Code 1990, § 4-40; Ord. No. 241, 3-26-1991)
Sec. 14-28. - Cat vaccination.
It shall be the duty of each owner or person in possession of any cat to have such animal inoculated with antirabies or similar vaccine by a veterinarian. There is no requirement that such animal wear a tag evidencing such inoculation.
(Code 1990, § 4-42; Ord. No. 241, 3-26-1991)
Secs. 14-29—14-46. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - RESTRAINT OF ANIMALS AND ENFORCEMENT

Sec. 14-47. - Restraint.
All dogs in the city shall be confined to the premises of the owner or person in possession or custody thereof and it shall be unlawful and a violation of this chapter for any owner or person in possession or custody thereof to permit any dog to run at large or stray from the premises of such person unless such animal is on a leash or otherwise under the immediate control of such owner, person in possession or custody thereof or their designee of sufficient age and abilities to be responsible for the dog.
(Code 1990, § 4-50; Ord. No. 241, 3-26-1991)
Sec. 14-48. - Enforcement.
(a)
In addition to the penalties provided in this section, any police officer of the city or any other person designated by the city administrator or appointed by the city council is authorized to impound any dog which does not carry rabies inoculation and license tags or is running at large or not under the control of the owner or person in possession thereof as provided above. The animal shall be impounded in whatever facility has been established by the city council for that purpose, including any veterinary hospital under contract with the city. The owner may obtain possession of any dog so impounded or seized by paying an impounding fee in the amount established by resolution of the city council as well paying the costs of keeping such animal during the time that it is impounded; provided, however, that the dog shall not be released until proof has been furnished to the impounding facility that the animal has been inoculated with antirabies vaccine and has been licensed as provided in this chapter.
(b)
The owner of any dog impounded for any reason authorized by this chapter which has been licensed and carries a license tag shall be notified immediately by the impounding officers but in no event later than 24 hours after impoundment.
(c)
Any dog so impounded which has not been licensed and does not carry a license tag shall be impounded for a period of 72 hours. If at the end of the 72-hour period no person claims ownership of that animal, the impounding facility is required to dispose of that animal in a humane manner.
(Code 1990, § 4-51; Ord. No. 241, 3-26-1991; Ord. No. 325, 3-13-2001)
Sec. 14-49. - Complaint for violation.
Upon complaint of an owner or other person in possession of private property that dogs are running at large unrestrained upon their property, or if any dog is found at large unrestrained, either upon private or public property, by any police officer or other employee or designated agent of the city or is unlicensed as required in article II of this chapter, a city warrant may be issued against the owner or person in possession or custody of such dog for violation of this chapter, requiring such offender to appear in the municipal court of this city for violation of this chapter.
(Code 1990, § 4-55; Ord. No. 241, 3-26-1991)
Sec. 14-50. - Prohibited conduct.
It shall be unlawful and a violation of this chapter for any person to interfere with any animal traps set out by the city and any of its authorized employees.
(Code 1990, § 4-56; Ord. No. 241, 3-26-1991)
Secs. 14-51—14-70. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - DANGEROUS DOGS[2]

Sec. 14-71. - Definitions.
modified
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Dangerous dog means any dog that according to the records of the city or any other appropriate authority:
(1)
Has without provocation inflicted severe injury on a human being on public or private property;
(2)
Aggressively bites, attacks, or endangers the safety of humans without provocation after the dog has been classified as a potentially dangerous dog and after the owner has been notified of such classification as provided in this article.
Dangerous dog control officer means any individual selected by the city to aid in the administration and enforcement of this article.
Owner means any natural person or other legal entity, including but not limited to a corporation, partnership, firm or trust owning, possessing, harboring, keeping, or having custody or control of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog within the limits of the city.
Potentially dangerous dog means any dog with a known propensity or disposition to attack unprovoked, to cause injury, or to otherwise threaten the safety of human beings; or the term means any dog that when unprovoked:
(1)
Inflicts bites on a human being on public or private property; or
(2)
Chases or approaches a human being upon the streets, sidewalks or any public grounds in a vicious or terrorizing manner in an apparent attitude of attack.
Proper enclosure means an enclosure for keeping a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog while on the owner's property securely confined indoors or in a securely enclosed and locked pen, fence or structure suitable to prevent the entry of young children and designed to prevent the dog from escaping. Any such pen or structure shall have secure tops and sides, and if the dog is enclosed within a fence, all sides of the fence shall be of sufficient height and the bottom of the fence shall be constructed or secured in such a manner as to prevent the dog's escape either from over or under the fence. Any such enclosure shall also provide protection of the dog from the elements.
Severe injury means any physical injury that results in broken bones or disfiguring lacerations requiring multiple sutures or cosmetic surgery or a physical injury that results in death.
(Code 1990, § 4-61; Ord. No. 213, § 2, 12-13-1988)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-22.
Sec. 14-72. - Jurisdiction.
The jurisdiction of the city for purposes of enforcement of this article shall be the incorporated area of the city.
(Code 1990, § 4-62; Ord. No. 213, § 3(a), 12-13-1988)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-22(a).
Sec. 14-73. - Intergovernmental contracts or agreements.
The city may enter into contract or agreement with the governing authority of the county or any other municipal corporation located in the county for purpose of joint dog control services or use of personnel, facilities, and equipment in carrying out the enforcement of this article.
(Code 1990, § 4-63; Ord. No. 213, § 3(b), 12-13-1988)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-22(b).
Sec. 14-74. - Designation of dog control officer.
The city council shall designate an individual to carry out the duties of dog control officer as provided in this article. The dog control officer may be an employee of another municipal corporation in the county or an employee of the board of commissioners of the county and may carry out the duties of dog control officer for more than one local government pursuant to contract or agreement as provided in this article. No person designated as dog control officer shall have the authority to make an arrest unless that person is a law enforcement officer having the powers of arrest.
(Code 1990, § 4-64; Ord. No. 213, § 4, 12-13-1988)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-22(c).
Sec. 14-75. - Animal control board designated.
The county board of health and its appointed members are hereby designated as the animal control board for purpose of conducting hearings as set forth in this article, and such board is hereby authorized and granted jurisdiction to conduct such hearings and determine such matters as provided for in this article.
(Code 1990, § 4-65; Ord. No. 213, § 5, 12-13-1988)
State Law reference— Animal control board authorized, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-22(d).
Sec. 14-76. - Identification of dangerous dogs and their owners.
The dog control officer shall make such investigations and inquiries as may be necessary to identify dangerous dogs and dangerous dog owners within the corporate limits of the city and shall have the authority to go upon private property for the purpose of carrying out his duties.
(Code 1990, § 4-66; Ord. No. 213, § 6(a), 12-13-1988)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-23(a).
Sec. 14-77. - Notice of classification of dog to owner.
(a)
When the dog control officer classifies a dog as a dangerous dog or reclassifies a potentially dangerous dog as a dangerous dog, the dog control officer shall notify the dog owner in writing by certified mail to the owner at the last known address of such classification or reclassification. Such notice shall be complete upon its mailing.
(b)
The written notice to the owner shall meet the following requirements:
(1)
The notice shall include a summary of the dog control officer's findings that formed the basis for the dog's classification as a dangerous or potentially dangerous dog.
(2)
The notice shall be dated and shall state that within 15 days after the date shown on the notice or the date mailed, as established by the receipt issued by the post office on mailing of the certified letter, the owner has a right to request a hearing on the dog control officer's determination that the dog is a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog.
(3)
The notice shall state that the hearing, if requested, shall be before the county board of health acting as the animal control board and shall specify the name and address of the individual responsible for receiving such request for hearings by the board of health.
(4)
The notice shall state that if a hearing is not requested, the dog control officer's determination that the dog is a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog will become effective for all purposes under this article on the date specified in the notice, which date shall be one day after the last day on which the owner has the right to request a hearing.
(5)
The notice shall include a form to request a hearing before the board of health, which form shall provide specific instructions on mailing or delivering such request to the board of health.
(Code 1990, § 4-67; Ord. No. 213, § 6(b), (c), 12-13-1988)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. §§ 4-8-23(b), 4-8-24(b), (c).
Sec. 14-78. - Hearing.
(a)
When the board of health receives a request for a hearing pursuant to this article, it shall schedule such hearing within 30 days after receiving the request. The board of health shall notify the dog owner in writing by certified mail of the date, time and place of the hearing and such notice shall be mailed to the dog owner at least ten days prior to the hearing. At the hearing, the dog owner shall be given the opportunity to testify and present evidence, and in addition, the board of health shall receive such other evidence and testimony as the dog control officer or any other person may desire to present to the board of health, or that the board of health may desire to present in order to make a determination to either sustain, modify, or overrule the dog control officer's classification of the dog.
(b)
Within ten days after the hearing, the board of health shall notify the dog owner in writing, as well as the dog control officer of its determination on the matter. If such determination is that the dog is a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog, the notice shall specify that the effective date of that determination is the day after the dog owner receives that notification.
(Code 1990, § 4-68; Ord. No. 213, § 7, 12-13-1988)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-24(d), (e).
Sec. 14-79. - Possession without registration prohibited.
It is unlawful and a violation of this article for any owner to have or to possess within the corporate limits of the city a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog without a certificate of registration as provided for in this article. The animal control officer or any officer of the police department is authorized to arrest any owner violating the dangerous dog control law and to have a warrant issued against such owner for such violation.
(Code 1990, § 4-69; Ord. No. 213, § 8, 12-13-1988)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-25(a).
Sec. 14-80. - Requirements for registration.
The dog control officer shall issue a certificate of registration to the owner of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog upon the owner presenting to the dog control officer the following:
(1)
Erection or construction of a proper enclosure to confine the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog;
(2)
The posting on the premises where the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog is located of a visible sign that there is a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog on the property and containing a symbol to warn children of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog;
(3)
A policy of insurance in the amount of at least $1,000,000.00 issued by an insurer authorized to transact business in this state, insuring the owner of the dangerous dog against liability for any personal injuries inflicted by the dangerous dog, or a surety bond in the amount of $1,000,000.00 or more issued by a surety company authorized to do business in this state, payable to any person injured by the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog.
(Code 1990, § 4-70; Ord. No. 213, § 9, 12-13-1988)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-25(b), (c).
Sec. 14-81. - Fee for annual registration.
The fee for the registration of a dangerous or potentially dangerous dog as required by this article shall be in the amount established by resolution of the city council. The fee shall be an annual fee for each calendar year regardless of the month in which the registration is obtained and the registration fee shall be for that year only. The registration shall end on December 31 of each year, and an owner may obtain a registration for the next calendar year at any time during the month of December or the current year of registration.
(Code 1990, § 4-71; Ord. No. 213, § 12, 12-13-1988)
State Law reference— Fee authorized, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-25(f).
Sec. 14-82. - Confiscation of dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog.
(a)
The dog control officer or any other law enforcement officer or any other person authorized by the dog control officer shall immediately confiscate a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog upon the following:
(1)
If the owner of the dangerous dog has not presented proof of liability insurance or bond as provided in this article;
(2)
If the dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog is not registered as required by this article or is not maintained in a proper enclosure or is outside the proper enclosure without being restrained by a substantial chain or leash and under the restraint of a responsible person and in the case of a dangerous dog is not also restrained by a muzzle.
(b)
Any dog that has been confiscated shall be returned to its owner upon the compliance with the provision of this article and upon the payment of the confiscation fee, in the amount established by resolution of the city council, together with any such actual costs as are incurred by the city in confiscation of that animal. If an owner has not complied with the provisions of this article within 20 days of the date the dog was confiscated, the dog shall be destroyed in an expeditious and humane manner.
(Code 1990, § 4-72; Ord. No. 213, § 10, 12-13-1988)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-27.
Sec. 14-83. - Notification of dog at large or disposed of.
(a)
The owner or other person having custody of a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog shall immediately notify the dog control officer or the police department not less than 24 hours after such dog is on the loose or unconfined, has attacked a human, has died, or has been sold or donated to another person.
(b)
If such dog has been sold or donated to another person, the owner or person in custody of such dangerous or potentially dangerous dog shall within 24 hours of such sale or donation, provide the dog control officer or the police department, with the name, address and telephone number of the new dog owner.
(Code 1990, § 4-73; Ord. No. 213, § 11, 12-13-1988)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-25(d).
Sec. 14-84. - Release from criminal liability.
No owner or other person in custody of a dangerous dog shall be held criminally liable under the provisions of this article for injuries inflicted by the owner's dangerous dog to any human being while on the owner's property.
(Code 1990, § 4-74; Ord. No. 213, § 13, 12-13-1988)
State Law reference— Similar provisions, O.C.G.A. § 4-8-28(f).
