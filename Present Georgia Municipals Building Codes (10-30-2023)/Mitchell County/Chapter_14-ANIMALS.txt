ARTICLE II. - DANGEROUS DOGS[2]

Sec. 14-19. - Definitions.
(a)
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Board means the animal control board.
Classified dog means any dog that has been classified as either a dangerous dog or vicious dog pursuant to this article.
Dangerous dog means any dog that, according to the records of an appropriate authority:
(1)
Causes a substantial puncture of a person's skin by teeth without causing serious injury; provided, however, that a nip, scratch, or abrasion shall not be sufficient to classify a dog as dangerous under this subsection;
(2)
Aggressively attacks in a manner that causes a person to reasonably believe that the dog posed an imminent threat of serious injury to such person or another person although no such injury occurs; provided, however, that the acts of barking, growling, or showing of teeth by a dog shall not be sufficient to classify a dog as dangerous under this subsection; or
(3)
While off the owner's property, kills a pet animal; provided, however, that this subsection shall not apply where the death of such pet animal is caused by a dog that is working or training as a hunting dog, herding dog, or predator control dog.
Dog control officer means an individual selected by the county pursuant to the provisions of O.C.G.A. § 4-8-22 to aid in the administration and enforcement of the provisions of this article.
Owner means any natural person or any legal entity, including, but not limited to, a corporation, partnership, firm, or trust owning, possessing, harboring, keeping, or having custody or control of a dog. In the case of a dog owned by a minor, the term "owner" includes the parents or person in loco parentis with custody of the minor.
Potentially dangerous dog means any dog that without provocation bites a human being or domesticated animal on public or private property at any time after March 31, 1989.
Proper enclosure means an enclosure for keeping a dangerous dog or potentially dangerous dog while on the owner's property securely confined indoors or in a securely enclosed and locked pen, fence, or structure suitable to prevent the entry of young children and designed to prevent the dog from escaping. Any such pen or structure shall have secure sides and a secure top, and, if the dog is enclosed within a fence, all sides of the fence shall be of sufficient height and the bottom of the fence shall be constructed or secured in such a manner as to prevent the dog's escape either from over or from under the fence. Any such enclosure shall also provide protection from the elements for the dog.
Records of an appropriate authority means records of any state, county, or municipal law enforcement agency; records of any county or municipal animal control agency; records of any county board of health; records of any federal, state, or local court; or records of a dog control officer as provided in this article and by the laws of the state.
Serious injury means any physical injury that creates a substantial risk of death; results in death, broken or dislocated bones, lacerations requiring multiple sutures, or disfiguring avulsions; requires plastic surgery or admission to a hospital; or results in protracted impairment of health, including transmission of an infection or contagious disease, or impairment of the function of any bodily organ.
Severe injury means any physical injury that creates a substantial risk of death; results in death, broken or dislocated bones, lacerations requiring multiple sutures or disfiguring avulsions; requires plastic surgery or admission to a hospital; or results in protracted impairment of health, including transmission of an infection or contagious disease, or impairment of the function of any bodily organ.
Vicious dog means a dog that inflicts serious injury on a person or causes serious injury to a person resulting from reasonable attempts to escape from the dog's attack.
(b)
The terms "dangerous dog" and "vicious dog," as defined in subsection (a) of this section, shall not include the following:
(1)
A dog that inflicts an injury upon a person when the dog is being used by a law enforcement or military officer to carry out the law enforcement or military officer's official duties; and
(2)
A dog that inflicts an injury within the meaning of this article if the injury was sustained by a person who, at the time, was committing a willful trespass or other tort, or was abusing the dog or was committing or attempting to commit a crime.
(Code 2004, § 14-31; Ord. of 2-11-1997, §§ 1, 2; Ord. No. 2016-006, exh. A(14-31), 4-12-2016)
Sec. 14-20. - Animal control board.
(a)
There is created and established an animal control board. The purpose of the board shall be to conduct the hearings provided for in section 14-22.
(b)
The animal control board shall consist of five members. All members shall be appointed by the board of commissioners. The initial terms of members shall be as follows: one member to serve for one year, two members to serve for two years, and two members to serve for three years. Thereafter, all members appointed to the board by the board of commissioners shall be appointed for terms of three years and until their successors are appointed.
(c)
No member of the board shall participate in a hearing on any matter in which such member previously participated in the classification of the dog at issue. In the event of a vacancy during the term of any member by reason of death, resignation, or otherwise, the appointment of a successor by the board of commissioners shall be for the remainder of the unexpired term of such member.
(Code 2004, § 14-33; Ord. of 2-11-1997, § 3; Ord. No. 2016-006, exh. A(14-32), 4-12-2016)
Sec. 14-21. - Dog control officer.
The county administrator shall appoint a dog control officer to administer and enforce the requirements contained within this article.
(Code 2004, § 14-34; Ord. of 2-11-1997, § 4; Ord. No. 2016-006, exh. A(14-33), 4-12-2016)
Sec. 14-22. - Procedures for classifying dangerous dogs and vicious dogs; notice; hearing.
(a)
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Animal shelter means any facility operated by or under contract for the state or any county, municipal corporation, or other political subdivision for the purpose of impounding or harboring seized, stray, homeless, abandoned, or unwanted dogs, cats, and other animals; any veterinary hospital or clinic operated by a veterinarian or veterinarians which operates for such purpose in addition to its customary purposes; and any facility operated, owned, or maintained by a duly incorporated humane society, animal welfare society, or other nonprofit organization for the purpose of providing for and promoting the welfare, protection, and humane treatment of animals.
(b)
The dog control officer, upon receiving a report of a dangerous dog or vicious dog within dog control officer's jurisdiction, shall make such investigations as necessary to determine whether such dog is subject to classification as a dangerous dog or vicious dog.
(c)
Should the dog control officer determine that a dog is subject to classification as a dangerous dog or vicious dog, the dog control officer shall classify such dog accordingly. The dog control officer shall then mail a dated notice to the dog's owner within 72 hours of the classification. The notice to the owner shall meet the following requirements:
(1)
The notice shall be in writing and mailed by certified mail to the owner's last known address;
(2)
The notice shall include a summary of the dog control officer's findings that formed the basis for the dog's classification as a dangerous or vicious dog;
(3)
The notice shall state that the owner, within seven days after the date shown on the notice, has a right to request a hearing on the dog control officer's determination that the dog is a dangerous dog or vicious dog;
(4)
The notice shall state that the hearing, if requested, shall be before the animal control board;
(5)
The notice shall state that if a hearing is not requested within the allotted time, the dog control officer's determination shall become effective for all purposes; and
(6)
The notice shall include a form to request a hearing before the animal control board and shall provide specific instructions on mailing or delivering such request to the board.
(d)
If an owner cannot be located within ten days of a dog control officer's determination that a dog is subject to classification as a dangerous dog or vicious dog, such dog may be released to an animal shelter or humanely euthanized, as determined by the dog control officer.
(e)
When the animal control board receives a request for a hearing as provided in subsection (c) of this section, it shall schedule such hearing within 30 days after receiving the request; provided, however, that such a hearing may be continued for good cause shown. The board shall notify the dog owner in writing by certified mail of the date, time, and place of the hearing. Such notice shall be mailed to the dog owner at least ten days prior to the date of the hearing. At the hearing, the owner of the dog shall be given the opportunity to testify and present evidence. In addition, the board shall receive at the hearing such other evidence and hear such other testimony as the board may find reasonably necessary to make a determination either to sustain, modify, or overrule the dog control officer's classification of the dog.
(f)
Within ten days after the date of the hearing, the animal control board shall notify the dog owner in writing by certified mail of its determination on the matter. If such determination is that the dog is a dangerous dog or a vicious dog, the notice shall specify the date upon which that determination is effective. If the determination is that the dog is to be euthanized pursuant to section 14-27, the notice shall specify the date by which euthanasia shall occur.
(Code 2004, § 14-35; Ord. of 2-11-1997, § 5; Ord. No. 2016-006, exh. A(14-34), 4-12-2016)
Sec. 14-23. - Requirements for possessing a dangerous or vicious dog.
(a)
No one shall have, own, or possess within the county a dangerous dog or vicious dog without a certificate of registration issued in accordance with the provisions of this section. Certificates of registration shall be nontransferable and shall only be issued to a person 18 years of age or older. No more than one certificate of registration shall be issued per domicile.
(b)
The dog control officer shall issue a certificate of registration to the owner of a dangerous dog if the owner presents to the dog control officer or the dog control officer otherwise finds sufficient evidence of:
(1)
A proper enclosure to confine the dangerous dog; and
(2)
The posting of the premises where the dangerous dog is located with a clearly visible sign warning that there is a dangerous dog on the property. The owner of a dangerous dog shall receive such sign from the dog control officer at the time the owner pays the annual registration fee as required in subsection (d) of this section. In addition to the annual registration fee, the costs incurred by the county for production of the sign shall be paid by the owner of a dangerous dog.
(c)
Except as provided in subsections (e) and (f) of this section, the dog control officer shall issue a certificate of registration to the owner of a vicious dog if the owner presents to the dog control officer or the dog control officer otherwise finds sufficient evidence of:
(1)
A proper enclosure to confine the vicious dog;
(2)
The posting of the premises where the vicious dog is located with a clearly visible sign warning that there is a vicious dog on the property. The owner of a vicious dog shall receive such a sign from the dog control officer at the time the owner pays the annual registration fee as required in subsection (d) of this section. In addition to the annual registration fee, the costs incurred by the county for production of the sign shall be paid by the owner of a vicious dog;
(3)
A microchip containing an identification number and capable of being scanned has been injected under the skin between the shoulder blades of the dog;
(4)
A policy of insurance in the amount of at least $50,000.00 issued by an insurer authorized to transact business in the state insuring the owner of the vicious dog against liability for any bodily injury or property damage inflicted by the dangerous dog; or
(5)
A surety bond in the amount of $50,000.00 or more issued by a surety company authorized to transact business in the state payable to any person or persons injured by the vicious dog.
(d)
The owner of a dangerous dog or vicious dog shall pay an annual registration fee at the time the annual certificate of registration is issued. At the time of renewal of a certificate of registration for a vicious dog, a dog control officer shall verify that the owner is continuing to comply with provisions of this article. The annual registration fee shall be established by the board of commissioners, from time to time, and made available for public examination in the office of the county clerk. Certificates of registration shall be renewed in the month of the initial registration. Failure to renew a certificate within ten days of the renewal date or initial classification date shall constitute a violation of this article.
(e)
The owner of a dangerous dog or vicious dog shall notify the dog control officer if the owner is moving from the county.
(f)
The owner of a dangerous dog or vicious dog who is a new resident of the state shall register such dog as required by this article within 30 days after becoming a resident of the state and the county.
(g)
The owner of a dangerous dog or vicious dog who moves to the county from any other county within the state shall register such dog as required by this article within ten days after becoming a resident of the county.
(h)
The owner of a classified dog shall notify the dog control officer within 24 hours if the dog is on the loose, is unconfined, has attacked a human, has died, or has been euthanized.
(i)
The owner of a dangerous dog shall notify the dog control officer within 24 hours of the dangerous dog being transferred, sold, or donated. If the dangerous dog has been transferred, sold, or donated, the owner shall also provide the dog control officer with the name, address, and telephone number of the new owner of the dog.
(j)
A vicious dog shall not be transferred, sold, or donated to any other person unless it is relinquished to a government facility or veterinarian to be euthanized.
(k)
No certificate of registration shall be issued to any person who has been convicted of two or more violations of this article.
(l)
No person shall be the owner of more than one vicious dog.
(m)
No certificate of registration for a vicious dog shall be issued to any person who has been convicted of:
(1)
A serious violent felony as defined in O.C.G.A. § 17-10-6.1;
(2)
The felony of dog fighting as provided for in O.C.G.A. § 16-12-37 or the felony of aggravated cruelty to animals as provided for in O.C.G.A. § 16-12-4;
(3)
A felony involving trafficking in cocaine, illegal drugs, marijuana, methamphetamine, or ecstasy as provided for in O.C.G.A. §§ 16-13-31 and 16-13-31.1;
from the time of conviction until two years after completion of his sentence, nor to any person residing with such person.
(Code 2004, § 14-36; Ord. of 2-11-1997, § 6; Ord. No. 2016-006, exh. A(14-35), 4-12-2016)
Sec. 14-24. - Restrictions on permitting dangerous or vicious dogs outside of a proper enclosure.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for an owner of a dangerous dog to permit the dog to be outside a proper enclosure unless:
(1)
The dog restrained by a leash not to exceed six feet in length and is under the immediate physical restraint of a person capable of preventing the dog from engaging any other human or animal when necessary;
(2)
The dog is contained in a closed and locked cage or crate; or
(3)
The dog is working or training as a hunting dog, herding dog, or predator control dog.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for the owner of a vicious dog to permit the dog to be:
(1)
Outside a proper enclosure unless:
a.
The dog is muzzled and restrained by a leash not to exceed six feet in length and is under the immediate physical control of a person capable of preventing the dog from engaging any other human or animal when necessary; or
b.
The dog is contained in a closed or locked cage or crate; or
(2)
Unattended with minors.
(c)
A person who violates subsection (b) of this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor of high and aggravated nature.
(d)
An owner with a previous conviction for a violation of this article whose classified dog causes serious injury to a human being under circumstances constituting another violation of this article shall be guilty of a felony and upon conviction thereof shall be punished by imprisonment for not less than one nor more than ten years, a fine of not less than $5,000.00 not more than $10,000.00, or both. In addition, the classified dog shall be euthanized at the cost of the owner.
(e)
Any irregularity in classification proceedings shall not be a defense to any prosecution under this article so long as the owner of the dog received actual notice of the classification and did not pursue a civil remedy for the correction of the irregularity.
(Code 2004, § 14-37; Ord. of 2-11-1997, § 7; Ord. No. 2005-012, § 6, 11-8-2005; Ord. No. 2016-006, exh. A(14-36), 4-12-2016)
Sec. 14-25. - Confiscation of dogs; grounds; disposition.
(a)
A dangerous dog or vicious dog shall be immediately confiscated by the dog control officer or a law enforcement officer. If the dog control officer is not a Peace Officer Standards and Training Council ("POST")-certified law enforcement officer, the dog control officer shall seek the assistance of a POST-certified law enforcement officer in the confiscation of a dangerous dog or vicious dog in the case of any violation of this article. A refusal to surrender a dog subject to confiscation shall be a violation of this article.
(b)
The owner of any dog that has been confiscated pursuant to this article may recover such dog upon payment of all reasonable confiscation and housing costs and proof of compliance with the provisions of this article, unless such confiscation is deemed to be in error by the animal control board or by a dog control officer. All fines and all charges for services performed by a law enforcement or dog control officer shall be paid prior to owner recovery of the dog. Criminal prosecution shall not be stayed due to owner recovery or euthanasia of the dog.
(c)
In the event the owner has not complied with the provisions of this article with 14 days of the date the dog was confiscated, such dog shall be released to an animal shelter, as such term is defined in section 14-22, or euthanized in an expeditious and humane manner. The owner may be required to pay the costs of housing and euthanasia.
(Code 2004, § 14-38; Ord. of 2-11-1997, § 8; Ord. No. 2016-006, exh. A(14-37), 4-12-2016)
Sec. 14-26. - Impoundment; threat to public safety.
A law enforcement officer or dog control officer shall immediately impound a dog if the officer believes the dog poses a threat to the public safety.
(Ord. No. 2016-006, exh. A(14-38), 4-12-2016)
Sec. 14-27. - Euthanasia for causing serious injury on more than one occasion.
A dog that is found, after notice and opportunity for a hearing as provided by section 14-22, to have caused a serious injury to a human on more than one occasion shall be euthanized; provided, however, that no injury occurring before the adoption date of the ordinance from which this article is derived shall count for purposes of this section.
(Ord. No. 2016-006, exh. A(14-39), 4-12-2016)
Sec. 14-28. - Violations.
Except as provided otherwise in sections 14-23 and 14-30, any person who violates the provisions of this article shall be issued a citation for that violation to appear before the magistrate court of the county and upon conviction shall be punished as provided in section 14-29. Such a citation may be issued by a dog control officer based upon his own personal knowledge or upon a sworn written statement of another person who witnessed the violation, in which case the witness shall be subpoenaed to testify for the county in the magistrate court proceeding.
(Code 2004, § 14-32; Ord. of 2-11-1997, § 9; Ord. No. 2005-012, §§ 1—5, 11-8-2005; Ord. No. 2016-006, exh. A(14-40), 4-12-2016)
Sec. 14-29. - Penalties for violations.
Except as provided for in sections 14-23 and 14-30, any person convicted of a violation of this article shall be punished by a fine of not less than $25.00 and not more than $50.00 or by confinement in the county jail for a period of one day, or both, for the first offense. A second offense shall be punished by a fine of not less than $50.00 and not more than $100.00 or by confinement in the county jail for a period of two days, or both. Applicable court costs shall be levied in addition to any such fine imposed for a first or second offense. Any subsequent offense may be punished up to the maximum amount allowed for the violation of county ordinances by state law.
(Code 2004, § 14-32; Ord. of 2-11-1997, § 9; Ord. No. 2005-012, §§ 1—5, 11-8-2005; Ord. No. 2016-006, exh. A(14-41), 4-12-2016)
Sec. 14-30. - Obstruction of officer.
It shall be unlawful for any person to hinder, harass, interfere, or otherwise obstruct the performance of any dog control officer in the official performance of his duties as provided for in this article. Any person convicted of a violation of this section shall be punished by a fine in an amount not to exceed $1,000.00 plus applicable court costs or by confinement in the county jail for a period not to exceed 60 days or both such fine and confinement as authorized by O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq.
(Ord. No. 2016-006, exh. A(14-42), 4-12-2016)

ARTICLE III. - NUISANCE DOGS

Sec. 14-49. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Animal means any vertebrate creature, domestic or wild, except human beings.
Animal control officer means an employee or agent of the county, designated by the county administrator to administer and enforce the requirements contained within this article.
Animal shelter means any facility operated by the county for the temporary care, confinement and detention of animals and for the humane euthanasia and other disposition of dogs. The term shall also include any private facility authorized by the county administrator to impound, confine, detain, care for or destroy any dog.
At large means that a dog is off the property of its owner and not under restraint. Exceptions are: a dog while actually working livestock, locating or retrieving wild game in season for a licensed hunter; a dog that is within the confines of the real property that is used as a commercial or licensed kennel (including hunting plantations); assisting law enforcement officers or while actually being trained for any of these specifically enumerated pursuits; and a dog that is within the confines of the real property that the owner had permission to use or is within a motor vehicle (including an unleashed dog in an open bed of a truck).
Attack dog means a dog trained to attack on command or to protect persons or property, and who will cease to attack upon command.
County means the unincorporated area of Mitchell County, Georgia.
Impounding means the taking into custody of a dog by any police officer, animal control officer, or any authorized representative thereof.
Owner means any person having temporary or permanent custody of, sheltering or having charge of, harboring, exercising control over, or having property rights to, any dog covered by this article. A dog shall be deemed to be harbored if it is fed or sheltered for three or more consecutive days.
Public nuisance dog means any dog that endangers the life or health of persons or other animals, or substantially interferes with the rights of citizens other than their owners, to enjoyment of life or property. The term "public nuisance dog" shall include, but not be limited to:
(1)
Any dog that is at large;
(2)
Any dog that makes disturbing noises, including, but not limited to, continued and repeated howling, barking, whining, or other utterances causing unreasonable annoyance, disturbance, or discomfort to neighbors or others in close proximity to the premises where the dog is kept or harbored;
(3)
Any dog that causes fouling of the air by noxious or offensive odors and thereby creates unreasonable annoyance or discomfort to neighbors or others in close proximity to the premises where the dog is kept or harbored;
(4)
Any dog, whether or not on the property of its owner, that, without provocation, molests, attacks, or otherwise interferes with the freedom of movement of persons on a public right-of-way; or
(5)
Any dog that chases motor vehicles, bicycles or individuals in a public right-of-way.
Under restraint means that an animal is either secured by a leash or lead and under the control of a person physically capable of restraining the animal or beside a competent person and is obedient to that person's commands.
(Ord. No. 2014-001, § 14-40, 3-11-2014; Ord. No. 2016-007, exh. A(14-61), 4-12-2016)
Sec. 14-50. - Nuisances.
No owner shall keep any dog on any property located within the county when keeping of such dog constitutes a public nuisance dog. Exceptions of a public nuisance dog are: a dog while actually working livestock, locating or retrieving wild game in season for a licensed hunter, a dog that is within the confines of the real property that is used as a commercial or licensed kennel (including hunting plantations); assisting law enforcement officers or while actually being trained for any of these specifically enumerated pursuits; and a dog that is within the confines of the real property that the owner had permission to use or is within a motor vehicle (including an unleashed dog in an open bed of a truck).
(Ord. No. 2014-001, § 14-41, 3-11-2014; Ord. No. 2016-007, exh. A(14-62), 4-12-2016)
Sec. 14-51. - Investigation; seizure; disposition.
(a)
Should a dog be reported and found to be a public nuisance dog and be found to be in violation of this article, law enforcement officers and animal control officers are hereby empowered to lawfully enter upon any premises upon which a dog is kept or harbored.
(b)
Law enforcement officers and animal control officers may lawfully enter any premises for the purpose of impounding a public nuisance dog or making a case against an owner after the public nuisance dog has been observed in violation of any section of this article.
(Ord. No. 2014-001, § 14-42, 3-11-2014; Ord. No. 2016-007, exh. A(14-63), 4-12-2016)
Sec. 14-52. - Violations.
Any person who violates the provisions of this article shall be issued a citation for that violation to appear before the magistrate court of the county and upon conviction shall be punished as provided in section 14-53. Such a citation may be issued by a dog control officer based upon his own personal knowledge or upon a sworn written statement of another person who witnessed the violation, in which case the witness shall be subpoenaed to testify for the county in the magistrate court proceeding.
(Ord. No. 2014-001, § 14-44, 3-11-2014; Ord. No. 2016-007, exh. A(14-64), 4-12-2016)
Sec. 14-53. - Penalties for violations.
Except as provided for in section 14-54, upon the first offense of any provisions of this article, a warning will be issued to the owner by any law enforcement officer, animal control officer, or any authorized representative thereof. Any subsequent offense shall result in the issuance of a citation and may be punished by fine in an amount not to exceed $1,000.00 plus applicable court costs or by confinement in the county jail not to exceed 60 days or both such fine and confinement as authorized by O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq.
(Ord. No. 2014-001, § 14-44, 3-11-2014; Ord. No. 2016-007, exh. A(14-65), 4-12-2016)
Sec. 14-54. - Obstruction of officer.
It shall be unlawful for any person to hinder, harass, interfere, or otherwise obstruct the performance of any animal control officer in the official performance of his duties as provided for in this article. Any person convicted of a violation of this section shall be punished by a fine in an amount not to exceed $1,000.00 plus applicable court costs or by confinement in the county jail for a period not to exceed 60 days or both such fine and confinement as authorized by O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq.
(Ord. No. 2014-001, § 14-43, 3-11-2014; Ord. No. 2016-007, exh. A(14-66), 4-12-2016)
