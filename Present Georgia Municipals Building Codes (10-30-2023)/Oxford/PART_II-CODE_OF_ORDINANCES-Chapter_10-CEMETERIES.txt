ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 10-1. - Division into lots; establishing walks and roads.
The City cemetery shall be laid off into such lots, in such sizes and shapes as the Mayor and City Council deem appropriate with suitable walks and road, as needed.
(Code 1997, § 24-101)
Secs. 10-2—10-20. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - BURIAL RIGHTS

Sec. 10-21. - Eligibility to purchase burial rights.
Lots shall be for sale to qualified person or persons who are residents of the City, or those persons who are buying lots for the burial of a city resident, or previous residents, or as may be approved under certain circumstances by the Mayor and City Council.
(Code 1997, § 24-102; Ord. of 12-2005)
Sec. 10-22. - Sale of burial rights.
Burial rights in any lots or fractional part thereof shall be sold by the City to proper persons for cemetery purposes, as before set out, at prices ranging from $750.00 per grave lot (one-third or $250.00 to the City and two-thirds or $500.00 for maintenance fees to the Oxford Historical Foundation). There shall be a limit of one four-grave site parcel allowed to each family. This dictates that no single family will be allowed to purchase burial rights to more than four grave sites. Upon an appeal to the Mayor and City Council for additional grave sites, special exception to this regulation can be made when approved by vote of the majority of the Mayor and City Council.
(Code 1997, § 24-103; Ord. of 6-2-2008)
Sec. 10-23. - Conveyance of burial rights.
The City shall issue an indenture conveying the burial rights when sold which indenture shall be executed by the Mayor and the City Clerk and the affixing of the City seal thereto and attested to by the City Clerk.
(Code 1997, § 24-104; Ord. of 4-7-2008; Ord. of 6-2-2008)
Secs. 10-24—10-49. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - USE AND MAINTENANCE

Sec. 10-50. - General maintenance.
Pursuant to the contract between the City and the Oxford Historical Cemetery Foundation, Inc. and by resolution of the Mayor and City Council on December 5, 2005, the City entered into a contract with the Oxford Historical Cemetery Foundation, Inc., for the responsibility of the maintenance and upkeep of the of the Oxford Historical Cemetery.
(Code 1997, § 24-109; Ord. of 2-5-2006)
Sec. 10-51. - Erecting, cleaning or repairing cemetery fixtures.
(a)
For the protection of all lot owners, it is necessary that persons erecting, cleaning or repairing materials obtain a permit, and in doing such work comply with the directions of the City or its designate. The persons or firms requesting such permit may be required to furnish satisfactory evidence of their ability to properly perform the work proposed.
(b)
Monuments, markers and tombs must be of first quality granite, marble or other natural stone suitable for outside memorials. They must be set in accordance with the trade standard and the workmanship must be first class. A permanent marker shall be placed on the grave site within 30 days after burial.
(c)
Should any monument or tomb become unsightly, dilapidated or a menace to visitors, the City or its designate shall first notify the owner to correct. When the owner does not comply, then the City shall have the right, at the expense of the lot owner, to correct the conditions or to remove same.
(d)
All workmen employed by outside firms, while in the cemetery, are subject to reasonable regulations of the City and must leave the premises in an orderly fashion.
(e)
No freestanding statuary shall be permitted. Carving of figures in bas-relief of proper design will be acceptable.
(f)
Old flowers will be removed as necessary.
(Code 1997, § 24-107; Ord. of 6-2-2008)
Sec. 10-52. - Burial depth.
The top of each vault must be covered by at least 18 inches of soil, with the top of the site for the grave, flush with the immediate area around the grave. Urns containing cremains must be covered with 18 inches of soil. Should no urn be used for cremains, ashes must be covered with 18 inches of soil. No more than two urns or cremains shall be placed in one gravesite.
(Code 1997, § 24-108)
Sec. 10-53. - Grave opening permit required.
(a)
No person shall dig or cause to be dug any graves on any lot in the cemetery without first obtaining a permit from the City and paying the grave opening fee in the amount provided in the city fee schedule.
(b)
To procure a grave opening permit, the person or persons opening the grave or doing the internment shall, before starting any digging or placement of any stone, sepulcher or burial chamber of any nature upon the cemetery, contact the person or persons designated by the City to supervise and confirm the location of the gravesite to be used.
(c)
The City Clerk or designated party, upon request, shall provide the name, address and telephone number, to the permit holder, of the person to be contacted, as set out above.
(d)
Persons receiving the permit and the grave opening shall make known to the designated city official, the name of the owner of the gravesite, if possible, copy of deed, and with whose permission same is being used and a signed affidavit as to the owner and permission so given.
(Code 1997, § 24-106)
