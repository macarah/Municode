ARTICLE VIII. - INDIGENT DEFENSE SERVICES[9]

Footnotes:
--- (9) ---
Editor's note— Ord. of March 8, 2005 amended the Code with the addition of a new article VII, sections 2-300-2-304. In order to avoid duplication of article numbers, the provisions of said ordinance have been included herein as article VIII, sections 2-300—2-304, at the discretion of the editor.

Sec. 2-300. - Application for indigent defense services.
Each person lacking the financial ability to provide for themselves representation in his of her defense in criminal proceedings in the magistrate court of the county or the probate court of the county, and desiring that the county provide such services to him or her, shall submit an application to the county requesting indigent defense representation and setting forth the specifics supporting such person's need for such indigent defense representation. Such application shall be submitted to such employee of the respective courts as determined by the judge of each such court on a form approved by the county manager.
(Ord. of 3-8-2005; Ord. of 5-28-2013)
Sec. 2-301. - Application fee.
Each applicant for indigent defense services shall pay an application fee of $50.00 to the clerk of the court, or to such other person as may be appointed for such purpose by the judge of such respective courts in which indigent defense representation is being sought. Such fee must be paid at the time the application is submitted.
(Ord. of 3-8-2005)
Sec. 2-302. - Waiver.
The fee required by section 2-301 of this article may be waived only upon order of the presiding judge scheduled to hear the case in which the applicant is seeking a waiver of the application fee. The judge may order waiver of the fee only upon a finding by the court that the defendant is unable to pay the fee or that paying the fee would cause hardship to the defendant.
(Ord. of 3-8-2005)
Sec. 2-303. - Transfer of funds.
Within ten days after the end of each month, funds received by the respective courts as application fees paid under section 2-301 of this article shall remit the total of all application fees collected during the previous calendar month to the governing authority of the county.
(Ord. of 3-8-2005)
Sec. 2-304. - Separate account.
The funds remitted to the governing authority pursuant to section 2-303 of this article shall be maintained in a separate fund for indigent defense services and shall be expended only for the purpose of providing legal representation to indigent defendants in the magistrate court of the county and in the probate court of the county.
(Ord. of 3-8-2005)
Secs. 2-305—2-399. - Reserved.
