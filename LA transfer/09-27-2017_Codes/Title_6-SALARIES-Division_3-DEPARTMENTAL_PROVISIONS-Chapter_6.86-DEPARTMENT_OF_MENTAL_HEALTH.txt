6.86.020 - Additional information.
A.
Standby Compensation for Persons Assigned to Mental Health Mobile Response Teams.
1.
In lieu of the compensation provided for in subsection B of Section 6.08.240 and Section 6.10.120 of this code, any person designated in subsection A2 of this section who is assigned to a mobile response team shall receive a $1.00-per-hour bonus, but not to exceed a maximum of $300.00 per month, for each hour such person is assigned to regularly scheduled standby periods which occur at off-duty times.
2.
The provisions of this subsection A shall be applicable only to persons who are employed by the department in one of the following classes:

  3.
Any person employed as a Physician, M.D., Physician Specialist, M.D., Chief Physician I, M.D., or Senior Physician, M.D. who is assigned to the mental health mobile response team on a standby basis and during such assignment is ordered to return to duty shall receive, upon his or her actual return, compensation pursuant to Section 6.08.240 C of this code; provided, however, the combination of pay provided under this subsection A and the provisions of subsections B, C and D of Section 6.08.240 shall not exceed 60 percent of a physician's base monthly salary, calculated twice each month, once for the period of the first through the fifteenth of the month, and once for the period of the sixteenth through the end of the month.
4.
Assignment to such standby service requires the prior annual authorization of the Chief Administrative Officer, and payment of said bonus for standby service requires the finding of the Chief Administrative Officer that such service meets the standards set forth in this subsection A.
B.
Any person employed in a nonrepresented full-time, permanent position requiring a California license to practice as a Registered Nurse shall, upon authorization of the department head, be allowed time necessary to be absent from work, at regular pay, to a maximum of 20 hours total during one fiscal year, for the purpose of meeting mandatory continuing-education requirements. On-the-job training (or in-house offerings) accredited for meeting applicable state relicensure or recertification requirements shall count toward meeting the county obligation of 20 hours. Further, any person employed in a nonrepresented part-time permanent position requiring a California license to practice as a Registered Nurse, who works at least 20 hours per week on a continuing basis, shall upon authorization of the department head, be allowed time necessary to be absent from work at regular pay to a maximum of 10 hours during the fiscal year. Where the department requires certification beyond 20 hours (10 hours in the case of permanent nonrepresented part-time employees), additional continuing education hours will be granted.
