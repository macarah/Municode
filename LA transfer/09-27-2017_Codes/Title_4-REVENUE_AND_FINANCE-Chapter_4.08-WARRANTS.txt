Chapter 4.08 - WARRANTS
Sections:

4.08.010 - Voiding Uncashed Warrants.
Any warrant issued is void if not presented to the County Treasurer and Tax Collector for payment within six months after its date.
(Ord. 2017-0023 § 1, 2017: Ord. 90-0165 § 1, 1990; Ord. 82-0192 § 1, 1982; Ord. 11613 § 1, 1977: Ord. 9886 § 1, 1969: Ord. 4099 Art. 6-B § 99.14, 1942.)
4.08.020 - Lost warrants.
A warrant is considered lost if it has been mailed and has not been received by the addressee within seven days after the date of mailing. This section is added pursuant to Section 29853 of the Government Code and applies to all county warrants, except those for public assistance.
(Ord. 86-0008 § 2, 1986: Ord. 82-0192 § 2, 1982; Ord. 11055 § 2 (part), 1975: Ord. 8502 § 1, 1963: Ord. 4099 Art. 6-B § 99.11.2, 1942.)
4.08.025 - Public assistance warrants.
A.
A public assistance warrant is considered lost if it has been mailed and has not been received by the addressee after five working days from the date the warrant was mailed.
B.
A public assistance warrant is considered lost immediately if the warrant was lost by the addressee after receipt of it.
(Ord. 86-0008 § 2, 1986.)
4.08.030 - Replacement of Warrants—Time.
At any time within five years from the date on which the original warrant was issued, the payee or assignee may declare by affidavit that such warrant was not received, or has been lost or destroyed. The auditor-controller is authorized to draw a replacement warrant in any case where the Auditor-Controller possesses records, which independently verify that the warrant has not been previously replaced and it would be inequitable or unreasonable not to draw the replacement warrant, and money is available in the County budget to make such payment on the indebtedness.
(Ord. 2017-0023 § 2, 2017: Ord. 2002-0016 § 1, 2002: Ord. 86-0009 § 1, 1986: Ord. 82-0192 § 3, 1982: Ord. 12081 § 1, 1980.)
