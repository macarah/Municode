Chapter 5.72 - CLOTHING
Sections:

5.72.010 - Suitable clothing to be worn.
All employees are required to wear clothing suitable to their occupations as may be determined by their respective department heads. Employees shall furnish and maintain in suitable and appropriate condition such clothing and associated articles at their own expense except as otherwise expressly provided for in Ordinance 4099.
(Ord. 88-0184 § 3, 1988: Ord. 7981 § 6, 1961: Ord. 6226 § 3 (part), 1953: Ord. 4099 Art. 3-C § 93.50, 1942.)
5.72.020 - Clothing furnished by county—Illegal use prohibited.
Each department head shall be held personally responsible on his bond for all clothing issued to employees of his department in accordance with provisions of Ordinance 4099 when it has been found that such clothing has been used contrary to the provisions and the purposes as stated in Ordinance 4099.
(Ord. 6226 § 3 (part), 1953: Ord. 4099 Art. 3-C § 93.79, 1942.)
5.72.030 - Personal safety devices and protective apparel.
A.
Employees shall be furnished personal safety devices or articles of protective apparel, or both, at county expense, when such apparel or devices are necessary to protect the health and safety of the employee at the work site. Requests for such apparel or devices must be approved by the director of personnel. Such personal safety devices and protective apparel shall be used or worn only when necessary in the course of county business and shall remain, at all times, the property of the county.
B.
Neither "personal safety devices" nor "articles of protective apparel," as used in this section, includes articles normally worn as everyday clothing.
C.
This section does not supersede or modify any other provision of Ordinance 4099 or any other ordinance or department rule which requires employees at their own expense to furnish their own clothing, uniforms, or associated articles.
(Ord. 82-0264 § 1, 1982; Ord. 9246 § 7 (part), 1967: Ord. 8512 § 8, 1963: Ord. 7917 § 1, 1961: Ord. 6226 § 3(part), 1953: Ord. 4099 Art. 3-C § 93.50.1, 1942.)
5.72.040 - Uniforms—Issuance conditions generally.
Employees now or hereinafter employed in classifications specifically authorized by the board shall have issued to them on a one-time-only basis certain uniforms and equipment items required by their department head and approved by the board. Additionally, if specifically authorized by the board, such uniforms and equipment may be issued on a recurring basis or may be maintained by the county. The director of personnel shall periodically list such authorized classifications and approved items of uniform and equipment. When persons who have previously had issued to them such items pursuant to this section cease to be employed in a classification for which such uniforms and equipment are authorized, they may be required to return all or part of such uniforms and equipment.
(Ord. 10275 § 1, 1971: Ord. 10094 § 1, 1970: Ord. 6226 § 3 (part), 1953: Ord. 4099 Art. 3-C § 93.50.3, 1942.)
5.72.050 - Uniforms—Deputy sheriffs, security officers, and firefighters.
Persons employed in the items listed below shall be entitled to have issued to them certain items of initial uniform and equipment. Such issue shall be restricted to the initial equipping of newly hired persons and shall remain the property of the county until such a time as normal wear makes them valueless to the county. The chief executive officer shall annually list those items to be issued and the period of time that they must be used before being presumed to be valueless to the county.

5.72.070 - Uniforms—Custodians.
Persons employed in custodian and window-washing positions shall be entitled to have issued to them uniforms consisting of shirts and slacks. Such uniforms shall be rented or purchased by the county, and shall be laundered at no expense to the employee. Such uniforms shall be returned to the department upon completion of active duty for the county, and at such times as may be required by the department head.
