ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 22-1. - Burials.
It shall be unlawful for any undertaker or any other person to bury or cause to be buried, or to in any manner aid or assist in the burial of the dead body of any human being in any cemetery or location in the unincorporated area of the county other than an authorized public cemetery or a private cemetery established and approved pursuant to this chapter.
(Ord. No. 2002-03, § VI(1), 2-19-2002)
Secs. 22-2—22-30. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - PRIVATE CEMETERIES
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 22-31. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Private cemetery means a cemetery located on privately owned property which is intended to be used for the burial of human remains by members of an identifiable class of persons that can be reasonably distinguished from the general public or citizenry, and which may not be used for the burial of members of the general public or citizenry.
(Ord. No. 2002-03, § II, 2-19-2002)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-4.
Sec. 22-32. - Authority of building official.
The building official shall be responsible for issuing permits and ensuring that the requirements of this article are satisfied. Other than maintaining in his office an original of the plat of survey of each private cemetery and maintaining an inventory of private cemeteries in the county, the building official and county shall have no responsibility for or duty to supervise the operation of any such private cemetery.
(Ord. No. 2002-03, § IV, 2-19-2002)
Sec. 22-33. - Records.
The building official shall maintain a record and inventory of the locations of all private cemeteries established and permitted pursuant to this article.
(Ord. No. 2002-03, § IV, 2-19-2002)
Sec. 22-34. - Enforcement officers.
All law enforcement agencies, officers, certified peace officers, and/or officials of the state, or any county code enforcement officer, are hereby authorized, empowered, and directed to enforce compliance with this article.
(Ord. No. 2002-03, § VIII, 2-19-2002)
Sec. 22-35. - Penalties for violation of article.
Violators of the provisions of this article shall be subject to the penalties set forth in section 1-19 of this Code.
(Ord. No. 2002-03, § VII, 2-19-2002)
Sec. 22-36. - Operation.
It shall be the sole responsibility and duty of the owner, creator, trust or other legal entity which shall own, supervise or have control of a private cemetery to ensure that the same shall not at any time constitute a health hazard or be or constitute a nuisance. The owner, creator, trust or other legal entity which shall own, supervise or have control of a private cemetery shall have the right and responsibility of creating rules and regulations necessary to or appropriate for the operation of such private cemetery.
(Ord. No. 2002-03, § V, 2-19-2002)
Sec. 22-37. - Marketing and advertising.
No private cemetery established and approved pursuant to this article shall market, advertise or sell lots or burial spaces to the general public.
(Ord. No. 2002-03, § VI(2), 2-19-2002)
Sec. 22-38. - Use.
All private cemeteries created pursuant to this article shall be and remain purely private, and all lots and spaces shall be reserved for relatives, friends, neighbors or other members of an identifiable class of persons that can be reasonably distinguished from the general public or citizenry.
(Ord. No. 2002-03, § VI(2), 2-19-2002)
Sec. 22-39. - Creation of nuisances.
No owner, creator, trust or other legal entity which shall own, supervise or have control of a private cemetery shall allow such private cemetery to accumulate weeds, trash, junk, filth, or other unsanitary or unsafe conditions so as to create a public health hazard or a general nuisance to those persons residing in the vicinity.
(Ord. No. 2002-03, § VI(3), 2-19-2002)
Secs. 22-40—22-60. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PERMIT

Sec. 22-61. - Required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate a private cemetery in the unincorporated area of the county unless done so in compliance with the provisions of this chapter.
(Ord. No. 2002-03, § III(intro. ¶), 2-19-2002)
Sec. 22-62. - Survey required.
A plat of survey of the private cemetery prepared by a state-registered land surveyor shall be submitted to the building official along with the application. Upon approval of the survey by the building official, the applicant shall file, or cause to have filed, two signed original copies of the survey with the superior court clerk for recording.
(Ord. No. 2002-03, § III(1), 2-19-2002)
Sec. 22-63. - Minimum area required.
No private cemetery shall be approved unless the survey provided for in this division indicates that the private cemetery contains not less than one acre, the entire area of which shall be dedicated to and used only for private cemetery purposes.
(Ord. No. 2002-03, § III(2), 2-19-2002)
Sec. 22-64. - Land use compatibility.
The location of the proposed private cemetery is to be compatible with adjacent land uses, existing or proposed highways and any other elements or factors deemed by the county to affect the public health, safety and welfare of the inhabitants of the area surrounding the proposed location.
(Ord. No. 2002-03, § III(3), 2-19-2002)
Sec. 22-65. - Buffer area.
The proposed location must contain a buffer area that is suitably landscaped with trees and/or shrubbery on all sides of the site that is identified on the survey and is not less than ten feet in width.
(Ord. No. 2002-03, § III(4), 2-19-2002)
Sec. 22-66. - Flood prevention.
No private cemetery shall lie in whole or in part within a floodplain and certification of the same must be provided upon the survey.
(Ord. No. 2002-03, § III(5), 2-19-2002)
Cross reference— Floods, ch. 38.
Sec. 22-67. - Identification of boundaries.
The boundaries of the proposed private cemetery must be clearly marked and delineated.
(Ord. No. 2002-03, § III(6), 2-19-2002)
Sec. 22-68. - Ingress and egress.
Adequate access, ingress and egress to and from the private cemetery must be available by public road or private drive.
(Ord. No. 2002-03, § III(7), 2-19-2002)
Sec. 22-69. - Health department approval.
The approval of the proposed private cemetery must be obtained from the health department and submitted with the application.
(Ord. No. 2002-03, § III(8), 2-19-2002)
Sec. 22-70. - Appeal from denial.
Appeals from the denial of a permit authorized by this division shall be to the board of commissioners.
(Ord. No. 2002-03, § IV, 2-19-2002)
