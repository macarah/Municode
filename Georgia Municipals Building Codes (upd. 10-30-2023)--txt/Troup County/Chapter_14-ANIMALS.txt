Sec. 14-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Abandoned animals means any domesticated animal which shall have been placed upon public property or within a public building unattended or uncared for, or upon or within the private property of another without the express permission of the owner, custodian or tenant of such property unattended or uncared for. Any domesticated animal shall also be considered abandoned which shall have been upon or within the property of the owner or custodian of such animal unattended and without food and water for any period of time sufficient to create a danger to the health of such animal.
Aggressive dog or animal, vicious dog or animal, or potentially aggressive or vicious dog or animal means any dog or other animal that:
(1)
Has caused physical injury to humans or domestic animals without provocation, whether on public or private property;
(2)
Because of temperament, conditioning, or training, has the propensity, tendency or disposition to attack, bite, or injure humans or domestic animals without provocation;
(3)
Aggressively bites, attacks, or endangers the safety of humans without provocation after it has been classified as a potentially dangerous animal and after the owner has been notified of such classification; or
(4)
Causes reasonable fear of bodily injury to any person by attacking or threatening to attack such person.
(5)
For purposes of this definition and article, an animal which is defending itself or its owner or keeper from attack shall be deemed to have been "provoked."
Animal means any domesticated or wild creature, including fowl, fish and reptiles.
Animal at large means any animal not under restraint and off the property of its owner.
Animal control department means the department established by the board of commissioners charged with enforcing the provisions of this article.
Animal under restraint means an animal controlled by a leash or lead; an animal while off the property of the owner at "heel" beside a competent person and obedient to that person's commands; an animal while on the property of the owner in the presence of a competent person and obedient to that person's commands; an animal confined on or within a vehicle being driven or parked on the streets; an animal in a building or fenced area within the property limits of its owner or keeper.
Animal shelter means any premises designated by the board of commissioners for the purpose of impounding and caring for animals found in violation of this article and for unwanted animals.
Cage means a facility designed to incarcerate a specific species of animal within a limited area.
Domesticated animal means any animal, bird, or reptile, of any species, which is owned, kept, harbored or maintained as a household pet, for food production, or for other purposes.
Fenced area means any area enclosed by a fence of such height and strength as to reasonably prevent an animal from leaving such area.
Kennel pet shop means any building or area used for the keeping, breeding, buying, selling, exhibiting and/or boarding of wild or domesticated animals commercially.
Nuisance means any animal which:
(1)
Is an abandoned or stray animal;
(2)
Is a female dog or cat running at large when in heat;
(3)
Is an unlicensed and untagged dog or cat;
(4)
Engages without provocation in incessant or untimely barking or other noise making in such a manner as to reasonably disturb any person or neighborhood. For purposes of this definition and article, an animal which barks as the result of the intentional and unauthorized actions of persons other than the owner or custodian of the animal shall be deemed to have been "provoked;"
(5)
Destroys property belonging to others;
(6)
Habitually chases passing persons or vehicles;
(7)
Is a vicious animal; or
(8)
Causes unsanitary conditions.
Owner means any person owning, keeping, selling or harboring any animal.
Permit means an application form provided by the health department which must be completed and approved by the health department for the purpose of obtaining a county business license to commercially keep, breed, buy, sell, exhibit or board animals.
Shelter means any type of sound structure which provides cover and protection from the wind, rain, heat and cold.
(Ord. No. 2007-04, § I(Exh. A), 11-17-2006; Ord. No. 2010-01 , § I, 7-7-2009; Ord. No. 2011-04 , §§ I, II, 5-17-2011)
Cross reference— Definitions generally, § 1-4.
Sec. 14-2. - Animal control department.
(a)
Created; staffing. There is hereby created the county animal control department which shall have primary responsibility for animal control in the unincorporated areas of the county. Such department shall be staffed by such employees as may be deemed feasible and appropriate from time to time by the board of commissioners, taking into account the need for such employees and available budgetary resources.
(b)
Responsibility and duties. The animal control department shall have the following responsibilities and duties:
(1)
Be responsible for the operation of any animal control shelter which may be established by the board of commissioners and the performance of such duties and the keeping of such records related to animal control and welfare as the board of commissioners may require.
(2)
Cooperate with the health department in the enforcement of rabies control regulations and animal control directives.
(3)
Cooperate with the county humane society.
(4)
Pick up and impound animals which are in violation of this article.
(5)
Prosecute in the county magistrate court, as well as any appeals therefrom, any violation of this article.
(Ord. No. 2007-04, § I(Exh. A), 11-17-2006; Ord. No. 2010-01 , § II, 7-7-2009)
Sec. 14-3. - Animal shelter.
The board of commissioners may construct or otherwise create or designate an animal shelter for the purpose of caring for and disposing of animals impounded by the animal control department in accordance with this article and for receiving unwanted animals. The board of commissioners may also contract with any person, including, any other governmental entity or agency, for the creation or operation of such an animal shelter.
(Ord. No. 2007-04, § I(Exh. A), 11-17-2006)
Sec. 14-4. - Rabies inoculation.
(a)
No person shall own, keep or harbor a dog or cat within the unincorporated limits of the county unless such animal has been vaccinated against rabies in accordance with the rules and regulations of the state department of human resources and the county department of health.
(b)
The owner or custodian of such dog or cat shall retain in such person's possession a certificate of vaccination or other written proof that such animal has been given a current vaccination.
(c)
The owner or custodian of any such dog shall also be responsible for obtaining a numbered vaccination tag furnished or approved by the state department of human resources at the time of such vaccination, which shall be affixed to a collar worn by the dog at all times the dog is off the premises of the owner. Cats and dogs while the same are on the premises of the owner, are not required to wear a rabies tag, but the owner shall have written proof of current vaccination.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person to attach a vaccination tag to the collar of any animal for which it was not issued, or to remove a vaccination tag from any animal without the consent of the owner or custodian.
(Ord. No. 2007-04, § I(Exh. A), 11-17-2006; Ord. No. 2011-04 , § III, 5-17-2011)
State Law reference— Rabies control, O.C.G.A. § 31-19-1 et seq.
Sec. 14-5. - Report of animal bites; confinement of animal.
Any person bitten by or discovering or reasonably suspecting any domestic animal to be suffering with rabies shall immediately report such fact to the department of health, giving the name and address of the owner or custodian of such dog. It shall be the duty of the owner or custodian of such animal to confine such animal in accordance with the rules and regulations of the state department of human resources and the county department of health.
(Ord. No. 2007-04, § I(Exh. A), 11-17-2006)
Sec. 14-6. - Impoundment.
(a)
Causes. The animal control department shall be authorized to take possession of and impound any of the following:
(1)
Any dog running at large or free from restraint without a current vaccination tag.
(2)
Any wild dog.
(3)
Any abandoned animal or domestic animal the ownership of which is unknown.
(4)
Any animal having rabies or reasonably suspected of having rabies if the owner or custodian of such animal refuses or fails to confine such animal as required by applicable rules, regulations or laws.
(5)
Any domestic animal in heat running at large or free from restraint other than within the limits of the property of the owner or custodian of such dog.
(6)
Any domestic animal which is running at large or free from restraint other than within the limits of the property of the owner of such animal.
(7)
Any animal in which the condition or the treatment of the animal is causing unnecessary pain and suffering.
(b)
Disposition of animals. Any dogs or other animals impounded by the animal control department pursuant to this article may be disposed of in a humane manner as detailed in the following subsections:
(1)
Animals delivered to the animal shelter by their owners residing within the limits of the unincorporated areas of the county shall be made available for adoption immediately. If not adopted, the animals shall be euthanized at the end of a ten-day (240-hour) period.
(2)
Animals delivered to the animal shelter by persons other than their owners residing within the limits of the unincorporated areas of the county are to be retained for a period of four days (96 hours) as a redeeming period and an additional six days (144 hours) as an adoption period. The first four days (96 hours) shall be sufficient time for owners to redeem an animal. The six additional days (144 hours) shall be a period for adoption.
(3)
The euthanizing of sick, injured or vicious animals shall be at the discretion of the director of the animal shelter. The date and time that the sick, injured or vicious animal is euthanized shall be at the discretion of the animal control department or the person in charge of the shelter.
(c)
Redemption. The owner or lawful custodian shall be entitled to claim and obtain possession of any impounded dog or other animal at any time before the expiration of such period upon compliance with the vaccination requirements of this article and payment of all applicable fees. Any animal impounded under this section may be reclaimed as provided in this section upon the payment by the owner or the custodian of any fee which may be imposed, from time to time, by the board of commissioners by resolution. In addition, the owner or lawful custodian shall pay the cost of any required vaccinations.
(Ord. No. 2007-04, § I(Exh. A), 11-17-2006)
Sec. 14-7. - Animal roaming free.
It shall be unlawful for any owner to:
(1)
Fail to keep their animal from running at large or under restraint;
(2)
Fail to confine their animal having rabies or reasonably suspected of having rabies.
(Ord. No. 2007-04, § I(Exh. A), 11-17-2006)
Sec. 14-8. - Nuisance animals.
In addition and supplementary to any other requirements and prohibitions of this article or this Code, it shall be unlawful and deemed a public nuisance and separate violation for any owner to:
(1)
Fail to keep their female dog or cat from running at large or under restraint when in heat.
(2)
Fail to keep their dog from engaging in incessant or untimely barking or other noise making in such a manner as to reasonably disturb any person or neighborhood. It shall not be a violation of this subpart if the owner or custodian of the dog shows by clear and convincing evidence that the incessant or untimely barking complained of was caused or substantially contributed to by the intentional acts of third parties.
(3)
Fail to keep their animal from destroying property belonging to others.
(4)
Fail to keep their animal from habitually chasing passing persons or vehicles.
(5)
Fail to keep their animal from causing unsanitary conditions.
(6)
Abandon any domesticated animal within unincorporated Troup County, Georgia.
(7)
Fail to prevent their animal from becoming a nuisance animal as defined in section 14-1 of this article.
(Ord. No. 2007-04, § I(Exh. A), 11-17-2006; Ord. No. 2010-01 , § III, 7-7-2009; Ord. No. 2011-04 , § IV, 5-17-2011)
Sec. 14-9. - Aggressive, vicious, or potentially aggressive or vicious dogs.
(a)
No person owning or having custody or control of any dog or other animal known by such person to be aggressive, vicious or potentially aggressive or vicious shall permit it to run at large, or permit it to run loose on or within the premises of such person in such a manner as to endanger the life or limb of any person lawfully entering such premises.
(b)
It shall be the duty of every owner of any aggressive, vicious or potentially aggressive or vicious animal, or anyone having any such animal in his possession or custody, to ensure that such animal is kept under restraint, as prescribed in subsections (d), (e) and (f) of this section and that reasonable care and precautions are taken to prevent the animal from leaving, while unattended, the real property limits of its owner, custodian, or harborer, and it is securely and humanely enclosed within a house, building, fence, locked pen, or other enclosure out of which it cannot climb, dig, jump, or otherwise escape on its own volition. Such enclosure must be securely locked at any time the animal is left unattended so that children are prevented from entry and to prevent the animal from escaping.
(c)
For owners of an aggressive, vicious or potentially aggressive or vicious animal which lives out-of-doors, a portion of their property should be fenced with a perimeter or area fence. Within this perimeter fence, the animal must be humanely confined inside a locked pen or kennel of adequate size. The kennel or pen must have secure sides that set securely into the ground or onto a concrete pad. The gate to the kennel must be locked when the animal is unattended. This enclosure shall provide protection from the elements.
(d)
An aggressive, vicious or potentially aggressive or vicious animal shall not be upon any street or public place except when securely restrained by a leash not more than six feet in length and humanely muzzled and in the charge of a competent person.
(e)
Whenever outside of its enclosure, as provided for in subsections (b) and (c) of this section, but on the owner's property, an aggressive, vicious or potentially aggressive or vicious animal must be attended by the owner or custodian and restrained by a secure collar, muzzled when appropriate, as determined by the animal control officer, and on a leash of sufficient strength to prevent escape.
(f)
No aggressive, vicious or potentially aggressive or vicious animal shall be chained, tethered, or otherwise tied while unattended by the owner or custodian to any inanimate object such as a tree, post, or building outside of its primary enclosure.
(g)
A warning sign (e.g., beware of dog) shall be conspicuously posted denoting an aggressive, vicious or potentially aggressive animal on the premises.
(h)
Failure to keep any aggressive, vicious or potentially aggressive or vicious animal confined or under restraint as provided for in this section shall be unlawful and shall be punishable as provided in this article.
(i)
In the event any person is cited for and convicted of a violation of this section, the animal shall be registered with the Animal Control Department. The annual fee for the registration shall be $500.00. The animal shall be registered annually so long as it is located in unincorporated Troup County. At the time of registration and annually thereafter when the registration is renewed, an animal control officer shall require evidence from the owner and make investigation as may be necessary to verify that the animal is confined as provided in this section and that the owner, keeper, responsible person or custodian is in compliance with the provisions of this section.
(j)
In the event any person is cited for and convicted of a violation of this section and the animal is further determined by the Magistrate Court to be of a breed, size, predisposition, temperament or nature so as to be capable of causing serious and permanent physical injury, the Magistrate Court shall be authorized to order the person owning or controlling or in possession of the animal to maintain as an additional registration requirement either (1) a policy of insurance in the amount of at least $50,000.00 issued by an insurer authorized to transact business in the state insuring the owner of the animal against liability for personal injuries inflicted by the animal; or (2) a surety bond in the amount of $50,000.00 or more issued by a surety company authorized to transact business in this state and payable to any person injured by the animal.
(k)
Violation of the registration requirements of this section shall be punishable as provided in chapter 1, section 1-19 of the Code of Troup County, Georgia, and each day any such violation shall continue shall constitute a separate offense. In addition, violation of the registration requirements of this section shall subject the unregistered animal(s) to seizure and impoundment by the Animal Control Department. Any animal so impounded shall be retained for ten days, during which time the owner may reclaim the animal upon proof of compliance with this section, proof of vaccination requirements of this article, and payment of the redemption fee and/or cost of any required vaccinations as provided in subsection 14-6(c) of this article. If the owner of the impounded animal is known, such owner shall be notified of the impoundment. Should the owner fail to reclaim the animal within ten days from impoundment, the animal shall be euthanized.
(Ord. No. 2007-04, § I(Exh. A), 11-17-2006; Ord. No. 2011-04 , § V, 5-17-2011)

Sec. 14-10. - Restraint of animals.
(a)
The following definitions shall apply in the interpretation and enforcement of this code section, unless otherwise specifically stated:
Leash or lead means a rope, chain, cord, strap or webbing, cable or similar device that is attached to an animal's collar or harness for purposes of restraining the animal and is held by or attached to a competent person.
Swivel means pivoting hardware that can be used in a trolley system to attach a cable run to a tether or a tether to an animal's collar or harness in order to minimize twisting and tangling of the tether.
Tether means a rope, chain, cord, strap or webbing, cable or similar device that is attached to an animal's collar or harness for purposes of restraining the animal.
Trolley system means a method of restraining an animal which utilizes a cable run, swivel and tether attached to an animal's collar or harness.
(b)
No animal may be restrained by tether in such a manner as to subject it to unreasonable pain, stress or risk of tangling and choking.
(c)
The only manner that any dog may be physically restrained outdoors, other than by leash or lead or fenced area, shall be by a trolley system in which the tether:
(1)
Is attached to a properly fitting collar, which may not be a choke collar, pinch collar, prong collar or similar device, or harness worn by the dog; and
(2)
Is ten feet in length or five times the length of the dog, measured from the tip of the dog's nose to the base of its tail, whichever is greater.
( Ord. No. 2018-11 , § I, 11-21-2017)
Editor's note— Ord. No. 2018-11 , § I, adopted Nov. 21, 2017, repealed § 14-10 in its entirety and enacted new provisions to read as herein set out. Former § 14-10 pertained to tethering of animals, and derived from Ord. No. 2007-04, § I(Exh. A), adopted Nov. 17, 2006.
Sec. 14-11. - Humane care of animals.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to overload, maim, tease, torture, abuse or kill any animal.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person to deprive any animal of shelter which shall protect the animal from the elements.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to deprive any animal of necessary medical attention when the animal is in a condition in which failure to provide medical attention would result in death or unreasonable pain or suffering.
(d)
It shall be unlawful for any person to deprive any animal of sufficient food and water as often as the feeding habits of such animal require to prevent dehydration, starvation or a significant risk to the animal's health from lack of food or water.
(e)
It shall be unlawful for any person to put out or cause to be put out any poison within the county for the purpose of harming any domesticated animal.
(f)
It shall be unlawful for any person to release or leave any animal on any private or public property with the intention of abandoning such animal.
(Ord. No. 2007-04, § I(Exh. A), 11-17-2006)
Sec. 14-12. - Penalties for violation.
Violations of this article shall be punishable by and subject to the penalties as provided in Chapter 1, Section 1-19 of the Code of Troup County, Georgia.
(Ord. No. 2007-04, § I(Exh. A), 11-17-2006)

Sec. 14-41. - Jurisdiction of article provisions.
This article shall apply to the unincorporated area of the county.
(Code 1987, § 6-20)
Sec. 14-42. - Interpretation of article provisions.
This article is not intended to annul any state or federal law, or other agreement between parties. However, where this article imposes a greater restriction upon the owner or permittee, the provisions of this article shall govern.
(Code 1987, § 6-21)
Sec. 14-43. - Permit.
(a)
Required. No person shall possess, buy, sell or display, commercially, any animal without a permit therefor from the county.
(b)
Exemptions. The occasional buying or selling of horses, cattle, sheep, hogs, and other domesticated animals (including dogs and cats) as an accessory to a normal farm operation shall be exempt from the provisions of this article requiring a permit.
(c)
Information furnished with application. The applicant, when applying for a permit, shall furnish the health department with a list of the kinds of animals to be kept, handled or exhibited, with the estimated maximum number included at any one time.
(Code 1987, §§ 6-22—6-24)
Sec. 14-44. - Care of animals.
In all establishments operated under a permit under this article, the following rules shall apply:
(1)
All animals must be supplied with sufficient, good and wholesome food and water as often as the feeding habits of such animals require.
(2)
All animals and animal quarters shall be kept in a clean and sanitary condition. Adequate ventilation shall be maintained.
(3)
Proper shelter and protection from the weather shall be provided and maintained for all animals at all times. Animals must not be overcrowded, nor exposed to excessive heat or cold. Quarters shall be draft free. Proper temperature for the well-being of the animals shall be maintained at all times.
(4)
Animals shall not be without care and attention by the permittee.
(5)
The permittee shall use every reasonable precaution to ensure that animals are not teased, abused, mistreated, tormented or in any manner made to suffer by any person or by any means.
(6)
The permittee shall not maintain, nor permit to exist, any condition that is or could be, injurious to the animals.
(7)
Animals which are worked, kept for sale or exhibited shall be healthy and in good condition of flesh.
(8)
Animals which are enemies by nature, of other animals, or are temperamentally unsuited, shall not be quartered with such other animals, nor so near thereto as to cause fear in any such animals, or to cause any animal to be abused or tormented.
(9)
Animals shall be maintained in quarters so constructed as to prevent their escape. The permittee assumes full responsibility for recapturing any animal that escapes from such person's premises. The permittee shall take all reasonable precautions to protect the public from the animals and the animals from the public.
(10)
Working animals shall be given proper rest periods.
(11)
Animal bedding shall be sufficient in size and quantity, and good quality and kept clean.
(12)
Sick or diseased animals shall be isolated from healthy animals at all times, and so segregated that the illness or disease shall not be transmitted to another animal.
(13)
The permittee shall maintain a register of the names and addresses of persons from whom animals are received and to whom animals are sold, traded, or given.
(14)
The permittee shall furnish to the marshal's office and to the health department the names, addresses and telephone numbers of a responsible person who has access to applicant's place of business and can be reached in an emergency at any time outside of business hours.
(15)
Animals in cages or behind windows or in any other location shall have protection from direct sunlight and excessive heat. Shaded areas shall be provided for all animals.
(16)
The permittee shall operate the business so as to eliminate excessive or untimely noise and offensive odors from animals.
(17)
The permittee shall notify the owners of boarded animals when such animals refuse to eat or drink, causing them to lose flesh, or when such animals are injured, or become weak or ill.
(Code 1987, § 6-25; Ord. No. 2010-01 , § V, 7-7-2009)
Sec. 14-45. - Pet shops.
The following provisions shall apply to pet shops:
(1)
No sick or injured animal shall be maintained on the premises, sold, or offered for sale.
(2)
Every effort shall be made to ensure that unweaned or weak animals are not sold, offered for sale, nor given away.
(3)
Every person maintaining a pet shop shall display in a location that is visible and legible from outside such building a notice of the names, addresses and telephone numbers of responsible persons who have access to applicant's place of business and can be reached in an emergency at any time outside of business hours.
(4)
The owner of the pet shop shall register a name for emergency contact with E 911.
(Code 1987, § 6-26)
Sec. 14-46. - Caged birds.
The permittee shall display and keep birds in cages under the following conditions:
(1)
Each bird confined in a cage shall have room to exercise freely and shall have perch space without excessive crowding.
(2)
Sick, injured and dead birds shall be removed from cages.
(Code 1987, § 6-27)
Sec. 14-47. - Fish.
The permittee shall display and keep fish in bowls or aquariums with adequate amounts of water to keep fish in a healthy condition.
(Code 1987, § 6-28)
Sec. 14-48. - Animal acts; pet shows; dog and cat shows and exhibitions.
The following provisions shall apply to animal acts, pet shows, cat shows, and exhibitions:
(1)
The permittee shall not display any animal whose condition is contrary to public decency.
(2)
No animal shall be exhibited under conditions that may be cruel or injurious to the animal.
(3)
No animal bearing evidence of malnutrition, ill health, or having been kept in an unsanitary condition, shall be exhibited.
(Code 1987, § 6-29)
Sec. 14-49. - Animal rental, stables, markets and similar pursuits.
The following provisions shall apply to animal rental, riding academies, livery stables, boarding stables, pony rides, horse markets, mule markets, and other such pursuits:
(1)
No animal shall be worked, used or rented when in an overheated, weakened, exhausted or crippled condition, or if diseased, ill or otherwise unfit for duty.
(2)
Animals shall be healthy, strong, and in good flesh.
(Code 1987, § 6-30)
Sec. 14-50. - Keeping wild animals.
The following provisions shall apply to the keeping of wild animals:
(1)
The permittee shall assume full responsibility for safekeeping and for recapturing any animal that escapes from such permittee's premises.
(2)
The permittee shall make adequate provisions and safeguards for the protection of the public.
(3)
The permittee who applies for a permit to keep and display wild animals for educational purposes shall have available printed information concerning the animal displayed, its habits, etc., for distribution or conduct lectures concerning the animal displayed, or have adequate and explanatory signs carrying information prominently displayed near the exhibit.
(Code 1987, § 6-31; Ord. No. 2010-01 , § VI, 7-7-2009)
Sec. 14-51. - Circuses.
The following provisions shall apply to circuses:
(1)
All animals exhibited must be strong, healthy, and in good flesh. No sick, injured, or crippled animal may be exhibited or worked.
(2)
All wild or dangerous animals must be so quartered and reasonable precautions taken to protect the public from such animals and the animals from the public.
(3)
The permittee assumes full responsibility for safekeeping and for recapturing any animal that escapes.
(Code 1987, § 6-32)
Sec. 14-52. - Rodeos.
The following provisions shall apply to rodeos:
(1)
The applicant, when applying for a permit, shall furnish the animal control department with a detailed diagram of the area, showing location of chutes, catch chutes, and pens, and a list of all proposed events and acts and a copy of the rules and regulations which will govern the conduct of the contestants and events. All of such information will be reviewed by the zoning appeals/planning board of the county before a permit is issued.
(2)
Any tack, equipment, device, substance or material that is or could be injurious or cause unnecessary cruelty is prohibited.
(3)
No animal which is unfit to perform the work intended may be used.
(4)
Chutes must be so constructed as to prevent injury to stock. The arena must be free of rocks, holes and all obstacles.
(Code 1987, § 6-33)
