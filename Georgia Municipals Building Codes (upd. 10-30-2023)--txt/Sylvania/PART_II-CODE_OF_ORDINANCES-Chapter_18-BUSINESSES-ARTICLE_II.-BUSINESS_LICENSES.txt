ARTICLE II. - BUSINESS LICENSES[2]

Sec. 18-26. - Application.
On or before January 1 of each year, every person who shall conduct or carry on any business, trade, profession or vocation within the corporate limits of the city must first, before engaging in this business, trade, profession or vocation, apply to the city clerk for a license and at the same time render to the city clerk the applicable license fee as hereinafter designated in this article. Each applicant for a city license shall disclose to the city clerk the type of business, trade, profession or vocation to be conducted; the locations thereof if more than one; the name and style thereof, and such other pertinent information as shall be necessary to carry out and enforce the provisions of this article.
(Code 1976, § 6-1001)
Sec. 18-27. - Issuance.
Upon receipt of the application of any person for a license to carry on any lawful business, trade, profession or vocation, not inconsistent with the laws of the United States, this state or the lawful ordinances of the city, and not contrary to or detrimental to the public health, morals and welfare of the citizens of the city, and when the application shall be accompanied by the prescribed fee and pertinent information required by this article, it shall be the duty of the city clerk to issue to this person a license for the conduct of the particular business, trade, profession or vocation to be carried on.
(Code 1976, § 6-1002)
Sec. 18-28. - Valid period.
All licenses issued by the city clerk under the provisions of this article shall be valid from January 1 of the fiscal year when purchased and shall expire on December 31 of the same year.
(Code 1976, § 6-1003)
Sec. 18-29. - Display of licenses.
All licenses issued under the provisions of this article must be prominently displayed in the place of business or office of the person to whom issued.
(Code 1976, § 6-1005)
Sec. 18-30. - Businesses having more than one location; multiple businesses at one location.
All persons engaging in or carrying on any business, trade, profession or vocation at two or more disjoined locations shall be required to pay the applicable license fee, as hereinafter designated for each of the business locations or places. Any person who shall conduct more than one type of business, trade, profession or vocation at the same location or place of business shall be required to pay the applicable license fee for each business, trade, profession or vocation being conducted.
(Code 1976, § 6-1006)
Sec. 18-31. - Suspension; revocation.
(a)
All licenses issued by the city clerk under the provisions of this article shall be subject to suspension by the mayor or revocation by the mayor and council upon the following terms and in the following manner:
Upon information being furnished by any person that the holder of any license issued under the provisions of this article has, in connection with the business, trade, profession or vocation for which this license was issued, committed acts or is guilty of conduct contrary to the laws of the United States, the state, the city or is guilty of conduct contrary to the public health, morals, peace or welfare of the citizens of the city, it shall be the duty of the mayor to issue or cause to be issued a written notice, directed to the holder of the license, requiring the license holder to appear before the mayor and council and show cause why the license should not be revoked. The notice shall disclose the charges against the holder of the license and shall state the time, date and place of hearing, which hearing shall not be held less than five days nor more than 15 days from the date of receipt of the notice by the license holder. If, upon final hearing, the mayor and council shall, in accordance with the opinion they entertain as to the evidence and testimony submitted, believe that the license holder is guilty of the charges made, it shall be their duty to revoke the license of the person to whom it was issued or by whom it was held.
(b)
The mayor may, if he shall deem it necessary for the protection or welfare of the people of the city, suspend the license of any person against whom charges have been made, pending final hearing on the issue of whether or not the license of this person should be revoked.
(Code 1976, § 6-1008)
Sec. 18-32. - Violations; penalty.
Any person who shall conduct any business, trade, profession or vocation within the corporate limits of the city without obtaining a license and any person who shall fail to comply with or violate the several sections of this article shall be deemed guilty of disorderly conduct and upon each conviction thereof shall be punished as provided in section 1-12.
(Code 1976, § 6-1011)
Sec. 18-33. - Late payment of license fee.
In addition to other penalties for the violation of this article, 20 percent of the license fee shall be added to this fee for each month that a business, trade, vocation or profession is carried on without a license.
(Code 1976, § 6-1012)
Sec. 18-34. - Transfer of license.
A license issued under this article to any person may not be transferred to any other person doing business within the city, whether engaged in the same business or not. Should a person holding a valid business license issued hereunder change its place of business from one location to another, but continue to do the same business in the new location, and where such person maintains only the one place of business, then such license may be transferred from one place of business to another place of business.
(Code 1976, § 6-1013)
