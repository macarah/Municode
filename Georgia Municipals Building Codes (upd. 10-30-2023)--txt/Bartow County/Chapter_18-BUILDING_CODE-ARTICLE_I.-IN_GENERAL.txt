ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 18-1. - Adoption of state minimum fire safety standards.
(a)
Bartow County does hereby, pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 25-2-12, adopt the state minimum fire safety standards adopted in the rules and regulations promulgated pursuant to Chapter 2 of Title 12 of the Georgia Code, including all subsequent revisions thereof, entitled the "Rules and Regulations for the State Minimum Fire Safety Standards" as set forth in the Georgia Administrative Code, Chapter 120-3-3.
(b)
The fire marshal for Bartow County, Georgia, is hereby authorized to enforce the provisions of this section.
(c)
The commissioner of Bartow County shall be authorized to adopt a fee schedule for such inspections and related activities as performed by the fire marshal.
(Ord. of 5-16-2012)
Sec. 18-2. - Existing buildings and special historical buildings.
(a)
In accordance with O.C.G.A. § 8-2-203, the provisions of Title 8, Chapter 2, Article 3 of the Georgia Code, "Application of Building and Fire Related Codes to Existing Buildings," (O.C.G.A. § 8-2-200 et seq.) are hereby adopted by reference.
(b)
The building official and fire marshal for Bartow County, Georgia are hereby authorized to enforce the provisions of this section and O.C.G.A. § 8-2-200 et seq.
(c)
The building official and fire marshal, upon reviewing an existing building or special historic building to which this section is applicable, may, in their discretion based on the standards contained in O.C.G.A. § 8-2-200 et seq., require compliance alternatives as indicated therein. However, this paragraph is only applicable as to buildings constructed prior to December 31, 1945, and no exceptions to this building or fire codes pursuant to this section are permitted on buildings constructed after January 1, 1946.
(d)
This section is not applicable to buildings being used for residential uses or those used for industrial uses as specified in the Bartow County Zoning Ordinance, Appendix A to the Code of Bartow County.
(e)
No exceptions or compliance alternatives are allowed with respect to the Georgia State Fire Code, the most current edition as adopted by the State of Georgia.
(f)
Appeals of decisions of the building official and/or fire marshal shall be made to the Bartow County Construction Board of Adjustment and Appeals pursuant to the procedures set forth in the article II, division 13 of chapter 18 of the Code of Bartow County.
(Ord. of 5-16-2012)

Sec. 18-4. - Emergency access provisions.
(a)
Definitions.
[The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:]
Commercial building means any building designed or used for commercial, industrial or multi-family purposes.
Existing building means a building which has been completed and lawfully occupied on or before effective date of this section.
Fire chief means the chief and director of the Bartow County Fire Department, or his or her designee.
Fire Department Connection (FDC) means an appliance used by the fire department to supply water to a building's sprinkler system.
Fire marshal means the fire marshal of Bartow County or his or her designee.
Lock box means a high security, U.L. 1037 listed, key vault system approved by the chief of the Bartow County Fire Department for secure containment for any or all of the following: access keys, material safety data sheets, and/or other pertinent site information which may assist responding emergency agency personnel to gain rapid entrance to a building or property.
Multi-family occupancy means an occupancy that provides sleeping accommodations for more than two dwelling units for purposes other than health care or detention/correction.
New building means a building newly constructed and occupied after the effective date of this section.
Owner means the owner of record, tenant, lessee or other person having a possessory or proprietary interest in a building or property.
Remodeled building means a building undergoing additions, alterations or repairs which shall cause the same to conform to current provisions of any of the building or property codes or ordinances of Bartow County at the time of the remodel.
Secured, for the purpose of this section, will refer to those properties that are locked or otherwise inaccessible to emergency vehicles or personnel by mechanical or other means.
Standpipe means a pipe with fire department hose connections for use by fire departments and those trained in handling fire streams.
(b)
Requirements. The fire chief shall be authorized to implement rules and regulations for the use of a key lock system. The fire chief shall designate the type of key lock system to be implemented within Bartow County and shall have the authority to require all structures to use the designated system.
The owner or operator of a structure required to have a lock box shall, at all times, keep a key in the box that will allow for access to the structure or premises. At any time it becomes necessary for the content of the box to change, the fire department shall be notified so that a fire department representative will be available to open the box. The owner or person in control of any building or facility required to have a lock box shall be present, personally or through an agent, during access to the box, except during an emergency at the property. Keys to open the lock boxes will be kept in a secure device on fire department vehicles and will be capable of monitoring the presence of the key and recording the time, date, and person removing the key.
(c)
Lock boxes.
(1)
Where required: A lock box shall be installed and maintained for the following facilities and locations:
a.
All new commercial and industrial buildings prior to the issuance of a certificate of occupancy or business license.
b.
All existing buildings where a change of occupancy takes place. This applies to either the building as a whole or where a change of occupancy takes place in an individual tenant space or suite.
c.
Existing buildings which are equipped with an automatic fire or smoke alarm system, a sprinkler system or other automatic extinguishing system within one year after the adoption of this section.
d.
Any premises with manual or power operated gates necessary to be opened for emergency access and where security personnel may not be available to provide immediate entrance.
e.
All existing structures undertaking additions, alterations or repairs which shall cause the same to conform with current provisions of any of the building or property codes or ordinances of Bartow County prior to the issuance of a certificate of occupancy.
f.
Any location deemed necessary by the fire chief or fire marshal.
Exceptions:
a.
Reserved.
b.
One- and two-family dwellings. Providing that owners/occupants of one and two family dwellings will not be prohibited from installing appropriate lock boxes as approved by the fire marshal.
c.
Any building owned or occupied by a state or federal unit of government.
d.
Individual apartments or rented rooms in residential occupancies.
e.
Where not possible or feasible to install a lock box, such as on a fence or gate, a padlock or suitable device purchased from the approved lock box company shall be used.
(2)
Content:
a.
Lock boxes will contain easily identified labeled keys to provide entrance to the building and access to all parts of the interior.
b.
If the building is equipped with elevators, an appropriate elevator key will be required in the box.
Exception: An elevator key lock box containing the appropriate key may be installed next to the elevator.
c.
For buildings manufacturing or storing hazardous or controlled chemicals or substances, safety data sheets (SDS) and/or other relevant data will be required to include locations and amounts of material.
d.
A card or sheet with information for a minimum of two emergency contacts for each business.
(3)
Installation:
a.
The lock box will be permanently affixed per the manufacturer's requirement to the building or structure in such a way as to prevent removal or damage of the box by vandals.
b.
For new construction, a recessed box shall be used.
Exception: Where approved by the fire marshal, surface mount boxes may be used.
c.
The lock box will be located at or near the opposite of the hinged side of the recognized public entrance on the exterior of the building or structure and/or at such other location required by the fire marshal.
d.
The lock box shall be located at a height of not less than four feet and not more than six feet above final grade.
Exception: Where approved by the fire marshal, the box may be installed at a greater height not to exceed eight feet.
e.
The final location shall be approved by a representative of the fire marshal.
f.
Where the building/premises is protected by an alarm system, tampering or opening of the box shall produce a supervisory signal through the alarm panel.
g.
Alert decals, approved by the fire marshal, to alert fire companies of the presence of security features covered by this section, will be displayed on any outside doors or windows as designated by the fire marshal.
(4)
Access:
a.
No displays, signs or other fixtures, or vegetation shall be allowed that will block or otherwise obscure the lock box.
b.
A clear minimum distance of 36 inches shall be maintained to and in front of the lock box.
(d)
Application for lock box. Application for a lock box shall be made on forms provided to the owner, occupant or their agent by the county building official's office upon application for building or remodel permits. Any fees shall be paid at the time of application. The owner, occupant, or agent shall be responsible for the cost to purchase, install and maintain the lock box. It will be the owner, occupant, or their agent's responsibility to order the appropriate lock box using instructions provided to them upon application.
(e)
Key switch. A key switch keyed the same as lock boxes shall be installed where required by the fire marshal.
(f)
Locking FDC and standpipe caps. Where an automatic sprinkler system or standpipe is installed, appropriate FDC plugs, caps, and fittings with locking devices which have been approved by the fire chief shall be provided by the building owner, occupant, or agent. These devices shall be provided for all new sprinkler and standpipe installations and when the installation of lock boxes are mandatory in existing buildings.
(g)
Reserved.
(h)
Violation and penalties. Any person who violates, neglects or refuses to comply with any provision of this section shall upon conviction thereof be fined not less than $100.00 nor more than $500.00 for each offense and such person shall be deemed guilty of a separate offense for each and every day during which said violation, neglect or refusal to comply shall continue.

Sec. 18-5. - Fireworks; noise ordinance.
(a)
Definitions. The terms "fireworks" and "consumer fireworks," as used in this section, shall have the definition as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 25-10-1.
(b)
Fees. Fees relating to fireworks shall be as set forth in section 18-3.
(c)
Restriction. The county finds that use or ignition of any fireworks or consumer fireworks, other than as authorized by state law, to be a nuisance on the basis of the noise created. Such fireworks and consumer fireworks can also pose a risk to public safety, in the form of fire or injury to persons or property. It shall therefore be unlawful to use or ignite any fireworks or consumer fireworks before 10:00 a.m. or after 9:00 p.m. on any day except as explicitly permitted by O.C.G.A. § 25-10-2(b)(3)(B)(iii) or (iv). The county shall not issue special use permits under O.C.G.A. § 25-10-2(b)(3)(B)(ii) since fireworks and consumer fireworks are hereby restricted on the basis of noise.
(d)
Public exhibition or display. Permits for public exhibition or display of fireworks are granted by the probate court judge under O.C.G.A. § 25-10-4. The restriction of subsection (a) shall not apply to public exhibition or display of fireworks under a permit granted by the probate court.
(e)
Temporary stands; permanent stands. Licenses for temporary consumer fireworks retail stands are granted by the county fire department pursuant to O.C.G.A. tit. 25, ch. 10. Permanent consumer fireworks retail sales facilities are permitted by the state fire marshal under O.C.G.A. tit. 25, ch. 10.
( Ord. of 5-11-2016, § II )
Sec. 18-6. - Special sprinkler and fire safety regulations for community living arrangements and personal care homes and similar uses.
(a)
Scope. This document covers all new buildings or portions thereof and all existing one- and two-family dwellings being converted to be used as personal care homes, community living arrangements, lodging or rooming houses and residential board and care homes of six or less residents who are not related to the owners or operators by either blood or marriage, whether licensed by a state agency or not. This also applies to changes of occupancy to such uses in existing buildings, and change of ownership of existing buildings. No portion of this document should be construed to refer to one- or two-family dwelling units used as a private residence. In the event of any conflict between this article and the state minimum fire safety standards as adopted by the State of Georgia, the more stringent code shall apply.
(b)
Definitions.
Board and care occupancy means a building or space used for lodging and boarding of four or more residents, not related by blood or marriage to the owners or operator, for the purpose of providing personal care services.
Change of occupancy means the change in occupancy classification of a structure or portion of a structure to another occupancy classification. Subject to the approval of the authority having jurisdiction (AHJ), the use or occupancy of an existing structure shall be allowed to be changed and the structure is allowed to be occupied for purposes in other groups without conforming to all of the requirements of the new occupancy provided the new or proposed use has less stringent requirements.
Change of use means a change in the purpose or level of activity within a structure that involves a change in application of the requirements of the Code.
Community living arrangement (CLA) means any residence whether operated for profit or not which is subject to being licensed by the state, that undertakes through its ownership or management to provide or arrange for the provision of daily personal services, supports, care or treatment exclusively for two or more adults who are not related to the owner or manager by blood or marriage and whose residential services are financially supported, in whole or in part, by funds designated through the department of human services, division of mental health, developmental disabilities, and addictive diseases.
Existing building means buildings, structures, facilities or conditions which are already in existence or constructed and officially authorized prior to the effective date for the adoption of the section.
Lodging or rooming house means a building or portion thereof that does not qualify as a one-or two-family dwelling, that provides sleeping accommodations for a total of 15 or fewer people on a transient or permanent basis, with or without meals, but without separate cooking facilities for individual occupants. Foster homes, group homes, battered spouse shelters, or similar facilities providing lodging and boarding for four but not more than 15 residents or clients, not related by blood or adoption to the owner(s) or operator(s) shall be deemed a lodging or rooming house.
NFPA means National Fire Protection Association.
Occupancies subject to this section means all community living arrangements, personal care homes, lodging and rooming houses, and all board and care occupancies regardless of building size where two or more clients are provided for.
Occupancy means the purpose for which a building or portion thereof is used or intended to be used.
One- and two-family dwelling unit means a building that contains not more than two dwelling units with independent cooking, bathroom and sanitation facilities where each dwelling unit is occupied by members of a single family with not more than three occupants who are not related either by blood, marriage, or adoption.
Personal care home means a facility licensed by the Georgia Department of Community Health as a personal care home. A personal care home is further defined as any dwelling, whether operated for profit or not, which undertakes through its ownership or management to provide or arrange for the provision of housing, food service, and one or more personal services for two or more adults who are not related to the owner or administrator by blood or marriage.
Substantial renovation means any construction project involving exits or internal features of such building or structure costing more than the building or structure's assessed value according to the Bartow County tax records at the time of renovation.
(c)
Fire protection sprinkler systems requirements.
(1)
Subject to the exceptions in subsection (g) below, all occupancies subject to this section shall have a minimum NFPA 13R sprinkler system installed.
(2)
When a complete automatic fire sprinkler system is provided, either as a requirement or as an alternate to another requirement of the Code, the system shall be adequately supervised to ensure reliable operation as follows:
a.
The extinguishing system shall be electrically connected, either directly to the fire department by such method as approved by the fire chief, or to the fire department through an approved central station or another of its equivalent as approved by the fire chief.
b.
Where a system may be disabled by the closing of valves, interruption of power, etc., adequate supervision acceptable to the building official and fire marshal shall be provided to sound at least a local trouble alarm when the system is deactivated. The alarm shall be in a location within the protected building or premises which is constantly attended by qualified personnel during periods the protected building is occupied, or in an approved remote receiving facility.
c.
When a fire alarm system(s) is required or provided, activation of the fire sprinkler system shall cause the building fire alarm system to sound.
d.
Portable fire extinguishers in accordance with 9.7.4.1 of NFPA 101, as adopted by the State of Georgia, shall be provided.
e.
At least one 2A-rated multipurpose ABC fire extinguisher shall be provided on each occupied floor and in the basement that shall be readily accessible.
f.
Required portable fire extinguishers shall be inspected and maintained annually by a licensed fire safety technician in accordance with NFPA 10.
(d)
Detection and alarm systems.
(1)
Subject to the exceptions in subsection (g) below, all occupancies subject to this section shall have a fire alarm system meeting the minimum requirements for single- and multiple-station alarms and household fire alarm systems per NFPA 72 installed.
(2)
Carbon monoxide detectors shall be provided in the residence where natural gas, LP gas or heating oil is used to heat the residence or for cooking.
(3)
Carbon monoxide detectors shall be provided in the residence if a solid fuel burning fireplace or fixed heating device is installed in the residence.
(e)
Signage. All occupancies subject to this section shall have signage in a form approved by the fire department attached to the building at a location approved by the fire department, to inform first responders of the nature of the activity in the building.
(f)
Egress.
(1)
Subject to the exceptions in subsection (g) below, in all occupancies subject to this section, every sleeping room and every living area shall have not less than one primary means of escape and one secondary means of escape.
(2)
A secondary means of escape shall not be required where the bedroom has a door leading directly to the outside of the room at or to grade level, or the living area has a door leading directly to the outside of the area at or to grade level.
(3)
A facility serving a resident dependent upon a wheelchair or other mechanical device for mobility shall provide at least two exits from the facility remote from each other, which are both accessible to the resident(s).
(g)
Exceptions. These requirements shall apply to any new building constructed for the purpose of one of the occupancies subject to this section. They shall also apply to a change of use—that is, the conversion of any existing building or portion of a building—to any one of the occupancies subject to this section. They shall also apply when the ownership changes of any existing occupancy subject to this section in an existing building. The signage requirements of subsection (e) shall apply to all existing and new occupancies subject to this section; this provision shall become effective September 1, 2018.
(h)
Administration and enforcement. This section shall be administered and enforced by the county fire marshal with the cooperation of the building official. The building official shall be authorized to revoke or withhold certificates of occupancy for non-compliant buildings and structures. The county shall be authorized to issue citations under section 1-19 of the Code or take legal action to enforce this section. The county shall be authorized to withhold other approvals under other chapters of the Code of Bartow County as a means to require compliance. The operation of an occupancy subject to this section in violation of this section is deemed a public nuisance, creating a hazard to public health, safety and welfare, and the county shall be authorized to exercise its remedies in magistrate or superior court to seek abatement of such nuisance by order enforcing this section or closing down the occupancy, or other such relief as the court may craft.

Sec. 18-7. - Residential sprinklers in drive-under garages.
This standard applies only to those sprinkler application in drive-under garages where the water supply is metal piping or approved plastic pipe for sprinkler application. This standard would not apply where the water service supply is from a well.
System design requirements:
(1)
The water supply for the sprinkler heads shall be provided by a minimum ¾ inch water supply line. Metal piping or approved plastic piping must be used to connect to sprinkler heads. Piping shall be a minimum ¾ inch in the protected area.
(2)
A back flow prevention device shall be installed when the sprinkler piping from the point of connection to the domestic water system to the end of the sprinkler piping exceeds 15 feet. (Exception: When the piping for the sprinkler is reconnected to the domestic water supply creating a loop in the system to allow the water in the system to circulate.)
(3)
Provisions shall be made to protect the piping from freezing in unheated areas.
Sprinkler heads:
(1)
Only listed residential quick response sprinkler heads with a maximum 7/16 inch orifice shall be installed.
(2)
The total garage area or the total compartment space between separations shall be protected. A lintel extending eight inches below the ceiling height shall be considered a separation.
(3)
Spacing of sprinkler heads shall be in accordance with manufacturer's specification but not to exceed 144 square feet of coverage per single sprinkler head.
(4)
The minimum distance between sprinkler heads within a compartment shall be eight feet.
(5)
Pendant and upright sprinkler heads shall be positioned so that the deflectors are within one to four inches from the ceiling.
(6)
Sidewall sprinkler heads shall be positioned so that the deflectors are within 4 to 4 ½ inches from the ceiling.
(7)
A minimum of 18 inches of clearance shall be provided around the sprinkler head.
Alarms:
(1)
Interconnected hard-wired smoke detectors shall be installed in garage area in accordance with the National Fire Protection Association, 72 National Fire Alarm Code, 1993 edition.
(2)
Carbon monoxide detector shall be installed in the living space above the garage area in accordance with manufacturer's recommended installation procedures.
