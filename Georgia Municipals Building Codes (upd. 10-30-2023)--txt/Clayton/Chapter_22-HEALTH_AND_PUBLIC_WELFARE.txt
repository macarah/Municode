ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 22-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Health officer means the county director of health or his authorized representative and such other city, county and other officials as may be designated from time to time by the city council.
(Code 1999, § 38-1)
Sec. 22-2. - County health department rules and regulations adopted.
The city adopts the rules and regulations of the Rabun County, Georgia, health department as fully as if set forth in full in this chapter. A copy of the county health department rules and regulations shall be available in the office of the city clerk.
Sec. 22-3. - State rules and regulations adopted.
Ga. Comp. Rules and Regs. ch. 290-5-26, On Site Sewage Management Systems; Ga. Comp. Rules and Regs. ch. 290-5-14, Food Service; and Ga. Comp. Rules and Regs. ch. 290-5-18, Tourist Accommodations are hereby adopted.
(Code 1999, § 38-2; Ord. of 2-14-2006, § 38-2)
Secs. 22-4—22-24. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - ADMINISTRATION AND ENFORCEMENT

Sec. 22-25. - Issuance of citation.
(a)
Upon information of a violation of any of the rules and regulations adopted in the ordinance from which this chapter is derived being made known to the county health officer or his designee, there shall cause a citation to be issued requiring such violator to appear before the judge of the city court on a day and time certain, then and there to stand trial for such violation.
(b)
Citations issued under this section shall be pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 15-10-63 and shall be personally served upon the person accused of such violation. Each citation shall state the time and place at which the accused is to appear for trial.
(Code 1999, § 38-3; Ord. of 2-14-2006, § 38-3)
Sec. 22-26. - Penalty for violation of chapter.
(a)
Any person violating the provisions of this chapter may be punished as provided in section 1-13. Separate offenses shall be deemed to be committed on each day during on or which a violation occurs or continues.
(b)
In addition to all other provisions of this article, any violation of this chapter is hereby deemed to be a continuing nuisance and may be abated by an application for injunction in the superior court or other court of competent jurisdiction.
(Code 1999, § 38-4)
