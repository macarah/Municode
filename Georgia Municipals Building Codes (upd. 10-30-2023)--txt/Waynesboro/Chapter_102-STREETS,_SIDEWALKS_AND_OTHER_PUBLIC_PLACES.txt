ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 102-1. - Obstructions.
No person shall place any obstruction upon any street or sidewalk, except where permission is granted by the council; and then only when placing sufficient barricades and lights at night.
(Code 1974, § 19-1; Code 1995, § 90-1)
Cross reference— Moving structures, § 22-2.
Sec. 102-2. - Excavations.
No person shall make any excavation in or across any sidewalk or street without permission of the council, nor shall any person leave any opening in any sidewalk or street overnight, without covering such excavation or opening and placing sufficient lights nearby, if in a street, to give ample notice of the danger.
(Code 1974, § 19-2; Code 1995, § 90-2)
Sec. 102-3. - Discharging water onto sidewalks.
It shall be unlawful for any person to pour or discharge water onto sidewalks.
(Code 1974, § 19-3; Code 1995, § 90-3)
Secs. 102-4—102-25. - Reserved.

Sec. 102-26. - Petition required.
The council will not consider paving, or adding curbs and gutters or sidewalks, of or to any street unless and until a petition shall be submitted requesting such paving signed by at least 60 percent in number of the landowners who shall represent at least 50 percent of all linear feet frontage of the lands abutting upon the street or streets or part of a street or streets or sidewalks proposed to be improved by paving. The petition will be addressed to the council, who will act on it at an early date.
(Code 1974, § 19-5; Code 1995, § 90-26)
Sec. 102-27. - Minimum street widths.
The council shall not accept a petition or consider the paving of any street which does not meet the minimum requirements prescribed for street classifications as set by the council. Property owners will not be required to pay for street improvements in excess of the cost of improving the width of residential streets.
(Code 1974, § 19-6; Code 1995, § 90-27)
Sec. 102-28. - Procedure upon approval of petition; costs.
If the council shall, after consideration of any petition, determine that the street referred to in such petition shall be paved in accordance with the petition, then the following policy shall be applicable.
(1)
The city will pay for the entire cost of grading and preparing the subgrade for all paving within any particular project approved by the council without any cost to the abutting property owners; the abutting property owners shall pay for all storm drainage, construction of which shall be subject to the approval of the city.
(2)
The entire cost of all paving up to and including the width of residential streets and the cost of construction of all curbs and gutters deemed necessary by the city shall be charged against and paid by the property owners and assessed upon lots and parcels of land abutting directly on the improvements according to the extent of their respective frontage and in such proportion of such costs as is specified in the petition; provided, however, that the city will pay the cost of paving intersections.
(3)
The cost of construction of sidewalks will be paid for entirely by the abutting property owner and must be constructed under the supervision of the city engineer or other individual designated such responsibility by the city administrator/clerk.
(4)
No streets and no sidewalks involved in any petition shall be constructed unless and until the persons who have signed the petition shall have deposited with the city the total amount of cost of making the improvements which may be necessary.
(5)
If all property owners do not sign the petition, then the city will enter up an assessment against that property and proceed to collect the cost according to law.
(6)
If it shall be necessary or advisable for sewer and water extensions to be made from the mains in the street to the property lines before the pavement is constructed, such connections from the main to the curb will be made by the city, but shall be paid for by the property owner before the work is done.
(7)
The property owners will pay for the construction, grading or paving of any driveway that may be constructed at his property.
(Code 1974, § 19-7; Code 1995, § 90-28)
Sec. 102-29. - Resurfacing at city expense.
Whenever it shall be necessary to resurface a street that has been paved as outlined in this article, the city will do the work at its own expense without any cost to the property owners.
(Code 1974, § 19-8; Code 1995, § 90-29)
Sec. 102-30. - Subdivision requirements.
The provisions of this article in no way relieve anyone whose activities come within the activities defined in chapter 94 from any responsibility incurred thereunder.
(Code 1974, § 19-9; Code 1995, § 90-30)
