Sec. 34-1. - Registration of solicitors; permit required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to solicit funds upon the streets, rights-of-way or sidewalks of the city, for charitable purposes, for himself or for other cause, without first registering his name, address, and the purpose for which he is soliciting, with the city manager/clerk, and obtaining a written permit for such purpose.
(Code 1974, § 15-2; Code 1995, § 34-1)
Sec. 34-2. - Religious services on streets; permit required.
It shall be unlawful for any person to preach or hold any type of religious service on the streets, sidewalks, parks, courthouse square, or other public place within the city where, as a part of such performance, contributions are solicited, either directly or indirectly, unless such person shall first register with the city manager/clerk, giving his full name, occupation, place of residence, and such other information as may be required by the city, and procure a written permit to cover such preaching or other religious service.
(Code 1974, § 15-3; Code 1995, § 34-2)
