Sec. 20-2. - Rules and regulations of board of health.
There are currently in existence certain rules and regulations adopted by the county board of health with the purpose of preserving the public health, safety, and welfare of the citizens of the county. This section specifically incorporates by reference the provisions of the following rules and regulations which are currently in force:
(1)
Rules and regulations pertaining to food service adopted by the department of public health and the county board of health and being Ga. Comp. R. & Regs. Ch. 511-6-1 as subsequently amended and updated, and policies of the county board of health pertaining to food service;
(2)
Rules and regulations pertaining to tourist accommodations adopted by the department of public health and the county board of health and being Ga. Comp. R. & Regs. Ch. 511-6-2 as subsequently amended and updated, and polices of the county board of health pertaining to tourist accommodations;
(3)
Rules and regulations for on-site sewage management systems and guidance manual adopted by the department of public health and the county board of health and being Ga. Comp. R. & Regs. Ch. 511-3-1 as subsequently amended and updated, and policies (Minimum Lot Sizing added June 13, 2006) of the county board of health pertaining to on-site sewage management systems;
(4)
Rules and regulations for portable sanitation contractors adopted by the department of public health and the county board of health and being Ga. Comp. R. & Regs. Ch. 511-3-6 as subsequently amended and updated, and policies of the county board of health pertaining to portable sanitation contractors;
(5)
Rules and regulations pertaining to drinking water supply adopted by the county board of health as subsequently amended and updated, and policies of the county board of health pertaining to drinking water supply;
(6)
Rules and regulations pertaining to public and semi-public swimming pools, spas and recreational water parks adopted by the county board of health and policies of the county board of health pertaining to public and semi-public swimming pools, spas and recreational water parks;
(7)
Rules and regulations pertaining to tattoo/body piercing studios and tattoo/body piercing artists adopted by the county board of health and being rules and regulations for body art studios and artists as subsequently amended and updated, and policies of the county board of health pertaining to tattoo/body piercing studios and tattoo/body piercing artists;
(8)
County board of health rules and regulations for the control of rabies as subsequently amended and updated, and polies of the county board of health pertaining to rabies control and the Georgia Rabies Control Manual (ch. 4-5);
(9)
County environmental health fee schedule approved by the county board of health and adopted by the county board of health and adopted by the board of commissioners as subsequently amended and updated, and policies and resolutions of the county board of health pertaining to fees; and
(10)
Rules and regulations pertaining to the Georgia Smokefree Air Act of 2005 adopted by the department of public health (Ga. Comp. R. & Regs. Ch. 511-3-7) and the county board of health being rules and regulations for the Georgia Smokefree Air Act of 2005 as subsequently amended and updated, and policies of the county board of health pertaining to the Georgia Smokefree Air Act of 2005.
(Code 1987, § 9-3; Ord. of 2-3-1987, §§ 1—4)
Sec. 20-3. - Implementation and enforcement.
The duty of implementation and enforcement of these regulations rests with the county board of commissioners, and by virtue of this section the same is hereby expressly delegated to the county board of health, which is hereby charged with the promulgation of sufficient procedures for ensuring compliance with these rules and regulations by the citizens of the county or those individuals within the boundaries of the unincorporated county. The county board of health is directed to implement procedures which will ensure that the citizens of the county or those individuals found within the boundaries of the unincorporated county will receive all procedural rights to which they are entitled under the laws of the state.
(1)
When the duly authorized agent of the county board of health determines that a violation of this chapter exists, he shall send by regular mail, certified mail or personally deliver a written notice of the violation to the owner of the property or his representative, the tenant or his representative and any other party who has control or ownership of the property. The notice of violation may also be personally served or served notoriously by placing on or adjacent to the front door in a conspicuous location.
(2)
The notice shall state the location of the property, the nature of the violation, a provision for remedying the violation and a timeframe for compliance. The notice shall also state that if the violation is not remedied within the timeframe, a citation for the violation may be issued to all parties controlling or owning the property.
(3)
Following the expiration of the time set forth in the notice, the duly authorized agent of the county board of health shall reinspect the property to determine if the violation still exists.
(4)
If the violation has not been remedied at the time of reinspection, a citation/summons to court may be issued by a code enforcement officer or law enforcement personnel pursuant to O.C.G.A § 15-10-63. However, in lieu of a citation, an accusation may be drafted and personally served.
(5)
If the district environmental health director, district program director or district health director determines that an emergency exists such that there is imminent harm or peril to life or property, he may authorize the immediate issuance of a citation or accusation without the notice that is specified in subsection (b)(1) of this section. Furthermore, lack of the notice letter specified in subsection (b)(2) of this section shall not invalidate a citation or accusation.
(Code 1987, § 9-3; Ord. of 2-3-1987, §§ 1—4)
Sec. 20-4. - Violation; penalty.
Any violation of the rules and regulations hereby referenced shall be a violation of this chapter and shall be a misdemeanor offense. Any violator shall be punished according to section 1-8. Any violator shall be subject to the imposition of a fine not to exceed $500.00 and/or imprisonment for a period not to exceed 60 days, or both. Any failure of payment of inspection or other environmental service fees listed within the fee schedule approved by the county board of health and adopted by the county board of commissioners within 60 days of the first notice may result in suspension of permit.
(Code 1987, § 9-3; Ord. of 2-3-1987, §§ 1—4)
Sec. 20-5. - Magistrate's court jurisdiction; adjudication.
The magistrate's court of the county shall have jurisdiction over violations of this section and all procedures for enforcement of this chapter shall be as provided in O.C.G.A. Title 15, Chapter 10, Article 4 (O.C.G.A. § 15-10-60 et seq.). Complaints of violations of any provision of this chapter shall be brought before the magistrate's court by a designated representative of the county board of health and shall be prosecuted through that court. A complaint shall be brought before the magistrate's court only after all notices and rights to a hearing before a hearing officer of the board of health, or designee, shall have been provided and/or all applicable county board of health and/or department of public health compliance procedures have been exhausted.
(Code 1987, § 9-3; Ord. of 2-3-1987, §§ 1—4)
