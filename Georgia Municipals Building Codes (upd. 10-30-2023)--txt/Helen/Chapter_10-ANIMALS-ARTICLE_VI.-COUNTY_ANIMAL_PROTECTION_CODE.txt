ARTICLE VI. - COUNTY ANIMAL PROTECTION CODE

Sec. 10-61. - Licenses for pet dealers, kennel, stable or animal shelter operators; requirement; issuance; application; display of licenses.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any person to act as a pet dealer or operate a kennel, stable or animal shelter unless such person has a valid license issued by the commissioner of agriculture. Any person acting without a license in violation of this section shall be guilty of a misdemeanor.
(b)
Any person licensed by the commissioner of agriculture as a bird dealer shall not be required to obtain a license under this article if such person does not deal in animals other than birds. If, however, a licensed bird dealer sells, offers to sell, exchanges or offers for adoption dogs, cats, fish, reptiles or other animals customarily obtained as pets, then such dealer shall be required to obtain a license under this article in addition to his bird dealer's license.
(c)
A license must be prominently displayed at each place of business of a pet dealer and at each kennel, stable and animal shelter.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-62. - Quarantine of animal, premises or any area by the animal control officer.
(a)
In the control, suppression, prevention and eradication of animal diseases, the animal control officer shall be authorized to quarantine an animal, premises or any area when it is determined:
(1)
The animals are infected with a contagious or infectious disease.
(2)
The place is in an unsanitary condition, which might cause the spread of disease.
(3)
An animal has or has been exposed to any contagious or infectious disease.
(b)
The animal control officer is authorized to issue and enforce stop sale, stop use or stop movement orders to the owners or custodians of any animals, ordering them to hold animals at a designated place, when the animal control officer finds such animals to:
(1)
Be infected with or to have been exposed to any contagious or infectious disease.
(2)
Be held by a person whose license has expired.
(3)
Be held by a person who has failed to obtain a license within ten days of the date on which written notice of the need to obtain a license was given by the animal control officer.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell, use or move any animal in violation of any quarantine or stop sale, stop use or stop removal order issued under this section.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-63. - Inspections; impoundment of animals; exceptions.
(a)
The animal control officer is authorized to enter upon any public or private property for the purpose of inspecting the business premises of any pet dealer, animal shelter, kennel or stable to determine if such facility is licensed and for the purpose of enforcing this article.
(b)
At any time, there is probable cause to believe that a violation of this article or any rule or regulation adopted pursuant to this article has occurred; a law enforcement officer may apply to the appropriate court for an inspection warrant.
(c)
The animal control officer is authorized to impound any animal:
(1)
That has not received humane care.
(2)
That has been subjected to cruelty in violation of section 10-47 (cruelty to animals)
(3)
That is used or intended for use in any violation of section 10-48 (dog fighting).
(4)
If it is determined that consent order or other order concerning the treatment of animals issued pursuant to this article is being violated.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-64. - Notification of owner; custody of animal.
(a)
It shall be the duty of any person impounding an animal under this article to immediately serve the owner, if known, of such animal with a notice of impoundment. Such notice shall contain:
(1)
The name and business address of the person impounding the animal.
(2)
The name and address of the agency retaining custody of the animal.
(3)
A description of the animal.
(4)
The reason the animal was impounded.
(5)
A statement regarding the time limitations for the owner to request a hearing as provided in for section 10-65.
(b)
The notice shall be provided by certified mail sent to the last known address of the owner.
(c)
Whenever under this chapter service is required or permitted to be made upon a party represented by an attorney, the service shall be made upon the attorney unless the court orders service upon the party.
(d)
If the owner of such animal is unknown or cannot be found, service of the notice of impoundment shall be made by posting the notice in a conspicuous place at the location where the animal was found and impounded and by publishing a notice once in a newspaper of general circulation.
(e)
An animal impounded pursuant to this article is deemed to be in the custody of the county.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-65. - Failure to respond; right to hearing; care; crime exception.
(a)
If the owner of an impounded animal fails to respond in writing within five business days of the date the notice of impoundment was served or if the owner is unknown or could not be found within 30 days of publication of the notice of impoundment, the impounded animal shall be disposed of pursuant to section 10-30.
(b)
If the owner of an impounded animal refuses to enter into a consent agreement ensuring that the animal will be given humane care and adequate and veterinary care, the owner may request, in writing, a hearing within five business days of the date the notice of impoundment was served.
(c)
If no hearing is requested within the time limits specified and the failure to request such hearing is due in whole or in part to a reasonably avoidable fault of the owner, the right to a hearing shall have been waived.
(d)
Within 30 days after receiving a written request for a hearing, the applicable governing body or animal control board shall conduct a hearing. The hearing shall be public and all testimony shall be received under oath.
(e)
The scope of the hearing shall be limited to whether the impounding of the animal was authorized by section 10-63.
(f)
The governing body or animal control board shall within five business days after such hearing forward a decision to the owner and the animal control officer.
(g)
If the governing body or animal control board finds that the animal was improperly impounded, the animal shall be returned to the owner and the cost incurred shall be paid by the county.
(h)
If the governing body or animal control board finds that the animal was lawfully impounded, the governing body or animal control board shall rule that:
(1)
The animal be disposed of as provided herein.
(2)
The animal be returned to the owner.
(3)
If the owner has previously been found guilty of failure to provide humane care to an animal, committed cruelty to animals or engaged in dog fighting, the return may be conditioned on the following:
a.
Such animal will be given humane care and adequate veterinary services.
b.
Such animal not be subjected to mistreatment.
c.
The owner complying with this article.
(i)
The provisions of this section shall not apply to an animal that was an object or instrumentality of a crime nor shall any such animal be returned to the owner or disposed of without the approval of the prosecuting attorney.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-66. - Animals raised, kept or maintained for human consumption.
The provisions of this article shall not apply to any person who raises, keeps or maintains animals solely for the purposes of human consumption.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-67. - Injunctions and restraining orders.
In addition to the remedies provided in this article or elsewhere in the laws of the state or the county and notwithstanding the existence of an adequate remedy at law, the county attorney is authorized to apply to the superior court for an injunction or restraining order. The court shall for good cause shown grant a temporary or permanent injunction or an ex parte or restraining order, restraining or enjoining any person, partnership, firm, corporation, or other entity from violating and continuing to violate this article. Such injunction or restraining order shall be issued without bond and may be granted notwithstanding the fact that the violation constitutes a criminal act and notwithstanding the pendency of any criminal prosecution for the same violation.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-68. - Filing a report regarding animal cruelty; immunity.
(a)
A licensed veterinarian having reasonable cause to believe that an animal has been subjected to animal cruelty or dog fighting may make or cause to be made a report of such violation to the animal control officer.
(b)
Any person participating in the making of a report pursuant to this section or participating in any administrative or judicial proceeding pursuant to this article shall be immune from any civil or criminal liability that might otherwise be imposed, provided such participation is made in good faith.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-69. - Article cumulative; does not prohibit enactment and enforcement of local ordinances by municipal or county governing authority.
The animal control officer shall be required to provide timely written notice to the commissioner of agriculture for enforcement action taken against an operator who is licensed by and who is alleged to be in violation of department of agriculture rules and regulations. The department shall be notified of the initiation of any such local enforcement action and of the conclusions or ultimate outcome of any such action.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05)
Sec. 10-70. - Penalties.
A person violating the provisions of this chapter shall be punished as hereinafter set out in section 10-101 (penalties). In the alternative, and in the discretion of the animal control officer, the person may be cited with a violation of state law, and be guilty of a misdemeanor if convicted.
(1)
Each violation of this article shall constitute a separate offense.
(Res. No. 2005-13, 7-5-05; Res. No. 2007-33, 10-2-07)
