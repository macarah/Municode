Sec. 34-802. - Permit fees to be set by city manager.
(a)
The city manager shall adopt and publish such permits fees for this article as may be consistent herewith, and any permit fees shall have the force and effect of law. Any such permit fees may be amended, modified, or supplemented by the city manager upon compliance with the requirement of this section.
(b)
The city commission has reviewed and approved the initial submission of permit fees dated January, 2007, for building permit fee schedule, moving fee, demolition fee, electrical permit fee schedule, plumbing permit fee schedule, meter valve fee schedule, labor, and mechanical permit fee schedule.
(c)
All such permit fees shall be maintained by the city clerk, as a separate volume, in both printed and electronic media form, and available for review and inspection by the public at all times during business operation at city hall. The city clerk shall, in both print and electronic format, maintain an archive of the initial permit fees and any subsequent change thereto, so that it can be conclusively determined the permit fees in effect on any date.
(d)
The city commission shall comply with any responsibility for changes to the permit fees which may be required pursuant to City Charter, by directing the city manager to make such changes as he or she shall deem appropriate, and present, at a public meeting, such changes for review and comment to the city commission. Unless the city commission, by vote, shall determine that some, or all, of the changes are inappropriate, such changes shall be effective on the fourteenth day following the meeting at which the changes are reviewed and commented upon, (unless a different effective date is stated within the changes themselves or by the commission, in which event the stated date shall be the effective date). Thereafter, the city clerk shall, within 60 days from the effective date of the changes, revise the printed and electronic permit fees to reflect the changes; indicate the volume revision; indicate the sections affected by the changes, indicate the effective date of each revision; and provide a publicly available archive of the prior volumes.
(e)
Any firm, person, or corporation who shall fail to timely pay such permit fees, as the same exists or as may hereafter be amended, modified, or supplemented, shall be guilty of a misdemeanor, amenable to the process of the municipal court of the city, and, upon conviction, shall be punished by a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 or by confinement in the county jail or correctional institution not to exceed 60 days, either fine or confinement, or both, in the discretion of the court. Each and every day that such violation exists shall be deemed a separate offense.
(f)
Nothing herein shall limit the right of the city commission to grant variance from, amend, void, or nullify all or any portion of any permit fees.
(Ord. No. 00-10-02, 11-21-00; Ord. No. 07-01-01, 2-6-07; Ord. No. 11-3-03, 4-5-2011)
Sec. 34-803. - Licenses required for plumbing, electrical and HVAC permits.
Plumbing, electrical and HVAC permits shall only be issued to tradesmen licensed by the state.
(Code 1987, § 18-103)
State Law reference— State regulation of electrical, plumbing and mechanical contractors, O.C.G.A. § 48-14-1 et seq.
Sec. 34-804. - Appeals.
Appeals from decisions made under the codes of this article shall go to the city board of appeals.
