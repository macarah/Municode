ARTICLE II. - TIMBER OPERATIONS

Sec. 10-19. - Purpose and intent.
This article is intended to require notice of timber harvesting operations within the unincorporated areas of the county pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 12-6-24, and, in accordance with state statute title 32, highways, bridges and ferries, O.C.G.A. § 32-1-1 et seq., to provide regulations further governing use of public roads by persons engaged in the timber harvesting operations in order to assure the public safety and to protect the county roadways and environment.
(Ord. of 11-17-2012)
Sec. 10-20. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Notice means a notice required to be given to the county public works department by the timber harvester as provided herein.
Surety bond means a valid surety bond, executed by a surety corporation authorized to transact business in this state, protecting the county against any damage caused by such person or firm in an amount specified by the county board of commissioners not exceeding $5,000.00 or, at the option of the timber harvester, a valid irrevocable letter of credit issued by a bank or savings and loan association, as defined in O.C.G.A. § 7-1-4 in the amount of an in lieu of such bond. Any such surety bond or letter of credit shall be in form prescribed by or acceptable to the county administrator and shall be for the calendar year in which delivered.
Timber means softwood or hardwood trees harvested for commercial purposes.
Timber harvester means any person harvesting standing timber in any unincorporated area of the county for delivery as pulpwood, logs, poles, posts, or wood chips to any wood yard or processing plant located inside or outside this state.
(Ord. of 11-17-2012)
Sec. 10-21. - Enforcement, violations and penalties.
(a)
Notice of violation. Upon notice from the issuing authority, work on any project that is being done contrary to the provisions of this article shall be immediately stopped. Such notice shall be in writing and shall be given to the timber harvester and the owner of the subject property, and shall state the conditions under which work may resume. When an emergency exists, written notice shall not be required. If the conditions are not brought into compliance within five business days of the notice, the county road department shall be authorized to accomplish the work with costs thereof to be reimbursed by the timber harvester, and any violation of the provisions of this article shall be punished as for a misdemeanor.
(b)
Prosecution and penalties. Any person violating any of the provisions of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor prosecutable in the county magistrate court and punishable by a fine not to exceed $500.00 and imprisonment in the county jail for a period of not longer than 12 months.
(c)
Liability for damages. The imposition of any fine or other sentence pursuant to this section shall in no way limit the liability of any person for damage done to public property or public rights-of-way, and the county shall be authorized to additionally pursue recovery for any such damage by any and all means permitted by law or in equity, to include, without limitation, the filing of a suit for damages, or the seeking of injunctive relief to stop any such damage.
(Ord. of 11-17-2012)
Sec. 10-22. - Notices of to be provided to county road department.
(a)
Notice of intent to harvest. Prior written notice shall be required of any timber harvester harvesting timber in the unincorporated areas of the county for each separate tract to be harvested. The written notice shall be delivered to the county road department in such form and containing such information as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 12-6-24(b)(1). Notice shall be effective only as to the tract described therein upon receipt by the county road department as confirmed by written acknowledgment from the county road department to the timber harvester and compliance with all other applicable requirements of this article.
(b)
Notice of completion of harvest. The timber harvester shall give written notification to the county road department supervisor upon completion of the timber harvesting operations.
(Ord. of 11-17-2012)
State Law reference— Authority of county to require notice, O.C.G.A. § 12-6-24.
Sec. 10-23. - Notices of to be provided to county board of tax assessors.
(a)
Notice of intent to harvest. Prior to initiating any timber harvesting activities in the county, the timber harvesting contractor shall give written notification to the county board of assessors within 24 hours prior to commencing start-up activities, moving in of equipment or cutting of any trees. The notice shall be in the form designated by the board of county commissioners, a copy of which is available in the office of the county clerk.
(b)
Notice of completion of harvest. Upon completion of the harvesting of the timber identified in the notice of intent to harvest, the timber harvesting contractor shall give oral notification in person or by telephone to the tax assessor's office that the harvesting activities have been completed.
(c)
Prosecution and penalties for failure to report. The penalty for failure to comply with the reporting requirements set out in this section shall be a fine not to exceed $1,000.00 or 12 months imprisonment in the county jail. Citations for violation of this section shall be prosecuted in the county magistrate court.
(Ord. of 11-17-2012)
State Law reference— Assessment of standing timber for ad valorem taxation, O.C.G.A. § 48-5-7.5.
Sec. 10-24. - Surety bond.
(a)
When required. A surety bond shall not be required prior to the effective date of the notice of intent to harvest. However, following the giving of any notice, in the event that the county road department superintendent determines that any activities relating to the timber harvesting are being conducted in a manner that poses likelihood of damage to any public rights-of-way or danger to the public safety, the county road department shall be authorized to demand a surety bond from the timber harvester.
(b)
Contest of bond requirement; administrative hearing. The surety bond shall be delivered to the county road department within three business days following receipt of written demand for the same, unless within the time a written notice of appeal is received by the county road department contesting the requirement of the surety bond. In the event of such contest, the county road department shall, no later than five business days, schedule an administrative hearing in such matter before the county administrator, who shall deliver a decision within three business days of the hearing. The decision of the county administrator shall be conclusive and binding for all purposes.
(c)
Suspension of harvesting during contest proceeding. Following any contest as provided in subsection (b) of this section, all timber harvesting authorized pursuant to the applicable notice of intent to harvest shall be suspended until the surety bond, if any, is delivered to the county road department as required by the county administrator.
(d)
Amount and character of bond. The amount of the bond shall not exceed $5,000.00. The bond shall indemnify the county against any and all damage caused, as a result of the timber harvesting and related activities, to any county roads, rights-of-way, points of egress or other public property. The surety bond shall also indemnify the county for any costs incurred in the cleanup of waste or debris left on public property as a result of the timber harvesting.
(e)
Release of bond. Upon completion of any necessary repairs to public property and removal of all waste and debris, the county will release the timber harvester from the surety bond.
(f)
Bond does not limit liability for damages. In no event shall the delivery of a surety bond limit the liability of any person engaged in timber harvesting and related activities for damages to public property or rights-of-way or for other personal injury or property damage.
(Ord. of 11-17-2012)
State Law reference— Authority of county to require bond, O.C.G.A. § 12-6-24.
Sec. 10-25. - Culverts.
Prior to commencing timber harvesting operations, the county road department superintendent shall inspect the point of access to the public road from the tract described in the notice in order to determine its suitability. If a culvert is required at the point of access, it shall be promptly installed by the timber harvester as directed by the county road department superintendent.
(Ord. of 11-17-2012)
Sec. 10-26. - Warning signs.
Prior to commencing any timber harvesting operations, the timber harvester shall post two signs in each direction from the point of access onto the involved public road. One sign at a distance of 1,000 feet from the point of access stating: "Warning: logging operations ahead" and one sign at a distance of 500 feet stating: "Slow truck entering highway." All signs shall be 36 inches by 36 inches, erected in a diamond shape, painted orange with black legend and posted at least three feet from the travel surface of the roadway.
(Ord. of 11-17-2012)
Sec. 10-27. - Roadway safety.
During the period of operations, the timber harvester shall comply with the following:
(1)
Operations on public ways prohibited. All harvesting operations shall be conducted on the tract identified in the notice and off public roads and rights-of-way. Logging and skidding of logs on public roads and rights-of-way are strictly prohibited.
(2)
Ditches to be kept free of debris. Ditches constituting a part of the public drainage system or otherwise benefitting a public right-of-way shall be kept clear of all debris and residue at all times to permit proper drainage.
(3)
Road and right-of-way obstruction prohibited. There shall be no blockage of public roads or rights-of-way at the access site, and all public roads and rights-of-way must remain open and kept free of debris and residue at all times. No equipment, trucks, trailers, or other personal property shall be parked or located on any public road or right-of-way.
(4)
Mud control. Mud shall not be carried onto paved public roads by logging vehicles. Where necessary, a temporary construction exit of gravel shall be maintained by the timber harvester to eliminate any hazard to traffic or unsightly conditions on the public right-of-way.
(5)
Vehicles prohibited on unimproved public roads during inclement weather. During periods of rain or inclement weather, logging vehicles shall not be operated over the unimproved public roads when such travel causes sufficient damage to the road system that the passage of smaller and lighter vehicles is hampered.
(Ord. of 11-17-2012)
Sec. 10-28. - Final inspection; notice of damages; effect of failure to repair damages.
The county road department supervisor shall inspect all affected public property and public rights-of-way to ensure that the same has not been damaged or has been restored to its original condition, including any shaping of ditches, grading or seeding as required. The timber harvester shall be notified in writing of any deficiencies and given 15 business days to correct the deficiencies. If not accomplished during that period, the county road department or its designee shall be authorized to complete the work at the cost of the timber harvester. When the county road department superintendent determines that the road and access site are in a proper state of repair, the superintendent shall provide a written release of liability to the timber harvester.
(Ord. of 11-17-2012)

ARTICLE III. - PUBLIC ENTERTAINMENT FACILITIES

Sec. 10-60. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Dance hall includes any business, establishment, facility or other entity, where one of its purposes is promoting, facilitating, conducting or permitting public dancing on its premises.
Entertainment means any musical or dance performance, live or recorded, theatrical performance, literary reading or disc jockey performance where the audience is present at the actual time of the occurrence or performance. Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, and except as otherwise excepted elsewhere herein, a jukebox, tape deck, stereo, sound system, compact disc player, television, video projection, or other means of playing music constitutes entertainment.
Public entertainment facility or entity means a business, establishment, enterprise, or facility, or any organization, located within the unincorporated area of the county that provides music, dancing or entertainment of any nature, except as specifically exempt herein.
(Ord. of 6-5-2007, § II)
Sec. 10-61. - Prohibited hours.
It is unlawful for any public entertainment facility or entity located within the unincorporated area of the county to offer or provide public entertainment between the hours of midnight and 6:00 a.m. Further, all public entertainment facilities located within the unincorporated area of the county shall be closed and cleared of all persons by 1:00 a.m. on Sunday and shall not resume business any earlier than 6:00 a.m. the following Monday morning, and shall be closed and cleared of all persons by 1:00 a.m. on all other days.
(Ord. of 6-5-2007, § III)
Sec. 10-62. - Parking.
Every entertainment facility must have adequate parking for all of its guests, customers, patrons and employees either on its own premises or at such other locations where the facility or business is authorized to park vehicles. Owners, operators, managers, temporary users, tenants and lessees of entertainment facilities shall be responsible for providing adequate parking for the facility's respective guests, customers, patrons and employees, and shall not permit any of their guests, customers, patrons or employees, to remain in or on the subject facility's premises unless such persons are parked on the premises or at such off-premises locations as the owner, operator, manager, temporary user, tenant or lessee of the facility has permission for persons attending the facility to park, or at locations authorized for public parking during the times the facility is open for business.
(Ord. of 6-5-2007, § IV)
Sec. 10-63. - Safety and security; noise restricted near occupied residences.
(a)
Security personnel. Every owner, operator, manager, temporary user, tenant or lessee of a public entertainment facility shall provide adequate security to ensure the safety and welfare of persons attending the facility, or events at the facility in such numbers as is reasonably necessary to protect the health, safety and welfare of those persons at the facility during its hours of operation. At a minimum, each facility must have at least one security person on duty for each 100 persons present during the facility's hours of operation when music is being played or when dancing is permitted.
(b)
Maximum capacity fire rating. Each facility providing public entertainment must be rated by a state fire marshal or the local fire department for the maximum number of persons which may be in the facility at any given time. A copy of the maximum capacity shall be posted in each public entertainment facility. The owner, operator, manager, temporary user, tenant or lessee of each public entertainment facility shall be responsible for limiting the number of persons at any given time to the approved maximum capacity for the facility. Failure to restrict the number of persons in the facility to the maximum capacity established by the fire marshal or local fire department shall be a violation of this article. if no maximum capacity sign is currently posted in a facility it shall be the responsibility of the owner, operator, tenant, lessee or manager to obtain and post such sign.
(c)
Noise control. Each entertainment facility subject to this article which is located within 1,000 feet of any occupied residence must keep all exterior doors and windows closed anytime music is being played in order to help keep the music and noise from inside the facility from being an annoyance or nuisance to persons not on the premises or attending the facility. The 1,000-foot distance shall be measured from the point on the facility building nearest the residence.
(d)
Adequate ventilation. In coordination with the noise control requirements, each entertainment facility must also provide adequate ventilation by air conditioning, fans, or other means for the health and safety of those persons in the facility.
(Ord. of 6-5-2007, § V)
Sec. 10-64. - Prosecution of violations; penalties.
(a)
Every person, whether acting as an individual owner, temporary user, tenant, lessee, employee of any owner or operator for an owner, or whether acting as an agent or independent contractor for the owner, employee, operator, temporary user, tenant or lessee unless specifically exempted or excluded elsewhere herein, who participates in or allows, directly or indirectly, the operation of a public entertainment facility in violation of this article shall be guilty of a misdemeanor offense hereunder, and upon conviction, shall be punishable by a maximum fine not to exceed $500.00 or by imprisonment in the county jail not to exceed 180 days or both.
(b)
Notwithstanding the foregoing, anyone violating this article upon the first offense shall be given a written warning, a copy of which shall be filed with the county sheriff's department and retained for a period of five years.
(c)
Summons for violations of this article shall be issued by the county sheriff's department and shall be returnable to the state court of the county for trial and disposition. The state court of the county shall have the jurisdiction to try all cases involving a violation of this article.
(d)
Each day any violation of this article occurs shall constitute a separate offense.
(Ord. of 6-5-2007, § VI)
Sec. 10-65. - Exemptions and exclusions.
No activity that constitutes constitutionally protected speech or any other activity that is protected by the federal or state constitutions is prohibited by this article. In addition, this article does not apply to the following:
(1)
Any activities or entertainment carried on in an individual's home or residence providing attendees are not required to pay an admission or cover charge.
(2)
Any activities which primarily consists of sports events or broadcasts or re-broadcasts of sports events by television, video, or radio, or otherwise, even though some music or dancing is a part of or may be incidentally connected to or integrated into the sports activity or the viewing of such sports activities or events.
(3)
Any school or school-related function, dance, prom, homecoming or other activity officially sponsored or sanctioned by the county board of education, school administration or any organization officially recognized or sanctioned by the county board of education or school administration.
(4)
Church or other religious activities and events that have as their primary purpose church or religious related goals, and other events organized or sponsored by recognized charitable organizations, where dancing and playing music during the prohibited hours is only nominal and is not a main part or primary goal of the activity or event. Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, church sponsored overnight youth activities, retreats and revivals would constitute exempt activities.
(Ord. of 6-5-2007, § VIII)
Sec. 10-66. - Variances.
(a)
Persons eligible. Any charitable, family, religious, civic or non-profit organization that desires to conduct an activity referenced in this article during the times prohibited by this article may apply to the county board of commissioners for a variance not to exceed three consecutive calendar days in a 12-month period. Variances are not required for entertainment activities unless they are scheduled to take place during the times prohibited by this article.
(b)
Limitations. No organization may obtain more than one variance within any consecutive 12-month period. Variances may be allowed for entertainment activities to continue for a reasonable time, but not later than 3:00 a.m.
(c)
Application content and filing. An application for a variance must include the name, address and type of organization, the dates and times of the activity, the nature of entertainment, and the location of the proposed activity. The application must be submitted to the office of the board of county commissioners no less than 120 days prior to the first day of the proposed activity or event for which the variance is requested. There is no fee for filing an application for a variance, except the actual cost of the legal ad referenced above.
(d)
Publication of notice of application for variance. When an application for a variance is filed, a legal notice of such application shall be published for two consecutive weeks in the county newspaper in which sheriff's sales are advertised, with the first such publication to be within ten days after the date the application is received by the board of commissioners.
(e)
Objections to variance. Any person have any objection to the application for variance must file his objection in writing and such written objection must be received in the office of the board of county commissioners no later than the 20th day after the day the first notice is published.
(f)
Consideration by board; final determination. The board of commissioners must meet to consider and vote on the variance application no earlier than the 40th day and not later than the 60th day after the date the application for variance is received in the board's office. The board will make a determination on the application no later than 60 days after the application is received, and may either grant or deny the application, or grant the application in part or with limitations or modifications.
(g)
Criteria for consideration. The board shall consider, in evaluating each application, the dates and times requested; the proposed location and its proximity to residences and residential areas; and whether the proposed activity will pose any serious threat to the health, safety or welfare of the general public.
(Ord. of 6-5-2007, § IX)

ARTICLE IV. - TATTOO AND BODY PIERCING

Sec. 10-91. - Board of health rules adopted.
The rules and regulations of the county board of health with regard to tattoo and body piercing studios and artists as amended from time to time are adopted as though fully set forth in this article. A copy of such rules and regulations are available from the county board of health and in the office of the county clerk.
Sec. 10-92. - Enforcement and penalties.
Enforcement of the adopted rules and regulations shall be in accordance with O.C.G.A. ch. 31-5. The county board of health is authorized to enforce the provisions of this article.
(Ord. of 4-8-2004, § XXIII)
