Chapter 2 - ADMINISTRATION[1]
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 2-1. - Records management.
The county clerk is authorized and directed, according to guidelines established by the Secretary of State, to develop and establish a formal records management program for the county. A copy of the records management program and policy is available in the office of the county clerk.

ARTICLE IV. - OFFICERS AND EMPLOYEES[4]

Sec. 2-75. - Personnel policies.
The county has adopted certain personnel policies and regulations, specifically including the county's drug-free work place policy, that are compiled in the county personnel manual, a copy of which is available in the office of the county clerk.
Secs. 2-76—2-93. - Reserved.
ARTICLE V. - FINANCE[5]

Sec. 2-94. - Fee schedule.
Fees and charges for county services shall be as set forth in the county's fee schedule, as amended from time to time by the board of commissioners, a copy of which is available in the office of the county clerk.
