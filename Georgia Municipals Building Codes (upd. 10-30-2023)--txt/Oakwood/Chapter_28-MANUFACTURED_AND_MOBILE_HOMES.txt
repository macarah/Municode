Chapter 28 - MANUFACTURED AND MOBILE HOMES
ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 28-1. - Number per lot limitation.
Only one trailer or mobile home shall be placed on any lot in the city limits.
(Ord. No. 01, § 1, 11-7-1966)
Sec. 28-2. - Rent or lease prohibited.
Trailers or mobile homes are to be occupied by owners and not rented or leased.
(Ord. No. 01, § 2, 11-7-1966)
Secs. 28-3—28-22. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - REGULATIONS

Sec. 28-23. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Mobile home means any structure defined as a mobile home under O.C.G.A. § 8-2-131, as amended.
(Ord. No. 26, § 1, 8-9-1976)
Sec. 28-24. - Codes adopted.
For the purposes of this article, the following standards and codes are hereby adopted the same as if fully set out herein:
(1)
American National Standards institute Standard A119.1: Standard for Mobile Homes.
(2)
International Plumbing Code, latest edition.
(3)
International Gas Code, latest edition.
(Ord. No. 26, § 2, 8-9-1976; Ord. No. 2020-CO-12-810, § 2, 3-8-2021)
Sec. 28-25. - Building permit required.
All mobile homes hereafter located or relocated in the county shall obtain a building permit previous to the movement of the structure to its residential location.
(Ord. No. 26, § 3, 8-9-1976)
Sec. 28-26. - Inspection required.
The building official, upon notification from the building permit holder or his agent, shall make a final inspection after the mobile home is set up and all service connections made. The building official may require additional inspections if necessary to ensure compliance with this Code.
(Ord. No. 26, § 4, 8-9-1976)
Sec. 28-27. - Service connection—Plumbing.
Current code reads: All water supply system connections and sewage collection system connections shall meet the minimum requirements of the International Plumbing Code and ANSI A119.1 as follows:
(1)
All materials for water supply lines shall meet or exceed the requirements of A119.1.
(2)
All materials for waste drainage systems shall meet or exceed the requirements of the International Plumbing Code.
(Ord. No. 26, § 7, 8-9-1976; Ord. No. 2020-CO-12-810, § 3, 3-8-2021)
Sec. 28-28. - Same—Electrical.
All electrical service connections shall meet the minimum requirements of the International Electrical Code as follows:
(1)
All service entrance cable shall consist of three No. 2 aluminum types AVL, RHW, TW, USE or XHHW and one uninsulated No. 6 aluminum or equivalent.
(2)
Service entrance cable for service greater than 100 amps shall be sized accordingly.
(Ord. No. 26, § 8, 8-9-1976; Ord. No. 2020-CO-12-810, § 4, 3-8-2021)
Sec. 28-29. - Same—Gas.
All LP gas and natural gas service connections shall meet the minimum requirements of the International Gas Code and ANSI A119.1.
(Ord. No. 26, § 9, 8-9-1976; Ord. No. 2020-CO-12-810, § 5, 3-8-2021)
Sec. 28-30. - Contractors license and bond.
It shall be the duty of every contractor under this section, who shall make contracts for the location of mobile homes for which a permit is required, and every contractor subletting the same, or any part thereof, to pay a license tax of $10.00 and to register his name in a book provided for that purpose, with building official, giving full name, residence and place of business; and it shall be the further duty of every such person to give good and sufficient bond in the sum of $5,000.00, to be approved by the county attorney, conditioned to conform to the building code, the regulations of this section and other ordinances or laws of the city council in reference to mobiles homes.
(Ord. No. 26, § 10, 8-9-1976)
Sec. 28-31. - Codes on file in clerk's office.
The various codes referred to in this article are on file with the clerk of the city and available for the general public.
(Ord. No. 26, § 11, 8-9-1976)
Sec. 28-32. - Permit fee.
A $35.00 permit fee shall be paid to the city before a permit for a mobile home is issued.
(Ord. No. 26, § 13, 8-9-1976)
Secs. 28-33—28-52. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - MOBILE HOME PARKS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 28-53. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Mobile home means a detached single-family dwelling with all of the following characteristics:
(1)
Designed for long-term occupancy and containing sleeping accommodations, a flush toilet, a tub or shower bath and kitchen facilities with plumbing and electrical connections provided for attachment to an outside system.
(2)
Designed to be transported after fabrication on its own wheels or on a flatbed or other trailer or detachable wheels.
(3)
Arriving at the site where it is to be occupied as a dwelling complete or in sections including major appliances and furniture and ready for occupancy except for minor and incidental unpacking and assembly operations, location on foundation supports, connection to utilities and the like.
(4)
Not used as a permanent or temporary office, classroom, store or for-hire work space.
Mobile home park means a parcel of land under single ownership that is occupied or designated for occupancy by two or more mobile homes for nontransient use.
Modular home means a detached single-family dwelling which is fabricated entirely or in part at some location other than its permanent site and which may be a complete unit or closed segments thereof.
(Ord. No. 257, § 101, 12-27-1995)
Sec. 28-54. - Service buildings.
Accessory structures and community service facilities are hereby permitted for the convenience and well-being of park residents. Such structures may include but are not limited to the following uses:
(1)
Park management offices, repair shops and storage.
(2)
Community sanitary facilities.
(3)
Community laundry facilities.
(4)
Community postal facilities.
(5)
Indoor community recreation areas.
(6)
Commercial uses supplying essential goods or services for the use of park residents.
(Ord. No. 257, § 103, 12-27-1995)
Sec. 28-55. - Water supply and sewage disposal.
Every park must have a public, community or private water and sewer system which shall be approved by the county health department and the city manager. Water and sewer availability shall be obtained prior to submission of a site plan to the city.
(Ord. No. 257, § 104, 12-27-1995; Ord. No. 2020-CO-12-810, § 6, 3-8-2021)
Sec. 28-56. - Permitted mobile homes.
All site-built houses, modular homes and mobile homes being moved into or relocated within the city for the purpose of permanent or temporary occupancy shall require permitting in accordance with article II of this chapter. All modular homes or mobile homes more than one year old being moved into the city shall not be permitted without the approval of the city council upon a show of extreme hardship.
(Ord. No. 257, § 105, 12-27-1995; Ord. No. 2020-CO-12-810, § 7, 3-8-2021)
Sec. 28-57. - Annual inspection fees.
The building inspector shall inspect annually, or at such other times as he deems necessary, each mobile home, mobile home park or modular home regulated by this article for the purpose of ascertaining whether the same is in compliance with all code requirements and the provisions set forth herein and whether any structure is in need of removal or repair. To meet the expense of such inspection, the permittee therefor shall pay to the sum of $10.00 per structure inspected inhabited by human and $10.00 per uninhabited structure. No inspection fee (other than the initial permit fee) shall be charged during the calendar year in which the mobile home, mobile home park or modular home is initially permitted/erected.
(Ord. No. 257, § 106, 12-27-1995)
Secs. 28-58—28-79. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - MINIMUM DESIGN STANDARDS AND REGULATIONS

Sec. 28-80. - Minimum park size.
The minimum land area of any mobile home park shall be 15 acres with a minimum road frontage on a public street of 200 feet. Each space within the park shall have a minimum area of not less than 10,000 square feet and a minimum space width of 40 feet. All mobile home space lot lines and the type of sewage disposal proposed for each space shall be clearly delineated on a site plan prior to approval by the city.
(Ord. No. 257, § 102(a), 12-27-1995; Ord. No. 2020-CO-12-810, § 8, 3-8-2021)
Sec. 28-81. - Setbacks.
(a)
No mobile home within the park shall be located closer than 50 feet to adjoining property lines and the front setback for each mobile home shall be at least 50 feet from the front lot line.
(b)
Mobile homes shall be separated from each other and from other buildings and structures by at least 30 feet.
(Ord. No. 257, § 102(b), 12-27-1995)
Sec. 28-82. - Foundations and tie-downs.
Each mobile home shall be supported by piers and foundations and shall be anchored to the ground in accordance with all state and local building code requirements to secure the mobile home against uplifting, sliding, rotating and overturning. The underpinning shall consist of a minimum of two courses plus a cap of four-inch concrete/cinder blocks with concrete footings built in accordance with applicable building codes. Said underpinning shall be constructed with adequate ventilation but shall enclose substantially all of the area under the structure to within three inches of the bottom edge of the structure's outside wall with the gap being covered by appropriate trim.
(Ord. No. 257, § 102(c), 12-27-1995)
Sec. 28-83. - Parking requirements.
Off-street parking for the park shall be provided at a rate of two parking spaces per each mobile home lot.
(Ord. No. 257, § 102(d), 12-27-1995)
Sec. 28-84. - Refuse collection.
Each mobile home park shall provide refuse collection pads at locations convenient to each mobile home space but in no case more than 50 feet from the street serving each mobile home.
(Ord. No. 257, § 102(e), 12-27-1995)
Sec. 28-85. - Space numbering.
Each mobile home space shall be provided with a sign not less than one square foot in area which indicates the appropriate space number/address.
(Ord. No. 257, § 102(f), 12-27-1995)
Sec. 28-86. - Driveway construction.
If a proposed street is to be greater in length than 300 feet, a plan profile for the road system shall be submitted along with the site plan. The city manager shall approve all road construction specifications and make a report to the city council regarding the finished road at the time the final plat is submitted.
(Ord. No. 257, § 102(g), 12-27-1995; Ord. No. 2020-CO-12-810, § 9, 3-8-2021)
Sec. 28-87. - Lighting requirements.
The park street system shall be adequately maintained and lighted by the owner. The lighting units shall be so spaced and equipped to provide for the safe movement of pedestrians and vehicle traffic at night.
(Ord. No. 257, § 102(h), 12-27-1995)
Sec. 28-88. - Recreation/community use requirements.
Parks designed to accommodate ten or more mobile homes shall provide one or more recreation areas. The size of the recreation area shall be based upon a minimum of 100 square feet for each mobile home space. Recreation areas shall be so located as to be free of traffic hazards and should be centrally located (topography permitting). Not less than ten percent of the total area of the development shall be devoted to recreation and other community use facilities.
(Ord. No. 257, § 102(i), 12-27-1995)
Sec. 28-89. - Swimming pools.
In the event that a swimming pool is constructed as a part of the park, this facility shall be enclosed by a chainlink, masonry or wood fence not less than four feet high.
(Ord. No. 257, § 102(j), 12-27-1995)
Sec. 28-90. - Perimeter screening.
All park grounds shall be maintained with grass, trees and/or shrubs to enhance the appearance of the park and to prevent soil erosion or the creation of dust during dry weather and to buffer the park from passing traffic. A landscaped screen consisting of dense evergreen trees and/or shrubs and having a minimum width of 35 feet adjacent to all public rights-of-way and 15 feet along all other property lines shall be required. A minimum six-foot-high, opaque fence or wall may be erected along property lines not abutting a public right-of-way in lieu of such landscaped screening. All perimeter screening must be properly maintained.
(Ord. No. 257, § 102(k), 12-27-1995)
Sec. 28-91. - Transfer restrictions.
No individual lot in any park may be sold or control of that lot transferred with the intent or effect of a sale unless that lot and park shall meet all requirements of the city subdivision regulations and the park owner shall hold a valid subdivision recording permit.
(Ord. No. 257, § 102(l), 12-27-1995)
Sec. 28-92. - Structure entrance requirements.
(a)
All buildings/structures which are intended for habitation by humans shall have and maintain a screen door. Porches are required at all entrances to said structures with the following minimum sizes:
(1)
Primary entrance: 40 square feet;
(2)
All secondary entrances: 20 square feet.
(b)
All porches shall have rails and steps constructed in accordance with the current building codes.
(Ord. No. 257, § 102(m), 12-27-1995)
