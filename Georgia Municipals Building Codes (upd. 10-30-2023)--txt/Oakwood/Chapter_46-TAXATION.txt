Footnotes:

State Law reference— Ad valorem taxation, O.C.G.A. § 48-5-1 et seq.

Sec. 46-19. - Freeport exemption law declared effective.
The general "constitutional freeport exemption" law as set forth in Ga. Const. art. 7, § 2, ¶ III and O.C.G.A. § 48-5-48.2 is hereby made effective within the corporate limits of the city.
(Ord. No. 291, § 2, 12-22-1997)
Sec. 46-20. - Ad valorem tax exemptions.
The city does exempt from ad valorem taxation all the value of the following types of tangible personal property or any combination thereof is and shall be exempted from ad valorem taxation:
(1)
Inventory of goods in the process of manufacture or production which shall include all partly finished goods and raw materials held for direct use or consumption in the ordinary course of the taxpayer's manufacturing or production business in the state. The exemption provided for herein shall apply only to tangible personal property which is substantially modified, altered or changed in the ordinary course of the taxpayer's manufacturing, processing or production operations in the state.
(2)
Inventory of finished goods manufactured or produced within the state in the ordinary course of the taxpayer's manufacturing or production business when held by the original manufacturer or producer of such finished goods. The exemption provided for herein shall be for a period not exceeding 12 months from the date such property is produced or manufactured.
(3)
Inventory of finished goods which, on January 1, are stored in a warehouse, dock or wharf, whether public or private, and which are destined for shipment to a final destination outside the state and inventory of finished goods which are shipped into the state from outside the state and stored for transshipment to a final destination outside the state. The exemption provided for herein shall be for a period not exceeding 12 months from the date such property is first stored in the state. All property that is claimed to be exempt under the provisions of this subsection shall be designated as being "in transit" upon the official books and records of the warehouse, dock, or wharf, whether public or private, where such property is being stored. Such official books and records shall contain a full, true and accurate inventory of all such property including the date of the receipt of the property, the date of the withdrawal of the property, the point of origin of the property and the point of final destination of the same, if known. The official books and records of any such warehouse, dock, or wharf, whether public or private, pertaining to any such 'in transit' property shall be at all times open to the inspection of all taxing authorities of the state and of any political subdivision of the state.
(Ord. No. 291, § 3, 12-22-1997)
Sec. 46-21. - Definitions.
(a)
As used in section 46-20, the following words, terms and phrases are defined as follows:
Finished goods means goods, wares and merchandise of every character and kind but shall not include unrecovered, extracted or unsevered natural resources, raw materials, goods in the process of manufacture or production or the stock-in-trade of a retailer.
Raw materials means any material, whether crude or processed, that can be converted by manufacture, processing or combination into a new and useful product but shall not include unrecovered, unextracted or unsevered natural resources.
(b)
All other definitions, terms and provisions of the aforesaid constitutional amendment and O.C.G.A. § 48-5-48.2 shall be and the same are hereby incorporated herein and made a part hereof by reference thereto.
(Ord. No. 291, § 4, 12-22-1997)
Sec. 46-22. - Exemption increase, reduction, etc., to comply with state law.
Any increase, reduction or revocation of such exemption may be accomplished only by the procedures set forth in O.C.G.A. § 48-5-48.2.
(Ord. No. 291, § 5, 12-22-1997)

ARTICLE III. - SPECIAL TAX DISTRICTS
DIVISION 1. - CREATION OF SPECIAL TAX DISTRICTS

Sec. 46-25. - Special tax districts created.
There is hereby created and established special districts for the provision of services within such districts within the City of Oakwood. Fees, assessments and taxes may be levied and collected within such districts to pay, wholly or partially, the cost of providing such services therein and to construct and maintain facilities therefor as authorized by Article IX, Section II, Paragraphs III and VI of the Constitution of the State of Georgia.
(Ord. No. 2014-CO-11-724, § 1, 12-8-2014)
Sec. 46-26. - Purpose for special tax district.
The special districts shall be created for the following purposes:
(a)
Street and road construction and maintenance, including curbs, sidewalks, street lights and devices to control the flow of traffic;
(b)
Storm water and sewage collection and disposal systems; or
(c)
Parks, recreational areas, programs and facilities.
(Ord. No. 2014-CO-11-724, § 1, 12-8-2014)
Sec. 46-27. - Creation of tax district.
(a)
When a service or maintenance of a facility, which is identified by this article, is requested by existing residents, these residents may request the creation of a special tax district to fund the service or maintenance of said facility as set forth in section 46-28 hereof.
(b)
When construction and maintenance of a facility, which is identified by this article, is requested by existing residents, these residents may request the creation of a special tax district to fund the construction and maintenance of said facility as set forth in section 46-28 hereof.
(Ord. No. 2014-CO-11-724, § 1, 12-8-2014)
Sec. 46-28. - Procedure for creation of tax district.
(a)
Any owner of land (or lot owner as hereinafter defined) in any area of the city may present a written request for the creation of a tax district to the city manager.
(b)
Owner of land or lot owner shall include all persons holding fee simple title according to the real estate records of Hall County, Georgia to real property within the proposed special tax district within the municipal limits of the city at the time the petition for the creation of said district is submitted as provided herein. For the purpose of petitioning the city council for the creation of a new special tax district, the owners of land shall have one vote per platted lot or parcel within a particular proposed special tax district. The term "owner of land" or "lot owner" specifically excludes tenants in possession of property within the special tax district. However, the term shall include the developer of any subdivision of less than ten lots or otherwise not covered by the city's subdivision ordinance who has fee simple title to an existing or proposed road and has promised to dedicate said road to the city.
(c)
Application fee. An application fee as set forth in Chapter 22 of the Oakwood City Code shall be paid at the time of application. In addition for section 46-27(a) tax districts, a sum equal to the customary rates charged by the utility or service provider for similar developments for a period of two years shall be tendered with the application.
(d)
Upon receipt of a complete application, the city manager shall delineate the appropriate boundaries of the tax district, prepare a sketch or plat showing the proposed tax district and prepare a petition for the establishment of a special tax district for circulation among the property owners in the proposed special tax district in accordance with Form 2 hereof. The petition for the creation of the proposed special tax district shall be returned by the applicant to the city manager within 90 days of the initial application.
(e)
If 75 percent of the lot owners in the proposed special tax district sign the petition for the creation of a tax district, the petition shall be presented to the city council for consideration. The city council shall then conduct a public hearing for the purpose of determining whether or not to create the proposed special tax district, giving consideration to health, safety and welfare concerns. Unless 100 percent of the lot owners sign the petition, the public hearing shall be advertised one time in the official organ of the county and at least one sign shall be posted in the proposed tax district giving notice of the hearing at least 15 days before the public hearing. The city manager shall verify the signatures on the petition and that the requisite number of lot owners has signed the petition prior to advertising the public hearing. For petitions submitted pursuant to section 46-27(b), said petition must be accompanied by a certificate of title or other document that shows the city's right-of-way in the facility or a letter of commitment from the land owner(s) to convey right-of-way to the city before implementation of the tax district and/or construction of the facility.
(Ord. No. 2014-CO-11-724, § 1, 12-8-2014)
Sec. 46-29. - Register.
There is created hereby special tax districts that shall be identified by a number assigned by the city and entered into a registry maintained by the city clerk. Each tax district shall be entered into said registry and shall contain the tax parcel numbers of each lot contained therein. Such registers shall be available to the public for inspection upon reasonable notice to either of these offices.
(Ord. No. 2014-CO-11-724, § 1, 12-8-2014)
Sec. 46-30. - Collection.
The charges for service in each district shall be paid as a district tax and shall be collected concurrently with the ad valorem tax billed to each lot or lot resident within each tax district. The city manager shall be responsible for the collection of the tax assessed to each tax district.
(Ord. No. 2014-CO-11-724, § 1, 12-8-2014)
Sec. 46-31. - Approval of city council.
Upon the recommendation of the staff, the city council shall approve or deny the creation of the special tax district. The special tax district shall be effective upon approval by the city council and the effective date shall be set forth on the final plat. When each requirement of this article is completed and the city council approves the creation of the special tax district, the city will assume the responsibility and make the periodic payments to the utility company or service provider for services rendered.
(Ord. No. 2014-CO-11-724, § 1, 12-8-2014)
DIVISION 2. - RESPONSIBILITY FOR COSTS FOR SPECIAL TAX DISTRICTS

Sec. 46-32. - Determination and modification of service charges for section 46-27(a) special tax districts.
(a)
For special tax districts created pursuant to section 46-27(a) hereof for the cost of providing services and maintenance, it shall be the landowner's responsibility to construct, install and equip the facility and make any initial improvements at their cost and any and all such improvements shall be installed on property where the city has title to land.
(b)
For continuing services and maintenance in said special tax districts, the said costs shall be borne by the owners of the property within the special tax district receiving that service. Service charges shall be determined and modified from time to time by resolution of the city council. In setting or modifying such rates it shall be the objective of the city council of establish rates, fees and charges that are fair and reasonable, reflect the value of services and maintenance of facilities to those properties who benefit therefrom and will be sufficient to meet the cost of budgeted programs, services and maintenance of facilities including, but not limited to, the payment of principal and interest on revenue bond obligations incurred for construction and necessary improvements to the district.
(c)
In the event of excessive vandalism to improvements within the special tax district that results in the city being billed for repairs, the city may pro-rate these repair bills and add a pro-rata share of these bills to the charge. Charges shall be assessed against all lots within the special tax district whether or not the lot is located within the boundaries of a particular subdivision, so long as the lot is located within the boundaries of the special tax districts.
(d)
The city may contract with the public utilities for the purpose of carrying out the terms of this article.
(Ord. No. 2014-CO-11-724, § 1, 12-8-2014)
Sec. 46-33. - Responsibility for costs for section 46-27(b) special tax districts.
The cost of construction or maintenance of a facility under section 46-27(b) shall be advanced by the city. The city shall have the authority to contract with the necessary parties to complete the job. This cost plus administrative costs shall then be paid by the land owners of the special tax district and amortized for up to a 15-year period. The amortized cost of the construction/ maintenance project shall be assigned to each platted lot. Each lot, whether vacant or improved, will pay the same assessment. The special tax district shall end upon the payment of the total amortized costs to the city.
(Ord. No. 2014-CO-11-724, § 1, 12-8-2014)
Sec. 46-34. - Inclusion of administrative costs.
Administrative costs incurred by the city in the collection of the special tax district tax shall be the responsibility of the owners of property in the district for which said costs were incurred. The administrative charge assessed each year shall be set by the city council, as provided in Chapter 22 of the Oakwood City Code, but shall not exceed 15 percent of the amortized amount due in the special tax district each year. The city council shall be authorized to establish different administrative charges for different special tax districts and to adjust the respective rates from time to time by resolution, after taking into consideration all of the cost factors involved in collecting the charges contemplated in this article including, but not limited to, the delinquency rate within a special tax district. The pro-rata portion of the administrative charges shall be charged to the owners of the property.
(Ord. No. 2014-CO-11-724, § 1, 12-8-2014)
DIVISION 3. - GENERAL PROVISIONS

Sec. 46-35. - Lien created.
In addition to any other rights of collection for late or unpaid charges, the city shall have the rights available under the laws of the State of Georgia for assessment, the creation of a lien upon the property of the owner receiving the service provided together with all rights of execution, levy, foreclosure and sale.
(Ord. No. 2014-CO-11-724, § 1, 12-8-2014)
Sec. 46-36. - Deposit required; forfeiture.
In addition to the other charges authorized under this article, the city shall have the right to require deposits from the lot owners receiving the service in such amount as shall be determined by the city council. This deposit shall be held by the city as security for payment for services rendered, and the city shall have the authority to set off service charges against the deposit for nonpayment for services at such times and on such occasions as shall be determined by the city. Notice of such setoff shall be given to the lot owner by mail at the address of the property services although advance notice of such setoff shall not be required. In the event of a reduction in the deposit of a property owner because of failure to pay charges assessed pursuant to this article, the lot owner shall be required to restore the deposit to its required level upon request by the city.
(Ord. No. 2014-CO-11-724, § 1, 12-8-2014)
Sec. 46-37. - Service charges.
The following rates shall apply:
(a)
Dwellings units. Each attached and each detached dwelling unit shall be charged the rate applicable to one equivalent residential unit according to the rate schedule that is adopted by the city council as may be amended or modified from time to time.
(b)
Subdivided lands. All subdivided lands within the district that are not deed restricted from constructing or installing a dwelling unit shall be charged at a rate of one equivalent residential unit multiplied by the number of dwelling units on the respective subdivided parcel of land.
(c)
Administrative service charge rate. The administration of the services herein provided and for all billing and accounting shall be the responsibility of the city council. An administrative fee that is based on an amount necessary to defer necessary expenses related to the administration of this article shall be set by the city council and added to each tax bill.
(Ord. No. 2014-CO-11-724, § 1, 12-8-2014)
Sec. 46-38. - Service charges, billing, delinquencies.
A charge bill may be sent through the United States mail or by alternative means notifying the customer of the amount of the bill, the date the payment is due, and the date when past due. Failure to receive a bill is not justification for non-payment. Regardless of the party to whom the bill is initially directed, the owner of each parcel of developed land, as shown from the public land records of Hall County shall be ultimately obligated to pay such fee. If a customer is under billed or if no bill is sent for developed land, the city may back bill for a period of up to four years but shall not assess penalties for any delinquency. A one and one-half percent late charge shall be billed based on the unpaid balance of any service that becomes delinquent.
(Ord. No. 2014-CO-11-724, § 1, 12-8-2014)
Sec. 46-39. - Service charges billed in common.
The service charge may be billed on a common statement and collected along with other city taxes and services.
(Ord. No. 2014-CO-11-724, § 1, 12-8-2014)
Sec. 46-40. - Appeals.
Any customer who believes the provision of this article has been applied in error may appeal in the following manner:
(a)
An appeal must be filed in writing with the city manager. In the case of service charge appeals, the appellant shall include sufficient information to conduct a review of the service charge.
(b)
Using the information provided by the appellant, the city manager shall conduct a review of the property and respond to the appeal in writing within 30 days.
(c)
In response to an appeal, the city manager may adjust the service charge applicable to a property in conformance with the general purpose and intent of this article.
(d)
A decision of the city manager that is adverse to an appellant may be further appealed to the mayor within 30 days of receipt of notice of the adverse decision. Notice of the appeal shall be served on the mayor by the appellant, stating the grounds for the appeal. The mayor shall issue a written decision on the appeal within 30 days. All decisions of the mayor shall be served on the appellant personally or by registered or certified mail, sent to the billing address of the appellant.
(e)
All decisions by the mayor shall be final.
(f)
The appeal process contained in this section shall be a condition precedent to an aggrieved customer seeking judicial relief. Any decision of the mayor may be reviewed upon application for writ of certiorari before the Superior Court of Hall County, filed within 30 days of the date of service of the decision.
(Ord. No. 2014-CO-11-724, § 1, 12-8-2014)
DIVISION 4. - ADMINISTRATIVE PROVISIONS

Sec. 46-41. - Conflict of laws.
If any provision of this article is in conflict with any state or federal law, or with any rule, regulation or order of any agency of this state or federal agency having jurisdiction of the subject matter of this article, it is hereby deemed to be the intention of the city council that the state or federal law or rule, regulation or order, as the case may be, shall prevail so that the remaining portion of this article shall be deemed to be of full force and effect.
(Ord. No. 2014-CO-11-724, § 1, 12-8-2014)
Sec. 46-42. - No waiver.
It is the intent of this article to protect the public health, safety and general welfare of all properties and persons in general, but not to create any special duty or relationship with any individual person or to any specified property within or without the municipal boundaries of the city. The City of Oakwood expressly reserves the right to assert all available immunities and defenses in any action seeking to impose monetary damages upon the city, its officers, employees and agents arising out of any alleged failure or breach of duty or relationship as may now exist or hereafter be created. To the extent any permit, plan approval, inspection or similar act is required by the city as a condition precedent to any activity by or upon property not owned by the city, pursuant to this or any other regulatory ordinance, regulation or rule of the city or under federal or state law, the issuance of such permit, plan approval, or inspection shall not be deemed to constitute a warranty, express or implied, nor shall afford the basis for any action, including any action based on failure to permit or negligent issuance of a permit, seeking the imposition of money damages against the city, its officers, employees or agents.
(Ord. No. 2014-CO-11-724, § 1, 12-8-2014)
