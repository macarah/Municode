ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 6-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Alcoholic beverage means and includes all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, malt beverage, wine or fortified wine.
Brown bagging.
(1)
The term "brown bagging" means the bringing, taking or carrying of any distilled spirits into any business licensed to operate within the city, with the exception of:
a.
A bona fide private club as set forth in O.C.G.A. § 3-7-1 et seq.;
b.
An offices for business uses such as insurance sales, accounting, medical, dental and legal; and
c.
A banquet type facility located within a motel, hotel or food service establishment as the latter is defined at O.C.G.A. § 26-2-370.
(2)
Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, brown bagging includes, but is not limited to:
a.
Any person who brown bags;
b.
Any person who participates in the consumption of an alcoholic beverage being brown bagged;
c.
Any person who consumes an alcoholic beverage on any premises holding a business license, except for those premises licensed for on-premises consumption of alcoholic beverages or otherwise exempted from the definition of brown bagging by virtue of the private residential character of the occupancy;
d.
Any employee of the business establishment in whose presence brown bagging knowingly or within reckless indifference occurs.
Church means a permanent stand alone building where persons regularly assemble for religious worship and shall be publicly designated as a church, but shall not include a residence also used for religious purposes.
Distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume, including, but not limited to, all fortified wines.
Fortified wine means any alcoholic beverage containing more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "fortified wine" includes, but is not limited to, brandy.
Growler means glass or plastic bottle or jug, not less than 12 ounces or more than 64 ounces, used to hold specialty malt beverages such as beer and hard cider, which are dispensed from a tap and sealed airtight before being sold to the customer for off-premises consumption.
Growler retailer means an establishment that holds a current brewer's license or an establishment that holds a current retail license from the City of Albany for the sale of malt beverages which derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross sales from the sale of prepared meals or food.
Legal age means the age established by O.C.G.A. § 3-3-23, as amended from time to time, as the age below which alcoholic beverages may not lawfully be sold to, furnished to, purchased by or possessed by a person.
Malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing not more than six percent alcohol by volume and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer and strong beer. The term does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Package means a bottle, can, keg, barrel or other original consumer container.
Premises means the space or area owned, leased, or controlled by the licensee and used by the licensee for the purpose of operating under the license; except that in the case of hotels and motels, the term "premises" includes only that portion of the property where alcoholic beverages are sold, delivered or served.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 21 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines and like products. The term does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage. A liquid shall first be deemed to be a wine at that point in the manufacturing process when it conforms to the definition of wine contained in this section.
(Code 1975, § 4-3; Code 1985, § 4-1; Ord. No. 81-161, § I(a), 6-9-1981; Ord. No. 85-223, § 1, 10-8-1985; Ord. No. 04-102, § 1, 1-27-2004; Ord. No. 04-111, § 1, 3-23-2003; Ord. No. 07-135, § 1, 11-27-2007; Ord. No. 18-113, § 1, 9-11-2018)
Sec. 6-2. - Activities declared privilege.
The business of manufacturing, distributing, selling, handling and otherwise dealing in or possessing alcoholic beverages are declared to be privileges in this city and not rights.
(Code 1975, § 4-2; Code 1985, § 4-2)
Sec. 6-3. - Proximity regulations.
(a)
Churches. No wines or distilled spirits shall be offered for sale, sold or dispensed in any form within 100 yards of any church, such distance under this subsection shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground from the nearest principal entrance of any church building to the nearest principal entrance of the premises on which such wines or distilled spirits are sold or dispensed. For purposes of measuring "direct route of travel" measure along a paved or otherwise improved surface appropriate for public vehicular, bicycle or pedestrian traffic as follows:
(1)
From the main public entrance of the structure from which the alcoholic beverage is sold or offered for sale go in a straight line to the nearest public route of travel;
(2)
Thence go in a straight line along said public route of travel by the nearest route;
(3)
Thence go to the nearest principal entrance of the church building;
(4)
No minor or principal arterial street, as identified by the Functional Classification Map in the 2030 DARTS (Dougherty Area Regional Transportation Study), a copy of which is on file in the office of planning and development services, shall be crossed except at the nearest city-, county- or state-approved pedestrian cross-walk or at the nearest intersection.
(b)
Schools and colleges. No distilled spirits shall be offered for sale or sold in any form within 200 yards of any school or college, and no malt beverages or wines shall be offered for sale or sold in any form within 100 yards of a school or college, such distance under this subsection shall be measured by the most direct route of travel on the ground from the nearest point on the property line of such school or college to the nearest principal entrance of the premises on which such wines or distilled spirits or malt beverages are offered for sale or sold. This subsection shall not apply at any location for which a new license is applied for if the sale of wine and beer was lawful at such location at any time during the 12 months immediately preceding such immediately preceding such application. For purposes of measuring "direct route of travel" measure along a paved or otherwise improved surface appropriate for public vehicular, bicycle or pedestrian traffic as follows:
(1)
From the main public entrance of the structure from which the alcoholic beverage is sold or offered for sale go in a straight line to the nearest public route of travel;
(2)
Thence go in a straight line along said public route of travel by the nearest route;
(3)
Thence go to the nearest point on the property line of the school or college campus;
(4)
No minor or principal arterial street, as identified by the Functional Classification Map in the 2030 DARTS (Dougherty Area Regional Transportation Study), a copy of which is on file in the office of planning and development services, shall be crossed except at the nearest city-, county- or state-approved pedestrian cross-walk or at the nearest intersection.
(c)
[Locations licensed in the future.] As to any location licensed in the future, if the proximity requirements set forth above are met at the time of application for license, the subsequent opening and operation of a church or school within the distance prohibitions set forth above shall not operate to prevent the (i) continuance of an existing license or (ii) the renewal thereof or (iii) the issuance of a new license to any subsequent owner of such property or the issuance of a new license to any subsequent tenant of the owner or subsequent owner of such property.
(d)
Adult entertainment premises.
(1)
No adult entertainment premises shall be licensed to sell alcoholic beverages if it shall be located within 1,000 feet of the boundaries of any residential, apartment or townhouse zoning district or within 1,000 feet of:
a.
Any public park which exceeds three acres in size;
b.
The property line of any church, shrine or other place used exclusively for religious services, except the chapel of a mortuary or cemetery;
c.
Any school ground or college campus;
d.
A branch of any public library; or
e.
A regular stop as designated by the county board of education where a school bus for the transportation of school children in the public schools of the county shall board or discharge school children.
(2)
As used herein, the term "adult entertainment premises" shall be defined as premises which sell alcoholic beverages and are used for commercial entertainment wherein live performers display any portion of the areola of the female breast or any portion of pubic hair, cleft to the buttocks, anus, vulva or genitals.
(3)
Distance under this subsection shall be measured by the same direct route of travel described in this subsection:
a.
From the main public entrance of the structure from which the alcoholic beverage is sold or offered for sale go in a straight line to the nearest public route of travel.
b.
Thence go in a straight line along said public route of travel by the nearest route.
c.
Thence to the nearest point on the property line of the church, school, college campus, residence, public park, public library or school bus stop.
d.
No minor or principal arterial street, as identified by the Functional Classification Map in the 2030 DARTS (Dougherty Area Regional Transportation Study), a copy of which is on file in the office of planning and development services, shall be crossed except at the nearest city-, county- or state-approved pedestrian cross-walk or at the nearest intersection.
(Code 1975, § 4-4; Code 1985, § 4-3; Ord. No. 84-108, § 1, 2-14-1984; Ord. No. 91-188, § 1, 12-31-1991; Ord. No. 07-133, § 2, 11-27-2007; Ord. No. 15-116, §§ 1, 2, 5-26-2015)
Sec. 6-4. - Sales to intoxicated persons prohibited.
It shall be unlawful for any person to sell, barter, exchange, give, provide or furnish any alcoholic beverage to any person who is in a state of noticeable intoxication.
(Code 1985, § 4-4; Ord. No. 81-152, § 1(4-5.1), 5-12-1981)
State Law reference— Furnishing alcoholic beverages to intoxicated persons, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-22.
Sec. 6-5. - Hours, days of sales—Generally.
(a)
Sales by the package. All persons licensed to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages by the package for off-premises consumption only, and the employees of such licensees, shall cease all sales no later than 2:30 a.m. Tuesday through Sunday and shall not resume such sales any earlier than 8:00 a.m. the following Monday. Sales of alcoholic beverages by the package shall be prohibited Tuesday through Friday between the hours of 2:30 a.m. and 8:00 a.m. the same morning for those stores licensed to sell beer and wine and not subject to an earlier closing time by state law.
(b)
Sales by the drink. All persons licensed to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages by the drink for consumption on the premises, and the employees of such licensees, shall cease all such sales no later than 2:55 a.m. Tuesday through Sunday and shall not resume such sales any earlier than 8:00 a.m. the following Monday. Sales of alcoholic beverages by the drink shall be prohibited Monday through Friday between the hours of 2:55 a.m. and 8:00 a.m. All premises licensed to engage in such sales by the drink may remain open until 3:30 a.m. Tuesday through Sunday, at which time such premises shall be closed and cleared of all persons, provided employees of the licensed premises may remain on the premises until 4:00 a.m.
(c)
Sunday sales. All establishments licensed to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages by the drink for consumption on the premises which derive at least 50 percent of their total annual gross sales from the sale of prepared meals or food in all of the combined retailed outlets of the individual establishment where food is served or derive at least 50 percent of their total annual gross income from the rental of rooms from overnight lodging, may sell alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises on Sundays from 11:00 a.m. until 12:00 midnight, provided in any year where December 31 falls on a Sunday, such sales may take place between the hours of 12:01 a.m. Monday January 1 until 2:55 a.m. Monday, January 1.
(d)
Game rooms. Game rooms as defined in section 8-19 who are licensed to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages by the drink for consumption on the premises and derive at least 50 percent of their total annual gross receipts from sales other than alcoholic beverage sales may be open from 12:00 p.m. Sunday until 10:00 p.m. Sunday provided sales of alcoholic beverages shall be prohibited.
(e)
Civic Center. Alcoholic beverages may be sold at the James H. Gray Civic Center on Sunday between the hours of 12:30 p.m. and midnight.

Sec. 6-6. - Same—On election days, holidays.
(a)
Election days. Pursuant to the delegation of authority granted to this governing authority by O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20(b)(2)(A), the sale by wholesale and retail of alcoholic beverages, to-wit, distilled spirits, wine, and malt beverages, shall be lawful during the polling hours of any election; provided, however, nothing herein shall authorize the sale of alcoholic beverages within 250 feet of a polling place during such time as the polls are opened.
(b)
Holidays. All persons licensed to engage in the sale of distilled spirits and other alcoholic beverages by the package for off-premises consumption only, and the employees of such licensees, shall cease all such sales no later than 11:55 p.m. the night before Thanksgiving Day and the night before Christmas Day, and shall close the premises and be clear of all persons at such time. Such premises shall remain closed and no such sales may resume earlier than 8:00 a.m. on the morning following Thanksgiving Day and Christmas Day. The sale of alcoholic beverages by the drink and the sale of malt beverages, fortified wine and wine on Thanksgiving Day and Christmas Day shall be lawful and shall be governed by the provisions of section 6-5.
(Code 1975, § 4-11; Code 1985, § 4-6; Ord. No. 81-218, § 1, 11-10-1981; Ord. No. 82-177, § 1, 9-14-1982; Ord. No. 85-239, § 1, 11-12-1985)
Sec. 6-7. - Same—By restaurants, bowling alleys.
(a)
Restaurants. Notwithstanding any provisions of this article to the contrary, bona fide full-service restaurants may remain open on any and all of the days and during any and all of the hours prescribed in sections 6-5 and 6-6 for the purpose of serving meals, but no sales of alcoholic beverages shall be made during any of such proscribed hours or any of such proscribed days. For purposes of this subsection, the term "bona fide full-service restaurant" means an established place of business:
(1)
Which is licensed to sell alcoholic beverages, distilled spirits, malt beverages or wines for consumption on the premises;
(2)
Where meals with substantial entrees selected by the patron from a menu are served;
(3)
Which has adequate facilities and sufficient employees for cooking or preparing and serving such meals for consumption at tables in dining rooms on the premises;
(4)
Which derives at least 50 percent of its gross income from the sale of such meals prepared, served and consumed on the premises.
(b)
Bowling alleys. Notwithstanding any of the provisions of this article to the contrary, bowling alley premises being served by waitresses from a licensed premise pursuant to the provisions of section 6-95, shall not be required to close during the hours and days proscribed in sections 6-5 and 6-6, but such sections shall apply to the licensed premise from which such service is rendered. No waitress service shall be afforded, or alcoholic beverages sold or delivered, in the bowling alley premises during any of such proscribed hours or proscribed days; provided that a bowling alley which is licensed to sell alcoholic beverages by the drink for consumption on the premises and which derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross revenues either from the rental of bowling lanes and bowling equipment or from the combination of the rental of bowling lanes and bowling equipment and the sale of prepared meals and other food products, may sell alcoholic beverages on Sundays from 12:30 p.m. until midnight.
(Code 1985, § 4-7; Ord. No. 81-137, § 7, 4-14-1981; Ord. No. 81-152, § 7, 5-12-1981; Ord. No. 81-224, § 1, 11-24-1981; Ord. No. 09-119, §§ 1, 2, 5-26-2009)
Sec. 6-8. - Same—Private clubs.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Bona fide private club means any nonprofit association organized under section 501 of the U.S. IRS Code and the laws of this state which:
(1)
Has been in existence at least one year prior to the filing of its application for an alcoholic beverage license to be issued pursuant to this chapter.
(2)
Has at least 75 regular dues paying members.
(3)
Owns, hires or leases a building or space within a building for the reasonable use of its members.
(4)
Has suitable kitchen and dining room space and equipment.
(5)
Is staffed with a sufficient number of employees for cooking, preparing, and serving meals for its members and guests.
(6)
Has no member officer, agent or employee directly or indirectly receiving, in the form of salary or other compensation, any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages beyond a fixed salary.
(7)
Has the occupational license required of private clubs.
Fixed salary means the amount of compensation paid any member, officer, agent or employee of a bona fide private club as may be fixed for him or her by its members at a prior annual meeting or by the governing body out of the general revenue of the club and shall not include any commission on any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages. For the purposes of this definition, tips, or gratuities which are added to the bills under club regulations shall not be considered as profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages.
(b)
Notwithstanding the provisions of sections 6-5 and 6-6, a bona fide private club as defined in this section may remain open on Sunday, Thanksgiving Day and Christmas Day for use by its members and guests and alcoholic beverages may be sold for consumption on the premises from 11:00 a.m. until 12 midnight, provided any year where December 31 falls on a Sunday, such sales may take place between the hours of 12:01 a.m. Monday January 1 until 2:55 a.m. Monday, January 1.
(Code 1985, § 4-8; Ord. No. 81-225, § 1(4-11.1), 11-24-1981; Ord. No. 19-127, § 1, 12-10-2019)
State Law reference— Sale of distilled spirits by private clubs generally, O.C.G.A. § 3-7-1 et seq.
Sec. 6-9. - Misrepresentation of beverages.
It shall be unlawful for licensees under this chapter to add to the contents of a bottle or to refill empty bottles or in any other manner to misrepresent the quantity, quality or brand name of any alcoholic beverage.
(Code 1975, § 4-32; Code 1985, § 4-9)
Sec. 6-10. - Telephone required.
Holders of licenses under the provisions of this chapter are required to have a telephone on all premises where alcoholic beverages are sold or served. Failure to have a telephone in compliance with the requirements of this section shall constitute grounds for suspension or revocation of all alcoholic beverages licenses under which the premises are being operated.
(Code 1975, § 4-13; Code 1985, § 4-10)
Sec. 6-11. - Regulation for the giving away or selling of alcoholic beverages at reduced prices.
Alcoholic beverage licensees are prohibited from selling or giving away alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises under the following circumstances:
(1)
Serving multiple drinks for a single price or offering all you can drink for a set price during a set time.
(2)
Making a single price the basis for a required purchase of two or more servings.
(3)
Selling or furnishing alcoholic beverages prior to 4:00 p.m. or after 8:00 p.m. at a price less than the normal retail price of the establishment for that drink. For purposes of this subsection, the term "normal retail price" shall be the highest price charged for that drink at any time during the preceding seven days. Between the hours of 4:00 p.m. and 8:00 p.m. beverages may not be sold for less than one-half normal retail price as herein defined.
(4)
Nothing herein contained shall be construed to prohibit the dispensing of drinks in pitchers or in jumbo sizes provided that such pitchers or jumbo sizes shall be available at all times that the licensee is open for business and the usual, customary or established retail price shall not be reduced; for the purposes of this subsection, a jumbo drink is defined as any drink containing more than 1¼ ounces of alcohol.
(5)
No licensee shall give away or sell coupons, receipts, or other devices authorizing the serving of alcoholic beverage drinks on a subsequent day.
(6)
Licensees may give discounts to employees, who for purposes of this section are defined as employees who work not less than five days a week or 20 hours per week.
(Code 1985, § 4-11; Ord. No. 85-140, § 1, 3-28-1985; Ord. No. 88-110, § 1, 2-9-1988)
Sec. 6-12. - Security camera systems.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms, and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this subsection, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Audio-video interleave (AVI) means a multimedia format that can contain both audio and video data that allows for synchronous playback.
Bayonet Neill-Concelman (BNC) means a type of RF (radio frequency) connector used for terminating coaxial cable.
Bitmap (BMP) means an image file format used to store digital images.
Coder-decoder, compressor-decompressor, compression-decompression algorithm, or Codec means a device or program capable of performing encoding and decoding on a digital data stream or signal.
Compact disc (CD) means an optical disc storage media used for storing digital data.
Digital versatile disc (DVD) is an optical disc storage media format primarily used for video and data storage.
Digital video (DV) means a compressed video format with a 5:1 compression ratio.
Field means a series of lines consisting of odd- and even-numbered lines which are interlaced to display a single frame.
FireWire (IEEE 1394 interface) means is a serial bus interface standard for high-speed communications and data transfer.
Frame means a single image of video, usually consisting of two interlaced fields in analog video.
Joint photographic experts group (JPEG) means a commonly used method of compression for photographic images.
Motion picture experts group MPEG means a set of standardized compression formats for video and audio primarily consisting of MPEG-1, MPEG-2, MPEG-3, and MPEG-4.
Multi-image display means an image consisting of split screens that allow for the viewing of multiple cameras on a screen simultaneously.
Multiplexer means a device that alternates between multiple cameras and adds an encoded signal that allows a picture from each camera to be viewed in succession (as with switchers) or simultaneously.
Radio Corporation of America connector or RCA connector means a type of electrical connector that is commonly used in the audio/video market.
Separate video connector or S-video connector means an analog video connector that carries the video data as two separate signals (brightness and color). The four-pin Mini-DIN (Deutsches Institut für Normung) connector is the most common type connector and, for purposes of this section, is the required standard.
Switcher means a device that alternates between multiple cameras so that the output at any one time is from a single camera. The output of the switcher usually serves as the input to the recording device.
Tagged information file format (TIFF) means a container format for storing images, including photographs and line art.
Universal serial bus (USB) means a serial bus standard to connect devices to a host computer.
Video cassette recorder (VCR) means a device used to record and play back analog video to and from a removable tape medium (usually VHS).
Video home system (VHS) means a recording and playing standard developed by Victor Company of Japan, Limited (JVC).
(b)
Required. Any establishment licensed by the city to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages by the package for off-premises consumption is required to install security camera systems of a type and number enumerated in this section.
(c)
Capability requirements. Camera systems meeting the requirements of this section must be capable of producing retrievable images on video cassette tape, or downloading to a compact disc, digital versatile disc, or other digital memory device that can be made a permanent record. Camera systems meeting the requirements of this section shall be maintained in proper working order at all times and shall be subject to periodic inspection by the chief of police or the chief of police's designee or the code enforcement director or the code enforcement director's designee.
(1)
All camera systems shall consist of, at a minimum, the following:
a.
Video cameras with a minimum resolution of 640 x 480 lines will be located at each exit door of the establishment with additional cameras located at each point of customer transaction (e.g., cash registers, teller machines, drive-throughs, etc.), and pointed at the customer. All video cameras must have a signal-to-noise ratio of at least 48dB.
b.
A recorder with retrieval capability.
1.
The recorder must record at a minimum resolution of 640 x 480 lines per frame.
2.
The recorder shall record at no less than five frames per second per camera.
c.
A monitor for playback review connected to the output of the recording device.
d.
If a switcher or multiplexer is used (either internal or external), video images shall not be recorded in multi-image display mode.
e.
All components must be compatible to produce sharp, detailed, video images. The line loss between the camera and the switcher, multiplexer or recorder shall not cause the signal to fall below 45dB.
(2)
Analog video cassette recorders must be capable of the following:
a.
The recorder must record onto a cassette tape.
b.
The recorder must have a BNC, RCA, or S-video type output connector (monitor output).
(3)
Any digital video recorder must be capable of the following:
a.
The recorder must have a BNC, RCA, S-video (monitor output), FireWire or USB type output connector.
b.
The recorder must be able to produce retrievable video on a CD, DVD or other removable memory storage device in its native file format with the proprietary player.
c.
The recorder must also be able to produce retrievable video in an open file format such as AVI, DV, or MPEG format on a CD, DVD or other removable memory storage device. Any codec file that is required to play the open file format must be included on the CD, DVD or memory device.
d.
If alarm activation is incorporated into the recorder, the recorder shall record at the highest number of frames per second, the highest resolution, and the least compression available when the alarm is activated. The recorder should have a buffer capable of retaining five minutes of video prior to alarm activation.
(d)
Responsibilities of owner. The business owner or the business owner's designee will be responsible for the maintenance and periodic checks of camera systems. In the event of an actual crime, the business will be responsible for producing a recording of the incident within 24 hours. The tape, CD, or DVD will become the property of the city police department to be used as evidence.
(e)
Violation and penalties. Violation of any provisions of this section shall constitute an offense hereunder and be punished as follows:
(1)
First offense. A fine of $200.00, and the judge of the municipal court may impose a suspension of all alcoholic beverage licenses from the city for a period not to exceed 30 days.
(2)
Second offense. An offense will be considered a second offense if it occurs within 365 calendar days from the date of the occurrence of the first offense. A second offense will incur a fine of $350.00, and all alcoholic beverage licenses at the establishment issued by the city will be suspended for a minimum period of 60 days.
Sec. 6-13. - Open containers.
(a)
Definitions. The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this section, shall have the following meaning, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Open container means any container which is immediately capable of being consumed from, or the seal of which has been broken.
Semi-public parking facility means any privately owned area wherein motor vehicles may be parked by the public in conjunction with any business, enterprise, commercial establishment, office building, church, school, or multifamily residential building.
(b)
Policy. The following shall be the policies for open containers:
(1)
It shall be unlawful for any person to have in such person's possession any alcoholic beverages in an open container while on the public streets, sidewalks or rights-of-way, or in any public or semi-public parking facility within the City of Albany, Georgia.
(2)
It is unlawful and punishable, as provided in this section, for any person to possess an open container of an alcoholic beverage while operating a vehicle on the public streets, rights-of-way or other public property in the city or while a passenger in or on a vehicle being operated on the public streets, rights-of-way or other public property in the city.
(c)
Exceptions. The prohibition announced in subsection (b) of this section shall not apply to:
(1)
Any events to which permission for outdoor consumption of an alcoholic beverage license has been granted by the board of city commissioners.
(2)
Any property owned by the board of regents of the state.
(3)
Patrons of a sidewalk cafe as defined in the applicable zoning ordinance, if such establishment is otherwise licensed to sell alcoholic beverages.
(d)
Notice requirements. All licensed alcoholic beverage establishments are hereby requested to post, in a conspicuous location inside such establishment, a notice informing patrons that open containers are prohibited on the public streets, sidewalks, rights-of-way, or in any public or semi-public parking facility within the City of Albany, Georgia.
(e)
Violation. Violation of the provisions of this section by any person shall constitute an offense punishable as provided in sections 1-17 and 1-18.
(Code 1985, § 4-13; Ord. No. 90-174, § 1, 12-27-1990; Ord. No. 95-160, §§ 1—5, 12-12-1995)
Sec. 6-14. - Types of entertainment, attire and conduct prohibited upon premises licensed to sell alcoholic beverages.
The following types of entertainment, attire and conduct are prohibited upon any premises licensed to sell, serve or dispense alcoholic beverages for consumption on such premises within the City of Albany, Georgia:
(1)
The employment or use of any person, in any capacity, in the sale or service of alcoholic beverages while such person is unclothed or in such attire, costume or clothing so as to expose to view any portion of the female breast below the top of the areola or of any portion of the male or female pubic hair, anus, cleft of the buttocks, vulva and genitals.
(2)
Live entertainment where any person appears in the manner described in subsection (1) of this section, or where such persons perform acts of or acts which simulate any of the following:
a.
Sexual intercourse, masturbation, sodomy, bestiality, oral copulation, flagellation, or any sexual act which is prohibited by law.
b.
The caressing or fondling of the breasts, buttocks, anus or genitals.
c.
The displaying of the male or female pubic hair, anus, vulva or genitals.
(3)
The holding, promotion, sponsoring or allowance of any contest, promotion, special night, event or any other activity where patrons of the licensed establishment are encouraged or allowed to engage in any of the conduct described in subsections (1) and (2) of this section; provided, however;
that nothing contained in this section shall apply to the premises of any mainstream performance house, museum or theater which derives less than 20 percent of its gross annual income from the sale of alcoholic beverages.

Sec. 6-15. - Alcoholic beverages prohibited in certain areas.
Alcoholic beverages, beer, and controlled substances are prohibited in or upon any park or other public property, building, or facility owned or operated by the city displaying such signs announcing the prohibition against alcoholic beverages, beer, and controlled substances.
