Sec. 28-1. - Penalty for violation of chapter.
Except as otherwise provided, any person violating any of the provisions of this chapter shall be guilty of an offense and, upon conviction, shall be punished as provided in the general penalties provisions appearing in section 1-12.
(Code 1965, ch. 13, § 13; Code 1996, § 54-1)
Sec. 28-2. - Leading and riding animals on sidewalks.
No person shall be permitted to lead, ride or drive any horse, mule or cattle of any kind upon any sidewalk of this city.
(Code 1965, ch. 13, § 8; Code 1996, § 54-3)
Sec. 28-3. - Use of closed streets; removal or destruction of barricade.
No person shall walk or drive any vehicle or drive or lead any animal over any street in the city which has been closed or blocked for the purpose of paving or repairing, nor shall any person remove or tear down any obstruction or barricade put upon any street or alley for the purpose of warning or notifying persons that such street or alley is closed.
(Code 1965, ch. 13, § 12; Code 1996, § 54-7)
Sec. 28-4. - Obstructions—Generally.
No person shall be allowed or permitted to obstruct any sidewalk or street in the city by putting any box, table steps, merchandise or any other thing thereon or to use the streets and sidewalks for the display of goods, wares or merchandise of any kind in such a manner as to impede pedestrian or vehicular traffic. Nor shall the streets or sidewalks be obstructed in any other manner whatsoever.
(Code 1965, ch. 13, § 14; Code 1996, § 54-8)
Sec. 28-5. - Same—By trash, lumber, wood, boxes, bricks or other substances.
No person shall put any trash, lumber, wood, boxes, bricks or other substances in any street, alley or sidewalk without a permit from the city manager or his designee.
(Code 1965, ch. 13, § 15; Code 1996, § 54-9)
Sec. 28-6. - Same—Danger signals to be placed as warning to public.
Any person who shall cause any obstruction to be placed in any street, alley or sidewalk of the city, as permitted by the city, shall display at such obstruction every night such obstruction may so remain, a sufficient danger signal to warn the traveling public of such obstruction and to enable them to avoid injury from the obstruction.
(Code 1965, ch. 13, § 16; Code 1996, § 54-10)
Sec. 28-7. - Obstruction of view and use—By trees, bushes and shrubs.
No person owning, occupying or in any way controlling property in this city shall permit any tree, bush or shrub on such property to project over any street or sidewalk of this city, so as to obstruct or interfere with the view and use of persons walking or riding in a vehicle on such street or sidewalk or of other persons or vehicles approaching from cross or intersecting streets.
(Code 1965, ch. 13, § 17; Code 1996, § 54-11)
Sec. 28-8. - Same—Failure to remove after notice.
It shall be unlawful for any person, whether the owner, tenant, agent or person controlling property in the city, to fail to remove any tree, bush or other obstruction from the streets or sidewalks of this city after ten days' notice by the police department.
(Code 1965, ch. 13, § 18; Code 1996, § 54-12)
Sec. 28-9. - Objects of danger; penalty for violation of section.
No person working, living, building or repairing, operating or in any manner of procedure, at any time, shall have on the streets or sidewalks of this city a charged electric line, wire or cord.
(Code 1965, ch. 13, § 19; Code 1996, § 54-13)
Sec. 28-10. - Driveway construction; permit required.
No person shall construct, establish, open, grade, or in any manner form or use a driveway, or other opening from private property into any street, highway or thoroughfare in the city, other than driveways to personal residences, without first obtaining a permit from the city. Before a permit is granted to a person so desiring to establish a driveway, the city manager or his designee will inspect the site, determine the appropriate location, and make any necessary requirements that will be required to be met.
(Code 1965, ch. 13, § 20; Code 1996, § 54-14)
Sec. 28-11. - Driveway culverts.
All driveway culverts must be installed so as not to impede the natural flow of water through the existing drain. Water and electricity will be withheld from houses until this section is complied with.
(Code 1965, ch. 13, § 22; Code 1996, § 54-15)
