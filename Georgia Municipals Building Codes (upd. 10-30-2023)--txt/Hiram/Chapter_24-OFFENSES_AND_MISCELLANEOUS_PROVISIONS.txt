Sec. 24-1. - Camping in public.
No person shall camp in the streets, sidewalks or public parks of the city without permission from the governing authority.
Sec. 24-2. - Building fires in public places.
No person shall build a fire in the streets, sidewalks or city park other than in established barbeque devices without permission from the fire official.
(Code 1965, ch. 10, § 5; Code 1996, § 46-2)
Sec. 24-3. - Fastening bills to poles and trees.
No person shall paste or in any manner fasten any bills, advertisements or any other notices to any telephone, electric or telegraph pole or any shade tree in the city.
(Code 1965, ch. 10, § 9; Code 1996, § 46-3)
Sec. 24-4. - Firearms and BB guns.
No person shall fire a gun, pistol or other firearm, or fire or discharge any airgun, commonly known as a BB gun, in the city.
(Code 1965, ch. 10, § 10; Code 1996, § 46-4)
Sec. 24-5. - Disorderly conduct.
It shall be unlawful and disorderly conduct for any person to:
(1)
Act in a violent or tumultuous manner toward another, whereby a reasonable person would be placed in fear of safety for life or limb;
(2)
Place the property of another in serious danger of being destroyed or damaged;
(3)
Use fighting words directed toward another, who becomes outraged and thus creates a turmoil;
(4)
Violently interfere with another's pursuit of a lawful occupation;
(5)
Congregate with others to halt the flow of vehicular or pedestrian traffic and refuse to clear the way when ordered by lawful authority to do so;
(6)
Use any type of profane or lewd language in a public place that is offensive to another, causes another to become outraged and thus creates a turmoil; or
(7)
Urinate, defecate, or release bodily fluids in any public area. As used in this subsection, the term "public area" means sidewalks, parking lots, streets, parks, trails or any area in which the public would reasonably be expected to frequent or visit.
State Law reference— Authority to punish disorderly conduct, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-34.
Sec. 24-6. - Public drunkenness.
It shall be unlawful for any person to appear in an intoxicated condition on any public street or highway or within the curtilage of any private residence not in exclusive possession of the person so intoxicated, or upon any railway passenger train or other public conveyance, or at or on ballgrounds, parks and swimming pools, or at any other place or public gathering or assembly, where such drunkenness is caused by the excessive use of intoxicating beverages or opiates. Such drunkenness must be made manifest by boisterousness or by indecent condition or behavior or by vulgar, profane, or unbecoming language, or loud and violent discourse of the person so intoxicated.
(Code 1965, ch. 10, § 24; Code 1996, § 46-8)
State Law reference— Public drunkenness, O.C.G.A. § 16-11-41.
Sec. 24-7. - Resisting an officer.
It shall be unlawful for any person to oppose or resist any police officer of this city in the discharge of his duties.
(Code 1965, ch. 10, § 25; Code 1996, § 46-9)
State Law reference— Hindering apprehension of criminals, O.C.G.A. § 16-10-50; fleeing or attempting to elude police officer, O.C.G.A. § 40-6-395.
Sec. 24-8. - Theft or injury of city property.
No person shall take, mar, deface, soil or in any manner injure, disturb or interfere with any city property.
(Code 1965, ch. 10, § 29; Code 1996, § 46-13)
State Law reference— Damaging, injuring or interfering with utilities, O.C.G.A. § 16-7-25.
Sec. 24-9. - Public nudity.
It shall be unlawful for any person over four years of age within the incorporated area of the city to knowingly or intentionally appear in a state of nudity in any public place including, but not limited to, establishments that accommodate the public. The term "nudity" means the showing of the human male or female genitals, pubic area, or buttocks with less than a fully opaque covering, the showing of the female breast with less than a fully opaque covering of any part of the nipple, or the showing of the covered male genitals in a discernibly turgid state. Any person who violates this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-12.
(Code 1996, § 46-16; Ord. No. 91-40, 9-3-1991)
Sec. 24-10. - Hours of operation for city parks.
(a)
The hours of operation for all city parks are from 7:00 a.m. until 11:00 p.m. Only authorized city employees or persons engaged in activities authorized by the city manager, or the designee thereof, are permitted in any city park outside of hours of operation.
(b)
It shall be unlawful for any person, except a properly authorized city employee, to be in or on any city park property beyond the established hours of operation without permission from the city manager, or the designee thereof.
(c)
It shall be unlawful for any person to disregard an order from a city police officer or code enforcement officer to leave city park property.
(d)
Any person who violates this section shall be punished as provided in section 1-12.
(Ord. No. 2018-03, § 1, 4-3-2018)
