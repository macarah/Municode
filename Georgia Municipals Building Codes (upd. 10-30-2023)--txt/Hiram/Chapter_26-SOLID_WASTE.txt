ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 26-1. - Unauthorized accumulations.
No person shall keep or store garbage, trash or solid waste outside of any residence or building within the city, except in proper containers for collection or otherwise prepared, as set forth in article II of this chapter or under the express prior approval of the city manager or his designee. No garbage, trash or solid waste improperly contained will be collected by the city garbage collection service. Any unauthorized accumulation of solid waste is further hereby declared to be a nuisance and is prohibited. The city manager or his designee, by written notice, shall notify the owner or occupant of any premises with such garbage, trash or solid waste accumulations to remove such accumulations. Failure to remove such accumulations within two weeks of the date of such written notice to do so shall be deemed a violation of this chapter and subject to penalties set forth in the general penalties described in section 1-12. A separate violation shall be deemed committed each day during or upon which such unlawful accumulation continues.
(Code 1996, § 50-1; Ord. of 3-3-1980)

Sec. 26-21. - Containers; specifications; replacement.
Garbage containers shall be provided by the owner or occupant of each residence or establishment sufficient in number to accommodate such waste generated by the residence or establishment between collections. Containers shall be of durable metal or plastic, watertight, nonabsorbent, rust resistant, rodentproof, and easily cleanable. They shall be equipped with handles and tightly fitted covers and shall have a capacity of not less than 15 gallons nor more than 55 gallons. Containers failing to comply with this section, or which have defects causing them to scatter waste upon the ground during collection, or causing them to hamper or to injure the persons collecting waste shall be promptly replaced.
(Code 1996, § 50-31; Ord. of 3-3-1980)
Sec. 26-22. - Collection; monthly charge.
Garbage, trash and solid waste shall be collected from residential premises weekly at a monthly charge as may be set from time to time by resolution of the governing authority and set forth in the schedule of fees and charges.
(Code 1996, § 50-32; Ord. of 3-3-1980)
