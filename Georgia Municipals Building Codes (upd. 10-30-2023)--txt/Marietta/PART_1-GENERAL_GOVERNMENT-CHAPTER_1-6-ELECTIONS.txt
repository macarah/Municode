CHAPTER 1-6 - ELECTIONS

1-6-010 - Election officials.
The city council shall by resolution appoint a municipal election superintendent, election manager, registrars, absentee ballot clerk, and any other officials as are necessary, all of whom shall exercise those powers and duties set forth in O.C.G.A. § 21-3-33 and other applicable provisions of the Georgia Municipal Election Code as now exists or as hereafter amended.
(Code 1978, § 1-2001; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
1-6-020 - Filing notice of candidacy; date.
Filing of notice of candidacy for municipal office shall be conducted in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 21-3-91 as amended. The qualifying period shall commence no earlier than 8:30 a.m. on the second Monday in September immediately preceding the general election and shall end no later than 4:30 p.m. on the following Wednesday.
(Code 1978, § 1-2002; Ord. No. 4167, 6/12/85; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
1-6-030 - Electors list.
The county board of registrars shall deliver to the chief registrar of the municipality, upon a basis mutually agreed upon between the board of registrars and the governing authority of the municipality, a copy of the list of electors for the municipality for the primary or election. Such list shall be delivered at least fourteen (14) days prior to such primary or election for the purpose of permitting the chief registrar to check the accuracy of the list and to challenge the disqualified. The municipal registrar shall, upon receipt of the county registration list, or as soon as practicable thereafter but in no event later than five days prior to such primary or election, purge such list of the names of all persons who will not be qualified to vote at such primary or election. In addition, the county registrars shall provide a list of inactive electors for the municipality. The registrar shall certify such lists and file with the city clerk a copy showing the names of electors entitled to vote at such primary or election in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 21-3-123(e) as amended.
(Code 1978, § 1-2003; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)

All challenges to the right of registration of any person whose name appears upon the electors' list shall be heard and decided by the Cobb County Board of Registrars following the same procedures used to hear challenges in all other elections held within Cobb County and supervised by the Cobb County Board of Registrars.

1-6-050 - Qualification fees.
The qualifications fee for candidates filing a notice of candidacy in any general or special election shall be in an amount as set forth in O.C.G.A § 21-3-90 as the same now exists or may hereafter be amended. Said fee shall be received by the municipal election superintendent at the time of filing the notice of candidacy and paid over to the city.
A.
At least two weeks prior to the opening of qualifications for a special or general election, the city council shall fix and publish a qualification fee, to be paid by candidates seeking election, and such fee shall be paid to the municipal superintendent at the time a candidate files the candidate's notice of candidacy, and paid over to the city. Such fee shall be three percent of the annual salary of the office if a salaried office. If not a salaried office, a reasonable fee shall be set by the city council, not to exceed thirty-five dollars ($35.00) for such office.
B.
A pauper's affidavit in accordance with O.C.G.A. § 21-3-90 may be filed in lieu of paying the qualifying fee on a form by the Secretary of State. The municipal superintendent shall place on the ballot the name of any candidate who subscribes and swears under oath that such candidate has neither the assets nor the income to pay the qualifying fee.
(Code 1978, § 1-2005; Ord. No. 4189, 8/14/85; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)

1-6-070 - Age to qualify for office lowered to eighteen.
Any person eighteen (18) years of age or older shall be qualified and eligible to serve in any elective office of the city.
(Code 1978, § 1-2007; Ord. No. 5575, 6/12/96)
