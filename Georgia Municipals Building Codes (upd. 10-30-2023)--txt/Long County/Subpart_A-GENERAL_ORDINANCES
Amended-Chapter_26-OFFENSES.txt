ARTICLE II. - FIREARMS[1]

Sec. 26-19. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this article, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
County-owned property means any real estate in which the county owns an interest-fee simple, leasehold, or otherwise and that is also designated as real estate owned by the county. This includes, but is not limited to, county office complexes, courthouse or recreational areas.
Firearm means any handgun, shotgun, or rifle which expels a projectile by action of an explosion.
Night means between the hours of 30 minutes after sunset and 30 minutes before sunrise.
Prohibited discharge area , subject to the exceptions outlined hereafter, means any and all real estate located within the unincorporated area of the county zoned as R-1 Single-Family Residential District; R-2 Single-Family Residential District; MFR Multi-Family Residential District; MHP Manufactured Home Park District; C-1 Neighborhood Commercial District; C-2 General Commercial District; I-1 Light Industrial; and I-2 General Industrial; or any lot of less than four acres, zoned RR Rural Residential.
(Ord. No. 2014-06-03b, § 1, 5-6-2014)
Sec. 26-20. - Jurisdiction.
This article shall be effective and applicable in the county, outside the corporate limits of any municipality therein.
(Ord. No. 2014-06-03b, § 2, 5-6-2014)
Sec. 26-21. - Firing of weapons in prohibited area.
It shall be unlawful for any person to discharge a firearm in any prohibited discharge area, unless such person is a law enforcement officer discharging his duties, or a citizen defending his home, property, person or liberty against threatened or imminent attack.
(Ord. No. 2014-06-03b, § 3, 5-6-2014)
Sec. 26-22. - Firing a weapon at night.
It shall be unlawful for any person to discharge a firearm at night, unless such person is a law enforcement officer discharging his duties, or a citizen defending his home, property, person or liberty against threatened or imminent attack, or is permitted to discharge a firearm by regulations of the state department of natural resources.
(Ord. No. 2014-06-03b, § 4, 5-6-2014)
Sec. 26-23. - Exceptions.
Excepted from this article are each of the following:
(1)
Turkey shoots. Permitted turkey shoots are defined as those authorized in advance by county code enforcement after submission by the applicant of a form designated by the county code enforcement office, and payment of a fee of $10.00.
(2)
Approved areas. Permitted shooting areas are defined as shooting areas inspected and approved by the county sheriff's department as to safety.
(3)
Hunting. Hunting is defined as the lawful taking of birds or animals as authorized by state law and other applicable game laws.
(Ord. No. 2014-06-03b, § 5, 5-6-2014)
