ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 22-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Casket means an ornamented and lined rectangular box used to place human remains in for burial.
Cemetery means the Blackshear Cemetery, as shown and depicted on all recorded and unrecorded plats.
Cemetery superintendent means an employee of the city designated by the city manager to supervise cemetery operations.
Coping means a raised border surrounding grave spaces or lots.
Cremains or cremated remains means residual ash fragments of human bones remaining after the process of cremation.
Cremation means the process of reducing human remains to ash through incineration.
Deceased human being or human remains or remains means the body or bodies of deceased persons, including bodies in any stage of decomposition and cremated remains.
Deed means the document issued only by the city that conveys ownership interest in a lot in the cemetery.
Deferred payment plan means a method of paying for a lot or lots by first depositing a portion of the total cost of the lot or lots with the city with the stipulation that the balance will be paid within 12 months.
Disinterment means removal of human remains from a grave.
Grave or grave space means an area of ground sufficient for the burial of human remains. See also lot.
Indigent means a destitute person.
Interment means burial of human remains in a grave.
Lot means one or more adjoining grave spaces.
Lot owner means one or more individuals whose names are shown on the deed. Any individual names on the deed may give written instructions to the city.
Marker means a small individual memorial placed at either the head or foot of a grave.
Memorial means a marker, monument or vase placed on a lot and shall include any monument, memorial, marker, or vase in whole or in part at ground level or above ground, or other structure on the cemetery lot.
Monument means a large slab placed flat on a lot.
Urn means a receptacle of cast bronze, marble, or other acceptable permanent material containing cremated remains, which is placed in the ground.
Vault means a reinforced concrete container used in the above or below ground burial of human remains in which the casket is placed.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 1, 7-9-13)
Sec. 22-2. - Reserved.
Sec. 22-3. - No liability for damages.
The city will not be held responsible for any vandalism or damages to any memorial, monument, urn or marker.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 1, 7-9-13)
Sec. 22-4. - Management; appointment of cemetery superintendent.
The control and management of the cemetery shall be under the direct supervision of the mayor, who shall administer this chapter. He shall assign a city employee as cemetery superintendent to take charge of burials and ensure the operational integrity of the cemetery.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 1, 7-9-13)
Sec. 22-5. - Duties of cemetery superintendent.
The cemetery superintendent shall issue burial permits and refuse burial until the permit and related or required documents comply with state law and the provisions of the City Code. The cemetery superintendent shall inspect the closed grave to insure that the remaining fill has been removed from the gravesite. The cemetery superintendent shall do all other work required in the operation of the cemetery.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 1, 7-9-13)
Sec. 22-6. - Executing instructions.
(a)
In all cases where a lot is owned by more than one person, the city may act upon the authority of any one of the owners. If all lot owners desire that no instructions be followed unless signed by all lot owners shown on the deed, all lot owners must submit a signed and notarized statement with that request.
(b)
All matters relating to the opening of graves, the interment of remains, disinterments, and reinterments shall be in writing from the lot owner. The city shall not execute instructions given by any other means.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 1, 7-9-13)
Sec. 22-7. - City's right to correct errors.
(a)
The city shall have the right to correct any errors that may be made by it in good faith by taking such actions as it deems necessary and appropriate in the circumstances. If the error shall involve the interment of remains, the city shall have the right, at its expense, to remove and reinter the remains to another lot of equal value and similar location.
(b)
The city shall have the right to correct any error it may make in placement of any memorial, marker, vase, or vault. The city shall not be liable for any error so made.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 1, 7-9-13)
Sec. 22-8. - Fees, gratuities and commissions.
No city employee shall receive any fee, gratuity or commission either directly or indirectly, except from the city or work performed in the cemetery. No city employee shall influence the city in regard to any allied service or commodity under penalty of immediate dismissal.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 1, 7-9-13)
Secs. 22-9—22-35. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - LOTS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 22-36. - Subdivision of cemetery.
The Blackshear City Council shall have sole authority to subdivide the cemetery into divisions, blocks, sections, lots, grave spaces, and streets, and to determine the dimensions and locations of each.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 2, 7-9-13)
Sec. 22-37. - City's right to replat, regrade and use property.
(a)
The city reserves the right to replat any parts of the property within the cemetery not sold for burial purposes, including the right to open, close, or change the location or grade of streets or walkways. The city shall have a perpetual right of ingress and egress over lots for the purpose of passage to and from other lots when no other means of passage is available.
(b)
The city reserves the right to close and sell the grounds lying between lots, known as walkways, provided that by said closing adequate access to adjoining lots is provided.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 2, 7-9-13)
Sec. 22-38. - Sale, place of payment; cemetery fund.
(a)
Sale of lots. The sale of lots shall be by deed. A comprehensive record of the cemetery deeds, plats, and other records related thereto, shall be kept by the city clerk. The record shall show all lots which are sold or are for sale and a record made of the date, name of the purchaser, division or block, section, lot and grave number. A deed shall be issued upon the receipt of full payment, naming any special restrictions, which are not embodied in this chapter, and signed by the proper city officials.
(b)
Place of payment; cemetery fund. All payments for the sale of lots and all service charges shall be payable to the city at the Blackshear City Hall. All executed deeds shall be delivered to the owner through the city clerk. All receipts from the sale of cemetery lots, burial fees, and beautification services shall be placed in the cemetery fund. Money placed in this fund shall only be used for cemetery purposes.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 2, 7-9-13)
Sec. 22-39. - Charges; bulk purchases.
(a)
The price for the purchase of grave space shall be according to the following schedule:
(1)
All grave spaces located in Blackshear Cemetery shall be sold for a sum to be set by resolution of the Blackshear City Council.
(2)
The city will provide a grave space for an indigent who is a city resident. Proof of residency and indigency shall be provided by signed affidavits, provided by the funeral home. The grave space will be determined by cemetery superintendent.
(b)
Any cemetery lot purchase in excess of eight lots requires written justification by the applicant and approval by the mayor.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 2, 7-9-13)
Sec. 22-40. - Deferred payment plan.
(a)
A prospective purchaser of grave lots may purchase up to four lots on a deferred payment plan. A written agreement will be required. Upon signing the agreement, a payment of one-fourth of the total cost of all the lots to be purchased shall be required. The balance will be due within 12 months. No interest shall be required during the initial, 12-month term of the agreement. If the balance is not paid in full within 12 months, one extension of six months may be allowed, and interest may be charged. In the case of an agreement for multiple lots, payments made will be credited to an individual lot, and when sufficient payments have been received to cover the cost of one lot, a deed may be issued for that lot.
(b)
If the purchaser fails to make full payment within the time period allocated, the city reserves the right to cancel the agreement. At the time of cancellation of the agreement, the city may list the grave lots for sale, and the purchaser shall have no right to any of the lots covered under the agreement. A deed will be issued for any lots which have been paid for in full during the term of the agreement. If the agreement has been cancelled for nonpayment, and the city has not received enough money to pay for a lot in full, the city may impose a cancellation fee to be established by the Blackshear City Council by resolution, and will then return any balance to the purchaser.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 2, 7-9-13)
Sec. 22-41. - Reserved.
Sec. 22-42. - Correct mailing address of lot owners.
Each lot owner shall have the duty to keep the city informed as to his or her correct mailing address. Any notice mailed to a lot owner at the last address on file with the city shall be equivalent to actual delivery of the notice.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 2, 7-9-13)
Sec. 22-43. - Transfer of ownership.
(a)
No lot owner shall allow interment in his or her lot for a remuneration, nor shall any transfer of interest in the lot be valid except by written consent of the city. Such transfer or assignment shall be recorded in the records of the cemetery. No transfer for consideration shall be authorized. Any attempted transfer of a lot or interest in a lot to one not authorized to own the lot shall be invalid and of no force and effect. The city shall not be obligated to honor the transfer.
(b)
The city may refuse to consent to a transfer or to an assignment as long as there is any indebtedness due the city from the lot owner for interment rights in the lot involved.
(c)
In every case where a lot owner wishes to dispose of vacant lots, the city shall have the option to repurchase the lot at a price agreed upon between the lot owner and the official representative of the city. The price shall not exceed 75 percent of the original purchase price, including perpetual care.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 2, 7-9-13)
Sec. 22-44. - Decorations.
(a)
No permanent flower receptacles may be placed on any lot unless, of an approved size, material and design, set wholly beneath the level of the grass. Only one vase will be permitted for each marker or grave space.
(b)
Potted plants, wreaths, floral arrangements, metal emblems, markers, or flags may be permitted upon lots on holidays. If not removed within five days, said decorations may, at the option of the city, be removed. Potted plants, wreaths and floral arrangements will be removed from the lot or grave at the time of mowing or trimming the grass.
(c)
The city shall have the right to remove all floral arrangements, wreaths and the like as soon after a funeral as in the judgment of the city they become wilted or unsightly.
(d)
The city shall not be responsible for any items of decoration that become lost, misplaced or damaged.
(e)
The city shall not be responsible for plantings damaged by the elements, thieves, vandals, or by other causes beyond its control.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 2, 7-9-13)
Secs. 22-45—22-55. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PERPETUAL CARE

Sec. 22-56. - Perpetual care trust fund.
There is established a perpetual care trust fund known as the Blackshear Cemetery Trust Fund. All money received from the owners of lots in the ancient section for perpetual care, together with 65 percent of the money received from the sale of the lots, shall be paid to the city and deposited in the fund. The city shall invest such funds in accordance with city investment policy. The income from the trust fund shall be used exclusively for the care and maintenance of the lots and cemetery grounds.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 2, 7-9-13)
Sec. 22-57. - Perpetual care services; fees.
(a)
Perpetual care service shall consist of cutting, trimming, top dressing, and annual fertilizing of all grass area. Any additional care desired will be the responsibility of the owner. All owners of grave spaces for which perpetual care has not been previously included shall purchase perpetual care prior to the opening and closing of a grave and shall pay the city a sum to be set by resolution of the Blackshear City Council.
(b)
Upon the payment of the perpetual care fee, the cemetery superintendent or designee is authorized to deliver to the purchaser a certificate evidencing the purchase, signed by him or her on behalf of the city. Full perpetual care payment for a grave space or lot must be paid prior to interment.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 2, 7-9-13)
Secs. 22-58—22-80. - Reserved.
ARTICLE III. - INTERMENT

Sec. 22-81. - Burial permit required; cost.
(a)
It shall be unlawful for any undertaker or other person legally entitled to bury the body of a deceased human being without first securing from the cemetery superintendent a burial permit signed by the cemetery superintendent or his or her designee. A burial permit shall be issued only when all laws, ordinances, rules and regulations relative to burials have been complied with and all fees and costs previously billed by the city have been paid. The cemetery superintendent may request a properly executed death certificate or a burial transit permit of the decedent. The burial permit shall show the name, address, sex, age, place of burial, and any other details and information as the cemetery superintendent may require.
(b)
The charge for a burial permit shall be a sum set by resolution of the Blackshear City Council.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 3, 7-9-13)
Sec. 22-82. - Prerequisites to burial; charges; minimum depth of graves.
(a)
The opening and closing of all graves shall be conducted under the supervision of the city. No burial shall be performed in the cemetery without the completion of state prescribed burial forms by a sub-registrar. A burial permit shall be obtained from the cemetery superintendent before the interment of any human remains.
(b)
To verify that a monument is installed on the proper grave and to mark and inspect the installation of a monument, marker, memorial or coping, there shall be a fee set by resolution of the Blackshear City Council.
(c)
The minimum depth of all graves shall be 4½ feet; the minimum depth for vaults shall be 3½ feet.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 3, 7-9-13)
Sec. 22-83. - Regulations for interments and disinterments.
(a)
All interments and disinterments shall be made subject to state law, city ordinances, and operating rules and regulations of the cemetery.
(b)
No interment will be made without written authority from the owner or one of the owners of the lot in question. If there is more than one owner, no disinterment will be made without the written consent of all owners or upon presentation of a court order. Disinterments shall be made at the city's convenience.
(c)
All disinterments shall be done by or under the supervision of the cemetery superintendent. The cemetery will exercise the utmost care in making a removal, but it assumes no liability for damage to any casket or urn incurred in the process of making a removal.
(d)
When instructions regarding the location of an interment space in a lot cannot be obtained, are indefinite, or when for any reason the interment space cannot be opened where specified, the city may, in its discretion, open a space in another location in the lot as it deems best and proper, so as not to delay the funeral.
(e)
Not more than one body shall be interred in any one grave space, except in cases where a parent and infant child, or two infant children, are buried at the same time. In such cases, only one memorial shall be permitted; however, the memorial may bear both names. Cremated remains may be interred at the foot of a grave space. The name of the person may be combined with an adjoining single marker, or incorporated into a companion marker for both graves. Two cremated remains instead of one body may be interred into a grave space. In that case, a single marker designating the two cremated remains may be installed in the grave space.
(f)
No interment of any body or the cremated remains of any body, other than that of a human being, shall be permitted.
(g)
Removal, by the heirs, of a body so that the lot may be sold for profit to themselves, or removal contrary to the expressed or implied wish of the original lot owners shall not be permitted.
(h)
Besides being subject to the rules and regulations set out in this article, all interments, disinterments, and removals are subject to the orders and laws of the properly constituted authorities of the city, Pierce County and the State of Georgia.
(i)
At the expense of the family a body or cremated remains may be removed from its original lot to a larger or more desirable lot in the cemetery where there has been an exchange or purchase for that purpose.
(j)
Once a casket containing a body is within the confines of the cemetery, no funeral director, embalmer, assistant, employee, or agent shall be permitted to open a casket or to touch the body without the consent of the legal representative of the deceased, a relative in charge of the funeral, or a court order.
(k)
All interments and disinterments must be made at the time, in the manner, and upon the charges as fixed from time to time by the city.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 3, 7-9-13)
Secs. 22-84—22-105. - Reserved.
ARTICLE IV. - REGULATIONS

Sec. 22-106. - Visitors; proper conduct.
(a)
Visiting hours are restricted to daylight hours and shall be as specified by the city. Any unauthorized person(s) found in the cemetery after dark will be considered a trespasser.
(b)
In order that the proper atmosphere may be maintained at the cemetery, children under 15 years of age, unless attended by an adult responsible for their conduct, and persons having firearms in their possession, except military personnel, law enforcement personnel and honor guards in attendance upon a funeral, are prohibited from entering the cemetery.
(c)
In order to safeguard the quiet and peaceful atmosphere of the cemetery visitors are prohibited from using loud, boisterous or obscene language from sitting or climbing on markers, monuments, or fixtures of any kind; from peddling, begging, soliciting, or collecting; and from displaying or distributing cards, handbills and circulars of any nature, unless approved by the cemetery superintendent.
(d)
Visitors are prohibited from bringing into the cemetery dogs, other than guide dogs for the handicapped, and alcoholic beverages. Visitors are prohibited from removing, scratching, marring, defacing, injuring, or disturbing any monument, memorial bench, vase, building, fence, or anything within the limits of the cemetery; and from putting paper, rubbish, dead or wilted flowers, shrubs, plants, or any other unsightly or improper thing in any place except in the containers provided for such materials. Persons within the cemetery shall use only the walks and roads. No person shall gather flowers, damage trees, shrubbery or plants.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 4, 7-9-13)
Sec. 22-107. - Planting of trees, shrubs and other improvements.
(a)
The city undertakes to maintain, as nearly as may be practical, the plantings of trees and shrubs which serve as a backdrop on the cemetery grounds and to preserve the landscape features, but it does not undertake to maintain plants upon individual lots.
(b)
Permanent planting of grass, shrubs, flowers, and the like shall be made by the city. Further planting may be permitted only at the discretion of the city and with a permit issued by the cemetery superintendent. The ownership of lots or grave spaces does not convey any right to do planting without the express approval of the city.
(c)
No elevated mounds shall be built over graves and no lot shall be filled above the grade established by the cemetery superintendent.
(d)
If any tree, shrub or plant standing upon any lot, by means of its roots, branches or otherwise, be or become detrimental to adjacent lots, walks or streets, or if for any reason its removal is deemed necessary, the cemetery superintendent shall have the right and duty to remove the tree, plant or shrub or any part thereof, or otherwise correct the existing condition.
(e)
No planting of flowers, shrubs, or herbage of any kind is permitted on single graves. All improvements and planting on lots shall be subject to prior approval of the cemetery superintendent. Any improvement or planting made on a lot by or for a lot owner without prior approval may be removed by the cemetery superintendent at the expense of the lot owner.
(f)
No trees, shrubs, plants, or herbage of any kind shall be removed from any lot without the prior consent of the cemetery superintendent.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 4, 7-9-13)
Sec. 22-108. - Vehicle operation.
(a)
All motor vehicles must be operated within the limits of the cemetery in a quiet, safe and orderly manner. Vehicles shall be driven at not more than 15 miles per hour, shall be driven on streets provided, and shall be operated in accordance with the laws of the city and the state. All vehicles shall be parked on the streets in a manner that will permit other vehicles to pass without leaving the street.
(b)
Except in the performance of duties within the cemetery grounds or in unusual circumstances, vehicles other than passenger cars and funeral cars, and in particular, trucks, bicycles, motorcycles, and motorbikes, are prohibited from entering the cemetery gates.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 4, 7-9-13)
Sec. 22-109. - Memorial and vault installations.
All memorial work shall be done between the hours of 8:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m. on weekdays. Persons in charge of memorial work shall notify the cemetery superintendent prior to entering the cemetery to perform the work, and must procure the required permit in accordance with subsection 22-82(b) for each setting. All memorial work must comply with the standards as provided by the cemetery superintendent.
(Ord. No. 2013-09, § 4, 7-9-13)
Secs. 22-110—22-135. - Reserved.
