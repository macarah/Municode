ARTICLE III. - AUCTIONEERS AND AUCTIONS[3]

State Law reference— Auctioneers generally, O.C.G.A. § 43-6-1 et seq.; regulation by political subdivisions, O.C.G.A. § 43-6-25.1.

Sec. 10-161. - Applicability of article.
This article shall apply to all auctions, auctioneers, auction houses and any city licenses issued for such business activity.
(Code 1976, § 7-3001; Code 1985, § 6-110)
Sec. 10-162. - Auctioneer's license exemption.
Auctioneers required to obtain separate licenses under the existing license schedule are exempt from the requirement of obtaining such license provided they are employed by a licensed auction house in the city.
(Code 1976, § 7-3002; Code 1985, § 6-111)
Sec. 10-163. - Auction house parking; limit on number of days.
No auction house shall be licensed hereunder unless it shall have available no less than 25 parking places reserved for its customers and unless it limits its conduct of auctions to no more than two days per week.
(Code 1976, § 7-3003; Code 1985, § 6-112)
Sec. 10-164. - Auction license; days per week limited; false advertising.
(a)
Except for judicial auctions or auctions under a power contained in a will or trust, no auction shall be conducted in the city unless a license therefor is first obtained from the community affairs department. If the auctions are limited to no more than one day, an auctioneer's license as otherwise provided will be sufficient. In the event auctions are to be conducted on a regular basis or more than once a year, an auction house license must be obtained before any auction can be held.
(b)
All auctions shall be in accordance with O.C.G.A. ch. 43-6. In addition, no auction shall be conducted more than two days per week in the city and all such auctions must be conducted on the premises of the licensed auction house.
(c)
No person shall make any statement or advertisement regarding an auction or the merchandise to be sold therein or the conditions or terms of sale, which advertisement or statement contains any assertion or representation which is untrue, misleading or deceptive.
(Code 1976, § 7-3004; Code 1985, § 6-113)
Sec. 10-165. - State sales tax return.
Each auction house upon applying for reissuance of its annual license shall attach thereto copies of its state sales tax return showing its gross sales for the year. The sales tax return shall be certified under oath by the applicant or applicant's accountant.
(Code 1976, § 7-3005; Code 1985, § 6-114)
