ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 4-1. - Definitions.
The following words, terms and phrases, when used in this chapter, and in addition to the definitions provided in section 1-2, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Administrator means the officer or employee designated to administer the provisions of this chapter.
Alcohol means ethyl alcohol, hydrated oxide of ethyl, or spirits of wine, from whatever source or by whatever process produced.
Alcoholic beverage means and includes all alcohol, distilled spirits, beer, malt beverage, wine or fortified wine.
Business area means any street length between intersections where 50 percent or more is in use for business purposes and the area is properly zoned in accordance with the city zoning ordinances.
Church means any permanent building, together with the tract of land upon which it is located, where persons regularly assemble for religious worship, but shall not include any other church-owned land or property not regularly used as an assembly area for religious worship services.
College means only state, county, city, church or other college that teaches the subjects commonly taught in the common colleges of this state and shall not include a private college where only specialized subjects such as law, stenography, business, music, art, medicine, dentistry, vocational occupations and other special subjects are taught.
Distance means the measurement in horizontal lineal feet from the front door of the proposed licensee's premises in a straight line which describes the shortest distance to the nearest church, school ground or college campus.
Distilled spirits means any alcoholic beverage obtained by distillation or containing more than 24 percent alcohol by volume, including, but not limited to, all fortified wines.
Family means and includes any person related to the holder of such license within the first degree of consanguinity or affinity computed according to the civil law.
Fortified wines means any alcoholic beverage containing more than 24 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "fortified wines" includes, but is not limited to, brandy.
Interest in license exists if the person involved is the outright owner of the license, a co-owner of the license, a partner in a partnership which owns all or any part of a license, a stockholder in any corporation organized for pecuniary gain which owns all or any part of a license, an owner, lessor, sublessor or stockholder in any corporation organized for pecuniary gain owning or leasing any real estate which is occupied by a liquor establishment or shares in any of the income or corpus of any trust fund or estate having any interest in a liquor establishment. Provided, however, that a stockholder shall not be deemed to have an interest in a liquor establishment where such stockholder owns any stock in a motel or hotel having 50 or more rooms with a liquor establishment located on the premises of such motel or hotel and owned by such motel or hotel. Notwithstanding any of the other provisions of this section, no one person shall have more than one interest in any one license in the city.
License means the authorization by the governing authority of the city to engage in the sale and consumption of alcoholic beverages on the premises.
Licensee means a person holding any class of license issued under the terms of this chapter.
Lounge means a separate room connected with a part of and adjacent to a restaurant, as defined herein, with all booths, stools and tables being unobstructed and open to view. All lounges shall be air conditioned and have a seating capacity of at least 75 except as provided in section 4-125.
Malt beverage means any alcoholic beverage obtained by the fermentation of any infusion or decoction of barley, malt, hops, or any other similar product, or any combination of such products in water, containing nor more than 14 percent alcohol by volume and including ale, porter, brown, stout, lager beer, small beer, and strong beer. The term "malt beverage" does not include sake, known as Japanese rice wine.
Manufacturer means any maker, producer, or bottler of an alcoholic beverage. The term "manufacturer" also means:
(1)
In the case of distilled spirits, any person engaged in distilling, rectifying, or blending any distilled spirits; provided, however, that a vintner that blends wine with distilled spirits to produce a fortified wine shall not be considered a manufacturer of distilled spirits.
(2)
In the case of malt beverages, any brewer.
(3)
In the case of wine, any vintner.
Package means a bottle, can, keg, barrel, or other original consumer container.
Premises means the definite closed or partitioned establishment, whether room, shop or building wherein alcoholic beverages are sold or consumed.
Private club means a corporation organized and existing under the laws of the state, having at least 100 members regularly paying monthly, quarterly, semi-annual or annual dues, organized and operated exclusively for pleasure, recreation, and other nonprofit activities; except as otherwise defined in section 4-125.
Restaurant means any public place kept, used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public as a place where meals are served and where meals are actually and regularly served, such place being provided with adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining room equipment, air conditioned, having employed therein a sufficient number and kind of employees to prepare, cook and serve suitable food for its guests; except as otherwise defined in section 4-125. At least one meal per day shall be served at least six days a week, with the exception of holidays, vacations and periods of redecorating.
Retail means retail sales packaged to go and not for consumption on the premises.
School means only those state, county, city, church or other schools that teach the subjects commonly taught in the common schools of this state and shall include the adjacent open land used for school purposes but this shall not include any portion of a private school where only specialized subjects such as law, stenography, business, music, art, medicine, dentistry, vocational occupations and other special subjects are taught.
Stockholders means and includes the direct, indirect, beneficial and absolute owners of the stock in a corporation and the beneficiaries and trustees of any voting trust for stock. When any stockholder is required to meet any criteria, if the ownership or voting control is so divided, or by any other means avoids or circumvents any requirements of this chapter, the failure of any holder of any such interest or right to meet such criteria shall cause a failure of that entire stockholder share to meet such criteria.
Wine means any alcoholic beverage containing not more than 24 percent alcohol by volume made from fruits, berries, or grapes either by natural fermentation or by natural fermentation with brandy added. The term "wine" includes, but is not limited to, all sparkling wines, champagnes, combinations of such beverages, vermouths, special natural wines, rectified wines, and like products. The term "wine" does not include cooking wine mixed with salt or other ingredients so as to render it unfit for human consumption as a beverage.
(Code 1976, §§ 7-2001, 7-2006(e); Code 1985, § 3-1; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 7-13-1981; Ord. of 9-12-1994, § 1; Ord. of 3-4-1996, §§ I—III)
Secs. 4-2—4-20. - Reserved.
ARTICLE II. - LICENSES AND PERMITS
DIVISION 1. - GENERALLY

Sec. 4-21. - License required; scope of article.
(a)
No malt beverage, wine, distilled spirits or any other alcoholic beverage shall be stored, delivered, sold or manufactured in the city except under a license issued pursuant to this article and then only for the specific beverage and manner of sale provided in the license.
(b)
The requirements of this article shall be in addition to any other requirements for business licenses under this Code, and, if other provisions of this Code conflict with this article, then this article shall control.
(c)
Licenses which may be issued under this article include:
(1)
Manufacturing:
a.
Malt beverages;
b.
Wine;
c.
Distilled spirits;
(2)
Wholesale:
a.
Malt beverages;
b.
Wine;
c.
Distilled spirits;
(3)
Packaged retail:
a.
Malt beverages;
b.
Wine;
c.
Distilled spirits;
(4)
Consumption on the premises:
a.
Malt beverages;
b.
Wine;
c.
Distilled spirits; or
(5)
Combinations:
a.
Any combination of subsection (c)(3)a, b and c of this section;
b.
Any combination of subsection (c)(4)a, b and c of this section;
c.
The combinations of subsections (c)(4)a and (c)(3)a of this section; subsections (c)(4)b and (c)(3)b of this section; subsections (c)(4)a, and b and (c)(3)a, and b of this section; subsections (c)(4)a, b, and c and (c)(3)a, and b of this section; subsections (c)(4)a, and c and (c)(3)a of this section; and subsections (c)(4)b, and c and (c)(3)b of this section.
(6)
Cabaret, in addition to subsection (c)(4) or (c)(5) of this section.
(Code 1976, § 7-2002; Code 1985, § 3-20; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 3-4-1996, § III)
Sec. 4-22. - Applications for new license.
(a)
All applications for new licenses under this article shall be accompanied by a payment in cash or by certified check for all the minimum fees and business taxes provided for in this Code for the classification into which the applicant falls.
(b)
The city application shall be a photostatic or other copy of all the appropriate state applications and forms along with a city supplemental application form provided by the city along with the following:
(1)
A legal description of the property upon which the premises are located;
(2)
A current photograph of the applicant;
(3)
Fingerprints of the applicant to be taken by the police department.
(c)
The application shall also contain a form of oath providing that the information disclosed in the application is true and correct, and providing further that the applicant will abide by, observe and conduct such business according to the rules and regulations prescribed by the city, the acts of the general assembly and the rules and regulations of the state department of revenue in respect thereto. The oath shall be taken by the applicant and the agent in charge of the establishment if different from or additional to the applicant, and the oath shall be taken by the true owners.
(d)
Any false statement or material misrepresentation or omission in any application hereunder shall be grounds for the revocation of any license granted hereunder.
(e)
All applications for new licenses shall include a plat of survey from a registered surveyor showing a scale drawing of the location of the proposed premises and the distance to the property line of the nearest church or school ground or that the distance is more than the limits set forth in section 4-34(a).
(Code 1976, § 7-2003(a)—(f); Code 1985, § 3-21; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 9-12-1994, § 1)
Sec. 4-23. - False statements in applications.
(a)
Any person who acquires a license or a renewal of the same in violation of this article by any misrepresentation or fraudulent statement shall be deemed guilty of an offense and upon conviction thereof shall be punished as provided in section 1-9.
(b)
Any untrue or misleading information contained in, or material omission left out of, an original, renewal, or transfer application for a license shall be cause for the denial thereof and, if any license has been granted under these circumstances, shall be cause for revocation.
(Code 1976, §§ 7-2003(g), (h), 7-2010; Code 1985, § 3-22; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-24. - Preliminary approval required; payment of debts; notice of reasons for denial; data; processing time.
(a)
The community affairs department shall cause an inquiry to be made into the city tax records to determine if the applicant or other parties with interest in the application has any outstanding taxes or special assessments that are delinquent against such persons' property or any other monies owing to the city. No license shall be issued or renewed until such debts are paid in full.
(b)
All applications shall be approved or disapproved by the city as a condition to action thereon and issuance of a license by the community affairs department. In the event that the police department determines that the application is not in order, in view of the requirements of federal or state law or this Code and any police department investigation, the community affairs department shall then notify the applicant of the reasons for not issuing the license to the applicant.
(c)
All applicants shall furnish all reasonable data, information and records requested of them by the community affairs department or police department and failure to furnish such data, information, contracts, documents, financial statements and other records within 30 days of the request shall automatically serve to dismiss the application.
(d)
The police department may require in addition to the licensee, fingerprinting and background investigation of any person interested in the application, including any person with a financial interest, such as stockholders, partners or employees, and any spouse, brother, sister, child or parent of any person with a financial interest. Should the applicant fail to produce such persons within 30 days of the request, the application may be denied by the police department.
(e)
Applicants by filing an application agree to produce for oral interrogation any persons requested by the police department and considered as being important in the ascertainment of the facts relative to such license. The failure to produce those persons within 30 days after being requested to do so may result in a denial of the application by the police department.
(f)
The city may require 45 days for processing any application for a license required under this article. For investigative purposes, the police chief may extend the 45 day processing period according to the licensee's compliance should the chief deem it necessary.
(Code 1976, § 7-2004; Code 1985, § 3-23; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 1-6-1997, § XII)
Sec. 4-25. - Issuance of license.
All licenses must be obtained and business taxes and fees paid not later than two weeks from the date of the approval of the application by the city and, if not so obtained, the license granted by the city shall be void. When a license has been approved and the applicant has deposited with the community affairs department the required taxes and fee, the license shall be issued.
(Code 1976, § 7-2011; Code 1985, § 3-24; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-26. - Managers; work permits for managers, employees, and license holders.
(a)
Every person licensed under this article for package sales of distilled spirits and for all consumption on the premises sales shall employ a person as manager, or a like position, for the licensed establishment and the manager shall obtain a manager's permit as provided in this section. This permit shall be valid for a period of 12 months from the date of issuance.
(b)
Prior to employment as manager or employee, or prior to obtaining an interest in a license, each person becoming so employed or acquiring such interest shall obtain an employee's or manager's work permit, as the case may be, from the city police department by making application therefor on a form provided by the department. The city police department shall have a complete and exhaustive search made relative to any police record of the person fingerprinted.
(c)
Licensees and managers under this section shall keep a copy of the permit on file at all times in the establishment and shall make these permits available for inspection during operating hours.
(d)
No person shall be eligible to receive a permit if such person shall have been convicted or shall have taken a plea of nolo contendere within ten years immediately prior to the filing of the application for any felony or misdemeanor of any state or of the United States or any municipal ordinance except traffic violations; the term "conviction" includes an adjudication of guilty of a plea of guilty or nolo contendere or the forfeiture of a bond when charged with a crime; where the violation is for a misdemeanor, or where there is a forfeiture of bond, or a violation of municipal ordinance, or where there is a plea of nolo contendere, the city may, after investigation, waive the same as a disqualification.
(e)
The managers of the licensee shall post their work permit next to the license. The city police shall keep a list of all such managers and check such posted managers' work permits against such list at any time.
(Code 1976, § 7-2034; Code 1985, § 3-25; Ord. of 1-11-1980; Ord. of 2-1-1982; Ord. of 3-4-1996, § III)
Sec. 4-27. - Licensee and manager disqualification for prior convictions.
No license for the sale of alcoholic beverages, nor any manager's permit or work permit shall be issued to any person where any individual having an interest either as owner, partner, stockholder, directly or indirectly, beneficial or absolute, or such person's spouse, shall have been convicted or shall have taken a plea of nolo contendere within ten years immediately prior to the filing of the application for any felony or misdemeanor of any state or of the United States or any local governmental ordinance except traffic violations; the term "conviction" includes an adjudication of guilty or a plea of guilty or nolo contendere or the forfeiture of a bond when charged with a crime; where the violation is for a misdemeanor, or there is a forfeiture of bond, or violation of a municipal ordinance, or where there is a plea of nolo contendere, the city may, after investigation, waive the same as a disqualification. The term "traffic violation" herein shall not include driving under the influence.
(Code 1976, § 7-2005; Code 1985, § 3-26; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-28. - Prohibited interests.
(a)
No person who has any direct financial interest in a license for the sale of alcoholic beverages at wholesale shall hold any other license or an interest in any other license under the terms of this article.
(b)
No person, related within the first degree of consanguinity or affinity under the civil law to any wholesale licensee or employee thereof or any person with any interest therein, shall hold any other license or an interest in any other license issued under this article.
(c)
No person or member of such person's family shall own, hold or control any interest whatsoever in more than one license to engage in the business of selling packaged distilled spirits at retail in the city.
(d)
No more than one license to engage in the sale of packaged distilled spirits at retail in the city shall be issued to any person which shall include all members of such person's family.
(e)
A licensee may take in partners or additional stockholders where it is determined that the additional capital furnished is to be used exclusively for additional inventory or expanding the facilities of the business or for building new facilities and where it appears that the licensee personally received directly none of the additional capital invested. Under this section an additional partner or stockholder must be approved by the city and, if approved, the business must obtain a new license.
(Code 1976, § 7-2006; Code 1985, § 3-27; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 3-4-1996, § III)
Sec. 4-29. - Residence requirements, agents.
(a)
All applicants must be owners of the business and shall have resided continuously within the state for 12 months prior to the date the application is made unless the applicant designates a local agent who meets such residency requirement. When the applicant is a corporation whose primary business is one licensed hereunder, the majority stockholders must meet the within requirements and the license shall be issued to the corporation and the majority stockholders. Where the applicant is a corporation whose primary business is other than the operation of an establishment requiring licensing hereunder, the license shall be issued to the corporation and an officer or agent of such corporation who meets the above requirements. If the applicant is a partnership, the same requirements pertaining to corporations shall apply to the partnership.
(b)
All applications for licenses under this article by a corporation shall name in the application one or more residents in the state as the agent and representative of the corporation to receive all communications, notices, service of process or other papers or documents, on behalf of the corporation in connection with any matter arising out of or connected with the issuance, holding, suspension, revocation or other action with respect to any city license. The application shall give the mailing addresses of the persons, and the mailing to any person at that address of any notice required to be given under this article or any other law shall be sufficient notice to the corporation. If any person shall cease to be a resident of the state, another person shall immediately be appointed in writing in such person's place, and written notice shall be given to the administrator stating the name and address of the new agent.
(Code 1976, § 7-2007; Code 1985, § 3-28; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 8-3-1987)
Sec. 4-30. - Denial of license application; granting; appeal.
(a)
The community affairs department shall deny a city license under this article on any of the following grounds:
(1)
Failure to meet state requirements for state license;
(2)
Failure to pay required fees and taxes;
(3)
Failure to provide valid information, documents and the like required by this article;
(4)
False information in the application or attached documents;
(5)
Improper residency of applicant, owner, or registered agents;
(6)
Failure to meet distance or location requirements;
(7)
Prior convictions as herein provided; or
(8)
Failure to meet any other requirements in this article for a license of the class applied for.
(b)
An application which has been denied shall not be resubmitted for 12 months after the denial unless there is a substantial change therein which would affect the reason for denial originally.
(c)
An application for a location which has been cited for a violation of this Code or state law, with regard to alcohol sales in the previous 12 months, shall come before the city council for review. In such instance the council may, after conducting a hearing with notice to the application, continue the effect and duration of any suspension or probation.
(d)
Otherwise, the community affairs department may issue any city license provided for in this article after approval of the city.
(Code 1976, § 7-2009; Code 1985, § 3-29; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 1-6-1997, § XII; Ord. of 7-10-2000, § 2)
Sec. 4-31. - License constitutes grant of privilege.
(a)
All licenses herein shall be a mere grant of privilege to carry on such business during the term of the license subject to all terms and conditions imposed by this Code relating to such businesses.
(b)
All licenses hereunder shall have printed on the front these words: "This license is a mere privilege subject to be revoked and annulled by the city council of the city and is subject to any future ordinances."
(Code 1976, § 7-2012; Code 1985, § 3-30; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-32. - Annual renewal.
All licensees hereunder shall be required to renew their licenses annually on forms prescribed by the community affairs department.
(Code 1976, § 7-2013; Code 1985, § 3-31; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 1-6-1997, § XII)
Sec. 4-33. - Duration and renewal of license.
Duration and renewal of licenses under this article shall be as for business licenses generally; and no licensee shall have any vested right to the renewal of any city license. No licenses under this article may be renewed if the licensee could be denied a new license under this article.
(Code 1976, § 7-2014; Code 1985, § 3-32; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-34. - Distance, area zoning and parking requirements.
(a)
No license shall be issued under this article where the place of business of the licensee is located a distance of less than 100 yards of any church, or 200 yards of any school building, educational building, school grounds or college campus; unless such place of business is a retail establishment for consumption on premises; in which case the distance requirement may be waived by the community affairs department if the establishment is located within the city's central business district. The mayor and council may waive at a public hearing the distance requirement for such businesses located in other commercially zoned areas at their discretion if the issuance of such license will not be deemed detrimental to property values or the general health and welfare of the general public.
(b)
No license to engage in the sale of alcoholic beverages shall be issued within the lawful areas described in this article unless the location is zoned commercial or industrial under the zoning laws of the city.
(c)
Where a retail liquor store exists in an area outside of the city limits, upon annexation of such area the same may be continued and shall be subject to all the provisions of this Code.
(d)
Locations which will retail alcoholic beverages in package only shall provide one off-street parking space for each 200 square feet of floor space in the building used for that purpose.
(Code 1976, §§ 2-2003(i), 7-2008; Code 1985, § 3-33; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 9-12-1994, § 1; Ord. No. 11-2010-01, 11-1-2010)
Sec. 4-35. - Completion of premises proposed to be licensed.
Where a building in which any person intends to operate under the provisions of this article is, at the time of the application for the license, not in existence or not yet completed, a license may be issued for the location provided the plans for the proposed building show clearly a compliance with the other provisions of this article. No sales or consumption shall be allowed in the establishment until it has been completed in accordance with the plans and is in conformity with all of the other provisions of this article and the city codes.
(Code 1976, § 7-2016; Code 1985, § 3-34; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-36. - Alteration of premises.
Where a building in which any person is licensed or intends to operate under the provisions of this article is to be altered, modified, or renovated, any such interior or exterior work shall be subject to the prior approval of the city and shall meet all applicable codes. If approval is not obtained, the license application or the next subsequent renewal license may be denied.
(Code 1976, § 7-2015; Code 1985, § 3-35; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-37. - Time limits on opening and continuing business.
(a)
All licenses issued under this article must, within 90 days after the issuance of a city license, open for business. Failure to open for business shall constitute a forfeiture and cancellation of the issued licenses and no refund of license fees or business taxes shall be made. Any applicant unable to comply with the time limit of this section may make written request to the administrator for an extension of time not to exceed 90 days; and the city at its discretion may grant or deny the request.
(b)
Any license issued under this article shall automatically be null and void where the licensed business has not operated or been open to the public for 30 days.
(Code 1976, § 7-2017; Code 1985, § 3-36; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-38. - Restrictions upon transfers.
(a)
Licenses hereunder shall not be transferable, except as otherwise provided herein.
(b)
In the case of the death of any person holding such a license, or any interest therein, the same may, at the discretion of the city, be transferred to the administrator, executor or the lawful heirs of the deceased person.
(c)
Nothing in this section, however, shall prohibit one or more of the partners in a partnership holding a license to withdraw from the partnership in favor of one or more of the partners, who were partners at the time of the issuance of the license. Such a withdrawal shall not, however, serve to bring any new ownership into the partnership.
(d)
Should a transfer of a location be approved, there shall be no pro rata return of any fee or business tax and the new location shall meet the requirements of a new license hereunder.
(e)
However, a licensee may take in partners or additional stockholders where it is determined that the additional capital furnished is to be used exclusively for additional inventory or expanding the facilities of the business or for building new facilities and where it appears that the licensee personally received directly not any of the additional capital invested. Under this section, an additional partner or new principal stockholder must be approved by the city.
(f)
All applications for transfer of locations shall comply with the provisions herein set forth governing new licenses.
(g)
No transfer shall be permitted when the license is subject to revocation, suspension or the licensee is on probation for violation of this Code.
(Code 1976, § 7-2018; Code 1985, § 3-37; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 7-10-2000, § 1)
Sec. 4-39. - Grounds for suspension or revocation; notice; hearing; refund.
(a)
No license which has been issued or which may hereafter be issued by the city to any licensee hereunder shall be suspended or revoked, except for due cause as hereinafter defined, and after a hearing and upon a prior five day written notice to the holder of such license of the time, place and purpose of such hearing and statement of the charge upon which such hearing shall be held.
(b)
Due cause for the suspension or revocation of such license shall consist of the violation of any laws or ordinances regulating such businesses, or violation of regulations made pursuant to authority granted for the purpose of regulating such businesses, or for the violation of any state or federal law, or for the violation of this Code other than traffic ordinances.
(c)
The mayor is delegated the authority to suspend any license hereunder for due cause in any emergency situation, and the suspension may be made effective immediately and remain in force until the next regular or called meeting of the city.
(d)
When a license is so revoked, the city shall not be required to refund any portion of the business license fees.
(Code 1976, § 7-2019; Code 1985, § 3-38; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-40. - Acceptance and consideration of application after rejection or revocation.
When an application for a license to sell alcoholic beverages is denied or revoked for cause by the city, it is unlawful for the administrator to accept or consider an application from the same applicant for such license to operate the same type of business within 12 months from the time of the rejection by the city.
(Code 1976, § 7-2020; Code 1985, § 3-39; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-41. - Removal of signs after revocation.
When any license for selling alcoholic beverages is revoked, all signs indicating that such beverages may be sold or purchased shall be removed from the place of business, both outside and inside. Upon receipt by the police department of notices of such revocation, the police department shall take the necessary steps to see that this section is enforced.
(Code 1976, § 7-2021; Code 1985, § 3-40; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-42. - Outstanding licenses, compliance.
(a)
So as to prevent an unconstitutional taking of property, all licensees for alcoholic beverages within the city in compliance with city ordinance requirements involving residency, distance, location, parking and type of building structure in effect at the time of first receiving the licenses and continuing to be in compliance therewith shall be exempt from the requirements of this article involving residency, distance, location, parking and type of building structure.
(b)
Renewals and transfers of business ownership at the same location shall also be deemed in compliance hereunder to the extent provided in subsection (a) of this section.
(c)
Business transfers to new locations and new licenses must comply with all requirements of this article.
(Code 1976, § 7-2022; Code 1985, § 3-41; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-43. - Licenses subject to state regulations.
All licenses issued under this article shall be subject to the rules and regulations authorized by state law and those prescribed by the state department of revenue and violation of these statutes and regulations shall be violations of this Code. In addition, all businesses, the applicant for the license, the agent in charge of the business and the owners thereof shall observe and obey the regulations provided in this article and any other applicable provisions of this article.
(Code 1976, § 7-2023; Code 1985, § 3-42; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-44. - Open to inspection.
Any establishment holding a license issued under this article shall at all times, during the period allowable by law for operation of the business, be open to inspection by any officer of the police department or any license inspector of the city or to any person designated by the police chief. In addition, if the premises are being used after hours by employees or the owners or their agents, the premises may be inspected at this time by the designated persons in this section.
(Code 1976, § 7-2032; Code 1985, § 3-14; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-45. - Penalties; license revocation.
(a)
Upon conviction in the municipal court of any violation of any penal provision of this article, unless otherwise provided, the punishment shall be as follows:
(1)
First offense: Minimum fine of $150.00;
(2)
Second and subsequent offenses, if within 12 months of the first: Minimum fine of $200.00.
(b)
Any license under this article may be revoked or suspended by the city for any conviction of the licensee or any agent, officer or employee thereof for violation of any portion of this article.
(c)
Any license issued under this article may be suspended by the mayor until the next session of the city for reason of any disturbance, acts of disorderly conduct, violence, gambling or by reason of any conviction for violation in subsection (a)(2) of this section having occurred in the establishment or on the premises thereof.
(d)
When the license of any establishment has been revoked, no further license shall be issued under this article to the same person at that location for a period of 12 months from the date of revocation.
(e)
Whenever the state shall revoke any permit or license to manufacture or sell at wholesale or retail any alcoholic beverages, the city license to manufacture or deal in those products shall thereupon be automatically revoked without any action by the city or any municipal officer.
(f)
All owners or officers of establishments licensed under this article may be held responsible for any violation of this article by an employee thereof.
(Code 1976, § 7-2033; Code 1985, § 3-15; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 7-10-2000, § 3)
Secs. 4-46—4-63. - Reserved.
DIVISION 2. - PACKAGED BEER AND WINE

Sec. 4-64. - Scope of division.
In addition to the general business license requirements of this Code and the other articles of this chapter, the following sections shall apply to the licensing and operation of retail malt beverage or wine establishments in the city and should any section of this division conflict with other provisions in this chapter or the general business license requirement of this Code, then this division shall control.
(Code 1976, § 7-2061; Code 1985, § 3-60; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-65. - Retailers—Delivery.
Retail dealers in malt beverages licensed under the applicable licensing provisions of the city shall not buy or accept deliveries of malt beverages from wholesalers or other persons offering for sale except from wholesalers duly licensed under this Code. Retail dealers shall not accept deliveries except directly to the premises for which their license and permit were issued, and by no means other than a conveyance owned and operated by a wholesaler licensed as required under this Code.
(Code 1976, § 7-2063; Code 1985, § 3-62; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-66. - Retailers—Storage.
All licensed retailers shall store all malt beverages in their possession on the premises for which the license was issued. All malt beverage stocks shall be available at all times for inspection by authorized city officials. Any brand or type of malt beverages found in any retailer's stock that is handled by a wholesaler who is not licensed to make sales and deliveries in the city shall be subject to immediate confiscation.
(Code 1976, § 7-2064; Code 1985, § 3-63; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-67. - Time of sale of wine only.
Wine in package or for consumption on premises may be sold at any time not prohibited by the state, except between 2:00 a.m. and 7:00 a.m. daily, and may be sold on any day.
(Code 1976, § 7-2065; Code 1985, § 3-64; Ord. of 4-16-1981)

DIVISION 3. - PACKAGED DISTILLED SPIRITS

Sec. 4-93. - Scope of division.
In addition to the general business license requirements of this Code and other articles of this chapter, the following sections shall apply to the licensing and operation of retail distilled spirits establishments in the city and should any section of this division conflict with other provisions in this chapter or the general business license requirements of this Code, then this division shall control.
(Code 1976, § 7-2041; Code 1985, § 3-50; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 3-4-1996, § III)
Sec. 4-94. - Applications; contents.
(a)
All applications for license, both original and renewal, must be accompanied by a full and complete statement under oath of information relative to any and all interest in retail packaged distilled spirits stores. This shall include the names and addresses of all persons interested in the ownership of the business of selling at retail packaged distilled spirits, together with any interest each person or any member of his immediate family has in any other retail packaged distilled spirits store; the ownership of the land and building where such retail business is operated; the amount of rental paid for such land and building and the manner in which the same is determined and to whom and at what intervals it is paid; the names and addresses (by affidavit from the owner, lessor or sublessor of such land and building) of all persons having any whole, partial, beneficial or other interest in and to the land and building on and in which the retail packaged distilled spirits store is located; and any other information called for by the mayor and council.
(b)
Any change in any relationship herein declared must be filed with the community affairs department when such change is made, and failure to so file within a period of 30 days after such change is made shall be grounds for revocation by the city.
(Code 1976, § 7-2042; Code 1985, § 3-51; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 3-4-1996, § III)
Sec. 4-95. - Restrictions on other merchandise.
A licensee of a retail establishment under this division shall not operate the business in connection with any other mercantile establishment; provided, this shall not prohibit a licensee from operating in the same building complex with other businesses. No retailer hereunder shall sell, offer for sale, display or keep in stock at his place of business where packaged distilled spirits are offered for sale any other commodity except the following, all of which may be sold by the retailer at his option:
(1)
Beverages containing no alcohol and commonly used to dilute distilled spirits;
(2)
Wines and malt beverages, when property licensed;
(3)
Glasses, stirring sticks and similar paraphernalia commonly used in the consumption of alcoholic beverages;
(4)
Tobacco products;
(5)
Packaged ice; and
(6)
Snack food items.
(Code 1976, § 7-2043; Code 1985, § 3-52; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 3-4-1996, § III)
Sec. 4-96. - Front window display.
If a licensee so desires he may have his name displayed on the inside of the window in neon or other electric lights. Such sign shall be parallel with the window and so constructed as not to extend more than six inches from the window. A neon sign may also be constructed along the border line or edge of the front window inside the glass.
(Code 1976, § 7-2044; Code 1985, § 3-53; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 3-4-1996, § III)
Sec. 4-97. - Display of license.
No retail package distilled spirits license shall be allowed in, or in connection with, any restaurant, cafe or eating place, or in the same room where a bar or soda fountain is maintained for the dispensing and sale of soft drinks or other beverages.
(Code 1976, § 7-2046; Code 1985, § 3-55; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 3-4-1996, § III)
Sec. 4-98. - Price lists or tags.
Each retail licensee shall have conspicuously displayed within the interior of the licensed premises not less than two copies of a printed price list of the distilled spirits offered for sale and one printed copy of the penal sections of this chapter; provided, however, that a licensee, in lieu of having two copies of a printed price list, may have the price placed on the bottles or on the bottom of the shelf where liquors are exhibited for sale.
(Code 1976, § 7-2047; Code 1985, § 3-56; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 3-4-1996, § III)
Secs. 4-99—4-124. - Reserved.
DIVISION 4. - CONSUMPTION ON THE PREMISES

Sec. 4-125. - Definitions.
In addition to definitions provided in section 4-1 and other sections of this Code, the following words, terms and phrases, when used in this division, shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section, except where the context clearly indicates a different meaning:
Lounge means a separate room connected with a part of, and adjacent to a restaurant as defined herein with all booths, stools and tables being unobstructed and open to view. All lounges shall be air conditioned and have a seating capacity of at least 40.
Private club means a corporation organized and existing under the laws of the state, having at least 100 members regularly paying monthly, quarterly, semi-annual or annual dues, organized and operated exclusively for pleasure, recreation and other nonprofitable purposes, no part of the net earnings of which inures to the benefit of any shareholder or member, and owning, hiring, or leasing a building or space therein for the reasonable use of its members with suitable kitchen, dining room space, and equipment, and maintaining and using a sufficient number of servants and employees for cooking, preparing, and serving meals for its members and guests, provided that no member, officer, agent, or employee of the club is paid, or directly or indirectly receives, in the form of salary or other compensation, any profits from the sale of alcoholic beverages to the club or its members or guests beyond the amount of such salary as may be fixed by its members at any annual meeting or by its governing board out of the general revenue of the club.
Restaurant means any public place kept, used, maintained, advertised and held out to the public as a place where meals are served and where meals are actually and regularly served, such place being provided with adequate and sanitary kitchen and dining room equipment, air conditioned, having employed therein a sufficient number and kind of employees to prepare and serve suitable hot meals for its guests. At least one meal per day shall be served at least six days a week, with the exception of holidays, vacations and periods of redecorating.
(Code 1976, § 7-2082; Code 1985, § 3-70; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-126. - Scope of division.
In addition to the general business license requirements of this Code and the other articles of this chapter, the following sections shall apply to the licensing and operation of all establishments selling alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises and should any section of this division conflict with other provisions in this chapter or the general business license requirements of this Code, then this division shall control.
(Code 1976, § 7-2081; Code 1985, § 3-71; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-127. - Additional application and license requirements.
In addition to the application and license requirements of this chapter, the following shall be required for licenses under this division:
(1)
No license to engage in the sale of distilled spirits by the drink at retail shall be granted except in the business areas zoned commercial or industrial under the zoning laws of the city.
(2)
No license under this division shall be issued to any applicant who does not meet the requirements of a restaurant, lounge or private club as defined herein.
(3)
The dining area or service area of any business licensed hereunder must be a minimum of 1,200 square feet.
(4)
Licenses shall be granted only to private clubs or restaurants, as defined hereinbefore, or to lounges operated in conjunction with such restaurants with direct access between the lounge and restaurant.
(5)
At least 50 percent of the business, by volume, shall be from the sale of prepared foods to be consumed on the premises except private clubs. Authorized officials of the city may examine the records of the businesses licensed hereunder at any reasonable time to ascertain that the requirements of this section are met.
(Code 1976, § 7-2083; Code 1985, § 3-72; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 3-4-1996, § III)
Sec. 4-128. - Additional regulations.
(a)
Drinks hereunder shall be served only by employees of the establishment.
(b)
It is unlawful for any licensee under this article to permit professional belly dancers, strip dancing, exotic dancing, or any exhibitions of any like kind by whatever name called in connection with the operation of a restaurant, lounge or private club, as defined herein.
(c)
Locations which will retail alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises shall provide one off-street parking space for each 100 square feet of floor space in the public assembly area in the building, or one off-street parking space for each four seats in the public assembly area in the building, whichever is greater. If the required off-street parking spaces cannot reasonably be provided on the same lot as the building it serves, the board of variance and zoning appeals may permit such space to be provided on other off-street property. Such property shall be within 400 feet of the premises to which it is appurtenant as measured along the nearest pedestrian walkway and will not require pedestrians to cross a state or national highway.
(Code 1976, § 7-2084; Code 1985, § 3-73; Ord. of 4-16-1981)

ARTICLE III. - BUSINESS OPERATION REQUIREMENTS

Sec. 4-155. - Open containers and consumption restricted.
No bottle, can or other container of any alcoholic beverage shall be opened, consumed or possessed in an open condition by any person in any parking area, street or sidewalk within 300 feet of any place of business licensed under this chapter, whether the container or beverage was obtained at that place of business or elsewhere, nor within the premises of any business licensed for package only sales under this article.
(Code 1976, § 7-2024; Code 1985, § 3-6; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-156. - Hours and days for sale and purchase.
Except as otherwise herein expressly provided, it is unlawful for any licensee under this article or person employed by a licensee to sell, offer for sale, transfer or offer to transfer to others any alcoholic beverage on Christmas Day; or between the hours of 2:00 a.m. on Sunday and 7:00 a.m. on the following Monday and between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 7:00 a.m. on the other days of the week; and it is unlawful for any person to purchase, receive or offer to receive from a licensee or person employed by a licensee any alcoholic beverage between the hours of 2:00 a.m. on Sunday and 7:00 a.m. on the following Monday and between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 7:00 a.m. on the other days of the week. Provided, alcoholic beverages may be purchased and sold for consumption on premises on Sundays from 12:30 p.m. until 12:00 midnight in any licensed establishment which derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross food and beverage sales from the sale of prepared meals or food in all of the combined retail outlets of the individual establishment where food is served, provided the licensee applies for and receives a permit for such sales from the city and pays the separate license fee therefor as established from time to time by the city. Such application for license shall have attached hereto an accounting by dollars and by percentage of total sales annually, revenues derived from the sale of food and alcoholic beverages. Provided further, the sale of alcoholic beverages in the city shall be permitted on any election day, as the same is authorized and defined in O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20.
(Code 1976, § 7-2025; Code 1985, § 3-7; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 3-7-1983; Ord. of 4-17-1983; Ord. of 6-2-1986; Ord. of 8-11-1986; Mo. of 10-2-1989; Ord. of 3-4-1996, § IV)
State Law reference— Sale on Sundays and election days, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-20.
Sec. 4-157. - Prohibited practices; hours and places for drinking.
(a)
It is unlawful for any person owning or operating a place of business licensed under this chapter for consumption on the premises to permit any person to drink any whiskey, wine, beer or other alcoholic beverage upon the business premises between the hours of 2:00 a.m. on Sunday and 7:00 a.m. on the following Monday and between the hours of 2:00 a.m. and 7:00 a.m. on the other days of the week.
(b)
It is unlawful for any person to drink any whiskey, wine, beer, or other alcoholic beverage while upon, or while upon or within any vehicle upon, the streets, sidewalks or parking areas in the city within 300 feet of a licensed premises; or upon, or while upon or within any vehicle upon, the public parks of the city without regard to their distance from a licensed premises. Provided, alcoholic beverages may be purchased and sold for consumption on premises on Sundays from 12:30 p.m. until 12:00 midnight in any licensed establishment which derives at least 50 percent of its total annual gross food and beverages sales from the sale of prepared meals or food in all of the combined retail outlets of the individual establishment where food is served, provided the licensee applies for and receives a permit for such sales from the city and pays the separate license fee therefor as established from time to time by the city. Such application for license shall have attached thereto an accounting by dollars and by percentage of total sales annually, revenues derived from the sale of food and alcoholic beverages.
(c)
This section shall be construed to cover, include and apply to every type of alcoholic beverages licensed to be sold in the city, including beer, wine, malt beverages or distilled spirits.
(d)
Each licensee shall maintain a daily schedule of the prices to be charged for all alcoholic beverages to be served and consumed on the licensed premises or in any room or part thereof. The licensee shall not vary the prices from hour to hour within a single day. The schedule of prices shall be maintained in a manner so as to be available to the paying public and law enforcement officers or agents of governmental authority. The schedule shall be effective for not less than a 24-hour period beginning at 2:01 a.m. and ending at 2:00 a.m. the following day and shall show thereon the date for which the schedule is effective.
(e)
No licensee or holder of any license to sell alcoholic beverages for consumption on the premises or in any part thereof, or employee or agency of a licensee, shall:
(1)
Offer or deliver any free alcoholic beverage to any individual or member of the general public, except as hereinafter provided;
(2)
Deliver more than two alcoholic beverages to one person at one time, except as hereinafter provided;
(3)
Sell, offer to sell, or deliver to any person or group of persons any alcoholic beverage at a price less than the price regularly charged for such alcoholic beverage during the same 24-hour period for which the price schedule herein required is in effect, except at private functions not open to the public;
(4)
Sell, offer to sell, or deliver to any person or group of persons two or more or an unlimited number of alcoholic beverages during any set period of time for a fixed price, except at private functions not open to the public;
(5)
Sell, offer to sell, or deliver alcoholic beverages to any person or group of persons on any one day at prices less than those charged the general public on that day, except at private functions not open to the public;
(6)
Sell, offer to sell, or deliver alcoholic beverages, by the pitcher or carafe, except to two or more persons at any one time or as hereinafter provided;
(7)
Increase the volume of alcohol contained in a drink without increasing proportionately the price regularly charged for such alcoholic beverage during the same 24-hour period for which the price schedule herein required is in effect;
(8)
Encourage or permit on the licensed premises any game or contest which involves the drinking of any alcoholic beverages; or the awarding of alcoholic beverages as a prize, unless such prize be in unopened containers and the containers remain unopened on the premises and licensee assumes the responsibility to see that the containers remain unopened until they are removed from the premises. If the containers are opened on the premises, both the licensee and the customer shall be in violation of this section;
(9)
Sell two or more alcoholic beverages for a price substantially the same as is charged one such alcoholic beverage;
(10)
Require or encourage the purchase of a second or subsequent alcoholic beverage at the same time another alcoholic beverage is purchased;
(11)
Knowingly allow an alcoholic beverage purchased on the premises to be removed from the premises without having been consumed. The term "premises," for the purpose of this subsection, shall be construed to mean the entire area under the supervision, management or control of the licensee, excluding areas for parking of motor vehicles, and, in the case of licensees whose licensed location is located on a portion of the premises of a club, organization, establishment or entity offering outdoor recreation (for instance, golf or tennis) then the term "premises" shall extend to cover all areas operated as a part of the club or entity excluding areas for parking of motor vehicles.
(f)
No licensee shall advertise or promote in any way, whether within or without the licensed premises, any of the practices prohibited under subsection (e) of this section.
(g)
No provision of this section shall be construed to prohibit licensees from offering free food or entertainment at any time, or to prohibit licensees from including an alcoholic beverage as a part of a meal, package, or to prohibit the sale or delivery of wine by the bottle or carafe when sold with meals or to more than one person, or to prohibit any hotel or motel from offering room services to registered guests, or to prohibit the sale of more than two drinks at one time which are to be consumed by the purchaser out of doors on the premises of the licensee, as hereinabove described, in connection with the purchaser's participation as a player in the games of golf or tennis; otherwise no food and alcoholic beverage package may be offered by any licensee; provided, however, nothing contained herein shall be construed to allow a licensee to circumvent the intent of this section by offering meals which include an alcoholic beverage as a device or scheme to promote drink sales at a price per beverage less than the daily listed price.
(h)
Violations of this section shall be punishable by a fine not to exceed $500.00 or imprisonment for 60 days, or both. Violations shall be considered due cause for suspension or revocation of any license.
(Code 1976, § 7-2026; Code 1985, § 3-8; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 3-7-1983; Ord. of 10-17-1983; Ord. of 2-3-1986; Ord. of 6-2-1986; Ord. of 3-4-1996, § III)
Sec. 4-158. - Drive-in and curb service.
(a)
Curb service by retail or consumption on the premises licensees under this chapter or delivery of any alcoholic beverages off the licensed premises, other than by wholesalers licensed hereunder or by the state is hereby prohibited.
(b)
Drive-in service windows at premises licensed under this chapter are hereby prohibited, except as provided in this section.
(c)
Any business authorized by the state to provide drive-in windows may do so only as follows:
(1)
The window shall be clear and unobstructed for a minimum of 24 inches wide and 36 inches in height.
(2)
The location of the window shall be lighted in a manner that will enable the attendant of the window to clearly view the entire interior of any vehicle being served at the window.
(Code 1976, § 7-2027; Code 1985, § 3-9; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-159. - Furnishing alcoholic beverages to minors; prohibited practices.
(a)
It is unlawful for any person, business or any person in charge of, or employed in, any place of business where alcoholic beverages are kept to furnish to a minor, to permit any minor to buy, or to allow to be bought for a minor any alcoholic beverages. A minor shall be defined as anyone under 21 years of age.
(b)
When any person in charge of, or employed in, any place of business sells any alcoholic beverage to a minor or otherwise violates this division the business shall be subject to the following:
(1)
On first offense, the alcoholic beverage license will be suspended for a period of 60 days.
(2)
On second offense, the alcoholic beverage license will be suspended for a period of six months.
(3)
On third offense, the alcoholic beverage license will be revoked.
Any licensee who is found to be selling alcoholic beverages to anyone while their license is in suspension shall have their license revoked. Returns of alcoholic beverages to wholesalers shall not be deemed to be sales within the meaning of this section.
(c)
Any attempt to commit any act made unlawful by this section shall be itself unlawful and subject to punishment in the municipal court.
(d)
In addition to citations for alcoholic beverage license violations, any person may be issued citations for violation of state law regarding furnishing alcoholic beverages to minors or purchase or possession of alcoholic beverage by minors or any related state offenses.
(e)
It is unlawful for any person to allow or require a person in such person's employment under 21 years of age to dispense, serve, sell or take orders for any alcoholic beverage; provided, however, that the provisions of this section shall not prohibit persons under 21 years of age who are employed in supermarkets, convenience stores or drug stores from selling or handling alcoholic beverages which are sold for consumption off the premises.
(f)
No business or person who holds a license to sell alcoholic beverages shall allow any minors to be in, frequent, or loiter about the premises of the licensee unless such minors are accompanied by a parent or legal guardian.
(g)
Any action with regard to an alcoholic beverage license shall be heard before the city council. Any ordinance violation not involving alcoholic beverage licensing shall be punishable in the municipal court.
(Code 1976, §§ 3-1008, 7-2028; Code 1985, § 3-10; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 6-2-1986; Ord. of 10-3-1988; Ord. of 12-2-1996, § 1)
State Law reference— Sale to persons noticeably intoxicated, O.C.G.A. § 3-3-22; sale, etc., to minors, O.C.G.A. §§ 3-3-23—3-3-24.2.
Sec. 4-160. - Sale or delivery to unlicensed premises.
It is unlawful for any licensee hereunder to make deliveries of any alcoholic beverage beyond the boundaries of the premises covered by the license.
(Code 1976, § 7-2029; Code 1985, § 3-11; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-161. - Misrepresentation of alcoholic beverages.
It is unlawful for licensees hereunder or their agents to add to the contents of a bottle or to refill empty bottles or in any other manner to misrepresent the quantity, quality or brand name of any alcoholic beverage.
(Code 1976, § 7-2030; Code 1985, § 3-12; Ord. of 4-16-1981)
Sec. 4-162. - Slot machines and pool tables prohibited on licensed premises.
No licensee under this chapter shall operate as a game room on the licensed premises without a game room license issued pursuant to this Code.
(Code 1976, § 7-2031; Code 1985, § 3-13; Ord. of 4-16-1981; Ord. of 4-5-1999, § 4)
