ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 15-1. - Definition.
For the purposes of this chapter, the term "public parks" shall mean and refer to all municipally owned or municipally maintained parks of the city, whether located wholly within, wholly without, or partly within and partly without, the corporate limits of the city.
(Code 1985, § 5-1-81)
Cross reference— Definitions and rules of construction generally, § 1-2.
Sec. 15-2. - Penalty for violation.
Violators of any rule or restriction in this chapter shall be subject to a fine set by the municipal court, in accordance with section 1-12.
(Code 1985, § 5-1-91)
Sec. 15-3. - Protection of park vegetation.
It shall be unlawful for any person to break, pluck, walk, step on or in any way injure or destroy any shrub, flowers or bush, or to dig, uproot, tear up or injure or destroy any shrub, any flowers, or bush, or to dig, uproot, tear up or injure or destroy any sod or grass in any of the public parks, or to walk, drive, sit or go upon any space or area in any of the public parks where a "Keep Off" sign has been posted.
(Code 1985, § 5-1-82)
Sec. 15-4. - Protection of park structures.
It shall be unlawful for any person to write on, cut, deface, injure or break any part of any building, grandstand or other structure or any chair, seat, bench, fence in any of the public parks or upset any benches, chairs, or seats in any public park.
(Code 1985, § 5-1-83)
Sec. 15-5. - Advertising in public parks.
It shall be unlawful for any person to erect any bill posters or to post or tack up or otherwise display any bills or advertising signs or to distribute handbills in any public park except with permission from the city.
(Code 1985, § 5-1-86)
Sec. 15-6. - Concessions in public parks.
It shall be unlawful for any person, except such as may have a permit or concession from the city commission of the city, to sell or offer for sale in any public park any cold drinks, foods, eatables, cigars, tobacco or other merchandise except with permission from the city.
(Code 1985, § 5-1-87)
Cross reference— Licenses and business regulations, ch. 10.
Sec. 15-7. - Alcohol in public parks.
No malt beverage, wine or alcoholic beverage of any kind shall be permitted in the city park.
(Code 1985, § 5-1-88)
Cross reference— Alcoholic beverages, ch. 3.
Sec. 15-8. - Internal combustion engines prohibited on waterworks lake.
It shall be unlawful for any person to operate any boat propelled by an internal combustion engine on the city waterworks lake.
(Code 1985, § 5-1-90)
Secs. 15-9—15-35. - Reserved.

State Law reference— Joint recreation systems, O.C.G.A. § 36-64-4 et seq.

Sec. 15-36. - General authority.
The Toccoa-Stephens County Parks and Recreation Department shall be operated utilizing the resources of the city and county.
(Ord. No. 90-039, § 1(2-7-1), 12-17-90)
Sec. 15-37. - Joint committee, chairman.
The members of the county commission and the city commission will serve as a joint committee to develop policies and procedures for the operation of the city-county parks and recreation department. For the year 1990 the mayor shall serve as chairman of this committee. Thereafter the chair shall alternate between the county commission chairman and mayor every year.
(Ord. No. 90-039, § 1(2-7-2), 12-17-90)
Sec. 15-38. - Annual budget.
The joint recreation committee shall approve an annual budget, including operations and capital, of which all costs shall be divided on a 50/50 percent basis with the city paying one-half and the county paying one-half.
(Ord. No. 90-039, § 1(2-7-3), 12-17-90)
Sec. 15-39. - Operation, management, ownership of facilities.
All public recreation facilities, parks, playgrounds, and equipment owned by the city and/or county shall be operated and managed by the city-county parks and recreation department. Ownership of all such facilities will remain with either the city or county and not with the recreation department.
(Ord. No. 90-039, § 1(2-7-4), 12-17-90)
Sec. 15-40. - Administrative organization.
The city-county parks and recreation department will operate as a department of the city, except as previously defined for budgetary and policy matters. The recreation director will work for the city manager. Employees of the recreation department will be employees of the city and entitled to all rights and privileges thereof, as provided for in the department's annual budget.
(Ord. No. 90-039, § 1(2-7-5), 12-17-90)
Sec. 15-41. - Operating procedures.
The county and the city agree to establish from time to time policies and procedures for the city-county parks and recreation department and to annually approve an operating and capital budget for the department. All actions taken by the county and the city in connection with the department shall be approved by a majority vote of the members of the city commission and a majority vote of the members of the county board of commissioners.
(Ord. No. 90-039, § 1(2-7-6), 12-17-90)
