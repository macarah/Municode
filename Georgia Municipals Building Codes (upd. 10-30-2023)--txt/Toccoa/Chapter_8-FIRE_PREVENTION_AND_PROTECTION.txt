ARTICLE I. - IN GENERAL

Sec. 8-1. - Interfering with, and obstructing or hindering members of the fire department.
It shall be unlawful for any person to interfere with any member of the fire department, or to obstruct or hinder any member of the fire department, while the member of the fire department is engaged in the performance of his official duties.
(Code 1985, § 4-2-12)
Sec. 8-2. - Opening fire hydrant.
It shall be unlawful for any person, other than a member of the fire department, to open or turn on any fire hydrant on the city system without permission from the city.
(Code 1985, § 4-2-14)
Secs. 8-3—8-30. - Reserved.

Sec. 8-31. - General responsibility, department head.
The fire department shall be responsible for fire prevention and protection in the city. The head of the department shall be the chief of the fire department.
(Code 1985, § 4-2-1)
Sec. 8-32. - Chief; powers and duties.
The chief of the fire department shall:
(1)
Execute all orders and directions of the city manager.
(2)
Under the general direction of the city manager, be in charge of the fire department and have supervision over its members.
(3)
Exercise and delegate police powers in the enforcement of any provision of this Code or any other ordinance pertaining to the fire department, right-of-way, inspections of buildings and fire escapes, or other provisions or ordinances pertaining to fire protection.
(4)
Perform such other duties as may be required by the city manager.
(Code 1985, § 4-2-2)
Sec. 8-33. - Service outside city.
The city fire department shall not serve any person located outside the city limits of the city, except for church buildings, schools or educational buildings and also anyone who registers with the city fire department and shall have paid the required fee. The required fee as established shall be due annually on or before March 1 in each year of registration. The rate and charges for fire service by the city shall be established from time to time by the city commission by duly enacted resolution.
(Ord. No. 90-011, § 1, 2-26-90)
